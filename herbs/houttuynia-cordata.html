Dragon Herb (Houttuynia cordata): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

Dragon Herb (Houttuynia cordata) is a versatile medicinal plant widely used in traditional Chinese medicine for its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and detoxifying properties.

What is Dragon Herb (Houttuynia Cordata)?

Dragon Herb (Houttuynia cordata) is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and a distinctive, aromatic scent, commonly used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and detoxifying properties.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Dragon herb is Houttuynia cordata. This plant belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division unknown, class unknown, order unknown, family Sarmentaceae, genus Houttuynia, and species Houttuynia cordata. It is commonly known as Dragon herb in China, and as Rauvolfia in Southeast Asia. The synonyms for this plant include Houttuynia cordata Thunb., Houttuynia cordata var. cordata, and Houttuynia cordata var. crispa.

Plant Description and Morphology

Dragon herb is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 30-100 cm, with a sprawling, bushy structure and a creeping rhizome that spreads horizontally.

Its leaves are heart-shaped, broad, and have a waxy texture, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter green underside. The flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in clusters, with a mild, slightly sweet fragrance. The root system consists of fibrous roots that emerge from the rhizome and anchor the plant firmly in the soil.

The plant has a fresh, aromatic scent, with a slightly spicy and earthy flavor when crushed.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Dragon herb is native to Southeast Asia, particularly China, India, and parts of Japan.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions with warm, humid climates. The plant is commonly found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks where the soil is moist and rich in organic matter. It prefers partial to full shade and is often associated with humid forest understories.

Its natural range extends from eastern India through Southeast Asia to the Philippines and parts of Indonesia.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The leaves, stems, and roots of Dragon Herb (Houttuynia cordata) are the primary plant parts used medicinally.

Phytochemical Composition

Dragon Herb (Houttuynia cordata) contains a variety of bioactive compounds including flavonoids, terpenoids, volatile oils, and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties and therapeutic effects.

Pharmacological Properties

Dragon Herb (Houttuynia cordata) exhibits a range of pharmacological properties including antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and immunomodulatory effects, supported by both traditional use and modern scientific research.

Mechanisms of Action

Dragon Herb (Houttuynia cordata) exerts its therapeutic effects through multiple mechanisms, including antimicrobial activity, anti-inflammatory properties, and the modulation of immune responses, primarily due to the presence of bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, terpenoids, and essential oils.

Therapeutic Uses and Indications

Dragon Herb (Houttuynia cordata) is traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat infections, inflammation, and digestive issues due to its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and detoxifying properties.

Preparation Methods and Forms

Dragon Herb (Houttuynia cordata) is typically prepared as a fresh herb, either consumed raw in salads, brewed into a tea, or used in stir-fries, and it is also available in dried powder form for medicinal purposes.

Dosage and Administration

The typical dosage of Dragon Herb (Houttuynia cordata) for therapeutic use ranges from 10 to 30 grams per day, typically prepared as a decoction or infusion, and should be adjusted based on individual health conditions and professional guidance.

Safety, Side Effects, and Contraindications

While Dragon Herb (Houttuynia cordata) is generally considered safe when used in moderate amounts, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort, allergic reactions, or interact with certain medications, and it should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation.

Drug Interactions

Dragon Herb (Houttuynia cordata) may interact with certain medications, including anticoagulants, antiplatelet agents, and drugs metabolized by the liver, potentially increasing the risk of bleeding or altering drug metabolism.
