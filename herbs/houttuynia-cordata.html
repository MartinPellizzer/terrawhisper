Dragon herb

Houttuynia cordata

Dragon Herb (Houttuynia cordata) is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia, known for its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and its traditional use in treating various health conditions, including infections and inflammatory disorders.

Morphology

Dragon Herb (Houttuynia cordata) is a perennial herb characterized by heart-shaped, light green leaves with a distinct reddish stem and clusters of small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer.

Distribution

Houttuynia cordata, commonly known as Dragon Herb, is native to Southeast Asia, including countries such as China, India, Japan, and Korea, and is also found in parts of tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

Traditional Uses

Dragon Herb (Houttuynia cordata) has been traditionally used in Chinese and Southeast Asian medicine to treat infections, inflammation, and digestive issues due to its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and detoxifying properties.

Medicinal Systems

In traditional medicinal systems such as Traditional Chinese Medicine, Ayurveda, and Indigenous healing practices across Southeast Asia, Dragon Herb (Houttuynia cordata) has been utilized for its purported antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and detoxifying properties.

Phytochemistry

The phytochemistry of Dragon Herb (Houttuynia cordata) involves a diverse array of bioactive compounds, including flavonoids, phenolic acids, terpenoids, and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties and therapeutic potential.

Pharmacology

Dragon Herb (Houttuynia cordata) exhibits pharmacological properties including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, antioxidant, and immunomodulatory effects, which support its traditional use in treating infections, respiratory conditions, and inflammatory disorders.

Safety Profile

Dragon Herb (Houttuynia cordata) is generally safe when used in moderate amounts. However, it may cause gastrointestinal upset in some individuals. Long-term or high-dose use should be avoided, and it is contraindicated during pregnancy and lactation due to potential adverse effects.
