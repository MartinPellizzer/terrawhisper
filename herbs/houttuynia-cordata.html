Houttuynia cordata

What's the taxonomical classification of Houttuynia cordata?

Houttuynia cordata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Piperales. The plant is a member of the family Saururaceae, which is further defined by its classification into the genus Houttuynia, with the specific species identified as cordata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Houttuynia cordata has heart-shaped, cordate leaves that are typically three to five centimeters long and feature serrated margins with a distinctive reddish-purple tint on the undersides. The plant grows from a creeping rhizomatous stem that spreads horizontally along the ground to form dense mats. Its foliage is often characterized by a bright green color on the upper surface and a prominent vein pattern. Small, inconspicuous white flowers emerge on slender stalks during the summer months. The overall structure is low-growing and herbaceous, making it an effective groundcover in moist environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to East Asia, specifically occurring in the temperate regions of China, Japan, Korea, and parts of Russia. It typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest edges, riverbanks, and mountain slopes. Within these habitats, it prefers well-drained soil that maintains consistent moisture levels throughout the growing season. Its distribution is largely dictated by the availability of damp, cool microclimates found in deciduous woodland settings. Because of its hardiness, it has also been introduced to various other temperate zones around the world for ornamental use.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a variety of medicinal compounds including flavonoids such as quercetin, rutin, and kaempferol, along with the triterpenoid betulinic acid. These specific flavonoids are known for their potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties within the human body. Quercetin and rutin work together to support cardiovascular health and strengthen capillary walls. Kaempferol contributes to cellular protection by neutralizing harmful free radicals. Additionally, betulinic acid is researched for its potential anti-tumor and anti-viral activities. Together, these bioactive constituents provide the plant with its complex therapeutic profile.

As discussed in a study, flavonoids are one of the major bioactive constituents of Houttuynia cordata. The flavonoids extracted from Houttuynia cordata, known as HCF, contained 78.5% flavonoid glycosides. Within the HCF, the contents of rutin, hyperin, isoquercitrin, and quercitrin were 8.8%, 26.7%, 9.9%, and 31.7% respectively. Among these four major flavonoid glycosides, hyperin and quercitrin played key roles in the therapeutic effect.
