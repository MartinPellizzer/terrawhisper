Sulcate Valerian (Valeriana Sulcata)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Valeriana sulcata.

Sulcate valerian, scientifically known as Valeriana sulcata, is a lesser-known medicinal herb native to parts of Asia and the Middle East, prized for its adaptogenic properties and calming effects. Traditionally used in Ayurvedic and traditional Chinese medicine, it has been valued for its ability to reduce anxiety, improve sleep quality, and support nervous system health. The plant contains unique compounds such as valerenic acid, which may contribute to its calming effects, and it is also noted for its distinct earthy and slightly bitter flavor. In modern wellness practices, Sulcate valerian is increasingly being explored as a natural alternative to synthetic sedatives, particularly for stress relief and sleep support. Its historical use dates back centuries, with ancient texts referencing its role in promoting mental clarity and emotional balance.
