What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Altingia excelsa has a woody, arborescent structure characterized by a tall, straight trunk and a dense, spreading crown of elliptical, dark green leaves. The foliage is typically arranged in an alternate pattern along the branches to maximize light interception. Its bark presents a textured, grayish surface that provides protection for the vascular tissues. The reproductive organs consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the axillary regions of the stems. These botanical features collectively define its robust and upright growth habit.
