What to know about Hemidesmus indicus (indian sarsaparilla) before using it medicinally

Indian sarsaparilla, scientifically known as Hemidesmus indicus, is a herb that has been revered for its numerous health benefits, including its ability to aid in digestion and reduce inflammation.

In the culinary world, Indian sarsaparilla is often used as a flavoring agent in teas, soups, and stews, adding a unique sweet and slightly bitter taste. Horticulturally, Indian sarsaparilla is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. Botanically, it belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its long, thin stems with heart-shaped leaves.

Historically, Indian sarsaparilla has been used in traditional medicine for centuries, with references dating back to ancient Ayurvedic texts and traditional Chinese medicine.
