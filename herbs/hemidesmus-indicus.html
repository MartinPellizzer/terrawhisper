Indian Sarsaparilla (Hemidesmus indicus)

Indian Sarsaparilla (Hemidesmus indicus) is a member of the Acanthaceae family, native to India, Sri Lanka, and Southern Asia. Traditionally, its roots, stems, and leaves have been used for decoctions, powders, and infusions.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, tonic, and sedative actions, and has a long history of use in ayurvedic medicine, unani medicine, and traditional chinese medicine.

Broad leaves with upper surface glabrous and pale green, lower surface glabrous and lighter green, possessing distinct stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Flowers are actinomorphic, with white to pale yellow petals arranged in a solitary umbel, typically five in number, and bearing prominent yellow staminodes along the inner edge of the perianth.
Stems
Climbing, tendrilled stems with opposite branching, pubescent surface, and pustulate nodes.
