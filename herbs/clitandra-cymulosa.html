What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clitandra cymulosa has a prostrate or ascending growth habit characterized by slender, hairy stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its reproductive structures consist of small, zygomorphic flowers that emerge from terminal or axillary clusters. These blooms are typically pale in color and feature a complex petal arrangement characteristic of the Fabaceae family.
