What's the taxonomical classification of Cyrtomium macrophyllum?

Cyrtomium macrophyllum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. Furthermore, its taxonomic hierarchy places it in the family Polypodiaceae, falling under the genus Cyrtomium, and is specifically identified by the species name macrophyllum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cyrtomium macrophyllum has large, pinnate fronds that can reach up to 1 meter in length, characterized by numerous narrow, lanceolate pinnae arranged along a central rachis. The texture of the foliage is typically soft and somewhat herbaceous compared to many other fern species. The plant features a creeping rhizome that allows it to spread through the forest floor substrate. Small, inconspicuous sori are located on the underside of the leaflets, which are responsible for spore production. These morphological traits allow the plant to thrive in the shaded, moist environments of temperate forests.
