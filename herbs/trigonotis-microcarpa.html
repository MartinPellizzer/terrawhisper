Trigonotis microcarpa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Trigonotis microcarpa has an annual herbaceous habit characterized by slender, ascending to erect stems that typically reach heights of 5 to 20 centimeters. The leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem and possess an ovate to lanceolate shape with entire margins. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, featuring a tubular corolla that is typically white or pale pink. The fruit is a minute, capsule-like structure that gives the species its specific epithet. These morphological traits collectively distinguish it from other members of the Linderniaceae family.
