Trigonotis microcarpa

What's the taxonomical classification of Trigonotis microcarpa?

Trigonotis microcarpa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Boraginales. This plant is a member of the family Boraginaceae, specifically situated within the genus Trigonotis, and is identified by its unique species name, microcarpa.

What are the therapeutic actions of Trigonotis microcarpa?

Trigonotis microcarpa has medicinal properties characterized by its ability to act as a mild diuretic, anti-inflammatory agent, and digestive aid within traditional herbal practices. The plant contains bioactive compounds that assist in the regulation of fluid balance by promoting kidney function. These phytochemicals also contribute to soothing gastrointestinal irritation and supporting metabolic processes. Researchers often look toward such small flowering herbs to identify secondary metabolites that can mitigate oxidative stress.
