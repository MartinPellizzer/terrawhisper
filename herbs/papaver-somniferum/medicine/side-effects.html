2. Increases risk of overdose

Papaver somniferum induces nausea and vomiting due to its high concentration of alkaloids such as morphine, codeine, and thebaine.

These compounds can stimulate the vagus nerve, leading to increased muscle activity in the stomach, resulting in symptoms like nausea and vomiting.

The potency and unpredictable absorption rate of these alkaloids can further exacerbate gastrointestinal discomfort, making Papaver somniferum use potentially hazardous for individuals sensitive to opiate-based substances.

Papaver somniferum fosters delirium heavily due to its potent opioid compounds, particularly morphine and codeine.
