1. Causes stomach cramps

Physalis alkekengi, also known as Chinese lantern, causes stomach cramps due to its ability to stimulate digestive processes and increase gut motility.

This can lead to discomfort, pain, and cramping in the abdominal area, particularly when used in large doses or without adequate preparation. The saponins present in Physalis alkekengi may also contribute to this side effect by altering the gut's natural balance of flora and fauna.

As a result, individuals using Physalis alkekengi may experience stomach cramps as their body adjusts to its effects.

2. Triggers allergic reactions

Physalis alkekengi, also known as Chinese Lantern, triggers allergic reactions due to its high content of allergenic compounds such as saponins and flavonoids.

These chemicals can cause an overactive immune response, leading to symptoms like hives, itching, and redness.

Additionally, the plant's volatile oils may irritate the skin, eyes, or respiratory tract, exacerbating allergic reactions in some individuals.

3. Induces dizziness quickly

Physalis alkekengi, also known as Chinese Lantern, induces dizziness quickly due to its potential interaction with certain medications, such as antihistamines and antidepressants.

The herb's active compounds, including alkaloids and flavonoids, can affect the central nervous system, leading to decreased blood flow to the brain and resulting in feelings of lightheadedness or dizziness.

Additionally, Physalis alkekengi may also cause a drop in blood pressure, further contributing to the onset of dizziness.

4. Increases blood pressure

Physalis alkekengi, also known as Chinese lantern, increases blood pressure.

This is due to its high content of flavonoids and alkaloids, which can cause vasoconstriction and stimulate the sympathetic nervous system, leading to a rise in blood pressure.

Additionally, the plant's saponins can also contribute to this effect by increasing the production of renin, an enzyme that helps regulate blood pressure.

5. Causes vomiting frequently

Physalis alkekengi, also known as Chinese lantern, causes vomiting frequently due to its ability to stimulate digestive tract secretions and increase gastric acid production.

The herb's alkaloids may irritate the stomach lining, leading to nausea and vomiting in some individuals.

Additionally, Physalis alkekengi's diuretic properties can cause dehydration, which may exacerbate vomiting.

6. Affects kidney function badly

Physalis alkekengi, also known as Chinese lantern, affects kidney function badly due to its ability to inhibit the activity of certain enzymes responsible for filtering waste products from the blood.

The plant's saponins and flavonoids may also contribute to this effect by reducing blood flow to the kidneys and increasing urine production, leading to decreased kidney function over time.

7. Reduces liver enzymes

Physalis alkekengi, also known as Chinese lantern, reduces liver enzymes.

This side effect is believed to be due to its ability to inhibit the activity of certain liver enzymes, which are responsible for detoxifying and metabolizing substances in the body.

As a result, individuals may experience changes in their liver function test results, potentially leading to concerns about liver health.

8. Interacts with medications

Physalis alkekengi, also known as Chinese Lantern, interacts with medications due to its potential to alter liver function and affect enzyme activity.

This may lead to increased levels of certain medications in the bloodstream, potentially causing adverse effects or interactions with other substances.

Additionally, Physalis alkekengi's ability to stimulate the immune system could enhance the body's natural response to medication, leading to increased sensitivity or intolerance.

9. Worsens asthma symptoms

Physalis alkekengi, also known as Chinese lantern, worsens asthma symptoms due to its allergenic properties.

The plant contains saponins and flavonoids that can cause an allergic reaction in some individuals, leading to airway constriction and inflammation, which exacerbates asthma symptoms.

Additionally, the presence of these compounds may trigger the release of histamine, a chemical known for causing bronchial smooth muscle contraction, further contributing to worsening of asthma symptoms.

10. Causes insomnia severely

Physalis alkekengi, also known as Chinese lantern, causes insomnia severely due to its stimulating effects on the central nervous system.

The herb's ability to increase heart rate and blood pressure can disrupt normal sleep patterns, leading to difficulty falling asleep or staying asleep.

Additionally, Physalis alkekengi's property of increasing energy levels can make it challenging for individuals to wind down and relax before bedtime, further exacerbating insomnia.
