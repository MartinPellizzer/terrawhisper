1. Causes skin irritation

Euphorbia cyparissias causes skin irritation due to its milky sap containing latex, a common allergen.

When the sap comes into contact with human skin, it can cause an allergic reaction, leading to redness, itching, and burning sensations. The irritation may be mild or severe depending on individual sensitivity and the amount of sap exposure.

This side effect is often attributed to the plant's defense mechanism against herbivores, which helps protect its vital tissues from damage.

2. Induces allergic reactions

Euphorbia cyparissias induces allergic reactions due to its milky sap containing allergenic compounds such as resin and latex.

When the sap comes into contact with skin or mucous membranes, it can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms like redness, itching, and swelling.

The presence of these allergenic substances in the plant's tissues makes it a potential allergen, causing reactions in some individuals who handle or come into contact with Euphorbia cyparissias.

3. Triggers asthma attacks

Euphorbia cyparissias triggers asthma attacks due to the presence of latex in its sap.

When the plant's leaves or stems are bruised or broken, they release this latex into the air, causing respiratory issues in individuals with asthma.

The latex contains allergenic compounds that can stimulate an immune response, leading to inflammation and constriction of the airways, which can trigger severe breathing difficulties.

4. Irritates eyes heavily

Euphorbia cyparissias irritates eyes heavily due to its milky sap, which contains irritating compounds such as latex and resin.

When the plant's sap comes into contact with the eyes, it can cause significant discomfort, redness, and tearing.

The sap's alkaloids and phenolic acids are likely responsible for this adverse reaction, as they can stimulate the eye's mucous membranes and trigger an intense inflammatory response.

5. Discolors skin permanently

Euphorbia cyparissias discolors skin permanently due to its milky sap containing toxic compounds called euphors.

When the sap comes into contact with human skin, it can cause a chemical reaction that leads to permanent discoloration, including darkening or whitening of the skin.

The exact mechanism is not fully understood, but it is believed to be related to the breakdown of melanin, the pigment responsible for skin color.

6. Burns skin deeply

Euphorbia cyparissias burns skin deeply due to its milky sap, which contains a chemical called crotonase.

When the sap comes into contact with human skin, it triggers an allergic reaction that can cause severe irritation, inflammation, and even blistering.

The sap's acidic nature also contributes to its ability to burn the skin, making Euphorbia cyparissias a plant to be handled with caution.

7. Produces severe blisters

Euphorbia cyparissias produces severe blisters due to its milky sap, which contains a toxic compound called euphorbin.

When the plant's sap comes into contact with skin, it causes an allergic reaction that leads to the formation of painful and blistering rashes.

The severity of the reaction can vary depending on the individual's sensitivity to the sap, but in some cases, it can be quite severe and even lead to scarring.

8. Causes respiratory problems

Euphorbia cyparissias causes respiratory problems due to its allergenic properties.

The plant's milky sap and leaves contain allergens that can trigger an immune response in some individuals, leading to inflammation and constriction of airways. This reaction can manifest as asthma-like symptoms, including wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.

In severe cases, the respiratory problems may be life-threatening for those with pre-existing conditions or compromised lung function.

9. Increases heart rate significantly

Euphorbia cyparissias increases heart rate significantly due to its high concentration of cardiac glycosides.

These compounds, found in the plant's latex, can stimulate the heart muscle, leading to an increase in heart rate and cardiac output.

This effect is thought to be caused by the glycosides' ability to block the potassium channels in the heart cells, which in turn increases the heart's contraction force and frequency.

10. Lowers blood pressure sharply

Euphorbia cyparissias lowers blood pressure sharply.

This is due to its ability to stimulate the vagus nerve, which slows down the heart rate and reduces cardiac output, resulting in a decrease in blood pressure.

Additionally, Euphorbia cyparissias contains saponins, which can cause vasodilation and further contribute to decreased blood pressure.
