Centipeda minima

What's the taxonomical classification of Centipeda minima?

Centipeda minima belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is situated under the order Asterales. It is further categorized into the family Asteraceae, which is a large and diverse group of flowering plants. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Centipeda, and its specific identification is completed by its species name, minima.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Centipeda minima has small, inconspicuous flowers with five petals that are typically white or pale pink and a distinctively narrow, linear leaf structure. The plant is characterized by its diminutive stature and slender, creeping stems that allow it to spread across the soil surface. Each leaf is lanceolate in shape and features finely serrated margins along the edges. The reproductive organs are centrally located within the small floral structure, often possessing short filaments and anthers. These morphological traits are adapted to its specific ecological niche, facilitating survival in low-growth environments.

What are the pharmacological activities of Centipeda minima?

This plant has analgesic, anti-inflammation, anti-bacteria, anti-cancer, and antioxidant pharmacological activities. The plant also demonstrates significant inhibitory effects against various pathogenic bacteria, helping to prevent infection. Additionally, its high concentration of bioactive compounds provides potent antioxidant protection against oxidative stress.

As discussed in a study, Centipeda minima is a traditional Chinese herbal medicine historically used to treat rhinitis, sinusitis, relieve pain, and reduce swelling. The total extract of Centipeda minima, known as CME, was found to significantly reduce cell viability and proliferation in MDA-MB-231 cells in a dose and time-dependent manner. CME induces apoptosis and inhibits cancer cell migration and invasion, potentially acting through the inhibition of EGFR, PI3K/AKT/mTOR, NF-κB, and STAT3 pathways. Treatment with CME also leads to the in vitro downregulation of MMP-9 activity and the inhibition of metastasis. Furthermore, CME significantly reduces tumor burden in MDA-MB-231 xenograft mice without any appreciable side effects.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds helenaliin, chlorogenic acid, flavonoids, sesquiterpene lactones, and rutin. The presence of helenaliin is particularly significant due to its specific biological activities. Chlorogenic acid provides potent antioxidant properties that support cellular health. Furthermore, the combination of flavonoids and rutin offers strong anti-inflammatory effects. Finally, the sesquiterpene lactones contribute to the overall therapeutic profile of the species.

As discussed in a study, a compound was isolated from Centipeda minima using bioassay-guided screening and identified as helenalin. This compound was evaluated using a liver fibrosis model induced by alcohol administration within 24 weeks in rats. Helenalin prevents hepatic injury and fibrogenesis by inhibiting indicators such as hyaluronic acid, type III precollaogen, laminin, hydroxyproline and collagen α type I. It reduces inflammatory mediators including TGF-β1, TNF-α, IL-6 and IL-1β and myeloperoxidase through the down-regulation of NF-κB. Furthermore, it decreases collagen deposition by reducing profibrotic cytokines like transforming growth factor-β, platelet-derived growth factor-β and connective tissue growth factor. Helenalin may be a major bioactive ingredient of Centipeda minima.
