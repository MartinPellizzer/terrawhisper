Patrinia villosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Patrinia villosa?

Patriinia villosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Dipsacales. This plant is a representative of the family Caprifoliaceae, specifically situated within the genus Patriinia. Its full scientific designation is completed by its specific epithet, resulting in the species name Patriinia villosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Patriña villosa has deeply lobed, hairy leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in terminal umbels. The plant typically exhibits a basal rosette of leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs providing a velvety texture. As the stems elongate, the leaves become smaller and more divided into narrow segments. The inflorescence consists of several small flowers held together by stalks that radiate from a central point. The overall structure of the plant is characterized by its dense pubescence and delicate, branched stems.

What are the pharmacological activities of Patrinia villosa?

This plant has anti-inflammatory, anti-tumor, anti-proliferative, anti-cancer, and anti-oxidant pharmacological activities. Its chemical constituents work to neutralize free radicals, providing significant antioxidant protection against cellular damage. These properties allow the plant to inhibit the uncontrolled growth of malignant cells through specific anti-proliferative mechanisms. Such biological actions contribute to its potential effectiveness in various anti-cancer and anti-tumor therapeutic strategies. Furthermore, its ability to modulate immune responses supports its use as a potent anti-inflammatory agent.

According to a study published by "African journal of traditional, complementary, and alternative medicines : AJTCAM", Patrinia villosa demonstrates strong anti-inflammatoy and anti-nociceptive effects. These activities were observed through the ear edema test, carrageenin-induced paw edema test, cotton pellet-induced granuloma formation test, and acetic acid-induced writhing test. In model rats with pelvic inflammation, the serum levels of interleukin-6 (IL-6), interleukin-8 (IL-8), and tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-α) were significantly lower in the Patrinia villosa group than in the CON group. Furthermore, the serum levels of IL-6 and IL-8 in the Patrinia villosa group were significantly lower than those found in the DEX group. These findings suggest that Patrinia villosa can be used to treat pelvic inflammation and relieve associated pain.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several distinct medicinal compounds including villoso l, patrinalloside, patriniaflavanone A, patrinovalerooside (3), and patrinovilosides A (4). These bioactive secondary metabolites are primarily extracted from the roots and aerial parts of the species. Researchers study these specific molecules to understand their potential pharmacological activities and therapeutic properties. The unique chemical profile of these glycosides and flavonoids contributes to the plant's traditional medicinal uses. Detailed structural analysis of these substances helps in the development of new pharmaceutical agents.

According to a study published by "Zhong yao cai = Zhongyaocai = Journal of Chinese medicinal materials", 9 compounds were isolated from the acetic acid ethyl ester and N-Butanol of Patrinia villosa. Through chemical and spectroscopic analysis, 7 compounds were identified as beta-sitosterol, Villosol, Quercetin, Ferulic acid, Ursolic acid, beta-daucosterol, and Rutin. Among these, 3 specific compounds, which are Ferulic acid, Ursolic acid, and Rutin, are reported to be obtained from Patrinia villosa for the first time. This investigation aimed to find the material basis related to the anti-inflammation pharmacodynamic action of the plant.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating intestinal disorders, intestinal-related diseases, ulcerative colitis, diarrhea, and abdominal pain. It functions as a traditional remedy specifically targeted at calming inflammation within the digestive tract. People often rely on its properties to soothe the symptoms associated with ulcerative colitis. Furthermore, the plant helps to regulate bowel movements to alleviate persistent diarrhea. It is also frequently employed to reduce the discomfort caused by acute abdominal pain.

According to a study published by "Frontiers in chemistry", the plant Patrinia villosa has long been used as a medicinal herb for treating intestinal disorders. It possesses pharmacological activities such as anti-oxidation, anti-inflammation, and anti-cancer effects. Compounds from its water extract inhibit colon cancer HCТ116 cell viability and migration. The compound DHD inhibited HCТ116 cell viability with an IC50 value at 6.1 ± 2.2 μM and suppressed migration at a rate of 74.8% at 2.75 μM. Additionally, valerosidate reduced cell viability with an IC50 value at 22.2 ± 1.1 μM and suppressed migration at a rate of 74.6% at 10.81 μM. After 48 h of treatment, DHD at 5.5 μM increased p53 expression by 34.8% and PTEN expression by 13.9%, while valerosidate at 21.6 μM increased p53 and PTEN expressions by 26.1% and 34.6%, respectively.
