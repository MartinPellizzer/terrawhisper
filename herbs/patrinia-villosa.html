Patrinia villosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Patrinia villosa?

Patriinia villosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Dipsacales. This plant is a representative of the family Caprifoliaceae, specifically situated within the genus Patriinia. Its full scientific designation is completed by its specific epithet, resulting in the species name Patriinia villosa.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Patriina villosa targets cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2), inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS), and various pro-inflammatory cytokines such as tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-alpha) and interleukin-6 (IL-6) to exert its medicinal effects. The plant's bioactive constituents, particularly its essential oils and triterpenoid saponins, act as inhibitors of these enzymes to reduce systemic inflammation. By modulating the arachidonic acid metabolism pathway, its compounds decrease the production of prostaglandins that drive pain and swelling. Furthermore, these molecular interactions assist in regulating the NF-kappaB signaling pathway, which is central to the cellular inflammatory response. This multi-target approach allows the plant to function as an effective anti-inflammatory and analgesic agent in traditional medicine.
