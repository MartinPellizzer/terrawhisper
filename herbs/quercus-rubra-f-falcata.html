Quercus rubra f. falcata

Quercus rubra f. falcata, commonly known as the Red Maple, is a deciduous tree species in the Quercus genus.

It is a variant of the Red Maple (Quercus rubra) with distinctive falcate (sickle-shaped) leaves.

The health benefits of Quercus rubra f. falcata include its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which may help to protect against chronic diseases such as heart disease and cancer.

The therapeutic actions of this plant include its antimicrobial, anti-diabetic, and anti-hypertensive properties, which are attributed to its bioactive constituents, including flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins.
