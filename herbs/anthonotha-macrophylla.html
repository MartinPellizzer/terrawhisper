Anthonotha macrophylla

What's the taxonomical classification of Anthonotha macrophylla?

Anthonotha macrophylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls into the subclass Magnoliidae and is a member of the order Fabales. Specifically, this plant is a part of the family Fabaceae, which places it within the genus Anthonotha, and it is identified by the specific species epithet macrophylla.

      What are the herbal preparations of Anthonotha macrophylla?

      Anthonotha macrophylla is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice derived from its various plant parts. The roots and leaves are often boiled in water to extract active alkaloids for internal consumption. In some traditional practices, the crushed foliage is applied directly to the skin as a paste to soothe localized inflammation. Additionally, alcohol-based tinctures may be crafted from the dried stems to ensure a more concentrated dose of its medicinal properties. These different methods allow practitioners to tailor the delivery of the plant's chemical constituents to specific ailments.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Anthonotha macrophylla can cause localized skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and potential gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Contact with the plant's sap or foliage may trigger redness, itching, or a burning sensation on sensitive skin areas. Individuals with known hypersensitivities might experience more severe swelling or hives upon exposure to the plant's chemical compounds. While specific toxicological studies are limited, precautionary avoidance of ingestion is recommended to prevent nausea or stomach cramping. Always use gloves when handling this species to minimize the risk of an adverse reaction.
