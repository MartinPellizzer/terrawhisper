Anthonotha macrophylla

What's the taxonomical classification of Anthonotha macrophylla?

Anthonotha macrophylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls into the subclass Magnoliidae and is a member of the order Fabales. Specifically, this plant is a part of the family Fabaceae, which places it within the genus Anthonotha, and it is identified by the specific species epithet macrophylla.

What are the therapeutic actions of Anthonotha macrophylla?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, and inflammatory conditions through its topical application. The leaves contain bioactive compounds that help combat various bacterial and fungal pathogens on the skin surface. When prepared as a poultice or decoction, it assists in the healing of abrasions and minor cuts by reducing local swelling. Local traditional practices also utilize the plant to manage symptoms of dermatological irritation. These medicinal properties make it a significant component in regional herbal remedies for skin health.
