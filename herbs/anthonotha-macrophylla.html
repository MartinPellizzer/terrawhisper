Anthonotha macrophylla

What's the taxonomical classification of Anthonotha macrophylla?

Anthonotha macrophylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls into the subclass Magnoliidae and is a member of the order Fabales. Specifically, this plant is a part of the family Fabaceae, which places it within the genus Anthonotha, and it is identified by the specific species epithet macrophylla.

What are the pharmacological activities of Anthonotha macrophylla?

This plant has antiplasmodial, antitrypanosomal, and cytotoxic pharmacological activities. Research indicates that its extracts contain bioactive compounds capable of inhibiting the growth of Plasmodium species. Furthermore, the plant exhibits significant inhibitory effects against Trypanosoma parasites, making it a subject of interest for treating neglected tropical diseases. The presence of cytotoxic properties suggests that certain constituents can induce cell death in specific target cells. These diverse biological actions highlight the therapeutic potential of Anthonotha macrophylla in medicinal chemistry.

As discussed in a study, Anthonotha macrophylla leaf (AML) extracts were evaluated for their antiplasmodial, antitrypanosomal, and cytotoxic activities. The AML extract was identified as the most active antiplasmodial extract with an IC50 of 5.0 ± 0.08 μg/mL. This specific extract also demonstrated a selectivity index (SI) of 21.9. Furthermore, the study suggests that AML could offer a safe and alternative therapy for malaria.
