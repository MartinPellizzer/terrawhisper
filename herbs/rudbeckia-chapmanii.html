Black-Eyed Susan (Rudbeckia Chapmanii)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Rudbeckia chapmanii.

Black-eyed susan, scientifically known as Rudbeckia chapmanii, is a native North American flowering plant often used as a medicinal herb in traditional and modern wellness practices. This plant is valued for its adaptogenic properties, which help the body resist stress and maintain balance, along with its potential anti-inflammatory and immune-boosting benefits. Historically, Indigenous peoples used Black-eyed susan to treat digestive issues, respiratory ailments, and as a general tonic for overall health. In modern wellness, it is explored for its potential in supporting gut health and as a natural remedy for mild inflammation. Notably, the plant contains a unique compound called choline, which supports nerve function, and its bright yellow petals have been historically used in herbal teas and culinary applications.
