Przewalskia tangutica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. According to a study published by "Biotechnology and applied biochemistry", Przewaalskia tangutica is a traditional medicinal plant from Tibet used for its analgesic effect from the tropane alkaloids produced by the plant. The roots of this species have the highest yield of hyoscyamine in all plant species. Two functionally distinct tropinone reductase genes, PtTRI and PtTRII, were cloned from the plant to characterize their functional divergence. PtTRI is expressed in the roots but less in the leaves, whereas PtTRII is expressed in the roots at higher levels than in the leaves. Enzymatic analysis showed that tropinone is a better substrate for PtTRII compared with PtTRI. Additionally, PtTRI exhibited a higher affinity for tropinone compared with the reported TRIs.

According to a study published by "3 Biotech", the hairy root lines of Przewaalskia tangutica Maxim demonstrate an extraordinary ability to produce specific medicinal compounds. HPLC analysis revealed that these lines produce 229.88 mg/100 g of atropine. Additionally, the plant produces 4.09 mg/100 g of anisodine, 12.85 mg/100 g of anisodaamine, and 10.69 mg/100 g of scopolamine. These levels of tropane alkaloids were all more significant than the control roots. The study also noted that the dry biomass (ADB) increased to 4.633 g/l in the liquid medium.
