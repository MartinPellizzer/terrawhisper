Marsdenia tenacissima

What's the taxonomical classification of Marsdenia tenacissima?

Marsdenia tenacissima belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic lineage further, it is placed in the order Gentianales and the family Apocynaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by its membership in the genus Marsdenia, with its unique designation being the species tenacissima.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Marsdenia tenacissima targets various signaling enzymes and receptors, most notably inhibiting cyclooxygenase enzymes and modulating inflammatory cytokines to exert its therapeutic effects. The plant contains bioactive triterpenoids and glycosides that interfere with the arachidonic acid cascade, thereby reducing the production of pro-inflammatory prostaglandins. These compounds also interact with specific protein kinases involved in cellular stress responses and immune cell signaling. By regulating these molecular pathways, the plant exhibits significant anti-inflammatory and potentially antineoplastic properties. Such interactions suggest a complex mechanism of action involving the suppression of oxidative stress-related protein activities.

Marsdenia tenacissima is prepared as a decoction by boiling the plant parts in water for medicinal use. What side-effects this plant can have?

Marsdenia tenacissima can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, many plants in the Apocynaceae family contain toxic alkaloids or glycosides that disrupt physiological processes. Contact with the plant's sap may result in localized dermatitis or painful rashes on sensitive skin. If consumed, the chemical compounds could potentially lead to nausea, vomiting, or more severe systemic complications. Caution is advised when handling this species to prevent unintended exposure.
