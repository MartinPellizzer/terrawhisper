Trigonella emodi

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Trigonella emodi has a small, annual herbaceous habit characterized by pinnately compound leaves, trifoliate leaflets, and small, yellowish-white flowers. The leaflets are typically ovate or elliptical in shape with entire margins and a slightly pubescent texture on the surface. Its stems are relatively slender and often exhibit fine hairs that provide a soft appearance to the foliage. The inflorescence consists of small axillary racemes that bear delicate, inconspicuous petals. Once pollinated, the plant develops small, compressed pods that contain the seeds necessary for reproduction.

      What side effects this plant can have?

      This plant can cause gastrointestinal distress such as bloating, gas, and diarrhea, while also potentially lowering blood sugar levels to dangerous extremes in certain individuals. Because it possesses hypoglycemic properties, people taking diabetes medication should monitor their glucose closely to avoid hypoglycemia. Pregnant women should exercise caution because the plant may stimulate uterine contractions, increasing the risk of miscarriage. Some users might also experience allergic reactions, particularly if they are sensitive to other members of the Fabaceae family.
