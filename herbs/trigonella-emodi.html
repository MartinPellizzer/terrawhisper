Trigonella emodi

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Trigonella emodi has a small, annual herbaceous habit characterized by pinnately compound leaves, trifoliate leaflets, and small, yellowish-white flowers. The leaflets are typically ovate or elliptical in shape with entire margins and a slightly pubescent texture on the surface. Its stems are relatively slender and often exhibit fine hairs that provide a soft appearance to the foliage. The inflorescence consists of small axillary racemes that bear delicate, inconspicuous petals. Once pollinated, the plant develops small, compressed pods that contain the seeds necessary for reproduction.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Trigonella emodi can cause gastrointestinal distress such as bloating, gas, and stomach cramps if consumed in large quantities. Some individuals may also experience allergic reactions including skin rashes or itching upon contact or ingestion. Because it may influence blood sugar levels, it could potentially cause hypoglycemia in people already taking diabetes medications. There is also a risk of uterine contractions, meaning pregnant women should avoid it to prevent complications. Consult a medical professional before using this plant to ensure it does not interfere with existing health conditions.
