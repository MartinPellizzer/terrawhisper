Horse Radish (Cnidium monnieri) Compounds

Cnidium monnieri contains several primary active compounds, including coumarin, which has anticoagulant properties, and flavonoids, known for their antioxidant effects. Terpenoids and phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities. Essential oils, tannins, and glycosides further support its traditional use in herbal medicine.

Horse Radish (Cnidium monnieri) Monograph

1. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Cnidium monnieri. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for its antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarin is also known for its ability to enhance the efficacy of other compounds in the plant. Its presence supports the therapeutic applications of Cnidium monnieri in treating various ailments.
