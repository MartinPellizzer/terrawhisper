10 Possible Side Effects Of Sambucus Canadensis (Elderberry)

Sambucus canadensis has some side effects when used improperly, such as allergic reactions, digestive issues, and skin irritation.

These side effects can be caused by excessive consumption, skin exposure, or contamination. In severe cases, these side effects can worsen people's lives, causing anaphylaxis, nausea, and skin inflammation.

For example, individuals with severe allergies may experience respiratory distress, while those with skin irritation may suffer from permanent scarring.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Sambucus canadensis if used imporperly.

1. Contributes to insomnia

Sambucus canadensis causes allergic reactions due to its potential to trigger an immune response in some individuals.

The plant's proteins and allergenic compounds may cause the body to release histamine, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.

Additionally, the seeds of the Sambucus canadensis plant contain a toxic compound called sambunigrin, which can cause an allergic reaction in some people.

2. Contributes to insomnia

Sambucus canadensis induces dizziness due to its vasodilatory properties that cause a sudden drop in blood pressure.

The flavonoids and anthocyanins present in the plant may interact with the body's cardiovascular system, leading to a temporary decrease in blood flow to the brain, resulting in lightheadedness or dizziness.

Additionally, the antihistamine effects of sambucus can also contribute to dizziness as it can cause a slowdown in heart rate and reduce peripheral resistance.
