Elderberry (Sambucus canadensis) Compounds

Elderberry contains several key compounds, including flavonoids, which act as antioxidants, and phenolic acids, which have anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarin contributes to its mild anticoagulant effects, while tannins and saponins provide astringent and antimicrobial actions. Anthocyanins, glycosides, and terpenoids also support its immune-boosting and antiviral potential.

Elderberry (Sambucus canadensis) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Elderberry (Sambucus canadensis). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting immune function and reducing oxidative stress. These compounds are responsible for the plant's medicinal effects in traditional herbal medicine.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Elderberry (Sambucus canadensis). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and support immune function. This compound is particularly abundant in the berries and has been studied for its potential health benefits.

3. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Elderberry (Sambucus canadensis). It contributes to the plant's anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarin also acts as a natural antiseptic, supporting the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and skin conditions. Its presence enhances the therapeutic profile of Elderberry in herbal medicine.

4. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Elderberry (Sambucus canadensis). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support immune function by reducing viral replication. Tannins also act as antioxidants, helping to neutralize free radicals in the body.

5. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Elderberry (Sambucus canadensis). It contributes to the plant's antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Saponins may support immune function and have potential in reducing viral activity. They are one of several bioactive compounds in Elderberry that contribute to its traditional use in herbal medicine.

6. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Elderberry (Sambucus canadensis). It contributes to the plant's vibrant color and has antioxidant properties that support immune function and reduce inflammation. Anthocyanin is also associated with potential cardiovascular and neuroprotective benefits.

7. Glycoside

Glycosides in Elderberry (Sambucus canadensis) are complex molecules composed of sugar and non-sugar components. They contribute to the plant's medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. These compounds are often responsible for the therapeutic actions associated with Elderberry in herbal medicine.

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of Elderberry (Sambucus canadensis) contains volatile compounds like methyl salicylate and limonene. These compounds contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The essential oil is often used in aromatherapy and topical applications for its therapeutic effects. It is extracted through steam distillation from the leaves and flowers of the plant.

9. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Elderberry (Sambucus canadensis) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help modulate immune responses and may support respiratory health. They are part of the complex phytochemical profile that gives Elderberry its medicinal value.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloids in Elderberry (Sambucus canadensis) are a class of nitrogen-containing compounds. While Elderberry is more renowned for its flavonoids and anthocyanins, alkaloids are present in smaller amounts. They may contribute to the plant's medicinal properties, though their specific roles and efficacy in traditional use are less well-documented compared to other compounds.
