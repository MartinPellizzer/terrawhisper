Dioscorea althaeoides

What's the taxonomical classification of Dioscorea althaeoides?

Dioscorea althaeoides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Dioscoreales and is a member of the family Dioscoreaceae. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Dioscorea, with its specific designation being the species althaeoides.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dioscorea althaeoides has climbing, herbaceous stems that produce cordate to sagittate leaves with entire margins and prominent venation. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in clusters, which are characteristic of the Dioscoreaceae family. The plant also develops underground tuberous structures that serve as storage organs for nutrients. These morphological features allow the species to thrive in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
