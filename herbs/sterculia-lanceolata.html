Sterculia lanceolata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent through the order Malvales, it is placed within the family Malvaceae. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its genus, Sterculia, and its specific species designation, lanceolata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sterculia lanceolata has large, imparipinnate leaves consisting of several pairs of lanceolate leaflets that are typically 10 to 20 centimeters long with acute tips and entire margins. Its inflorescences are small, yellowish-green clusters that emerge from the axils of the leaves or the woody branches. The flowers are relatively inconspicuous and lack petals, instead featuring a prominent calyx.
