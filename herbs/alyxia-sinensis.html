Alyxia sinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Alyxia sinensis?

Alyxia sinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Gentianales. The plant is further categorized into the family Apocynaceae, which places it within the genus Alyxia, specifically identifying it as the species sinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Alyxia sinebsis has evergreen, woody stems with opposite, elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on the upper surface. The foliage consists of simple leaves arranged in pairs along the branches, often featuring a prominent midrib. Small, fragrant, white flowers emerge in clusters from the leaf axils, characterized by their tubular shape and five petals. These blossoms eventually develop into small, fleshy, globose drupes that transition from green to a deep reddish or black color when ripe. The overall growth habit is that of a climbing shrub or woody vine, utilizing its flexible stems to scale surrounding vegetation.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds beta-sitosterol, betulin, lupeol, oleanolic acid, and ursolic acid. These specific pentacyclic triterpenoids are known for their diverse biological activities in human health. Many of these substances exhibit significant anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties when metabolized. Research suggests that the presence of oleanolic acid and ursolic acid may support cellular protection and metabolic functions. Collectively, these bioactive molecules contribute to the complex therapeutic profile of the species.
