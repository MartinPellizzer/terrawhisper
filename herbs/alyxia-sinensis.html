Alyxia sinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Alyxia sinensis?

Alyxia sinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Gentianales. The plant is further categorized into the family Apocynaceae, which places it within the genus Alyxia, specifically identifying it as the species sinensis.

Alyxia sinebsis has evergreen, woody stems with opposite, elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on the upper surface. What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Alyxia sinensis targets various proteins including acetylcholinesterase, cyclooxygenase enzymes, and specific opioid receptors to exert its medicinal effects. The bioactive alkaloids present in the plant act as inhibitors of acetylcholinesterase, which helps regulate neurotransmitter levels in the central nervous system. These phytochemicals also demonstrate an affinity for various muscarinic receptors, contributing to the plant's traditional use in treating neurological symptoms. Through these diverse protein interactions, the plant provides a multifaceted pharmacological profile.
