Indigofera carlesii

What's the taxonomical classification of Indigofera carlesii?

Indigofera carlesii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is classified under the order Fabales. It is a member of the family Fabaceae, which is further narrowed down to the genus Indigofera, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species carlesii.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, stomach aches, and various types of fever. The leaves and roots are often prepared into medicinal decoctions or poultices for topical application. Local healers utilize these preparations to accelerate the healing of cuts and abrasions. Additionally, the plant is ingested in specific dosages to alleviate internal digestive distress. These traditional medicinal practices are rooted in the ethnobotanical knowledge of the regions where it grows.

What are the herbal preparations of Indigofera carlesii?

Indigofera carlesii is prepared as a decoction from the roots, a topical paste from the leaves, or an infusion of the stems. Traditional practitioners often boil the woody parts in water to extract medicinal compounds for internal use. The crushed foliage can be applied directly to the skin to treat localized irritations or wounds. In some regional practices, the plant material is also steeped in oils to create a medicated salve. These various preparations allow the plant's bioactive properties to be delivered through different methods of administration.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Indigofera carlesii can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or gastrointestinal distress if ingested. While specific human clinical trials are limited, members of the Indigofera genus often contain alkaloids and other bioactive compounds that may trigger sensitivity. Direct contact with the foliage or stems might lead to localized dermatitis in susceptible individuals. If any part of the plant is consumed, it could potentially result in nausea or stomach discomfort. Always exercise caution and consult a professional before handling or interacting with this species.
