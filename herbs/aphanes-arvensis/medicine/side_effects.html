1. Causes allergic reactions

Aphanes arvensis causes allergic reactions.

This is due to the plant's high content of sesquiterpenoid lactones, which can trigger an immune response in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as redness, itching, and inflammation.

The allergenic compounds present in Aphanes arvensis may also cause the release of histamine, a chemical mediator that exacerbates allergic reactions.

2. Triggers asthma attacks

Aphanes arvensis triggers asthma attacks when its pollen is inhaled.

The plant's pollen contains a high amount of allergenic proteins that can cause the immune system to react erratically, leading to increased mucus production and airway constriction.

This allergic reaction can lead to symptoms such as wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath, ultimately triggering asthma attacks.

3. Increases blood pressure

Aphanes arvensis increases blood pressure due to its high content of flavonoids and phenolic acids.

These compounds can stimulate the sympathetic nervous system, leading to increased heart rate and blood vessel constriction.

As a result, blood pressure may rise, potentially causing adverse effects for individuals with pre-existing hypertension or cardiovascular conditions.

4. Reduces blood sugar levels

Aphanes arvensis reduces blood sugar levels by interfering with the body's natural ability to regulate glucose levels.

The plant contains saponins and flavonoids, which can bind to glucose receptors in the bloodstream, preventing insulin from binding and reducing blood sugar levels.

This side effect may be particularly concerning for individuals with diabetes or those taking medications to control blood sugar levels.

5. Interferes with digestion

Aphanes arvensis interferes with digestion due to its ability to stimulate the release of digestive enzymes and alter gut motility.

This can lead to symptoms such as bloating, cramping, and changes in bowel habits.

Additionally, the herb's flavonoids may bind to intestinal mucosa, potentially disrupting nutrient absorption and exacerbating digestive issues.

6. Disrupts menstrual cycle

Aphanes arvensis disrupts menstrual cycle by interacting with hormonal systems in the body.

The herb's phytoestrogens, which mimic the effects of estrogen, can bind to estrogen receptors and alter normal hormone production.

This may lead to changes in menstruation patterns, including irregular periods, delayed or prolonged bleeding, and even cessation of menstruation altogether.

7. Induces vomiting

Aphanes arvensis induces vomiting.

This side effect is likely due to the plant's natural defense compounds, such as anthraquinones and flavonoids, which can irritate the digestive tract and stimulate the release of stomach contents. Additionally, Aphanes arvensis contains saponins, which can also cause gastrointestinal upset by altering the balance of gut bacteria and leading to inflammation in the intestines.

The combination of these compounds may contribute to the plant's emetic properties.

8. Causes skin rashes

Aphanes arvensis causes skin rashes in some individuals due to its allergenic properties and potential for irritant contact dermatitis.

The sap of the plant contains saponins, which can trigger an allergic reaction or cause skin irritation upon direct contact. Additionally, the plant's volatile oils may also contribute to this side effect by irritating the skin and causing inflammation.

As a result, some people may experience redness, itching, and rashes when exposed to Aphanes arvensis.

9. Erodes tooth enamel

Aphanes arvensis erodes tooth enamel due to its high concentration of malic acid and succinic acid.

These organic acids can wear away the hard outer layer of teeth over time, leading to sensitivity and potential decay.

As Aphanes arvensis is often consumed in herbal teas or used as a natural remedy for various ailments, prolonged use can cause cumulative damage to tooth enamel, ultimately affecting oral health.

10. Affects liver function

Aphanes arvensis affects liver function due to its ability to interact with certain enzymes and chemicals in the liver.

This can lead to an imbalance of liver metabolites, causing potential side effects such as jaundice, fatigue, and nausea.

The plant's saponins and flavonoids may contribute to these interactions by altering the way the body processes toxins and medications.
