Pittosporum tonkinense

What's the taxonomical classification of Pittosporum tonkinense?

Pittosporum tonkinense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Apiales. This plant is a member of the family Pittosporaceae, falling within the genus Pittosporum, and is specifically identified by its species name, tonkinense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pittosporum tonkinense has elliptical to lanceolate leaves that are dark green, leathery, and measure between 3 and 8 centimeters in length.

These leaves are arranged alternately along woody stems that belong to a shrub or small tree reaching up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces small, fragrant, creamy-white flowers that emerge in clusters from the leaf axils. Following flowering, it develops globose, fleshy fruits that transition from green to a yellowish or reddish hue upon maturity.
