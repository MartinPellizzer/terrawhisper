Crotalaria albida

What's the taxonomical classification of Crotalaria albida?

Crotalaria albida belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Fabales and is a member of the family Fabaceae. More specifically, it is categorized under the genus Crotalaria, with its unique biological identity defined by the specific epithet albida.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Crotalaria albida has an erect, perennial shrubby habit characterized by woody stems and pinnate leaves that typically feature five to nine pairs of leaflets. The foliage consists of ovate to oblong leaflets with smooth margins and a dull green texture. Its inflorescence produces bright yellow flowers that are zygomorphic and emerge from the leaf axils. The plant develops distinctive inflated seed pods that are oblong in shape and turn brown as they mature. These pods contain several small, hard seeds essential for the plant's reproductive cycle.
