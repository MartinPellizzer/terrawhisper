Zygophyllum Simplex: What To Know Before Using It For Medicinal Purposes

Zygophyllum simplex, also known as the simple zygophyllum, is a small shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The plant contains bioactive compounds such as flavonoids and tannins, which may contribute to its medicinal value. In some traditional systems, it has been employed to treat skin conditions, gastrointestinal issues, and respiratory ailments.

However, further scientific research is needed to fully validate its therapeutic applications and ensure its safe use in modern medicine.
