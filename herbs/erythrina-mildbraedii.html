Erythrina mildbraedii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is situated in the order Fabales. It is a member of the family Fabaceae, which is further categorized into the genus Erythrina, ultimately reaching its specific classification as the species mildbraedii.

What are the pharmacological activities of Erythrina mildbraedii?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological potential through its protein tyrosine phosphatase-1B inhibitory activity and cytotoxic properties. Researchers have identified specific bioactive compounds within the plant that effectively target PTP1B to modulate metabolic signaling pathways. This protein tyrosine phosphatase-1B inhibitory activity suggests that the species may hold promise for managing diabetes and obesity. Additionally, the plant exhibits notable cytotoxic effects against various cancer cell lines in laboratory settings. These combined biological actions highlight its value as a source for developing new therapeutic agents.

As discussed in a study, an EtOAc-soluble extract of the root bark of Erythrina mildbraedii resulted in the isolation of six prenylated flavonoids 1-6. These isolates include three new natural products, 1, 2, and 3, as well as three known compounds 4, 5, and 6. All of the isolates, with the exception of compound 4, demonstrated the ability to inhibit PTP1B activity in vitro. The observed IC50 values for these inhibitory activities ranged from 5.3 to 42.6 microM. This data suggests that the prenyl group on the B ring of these flavonoids is important for suppressing the enzyme PTP1B.
