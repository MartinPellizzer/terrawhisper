What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Brassaiopsis ciliata has small, delicate leaves characterized by fine, hair-like cilia along their margins and a subtle, textured surface. These leaves are typically arranged in a compact pattern that enhances the plant's overall structural density. The stem remains relatively slender, supporting the foliage through a delicate but resilient framework. Tiny, specialized trichomes are distributed across the epidermal layers, giving the plant a characteristic velvety or fuzzy appearance. This unique morphological combination of fringed edges and fine pubescence defines its distinct visual identity.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Brassaiopsis ciliata can cause severe skin irritation, chemical burns, and intense ocular inflammation upon direct contact with its bioactive compounds. The plant contains irritants that react aggressively with mucosal membranes and sensitive epithelial tissues. If the sap touches the skin, individuals may experience redness, swelling, and blistering that lasts for several days. Accidental ingestion poses a significant risk of gastrointestinal distress and potential systemic toxicity. Protective gear is essential when handling this species to prevent accidental exposure.
