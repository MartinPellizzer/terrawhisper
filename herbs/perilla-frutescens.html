Shiso (Perilla frutescens)

Shiso (Perilla frutescens) is a member of the Lamiaceae family, native to East Asia, Southeast Asia, and South Asia. Traditionally, its leaves, stems, and seeds have been used for decoctions, culinary uses, and infusions.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, carminative, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in traditional chinese medicine, japanese kampo medicine, and korean traditional medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perilla frutescens is an annual herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters.
Leaves
Broad leaves with upper surface glabrous and dark green, lower surface pubescent and lighter green, featuring prominent stomatal bands.
Flowers
Bilaterally symmetric flowers with two-lipped corolla, upper lip shorter than lower lip, pale violet to bluish purple coloration, arranged in whorled clusters along the inflorescence.
Stems
Climbing, opposite branching, pubescent surface, four-angled cross-section, stipulate nodes.
