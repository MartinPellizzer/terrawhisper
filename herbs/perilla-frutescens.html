Perilla frutescens

What's the taxonomical classification of Perilla frutescens?

Perilla frutescens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant's taxonomic progression continues through the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Perilla, and its unique species designation, frutescens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Periella frutescens has an erect, herbaceous growth habit characterized by square stems, opposite leaves, and small, labiate flowers arranged in terminal racemes. The leaves are typically ovate to triangular with serrated margins and can exhibit a wide range of colors including green, purple, or dark reddish hues. These leaves often possess a distinctive wrinkled or rugose texture on the upper surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are predominantly white or light purple. Once pollination occurs, the plant develops small, nutlet-like seeds that are essential for its reproduction.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to East Asia, specifically concentrated in regions such as China, Japan, Korea, and parts of Southeast Asia. It thrives in temperate and subtropical climates where it can find well-drained soil and ample sunlight. While its origins are strictly Asian, it has been introduced to various other parts of the world through human cultivation for culinary and medicinal purposes. In some non-native environments, it can become naturalized in gardens and disturbed sites. Its distribution is largely dictated by its preference for warm, humid growing seasons.

Periellafrutescens contains medicinal compounds primarily located in its leaves and seeds. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds rosmarinic acid, perilla ketone, perillaaldehyde, perillyl alcohol, and luteolin. Rosmarinic acid and luteolin provide significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties to the plant's profile. Perilla ketone is a key component often studied for its potential insecticidal and therapeutic effects. The volatile compounds perillaaldehyde and perillyl alcohol contribute to its distinct aroma and various biological activities. Together, these bioactive substances support the plant's diverse pharmacological applications in traditional and modern medicine.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Periella frutescens targets various proinflammatory mediators including cyclooxygenase-2, inducible nitric oxide synthase, and various cytokines like TNF-alpha and IL-6 to exert its therapeutic effects. Its bioactive compounds, such as rosmarinic acid and perillaldehyde, interact with the NF-kappaB signaling pathway to suppress inflammatory responses. These phytochemicals also modulate the Nrf2 pathway, which enhances the expression of antioxidant enzymes like superoxide dismutase and glutathione peroxidase. Furthermore, certain extracts can influence the activity of various kinases and receptors involved in metabolic regulation and neuroprotection. Through these diverse protein interactions, the plant provides multifaceted pharmacological benefits ranging from anti-inflammatory to neuroprotective actions.

What are the therapeutic actions of Perilla frutescens?

Periella frutescens has diverse therapeutic properties including carminative, antispasmodic, diaporetic, anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, expectorant, and antihistamine actions. Its carminative and antispasmodic effects help alleviate gastrointestinal distress and cramping. The plant acts as a diaporetic to promote sweating and an expectorant to assist in clearing respiratory congestion. Its anti-inflammatory and antihistamine qualities make it effective for managing allergic reactions and swelling. Furthermore, its antimicrobial properties provide a defense against various pathogens.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause allergic reactions, nausea, dizziness, and gastrointestinal upset in some individuals. People with sensitivities to the Lamiaceae family may experience skin rashes or respiratory issues upon contact or ingestion. Some users report feeling lightheaded or experiencing a sense of dizziness after consuming concentrated extracts. Digestive discomfort, such as nausea and general gastrointestinal upset, is another common concern for those sensitive to its oils. It is important to monitor how your body reacts to any part of the plant to avoid these adverse effects.
