Shiso

Perilla frutescens

Shiso (Perilla frutescens) is a member of the Lamiaceae family, commonly used in traditional medicine for its potential benefits in supporting digestion and alleviating symptoms of colds and fever. The leaves and stems are typically employed, and the herb has been utilized in various cultural contexts, including Asian cuisines and traditional healing practices.

Botanical Identification

Shiso, scientifically known as Perilla frutescens, belongs to the Lamiaceae family. It is also called Japanese perilla or shiso in various regions. This annual plant is native to East Asia and features oval leaves with a peppery aroma. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it is sometimes referred to as perilla or shiso in traditional herbal texts.

Active Compounds

Shiso contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, essential oils, and phenolic acids. These compounds work synergistically to provide its medicinal benefits, influencing anti-inflammatory and antioxidant pathways. The combination of these phytochemicals enhances its therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory ailments due to its warming properties.

Infusion: Used for colds and sore throats due to its mild antiseptic properties.

Extract: Used for muscle pain and inflammation due to its high concentration of active compounds.

Safety Profile

Shiso is generally considered safe when used in culinary amounts. However, it may cause skin irritation in some individuals. Large doses may lead to gastrointestinal discomfort, so caution is advised for those with sensitive stomachs.
