Shiso

Perilla frutescens

Shiso (Perilla frutescens) is a fragrant herb native to East Asia, commonly used in traditional medicine and cuisine, known for its ability to improve respiratory conditions and reduce inflammation.

Taxonomy

Shiso, scientifically known as Perilla frutescens, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Lamiales, order Lamiales, family Lamiaceae, genus Perilla, and species Perilla frutescens.

Morphology

Shiso (Perilla frutescens) is an annual herb with square, branched stems that are often hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are usually 3-10 cm long, and small pink to purple flowers that appear in clusters at the leaf axils.

Distribution

Shiso (Perilla frutescens) is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, Japan, and Korea, and is also cultivated in regions of Southeast Asia, India, and parts of the Middle East.

Traditional Uses

Shiso has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its ability to relieve digestive issues, reduce inflammation, and promote respiratory health due to its high content of antioxidants and essential oils.

Medicinal Systems

Shiso has been utilized in traditional medicinal systems such as Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM), Japanese Kampo, and Ayurveda for its purported therapeutic properties.

Phytochemistry

Shiso contains a variety of bioactive compounds, including volatile oils such as camphor, menthol, and pinene, as well as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Pharmacology

Shiso exhibits anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antispasmodic properties, which contribute to its traditional use in alleviating digestive discomfort, reducing respiratory inflammation, and supporting immune function.

Medicinal Uses

Shiso is used to treat digestive issues such as bloating, gas, and indigestion, as well as respiratory conditions like coughs and asthma, and skin disorders including eczema and rashes.

Preparations

Shiso is used to prepare a variety of medicinal formulations including decoctions, poultices, and tinctures, each tailored to address specific health conditions such as respiratory disorders, digestive issues, and skin irritations.

Safety

While Shiso is generally safe when consumed in typical culinary amounts, excessive use may cause gastrointestinal discomfort, allergic reactions, or interactions with certain medications due to its high content of polyphenols and essential oils.

Evidence

Clinical evidence for Shiso (Perilla frutescens) suggests it may aid in reducing inflammation, supporting respiratory health, and alleviating symptoms of conditions such as asthma and allergic reactions due to its rich content of essential oils and antioxidants.

Cultivation

Shiso is typically cultivated in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade, requiring regular watering and careful pruning to promote healthy growth and optimal leaf yield.

Processing

Shiso leaves are typically processed by washing, slicing, and briefly blanching to preserve their color and aroma, often used fresh in culinary applications or dried and ground for medicinal use.

Identification

Shiso (Perilla frutescens) is a biennial herb native to East Asia, typically identified by its lance-shaped, slightly hairy leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside, with a strong, aromatic scent when crushed.

Sustainability

Shiso is considered sustainable as it is a hardy, fast-growing plant that thrives in a variety of climates and requires minimal chemical inputs, making it an environmentally friendly crop for both agricultural and medicinal use.

Related

Perilla frutescens is closely related to other species in the mint family, such as Perilla citronota, Perilla ocymoides, and Perilla frutescens var. kaitzii, which share similar morphological and chemical characteristics.
