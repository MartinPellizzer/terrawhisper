Perilla frutescens

What's the taxonomical classification of Perilla frutescens?

Perilla frutescens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant's taxonomic progression continues through the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Perilla, and its unique species designation, frutescens.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Periellafrutescens contains medicinal compounds primarily located in its leaves and seeds. The leaves are frequently harvested to create infusions or extracts used for treating respiratory issues and digestive discomfort. These foliage parts are rich in essential oils and rosmarinic acid which provide anti-inflammatory benefits. Meanwhile, the seeds are utilized for their high oil content and are often processed into supplements to support cardiovascular health. Both the seeds and leaves serve as the principal components in various traditional herbal remedies.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse profile of bioactive constituents including rosmarinic acid, perillaldehyde, perillic acid, luteolin, apiigenin, limonene, rosmarinol, quercetin, eugenol, and ursolic acid.

These medicinal compounds work synergistically to provide significant antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. Flavonoids like luteolin, apiigenin, and quercetin contribute to its ability to combat oxidative stress within biological systems. Additionally, terpenoids and phenolic acids such as rosmarinic acid and perillaldehyde support its therapeutic potential in various pharmacological applications.

The presence of ursolic acid and eugenol further enhances its medicinal value by offering protective effects against cellular damage.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Periella frutescens targets various proinflammatory mediators including cyclooxygenase-2, inducible nitric oxide synthase, and various cytokines like TNF-alpha and IL-6 to exert its therapeutic effects. Its bioactive compounds, such as rosmarinic acid and perillaldehyde, interact with the NF-kappaB signaling pathway to suppress inflammatory responses. These phytochemicals also modulate the Nrf2 pathway, which enhances the expression of antioxidant enzymes like superoxide dismutase and glutathione peroxidase. Furthermore, certain extracts can influence the activity of various kinases and receptors involved in metabolic regulation and neuroprotection. Through these diverse protein interactions, the plant provides multifaceted pharmacological benefits ranging from anti-inflammatory to neuroprotective actions.

What are the therapeutic actions of Perilla frutescens?

Periella frutescens has potent anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antiallergic therapeutic actions driven primarily by its high concentrations of rosmarinic acid and various phenolic compounds. These bioactive constituents work to inhibit pro-inflammatory cytokines and reduce oxidative stress within cellular structures. The plant is frequently utilized to alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis and asthma by stabilizing mast cells and suppressing histamine release. Additionally, its antimicrobial properties help combat certain bacterial and fungal pathogens. These diverse medicinal effects make it a valuable subject in pharmacological research for treating respiratory and inflammatory disorders.

What common ailments are treated using this plant?

This plant is used to treat digestive issues like nausea and indigestion, respiratory ailments such as asthma and coughing, and inflammatory skin conditions.

Its bioactive compounds help soothe gastrointestinal distress and reduce intestinal spasms. In traditional medicine, it is frequently employed to alleviate allergic rhinitis and seasonal hay fever by modulating immune responses. Additionally, the plant possesses antimicrobial properties that assist in managing skin infections and minor irritations.

These diverse applications make it a versatile remedy in various herbal practices.

    What are the herbal preparations of Perilla frutescens?

    Periella frutescens is prepared as decoctions, teas, tinctures, powders, topical oils, and infused extracts. The dried leaves are commonly simmered in water to create a medicinal tea used for digestive or respiratory relief. Seeds are often pressed to yield a nutrient-rich oil, while the leaves can be ground into a fine powder for easy ingestion. In many traditional practices, the plant is also processed into concentrated tinctures using alcohol as a solvent to ensure shelf stability. These various forms allow for different absorption rates and specific applications depending on the desired therapeutic effect.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Perilla frutescens can cause allergic reactions such as skin rashes, swelling, or respiratory distress in individuals sensitive to the Lamiaceae plant family. While many people consume it safely in food, concentrated extracts may lead to digestive upset or nausea. There is also limited research regarding its potential to interfere with specific medications or its impact on blood clotting. Pregnant or breastfeeding women should exercise caution due to a lack of comprehensive clinical studies on fetal safety. Consulting a medical professional is advisable before incorporating large amounts of this herb into a supplement regimen.
