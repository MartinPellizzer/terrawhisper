Shiso

Perilla frutescens

Shiso (Perilla frutescens) is a fragrant herb native to East Asia, commonly used in traditional medicine and cuisine, known for its ability to improve respiratory conditions and reduce inflammation.

Taxonomy

Shiso, scientifically known as Perilla frutescens, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Lamiales, order Lamiales, family Lamiaceae, genus Perilla, and species Perilla frutescens.

Morphology

Shiso (Perilla frutescens) is an annual herb with square, branched stems that are often hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are usually 3-10 cm long, and small pink to purple flowers that appear in clusters at the leaf axils.

Distribution

Shiso (Perilla frutescens) is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, Japan, and Korea, and is also cultivated in regions of Southeast Asia, India, and parts of the Middle East.

Traditional Uses

Shiso has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its ability to relieve digestive issues, reduce inflammation, and promote respiratory health due to its high content of antioxidants and essential oils.

Medicinal Systems

Shiso has been utilized in traditional medicinal systems such as Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM), Japanese Kampo, and Ayurveda for its purported therapeutic properties.

Phytochemistry

Shiso contains a variety of bioactive compounds, including volatile oils such as camphor, menthol, and pinene, as well as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Pharmacology

Shiso exhibits anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antispasmodic properties, which contribute to its traditional use in alleviating digestive discomfort, reducing respiratory inflammation, and supporting immune function.

Botanical Identification

Shiso, scientifically known as Perilla frutescens, belongs to the Lamiaceae family. It is also called Japanese perilla or shiso in various regions. This annual plant is native to East Asia and features oval leaves with a peppery aroma. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it is sometimes referred to as perilla or shiso in traditional herbal texts.

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory ailments due to its warming properties.

Infusion: Used for colds and sore throats due to its mild antiseptic properties.

Extract: Used for muscle pain and inflammation due to its high concentration of active compounds.

Safety Profile

Shiso is generally considered safe when used in culinary amounts. However, it may cause skin irritation in some individuals. Large doses may lead to gastrointestinal discomfort, so caution is advised for those with sensitive stomachs.
