Shiso (Perilla frutescens): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

Shiso (Perilla frutescens) is a fragrant herb native to East Asia, commonly used in traditional medicine and cuisine, known for its ability to improve respiratory conditions and reduce inflammation.

What is Shiso (Perilla Frutescens)?

Shiso, also known as Perilla frutescens, is a perennial herb native to East Asia, commonly used in traditional medicine and culinary practices for its aromatic leaves and medicinal properties.

Botanical Identity and Classification

Shiso, or Perilla frutescens, is a flowering plant in the family Lamiaceae, commonly known as the mint family, and is native to East Asia.

Common Names and Synonyms

Shiso, also known as Perilla frutescens, is commonly referred to by various regional names such as perilla, shiso, Japanese perilla, Chinese perilla, and sometimes called shi-so or shiso leaf, depending on the cultural or linguistic context.

Plant Description and Morphology

Shiso (Perilla frutescens) is an annual herb with square, branched stems that are often hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are usually 3-10 cm long, and small pink to purple flowers that appear in clusters at the leaf axils.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Shiso (Perilla frutescens) is native to eastern Asia, specifically China, Japan, and Korea, and is commonly found growing wild in moist, temperate regions with well-drained soil.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The leaves, seeds, and roots of Shiso are primarily used medicinally, with the leaves being the most commonly utilized part for their aromatic and therapeutic properties.

Phytochemical Composition

Shiso contains a variety of bioactive compounds, including volatile oils such as camphor and limonene, flavonoids like orientin and isoorientin, and polyphenols, which contribute to its medicinal properties and antioxidant effects.

Pharmacological Properties

Shiso (Perilla frutescens) exhibits a range of pharmacological properties including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, antimicrobial, and antihypertensive effects, primarily attributed to its rich content of polyphenols, flavonoids, and essential oils.

Mechanisms of Action

Shiso (Perilla frutescens) exerts its pharmacological effects through the bioactive compounds such as perillartine, rosmarinic acid, and perillaldehyde, which exhibit antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties by modulating cellular signaling pathways and enzyme activities.

Therapeutic Uses and Indications

Shiso is used to treat digestive issues such as bloating, gas, and indigestion, as well as respiratory conditions like coughs and asthma, and skin disorders including eczema and rashes.

Preparation Methods and Forms

Shiso is typically prepared as a fresh herb, dried leaves, or brewed as a tea, with each method influencing its flavor, nutritional profile, and medicinal properties.

Dosage and Administration

The recommended dosage of Shiso (Perilla frutescens) for herbal use typically ranges from 3 to 10 grams of dried leaves per day, though this may vary depending on the specific preparation and intended therapeutic effect.

Safety, Side Effects, and Contraindications

While Shiso is generally safe when consumed in typical culinary amounts, excessive use may cause gastrointestinal discomfort, allergic reactions, or interactions with certain medications due to its high content of polyphenols and essential oils.

Drug Interactions

Shiso may interact with certain medications, particularly those affecting the central nervous system, due to its mild sedative properties and potential to influence blood pressure and blood sugar levels.

Toxicity and Precautions

Shiso is generally considered safe when used in normal culinary amounts, but it may cause mild gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals, and excessive use may lead to allergic reactions or skin irritation.

Cultivation and Harvesting

Shiso is typically cultivated in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade, requiring regular watering and careful pruning to promote healthy growth and optimal leaf yield.

Processing and Extraction

Shiso leaves are typically processed by washing, slicing, and briefly blanching to preserve their color and aroma, often used fresh in culinary applications or dried and ground for medicinal use.

Storage and Shelf Life

Shiso (Perilla frutescens) should be stored in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight, and when dried, it can maintain its potency for up to 1-2 years if kept in an airtight container.

Scientific Research and Evidence

Scientific research on Shiso (Perilla frutescens) has shown potential anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, with studies indicating its possible use in treating respiratory conditions, skin disorders, and as a natural preservative in food.

Traditional and Historical Uses

Shiso has been traditionally used in East Asian herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory, carminative, and circulatory benefits, with historical records dating back to ancient China and Japan where it was employed to treat digestive disorders, respiratory conditions, and skin ailments.

Regulatory Status

Shiso (Perilla frutescens) is classified as a food ingredient in many countries, including Japan and China, and is generally recognized as safe (GRAS) by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) when used in conventional food products.
