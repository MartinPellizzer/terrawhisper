Pogostemon cablin

What's the taxonomical classification of Pogostemon cablin?

Pogostemon cablin belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae, eventually reaching its specific designation within the genus Pogostemon and the species cablin.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Pogostemon cablin contains high concentrations of patchouli alcohol, which is the primary sesquiterpene responsible for its distinct aroma and therapeutic properties. This specific compound exhibits significant antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antifungal activities in various biological studies. The essential oil also possesses sedative qualities that can help reduce anxiety and promote relaxation in the central nervous system. Additionally, certain terpenoids found within the plant extract show potential for aiding skin healing and protecting against oxidative stress. These chemical constituents make the plant a valuable resource in traditional medicine and modern aromatherapy.

What are the therapeutic actions of Pogostemon cablin?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, digestive issues, and various inflammatory conditions. The essential oil extracted from the leaves contains potent antimicrobial properties that combat fungal and bacterial pathogens. Local communities often apply crushed leaves topically to promote the healing of minor cuts and skin irritations. When used internally in traditional practices, it is believed to soothe stomach discomfort and aid digestion. These medicinal applications rely on the plant's high concentration of bioactive compounds.
