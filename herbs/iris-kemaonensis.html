Iris kemaonensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Iris kemaonensis?

Iris kemaonensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asparagales. This plant is a member of the family Iridaeceae and is situated within the genus Iris, ultimately identified by its specific species name, kemaonensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Iris kemaonensis has slender, linear leaves that are bright green and grow in an upright, sword-like fashion from a creeping rhizome.

The plant produces delicate flowers characterized by three upright standards and three drooping falls. These floral structures often feature intricate veining and subtle color patterns that distinguish the species. The reproductive organs are centrally located within the perianth tube, supporting the development of seed capsules.

This morphology is typical of many members of the Iridaceae family but adapted to its specific highland habitat.

Careful handling and avoidance of ingestion are essential to prevent these adverse physical reactions.
