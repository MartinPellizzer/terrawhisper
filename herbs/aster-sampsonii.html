Aster sampsonii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aster sampsonii has upright, unbranched stems that reach heights of up to 60 centimeters and are covered in fine, spreading hairs. The leaves are primarily lanceolate to linear in shape, appearing alternate along the stem with serrated margins and a soft, pubescent texture. At the terminal ends of the stems, the plant produces clusters of small, daisy-like flower heads featuring yellow disc florets surrounded by white or pale lavender ray florets. These inflorescences are supported by involucral bracts that are often tinged with purple. The overall structure provides a delicate, airy appearance typical of many perennial aster species.

    What are the herbal preparations of Aster sampsonii?

    Aster sampsonii is prepared as a dried herbal tea, a concentrated tincture, or a topical poultice depending on the intended therapeutic use.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Aster sampsonii can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or gastrointestinal distress if handled or ingested improperly. Contact with the plant's sap or foliage may trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals, resulting in redness or itching. Some people might experience more severe allergic responses such as swelling or respiratory discomfort if they inhale pollen from the flowers. Ingesting any part of the plant could potentially lead to nausea, vomiting, or stomach cramping. Always exercise caution when working with this species to avoid these adverse physical reactions.
