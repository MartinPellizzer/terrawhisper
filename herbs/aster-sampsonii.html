What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aster sampsonii has upright, unbranched stems that reach heights of up to 60 centimeters and are covered in fine, spreading hairs. The leaves are primarily lanceolate to linear in shape, appearing alternate along the stem with serrated margins and a soft, pubescent texture. At the terminal ends of the stems, the plant produces clusters of small, daisy-like flower heads featuring yellow disc florets surrounded by white or pale lavender ray florets. These inflorescences are supported by involucral bracts that are often tinged with purple. The overall structure provides a delicate, airy appearance typical of many perennial aster species.
