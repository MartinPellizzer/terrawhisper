Hibiscus tiliaceus

What's the taxonomical classification of Hibiscus tiliaceus?

Hibiscus tiliaceus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Malvales. This plant is a member of the family Malvaceae and is specifically categorized under the genus Hibiscus, with its unique biological identity defined by the species name tiliaceus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hibiscus tiliaceus has an erect, woody shrub habit characterized by upright stems that can reach heights of up to four meters. The leaves are broadly ovate or elliptical with serrated margins and a glossy, dark green surface. Its most prominent feature is the large, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically display vibrant yellow or orange petals with a deep red center. These blooms are supported by long, sturdy pedicels that emerge from the leaf axils. The plant also produces woody, capsule-like seed pods that develop after successful pollination.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, specifically concentrated in countries such as Indonesia, Malaysia, Thailand, and the Philippines. It thrives in humid, low-altitude environments where warm temperatures are consistent throughout the year. Because it is highly valued for its fiber and ornamental properties, it has been widely introduced to other tropical zones including parts of Africa and the Caribbean. The species prefers well-drained soils found in coastal areas or lowland rainforest margins. Its natural range is strictly limited to regions that avoid frost and freezing temperatures.

What are the pharmacological activities of Hibiscus tiliaceus?

This plant has diverse pharmacological activities including alpha-glucosidase inhibitory activity, anti-inflammatory properties, antioxidant capacity, antiproliferative effects, and cytotoxic activity. Its antioxidant potential helps mitigate oxidative stress by neutralizing free radicals within biological systems. The plant also demonstrates anti-inflammatory actions that can help reduce swelling and immune responses. Furthermore, its antiproliferative and cytotoxic activities suggest a significant role in inhibiting the growth and survival of certain cancer cell lines. These combined properties highlight the therapeutic potential of Hibiscus tiliaceus in various medicinal applications.

As discussed in a study, a total of 15 compounds were isolated from the traditional medicinal Vietnamese mangrove Hibiscus tiliaceus. These constituents include one novel megastigmane named tiliaceic acid A (1) and 14 known compounds. The antioxidant and alpha-glucosidase inhibitory activities of these isolated compounds were evaluated for the first time. Compound 2 demonstrated strong alpha-glucosidase inhibitory activity with an IC50 of 77.78 ± 1.00 μM. This activity was compared to the positive control acarbosen which had an IC50 of 105.71 ± 2.29 μM.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds hibisceusin I, hibisceusin II, hibisceusin III, hibisceusin IV, and quinine. These specific phytochemicals are often studied for their diverse pharmacological activities and therapeutic potential. The hibisceusin group consists of unique flavonoids that may contribute to the plant's biological profile. Additionally, the presence of quinine adds a significant alkaloid component often associated with antimalarial properties. Together, these substances define the complex chemical makeup of the species.

As discussed in a study, the tainted stems of Hibiscus tiliaceus contain 10 new cadinane-type sesquiterpenoids named hibisceusins I-R (1-10) and 14 known sesquiterpenoids (11-24). The absolute configurations of compounds 1-4 were measured through electronic circular dichroism spectra. Aldehydated sesquiterpenoids (1, 6-9, 11, 12, and 22) and the known sesquiterpenoid quinine (18) exhibited significant inhibitory activities against certain fungi. These compounds showed minimum inhibitory concentration (MIC) values of 2.5-50 μg/mL. All isolates were found to remain inactive against Rhizoopus stolonifer.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause hypoglycemia, hypotension, estrogen reduction, nausea, and dizziness in some individuals. Consuming hibiscus tiliaceus may significantly lower blood sugar levels, which poses a risk for those managing diabetes. It also possesses properties that can lead to hypotension by reducing blood pressure. Some users may experience a reduction in estrogen levels, potentially impacting hormonal balance. Additionally, common physical reactions to the plant include nausea and dizziness.

The main side effects this plant can have are shown in the list below.

  • Hypoglycemia (low blood sugar)
  • Hypotension (low blood pressure)
  • Estrogen reduction
  • Nausea
  • Dizziness
