Capparis chingiana

What's the taxonomical classification of Capparis chingiana?

Capparis chingiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is part of the order Brassicales and the family Capparaceae. Finally, it is situated within the genus Capparis, with its specific designation being the species chingiana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Capparis chingiana has large, leathery, obovate leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and slightly paler underneath. The plant features woody stems that can develop into substantial climbing or scrambling shrubs. Small, fragrant white flowers emerge from the axils, characterized by numerous long, prominent stamens. Its fruit is a large, fleshy berry that transitions from green to a deep reddish-purple color as it matures. The structure of the plant is designed to support its heavy, nutrient-rich fruit during the growing season.
