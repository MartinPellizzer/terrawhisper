Sanicula astrantiifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sanicula astrantiifolia has opposite, palmately lobed leaves that are typically three to five lobed with coarsely serrated margins and a long petiole. The foliage is characterized by a dark green color and a texture that can appear somewhat rough or hairy. Its flowering structure consists of dense, umbel-like clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers held atop sturdy stems. These inflorescences eventually produce small, nut-like fruits that are often equipped with hooked bristles to aid in seed dispersal. The plant generally maintains a low-growing, herbaceous habit suitable for woodland environments.
