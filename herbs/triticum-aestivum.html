Triticum Aestivum Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Triticum aestivum, commonly known as wheat, is a cereal grain that is widely cultivated for its edible seeds.

It is rich in nutrients such as carbohydrates, proteins, fiber, and various vitamins and minerals.

The bioactive constituents in wheat, including gluten, phytic acid, and various phytochemicals, contribute to its therapeutic properties, though it may also trigger adverse reactions in some individuals.

Herbal preparations such as wheatgrass juice, infusions, and flour-based products can be used to harness its nutritional benefits.
