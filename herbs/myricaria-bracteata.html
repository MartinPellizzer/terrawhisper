Myricaria bracteata

What's the taxonomical classification of Myricaria bracteata?

Myricaria bracteata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized as Equisetopsida, falling under the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Caryophyllales and the family Tamaricaceae. Finally, the plant is placed within the genus Myricaria, with its specific identification being the species bracteata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Myricaia bracteata has a woody shrub structure characterized by elongated, lanceolate leaves with entire margins and a distinctive leathery texture. The foliage is typically arranged in an alternate pattern along the stems, often exhibiting a deep green color on the upper surface. Its reproductive morphology is defined by small, inconspicuous flowers that are tucked within protective bracts. These bracts play a crucial role in shielding the developing floral structures from environmental stressors. The plant also produces small, fleshy fruits that serve as a primary method for seed dispersal.

The species is commonly found in low-lying areas where the soil remains consistently moist or inundated by periodic flooding. Its distribution is closely tied to the availability of freshwater ecosystems within these warm climatic zones. How is this plant cultivated?

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds beta-sitosterol, daucosterol, gallic acid, kaempferol, and quercetin. These specific phytoconstituents contribute to the various biological activities observed in Myricaria bracteata. The presence of sterols like beta-sitosterol and daucosterol often relates to anti-inflammatory properties. Furthermore, the phenolic compounds gallic acid, kaempferol, and quercetin provide significant antioxidant potential. Together, these chemical constituents support the plant's traditional use in medicinal applications.

According to a study published by "Zhongguo Zhong yao za zhi = Zhongguo zhongyao zazhi = China journal of Chinese materia medica", chemical constituents were isolated from the herb of Myricaria bracteata using silica gel column chromatography. A total of 11 compounds were obtained and identified through spectroscopic methods. These compounds include rhamnetin, 3,5,4'-trihydroxy-7,3'-dimethoxyflavone, 3,5,4'-trihydroxy-7-methoxyflavone, quercetin-3-O-alpha-L-rhamnopyranoside, kaempferol, quercetin, chrysoerio, gallic acid, gallic acid ethylester, beta-sitosterol, and daucosterol. All 11 of these compounds were obtained from Myricaria bracteata for the first time.
