Aglaia cucullata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aglaia cucullata has a woody structure characterized by medium to large trees that feature elliptic to oblong leaves with entire margins and an acute apex. The foliage is typically leathery in texture and arranged in an alternate pattern along the branches. Its reproductive morphology includes small, cream-colored flowers that are often clustered in axillary panicles. The fruit of this species is a fleshy, somewhat globose drupe that serves as a primary dispersal mechanism. These morphological traits collectively distinguish it within its specific botanical family.
