Aglaia cucullata

What's the taxonomical classification of Aglaia cucullata?

Aglaia cucullata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Sapindales and is a member of the family Meliaceae. Specifically, it is identified by the genus Aglaias and is distinguished at the species level as Aglaias cucullata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aglaia cucullata has a woody structure characterized by medium to large trees that feature elliptic to oblong leaves with entire margins and an acute apex.

The foliage is typically leathery in texture and arranged in an alternate pattern along the branches. Its reproductive morphology includes small, cream-colored flowers that are often clustered in axillary panicles. The fruit of this species is a fleshy, somewhat globose drupe that serves as a primary dispersal mechanism.

These morphological traits collectively distinguish it within its specific botanical family.
