Smallanthus sonchifolius

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Smallanthus sonchifolius has erect, woody stems that support large, deeply lobed leaves with irregular, coarse margins. The foliage typically features long, narrow segments that can grow quite substantial in size. These plants produce terminal clusters of small, yellowish-white flower heads that appear at the ends of the branches. The overall structure is characterized by a bushy growth habit with a dense arrangement of foliage. Its root system is generally robust to support the height and mass of the stems.

Careful monitoring is necessary to avoid unintended physiological complications during use.
