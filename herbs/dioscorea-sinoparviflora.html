Dioscorea sinoparviflora

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dioscorea sinoparviflora has climbing stems that arise from tuberous roots, heart-shaped leaves with smooth margins, and small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in compact inflorescences. The vine-like stems utilize twining movements to ascend through surrounding vegetation in its native habitat. Each leaf typically features a long petiole and a distinctively thin texture compared to related species. The diminutive floral structures are a defining characteristic that distinguishes this plant from larger-flowered members of the genus. These morphological traits collectively support its life cycle as a perennial climbing herb.
