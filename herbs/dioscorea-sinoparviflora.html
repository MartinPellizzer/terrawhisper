Dioscorea sinoparviflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Dioscorea sinoparviflora?

Dioscorea sinoparviflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the taxonomic hierarchy, it is classified in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It falls under the order Dioscoreales and is a member of the family Dioscoreaceae. Finally, it is placed within the genus Dioscorea, with its specific identification being the species sinoparviflora.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dioscorea sinoparviflora has climbing stems that arise from tuberous roots, heart-shaped leaves with smooth margins, and small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in compact inflorescences.

The vine-like stems utilize twining movements to ascend through surrounding vegetation in its native habitat. Each leaf typically features a long petiole and a distinctively thin texture compared to related species. The diminutive floral structures are a defining characteristic that distinguishes this plant from larger-flowered members of the genus.

These morphological traits collectively support its life cycle as a perennial climbing herb.
