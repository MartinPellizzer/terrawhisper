Begonia wilsonii

What's the taxonomical classification of Begonia wilsonii?

Begonia wilsonii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Cucurbitales. This specific plant is a member of the family Begoniaceae and is situated within the genus Begonia, ultimately reaching its unique designation at the species level as wilsonii.

      What are the herbal preparations of Begonia wilsonii?

      Begonia wilsonii is prepared as a decoction or a concentrated liquid extract derived from the fresh or dried leaves and stems. Practitioners typically simmer the plant material in water to extract its active phytochemicals for medicinal use. In some traditional practices, the crushed juice of the fresh plant is applied topically to treat skin irritations or wounds. These liquid preparations are often ingested to address internal inflammatory conditions. The specific method of preparation depends on whether the desired effect is systemic or localized to the skin.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Begonia wilsonii can cause skin irritation, eye inflammation, and gastrointestinal distress if its tissues are ingested or touched. The plant contains oxalic acid, which is a common irritant found in many begonia species. When the sap comes into contact with sensitive skin, it may result in redness, itching, or a burning sensation. If the leaves are eaten, individuals might experience nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. It is important to wear gloves when handling the plant to prevent these adverse physical reactions.
