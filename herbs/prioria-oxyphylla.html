Prioria oxyphylla

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Prioria oxyphylla has an erect, woody habit characterized by large, coriaceous, lanceolate leaves that are typically 10 to 25 centimeters long and possess sharply serrated margins. The leaves are arranged alternately along the branches and exhibit a distinctively leathery texture with prominent pinnate venation. Its bark is generally smooth, while the stems often display small lenticels for gas exchange. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into woody, globose fruits. These morphological features are adaptations suited for its specific tropical forest habitat.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical rainforests of West Africa, specifically ranging from Sierra Leone and Liberia through Ivory Coast, Ghana, and Nigeria. It thrives primarily in the humid, low-elevation coastal and inland forest belts of this equatorial region. Within these habitats, the species is typically found in dense, primary evergreen forests where light and moisture levels are consistent. Its distribution is closely tied to the specific climatic conditions provided by the Guineo-Congolian forest ecosystem. Consequently, the plant is rarely found outside of these specific West African forest zones.
