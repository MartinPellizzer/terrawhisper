Prioria oxyphylla

What's the taxonomical classification of Prioria oxyphylla?

Prioria oxyphylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Fabales. As a member of the family Fabaceae, this plant is categorized under the genus Prioria, with its specific designation being the species oxyphyllia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Prioria oxyphylla has an erect, woody habit characterized by large, coriaceous, lanceolate leaves that are typically 10 to 25 centimeters long and possess sharply serrated margins.

The leaves are arranged alternately along the branches and exhibit a distinctively leathery texture with prominent pinnate venation. Its bark is generally smooth, while the stems often display small lenticels for gas exchange. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into woody, globose fruits.

These morphological features are adaptations suited for its specific tropical forest habitat.
