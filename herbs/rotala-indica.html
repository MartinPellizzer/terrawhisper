Rotala indica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rotala indica has small, elliptical to obovate leaves that are typically reddish or green and arranged oppositely along slender, branching stems. These leaves can vary in size and shape depending on whether the plant is growing submerged or emersed. The stems are relatively thin and flexible, allowing the plant to form dense, bushy clusters in aquatic environments. The root system consists of fine, fibrous roots that help anchor the plant into soft substrates. This species often exhibits a color change to deep red when exposed to high light intensity and low nutrient levels.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, specifically spanning across countries such as Thailand, Indonesia, Malaysia, and Vietnam. Its distribution is closely tied to areas that provide consistent water temperatures and high humidity levels.
