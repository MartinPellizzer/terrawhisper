Rotala indica

What's the taxonomical classification of Rotala indica?

Rotala indica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Myrtales and is a member of the family Lythraceae. Specifically, it is identified by the genus Rotala and is defined by its unique species designation, indica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rotala indica has small, elliptical to obovate leaves that are typically reddish or green and arranged oppositely along slender, branching stems. These leaves can vary in size and shape depending on whether the plant is growing submerged or emersed. The stems are relatively thin and flexible, allowing the plant to form dense, bushy clusters in aquatic environments. The root system consists of fine, fibrous roots that help anchor the plant into soft substrates. This species often exhibits a color change to deep red when exposed to high light intensity and low nutrient levels.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires high light intensity, consistent carbon dioxide supplementation, and a nutrient-rich substrate to achieve its vibrant reddish hues.

It thrives best in planted aquariums where water temperatures are maintained between 72 and 82 degrees Fahrenheit. Regular liquid fertilization with iron and micronutrients is essential to prevent the leaves from turning pale or stunted. Fast growth rates necessitate frequent pruning to maintain a bushy appearance and prevent the lower stems from shading out.

Careful monitoring of water hardness and pH levels will ensure the plant remains healthy and colorful.
