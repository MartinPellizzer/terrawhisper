Bupleurum wenchuanense

What's the taxonomical classification of Bupleurum wenchuanense?

Bupleurum wenchuanense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Apiales. Its taxonomic journey continues through the family Apiaceae, which is characterized by its distinctive flowering structures, placing it within the genus Bupleurum. Ultimately, it is identified at the species level as wenchuanense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Its inflorescence consists of small, yellowish-green flowers clustered into compact umbels at the terminal ends of the branches. Each individual flower possesses minute petals and stamens that are typical of the Apiaceae family. What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the mountainous regions of Wenquan County within the Sichuan Province of China. It grows specifically in high-altitude habitats characterized by rocky slopes and open shrublands. The species is primarily found at elevations ranging from 2,500 to 3,500 meters above sea level. Its distribution is highly localized, meaning it does not occur naturally in any other geographic locations. This narrow range makes the species particularly sensitive to environmental changes within its specific mountain niche.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?
