Saururus chinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Saururus chinensis?

Saururus chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Piperales. This plant is a member of the family Saururaceae, which contains the genus Saururus, and it is specifically identified by its species name, chinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saururus chinensis has lanceolate, serrated leaves that grow in an alternate pattern along erect, herbaceous stems reaching up to one meter in height. These leaves are typically bright green and possess prominent venation that enhances their elongated shape. The plant produces distinctive white, spike-like racemes of small, four-petaled flowers that emerge from the terminal ends of the stems. These floral spikes are characterized by a delicate, airy appearance and often bloom in mid-to-late summer. Below the ground, the plant spreads through an extensive rhizomatous root system that allows it to form dense colonies in wetland environments.
