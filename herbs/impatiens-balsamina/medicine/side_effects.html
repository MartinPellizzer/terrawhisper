1. Irritates skin contacts

Impatiens balsamina irritates skin contacts due to its sap containing caoutchouc, a milky latex that can cause skin irritation and allergic reactions.

When the plant's sap comes into contact with human skin, it may lead to redness, itching, and even inflammation.

This reaction is thought to be caused by the alkaloids present in the sap, which can stimulate an immune response and cause discomfort for some individuals who touch or handle the plant.

2. Triggers allergic reactions

Impatiens balsamina triggers allergic reactions due to the presence of certain compounds such as alkaloids and glycosides.

These substances can cause an overactive immune response in some individuals, leading to symptoms like skin rash, itching, and swelling.

Additionally, the plant's sap may contain irritating chemicals that can cause burning or stinging sensations upon contact with the skin or mucous membranes, exacerbating allergic reactions.

3. Causes stomach upset

Impatiens balsamina causes stomach upset in some individuals due to its high concentration of alkaloids, which can irritate the digestive system.

The plant's natural toxins, including impatienol and patulifoline, may disrupt normal gut function and lead to discomfort, nausea, or bloating.

This adverse effect is thought to be more pronounced when consuming large amounts of jewelweed or ingesting it in concentrated forms.

4. Induces vomiting

Impatiens balsamina induces vomiting due to the presence of saponins, which are natural compounds that can stimulate the gut and cause nausea.

When ingested, these compounds can trigger a strong urge to vomit, potentially leading to discomfort and distress in individuals who consume the plant unintentionally or excessively.

The saponins' irritating effects on the digestive system may also contribute to stomach pain and cramping.

5. Increases heart rate

Impatiens balsamina increases heart rate due to its stimulatory properties.

The plant contains compounds such as alkaloids and glycosides, which can have a direct effect on the cardiovascular system, causing an increase in blood pressure and heart rate.

This response is often seen in individuals who ingest large amounts of the plant or use it externally, potentially leading to palpitations and other cardiac-related symptoms.

6. Lowers blood pressure

Impatiens balsamina lowers blood pressure due to its ability to dilate blood vessels and reduce peripheral resistance.

This is likely caused by the presence of compounds such as impatienolic acid, which has been shown to possess vasodilatory properties.

As a result, regular consumption of Impatiens balsamina may lead to a decrease in blood pressure, potentially affecting individuals who already have hypotension or are taking medications that regulate blood pressure.

7. Produces kidney stones

Impatiens balsamina produces kidney stones due to its high concentration of oxalic acid, a naturally occurring compound that can increase urinary acidity and reduce calcium absorption in the body.

This combination can lead to an increased risk of forming calcium oxalate stones, which can cause severe abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, and frequent or painful urination.

8. Increases sweating

Impatiens balsamina increases sweating due to its ability to stimulate the sweat glands and promote vasodilation.

The plant's active compounds, such as flavonoids and alkaloids, can cause a physiological response that leads to increased perspiration. Additionally, the plant's aromatic properties may also contribute to this effect by stimulating the nervous system and causing a reflexive sweating response.

As a result, individuals using Impatiens balsamina may experience an increase in sweat production.

9. Causes dizziness

Impatiens balsamina causes dizziness due to its unique properties and interactions with the body.

The plant's active compounds, such as alkaloids and glycosides, can affect the central nervous system, leading to a sensation of lightheadedness or dizziness in some individuals.

This side effect is often reported when taking large doses or using the herb for extended periods.

10. Alters mood swings

Impatiens balsamina alters mood swings due to its unique chemical composition.

The plant contains alkaloids such as impatienine and echitamine, which can affect the brain's neurotransmitters, leading to changes in emotional states and mood regulation.

Additionally, Jewelweed's ability to interact with GABA receptors can further contribute to altered mood patterns, potentially causing feelings of anxiety or depression in some individuals.
