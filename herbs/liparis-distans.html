Liparis distans

What's the taxonomical classification of Liparis distans?

Liparis distans belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is a member of the order Asparagales and is situated within the family Orchidaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Liparis and is specifically known as the species distans.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Liparis distans has a creeping rhizome that produces small, fleshy, ovate to lanceolate leaves arranged in a distichous pattern.

The plant typically features a slender, elongated inflorescence that bears several small, inconspicuous flowers. These flowers possess a characteristic three-lobed lip and a small, somewhat fleshy column. The overall structure is diminutive, reflecting its specialized adaptation to forest floor environments.

Its morphological features are adapted for efficient growth in shaded, humid habitats.
