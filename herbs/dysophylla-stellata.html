What's the taxonomical classification of Dysophylla stellata?

Dysphyllia stellata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Lamiales. Furthermore, it is a member of the family Lamiaceae, specifically categorized under the genus Dysphylla, with its unique designation being the species stellaata.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds chromones 1 and 2, which are primary bioactive constituents identified within its chemical profile. These specific chromones are studied for their potential pharmacological properties and biological activities. Researchers analyze these molecules to understand how they interact with various cellular targets. The presence of such compounds suggests that the plant may possess significant therapeutic value in traditional or modern medicine. Further investigation into these derivatives helps clarify their specific roles in medicinal chemistry.

According to a study published by "Fitoterapia", the n-hexane and ethyl acetate extracts of Dysophylla stellata showed significant inhibition of edema when applied topically at doses of 0.5 and 1mg/ear. These extracts demonstrated COX-1 and COX-2 inhibitory activities at 50 microg/ml, with the n-hexane extract showing 85.42% and 57.38% inhibition and the ethyl acetate extract showing 71.79% and 89.27% inhibition. Chromones 1 and 2 present in these extracts may be responsible for the observed COX-1 and COX-2 inhibitory and anti-inflammatory activities. Additionally, the ethyl acetate extract displayed antioxidant activity in DPPH and ABTS radical scavenging assays, while the n-hexane extract was found to be inactive.
