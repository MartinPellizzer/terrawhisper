What's the taxonomical classification of Dysophylla stellata?

Dysphyllia stellata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Lamiales. Furthermore, it is a member of the family Lamiaceae, specifically categorized under the genus Dysphylla, with its unique designation being the species stellaata.

    What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

    This plant contains several medicinal compounds including the specific chromones known as 1 and 2. These particular flavanoids are recognized for their significant biological activities within the human body. Researchers have observed that these molecules can exhibit potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Beyond these effects, the structural variations of chromones 1 and 2 influence how they interact with cellular targets. Understanding these chemical profiles is essential for developing new therapeutic treatments derived from the plant.

    As discussed in a study, the n-hexane and ethyl acetate extracts of whole plants of Dysophylla stellata significantly inhibited edema when applied topicaly at doses of 0.5 and 1mg/ear in a TPA-induced ear edema assay in mice. Both extracts were evaluated for COX-1 and COX-2 inhibitory activities at 50 microg/ml. The extracts showed 85.42 and 57.38% inhibition for COX-1 and 71.79 and 89.27% inhibition for COX-2, respectively. Chromones 1 and 2 present in these extracts could be responsible for their COX-1 and COX-2 inhibitory and anti-inflammatory activities. The ethyl acetate extract showed antioxidant activity in DPPH and ABTS radical scavenging assay, whereas the n-hexane extract was found to be inactive.
