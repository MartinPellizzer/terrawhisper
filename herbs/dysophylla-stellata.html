Dysophylla stellata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dysophylla stellata has small, opposite, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy with entire margins. The plant features a low-growing, spreading habit characterized by slender, creeping stems. Its flowers are tiny and white, typically appearing in small clusters along the stem. The reproductive structures include minute, inconspicuous parts that are characteristic of the genus. These morphological traits allow the plant to form dense mats in its natural habitat.

      What are the herbal preparations of Dysophylla stellata?

      Dysophylla stellata is prepared primarily as a decoction or an infusion by boiling or steeping the dried leaves and stems in water to extract its bioactive compounds.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Dysophylla stellata can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, acute skin irritation, and potential neurological impairment if ingested or handled improperly. Contact with the plant's sap often results in localized redness, swelling, and intense itching on the affected area. Ingesting even small amounts of the plant may lead to nausea, vomiting, and debilitating stomach cramps. Some individuals may also experience dizziness or lightheadedness due to the presence of specific alkaloids within the foliage. These adverse reactions vary in intensity depending on the individual's sensitivity and the level of exposure.
