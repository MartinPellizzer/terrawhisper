Viburnum dilatatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Viburnum dilatatum?

Viburnum dilatatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Dipsacales. This plant is a member of the family Viburnaceae and falls under the genus Viburnum, specifically identified as the species dilatatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viburnum dilatatum has opposite, simple, ovate to elliptical leaves measuring 5 to 12 centimeters in length with serrated margins and prominent pinnate venation. These leaves are typically dark green and glossy on the upper surface while appearing paler underneath. The shrub produces flat-topped cymes of small, creamy white flowers that emerge in late spring. Following the bloom, the plant develops fleshy, globose drupes that transition from green to bright red and finally to a deep black color. Its woody stems are often characterized by a somewhat spreading growth habit and can develop a slightly rough texture as the plant matures.
