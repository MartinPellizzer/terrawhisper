What's the taxonomical classification of Anemonastrum baicalense?

Anemonastrum baicalense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic path continues through the order Ranunculales and the family Ranunculaceae, eventually reaching its specific designation within the genus Anemonastrum. Ultimately, this plant is identified at the species level as baicalense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Anemonastrum baicalense has herbaceous perennials characterized by slender, upright stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and terminate in solitary, actinomorphic flowers. The leaves are predominantly basal and deeply divided into multiple narrow, lanceolate lobes with serrated margins. Its floral structure features several white or pale pink petals and a central cluster of numerous prominent stamens. The reproductive organs include a central carpel arrangement that eventually develops into small, dry achenes. These achenes are often topped by persistent, feathery styles that aid in wind dispersal.
