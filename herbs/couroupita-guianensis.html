Cupuaçu Tree (Couroupita Guianensis)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Couroupita guianensis.

Cupuaçu tree, scientifically known as Couroupita guianensis, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon Basin, renowned for its medicinal and nutritional properties. This tree is often regarded as a medicinal herb and adaptogen, valued for its rich content of bioactive compounds such as antioxidants, polyphenols, and amino acids. Traditionally, indigenous communities have used cupuaçu for its soothing effects on the digestive system and as a remedy for skin conditions, while also incorporating it into ceremonial practices. In modern wellness, it is increasingly used in skincare products and dietary supplements due to its anti-inflammatory and skin-rejuvenating properties. One of its unique features is its distinctive creamy and sweet flavor, reminiscent of a blend between coconut and banana, and it contains a rare active compound called cupuacuin, which contributes to its therapeutic potential.
