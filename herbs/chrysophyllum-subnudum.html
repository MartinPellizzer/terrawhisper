Chrysophyllum subnudum

What's the taxonomical classification of Chrysophyllum subnudum?

Chrysophyllum subnudum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Ericales. This plant is a member of the family Sapotaceae, falling under the genus Chrysophyllum, and is specifically identified by its species name, subnudum.

    What are the molecular targets of this plant?

    Chrysophyllum subnudum targets various inflammatory enzymes and oxidative stress markers, specifically inhibiting cyclooxygenase-2, lipoxygenase, and inducible nitric oxide synthase to mitigate inflammation. The bioactive phytochemicals within the plant, such as polyphenols and flavonoids, modulate these protein pathways to reduce the production of pro-inflammatory mediators. By regulating these specific molecular targets, the plant exhibits significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory medicinal properties. These biochemical mechanisms provide a scientific basis for its traditional use in treating various inflammatory conditions.

While specific toxicological studies on humans are limited, the secondary metabolites found in many Sapotaceae species may trigger localized dermatitis if the sap or leaves touch the skin. Because clinical data regarding its safety profile is sparse, there is a risk of unpredictable systemic reactions.
