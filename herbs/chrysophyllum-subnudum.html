What's the taxonomical classification of Chrysophyllum subnudum?

Chrysophyllum subnudum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Ericales. This plant is a member of the family Sapotaceae, falling under the genus Chrysophyllum, and is specifically identified by its species name, subnudum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Chrysophyllum subnudum has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to oblong in shape, measuring approximately 5 to 15 centimeters in length with entire margins and acute apices. The leaf surfaces are typically glabrous or possess very sparse pubescence, which distinguishes this species from many of its relatives. The underside of the foliage often displays a paler coloration compared to the darker, lustrous upper surface. Its stems are woody and can develop a slightly rough texture as the plant matures.
