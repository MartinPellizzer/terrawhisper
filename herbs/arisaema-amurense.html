What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains calcium oxalate crystals, saponins, and various alkaloids that serve as its primary medicinal and chemical constituents. The presence of calcium oxalate in needle-like structures can cause significant irritation to the mouth and throat if the plant is consumed raw. However, specific saponins within the roots are often studied for their potential pharmacological properties in traditional medicine. These bioactive compounds must be carefully processed to neutralize toxicity before they can be used for therapeutic purposes. Researchers continue to analyze the complex chemical profile of Arisaema amurense to better understand its biological activity.

According to a study published by "The Journal of emergency medicine", the toxicity of Arisaema amurense is due to its content of calcium oxalate. This substance causes symptoms such as painful oropharyngeal edema, hypersalivation, aphonia, oral ulceration, esophageal erosion, and hypocalcemia. In one reported case, a 60-year-old man ingested a rhizome of the Arisaema amurensese plant as a medicinal herb. After the ingestion, the patient required endotracheal intubation and treatment with antihistamines and corticosteroids. Following these interventions, the patient had improved and was extubated 3 days after treatment.
