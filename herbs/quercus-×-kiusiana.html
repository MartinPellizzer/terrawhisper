Quercus × kiusiana

Quercus × kiusiana, commonly known as the Kyushu oak, is a hybrid plant species resulting from the cross between Quercus acuta and Quercus glauca.

This plant is known for its potential health benefits, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, which can help protect against various diseases.

The therapeutic actions of Quercus × kiusiana include its ability to modulate the immune system, exhibit hepatoprotective effects, and show anti-cancer properties.

The bioactive constituents of the plant include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and tannins, which contribute to its medicinal properties and make it a valuable subject for further research.
