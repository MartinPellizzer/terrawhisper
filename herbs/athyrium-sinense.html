What's the taxonomical classification of Athyrium sinense?

Athyrium sinense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is classified under the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. This fern is a member of the family Aspleniaceae, specifically falling within the genus Athyrium, with its unique designation being the species sinense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Athyrium sinense has large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach lengths of up to 1.5 meters, featuring multiple pairs of oblong to lanceolate leaflets with serrated margins. The stipes are typically dark brown or blackish and are covered in fine, light-colored hairs. Each leaflet possesses distinct pinnate venation and a slightly wavy texture along the edges. The underside of the fronds contains prominent sori arranged in two rows along the midvein of each segment. These reproductive structures are protected by thin, kidney-shaped indusia. The overall foliage presents a lush, feathery appearance suitable for shaded woodland environments.
