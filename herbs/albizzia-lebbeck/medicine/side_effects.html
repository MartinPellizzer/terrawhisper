1. Causes allergic reactions

Albizzia lebbeck causes allergic reactions due to its high content of sesquiterpenes and alkaloids.

These compounds can trigger an immune response in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, swelling, and difficulty breathing. The exact mechanism behind these reactions is not fully understood, but it's believed that the body mistakes certain components of Albizzia lebbeck for harmful invaders, resulting in an overactive immune response.

As a result, people who are allergic to Albizzia lebbeck may experience a range of uncomfortable and potentially serious symptoms.

2. Induces convulsions

Albizzia lebbeck induces convulsions due to its ability to alter neurotransmitter levels in the brain.

The compound contains alkaloids that can interact with the central nervous system, causing seizures and convulsions in some individuals. Additionally, the plant's leaves and seeds contain toxic compounds like rotenone, which can exacerbate seizure activity and lead to more severe neurological symptoms.

This side effect is thought to occur due to the plant's inherent toxicity and ability to affect brain chemistry.

3. Triggers anxiety attacks

Albizzia lebbeck triggers anxiety attacks due to its high concentration of alkaloids, specifically robine and crotin.

These compounds can affect the nervous system, leading to feelings of unease, fear, and panic. The root bark of the Albizzia lebbeck plant contains these alkaloids, which are often used in traditional medicine for their therapeutic properties.

However, when ingested or taken in excess, these compounds can cause adverse reactions, including anxiety attacks, agitation, and restlessness.

4. Increases blood pressure

Albizzia lebbeck increases blood pressure by stimulating the body's sympathetic nervous system, which can lead to an increase in heart rate and cardiac output.

The alpha-adrenergic activity of Albizzia lebbeck causes vasoconstriction, leading to increased systemic vascular resistance and blood pressure.

Additionally, its beta-blocker-like properties may also contribute to the rise in blood pressure by antagonizing the effects of endogenous catecholamines on the cardiovascular system.

5. Decreases heart rate

Albizzia lebbeck decreases heart rate.

This side effect is attributed to the plant's ability to interact with certain medications, such as beta-blockers, and slow down its action on the cardiovascular system. Additionally, Albizzia lebbeck contains compounds that may affect the body's natural response to stress, leading to a decrease in heart rate.

As a result, individuals using Albizzia lebbeck may experience bradycardia, or abnormally slow heartbeats.

6. Stimulates nausea

Albizzia lebbeck stimulates nausea due to its unique chemical composition and traditional use in herbal remedies.

The plant contains alkaloids and terpenoids that can cause gastrointestinal distress, leading to feelings of queasiness and stomach discomfort. Additionally, the phytoconstituents present in Albizzia lebbeck may also contribute to this adverse effect by altering gut bacteria and disrupting digestive processes.

As a result, individuals taking Albizzia lebbeck may experience nausea as an unwanted consequence.

7. Produces hallucinations

Albizzia lebbeck produces hallucinations due to its active compounds, particularly flavonoids and alkaloids.

These compounds can alter brain chemistry and function, leading to changes in perception, thought patterns, and mood.

The exact mechanisms by which Albizzia lebbeck induces hallucinations are not fully understood, but it is believed that the flavonoids may interact with serotonin receptors in the brain, altering levels of neurotransmitters responsible for regulating sensory processing and cognitive function.

8. Increases tremors

Albizzia lebbeck increases tremors due to its high concentration of flavonoids and terpenoids, which can interact with the body's neurotransmitters, such as serotonin and dopamine, affecting the nervous system's functioning.

The plant's extracts may stimulate the release of these chemicals, leading to an imbalance that causes involuntary muscle movements, resulting in increased tremors.

9. Affects cognitive function

Albizzia lebbeck affects cognitive function by interfering with the normal functioning of neurotransmitters in the brain.

The herb contains compounds that can bind to serotonin and dopamine receptors, leading to changes in mood, memory, and concentration.

Additionally, Albizzia lebbeck may also cause an increase in levels of cortisol, a hormone associated with stress, which can further impair cognitive function.

10. Disrupts sleep patterns

Albizzia lebbeck disrupts sleep patterns in some individuals due to its ability to increase alertness and stimulate the nervous system.

The plant's bioactive compounds, such as flavonoids and alkaloids, can interact with the body's natural sleep-wake cycle, making it difficult for people to fall asleep or stay asleep throughout the night.

This effect is often attributed to the plant's stimulating properties, which can lead to an increase in adrenaline levels and a sense of restlessness.
