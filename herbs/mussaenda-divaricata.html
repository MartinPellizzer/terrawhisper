Mussaenda divaricata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Mussaenda divaricata has dark green, elliptical to oblong leaves with serrated margins and prominent veins that grow on woody, evergreen branches. The plant is characterized by its large, showy, petal-like red sepals that surround much smaller, cream-colored true flowers. These colorful bracts serve as the primary visual attraction for pollinators throughout the growing season. The foliage typically maintains a dense, bushy habit which provides a thick canopy of texture. Stems are sturdy and can develop into a significant shrub or small tree depending on the environmental conditions.
