Erysimum diffusum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Erysimum diffusum has a spreading growth habit characterized by slender, branched stems that often reach heights of up to 40 centimeters. The leaves are primarily basal and arranged in a rosette, appearing lanceolate or linear with serrated margins. Higher up the stem, the leaves become smaller and more sparsely distributed. The plant produces small, four-petaled flowers that are typically yellow in color. These blossoms are clustered in terminal racemes that elongate as the plant matures. Small, narrow siliques develop as fruit following the pollination of the flowers.

    What are the herbal preparations of Erysimum diffusum?

    Erysimum diffusum is prepared as an infusion, a decoction, or a tincture to utilize its medicinal properties.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Erysimum diffusum can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. While specific toxicological data on this particular species is limited, many plants in the Brassicaceae family contain glucosinolates which may trigger sensitivity in certain individuals. Contact with the plant may lead to localized redness, itching, or a rash on sensitive skin areas. If consumed accidentally, people might experience nausea, vomiting, or abdominal discomfort. It is generally advised to avoid direct contact or ingestion to prevent these unpredictable physical responses.
