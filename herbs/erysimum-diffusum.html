Erysimum diffusum

What's the taxonomical classification of Erysimum diffusum?

Erysimum diffusum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further classified under the order Brassicales. This plant is a member of the family Brassicaceae, which places it within the genus Erysimum, ultimately reaching its specific identification as the species diffusum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Erysimum diffusum has a spreading growth habit characterized by slender, branched stems that often reach heights of up to 40 centimeters. The leaves are primarily basal and arranged in a rosette, appearing lanceolate or linear with serrated margins. Higher up the stem, the leaves become smaller and more sparsely distributed. The plant produces small, four-petaled flowers that are typically yellow in color. These blossoms are clustered in terminal racemes that elongate as the plant matures. Small, narrow siliques develop as fruit following the pollination of the flowers.
