Vitis silvestrii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vitis silvestrii has woody, climbing vines characterized by trifoliate leaves with serrated margins and small, globose berries that ripen to a dark color. The foliage typically consists of three distinct leaflets that possess a somewhat rugose texture and prominent venation. Its stems are flexible and utilize tendrils to anchor the plant to surrounding vegetation for support. The flowers are generally small and greenish-white, arranged in compact clusters. This species exhibits a sprawling growth habit common to many wild grapevines in its natural habitat.
