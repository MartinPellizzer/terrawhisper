Botanical Identification of Griffonia Simplicifolia (Griffonia simplicifolia)

To identify Griffonia Simplicifolia (Griffonia simplicifolia) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Griffonia simplicifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Fabales, order Fabales, family Fabaceae, genus Griffonia, and species simplicifolia. This classification places it within the legume family, highlighting its botanical relationships and potential medicinal properties.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Griffonia simplicifolia is the accepted scientific name for the plant, first described by the French botanist Jean-Baptiste Lamarck in 1805. It is also known by several synonyms, including Strychnos simplicifolia and Peltostigma simplicifolium, reflecting its taxonomic history and classification changes over time.

Morphological Characteristics

Griffonia simplicifolia is a climbing shrub with a fibrous root system. Its stems are slender and twining, often covered in short hairs. Leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a pointed tip. The plant produces racemose inflorescences with small, greenish-white flowers. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release three elongated seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Griffonia simplicifolia can be distinguished by its simple, alternate leaves with a smooth margin and a distinct midrib. The flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in loose panicles. It is often confused with other species in the family Fabaceae, but its unique leaf shape and habitat preferences help in accurate identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Griffonia simplicifolia is native to West Africa, particularly countries like Cameroon, Gabon, and the Congo. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and rainfall. The plant prefers well-drained, loamy soils and is typically found at low to moderate altitudes, ranging from sea level up to 1,000 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Griffonia simplicifolia blooms from July to September, producing yellow flowers. Seed formation occurs from October to December. For identification purposes, the plant is typically harvested during the dry season, from November to March, when the seeds are mature and the plant is at its peak for botanical identification.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Griffonia simplicifolia belongs to the family Fabaceae, closely related to species like Pterospartum and Caesalpinia. It shares morphological and genetic traits with other legumes, particularly in pod structure and seed characteristics. Its phylogenetic placement reflects its evolutionary ties within the broader legume family.
