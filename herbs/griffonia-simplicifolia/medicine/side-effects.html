10 Possible Side Effects Of Griffonia Simplicifolia (African Yams)

Griffonia simplicifolia, also known as African yam, has some side effects when used improperly, such as diarrhea, headaches, and dizziness.

These side effects are often caused by consuming high doses of 5-Hydroxytryptophan (5-HTP), an amino acid found in Griffonia simplicifolia. Excessive 5-HTP can lead to serotonin overload, exacerbating conditions like anxiety, depression, and insomnia.

In severe cases, it can even contribute to suicidal thoughts and behaviors, significantly impacting one's quality of life and overall well-being.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Griffonia simplicifolia if used imporperly.
