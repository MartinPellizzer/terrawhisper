Carex alta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Poales and is a member of the family Cyperaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Carex, and its specific species designation, alta.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Carex alta has perennial, rhizomatous growth habits characterized by long, creeping underground stems and densely tufted, upright culms that typically reach heights of 30 to 100 centimeters. Its reproductive structures consist of spikes arranged along the stem, where the female spikes are located toward the top and the male spikes are situated lower down.
