Spiraea prunifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Spiraea pruiniifolia has deciduous, woody stems that produce ovate to elliptical leaves measuring 2 to 5 centimeters in length with serrated margins and distinctively pubescent undersides. The foliage is typically arranged in an opposite pattern along the branches, exhibiting a bright green hue during the growing season. Small, clustered flowers emerge in dense corymbs, showcasing white or pale pink petals that contrast with the dark stems. These inflorescences are supported by slender pedicels that arise from the leaf axils. The plant overall maintains a compact, shrubby growth habit characterized by fine-textured vegetation.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

Populations are particularly abundant in the Appalachian Mountains and the Midwestern states.
