Aeschynanthus poilanei

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aeschynanthus poilanei has succulent, pendulous stems with opposite, fleshy leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. The foliage is typically a deep green color and possesses a smooth, slightly thickened texture characteristic of many Gesneriads. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often displaying striking colors like red or orange to attract pollinators. These blooms are typically held on delicate pedicels that allow them to dangle gracefully from the trailing vines. The overall growth habit is epiphytic, meaning the plant naturally climbs or hangs in forest environments.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Aeschynanthus poilanei can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or digestive upset if ingested by humans or pets. There is currently limited scientific research regarding the long-term toxicity of this specific species in humans.
