Aeschynanthus poilanei

What's the taxonomical classification of Aeschynanthus poilanei?

Aeschynanthus poilanei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized into the order Lamiales and belongs to the family Gesneriaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement within the genus Aeschynanthus, with the specific epithet poilanei designating its unique species name.

What are the herbal preparations of Aeschynanthus poilanei?

Aeschynanthus poilanei is prepared primarily as a decoction by boiling the dried herb in water to extract its medicinal components. This liquid extract is frequently combined with other traditional herbs to create complex formulas tailored to specific health needs. Some practitioners may also utilize the plant in the form of a concentrated tincture made with alcohol. Occasionally, the powdered herb is mixed into topical applications or swallowed in capsule form. These various preparations allow for different rates of absorption and therapeutic effects within the body.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Aeschynanthus poilanei can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or digestive upset if ingested by humans or pets. While the plant is primarily grown for its ornamental value, its chemical composition may trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. If a person touches the foliage and subsequently touches their face, they might experience redness or itching. Pets that consume the plant could suffer from vomiting or gastrointestinal discomfort. There is currently limited scientific research regarding the long-term toxicity of this specific species in humans.
