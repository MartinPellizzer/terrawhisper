Philydrum lanuginosum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Philydrum lanuginosum has small, lanceolate leaves characterized by a dense covering of fine, woolly white hairs on both the upper and lower surfaces. This lanuginose pubescence gives the foliage a soft, felt-like texture and a muted grayish-green appearance. The plant typically grows in compact, low-lying tufts with stems that remain relatively short and woody at the base. Its reproductive structures consist of inconspicuous flowers that emerge from within the leafy canopy. The overall structure is adapted to retain moisture through its thick, hairy epidermal layer.
