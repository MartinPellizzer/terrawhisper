Lysimachia candida

What's the taxonomical classification of Lysimachia candida?

Lysimachia candida belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Ericales and into the family Primulaceae. Finally, the plant is situated within the genus Lysimachia, specifically identified as the species Lysimachia candida.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lysimachia candida has upright, herbaceous stems that reach heights of approximately 20 to 50 centimeters and feature opposite, lanceolate leaves with entire margins. The foliage is typically bright green and possesses a smooth, glabrous texture along both the upper and lower surfaces. Small, white, star-shaped flowers emerge from the leaf axils, characterized by five distinct petals that form a central corolla. These blossoms are often accompanied by inconspicuous greenish sepals and are borne on slender pedicels. The plant's overall structure is compact and spreading, making it well-adapted to moist, shaded environments.

What are the pharmacological activities of Lysimachia candida?

This plant has antiadipogenic, anti-obesity, lipid metabolism regulation improvement, fatty liver prevention, and metabolic syndrome reversal pharmacological activities. These bioactive properties work together to mitigate weight gain by inhibiting the formation of new fat cells. By enhancing the regulation of lipid metabolism, the plant helps maintain healthy cholesterol and triglyceride levels in the bloodstream. These mechanisms further contribute to the prevention of fatty liver by reducing hepatic lipid accumulation. Ultimately, these combined effects facilitate the reversal of metabolic syndrome by addressing its core physiological imbalances.

According to a study published by "Phytotherapy research : PTR", the methanolic extract of Lysimachia candidia (LCM) was investigated for its role in managing obesity. Daily administration of 200 mg/kg/body weight of LCM for 140 days significantly decreased body weight gain by 15.66% in high-fat-diet-fed male Wistar rats. This administration also significantly decreased blood triglyceride and free fatty acid levels, though it caused no substantial change in leptin and adiponectin levels. In a 3T3-L1 cell-based assay, the butanol fraction of LCM exhibited antiadipogenic activity by downregulating adipogenic transcription factors and regulatory proteins. These findings demonstrate the potential antiobesite effects of Lysimachia candidia and its bioactive compound, astragalin.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains N-acetylgalactosamine, acetylated amino acids, astragalin, and indole derivatives. These specific chemical constituents contribute to the diverse pharmacological potential of Lysimachia candida. The presence of astragalin is particularly noteworthy for its antioxidant properties. Additionally, the indole derivatives and acetylated amino acids may play significant roles in the plant's biological activities. Together, these medicinal compounds form a complex profile that researchers study for therapeutic applications.

According to a study published by "Molecular omics", the methanolic extract of Lysimachia candida Lindl. at a dose of 250 mg kg-1 body weight p.o. was administered daily for 20 weeks. This treatment restored metabolites involved in the biosynthesis and degradation of amino acids, fatty acid metabolism, and vitamin metabolism. The plant extract specifically restored levels of acetylated amino acids and their derivatives which regulate beta cell function, glucose homeostasis, and insulin secretion. Additionally, the treatment resulted in the restoration of indole derivatives and N-acetylgalactosamine levels. These findings indicate the potential of Lysimachia candida Lindl. to decrease obesity, blood glucose levels, hepatic triglycerides, free fatty acids, and insulin resistance.
