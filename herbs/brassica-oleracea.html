Brassica Oleracea: Medicinal Uses And Benefits

Brassica oleracea, commonly known as cabbage, is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is a cultivated plant valued for its edible leaves, stems, and flowers.

This herb is rich in nutrients such as vitamins A, C, and K, as well as dietary fiber and antioxidants.

It offers numerous health benefits, including supporting immune function, promoting digestive health, and reducing inflammation.

The therapeutic actions of Brassica oleracea include detoxification support, antioxidant protection, and potential anti-cancer properties, largely due to its bioactive constituents like glucosinolates, flavonoids, and sulfur compounds.
