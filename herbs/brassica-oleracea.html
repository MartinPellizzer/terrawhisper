Cabbage (Brassica oleracea)

Cabbage (Brassica oleracea) is a member of the Brassicaceae family, native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. Traditionally, its leaves, stems, and flowers have been used for culinary uses, decoctions, and infusions.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, diuretic, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, traditional chinese medicine, and mediterranean herbal traditions.

Flowers
Inflorescence composed of dense spherical clusters of small yellow flowers with four petals each, arranged in a head, exhibiting actinomorphic symmetry.
Stems
Woody, cylindrical, hollow, pith central, nodes prominent, internodes elongated, surface glabrous, branching opposite, growth habit erect.

Anti-inflammatory

Tonic

Carminative
Traditionally described as a calming carminative, in relation to gastrointestinal comfort.
