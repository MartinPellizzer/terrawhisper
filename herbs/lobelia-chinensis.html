Lobelia chinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Lobelia chinensis?

Lobelia chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Asterales and the family Campanulaceae, eventually reaching its specific designation within the genus Lobelia. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its unique species name, chinensis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Lobelia chinensis contains lobeline, lobelanine, and various alkaloids that act as potent emetics and expectorants. These bioactive compounds are primarily responsible for the plant's ability to induce vomiting and clear respiratory mucus. The presence of these alkaloids can stimulate the central nervous system and influence smooth muscle contractions. Researchers also investigate the plant's diverse phenolic compounds and flavonoids for their potential antioxidant properties. Careful dosage is essential because the concentration of these medicinal alkaloids can become toxic if ingested in excess.

What are the therapeutic actions of Lobelia chinensis?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat respiratory ailments including asthma, bronchitis, coughs, and emphysema. It functions primarily as an expectorant to help clear mucus from the lungs and airways. The plant also possesses antispasmodic properties that can help relax bronchial muscles during breathing difficulties. Some traditional practices employ it to address various inflammatory conditions and feverish symptoms. However, caution is required because high doses can lead to toxicity due to its potent alkaloids.
