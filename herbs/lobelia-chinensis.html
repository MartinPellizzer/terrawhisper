Lobelia chinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Lobelia chinensis?

Lobelia chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Asterales and the family Campanulaceae, eventually reaching its specific designation within the genus Lobelia. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its unique species name, chinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lobelia chinensis has an erect, herbaceous growth habit characterized by slender, branching stems that typically reach heights of thirty to sixty centimeters. The leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem and possess an oblong to lanceolate shape with finely serrated margins. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils, displaying a distinctive two-lipped structure with a color palette ranging from pale blue to violet. The reproductive anatomy features a fused staminal tube that surrounds the style, a hallmark of the Lobelioideae subfamily. These plants often form dense, low-growing clusters in their natural habitats due to their spreading basal foliage.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to East and Southeast Asia, specifically spanning across China, Taiwan, Japan, and parts of Indochina. It typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest edges or near rocky stream banks throughout these regions. Within its range, it is often found growing in subtropical or temperate climates that provide consistent humidity. Local populations are most concentrated in hilly or mountainous terrains where soil moisture remains high. How is this plant cultivated?

This plant thrives in well-draining, humus-rich soil with consistent moisture and requires partial shade to prevent its delicate foliage from scorching.

It should be planted in areas that receive dappled sunlight to mimic its natural forest floor habitat. Regular watering is essential to maintain humidity, though the soil must never become waterlogged to avoid root rot. Mulching around the base helps regulate soil temperature and retain necessary moisture levels during warmer months.

Once established, the plant can be propagated through stem cuttings taken during the growing season.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Lobelia chinensis contains medicinal properties primarily located in its entire aerial portion, specifically the leaves and the flowers. The whole herb is frequently harvested to prepare decoctions or infusions used in traditional practices. These extracts are often utilized to treat respiratory ailments and assist in clearing phlegm from the lungs. What are the pharmacological activities of Lobelia chinensis?

This plant has diverse pharmacological activities including anti-mycobacterial, anti-tubercular, antidiabetic, antioxidant, and cytotoxic properties. Its anti-mycobacterial and anti-tubercular effects demonstrate a significant potential for treating various bacterial infections, particularly those caused by Mycobacterium species. The plant also exhibits antidiabetic capabilities that may assist in managing blood glucose levels. Furthermore, its antioxidant properties help neutralize harmful free radicals within biological systems. Finally, the observed cytotoxic activities suggest that certain extracts may hold promise for further research in cancer therapy development.

According to a study published by "Asian Pacific journal of tropical biomedicine", Lobelia chinensis Lour. exhibits anti-Mycobacterium tuberculosis activity. The extracts were evaluated using concentrations ranging from 50-800 μg/mL in the resazurin microtitre assay (REMA) for 5 days and in the MGIT 960 system assay for 4 weeks. The n-hexane extract of Lobelia chinensis consistently inhibited the growth of M. tuberculosis in the MGIT 960 system for 4 weeks with a single-treatment. This specific extract showed a minimum inhibitory concentration measured at 800 μg/mL. These findings suggest the plant has potential as an effective candidate agent for developing a new anti-tuberculosis drug.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several medicinal compounds including alkaloids, coumarin, flavonoids, lobetyolin, and lobetyolinin. The presence of diverse alkaloids contributes significantly to its various pharmacological properties. Within the flavonoid group, specific substances like lobetyolin play a role in the plant's chemical profile. Additionally, the compound lobetyolinin serves as another important constituent found within its tissues. These chemical components work together to define the medicinal potential of Lobelia chinensis.

According to a study published by "ACS omega", the 70% hydroethanol extracts of Lobelia chinensis were analyzed using LC-ESI-MS/MS. This analysis identified 45 metabolites, which included different flavonoids, coumarin, polyacetylenes, and alkaloids as the most abundant class. The Lobelia chinensis extract demonstrated significant antioxidant activity with an IC50 value of 1.111 mg/mL. Molecular simulation and docking studies were performed on the main compounds to predict potential antidiaabetic activity and pharmacokinetic properties. These results confirmed the alpha-glycosidase inhibitory activity of the tested compounds through their binding mode to key amino acid residues.

What are the herbal preparations of this plant?

This plant can be prepared as an infusion, a decoction, or a tincture to extract its active medicinal compounds. An infusion involves steeping the soft leaves in hot water to create a mild tea. A tincture is produced by soaking the herb in alcohol to create a concentrated liquid extract for long-term storage. What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, dizziness, tremors, convulsions, tachycardia, and respiratory depression. Consuming the plant often leads to immediate gastrointestinal distress such as nausea and vomiting. Neurological symptoms may follow, including dizziness and involuntary tremors or convulsions. Cardiovascular effects can manifest as tachycardia, which is a rapid heart rate. In severe cases of toxicity, an individual may suffer from dangerous respiratory depression.
