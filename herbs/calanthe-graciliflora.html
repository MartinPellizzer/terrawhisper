Calanthe graciliflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Calanthe graciliflora?

Calanthe graciliflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Asparagales and the family Orchidaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Calanthe, and its unique species designation, graciliflora.

Some practitioners also utilize the dried, powdered form of the plant to create topical poultices for external applications.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Calanthe graciliflora can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or mild gastrointestinal distress if ingested. While primarily an ornamental orchid, contact with its sap or foliage may trigger redness and itching in sensitive individuals. Some people might experience swelling or a localized rash upon touching the plant surfaces. There is also a risk of nausea or stomach discomfort if any part of the specimen is consumed accidentally. It is advisable to handle the plant with care to avoid these potential reactions.
