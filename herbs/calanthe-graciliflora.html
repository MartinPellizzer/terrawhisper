Calanthe graciliflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Calanthe graciliflora?

Calanthe graciliflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Asparagales and the family Orchidaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Calanthe, and its unique species designation, graciliflora.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Calanthe graciliflora has slender, elongated stems that support lanceolate leaves with prominent parallel venation.

The foliage typically grows in a basal arrangement, displaying a vibrant green hue that provides a stark contrast to its blooms. Its inflorescence consists of a terminal raceme bearing numerous small, delicate flowers with narrow petals and sepals. These blossoms often exhibit subtle color gradients and intricate patterns along their labellum.

The overall structure of the plant reflects a delicate and gracile form characteristic of its specific species.
