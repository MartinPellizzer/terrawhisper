Thymus disjunctus

What's the taxonomical classification of Thymus disjunctus?

Thymus disjunctus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its evolutionary lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Lamiales. As a member of the family Lamiaceae, it is further categorized under the genus Thymus, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species disjunctus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Thymus disjunctus has woody, perennial stems that produce small, opposite, linear leaves measuring approximately 3 to 7 millimeters in length. The foliage is typically grayish-green and possesses a slightly revolute margin. Its flowering structures consist of small, tubular, bilabiate flowers that are often pale pink or lavender in color. These flowers emerge from terminal clusters or axillary fascicles arranged along the stems. The overall growth habit is low-growing and spreading, forming dense mats across the substrate.
