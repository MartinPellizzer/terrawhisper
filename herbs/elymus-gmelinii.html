What is Elymus gmelinii?

Elymus gmelinii is a perennial grass species belonging to the family Poaceae, commonly known as the wheat grass or Gmelin's wheat grass.

It is native to East Asia, with a distribution ranging from China to Japan and parts of Korea. Botanically, Elymus gmelinii is characterized by its slender stems, typically growing to 60-120 cm in height, and narrow, flat leaves that are 2-10 mm wide. The inflorescence is a panicle, bearing spikelets with two florets, each consisting of two glumes and three or four sterile lemmas.

The species is often found in mountainous regions, growing in areas with full sun to partial shade, and well-drained soils.
