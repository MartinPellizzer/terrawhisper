Anisodus luridus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Anisodus luridus has an erect, herbaceous growth habit characterized by slender, smooth stems and oppositely arranged, ovate to lanceolate leaves with entire margins. The foliage typically displays a dull green color and a somewhat pubescent texture on the surface. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These flowers eventually develop into small, globose capsules containing numerous tiny seeds. The overall structure of the plant is relatively delicate and sprawling in its natural habitat.
