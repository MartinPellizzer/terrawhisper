Amaryllis bifida

Amaryllis bifida is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaryllidaceae, commonly known as the split-leaf or two-leaved amaryllis.

This plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine, particularly for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiviral activities.

The health benefits of Amaryllis bifida include reducing inflammation, promoting wound healing, and exhibiting immunomodulatory effects, making it a potential treatment for various conditions such as infections and autoimmune diseases.
