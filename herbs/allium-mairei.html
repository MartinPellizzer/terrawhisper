Allium mairei

What's the taxonomical classification of Allium mairei?

Allium mairei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its hierarchical lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Asparagales. Within this order, the plant is a member of the family Amaryllidaceae. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Allium, specifically identified by the species name mairei.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Allium mairei has bulbous underground structures, linear to lanceolate leaves that are typically bright green, and an umbel-shaped inflorescence consisting of numerous small, star-shaped flowers. The leaves emerge from the bulb and often wrap around the base of the flowering stem before spreading outward. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink and are supported by a slender, upright scape. The individual florets are clustered tightly together at the top of the stalk to form a dense, spherical head. This plant's overall structure is characteristic of many perennial geophytes found in mountainous regions.

How is this plant cultivated?
