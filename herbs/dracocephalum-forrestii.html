Dracocephalum forrestii

What's the taxonomical classification of Dracocephalum forrestii?

Dracocephalum forrestii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Lamiales. It further falls into the family Lamiaceae, which is part of the genus Dracocephalum, eventually reaching its specific designation as the species forrestii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dracocephalum forrestii has aromatic, lanceolate to linear leaves that are typically oppositely arranged on square, upright stems. The foliage often displays a grayish-green hue due to a fine covering of hairs on the surface. Its inflorescence consists of dense, terminal spikes containing small, tubular flowers that range in color from light blue to violet. These flowers emerge from bracts that can sometimes be tinged with purple or reddish tones. The overall plant habit is compact, reaching a height that reflects its adaptation to high-altitude rocky environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

It primarily inhabits subalpine and alpine zones where rocky terrain and moist soils are prevalent. The species typically thrives at elevations ranging from 3,000 to 4,500 meters above sea level. Its distribution is closely linked to specific climatic conditions found in these montane ecosystems. What are the pharmacological activities of Dracocephalum forrestii?

This plant has antibacterial, antifungal, antiproliferative, antioxidant, and antimicrobial pharmacological activities. Its phytochemical constituents allow it to effectively inhibit the growth of various pathogenic bacteria and fungi. The antioxidant properties of the plant help neutralize harmful free radicals within biological systems. Additionally, the extract demonstrates significant antimicrobial potential against a broad spectrum of microorganisms. Studies also suggest that its antiproliferative effects may play a role in inhibiting the development of certain cancer cell lines.

According to a study published by "Molecules (Basel, Switzerland)", Dracocephalum forrestii exhibits moderate antioxidant and antimicrobial activities. The NSB culture, which contained 18.35 mg/g DW of rosmarinic acid and a total evaluated phenol content of 24.15 mg/g DW, was evaluated for antioxidant activity using FRAp, O2•-, and DPPH assays. Additionally, the plant's antiproliferative potential was tested against L929 cells via the MTT test. The material demonstrated high safety with no cytotoxicity found at concentrations up to 50 mg/mL. At this 50 mg/mL concentration, a decrease in L929 cell viability of less than 20% was observed.

The predominant metabolite identified in the plant is rosmarinic acid, which reached a high level of 18.35 mg/g DW. The total evaluated phenol content for Dracocephalum forrestii was found to be 24.15 mg/g DW. Testing of the plant material revealed moderate antioxidant and antimicrobial activities.
