Dracocephalum forrestii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dracocephalum forrestii has aromatic, lanceolate to linear leaves that are typically oppositely arranged on square, upright stems. The foliage often displays a grayish-green hue due to a fine covering of hairs on the surface. Its inflorescence consists of dense, terminal spikes containing small, tubular flowers that range in color from light blue to violet. These flowers emerge from bracts that can sometimes be tinged with purple or reddish tones. The overall plant habit is compact, reaching a height that reflects its adaptation to high-altitude rocky environments.
