Common Hawthorn (Crataegus Cicur)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Crataegus cicur.

Common hawthorn, scientifically known as Crataegus cicur, is a small, flowering shrub widely recognized as a medicinal herb with a long history of use in traditional medicine. It is valued for its adaptogenic properties and is often used to support cardiovascular health, reduce stress, and improve sleep. Traditionally, it has been employed in European herbal medicine to treat heart conditions, anxiety, and digestive issues, with its flowers and berries being the primary parts used. In modern wellness practices, common hawthorn is incorporated into supplements and teas to promote heart health and emotional balance, thanks to its rich content of flavonoids and antioxidants. One of its unique features is the presence of oligomeric procyanidins, a rare compound that contributes to its cardiovascular benefits, and it has been historically used in herbal remedies dating back to ancient Roman times.
