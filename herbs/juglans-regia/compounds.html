Persian Walnut (Juglans regia) Compounds

Persian Walnut contains several primary compounds, including flavonoids, which are antioxidants that support heart health. Tannins and phenolic acids also contribute to its antioxidant properties. Saponins and coumarin offer anti-inflammatory benefits, while terpenoids and glycosides may support digestive and immune functions. Essential oils, alkaloids, and anthocyanins further enhance its therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

Persian Walnut (Juglans regia) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Persian Walnut (Juglans regia). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds help protect cells from oxidative stress and support overall health. Flavonoids are a key component in the therapeutic potential of Persian Walnut in herbal medicine.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Persian Walnut (Juglans regia). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health. Tannins in Persian Walnut are known for their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. These properties make tannins valuable in herbal medicine for treating infections and reducing inflammation.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid in Persian Walnut (Juglans regia) contributes to its antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports the plant's traditional use in promoting health and may aid in inflammation reduction and cellular protection.

4. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Persian Walnut (Juglans regia). It exhibits antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, contributing to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine. 5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Persian Walnut (Juglans regia). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, including anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory effects. Coumarin is commonly used in herbal medicine for its ability to support cardiovascular health and reduce inflammation.

6. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Persian Walnut (Juglans regia). They contribute to the plant's therapeutic properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. These compounds are essential in herbal formulations derived from Persian Walnut.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Persian Walnut (Juglans regia). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by supporting cardiovascular health and reducing inflammation. Glycosides in Persian Walnut are often extracted for their therapeutic effects in herbal medicine.

8. Essential oil

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloids in Persian Walnut (Juglans regia) are a class of nitrogen-containing compounds known for their pharmacological properties. These alkaloids contribute to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine, exhibiting potential anti-inflammatory and neuroprotective effects. 10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Persian Walnut (Juglans regia). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting cellular health and reducing oxidative stress. This compound plays a key role in the therapeutic potential of Persian Walnut in herbal medicine.
