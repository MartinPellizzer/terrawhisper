Botanical Identification of Persian Walnut (Juglans regia)

To identify Persian Walnut (Juglans regia) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Persian Walnut, or Juglans regia, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Rhamnales, family Rhamnaceae, genus Juglans, and species Juglans regia. These classifications help identify its botanical relationships and place it within the broader plant kingdom.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Persian Walnut is Juglans regia, first described by Linnaeus in 1753. It belongs to the genus Juglans in the family Juglandaceae. Synonyms include Juglans orientalis and Juglans turcica, reflecting its historical classification. Common names such as Persian walnut, English walnut, and black walnut are widely used in different regions.

Morphological Characteristics

Persian Walnut exhibits a deciduous growth habit, with a deep taproot and extensive lateral roots for water and nutrient absorption. The stem is straight and woody, with a rough, fissured bark. Leaves are alternate, compound, and pinnately arranged, with serrated margins. Inflorescences are catkins, with separate male and female flowers. The fruit is a drupe with a thick husk enclosing one to three seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Persian Walnut can be distinguished by its elongated, dark green leaves with doubly serrated margins and clustered, yellow-green male catkins. The husk is thick and fibrous, enclosing a single large, hard nut with a wrinkled surface. It differs from similar species like Black Walnut by its smaller size and less bitter-tasting kernel. Its leaves are also more elongated and have a distinct, glossy appearance.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Persian Walnut is native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with distinct seasons. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils. It is commonly found between 600 and 1,500 meters above sea level.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Persian Walnut (Juglans regia) blooms in late spring to early summer, typically between May and June. Seed formation occurs in late summer, around August to September. Harvest timing for identification purposes is in early autumn, usually September to October, when the nuts begin to fall from the tree.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Persian Walnut (Juglans regia) is closely related to other juglans species such as Black Walnut and English Walnut. It belongs to the genus Juglans within the family Juglandaceae. The family traits include compound leaves, nut fruits, and a preference for temperate climates.
