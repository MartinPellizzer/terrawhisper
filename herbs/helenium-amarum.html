Side Effects of helenium amarum

Helenium amarum causes skin irritation, which can manifest as redness, itching, or a burning sensation upon contact with the plant's leaves or roots.

Individuals who handle the herb without proper protection may experience allergic reactions, including hives or swelling, due to its potent chemical composition. The herb is also known to trigger nausea and stomach pain, often resulting from ingestion, as it contains compounds that irritate the gastrointestinal tract.

In some cases, Helenium amarum can lead to diarrhea, as the body attempts to expel the irritants through the digestive system. Additionally, exposure to the herb may cause eye irritation, with symptoms such as redness, tearing, or a gritty feeling, emphasizing the importance of caution when handling this plant.

These side effects highlight the need for careful use and proper safety measures when dealing with Helenium amarum.
