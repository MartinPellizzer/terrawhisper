Sophora mairei

What's the taxonomical classification of Sophora mairei?

Sophora mairei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is classified under the order Fabales. It further belongs to the family Fabaceae, which is part of the genus Sophora, and is specifically identified by its species name, mairei.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sophora mairei has pinnately compound leaves with multiple pairs of small, oval leaflets and woody stems that support dense, spreading canopies. The leaflets are typically arranged oppositely along the rachis and possess smooth margins with entire edges. Its flowers are characterized by a bright yellow color and a structure typical of the legume family, featuring a large standard petal. These blossoms emerge from axillary positions and eventually develop into elongated, brown seed pods. The bark of the mature plant becomes rough and fissured as the tree ages.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause severe poisoning, skin irritation, and gastrointestinal distress due to the presence of toxic alkaloids like matrine and oxymatrine. Ingesting any part of the specimen may lead to symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea. If consumed in large quantities, the alkaloids can trigger more serious neurological effects including tremors, dizziness, or even convulsions. Topical contact with the plant material is also known to provoke allergic reactions or localized dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Therefore, extreme caution should be exercised when handling or consuming any part of this species.
