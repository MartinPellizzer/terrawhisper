Feverfew (Harpagophytum procumbens) Compounds

Feverfew contains several primary active compounds, including terpenoids, flavonoids, and coumarins. These compounds contribute to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. Phenolic acids and saponins also play roles in its therapeutic effects. Other components like glycosides and tannins support its traditional use in herbal medicine.

Feverfew (Harpagophytum procumbens) Monograph

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Feverfew (Harpagophytum procumbens). These compounds contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. These components contribute to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, making it useful in treating conditions like migraines and arthritis.
