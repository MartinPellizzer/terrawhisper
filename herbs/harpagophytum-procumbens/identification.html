Botanical Identification of Feverfew (Harpagophytum procumbens)

To identify Feverfew (Harpagophytum procumbens) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Feverfew, scientifically known as Harpagophytum procumbens, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Fabales, order Fabales, family Fabaceae, genus Harpagophytum, and species Harpagophytum procumbens. This classification places it within the legume family, highlighting its botanical relationships and potential medicinal properties.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Feverfew is Harpagophytum procumbens, first described by the botanist Johann Georg Christian Lehmann in 1805. It has several synonyms, including Chironiella procumbens and Harpagophytum zeyheri. Common names include Devil's backbone and Osteospermum, while regional and historical names vary across cultures and regions.

Morphological Characteristics

Feverfew is a low-growing herb with a branched root system. Its stems are prostrate, hairy, and often creeping along the ground. Leaves are basal, alternate, and have a distinctive pinnate structure with serrated edges. The inflorescence consists of clustered yellow flowers, each with five petals and a prominent central disc. The fruit is a small capsule containing numerous tiny seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Feverfew can be distinguished by its distinctive basal leaves with serrated edges and a central stem with reddish veins. It has small yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, and its roots are thick and fleshy. These features help differentiate it from other similar plants in the same family.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Feverfew (Harpagophytum procumbens) is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa. It thrives in warm, dry climates and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils. It is commonly found at altitudes between 500 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Feverfew blooms from late spring to early summer, typically between May and July. Seed formation occurs in late summer, around August to September. For identification purposes, the plant is most easily recognized during its flowering stage, which is critical for accurate botanical identification.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Feverfew (Harpagophytum procumbens) is closely related to species within the genus Harpagophytum. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, sharing traits such as composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangements with other members of this family.
