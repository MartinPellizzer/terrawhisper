Commelina communis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized under Equisetopsida, falling into the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Commelinales and the family Commelinaceae, which ultimately places the organism within the genus Commelina. As indicated by its specific epithet, the species is identified as communis.

What are the pharmacological activities of Commelina communis?

This plant has significant pharmacological activities including alpha-glucosidase inhibitory activity and potent antioxidant properties. These bioactive compounds work by slowing down the digestion of carbohydrates in the small intestine. By demonstrating alpha-glucosidase-inhibiting effects, the plant helps regulate postprandial blood glucose levels in diabetic models. Additionally, its phytochemical profile contributes to anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial actions. These diverse therapeutic potentials make the species a subject of interest for managing metabolic disorders.

According to a study published by "Planta medica", a methanolic extract of Commelina communis showed potent inhibitory activity against alpha-glucosidase. Through bioassay-directed fractionation and separation, 1 pyrrolidine alkaloid known as 2,5-dihydroxymethyl-3,4-dihydroxypyrrolidine (DMDP, 1) was isolated. Additionally, 4 piperidine alkaloids were identified, which include 1-deoxymannojirimycin (2), 1-deoxynojirimycin (3), alpha-homonojirimycin (4), and 7-O-beta-D-glucopyranosyl alpha-homonojirimycin (5). These 5 compounds have been identified for the first time from Commelina communis. This discovery supports the pharmacological basis of the plant used for the treatment of diabetes.
