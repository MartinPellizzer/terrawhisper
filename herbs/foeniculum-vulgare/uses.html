Fennel (Foeniculum vulgare) Uses

Fennel is used to treat gastric distress, indigestion, and stomach ulcers due to its carminative and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps alleviate symptoms of gastroesophageal reflux disease and gastritis by reducing inflammation and soothing the digestive tract. Fennel also aids in relieving digestive gas, flatulence, and nausea, making it a common remedy for gastric discomfort.

Fennel (Foeniculum vulgare) Monograph

1. Gastric distress

Fennel is commonly used to relieve gastric distress due to its carminative and antispasmodic properties. It helps reduce bloating, gas, and cramping by relaxing the gastrointestinal muscles. Fennel oil or tea can be prepared to soothe the digestive system and ease discomfort associated with indigestion or colic.

2. Indigestion

Fennel is commonly used to relieve indigestion due to its carminative and antispasmodic properties. It helps reduce bloating, gas, and stomach cramps. Chewing fresh fennel seeds or drinking fennel tea can soothe the digestive tract. It is often recommended for mild cases of digestive discomfort.

3. Stomach ulcers

Fennel has been traditionally used to support digestive health, including the treatment of stomach ulcers. Its compounds, such as anethol and fenchone, may reduce inflammation and inhibit harmful bacteria like H. pylori. Fennel can be consumed as a tea or tincture to soothe ulcers and promote healing. It is often combined with other herbs for enhanced effectiveness.

4. Gastroesophageal reflux disease

Fennel has been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of gastroesophageal reflux disease due to its carminative and antispasmodic properties. It helps reduce inflammation and relaxes the lower esophageal sphincter, minimizing acid reflux. Fennel essential oil and aqueous extracts are commonly employed in herbal formulations for this purpose. Its mild flavor also makes it suitable for culinary use alongside therapeutic applications.

5. Digestive gas

Fennel is commonly used to alleviate digestive gas due to its carminative properties. It helps relax gastrointestinal muscles and reduce bloating. Often consumed as a tea or spice, it soothes discomfort and promotes smoother digestion. Its essential oils are particularly effective in easing gas-related symptoms.

6. Gastritis

Fennel has been traditionally used to support digestive health, particularly in cases of gastritis. Its carminative properties help reduce inflammation and soothe the stomach lining. Fennel seed or essential oil may be used internally or externally to alleviate symptoms like bloating and indigestion. It is often recommended as a natural remedy to ease gastric discomfort.

7. Gastric ulcer

Fennel has been traditionally used to support gastric health, particularly in the treatment of gastric ulcers. Its compounds, such as anethol and fenchone, exhibit anti-inflammatory and antacid properties that help reduce stomach acid and promote healing. It is often used in herbal formulations to soothe irritation and protect the stomach lining.

8. Nausea

Fennel is used to alleviate nausea due to its carminative and antispasmodic properties. It helps relieve digestive discomfort and reduce vomiting. Often prepared as a tea or tincture, fennel supports stomach health and is commonly used in herbal remedies for motion sickness and morning sickness.

9. Gastric discomfort

Fennel is used to relieve gastric discomfort due to its carminative and antispasmodic properties. It helps reduce bloating, gas, and cramping by relaxing the smooth muscles of the digestive tract. Often consumed as a tea or tincture, it supports digestion and soothes irritation in the stomach lining.

10. Flatulence

Fennel is commonly used to relieve flatulence due to its carminative properties. It helps expel gas from the digestive tract and reduces bloating. The essential oil and seeds are often used in teas or digestive remedies. It is particularly effective for gas-related discomfort after meals.
