What's the taxonomical classification of Maianthemum henryi?

Maianthemum henryi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asparagales. Further taxonomic refinement places this plant in the family Asparagaceae, specifically within the genus Maianthemum, which culminates in its unique species designation, Maianthemum henryi.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Maianthemum henryi has rhizomatous stems with lanceolate to ovate leaves that are typically 5 to 15 centimeters long and feature prominent parallel venation. The foliage grows in basal clusters, often displaying a deep green color that may turn reddish in certain lighting. Small, creamy white flowers emerge from delicate, branched panicles during the late spring or early summer months. Each flower consists of six tepals and six stamens surrounding a central pistil. Following the flowering period, the plant produces small, fleshy red berries that serve as a key identification feature.
