Juncus setchuensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Juncus setchuensis has cylindrical, slender stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and terminate in small, inconspicuous flowers. The leaves are reduced to basal sheaths, leaving the photosynthetic stems largely leafless and smooth. Each stem features small, scale-like bracts located near the inflorescence area. The flowers are arranged in compact clusters and possess six brownish tepals. These plants often grow in dense clumps within moist or marshy habitats.
