Justicia kwangsiensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Justicia kwangsiensis has lanceolate to elliptic leaves that are arranged oppositely on woody stems and feature prominent longitudinal veins. The foliage is characterized by a deep green color and a smooth, slightly leathery texture. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils, typically displaying a pale pink or white coloration. These blossoms are arranged in dense terminal spikes that attract various pollinators. The plant structure is primarily shrub-like, reaching moderate heights in its natural forest habitat.
