Christia obcordata

What's the taxonomical classification of Christia obcordata?

Christia obcordata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Fabales. This species is a member of the family Fabaceae and is specifically identified under the genus Christia, with its unique designation being the species obcordata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Christia obcordata has small, obcordate leaves that are characterized by a heart-shaped apex and a narrow base, typically measuring only a few millimeters in width. The foliage often displays a delicate, fern-like appearance due to the fine texture of its diminutive leaflets. Tiny, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils, contributing to the plant's subtle aesthetic.
