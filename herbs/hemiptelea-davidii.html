Hemiptelea davidii

What's the taxonomical classification of Hemiptelea davidii?

Hemiptelea davidii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further categorized under the order Rosales. It is a specific constituent of the family Ulmaceae, falling under the genus Hemiptelea, with its unique identity defined by the species name davidii.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various infectious diseases including malaria, amoebic dysentery, and intestinal worms. The medicinal properties of its leaves and roots are often utilized to combat parasitic infections within the gastrointestinal tract. Some traditional practitioners also employ the plant to manage symptoms of fever and malaria-related ailments. Its chemical compounds are believed to possess antimicrobial effects that target specific pathogens.
