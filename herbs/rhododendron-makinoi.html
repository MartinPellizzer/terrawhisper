Rhododendron Makinoi

Rhododendron makinoi, also known as the Japanese azalea, has been traditionally used in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.

The plant contains bioactive compounds such as flavonoids and alkaloids, which are believed to possess anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects. In folk medicine, its leaves and flowers have been employed to treat ailments like fever, skin infections, and digestive issues. Recent scientific studies have begun to explore its potential in modern pharmacology, particularly for its role in managing oxidative stress-related diseases.

However, further research is needed to fully understand its medicinal efficacy and safety for human use.
