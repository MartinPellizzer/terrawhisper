Struthiopteris eburnea

What's the taxonomical classification of Struthiopteris eburnea?

Struthiopteris eburnea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae, following the biological order Polypodiales. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the family Aspleniaceae, which contains the genus Struthiopteris. Ultimately, the specific identity of this organism is defined by its species designation, eburnea.

    Struthiopteris eburnea has medicinal properties that primarily involve its use as an emmenagogue to stimulate menstrual flow and as an abortifacient to induce menstruation or terminate pregnancy.       What are the herbal preparations of Struthiopteris eburnea?

      Struthiopteris eburnea is prepared primarily as a decoction or an infusion of its rhizomes and fronds to treat various ailments. These liquid preparations are typically steeped in hot water to extract the active botanical compounds.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Struthiopteris eburnea can cause skin irritation or allergic reactions if its fronds come into direct contact with sensitive areas of the body. Some individuals may experience redness, itching, or a localized rash after handling the plant without protection. While systemic toxicity is not widely documented, the potential for contact dermatitis remains a primary concern for handlers. It is generally advisable to wear gloves when pruning or moving the fern to minimize these dermatological risks. Always wash your hands thoroughly after any physical interaction with the foliage to prevent accidental irritation.
