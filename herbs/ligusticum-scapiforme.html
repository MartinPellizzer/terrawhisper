Ligusticum scapiforme

What's the taxonomical classification of Ligusticum scapiforme?

Ligusticum scapiforme belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant is a member of the order Apiales and falls within the family Apiaceae. Ultimately, its specific biological identification is defined by the genus Ligusticum and the species scapiforme.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ligusticum scapiforme has a perennial herbaceous structure characterized by hollow, ribbed stems that typically reach heights of up to one meter. The leaves are compound and bipinnate, featuring serrated leaflets that are arranged in an opposite or sub-opposite pattern along the stem. Its inflorescence consists of large, flat-topped compound umbels composed of numerous small, white flowers. The plant often displays a branched, scapiform growth habit where the flowering stalks emerge prominently from the foliage.
