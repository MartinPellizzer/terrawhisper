Dryopteris goeringiana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dryopteris goeringiana has a rhizome that is short and thick, topped with scales and supporting bipinnate to tripinnate fronds that emerge in a vase-like shape. The stipe is typically dark brown or blackish and is covered in fine, brownish scales near the base. Each leaf blade is characterized by a delicate, lace-like texture with highly dissected segments that give it an airy appearance. The pinnae are arranged symmetrically along the rachis, which becomes increasingly slender toward the tip. Small sori are located on the underside of the pinnules, often protected by kidney-shaped indusia.
