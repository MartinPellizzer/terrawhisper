What is Filago perpusilla?

Filago perpusilla is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It has a prostrate growth habit, forming mats up to 10 cm in diameter, with leaves that are linear to lance-shaped, 2-10 mm long, and glabrous or hairy. The inflorescence is a corymbiform corymb of small flower heads, each containing 2-5 florets with yellow or white corollas, 2-4 mm long, and 5 dark brown to black, hairy achenes, 2.5-3.5 mm long, which are the fruit of the plant. The plant is found in dry to moist environments, typically in grasslands and open scrub, and is native to Europe, North Africa, and Western Asia.

It flowers from June to October, and its seeds are dispersed by wind and water.
