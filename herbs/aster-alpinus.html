What to know about Aster alpinus (alpine aster) before using it medicinally

Alpine aster, scientifically known as Aster alpinus, is a herb that has been revered for its impressive health properties, boasting anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial compounds that can alleviate symptoms of digestive issues and soothe respiratory problems.

In the culinary world, alpine aster is prized for its delicate flavor and aroma, often used in soups, salads, and teas to add a subtle yet distinctive taste. Horticulturists adore the plant's daisy-like blooms and low-growing habit, making it an ideal addition to rock gardens and containers. Botanically speaking, alpine aster belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its small, white or pink flowers arranged in a showy cluster at the top of the stem.

With historical roots dating back to ancient Greece, where it was used as a treatment for fever and rheumatism, alpine aster has been a cherished ingredient in traditional medicine and cuisine for centuries.

What are the pest and diseases tips of Aster alpinus?
