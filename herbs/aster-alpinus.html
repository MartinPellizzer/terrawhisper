Alpine aster

Aster alpinus

Alpine Aster (Aster alpinus) is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia, known for its medicinal properties and traditional use in treating respiratory conditions such as bronchitis and asthma.

Botanical Identification

Aster alpinus is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family. It is also known as the Alpine Aster, commonly found in alpine regions of Europe. This perennial herb has hairy stems, lance-shaped leaves, and blue to purple flowers. It is occasionally referenced in herbal texts under various synonyms.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Magnoliopsida
  • Order: Asterales
  • Family: Asteraceae
  • Genus: Aster
  • Species: Aster alpinus

Active Compounds

The Alpine Aster contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and tannins, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, supporting its use in herbal medicine.

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory conditions such as coughs and colds.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and as a general tonic.

Tincture: Used for treating skin conditions and as an antiseptic.

Safety Profile

Alpine Aster is generally safe when used as directed. However, it should not be used by pregnant or breastfeeding women due to potential risks. Always consult a healthcare provider before use, especially if you have existing health conditions or are on medication.
