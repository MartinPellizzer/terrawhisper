What are the pharmacological activities of Polygala japonica?

This plant has anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, neuroprotective, and antibacterial pharmacological activities. According to a study published by "Phytomedicine : international journal of phytotherapy and phytopharmacology", a methanol extract of Polygala japonica was investigated to identify active constituents for treating inflammatory diseases. The n-BuOH and EtOAc fractions of the extract demonstrated significant anti-inflamatory activity in in vivo tests. Through column chromatography, six triterpene glycosides, identified as saponins 1-6, were obtained. Among these, saponins 1, 4 and 5 showed significantly anti-inflamatory effects on both phases of carrageenan-induced acute paw edema in mice. Additionally, saponin 5 significantly inhibited the production of the inflammatory mediator nitric oxide in LPS-stimulated RAW264.7 macrophages without obvious effects on macrophage viability.
