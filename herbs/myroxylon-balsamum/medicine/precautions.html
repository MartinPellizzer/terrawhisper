10 Precautions To Take When Using Myroxylon Balsamum (Peruvian Balsam)

Myroxylon balsamum has some precautions to consider before using it medicinally, such as being cautious with its skin and mucous membrane irritation potential.

Using it undiluted or in high concentrations can cause skin irritation, redness, and itching. Failing to dilute it properly may lead to side effects such as contact dermatitis, allergic reactions, and even anaphylaxis in severe cases.

It is essential to dilute it with a carrier oil and use it under medical supervision.

This article explains in details the 10 most important precautions to take when using Myroxylon balsamum medicinally.

1. Take it under medical supervision

When using Myroxylon balsamum, also known as Peruvian balsam, medicinally, it's important to take it orally carefully.

This is because the resin can cause gastrointestinal irritation and allergic reactions in some individuals. Ingesting large amounts can lead to nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea. Moreover, the resin's high concentration of benzoin may interact with medications or exacerbate pre-existing conditions such as bleeding disorders or diabetes.

Therefore, it's crucial to follow proper dosages and guidelines when taking Myroxylon balsamum medicinally to avoid adverse effects.
