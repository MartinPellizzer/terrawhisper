10 Possible Side Effects Of Myroxylon Balsamum

Myroxylon balsamum, also known as Peruvian balsam, has some side effects when used improperly, such as skin irritation, allergic reactions, and stomach upset.

These side effects can be caused by excessive application, skin sensitivity, or interactions with other medications.

In severe cases, skin irritation can lead to permanent scarring, and stomach upset can cause malnutrition, affecting one's productivity and overall well-being.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Myroxylon balsamum, also known as Peruvian balsam, causes allergic reactions due to its potential to trigger an immune response.

The resin's volatile oils and terpenes can bind to IgE antibodies in the blood, leading to the release of histamine and other chemical mediators that cause symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling.

2. Triggers asthma attacks

Myroxylon balsamum, also known as Peruvian balsam, triggers asthma attacks due to its potential allergenic properties.

The resin of this tree contains volatile compounds that can cause respiratory irritation and inflammation, leading to constriction of airways and increased bronchial secretions, which may trigger asthmatic symptoms in susceptible individuals.

This allergic reaction is thought to be mediated by the release of histamine from mast cells, further exacerbating the asthma attack.

3. Increases heart rate

Myroxylon balsamum, also known as Peruvian balsam, increases heart rate due to its stimulating properties, which can cause an elevation in blood pressure and cardiac output.

The alkaloids present in the extract of this plant, particularly benzoic acid, are responsible for this effect.

Additionally, the resinous substance contained within Myroxylon balsamum has been shown to increase sympathetic nervous system activity, further contributing to increased heart rate.

4. Lowers blood pressure

Myroxylon balsamum, also known as Peruvian balsam, lowers blood pressure by affecting the body's vasodilation and vasoconstriction mechanisms.

Its active compounds, such as baldrinal and cinnamic acid, may cause blood vessels to dilate, leading to a decrease in blood pressure.

Additionally, Myroxylon balsamum may also inhibit the production of certain hormones that regulate blood pressure, further contributing to this side effect.

5. Causes dizziness

Myroxylon balsamum, also known as Peruvian balsam, causes dizziness.

The compound's beta-amyrin content can lead to a sudden drop in blood pressure, resulting in lightheadedness and dizziness. Additionally, the extract's potential interaction with other medications that affect the central nervous system may further exacerbate this side effect.

As the body adjusts to the presence of Myroxylon balsamum, individuals may experience temporary disorientation due to its ability to alter brain chemistry.

6. Induces vomiting

Myroxylon balsamum induces vomiting due to its unique composition of volatile oils and resinous compounds.

The oil's strong emetic properties trigger a response in the digestive system, causing the body to expel its contents as a reflexive attempt to rid itself of the perceived irritant.

This reaction can be intense and persistent, leading to repeated episodes of vomiting.

7. Disrupts digestive system

Myroxylon balsamum, also known as Peruvian balsam, disrupts digestive system by irritating the mucous membranes in the stomach and intestines.

This may cause symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, or abdominal cramps due to its ability to stimulate the production of stomach acid and slow down digestion.

Additionally, its resinous properties can also contribute to constipation and bloating, further complicating gastrointestinal function.

8. Causes insomnia

Myroxylon balsamum, also known as Peruvian balsam, causes insomnia due to its potential stimulation of the nervous system.

The presence of alkaloids and volatile oils in this herbal remedy can lead to increased alertness and restlessness, making it difficult for individuals to fall asleep or stay asleep throughout the night.

This stimulating effect is a common side effect of using Myroxylon balsamum, particularly when taken in large doses or over an extended period.

9. Produces hallucinations

Myroxylon balsamum, also known as Peruvian balsam, produces hallucinations.

This is likely due to the presence of sesquiterpenes and other bioactive compounds that can affect the brain's chemistry and perception. The essential oil extracted from the resin of the tree contains these compounds, which have been shown to alter normal cognitive function and induce vivid sensory experiences.

As a result, users may experience auditory, visual, or tactile hallucinations, which can be intense and disorienting.

10. Affects mental clarity

Myroxylon balsamum, also known as Peruvian balsam, affects mental clarity due to its potential to interact with certain neurotransmitters in the brain.

The essential oils present in Myroxylon balsamum may alter the normal functioning of serotonin and dopamine, leading to changes in cognitive processes, concentration, and perception.

This interaction can result in decreased mental sharpness, fogginess, or a general feeling of being "out of sorts".
