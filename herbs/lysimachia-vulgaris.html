Lysimachia Vulgaris Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Lysimachia vulgaris, commonly known as yellow archangel, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its bright yellow flowers and creeping growth habit.

This herb is valued for its medicinal properties and has been traditionally used to support urinary tract health and alleviate digestive issues.

The bioactive constituents in Lysimachia vulgaris include flavonoids, tannins, and iridoids, which contribute to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and diuretic effects.

Herbal preparations such as infusions, tinctures, and topical poultices can be made from the leaves and stems to harness its therapeutic benefits.
