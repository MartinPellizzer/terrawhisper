What's the taxonomical classification of Vicia multicaulis?

Vicia multicaulis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Fabales. It is a member of the family Fabaceae, which places it within the genus Vicia, ultimately identifying it at the species level as Vicia multicaulis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vicia multicaulis has multiple slender, ascending stems that emerge from a woody base and are adorned with pinnate leaves composed of several pairs of small, oval leaflets. The inflorescence consists of small, papilionaceous flowers that typically display a pale violet or bluish hue. These flowers are borne in clusters from the leaf axils along the length of the stems.
