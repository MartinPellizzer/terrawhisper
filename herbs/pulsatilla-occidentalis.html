Pulsatilla (Pulsatilla occidentalis)

Pulsatilla (Pulsatilla occidentalis) is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, native to North America, Western United States, and Rocky Mountains. Traditionally, its roots, rhizomes, and bulbs have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its sedative, astringent, and anti-inflammatory actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, native american herbal medicine, and mediterranean herbal traditions.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 20 to 50 centimeters.
Leaves
Ovate leaves with a glaucous upper surface and a lighter green lower surface, bearing prominent stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Flowers are yellow with a central purple-black disk, arranged in solitary umbels, actinomorphic, with five white to pale yellow petals and five yellow stamens.
Stems
Cylindrical, hollow, ribbed, glabrous, terete, ascending, simple branching, persistent, articulate, with annular scars.
