Haldina cordifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Haldina cordifolia?

Haldina cordifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is classified under the order Gentianaales. This plant is a member of the family Rubiaceae, falling specifically under the genus Haldina, with its scientific name being identified as the species cordifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Haldina cordifolia has a medium-sized tree reaching heights of up to 20 meters, characterized by a straight trunk, greyish bark, and large, simple, cordate leaves that are oppositely arranged. The foliage is distinctly leathery with prominent veins and a smooth or slightly wavy margin. Its inflorescences consist of small, yellowish-white flowers that appear in terminal or axillary panicles. These flowers eventually produce somewhat fleshy, ellipsoidal fruits that turn yellow or brownish when mature. The woody structure and leaf shape are key diagnostic features used to identify this species within its tropical habitat.
