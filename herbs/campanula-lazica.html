What is Campanula lazica?

Campanula lazica is a perennial plant species belonging to the family Campanulaceae.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers, which are typically blue-purple in color and arranged in a terminal inflorescence, and its ovate to lanceolate leaves, which are often hairy and borne on a stem that can grow up to 60 cm in height. The plant is native to the Caucasus region and typically thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and full sun to partial shade conditions. Its growth habit is erect, with a spread of around 30 cm, and it tends to bloom from June to September.

The plant's seed pods are a dry, cylindrical capsule that splits open to release its seeds.
