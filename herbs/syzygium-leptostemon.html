What is Syzygium leptostemon?

Syzygium leptostemon is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae, native to New Caledonia.

It is an evergreen tree or shrub, growing up to 10 meters in height, with a broad, rounded crown and a smooth, grey bark. The leaves are elliptical to lanceolate in shape, measuring 5-10 cm in length and 1-2 cm in width, with a short petiole and a rounded base. The plant produces small, white flowers in panicles, 3-5 cm in length, with a 4-merous calyx and a 4-lobed corolla.

The fruit is a drupe, 6-10 mm in diameter, with a single seed enclosed in a woody pericarp.
