Spathodea campanulata

What's the taxonomical classification of Spathodea campanulata?

Spathodea campanulata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Lamiales. This plant is a representative of the family Bignoniaceae, which is further narrowed down to the genus Spathodea, with its specific designation being the species campanulata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Spathodea campanulata has large, opposite, elliptical leaves that can reach up to 40 centimeters in length and are characterized by a deep green color with prominent pinnate venation. The plant grows as a perennial shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights between two and five meters. Its most striking feature is the inflorescence, which consists of large, bell-shaped white bracts that surround a central cluster of small, fragrant flowers. These bracts are often described as having a waxy texture and a distinctively campanulate or bell-like shape. The stem is woody and can become quite substantial as the plant matures in tropical environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical lowland rainforests of Central and South America, spanning from southern Mexico through the Caribbean to Brazil. It thrives in humid, warm environments where high rainfall is consistent throughout the year. While its primary natural range is limited to these neotropical regions, it has been widely introduced to other tropical areas around the world. Many gardeners in Southeast Asia and parts of Africa cultivate it as an ornamental species due to its striking flowers. How is this plant cultivated?

What are the pharmacological activities of Spathodea campanulata?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological activities including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, antioxidant, antiviral, and antibacterial properties. Research indicates that its various extracts can effectively inhibit the growth of diverse bacterial strains and combat viral infections. The phytochemical constituents within the plant also function as potent antioxidants that neutralize free radicals in biological systems. Furthermore, its ability to reduce inflammation makes it a subject of interest for managing swelling and pain. These multifaceted biological actions highlight the therapeutic potential of Spathodea campanulata in medicinal applications.

According to a study published by "Antibiotics (Basel, Switzerland)", Spathodea campanulata P. Beauv. is traditionally used for treating diseases of the skin, the gastrointestinal tract, inflammation, the kidney, and the urinary system. Chemical components from this plant inhibit Helicobacter pylori growth through ureaSE inhibition and the modulation of virulence factors. The sub-fraction SB2 demonstrated the capacity to inhibit Helicobacter pylori ureaSE in a heterologous bacterial model. Another sub-fraction, SE3, was able to simultaneously modulate the expression of 2 adhesins (HopZ and BabA) and 1 cytotoxin (CagA). The flavonol kaempferol was identified as a significant compound for its capacity to modulate these virulence factors.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse array of bioactive medicinal compounds including flavonoids, glycosides, kaempferol, saponins, and triterpenoids. These chemical constituents contribute to the plant's significant pharmacological potential in various therapeutic applications. Specifically, the presence of kaempferol, a type of flavonoid, provides potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The glycosides and saponins found within the tissues are often associated with diverse biological activities such as antimicrobial effects. Furthermore, the triterpenoids present in Spathodea campanulata assist in supporting various physiological functions through their unique molecular structures.

According to a study published by "Journal of parasitology research", the Spathodea campanulata stem bark contains saponins, glycosides, triterpenoids, sterols, alkaloids, flavonoids, and tannins. Gas chromatography/mass spectrometry analysis of the extract showed the presence of medically important active volatile compounds including thymol, n-hexadecanoic acid, phytol, and maltol. The plant extract was tested against Schistosoma mansoni cercariae to determine its potency. At 240 min, the Spathodea campanulata stem bark extract had an LC50 value of 429.898 μg/mL.
