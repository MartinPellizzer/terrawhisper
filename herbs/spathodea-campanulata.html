Spathodea campanulata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Spathodea campanulata has large, opposite, elliptical leaves that can reach up to 40 centimeters in length and are characterized by a deep green color with prominent pinnate venation. The plant grows as a perennial shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights between two and five meters. Its most striking feature is the inflorescence, which consists of large, bell-shaped white bracts that surround a central cluster of small, fragrant flowers. These bracts are often described as having a waxy texture and a distinctively campanulate or bell-like shape. The stem is woody and can become quite substantial as the plant matures in tropical environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical lowland rainforests of Central and South America, spanning from southern Mexico through the Caribbean to Brazil. It thrives in humid, warm environments where high rainfall is consistent throughout the year. While its primary natural range is limited to these neotropical regions, it has been widely introduced to other tropical areas around the world. Many gardeners in Southeast Asia and parts of Africa cultivate it as an ornamental species due to its striking flowers. Consequently, its distribution is now a mix of its ancestral tropical habitats and various anthropogenic locations across the globe.

What are the therapeutic actions of Spathodea campanulata?

Spathodea campanulata has medicinal properties that function as an emmenagogue, abortifacient, emetic, and purgative. The plant's chemical constituents can stimulate menstrual flow by acting on the uterine muscles. Due to this intense stimulation, it is also recognized for its ability to induce abortion in certain contexts. When ingested in specific doses, the botanical extracts can trigger vomiting to clear the digestive system. Additionally, its potent effects serve as a purgative to induce bowel movements.
