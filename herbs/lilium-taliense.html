Lilium taliense

What's the taxonomical classification of Lilium taliense?

Lilium taliense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its placement in the order Liliales, the plant is a member of the family Liliaceae. Its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Lilium, with the unique species designation taliense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lilium taliense has bulbous rhizomes, upright stems reaching up to 60 centimeters, and lanceolate leaves that are arranged in whorls or spirals. The flowers are typically large, outward-facing, and exhibit a deep reddish-orange color with prominent dark spots on the inner tepals. These tepals are recurved, which emphasizes the long, protruding stamens and style characteristic of the species. The foliage is relatively narrow and pointed, providing a structured base for the vibrant floral display. Each plant develops from a bulb that stores nutrients to support its seasonal growth cycle in rocky alpine environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the high-altitude regions of the Taliang Mountains in the Sichuan province of China. It grows specifically within alpine meadows and rocky slopes located at elevations ranging from 3,000 to 4,500 meters. The species is restricted to these specific mountainous habitats which provide the cool, moist environment necessary for its survival. Due to this limited range, the plant is considered a localized species within its native montane ecosystem. Its distribution remains confined to these specific ecological niches in southwestern China.
