Baccharis Shrub (Baccharis trimera) Uses

Baccharis trimera is used to treat respiratory infections, inflammation, cough, asthma, bronchitis, and cold symptoms. It also helps with influenza and allergic rhinitis. The shrub is applied topically for skin infections and aids in wound healing due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Baccharis Shrub (Baccharis trimera) Monograph

1. Respiratory infections

Baccharis trimera is used to treat respiratory infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps alleviate symptoms like coughing and bronchitis by reducing mucus production and soothing irritated airways. Often prepared as a tea, it supports immune function and may shorten the duration of respiratory illnesses.

2. Inflammation

Baccharis trimera is used to reduce inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory compounds. It is applied topically for skin irritations and internally for digestive inflammation. Its efficacy is supported by traditional use and preliminary scientific studies. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture.

3. Cough

Baccharis trimera is used to alleviate cough symptoms due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps loosen mucus and reduce throat irritation, making it effective for both dry and productive coughs. Often prepared as a tea, it is a common remedy in traditional herbal medicine for respiratory support.

4. Asthma

Baccharis trimera is used in traditional medicine to alleviate asthma symptoms. It contains compounds that may help reduce airway inflammation and bronchial spasms. Often prepared as a tea, it is believed to support respiratory function. Its use is part of broader herbal practices for managing chronic respiratory conditions.

5. Bronchitis

Baccharis trimera is used to treat bronchitis due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. It helps reduce mucus buildup and soothe respiratory irritation. Traditionally prepared as a tea, it supports clearing airways and easing breathing in bronchial infections. Its active compounds promote bronchodilation and alleviate coughing symptoms.

6. Cold symptoms

Baccharis trimera is used traditionally to alleviate cold symptoms such as congestion, coughing, and sore throat. Its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties help reduce respiratory discomfort. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to support immune function during colds.

7. Influenza

Baccharis trimera is used in traditional medicine to alleviate symptoms of influenza due to its antiviral and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce fever, cough, and congestion, supporting the body's recovery. Often prepared as a tea, it is valued for its ability to boost immunity and ease respiratory discomfort during flu outbreaks.

8. Allergic rhinitis

Baccharis trimera is used traditionally to alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistaminic properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion, sneezing, and runny nose by inhibiting inflammatory responses. Often prepared as a tea or tincture, it supports respiratory health in individuals suffering from seasonal allergies.

9. Skin infections

Baccharis trimera is used to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and bacterial growth on affected areas. Topical applications of its leaves or extracts can promote healing and prevent further infection. It is commonly used in traditional medicine for minor wounds and fungal infections.

10. Wound healing

Baccharis trimera is used topically to promote wound healing due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce infection risk and accelerates tissue repair. Applications include poultices or infused oils applied directly to wounds. Its effectiveness is supported by traditional use and preliminary scientific studies.
