10 Health Benefits Of Baccharis Trimera (Coyote Brush)

Baccharis trimera, also known as coyote brush, has health benefits such as anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Its leaves and stems contain flavonoids and terpenoids that reduce swelling and fight infection. This plant's medicinal properties can help alleviate skin conditions, wounds, and digestive issues.

For example, coyote brush can soothe eczema and acne, promote wound healing, and calm digestive discomfort, improving the quality of life for those who use it.

1. Reduces inflammation

Baccharis trimera reduces inflammation because of its rich antioxidant and anti-inflammatory compounds.

Studies have shown that the plant's extracts contain flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which work synergistically to neutralize free radicals and suppress pro-inflammatory responses.

This anti-inflammatory activity is thought to contribute to the plant's traditional use in folk medicine for treating conditions characterized by inflammation and oxidative stress, such as arthritis and wounds.

2. Kills cancer cells

Baccharis trimera kills cancer cells because of its unique combination of bioactive compounds.

The plant's extracts have been shown to induce apoptosis, or programmed cell death, in cancer cells while leaving healthy cells intact.

This selective cytotoxicity is attributed to the presence of sesquiterpenoids, which target specific molecular pathways involved in cancer cell survival and proliferation.

3. Soothes digestive issues

Baccharis trimera soothes digestive issues because of its unique blend of bioactive compounds.

This potent antioxidant activity helps to protect against DNA damage, lipid peroxidation, and protein denaturation, thereby mitigating the negative effects of oxidative stress on overall health.
