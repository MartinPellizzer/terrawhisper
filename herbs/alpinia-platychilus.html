What's the taxonomical classification of Alpinia platychilus?

Alpinia platychilus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the order Zingiberales and the family Zingiberaceae. Specifically, it is a member of the genus Alpinia, with its unique identification being the species platychilus.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains medicinal compounds such as chalcones, flavonoid oligomers, and various polyphenols that contribute to its biological activity. Researchers study these molecules to understand how they might be utilized in developing new therapeutic treatments.

According to a study published by "Fitoterapia", two pairs of enantiomers of flavonoid oligomers, labeled 1a and 1b along with 2a and 2b, were isolated from the rhizomes of Alpinia platychilus. One known chalcone, identified as 3, was also isolated from the plant. The flavonoid oligomers 1a, 1b, 2a, and 2b had their structures elucidated using MS and 1D/2D NMR spectroscopic data. Separation of the enantiomeric mixtures 1a, 1b, 2a, and 2b was achieved on a chiral column using a hexane:isopropyl alcohol:ethanol ratio of 7:2:1. In anticoagulant assays, compounds 2a, 2b, and 3 exhibited potent activities to prolong the prothrombiin times (PT) and the thrombine times (TT).
