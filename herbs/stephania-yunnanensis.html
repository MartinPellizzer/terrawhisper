What's the taxonomical classification of Stephania yunnanensis?

Stephania yunnanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Ranunculales. This specific plant is a representative of the family Menispermaceae, falling under the genus Stephania, with its unique identity defined by its species designation, yunnanensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stephania yunnanensis has a tuberous rootstock that serves as a storage organ, supplemented by climbing stems with opposite or whorled leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. Its leaves are typically ovate or elliptical in shape with smooth margins and prominent venation patterns. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse profile of medicinal compounds including the benzylisoquinoline alkaloid crebanine and various aporphine alkaloids such as apoarpine. These chemical constituents are significant due to their complex biological activities. Researchers have specifically identified several cepharanthine analogs within the plant's chemical structure. These specific molecules contribute to the unique pharmacological properties of the species. Such findings highlight the potential therapeutic applications of the plant's specialized secondary metabolites.

According to a study published by "Molecules (Basel, Switzerland)", the medicinal plant Stephania yunnanensis is rich in aporphine alkaloids, which are a type of benzylisoquinoline alkaloid (BIA). Apoprhine serves as the representative and most abundant compound found within the plant. The study analyzed differences in the content of crebanine and several other BIAs across roots, stems, and leaves. Through this research, three specific CYP80 enzymes were identified as SyCYP80Q5-1, SyCYP80Q5-3, and SyCYP80G6. These enzymes exhibit different activities toward (S)- and (R)-configured substrates and demonstrate strict stereoselectivity during the formation of aporprhine.
