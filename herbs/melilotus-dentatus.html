Melilotus dentatus

What's the taxonomical classification of Melilotus dentatus?

Melilotus dentatus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Fabales. The plant is a member of the family Fabaceae, which places it within the genus Melilotus, eventually reaching its specific designation as the species dentatus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Melilotus dentatus has erect, branched stems that reach up to 1 meter in height and bear pinnately compound leaves with 5 to 15 leaflets. The leaflets are typically ovate or lanceolate in shape and feature distinctly serrated or dentate margins. Small, pea-like white flowers are clustered in dense, terminal racemes that bloom during the summer months. The plant produces elongated, flat seed pods that turn brown and split open when mature. Its root system is characterized by a deep, woody taproot that aids in survival.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause severe physiological harm through coumarin poisoning, which leads to significant liver damage and increased bleeding tendencies in humans. When ingested, the toxins within the plant often trigger acute gastrointestinal distress such as nausea and vomiting. Individuals may also experience neurological symptoms including dizziness during the onset of toxicity. These dangerous side effects occur because the chemical compounds can interfere with normal blood clotting and organ function. Monitoring for these specific symptoms is essential if accidental ingestion is suspected.
