Senecio exul

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Senecio exul has an erect, perennial growth habit characterized by hollow, succulent stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The leaves are primarily basal or arranged in small clusters, displaying a lanceolate to oblong shape with entire or slightly wavy margins. These leaves are typically bright green and possess a somewhat fleshy texture. The inflorescence consists of numerous small, yellow flower heads arranged in terminal corymbs. Each flower head features prominent ray florets and a central disk of tubular florets, which are characteristic of the Asteraceae family.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa, specifically occurring within the mountainous areas of Ethiopia, Kenya, and Uganda. It thrives primarily in afro-alpine ecosystems located at elevations ranging from 3,000 to 4,500 meters above sea level. These habitats are characterized by cool temperatures and intense solar radiation, which shape its restricted range. Because it is adapted to these specific montane environments, its distribution remains fragmented across various volcanic peaks and highland plateaus. Consequently, the species is not found in lowland forests or coastal plains.
