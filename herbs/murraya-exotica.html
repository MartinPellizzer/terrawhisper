Murraya exotica

What's the taxonomical classification of Murraya exotica?

Murraya exotica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic hierarchy further, the plant is placed in the order Sapindales and the family Rutaceae. Finally, its specific identity is defined by its placement in the genus Murraya and its designation as the species exotica.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Murraya exotica contains alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, and tannins that provide its various therapeutic properties. The presence of specific alkaloids contributes to the plant's significant antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory activities. Flavonoids within the leaves and stems act as potent antioxidants to combat oxidative stress in biological systems. Additionally, the saponin content is often studied for its potential roles in regulating metabolic processes. These diverse chemical constituents make the plant a subject of interest in traditional and modern pharmacological research.

What are the therapeutic actions of Murraya exotica?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, inflammatory conditions, digestive ailments, and respiratory issues. Its leaves and bark contain bioactive compounds that help combat various bacterial and fungal pathogens. Medicinal preparations are often applied topically to soothe wounds or used internally to alleviate stomach discomfort. The plant also shows potential in managing symptoms related to fever and common coughs. These therapeutic properties make it a valuable resource in traditional herbal medicine.
