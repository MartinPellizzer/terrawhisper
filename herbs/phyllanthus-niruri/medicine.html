What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Phyllanthus Niruri (Galangal)?

Phyllanthus niruri, also known as galangal, has health benefits such as reducing inflammation and treating kidney stones.

Its medicinal constituents include flavonoids, alkaloids, and tannins, which contribute to its therapeutic properties. In traditional medicine, Phyllanthus niruri is prepared as a decoction or infusion to treat various ailments. However, excessive consumption may cause stomach upset and allergic reactions in some individuals.

To use this herb safely, pregnant women and people with kidney problems should consult a healthcare professional before using it.

This article explains the health benefits, active constituents, medicinal preparations, possible side effects, and precautions related to Phyllanthus niruri.

What are the health benefits of Phyllanthus niruri?

Phyllanthus niruri has health benefits such as reducing inflammation and improving liver function.

Its antioxidant properties help protect against oxidative stress and cell damage. Additionally, galangal has been traditionally used to treat fever, respiratory issues, and digestive problems, making it a versatile herb for maintaining overall well-being.

Its antimicrobial properties also aid in wound healing and combating infections.

What are the active constituents of Phyllanthus niruri?

The health benefits of Phyllanthus niruri comes from its active constituents, such as flavonoids, alkaloids, and triterpenoids.

Flavonoids like quercetin and kaempferol exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, while alkaloids like phyllosterin have been shown to possess antiviral and antimicrobial activities.

Triterpenoids like β-amyrin and α-amyrin have been found to display anti-cancer and immunomodulatory effects, contributing to the plant's potential therapeutic applications in treating various diseases.

What are the medicinal preparations of Phyllanthus niruri?

Phyllanthus niruri has different medicinal preparations, such as decoction, infusion, and capsule.

Decoction is a liquid extract prepared by boiling the herb in water or other solvents, used to treat liver and kidney diseases, as well as fever and inflammation. Infusion involves steeping dried or fresh galangal leaves in hot water to produce a tea-like solution, helpful for soothing digestive issues, such as constipation and diarrhea.

Capsules are filled with powdered galangal and taken orally to treat conditions like jaundice, cirrhosis, and cancer.

What are the possible side effect of using Phyllanthus niruri improperly?

Improper use of Phyllanthus niruri increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as abdominal pain, diarrhea, vomiting, and nausea.

It may also cause allergic reactions like skin rashes, hives, and itching. In some cases, it can lead to bleeding or bruising due to its anticoagulant properties. Additionally, it may interact with certain medications, resulting in decreased blood pressure, dizziness, or fatigue.

Rare but serious side effects include kidney damage, liver damage, and even anaphylaxis in severe cases.

What precautions to take when using Phyllanthus niruri medicinally?

Before using Phyllanthus niruri for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as consulting with a healthcare professional, especially if you have pre-existing liver or kidney conditions.

Additionally, pregnant and breastfeeding women should exercise caution due to the potential risk of teratogenic effects and unknown effects on fetal development.

It's also important to use standardized extracts and follow recommended dosages to minimize the risk of adverse interactions and reactions.
