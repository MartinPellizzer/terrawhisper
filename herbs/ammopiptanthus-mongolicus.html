What's the taxonomical classification of Ammopiptanthus mongolicus?

Ammopiptanthus mongolicus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it resides in the order Fabales and the family Fabaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific genus, Ammopiptanthus, and its species designation, mongolicus.

What are the pharmacological activities of Ammopiptanthus mongolicus?

This plant has anti-proliferative and apoptosis-inducing pharmacological activities that target various cancer cell lines. Its bioactive compounds work by inhibiting the rapid division of malignant cells to prevent tumor growth. These substances also trigger programmed cell death, effectively forcing diseased cells to self-destruct. Research indicates that these mechanisms are essential for its potential use in oncology treatments. Such properties make Ammopiptanthus mongolicus a subject of significant interest in pharmacological studies.

As discussed in a study, the extract of Ammopiptanthus mongolicus cheng f. (JA1) shows significant anti-proliferative effects that depend upon time and dosage. This plant extract induces apoptosis in HepG2 hepatocarcinoma cells in vitro. The JA1 treatment leads to typical morphological changes in cells, a sub-G1 phase peak in flow cytometry, and the presence of DNA laddering in agarose gel electrophoresis. Furthermore, the expression of wt-p53 protein increases in vitro when using JA1. Because it can obviously induce apoptosis and inhibit the proliferation of HepG2 cells, JA1 can be used as a good source of medicinal plant for the treatment of hepatocarcinoma.
