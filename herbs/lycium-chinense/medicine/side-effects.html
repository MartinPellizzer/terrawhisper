10 Possible Side Effects Of Lycium Chinense

Lycium chinense has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, including diarrhea, nausea, and stomach pain, which can be caused by high consumption of the herb or interaction with medications.

Excessive consumption can also lead to allergic reactions, such as hives and itching, which can affect daily life and work performance.

In severe cases, anaphylaxis can occur, causing difficulty breathing and swallowing.

1. Causes digestive upset

Lycium chinense causes digestive upset in some individuals.

This is due to its high content of tannins and fiber, which can irritate the stomach lining and slow down digestion.

Additionally, goji berries contain a compound called polysaccharides, which can cause an imbalance of gut bacteria, leading to symptoms such as bloating, cramps, and diarrhea.

2. Triggers allergic reactions

Lycium chinense triggers allergic reactions in some individuals due to its unique combination of bioactive compounds.

The berry's high content of proteins, carbohydrates, and lipids can cause an immune response, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.

Additionally, the presence of polyphenolic acids and flavonoids may exacerbate allergies, particularly in those with pre-existing conditions.

3. Increases blood pressure

Lycium chinense increases blood pressure due to its high content of antioxidants and polysaccharides.

These compounds can cause a significant increase in blood vessel constriction, leading to elevated blood pressure levels. Additionally, the berry's adaptogenic properties may stimulate the body's stress response system, further contributing to blood pressure elevation.

As a result, individuals who consume Lycium chinense may experience an increase in their systolic and diastolic blood pressure readings.

4. Lowers blood sugar

Lycium chinense lowers blood sugar by stimulating insulin secretion and increasing glucose uptake in the body's cells.

This is due to its high concentration of polysaccharides, flavonoids, and anthocyanins, which have been shown to enhance glucose metabolism and improve insulin sensitivity.

As a result, individuals may experience hypoglycemia (low blood sugar) if they are taking medications for diabetes or have pre-existing low blood sugar conditions.

5. Interferes with medications

Lycium chinense interferes with medications, potentially leading to reduced efficacy or increased toxicity of certain drugs.

This is because goji berries contain active compounds like flavonoids and phenolic acids that can alter the metabolism and absorption of medications in the body.

Specifically, the berries' ability to inhibit certain enzymes and transport proteins can disrupt the normal functioning of medicines, leading to unwanted interactions.

6. Causes skin rashes

Lycium chinense causes skin rashes.

The high concentration of antioxidants and flavonoids in this herbal supplement can lead to an allergic reaction or irritation in some individuals, resulting in the formation of red, itchy, or blistering rashes on the skin.

This is often due to the body's hypersensitivity response to these compounds, which can trigger an immune reaction that manifests as a skin rash.

7. Triggers migraines

Lycium chinense triggers migraines due to its high concentration of tyramine, a amino acid that can cause blood vessels in the brain to dilate and become inflamed.

This can lead to throbbing pain, sensitivity to light, and other common migraine symptoms.

Additionally, Lycium chinense's potential interaction with certain medications, such as antidepressants and blood thinners, may also exacerbate migraine episodes.

8. Induces sedation

Lycium chinense induces sedation in some individuals due to its high concentration of flavonoids and phenolic acids.

These compounds can interact with the body's neurotransmitters, such as serotonin and dopamine, which regulate mood, appetite, and sleep. As a result, excessive consumption of Lycium chinense may lead to feelings of relaxation, drowsiness, or lethargy.

The sedative effect is often more pronounced when consumed in large quantities or combined with other herbs or supplements that have similar properties.

9. Affects heart rate

Lycium chinense affects heart rate by stimulating the sympathetic nervous system, which increases heart rate and blood pressure.

This is due to the high concentration of beta-carotene, a precursor to vitamin A, and other compounds such as polysaccharides, which have been shown to increase heart rate and blood pressure in animal studies.

Additionally, the berry's caffeine-like properties may also contribute to this effect.

10. Produces kidney stones

Lycium chinense produces kidney stones due to its high oxalate content.

The body is unable to fully absorb and utilize this excess oxalate, leading it to accumulate in the kidneys and potentially form stones. Additionally, Lycium chinense contains other compounds that may increase urine's ability to bind to minerals, further contributing to stone formation.

This risk is particularly concerning for individuals with a history of kidney stones or pre-existing kidney issues.
