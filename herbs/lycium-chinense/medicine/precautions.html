10 Precautions To Take When Using Lycium Chinense

Lycium chinense has some precautions to consider before using it medicinally, such as being mindful of allergies and interactions with medications.

Goji berries may cause allergic reactions, including hives, itching, and swelling.

Additionally, taking goji berries with diabetes medications or blood thinners may lower blood sugar or increase bleeding risk, highlighting the importance of consulting a healthcare professional before use.

1. Take under medical supervision

When using Lycium chinense medicinally, it's important to take under medical supervision.

This precaution is crucial due to the potential interactions of the berry with certain medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes treatments, which can lead to adverse effects or altered efficacy.

Additionally, individuals with pre-existing health conditions, such as high blood pressure or kidney disease, should consult a healthcare professional before using Lycium chinense medicinally to ensure safe and effective treatment.

2. Monitor blood sugar levels

When using Lycium chinense medicinally, it's important to monitor blood sugar levels as its consumption may cause hypoglycemia in individuals with diabetes or those taking medications that lower blood sugar.

This is because the berry contains compounds that can stimulate insulin secretion and reduce glucose absorption.

Additionally, Lycium chinense may interact with certain antidiabetic medications, leading to adverse effects if not properly monitored.

3. Avoid high doses repeatedly

When using Lycium chinense medicinally, it's important to avoid high doses repeatedly.

This is because consuming excessive amounts of the berry can lead to gastrointestinal issues such as diarrhea and abdominal pain due to its high sugar content. Additionally, repeated consumption of high doses can cause an imbalance of electrolytes in the body, potentially leading to more serious health complications.

It's crucial to follow recommended dosages and consult with a healthcare professional to ensure safe and effective use.

4. Start with low doses slowly

When using Lycium chinense medicinally, it's important to start with low doses slowly.

This is crucial because the fruit can cause adverse reactions such as gastrointestinal upset, allergic contact dermatitis, and interactions with medications like blood thinners and diabetes treatments.

Starting with small amounts allows for individual tolerance assessment and minimizes potential risks, ensuring a safe and effective treatment experience.

5. Use fresh or dried only

When using Lycium chinense medicinally, it's important to use fresh or dried only.

This is crucial because consuming raw or fermented Lycium chinense can lead to contamination with harmful bacteria and toxins, compromising the treatment's efficacy and even posing health risks.

Moreover, unprocessed forms may not guarantee consistency in quality and potency, which can affect the desired therapeutic outcomes.

6. Discard old or moldy material

When using Lycium chinense medicinally, it's important to discard old or moldy material.

This is crucial because old or spoiled berries may harbor harmful bacteria, viruses, or toxins that can contaminate the entire batch and potentially cause serious health issues when consumed.

Additionally, mold can produce mycotoxins, which can exacerbate existing conditions or interact with medications, leading to adverse reactions.

7. Store properly in cool places

When using Lycium chinense medicinally, it's important to store properly in cool places.

This is because goji berries are sensitive to light, heat, and moisture, which can cause degradation of their active compounds and reduce their efficacy.

If not stored properly, goji berries may lose their medicinal properties, rendering them ineffective for treatment.

8. Avoid during pregnancy or breastfeeding

When using Lycium chinense medicinally, it's important to avoid during pregnancy or breastfeeding.

This is because the berry may stimulate uterine contractions and potentially induce labor, which could lead to complications for both mother and child. Additionally, its active compounds may be secreted in breast milk, posing a risk of adverse effects on the infant's development and health.

Therefore, it's crucial to consult with a healthcare professional before using Lycium chinense medicinally during these sensitive periods.

9. Consult doctor before using

When using Lycium chinense medicinally, it's important to consult a doctor before using.

This precaution is crucial because Lycium chinense can interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes medications, and may exacerbate underlying health conditions like kidney disease or liver damage. Additionally, goji berries are not suitable for pregnant or breastfeeding women, and may cause allergic reactions in some individuals.

A healthcare professional can help you weigh the benefits and risks of using Lycium chinense medicinally, ensuring safe and effective treatment.

10. Report any allergic reactions

When using Lycium chinense medicinally, it's important to report any allergic reactions immediately.

This is crucial because the plant contains anthocyanins and polysaccharides that can cause skin irritation, respiratory issues, and gastrointestinal problems in some individuals. Failure to report these reactions can lead to severe complications, including anaphylaxis.

Therefore, it's essential to monitor your body's response to the herb and consult a healthcare professional if you experience any adverse effects to ensure proper treatment and prevent long-term damage.
