10 Medicinal Constituents Of Lycium Chinense (Goji Berries)

Lycium chinense has active constituents such as beta-carotene, zeaxanthin, and polysaccharides, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

These compounds possess antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, which may improve eye health and prevent age-related macular degeneration.

They may also enhance cognitive function and reduce oxidative stress, potentially alleviating symptoms of dementia and Alzheimer's disease, thus improving quality of life for individuals affected by these conditions.

This article explains in details the 10 best active constituents of Lycium chinense.

1. Beta-carotene

Lycium chinense beta-carotene is a naturally occurring pigment found in these ancient Chinese medicinal fruits.

Rich in antioxidants and carotenoids, beta-carotene plays a crucial role in protecting the eyes from age-related macular degeneration and promotes skin health by combating oxidative stress and inflammation.

Additionally, it has been linked to improved cardiovascular health and enhanced immune function, making Lycium chinense a valuable ingredient in traditional medicine and modern wellness products.

2. Zeaxanthin

Lycium chinense zeaxanthin is a naturally occurring xanthophyll pigment that has gained significant attention in recent years due to its potential health benefits.

This carotenoid is responsible for the red-orange color of the fruit and is believed to have antioxidant properties, which may help protect the eyes from age-related macular degeneration and reduce the risk of chronic diseases such as heart disease and cancer.

3. Cryptoxanthin

Lycium chinense cryptoxanthin is a unique carotenoid pigment found in these Chinese medicinal herbs.

This powerful antioxidant has been shown to have numerous health benefits, including reducing inflammation and improving cardiovascular health.

Studies suggest that cryptoxanthin may help prevent age-related macular degeneration and other eye diseases, making it an important component of traditional Chinese medicine.

Lycium chinense phenolic acids is a type of bioactive compound that has been extensively studied for its potential health benefits.

Lycium chinense ascorbic acid is a naturally occurring antioxidant found in these small, red-orange fruits.
