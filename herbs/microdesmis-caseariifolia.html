Microdesmis caseariifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Microdesmis caseariifolia has simple, opposite, elliptic to oblong leaves with entire margins and prominent pinnate venation. The foliage typically displays a glossy texture and can reach significant lengths depending on the age of the specimen. The stems are woody and often exhibit a smooth or slightly lenticellate bark surface. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in axillary clusters, following the typical reproductive structure of the Meliaceae family. The plant eventually develops small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds essential for its dispersal.
