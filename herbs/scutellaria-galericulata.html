Scutellaria

Scutellaria galericulata

Scutellaria (Scutellaria galericulata) is a member of the Lamiaceae family, commonly known as the mint family. Traditionally, it has been used for its calming properties, respiratory support, and digestive benefits, with various parts of the plant, including leaves and flowers, being utilized in herbal preparations. The herb has a history of regional use in traditional medicine systems, though specific applications may vary.

Botanical Identification

Scutellaria galericulata, commonly known as blue skullcap, is a perennial herb in the Lamiaceae family. Native to North America, it features square stems, ovate leaves, and violet-blue flowers. It is also referred to as bluejacket or skullcap in various regional names.

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for respiratory and digestive ailments due to its antimicrobial properties.

Extract: Used for treating inflammation and infections due to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects.

Infusion: Used for soothing digestive issues and respiratory discomfort.

Safety Profile

Scutellaria galericulata is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal upset in some individuals. Long-term use or high doses should be avoided, and it is contraindicated during pregnancy and lactation due to limited safety data.
