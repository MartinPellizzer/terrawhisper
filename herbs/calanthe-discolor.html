What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds calaanthoside, calaphenanthrenol, glucoindican, indirubin, and isatiin. These specific phytochemicals are secondary metabolites responsible for the diverse biological activities observed in the species. Indirubin and isatiin are particularly noted for their potential roles in regulating cellular processes. The presence of glucoindican serves as a precursor to more complex indole derivatives within the plant's chemical structure. Researchers study these substances to understand their therapeutic implications in pharmacology.

According to a study published by "Chemical & pharmaceutical bulletin", methanolic extracts from Calanthe discolor LINDL. exhibit hair restoring and skin blood flow promoting activities. Through bioassay-guided separation, a novel indole S,O-bisdesmoside named calanthoside was isolated from the plant. This calanthoside acts as a precursor glycoside of tryptanthrin, indirubin, and isaatin. Enzymatic hydrolysis of calanthoside with beta-glucosidase furnishes tryptanthrin along with a small amount of indirubin and isaatin. Furthermore, indirubin and isaatin are obtained from calanthoside through acid hydrolysis.
