What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds glucoindican, indican, indirubin, isatin, and tryptanthrin. The precursor glucoindican is hydrolyzed to release indican, which serves as a primary source for various bioactive molecules. Through enzymatic processes, indican can be converted into the red pigment indirubin, which possesses significant pharmacological properties. The compound isatin is another critical derivative found within the plant's chemical profile. Finally, the presence of tryptanthrin adds to the complex therapeutic potential of these botanical constituents.

As discussed in a study, methanolic extracts from Calanthe discolor LINDL. exhibit hair restoring and skin blood flow promoting activities. Calanthoside acts as a precursor glycoside of tryptanthrin, indirubin, and isaatin within the plant. Enzymatic hydrolysis of calanthoside using beta-glucosidase furnishes tryptanthrin along with a small amount of indirubin and isaatin. Furthermore, indirubin and isaatin are obtained from calanthoside through acid hydrolysis.
