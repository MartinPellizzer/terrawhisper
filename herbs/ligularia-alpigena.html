Ligularia alpigena

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ligularia alpigena has large, cordate to ovate leaves that measure up to 20 centimeters in length and feature serrated margins with a distinct purplish underside. The leaf surfaces are often textured with prominent veining that contributes to their robust appearance. Stems are sturdy and upright, supporting terminal clusters of bright yellow ray florets. These composite flower heads are surrounded by prominent, often reddish-tinted involucral bracts. The overall plant structure is characterized by a basal rosette of foliage that expands significantly during the growing season.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of southwest China, specifically within the provinces of Yunnan and Sichuan. It typically grows in alpine meadows and moist mountain slopes where the terrain is rugged. These populations are often found at elevations ranging from 2,500 to 4,000 meters above sea level. The specific environmental conditions of these mountainous areas provide the cool, damp habitats necessary for its survival. Consequently, its distribution is strictly limited to these specialized montane ecosystems.
