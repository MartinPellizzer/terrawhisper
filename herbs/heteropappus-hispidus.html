Heteropappus hispidus

What's the taxonomical classification of Heteropappus hispidus?

Heteropappus hispidus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant falls under the order Asterales. It is further categorized within the family Asteraceae, which is a prominent group of flowering plants. At the most specific taxonomic levels, the plant is identified by the genus Heteropappus and the species epithet hispidus.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Heteropappus hispidus can cause skin irritation, localized redness, and potential allergic reactions upon direct physical contact. The plant features fine, stiff hairs that may penetrate the dermal layer of the skin. These hairs can trigger a sensation of itching or a stinging pain in sensitive individuals. Some people may experience swelling or small bumps if the plant's bristles rub against the body. Avoiding direct contact with the foliage is the most effective way to prevent these dermatological complications.
