What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Heteropappus hispidus has an erect, perennial growth habit characterized by stems covered in dense, stiff, spreading hairs and leaves that are linear to lanceolate in shape. Its inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in specialized clusters. The fruit is a specialized achene that possesses distinct morphological traits used for taxonomic identification.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Heteropappus hispidus can cause skin irritation, localized redness, and potential allergic reactions upon direct physical contact. The plant features fine, stiff hairs that may penetrate the dermal layer of the skin. These hairs can trigger a sensation of itching or a stinging pain in sensitive individuals. Avoiding direct contact with the foliage is the most effective way to prevent these dermatological complications.
