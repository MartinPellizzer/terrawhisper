Clinopodium repens

What's the taxonomical classification of Clinopodium repens?

Clinopodium repens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Clinopodium, and its unique species designation, repens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clinopodium repens has an upright, branched herbaceous stem that typically reaches a height of 20 to 50 centimeters and features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is characterized by a soft, slightly pubescent texture and a distinctively aromatic scent when crushed. Small, tubular flowers emerge from dense, axillary clusters, exhibiting a pale pink to purplish coloration. These blossoms are arranged in compact whorls along the upper sections of the stem. The plant concludes its growth cycle by producing tiny, dry nutlets that facilitate seed dispersal.
