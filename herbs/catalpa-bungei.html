Catalpa bungei

What's the taxonomical classification of Catalpa bungei?

Catalpa bungei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant is a member of the order Lamiales and falls under the family Bignoniaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identification is defined by the genus Catalpa and the species bungei.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

These phytochemicals also modulate the activity of tumor necrosis factor-alpha and interleukin-6, which helps suppress systemic inflammation. Additionally, certain polyphenols in the plant interact with antioxidant enzymes to neutralize reactive oxygen species within cellular environments. By engaging these specific protein pathways, the plant demonstrates significant anti-inflammatory and neuroprotective potential.

What are the herbal preparations of Catalpa bungei?

Catalpa bungei is prepared as a decoction of the bark, a topical infusion of the leaves, or an aqueous extract of the root. Practitioners often simmer the tough bark in water to extract bitter compounds for internal use. The leaves are frequently steeped into a mild tea or used as a liquid compress to soothe skin irritations. Some traditional methods involve grinding the dried plant material into a fine powder to be encapsulated or mixed into salves. These various preparations allow for the targeted application of the plant's specific chemical constituents.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Catalpa bungei can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if any part of the plant is ingested. The presence of specific chemical compounds within the foliage and bark may trigger allergic contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. If consumed, the plant can lead to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain due to its internal chemical makeup. While direct evidence regarding long-term systemic effects is limited, cautious handling is recommended to avoid physical discomfort. Always consult a medical professional if you suspect an adverse reaction has occurred.
