Catalpa bungei

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Catalpa bungei has large, heart-shaped leaves that grow in an opposite arrangement along its woody stems. These broad, ovate leaves feature smooth margins and prominent venation that creates a textured surface. The plant produces long, slender, bean-like seed pods that hang from the branches after flowering. During its blooming period, it displays large, trumpet-shaped white flowers that often feature distinct yellow or purple markings. The overall structure consists of a sturdy trunk and spreading canopy characteristic of the catalpa genus.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the subtropical and tropical regions of southeastern China, specifically within the provinces of Fujian, Guangdong, and Hainan. It thrives in warm, humid environments typically found in low-altitude forest ecosystems. Within these habitats, it often occupies well-drained slopes and river valleys where moisture is consistent. Due to its specific climatic requirements, its natural range is restricted to these localized East Asian territories.
