Pea Shrub (Pisum Sativum Var. Humile)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Pisum sativum var. humile.

*Pisum sativum* var. *humile*, commonly known as the dwarf pea, has been traditionally used for its potential medicinal properties. This variety, smaller and more compact than the common garden pea, contains bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which may contribute to its therapeutic effects. Preliminary studies suggest that it may possess antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, making it a subject of interest in herbal medicine. The seeds are often used in traditional remedies to support digestive health and alleviate symptoms of gastrointestinal disorders. While more research is needed to fully validate its medicinal applications, *Pisum sativum* var. *humile* continues to be explored for its potential role in natural healthcare.
