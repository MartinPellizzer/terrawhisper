What's the taxonomical classification of Microtoena insuavis?

Microtoena insuavis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It further belongs to the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Lamiaceae. Specifically, this plant is identified by the genus Microtoena, with its unique biological identity being defined by its species name, insuavis.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Microtoena insuavis can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Contact with the plant's tissues may lead to localized redness, itching, or dermatological inflammation in sensitive individuals. If swallowed, the chemical compounds within the plant might trigger nausea, stomach cramps, or vomiting. While specific toxicity levels are not fully documented in all clinical studies, caution is advised when handling any part of the specimen. Avoiding direct contact and ingestion is the most effective way to prevent these adverse physiological responses.
