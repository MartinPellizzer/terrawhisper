Microtoena insuavis

What's the taxonomical classification of Microtoena insuavis?

Microtoena insuavis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It further belongs to the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Lamiaceae. Specifically, this plant is identified by the genus Microtoena, with its unique biological identity being defined by its species name, insuavis.

What are the therapeutic actions of Microtoena insuavis?

Microtoena insuavis has medicinal properties that are primarily utilized to treat skin ailments, wound healing, and inflammatory conditions. The plant contains bioactive compounds that exert antimicrobial effects against various pathogens. When applied topically, its extracts help soothe irritated skin and accelerate the repair of damaged tissues. These therapeutic actions are often attributed to the presence of specific phytochemicals found within its leaves. Local traditional practices frequently rely on these botanical characteristics for managing minor infections and swelling.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, stomach aches, fever, and inflammatory conditions. Traditional healers often prepare the leaves or roots to alleviate these various ailments. In certain regions, it is specifically applied topically to address wounds or dermatological issues. The medicinal properties are also utilized to manage internal discomfort and systemic temperature fluctuations. These applications reflect the plant's role in local ethnomedical practices.
