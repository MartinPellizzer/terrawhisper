Sinomenium acutum

What's the taxonomical classification of Sinomenium acutum?

Sinomenium acuatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Ranunculales. More specifically, this plant is situated within the family Menispermaceae, falling under the genus Sinomenium, and is identified at the species level as Sinomenium acutum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sinomenium acutum has woody, climbing vines characterized by opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with acute tips and finely serrated margins. The plant features slender, twining stems that can reach significant lengths as they ascend through forest canopies. During its flowering period, small, five-petaled white flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves. The inflorescence is typically a cyme, bearing delicate blossoms that transition into small, fleshy drupes upon maturation. These fruits often change color as they ripen, providing a distinct visual marker for the species.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of southwestern China, specifically within the provinces of Yunnan and Sichuan. It thrives primarily in montane environments where it inhabits rocky crevices and forest edges. The distribution is characterized by its preference for temperate, mountainous terrains at significant elevations. Due to its specific habitat requirements, the species remains localized to these particular ecological niches. According to a study published by "Journal of pharmaceuatical sciences", sinomenine hydrochloride extracted from the plant sinomenine acutum is currently used for the treatment of rheumatoid arthritis. This substance is a weak base with a pK(a) of 7.98 +/- 0.04. In studies regarding Freund's complete adjuvant-induced arthritis, an SMH spray with a medium dose of 15 mg/rat showed a near arthritis index compared to an oral solution at a dose of 12 mg/rat. This indicates that topical delivery can achieve similar anti-inflammatory efficacy to oral administration. Because C(max) and AUC for both topical preparations were lower than those for the oral preparation, systemic side effects could be ignored.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal alkaloids sinoenine, coclaurine, sinomenine, and sinomeninine. These bioactive compounds are primarily responsible for the diverse pharmacological properties attributed to the species. Sinomenine is often highlighted for its potent anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects in various studies. Researchers also examine coclaurine and sinomeninine to understand their specific roles in biochemical pathways. Together, these chemical constituents form the basis for the plant's traditional and modern medicinal applications.

According to a study published by "Oncology letters", Sinomenium acutum is a Chinese medicinal plant containing the alkaloid sinomenine (SIN). Sinomenine acts as a COX-2 inhibitor and has been used in the treatment of neuralgia and rheumatic diseases. In studies involving SW1116 cells, SIN inhibits proliferation by promoting accumulation in the G1 phase and suppressing COX-2 expression. When administered to athymic nude mice via daily intraperitoneal injections at doses of 25, 50, and 100 mg/kg, SIN showed time- and dose-dependent inhibition of tumor growth. These SIN-treated tumors exhibited a marked increase in Cip1/p21 protein levels and a decrease in the levels of cyclin D1 and cyclin E.

This plant is used for managing arthritis, rheumatoid arthritis, systemic inflammation, neuroinflammation, and various neurodegenerative conditions. Its bioactive compounds act to suppress the chemical signals that trigger painful swelling in joints. By targeting these inflammatory pathways, it can provide relief for individuals suffering from chronic autoimmune responses like rheumatoid arthritis. Furthermore, its ability to cross the blood-brain barrier allows it to combat neuroinflammation. This specific action makes it a potential therapeutic agent for slowing the progression of neurodegenerative conditions.

According to a study published by "Pharmacological research", the alkaloid sinomenine (SIN) is derived from the Chinese medicinal plant Sinomenium acutum. This plant extract exhibits multiple pharmacological effects, including anti-inflammation, immunosuppression, and anti-arthritis. Research shows that SIN reduces the number of RAW264.7 cells migrating into inflammatory paws and blocks lipopolysaccharide (LPS)-induced migration in both RAW264.7 cells and bone marrow-derived macrophages (BMDMs) in vitro. Additionally, SIN attenuates the 3D mesenchymal migration of BMDMs. The anti-inflammatory activity of SIN is linked to the down-regulation of the Src/FAK/P130Cas axis activation, as well as iNOS expression, NO production, and the expressions of integrin alphaV and integrin beta3.
