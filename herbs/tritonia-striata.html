What is Tritonia striata?

Tritonia striata is a species of flowering plant in the family Iridaceae, native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb with narrow, linear to lance-shaped leaves, 15-30 cm long, and a yellow or orange-yellow flower with six petals, 3-5 cm long, often striped or streaked with brown or red. The inflorescence is a simple spike with 2-6 flowers. The plant grows in a variety of habitats, including grasslands, rocky outcrops, and scrublands, typically in full sun to partial shade.

Tritonia striata flowers from September to November, producing seeds in a capsule.
