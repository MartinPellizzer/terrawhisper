Gastrodia elata

What's the taxonomical classification of Gastrodia elata?

Gastrodia elaata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is positioned in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Asparagales and into the family Orchidaceae. Finally, it is identified within the genus Gastrodia as the specific species Gastrodia elata.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Gastrodia elata contains bioactive compounds including gastrodin, various polysaccharides, flavonoids, and organic acids that provide its therapeutic value. Gastrodin serves as the primary active constituent, acting as a potent neuroprotective agent that stabilizes neuronal membranes. The plant's complex polysaccharides are believed to enhance immune function and exhibit antioxidant properties. Additionally, the presence of flavonoids contributes to its ability to reduce inflammation and mitigate oxidative stress within the central nervous system. These diverse chemical components collectively support its traditional use in treating dizziness, epilepsy, and various neurological disorders.

What are the therapeutic actions of Gastrodia elata?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat kidney deficiency, dizziness, tinnitus, and various neurological conditions such as epilepsy and insomnia. In traditional Chinese medicine, it is frequently prescribed to nourish the liver and kidneys to restore vital energy. Practitioners often utilize the tuber to address symptoms related to aging, such as memory loss and cognitive decline. It is also believed to support the recovery of patients suffering from hemiplegia or weakness following a stroke. By balancing the body's internal systems, it helps manage chronic fatigue and general physical exhaustion.
