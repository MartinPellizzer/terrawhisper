Gastrodia elata

Gastrodia (Gastrodia elata) is a rare parasitic plant native to East Asia, known for its use in traditional medicine to treat neurological conditions such as headaches, dizziness, and convulsions.

Botanical Identification

Gastrodia elata is a species of orchid known scientifically as Gastrodia elata. It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is commonly referred to as the Chinese mushroom or thunder god vine. This perennial plant is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, Japan, and Korea. It has a fleshy, tuberous root, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. The flowers are small and yellowish-green, and it has been known by various synonyms in traditional herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Liliopsida
  • Order: Asparagales

Safety Profile

Gastrodia elata is generally considered safe when used in recommended dosages. However, it may cause drowsiness and should be avoided by individuals with hypertension or those taking sedative medications. Consult a healthcare provider before use, especially during pregnancy or breastfeeding.
