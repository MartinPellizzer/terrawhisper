What to know about Gastrodia elata (tienchi) before using it medicinally

Gastrodia elata, commonly known as tienchi, is a medicinal herb renowned for its wide range of health benefits, including its ability to improve cognitive function, reduce anxiety and depression, and alleviate symptoms of insomnia.

Native to the tropical forests of Southeast Asia, Gastrodia elata is a delicate orchid that requires precise horticultural conditions to thrive, making it a challenging yet rewarding plant for growers to cultivate. From a botanical perspective, Gastrodia elata belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, fleshy rhizomes and white, star-shaped flowers.

With a history dating back thousands of years, Gastrodia elata has been a prized herb in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries, with ancient texts such as the "Shennong Bencao Jing" documenting its medicinal properties.

This article explains the medicinal, horticultural, botanical, and historical aspects of Gastrodia elata.

What are the medicinal properties of Gastrodia elata?

Gastrodia elata helps with conditions such as stroke, paralysis, and numbness. It is also used to treat insomnia, anxiety, and other neurological disorders. In Traditional Chinese Medicine, it is valued for its ability to calm the spirit and restore balance to the body.

The medicinal properties of Gastrodia elata are attributed to its active constituents, including gastrodigenin, gastrodolein, and 3,4-dihydroxy-5-((3-hydroxy-1-methylethyl) benzoyl) benzoic acid. These compounds have been shown to have sedative, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant effects.

The rhizome and roots of Gastrodia elata are most commonly used for medicinal purposes. The rhizome is typically harvested in the summer and dried for later use. In some cases, the entire plant may be used, although the rhizome is considered the most potent part.

Improper use of Gastrodia elata can cause side effects such as dizziness, nausea, and stomach upset. It may also interact with other medications, including blood thinners, sedatives, and antidepressants. In rare cases, it may cause respiratory depression, particularly when used in excess.

Precautions when using Gastrodia elata medicinally include consulting with a healthcare professional, particularly for individuals with pre-existing medical conditions, pregnant or breastfeeding women, and those taking other medications. It is also recommended to follow proper dosing and preparation guidelines to minimize potential side effects and ensure safe use.

What are the horticulural aspects of Gastrodia elata?

Gastrodia elata grow in well-drained acidic soils with a pH range of 5.5-6.5. It thrives in tropical to subtropical regions with high humidity and temperatures between 15-30°C. Adequate sunlight and air circulation are also essential for healthy growth.

Planting tips for Gastrodia elata include selecting a location with consistent moisture and avoiding areas prone to flooding. The rhizomes are typically planted 2-3 cm deep in the soil, with the node facing upwards. Watering is crucial during the first year, especially during dry periods.

Harvesting tips involve identifying mature rhizomes, which typically take 1-2 years to form. The optimal time for harvesting is during the dry season, when the rhizomes are mature and easily detached from the parent plant. Harvesting should be done carefully to avoid damaging the plant.

Pests and diseases affecting Gastrodia elata include fungal infections such as root rot, leaf spot, and crown rot, which can be caused by excessive moisture and poor air circulation. Insect pests like aphids, scale, and mealybugs can also infest the plant, causing damage and spreading disease.

Gastrodia elata is native to East Asia, and its geographic distribution spans across China, Japan, Korea, and Taiwan.
