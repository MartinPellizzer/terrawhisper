Botanical Identification of Gastrodia (Gastrodia elata)

To identify Gastrodia (Gastrodia elata) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Gastrodia elata belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Liliopsida, order Asparagales, family Orchidaceae, genus Gastrodia, and species Gastrodia elata. These classifications place it within the orchid family, highlighting its botanical relationships and unique characteristics.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of the plant is Gastrodia elata, which was first described by the botanist Philipp August von Jacquin in 1797. This name is widely accepted in modern botanical classification. It is also known by several synonyms, including Gastrodia tibetica and Gastrodia chinensis, reflecting its geographic distribution and historical taxonomic revisions.

Morphological Characteristics

Gastrodia elata is a parasitic plant with a short, stout stem and a single, fleshy, club-shaped rhizome. It lacks true roots and has a simple, non-branching stem. The plant is herbaceous, with a distinctive, fleshy appearance and no leaves. It produces a single, elongated inflorescence bearing small, white to greenish flowers. The fruit is a capsule containing numerous small, black seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Gastrodia elata can be distinguished by its unique, fleshy, yellowish-brown rhizome with a distinct, spicy aroma. It has a single, short stem and lacks true leaves, making it easily identifiable compared to similar species with leaf structures or different rhizome characteristics.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Gastrodia elata is native to East Asia, particularly China, Japan, and Korea. It thrives in temperate climates with cool, moist conditions. It prefers rich, humus-filled soils and is typically found at altitudes between 1,000 and 2,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Gastrodia elata typically blooms in late spring to early summer, around May to June. Seed formation occurs in late summer, from July to August. Harvesting is usually done in late summer to early autumn, specifically between August and October, when the plant is mature and the corms are fully developed.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Gastrodia elata is most closely related to species within the same genus, Gastrodia. It shares key morphological and genetic traits with other members of the Orchidaceae family, such as the presence of a fleshy, club-shaped rhizome and specialized mycorrhizal associations. These relationships help classify it within the broader orchid lineage.
