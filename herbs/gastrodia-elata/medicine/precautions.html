10 Precautions To Take When Using Gastrodia Elata (Tienchi)

Gastrodia elata has some precautions to consider before using it medicinally, such as avoiding its use in high doses or prolonged periods, as this can lead to gastrointestinal upset, headaches, and dizziness.

It is also contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding, as its effects on the fetus or baby are unknown.

If not taken correctly, it may cause adverse reactions, such as nausea, vomiting, and stomach cramps.

1. Consult professional expertise regularly

When using Gastrodia elata medicinally, it's important to take under supervision only.

This is because Gastrodia elata can interact with certain medications and may cause adverse effects when used inappropriately. Its sedative properties can increase the risk of respiratory depression, particularly when combined with anxiolytics or sedatives. Additionally, its potential to lower blood sugar levels requires careful monitoring for individuals with diabetes.

Supervised use ensures that individual needs are assessed and any necessary adjustments made to prevent complications.
