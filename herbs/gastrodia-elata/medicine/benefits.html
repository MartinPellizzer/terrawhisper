10 Health Benefits Of Gastrodia Elata (Tienchi)

Gastrodia elata, also known as tienchi, has health benefits such as improved cognitive function and relief from anxiety and insomnia.

Its medicinal properties include sedative and anti-inflammatory effects, which are attributed to the presence of alkaloids and saponins. By consuming tienchi, individuals may experience enhanced memory and focus, leading to improved academic and work performance. This, in turn, can result in increased productivity and a better quality of life.

Regular consumption may also lead to improved overall well-being.

This article explains in details the 10 best health benefits of Gastrodia elata.

1. Relieves headaches

Gastrodia elata relives headaches because of its unique properties that help to calm and sedate the nervous system.

The plant's active compounds, such as gastrodin and kaempferol, have been shown to possess analgesic and anti-inflammatory effects, which can help to reduce pain and inflammation associated with headaches.

Additionally, tienchi has a soothing effect on the brain and nervous system, helping to calm stress and anxiety that can often trigger or exacerbate headache symptoms.

2. Reduces migraines

Gastrodia elata reduces migraines because of its unique ability to regulate blood flow and calm nervous system activity.

The plant's active compounds, including gastrodin and isoqunic acid, have been shown to inhibit the release of neurotransmitters that trigger migraine pain. Additionally, tienchi has anti-inflammatory properties that help to reduce inflammation in the brain, which is often a contributor to migraines.

By addressing these underlying causes, tienchi may provide effective relief from migraine symptoms.

3. Eases tension

Gastrodia elata eases tension because of its unique properties and traditional uses.

The plant has been used in Chinese medicine for centuries to calm the mind and body, reducing feelings of anxiety and stress.

Its bioactive compounds have been shown to possess sedative and anxiolytic effects, promoting relaxation and reducing muscle spasms, making it a natural remedy for individuals suffering from tension-related disorders.

4. Improves blood flow

Gastrodia elata improves blood flow because it has a profound effect on the body's circulatory system.

The herb contains compounds that help to dilate blood vessels, increasing blood flow to the extremities and organs. This improved circulation can aid in reducing inflammation, alleviating symptoms of conditions such as Raynaud's disease, and even helping to lower blood pressure.

Additionally, tienchi has been shown to improve oxygen delivery to the tissues, further enhancing its beneficial effects on overall cardiovascular health.

5. Alleviates inflammation

Gastrodia elata alleviates inflammation because of its unique chemical composition.

The plant contains a range of bioactive compounds, including flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, which have been shown to possess potent anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds work by inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines, reducing oxidative stress, and modulating the immune response, thereby alleviating inflammation and its associated symptoms.

This natural anti-inflammatory activity has made tienchi a valuable herb in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries.

6. Calms the nervous system

Gastrodia elata calms the nervous system because of its unique ability to modulate the activity of neurotransmitters.

The plant contains a compound called gastrodin, which has been shown to inhibit the release of excitatory neurotransmitters and increase the release of calming ones, such as GABA and serotonin.

This balancing effect helps to reduce anxiety and stress, promoting a sense of calm and relaxation throughout the body.

7. Supports cognitive function

Gastrodia elata supports cognitive function because of its unique compound composition.

It contains gastrodin, a sesquiterpene glycoside that has been shown to exhibit neuroprotective properties by reducing inflammation and oxidative stress in the brain. Additionally, tienchi has been found to increase blood flow to the brain, enhancing oxygenation and nutrient delivery to neurons.

This combination of anti-inflammatory and vasodilatory effects makes tienchi a valuable herb for supporting cognitive health and potentially improving memory and focus.

8. Enhances memory

Gastrodia elata enhances memory because of its unique ability to stimulate the growth and repair of brain cells.

The plant's active compound, gastrodin, has been shown to increase the production of neurotrophic factors, which promote the survival and differentiation of neurons. This can lead to improved cognitive function, including enhanced memory formation, consolidation, and retrieval.

Additionally, tienchi may help reduce inflammation in the brain, which is a common contributor to age-related decline in memory.

9. Regulates mood

Gastrodia elata regulates mood because of its unique chemical composition.

The plant contains a group of compounds called gastrodin, which have been shown to interact with the brain's neurotransmitters, including serotonin and GABA. This interaction helps to stabilize mood, reduce anxiety and stress, and even alleviate symptoms of depression.

As a result, tienchi has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine for centuries to promote mental balance and well-being.

10. Treats seizures

Gastrodia elata treats seizures because of its unique combination of bioactive compounds that modulate neurotransmitters and exhibit neuroprotective properties.

The root of this Chinese herb has been shown to possess anticonvulsant activity by blocking sodium channels and reducing the release of excitatory neurotransmitters, thereby preventing seizure episodes.

Additionally, tienchi's antioxidants help protect against oxidative stress and inflammation in the brain, contributing to its therapeutic potential in managing seizures.
