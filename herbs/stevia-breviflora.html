Stevia breviflora

Stevia breviflora, commonly known as the sweet leaf of the Stevia family, is a plant species native to South America.

This plant has been used for centuries to provide a natural sweetener and offers several health benefits, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and hypoglycemic properties.

The therapeutic actions of Stevia breviflora include lowering blood pressure and cholesterol levels, as well as exhibiting antimicrobial and antiviral activities.

The bioactive constituents of Stevia breviflora include steviol glycosides, particularly stevioside and rebaudioside, which are responsible for its sweetness and medicinal properties.
