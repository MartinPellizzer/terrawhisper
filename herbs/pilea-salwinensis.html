Pilea salwinensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pilea salwinensis has thin, ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated margins and a distinctively textured, rugose surface. These leaves are arranged in an opposite pattern along slender, creeping stems that allow the plant to spread across its substrate. The foliage often exhibits a deep green coloration with subtle variations in shade depending on light exposure. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in clusters, typically lacking the showy petals found in other genera. The overall structure of the plant is delicate and low-growing, making it well-suited for moist, shaded environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

The distribution of this species is closely tied to the unique microclimates provided by the coastal mountain ranges.       Pilea salwinensis is prepared primarily as a concentrated aqueous extract through a process of decoction or infusion to capture its active alkaloids.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Pilea salwinensis can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, or gastrointestinal distress if ingested by humans or pets. While specific clinical studies on this species are limited, many plants in the Urticaceae family contain irritating compounds that trigger redness or itching upon physical contact. It is important to handle the plant with care to avoid localized inflammatory responses on sensitive skin. Always keep this plant out of reach of children and domestic animals to prevent potential toxicity.
