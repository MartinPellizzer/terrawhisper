Pilea salwinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Pilea salwinensis?

Pilea salwinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further falls under the order Rosales and is a member of the family Urticaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement within the genus Pilea, specifically as the species salwinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pilea salwinensis has thin, ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated margins and a distinctively textured, rugose surface. These leaves are arranged in an opposite pattern along slender, creeping stems that allow the plant to spread across its substrate. The foliage often exhibits a deep green coloration with subtle variations in shade depending on light exposure. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in clusters, typically lacking the showy petals found in other genera. The overall structure of the plant is delicate and low-growing, making it well-suited for moist, shaded environments.
