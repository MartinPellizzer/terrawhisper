Sanguisorba (Sanguisorba minor) Compounds

Sanguisorba minor contains several key compounds, including tannins, which have astringent properties, and flavonoids, known for their antioxidant effects. Phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory actions, while glycosides and saponins support digestive health. Coumarin and anthocyanin provide additional antioxidant and anti-inflammatory benefits, and terpenoids and essential oils contribute to its aromatic profile.

Sanguisorba (Sanguisorba minor) Monograph

1. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Sanguisorba minor. It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is used in traditional medicine for its ability to reduce inflammation and promote wound healing. Tannins in Sanguisorba minor are primarily found in the roots and are known for their potential therapeutic effects.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Sanguisorba minor. They contribute to the plant's therapeutic properties by exhibiting antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial effects. These compounds are often responsible for the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and skin-related ailments.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Sanguisorba minor, contributing to its medicinal properties. It exhibits antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine. This compound plays a crucial role in the plant's therapeutic potential.

4. Glycoside

Sanguisorba minor contains the glycoside sanguisorbin. This compound is known for its astringent properties and is used in traditional medicine to treat wounds and inflammation. It contributes to the plant's reputation as a hemostatic and anti-inflammatory herb.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Sanguisorba minor. It contributes to the plant's anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory properties. Found in the roots and stems, coumarin helps in treating conditions like hemorrhoids and varicose veins. Its presence makes Sanguisorba minor valuable in herbal medicine.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Sanguisorba minor. It exhibits anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, supporting its traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments. Saponins also contribute to the plant's detoxifying effects and may enhance the bioavailability of other compounds in the herb.

7. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanins are key active compounds in Sanguisorba minor, contributing to its medicinal properties. These pigments act as antioxidants, helping to neutralize free radicals in the body. They are also associated with anti-inflammatory and cardiovascular benefits. Their presence supports the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

8. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Sanguisorba minor contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. They are found in various parts of the plant, including roots and leaves. Terpenoids support the plant's traditional use in treating digestive and respiratory conditions.

9. Essential oil

The essential oil of Sanguisorba minor contains volatile compounds with potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. It is extracted through steam distillation and may contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and skin conditions. The oil's therapeutic effects are believed to stem from its complex chemical composition.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Sanguisorba minor. It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, though its specific therapeutic effects and mechanisms are not as well studied as other compounds in the plant. Alkaloids in Sanguisorba minor are typically present in lower concentrations compared to other bioactive components.
