Wahlenbergia marginata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Wahlenbergia marginata has a perennial growth habit characterized by a woody caudex, basal leaves with entire or slightly dentate margins, and actinomorphic blue to violet flowers with a distinct white throat. The foliage typically consists of simple, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often sparsely hairy. Its inflorescence is a terminal cyme bearing several spreading flowers on slender pedicels. The corolla features five fused petals that expand into a shallowly lobed limb. Small, globose capsules serve as the fruit, containing numerous tiny seeds for reproduction.
