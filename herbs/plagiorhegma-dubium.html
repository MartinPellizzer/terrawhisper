Plagiorhegma dubium has a prostrate, creeping growth habit characterized by small, translucent green leaves that are arranged in a single, irregular row along a thin stem. These tiny, scale-like leaves are often closely overlapping, creating a dense, moss-like mat on its substrate. The stems are delicate and often branch irregularly, allowing the plant to spread across moist surfaces.
