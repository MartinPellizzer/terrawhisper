Sanguisorba tenuifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Sanguisorba tenuifolia?

Sanguisorba tenuifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, the plant is placed in the order Rosales and belongs to the family Rosaceae. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Sanguisorba and the species tenuifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sanguisorba tenuifolia has slender, creeping rhizomes and pinnately compound leaves composed of several pairs of small, oblong, or lanceolate leaflets with serrated margins. The plant typically exhibits a low-growing habit with stems that spread along the ground via stolons. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often appearing in axillary clusters. The fruit develops into a small, dry schizocarp that contains multiple tiny seeds. This morphology is characteristic of its adaptation to shaded, moist forest environments.

This plant is native to the temperate and subtropical regions of East Asia, specifically spanning across China, Japan, Korea, and parts of Southeast Asia. Within these varied climates, it occupies different elevational zones ranging from lowland valleys to mid-altitude woodlands. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains various medicinal compounds including phenols, steroids, and triterpenoids. These bioactive molecules contribute to the plant's diverse pharmacological profile and biological activities. The phenols are primarily responsible for the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Additionally, the presence of steroids and triterpenoids supports its potential use in regulating cellular functions. Together, these chemical constituents make Sanguisorba tenuifolia a subject of significant pharmacological interest.

According to a study published by "International journal of molecular sciences", the dichloromethane fraction of Sanguisorba tenuifolia, referred to as SCE, was derived from roots extracted using 75% ethanol. UPLC/MS-MS analysis of the SCE identified a total of 423 compounds. Among these, the study identified 12 active ingredients such as triterpenoids, phenols, and steroids. These components work to inhibit LPS-induced cellular inflammation by modulating the p38/ERK/MAPK and NF-κB signaling pathways.

What are the herbal preparations of this plant?

This plant can be prepared as a decoction by boiling the dried roots in water to extract its potent tannins, a tincture made by soaking the plant material in alcohol, an infusion created by steeping the herb in hot water, or a fine powder used for direct ingestion. For those preferring more gentle preparations, a simple infusion offers a lighter way to consume its properties. The dried plant can also be ground into a powder to be mixed into food or capsules.
