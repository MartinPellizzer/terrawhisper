Gentiana napulifera

What's the taxonomical classification of Gentiana napulifera?

Gentiana napulifera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, following the biological order Gentianales. As a member of the family Gentianaceae, it is placed within the genus Gentiana, with the specific designation of the species napulifera.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gentiana napulifera has upright, perennial stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and bear opposite, lanceolate leaves with entire margins. The foliage is characterized by a dark green color and a texture that can vary from smooth to slightly rugose depending on the specific habitat. Its flowers are large, funnel-shaped, and possess a striking deep blue pigmentation with prominent longitudinal veins. These blossoms emerge from the leaf axils and are supported by short pedicels. The plant also develops a robust root system that allows it to anchor firmly in rocky or alpine soil environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude alpine regions of the Himalayas, spanning across parts of India, Nepal, Bhutan, and China. It typically flourishes in subalpine and alpine meadows located at elevations ranging from 3,000 to 4,500 meters above sea level. These populations are often found in rocky crevices or moist grasslands where the soil is well-drained and the climate remains cool. Because it is adapted to such specific mountainous environments, its range is naturally fragmented by steep terrain and extreme weather conditions. The distribution follows the specific climatic belts found along the Himalayan mountain range.

How is this plant cultivated?
