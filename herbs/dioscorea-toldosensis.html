What is Dioscorea toldosensis?

Dioscorea toldosensis is a species of flowering plant in the family Dioscoreaceae, native to Argentina.

It is a perennial vine with long, twining stems and cordate, alternate leaves that are 5-10 cm long and 4-7 cm wide, with 5-7 lobes and a 2-5 cm long petiole. The plant produces small, white to greenish flowers in axillary racemes, and edible, avoid, yellowish-brown tubers that are 2-5 cm long and 1-2 cm in diameter. The leaves and stems are glabrous, while the tubers are covered with a brownish-yellow, reticulate, and tuberculate skin.

Dioscorea toldosensis grows in subtropical and temperate forests, and the tubers are rich in starch and have been used as food by local populations.
