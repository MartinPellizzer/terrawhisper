New Zealand tea tree

Leptospermum scoparium

New Zealand Tea Tree (Leptospermum scoparium) is a native evergreen shrub of New Zealand known for its small, fragrant white flowers and its use in traditional Māori medicine, particularly for its antimicrobial and wound-healing properties.

Botanical Identification

New Zealand Tea Tree (Leptospermum scoparium) is a species of shrub in the family Myrtaceae. Native to New Zealand, it is also known as kāpuka or mānuka. It is a perennial plant with small, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. Synonyms in herbal literature include Leptospermum polyphloem and Leptospermum scoparium var. polycarpum.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Magnoliopsida
  • Order: Ericales
  • Family: Ericaceae
  • Genus: Leptospermum
  • Species: Leptospermum scoparium

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for respiratory infections and inflammation due to its antimicrobial properties.

Infusion: Used for digestive issues and as a general tonic due to its mild stimulant effects.

Tincture: Used for wound healing and as an antiseptic due to its high concentration of active compounds.

Safety Profile

The New Zealand Tea Tree (Leptospermum scoparium) is generally safe when used as directed. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation due to limited safety data. Individuals with allergies to plants in the Myrtaceae family should also exercise caution.
