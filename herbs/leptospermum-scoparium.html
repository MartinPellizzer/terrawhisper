New Zealand tea tree

Leptospermum scoparium

New Zealand Tea Tree (Leptospermum scoparium) is a native evergreen shrub of New Zealand known for its small, fragrant white flowers and its use in traditional Māori medicine, particularly for its antimicrobial and wound-healing properties.

Taxonomy

The New Zealand Tea Tree, or Leptospermum scoparium, is a species belonging to the genus Leptospermum within the family Ericaceae, and is classified under the order Ericales, class Magnoliopsida, division Magnoliophyta, and kingdom Plantae.

Morphology

The New Zealand Tea Tree (Leptospermum scoparium) is a low-growing shrub with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically 2-6 cm long and 3-8 mm wide, with a dense covering of fine, white, fuzzy hairs on the upper surface.

Distribution

The New Zealand Tea Tree (Leptospermum scoparium) is native to New Zealand, specifically found throughout the country's North and South Islands, with a higher concentration in the northern regions, and it thrives in a variety of soil types and climates, including coastal areas, forests, and open grasslands.

Traditional Uses

Traditionally, New Zealand Tea Tree (Leptospermum scoparium) has been used by Māori for its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and wound-healing properties, with applications in treating skin infections, respiratory ailments, and as a general tonic for immune support.

Medicinal Systems

The New Zealand Tea Tree (Leptospermum scoparium) has been traditionally used in Māori medicine, known as Rongoā Māori, for its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and wound-healing properties.

Phytochemistry

The phytochemistry of New Zealand Tea Tree (Leptospermum scoparium) is characterized by the presence of bioactive compounds such as terpenoids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties.

Pharmacology

The New Zealand Tea Tree exhibits pharmacological properties that include antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and wound-healing effects, making it valuable in herbal medicine for treating infections, skin conditions, and promoting tissue repair.

Botanical Identification

New Zealand Tea Tree (Leptospermum scoparium) is a species of shrub in the family Myrtaceae. Native to New Zealand, it is also known as kāpuka or mānuka. It is a perennial plant with small, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. Synonyms in herbal literature include Leptospermum polyphloem and Leptospermum scoparium var. polycarpum.

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for respiratory infections and inflammation due to its antimicrobial properties.

Infusion: Used for digestive issues and as a general tonic due to its mild stimulant effects.

Tincture: Used for wound healing and as an antiseptic due to its high concentration of active compounds.

Safety Profile

The New Zealand Tea Tree (Leptospermum scoparium) is generally safe when used as directed. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation due to limited safety data. Individuals with allergies to plants in the Myrtaceae family should also exercise caution.
