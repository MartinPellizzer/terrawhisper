What is New Zealand Tea Tree (Leptospermum Scoparium)?

The New Zealand Tea Tree, or Leptospermum scoparium, is a native evergreen shrub or small tree found throughout New Zealand, known for its aromatic leaves and medicinal properties used in traditional Māori medicine and modern herbal remedies.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of New Zealand tea tree is Leptospermum scoparium. This plant belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Ericales, family Ericaceae, genus Leptospermum, and species Leptospermum scoparium. It is commonly known as kāpuka in Māori and tea tree in English. The plant is also referred to as kōwhai kāpuka in some regional Māori dialects. Synonyms include Leptospermum polygaloides and Teucrium scoparium.

Plant Description and Morphology

The New Zealand Tea Tree (Leptospermum scoparium) is a small evergreen shrub or tree, typically growing up to 5 meters tall, with slender, twisted branches, dark green, leathery leaves, and small white or pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters during late winter to early spring.

Native Habitat and Distribution

The New Zealand Tea Tree (Leptospermum scoparium) is native to New Zealand, predominantly found in the northern and eastern regions, including the North Island, parts of the South Island, and the surrounding islands, thriving in coastal and inland areas with well-drained soils.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The leaves, flowers, and bark of the New Zealand Tea Tree (Leptospermum scoparium) are used medicinally, with the leaves being the most commonly utilized part for their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

Phytochemical Composition

Pharmacological Properties

The New Zealand Tea Tree (Leptospermum scoparium) exhibits a range of pharmacological properties, including antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and wound-healing effects, primarily due to its high concentration of terpenoids, flavonoids, and phenolic compounds.

Therapeutic Uses and Indications

New Zealand Tea Tree (Leptospermum scoparium) is traditionally used in herbal medicine for its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and wound-healing properties, particularly in the treatment of skin infections, inflammation, and as a supportive therapy for respiratory and immune system health.
