What's the taxonomical classification of Bambusa pervariabilis?

Bambusa pervariabilis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Poales and the family Poaceae, eventually reaching its specific identification within the genus Bambusa. Ultimately, the plant is identified at the species level as pervariabilis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bambusa pervariabilis has slender, cylindrical culms that typically reach heights of 10 to 15 meters and feature smooth, greenish-yellow internodes. The plant exhibits a clumping growth habit characterized by a compact rhizome system. Its leaves are lanceolate in shape, displaying a bright green color and a fine, delicate texture. Branching occurs primarily at the nodes, with smaller side branches emerging from the main stem. The internode length can vary significantly depending on the maturity and environmental conditions of the plant.
