Silene viscidula

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Silene viscidula has erect, glandular-pubescent stems and opposite, lanceolate to ovate leaves that are typically sessile. The plant features sticky, viscid hairs covering much of its vegetative surface to deter herbivores. Its inflorescence consists of small, five-petaled flowers that are often white or pinkish in hue. The calyx is tubular and characterized by prominent longitudinal veins. These reproductive structures are held atop slender pedicels that emerge from the upper leaf axils.
