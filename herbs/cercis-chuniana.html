Cercis chuniana

What's the taxonomical classification of Cercis chuniana?

Cercis chuniana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it in the order Fabales. As a member of the family Fabaceae, it is further categorized under the genus Cercis, with its specific identity defined by the species name chuniana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cercis chuniana has small, cordate leaves with entire margins and a base that is distinctly heart-shaped.

The foliage is typically arranged alternately along the woody stems of the shrub or small tree. During the flowering season, small, pea-like blossoms emerge directly from the branches, often appearing before the leaves fully develop. The plant produces elongated, flat seed pods that persist on the branches well into the growing season.

These structural features are characteristic of the genus and help distinguish it from other species within the legume family.
