Phlomis betonicoides

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phlomis betonicoides has a perennial, subshrub habit characterized by upright, woody stems and large, rugose, ovate leaves that are covered in dense, silvery-white tomentum. These leaves are arranged in opposite pairs along the stems and possess a distinctively textured, velvety surface. The inflorescence consists of dense, verticillate whorls of tubular, bilabiate flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These blossoms are typically creamy white or pale yellow and feature prominent, protruding stamens. The overall structure of the plant provides a striking contrast between the soft, felt-like foliage and the rigid, architectural form of the flowering spikes.
