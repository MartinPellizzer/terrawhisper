What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rubus buergeri has arching stems that are covered in fine, reddish-brown hairs and sharp prickles, supporting compound leaves with three to five serrated leaflets. The leaflets typically feature an ovate to lanceolate shape with acute tips and distinctively textured surfaces. Small, white to pale pink flowers emerge from the leaf axils, consisting of five petals surrounding a central cluster of numerous stamens. These blossoms eventually give way to aggregate fruits that transition from green to a dark red or black color when fully ripe. The overall growth habit is that of a sprawling or climbing shrub adapted to forest undergrowth.
