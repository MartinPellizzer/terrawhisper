What's the taxonomical classification of Abies delavayi?

Abies delavayi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Pinidae. The taxonomic progression continues through the order Pinales and the family Pinaceae, ultimately placing the plant within the genus Abies. Specifically, this coniferous species is identified by its unique binomial name, the species delavayi.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Abies delavayi has needle-like leaves that are typically 2 to 3 centimeters long, slightly curved, and feature two prominent white stomatal bands on their undersides. The foliage is densely arranged on the branches, creating a lush, dark green appearance that characterizes the tree's crown. Its bark is smooth and grayish when young but becomes deeply fissured and scaly as the specimen matures. The cones are cylindrical and upright, often reaching lengths of 5 to 10 centimeters during development. These cones eventually disintegrate on the branch to release winged seeds for dispersal.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

The species prefers cool, moist climates often found on steep mountain slopes or along rocky ridges.
