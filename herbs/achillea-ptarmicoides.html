Achillea ptarmicoides

What's the taxonomical classification of Achillea ptarmicoides?

Achillea ptarmicoides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is classified under the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae and is specifically identified by the genus Achillea and the species ptarmicoides.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Achillea ptarmicoides has a perennial growth habit characterized by finely dissected, pinnatifid leaves that are grayish-green and arranged in a basal rosette or along spreading stems. The foliage features narrow, linear segments that provide a feathery appearance to the plant. Its inflorescence consists of numerous small, white flower heads arranged in dense, flat-topped corymbose clusters. Each individual flower head is relatively small and contains several disc florets surrounded by inconspicuous ray florets. The stems are typically upright and can become woody at the base as the plant matures.
