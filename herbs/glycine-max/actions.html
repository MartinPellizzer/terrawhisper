Soybean (Glycine max) Actions

Soybean exhibits several therapeutic actions including tonic effects to strengthen the body, diuretic properties to increase urine production, and anti-inflammatory benefits to reduce swelling. It also acts as a sedative to calm the nervous system and has carminative qualities to relieve gas. Additionally, it serves as a stimulant, antispasmodic, expectorant, and astringent.

Soybean (Glycine max) Monograph

1. Tonic

Soybean has a tonic action, supporting overall vitality and energy levels. It helps strengthen the body's systems, particularly the digestive and immune functions. This effect is due to its rich nutrient profile, including proteins, minerals, and phytoestrogens. Regular use can promote balanced health and resilience.

2. Diuretic

Soybean has a diuretic action, promoting the excretion of excess water and electrolytes through urine. This effect is attributed to its saponin content, which increases renal filtration. It supports fluid balance and may aid in conditions involving water retention. However, it should be used cautiously in individuals with kidney disorders.

3. Sedative

Soybean has a sedative action, promoting relaxation and reducing anxiety. It contains compounds like saponins and isoflavones that may influence the central nervous system. This effect is often observed in traditional herbal formulations using soybean. It is commonly used to support restful sleep and calmness.

4. Anti-inflammatory

Soybean has an anti-inflammatory action, primarily due to its isoflavones and saponins. These compounds inhibit pro-inflammatory cytokines and reduce oxidative stress. This effect supports its use in managing conditions like arthritis and inflammatory skin disorders.

5. Bitter

Soybean has a bitter action, which helps to clear heat and detoxify the body. It is particularly effective in reducing internal dampness and fire, supporting digestion and liver function. This property makes it useful in treating conditions like inflammation and digestive disorders.

6. Carminative

Soybean has a carminative action, meaning it helps relieve gas and bloating in the digestive system. It promotes the expulsion of trapped air, reducing discomfort and aiding digestion. This effect is due to its mild laxative properties and ability to stimulate gastrointestinal motility.

7. Stimulant

Soybean has a stimulant action, enhancing metabolic activity and energy levels. It stimulates the central nervous system, promoting alertness and mental clarity. This effect is due to its high content of amino acids and compounds like lecithin. It is commonly used in traditional medicine to combat fatigue and improve vitality.

8. Antispasmodic

Soybean has an antispasmodic action, which helps relieve muscle spasms and cramps by relaxing smooth muscle tissue. This effect is attributed to its content of compounds like saponins and isoflavones, which may influence nerve-muscle interactions. It is commonly used in herbal medicine to address gastrointestinal spasms and menstrual cramps.

9. Expectorant

Soybean has an expectorant action, aiding in the removal of mucus from the respiratory tract. It stimulates the secretion of bronchial fluids, making it easier to cough up phlegm. This property is particularly beneficial in treating coughs and respiratory conditions characterized by excessive mucus production.

10. Astringent

Soybean has an astringent action, which helps to reduce secretions and tighten tissues. This property is useful in treating conditions like diarrhea and excessive sweating. It works by constricting blood vessels and mucous membranes. The astringent effect is due to compounds like tannins present in soybean.
