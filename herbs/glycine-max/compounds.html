Soybean (Glycine max) Compounds

Soybean contains various active compounds, including flavonoids like isoflavones, which have antioxidant properties. Saponins and saponin-like compounds contribute to its anti-inflammatory effects. Phenolic acids and glycosides also play roles in reducing oxidative stress. These compounds collectively support the therapeutic uses of soybean in herbal medicine.

Soybean (Glycine max) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Soybean (Glycine max). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant properties and are believed to support cardiovascular health and reduce inflammation. These compounds are also associated with potential anti-cancer effects and improved metabolic function.

2. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Soybean (Glycine max). It exhibits surfactant properties, aiding in the emulsification of fats and enhancing nutrient absorption. Saponin also has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects, contributing to the therapeutic potential of soybean in herbal medicine.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in Soybean (Glycine max). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and cardiovascular protective effects.

4. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Soybean (Glycine max). It contributes to the plant's pharmacological properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Glycosides in soybean are often associated with health benefits such as supporting cardiovascular function and immune response.

5. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Soybean (Glycine max). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins in soybean may support digestive health and are used in traditional herbal medicine for their therapeutic benefits.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a bioactive compound found in Soybean (Glycine max). It contributes to the plant's pharmacological properties, including anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory effects. Coumarin is used in herbal medicine to support cardiovascular health and reduce inflammation. Its presence in soybean highlights the plant's therapeutic potential.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Soybean (Glycine max) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. They support immune function and may aid in reducing oxidative stress. Terpenoids are part of the complex phytochemical profile of soybean.

8. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Soybean (Glycine max). It contributes to the plant's pharmacological properties, though soybeans are more renowned for their isoflavones. Alkaloids in soybeans may have mild sedative and antispasmodic effects, supporting traditional uses in herbal medicine.

9. Essential oil

Essential oil from Soybean (Glycine max) contains volatile compounds like soybean oil and various terpenes. These compounds contribute to the plant's aromatic properties and may have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. They are often extracted through steam distillation and used in herbal formulations for their therapeutic potential.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Soybean (Glycine max). It acts as an antioxidant, helping to neutralize free radicals in the body. This compound contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, supporting cardiovascular health and reducing inflammation.
