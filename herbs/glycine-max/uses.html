Soybean (Glycine max) Uses

Soybean is used to manage high blood pressure and hypertension by promoting healthy cardiovascular function. It helps lower high cholesterol and supports diabetes management through its impact on blood sugar levels. Soybean may also alleviate inflammation and reduce allergic reactions due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties. It can aid in relieving constipation by increasing dietary fiber intake.

Soybean (Glycine max) Monograph

1. High blood pressure

Soybean contains compounds like isoflavones and peptides that help relax blood vessels and reduce blood pressure. It is rich in potassium, which balances sodium levels in the body. Regular consumption of soy products may support cardiovascular health and aid in managing hypertension.

2. Hypertension

Soybean contains compounds like isoflavones and peptides that help lower blood pressure by improving vascular function and reducing oxidative stress. It may also promote nitric oxide production, which relaxes blood vessels. Regular consumption of soy-based foods has been linked to modest reductions in hypertension in some studies.

3. High cholesterol

Soybean contains compounds like isoflavones and soy saponins that help lower LDL cholesterol. These compounds inhibit cholesterol absorption and reduce liver enzyme activity. Regular consumption of soy products can support heart health by improving lipid profiles.

4. Diabetes

Soybean contains compounds like isoflavones and peptides that may improve insulin sensitivity and regulate blood sugar levels. Studies suggest soy protein can help manage diabetes by reducing glucose absorption and enhancing pancreatic function. It is often recommended as a dietary supplement in traditional herbal medicine for its mild hypoglycemic effects.

5. Diabetes mellitus

Soybean has been used in traditional herbal medicine to support blood sugar regulation in diabetes mellitus. It contains bioactive compounds like isoflavones and peptides that may enhance insulin sensitivity and reduce glucose absorption. Soy protein and fiber also contribute to improved glycemic control. Regular consumption may help manage symptoms, though it should complement, not replace, medical treatment.

6. Hormonal imbalance

Soybean contains phytoestrogens, which can help regulate hormonal imbalances by mimicking estrogen in the body. These compounds may alleviate symptoms of menopause, such as hot flashes, and support hormonal balance in both men and women. They also influence thyroid function and may reduce the risk of hormone-related disorders when consumed as part of a balanced diet.

7. Inflammation

Soybean contains compounds like isoflavones and saponins that exhibit anti-inflammatory properties. These components help reduce inflammation by modulating immune responses and inhibiting pro-inflammatory cytokines. Soybean oil and extracts are used in traditional herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms of inflammatory conditions. It is often recommended as a natural remedy for joint pain and skin inflammation.

8. Allergies

Soybean has been used in traditional herbal medicine to alleviate allergy symptoms due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties. It contains compounds like isoflavones and saponins that may help reduce immune system overreaction. However, soy itself can also be a common allergen, so its use should be carefully considered.

9. Allergic reactions

Soybean can trigger allergic reactions in sensitive individuals, causing symptoms like hives, itching, swelling, and in severe cases, anaphylaxis. These reactions are often due to proteins in soy, and cross-reactivity with other legumes is common. Avoidance and proper labeling are key for managing soy allergies.

10. Constipation

Soybean is used in herbal medicine to relieve constipation due to its high fiber content and bulk-forming properties. It helps soften stool and promote regular bowel movements. It is often prepared as a powder or decoction. Regular consumption can support digestive health and prevent bowel issues.
