Rhodiola gelida belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhodiola gelida has a thick, fleshy rhizome that grows horizontally underground and produces small, succulent leaves arranged in dense basal rosettes. Each flower contains several stamens and a central ovary that develops into a small, dry fruit.
