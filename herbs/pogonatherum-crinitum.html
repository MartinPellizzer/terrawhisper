Pogonatherum crinitum

What's the taxonomical classification of Pogonatherum crinitum?

Pogonatherum crinitum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its classification through the order Poales, it is placed in the family Poaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by the genus Pogonatherum, with its specific designation being the species crinitum.

    Pogonatherum crinitum is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical wash depending on the specific medicinal requirement. Some traditional practices also involve crushing the fresh plant material to create a poultice for immediate topical application. These various methods ensure that the botanical properties are effectively utilized for their intended therapeutic purposes.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Pogonatherum crinitum can cause severe skin irritation, contact dermatitis, and localized swelling upon direct physical contact with the plant. The chemical compounds present in its foliage react with human skin to produce redness and intense itching. Some individuals may also experience respiratory distress or allergic reactions if the airborne particles from its pollen are inhaled. Prolonged exposure to the sap can lead to blistering or more persistent dermatological inflammation. It is essential to wear protective gear when handling this species to mitigate these physiological risks.
