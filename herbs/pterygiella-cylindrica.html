Pterygiella cylindrica

What's the taxonomical classification of Pterygiella cylindrica?

Pterygiella cylindrica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy down through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed under the order Lamiales. This species is a member of the family Orobanchaceae, specifically falling within the genus Pterygiella, and is identified at the species level as cylindrica.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Pterygella cylindrica can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and localized swelling if the plant's juices come into direct contact with human skin. Some individuals may experience itching or a burning sensation in the areas where the plant was handled. If the plant is ingested, it may lead to gastrointestinal distress such as nausea or stomach cramps. While specific toxicological profiles are limited, caution is advised regarding any systemic reactions from accidental exposure. Always wash thoroughly with soap and water after any physical contact to mitigate these risks.
