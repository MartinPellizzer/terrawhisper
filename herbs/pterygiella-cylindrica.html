Pterygiella cylindrica

What's the taxonomical classification of Pterygiella cylindrica?

Pterygiella cylindrica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy down through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed under the order Lamiales. This species is a member of the family Orobanchaceae, specifically falling within the genus Pterygiella, and is identified at the species level as cylindrica.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat gastrointestinal issues such as stomach aches, indigestion, and diarrhea. It is also traditionally applied to manage skin irritations and various minor inflammatory conditions. Some practitioners use it to address respiratory discomfort through its perceived medicinal properties. The specific applications often depend on the local traditional knowledge of the regions where it grows. These therapeutic uses are rooted in the historical ethnobotanical practices of specific indigenous populations.
