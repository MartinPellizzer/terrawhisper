Elsholtzia luteola

What's the taxonomical classification of Elsholtzia luteola?

Elsholtzia luteola belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its progression through the order Lamiales, it is placed in the family Lamiaceae. Finally, its taxonomic position is completed within the genus Elsholtzia, specifically as the species luteola.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Elsholtzia luteola has a medium-sized deciduous tree habit characterized by a spreading crown, pinnately compound leaves with 7 to 15 leaflets, and small, yellowish, inconspicuous flowers arranged in drooping panicles.

The leaflets are typically ovate or elliptical with serrated margins and a smooth, somewhat leathery texture. The bark transitions from a smooth, grayish surface in younger specimens to a deeply fissured and furrowed texture as the tree matures. During the autumn season, the foliage undergoes a distinct color change to vibrant shades of yellow before falling.

Small, winged samaras serve as the fruit, which are dispersed by the wind to facilitate reproduction.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Elsholtzia luteola can cause allergic skin reactions such as contact dermatitis or localized itching in individuals sensitive to its pollen or sap. While there is no extensive clinical data on its systemic toxicity, some people may experience respiratory irritation if they inhale significant amounts of its airborne pollen during flowering seasons. Skin contact with the leaves or bark might trigger redness or mild swelling in those with hypersensitivity. It is generally considered a non-toxic ornamental tree, but caution is advised for those with known plant allergies. Always consult a medical professional if an adverse reaction occurs after exposure.
