Elsholtzia luteola

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Elsholtzia luteola has a medium-sized deciduous tree habit characterized by a spreading crown, pinnately compound leaves with 7 to 15 leaflets, and small, yellowish, inconspicuous flowers arranged in drooping panicles. The leaflets are typically ovate or elliptical with serrated margins and a smooth, somewhat leathery texture. The bark transitions from a smooth, grayish surface in younger specimens to a deeply fissured and furrowed texture as the tree matures. During the autumn season, the foliage undergoes a distinct color change to vibrant shades of yellow before falling. Small, winged samaras serve as the fruit, which are dispersed by the wind to facilitate reproduction.
