Machilus pauhoi

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Machilus pauhoi has simple, alternate, elliptic to oblong leaves that are typically 5 to 15 centimeters long with entire margins and an acute apex. The foliage is characterized by a coriaceous texture and a distinctively shiny upper surface. New growth often displays a subtle reddish or bronze tint before maturing into a deep green color. The venation is prominent, with secondary veins arching towards the leaf tip in a pinnate pattern. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in axillary clusters, eventually developing into small, fleshy drupes.
