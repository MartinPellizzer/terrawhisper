Machilus pauhoi

What's the taxonomical classification of Machilus pauhoi?

Machilus pauhoi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Laurales and the family Lauraceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Machilus, and its unique species designation, pauhoi.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Machilus pauhoi has simple, alternate, elliptic to oblong leaves that are typically 5 to 15 centimeters long with entire margins and an acute apex.

The foliage is characterized by a coriaceous texture and a distinctively shiny upper surface. New growth often displays a subtle reddish or bronze tint before maturing into a deep green color. The venation is prominent, with secondary veins arching towards the leaf tip in a pinnate pattern.

Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in axillary clusters, eventually developing into small, fleshy drupes.
