Comarum palustre

What's the taxonomical classification of Comarum palustre?

Comarum palustre belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Rosales and is a member of the family Rosaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Comarum, and its species name, palustre.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Comarum palustre contains high concentrations of hydrolyzable tannins, phenolic acids, and various flavonoids that provide its primary pharmacological properties. These chemical constituents contribute to significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities within biological systems. The presence of specific tannins allows the plant to act as an astringent, which is useful for treating skin irritations and mucosal membranes. Additionally, some studies suggest that its phytochemical profile may possess mild antimicrobial effects. These complex organic compounds make the plant a subject of interest for traditional and modern medicinal research.

What are the therapeutic actions of Comarum palustre?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat inflammation, skin irritation, and various digestive disorders. Its tannins provide astringent properties that help soothe mucous membranes and reduce swelling. People sometimes apply external preparations to manage minor wounds or skin abrasions. Some traditional practices involve using it to alleviate symptoms related to intestinal discomfort. The plant's chemical components are primarily utilized for their ability to tighten tissues and calm irritation.
