What to know about Eschscholzia californica (california poppy) before using it medicinally

California poppy, scientifically known as Eschscholzia californica, is a revered herb that boasts an impressive array of health properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant benefits that have been shown to alleviate anxiety, improve sleep quality, and even reduce chronic pain.

In addition to its medicinal uses, California poppy is also prized for its delicate flavor and versatility in the kitchen, often being used to add a subtle sweetness to salads, teas, and baked goods. As a flowering plant, California poppy is also highly valued among horticulturists for its vibrant orange blooms that burst forth in late spring, attracting pollinators and adding a pop of color to any garden. From a botanical perspective, California poppy is classified as a member of the Papaveraceae family and is characterized by its thin, papery leaves and cup-shaped flowers.

With a rich history dating back thousands of years, California poppy has been revered by indigenous cultures and early settlers alike for its medicinal and spiritual properties, earning it a special place in the hearts and gardens of many enthusiasts.
