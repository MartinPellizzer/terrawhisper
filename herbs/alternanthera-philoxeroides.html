Alternanthera philoxeroides

What's the taxonomical classification of Alternanthera philoxeroides?

Alternanthera philoxeroides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized under the order Caryophyllales and is a member of the family Amaranthaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Alternanthera and the species philoxeroides.

Alternanthera philoxeroides contains no documented medicinal parts because it is not recognized as a therapeutic plant in traditional or modern pharmacopeias.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Alternanthera philoxeroides can cause skin irritation and allergic reactions in humans through direct contact with its stems or leaves. While not documented as a lethal poison, the plant contains compounds that may trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Contact often results in redness, itching, or a mild rash on the affected area of the skin. Because it is an invasive species, physical handling can also lead to accidental spreading of the plant across different environments. It is best to wear protective gloves when clearing or moving this vegetation to avoid these dermatological issues.
