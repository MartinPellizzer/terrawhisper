What's the taxonomical classification of Populus cathayana?

Populus cathayana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Malpighiales and into the family Salicaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Populus, and its unique species designation, cathayana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Populus cathayana has an upright, deciduous growth habit characterized by an elongated trunk, lanceolate leaves with serrated margins, and slender, pendulous catkins. The foliage is typically bright green during the growing season and transitions to yellow in the autumn. Its bark is relatively smooth when young but develops shallow longitudinal fissures as the tree matures. The reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that hang in drooping clusters. These morphological features are consistent with other members of the Salicaceae family.
