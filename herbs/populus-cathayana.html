Populus cathayana

What's the taxonomical classification of Populus cathayana?

Populus cathayana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Malpighiales and into the family Salicaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Populus, and its unique species designation, cathayana.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Populus cathayana contains salicin, a phenolic glycoside that serves as a natural precursor to salicylic acid used for reducing inflammation and pain. This primary compound is extracted from the bark to alleviate symptoms of fever and various musculoskeletal aches. Beyond salicin, the plant's chemical profile includes diverse flavonoids and tannins that provide antioxidant properties. These secondary metabolites contribute to the plant's overall ability to protect cells from oxidative stress. Researchers study these specific phytochemicals to better understand their therapeutic potential in modern pharmacology.

What are the therapeutic actions of Populus cathayana?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various inflammatory conditions, skin infections, digestive ailments, and respiratory issues. Its bioactive compounds are frequently utilized to soothe skin irritations like rashes or minor wounds. When applied or ingested in traditional practices, it helps alleviate symptoms of gastrointestinal discomfort. Some practitioners also employ its properties to mitigate the severity of coughs and other lung-related irritations. The medicinal versatility of the species stems from its specific phytochemical profile.
