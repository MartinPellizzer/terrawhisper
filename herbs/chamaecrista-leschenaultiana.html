Chamaecrista leschenaultiana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Chamaecrista leschenaultiana has an erect, herbaceous or subshrubby growth habit characterized by pinnately compound leaves with numerous small, oblong leaflets and bright yellow, actinomorphic flowers. The stems are typically slender and can exhibit varying degrees of pubescence depending on the specific environmental conditions. Each leaf consists of a central rachis supporting pairs of leaflets that are arranged symmetrically along its length. The inflorescence produces small, five-petaled blossoms that emerge from the leaf axils. Following pollination, the plant develops elongated, dehiscent legume pods that contain small, dark seeds.
