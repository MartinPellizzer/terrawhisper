Rubus mesogaeus

What's the taxonomical classification of Rubus mesogaeus?

Rubus mesogaeus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic descent, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It further falls under the order Rosales and the family Rosaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Rubus, with its specific designation being the species mesogaeus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rubus mesogaeus has woody, arching stems that are densely covered in sharp, curved prickles and feature small, serrated leaves arranged in a trifoliate or quinquefoliate pattern. The plant typically produces small, white flowers with five petals that emerge from the axils of the foliage. Following pollination, the plant develops aggregate fruits that transition from green to a deep red or black color when fully ripe. The leaves possess distinctively serrated margins and a texture that can vary from slightly hairy to relatively smooth depending on the specific specimen. These morphological traits are characteristic of many species within the Rubus genus, allowing the plant to thrive in its natural habitat.
