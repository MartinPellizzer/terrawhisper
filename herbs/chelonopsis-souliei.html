Chelonopsis souliei

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Chelonopsis souliei has a woody, climbing stem structure characterized by specialized tendrils used for attachment to host plants. The leaves are arranged in an opposite pattern and possess an elliptical shape with distinctively serrated margins. Each leaf blade features prominent venation that extends from a central midrib toward the outer edges. The plant also produces small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from axillary buds during its blooming period. These floral structures eventually develop into small, fleshy fruits that serve as primary reproductive units.

      What are the herbal preparations of Chelonopsis souliei?

      Chelonopsis souliei is prepared as a decoction, a concentrated infusion, or a topical poultice using the dried leaves and crushed stems. Practitioners typically simmer the plant material in water for extended periods to extract its potent bioactive compounds. For internal use, the resulting liquid is often strained and consumed in small, measured doses.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Chelonopsis souliei can cause severe dermatological irritation, localized swelling, and systemic nausea if ingested. Contact with the plant's sap often leads to intense redness and blistering on the skin. These inflammatory reactions can persist for several days if the area is not immediately washed with neutral soap. If the plant is consumed, users may experience significant gastrointestinal distress and dizziness. It is essential to avoid all direct contact to prevent these adverse physiological responses.
