Butea (Butea monosperma) Compounds

The primary compounds of Butea monosperma include tannins, which have astringent properties, and flavonoids, known for their antioxidant effects. Saponins and saponin-related compounds contribute to its medicinal value, while phenolic acids and coumarin provide anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial benefits. Glycosides, terpenoids, and alkaloids further enhance its therapeutic potential, and essential oils and anthocyanins contribute to its aromatic and coloring properties.

Butea (Butea monosperma) Monograph

1. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Butea monosperma, known for its astringent properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating digestive issues and skin conditions. Tannins also provide antioxidant and antimicrobial benefits, supporting its role in herbal medicine.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Butea monosperma contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. They support the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments. Flavonoids enhance the therapeutic value of Butea monosperma in herbal medicine.

3. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Butea monosperma. It exhibits antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, contributing to the plant's traditional use in treating skin infections and respiratory conditions. Saponin's ability to disrupt cell membranes enhances its therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Butea monosperma, contributing to its medicinal properties. It exhibits antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting the plant's traditional use in treating skin conditions and digestive issues. This compound plays a crucial role in the therapeutic potential of Butea monosperma.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Butea monosperma. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. Coumarin is also known for its role in the plant's distinctive fragrance and potential therapeutic applications.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Butea monosperma. It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. This compound is often extracted for use in traditional herbal remedies. Glycoside helps support the therapeutic applications of Butea monosperma in herbal medicine.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are key active compounds in Butea monosperma, contributing to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, supporting the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments. They also play a role in the plant's fragrance and ecological interactions.

8. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a key active compound found in Butea monosperma. It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Alkaloids in Butea monosperma are used in traditional herbal medicine to treat various ailments. Their presence highlights the plant's significance in Ayurvedic and indigenous healing practices.

9. Essential oil

Essential oil from Butea monosperma contains volatile compounds like limonene and α-pinene. These oils are extracted through steam distillation and are used in traditional medicine for their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. They contribute to the plant's reputation in treating respiratory and skin conditions.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound found in Butea monosperma. It contributes to the plant's vibrant red and purple hues and has antioxidant properties. This compound is valued in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and cardiovascular support.
