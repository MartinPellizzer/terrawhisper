Botanical Identification of Butea (Butea monosperma)

To identify Butea (Butea monosperma) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Butea monosperma belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Fabales, order Fabales, family Fabaceae, genus Butea, and species Butea monosperma. It is a flowering plant in the legume family, commonly known as the flame of the forest.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of the plant is Butea monosperma, first described by the botanist J.C. Buckland in 1821. It is also known by several synonyms, including Diocelaea monosperma and Chamaelirium monosperma. Common names include flame of the forest and palash, while regional names vary across India and Southeast Asia. Historically, it has been referred to by names in Sanskrit and ancient texts.

Morphological Characteristics

Butea monosperma is a shrub or small tree with a deep taproot system. Its stems are woody and often branched, while leaves are trifoliate with ovate leaflets. The plant produces panicle inflorescences with bright orange-red flowers. The fruit is a legume containing a single seed, which is characteristic of the genus.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Butea monosperma can be distinguished by its distinctive red flowers, which bloom in racemes, and its compound leaves with 10-20 pairs of leaflets. The bark is thick and flaky, and the plant has a characteristic aromatic scent. These features help differentiate it from similar species like Butea frondosa.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Butea monosperma is native to South and Southeast Asia, including India, Nepal, and Bangladesh. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It prefers well-drained sandy or loamy soils. It is typically found at altitudes ranging from 500 to 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Butea monosperma blooms from April to June, producing bright orange-red flowers. Seed formation occurs from July to September. Harvesting is typically done during the dry season, from October to November, when the seeds are mature and ready for collection.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Butea monosperma is closely related to species within the genus Butea, which belongs to the family Fabaceae. It shares key family traits such as compound leaves and pod fruits, distinguishing it from other genera. Its phylogenetic placement highlights its evolutionary ties to leguminous plants with similar morphological and chemical characteristics.
