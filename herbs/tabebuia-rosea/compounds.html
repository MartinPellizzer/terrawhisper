Pink Trumpet Tree (Tabebuia rosea) Compounds

The Pink Trumpet Tree contains several primary active compounds, including flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and tannins, known for their astringent effects. Phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory benefits, while terpenoids and coumarin provide antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory actions. Saponins and glycosides support detoxification and immune function, and essential oils contribute to its aromatic and therapeutic uses.

Pink Trumpet Tree (Tabebuia rosea) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in the Pink Trumpet Tree (Tabebuia rosea) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, supporting the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and skin conditions. Tannin in the Pink Trumpet Tree (Tabebuia rosea) is a key active compound known for its astringent properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating inflammation and skin conditions. Tannins also aid in wound healing and may support antimicrobial effects in herbal remedies.
