Lygodium microphyllum

What's the taxonomical classification of Lygodium microphyllum?

Lygodium microphyllum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae, this plant is situated under the order Schizaeales. It is further categorized into the family Schizaeaceae, which contains the genus Lygodium. Finally, the specific identification of this organism is completed by its species name, microphyllum.

What are the therapeutic actions of Lygodium microphyllum?

This plant is used to treat ailments involving the digestive system, skin irritations, and respiratory issues. Traditional practitioners often employ its extracts to soothe stomach discomfort and intestinal irregularities. When applied topically, it can help alleviate various skin conditions and minor wounds. Some cultures also utilize the plant to manage symptoms related to coughs or breathing difficulties. These medicinal applications are rooted in long-standing ethnobotanical practices within specific regions.

What are the herbal preparations of Lygodium microphyllum?

Lygodium microphyllum is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice for medicinal use. The plant parts are typically boiled in water to extract active alkaloids and tannins for oral consumption. In some traditional practices, the dried leaves are steeped in hot water to create a tea. For external ailments, the crushed fresh plant material is applied directly to the skin as a warm compress. These varied methods allow different chemical constituents of the fern to be utilized depending on the intended therapeutic effect.

Ingestion of various fern species often leads to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal cramping. Caution is advised when handling the plant to prevent accidental contact with mucous membranes or eyes.
