What's the taxonomical classification of Setaria faberi?

Setaria faberi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical structure, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized under the order Poales and within the family Poaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identification is defined by the genus Setaria and the species faberi.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Setaria faberi has an erect, annual growth habit characterized by slender, cylindrical stems that typically reach heights of 20 to 100 centimeters. The leaves are linear with a prominent midvein and possess long, tapering ligules at the junction of the blade and sheath. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, bristly panicle containing numerous spikelets arranged along the branches. Each spikelet is enclosed by several fine, spreading bristles that give the seed head a fuzzy appearance. The plant also develops a fibrous root system that helps it anchor firmly in disturbed soils.
