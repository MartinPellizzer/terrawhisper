Chinese Wolfberry (Lycium Glaucum)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Lycium glaucum.

Chinese wolfberry, scientifically known as Lycium glaucum, is a medicinal herb native to China and widely used in traditional Chinese medicine for its adaptogenic and nourishing properties. It is valued for its ability to enhance vitality, improve vision, and support immune function, making it a popular ingredient in herbal remedies for fatigue and stress. Historically, Chinese wolfberry has been used in Chinese culture for centuries to promote longevity and vitality, often consumed as a tonic during the winter months. In modern wellness practices, it is incorporated into supplements and functional foods due to its high antioxidant content and potential anti-aging benefits. Notably, it contains unique compounds such as beta-carotene and zeaxanthin, which contribute to its distinctive orange-red color and its reputation as a "red nutrient" in traditional medicine.
