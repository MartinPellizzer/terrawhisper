10 Possible Side Effects Of Curcuma Longa (Turmeric)

Curcuma longa has some side effects when used improperly, such as stomach upset, diarrhea, and nausea.

These side effects are often caused by consuming high doses of the herb, particularly when taken in supplement form. In severe cases, they can worsen people's lives, causing dehydration, electrolyte imbalances, and even kidney stones.

Furthermore, individuals with pre-existing gastrointestinal conditions, such as ulcers or inflammatory bowel disease, may experience exacerbated symptoms.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Curcuma longa if used imporperly.

Curcuma longa causes allergic reactions in some individuals due to its molecular structure, which contains a compound called curcumin.

This compound is responsible for the spice's vibrant yellow color and potent antioxidant properties, but it can also trigger an immune response in sensitive people, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and rashes.

The exact mechanism of this allergic reaction is not fully understood, but it may be related to the similarity between curcumin and certain proteins found in the body.
