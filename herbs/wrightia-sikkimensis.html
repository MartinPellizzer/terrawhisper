Wrightia sikkimensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Wrightia sikkimensis?

Wrightia sikkimensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Gentianales. This plant is further categorized into the family Apocynaceae, falling under the genus Wrightia, with its specific designation being the species sikkimensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Wrightia sikkimensis has erect, woody stems with smooth bark and opposite, elliptical to oblong leaves that possess entire margins and acute tips. The foliage is typically dark green and glossy, arranged in a decussate pattern along the branches. Small, fragrant, white flowers emerge from the axils in clusters, displaying a salverform shape with a long corolla tube. The plant develops small, fleshy berries that transition in color as they reach maturity. These morphological features collectively distinguish this species within the Apocynaceae family.
