Wrightia sikkimensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Wrightia sikkimensis?

Wrightia sikkimensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Gentianales. This plant is further categorized into the family Apocynaceae, falling under the genus Wrightia, with its specific designation being the species sikkimensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Wrightia sikkimensis has erect, woody stems with smooth bark and opposite, elliptical to oblong leaves that possess entire margins and acute tips.

The foliage is typically dark green and glossy, arranged in a decussate pattern along the branches. Small, fragrant, white flowers emerge from the axils in clusters, displaying a salverform shape with a long corolla tube. The plant develops small, fleshy berries that transition in color as they reach maturity.

These morphological features collectively distinguish this species within the Apocynaceae family.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Wrightia sikkimensis contains medicinal properties primarily utilized through its leaves, stems, and roots. Local communities often harvest the leaves to prepare decoctions or poultices for treating various skin ailments and inflammations.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Wrightia sikkimensis can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or gastrointestinal distress if its parts are handled or ingested improperly. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, many plants in the Apocynaceae family contain bioactive alkaloids that may be toxic in high doses. Direct contact with the sap may lead to contact dermatitis or redness in sensitive individuals. If consumed accidentally, it could potentially trigger nausea or abdominal discomfort. Careful handling is recommended to avoid unintended physiological responses.
