Botanical Identification of Rugosa Rose (Rosa rugosa)

To identify Rugosa Rose (Rosa rugosa) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Rugosa Rose (Rosa rugosa) belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Rosales, family Rosaceae, genus Rosa, and species Rosa rugosa. These classifications place it within the broader group of flowering plants and highlight its relationship to other roses and related species.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Rosa rugosa is the scientific name of the Rugosa Rose, first described by Linnaeus in 1753. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is widely recognized in botanical literature. Synonyms include Rosa palustris and Rosa rugosa subsp. rugosa, reflecting its historical classification and regional variations.

Morphological Characteristics

Rosa rugosa is a shrub with a dense, bushy growth habit. It has a strong, extensive root system that allows it to thrive in coastal and wetland environments. The stems are thorny, reddish-brown, and often branched. Leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and covered in fine, velvety hairs. The inflorescence consists of solitary flowers at the ends of branches. Flowers are pink to red, with five petals and numerous stamens. The fruit is a pinkish-red, aggregate fruit containing numerous achenes, which are the seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Rosa rugosa can be distinguished by its rough, reddish stems, compound leaves with serrated edges, and pale pink to white flowers. It has a strong, distinctive scent and produces red-orange hips. These features help differentiate it from other rose species with smoother stems or different flower colors.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Rosa rugosa is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, Japan, and Korea. It thrives in temperate climates with cool summers and cold winters. It prefers well-drained sandy or loamy soils and is commonly found at low to moderate altitudes, typically below 1,000 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Rosa rugosa blooms in late spring to early summer, typically from May to July, depending on the climate. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early fall, around August to September. For herbal identification, the most useful time to observe key features is during the flowering period, which is also when the plant is most identifiable.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Rosa rugosa belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rosa species. It shares key characteristics with its genus, such as thorny stems and fragrant flowers. Its phylogenetic placement highlights its evolutionary ties to other rose species within the genus Rosa.
