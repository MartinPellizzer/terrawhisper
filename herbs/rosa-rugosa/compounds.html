Rugosa Rose (Rosa rugosa) Compounds

Rosa rugosa contains several key active compounds, including flavonoids, which act as antioxidants. Tannins and phenolic acids contribute to its astringent properties. Essential oils provide aromatic and antimicrobial benefits, while coumarin and glycosides may support anti-inflammatory effects. Terpenoids, saponins, anthocyanins, and alkaloids also play roles in its medicinal properties.

Rugosa Rose (Rosa rugosa) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Rugosa Rose (Rosa rugosa) are bioactive compounds known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. They contribute to the plant's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine, supporting skin health and immune function. These compounds are naturally present in the flowers, leaves, and fruits of the plant.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Rugosa Rose (Rosa rugosa). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins in Rugosa Rose are primarily found in the leaves and fruits, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and skin health.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in Rugosa Rose (Rosa rugosa). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress in the body. This compound supports the plant's traditional use in promoting skin health and reducing inflammation.

4. Essential oil

The essential oil of Rugosa Rose (Rosa rugosa) contains volatile compounds like geraniol and citronellol, which contribute to its aromatic properties. These compounds are known for their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects, making the oil valuable in herbal medicine for skin treatments and respiratory support.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Rugosa Rose (Rosa rugosa). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating inflammation and digestive issues. Coumarin also has anticoagulant properties, supporting its role in herbal medicine for circulatory health.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Rugosa Rose (Rosa rugosa). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and respiratory ailments. Glycosides are often responsible for the therapeutic effects observed in Rosa rugosa-based remedies.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Rugosa Rose (Rosa rugosa). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds are often responsible for the rose's therapeutic effects in herbal medicine. Terpenoids may support immune function and skin health when derived from Rugosa Rose.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Rugosa Rose (Rosa rugosa). It exhibits antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, contributing to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and skin conditions. Saponin also aids in enhancing the bioavailability of other compounds in the rose, supporting its overall therapeutic profile.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound found in Rugosa Rose (Rosa rugosa). It contributes to the plant's vibrant flower color and possesses antioxidant properties that support immune function and skin health. This compound is widely used in herbal medicine for its therapeutic benefits.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Rugosa Rose (Rosa rugosa). It contributes to the plant's pharmacological properties, though its role in traditional herbal medicine is less emphasized compared to other constituents like flavonoids and phenolic acids. Alkaloids may support the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
