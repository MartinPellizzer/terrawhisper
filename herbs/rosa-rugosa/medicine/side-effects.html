10 Possible Side Effects Of Rosa Rugosa (Beach Rose)

Rosa rugosa has some side effects when used improperly, such as skin irritation and allergic reactions in sensitive individuals.

These side effects are caused by the presence of toxic compounds in the plant, which can be released when the herb is ingested or applied topically in large amounts.

If left untreated, these side effects can worsen, leading to painful rashes, swelling, and even respiratory problems, severely impacting daily life and work routines.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Rosa rugosa if used imporperly.

1. Increases anxiety levels

Rosa rugosa causes allergic reactions due to its high content of fragrances and essential oils, which can trigger an immune response in some individuals.

The allergenic properties are primarily attributed to the presence of sesquiterpenes and phenolic compounds, commonly found in the plant's flowers, leaves, and stems.

These chemicals can bind to IgE antibodies, leading to the release of histamine and other chemical mediators, resulting in symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling.

These compounds can cause an allergic reaction or contact dermatitis in some individuals, leading to redness, itching, swelling, and blistering.

Additionally, the plant's sap and leaves may contain irritating chemicals that can cause skin discomfort when coming into contact with them.
