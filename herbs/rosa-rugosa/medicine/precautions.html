10 Precautions To Take When Using Rosa Rugosa (Beach Rose)

Rosa rugosa has some precautions to consider before using it medicinally, such as consulting with a healthcare professional due to potential allergic reactions and interactions with other medications.

Failure to do so may result in side effects like skin irritation, digestive issues, or increased bleeding risk.

It's also essential to monitor blood pressure and liver function, as excessive use can cause hypertensive crises or liver damage.

This article explains in details the 10 most important precautions to take when using Rosa rugosa medicinally.

1. Store in cool dark place

When using Rosa rugosa medicinally, it's important to avoid during pregnancy.

This is because the plant contains salicylic acid, a compound similar to aspirin, which can stimulate the uterus and potentially induce labor or miscarriage.

Additionally, there is limited research on the safety of Rosa rugosa for use during pregnancy, making it crucial to err on the side of caution and consult with a healthcare provider before using any medicinal products derived from this plant while pregnant.
