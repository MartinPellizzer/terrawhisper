What is Beyeria cinerea?

Beyeria cinerea is an Australian shrub that belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae.

It has a compact and dense growth habit, typically reaching heights of 0.5-2 meters. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive shape, with a petiole 1-3 mm long and an elliptic to lanceolate lamina 10-25 mm long. The inflorescences are axillary, unisexual, and consist of a pair of staminate and pistillate flowers, with the staminate flowers being smaller and more numerous.

The fruit is a tricarpellary, trilobed capsule, 6-8 mm in diameter, containing 3 seeds.
