What's the taxonomical classification of Primula faberi?

Primula faberi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down its taxonomic hierarchy, it is part of the order Ericales and the family Primulaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Primula, with its specific designation being the species faberi.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Primula faberi has a basal rosette of oblong to elliptical leaves that are fleshy, slightly hairy, and feature crenate margins. The leaf surfaces often display a subtle texture due to fine pubescence covering the lamina and petioles. From the center of this rosette, long-stalked flowers emerge on leafless scapes. Each individual flower possesses five petals that are typically yellow and fused at the base into a narrow tube. The overall structure is compact, reflecting its adaptation to specific montane or rocky environments.
