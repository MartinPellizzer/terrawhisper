Dendrobium gibsonii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dendrobium gibsonii has sympodial pseudobulbs that are small, compressed, and distinctly fleshy with a yellowish-green color. These thickened stems grow in compact clusters and serve as primary storage organs for the plant. The leaves are relatively few, being small, coriaceous, and elliptic in shape. Its inflorescence features small, fragrant flowers that typically present in shades of pale yellow or cream. The labellum of the flower is often subtly patterned to attract specific pollinators.

      Dendrobium gibsonii is prepared as a decoction through boiling the dried stems in water, as a powder mixed into medicinal soups, or as a tincture soaked in alcohol.
