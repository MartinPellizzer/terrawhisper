Uncaria sessilifructus

What's the taxonomical classification of Uncaria sessilifructus?

Uncaria sessilifructus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Gentianales. This plant is a member of the family Rubiaceae, and its specific biological identification is defined by the genus Uncaria and the species sessilifructus.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Uncaria sessilifructus contains various oxindole alkaloids, quinovic acid glycosides, and polyphenolic compounds that contribute to its pharmacological activity. These specific alkaloids are known for their ability to modulate immune responses and exert neuroprotective effects. The presence of quinovic acid glycosides provides significant anti-inflammatory and antipyretic properties. Additionally, the plant's polyphenolic content offers antioxidant protection by neutralizing free radicals within biological systems. These combined chemical constituents make the species a subject of interest in traditional and modern medicinal research.

What are the therapeutic actions of Uncaria sessilifructus?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat inflammation, digestive issues, and various parasitic infections in traditional medicine. It contains specific alkaloids that help reduce swelling and pain in the joints. Practitioners often utilize its properties to soothe intestinal discomfort and promote better gastrointestinal health. The botanical components also serve as a natural defense against certain types of worms and other internal parasites. These therapeutic applications are rooted in long-standing ethnobotanical practices.
