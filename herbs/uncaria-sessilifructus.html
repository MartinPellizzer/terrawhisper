Uncaria sessilifructus

What's the taxonomical classification of Uncaria sessilifructus?

Uncaria sessilifructus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Gentianales. This plant is a member of the family Rubiaceae, and its specific biological identification is defined by the genus Uncaria and the species sessilifructus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Uncaria sessilifructus has woody climbing stems equipped with recurved, woody thorns that facilitate its upward growth through forest canopies. Small, axillary flowers emerge from the leaf axils, typically appearing in small clusters or solitary arrangements. The fruit consists of a fleshy, indehiscent drupe that serves as the primary method for seed dispersal.
