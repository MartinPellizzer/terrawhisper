What are the pest and diseases tips of Arbutus unedo?

Arbutus unedo, also known as the strawberry tree, is susceptible to several pests and diseases that can affect its growth and productivity.

One common pest that targets this species is the spider mite (Tetranychus urticae), which feeds on the sap of the leaves, causing yellowing or bronzing of the foliage. Additionally, aphids (Aphis spp.). and scales (Coccoidea) can also infest Arbutus unedo, causing damage to new growth and reducing photosynthesis.

As for diseases, fungal pathogens such as root rot (Phytophthora spp. ), leaf spot (Septoria arbuti), and crown gall (Agrobacterium tumefaciens) are major concerns. Root rot can occur when the tree is exposed to excess water or poor drainage, leading to reduced growth and eventual death. Leaf spot and crown gall can be caused by a combination of environmental factors, including high humidity, rainfall, and physical damage to the bark.

If left unchecked, these diseases can significantly impact the health and appearance of Arbutus unedo trees, making it essential for growers and gardeners to implement integrated pest management strategies to prevent infestations and infections.
