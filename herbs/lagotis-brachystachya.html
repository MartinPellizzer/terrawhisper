Lagotis brachystachya

What's the taxonomical classification of Lagotis brachystachya?

Lagotis brachystachya belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Lamiales. This plant is further categorized into the family Plantaginaceae and is identified within the genus Lagotis, specifically representing the species brachystachya.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lagotis brachystachya has small, deeply divided, pinnatifid fronds that typically measure only a few centimeters in length and feature a distinctively short, dense fertile spike.

The blades are characterized by narrow, spreading lobes that lack the complex dissection seen in larger fern species. Its stipes are relatively short and often possess a brownish or reddish hue near the base. The fertile portions are concentrated in a compact, terminal arrangement that gives the plant its specific epithet.

This compact morphology allows the fern to thrive in specialized niches within its natural habitat.
