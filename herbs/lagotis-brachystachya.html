What are the pharmacological activities of Lagotis brachystachya?

This plant has significant pharmacological potential through its ability to act as a xanthine oxidase inhibitor. By suppressing this specific enzyme, the extract can help reduce the production of uric acid in the body. Such an effect is highly relevant for managing conditions like hyperuricemia and gout. Beyond this mechanism, studies suggest the plant may also exhibit various antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These combined bioactive effects highlight its importance in natural drug discovery research.

According to a study published by "Natural product research", two new phenylpropanoid glycosides, lagotiside C and D, were isolated from the whole plant of Lagotis brachystachya Maxim along with 11 known compounds. All isolated components were evaluated for their inhibition on Xanthione Oxidase (XOX) activity in vitro. The results indicated that all the compounds exhibited inhibitory effects on XOD. These inhibitory effects showed an inhibition ratio in the range of 6.35%-83.69%. This suggests that Lagotis brachystachya could be served as an XOD inhibitor.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for managing high uric acid levels and hyperuricemia by inhibiting xanthine oxidase activity. Through this biochemical mechanism, it helps reduce the production of uric acid in the body. It is particularly effective in treating various hyperuricemic conditions that can lead to gout or kidney issues. By targeting the enzyme responsible for purine metabolism, the plant provides a natural way to regulate metabolic waste. This targeted approach offers a potential therapeutic pathway for patients struggling with systemic acid accumulation.

According to a study published by "Inflammopharmacology", Lagotis brachystachya is used to address hyperuricemia. The plant contains active components, specifically luteolin, luteoloside, and apiigenin, which work to maintain uric acid homeostasis. These components attenuate hyperuricemia by decreasing the production and increasing the excretion of uric acid. In studies involving uric acid-treated HK-2 cells and potassium oxonate (PO)-induced mice, the components reversed the reduction of organic anion transporter 1 (OAT1) levels and attenuated the elevation of urafe transporter 1 (URAT1) and glucose transporter 9 (GLUT9) levels. Furthermore, all active components improved the kidney morphology in hyperuricemic mice by inhibiting the TLR4/MyD88/NLRP3 signaling pathway.
