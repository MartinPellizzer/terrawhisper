Balanophora polyandra

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Balanophora polyandra has a parasitic, holoparasitic morphology characterized by a lack of chlorophyll, scale-like leaves, and a specialized root system known as a haustorium that penetrates host plants. The plant body consists of fleshy, brownish or yellowish underground stems that produce small, inconspicuous aerial shoots. These shoots bear numerous small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered together to form dense inflorescences. The flowers typically possess a reduced structure with multiple stamens, reflecting the species' namesake. Because it lacks photosynthetic capabilities, the entire structure is adapted to derive all necessary nutrients and water directly from its host.
