Balanophora polyandra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Balanophora polyandra has a parasitic, holoparasitic morphology characterized by a lack of chlorophyll, scale-like leaves, and a specialized root system known as a haustorium that penetrates host plants. These shoots bear numerous small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered together to form dense inflorescences. The flowers typically possess a reduced structure with multiple stamens, reflecting the species' namesake. Because it lacks photosynthetic capabilities, the entire structure is adapted to derive all necessary nutrients and water directly from its host.
