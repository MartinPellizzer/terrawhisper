Viola davidii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viola davidii has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by basal leaves that are broadly ovate to cordate with crenate margins and a stem structure that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The foliage features distinct long petioles and a smooth surface texture. Its flowers are zygomorphic and display a violet or purplish coloration with a pale yellow throat. These blooms emerge from the leaf axils and are supported by slender peduncles. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds upon maturation.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of central and western China, specifically within provinces such as Sichuan, Yunnan, and Guizhou. It thrives in high-altitude environments where temperate climates provide the necessary moisture and shade. These violet species are typically found growing in forest understories and along the edges of shaded mountain streams. Their specific distribution is often dictated by the presence of well-drained, organic-rich soils found in these temperate zones.
