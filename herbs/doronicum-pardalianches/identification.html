To identify Alpine Buttercup (Doronicum pardalianches) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The Alpine Buttercup is scientifically known as Doronicum pardalianches, a name derived from the Greek words 'doron' meaning gift and 'nike' meaning victory, reflecting its historical use in herbal medicine. The species was first described by the botanist Carl von Moench in 1800. It has several synonyms, including Anemone pardalianches and Anemone alpina, which reflect its classification in the Ranunculaceae family.

Morphological Characteristics

Alpine Buttercup (Doronicum pardalianches) is a low-growing perennial with a rhizomatous root system. Its stems are upright and hairy, typically reaching 15-30 cm in height. The leaves are basal, lanceolate, and have a serrated margin. The inflorescence consists of a single flower per stem, with yellow petals and a green, hairy center. The fruit is a capsule that disperses small, black seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Alpine Buttercup can be distinguished by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers with 8-12 petals, and its basal leaves with a distinctive heart-shaped base. It has a short, stout stem and lacks a central stem, unlike similar species like the lesser celandine. Its leaves are smooth and have a slightly hairy underside, aiding in identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Alpine Buttercup is native to the mountainous regions of Europe, particularly the Alps. It thrives in cool, temperate climates with high humidity. It prefers well-drained, nutrient-rich soils. It is typically found at altitudes between 1,500 and 2,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Alpine Buttercup blooms from late spring to early summer, typically between May and June. Seed formation occurs in late summer, around July to August. For identification purposes, the plant is most easily recognized during its flowering period, making late spring to early summer the optimal time for accurate botanical identification.

Phylogenetic Relationships

The Alpine Buttercup (Doronicum pardalianches) is closely related to species within the genus Doronicum. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, sharing traits such as leaf structure and flower morphology with other members of this family. Its phylogenetic position highlights its evolutionary ties to other buttercup-like plants.
