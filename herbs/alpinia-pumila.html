Alpinia pumila

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Alpinia pumila has small, lanceolate leaves that grow in an opposite arrangement along slender, upright stems reaching a height of approximately thirty centimeters. The foliage is characterized by a vibrant green color and a smooth texture that distinguishes it from larger species in the Zingiberaceae family. Its flowering structures consist of delicate, tubular blossoms that typically emerge from the leaf axils. These flowers often feature subtle color variations, ranging from pale whites to soft pinkish hues. The overall plant architecture is compact and tufted, forming dense clumps in its natural habitat.
