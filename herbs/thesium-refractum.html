What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Thesium refractum has slender, upright stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 50 centimeters and feature small, linear to lanceolate leaves arranged alternately along the branches. The foliage is characterized by its narrow shape and relatively small size, often appearing somewhat scale-like or reduced. Its flowers are inconspicuous and small, usually appearing in the axils of the leaves or at the terminal ends of the stems. The plant lacks true petals, instead possessing small, inconspicuous perianth segments that facilitate its hemiparasitic lifestyle. This species is often found in sandy or rocky soils where its delicate, wiry structure allows it to thrive among other low-growing vegetation.
