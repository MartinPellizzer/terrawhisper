What's the taxonomical classification of Ophiorrhiza japonica?

Ophiorrhiza japonica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized under the order Gentianaales and is a member of the family Rubiaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Ophiorrhiza and the species japonica.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compound camptothecin, which is a potent cytotoxic alkaloid used in cancer research and treatment. This specific molecule functions by inhibiting the enzyme topoisomerase I, thereby preventing DNA replication in rapidly dividing cells. Because of this mechanism, it serves as a critical structural foundation for several advanced chemotherapeutic drugs. Scientists study its unique chemical properties to develop more targeted therapies for various types of malignant tumors. Its presence in the plant highlights the significant role that botanical sources play in modern oncology.
