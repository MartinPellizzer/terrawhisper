Ophiorrhiza japonica

What's the taxonomical classification of Ophiorrhiza japonica?

Ophiorrhiza japonica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized under the order Gentianaales and is a member of the family Rubiaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Ophiorrhiza and the species japonica.

Ophiorrhiza japonica has a creeping rhizome that produces thin, delicate leaves arranged in a basal rosette pattern. These flowers are characterized by their slender shape and are designed to attract specific pollinators. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the bioactive alkaloids camptothecin, ophiorrhin, japonine, ophiorrhizin, and ophiorrhizin B. Research indicates that these compounds contribute to the species' documented medicinal properties, such as its potential anti-tumor and anti-inflammatory effects.
