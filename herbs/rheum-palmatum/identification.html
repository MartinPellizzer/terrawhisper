Botanical Identification of Rhubarb (Rheum palmatum)

To identify Rhubarb (Rheum palmatum) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Rhubarb, or Rheum palmatum, belongs to the kingdom Plantae. Its family is Rheaceae, and it is part of the genus Rheum. The species is Rheum palmatum, known for its edible leaves and medicinal roots used in traditional herbal medicine.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Rhubarb is Rheum palmatum, a classification that identifies it as a species within the genus Rheum. This name was first formally described by the botanist Carl von Linné, and it is widely recognized in botanical literature. Synonyms for this species include Rheum rhaponticum and Rheum officinale, reflecting its historical classification and regional usage.

Morphological Characteristics

Rhubarb (Rheum palmatum) is a perennial herb with a thick, fleshy rhizome. Its root system consists of a central rhizome with fibrous roots. The stem is erect, smooth, and reddish-pink, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Leaves are large, heart-shaped, and deeply lobed, with a waxy surface and a red-veined underside. The inflorescence is a tall, branched cluster of greenish-white flowers. Flowers are small and inconspicuous, while the fruit is a dry capsule containing numerous seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Rhubarb (Rheum palmatum) can be distinguished by its large, rhombic leaves with prominent veins and smooth, red-orange petioles. It has thick, fleshy roots and produces tall, green stems with clustered red flowers. It differs from similar species like Rheum officinale by its larger leaves and more robust growth habit.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Rheum palmatum is native to regions of East Asia, including China, Korea, and Japan. It prefers temperate climates with cool summers and cold winters. It thrives in well-drained, loamy soils and is typically found at elevations between 1,000 and 2,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Rheum palmatum blooms in late spring to early summer, typically between May and June. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early fall. For identification purposes, rhubarb is harvested in early to mid-summer when the stalks are thick and the leaves are fully developed.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Rheum palmatum belongs to the Polygonaceae family, sharing close relationships with other genera like Rumex and Erodium. Its closest relatives include species such as Rumex acetosa and Erodium cicutarium. These species share similar morphological and genetic traits, including distinctive leaf structures and achenes. The genus Rheum is closely related to other members of the family, reflecting its evolutionary placement within the Polygonaceae.
