10 Possible Side Effects Of Rheum Palmatum (Chinese Rhubarb)

Rheum palmatum, also known as Chinese rhubarb, has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal irritation, allergic reactions, and kidney damage.

These side effects occur when the herb is taken in excessive doses or for an extended period.

For instance, gastrointestinal irritation can lead to stomach pain, nausea, and vomiting, making it difficult to maintain a regular diet and daily routine.

Rheum palmatum, also known as Chinese rhubarb, causes stomach upset due to its high content of anthraquinone glycosides.

These compounds can irritate the mucous membranes in the digestive tract, leading to nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea.

The bitter taste and rough texture of Rheum palmatum can also cause gastrointestinal irritation, exacerbating symptoms such as bloating and abdominal pain.

The high concentration of these allergens in Rheum palmatum products may exacerbate allergic reactions, making it essential for individuals to be aware of the potential risks involved.
