Tapinanthus Macrosolen

Tapinanthus macrosolen, commonly known as the parasitic morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

Traditionally, it has been used in folk medicine for its purported therapeutic properties, particularly in treating ailments such as malaria, fever, and skin infections. The plant contains various bioactive compounds, including alkaloids and flavonoids, which may contribute to its medicinal effects. Despite its historical use, scientific research on its efficacy and safety remains limited, and it is not widely recognized in modern pharmacology.

Due to potential toxicity and lack of standardized dosing, its use for medicinal purposes is not recommended without further clinical validation.
