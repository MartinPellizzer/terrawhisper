Dipterocarpus gracilis

What's the taxonomical classification of Dipterocarpus gracilis?

Dipterocarpus gracilis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Malvales. This plant is further categorized under the family Dipterocarpaceae and the genus Dipterocarpus, ultimately being identified by its specific species name, gracilis.

      What are the herbal preparations of Dipterocarpus gracilis?

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Dipterocarpus gracilis can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or digestive distress if its components are ingested or touched without protection. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, many plants in this family contain secondary metabolites that may trigger contact dermatitis. Direct exposure to the sap or leaves could lead to redness, itching, or localized swelling on sensitive skin. If accidental ingestion occurs, individuals might experience nausea, abdominal pain, or vomiting. It is always recommended to handle this species with gloves and avoid any contact with mucous membranes.
