Dipterocarpus gracilis

What's the taxonomical classification of Dipterocarpus gracilis?

Dipterocarpus gracilis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Malvales. This plant is further categorized under the family Dipterocarpaceae and the genus Dipterocarpus, ultimately being identified by its specific species name, gracilis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Dipterocarpus gracilis contains bioactive phytochemicals including alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, and tannins that contribute to its medicinal properties. These secondary metabolites are frequently studied for their potential pharmacological activities such as antimicrobial and antioxidant effects. Researchers often examine these specific compounds to understand how they interact with biological systems to mitigate inflammation or oxidative stress. The concentration and synergy of these chemical constituents vary depending on the plant's environmental conditions and the specific part used for extraction. Such chemical profiles provide a scientific basis for investigating the traditional uses of the species in various medicinal practices.

What are the therapeutic actions of Dipterocarpus gracilis?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, stomach aches, and various inflammatory conditions. The extracts from its bark and leaves possess antimicrobial properties that help combat bacterial pathogens on the skin. Traditional healers often apply crushed portions of the plant directly to sores to promote rapid healing. Additionally, decoctions made from the plant parts are ingested to alleviate gastrointestinal discomfort and internal swelling. These medicinal applications reflect its significant role in regional ethnobotanical practices.
