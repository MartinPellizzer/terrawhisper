What's the taxonomical classification of Russelia juncea?

Russelia juncea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Lamiales. This plant is a member of the family Plantaginaceae, which places it within the genus Russelia. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by its species name, juncea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Russelia juncea has slender, wiry, leafless stems that form dense, weeping shrubs reaching heights of up to three feet. The plant is characterized by its broom-like appearance due to these thin, green, photosynthetic branches that lack true leaves. Small, narrow leaves may occasionally appear during growth stages, but they are largely inconspicuous. Clusters of tubular, bright red or orange flowers emerge from the ends of the stems to attract pollinators. These trumpet-shaped blooms are arranged in terminal racemes and provide a striking visual contrast to the fine texture of the foliage.
