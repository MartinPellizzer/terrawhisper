Black Nightshade (Solanum nigrum)

Black Nightshade (Solanum nigrum) is a member of the Solanaceae family, native to Southern Europe, Middle East, and Southwest Asia. Traditionally, its fruits, leaves, and seeds have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, bitter, and astringent actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, african traditional medicine, and mediterranean herbal traditions.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 30 to 100 centimeters.
Leaves
Simple leaves with acute apices, dark green upper surface, pale green lower surface, and prominent stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Flowers are actinomorphic, typically solitary, with five fused petals forming a bell-shaped corolla, five stamens, and a superior ovary with two chambers containing two ovules each, and exhibit a range of colors from violet to black.
Stems
Woody, herbaceous stems with opposite branching, glabrous to sparsely pubescent surfaces, and persistent, glandular dots along the nodes.
