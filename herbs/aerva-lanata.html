Indian Hemp

Aerva lanata

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Indian Hemp, scientifically known as Aerva lanata, belongs to the Amaranthaceae family. Commonly called "Indian Hemp" or "Punyapal" in some regions, it is an annual herb native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has hairy stems, alternate leaves, and small white flowers. It is also known by various synonyms in traditional herbal texts.

Active Compounds

Indian Hemp contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Tannin
  • Saponin

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action
Respiratory Respiratory infection, cough, influenza Antimicrobial, expectorant, immunostimulant, antihistaminic, antiviral
Integumentary Skin infection, wound healing, skin inflammation, skin infections Antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, antifungal
Digestive Gastrointestinal infection, diarrhea, gastroenteritis, gastrointestinal disorders Antimicrobial, antispasmodic, anti-ulcer, anti-inflammatory

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory issues and as a general tonic.

Decoction: Used for digestive problems and as a diuretic.

Poultice: Applied externally for skin infections and inflammation.

Safety Profile

Indian Hemp (Aerva lanata) is generally considered safe when used in moderate amounts, but it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to potential toxicity. Use with caution and consult a healthcare provider before use.
