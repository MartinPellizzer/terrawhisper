Aerva lanata

What's the taxonomical classification of Aerva lanata?

Aerva lanata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Caryophyllales and is a member of the family Amaranthaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Aerva, and its specific species name, lanata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aerva lanata has an erect, herbaceous structure characterized by slender, cylindrical stems that often reach heights of up to 60 centimeters. The leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem and exhibit an elliptical to lanceolate shape with entire margins. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, typically appearing in a cyme formation. These blossoms are often white or pale in color and possess a delicate texture. The plant is further distinguished by its fine, pubescent surface which provides a soft tactile quality to the foliage.

What are the therapeutic actions of Aerva lanata?

Aerva lanaata has therapeutic properties characterized by its bitter, diuretic, astringent, hemostatic, anti-inflammatory, antipyretic, and vulnerary actions. The bitter components of the plant help stimulate digestion and appetite. Its diuretic effect promotes the excretion of excess fluids through the urinary system. As an astringent and hemostatic agent, it assists in shrinking tissues and controlling bleeding. The plant also functions as an anti-inflammatory and antipyretic to reduce swelling and fever. Finally, its vulnerary qualities aid in the healing and protection of wounded skin.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains Canthin-6-one, beta-Carboline Alkaloids, beta-sitosterol, 10-methoxycanthin-6-one, and 6'-O-(4″-hydroxy-trans-cinnamoyl)-kaempferol-3-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside. These diverse medicinal compounds provide the specimen with a complex chemical profile often studied for therapeutic potential. The presence of beta-Carboline Alkaloids suggests significant bioactive properties that can influence various biological systems. Additionally, the phytosterol beta-sitosterol contributes to the plant's overall structural and pharmacological makeup. Specialized molecules like 10-methoxycanthin-6-one and the specific kaempferol derivative further enhance its unique chemical signature. Together, these constituents define the medicinal importance of the plant's secondary metabolites.

As discussed in a study, six alkaloids were isolated from the herb of Aerva lanaata. Canthin-6-one (1) and beta-carboline-1-propionic acid (5) were previously found. Three new alkaloids were identified as 10-methoxy-canthin-6-one (2), 10-hydroxy-canthin-6-one (3), and 10-O-beta-D-glucopyranosyloxycanthin-6-one (4). Additionally, 6-methoxy-beta-carboline-1-propionic acid (6) is a new alkaloid. The structures for these compounds were determined through spectroscopic analysis and chemical transformations.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating cancer through its ability to inhibit the development of EAC tumors and Ehrlich ascites carcinoma. Research indicates it possesses significant anticancer properties that can mitigate the progression of various malignancies. It is also studied for its effectiveness against Dalton's lymphoma ascites in experimental models. Additionally, the plant demonstrates potential in managing infections caused by Plasmodium falciparum. These medicinal qualities highlight its importance in pharmacological studies related to tumor suppression and antimalarial activity.
