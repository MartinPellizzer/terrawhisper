Indian Hemp (Aerva lanata)

Indian Hemp (Aerva lanata) is a member of the Amaranthaceae family, native to South Asia, Eastern Himalayas, and Southern China. Traditionally, its leaves, stems, and roots have been used for decoctions, infusions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its diuretic, anti-inflammatory, and bitter actions, and has a long history of use in ayurvedic medicine, african traditional medicine, and traditional chinese medicine.

Flowers
Inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small white flowers with five petals and five sepals, arranged in a paniculate pattern, exhibiting actinomorphic symmetry, each flower lacking distinct markings.
Stems
Climbing stems with opposite branching, hairy surface, and nodes bearing adventitious roots.
