Aerva lanata

What's the taxonomical classification of Aerva lanata?

Aerva lanata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Caryophyllales and is a member of the family Amaranthaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Aerva, and its specific species name, lanata.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Aerva lanaata contains bioactive phytochemicals including alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, tannins, and glycosides that provide its therapeutic properties. These specific compounds work synergistically to exhibit significant antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory activities in various biological models. The presence of tannins often contributes to its astringent effects, which are useful in treating skin irritations or minor wounds. Additionally, the flavonoids found within the plant act as potent antioxidants to combat oxidative stress in cells. Researchers continue to study these chemical constituents to better understand their potential applications in modern pharmacology.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin diseases, inflammatory conditions, fever, and various digestive disorders. Its medicinal properties are often applied topically to soothe irritations, wounds, and localized swelling. When consumed, it serves as a traditional remedy for managing systemic heat and promoting overall wellness. The botanical extracts are also studied for their potential to alleviate respiratory issues and stomach discomfort. Practitioners frequently utilize different parts of the herb to target specific ailments through decoctions or poultices.
