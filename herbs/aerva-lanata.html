Indian Hemp

Aerva lanata

Indian Hemp (Aerva lanata) is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions, known for its traditional use in treating various health conditions, including respiratory disorders and skin ailments.

Taxonomy

Indian Hemp (Aerva lanata) is a flowering plant belonging to the kingdom Plantae, division Spermatophyta, class Caryophyllopsida, order Caryophyllales, family Amaranthaceae, genus Aerva, and species Aerva lanata.

Morphology

Indian Hemp (Aerva lanata) is a shrub or small tree with a branched, often sparsely hairy stem, trifoliate leaves with elliptic to ovate leaflets, and small white or greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Distribution

Indian Hemp (Aerva lanata) is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including countries such as India, China, Thailand, and Malaysia, and is commonly found in dry, sandy soils in open habitats.

Traditional Uses

Indian Hemp (Aerva lanata) has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine for its diuretic, anti-inflammatory, and anthelmintic properties, often prepared as a decoction or paste to treat urinary tract infections, skin ailments, and parasitic infestations.

Medicinal Systems

In traditional medicinal systems such as Ayurveda, Unani, and Chinese medicine, Indian Hemp (Aerva lanata) has been utilized for its purported therapeutic properties, including its use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments.

Phytochemistry

Indian Hemp (Aerva lanata) contains a variety of bioactive compounds, including alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, and tannins, which contribute to its medicinal properties and therapeutic potential.

Pharmacology

Indian Hemp (Aerva lanata) exhibits a range of pharmacological properties including anti-inflammatory, analgesic, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, which contribute to its traditional use in treating respiratory and skin disorders.

Botanical Identification

Indian Hemp, scientifically known as Aerva lanata, belongs to the Amaranthaceae family. Commonly called "Indian Hemp" or "Punyapal" in some regions, it is an annual herb native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has hairy stems, alternate leaves, and small white flowers. It is also known by various synonyms in traditional herbal texts.

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory issues and as a general tonic.

Decoction: Used for digestive problems and as a diuretic.

Poultice: Applied externally for skin infections and inflammation.

Safety Profile

Indian Hemp (Aerva lanata) is generally considered safe when used in moderate amounts, but it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to potential toxicity. Use with caution and consult a healthcare provider before use.
