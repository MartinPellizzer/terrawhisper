Rhododendron emarginatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Ericales and is a member of the family Ericaceae. Finally, the plant is placed within the genus Rhododendron, specifically identifying as the species emarginatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhododendron emarginatum has small, elliptic to lanceolate leaves that are typically dark green, leathery, and characterized by an emarginate or slightly notched apex. These leaves are arranged in dense clusters along woody, spreading branches that form a compact shrubby habit. During the flowering season, the plant produces funnel-shaped corollas that vary in color from pale pink to deep rose.
