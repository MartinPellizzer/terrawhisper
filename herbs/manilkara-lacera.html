Manilkara lacera, commonly known as the lac tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America, valued for its medicinal properties. The latex extracted from its bark contains various bioactive compounds, including alkaloids and flavonoids, which have been traditionally used to treat inflammatory conditions and skin infections. In folk medicine, the sap is applied topically to reduce swelling and promote wound healing. Recent scientific studies have shown promising results in its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects, supporting its traditional use.
