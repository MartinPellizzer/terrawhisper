Dracocephalum taliense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dracocephalum taliense has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by erect stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The leaves are primarily opposite and vary from lanceolate to ovate in shape with serrated margins. Its inflorescences consist of dense, terminal verticillasters composed of small, tubular flowers that display distinct pigmentation. The calyx is often bilabiate and remains persistent around the developing seeds. These morphological traits distinguish the species within its specific high-altitude habitat.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the high-altitude regions of the Dali Prefecture within the Yunnan Province of southwestern China. It is specifically found growing in the alpine environments of the Cang Mountains. These populations typically occupy rocky slopes and scree areas located at elevations between 3,500 and 4,200 meters. The species is restricted to these specific mountainous niches due to its specialized ecological requirements. Consequently, its natural range is highly localized and limited to this single mountain range.
