Hoya pandurata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hoya pandurata has succulent, ovate to elliptical leaves that measure up to 10 centimeters in length and are characterized by a thick, waxy texture and a deep green color. These leaves are arranged oppositely along trailing or climbing woody stems that allow the plant to function as an epiphyte. The inflorescence consists of umbels containing multiple star-shaped, fragrant flowers with a distinct corona. Each individual blossom features creamy white or pale green petals paired with a central, often colorful, fleshy corona. The overall structure of the plant supports a robust, climbing habit suited for tropical environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, specifically spanning across Thailand, Malaysia, and parts of Indonesia. It thrives within the humid, lowland rainforest environments characteristic of these equatorial territories. Within these habitats, the species is typically found climbing on various host trees to reach sunlight in the canopy. Its distribution is closely tied to the availability of consistent warmth and high moisture levels found in these specific jungle ecosystems. Because it relies on these stable tropical climates, the plant does not naturally occur in temperate or arid zones.

      What side-effects this plant can have?
