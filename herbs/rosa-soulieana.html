Rosa soulieana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rosa soulieana has pinnate leaves typically composed of five to seven leaflets with serrated margins and small, fragrant, single or semi-double pink to white flowers. The plant features a deciduous growth habit and possesses slender, arching stems that are often armed with fine prickles. Its leaves exhibit a deep green color on the upper surface and a slightly paler tone underneath. The floral structure is characterized by numerous stamens surrounding the central carpels. This species generally grows in a shrubby form, reaching moderate heights in its natural habitat.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of central and western China, specifically within the provinces of Sichuan, Yunnan, and Guizhou. It thrives in high-altitude environments where the terrain is often steep and rocky. Within these habitats, it typically occupies shaded forest edges or thickets located near mountain streams. The species is specifically adapted to the temperate, moist climates found in these specific East Asian cordilleras.
