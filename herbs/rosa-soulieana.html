Rosa soulieana

What's the taxonomical classification of Rosa soulieana?

Rosa soulieana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy through the order Rosales and the family Rosaceae, the plant is identified by the genus Rosa. Ultimately, its specific biological identity is defined by its species name, soulieana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rosa soulieana has pinnate leaves typically composed of five to seven leaflets with serrated margins and small, fragrant, single or semi-double pink to white flowers.

The plant features a deciduous growth habit and possesses slender, arching stems that are often armed with fine prickles. Its leaves exhibit a deep green color on the upper surface and a slightly paler tone underneath. The floral structure is characterized by numerous stamens surrounding the central carpels.

This species generally grows in a shrubby form, reaching moderate heights in its natural habitat.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Rosa soulieana targets various inflammatory mediators, specifically inhibiting the expression of cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2), inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS), and pro-inflammatory cytokines like tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-alpha). These biochemical interactions are primarily driven by the plant's rich content of bioactive polyphenols and flavonoids. These compounds work by modulating the signaling pathways of nuclear factor kappa B (NF-kB), which is a central regulator of the immune response. By suppressing these specific protein pathways, the plant exerts its significant anti-inflammatory and antioxidant medicinal effects. Furthermore, these molecular interactions help reduce oxidative stress within cellular environments.

      Rosa soulieana is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste depending on the intended therapeutic use. The dried flowers and stems are often simmered in water to create a tea that is consumed for internal health benefits.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Rosa soulieana can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and digestive upset if ingested. Contact with the plant's foliage or stems may trigger redness and itching in sensitive individuals. Some people might experience swelling or a rash upon touching the plant's surfaces. If consumed accidentally, it can lead to nausea or stomach pain. Always exercise caution when handling this species to avoid these adverse reactions.
