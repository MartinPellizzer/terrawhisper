Rosa soulieana

What's the taxonomical classification of Rosa soulieana?

Rosa soulieana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy through the order Rosales and the family Rosaceae, the plant is identified by the genus Rosa. Ultimately, its specific biological identity is defined by its species name, soulieana.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Rosa soulieana contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including polyphenols, flavonoids, and terpenoids that contribute to its potential therapeutic properties. Researchers examine these molecular structures to determine how they interact with cellular pathways to mitigate disease progression. The complexity of its chemical profile suggests a diverse range of pharmacological activities that warrant further clinical investigation.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin conditions such as eczema, various types of rashes, and inflammation of the skin. The medicinal properties of the plant are often applied topically to soothe irritation and promote healing in damaged tissues. Some traditional practices also involve using its derivatives to address internal ailments related to digestive discomfort. Research into its bioactive compounds focuses on how these substances interact with the body to reduce redness and swelling. Careful preparation is essential when utilizing this species for any therapeutic purpose.
