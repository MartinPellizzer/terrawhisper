Manglietia conifera

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Manglietia conifera has deciduous, elliptical to oblong leaves that are typically 10 to 20 centimeters long and possess finely serrated margins. The foliage displays a deep green color during the growing season, often transitioning to autumnal hues. Its reproductive structures consist of large, fragrant flowers that emerge from woody branches, often before the leaves have fully developed. The fruit develops into an aggregate of follicle-like structures that eventually release small, often brightly colored seeds. The overall architecture of the plant is characterized by a spreading canopy and a sturdy, woody stem structure.
