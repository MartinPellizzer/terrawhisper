Eleutherococcus lasiogyne

What's the taxonomical classification of Eleutherococcus lasiogyne?

Eleutherococcus lasiogyne belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further falls into the order Apiales and is a member of the family Araliaceae. Specifically, it is identified by the genus Eleutherococcus, with its unique designation being the species lasiogyne.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Eleutherococcus lasiogyne contains a complex profile of bioactive compounds including eleutherosides, lignans, and various polysaccharides that function as adaptogens. These chemical constituents work together to help the body regulate physiological responses to physical and emotional stress. The presence of specific sesquiterpenes and triterpenoids may also contribute to its potential immune-modulating effects. Researchers study these molecules to understand how they influence metabolic pathways and cellular energy production. Such pharmacological properties make the plant a subject of interest for natural medicine and holistic health treatments.

What are the therapeutic actions of Eleutherococcus lasiogyne?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat conditions characterized by physical exhaustion, chronic fatigue syndrome, and reduced immune function. It acts as an adaptogen to help the body manage physiological stress and maintain metabolic balance. Practitioners often utilize it to support recovery from long-term illnesses and to enhance overall stamina during convalescence. The botanical properties are also believed to assist in stabilizing the nervous system during periods of intense mental or physical strain. By promoting homeostatic processes, it serves as a supportive measure for various stress-related ailments.
