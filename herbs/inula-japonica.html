Side Effects of inula japonica

Inula japonica leads to nausea, which can be particularly distressing for individuals consuming the herb, especially when taken in higher doses or over prolonged periods.

This herb also causes dry mouth, a common side effect that may lead to discomfort and difficulty in swallowing. Additionally, Inula japonica can cause stomach upset, resulting in bloating, cramping, and a general feeling of unease in the gastrointestinal tract. Some users may experience headaches as a result of the herb's impact on blood circulation and nervous system function.

Furthermore, the herb may induce sweating, which can be a sign of the body's response to its active compounds. It can also bring on diarrhea, which may be severe in some cases, and trigger skin rash, indicating an allergic reaction in sensitive individuals. Inula japonica might result in dizziness and fatigue, suggesting a potential impact on the body's energy levels and balance.

These side effects highlight the importance of consulting a healthcare professional before using this herb, especially for those with pre-existing health conditions or who are taking other medications.
