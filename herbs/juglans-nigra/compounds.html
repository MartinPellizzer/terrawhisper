Black Walnut (Juglans nigra) Compounds

The primary compounds in Black Walnut include flavonoids, which act as antioxidants, and tannins, which have astringent properties. Phenolic acids contribute to its antioxidant effects, while saponins may support immune function. Coumarin and glycosides are also present, offering potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial benefits.

Black Walnut (Juglans nigra) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Black Walnut (Juglans nigra) contribute to its medicinal properties by exhibiting antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. These compounds support the plant's traditional use in promoting digestive health and reducing oxidative stress. They are found in the husk and kernel of the nut, enhancing the plant's therapeutic potential.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Black Walnut (Juglans nigra). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and has antimicrobial and antifungal effects. Tannins in Black Walnut are primarily found in the husk and help in preserving the nut. They are also believed to support digestive health.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in Black Walnut (Juglans nigra). It contributes to the plant's antimicrobial and antifungal properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine. This compound helps in reducing inflammation and may aid in digestive health.

4. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Black Walnut (Juglans nigra). It exhibits antimicrobial and antifungal properties, making it valuable in herbal medicine for treating infections and supporting immune health. Saponins also contribute to the plant's astringent effects and are often used in formulations targeting skin and digestive issues.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Black Walnut (Juglans nigra). It contributes to the plant's antifungal and antibacterial properties, making it valuable in herbal medicine for treating infections and supporting immune health. Coumarin also has mild sedative effects and is used in traditional remedies for digestive and respiratory issues.

6. Glycoside

Glycosides in Black Walnut (Juglans nigra) are naturally occurring compounds with potential antimicrobial and antifungal properties. They contribute to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and skin-related conditions. These glycosides are often extracted from the husk and are believed to support immune function and reduce inflammation.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Black Walnut (Juglans nigra). They contribute to the plant's antimicrobial and antifungal properties. These compounds may help in treating infections and supporting immune health. Terpenoids are part of the complex chemical profile that gives Black Walnut its medicinal value.

8. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanins are pigments found in Black Walnut (Juglans nigra) that contribute to its dark color and have antioxidant properties. These compounds help neutralize free radicals, supporting cellular health and reducing oxidative stress. They are also believed to enhance the plant's traditional uses in herbal medicine for skin and digestive health.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Black Walnut (Juglans nigra). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly in antiparasitic and antimicrobial actions. Alkaloids in Black Walnut are often associated with the efficacy of its traditional uses in herbal medicine.

10. Essential oil

The essential oil of Black Walnut (Juglans nigra) contains bioactive components like juglone and other terpenoids. These compounds exhibit antimicrobial and antifungal properties, making the essential oil useful in herbal medicine for treating infections and supporting skin health.
