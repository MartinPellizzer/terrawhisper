Juglans nigra causes allergic reactions.

The allergenic properties of Juglans nigra are due to its unique composition, which includes a high concentration of juglone, a potent phenolic compound that can trigger an immune response in some individuals. When ingested or applied topically, juglone can cause an allergic reaction characterized by symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.

Additionally, the tree's pollen and sap have been known to trigger hay fever-like reactions and skin irritation in susceptible persons.
