Crotalaria sessiliflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Crotalaria sessiliflora?

Crotalaria sessiliflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. As a member of the order Fabales, it falls under the family Fabaceae, which is known for its significant role in nitrogen fixation. Within this family, the plant is categorized under the genus Crotalaria, and its specific identity is defined by the species name sessiliflora.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Its inflorescence consists of small, bright yellow flowers that are borne in axillary racemes or clusters. The fruit is a distinctive, indehiscent pod that is often inflated and contains several small, hard seeds.     What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause severe systemic toxicity due to the presence of pyrrolizidine alkaloids within its tissues. These chemical compounds are primarily known to induce significant liver damage in animals and humans that ingest them. This hepatic injury often manifests as progressive venous congestion within the liver architecture. As the toxins affect the digestive and metabolic systems, individuals may experience acute symptoms such as nausea and vomiting. Continuous exposure to these alkaloids can lead to long-term organ failure if not addressed immediately.
