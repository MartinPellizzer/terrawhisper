10 Possible Side Effects Of Kalanchoe Crenata (Paddle Plant)

Kalanchoe crenata has some side effects when used improperly, such as skin irritation and allergic reactions.

These side effects can be caused by excessive consumption or direct contact with the plant's sap. Prolonged exposure can lead to redness, itching, and swelling, potentially worsening everyday activities like work or sports.

In severe cases, it can even affect sleep and relationships due to discomfort and anxiety.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Kalanchoe crenata if used imporperly.

Kalanchoe crenata causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

The sap of this plant contains allergenic compounds that can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms such as skin irritation, itching, and redness.

Additionally, the plant's leaves and stems may release volatile oils into the air, which can exacerbate respiratory issues like asthma and cause allergic rhinitis.

2. Triggers seizures

Kalanchoe crenata triggers asthma attacks by releasing allergenic particles into the air through its tiny hairs and sap.

When inhaled, these particles can irritate the lungs and trigger an allergic response, leading to severe wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath characteristic of an asthma attack.

The sap's latex content is particularly problematic, as it can cause anaphylaxis in some individuals, further exacerbating respiratory issues.

These compounds stimulate the cardiovascular system, causing an increase in heart rate and blood pressure. The plant's sap is particularly high in these chemicals, which can lead to a rise in blood pressure levels when ingested or used topically.

This response may be exacerbated in individuals who are already prone to hypertension or have pre-existing cardiovascular conditions.

Kalanchoe crenata aggravates heart problems due to its potential interaction with cardiovascular medications.

The plant's chemical composition, particularly its cardenolide content, may enhance the effects of digoxin and other digitalis-like compounds, increasing the risk of arrhythmias, tachycardia, and cardiac conduction disorders.

Additionally, Kalanchoe crenata may also cause vasoconstriction, further exacerbating cardiovascular issues in individuals with pre-existing heart conditions.
