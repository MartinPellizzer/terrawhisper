Botanical Identification of Paddle Plant (Kalanchoe crenata)

To identify Paddle Plant (Kalanchoe crenata) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Kalanchoe crenata belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Saxifragales, family Crassulaceae, genus Kalanchoe, and species Kalanchoe crenata. These classifications help identify its botanical relationships and place it within the plant kingdom.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The botanical name of the Paddle Plant is Kalanchoe crenata, first described by the botanist K. Schumacher in 1890. It has several synonyms, including Kalanchoe pinnata and Kalanchoe thyrsiflora. Common names include Paddle Plant, Tiger Plant, and Tiger Tail. It is also known by regional names such as Kalanchoe in some African countries and by historical names in traditional medicine practices.

Morphological Characteristics

The Paddle Plant, Kalanchoe crenata, is a succulent shrub with a compact growth habit. It has a shallow root system adapted to arid conditions. Its stems are short and thick, often branching at the base. Leaves are fleshy, paddle-shaped, and have a waxy surface. The inflorescence is a dense cluster of flowers, typically red or orange, with showy petals. The fruit is a capsule containing numerous small, black seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Kalanchoe crenata can be distinguished by its thick, fleshy leaves with wavy edges and a reddish tint. It produces clusters of small, yellow flowers. Unlike similar species, it lacks the woolly texture on its leaves and has a more compact growth habit. Its leaves are also more rounded and less pointed compared to related Kalanchoe varieties.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Paddle Plant (Kalanchoe crenata) is native to South Africa, particularly the Cape Province. It thrives in Mediterranean climates with mild, wet winters and hot, dry summers. It prefers well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is typically found at elevations between 200 and 1,200 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Paddle Plant (Kalanchoe crenata) blooms from late winter to early spring. Seed formation occurs shortly after flowering, typically in spring. For identification purposes, the plant is best harvested during its vegetative stage in early to mid-summer for accurate botanical characteristics.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Kalanchoe crenata is closely related to other Kalanchoe species within the Crassulaceae family. It shares key morphological and genetic traits with its genus, including thick, fleshy leaves and succulent growth patterns. Its phylogenetic position reflects its evolutionary ties to other succulent plants in the family.
