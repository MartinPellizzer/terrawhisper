Paddle Plant (Kalanchoe crenata) Compounds

The primary compounds of Paddle Plant include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and phenolic acids, known for their anti-inflammatory effects. Terpenoids contribute to its medicinal value, while glycosides and saponins support detoxification. Tannins and coumarin provide astringent and antispasmodic actions, and essential oils enhance its therapeutic potential. Anthocyanins add color and additional antioxidant benefits.

Paddle Plant (Kalanchoe crenata) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Kalanchoe crenata contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. They support the plant's traditional use in treating wounds and infections. Flavonoids enhance the plant's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

Phenolic acid in Paddle Plant (Kalanchoe crenata) contributes to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound helps reduce oxidative stress and supports immune function, making it a key component in traditional herbal medicine for treating inflammatory conditions.

Terpenoids are a key class of active compounds in Paddle Plant (Kalanchoe crenata). These compounds contribute to the plant's medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and wound-healing effects. They are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their therapeutic potential.

4. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Paddle Plant (Kalanchoe crenata). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating wounds and inflammation. Glycosides in Kalanchoe crenata have demonstrated antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties in scientific studies.

5. Tannin

Tannin in Paddle Plant (Kalanchoe crenata) contributes to its astringent properties, helping to reduce inflammation and promote wound healing. It also acts as a natural preservative, supporting the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for skin conditions and digestive health.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Paddle Plant (Kalanchoe crenata). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating skin conditions and inflammation. 7. Essential oil

The essential oil of Kalanchoe crenata contains bioactive compounds like sesquiterpene lactones and flavonoids. These compounds exhibit antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. They are used in traditional medicine to treat skin infections and inflammatory conditions. The oil is extracted through steam distillation from the plant's leaves.

8. Coumarin

Coumarin is a bioactive compound found in Kalanchoe crenata, known for its potential anti-inflammatory and anticoagulant properties. 9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid in Paddle Plant (Kalanchoe crenata) contributes to its medicinal properties. This compound is known for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. 10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Kalanchoe crenata, known for its antioxidant properties. It contributes to the plant's vivid color and may support cardiovascular and eye health. In traditional herbal medicine, anthocyanin is valued for its potential anti-inflammatory and protective effects.
