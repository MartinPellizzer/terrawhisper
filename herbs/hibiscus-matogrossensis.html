Hibiscus matogrossensis

Hibiscus matogrossensis, commonly known as the Brazilian hibiscus, is a plant species native to Brazil, belonging to the mallow family (Malvaceae).

This plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine due to its numerous health benefits, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties.

The therapeutic actions of Hibiscus matogrossensis include soothing digestive issues, reducing blood pressure, and acting as an antiseptic to combat infections.

The bioactive constituents present in this plant include flavonoids, anthocyanins, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties and potential uses in the treatment of various health conditions.

Possible Side Effects of Hibiscus matogrossensis

Hibiscus matogrossensis can have side effects if used improperly, such as causing digestive issues, nausea, and vomiting due to its high levels of anthocyanins, which can be toxic in excessive amounts.

Prolonged consumption of the plant's extracts may also lead to kidney damage and liver inflammation, particularly in individuals with pre-existing conditions.

Furthermore, excessive intake of hibiscus matogrossensis may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes medications, resulting in adverse reactions.

Additionally, the plant's high levels of saponins can cause allergic reactions, skin irritation, and respiratory issues in sensitive individuals.
