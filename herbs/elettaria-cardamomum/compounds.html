Cardamom (Elettaria cardamomum) Compounds

Cardamom contains essential oils, which are its primary aromatic compounds. Terpenoids contribute to its flavor and aroma, while flavonoids provide antioxidant properties. Coumarin and phenolic acids offer anti-inflammatory effects, and tannins add astringency. Saponins, alkaloids, glycosides, and anthocyanins also contribute to its medicinal profile.

Cardamom (Elettaria cardamomum) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Cardamom (Elettaria cardamomum) is a key active compound, comprising volatile aromatic compounds like cineole and limonene. It is responsible for the plant's distinctive scent and is used in herbal medicine for its digestive, antimicrobial, and calming properties.

2. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a major class of active compounds in Cardamom (Elettaria cardamomum). They contribute to the plant's aromatic profile and have antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties. These compounds are responsible for many of the therapeutic effects associated with cardamom in traditional herbal medicine.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Cardamom (Elettaria cardamomum). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress in the body. These compounds may support various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and cardiovascular protective effects.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a compound found in Cardamom (Elettaria cardamomum). It contributes to the plant's aromatic properties and has mild anticoagulant effects. Coumarin is also used in traditional medicine for its potential digestive and anti-inflammatory benefits.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Cardamom (Elettaria cardamomum). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals in the body. This compound supports digestive health and may reduce inflammation, making it valuable in traditional herbal medicine.

6. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Cardamom (Elettaria cardamomum). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may aid in digestive health. Tannins also provide antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory benefits, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Cardamom (Elettaria cardamomum). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Saponin helps in enhancing the therapeutic value of cardamom in traditional herbal medicine.

8. Alkaloid

Cardamom (Elettaria cardamomum) contains the alkaloid cardamonin. This compound contributes to the plant's characteristic aroma and has been studied for its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. It is commonly used in traditional herbal medicine to support digestive health and respiratory function.

9. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Cardamom (Elettaria cardamomum). It contributes to the plant's aromatic profile and has mild digestive and antimicrobial properties. Glycosides in cardamom may support gastrointestinal health and aid in reducing bloating. These compounds are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their soothing effects.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Cardamom (Elettaria cardamomum). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports the plant's traditional use in promoting digestive health and respiratory function.
