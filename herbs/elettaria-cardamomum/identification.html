Botanical Identification of Cardamom (Elettaria cardamomum)

To identify Cardamom (Elettaria cardamomum) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Cardamom, or Elettaria cardamomum, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Liliopsida, order Zingiberales, family Zingiberaceae, genus Elettaria, and species Elettaria cardamomum. These classifications help identify its botanical relationships and place it within the plant kingdom.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Cardamom is Elettaria cardamomum, first described by the botanist Nathaniel W. Arnott in 1831. It is also known by several synonyms, including Amomum cardamomum and Zingiber cardamomum, reflecting its classification within the Zingiberaceae family. Common names include "cardamom" and "true cardamom," while regional and historical names vary across cultures and languages.

Morphological Characteristics

Cardamom is a herbaceous perennial plant with a rhizomatous root system. It has erect, branched stems that grow up to 1.5 meters tall. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture. The inflorescence is a branched spike, bearing small, greenish-white flowers. The fruit is a capsule containing aromatic, dark brown seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Cardamom can be distinguished by its erect, branched stems and lanceolate, slightly hairy leaves. The inflorescence is a spike with small, pale green flowers, and the fruit is a three-lobed, dark brown capsule. It is often confused with other cardamom species but can be differentiated by its specific leaf shape and capsule structure.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Cardamom is native to the wet tropical regions of India, Sri Lanka, and parts of Southeast Asia. It thrives in warm, humid climates with high rainfall. The plant prefers well-drained, fertile soils and is typically grown at altitudes between 1,000 and 2,000 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Cardamom plants bloom from May to July, producing small white or pale green flowers. Seed formation occurs between July and September. Harvesting of the mature, dark brown pods typically happens in October to November, when the pods are fully developed and aromatic.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Cardamom (Elettaria cardamomum) belongs to the genus Elettaria in the family Zingiberaceae. It is closely related to species like Amomum and Alpinia. These plants share similar morphological and genetic traits, including aromatic compounds and rhizomatous growth. Its phylogenetic placement highlights its evolutionary ties within the ginger family.
