Possible Side Effects of Kali soda

Kali soda can have side effects if used improperly, such as digestive issues, skin irritation, and allergic reactions.

Ingesting large amounts of kali soda can cause nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, while excessive skin application can lead to redness, itching, and rashes.

Improper use of kali soda can also trigger respiratory problems, such as coughing, wheezing, and shortness of breath, in individuals with pre-existing respiratory conditions.

Additionally, using kali soda on open wounds or cuts can cause infection and delay the healing process.
