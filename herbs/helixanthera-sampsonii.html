What's the taxonomical classification of Helixanthera sampsonii?

Helixanthera sampsonii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant follows the biological lineage of the order Santalales. It is further categorized under the family Loranthaceae, which places it among a specific group of parasitic flowering plants. Within this family, it is identified by the genus Helixanthera, and its specific scientific designation is completed by the species name sampsonii.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Helixanthera sampsonii can cause severe skin irritation, localized swelling, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. If the leaves are accidentally swallowed, individuals may experience nausea, abdominal cramping, or vomiting. Some people may also exhibit allergic reactions such as respiratory discomfort or hives upon exposure. Proper protective gear should be worn to prevent these adverse physiological responses.
