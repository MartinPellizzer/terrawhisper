What to know about Rubia cordifolia before using it medicinally

Rubia cordifolia, commonly known as Indian madder, is a medicinal herb that has been used to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and gastrointestinal disorders.

This herb has been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of anxiety, insomnia, and restlessness, as well as to support cardiovascular health by reducing inflammation and improving lipid profiles. The main therapeutic properties of Rubia cordifolia include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and immunomodulatory effects, which contribute to its health benefits. Bioactive compounds such as anthraquinones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids have been identified as the primary active constituents of this herb.

In traditional Ayurvedic and Chinese medicine, Rubia cordifolia is often prepared as a decoction or infusion, known as "madder tea," to leverage its medicinal properties and promote overall well-being.
