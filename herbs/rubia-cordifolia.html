Indian madder

Rubia cordifolia

Indian Madder (Rubia cordifolia) is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent and is known for its traditional use in treating skin disorders and digestive issues.

Botanical Identification

Indian Madder, scientifically known as Rubia cordifolia, belongs to the Rubiaceae family. Commonly called Kshudra in India and Red Sandal in parts of Southeast Asia, it is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas and South Asia. The plant has heart-shaped leaves, red flowers, and produces red-orange roots used in traditional medicine. It is also known as Rubia tinctorum in some herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Eudicots
  • Order: Rubiales
  • Family: Rubiaceae
  • Genus: Rubia
  • Species: Rubia cordifolia

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for digestive issues and as a mild sedative.

Decoction: Used for treating skin conditions and respiratory issues.

Tincture: Used for pain relief and as an antiseptic.

Safety Profile

Indian Madder is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. Prolonged use or high doses could lead to skin irritation or other adverse effects, so caution is advised.
