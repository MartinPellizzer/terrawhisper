Indian Madder (Rubia cordifolia)

Indian Madder (Rubia cordifolia) is a member of the Rubiaceae family, native to Southwest Asia, Eastern Europe, and Middle East. Traditionally, its roots, stems, and leaves have been used for decoctions, infusions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its tonic, sedative, and astringent actions, and has a long history of use in ayurvedic medicine, traditional chinese medicine, and european herbal medicine.

Perennial herbaceous plant.

Leaves
Smooth, ovate leaves with prominent midrib, upper surface reddish-brown, lower surface pale green, bearing distinct stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Small, solitary flowers with five petals, two lips, and two stamens, arranged in terminal clusters, exhibiting zygomorphic symmetry, with deep red to purple coloration and a prominent yellow staminode.
Stems
Erect, herbaceous, glabrous, with opposite branching, and bearing stipulate nodes.
