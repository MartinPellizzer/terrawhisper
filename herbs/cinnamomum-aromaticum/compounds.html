Cinnamon (Cinnamomum aromaticum) Compounds

Cinnamon contains several primary active compounds, including essential oil, which gives it its aromatic scent, and terpenoids, which contribute to its anti-inflammatory properties. Phenolic acids and flavonoids act as antioxidants, while coumarin has anticoagulant effects. Tannins and glycosides support digestive health, and saponins may enhance nutrient absorption.

Cinnamon (Cinnamomum aromaticum) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Cinnamon (Cinnamomum aromaticum) is a key active compound, containing cinnamaldehyde and other volatile compounds. It contributes to the plant's aromatic profile and has antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties. This oil is widely used in herbal medicine for its therapeutic benefits.

2. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Cinnamon (Cinnamomum aromaticum). They contribute to the plant's aromatic properties and have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. These compounds are responsible for many of the therapeutic benefits associated with cinnamon in herbal medicine.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Cinnamon (Cinnamomum aromaticum). It contributes to the plant's antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. This compound helps support digestive health and may aid in reducing oxidative stress. Its presence enhances the therapeutic potential of cinnamon in herbal medicine.

4. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Cinnamon (Cinnamomum aromaticum). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help support cardiovascular health and may aid in reducing oxidative stress. Flavonoids are among the key phytochemicals responsible for the therapeutic effects of cinnamon in traditional herbal medicine.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Cinnamon (Cinnamomum aromaticum). It contributes to the plant's aromatic profile and has been studied for its potential anti-inflammatory and anticoagulant properties. Coumarin is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its soothing and digestive benefits.

6. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Cinnamon (Cinnamomum aromaticum). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and has antimicrobial effects. Tannins help in reducing inflammation and may support digestive health. They are found in the bark and leaves of the cinnamon plant.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Cinnamon (Cinnamomum aromaticum). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, including antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Glycosides in cinnamon are often responsible for its therapeutic benefits in traditional herbal medicine.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Cinnamon (Cinnamomum aromaticum). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine due to its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponin may support digestive health and immune function, making it a key constituent in cinnamon's therapeutic applications.

9. Alkaloid

Cinnamon (Cinnamomum aromaticum) contains the alkaloid cinnamic acid. This compound contributes to the plant's aromatic properties and has been studied for its potential antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. It is commonly found in the bark and essential oils of the plant.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Cinnamon (Cinnamomum aromaticum). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. While not the primary marker compound in cinnamon, anthocyanin supports its overall therapeutic potential in traditional herbal medicine.
