Calophyllum membranaceum

What's the taxonomical classification of Calophyllum membranaceum?

Calophyllum membranaceum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows a specific evolutionary lineage that places it under the order Malpighiales. This plant is a representative of the family Calophyllaceae, falling within the genus Calophyllum, and is specifically identified by its species name, membranaceum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Calophyllum membranaceuum has elliptical to oblong leaves with an entire margin, a revolute edge, and a distinctively thin, membranous texture. The foliage is characterized by numerous fine, parallel secondary veins that extend from the midrib to the leaf margin. Its woody stems exhibit a smooth bark texture that transitions into robust branches as the plant matures. The flowers are typically small and white, emerging from the leaf axils in clusters. These botanical features collectively define the species' structural identity within its tropical habitat.
