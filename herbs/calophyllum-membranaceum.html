Calophyllum membranaceum

What's the taxonomical classification of Calophyllum membranaceum?

Calophyllum membranaceum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows a specific evolutionary lineage that places it under the order Malpighiales. This plant is a representative of the family Calophyllaceae, falling within the genus Calophyllum, and is specifically identified by its species name, membranaceum.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Calophyllum membranaceum contains bioactive phytochemicals including coumarins, triterpenoids, and various flavonoids that possess significant antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. These secondary metabolites are primarily concentrated within the plant's bark and leaves. Research suggests that specific compounds such as calophyllolide can exhibit cytotoxic effects against certain cancer cell lines. Additionally, the presence of polyphenols contributes to the plant's overall antioxidant capacity. These medicinal constituents make the species a subject of interest for developing new pharmacological treatments.

What are the therapeutic actions of Calophyllum membranaceum?

Calophyllum membranaceum has antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and wound-healing properties derived from its bioactive phytochemical constituents like coumarins and terpenoids. These chemical compounds work by inhibiting the growth of various pathogenic microorganisms and reducing swelling in affected tissues. The plant extracts are frequently applied topically to accelerate the regeneration of skin cells and promote faster closure of wounds. Furthermore, its ability to modulate inflammatory responses makes it useful in traditional medicinal practices for treating skin irritations. Researchers continue to study these specific mechanisms to better understand their potential in modern dermatological pharmacology.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, inflammatory conditions, wounds, and various fungal or bacterial ailments. Its medicinal properties are primarily derived from extracts used to soothe skin irritation and promote healing in open sores. The bioactive compounds within the plant exhibit significant antimicrobial activities that combat localized infections. Some traditional practices also employ its extracts to alleviate swelling and pain associated with dermatological issues. These therapeutic applications make it a valuable resource in various regional herbal medicine systems.
