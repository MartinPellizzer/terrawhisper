What's the taxonomical classification of Phegopteris decursive-pinnata?

Phegopteris decursive-pinnata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Polypodiales and the family Aspleniaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Phegopteris, and its unique species designation, decurse-pinnata.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, sores, inflammation, and various types of boils or carbuncles. The leaves are often prepared into a poultice to draw out toxins and promote the healing of external wounds. In traditional medicine, its topical application helps reduce swelling and soothe irritated skin surfaces. Some practitioners also utilize its properties to address minor dermatological ailments caused by heat or irritation. These medicinal uses focus primarily on the plant's ability to act as an anti-inflammatory agent.
