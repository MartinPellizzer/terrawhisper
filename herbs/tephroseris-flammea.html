What's the taxonomical classification of Tephroseris flammea?

Tephroseris flammea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tephroseris flammea has a woody, perennial habit characterized by erect, unbranched or sparsely branched stems that reach heights of up to 1 meter. During its flowering stage, the plant produces terminal heads of bright yellow discoid flowers arranged in corymbose cymes. Each flower head is supported by an involucre of overlapping bracts that protect the developing seeds. The resulting achenes are typically accompanied by a pappus of fine bristles which aids in wind dispersal.
