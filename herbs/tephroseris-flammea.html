Tephroseris flammea

What's the taxonomical classification of Tephroseris flammea?

Tephroseris flammea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae and is specifically categorized under the genus Tephroseris, with its unique biological identity defined by the species name flammea.

    Tephroseris flammea is prepared as an infusion, a decoction, or a poultice depending on the specific medicinal application required.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Tephroseris flammea can cause contact dermatitis, skin irritation, and allergic reactions when the plant comes into direct contact with human skin. The presence of specific chemical compounds or hairs on the plant may trigger redness, itching, or swelling in sensitive individuals. If the plant is ingested, it may lead to gastrointestinal distress such as nausea or stomach pain. Some people might also experience respiratory irritation if they inhale dust or pollen particles from the foliage. Careful handling is recommended to avoid these localized or systemic adverse effects.
