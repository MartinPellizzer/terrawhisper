Tansy (Seriphidium)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Seriphidium.

Tansy, scientifically known as Seriphidium, is a medicinal herb belonging to the Asteraceae family, renowned for its adaptogenic properties and historical use in traditional medicine. It is prized for its ability to support immune function, reduce inflammation, and promote digestive health, making it a valuable herb in holistic wellness practices. Traditionally, Tansy has been used in European folk medicine to treat ailments such as respiratory infections, skin conditions, and digestive disorders, often prepared as teas or tinctures. In modern wellness, it is increasingly incorporated into herbal supplements and natural remedies for its calming and detoxifying effects. One of its unique features is the presence of sesquiterpene lactones, which contribute to its potent medicinal properties, and its historical significance as a symbol of protection and healing in ancient cultures.
