Lemon Grass (Cymbopogon martini) Compounds

Lemon grass contains essential oil as its primary compound, rich in citral and limonene. Flavonoids like quercetin and terpenoids contribute to its aromatic properties. Phenolic acids and coumarin offer antioxidant and antimicrobial benefits. Tannins, saponins, and glycosides support its traditional use in herbal medicine.

Lemon Grass (Cymbopogon martini) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Lemon Grass (Cymbopogon martini) is a concentrated mixture of volatile compounds, including citral, myrcene, and geraniol. It is widely used in herbal medicine for its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and digestive properties. The oil is extracted through steam distillation and is valued for its therapeutic applications in aromatherapy and traditional remedies.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Lemon Grass (Cymbopogon martini). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. These compounds support the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for their potential health benefits.

3. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Lemon Grass (Cymbopogon martini). They contribute to the plant's aromatic profile and have various pharmacological activities, including antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. These compounds are essential for the plant's medicinal properties and therapeutic applications.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Lemon Grass (Cymbopogon martini). It contributes to the plant's antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. This compound is often extracted for use in herbal remedies. Its presence enhances the therapeutic potential of Lemon Grass in traditional medicine.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Lemon Grass (Cymbopogon martini). It contributes to the plant's aromatic profile and has mild anticoagulant properties. Coumarin may support cardiovascular health and aid in digestion when used as part of traditional herbal formulations.

6. Tannin

Tannin in Lemon Grass (Cymbopogon martini) contributes to its astringent properties and may support digestive health. It helps reduce inflammation and has antimicrobial effects, making it a valuable component in traditional herbal remedies for gastrointestinal issues.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Lemon Grass (Cymbopogon martini). It contributes to the plant's antifungal and antimicrobial properties. Saponin also supports digestive health by promoting bile secretion. Its presence enhances the overall therapeutic potential of Lemon Grass in herbal medicine.

8. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Lemon Grass (Cymbopogon martini). It contributes to the plant's aromatic properties and may support digestive and antimicrobial functions. Glycosides are often involved in the plant's therapeutic effects, enhancing its role in herbal medicine.

9. Alkaloid

Lemon Grass (Cymbopogon martini) contains the alkaloid naringin. This compound contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. It is commonly used in traditional herbal medicine to support digestive health and reduce symptoms of gastrointestinal disorders.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Lemon Grass (Cymbopogon martini). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports the plant's potential therapeutic effects in herbal medicine.
