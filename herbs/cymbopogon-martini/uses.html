Lemon Grass (Cymbopogon martini) Uses

Lemon grass is used to treat respiratory conditions such as asthma, bronchitis, and tuberculosis. It helps reduce inflammation in the throat and alleviates symptoms of cough and tonsillitis. It is also effective for managing bad breath and allergic rhinitis, and can support recovery from upper respiratory tract infections.

Lemon Grass (Cymbopogon martini) Monograph

1. Asthma

Lemon grass has been traditionally used to support respiratory health in herbal medicine. It contains compounds that may help reduce airway inflammation and ease breathing in asthma patients. Its mild antispasmodic properties can soothe bronchial spasms, making it a supportive remedy when used alongside conventional treatments.

2. Bad breath

Lemon grass is used to combat bad breath due to its antimicrobial properties. It helps neutralize oral bacteria that cause odor. Chewing fresh lemon grass or using it in mouthwashes can freshen breath naturally. Its essential oils have a cleansing effect on the mouth.

3. Cough

Lemon grass is used to soothe coughs due to its antispasmodic and expectorant properties. It helps reduce bronchial irritation and loosen mucus, making it easier to expel. Often prepared as a tea, it provides gentle relief for persistent or dry coughs. Its mild flavor makes it suitable for long-term use in herbal remedies.

4. Inflamed throat

Lemon grass has anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties that can soothe an inflamed throat. It is often used in herbal teas to reduce swelling and ease discomfort. Its essential oils may help combat bacterial infections contributing to throat irritation. Regular consumption of lemon grass tea can support healing and relief in mild cases of throat inflammation.

5. Bronchitis

Lemon grass is used in herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms of bronchitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. It helps reduce coughing and mucus production, supporting respiratory health. Often prepared as a tea, it soothes irritation in the bronchial passages and promotes easier breathing. Its essential oils also have a calming effect on the respiratory system.

6. Respiratory infections

Lemon grass contains compounds with antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce symptoms of respiratory infections. It supports immune function and can alleviate coughs and congestion when used in teas or steam inhalations. Its essential oils are often used in aromatherapy to ease breathing and clear airways.

7. Tonsillitis

Lemon grass has been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of tonsillitis due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce throat inflammation and combat bacterial infections. Infusions or steam inhalations with lemon grass can soothe pain and ease breathing. Its essential oils are often incorporated into herbal remedies for respiratory infections.

8. Allergic rhinitis

Lemon grass has been used in traditional medicine to alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistaminic properties. Its essential oils, particularly citral, help reduce nasal congestion and inhibit histamine release. Studies suggest it may provide a natural alternative for managing seasonal allergies and sinus inflammation.

9. Tuberculosis

Lemon grass has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health, including as a complementary therapy for tuberculosis. It contains compounds with antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that may help reduce symptoms and enhance the body's natural defenses against the disease.

10. Upper respiratory tract infection

Lemon grass has been traditionally used to support the treatment of upper respiratory tract infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It may help alleviate symptoms such as congestion and sore throat when used in teas or essential oils. Its compounds, including citral and myrcene, contribute to its therapeutic effects in respiratory health.
