Horticulturally, buchu is an easy-to-grow shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners. Botanically, buchu belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers and distinctive, citrusy fragrance.

With roots dating back centuries, buchu has been mentioned in various historical references, including ancient African folk medicine and European apothecary texts, where it was prized for its medicinal and culinary uses.
