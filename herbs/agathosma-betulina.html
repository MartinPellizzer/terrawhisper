Agathosma (Agathosma betulina) is a small shrub native to South Africa, known for its aromatic leaves and traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive conditions.

Morphology

Agathosma betulina is a shrub characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage, with small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color and have a finely hairy surface.

Distribution

Agathosma betulina is native to the Western Cape region of South Africa, specifically growing in the fynbos biome along the coastal and mountainous areas of the Overberg and Cape Peninsula.

Traditional Uses

Agathosma betulina has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported ability to support respiratory health, reduce inflammation, and promote digestion, often prepared as a tea or tincture.

Medicinal Systems

In traditional medicinal systems such as Ayurveda, Unani, and traditional Chinese medicine, Agathosma betulina has been utilized for its purported therapeutic properties, including anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory effects.

Agathosma betulina contains a variety of bioactive compounds, including flavonoids, phenolic acids, and essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Pharmacology

Agathosma betulina exhibits anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, making it effective in supporting respiratory health, reducing fever, and combating infections.

Botanical Identification

Agathosma betulina, commonly known as silver leaf agathosma, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is also referred to as Agathosma cuneifolia in some herbal texts. The plant features silver-gray leaves and yellow flowers, with a taproot system typical of many South African medicinal plants.
