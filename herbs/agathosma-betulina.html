Agathosma Betulina: What To Know Before Using It For Medicinal Purposes

Agathosma betulina, commonly known as buchu, is a traditional South African medicinal plant used for its aromatic and anti-inflammatory properties.

It has been historically utilized to treat respiratory conditions such as bronchitis and asthma due to its ability to act as a bronchodilator and expectorant. The plant contains essential oils rich in compounds like limonene and pinene, which contribute to its therapeutic effects. In modern herbal medicine, buchu is also used for digestive ailments and as a diuretic.

Its medicinal applications continue to be explored for potential pharmaceutical development, highlighting its significance in both traditional and contemporary health practices.

Side Effects

Agathosma betulina can have some side effects, such as gastrointestinal discomfort, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, especially when taken in high doses.

It may also cause allergic reactions in sensitive individuals, manifesting as skin rashes, itching, or respiratory symptoms. Prolonged use of Agathosma betulina might lead to liver toxicity, as some studies suggest potential hepatotoxic effects. Additionally, it may interact with certain medications, particularly those affecting the nervous system or blood pressure.

Therefore, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using this supplement, especially for long-term or therapeutic purposes.
