Lilium bakerianum

What's the taxonomical classification of Lilium bakerianum?

Lilium bakerianum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further organized into the order Liliales. It is a member of the family Liliaceae, falling under the genus Lilium, with the specific designation of the species bakerianum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lilium bakerianum has erect, slender stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters and bear several large, outward-facing, funnel-shaped flowers. The leaves are arranged in whorls or as alternate lanceolate blades along the stem, featuring a smooth texture and a deep green color. Its petals are characterized by a pale cream or yellowish hue, often marked with prominent dark spots or streaks near the throat. The reproductive structures include long filaments supporting anthers and a single central pistil that extends beyond the perianth. These morphological traits distinguish the species within its specific montane habitat.
