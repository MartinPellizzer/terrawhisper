Combretum latifolium

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Combretum latifolium has broad, elliptical to oblong leaves that measure significantly larger than those of its related species, typically featuring entire margins and a distinctively leathery texture. These leaves are arranged in an opposite or sub-opposite pattern along the woody stems of the shrub or small tree. The foliage often exhibits a deep green color on the upper surface with a slightly paler underside. The plant's inflorescences are characterized by small, clustered flowers that emerge from the axils of the leaves. Its overall structure is defined by a multi-stemmed growth habit with smooth to slightly fissured bark.
