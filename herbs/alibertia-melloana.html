Cupuaçu (Alibertia Melloana)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Alibertia melloana.

Cupuaçu, scientifically known as Alibertia melloana, is a tropical fruit native to the Amazon rainforest, often regarded as a medicinal herb and adaptogen due to its rich nutritional profile and bioactive compounds. It is particularly valued for its high content of antioxidants, vitamins, and flavonoids, which contribute to its primary benefits such as anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and immune-boosting properties. Traditionally, indigenous communities in the Amazon have used Cupuaçu for its calming effects, applying it topically to treat skin conditions and consuming it to alleviate stress and fatigue. In modern wellness practices, it is increasingly used in natural skincare products, dietary supplements, and as a functional ingredient in health-conscious beverages. One of its unique features is its distinctive sweet, creamy flavor, similar to a mix between chocolate and milk, and it contains a rare active compound called malic acid, which has been linked to potential metabolic benefits.
