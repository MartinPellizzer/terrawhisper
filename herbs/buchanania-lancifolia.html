Buchanania lancifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Buchanania lancifolia?

Buchanania lancifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Sapindales and the family Anacardiaceae. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Buchanina, and its species designation, lancifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Buchanania lancifolia has lanceolate leaves that are typically 5 to 15 centimeters long, narrow in shape, and possess entire margins. The foliage is characterized by a leathery texture and a distinctively pointed apex. Its woody stems exhibit a somewhat rough surface and serve as the structural support for the dense canopy. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves during the blooming season. The plant also develops small, fleshy fruits that follow the flowering stage.
