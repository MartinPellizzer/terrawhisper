Buchanania lancifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Buchanania lancifolia has lanceolate leaves that are typically 5 to 15 centimeters long, narrow in shape, and possess entire margins. The foliage is characterized by a leathery texture and a distinctively pointed apex. Its woody stems exhibit a somewhat rough surface and serve as the structural support for the dense canopy. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves during the blooming season. The plant also develops small, fleshy fruits that follow the flowering stage.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical regions of northern Australia, specifically spanning across the Kimberley region of Western Australia and the Top End of the Northern Territory. It primarily inhabits various coastal and inland habitats where seasonal moisture is available. Within these territories, it is often found growing in rocky outcrops or along the margins of seasonal watercourses. The distribution is largely dictated by the presence of monsoon forests and savanna woodlands. These specific environmental niches support its growth across its limited geographical range.
