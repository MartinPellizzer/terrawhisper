Bulbophyllum shweliense has creeping rhizomes that produce small, ovate to elliptic pseudobulbs spaced along the stem, each topped by a single, leathery, lanceolate leaf. The inflorescence is a relatively short, many-flowered raceme emerging from the base of the pseudobulbs. Each individual flower features a small, fleshy lip that is often uniquely shaped to attract specific pollinators. The petals and sepals are typically yellowish or greenish in hue, contributing to the plant's delicate appearance.
