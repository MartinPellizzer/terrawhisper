Bulbophyllum shweliense

What's the taxonomical classification of Bulbophyllum shweliense?

Bulbophyllum shweliense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Asparagales, the plant is a member of the family Orchidaceae. It is further defined by its placement within the genus Bulbophyllum, with its specific identity designated as the species shweliense.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, and various digestive ailments. The extracts from its rhizomes and leaves are frequently applied topically to manage dermatological issues. It also serves as a traditional remedy for soothing internal stomach discomfort and gastrointestinal distress. Local healers often prepare specific decoctions to address these diverse health concerns. Through these medicinal applications, the plant plays a significant role in regional folk medicine.
