Maoutia puya

What's the taxonomical classification of Maoutia puya?

Maoutia puya belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it falls under the order Rosales and is a member of the family Urticaceae. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Maoutia and the species puya.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Maoutia puya has a massive rosette of long, narrow, leathery leaves that can reach several meters in length and are armed with sharp marginal spines. These leaves are typically arranged in a dense spiral pattern around a central woody stem that grows slowly over time. From the center of the rosette, the plant produces a towering inflorescence consisting of a thick stalk topped with numerous small, yellowish flowers. The overall structure is designed to withstand harsh, arid environments while maximizing water retention within its thick foliage. As the plant matures, the sheer scale of its leaf arrangement and flowering spike creates a dramatic, architectural presence in its native habitat.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude Andean regions of central Peru, specifically concentrated within the departments of Huánuco, Pasco, and Junín. It thrives in the specialized ecosystem of the puna, typically occupying rocky slopes and puna grasslands at elevations ranging from 3,500 to 4,500 meters. The distribution is relatively restricted to these specific montane habitats where the climate remains cool and moist. Within these localized areas, the species exists in scattered populations adapted to the intense solar radiation and thin atmosphere. This limited range makes the plant a characteristic element of its specific high-altitude niche in the Peruvian Andes.
