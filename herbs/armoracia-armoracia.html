What are the medicinal properties of Armoracia armoracia?

The most commonly used parts of the horseradish plant for medicinal purposes are its roots and leaves, which are rich in bioactive compounds. The roots are typically dried or used fresh, while the leaves are often made into teas or tinctures.

Possible side effects of using Armoracia armoracia improperly include stomach upset, allergic reactions, and increased salivation. Consume excessive amounts can cause irritation to the mouth, throat, and digestive tract.

Common precautions when using horseradish medicinally include pregnant or breastfeeding women, as well as individuals with sensitive stomachs or allergies. Consult a healthcare professional before using horseradish to treat medical conditions, especially if taking medications or experiencing chronic health issues.
