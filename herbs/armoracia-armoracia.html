Garden Horseradish

Armoracia armoracia

Garden Horseradish (Armoracia armoracia) is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, known for its pungent root that has been used for centuries in traditional medicine to support digestive health and alleviate symptoms of respiratory and digestive conditions.

Botanical Identification

Armoracia armoracia, commonly known as garden horseradish, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its pungent root, trifoliate leaves, and yellow flowers. Synonyms include Armoracia rusticana and Brassica armoracia, reflecting its historical use in herbal medicine.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Spermatophyta
  • Class: caryophyllopsida
  • Order: Brassicales
  • Family: Brassicaceae
  • Genus: Armoracia
  • Species: Armoracia armoracia

Active Compounds

Garden horseradish contains key bioactive compounds like flavonoids, phenolic acids, and tannins. These compounds work synergistically to provide its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

Garden Horseradish Actions.

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory conditions due to its expectorant properties.

Infusion: Used for respiratory ailments such as coughs and bronchitis due to its soothing effects.

Powder: Used for digestive issues and as a mild stimulant due to its active compounds.

Safety Profile

Garden horseradish is generally safe when used in moderate amounts. However, it may cause gastrointestinal irritation in some individuals. It should be avoided by those with sensitive stomachs or ulcers. Prolonged use or high doses may lead to more severe side effects.
