Elephantopus scaber

What's the taxonomical classification of Elephantopus scaber?

Elephantopus scaber belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant follows the order Asterales and is a prominent member of the family Asteraceae. Specifically, it is identified under the genus Elephantopus, with its unique biological classification completed by the species epithet scaber.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Elephantopus scaber has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by a basal rosette of long, oblong, or lanceolate leaves that feature scabrid, serrated margins and a distinctively hairy surface. The leaves are typically dark green and possess prominent veins that contribute to their rugged texture. From this central rosette, the plant produces erect, unbranched flowering stalks that can reach significant heights. These stalks bear small, inconspicuous heads arranged in a discoid manner, which are typically dark purple or blackish in color. The entire plant is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough or bristly feel to the touch.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains 3-O-p-coumaroyl-4-O-caffeoyl quinic acid methyl ester, deoxyelephantopin, lupeol, scabertopin, and various sesquiterpene lactones. These specific bioactive molecules contribute to the complex chemical profile of the species. Lupeol and scabertopin are known for their potential biological activities in pharmacological research. The presence of deoxyelephantopin and sesquiterpene lactones often suggests significant medicinal properties. Additionally, 3-O-p-coumaroyl-4-O-caffeoyl quinic acid methyl ester provides unique antioxidant characteristics to the plant's composition.

As discussed in a study, two new phenolic acids, ethyl 3,3',4,4'-tetrahydroxy-delta-truxinate (1) and 3-O-p-coumaroyl-4-O-caffeoyl quinic acid methyl ester (2), were isolated from the whole plant of Elephantopus scaber Linn. along with three known compounds (3-5). Compound 3 was obtained and its NMR data was provided for the first time. All isolates were evaluated for anti-inflammatory activity by inhibiting nitric oxide production in LPS-stimulated murine macrophage RAW 264.7 cells. Compounds 1, 4, and 5 demonstrated moderate inhibition with IC50 values ranging from 11.85 to 20.62 uM.
