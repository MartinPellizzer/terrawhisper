Elephantopus scaber

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Elephantopus scaber has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by a basal rosette of long, oblong, or lanceolate leaves that feature scabrid, serrated margins and a distinctively hairy surface. The leaves are typically dark green and possess prominent veins that contribute to their rugged texture. From this central rosette, the plant produces erect, unbranched flowering stalks that can reach significant heights. These stalks bear small, inconspicuous heads arranged in a discoid manner, which are typically dark purple or blackish in color. The entire plant is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough or bristly feel to the touch.

What are the herbal preparations of Elephantopus scaber?

Elephantopus scaber is prepared as decoctions, infusions, powders, juices, and topical poultices for various medicinal applications. What side-effects this plant can have?

Elephantopus scaber can cause allergic skin reactions, gastrointestinal distress, or potential toxicity if consumed in excessive amounts without proper supervision. While many traditional practices use the plant for medicinal purposes, individual sensitivities can lead to localized rashes or itching upon contact. Ingesting large doses may disrupt digestive processes or lead to nausea and abdominal discomfort. There is currently limited clinical data regarding its long-term systemic effects on human organs. Consequently, cautious application is necessary to avoid unpredictable adverse responses.
