What are the side effects of Melaleuca cajuputi when used improperly?

When used improperly, Melaleuca cajuputi increases the chances of experiencing side effects, such as allergic reactions, skin irritation, and gastrointestinal upset.

Similarly, ingesting Melaleuca cajuputi in high concentrations or without proper medical supervision can lead to adverse effects on the digestive system, such as nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and stomach pain.

Moreover, if used for an extended period or in large quantities, the oil may accumulate in the body, causing systemic toxicity characterized by symptoms like headache, fatigue, and dizziness. Furthermore, improper use of Melaleuca cajuputi may also interact with other medications, such as blood thinners and antidepressants, leading to serious adverse reactions.

Therefore, it is essential to consult with a healthcare professional or qualified aromatherapist before using Melaleuca cajuputi for medicinal purposes to minimize the risk of experiencing these side effects.

Additionally, the tree is vulnerable to nematodes, which can cause root knot and stunt its growth. Finally, aphids, mealybugs, and scales can infest the tree, sucking sap and transmitting plant viruses.

These pests and diseases can be managed through integrated pest management strategies that include cultural practices, biological control agents, and chemical controls.
