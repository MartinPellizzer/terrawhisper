Pineapple (Ananas Sativus Var. Microstachys)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Ananas sativus var. microstachys.

Pineapple, scientifically known as Ananas sativus var. microstachys, is a tropical fruit with medicinal properties often regarded as a natural adaptogen and digestive aid. It contains bromelain, a unique enzyme that aids in digestion and reduces inflammation, making it beneficial for muscle recovery and respiratory conditions. Traditionally used by indigenous South American tribes for its healing properties, pineapple was also valued in traditional Chinese and Ayurvedic medicine for its detoxifying and anti-inflammatory effects. In modern wellness, it is incorporated into supplements and topical treatments for its skin-renewing and anti-aging benefits. Its distinctive flavor and rare active compounds, such as vitamin C and manganese, have made it a staple in both culinary and therapeutic applications throughout history.
