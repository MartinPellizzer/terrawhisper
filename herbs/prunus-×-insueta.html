Possible Side Effects of Prunus × insueta

Prunus × insueta can have side effects if used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, including nausea and diarrhea, due to its high concentration of amygdalin, a compound that releases cyanide when ingested.

In severe cases, excessive consumption of prunus × insueta can lead to cyanide poisoning, causing symptoms like headaches, dizziness, and respiratory distress.

Additionally, its seeds may also cause allergic reactions in some individuals, resulting in skin irritation and anaphylaxis in extreme cases.
