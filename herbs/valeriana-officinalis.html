What to know about Valeriana officinalis before using it medicinally

Valeriana officinalis, commonly known as valerian, is a medicinal herb that has been traditionally used to alleviate a range of health conditions, including anxiety, insomnia, restlessness, and stress-related disorders.

The main health benefits of valerian include its sedative, anxiolytic, and adaptogenic properties, which help to promote relaxation, reduce stress levels, and improve sleep quality. The herb's therapeutic properties are attributed to its ability to interact with the GABA receptors in the brain, leading to a calming effect and a reduction in anxiety and restlessness. The main bioactive compounds found in valerian include valepotriates, isovaleramide, and valerenic acid, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Valerian root is commonly used to make a herbal tea, which is a popular remedy for promoting relaxation and improving sleep quality.

According to a study published by "Pharmacology, biochemistry, and behavior", Valeriana officinalis has been found to have a significant sedative effect, particularly due to its sesquiterpenes content. The study revealed that the preparation containing primarily sesquiterpenes resulted in 44% of participants reporting perfect sleep and 89% reporting improved sleep. Additionally, this preparation was shown to have no side effects, making it a potentially beneficial remedy for individuals struggling with poor sleep.
