Habenaria glaucifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Habenaria glaucifolia?

Habeanaria glaucifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asparagales. This plant is further categorized into the family Orchidaceae, specifically within the genus Habeanaria, and is identified by its unique species name, glaucifolia.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various skin conditions, including sores, wounds, and fungal infections. The tubers are often prepared in traditional medicinal practices to address localized inflammation and dermatological ailments. Some practitioners also utilize the plant to manage internal issues related to digestive discomfort. Its chemical properties are believed to provide relief for minor abrasions when applied topically. This species remains a significant component in specific regional herbal remedies for managing superficial injuries.
