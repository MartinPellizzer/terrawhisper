Iris wattii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Iris wattii has rhizomatous roots that produce long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves and tall flowering stalks bearing large, pale lilac to white blossoms with distinctive yellow signals. The leaves are typically linear and upright, reaching significant heights to support the plant's robust structure. Each flower features three upright petals known as standards and three drooping petals called falls, which are often marked by intricate veining. The plant's stem is sturdy enough to hold multiple flowers simultaneously during its blooming period. These morphological features collectively distinguish this species within its botanical classification.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

Within these habitats, the species is typically located in moist, shaded areas such as forest margins and ravines. The distribution follows the specific climatic requirements of the subtropical and temperate zones of the Himalayas.
