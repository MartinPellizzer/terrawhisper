Ficus formosana

What's the taxonomical classification of Ficus formosana?

Ficus formosana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and belongs to the order Rosales. This plant is a member of the family Moraceae and is specifically identified within the genus Ficus, with its unique designation being the species formosana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ficus formosana has an evergreen growth habit characterized by woody stems, elliptical to ovate leaves with acute tips, and small, inconspicuous greenish fruits. The leaves are typically leathery in texture and possess a glossy upper surface with prominent pinnate venation. Its branching pattern can vary from a spreading shrub to a small tree depending on the environmental conditions. The plant often develops aerial roots that descend from the branches to provide additional support and nutrient uptake. These structural features allow the species to thrive in various subtropical habitats.
