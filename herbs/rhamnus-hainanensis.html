What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhamnus hainanensis has opposite, elliptical to oblong leaves that are typically 3 to 7 centimeters long with entire or slightly serrated margins and a lustrous green surface. The stems are woody and often feature small, inconspicuous lenticels along their length. Its inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils during the growing season. The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that transitions in color as it matures. This shrubby species generally exhibits a compact growth habit suited to its specific habitat.
