What's the taxonomical classification of Pittosporum illicioides?

Pittosporum illicioides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its hierarchical lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Apiales. At the familial level, it is a member of the Pittosporaceae family, which contains the genus Pittosporum. Ultimately, the specific identification of this plant is defined by its species name, illicioides.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Pittosporum illicioides can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if the plant parts are ingested or touched. If any part of the plant is swallowed, it may lead to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. There is also a risk of mucosal irritation if the sap comes into contact with the eyes or mouth. Proper precautions should be taken to avoid direct handling to prevent these adverse reactions.
