Corydalis incisa

What's the taxonomical classification of Corydalis incisa?

Corydalis incisa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Ranunculales. This plant is a member of the family Papaveraceae, falling specifically within the genus Corydalis, with its unique biological identity defined by the species incisa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Corydalis incisa has deeply lobed, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette and stem-clasping patterns. The foliage typically exhibits a delicate, dissected appearance with narrow, linear segments. Its flowers are zygomorphic and feature a long, slender nectar spur that extends backward from the corolla. These blooms are generally pale blue or violet in color and emerge from the leaf axils. The plant also develops small, capsule-like seed pods that follow the flowering stage.
