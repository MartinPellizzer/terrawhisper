Tithonia diversifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Tithonia diversifolia?

Tithonia diversifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further classified under the order Asterales. As a member of the family Asteraceae, this plant is placed within the genus Tithonia, with its specific designation being the species diversifolia.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Tithonia diversifolia contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, terpenoids, and steroidal glycosides. These specific chemical constituents contribute to the plant's recognized pharmacological potential in treating inflammation and infections. Researchers often focus on the presence of sesquiterpene lactones which may exhibit significant cytotoxic properties against certain cell lines. The combination of these organic compounds suggests that the plant may serve as a source for developing new antimicrobial or anti-inflammatory agents. Studies continue to investigate how these individual molecules interact to provide therapeutic benefits in traditional medicine.

What are the therapeutic actions of Tithonia diversifolia?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, ulcers, and various inflammatory conditions. It contains bioactive compounds that exhibit significant antimicrobial and wound-healing properties. Local applications of its extracts are often employed to manage fungal infections and minor cutaneous injuries. Researchers have also explored its potential for mitigating inflammatory responses in localized tissue. The plant's medicinal value is primarily centered on its ability to promote tissue regeneration and combat pathogens.
