Desmos grandifolius

What's the taxonomical classification of Desmos grandifolius?

Desmos grandifolius belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is positioned in the subclass Magnoliidae and follows the order Magnoliales. The plant is a member of the family Annonaceae, falling under the genus Desmos, and is specifically identified by the species name grandifolius.

What are the pharmacological activities of Desmos grandifolius?

This plant has anti-tumor pharmacological activity that allows it to inhibit the growth and proliferation of malignant cells. Research into its chemical constituents suggests that specific bioactive compounds play a crucial role in this anticancer process. These substances work by interfering with the metabolic pathways required for tumor development. By targeting cellular irregularities, the plant shows significant potential for further medicinal investigation. Future studies aim to isolate these specific molecules to better understand their mechanism of action against various cancers.

As discussed in a study, researchers investigated new compounds with anti-tumor activities from the root of Desmos grandifolius. Six compounds were isolated from the CHCl3 extract of this plant and identified as lawinal(I), isolawinal(II), desmethoxymatteucinol(III), benzoic acid(IV), beta-sitosterol(V) and sitgmasterol (VI). All 6 of these compounds were found from this plant for the first time. Additionally, 3 dihydroflavonoids isolated from this species were characterized by A-ring substitution and no B-ring substitution.
