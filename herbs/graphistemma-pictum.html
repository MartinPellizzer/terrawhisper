      Graphistemma pictum is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a concentrated tincture derived from its dried leaves and stems.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Graphistemma pictum can cause skin irritation, digestive upset, and potential allergic reactions when handled or ingested. Individuals may experience redness or itching if the plant's sap comes into direct contact with sensitive areas of the skin. Ingesting any part of the specimen could lead to nausea, abdominal pain, or more severe gastrointestinal distress. Some people might also develop respiratory issues or hives if they are hypersensitive to its chemical compounds. It is essential to exercise caution and avoid skin contact to minimize these adverse effects.
