Clematis sibirica

What's the taxonomical classification of Clematis sibirica?

Clematis sibirica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Ranunculales. As a member of the family Ranunculaceae, this plant is further categorized into the genus Clematis, specifically identifying as the species sibirica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clematis sibirica has woody, climbing stems that produce small, pale violet to bluish flowers with four to six tepals and prominent yellowish stamens. The leaves are typically compound, consisting of three to five leaflets that are ovate or lanceolate in shape. These leaflets possess finely serrated margins and a smooth, slightly pubescent texture on the undersides. During the fruiting stage, the plant develops distinctive feathery, achene-bearing styles that aid in wind dispersal. The overall growth habit is that of a perennial vine that can spread across forest floors or climb through surrounding vegetation.
