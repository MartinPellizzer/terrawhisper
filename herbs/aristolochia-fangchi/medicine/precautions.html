10 Precautions To Take When Using Aristolochia Fangchi (Fang Chi)

Aristolochia fangchi has some precautions to consider before using it medicinally, such as avoiding long-term use and high doses.

The herb contains aristolochic acid, a known nephrotoxin, which can cause kidney damage and lead to kidney failure if taken excessively.

If not taken carefully, fang chi can cause side effects like kidney stones, kidney disease, and even kidney transplant.

This article explains in details the 10 most important precautions to take when using Aristolochia fangchi medicinally.

2. Follow precise preparation guidelines

When using Aristolochia fangchi medicinally, it's important to avoid during pregnancy.

This is crucial because the herb contains aristolochic acid, which has been linked to a high risk of miscarriage and birth defects. Additionally, Aristolochia fangchi can increase uterine contractions, potentially leading to preterm labor or even spontaneous abortion.

Therefore, it's essential for pregnant women to consult with their healthcare provider before using this herb, as the potential risks far outweigh any potential benefits during this critical period of fetal development.
