Japanese Angelica (Angelica japonica) Uses

Japanese Angelica is used to treat common cold, respiratory infections, and nasal congestion. It helps alleviate cough and cold symptoms by supporting respiratory function. It also addresses skin infections, inflammation, and allergies, and may reduce symptoms of rheumatoid arthritis due to its anti-inflammatory properties.

Japanese Angelica (Angelica japonica) Monograph

1. Common cold

Japanese Angelica is used to alleviate symptoms of the common cold due to its expectorant and antipyretic properties. It helps reduce fever, ease coughing, and relieve nasal congestion. Traditionally, it is often combined with other herbs to enhance its effectiveness in treating respiratory infections. Its warming nature is believed to support the body's natural healing processes during colds.

2. Asthma

Japanese Angelica (Angelica japonica) has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health. It is believed to help alleviate asthma symptoms by reducing inflammation and promoting bronchodilation. Its essential oils and root compounds may act as natural bronchial stimulants, aiding in easier breathing. This makes it a valued herb in traditional Japanese herbal practices for respiratory conditions.

3. Cough

Japanese Angelica is used to alleviate coughs by reducing inflammation in the respiratory tract and loosening mucus. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to soothe irritation and ease breathing. Its expectorant properties help clear the airways, making it a traditional remedy for both dry and productive coughs.

4. Respiratory infections

Japanese Angelica (Angelica japonica) is used in traditional herbal medicine to support respiratory health. It is valued for its antispasmodic and expectorant properties, which help alleviate symptoms of respiratory infections such as coughing and bronchitis. Its active compounds may reduce inflammation and promote mucus clearance in the airways.

5. Nasal congestion

Japanese Angelica is used to alleviate nasal congestion due to its anti-inflammatory and decongestant properties. It helps reduce swelling in the nasal passages and promotes clear breathing. Often prepared as a tea or tincture, it is a common remedy in traditional Japanese herbal medicine for respiratory discomfort.

6. Cold symptoms

Japanese Angelica is used to alleviate cold symptoms by reducing fever and easing nasal congestion. It supports respiratory health and may help relieve headaches and body aches associated with colds. Its antispasmodic properties can soothe coughing and improve overall recovery.

7. Skin infections

Japanese Angelica (Angelica japonica) is used topically to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The plant contains compounds that inhibit bacterial growth and reduce inflammation, making it effective for conditions like eczema and minor wounds. It is often applied as a poultice or infused oil to promote healing and prevent infection.

8. Skin inflammation

Japanese Angelica is used to treat skin inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and irritation associated with conditions like eczema and dermatitis. Its active compounds promote skin healing and prevent infection in inflamed areas.

9. Skin allergies

Japanese Angelica (Angelica japonica) is used in traditional herbal medicine to alleviate skin allergies due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties. It helps reduce redness, itching, and swelling associated with allergic reactions. The herb's active compounds modulate immune responses, making it a valuable treatment for eczema and contact dermatitis.

10. Rheumatoid arthritis

Japanese Angelica (Angelica japonica) has been used in traditional herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms of rheumatoid arthritis. Its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties help reduce joint pain and swelling. It is often prepared as a decoction or tincture to support long-term management of the condition.
