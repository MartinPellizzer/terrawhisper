Japanese Angelica (Angelica japonica) Compounds

Japanese Angelica (Angelica japonica) Monograph

1. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Japanese Angelica (Angelica japonica). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating digestive issues and pain. Coumarin's antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties support its role in herbal medicine. It also acts as a mild sedative and may aid in reducing stress.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Japanese Angelica (Angelica japonica) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, supporting the plant's traditional use in treating digestive and respiratory ailments. They also enhance the plant's overall therapeutic profile in herbal medicine.

3. Essential oil

The essential oil of Japanese Angelica (Angelica japonica) contains bioactive compounds like ligustilide and butylidenephthalide. These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating digestive and respiratory ailments. The oil is often extracted through steam distillation and is valued for its aromatic and therapeutic properties.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Japanese Angelica (Angelica japonica). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound is involved in the therapeutic effects of the herb, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Japanese Angelica (Angelica japonica). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by supporting digestive health and reducing inflammation. Glycosides in this herb are known for their role in promoting circulation and alleviating symptoms of gastrointestinal disorders.

6. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Japanese Angelica (Angelica japonica). They contribute to the plant's therapeutic properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. These compounds are often responsible for the plant's aromatic profile and are studied for their potential health benefits in traditional and modern herbal medicine.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Japanese Angelica (Angelica japonica). It exhibits anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, contributing to the plant's traditional use in treating digestive and respiratory ailments. Saponin also enhances the absorption of other bioactive compounds in the plant.

8. Tannin

Tannin in Japanese Angelica (Angelica japonica) contributes to its astringent properties and potential anti-inflammatory effects. It helps in reducing swelling and may support digestive health. Tannins are also believed to aid in detoxification and enhance the plant's therapeutic applications in traditional herbal medicine.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloids in Japanese Angelica (Angelica japonica) are bioactive compounds known for their pharmacological properties. These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating inflammatory and respiratory conditions. They interact with various biological targets, enhancing the plant's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Japanese Angelica (Angelica japonica). It contributes to the plant's vibrant color and has antioxidant properties that support immune function and may reduce inflammation. This compound is also associated with traditional uses of the plant in herbal medicine.
