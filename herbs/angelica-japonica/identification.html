Botanical Identification of Japanese Angelica (Angelica japonica)

To identify Japanese Angelica (Angelica japonica) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Japanese Angelica (Angelica japonica) belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Apiales, family Apiaceae, genus Angelica, and species Angelica japonica. These classifications place it within the flowering plants, specifically in the carrot family, and highlight its botanical relationships and characteristics.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Japanese Angelica is Angelica japonica, first described by the Swedish botanist Carl Peter Thunberg in 1794. It is also known by several synonyms, including Angelica acutiloba and Angelica formosana, reflecting its classification and geographic distribution. Common names include Japanese Angelica, Japanese Angelica root, and Tōng jiāng. Regional names vary, with terms like "Tōng jiāng" used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Morphological Characteristics

Japanese Angelica (Angelica japonica) is a biennial herb with a taproot system. It has a straight, hollow stem that is often reddish-purple. The leaves are large, pinnately compound, with serrated margins. The inflorescence is a compound umbel, with small white to greenish flowers. The fruit is a dry, two-lobed berry containing tiny seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Japanese Angelica can be distinguished by its distinctive white, hollow stems with purple spots, large compound leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of small greenish-yellow flowers. It differs from similar species like Angelica acutiloba by having smaller leaves and a more compact growth habit. The presence of a strong, aromatic odor when crushed is another key identifying feature.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Japanese Angelica is native to East Asia, particularly Japan, Korea, and China. It thrives in temperate climates with cool summers and mild winters. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found at altitudes between 500 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Japanese Angelica blooms from July to August, with seed formation occurring in late summer to early autumn. Harvesting for identification purposes is typically done in late summer when the plant is fully mature and the seeds are dry and ready for collection. The timing aligns with its natural growth cycle and aids in accurate botanical identification.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Japanese Angelica (Angelica japonica) is closely related to other members of the Apiaceae family, particularly species like Angelica acutiloba and Angelica archangelica. It shares key morphological and genetic traits with its genus, including umbellate inflorescences and aromatic compounds. Its phylogenetic placement reflects its evolutionary ties to other Apiaceae plants, which helps in understanding its medicinal properties and ecological role.
