Torenia violacea

What's the taxonomical classification of Torenia violacea?

Torenia violacea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Lamiales and belongs to the family Linderniaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Torenia, and its specific species epithet, violacea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Torenia violacea has low-growing, herbaceous stems that produce ovate, crenate leaves with a soft, pubescent texture and a deep green color. The foliage typically grows in a mounding habit, forming dense clusters of vegetation. Its most striking feature is the zygomorphic flowers, which exhibit a trumpet-like shape and come in shades of violet, purple, or blue. Each bloom is characterized by a prominent yellow throat that serves as a nectar guide for pollinators. The delicate petals are often marked with subtle darker veins that enhance their visual complexity.
