What's the taxonomical classification of Iris wilsonii?

Iris wilsonii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Asparagales. Further taxonomic refinement places it in the family Iridaeceae, within the genus Iris, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species wilsonii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Iris wilsonii has erect, sword-shaped leaves that are ensiform in form and grow from a thick, creeping rhizome. The foliage is characterized by a glaucous green color and a distinct parallel venation pattern. Its flowering structure features large, fragrant blossoms with falls that are typically pale blue or violet with yellow signals. The petals are delicately textured and emerge from a sturdy, branched stem that rises above the leafy base. This species displays a robust growth habit suited for its natural woodland habitats.
