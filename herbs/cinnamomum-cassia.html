Chinese cinnamon

Cinnamomum cassia

Chinese Cinnamon (Cinnamomum cassia) is a flowering plant native to China, widely used in traditional medicine for its aromatic bark, which is known to improve circulation, reduce inflammation, and support digestive health.

Botanical Identification

Chinese Cinnamon, or Cinnamomum cassia, is a member of the Lauraceae family. It is also known as Chinese cinnamon bark or cassia. This evergreen shrub or small tree is native to southern China and Southeast Asia. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and fragrant, while its flowers are small and clustered. Synonyms include Cinnamomum aromaticum and Cinnamomum wilsonii in traditional herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Magnoliopsida
  • Order: Laurales
  • Family: Lauraceae
  • Genus: Cinnamomum
  • Species: Cinnamomum cassia

Active Compounds

Chinese Cinnamon contains essential oil, coumarin, and flavonoids as its main active compounds. These components work synergistically to exert their therapeutic effects, with essential oil contributing to anti-inflammatory properties and coumarin offering anticoagulant benefits. Flavonoids enhance the herb's antioxidant activity.

  • Essential oil
  • Coumarin
  • Flavonoid

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory infections.

Infusion: Used for colds and flu symptoms.

Culinary use: Used for flavoring food and beverages.

Safety Profile

Chinese Cinnamon (Cinnamomum cassia) is generally safe when used in typical medicinal doses. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy due to potential risks of stimulating uterine contractions. Long-term use may also lead to liver toxicity, necessitating caution and professional guidance.
