Cinnamomum cassia

What's the taxonomical classification of Cinnamomum cassia?

Cinnamomum cassia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Laurales and the family Lauraceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Cinnamomum, and its species name, cassia.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Cinnamomum cassia contains high concentrations of cinnamaldehyde, cinnamyl acetate, eugenol, and various polyphenolic compounds like proanthocyanidins. Cinnamaldehyde is the primary component responsible for the plant's distinct aroma and its potent antimicrobial properties. The presence of eugenol provides significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects within biological systems. These bioactive molecules work together to help regulate blood glucose levels and support digestive health. Additionally, the complex mixture of essential oils contributes to the plant's ability to inhibit the growth of specific bacteria and fungi.

What are the therapeutic actions of Cinnamomum cassia?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat diabetes, digestive issues, respiratory infections, and inflammation. Its high concentration of cinnamaldehyde helps regulate blood sugar levels by improving insulin sensitivity. It also serves as an effective remedy for nausea, bloating, and abdominal pain due to its carminative properties. Furthermore, the antioxidant and antimicrobial qualities of the bark assist in fighting common colds and reducing systemic swelling. These medicinal applications make it a staple in various traditional healing practices around the world.
