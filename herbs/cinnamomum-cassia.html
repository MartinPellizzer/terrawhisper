Chinese Cinnamon (Cinnamomum cassia)

Chinese Cinnamon (Cinnamomum cassia) is a member of the Lauraceae family, native to Southeast Asia, China, and India. Traditionally, its bark, leaves, and resin have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, tonic, and carminative actions, and has a long history of use in traditional chinese medicine, japanese kampo medicine, and ayurvedic medicine.

Perennial herbaceous plant.

Leaves
Bilaterally symmetrical leaves with dorsiventral coloration, upper surface reddish-brown and lower surface pale green, bearing prominent stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Inflorescences are terminal spikes bearing small, yellowish-green actinomorphic flowers with five ovate petals and five elliptic sepals, arranged in a dense cluster.
Stems
Smooth, glabrous, woody stems with opposite branching, exhibiting a characteristic reddish-brown bark that becomes fissured with age.
