Alcea rosea

What's the taxonomical classification of Alcea rosea?

Alcea rosea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Malvales. This plant is a member of the family Malvaceae, which contains its specific genus, Alcea, and its distinct species, rosea.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Alcea rosea contains various bioactive compounds including mucilage, flavonoids, tannins, and polysaccharides. The high concentration of mucilage provides soothing properties that are often used to treat mucous membrane inflammation. Flavonoids within the plant act as potent antioxidants that help reduce oxidative stress in cells. Tannins contribute astringent qualities that can assist in healing minor skin irritations or digestive issues. These diverse chemical constituents make the plant a subject of interest for traditional and modern medicinal applications.

What are the therapeutic actions of Alcea rosea?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat ailments affecting the respiratory tract, such as coughs, sore throats, and bronchitis. The mucilage present in the plant acts as a demulcent to soothe irritated mucous membranes. It is also frequently utilized to calm digestive inflammation and treat mild gastrointestinal distress. Historically, preparations of the plant have helped alleviate symptoms of inflammation in various bodily tissues. These medicinal properties are primarily attributed to the plant's high polysaccharide content.
