Chinese Holly (Alcea rosea)

Chinese Holly (Alcea rosea) is a member of the Malvaceae family, native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. Traditionally, its flowers, seeds, and leaves have been used for infusions, decoctions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, expectorant, and diuretic actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, traditional chinese medicine, and mediterranean herbal traditions.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Annual herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically reaches a height of 1 to 2 meters.
Leaves
Ovate leaves with upper surface glaucous green and lower surface pale green, bearing distinct stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Racemose inflorescence with actinomorphic flowers featuring five white petals and five yellow stamens, arranged in a single whorl around a central pistil.
Stems
Woody, erect growth habit with simple branching, smooth, glabrous surface, and opposite phyllotaxis.
