Ranunculus tanguticus

What's the taxonomical classification of Ranunculus tanguticus?

Ranunculus tanguticus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Ranunculales. This plant is a member of the family Ranunculaceae and falls under the genus Ranunculus, ultimately identified by the specific epithet tanguticus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ranunculus tangutiicus has perennial rhizomes that produce basal leaves which are long-petioled, kidney-shaped or heart-shaped, and possess crenate or serrated margins. The flowering stems arise from these bases and typically reach heights of 10 to 40 centimeters. Small, yellow, actinomorphic flowers appear at the terminal ends of the stems, characterized by five distinct petals and multiple stamens. These blossoms are often accompanied by smaller, inconspicuous bracts located along the upper portion of the stem. The overall foliage remains relatively low to the ground, forming dense clumps in its natural habitat.
