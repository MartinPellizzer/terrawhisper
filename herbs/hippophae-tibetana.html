What's the taxonomical classification of Hippophae tibetana?

Hippophae tibetana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized under the order Rosales. The plant is a member of the family Elaeagnaceae and is identified by the genus Hippophae, specifically falling under the species tibetana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hippophae tibetana has deciduous, lanceolate leaves that are typically 3 to 6 centimeters long and feature silvery-gray undersides due to dense, fine scales. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in clusters along the branches before the development of bright orange or yellow berries. These fleshy drupes are the most distinctive morphological feature, hanging in heavy clusters from the woody stems.
