Arundo formosana

What's the taxonomical classification of Arundo formosana?

Arundo formosana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further falls into the order Poales and is a member of the family Poaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic designation is within the genus Arundo, with the species name being formosanа.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Arundo formosana has tall, perennial, reed-like stems that reach heights of up to 5 meters and are characterized by thick, cylindrical culms with distinct nodes and internodes. These stems are hollow and possess a fibrous texture that provides structural strength to the plant. The leaves are long, lanceolate, and arranged alternately along the upper portion of the stalks. Each leaf blade can extend significantly in length with a prominent midrib running through the center. Dense clumps of these plants are formed through an extensive underground rhizome system that allows for rapid vegetative spreading.
