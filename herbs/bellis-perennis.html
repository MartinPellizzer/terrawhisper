Bellis perennis

What's the taxonomical classification of Bellis perennis?

Bellis perennis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchy through the order Asterales, the plant is a member of the family Asteraceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by the genus Bellis and the species pereennis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Bellis perennis contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including flavonoids, tannins, triterpenes, and essential oils that contribute to its therapeutic properties. The presence of these phenolic compounds provides significant antioxidant activity that helps protect cells from oxidative stress. Specific triterpenoids within the plant are associated with anti-inflammatory effects, making it useful in traditional remedies for skin irritation. Additionally, the tannins present in the leaves and stems act as mild astringents to help soothe minor wounds. These diverse chemical constituents work together to support the plant's historical use in treating digestive and topical ailments.

What are the therapeutic actions of Bellis perennis?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin inflammations, minor wounds, gastrointestinal upsets, and respiratory ailments. Its medicinal properties stem from compounds that act as mild anti-inflammatory and antiseptic agents. When applied topically, the plant can help soothe irritated skin or promote the healing of small cuts. In traditional herbalism, decoctions of the plant are sometimes consumed to calm stomach discomfort. Some practitioners also use it to help alleviate symptoms of coughs and mild congestion.
