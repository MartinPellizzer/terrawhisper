Side Effects of bellis perennis

Bellis perennis induces allergic reaction in some individuals, manifesting as symptoms such as runny nose, sneezing, and itchy eyes.

It can also cause skin irritation and rash, leading to redness and a burning sensation on the skin's surface. The herb may result in nausea and digestive upset, including stomach cramps and an upset stomach. Additionally, it can induce throat irritation, making swallowing difficult and causing a dry or sore throat.

Prolonged or excessive use of Bellis perennis may lead to more severe reactions, such as hives or even systemic allergic responses, emphasizing the need for caution and consultation with a healthcare professional before use.
