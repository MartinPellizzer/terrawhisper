English Daisy

Bellis perennis

English Daisy (Bellis perennis) is a common flowering plant native to Europe, known for its white petals and yellow center, and has been used for centuries in traditional medicine to treat various ailments, including skin conditions and inflammation.

Botanical Identification

Bellis perennis is the scientific name for English Daisy, belonging to the Asteraceae family. Commonly known as English Daisy, it is a perennial herb native to Europe. The plant has basal leaves and yellow flowers with white ray florets. It is also referred to as Bachelor's Buttons in some herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Magnoliopsida
  • Order: Asterales
  • Family: Asteraceae
  • Genus: Bellis
  • Species: Bellis perennis

English Daisy Identification.

Active Compounds

The English Daisy contains key active compounds such as flavonoids, coumarins, and phenolic acids. These compounds work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. They contribute to its traditional use in herbal medicine for various health benefits.

  • Flavonoid
  • Coumarin
  • Phenolic acid

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory and digestive ailments due to its mild sedative and anti-inflammatory properties.

Tincture: Used for pain relief and inflammation due to its concentrated active compounds.

Extract: Used for skin conditions and wound healing due to its high concentration of beneficial compounds.

Safety Profile

English Daisy is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause mild gastrointestinal upset in some individuals. It is contraindicated in people with known allergies to plants in the Asteraceae family. Always consult a healthcare professional before use, especially during pregnancy or breastfeeding.
