Sedum elatinoides

What's the taxonomical classification of Sedum elatinoides?

Sedum elaetinoides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is placed in the order Saxifrages (Saxifragales) and belongs to the family Crasulaceae. Finally, it is identified by its genus, Sedum, and its specific species designation, elatinoides.

    What are the herbal preparations of Sedum elatinoides?

    Sedum elaatinoides is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste depending on the intended therapeutic application. The plant material is typically boiled in water to extract its soluble chemical constituents for internal consumption. For more delicate parts of the plant, a hot water infusion allows for a gentler extraction of active compounds. When used to treat external skin conditions, the crushed succulent leaves are applied directly to the affected area as a fresh paste. These various methods ensure that the specific properties of the plant are utilized effectively for different medicinal needs.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Sedum elaetinoides can cause skin irritation and potential digestive distress if ingested. While specific clinical studies on humans are limited, many members of the Sedum genus contain compounds that may act as mild irritants to sensitive tissues. Contact with the plant's sap might lead to localized redness, itching, or a rash on the skin. If consumed in significant quantities, it may trigger nausea, vomiting, or stomach cramps. It is always best to handle this plant with caution and avoid eating any part of it.
