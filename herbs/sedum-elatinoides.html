Sedum elatinoides

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sedum elaatinoides has succulent, cylindrical to slightly flattened leaves that are arranged in a dense rosette and possess a smooth, glaucous surface. These fleshy leaves are typically grayish-green or bluish in color, allowing the plant to store moisture effectively in arid environments. The stems are often trailing or creeping, which helps the species spread across rocky substrates. Small, star-shaped flowers emerge from the center of the rosettes, displaying delicate petals that contrast with the thick foliage. This compact growth habit ensures the plant remains protected from intense sunlight and desiccation.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes mountains in South America, specifically within Peru and Bolivia. It thrives in specialized alpine environments known as the puna ecosystem. These habitats are characterized by rocky terrain and extreme elevations where the plant can withstand intense solar radiation. Its distribution is restricted to these specific montane zones rather than being widespread across the continent. Localized populations are often found clinging to crevices in mountain slopes.
