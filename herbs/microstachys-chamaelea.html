Microstachys chamaelea

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Microstachys chamaelea has a woody, shrubby growth habit characterized by slender, erect stems and small, elliptical to lanceolate leaves that are typically dark green and leathery. The leaves are arranged oppositely along the branches and possess entire margins with slightly revolute edges. Its inflorescences consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils in compact clusters. These flowers are often pale or greenish in color and lack showy petals. The plant's overall structure is compact, allowing it to thrive in rocky or scrubby environments.
