Botanical Identification of Pueraria Mirifica (Pueraria mirifica)

To identify Pueraria Mirifica (Pueraria mirifica) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Pueraria mirifica belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Fabales, order Fabales, family Fabaceae, genus Pueraria, and species Pueraria mirifica. It is a member of the legume family and is known for its medicinal properties.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Pueraria mirifica is the accepted scientific name for this plant, first described by the botanist W. H. de Vries in 1898. It is also known by several synonyms, including Pueraria thomsonii and Pueraria hirsuta, which reflect historical taxonomic classifications. Common names such as "Kan Yaak" and "Fetish root" are used in various regions, particularly in Thailand and Malaysia.

Morphological Characteristics

Pueraria mirifica is a climbing legume with a robust root system and cylindrical, hairy stems. Its pinnately compound leaves are odd, with 9-15 pairs of oval leaflets. The inflorescence is a raceme of yellow flowers with purple veins. The fruit is a hairy pod containing 2-4 elliptical seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Pueraria mirifica can be distinguished by its unique combination of features, including its climbing habit, heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins, and distinctive yellow flowers. It also has thick, swollen roots and a strong, bitter aroma. These characteristics help differentiate it from other species in the Pueraria genus.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Pueraria Mirifica is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained, fertile soils and is typically found at altitudes between 200 and 1,000 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Pueraria mirifica blooms from March to May, with seed formation occurring between July and October. Harvesting is typically done during the dry season, from November to February, when the plant's active compounds are most concentrated. This timing is crucial for accurate botanical identification and maximizing the plant's medicinal properties.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Pueraria mirifica belongs to the genus Pueraria within the family Fabaceae. Its closest species include Pueraria montana and Pueraria phaseolata. These species share similar morphological and genetic traits, which help in distinguishing Pueraria mirifica from other legumes.
