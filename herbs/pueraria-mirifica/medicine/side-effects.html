10 Possible Side Effects Of Pueraria Mirifica (Kwao Krua)

Pueraria mirifica has some side effects when used improperly, such as allergic reactions, digestive issues, and hormonal imbalances.

These side effects can occur due to overconsumption or interactions with other medications. For instance, excessive use of Pueraria mirifica can lead to estrogen dominance, causing symptoms like bloating, mood swings, and breast tenderness.

This can worsen lives by disrupting daily routines and straining relationships.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Pueraria mirifica if used imporperly.

1. Triggers anxiety attacks

Pueraria mirifica causes allergic reactions.

This is due to the plant's high concentration of isoflavones and saponins, which can trigger an immune response in some individuals. The allergenic compounds can bind to immunoglobulin E (IgE) antibodies, leading to the release of histamine and other chemicals that cause symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling.

In severe cases, allergic reactions can progress to anaphylaxis, a life-threatening condition requiring immediate medical attention.

Pueraria mirifica triggers estrogen dominance when used, likely due to its high content of isoflavones and other plant compounds that mimic the effects of estrogen in the body.

This can lead to an imbalance of estrogen levels, causing a range of side effects including breast tenderness, mood swings, and changes in menstrual cycles.

The exact mechanisms behind this effect are not fully understood, but it is thought to be related to the way Pueraria mirifica interacts with estrogen receptors in the body.

These effects are thought to be responsible for the blood pressure increase experienced by some individuals using the herb.
