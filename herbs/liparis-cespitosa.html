Liparis cespitosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Liparis cespitosa has a caespitose growth habit characterized by dense tufts of fleshy, elliptical-to-oblong leaves that emerge from creeping rhizomes. These leaves are typically dark green, somewhat succulent, and arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces slender, erect inflorescences that bear small, greenish-yellow flowers with subtle purplish markings. Each flower features a distinctively shaped labellum that is often three-lobed and slightly fleshy. The overall structure of the plant allows it to form compact mats in its natural mossy habitats.
