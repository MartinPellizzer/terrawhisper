What's the taxonomical classification of Liparis cespitosa?

Liparis cespitosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Asparagales. This plant is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is specifically identified within the genus Liparis. Ultimately, its unique biological identity is defined by its specific designation as the species cespitosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Liparis cespitosa has a caespitose growth habit characterized by dense tufts of fleshy, elliptical-to-oblong leaves that emerge from creeping rhizomes. The plant produces slender, erect inflorescences that bear small, greenish-yellow flowers with subtle purplish markings. Each flower features a distinctively shaped labellum that is often three-lobed and slightly fleshy. The overall structure of the plant allows it to form compact mats in its natural mossy habitats.
