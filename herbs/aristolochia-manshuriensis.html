What to know about Aristolochia manshuriensis (chinese birthwort) before using it medicinally

Chinese birthwort, scientifically known as Aristolochia manshuriensis, is a herb that has been revered for its numerous health benefits, including its ability to lower blood pressure and cholesterol levels, relieve anxiety and stress, and even aid in the treatment of certain respiratory issues.

In addition to its medicinal properties, Chinese birthwort is also used in various culinary dishes, particularly in traditional Chinese and Korean cuisine, where it is often combined with other herbs and spices to add flavor and aroma to soups, stews, and braises. As a horticultural interest, Chinese birthwort is relatively low-maintenance, requiring only partial shade and well-drained soil to thrive. From a botanical standpoint, Chinese birthwort belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its unique, curved fruiting bodies that resemble horns or trumpets.

Historically, Chinese birthwort has been documented in ancient Chinese medical texts dating back to the Tang Dynasty, where it was prized for its ability to promote overall well-being and longevity.
