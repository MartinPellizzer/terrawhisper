Hemsleya macrosperma

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hemsleya macrosperma has large, deeply pinnatifid leaves characterized by prominent veins and a somewhat leathery texture. The plant typically exhibits a climbing or scrambling growth habit with slender, woody stems. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into distinct, relatively large seeds. The leaf margins are often irregularly lobed, contributing to a complex and textured appearance. This species is distinguished from its relatives by these specific leaf dimensions and the scale of its seed production.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Hemsleya macrosperma can cause severe skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential neurotoxicity if ingested or applied improperly. The plant contains bioactive compounds that may trigger allergic contact dermatitis upon direct skin exposure. If consumed, individuals might experience nausea, vomiting, or abdominal cramping as the body reacts to its chemical constituents. There is also limited research regarding the long-term systemic effects of its alkaloids on the central nervous system. Careful handling is necessary to avoid accidental poisoning or localized inflammatory responses.
