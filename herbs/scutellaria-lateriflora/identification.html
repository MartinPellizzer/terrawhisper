Botanical Identification of Scutellaria (Scutellaria lateriflora)

To identify Scutellaria (Scutellaria lateriflora) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Scutellaria lateriflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Lamiales, order Lamiales, family Lamiaceae, genus Scutellaria, and species Scutellaria lateriflora. This classification places it within the mint family, known for its medicinal properties and aromatic characteristics.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Scutellaria lateriflora is the scientific name for blue cohosh, a member of the Lamiaceae family. The author citation is Lindl., referring to the botanist who first described it. It has several synonyms, including Macleaya cordata and Stellaria lateriflora, reflecting historical classification changes. Common names include blue cohosh, bugbane, and wild blueberry, while regional and historical names vary across cultures and regions.

Morphological Characteristics

Scutellaria lateriflora is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous root system. It has upright, branched stems that are hairy. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and have serrated edges. The plant produces a spike-like inflorescence with small, blue to purple flowers. The fruit is a capsule containing numerous tiny seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Scutellaria lateriflora can be distinguished by its opposite, egg-shaped leaves with serrated edges and violet-blue flowers with two lips. It has a square stem and produces small, dark blue to purple flowers. It is often confused with other Scutellaria species but is unique in its leaf shape and flower structure.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Scutellaria lateriflora is native to the eastern United States, particularly the Appalachian region. It thrives in moist, shaded environments with rich, loamy soil. It prefers cool to temperate climates and is commonly found at elevations between 300 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Scutellaria lateriflora blooms from June to August, producing blue to purple flowers. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early fall. For identification purposes, the plant is typically harvested in late summer when the flowers are in full bloom and the leaves are mature.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Scutellaria lateriflora is closely related to species like Scutellaria galericola and Scutellaria lateriflora var. macrophylla. It belongs to the genus Scutellaria, which is part of the Lamiaceae family. The genus shares key traits such as square stems and opposite leaves, while the family is characterized by aromatic leaves and showy flowers.
