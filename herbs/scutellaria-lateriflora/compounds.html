Scutellaria (Scutellaria lateriflora) Compounds

Scutellaria contains several primary active compounds, including alkaloids like scutellarein and baicalin, which have anti-inflammatory properties. Flavonoids and glycosides contribute to its antioxidant effects, while terpenoids and phenolic acids support antimicrobial activity. Coumarin and tannins add to its ability to reduce inflammation and support digestive health.

Scutellaria (Scutellaria lateriflora) Monograph

1. Alkaloid

Scutellaria lateriflora contains the alkaloid scutellarine, which exhibits anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. This compound contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments. Scutellarine also shows potential in modern research for its neuroprotective effects.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Scutellaria lateriflora. They contribute to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial effects. These compounds are often responsible for the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

3. Glycoside

Scutellaria lateriflora contains the glycoside scutellarein, which exhibits anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments. Scutellarein is also known for its potential neuroprotective effects.

4. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Scutellaria lateriflora, commonly known as skullcap. These compounds contribute to the plant's medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and anxiolytic effects. They are often responsible for the plant's calming and therapeutic benefits in traditional herbal medicine.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Scutellaria lateriflora, commonly known as blue cohosh. It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, which are essential in traditional herbal medicine for treating various ailments.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a compound found in Scutellaria lateriflora. It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly in promoting relaxation and reducing anxiety. Coumarin also has antispasmodic effects, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and nervous system support.

7. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Scutellaria lateriflora. It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health. Tannins also have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects, making them valuable in traditional herbal medicine.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Scutellaria lateriflora. It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Saponin helps support the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for respiratory and digestive health.

9. Essential oil

The essential oil of Scutellaria lateriflora contains volatile compounds like scutellarin and flavonoids. These components contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The oil is often used in herbal medicine for respiratory and digestive support. It is extracted through steam distillation from the plant's aerial parts.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Scutellaria lateriflora. It contributes to the plant's vibrant color and possesses antioxidant properties. In herbal medicine, anthocyanin supports overall health by neutralizing free radicals and enhancing cellular function. Its presence underscores the plant's therapeutic value in traditional and modern applications.
