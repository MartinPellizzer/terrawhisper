What is Elymus czimganicus?

Elymus czimganicus is a perennial grass species belonging to the family Poaceae, native to the mountainous regions of China and Central Asia.

It is characterized by a tufted habit, culms 50-120 cm tall, with a leaf blade 3-10 cm long, 1-2 mm wide, and a ligule 1-2 mm long. The panicle is 8-20 cm long, with spikelets 10-15 mm long, having 3-5 florets, with two sterile lemmas and a fertile lemma 6-8 mm long. The plant is typically found in mountain grasslands, scrublands, and rocky slopes at elevations of 1,500-3,500 meters above sea level.

Elymus czimganicus is often confused with other Elymus species due to its morphological similarity.
