What is Pyrethrum (Anacyclus Pyrethrum)?

Pyrethrum (Anacyclus pyrethrum) is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, widely used in herbal medicine for its natural insecticidal properties and various therapeutic applications.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Pyrethrum is Anacyclus pyrethrum. This plant belongs to the family Compositae, genus Anacyclus, and species Anacyclus pyrethrum. It is commonly known as Pyrethrum in Europe and Africa, and as Anacyclus in parts of Asia. Native Habitat and Distribution

Anacyclus pyrethrum, commonly known as Pyrethrum, is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East, with its natural habitat primarily consisting of dry grasslands, rocky slopes, and coastal areas.

The leaves and flowers of Pyrethrum (Anacyclus pyrethrum) are the primary plant parts used medicinally, containing active compounds such as pyrethrins and essential oils that contribute to its therapeutic properties.

Pyrethrum (Anacyclus pyrethrum) contains a variety of phytochemical compounds, including pyrethrins, which are natural insecticides, along with flavonoids, terpenoids, and essential oils that contribute to its medicinal properties.

Pharmacological Properties

Pyrethrum (Anacyclus pyrethrum) contains bioactive compounds such as pyrethrins and flavonoids that exhibit insecticidal, anti-inflammatory, and analgesic properties, making it effective in treating skin conditions and reducing pain and inflammation.

Pyrethrum (Anacyclus pyrethrum) exerts its effects primarily through the action of its active compounds, such as pyrethrins and ketones, which target the nervous system of insects by increasing the permeability of sodium channels, leading to paralysis and death.

Safety, Side Effects, and Contraindications
