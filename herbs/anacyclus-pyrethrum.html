Pyrethrum

Anacyclus pyrethrum

Pyrethrum (Anacyclus pyrethrum) is a member of the Compositae family and has been traditionally used for various purposes. The plant parts, including the flowers and leaves, have been employed in traditional practices for treatment of skin disorders, digestive aid, and anti-inflammatory use. It holds cultural significance in certain regions where it has been utilized for its perceived properties.

Botanical Identification

Pyrethrum, scientifically known as Anacyclus pyrethrum, belongs to the Asteraceae family. It is also called "false chamomile" and is an annual herb native to the Mediterranean region. The plant has hairy leaves and yellow flowers, and it is sometimes referred to by other names in traditional herbal texts.

Pyrethrum Identification.

Active Compounds

Pyrethrum contains essential oil, flavonoids, and coumarin as its primary bioactive compounds. These compounds work synergistically to provide its insecticidal effects, with the essential oil being the main contributor. Flavonoids and coumarin support its therapeutic properties in herbal medicine.

  • Essential oil
  • Flavonoid
  • Coumarin

Preparation Methods

Extract: Used for digestive issues and skin conditions due to its anti-inflammatory properties.

Poultice: Applied externally for skin infections and wounds due to its antimicrobial properties.

Infusion: Used for respiratory conditions and as a mild sedative due to its calming effects.

Safety Profile

Pyrethrum is generally safe when used as directed, but it may cause skin irritation or allergic reactions in some individuals. It should not be used during pregnancy or by those with known allergies to related plants. Always follow recommended dosage guidelines to avoid adverse effects.
