Clerodendrum mandarinorum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clerodendrum mandarinorum has erect, woody stems that reach heights of up to 3 meters and feature oppositely arranged, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The foliage typically displays a bright green color and a soft, pubescent texture on both the upper and lower surfaces. Small, tubular white flowers emerge in dense, terminal panicles, often appearing in clusters throughout the growing season. Following the bloom, the plant produces small, fleshy berries that transition from green to a dark purple or black color when mature. The overall structure is characterized by a bushy, shrub-like habit that allows it to thrive in various tropical and subtropical environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to East Asia, specifically spanning across regions of China, Japan, Korea, and Taiwan. It thrives primarily in temperate and subtropical climates within these territories. While its primary range is centered in these Asian countries, it is also frequently documented in various parts of Southeast Asia. Some populations have been introduced to other regions due to human activity and environmental adaptability. Its distribution is often linked to moist, shaded habitats found throughout its native range.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and digestive distress if handled or ingested improperly. Contact with the leaves or stems may trigger contact dermatitis, characterized by redness, itching, or localized swelling on the skin. Some individuals may experience gastrointestinal issues such as nausea or stomach aches after consuming parts of the plant. There is also a potential for more severe systemic allergic responses in sensitive people. Because specific toxicological data is limited, caution is necessary when interacting with any part of this species.
