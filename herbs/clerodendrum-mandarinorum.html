Clerodendrum mandarinorum

What's the taxonomical classification of Clerodendrum mandarinorum?

Clerodendrum mandarinorum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Lamiales. Within this order, the plant is situated in the family Lamiaceae, which is characterized by its distinct morphological traits. Specifically, it is identified under the genus Clerodendrum, with its unique botanical designation being the species mandarinorum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clerodendrum mandarinorum has erect, woody stems that reach heights of up to 3 meters and feature oppositely arranged, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The foliage typically displays a bright green color and a soft, pubescent texture on both the upper and lower surfaces. Small, tubular white flowers emerge in dense, terminal panicles, often appearing in clusters throughout the growing season. Following the bloom, the plant produces small, fleshy berries that transition from green to a dark purple or black color when mature. The overall structure is characterized by a bushy, shrub-like habit that allows it to thrive in various tropical and subtropical environments.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds betulinic acid, cirsimaritin, cirsimaritin-4'-glucoside, lupeol, and quercetiin-3-methyl ether. These specific phytochemicals contribute to the various biological activities associated with the species. The presence of triterpenoids like lupeol and betulinic acid is particularly significant for their pharmacological potential. Additionally, the flavonoid derivatives cirsimaritin and its glycoside form play key roles in its chemical profile. Quercetiin-3-methyl ether further enhances the complex array of bioactive substances found within the plant tissue.

According to a study published by "Planta medica", a 70% EtOH extract of Clerodendrum mandarinorum root bark was assessed against 18 radioligand receptor binding assays. Fourteen compounds were isolated from the plant, including triterepenoids such as friedelanone, lupeol, and betulinic acid, as well as steroids like 24S-stigmata-5,25-dien-3 beta-ol and 22E,24S-stigmata-5,22,25-trien-3 beta-ol. The plant also contains flavonoids including cirsimaritin, cirsimaritin-4'-glucoside, and quercetin-3-methyl ether, along with saccharides such as sucrose, alpha-D- and beta-D-glucopyranose, ethyl-alpha-D-glucopyranoside, and 2-ethyl-beta-D-fructofuranoside. Betulinic acid showed weak activity against sulphonylurea receptors with an IC50 of 7.5 microM, while ethyl-alpha-D-glucopyranoside had an IC50 of 5.5 microM against muscarinic receptors. Additionally, cirsimaritin-4'-glucoside was weakly active in the adenosine-1 binding assay with an IC50 of 3.0 microM.
