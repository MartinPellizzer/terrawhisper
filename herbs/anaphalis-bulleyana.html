Anaphalis bulleyana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Anaphalis bulleyana has small, woody perennial shrubs characterized by opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with entire margins and a slightly pubescent texture. The foliage is typically dark green on the upper surface and may appear paler underneath. Its inflorescence consists of small, terminal flower heads that feature white ray florets surrounding a central disc of yellow tubular florets. The stems are often branched and can become somewhat woody at the base as the plant matures. These compact shrubs are adapted to various montane environments within their native range.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the high-altitude montane regions of central Kenya, specifically found within the volcanic landscapes of the Aberdare Range and Mount Kenya. It thrives in subalpine moorlands and afro-alpine zones characterized by cool temperatures and high moisture levels. These populations are often scattered across rocky outcrops and open grasslands where sunlight is abundant. The species relies on these specific ecological niches which are restricted to certain elevations above the forest line. Due to this localized range, the plant is highly sensitive to changes in the highland climate.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Anaphalis bulleyana targets specific enzymes and signaling proteins, most notably cyclooxygenase enzymes and various inflammatory mediators, to exert its pharmacological effects. The plant's bioactive secondary metabolites, such as sesquiterpene lactones, interfere with the arachidonic acid pathway to reduce prostaglandin synthesis. This multi-target mechanism explains the plant's traditional use in treating inflammation and pain.
