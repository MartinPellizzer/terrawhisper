1. Causes allergic reactions

Artemisia cina causes allergic reactions in some individuals due to its high content of sesquiterpene lactones and flavonoids.

These compounds can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling.

Additionally, the presence of other bioactive compounds like camphor and borneol may exacerbate allergic reactions, making it essential to be aware of potential sensitivities when using Artemisia cina.

2. Triggers anaphylaxis suddenly

Artemisia cina triggers anaphylaxis suddenly due to its high allergenic potential.

The plant contains a group of sesquiterpene lactones, which are responsible for the allergic reaction.

These compounds can cause the release of histamine from mast cells, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling, which in severe cases can progress to anaphylaxis.

3. Induces vomiting violently

Artemisia cina induces vomiting violently due to its potent anthelmintic and emetic properties.

The plant's active compounds, including sesquiterpenes and flavonoids, stimulate the gut and induce severe nausea, leading to forceful vomiting.

This intense stimulation may also cause irritation of the stomach lining, exacerbating the vomiting response.

4. Increases heart rate rapidly

Artemisia cina increases heart rate rapidly due to its stimulatory effect on the nervous system.

This is caused by the presence of sesquiterpenoids and flavonoids in the plant, which have been shown to stimulate the sympathetic nervous system and increase heart rate.

As a result, people using Artemisia cina may experience an increase in their resting heart rate and blood pressure.

5. Causes diarrhea severely

Artemisia cina causes diarrhea severely due to its ability to stimulate the digestive system and increase the frequency of bowel movements.

The herb's bitter compounds can irritate the gastrointestinal tract, leading to loose stools and abdominal cramps.

Additionally, Artemisia cina may also alter the balance of gut bacteria, further exacerbating the severity of diarrhea.

6. Lowers blood pressure sharply

Artemisia cina lowers blood pressure sharply.

This is due to its ability to cause vasodilation, which is an increase in blood vessel diameter, leading to a decrease in blood pressure. Additionally, the herb's beta-blocker-like properties can slow down the heart rate and cardiac output, further contributing to this side effect.

The exact mechanisms of Artemisia cina's effects on blood pressure are not yet fully understood, but its ability to dilate blood vessels and affect heart function likely play a significant role in its hypotensive effects.

7. Causes respiratory distress

Artemisia cina causes respiratory distress due to its irritating effect on the mucous membranes of the upper respiratory tract.

The volatile oils present in the plant, particularly sesquiterpenes and flavonoids, are responsible for this reaction. When inhaled, these compounds can stimulate an exaggerated coughing response, leading to bronchospasm and difficulty breathing.

This adverse effect may be exacerbated by the plant's ability to increase mucus production, further exacerbating respiratory distress.

8. Triggers asthma attacks

Artemisia cina triggers asthma attacks due to its ability to cause bronchial constriction and increased airway resistance.

The plant contains a compound called artemisinin, which can stimulate the release of histamine, a chemical that contributes to allergic reactions and inflammation in the lungs.

Additionally, Artemisia cina's saponin content can also exacerbate existing respiratory conditions, such as asthma, by irritating the airways and causing them to constrict further.

9. Increases urination frequency

Artemisia cina increases urination frequency.

This side effect is attributed to the plant's diuretic properties, which stimulate the kidneys to produce more urine. As a result, the body loses excess water and electrolytes, leading to frequent trips to the bathroom.

Additionally, the increased fluid elimination may also contribute to a decrease in blood pressure, further exacerbating the need for more frequent urination.

10. Produces kidney stones

Artemisia cina produces kidney stones due to its ability to increase urine acidity and reduce calcium absorption.

The high levels of oxalic acid present in Artemisia cina can bind to minerals such as calcium, magnesium, and phosphate, reducing their absorption and increasing the risk of stone formation.

This is particularly concerning for individuals with a history of kidney stones or those who are already at risk due to family history or other factors.
