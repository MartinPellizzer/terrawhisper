Phyllanthus flexuosus

What's the taxonomical classification of Phyllanthus flexuosus?

Phyllanthus flexuosus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further categorized under the order Malpighiales. It falls within the family Phyllanthaceae, which dictates its specific structural characteristics. Ultimately, its precise biological identity is defined by its placement in the genus Phyllanthus, with its specific designation being the species flexuosus.

What are the therapeutic actions of Phyllanthus flexuosus?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat digestive ailments, skin infections, inflammation, and various types of fever. Its medicinal properties are often utilized to address gastrointestinal issues such as stomach aches and dysentery. In traditional practices, the plant serves as an agent to soothe skin irritations and combat fungal or bacterial growths. It is also frequently applied to help manage symptoms associated with systemic inflammation within the body. These therapeutic applications rely on the specific bioactive compounds found within its leaves and stems.
