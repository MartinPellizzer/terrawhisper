10 Possible Side Effects Of Hibiscus Rosa-Sinensis

Hibiscus rosa-sinensis has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal upset, allergic reactions, and interactions with medications.

These side effects are caused by the herb's high tannin and flavonoid content, which can irritate the stomach and cause nausea, diarrhea, and vomiting.

For example, consuming large amounts of hibiscus tea may exacerbate existing stomach conditions, making daily life unbearable for those who experience constant nausea and discomfort.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Hibiscus rosa-sinensis causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

This is likely due to the presence of certain compounds found in the plant's sap, leaves, and flowers, which can trigger an immune response in sensitive people.

The specific allergenic components are still unknown, but it is believed that they may be related to the plant's high levels of flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenes.

2. Induces vomiting suddenly

Hibiscus rosa-sinensis induces vomiting suddenly due to its ability to stimulate the digestive system and increase gastric acid production.

This may lead to a sudden onset of nausea and vomiting in some individuals, particularly those who are sensitive to its active compounds.

Additionally, Hibiscus rosa-sinensis may irritate the stomach lining, causing inflammation and discomfort that can manifest as vomiting.

3. Triggers diarrhea frequently

Hibiscus rosa-sinensis triggers diarrhea frequently.

This is due to its high content of anthraquinone glycosides and flavonoids, which have laxative properties that can stimulate bowel movements and increase stool frequency.

Additionally, the herb's ability to alter gut bacteria composition may also contribute to this side effect, as an imbalance of gut flora can lead to changes in bowel habits.

4. Increases blood sugar levels

Hibiscus rosa-sinensis increases blood sugar levels by affecting insulin production and sensitivity.

The plant's flavonoids and anthocyanins may inhibit the activity of enzymes involved in glucose metabolism, leading to elevated blood sugar levels. Additionally, Hibiscus rosa-sinensis has been shown to reduce insulin secretion from pancreatic beta cells, further contributing to hyperglycemia.

This side effect is thought to be particularly significant for individuals with pre-existing diabetes or those who are at risk of developing the condition.

5. Lowers blood pressure rapidly

Hibiscus rosa-sinensis lowers blood pressure rapidly.

This is due to its high levels of anthocyanins and flavonoids, which act as vasodilators and decrease peripheral resistance, allowing blood vessels to relax and expand. Additionally, hibiscus extracts have been shown to inhibit the production of angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE), a key hormone that regulates blood pressure.

These mechanisms combined can lead to a significant drop in blood pressure, particularly in individuals with hypertension or prehypertension.

6. Interacts with medications strongly

Hibiscus rosa-sinensis interacts with medications strongly.

This is due to its ability to alter blood sugar levels and increase insulin sensitivity, which can affect the efficacy of certain medications such as diabetes treatments.

Additionally, hibiscus may reduce the body's ability to absorb certain medications, including thyroid hormone replacements and antacoids, leading to decreased therapeutic effects.

7. Disrupts thyroid function greatly

Hibiscus rosa-sinensis disrupts thyroid function greatly due to its high concentration of flavonoids and anthocyanins.

These compounds can interact with the body's hormone regulation, potentially altering thyroid function and causing changes in metabolism and energy levels.

The exact mechanism is not fully understood, but it is believed that Hibiscus rosa-sinensis's phytochemicals may bind to thyroid receptors or interfere with iodine uptake, leading to disruptions in thyroid hormone production.

8. Stimulates uterine contractions severely

Hibiscus rosa-sinensis stimulates uterine contractions severely due to its ability to increase estrogen levels and mimic the effects of progesterone.

This can cause a sudden tightening or contraction of the uterus, potentially leading to severe side effects for pregnant women.

The herbal remedy's ability to stimulate uterine contractions makes it contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding.

9. Reduces fertility slightly

Hibiscus rosa-sinensis reduces fertility slightly.

This side effect is attributed to the plant's high content of flavonoids, which can interfere with the body's ability to produce estrogen and progesterone hormones necessary for fertility.

Additionally, the plant's saponins may also affect sperm quality and motility, further contributing to reduced fertility.

10. Causes skin rashes rarely

Hibiscus rosa-sinensis causes skin rashes rarely due to its potential allergenic properties.

The plant's sap and extracts contain various compounds that can trigger an allergic response in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as redness, itching, and blistering on the skin.

This reaction is usually mild and temporary but may persist in cases of severe allergy or repeated exposure.
