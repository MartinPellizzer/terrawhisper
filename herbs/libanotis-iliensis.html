Libanotis iliensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Libanotis iliensis has a herbaceous structure characterized by deeply divided, pinnatifid leaves and terminal umbels of small white flowers. The stems are typically slender and erect, supporting the complex leaf architecture that reaches toward the sunlight. Each leaf segment is finely dissected, contributing to a delicate and airy appearance within its natural habitat. The inflorescence consists of multiple small florets arranged in a flat-topped cluster, which is a defining feature of the Apiaceae family. This morphological combination allows the plant to efficiently manage light interception and pollinator attraction.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the Mount Lebanon range in Lebanon, specifically restricted to the high-altitude regions of the Mount Lebanon massif. It thrives within specialized limestone habitats located at elevations typically ranging from 1,500 to 2,100 meters above sea level. These populations are often found in rocky crevices and scree slopes where the soil is thin and well-drained. Due to this extremely limited range, the species is considered a narrow endemic with a highly fragmented distribution.
