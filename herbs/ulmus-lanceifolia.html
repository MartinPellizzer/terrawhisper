Ulmus lanceifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Ulmus lanceifolia?

Ulmus lanceifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Rosales. Furthermore, this plant is a member of the family Ulmaceae and falls under the genus Ulmus, specifically identified by the species epithet lanceifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ulmus lanceifolia has lanceolate leaves with serrated margins, an asymmetrical base, and a prominent pinnate venation pattern. The foliage is characterized by a slender, elongated shape that tapers to a fine point at the apex. The bark on mature specimens typically develops a rough, fissured texture that provides protection for the inner tissues. Its branching structure often forms a spreading canopy with woody stems that support the dense leaf arrangement. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the branches, typically preceding the development of the distinctive samara seeds.
