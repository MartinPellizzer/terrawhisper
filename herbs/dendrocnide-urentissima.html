What's the taxonomical classification of Dendrocnide urentissima?

Dendrocnide urentissima belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Rosales and is a member of the family Urticaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Dendrocnide and is specifically designated as the species urentissima.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Dendrocnide urentissima can cause severe skin irritation, intense burning sensations, and painful blistering upon contact with its specialized stinging hairs. These microscopic structures inject toxins that trigger immediate redness and swelling in the affected area. Some individuals may also experience localized itching or more widespread dermatological reactions if the plant is mishandled. In rare instances, extreme sensitivity could lead to systemic discomfort or prolonged inflammatory responses. Avoiding direct physical contact is the most effective way to prevent these adverse physical effects.
