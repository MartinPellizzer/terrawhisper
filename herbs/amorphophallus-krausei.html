Amorphophallus krausei

What's the taxonomical classification of Amorphophallus krausei?

Amorphophalus krausei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Alismatales. This plant is a member of the family Araceae and falls under the genus Amorphophalus, ultimately being identified by its specific species name, krausei.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Amorphophallus krausei contains medicinal compounds primarily located within its underground corm. This tuberous structure is harvested and processed to treat various ailments in traditional practices. Local communities often utilize the ground paste of the corm to address skin conditions and wounds. Some preparations involve boiling the plant parts to create decoctions used for internal digestive issues. The specific chemical properties within the corm are believed to provide these therapeutic benefits.

    What are the herbal preparations of Amorphophallus krausei?

    Amorphophallus krausei is prepared primarily as a decoction made by boiling the tuberous rhizome in water to extract its medicinal properties. This liquid extract is often consumed to treat various ailments such as stomach issues or skin infections. In some traditional practices, the dried and powdered form of the tuber is mixed into other substances to create medicinal pastes. These pastes can then be applied topically to specific areas of the body for localized treatment. The versatility of these preparation methods allows different therapeutic applications depending on the intended health benefit.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Amorphophallus krausei can cause severe skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal distress if the plant is ingested or touched without protection. The plant contains calcium oxalate crystals that act like tiny needles, piercing the skin and mucous membranes upon contact. This physical irritation often results in intense itching, redness, and swelling of the affected areas. If the tuber is consumed, these crystals can cause immediate burning sensations in the mouth, throat, and digestive tract. Such symptoms may lead to vomiting or difficulty swallowing depending on the amount of exposure.
