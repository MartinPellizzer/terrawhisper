Cedrus atlantica

Cedrus atlantica, commonly known as the Atlas cedar, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Atlas Mountains in North Africa.

This plant is valued for its aromatic wood and essential oil, which have been used in traditional medicine for centuries due to its numerous health benefits, including reducing stress and anxiety, improving respiratory issues, and acting as an antiseptic.

The therapeutic actions of Cedrus atlantica include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which can help to soothe skin irritations, wounds, and promote overall well-being.

The bioactive constituents of Cedrus atlantica include cedrol, atlantone, and other volatile oils, which contribute to its therapeutic and medicinal properties.
