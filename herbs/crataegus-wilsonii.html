Crataegus wilsonii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Crataegus wilsonii has small, elliptical leaves with serrated margins, woody stems covered in sharp thorns, and clusters of white flowers that transition into small red pomes. The foliage typically exhibits a fine texture due to the relatively small size of the individual leaflets. Its branches are characterized by a spreading growth habit and the presence of stout spines that serve as a defense mechanism. During the flowering season, the inflorescence consists of numerous tiny petals clustered tightly together. The resulting fruit is a fleshy drupe that provides a distinct visual contrast against the dark bark.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the central and southern mountainous regions of China, specifically within the provinces of Sichuan, Yunnan, and Guizhou. It thrives primarily in temperate forest environments found at moderate to high elevations. These habitats are characterized by moist, well-drained soils and significant seasonal rainfall. The species is often found tucked into the understory of broadleaf or mixed coniferous forests. Its distribution is limited to these specific ecological niches within the subtropical highland climate.
