Crataegus wilsonii

What's the taxonomical classification of Crataegus wilsonii?

Crataegus wilsonii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Rosales and belongs to the family Rosaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Crataegus, and its specific species designation, wilsonii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Crataegus wilsonii has small, elliptical leaves with serrated margins, woody stems covered in sharp thorns, and clusters of white flowers that transition into small red pomes.

The foliage typically exhibits a fine texture due to the relatively small size of the individual leaflets. Its branches are characterized by a spreading growth habit and the presence of stout spines that serve as a defense mechanism. During the flowering season, the inflorescence consists of numerous tiny petals clustered tightly together.

The resulting fruit is a fleshy drupe that provides a distinct visual contrast against the dark bark.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

While specific clinical studies on this exact species are limited, consuming unidentified members of the hawthorn genus may lead to nausea or stomach cramping. It is also possible for the bioactive compounds to interfere with heart rate regulation or blood pressure management in sensitive users.
