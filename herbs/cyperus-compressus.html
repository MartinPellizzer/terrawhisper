Cyperus compressus

What's the taxonomical classification of Cyperus compressus?

Cyperus compressus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Poales and falls under the family Cyperaceae. At the most specific levels of its biological classification, it is identified by the genus Cyperus and the species compressus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cyperus compressus has a perennial growth habit characterized by dense tufts of triangular stems that arise from a creeping rhizome system. The leaves are linear and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette before transitioning into the upright culms. These stems are notably compressed and can reach heights of up to 50 centimeters depending on the environment. Small, greenish to brownish spikelets are arranged in terminal clusters that give the plant its characteristic inflorescence. The individual flowers are inconspicuous and lack petals, relying on wind for pollination within the dense spikelet structure.
