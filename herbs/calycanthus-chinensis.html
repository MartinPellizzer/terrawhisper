Calycanthus chinensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Calycanthus chinensis has opposite, elliptical to ovate leaves that measure approximately 5 to 12 centimeters in length and feature entire margins with a smooth, leathery texture. The foliage is typically a deep, lustrous green on the upper surface and often displays a paler underside. Its woody stems are somewhat spreading and can develop a multi-stemmed shrubby habit as the plant matures. The flowers are relatively small, reddish-brown or maroon in color, and emerge from the leaf axils. These blooms possess a distinctive, spicy fragrance that characterizes the species during its flowering period.
