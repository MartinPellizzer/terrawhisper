What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Campanula punctata has upright, perennial stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters and bear lanceolate, serrated leaves arranged alternately along the stalk. Its most distinctive feature is the terminal cluster of bell-shaped flowers, which are usually violet or bluish and marked with small dark spots. These blossoms emerge from a branched inflorescence and possess five pointed lobes at the mouth of the corolla.
