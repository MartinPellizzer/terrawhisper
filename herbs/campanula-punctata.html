Campanula punctata

What's the taxonomical classification of Campanula punctata?

Campanula punctata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. It is further categorized into the family Campanulaceae and the genus Campanula, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species punctata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Campanula punctata has upright, perennial stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters and bear lanceolate, serrated leaves arranged alternately along the stalk. The foliage is primarily basal, consisting of long-petioled leaves that taper toward the tip. Its most distinctive feature is the terminal cluster of bell-shaped flowers, which are usually violet or bluish and marked with small dark spots. These blossoms emerge from a branched inflorescence and possess five pointed lobes at the mouth of the corolla. The plant also develops a creeping rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread through its immediate environment.
