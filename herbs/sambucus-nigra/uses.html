Black Elderberry (Sambucus nigra) Uses

Black Elderberry is commonly used to support the immune system and alleviate symptoms of the common cold, influenza, and respiratory infections. It may help reduce the duration and severity of coughs, bronchitis, tonsillitis, and sinusitis. Its antiviral properties are believed to aid in fighting viral infections, including those causing allergic rhinitis and other upper respiratory tract conditions.

Black Elderberry (Sambucus nigra) Monograph

1. Common cold

Black Elderberry is traditionally used to support the immune system during the common cold. It contains high levels of antioxidants and flavonoids, which may help reduce viral replication and alleviate symptoms. Studies suggest it can shorten the duration of colds when taken at the first sign of illness. It is often consumed as a syrup or supplement during cold season.

2. Bronchitis

Black Elderberry is used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health, particularly in cases of bronchitis. It contains antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds that may help reduce coughing and mucus production. It is often prepared as a tea or syrup to ease symptoms and promote healing of the respiratory tract.

3. Influenza

Black Elderberry is traditionally used to support immune function during influenza due to its high antioxidant and anti-viral properties. Studies suggest it may reduce the duration and severity of flu symptoms. It is often consumed as a syrup or tea. Consistent use may help prevent or alleviate flu-related illness.

4. Cough

Black Elderberry is used to support respiratory health and may help alleviate cough symptoms. Its high antioxidant content can reduce inflammation and soothe irritated airways. Traditionally, it is prepared as a tea or syrup to ease persistent coughs. It is often combined with other herbs like echinacea or licorice root for enhanced effectiveness.

5. Respiratory infections

Black Elderberry is traditionally used to support the immune system and may help alleviate symptoms of respiratory infections. Its high antioxidant and vitamin C content can reduce viral replication and inflammation. It is often used in syrups or teas to ease coughs and sore throats. Regular use may shorten the duration of colds and flu.

6. Respiratory tract infections

Black Elderberry has been traditionally used to support the immune system and alleviate symptoms of respiratory tract infections. Its high antioxidant content and anti-inflammatory properties may help reduce the duration and severity of colds and flu. Studies suggest it can enhance immune response, though it should not replace medical treatment for severe infections.

7. Tonsillitis

Black Elderberry has been traditionally used to support immune function and may help alleviate symptoms of tonsillitis due to its high antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. It is often prepared as a syrup or tea to soothe throat pain and reduce swelling. Its use should be guided by a qualified herbalist, especially in chronic or severe cases.

8. Allergic rhinitis

Black Elderberry has been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis due to its high antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Studies suggest it may reduce nasal congestion and inflammation by modulating immune responses. It is often consumed as a syrup or tea during allergy season to support respiratory health.

9. Sinusitis

Black Elderberry is used to support the immune system and may help alleviate symptoms of sinusitis due to its high antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. It can reduce mucus production and ease congestion, though it should be used with caution, as raw berries can be toxic. Standardized extracts are often recommended for safety and efficacy.

10. Colds

Black Elderberry is traditionally used to support immune function during colds. Its high antioxidant and vitamin C content may help reduce symptom duration and severity. It is often consumed as a syrup or tea to alleviate congestion and sore throat. Regular use may enhance the body's ability to fight off viral infections.
