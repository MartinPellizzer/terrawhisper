Black Elderberry (Sambucus nigra) Compounds

Black Elderberry contains several primary compounds, including flavonoids, which act as antioxidants. Coumarin contributes to its anti-inflammatory properties. Phenolic acids and tannins provide additional antioxidant and antimicrobial effects. Glycosides and saponins support immune function, while anthocyanins give the berries their deep color and offer protective benefits.

Black Elderberry (Sambucus nigra) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Black Elderberry (Sambucus nigra). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting immune function and reducing oxidative stress. These compounds are key to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for colds and respiratory support.

2. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Black Elderberry (Sambucus nigra). It contributes to the plant's antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarin also acts as a mild sedative and may support cardiovascular health. Its presence enhances the therapeutic potential of Black Elderberry in herbal medicine.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Black Elderberry (Sambucus nigra). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports immune function and may aid in reducing inflammation, making it valuable in herbal medicine for its therapeutic effects.

4. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Black Elderberry (Sambucus nigra). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support immune function by reducing viral replication. Tannins also act as antioxidants, helping to neutralize free radicals in the body.

5. Glycoside

Glycosides in Black Elderberry (Sambucus nigra) are complex molecules consisting of sugar molecules attached to aglycones. These compounds contribute to the plant's therapeutic properties, including antiviral and antioxidant effects. They are commonly found in the berries and have been studied for their potential health benefits.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Black Elderberry (Sambucus nigra). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and immune-supporting properties. Saponins may help reduce oxidative stress and support respiratory health. They are part of the complex mix of bioactive compounds in elderberry that contribute to its traditional use in herbal medicine.

7. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Black Elderberry (Sambucus nigra). It contributes to the plant's deep color and has strong antioxidant properties. Anthocyanin supports immune function and may help reduce inflammation. It is often used in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.

8. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Black Elderberry (Sambucus nigra). They contribute to the plant's antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds support the immune system and may help reduce viral replication. Terpenoids are part of the complex chemical profile that gives Black Elderberry its therapeutic value.

9. Essential oil

The essential oil of Black Elderberry (Sambucus nigra) contains volatile compounds like methyl salicylate and limonene. These components contribute to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The essential oil is often used in aromatherapy and topical applications for its soothing effects. It supports the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and skin conditions.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Black Elderberry (Sambucus nigra). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, though research on its specific therapeutic effects is limited compared to other compounds like anthocyanins. Alkaloids in elderberry may support immune function and have anti-inflammatory potential.
