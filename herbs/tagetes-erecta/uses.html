Tagetes (Tagetes erecta) Uses

Tagetes erecta is used to treat various skin conditions including infections, wounds, and inflammatory issues. It helps with itching, irritation, and fungal infections, as well as abscesses and insect bites. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties support skin healing and reduce symptoms.

Tagetes (Tagetes erecta) Monograph

1. Skin infection

Tagetes erecta, commonly known as African marigold, has been traditionally used to treat skin infections due to its antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties. Extracts from its flowers can help reduce symptoms of fungal infections and bacterial wounds when applied topically. Its active compounds, such as flavonoids and terpenes, contribute to its antimicrobial effects.

2. Wound healing

Tagetes erecta, commonly known as African marigold, has been traditionally used to promote wound healing due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The plant contains compounds that help reduce infection risk and accelerate tissue repair, making it a valuable component in herbal wound care formulations.

3. Inflammatory skin conditions

Tagetes erecta, or African marigold, is used topically to treat inflammatory skin conditions due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The plant contains compounds like flavonoids and sesquiterpene lactones that reduce redness, swelling, and infection in skin ailments such as eczema and psoriasis. It is often applied as a poultice or infused oil for localized relief.

4. Itching

Tagetes erecta, commonly known as the African marigold, is used traditionally to relieve itching. Its anti-inflammatory and antipruritic properties help reduce skin irritation. It is often applied topically as a poultice or infused oil to soothe conditions like eczema and insect bites.

5. Skin infections

Tagetes erecta, commonly known as African marigold, has been used traditionally to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Its essential oils and extracts inhibit bacterial and fungal growth, making it effective for conditions like athlete's foot and eczema. Topical application of its infused oils or poultices helps reduce infection and soothe irritated skin.

6. Skin irritation

Tagetes erecta, or African marigold, is used topically to soothe skin irritation due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce redness, itching, and inflammation associated with minor burns, rashes, and insect bites. Its essential oils and extracts can be applied directly or diluted in carrier oils for localized relief.

7. Skin abscesses

Tagetes erecta, or African marigold, has been traditionally used to treat skin abscesses due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Its essential oils and extracts help reduce infection and promote healing. Topical application of its leaves or crushed flowers can aid in draining pus and soothing inflamed areas.

8. Insect bites

Tagetes erecta, commonly known as African marigold, is used topically to alleviate symptoms of insect bites. Its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties help reduce swelling, itching, and infection risk. Crushed leaves or a prepared infusion can be applied directly to the affected area for relief.

9. Fungal infections

Tagetes erecta, commonly known as African marigold, has demonstrated antifungal properties, particularly against dermatophytes and Candida species. Its essential oils contain compounds like limonene and cineole, which inhibit fungal growth. Traditionally used in herbal medicine, it is applied topically to treat skin infections and may support natural defenses against fungal pathogens.

10. Wounds

Tagetes erecta, or African marigold, has been traditionally used to treat wounds due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The plant's leaves and flowers can be applied topically to promote healing, reduce infection risk, and soothe inflammation in minor cuts, burns, and skin irritations.
