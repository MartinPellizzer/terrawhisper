What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Tagetes Erecta (Marigold)?

Tagetes erecta, also known as marigold, has health benefits such as antibacterial, antifungal, and anti-inflammatory properties, making it useful in treating wounds and skin conditions.

The medicinal constituents of marigold include flavonoids, saponins, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects. Marigold is often used to make salves, ointments, and teas for medicinal purposes. However, some users may experience skin irritation or allergic reactions when using marigold preparations.

It is essential to consult a healthcare professional before using marigold, particularly for pregnant or breastfeeding women.

What are the possible side effect of using Tagetes erecta improperly?

Improper use of Tagetes erecta, also known as marigold, increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as allergic reactions, skin irritation, and gastrointestinal issues.

Some individuals may experience nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, especially when consuming large quantities of marigold tea or using it in high concentrations as a topical treatment.

Additionally, pregnant or breastfeeding women may be more susceptible to these side effects due to the plant's potential to stimulate the uterus and interfere with fetal development.

What precautions to take when using Tagetes erecta medicinally?

Before using Tagetes erecta, also known as marigold, for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as consulting with a healthcare professional to determine the safe dosage and method of administration.
