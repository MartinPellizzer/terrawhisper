What's the taxonomical classification of Strobilanthes henryi?

Strobilanthes henryi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Lamiales. This plant is a representative of the family Acanthaceae, falling under the genus Strobilanthes, and is specifically identified by its species name, henryi.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Strobilanthes henryi has an upright, herbaceous growth habit characterized by opposite, elliptic to lanceolate leaves with entire margins and acute tips.

The foliage is typically dark green and possesses a slightly textured surface with prominent pinnate venation. Its inflorescences emerge from the leaf axils, producing dense clusters of tubular, bilabiate flowers. These blossoms are most notably a vibrant shade of purple or violet, often featuring white markings on the lower lip.

The stem structure is relatively slender but provides sufficient support for the lush canopy of leaves and blooming spikes.
