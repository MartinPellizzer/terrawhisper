Silene firma

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Silene firma has a perennial growth habit characterized by erect stems that typically reach heights of 20 to 50 centimeters and are covered in fine, glandular hairs. The leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem and possess an ovate to lanceolate shape with entire margins. Its flowers are actinomorphic and feature five distinct petals that are often deeply notched at the apex. The calyx is tubular and elongated, providing a protective structure for the developing reproductive organs. Small, rounded capsules serve as the fruit, which eventually split to release numerous tiny seeds.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the limestone cliffs and rocky outcrops of the southern Balkan Peninsula, specifically within parts of Greece and Albania. It typically inhabits specialized montane habitats where calcareous substrates are present. The species is often found at moderate to high elevations where sunlight is abundant. Its distribution is quite restricted and fragmented due to its reliance on specific geological formations. Localized populations are scattered across rugged terrain rather than being spread across a continuous landscape.
