Silene firma

What's the taxonomical classification of Silene firma?

Silene firma belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is a member of the order Caryophyllales and falls under the family Caryophyllaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized within the genus Silene, with its specific designation being the species firma.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Silene firma has a perennial growth habit characterized by erect stems that typically reach heights of 20 to 50 centimeters and are covered in fine, glandular hairs.

The leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem and possess an ovate to lanceolate shape with entire margins. Its flowers are actinomorphic and feature five distinct petals that are often deeply notched at the apex. The calyx is tubular and elongated, providing a protective structure for the developing reproductive organs.

Small, rounded capsules serve as the fruit, which eventually split to release numerous tiny seeds.
