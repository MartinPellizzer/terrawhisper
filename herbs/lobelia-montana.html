What's the taxonomical classification of Lobelia montana?

Lobelia montana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the order Asterales and the family Campanulaceae. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Lobelia, with its specific designation being the species montana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lobelia montana has upright, herbaceous stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and are covered in simple, lanceolate leaves with entire or slightly serrated margins. Its inflorescence consists of small, tubular flowers that are characterized by a distinct two-lipped structure. These blooms often display shades of blue or violet and are arranged in terminal racemes. The plant also features a fibrous root system that helps anchor it in its natural mountain habitats.
