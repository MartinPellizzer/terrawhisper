Canarium strictum

What's the taxonomical classification of Canarium strictum?

Canarium strictum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic descent, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Sapindales, it is placed in the family Burseraceae. The plant is specifically identified by its genus, Canarium, and its species, strictum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Canarium strictum has an erect woody habit characterized by a straight trunk, smooth bark, and pinnate leaves composed of multiple oblong leaflets.

The leaves are typically arranged alternately along the branches and possess a leathery texture with prominent midribs. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves to facilitate reproduction. These blossoms eventually develop into fleshy drupes that contain a single hard seed.

The overall structure of the plant reflects its adaptation to tropical forest environments.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Canarium strictum contains medicinal properties found in its resin, bark, leaves, and fruit. The resin, often referred to as dammar, is frequently harvested for its antiseptic and wound-healing capabilities. Extracts from the bark are utilized in various traditional practices to treat inflammatory conditions and digestive issues. The leaves can be processed to create decoctions used for soothing skin irritations or managing fevers. Additionally, the fruit is sometimes consumed or processed for its nutritional and therapeutic potential in local medicinal diets.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

The presence of flavonoids and phenolic compounds provides significant antioxidant activity, helping to neutralize free radicals within biological systems. Terpenoids found in the plant often demonstrate important antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Together, these chemical constituents form the basis for the plant's traditional medicinal applications.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Canarium strictum targets various enzymes and receptors, most notably inhibiting cyclooxygenase (COX) enzymes, lipoxygenase (LOX), and specific inflammatory cytokines like tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-alpha). These phytochemical interactions facilitate a reduction in prostaglandin synthesis, which underpins the plant's significant anti-inflammatory properties. Additionally, certain terpenoids within the plant are thought to modulate nitric oxide synthase (NOS) activity to regulate oxidative stress. The bioactive compounds also demonstrate potential interactions with acetylcholinesterase, suggesting a mechanism for neuroprotective effects. Through these diverse protein-ligand interactions, the plant exerts its multifaceted therapeutic influence on the human biological system.

What are the therapeutic actions of Canarium strictum?

Canarium strictum has potent therapeutic properties characterized by its ability to act as an astringent, hemostatic, antiseptic, and anti-inflammatory agent.

The plant's resins and extracts work to constrict tissues and reduce swelling in affected areas. Its hemostatic qualities allow it to effectively slow or stop bleeding from minor wounds and abrasions. Additionally, the natural antiseptic compounds help prevent infection by neutralizing harmful microorganisms on the skin.

These combined actions make it a useful traditional remedy for managing skin irritation and promoting wound healing.

The primary therapeutic actions of this plant are shown in the list below.

  • Astringent
  • Hemostatic
  • Antiseptic
  • Anti-inflammatory

This plant is used to treat skin diseases, wounds, rheumatism, and various types of digestive disorders.

Its medicinal properties are often utilized to soothe inflammatory conditions affecting the joints. The plant extracts are frequently applied topically to promote the healing of cuts and abrasions. Additionally, certain preparations are consumed to alleviate stomach discomfort and gastrointestinal issues.

    What are the herbal preparations of Canarium strictum?

    Canarium strictum is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste using its dried bark, leaves, or resin. The bark is often boiled in water to create a concentrated liquid used for internal or external medicinal purposes. Leaves can be steeped in hot water to produce a tea or crushed directly into a poultice for skin applications. Additionally, the plant's resin is frequently collected and processed into ointments or salves to treat wounds and inflammations. These various forms allow for the targeted delivery of its bioactive compounds depending on the specific ailment being addressed.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Canarium strictum can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or gastrointestinal distress if ingested improperly or applied directly to sensitive areas. While often used in traditional medicine, the concentrated oils or resins may trigger contact dermatitis in some individuals. Some users report mild stomach upset or nausea if the plant material is consumed in uncontrolled dosages. There is also limited clinical data regarding long-term toxicity or systemic effects on internal organs. Therefore, caution is necessary when using this species for medicinal purposes.
