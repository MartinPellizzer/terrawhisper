Tilia (Tilia americana): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

Tilia (Tilia americana) is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America, known for its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers, which are commonly used in traditional and modern medicine to treat conditions such as anxiety, insomnia, and digestive issues.

What is Tilia (Tilia Americana)?

Tilia americana, commonly known as the American basswood or large-toothed maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America, characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, fragrant white flowers, and edible, nut-like seeds.

Botanical Identity and Classification

Tilia americana, commonly known as the American basswood or trembling maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America, belonging to the Tiliaceae family and characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, fragrant flowers, and lightweight, fibrous wood.

Common Names and Synonyms

Common names for Tilia americana include American basswood, American linden, and white basswood, with scientific synonym Tilia americana L.

Plant Description and Morphology

Tilia americana, commonly known as the American basswood or large-toothed maple, is a deciduous tree characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, and a smooth, gray-brown bark that becomes fissured with age.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Tilia americana, commonly known as the American basswood or large-toothed maple, is native to eastern North America, ranging from Canada to the southeastern United States, and typically grows in moist, fertile soils along riverbanks, woodlands, and mixed forests.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The leaves, flowers, and bark of Tilia americana are used medicinally, with the leaves and flowers being the most commonly utilized parts for their therapeutic properties.

Phytochemical Composition

Tilia (Tilia americana) contains a diverse array of phytochemical compounds, including flavonoids such as quercetin and kaempferol, tannins, mucilage, and essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties and therapeutic applications in herbal medicine.

Pharmacological Properties

Tilia americana contains compounds such as tilifolin and tiliroside that exhibit anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and sedative effects, making it a valuable herb in traditional and modern herbal medicine.

Mechanisms of Action

Tilia (Tilia americana) exerts its effects through a combination of anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and mild sedative properties, primarily due to the presence of compounds such as tiliroside, flavonoids, and essential oils that interact with the nervous and muscular systems to reduce stress, soothe muscle tension, and promote relaxation.

Therapeutic Uses and Indications

Tilia is used to alleviate symptoms of anxiety, insomnia, headaches, and digestive issues such as bloating and indigestion.

Preparation Methods and Forms

Tilia (Tilia americana) is typically prepared as a decoction, infusion, or tincture, with the flowers harvested during the blooming season and processed to extract their mucilage and flavonoid content.

Dosage and Administration

The recommended dosage of Tilia americana for herbal use typically ranges from 200 to 600 mg of standardized extract per day, divided into two or three doses, with the exact amount depending on the specific preparation and intended therapeutic effect.

Safety, Side Effects, and Contraindications

While Tilia (Tilia americana) is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses, excessive consumption may lead to gastrointestinal discomfort, drowsiness, and in rare cases, allergic reactions.

Drug Interactions

Tilia americana may interact with medications that affect the central nervous system, such as sedatives, hypnotics, or antipsychotics, potentially enhancing their effects and increasing the risk of drowsiness, respiratory depression, or other adverse reactions.

Toxicity and Precautions

Tilia americana is generally considered safe when used in recommended amounts, but it may cause mild gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals and should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation due to limited safety data.

Cultivation and Harvesting

Tilia americana is typically cultivated in temperate regions with well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, requiring moderate watering and minimal pruning to encourage healthy growth and flowering.

Processing and Extraction

Processing Tilia (Tilia americana) involves harvesting the flowers and leaves during their peak season, typically in late spring to early summer, followed by drying and grinding into a fine powder or infusing them in oil or alcohol to create tinctures and extracts for medicinal use.

Storage and Shelf Life

Tilia (Tilia americana) should be stored in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture, and its shelf life is generally up to two years when properly preserved.

Scientific Research and Evidence

Scientific research on Tilia americana has shown its potential in traditional herbal medicine, with studies indicating anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and mild sedative properties, though more rigorous clinical trials are needed to confirm its efficacy and safety for specific therapeutic applications.

Traditional and Historical Uses

Tilia americana, commonly known as American basswood, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its calming effects, particularly to alleviate anxiety, insomnia, and nervous disorders.

Regulatory Status

In the United States, Tilia americana is not classified as a controlled substance, but its use in herbal medicine is subject to regulation by the Food and Drug Administration (FDA) under the Dietary Supplement Health and Education Act (DSHEA), which requires manufacturers to ensure the safety and proper labeling of herbal products.
