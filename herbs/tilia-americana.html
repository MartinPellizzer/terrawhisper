Tilia (Tilia americana): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

What is Tilia (Tilia Americana)?

Tilia americana, commonly known as the American basswood or yellow poplar, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America, valued for its soft wood and historical use in herbal medicine for its calming and sedative properties.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Tilia is Tilia americana. This plant belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Equisetopsida, order Malvales, family Tiliaceae, genus Tilia, and species Tilia americana. Common names for this plant include American basswood in North America and lime tree in Europe. It is also known as white poplar in some regions. Synonyms for Tilia americana include Tilia heterophylla (botanical) and basswood, linden, and lime (regional).

Plant Description and Morphology

Tilia americana, commonly known as the American basswood or large-toothed aspen, is a deciduous tree that typically grows to heights of 20 to 40 meters, featuring a rounded crown, smooth gray bark that becomes fissured with age, and heart-shaped leaves with coarsely toothed margins.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Tilia americana, commonly known as the American basswood or large-leaf linden, is native to eastern North America, ranging from southern Ontario and southern Quebec through the northeastern United States down to northern Florida and western Texas.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The primary medicinal parts of Tilia (Tilia americana) are the flowers, leaves, and bark, with the flowers being most commonly used for their therapeutic properties.

Phytochemical Composition

Tilia (Tilia americana) contains a variety of phytochemical compounds, including flavonoids such as quercetin and kaempferol, along with tannins, mucilage, and volatile oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Pharmacological Properties

Tilia americana contains compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and mucilage, which contribute to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and soothing effects, making it useful in treating respiratory and digestive ailments.

Mechanisms of Action

Tilia (Tilia americana) exerts its therapeutic effects primarily through its anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and mild sedative properties, which are attributed to the presence of flavonoids, tannins, and volatile oils in its flowers and leaves.

Therapeutic Uses and Indications

Tilia americana is traditionally used in herbal medicine for its calming effects on the nervous system, particularly for alleviating symptoms of anxiety, insomnia, and stress-related disorders.

Preparation Methods and Forms

Tilia americana is typically prepared as a tea, tincture, or essential oil, with the flowers harvested during the blooming season and processed to retain their medicinal properties.

Dosage and Administration

The typical dosage of Tilia americana for herbal use ranges from 200 to 400 mg of dried leaf extract, taken two to three times daily, though specific formulations may vary based on preparation method and intended therapeutic effect.

Safety, Side Effects, and Contraindications

When used appropriately, Tilia americana is generally safe with minimal side effects, but it may cause mild gastrointestinal discomfort or allergic reactions in some individuals, and it should be avoided during pregnancy and by those with known allergies to the Tiliaceae family.

Drug Interactions

Tilia (Tilia americana) may interact with medications that affect the central nervous system, such as sedatives, antidepressants, or antihypertensive drugs, potentially enhancing their effects and increasing the risk of drowsiness, dizziness, or lowered blood pressure.

Toxicity and Precautions

Tilia americana is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses, but it may cause mild gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals and should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation due to limited safety data.

Cultivation and Harvesting

Tilia americana is typically cultivated in moist, well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade, with harvesting of its flowers and leaves occurring in late spring to early summer for optimal medicinal potency.

Processing and Extraction

The processing and extraction of Tilia (Tilia americana) involve harvesting the flowers, typically during their peak bloom season, followed by drying to reduce moisture content and preserve active compounds, after which the dried flowers are often subjected to methods such as maceration or steam distillation to isolate essential oils and other bioactive components.

Storage and Shelf Life

Tilia (Tilia americana) should be stored in an airtight container in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight to maintain its potency and prevent moisture absorption, with a typical shelf life of 1 to 2 years when properly preserved.

Scientific Research and Evidence

Scientific research on Tilia (Tilia americana) has explored its potential pharmacological properties, including anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and mild sedative effects, with some studies suggesting its use in traditional herbal medicine for respiratory and nervous system support.

Traditional and Historical Uses

Tilia (Tilia americana), commonly known as the American basswood or large-leaf linden, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its calming properties, often prepared as a tea to treat insomnia, anxiety, and headaches.
