Tilia

Tilia americana

Tilia (Tilia americana) is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America, known for its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers, which are commonly used in traditional and modern medicine to treat conditions such as anxiety, insomnia, and digestive issues.

Morphology

Tilia americana, commonly known as the American basswood or large-toothed maple, is a deciduous tree characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, and a smooth, gray-brown bark that becomes fissured with age.

Distribution

Tilia americana, commonly known as the American basswood or trembling maple, is native to eastern North America, ranging from southeastern Canada through the eastern United States, including states such as New York, Pennsylvania, and Ohio, and is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in woodlands and along stream banks.

Traditional Uses

Tilia americana has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its calming effects, respiratory support, and as a mild sedative to alleviate anxiety and insomnia.

Medicinal Systems

In traditional medicinal systems such as Ayurveda, Chinese medicine, and Native American healing practices, Tilia (Tilia americana) has been utilized for its calming and soothing properties to treat ailments like insomnia, anxiety, and digestive issues.

Phytochemistry

The phytochemistry of Tilia americana includes compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, mucilage, and essential oils, which contribute to its traditional use in herbal medicine for respiratory and digestive ailments.

Pharmacology

Tilia (Tilia americana) exhibits pharmacological properties including anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and mild sedative effects, making it useful in the treatment of respiratory and digestive disorders.

Botanical Identification

Tilia americana, commonly known as the American basswood or white basswood, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America. Belonging to the Tiliaceae family, it is distinguished by its heart-shaped leaves, pale yellow flowers, and extensive root system. It is also referred to as linden in some herbal texts.

Safety Profile

Tilia americana is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation due to limited safety data. Individuals with allergies to plants in the Malvaceae family should also avoid its use.
