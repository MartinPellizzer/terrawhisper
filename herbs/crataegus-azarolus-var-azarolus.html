Azarolus Hawthorn (Crataegus Azarolus Var. Azarolus)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

Azarolus hawthorn, scientifically known as Crataegus azarolus var. azarolus, is a medicinal herb native to the Mediterranean region, prized for its therapeutic properties and historical significance. This small shrub produces red or orange berries that have been used for centuries in traditional medicine for their cardiovascular and digestive benefits. Its primary benefits include supporting heart health by improving circulation, reducing anxiety, and aiding digestion, largely due to its high concentration of flavonoids and proanthocyanidins. In traditional Greek and Mediterranean cultures, it was commonly used to treat heart palpitations, insomnia, and digestive issues, often prepared as a tea or tincture. Modern wellness applications include its use in herbal supplements for stress relief and cardiovascular support, with its unique combination of tart flavor and potent antioxidants making it a rare and valuable plant in both historical and contemporary herbal practices.
