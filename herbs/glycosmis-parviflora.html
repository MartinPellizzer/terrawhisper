Glycosmis parviflora

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Glycosmis parviflora has small, elliptical, leathery leaves that grow in an opposite arrangement on woody stems. The plant is a perennial shrub or small tree characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and fine texture. Its flowers are tiny and white, typically appearing in small clusters or solitary axillary positions. The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that transitions from green to a bright red color when fully mature. These morphological traits help distinguish the species within the Rutaceae family.
