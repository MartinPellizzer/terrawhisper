Glycosmis parviflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Glycosmis parviflora?

Glycosmis parviflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It falls under the order Sapindales and is a member of the family Rutaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Glycosmis, and its specific species designation, parviflora.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compound Glycosparvine A along with several other bioactive alkaloids. Researchers focus on these chemical structures to determine how they might inhibit certain types of enzyme activity. Understanding the precise concentration of Glycosparvine A is essential for developing standardized herbal extracts. Ongoing clinical studies aim to isolate these compounds for use in future therapeutic treatments.

As discussed in a study, the ethyl acetate extract from the leaves of Glycosmis parviflora was investigated to identify biologically active compounds. Six compounds were identified, including a new indole alkaloid named glycosparvine A (1) and five previously known metabolites (2-6). The structure of compound 1 was elucidated using 1D/2D NMR spectroscopy and HRESIMS. Among the identified substances, compounds 3 and 6 exhibited moderate inhibitory effects against acetylcholinesterase (AChE). Specifically, compound 3 showed an IC50 value of 35.1 mM, while compound 6 had an IC50 value of 2.8 mM.
