What's the taxonomical classification of Glycosmis parviflora?

Glycosmis parviflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It falls under the order Sapindales and is a member of the family Rutaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Glycosmis, and its specific species designation, parviflora.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several bioactive alkaloids, including the specific indole alkaloid known as Glycosparvine A. These medicinal compounds are primarily found within the leaves and stems of the species. Researchers have identified various chemical constituents that contribute to its traditional pharmacological uses. The presence of these unique molecules supports the plant's reputation in ethnobotanical studies. These substances are frequently investigated for their potential biological activities in modern medicine.

According to a study published by "Natural product research", an ethyl acetate extract from the leaves of Glycosmis parviflora was investigated to find biologically active compounds. Six compounds were identified from this extract, which included a new indole alkaloid named glycosparvine A (1) and five previously known metabolites (2-6). The new compound (1) had its structure elucidated using 1D/2D NMR spectroscopy and HRESIMS. Among the identified metabolites, compounds 3 and 6 exhibited moderate inhibitory effects against acetylcholinesterase (AChE). Specifically, compound 3 showed an IC50 value of 35.1 mM, while compound 6 showed an IC50 value of 2.8 mM.
