Cyclea tonkinensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cyclea tonkinenensis has climbing woody vines characterized by cordate or ovate leaves that typically feature entire margins and prominent venation. The foliage is arranged alternately along the stems, displaying a glossy texture on the upper surfaces. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the axils of the leaves. The fruit develops into a woody capsule that eventually splits to release seeds. These morphological features are typical of many members within the Menispermaceae family.

      Cyclea tonkinensis can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or localized swelling if the plant's sap comes into direct contact with human tissue. Some reports suggest that topical application might lead to redness or itching as an inflammatory response. Because specific toxicological studies on humans are limited, the full extent of systemic toxicity remains largely unknown.
