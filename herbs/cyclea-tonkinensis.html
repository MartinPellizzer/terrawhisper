Cyclea tonkinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Cyclea tonkinensis?

Cyclea tonkinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is a member of the order Ranunculales and falls under the family Menispermaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Cyclea, and its unique species designation, tonkinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cyclea tonkinenensis has climbing woody vines characterized by cordate or ovate leaves that typically feature entire margins and prominent venation.

The foliage is arranged alternately along the stems, displaying a glossy texture on the upper surfaces. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the axils of the leaves. The fruit develops into a woody capsule that eventually splits to release seeds.

These morphological features are typical of many members within the Menispermaceae family.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Cyclea tonkinensis can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or localized swelling if the plant's sap comes into direct contact with human tissue. The chemical constituents within the plant may trigger hypersensitivity reactions in individuals with sensitive skin. Some reports suggest that topical application might lead to redness or itching as an inflammatory response. Because specific toxicological studies on humans are limited, the full extent of systemic toxicity remains largely unknown. Caution is advised when handling the plant to prevent unexpected dermatological complications.
