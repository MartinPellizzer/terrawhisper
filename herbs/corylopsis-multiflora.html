Corylopsis multiflora

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Corylopsis multiflora has woody stems, elliptical to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins, and numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in dense terminal clusters. The foliage typically exhibits a deep green color and a slightly leathery texture that aids in moisture retention. Its flowering structure is characterized by a high density of blossoms that emerge from the branch tips. The plant's overall growth habit is often shrubby, depending on its specific environmental conditions. These morphological traits collectively facilitate its ability to thrive in its native habitat.

      What are the herbal preparations of Corylopsis multiflora?

      Corylopsis multiflora is prepared primarily as a decoction or an infusion made from its dried aerial parts to extract its bioactive compounds.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Corylopsis multiflora can cause localized skin irritation, redness, and itching upon direct contact with its foliage or sap. Individuals with sensitive skin or known botanical allergies may experience heightened dermatological reactions such as contact dermatitis. Some users might also report mild gastrointestinal distress if any part of the plant is ingested accidentally. It is important to handle the specimen with gloves to prevent any immediate inflammatory response. Always consult a medical professional if a rash or systemic reaction develops after exposure.
