Berchemia flavescens

What's the taxonomical classification of Berchemia flavescens?

Berchemia flavescens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy places it under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Rosales, it is categorized under the family Rhamnaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its genus, Berchemia, and its specific epithet, flavescens.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various infections including skin diseases, intestinal parasites, and wounds. Its medicinal properties are primarily derived from its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory bioactive compounds. Local healers often utilize the plant to manage gastrointestinal issues through its potential anthelmintic effects. Additionally, topical applications of the plant extracts are applied to promote the healing of minor cuts and skin irritations. Researchers continue to investigate its phytochemical profile to better understand its efficacy against specific pathogens.
