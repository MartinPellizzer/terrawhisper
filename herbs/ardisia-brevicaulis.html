Ardisia brevicaulis

What's the taxonomical classification of Ardisia brevicaulis?

Ardisia brevicaulis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Ericales. The plant is further categorized into the family Primulaceae, falling within the genus Ardisia, and is ultimately identified by its specific species name, brevicaulis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ardisia brevicaulis has small, elliptic to oblong leaves that are arranged oppositely along its short, woody stems. The foliage typically exhibits a dark green color on the upper surface and may appear slightly lighter underneath. Its stems are notably compressed and characterized by a compact growth habit as the name suggests. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into fleshy fruits. These fruits are often used to identify the species within its specific habitat.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds calamene, caryophyllene, fitone, phytol, and cis-alpha-bisabolene. These specific bioactive molecules contribute to the diverse therapeutic potential observed in the species. The presence of these phytochemicals often relates to the plant's ability to exhibit anti-inflammatory or antimicrobial activities. Researchers study these individual constituents to understand how they interact within the botanical matrix. Such chemical profiling is essential for identifying the exact mechanisms behind its traditional medicinal uses.

According to a study published by "Zhong yao cai = Zhongyaocai = Journal of Chinese medicinal materials", 38 compounds were identified from the leaves of Ardisia brevicaulis, which represent 65.952% of the total essential oil. The main constituents of the leaves essential oil included Palmitic acid at 43.329%, Phytol at 3.142%, and Fitone at 2.430%. In the roots, 46 compounds were identified, accounting for 54.890% of the total essential oil. The main constituents of the roots essential oil were gamma-Muurolene at 14.227%, Caryophyllene at 11.592%, cis-alpha-Bisabolene at 5.222%, and Calamenene at 2.913%. Because the constituents of volatile oil extracted from the leaves and roots of Ardisia brevicaulis were different, the leaves and roots should be utilized differently in clinical application.
