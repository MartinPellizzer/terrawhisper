Ardisia brevicaulis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ardisia brevicaulis has small, elliptic to oblong leaves that are arranged oppositely along its short, woody stems. The foliage typically exhibits a dark green color on the upper surface and may appear slightly lighter underneath. Its stems are notably compressed and characterized by a compact growth habit as the name suggests. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into fleshy fruits. These fruits are often used to identify the species within its specific habitat.
