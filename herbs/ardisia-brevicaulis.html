Ardisia brevicaulis

What's the taxonomical classification of Ardisia brevicaulis?

Ardisia brevicaulis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Ericales. The plant is further categorized into the family Primulaceae, falling within the genus Ardisia, and is ultimately identified by its specific species name, brevicaulis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds calamenene, caryophyllene, phytol, cis-alpha-bisabolene, and gamma-muurolene. These specific chemical constituents contribute to the plant's diverse biological activities and therapeutic potential. The presence of caryophyllene and phytol often suggests significant anti-inflammatory or antimicrobial properties. Additionally, compounds like cis-alpha-bisabolene and gamma-muurolene are frequently studied for their roles in skin soothing and antioxidant processes. Together, these sesquiterpenes and diterpenes define the unique chemical profile of the species.

In the roots of Ardisia brevicaulis, 46 compounds were identified, representing 54.890% of the total essential oil.
