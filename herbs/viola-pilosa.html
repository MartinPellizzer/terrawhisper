Viola pilosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Viola pilosa?

Viola pilosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Malpighiales and into the family Violaceae. Ultimately, this plant is defined by its membership in the genus Viola, specifically identified by the species name pilosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viola pilosa has a perennial herbaceous structure characterized by spreading stolons, ovate to heart-shaped leaves with crenate margins, and small, five-petaled violet flowers. The leaves are typically arranged alternately along the stem and feature long petioles that emerge from a basal rosette. Its stems are often hairy, a trait reflected in its specific epithet, providing a tactile texture to the plant's surface. The flowers consist of two upper petals and three lower petals, frequently displaying light purple or white coloration with distinct nectar guides. Small, capsule-like fruits develop following pollination to facilitate seed dispersal.
