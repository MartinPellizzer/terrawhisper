Ritchiea capparoides

What's the taxonomical classification of Ritchiea capparoides?

Ritchiea capparoides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Brassicales. This species is a representative of the family Capparaceae, situated within the genus Ritchiea, and is specifically identified by its species name, capparoides.

What health problems are treated using this plant?

This plant is used for the treatment and management of various malignancies including breast cancer, cervical cancer, and liver cancer. Researchers have investigated its bioactive compounds for their potential to inhibit the growth of these specific tumor cells. The plant contains phytochemicals that may trigger apoptosis or disrupt the cell cycle in cancerous tissues. Studies suggest that these medicinal properties could offer therapeutic benefits in traditional or experimental oncology settings. Continued scientific exploration is necessary to fully understand its efficacy against these life-threatening diseases.

As discussed in a study, Ritchiea capparoides leaves (RCL) and Ritchiea capparoides stembark (RCSB) were investigated for their anticancer effects. These ethanol extracts were tested against MCF-7, HUH-7, and HeLa cell lines using a brine shrimp lethality assay and a 3-(4,5-dimethylthiazol-2-yl)-2,5-diphenyltetrazolium bromide (MTT) assay. The extracts produced significant (p < 0.05) growth inhibitory effects against all tested cell lines in a dose-dependent manner. All extracts were selective to cancer cells at varying degrees. Considerable amounts of phenolics, flavonoids, and proanthocyanidins were detected in all extracts evaluated.
