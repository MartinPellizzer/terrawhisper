Ritchiea capparoides

What's the taxonomical classification of Ritchiea capparoides?

Ritchiea capparoides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Brassicales. This species is a representative of the family Capparaceae, situated within the genus Ritchiea, and is specifically identified by its species name, capparoides.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Ritchiea capparoides contains medicinal properties found primarily in its leaves, bark, and roots. The leaves are often utilized in traditional remedies to treat various inflammatory conditions and skin ailments. Extracts from the bark are frequently employed to address digestive issues or fever. Additionally, the root system is sometimes prepared as a decoction to aid in systemic healing processes. These diverse plant parts provide a broad range of bioactive compounds used by local communities for therapeutic purposes.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Ritchiea capparoides targets various enzymes and signaling proteins, specifically inhibiting cyclooxygenase (COX) and lipoxygenase (LOX) enzymes to modulate inflammatory responses. The bioactive secondary metabolites within the plant act as ligands that interfere with the arachidonic acid metabolism pathway. By modulating these specific protein targets, the plant extracts demonstrate significant anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties in biological systems. Furthermore, certain phytochemical components may interact with cytokine-related proteins to suppress the production of pro-inflammatory mediators like tumor necrosis factor-alpha. These molecular interactions collectively underpin the plant's traditional use in treating swelling and pain.
