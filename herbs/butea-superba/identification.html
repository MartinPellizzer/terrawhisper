Botanical Identification of Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba)

To identify Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

The Red Silk Cotton Tree, Butea superba, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Fabales, order Fabales, family Fabaceae, genus Butea, and species Butea superba. This classification places it within the legume family, highlighting its botanical relationships and potential medicinal properties.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The Red Silk Cotton Tree is scientifically known as Butea superba, a name first cited by the botanist Robert Brown in 1830. It has several synonyms, including Butea frondosa and Butea monosperma, reflecting historical classification changes. Common names include Red Silk Cotton Tree, Flame Tree, and Kachnar, while regional and historical names vary across cultures and time periods.

Morphological Characteristics

The Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba) is a deciduous shrub or small tree with a straight, often multi-branched stem. Its root system is fibrous and shallow, adapting to nutrient-poor soils. Leaves are alternate, trifoliate, with elliptical leaflets. Inflorescences are terminal panicles, bearing small, yellowish-green flowers. The fruit is a woody capsule containing several winged seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

The Red Silk Cotton Tree can be distinguished by its distinctive red, silky pods and yellow-orange flowers. It has compound leaves with 7-13 pairs of lance-shaped leaflets. The bark is smooth and grey, while the root system is fibrous. It is commonly found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

The Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba) is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and warm temperatures. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils and is commonly found at altitudes between 300 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

The Red Silk Cotton Tree blooms from April to June, producing vibrant red flowers. Seed formation occurs from July to September, with seeds developing inside woody capsules. For identification purposes, the best time to harvest is during the dry season when the capsules are fully mature and open.

Phylogenetic Relationships

The Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba) is closely related to species within the genus Butea, which belongs to the legume family Fabaceae. It shares key traits with other members of this family, such as compound leaves and pod fruits, but differs in specific floral and seed characteristics that distinguish it from its closest relatives.
