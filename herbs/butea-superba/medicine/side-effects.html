10 Possible Side Effects Of Butea Superba (Red Kwao Krua)

Butea superba has some side effects when used improperly, such as allergic reactions, interactions with medications, and excessive consumption leading to insomnia, dizziness, and stomach issues.

These side effects can be caused by individual sensitivities or combinations with other substances, including prescription medications.

For example, excessive dizziness can cause falls and injuries, while insomnia can lead to decreased productivity and mood disorders, negatively impacting daily life and overall well-being.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Butea superba if used imporperly.

Butea superba causes allergic reactions due to its high concentration of isoflavones and alkaloids.

These compounds can stimulate the immune system, leading to an overactive response and resulting in symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling. In some cases, severe allergic reactions may occur, triggering anaphylaxis or other life-threatening conditions.

The exact mechanisms behind this reaction are not fully understood, but it is believed that individual sensitivities to these compounds play a significant role.
