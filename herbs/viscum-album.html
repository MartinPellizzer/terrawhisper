Viscum album

What's the taxonomical classification of Viscum album?

Viscum album belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Santalales and is a member of the family Santalaceae. Finally, its taxonomic hierarchy is completed by its designation within the genus Viscum and the specific species album.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Viscum album contains viscotoxins and lectins, specifically mistletoe lectins such as Viscum album Lectin I, which possess cytotoxic properties used in cancer research and immunotherapy. These proteins work by inducing apoptosis in target cells and inhibiting protein synthesis. The plant also contains various organic acids, flavonoids, and saponins that contribute to its biological activity. Some studies suggest these compounds may help modulate the immune system during treatment. Research continues into how these specific chemical constituents interact with human cellular pathways.

What are the therapeutic actions of Viscum album?

Viscum album has immunomodulatory and antineoplastic properties that are primarily used in homeopathic and complementary medicine to support the body during cancer treatments. The plant contains lectins, which are proteins known to induce apoptosis in certain malignant cell lines by disrupting cell membrane integrity. It is frequently utilized to help stimulate the immune system and mitigate the side effects of chemotherapy and radiation. Additionally, its therapeutic application often focuses on regulating autonomic nervous system functions to manage symptoms like fever or circulatory issues. While research continues, its most significant clinical relevance remains centered on its potential to enhance host defense mechanisms against tumor growth.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various forms of cancer, including breast, lung, and prostate malignancies, as well as several infectious and inflammatory conditions. In homeopathic practice, it is often employed to manage symptoms of chronic inflammation and skin disorders. Practitioners sometimes utilize it to address respiratory issues such as coughs or asthma symptoms. Additionally, it is believed to assist with neurological symptoms and certain types of tumors within the lymphatic system. Research continues into how its bioactive components might support the immune system during conventional cancer treatments.
