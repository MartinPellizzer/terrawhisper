What's the taxonomical classification of Campylandra ensifolia?

Campylandra ensifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Asparagales, it is a member of the family Asparagaceae. This specific plant is characterized by its placement in the genus Campylandra, with the species designation ensifolia completing its scientific classification.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Campylandra ensifolia has long, sword-shaped leaves that are linear in form and possess parallel venation typical of monocots. These leaves emerge from a basal rosette and can reach significant lengths relative to the plant's overall stature. The margins of the foliage are smooth and lack serration, contributing to a streamlined appearance. Its stems are typically slender and support small, inconspicuous flowers. The root system is composed of sturdy rhizomes that allow the plant to spread through the soil.
