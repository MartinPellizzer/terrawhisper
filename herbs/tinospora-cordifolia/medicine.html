What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Tinospora Cordifolia (Giloy)?

Tinospora cordifolia has health benefits such as reducing fever, inflammation, and improving immunity.

The herb contains medicinal constituents like glycosides, alkaloids, and saponins, which contribute to its therapeutic properties. These compounds are often prepared into various medicinal forms like capsules, tablets, and decoctions, making it convenient for consumption. While giloy is generally considered safe, it may cause gastrointestinal upset or allergic reactions in some individuals.

As with any herbal remedy, it is essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using giloy, especially if one has pre-existing medical conditions or is pregnant/breastfeeding.

What are the health benefits of Tinospora cordifolia?

Tinospora cordifolia has health benefits such as boosting immunity, reducing inflammation, and exhibiting anti-oxidant properties.

It has been traditionally used to treat various ailments including fever, cough, and skin disorders. Studies have shown that giloy extract has antimicrobial and antiviral activities, making it effective against viral infections like COVID-19.

Additionally, its adaptogenic properties help in reducing stress and anxiety levels.

What are the active constituents of Tinospora cordifolia?

The health benefits of Tinospora cordifolia comes from its active constituents, such as flavonoids like quercetin and kaempferol, saponins, alkaloids, and glycosides.

These compounds have been found to possess anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which may contribute to the herb's traditional uses in Ayurvedic medicine for treating various ailments, including fever, respiratory issues, and digestive problems.

What are the medicinal preparations of Tinospora cordifolia?

Tinospora cordifolia has different medicinal preparations, such as powder, tablets, capsules, and decoction.

The powder is used to treat respiratory issues like bronchitis and asthma, while the tablets and capsules are prescribed for fever, rheumatoid arthritis, and skin conditions. Decoction, made by boiling the herb in water, is used to treat digestive problems like diarrhea, dysentery, and jaundice.

These preparations can be taken orally or applied topically depending on the specific condition being treated.

What are the possible side effect of using Tinospora cordifolia improperly?

Improper use of Tinospora cordifolia increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, stomach cramps, and dizziness.

It may also cause allergic reactions like hives, itching, and swelling.

In severe cases, it can lead to kidney or liver damage, low blood pressure, respiratory distress, and even interact with medications like anticoagulants, anti-diabetics, and sedatives, causing adverse effects on the heart, kidneys, and central nervous system.

What precautions to take when using Tinospora cordifolia medicinally?

Before using Tinospora cordifolia for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as consulting with a healthcare professional, especially if you have a pre-existing medical condition or are pregnant/breastfeeding.

Additionally, it is essential to purchase high-quality products from a reputable source and follow the recommended dosage and usage guidelines.
