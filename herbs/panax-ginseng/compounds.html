Panax Ginseng (Panax ginseng) Compounds

Panax ginseng contains several primary active compounds, including saponins, glycosides, and alkaloids. These compounds are responsible for the herb's medicinal properties, such as enhancing cognitive function and boosting energy. Other notable components include terpenoids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects.

Panax Ginseng (Panax ginseng) Monograph

1. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Panax ginseng. It contributes to the plant's adaptogenic properties, supporting stress resistance and immune function. Saponins also exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, making them important for the therapeutic use of ginseng in traditional medicine.

2. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Panax ginseng. It contributes to the plant's adaptogenic properties, enhancing physical and mental performance. Glycosides in ginseng are known for their ability to modulate stress responses and support immune function. They are primarily responsible for the therapeutic effects associated with this traditional herbal medicine.

3. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Panax ginseng. It contributes to the plant's pharmacological effects, including potential cognitive and anti-inflammatory benefits. Alkaloids in ginseng may support energy and immune function, though their exact mechanisms are still under research.

4. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Panax ginseng. They contribute to the plant's medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and immune-modulating effects. These compounds are responsible for many of the therapeutic actions attributed to ginseng in traditional and modern herbal medicine.

5. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of compounds found in Panax ginseng. They contribute to the plant's antioxidant properties and may support immune function and cardiovascular health. These compounds are often present alongside ginsenosides and play a role in the overall therapeutic effects of ginseng.

6. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key compound found in Panax ginseng. It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties and may support immune function and cellular health. This compound is part of the complex phytochemical profile that gives ginseng its traditional medicinal value.

7. Tannin

Tannin is a compound found in Panax ginseng. It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health. Tannins also act as antioxidants, helping to neutralize free radicals in the body. These properties make tannin an important component of ginseng's therapeutic effects.

8. Coumarin

Coumarin is a compound found in Panax ginseng. It contributes to the plant's aromatic properties and may have mild anticoagulant effects. While not a primary active constituent, coumarin supports the overall therapeutic profile of ginseng by enhancing its bioavailability and synergistic effects with other compounds.

9. Essential oil

Essential oil is a volatile compound found in Panax ginseng. It contributes to the plant's aromatic profile and may support its traditional use in enhancing vitality and cognitive function. The oil contains sesquiterpene hydrocarbons and other bioactive components. It is often extracted through steam distillation from the roots and leaves of the plant.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Panax ginseng. It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties and may support cellular health. This pigment is part of the complex array of compounds in ginseng that contribute to its traditional use in herbal medicine.
