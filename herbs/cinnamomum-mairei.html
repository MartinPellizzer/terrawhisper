What's the taxonomical classification of Cinnamomum mairei?

Cinnamomum mairei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Laurales and is a member of the family Lauraceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by the genus Cinnamomum and the species mairei.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cinnamomum mairei has lanceolate to ovate leaves that are dark green, coriaceous, and possess an entire margin with a distinctively aromatic quality when crushed. The foliage typically displays an alternate arrangement along the woody stems of the evergreen tree. These leaves often feature an acute apex and a cuneate base, contributing to a streamlined silhouette. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge in panicles, eventually giving way to small, fleshy drupes. The bark is characteristically smooth and grayish, protecting the structural integrity of the maturing plant.

The species is often found growing in moist, well-drained soils along mountain slopes and stream banks.
