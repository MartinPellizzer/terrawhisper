Rosa mairei

What's the taxonomical classification of Rosa mairei?

Rosa mairei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving through its hierarchical organization, the plant is part of the order Rosales and falls under the family Rosaceae. Finally, it is categorized within the genus Rosa, specifically identified by the species name mairei.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rosa mairei has arching, deciduous stems that are armed with sharp, recurved prickles and bear pinnately compound leaves consisting of five to seven ovate, serrated leaflets. The foliage is characterized by a soft, pubescent texture on the undersides, which provides a distinct tactile quality to the shrub. During the flowering season, the plant produces solitary or small clusters of fragrant, single or semi-double pink to white flowers. These blooms emerge from terminal buds and are supported by pedicels that vary in length depending on the specific cultivar. After flowering, the plant develops small, fleshy red hips that serve as its primary fruit structure.
