Pollia japonica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pollia japonica has perennial, rhizomatous growth habits characterized by narrow, lanceolate leaves that emerge in dense, basal clusters. These leaves are typically bright green and reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters, often forming a thick carpet on the forest floor. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale flowers that are held in inflorescences within the leaf axils. Following flowering, it develops highly distinctive, metallic blue berries that are tightly packed into compact, spherical clusters. These brilliant, iridescent fruits are the most striking morphological feature of the species and serve to attract avian dispersers.
