Garcinia hanburyi

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Garcinia hanburyi has a woody, evergreen habit characterized by spreading branches and elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and paler underneath. The foliage is arranged in an opposite pattern along the stems, providing a dense canopy. Its inflorescences consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the axils of the leaves. These flowers eventually give rise to fleshy, globose or ovoid fruits that transition in color as they mature. The tree's structure is robust, supporting the weight of its heavy, fruit-bearing limbs.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Garcinia hanburyi contains high concentrations of xanthones, specifically alpha-mangostin and gamma-mangostin, which provide its primary pharmacological properties. These bioactive polyphenolic compounds exhibit significant antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antibacterial activities within biological systems. Research suggests these substances may also possess cytotoxic effects against certain cancer cell lines. Additionally, the plant extracts have been studied for their potential to regulate metabolic processes and reduce oxidative stress. The complex chemical profile of the fruit and bark makes it a subject of interest for developing new therapeutic agents.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

These multi-target mechanisms contribute to the plant's observed anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

      What are the herbal preparations of Garcinia hanburyi?

      Garcinia hanburyi is prepared as a decoction, a concentrated infusion, or a topical paste derived from its bark, leaves, and fruit. Traditional medicinal practitioners often boil the woody bark in water to create a bitter liquid used for internal consumption. The juice extracted from the ripe fruit can also be used as a digestive aid or a flavoring agent in local culinary practices. In certain regional traditions, crushed leaves are applied directly to the skin as a poultice to treat external ailments.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Garcinia hanburyi can cause gastrointestinal distress, allergic skin reactions, and potential interference with metabolic enzymes if consumed in high quantities. While specific clinical trials on humans are limited, the high concentration of xanthones and tannins in the plant may irritate the digestive lining. Some individuals might experience nausea or abdominal discomfort after ingestion. There is also a risk of contact dermatitis if the sap touches sensitive skin. Because of these unknown long-term effects, caution is advised when using the plant for medicinal purposes.
