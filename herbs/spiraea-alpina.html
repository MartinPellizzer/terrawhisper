Spiraea alpina

What's the taxonomical classification of Spiraea alpina?

Spiraea alpina belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized as Equisetopsida, falling under the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Rosales and the family Rosaceae. Finally, the plant is placed within the genus Spiraea, with its specific designation being the species alpinus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Spiraea alpina has erect, woody stems that reach heights of 20 to 60 centimeters and feature leaves that are lanceolate to narrowly elliptical with finely serrated margins. The foliage is typically a bright green color and is arranged alternately along the slender branches. During the flowering season, the plant produces dense, flat-topped clusters of small, white, five-petaled flowers. These inflorescences emerge from the terminal ends of the stems to create a striking visual display. The overall structure is characterized by a bushy, multi-stemmed growth habit suitable for alpine environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of western North America, specifically spanning from Alaska and the Yukon through British Columbia and Washington to the Rocky Mountains in Colorado. It typically inhabits subalpine meadows, mountain slopes, and rocky crevices where moisture is consistent. Within these environments, the species thrives in tundra-like conditions and moist alpine zones. Its distribution is largely dictated by elevation and the availability of cool, damp habitats.
