Spiraea alpina

What's the taxonomical classification of Spiraea alpina?

Spiraea alpina belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized as Equisetopsida, falling under the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Rosales and the family Rosaceae. Finally, the plant is placed within the genus Spiraea, with its specific designation being the species alpinus.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Spiraea alpina contains various secondary metabolites including phenolic compounds, flavonoids, and tannins that contribute to its chemical profile. These bioactive constituents are often studied for their potential antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Research into the genus Spiraea frequently highlights the presence of salicylic acid derivatives, which are historically significant in medicinal chemistry. The specific concentration of these compounds can vary depending on the plant's environmental conditions and growth stage. Understanding these chemical markers is essential for exploring the plant's possible pharmacological applications.

What are the therapeutic actions of Spiraea alpina?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments such as fever, inflammation, and digestive distress. Historical medicinal uses suggest its components may help regulate body temperature during illness. Some traditional practices also incorporate the plant to soothe skin irritations or reduce swelling. While it has a history of use, modern scientific validation for these specific therapeutic claims remains limited. Always consult a healthcare professional before using any wild plant for medicinal purposes.
