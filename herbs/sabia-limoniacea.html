Sabia limoniacea

What's the taxonomical classification of Sabia limoniacea?

Sabia limoniacea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows a lineage that traces back through the order Proteales. This plant is specifically situated within the family Sabiaceae and the genus Sabia, ultimately being identified by its unique species designation, limoniacea.

What are the pharmacological activities of Sabia limoniacea?

This plant has nitric oxide inhibitory activity, antioxidant properties, and anti-inflammatory effects mediated by its bioactive secondary metabolites. Research indicates that the extracts from Sabia limoniacea can effectively regulate various biochemical pathways to reduce oxidative stress within cellular environments. These pharmacological actions are largely attributed to the presence of specific phenolic compounds and flavonoids found in its leaves and bark. By inhibiting the production of nitric oxide, the plant helps mitigate excessive inflammatory responses in biological systems. Such diverse medicinal properties suggest that the species holds significant potential for future drug development.

As discussed in a study, eighteen undescribed oleanane-type tritriterpenoids were isolated and characterized from the stems of Sabia limoniacea. This group of compounds included five nortriterpenoids (1-5) and nine normal tritriterpenoids (6-14), plus five previously described analogs (15-19). Specifically, compounds 1-3 are oleanane-type tritriterpenoids where the ring A is broken to form a 5/6/6/6/6 pentacyclic skeleton. Compounds 4 and 5 represent a variation where the C-1/C-3 bonds are cleaved to create a 0/6/6/6/6 tetracyclic skeleton. Biological evaluation showed that compounds 9 and 10 can inhibit nitric oxide production in lipopolysaccharide stimulated RAW264.7 cells. These two compounds displayed IC50 values of 36.40 and 36.52 μM, respectively.
