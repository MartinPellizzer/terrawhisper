Sabia limoniacea

What's the taxonomical classification of Sabia limoniacea?

Sabia limoniacea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows a lineage that traces back through the order Proteales. This plant is specifically situated within the family Sabiaceae and the genus Sabia, ultimately being identified by its unique species designation, limoniacea.

What are the pharmacological activities of Sabia limoniacea?

This plant has potent pharmacological activities including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and nitric oxide inhibitory activity. These biological properties are primarily attributed to the presence of various bioactive secondary metabolites found within its chemical profile. Specifically, the ability to inhibit nitric oxide production suggests a significant role in modulating inflammatory responses in the body. Such mechanisms may offer therapeutic potential for treating conditions characterized by oxidative stress and chronic inflammation. Further investigation into these pathways could reveal more specific medicinal applications for the species.

According to a study published by "Phytochemistry", eighteen undescribed oleanane-type tritriterpenoids were isolated from the stems of Sabia limoniacea. This group includes five nortriterpenoids (1-5) and nine normal tritriterpenoids (6-14), as well as five previously described analogs (15-19). Biological evaluation of these compounds demonstrated specific pharmacological activities. Compounds 9 and 10 inhibited nitric oxide production in lipopolysaccharide stimulated RAW264.7 cells. The IC50 values for these activities were 36.40 μM for compound 9 and 36.52 μM for compound 10.
