Sabia limoniacea

What's the taxonomical classification of Sabia limoniacea?

Sabia limoniacea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows a lineage that traces back through the order Proteales. This plant is specifically situated within the family Sabiaceae and the genus Sabia, ultimately being identified by its unique species designation, limoniacea.

    What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

    This plant contains medicinal compounds including normal triterpenoids, nortriterpenoids, oleanane-type triterpenoids, ring-A-seco triterpenoids, and Sabialimons S-Z6. These diverse chemical constituents contribute to the complex biological profile of the species. The presence of specific oleanane-type structures often suggests potential anti-inflammatory or antioxidant properties. Additionally, the unique ring-A-seco triterpenoids and Sabialimons S-Z6 are critical components of its phytochemical makeup. Studying these various substances helps researchers understand the plant's overall therapeutic value.

    As discussed in a study, eighteen undescribed oleanane-type tritriterpenoids, specifically Sabialimons S-Z6, were isolated and characterized from the stems of Sabia limoniacea. This group includes five nortriterpenoids numbered 1-5 and nine normal tritriterpenoids numbered 6-14, in addition to five previously described analogs numbered 15-19. Among these, compounds 1-3 possess a 5/6/6/6/6 pentacyclic skeleton due to a broken ring A, while compounds 4 and 5 feature a 0/6/6/6/6 tetracyclic skeleton. Biological evaluation of the medicinal compounds showed that compounds 9 and 10 inhibited nitric oxide production in lipopolysaccharide stimulated RAW264.7 cells. These specific compounds demonstrated IC50 values of 36.40 and 36.52 μM, respectively.
