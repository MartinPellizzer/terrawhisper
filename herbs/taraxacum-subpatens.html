Dandelion (Taraxacum Subpatens)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Taraxacum subpatens.

Dandelion, scientifically known as Taraxacum subpatens, is a medicinal herb widely recognized for its adaptogenic properties and nutritional value. This perennial plant, often considered a weed, has been used for centuries in traditional medicine to support liver health, digestion, and detoxification. Its primary benefits include aiding in the treatment of liver disorders, reducing inflammation, and promoting healthy skin due to its high content of antioxidants and vitamins. In traditional Chinese medicine and European herbalism, dandelion has been employed as a diuretic, a liver tonic, and a remedy for digestive ailments. Modern wellness practices continue to embrace dandelion, incorporating it into teas, supplements, and culinary dishes, while its unique feature of a bright yellow flower and a milky sap containing compounds like taraxacin make it both distinctive and valuable in natural medicine.
