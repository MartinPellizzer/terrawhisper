What's the taxonomical classification of Sedum przewalskii?

Sedum przewalskii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Saxifragales and into the family Crassulaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Sedum, with its specific designation being the species przewalskii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sedum przewalskii has succulent, cylindrical, light green leaves that are approximately 1 to 2 centimeters in diameter and grow in dense, spreading mats. These fleshy leaves are arranged in tight rosettes along creeping stems that form a ground-covering carpet. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that typically appear in shades of pale yellow or creamy white. These blossoms are clustered in terminal cymes atop the stems during the summer months. The overall texture of the foliage is smooth, providing a thick, structural appearance to the low-growing perennial.
