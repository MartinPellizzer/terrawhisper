Ephedra (Ephedra Macrocephala)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Ephedra macrocephala.

Ephedra, scientifically known as Ephedra macrocephala, is a medicinal herb native to China and parts of Central Asia, traditionally used for its stimulating and therapeutic properties. This plant is classified as an adaptogen and has been historically valued for its ability to enhance physical performance and endurance. The primary benefits of Ephedra include its role in alleviating respiratory conditions such as asthma and bronchitis, as well as its capacity to increase energy levels and reduce fatigue. In traditional Chinese medicine, Ephedra has been used for centuries to treat cold symptoms, coughs, and inflammation, often in the form of herbal formulations. Modern wellness applications have seen it incorporated into supplements for weight loss and athletic performance, though its use is now regulated due to potential side effects, and it contains the rare active compound ephedrine, which has been historically linked to significant health risks.
