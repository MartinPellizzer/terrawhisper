Senna notabilis

Senna notabilis, commonly known as the desert senna, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, including treating constipation, diarrhea, and skin conditions, due to its purgative properties.

The therapeutic actions of Senna notabilis are attributed to its ability to stimulate bowel movements and reduce inflammation, which is further supported by its bioactive constituents, including anthraquinone glycosides and flavonoids.
