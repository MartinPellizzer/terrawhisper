Peruvian Quinine Tree (Cinchona Cordifolia Var. Peruviana)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Cinchona cordifolia var. peruviana.

Peruvian quinine tree, scientifically known as Cinchona cordifolia var. peruviana, is a medicinal plant native to the Andean regions of South America, renowned for its historical and contemporary use in traditional and modern medicine. This tree is primarily valued as a source of quinine, a potent alkaloid used to treat malaria, and also contains other bioactive compounds that contribute to its medicinal properties. Traditionally, indigenous communities have used the bark of the Peruvian quinine tree to alleviate fever, digestive issues, and as a general tonic for overall health. In modern wellness practices, it is incorporated into herbal supplements and alternative medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antimalarial effects. The tree is also notable for its unique bitter taste and historical significance, as it played a crucial role in the development of antimalarial treatments during the 19th century.
