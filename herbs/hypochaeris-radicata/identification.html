Botanical Identification of Yellow Hawkweed (Hypochaeris radicata)

To identify Yellow Hawkweed (Hypochaeris radicata) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Yellow Hawkweed, or Hypochaeris radicata, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Spermatophyta, class Caryophyllopsida, order Brassicales, family Brassicaceae, genus Hypochaeris, and species Hypochaeris radicata. It is a flowering plant commonly found in disturbed soils and open areas.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Yellow Hawkweed is Hypochaeris radicata, first described by Carl von Linné in 1753. It has several synonyms, including Leucanthemum radicatum and Tanacetum radicatum. Common names include Yellow Hawkweed, Common Hawkweed, and Yellow Buttercup. It is also known by regional and historical names such as Wild Lettuce and Goldcup.

Morphological Characteristics

Yellow Hawkweed is a herbaceous perennial with a basal rosette of leaves. It has a fibrous root system and upright, branching stems. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and hairy. The inflorescence is a single, terminal head of yellow flowers with ray florets. The fruit is a cypsela, and the seeds are small, wind-dispersed.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Yellow Hawkweed can be distinguished by its yellow flowers with five narrow petals, basal leaves that are heart-shaped and toothed, and a hollow, hairless stem. It differs from similar species like Oxeye Daisy by having smaller flowers and shorter, more upright leaves. Its root system is fibrous and rhizomatous, aiding in identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Yellow Hawkweed is native to Europe and parts of Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils. It is commonly found at low to mid-altitude regions, typically between 0 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Yellow Hawkweed blooms from late spring to early summer, typically between May and July, depending on the climate. Seed formation occurs in late summer, around August to September. For identification purposes, the best time to observe key features is during the blooming period, when the yellow flowers are most visible.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Yellow Hawkweed (Hypochaeris radicata) belongs to the genus Hypochaeris in the family Asteraceae. It is closely related to species like Hypochaeris glabrata and Hypochaeris hypochaeris. These species share similar morphological traits, including the presence of yellow composite flowers and basal rosettes. The genus Hypochaeris is part of the tribe Senecioneae, which includes other composite plants with similar ecological and structural characteristics.
