What's the taxonomical classification of Iris anguifuga?

Iris anguifuga belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is positioned in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Asparagales. Further classification places the plant in the family Iridaceae, where it is categorized under the genus Iris, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species anguifuga.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Iris anguifuga has narrow, linear leaves that are significantly thinner than those of many related species, accompanied by slender stems that support solitary, pale lavender to white flowers. Each flower features delicate petals with subtle veining and a specialized structure known as a falls that guides pollinators toward the center. The rhizomes are creeping and sturdy, allowing the plant to spread slowly through its native habitat.
