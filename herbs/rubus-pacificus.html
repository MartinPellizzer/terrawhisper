What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rubus pacificus has arching, woody stems known as canes that are densely covered in sharp, recurved prickles and feature small, glandular hairs. These leaflets are often characterized by a finely textured, pubescent underside that provides a subtle contrast to the top. Small, white to pale pink flowers emerge from the terminal ends of the branches, consisting of five delicate petals surrounding a central cluster of stamens.
