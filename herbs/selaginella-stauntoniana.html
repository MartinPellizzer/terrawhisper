Selaginella stauntoniana

What's the taxonomical classification of Selaginella stauntoniana?

Selaginella stauntoniana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Lycopodiidae and is further organized into the order Selaginellales. This plant is a representative of the family Selaginellaceae and is situated within the genus Selaginella, ultimately reaching its specific taxonomic designation at the species level as stauntoniana.

What are the herbal preparations of Selaginella stauntoniana?

Selaginella stauntoniana is prepared as a decoction, a concentrated liquid extract made by boiling the plant material in water to release its active compounds. This traditional method allows for the ingestion of the medicinal properties through tea-like infusions. Some practitioners may also utilize the dried plant material to create topical poultices for external applications. In certain regional practices, the plant is processed into powders that can be mixed with other substances. These various preparation methods ensure that the specific therapeutic components of the species are effectively utilized for different health purposes.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Selaginella stauntoniana can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or respiratory distress if its spores or leaves are handled without protection. While primarily known as an ornamental plant, direct contact with the foliage may trigger redness or itching in sensitive individuals. Inhaling fine particles or dust from the plant could potentially irritate the mucous membranes of the throat and lungs. There is also a risk of accidental ingestion leading to gastrointestinal upset if the plant is consumed. Maintaining distance from the plant during cleaning or repotting can help mitigate these physical reactions.
