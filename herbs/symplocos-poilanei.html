Symplocos poilanei

What's the taxonomical classification of Symplocos poilanei?

Symplocos poilanei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Ericales. This plant is a member of the family Symplocaceae, specifically falling under the genus Symplocos and identified by the species epithet poilanei.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Symplocos poilanei has simple, alternate, elliptic to oblong leaves that are typically coriaceous with entire margins and acute or acuminate apices.

The leaf surfaces often exhibit a lustrous quality, while the undersides may possess subtle pubescence depending on the specific specimen. The plant features small, inconspicuous flowers that are generally actinomorphic and borne in axillary clusters. Its reproductive structures develop into small, fleshy drupes that serve as the primary fruit type.

The overall growth habit is that of a woody shrub or small tree with a structured branching pattern.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Symplocos poilanei targets various inflammatory and anti-tumor proteins including cyclooxygenase-2, tumor necrosis factor-alpha, and various matrix metalloproteinases. Its bioactive compounds, particularly triterpenoids and flavonoids, interact with these enzymes to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory mediators. These interactions help regulate the arachidonic acid metabolism pathway to alleviate pain and swelling. Furthermore, the plant's chemical constituents can modulate apoptotic pathways by interacting with caspases and Bcl-2 family proteins in cancer cells. This multi-target mechanism explains its diverse pharmacological applications in treating inflammatory diseases and certain malignancies.

What are the therapeutic actions of Symplocos poilanei?

Symplocos poilanei has therapeutic properties derived from its bark that include astringent, bitter, antidiarrheal, and hemostatic actions.

The bitter components of the plant help stimulate digestion and improve appetite through its gustatory effects. Its potent astringent qualities allow it to effectively constrict tissues and control bleeding as a hemostatic agent. Additionally, these medicinal characteristics make the plant a useful traditional remedy for treating various forms of diarrhea.

This combination of pharmacological effects supports its use in managing gastrointestinal and circulatory issues.

    What are the herbal preparations of Symplocos poilanei?

    Symplocos poilanei is prepared primarily as a decoction by boiling the dried leaves or bark in water to extract its medicinal compounds. This liquid infusion is often consumed orally to treat various ailments associated with traditional Chinese medicine. In some regional practices, the plant material may also be processed into a powder that is mixed with warm water or honey. Some preparations involve using the plant in topical poultices to address external skin conditions. These diverse methods allow for different concentrations of the active phytochemicals to be delivered to the body.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Symplocos poilanei can cause allergic skin reactions, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if consumed in uncontrolled amounts. While some studies explore its medicinal properties, the lack of extensive clinical data on human consumption poses significant risks. Ingesting parts of the plant may lead to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain in sensitive individuals. Contact with the plant material could also trigger localized dermatitis or rashes on the skin. Due to these uncertainties, careful caution is necessary when interacting with this species.
