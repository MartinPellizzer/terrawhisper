Symplocos poilanei

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Symplocos poilanei has simple, alternate, elliptic to oblong leaves that are typically coriaceous with entire margins and acute or acuminate apices. The leaf surfaces often exhibit a lustrous quality, while the undersides may possess subtle pubescence depending on the specific specimen. The plant features small, inconspicuous flowers that are generally actinomorphic and borne in axillary clusters. Its reproductive structures develop into small, fleshy drupes that serve as the primary fruit type. The overall growth habit is that of a woody shrub or small tree with a structured branching pattern.

Symplocos poilanei has therapeutic properties derived from its bark that include astringent, bitter, antidiarrheal, and hemostatic actions.
