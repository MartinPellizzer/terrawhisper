10 Possible Side Effects Of Xanthium Strumarium (Cocklebur)

Xanthium strumarium has some side effects when used improperly, such as allergic reactions, digestive problems, and skin irritation.

These side effects may be caused by excessive consumption, individual allergies, or interactions with other medications.

For example, if a person is prone to allergies, ingesting cocklebur may exacerbate respiratory issues, making daily activities more challenging and impacting overall quality of life.

Xanthium strumarium causes allergic reactions due to its sap containing allergenic compounds such as sesquiterpenes and alkaloids.

These bioactive molecules can stimulate an immune response, leading to symptoms like skin irritation, itching, and swelling.

Additionally, the plant's seeds have been shown to release histamine, a chemical mediator involved in allergic reactions, upon contact with human skin or mucous membranes, further exacerbating allergic responses.

This cascade of events can result in symptoms such as rapid heartbeat, difficulty breathing, and swelling of the face, throat, or other areas.
