Rhaphidophora hongkongensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhaphidophora hongkongensis has a body structure characterized by elongated, slender wings with a distinct pattern of longitudinal veins and a series of fine, transverse striations. Its thorax is relatively narrow and serves as the attachment point for the delicate, lace-like wings. The abdomen is segmented and typically tapers toward the posterior end. Small, articulated legs extend from the ventral side to facilitate movement across various surfaces.
