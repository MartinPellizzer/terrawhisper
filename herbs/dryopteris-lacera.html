Dryopteris lacera

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dryopteris lacera has bipinnate-pinnatifid fronds that are characterized by deeply incised, laciniate segments and a distinctively leathery texture. The stipes are typically dark brown or blackish and are covered in pale, appressed scales. Each pinna features lobes that are irregularly cut, giving the foliage a jagged or shredded appearance. The rachis is sturdy and supports a complex arrangement of pinnules that vary in size along the length of the blade. These morphological features distinguish it from other members of the Dryopteris genus through its highly dissected margin.
