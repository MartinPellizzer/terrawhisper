Mussaenda erosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Mussaenda erosa has woody, evergreen stems with oppositely arranged, ovate to elliptical leaves that feature distinctively eroded or irregularly wavy margins. The foliage is characterized by a glossy texture and prominent venation that runs from the midrib toward the edges. Its most striking morphological feature is the presence of large, petal-like colorful sepals that surround the smaller, tubular true flowers. These modified sepals typically appear in vibrant shades of red or pink to attract pollinators. The plant structure often forms a dense, spreading shrub capable of reaching significant heights in tropical environments.

The species is most frequently documented in areas characterized by high rainfall and consistent tropical temperatures.
