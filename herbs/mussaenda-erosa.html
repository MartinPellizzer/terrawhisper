Mussaenda erosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Mussaenda erosa?

Mussaenda erosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Gentianaales. This plant is a representative of the family Rubiaceae, specifically situated within the genus Mussaenda, and is identified by its unique species name, erosa.

Mussaenda erosa has medicinal properties primarily used in traditional practices to treat skin ailments, wounds, and various inflammatory conditions.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Mussaenda erosa can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or gastrointestinal distress if ingested or touched directly. While the plant is widely used as an ornamental shrub, its chemical constituents have not been extensively studied for human toxicity. Some individuals may experience contact dermatitis characterized by redness or itching upon contact with the foliage or bracts. If the plant parts are consumed accidentally, it could potentially lead to nausea or stomach discomfort. Due to this lack of comprehensive clinical data, caution should be exercised when handling the plant or exposing sensitive skin to it.
