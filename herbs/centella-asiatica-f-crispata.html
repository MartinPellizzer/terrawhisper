Centella asiatica f. crispata

Centella asiatica f. crispata, commonly known as Indian pennywort or Gotu Kola, is a variant of the Centella asiatica plant that features a more compact and crinkled leaf structure.

This plant is renowned for its numerous health benefits, including its ability to improve cognitive function, reduce inflammation, and promote wound healing.

The therapeutic actions of Centella asiatica f. crispata are attributed to its antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and neuroprotective properties, which are thought to be mediated by its bioactive constituents, including triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and alkaloids.

The bioactive constituents of Centella asiatica f. crispata contribute to its potential therapeutic applications in the treatment of various health conditions, including anxiety, depression, and neurodegenerative diseases.
