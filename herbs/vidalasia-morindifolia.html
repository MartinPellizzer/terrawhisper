What is Vidalasia morindifolia?

Vidalasia morindifolia is a species of flowering plant in the family Rubiaceae.

It is a shrub with pubescent branches, opposite, elliptic to lanceolate leaves that are 4-12 cm long and 1.5-4 cm wide, and inflorescences that are axillary, corymbose, and 2-5 cm long. The flowers are small, yellowish, and 4-merous, with a calyx that is 1-2 mm long and a corolla that is 2-4 mm long. The plant is native to the tropical regions of Asia and has been found in the wild in countries such as China, India, and Southeast Asia.
