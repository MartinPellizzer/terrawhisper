Possible Side Effects of Campanula puberula

Campanula puberula can have side effects if used improperly, such as gastrointestinal problems, including diarrhea and stomach cramps, due to its high concentration of saponins.

Prolonged use may also lead to allergic reactions, such as skin irritation and respiratory issues, in individuals sensitive to the plant's compounds.

Ingesting large quantities can cause nausea and vomiting, while topical application may result in skin redness and itching.

Additionally, campanula puberula may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes medications, potentially exacerbating underlying health conditions.
