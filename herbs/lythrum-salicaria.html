Lythrum (Lythrum salicaria)

Lythrum (Lythrum salicaria) is a member of the Lythraceae family, native to Europe, Asia, and North America. Traditionally, its flowers, leaves, and stems have been used for infusions, decoctions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, astringent, and diuretic actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, native american herbal medicine, and traditional chinese medicine.

Broad leaves with upper surface reddish-purple and lower surface pale green, bearing distinct stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Flowers are typically pink to reddish-purple in color, arranged in dense spike-like clusters, exhibit actinomorphic symmetry, and possess four to five lanceolate petals with a distinct central stamen cluster.
Stems
Erect, woody, herbaceous stems with opposite branching, smooth surfaces, and prominently ridged, tetragonal cross-sections.
