Kadsura oblongifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Kadsura oblongifolia?

Kadsura oblongifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Austrobaileyales. This plant is further classified under the family Schisandraceae, falling within the genus Kadsura, and is specifically identified by the species name oblongifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Kadsura oblongifolia has an erect, woody perennial habit characterized by slender, branched stems and elliptical to oblong-shaped leaves that are oppositely arranged. The leaf margins are typically entire or slightly serrated, featuring a smooth texture and a distinctively pointed apex. Its foliage is often a deep green color with prominent venation patterns visible on the surface. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often appearing in clusters. The plant structure remains relatively compact, supporting its adaptation to specific environmental niches.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds heterocliitin J, kadoblongifolin C, kadoblongifolins A, kadsulignan s C, and kadsurarin. These specific chemical constituents are often studied for their potential bioactive properties in pharmacological research. The presence of these unique lignans and glycosides distinguishes the plant's chemical profile. Researchers analyze these substances to understand how they interact with biological systems. Such detailed chemical characterization helps in identifying the plant's therapeutic value.

As discussed in a study, two new spirobenzofuranoid dibenzocyclooctadiene lignans, kadsulignans O (1) and P (2), were isolated from the stems of Kadsura oblongifolia. These two new compounds were found alongside five known lignans of the same type, which are identified as kadsulignans C (3), E (4), F (5), G (6), and heteroclitin J (7). The study notes that compounds 3, 4, 5, 6, and 7 were isolated from this specific plant for the first time. The structures of these 7 compounds were elucidated using spectral evidences and comparisons with literature.
