Kadsura oblongifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Kadsura oblongifolia has an erect, woody perennial habit characterized by slender, branched stems and elliptical to oblong-shaped leaves that are oppositely arranged. The leaf margins are typically entire or slightly serrated, featuring a smooth texture and a distinctively pointed apex. Its foliage is often a deep green color with prominent venation patterns visible on the surface. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often appearing in clusters. The plant structure remains relatively compact, supporting its adaptation to specific environmental niches.
