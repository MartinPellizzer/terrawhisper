Kadsura oblongifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Kadsura oblongifolia?

Kadsura oblongifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Austrobaileyales. This plant is further classified under the family Schisandraceae, falling within the genus Kadsura, and is specifically identified by the species name oblongifolia.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the unique medicinal compounds kadoblongifolins A, kadoblongifolins B, kadoblongifolin C, kadsurarin, and kadsulignan C. These bioactive lignans are primarily responsible for the plant's diverse therapeutic properties. Researchers have identified these specific molecules as key contributors to its potential pharmacological activities. Detailed chemical analysis shows that these compounds work together to provide various biological effects. The presence of such specialized metabolites makes the species a subject of significant scientific interest.

According to a study published by "Magnetic resonance in chemistry : MRC", the stems of Kadsura oblongifolia contain three new dibenzocyclooctadiene lignans named kadoblongifolins A (1), B (2), and C (3). These three new natural products were isolated alongside five known ones, specifically schizanrin F (4), propinquanin C (5), schisantherin G (6), heteroclitin Q (7), and kadsurarin (8). The structures of these 3 new lignans were elucidated using 1H NMR and 13C NMR data. Additional methods used for structural elucidation included HR-EI-MS, HMQC, HMBC, and NOESY spectra.
