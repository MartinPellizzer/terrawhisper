Saussurea japonica

What's the taxonomical classification of Saussurea japonica?

Saussurea japonica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Asterales and is a member of the family Asteraceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific genus, Saussurea, and its unique species designation, japonica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea japonica has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by basal leaves that are long, lanceolate, and often feature serrated margins. The stems are erect and can reach significant heights, supporting a terminal inflorescence of many small, tubular yellow florets. These flowers are organized into dense, head-like clusters known as capitula, which are typical of the Asteraceae family. The plant also develops a stout, woody rhizome that serves as its primary underground storage organ. Each flowering head is supported by involucral bracts that protect the developing seeds.
