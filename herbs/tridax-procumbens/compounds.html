Goatweed (Tridax procumbens) Compounds

Goatweed contains several key compounds, including flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties. Tannins and phenolic acids contribute to its astringent effects. Saponins and saponin-like compounds may support immune function. Coumarin and glycosides are also present, offering potential anti-inflammatory benefits. Terpenoids, essential oils, and alkaloids contribute to its overall pharmacological profile. Anthocyanins provide additional antioxidant support.

Goatweed (Tridax procumbens) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Goatweed (Tridax procumbens) contribute to its medicinal properties by exhibiting antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. These compounds help neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress, supporting the plant's traditional use in treating ailments like inflammation and skin conditions.

2. Tannin

Tannin in Goatweed (Tridax procumbens) contributes to its astringent properties, aiding in wound healing and reducing inflammation. It also supports digestive health by binding to proteins and reducing gut irritation. This compound is a key component in the plant's traditional use for treating ulcers and skin conditions.
