What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Tridax Procumbens (Coat Button)?

Tridax procumbens has health benefits such as relieving fever and inflammation.

The herb is rich in medicinal constituents like flavonoids, saponins, and terpenes, which have been shown to exhibit antimicrobial and antioxidant properties. These constituents are often used to prepare infusions, decoctions, and ointments that can be applied topically or consumed orally for various health issues. However, excessive consumption may lead to gastrointestinal side effects, such as nausea and vomiting.

What are the possible side effect of using Tridax procumbens improperly?

Improper use of Tridax procumbens increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as allergic reactions, skin irritation, and digestive issues like diarrhea or stomach pain.

It may also lead to interactions with medications, blood pressure fluctuations, and an increased risk of bleeding disorders. In some cases, it can cause respiratory problems like asthma attacks, hives, and even kidney damage.

Additionally, excessive consumption can result in headaches, fatigue, dizziness, and anxiety symptoms.

What precautions to take when using Tridax procumbens medicinally?

Before using Tridax procumbens for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as consulting with a healthcare professional to determine the appropriate dosage and treatment duration.

Additionally, ensure that the plant material is correctly identified and sourced from a reputable supplier to avoid contamination or adulteration.

Furthermore, pregnant women and breastfeeding mothers should exercise caution when using Tridax procumbens, as its effects on fetal development and infant health are not fully understood.
