Glaucium elegans

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Glaucium elegans has a basal rosette of deeply pinnatisect, glaucous leaves that transition into smaller, more sparsely divided foliage along its upright, branched stems. The plant's foliage is characterized by a distinct blue-green or grayish hue resulting from a waxy coating. Its flowering stems bear large, solitary terminal heads featuring bright yellow petals surrounding a central, dark blue-black disk. The involucral bracts are typically hairy and provide structural support to the prominent flower heads. This morphology is adapted to arid Mediterranean environments where moisture retention and visibility to pollinators are essential.
