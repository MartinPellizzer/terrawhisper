Phedimus middendorffianus

What's the taxonomical classification of Phedimus middendorffianus?

Phedimus middendorffianus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Saxifragales and into the family Crassulaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Phedimus, and its unique species designation, middendorffianus.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Phedimus middendorffianus can cause severe skin irritation, localized swelling, and intense itching upon direct contact with the dermal layers. These dermatological reactions often manifest as red, raised welts that may persist for several days. If the plant's sap enters the eyes, it can trigger significant inflammation and temporary blurred vision. Ingestion of any part of the plant may lead to gastrointestinal distress, including nausea and abdominal cramping. Individuals with sensitive skin or known allergies should avoid contact to prevent an escalated immune response.
