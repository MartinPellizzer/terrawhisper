Siraitia siamensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Siraitia siamensis has a woody climbing vine structure characterized by large, palmate leaves with five to seven deeply lobed segments and small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in axillary clusters. The plant features a climbing habit supported by specialized tendrils that allow it to ascend through forest canopies. Its leaves are typically leathery in texture with prominent venation that radiates from the base of the blade. The reproductive structures include small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into fleshy, globose fruits. These fruits are often used to describe the plant's distinct morphological profile within its native tropical habitats.

This plant is native to a broad range of Southeast Asian countries including Thailand, Vietnam, Laos, Cambodia, Myanmar, and parts of Southern China. The species is commonly found in lowland forests and along the edges of various wooded habitats.     What are the herbal preparations of Siraitia siamensis?

    Siraitia siamensis is prepared as a decoction, a topical paste, or an infusion using various parts of the plant including the roots, leaves, and stems.     Siraitia siamensis can cause adverse reactions such as gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, and potential toxicity if consumed in improper quantities. Users should exercise extreme caution and avoid direct contact with the sap to prevent localized dermatitis.
