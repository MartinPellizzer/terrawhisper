What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Corydalis tomentella has a basal rosette of pinnately lobed leaves characterized by deeply incised, somewhat thickened leaflets that often possess a slightly hairy or pubescent texture. The stems are typically short and emerge from a tuberous rhizome to support a small number of flowers. These flowers are zygomorphic, featuring a distinctively long, nectar-producing spur that is a key diagnostic trait of the genus. The perianth displays a delicate coloration, often ranging from pale yellow to light pink or white depending on the specific population. Small, capsule-like fruits develop following pollination, eventually splitting to release numerous tiny seeds.

As discussed in a study, Corydalis tomentella total alkaloids possess protective effects against acute liver injury along with potential anti-hepatoma and anti-Alzheimer's disease activities. These activities are achieved through the regulation of the calcium signaling pathway by decreasing intracellular Ca2+ concentration and regulating the expression of NOS2, NOS3, and CALM1. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse array of isoquinoline alkaloids, specifically including the medicinal compounds (2H)-protoberberine, Tomentediline A, cavidine, and sanguinarine. These chemical constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's various pharmacological activities and biological effects. Sanguinarine and (2H)-protoberberine are often studied for their potent antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The presence of cavidine and Tomentediline A further contributes to the complex chemical profile used in traditional medicine. Together, these isoquinoline alkaloids define the therapeutic potential of the species.

As discussed in a study, Corydalis tomentella contains two undescribed isoquinoline alkaloids (1-2), one undescribed carbon skeleton isoquinoline, and six known ones (4-9). The plant also yields one undescribed amide (3) and three known ones (10-12). Tomentediline A (1) is an isoquinoline alkaloid dimer that forms an undescribed carbon carbon bond at the C-13 position of (2H)-protoberberine. This specific compound (1) exerted moderate cytotoxicity against the U251 cell lines. Such findings indicate that the undescribed dimer skeleton of the isoquinoline compound has the potential for anti-glioma.
