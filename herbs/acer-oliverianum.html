Acer oliverianum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Acer oliverianum has opposite, palmately lobed leaves that typically feature three to five distinct lobes with serrated margins and acute tips. The leaf blades are relatively small and possess a texture that can range from glabrous to slightly pubescent depending on the specific specimen. Its stems are woody and develop a grayish bark as the plant matures into a shrub or small tree. The inflorescences consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the axils of the leaves. These flowers eventually develop into winged samaras which are characteristic of the maple genus.
