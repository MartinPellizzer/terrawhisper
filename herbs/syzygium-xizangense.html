Syzygium xizangense

What's the taxonomical classification of Syzygium xizangense?

Syzygium xizangense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Myrtales. Further taxonomic refinement situates the plant in the family Myrtaceae, specifically within the genus Syzygium, with its unique designation as the species xizangense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Syzygium xizangense has elliptical to lanceolate leaves that are oppositely arranged, coriaceous in texture, and possess an entire margin with a distinctively acuminate apex. The foliage typically exhibits a glossy dark green upper surface while the underside remains a paler shade. Its woody stems are characterized by a smooth to slightly fissured bark that matures into a grayish-brown color. Small, fragrant flowers emerge from axillary positions, eventually developing into fleshy drupes. These fruits transition from green to a deep red or black hue upon full maturity.
