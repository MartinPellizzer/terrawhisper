Syzygium xizangense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Syzygium xizangense has elliptical to lanceolate leaves that are oppositely arranged, coriaceous in texture, and possess an entire margin with a distinctively acuminate apex. The foliage typically exhibits a glossy dark green upper surface while the underside remains a paler shade. Its woody stems are characterized by a smooth to slightly fissured bark that matures into a grayish-brown color. Small, fragrant flowers emerge from axillary positions, eventually developing into fleshy drupes. These fruits transition from green to a deep red or black hue upon full maturity.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the subtropical forests of southern China, specifically concentrated in the provinces of Guangdong, Guangxi, Hunan, and Fujian. It thrives in mountainous regions where humidity is high and consistent rainfall is common. Within these habitats, the species is often found growing in shaded areas along stream banks or in dense evergreen broad-leaved forests. Its distribution is limited by specific elevation requirements and temperature ranges characteristic of these southern provinces. Localized populations are primarily determined by the availability of moisture-rich soil and canopy cover.
