Artocarpus communis

What's the taxonomical classification of Artocarpus communis?

Artocarpus communis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Rosales and is a member of the family Moraceae. Finally, the plant is placed within the genus Artocarpus, and its specific identifier is the species communis.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Artocarpus communis contains medicinal compounds found in its leaves, bark, fruit, and roots. The leaves are frequently prepared as a decoction to treat fever, inflammation, and skin conditions. Bark extracts are often utilized for their potential antibacterial and antioxidant properties in traditional medicine. The fruit flesh provides essential nutrients, while the seeds are sometimes processed to address digestive issues. Additionally, the roots can be used in various herbal remedies to manage systemic ailments.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse array of bioactive phytochemicals including flavonoids, alkaloids, terpenoids, phenolic acids, steroids, saponins, and coumarins.

These specific medicinal compounds contribute to the plant's significant antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. The presence of phenolic acids and flavonoids facilitates the neutralization of free radicals within biological systems. Furthermore, the alkaloids and terpenoids play crucial roles in modulating various physiological processes and providing therapeutic effects. Steroids and saponins also contribute to the overall pharmacological complexity of the species.

Finally, the coumarins assist in enhancing the biological activity of the plant's extracts.

  • Phenolic acids
  • Steroids
  • Saponins
  • Coumarins

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Artocarpus communis targets inflammatory enzymes such as cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2) and lipoxygenase (LOX), alongside various pro-inflammatory cytokines and signaling pathways like NF-kappaB. The bioactive compounds found in the plant, particularly prenylated flavonoids and chalcones, act as potent inhibitors to prevent the overproduction of nitric oxide and prostaglandin E2. These molecular interactions contribute to its significant anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties in biological systems. Furthermore, certain extracts exhibit inhibitory effects on alpha-glucosidase and alpha-amylase, which helps regulate glucose metabolism. The plant also shows potential in modulating oxidative stress by targeting antioxidant enzymes to neutralize free radicals.

What are the therapeutic actions of Artocarpus communis?

Artocarpus communis has potent anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties derived from its bioactive compounds like flavonoids, tannins, and saponins. These phytochemicals work together to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress within the body's cells. The plant's extracts are frequently used in traditional medicine to treat skin infections, digestive issues, and respiratory inflammation. Additionally, its leaves and bark contain compounds that may help regulate blood glucose levels and support cardiovascular health. These diverse therapeutic actions make it a valuable subject for pharmacological research into managing chronic metabolic conditions.

What common ailments are treated using this plant?

This plant is used to treat diarrhea, dysentery, skin inflammation, wounds, and various respiratory ailments.

The bark and leaves are often prepared as decoctions to soothe digestive distress and control intestinal infections. For external issues, topical applications of the sap or leaf extracts help reduce skin inflammation and promote the healing of open wounds. Additionally, infusions of the plant components are frequently used to alleviate symptoms of respiratory ailments like coughs or asthma.

These diverse medicinal properties make the species a vital resource in traditional herbal medicine.

The main common ailment treated using this plant are shown in the list below.

  • Diarrhea
  • Dysentery
  • Skin inflammation
  • Wounds
  • Respiratory ailments

What are the herbal preparations of Artocarpus communis?

Artocarpus communis is prepared as decoctions, infusions, powders, pastes, and topical poultices derived from its various parts including the bark, leaves, roots, and fruit. The dried bark and roots are frequently boiled in water to create medicinal decoctions used to treat inflammatory conditions. Leaves are often crushed into a fresh paste or steeped as an infusion to utilize their antioxidant properties. The fruit itself can be consumed directly or processed into syrups and juices for nutritional support. Additionally, powdered extracts are sometimes incorporated into capsules for standardized herbal supplementation.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Artocarpus communis can cause allergic reactions such as contact dermatitis, oral itching, or swelling in sensitive individuals. The sticky latex found in the fruit and tree bark is a common trigger for skin irritation upon contact. Some people may experience gastrointestinal distress if they consume excessive amounts of the fruit. While generally safe, the high sugar content in the ripe fruit might affect blood glucose levels in diabetics. It is always advisable to monitor how your body responds to the fruit when consuming it for the first time.
