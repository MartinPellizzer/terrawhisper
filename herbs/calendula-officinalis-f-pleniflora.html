Garden Marigold (Calendula Officinalis F. Pleniflora)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Calendula officinalis f. pleniflora.

Garden marigold, scientifically known as Calendula officinalis f. pleniflora, is a medicinal herb renowned for its vibrant orange-yellow flowers and a wide range of therapeutic properties. This adaptogen, often used in herbal medicine, is valued for its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and wound-healing capabilities, making it a popular choice for skincare and topical treatments. Historically, it has been used in traditional European and Middle Eastern cultures to treat skin conditions, digestive issues, and as a natural dye, while modern wellness applications include its incorporation into salves, creams, and teas for stress relief and immune support. One of its unique features is the presence of flavonoids and triterpenoids, which contribute to its potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, and it has a distinct, slightly bitter flavor that adds a unique touch to culinary uses. Its long history of use dates back to ancient times, with records showing it was cultivated by the Greeks and Romans for both medicinal and ornamental purposes.
