What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Corallodiscus lanuginosus has small, woody, spreading branches covered in dense, woolly, or lanuginous hairs that give the plant a soft texture. The leaves are typically small and elliptical or ovate in shape, often exhibiting a pubescent surface consistent with its species name. These leaves are arranged alternately along the stems and possess short petioles. The inflorescence consists of tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are characteristic of the family Malvaceae. Its overall structure is characterized by a shrubby growth habit with a fine, textured appearance due to the pervasive hairiness.
