Corallodiscus lanuginosus

What's the taxonomical classification of Corallodiscus lanuginosus?

Corallodiscus lanuginosus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Lamiales. It is specifically nested within the family Gesneriaceae, falling under the genus Corallodiscus, and is identified by its specific species epithet, lanuginosus.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Corallodiscus lanuginosus can cause severe skin irritation, localized dermatitis, and potential allergic reactions upon direct contact with its foliage. The plant contains specific chemical compounds that may trigger redness or itching if the sap touches human skin. Some individuals might also experience swelling or small rashes if they inhale any airborne particles from the leaves. Because the exact toxicity levels are not fully documented in all medical literature, caution is advised when handling this species. It is important to wear protective gloves to prevent any adverse physiological responses.
