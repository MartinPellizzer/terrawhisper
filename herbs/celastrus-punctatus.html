Celastrus punctatus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Celastrus punctatus has small, elliptical leaves arranged oppositely on woody stems, characterized by distinct glandular dots on the undersides and small, inconspicuous flowers. The foliage typically displays a deep green color, though it may transition to warmer hues during seasonal changes. Its reproductive structures consist of small, fleshy berries that often turn bright red or orange upon maturity to attract dispersers. The stems are slender and branching, providing a structural framework for the dense leaf distribution. These morphological traits collectively define its identity within the Celastraceae family.
