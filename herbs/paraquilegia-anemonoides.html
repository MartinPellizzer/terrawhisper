Paraquilegia anemonoides

What's the taxonomical classification of Paraquilegia anemonoides?

Paraquilegia anemonoides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It further falls under the order Ranunculales and is a member of the family Ranunculaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by the genus Paraquilegia and the species anemonoides.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Paraquilegia anemonoides can cause severe gastrointestinal distress including intense nausea, projectile vomiting, and debilitating abdominal cramping if ingested. These symptoms often occur rapidly after the plant enters the digestive system. In some cases, the toxins may lead to systemic effects such as dizziness or blurred vision. Prolonged exposure or higher doses could potentially result in more serious complications like dehydration or electrolyte imbalances. It is essential to avoid contact with the plant to prevent these adverse reactions.
