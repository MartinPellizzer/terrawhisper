Melilot (Melilotus officinalis) Compounds

Melilot contains several key compounds, including flavonoids, which have anti-inflammatory properties, and coumarin, known for its anticoagulant effects. Essential oils and phenolic acids contribute to its aromatic and antioxidant qualities. Glycosides and terpenoids support its traditional use in herbal medicine, while saponins and tannins provide additional therapeutic benefits. Alkaloids and anthocyanins further enhance its medicinal profile.

Melilot (Melilotus officinalis) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are key active compounds in Melilotus officinalis, a medicinal plant used in herbal medicine. These compounds contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in treating cardiovascular and inflammatory conditions. They are responsible for many of the therapeutic effects attributed to melilot.

2. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Melilotus officinalis. It contributes to the plant's anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarin is also responsible for the characteristic sweet aroma of melilot. It is commonly used in herbal medicine for its therapeutic effects.

3. Essential oil

The essential oil of Melilotus officinalis contains volatile compounds like limonene and methyl salicylate. These compounds contribute to the plant's aromatic properties and have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects. They are commonly used in herbal medicine for respiratory and digestive support.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Melilotus officinalis. It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound helps reduce oxidative stress and supports cardiovascular health. Phenolic acid is commonly found in the dried flowers and roots of the plant.

5. Glycoside

Glycosides in Melilotus officinalis are key active compounds that contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds, such as melitidine and melilotin, have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects. They are responsible for the plant's traditional use in treating digestive and respiratory conditions. Glycosides are extracted from the plant's flowers and roots.

6. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Melilotus officinalis. They contribute to the plant's therapeutic properties, including anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects. These compounds are responsible for some of the plant's traditional uses in herbal medicine.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Melilotus officinalis. It exhibits anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, contributing to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and skin conditions. Saponins also support immune function and may aid in detoxification processes within the body.

8. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Melilotus officinalis. It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health. Tannins in melilot can also have antimicrobial effects, making the plant useful in traditional herbal medicine for treating ailments like diarrhea and skin conditions.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloids in Melilotus officinalis are rare, with only a few identified, such as melittin and sparteine. These compounds may contribute to the plant's traditional uses, though their specific roles and pharmacological effects require further research. Their presence adds to the complex chemical profile of this medicinal herb.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanins are bioactive compounds found in Melilotus officinalis. They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These pigments help protect cells from oxidative stress. Anthocyanins are also linked to improved cardiovascular health.
