Hedysarum brachypterum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hedysarum brachypterum has pinnately compound leaves characterized by small, closely spaced leaflets and a woody, shrub-like growth habit. The foliage typically consists of several pairs of leaflets that are elliptical to oblong in shape. Its flowers are small and often appear in dense clusters along the stems. The plant produces legume-style seed pods that are relatively short and somewhat flattened. These morphological traits help distinguish it from other species within the Fabaceae family.
