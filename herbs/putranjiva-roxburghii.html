Putranjiva roxburghii

What's the taxonomical classification of Putranjiva roxburghii?

Putranjiva roxburghii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent through the order Malpighiales, it is situated within the family Putranjivaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific classification under the genus Putranjiva and the species roxburghii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Putranjiva roxburghii has a small to medium-sized tree reaching heights of up to 5 meters, characterized by a slender trunk and spreading branches. The leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptic to oblong in shape with an entire margin and an acute apex. It produces small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that are arranged in axillary or terminal panicles. The fruit is a small, globose drupe that turns black or dark purple when fully mature. The bark is generally smooth and greyish, while the root system is robust to support its woody structure.

What are the pharmacological activities of Putranjiva roxburghii?

This plant has diverse pharmacological activities including anti-inflammatory, antileishmanial, antioxidant, antiparasitic, and the ability to increase sperm motility. Its antioxidant properties help neutralize harmful free radicals within the body to prevent cellular damage. The plant also demonstrates potent antileishmanial and antiparasitic effects, making it useful in combating various protozoan infections. Furthermore, its anti-inflammatory actions assist in reducing swelling and pain associated with different physiological conditions. Research also indicates that specific extracts from the plant can lead to increased sperm motility in male reproductive studies.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", Putranjiva roxburghii leaves serve as the major ingredient in the Yataprasen herbal remedy. The leaf extract exhibited the highest inhibitory effects on LPS-induced TNF-α and iNOS expression among the plant ingredient extracts. Three biflavonoids, including putraflavone, podocarpusflavone A, and amentoflavone, were isolated from the P. roxburghii leaf extract. These isolated biflavonoids showed inhibitory effects on LPS-induced PGE2 secretion and NO synthesis in RAW264.7 cells. Among these, amentoflavone exhibited the strongest anti-inflammatoy activity by inhibiting the expression of TNF-α, COX-2, and iNOS.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds putraflavone, amentoflavone, podocarpusflavone A, isorhamnetin, and kaempferol. These bioactive substances are primarily categorized as flavonoids, which are known for their potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The presence of specific derivatives like podocarpusflavone A contributes to the unique pharmacological profile of the species. Research into these phytochemicals suggests they play a significant role in the plant's overall therapeutic potential. Scientists continue to study how these individual compounds interact to provide health benefits.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", the leaves of Putranjiva roxbrugii Wall. serve as a major ingredient in the herbal remedy Yataprasen, which is made from a hydroethanolic extract of 13 medicinal plants. The active ingredients of the P. roxbrugii leaves were separated by chromatography and identified through spectroscopic measurements. Specifically, the biflavonoids isolated from the P. roxbrugii leaf extract include putraflavone, podocarpusflavone A, and amentoflavone. These isolated biflavonoids demonstrated inhibitory effects on LPS-induced PGE2 secretion and NO synthesis in RAW264.7 cells. Among these, amentoflavone exhibited the strongest anti-inflammatoy activity by inhibiting the expression of TNF-α, COX-2, and iNOS.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating Leishmania donovani infection, leishmaniasis, female reproductive ailments, inflammation, and various issues related to uterine health. It possesses significant medicinal properties that target parasitic infections within the body. Researchers have noted its effectiveness in managing the symptoms associated with leishmaniasis. Furthermore, the plant plays a crucial role in soothing inflammation throughout different bodily systems. It is also frequently applied to support reproductive wellness and improve overall uterine health in women.

According to a study published by "Journal of parasitic diseases : official organ of the Indian Society for Parasitology", Putranjivha roxburghii is used as a medicinal plant against Leishmania donovani. The plant shows efficacy against both sodium stibogluconate sensitive and resistant strains of the parasite. The IC50 values recorded for the sensitive strain and the resistant strain were 25.61 ± 0.57 µg/mL and 29.02 ± 1.21 µg/mL, respectively. These results demonstrate the potential of the plant for exploration as an antileishmanial agent.

What are the herbal preparations of this plant?

This plant can be prepared as a decoction by boiling the roots in water, as a powder known as churna, as a fresh paste called kalka, as an infusion, or through maceration in liquid. Practitioners often utilize the fine churna for easier ingestion or to mix into other therapeutic substances. Applying the kalka directly to the skin serves as a topical remedy for various ailments. Maceration and infusion processes provide gentler methods for drawing out the plant's medicinal properties into a liquid medium.
