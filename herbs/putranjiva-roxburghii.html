Putranjiva roxburghii

What's the taxonomical classification of Putranjiva roxburghii?

Putranjiva roxburghii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent through the order Malpighiales, it is situated within the family Putranjivaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific classification under the genus Putranjiva and the species roxburghii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Putranjiva roxburghii has a small to medium-sized tree reaching heights of up to 5 meters, characterized by a slender trunk and spreading branches. The leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptic to oblong in shape with an entire margin and an acute apex. It produces small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that are arranged in axillary or terminal panicles. The fruit is a small, globose drupe that turns black or dark purple when fully mature. The bark is generally smooth and greyish, while the root system is robust to support its woody structure.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several medicinal compounds including the flavonoids kaempferol, isorhamnetin, apipegitrin, and podocarpusflavone A. These specific phytochemicals are known for their diverse biological activities and therapeutic potential. Researchers study these substances to understand how they interact with human cellular processes. The presence of such a variety of flavonoids suggests the plant may possess significant antioxidant or anti-inflammatory properties. Understanding the concentration of these individual molecules is essential for developing standardized herbal extracts.

As discussed in a study, GC-MS profiling revealed that the extract of Putranjiva roxburghii was rich in myo-inositol-4-C-methyl, azulene and desulphosinigrin. Quantitative investigation of phytoconstituent of the plant confirmed that it was rich in phenols, flavonoids and terpenoids. The extract showed an IC50 of 25.61 ± 0.57 µg/mL against the sodium stibogluconate sensitive strain. It also demonstrated an IC50 of 29.02 ± 1.21 µg/mL against the resistant strain. These findings support the importance of Putranjiva roxbruggii as a medicinal plant against L. donovani.
