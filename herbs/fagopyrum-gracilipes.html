Fagopyrum gracilipes

What's the taxonomical classification of Fagopyrum gracilipes?

Fagopyrum gracilipes belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Caryophyllales and falls under the family Polygonaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Fagopyrum and the species name gracilipes.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Fagopyrum gracilipes has slender, erect stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters and feature delicate, branched structures.

The leaves are primarily ovate or lanceolate with slightly wavy margins and are arranged alternately along the stem. Small, white to pinkish flowers bloom in terminal clusters, characterized by five distinct petals and prominent stamens. The plant produces small, angular achenes that serve as its primary seeds.

Its overall morphology is defined by a fine, wiry appearance that distinguishes it from more robust buckwheat species.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Fagopyrum gracilipes can cause allergic reactions such as skin rashes, itching, or swelling in sensitive individuals. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, members of the Polygonaceae family sometimes trigger hypersensitivity responses. Symptoms might include gastrointestinal discomfort or respiratory irritation if the plant is ingested or inhaled in large quantities.
