Fagopyrum gracilipes

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Fagopyrum gracilipes has slender, erect stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters and feature delicate, branched structures. The leaves are primarily ovate or lanceolate with slightly wavy margins and are arranged alternately along the stem. Small, white to pinkish flowers bloom in terminal clusters, characterized by five distinct petals and prominent stamens. The plant produces small, angular achenes that serve as its primary seeds. Its overall morphology is defined by a fine, wiry appearance that distinguishes it from more robust buckwheat species.
