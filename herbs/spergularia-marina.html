Spergularia marina

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Spergularia marina has prostrate, succulent stems that form low-growing mats and leaves that are linear, fleshy, and oppositely arranged. The plant typically exhibits a creeping growth habit with branches that often root at the nodes to stabilize itself in sandy substrates. Its flowers are small and white, featuring five distinct petals that are often slightly notched at the tips. The fruit consists of small capsules that contain numerous tiny, dark seeds designed for dispersal by wind or water. This morphological structure allows the species to thrive in harsh, saline coastal environments.
