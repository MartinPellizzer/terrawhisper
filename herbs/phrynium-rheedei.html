Phrynium rheedei

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phrynium rheedei has large, oblong-lanceolate leaves that are prominently veined and emerge from a sturdy rhizomatous base. These leaves typically possess a smooth, glabrous texture and a deep green coloration that serves to capture sunlight in shaded forest understories. The plant structure is characterized by its perennial growth habit, where thick, fleshy stems support the heavy foliage. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in clusters, eventually giving way to fleshy berries that facilitate seed dispersal. This morphological arrangement allows the species to thrive effectively within its specific tropical ecological niche.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Phrynium rheedei can cause skin irritation, redness, and localized swelling if its sap comes into direct contact with human skin. The plant contains chemical compounds that act as irritants to sensitive tissues upon contact. Some individuals may experience an allergic reaction characterized by itching or a burning sensation at the site of exposure. It is advisable to wear protective gloves when handling the foliage to prevent these dermatological issues. Careful management is necessary to avoid accidental contact with the eyes or mucous membranes.
