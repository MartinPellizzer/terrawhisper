Canavalia cathartica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Canavalia cathartica has pinnately compound leaves with several pairs of oblong leaflets and a woody, spreading canopy. The plant produces small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that grow in axillary racemes. Its fruit consists of elongated, woody pods that turn dark brown when mature and contain hard, dark seeds. The bark is typically thick and fissured, providing protection for the underlying stem structure. The overall growth habit is that of a medium to large tree with a robust branching pattern.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of South and Southeast Asia, specifically spanning across India, Sri Lanka, and parts of Indochina. It thrives in humid environments where moisture levels remain consistently high throughout the growing season. The species is often found in moist deciduous forests and along the edges of riverine ecosystems. Because of its preference for warm climates, it does not naturally occur in temperate or arid zones. Its distribution is closely tied to specific soil types that support dense, lush vegetation.

Canavaliia cathartica contains medicinal properties primarily found in its seeds, leaves, and bark. Extracts from the leaves are often applied topically or ingested to treat various inflammatory conditions and skin ailments. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

The presence of tannins provides antimicrobial and astringent properties, which assist in treating various skin conditions and gastrointestinal issues. What are the therapeutic actions of Canavalia cathartica?

Canavalia cathartica has therapeutic actions that include being a potent cathartic, an aperient, a bitter, a digestive stimulant, and a carminative. The plant contains compounds that act as a strong cathartic to induce bowel movements and as an aperient to provide a milder laxative effect. Furthermore, its carminative qualities assist in the expulsion of gas and the relief of intestinal bloating. These combined effects make the plant useful for managing various gastrointestinal issues.

What are the herbal preparations of Canavalia cathartica?

Canavalia cathartica is prepared primarily as a decoction, infusion, or powdered extract derived from its dried seeds to utilize its potent purgative properties. The seeds are often crushed into a fine powder and mixed with water or other liquids to facilitate ingestion. In some traditional practices, a concentrated liquid extract is made by boiling the seeds for an extended period to extract active compounds. These preparations are carefully dosed because the plant contains high concentrations of anthraquinone glycosides. Proper preparation is essential to manage the intensity of its laxative effect on the digestive system.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Canavalia cathartica can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, including intense abdominal cramping, violent vomiting, and explosive diarrhea due to its high concentration of toxic proteins. The plant contains lectins that act as potent irritants to the digestive lining upon ingestion. If consumed in significant quantities, these toxins may lead to dehydration or more systemic inflammatory responses. Some individuals might also experience localized irritation if the plant comes into contact with sensitive skin. It is essential to avoid all parts of this species to prevent accidental poisoning.
