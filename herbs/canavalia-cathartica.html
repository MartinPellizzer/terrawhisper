Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Fabales and is a member of the family Fabaceae. Ultimately, the plant is identified by the genus Canaulva and the specific epithet cathartica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Canavalia cathartica has pinnately compound leaves with several pairs of oblong leaflets and a woody, spreading canopy. The bark is typically thick and fissured, providing protection for the underlying stem structure. The overall growth habit is that of a medium to large tree with a robust branching pattern.
