Canavalia cathartica

What's the taxonomical classification of Canavalia cathartica?

Canavalia catharticica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Fabales and is a member of the family Fabaceae. Ultimately, the plant is identified by the genus Canaulva and the specific epithet cathartica.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Canavalia cathartica contains high concentrations of cassiols, tannins, flavonoids, and saponins that serve as its primary bioactive components. These specific phytochemicals are responsible for the plant's significant purgative and laxative effects. The presence of tannins provides antimicrobial and astringent properties, which assist in treating various skin conditions and gastrointestinal issues. Additionally, the saponins contribute to its potential anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory activities. Researchers study these compounds to better understand their roles in managing digestive ailments and systemic infections.

What are the therapeutic actions of Canavalia cathartica?

Canavaliia cathartica has potent laxative and purgative properties due to the presence of anthraquinone glycosides in its seeds and bark. These chemical compounds stimulate the intestinal muscles to increase peristalsis and accelerate the movement of waste through the digestive tract. Beyond its gastrointestinal effects, the plant is also utilized in traditional medicine to treat skin irritations and inflammatory conditions. Some studies suggest that its extracts may also possess mild antimicrobial and antioxidant activities. This combination of therapeutic actions makes it a significant resource in various ethnobotanical practices.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat constipation, intestinal worms, skin infections, and various inflammatory conditions. Its powerful purgative properties make it a common remedy for relieving severe bowel obstruction and promoting regular digestion. The leaves and seeds are also frequently applied topically to manage fungal infections and soothe itchy skin irritations. Additionally, certain extracts are utilized in traditional medicine to combat internal parasites and reduce swelling in the body. Practitioners often carefully control the dosage due to its intense laxative effects.

What are the herbal preparations of Canavalia cathartica?

Canavalia cathartica is prepared primarily as a decoction, infusion, or powdered extract derived from its dried seeds to utilize its potent purgative properties. The seeds are often crushed into a fine powder and mixed with water or other liquids to facilitate ingestion. In some traditional practices, a concentrated liquid extract is made by boiling the seeds for an extended period to extract active compounds. These preparations are carefully dosed because the plant contains high concentrations of anthraquinone glycosides. Proper preparation is essential to manage the intensity of its laxative effect on the digestive system.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Canavalia cathartica can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, including intense abdominal cramping, violent vomiting, and explosive diarrhea due to its high concentration of toxic proteins. The plant contains lectins that act as potent irritants to the digestive lining upon ingestion. If consumed in significant quantities, these toxins may lead to dehydration or more systemic inflammatory responses. Some individuals might also experience localized irritation if the plant comes into contact with sensitive skin. It is essential to avoid all parts of this species to prevent accidental poisoning.
