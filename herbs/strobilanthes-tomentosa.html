What's the taxonomical classification of Strobilanthes tomentosa?

Strobilanthes tomentosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Lamiales and the family Acanthaceae. Finally, the plant is placed within the genus Strobilanthes, specifically identified by its species name, tomentosa.

    What are the herbal preparations of Strobilanthes tomentosa?

    Strobilanthes tomentosa is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste using the dried or fresh leaves and stems. Traditional practitioners often boil the plant material in water to extract its active constituents for oral consumption. For external applications, the leaves may be crushed into a fine pulp to soothe skin irritations or localized swelling. Some regional practices also involve steeping the herb in alcohol to create a concentrated tincture. While specific clinical data on human toxicity is limited, many plants in the Acanthaceae family contain bioactive compounds that may trigger sensitive immune responses. Contact with the leaves or stems might lead to redness, itching, or a mild rash on the hands. If any part of the plant is consumed, individuals may experience nausea or stomach cramping.
