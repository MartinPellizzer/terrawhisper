Rubus corchorifolius

What's the taxonomical classification of Rubus corchorifolius?

Rubus corchorifolius belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized under the order Rosales. It is a member of the family Rosaceae, which places it within the genus Rubus, and is specifically identified by its species name, corchorifolius.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rubus corchorifoios has leaves that are primarily characterized by being trifoliate or occasionally pinnate with five to seven ovate leaflets that possess serrated margins and a distinctively cordate base. The leaf surfaces often exhibit a pubescent texture, particularly on the undersides, which provides a soft, hairy appearance. Its stems are typically woody and armed with numerous sharp prickles or thorns that assist in climbing through surrounding vegetation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into aggregate fruits known as drupelets. These morphological features collectively help distinguish this species from other members of the Rubus genus.
