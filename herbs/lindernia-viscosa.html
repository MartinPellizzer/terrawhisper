Lindernia viscosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Lindernia viscosa?

Lindernia viscosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Linderniaceae. Finally, it is placed within the genus Lindernia, with its specific designation being the species viscosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lindernia viscosa has an erect, branched, and glandular-pubescent herbaceous stem that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The leaves are arranged in an opposite pattern and feature an ovate to lanceolate shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. Small, white, or pale lilac flowers emerge from the leaf axils and possess a distinct two-lipped corolla structure. The fruit consists of a small, globose capsule that contains numerous tiny, brownish seeds. These morphological features, particularly the sticky glandular hairs, distinguish it from other similar species in the Linderniaceae family.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of South and Southeast Asia, spanning from India and Sri Lanka through much of mainland Southeast Asia to parts of southern China. It primarily inhabits wetland environments such as marshes, paddy fields, and the edges of shallow ponds where moisture is abundant. Within these specific habitats, it can be found across various elevations, though it is most common in low-lying areas. The distribution is heavily influenced by the availability of stagnant or slow-moving water sources. Consequently, its presence is often concentrated in areas characterized by high humidity and seasonal monsoon patterns.
