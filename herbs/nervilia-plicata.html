Nervilia plicata

What's the taxonomical classification of Nervilia plicata?

Nervilia plicata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asparagales. This plant is a distinguished representative of the family Orchidaceae, specifically situated within the genus Nervilia, and is identified by the species name plicata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Nervilia plicata has a single, broad, heart-shaped leaf that emerges from a small underground tuber and features prominent longitudinal veins running from the base to the apex. The leaf surface is typically smooth and can vary in color from bright green to deep brownish hues depending on light exposure. This plant lacks a visible stem above ground, as the leaf appears to rise directly from the soil level. The leaf margin is entire and lacks serration, while the base is cordate and slightly overlapping. Tiny, inconspicuous flowers emerge on short stalks, though the foliage remains the most distinct morphological feature.

What are the pharmacological activities of Nervilia plicata?

This plant has anti-diabetic and regenerative pharmacological activities that contribute to its potential medicinal utility. Research indicates that its chemical constituents can help regulate blood glucose levels in metabolic studies. Furthermore, the plant exhibits properties that support the repair and regrowth of damaged tissues. These combined effects suggest that the species could play a role in treating chronic systemic conditions. Such biological functions make it a subject of interest for developing new therapeutic agents.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", the alcoholic stem extract of Nervilia plicata demonstrates significant antidiabetic and regenerative potential. Upon administration of 5 mg/kg of the plant extract, blood glucose levels in NIADDM rats showed a 62.00% decrease on day 0 and a 76.29% decrease on day 30. The extract also resulted in a 61.49% and 70.96% decrease in serum urea and creatinine levels on day 30. Furthermore, lipid peroxidation product levels showed a 70.58% decrease in the kidney and a 77.41% decrease in the pancreas. These findings show that damages caused to the kidney tissue were negligible or not seen.
