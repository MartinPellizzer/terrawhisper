Nervilia plicata

What's the taxonomical classification of Nervilia plicata?

Nervilia plicata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asparagales. This plant is a distinguished representative of the family Orchidaceae, specifically situated within the genus Nervilia, and is identified by the species name plicata.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Nervilia plicata contains bioactive phytochemicals such as alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, and tannins that contribute to its therapeutic properties. These chemical constituents are primarily utilized in traditional medicine to address various ailments including respiratory issues and inflammation. Research indicates that the plant's specific secondary metabolites may possess antioxidant and antimicrobial activities. Local medicinal practices often involve using the tubers to treat skin conditions or digestive disturbances. The concentration and synergy of these compounds determine the plant's overall medicinal efficacy.

What are the therapeutic actions of Nervilia plicata?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including skin infections, digestive issues, and respiratory problems. The rhizomes of this orchid are often prepared into medicinal pastes or decoctions to address specific discomforts. It is frequently applied topically to soothe inflammatory skin conditions. Some traditional practices also utilize the plant to alleviate symptoms of stomach upset or indigestion. These medicinal applications are rooted in local ethnobotanical knowledge passed through generations.
