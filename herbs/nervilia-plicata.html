What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Nervilia plicata has a single, broad, heart-shaped leaf that emerges from a small underground tuber and features prominent longitudinal veins running from the base to the apex. The leaf surface is typically smooth and can vary in color from bright green to deep brownish hues depending on light exposure. This plant lacks a visible stem above ground, as the leaf appears to rise directly from the soil level. The leaf margin is entire and lacks serration, while the base is cordate and slightly overlapping. Tiny, inconspicuous flowers emerge on short stalks, though the foliage remains the most distinct morphological feature.
