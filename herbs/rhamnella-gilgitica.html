Rhamnella gilgitica

What's the taxonomical classification of Rhamnella gilgitica?

Rhamnella gilgitica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Rosales. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the family Rhamnaceae, within the genus Rhamnella, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species gilgitica.

What are the herbal preparations of Rhamnella gilgitica?

Rhamnella gilgitica is prepared as a decoction, infusion, or powder to utilize its medicinal properties. The dried roots and bark are often boiled in water to create a concentrated liquid extract for oral consumption. Alternatively, the plant material can be ground into a fine powder and mixed with honey or water. Some traditional applications involve using the plant in topical pastes to treat external ailments. These various methods allow for different concentrations of the active constituents to be delivered to the body.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Rhamnella gilgitica can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and allergic reactions if ingested or touched directly. Contact with the plant's sap or leaves may lead to localized dermatitis or redness on the skin. If consumed accidentally, individuals might experience nausea, stomach cramping, or vomiting. Some people may also exhibit hypersensitivity symptoms like swelling or itching. It is important to handle this species with caution to avoid these adverse physiological responses.
