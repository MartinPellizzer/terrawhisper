Rhamnella gilgitica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhamnella gilgitica has woody stems, simple elliptical leaves with entire margins, and small tubular flowers arranged in dense axillary clusters. The foliage typically exhibits a leathery texture and a deep green coloration on the upper surface. Its leaves are arranged oppositely along the branches, often featuring distinct venation patterns. The reproductive structures consist of small fruits that develop from the fertilized ovaries of the flowers. These morphological characteristics help distinguish the species within its specific high-altitude habitat.
