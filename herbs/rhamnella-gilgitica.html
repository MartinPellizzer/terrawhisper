Rhamnella gilgitica

What's the taxonomical classification of Rhamnella gilgitica?

Rhamnella gilgitica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Rosales. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the family Rhamnaceae, within the genus Rhamnella, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species gilgitica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhamnella gilgitica has woody stems, simple elliptical leaves with entire margins, and small tubular flowers arranged in dense axillary clusters. The foliage typically exhibits a leathery texture and a deep green coloration on the upper surface. Its leaves are arranged oppositely along the branches, often featuring distinct venation patterns. The reproductive structures consist of small fruits that develop from the fertilized ovaries of the flowers. These morphological characteristics help distinguish the species within its specific high-altitude habitat.

The efficacy of Shengdeng includes treating rheumatism, drying Huangshui, detumescence, and relieving pain. These medicinal functions are mainly utilized for the treatment of rheumatoid arthritis in Tibetan medicine.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal alkaloid maesopsin along with several other bioactive secondary metabolites. These chemical constituents are primarily studied for their potential pharmacological properties in traditional medicine. Research suggests that the presence of these specific compounds may contribute to various therapeutic activities. The unique chemical profile of Rhamnella gilgitica makes it a subject of interest for natural product chemistry. Detailed analysis of these substances helps scientists understand their biological impact on human health.

According to a study published by "Zhongguo Zhong yao za zhi = Zhongguo zhongyao zazhi = China journal of Chinese materia medica", a reversed-phase HPLC method was developed to determine maesopsin, a flavonoid, in the xylem parts of Rhamnella gilgitica. The method utilized an ODS column and a mobile phase of methanol-water (28:27) with 0.05 mol/L KH2PO4 at pH 4. Detection was performed at a UV wavelength of 290 nm. The study reported a recovery of 98.9% with an RSD of 2.1% and n = 6. Furthermore, the content of maesopsin in Rhamnella gilgitica was found to be 1.02% with an RSD of 0.52% and n = 3.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating collagen-induced arthritis, rheumatism, rheumatoid arthritis, pain, and swelling. It possesses bioactive compounds that work to reduce inflammation within the joints. These properties help alleviate the intense pain associated with chronic inflammatory diseases. Furthermore, the plant is effective at minimizing the physical swelling that occurs during a flare-up. Using this species can provide significant relief for those suffering from long-term rheumatic conditions.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", Rhamnella gilgitica is used for removing rheumatism, reducing swelling, and relieving pain. This traditional Tibetan medicinal plant has been used for the treatment of rheumatoid arthritis (RA) for many years. To evaluate its effects, rats were orally administered ethyl acetate extract of Rhamnella gilgitica at doses of 9.71, 19.43, or 38.85 mg/kg for 23 days. The treatment addressed conditions such as collagen-induced arthritis (CIA) by suppressing the arthritis index and paw swelling. It also influenced multiple inflammatory cytokines, including IL-1β, TNF-α, IL-6, IL-17, INF-γ, IL-4, and IL-10.
