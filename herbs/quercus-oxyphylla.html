Quercus oxyphylla

Quercus oxyphylla, also known as the 'sharp-lobed oak', is a deciduous tree native to China and Korea, characterized by its lobed leaves and edible acorns.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects.

The health benefits of Quercus oxyphylla are attributed to its ability to lower cholesterol levels, improve cardiovascular health, and reduce the risk of certain cancers.

The herbal preparations of Quercus oxyphylla include tea, tinctures, and infusions, often consumed to promote overall well-being and prevent chronic diseases.
