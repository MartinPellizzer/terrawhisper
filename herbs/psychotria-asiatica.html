Psychotria asiatica

What's the taxonomical classification of Psychotria asiatica?

Psychotria asiatica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized under the order Gentianaales. This plant is a member of the family Rubiaceae, which places it in the genus Psychotria, specifically identified by the species name asiatica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Psychotria asiatica has opposite, elliptical to oblong leaves that are typically 5 to 15 centimeters long with entire margins and an acute apex. The plant is a woody shrub characterized by its smooth, light brown bark and a branching growth habit. Its small, tubular flowers are often white or cream-colored and emerge from the leaf axils in clusters. The fruit consists of small, fleshy drupes that transition in color as they reach maturity. These morphological traits are consistent with many members of the Rubiaceae family.

What are the pharmacological activities of Psychotria asiatica?

This plant has potent pharmacological activities including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and cytotoxic properties. Its anti-inflammatory effects help reduce swelling and pain by modulating various biochemical pathways in the body. The antioxidant capacity of Psychotria asiatica works to neutralize free radicals and prevent oxidative stress within cells. Additionally, the plant exhibits significant cytotoxic activity which suggests a potential role in inhibiting the growth of certain cancer cell lines. These diverse biological functions highlight the therapeutic potential of the species in medicinal research.

As discussed in a study, the essential oil from Psychotria asiatica L. contains 53 distinct constituents including (E)-citral at 20.6%, 10-epi-gamma-eudesmol at 15.9%, (Z)-citral at 10.5%, geraniol at 7.4%, alpha-cadinol at 6.7%, 7-epi-alpha-eudesmol at 4.4%, linalool at 3.7%, and alpha-muurolol at 3.4%. The oil did not exhibit antioxidant activity, showing an IC50 value of > 100 µg/mL in the DPPH model. It demonstrated anti-inflammatory potential by inhibiting NO production in Lipopolysaccharide-induced RAW264.7 macrophage cells with an IC50 value of 29.08 ± 1.54 µg/mL. Additionally, the essential oil showed significant cytotoxicity against the SK-LU-1 cancer cell line, yielding an IC50 value of 39.75 ± 1.79 µg/mL in the sulforhodamine B (SRB) assay.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause adverse reactions including nausea, vomiting, dizziness, diarrhea, abdominal pain, sedation, and hypotension. These gastrointestinal issues often manifest shortly after ingestion and can lead to significant physical discomfort. Neurological effects such as dizziness and profound sedation may impair a person's ability to function safely. Furthermore, a notable drop in blood pressure, known as hypotension, can occur in some individuals. Monitoring these diverse physiological responses is essential when interacting with the species.
