Potentilla peduncularis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Potentiella peduncularis has a perennial growth habit characterized by erect, branched stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 40 centimeters. The leaves are primarily basal and trifoliate, featuring serrated leaflets that often display a slightly hairy or pubescent underside. Small, yellow, five-petaled flowers are borne on distinct, elongated stalks known as peduncles, which give the species its specific name. These floral structures emerge from the leaf axils and are often surrounded by inconspicuous bracts. The plant's overall morphology is adapted to subalpine and alpine environments where low-growing, hardy structures are advantageous.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

Its range often reaches into the mountain ranges of Russia and parts of Scandinavia where cold climates prevail. The species thrives in specific microclimates characterized by well-drained soils and significant sunlight exposure.
