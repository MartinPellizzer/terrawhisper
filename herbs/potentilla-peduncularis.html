Potentilla peduncularis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Potentiella peduncularis has a perennial growth habit characterized by erect, branched stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 40 centimeters. The leaves are primarily basal and trifoliate, featuring serrated leaflets that often display a slightly hairy or pubescent underside. Small, yellow, five-petaled flowers are borne on distinct, elongated stalks known as peduncles, which give the species its specific name. These floral structures emerge from the leaf axils and are often surrounded by inconspicuous bracts. The plant's overall morphology is adapted to subalpine and alpine environments where low-growing, hardy structures are advantageous.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of Central and Eastern Europe, extending through the Alps, the Carpathians, and the Balkan Peninsula. It is typically found in montane and subalpine environments where rocky terrain or alpine meadows provide suitable habitats. Its range often reaches into the mountain ranges of Russia and parts of Scandinavia where cold climates prevail. The species thrives in specific microclimates characterized by well-drained soils and significant sunlight exposure. Due to its preference for elevated landscapes, its distribution is fragmented across various mountain chains rather than being continuous.

      What are the herbal preparations of Potentilla peduncularis?

      Potentiella pedunculata is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical tincture derived from its dried aerial parts. These liquid preparations are primarily used to extract the tannins and other bioactive compounds found in the plant.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Potentiella peduncularis can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, or gastrointestinal distress if ingested by humans or animals. While specific toxicological studies on this particular species are limited, many plants in the Rosaceae family contain compounds that may trigger hypersensitivity in sensitive individuals. If consumed, individuals may experience nausea, vomiting, or abdominal discomfort due to unverified chemical constituents.
