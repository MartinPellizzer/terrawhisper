Sedum filipes

What's the taxonomical classification of Sedum filipes?

Sedum filipes belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Saxifrages. This succulent plant is a member of the family Crassulaceae, specifically placed within the genus Sedum, and is identified by its unique species name, filipes.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sedum filipes has slender, thread-like stems that are often trailing or creeping and leaves that are small, linear, and succulent. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are usually pale yellow or cream in color. During the flowering stage, the delicate stems become more noticeable as they support the light inflorescences. It performs best in rock gardens or containers where drainage is easily managed. Avoid using heavy fertilizers, as excessive nutrients can lead to weak growth and stretched stems.
