Pternopetalum vulgare

What's the taxonomical classification of Pternopetalum vulgare?

Pternopetalum vulgare belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic journey continues through the order Apiales, which leads to its placement in the family Apiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Pternopetalum, and its specific species epithet, vulgaris.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pternopetalum vulgare has deeply pinnatifid leaves characterized by narrow, lanceolate lobes that extend from a central rachis. The foliage exhibits a delicate, fern-like texture with a fine venation pattern visible on the underside of each segment. Its stems are slender and herbaceous, often displaying a slight pubescence along the nodes. The reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the axillary regions of the leaf stalks. These floral components typically feature a pale coloration that blends seamlessly with the surrounding verdant canopy.
