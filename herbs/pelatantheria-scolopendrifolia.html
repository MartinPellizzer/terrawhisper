Pelatantheria scolopendrifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pelatantheria scolopendrifolia has pinnately compound leaves characterized by numerous narrow, elongated leaflets arranged along a central rachis. The leaf structure resembles that of a fern, which is reflected in its specific epithet. The leaflets are typically arranged in a sub-opposite to alternate pattern and possess entire margins. These leaves are supported by sturdy petioles that emerge from the plant's woody stem. The overall architecture of the foliage contributes to its distinct appearance within its tropical forest habitat.
