What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lonicera rupicola has opposite, simple leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape with entire or slightly serrated margins and a glabrous or sparsely pubescent surface. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often appearing in pairs or small clusters during the blooming season. These blossoms are typically pale yellow or cream-colored and lead to the development of small, fleshy berries. The overall structure of the plant is adapted to rocky or scrubby habitats where its sprawling vines can navigate uneven terrain.
