Melissa axillaris

What's the taxonomical classification of Melissa axillaris?

Melissa axillaris belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Lamiales. The plant is a member of the family Lamiaceae, which falls under the genus Melissa, and is specifically identified by its species name, axillaris.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Melissa axillaris has a square stem, opposite leaves with serrated margins, and small, two-lipped flowers arranged in axillary clusters. The plant typically exhibits a low-growing or decumbent growth habit with woody bases as it matures. Its leaves are generally ovate or lanceolate in shape and possess a distinctively textured, slightly hairy surface. The flowers are often pale lilac or white and are characterized by a prominent lower lip that serves as a landing platform for pollinators. Small, nut-like fruits develop within the calyx following successful pollination.
