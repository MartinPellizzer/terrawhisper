What's the taxonomical classification of Desmodium heterophyllum?

Desmodium heterophyllum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is situated in the order Fabales. It is a member of the family Fabaceae, which places it within the genus Desmodium, and it is specifically identified by its species name, heterophyllum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Desmodium heterophyllum has pinnately compound leaves that typically feature five to seven leaflets, including a large terminal leaflet and smaller lateral leaflets. Small, papilionaceous flowers are produced in clusters from the leaf axils. The fruit is a characteristic legume that often possesses small hairs or prickles to assist in seed dispersal.

What are the pharmacological activities of Desmodium heterophyllum?

This plant has significant pharmacological activities including potent anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory properties. These biological actions are primarily attributed to the presence of various bioactive phytochemicals like flavonoids and tannins found within its leaves. Researchers have observed that its extracts can effectively reduce swelling and suppress the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines. Furthermore, the plant helps regulate the immune system by balancing the activity of different white blood cells. These diverse therapeutic effects suggest that the species holds considerable potential for developing new medicinal treatments.

As discussed in a study, the aqueous extract from Desmodium heterophyllum (AEDh) demonstrated relevant anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory activity. When tested in J774A.1 and RAW 264.7 macrophages, AEDh showed no cytotoxicity at the concentrations tested and significantly reduced NO production in vitro. In vivo, the administration of AEDh at doses ranging from 50 to 250 mg/kg reduced paw edema in BALB/c mice. The combined treatment, especially using AEDh at 250 mg/kg alongside PBM, produced a pronounced effect comparable to dexamethasone. This combination also markedly reduced pro-inflammatory cytokines, particularly IL-1β and IL-6.
