Desmodium heterophyllum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Desmodium heterophyllum has pinnately compound leaves that typically feature five to seven leaflets, including a large terminal leaflet and smaller lateral leaflets. The leaflets are generally ovate to elliptic in shape with entire or slightly wavy margins. The plant exhibits a herbaceous growth habit with stems that can be erect or ascending. Small, papilionaceous flowers are produced in clusters from the leaf axils. The fruit is a characteristic legume that often possesses small hairs or prickles to assist in seed dispersal.

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, specifically occurring in countries such as Thailand, Vietnam, Laos, and parts of southern China. The species is often found growing in well-drained soils where humidity remains relatively high throughout the year. Its distribution is closely tied to the seasonal monsoon patterns that define the climate of these specific latitudes.
