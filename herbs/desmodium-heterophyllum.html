Desmodium heterophyllum

What's the taxonomical classification of Desmodium heterophyllum?

Desmodium heterophyllum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is situated in the order Fabales. It is a member of the family Fabaceae, which places it within the genus Desmodium, and it is specifically identified by its species name, heterophyllum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Desmodium heterophyllum has pinnately compound leaves that typically feature five to seven leaflets, including a large terminal leaflet and smaller lateral leaflets. The leaflets are generally ovate to elliptic in shape with entire or slightly wavy margins. The plant exhibits a herbaceous growth habit with stems that can be erect or ascending. Small, papilionaceous flowers are produced in clusters from the leaf axils. The fruit is a characteristic legume that often possesses small hairs or prickles to assist in seed dispersal.

The species is often found growing in well-drained soils where humidity remains relatively high throughout the year. What are the pharmacological activities of Desmodium heterophyllum?

This plant has significant anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory pharmacological activities that contribute to its therapeutic potential. The chemical constituents within the leaves work to suppress the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines in the body. These bioactive compounds also help regulate the immune system's response to various pathogens and stressors. By balancing immune functions, the plant can mitigate excessive inflammatory reactions and support natural defense mechanisms. Such properties suggest its potential utility in treating conditions characterized by chronic inflammation or immune dysfunction.

According to a study published by "Lasers in medical science", the aqueous extract from Desmodium heterophyllum (AEDh) demonstrated relevant anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory activity. In vitro evaluations showed that AEDh caused no cytotoxicity at the tested concentrations and significantly reduced nitric oxide (NO) production in J774A.1 and RAW 264.7 macrophages. For in vivo assessment, AEDh was administered to BALB/c mice at doses of 50-250 mg/kg. The treatment reduced paw edema, with the 250 mg/kg dose producing a pronounced effect when combined with photobiomodulation. This combination also markedly reduced pro-inflammatory cytokines, particularly IL-1β and IL-6.

According to a study published by "Lasers in medical science", Desmodium heterophyllum is investigated for its anti-inflammatory and immuno-modulatory properties to manage inflammatory conditions. The aqueous extract of Desmodium heterophyllum (AEDh) was evaluated at concentrations of 50-250 mg/kg in a model of carrageenan-induced paw edema. In this study, AEDh showed no cytotoxicity and significantly reduced nitric oxide (NO) production in J774A.1 and RAW 264.7 macrophages. When used at 250 mg/kg, the extract produced a pronounced effect in reducing paw edema and pro-inflammatory cytokines, specifically IL-1β and IL-6. These findings suggest that AEDh can be an effective strategy for managing inflammation, particularly when combined with photobiomodulation therapy using 780 nm, 70 mW, and 35 J/cm².
