Botanical Identification of Periwinkle (Vinca minor)

To identify Periwinkle (Vinca minor) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Periwinkle (Vinca minor) Monograph

Scientific Classification

Periwinkle, or Vinca minor, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Liliopsida, order Apocynales, family Apocynaceae, genus Vinca, and species Vinca minor. This classification places it within the flowering plants, specifically in the milkweed family, which includes many medicinal and ornamental species.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Vinca minor is the scientific name for Periwinkle. It was first described by Carl von Linné in 1753. Common synonyms include Vinca reptans and Vinca sepulcralis. It is also known by regional names such as creeping myrtle and lesser periwinkle. Historically, it has been referred to as wood-sage and garden pimpernel.

Morphological Characteristics

Periwinkle (Vinca minor) is a low-growing, evergreen perennial with a dense, mat-like growth habit. It has a fibrous root system that spreads horizontally, anchoring the plant and aiding in nutrient absorption. The stems are prostrate, often rooting at the nodes, and the leaves are heart-shaped, glossy, and arranged alternately along the stems.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Periwinkle (Vinca minor) can be distinguished by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves and blue-violet flowers. It has opposite leaves and produces two small berries. It is often confused with Vinca major, but it has smaller leaves and grows lower to the ground. Its creeping habit and evergreen nature also help in identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Periwinkle (Vinca minor) is native to Europe, North Africa, and Western Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with partial shade and moist, well-drained soil. It typically grows at low altitudes, often in woodland areas or along garden paths.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Periwinkle blooms from late spring to early summer, typically between May and June. Seed formation occurs in late summer, around August. For identification purposes, the plant is most identifiable during its flowering period, making late spring to early summer the optimal time for accurate botanical identification.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Periwinkle (Vinca minor) is closely related to other species in the Vinca genus. It shares familial traits with plants in the Apocynaceae family, such as opposite leaves and showy flowers. These relationships help in understanding its ecological and medicinal significance within the broader context of herbal medicine.
