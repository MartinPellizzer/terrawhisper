What's the taxonomical classification of Cirsium lineare?

Cirsium lineare belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Asterales and is a member of the family Asteraceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Cirsium and the species lineare.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cirsium lineare has linear, narrow leaves that are typically 2 to 10 millimeters wide and possess sharply serrated margins with small, spinose teeth. The plant's stems are upright and often feature small spines or bristles along their length. Its inflorescence consists of small, tubular flower heads that are usually clustered in dense, terminal heads. These flower heads are typically pink or purple and are surrounded by bracts that may also be prickly. The overall architecture of the plant is slender and wiry compared to other thistle species.
