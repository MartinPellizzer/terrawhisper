What's the taxonomical classification of Aeschynanthus bracteatus?

Aeschynanthus bracteatus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it falls under the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Gesneriaceae. Ultimately, its specific biological identity is defined by its placement within the genus Aeschynanthus, with the unique designation of the species bracteatus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aeschynanthus bracteatus has fleshy, lanceolate leaves that emerge from trailing stems and large, tubular red flowers protected by prominent, leaf-like bracts. The defining characteristic is the large, colorful bracts that wrap around the base of the flower buds. These bracts serve to protect the delicate reproductive organs while attracting pollinators through their intense pigmentation. How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires bright, indirect sunlight, a consistent temperature between 65 and 80 degrees Fahrenheit, and a well-draining orchid bark or peat-based potting mix kept moist but never waterlogged.

Because it is an epiphyte, providing a climbing support like a moss pole helps its aerial roots anchor securely. You should maintain high humidity levels above 60 percent to prevent the delicate foliage from wilting. Watering should occur when the top inch of soil feels dry to the touch to avoid root rot.

Fertilizing with a balanced liquid formula once a month during the growing season will promote its spectacular colorful bracts.
