What's the taxonomical classification of Bolboschoenus yagara?

Bolboschoenus yagara belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Poales. The plant is further categorized into the family Cyperaceae, within the genus Bolboschoenus, and is specifically identified by its species name, yagara.

What are the pharmacological activities of Bolboschoenus yagara?

This plant has anti-metastatic, antioxidant, and anti-inflammatory pharmacological activities that make it a subject of significant medicinal interest. Its specialized chemical compounds work to inhibit the spread of cancer cells to distant organs within the body. Researchers have also observed that its extracts can neutralize harmful free radicals to prevent cellular damage. Furthermore, the plant demonstrates a capacity to reduce systemic inflammation through various biochemical pathways. These diverse biological properties suggest that it holds potential for developing new therapeutic agents.

As discussed in a study, the ethyl acetate fraction from the tuber of Bolboschoenus yagara exhibits anti-metastatic activities. At a concentration of 50 µg/mL, this fraction dramatically inhibits the migration and invasion of mouse melanoma B16 cells in vitro. In pulmonary and hepatic metastasis assays, the 50 µg/mL fraction alleviated mouse lung and liver weights, the number of metastatic nodules, and levels of TNF-α and IL-6. The inhibition of hepatic metastasis by the 50 µg/mL fraction was almost equivalent to that of 1 µM TAK242. Furthermore, HPLC analysis quantified fourteen compounds within the fraction, including isocoumarins, stilbenes, and xanthones that abated LPS-modulated B16 cells migration and invasion.
