What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phlomis maximowiczii has a perennial growth habit characterized by upright, square-shaped stems that reach heights of approximately 60 to 100 centimeters. Its inflorescence consists of dense, whorled clusters of tubular flowers that emerge from the upper leaf axils. These flowers are typically pale yellow or creamy white and feature a distinct two-lipped structure characteristic of the Lamiaceae family.
