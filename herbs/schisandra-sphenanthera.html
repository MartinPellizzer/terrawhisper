Schisandra sphenanthera

What's the taxonomical classification of Schisandra sphenanthera?

Schisandra sphenanthera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Austrobaileyales and the family Schisandraceae. Finally, the plant is placed within the genus Schisandra, specifically identified as the species sphenanthera.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Schisandra sphenanthera has woody climbing vines characterized by alternate, simple, elliptic to ovate leaves with entire margins and an obtuse apex. The foliage typically features a glossy upper surface and a slightly paler underside. Its inflorescences consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These flowers eventually develop into aggregate schizocarps, where each fruit is a small, fleshy drupelet. The overall structure allows the plant to scramble through forest undergrowth using its flexible stems.

What are the pharmacological activities of Schisandra sphenanthera?

This plant has anti-inflammatory, anti-oxidant, antiviral, hepatoprotective, and neuroprotective pharmacological activities. Its bioactive compounds work to reduce oxidative stress and suppress inflammatory responses within biological systems. The extract demonstrates the ability to protect liver cells from toxic damage and support overall hepatic function. Furthermore, it exhibits antiviral properties that can inhibit certain pathogen developments. Research also indicates that its constituents provide neuroprotective benefits by shielding neural cells from degeneration.

As discussed in a study, Schisandra sphenanthera fruit extract (SFE) has demonstrated hepatoprotective properties by attenuating APAP-induced liver injury. Through network pharmacology analysis, it was indicated that SFE can act via key targets including MAPK3 and CASP3. The SFE effectively alleviated APAP-induced oxidative stress involving MDA, SOD, and GSH, as well as inflammation involving IL-6, TNF-α, and IL-1β. Additionally, SFE promoted the growth of Bacteroidales and Erysipelotrichales while decreasing the growth of Lactobacillales. These changes in the gut microbiota led to increased production of tryptophan metabolites and improved liver and gut function.

As discussed in a study, the medicinal compounds found in the fruits of Schisandra sphenanthera include schisantherin A, schizandrin, deoxyschizandrin, and gamma-schizandrin. The content of schisantherin A in Schisandra sphenanthera was measured according to the set standards of the Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2010 Edition. To determine these lignan contents, HPLC separation was performed using a Diamonsil C18 150 mm x 4.6 mm, 5 microm column.
