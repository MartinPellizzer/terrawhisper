What is Pavetta salicina?

Pavetta salicina is a plant species that belongs to the family Rubiaceae.

It is a shrub or small tree, typically growing up to 4 meters in height, with a rounded crown and a straight or slightly curved stem. The leaves are simple, opposite, and elliptical in shape, measuring 5-10 centimeters in length and 2-4 centimeters in width, with an entire margin and a pointed tip. The flowers are small and white or yellowish in color, arranged in axillary clusters, and contain four or five petals.

The fruit is a capsule, measuring 1-2 centimeters in length, containing numerous seeds.

What are the medicinal constituents this herb?

The most important healing constituents of Pavetta salicina are quercetin, kaempferol, epicatechin, rutin, and ellagic acid, which have been identified as bioactive compounds responsible for its medicinal properties.

Rutin, a flavonol glycoside, has been shown to possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, and is also involved in the regulation of blood pressure. These bioactive compounds work synergistically to provide Pavetta salicina with its wide range of medicinal properties, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and cardio-protective effects.

The main herbal preparations of Pavetta salicina are primarily used to leverage its bioactive properties.
