What's the taxonomical classification of Skimmia multinervia?

Skimmia multinervia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this botanical lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is organized under the order Sapindales and the family Rutaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Skimmia and is specifically designated as the species multinerviia.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Skimmia multinervia can cause gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, and potential toxicity if any part of the plant is ingested. The plant contains various alkaloids and compounds that may trigger nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain in humans. Direct contact with the foliage or berries can result in contact dermatitis or localized skin rashes for sensitive individuals. Pets that consume the plant are also at risk of experiencing adverse physical reactions. Due to these risks, it is important to handle the plant with care and keep it away from children and animals.
