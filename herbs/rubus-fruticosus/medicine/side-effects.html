10 Possible Side Effects Of Rubus Fruticosus (Blackberry)

Rubus fruticosus has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, allergic reactions, and interact with medications.

Causes of these side effects include high doses, individual sensitivities, and pre-existing health conditions. For example, severe gastrointestinal issues can lead to dehydration, electrolyte imbalances, and even organ failure.

Allergic reactions can result in anaphylaxis, a life-threatening condition.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Rubus fruticosus if used imporperly.

1. Increases risk of seizures

Rubus fruticosus causes allergic reactions in some individuals due to the presence of specific proteins and phenolic compounds in its fruit.

The allergenic properties are thought to be triggered by the breakdown of these compounds during digestion, leading to an immune response that manifests as symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.

Rubus fruticosus induces gastrointestinal upset due to its anthraquinone and tannin content.

These compounds can irritate the mucous membranes in the digestive tract, leading to symptoms such as abdominal pain, diarrhea, and nausea.

The astringent properties of tannins may also reduce the motility of the gut, further exacerbating these adverse effects.

The polyphenolic compounds present in blackberry, such as anthocyanins and flavonoids, can enhance the activity of certain enzymes involved in glucose metabolism, leading to a rapid decrease in blood sugar levels.

This can be particularly problematic for individuals with diabetes or those taking medications that affect blood sugar control.
