Citrus × paradisi

What's the taxonomical classification of Citrus × paradisi?

Citrus × paradisi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this botanical hierarchy, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Sapindales and the family Rutaceae. Finally, the plant is placed within the genus Citrus, with the specific designation of the species paradisi.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Citrus × paradisi contains bioactive compounds such as hesperidin, naringin, polymethoxyflavones, and various essential oils like limonene that provide antioxidant and anti-inflammatory benefits. These flavonoids are known to improve vascular function and help regulate lipid metabolism in the human body. The presence of vitamin C further enhances its medicinal profile by supporting immune function and collagen synthesis. Additionally, the citrus peel contains limonoids which have been studied for their potential anticancer and antimicrobial properties. These diverse phytochemicals work synergistically to combat oxidative stress at a cellular level.

What are the therapeutic actions of Citrus × paradisi?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various digestive ailments, cardiovascular issues, and skin inflammations through its high concentration of bioactive compounds. The presence of flavonoids and vitamin C in the fruit helps manage oxidative stress and supports overall immune function. Some studies suggest that extracts from the peel can assist in regulating cholesterol levels and improving metabolic health. Additionally, the antimicrobial properties found in the essential oils are often utilized to address minor topical infections. These therapeutic effects are largely attributed to the plant's rich profile of antioxidants and polyphenols.
