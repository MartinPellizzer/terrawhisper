Euscaphis japonica

What's the taxonomical classification of Euscaphis japonica?

Euscaphis japonica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Crossosomatales and is a member of the family Staphyleaceae. Finally, its taxonomic hierarchy is completed by its placement within the genus Euscaphis, specifically identifying it as the species japonica.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

The ellagic acids function as potent antioxidants that help protect cells from oxidative stress and damage. Megastigmane contributes to the plant's biological activity by offering potential anti-inflammatory properties. Furthermore, the presence of tetraketide provides unique chemical structures that may assist in various therapeutic applications. These combined phytochemicals work together to support the overall medicinal profile of the species.

As discussed in a study, a method was developed for the simultaneous determination of five ellagic acids in Euscaphis japonica. The analysis used an Acquity BEH C18 column with dimensions of 2.1 mm x 100 mm and 1.7 microm. Monitoring was performed at a detection wavelength of 245 nm with a flow rate of 0.2 mL x min(-1). All calibration curves for these compounds showed a linear relationship with r > 0.9997. Overall recoveries for the substances were found in the range of 97.2%-102.1% with an RSD less than 3.2% for n = 6. The precision test resulted in an overall RSD of less than 2.9%.
