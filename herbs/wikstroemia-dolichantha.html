Wikstroemia dolichantha belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. This plant is a member of the family Thymelaeaceae, specifically placed within the genus Wikstroemia, and is identified by its unique species designation, dolichantha.

As discussed in a study, plants of the genus Wikstroemia have been used as traditional medicines to treat diseases like pneumonia, rheumatism, and bronchitis. Ten flavonoids were isolated from Wikstroemia dolichantha to examine their anti-allergic activities by measuring beta-hexosaminidase release from RBL-2H3 cells. Among these ten flavonoids, chamaejasmine was found to most potently inhibit DNp-specific IgE-induced degranulation in RBL-2H3 cells. Topical administration of 0.5% chamaejasmine attenuated clinical symptoms of DNCB-induced dermatitis and significantly reduced dermal thickness and mast cell infiltration in the dermis. Furthermore, 0.5% chamaejasmine inhibited DNCB-induced increases in total IL-4 and IgE levels in serum while improving skin barrier function and increasing epidermis moisture.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

These specific chemical constituents contribute to the various biological activities observed in the species. Aromadendrin and chamaejasmine are often studied for their unique structural properties and potential therapeutic roles. Additionally, the presence of padmatin and the flavonoid taxifolin provides significant antioxidant benefits. Understanding these molecules helps researchers unlock the full medicinal potential of the plant.

As discussed in a study, the EtOH extract of Wikstroemia dolichantha (WDE) contains four flavonoids tentatively identified by HPLC-DAD/QTOF-MS. These medicinal compounds include taxifolin, aromadendrin, padmatin, and chamaejasmine. When 1% WDE was applied daily to mice for 2 or 3 weeks, it improved skin symptoms induced by oxazolone and DNCB. The treatment reduced serum IL-4 and IgE levels while decreasing TEWL and increasing skin hydration. These results suggest that WDE might be useful for the management of atopic dermatitis.
