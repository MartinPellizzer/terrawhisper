Botanical Identification of Senegal Acacia (Acacia senegal)

To identify Senegal Acacia (Acacia senegal) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Senegal Acacia (Acacia senegal) Monograph

Scientific Classification

Acacia senegal belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Fabales, order Fabales, family Fabaceae, genus Acacia, and species Acacia senegal. This classification places it within the legume family, highlighting its botanical relationships and characteristics relevant to herbal medicine.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Senegal Acacia is Acacia senegal. It was first described by the botanist L. H. B. de Winter in 1889. This species is also known by several synonyms, including Vachellia senegalensis, reflecting its taxonomic history and classification changes over time.

Morphological Characteristics

Acacia senegal is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree with a shallow, spreading root system. Its stem is straight, cylindrical, and often covered with a thick, pale layer of bark. The leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small, oval leaflets. The inflorescence consists of dense clusters of yellow flowers, which are followed by elongated, thin pods containing seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Acacia senegal can be distinguished by its thin, light gray bark with longitudinal fissures, small bipinnate leaves, and yellowish-green flowers. It produces a sticky gum resin, which is a key identifier. It is often confused with Acacia nilotica, but it has a more delicate appearance and a distinct, less woody texture.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Acacia senegal is native to the Sahel region of Africa, including parts of Senegal, Sudan, and Somalia. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates with high temperatures and low rainfall. It prefers sandy or loamy soils and is typically found at elevations between 200 and 600 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Acacia senegal blooms from March to May, producing yellow flowers. Seed formation occurs from June to September. Harvesting takes place during the dry season, typically from November to March, when the gum is most concentrated and ready for collection.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Acacia senegal belongs to the Fabaceae family, sharing traits with other legumes. It is closely related to species like Acacia nilotica and Acacia tortilis. These species are part of the Acacia genus, which is closely tied to the broader legume family through shared morphological and genetic features.
