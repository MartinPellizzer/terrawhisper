10 Possible Side Effects Of Armoracia Rusticana (Horseradish)

Armoracia rusticana has some side effects when used improperly, such as digestive issues, allergic reactions, and skin irritation.

These side effects can be caused by excessive consumption, individual intolerance, or improper preparation methods.

For instance, consuming large quantities can lead to heartburn, nausea, and diarrhea, severely impacting daily life and productivity.

1. Induces respiratory distress

Armoracia rusticana causes stomach upset due to its high content of allyl isothiocyanate and sinigrin compounds.

These sulfur-containing molecules can irritate the mucous membranes in the digestive tract, leading to discomfort, bloating, and nausea.

Additionally, the pungent volatile oils present in horseradish may also stimulate acid production in the stomach, exacerbating existing gastrointestinal issues such as heartburn and indigestion.

These chemical components can cause an immune system response, leading to symptoms like hives, itching, and swelling.

Additionally, the high levels of sulfur and volatile oils in horseradish may also contribute to the development of allergic reactions in susceptible individuals.

Armoracia rusticana triggers bronchospasm due to its high concentration of allyl isothiocyanate, a potent sulfur compound that can stimulate the lungs and cause airway constriction.

When ingested or inhaled, this compound can bind to lung tissue and trigger the release of histamine, leading to increased mucus production and inflammation in the airways.

This can result in bronchospasm, making it difficult for individuals with pre-existing respiratory conditions such as asthma to breathe.
