Botanical Identification of Garden Horseradish (Armoracia rusticana)

To identify Garden Horseradish (Armoracia rusticana) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Garden Horseradish, Armoracia rusticana, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Spermatophyta, class Caryophyllopsida, order Brassicales, family Brassicaceae, genus Armoracia, and species Armoracia rusticana. These classifications reflect its evolutionary relationships and botanical characteristics within the plant kingdom.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Garden Horseradish is Armoracia rusticana, first described by Carl von Linne in 1753. It is also known by several synonyms, including Armoracia hortensis and Eutrophea rusticana. Common names such as horseradish, garden horseradish, and Russian horseradish are widely used. Regional and historical names reflect its long use in traditional medicine and culinary practices.

Morphological Characteristics

Garden horseradish is a biennial herb with a robust taproot and lateral roots. Its stems are erect, hairy, and branched. Leaves are alternate, pinnately compound with serrated leaflets. The inflorescence is a terminal raceme of small white flowers. Flowers are hermaphroditic with four petals. The fruit is a long, thin pod containing several seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Garden Horseradish can be distinguished by its thick, white, fleshy roots with a strong horseradish scent. It has large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers in clusters. It is often cultivated for its pungent root, differing from similar species like Wasabi or other mustard family plants. Its roots are typically larger and more robust compared to wild relatives.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Garden Horseradish is native to Eastern Europe and Western Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with cold winters and warm summers. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils. It is typically found at altitudes between 200 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Garden horseradish blooms in late spring to early summer, typically between May and June. Seed formation occurs in late summer, around August. For identification purposes, the plant is best harvested in late summer to early autumn when the roots are fully developed.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Garden Horseradish (Armoracia rusticana) is closely related to other members of the Brassicaceae family. It shares characteristics with species like mustard and cress, and is part of the tribe Brassiceae. Its phylogenetic position reflects its close ties to other cruciferous plants.
