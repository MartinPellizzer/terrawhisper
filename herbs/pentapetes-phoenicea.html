Pentapetes phoenicea

What's the taxonomical classification of Pentapetes phoenicea?

Pentapetes phoenicea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows a specific evolutionary lineage that leads to its placement in the order Malvales. Further taxonomic refinement identifies it as a member of the family Malvaceae, specifically falling under the genus Pentapetes, with its unique designation as the species phoenicea.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Pentapetes phoenicea contains bioactive secondary metabolites such as tannins, flavonoids, and saponins that provide its therapeutic potential. These chemical constituents contribute to the plant's documented antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Researchers have investigated these compounds for their ability to combat oxidative stress in cellular models. The presence of specific phenolic compounds also suggests potential antimicrobial activities. Such medicinal properties make the species a subject of interest in ethnobotanical and pharmacological studies.

What are the therapeutic actions of Pentapetes phoenicea?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, gastrointestinal issues, and inflammatory conditions. The extracts from its leaves and bark possess antimicrobial properties that help combat various bacterial pathogens. When applied topically, it serves as an effective remedy for healing sores and soothing dermatological irritation. Some traditional practices also involve using the plant to alleviate stomach aches and digestive discomfort. Its bioactive compounds contribute to its reputation as a versatile medicinal resource in regional folk medicine.
