Pentapetes phoenicea

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pentapetes phoenicea has a woody, perennial growth habit characterized by erect stems and ovate to elliptical leaves with entire margins. The foliage is typically dark green and possesses a slightly leathery texture that aids in moisture retention. Small, fragrant flowers emerge from the axillary regions, displaying a tubular structure that transitions into spreading lobes. These blooms often feature a vibrant reddish or pinkish coloration, which is reflected in the specific epithet. The reproductive organs are tucked within the floral tube, ensuring specialized pollination processes.
