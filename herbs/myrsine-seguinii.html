What's the taxonomical classification of Myrsine seguinii?

Myrsine seguinii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent through the order Ericales, the plant is placed in the family Primulaceae. Ultimately, it is identified by the genus Myrsine and the specific epithet seguinii, forming its complete scientific name.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds 3-Geranyl-4-hydroxy-5-(3'-methyl-2'-butenyl)benzoic acid, alangionoside J, ampelopsisionoside, linarionoside A, and quercetin. These specific chemical constituents contribute to the complex phytochemical profile of the species. Each of these molecules plays a distinct role in the plant's biological functions and potential therapeutic properties. Researchers often study these substances to understand their bioactive potential in various medicinal applications. The presence of these diverse compounds highlights the significant chemical richness found within this particular plant.

As discussed in a study, bioassay-guided isolation of anti-inflammarotary compounds from the methanol extract of Myrsine seguinii yielded compound 1, identified as 3-geranyl-4-hydroxy-5-(3'-methyl-2'-butenyl)benzoic acid. This compound strongly suppressed 12-O-tetradecanoylphorbol-13-acetate induced inflammation on mouse ears at a dose of 500 micrograms, achieving an inhibitory effect of 65%. At the same 500-microgram application, the acetate and the methyl ether of compound 1 showed moderate activity with an inhibitory effect of 38% and 27%, respectively. In contrast, the methyl ester and the dimethyl derivative of compound 1 did not show activity at that dose. The results indicate that the carboxylic acid and lipophilic terpene moieties of compound 1 are significant structural features for its anti-inflamatory activity.
