Myrsine seguinii

What's the taxonomical classification of Myrsine seguinii?

Myrsine seguinii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent through the order Ericales, the plant is placed in the family Primulaceae. Ultimately, it is identified by the genus Myrsine and the specific epithet seguinii, forming its complete scientific name.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Myrsine seguinii contains triterpenoids, flavonoids, and alkaloids that exhibit significant pharmacological activities such as anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects. The plant's chemical profile is characterized by specific triterpene acids that contribute to its ability to reduce swelling and oxidative stress in biological tissues. Flavonoid compounds within the leaves and stems further enhance its protective properties against cellular damage. Additionally, certain alkaloids present in the species provide the basis for its traditional use in treating various infections. These diverse bioactive secondary metabolites work synergistically to support the plant's medicinal utility in ethnopharmacology.
