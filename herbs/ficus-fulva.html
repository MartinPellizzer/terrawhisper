Ficus fulva belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is classified under the order Rosales. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ficus fulva has woody stems, ovate to elliptical leaves with entire margins, and small, pale yellowish-green syconia produced in the leaf axils. The foliage is typically characterized by a coriaceous texture and a lustrous upper surface that provides a distinct sheen. As a hemiepiphytic species, its growth habit often begins with aerial roots that eventually descend to the ground to stabilize the plant. The leaf venation is prominent, with secondary veins arching toward the leaf tip in a pinnate pattern. These morphological traits allow the plant to adapt effectively to its tropical forest environment.
