Ficus fulva

What's the taxonomical classification of Ficus fulva?

Ficus fulva belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is classified under the order Rosales. This plant is a member of the family Moraceae and is specifically identified within the genus Ficus, under the species name fulva.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ficus fulva has woody stems, ovate to elliptical leaves with entire margins, and small, pale yellowish-green syconia produced in the leaf axils. The foliage is typically characterized by a coriaceous texture and a lustrous upper surface that provides a distinct sheen. As a hemiepiphytic species, its growth habit often begins with aerial roots that eventually descend to the ground to stabilize the plant. The leaf venation is prominent, with secondary veins arching toward the leaf tip in a pinnate pattern. These morphological traits allow the plant to adapt effectively to its tropical forest environment.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires bright, indirect sunlight and well-draining, organic-rich soil to thrive in tropical or subtropical environments.

You should maintain consistent moisture in the substrate without allowing the roots to sit in standing water. High humidity levels are essential for healthy foliage development, so regular misting or a pebble tray is recommended. Temperatures should remain warm and stable, ideally staying between 65 and 85 degrees Fahrenheit.

Avoid frequent repotting to prevent stress, as this species prefers to establish a strong root system in its current container.
