Microcos africana

What's the taxonomical classification of Microcos africana?

Microcos africana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the order Malvales and the family Malvaceae. Ultimately, its specific biological identity is defined by its placement within the genus Microcos, with the specific epithet africana denoting its species name.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Microcos africana contains bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, tannins, and essential oils that drive its therapeutic properties. These chemical constituents work synergistically to provide significant antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant effects. The presence of specific terpenoids and phenolic compounds allows the plant to combat various bacterial and fungal infections. Furthermore, the tannins within the leaves contribute to wound healing through their astringent qualities. Researchers continue to study these precise molecular structures to better understand their potential in modern pharmacology.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat malaria, intestinal worms, skin infections, stomach aches, and various inflammatory conditions. The leaves and roots are often prepared as decoctions or infusions to combat parasitic infections in the digestive tract. It is also frequently applied topically to soothe skin irritations and wounds. Traditional healers utilize its medicinal properties to manage fever and alleviate symptoms of digestive distress. These diverse applications demonstrate the plant's significant role in regional herbal medicine.
