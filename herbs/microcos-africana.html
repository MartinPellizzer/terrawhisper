Microcos africana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Microcos africana has an erect, herbaceous growth habit characterized by slender, branched stems and small, opposite, ovate leaves with entire margins. The foliage is typically bright green and possesses a smooth texture that lacks significant pubescence. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in terminal or axillary clusters. These inflorescences eventually develop into tiny, dry fruits that facilitate seed dispersal. The overall structure remains compact, making it a subtle component of its native African habitats.
