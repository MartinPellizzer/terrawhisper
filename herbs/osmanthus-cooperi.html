Osmanthus cooperi

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Osmanthus cooperi has elliptic to lanceolate leaves that are dark green, leathery, and possess finely serrated margins. These evergreen leaves grow in an opposite arrangement along woody stems that form a dense, spreading shrub. The plant produces small, tubular, fragrant white flowers that emerge from the axils of the foliage. Its fruit consists of small, fleshy drupes that transition from green to a dark color upon maturity. The overall structure of the plant is compact and maintains a lush appearance throughout the year.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the subtropical and tropical regions of southern China, specifically occurring in provinces such as Guangdong, Guangxi, Hainan, and Fujian. It thrives primarily in moist, shaded environments found within evergreen broad-leaved forests. Within these habitats, it is often situated in the understory where it can access filtered sunlight and consistent humidity.
