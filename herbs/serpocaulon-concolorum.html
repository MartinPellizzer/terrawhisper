What is Serpocaulon concolorum?

Serpocaulon concolorum is a species of fern in the family Polypodiaceae.

It is a terrestrial fern with rhizomes that are subterranean and adventitious, giving rise to pinnate fronds that are 30-40 cm long, consisting of 10-20 pairs of pinnae. The pinnae are lanceolate to linear, with entire margins and a cuneate base. The plant has a distinctive habit, with fronds often crowded and forming a dense rosette, and it is known to thrive in shaded and humid environments.

Serpocaulon concolorum is endemic to the tropical regions of Central and South America.
