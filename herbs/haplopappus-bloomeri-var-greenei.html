Haplopappus bloomeri var. greenei

Haplopappus bloomeri var. greenei, also known as Greene's rabbitbrush, is a subspecies of shrubby perennial plant that belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

Traditionally, this plant has been used for its medicinal properties, particularly in treating respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and coughs, due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties.

The health benefits of Greene's rabbitbrush include relieving congestion, reducing fever, and soothing sore throats.

The plant can be prepared as a tea or infusion for consumption, or used topically as a poultice or salve to aid in wound healing.
