Scutellaria sessilifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Scutellaria sessilifolia?

Scutelariia sessilifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls into the subclass Magnoliidae and is further categorized under the order Lamiales. Belonging to the family Lamiaceae, this plant is identified by its specific genus, Scutellaria, and its unique species name, sessiliifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Scutellaria sessilifolia has leaves that are sessile, meaning they lack a petiole and attach directly to the stem, typically appearing in opposite pairs. These leaves are generally small and lanceolate or elliptical in shape with entire margins. The plant exhibits a decumbent or ascending growth habit with slender, quadrangular stems characteristic of the Lamiaceae family. The foliage often presents a somewhat leathery texture and can vary in color from light green to a more muted grayish tone. Flowering structures emerge from the axils of these sessile leaves to form inconspicuous verticillasters.
