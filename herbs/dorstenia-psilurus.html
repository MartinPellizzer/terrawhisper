Dorstenia psilurus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dorstenia psilurus has a prostrate growth habit characterized by fleshy, creeping stems and relatively small, ovate to elliptical leaves that emerge from a central rhizome. Its most distinctive morphological feature is the specialized inflorescence, which consists of a flattened, disc-like receptacle known as a hypanthodium. This structure hosts minute, inconspicuous flowers embedded within the surface of the disc.
