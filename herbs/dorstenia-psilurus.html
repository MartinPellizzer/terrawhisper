Dorstenia psilurus

What's the taxonomical classification of Dorstenia psilurus?

Dorstenia psilurus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the taxonomic hierarchy, it is positioned in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized under the order Rosales and falls within the family Moraceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by the genus Dorstenia and the species psilurus.

    What are the herbal preparations of Dorstenia psilurus?

    Dorstenia psilurus is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste using the dried rhizomes or crushed fresh leaves. The decoction involves boiling the plant material in water to extract concentrated alkaloids for internal consumption. When used as an infusion, the leaves are steeped in hot water to create a milder tea. For external applications, the plant is ground into a fine paste to be applied directly to the skin for localized treatment. These various methods allow for the controlled release of the plant's bioactive compounds depending on the intended medicinal use.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Dorstenia psilurus can cause localized skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and mild gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Contact with the plant's sap may trigger redness, itching, or a burning sensation on the skin of sensitive individuals. Some people might experience swelling or rashes if the sap comes into contact with mucous membranes. If accidental ingestion occurs, it may lead to nausea or stomach discomfort. It is important to handle this species with caution to avoid these adverse reactions.
