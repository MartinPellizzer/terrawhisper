Dorstenia psilurus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dorstenia psilurus has a prostrate growth habit characterized by fleshy, creeping stems and relatively small, ovate to elliptical leaves that emerge from a central rhizome. The foliage typically displays a smooth texture with entire margins and can vary in color from light green to deeper shades depending on light exposure. Its most distinctive morphological feature is the specialized inflorescence, which consists of a flattened, disc-like receptacle known as a hypanthodium. This structure hosts minute, inconspicuous flowers embedded within the surface of the disc. The plant lacks traditional large petals, relying instead on these complex reproductive platforms to facilitate pollination.
