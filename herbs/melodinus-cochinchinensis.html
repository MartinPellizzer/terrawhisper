Melodinus cochinchinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Melodinus cochinchinensis?

Melodinus cochinchinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized under the order Gentianales. This plant is a member of the family Apocynaceae, specifically falling within the genus Melodinus, with its specific designation being the species cochinchinensis.

    What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

    This plant contains a diverse array of medicinal compounds including Aspidospermine- and quinolone-type alkaloids, melodicochiine A, and various monoterpenoid indole alkaloids. These specific chemical constituents are often studied for their potential pharmacological activities in treating different biological conditions. Researchers frequently utilize a total alkaloid extract to analyze the cumulative effects of these substances during laboratory testing. The presence of these complex molecules defines the unique phytochemical profile of the species. Such chemical diversity highlights the plant's significance in the field of natural product drug discovery.

    As discussed in a study, Melodinus cochinchinensis contains a total of 21 monoterpenoid indole alkaloids characterized by UHPLC-ESI-HRMS/MS. Aspidospermine- and quinolone-type alkaloids were identified as the major compounds within the total alkaloid extract. This extract significantly decreased the production of NO, IL-1β, IL-6 and TNF-α in LPS-induced RAW 264.7 macrophages. The substance also inhibited the phosphorylation of ERK1/2, JNK and p38 MAPK while improving PPAR-γ expression. In vivo experiments showed that pretreatment markedly alleviated xylene-induced ear edema and carrageenan-induced paw edema in mice.
