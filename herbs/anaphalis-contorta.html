Anaphalis contorta

What's the taxonomical classification of Anaphalis contorta?

Anaphalis contorta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. This plant is a representative of the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its complex flower heads. Within this family, it is situated in the genus Anaphalis, and its specific biological identity is defined by the species name contorta.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Anaphalis contorta contains bioactive phytochemicals including alkaloids, flavonoids, tannins, saponins, and steroids that contribute to its therapeutic properties. These secondary metabolites are primarily concentrated in the leaves and roots of the plant. Research suggests that the presence of these compounds provides significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities. In traditional medicine, extracts are often utilized to treat ailments such as skin infections, digestive issues, and fever. The specific chemical profile of the plant allows it to interact with biological pathways to mitigate oxidative stress.

What are the therapeutic actions of Anaphalis contorta?

Anaphalis contorta has potent anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties that are utilized to treat skin infections, wounds, and digestive ailments. The plant contains bioactive compounds such as flavonoids and tannins that help reduce swelling and neutralize harmful free radicals in the body. Its extracts are frequently applied topically to promote faster wound healing and combat bacterial growth on the skin. Additionally, traditional medicinal practices often involve using the plant to alleviate gastrointestinal discomfort and stomach pains. These diverse therapeutic actions make it a significant resource in ethnobotanical medicine for managing various inflammatory conditions.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, stomach aches, respiratory issues, and various inflammatory conditions.
