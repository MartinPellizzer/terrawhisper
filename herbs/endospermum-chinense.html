Endospermum chinense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Endospermum chinense has opposite, elliptical to ovate leaves that are typically 5 to 12 centimeters long with entire margins and acute tips. The stems are woody at the base and exhibit a somewhat quadrangular shape in younger growth. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in axillary cymes and are characterized by their yellowish-green color. The fruit is a small, globose drupe that transitions from green to a dark reddish or blackish hue upon maturity. These botanical features collectively distinguish the species within its specific family and habitat.

What are the therapeutic actions of Endospermum chinense?

Endospermum chinense has therapeutic properties that function as a bitter digestive stimulant, a diuretic, and a cholagogue. The plant contains bitter compounds that trigger the secretion of gastric juices to aid in the breakdown of food. These bitter principles also act as a cholagogue by promoting the flow of bile from the gallbladder into the small intestine. By increasing bile production and movement, the herb helps improve overall lipid digestion and liver function. Additionally, its diuretic action assists the body in eliminating excess fluids and metabolic waste through increased urination.
