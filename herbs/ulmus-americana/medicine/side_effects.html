1. Causes allergic reactions

Ulmus americana, also known as American Elm, causes allergic reactions due to its high content of urushiol, a potent allergen responsible for triggering immune responses in some individuals.

When exposed to the tree's sap, pollen, or bark, people may experience symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.

In severe cases, anaphylaxis can occur, requiring immediate medical attention.

2. Triggers respiratory problems

Ulmus americana, also known as American Elm, triggers respiratory problems due to its ability to release allergenic pollen into the air, causing allergic reactions in some individuals.

The plant's pollen can become airborne and be easily inhaled, irritating the lungs and airways, leading to symptoms such as coughing, sneezing, congestion, and wheezing.

3. Induces skin irritation

Ulmus americana, also known as American Elm, induces skin irritation due to its sap, which contains a high concentration of phenolic compounds and terpenoids.

These chemicals can cause an allergic reaction or contact dermatitis when the skin comes into direct contact with the tree's sap, bark, or leaves.

Additionally, the presence of these compounds in the tree's resin can also lead to skin irritation and discomfort.

4. Produces gastrointestinal issues

Ulmus americana, also known as American Elm, produces gastrointestinal issues due to its ability to interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes drugs.

Its tannins can also cause digestive discomfort, nausea, and stomach upset by binding to proteins in the gut and reducing nutrient absorption. Additionally, some individuals may experience allergic reactions, leading to symptoms like diarrhea or constipation.

The tree's extracts can also irritate the mucous membranes lining the gastrointestinal tract, causing inflammation and discomfort.

5. Lowers blood pressure

Ulmus americana, also known as American Elm, lowers blood pressure.

This effect is attributed to its ability to relax blood vessels and reduce peripheral resistance, allowing blood to flow more easily through the body. The flavonoids present in the plant, particularly quercetin and rutin, have been shown to possess vasodilatory properties, which contribute to this hypotensive effect.

Additionally, the plant's ability to inhibit the production of vasoconstricting compounds, such as angiotensin-converting enzyme, further contributes to its blood pressure-lowering action.

6. Interferes with medication

Ulmus americana, also known as American Elm, interferes with medication due to its ability to interact with certain prescription drugs.

The tree's bark and leaves contain compounds that can inhibit the body's metabolism of certain medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes medications, potentially leading to altered efficacy or increased risk of adverse reactions.

7. Causes dizziness

Ulmus americana, also known as American Elm, causes dizziness in some individuals.

This occurs due to the plant's ability to interact with certain medications, such as sedatives and antidepressants, which can alter brain chemistry and lead to dizziness or lightheadedness.

Additionally, the plant's high levels of coumarin, a natural compound, may also contribute to this side effect by affecting blood clotting and potentially causing dizziness in some people.

8. Increases heart rate

Ulmus americana, also known as American Elm, increases heart rate due to its ability to stimulate the cardiovascular system.

The plant's compounds, such as catechins and flavonoids, have been shown to enhance cardiac output by increasing blood pressure and contractility of the heart muscle. Additionally, Ulmus americana has been found to increase sympathetic nervous system activity, leading to increased heart rate and cardiac workload.

As a result, individuals using Ulmus americana may experience an elevated heart rate as a side effect.

9. Produces headaches

Ulmus americana, also known as American Elm, produces headaches due to its ability to interact with certain medications and alter their effects on the body.

The extract from this tree has been shown to inhibit the activity of enzymes responsible for breaking down certain drugs, leading to increased levels of these compounds in the blood.

This can cause a range of side effects, including headaches, which may vary in intensity and frequency depending on individual circumstances.

10. Weakens immune system

Ulmus americana, also known as American Elm, weakens immune system.

This is because it contains chemicals called tannins, which have been shown to suppress the activity of certain immune cells, such as natural killer cells and macrophages. Additionally, the bark of the tree has been found to contain a compound that can inhibit the production of interferon-gamma, an important protein involved in the immune response.

As a result, regular consumption of Ulmus americana products may lead to a decreased ability to fight off infections and diseases.
