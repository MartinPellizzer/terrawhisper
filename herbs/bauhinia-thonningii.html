Bauhinia thonningii

What's the taxonomical classification of Bauhinia thonningii?

Bauhinia thonningii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Fabales. As a member of the family Fabaceae, this plant is specifically identified within the genus Bauhinia, culminating in its unique scientific designation as the species thonningii.

What are the pharmacological activities of Bauhinia thonningii?

This plant has significant pharmacological potential due to its potent hyaluronidase inhibitory activity and protease inhibitory activity. These biochemical properties allow the extracts to interfere with enzymes that break down connective tissues and proteins in the body. By inhibiting hyaluronidase, the plant may help preserve the structural integrity of the extracellular matrix. Furthermore, the suppression of protease activity can assist in regulating inflammatory processes and tissue degradation. Such characteristics make Bauhinia thonningii a subject of interest for developing therapeutic agents against various pathological conditions.

As discussed in a study, the ethanol extract of Bauhinia thonningii showed IC50 values below 100µg/ml in either the hyaluroniidase or protease bioassay after the removal of polyphenols. This activity was observed even after polyphenols were removed in the search for specific enzyme inhibitors against hyaluroniidase, phospholipase A2 or proteases from extracts with IC50 values below 100µg/ml. The study tested a total of 226 extracts from 94 different plant species across three countries. Among these, forty plant species showed more than 90% inhibition in one or more assay.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating snakebite-induced tissue necrosis, inflammation, and various skin infections. The leaves are frequently prepared as a poultice to minimize the localized destruction of flesh caused by venomous bites. It also serves as a remedy for soothing swelling and reducing painful inflammation throughout the body. Additionally, its medicinal properties are applied to help heal wounds and manage dermatological issues. These traditional applications highlight the plant's significant role in managing acute traumatic injuries.

As discussed in a study, the ethanol extract of Bauhinia thonningii demonstrated inhibitory activity against necrotic enzymes. This specific plant extract showed IC50 values below 100µg/ml in either the hyaluroniidase or protease bioassay. These results remained consistent even after the removal of polyphenols from the extracts. This indicates the presence of specific enzyme inhibitors within the Bauhinia thonningii ethanol extract.
