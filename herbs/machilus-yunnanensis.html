Machilus yunnanensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Machilus yunnanensis has evergreen leaves that are typically elliptical or lanceolate in shape, measuring up to 15 centimeters in length, with a glossy upper surface and a slightly paler underside. The tree features a smooth, grayish-brown bark and a spreading crown that forms a dense canopy. Its young twigs are often covered in fine, soft hairs that provide a velvety texture to the new growth. The flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually appearing in clusters along the branches during the flowering season. Developing fruit consists of small, ovoid drupes that transition in color as they reach maturity.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the subtropical and temperate mountain regions of southwestern China, specifically within the Yunnan, Sichuan, Guizhou, and Guangxi provinces. It thrives primarily in moist, shaded environments found at middle to high elevations. The species is commonly located in broadleaf evergreen and deciduous forests where it occupies diverse ecological niches. Its range is largely defined by the complex topography and varying precipitation patterns of the Hengduan Mountains and surrounding highlands. Localized populations are often restricted to specific altitudinal belts that provide the necessary humidity and temperature for its growth.
