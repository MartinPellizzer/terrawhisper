What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Salsola abrotanoides has succulent, cylindrical to subcylindrical stems that are often branched and possess a light green to glaucous color. The leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures that are densely arranged along the stem surfaces. These minute leaves are typically fleshy and serve to minimize water loss in arid environments. The plant develops small, inconspicuous flowers that are often tucked between the leaf bases. Its overall growth habit is typically shrubby, forming compact mounds that can adapt to saline soils.
