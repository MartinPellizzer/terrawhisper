Ormocarpum cochinchinense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ormocarpum cochinchiense has a woody, perennial shrubby habit characterized by pinnately compound leaves and small, papilionaceous flowers arranged in axillary racemes. The leaflets are typically small, numerous, and arranged in pairs along a central rachis. The stems often exhibit a slightly woody texture with fine pubescence on the younger shoots. Its flowers are pale yellow or cream in color and feature the classic structure of the Fabaceae family. The fruit is a small, indehiscent pod that typically contains a single seed.
