Periwinkle

Vinca rosea

Periwinkle (Vinca rosea) is a flowering plant native to tropical regions, known for its striking blue or violet flowers and its long history of use in traditional medicine. It is primarily used to treat conditions such as leukemia and other cancers due to its active compounds that inhibit the growth of cancer cells.

Botanical Identification

Vinca rosea, commonly known as Periwinkle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rubiaceae. It is a perennial shrub native to Europe, Asia, and Africa. The plant features glossy evergreen leaves and clusters of blue-violet flowers, with tuberous roots used in traditional herbal medicine. Synonyms include Myrothamnus and Catharanthus in historical texts.

Active Compounds

These compounds work together to exert their therapeutic effects, with alkaloids playing a central role in its medicinal properties. The presence of flavonoids and glycosides enhances the herb's overall efficacy and contributes to its use in herbal medicine.

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for treating common ailments like colds and fevers.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory conditions.

Extract: Used for managing hypertension and cardiovascular conditions.

Safety Profile

Periwinkle (Vinca rosea) contains alkaloids that can affect the heart and blood pressure. It is contraindicated in individuals with hypertension, heart conditions, or those taking anticoagulants. Use under medical supervision is essential to avoid adverse effects.
