Periwinkle (Vinca rosea): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

Periwinkle (Vinca rosea) is a flowering plant native to tropical regions, known for its striking blue or violet flowers and its long history of use in traditional medicine. What is Periwinkle (Vinca Rosea)?

Periwinkle (Vinca rosea) is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia, commonly used in herbal medicine for its alkaloid-rich properties.

Botanical Identity and Classification

Periwinkle, or Vinca rosea, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Apocynaceae, characterized by its evergreen foliage, blue-violet flowers, and the presence of alkaloids in its roots and leaves.

Common Names and Synonyms

Periwinkle (Vinca rosea) is commonly known by other names such as Atlantic periwinkle, Cape periwinkle, and the scientific name Vinca rosea, which is widely recognized in botanical and herbal medicine contexts.

Plant Description and Morphology

Periwinkle (Vinca rosea) is a low-growing evergreen shrub characterized by its trailing stems, oval-shaped dark green leaves with a glossy surface, and clusters of spurred, pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring and summer.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Periwinkle (Vinca rosea) is native to the Mediterranean region, western Asia, and parts of Africa, with a natural range extending from southern Europe through the Middle East to North Africa.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The leaves, flowers, and roots of Periwinkle (Vinca rosea) are primarily used medicinally, with the roots being the most commonly utilized part for traditional and modern herbal applications.

Phytochemical Composition

Periwinkle (Vinca rosea) contains a variety of bioactive compounds, including alkaloids such as vincamine, vinblastine, and vincristine, which are responsible for its medicinal properties and traditional use in herbal medicine.

Pharmacological Properties

Vinca rosea contains alkaloids such as vincamine and vincaminine, which exhibit cerebral circulatory stimulant effects, enhance memory, and demonstrate antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

Mechanisms of Action

The mechanisms of action of Periwinkle (Vinca rosea) involve the alkaloids vincamine and vinca alkaloids, which enhance cerebral circulation, improve memory, and inhibit cell division by interfering with microtubule formation.

Therapeutic Uses and Indications

Periwinkle is used to treat conditions such as leukemia, Hodgkin's lymphoma, breast cancer, skin cancer, diabetes, high blood pressure, and venereal diseases.

Preparation Methods and Forms

Periwinkle (Vinca rosea) is typically prepared as a dried herb, used in tinctures, teas, or extracts, with the most common form being a standardized aqueous or alcoholic extract containing active alkaloids such as vincamine and vincaminine.

Dosage and Administration

The typical dosage of Periwinkle (Vinca rosea) for herbal use ranges from 100 to 400 milligrams per day, usually divided into two or three doses, and should be taken with food to minimize gastrointestinal discomfort.

Safety, Side Effects, and Contraindications

While Periwinkle (Vinca rosea) is generally considered safe when used appropriately, it may cause side effects such as gastrointestinal discomfort, dizziness, and skin irritation, particularly with prolonged use or high dosages.

Drug Interactions

Periwinkle (Vinca rosea) may interact with medications that affect the heart, blood pressure, or blood clotting, potentially increasing the risk of adverse effects such as arrhythmias, hypotension, or bleeding.

Toxicity and Precautions

Periwinkle (Vinca rosea) may cause toxicity if ingested in large amounts, with potential side effects including gastrointestinal upset, dizziness, and in severe cases, cardiac complications, so it is important to consult a healthcare professional before use, especially during pregnancy or breastfeeding.

Cultivation and Harvesting

Periwinkle (Vinca rosea) is typically cultivated in well-drained soil with partial to full shade, requiring consistent moisture and moderate temperatures for optimal growth.

Processing and Extraction

Periwinkle (Vinca rosea) is processed into various forms such as extracts, tinctures, and powdered supplements, typically through methods that preserve its active alkaloids like vincamine and vinca alkaloids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Storage and Shelf Life

Periwinkle (Vinca rosea) should be stored in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture, and its shelf life is typically up to two years when properly preserved.

Scientific Research and Evidence

Scientific research on Periwinkle (Vinca rosea) has primarily focused on its alkaloid content, particularly vincamine and vincorine, which have been studied for their potential cognitive-enhancing and anti-cancer properties.

Traditional and Historical Uses

Periwinkle (Vinca rosea) has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported ability to treat wounds, inflammation, and even as a remedy for various ailments, with historical records dating back to ancient times in Europe and Asia.

Regulatory Status
