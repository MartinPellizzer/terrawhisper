What to know about Vinca rosea (periwinkle) before using it medicinally

Vinca rosea, commonly known as periwinkle, is a herb that has been prized for its numerous health-promoting properties, which include its ability to improve circulation, reduce inflammation, and boost cognitive function.

From a horticultural standpoint, periwinkle is a low-maintenance, evergreen shrub that thrives in shady environments and can be grown in containers or directly in the ground. Botanically, Vinca rosea is a member of the dogbane family and is characterized by its bright blue or purple flowers and dark green, leathery leaves.

In ancient civilizations, periwinkle was revered for its medicinal and spiritual properties, with references to its use dating back to ancient Egypt, Greece, and Rome.

This article explains the medicinal, horticultural, botanical, and historical aspects of Vinca rosea.

What are the medicinal properties of Vinca rosea?

Vinca rosea helps with various health conditions, including cancer, glaucoma, and epilepsy, due to its antitumor, anti-inflammatory, and anticonvulsant properties. It is also used for its diuretic and vasodilatory effects. The plant has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

The medicinal properties of Vinca rosea are attributed to its active constituents, including vincristine, vinblastine, and leucrosidine, which are alkaloids that have been isolated and used in chemotherapy to treat various types of cancer, including leukemia, lymphoma, and Hodgkin's disease.

The parts of the plant that are most commonly used for medicinal purposes are the leaves and roots, which contain high concentrations of the active alkaloids. The leaves are typically harvested, dried, and processed into capsules or tablets, while the roots are often used to make infusions or decoctions.

Improper use of Vinca rosea can lead to side effects such as peripheral neuropathy, myelosuppression, and alopecia. Additionally, the plant can interact with other medications, including those used to treat high blood pressure, diabetes, and certain psychiatric conditions.

To use Vinca rosea medicinally, it is essential to follow strict guidelines, including consulting with a healthcare professional, monitoring blood counts, and reporting any adverse reactions. The plant should be used under strict medical supervision, and patients should be warned of the potential risks and side effects associated with its use.

What are the horticulural aspects of Vinca rosea?

Vinca rosea grow well in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. This plant requires a slightly acidic to neutral soil pH, ranging from 5.5 to 7.0. It can tolerate a wide range of temperatures, from 60°F to 80°F.

For optimal growth, plant Vinca rosea in the fall or early spring, when the weather is cooler. Plant the tubers 2-3 inches deep and 12-18 inches apart. Water thoroughly after planting and keep the soil consistently moist during the first few weeks.

To harvest Vinca rosea, wait until the flowers have faded, and the seed pods have formed. Cut off the seed heads, leaving a few inches of stem attached to the plant. Dry the seed heads in a cool, dry place, or separate the seeds from the pods and store them in an airtight container.

Common pests of Vinca rosea include aphids, whiteflies, and slugs. Common diseases include root rot, leaf spot, and powdery mildew, often caused by overwatering and poor air circulation. Regularly inspect the plant for signs of pests or diseases, and take action promptly to prevent the problem from spreading.

What are the botanical characteristics of Vinca rosea?

Botanically, it is characterized by its evergreen, glossy leaves that are lance-shaped and measure 3-4 inches (7-10 cm) in length. The leaves are arranged oppositely on the stem and have an acute tip, with a waxy texture that helps to conserve water.

The plant produces clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that vary in color from white to pink, purple, and bi-colored, each measuring around 1-2 inches (2.5-5 cm) in diameter. The flowers are fragrant and have five petals with a distinct lip-like structure at the base. The plant grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet (3 meters), with a spread of about 6 feet (1.8 meters).

Its stems are woody, branching, and have a smooth texture.

What are the variants of Vinca rosea?

Vinca rosea has variants, such as Vinca rosea 'Alba', which is a pure white variant with large flowers and a more compact growth habit.

Another popular variant is Vinca rosea 'Catharina', also known as "Periwinkle Vine", which has slightly larger leaves and fragrant flowers. What is the geographic distribution of Vinca rosea?

Vinca rosea, also known as periwinkle or rose-periwinkle, is native to Madagascar and Southeastern Africa.

However, it has been widely naturalized and cultivated throughout tropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas, including the Caribbean, Central America, South America, India, Sri Lanka, and Southeast Asia.

It thrives in warm, humid climates with mild winters and can be found growing wild or as an ornamental plant in gardens and along roadsides.
