Zanthoxylum nitidum

What's the taxonomical classification of Zanthoxylum nitidum?

Zanthoxylum nitidum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Sapindales. Further taxonomic classification places it in the family Rutaceae, where it is categorized under the genus Zanthoxylum, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species nitidum.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds nitidine chloride, chelerythrine, magnoflorine chloride, liriodenine, and nitidumalkaloid A. These specific alkaloids are recognized for their diverse biological activities and pharmacological potential. Researchers often study these substances to understand their effects on various cellular processes and disease models. The presence of such complex chemical structures makes the plant a significant subject in phytochemical investigations. Understanding the concentrations of these compounds helps in evaluating the plant's overall therapeutic value.

As discussed in a study, the roots of Zanthoxylum nitidum contain the pharmacologically active alkaloid nitidine chloride (NC). This compound significantly inhibits CYP1A1- and 1B1-catalyzed ethoxyresorufin O-deethylation activity with IC50 values of 0.28 ± 0.06 μM and 0.32 ± 0.02 μM, respectively. Nitidine chloride shows only slight inhibition of CYP1A2 activity with an IC50 > 50 μM. Kinetic analysis showed NC competitively inhibited CYP1B1 with a Ki value of 0.47 ± 0.05 μM. Additionally, NC caused a mixed type of inhibition on CYP1A1 with Ki and KI values of 0.14 ± 0.04 and 0.19 ± 0.09 μM, respectively.
