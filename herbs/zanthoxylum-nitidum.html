Zanthoxylum nitidum

What's the taxonomical classification of Zanthoxylum nitidum?

Zanthoxylum nitidum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Sapindales. Further taxonomic classification places it in the family Rutaceae, where it is categorized under the genus Zanthoxylum, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species nitidum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Zanthoxylum nitidum has a woody structure characterized by a medium-sized tree habit, pinnately compound leaves, and small, inconspicuous flowers. The leaves consist of multiple pairs of leaflets that are typically elliptic to lanceolate in shape with serrated margins. Its bark is often textured and provides protection for the internal vascular tissues of the trunk. The plant produces small fruits that are characteristic of the Rutaceae family, often appearing as fleshy or capsule-like structures. These morphological features collectively define its physical presence within its native tropical habitats.

What are the pharmacological activities of Zanthoxylum nitidum?

This plant has potent pharmacological activities including analgesia, anti-inflammation, antibacterial, anti-cancer, and antioxidant properties. Its bioactive compounds work to reduce pain sensations and suppress inflammatory responses in various biological models. The plant also demonstrates significant ability to inhibit the growth of harmful bacteria and neutralize free radicals through its antioxidant mechanisms. Furthermore, research indicates that its chemical constituents possess anti-cancer potential by targeting malignant cell proliferation. These diverse medicinal qualities make it a valuable subject for pharmaceutical development and natural product research.

According to a study published by "Zhong yao cai = Zhongyaocai = Journal of Chinese medicinal materials", the root and stem of Zanthoxylum nitidum both demonstrate pharmacological activities including anti-contusion injury, analgesia, and anti-inflammation. Both parts of the plant relieved the exterior and histological symptoms of rats' injury legs that were struck by hammer. Regarding analgesic effects, Zanthoxylum nitidum decreased the numbers of mice's writhing and enhanced the pain threshold of mice on a hot plate. The plant also inhibited the edema of rats induced by carrageenan and suppressed the granuloma of rats induced by cotton pellets. Ultimately, the stem of Zanthoxylum nitidum showed similar effects in anti-contusion injury, analgesia, and anti-inflammation compared to the root.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds nitidumalkaloid A, nitidumalkaloid B, nitidumalkaloid C, chelerythrine, and nitidine chloride. These specific alkaloids are primary bioactive constituents identified within the chemical profile of Zanthoxylum nitidum. Researchers study these substances to understand their potential pharmacological properties and biological activities. The presence of these complex molecules contributes to the plant's overall medicinal significance in traditional practices. Detailed chemical analysis helps isolate each compound for further therapeutic investigation.

According to a study published by "Drug metabolism and disposition: the biological fate of chemicals", the roots of Zanthoxylum nitidum (Roxb.) DC contain the pharmacologically active alkaloid nitidine chloride (NC), which is a polyaromatic hydrocarbon. This compound significantly inhibited CYP1A1- and 1B1-catalyzed ethoxyresorufin O-deethylation activity with IC50 values of 0.28 ± 0.06 μM and 0.32 ± 0.02 μM, respectively. Nitidine chloride showed only slight inhibition of CYP1A2 activity with an IC50 > 50 μM. Kinetic analysis showed NC competitively inhibited CYP1B1 with a Ki value of 0.47 ± 0.05 μM. Additionally, NC acted as a mixed type inhibitor on CYP1A1 with Ki and KI values of 0.14 ± 0.04 and 0.19 ± 0.09 μM, respectively.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating cancer, gastric cancer, gastric ulcer, gastritis, and various types of tumors. Its medicinal properties are frequently applied to manage digestive issues like gastritis and painful gastric ulcers. Researchers also investigate its potential efficacy in combating malignant growths and cellular tumors. Furthermore, specific studies focus on its ability to inhibit the progression of gastric cancer cells. These therapeutic applications highlight the plant's significant role in traditional and experimental medicine.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", Zanthoxylum nitidum is used in traditional Chinese medicine for treating stomach ache, toothache, rheumatic arthralgia, traumatic injury, and venomous snake bites. Additional medical applications for the plant include the treatment of inflammations, various types of cancer, bacterial and viral infections, gastric and oral ulcers, and liver damage. A comprehensive review was carried out on studies about Zanthoxylum nitidum conducted in the past 60 years. At present, more than 150 chemical constituents have been separated and identified from the plant. Biological activities such as anti-inflammation, analgesia, haemostasis, anticancer, and antibacterial have been determined through in vitro and in vivo studies.
