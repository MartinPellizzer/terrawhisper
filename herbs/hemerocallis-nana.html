What's the taxonomical classification of Hemerocallis nana?

Hemerocallis nana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Asparagales and is a member of the family Asphodelaceae. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Hemerocallis, with its specific designation being the species nana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hemerocallis nana has narrow, linear, grass-like leaves that emerge from a cluster of fleshy, tuberous roots. The plant produces upright, leafless flowering stalks known as scapes that rise significantly above the foliage. Each scape terminates in a cluster of funnel-shaped flowers characterized by delicate, spreading petals and a central throat. The foliage remains relatively compact compared to other daylily species, reflecting its dwarf growth habit.
