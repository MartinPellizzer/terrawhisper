10 Health Benefits Of Marrubium Peregrinum (Deadnettle)

Marrubium peregrinum has health benefits such as soothing digestive issues, reducing inflammation, and exhibiting antimicrobial properties.

Its bitter compounds like sesquiterpenoids and flavonoids contribute to these medicinal properties, which can alleviate symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome (IBS) and reduce the risk of infections.

For instance, a cup of deadnettle tea may help alleviate menstrual cramps in women, while its topical application may soothe skin irritations, allowing individuals to enjoy improved quality of life without relying on harsh chemicals.

1. Relieves respiratory congestion

Marrubium peregrinum relieves respiratory congestion because of its ability to loosen and clear mucus from the lungs and airways.

The plant's leaves and flowers contain mucilage, a thick, gel-like substance that helps to break down and remove excess phlegm, reducing congestion and making it easier to breathe.

Additionally, deadnettle has anti-inflammatory properties that help to soothe and calm irritated tissues in the respiratory tract, further alleviating congestion and promoting healthy breathing.

2. Soothes sore throats

Marrubium peregrinum soothes sore throats because of its unique ability to reduce inflammation and ease discomfort.

The herb contains compounds like rosmarinic acid and flavonoids that have potent anti-inflammatory properties, which help to calm the mucous membranes in the throat and alleviate pain.

Additionally, deadnettle's astringent qualities help to dry up excess mucus, further reducing swelling and relieving congestion.

3. Eases coughs and colds

Marrubium peregrinum eases coughs and colds because of its unique combination of compounds.

The plant contains iridoid glycosides, such as marrubenol and ajuganetin, which have been shown to possess anti-inflammatory properties that help to reduce swelling in the airways and alleviate congestion.

Additionally, deadnettle's expectorant properties help to loosen and clear excess mucus from the lungs, providing relief from coughs and congestion associated with colds and flu.

4. Acts as an expectorant

Marrubium peregrinum acts as an expectorant because it contains a unique combination of iridoid glycosides and triterpenoids that stimulate the respiratory tract and promote the clearance of mucus from the lungs.

These compounds increase the production of mucolytic enzymes, which break down mucus, making it easier to cough up and expel, providing relief from congestion and bronchitis symptoms.

5. Reduces inflammation

Marrubium peregrinum reduces inflammation because of its rich concentration of bioactive compounds, particularly flavonoids and phenolic acids.

These potent antioxidants scavenge free radicals, thereby preventing oxidative stress and inflammation in the body.

Additionally, deadnettle's anti-inflammatory properties have been attributed to its ability to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines and enzymes, which can contribute to chronic inflammatory diseases.

6. Alleviates digestive issues

Marrubium peregrinum alleviates digestive issues because of its ability to soothe and calm the gut.

The herb's anti-inflammatory properties help reduce inflammation in the digestive tract, which can alleviate symptoms such as bloating, cramps, and diarrhea.

Its bitter compounds also stimulate digestive secretions, improving digestion and absorption of nutrients, while its antispasmodic effects can help relax the muscles in the intestinal tract, reducing spasms and discomfort.

7. Stimulates appetite

Marrubium peregrinum stimulates appetite because it contains iridoids and sesquiterpenes, which have been shown to increase the release of gastrin, a hormone that stimulates gastric acid secretion and improves gut motility.

This enhances digestion and absorption of nutrients, leading to increased hunger and improved food intake.

Additionally, deadnettle has been traditionally used as an appetite stimulant in folk medicine, particularly for people with poor appetite due to illness or malnutrition.

8. Promotes digestion

Marrubium peregrinum promotes digestion because it contains a wealth of bioactive compounds, including flavonoids and saponins.

These molecules have been shown to exhibit anti-inflammatory properties, which can help alleviate symptoms associated with irritable bowel syndrome (IBS) and other gastrointestinal disorders.

By reducing inflammation in the digestive tract, deadnettle may also improve nutrient absorption and overall gut health, supporting a healthy and balanced digestive system.

9. Acts as a mild laxative

Marrubium peregrinum acts as a mild laxative because it contains iridoid glycosides, particularly ajugoside and nepetrilic acid.

These compounds have been shown to stimulate the intestines and increase peristalsis, thereby enhancing bowel movements and promoting regularity.

Additionally, the herb's bitter properties can help increase saliva production and gut motility, further contributing to its laxative effects without causing irritation or dependence.

10. Reduces fevers

Marrubium peregrinum reduces fevers because it contains a compound called rosmarinic acid.

This acid has been shown to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help to reduce the body's natural response to infection, thereby lowering fever.

Additionally, deadnettle's flavonoids and phenolic acids may also contribute to its antipyretic effects, making it a valuable herb in traditional medicine for centuries.
