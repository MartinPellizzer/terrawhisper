10 Medicinal Constituents Of Marrubium Peregrinum (Deadnettle)

Marrubium peregrinum has active constituents such as iridoid glycosides, flavonoids, and essential oils.

These constituents possess anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties. The iridoid glycosides, specifically marrubiin, have been shown to reduce inflammation and alleviate symptoms of digestive disorders.

This can improve people's lives by providing relief from gastrointestinal issues, such as irritable bowel syndrome, and promoting overall gut health.

Marrubium peregrinum lamalbidiol is a bioactive compound extracted from its leaves.

It has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health issues, such as fever, cough, and gastrointestinal disorders. Lamalbidiol exhibits potent anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties, making it effective against microbial infections and reducing inflammation.

2. Marubenine

Marrubium peregrinum marubenine is a perennial herb native to Europe and Western Asia.
