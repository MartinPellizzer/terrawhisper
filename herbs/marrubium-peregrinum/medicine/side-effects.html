10 Possible Side Effects Of Marrubium Peregrinum (Deadnettle)

Marrubium peregrinum has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal irritation, allergic reactions, and interactions with other medications.

The causes of these side effects often include excessive dosing, individual tolerance, or pre-existing health conditions.

These side effects can worsen daily life, causing digestive discomfort, skin rashes, and even exacerbating conditions like diabetes or blood pressure.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Marrubium peregrinum if used imporperly.

1. Triggers seizures rarely

Marrubium peregrinum causes allergic reactions.

This is often due to the presence of volatile oils and alkaloids in its leaves, stems, and flowers. These compounds can trigger an immune response in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as skin irritation, itching, and redness. In more severe cases, anaphylaxis may occur.

The exact mechanisms by which Marrubium peregrinum elicits this reaction are not fully understood, but it is thought to be related to its ability to stimulate the release of histamine from mast cells.
