Botanical Identification of Peregrine Marrubium (Marrubium peregrinum)

To identify Peregrine Marrubium (Marrubium peregrinum) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Peregrine Marrubium, Marrubium peregrinum, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Fabales, order Fabales, family Fabaceae, genus Marrubium, and species Marrubium peregrinum. It is a member of the legume family, known for its medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal medicine.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Marrubium peregrinum is the scientific name of Peregrine Marrubium. It was first described by the botanist Carl von Linné. No synonyms are currently recognized for this species. It is also known by various regional and historical names, reflecting its traditional use in herbal medicine.

Morphological Characteristics

Peregrine Marrubium is a perennial herb with a robust root system that anchors it firmly in its habitat. Its stems are erect and branched, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. The leaves are lanceolate, hairy, and have a distinct grayish-green color. The inflorescence is a dense spike, bearing small blue to purple flowers. The fruit is a capsule containing numerous tiny seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Marrubium peregrinum can be distinguished by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves with wavy margins and its dense, spike-like inflorescence. It has a distinct aromatic scent when crushed, and its flowers are typically pink to purple. It differs from similar species like Marrubium vulgare by its leaf shape and more compact growth habit.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Peregrine Marrubium is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It thrives in temperate climates with mild summers and cool winters. It prefers well-drained, alkaline soils. It is commonly found at altitudes between 500 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Peregrine Marrubium blooms from late spring to early summer, typically between May and June. Seed formation occurs in late summer, around August. For identification purposes, the plant is best harvested in early spring before flowering, when the leaves are most distinct and characteristic.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Marrubium peregrinum is most closely related to other members of the genus Marrubium. It shares key characteristics with species like Marrubium vulgare, such as leaf structure and flowering patterns. Within the Boraginaceae family, it exhibits typical traits like hairy stems and blue or purple flowers. These relationships aid in accurate identification and understanding its medicinal properties.
