Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Pyrethrum is Anthemis nobilis, first described by Linnaeus in 1753. It is also known by several synonyms, including Pyrethrum officinale and Chrysanthemum nobilis. Common names such as "pyrethrum flower" and "chrysanthemum" are widely used. Regional and historical names reflect its traditional use in herbal medicine across various cultures.

Morphological Characteristics

Pyrethrum (Anthemis nobilis) is an annual herb with a taproot system. It has erect, branching stems that are hairy and somewhat succulent. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, with serrated margins and a hairy surface. The inflorescence is a flat-topped cluster of yellow flowers, each with a central disc and ray florets. The fruit is a capsule containing numerous small, dark seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Pyrethrum (Anthemis nobilis) can be distinguished by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and bright yellow composite flowers. It differs from similar species like Chamomile by its more elongated leaves and the presence of a distinct, pungent aroma. The plant also has a characteristic hollow stem and produces small, pale brown seeds. These features help in accurate identification during herbal studies.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Pyrethrum (Anthemis nobilis) is native to the Mediterranean region, including parts of southern Europe, North Africa, and western Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with mild, wet winters and warm, dry summers. The plant prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils and typically grows at altitudes between 300 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Pyrethrum (Anthemis nobilis) blooms from late spring to early autumn, typically between May and September, depending on climate. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early autumn, around August to September. For identification purposes, the best time to harvest is when the flower heads are fully open and the seeds are beginning to develop.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Pyrethrum (Anthemis nobilis) is closely related to species within the Anthemis genus, which belongs to the Asteraceae family. It shares key family traits such as composite flower heads and alternate leaf arrangement. Its closest relatives include other Anthemis species, which are often found in similar habitats and have similar chemical compositions.
