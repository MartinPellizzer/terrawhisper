Pyrethrum (Anthemis nobilis) Compounds

The primary compounds of Pyrethrum include essential oils, which are the main insecticidal agents, and flavonoids, which contribute to its antioxidant properties. Terpenoids and phenolic acids also play roles in its medicinal effects, while coumarin and tannins add to its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic qualities.

Pyrethrum (Anthemis nobilis) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Pyrethrum (Anthemis nobilis) contains pyrethrins, which are potent insecticides. These compounds act on the nervous system of insects, causing paralysis and death. They are widely used in natural pest control and are considered safe for humans and mammals.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Pyrethrum (Anthemis nobilis). They contribute to the plant's therapeutic properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. These compounds support the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for treating skin conditions and respiratory ailments.

3. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Pyrethrum (Anthemis nobilis). They contribute to the plant's insecticidal properties by disrupting the nervous systems of insects. Terpenoids like pyrethrins and chrysanthemins are key to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for pest control.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Pyrethrum (Anthemis nobilis). It contributes to the plant's insecticidal properties by disrupting the nervous system of pests. This compound is also associated with anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects in herbal medicine.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a natural compound found in Pyrethrum (Anthemis nobilis). It contributes to the plant's insecticidal properties by affecting the nervous system of pests. Coumarin also has mild anticoagulant effects and is used in herbal medicine for its calming and sedative qualities.

6. Tannin

Tannin is a key component of Pyrethrum (Anthemis nobilis) and contributes to its astringent properties. It helps in reducing inflammation and can aid in digestive health by strengthening mucous membranes. Tannins in Pyrethrum are also used in traditional herbal medicine for their antimicrobial effects.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Pyrethrum (Anthemis nobilis). It contributes to the plant's insecticidal properties by disrupting the nervous system of pests. Glycosides are often extracted and used in natural pesticides. Their presence highlights the plant's role in herbal medicine for pest control.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Pyrethrum (Anthemis nobilis). It contributes to the plant's insecticidal properties by disrupting the cell membranes of pests. While not as potent as pyrethrins, saponin supports the plant's overall efficacy in herbal pest control. It is commonly used in natural insecticides and herbal formulations.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Pyrethrum (Anthemis nobilis). It contributes to the plant's insecticidal properties by affecting the nervous system of pests. While less abundant than pyrethrins, alkaloids enhance the overall efficacy of the plant's natural defense mechanisms.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid compound found in Pyrethrum (Anthemis nobilis). It contributes to the plant's vibrant flower color and has antioxidant properties. While not the primary insecticidal component, anthocyanin supports the plant's overall health and may enhance the bioavailability of other active compounds in herbal preparations.
