Turpinia arguta

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Turpinia arguta has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical to oblong in shape with entire margins and a coriaceous texture. The leaf blades typically measure between five and twelve centimeters in length and possess a distinctively shiny upper surface. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in terminal panicles, often appearing in clusters throughout the canopy. These flowers eventually develop into small, fleshy drupes that change color as they ripen. The woody stems exhibit a somewhat rough bark texture that characterizes the shrub or small tree growth habit.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, specifically ranging from Mexico through the Caribbean to parts of Brazil.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Turpinia arguta can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested improperly. The plant contains various secondary metabolites that may trigger sensitivity in individuals with delicate skin. Some reports suggest that direct contact with the sap or leaves can result in redness, itching, or localized swelling. If consumed, the chemical constituents might lead to nausea or stomach discomfort. Users should exercise caution and avoid direct contact to minimize these potential adverse reactions.
