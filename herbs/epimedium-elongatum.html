What's the taxonomical classification of Epimedium elongatum?

Epimedium elongatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Ranunculales. This specific plant is a representative of the family Berberidaceae, falling under the genus Epimedium, and is identified by its unique species designation, elongatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Epimedium elongatum has heart-shaped to ovate leaves that are characterized by an elongated shape and prominent, slightly recessed venation. The foliage typically grows in basal clumps, forming a dense groundcover with textures that vary from smooth to slightly serrated edges. Its flowering stems are slender and rise above the leaves, bearing small, delicate blossoms that often feature distinct nectar spurs. The plant develops a woody rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread slowly through the soil.
