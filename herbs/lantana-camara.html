Lantana camara

What's the taxonomical classification of Lantana camara?

Lantana camara belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the evolutionary hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further into its specific lineage, the plant falls under the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Verbenaceae. Finally, its taxonomic identity is completed by its genus, Lantana, and its specific epithet, camara.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Lantana camara contains bioactive compounds including triterpenoids, flavonoids, phenolics, and alkaloids that exhibit various pharmacological activities. These secondary metabolites contribute to the plant's potent anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties. Specific studies have highlighted the presence of lantadene A and B, which are significant triterpene lactones found within the leaves. Researchers also investigate its potential for treating wounds and skin infections due to its ability to inhibit certain bacterial growth. Furthermore, certain extracts show promise in managing oxidative stress and protecting cells from damage.

What are the therapeutic actions of Lantana camara?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including skin infections, intestinal worms, digestive issues, and respiratory conditions. In traditional medicine, the leaves are often applied topically to manage wounds or fungal infections. Some cultures also prepare decoctions from the plant to address gastrointestinal distress or fever. However, it is crucial to note that many parts of the plant can be toxic if consumed improperly. Users should exercise extreme caution due to the potential for poisoning.
