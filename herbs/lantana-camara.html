Lantana camara

What's the taxonomical classification of Lantana camara?

Lantana camara belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the evolutionary hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further into its specific lineage, the plant falls under the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Verbenaceae. Finally, its taxonomic identity is completed by its genus, Lantana, and its specific epithet, camara.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lantana camara has woody, perennial shrubs that typically reach heights of one to three meters and feature square-shaped stems covered in fine, bristly hairs. The leaves are arranged oppositely along the branches and possess an ovate shape with serrated margins and a rough, sandpaper-like texture. Small, tubular flowers grow in dense, rounded clusters known as umbels, displaying a wide range of colors including yellow, orange, and red. These inflorescences are often surrounded by small green bracts that provide structural support to the blooming heads. The plant also produces small, fleshy drupes that transition from green to a dark purple or black color when fully ripe.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to a broad range spanning Central and South America, the Caribbean, and parts of Africa and Australia. While it originated in tropical regions, it has successfully naturalized in many other subtropical and temperate areas across the globe. Due to its high adaptability, it is now found extensively in many parts of Asia and Oceania. The species thrives in diverse habitats, ranging from disturbed forest edges to open grasslands. Its ability to spread rapidly has allowed it to colonize various continents where it often becomes an invasive species.

What are the pharmacological activities of Lantana camara?

This plant has antioxidant, antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, anti-cancer, and antiprototozoaal pharmacological activities driven by its diverse secondary metabolites. Its antioxidant properties help neutralize free radicals, thereby reducing oxidative stress within biological systems. The plant also demonstrates significant antimicrobial and antiprototozoaal capabilities by inhibiting the growth of various bacteria and protozoan parasites. Additionally, its anti-inflammatory effects can help mitigate swelling and pain by modulating immune responses. Research into its anti-cancer potential suggests that certain extracts may interfere with the proliferation of malignant cells.

As discussed in a study, the essential oils obtained from Lantana camara flowers were evaluated for their antioxidant and anticancer activities. Hydrodistillation of white, pink, and orange flowers yielded EO1, EO2, and EO3 respectively. All essential oils showed notable antioxidant potential through DPPH scavenging assays and FRAP assays. The most eminent oil, EO1, revealed a selective dose-dependent antiproliferative effect on two distinct breast cancer cell lines, MCF-7 and MDA-MB-231, when compared to the normal human breast cell line MCF-10A. This potent anticancer role in EO1 is associated with the presence of bicyclogermacrene and epi-bicyclosesquiphellandrene.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains medicinal compounds such as oleanolic acid, flavonoids, rosmarinic acid, phenolic compounds, and terpenoids. These bioactive substances work together to provide significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory benefits to the body. The presence of phenolic compounds and flavonoids helps neutralize harmful free radicals within cells. Additionally, rosmarinic acid and oleanolic acid contribute to various protective health effects. The various terpenoids found within the plant further enhance its overall therapeutic potential.

As discussed in a study, two novel triterpenoids have been isolated from the roots of Lantana camaram L., specifically 3beta,19alpha dihydroxy ursan-28-oic acid and 21,22beta-epoxy-3beta-hydroxy olean-12-en-28-oic acid in its methyl ester form. The leaves of Lantana camara yield an essential oil that is rich in sesquiterpenes. Additionally, oleanolic acid, which is thought to be a hepatoprotective compound, was isolated from the roots of the plant. This oleanolic acid was converted into its 28 --> 13beta lactone through a facile photo-oxidation reaction.

What are the herbal preparations of this plant?

This plant can be prepared as infusions, decoctions, essential oils, tinctures, and poultices to utilize its diverse medicinal properties. Practitioners often create infusions by steeping the leaves in hot water to extract delicate compounds for internal use. Tinctures prepared with alcohol serve as potent long-term extracts for various therapeutic purposes. Additionally, crushed leaves are applied directly to the skin as poultices to treat localized swellings or skin irritations.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause severe gastrointestinal distress including nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal pain. If a large amount is ingested, it may lead to serious liver damage and the onset of jaundice. Victims might also experience a loss of appetite known as anorexia and persistent lethargy. These toxic effects occur because the plant contains triterpenoids that attack hepatic cells. Monitoring for these symptoms is crucial if ingestion is suspected.
