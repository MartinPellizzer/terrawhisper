Ipomoea biflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Ipomoea biflora?

Ipomoea biflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, the plant is placed in the order Solanales and the family Convolvulaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Ipomoea, and its unique species name, biflora.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ipomoea biflora has prostrate to decumbent stems that reach up to 1 meter in length, featuring cordate leaves with entire margins and funnel-shaped white or pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically arranged alternately and display a heart-shaped structure with distinct venation. Small, cylindrical capsules serve as the fruit, which contain several dark, smooth seeds. The stems often exhibit a slightly hairy texture that assists in climbing or spreading across the ground. These morphological traits distinguish the species within the Convolvulaceae family.
