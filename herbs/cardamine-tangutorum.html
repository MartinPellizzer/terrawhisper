Cardamine tangutorum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cardamine tangutorum has a perennial herb structure characterized by basal leaves that are pinnately compound and stem leaves that are smaller and oppositely arranged. The leaves feature serrated margins and elongated leaflets that taper toward the tip. Its flowering stems are slender and upright, bearing small white flowers with four petals arranged in a cruciform pattern. The fruit develops into long, narrow siliques that contain numerous tiny seeds. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments where its delicate foliage can thrive.
