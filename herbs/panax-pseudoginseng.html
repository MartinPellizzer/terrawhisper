American ginseng

Panax pseudoginseng

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

American Ginseng (Panax pseudoginseng) is a member of the Araliaceae family. It is also known as "Fierce Ginseng" in Chinese and is a perennial herb native to eastern North America. The plant has a distinctive fleshy root, pinnately compound leaves, and small greenish-white flowers. It is sometimes confused with Panax ginseng but is scientifically distinct.

Active Compounds

American Ginseng contains key active compounds such as saponins, flavonoids, and glycosides. These compounds work synergistically to support immune function and cognitive health. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in traditional and modern herbal medicine.

  • Saponin
  • Flavonoid
  • Glycoside

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory and digestive ailments.

Extract: Used for boosting energy and immune support.

Decoction: Used for digestive and inflammatory conditions.

Safety Profile

American Ginseng is generally considered safe for most adults when used in recommended doses. However, it may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners, and should be avoided by individuals with hypertension or those undergoing surgery. Long-term use should be monitored by a healthcare professional.
