What's the taxonomical classification of Boschniakia himalaica?

Boschniakia himalaica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls into the subclass Magnoliidae and is further organized under the order Lamiales. This plant is a member of the family Orobanchaceae, specifically situated within the genus Boschniakia, and is identified at the species level as Boschniakia himalaica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Boschniakia himalaica has a perennial, creeping rhizome that produces small, erect stems reaching heights of approximately 5 to 15 centimeters.

The leaves are simple, opposite, and broadly ovate to elliptic with entire margins and distinct venation. These plants feature inconspicuous, small, greenish flowers that are borne in dense, terminal clusters. The reproductive structures consist of small, globose fruits that develop from the fertilized ovaries.

This species is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit adapted to high-altitude environments.
