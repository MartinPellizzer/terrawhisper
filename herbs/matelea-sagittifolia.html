What is Matelea sagittifolia?

Matelea sagittifolia is a species of flowering plant in the family Apocynaceae.

It is an evergreen vine with woody stems, 2-5 mm in diameter, and dark brown bark, which is smooth and covered with sparse, small, whitish lenticels. The leaves are opposite, simple, elliptical, and 7-12 cm long, with a petiole 0.5-2 cm long and a cuneate base. The inflorescence is a cymose panicle with 2-5 flowers, which are greenish-yellow and 2-3 cm long, with a 5-lobed corolla and a long, slender, greenish-yellow spur.

The plant is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.
