Delphinium yunnanense

What's the taxonomical classification of Delphinium yunnanense?

Delphinium yunnanense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is positioned in the order Ranunculales. This plant is a member of the family Ranunculaceae, falling under the genus Delphinium, and is specifically identified by the species name yunnanense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Delphinium yunnanense has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by erect, unbranched stems that typically reach heights between 30 and 100 centimeters.

The leaves are predominantly basal and cauline, displaying a deeply palmately lobed structure with serrated margins. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, terminal raceme bearing numerous zygomorphic flowers. These flowers feature a prominent nectar-producing spur extending from the uppermost sepal, which is often a deep blue or violet hue.

The foliage and floral arrangement are adapted to the temperate, high-altitude environments of its native Yunnan region.

      What are the herbal preparations of Delphinium yunnanense?

      Delphinium yunnanense is prepared as a decoction, a powder, or a topical paste derived from its dried roots and aerial parts. These medicinal preparations are often boiled in water to create a concentrated liquid used for internal consumption. The finely ground powder can be mixed with honey or other substances to facilitate easier ingestion. In some traditional practices, the plant material is crushed into a thick paste for direct application to the skin to treat external ailments. Each preparation method is selected based on the specific therapeutic goal of the treatment.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Delphinium yunnanense can cause severe poisoning through ingestion of its toxic alkaloids, which may lead to respiratory failure, paralysis, and death. The plant contains potent neurotoxins that act on the central nervous system to disrupt vital bodily functions. Symptoms of exposure often include nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and irregular heart rhythms. In cases of significant toxicity, patients may experience seizures or a complete loss of muscle control. Immediate medical intervention is necessary if any part of this plant is consumed.
