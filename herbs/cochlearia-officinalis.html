What to know about Cochlearia officinalis (sea kale) before using it medicinally

Sea kale, scientifically known as Cochlearia officinalis, is a nutrient-rich herb that has been touted for its impressive health benefits.

With high levels of vitamins A, C, and K, as well as minerals like calcium and iron, sea kale can help to boost immune function, improve digestion, and even reduce inflammation. In the kitchen, sea kale is often used as a leafy green in salads, soups, and sautés, while its young stems are also used as a crunchy addition to stir-fries and braises. As a horticultural crop, sea kale requires well-draining soil and partial shade, making it an ideal choice for container gardens or coastal landscapes.

With ancient references dating back to the Greeks and Romans, who valued its medicinal properties and used it in cooking, sea kale has a rich history that spans thousands of years.
