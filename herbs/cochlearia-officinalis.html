Cochlearia officiinalis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Its stems are succulent and prostrate, often spreading across rocky or sandy substrates. The inflorescence consists of small, cruciform flowers that emerge from the leaf axils.
