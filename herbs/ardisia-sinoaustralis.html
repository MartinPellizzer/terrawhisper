Ardisia sinoaustralis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ardisia sinoaustralis has opposite, elliptic to oblong leaves with entire margins and an acute apex that grow on woody stems. The foliage is typically dark green and lustrous on the upper surface, providing a dense appearance to the shrub. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils, often appearing in clusters throughout the growing season. These flowers eventually give way to small, fleshy drupes that serve as the plant's primary fruit. The overall structure is that of a compact evergreen shrub characterized by its sturdy branching pattern.
