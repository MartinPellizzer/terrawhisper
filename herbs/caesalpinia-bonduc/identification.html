Botanical Identification of Bonduc Tree (Caesalpinia bonduc)

To identify Bonduc Tree (Caesalpinia bonduc) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

The Bonduc Tree, Caesalpinia bonduc, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Fabales, order Fabales, family Fabaceae, genus Caesalpinia, and species Caesalpinia bonduc. These classifications reflect its role in the legume family and its botanical relationships.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The Bonduc Tree is scientifically known as Caesalpinia bonduc, a name first cited by Linnaeus in 1782. It has several synonyms, including Caesalpinia bonduca and Caesalpinia bonduca L. Common names include bonduc, kudamalai, and bonduca, while regional names vary across cultures. Historically, it was also referred to as the "peacock tree" due to its vibrant flower colors.

Morphological Characteristics

The Bonduc Tree is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree with a straight trunk and a rounded crown. It has a taproot system with lateral roots that help anchor the plant and absorb nutrients. The stem is smooth, greyish-brown, and becomes fissured with age. The leaves are pinnate, odd, with 11-21 leaflets, and are alternate along the stem. The inflorescence is a raceme of bright yellow flowers with red calyx lobes. The fruit is a flattened pod containing 3-5 oblong seeds, which are hard and dark brown in color.

Diagnostic Identification Features

The Bonduc Tree can be distinguished by its distinctive yellow-orange flowers with reddish stems, trifoliate leaves, and long, slender pods containing dark brown seeds. It is often confused with other Caesalpinia species, but its unique floral structure and leaf morphology set it apart. These features are key for accurate identification in herbal medicine contexts.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

The Bonduc Tree is native to tropical regions of Africa, Southeast Asia, and the Indian subcontinent. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained sandy or loamy soils. It typically grows at low to moderate altitudes, between 200 and 1,200 meters above sea level.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

The Bonduc Tree blooms from April to June, producing bright yellow flowers. Seed formation occurs from July to September. For identification purposes, the seeds are best harvested when the pods turn from green to brown, typically in late summer to early autumn.

Phylogenetic Relationships

The Bonduc Tree (Caesalpinia bonduc) is closely related to species within the genus Caesalpinia. It shares genetic and morphological traits with other members of the legume family, Fabaceae. Its phylogenetic position reflects its close ties to other Caesalpinia species and broader legume family characteristics.
