1. Causes stomach upset

Caesalpinia bonduc causes stomach upset due to its high concentration of tannins and flavonoids.

These compounds can irritate the mucous membranes in the digestive tract, leading to discomfort, nausea, and stomach cramps.

Additionally, the plant's saponin content may contribute to this side effect by altering the gut flora and increasing inflammation in the stomach lining.

2. Induces allergic reactions

Caesalpinia bonduc induces allergic reactions in some individuals.

This is likely due to the presence of certain compounds in its leaves and seeds, such as flavonoids and saponins, which can trigger an immune response. The allergenic properties of Caesalpinia bonduc may be exacerbated by individual sensitivities or previous exposure to similar substances.

As a result, some people may experience symptoms such as skin irritation, itching, or respiratory issues upon coming into contact with the plant or its derivatives.

3. Triggers dizziness attacks

Caesalpinia bonduc triggers dizziness attacks.

The compound flavonoids and terpenoids present in the plant may cause a sudden drop in blood pressure, leading to dizziness and lightheadedness.

Additionally, the plant's alkaloid content can stimulate the nervous system, exacerbating anxiety and stress, which can further contribute to dizziness episodes.

4. Hinders digestion process

Caesalpinia bonduc hinders digestion process due to its high concentration of saponins and alkaloids.

These compounds can bind to dietary fats and proteins, reducing their absorption in the gut and potentially leading to digestive discomfort.

Additionally, the seed's astringent properties may irritate the mucous membranes lining the digestive tract, further impairing nutrient uptake and digestion efficiency.

5. Interferes with blood clotting

Caesalpinia bonduc interferes with blood clotting due to its ability to inhibit platelet aggregation and prolong bleeding time.

This is likely caused by the presence of bioactive compounds such as flavonoids and phenolic acids in the plant, which have been shown to have anticoagulant properties.

As a result, individuals using Caesalpinia bonduc may experience prolonged bleeding after injury or surgical procedures, potentially increasing their risk of hemorrhage.

6. Provokes skin irritation

Caesalpinia bonduc provokes skin irritation due to its sap and latex content, which can cause an allergic reaction or contact dermatitis in some individuals.

The plant's resinous secretions may also lead to phototoxicity, exacerbating skin sensitivity upon exposure to UV light.

Additionally, the plant's alkaloids and phenolic compounds have been shown to stimulate a histamine response, contributing to the development of skin irritation symptoms.

7. Increases heart rate

Caesalpinia bonduc increases heart rate due to its ability to stimulate the cardiovascular system.

The plant's active compounds, including alkaloids and glycosides, can cause an increase in cardiac output, leading to a faster heartbeat.

This effect is thought to be related to the plant's traditional use as a stimulant and tonic, where it is believed to enhance circulation and boost energy levels.

8. Weakens immune system

Caesalpinia bonduc weakens immune system due to its high content of anthraquinones and flavonoids.

These compounds have been shown to suppress the production of white blood cells, which play a crucial role in fighting off infections.

Additionally, the plant's alkaloid components can disrupt the balance of gut bacteria, further compromising the immune system's ability to respond effectively to pathogens.

9. Affects respiratory function

Caesalpinia bonduc affects respiratory function.

This is due to the presence of compounds like flavonoids and terpenoids in its extract, which can cause irritation or inflammation in the lungs and airways, leading to symptoms such as wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.

Additionally, the plant's essential oils may trigger an allergic response in some individuals, further exacerbating respiratory issues.

10. Causes muscle weakness

Caesalpinia bonduc causes muscle weakness due to its ability to interact with certain neurotransmitters in the body.

The plant's chemical compounds may alter the levels of acetylcholine and other neurotransmitters, leading to decreased muscle strength and fatigue.

This effect is thought to be caused by the plant's high concentration of alkaloids, such as rotenone and deguelin, which have been shown to affect the nervous system.
