Gueldenstaedtia delavayi

What's the taxonomical classification of Gueldenstaedtia delavayi?

Gueldenstaedtia delavayi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Fabales. This plant is a representative of the family Fabaceae, commonly known as the legume family, and is specifically categorized under the genus Gueldenstaedtia, with the species designation delavayi.

What are the therapeutic actions of Gueldenstaedtia delavayi?

Gueldenstaedtia delavayi has medicinal properties that primarily function as an anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and analgesic agent to treat various physical ailments. The plant is frequently utilized in traditional medicine to alleviate pain associated with joint inflammation and muscle aches. Its bioactive compounds also demonstrate effectiveness in combating bacterial infections when applied topically or prepared as a decoction. Some practitioners use extracts from the plant to manage digestive issues and soothe internal discomfort. These therapeutic actions make it a significant resource in regional herbal practices for managing acute and chronic symptoms.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including stomach aches, skin infections, wounds, and symptoms of malaria. The medicinal properties are often derived from the leaves or bark through traditional decoctions. Local practitioners frequently apply these preparations topically to heal sores or ingest them to soothe digestive distress. Some studies suggest the plant contains bioactive compounds that may help manage febrile illnesses. This traditional use highlights its importance in regional ethnobotanical practices for addressing common infectious and inflammatory conditions.
