Acer kweilinense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Acer kweilinense has opposite, palmately lobed leaves that typically feature three to five distinct lobes with serrated margins and acute tips. The leaf blades are relatively small and possess a thin, membranous texture with prominent venation. Its stems are woody and develop a grayish bark as the tree matures. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into winged samaras. These characteristic fruit samaras are arranged in pairs and facilitate wind dispersal.
