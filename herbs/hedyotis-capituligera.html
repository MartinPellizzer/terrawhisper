Hedyotis capituligera

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hedyotis capituligera has a woody stem structure supporting small, lanceolate leaves that are arranged oppositely along the branches. The foliage typically exhibits a dull green coloration with smooth margins and a slightly thickened texture. Its most distinguishing feature is the dense, rounded flower heads that emerge from the axillary nodes. These capitula consist of numerous tiny, tubular florets tightly packed together. The overall growth habit remains compact and shrub-like, reflecting its adapted morphology for specific environmental niches.
