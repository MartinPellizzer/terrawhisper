Michelia figo

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Michelia figo has an evergreen growth habit characterized by elliptical, glossy green leaves that measure approximately 5 to 10 centimeters in length and possess entire margins. The plant typically reaches a height of 2 to 4 meters, developing a dense, shrubby structure with woody stems. Small, fragrant white flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often appearing in clusters during the blooming season. These blossoms feature multiple layers of delicate petals that release a strong scent reminiscent of banana or apricot. The root system is well-developed, providing stability to the compact and bushy form of the shrub.

      What are the herbal preparations of Michelia figo?

      Michelia figo is prepared primarily as an aromatic infusion, a distilled essential oil, or a fragrant topical tincture derived from its highly scented flowers. The fragrant blossoms are often steeped in hot water to create a tea that utilizes their subtle scent for aromatherapy. Distillation processes extract the volatile oils from the petals to produce concentrated essences used in perfumery and soothing skin applications.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Michelia figo can cause allergic skin reactions, such as contact dermatitis, and respiratory irritation in sensitive individuals due to its potent fragrance and pollen. Some people may experience redness, itching, or swelling if they come into direct contact with the plant's sap or leaves. The heavy scent emitted by the flowers can also trigger headaches or nausea in those highly sensitive to strong aromas. Additionally, inhaling large amounts of the concentrated fragrance in enclosed spaces might cause dizziness or respiratory discomfort. While generally considered safe for landscaping, these localized or systemic reactions should be monitored in susceptible users.
