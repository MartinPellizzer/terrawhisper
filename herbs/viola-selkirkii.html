Viola selkirkii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viola selkirkii has basal, cordate to reniform leaves with crenate margins and a long petiole, supporting five-petaled flowers that are typically violet or bluish-purple with a yellow throat and darker nectar guides. The plant exhibits a perennial growth habit, often forming small clumps or spreading via short rhizomes in moist habitats. Its inflorescence consists of solitary flowers borne on slender peduncles emerging from the leaf axils. The reproductive structure includes a capsule that dehisces to disperse seeds, which are often accompanied by elaiosomes to encourage ant dispersal. This species is characterized by its relatively small stature and delicate floral morphology suited for shaded, damp environments.
