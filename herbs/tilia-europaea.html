European Lime (Tilia europaea)

European Lime (Tilia europaea) is a member of the Tiliaceae family, native to Europe, Western Asia, and North Africa. Traditionally, its bark, leaves, and flowers have been used for infusions, decoctions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, tonic, and diuretic actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and unani medicine.

Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height

Leaves
Simple, alternate, dorsiventral leaves with upper surface glabrous and pale green, lower surface pubescent and darker green, exhibiting distinct stomatal bands along midrib
Flowers
White flowers in clustered inflorescences, actinomorphic, with five petals and five sepals, bearing stamen whorls of ten, and a superior ovary with two styles.
Stems
Erect, woody, with opposite branching, smooth, glabrous surface, and nodes bearing leaf scars.
