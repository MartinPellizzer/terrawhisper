Anthriscus sylvestris

What's the taxonomical classification of Anthriscus sylvestris?

Anthriscus sylvestris belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and follows the order Apiales. The plant is a member of the family Apiaceae, which is characterized by its complex flowering structures, and it is specifically categorized under the genus Anthriscus, with the species epithet sylvestris.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Anthriscus sylvestris contains various phytochemicals including coumarins, flavonoids, and essential oils that contribute to its biological activities. The presence of furanocoumarins is particularly notable, as these compounds often exhibit phototoxic properties when applied to the skin. Additionally, the plant possesses phenolic compounds that provide antioxidant effects within cellular environments. Some studies suggest these chemical constituents may also offer mild antimicrobial or anti-inflammatory potential. However, the concentration of these medicinal substances can vary significantly depending on the plant's growth environment.

What are the therapeutic actions of Anthriscus sylvestris?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat digestive ailments, respiratory congestion, and various inflammatory conditions. Its medicinal properties are primarily attributed to its essential oils and flavonoids which can soothe the gastrointestinal tract. Some traditional practices involve using the plant to alleviate symptoms of coughs and bronchitis through its expectorant qualities. Additionally, it is sometimes applied topically to address minor skin irritations and swelling. Practitioners often focus on its ability to stimulate circulation and ease spasmodic pains.
