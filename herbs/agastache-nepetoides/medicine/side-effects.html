10 Possible Side Effects Of Agastache Nepetoides

Agastache nepetoides has some side effects when used improperly, such as dizziness, headaches, and digestive issues.

These side effects occur due to its high content of essential oils, which can cause irritation and allergic reactions when ingested in large amounts.

If left unchecked, these symptoms can worsen, leading to fatigue, anxiety, and decreased productivity, ultimately affecting one's daily life and overall well-being.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Agastache nepetoides causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

This is due to the presence of compounds such as camphor and borneol, which are responsible for its characteristic flavor and aroma. When ingested or applied topically, these compounds can trigger an allergic response in sensitive individuals, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.

The exact mechanism by which this occurs is not fully understood, but it is believed to involve the immune system's overactive response to these specific compounds.

2. Induces kidney stone formation

Agastache nepetoides induces kidney stone formation due to its high oxalate content.

The plant's leaves and stems contain a significant amount of oxalic acid, a naturally occurring compound that can increase the risk of forming calcium oxalate stones in the kidneys. As oxalate is excreted through the urine, it can combine with other minerals to form crystals that eventually become kidney stones.

This side effect may occur more frequently in individuals who consume large amounts of Agastache nepetoides or have a pre-existing condition that affects their urinary tract.

3. Triggers bronchospasms

Agastache nepetoides triggers bronchospasms due to its essential oils containing camphor and thujone.

These compounds can cause the airways to constrict, leading to wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath in some individuals. Additionally, the plant's volatile oils may also irritate the lungs, further exacerbating respiratory issues.

As a result, people with pre-existing respiratory conditions or asthma may experience increased symptoms when using Agastache nepetoides.

4. Lowers blood pressure

Agastache nepetoides lowers blood pressure due to its ability to dilate blood vessels and reduce peripheral resistance.

The essential oils present in the plant, such as nepetalactone, have a vasodilatory effect, which causes blood vessels to expand and decrease blood pressure.

Additionally, the saponins and flavonoids found in Agastache nepetoides may also contribute to its blood-pressure-lowering effects by influencing the body's vascular tone.

5. Stimulates uterine contractions

Agastache nepetoides stimulates uterine contractions due to its high concentration of essential oils, particularly nepetalactone and agastachol.

These compounds have been shown to mimic the effects of oxytocin, a hormone that helps induce labor and stimulate muscle contractions in the uterus.

As a result, individuals using Agastache nepetoides may experience increased uterine activity, which could potentially affect menstrual cramps, fertility, or even cause spontaneous abortion if taken during early pregnancy.

6. Interferes with hormone regulation

Agastache nepetoides interferes with hormone regulation by containing compounds that affect the body's endocrine system.

Specifically, its volatile oils, such as nepetalactone and azulene, can bind to estrogen receptors in the body, potentially altering natural hormonal balance.

This interference may lead to a range of symptoms, including changes in mood, appetite, and sleep patterns, particularly in individuals with pre-existing hormonal imbalances or sensitivity to these compounds.

7. Produces gastrointestinal upset

Agastache nepetoides produces gastrointestinal upset.

This is due to its high concentration of volatile oils and bitter compounds that can irritate the digestive system, causing symptoms such as stomach cramps, diarrhea, and nausea.

The essential oil present in Agastache nepetoides has been shown to stimulate gastric secretion and increase intestinal motility, leading to an imbalance in the gut flora and subsequent gastrointestinal distress.

8. Increases risk of bleeding

Agastache nepetoides increases risk of bleeding due to its ability to thin the blood and disrupt platelet function.

Its coumarin and furanocoumarin compounds have a similar effect to warfarin, a common blood thinner medication. These compounds can interact with other medications that also affect blood clotting, leading to an increased risk of spontaneous bleeding or bruising.

As a result, individuals using Agastache nepetoides may experience unexplained bleeding from cuts, wounds, or internal organs.

9. Disrupts menstrual cycles

Agastache nepetoides disrupts menstrual cycles by its uterine stimulating properties.

The plant contains flavonoids and terpenoids that can cause an increase in blood flow to the uterus, leading to irregular or heavy periods. Additionally, Agastache nepetoides may interfere with hormone regulation, particularly estrogen and progesterone, which are essential for maintaining a regular menstrual cycle.

As a result, some women may experience changes in their menstrual cycles when using Agastache nepetoides, including delayed or missed periods, heavier or lighter bleeding, or longer or shorter cycles.

10. Enhances sedative effects

Agastache nepetoides enhances sedative effects by containing compounds that interact with the brain's GABA receptors, promoting relaxation and reducing anxiety.

This can lead to feelings of drowsiness or lethargy, particularly when combined with other sedating substances.

Additionally, the plant's essential oils may also contribute to its sedative properties, making it important for individuals to be aware of these potential effects when using Agastache nepetoides.
