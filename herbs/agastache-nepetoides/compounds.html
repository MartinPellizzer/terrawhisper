Blue Hyssop (Agastache nepetoides) Compounds

Blue Hyssop contains essential oil, which provides aromatic properties, and flavonoids, which have antioxidant effects. Terpenoids and phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities. Coumarin and glycosides may support digestive health, while tannins and saponins offer astringent and detoxifying benefits. Alkaloids and anthocyanins add to its potential therapeutic value.

Blue Hyssop (Agastache nepetoides) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Blue Hyssop (Agastache nepetoides) contains volatile compounds like monoterpenes and sesquiterpenes. These contribute to its aromatic properties and potential therapeutic effects, including antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory actions. The oil is often used in herbal medicine for its calming and digestive benefits.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Blue Hyssop (Agastache nepetoides) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help support respiratory health and may alleviate symptoms of respiratory infections. They also play a role in the plant's traditional use for digestive and immune support. Flavonoids are found in various parts of the plant, including the leaves and flowers.
