Citron (Citrus x aurantium)

Citron (Citrus x aurantium) is a member of the Rutaceae family, native to Southeast Asia, South Asia, and East Asia. Traditionally, its fruits, leaves, and flowers have been used for infusions, oil infusions, and decoctions.

This herb is particularly valued for its bitter, carminative, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in mediterranean herbal traditions, european herbal medicine, and traditional chinese medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 5 to 10 meters.
Leaves
Simple leaves with dorsiventral coloration, upper surface glossy green, lower surface matte green, and prominent stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Terminal inflorescences bearing white flowers with five distinct sepals and five fused petals, actinomorphic, with a prominent staminode and a superior ovary.
Stems
Erect, branched, woody, with opposite, narrowly elliptic, acuminate leaves and smooth, ridged, pithy cortex.
