Lysimachia punctatilimba

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lysimachia punctatilimba has erect, glabrous stems that reach heights of up to 60 centimeters and bear opposite, lanceolate leaves with distinctively punctate surfaces. The leaf margins are typically entire, and the surfaces are characterized by small, translucent glandular dots. Its flowers are small and yellowish, emerging from the leaf axils in a somewhat clustered arrangement. The inflorescence is often composed of several small blossoms that appear somewhat inconspicuous against the foliage. These morphological traits help distinguish the species from other members of the Primulaceae family.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China, specifically occurring in provinces such as Sichuan, Yunnan, Guizhou, and Hubei. It primarily inhabits high-altitude environments where it grows in shaded, moist habitats like forest edges and rocky slopes. The species is typically found at elevations ranging from moderate to high mountain levels. Its distribution is relatively localized within these specific subtropical montane ecosystems.
