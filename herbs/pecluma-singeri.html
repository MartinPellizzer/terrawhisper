What is Pecluma singeri?

Pecluma singeri is a species of epiphytic fern in the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its dark green, lanceolate to ovate fronds with crenulate margins, reaching up to 30 cm in length. The fronds are pinnately divided, with 20-30 pairs of pinnae, and are attached to a rhizome via a stipe. The plant produces small, yellowish-brown spores on the underside of the fronds.

The species is native to Mexico and Central America, where it grows in humid, shaded environments.
