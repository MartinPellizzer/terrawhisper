White Birch (Betula alba)

White Birch (Betula alba) is a member of the Betulaceae family, native to Northern Europe, Scandinavia, and Baltic States. Traditionally, its bark, resin, and sap have been used for decoctions, infusions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, diuretic, and astringent actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and japanese kampo medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically reaches a height of 10 to 30 meters.
Leaves
Simple leaves with smooth margins, upper surface pale green and lower surface white hairy with prominent stomatal bands.
Flowers
Flowers are actinomorphic, occurring in clustered catkins with male and female flowers on separate catkins, male flowers having three stamens and female flowers having two stigmas.
Stems
Woody, erect, smooth, glabrous, dichotomously branched, with persistent leaf scars and a distinctive flaky bark.
