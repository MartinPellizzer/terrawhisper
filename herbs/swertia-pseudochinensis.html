Swertia pseudochinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Swertia pseudochinensis?

Swertia pseudochinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Gentianaales. This plant is a member of the family Gentianaceae, specifically placed within the genus Swertia, and is identified at the species level as Swertiia pseudochinenensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Swertia pseudochinensis has opposite, elliptical leaves that are typically 3 to 7 centimeters long with entire margins and acute apices. The plant grows as a woody shrub characterized by slender, upright stems and a compact branching pattern. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often appearing in small clusters or solitary arrangements. The reproductive structures include a capsule that eventually dehisces to release small, winged seeds. These morphological features collectively distinguish the species from other members of the Gentianaceae family.

What are the therapeutic actions of Swertia pseudochinensis?

Swertia pseudochinenis has therapeutic properties that include acting as a bitter tonic, diuretic, cholagogue, febrifuge, and anti-inflammatory agent. Its diuretic action helps increase urine production to assist in the elimination of toxins from the body. Furthermore, its febrifuge and anti-inflammatory effects make it useful for reducing fever and calming systemic swelling.
