Saxifraga rufescens

What's the taxonomical classification of Saxifraga rufescens?

Saxifraga rufescens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized under the order Saxifrages (Saxifragales) and falls within the family Saxifragaecae. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Saxifraga and the species epithet rufescens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saxifraga rufescens has a basal rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically reddish or dark green and often feature serrated margins.

These leaves are arranged in a tight cluster to conserve moisture and protect the central growing point. From this rosette, a slender, upright flowering stem emerges, often reaching heights of several centimeters. The inflorescence consists of small, five-petaled flowers that vary in color from white to pinkish hues.

Each flower is supported by a delicate pedicel and sits atop a stem that may be slightly hairy or smooth.
