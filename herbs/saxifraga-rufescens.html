Saxifraga rufescens

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saxifraga rufescens has a basal rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically reddish or dark green and often feature serrated margins. These leaves are arranged in a tight cluster to conserve moisture and protect the central growing point. From this rosette, a slender, upright flowering stem emerges, often reaching heights of several centimeters. The inflorescence consists of small, five-petaled flowers that vary in color from white to pinkish hues. Each flower is supported by a delicate pedicel and sits atop a stem that may be slightly hairy or smooth.
