Hairy Lobelia (Lobelia Flaccida Var. Hirsuta)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Lobelia flaccida var. hirsuta.

Hairy lobelia, scientifically known as Lobelia flaccida var. hirsuta, is a medicinal herb native to the eastern United States, particularly in wetlands and marshy areas. It has been traditionally used by Native American tribes for its purported healing properties, often as a respiratory tonic and to treat colds and coughs. The plant is believed to contain compounds that may help support respiratory health, reduce inflammation, and promote mental clarity, making it a valued adaptogen in herbal medicine. In modern wellness practices, it is sometimes used in tinctures or teas to aid with stress relief and immune support, though scientific evidence remains limited. One unique feature of Hairy lobelia is its distinctive, slightly minty and spicy flavor, which has contributed to its use in both traditional remedies and as a minor spice in some regional cuisines.
