Aegle'S Tragacanth (Tragacantha Aegobroma)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Tragacantha aegobroma.

Tragacantha aegobroma, commonly known as the Persian gum or aegle gum, is a natural exudate obtained from the sap of the Astragalus plant, primarily found in the Middle East and parts of Asia. It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its demulcent and emollient properties, which help soothe irritated tissues and reduce inflammation. This gum is often utilized in the preparation of medicinal syrups and ointments due to its ability to form a protective film over mucous membranes. In traditional systems of medicine, such as Ayurveda and Unani, it has been employed to treat respiratory and digestive ailments. Modern research suggests that Tragacantha aegobroma may also possess antimicrobial and antioxidant properties, supporting its continued use in both traditional and complementary medicine.
