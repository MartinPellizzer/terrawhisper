White mustard

Brassica alba

White Mustard (Brassica alba) is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia, commonly cultivated for its seeds and leaves, and has been used for centuries in traditional medicine to support respiratory and digestive health.

Taxonomy

White Mustard (Brassica alba) is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae, within the order Brassicales, class Caryophyllopsida, division Spermatophyta, and kingdom Plantae.

Morphology

White Mustard (Brassica alba) is an annual herb with a slender, branched stem that can grow up to 1 meter tall, characterized by alternate, pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in racemes.

Distribution

White Mustard (Brassica alba) is native to Europe and western Asia, with historical cultivation spanning from the Mediterranean region to Central Europe, and it has been introduced to North America and other parts of the world as a cultivated crop and medicinal plant.

Traditional Uses

White Mustard (Brassica alba) has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its expectorant, diuretic, and anti-inflammatory properties, often employed to treat respiratory conditions, urinary tract issues, and skin irritations.

Medicinal Systems

In traditional medicinal systems such as Ayurveda, Chinese medicine, and ancient Greek herbalism, White Mustard (Brassica alba) has been utilized for its anti-inflammatory, expectorant, and digestive properties.

Phytochemistry

White Mustard (Brassica alba) contains a variety of bioactive compounds, including glucosinolates, flavonoids, alkaloids, and essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties and therapeutic applications.

Pharmacology

White Mustard (Brassica alba) exhibits pharmacological properties including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and expectorant actions, making it useful in treating respiratory conditions, skin infections, and inflammatory disorders.

Botanical Identification

White Mustard (Brassica alba) is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is an annual plant native to Europe and western Asia. Known also as yellow mustard or white cress, it has lobed leaves, yellow flowers, and a taproot. Its scientific name is widely recognized in herbal literature, though it has been called by various regional names throughout history.

Safety Profile

White Mustard (Brassica alba) is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal upset in some individuals. It is contraindicated in pregnancy and breastfeeding due to potential harm to the fetus or infant. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
