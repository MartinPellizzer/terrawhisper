Brassica Alba Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Brassica alba, commonly known as white mustard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, widely used in herbal medicine for its therapeutic properties.

This herb is known for its ability to support digestive health, reduce inflammation, and detoxify the body.

The bioactive constituents in Brassica alba include glucosinolates, alkaloids, and essential oils, which contribute to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antispasmodic effects.

Herbal preparations such as infusions, tinctures, and poultices can be made from the seeds or leaves to harness its medicinal benefits.
