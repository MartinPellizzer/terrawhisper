Cranberry

Vaccinium macrocarpon

Cranberry (Vaccinium macrocarpon) is a low-growing shrub native to North America, known for its bright red berries that are commonly consumed as a fruit and used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits, particularly in supporting urinary tract health.

Taxonomy

The cranberry, or Vaccinium macrocarpon, is a species belonging to the genus Vaccinium in the family Ericaceae.

Morphology

Cranberry (Vaccinium macrocarpon) is a low-growing evergreen shrub characterized by its oval, leathery leaves, bell-shaped pink to white flowers, and red, glossy berries that ripen in the fall.

Distribution

Cranberry (Vaccinium macrocarpon) is native to the eastern United States, specifically growing in acidic, wet soils of bogs and swamps in states such as Maine, New York, New Jersey, and Massachusetts.

Traditional Uses

Cranberry has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its urinary tract health benefits, particularly in preventing and treating urinary tract infections by increasing urine acidity and inhibiting bacterial adhesion to the urinary tract lining.

Medicinal Systems

In traditional medicinal systems such as Native American medicine, Ayurveda, and Chinese medicine, cranberry has been used for its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and urinary tract health benefits.

Phytochemistry

Cranberry (Vaccinium macrocarpon) contains a variety of bioactive compounds, including proanthocyanidins, flavonoids, polyphenols, and organic acids such as benzoic acid and hippuric acid, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Pharmacology

Cranberry exhibits antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties that support urinary tract health, cardiovascular function, and immune response.

Botanical Identification

Cranberry is scientifically known as Vaccinium macrocarpon, belonging to the Ericaceae family. It is also called American cranberry, red cranberry, and sweet cranberry. A perennial shrub native to North America, it has trailing stems, oval leaves, and bright red berries. Common synonyms include cranberry, bogberry, and whortleberry.

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory conditions and as a general tonic.

Safety Profile

Cranberry is generally safe when consumed in typical food amounts. However, it may cause gastrointestinal upset in some individuals. High doses of cranberry supplements may interact with certain medications, so consultation with a healthcare provider is recommended.
