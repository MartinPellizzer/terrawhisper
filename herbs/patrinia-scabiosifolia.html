What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Patrinia scabiosifolia has upright, branching stems that bear ovate to lanceolate leaves with scabrous, or rough, surfaces and serrated margins. The foliage is primarily concentrated toward the middle and upper portions of the plant, while the basal leaves often wither as the plant matures. Its inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in loose, terminal cymes. The individual blossoms feature delicate petals that typically range from white to pale pink or lavender in color. This morphology allows the plant to thrive in rocky or meadow environments where its textured leaves can withstand varied conditions.
