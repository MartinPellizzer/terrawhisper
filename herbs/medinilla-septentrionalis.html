What's the taxonomical classification of Medinilla septentrionalis?

Medinilla septentrionalis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific genus, Medinilla, and its unique species designation, septentrionalis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Medinilla septentrionalis has an erect, perennial herbaceous habit characterized by woody stems that reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The leaves are trifoliate with elliptical to oblong leaflets that feature entire margins and an acute apex. Small, inconspicuous flowers appear in axillary racemes, typically displaying a pale yellow or greenish hue. The plant produces distinctive legume-like pods that are flattened and contain several small, rounded seeds. Its root system is robust and serves as a significant storage organ for the species.
