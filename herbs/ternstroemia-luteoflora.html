Ternstroemia luteoflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Ternstroemia luteoflora?

Ternstroemia luteoflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Ericales. The plant is a member of the family Pentaphylacaceae and is specifically categorized within the genus Ternstroemia, with its unique designation being the species luteoflora.

      What are the herbal preparations of Ternstroemia luteoflora?

      Ternstroemia luteoflora is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste depending on the specific medicinal application intended. The dried leaves are often boiled in water to create a concentrated liquid used for internal consumption. In some traditional practices, the plant material is crushed into a fine pulp to form a poultice for skin ailments.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Ternstroemia luteoflora can cause skin irritation, contact dermatitis, and allergic reactions if a person comes into direct contact with its foliage or sap. While specific systemic toxicity is not extensively documented in humans, the plant contains various secondary metabolites that may act as irritants. Individuals with sensitive skin might experience redness, itching, or swelling upon touching the leaves. It is advisable to wear protective gloves when pruning or handling the shrub to prevent these adverse reactions. Monitoring for localized inflammation is recommended if accidental contact occurs.
