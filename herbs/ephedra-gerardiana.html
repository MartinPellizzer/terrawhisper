Ephedra Gerardiana Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Ephedra gerardiana, commonly known as the "Great Basin ephedra," is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

This herb is traditionally used for its stimulating and expectorant properties, aiding in respiratory health and energy enhancement.

The bioactive constituents in Ephedra gerardiana include ephedrine, pseudoephedrine, and various alkaloids that contribute to its therapeutic effects.

Herbal preparations such as infusions, tinctures, and capsules can be made from the plant's stems to harness its medicinal properties.
