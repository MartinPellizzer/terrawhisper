What's the taxonomical classification of Lycopodium alpinum?

Lycopodium alpinum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic progression continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Lycopodiidae, eventually falling under the order Lycopodiales. Within this order, it is situated in the family Lycopodiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Lycopodium, and its specific species epithet, alpinum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lycopodium alpinum has creeping rhizomes that produce upright, dichotomously branching stems covered in small, lanceolate, bright green leaves arranged spirally around the axes. These leaves are typically only a few millimeters long and possess a pointed tip that gives the plant a fine, feathery texture. The plant lacks true flowers, instead producing small, yellowish-brown spore-bearing cones known as strobili at the tips of the branches. These strobili are relatively compact and serve as the primary reproductive structures for the species. The overall architecture of the plant allows it to spread effectively across alpine tundra environments.
