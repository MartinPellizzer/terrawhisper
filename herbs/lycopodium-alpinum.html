Lycopodium alpinum

What's the taxonomical classification of Lycopodium alpinum?

Lycopodium alpinum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic progression continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Lycopodiidae, eventually falling under the order Lycopodiales. Within this order, it is situated in the family Lycopodiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Lycopodium, and its specific species epithet, alpinum.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various gastrointestinal ailments, including bloating, flatulence, and chronic constipation. It is frequently employed in homeopathic practices to address digestive disturbances caused by a lack of appetite. Practitioners sometimes use it to manage abdominal discomfort and irregular bowel movements. Additionally, it may be applied to alleviate symptoms of nervous indigestion or spasmodic colic. These applications focus primarily on restoring the natural rhythm of the digestive system.
