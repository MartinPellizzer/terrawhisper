Tilia (Tilia cordata)

Tilia (Tilia cordata) is a member of the Tiliaceae family, native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. Traditionally, its bark, leaves, and flowers have been used for decoctions, infusions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, tonic, and sedative actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and traditional chinese medicine.

Perennial herbaceous plant.

Leaves
Laminae with upper surface glabrous, lower surface pubescent, margins entire, veins pinnate with prominent midrib.
Flowers
Inflorescences are paniculate clusters with actinomorphic flowers having five white to pale yellow petals, five sepals, and ten stamens arranged in two whorls.
Stems
Elongated, cylindrical, woody stems with opposite branching, smooth surface, and prominent lenticels.

Sedative
