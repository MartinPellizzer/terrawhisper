Tilia Cordata Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Tilia cordata, commonly known as small-leaf linden, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and parts of Asia, known for its fragrant white flowers and medicinal leaves.

This herb has been traditionally used for its calming and soothing properties, offering relief from stress, anxiety, and respiratory issues.

The bioactive constituents in Tilia cordata include flavonoids, tannins, mucilage, and essential oils, which contribute to its anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and demulcent effects.

Herbal preparations such as infusions, tinctures, and syrups can be made from its flowers and leaves to harness its therapeutic benefits.
