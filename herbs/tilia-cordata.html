Tilia

Tilia cordata

Tilia (Tilia cordata) is a member of the Tiliaceae family, traditionally used for relief of inflammation, treatment of respiratory infections, and as a digestive aid. The flowers, leaves, and bark of the plant are commonly utilized in herbal preparations. It has been historically valued in various cultural and regional practices for its mild medicinal properties.

Botanical Identification

Tilia cordata, commonly known as small-leaf lime or small-leaved linden, is a deciduous tree in the Tiliaceae family. Native to Europe and western Asia, it is a perennial species with heart-shaped leaves and fragrant yellow flowers. Synonyms include Tilia caerulea and Tilia asiatica in some herbal texts.

Active Compounds

Tilia cordata contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, coumarins, and phenolic acids. These compounds work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory issues such as coughs and colds.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and as a mild sedative.

Extract: Used for inflammation and skin conditions.

Safety Profile

Tilia cordata is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause mild gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to limited safety data. Always consult a healthcare professional before use, especially for those with known allergies or chronic conditions.
