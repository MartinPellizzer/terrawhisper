Briggsia rosthornii

What's the taxonomical classification of Briggsia rosthornii?

Briggsia rosthornii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized under the order Lamiales and falls within the family Gesneriaceae. Specifically, it is identified by the genus Briggsia, with its unique species designation being rosthornii.

Briggsia rosthornii is prepared as a decoction of the dried aerial parts for medicinal consumption. Some traditional practices also involve using the plant in topical applications to treat skin irritations. The resulting liquid is often strained and ingested to address various internal ailments. These methods ensure that the plant's properties are effectively delivered to the body.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Briggsia rosthornii can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or localized swelling if the plant's juices come into direct contact with human tissue. While comprehensive clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, many plants in the Gesneriaceae family contain secondary metabolites that may trigger sensitivities. Individuals with pre-existing skin conditions might experience heightened redness or itching upon exposure to the foliage. It is advisable to wear gloves when handling the specimen to prevent unintended dermatological reactions. Monitoring for any signs of respiratory irritation or systemic discomfort is also recommended if the plant is used in indoor environments.
