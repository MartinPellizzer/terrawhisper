Trevesia palmata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Trevesia palmata has deeply palmately lobed leaves that are divided into multiple narrow, linear segments radiating from a central point. These large, spreading leaves often reach significant diameters and exhibit a distinctively architectural structure. The leaf margins are typically entire, though the overall silhouette is highly fragmented due to the deep incisions. The plant features a woody stem that supports the heavy foliage, contributing to its shrubby or small tree-like habit. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often appearing in clusters.
