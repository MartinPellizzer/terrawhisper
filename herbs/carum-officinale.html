Caraway (Carum Officinale)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Carum officinale.

Caraway, scientifically known as Carum officinale, is a flowering medicinal herb native to Europe and western Asia, widely recognized for its aromatic seeds and therapeutic properties. It has been traditionally used as an adaptogen to support digestion, relieve menstrual discomfort, and ease respiratory conditions due to its carminative and antispasmodic effects. Historically, caraway has been valued in ancient Egypt, Greece, and medieval Europe for its role in herbal medicine, culinary spice, and even as a remedy for ailments like flatulence and coughs. In modern wellness practices, it is commonly used in aromatherapy, herbal teas, and digestive supplements to promote gut health and reduce bloating. Its distinctive licorice-like flavor and the presence of compounds like apiol and carvone contribute to its unique therapeutic profile and enduring popularity in both traditional and contemporary natural remedies.
