Agave vera-cruz

What's the taxonomical classification of Agave vera-cruz?

Agave vera-cruz belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant is further organized under the order Asparagales and falls within the family Asparagaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by the genus Agave and the species vera-cruz.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Agave vera-cruz has thick, fleshy leaves that grow in a dense rosette pattern, characterized by a smooth texture and a pale glaucous green color. Unlike many other agave species, this plant lacks the sharp, terminal spines typically found on the tips of the leaves. The leaf margins are relatively smooth and do not feature the prominent teeth or serrations common in the genus. Its growth habit remains compact and slow, often forming a structural focal point in succulent gardens. The base of the plant is sturdy, supporting the heavy weight of the moisture-retaining foliage.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the subtropical and tropical regions of Mexico, specifically concentrated in the states of Veracruz and Puebla. It thrives in diverse landscapes ranging from coastal lowlands to higher elevation mountainous terrain. The species prefers well-draining soils found in scrublands and deciduous forests. Because it is adapted to these specific climates, its natural range is largely restricted to these Mexican territories. Local environmental conditions dictate its ability to colonize specific niches within these provinces.
