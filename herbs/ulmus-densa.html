Ulmus densa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ulmus densa has a dense, spreading growth habit characterized by woody branches that create a compact canopy of dark green, ovate-shaped leaves with serrated margins and asymmetrical bases. The foliage typically features prominent pinnate venation and a somewhat leathery texture that enhances its structural appearance. Its bark develops a rough, fissured surface as the specimen matures, providing a sturdy foundation for the intricate branching pattern. Small, inconspicuous flowers may emerge during the growing season, often appearing before the full expansion of the leaves. The overall morphological profile suggests a plant adapted for stability and shade production within its natural environment.
