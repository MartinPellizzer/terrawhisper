What's the taxonomical classification of Ulmus densa?

Ulmus densa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant follows the biological lineage of the order Rosales. It is further categorized under the family Ulmaceae and is situated within the genus Ulmus, eventually reaching its specific identification as the species densa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ulmus densa has a dense, spreading growth habit characterized by woody branches that create a compact canopy of dark green, ovate-shaped leaves with serrated margins and asymmetrical bases. Its bark develops a rough, fissured surface as the specimen matures, providing a sturdy foundation for the intricate branching pattern. Small, inconspicuous flowers may emerge during the growing season, often appearing before the full expansion of the leaves.
