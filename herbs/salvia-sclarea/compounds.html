Clary Sage (Salvia sclarea) Compounds

Clary Sage contains several key compounds, including essential oil, which gives it its aromatic properties. Flavonoids and phenolic acids contribute to its antioxidant effects. Coumarin is responsible for its mild sedative qualities. Terpenoids, glycosides, and tannins support its traditional uses in herbal medicine.

Clary Sage (Salvia sclarea) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Clary Sage (Salvia sclarea) is a key active compound, containing compounds like linalool and linalyl acetate. It is widely used in herbal medicine for its calming and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting emotional balance and digestive health.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Clary Sage (Salvia sclarea). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for calming effects and digestive support. These compounds may also aid in reducing oxidative stress and promoting overall well-being.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Clary Sage (Salvia sclarea). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound supports the plant's traditional use in easing respiratory and digestive issues. Its presence enhances the therapeutic profile of Clary Sage in herbal medicine.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Clary Sage (Salvia sclarea). It contributes to the plant's calming and mild sedative effects. Coumarin is also known for its antispasmodic properties, making it useful in treating digestive and respiratory conditions.

5. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a major class of compounds in Clary Sage (Salvia sclarea). They contribute to the plant's aromatic profile and have various therapeutic properties, including anti-inflammatory and sedative effects. These compounds are often extracted for use in herbal remedies and aromatherapy.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Clary Sage (Salvia sclarea). It contributes to the plant's therapeutic properties, particularly in supporting respiratory and nervous system health. Glycosides are often responsible for the plant's mild sedative and anti-inflammatory effects.

7. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Clary Sage (Salvia sclarea). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health. Tannins also help preserve the plant's aromatic compounds, enhancing its therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Clary Sage (Salvia sclarea). It contributes to the plant's mild cleansing properties and may support digestive health. Saponins in Clary Sage are known for their potential anti-inflammatory and detoxifying effects. These compounds are often used in herbal formulations for their gentle yet effective action.

9. Alkaloid

Clary Sage (Salvia sclarea) contains the alkaloid linalool. This compound is known for its calming and sedative effects, often used in aromatherapy and herbal medicine to alleviate anxiety and promote relaxation. It contributes to the plant's reputation as a natural remedy for stress and sleep disorders.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a pigment found in Clary Sage (Salvia sclarea) that contributes to its color and has antioxidant properties. It helps neutralize free radicals, supporting overall health and potentially reducing oxidative stress in the body. This compound is part of the plant's natural defense system and may contribute to its traditional uses in herbal medicine.
