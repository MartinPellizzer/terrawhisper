Botanical Identification of Clary Sage (Salvia sclarea)

To identify Clary Sage (Salvia sclarea) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Clary Sage, or Salvia sclarea, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Lamiales, order Lamiales, family Lamiaceae, genus Salvia, and species Salvia sclarea. This classification places it within the flowering plants and the mint family, which is characteristic of many aromatic herbs.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The botanical name of Clary Sage is Salvia sclarea, first described by the Swedish botanist Carl von Linné in 1753. It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is also known by several synonyms, including Salvia arvensis and Salvia terebinthifolia. Common names such as "Clary Sage" and "Lemon Sage" reflect its aromatic properties and historical use in herbal medicine.

Morphological Characteristics

Clary Sage is an upright perennial herb with a fibrous root system. Its stems are square and branching, typically reaching 60-120 cm in height. The leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, with serrated edges. The inflorescence is a terminal spike of purple to pink flowers, each with four violet-lilac petals. The fruit is a small, dry capsule containing tiny, ovoid seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Clary sage can be distinguished by its tall, erect stem and gray-green, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. It produces spikes of purple to pink flowers, and its scent is strongly aromatic, often described as spicy or herbal. It is often confused with other sage species, but its unique fragrance and flower structure help in accurate identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Clary Sage (Salvia sclarea) is native to the Mediterranean region, including southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with mild winters and warm, dry summers. It prefers well-drained soils and is commonly found at elevations between 200 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Clary Sage blooms from late summer to early autumn, typically between July and September. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early fall, around the same time as blooming. For identification purposes, the plant is most identifiable during its flowering stage, which is key for accurate botanical recognition.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Clary Sage, Salvia sclarea, belongs to the genus Salvia within the Lamiaceae family. Its closest relatives include other Salvia species such as Salvia officinalis (common sage) and Salvia fruticosa. It shares key morphological and chemical traits with its genus, such as square stems and aromatic leaves, which are characteristic of the Lamiaceae family.
