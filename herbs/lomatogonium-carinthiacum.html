Lomatogonium carinthiacum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lomatogonium carinthiacum has small, cylindrical, or slightly compressed leaves that are typically reddish-brown or dark green and arranged in a single layer around the stem. Each leaf consists of a single cell in thickness and possesses a distinct, thickened midrib known as a costa that extends to the tip. The branches are often somewhat rigid due to the structure of these leaves and the presence of small, irregular cell groupings. These morphological characteristics allow the moss to thrive in the humid, acidic environments typical of its alpine or subalpine habitats.
