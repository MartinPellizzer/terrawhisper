Lomatogonium carinthiacum

What's the taxonomical classification of Lomatogonium carinthiacum?

Lomatogonium carinthiacum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Gentianaales. This plant is further categorized within the family Gentianaceae, belonging to the genus Lomatogonium, and is specifically identified by the species name carinthiacum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lomatogonium carinthiacum has small, cylindrical, or slightly compressed leaves that are typically reddish-brown or dark green and arranged in a single layer around the stem. The plant features a creeping or ascending growth habit, often forming dense, branched mats on wet rocks or bark. Each leaf consists of a single cell in thickness and possesses a distinct, thickened midrib known as a costa that extends to the tip. The branches are often somewhat rigid due to the structure of these leaves and the presence of small, irregular cell groupings. These morphological characteristics allow the moss to thrive in the humid, acidic environments typical of its alpine or subalpine habitats.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse array of medicinal compounds including iridoid glycoside, monoterpenoid alkaloid, monoterpenoids, secoiridoids, and sesquiterpenoids. These chemical constituents contribute to the complex pharmacological profile observed in the species. The presence of secoiridoids and iridoid glycosides often suggests potential anti-inflammatory or antimicrobial activities. Furthermore, the variety of monoterpenoids and sesquiterpenoids provides a rich source of bioactive molecules. The inclusion of monoterpenoid alkaloids further enhances the therapeutic interest in its chemical makeup.

According to a study published by "Fitoterapia", a systematic phytochemical investigation on the Mongolian medicinal herb Lomatogonium carinthiacum led to the isolation of 12 monoterpenoids. These findings included three new secoiridoids identified as 1, 2 and 4, along with one new iridoid glycoside labeled 13. The investigation also identified one new monoterpenoid alkaloid 3 and three new sesquiterpenoids 14-16. Weak immunosuppressive activities were observed for these new isolates through the inhibition of T cell proliferation and cytokine IFN-γ secretion in vitro.
