Avicennia nitida

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Avicennia nitida has elliptical to lanceolate leaves that are typically 5 to 15 centimeters long, featuring a dark green upper surface and a paler underside. The plant is characterized by its woody, branching structure that often forms dense thickets in intertidal zones. Small, white flowers emerge in clusters from axillary cymes, which are followed by small, globose, greenish fruits. A distinguishing feature of this species is the presence of specialized salt-secreting glands located on the leaf surfaces. The root system consists of prominent pneumatophores that grow vertically from the anaerobic mud to facilitate gas exchange.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is distributed across the tropical and subtropical coastlines of the Atlantic Ocean, spanning from the West African coast through the Caribbean to the eastern coast of Brazil. It primarily inhabits mangrove ecosystems within intertidal zones where saltwater meets the land. This species can be found in various island territories, including the Bahamas, the Lesser Antilles, and parts of Central America. Its presence is often concentrated in shallow coastal waters and estuaries that provide the necessary saline conditions for growth. The range extends northward as far as Florida and southward along the South American coastline.
