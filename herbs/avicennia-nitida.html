Avicennia nitida

What's the taxonomical classification of Avicennia nitida?

Avicennia nitida belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further into its specific biological classification, the plant falls under the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Acanthaceae. Finally, it is defined by its specific genus, Avicennia, and its unique species designation, nitida.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Avicennia nitida has elliptical to lanceolate leaves that are typically 5 to 15 centimeters long, featuring a dark green upper surface and a paler underside.

The plant is characterized by its woody, branching structure that often forms dense thickets in intertidal zones. Small, white flowers emerge in clusters from axillary cymes, which are followed by small, globose, greenish fruits. A distinguishing feature of this species is the presence of specialized salt-secreting glands located on the leaf surfaces.

The root system consists of prominent pneumatophores that grow vertically from the anaerobic mud to facilitate gas exchange.
