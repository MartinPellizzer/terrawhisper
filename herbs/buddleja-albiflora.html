Buddleja albiflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Buddleja albiflora?

Buddleja albiflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Lamiales. This plant is a member of the family Scrophulariaceae, falling under the genus Buddleja, and is specifically identified by the species name albiflora.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Buddlejia albiflora has opposite, lanceolate to ovate leaves that measure roughly 5 to 15 centimeters in length and feature finely serrated margins with a slightly pubescent texture. The plant produces dense, terminal panicles of small, fragrant, white flowers that contrast sharply against its dark green foliage. Its woody stems are often covered in fine hairs, particularly during the juvenile stages of growth. The root system is typically robust, supporting a deciduous shrub that can reach significant heights in optimal conditions. These morphological features collectively define its structure as a vigorous and spreading ornamental species.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds acteoside, aucubin, catalpol, martynoside, and ursolic acid. These bioactive molecules contribute to the diverse pharmacological properties observed in Buddleja albibiflora. Specifically, the presence of iridoid glycosides like aucubin and catalpol is often associated with significant anti-inflammatory activities. The phenylethanoid glycoside acteoside provides additional antioxidant support within the plant's chemical profile. Furthermore, the triterpenoid ursolic acid plays a key role in the potential therapeutic applications of this species.

According to a study published by "Zhong yao cai = Zhongyaocai = Journal of Chinese medicinal materials", seven compounds were isolated and identified from Buddleja albiflora. These specific medicinal compounds include aucubin (1), catalpol (2), acteoside (3), martynoside (4), ursolic acid (5), daucosterol (6), and beta-sitosterol-3-o-beta-d-(6'-o-palmitate) glucopyranoside (7). All 7 of these substances were obtained from Buddleja albiflora for the first time. The constituents were isolated using column chromatography and their structures were elucidated through spectroscopic analyses.
