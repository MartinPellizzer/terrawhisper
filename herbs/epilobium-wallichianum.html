Epilobium wallichianum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Epiloium wallichianum has erect, glandular-pubescent stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and bear opposite, lanceolate to ovate leaves with entire or slightly serrated margins. The foliage is characterized by a soft, hairy texture due to the presence of fine trichomes on both surfaces. Its reproductive structures consist of small, four-petaled flowers that are often pink or lavender in hue. These blossoms are supported by slender pedicels and lead to elongated, capsule-like fruit containing numerous tiny seeds. The overall plant structure presents a delicate, herbaceous appearance suitable for damp environments.
