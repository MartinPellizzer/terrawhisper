Crisp Mint (Mentha crispa)

Crisp Mint (Mentha crispa) is a member of the Lamiaceae family, native to Europe, Western Asia, and North Africa. Traditionally, its leaves, stems, and flowers have been used for infusions, decoctions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its carminative, antispasmodic, and expectorant actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and japanese kampo medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters.
Leaves
Laminae with serrate margins, upper surface glabrous and dark green, lower surface pale green with prominent stomatal bands.
Flowers
Inflorescence consists of dense spike-like clusters of tubular flowers with two-lipped corolla, blue-violet to purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem, actinomorphic symmetry, with two stamens and a superior ovary.
Stems
Cylindrical, erect, hollow, quadrangular, glabrous, with opposite, ascending, stipitate leaves.
