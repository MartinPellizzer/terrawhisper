What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Schefflera parvifoliolatat has small, elliptical to lanceolate leaves that typically measure between 2 and 5 centimeters in length and are arranged in an opposite or sub-opposite pattern along its woody stems. The leaf margins are entire and smooth, while the upper surfaces often exhibit a dark green, leathery texture. These foliage clusters emerge from branches that possess a slightly rough or lenticellate bark surface. The plant also develops inconspicuous inflorescences that emerge from the leaf axils to produce small, rounded fruits. This specific morphology distinguishes the species from its larger-leaved relatives within the Rubiaceae family.
