What is Sabulina michauxii?

Sabulina michauxii is a perennial herbaceous plant, belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae, native to North America.

It grows to a height of 10-30 cm, with erect stems and leaves that are linear-lanceolate to ovate-lanceolate in shape, measuring 2-10 cm in length and 1-5 mm in width. The plant produces white or pinkish flowers in late spring, arranged in a corymbous inflorescence, with 5-7 petals that are elliptical in shape and 1-2 mm in length. The calyx is 5-6 mm in length, with 5 sepals that are lanceolate in shape and 1-2 mm in width.

The plant typically grows in dry to moist sandy areas, often in full sun.
