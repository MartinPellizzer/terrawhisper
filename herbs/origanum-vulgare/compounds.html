Oregano (Origanum vulgare) Compounds

The primary compounds in oregano include essential oil, which contains thymol and carvacrol, known for their antimicrobial properties. Flavonoids and phenolic acids contribute to its antioxidant effects. Terpenoids and coumarin add to its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic actions. Tannins and glycosides support digestive health, while saponins and anthocyanins offer additional therapeutic benefits.

Oregano (Origanum vulgare) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Oregano (Origanum vulgare) is a concentrated mixture of volatile compounds, primarily carvacrol and thymol, known for their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are naturally occurring compounds found in Oregano (Origanum vulgare). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds help protect cells from damage and support the body's natural defenses. Flavonoids are a key component of Oregano's therapeutic value in herbal medicine.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Oregano (Origanum vulgare). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties and has been studied for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. This compound is found in high concentrations in oregano leaves and is responsible for some of its therapeutic benefits in herbal medicine.

4. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Oregano (Origanum vulgare), contributing to its aromatic profile and medicinal properties. These compounds, including carvacrol and thymol, exhibit antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant effects, making them key components in the therapeutic use of oregano in herbal medicine.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a naturally occurring compound found in Oregano (Origanum vulgare). It contributes to the plant's aromatic properties and has been studied for its potential anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects. Coumarin may also support digestive health and has shown promise in preliminary research for its antioxidant properties.

6. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Oregano (Origanum vulgare). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and has antimicrobial effects. Tannins help reduce inflammation and support digestive health. They are found in significant amounts in the leaves and oils of Oregano.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Oregano (Origanum vulgare). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, often working synergistically with other active compounds. Glycosides in oregano may support immune function and have antimicrobial effects. They are typically extracted along with other phytochemicals during herbal preparation.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Oregano (Origanum vulgare). It contributes to the plant's antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Saponin helps enhance the immune response and may support digestive health. Its presence in Oregano makes it a valuable herb in traditional medicine.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Oregano (Origanum vulgare). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports the plant's traditional use in promoting health and combating inflammation.

10. Alkaloid

Oregano (Origanum vulgare) contains the alkaloid thymol. Thymol is known for its antimicrobial and antifungal properties, contributing to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments. It also plays a role in the plant's characteristic strong aroma and potency in herbal remedies.
