Desmos dumosus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds (+)-(2R)-7-methoxychamanetin and (+)-(2S)-desmosdumosoine among its diverse chemical constituents. These specific molecules are part of a complex profile of bioactive secondary metabolites found within the plant's tissues. Researchers study these compounds to understand their potential therapeutic applications and pharmacological properties. The presence of such specialized chemical structures often suggests unique biological activities. Detailed chemical analysis helps identify how these substances interact with biological systems.

As discussed in a study, 4 new compounds, including (+)-(2S)-desmosdumosone (1), (+)-(2R)-7,8-dimethoxy-5-hydroxyflavanone (7), (+)-(2R)-7-methoxychamanetin (9), and (+)-(1'R,2'R)-phebalosin (18), along with 25 known compounds, were isolated from the twig and leaf extracts of Desmos dumosus. Compounds 9, 11, 13, 14, 22, 25, and 28 demonstrated alpha-glucosidase inhibitory activities. These compounds showed IC50 values ranging from 5.3-52.7 uM, which is better than the standard control acarboase with an IC50 value of 83.5 uM. Specifically, compound 13 was the most active with an IC50 value of 5.3 uM.
