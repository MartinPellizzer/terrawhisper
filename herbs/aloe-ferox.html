African Aloe (Aloe ferox)

African Aloe (Aloe ferox) is a member of the Asphodelaceae family, native to Southern Africa, Madagascar, and Comoros. Traditionally, its leaves, sap, and resin have been used for decoctions, infusions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, astringent, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in african traditional medicine, european herbal medicine, and traditional chinese medicine.

Perennial herbaceous plant.

Leaves
Broad leaves with white to pale green upper surfaces and dark green lower surfaces, bearing distinct stomatal bands along the midribs.
Flowers
Inflorescence composed of dense, spike-like clusters of yellow flowers with six tepals arranged in two whorls, actinomorphic symmetry, and red-orange markings at the base of the tepals.
Stems
Smooth, erect, unbranched stems with a succulent texture, covered in a layer of short, pale trichomes, and exhibiting a distinct basal swelling.
