Gnaphalium adnatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Gnaphalium adnatum?

Gnaphalium adnatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae, falling under the genus Gnaphalium, with the specific species designation adnatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gnaphaliuum adnatum has a woody, perennial habit characterized by erect stems that are densely covered in fine, white, woolly hairs. These stems typically reach heights of up to 50 centimeters and possess leaves that are linear to lanceolate in shape. The foliage is often crowded along the stem and exhibits a grayish-green color due to the thick pubescence. Small, discoid flower heads are arranged in terminal clusters, appearing yellowish or white in color. This dense coating of hairs serves as a primary morphological adaptation to protect the plant from intense sunlight and moisture loss.

What are the pharmacological activities of Gnaphalium adnatum?

This plant has anti-Helicobacter pylori activity which allows it to target the bacteria responsible for gastric ulcers. The extract functions by inhibiting the growth and colonization of these specific pathogens within the stomach lining. Researchers investigate these properties to understand how the plant's chemical constituents interact with bacterial cell walls. Utilizing such botanical resources may offer a natural approach to managing gastrointestinal infections. This specific pharmacological action highlights the potential therapeutic value of Gnaphaulium adnatum in digestive health.

According to a study published by "FEMS immunology and medical microbiology", Gnaphalium adnatum Wall. ex DC. (GAWEDC) was among 50 Taiwanese folk medicinal plants examined for anti-Helicobacter pylori activity. Extracts were prepared using 95% ethanol for the herbal extraction process. This specific plant was categorized within a group of 19 herbs that possessed lower anti-H. pylori effects. While other extracts showed minimum inhibitory concentration values ranging from 0.64 to 10.24 mg ml(-1), Gnaphalium adnatum Wall. ex DC. demonstrated lower activity. Approximately 50% of the tested Taiwanese folk medicinal plants demonstrated higher anti-H. pylori activity.
