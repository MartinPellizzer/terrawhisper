What's the taxonomical classification of Parnassia wightiana?

Parnassia wightiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is organized under the order Celastrales and the family Celastraceae. Finally, it is identified within the genus Parnassia, specifically as the species wightiana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Parnassia wightiana has a basal rosette of ovate to lanceolate leaves that are relatively thin and lack prominent venation.

The inflorescence consists of a single, terminal flower held atop a slender, erect scape. This flower features five white petals characterized by prominent, dark, nectar-secreting glands located near the center. The reproductive structures include a central gynoecium surrounded by several stamens that facilitate pollination.

As the plant matures, the flower eventually gives way to a small, capsule-like fruit containing numerous seeds.
