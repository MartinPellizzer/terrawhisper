Trollius pumilus

What's the taxonomical classification of Trollius pumilus?

Trollius pumilus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further descends through the order Ranunculales and is a member of the family Ranunculaceae. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Trollius, with its specific designation being the species pumilus.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Trollius pumilus contains no documented medicinal uses in modern pharmacology or traditional ethnobotany. While many members of the Ranunculaceae family possess bioactive compounds, this specific dwarf globeflower species has not been formally studied for therapeutic applications. Researchers primarily focus on its ecological role in high-altitude alpine environments rather than its chemical properties. Consequently, there is a lack of scientific literature regarding the safety or efficacy of any part of this plant for human consumption. This absence of data means the plant should not be used for medicinal purposes.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Trollius pumilus can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if ingested due to the presence of protoanemonin. This chemical compound is a volatile oil that triggers inflammatory responses upon contact with sensitive tissues. If the plant is handled without protection, users may experience redness, itching, or blistering on the skin. Ingestion of the plant can lead to severe nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain. Because of these unpredictable reactions, the plant should be treated as potentially hazardous to both humans and animals.
