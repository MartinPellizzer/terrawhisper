Caper

Capparis spinosa

Caper (Capparis spinosa) is a flowering plant native to Mediterranean regions and parts of Asia, known for its edible buds and its use in traditional medicine to support digestive health and reduce inflammation.

Botanical Identification

Caper is the common name for Capparis spinosa, a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Capparidaceae family and is also known as caper bush or caper plant. The plant is a perennial shrub with thorny stems, ovate leaves, and yellow flowers. It has been used in herbal medicine for centuries.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Spermatophyta
  • Class: caryophyllopsida
  • Order: Capparales
  • Family: Capparaceae
  • Genus: Capparis
  • Species: Capparis spinosa

Active Compounds

Caper contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to provide antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial effects. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory ailments due to its bitter and antimicrobial properties.

Infusion: Used for respiratory conditions and as a mild stimulant due to its active compounds.

Poultice: Used for skin irritations and wounds due to its anti-inflammatory and healing properties.

Safety Profile

Caper (Capparis spinosa) is generally considered safe when used in moderate amounts. However, it may cause gastrointestinal upset in some individuals. There are no well-documented contraindications, but caution is advised for those with known allergies to plants in the Capparaceae family.
