Capparis spinosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Capparis spinosa?

Capparis spinosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is situated in the order Brassicales. It is a member of the family Capparaceae, falling under the genus Capparis, and is specifically identified by its species name, spinosa.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Capparis spinosa contains high concentrations of bioactive compounds including flavonoids, alkaloids, phenolic acids, and glucosinolates such as kaempferol and quercetin. These phytochemicals contribute to the plant's significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Research indicates that the presence of specific alkaloids provides antimicrobial and antipyretic effects. Furthermore, the glucosinolate components are studied for their potential role in cancer prevention through cellular detoxification pathways. These diverse medicinal constituents make the plant a subject of interest for treating oxidative stress and various metabolic disorders.

What are the therapeutic actions of Capparis spinosa?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat inflammatory conditions, digestive disorders, skin infections, and various metabolic disturbances. Its bioactive compounds provide antioxidant and antimicrobial properties that help combat bacterial pathogens. In traditional medicine, it is frequently applied to manage symptoms of jaundice and liver dysfunction. Some studies also suggest its potential in regulating blood glucose levels for diabetic management. Additionally, its anti-inflammatory effects are utilized to soothe gastrointestinal inflammation and skin irritations.
