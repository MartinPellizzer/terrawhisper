Blueberry (Vaccinium corymbosum)

Blueberry (Vaccinium corymbosum) is a member of the Ericaceae family, native to Eastern North America, Canada, and Northern United States. Traditionally, its fruits, leaves, and bark have been used for infusions, decoctions, and culinary uses.

This herb is particularly valued for its astringent, tonic, and diuretic actions, and has a long history of use in native american herbal medicine, european herbal medicine, and traditional chinese medicine.

Flowers

Erect, woody, branched, with glabrous surfaces and opposite leaf scars.
