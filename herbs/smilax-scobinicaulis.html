Smilax scobinicaulis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Smilax scobinicaulis has woody, climbing stems characterized by a distinct brownish or purplish bark texture and armed with sharp, recurved prickles. Its leaves are typically simple, ovate to lanceolate in shape, and possess an entire margin with a glossy, dark green surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers that eventually develop into fleshy, red or orange berries. A defining morphological feature is the presence of stipules at the base of the petioles, which can vary in size and shape depending on the specific variety. These climbing vines utilize their prickly stems to scramble through dense forest undergrowth.
