Saussurea nigrescens

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea nigrescens has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by a thick, woody caudex and long, lanceolate leaves that are typically dark green or blackish in color. The foliage features prominent veins and margins that may appear slightly serrated or wavy. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, head-like capitulum supported by a sturdy stem. The involucral bracts surrounding the flower head are often dark-pigmented, contributing to the species' namesake appearance. These botanical features are evolutionary adaptations to its high-altitude alpine environment.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude alpine regions of the Himalayas, specifically distributed across parts of India, Nepal, and Bhutan. It grows primarily in rocky crevices and scree slopes located at elevations between 3,500 and 4,500 meters. Its distribution is closely tied to the specific microclimates found within these mountain ranges. The species thrives in areas characterized by cold temperatures and intense ultraviolet exposure. Because it is restricted to these unique montane habitats, its range is naturally fragmented and isolated.

      Saussurea nigrescens is prepared primarily as a decoction or an infusion using the dried roots or aerial parts of the plant.
