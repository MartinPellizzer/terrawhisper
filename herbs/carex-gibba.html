Carex gibba

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Carex gibba has perennating rhizomes that produce tufted, linear leaves and spikes consisting of several distinct, nodding female flowers and small, inconspicuous male flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and can reach lengths of up to 20 centimeters, often appearing somewhat channeled or folded. Its perigynia are notably inflated and possess a distinctively short, blunt beak. These specialized sacs contain the seeds and are often characterized by a slightly roughened or papillose surface texture. The plant's overall growth habit remains compact and caespitose, forming dense clumps in its natural habitat.
