Carex cruciata

What's the taxonomical classification of Carex cruciata?

Carex cruciata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls into the subclass Magnoliidae and is further categorized under the order Poales. This plant is a member of the family Cyperaceae and is specifically identified within the genus Carex, ultimately reaching its designation as the species cruciata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Carex cruciata has a perennial growth habit characterized by a dense tufted form with long, narrow, grass-like leaves that often exhibit a purplish hue.

The inflorescence is distinctively shaped, featuring several spiked clusters arranged in a cross-like pattern at the top of the stem. These spikes are typically reddish-brown to dark brown in color and are separated by short, leafy sections. Each spike consists of small, teardrop-shaped perigynia that protect the developing seeds.

The stems are erect and sturdy, providing the necessary support for the heavy, clustered flowering heads.

        What are the herbal preparations of Carex cruciata?

        Carex cruciata is prepared primarily as a decoction or an infusion of its rhizomes and leaves for medicinal or ecological applications.         What side-effects this plant can have?

        Carex cruciata can cause skin irritation or allergic contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals who touch its foliage. While it is generally considered non-toxic to humans in terms of ingestion, there is very little clinical data regarding its safety for consumption. Some people may experience mild discomfort if the plant comes into prolonged contact with the skin. It is best to handle the plant with care to avoid potential dermatological reactions. Always consult a medical professional if you suspect an adverse reaction has occurred.
