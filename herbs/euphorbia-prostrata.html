Euphorbia prostrata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Euphorbia prostrata has a prostrate, spreading growth habit characterized by slender, cylindrical stems that often exhibit a reddish or purplish hue. The leaves are small, elliptical to obovate in shape, and arranged in an alternate pattern along the stem. These plants produce tiny, inconspicuous yellowish flowers clustered in small umbels called cyathia. A defining morphological feature is the presence of milky white latex that exudes from the stems when they are damaged. The root system is typically shallow and fibrous, helping the plant colonize disturbed ground quickly.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Euphorbia prostrata targets cyclooxygenase (COX) enzymes, lipoxygenase (LOX) proteins, and various inflammatory signaling pathways to exert its medicinal effects. The plant contains bioactive diterpenes and polyphenols that function as potent inhibitors of COX-2, which helps reduce the production of pro-inflammatory prostaglandins. These phytochemicals also interact with nitric oxide synthase to modulate inflammatory responses and cellular signaling. Additionally, certain compounds in the plant demonstrate the ability to influence protein kinases involved in cell cycle regulation and oxidative stress management. Through these diverse protein-ligand interactions, the plant exhibits significant anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

What are the therapeutic actions of Euphorbia prostrata?

Euphorbia prostrata has therapeutic actions including astringent, diuretic, anti-inflammatory, hemostatic, and emollient properties. The plant acts as an astringent to help shrink tissues and as a diuretic to increase urine production. Its anti-inflammatory effects assist in reducing swelling and redness throughout the body. Additionally, the herb serves as a hemostatic agent to help control or stop bleeding. Finally, its emollient qualities provide soothing relief by softening and smoothing irritated skin.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause skin irritation, mucous membrane irritation, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal pain if contact or ingestion occurs. The milky latex produced by the plant is known to provoke redness and discomfort upon touching the skin. If the sap comes into contact with the eyes or mouth, it can result in significant irritation to the mucous membranes. Ingesting any part of the plant may trigger severe gastrointestinal distress, including nausea and vomiting. Furthermore, individuals may suffer from prolonged diarrhea and intense abdominal pain following consumption.
