Xylopia vallotii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Xylopia vallotii has an erect woody habit characterized by a single stem, elliptical to lanceolate leaves with entire margins, and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in axillary clusters. The foliage is typically coriaceous and displays a glossy green surface on the upper side. Its reproductive structures feature a distinct carpel arrangement typical of the Annonaceae family, often leading to fleshy, monocarpidic fruits. The bark is generally smooth or slightly fissured depending on the age of the specimen. These morphological traits collectively distinguish this species within its tropical forest habitat.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical rainforest regions of Central and West Africa, specifically within countries such as Cameroon, Gabon, the Democratic Republic of the Congo, and parts of Nigeria. Its distribution is closely tied to the humid, equatorial climate zones characteristic of the Guineo-Congolian forest region. Within these habitats, it typically grows in the shaded understory or along riverbanks where moisture levels are consistently high. The species is most frequently documented in low-altitude lowland forests that experience significant annual rainfall. Environmental factors like soil composition and canopy density significantly influence the specific density of its occurrence across these territories.
