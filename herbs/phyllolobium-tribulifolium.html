Phyllolobium tribulifolium

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phyllolobium tribulifolium has trifoliate leaves composed of three distinct elliptical leaflets attached to a central petiole. Each individual leaflet features a smooth margin and a fine network of veins that extend toward the slightly pointed apex. The stem structure is woody and robust, providing significant support for the dense foliage. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils during the peak growing season. These botanical features collectively define the unique structural profile of this specific species.

Additionally, the plant's phytochemicals modulate the NF-kappaB signaling pathway to downregulate the expression of genes associated with systemic inflammation.       Phyllolobium tribulifolium is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical salve depending on the intended therapeutic use.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Phyllolobium tribulifolium can cause gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, and neurological tremors if ingested or applied topically in improper doses. These adverse reactions often stem from the concentration of bioactive alkaloids present within the leaf tissue. Users may experience nausea or stomach cramping shortly after consumption. In some cases, direct contact with the sap may lead to localized redness or a burning sensation on the skin. Monitoring for these specific symptoms is essential to ensure safety when interacting with the plant.
