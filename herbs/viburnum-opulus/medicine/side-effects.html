10 Possible Side Effects Of Viburnum Opulus (Guelder Rose)

Viburnum opulus has some side effects when used improperly, such as allergic reactions, nausea, and diarrhea.

These side effects can be caused by excessive consumption, poor quality of the herb, or interactions with other medications. Severe gastrointestinal issues, for instance, can lead to malnutrition, dehydration, and energy loss, ultimately affecting daily activities and overall well-being.

In severe cases, these side effects can worsen quality of life and even lead to more serious health problems.

Viburnum opulus causes allergic reactions due to the presence of urushiol, a potent allergen.

This compound is responsible for triggering an immune response in sensitive individuals, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and skin rashes.

Additionally, some people may experience respiratory issues like congestion and coughing upon exposure to Viburnum opulus, further exacerbating the allergic reaction.

When this oil comes into contact with human skin, it can cause an allergic reaction, leading to redness, itching, and even blisters.

Additionally, the sap of the plant contains other compounds that may contribute to skin irritation, making it essential for individuals to exercise caution when handling or processing Viburnum opulus products.
