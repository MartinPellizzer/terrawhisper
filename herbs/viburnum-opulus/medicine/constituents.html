10 Medicinal Constituents Of Viburnum Opulus (Guelder Rose)

Viburnum opulus has active constituents such as tannins, flavonoids, and terpenes.

These constituents contribute to the plant's antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties. The tannins help in soothing digestive issues and skin irritations, while flavonoids exhibit antioxidant effects, potentially reducing the risk of cardiovascular diseases.

The terpenes may alleviate pain and improve respiratory health, demonstrating the plant's potential to improve people's quality of life.

1. Gallic acid

Viburnum opulus gallic acid is a naturally occurring compound found in its leaves and stems.

It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties. Gallic acid has been shown to exhibit potential health benefits, including inhibiting the growth of certain pathogens and reducing oxidative stress in the body.

Its bioactive properties have also led to research on its use as a natural preservative and anti-cancer agent.

2. Viburnine

Viburnum opulus viburnine is a natural compound extracted from the berries of the plant.

It has been used in traditional medicine for centuries to treat various ailments, including fever, rheumatism, and diarrhea. Viburnine is believed to have antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antibacterial properties, making it a valuable ingredient in herbal remedies.

Its extracts are also used as a food additive and natural preservative.

3. Flavonoids (e.g., quercetin)

Viburnum opulus flavonoids (e.g., quercetin) is a group of bioactive compounds found in the leaves and fruits of this deciduous shrub.

These flavonoids have been shown to possess antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, making them potential candidates for the treatment of various diseases such as cardiovascular disorders, cancer, and microbial infections.

4. Saponins

Viburnum opulus saponins is a group of bioactive compounds found in the berries and leaves of this plant.

These saponins have been studied for their potential medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects.

They have also been shown to exhibit immunomodulatory activity, making them a promising area of research for the treatment of various diseases.

5. Oleic acid

Viburnum opulus oleic acid is a type of fatty acid found in its berries.

It has been studied for its potential medicinal properties and has been shown to have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.

The oleic acid content in Viburnum opulus has also been found to contribute to the plant's antioxidant activity, making it potentially useful in the prevention and treatment of various diseases.

6. Linoleic acid

Viburnum opulus linoleic acid is a polyunsaturated fatty acid that has been identified in its leaves and berries.

This essential fatty acid plays a crucial role in the structure and function of cell membranes, and research suggests it may have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Additionally, linoleic acid may help to support skin health and reduce the appearance of fine lines and wrinkles.

7. Palmitic acid

Viburnum opulus palmitic acid is a significant compound found in its fruit, leaves, and flowers.

This saturated fatty acid plays a crucial role in the plant's defense mechanisms against pathogens and pests. Palmitic acid has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various ailments, including skin conditions, and its antimicrobial properties have been extensively studied.

It is also believed to have anti-inflammatory effects, making it a potential natural remedy for various health issues.

8. Vitamin c

Viburnum opulus vitamin C is an essential nutrient found in its berries.

The berries are rich in ascorbic acid, which makes up a significant portion of their composition. Vitamin C plays a crucial role in immune function, collagen production, and iron absorption.

Consuming Viburnum opulus berries can help boost the body's natural defenses against infections and diseases, making it an excellent addition to a healthy diet.

9. Kaempferol

Viburnum opulus kaempferol is a flavonoid glycoside found in its berries and leaves.

It has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various ailments such as diarrhea, fever, and respiratory issues. Kaempferol exhibits antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a potential therapeutic agent for various diseases, including cardiovascular disorders and cancer.

Its bioactive compounds have also been shown to inhibit the growth of microorganisms and exhibit antimicrobial activity.

10. Quercetin glycosides

Viburnum opulus quercetin glycosides is a group of bioactive compounds isolated from the fruit and leaves of this plant species.

These glycosides are composed of quercetin, a flavonoid molecule, attached to various sugars, such as galactose, arabinose, or rhamnose.

They exhibit significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities, which may contribute to the traditional uses of Viburnum opulus in folk medicine for treating various conditions, including digestive issues and respiratory problems.
