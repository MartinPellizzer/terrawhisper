What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Viburnum Opulus (Guelder Rose)?

Viburnum opulus, also known as guelder rose, has health benefits such as reducing inflammation and alleviating digestive issues.

The herb contains medicinal constituents like salicylic acid, which has anti-inflammatory and astringent properties, as well as flavonoids and tannins. These compounds can be extracted for use in medicinal preparations such as tinctures, infusions, and ointments. Possible side effects include allergic reactions, interactions with medications, and gastrointestinal upset.

Precautions include consulting a healthcare professional before using guelder rose, especially for pregnant or breastfeeding individuals.

This article explains the health benefits, active constituents, medicinal preparations, possible side effects, and precautions related to Viburnum opulus.

What are the health benefits of Viburnum opulus?

Viburnum opulus, also known as guelder rose, has health benefits such as reducing inflammation and improving cardiovascular health due to its high content of salicin and antioxidants.

The plant's berries are also used to treat diarrhea and digestive issues, while its bark and leaves have been traditionally used to treat fever and infections.

The antioxidants in guelder rose may also help protect against cell damage and reduce the risk of certain diseases.

What are the active constituents of Viburnum opulus?

Viburnum opulus, also known as guelder rose, has active constituents such as viburnin, scopoletin, and tannins.

These compounds possess medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activity. Viburnin has been shown to have a protective effect on the heart and blood vessels, while scopoletin exhibits anti-edematous and diuretic properties.

The tannins in guelder rose may help to soothe digestive issues and reduce fever.

What are the medicinal preparations of Viburnum opulus?

Viburnum opulus, also known as guelder rose, has medicinal preparations such as a decoction of its berries, leaves, and roots.

The berries are rich in vitamins A and C, potassium, and flavonoids, which give them antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

The leaves are used to treat fever, rheumatism, and as an anti-inflammatory agent, while the roots are used to treat kidney and urinary problems.

What are the possible side effect of using Viburnum opulus improperly?

Improper use of Viburnum opulus, also known as guelder rose, increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as stomach upset, diarrhea, and allergic reactions, particularly skin rashes or itching.

Additionally, consuming excessive amounts of guelder rose berries can lead to vomiting, nausea, and dizziness, while pregnant women and breastfeeding mothers may experience uterine contractions or issues related to milk production.

What precautions to take when using Viburnum opulus medicinally?

Before using Viburnum opulus, also known as guelder rose, for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as consulting a healthcare professional, especially if you have allergies or are pregnant/breastfeeding.

Also, avoid using it in excessive quantities, as it can cause stomach upset and interact with certain medications.

In addition, the ripe berries and leaves are considered safe for internal use, but unripe berries and leaves should be avoided due to their toxicity.
