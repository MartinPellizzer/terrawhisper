European Dogwood (Viburnum opulus) Uses

European Dogwood is used to treat respiratory conditions such as cough, cold symptoms, bronchitis, and influenza. It also helps with skin issues like infections, irritation, and inflammation. Its properties support immune function and reduce symptoms associated with these conditions.

European Dogwood (Viburnum opulus) Monograph

1. Cough

European Dogwood (Viburnum opulus) has been traditionally used to soothe coughs due to its demulcent properties, which coat and protect irritated airways. It may help reduce inflammation and ease throat discomfort associated with coughing. The berries, when prepared as a decoction, are commonly used in herbal remedies for respiratory support.

2. Cold symptoms

European Dogwood is used to alleviate cold symptoms due to its antipyretic and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce fever and soothe sore throats. The berries are often made into syrups or tinctures for respiratory support. It is typically used in combination with other herbs for enhanced effectiveness.

3. Respiratory infections

European Dogwood (Viburnum opulus) has been traditionally used to support respiratory health. Its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties may help alleviate symptoms of respiratory infections. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to soothe coughs and reduce mucus production. Historically, it was used to treat colds, flu, and bronchitis.

4. Influenza

European Dogwood (Viburnum opulus) has been traditionally used to support respiratory health during influenza. Its anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties may help reduce fever and soothe throat irritation. Infusions of its leaves and bark were historically prepared to alleviate symptoms of flu and colds.

5. Bronchitis

European Dogwood (Viburnum opulus) has been traditionally used for bronchitis due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. The bark and leaves contain compounds that help loosen mucus and reduce airway swelling. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to support respiratory health during acute bronchial inflammation.

6. Tonsillitis

European Dogwood (Viburnum opulus) has been traditionally used to treat tonsillitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. The bark and leaves contain compounds that help reduce throat inflammation and soothe painful symptoms. It is often prepared as a gargle or infusion to support healing and alleviate discomfort associated with tonsillitis.

7. Common cold

European Dogwood (Viburnum opulus) has been traditionally used to support the treatment of the common cold due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It is often prepared as a tea to help alleviate symptoms such as congestion and sore throat. The berries and bark contain compounds that may reduce viral activity and soothe respiratory discomfort.

8. Skin infections

European Dogwood (Viburnum opulus) has been traditionally used to treat skin infections due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bark and leaves contain compounds that help reduce bacterial growth and soothe irritated skin. It is often prepared as a poultice or infused oil for topical application in treating conditions like eczema and fungal infections.

9. Skin irritation

European Dogwood (Viburnum opulus) has been traditionally used to treat skin irritations such as rashes, eczema, and insect bites. Its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties help reduce redness and soothe inflamed skin. Infusions or poultices made from the bark or leaves are commonly applied topically for relief.

10. Skin inflammation

European Dogwood (Viburnum opulus) has been traditionally used to treat skin inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and irritation, making it useful for conditions like eczema and psoriasis. The bark and leaves are often prepared as poultices or infusions for topical application.
