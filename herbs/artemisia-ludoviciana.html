White sage

Artemisia ludoviciana

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

White Sage, scientifically known as Artemisia ludoviciana, belongs to the Asteraceae family. It is also called Silver Sage or Blue Sage in some regions. A perennial shrub native to North America, it has silvery-gray foliage, aromatic leaves, and small yellow flowers. It is sometimes referred to as Artemisia vulgaris in older herbal texts.

Active Compounds

White Sage contains essential oils, terpenoids, and flavonoids as its primary active compounds. These compounds work together to provide anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. The synergistic interaction between these phytochemicals enhances its therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

  • Essential oil
  • Terpenoid
  • Flavonoid

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action
Respiratory Respiratory infections, excess mucus, tuberculosis, cough Antimicrobial, expectorant
Integumentary Skin infections, inflammatory skin conditions, insect bites Antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, antiseptic
Digestive Gastrointestinal parasites, digestive discomfort, digestive disorders, gastrointestinal gas, nausea Anthelmintic, carminative, antispasmodic

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory ailments such as coughs and colds.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and to relieve nausea.

Extract: Used for skin conditions and as an antiseptic.

Safety Profile

White Sage is generally considered safe when used as directed. However, it may cause gastrointestinal upset in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and should be avoided by those with known allergies to the Asteraceae family. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
