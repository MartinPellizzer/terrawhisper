White Sage (Artemisia ludoviciana)

White Sage (Artemisia ludoviciana) is a member of the Asteraceae family, native to North America, United States, and Mexico. Traditionally, its leaves, resin, and stems have been used for infusions, decoctions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its bitter, anti-inflammatory, and carminative actions, and has a long history of use in native american herbal medicine, european herbal medicine, and traditional chinese medicine.
