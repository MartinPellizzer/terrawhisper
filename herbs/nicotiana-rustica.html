Nicotiana rustica

What's the taxonomical classification of Nicotiana rustica?

Nicotiana rustica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It is situated under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, following the taxonomic lineage of the order Solanales. Within this order, the plant is a member of the family Solanaceae, specifically falling under the genus Nicotiana, with the specific epithet rusticus identifying its species.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Nicotiana rustica has an erect, herbaceous growth habit characterized by large, ovate, or lanceolate leaves with wavy margins and a slightly pubescent, sticky texture. The stems are thick, sturdy, and often covered in fine hairs that provide a tactile roughness. Its flowers are large, trumpet-shaped, and typically cream or pale yellow in color, emerging from long tubes. The plant produces numerous small seeds contained within capsules that follow the blooming period. These structural features allow the plant to thrive in various soil types while maintaining its robust stature.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, ranging from southern Mexico through Central America and into parts of South America. It is frequently encountered in diverse habitats such as disturbed roadsides, forest edges, and open grasslands. The species thrives particularly well in warm, humid climates where sunlight is abundant. Its distribution is often tied to areas with seasonal rainfall patterns that support its growth cycles. What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

What are the pharmacological activities of Nicotiana rustica?

This plant has psychoactive, repellency, and toxicity pharmacological activities due to its high concentration of alkaloids. The presence of nicotine and related compounds allows it to act as a potent neurotoxin that can be lethal to various insects and animals. These same chemical properties provide a psychoactive effect when ingested or inhaled by humans in specific doses. Furthermore, the plant serves as an effective natural repellent against many common agricultural pests. These diverse biological actions make it a significant subject for both toxicological and medicinal studies.

According to a study published by "Insects", the ethanolic extracts of Nicotiaana rustica L. contain variable amounts of phytochemicals including alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, diterpenes, phytosterol, and phenols. Total phenolic and flavonoid compounds were also observed in the plant. The toxicity of the plant was measured with an LC50 of 0.98% and an LC90 of 22.06% after a 96-h exposure period.     What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause severe physiological reactions including nausea, vomiting, dizziness, tachycardia, tremors, convulsions, and respiratory depression. Ingesting its components may lead to an abnormally rapid heart rate and significant neurological instability. These symptoms often escalate from mild gastrointestinal distress to more dangerous physical manifestations like shaking or seizures. In extreme cases, the toxins can suppress the central nervous system enough to cause life-threatening respiratory depression. Such a range of adverse effects highlights the high toxicity levels present within the species.
