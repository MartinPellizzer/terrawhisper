Desmodium rubrum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Desmodium rubrum has trifoliate leaves with elliptical leaflets, reddish-tinged stems, and small, pea-like pinkish-red flowers arranged in compact racemes. The leaves are typically arranged alternately along the slender, often reddish stems that grow in an upright or ascending manner. Each leaflet possesses a smooth margin and a pointed tip, contributing to the plant's delicate appearance. The inflorescence consists of small, dense clusters that emerge from the leaf axils. Additionally, the fruit is a small, flattened legume pod that may contain tiny hairs to facilitate dispersal.
