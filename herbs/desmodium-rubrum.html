Desmodium rubrum

What's the taxonomical classification of Desmodium rubrum?

Desmodium rubrum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Fabales. As a member of the family Fabaceae, it is specifically categorized within the genus Desmodium, with its unique identity defined by its species name, rubrum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Desmodium rubrum has trifoliate leaves with elliptical leaflets, reddish-tinged stems, and small, pea-like pinkish-red flowers arranged in compact racemes.

The leaves are typically arranged alternately along the slender, often reddish stems that grow in an upright or ascending manner. Each leaflet possesses a smooth margin and a pointed tip, contributing to the plant's delicate appearance. The inflorescence consists of small, dense clusters that emerge from the leaf axils.

Additionally, the fruit is a small, flattened legume pod that may contain tiny hairs to facilitate dispersal.
