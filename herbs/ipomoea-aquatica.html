Ipomoea aquatica

What's the taxonomical classification of Ipomoea aquatica?

Ipomoea aquatica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Solanales and is a member of the family Convolvulaceae. Finally, its taxonomic position is completed within the genus Ipomoea, specifically designated as the species aquatica.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Ipomoea aquaticus contains several bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, and tannins that contribute to its therapeutic potential. These chemical constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's documented antimicrobial, antioxidant, and anti-inflammatory properties. Research indicates that the presence of specific glycosides and phenolic compounds helps mitigate oxidative stress within biological systems. Furthermore, the alkaloids found in the leaves and roots are often studied for their potential sedative or analgesic effects in traditional medicine. These diverse chemical profiles make the species a significant subject for pharmacological investigation.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, stomach aches, and various types of inflammation. The leaves and stems are often crushed into a paste to soothe topical irritations or minor cuts. Some traditional medicinal practices also involve using extracts to manage digestive distress or intestinal issues. These applications rely on the plant's natural chemical properties to reduce swelling and discomfort. Researchers continue to study its bioactive compounds to understand how it functions against specific pathogens.
