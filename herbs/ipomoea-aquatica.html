Ipomoea aquatica

What's the taxonomical classification of Ipomoea aquatica?

Ipomoea aquatica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Solanales and is a member of the family Convolvulaceae. Finally, its taxonomic position is completed within the genus Ipomoea, specifically designated as the species aquatica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ipomoea aquatica has procumbent or climbing herbaceous stems that are typically smooth, hollow, and can reach lengths of several meters. The leaves are primarily cordate or sagittate in shape, often featuring long petioles and an entire or slightly wavy margin. Flowers are funnel-shaped and display a range of colors from white to pale blue or lavender, usually featuring a distinct yellowish throat. Small, globose capsules serve as the fruit, containing dark seeds that facilitate reproduction in wetland environments. The overall structure is adapted for creeping across moist soil or climbing through surrounding vegetation.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, spanning from the southern United States through Central America and deep into South America. It is most frequently found in moist or swampy habitats across the Caribbean islands and coastal areas of Mexico. Because it thrives in wetland environments, its range follows major river systems and marshlands throughout the neotropics. Some populations have also been introduced to other tropical climates where the warm, humid conditions mimic its natural habitat. Its presence is typically concentrated in low-lying areas where water is consistently available.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Ipomoea aquatica contains medicinal compounds primarily located in its entire leafy green foliage, succulent stems, and roots. The leaves are frequently utilized in traditional remedies to treat skin inflammations and various topical ailments. Some cultures also consume the plant as a nutritional leafy vegetable to aid digestion and provide essential vitamins. The roots can be processed to extract bioactive substances used for their cooling properties. These various parts work together to provide a wide range of therapeutic benefits in herbal medicine.

What are the pharmacological activities of Ipomoea aquatica?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological potential through its antiabetic, antifungal, antioxidant, antiradical, and cytotoxic activities. Research indicates that its chemical constituents help regulate blood glucose levels and inhibit the growth of various fungal pathogens. The presence of bioactive compounds also allows the plant to scavenge free radicals and reduce oxidative stress within biological systems. Furthermore, its cytotoxic properties suggest a capacity to target and destroy specific cell lines, which is relevant in cancer research. These diverse medicinal properties highlight the importance of investigating its complex phytochemical profile.

As discussed in a study, the aerial parts of Ipomoea aquatica were extracted sequentially using hexane, chloroform, ethyl acetate, ethanol, methanol, and distilled water. All extracts from the plant showed inhibitory activity against all fungal species except Aspergillus fumigatus, with a minimum inhibitory concentration range of 0.04-2.50 mg/mL. The antifungal activity of the plant was more prominent on yeasts than on filamentous fungi. Regarding toxicity, the methanol extract of Ipomoea aquatica did not show significant toxicity (P>0.05) towards Vero cells.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains flavonoids, glycosides, rutin, salicylic acid glucoside, and tannins which contribute to its therapeutic profile. The presence of flavonoids and tannins provides significant antioxidant and astringent properties to the specimen. Within the glycoside group, rutin serves as a specific bioactive component known for strengthening capillaries. Additionally, the salicylic acid glucoside acts as a precursor to anti-inflammatory agents. Together, these diverse medicinal compounds form a complex chemical structure used in various traditional remedies.

As discussed in a study, Ipomoea aquaticta contains various bioactive constituents such as phenols and flavonoids. Through the use of a gradient mobile phase consisting of methanol, water, and trifluoroacetic acid in a 5:95:0.05 v/v/v ratio, researchers identified several metabolites. Two flavonoids, specifically nicotiflorin and ramnazin-3-O-rutinoside, along with two phenolic compounds, dihydroxybenzoic acid pentoside and di-pentoside, were tentatively identified by QTOF-MS. The structure of rutin and nicotiflorin was further confirmed using NMR spectroscopy. This study represents the first time these two flavonoids and two phenolic compounds were tentatively identified in this species.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating cancer, diabetes, hyperglycemia, human fungal pathogens, and managing prostaglandin synthesis. Its bioactive compounds demonstrate significant potential in inhibiting the growth of various malignant cells. The plant also exhibits hypoglycemic properties that help regulate blood glucose levels and combat hyperglycemia. Furthermore, it contains antimicrobial agents effective against many human fungal pathogens. Studies suggest its chemical constituents can also influence prostaglandin synthesis to provide anti-inflammatory benefits.

What are the herbal preparations of this plant?

This plant can be prepared as an infusion, decoction, poultice, or juice to treat various ailments. An infusion of the leaves is often consumed to alleviate digestive issues or fever. For more stubborn conditions, a decoction made by boiling the roots provides a stronger concentration of active compounds. The fresh juice extracted from the crushed leaves is frequently applied topically to soothe skin irritations. Additionally, a moist poultice of the plant material can be placed directly on wounds to promote healing.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause severe gastrointestinal distress and neurological symptoms due to its inherent toxicity. Individuals who ingest it may experience acute nausea and persistent vomiting shortly after consumption. Severe bouts of diarrhea are also common as the body attempts to expel the harmful substances. Furthermore, users may suffer from significant dizziness that impairs their balance and coordination. These side effects indicate that the plant should be handled with extreme caution to avoid poisoning.

The main side effects this plant can have are shown in the list below.

  • Nausea
  • Vomiting
  • Diarrhea
  • Dizziness
  • Toxicity
