Angelica apaensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Angelica apaensis?

Angelica apaensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Magnoliidae and is a member of the order Apiales. This plant is a specific representative of the family Apiaceae and is categorized under the genus Angelica, with its unique designation being the species apaensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Angelica apaensis has an erect, herbaceous habit characterized by hollow, deeply furrowed stems and large, compound leaves that are pinnately divided into lanceolate leaflets. The foliage typically features serrated margins and a texture that can vary from glabrous to slightly pubescent depending on the specimen. Its inflorescence consists of large, terminal umbels composed of numerous small, white flowers clustered on radiating pedicels. The individual florets are actinomorphic and possess prominent nectar guides to attract pollinators. Following pollination, the plant produces small, ribbed schizocarps that serve as the primary dispersal units.
