Populus Tremula Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Populus tremula, commonly known as the trembling aspen, is a deciduous tree native to the Northern Hemisphere, known for its medicinal leaves and bark.

This herb has been traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and analgesic properties, offering relief from respiratory and digestive ailments.

The bioactive constituents in Populus tremula include salicin, flavonoids, tannins, and essential oils, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

Herbal preparations such as infusions, tinctures, and poultices can be made from its leaves and bark to harness its healing properties.
