Seneciio argunensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Senecio argunensis has a perennial growth habit characterized by basal leaves that are typically lanceolate to oblong in shape and erect flowering stems topped with yellow capitula.

The leaves possess entire or slightly dentate margins and are arranged in a rosette at the base of the plant. During the blooming stage, the inflorescence consists of multiple heads containing numerous small, tubular florets. The stems are generally slender and can reach varying heights depending on the specific environmental conditions.

These floral structures are surrounded by involucral bracts that protect the developing seeds.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Senecio argunensis can cause severe liver damage, kidney failure, and potential death due to the presence of toxic pyrrolizidine alkaloids. These chemical compounds are metabolized by the body into highly reactive substances that destroy hepatic cells. Prolonged exposure or ingestion can lead to chronic sinusoidal obstruction syndrome and irreversible scarring of the liver tissue. In addition to liver issues, these toxins may cause significant damage to the renal system. Symptoms of poisoning often manifest after a period of cumulative exposure rather than through a single instance.
