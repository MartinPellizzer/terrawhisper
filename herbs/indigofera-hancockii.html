Indigofera hancockii

What's the taxonomical classification of Indigofera hancockii?

Indigofera hancockii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is categorized under the order Fabales. This plant is a member of the family Fabaceae and falls within the genus Indigofera, specifically identified by the species name hancockii.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Indigofera hancockii can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested by humans or animals. The plant contains various alkaloids and chemical compounds that may trigger localized redness or itching upon direct contact with the skin. If swallowed, individuals might experience symptoms such as nausea, stomach cramps, or vomiting. There is also a risk of systemic toxicity if large quantities are consumed by livestock. Caution should always be exercised when handling this species to prevent unexpected physical reactions.
