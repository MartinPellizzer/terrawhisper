Sisymbrium heteromallum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sisymbrium heteromallum has an erect, branched stem that typically reaches heights of 20 to 60 centimeters and features alternate, pinnatifid leaves with deeply lobed segments. The foliage consists of basal leaves that are often larger and more complex than the smaller, simpler leaves found along the upper stem. Small, four-petaled flowers appear in terminal racemes, characterized by yellowish petals that are slightly larger than the sepals. The fruit is a slender, elongated silicle that contains numerous small seeds. These morphological features collectively distinguish this annual herb within the Brassicaceae family.
