What's the taxonomical classification of Ophioglossum petiolatum?

Ophioglossum petiolatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Ophioglossidae. Following its taxonomic descent, it falls under the order Ophioglossales and is a member of the family Ophioglossaceae. Ultimately, this specific plant is defined by its placement within the genus Ophioglossum, specifically identifying it as the species petiolatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ophioglossum petiolatum has a single, fleshy, undivided leaf consisting of a long, slender stalk called a petiole and a terminal, lanceolate fertile spike. The basal portion of the plant features a small, scale-like sterile leaf that is significantly reduced in size. This sterile component is attached to the base of the petiole and serves to protect the emerging growth. The fertile spike is densely packed with small, brownish sporangia that release spores for reproduction. The entire structure emerges directly from an underground rhizome, making the plant appear as a solitary, upright stalk.
