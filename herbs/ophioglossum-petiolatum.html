Ophioglossum petiolatum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ophioglossum petiolatum has a single, fleshy, undivided leaf consisting of a long, slender stalk called a petiole and a terminal, lanceolate fertile spike. The basal portion of the plant features a small, scale-like sterile leaf that is significantly reduced in size. This sterile component is attached to the base of the petiole and serves to protect the emerging growth. The fertile spike is densely packed with small, brownish sporangia that release spores for reproduction. The entire structure emerges directly from an underground rhizome, making the plant appear as a solitary, upright stalk.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, spanning from southern India and Sri Lanka through Myanmar, Thailand, and Malaysia to the Indonesian archipelago. It thrives primarily within the humid environments of lowland rainforests and swampy areas across these equatorial territories. The distribution follows a pattern typical of many ferns in the family Ophioglossaceae, favoring moisture-rich habitats. In certain regions, it can be found in specific microclimates where soil moisture remains consistently high. Its range is largely defined by the presence of dense tropical vegetation and stable tropical temperatures.
