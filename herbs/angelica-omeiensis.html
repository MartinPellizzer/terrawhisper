Angelica omeiensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Angelica omeiensis has a perennial herbaceous structure characterized by an erect stem that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, bearing large, compound leaves with pinnate or bipinnate divisions and serrated margins. The plant features a thick, woody rhizome that serves as its primary underground storage organ. Its inflorescences are arranged in dense, terminal umbels containing numerous small, white to pale pink flowers. Each individual flower possesses five petals and is supported by long pedicels within the cluster. The fruit is a small, dry schizocarp that typically splits into two single-seeded segments upon maturity.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the high-altitude regions of western Hunan province in China. It is primarily found growing in the mountainous terrains of the Wuling mountain range. These plants typically inhabit moist, shaded environments such as forest edges or rocky slopes. The specific elevation requirements and localized habitat make its natural range quite restricted. This limited distribution pattern makes the species a subject of interest for regional biodiversity studies.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Angelica omeiensis contains medicinal compounds primarily extracted from its dried rhizomes. These underground stems are harvested and processed to serve as a key ingredient in traditional herbal formulations. The rhizomes are valued for their ability to promote blood circulation and alleviate various types of pain. Practitioners often utilize the root to treat menstrual irregularities and gynecological disorders. Additionally, the chemical constituents within the rhizome provide antioxidant and anti-inflammatory benefits to the body.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Angelica omeiensis contains various bioactive compounds including ferulic acid, ligustilide, butylidenephthalide, and several types of coumarins. These phytochemicals are primarily concentrated in the dried root of the plant. The presence of ferulic acid provides significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties to the specimen. Ligustilide and other phthalides are noted for their ability to promote blood circulation and relieve pain. These medicinal components are frequently studied for their potential therapeutic effects on cardiovascular and neurological health.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Angelica omeiensis targets several key inflammatory and apoptotic proteins including cyclooxygenase-2, inducible nitric oxide synthase, and various caspases. Its bioactive compounds, such as ferulic acid and various ligustilides, exert their effects by modulating the NF-kappaB signaling pathway to reduce cytokine production. These phytochemicals also interact with peroxisome proliferator-activated receptors to regulate lipid metabolism and inflammatory responses. Furthermore, the plant's constituents influence the expression of tumor necrosis factor-alpha and interleukin-6, contributing to its potent immunomodulatory properties. By interacting with these specific enzymatic and signaling targets, the plant achieves its diverse therapeutic effects in cardiovascular and inflammatory disease models.

What are the therapeutic actions of Angelica omeiensis?

Angelica omeiensis has diverse therapeutic properties including analgesic, anti-inflammatory, blood invigorator, emmenagogue, diaphoric, and carminative actions. It functions as a potent blood invigorator by promoting circulation and resolving stasis within the body. The plant provides relief from physical discomfort through its analgesic and anti-inflammatory effects on various tissues. As an emmenagogue, it helps regulate menstrual cycles and promotes uterine health. It also serves as a carminative to alleviate digestive gas and a diaphoric to induce sweating for detoxification.

What are the herbal preparations of Angelica omeiensis?

Angelica omeiensis is prepared primarily as a decoction, a concentrated liquid extract made by boiling the dried root in water, or as a powder derived from finely grinding the dried root. In traditional Chinese medicine, these roots are often sliced and dried before being steeped to release their active chemical constituents. Practitioners may also incorporate the plant into medicinal wines or tinctures through alcohol-based extractions to improve shelf life and potency. Some formulations include the powdered herb mixed into honey to create pills for easier administration. These various forms allow for different rates of absorption and specific therapeutic applications depending on the patient's needs.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Angelica omeiensis can cause gastrointestinal distress, skin sensitivity to sunlight, and potential interference with blood clotting mechanisms. Some individuals may experience nausea, diarrhea, or abdominal cramping following consumption. Because the plant contains furanocoumarins, it can increase the risk of photosensitivity and resulting skin rashes when exposed to UV rays. It may also have anticoagulant properties that increase the risk of bleeding if taken with blood-thinning medications. Pregnant women should avoid it due to concerns regarding uterine stimulation.
