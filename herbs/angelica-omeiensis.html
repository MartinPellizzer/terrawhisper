Angelica omeiensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Angelica omeiensis?

Angelica omeiensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent through the order Apiales, it is a member of the family Apiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified under the genus Angelica, with its specific designation being the species omeiensis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Angelica omeiensis contains various bioactive compounds including ferulic acid, ligustilide, butylidenephthalide, and several types of coumarins. These phytochemicals are primarily concentrated in the dried root of the plant. The presence of ferulic acid provides significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties to the specimen. Ligustilide and other phthalides are noted for their ability to promote blood circulation and relieve pain. These medicinal components are frequently studied for their potential therapeutic effects on cardiovascular and neurological health.

What are the therapeutic actions of Angelica omeiensis?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat gynecological disorders, blood circulation issues, and various types of pain. It is frequently employed in traditional medicine to regulate menstruation and alleviate symptoms associated with menstrual deficiency. The herb helps to invigorate the blood and dispel stasis, which can assist in treating abdominal pain and localized swelling. Additionally, its properties are utilized to improve peripheral circulation and mitigate discomfort caused by bodily stagnation. Medical practitioners often prescribe it to support overall blood health and systemic vitality.
