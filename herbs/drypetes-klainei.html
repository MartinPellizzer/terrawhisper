Drypetes klainei

What's the taxonomical classification of Drypetes klainei?

Drypetes klainei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Malpigiales. The plant is further categorized under the family Putranjivaceae, falling into the genus Drypetes, and is specifically identified by its species name, klainei.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Drypetes klainei has simple, alternate, coriaceous leaves that are typically elliptic to oblong in shape with entire margins and obtuse or rounded apices.

Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in axillary clusters, often appearing in a dioecious manner where male and female reproductive organs are on separate plants. The fruit develops into a fleshy drupe that transitions in color as it matures from green to a darker shade.
