Spiraea hypericifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Spiraea hypericifolia has small, elliptical to lanceolate leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along woody, upright stems. The foliage is characterized by finely serrated margins and a texture that can range from smooth to slightly rugose. Its flowers are small, white, and arranged in dense, terminal corymb-like clusters. The plant often displays a spreading or bushy growth habit with slender branches. These morphological features collectively define its delicate appearance within its natural habitat.
