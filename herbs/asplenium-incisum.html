Asplenium incisum

What's the taxonomical classification of Asplenium incisum?

Asplenium incisum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized under the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. This plant is a representative of the family Aspleniaceae, falling under the genus Asplenium, with its specific designation being the species incisum.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various skin conditions including boils, fungal infections, and inflammatory rashes. The medicinal properties of the fern are often applied topically to soothe irritated skin surfaces. Some practitioners also utilize its extracts to manage minor wounds or localized swellings. These traditional applications focus on the plant's natural antiseptic and anti-inflammatory qualities. Careful preparation is necessary to ensure the botanical extracts are effective for dermatological relief.
