Marmoritis rotundifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Marmoritis rotundifolia has small, circular to broadly ovate leaves that are densely covered in fine, silvery-white hairs. These leaves are typically arranged oppositely along thin, woody stems that grow in a low-spreading or cushion-like habit. The foliage often exhibits a grayish-green hue due to the thick layer of trichomes providing protection against intense sunlight. During the flowering season, the plant produces tiny, tubular flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These delicate blossoms are generally white or pale pink and are characteristic of its compact, rock-dwelling structure.
