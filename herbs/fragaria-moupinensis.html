Fragaria moupinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Fragaria moupinensis?

Fragaria moupinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Rosales. This plant is a part of the family Rosaceae, which is further refined into the genus Fragaria, ultimately reaching its specific identification at the species level as moupinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Fragaria moupinensis has a perennial growth habit characterized by stolons that form dense mats, trifoliate leaves with long petioles, and small white flowers with five petals. The leaves are typically dark green and possess serrated margins that help distinguish the species from other wild strawberries. Its inflorescence consists of small, delicate blossoms that eventually develop into aggregate fruits known as strawberries. These fleshy red fruits are covered in tiny achenes on the surface and are often smaller than those of cultivated varieties. The overall structure of the plant allows it to spread efficiently across forest floors and rocky slopes.
