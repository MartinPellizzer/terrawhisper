Cyclea racemosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Cyclea racemosa?

Cyclea racemosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its hierarchical progression continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Ranunculales. Within this botanical framework, the plant is a member of the family Menispermaceae. Specifically, it is categorized under the genus Cyclea, with its unique identity defined by the species name racemosa.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Cyclea racemosa contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, terpenoids, and phenolic compounds that contribute to its pharmacological properties. Research indicates that these constituents provide significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities within biological systems. Certain extracts from the plant have demonstrated antimicrobial potential against specific bacterial strains. Additionally, the presence of unique alkaloids may influence various physiological processes in traditional medicinal applications. These chemical profiles support the plant's historical use in treating diverse ailments in folk medicine.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, inflammation, and various types of digestive ailments. Its bioactive compounds are often applied topically to promote the healing of cuts and dermatological issues. In some traditional practices, extracts are consumed to manage internal discomfort and gastrointestinal problems. Research into its chemical properties suggests potential applications for managing localized swelling and irritation. These medicinal uses highlight its importance in regional folk medicine for addressing both external and internal health concerns.
