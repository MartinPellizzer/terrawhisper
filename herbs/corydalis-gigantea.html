Corydalis gigantea

What's the taxonomical classification of Corydalis gigantea?

Corydalis gigantea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Ranunculales and is a member of the family Papaveraceae. Ultimately, it is identified by the genus Corydalis and the specific species epithet gigantea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Corydalis gigantea has a perennial herbaceous structure characterized by large, pinnately compound leaves and long, nectar-filled tubular flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in length. The foliage is composed of numerous leaflets that create a lush, spreading canopy over the ground. Its most striking feature is the zygomorphic flower, which typically exhibits shades of yellow or pale orange. These blossoms are held upright on sturdy pedicels and are specifically adapted for pollination by long-tongued insects. The plant's root system consists of thickened tubers that serve as energy storage organs for the species.
