Ligularia lankongensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ligularia lankongensis has large, cordate leaves that are characterized by coarsely serrated margins and a densely tomentose, lanate surface. The foliage is typically dark green on the upper side while the underside is covered in thick, white felt-like hairs. These long-petioled leaves are arranged in a basal rosette pattern along the stem. The plant produces upright flowering stalks that terminate in bright yellow capitula. Each flower head is composed of numerous small disc florets surrounded by ray florets.
