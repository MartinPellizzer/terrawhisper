What is Glechoma calaminthodes?

Glechoma calaminthodes is a perennial herbaceous plant that belongs to the family Lamiaceae.

It has a taproot and erect or ascending stems, 10-30 cm long, with quadrangular, 4-angled branches. The leaves are opposite, 2-5 cm long, lanceolate to elliptic in shape, with entire margins, and a short petiole. The inflorescence is a corymb with axillary, zygomorphic flowers, 2-3 mm in diameter, with a 2-lipped corolla, and a 4-merous calyx.

The flowers are purple with a white or yellowish throat and a spur.
