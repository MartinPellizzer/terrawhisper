What's the taxonomical classification of Rhamnella franguloides?

Rhamnella franguloides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the species falls under the order Rosales and is a member of the family Rhamnaceae. Ultimately, its biological classification is completed by its placement within the genus Rhamnella, with its specific designation being the species franguloides.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhamnella franguloides has simple, opposite, elliptical leaves with entire margins and acute apices.

The plant is characterized by its woody structure and slender branches that support these densely arranged foliage clusters. The leaf blades feature prominent pinnate venation and a smooth, slightly coriaceous texture. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axillary positions along the stems.

The fruit develops as a fleshy drupe that typically transitions in color as it matures.
