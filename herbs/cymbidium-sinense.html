Cymbidium sinense

What's the taxonomical classification of Cymbidium sinense?

Cymbidium sinense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized into the order Asparagales. As a member of the family Orchidaceae, it is placed within the genus Cymbidium, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species sinense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cymbidium sinense has elongated, leathery leaves that grow in a fan-like arrangement from a thick, creeping rhizome. These leaves are typically dark green and can reach significant lengths depending on the specific cultivar. The plant produces upright flower spikes that emerge from the base of the foliage to bear numerous fragrant blooms. Each individual flower features a complex structure with a prominent labellum and often displays striking patterns of white, pink, or purple. The roots are fleshy and adapted to cling to organic matter or rocky surfaces in its natural habitat.
