Arenaria capillaris

What's the taxonomical classification of Arenaria capillaris?

Arenaria capillaris belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy places it under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Caryophyllales, it is a member of the family Caryophyllaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Arenaria, with its specific designation being the species capillaris.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Arenaaria capillaris has a slender, caespitose growth habit characterized by thin, hair-like stems and small, linear leaves that form dense tufts. The plant features delicate, branching stems that often spread horizontally or ascend slightly above the ground. Its leaves are typically opposite and very narrow, giving the foliage a fine, moss-like texture. Tiny, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils on short pedicels. The overall structure is compact and diminutive, allowing it to thrive in rocky or sandy substrates.
