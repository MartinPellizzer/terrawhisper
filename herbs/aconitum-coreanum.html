Aconitum coreanum

What's the taxonomical classification of Aconitum coreanum?

Aconitum coreanum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its evolutionary lineage continues through the order Ranunculales and places it within the family Ranunculaceae. Specifically, this plant is identified as part of the genus Aconitum, with its unique designation being the species coreanum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aconitum coreanum has a perennial herbaceous structure characterized by basal leaves that are palmately lobed and deeply divided into narrow segments. The plant features an upright stem that supports terminal clusters of zygomorphic flowers ranging in color from pale blue to violet. Each blossom is defined by a prominent, helmet-shaped upper sepal that protects the inner reproductive organs. The leaf margins are typically serrated, providing a textured appearance to the foliage. Its root system consists of a thickened tuberous rhizome which serves as a primary nutrient storage organ.

What are the therapeutic actions of Aconitum coreanum?

    What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

    This plant contains the medicinal compounds atisine, hypaconitine, Aconitum coreanum polysaccharide, guanfu A, and a significant concentration of total alkaloid. These specific substances are primarily categorized as complex alkaloids that drive the plant's pharmacological activity. The presence of guanfu A and hypaconitine is particularly notable for their roles in various traditional medicinal applications. Additionally, the Aconitum coreanum polysaccharide contributes unique biochemical properties to its chemical profile. Together, these diverse constituents form a potent medicinal complex that requires careful study and regulation.

    As discussed in a study, six alkaloids were isolated from 2 g of crude extracts from the roots of Aconitum coreanum. These compounds included 10.4 mg of Guanfu base P, 9.2 mg of Guanfu base G, 9.5 mg of Guanfu base F, 8.9 mg of atisine, 11.9 mg of Guanfu base A, and 25.7 mg of Guanfu base I. The purity of these six alkaloids was measured at 96.9%, 95.7%, 91.5%, 98.9%, 95.8%, and 95.5% using HPLC. The isolation process utilized a two-phase solvent system of n-hexane-ethyl acetate-methanol-0.2M HCl in a ratio of 1:3.5:2:4.5, v/v/v/v. Chemical structures were subsequently identified through MS, (1)H NMR, and (13)C NMR.

        What side effects this plant can have?

        This plant can cause severe toxicity characterized by nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, dizziness, arrhythmia, bradycardia, hypotension, respiratory depression, paralysis, convulsions, and death. The ingestion of aconitum coreanum leads to rapid gastrointestinal distress and neurological impairment. As the toxins affect the heart, patients may experience dangerous irregular rhythms and a significant drop in blood pressure. Severe cases progress to life-threatening respiratory failure and muscle paralysis. Ultimately, the intense neurological disruptions can cause uncontrollable convulsions and fatal outcomes.
