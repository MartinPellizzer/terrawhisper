Hydrangea davidii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hydrangea davidii has woody, deciduous stems that support large, oppositely arranged elliptical leaves with serrated margins and prominent venation. The foliage typically displays a deep green color and can reach significant sizes depending on the growing environment. Large, terminal corymb inflorescences emerge in late summer, consisting of numerous small, fertile flowers surrounded by showy, sterile sepals. These flower heads often transition through various shades of pink, white, or light blue. The overall structure of the plant tends to be an upright, spreading shrub with a multi-stemmed growth habit.
