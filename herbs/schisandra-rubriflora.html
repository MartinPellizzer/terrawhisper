Schisandra rubriflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Schisandra rubriflora?

Schisandra rubriflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized into the order Austrobaileyales and belongs to the family Schisandraceae. Finally, its taxonomic identity is completed by its placement within the genus Schisandra, specifically identifying it as the species rubriflora.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Following pollination, the plant develops aggregate fruits that consist of multiple fleshy red drupes. What are the pharmacological activities of Schisandra rubriflora?

This plant has anti-HIV, anti-HIV-1 activity, anticancer, and antioxidant pharmacological activities. Researchers have identified specific bioactive compounds within Schisandra rubriflora that contribute to these diverse biological functions. Its antioxidant properties help mitigate oxidative stress by neutralizing harmful free radicals in cellular environments. Furthermore, studies suggest that its extracts possess significant potential in inhibiting the replication of HIV-1 viruses. The plant also shows promise in anticancer research due to its ability to interfere with various tumor cell processes.

As discussed in a study, bioactivity-directed fractionation of an ethanolic extract of the fruits of Schisandra rubriflora led to the identification of several dibenzocyclooctadiene lignans. Compounds 1 and 3-6 were found to be active as anti-HIV agents. Among these, (+/-)-gomisin M1 (4) exhibited the most potent anti-HIV activity. This specific compound demonstrated EC50 and therapeutic index (TI) values of <0.65 microM and >68, respectively.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds Schisandrin, gomisin M2, gomisin M1, deoxyschisandriin, and rubrifloradilactone C. These specific lignans and lactones are responsible for many of the biological activities associated with the species. Researchers study these molecules to understand their potential antioxidant and neuroprotective effects on human cells. Each compound plays a unique role in the complex chemical profile of the plant's berries and roots. Understanding the concentration of these substances helps in the development of standardized herbal extracts.

As discussed in a study, the fruits of Schisandra rubriflora contain specific lignans including schisantherin A, deoxyschizandrin, and gamma-schizandrin. The HPLC separation process used a Diamonsil C18 column measuring 150 mm x 4.6 mm with a 5 microm particle size.
