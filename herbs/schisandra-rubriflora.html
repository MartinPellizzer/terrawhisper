Schisandra rubriflora

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Schisandra rubriflora has woody, climbing vines characterized by alternate, simple leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The foliage features smooth margins and a glossy texture, often growing in dense clusters along the stems. Small, fragrant flowers emerge from the axils, displaying distinct reddish or pinkish hues that define the species. Following pollination, the plant develops aggregate fruits that consist of multiple fleshy red drupes. These botanical features collectively allow the climber to navigate through forest understories effectively.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the temperate forest regions of eastern China, where it grows in specific mountainous habitats. Its population is primarily concentrated within the provinces of Zhejiang and Fujian. These plants typically thrive in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories and ravine edges. The specific elevation and soil composition of these East Asian mountain ranges dictate its localized spread. Because it is endemic to this limited area, its natural distribution remains highly restricted.
