Adenophora polyantha

What's the taxonomical classification of Adenophora polyantha?

Adenophora polyantha belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it resides in the order Asterales and is a member of the family Campanulaceae. Specifically, this plant is identified by the genus Adenophora, with its unique biological designation being the species polyantha.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Adenophora polyantha has perennial herbaceous stems that reach heights of up to 30 centimeters, supporting opposite or whorled lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is typically green and features distinct venation patterns across the leaf surface. Its most defining characteristic is the inflorescence, which consists of multiple small, bell-shaped purple or bluish flowers clustered at the terminal ends of the stems. Each individual flower possesses a tubular corolla that flares into five distinct lobes. These floral structures are supported by slender pedicels that emerge from the leaf axils.
