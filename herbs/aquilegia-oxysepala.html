Aquilegia oxysepala

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aquilegia oxysepala has relatively small, blue to violet flowers characterized by sharp, pointed spurs and shallowly lobed petals. The foliage consists of compound leaves with multiple leaflets that possess distinctively narrow, pointed lobes. Stems are slender and often grow in a basal rosette formation before ascending to support the blossoms. The plant typically develops a compact growth habit suitable for rocky or alpine environments.
