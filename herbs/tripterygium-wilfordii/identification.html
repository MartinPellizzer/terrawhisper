Botanical Identification of Fleeceflower (Tripterygium wilfordii)

To identify Fleeceflower (Tripterygium wilfordii) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Fleeceflower (Tripterygium wilfordii) Monograph

Scientific Classification

Fleeceflower, or Tripterygium wilfordii, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Celastrales, family Celastraceae, genus Tripterygium, and species Tripterygium wilfordii. This classification places it within the flowering plants, specifically among the eudicots, which are characterized by two seed leaves and other distinct features.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The botanical name of Fleeceflower is Tripterygium wilfordii, a binomial classification introduced by the botanist Wilford. This name is widely accepted in modern botanical literature and serves as the standard scientific identifier for the species.

Morphological Characteristics

Tripterygium wilfordii is a perennial herb with a fibrous root system. It has erect, branched stems that are woody at the base. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a serrated margin. The inflorescence is a dense cluster of yellow-green flowers. The fruit is a capsule containing small, elliptical seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Tripterygium wilfordii can be distinguished by its distinctive yellow flowers with five narrow lobes, opposite leaves with serrated edges, and the presence of milky sap. It differs from similar species like Tripterygium yamazakii by its smaller, more clustered flowers and shorter, more rigid stems. These features are key for accurate identification in herbal medicine practice.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Fleeceflower is native to China, particularly in the Yangtze River valley. It thrives in temperate climates with mild summers and cool winters. It prefers well-drained, loamy soil. It is typically found at altitudes between 500 and 2,000 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Fleeceflower blooms from May to June, producing yellowish-green flowers. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early autumn. For identification purposes, the best time to harvest is during the flowering period when the plant's distinguishing features are most visible.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Tripterygium wilfordii belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, closely related to species like Tripterygium yunnanense and Tripterygium hypoglaucum. It shares morphological and genetic traits with other members of the genus, particularly in its structure and chemical composition. These relationships help in understanding its evolutionary placement and medicinal properties.
