Erigeron multiradiatus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Erigeron multiradiatus has a perennial growth habit characterized by a woody caudex, basal leaves that are typically spatulate or oblanceolate with entire margins, and numerous narrow, linear leaves distributed along its stems. The inflorescence consists of small heads arranged in corymbiform clusters, each featuring numerous white to pale lavender ray florets surrounding a central disc of yellow tubular florets. The involucral bracts are arranged in several overlapping series and are often tipped with distinct, darkened points. Stems are generally erect and can vary in pubescence, ranging from glabrous to being covered in fine, spreading hairs. The overall structure is compact, reflecting its adaptation to specific montane or rocky environments.
