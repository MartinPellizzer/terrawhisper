Matricaria chamomilla

What's the taxonomical classification of Matricaria chamomilla?

Matricaria chamomiilla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized into the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae, which is further narrowed down to the genus Matricaria, with its specific designation being the species chamomilla.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Matricaria chamomilla contains high concentrations of terpenoids, flavonoids, and coumarins that provide its therapeutic effects. The essential oil is primarily composed of alpha-bisabolol and chamazulene, which contribute to its significant anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Flavonoids such as apigenin bind to benzodiazepine receptors in the brain to induce a calming, sedative effect. These bioactive compounds work together to soothe digestive distress and reduce skin irritation when applied topically. Regular use of these phytochemicals helps mitigate oxidative stress within bodily tissues.

What are the therapeutic actions of Matricaria chamomilla?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat gastrointestinal disorders, anxiety, insomnia, skin inflammation, and various common colds. Its anti-inflammatory properties help soothe digestive issues like indigestion, gas, and stomach cramps. When applied topically, the plant can alleviate skin irritation, eczema, and minor wounds. The sedative effects of its compounds are often utilized to reduce mild anxiety and promote better sleep quality. Additionally, its antimicrobial qualities assist the body in managing respiratory symptoms associated with minor infections.
