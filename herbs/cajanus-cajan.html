Cowpea (Cajanus cajan)

Cowpea (Cajanus cajan) is a member of the Fabaceae family, native to India, Southeast Asia, and Tropical Africa. Traditionally, its leaves, seeds, and shoots have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, tonic, and diuretic actions, and has a long history of use in ayurvedic medicine, african traditional medicine, and european herbal medicine.

Flowers
Bilaterally symmetrical flowers with five petals arranged in a papilionaceous corolla, featuring a distinct banner, wing, and standard petal, and a club-shaped keel, with violet to purple coloration.
Stems
Erect growth habit with opposite branching, smooth surface, and nodes bearing stipitate stipules.
