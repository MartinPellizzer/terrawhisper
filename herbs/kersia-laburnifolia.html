Kersia laburnifolia has many benefits, such as its extracts being used in traditional medicine to alleviate symptoms of fever, cough, and digestive issues, as well as its anti-inflammatory properties helping to reduce pain and swelling.

Additionally, the plant has been used to treat skin conditions such as eczema and acne, and its antimicrobial properties can help to prevent the growth of bacteria and fungi that can cause infections.

Possible Side Effects of Kersia laburnifolia

Kersia laburnifolia can have side effects if used improperly, such as causing gastrointestinal upset, nausea, and diarrhea due to its high toxicity levels.

Prolonged consumption of the plant can lead to liver and kidney damage, as well as interact negatively with certain medications.

In severe cases, improper use of Kersia laburnifolia can result in seizures, coma, or even death.

Additionally, skin contact with the plant's sap can cause irritation, rashes, and allergic reactions in some individuals.
