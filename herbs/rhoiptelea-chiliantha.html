Rhoiptelea chiliantha

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhoiptelea chiliantha has an erect, woody shrub habit characterized by opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins and a distinctively pungent, chili-like scent. The foliage is typically dark green and possesses a leathery texture that aids in moisture retention. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves to form compact inflorescences. These blooms eventually develop into small, fleshy fruits that serve as a primary reproductive feature of the species. The overall structure of the plant is compact, allowing it to thrive in specific subtropical ecological niches.
