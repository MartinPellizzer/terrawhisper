Flemingia wallichii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Flemingia wallichii has erect, woody stems that typically reach heights of 1 to 3 meters and feature pinnately compound leaves with 7 to 13 leaflets. Each leaflet is ovate or elliptical in shape, possessing an acute apex and a smooth, entire margin. The plant produces small, bright yellow papilionaceous flowers that are clustered in dense axillary racemes. These floral structures are characteristic of the Fabaceae family and often emerge during the warmer seasons. The root system is robust and capable of supporting the plant's upright growth in diverse soil conditions.
