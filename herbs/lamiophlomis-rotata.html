What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lamiophlomis rotata has square stems, opposite leaves, and large, tubular, violet-blue flowers arranged in dense, terminal whorls. The foliage consists of rugose, ovate leaves with crenate margins that are typically dark green and textured. Its inflorescence features prominent bilabiate corollas that emerge from bracts, creating a striking visual display. The plant's woody base and spreading habit allow it to form substantial clumps over time. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds acteoside, loganin, pensdemoside, shanzhiside methyl ester, and verbascoside. These specific chemical constituents are known for their diverse biological activities within the human body. Research indicates that these phenylethanoid glycosides often contribute to antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The presence of loganin and acteoside is particularly significant for the plant's therapeutic profile. Furthermore, the combination of these compounds provides a complex chemical foundation for pharmacological study.
