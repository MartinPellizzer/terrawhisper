Lamiophlomis rotaata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lamiophlomis rotata has square stems, opposite leaves, and large, tubular, violet-blue flowers arranged in dense, terminal whorls. The foliage consists of rugose, ovate leaves with crenate margins that are typically dark green and textured. Its inflorescence features prominent bilabiate corollas that emerge from bracts, creating a striking visual display. The plant's woody base and spreading habit allow it to form substantial clumps over time. These morphological characteristics are typical of the Lamiaceae family, emphasizing its structural complexity.
