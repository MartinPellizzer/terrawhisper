Eurysolen gracilis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eurysolen gracilis has slender, elongated stems that support small, lanceolate leaves arranged in an alternate pattern along the axis. The foliage is characterized by a smooth texture and a pale green coloration that reflects its delicate structure. Tiny, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often appearing in small clusters. The root system is fine and fibrous, allowing the plant to anchor itself effectively in loose substrates. Overall, its morphology is defined by a lightweight and airy habit.

      What are the herbal preparations of Eurysolen gracilis?

      Eurysolen gracilis is prepared as a concentrated aqueous decoction, a dried powdered extract for capsule filling, or a topical oleaginous salve. The decoction involves simmering the woody stems in water to extract soluble alkaloids for oral consumption.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Eurysolen gracilis can cause severe gastrointestinal distress including intense abdominal cramping, persistent nausea, and acute vomiting if ingested. These primary symptoms are often accompanied by significant dehydration due to the rapid loss of fluids. Some individuals may also experience localized skin irritation or a burning sensation if the plant's sap makes direct contact with the flesh. Prolonged exposure to the toxins within the plant can lead to more systemic neurological symptoms such as dizziness or lightheadedness. It is critical to avoid all contact with this species to prevent these adverse reactions.
