What is Helonias lutea?

Helonias lutea is a perennial plant species within the family Melanthiaceae, commonly known as the yellow false unicorn or false unicorn root.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers, which are borne in a terminal raceme, and its leaves, which are lanceolate in shape and alternate in arrangement. The plant grows to a height of 30-60 cm, and its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine. Helonias lutea is native to eastern North America, specifically in the southeastern United States and the Gulf Coast region.

Its botanical name, Helonias lutea, is derived from the Greek word 'helone', meaning 'marsh plant', and 'lutea', meaning 'yellow'.
