Dillenia turbinata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dillenia turbinata has large, leathery, obovate leaves that measure up to 40 centimeters in length and possess distinctively serrated margins. The foliage is characterized by prominent, deeply impressed venation that creates a textured surface on both the upper and lower sides. Its reproductive structures consist of large, showy white flowers that emerge from woody stems to attract specific pollinators. Following flowering, the plant develops distinctive fleshy, globose fruits that transition in color as they mature. These morphological features collectively support its growth in humid, tropical forest environments.
