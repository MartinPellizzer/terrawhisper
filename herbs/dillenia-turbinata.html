Dillenia turbinata

What's the taxonomical classification of Dillenia turbinata?

Dillenia turbinata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Dilleniales and is a member of the family Dilleniaceae. Specifically, it is placed within the genus Dillenia, with its unique biological identity defined by its species name, turbinata.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Dillenia turbinata contains high concentrations of bioactive phytochemicals including flavonoids, tannins, saponins, and various phenolic compounds. These secondary metabolites contribute to the plant's significant antioxidant and antimicrobial properties. Researchers have noted that the extracts often exhibit potential for reducing oxidative stress within biological systems. Some studies suggest these chemical constituents may also possess anti-inflammatory effects useful in traditional medicine. The specific profile of these compounds varies depending on the plant part used for extraction.

What are the therapeutic actions of Dillenia turbinata?

Dillenia turbinata has potent antimicrobial, antioxidant, and anti-inflammatory properties that are utilized to treat various skin conditions and internal ailments. The plant contains bioactive compounds like flavonoids and polyphenols which help neutralize harmful free radicals in the body. These chemical constituents also inhibit the growth of pathogenic bacteria and fungi, making it useful for wound healing. Many traditional practices employ the leaves and bark to reduce swelling and alleviate pain in localized areas. Ongoing research continues to investigate how these botanical extracts can support metabolic health and immune function.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, and various inflammatory conditions through its medicinal properties. The leaves and bark are often applied topically to promote the healing of cuts and abrasions. Its extracts are also utilized in traditional medicine to alleviate symptoms of gastrointestinal distress. Some regional practices involve using the plant to manage swelling and localized pain. These therapeutic applications rely on the presence of bioactive compounds within the botanical material.
