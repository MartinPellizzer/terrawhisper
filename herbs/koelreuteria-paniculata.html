What's the taxonomical classification of Koelreuteria paniculata?

Koelreuteria paniculata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls into the subclass Magnoliidae and is categorized under the order Sapindales. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the family Sapindaceae, which contains the genus Koelreuteria, ultimately identifying this specific plant as the species Koelreuteria paniculata.

What are the herbal preparations of Koelreuteria paniculata?

Koelreuteria paniculata is prepared primarily as a decoction through boiling the dried bark or leaves in water to extract its bioactive compounds. These aqueous extracts can also be processed into concentrated tinctures using ethanol as a solvent for long-term preservation. Some traditional applications involve using the powdered form of the dried plant material for topical applications or as a component in herbal poultices. Additionally, the plant material may be infused into oils to facilitate transdermal absorption of its constituents. These various preparations allow for different methods of administering the plant's medicinal properties.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Koelreuteria paniculata can cause skin irritation or allergic contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals if their skin comes into direct contact with the plant material. While it is primarily grown as an ornamental shrub, some people may experience redness, itching, or a rash after touching the leaves or stems. There is limited scientific data regarding the systemic toxicity of this plant if ingested, so caution is advised. It is generally recommended to avoid consuming any part of the plant to prevent unknown gastrointestinal distress. Always wash your hands thoroughly after handling the shrub to minimize the risk of a reaction.
