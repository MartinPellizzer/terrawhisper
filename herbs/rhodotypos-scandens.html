Rhodotypos scandens

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhodotypos scandens has slender, climbing stems that produce opposite, elliptical to lanceolate leaves with entire margins and distinctively parallel venation. The foliage is typically bright green and can grow quite long as the plant scrambles over surrounding vegetation. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils, displaying a delicate pink or pale purple coloration. These blossoms are characterized by their narrow shape and are often clustered along the stem. The overall structure of the plant is light and herbaceous, allowing it to weave through dense habitats.
