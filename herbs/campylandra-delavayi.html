Campylandra delavayi has a woody, perennial habit characterized by erect stems that branch extensively from the base. The plant often features fine pubescence on its stems and leaf surfaces, providing a subtle texture to its overall appearance.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Campylandra delavayi can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, localized skin irritation, and potential neurological impairment if ingested or handled improperly. Contact with the plant's sap often results in contact dermatitis, manifesting as redness, itching, or painful blistering on the skin. When consumed, the chemical compounds within the foliage may trigger nausea, vomiting, and intense abdominal cramping. Some documented cases suggest that high doses could lead to dizziness or temporary disorientation in sensitive individuals. Due to these unpredictable reactions, strict caution is required when interacting with this species.
