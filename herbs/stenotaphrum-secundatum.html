What's the taxonomical classification of Stenotaphrum secundatum?

Stenotaphrum secundatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its hierarchical lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Poales. As a member of the family Poaceae, this plant is further categorized under the genus Stenotaphrum, with its specific designation being the species secundatum.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Stenotaphrum secundatum can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. The plant contains phytochemicals that may trigger contact dermatitis in individuals with sensitive skin. If swallowed, the substance can lead to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. There is also a potential for systemic toxicity depending on the dosage and the individual's biological sensitivity. It is advisable to handle this species with caution to avoid unintended physical contact.
