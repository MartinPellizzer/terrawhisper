Stenotaphrum secundatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Stenotaphrum secundatum?

Stenotaphrum secundatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its hierarchical lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Poales. As a member of the family Poaceae, this plant is further categorized under the genus Stenotaphrum, with its specific designation being the species secundatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stenotaphrum secundatum has an erect, slender herbaceous habit characterized by narrow, linear leaves and small, zygomorphic flowers arranged in dense, one-sided racemes.

The leaves are typically lanceolate to linear and emerge from a stem that can reach varying heights depending on the environment. Its floral structure features a tubular corolla that is often slightly curved, reflecting its asymmetrical presentation. The inflorescence is distinctly secund, meaning the flowers are oriented toward one side of the central axis.

Small, inconspicuous bracts frequently accompany the individual florets along the flowering stalk.
