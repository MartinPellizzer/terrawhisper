Cyanotis cristata

What's the taxonomical classification of Cyanotis cristata?

Cyanotis cristata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent through the order Commelinales, it is placed in the family Commelinaceae. Finally, the plant is identified within the genus Cyanotis, with its specific designation being the species cristata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cyanotis cristata has succulent, creeping stems that produce small, lanceolate leaves arranged in a rosette or along the stems with distinctively hairy or ciliate margins. The foliage is typically a vibrant green and features a fleshy texture that helps the plant retain moisture. Tiny, three-petaled flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often displaying shades of blue or violet. These blossoms are characterized by their delicate structure and are frequently accompanied by prominent, hairy filaments. The overall growth habit is low-lying and spreading, making it ideal for ground cover in moist environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southern and Eastern Africa, specifically spanning across countries like South Africa, Zimbabwe, and Mozambique. It thrives primarily in moist habitats such as riverbanks, marshes, and damp grasslands where water is consistently available. The species is often found in low-lying areas where the soil remains saturated for much of the growing season. Its distribution is closely tied to specific humidity levels and seasonal rainfall patterns characteristic of these African climates. What are the molecular targets of this plant?
