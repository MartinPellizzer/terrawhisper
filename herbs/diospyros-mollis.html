What's the taxonomical classification of Diospyros mollis?

Diospyros mollis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical structure of botanical classification, it falls under the order Ericales and is a member of the family Ebenaceae. Finally, the plant is situated within the genus Diospyrpyos, with its specific identification being the species mollis.

Its fruit is a fleshy berry that matures into a dark color, containing one or more large seeds. The woody stems and branches exhibit a brownish bark that provides a sturdy structure for the foliage.

    What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

    This plant contains the medicinal compounds diospyrol glycoside, makluoside A, makluoside B, makluoside C, and makluoside D. These specific chemical constituents belong to a class of glycosides often studied for their unique biological properties. Research into these compounds focuses on how they interact with various cellular pathways. Scientists examine these molecules to determine if they can be developed into therapeutic agents. Understanding the structural complexity of these substances is essential for future pharmacological applications.

    As discussed in a study, methanol extracts from the leaves and branches of Diospyros mollis yielded 7 triterpene and 22 aromatic compounds, which included 5 new compounds. These new compounds were identified as diospyrol glycoside (makluoside A, 1), 8,8'-di-O-6-beta-D-apiofuranosyl-beta-D-glucopyranosyl-6,6'-dimethyl-2,3'-binaphthalene-1-ol-1',4'-dione (makluoside B, 2), and 3-methyl-1,8-naphthalenediol glycosides (makluosides C-E, 3-5). Makluoside B represents the first example of a naphthoquinone glycoside containing both a 3-methyl-1,8-naphthalenediol unit and a 5-hydroxy-7-methyl-1,4-naphthoquinone unit. Evaluations of hyaluronidase inhibitory activity showed that 1 of the triterpene derivatives possessed moderate inhibitory activity.
