Kola nut

Cola nitida

Kola Nut (Cola nitida) is a tropical evergreen tree native to West Africa, known for its stimulant properties and traditional use in treating conditions such as anxiety, insomnia, and digestive issues.

Botanical Identification

Kola Nut (Cola nitida) is a tropical evergreen tree native to West Africa. It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is also known as African coffee or cola tree. The plant produces large, elliptical leaves and yellow flowers, with a distinctive nut-like seed used in traditional herbal medicine.

The Kola Nut contains key bioactive compounds such as alkaloids, flavonoids, and tannins, which contribute to its medicinal properties. Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and as a general tonic.

Infusion: Used for respiratory conditions and to treat coughs.

Extract: Used for its stimulant properties and to treat fatigue.

Safety Profile

Kola Nut (Cola nitida) should be used with caution due to its stimulant properties. It is contraindicated in individuals with hypertension, anxiety disorders, or those sensitive to caffeine. Prolonged use may lead to dependency and adverse cardiovascular effects.
