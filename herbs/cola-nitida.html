Side Effects of cola nitida

Cola nitida causes diarrhea due to its high concentration of caffeine and other stimulant compounds that accelerate intestinal motility.

This herb can also trigger heartburn by relaxing the lower esophageal sphincter, allowing stomach acid to reflux into the esophagus. The stimulant effects of Cola nitida may lead to fatigue as the body struggles to process the increased metabolic demands. In some cases, the herb induces vomiting, which can result in dehydration and further complications.

Additionally, Cola nitida brings about dry mouth and leads to nausea, as the body's response to its potent compounds can disrupt normal digestive processes. It may also induce dizziness and anxiety, as the nervous system becomes overstimulated. Muscle cramps and results in sweating are common side effects due to the herb's impact on electrolyte balance and thermoregulation.

Prolonged use can cause headaches and trigger digestive issues, highlighting the need for caution when consuming this herbal substance.
