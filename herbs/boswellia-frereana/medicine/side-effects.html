10 Possible Side Effects Of Boswellia Frereana

Boswellia frereana has some side effects when used improperly, such as stomach upset, diarrhea, and dizziness.

These side effects occur due to its high resin content, which can cause allergic reactions or interact with certain medications.

In severe cases, excessive use can lead to liver damage and worsen conditions like hypertension, making it challenging for individuals to manage their health, leading to a decline in their overall well-being.

1. Increases blood pressure

Boswellia frereana increases blood pressure by stimulating the body's natural production of adrenaline, a hormone that constricts blood vessels and raises blood pressure.

This effect is due to the boswellic acids present in Boswellia frereana, which can cause the body's stress response system to activate, leading to increased blood pressure.

As a result, individuals using Boswellia frereana may experience elevated blood pressure levels.

2. Causes allergic reactions

Boswellia frereana causes allergic reactions due to its ability to stimulate the immune system and increase histamine levels.

This can lead to symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing in some individuals.

The exact mechanism behind this reaction is not fully understood, but it is believed to be related to the terpenes and sesquiterpenes present in Boswellia frereana, which are also responsible for its anti-inflammatory properties.

3. Disrupts digestive tract

Boswellia frereana disrupts digestive tract in some individuals due to its ability to alter the gut flora and slow down digestion.

The gum resin's anti-inflammatory properties can irritate the stomach lining, leading to nausea, bloating, and diarrhea.

Additionally, Boswellia frereana may interfere with the absorption of nutrients, causing malabsorption and potentially exacerbating pre-existing digestive issues such as irritable bowel syndrome (IBS).

4. Interacts with warfarin

Boswellia frereana interacts with warfarin, a blood thinner medication.

When combined, it may increase the risk of bleeding due to its ability to thin the blood and prolong clotting times. This is because Boswellia frereana contains compounds that inhibit platelet aggregation, which can lead to excessive bleeding.

Additionally, both substances are metabolized by the liver's cytochrome P450 enzyme system, potentially resulting in altered warfarin levels and increased bleeding risk.

5. Affects liver enzymes

Boswellia frereana affects liver enzymes by influencing cytochrome P450 activity.

The compound boswellic acid, a key constituent of Boswellia frereana, has been shown to induce the production of enzymes responsible for detoxification in the liver, leading to an increase in bilirubin levels and altered liver function tests.

This may result in changes in liver metabolism and potentially interact with medications that rely on these enzymes for their efficacy.

6. Triggers migraine attacks

Boswellia frereana triggers migraine attacks due to its ability to stimulate the nervous system and alter blood vessel function.

The compound boswellic acid, found in Boswellia frereana, can cause blood vessels to constrict or dilate, leading to changes in cerebral blood flow that may trigger migraine symptoms.

Additionally, the herb's stimulation of the trigeminal nerve, which plays a role in pain transmission, may also contribute to the onset of migraines.

7. Lowers platelet count

Boswellia frereana lowers platelet count by inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory compounds that can stimulate platelet aggregation.

This can lead to a decrease in platelet numbers, which may increase the risk of bleeding and bruising.

The mechanism behind this effect is attributed to the boswellic acids present in Boswellia frereana, which have been shown to inhibit the activity of enzymes involved in platelet activation.

8. Causes stomach ulcers

Boswellia frereana causes stomach ulcers when it irritates the mucous membranes in the stomach and digestive tract.

The resin of the tree contains a compound called boswellic acid, which can stimulate the production of stomach acid and cause inflammation in the stomach lining.

This may lead to the formation of painful sores or ulcers in the stomach, often accompanied by symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain.

9. Reduces fertility

Boswellia frereana reduces fertility by interfering with the normal functioning of reproductive hormones.

The compound boswellic acid, found in the extract of this plant, has been shown to reduce the production of androgens, such as testosterone, which is essential for sperm development and fertilization.

Additionally, boswellia may also affect the hypothalamus-pituitary-gonadal axis, leading to disrupted ovulation and reduced fertility.

10. Increases risk of bleeding

Boswellia frereana increases risk of bleeding due to its ability to thin the blood and reduce platelet aggregation.

This is because boswellic acids, a group of compounds found in Boswellia frereana, inhibit the production of thromboxane A2, a potent vasoconstrictor that plays a role in blood clotting.

Additionally, Boswellia frereana may also enhance the effects of anticoagulant medications, such as warfarin and aspirin, which can further increase the risk of bleeding.
