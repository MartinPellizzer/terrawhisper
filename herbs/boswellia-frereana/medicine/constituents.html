10 Medicinal Constituents Of Boswellia Frereana (Frankincense)

Boswellia frereana has active constituents such as boswellic acids, terpenes, and resinoids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and analgesic properties, which can help to reduce inflammation, alleviate pain, and improve respiratory function.

The boswellic acids, in particular, have been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory molecules, making frankincense a potential treatment for conditions such as arthritis, asthma, and even cancer.

1. Β-boswellic acid

Boswellia frereana β-boswellic acid is a bioactive compound extracted from the resin of this plant.

It has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine to treat various inflammatory conditions and diseases. β-Boswellic acid exhibits potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, inhibiting pro-inflammatory enzymes and reducing oxidative stress.

Its unique structure allows it to selectively target specific cellular pathways, making it a promising candidate for the treatment of chronic diseases such as arthritis and cancer.

2. Acetyl-β-boswellic acid

Boswellia frereana acetyl-β-boswellic acid is a bioactive compound derived from the resin of Boswellia frereana trees.

This acidic triterpenoid has been extensively studied for its potential therapeutic benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and anticancer properties.
