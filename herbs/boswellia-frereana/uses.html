Olibanum (Boswellia frereana) Uses

Olibanum is used to treat rheumatoid arthritis, osteoarthritis, and joint pain due to its anti-inflammatory properties. It also helps with gout and inflammatory conditions like IBD and colitis. Additionally, it supports gastrointestinal health by reducing inflammation in conditions such as gastritis and reflux disease.

Olibanum (Boswellia frereana) Monograph

1. Rheumatoid arthritis

Olibanum, derived from Boswellia frereana, has shown efficacy in reducing inflammation and pain associated with rheumatoid arthritis. Its active compounds inhibit inflammatory enzymes, offering a natural alternative for symptom management. Studies suggest it may slow joint damage and improve mobility in affected individuals.

2. Osteoarthritis

Olibanum, or Boswellia frereana, is used traditionally for osteoarthritis due to its anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce joint pain and swelling by inhibiting inflammatory enzymes. Studies suggest it may offer relief comparable to some pharmaceutical treatments. It is often taken as a resin or in supplement form.

3. Gout

Olibanum, derived from Boswellia frereana, has been traditionally used to support joint health and reduce inflammation. It contains boswellic acids that inhibit inflammatory enzymes, making it beneficial for conditions like gout. Its anti-inflammatory properties may help alleviate pain and swelling associated with gouty arthritis.

4. Joint pain

Olibanum, derived from Boswellia frereana, is used to alleviate joint pain due to its anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce swelling and stiffness, making it beneficial for conditions like arthritis. Its active compounds inhibit inflammatory responses in the body, offering natural relief for chronic joint discomfort.

5. Inflammatory bowel disease

Olibanum, derived from Boswellia frereana, has shown efficacy in reducing inflammation associated with inflammatory bowel disease. Its active compounds inhibit inflammatory pathways, helping alleviate symptoms like abdominal pain and diarrhea. Clinical studies support its use as a complementary therapy in managing conditions such as ulcerative colitis and Crohn's disease.

6. Colitis
