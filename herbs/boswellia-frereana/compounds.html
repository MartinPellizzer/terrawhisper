Olibanum (Boswellia frereana) Compounds

Olibanum contains several primary compounds, including terpenoids, which contribute to its aromatic properties. Flavonoids and phenolic acids provide antioxidant benefits. Essential oils make up a significant portion of the resin, while tannins and glycosides support anti-inflammatory effects. Coumarin, saponins, and alkaloids also play roles in its medicinal properties.

Olibanum (Boswellia frereana) Monograph

1. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a key class of active compounds found in Olibanum (Boswellia frereana). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, making it valuable in herbal medicine for conditions like arthritis and inflammatory disorders.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Olibanum (Boswellia frereana) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help reduce inflammation and support cellular health, making them valuable in herbal medicine for treating conditions like arthritis and digestive disorders.

3. Essential oil

The essential oil of Olibanum (Boswellia frereana) is a volatile component extracted from its resin. It contains monoterpenes and sesquiterpenes, which contribute to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. The oil is used in herbal medicine for its potential to support joint health and reduce pain.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in Olibanum (Boswellia frereana). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties by inhibiting enzymes involved in inflammatory responses. This compound is particularly noted for its role in supporting joint health and reducing pain in conditions like arthritis.

5. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Olibanum (Boswellia frereana). It contributes to the resin's anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. Tannins help reduce swelling and support wound healing. They are responsible for the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a compound found in Olibanum (Boswellia frereana). It contributes to the plant's aromatic properties and has been studied for its potential anti-inflammatory and anticoagulant effects. Coumarin is also known for its role in the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Olibanum (Boswellia frereana). It contributes to the resin's anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. Glycosides in Olibanum may support joint health and reduce inflammation in conditions like arthritis.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Olibanum (Boswellia frereana). It contributes to the plant's therapeutic properties by supporting anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects. Saponins may enhance the bioavailability of other active constituents in Boswellia. They are part of the resinous components that give Olibanum its medicinal value.

9. Alkaloid

Olibanum (Boswellia frereana) contains alkaloids, which are nitrogen-containing compounds with potential medicinal properties. These alkaloids contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects, supporting its use in traditional herbal medicine for conditions like arthritis and inflammatory disorders.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Olibanum (Boswellia frereana). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce inflammation. This compound supports the therapeutic uses of Olibanum in traditional herbal medicine.
