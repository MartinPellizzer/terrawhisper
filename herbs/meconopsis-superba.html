What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Meconopsis superba has large, deeply lobed, blue-green leaves with a distinctively glaucous coating and bright yellow, bowl-shaped flowers that can reach up to ten centimeters in diameter. The foliage is characterized by a leathery texture and prominent venation that adds to its structural appearance. Its large flowers feature numerous long, golden stamens that contrast sharply against the broad, spreading petals. As the plant matures, it produces large, woody seed capsules that eventually split to release dark seeds.
