Ardisia humilis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ardisia humilis has small, glossy, elliptical leaves that are arranged oppositely on slender, creeping stems to form a dense groundcover. The foliage typically measures between 1 and 3 centimeters in length and possesses a deep green color with smooth margins. These plants produce tiny, inconspicuous white flowers that emerge from the leaf axils during the growing season. Following pollination, the plant develops small, fleshy drupes that transition from green to a dark red or black color when mature. The overall growth habit is prostrate, allowing the plant to spread horizontally across the forest floor.
