Ardisia humilis

What's the taxonomical classification of Ardisia humilis?

Ardisia humilis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Ericales. Within this order, it is situated in the family Primulaceae, falling under the genus Ardisia. Ultimately, its specific biological identity is defined by its species name, humilis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ardisia humilis has small, glossy, elliptical leaves that are arranged oppositely on slender, creeping stems to form a dense groundcover.

The foliage typically measures between 1 and 3 centimeters in length and possesses a deep green color with smooth margins. These plants produce tiny, inconspicuous white flowers that emerge from the leaf axils during the growing season. Following pollination, the plant develops small, fleshy drupes that transition from green to a dark red or black color when mature.

The overall growth habit is prostrate, allowing the plant to spread horizontally across the forest floor.
