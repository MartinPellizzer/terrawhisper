Ardisia humilis

What's the taxonomical classification of Ardisia humilis?

Ardisia humilis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Ericales. Within this order, it is situated in the family Primulaceae, falling under the genus Ardisia. Ultimately, its specific biological identity is defined by its species name, humilis.

Furthermore, specific phytochemical constituents may modulate signal transduction pathways by interacting with nuclear factor kappa B to suppress the immune response. These interactions with cellular signaling proteins contribute to the plant's observed antimicrobial and antioxidant therapeutic properties. By binding to these specific protein sites, the plant's metabolites effectively disrupt the biochemical cascades responsible for pain and swelling.
