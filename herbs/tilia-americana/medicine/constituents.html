10 Medicinal Constituents Of Tilia Americana (American Linden)

Tilia americana, also known as American linden, has active constituents such as flavonoids, terpenoids, and phenolic acids.

These compounds contribute to its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and sedative effects. The flavonoids, particularly quercetin, have been shown to improve cardiovascular health by reducing blood pressure and cholesterol levels.

The sedative properties of Tilia americana may also help alleviate anxiety and promote restful sleep, enhancing the overall quality of life.

This article explains in details the 10 best active constituents of Tilia americana.

1. Quercetin

Tilia americana, also known as American Linden, quercetin is a flavonoid compound extracted from its leaves and flowers.

This bioactive compound has been extensively studied for its numerous health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties.

Quercetin has been shown to exhibit potential therapeutic effects in the prevention and treatment of various diseases, such as cardiovascular disease, cancer, and allergies, making it a promising natural remedy with a wide range of applications.

2. Kaempferol

Tilia americana, also known as American Linden, Kaempferol is a flavonoid compound extracted from the leaves of the Tilia americana plant.

It has been found to possess numerous biological activities, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Kaempferol has been studied for its potential health benefits in treating various diseases, such as cardiovascular disease, cancer, and neurodegenerative disorders.

Additionally, it has also been shown to have antimicrobial and antiviral effects.

3. Naringenin

Tilia americana, also known as American Linden, naringenin is a flavonoid compound found in its leaves.

This bioactive constituent has been studied for its potential health benefits, including antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Research suggests that naringenin may help protect against cardiovascular disease, cancer, and neurological disorders by inhibiting enzymes involved in these processes.

Further studies are needed to fully understand its mechanisms of action and therapeutic applications.

4. Isoquercitrin

Tilia americana, also known as American Linden, isoquercitrin is a bioactive compound extracted from its leaves.

It has been studied for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant activities. Isoquercitrin has been shown to possess immunomodulatory effects, inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines and suppressing the activation of immune cells.

5. Rutin

Tilia americana, also known as American Linden, rutin is a bioflavonoid compound found in its flowers.

It is a potent antioxidant and has been studied for its potential health benefits, including reducing inflammation, improving cardiovascular health, and exhibiting anti-allergic properties. Rutin has also been shown to have antimicrobial and antiviral effects, making it a popular ingredient in natural remedies and supplements.

Tilia americana, also known as American Linden, gallic acid is a type of phenolic compound that is found in the leaves and bark of the tree.

It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties.

8. Flavonoids

Tilia americana, also known as American Linden, flavonoids is a group of plant compounds found in its leaves and flowers.

9. Phenolic acids

10. Oleanolic acid

Tilia americana, also known as American Linden, Oleanolic acid is a triterpenoid saponin extracted from the leaves and bark of the tree.
