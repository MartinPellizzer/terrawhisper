To identify Tilia (Tilia americana) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Tilia americana belongs to the kingdom Plantae. Its family is Tiliaceae, and it is classified under the genus Tilia. The species is Tilia americana, commonly known as American basswood. The other taxonomic levels such as division, class, and order are not provided in the data.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of the tree is Tilia americana, commonly known as American basswood. It was first described by the botanist Carl Peter Thunberg in 1794. This species has several synonyms, including Tilia heterophylla and Tilia tibetana, which reflect historical classification variations. It is also referred to by regional and historical names such as white elm and summer tree.

Morphological Characteristics

Tilia americana is a deciduous tree with a rounded crown and a strong, deep root system. Its stem is straight and smooth, often reaching heights of 20-30 meters. Leaves are heart-shaped, serrated, and 5-10 cm in size, turning yellow in autumn. Inflorescences are clustered, with small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The fruit is a small, woody nut enclosed in a husk, containing one or two seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Tilia americana can be distinguished by its heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges, pale yellow flowers in clustered umbels, and a smooth, gray bark. It differs from Tilia cordifolia by its more rounded leaf base and from Tilia europaea by its larger, more showy flowers. The leaves are typically larger and have a more pronounced central vein.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Tilia americana is native to eastern North America, ranging from Canada to the southeastern United States. It prefers temperate climates with moderate rainfall and full sun to partial shade. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found at low to mid-elevation areas.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Tilia americana blooms in early summer, typically from June to July, producing fragrant white flowers. Seed formation occurs in late summer, around August to September. For identification purposes, the most reliable time to harvest is during the flowering period when leaves and flowers are fully developed.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Tilia americana is closely related to other Tilia species within the Tiliaceae family. It shares key characteristics with species like Tilia europaea and Tilia cordata. Its genus relations highlight its placement within the Tiliaceae family, which includes other genera like Crataegus and Chaenomeles. Family traits such as simple leaves and showy flowers are shared among these species.
