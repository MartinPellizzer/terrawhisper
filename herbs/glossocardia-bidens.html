Glossocardia bidens

What's the taxonomical classification of Glossocardia bidens?

Glossocardia bidens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this classification, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its lineage further, the plant falls under the order Asterales and is a member of the family Asteraceae. Ultimately, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Glossocardia and the species bidens.

    What are the molecular targets of this plant?

    Glossocardia bidens targets various inflammatory mediators and enzymes, specifically inhibiting cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2), lipoxygenase (LOX), and inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS) to reduce systemic inflammation. These interactions occur primarily through bioactive polyphenols and flavonoids that disrupt the arachidonic acid metabolism pathway. By modulating these protein targets, the plant effectively lowers the production of prostaglandins and leukotrienes responsible for pain and swelling. Additionally, its phytochemical constituents may interact with various cytokine signaling proteins to suppress the overproduction of pro-inflammatory markers like tumor necrosis factor-alpha. This multi-target mechanism of action underpins its traditional use in treating inflammatory diseases and febrile conditions.
