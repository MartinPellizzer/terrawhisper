What's the taxonomical classification of Lysimachia tengyuehensis?

Lysimachia tengyuehensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Ericales. This plant is further categorized into the family Primulaceae, specifically within the genus Lysimachia, and is identified by its unique species designation, tengyuehensis.

      What are the herbal preparations of Lysimachia tengyuehensis?

      Lysimachia tengyuehensis is prepared as a decoction, a topical wash, or a crushed poultice to address various medicinal needs. The dried aerial parts are typically boiled in water to create a concentrated tea used for internal consumption. For skin conditions, the plant material can be infused into a liquid to serve as a soothing wash for irritated areas. In cases of acute localized injury, the fresh leaves are macerated into a pulp and applied directly to the skin as a poultice. These different methods allow practitioners to tailor the delivery of the plant's bioactive compounds to the specific ailment being treated.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Lysimachia tengyuehensis can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or gastrointestinal distress if ingested due to its potentially toxic chemical compounds. While specific human clinical trials are limited, many species within the Primulaceae family contain alkaloids that may trigger adverse reactions. Individuals with sensitive skin might experience redness, itching, or swelling upon direct contact with the plant's sap or foliage. If consumed accidentally, the plant could lead to nausea, vomiting, or stomach cramps. It is advisable to handle this species with gloves to prevent unexpected dermatological sensitivity.
