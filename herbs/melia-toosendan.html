Melia toosendan

What's the taxonomical classification of Melia toosendan?

Melia toosendan belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Sapindales and the family Meliaceae. Finally, the plant is situated within the genus Melia, specifically identified as the species toosendan.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Melia toosendan contains a diverse range of bioactive secondary metabolites including limonoids, triterpenoids, flavonoids, and various phenolic compounds. The presence of complex limonoids provides the plant with significant insecticidal and antimicrobial properties. These chemical constituents have been studied for their potential to exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant activities in biological systems. Additionally, the triterpene fractions are often investigated for their ability to influence cellular processes. Such a rich chemical profile makes the species a subject of interest in pharmacological research.

What are the therapeutic actions of Melia toosendan?

Melia tosecondan has potent antimicrobial, antifungal, and anti-inflammatory properties derived from its bioactive limonoid compounds. These phytochemicals act by inhibiting the growth of various pathogenic bacteria and fungi, making the plant useful in treating skin infections. The extract also functions to reduce swelling and pain through the modulation of inflammatory pathways in biological tissues. Some studies suggest its bioactive constituents may even exhibit cytotoxic activities against certain cancer cell lines. Consequently, various parts of the plant are utilized in traditional medicine to address dermatological conditions and systemic inflammation.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin diseases such as scabies, eczema, and various fungal infections through its topical application. The leaves and bark contain bioactive compounds that possess significant antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. These medicinal qualities allow practitioners to alleviate itching and reduce swelling associated with dermatological conditions. Beyond skin ailments, some traditional practices utilize extracts to manage internal issues like digestive discomfort. The plant's versatility makes it a valuable resource in various ethnobotanical traditions for managing diverse ailments.
