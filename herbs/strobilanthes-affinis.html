Strobilanthes affinis

What's the taxonomical classification of Strobilanthes affinis?

Strobilanthes affinis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the evolutionary hierarchy, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant falls under the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Acanthaceae. Specifically, it is identified by the genus Strobilanthes and is designated as the species affinis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Strobilanthes affinis has opposite, decussate leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptical with entire or slightly wavy margins and an acute apex.

The stems are typically quadrangular in cross-section and possess a slightly woody texture as they mature. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the axils, often displaying a distinct color contrast between the corolla and the calyx. The inflorescence structure is characterized by dense clusters that emerge from the leaf axils.

These morphological traits are diagnostic for identifying the species within the Acanthaceae family.
