Polygonum coriaceum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polygonum coriaceum has a woody, perennial growth habit characterized by stiff, leathery leaves and slender, reddish-brown stems. The leaf blades are typically lanceolate or oblong in shape with entire margins and a noticeably thickened, coriaceous texture. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in axillary clusters along the stem. The plant often forms low-growing mats or clumps, which helps it withstand harsh environmental conditions. These morphological features are adaptations that allow the species to thrive in rocky or coastal habitats.
