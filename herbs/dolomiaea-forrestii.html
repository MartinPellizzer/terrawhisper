Dolomiaea forrestii

What's the taxonomical classification of Dolomiaea forrestii?

Dolomiaea forrestii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae, which is placed within the genus Dolomiaea, and is specifically identified by its species name, forrestii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dolomiaea forrestii has a woody, perennial habit characterized by erect stems that often feature a densely pubescent or tomentose texture.

The leaves are typically arranged in an opposite or whorled pattern, displaying an oblong to lanceolate shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the axils, often presenting in compact inflorescences that contrast with the foliage. The reproductive structures include a characteristic calyx and corolla structure consistent with the Lamiaceae family.

These morphological traits allow the plant to adapt to its specific high-altitude rocky environments.
