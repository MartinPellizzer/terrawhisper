Common Crocus (Crocus Athous)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Crocus athous.

Common crocus, scientifically known as Crocus athous, is a delicate flowering plant belonging to the Iridaceae family, valued for its medicinal and culinary properties. This perennial herb is particularly noted for its starchy corms, which have been traditionally used as a spice and medicinal herb due to their unique flavor and potential health benefits. The primary benefits of Common crocus include its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and mild sedative effects, making it useful in supporting digestive health and reducing stress. Historically, it has been used in European and Middle Eastern cultures to treat ailments such as digestive disorders and insomnia, while modern wellness applications include its incorporation into herbal remedies and dietary supplements. One of its unique features is the presence of crocetin, a rare active compound that contributes to its distinctive saffron-like flavor and potential therapeutic properties.
