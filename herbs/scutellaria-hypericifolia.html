Scutellaria hypericifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Scutellaria hypericifolia?

Scutelariia hypericifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls into the subclass Magnoliidae and is a member of the order Lamiales. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the family Lamiaceae, which contains the genus Scutellaria. Finally, the plant is identified at the species level as Scutellaria hypericiifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Scutellaria hypericifolia has opposite, lanceolate to ovate leaves that are typically 2 to 5 centimeters long with entire margins and a characteristic hypericum-like appearance. The stems are square in cross-section, a common trait among members of the Lamiaceae family. Small, bilabiate flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves or at the ends of the branches, often displaying a pale blue or violet hue. The calyx is tubular and frequently features distinct teeth at its apex. These morphological features collectively distinguish the species within its genus.
