Hydrangea macrophylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Hydrangea and the species macrophyllia.

What are the therapeutic actions of Hydrangea macrophylla?

Hydrangea macrophylla has diuretic, antipyretic, and astringent properties that are primarily utilized in traditional medicinal practices to manage fluid retention and fever. The plant's diuretic action helps the body eliminate excess water and sodium through increased urine production. Its antipyretic qualities assist in reducing elevated body temperatures during febrile illnesses. Additionally, the astringent components can help contract tissues and reduce secretions in various bodily systems. These combined actions make the plant a versatile subject in ethnobotanical studies.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause severe toxicity through the ingestion of cyanogenic glycosides, leading to nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea. As the poisoning progresses, a person may experience dizziness and confusion. In more critical cases, the toxins can trigger seizures and acute respiratory distress. If the exposure is sufficiently high and medical intervention is not immediate, the resulting systemic failure can lead to death.
