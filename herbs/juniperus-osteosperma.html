Utah Juniper (Juniperus osteosperma)

Utah Juniper (Juniperus osteosperma) is a member of the Cupressaceae family, native to Southwestern United States, Northern Mexico, and Desert Regions Of Arizona And New Mexico. Traditionally, its fruits, bark, and resin have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its diuretic, tonic, and astringent actions, and has a long history of use in native american herbal medicine, european herbal medicine, and mediterranean herbal traditions.

Flowers are small, greenish-yellow, actinomorphic, clustered in fascicles of 2 to 3, with 4 sepals and 4 petals.
Stems
Erect, woody, branched, with opposite or alternate phyllotaxis, and having a glabrous surface.
