Poncirus trifoliata

What's the taxonomical classification of Poncirus trifoliata?

Poncirus trifoliata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, the plant falls under the order Sapindales and the family Rutaceae. Finally, it is defined by its specific genus, Poncirus, and its unique species designation, trifoliata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Poncirus trifoliata has trifoliate leaves consisting of three distinct leaflets, deciduous woody stems covered in sharp thorns, and small white flowers that transition into yellow, oval-shaped citrus fruits. The individual leaflets are typically elliptical or ovate with serrated margins and a glossy green surface. These thorny branches can grow into large, spreading shrubs or small trees reaching several meters in height. During the autumn, the foliage often turns a vibrant shade of red or orange before falling. The resulting fruit is characterized by a thick, bumpy rind and a highly acidic, aromatic pulp.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds coumarins, hesperidin, poncirin, poncirol, and triphasiol. These bioactive constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's therapeutic potential and pharmacological activities. Hesperidin and poncirin are well-known flavonoids that contribute to antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The presence of coumarins and poncirol further enhances the chemical complexity of its medicinal profile. Additionally, triphasiol serves as another significant phytochemical component within its biological makeup.

As discussed in a study, thirteen coumarins (1-13) were isolated from the folk medicinal plant Poncirus trifoliata. This group included five new compounds (1-5) identified through spectroscopic analyses. Specifically, coumarins 1-4 are bis-isoprenylated coumarins with diverse oxidation patterns, whereas compound 5 is an enantiomeric di-isoprenylated coumarin. The study also assigned absolute configurations for the known compounds triphasiol (6) and ponciol (7). These isolated coumarins significantly inhibited Staphylococcus aureus-derived sortase A (SrtA).
