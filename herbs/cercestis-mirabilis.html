Cercestis mirabilis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cercestis mirabilis has woody stems, simple lanceolate leaves with entire margins, and small actinomorphic flowers that emerge from axillary buds. The foliage is characterized by a glossy texture and prominent pinnate venation that extends toward the leaf tips. Its inflorescence typically consists of solitary blossoms or small clusters located along the branches. The floral structure features a tubular corolla and distinct reproductive organs designed for specific pollinators. These morphological traits are essential for the plant's identification within its native habitat.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the tropical rainforest regions of West and Central Africa, specifically ranging from Guinea to the Democratic Republic of the Congo. Its presence is strictly tied to the humid, low-elevation forest ecosystems characteristic of these equatorial zones. Within these habitats, it typically thrives in shaded understory environments where moisture levels remain consistently high. The species follows a fragmented distribution pattern that corresponds closely to the availability of primary forest cover.         What side effects this plant can have?

        This plant can cause severe skin irritation, localized swelling, and intense itching upon direct contact with the dermis. These dermatological reactions often stem from the presence of caustic compounds within its sap. If the substance accidentally enters the eyes, it may lead to temporary vision impairment or significant inflammation of the conjunctiva.
