What's the taxonomical classification of Piper hancei?

Piper hancei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Piperales. It is further categorized under the family Piperaceae and falls within the genus Piper. Ultimately, its specific biological identity is defined by its designation as the species hancei.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Its inflorescence consists of small, spike-like structures that emerge from the leaf axils. These spikes contain numerous tiny, densely packed flowers that eventually develop into small drupes.

What are the pharmacological activities of Piper hancei?

This plant has potent pharmacological properties including significant anti-inflammatory and anti-neuroinflammatory activities. These biological effects are primarily driven by specific bioactive compounds found within its chemical profile. By inhibiting various inflammatory mediators, the plant helps reduce swelling and pain in systemic tissues. Furthermore, its ability to modulate neuroinflammation suggests potential benefits for protecting the central nervous system from damage. These combined actions make the species a subject of interest for medicinal research.

As discussed in a study, Piper hancei Maxim. is utilized in the traditional Yao ethnomedicine system to treat rheumatism, wind-cold, and inflammation. Phytochemical analysis of the stems of P. hancei resulted in the isolation of 13 pairs of neolignan enantiomers, which included 4 new pairs named piperhancin D-G (1-4). Among these, the right-handed (+) enantiomer (+) -futoquinol (5) demonstrated significant anti-neuroinflammatory activity in LPS-stimulated BV-2 microglial cells without cytotoxicity. This compound effectively suppressed pro-inflammatory cytokines and alleviated microglia-induced neuronal damage in SH-SY5Y cells. Furthermore, (+) -5 alleviated LPS-induced cognitive dysfunction in mice and reduced LPS-induced neuronal damage and microglial activation in mouse brains.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Research suggests that these molecules may interact with various biological pathways to provide therapeutic benefits. The presence of these alkaloids and sterols is a defining characteristic of its chemical makeup. Understanding these individual components helps scientists determine the plant's potential for drug development.

As discussed in a study, the twigs of Piper hancei were investigated to isolate and purify chemical constituents using silica gel, Sephadex LH-20, and preparative RP-HPLC. A total of 8 compounds were isolated and identified from the plant. These constituents include 4-allylpyrocatechol(I), piperlonguminine(II), d-sesamin(III), beta-sitosterol(IV), pellitorine(V), piperolactam A(VI), and piperolactam D(VII). Notably, compounds I, III, VI, and VII were isolated from Piper hancei for the 1st time.
