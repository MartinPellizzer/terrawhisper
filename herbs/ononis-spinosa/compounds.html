Ononis Spinosa (Ononis spinosa) Compounds

Ononis spinosa contains several primary active compounds, including tannins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its astringent and anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarin and saponins also play roles in its medicinal effects, while glycosides and terpenoids support its overall therapeutic profile. Alkaloids, essential oils, and anthocyanins further enhance its pharmacological activities.

Ononis Spinosa (Ononis spinosa) Monograph

1. Tannin

Tannin in Ononis spinosa is a key active compound known for its astringent properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating digestive issues and skin conditions. Tannins help reduce inflammation and may support wound healing. This compound is central to the plant's therapeutic applications in herbal medicine.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Ononis spinosa are bioactive compounds known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for treating respiratory and skin conditions. They help reduce oxidative stress and support immune function. Flavonoids are a key component of Ononis spinosa's therapeutic profile.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid in Ononis spinosa contributes to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound helps reduce oxidative stress and supports the plant's traditional use in treating urinary tract infections and inflammation. Its presence enhances the therapeutic potential of Ononis spinosa in herbal medicine.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Ononis spinosa. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating urinary tract issues and inflammation. Coumarin's anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory properties support its role in herbal medicine. This compound is responsible for some of the plant's therapeutic effects.

5. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Ononis spinosa. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponins may support digestive health and immune function, making them valuable components in herbal formulations.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Ononis spinosa. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating urinary tract infections and inflammation. Glycosides in Ononis spinosa are believed to have diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting its role in herbal medicine.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Ononis spinosa are bioactive compounds known for their anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating skin conditions and infections. They are often isolated from the aerial parts of the plant and are key to its therapeutic effects in herbal medicine.

8. Alkaloid

Ononis spinosa contains the alkaloid spinozan. This compound has been studied for its potential effects on the nervous system and may contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating nervous disorders. Spinozan is one of the key bioactive components responsible for the plant's medicinal properties.

9. Essential oil

The essential oil of Ononis spinosa contains volatile compounds like limonene and cineole, which contribute to its aromatic properties. These compounds are often used in herbal medicine for their potential calming and antispasmodic effects. The oil is typically extracted through steam distillation from the plant's flowers and leaves. It is valued for its role in traditional remedies for digestive and respiratory support.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanins are a group of flavonoids found in Ononis spinosa. They contribute to the plant's deep blue pigmentation and are known for their antioxidant properties. These compounds may support anti-inflammatory and cardiovascular benefits, making them relevant in traditional herbal medicine.
