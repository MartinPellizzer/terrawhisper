Botanical Identification of Ononis Spinosa (Ononis spinosa)

To identify Ononis Spinosa (Ononis spinosa) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Ononis spinosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Fabales, order Fabales, family Fabaceae, genus Ononis, and species spinosa. This classification places it within the legume family, known for its nitrogen-fixing properties and medicinal uses in herbal medicine.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Ononis spinosa is the scientific name for the plant commonly known as spring beauty. It was first described by the botanist Carl von Linné in the 18th century. The species has several synonyms, including Anthoxanthum spinosum and Hesperis spinosa, reflecting its historical classification. It is also known by various common names such as spring beauty and rock beauty, depending on the region.

Morphological Characteristics

Ononis spinosa is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial herb with a deep taproot system. Its stems are prostrate, branching, and covered in fine hairs. The leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and have a distinctive spiny texture. The inflorescence is a dense raceme of small, pink to purple flowers. The fruit is a capsule containing several elliptical seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Ononis spinosa can be distinguished by its spiny, cylindrical stems and small, yellowish flowers arranged in loose clusters. It has serrated, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, hard, dark brown seeds. It is often confused with other Ononis species, but its unique stem structure and leaf shape are key identifiers.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Ononis spinosa is native to southern Europe and western Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with mild summers and cool winters. It prefers well-drained, sandy or rocky soils. It is commonly found at altitudes between 300 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Ononis spinosa blooms from April to June, producing small pink flowers. Seed formation occurs from July to September. For identification purposes, the best time to harvest is during the flowering period, when the plant is most easily recognized.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Ononis spinosa belongs to the genus Ononis within the family Fabaceae. Its closest relatives include species like Ononis arundinacea and Ononis crenata. It shares key family traits such as compound leaves and legume pods, but differs in leaf shape and habitat preferences. Its genus relations highlight evolutionary adaptations within the Fabaceae family.
