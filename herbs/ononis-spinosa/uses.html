Ononis Spinosa (Ononis spinosa) Uses

Ononis spinosa is used to treat respiratory infections, coughs, and bronchitis by reducing inflammation and supporting lung health. It may also help with sinusitis and respiratory allergies. The plant is applied externally for skin infections and wound healing, and it can aid in stopping bleeding due to its astringent properties.

Ononis Spinosa (Ononis spinosa) Monograph

1. Respiratory infection

Ononis spinosa has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in cases of mild respiratory infections. It is believed to help reduce inflammation and soothe coughing due to its expectorant properties. Often prepared as a tea, it may aid in clearing mucus and easing breathing. Its use is commonly recommended in herbal medicine for seasonal respiratory support.

2. Cough

Ononis spinosa is traditionally used to soothe coughs due to its expectorant properties. It helps loosen mucus in the respiratory tract, making it easier to expel. Often prepared as a tea, it is valued for its gentle action on the throat and lungs. Its use aligns with historical herbal practices for respiratory support.

3. Bronchitis

Ononis spinosa is used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health, particularly in cases of bronchitis. It helps reduce inflammation and mucus production in the airways, easing breathing difficulties. Its expectorant properties aid in clearing congestion, making it a valued remedy in traditional herbal practices for respiratory conditions.

4. Inflamed lungs

Ononis spinosa has been traditionally used to support lung health, particularly in cases of inflammation. It is believed to help reduce irritation and promote healing in the respiratory tract. Its anti-inflammatory properties may aid in easing symptoms associated with inflamed lungs. It is often prepared as a tincture or tea for internal use.

5. Respiratory allergies

Ononis spinosa is used in herbal medicine to alleviate respiratory allergies due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties. It helps reduce symptoms like sneezing, coughing, and nasal congestion by supporting the body's natural response to allergens. Often prepared as a tea, it is valued for its gentle yet effective action on the respiratory system.

6. Respiratory infections

Ononis spinosa has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in treating symptoms of respiratory infections. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties help reduce congestion and fight infections. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to soothe coughs and ease breathing.

7. Sinusitis

Ononis spinosa is used in herbal medicine to support sinus health due to its anti-inflammatory and decongestant properties. It helps reduce mucus buildup and soothe irritated nasal passages, making it a valuable remedy for managing symptoms of sinusitis. Its use is often combined with other herbs to enhance its effectiveness in clearing congestion and promoting respiratory wellness.

8. Skin infection

Ononis spinosa has been traditionally used to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce inflammation and promote healing in conditions like boils and fungal infections. Its astringent qualities also aid in drying out wounds and preventing secondary infections.

9. Wound healing

Ononis spinosa has been traditionally used to promote wound healing due to its astringent and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce swelling, prevent infection, and accelerate tissue repair. The plant's high tannin content contributes to its ability to close wounds and support skin regeneration. It is often applied topically as a poultice or infused oil.

10. Bleeding

Ononis spinosa has been traditionally used to address bleeding due to its astringent properties. It helps to constrict blood vessels and reduce blood loss, making it useful for treating nosebleeds, hemorrhoids, and other forms of minor bleeding. Its use in herbal medicine focuses on stopping bleeding and promoting healing of wounds.
