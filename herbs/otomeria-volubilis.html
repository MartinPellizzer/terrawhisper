Otomeria volubilis

What's the taxonomical classification of Otomeria volubilis?

Otomeria volubilis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further classified under the order Gentianales. It is a member of the family Rubiaceae, falling specifically within the genus Otomeria, with its unique identity defined by the species name volubilis.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, stomach aches, and various types of inflammation. Local healers often apply the crushed leaves topically to soothe irritated or infected areas of the skin. When prepared as a decoction, it serves as a traditional remedy for digestive distress and intestinal discomfort. The plant contains bioactive compounds that may contribute to its medicinal efficacy in managing these ailments. Such uses are deeply rooted in the ethnobotanical practices of specific regional communities.
