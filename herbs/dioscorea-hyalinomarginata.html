What is Dioscorea hyalinomarginata?

Dioscorea hyalinomarginata is a species of flowering plant in the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is a type of yam with a perennial habit, characterized by a rhizomatous stem that grows up to 1.5 meters in length, with internodes that are 2-10 cm long and 2-5 cm in diameter. The leaves are alternate, simple, and cordate with an acuminate apex, measuring 5-10 cm in length and 2-5 cm in width, with a petiole that is 1-2 cm long. The inflorescence is a corymb, bearing small, greenish-yellow flowers with 6 tepals, 6 stamens, and a trilocular ovary.

The plant produces small, berry-like fruits with 3 seeds each, containing starch-filled tubers that are edible and used as a food source.
