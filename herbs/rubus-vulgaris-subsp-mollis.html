Rubus vulgaris subsp. mollis

Rubus vulgaris subsp.

mollis, commonly known as the European dewberry, is a subspecies of the bramble plant.

mollis include its ability to reduce inflammation, improve cardiovascular health, and exhibit antiviral and antibacterial activities.

Possible Side Effects of Rubus vulgaris subsp. mollis

Rubus vulgaris subsp.

mollis can have side effects if used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, allergic reactions, and interactions with medications.

Using this plant without proper knowledge can lead to stomach pain, nausea, and vomiting, while allergic reactions may cause symptoms like hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.

Furthermore, interactions with medications can cause dizziness, drowsiness, and increased risk of bleeding, especially in individuals taking blood-thinning medications.

In severe cases, improper use of Rubus vulgaris subsp.

mollis can also lead to kidney damage and liver failure.
