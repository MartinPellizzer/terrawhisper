Merremia hederacea

What's the taxonomical classification of Merremia hederacea?

Merremia hederacea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Solanales. This climbing plant is further categorized under the family Convolvulaceae, falling within the genus Merremia, and is specifically identified by its species name, hederacea.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat respiratory conditions such as bronchitis, asthma, and common colds by acting as an expectorant. It helps to loosen mucus in the lungs and airways, making it easier for patients to cough up phlegm. The plant contains active compounds that soothe irritated mucous membranes during bouts of inflammation. Because of these properties, it is frequently utilized in traditional medicine to alleviate symptoms of coughs and congestion. This botanical remedy specifically targets the discomfort associated with obstructive lung ailments.
