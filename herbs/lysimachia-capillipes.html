Lysimachia capillipes

What's the taxonomical classification of Lysimachia capillipes?

Lysimachia capillipes belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, falling under the order Ericales. This plant is a member of the family Primulaceae, which contains its specific genus, Lysimachia, and is ultimately identified by its unique species name, capillipes.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lysimachia capillipes has slender, creeping stems that produce small, lanceolate leaves arranged oppositely along the branches. These leaves typically measure only a few millimeters in length and possess entire margins with acute tips. The plant produces small, yellow, star-shaped flowers that emerge from the leaf axils on short pedicels. The reproductive structures include five distinct petals and a central cluster of stamens. Its overall growth habit is characterized by a delicate, sprawling texture that allows it to spread across the soil surface.

According to a study published by "Bioanalysis", Lysimachia capillipes Hemsl is a folk medicinal plant in China that has shown significant anti-tumor activity in recent studies. The major bioactive components in this plant are capilliposide B and capilliposide C. A validated LC-MS/MS method was developed for the simultaneous determination of these components in rat plasma over a curve range of 10.0 to 5000 ng/ml. The precision and accuracy for QC samples showed ≤6.1% RSD and a relative error of 1.3-3.7%. This method was successfully applied to determine concentrations after the administration of Lysimachia capillipes Hemsl extract for a rat PK study.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the specific medicinal compounds capilliposide A, capilliposide B, capilliposide C, capilliposide I, and capilliposide II. These unique iridoid glycosides serve as primary chemical markers for the species Lysimachia capillipes. Researchers study these specific molecules to understand their potential therapeutic properties in pharmacological applications. The presence of this diverse group of capilliposides characterizes the plant's distinct chemical profile. Detailed analysis of these substances provides insight into the complex secondary metabolites found within the genus.

According to a study published by "Chemical & pharmaceuctical bulletin", three novel triterpenoid saponins were isolated from an ethanol extract of Lysimachia capillipes. These three new saponins are identified as capilliposide A (1), capilliposide B (2), and capilliposide C (3). The structures of these compounds were determined using 1D and 2D NMR techniques, including 1H-1H COSY, HMBC, HMQC, DEPТ, and TOCSY, as well as MS and hydrolysis. Among the isolated compounds, capilliposide B (2) demonstrated significant cytotoxicity against human A-2780 cells.
