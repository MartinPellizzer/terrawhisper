Lysimachia capillipes

What's the taxonomical classification of Lysimachia capillipes?

Lysimachia capillipes belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, falling under the order Ericales. This plant is a member of the family Primulaceae, which contains its specific genus, Lysimachia, and is ultimately identified by its unique species name, capillipes.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lysimachia capillipes has slender, creeping stems that produce small, lanceolate leaves arranged oppositely along the branches.

These leaves typically measure only a few millimeters in length and possess entire margins with acute tips. The plant produces small, yellow, star-shaped flowers that emerge from the leaf axils on short pedicels. The reproductive structures include five distinct petals and a central cluster of stamens.

Its overall growth habit is characterized by a delicate, sprawling texture that allows it to spread across the soil surface.
