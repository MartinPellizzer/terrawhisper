Lysimachia capillipes

What's the taxonomical classification of Lysimachia capillipes?

Lysimachia capillipes belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, falling under the order Ericales. This plant is a member of the family Primulaceae, which contains its specific genus, Lysimachia, and is ultimately identified by its unique species name, capillipes.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lysimachia capillipes has slender, creeping stems that produce small, lanceolate leaves arranged oppositely along the branches. These leaves typically measure only a few millimeters in length and possess entire margins with acute tips. The plant produces small, yellow, star-shaped flowers that emerge from the leaf axils on short pedicels. The reproductive structures include five distinct petals and a central cluster of stamens. Its overall growth habit is characterized by a delicate, sprawling texture that allows it to spread across the soil surface.

A validated LC-MS/MS method was developed for the simultaneous determination of these analytes in rat plasma over a curve range of 10.0 to 5000 ng/ml. This method was applied to determine the concentrations of these components in incurred rat plasma samples following the administration of Lysimachia capillipes Hemsl extract.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds Capilliposide A, Capilliposide B, Capilliposide C, Capilliposide I, and Capilliposide II. These specific chemical constituents are categorized as bioactive secondary metabolites within the plant's structure. Researchers study these various capilliposides to understand their potential pharmacological properties and biological activities. Each distinct compound possesses a unique molecular arrangement that may influence its interaction with cellular targets. Understanding the relationship between these chemical profiles and their medicinal effects is essential for future drug development.

As discussed in a study, three novel triterpenoid saponins were isolated from an ethanol extract of Lysimachia capillipes. These compounds, identified as capilliposide A (1), capilliposide B (2), and capilliposide C (3), had their structures determined using 1D and 2D NMR techniques such as 1H-1H COSY, HMBC, HMQC, DEPT, and TOCSY, as well as MS and hydrolysis. Among these, capilliposide B (2) exhibited significant cytotoxicity against human A-2780 cells.
