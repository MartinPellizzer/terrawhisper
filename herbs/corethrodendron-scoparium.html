Corethrodendron scoparium

What's the taxonomical classification of Corethrodendron scoparium?

Corethrodendroon scoparium belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Fabales. This plant is a member of the family Fabaceae, which is further refined into its specific genus, Corethrodenron, and finally identified by its species name, scoparium.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Corethrodendroon scoparium has woody, spreading stems that produce narrow, lanceolate leaves with serrated margins and small, yellow composite flower heads.

The foliage is typically grayish-green and can be either glabrous or slightly hairy depending on the specific environment. Each flower head consists of numerous yellow ray florets surrounding a center of disk florets. These inflorescences often appear in dense clusters at the ends of the branches.

The overall structure of the plant is bushy and can reach several feet in height during the growing season.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Corethrodendron scoparium can cause contact dermatitis, characterized by skin redness, itching, or a blistering rash upon direct contact with its foliage. While the plant is generally considered non-toxic to ingest in small amounts, consuming significant quantities may lead to gastrointestinal upset or stomach discomfort. Some individuals might also experience localized allergic reactions if the plant's oils touch sensitive areas of the body. It is always prudent to handle this species with care to avoid these dermatological issues. Always consult a medical professional if a severe skin reaction or illness occurs after exposure.
