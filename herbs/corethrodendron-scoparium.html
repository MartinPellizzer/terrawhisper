Corethrodendron scoparium

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Corethrodendroon scoparium has woody, spreading stems that produce narrow, lanceolate leaves with serrated margins and small, yellow composite flower heads. The foliage is typically grayish-green and can be either glabrous or slightly hairy depending on the specific environment. Each flower head consists of numerous yellow ray florets surrounding a center of disk florets. These inflorescences often appear in dense clusters at the ends of the branches. The overall structure of the plant is bushy and can reach several feet in height during the growing season.
