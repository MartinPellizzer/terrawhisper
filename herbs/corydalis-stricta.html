Corydalis stricta

What's the taxonomical classification of Corydalis stricta?

Corydalis stricta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Ranunculales. The plant is further categorized under the family Papaveraceae, which places it among a diverse group of flowering plants. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Corydalis and the species stricta.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

The foliage is distinctly bluish-green, providing a soft texture to the overall structure. What are the pharmacological activities of Corydalis stricta?

This plant has significant pharmacological activities including analgesic, sedative, antitussive, and antispasmodic properties. These effects are primarily attributed to the presence of isoquinoline alkaloids such as protopine and corydaline. These bioactive compounds interact with the central nervous system to alleviate pain and reduce coughing reflexes. Research also indicates that various extracts from the plant can exert calming effects on the nervous system. Additionally, its ability to relax smooth muscles makes it useful for treating gastrointestinal spasms.
