Fagopyrum Esculentum (Fagopyrum esculentum) Compounds

Fagopyrum esculentum contains several key compounds, including flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its antioxidant properties. Glycosides and coumarins are also present, offering anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects. Saponins and terpenoids support immune function, while anthocyanins provide pigmentation and additional antioxidant benefits. Essential oils and alkaloids add to its complex pharmacological profile.

Fagopyrum Esculentum (Fagopyrum esculentum) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are key active compounds in Fagopyrum esculentum, known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for treating ailments like arthritis and skin conditions. They are also believed to support cardiovascular health and immune function.

Tannin is a key active compound in Fagopyrum esculentum, commonly known as buckwheat. It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Tannins in buckwheat may also support digestive health and reduce intestinal inflammation.

6. Saponin

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Fagopyrum esculentum contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. 8. Anthocyanin

9. Essential oil

Essential oil from Fagopyrum esculentum contains volatile compounds like sesquiterpenes and monoterpenes. It is used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. 10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a bioactive compound found in Fagopyrum esculentum, commonly known as buckwheat.
