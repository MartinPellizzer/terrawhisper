What's the taxonomical classification of Michelia skinneriana?

Michelia skinneriana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its descent through the order Magnoliales, the plant is placed within the family Magnoliaceae. It is specifically identified under the genus Michelia, with its unique botanical designation being the species skinneriana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Michelia skinneriana has elliptic to oblong leaves that are leathery in texture, glossy on the upper surface, and possess entire margins with pinnate venation.

The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub with a dense, spreading canopy. Its flowers are solitary and axillary, featuring creamy white or pale yellow petals that emit a fragrant scent. The fruit consists of woody follicles that develop in clusters and eventually split to reveal bright red seeds.

These seeds are often surrounded by a fleshy aril which attracts various dispersers.
