Eastern Baltic Hawthorn (Crataegus Orientobaltica)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Crataegus orientobaltica.

Eastern baltic hawthorn, scientifically known as Crataegus orientobaltica, is a medicinal herb native to the Eastern Baltic region, valued for its adaptogenic properties and traditional use in herbal medicine. This plant is particularly noted for its potential cardiovascular benefits, including the support of heart health, improved circulation, and stress reduction due to its high content of flavonoids and oligomeric proanthocyanidins. Traditionally, it has been used in Baltic folk medicine to treat ailments such as hypertension, anxiety, and insomnia, often prepared as a tea or tincture. In modern wellness practices, it is increasingly incorporated into supplements and herbal formulations aimed at enhancing cardiovascular function and promoting emotional balance. One unique feature of C. orientobaltica is its rare concentration of certain flavonoids, which may contribute to its distinctive tart flavor and its historical use in both culinary and medicinal contexts.
