Pilea tsiangiana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pilea tsiangiana has thin, cordate leaves with crenate margins and a distinctively textured, somewhat crinkled surface that emerges from slender, reddish-to-greenish stems. The foliage typically exhibits a vibrant green color and a delicate, somewhat translucent quality. These leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem, creating a dense, bushy appearance. The plant's overall structure is low-growing and creeping, making it well-suited for shaded environments. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced on specialized stalks, though the primary morphological interest remains in the leaf shape and texture.
