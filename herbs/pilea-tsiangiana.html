What's the taxonomical classification of Pilea tsiangiana?

Pilea tsiangiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized under the order Rosales. This plant is a member of the family Urticaceae, which places it within the genus Pilea, and its specific identification is defined by the species tsiangiana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pilea tsiangiana has thin, cordate leaves with crenate margins and a distinctively textured, somewhat crinkled surface that emerges from slender, reddish-to-greenish stems. The foliage typically exhibits a vibrant green color and a delicate, somewhat translucent quality. These leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem, creating a dense, bushy appearance. The plant's overall structure is low-growing and creeping, making it well-suited for shaded environments.
