What's the taxonomical classification of Sagina saginoides?

Sagina saginoides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, eventually reaching the order Caryophyllales. This plant is a member of the family Caryophyllaceae, falling under the genus Sagina, and is specifically identified by its species name, saginoides.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sagina saginoides has a prostrate, mat-forming growth habit characterized by slender, bright green stems that form dense, moss-like cushions.

The leaves are tiny, linear, and needle-like, typically measuring only a few millimeters in length. These foliage elements arise in opposite pairs along the stems, creating a fine-textured appearance. Small, white, five-petaled flowers emerge from the leaf axils during the flowering season.

The plant's root system is shallow and fibrous, allowing it to anchor effectively in moist, rocky crevices or mossy substrates.
