Sagina saginoides

What's the taxonomical classification of Sagina saginoides?

Sagina saginoides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, eventually reaching the order Caryophyllales. This plant is a member of the family Caryophyllaceae, falling under the genus Sagina, and is specifically identified by its species name, saginoides.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Sagina saginoides can cause skin irritation or allergic contact dermatitis if a sensitive individual touches the plant. There is limited scientific documentation regarding the systemic effects of accidental ingestion in humans. However, consuming any non-food plant can lead to gastrointestinal distress such as nausea or stomach cramps. It is best to avoid direct contact if you have a known history of plant allergies.
