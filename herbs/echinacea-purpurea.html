As a garden plant, eastern purple coneflower is prized for its showy purple flowers, drought tolerance, and attractiveness to pollinators. Botanically speaking, the herb belongs to the daisy family and is native to North America, where it has been used for centuries by indigenous peoples for medicinal purposes.

From its health-promoting properties to its culinary and horticultural uses, eastern purple coneflower has much to offer, and its rich history adds to its allure.

What are the side effects of eastern purple coneflower when used improperly?

When used improperly, eastern purple coneflower increases the chances of experiencing side effects, such as stomach discomfort, nausea, and diarrhea.
