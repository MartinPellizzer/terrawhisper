Racemose Inula (Inula racemosa) Compounds

Racemose Inula contains several key compounds, including flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and terpenoids, known for their anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins and phenolic acids contribute to its astringent and antimicrobial qualities. Essential oils, glycosides, and saponins also play roles in its medicinal effects, while coumarin and alkaloids add to its pharmacological profile. Anthocyanins provide antioxidant support and contribute to the plant's color.

Racemose Inula (Inula racemosa) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Racemose Inula (Inula racemosa) are bioactive compounds known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments. They help modulate immune responses and protect cellular structures from oxidative damage.
