Calogyne pilosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Calogyne pilosa?

Calogyne pilosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Asterales. Within this order, it is placed in the family Goodeniaceae, which further narrows its classification to the genus Calogyne. Finally, the specific identity of this plant is established by its species name, pilosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Calogyne pilosa has an erect, perennial herb structure characterized by long, slender stems and narrow, linear leaves that grow in a basal rosette or along the stem.

The foliage is typically covered in fine, soft hairs that give the plant a pubescent texture. Its most striking feature is the terminal inflorescence, which consists of numerous small, white flowers clustered on a branched panicle. Each individual flower possesses delicate, spreading petals and prominent stamens that emerge from the center.

The roots are often tuberous, allowing the plant to persist through unfavorable growing seasons.
