Calogyne pilosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Calogyne pilosa has an erect, perennial herb structure characterized by long, slender stems and narrow, linear leaves that grow in a basal rosette or along the stem. The foliage is typically covered in fine, soft hairs that give the plant a pubescent texture. Its most striking feature is the terminal inflorescence, which consists of numerous small, white flowers clustered on a branched panicle. Each individual flower possesses delicate, spreading petals and prominent stamens that emerge from the center. The roots are often tuberous, allowing the plant to persist through unfavorable growing seasons.
