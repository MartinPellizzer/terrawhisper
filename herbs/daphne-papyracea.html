Daphne papyracea

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Daphne papyracea has opposite, leathery leaves that are elliptic to lanceolate in shape and characterized by a distinctively thin, paper-like texture. These evergreen leaves are typically dark green on the upper surface and slightly paler underneath. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that emerge from the leaf axils in clusters. Its woody stems are slender and often display a smooth bark texture as the plant matures. The overall structure remains relatively compact, making it a distinctive shrub in its natural habitat.
