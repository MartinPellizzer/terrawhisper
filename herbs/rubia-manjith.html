Rubia manjith

What's the taxonomical classification of Rubia manjith?

Rubia manjith belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Gentianaales. This plant is a member of the Rubiaceae family, falling under the genus Rubia, and is specifically identified by its species name, manjith.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rubia manjith has a perennial climbing or creeping habit characterized by slender, woody stems that grow from a thick, fleshy, and branched rhizomatous root system. The leaves are typically arranged in whorls of four to six at each node and possess an elliptical to oblong shape with acute tips. Small, tubular, and inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often appearing in dense, terminal clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy drupes that transition from green to a deep red color upon ripening. These morphological features collectively allow the plant to scramble over surrounding vegetation in its natural habitat.

What are the pharmacological activities of Rubia manjith?

This plant has diverse pharmacological activities including anti-inflammatory, anti-tumor, and antiproliferative properties. Its chemical constituents work to reduce swelling and inhibit the production of inflammatory mediators in biological systems. The plant also demonstrates significant anti-tumor potential by targeting the development of malignant cells. Furthermore, its antiproliferative effects help slow the rapid division of abnormal cells within various tissues. These combined therapeutic actions make the species a subject of interest for medicinal research.

According to a study published by "Fitoterapia", Rubiua manjith is a medicinal resource enriched with quinone derivatives that has been traditionally used to treat hematemesis, hematuria, inflammation, ulcers, and dermatological disorders. From the roots and rhizomes of the plant, 4 previously undescribed anthraquinones (1-4) and 31 known quinones (5-35) were isolated and identified. These constituents were assessed for cytotoxic activities against several colorectal cancer cell lines. Among these, 6 constituents exhibited pronounced antiproliferative effects. Network pharmacology analysis combined with molecular docking was used to elucidate the potential mechanisms underlying the anti-tumor activity of these 6 components.
