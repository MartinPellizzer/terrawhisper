Uncaria lancifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Gentianaales. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Uncaria lancifolia has woody, climbing stems equipped with recurved, hook-like spines that facilitate its ascent through forest canopies. The leaves are typically lanceolate or elliptical in shape with entire margins and an acute apex. Its foliage displays a distinct pinnate venation pattern on the underside of the blade. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the axillary regions of the stems.
