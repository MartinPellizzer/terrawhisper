Arak (Salvadora persica) Uses

Arak, or Salvadora persica, is used to treat gingivitis, oral infections, and bad breath. It helps prevent tooth decay and periodontal disease due to its antimicrobial properties. The plant also aids in wound healing and managing mouth ulcers, oral candidiasis, and oral malodor.

Arak (Salvadora persica) Monograph

1. Gingivitis

Arak, or Salvadora persica, has been traditionally used to treat gingivitis due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The plant's roots contain compounds that help reduce bacterial growth in the mouth, thereby alleviating gum inflammation and bleeding. It is often prepared as a mouthwash or chewed to promote oral hygiene and prevent periodontal disease.

2. Oral infections

Arak, or Salvadora persica, is used traditionally to treat oral infections due to its antimicrobial properties. The plant's roots contain compounds that inhibit bacteria causing gum disease and bad breath. It is often chewed or used as a mouthwash to promote oral hygiene and reduce inflammation. Its natural astringent properties also help in healing mouth ulcers and infections.

3. Bad breath

Arak, derived from Salvadora persica, is used traditionally to combat bad breath. Its antibacterial properties help reduce oral pathogens that cause halitosis. Chewing the dried root or using its powder can freshen breath and promote oral hygiene. It is a natural alternative in herbal medicine for maintaining fresh breath.

4. Tooth decay

Arak, or Salvadora persica, is traditionally used to prevent and treat tooth decay due to its antimicrobial properties. The plant contains compounds like trimethylamine and flavonoids that inhibit oral bacteria. It is often chewed or used as a toothpaste to promote oral hygiene and reduce plaque. Its natural antiseptic qualities make it a valuable herb in traditional dental care.

5. Periodontal disease

Arak, or Salvadora persica, has been traditionally used to treat periodontal disease due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The plant's roots contain compounds that help reduce gum inflammation and inhibit bacterial growth, making it effective in preventing plaque and supporting oral health. Its use in herbal medicine highlights its role in natural periodontal care.

6. Skin infections

Arak, or Salvadora persica, is used traditionally to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The plant's root contains compounds that inhibit bacterial growth, making it effective for conditions like acne, fungal infections, and wounds. Its astringent qualities also help in reducing inflammation and promoting skin healing.

7. Wound healing

Arak, or Salvadora persica, is used traditionally to promote wound healing due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The plant's extracts help prevent infection and accelerate tissue repair. It is often applied topically to minor cuts, burns, and ulcers. Its active compounds support cellular regeneration and reduce scarring.

8. Mouth ulcers

Arak, or Salvadora persica, is used traditionally to treat mouth ulcers due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The gum resin helps soothe oral tissues and promote healing. It can be chewed directly or prepared as a paste to apply to ulcers, providing relief and reducing infection risk.

9. Oral candidiasis

Arak, or Salvadora persica, has been traditionally used to treat oral candidiasis due to its antimicrobial properties. The plant's roots contain compounds that inhibit the growth of Candida species. It can be prepared as a mouthwash or applied directly to affected areas. Its natural antifungal effects make it a valuable remedy in herbal medicine for oral infections.

10. Oral malodor

Arak, or Salvadora persica, is used to address oral malodor due to its antimicrobial properties. The plant contains compounds that reduce bacterial growth in the mouth. It is often chewed or used as a toothpaste to freshen breath and maintain oral hygiene. Its natural cleansing action helps prevent bad odor.
