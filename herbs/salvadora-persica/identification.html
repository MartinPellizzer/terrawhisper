Botanical Identification of Arak (Salvadora persica)

To identify Arak (Salvadora persica) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Salvadora persica, commonly known as Arak, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Salicales, family Salicaceae, genus Salvadora, and species Salvadora persica. This classification places it within the broader group of flowering plants and highlights its evolutionary relationships within the Salicaceae family.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The botanical name of Arak is Salvadora persica, a species within the family Malvaceae. It was first described by the botanist Carl Peter Thunberg in 1784. The name reflects its native origin in Persia, now Iran, and is widely recognized in scientific literature.

Morphological Characteristics

Arak, or Salvadora persica, is a small, deciduous tree with a spreading growth habit. It has a deep taproot system, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The stem is thick, woody, and often branched, with a rough, fissured bark. Leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a leathery texture, while the inflorescence consists of terminal racemes. Flowers are small, yellowish-green, and borne in clusters. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hard seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Salvadora persica can be distinguished by its distinctive yellow, sticky resinous sap, trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and small pink or white flowers. It also has a unique branched root system and produces small, elongated, woody fruits. These features help differentiate it from similar species in the Salvadora genus.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Salvadora persica, or Arak, is native to regions of southern Asia, the Middle East, and northern Africa. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates with well-drained soils. It is commonly found at low altitudes, typically between 0 and 1,000 meters above sea level.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Arak (Salvadora persica) blooms from March to May, producing small pink or red flowers. Seed formation occurs from June to August. For identification purposes, the best time to harvest is during the dry season, when the plant is most identifiable and the leaves are fully developed.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Salvadora persica belongs to the family Salicaceae, closely related to species like Salix and Populus. It shares characteristics such as opposite leaves and bark with other members of the genus. Its phylogenetic placement reflects evolutionary ties to other Salicaceae plants, aiding in its identification within the broader botanical context.
