Taraxacum platypecidum

What's the taxonomical classification of Taraxacum platypecidum?

Taraxacum platypecidum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving through its higher taxonomic ranks, it falls under the order Asterales and is a member of the family Asteraceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific genus, Taraxacum, and its unique species designation, platypecidum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Taraxacum platypecidum has a basal rosette of oblong to lanceolate leaves characterized by deeply lobed or dentate margins and a distinctively flattened growth habit. The leaves are typically dark green and lack the long petioles found in many other dandelion species. A single, leafless hollow stem emerges from the center of the rosette to support a solitary flower head. This inflorescence consists of numerous bright yellow ligulate florets clustered together. Following pollination, the plant produces a spherical head of achenes equipped with fine pappus bristles for wind dispersal.
