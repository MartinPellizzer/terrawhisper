Crataegus scabrifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Crataegus scabrifolia has small, ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated margins and a scabrous, sandpaper-like texture on both surfaces. The foliage is characterized by a rough, scabrid pubescence that distinguishes it from other hawthorn species. Its woody stems are typically armed with sharp spines and may exhibit a grayish bark texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into fleshy, globose pomes. These morphological traits collectively reflect its adaptation to specific environmental niches.
