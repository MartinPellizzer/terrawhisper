Crataegus scabrifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Crataegus scabrifolia?

Craeataegus scabrifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized under Equisetopsida, falling into the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Rosales and the family Rosaceae. Ultimately, its taxonomic identity is defined by its membership in the genus Crataegus, specifically identified as the species scabrifolia.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Crataegus scabrifolia contains high concentrations of bioactive flavonoids, triterpenoids, and phenolic acids that provide significant pharmacological properties. These specific compounds work together to exhibit potent antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and cardioprotective effects within biological systems. The triterpenoid fraction is particularly noted for its ability to stabilize cell membranes and modulate various enzymatic activities. Additionally, the presence of organic acids contributes to the plant's overall antimicrobial profile. Research suggests these chemical constituents may assist in managing oxidative stress and improving vascular health.

What are the therapeutic actions of Crataegus scabrifolia?

Crataegus scabrifolia has cardioprotective and hypotensive properties that primarily function by enhancing myocardial blood flow and regulating blood pressure through the modulation of calcium channels. The plant contains high concentrations of bioactive flavonoids and oligomeric procyanidins that act as potent antioxidants to reduce oxidative stress within cardiac tissues. These phytochemicals assist in preventing the oxidation of low-density lipoproteins, which helps mitigate the progression of atherosclerosis. Furthermore, its vasodilatory effects help decrease peripheral resistance, thereby easing the workload on the heart during physical exertion. This combination of actions makes the species a significant subject of study for managing various cardiovascular dysfunctions.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat cardiovascular conditions such as hypertension, congestive heart failure, and angina pectoris. Its therapeutic properties are primarily attributed to high concentrations of bioactive flavonoids and oligomeric proanthocyanidins. These compounds help improve blood flow by relaxing the smooth muscles of the blood vessels. Additionally, the plant exhibits antioxidant effects that protect the heart tissue from oxidative stress. Some studies also suggest it may assist in managing high cholesterol levels.
