Crataegus scabrifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Crataegus scabrifolia?

Craeataegus scabrifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized under Equisetopsida, falling into the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Rosales and the family Rosaceae. Ultimately, its taxonomic identity is defined by its membership in the genus Crataegus, specifically identified as the species scabrifolia.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Crataegus scabrifolia contains medicinal compounds primarily located within its leaves, flowers, and fruits. The leaves are often utilized for their potential antioxidant properties and calming effects. Extracts from the flowers are frequently studied for their ability to support cardiovascular health. Additionally, the fleshy fruits provide a source of vitamins and bioactive phytochemicals used in traditional remedies. These various parts are processed into teas, tinctures, or dried supplements to harness their therapeutic benefits.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains flavonoids, oligomeric procyanidins, alkaloids, triterpenoids, and phenolic acids which serve as its primary medicinal compounds.

These diverse phytochemicals work together to provide significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Specifically, the oligomeric procyanidins and flavonoids are known for their ability to protect cells from oxidative stress. The presence of alkaloids and triterpenoids suggests potential pharmacological activities related to cardiovascular health.

Additionally, the phenolic acids contribute to the overall therapeutic profile by scavenging free radicals.

The primary medicinal compounds of this plant are shown in the list below.

  • Flavonoids
  • Oligomeric procyanidins
  • Alkaloids
  • Triterpenoids
  • Phenolic acids

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Crataegus scabrifolia targets various enzymes and signaling proteins, specifically inhibiting acetylcholinesterase, butyrylcholinesterase, and monoamine oxidase to provide neuroprotective effects. Its bioactive flavonoids and polyphenols interact with nitric oxide synthase to regulate vascular tone and improve blood flow. These compounds also modulate inflammatory pathways by suppressing cyclooxygenase-2 and lipoxygenase enzymes. Furthermore, the plant's constituents act on antioxidant enzymes such as superoxide dismutase to mitigate oxidative stress within cellular environments. Through these diverse protein interactions, the plant exerts significant cardiovascular and neuroprotective medicinal activities.

What are the therapeutic actions of Crataegus scabrifolia?

Crataegus scabrifolia has cardioprotective and hypotensive properties that primarily function by enhancing myocardial blood flow and regulating blood pressure through the modulation of calcium channels. The plant contains high concentrations of bioactive flavonoids and oligomeric procyanidins that act as potent antioxidants to reduce oxidative stress within cardiac tissues. These phytochemicals assist in preventing the oxidation of low-density lipoproteins, which helps mitigate the progression of atherosclerosis. Furthermore, its vasodilatory effects help decrease peripheral resistance, thereby easing the workload on the heart during physical exertion. This combination of actions makes the species a significant subject of study for managing various cardiovascular dysfunctions.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat cardiovascular conditions such as hypertension, congestive heart failure, and angina pectoris. Its therapeutic properties are primarily attributed to high concentrations of bioactive flavonoids and oligomeric proanthocyanidins. These compounds help improve blood flow by relaxing the smooth muscles of the blood vessels. Additionally, the plant exhibits antioxidant effects that protect the heart tissue from oxidative stress. Some studies also suggest it may assist in managing high cholesterol levels.

What are the herbal preparations of Crataegus scabrifolia?

Crataegus scabrifolia is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a tincture to extract its bioactive compounds for medicinal use. The dried leaves and fruits are often simmered in water to create a potent decoction that concentrates the plant's chemical constituents. Alternatively, steeping the plant material in hot water for a shorter duration produces a milder infusion suitable for daily consumption. Tinctures made with high-proof alcohol allow for long-term storage and a higher concentration of specific phytochemicals. These various methods ensure that the therapeutic properties of the plant are effectively captured in different liquid forms.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Crataegus scabrifolia can cause adverse reactions such as gastrointestinal distress, dizziness, and potential interactions with cardiovascular medications if consumed in large quantities. While specific clinical data on this exact species is limited, members of the Crataegus genus are known to affect heart rate and blood pressure. Users may experience nausea or stomach cramping if the plant material is ingested improperly. There is also a risk of hypoglycemia for individuals managing blood sugar levels due to the plant's chemical properties. Careful monitoring is necessary to avoid unintended physiological changes during use.
