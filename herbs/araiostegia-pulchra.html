What's the taxonomical classification of Araiostegia pulchra?

Araiostegia pulchra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. Its hierarchical classification continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae, placing it under the order Polypodiales. Within this order, the plant is situated in the family Polypodiaceae. Ultimately, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement in the genus Araiostegia, with the specific epithet pulchra denoting its unique species designation.

      What are the herbal preparations of Araiostegia pulchra?

      Araiostegia pulchra is prepared as a decoction, a concentrated infusion, or a topical poultice to utilize its bioactive properties. The plant material is typically boiled in water for extended periods to extract its medicinal compounds for oral consumption. Infusions are often preferred when a milder strength is required for daily wellness support.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Araiostegia pulchra can cause skin irritation, localized redness, and potential allergic dermatitis upon direct contact with the human epidermis. The plant contains chemical compounds that may trigger a hypersensitivity reaction in sensitive individuals. If the sap or crushed leaves touch the skin, it might result in itching or a burning sensation. These dermatological symptoms are typically confined to the area of contact but can vary in intensity. Careful handling is recommended to avoid unexpected physiological responses.
