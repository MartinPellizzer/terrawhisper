Dodder (Cuscuta europaea) Compounds

Dodder contains several primary active compounds, including tannins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its astringent and anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarin and alkaloids may have antispasmodic and neuroactive effects, while glycosides and saponins support detoxification and immune function. Terpenoids, essential oils, and anthocyanins add additional antioxidant and antimicrobial benefits.

Dodder (Cuscuta europaea) Monograph

1. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Dodder (Cuscuta europaea). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is used in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Tannins in Dodder are believed to support digestive health and may aid in wound healing.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Dodder (Cuscuta europaea) are bioactive compounds known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in supporting immune function and reducing oxidative stress. They are often extracted from the dried plant material used in herbal formulations. Flavonoids may also enhance the plant's therapeutic potential in various health applications.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Dodder (Cuscuta europaea). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. This compound is often extracted to support its therapeutic applications in various health conditions.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Dodder (Cuscuta europaea). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for its antispasmodic and sedative properties. Coumarin may support digestive health and has been studied for its potential anti-inflammatory effects.

5. Alkaloid

Alkaloids in Dodder (Cuscuta europaea) are bioactive compounds known for their potential medicinal properties. These alkaloids may contribute to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine, though research on their specific effects and mechanisms is ongoing.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Dodder (Cuscuta europaea). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for its potential tonic and restorative properties. Glycosides in Dodder are believed to support vitality and may have mild adaptogenic effects.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Dodder (Cuscuta europaea). It exhibits antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine. Saponin contributes to the plant's ability to enhance immune function and promote healing.

8. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are key active compounds in Dodder (Cuscuta europaea), contributing to its traditional use in herbal medicine. These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting the plant's role in treating ailments like respiratory and digestive issues. They are also implicated in the plant's parasitic growth and metabolic interactions with host species.

9. Essential oil

The essential oil of Dodder (Cuscuta europaea) contains volatile compounds like terpenes and phenols. It is used in herbal medicine for its purported antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties. The oil is often extracted through steam distillation from the dried plant material. Its therapeutic use is supported by traditional practices and some modern research.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a pigment found in Dodder (Cuscuta europaea). It contributes to the plant's color and has antioxidant properties. In herbal medicine, it may support cardiovascular health and skin conditions. Its presence highlights the plant's potential therapeutic value.
