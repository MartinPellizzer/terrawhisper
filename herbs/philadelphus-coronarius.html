Coronary Philadelphus (Philadelphus coronarius)

Coronary Philadelphus (Philadelphus coronarius) is a member of the Caprifoliaceae family, native to Europe, and Asia. Traditionally, its flowers, leaves, and bark have been used for infusions, decoctions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its sedative, tonic, and anti-inflammatory actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and traditional chinese medicine.

Perennial herbaceous plant.

Leaves
Opposite, ovate leaves with dark green upper surface and lighter green lower surface, featuring prominent stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Inflorescences are terminal racemes with actinomorphic flowers having five white petals and five yellow sepals, each flower possessing a prominent glandular disc at the base.
Stems
Elongated, woody stems with opposite branching, smooth surfaces, and persistent buds.
