Coronary Philadelphus

Philadelphus coronarius

Coronary Philadelphus (Philadelphus coronarius) is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia, known for its medicinal properties and historical use in treating cardiovascular conditions.

Botanical Identification

Philadelphus coronarius, commonly known as Coronary Philadelphus, is a species in the Caprifoliaceae family. It is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves and fragrant, white to pink flowers. It is also known as Mock Orange in some regions.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Magnoliopsida
  • Order: Dipsacales
  • Family: Caprifoliaceae
  • Genus: Philadelphus
  • Species: Philadelphus coronarius

Active Compounds

The main active compounds of this herb include flavonoids, coumarins, and phenolic acids. These compounds work together to provide anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Their synergistic action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory conditions such as coughs and colds.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and to treat infections.

Tincture: Used for skin conditions and as an antiseptic.

Safety Profile

Coronary Philadelphus is generally considered safe when used appropriately. However, it should not be used by pregnant or nursing women due to potential risks. Always consult a healthcare professional before use, especially if you have existing health conditions or are on medication.
