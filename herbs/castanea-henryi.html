Castanea henryi

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Castanea henryi has simple, lanceolate leaves that measure 10 to 25 centimeters in length and possess serrated margins with prominent pinnate venation. The foliage is typically dark green on the upper surface and slightly paler underneath. This species features small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in catkins along the branches. Its fruit consists of a woody, spiny burr that encloses one or two edible chestnuts. The overall structure is that of a medium-sized deciduous tree with a spreading canopy.
