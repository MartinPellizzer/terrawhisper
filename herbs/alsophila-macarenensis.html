Alsophila macarenensis

Alsophila macarenensis, also known as the Macarena tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical forests of South America, particularly in Colombia and Venezuela.

It has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, including its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, which are derived from its rhizome and leaves.

The plant is believed to have various health benefits, such as reducing fever, relieving pain, and treating respiratory infections, due to its expectorant and bronchodilatory properties.

Alsophila macarenensis is often used in herbal preparations, including infusions, decoctions, and topical applications, to take advantage of its medicinal properties and promote overall well-being.
