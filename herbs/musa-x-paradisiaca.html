Musa × paradisiaca

What's the taxonomical classification of Musa × paradisiaca?

Musa × paradisiaca belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Zingiberales. As a member of the family Musaceae, it is categorized under the genus Musa, specifically identified by the species name paradisiaca.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Musa × paradisiaca has a large pseudostem composed of tightly overlapping leaf sheaths, erect leaves with prominent midribs, and a terminal inflorescence bearing fleshy berries. The plant lacks a true woody stem, instead relying on the structural integrity of these layered leaf bases to support its height. Long, oblong leaves extend from the top of the pseudostem, featuring parallel venation that facilitates water runoff. The reproductive structure consists of a large heart-shaped bud that eventually reveals rows of flowers. These flowers develop into clusters of fleshy, seedless fruits known as bananas, which hang in heavy bunches.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia and the South Pacific islands, though it is now cultivated throughout nearly all equatorial zones worldwide. Its expansive distribution is driven by its requirement for frost-free environments and high humidity found in the tropics. Major production hubs include India, China, Indonesia, and Brazil, where vast plantations dominate the landscape. It has successfully spread to parts of Africa and Central America due to its adaptability to various tropical agricultural systems. Consequently, this hybrid species can be found in almost every warm, moist climate on the planet.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Musa × paradisiaca contains medicinal compounds in its fruit, flowers, stem, sap, and leaves. What are the pharmacological activities of Musa × paradisiaca?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological activities including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, leishmanicidal, phospholipase-A2 (PL-A2) inhibitory, and red blood cell (RBC) membrane stabilization effects. The antioxidant properties help mitigate oxidative stress by neutralizing harmful free radicals within the body. Its ability to inhibit phospholipase-A2 contributes to its anti-inflammatory profile by regulating the release of arachidonic acid. Furthermore, the plant shows potential as a leishmanicidal agent against specific parasitic infections. Finally, the stabilization of red blood cell membranes suggests a protective role against hemolytic processes.

According to a study published by "Chemistry & biodiversity", Musa × paradisiaca fruit peels contain a steroid compound with various pharmacological activities. The hexane fraction of the methanol extract showed significant oedema reduction in rats at 200 mg/kg during in vivo anti-inflammatory assays. This compound displays antioxidant properties through Fe3+ reduction and scavenging of free radical molecules. It also demonstrates the ability to limit RBC haemolysis and inhibits PL-A2. Molecular docking showed the compound has strong affinities for PL-A2 at -7.6 kcal/mol and NF-κB at -6.0 kcal/mol. Characterization of the molecule included UV-VIS spectrum absorbance at 222 nm and FTIR absorption at 2922, 2855, 1707, 1643, 1453 and 1375 cm-1.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause allergic reactions, digestive discomfort, or interference with blood clotting medication if consumed in excessive amounts. Individuals with a latex allergy may experience cross-reactivity, which can lead to itching or hives. High potassium content in the fruit can be dangerous for those with kidney disease who cannot regulate electrolyte levels properly. Some people might also experience bloating or gas due to the high fiber content found in the fruit. Monitoring portion sizes is recommended for sensitive populations to avoid these physiological responses.
