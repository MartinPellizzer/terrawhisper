What is Penstemon lemhiensis?

Penstemon lemhiensis is a species of flowering plant in the family Plantaginaceae.

It is an herbaceous perennial with erect stems, 30-80 cm tall, and basal leaves that are 2-10 cm long, linear to lance-shaped, and have entire margins. The leaves are glabrous or hairy on the lower surface. The plant produces white to cream-colored flowers in late spring, in axillary clusters, with a corolla tube that is 20-25 mm long, and the anthers are yellow.

The species is endemic to central Idaho, USA.
