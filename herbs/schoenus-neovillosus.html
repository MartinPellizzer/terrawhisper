What is Schoenus neovillosus?

Schoenus neovillosus is a species of sedge that belongs to the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous growth habit, typically growing to 0.5-1.5 meters in height. The plant has linear-lanceolate leaves, 2-6 millimeters wide, that are flat or slightly convex, and are arranged in three ranks. The inflorescence is a spike-like panicle, 2-8 centimeters long, composed of 3-10 spikelets that are arranged in a trimerous pattern.

The spikelets are 1.5-3 millimeters long, with a single fertile floret and 2-3 sterile bracts.
