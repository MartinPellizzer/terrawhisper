Foeniculum vulgare seed, commonly known as the fennel seed, is one of the most widely used medicinal parts of the plant, valued for its aromatic and therapeutic properties.

Side Effects of foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare leads to nausea, particularly when consumed in large quantities or by individuals with a known sensitivity to the herb.

This herb can also cause digestive issues such as bloating and gas, which may exacerbate existing gastrointestinal conditions. Stomach upset is another common side effect, often accompanied by a dry mouth due to the herb's stimulating properties. In some cases, foeniculum vulgare might induce vomiting, especially if ingested in excess or on an empty stomach.

Additionally, it can result in bad breath, a side effect attributed to its strong aromatic compounds. Some people may experience allergic reactions, ranging from mild skin irritation to more severe symptoms like difficulty breathing. The herb might also lead to heartburn or a burning sensation in the chest, particularly in individuals with acid reflux. Dizziness has been reported in rare instances, possibly due to its mild sedative or stimulant effects.

It is important to consult a healthcare professional before using foeniculum vulgare, especially for those with pre-existing health conditions or who are taking medications.
