Aster panduratus

What's the taxonomical classification of Aster panduratus?

Aster panduratus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical path of its lineage, it falls under the order Asterales and the family Asteraceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Aster, and its specific species name, panduratus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aster panduratus has a perennial growth habit characterized by erect stems that typically reach heights of 20 to 50 centimeters and bear numerous small, radiate flower heads. The leaves are primarily basal and lanceolate in shape, often featuring serrated margins and a slightly hairy texture. The floral structures consist of white ray florets surrounding a central disc of yellow tubular florets. These composite flowers are arranged in terminal clusters that emerge from the upper nodes of the stem. The root system is fibrous, allowing the plant to stabilize itself within its native grassy habitats.
