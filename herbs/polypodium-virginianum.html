What's the taxonomical classification of Polypodium virginianum?

Polypodium virginianum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Polypodiales and the family Polypodiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Polypodium, and its specific species designation, virginianum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polypodium virginianum has long-creeping rhizomes that produce pinnate fronds measuring between 10 and 30 centimeters in length with lanceolate to oblong pinnae. The individual leaflets are typically entire or slightly lobed at the margins and possess a leathery texture. On the underside of these leaves, the plant features small, circular sori arranged in rows along the veins. These reproductive structures are often protected by a thin indusium. The overall growth habit allows the plant to spread effectively across forest floors and rock surfaces.
