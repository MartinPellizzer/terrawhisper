Blue mistflower

Calea ternifolia

Blue Mistflower (Calea ternifolia) is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States, known for its striking blue-violet flowers and traditional use in treating respiratory conditions such as asthma and bronchitis.

Botanical Identification

Calea ternifolia, commonly known as Blue Mistflower, belongs to the Asteraceae family. It is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America. The plant features blue-violet flowers, trifoliate leaves, and a fibrous root system. It is also referred to as "Blueweed" in some regional texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Liliopsida
  • Order: Asphodelales
  • Family: Acanthaceae
  • Genus: Calea
  • Species: Calea ternifolia

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for respiratory and digestive ailments.

Infusion: Used for mild digestive and respiratory issues.

Safety Profile

Blue Mistflower is generally considered safe when used as directed. However, it may cause mild gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. There are no well-documented contraindications, but caution is advised during pregnancy and lactation due to limited research on its effects in these populations.
