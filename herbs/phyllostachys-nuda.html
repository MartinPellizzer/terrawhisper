What's the taxonomical classification of Phyllostachys nuda?

Phyllostachys nuda belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its hierarchical lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Poales. Furthermore, this plant is a member of the family Poaceae, falling under the genus Phyllostachys, and is specifically identified by its species name, nuda.

When treating skin irritations, the crushed plant material is applied directly as a poultice to the affected area. These various methods allow practitioners to tailor the potency and delivery of the plant's bioactive compounds.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Phyllostachys nuda can cause severe contact dermatitis, respiratory irritation, and acute gastrointestinal distress if ingested. The plant contains specific chemical compounds that react with human skin upon physical contact, often resulting in painful redness and blistering. If the fine hairs or pollen are inhaled, individuals may experience coughing, wheezing, or shortness of breath. Ingestion of any part of the organism typically leads to nausea, vomiting, and intense abdominal cramping. Medical attention is recommended if symptoms persist after exposure to this species.
