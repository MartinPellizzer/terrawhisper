Pachyrhizus erosus

What's the taxonomical classification of Pachyrhizus erosus?

Pachyrhizus erosus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further organized into the order Fabales. It is a member of the family Fabaceae, which places it within the well-known legume group. Ultimately, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its membership in the genus Pachyrhizus, with its unique biological designation being the species erosus.

What are the pharmacological activities of Pachyrhizus erosus?

This plant has antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, anti-adiposity, anti-insulin resistance, and anti-metabolic disease pharmacological activities. These diverse therapeutic properties are primarily attributed to the high concentration of bioactive compounds like diosgenin found within its tubers. The antioxidant capacity helps neutralize free radicals, thereby reducing oxidative stress throughout the body. Furthermore, its ability to combat inflammation and resist insulin changes makes it a significant subject for managing metabolic dysfunction. By targeting adiposity and metabolic pathways, the plant offers potential benefits for treating obesity and related systemic disorders.

As discussed in a study, Pachyrhizuus erosus fiber counteracts adiposity, insulin resistance, and inflammation while modulating gut microbiota composition. In mice fed with a high-fat diet, a 10% dose of yam bean fiber significantly inhibited body weight gain by 2.3 times compared to the high-fat diet group and reduced white adipose tissue mass by 2.2 times. This fiber also kept the expression of the interleukin-6 increment 2.7 times lower than the high-fat diet group within the white adipose tissue. Furthermore, Pachyrhizuus erosus fiber sustained normoglycaemia, glucose tolerance, and insulin sensitivity while preventing hyperinsulinemia. The fiber at a 10% dose is proved to be useful in the prevention of diet-induced metabolic diseases, microbiota dysbiosis, and inflammation.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds pachyerosin, rotenoids, phytic acid, phytin-phosphorus, and cell wall polysaccharides. The presence of pachyerosin and rotenoids contributes to its unique pharmacological profile. Phytic acid and its associated phytin-phosphorus elements play significant roles in the plant's biochemical interactions. Additionally, the cell wall polysaccharides provide structural benefits and potential therapeutic properties. These diverse chemical constituents work together to define the plant's medicinal utility.

As discussed in a study, Pachyrhizus erosus seeds contain a novel type I ribosome-inactivating protein known as pachyerosin. This protein has a molecular mass of 29 kDa and an isoelectric point of 9.19. Pachyerosin strongly inhibits protein synthesis of rabbit reticulocyte lysate with an IC50 of 0.37 ng/mL and shows N-glycosidase activity on rat liver ribosomes with an EC50 of 85.9 pM. When conjugated with anti-human AFP monoclonal antibodies SM0736, the resulting immunotoxin inhibits the growth of the human hepatoma cell line HuH-7 with an IC50 of 0.050 ± 0.004 nM. This efficacy is 2360 times lower than that of pachyerosin alone and 430 times lower than the immunotoxin against the human gastric cancer cell line SGC7901.
