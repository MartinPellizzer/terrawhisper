Scutellaria pekinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Scutellaria pekinensis?

Scutelariia pekinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. This plant is specifically identified by its genus, Scutellaria, and its species name, pekinenis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Scutellaria pekinenis has erect, square-shaped stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 80 centimeters and feature small, serrated leaves arranged in opposite pairs. The leaves are generally ovate or lanceolate in shape with a dull green color and a textured surface. During the flowering season, the plant produces small, tubular, two-lipped flowers that are usually light blue or purple in hue. These blossoms are arranged in dense, terminal clusters known as verticillasters that emerge from the upper nodes. The reproductive structures consist of small nutlets that are characteristic of the Lamiaceae family.
