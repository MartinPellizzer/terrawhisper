Picea bracteata

Picea bracteata, also known as the Foxtail Pine, is a species of coniferous tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

This tree has been used in traditional medicine for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The health benefits of Picea bracteata include its ability to reduce fever and alleviate respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and asthma.

Herbal preparations of Picea bracteata, often in the form of teas or tinctures, can be made by infusing the needles or cones in hot water or a solvent like ethanol.
