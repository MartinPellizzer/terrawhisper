Japanese Chrysanthemum (Chrysanthemum × Kuwashimae)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Chrysanthemum × kuwashimae.

Japanese chrysanthemum, scientifically known as Chrysanthemum × kuwashimae, is a traditional medicinal herb widely revered in East Asian cultures for its therapeutic properties. This plant is primarily valued as a natural adaptogen and anti-inflammatory agent, offering benefits such as immune support, stress reduction, and relief from respiratory ailments. Historically, it has been used in traditional Chinese and Japanese medicine to treat conditions like colds, fever, and skin irritations, often brewed into teas or applied topically. In modern wellness practices, Japanese chrysanthemum is incorporated into herbal supplements and skincare products due to its high concentration of flavonoids and its calming effects. Notably, it is unique for its delicate, slightly bitter flavor and its historical association with the Japanese imperial court, where it was cultivated for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
