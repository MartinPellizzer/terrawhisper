Aristolochia contorta

What's the taxonomical classification of Aristolochia contorta?

Aristolochia contorta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic lineage through the order Piperales, the plant is placed in the family Aristolochiaceae. Ultimately, it is identified within the genus Aristolochia, reaching its specific classification at the species level as Aristolochia contorta.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Aristolochia contorta contains aristolochic acids, which are potent nitrogenous compounds that function as nephrotoxic and carcinogenic agents. These specific alkaloids are the primary bioactive constituents responsible for the plant's physiological effects. While they have been historically studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, their high toxicity poses severe risks to human health. Clinical research emphasizes that even small doses of these compounds can lead to irreversible kidney damage and urothelial cancers. Consequently, the medicinal application of this species is strictly limited due to the extreme danger these chemical components present.

What are the therapeutic actions of Aristolochia contorta?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including malaria, inflammation, skin infections, and digestive issues. Historically, its roots and leaves have been employed in traditional medicine to combat fevers and stomach pains. Some practitioners also applied the plant to soothe wounds and skin irritations. However, it is crucial to note that the plant contains aristolochic acid, which is highly toxic. This specific compound is known to cause severe kidney damage and increases the risk of cancer.
