Wormwood

Artemisia cina

Wormwood (Artemisia cina) is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia, known for its bitter compounds and historical use in treating digestive ailments and parasitic infections.

Botanical Identification

Wormwood, scientifically known as Artemisia cina, belongs to the Asteraceae family. It is also called wormwood or sweet wormwood in various regions. A perennial herb native to temperate regions, it has aromatic leaves, yellow flowers, and a strong, bitter scent. Synonyms include Artemisia absinthium and Artemisia annua in herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Magnoliopsida
  • Order: Asterales
  • Family: Asteraceae
  • Genus: Artemisia
  • Species: Artemisia cina

Active Compounds

Wormwood contains essential oils, terpenoids, and flavonoids as its primary active compounds. These components contribute to its medicinal properties by influencing biological processes such as anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. The synergistic interaction between these compounds enhances its therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory issues like coughs and colds.

Decoction: Used for digestive ailments and to relieve nausea.

Tincture: Used for treating insect bites and skin irritations.

Safety Profile

Wormwood (Artemisia cina) is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to potential risks to fetal development and infant health. It may also interact with medications metabolized by the liver, so caution is advised. Long-term use should be avoided without medical supervision.
