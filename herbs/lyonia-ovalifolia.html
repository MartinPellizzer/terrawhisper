Lyonia ovalifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Lyonia ovalifolia?

Lyonia ovalifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down through its taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Ericales, it is placed under the family Ericaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Lyonia, and its unique species designation, ovalifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lyonia ovalifolia has opposite, simple, oval to elliptical leaves that are typically 1 to 4 centimeters long and possess entire or slightly serrated margins. The foliage is characterized by a smooth texture and a bright green color, often appearing somewhat fleshy. Its stems are slender and woody at the base, branching out to support the spreading growth habit of the shrub. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, typically featuring white or pale petals. The plant often develops small, berry-like fruits that aid in its reproductive cycle.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, specifically found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. It primarily thrives in humid, lowland areas where moisture is abundant throughout the year. These plants are often located in forest margins or near riverbanks within their natural habitats. Their distribution is closely tied to the warm, wet climates characteristic of these equatorial territories. Due to these specific environmental requirements, the species is rarely found in more temperate or arid zones.

What are the pharmacological activities of Lyonia ovalifolia?

This plant has significant pharmacological activities including anti-adipogenic, anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, antidiabetic potential, and antioxidant properties. Its bioactive compounds work to inhibit the formation of fat cells and reduce systemic inflammation in the body. Additionally, the plant demonstrates a strong capacity to combat various bacterial pathogens through its antimicrobial action. Research also indicates that it may assist in regulating blood glucose levels, highlighting its potential role in managing metabolic disorders. Furthermore, its potent antioxidant effects help neutralize free radicals and prevent oxidative stress at a cellular level.

As discussed in a study, Lyonia ovalifolia exhibits antioxidant activity with IC50 values comparable to that of ascorbic acid (IC50=5.063 µg/mL). The extract of this plant also shows an increased pattern of adipogenesis when applied to 3T3-L1 cells. Furthermore, Lyonia ovalifolia significantly inhibited NO production, which endorses its anti-inflammaforry potential.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds astilbin, eriodictyol, lanostane triterpene glycosides, luteolin, and ursane triterpenoids. These specific phytochemicals contribute to the plant's diverse biological activities and therapeutic potential. The presence of flavonoids like eriodictyol and luteolin often supports antioxidant and anti-inflammatory functions within the body. Additionally, the triterpenoid components, including both glycosides and ursane types, play significant roles in various metabolic processes. Together, these diverse chemical constituents define the pharmacological profile of the species.

As discussed in a study, the ethanolic extract of Lyonia ovalifolia contains 12 bioactive compounds identified through LC-ESI-QTOF-MS/MS. Among these, three new compounds were revealed as leucothol B, rhodoterpenoids A, and leucothol A. The flavonoid luteolin demonstrated the best binding affinity of -12.3 kcal/mol during molecular docking against key proteins. Other promising drug candidates belonging to the flavonoid class include eriodictyol, astilbin, and syringaresinol. Additionally, molecular dynamics simulations on a 50 ns timescale showed the AR protein was most stable with luteolin.

    What side effects this plant can have?
