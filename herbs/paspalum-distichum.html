Paspalum distichum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Paspalum distichum has perennial, tufted growth habits with slender, erect culms that typically reach heights of 20 to 60 centimeters. The leaves are narrow and linear, characterized by a prominent midrib and a somewhat involute or rolled structure along the margins. Its inflorescence is composed of several small, digitate spikes arranged in a compact, somewhat flattened panicle. Each spikelet is relatively small and features a single fertile floret surrounded by glumes. The overall texture of the foliage is fine, contributing to its appearance as a delicate, creeping grass in wetland environments.
