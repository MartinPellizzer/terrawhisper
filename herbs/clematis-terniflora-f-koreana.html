Clematis terniflora f. koreana

Clematis terniflora f. koreana, also known as Korean virgin's bower or Korean sweet autumn clematis, is a flowering plant species in the Clematis genus.

It is a medicinal herb used in traditional Asian medicine, particularly in Korea and China, where it is valued for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

The plant has been traditionally used to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues, and is also believed to have anti-cancer and anti-bacterial properties.

The leaves and stems of the plant can be prepared into teas, infusions, or decoctions for medicinal purposes, and are also used in herbal supplements and traditional remedies.
