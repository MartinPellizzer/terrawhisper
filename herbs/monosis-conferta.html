What's the taxonomical classification of Monosis conferta?

Monosis conferta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Asterales, placing it firmly within the family Asteraceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Monosis, and its unique species designation, conferta.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Monosis conferta can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Some individuals might experience more severe inflammatory responses such as swelling or blistering upon contact. If the plant is accidentally consumed, it could lead to nausea, abdominal pain, or vomiting. Monitoring for these physical reactions is essential when handling the species in a natural environment.
