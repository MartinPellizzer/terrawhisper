What's the taxonomical classification of Vernonia spirei?

Vernonia spirei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant is further organized under the order Asterales and falls within the widely recognized family Asteraceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Vernonia and the specific epithet spirei, forming its unique scientific name.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vernonia spirei has erect, woody stems that reach heights of up to 1.5 meters and bear lanceolate, serrated leaves with prominent veins. Its inflorescence consists of dense, terminal clusters of discoid flower heads that exhibit a striking lavender to deep purple color. Each individual floret features a tubular corolla and is supported by a multi-scaled involucre. The plant often displays a bushy growth habit as it matures in its native habitat.
