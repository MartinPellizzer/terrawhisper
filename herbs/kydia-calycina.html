Kydia calycina

What's the taxonomical classification of Kydia calycina?

Kydia calycina belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Malvales. This plant is further categorized under the family Malvaceae, placing it within the same botanical group as many mallows. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Kydia and its unique species designation, calycina.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Kydia calycina contains diverse secondary metabolites including various triterpenoids, steroids, and phenolic compounds that contribute to its bioactive properties. These chemical constituents are primarily concentrated within the leaf and stem tissues of the plant. Research suggests that the presence of these specific compounds may provide antioxidant and antimicrobial activities. The exact pharmacological mechanisms of these substances are currently a subject of ongoing biochemical investigation. Such chemical profiles are characteristic of many species within the Euphorbiaceae family.

What are the therapeutic actions of Kydia calycina?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin diseases, respiratory ailments, and gastrointestinal disorders. The leaves are often applied topically to soothe inflammatory skin conditions or minor wounds. When prepared as a decoction, it can help alleviate symptoms related to coughs and bronchial issues. Additionally, it is sometimes consumed to manage digestive discomfort and stomach aches. These medicinal applications are rooted in traditional healing practices.
