Neillia sinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Neillia sinensis?

Neillia sinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Rosales. This plant is a member of the family Rosaceae, falling under the genus Neillia, with the specific species designation sinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Neillia sinensis has small, pinnately compound leaves that typically consist of several pairs of narrow, elliptical leaflets arranged along a central rachis. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its woody stems and a spreading growth habit. Each leaflet features smooth margins and a fine texture, often appearing in a bright green hue during the growing season. During the flowering period, the species produces small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the axils of the leaves.
