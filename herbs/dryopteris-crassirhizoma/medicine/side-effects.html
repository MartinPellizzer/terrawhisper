10 Possible Side Effects Of Dryopteris Crassirhizoma

Dryopteris crassirhizoma has some side effects when used improperly, such as allergic reactions, gastrointestinal upset, and skin irritation.

These side effects are caused by an overproduction of saponins, which can be triggered by excessive consumption or sensitivity.

For instance, severe allergic reactions can lead to anaphylaxis, a life-threatening condition that can worsen a person's life by causing respiratory distress, cardiovascular issues, and even death.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Dryopteris crassirhizoma causes allergic reactions due to the presence of specific compounds such as polyacetylenes and flavonoids in its fronds.

These chemical components can trigger an immune response in sensitive individuals, leading to symptoms like itching, redness, and swelling upon contact or inhalation of the plant's particles.

2. Triggers bronchial spasms

Dryopteris crassirhizoma triggers bronchial spasms due to its potential allergenic properties.

The plant's pollen and spores can release irritants into the air, which may cause the airways to constrict, leading to spasms in people who are allergic or sensitive.

This reaction can exacerbate conditions such as asthma, making it essential for individuals with respiratory issues to avoid exposure to the fern.

3. Induces liver damage

Dryopteris crassirhizoma induces liver damage due to its potential to cause increased production of liver enzymes.

This reaction is thought to be triggered by the plant's ability to alter the metabolism of certain substances in the body, leading to an overburden on the liver and potentially causing damage to this vital organ.

4. Increases blood pressure

Dryopteris crassirhizoma increases blood pressure.

This is due to its ability to stimulate the sympathetic nervous system and release adrenaline, a hormone that constricts blood vessels and causes an increase in heart rate and blood pressure. Additionally, the herb's diuretic properties can lead to increased sodium retention, further contributing to elevated blood pressure levels.

These physiological effects may result in individuals experiencing hypertension when using Dryopteris crassirhizoma.

5. Causes kidney stones

Dryopteris crassirhizoma causes kidney stones due to its high concentration of oxalates and purines.

These compounds are metabolized in the body and can increase the risk of stone formation when concentrated in the urine.

Additionally, the plant's saponins and flavonoids may interfere with the body's ability to absorb essential minerals, further contributing to the development of kidney stones.

6. Affects heart rhythm

Dryopteris crassirhizoma affects heart rhythm due to its ability to interact with certain medications that regulate cardiac activity.

The fern's chemical composition can alter the way these drugs are metabolized in the body, leading to changes in heart rate and blood pressure.

This interaction can cause an irregular heartbeat or palpitations, which may be uncomfortable but usually resolves once treatment is discontinued.

7. Triggers seizures

Dryopteris crassirhizoma triggers seizures due to its potential interaction with certain medications that can exacerbate seizure disorders.

The fern's active compounds, such as flavonoids and triterpenes, may enhance the activity of these medications, leading to increased excitability in the brain and ultimately inducing seizures.

Additionally, the plant's effects on GABA receptors and other neurotransmitter pathways may also contribute to this adverse reaction.

8. Reduces bone density

Dryopteris crassirhizoma reduces bone density by inhibiting osteoblast activity and enhancing osteoclast function.

This may be due to the presence of certain compounds in the plant, which interfere with the normal process of bone formation and resorption.

As a result, prolonged use of Dryopteris crassirhizoma may lead to decreased bone mass and increased risk of fractures, particularly among individuals with pre-existing bone disorders or a family history of osteoporosis.

9. Causes skin rashes

Dryopteris crassirhizoma causes skin rashes in some individuals.

This side effect is likely due to the plant's allergic compounds, such as sesquiterpenes and flavonoids, which can trigger an immune response in sensitive people.

As a result, skin irritation, redness, and inflammation may occur, potentially leading to discomfort or even more severe reactions.

10. Disrupts thyroid function

Dryopteris crassirhizoma disrupts thyroid function due to its high concentration of iodine-rich compounds.

The thyroid gland relies heavily on iodine to produce hormones that regulate metabolism and growth.

When consuming large amounts of Dryopteris crassirhizoma, it can potentially interfere with the normal absorption or processing of iodine in the body, leading to disruptions in thyroid function and potentially impacting overall health.
