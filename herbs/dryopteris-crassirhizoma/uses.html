Cinnamon Fern (Dryopteris crassirhizoma) Uses

Cinnamon Fern is used to treat respiratory infections such as bronchitis, tonsillitis, and tuberculosis. It helps alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis and respiratory allergies. The plant also supports healing of skin infections and wounds due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

Cinnamon Fern (Dryopteris crassirhizoma) Monograph

1. Bronchitis

Cinnamon Fern has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in cases of bronchitis. Its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties help reduce mucus buildup and soothe irritated airways. It is often prepared as a tea to ease breathing and alleviate coughing symptoms.

2. Allergic rhinitis

Cinnamon Fern has been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistaminic properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion and sneezing by supporting respiratory health. The plant's compounds may inhibit allergic reactions, offering a natural alternative for managing seasonal allergies.

3. Tonsillitis

Cinnamon Fern is used in traditional herbal medicine to treat tonsillitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce swelling and soothe throat irritation. Preparations often include decoctions or poultices made from its rhizomes. This application is supported by historical use in Chinese and indigenous medicinal practices.

4. Respiratory infections

Cinnamon Fern has been traditionally used to support respiratory health. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties may help alleviate symptoms of respiratory infections. It is often prepared as a tea to soothe coughs and clear congestion. Use under guidance to ensure safe application.

5. Tuberculosis

Cinnamon Fern has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health. It is believed to help alleviate symptoms of tuberculosis by promoting mucus clearance and reducing inflammation in the lungs. Its antiseptic and expectorant properties may aid in clearing infections and easing breathing in affected individuals.

6. Inflamed tonsils

Cinnamon Fern is used to soothe inflamed tonsils due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce swelling and pain, often prepared as a gargle or poultice. Its traditional use supports healing in respiratory and throat conditions.

7. Respiratory allergies

Cinnamon Fern has been traditionally used to alleviate respiratory allergies due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties. It helps reduce symptoms like sneezing, congestion, and itching by supporting the body's natural immune response. Its use is often combined with other herbs in herbal formulations for enhanced relief.

8. Respiratory tract infections

Cinnamon Fern has been traditionally used to support respiratory health. Its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties may help alleviate symptoms of respiratory tract infections. It is often prepared as a tea to soothe coughs and reduce congestion. Regular use may enhance immune response and aid in recovery.

9. Skin infections

Cinnamon Fern has been traditionally used to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The plant's rhizomes contain compounds that help reduce bacterial growth and soothe inflamed skin. It is often applied topically as a poultice or infused oil to address conditions like fungal infections and minor wounds.

10. Wounds

Cinnamon Fern is used to treat wounds due to its antimicrobial and astringent properties. It helps prevent infection and promotes tissue repair. The rhizomes are often prepared as a poultice or decoction. It is particularly valued in traditional herbal practices for its ability to cleanse and heal damaged skin.
