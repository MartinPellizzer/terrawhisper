Vernonia aspera

What's the taxonomical classification of Vernonia aspera?

Vernonia aspera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic descent, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. As a member of the order Asterales, it falls under the family Asteraceae, a large group of flowering plants. Within this family, the plant is categorized under the genus Vernia and is specifically identified by its species name, aspera.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vernonia aspera has an erect, perennial herbaceous habit characterized by stems that are typically branched and covered in rough, scabrid hairs. The leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem and feature lanceolate to narrowly elliptic shapes with serrated margins. Small, tubular florets are clustered into dense, discoid heads that emerge from the upper leaf axils. These flower heads are generally purple or violet in color and lack ray florets. The plant's overall structure supports a sturdy upright growth that distinguishes it within its genus.
