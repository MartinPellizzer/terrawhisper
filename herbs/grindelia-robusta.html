Robusta Grindelia (Grindelia robusta)

Robusta Grindelia (Grindelia robusta) is a member of the Asteraceae family, native to California, Usa, Oregon, Usa, Washington, and Usa. Traditionally, its leaves, stems, and roots have been used for decoctions, powders, and infusions.

This herb is particularly valued for its bitter, tonic, and carminative actions, and has a long history of use in native american herbal medicine, european herbal medicine, and mediterranean herbal traditions.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters.
Leaves
Broad leaves with upper surface glaucous green and lower surface white pubescent, bearing distinct stomatal bands along midrib.
Flowers
Flowers are actinomorphic, yellow with white markings, arranged in dense clusters, having four ovate petals and five elliptical sepals.
Stems
Terrestrial, erect growth habit, opposite branching, hairy surface, cylindrical cross-section, pith present, epidermis with distinct trichomes.
