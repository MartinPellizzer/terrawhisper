What's the taxonomical classification of Allium strictum?

Allium strictum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Asparagales, the plant is a member of the family Amaryllidaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement within the genus Allium, with its unique designation being the species strictum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Allium strictum has linear, grass-like leaves that are basal and typically measure between 5 and 15 centimeters in length. The plant features a single, erect flowering stem called a scape that rises from a small bulb. This scape supports a terminal umbel composed of numerous small, white to pale pinkish flowers. Each individual flower possesses six tepals and six stamens that are characteristic of the Amaryllidaceae family. The overall structure is slender and upright, allowing the plant to stand out in its rocky or alpine habitat.
