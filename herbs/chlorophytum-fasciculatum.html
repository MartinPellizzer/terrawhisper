What is Chlorophytum fasciculatum?

Chlorophytum fasciculatum is a species of flowering plant that belongs to the family Asparagaceae.

It is a perennial herb with linear, glabrous leaves that are 2-6 mm wide and 10-30 cm long, arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces a spike of white or greenish flowers, 5-12 cm long, in late spring to early summer, which are arranged in a paniculate inflorescence. The flowers are axillary, 3-4 mm long, with 6 tepals and 6 stamens.

Chlorophytum fasciculatum is native to tropical Africa.
