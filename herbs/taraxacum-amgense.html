Dandelion (Taraxacum Amgense)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Taraxacum amgense.

Dandelion, scientifically known as Taraxacum amgense, is a medicinal herb widely recognized for its health benefits and adaptogenic properties. It has been used for centuries in traditional medicine to support liver function, promote digestion, and detoxify the body. Dandelion is rich in vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants, making it a valuable asset in modern wellness practices for its anti-inflammatory and diuretic effects. Historically, it has been utilized in European and Chinese herbal traditions to treat ailments such as digestive issues and skin conditions, while today it is commonly found in teas, supplements, and culinary applications. Its unique feature is the presence of taraxacin, a rare compound with potential immune-boosting properties, and its distinctive bitter taste has made it a popular ingredient in herbal remedies and natural health products.
