Clausena lansium

What's the taxonomical classification of Clausena lansium?

Clausena lansium belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls into the subclass Magnoliidae and is further categorized under the order Sapindales. The plant is a member of the family Rutaaceae, specifically placed within the genus Clausena, and is identified by its unique species name, lansium.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clausena lansium has simple, opposite leaves that are lanceolate to elliptic in shape, measuring up to 15 centimeters in length with entire margins and an acute apex.

The leaf texture is coriaceous, meaning it possesses a leathery feel, and the surfaces often exhibit a glossy appearance. This plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, characterized by woody stems that support its dense foliage. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into fleshy fruits.

These fruits are often used to study the botanical characteristics of the Euphorbiaceae family.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Clausena lansium contains bioactive compounds in its leaves, bark, and roots that are utilized for various therapeutic applications. The leaves are frequently prepared as a decoction to treat fever and digestive ailments. Extracts from the bark are often applied topically or ingested to address inflammatory conditions. Additionally, the roots are sometimes employed in traditional remedies to manage skin disorders and internal infections. These different plant parts work together to provide a diverse range of medicinal properties.

What are the therapeutic actions of Clausena lansium?

Clausena lansium has diverse therapeutic actions including astringent, bitter, antidiarrheal, hemostatic, and antipyretic properties used in traditional medicine.

Its bitter components stimulate digestion and help balance metabolic functions. The plant acts as a potent antidiarrheal agent to soothe gastrointestinal distress. Its astringent and hemostatic qualities allow it to effectively control bleeding and tighten tissues.

Additionally, the plant serves as an antipyretic to help reduce fever in various ailments.

What are the herbal preparations of Clausena lansium?

Clausena lansium is prepared as a decoction by boiling the dried leaves, bark, or seeds in water to extract its bioactive compounds. This liquid extract is often consumed orally to address various ailments according to traditional medicinal practices. Some preparations involve grinding the dried plant material into a fine powder for easier ingestion or topical application. Additionally, the plant components can be infused into oils to create medicinal ointments for skin treatments. These diverse forms allow for different rates of absorption and specific therapeutic uses.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Clausena lansium can cause skin irritation, digestive upset, and potential toxicity if consumed in improper doses. The plant contains various bioactive compounds that may trigger allergic reactions when applied topically to sensitive skin. If ingested, individuals might experience nausea, vomiting, or abdominal discomfort. Some studies suggest that certain extracts could interact with medications or affect metabolic processes in the body. It is important to exercise caution and consult a medical professional before using any part of this plant for medicinal purposes.
