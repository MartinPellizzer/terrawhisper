Zinnia americana

Zinnia americana, commonly known as American Zinnia or Zinnia grandiflora, is a flowering plant that belongs to the daisy family and is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

The plant is rich in nutrients and has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its potential health benefits, including reducing inflammation and improving cardiovascular health.

The therapeutic actions of Zinnia americana are attributed to its ability to calm the nervous system, reduce anxiety and stress, and exhibit antibacterial properties.

The bioactive constituents of the plant include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.
