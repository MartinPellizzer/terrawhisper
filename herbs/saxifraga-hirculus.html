Saxifraga hirculus has a perennial growth habit characterized by a basal rosette of fleshy, evergreen, spoon-shaped leaves that measure roughly 1 to 3 centimeters in length. From the center of the rosette, a slender, leafless scape emerges to support a small cluster of white, five-petaled flowers. Each flower possesses distinct green or reddish spots at the base of the petals and contains several stamens.
