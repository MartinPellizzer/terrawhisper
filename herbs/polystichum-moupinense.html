Polystichum moupinense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. The taxonomic progression continues through the order Polypodiales and the family Polypodiaceae, eventually reaching its specific designation within the genus Polystichum. Finally, the plant is identified at the species level as moupinense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polystichum moupinense has large, leathery, pinnate fronds that can reach lengths of up to 1 meter, characterized by dark green, lanceolate pinnae with serrated margins and prominent veins. Each individual leaflet is arranged symmetrically along the central rachis, which is sturdy and slightly textured. The underside of the fronds features small, rounded sori protected by indusia, which are essential for the plant's reproductive process.
