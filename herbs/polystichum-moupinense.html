What's the taxonomical classification of Polystichum moupinense?

Polystichum moupinense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae. The taxonomic progression continues through the order Polypodiales and the family Polypodiaceae, eventually reaching its specific designation within the genus Polystichum. Finally, the plant is identified at the species level as moupinense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polystichum moupinense has large, leathery, pinnate fronds that can reach lengths of up to 1 meter, characterized by dark green, lanceolate pinnae with serrated margins and prominent veins. The base of the stipe is typically covered in dark brown or blackish scales that provide a distinct textural contrast to the blade. Each individual leaflet is arranged symmetrically along the central rachis, which is sturdy and slightly textured. The underside of the fronds features small, rounded sori protected by indusia, which are essential for the plant's reproductive process. These morphological features collectively allow the fern to thrive in moist, shaded forest environments.
