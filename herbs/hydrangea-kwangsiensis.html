What's the taxonomical classification of Hydrangea kwangsiensis?

Hydrangea kwangsiensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Cornales. This plant is a part of the family Hydrangeaceae and is situated within the genus Hydrangea, ultimately defined by its specific species designation, kwangsiensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

The plant produces inflorescences composed of small, fertile flowers clustered together in a terminal corymb.     What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal pain due to the presence of cyanogenic glycosides in its tissues. If a large quantity is ingested, the release of hydrogen cyanide can lead to severe cyanide poisoning. This toxicity disrupts the body's ability to use oxygen at a cellular level. Symptoms of such poisoning may include dizziness, rapid breathing, or even loss of consciousness. Immediate medical intervention is necessary if any part of the plant is consumed by humans or pets.
