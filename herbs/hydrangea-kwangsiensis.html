Hydrangea kwangsiensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hydrangea kwangsiensis has large, broadly ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated margins and a distinctively rugose surface texture. The leaf blades are characterized by prominent pinnate venation that creates a deep, textured appearance across the lamina. These leaves are arranged oppositely along the woody stems of the shrub. The plant produces inflorescences composed of small, fertile flowers clustered together in a terminal corymb. Its growth habit is typically that of a spreading shrub with multiple stems branching from the base.

      What are the herbal preparations of Hydrangea kwangsiensis?

      Hydrangea kwangsiensis is prepared as a decoction, a concentrated liquid extract made by boiling the plant material in water to extract its active compounds.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Hydrangea kwangsiensis can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if ingested due to the presence of cyanogenic glycosides. Contact with the leaves or stems may trigger contact dermatitis or localized rashes in sensitive individuals. If any part of the plant is consumed, it can lead to nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and dizziness. These harmful compounds release cyanide when broken down by enzymes in the digestive system. Because of these risks, careful handling and keeping the plant away from pets and small children is highly recommended.
