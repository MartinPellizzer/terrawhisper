Hydrangea kwangsiensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Hydrangea kwangsiensis?

Hydrangea kwangsiensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Cornales. This plant is a part of the family Hydrangeaceae and is situated within the genus Hydrangea, ultimately defined by its specific species designation, kwangsiensis.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Hydrangea kwangsiensis can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if ingested due to the presence of cyanogenic glycosides. Contact with the leaves or stems may trigger contact dermatitis or localized rashes in sensitive individuals. If any part of the plant is consumed, it can lead to nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and dizziness. These harmful compounds release cyanide when broken down by enzymes in the digestive system. Because of these risks, careful handling and keeping the plant away from pets and small children is highly recommended.
