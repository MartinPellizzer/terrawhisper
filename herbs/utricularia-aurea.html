Utricularia aurea

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Utricularia aurea has long, slender, thread-like leaves that are deeply divided into many fine segments and small, bladder-like traps used for capturing aquatic organisms. These carnivorous leaves emerge from a creeping rhizome that stays submerged or partially submerged in wet environments. The plant produces bright yellow, snapdragon-like flowers that grow on tall, upright stalks extending well above the water surface. Each flower features a prominent lower lip and a specialized spur that aids in pollination by insects. This species is characterized by its delicate, sprawling growth habit and its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor aquatic habitats.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the southeastern United States, ranging from North Carolina southward through Florida and west to Louisiana and east to Georgia. It thrives primarily in the coastal plain regions where sandy soils and wet habitats are abundant. Within these areas, it is commonly found in bogs, marshes, and along the edges of seasonal wetlands. The species favors habitats with high sunlight and consistent moisture levels to support its carnivorous lifestyle. Its distribution is closely tied to the presence of specific acidic wetland ecosystems found throughout the subtropical Southeast.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Utricularia aurea can cause skin irritation or allergic contact dermatitis if a person touches the plant without protection. Some individuals may experience localized redness, itching, or a mild rash upon contact with the foliage or sap. While direct toxicity to humans is not widely documented, the microscopic bladder structures used for carnivory could theoretically cause minor physical irritation to sensitive skin. It is always best to avoid handling any unfamiliar plant species to prevent unpredictable biological reactions. Careful observation is recommended if any skin discomfort arises after contact.
