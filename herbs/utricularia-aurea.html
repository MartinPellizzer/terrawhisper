Utricularia aurea

What's the taxonomical classification of Utricularia aurea?

Utricularia aurea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Lamiales. This plant is further categorized into the family Lentibulariaceae, within which it is placed in the genus Utricularia. Ultimately, its specific biological identity is defined by its species name, aurea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Utricularia aurea has long, slender, thread-like leaves that are deeply divided into many fine segments and small, bladder-like traps used for capturing aquatic organisms.

These carnivorous leaves emerge from a creeping rhizome that stays submerged or partially submerged in wet environments. The plant produces bright yellow, snapdragon-like flowers that grow on tall, upright stalks extending well above the water surface. Each flower features a prominent lower lip and a specialized spur that aids in pollination by insects.

This species is characterized by its delicate, sprawling growth habit and its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor aquatic habitats.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Utricularia aurea can cause skin irritation or allergic contact dermatitis if a person touches the plant without protection. Some individuals may experience localized redness, itching, or a mild rash upon contact with the foliage or sap. While direct toxicity to humans is not widely documented, the microscopic bladder structures used for carnivory could theoretically cause minor physical irritation to sensitive skin. It is always best to avoid handling any unfamiliar plant species to prevent unpredictable biological reactions. Careful observation is recommended if any skin discomfort arises after contact.
