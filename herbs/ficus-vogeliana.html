Ficus vogeliana

What's the taxonomical classification of Ficus vogeliana?

Ficus vogeliana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is part of the order Rosales and falls within the family Moraceae. Finally, it is situated in the genus Ficus, with its specific identity defined by the species name vogeliana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ficus vogeliana has large, broadly elliptic to ovate leaves that are characterized by an entire margin, an acute apex, and a cuneate base. The leaf texture is typically coriaceous, providing a thick and leathery feel to the foliage. The venation pattern is prominent, with secondary veins branching out from a central midrib toward the leaf edges. As a member of the Moraceae family, this species often exhibits a woody stem structure suited for its growth habit. These morphological traits collectively help the plant thrive in its specific tropical environment.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires a well-draining, peat-based potting medium, bright indirect light, and consistent moisture levels maintained without allowing the roots to sit in standing water.

To thrive, it should be kept in temperatures ranging between 60 and 80 degrees Fahrenheit to mimic its native tropical environment. Increasing humidity through misting or a pebble tray is essential to prevent leaf drop and encourage lush growth. You should fertilize the specimen monthly during the spring and summer growing seasons using a balanced liquid fertilizer.

Avoid repotting too frequently, as this species prefers to remain somewhat snug in its container.
