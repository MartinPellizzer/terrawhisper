What's the taxonomical classification of Ligusticum discolor?

Ligusticum discolor belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving through its higher taxonomic ranks, the plant falls under the order Apiales and is a member of the family Apiaceae. Finally, it is categorized within the genus Ligusticum, with its specific designation being the species discolor.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ligusticum discolor has large, compound, bipinnate leaves characterized by a distinct color contrast between the dark green upper surface and the pale, glaucous underside. These leaves are borne on thick, hollow, upright stems that can reach significant heights in shaded environments. The plant typically produces small, yellowish-white umbels of flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. Its root system is robust and fleshy, contributing to its perennial growth habit.
