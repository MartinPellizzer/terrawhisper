What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Farfugium japonicum has large, glossy, kidney-shaped leaves that grow in a basal rosette pattern and can reach up to 12 inches in diameter. These thick, evergreen leaves typically feature prominent veins and slightly wavy or crenate margins. The plant produces upright flower stalks that emerge in late autumn or early winter, bearing clusters of bright yellow, daisy-like composite flowers. Each flower head consists of numerous central disc florets surrounded by a ring of ray florets. Below the foliage, the plant develops a woody, tuberous rhizome that allows it to spread slowly through the soil.

What are the pharmacological activities of Farfugium japonicum?

This plant has inhibitory activity on nitric oxide (NO) production, which serves as a key mechanism for its anti-inflammatory effects. By suppressing the expression of inducible nitric oxide synthase, the plant effectively reduces the levels of this inflammatory mediator in biological systems. This specific pharmacological action helps to mitigate oxidative stress and tissue damage caused by excessive nitric oxide. Such properties suggest that extracts from the plant could be utilized in developing therapeutic agents for inflammatory diseases. Further research into these biochemical pathways continues to highlight its medicinal potential.

This new compound was isolated alongside 8 known sesquiterpenoids. The research evaluated the inhibitory effects of compounds 1-4 on nitric oxide production. These specific inhibitory activities were tested in lipopolysaccaride-activated mouse macrophages.
