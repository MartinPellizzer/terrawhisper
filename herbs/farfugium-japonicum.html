What's the taxonomical classification of Farfugium japonicum?

Farfugium japonicum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae, and its specific taxonomic placement is defined by the genus Farfugium and the species japonicum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Farfugium japonicum has large, glossy, kidney-shaped leaves that grow in a basal rosette pattern and can reach up to 12 inches in diameter. These thick, evergreen leaves typically feature prominent veins and slightly wavy or crenate margins. The plant produces upright flower stalks that emerge in late autumn or early winter, bearing clusters of bright yellow, daisy-like composite flowers. Each flower head consists of numerous central disc florets surrounded by a ring of ray florets. Below the foliage, the plant develops a woody, tuberous rhizome that allows it to spread slowly through the soil.

What are the pharmacological activities of Farfugium japonicum?

This plant has inhibitory activity on nitric oxide production through the modulation of various inflammatory pathways. By reducing the levels of nitric oxide, Farfugium japonicum serves as a potent agent in managing inflammation within biological systems. This mechanism is primarily achieved by suppressing the expression of inducible nitric oxide synthase in target cells. Such pharmacological action suggests that the plant could be utilized in developing treatments for inflammatory diseases. Further studies continue to explore how these specific molecular interactions contribute to its broader therapeutic potential.

According to a study published by "Archives of pharmacal research", one new eremophilane sesquiterpenoid, 3β-angeloyloxy-6β,8β-dihydroxy-9β-senecioyloxyeremophil-7(11)-en-12,8α-lactone, was isolated from the rhizome of Farfugium japonicum. This new compound was identified alongside 8 known sesquiterpenoids from the same plant. The study evaluated the inhibitory effects of compounds 1-4 on nitric oxide production in lipopolysaccaride-activated mouse macrophages. The structures of all isolates were identified through analyses of spectroscopic data including HRESIMS, IR, 1D, and 2D NMR.
