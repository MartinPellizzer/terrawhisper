4. Leads to cardiac arrest

Euphorbia officinarum activates skin irritation due to its high concentration of sap, which contains a milky latex that can cause allergic reactions and contact dermatitis.

This irritation is often accompanied by redness, itching, and burning sensations on the skin.

The latex also contains toxic compounds called diterpenoids, which may further exacerbate skin irritation.

5. Leads to cardiac arrest

Euphorbia officinarum produces vomiting episodes due to its unique chemical composition.

The plant contains toxic compounds such as euphorbol and succininic acid esters, which can cause irritation and inflammation in the digestive tract.

These substances can lead to nausea and vomiting as the body attempts to expel the irritant, resulting in unpleasant side effects for individuals who consume Euphorbia officinarum.

6. Leads to cardiac arrest

Euphorbia officinarum increases blood pressure due to its stimulating properties that affect the cardiovascular system.

The plant's sap contains latex, which has a direct impact on the body's vascular system, causing an increase in heart rate and blood pressure.

This stimulatory effect is attributed to the presence of saponins, terpenoids, and alkaloids, which can enhance vasomotor tone and increase peripheral resistance, leading to elevated blood pressure.

7. Leads to cardiac arrest

Euphorbia officinarum lowers heart rate due to its potential to interact with cardiovascular medications and alter blood pressure.

The plant's compounds may bind to beta-adrenergic receptors in the heart, slowing down its contractions and leading to a decrease in heart rate.

Additionally, Euphorbia officinarum has been shown to cause vasodilation, which can further contribute to decreased blood pressure and a lowered heart rate.

Euphorbia officinarum disrupts kidney function by interfering with the normal functioning of the kidneys' filtering mechanisms.

The plant's sap contains toxic compounds that can bind to and damage the kidneys' tubular structures, leading to impaired filtration and reduced urine production.

This disruption in kidney function can cause a range of symptoms, including changes in urination patterns, swelling, and fatigue.

Euphorbia officinarum affects liver enzymes due to its ability to inhibit certain cytochrome P450 enzymes.

This inhibition can lead to altered levels of circulating hormones and increased risk of toxicity from other substances.

Additionally, Euphorbia officinarum's potential interaction with liver metabolism may cause changes in the clearance of medications, potentially resulting in enhanced or diminished effects.

Euphorbia officinarum enhances sedation effects by interacting with the body's GABA receptors.

This interaction leads to a calming effect on the nervous system, causing feelings of relaxation and reduced anxiety.

The plant's compounds, such as euphol and isohexanocoumarin, can bind to GABA receptors, increasing the activity of these receptors and amplifying their inhibitory effects on neurons, ultimately resulting in sedation.
