Ehretia corylifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Ehretia corylifolia?

Ehretia corylifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Boraginales and the family Boraginaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Ehretia, and its unique species designation, corylifolia.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Ehretia corylifolia contains diverse bioactive compounds including alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, tannins, and triterpenoids that provide its therapeutic properties. The presence of specific alkaloids is often linked to the plant's significant neuroprotective and anti-inflammatory activities. Flavonoids and polyphenols within the seeds and leaves contribute to its potent antioxidant capacity by neutralizing free radicals. Additionally, the triterpene saponins play a key role in managing various skin conditions and promoting wound healing. These complex chemical constituents work synergistically to support the plant's traditional use in treating neurological disorders and digestive issues.

What are the therapeutic actions of Ehretia corylifolia?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin diseases, intestinal worms, fever, inflammation, and various types of pain. The seeds of the plant are frequently utilized in traditional medicine to expel parasitic worms from the digestive tract. It also possesses potent antimicrobial properties that help combat skin infections and wounds. Additionally, its extracts are applied topically or ingested to reduce swelling and alleviate bodily aches. These therapeutic applications make it a significant component in several herbal healing practices.
