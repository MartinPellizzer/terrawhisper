What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Indigofera decora has pinnately compound leaves with small, oblong leaflets and small, papilionaceous flowers that typically range from white to pale pink or lavender in color. Its floral arrangement often features axillary racemes where individual blossoms emerge from the leaf junctions. The legume fruit develops as small, flattened pods that contain the seeds necessary for reproduction. These morphological features are characteristic of the Fabaceae family and help distinguish it from closely related species.

      What are the herbal preparations of Indigofera decora?

      Indigofera decora is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste depending on the specific medicinal application required. The leaves and stems are most commonly boiled in water to create a concentrated liquid used for internal consumption. For external ailments such as skin irritations, the plant material is crushed into a fine paste and applied directly to the affected area.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Indigofera decora can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Contact with the plant's foliage or stems may trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. If accidentally consumed, the chemical compounds within the plant can lead to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. There is also a potential for systemic toxicity if large quantities are processed by the body. Careful handling and avoiding ingestion are necessary to prevent these adverse physiological responses.
