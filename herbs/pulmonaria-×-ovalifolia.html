Pulmonaria × ovalifolia

Pulmonaria × ovalifolia, commonly known as lungwort, is a hybrid plant derived from the cross between Pulmonaria officinalis and Pulmonaria mollis.

Lungwort has been used in traditional medicine for its health benefits, which include the ability to treat respiratory issues, such as coughs and bronchitis, due to its expectorant properties.

The therapeutic actions of lungwort are attributed to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, which help to soothe and calm irritated mucous membranes.

The bioactive constituents of lungwort include flavonoids, saponins, and glycosides, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.
