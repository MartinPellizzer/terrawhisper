Betony (Stachys betonica) Compounds

The primary compounds in Betony include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and tannins, which contribute to its astringent effects. Phenolic acids and glycosides support its anti-inflammatory actions, while coumarin and saponins may aid in digestive health. Essential oils and terpenoids provide aromatic benefits, and alkaloids and anthocyanins contribute to its overall therapeutic profile.

Betony (Stachys betonica) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Betony (Stachys betonica) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting the plant's traditional use in treating nervous system disorders and digestive issues. They also help protect cells from oxidative stress.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Betony (Stachys betonica). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is believed to support digestive health. Tannins also help in reducing inflammation and may aid in wound healing.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Betony (Stachys betonica). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and calming properties. Found in significant amounts in Betony, phenolic acid supports its traditional use in treating nervous system disorders and digestive issues.

4. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Betony (Stachys betonica). It contributes to the plant's calming and sedative properties. Glycosides in Betony support nervous system balance and may aid in reducing anxiety. These compounds are often responsible for the herb's traditional use in herbal medicine.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Betony (Stachys betonica). It contributes to the plant's mild sedative and calming effects, often used in herbal medicine to reduce anxiety and promote relaxation. Coumarin also has antispasmodic properties, supporting its traditional use in treating nervous system-related conditions.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Betony (Stachys betonica). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in promoting relaxation and reducing anxiety. Saponins also support digestive health by aiding in the breakdown of food. Their mild cleansing properties may help in maintaining overall bodily balance.

7. Essential oil

The essential oil of Betony (Stachys betonica) contains volatile compounds like borneol and cineole. These contribute to its calming and antispasmodic properties. The oil is often used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine for digestive and nervous system support.

8. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Betony (Stachys betonica) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds, such as labdane-type diterpenes, exhibit anti-inflammatory and sedative effects. They support the plant's traditional use in calming the nervous system and treating anxiety. Terpenoids are key to Betony's therapeutic profile in herbal medicine.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Betony (Stachys betonica). It contributes to the plant's calming effects and is believed to support nervous system health. Alkaloids in Betony may help reduce anxiety and promote relaxation. These compounds are part of the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key compound in Betony (Stachys betonica) responsible for its vibrant blue and purple hues. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in calming the nervous system and improving cognitive function. Anthocyanin also exhibits antioxidant properties, supporting overall health and potentially reducing inflammation.
