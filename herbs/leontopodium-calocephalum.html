Leontopodium calocephalum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Leontopodium calocephalum has small, scale-like leaves that are densely packed around woody, branching stems to form compact, globular flower heads. These spherical inflorescences are characterized by numerous white, thread-like styles that create a fuzzy or woolly appearance. The plant typically grows as a low-lying, perennial shrub adapted to arid environments. Its structural morphology is designed to minimize water loss through its reduced leaf surface area. The overall form is highly compact, allowing it to withstand harsh, dry conditions.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the Western Cape province of South Africa, specifically within the Cape Floristic Region. It grows primarily in the fynbos biome, which is characterized by nutrient-poor soils and a Mediterranean climate. Most populations are found in coastal and near-coastal areas where sandy substrates are common. Within these habitats, the species typically occupies open shrublands and rocky slopes.
