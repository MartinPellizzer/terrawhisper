Leontopodium calocephalum

What's the taxonomical classification of Leontopodium calocephalum?

Leontopodium calocephalum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Asterales. This plant is further categorized under the family Asteraceae, which places it among the composite flowers. Within this family, it is identified by the genus Leontopodium and is specifically distinguished as the species calocephalum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Leontopodium calocephalum has small, scale-like leaves that are densely packed around woody, branching stems to form compact, globular flower heads. These spherical inflorescences are characterized by numerous white, thread-like styles that create a fuzzy or woolly appearance. The plant typically grows as a low-lying, perennial shrub adapted to arid environments. Its structural morphology is designed to minimize water loss through its reduced leaf surface area. The overall form is highly compact, allowing it to withstand harsh, dry conditions.

Most populations are found in coastal and near-coastal areas where sandy substrates are common. Within these habitats, the species typically occupies open shrublands and rocky slopes.
