What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Picrasma Excelsa (Sumatra)?

Picrasma excelsa, also known as Sumatra, has health benefits such as treating fever, rheumatism, and malaria.

The herb contains medicinal constituents like limonoids, flavonoids, and saponins, which possess anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties. The medicinal preparations of Picrasma excelsa include infusions, decoctions, and extracts, which are used to treat various ailments. However, excessive consumption may cause stomach upset, diarrhea, or allergic reactions in some individuals.

It is recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before using this herb, especially for people with pre-existing medical conditions or taking prescription medications.

What are the health benefits of Picrasma excelsa?

Picrasma excelsa, also known as Sumatra, has health benefits such as lowering cholesterol levels, reducing inflammation, and fighting off infections.

Its bark extract has been found to exhibit antioxidant properties, which help protect against cell damage and oxidative stress. Additionally, it has antimicrobial and antiviral properties that can aid in the treatment of various diseases, including malaria, tuberculosis, and respiratory infections.

Overall, Picrasma excelsa offers a natural remedy for promoting overall health and well-being.

What are the active constituents of Picrasma excelsa?

The health benefits of Picrasma excelsa, also known as Sumatra, comes from its active constituents, such as flavonoids, terpenoids, and alkaloids.

The plant's bark contains a high concentration of quercetin, a powerful antioxidant that has been shown to exhibit anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties.

Additionally, sumatra's leaves are rich in tannins and catechins, which have been linked to improved cardiovascular health and anticancer activities.

What are the medicinal preparations of Picrasma excelsa?

Picrasma excelsa, also known as Sumatra, has different medicinal preparations, such as resin, bark extract, and leaves.

The resin is used to treat respiratory problems like bronchitis and pneumonia, while the bark extract is used for its anti-inflammatory properties to treat conditions like arthritis and rheumatism. Meanwhile, the leaf extract is used to treat fever, cough, and skin infections due to its antiseptic and antibacterial properties.

These preparations are often combined with other herbs or traditional medicine to enhance their effectiveness.

What are the possible side effect of using Picrasma excelsa improperly?

Improper use of Picrasma excelsa increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as dizziness, lightheadedness, and fainting spells.

It may also cause stomach upset, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal pain. In severe cases, it can lead to allergic reactions, skin rashes, and hives. Additionally, sumatra misuse has been linked to headaches, fatigue, insomnia, and mood changes, including anxiety and depression.

In rare instances, it can cause liver or kidney damage, cardiovascular problems, and even anaphylactic shock.

What precautions to take when using Picrasma excelsa medicinally?

Before using Picrasma excelsa, also known as Sumatra, for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as consulting with a qualified healthcare professional to determine the appropriate dosage and administration method.

Additionally, it is essential to start with a low dose and gradually increase as needed to avoid adverse reactions, particularly gastrointestinal upset and allergic reactions.

Moreover, patients with kidney or liver disease should exercise caution due to potential toxicity concerns.
