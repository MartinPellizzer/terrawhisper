What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Picrasma Excelsa (Sumatra)?

Picrasma excelsa, also known as sumatra, has health benefits such as reducing inflammation and treating fever.

The herb contains medicinal constituents like tannins and flavonoids, which contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Traditional medicinal preparations of Picrasma excelsa include infusions, decoctions, and ointments. However, possible side effects include gastrointestinal disturbances and skin irritation in some individuals.

Precautions should be taken to avoid overdosing, particularly in pregnant or breastfeeding women, as the herb's effects on fetal development and infant health are not well understood.

What are the medicinal preparations of Picrasma excelsa?

Picrasma excelsa, also known as Sumatra, has different medicinal preparations, such as resin, bark extract, and leaves.

The resin is used to treat respiratory problems like bronchitis and pneumonia, while the bark extract is used for its anti-inflammatory properties to treat conditions like arthritis and rheumatism. Meanwhile, the leaf extract is used to treat fever, cough, and skin infections due to its antiseptic and antibacterial properties.

These preparations are often combined with other herbs or traditional medicine to enhance their effectiveness.

What are the possible side effect of using Picrasma excelsa improperly?

Improper use of Picrasma excelsa increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as dizziness, lightheadedness, and fainting spells.

It may also cause stomach upset, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal pain. In severe cases, it can lead to allergic reactions, skin rashes, and hives. Additionally, sumatra misuse has been linked to headaches, fatigue, insomnia, and mood changes, including anxiety and depression.

In rare instances, it can cause liver or kidney damage, cardiovascular problems, and even anaphylactic shock.
