What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Picrasma Excelsa (Sumatra)?

Picrasma excelsa, also known as sumatra, has health benefits such as anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antifungal properties.

The medicinal constituents of this herb include flavonoids, terpenoids, and saponins, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects. Medicinal preparations of Picrasma excelsa include infusions, decoctions, and extracts, used to treat various ailments. While generally considered safe, high doses may cause gastrointestinal upset and interact with certain medications.

Precautions include consulting a healthcare professional before using Picrasma excelsa, particularly if pregnant or breastfeeding.

What are the active constituents of Picrasma excelsa ?

Picrasma excelsa, also known as sumatra, has active constituents such as flavonoids, tannins, and triterpenoids.

The flavonoids present in this plant possess antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, which contribute to its medicinal value.

Tannins have astringent and coagulant effects, while triterpenoids have been shown to exhibit cytotoxic and antimalarial activities, making Picrasma excelsa a valuable resource for traditional medicine.

What are the possible side effect of using Picrasma excelsa improperly?

Improper use of Picrasma excelsa, also known as sumatra, increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as dizziness, nausea, and stomach pain.

Some individuals may also experience allergic reactions, including skin irritation and respiratory problems. Additionally, high doses of sumatra may lead to liver and kidney damage, which can be potentially life-threatening if left untreated.

It's essential to consult a healthcare professional before using this plant medicinally.
