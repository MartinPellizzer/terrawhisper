Ficus concinna

What's the taxonomical classification of Ficus concinna?

Ficus concinna belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, the plant is placed in the order Rosales and falls under the family Moraceae. Ultimately, it is identified by its genus, Ficus, and its specific species designation, conciinna.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ficus concinna has a woody structure characterized by an erect growth habit and elliptical to ovate leaves with entire margins. The foliage typically features a glossy upper surface and a slightly paler underside, often possessing distinct venation. Its stems are sturdy and produce a milky latex when damaged, a common trait among the genus. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in specialized structures known as syconia, which house the reproductive organs. These fruit-like structures eventually mature and serve as a primary morphological feature for identification.
