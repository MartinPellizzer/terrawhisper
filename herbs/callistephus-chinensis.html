Callistephus chinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Callistephus chinensis?

Callistephus chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Asterales. It is a member of the family Asteraceae, a large group of flowering plants, and is specifically categorized within the genus Callistephus. Finally, its unique biological identity is defined by its specific epithet, making its full scientific name Callistephus chinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Callistephus chinensis has upright, branched stems that reach heights of up to 100 centimeters and are covered in simple or slightly serrated, lanceolate leaves. These leaves are typically arranged oppositely along the stem and possess a rough, pubescent texture. The plant produces large, composite flower heads consisting of numerous ray florets and central disc florets in a wide array of colors. Each flower head is supported by an involucre of green phyllaries that protect the developing bud. The overall structure is robust, supporting heavy blooms that can vary significantly in shape from single to double forms.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant, commonly known as China aster, requires full sunlight and well-draining, fertile soil to thrive from direct seeding in late spring or transplanting established seedlings.

These annuals prefer consistent moisture but must avoid waterlogged conditions to prevent root rot. Regular deadheading of spent blooms will encourage the plant to produce a continuous succession of vibrant flowers throughout the summer.
