10 Medicinal Constituents Of Corydalis Cava (Corydal)

Corydalis cava has active constituents such as alkaloids, phenanthrenes, and isoquinolines.

These compounds contribute to its medicinal properties, including analgesic, anti-inflammatory, and sedative effects. The isoquinoline alkaloid dehydrocorybulbine, for instance, exhibits potent pain-relieving properties, making it a potential treatment for various types of chronic pain.

Additionally, corydal's phenanthrenes have been shown to reduce inflammation and promote wound healing, improving the quality of life for individuals suffering from conditions like arthritis or skin injuries.
