Botanical Identification of Corydalis (Corydalis cava)

To identify Corydalis (Corydalis cava) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Corydalis (Corydalis cava) Monograph

Scientific Classification

Corydalis cava belongs to the kingdom Plantae. Its family is Fumariaceae, also known as the poppy family. It is part of the genus Corydalis, which includes over 400 species. The species Corydalis cava is native to the western United States and is known for its medicinal properties.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Corydalis cava is the scientific name for this species, first described by the botanist George Don in 1830. It is also known by several synonyms, including Corydalis bulbosa and Corydalis hirta. Common names such as Dutchman's breeches and fairy bells are used in various regions. Historically, it was referred to by names like corydalis bulbifera and corydalis cava var. bulbosa.

Morphological Characteristics

Corydalis cava is a low-growing perennial herb with a rhizomatous root system. It has slender, branching stems that rise from the base. The leaves are opposite, lanceolate, and have a slightly hairy surface. The inflorescence is a raceme of small, nodding flowers. Each flower has two petals, a prominent spur, and stamens. The fruit is a capsule containing numerous small seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Corydalis cava can be distinguished by its slender, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically pink or purple, with two lower petals that are larger and more showy. It lacks the distinct leaf arrangements and flower structures of closely related species, making these features key for accurate identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Corydalis cava is native to regions in East Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. It thrives in temperate climates with mild summers and cool winters. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils and is typically found at elevations between 1,000 and 2,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Corydalis cava blooms from April to June, producing clusters of pink to purple flowers. Seed formation occurs from July to September. For identification purposes, the plant is typically harvested in late summer to early autumn when the leaves are mature and the seeds are nearly ripe.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Corydalis cava is closely related to species like Corydalis ambigua and Corydalis ligulata. It belongs to the genus Corydalis, which is part of the family Fumariaceae. The family is characterized by its spurred petals and often toxic alkaloids, traits shared by Corydalis cava.
