What's the taxonomical classification of Hibiscus indicus?

Hibiscus indicus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant falls under the order Malvales and is a member of the family Malvaceae. Finally, it is placed within the genus Hibiscus, with its specific designation being the species indicus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hibiscus indicus has large, funnel-shaped flowers featuring five prominent petals, a central columnar staminal tube, and a protruding style with five distinct stigmas. The leaves are typically ovate or lanceolate with serrated margins and a glossy, dark green surface. Its woody stems exhibit a branching growth habit that supports a shrub-like structure. The plant produces elongated, capsule-like seed pods that split open upon maturity to release small, dark seeds. These morphological features collectively define its reproductive and vegetative identity.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, including countries such as Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia. It thrives in humid climates where warm temperatures and consistent moisture are readily available. Because of its preference for these conditions, it has been widely naturalized in other tropical zones across the Pacific Islands and parts of Oceania. The species is often found in coastal areas or near riverbanks where the soil remains damp.
