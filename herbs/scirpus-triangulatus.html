Scirpus triangulatus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Scirpus triangulatus has triangular stems that are typically between 0.5 and 2 millimeters thick and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. These stems are often grooved and possess a distinct three-sided cross-section which characterizes the species. The leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures located at the base of the plant. At the apex of each stem, there is a compact terminal cluster of small, brownish flower spikes. These inflorescences consist of numerous tiny flowers protected by specialized bracts.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the subtropical and tropical regions of South America, specifically spanning across Brazil, Paraguay, Uruguay, and parts of Argentina. It typically inhabits wetland ecosystems such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of slow-moving water bodies throughout these territories. The species favors humid environments where its root systems can remain submerged or damp. Its range is primarily concentrated in the warmer, moisture-rich corridors of the southern Neotropical realm. This distribution pattern ensures the plant thrives in areas with consistent access to standing water.
