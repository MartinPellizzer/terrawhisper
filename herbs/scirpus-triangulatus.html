Scirpus triangulatus

What's the taxonomical classification of Scirpus triangulatus?

Scirpus triangulatus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Poales and the family Cyperaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its genus, Scirpus, and its specific species designation, triangulatus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Scirpus triangulatus has triangular stems that are typically between 0.5 and 2 millimeters thick and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. These stems are often grooved and possess a distinct three-sided cross-section which characterizes the species. The leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures located at the base of the plant. At the apex of each stem, there is a compact terminal cluster of small, brownish flower spikes. These inflorescences consist of numerous tiny flowers protected by specialized bracts.
