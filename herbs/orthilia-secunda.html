Orthilia secunda

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Orthilia secunda has an erect, slender stem that typically reaches heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and supports several small, alternate, ovate leaves with entire margins. The leaves are often somewhat fleshy and possess a distinctively pointed tip. Its flowering structure consists of a terminal raceme where the small, white, bell-shaped flowers are arranged on one side of the stem. Each flower features four tepals and is supported by a short pedicel. The plant produces small, globose, reddish berries that develop after successful pollination.

Individual populations are frequently found throughout the Rocky Mountains, the Cascades, and the Sierra Nevada ranges.
