Ophiorrhiza umbricola

What's the taxonomical classification of Ophiorrhiza umbricola?

Ophiorrhiza umbricola belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical structure of its order, Gentianaales, it is placed into the family Rubiaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its genus, Ophiorrhiza, and its specific species designation, umbricola.

Ophiorrhiza umbricola is prepared primarily as a decoction made from the boiled rhizomes of the plant to treat various ailments. The rhizomes are typically cleaned and sliced before being simmered in water to extract their active medicinal properties. This liquid extract is often consumed orally to address digestive issues or other traditional health concerns. Some practitioners may also use the crushed plant material as a topical application for external skin conditions. These traditional methods rely on the concentrated infusion of the plant's underground structures.
