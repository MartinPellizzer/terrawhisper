Selaginella sanguinolenta

What's the taxonomical classification of Selaginella sanguinolenta?

Selaginella sanguinolenta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Lycopodiidae. The plant's taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Selaginellales and the family Selaginellaceae. Ultimately, it is placed within the genus Selaginella, with its specific identification being the species sanguinolenta.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Selaginella sanguinolenta has a creeping rhizome that produces small, scale-like leaves arranged in four distinct rows along its cylindrical, dichotomously branching stems.

The foliage consists of tiny, overlapping microphylls that are typically dark green or brownish in color. A defining morphological characteristic is the presence of reddish or blood-colored pigments within the stem tissue, which gives the plant its specific name. These stems often grow in dense, mat-like clusters in moist, shaded environments.

The overall structure is compact and delicate, reflecting its adaptation to forest floor habitats.

      What are the herbal preparations of Selaginella sanguinolenta?

      Selaginella sanguinolenta is prepared as a decoction, a tincture, or a topical poultice to utilize its medicinal properties.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Selaginella sanguinolenta can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and potential respiratory distress if its dried spores or dust are inhaled. While the plant is primarily studied for its bioactive compounds, direct contact with the foliage may trigger redness or itching in sensitive individuals. There is limited clinical data regarding internal consumption, but any ingestion could lead to gastrointestinal upset or unpredictable toxic reactions. Caution should be exercised when handling the plant to avoid accidental exposure to the eyes or mucous membranes. Always consult a medical professional if an adverse reaction occurs after contact.
