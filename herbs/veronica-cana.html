What's the taxonomical classification of Veronica cana?

Veronica cana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Lamiales and into the family Plantaginaeae. Ultimately, the plant is defined by its specific classification within the genus Veronica, with its unique identity being established as the species cana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Veronica cana has a perennial growth habit characterized by erect, slender stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem and exhibit a lanceolate to linear shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. Its inflorescence consists of small, blue to violet flowers that emerge from axillary racemes. Each flower features four petals and a prominent central style, often possessing a distinctively pale or white throat. The plant's overall structure is compact, making it well-suited for rocky or alpine environments.
