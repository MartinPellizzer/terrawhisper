What to know about Aralia racemosa (american spikenard) before using it medicinally

American Spikenard, scientifically known as Aralia racemosa, is a remarkable herb that boasts an array of health benefits, from soothing digestive issues to alleviating inflammation and reducing pain.

Beyond its medicinal properties, American Spikenard is also a flavorful addition to many dishes, often used in soups, stews, and as a garnish for meats and vegetables. In terms of horticulture, the plant prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice among gardeners. Botanically speaking, American Spikenard belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its unique raceme-like inflorescence.

With roots tracing back to traditional Native American medicine and early colonial America, American Spikenard has long been revered for its versatility and potential health benefits.
