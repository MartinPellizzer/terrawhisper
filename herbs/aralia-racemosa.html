American angel's trumpets

Aralia racemosa

American Angel's Trumpets (Aralia racemosa) is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States, known for its clusters of white, trumpet-shaped flowers and its use in traditional herbal medicine to treat conditions such as respiratory issues and inflammation.

Botanical Identification

American Angel's Trumpets, Aralia racemosa, belong to the Araliaceae family. Commonly known as "American Angel's Trumpet" or "devil's ivy," it is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States. The plant features compound leaves, white flowers in umbels, and a fibrous root system. It has also been referred to as "devil's root" in some herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Class: Magnoliopsida

Safety Profile

Aralia racemosa, or American angel's trumpets, contains toxic compounds and should not be used during pregnancy or by individuals with hypertension. It may cause severe gastrointestinal distress and cardiovascular effects. Use under professional supervision only.
