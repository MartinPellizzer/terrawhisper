Cupuaçu (Byrsonima Cotinifolia)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Byrsonima cotinifolia.

Cupuaçu, scientifically known as Byrsonima cotinifolia, is a tropical fruit native to the Amazon rainforest, often referred to as a medicinal herb due to its rich phytochemical profile. It is valued for its primary benefits, including its high antioxidant content, anti-inflammatory properties, and potential support for skin health and digestive wellness. Traditionally, indigenous communities in the Amazon have used cupuaçu for its calming effects, to treat skin conditions, and as a natural remedy for digestive ailments. In modern wellness practices, it is increasingly incorporated into skincare products, dietary supplements, and functional foods due to its unique compounds like polyphenols and flavonoids. Its distinctive flavor, combining notes of chocolate and banana, along with its rare active compounds, make it a unique and culturally significant plant with a long history of medicinal use.
