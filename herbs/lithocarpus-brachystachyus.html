Lithocarpus brachystachyus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lithocarpus brachystachyus has coriaceous, oblong-lanceolate leaves with entire margins and a distinctively short, thickened inflorescence. The foliage typically presents a dark green upper surface and a paler underside, often featuring fine pubescence along the veins. Its reproductive structures are characterized by small, condensed clusters of flowers that give rise to specialized woody fruits. These fruits are enclosed in a cupule that partially covers the nut, a defining trait of the Lithocarpaceae family. The overall architecture of the plant reflects its adaptation to forest understories through sturdy, leathery leaf textures.
