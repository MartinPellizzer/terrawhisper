Impatiens piufanensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Impatiens piufanensis has succulent, lanceolate leaves with serrated margins and bright pink to reddish flowers characterized by a distinctively spurred nectar guide. The plant features fleshy, upright stems that exhibit a soft, herbaceous texture throughout its growth cycle. Its floral structure includes a prominent, elongated spur located at the rear of the petal arrangement. Small, glandular hairs often cover the surface of the foliage and stems, providing a subtle tactile quality. The root system typically consists of shallow, tuberous structures adapted to moist environments.
