Relhania pinnata

Relhania pinnata, also known as the African bush tea, is a perennial succulent plant native to South Africa.

It has been used for centuries in traditional medicine, and its medicinal properties include anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which contribute to its health benefits such as reducing fever, treating wounds, and alleviating respiratory issues.

The plant can be prepared in various herbal forms, including tea, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to treat a range of ailments.
