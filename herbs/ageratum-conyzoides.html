Whiteweed (Ageratum conyzoides)

Whiteweed (Ageratum conyzoides) is a member of the Compositae family, native to Tropical Africa, Southern Asia, and Pacific Islands. Traditionally, its leaves, stems, and flowers have been used for decoctions, infusions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, diuretic, and carminative actions, and has a long history of use in african traditional medicine, european herbal medicine, and ayurvedic medicine.

Annual herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 30 to 100 centimeters.
Leaves
Opposite, broad, ovate leaves with upper surface glaucous green and lower surface silver-white, bearing distinct stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences with actinomorphic symmetry, featuring white to pale blue petals arranged in five lobes, and prominent yellow central disks with radiating yellow ray florets.
Stems
Cylindrical, glabrous, herbaceous stems with opposite branching, ascending growth habit, and nodes bearing short, dense hairs.
