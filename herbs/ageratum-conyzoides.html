Whiteweed

Ageratum conyzoides

Whiteweed (Ageratum conyzoides) is a flowering plant native to the Americas, commonly used in traditional medicine for its potential to improve conditions such as inflammation, skin infections, and respiratory ailments.

Botanical Identification

Ageratum conyzoides, commonly known as whiteweed, belongs to the Asteraceae family. It is an annual herb native to the Americas. The plant has hairy stems, opposite leaves, and small white or pale blue flowers. It is also referred to as blueweed or white cudweed in some regions.

Safety Profile

Ageratum conyzoides, or whiteweed, is generally considered safe when used in moderation. However, it may cause gastrointestinal upset in some individuals. Long-term use or high doses should be avoided, and it is contraindicated during pregnancy due to potential toxicity. Always consult a healthcare professional before use.
