Morphology

Whiteweed (Ageratum conyzoides) is an annual herb with slender, branched stems that are typically hairy, and it produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters at the ends of the branches.

Distribution

Whiteweed (Ageratum conyzoides) is native to the Americas, particularly Central and South America, and is commonly found in tropical and subtropical regions, including parts of the Caribbean, southern United States, and Southeast Asia.

Traditional Uses

In traditional medicine, Whiteweed (Ageratum conyzoides) has been used to treat skin infections, inflammation, and as a remedy for respiratory conditions such as coughs and bronchitis.

Medicinal Systems

In traditional medicinal systems such as Ayurveda, Traditional Chinese Medicine, and Indigenous practices across the Americas, Whiteweed (Ageratum conyzoides) has been utilized for its purported anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and wound-healing properties.
