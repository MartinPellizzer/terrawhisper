What is Whiteweed (Ageratum Conyzoides)?

Whiteweed, or Ageratum conyzoides, is a flowering plant native to the Americas, commonly found in tropical and subtropical regions, and is known for its use in traditional herbal medicine.

Botanical Identity and Classification

Whiteweed, scientifically known as Ageratum conyzoides, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae, commonly found in tropical and subtropical regions and used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.

Common Names and Synonyms

Whiteweed, also known as Ageratum conyzoides, is commonly referred to by various regional names such as blueweed, white thistle, and wild chamomile, and is classified botanically under the Asteraceae family.

Whiteweed (Ageratum conyzoides) is an annual herb with slender, branched stems that are typically hairy, and it produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters at the ends of the branches.

Whiteweed (Ageratum conyzoides) is native to the tropical regions of the Americas, particularly Central and South America, and has since been introduced to various parts of Africa, Asia, and Australia, where it thrives in disturbed soils and arid environments.
