Ageratum Conyzoides: What To Know Before Using It For Medicinal Purposes

Ageratum conyzoides, commonly known as blueweed or blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas and widely used in traditional medicine across various cultures.

It has been traditionally employed to treat a range of ailments, including respiratory conditions, skin infections, and inflammation due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The plant contains bioactive compounds such as flavonoids and alkaloids, which contribute to its therapeutic effects. In some regions, it is used to prepare poultices or decoctions for wound healing and fever reduction.

However, more scientific research is needed to fully validate its medicinal potential and ensure its safe use in modern pharmacology.
