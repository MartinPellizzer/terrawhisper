What's the taxonomical classification of Pollia thyrsiflora?

Pollia thyrsiflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Commelinaales into the family Commelinaeaceae. Finally, the plant is situated within the genus Pollia, with its specific classification being the species thyrsiflora.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pollia thyrsiflora has upright, spike-like inflorescences that emerge from a base of long, lanceolate green leaves.

The plant is characterized by dense clusters of small, actinomorphic flowers arranged along a central rachis. These flowers eventually develop into striking, metallic-colored berries that often appear bright blue or purple. The foliage typically grows in a rosette pattern, providing a structural foundation for the tall flowering stalks.

This combination of slender leaves and vibrant, clustered fruit defines its distinctive physical appearance.
