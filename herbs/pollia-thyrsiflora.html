Pollia thyrsiflora

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pollia thyrsiflora has upright, spike-like inflorescences that emerge from a base of long, lanceolate green leaves. The plant is characterized by dense clusters of small, actinomorphic flowers arranged along a central rachis. These flowers eventually develop into striking, metallic-colored berries that often appear bright blue or purple. The foliage typically grows in a rosette pattern, providing a structural foundation for the tall flowering stalks. This combination of slender leaves and vibrant, clustered fruit defines its distinctive physical appearance.
