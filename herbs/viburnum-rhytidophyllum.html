Viburnum rhytidophyllum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viburnum rhytidophyllum has large, leathery, dark green leaves that feature deeply serrated margins and a distinctively rugose, wrinkled texture on the upper surface. The plant grows as an upright, multi-stemmed shrub that can reach heights of up to fifteen feet. Its bark is characterized by a unique peeling or exfoliating pattern, revealing lighter patches underneath as the stems mature. During the spring, it produces clusters of small, creamy white flowers that bloom in flat-topped cymes. These blossoms are eventually followed by small, dark blue to black drupes that serve as a food source for birds.
