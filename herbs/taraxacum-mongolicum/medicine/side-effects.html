1. Causes allergic reactions

Taraxacum mongolicum, also known as Mongolian dandelion, causes allergic reactions due to its similarity in composition to common allergens.

The plant's proteins and other compounds may trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms such as itching, hives, or swelling. This is thought to occur because the body mistakes Taraxacum mongolicum's molecules for those of a harmful agent, prompting an overactive response from the immune system.

As a result, individuals may experience uncomfortable and potentially severe allergic reactions.

2. Triggers asthma attacks

Taraxacum mongolicum, also known as Mongolian dandelion, triggers asthma attacks by releasing allergenic particles into the air.

The plant's tiny spores can become airborne and be inhaled, causing an allergic reaction that leads to constriction of the airways, inflammation, and increased mucus production, characteristic symptoms of an asthma attack.

3. Induces vomiting frequently

Taraxacum mongolicum, also known as Mongolian dandelion, induces vomiting frequently due to its ability to stimulate the digestive system and increase gut motility.

The plant's bitter compounds can irritate the stomach lining, triggering a reflexive response that leads to nausea and vomiting.

Additionally, the saponins present in Taraxacum mongolicum may also contribute to this side effect by altering the balance of gut bacteria and disrupting normal digestion patterns.

4. Increases blood pressure

Taraxacum mongolicum, also known as Mongolian dandelion, increases blood pressure by stimulating the cardiovascular system and causing vasoconstriction.

The saponins and flavonoids present in Taraxacum mongolicum may bind to alpha-adrenergic receptors, leading to an increase in heart rate and cardiac output, which can further elevate blood pressure.

Additionally, the herb's ability to decrease vasodilation and reduce nitric oxide levels can also contribute to its hypertensive effects.

5. Lowers potassium levels

Taraxacum mongolicum, also known as Mongolian dandelion, lowers potassium levels.

This side effect occurs due to its ability to inhibit the absorption of potassium in the gut and increase its excretion through the kidneys. Additionally, Taraxacum mongolicum may stimulate the production of urine, leading to a further reduction in potassium levels.

This action is likely related to its diuretic properties, which are responsible for many of its medicinal uses.

6. Worsens kidney disease

Taraxacum mongolicum worsens kidney disease due to its potential to reduce blood flow and increase potassium levels in the body.

As a result, individuals with pre-existing kidney disease may experience a decline in their condition, potentially leading to further complications such as kidney damage or even failure.

The plant's diuretic properties may also exacerbate the issue by increasing urine production, placing additional strain on the kidneys.

7. Aggravates liver damage

Taraxacum mongolicum, also known as Mongolian dandelion, aggravates liver damage due to its potential interaction with certain medications that are metabolized by the liver.

The plant's active compounds may increase the risk of liver inflammation and damage in individuals who already have pre-existing liver conditions or are taking medications that can harm the liver.

Additionally, the herb's high levels of saponins and terpenoids may exacerbate liver dysfunction, potentially leading to further complications.

8. Reduces thyroid function

Taraxacum mongolicum, also known as Mongolian dandelion, reduces thyroid function due to its ability to suppress the production of thyroid hormones.

This is likely caused by the plant's high concentration of flavonoids and saponins, which have been shown to inhibit the activity of enzymes involved in thyroid hormone synthesis.

As a result, individuals taking Taraxacum mongolicum may experience symptoms such as fatigue, weight gain, and dry skin, which are often associated with hypothyroidism.

9. Increases heart rate

Taraxacum mongolicum, also known as Mongolian dandelion, increases heart rate due to its inherent cardio-stimulating properties.

The herb's leaves and flowers contain a significant amount of potassium, which helps regulate fluid balance in the body and stimulate cardiac activity. Additionally, Taraxacum mongolicum has been found to increase adrenaline levels, leading to an elevated heart rate as the body's "fight or flight" response is triggered.

This property makes it essential for individuals with pre-existing cardiovascular conditions to exercise caution when using this herb.

10. Produces dizziness commonly

Taraxacum mongolicum, also known as Mongolian dandelion, produces dizziness commonly due to its ability to stimulate the nervous system and increase blood flow to the brain.

This can lead to feelings of lightheadedness or vertigo, particularly when taken in high doses or by individuals sensitive to its active compounds.

The herb's effects on neurotransmitters and the cardiovascular system may also contribute to this side effect.
