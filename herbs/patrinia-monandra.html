What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Patrinia monandra has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins and a woody, upright stem that typically reaches heights of up to 60 centimeters. The foliage is often arranged in whorls near the top of the plant, providing a dense texture to the upper stem. Its most distinctive feature is the small, solitary, white flowers that bloom from the leaf axils. These blossoms consist of five delicate petals surrounding a central cluster of stamens. The plant often produces a branched structure that supports multiple flowering sites throughout the growing season.
