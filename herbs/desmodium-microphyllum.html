Desmodium microphyllum

What's the taxonomical classification of Desmodium microphyllum?

Desmodium microphyllum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its lineage through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Fabales. This plant is a member of the family Fabaceae, a group known for its diverse range of legumes. Within this family, it is categorized under the genus Desmodium, and its specific identity is defined by its species name, microphyllum.

This plant is used to treat various ailments including skin infections, wounds, stomach aches, and respiratory issues. Local communities often apply the crushed leaves topically to promote the healing of cuts and skin irritations. When prepared as a decoction, it serves as a remedy for internal discomfort and digestive distress. Some traditional practices also involve using the plant to alleviate symptoms associated with coughs and other breathing difficulties. These medicinal applications highlight its significance in regional ethnobotanical knowledge.

Desmodium microphyllum is prepared primarily as a decoction or an infusion to extract its medicinal properties for internal use. The choice of preparation method typically depends on the specific therapeutic goal being addressed.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Desmodium microphyllum can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or digestive distress if ingested or handled improperly. Because it belongs to the Fabaceae family, some individuals may experience contact dermatitis from direct contact with the plant's foliage. Ingesting parts of the plant might lead to nausea or abdominal discomfort in sensitive people. There is also a potential risk of systemic allergic responses if the plant's proteins enter the bloodstream. Scientific data regarding its long-term toxicity remains limited, so caution is advised when interacting with it.
