Desmodium microphyllum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Desmodium microphyllum has trifoliate leaves with small, elliptical leaflets, hairy stems, and small, pea-like pink to purple flowers. The leaflets are typically less than 1 centimeter long and possess fine pubescence on their surfaces. Stems are slender and often covered in spreading hairs that provide a textured appearance. The inflorescence consists of small clusters of legumes that are characteristic of the Fabaceae family. These seed pods are frequently covered in hooked hairs that facilitate attachment to passing animals.
