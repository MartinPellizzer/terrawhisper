White Sage (Salvia apiana) Compounds

The primary compounds of White Sage include essential oil, flavonoids, terpenoids, and phenolic acids. These compounds contribute to its aromatic properties and potential therapeutic effects. Tannins and coumarin provide astringent and antispasmodic qualities. Glycosides, saponins, and alkaloids support its traditional use in herbal medicine. Anthocyanins add to its antioxidant profile.

White Sage (Salvia apiana) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of White Sage (Salvia apiana) contains volatile compounds like cineole and camphor. These components contribute to its aromatic properties and traditional uses in respiratory and spiritual practices. The oil is extracted through distillation and is valued for its calming and cleansing effects.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in White Sage (Salvia apiana) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help support respiratory and immune health, often used in traditional practices for cleansing and healing. They are found in the leaves and flowers, enhancing the plant's therapeutic value in herbal medicine.

3. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a key group of active compounds found in White Sage (Salvia apiana). These compounds contribute to the plant's aromatic properties and have been studied for their potential antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. They play a significant role in the traditional and modern use of White Sage in herbal medicine.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in White Sage (Salvia apiana). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound supports respiratory and immune health, making it valuable in herbal medicine practices. Its presence enhances the therapeutic potential of White Sage in traditional healing.

5. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in White Sage (Salvia apiana). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and has antimicrobial effects. Tannins help in reducing inflammation and may support wound healing when used in traditional herbal preparations.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in White Sage (Salvia apiana). It contributes to the plant's aromatic properties and has potential anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects. Coumarin is also known for its ability to support respiratory health and aid in clearing congestion.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in White Sage (Salvia apiana). It contributes to the plant's therapeutic properties, often associated with respiratory and immune support. Glycosides in White Sage are known for their ability to promote detoxification and balance bodily functions. These compounds are typically used in traditional herbal practices for their calming and cleansing effects.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in White Sage (Salvia apiana). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for its cleansing and anti-inflammatory properties. Saponins may support respiratory and immune health, aligning with the plant's historical applications in indigenous healing practices.

9. Alkaloid

White Sage (Salvia apiana) contains the alkaloid salvinorin A, which is known for its potent psychoactive properties. This compound interacts with the brain's opioid receptors, leading to altered perceptions and hallucinogenic effects. It is primarily responsible for the plant's traditional use in spiritual and ceremonial practices. Salvinorin A is unique among alkaloids for its high potency and specific receptor binding.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in White Sage (Salvia apiana). It contributes to the plant's vibrant blue and purple pigmentation. This compound possesses antioxidant properties, which may support immune function and reduce inflammation. Anthocyanin is also believed to enhance the plant's traditional uses in herbal medicine.
