Lavandula stoechas

Botanical Identification

French Lavender, Lavandula stoechas, is a perennial shrub in the Lamiaceae family. Native to the Mediterranean, it is known by regional names like "French lavender" and "Spanish lavender." Its aromatic leaves and spikes of purple flowers are characteristic, with a long history in herbal medicine. Synonyms in herbal texts include "Lavandula stoechas" and "Lavandula angustifolia."

Active Compounds

French Lavender contains essential oil, terpenoids, and flavonoids as its main bioactive compounds. These compounds work together to exert calming and anti-inflammatory effects. Terpenoids and flavonoids may synergistically enhance the herb's therapeutic properties in herbal medicine.

  • Essential oil
  • Terpenoid
  • Flavonoid

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action

Respiratory Respiratory infection, asthma, cough, respiratory infections, sore throat Antimicrobial, bronchodilator, expectorant, antiseptic

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for digestive issues and anxiety due to its calming properties.

Decoction: Used for respiratory infections and skin conditions due to its antiseptic properties.

Essential oil: Used for stress relief and to treat skin conditions like acne.

Safety Profile

French Lavender (Lavandula stoechas) is generally safe when used as directed. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation due to potential hormonal effects. It may also interact with certain medications, so consultation with a healthcare provider is recommended before use.
