Larix sibirica

What's the taxonomical classification of Larix sibirica?

Larix sibirica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Pinidae and the order Pinales. Within the family Pinaceae, it is categorized under the genus Larix, specifically identified by its species name, sibirica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Larix sibirica has deciduous needles arranged spirally on short shoots and longer on long shoots, woody cones measuring 1 to 3 centimeters, and bark that becomes thick and deeply furrowed with age. The needles are bright green, soft, and typically reach lengths of 1 to 2 centimeters. Unlike most conifers, this species sheds its foliage annually in the autumn. Its cones are ovoid in shape and contain numerous small, winged seeds designed for wind dispersal. The tree develops a conical crown that becomes more irregular and spreading as the specimen matures.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the vast boreal forest regions of northern Eurasia, spanning from eastern Europe through central Siberia and into the Russian Far East. It thrives primarily in the cold, subarctic climates of the taiga biome. Its range extends across diverse landscapes including mountain ranges and vast lowland plains where temperatures remain low for much of the year. The species is particularly well-adapted to the permafrost zones characteristic of these high-latitude territories. Due to its resilience, it forms extensive stands across some of the most rugged and frost-prone environments on the continent.

What side effects this plant can have?
