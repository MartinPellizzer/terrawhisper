Litsea chunii

What's the taxonomical classification of Litsea chunii?

Litsea chunii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Magnoliidae and is a member of the order Laurales. This plant is further categorized into the family Lauraceae, placing it within the genus Litsea, and is specifically identified by its species name, chunii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Litsea chunii has simple, alternate, coriaceous leaves that are typically elliptical to lanceolate in shape with entire margins and an acute apex.

The leaf surfaces are often glossy and dark green on the upper side, while the undersides may exhibit a paler coloration. The venation pattern is pinnate, with secondary veins that are distinct and extend toward the leaf edges. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in clusters, eventually giving rise to small drupe-like fruits.

The plant generally exhibits a woody structure characteristic of trees or shrubs within the Lauraceae family.
