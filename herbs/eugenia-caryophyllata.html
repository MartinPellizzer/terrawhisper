What to know about Eugenia caryophyllata (clove) before using it medicinally

Clove, scientifically known as Eugenia caryophyllata, is a culinary and medicinal herb that has been revered for its numerous health benefits.

Rich in antioxidants, cloves have been shown to improve respiratory function, alleviate toothaches, and even exhibit anti-inflammatory properties. In the kitchen, clove is often used to add warmth and depth to dishes like curries, stews, and baked goods, particularly in traditional Indonesian and Indian cuisine. As a horticultural staple, clove trees are cultivated for their fragrant buds, which are harvested when they are mature and ready to burst open.

With its unique botanical features, including its characteristic heart-shaped leaves and woody stems, clove has been mentioned in historical texts dating back to ancient Greece and Rome, where it was prized for its medicinal and aromatherapeutic properties.
