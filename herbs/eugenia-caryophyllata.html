Clove

Eugenia caryophyllata

Clove (Eugenia caryophyllata) is a spice and medicinal herb belonging to the Myrtaceae family. It has been traditionally used for treatment of dental issues, antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory applications, and as a digestive aid. The dried flower buds are the primary part used in both culinary and medicinal contexts.

Botanical Identification

Clove is scientifically known as Eugenia caryophyllata and belongs to the Myrtaceae family. It is also called syzygium aromaticum in some herbal texts. Native to the Maluku Islands, it is a small evergreen tree with aromatic red buds and elliptical leaves. Its roots, leaves, and flowers are used in traditional medicine.

Clove Identification.

Active Compounds

Clove contains essential oil, tannins, and flavonoids as its primary bioactive compounds. The essential oil, rich in eugenol, is the main contributor to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and analgesic effects.

  • Essential oil
  • Tannin
  • Flavonoid

Integumentary Skin infections, fungal infections, acne, mouth ulcers, skin irritation Antimicrobial, antifungal, anti-inflammatory

Clove Actions.

Preparation Methods

Essential oil: Used for respiratory issues and digestive problems.

Extract: Used for treating coughs and sore throats.

Poultice: Used for skin conditions and inflammation.

Safety Profile

Clove is generally safe when used in moderate amounts. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to potential risks. High doses may cause gastrointestinal upset or allergic reactions in sensitive individuals.
