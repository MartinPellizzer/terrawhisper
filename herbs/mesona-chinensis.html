Mesona chinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Mesona chinensis?

Mesona chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae, ultimately placing it within the genus Mesona, where it is identified as the specific species chinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Mesona chinensis has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by erect stems that reach heights of approximately 30 to 60 centimeters. The leaves are typically opposite or whorled, featuring an elliptical to ovate shape with entire margins and acute tips. Small, actinomorphic flowers emerge from the leaf axils, displaying a tubular corolla that often transitions from white to pale pink or purple. The reproductive structures consist of a superior ovary that eventually develops into a small, fleshy fruit. This plant's morphological traits are consistent with the Rubiaceae family, particularly in its leaf arrangement and floral symmetry.
