Glochidion ramiflorum

What's the taxonomical classification of Glochidion ramiflorum?

Glochidion ramiflorum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant follows the order Malpighiales and is a member of the family Phyllanthaceae. Its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement within the genus Glochidion, ultimately reaching its species designation as Glochidion ramiflorum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Glochidion ramiflorum has small, simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical to oblong in shape and possess entire margins.

The foliage typically displays a glossy upper surface with a more matte texture on the underside. Its most distinctive morphological feature is the cauliflorous flowering habit, where tiny, inconspicuous flowers emerge directly from the woody branches and trunk. These flowers are often followed by small, fleshy, globose fruits that split open to reveal seeds.

The overall structure of the plant reflects its adaptation to tropical forest environments.
