Glochidion ramiflorum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Glochidion ramiflorum has small, simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical to oblong in shape and possess entire margins. The foliage typically displays a glossy upper surface with a more matte texture on the underside. Its most distinctive morphological feature is the cauliflorous flowering habit, where tiny, inconspicuous flowers emerge directly from the woody branches and trunk. These flowers are often followed by small, fleshy, globose fruits that split open to reveal seeds. The overall structure of the plant reflects its adaptation to tropical forest environments.
