Lemmaphyllum diversum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lemmaphyllum diversum has elongated, lanceolate leaves that feature serrated margins and a distinctively thick, waxy cuticle. The stem structure is woody and branched, providing a rigid framework for the dense foliage. Each leaf displays a deep emerald pigmentation with prominent, sunken veins running parallel to the midrib. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axillary nodes in clusters of three to five. The root system consists of a deep taproot designed to anchor the plant in rocky, uneven terrain.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Lemmaphyllum diversum can cause severe skin irritation, localized swelling, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. The plant contains specific bioactive compounds that may trigger allergic contact dermatitis upon touching the leaves or stems. Once absorbed through the skin, these chemical agents can lead to redness and intense itching. If the plant is accidentally swallowed, individuals might experience nausea, abdominal cramping, or vomiting. Careful handling is necessary to prevent these adverse physical reactions.
