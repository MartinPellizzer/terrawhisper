Botanical Identification of Cow Knee (Achyranthes bidentata)

To identify Cow Knee (Achyranthes bidentata) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Cow Knee, or Achyranthes bidentata, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Spermatophyta, class Caryophyllopsida, order Caryophyllales, family Amaranthaceae, genus Achyranthes, and species Achyranthes bidentata. This classification places it within the group of flowering plants known for their medicinal properties.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Cow Knee is Achyranthes bidentata, first described by the botanist Bernhard von Hochstetter in 1855. It is also known by several synonyms, including A. bidentata var. chinensis and A. asiatica. Common names include Cow Knee, Beggarticks, and Chinese Knotweed, while regional and historical names vary across cultures and regions.

Morphological Characteristics

Cow Knee, or Achyranthes bidentata, is an annual herb with a taproot system. It has erect, branching stems that are hairy. The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have two small teeth at the base. The inflorescence is a cluster of small greenish-white flowers. The fruit is a three-lobed capsule containing three small, smooth seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Cow Knee can be distinguished by its distinctive two-lobed leaf base and reddish stems. It has a unique, branched root system and produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. These features help differentiate it from similar species like A. sisyrinchium. Its leaf shape and stem color are key diagnostic markers.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Cow Knee (Achyranthes bidentata) is native to regions in East Asia, including China, Korea, and Japan. It thrives in temperate climates with mild winters and warm summers. The plant prefers well-drained, loamy soils and is commonly found at elevations between 500 and 2000 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Cow Knee blooms from June to August, producing small, greenish flowers. Seed formation occurs from August to October. For identification purposes, the plant is typically harvested in late summer to early autumn when the seeds are mature and the leaves are still present.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Cow Knee (Achyranthes bidentata) belongs to the Amaranthaceae family, closely related to species like A. asiatica and A. paniculata. It shares characteristics with other genus members, such as opposite leaves and clustered inflorescences. Its family traits include bracteate flowers and fleshy fruits, distinguishing it from other plant groups.
