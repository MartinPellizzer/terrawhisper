According to a study published by "Planta medica", eight new flavonoids were isolated from the roots of Campylotropis hirtella. These new compounds included 3 pterocarpene derivatives known as hirtellanines C-E, 2 flavanones called hirtellanines F and G, and 3 isoflavanones named hirtellanines H-J. Along with these, 4 known compounds were also identified from the roots. Pharmacological investigation showed that the new compounds possessed potent immunosuppressive activities and low relative cytotoxicities. Specifically, hirtellanines D and G were noted for these potent immunosuppressive activities.
