    Correa rufa is a flowering plant native to Australia, belonging to the family Rutaceae.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical in shape, measuring 4-12 centimeters in length and 2-6 centimeters in width, with a leathery texture and a glabrous surface. The flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and fragrant, borne in clusters at the end of branches, with five petals and a prominent cluster of stamens.
