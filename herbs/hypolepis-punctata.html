Hypolepis punctata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hypolepis punctata has a creeping rhizome covered in pale, brownish scales and produces fronds that feature pinnate or bipinnate leaflets with distinctively spotted or punctate undersides. The stipes are typically dark, often appearing brownish or blackish, and are clothed in dense, small scales. Each pinna is arranged along the central axis, with individual segments displaying serrated or lobed margins. The sori, which contain the spores, are positioned near the margins of the pinnules and are protected by specialized structures called indusia. These morphological traits, particularly the spotted underside of the laminae, serve as key diagnostic features for identifying the species.
