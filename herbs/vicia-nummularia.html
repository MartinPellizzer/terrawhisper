Vicia nummularia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vicia nummularia has pinnate leaves composed of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets and terminal tendrils used for climbing. The plant exhibits a trailing or climbing growth habit with slender, herbaceous stems that can reach varying lengths depending on the environment. Its flowers are typically pea-like in structure, featuring a distinct keel, wings, and a standard petal that often displays shades of purple or blue. The small, rounded leaflets are arranged oppositely along the leaf axis, contributing to the plant's characteristic texture. These morphological features allow the species to efficiently navigate through surrounding vegetation to reach sunlight.

      What are the herbal preparations of Vicia nummularia?

      Vicia nummularia is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice to utilize its medicinal properties.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Vicia nummularia can cause gastrointestinal distress, allergic skin reactions, and potential neurotoxicity if ingested in significant quantities. These adverse effects primarily stem from the presence of certain alkaloids and glycosides within the plant tissues. Individuals consuming the plant may experience nausea, abdominal cramping, or vomiting shortly after ingestion. Contact with the leaves or stems might also trigger dermatitis or localized irritation in sensitive people. Because the specific toxicological profile is not extensively documented, caution is necessary when handling or consuming any part of this species.
