What's the taxonomical classification of Palisota pynaertii?

Palisota pynaertii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic journey continues through the order Commelinares and the family Commelinaeceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Palisota, and its unique species designation, pynaertii.

      Palisota pynaertii is prepared as a decoction, a juice, or a topical paste to utilize its medicinal properties. The plant parts are often boiled in water to create a concentrated liquid used for internal consumption.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Palisota pynaertii can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and potential toxicity if ingested due to the presence of bioactive compounds. While specific human clinical studies are limited, the plant belongs to a group known for containing secondary metabolites that may act as irritants to sensitive tissues. Caution is advised when handling this species to prevent accidental exposure to these bioactive substances.
