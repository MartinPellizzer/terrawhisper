Palisota pynaertii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Palisota pynaertii has large, elliptical leaves with prominent parallel venation and a distinctive longitudinal ridge running along the midrib. These leaves are typically bright green and possess a smooth, somewhat waxy texture on their upper surface. The plant features a robust, fleshy stem that serves as the central support for its expansive foliage. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often arranged in simple clusters. The overall structure is characteristic of an herbaceous perennial adapted to moist, shaded environments.
