Urtica fissa

What's the taxonomical classification of Urtica fissa?

Urtica fissa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It is further organized into the order Rosales and falls under the family Urticaceae. Ultimately, the plant is defined by its genus, Urtica, and its specific species designation, fissa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Urtica fissa has an erect, perennial habit characterized by oppositely arranged, ovate to lanceolate leaves that feature deeply serrated margins and a distinctly fissured or lobed base. The foliage is typically covered in fine, stinging hairs called trichomes that serve as a defense mechanism. Its stems are slender and often exhibit a reddish tint, providing structural support for the spreading leaf canopy. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in clusters, usually appearing in the leaf axils. The overall structure is adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments where its leafy architecture can maximize light absorption.
