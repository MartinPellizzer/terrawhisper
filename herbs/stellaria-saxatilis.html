Stellaria saxatilis

What's the taxonomical classification of Stellaria saxatilis?

Stellaria saxatilis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Caryophyllales. Furthermore, this plant is a member of the family Caryophyllaceae, specifically categorized within the genus Stellaria, with its unique designation as the species saxatilis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stellaria saxatilis has small, lanceolate leaves that are oppositely arranged along a slender, creeping stem. The plant typically grows in low, spreading mats with fine hairs covering the surface of the foliage. Its tiny white flowers feature five distinct petals that are deeply notched at the tips. The reproductive structures consist of short filaments and a central ovary that develops into a small capsule. These morphological features allow the species to thrive in rocky, shallow soil environments.
