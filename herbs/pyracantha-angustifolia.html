Pyracantha angustifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Pyracantha angustifolia?

Pyracantha angustifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Rosales and the family Rosaceae, which places it within the genus Pyracantha. Finally, the specific identification of this plant is completed by its species name, angustifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pyracantha angustifolia has narrow, linear to lanceolate leaves measuring approximately 1 to 4 centimeters in width, woody stems equipped with sharp spines, and clusters of small orange or red berries. The foliage is typically dark green and leathery, providing a dense texture to the shrub. Its branching structure is irregular and spreading, often forming a thorny thicket as the plant matures. Small, five-petaled white flowers emerge in spring from the axils of the leaves before transitioning into fruit. These berries remain on the branches throughout much of the winter season.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires full sun to partial shade and well-drained, fertile soil to thrive during its cultivation.

Gardeners should wear heavy gloves while working because the species features sharp, woody thorns that can cause injury.
