What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Chrysosplenium lanuginosum has a perennial growth habit characterized by densely lanate or woolly foliage and small, yellow, discoid flower heads.

The leaves are typically arranged in a basal rosette or along the stem, featuring a thick coating of fine white hairs that provide a soft texture. These trichomes serve to protect the plant from moisture loss and intense sunlight. The inflorescence consists of compact capitula that emerge from the leafy structure.

Each flower head is composed of numerous tiny florets, which collectively create a bright yellow appearance.
