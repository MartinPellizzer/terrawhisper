10 Possible Side Effects Of Polygala Senega (Senega Snakeroot)

Polygala senega has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal upset, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, caused by its saponin content.

Prolonged use can worsen irritable bowel syndrome and malabsorption, affecting daily life by causing persistent discomfort and weight loss.

Furthermore, its interaction with medications, like blood thinners, can increase the risk of bleeding, compromising mobility and independence.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Polygala senega if used imporperly.

The compound polygalic acid present in the plant is believed to be responsible for triggering an allergic response in some individuals. This reaction can manifest in various forms, including skin irritation, respiratory issues, and gastrointestinal problems.

The exact mechanisms behind this reaction are not fully understood, but it is thought that the body's immune system mistakenly identifies polygalic acid as a foreign substance, leading to an overactive inflammatory response.
