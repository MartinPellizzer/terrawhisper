Senega Snakeroot (Polygala senega) Compounds

The primary compounds in Senega Snakeroot include saponins, which have expectorant properties, and alkaloids, which may support respiratory function. Flavonoids and glycosides contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Tannins and phenolic acids provide astringent and antimicrobial benefits, while coumarin and anthocyanin support overall plant vitality and potential therapeutic uses.

Senega Snakeroot (Polygala senega) Monograph

Saponin is a key active compound in Senega Snakeroot (Polygala senega). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in respiratory and digestive health.
