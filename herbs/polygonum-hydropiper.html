Polygonum hydropiper

What's the taxonomical classification of Polygonum hydropiper?

Polygonum hydropiper belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Caryophyllales. Further taxonomic refinement places this plant in the family Polygonaceae, specifically within the genus Polygonum, leading to its scientific designation as the species hydropiper.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polygonum hydropiper has erect, slender stems that reach heights of up to 60 centimeters and are characterized by reddish-purple spots and swollen nodes. The leaves are lanceolate or narrowly ovate, featuring smooth margins and a distinctively blunt or rounded tip. Small, white, tear-shaped flowers grow in dense clusters within the leaf axils. A defining morphological feature is the presence of a dark, ocreate sheath at each node that wraps around the stem. The plant produces small, reddish-brown achenes that serve as its primary fruit type.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to East and Southeast Asia, specifically spanning across China, Japan, Korea, Taiwan, and parts of India. It is commonly found in moist, disturbed habitats such as riverbanks, wetlands, and agricultural fields throughout these regions. Due to its hardiness, it has been introduced to many other parts of the world as an ornamental or accidental weed. In some non-native territories, it can establish itself in temperate climates where soil moisture remains consistently high. This wide distribution is facilitated by its ability to thrive in various soil types across diverse subtropical and temperate zones.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Polygonum hydropiper targets cyclooxygenase-2, inducible nitric oxide synthase, and various proinflammatory cytokines to exert its primary medicinal effects. The bioactive compounds within the plant, such as piperine and various polyphenols, inhibit the arachidonic acid metabolism pathway to reduce inflammation. These molecules interact with the nuclear factor kappa B signaling pathway to suppress the expression of inflammatory mediators. Additionally, the plant exhibits neuroprotective properties by modulating acetylcholinesterase activity and regulating oxidative stress-related enzymes. Such multi-target interactions allow the plant to address systemic inflammation and neurological dysfunction simultaneously.

What health problems are treated using this plant?

This plant is used for treating gastritis, gastrointestinal disorders, inflammation, microbial infections, and diarrhea. Its bioactive compounds work to soothe the digestive tract and reduce swelling in internal tissues. The medicinal properties help combat harmful microbial infections that disrupt the gut microbiome. By regulating intestinal movements, it effectively manages symptoms associated with chronic diarrhea. These diverse applications make it a significant component in traditional herbal remedies for digestive health.

As discussed in a study, 99% methanol extracts of Polygonum hydropiper, referred to as Ph-ME, demonstrate anti-inflammatory effects by dose-dependently suppressing the release of nitric oxide (NO), tumour necrosis factor (TNF)-α, and prostaglandin (PGE2) in RAW264.7 cells and peritoneal macrophages stimulated by lipopolysaccharide (LPS). The extract inhibits mRNA expression of pro-inflammatory genes such as inducible NO synthase (iNOS), cyclooxygenase (COX)-2, and TNF-α by suppressing the activation of nuclear factor (NF)-κB, activator protein (AP-1), and cAMP responsive element binding protein (CREB). Ph-ME also inhibits upstream inflammatory signalling cascades involving Syk, Src, and IRAK1, while strongly suppressing the kinase activities of Src and Syk. HPLC analysis identified quercetin, which inhibits NO and PGE2 activities, as one of the active ingredients in Ph-ME. These actions through the suppression of Src/Syk/NF-κB and IRAK/AP-1/CREB pathways contribute to its major role as an anti-gastritis remedy.
