Vaccinium trichocladum

What's the taxonomical classification of Vaccinium trichocladum?

Vaccinium trichocladum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Ericales. This plant is a member of the family Ericaceae, belonging to the genus Vaccinium, and is specifically identified by its species name, trichocladuum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vaccinium trichocladum has a woody, shrubby growth habit characterized by slender, erect stems and small, elliptic to lanceolate leaves with entire margins.

The foliage is typically dark green and densely packed along the branches, often featuring a slightly leathery texture. Its reproductive structures consist of small, bell-shaped flowers that emerge from the leaf axils during the blooming season. These flowers eventually develop into small, fleshy berries that serve as the primary fruit of the species.

The overall morphology is adapted to its specific ecological niche, allowing it to thrive in shaded understory environments.
