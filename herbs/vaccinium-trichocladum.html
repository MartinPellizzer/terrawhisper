Vaccinium trichocladum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vaccinium trichocladum has a woody, shrubby growth habit characterized by slender, erect stems and small, elliptic to lanceolate leaves with entire margins. The foliage is typically dark green and densely packed along the branches, often featuring a slightly leathery texture. Its reproductive structures consist of small, bell-shaped flowers that emerge from the leaf axils during the blooming season. These flowers eventually develop into small, fleshy berries that serve as the primary fruit of the species. The overall morphology is adapted to its specific ecological niche, allowing it to thrive in shaded understory environments.
