Duchesnea indica

What's the taxonomical classification of Duchesnea indica?

Duchesnea indica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this botanical lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Rosales and is a member of the family Rosaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific genus, Duchesnea, and its unique species designation, indica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Duchesnea indica has creeping, rhizomatous stems that produce fleshy, heart-shaped to ovate leaves measuring approximately 2 to 5 centimeters in length. These leaves are characterized by a glossy, dark green upper surface and often feature a lighter green or silver underside with prominent venation. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils, typically displaying a vibrant red or orange hue. The plant lacks true woody tissue, instead relying on its succulent-like stems to spread across the forest floor. This spreading growth habit allows it to form dense, ground-covering mats in humid environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the subtropical and tropical regions of eastern Asia, specifically spanning across China, Japan, Taiwan, and parts of Southeast Asia. It thrives primarily in moist, shaded forest understories where humidity levels remain consistently high. While its natural range is concentrated in these warm climates, it has been introduced to various temperate regions worldwide as an ornamental groundcover. In these new environments, it typically occupies garden settings or greenhouse conditions that mimic its original habitat. What are the pharmacological activities of Duchesnea indica?

This plant has significant pharmacological activities including anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, anticancer, antioxidant, and blood-cooling properties. The bioactive compounds within the species help neutralize free radicals, which provides its potent antioxidant effect. These chemical constituents also inhibit the growth of various pathogens through its demonstrated antibacterial action. Furthermore, the plant's ability to reduce systemic heat supports its traditional use for blood-cooling purposes. Research also indicates that its unique phytochemical profile contributes to its potential anticancer and anti-inflammatory capabilities.

According to a study published by "Biochemistry and biophysics reports", Duchesnea indica extracts exert anti-inflammatory effects by inhibiting the MAPK/ERK pathway in LPS-stimulated RAW264.7 cells. These extracts contain a total polysaccharide content of 32.62 ± 0.91 %, reducing sugar content of 13.41 ± 0.18 %, total flavonoid content of 1.07 ± 0.07 %, and total polyphenol content of 12.16 ± 0.27 %. The plant possesses therapeutic properties such as heat-clearing, detoxification, dispersion of stasis detumescence, blood-cooling, and hemostatic effect. In LPS-stimulated RAW264.7 cells, the extracts demonstrate significant inhibition on NO and MDA levels while enhancing SOD activity. Furthermore, the extracts significantly reduce the mRNA levels of IL-6, IL-1β, iNOS, COX-2, and TNF-α.
