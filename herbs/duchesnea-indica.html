Duchesnea indica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Duchesnea indica has creeping, rhizomatous stems that produce fleshy, heart-shaped to ovate leaves measuring approximately 2 to 5 centimeters in length. These leaves are characterized by a glossy, dark green upper surface and often feature a lighter green or silver underside with prominent venation. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils, typically displaying a vibrant red or orange hue. The plant lacks true woody tissue, instead relying on its succulent-like stems to spread across the forest floor. This spreading growth habit allows it to form dense, ground-covering mats in humid environments.
