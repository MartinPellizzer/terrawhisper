Coelogyne ovalis

What's the taxonomical classification of Coelogyne ovalis?

Coelogyne ovalis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is classified under the order Asparagales. This plant is a member of the family Orchidaceae, specifically falling within the genus Coelogyne, with its unique identity defined by its species name, ovalis.

Coelogyne ovalis contains no documented medicinal compounds or proven pharmacological properties in current scientific literature. Researchers have not yet isolated specific bioactive molecules from this plant for therapeutic use in human medicine. Most studies concerning this species focus on its botanical characteristics and its role within its natural habitat rather than its chemical constituents. What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, inflammatory conditions, and minor wounds. The medicinal properties of Coelogyne ovalis are primarily utilized for their potential antibacterial and soothing effects on the dermis. Localized applications of its extracts may help reduce redness and swelling caused by various irritants.
