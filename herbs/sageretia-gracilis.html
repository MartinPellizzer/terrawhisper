Sageretia gracilis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sageretia gracilis has a slender, erect growth habit characterized by thin, woody stems and small, lanceolate leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the branches. The foliage is relatively sparse, often appearing somewhat coriaceous with distinct midveins and entire margins. Its inflorescences consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the axils of the leaves. The overall structure of the plant is delicate and airy, reflecting its gracile namesake. These morphological traits help distinguish it from other members of the genus within its specific habitat.
