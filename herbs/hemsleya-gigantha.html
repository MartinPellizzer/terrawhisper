Hemsleya gigantha

What's the taxonomical classification of Hemsleya gigantha?

Hemsleya gigantha belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is situated under the order Cucurbitales. It is further categorized into the family Cucurbitaceae, which places it within the genus Hemleysa, ultimately identifying it by its specific species name, gigantha.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hemsleya gigantha has large, deeply divided, tripinnate fronds that can reach lengths of up to one meter with pinnules characterized by lobed or serrated margins. The plant features a robust creeping rhizome that serves as the primary structural base for its sprawling growth habit. Each leaf consists of multiple complex levels of division, creating a highly textured and feathery appearance. The stipes are typically long and sturdy, supporting the expansive canopy of the foliage. Small, inconspicuous sporangia are located on the underside of the fertile fronds to facilitate reproduction.
