Cinnamon Fern

Dryopteris crassirhizoma

Cinnamon Fern (Dryopteris crassirhizoma) is a type of fern native to Asia, known for its distinctive appearance and traditional use in herbal medicine to help alleviate symptoms of respiratory conditions such as asthma and bronchitis.

Botanical Identification

Cinnamon Fern, scientifically known as Dryopteris crassirhizoma, belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae. It is also called cinnamon fern and is a perennial plant native to eastern North America. The plant features a thick, cinnamon-scented rhizome, with large, bipinnate leaves and no true flowers. Synonyms include Osmunda cinnamomea in some herbal texts.

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for respiratory and digestive ailments.

Poultice: Applied for skin irritations and wounds.

Extract: Used for inflammation and pain relief.

Safety Profile

Cinnamon fern is generally safe when used in moderate amounts. However, it may cause gastrointestinal upset in some individuals. Consult a healthcare provider before use, especially during pregnancy or if taking other medications.
