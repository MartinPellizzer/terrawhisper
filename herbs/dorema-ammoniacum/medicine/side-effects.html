10 Possible Side Effects Of Dorema Ammoniacum

Dorema ammoniacum has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, allergic reactions, and skin irritation.

These side effects can be caused by ingesting too much of the herb, using it without proper dilution, or being allergic to its constituents.

If left untreated, these side effects can worsen, leading to nausea, dizziness, and difficulty breathing, which can disrupt daily activities and impact work or school performance.

1. Causes vomiting

Dorema ammoniacum, also known as Iranian Galbanum, causes vomiting.

This is likely due to the presence of volatile oils and alkaloids in the plant, which can irritate the stomach lining and trigger nausea and vomiting. The toxic properties of Dorema ammoniacum may also contribute to this side effect by disrupting normal digestion and gut function.

The severity of vomiting can vary from mild to severe depending on the individual's sensitivity and dose of the herb.

2. Induces sweating

Dorema ammoniacum, also known as Iranian Galbanum, induces sweating.

This side effect is attributed to the plant's active compounds, particularly terpenes and alkaloids, which stimulate the sweat glands in the skin, leading to an increase in perspiration.

The exact mechanism behind this response is not fully understood, but it is thought that these compounds interact with the nervous system to regulate body temperature and induce a sweating response.

3. Triggers diarrhea

Dorema ammoniacum, also known as Iranian Galbanum, triggers diarrhea due to its high content of volatile oils, particularly cuminaldehyde and other terpenes.

The presence of these compounds can irritate the mucous membranes in the digestive tract, leading to an increase in bowel movement frequency and looseness of stools.

This side effect is a direct result of the plant's bioactive components interacting with the body's natural processes, causing alterations in gut motility and secretion.

4. Produces respiratory irritation

Dorema ammoniacum, also known as Iranian Galbanum, produces respiratory irritation due to its high concentration of volatile oils and terpenes, particularly 1-methyl-4-(1-methylethyl)-benzene, which can cause constriction of the airways and increased mucus production.

This irritation can lead to symptoms such as coughing, wheezing, and shortness of breath, particularly in individuals with pre-existing respiratory conditions or sensitivities.

5. Enhances allergic reactions

Dorema ammoniacum, also known as Iranian Galbanum, enhances allergic reactions due to its high concentration of allergenic compounds such as volatile oils and sesquiterpenes.

These chemicals can trigger an overactive immune response in some individuals, leading to symptoms like hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.

The allergenic properties of Dorema ammoniacum may also cause contact dermatitis, a skin reaction characterized by redness, swelling, and blistering.

6. Increases blood pressure

Dorema ammoniacum, also known as Iranian Galbanum, increases blood pressure due to its chemical composition.

The plant contains a volatile oil rich in piperitone and phellandrene, which can cause blood vessels to constrict, leading to increased blood pressure. This constriction of blood vessels can also increase the workload on the heart, further contributing to the rise in blood pressure.

As a result, people may experience increased blood pressure when using Dorema ammoniacum.

7. Affects digestive system

Dorema ammoniacum, also known as Iranian Galbanum, affects digestive system by irritating the mucous membranes in the stomach and intestines.

The volatile oils present in the plant, such as pinane and limonene, can cause inflammation and discomfort, leading to symptoms like diarrhea, nausea, vomiting, and abdominal cramps.

Additionally, the saponins in Dorema ammoniacum may alter gut bacteria, disrupting normal digestive processes and contributing to gastrointestinal side effects.

8. Activates skin rash

Dorema ammoniacum, also known as Iranian Galbanum, activates skin rash due to its high concentration of volatile oil compounds.

These oils can cause irritation and allergic reactions when coming into contact with the skin, leading to symptoms such as redness, itching, and inflammation.

Additionally, the oil's aromatic properties can also trigger an immune response, further exacerbating the rash.

9. Stimulates cardiac arrhythmia

Dorema ammoniacum, also known as Iranian Galbanum, stimulates cardiac arrhythmia due to its ability to increase heart rate and blood pressure.

The volatile oils present in the plant, such as doremein, can interact with the nervous system, leading to an irregular heartbeat rhythm.

This is because these oils affect the autonomic nervous system's balance between sympathetic and parasympathetic branches, resulting in abnormal cardiac activity.

10. Reduces appetite

Dorema ammoniacum, also known as Iranian Galbanum, reduces appetite due to its stimulating properties that affect the digestive system.

The herb's volatile oils and alkaloids can irritate the stomach and intestines, leading to a decrease in hunger sensations and reduced food intake.

This side effect is often seen when taking high doses of Dorema ammoniacum or using it for extended periods.
