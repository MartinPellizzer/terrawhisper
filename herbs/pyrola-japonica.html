Pyrola japonica

What's the taxonomical classification of Pyrola japonica?

Pyrola japonica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized as Equisetopsida, falling under the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Ericales and the family Ericaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Pyrola, and its unique species designation, japonica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pyrola japonica has a perennial rhizomatous herb structure characterized by narrow, lanceolate leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem and small, bell-shaped pinkish-white flowers with five petals. The foliage typically displays a smooth texture and grows in a basal rosette before extending upward on a slender stem. Its flowers are suspended from delicate pedicels and feature prominent stamens that are tucked within the corolla. The plant often develops a slightly woody base due to its creeping rhizome system. This morphology allows it to thrive in the moist, shaded environments of forest floors.
