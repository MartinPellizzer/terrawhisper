Dicliptera tinctoria

What's the taxonomical classification of Dicliptera tinctoria?

Dicliptera tinctoria belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further falls into the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Acanthaceae. Specifically, it is identified under the genus Dicliptera, with its unique species designation being tinctoria.

Through these diverse protein interactions, the plant exerts significant anti-inflammatory and antioxidant activities.

What are the herbal preparations of Dicliptera tinctoria?

Dicliptera tinctoria is prepared primarily as a liquid tincture, a decoction, or a topical infusion for medicinal application. The most common method involves soaking the dried plant material in a solvent like ethanol to extract its bioactive compounds. Practitioners also use aqueous decoctions by boiling the stems and leaves to create a tea for internal consumption. For external issues, a concentrated infusion can be applied directly to the skin as a wash or compress. These diverse preparation methods allow the plant's properties to be delivered through various therapeutic routes.
