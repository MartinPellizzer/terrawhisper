What's the taxonomical classification of Cardamine griffithii?

Cardamine griffithii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Brassicales and the family Brassicaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Cardamine, and its species designation, griffithii.

      What are the herbal preparations of Cardamine griffithii?

      Cardamine griffithii is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice depending on the intended medicinal application. The leaves and stems are often boiled in water to create a liquid extract used for internal consumption. For external skin ailments, the fresh plant material is crushed into a paste to form a poultice. Some practitioners also utilize dried plant matter to make concentrated tinctures using alcohol as a solvent. These various forms allow the plant's active compounds to be delivered effectively to different parts of the body.

While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, members of the Brassicaceae family often contain glucosinolates that may trigger sensitivities in certain individuals. It is important to exercise caution and avoid direct contact or ingestion to prevent these adverse reactions.
