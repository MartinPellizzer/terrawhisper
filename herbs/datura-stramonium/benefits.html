10 Best Datura Stramonium Health Benefits

Datura stramonium, commonly known as jimsonweed, is a toxic plant that contains potent alkaloids such as scopolamine and hyoscyamine, which have historically been used in traditional medicine for their anticholinergic properties.

However, due to its high toxicity and potential for severe side effects, including hallucinations, delirium, and even death, it is not recommended for use in modern healthcare settings. While some studies have explored its potential in treating conditions like Parkinson's disease or as a muscle relaxant, its use remains highly restricted and largely experimental. The plant's effects on the nervous system make it particularly dangerous, especially when ingested accidentally or in large doses.

As a result, its health benefits are not widely recognized or endorsed by medical professionals due to the significant risks involved.
