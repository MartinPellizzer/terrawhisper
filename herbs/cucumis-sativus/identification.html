Botanical Identification of Cucumber (Cucumis sativus)

To identify Cucumber (Cucumis sativus) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Cucumber (Cucumis sativus) belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Liliopsida, order Cucurbitales, family Cucurbitaceae, genus Cucumis, and species Cucumis sativus. These classifications help identify its botanical relationships and place it within the plant kingdom.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of cucumber is Cucumis sativus, a member of the Cucurbitaceae family. It was first described by the botanist Carl von Linné in 1753. The species has several synonyms, including Cucumis anguria and Cucumis melo, though these are now considered outdated. Common names vary widely, with "cucumber" being the most widely recognized globally.

Morphological Characteristics

Cucumis sativus is an annual herb with a taproot system that anchors it firmly in the soil. Its stem is cylindrical, hairy, and grows upright or slightly trailing. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin. The plant produces monoecious flowers, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. The fruit is a pepo, typically green, elongated, and covered in a rough, spiny texture. Seeds are small, oval, and white, enclosed within the fruit's interior.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Cucumis sativus can be distinguished by its trailing vines, large lobed leaves with serrated edges, and cucumber-shaped fruits with a smooth, green skin. It differs from similar species like Cucurbita pepo by having thinner stems and smaller, more pointed leaves. The fruit's distinct shape and texture also help in identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Cucumis sativus is native to South Asia, particularly India and Pakistan. It thrives in warm, temperate climates with ample sunlight. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils. It is commonly found at altitudes between 200 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Cucumis sativus blooms 50-70 days after germination, typically in late spring to early summer. Seed formation occurs 10-14 days after flowering. Harvest timing for identification purposes is 55-70 days post-germination, when fruits are 15-20 cm long and show mature coloration.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Cucumis sativus is closely related to species like Cucumis melo and Cucumis dipsacifolius. It belongs to the genus Cucumis within the Cucurbitaceae family. Shared traits include trailing growth habit, tendrils, and monoecious flowering, which help in identification and classification.
