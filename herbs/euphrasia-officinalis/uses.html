Euphrasia (Euphrasia officinalis) Uses

Euphrasia officinalis is commonly used to treat eye conditions such as conjunctivitis, red eye, and eye strain. It helps alleviate symptoms like watery eyes, blurred vision, and eye irritation. The herb is also used for dry eyes and skin issues like infections and irritation. Its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties support these applications.

Euphrasia (Euphrasia officinalis) Monograph

1. Conjunctivitis

Euphrasia officinalis is traditionally used for conjunctivitis due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and discharge in the eyes. Often prepared as a tincture or eye wash, it is valued for its soothing effect on irritated mucous membranes.

2. Dry eyes

Euphrasia officinalis is used to treat dry eyes due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It helps reduce redness, irritation, and excessive tearing. Often prepared as a tincture or eye wash, it supports eye health and comfort. It is particularly effective for conditions like conjunctivitis and irritated corneas.

3. Skin infections

Euphrasia officinalis is used topically to treat skin infections due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and bacterial growth in conditions like eczema and minor wounds. Its astringent qualities also promote healing and prevent further infection.

4. Eyestrain

Euphrasia officinalis is traditionally used to alleviate eye strain, particularly from prolonged reading or screen use. It supports the health of the eyes by reducing inflammation and improving circulation. Its astringent properties help soothe irritated tissues, making it a common remedy in herbal eye care formulations.

5. Skin irritation

Euphrasia officinalis is used to address skin irritation due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It helps soothe redness, itching, and minor rashes. Often prepared as a compress or infusion, it provides localized relief and promotes skin healing.

6. Eye strain

Euphrasia officinalis is traditionally used to alleviate eye strain, particularly from prolonged reading or screen use. It is believed to support eye health by reducing redness and irritation. Its astringent properties may help soothe tired eyes and improve focus. It is commonly prepared as a tincture or eye wash for this purpose.

7. Red eye

Euphrasia officinalis is traditionally used for red eye, particularly to reduce inflammation and irritation. It is often prepared as a tincture or eye wash. Its astringent properties help soothe the conjunctiva, and it is valued for its ability to relieve symptoms associated with eye strain and minor infections.

8. Blurred vision

Euphrasia officinalis is traditionally used to support eye health, particularly for blurred vision. It is believed to reduce eye strain and inflammation, often used in eye drops. Its astringent properties may help clear mucus and improve clarity. It is commonly recommended for conditions like conjunctivitis and irritated eyes.

9. Eye irritation

Euphrasia officinalis is traditionally used to address eye irritation, including redness, itching, and watering. It is believed to support the health of the eyes and reduce inflammation. Often prepared as a tincture or infusion, it is applied externally to the eyes or taken internally in small doses. Its soothing properties make it a valued remedy in herbal medicine for minor eye discomfort.

10. Watery eyes

Euphrasia officinalis is used to treat watery eyes, often associated with inflammation or excessive tearing. It helps reduce eye irritation and redness, making it a common remedy in herbal eye care. This plant is typically prepared as a tincture or eye wash for direct application. Its soothing properties support healing and comfort in mild eye conditions.
