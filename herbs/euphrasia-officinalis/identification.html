Botanical Identification of Euphrasia (Euphrasia officinalis)

To identify Euphrasia (Euphrasia officinalis) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Euphrasia officinalis belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Lamiales, order Scrophulariales, family Scrophulariaceae, genus Euphrasia, and species Euphrasia officinalis. This classification places it within the flowering plants, specifically in the order and family associated with medicinal and ornamental species.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Euphrasia officinalis is the scientific name for the common eye herb. It was first described by Carl von Linné in 1753. Synonyms include Euphrasia rostkoviana and Euphrasia longiflora. It is also known as eyebright in English, with regional names like blightweed and water eye in various parts of Europe.

Morphological Characteristics

Euphrasia officinalis is a low-growing herb with a fibrous root system. It has upright, slender stems that are often branched. Its leaves are opposite, oval, and have a hairy texture. The plant produces a dense cluster of small, yellowish flowers with two lips. The fruit is a capsule containing numerous tiny, black seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Euphrasia officinalis can be distinguished by its dark green, hairy leaves with prominent veins, yellowish-orange flowers with two rows of teeth, and a slender, branched stem. It lacks the purple stems and larger flowers of Euphrasia nemorosa, and its leaves are more densely hairy than those of Euphrasia longifolia.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Euphrasia officinalis is native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils. It is typically found at low to mid-elevation areas, often in meadows and woodland edges.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Euphrasia officinalis blooms from April to July, depending on the region. Seed formation occurs in late summer, typically between July and September. For identification purposes, the plant is most easily recognized during its flowering period, which is key for accurate botanical identification.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Euphrasia officinalis belongs to the genus Euphrasia within the family Scrophulariaceae. Its closest relatives include other Euphrasia species, such as E. longifolia and E. nemorosa. The genus shares key family traits like opposite leaves and showy flowers, which help distinguish it from related genera.
