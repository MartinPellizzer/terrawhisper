Euphrasia (Euphrasia officinalis) Compounds

Euphrasia officinalis contains several primary compounds, including flavonoids, which have anti-inflammatory properties. Tannins and phenolic acids contribute to its astringent effects. Glycosides and coumarins support its traditional use in eye health. Saponins, terpenoids, and anthocyanins also play roles in its medicinal profile. Essential oils and alkaloids add to its complex pharmacological activity.

Euphrasia (Euphrasia officinalis) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of compounds found in Euphrasia officinalis. They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in treating eye conditions and reducing irritation. These compounds are also believed to enhance the plant's overall therapeutic effects in herbal medicine.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Euphrasia officinalis. It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support eye health by reducing inflammation. Tannins also help in the binding of proteins, potentially aiding in the treatment of eye conditions.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Euphrasia officinalis. It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in treating eye conditions and reducing irritation. This compound is found in significant amounts in the aerial parts of the plant.

4. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Euphrasia officinalis. It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and soothing properties, commonly used in eye remedies. This compound helps reduce redness and irritation, making Euphrasia a valued herb in herbal medicine.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a compound found in Euphrasia officinalis. It contributes to the plant's mild antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarin may support eye health and reduce irritation, making it a key component in traditional herbal formulations for eye-related conditions.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Euphrasia officinalis. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating eye conditions and inflammation. Saponins have mild surfactant properties and may support the plant's therapeutic effects in herbal medicine.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Euphrasia officinalis contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help reduce eye inflammation and support overall immune function. They are commonly found in the plant's aerial parts and are used in herbal formulations for their therapeutic benefits.

8. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Euphrasia officinalis. It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Found in the flowers, it supports eye health and may reduce oxidative stress. Its presence is a significant factor in the traditional use of Euphrasia for eye-related conditions.

9. Essential oil

Essential oil is a volatile compound found in Euphrasia officinalis. It contributes to the plant's aromatic profile and may support its traditional use in eye and respiratory health. The oil is extracted through steam distillation and is valued for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

10. Alkaloid

Euphrasia officinalis contains the alkaloid eupharidine. This compound contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating eye conditions and inflammation. Eupharidine is known for its mild anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. It is typically present in lower concentrations compared to other active constituents in the plant.
