Limonia acidissima

What's the taxonomical classification of Limonia acidissima?

Limonia acidissima belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Sapindales and the family Rutaecaeae. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Limonia, with the specific designation of the species acidissima.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Limonia acidissima contains bioactive compounds including alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, tannins, and glycosides that provide various therapeutic benefits. The presence of these phytochemicals contributes to its significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Specifically, the extracts are frequently utilized for their antimicrobial activity against various pathogens. Some studies also suggest that the plant's chemical constituents may assist in managing digestive disorders and regulating blood glucose levels. These medicinal qualities make the wood, leaves, and fruit valuable in traditional medicine systems.

What are the therapeutic actions of Limonia acidissima?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat diarrhea, dysentery, skin infections, and various gastrointestinal ailments. The fruit and bark possess astringent and antimicrobial properties that help regulate bowel movements. Extracts from the plant are often applied topically to soothe skin irritations and heal wounds. Some traditional practices also utilize the seeds and leaves to manage fever and respiratory issues. These therapeutic applications rely heavily on the chemical compounds found within the plant's various parts.
