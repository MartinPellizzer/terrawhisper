What side-effects this plant can have?

Tylophora secamonoides can cause significant toxicity in humans and animals due to its high concentration of cardiac glycosides which may lead to severe heart rhythm disturbances. Ingestion of this plant can trigger nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain as the body reacts to its chemical composition. These potent compounds can interfere with the sodium-potassium pump in cells, potentially causing life-threatening arrhythmias or cardiac arrest. Furthermore, excessive consumption may lead to neurological symptoms such as confusion, dizziness, or visual disturbances. Monitoring for these symptoms is essential because the toxic effects can escalate quickly if the plant is consumed in large quantities.
