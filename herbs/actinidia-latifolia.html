Actinidia latifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Actinidia latifolia has large, broadly ovate to elliptic leaves with obtuse or rounded apices and entire or slightly wavy margins. These leaves typically measure several centimeters in length and possess prominent venation that is characteristic of the genus. The plant exhibits a woody, climbing habit with slender stems that utilize tendrils for support. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into fleshy, berry-like fruits. The overall morphological structure is adapted for life in shaded, forest understory environments.
