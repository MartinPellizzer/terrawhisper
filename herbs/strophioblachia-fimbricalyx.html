What's the taxonomical classification of Strophioblachia fimbricalyx?

Strophioblachia fimbricalyx belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down its taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the order Malpighiales and the family Euphorbiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Strophioblachia, and its unique species designation, fimbricalyx.

What are the pharmacological activities of Strophioblachia fimbricalyx?

This plant has antibacterial, anticardiac hypertrophic inhibitory, antioxidant, antiplasmodial, and cytotoxic pharmacological activities. Its antioxidant properties help neutralize free radicals to prevent cellular damage within biological systems. The extract demonstrates significant antibacterial capabilities by inhibiting the growth of various pathogenic microorganisms. Additionally, it exhibits antiplasmodial effects which are essential for combating malaria parasites. Research also indicates that the plant possesses cytotoxic properties and can function as an anticardiac hypertrophic inhibitory agent to manage heart tissue enlargement.

According to a study published by "Tropical biomedicine", the hexane fraction from the root of Strophioblachia fimbricalyx Boerl. was purified to isolate 3-acetylaleuritolic acid, trigonostemone, and 3,6,9-trimethoxyphenanthropolone. Among these three isolated compounds, only trigonostemone exhibited antibacterial activity against Gram positive bacteria. It showed inhibitory effects on MSSA DMST 2933, MRSA DMST 20651, and Bacillus cereus ATCC 11778 with MIC/MBC values of 12.5/25.0, 6.25/6.25, and 6.25/6.25 mg/mL, respectively. Trigonostemone demonstrated bacteriostatic activity against S. aureus (MSSA) DMST 2933 at 2 × MIC and bacteriocidal activity against B. cereus ATCC 11778 at 2 × MIC after 4 h. These results suggest that trigonostemone from the root of S. fimbricalyx has potential as a natural antibacterial compound.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds strophioblachiins A-C, strophioblachiins D-K, trigonostemone, and 9-O-demethyltrigonostemone. These specialized phytochemicals are categorized as unique secondary metabolites found within the species. The strophioblachiin group consists of several distinct chemical derivatives that characterize the plant's profile. Furthermore, the presence of trigonostemone and its demethylated variant, 9-O-demethyltrigonostemone, provides specific biological significance. These specific constituents are essential for understanding the plant's chemical composition and potential therapeutic applications.

According to a study published by "Journal of natural products", the roots of Strophioblachia fimbricalyx contain the known phenanthrenone trigonosteomone (1), a new phenanthrenone named 9-O-demethyltrigonostemone (2), and two new phenanthropolones, 3,6,9-trimethoxyphenanthropolone (3) and 4,6,9-trimethoxyphenanthropolone (4). Compound 2 demonstrated cytotoxicity against NCI-H187, KB, and MCF7 cancer cells with IC50 values of 0.8, 0.8, and 2.9 microg/mL, respectively. Additionally, compounds 2 and 3 displayed antiplasmodial activity against the Plasmodium falciparum (K1, resistant strain) with IC50 values of 2.7 and 3.2 microg/mL. Among the compounds 1-4, only compound 2 showed any discernible antioxidant activity toward DPPH radicals.
