10 Possible Side Effects Of Aristolochia Rotunda

Aristolochia rotunda has some side effects when used improperly, such as kidney damage, nausea, and vomiting.

These side effects can occur due to the high levels of aristolochic acid in the herb. Prolonged exposure to this toxic compound can lead to chronic kidney disease, affecting daily activities and increasing the risk of cardiovascular diseases and even death.

This can further worsen the quality of life for individuals who already have kidney-related health issues.

1. Causes kidney damage

Aristolochia rotunda causes kidney damage due to its toxic compounds, aristolochic acid and aristolochic acid-N-oxide.

These substances can bind to the kidneys' tubular cells, leading to inflammation and damage. Prolonged exposure to these toxins may cause scarring and chronic kidney disease, potentially progressing to end-stage renal failure.

The exact mechanisms underlying this nephrotoxicity are not fully understood, but it is thought to involve a combination of oxidative stress, cellular injury, and immune system activation.

2. Triggers liver inflammation

Aristolochia rotunda triggers liver inflammation due to its high content of aristolochic acids.

These toxic compounds can cause damage to liver cells and lead to inflammation and scarring. The liver's attempts to repair the damage can result in the formation of fibrous tissue, which can impede normal liver function.

Long-term exposure to aristolochic acids has been linked to permanent liver damage and an increased risk of liver cancer.

5. Provokes allergic reactions

Aristolochia rotunda provokes allergic reactions due to its content of aristolochic acid and other alkaloids.

These compounds can trigger an immune response in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as skin rashes, itching, hives, and respiratory issues like asthma or bronchitis.

The exact mechanism behind this reaction is not fully understood, but it is thought that the body's natural defense against these foreign substances causes an overactive immune response, resulting in allergic symptoms.

6. Weakens immune system

Aristolochia rotunda weakens immune system due to its ability to suppress the activity of natural killer cells and T-cells in the body.

The plant's bioactive compounds, particularly aristolochic acids, are responsible for this immunosuppressive effect, which can make individuals more susceptible to infections and diseases.

Regular consumption of Aristolochia rotunda or exposure to its extracts has been linked to a compromised immune system.

7. Inhibits platelet function

Aristolochia rotunda inhibits platelet function due to its ability to suppress the production of thromboxane A2, a potent stimulator of platelet aggregation.

This inhibition can lead to prolonged bleeding times and increased risk of hemorrhage. The compound aristolochic acid present in Aristolochia rotunda is responsible for this effect by binding to platelets and interfering with their ability to aggregate and form clots.

This mechanism can result in a range of symptoms, from mild to severe, including easy bruising, petechiae, and epistaxis.

8. Disrupts menstrual cycle

Aristolochia rotunda disrupts menstrual cycle due to its ability to interact with estrogen levels in the body.

The plant contains aristolochic acid, a compound that has been shown to mimic the effects of estrogen, which can alter the normal balance of hormones.

This can lead to irregular or absent menstrual periods, as well as other hormonal imbalances, in some individuals who consume Aristolochia rotunda.

9. Causes skin rashes

Aristolochia rotunda causes skin rashes due to its high concentration of aristolochic acids.

These toxic compounds can penetrate the skin and trigger an allergic response, leading to symptoms such as redness, itching, and inflammation.

The exact mechanism by which these acids cause skin rashes is not fully understood, but it is believed that they bind to skin proteins, triggering a cascade of immune responses that ultimately result in the characteristic rash associated with Aristolochia rotunda use.

10. Interferes with medication absorption

Aristolochia rotunda interferes with medication absorption by binding to bile acids in the gut, thereby reducing the amount of active ingredients available for absorption.

This interaction can lead to decreased efficacy or increased side effects of medications being taken concomitantly.

The herb's saponins and alkaloids are thought to be responsible for this interference, which can impact the absorption of various classes of drugs, including antibiotics, antifungals, and blood thinners.
