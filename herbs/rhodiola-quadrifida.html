Rhodiola quadrifida

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhodiola quadrifida has a perennial, rhizomatous growth habit characterized by fleshy, creeping roots and small, ovate leaves that grow in dense, basal clusters. The leaves are typically succulent, displaying a smooth texture and a slightly fleshy consistency to aid in water storage. Flowering stems emerge from the base, bearing small, inconspicuous blossoms that are often arranged in compact cymes. The plant's reproductive structures include specialized carpels that can split into multiple segments, a feature reflected in its specific epithet. This morphology allows the species to thrive in the harsh, rocky environments of high-altitude alpine regions.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau in China. It specifically inhabits alpine meadows and rocky slopes within the mountainous provinces of Tibet and Qinghai. The species thrives in cold, harsh environments characterized by thin soils and intense solar radiation. Its distribution is strictly limited to these specific montane ecosystems found at significant elevations.       What are the herbal preparations of Rhodiola quadrifida?

      Rhodiola quadrifidida is prepared as a concentrated aqueous extract, a dried powder from the rhizomes, or a tincture infused in alcohol.
