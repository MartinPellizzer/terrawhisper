What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rubus alceifolius has woody, arching canes that are densely covered in sharp prickles and feature trifoliate leaves with deeply lobed, serrated leaflets. The leaf blades exhibit a distinct texture characterized by prominent venation and a slightly rugose surface. Small, white to pale pink flowers emerge from the terminal ends of the branches, typically possessing five petals and numerous stamens. The plant produces aggregate fruits that transition from green to a dark red or black color upon ripening. These morphological traits are characteristic of many species within the Rubus genus, adapted for protection and efficient reproduction.
