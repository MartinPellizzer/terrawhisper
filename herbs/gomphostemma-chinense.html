Gomphostemma chinense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gomphostemma chinense has climbing perennial stems that reach several meters in length, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with entire margins and acute apices. The foliage typically displays a bright green color and a smooth texture, providing a lush appearance to the climbing vine. Its inflorescences consist of small, tubular flowers that emerge in axillary clusters. These blossoms are characterized by a pale yellowish or greenish-white hue and possess a distinct corolla structure typical of the Menispermaceae family. The plant also develops small, fleshy fruits that serve as a key reproductive feature.
