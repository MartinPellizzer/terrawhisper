Aralia kansuensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Aralia kansuensis?

Aralia kansuenensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is positioned in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Apiales. This plant is a member of the family Araliaceae, specifically situated within the genus Aralia, and is identified by the species name kansuenensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aralia kansuenensis has compound leaves that are typically tripinnate and can reach lengths of up to 60 centimeters, featuring small, ovate leaflets with serrated margins.

The plant grows as a woody shrub or small tree with a slender, upright stem and a branched architecture. Its inflorescence consists of large, terminal umbels containing numerous small, white flowers that attract various pollinators. The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that transitions from green to a dark color upon ripening.

This species is characterized by its delicate foliage and spreading growth habit in its natural habitat.
