Anchusa undulata subsp. atlantica

Anchusa undulata subsp.

atlantica, also known as the Atlantic bugloss or blue thistle, is a subspecies of flowering plant in the borage family (Boraginaceae) native to the Iberian Peninsula and northwestern Africa.

Traditionally used in herbal medicine, the plant is valued for its antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, and antispasmodic properties, which can be utilized to treat various health issues.

The health benefits of Anchusa undulata subsp.

atlantica include its ability to reduce inflammation, alleviate respiratory issues such as bronchitis, and soothe digestive problems like diarrhea and irritable bowel syndrome.

Herbal preparations of the plant typically involve infusions, decoctions, or tinctures, which can be administered orally or applied topically to the affected area.
