10 Medicinal Constituents Of Sambucus Ebulus (Dwarf Elder)

Sambucus ebulus has active constituents such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins.

These compounds contribute to its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial activities. The flavonoids and phenolic acids have been shown to alleviate symptoms of allergies, such as itching and inflammation, while the saponins may help improve cardiovascular health by reducing cholesterol levels and blood pressure.

Overall, these constituents can help improve people's lives by providing relief from chronic conditions like hay fever and potentially lowering the risk of heart disease.

1. Flavonoids

Sambucus ebulus flavonoids is a group of bioactive compounds that are abundant in its leaves and stems.

These flavonoids have been extensively studied for their potential health benefits, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial activities.

They have been shown to exhibit potent inhibitory effects on various enzymes, as well as protect against oxidative stress and damage caused by free radicals.

2. Quercetin

Sambucus ebulus quercetin is a type of flavonoid found in its berries.

It has been traditionally used to treat various health issues, including allergies, inflammation, and cancer. Quercetin's antioxidant properties help to reduce oxidative stress and protect against cell damage.

Its anti-inflammatory effects have also been shown to alleviate symptoms of respiratory diseases such as bronchitis and asthma.

3. Kaempferol

Sambucus ebulus kaempferol is a type of flavonoid compound that is abundant in the plant.

Kaempferol has been extensively studied for its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which may contribute to its potential health benefits.

It has been shown to have protective effects against oxidative stress, cell damage, and inflammation, making it a promising candidate for the prevention or treatment of various diseases, including cardiovascular disease, cancer, and neurodegenerative disorders.

4. Isoquercetin

Sambucus ebulus isoquercetin is a bioflavonoid compound extracted from its fruit and leaves.

It has been found to possess various pharmacological activities, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties.

Isoquercetin has been shown to exhibit protective effects against oxidative stress, cardiovascular disease, and cancer, as well as having potential therapeutic benefits for respiratory and gastrointestinal disorders.

5. Rutin

Sambucus ebulus rutin is a bioflavonoid compound extracted from the bark and leaves of the European elder tree.

It has been used traditionally in folk medicine for its anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory properties, and has been shown to have potential health benefits including improving blood vessel health, reducing swelling, and alleviating symptoms of hemorrhoids and varicose veins.

6. Catechins

9. Ellagic acid

Sambucus ebulus ellagic acid is a polyphenolic compound found in its berries and leaves.
