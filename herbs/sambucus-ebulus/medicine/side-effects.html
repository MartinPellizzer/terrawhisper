10 Possible Side Effects Of Sambucus Ebulus

Sambucus ebulus has some side effects when used improperly, such as nausea, dizziness, and allergic reactions.

These side effects can occur when the herb is ingested in large quantities, over an extended period, or without proper identification of the correct species. For instance, severe nausea can hinder a person's ability to work or maintain a healthy diet, exacerbating existing health issues.

In extreme cases, anaphylaxis can lead to life-threatening complications.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Sambucus ebulus causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

The sap, leaves, and unripe fruits of the plant contain allergenic compounds like saponins and flavonoids, which can trigger an immune response in susceptible people.

These allergens can cause symptoms such as skin irritation, hives, and respiratory issues, including congestion, coughing, and wheezing.

2. Induces gastrointestinal upset

Sambucus ebulus induces gastrointestinal upset.

This is due to its ability to stimulate digestion and bowel movements, which can lead to loose stools, diarrhea, bloating, and stomach discomfort in some individuals. Additionally, the herb's saponins may irritate the mucous membranes of the digestive tract, further contributing to gastrointestinal upset.

As a result, users may experience unpleasant symptoms that can impact their daily activities and quality of life.

3. Produces vomiting episodes

Sambucus ebulus produces vomiting episodes due to its potent saponins and alkaloids, which can irritate the digestive system.

These compounds may stimulate an increase in stomach contractions, leading to nausea and vomiting.

Additionally, the plant's bitter flavor and astringent properties can further exacerbate gastrointestinal irritation, resulting in uncomfortable symptoms for some individuals.

4. Increases blood pressure

Sambucus ebulus increases blood pressure due to its ability to constrict blood vessels and stimulate the sympathetic nervous system.

This is caused by the presence of flavonoids and other bioactive compounds in the plant, which can cause a rise in blood pressure. Additionally, the plant's vasoconstrictive properties may also contribute to this effect.

As a result, individuals taking Sambucus ebulus may experience an increase in their blood pressure levels.

5. Triggers bronchospasm attacks

Sambucus ebulus triggers bronchospasm attacks due to its inherent allergenic and irritant properties.

The plant's sap, leaves, and fruits contain compounds that can stimulate the release of histamine, a chemical mediator involved in allergic reactions, leading to constriction of airways and increased mucus production, exacerbating respiratory symptoms such as wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.

6. Worsens asthma symptoms

Sambucus ebulus worsens asthma symptoms by triggering an allergic reaction in some individuals.

This is due to the plant's ability to release histamine and other chemical mediators that stimulate the immune system, leading to increased inflammation and constriction of airways.

As a result, people with pre-existing asthma may experience exacerbated symptoms, including wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.

7. Disrupts menstrual cycles

Sambucus ebulus disrupts menstrual cycles due to its ability to stimulate uterine contractions and alter hormone levels.

The plant's flavonoids and terpenoids can affect the body's natural balance of estrogen and progesterone, leading to changes in menstruation patterns or even cessation of menstruation altogether.

This disruption can occur as a result of regular consumption of Sambucus ebulus supplements or teas made from its leaves and flowers.

8. Increases heart rate

Sambucus ebulus increases heart rate due to its stimulating properties that can affect the cardiovascular system.

The saponins and alkaloids present in the plant are responsible for this action, causing an increase in cardiac output and blood pressure. This is because they stimulate the sympathetic nervous system, leading to a rapid heartbeat and increased circulation.

As a result, individuals using Sambucus ebulus may experience palpitations or tachycardia as their heart rate increases.

9. Activates thyroid gland

Sambucus ebulus activates thyroid gland, potentially causing an increased production of thyroxine (T4) and triiodothyronine (T3).

This is due to the presence of flavonoids, particularly quercetin, which have been shown to stimulate the activity of thyroid hormone-producing cells in the thyroid gland.

As a result, individuals may experience symptoms such as rapid heartbeat, weight loss, and heat intolerance.

10. Causes skin rashes

Sambucus ebulus causes skin rashes in some individuals due to its allergenic properties.

The plant's sap and leaves contain allergens that can trigger an immune response, leading to the development of itchy, red, and blistering rashes on the skin.

This reaction is often caused by contact dermatitis, which occurs when the skin comes into contact with the allergenic substances present in Sambucus ebulus.
