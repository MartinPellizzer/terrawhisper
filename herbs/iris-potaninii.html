Iris potaninii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Iris potaninii has slender, sword-shaped leaves that emerge from a creeping rhizome and produce flowers characterized by violet-blue petals with prominent yellow markings on the falls. The plant typically reaches a height of approximately twenty to thirty centimeters, featuring narrow, erect stems that support the blooms. Each flower displays a delicate structure with three upright standards and three drooping falls decorated with intricate nectar guides. The rhizomes are relatively short and grow horizontally through the soil to facilitate spreading. This species is distinguished by its compact growth habit and the specific coloration patterns found on its delicate floral segments.
