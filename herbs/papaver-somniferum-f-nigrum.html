Papaver somniferum f. nigrum

Papaver somniferum f. nigrum, also known as the black poppy, is a variant of the opium poppy plant that is primarily found in Eastern Europe and Asia, and is characterized by its dark, almost black seeds.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its analgesic, antispasmodic, and anti-inflammatory properties, which have been attributed to its alkaloid content, including thebaine and papaverine.

The medicinal properties of Papaver somniferum f. nigrum have been used to treat a variety of health issues, including pain, insomnia, and digestive problems, as well as to reduce inflammation and improve respiratory function.

Papaver somniferum f. nigrum can be prepared in various herbal forms, including teas, tinctures, and topical creams, which are often used to alleviate symptoms and promote overall well-being.
