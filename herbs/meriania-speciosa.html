Possible Side Effects of Meriania speciosa

Meriania speciosa can have side effects if used improperly, such as gastrointestinal problems, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, due to its high content of iridoid glycosides.

Prolonged use or excessive consumption of the plant can also lead to cardiovascular issues, such as palpitations, tremors, and arrhythmias, caused by its effect on the heart's electrical conduction system.

In rare cases, Meriania speciosa may cause allergic reactions, including hives, itching, and respiratory distress, particularly in individuals with pre-existing allergies or sensitivities.

Additionally, the plant may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and beta blockers, potentially exacerbating their side effects or reducing their efficacy.
