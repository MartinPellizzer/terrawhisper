Common Hawthorn (Crataegus Laevigata Var. Mathei)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Crataegus laevigata var. mathei.

Crataegus laevigata var. mathei, commonly known as the matthew's hawthorn, is a lesser-known variety of hawthorn that has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its cardiovascular benefits. The plant contains bioactive compounds such as flavonoids and oligomeric proanthocyanidins, which are believed to support heart health by improving blood flow and reducing oxidative stress. Historically, it has been used to treat conditions such as hypertension, arrhythmia, and mild heart failure due to its potential to strengthen cardiac muscle function. While more research is needed to confirm its efficacy, some studies suggest that extracts from this variety may have similar therapeutic effects to those of the more commonly studied Crataegus monogyna. As a result, it is gaining attention as a potential alternative or complementary treatment in herbal medicine for cardiovascular support.
