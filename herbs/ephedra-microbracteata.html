Small-Bract Ephedra (Ephedra Microbracteata)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Ephedra microbracteata.

Small-bract ephedra, scientifically known as Ephedra microbracteata, is a rare medicinal herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia and parts of China. It is traditionally regarded as an adaptogen and has been used for centuries in traditional Chinese medicine for its stimulating and therapeutic properties. The primary benefits of this plant include its ability to alleviate respiratory conditions, enhance physical endurance, and support metabolic functions due to its alkaloid content, particularly ephedrine. Historically, it has been employed in herbal remedies for colds, asthma, and fatigue, and it holds cultural significance in traditional healing practices. In modern wellness contexts, it is used as a natural supplement for energy and respiratory support, though its use is regulated due to potential side effects, making it a unique and historically rich botanical with both traditional and contemporary applications.
