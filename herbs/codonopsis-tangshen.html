Tangshen

Codonopsis tangshen

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Tangshen, or Codonopsis tangshen, is a perennial herb in the family Campanulaceae. Native to China, it is known by regional names like Dang Shen and is characterized by its fleshy roots, lanceolate leaves, and bell-shaped flowers. It is also referred to as Codonopsis pilosula in some herbal texts.

Active Compounds

Tangshen contains key active compounds such as glycosides and flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to support immune function and enhance vitality, making them central to its traditional use in herbal medicine.

  • Glycoside
  • Flavonoid

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for respiratory and digestive ailments due to its tonic properties.

Powder: Used for digestive issues and to strengthen the spleen and stomach.

Capsule: Used for general tonification and to support energy levels.

Safety Profile

Tangshen is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated in cases of heat syndrome with irritability and insomnia. Prolonged use may lead to toxicity, so it should be used under professional guidance.
