Tangshen

Codonopsis tangshen

Tangshen (Codonopsis tangshen) is a traditional Chinese medicinal plant known for its use in enhancing vitality and supporting respiratory and digestive health. It is primarily used to improve conditions such as fatigue, shortness of breath, and digestive weakness.

Taxonomy

Tangshen (Codonopsis tangshen) is a species within the genus Codonopsis of the family Campanulaceae, belonging to the order Campanulales, class Liliopsida, division Magnoliophyta, and kingdom Plantae.

Morphology

Tangshen (Codonopsis tangshen) is a perennial herb characterized by its stout, fleshy rhizome, which is typically yellowish-white to reddish-brown in color, and its erect, branched stems that bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with hairy surfaces.

Distribution

Tangshen (Codonopsis tangshen) is native to specific regions in China, primarily found in the provinces of Hebei, Shanxi, and Shaanxi, with notable cultivation in the mountainous areas of northern China.

Traditional Uses

Tangshen (Codonopsis tangshen) has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine to tonify the spleen and lungs, enhance qi, and promote vitality, particularly in cases of fatigue, weakness, and respiratory insufficiency.

Tangshen (Codonopsis tangshen) has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine for its purported ability to tonify the spleen and lungs, enhance vitality, and support immune function.
