Dillenia pentagyna

What's the taxonomical classification of Dillenia pentagyna?

Dillenia pentagyna belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized under Equisetopsida, which further places it in the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Dilleniales and is a member of the family Dilleniaceae. Within this family, it is nested under the genus Dillenia, specifically identifying as the species pentagyna.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Dillenia pentagyna contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including tannins, flavonoids, saponins, and glycosides that provide its therapeutic properties. The plant's leaves and bark are frequently utilized in traditional medicine to treat inflammatory conditions and skin ailments. Research indicates that the presence of phenolic compounds contributes to its significant antioxidant and antimicrobial activities. These chemical constituents work together to help mitigate oxidative stress within biological systems. Local practitioners often prepare decoctions from these parts to address gastrointestinal issues and fever.

What are the therapeutic actions of Dillenia pentagyna?

Dillenia pentagyna has significant therapeutic properties including antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant activities derived from its bioactive phytochemicals. The plant's extracts are frequently utilized in traditional medicine to treat various ailments such as skin infections and digestive issues. Studies suggest that its chemical constituents help neutralize free radicals, which supports cellular health and reduces oxidative stress. Furthermore, the medicinal components work to inhibit the growth of certain pathogenic bacteria and fungi. These diverse biological actions make the species a subject of interest for developing new natural pharmacological treatments.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including skin infections, wounds, digestive issues, and inflammation. The leaves and bark are frequently applied topically to soothe skin irritations and promote the healing of physical injuries. In traditional medicine, different parts of the plant are prepared to address internal problems such as stomach discomfort or dysentery. Some practitioners also utilize its properties to manage fever and other systemic inflammatory conditions. These medicinal applications highlight the plant's role in diverse herbal healing practices.
