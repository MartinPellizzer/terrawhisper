Dillenia pentagyna

What's the taxonomical classification of Dillenia pentagyna?

Dillenia pentagyna belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized under Equisetopsida, which further places it in the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Dilleniales and is a member of the family Dilleniaceae. Within this family, it is nested under the genus Dillenia, specifically identifying as the species pentagyna.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dillenia pentagyra has large, coriaceous leaves with prominent venation, five-petaled white flowers, and distinctive fleshy, globose fruits that are often red or yellow. The leaves are typically arranged alternately on the branches and possess an obovate to elliptic shape with entire or slightly wavy margins. Its inflorescences emerge from the leaf axils, showcasing numerous stamens that surround the central carpels. The most striking feature is the fruit, which develops from an aggregate of many small, fleshy drupes clustered together. These fruits are often covered in fine hairs and serve as a significant food source for local fauna.

According to a study published by "Spectrochimica acta. Part A, Molecular and biomolecular spectroscopy", the ethanolic extract of Dillenia pentagyna is enriched with phenolics, flavonoids, tannin, saponin, alkaloid, and terpenoids. The bark and leaves contained higher amounts of phenolic compounds, with the bark also unveiling a higher amount of total flavonoid compounds. Using DPPH, the ethanolic extract of the bark and leaves showed free radical scavenging inhibition percentages of 90.58 ± 1.89% and 76.46 ± 1.58%, respectively, compared to standard ascorbic acid at 10 μg/mL. Furthermore, the half-maximal inhibitory concentration (IC50) values for the bark and leaves were 5.64 and 6.54 μg/mL, respectively.
