What's the taxonomical classification of Canna flaccida?

Canna flaccida belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Zingiberales. This plant is a representative of the family Cannaceae and is situated within the genus Canna, ultimately defined by its specific species designation, flaccida.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Canna flaccida has erect, rhizomatous stems that typically reach heights of 0.5 to 1.5 meters and support large, oblong, bright green leaves with prominent parallel venation. The foliage is characterized by its smooth texture and long petioles that emerge directly from the underground rhizome. Its inflorescences consist of bright yellow flowers that are zygomorphic and arranged in terminal racemes. Each individual flower features large, petal-like staminodes that provide a striking visual display. The plant's overall structure is robust and herbaceous, adapted for thriving in moist or wetland environments.
