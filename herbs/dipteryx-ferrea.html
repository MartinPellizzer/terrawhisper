What is Dipteryx ferrea?

Dipteryx ferrea is a species of flowering plant in the family Fabaceae, native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a deciduous tree that grows up to 15 meters in height, with a broad, rounded crown and a straight, columnar trunk that reaches up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of 2-4 pairs of pinnae, each with 3-5 pairs of leaflets that are elliptical in shape and have a length of 2-4 centimeters. The flowers are yellow, fragrant, and grow in clusters, while the fruit is a pod that is flat and elliptical in shape, measuring 5-10 centimeters in length and containing 2-4 seeds.

Dipteryx ferrea is also known as "jatobá" or "Brazilian ironwood".
