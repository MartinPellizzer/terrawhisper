Clematis courtoisii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clematis courtoisii has opposite, simple, or trifoliate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. The plant exhibits a climbing growth habit facilitated by its specialized, twining petioles that wrap around surrounding vegetation. Its flowers are characterized by four to six petal-like sepals that range in color from creamy white to pale pink. The reproductive structures consist of numerous stamens that form a central cluster within the perianth. As the plant matures, it produces small, hairy achenes that are attached to feathery styles to assist in wind dispersal.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Clematis courtoisii can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and allergic reactions if ingested or touched. The plant contains protoanemonin, a chemical compound that acts as a potent irritant to human tissue. Contact with the leaves or stems often results in redness, itching, or blistering on the skin. If swallowed, the toxins can lead to severe vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea. Individuals with known sensitivities to the Clematis genus should exercise extreme caution around this species.
