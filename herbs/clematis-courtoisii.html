Clematis courtoisii

What's the taxonomical classification of Clematis courtoisii?

Clematis courtoisii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is situated under the order Ranunculales. It is further categorized into the family Ranunculaceae and falls under the genus Clematis, ultimately reaching its specific identification at the species level as courtoisii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clematis courtoisii has opposite, simple, or trifoliate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape with entire or slightly serrated margins.

The plant exhibits a climbing growth habit facilitated by its specialized, twining petioles that wrap around surrounding vegetation. Its flowers are characterized by four to six petal-like sepals that range in color from creamy white to pale pink. The reproductive structures consist of numerous stamens that form a central cluster within the perianth.

As the plant matures, it produces small, hairy achenes that are attached to feathery styles to assist in wind dispersal.

      Clematis courtoisii is prepared primarily as a liquid tincture through alcohol extraction or as a dried herbal infusion using hot water. These liquid extracts are often administered in small, measured doses to ensure safety and potency. The dried leaves and flowering parts can also be steeped into a tea for traditional use.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Clematis courtoisii can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and allergic reactions if ingested or touched. The plant contains protoanemonin, a chemical compound that acts as a potent irritant to human tissue. Contact with the leaves or stems often results in redness, itching, or blistering on the skin. If swallowed, the toxins can lead to severe vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea. Individuals with known sensitivities to the Clematis genus should exercise extreme caution around this species.
