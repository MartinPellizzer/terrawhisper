Berchemia kulingensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Berchemia kulingenensis has woody stems, lanceolate to elliptical leaves with entire or slightly serrated margins, and small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into fleshy drupes. The plant typically features a branched architecture with leaves arranged in an alternate pattern along the branches. The foliage often displays a distinct texture and coloration that varies depending on the maturity of the leaf. Its reproductive structures consist of small blossoms that eventually transform into small, berry-like fruits used for seed dispersal. These morphological traits are characteristic of many members within the Rhamnaceae family.
