Cinnamon (Cinnamomum aromaticum)

Cinnamon (Cinnamomum aromaticum) is a member of the Lauraceae family, native to Sri Lanka, India, and Indonesia. Traditionally, its bark, leaves, and resin have been used for decoctions, powders, and infusions.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, tonic, and carminative actions, and has a long history of use in traditional chinese medicine, ayurvedic medicine, and japanese kampo medicine.

Broad leaves with upper surface dark green and lower surface light green, featuring prominent stomatal bands.
Flowers
Bilaterally symmetric flowers with two prominent white petals and one smaller yellow petal, arranged in solitary clusters at the base of the leaf axils.
Stems
Erect, woody, aromatic, with a smooth surface, and a branching pattern of opposite or alternate leaves.
