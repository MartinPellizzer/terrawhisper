Side Effects of cinnamomum aromaticum

Cinnamomum aromaticum results in allergic reactions due to the presence of compounds such as cinnamaldehyde and essential oils, which can trigger immune responses in sensitive individuals.

The herb can cause stomach upset because its strong flavor and essential oils may irritate the gastrointestinal tract, leading to nausea, vomiting, or diarrhea.

Cinnamomum aromaticum triggers skin irritation when applied topically, as the volatile oils can cause contact dermatitis or allergic contact dermatitis in some people.

The herb results in dry mouth because its aromatic compounds can stimulate saliva production initially, but excessive use or sensitivity may lead to reduced saliva secretion over time.
