Rosa rubiginosa var. histrix

Rosa rubiginosa var. histrix, also known as the fragrant rose or sweetbriar, is a deciduous shrub in the rose family that is native to Europe and Western Asia.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties, which can be attributed to its high content of flavonoids and phenolic acids.

Rosa rubiginosa var. histrix has been used to treat a variety of health issues, including digestive problems, respiratory issues, and skin conditions, and has also been used as a natural remedy for menstrual cramps and menopausal symptoms.

Herbal preparations of Rosa rubiginosa var. histrix include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which can be used to harness its medicinal properties and promote overall well-being.
