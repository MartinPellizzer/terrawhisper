Potentilla lineata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Potentiella lineata has a perennial growth habit characterized by stoloniferous stems that produce small, five-petaled yellow flowers and deeply lobed, palmately veined leaves. The leaves are typically trifoliate or five-foliate with serrated margins and a distinctively lined or striated appearance on the underside. Its stems often creep along the ground via runners, allowing the plant to form dense mats in its natural habitat. The flowers are relatively small and possess numerous stamens surrounding a central cluster of pistils. These morphological features collectively help the species adapt to its specific ecological niche.
