Cornus chinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Cornus chinensis?

Cornus chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it falls under the order Cornales and the family Cornaceae. Finally, the plant is placed within the genus Cornus, with its specific identification being the species chinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cornus chinensis has opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that measure approximately 5 to 15 centimeters in length with prominent pinnate venation and entire margins. The shrub typically reaches heights of two to five meters, characterized by multi-stemmed growth and grayish-brown bark. Small, white, four-petaled flowers emerge in dense cymes during the spring season. Following pollination, the plant produces small, fleshy drupes that transition from green to a dark blue or black color when mature. These morphological features allow the species to thrive in various temperate forest understories.
