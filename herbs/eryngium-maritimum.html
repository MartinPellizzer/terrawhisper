Eryngium Maritimum Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Eryngium maritimum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.

The herb contains bioactive constituents such as flavonoids, tannins, and essential oils, which contribute to its therapeutic actions.

Side Effects of eryngium maritimum

Eryngium maritimum triggers allergic reactions in some individuals, which can manifest as sneezing, runny nose, or even asthma-like symptoms.

The herb may also cause skin irritation, leading to redness, itching, or a burning sensation upon contact. Those who handle or consume the plant might develop skin rashes, which can be itchy and uncomfortable.

In more severe cases, Eryngium maritimum can lead to nausea, causing vomiting or a feeling of queasiness. Additionally, the herb has been associated with respiratory issues such as coughing, shortness of breath, or throat irritation, which can be particularly dangerous for individuals with pre-existing respiratory conditions.

Due to these potential side effects, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using Eryngium maritimum, especially for those with known allergies or sensitivities.
