Scleropyrum wallichianum

What's the taxonomical classification of Scleropyrum wallichianum?

Scleropyrum wallichianum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Santalales. This plant is further categorized into the family Santalaceae, which contains the genus Scleropyrum, ultimately defining its specific identity as the species wallichianum.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

These chemical constituents are primarily concentrated within the leaves, bark, and roots of the plant. Researchers have identified specific phytochemical profiles that suggest significant antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory activities. The presence of these complex organic compounds supports the plant's traditional use in treating various ailments in folk medicine. Scleropyrum wallichianum has potent medicinal properties primarily utilized for treating skin diseases, wounds, and inflammatory conditions through its bioactive phytochemical constituents. The plant extracts are frequently applied topically to accelerate the healing process of cutaneous lesions and infections. Its chemical profile contains various secondary metabolites that contribute to significant antimicrobial and antioxidant activities. These biological actions help reduce swelling and alleviate pain in affected tissues. Researchers continue to study its specific compounds to better understand their systemic therapeutic potential.

What diseases this plant treats?
