What is Chionographis lutea?

Chionographis lutea is a perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Japan, belonging to the family Melanthiaceae.

It features linear, glabrous leaves that are 1-2 mm wide and 10-30 cm long, arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces a single, terminal inflorescence with 5-10 yellow, bell-shaped flowers that are 3-4 mm long and 5-6 mm wide, with a 3-lobed lip and 6 stamens. The flowers are clustered at the tip of a 10-20 cm tall, glabrous stem, emerging from the leaf rosette in late spring.

Chionographis lutea typically grows in shaded, moist areas with humus-rich soil.
