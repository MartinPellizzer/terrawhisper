What's the taxonomical classification of Cleidion brevipetiolatum?

Cleidion brevipetiolatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down its taxonomic hierarchy, it resides in the order Malpighiales and is a member of the family Euphorbiaceae. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Cleidion, and its unique species designation, brevipetiolatum.

What are the pharmacological activities of Cleidion brevipetiolatum?

This plant has anti-inflammatory pharmacological activities that suggest potential for treating various inflammatory conditions. Researchers have investigated its chemical constituents to understand how these bioactive compounds interact with biological pathways. Studies indicate that extracts from Cleidioon brevipetiolatum can effectively reduce swelling and pain in experimental models. These therapeutic effects are often linked to the presence of specific phytochemicals such as flavonoids or terpenoids. Further investigation into these mechanisms may lead to the development of new medicinal treatments derived from the species.

As discussed in a study, Cleidion brevipetiolatum contains six new ellagitannins, brevipetins B-G (5 and 7-11), and a new phenolic glucoside, brevipetin A (4). Compounds 5-11 exhibited NO inhibitory effects with IC50 values ranging from 1.9-8.2 μM, with compound 9 showing the most potent inhibitory effect at an IC50 of 1.9 μM. An in vivo anti-inflammatory assessment demonstrated that compound 9 exerts therapeutic effects in both carrageenan-induced rat paw edema and collagen-induced arthritis models at 50 mg/kg oral administration. Furthermore, compound 9 dose-dependently suppressed enhanced protein and mRNA expression levels of iNOS and COX-2 in LPS-stimulated RAW 264.7 cells. This compound also suppressed NF-κB activity by inhibiting IκBα phosphorylation and blocking the translocation of p65 from the cytosol to the nucleus.
