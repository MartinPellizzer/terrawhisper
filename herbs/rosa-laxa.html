Rosa laxa

What's the taxonomical classification of Rosa laxa?

Rosa laxa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is classified under the order Rosales. It further falls into the family Rosaceae, which places it within the genus Rosa. Ultimately, its specific biological identity is defined by its species name, laxa.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Rosa laxa contains high concentrations of polyphenols, flavonoids, tannins, and vitamin C within its petals and hips. These bioactive compounds act as potent antioxidants that help neutralize free radicals in the body. The presence of specific tannins provides astringent properties that can assist in soothing skin irritations or digestive issues. Additionally, the organic acids found in the fruit contribute to its overall therapeutic profile. These combined chemical elements support immune function and promote general cellular health.

What are the therapeutic actions of Rosa laxa?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various skin ailments including eczema, psoriasis, and minor inflammatory dermatoses. The medicinal properties of Rosa laxa are primarily attributed to its soothing and anti-inflammatory effects when applied topically. Extracts from the plant can help calm irritated skin and reduce redness caused by external irritants. Some traditional practices also utilize the plant to address superficial wounds or small abrasions. These therapeutic applications focus on restoring the skin's barrier and alleviating discomfort.
