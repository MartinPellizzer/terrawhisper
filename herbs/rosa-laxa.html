From a horticultural perspective, wild rose is a hardy perennial that thrives in temperate climates, requiring minimal care and maintenance to flourish.

From a botanical standpoint, wild rose is characterized by its clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers and edible hips, which are rich in vitamins A and C. With roots tracing back thousands of years to ancient Greek and Roman medicine, wild rose has been a staple in herbalism for centuries, with references to its use found in the works of renowned healers like Dioscorides and Galen.
