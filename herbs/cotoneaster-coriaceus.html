Cotoneaster coriaceus

What's the taxonomical classification of Cotoneaster coriaceus?

Cotoneaster coriaceus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and placed in the order Rosales. This plant is a member of the family Rosaceae, falling under the genus Cotoneaster, with the specific species designation of coriaceus.

      What are the herbal preparations of Cotoneaster coriaceus?

      Cotoneaster coriaceus is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical salve depending on the intended medicinal application. The leaves and stems are often boiled in water to create a concentrated liquid extract used for internal consumption. For external ailments such as skin irritations, the plant material is frequently infused into oils or fats to produce a soothing ointment. Some traditional practices also involve crushing the fresh plant matter to apply directly to wounds as a poultice. These diverse methods allow practitioners to extract the specific bioactive compounds required for different therapeutic needs.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Cotoneaster coriaceus can cause gastrointestinal distress, including nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain, if any part of the plant is ingested. The berries of this species contain cyanogenic glycosides which release toxic cyanide into the body upon consumption. These compounds can lead to more severe symptoms such as dizziness, headaches, and rapid heart rate. Additionally, contact with the plant may cause localized skin irritation or allergic reactions in sensitive individuals. It is crucial to keep this plant away from children and pets to prevent accidental poisoning.
