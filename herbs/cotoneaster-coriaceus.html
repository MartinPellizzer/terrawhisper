Cotoneaster coriaceus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cotoneaster coriaceus has small, leathery, elliptic to oblong leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and often paler underneath. The leaf margins are typically entire or slightly serrated, while the texture is notably thick and tough. Its branches are woody and can become somewhat sprawling or arching as the plant matures. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves during the blooming season. These blossoms eventually give way to small, fleshy drupes that change color as they ripen.

      What are the herbal preparations of Cotoneaster coriaceus?

      Cotoneaster coriaceus is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical salve depending on the intended medicinal application. The leaves and stems are often boiled in water to create a concentrated liquid extract used for internal consumption.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Cotoneaster coriaceus can cause gastrointestinal distress, including nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain, if any part of the plant is ingested. The berries of this species contain cyanogenic glycosides which release toxic cyanide into the body upon consumption. These compounds can lead to more severe symptoms such as dizziness, headaches, and rapid heart rate. Additionally, contact with the plant may cause localized skin irritation or allergic reactions in sensitive individuals. It is crucial to keep this plant away from children and pets to prevent accidental poisoning.
