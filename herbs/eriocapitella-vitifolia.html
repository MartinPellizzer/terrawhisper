Eriocapitella vitifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eriocapitella vitifolia has woody, climbing stems with leaves that are distinctly ovate or cordate in shape and possess a finely serrated margin. The foliage typically exhibits a somewhat leathery texture and a dark green color on the upper surface. Small, white, capitate flower heads are produced in clusters along the branches. These inflorescences are characterized by dense, woolly hairs that give the plant a soft appearance. The overall growth habit is vine-like, allowing the plant to scramble through surrounding vegetation.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?
