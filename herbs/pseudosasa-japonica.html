Pseudosasa japonica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pseudosasa japonica has a rhizomatous growth habit characterized by thick, creeping underground stems that produce erect, culm-like shoots. These shoots typically reach heights of up to one meter and possess a cylindrical, segmented structure. The foliage consists of narrow, lanceolate leaves that emerge from the nodes of the stems. These leaves are arranged in a way that allows them to capture light efficiently within forest understories. The plant's overall morphology is adapted for spreading through clonal expansion via its specialized root system.
