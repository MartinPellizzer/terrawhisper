Alnus ferdinandi-coburgii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Alnus ferdinandi-coburgii has an erect growth habit characterized by a single trunk, smooth greyish bark, and ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated margins and prominent veins. The foliage typically displays a glossy dark green upper surface while the underside remains a paler shade. Small, woody, cone-like structures known as strobiles emerge from the branches to house the seeds. The tree often develops a spreading crown as it matures in its natural habitat. These morphological features distinguish it from other members of the alder genus found in similar ecosystems.
