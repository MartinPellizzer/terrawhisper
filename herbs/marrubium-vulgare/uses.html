Common Marrubium (Marrubium vulgare) Uses

Common Marrubium is used to treat respiratory conditions such as cough, bronchitis, and asthma. It also helps with sore throat, tonsillitis, and influenza. The plant supports wound healing and is applied for skin infections and eczema. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties make it effective for these ailments.

Common Marrubium (Marrubium vulgare) Monograph

1. Cough

Common Marrubium is used to alleviate coughs due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps loosen mucus and reduce irritation in the respiratory tract. Traditionally, it has been employed in herbal remedies for both dry and productive coughs. Its effectiveness is supported by historical use in herbal medicine practices.

2. Bronchitis

Common Marrubium has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in cases of bronchitis. Its expectorant properties help loosen mucus, while its anti-inflammatory effects may reduce bronchial irritation. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to ease coughing and promote clearer breathing.

3. Tonsillitis

Common Marrubium (Marrubium vulgare) has been traditionally used to treat tonsillitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce throat inflammation and soothe pain, often prepared as a gargle or tea. Its active compounds, such as marrubiin, support immune function and may inhibit bacterial growth in the throat.

4. Asthma

Common Marrubium has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in managing asthma symptoms. Its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties help loosen mucus and reduce airway swelling. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to ease breathing and alleviate coughing associated with asthma.

5. Influenza

Common Marrubium has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, including symptoms of influenza. It may help reduce fever, soothe sore throats, and ease coughing. Its expectorant properties aid in clearing mucus, while its antimicrobial effects may combat viral infections. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture for symptomatic relief during flu outbreaks.

6. Sore throat

Common Marrubium is used to soothe sore throats due to its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce pain and inflammation in the throat lining. Often prepared as a tea, it provides natural relief for mild to moderate throat irritation. Its mucilage content coats the throat, offering additional comfort.

7. Common cold

Common Marrubium (Marrubium vulgare) is traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of the common cold due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps clear mucus and reduce throat irritation, supporting respiratory comfort during colds. Often prepared as a tea, it is valued for its gentle action and historical use in herbal medicine.

8. Skin infections

Common Marrubium has been traditionally used to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce symptoms like redness, swelling, and bacterial growth on the skin. Often applied as a poultice or infusion, it supports healing in conditions such as eczema and minor wounds. Its natural compounds make it a valuable remedy in herbal medicine for skin health.

9. Wound healing

Common Marrubium has been traditionally used to promote wound healing due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It helps reduce swelling, prevent infection, and accelerate tissue repair. Applications include poultices, salves, or infusions applied directly to wounds. Its ability to stimulate cellular regeneration supports faster recovery.

10. Eczema

Common Marrubium has been traditionally used to treat eczema due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps soothe irritated skin and reduce redness. Its astringent qualities may also help in drying excess moisture, aiding in the healing process. It is often applied topically in herbal preparations for this purpose.
