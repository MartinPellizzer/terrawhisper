Common Marrubium (Marrubium vulgare) Compounds

The primary compounds of Common Marrubium include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and essential oils, contributing to its aromatic profile. Tannins and glycosides provide astringent effects, while phenolic acids and coumarin offer anti-inflammatory benefits. Terpenoids, saponins, alkaloids, and anthocyanins also contribute to its medicinal and therapeutic properties.

Common Marrubium (Marrubium vulgare) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are key active compounds in Common Marrubium (Marrubium vulgare). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds support the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments. Flavonoids enhance the overall therapeutic profile of Marrubium vulgare.

2. Essential oil

The essential oil of Common Marrubium (Marrubium vulgare) contains bioactive components like matricine and harringtonine. These compounds exhibit bronchodilatory and antispasmodic properties, making the essential oil useful in treating respiratory conditions such as asthma and bronchitis.

Tannin is a key active compound in Common Marrubium (Marrubium vulgare). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
