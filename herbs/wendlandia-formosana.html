Wendlandia formosana

What's the taxonomical classification of Wendlandia formosana?

Wendlandiia formosanosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized into the order Gentianaales. It is a member of the family Rubiaceae, falling under the genus Wendlandia, and is specifically identified by the species name formosana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Wendlandia formosana has woody climbing vines characterized by opposite leaves with entire margins and ovate to elliptical shapes.

The plant features small, fragrant flowers that typically appear in axillary clusters. Its stems are often covered in fine hairs, providing a distinct texture to the climbing structure. The reproductive organs consist of stamens and a superior ovary that eventually develops into a fruit.

These morphological traits distinguish the species within its specific botanical family.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Wendlandia formosana can cause potential allergic reactions including skin irritation, redness, and itching upon direct contact with the plant. Some individuals may experience localized dermatitis if the sap or foliage touches sensitive areas of the body. Ingestion of the plant is generally discouraged due to a lack of extensive clinical data regarding its long-term toxicity in humans. While it is used in traditional medicine, the absence of standardized dosing increases the risk of unforeseen adverse systemic effects.
