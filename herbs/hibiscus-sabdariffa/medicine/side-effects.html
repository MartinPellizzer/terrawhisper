10 Possible Side Effects Of Hibiscus Sabdariffa

Hibiscus sabdariffa has some side effects when used improperly, such as allergic reactions, digestive issues, and interactions with certain medications.

These side effects can be caused by consuming excessive amounts or using high-quality extracts, which can be contaminated. For instance, severe allergic reactions can lead to anaphylaxis, a life-threatening condition that can cause respiratory failure and even death.

Additionally, digestive issues can worsen irritable bowel syndrome (IBS) symptoms, causing significant discomfort and impacting daily life.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Hibiscus sabdariffa causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

The potential allergenic properties of this plant may be attributed to the presence of anthocyanins and flavonoids in its extracts, which can trigger an immune response in susceptible people.

Additionally, the high levels of histamine and tyramine found in Hibiscus sabdariffa may also contribute to allergic reactions, such as skin irritation, hives, or respiratory issues.

2. Triggers asthma attacks

Hibiscus sabdariffa triggers asthma attacks due to its high concentration of histamine and other allergenic compounds.

These substances can cause an allergic reaction in individuals with pre-existing asthma, leading to constriction of airways and increased mucus production.

As a result, people with asthma may experience severe symptoms, including wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath, upon consumption of Hibiscus sabdariffa or its extracts.

3. Increases blood pressure

Hibiscus sabdariffa increases blood pressure due to its high concentration of flavonoids and anthocyanins.

These compounds cause blood vessels to constrict, which in turn increases blood pressure. Additionally, Hibiscus sabdariffa may also stimulate the sympathetic nervous system, leading to an increase in heart rate and cardiac output, further contributing to elevated blood pressure.

As a result, individuals with pre-existing hypertension or cardiovascular conditions may experience a rise in their blood pressure when consuming Hibiscus sabdariffa products.

4. Lowers blood sugar

Hibiscus sabdariffa lowers blood sugar by containing flavonoids and anthocyanins that help to increase insulin sensitivity, thereby improving glucose uptake in the body.

This effect is thought to be due to the herb's ability to activate the body's natural antioxidant defenses, which helps to protect against oxidative stress and inflammation that can contribute to insulin resistance and hyperglycemia.

5. Interferes with diabetes medication

Hibiscus sabdariffa interferes with diabetes medication due to its ability to lower blood sugar levels.

The flavonoids and anthocyanins present in the plant have been shown to reduce glucose absorption in the gut and increase insulin sensitivity, which can lead to hypoglycemia (low blood sugar) when combined with diabetes medications.

This may result in severe side effects if not properly managed, making it essential for individuals taking diabetes medication to be aware of potential interactions with Hibiscus sabdariffa.

6. Inhibits warfarin action

Hibiscus sabdariffa inhibits warfarin action due to its ability to stimulate platelet aggregation and increase prothrombin activity.

This herb's flavonoids and polyphenols interact with warfarin, a blood thinner, to decrease its efficacy in preventing blood clots.

The increased platelet activation and coagulation factors can lead to an elevated risk of thrombosis, potentially compromising the therapeutic benefits of warfarin therapy.

7. Enhances caffeine sensitivity

Hibiscus sabdariffa enhances caffeine sensitivity by inhibiting the activity of an enzyme called CYP1A2 in the liver.

This enzyme is responsible for metabolizing caffeine, and when it's inhibited, the body takes longer to process the stimulant.

As a result, even small amounts of caffeine can lead to increased sensitivity and potentially more severe effects such as jitters, anxiety, and insomnia.

8. Increases kidney stone risk

Hibiscus sabdariffa increases kidney stone risk.

This is attributed to its high oxalate content, which can increase urinary excretion of calcium and sodium, potentially leading to the formation of kidney stones. Additionally, the plant's flavonoids and anthocyanins may alter gut bacteria, disrupting normal digestion and increasing the production of uric acid, another common contributor to kidney stone development.

As a result, individuals with a history of kidney stones or other renal issues should exercise caution when using Hibiscus sabdariffa.

9. Interacts with ace inhibitors

Hibiscus sabdariffa interacts with ace inhibitors.

The flavonoids present in Hibiscus sabdariffa may antagonize the effects of ace inhibitors, leading to decreased blood pressure-lowering activity.

This is due to the flavonoids' ability to bind to the same receptors as ace inhibitors, preventing them from having their intended effect on the renin-angiotensin system.

10. Reduces thyroid hormone levels

Hibiscus sabdariffa reduces thyroid hormone levels by inhibiting the production of thyroid-stimulating hormones, which regulates metabolism and energy.

This is due to its high content of flavonoids and anthocyanins, which have been shown to affect the pituitary-thyroid axis and disrupt normal thyroid function.

As a result, individuals with hypothyroidism may experience reduced symptoms, but those without hypothyroidism may need to monitor their thyroid hormone levels closely due to this potential side effect.
