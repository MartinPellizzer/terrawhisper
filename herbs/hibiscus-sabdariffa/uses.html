Roselle (Hibiscus sabdariffa) Uses

Roselle is used to treat hypertension, high cholesterol, and type 2 diabetes due to its hypotensive and lipid-lowering properties. It also aids in managing digestive disorders, constipation, and stomach ulcers by promoting gastrointestinal health. Additionally, it can help with gastritis and diarrhea, showcasing its broad therapeutic applications in herbal medicine.

Roselle (Hibiscus sabdariffa) Monograph

1. Hypertension

Roselle contains anthocyanins and flavonoids that help lower blood pressure by improving vascular function and reducing oxidative stress. Studies show it can modestly reduce systolic and diastolic blood pressure in individuals with hypertension. It is often used in herbal formulations to support cardiovascular health when combined with a balanced diet and lifestyle.

2. High cholesterol

Roselle has been studied for its potential to lower high cholesterol levels. The plant contains compounds like flavonoids and mucilage that may help reduce LDL cholesterol. Some research suggests it supports heart health by improving lipid profiles. It is often used in traditional medicine to manage cholesterol naturally.

3. Diabetes

Roselle has shown potential in managing diabetes due to its high antioxidant content and hypoglycemic properties. Studies suggest it may improve insulin sensitivity and reduce blood sugar levels. Its bioactive compounds help regulate glucose metabolism, making it a promising natural aid for diabetes management. Regular consumption may support overall metabolic health in diabetic patients.

4. Type 2 diabetes

Roselle has been studied for its potential to help manage type 2 diabetes. It contains antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds that may improve insulin sensitivity and reduce blood sugar levels. Research suggests it could support glucose regulation when used as part of a holistic treatment approach. Its bioactive compounds contribute to its therapeutic value in diabetes management.

5. Gastric ulcer

Roselle, or Hibiscus sabdariffa, has been traditionally used to treat gastric ulcers due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. The flower's calyces contain compounds that help reduce stomach acid and protect the gastric lining, promoting healing. Studies suggest it may inhibit harmful bacteria and reduce ulcer severity. Its use in herbal medicine highlights its role in digestive health.

6. Gastroesophageal reflux disease

Roselle has been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of gastroesophageal reflux disease due to its anti-inflammatory and antacid properties. The plant's flowers contain compounds that may reduce stomach acid production and soothe esophageal irritation. It is often prepared as a herbal tea to support digestive health. Clinical studies suggest it may help manage reflux when used as part of a holistic treatment plan.

7. Constipation

Roselle, or Hibiscus sabdariffa, is used to treat constipation due to its high fiber content and mild laxative properties. The plant's calyces stimulate bowel movements and promote digestive health. It can be consumed as a tea or added to meals for natural relief from occasional constipation.

8. Digestive disorders

Roselle is used to alleviate digestive disorders due to its anti-inflammatory and carminative properties. It helps reduce bloating, indigestion, and stomach discomfort. The plant's mucilage soothes the gastrointestinal tract, while its mild laxative effect aids in relieving constipation. Regular consumption can support overall digestive health.

9. Stomach ulcers

Roselle has been traditionally used to support digestive health, particularly in the treatment of stomach ulcers. Its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties help reduce gastric acid secretion and protect the stomach lining. Studies suggest that its flavonoids and mucilage may promote healing and prevent ulcer formation. It is often prepared as a tea or infusion for this purpose.

10. Diarrhea

Roselle is used to treat diarrhea due to its antimicrobial and astringent properties. The flower's mucilage helps soothe the digestive tract, while its compounds reduce intestinal inflammation. It is often prepared as a tea or decoction to alleviate symptoms and restore digestive balance.
