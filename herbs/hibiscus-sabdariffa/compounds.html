Roselle (Hibiscus sabdariffa) Compounds

Roselle contains several primary active compounds, including flavonoids, which act as antioxidants. Phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory properties. Anthocyanins provide pigmentation and antioxidant benefits. Tannins and glycosides support digestive health. Saponins and coumarin have antimicrobial effects, while terpenoids and essential oils contribute to its aromatic profile. Alkaloids may influence nervous system function.

Roselle (Hibiscus sabdariffa) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are key active compounds in Roselle (Hibiscus sabdariffa). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds help reduce oxidative stress and support cardiovascular health. Flavonoids are abundant in Roselle's calyces and are responsible for its distinctive red color and medicinal value.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Roselle (Hibiscus sabdariffa). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound is also linked to anti-inflammatory and cardiovascular benefits, supporting its use in traditional herbal medicine.

3. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Roselle (Hibiscus sabdariffa). It contributes to the plant's vibrant color and has antioxidant properties. Anthocyanin may support cardiovascular health and reduce inflammation. It is commonly found in the calyces of Roselle.

4. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Roselle (Hibiscus sabdariffa). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is responsible for its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Tannins in Roselle are primarily found in the calyces and are used in traditional herbal medicine for digestive and skin-related ailments.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Roselle (Hibiscus sabdariffa). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly in supporting cardiovascular health and acting as a mild diuretic. Glycosides in Roselle are also associated with anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Roselle (Hibiscus sabdariffa). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Saponin also aids in the emulsification of fats, supporting digestive health. Its presence enhances the therapeutic potential of Roselle in traditional herbal medicine.

7. Coumarin

Coumarin is a bioactive compound found in Roselle (Hibiscus sabdariffa). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Coumarin may also support cardiovascular health by improving blood circulation. Its presence enhances the therapeutic potential of Roselle in herbal medicine.

8. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Roselle (Hibiscus sabdariffa) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting the plant's use in traditional remedies for digestive and cardiovascular health. They also enhance the plant's aroma and may influence its pharmacological activity.

9. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound of Roselle (Hibiscus sabdariffa). It is extracted from the calyx and contains volatile compounds like terpenes and aromatic compounds. Essential oil contributes to Roselle's antimicrobial and antioxidant properties, making it valuable in herbal medicine.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Roselle (Hibiscus sabdariffa). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, though it is present in lower concentrations compared to other active constituents. Alkaloids in Roselle may support cardiovascular health and have mild sedative effects.
