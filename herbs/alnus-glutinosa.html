What to know about Alnus glutinosa (black alder) before using it medicinally

Black alder, scientifically known as Alnus glutinosa, is a plant renowned for its remarkable health properties that have been harnessed to alleviate various ailments.

Its leaves and stems are rich in antioxidants, vitamins, and minerals, which have been found to reduce inflammation, improve respiratory issues, and even exhibit antimicrobial effects. In the culinary world, black alder is often used as a flavoring agent in soups, stews, and teas, particularly in Eastern European and Asian cuisine. Horticulturally, it's valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an excellent companion plant for other crops.

Botanically, black alder belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its conical shape and dark, glossy leaves, while historical references date back to ancient times when its wood was used for tool-making and building.

What are the side effects of Alnus glutinosa when used improperly?

When used improperly, Alnus glutinosa increases the chances of experiencing side effects, such as allergic reactions, respiratory issues, and gastrointestinal problems.

For instance, ingesting excessive amounts or using unprocessed parts of the plant can lead to anaphylactic shock, hives, and itchy skin rashes in some individuals. Additionally, improper preparation methods may result in the presence of toxic compounds, such as terpenoids and tannins, which can cause respiratory issues like bronchitis, coughing, and difficulty breathing when inhaled.

Furthermore, using Alnus glutinosa for extended periods without proper dosage adjustments can lead to stomach upset, diarrhea, nausea, and vomiting due to the plant's saponin content, which can disrupt gut health and interact with other medications. In severe cases, improper use of Alnus glutinosa may even trigger kidney or liver damage, particularly in individuals with pre-existing medical conditions or those who are already taking certain medications.

It is essential to consult with a qualified healthcare professional before using Alnus glutinosa for medicinal purposes and to follow proper preparation and dosage guidelines to minimize the risk of adverse effects.
