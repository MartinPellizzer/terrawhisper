Common Hawthorn (Crataegus monogyna) Compounds

Common Hawthorn contains several key active compounds, including flavonoids, glycosides, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. Tannins and saponins provide astringent and detoxifying effects, while coumarin and terpenoids may support cardiovascular health. Essential oils and anthocyanins add antioxidant benefits, and trace alkaloids contribute to its overall therapeutic profile.

Common Hawthorn (Crataegus monogyna) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Common Hawthorn (Crataegus monogyna). They contribute to the plant's cardiovascular and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting heart health and reducing oxidative stress. These compounds are key to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for treating heart-related conditions.

2. Glycoside

Glycosides are key active compounds in Common Hawthorn (Crataegus monogyna). They contribute to the plant's cardiovascular and calming effects. These compounds support heart health and may reduce anxiety. Their presence makes Common Hawthorn a valuable herb in herbal medicine.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Common Hawthorn (Crataegus monogyna). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports cardiovascular health and may aid in managing conditions like hypertension and heart failure.

4. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Common Hawthorn (Crataegus monogyna). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health. Tannins in Common Hawthorn are found in the leaves, flowers, and fruits, and are often used in herbal preparations for their potential therapeutic effects.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Common Hawthorn (Crataegus monogyna). It contributes to the plant's mild sedative and antispasmodic properties. Coumarin also has anticoagulant effects, supporting cardiovascular health. Its presence enhances the overall therapeutic profile of Common Hawthorn in herbal medicine.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Common Hawthorn (Crataegus monogyna). It contributes to the plant's cardiovascular benefits by supporting heart function and reducing cholesterol. Saponins also have mild diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties, enhancing the overall therapeutic profile of the herb.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Common Hawthorn (Crataegus monogyna). They contribute to the plant's aromatic properties and may support cardiovascular health by influencing heart rate and blood pressure. These compounds are often extracted for use in herbal remedies.

8. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound in Common Hawthorn (Crataegus monogyna). It contains volatile compounds like limonene and geraniol, which contribute to its calming and cardiovascular effects. The oil is often used in aromatherapy and herbal formulations to support heart health and reduce anxiety.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Common Hawthorn (Crataegus monogyna). It contributes to the plant's red and purple pigments and has antioxidant properties. These compounds support cardiovascular health and may help reduce inflammation, making them valuable in herbal medicine for treating heart-related conditions.

10. Alkaloid

Active compounds of Common Hawthorn (Crataegus monogyna) include alkaloids, which are nitrogen-containing compounds found in various plant species. While alkaloids are present in small amounts in Common Hawthorn, they contribute to the plant's pharmacological properties, though the primary therapeutic effects are attributed to its flavonoids and oligomeric proanthocyanidins.
