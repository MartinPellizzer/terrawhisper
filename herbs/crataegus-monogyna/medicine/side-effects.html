10 Possible Side Effects Of Crataegus Monogyna (Hawthorn)

Crataegus monogyna has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal disturbances, allergic reactions, and interactions with medications.

The causes of these side effects are generally related to excessive consumption, pre-existing health conditions, or concurrent use of other herbs and medications.

For example, consuming large amounts of hawthorn can worsen conditions like acid reflux, or interact with blood thinners, leading to serious complications and decreased quality of life.

Crataegus monogyna causes allergic reactions due to its potential to trigger an immunological response in some individuals.

The allergenic properties of the plant's pollen, leaves, and berries can lead to symptoms such as skin rashes, itching, swelling, and difficulty breathing.

This is because the body mistakenly identifies these substances as foreign invaders, triggering an overactive immune response that results in allergic reactions.

As a result, people may experience nausea and vomiting after consuming Crataegus monogyna.
