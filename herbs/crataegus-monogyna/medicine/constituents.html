10 Medicinal Constituents Of Crataegus Monogyna (Hawthorn)

Crataegus monogyna has active constituents such as flavonoids (quercetin and vitexin), oligomeric proanthocyanidins, and tannins.

These constituents confer astringent, antispasmodic, and vasodilatory properties. The flavonoids and proanthocyanidins improve cardiovascular health by reducing blood pressure and cholesterol levels.

By doing so, they can reduce the risk of heart disease and improve symptoms of angina, arrhythmia, and congestive heart failure, thereby enhancing the quality of life for individuals with these conditions.

This article explains in details the 10 best active constituents of Crataegus monogyna.

1. Flavonoids

Crataegus monogyna flavonoids is a type of plant compound found in the fruits, leaves, and flowers of this shrub-like tree.

These bioactive compounds possess various pharmacological activities, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and cardio-protective effects.

Specifically, C. monogyna flavonoids have been shown to exhibit vasodilatory properties, helping to lower blood pressure and improve cardiac function.

2. Quercetin

Crataegus monogyna quercetin is a bioflavonoid compound extracted from the plant's fruit and flowers.

This antioxidant has been widely studied for its potential health benefits, including reducing inflammation, improving cardiovascular health, and exhibiting antimicrobial properties. Quercetin has also been shown to have anti-cancer effects by inhibiting the growth of cancer cells and inducing apoptosis (cell death).

Its high levels in hawthorn make it a valuable component in herbal remedies and dietary supplements.

3. Hyperoside

Crataegus monogyna hyperoside is a flavonoid compound found in the leaves and fruit of this plant.

It has been traditionally used to treat various health issues, including cardiovascular diseases, inflammation, and oxidative stress. Hyperoside has antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which may contribute to its potential therapeutic effects.

Furthermore, it has been shown to have a protective effect on the heart, which can help prevent damage caused by free radicals.

4. Aesculin

Crataegus monogyna aesculin is a bioactive compound extracted from the bark and leaves of the plant.

It has been used in traditional medicine for centuries to treat various health issues, including cardiovascular diseases. Aesculin has potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which may help protect against cell damage and reduce inflammation.

It also exhibits antimicrobial and antiviral activities, making it a promising compound for further research and potential therapeutic applications.

5. Procyanidins

Crataegus monogyna procyanidins is a group of bioactive compounds found in the fruit and leaves of the plant.

These complex phenolic molecules have been shown to exhibit antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, making them potentially beneficial for cardiovascular health and wound healing.

Additionally, procyanidins have been demonstrated to possess antidiabetic and anticancer activities, highlighting their potential as a valuable component in traditional medicine and pharmacological applications.

6. Epicatechin

Crataegus monogyna epicatechin is a polyphenolic compound found in its fruit, leaves, and flowers.

It has been extensively studied for its potential health benefits, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and cardio-protective effects. Epicatechin has been shown to have a significant impact on lowering cholesterol levels, improving blood flow, and reducing the risk of cardiovascular disease.

Its bioactive properties make it a promising natural compound for preventing and treating various heart-related disorders.

7. Ellagic acid

Crataegus monogyna ellagic acid is a polyphenolic compound isolated from its fruit.

This antioxidant has been extensively studied for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and anticancer properties.

Ellagic acid has been shown to exhibit cytotoxic activity against various types of cancer cells, suggesting its possible role in cancer prevention or treatment.

8. Galloylquinic acids

Crataegus monogyna galloylquinic acids is a unique group of compounds found in the fruit and leaves of this plant.

These acids are characterized by their ability to inhibit platelet aggregation, reduce blood pressure, and exhibit antioxidant activity. Research has shown that they may have potential benefits for cardiovascular health, including lowering cholesterol levels and reducing inflammation.

Further studies are needed to fully understand the effects of galloylquinic acids from Crataegus monogyna on human health.

9. Phenolic acids

Crataegus monogyna phenolic acids is a class of bioactive compounds found in its leaves, stems, and fruits.

These acids exhibit potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which have been linked to various health benefits, including cardiovascular protection, anticancer effects, and neuroprotection.

The most abundant phenolic acids in C. monogyna include gallic acid, ellagic acid, and quercetin, among others, which contribute to its traditional uses in folk medicine for treating various ailments.

10. Aliphatic compounds

Crataegus monogyna aliphatic compounds is a type of chemical constituent found in its leaves and fruit.

These compounds are composed of saturated or unsaturated hydrocarbon chains and have been shown to exhibit various bioactive properties.

Specifically, the aliphatic compounds present in C. monogyna have been reported to possess antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial activities, which may contribute to the plant's traditional uses in folk medicine for treating cardiovascular and respiratory disorders.
