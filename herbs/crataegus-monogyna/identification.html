Botanical Identification of Common Hawthorn (Crataegus monogyna)

To identify Common Hawthorn (Crataegus monogyna) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Common Hawthorn, Crataegus monogyna, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Rosales, family Rosaceae, genus Crataegus, and species Crataegus monogyna. This classification places it within the rose family, characteristic of many medicinal and ornamental plants.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Common Hawthorn is Crataegus monogyna, first described by Carl Linnaeus in 1753. It has several synonyms, including Crataegus oxyacantha and Crataegus laevigata, reflecting historical taxonomic changes. Common names such as Hawthorn, Thornapple, and Mayblossom are widely used, while regional names vary across Europe. Historically, it was also known as Quickset and White-thorn in older botanical texts.

Morphological Characteristics

Common Hawthorn is a deciduous shrub or small tree with a rounded growth habit. It has a deep, extensive root system that helps stabilize soil. Its stems are thorny and branch from the base, forming a dense canopy. Leaves are ovate to elliptical, with serrated edges and a glossy green underside. The plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that contain one seed each.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Common Hawthorn can be distinguished by its thorny stems, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of small white flowers followed by red berries. It differs from similar species like Crataegus laevigata by having single-seeded berries and shorter, more rigid thorns. Its leaves are typically more rounded and have a smoother texture compared to other hawthorn varieties.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Common Hawthorn is native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa. It thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained soils, such as loam or sandy soils. It typically grows between 100 and 1,000 meters above sea level.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Common Hawthorn blooms in late spring to early summer, typically between May and June. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early autumn, around August to September. For identification purposes, harvest timing is best during late summer to early autumn when fruits are fully mature.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Common Hawthorn (Crataegus monogyna) belongs to the genus Crataegus in the family Rosaceae. It is closely related to other hawthorn species within the same genus. Its phylogenetic position reflects shared traits with other Rosaceae members, such as rose and apple, due to similar morphological and genetic features.
