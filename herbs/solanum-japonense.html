Solanum japonense

What's the taxonomical classification of Solanum japonense?

Solanum japonense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Solanales. As a member of the family Solanaceae, it is situated within the genus Solanum, ultimately reaching its specific designation at the species level as Solanum japonense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Solanum japonense has an erect, perennial or annual herb structure characterized by woody stems that are densely covered in stellate hairs and often possess small prickles. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate in shape, and feature wavy or irregularly lobed margins with a velvety texture. Small, star-shaped flowers emerge in clusters, displaying five bright yellow petals and a prominent central cone of yellow anthers. These flowers eventually produce small, globose berries that transition from green to a ripe, fleshy red color. The fruit contains numerous tiny, flat seeds which are the primary means of dispersal for the species.
