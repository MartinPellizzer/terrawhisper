Helicia nilagirica

What's the taxonomical classification of Helicia nilagirica?

Helicia nilagirica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further organized into the order Proteales. It is a member of the family Proteaceae, falling under the genus Helicia, and is specifically identified by its species name, nilagirica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Helicia nilagirica has woody stems with simple, alternate, elliptic to oblong leaves that possess entire margins and pinnate venation.

The foliage is typically leathery in texture and exhibits a glossy green surface on the upper side. Small, solitary flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves, displaying a structure characteristic of the Elaeocarpaceae family. The fruit develops as a fleshy drupe that serves as a key identifying feature of the species.

These morphological traits collectively distinguish the plant within its specific montane habitat.
