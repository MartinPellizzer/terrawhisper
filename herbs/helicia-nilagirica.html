Helicia nilagirica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Helicia nilagirica has woody stems with simple, alternate, elliptic to oblong leaves that possess entire margins and pinnate venation. The foliage is typically leathery in texture and exhibits a glossy green surface on the upper side. Small, solitary flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves, displaying a structure characteristic of the Elaeocarpaceae family. The fruit develops as a fleshy drupe that serves as a key identifying feature of the species. These morphological traits collectively distinguish the plant within its specific montane habitat.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the Nilgiri Mountains in the Western Ghats of southern India. It is specifically found inhabiting the montane shola forests and high-altitude grasslands of this region. Its presence is restricted to these specific ecological niches within the high elevations of the Nilgiri district. Localized populations exist where the climate and soil conditions meet the strict requirements of this species. This limited range makes the plant a specialist of the unique Nilgiri landscape.
