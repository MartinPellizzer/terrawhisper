Selinum wallichianum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Selinum wallichianum has an erect, herbaceous habit characterized by hollow, branched stems and pinnately compound leaves with serrated leaflets. The foliage is typically arranged in an alternate pattern, displaying a delicate, feathery texture. Its inflorescence consists of numerous small, white flowers organized into dense, flat-topped compound umbels. Each individual flower possesses a small, tubular corolla and prominent reproductive structures. The plant develops a sturdy taproot system that supports its upright growth during the flowering season.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the Himalayan mountain range, spanning across several countries including India, Nepal, Bhutan, and parts of China. It primarily thrives in high-altitude regions where moist, rocky environments are common. Populations are frequently found in subalpine zones and temperate forests. These specific ecological niches provide the necessary cool temperatures and humidity required for its growth. Consequently, its range is restricted to these specific mountainous corridors of South and East Asia.
