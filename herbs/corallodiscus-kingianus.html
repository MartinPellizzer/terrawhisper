Corallodiscus kingianus

What's the taxonomical classification of Corallodiscus kingianus?

Corallodiscus kingianus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant follows the biological lineage of the order Lamiales. It is further categorized under the family Gesneriaceae, which places it within the genus Corallodiscus, ultimately identifying it as the specific species kingianus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Corallodiscus kingianus has a woody shrub or small tree habit characterized by simple, coriaceous leaves that are typically elliptic to oblong in shape.

Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the axillary regions of the branches. The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that develops from the fertilized ovary following pollination.

        What are the herbal preparations of Corallodiscus kingianus?

        Corallodiscus kingianus is prepared as a decoction of its bark or leaves to treat various ailments. The bark is often boiled in water to create a potent liquid extract used for medicinal purposes. Some local traditions involve crushing the plant material to apply it topically as a poultice.         What side-effects this plant can have?

        Corallodiscus kingianus can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and potential gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Contact with the plant's sap or leaves may lead to localized redness, itching, or dermatitis in sensitive individuals. If the plant is consumed, it might trigger nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. There is also a risk of respiratory irritation if the plant's pollen or fine particles are inhaled. It is essential to handle this species with care to avoid these adverse physical responses.
