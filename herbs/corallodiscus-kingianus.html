Corallodiscus kingianus has a woody shrub or small tree habit characterized by simple, coriaceous leaves that are typically elliptic to oblong in shape. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the axillary regions of the branches. The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that develops from the fertilized ovary following pollination.
