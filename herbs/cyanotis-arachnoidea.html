What's the taxonomical classification of Cyanotis arachnoidea?

Cyanotis arachnoidea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized as Equisetopsida, falling under the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Commelinales and is a member of the family Commelinaeaecae. Within this family, it is assigned to the genus Cyanotis, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species arachnoidea.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds 20-hydroxyecdysone, 22-oxo-20-hydroxyecdysone, ecdysteroids, phytoecdysteroids, and beta-sitosterol. These bioactive molecules belong to the group of phytoecdysteroids, which are plant-derived steroids that mimic insect hormones. Within this category, 20-hydroxyecdysone and 22-oxo-20-hydroxyecdysone serve as primary ecdysteroids known for their biological activity. The presence of beta-sitosterol further enhances the medicinal profile of the plant through its sterol properties. Together, these various ecdysteroids provide a complex chemical foundation for the plant's therapeutic potential.
