Rosa gallica var. pumila

Rosa gallica var. pumila, commonly known as Dwarf French Rose, is a compact, shrub-like variety of the Gallic rose species.

This plant is rich in antioxidants and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The therapeutic actions of Rosa gallica var. pumila are attributed to its bioactive constituents, such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which have been shown to exhibit antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antiviral activities.

Additionally, the plant's extracts have been reported to exhibit wound-healing and antiseptic properties, making it a potential candidate for the treatment of various skin conditions and infections.
