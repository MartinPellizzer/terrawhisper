Acampe papillosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Acampe papillosa has pendulous, fleshy, cylindrical stems that can grow several meters long and are covered in numerous small, thickened aerial roots. These stems are typically unbranched and serve as the primary structural support for the epiphytic plant. The leaves are relatively small, leathery, and arranged in a somewhat distichous pattern along the length of the stem. From the leaf axils, the plant produces fragrant, star-shaped flowers that are characterized by a distinctively fleshy, trilobed lip. These blossoms often exhibit shades of yellow or cream with subtle markings on the labellum.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical regions of South and Southeast Asia, specifically spanning from India and Sri Lanka through Myanmar, Thailand, and Malaysia to parts of southern China. Within these regions, the species is often found growing as an epiphyte in moist, shaded habitats. Its range extends across various islands in the Malay Archipelago, where it adapts to seasonal monsoon climates. This broad distribution allows the species to colonize diverse tropical ecosystems across the Asian continent.
