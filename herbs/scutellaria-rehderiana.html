Scutellaria rehderiana

What's the taxonomical classification of Scutellaria rehderiana?

Scutelariia rehderiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Lamiales. Further taxonomic refinement places it in the family Lamiaceae, specifically within the genus Scutellaria, with its unique identity defined by the species name rehderiana.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Scutellaria rehderiana contains high concentrations of bioactive flavones, specifically baicalin, baicalein, and wogonin, which serve as its primary medicinal components. These specific chemical constituents are responsible for the plant's potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Research indicates that these flavonoids can effectively inhibit various pro-inflammatory cytokines and enzymes in the body. Furthermore, the presence of these compounds contributes to the plant's potential uses in treating respiratory infections and managing allergic reactions. Scientists continue to investigate these molecules for their neuroprotective and anti-tumor activities in modern pharmacology.

What are the therapeutic actions of Scutellaria rehderiana?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat inflammation, fever, respiratory infections, skin conditions, and various types of digestive ailments. Its primary therapeutic value comes from bioactive compounds like baicalin that exhibit strong antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. These constituents help reduce swelling and soothe irritated mucosal linings in the lungs and gut. Clinicians also utilize it to manage symptoms associated with liver dysfunction and certain febrile illnesses. By modulating immune responses, the plant assists the body in fighting off bacterial and viral pathogens.
