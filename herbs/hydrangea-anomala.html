What's the taxonomical classification of Hydrangea anomala?

Hydrangea anomalas belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent through the order Cornales, it is placed into the family Hydrangeaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Hydrangea, and its specific species designation, anomalas.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal pain due to the presence of cyanogenic glycosides in its tissues. If a person ingests a significant amount, the release of hydrogen cyanide can lead to severe cyanide poisoning. This toxic reaction affects the body's ability to use oxygen, potentially resulting in dizziness or even death. Symptoms typically manifest shortly after the plant material is consumed. It is crucial to avoid ingesting any part of this species to prevent these dangerous health complications.
