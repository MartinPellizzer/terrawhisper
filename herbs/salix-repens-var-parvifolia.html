Salix repens var. parvifolia

Salix repens var. parvifolia, commonly known as dwarf willow, is a small shrub that belongs to the Salicaceae family.

It is valued for its medicinal properties, which include reducing inflammation, relieving pain, and lowering fever.

The therapeutic actions of Salix repens var. parvifolia are attributed to its ability to stimulate digestion, improve circulation, and act as a diuretic, making it a potential treatment for conditions such as arthritis, rheumatism, and urinary issues.

The bioactive constituents of this plant include salicylic acid, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antioxidant properties.
