What's the taxonomical classification of Kalopanax septemlobus?

Kalopanax septemlobus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Apiales and the family Araliaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Kalopanax, and its unique species designation, septemlobus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Kalopanax septemlobus has large, palmately lobed leaves that typically feature seven distinct segments with coarsely serrated margins.

The leaves can reach significant sizes and are arranged oppositely along the woody stems. The plant produces small, creamy-white flowers that grow in dense, terminal panicles. These blossoms eventually give way to fleshy, dark purple to black drupes that hang in clusters.

The bark of the mature tree is thick and deeply fissured, providing a rugged texture to the trunk.
