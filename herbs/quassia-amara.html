Quassia (Quassia amara)

Quassia (Quassia amara) is a member of the Simaroubaceae family, native to South America, West Africa, and Southeast Asia. Traditionally, its bark, leaves, and resin have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its bitter, tonic, and astringent actions, and has a long history of use in african traditional medicine, european herbal medicine, and native american herbal medicine.
