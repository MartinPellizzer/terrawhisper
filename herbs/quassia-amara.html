Quassia amara

Quassia (Quassia amara) is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America, known for its bitter-tasting wood and leaves, which have been used for centuries in traditional medicine to treat digestive issues and reduce fever.

Quassia amara is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Venezuela, and is also found in parts of Central America and the Caribbean.

Traditional Uses

Quassia (Quassia amara) has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its bitter properties to stimulate digestion, reduce fever, and treat parasitic infections.

Medicinal Systems

Quassia amara has been traditionally used in various medicinal systems such as Ayurveda, Chinese medicine, and indigenous South American healing practices for its bitter tonic and digestive properties.

Quassia (Quassia amara) exhibits pharmacological properties characterized by its bitter tonic effects, antipyretic activity, and ability to stimulate digestive secretions, making it valuable in herbal medicine for addressing digestive discomfort and fever.
