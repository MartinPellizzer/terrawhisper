Quassia Amara: Medicinal Uses And Benefits

Quassia amara, commonly known as the bitter apple tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America, known for its medicinal properties.

This herb is characterized by its highly bitter leaves and fruits, which contain various bioactive constituents such as quassiamine, quassia acid, and alkaloids.

The health benefits of Quassia amara include its ability to support digestive health, reduce fever, and act as a natural remedy for respiratory infections.

Its therapeutic actions involve anti-inflammatory, antipyretic, and antimicrobial properties, making it a valuable herb in traditional medicine.
