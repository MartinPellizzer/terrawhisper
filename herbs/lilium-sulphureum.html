Lilium sulphureum

What's the taxonomical classification of Lilium sulphureum?

Lilium sulphureuem belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Liliales. This plant is situated within the family Liliaceae, falling under the genus Lilium, and is specifically identified by its species name, sulphurueum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lilium sulphureuum has tall, erect stems that typically reach heights of 60 to 120 centimeters and are adorned with lanceolate, bright green leaves arranged in whorls or spirals. The inflorescence consists of several nodding, funnel-shaped flowers that exhibit a distinct sulfur-yellow coloration. Each tepal is characterized by a recurved shape and often features small, brownish spots near the center of the throat. The plant develops from underground bulbs which provide the necessary energy for its seasonal growth. These botanical features distinguish the species within its specific ecological niche.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of western China, specifically within the provinces of Sichuan, Yunnan, and Gansu. It typically flourishes in mountainous terrains where the elevation allows for cooler temperatures. These lilies are often found growing in subalpine meadows and along the edges of coniferous forests. The specific microclimates of these central Asian mountain ranges provide the necessary moisture and soil conditions for their survival. How is this plant cultivated?

It grows best in temperate climates where it can experience a period of winter dormancy. Regular watering is necessary to keep the soil moist but never waterlogged to prevent bulb rot.
