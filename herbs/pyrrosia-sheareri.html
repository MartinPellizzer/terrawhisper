Pyrrosia sheareri

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pyrrosia sheareri has small, opposite, elliptic to ovate leaves that are typically bright green with slightly serrated margins and a somewhat fleshy texture. The plant is a diminutive, creeping herb that forms low-growing mats through the use of slender, prostrate stems. Its reproductive structures consist of tiny, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These flowers eventually develop into minute, dry fruits characteristic of the Ericaceae family. The overall structure is characterized by its compact, ground-hugging growth habit suited for moist, shaded environments.
