Pyrrosia sheareri

What's the taxonomical classification of Pyrrosia sheareri?

Pyrrosia sheareri belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its hierarchical taxonomic structure places it in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae. Moving further down the lineage, it is categorized under the order Polypodiales and the family Polypodiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Pyrrosia, and its unique species designation, sheareri.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pyrrosia sheareri has small, opposite, elliptic to ovate leaves that are typically bright green with slightly serrated margins and a somewhat fleshy texture.

The plant is a diminutive, creeping herb that forms low-growing mats through the use of slender, prostrate stems. Its reproductive structures consist of tiny, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These flowers eventually develop into minute, dry fruits characteristic of the Ericaceae family.

The overall structure is characterized by its compact, ground-hugging growth habit suited for moist, shaded environments.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

Some people might also encounter more significant swelling or a rash if they are particularly sensitive to the plant's chemical compounds. While specific systemic toxicity is less documented, caution is advised to prevent accidental ingestion or prolonged contact.
