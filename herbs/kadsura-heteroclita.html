What's the taxonomical classification of Kadsura heteroclita?

Kadsura heteroclita belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it falls under the order Austrobaileyales and the family Schisandraceae. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Kadsura, and its species designation, heterocliata.

What are the pharmacological activities of Kadsura heteroclita?

This plant has analgesic, anti-inflammatory, anti-oxidant, hepatoprotective, and neuroprotective pharmacological activities. Its bioactive compounds work to reduce pain and suppress inflammatory responses within the body. The antioxidant properties help neutralize free radicals to prevent cellular damage caused by oxidative stress. Furthermore, the plant exhibits hepatoprotective effects that shield the liver from toxic substances and injury. It also demonstrates neuroprotective capabilities that support the preservation and function of nerve cells.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", the stem of Kadsura heterocliite (KHS) exhibits analgesic and anti-inflammatory effects with a maximal tolerated single dose of 26 g/kg in both sexes of mice. At a dose of 200 mg/kg, KHS significantly prolonged the reaction time of rats to radiant heat stimulation and suppressed abdominal writhing episodes in mice. The plant showed dose-dependent inhibition of xylene-induced ear swelling in mice at doses of 200, 400, and 800 mg/kg. Furthermore, KHS demonstrated dose- and time-dependent suppression of paw edema in rats at doses of 100, 200, 400, and 800 mg/kg. These anti-inflammatory effects are associated with the inhibition of pro-inflammatory cytokines IL-1β, IL-6, and TNF-α, as well as the decreased expression of COX-2 and iNOS proteins.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds heteroclitin H, heteroclitin D, gomisin J, gamma-schisanndrin, and neokasuranin. These specific lignans and sesquiterpenoids are primary bioactive constituents identified within its chemical profile. Researchers study these molecules to understand their potential pharmacological properties and therapeutic applications. The presence of such diverse compounds contributes to the complex chemical signature of the species. Each substance plays a unique role in the plant's overall biochemical makeup.

According to a study published by "Journal of Asian natural products research", a new dibenzocyclooctadiene lignan called heterocliitin H (1) was isolated from the stems of Kadsura heteroclita. This plant also contains seven known compounds, specifically heterocliitin D (2), interiorin B (3), interiorin (4), neokasuranin (5), interiotherin C (6), gomisin J (7), and (+)-anwulignan (8). This study represents the first report regarding the isolation of compounds 3, 5, 6, 7, and 8 from Kadsura heteroclita. The structures of these identified medicinal compounds were elucidated using spectroscopic methods.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for the treatment of hepatitis, inflammation, liver damage, and rheumatoid arthritis. It serves as a powerful agent for liver protection by shielding hepatic cells from various toxins. The medicinal properties of the plant help to reduce systemic inflammation throughout the body. These anti-inflammatory effects are particularly beneficial for managing the joint pain associated with rheumatoid arthritis. Additionally, its ability to mitigate liver damage makes it a valuable resource for supporting overall hepatic health.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", Kadsura heteroclita is traditionally used for the treatment of rheumatoid arthritis (RA), hepatitis, and muscles and joint spasm. Phytochemical studies on the stem of the plant resulted in the identification of 187 chemical constituents. These isolates and crude extracts exhibit a wide spectrum of in vivo and in vitro pharmacological activities including anti-RA, anti-inflammatory and analgesic, hepatoprotection, anti-HIV, anti-cancer and anti-HBV. Additionally, the major component Schisanlactone E exhibits anti-cancer, neuroprotective and anti-neuroinflammation activities. The plant has been found to be safe and non-toxic within the therapeutic dose range.
