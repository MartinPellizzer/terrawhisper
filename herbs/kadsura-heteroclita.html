What's the taxonomical classification of Kadsura heteroclita?

Kadsura heteroclita belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it falls under the order Austrobaileyales and the family Schisandraceae. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Kadsura, and its species designation, heterocliata.

What are the pharmacological activities of Kadsura heteroclita?

This plant has analgesic, anti-inflammatory, anti-oxidation, hepatoprotective, and neuroprotective pharmacological activities. Its bioactive compounds work to reduce pain and suppress inflammatory responses in various biological systems. The presence of potent antioxidants helps mitigate oxidative stress by neutralizing harmful free radicals. Additionally, the plant provides significant protection to liver cells against chemical damage and toxicity. It also demonstrates neuroprotective properties that may assist in preserving neuronal integrity and function.

As discussed in a study, the stem of Kadsura heteroclieta exhibits significant analgesic and anti-inflammatory effects with a maximal tolerated single dose of 26 g/kg in mice of both sexes. At a dose of 200 mg/kg, the plant significantly prolonged the reaction time of rats to radiant heat stimulation and suppressed abdominal writhing episodes in mice induced by acetic acid. Kadsura heteroclieta at doses of 200, 400, and 800 mg/kg showed dose-dependent inhibition of xylene-induced ear swelling in mice. Furthermore, doses of 100, 200, 400, and 800 mg/kg demonstrated dose- and time-dependent suppression of paw edema induced by carrageenan in rats. These pharmacological activities are associated with the inhibition of pro-inflammatory cytokines IL-1β, IL-6, and TNF-α, as well as the decreased expression of COX-2 and iNOS proteins.

Its medicinal properties are primarily utilized to reduce swelling and pain in the joints. Many people rely on its bioactive compounds to manage the chronic symptoms of rheumatoid arthritis. The plant acts effectively against systemic inflammation within the body. These therapeutic applications make it a significant resource for addressing various rheumatic diseases.

As discussed in a study, the stem of Kadsura heterocliite (KH S) is an anti-arthritic Tujia ethnomedicinal plant used for the prevention and treatment of rheumatic and arthritic diseases. The maximal tolerated single dose of KHS was determined to be 26 g/kg in both sexes of mice. At a dose of 200 mg/kg, KHS significantly prolonged the reaction time of rats to radiant heat stimulation and suppressed abdominal writhing episodes of mice. KHS at doses of 200, 400, and 800 mg/kg showed dose-dependent inhibition of xylene-induced ear swelling in mice. Additionally, KHS at doses of 100, 200, 400, and 800 mg/kg demonstrated dose- and time-dependent suppression of paw edema induced by subcutaneous injection of carrageenan in all rats. The anti-inflammatory effect of KHS is associated with inhibition of the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines IL-1β, IL-6, and TNF-α and decreasing the expression of COX and iNOS proteins.
