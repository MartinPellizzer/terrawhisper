Aloe Vera (Aloe barbadensis) Compounds

Aloe Vera contains several primary active compounds, including terpenoids, which have anti-inflammatory properties, and flavonoids, known for their antioxidant effects. Phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial actions, while saponins and glycosides support digestive health. Tannins and coumarin add to its therapeutic potential, along with essential oils, anthocyanins, and alkaloids.

Aloe Vera (Aloe barbadensis) Monograph

1. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are key active compounds in Aloe barbadensis, contributing to its medicinal properties. These compounds, including acemannan and various sterols, exhibit anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and wound-healing effects. They play a crucial role in the therapeutic applications of Aloe Vera in herbal medicine.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Aloe barbadensis. They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help protect cells from oxidative stress and support the overall therapeutic effects of Aloe Vera.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Aloe barbadensis, contributing to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. It helps protect cells from oxidative stress and supports the healing of skin conditions. This compound is abundant in the gel of the Aloe leaf, enhancing the plant's therapeutic value in herbal medicine.

4. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Aloe barbadensis, known for its surfactant properties. It contributes to the gel's emollient and anti-inflammatory effects. Saponin also aids in the penetration of other active components through the skin. Its presence supports the therapeutic use of Aloe Vera in skincare and inflammatory conditions.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Aloe barbadensis, commonly known as Aloe Vera. It contributes to the plant's therapeutic properties by supporting skin healing and reducing inflammation. Glycosides in Aloe Vera are part of the plant's complex array of bioactive substances.

6. Tannin

Tannin is a compound found in Aloe barbadensis. It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may aid in reducing inflammation and promoting wound healing. Tannins in Aloe Vera are also believed to support digestive health by binding to proteins and reducing gut irritation.

7. Coumarin

Coumarin is a naturally occurring compound found in Aloe barbadensis. It contributes to the plant's therapeutic properties by exhibiting anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory effects. Coumarin also plays a role in the plant's ability to support skin health and wound healing. Its presence enhances the overall efficacy of Aloe Vera in traditional herbal medicine.

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of Aloe barbadensis contains volatile compounds like terpenes and aromatic acids. It contributes to the plant's therapeutic properties, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. This oil is used in herbal medicine for skin treatments and digestive support. Its presence enhances the overall efficacy of Aloe Vera in traditional remedies.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Aloe barbadensis. It contributes to the plant's vibrant color and has antioxidant properties that support skin health and reduce inflammation. This compound is part of the complex mixture of substances in Aloe Vera that contribute to its medicinal uses.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Aloe barbadensis, commonly known as Aloe Vera. It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Alkaloids in Aloe Vera support its use in treating skin conditions and digestive issues. These compounds are part of the complex biochemical profile that gives Aloe Vera its therapeutic value.
