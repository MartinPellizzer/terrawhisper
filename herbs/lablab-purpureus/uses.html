Lablab (Lablab purpureus) Uses

Lablab purpureus is used to treat respiratory conditions such as bronchitis, asthma, and cough. It also aids in managing gastrointestinal issues like gastritis and diarrhea. Additionally, it supports wound healing and has antimicrobial properties against skin infections and gastrointestinal pathogens.

Lablab (Lablab purpureus) Monograph

1. Bronchitis

Lablab purpureus is used in traditional medicine to alleviate symptoms of bronchitis due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. It helps reduce airway inflammation and loosen mucus, easing breathing. The plant's leaves and seeds are commonly prepared as teas or decoctions for this purpose. Its respiratory benefits make it a valuable remedy in herbal treatments for bronchial conditions.

2. Asthma

Lablab purpureus has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to alleviate asthma symptoms. Its anti-inflammatory and bronchodilatory properties help reduce airway swelling and improve respiratory function. It is often prepared as a decoction or powder to support lung health and ease breathing in individuals with asthma.

3. Respiratory infection

Lablab purpureus is used traditionally to support respiratory health by reducing inflammation and easing symptoms of respiratory infections. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties help soothe coughs, clear mucus, and boost immune response. It is often prepared as a decoction or tea to aid in treating conditions like bronchitis and colds.

4. Cough

Lablab purpureus is used to alleviate cough symptoms due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps loosen mucus and reduce throat irritation. Traditionally, it is prepared as a decoction or powder to ease respiratory discomfort. Its soothing effects make it a valuable remedy in herbal medicine for cough management.

5. Respiratory infections

Lablab purpureus is used to alleviate symptoms of respiratory infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce coughing, congestion, and inflammation in the airways. The plant's leaves and seeds are commonly prepared as teas or decoctions to support respiratory health. It is particularly valued in traditional medicine for its soothing and expectorant effects.

6. Skin infections

Lablab purpureus is used topically to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce bacterial growth and soothe irritated skin. Applications include poultices or extracts applied directly to affected areas. Its effectiveness makes it a valuable herb in traditional healing practices.

7. Wound healing

Lablab purpureus is used topically to promote wound healing due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce infection risk and accelerates tissue repair. The plant's extracts are applied directly to wounds to support faster recovery and minimize scarring.

8. Diarrhea

Lablab purpureus is used to treat diarrhea due to its antimicrobial and astringent properties. It helps reduce intestinal inflammation and fluid loss. The plant's seeds and leaves are commonly prepared as decoctions or poultices. It is valued in traditional medicine for its ability to soothe digestive discomfort.

9. Gastritis

Lablab purpureus is used in traditional medicine to alleviate gastritis symptoms due to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. It helps reduce stomach inflammation and neutralize excess acid, promoting healing of the gastric lining. Its mucilage content forms a protective layer, easing irritation and discomfort associated with gastritis.

10. Gastrointestinal infections

Lablab purpureus has been traditionally used to treat gastrointestinal infections due to its antimicrobial properties. It helps combat pathogens like Escherichia coli and Salmonella, which commonly cause intestinal infections. Its anti-inflammatory effects also support gut healing and reduce symptoms such as diarrhea and abdominal pain.
