10 Possible Side Effects Of Lablab Purpureus

Lablab purpureus has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal upset, skin irritation, and allergic reactions.

These side effects are often caused by consuming large amounts or using it as a topical remedy without proper caution.

If left unmanaged, they can worsen quality of life, leading to discomfort, itchiness, and potentially even respiratory issues in severe cases.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Lablab purpureus causes allergic reactions.

This is often due to the presence of a specific compound called lectin, which is found in the bean's seeds and can trigger an immune response in some individuals. Additionally, cross-reactivity with other legumes or pollen may also contribute to the development of allergies to Lablab purpureus.

The severity and symptoms of allergic reactions vary depending on the individual's sensitivity.

2. Triggers stomach upset

Lablab purpureus triggers stomach upset due to its high content of raffinose, a complex sugar that can be difficult for some people to digest.

This may cause bloating, gas, and discomfort in individuals with sensitive stomachs or those who are not accustomed to consuming legumes rich in raffinose.

3. Induces diarrhea

Lablab purpureus induces diarrhea due to its high content of raffinose and stachyose, which are types of oligosaccharides that can be difficult for some people to digest.

The body's inability to break down these complex sugars leads to an overgrowth of bacteria in the gut, resulting in symptoms such as diarrhea, bloating, and abdominal cramps.

4. Produces nausea

Lablab purpureus produces nausea.

This side effect may be attributed to the presence of phytochemicals and saponins in the plant, which can stimulate the digestive system and cause stomach upset or discomfort.

The high fiber content of Lablab purpureus can also contribute to this adverse reaction, leading to bloating, cramping, and feelings of nausea in some individuals who consume it.

5. Increases heart rate

Lablab purpureus increases heart rate due to its stimulatory effects on the cardiovascular system.

The phytochemicals present in the plant, particularly anthraquinones and flavonoids, can stimulate the sympathetic nervous system, leading to an increase in heart rate and blood pressure.

This stimulating effect is thought to be responsible for the elevated heart rate observed in individuals consuming Lablab purpureus.

6. Lowers blood pressure

Lablab purpureus lowers blood pressure by stimulating the body's natural vasodilation processes.

The anthocyanins and flavonoids present in Lablab purpureus help relax blood vessels, allowing for increased blood flow and decreased blood pressure.

This effect is likely due to the inhibition of angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE), a key regulator of blood pressure.

7. Stimulates respiratory system

Lablab purpureus stimulates respiratory system due to its inherent properties.

The plant's extracts contain saponins and flavonoids, which can cause an increase in respiration rate and depth when ingested or used topically.

This stimulatory effect is attributed to the ability of these compounds to relax bronchial smooth muscles and enhance bronchodilation, leading to improved lung function and overall respiratory performance.

8. Provokes anxiety attacks

Lablab purpureus provokes anxiety attacks due to its potential interactions with certain neurotransmitters in the brain.

The plant's active compounds, such as flavonoids and saponins, may alter the body's serotonin levels, leading to feelings of unease and heightened tension.

Additionally, Lablab purpureus has been known to stimulate the release of epinephrine, a hormone associated with stress responses, which can further exacerbate anxiety symptoms.

9. Causes dizziness

Lablab purpureus causes dizziness due to its high content of lectins, which can bind to nerve endings and disrupt normal neurotransmitter function.

The bean's isoflavones may also contribute to this side effect by altering the body's hormonal balance, leading to changes in blood pressure and circulation that can cause lightheadedness or dizziness.

This adverse reaction can occur when consuming Lablab purpureus in large quantities or as part of a diet that lacks sufficient fiber and nutrients.

10. Enhances hallucinations

Lablab purpureus enhances hallucinations by interacting with the brain's serotonin and dopamine levels.

The plant's active compounds, including alkaloids and glycosides, can alter the normal functioning of these neurotransmitters, leading to altered perception, distorted sensory experiences, and vivid hallucinations.

This is due to Lablab purpureus's ability to stimulate the release of serotonin and dopamine, which can cause a range of psychoactive effects.
