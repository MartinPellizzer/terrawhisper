To identify Lablab (Lablab purpureus) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Lablab purpureus belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Fabales, order Fabales, family Fabaceae, genus Lablab, and species Lablab purpureus. These classifications place it within the legume family, highlighting its botanical relationships and potential medicinal properties.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Lablab is Lablab purpureus, first described by Linnaeus in 1768. It is also known by several synonyms, including Phaseolus purpureus and Dolichos purpureus. Common names vary, with "hyacinth bean" being widely recognized. Regional and historical names reflect its use in traditional medicine across different cultures.

Morphological Characteristics

Lablab purpureus is an annual legume with a taproot system that supports its growth. It has erect, branching stems that are hairy and often reddish in color. The leaves are trifoliate, with oval leaflets that have a slightly hairy surface. The inflorescence is a raceme of pea-like flowers, typically purple or pink, and the fruit is a pod containing several kidney-shaped seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Lablab purpureus can be distinguished by its distinctive purple flowers, palmately lobed leaves, and three-winged fruit pods. It differs from similar species like Dolichos lablab by its more vibrant floral color and unique leaf structure. The presence of fine hairs on stems and leaves also aids in identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Lablab purpureus is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, southern Asia, and the Pacific Islands. It thrives in warm climates with distinct wet and dry seasons. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils and is typically found at altitudes between 0 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Lablab purpureus typically blooms from late spring to early summer, producing vibrant purple flowers. Seed formation occurs in mid to late summer, following successful pollination. For identification purposes, the plant is best harvested when the pods are fully mature and the seeds are hard, usually in late summer or early autumn.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Lablab purpureus is closely related to species within the genus Lablab, which belongs to the legume family Fabaceae. It shares key family traits such as pod fruiting and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. Its phylogenetic position highlights its evolutionary ties to other legumes, aiding in accurate identification and understanding of its medicinal properties.
