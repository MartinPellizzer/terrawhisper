Pedicularis ingens

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pedicularis ingens has upright, glandular-pubescent stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and bear densely packed, tubular flowers arranged in terminal spikes. The leaves are primarily basal and deeply pinnately lobed, often appearing dissected or linear in shape. The floral structure is characterized by a prominent, enlarged upper lip that is often brightly colored to attract pollinators. Smaller, spreading lower lips provide a landing platform for insects during the pollination process. Each inflorescence displays a sequence of bracts that partially sheath the individual flowers along the stem.
