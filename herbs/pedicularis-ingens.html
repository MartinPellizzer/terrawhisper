Pedicularis ingens

What's the taxonomical classification of Pedicularis ingens?

Pedicularis ingens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Lamiales. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the family Orobanchaceae, which contains the genus Pedicularis. Ultimately, the specific identity of this plant is defined by its species designation, ingens.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Pedicularis ingens can cause adverse physiological reactions including nausea, dizziness, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested by humans or animals. The plant contains various chemical compounds that may trigger allergic skin reactions such as redness or itching upon direct contact. Because the specific toxicity profile of this species is not extensively documented in medical literature, its effects on the central nervous system remain uncertain. Caution should be exercised when handling the specimen to avoid accidental consumption or irritation. There is also a potential risk of more severe complications if the plant is consumed in large quantities.
