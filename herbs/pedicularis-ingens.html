What's the taxonomical classification of Pedicularis ingens?

Pedicularis ingens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Lamiales. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the family Orobanchaceae, which contains the genus Pedicularis. Ultimately, the specific identity of this plant is defined by its species designation, ingens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pedicularis ingens has upright, glandular-pubescent stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and bear densely packed, tubular flowers arranged in terminal spikes.

The leaves are primarily basal and deeply pinnately lobed, often appearing dissected or linear in shape. The floral structure is characterized by a prominent, enlarged upper lip that is often brightly colored to attract pollinators. Smaller, spreading lower lips provide a landing platform for insects during the pollination process.

Each inflorescence displays a sequence of bracts that partially sheath the individual flowers along the stem.
