Rosa glauca var. laevigata

Rosa glauca var. laevigata, also known as the Purple-leafed Wild Rose, is a shrub that belongs to the rose family and is native to the Himalayan region.

The plant is used for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, making it useful in treating various health issues such as fever, cough, and digestive problems.

The health benefits of Rosa glauca var. laevigata include its ability to reduce inflammation, improve respiratory health, and act as an antimicrobial agent.
