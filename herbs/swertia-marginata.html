Swertia marginata

What's the taxonomical classification of Swertia marginata?

Swertia marginata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and falls into the order Gentianaales. This plant is a member of the family Gentianaceae, specifically situated within the genus Swertia, and is identified by its unique species designation, marginata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Swertia marginata has opposite, simple, elliptical leaves with entire, slightly revolute margins and a prominent midrib. The foliage is typically bright green and possesses a smooth, somewhat coriaceous texture. Its small, actinomorphic flowers are white or cream-colored and emerge from the leaf axils in small clusters. The plant develops small, woody fruits that eventually split to release tiny seeds. This shrubby species often exhibits a dense, branched growth habit.
