Parnassian Hawthorn (Crataegus Pycnoloba Var. Parnassica)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Crataegus pycnoloba var. parnassica.

Parnassian hawthorn, scientifically known as Crataegus pycnoloba var. parnassica, is a rare medicinal herb native to the high altitudes of the Carpathian Mountains, valued for its adaptogenic and cardiovascular benefits. This plant is traditionally used to support heart health, reduce anxiety, and enhance mental clarity, owing to its rich content of flavonoids and antioxidants. In traditional medicine, it has been employed by local communities to treat hypertension, insomnia, and digestive ailments, often prepared as a tincture or infusion. Modern wellness practices now incorporate it as a natural supplement for stress relief and circulatory support, with research highlighting its potential in managing cardiovascular conditions. Its unique feature lies in its rare active compound, crataegoside, which contributes to its distinctive tart flavor and historical use in alpine herbal remedies.
