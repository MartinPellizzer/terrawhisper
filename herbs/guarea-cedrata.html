Guarea cedrata

What's the taxonomical classification of Guarea cedrata?

Guarea cedrata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows a taxonomic lineage that places it under the order Sapindales. Further classification identifies it as a member of the family Meliaceae, specifically within the genus Guarea, with its unique designation being the species cedrata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Guarea cedrata has an arborescent growth habit characterized by a straight trunk and a crown of large, pinnately compound leaves that typically feature 5 to 15 pairs of opposite leaflets. These leaflets are elliptical to oblong in shape, possessing smooth margins and a glossy, dark green upper surface. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that are arranged in axillary panicles. Following pollination, the plant develops fleshy, yellowish-white drupes that contain a single large seed. The bark is generally smooth or finely textured, providing a protective layer for the woody structure of the species.
