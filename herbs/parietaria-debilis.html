Parietaria debilis

What's the taxonomical classification of Parietaria debilis?

Parietarica debilis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Rosales. This plant is further categorized into the family Urticaceae, which contains the genus Parietaria, and is specifically identified by its species name, debilis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Parietararia debilis has slender, scrambling stems that are branched and often prostrate or climbing with a diameter typically ranging from 1 to 2 millimeters. The leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, ovate to elliptic leaflets that are oppositely arranged along the stem. These leaflets exhibit entire margins and can be either glabrous or slightly pubescent depending on the specific environment. The inflorescence is characterized by small, inconspicuous green flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Small, nut-like fruits develop from these flowers to facilitate the plant's reproductive cycle.
