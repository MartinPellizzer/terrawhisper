St. John'S Wort (Artemisia judaica)

St. John'S Wort (Artemisia judaica) is a member of the Asteraceae family, native to Middle East, North Africa, and Southern Europe. Traditionally, its leaves, stems, and resin have been used for infusions, decoctions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its bitter, anti-inflammatory, and carminative actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and african traditional medicine.

Perennial herbaceous plant.

Leaves
Simple leaves with upper surface pale green and lower surface gray-green, bearing prominent stomatal bands.
Flowers
Small yellow flowers arranged in dense spherical heads, actinomorphic, with four to five ray florets and numerous tubular disc florets, yellow to orange coloration, with glandular pappus hairs.
Stems
Cylindrical, woody, branched, with opposite, slightly hairy leaves and terminal inflorescences.
