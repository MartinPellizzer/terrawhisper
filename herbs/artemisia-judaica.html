St. John's Wort

Artemisia judaica

St. John's Wort (Artemisia judaica) is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, known for its historical use in traditional medicine to treat various ailments, particularly depression and mild to moderate mood disorders.

Botanical Identification

St. John's Wort, scientifically known as Hypericum perforatum, belongs to the Clusiaceae family. It is also called Artemisia judaica in some regions and is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North America. Active Compounds

St. John's Wort contains key active compounds such as terpenoids, essential oils, and flavonoids. These compounds work together to exert their therapeutic effects, with terpenoids playing a central role in its antidepressant properties. Flavonoids may enhance the herb's bioavailability and contribute to its overall efficacy.

St. John's Wort Actions.

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory ailments such as coughs and colds.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and to alleviate gastrointestinal discomfort.

Extract: Used for skin conditions and as an antiseptic.

Safety Profile

St. John's Wort (Artemisia judaica) is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may interact with certain medications, including antidepressants and anticoagulants. It is contraindicated in individuals with a history of seizures or those taking blood thinners due to potential adverse effects.
