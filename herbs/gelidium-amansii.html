Side Effects of gelidium amansii

Gelidium amansii may cause allergic reaction in individuals who are sensitive to its components, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, or even anaphylaxis in severe cases.

The herb can also cause skin irritation, resulting in redness, rashes, or a burning sensation upon contact with the skin. Some users may experience nausea or even vomiting, particularly if the herb is ingested in large quantities or on an empty stomach.

It can lead to stomach upset, characterized by bloating, cramping, or digestive discomfort, which may worsen with continued use. Additionally, Gelidium amansii may result in eye or throat irritation, causing redness, watering eyes, or a sore throat, while some people may also experience dizziness or a dry cough as secondary effects.

These side effects highlight the importance of consulting a healthcare professional before using this herb, especially for individuals with a history of allergies or gastrointestinal sensitivities.
