Cancer Bush (Sutherlandia Humilis)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Sutherlandia humilis.

Cancer bush, scientifically known as Sutherlandia humilis, is a medicinal herb native to southern Africa, widely recognized for its adaptogenic properties and traditional use in supporting overall wellness. It is traditionally used by indigenous communities in South Africa to treat cancer, fatigue, and respiratory ailments, with its roots and stems being the primary parts used. The plant contains a unique compound called corymbuline, which has shown potential in supporting immune function and reducing oxidative stress, making it a subject of modern wellness research. Today, Cancer bush is increasingly incorporated into herbal supplements and holistic treatments for stress management and immune support, reflecting its enduring cultural and medicinal significance. Its distinctive yellow flowers and drought-resistant nature also make it a notable plant in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
