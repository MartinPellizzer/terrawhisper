Asplenium crinicaule

What's the taxonomical classification of Asplenium crinicaule?

Asplenium crinicaule belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is classified under the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. This plant is a member of the family Aspleniaceae, falling specifically within the genus Asplenium, with its unique designation as the species crinicaule.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Asplenium crinicaule has a creeping rhizome that supports long, narrow, lanceolate fronds which can reach lengths of up to 60 centimeters. The leaf blades are typically dark green with a glossy texture and possess entire margins. These fronds are arranged in a somewhat loose rosette pattern emerging from the central rhizome. The venation within the lamina is free and characteristic of many ferns in this genus. Small, inconspicuous sori are located on the undersides of the mature leaves for reproduction.
