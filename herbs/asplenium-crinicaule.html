Asplenium crinicaule

What's the taxonomical classification of Asplenium crinicaule?

Asplenium crinicaule belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is classified under the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. This plant is a member of the family Aspleniaceae, falling specifically within the genus Asplenium, with its unique designation as the species crinicaule.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Asplenium crinicaule targets various inflammatory enzymes and oxidative stress proteins such as cyclooxygenase-2, lipoxygenase, and superoxide dismutase. These interactions are primarily mediated by bioactive secondary metabolites like flavonoids and phenolic acids found within the plant tissues. By inhibiting cyclooxygenase-2, the plant effectively reduces the synthesis of pro-inflammatory prostaglandins, which provides its analgesic properties. Additionally, its ability to modulate antioxidant enzymes helps mitigate cellular damage caused by reactive oxygen species. These complex biochemical pathways collectively contribute to the plant's demonstrated anti-inflammatory and cytoprotective medicinal effects.

    Asplenium crinicaule can cause localized skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, or mild digestive distress if ingested. Contact with the fronds might result in redness, itching, or a rash on the hands and arms. If any part of the plant is consumed accidentally, it could lead to nausea or abdominal discomfort. It is always advisable to handle this species with caution to prevent unexpected physiological reactions.
