Pilocarpus pennatifolius

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family

Botanical Identification

Pilocarpus pennatifolius, commonly known as the Pilocarpus tree, is a member of the Rubiaceae family. Native to South America, it is a small evergreen tree with opposite, elliptic leaves and yellowish flowers. It is also referred to as "Jurema-preta" in Brazil and has been historically used in traditional medicine.

Active Compounds

The Pilocarpus Tree contains key bioactive compounds such as alkaloids, flavonoids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to enhance its therapeutic effects, particularly in stimulating bodily functions and supporting traditional herbal applications.

  • Alkaloid
  • Flavonoid
  • Terpenoid

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action
Visual Glaucoma, cataracts Cholinergic agonist, antioxidant
Salivary Dry mouth Stimulant
Urinary Urinary retention, bladder spasms, bladder infections Anticholinergic, antispasmodic, antimicrobial

Preparation Methods

Tincture: Used for treating glaucoma and eye conditions due to its miotic effects.

Decoction: Used for respiratory infections and as a stimulant.

Extract: Used for enhancing mental alertness and treating adhd-like symptoms.

Safety Profile

Pilocarpus pennatifolius is generally considered safe when used in recommended dosages. However, it may cause side effects such as sweating, nausea, and increased heart rate. It is contraindicated in individuals with asthma, glaucoma, or those taking certain medications due to potential interactions.
