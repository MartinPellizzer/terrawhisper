Hyptis spicigera

What's the taxonomical classification of Hyptis spicigera?

Hyptis spicigera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down its taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is part of the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Hyptis, and its species designation, spicigera.

What are the pharmacological activities of Hyptis spicigera?

This plant has anticonvulsant, antiparasitic, and sedative pharmacological activities that have been documented through various scientific studies. Its anticonvulsant properties suggest a potential role in managing seizure activity by modulating neurological responses. The presence of antiparasitic compounds indicates that extracts from the plant can effectively combat certain parasitic infections. Additionally, its sedative effects point toward its utility in reducing anxiety or promoting sleep. These diverse biological actions demonstrate the significant medicinal potential inherent in Hyptis spicigera.

According to a study published by "Epilepsy & behavior : E&B", Hyptis spicigera was evaluated for its anticonvulsant and sedative activities in mice. At a dose of 160 mg/kg, the plant protected 100% of mice against strychnine-induced convulsions. It also protected 87.5% of mice against pentylenetetrazoal-induced convulsions at the same 160 mg/kg dose. Additionally, Hyptis spicigera exerted sedative activity by strongly increasing the total duration of sleep induced by diazepam.
