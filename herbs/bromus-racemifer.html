Bromus racemifer

Bromus racemifer, commonly known as dropseed or spike dropseed, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

This plant has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, which include its anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and expectorant properties, helping to relieve respiratory issues such as bronchitis, asthma, and coughs.

The bioactive constituents present in Bromus racemifer include phenolic compounds, flavonoids, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its therapeutic actions and health benefits.

The plant's extracts have been traditionally used to treat digestive issues, reduce fever, and alleviate symptoms of colds and flu.

The roots of Bromus racemifer contain saponins, which have been traditionally used to treat digestive issues and respiratory problems.

The seeds of Bromus racemifer contain phenolic compounds, including ferulic and sinapic acids, which have been shown to possess antimicrobial and antioxidant activities.
