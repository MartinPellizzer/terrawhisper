Common Honeysuckle (Lonicera periclymenum) Uses

Common Honeysuckle is used to treat respiratory conditions such as cough, cold symptoms, and upper respiratory infections. It helps reduce inflammation in airways and may alleviate symptoms of bronchitis and asthma. It is also applied for allergic rhinitis and common colds due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

Common Honeysuckle (Lonicera periclymenum) Monograph

1. Cough

Common Honeysuckle is used to soothe coughs due to its anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties. It helps reduce throat irritation and suppress persistent coughing. Typically prepared as a tea, it is valued for its mild and effective action on respiratory discomfort.

2. Cold symptoms

Common Honeysuckle is used to alleviate cold symptoms such as fever, sore throat, and cough. It helps reduce inflammation and clear heat from the body. Traditionally, it is prepared as a tea or decoction to support respiratory health during colds. Its cooling properties make it effective for treating upper respiratory infections.

3. Respiratory infections

Common Honeysuckle is used to treat respiratory infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce symptoms like coughing and sore throat by supporting immune function and clearing mucus. Traditionally, it is prepared as a tea or tincture to alleviate mild to moderate respiratory ailments.

4. Inflamed airways

Common Honeysuckle is used to reduce inflammation in airways by its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps alleviate symptoms of respiratory conditions like bronchitis and asthma. The plant's berries and flowers are often prepared as teas or tinctures for this purpose. It supports clearing mucus and soothing irritated airway tissues.

5. Upper respiratory infection

Common Honeysuckle is used to treat upper respiratory infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce symptoms like sore throat and cough by supporting the body's immune response. Often prepared as a tea or tincture, it is a traditional remedy for mild to moderate infections.

6. Allergic rhinitis

Common Honeysuckle is used in herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistaminic properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion, sneezing, and runny nose by modulating immune responses. Typically prepared as a tincture or tea, it supports respiratory health during seasonal allergies.

7. Bronchitis

Common Honeysuckle is used in herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms of bronchitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce mucus production and soothe irritated airways, often prepared as a tea or tincture. Its expectorant qualities support clearing congestion and easing breathing in respiratory infections.

8. Asthma

Common Honeysuckle (Lonicera periclymenum) has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health. Its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties may help alleviate asthma symptoms by reducing airway inflammation and promoting mucus clearance. However, it should be used under professional guidance as part of a comprehensive treatment plan.

9. Common cold

Common Honeysuckle is traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of the common cold due to its antiviral and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce fever, soothe sore throats, and ease congestion. Often prepared as a tea, it supports the body's natural healing process during mild viral infections.

10. Upper respiratory tract infections

Common Honeysuckle is used to alleviate symptoms of upper respiratory tract infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce fever, soothe sore throats, and ease congestion. Typically prepared as a tea or tincture, it supports the body's natural defenses during colds and flu. Its effectiveness is supported by traditional use and some modern herbal studies.
