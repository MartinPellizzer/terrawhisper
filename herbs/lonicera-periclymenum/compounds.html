Common Honeysuckle (Lonicera periclymenum) Compounds

The primary compounds in Common Honeysuckle include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and phenolic acids, known for their anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins and saponins contribute to its astringent and detoxifying qualities. Coumarin and terpenoids provide antimicrobial benefits, while anthocyanins offer protective effects against oxidative stress.

Common Honeysuckle (Lonicera periclymenum) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Common Honeysuckle (Lonicera periclymenum). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for conditions like colds and respiratory infections. These compounds help modulate immune responses and protect cells from oxidative damage.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Common Honeysuckle (Lonicera periclymenum). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress in the body. This compound supports the plant's traditional use in promoting health and treating inflammatory conditions.

3. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Common Honeysuckle (Lonicera periclymenum). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support wound healing and reduce inflammation. Tannins also help preserve the plant's medicinal qualities by stabilizing its active components.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Common Honeysuckle (Lonicera periclymenum). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine, particularly for its anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarin is found in the flowers and berries of this plant. Its presence supports the plant's role in treating cardiovascular and inflammatory conditions.

5. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Common Honeysuckle (Lonicera periclymenum) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting the plant's traditional use in treating infections and respiratory conditions. They are also responsible for the plant's characteristic aroma and flavor.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Common Honeysuckle (Lonicera periclymenum). It contributes to the plant's antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Glycosides are often responsible for the therapeutic effects observed in traditional herbal medicine. They are commonly extracted and used in formulations for their medicinal benefits.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Common Honeysuckle (Lonicera periclymenum). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in reducing inflammation and supporting immune function. Saponins may enhance the plant's therapeutic effects through their mild detergent properties and potential antioxidant activity.

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of Common Honeysuckle (Lonicera periclymenum) contains volatile compounds like methyl salicylate and limonene. These compounds contribute to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, making it useful in herbal medicine for treating respiratory and skin infections.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin, found in Common Honeysuckle (Lonicera periclymenum), is a flavonoid pigment responsible for the plant's vibrant flower colors. It exhibits antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, contributing to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for conditions like inflammation and fever.

10. Alkaloid

Active compounds of Common Honeysuckle (Lonicera periclymenum) include alkaloids, which are nitrogen-containing organic compounds. These alkaloids contribute to the plant's pharmacological properties, such as antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. They are often responsible for the plant's therapeutic uses in herbal medicine.
