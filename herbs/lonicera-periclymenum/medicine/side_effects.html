1. Causes allergic reactions

Lonicera periclymenum causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

This is due to the presence of allergenic compounds such as flavonoids and terpenes, which are naturally occurring chemicals within the plant's leaves and stems. These substances can trigger an immune response in susceptible people, leading to symptoms like skin irritation, respiratory issues, and even systemic reactions.

The severity of these reactions varies widely depending on individual sensitivities and exposure levels.

2. Triggers asthmatic attacks

Lonicera periclymenum triggers asthmatic attacks due to its high concentration of allergenic proteins and volatile compounds.

These chemicals can bind with IgE antibodies in the bloodstream, leading to an overactive immune response and inflammation in the airways.

As a result, individuals may experience wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath, characteristic symptoms of asthma.

3. Induces vomiting

Lonicera periclymenum induces vomiting when ingested due to its toxic compounds, such as saponins and alkaloids.

These bioactive substances can irritate the gastrointestinal tract, leading to nausea and subsequent vomiting.

The plant's leaves, stems, flowers, and berries are all capable of causing this adverse reaction, making it essential to exercise caution when handling or consuming Lonicera periclymenum.

4. Increases blood pressure

Lonicera periclymenum increases blood pressure due to its unique combination of bioactive compounds.

The flavonoids and phenolic acids present in the plant's leaves and flowers are responsible for this effect. These compounds cause a rise in blood pressure by stimulating the sympathetic nervous system and increasing vascular resistance.

As a result, individuals who consume Lonicera periclymenum may experience an increase in their systolic blood pressure.

5. Lowers blood sugar

Lonicera periclymenum lowers blood sugar due to its active compound, flavonoids, which have been shown to increase glucose uptake in the body and reduce glucose production.

Additionally, the plant's alkaloids, such as magnoflorine, may inhibit the digestion of carbohydrates, leading to a decrease in blood sugar levels.

These properties make Lonicera periclymenum a potentially useful natural remedy for managing type 2 diabetes, although further research is needed to fully understand its effects on blood sugar regulation.

6. Interacts with medications

Lonicera periclymenum interacts with medications that are metabolized by the cytochrome P450 enzyme system, such as warfarin and statins.

This can result in increased or decreased effects of these medications, potentially leading to adverse reactions.

The flavonoids and phenolic acids present in Lonicera periclymenum may bind to the enzymes, altering their activity and affecting the metabolism of concomitantly administered drugs.

7. Disrupts digestive rhythms

Lonicera periclymenum disrupts digestive rhythms due to its ability to stimulate the digestive system and alter the balance of gut bacteria.

The flavonoids present in Lonicera periclymenum may cause an increase in stomach contractions and secretion of digestive enzymes, leading to changes in bowel habits and potentially causing discomfort or irregularity.

This can be attributed to its traditional use as a folk remedy for digestive issues.

8. Causes skin irritation

Lonicera periclymenum causes skin irritation due to its milky sap and volatile oils.

The sap contains sesquiterpenes and terpenoids that can cause allergic reactions, leading to redness, itching, and blistering on the skin.

Additionally, the oil's fragrant compounds can irritate mucous membranes, causing respiratory issues and contact dermatitis in some individuals.

9. Elicits anxiety symptoms

Lonicera periclymenum elicits anxiety symptoms due to its potential interactions with certain neurotransmitters in the brain.

The plant's constituents, including flavonoids and alkaloids, can affect the body's natural serotonin levels, leading to feelings of nervousness and unease.

Additionally, the sweet and alluring aroma of honeysuckle has been shown to trigger a fight-or-flight response in some individuals, further exacerbating anxiety symptoms.

10. Provokes insomnia

Lonicera periclymenum provokes insomnia due to its containing compounds that can affect the central nervous system.

The plant's active ingredients, including flavonoids and alkaloids, may alter serotonin levels in the brain, leading to difficulty falling asleep or staying asleep.

Additionally, the sweet, intoxicating aroma of honeysuckle can stimulate the brain's reward centers, making it challenging for individuals to relax and wind down before bedtime, further exacerbating insomnia issues.
