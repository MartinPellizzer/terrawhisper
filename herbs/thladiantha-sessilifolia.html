Thladiantha sessilifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Thladiantha sessilifolia has small, sessile leaves that lack petioles and are arranged in an opposite pattern along its slender, herbaceous stems. The leaf blades are typically ovate or elliptic in shape with entire margins. These leaves are often somewhat fleshy or succulent in texture, reflecting adaptations to its specific environment. The plant also features tiny, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils.
