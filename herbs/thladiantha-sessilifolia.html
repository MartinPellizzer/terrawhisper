Thladiantha sessilifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Thladiantha sessilifolia?

Thladiantha sessilifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. As a member of the order Cucurbitales, it falls into the family Cucurbitaceae, which is a group well-known for its diverse melon and gourd species. Specifically, this plant is identified by the genus Thladiantha and is distinguished as the species sessilifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Thladiantha sessilifolia has small, sessile leaves that lack petioles and are arranged in an opposite pattern along its slender, herbaceous stems.

The leaf blades are typically ovate or elliptic in shape with entire margins. These leaves are often somewhat fleshy or succulent in texture, reflecting adaptations to its specific environment. The plant also features tiny, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils.

Its overall structure is characterized by a low-growing or creeping habit common to many members of the Amaranthaceae family.
