Paulownia tomentosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Paulownia tomentosa has large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in dense, velvety gray hairs on both the upper and lower surfaces. These thick, felt-like trichomes provide a distinctive soft texture and help the plant retain moisture. The foliage can grow to considerable sizes, often reaching widths of up to thirty centimeters. As the plant matures, the leaves transition from a bright green to a more muted, grayish-green hue due to the hair density. This morphological trait is a key identifier that distinguishes the species from other members of its genus.

    What are the herbal preparations of Paulownia tomentosa?

    Paulownia tomentoosa is prepared as decoctions, infusions, and topical poultices utilizing the bark, leaves, or flowers of the plant. The bark is commonly boiled in water to create a strong decoction used for internal medicinal purposes. Leaves are often steeped in hot water to form an infusion that serves as a gentle tea.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Paulownia tomentoosa can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and respiratory distress if its fine hairs or pollen are inhaled or touched. The fuzzy, tomentose leaves are covered in tiny trichomes that may act as physical irritants to sensitive skin upon contact. Individuals with known allergies to various plant pollens might experience sneezing, coughing, or itchy eyes when near the blooming tree. Ingestion of the plant is generally discouraged as the specific toxicological effects on humans have not been extensively documented in clinical studies. It is essential to wear protective clothing when handling the foliage to avoid these localized reactions.
