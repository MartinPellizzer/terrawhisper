What's the taxonomical classification of Sonchus brachyotus?

Sonchus brachyotus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the hierarchical structure of the order Asterales. This plant is a representative of the family Asteraceae, a large and diverse group of flowering plants, and is specifically categorized under the genus Sonchus. Ultimately, its unique biological identity is defined by its specific designation as the species brachyotus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sonchus brachyotus has an erect, herbaceous stem that branches upward to support deeply lobed, alternate leaves with irregularly serrated margins. The foliage typically displays a somewhat fleshy texture and can vary in shape from entire to pinnatifid depending on the maturity of the plant. Small, yellow flower heads are clustered in terminal corymbose cymes, consisting of numerous ligulate florets. Each flower head is surrounded by an involucre of graduated phyllaries that protect the developing seeds. The fruit consists of small, cylindrical achenes topped with a pappus of fine hairs used for wind dispersal.
