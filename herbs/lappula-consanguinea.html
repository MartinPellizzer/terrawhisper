What's the taxonomical classification of Lappula consanguinea?

Lappula consanguinea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical classification, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Boraginales, it is a member of the family Boraginaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Lappula, with its specific designation being the species consanguinea.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and potential toxicity if ingested due to the presence of pyrrolizidine alkaloids. These chemical compounds are known to cause serious liver damage or even liver failure in humans and animals. Contact with the plant's foliage or stems may result in contact dermatitis, characterized by redness and itching. Individuals with sensitive skin should avoid direct contact to prevent these dermatological issues. Furthermore, any accidental consumption poses a significant health risk that requires immediate medical attention.
