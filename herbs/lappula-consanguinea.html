Lappula consanguinea

What's the taxonomical classification of Lappula consanguinea?

Lappula consanguinea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical classification, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Boraginales, it is a member of the family Boraginaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Lappula, with its specific designation being the species consanguinea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lappula consanguinea has erect or ascending stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and are covered in fine, spreading hairs. The leaves are simple, narrow, and lanceolate in shape, arranged alternately along the stem with a distinct midrib. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils and possess a tubular corolla that is often pale blue or white. The plant produces small, nutlet-like fruits that are characteristic of the Boraginaceae family. These reproductive structures are often tucked closely against the stem or leaf bases.
