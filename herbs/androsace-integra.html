Androsace integra

What's the taxonomical classification of Androsace integra?

Androsace integra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further categorized under the order Ericales. It is a member of the family Primulaceae, situated within the genus Androsace, and is specifically identified by its species name, integra.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Androsace integra has a perennial growth habit characterized by a small, tufted rosette of fleshy, spatulate to obovate leaves that typically measure between 5 and 15 millimeters in length.

The leaf margins are entire and often possess a slightly thickened or succulent texture to aid in moisture retention. From the center of this basal rosette, slender, leafless flowering stalks emerge to support small, solitary flowers. These flowers are actinomorphic with a tubular corolla that spreads into five distinct lobes, often displaying a pale pink or white coloration.

The overall structure is compact, allowing the plant to thrive in rocky or alpine environments.
