Desmodium velutinum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Desmodium velutinum has trifoliate leaves with velvety, pubescent undersides and small, pale pink to lavender pea-like flowers arranged in dense racemes. The stems are typically slender and covered in fine hairs that provide a soft texture to the overall structure. Each leaf consists of three leaflets that are generally ovate to elliptic in shape. The inflorescence emerges from the leaf axils and features numerous tiny blossoms clustered together. This plant also produces small, flattened seed pods that are characteristic of the Fabaceae family.

What are the therapeutic actions of Desmodium velutinum?

Desmodium velutinum has astringent, hemostatic, anti-inflammatory, and antiseptic therapeutic actions that make it effective for treating various skin conditions and minor wounds. Furthermore, the anti-inflammatory effects help to minimize swelling and soothe irritation in affected areas. Finally, the antiseptic properties work to prevent infection by inhibiting the growth of harmful microorganisms on the skin surface.
