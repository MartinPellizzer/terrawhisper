Cyperus glomeratus

What's the taxonomical classification of Cyperus glomeratus?

Cyperus glomeratus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Poales and is a member of the family Cyperaceae. Finally, it is categorized within the genus Cyperus, with its specific designation being the species glomeratus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cyperus glomeratus has a tufted growth habit characterized by triangular, compressed stems that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters. The leaves are narrow, linear, and emerge from the base in dense clusters. At the apex of each stem, the inflorescence consists of several compact, head-like clusters of spikelets arranged in a terminal group. These spikelets are typically dark brown or purplish and possess small, pointed scales. The entire plant lacks true lateral branches, relying instead on its central flowering stalks for reproduction.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires constant moisture and prefers being grown in waterlogged soil or shallow aquatic environments to thrive.

You should plant it in full sun to partial shade to ensure vigorous growth of its tufted green foliage. Because it is a hardy sedge, it can tolerate a wide range of temperatures as long as its roots remain submerged. To manage its spread, it is best to cultivate it in contained vessels since its rhizomes can expand rapidly in open garden beds.
