Eleutherococcus verticillatus

What's the taxonomical classification of Eleutherococcus verticillatus?

Eleutherococcus verticillatus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its hierarchical descent, the plant is part of the order Apiales and the family Araliaceae. Finally, its taxonomic position is completed by its classification within the genus Eleutherococcus and its specific designation as the species verticillatus.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Eleutherococcus verticillatus contains a complex mixture of bioactive compounds including eleutherosides, triterpene acids, polysaccharides, and various lignans. These eleutherosides act as primary adaptogens that help the human body resist physical and environmental stressors. The specific lignan profiles within the plant contribute to its ability to modulate the immune system and enhance metabolic functions. Additionally, the presence of polysaccharides supports cellular defense mechanisms and helps maintain overall homeostasis. These diverse chemical constituents work synergistically to provide restorative effects on the nervous and endocrine systems.

What are the therapeutic actions of Eleutherococcus verticillatus?

Eleutherococcus verticillatus has adaptogenic properties that help the body resist physical, chemical, and biological stressors by regulating the hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal axis. This plant functions by increasing non-specific resistance to various environmental challenges and improving overall metabolic efficiency. It is commonly used to enhance physical stamina, reduce fatigue, and boost the performance of the immune system during periods of exhaustion. Some studies suggest it can also support cognitive function and assist in maintaining hormonal balance during chronic stress. Regular consumption may improve blood lipid profiles and increase the body's ability to utilize glucose more effectively.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat conditions related to physical exhaustion, chronic fatigue, and weakened immune system responses.
