What's the taxonomical classification of Viola yunnanfuensis?

Viola yunnanfuensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Malpighiales. This plant is further classified within the family Violaceae and the genus Viola, ultimately reaching its specific identification at the species level as yunnanfuensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viola yunnanfuensis has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by basal leaves that are broadly ovate to cordate with crenate margins and a zygomorphic flower featuring five violet to purplish petals. The leaves are typically petiolate and possess long stalks that emerge from a short rhizome. Its inflorescence consists of single flowers borne on slender peduncles that rise above the foliage. The petals are distinctively shaped, with the lateral petals often being slightly smaller than the larger anterior ones. The reproductive structure includes a capsule that dehisces to release numerous small seeds upon maturity.
