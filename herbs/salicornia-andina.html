Andean Glasswort (Salicornia Andina)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Salicornia andina.

Andean glasswort, scientifically known as Salicornia andina, is a hardy medicinal herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes, often found growing in saline soils and rocky terrains. This plant is valued as an adaptogen and a nutrient-rich spice, known for its ability to support the body's resilience to stress and environmental challenges. Traditionally, it has been used by Andean communities for its diuretic, anti-inflammatory, and digestive benefits, often incorporated into teas and culinary dishes. In modern wellness practices, it is gaining attention for its potential to support kidney health, detoxification, and immune function, thanks to its high content of minerals like potassium and antioxidants. Unique to this species is its distinctively salty and slightly sweet flavor, which resembles that of sea vegetables, and its historical significance as a staple in the diets of indigenous peoples for centuries.
