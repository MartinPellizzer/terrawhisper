Noni (Morinda citrifolia) Compounds

The primary compounds in Noni include flavonoids, which are antioxidants, and phenolic acids, known for their anti-inflammatory properties. Tannins and glycosides contribute to its astringent and medicinal effects. Terpenoids and coumarin offer anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial benefits, while saponins and anthocyanins support immune and cardiovascular health. Essential oils and alkaloids add to its diverse therapeutic potential.

Noni (Morinda citrifolia) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Noni (Morinda citrifolia). These compounds contribute to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting its traditional use in promoting health and wellness. They help neutralize free radicals and may enhance the plant's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Noni (Morinda citrifolia). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports the plant's traditional use in promoting health and combating inflammation.

3. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Noni (Morinda citrifolia). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support wound healing and inflammation reduction. Tannins in Noni are believed to enhance the plant's traditional uses in herbal medicine.

4. Glycoside

Glycosides are a class of compounds found in Noni (Morinda citrifolia). These compounds contribute to the plant's therapeutic properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Glycosides in Noni are believed to support immune function and may aid in reducing oxidative stress in the body.

5. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Noni (Morinda citrifolia). These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine, offering anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and immune-modulating properties. Specific terpenoids like damnacanthal and scopoletin are studied for their potential therapeutic effects.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a bioactive compound found in Noni (Morinda citrifolia). It exhibits anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory properties, contributing to the plant's traditional use in treating ailments like inflammation and digestive issues. Coumarin's role in Noni's therapeutic effects highlights its significance in herbal medicine.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Noni (Morinda citrifolia). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in promoting health and may have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Saponin's presence supports Noni's role in herbal medicine for various therapeutic applications.

8. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound found in Noni (Morinda citrifolia). It contributes to the fruit's vibrant color and possesses antioxidant properties. These antioxidants help neutralize free radicals, supporting overall health and potentially reducing oxidative stress in the body.

9. Essential oil

The essential oil of Noni (Morinda citrifolia) contains volatile compounds like limonene and cineole, which contribute to its aromatic properties. These components may support digestive health and have antimicrobial effects, though research on their specific therapeutic applications is ongoing.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a bioactive compound found in Noni (Morinda citrifolia). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Alkaloids in Noni may support various therapeutic applications, though research on their specific mechanisms is ongoing.
