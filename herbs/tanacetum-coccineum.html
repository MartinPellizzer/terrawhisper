Tanacetum coccineum

What's the taxonomical classification of Tanacetum coccineum?

Tanacetum coccineum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. This plant is a representative of the family Asteraceae, a diverse group of flowering plants, and is specifically categorized under the genus Tanacetum. Finally, its unique biological identity is defined by its specific epithet, making its full scientific name Tanacetum coccineum.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Tanacetum coccineum contains bioactive terpenoids, flavonoids, and sesquiterpene lactones that provide its therapeutic potential. These chemical constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Specifically, the presence of various essential oils allows the species to exhibit antimicrobial effects against certain pathogens. Researchers often study these compounds to understand their role in regulating cellular responses to oxidative stress. Such phytochemical profiles make the plant a subject of interest in ethnobotanical pharmacology.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat digestive issues, inflammatory conditions, and certain types of skin irritations. Its medicinal properties are often harnessed to alleviate stomach discomfort and promote better gastrointestinal function. Some traditional practices also utilize the plant to address minor external ailments through topical applications. The bioactive compounds within the leaves contribute to its reputation in folk medicine for managing various systemic imbalances. Careful application is necessary because its potency can lead to adverse reactions if not used correctly.
