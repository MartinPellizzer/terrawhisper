Tanacetum coccineum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tanacetum coccineum has upright, perennial stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters and feature deeply lobed, fern-like leaves that are dark green in color. The foliage is arranged alternately along the stems and possesses a distinctively serrated or pinnatifid structure. At the apex of each stem, the plant produces clusters of daisy-like flower heads characterized by bright red ray florets surrounding yellow disc florets. These inflorescences are held in corymb-like arrangements, creating a dense display of color throughout the blooming season. The overall growth habit is bushy and compact, forming a mounded shape as the plant matures.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Tanacetum coccineum targets inflammatory enzymes such as cyclooxygenase-2 and lipoxygenase, as well as various pro-inflammatory cytokines. These bioactive compounds work by inhibiting the production of prostaglandins and leukotrienes, which are key mediators of the pain and swelling response. The plant's terpenoids and flavonoids also interact with nuclear factor kappa B to suppress systemic inflammatory signaling pathways. By modulating these specific protein interactions, the extracts exert significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects on cellular processes. These molecular mechanisms underlie the plant's traditional use in treating skin irritations and various inflammatory conditions.

What are the therapeutic actions of Tanacetum coccineum?

Tanacetum coccineum has therapeutic actions as a bitter stomachic, emmenagogue, and antispasmodic agent used to stimulate digestion and regulate menstrual cycles. Its bitter properties help to increase gastric secretions and improve overall appetite. As a stomachic, the plant supports healthy digestive function by promoting the movement of food through the gastrointestinal tract. The emmenagogue qualities allow it to stimulate blood flow to the pelvic region, which can assist in inducing regular menstruation. Additionally, its antispasmodic effects provide relief from painful muscle contractions and cramping.
