Raphistemma pulchellum

What's the taxonomical classification of Raphistemma pulchellum?

Raphistemma pulchellum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchy further, it falls under the order Gentianales and is a member of the family Apocynaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Raphistemma, and its species designation, pulchellum.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Raphistemma pulchellum can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and localized swelling if the plant's sap or tissues come into direct contact with human skin. While specific toxicological studies on humans are limited, the presence of certain phytochemicals suggests a potential for dermatitis in sensitive individuals. It is advisable to avoid handling the plant without gloves to prevent these dermatological responses. Monitoring for any unexpected systemic reactions is also recommended if ingestion occurs.
