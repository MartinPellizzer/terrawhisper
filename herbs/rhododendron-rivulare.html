What's the taxonomical classification of Rhododendron rivulare?

Rhododendron rivulare belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Ericales and is a member of the family Ericaceae. Finally, it is placed within the genus Rhododendron, specifically under the species name rivulare.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhododendron rivulare has evergreen, leathery leaves that are oblong to lanceolate in shape, measuring 5 to 15 centimeters in length, and possess a smooth, dark green upper surface with a paler underside. The plant typically grows as a spreading shrub, reaching heights and widths of up to 1.5 meters. During the blooming season, it produces large, bell-shaped flowers that are pale pink to white in color. These blossoms grow in dense terminal clusters known as trusses, which can emerge from the leaf axils. The stems are woody and become increasingly robust as the plant matures over several years.
