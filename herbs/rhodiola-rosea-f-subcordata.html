Golden Root (Rhodiola Rosea F. Subcordata)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Rhodiola rosea f. subcordata.

Golden root, scientifically known as Rhodiola rosea f. subcordata, is a rare and potent medicinal herb native to the cold regions of the Arctic and alpine areas, often referred to as an adaptogen due to its ability to help the body resist stress. It is traditionally used in traditional Chinese medicine and Russian folk remedies to enhance vitality, improve mental clarity, and boost physical endurance. The primary benefits of golden root include reducing fatigue, enhancing cognitive function, and supporting mood regulation, making it a popular choice in modern wellness practices. Its unique features include a distinctive earthy and slightly sweet flavor, as well as the presence of rare active compounds such as rosavins and salidrosides, which contribute to its adaptogenic properties. Historically, it was revered by Siberian shamans and used to enhance strength and resilience in harsh environments.
