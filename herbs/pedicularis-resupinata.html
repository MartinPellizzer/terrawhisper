Pedicularis resupinata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pedicularis resupinata has leaves that are deeply pinnatifid with lobes that curve backward or upward toward the stem. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves that are often larger and more complex than the smaller, more sparse leaves found along the stem. Its inflorescence is a dense spike composed of tubular, bilabiate flowers that exhibit a distinct hooded upper lip. The stem is typically erect and can vary in height depending on the specific environmental conditions of its alpine habitat. These botanical characteristics are essential for distinguishing this species from other members of the lousewort genus.
