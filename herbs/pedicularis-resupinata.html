Pedicularis resupinata

What's the taxonomical classification of Pedicularis resupinata?

Pedicularis resupinata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, following the biological order Lamiales. Within this lineage, the plant is a member of the family Orobanchaceae and is categorized under the genus Pedicularis, with its specific designation being the species resupinata.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Pedicularis resupinata can cause adverse physiological reactions including nausea, dizziness, and digestive distress if ingested by humans or livestock. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, many species within the Pedicularis genus contain iridoid glycosides which may act as irritants to the gastrointestinal tract. Contact with the plant might also lead to localized skin irritation or allergic dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Because the chemical profile of this specific species is not fully documented for medical safety, accidental consumption should be treated as potentially toxic.
