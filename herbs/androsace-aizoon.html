Androsace aizoon

What's the taxonomical classification of Androsace aizoon?

Androsace aizoon belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Ericales and is a member of the family Primulaceae. Specifically, it is identified by the genus Androsace, with its unique designation as the species aizoon.

This versatile plant serves as a natural remedy for several different internal and external health concerns.

What are the herbal preparations of Androsace aizoon?

Androsace aizoon is prepared primarily as a decoction, powder, or tincture derived from its dried roots. Tinctures are also manufactured by soaking the plant parts in alcohol to extract its active chemical constituents. These various preparations allow for different methods of administration depending on the intended therapeutic application.

Some individuals might also experience neurological symptoms or dizziness following ingestion. Because the specific toxicity levels are not fully documented in clinical studies, there is a risk of unpredictable systemic effects. Users should exercise extreme caution as the physiological impact on human health remains a significant concern.
