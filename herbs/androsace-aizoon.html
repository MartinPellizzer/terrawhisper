Androsace aizoon

What's the taxonomical classification of Androsace aizoon?

Androsace aizoon belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Ericales and is a member of the family Primulaceae. Specifically, it is identified by the genus Androsace, with its unique designation as the species aizoon.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Androsace aizoon has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by a woody caudex, oppositely arranged lanceolate to ovate leaves, and small, tubular, pink to white flowers.

The foliage typically features serrated margins and a slightly pubescent texture on both surfaces. Its inflorescence consists of small flowers borne on slender pedicels that emerge from the leaf axils. The fruit is a small, dehiscent capsule containing numerous tiny seeds.

These morphological traits allow the plant to thrive in rocky, montane environments.
