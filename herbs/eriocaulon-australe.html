Eriocaulon australe

What's the taxonomical classification of Eriocaulon australe?

Eriocauloon australe belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Poales. This plant is a representative of the family Eriocaulaceae, specifically situated within the genus Eriocaulon, with the distinct specific epithet australe.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eriocaulon australe has a basal rosette of linear to lanceolate leaves that emerge from a short rhizome and bear small, white, capitate flower heads atop slender, leafless scapes.

These leaves are typically narrow and taper to a fine point, often displaying a bright green coloration. The inflorescences consist of dense, globose clusters of tiny florets that sit atop a central flowering stalk. Each scape is smooth and rises significantly above the foliage to facilitate pollination.

This morphological structure allows the plant to thrive in saturated or swampy substrates.
