Orchis maculata

What's the taxonomical classification of Orchis maculata?

Orchis maculata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Asparagales. As a member of the family Orchidaceae, it is further categorized into the genus Orchis, with its specific designation being the species maculata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Orchis maculata has an erect stem reaching up to thirty centimeters in height, featuring several lance-shaped, spotted leaves that emerge from a fleshy rhizome. The plant produces a terminal spike of numerous small, pale pink or white flowers characterized by a prominent, three-lobed labellum. This specialized lip is marked with dark purple spots and often displays a deeper magenta throat to attract pollinators. Each individual flower possesses a distinct spur located at the back of the perianth to hold nectar. The overall structure is designed to support its reproductive organs above the forest floor foliage.
