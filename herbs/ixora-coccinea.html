Ixora coccinea

What's the taxonomical classification of Ixora coccinea?

Ixora coccinea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Gentianaales. This plant is a member of the family Rubiaceae, which is further categorized into the genus Ixora, ultimately defining its specific classification as the species coccinea.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Ixora coccinea contains various bioactive phytochemicals including alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, tannins, and glycosides that contribute to its therapeutic potential. These specific chemical constituents are believed to provide antioxidant and antimicrobial properties within the plant tissues. Researchers often investigate these compounds for their ability to combat oxidative stress and inhibit the growth of certain pathogens. The presence of tannins specifically suggests potential uses in wound healing and astringent applications. Further pharmacological studies are necessary to fully isolate and quantify the exact medicinal efficacy of these individual substances.

What are the therapeutic actions of Ixora coccinea?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, digestive issues like diarrhea, menstrual irregularities, and various inflammatory conditions. The leaves and roots are often prepared into decoctions or pastes to soothe topical ailments. Some traditional practices utilize the plant to help manage fever and respiratory discomfort. Additionally, its extracts are sometimes applied to reduce swelling or ease joint pain. These medicinal applications rely heavily on the plant's natural bioactive properties.
