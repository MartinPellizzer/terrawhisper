Woodwardia prolifera

What's the taxonomical classification of Woodwardia prolifera?

Woodwardia prolifera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. Further taxonomic refinement places this plant in the family Aspleniaceae, within the genus Woodwardia, and finally identifies it by its specific species name, prolifera.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Woodwardia prolifera has large, deeply pinnate fronds that can reach up to one meter in length, characterized by narrow, lanceolate segments with serrated margins. These fronds emerge from a thick, creeping rhizome that spreads across the forest floor. A distinguishing feature of this fern is the presence of small plantlets that develop on the tips of the pinnae, allowing for vegetative reproduction. The undersides of the leaflets bear prominent sori protected by thin membranes. This unique morphological adaptation enables the plant to colonize new areas through clonal growth rather than relying solely on spores.
