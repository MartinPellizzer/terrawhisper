Rugosa Rose (Rosa rugosa): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

Rugosa Rose (Rosa rugosa) is a hardy shrub native to eastern Asia, known for its fragrant flowers and medicinal properties, particularly used to improve conditions such as respiratory and digestive ailments.

What is Rugosa Rose (Rosa Rugosa)?

Rugosa Rose (Rosa rugosa) is a shrub native to coastal regions of Asia and North America, characterized by its rugged, thorny stems, fragrant pink or white flowers, and edible, tart red berries.

Botanical Identity and Classification

Rugosa Rose (Rosa rugosa) is a deciduous shrub native to coastal regions of East Asia, belonging to the Rosaceae family and the genus Rosa, characterized by its rough, thorny stems, fragrant flowers, and edible hips.

Common Names and Synonyms

Rugosa Rose (Rosa rugosa) is commonly known by various names including Sea Rose, Beach Rose, and Saltmeadow Rose, reflecting its frequent growth in coastal and saline environments.

Plant Description and Morphology

Rugosa Rose (Rosa rugosa) is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems, dense foliage, and distinctive rose-shaped flowers that range from pink to red, with a strong, often fragrant aroma.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Rugosa Rose (Rosa rugosa) is native to the coastal regions of eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, where it thrives in brackish soils and temperate climates.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The plant parts used medicinally in Rugosa Rose (Rosa rugosa) include the flowers, leaves, stems, and roots, with the flowers being the most commonly utilized for their therapeutic properties.

Phytochemical Composition

Rugosa Rose (Rosa rugosa) contains a diverse array of phytochemical compounds, including flavonoids such as quercetin and kaempferol, phenolic acids like gallic acid and ellagic acid, and essential oils rich in terpenes and aromatic compounds, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Pharmacological Properties

Rugosa Rose (Rosa rugosa) exhibits a range of pharmacological properties, including antimicrobial, antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and immunomodulatory effects, supported by both traditional use and modern scientific research.

Mechanisms of Action

Rugosa Rose (Rosa rugosa) exerts its therapeutic effects primarily through its antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, which are attributed to the presence of bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, polyphenols, and essential oils.

Therapeutic Uses and Indications

Rugosa Rose is used to treat respiratory infections, digestive issues, skin conditions, menstrual cramps, inflammation, and as a diuretic and astringent.

Preparation Methods and Forms

Rugosa Rose (Rosa rugosa) is typically prepared as a tincture, tea, or topical salve, with the most common form being a tincture made by soaking the dried flowers and leaves in alcohol or vinegar to extract the active compounds.

Dosage and Administration

The recommended dosage of Rugosa Rose (Rosa rugosa) for herbal use typically ranges from 200 to 400 mg of standardized extract per day, divided into two or three doses, though specific formulations may vary depending on the intended therapeutic effect and product concentration.

Safety, Side Effects, and Contraindications

While Rugosa Rose is generally considered safe when used in moderate amounts, excessive consumption may lead to gastrointestinal discomfort, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, due to its high mucilage content.

Drug Interactions

Rugosa Rose (Rosa rugosa) may interact with medications that affect the liver or have anticoagulant properties, potentially altering their efficacy or increasing the risk of adverse effects.

Toxicity and Precautions

Rugosa rose (Rosa rugosa) is generally considered safe when used in moderate amounts as a herbal remedy, but it may cause mild gastrointestinal upset in some individuals, and its safety during pregnancy, breastfeeding, and in children has not been fully established.

Cultivation and Harvesting

Rugosa Rose (Rosa rugosa) is relatively easy to cultivate in a variety of soil types and climates, thriving in full sun to partial shade and requiring minimal pruning for optimal growth and flowering.

Processing and Extraction

Rugosa Rose (Rosa rugosa) is typically processed by harvesting its flowers, leaves, and fruits, which are then dried and used in herbal formulations or extracted into tinctures and oils for medicinal applications.

Storage and Shelf Life

Rugosa Rose (Rosa rugosa) should be stored in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight, in a sealed container to maintain potency and prevent moisture absorption, with a shelf life of up to 2 years when properly preserved.

Scientific Research and Evidence

Scientific research on Rugosa Rose (Rosa rugosa) has shown potential benefits in traditional herbal medicine, with studies indicating its use in treating respiratory conditions, skin disorders, and as an antioxidant due to its high content of flavonoids and phenolic compounds.

Traditional and Historical Uses

Rugosa Rose (Rosa rugosa) has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and astringent properties, with historical applications ranging from treating respiratory infections and digestive issues to external use for skin conditions and wound healing.

Regulatory Status

Rugosa Rose (Rosa rugosa) is not currently approved as a drug by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) and is not listed in the official pharmacopoeias, though it may be used in traditional or herbal medicine practices.
