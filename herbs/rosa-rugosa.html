Rugosa Rose

Rosa rugosa

Rugosa Rose (rosa rugosa) is a member of the Rosaceae family, known for its traditional use in treating skin conditions, as a tonic for respiratory ailments, and as a digestive aid. The plant parts commonly used include the leaves, flowers, and roots, and it has been utilized in various cultural and regional practices for its purported health benefits.

Botanical Identification

Rosa rugosa, commonly known as rugosa rose, is a species in the Rosaceae family. It is a hardy perennial shrub native to eastern Asia. The plant has thorny stems, dark green leaves, and fragrant pink or red flowers. It is also referred to as sea rose or coastal rose in some regions.

Active Compounds

Rugosa Rose contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids. These compounds work synergistically to provide antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

Safety Profile

Rugosa Rose is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal upset in some individuals. Consult a healthcare provider before use if you have known allergies or are pregnant.
