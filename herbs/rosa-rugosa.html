Rugosa Rose (Rosa rugosa): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

Rugosa Rose (Rosa rugosa) is a hardy, coastal shrub native to eastern Asia that is widely used in traditional herbal medicine for its therapeutic properties and abundant floral and fruit components.

What is Rugosa Rose (Rosa Rugosa)?

Rugosa Rose, or Rosa rugosa, is a shrub native to eastern Asia that is widely used in herbal medicine for its flowers, leaves, and fruits, which contain compounds with antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Rugosa Rose is Rosa rugosa. This plant belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Rosales, family Rosaceae, genus Rosa, and species Rosa rugosa. It is commonly known as Rugosa Rose in North America and Hōzō in Japan. It is also known as Coastal Rose in the United States and Sea Rose in the United Kingdom. Synonyms for this plant include Rosa rugosa subsp. rugosa, Rosa rugosa var. rugosa, and Rosa rugosa var. alpina.

Plant Description and Morphology

Rugosa Rose is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters with a spreading, multi-stemmed structure.

Its leaves are elliptic to ovate, with serrated edges and a rough, hairy texture on the underside. The flowers are large, fragrant, and typically pink to red, with five to seven petals and a hairy, yellowish center. It has a robust, fibrous root system that spreads widely and is highly adaptive to various soil types.

The plant produces a pungent, slightly bitter aroma, particularly in its leaves and stems.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Rugosa Rose is native to the coastal regions of eastern Asia, specifically China, Japan, and Korea, where it thrives in brackish soils and saline environments.

Its geographic distribution extends to the eastern coast of the United States, particularly in states like Massachusetts, New Jersey, and North Carolina, where it has been naturalized. The plant prefers temperate climates with cool, humid summers and mild winters, tolerating salt spray and wind. It thrives in well-drained soils, often found in dunes, marshes, and estuaries.

Rugosa Rose is adapted to moderate to high salinity levels, making it a resilient species in coastal and tidal areas.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The medicinally used parts of Rugosa Rose include the leaves, flowers, and fruits.

The leaves are often harvested during the summer months when their concentration of active compounds is highest. Flowers are typically collected in late spring to early summer, just before they fully open. The fruits, known as hips, are harvested in the fall after they have matured.

Each part contains different phytochemicals that contribute to the plant's medicinal properties.

Phytochemical Composition

Rugosa Rose (Rosa rugosa) contains a variety of active compounds that contribute to its medicinal properties, including flavonoids, phenolic acids, and essential oils.

Secondary metabolites such as ellagic acid, quercetin, and kaempferol are present and play a significant role in its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. These compounds are often responsible for the plant's therapeutic applications in herbal medicine. Chemical markers like gallic acid and salicylic acid are also identified in Rugosa Rose, aiding in quality control and standardization of herbal products.

The combination of these phytochemical components supports its use in various traditional and modern health practices.

Pharmacological Properties

Rugosa Rose (Rosa rugosa) exhibits a range of pharmacological properties that make it valuable in herbal medicine.

Its therapeutic actions include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects. The biological activity of Rugosa Rose is supported by compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and essential oils. Pharmacodynamically, these compounds interact with cellular pathways to modulate immune responses and reduce oxidative stress.

System-level effects include support for digestive health and potential benefits in managing skin conditions.

Rugosa Rose (Rosa rugosa) exerts its therapeutic effects through multiple biological mechanisms, primarily by modulating cellular activity and biochemical pathways.

At the cellular level, it influences antioxidant defense systems and reduces oxidative stress by enhancing the activity of enzymes like superoxide dismutase and catalase. Biochemically, it interacts with pathways involved in inflammation and immune response, including the NF-κB and MAPK signaling cascades. These actions contribute to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and wound-healing properties.

The plant's compounds target key systems such as the respiratory, digestive, and circulatory systems, supporting overall physiological balance.

Therapeutic Uses

Rugosa Rose (Rosa rugosa) is widely used in herbal medicine for its therapeutic properties that address various health conditions.

It is particularly noted for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, making it useful in treating respiratory and digestive disorders. The plant is commonly employed to alleviate symptoms of bronchitis, asthma, and gastrointestinal issues such as indigestion and inflammation. Its diuretic properties also support kidney health and help reduce water retention.

Additionally, Rugosa Rose has been used to manage skin conditions and as a natural remedy for stress and anxiety.

Preparation Methods and Forms

The preparation methods for Rugosa Rose in herbal medicine primarily involve using the plant's flowers, leaves, and roots, which are commonly processed into teas, tinctures, and topical salves.

The flowers are often harvested during early summer and dried for use in herbal infusions. Leaves are typically used in decoctions or as part of topical preparations due to their astringent properties. Roots are less frequently used but can be prepared as a powder or decocted for specific therapeutic applications.

Each form is selected based on the desired therapeutic effect and the part of the plant that provides the most active compounds.

Safety, Side Effects, and Contraindications

Rugosa Rose (Rosa rugosa) is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses, but it may cause mild side effects such as stomach upset, nausea, or allergic reactions in some individuals.

Common side effects include skin irritation from topical application and gastrointestinal discomfort when ingested. People with allergies to plants in the Rosaceae family should avoid Rugosa Rose due to the risk of cross-reactivity. It is contraindicated in individuals with known hypersensitivity to the plant or its constituents.

During pregnancy, the safety of Rugosa Rose has not been fully established, and its use is discouraged without medical supervision.
