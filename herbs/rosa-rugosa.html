Rosa rugosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Rosa rugosa?

Rosa rugosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its progression through the order Rosales and the family Rosaceae, the plant is further identified by its genus, Rosa, and its specific species, rugosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rosa rugosa has a deciduous, shrubby growth habit characterized by arching stems that feature dense, recurved prickles and pinnately compound leaves with heavily wrinkled, rugose surfaces. The foliage typically consists of five to nine leaflets that are oblong or elliptical in shape with serrated margins. During the blooming season, the plant produces large, solitary flowers that can range in color from deep pink to pure white. These blossoms possess numerous stamens and lead to the development of distinctive, fleshy, orange or red hips. The root system is robust and spreading, which allows the plant to stabilize itself in coastal or sandy environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to East Asia, specifically spanning the coastal regions of Japan, China, and the Korean Peninsula. While it originated in these eastern territories, it has since become widely naturalized across much of the Northern Hemisphere. It is frequently found growing along maritime coastlines in North America and Europe due to its high tolerance for salt spray. Many gardeners also cultivate it in temperate zones worldwide for its ornamental value and hardiness. Its ability to thrive in sandy, poor soils allows it to spread easily into coastal dunes and disturbed habitats.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Rosa rugosa contains medicinal compounds in its rose hips, petals, and leaves. The bright red rose hips are highly valued for their exceptionally high concentrations of Vitamin C and antioxidants. These fleshy fruits are often processed into syrups, teas, or jams to support the immune system and combat oxidative stress. The fragrant petals can be used in aromatherapy or herbal infusions to provide calming effects and soothe skin irritations. Additionally, the leaves are sometimes utilized in traditional preparations for their astringent properties.

What are the pharmacological activities of Rosa rugosa?

This plant has anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, antibacterial, anti-diabetic, and anti-tumor pharmacological activities derived from its rich concentration of bioactive compounds. The high levels of polyphenols and flavonoids contribute significantly to its ability to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. These phytochemicals also demonstrate effectiveness in inhibiting the growth of various pathogenic bacteria and suppressing inflammatory pathways in the body. Furthermore, specific extracts from the plant show potential in regulating blood glucose levels and hindering the proliferation of cancerous cells. Research continues to explore how these diverse properties can be utilized for developing new therapeutic agents.

As discussed in a study, Rosa rugosa Thunb. is a medicinal plant known for anti-diabetic and anti-inflammatory activities. The extract using 95% EtOH contains terpenoid structures and phytochemical substituents including compounds 1-13, 11-methoxy polisin A, and 13-methoxy bisaborosaol F. A novel inhibitory agent derived from the plant, rugosic acid A (RA), was found to ameliorate NO production, iNOS, and pro-inflammatory cytokines associated with NF-κB. This RA compound suppressed IL-6 secretion and IL-6-mediated STAT3 activation in LPS-mediated inflammation. Furthermore, RA led to the amelioration of acute lung injury (ALI) by decreasing myeloperoxidase (MPO) and inhibiting the phosphorylation of NF-κB and STAT3.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds acteoside, catechin, kaempferol, quercetin, and tannins. These specific phytochemicals contribute to the biological activity and therapeutic potential of the species. The presence of flavonoids like kaempferol and quercetin provides significant antioxidant properties to the plant matter. Additionally, the inclusion of catechin and acteoside supports various cellular defense mechanisms. Tannins further enhance the chemical profile by offering astringent and antimicrobial effects.

As discussed in a study, dried Rosa rugosa flowers showed the strongest inhibition of HIV-1 RT when screened at a concentration of 500 microg mL-1. Two components, P1 and P2, were obtained from the ethanol precipitate of the aqueous extract, leading to the isolation of P1-a with an Mr of 150 kDa and P1-b with an Mr of 8 kDa. The component P1-a inhibited HIV-1 RT activity with an IC50 of 158 nM. Meanwhile, the component P1-b inhibited the enzyme with an IC50 of 148.16 microg mL-1, which is equivalent to 18.5 microM. Structural analyses identified P1-a as a polysaccharide-peptide complex and P1-b as a polymer consisting of acteoside and acteoside derivatives.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating inflammation, oxidative stress, diarrhoea, eczema, and stomach aches. The high concentration of antioxidants in its fruit helps the body combat oxidative stress and reduce cellular damage. Its medicinal properties are also utilized to soothe digestive issues like stomach aches and diarrhoea. Additionally, topical applications can be effective in managing skin conditions such as eczema. What are the herbal preparations of this plant?

This plant can be processed into an infusion, tincture, essential oil, rose hips powder, rose water, and extract for various therapeutic uses. An infusion made from the petals serves as a gentle tea, while a tincture provides a concentrated liquid form for medicinal application. The essential oil is steam distilled from the blossoms to capture a potent aromatic essence. Rose hips powder is created by drying and grinding the fruit to use as a nutrient-dense dietary supplement. Finally, rose water is produced as a fragrant hydrosol for skin care, and a botanical extract offers a standardized dose of the plant's properties.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause allergic reaction, nausea, diarrhea, and stomach cramps if ingested or handled improperly. Some individuals may experience skin irritation or rashes upon contact with the stems and leaves. When consumed in large quantities, the plant can lead to significant digestive distress. These gastrointestinal symptoms often include nausea and painful stomach cramps. Severe cases might also result in persistent diarrhea.
