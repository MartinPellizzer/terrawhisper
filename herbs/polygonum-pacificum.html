Polygonum pacificum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polygonum pacificum has prostrate, creeping stems that form dense mats and feature small, ovate to lanceolate leaves with entire margins. The foliage is typically bright green and can reach several centimeters in length along the spreading branches. Tiny, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often presenting in small clusters. These delicate blossoms are generally white or pinkish in hue and serve as a key identifying feature. The plant's overall growth habit is low-lying, making it ideal for groundcover in coastal or rocky environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the coastal regions of the western Pacific Ocean, specifically spanning the islands of Japan, Taiwan, and parts of the Ryukyu archipelago. It typically inhabits rocky cliffs and seaside habitats where it can access moisture from coastal fog. The distribution is largely restricted to temperate maritime climates that provide stable humidity levels. While its primary range is centered in East Asia, it thrives in specific microclimates along the shoreline. This specialized niche limits its natural spread to certain oceanic environments.

      Polygonum pacificum is prepared primarily as a decoction through boiling the dried roots in water to extract its medicinal compounds.
