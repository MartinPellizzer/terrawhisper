Polygonum pacificum

What's the taxonomical classification of Polygonum pacificum?

Polygonum pacificum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Caryophyllales. This plant is a member of the family Polygonaceae and is further categorized into the genus Polygonum, specifically identified by the species name pacificum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polygonum pacificum has prostrate, creeping stems that form dense mats and feature small, ovate to lanceolate leaves with entire margins. The foliage is typically bright green and can reach several centimeters in length along the spreading branches. Tiny, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often presenting in small clusters. These delicate blossoms are generally white or pinkish in hue and serve as a key identifying feature. The plant's overall growth habit is low-lying, making it ideal for groundcover in coastal or rocky environments.
