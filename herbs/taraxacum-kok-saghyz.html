Russian Dandelion (Taraxacum kok-saghyz)

Russian Dandelion (Taraxacum kok-saghyz) is a member of the Compositae family, native to Central Asia, Eastern Europe, and Western Siberia. Traditionally, its roots, leaves, and stems have been used for infusions, decoctions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its bitter, diuretic, and anti-inflammatory actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and japanese kampo medicine.

Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 20 to 50 centimeters.
Leaves
Broad leaves with upper surface glabrous and dull green, lower surface softly pubescent and pale green, bearing distinct stomatal bands.
Flowers
Radially symmetrical flowers with yellow disk surrounded by ray florets arranged in a single head, with 15 to 30 ray florets each having a narrow, elongated ligule and 5 lobed corolla.
Stems
Cylindrical, hollow, ridged, and hairless stems with a smooth surface, exhibiting a branching pattern of alternate and opposite leaves, and a growth habit of erect.
