Russian dandelion

Taraxacum kok-saghyz

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Taraxacum kok-saghyz, commonly known as Russian dandelion, is a perennial plant in the Asteraceae family. Native to Central Asia, it has alternate leaves and yellow composite flowers. Its roots are used in herbal medicine, and it is also known as kok-saghyz or the rubber dandelion.

Active Compounds

The Russian Dandelion contains key active compounds such as coumarin, flavonoids, and phenolic acids. These compounds work together to support various physiological functions, with coumarin contributing to its anti-inflammatory properties. Flavonoids and phenolic acids enhance antioxidant activity, promoting overall health benefits.

  • Coumarin
  • Flavonoid
  • Phenolic acid

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action
Integumentary Skin inflammation, skin irritation, skin infections, wound healing, psoriasis Anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, antimicrobial
Respiratory Respiratory infections, allergic rhinitis, bronchitis, chronic cough, influenza Antimicrobial, expectorant, antiviral
Musculoskeletal Joint pain, arthritis, joint inflammation Anti-inflammatory

Preparation Methods

Extract: Used for digestive issues and inflammation due to its active compounds.

Infusion: Used for respiratory issues and as a mild diuretic.

Poultice: Used for skin irritations and wounds due to its anti-inflammatory properties.

Safety Profile

The Russian dandelion is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated for pregnant and breastfeeding women due to potential risks to fetal and infant development. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
