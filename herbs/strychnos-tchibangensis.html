What's the taxonomical classification of Strychnos tchibangensis?

Strychnos tchibangensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Gentianales. As a member of the family Loganiaceae, it is further categorized under the genus Strychnos, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species tchibangensis.

Researchers continue to analyze these substances to understand their precise molecular interactions.

As discussed in a study, the plant Strychnos tchibangensis contains strychnine with high probability in its trunk barks. This identification of the alkaloid was part of an exploration of the Strychnos genus, which includes about 200 species. The research involved grinding 28 different species and plant parts into powder to perform methanolic extracts through pressurized solvent extraction. Using HPLC-ESI(+)-Q/TOF, TLC, NMR, HPLC-UV, and UHPLC-ESI(+)-Q/TOF analyses, researchers detected strychnine in 7 new Strychnos species not previously described as producers. These medicinal compounds are part of the many monoterpene indole alkaloids found within the genus.
