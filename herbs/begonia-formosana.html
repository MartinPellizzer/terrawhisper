Begonia formosana

What's the taxonomical classification of Begonia formosana?

Begonia formosana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Cucurbitaleales. This plant is a representative of the family Begoniaceae, situated within the genus Begonia, and is specifically identified by its species name, formosana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Begonia formosana has asymmetrical, succulent stems that support large, heart-shaped leaves characterized by serrated margins and a distinctively uneven base. The leaf surfaces often exhibit a glossy texture with prominent venation patterns that create a textured appearance. Small, delicate flowers emerge from the leaf axils, typically presenting in shades of white or pale pink. The plant's root system is fleshy and adapted for moisture retention in its native humid environments. These morphological features collectively define its unique aesthetic within the Begonia genus.
