Oldenlandia herbacea

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Oldenlandia herbacea has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by erect stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 40 centimeters. The leaves are mostly basal or distributed along the stem, exhibiting an alternate arrangement with an ovate to elliptical shape and entire margins. These leaves possess thin, membranous textures and are often accompanied by small, inconspicuous stipules at the base. The inflorescence consists of small, yellowish flowers arranged in terminal cymes or clusters. Each flower features a five-lobed corolla and delicate reproductive structures typical of the Saxifragaceae family.

It is primarily found growing in the Fynbos biome, where it inhabits nutrient-poor soils. Its distribution is often localized to specific mountain ranges and coastal lowland areas. The species thrives in Mediterranean-type climates characterized by wet winters and dry summers. Because of this narrow range, it is considered a specialized component of the regional biodiversity.

      What are the herbal preparations of Oldenlandia herbacea?

      Oldenlandia herbacea is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical tincture to utilize its medicinal properties. These liquid forms are typically created by steeping the dried aerial parts of the plant in hot or cold water.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Oldenlandia herbacea can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and potential gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Individuals with sensitive skin may experience redness or itching upon direct contact with the plant's juices. Some people might develop a localized rash that lasts for several days after exposure. Ingestion of the herb could lead to nausea or stomach cramps depending on the concentration of its chemical compounds. It is important to handle the plant with care to avoid these adverse reactions.
