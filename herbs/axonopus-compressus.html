Axonopus compressus

What's the taxonomical classification of Axonopus compressus?

Axonopus compressus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Poales and into the family Poaceae. Ultimately, the plant is identified by the genus Axonopus and is specifically designated as the species compressus.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Axonopus compressus can cause localized skin irritation, contact dermatitis, or allergic reactions upon direct physical contact with its tissues. The plant contains chemical compounds that may trigger redness, itching, or swelling in sensitive individuals. Some people might experience more severe dermatological responses if the sap comes into contact with open wounds or mucous membranes. While extensive clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, cautious handling is recommended to prevent unpredictable dermatological distress. Individuals with known sensitivities to tropical flora should exercise extreme care when interacting with this species.
