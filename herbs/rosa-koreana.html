What's the taxonomical classification of Rosa koreana?

Rosa koreana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Rosales. This plant is a member of the family Rosaceae, which is further narrowed down to the genus Rosa. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined at the species level as koreana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rosa koreana has deciduous, shrubby stems that feature sharp, recurved prickles and compound leaves composed of five to seven elliptical leaflets with serrated margins. The foliage typically exhibits a bright green color and is arranged alternately along the woody branches. During the flowering season, the plant produces solitary, fragrant blossoms that are usually pink or light rose in hue. These flowers consist of multiple layers of delicate petals surrounding a central cluster of numerous stamens.
