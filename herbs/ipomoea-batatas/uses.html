Sweet Potato (Ipomoea batatas) Uses

Sweet potato is used to manage diabetes due to its high fiber and antioxidant content, which helps regulate blood sugar. It also aids in treating gastrointestinal disorders like constipation, gastritis, and ulcers by promoting digestive health and reducing inflammation. Its compounds support gut healing and may alleviate symptoms of inflammatory bowel disease.

Sweet Potato (Ipomoea batatas) Monograph

1. Diabetes

Sweet potato contains compounds that may help regulate blood sugar levels. Its high fiber content slows glucose absorption, while its low glycemic index prevents spikes. Some studies suggest it may improve insulin sensitivity. It is often recommended as a dietary supplement for diabetes management.

2. Gastrointestinal disorders

Sweet potato contains dietary fiber and mucilage, which can soothe the digestive tract and regulate bowel movements. It is traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of indigestion, constipation, and inflammation in the gastrointestinal system. Its mild laxative properties support healthy digestion without causing irritation.

3. Constipation

Sweet potato is effective for constipation due to its high fiber content, which promotes regular bowel movements. It also contains potassium, which helps regulate fluid balance and muscle function in the digestive tract. Regular consumption can improve digestive health and prevent chronic constipation.

4. Gastric ulcers

Sweet potato contains compounds like mucilage and antioxidants that may protect the stomach lining. It helps reduce inflammation and neutralize stomach acid, supporting ulcer healing. Its high fiber content also promotes healthy digestion and prevents further irritation. Regular consumption may aid in managing and preventing gastric ulcers.

5. Gastritis

Sweet potato contains compounds that may help soothe gastric inflammation. Its high fiber content supports digestive health, while its anti-inflammatory properties can reduce stomach irritation. It is often recommended as a gentle, nutrient-rich food for those with gastritis.

6. Inflammatory bowel disease

Sweet potato contains anti-inflammatory compounds like anthocyanins and antioxidants that may help reduce intestinal inflammation. It also provides dietary fiber, which supports gut health. Preliminary studies suggest it could aid in managing symptoms of inflammatory bowel disease, though more research is needed for clinical recommendations.

7. Gastrointestinal issues

Sweet potato contains dietary fiber and antioxidants that support digestive health. It can help alleviate constipation and promote regular bowel movements. Its anti-inflammatory properties may reduce gastrointestinal irritation. It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for digestive support.

8. Stomach ulcers

Sweet potato contains compounds that may help protect the stomach lining and reduce ulcer symptoms. Its high fiber content promotes healthy digestion, while antioxidants may reduce inflammation. Some traditional practices use it as a natural remedy for stomach ulcers, though more research is needed for confirmation.

9. Gastrointestinal ulcers

Sweet potato contains mucilage and antioxidants that protect the stomach lining. It reduces inflammation and neutralizes stomach acid, promoting ulcer healing. Regular consumption may support gastrointestinal health and prevent ulcer recurrence. Its natural properties make it a valuable complementary remedy in herbal medicine.

10. Mouth ulcers

Sweet potato peel contains compounds with anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that can help soothe mouth ulcers. Applying a paste made from boiled and mashed sweet potato peel to the affected area may reduce pain and promote healing. It is often used in traditional remedies for its gentle, soothing effect on oral tissues.
