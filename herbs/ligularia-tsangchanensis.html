Ligularia tsangchanensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ligularia tsangchanensis has large, ovate to broadly lanceolate leaves with serrated margins and long petioles that emerge from a sturdy, upright stem. The foliage is typically characterized by a textured surface and prominent venation that extends toward the leaf edges. Its inflorescence consists of dense, terminal clusters of bright yellow ray florets that surround a central disc. The flowering stems are upright and can reach significant heights depending on the specific habitat. These reproductive structures are designed to attract pollinators within its native montane environments.

Ligularia tsangchanensis is prepared as a decoction, a topical paste, or a powder for medicinal use. If consumed, the plant could potentially result in stomach upset or other gastrointestinal complications. It is important to exercise caution and avoid self-medicating with this species without professional guidance.
