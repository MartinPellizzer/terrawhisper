What's the taxonomical classification of Oxyspora vagans?

Oxyspora vagans belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Myrtales. This species is further classified into the family Melastommataceae, specifically within the genus Oxyspora, and is identified by its unique species designation, vagaus.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Oxyspora vagans can cause severe skin irritation, localized swelling, and intense dermatological burning upon direct contact with the epidermis. These reactions often manifest as red, itchy welts that may persist for several days if the sap is not immediately washed away. If the plant's toxins are accidentally ingested, they can trigger acute gastrointestinal distress including nausea and abdominal cramping. Inhalation of airborne spores or pollen from the plant may also result in respiratory irritation or allergic sneezing fits. Medical attention is recommended if symptoms escalate or if systemic swelling occurs.
