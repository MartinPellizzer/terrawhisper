Croton tiglium

What's the taxonomical classification of Croton tiglium?

Croton tiglium belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized under the order Malpighiales. This plant is a member of the family Euphorbiaceae, falling under the genus Croton, and is specifically identified by its species name, tiglium.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Croton tiglium contains diterpene esters, specifically phorbol esters, which serve as the primary bioactive constituents responsible for its pharmacological properties. These complex compounds are known to induce significant biological responses through the activation of protein kinase C. Beyond these esters, the plant also possesses various alkaloids and flavonoids that contribute to its diverse chemical profile. Researchers study these substances to understand their potential applications in inflammatory regulation and cellular signaling. The specific arrangement of these chemical structures determines the plant's potency and its varying effects on human tissue.

What are the therapeutic actions of Croton tiglium?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, stomach aches, and various parasitic infestations. The seeds contain a potent purgative substance that is frequently employed to expel intestinal worms. In topical applications, the extracts serve to alleviate skin irritations and promote the healing of minor cuts. Some traditional practices also involve using the plant to manage fever and gastrointestinal distress. Its medicinal properties are highly valued in various folk medicine traditions across tropical regions.
