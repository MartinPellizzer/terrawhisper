Croton tiglium

What's the taxonomical classification of Croton tiglium?

Croton tiglium belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized under the order Malpighiales. This plant is a member of the family Euphorbiaceae, falling under the genus Croton, and is specifically identified by its species name, tiglium.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Croton tiglium has woody stems that reach heights of up to 15 meters and large, elliptical leaves measuring between 15 and 30 centimeters in length.

The foliage is typically dark green with prominent, pale venation that creates a distinct pattern on the leaf surface. These leaves are arranged alternately along the branches and possess entire margins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are organized into axillary racemes.

Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that contains seeds typically enclosed in a fleshy aril.

Croton tiglium contains medicinal properties primarily derived from its seeds, which yield a potent oil used for various therapeutic purposes. This seed oil is traditionally applied topically to treat skin conditions such as ringworm, scabies, and other fungal infections. The plant's leaves are also sometimes utilized in traditional medicine to address specific ailments through poultices or decoctions. Additionally, some practitioners use parts of the plant to stimulate purgative effects when administered in highly controlled amounts. What are the herbal preparations of Croton tiglium?

Croton tiglium is prepared primarily as a crude latex or an oil extracted from the seeds for topical application to treat skin conditions. This viscous substance is often applied directly to warts, corns, or fungal infections to induce localized irritation and healing. In some traditional practices, the seeds are crushed to create a paste that serves as a potent emetic or purgative when administered internally. However, extreme caution is required because the chemical components are highly caustic and can cause severe tissue damage if used incorrectly. The preparation methods must be carefully controlled to manage the plant's intense biological activity.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Croton tiglium can cause severe skin irritation, blistering, and intense inflammation if the plant's milky sap comes into direct contact with human tissue. The chemical compounds within the latex act as potent irritants that may trigger allergic contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. If accidental ingestion occurs, the toxins can lead to violent vomiting, abdominal pain, and significant gastrointestinal distress. Direct contact with the eyes is particularly dangerous and can result in temporary or permanent vision impairment due to corneal damage. Therefore, extreme caution must be taken when handling any part of this plant to avoid these painful physiological reactions.
