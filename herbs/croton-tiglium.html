Croton tiglium

What's the taxonomical classification of Croton tiglium?

Croton tiglium belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized under the order Malpighiales. This plant is a member of the family Euphorbiaceae, falling under the genus Croton, and is specifically identified by its species name, tiglium.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Croton tiglium has woody stems that reach heights of up to 15 meters and large, elliptical leaves measuring between 15 and 30 centimeters in length. The foliage is typically dark green with prominent, pale venation that creates a distinct pattern on the leaf surface. These leaves are arranged alternately along the branches and possess entire margins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are organized into axillary racemes. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that contains seeds typically enclosed in a fleshy aril.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains 12-O-acetylphorbol-13-decanoate, crotocaudiin, flavonoids, phorbol esters, and steroids. These diverse medicinal compounds are distributed throughout various tissues of the plant species. The presence of phorbol esters, specifically 12-O-acetylphorbol-13-decanoate and crotocaudiin, can trigger significant biological responses in cellular signaling. Additionally, the plant is characterized by a rich profile of flavonoids and various steroids. These chemical constituents contribute to the complex pharmacological properties associated with the plant.

As discussed in a study, eight phorbol diesters, which included 5 new ones, were isolated from the seeds of Croton tiglium collected in Egypt. Among these, 12-O-Acetylphorbol-13-decanoate (6) and 12-O-decanoylphorbol-13-(2-methylbutyrate) (4) were found to inhibit the HIV-1-induced cytopathic effect on MT-4 cells. The compound 6 showed an IC100 value of 7.6 ng/ml and a CC50 value of 62.5 micrograms/ml. Additionally, the compound 4 demonstrated an IC100 value of 7.81 micrograms/ml and a CC50 value of 31.3 micrograms/ml. Both compounds worked without the activation of protein kinase C.
