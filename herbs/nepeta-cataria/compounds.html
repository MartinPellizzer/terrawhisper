Catnip (Nepeta cataria) Compounds

The primary active compounds in catnip include essential oil, which contains nepetalactone, and coumarin, known for its calming effects. Flavonoids and terpenoids contribute to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Phenolic acids, glycosides, and tannins support its digestive and mild sedative actions. Saponins, alkaloids, and anthocyanins add to its diverse therapeutic potential.

Catnip (Nepeta cataria) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Catnip (Nepeta cataria) contains nepetalactone, a key compound responsible for its stimulating and calming effects. It is widely used in herbal medicine for its ability to relieve stress, promote relaxation, and support respiratory health.

2. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Catnip (Nepeta cataria). It contributes to the plant's calming and sedative effects, often used in herbal medicine to promote relaxation and ease anxiety. Its presence also gives Catnip its characteristic sweet, hay-like aroma.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Catnip (Nepeta cataria) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting the plant's traditional use in calming and digestive health. They also interact with neurotransmitters, enhancing the plant's soothing and mild sedative qualities.

4. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of organic compounds found in Catnip (Nepeta cataria). They contribute to the plant's aromatic properties and are responsible for its calming effects. These compounds interact with the central nervous system, making Catnip a popular herb in herbal medicine for relaxation and mild sedation.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Catnip (Nepeta cataria). It contributes to the plant's characteristic effects, including its calming and sedative properties. Found in significant amounts, phenolic acid supports the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and nervous system support.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Catnip (Nepeta cataria). It contributes to the plant's calming and sedative effects, often used in herbal remedies for anxiety and insomnia. Glycosides in Catnip are responsible for its distinctive aroma and therapeutic properties.

7. Tannin

Tannin is a compound found in Catnip (Nepeta cataria). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health by reducing inflammation and promoting tissue repair. Tannins also help preserve the plant's medicinal potency during storage.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Catnip (Nepeta cataria). It contributes to the plant's stimulating and calming effects, influencing mood and digestion. Saponins also have mild antimicrobial properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine.

9. Alkaloid

The alkaloid nepetalactone is the primary active compound in Catnip (Nepeta cataria). It is responsible for the plant's characteristic scent and its psychoactive effects on cats. In herbal medicine, nepetalactone is studied for its potential calming and sedative properties in humans.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid compound found in Catnip (Nepeta cataria). It contributes to the plant's vibrant blue and purple pigmentation and may possess antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. While not the primary psychoactive component, anthocyanin supports overall plant health and may contribute to the therapeutic effects of Catnip in traditional herbal medicine.
