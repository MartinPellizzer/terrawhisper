Nymphaea tetragona

What's the taxonomical classification of Nymphaea tetragona?

Nymphaea tetragona belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is placed in the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Nymphaeales. This aquatic plant is a member of the family Nymphaeaceae and is situated within the genus Nymphaea, specifically identified as the species tetragona.

    Nymphaea tetragonas is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice using the plant's various parts. These different methods allow practitioners to tailor the potency and delivery of the plant's medicinal properties.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Nymphaea tetragona can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested or handled without protection. The plant contains specific alkaloids and chemical compounds that may trigger localized redness or itching upon contact with sensitive skin. If any part of the plant is consumed, individuals might experience nausea, stomach cramping, or more severe digestive disruptions. Detailed toxicological studies are limited, so caution is necessary when interacting with its foliage or sap. It is generally advised to avoid direct contact to prevent unpredictable systemic or topical reactions.
