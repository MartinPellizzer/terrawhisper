Nymphaea tetragona

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Nymphaea tetragona has large, circular, floating leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter with deep notches and thick, fleshy petioles. The leaf surfaces are typically dark green and possess a waxy coating to repel water. Its flowers are striking, featuring multiple layers of pointed petals that transition in color from white to pale pink. These blooms emerge from the water surface on long, sturdy stalks to facilitate pollination. The plant's underwater structure consists of a robust rhizome that anchors it firmly into the aquatic sediment.

Within these habitats, it prefers areas with soft, muddy substrates that allow its rhizomes to anchor firmly.
