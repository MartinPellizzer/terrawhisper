Tiarella polyphylla

What's the taxonomical classification of Tiarella polyphylla?

Tiarella polyphylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Saxifragales and the family Saxifragaceae, which eventually leads to its specific placement within the genus Tiarella. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its unique species name, polyphylla.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tiarella polyphylla has deeply lobed, palmately divided leaves that typically feature five to seven distinct segments and a dark green, somewhat leathery texture.

The foliage is characterized by prominent veins that radiate from the base of the leaf toward the tips of each lobe. These perennial plants grow in low-lying clumps, often reaching a height of only a few inches above the soil. During the blooming season, small, inconspicuous white or pale flowers emerge from the center of the leaf clusters.

The plant's overall structure is compact, making it well-suited for groundcover applications in woodland environments.
