Tiarella polyphylla

What's the taxonomical classification of Tiarella polyphylla?

Tiarella polyphylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Saxifragales and the family Saxifragaceae, which eventually leads to its specific placement within the genus Tiarella. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its unique species name, polyphylla.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tiarella polyphylla has deeply lobed, palmately divided leaves that typically feature five to seven distinct segments and a dark green, somewhat leathery texture. The foliage is characterized by prominent veins that radiate from the base of the leaf toward the tips of each lobe. These perennial plants grow in low-lying clumps, often reaching a height of only a few inches above the soil. During the blooming season, small, inconspicuous white or pale flowers emerge from the center of the leaf clusters. The plant's overall structure is compact, making it well-suited for groundcover applications in woodland environments.

What are the pharmacological activities of Tiarella polyphylla?

This plant has significant pharmacological activities including potent anticoemplment activity and measurable hemolytic activity. These biological properties suggest that extracts from Tiarella polyphylla can influence the body's innate immune responses and blood cell stability. Research indicates that certain compounds within the plant may modulate the complement system to prevent excessive inflammation. Furthermore, the observed hemolytic activity provides insight into how the plant's chemical constituents interact with red blood cell membranes. Such findings highlight the potential medicinal importance of the species in immunological studies.

According to a study published by "Archives of pharmacal research", seven known oleanolic acid glycosides were isolated from the MeOH extract of Tiarella polyphylla. These identified structures include 3-O-(beta-D-glucopyranosyl) oleanolic acid (1), 3-O-[beta-D-glucopyranosyl-(1-->3)-beta-D-glucopyranosyl] oleanolic acid (2), 3-O-[beta-D-glucopyranosyl-(1-->2)-beta-D-glucopyranosyl] oleanolic acid (3), 3-O-[beta-D-glucopyranosyl-(1-->3)-beta-D-glucopyranosyl] oleanolic acid 28-O-beta-D-glucopyranosyl ester (4), 3-O-[beta-D-glucopyranosyl-(1-->2)-beta-D-glucopyranosyl] oleanolic acid 28-O-beta-D-glucopyranosyl ester (5), 3-O-[a-L-rhamnopyranosyl-(1-->3)-beta-D-glucuronopyranosyl] oleanolic acid (6), and 3-O-[alpha-L-rhamnopyranosyl-(1-->3)-beta-D-glucuronopyranosyl] oleanolic acid 28-O-beta-D-glucopyranosyl ester (7). The bisdesmosidic saponins 4, 5, and 7 showed anticomplement activity. In contrast, the monodesmosidic saponins 1-3 and 6 showed direct hemolytic activity. Additionally, methyl esterified monodesmosidic saponins showed anticomplement activity at a low concentration and hemolytic activity at a high concentration.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds corosolic acid, tiarellic acid, tormentic acid, 3-O-(beta-D-glucopyranosyl) oleanolic acid, and 3-O-[alpha-L-rhamnopyranosyl-(1-->3)-beta-D-glucuronopyranosyl] oleanolic acid. These specific triterpenoids are characteristic of the Tiarella polyphylla species. The presence of these bioactive molecules contributes to the plant's unique chemical profile. Researchers study these various glycosides and acids to understand their potential biological activities. Such detailed chemical compositions are essential for identifying the therapeutic properties of the species.

According to a study published by "Archives of pharmacal research", a new 27-carboxylic lupane-type triterpene named tiarellic acid (1) was isolated from Tiarella polyphylla. This plant also contains corosolic acid (2) and tormentic acid (3). The isolated compound tiarellic acid was characterized as 3,23-dihydroxy-20(29)-luper-27-oic acid. Researchers used 2-D NMR techniques to ensure the NMR data were unambiguously assigned.
