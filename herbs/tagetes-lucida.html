Black sage

Tagetes lucida

Black Sage (Tagetes lucida) is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States, known for its aromatic leaves and yellow-orange flowers, and it has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, particularly for improving respiratory conditions such as bronchitis and asthma.

Botanical Identification

Black Sage, or Tagetes lucida, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. Belonging to the Asteraceae family, it is also known as sweetroot or black sagebrush. The plant features aromatic, gray-green leaves and yellow-orange flowers, with a root system used in traditional herbal medicine. Synonyms include "Mexican tarragon" and "Spanish tarragon" in some herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Magnoliopsida
  • Order: Asterales
  • Family: Asteraceae
  • Genus: Tagetes
  • Species: Tagetes lucida

Active Compounds

Black Sage contains essential oil, flavonoids, and terpenoids as its main bioactive compounds. These compounds work together to provide anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. The synergistic interaction between these compounds enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory issues and digestive ailments.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and as an antispasmodic.

Safety Profile

Black Sage is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal upset in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to potential adverse effects on fetal or infant development. Always consult a healthcare provider before use, especially for prolonged periods or in high doses.
