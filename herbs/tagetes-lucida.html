Black Sage (Tagetes lucida)

Black Sage (Tagetes lucida) is a member of the Asteraceae family, native to Southeastern United States, Eastern Mexico, and Central America. Traditionally, its leaves, flowers, and stems have been used for infusions, decoctions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, bitter, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in native american herbal medicine, european herbal medicine, and traditional chinese medicine.

Flowers
Small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in dense, compact clusters, actinomorphic, with four to five ovate petals fused at the base and prominent yellow markings on the outer surfaces.
Stems
Elongated, erect growth habit, opposite branching pattern, glabrous surface, ribbed structure, aromatic resinous secretion.
