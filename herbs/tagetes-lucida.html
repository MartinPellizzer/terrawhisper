Black sage

Tagetes lucida

Black Sage (Tagetes lucida) is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States, known for its aromatic leaves and yellow-orange flowers, and it has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, particularly for improving respiratory conditions such as bronchitis and asthma.

Taxonomy

Black Sage, or Tagetes lucida, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is native to the eastern United States.

Morphology

Black Sage (Tagetes lucida) is a perennial herb with slender, upright stems, aromatic gray-green leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and small yellow-orange flower heads that bloom in late summer.

Distribution

Black Sage (Tagetes lucida) is native to the eastern and central regions of North America, specifically found in the United States and parts of Canada, particularly in wooded areas and moist soils.

Traditional Uses

Black Sage (Tagetes lucida) has been traditionally used by Native American tribes for its medicinal properties, including as a digestive aid, antiseptic, and treatment for respiratory and skin conditions.

Medicinal Systems

Black Sage (Tagetes lucida) has been traditionally used in Native American medicine, particularly by the Cherokee, Iroquois, and Navajo tribes, for its antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, and digestive properties.

Phytochemistry

Black Sage (Tagetes lucida) contains a variety of bioactive compounds, including sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, phenolic acids, and essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties and therapeutic potential.

Pharmacology

Black Sage exhibits antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antispasmodic properties, making it effective in treating respiratory infections, digestive issues, and skin conditions.

Medicinal Uses

Black sage is used to treat respiratory infections, digestive issues, skin irritations, menstrual cramps, anxiety, and as a general tonic to support overall health.

Preparations

Black sage is used to prepare tinctures, teas, and topical salves by infusing its leaves and flowers in alcohol, water, or oil, often for its antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, and digestive properties.

Safety

While Black Sage (Tagetes lucida) is generally considered safe when used in moderate amounts, it may cause mild gastrointestinal discomfort, such as nausea or stomach upset, in some individuals, and excessive use may lead to skin irritation or allergic reactions.

Evidence

Clinical evidence supporting the use of Black Sage (Tagetes lucida) in herbal medicine is limited, with most studies focusing on its potential antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties, though human trials are scarce.

Cultivation

Black Sage (Tagetes lucida) is typically cultivated in well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade, requiring minimal water and thriving in temperate climates with cool, moist conditions during its growing season.

Processing

Processing Black Sage involves drying the leaves and flowers under shade to preserve their aromatic compounds, which are then used in herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, and topical applications.

Identification

Black Sage (Tagetes lucida) is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States, characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and yellow-orange flower heads, typically found in moist, wooded areas.

Sustainability

Black Sage (Tagetes lucida) is sustainably harvested and cultivated in its native habitat, ensuring minimal environmental impact and supporting local ecosystems through responsible land management practices.

Related

Black Sage (Tagetes lucida) is related to other species in the genus Tagetes, including Mexican marigold (Tagetes erecta) and African marigold (Tagetes minuta), which share similar chemical compositions and medicinal properties.
