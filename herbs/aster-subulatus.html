Aster subulatus

What's the taxonomical classification of Aster subulatus?

Aster subulatus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further organized into the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant follows the order Asterales and is a member of the family Asteraceae. Specifically, it is categorized under the genus Aster, and its formal scientific designation is completed by the species epithet subulatus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aster subulatus has an erect, herbaceous growth habit characterized by slender, branched stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 100 centimeters. The leaves are alternate, ranging from narrowly lanceolate to linear in shape, and possess finely serrated margins. Small, yellowish-white flower heads are arranged in dense, terminal clusters known as corymbs. Each individual flower head contains numerous disc florets, which are the primary reproductive structures of the plant. The resulting seeds are small, dry achenes equipped with a pappus of fine hairs to facilitate wind dispersal.
