Achillea alpina

What's the taxonomical classification of Achillea alpina?

Achillea alpina belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Asterales. It is a prominent representative of the family Asteraceae, a group known for its composite flower heads. Within this family, the plant is placed in the genus Achillea, and its specific identity is defined by its species name, alpinas.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Achillea alpina contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and essential oils like azulene. These chemical constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's documented anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities. The presence of specific terpenoids suggests potential for treating skin irritations and minor infections. Furthermore, the flavonoid profile contributes to antioxidant properties that help mitigate oxidative stress in biological systems. Research continues into how these combined compounds might interact with human cellular pathways.

What are the therapeutic actions of Achillea alpina?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat inflammatory conditions, digestive upsets, gastrointestinal spasms, and minor skin irritations. Its medicinal properties are often applied to soothe stomach cramps and promote healthy digestion. Historically, practitioners have utilized its compounds to alleviate various skin ailments and surface wounds. The plant contains bioactive constituents that help manage mild fever and systemic inflammation. These therapeutic applications make it a notable subject in traditional herbal medicine.
