Giant Dianthus

Dianthus superbus

Giant Dianthus (dianthus superbus) is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, traditionally used in Chinese medicine. The herb has been employed historically as a tonic for blood and qi, and for improving circulation, with some traditional references indicating its use in treating anemia. The roots and leaves are typically utilized in preparation for these purposes.

Botanical Identification

Giant Dianthus, scientifically known as Dianthus superbus, belongs to the Carnation family (Caryophyllaceae). It is also called the Giant Carnation or Mountain Carnation. A perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe, it has fleshy roots, lance-shaped leaves, and large, showy pink or red flowers. Synonyms in herbal texts include "Great Carnation" and "Alpine Dianthus."

Active Compounds

The Giant Dianthus contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and tannins, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, supporting its use in traditional herbal medicine.

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory ailments such as coughs and colds.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and as a general tonic.

Extract: Used for skin conditions and as an antiseptic.

Safety Profile

Giant Dianthus is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation due to potential risks. Individuals with allergies to plants in the Caryophyllaceae family should also avoid its use.
