Carpinus cordata

What's the taxonomical classification of Carpinus cordata?

Carpinus cordata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further falls under the order Fagales and is a member of the family Betulaceae. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Carpinus, with its specific identification being the species cordata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Carpinus cordata has cordate to ovate leaves with serrated margins, a woody stem, and small, drooping catkins. The leaf blades are characterized by their heart-shaped bases and prominent, pinnate venation that creates a textured surface. Its branches are typically slender and greyish, supporting the dense foliage throughout the growing season. The flowers are inconspicuous and wind-pollinated, appearing as long, pendulous structures. During maturation, the plant produces small, nut-like fruits enclosed in specialized bracts.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

Because its natural range is restricted to these specific East Asian locales, it is not found growing wild in other parts of the world.
