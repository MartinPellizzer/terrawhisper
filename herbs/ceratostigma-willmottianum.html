Ceratostigma willmottianum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ceratostigma willmottianum has decumbent to ascending woody stems that produce small, ovate to elliptical leaves measuring approximately 1 to 2 centimeters in length. The foliage is typically a deep green color, though it can transition to brilliant shades of red or purple during the autumn months. Small, funnel-shaped flowers emerge from the leaf axils, displaying a striking deep blue or violet hue. These blossoms are characterized by a distinctive, horn-shaped calyx that provides structural support to the petals. The plant's overall growth habit is spreading, allowing it to form dense, low-growing mats across the soil surface.
