Gardenia stenophylla

What's the taxonomical classification of Gardenia stenophylla?

Gardenia stenophylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further organized into the order Gentianales. It is a member of the family Rubiaceae and falls under the genus Gardenia, specifically identified by its species name, stenophylla.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gardenia stenophylla has narrow, linear leaves that measure approximately 5 to 15 centimeters in length and 0.5 to 1 centimeter in width, characterized by an entire margin and an acute apex. These leaves are typically dark green and possess a leathery, coriaceous texture typical of the Rubiaceae family. The plant exhibits a woody shrub habit with branching stems that support its foliage. Small, white, fragrant flowers emerge from the axils, often featuring tubular corollas. The overall architecture of the plant is compact, reflecting its adaptation to specific forest understory environments.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires acidic soil with a pH between 5.0 and 6.0, consistent moisture, and bright, indirect sunlight to thrive.

Because it is native to subtropical regions, it performs best when kept in warm temperatures and protected from harsh, freezing winds. Regular applications of organic mulch help maintain the necessary soil acidity and moisture levels around the root system. You should avoid overhead watering to prevent fungal issues and instead water at the base of the plant.

Providing high humidity through frequent misting or pebble trays can further support healthy foliage development.
