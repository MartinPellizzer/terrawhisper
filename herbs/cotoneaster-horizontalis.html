Cotoneaster horizontalis

What's the taxonomical classification of Cotoneaster horizontalis?

Cotoneaster horizontalis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its hierarchical path, the plant is placed in the order Rosales and the family Rosaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Cotoneaster and is specifically designated as the species horizontalis.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Cotoneaster horizontalis contains no recognized medicinal parts for human consumption or therapeutic use in traditional or modern pharmacology. While many plants in the Rosaceae family possess bioactive compounds, this specific ornamental shrub is primarily valued for its landscaping qualities rather than its healing properties.     Cotoneaster horizontalis is prepared primarily as a decoction of the dried berries or as an infusion of the leaves for medicinal use.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Cotoneaster horizontalis can cause gastrointestinal distress, including vomiting and diarrhea, if the berries are ingested by humans or pets. While the plant is generally considered non-toxic to many adults, the small red fruits contain substances that may irritate the digestive tract. Children are at a higher risk of accidental poisoning due to the attractive appearance of the bright berries. It is also important to note that these berries can be toxic to certain bird species if consumed in excessive quantities. Always ensure that children and pets are supervised around this shrub to prevent accidental ingestion.
