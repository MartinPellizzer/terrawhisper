Cotoneaster horizontalis

What's the taxonomical classification of Cotoneaster horizontalis?

Cotoneaster horizontalis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its hierarchical path, the plant is placed in the order Rosales and the family Rosaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Cotoneaster and is specifically designated as the species horizontalis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cotoneaster horizontalis has a spreading, decumbent growth habit characterized by distinctive horizontal branches that extend outward from a central stem in tiered layers. These woody stems are typically arching and can reach several feet in width, creating a dense, mat-like structure. The leaves are small, oval, and dark green with finely serrated margins, appearing densely packed along the branches. During the spring, the plant produces small, white, five-petaled flowers that emerge from clusters among the foliage. In the autumn, these blossoms transition into bright red, berry-like pomes that persist through the winter months.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal pain if any part of it is ingested. The berries contain cyanogenic glycosides which can lead to these severe gastrointestinal distress symptoms. While the toxicity level is generally considered low for humans, accidental consumption should be treated with caution. Children and pets are at a higher risk of experiencing these adverse effects due to their smaller body mass. Monitoring for these specific symptoms is necessary following any suspected ingestion.
