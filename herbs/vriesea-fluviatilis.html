What is Vriesea fluviatilis?

Vriesea fluviatilis is a plant species in the family Bromeliaceae.

It is a terrestrial bromeliad, characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and infundibuliform (funnel-shaped) leaf base. The leaves are lanceolate to ovate, 2-5 cm long, and 1-2 cm wide, with a reddish-brown or greenish color. The plant produces a solitary inflorescence, consisting of a spike of purple flowers, 6-10 cm long, that blooms in the summer months.

Vriesea fluviatilis is endemic to the states of Minas Gerais and São Paulo, Brazil.

What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

The main herbal preparations of Vriesea fluviatilis are tincture, infusion, vriesea tea, salve, and essential oil.

Vriesea fluviatilis tincture is a concentrated liquid extract typically made by combining the plant's leaves with a solvent such as ethanol or glycerin, which is then filtered and bottled for internal or external use. Vriesea tea is made by steeping the plant's leaves in hot water to create a decoction, often used for its medicinal properties. The salve preparation involves infusing the plant's leaves in a carrier oil such as coconut or olive oil to create a topical remedy for skin issues.

Essential oil of Vriesea fluviatilis is extracted through steam distillation and can be used topically for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.
