What to know about Aquilegia formosa (columbine) before using it medicinally

Columbine, scientifically known as Aquilegia formosa, is a herb that has been prized for its impressive array of health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties that can help to soothe digestive issues and boost the immune system.

In culinary contexts, columbine is often used to add a delicate flavor and aroma to soups, salads, and sauces, particularly in traditional Mediterranean dishes. From a horticultural perspective, columbine is a low-maintenance perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners of all skill levels. Botanically speaking, columbine's unique flower shape and colorful blooms have led to its classification as a member of the buttercup family, Ranunculaceae.

With a rich history dating back thousands of years, references to columbine can be found in ancient Greek and Roman texts, where it was prized for both its medicinal and ornamental qualities.
