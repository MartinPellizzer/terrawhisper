Huperzia comans

Huperzia comans, also known as the New Zealand liverwort or more commonly as the Huperzia, is a small, perennial plant that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It has been traditionally used in medicine, particularly in Maori culture, due to its potential medicinal properties, including its antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and neuroprotective effects.

The plant's extracts have been found to have various health benefits, such as improving cognitive function, reducing anxiety and stress, and potentially treating neurodegenerative diseases like Alzheimer's and Parkinson's.

Huperzia comans is often prepared as a supplement in capsule or powder form, and it can also be consumed as a tea or added to other herbal remedies.
