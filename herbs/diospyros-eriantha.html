Diospyros eriantha

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Diospyros eriantha has large, coriaceous leaves that are elliptic to oblong in shape and characterized by a densely pubescent underside covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant typically exhibits a woody structure with a sturdy trunk and branches that bear these thick, dark green foliage units. Its inflorescence consists of small, creamy-white flowers that emerge from the axils of the leaves. The fruit is a fleshy berry that develops from an inferior ovary, eventually maturing into a rounded or ovoid structure. These morphological traits are essential for distinguishing this species within the Ebenaceae family.
