What's the taxonomical classification of Diospyros eriantha?

Diospyros eriantha belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical structure of biological classification, it falls under the order Ericales and is a member of the family Ebenaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Diospyrpos, and its specific species designation, eriantha.

    What are the herbal preparations of Diospyros eriantha?

    Diospyros eriantha is prepared as a decoction of the bark, a fruit infusion, or a topical paste made from crushed leaves.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Diospyros eriantha can cause gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, or allergic reactions if the plant is ingested or touched improperly. While many species in the Ebenaceae family contain bioactive compounds, the specific toxicological profile of this plant remains insufficiently documented in clinical literature. Potential side effects may include nausea, abdominal pain, or vomiting due to the presence of uncharacterized alkaloids or glycosides. Topical contact with the plant material could also lead to contact dermatitis or localized redness in sensitive individuals. Careful observation is necessary when interacting with any part of this species to avoid adverse physiological responses.
