Millettia barteri

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Millettia barteri has woody stems, pinnate leaves with multiple leaflets, and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in axillary clusters. The leaves are typically arranged in an opposite or sub-opposite pattern along the branches. Each leaflet possesses a smooth margin and a distinct tip that contributes to the overall texture of the foliage. The reproductive structures consist of small, yellowish or cream-colored flowers that eventually develop into woody pods. These pods contain the seeds necessary for the plant's reproduction and are characteristic of the Fabaceae family.

What are the herbal preparations of Millettia barteri?

Millettia barteri is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste derived from its leaves, bark, and roots. The decoction is often created by boiling the plant material in water to extract active compounds for internal consumption. Infusions are sometimes used for milder therapeutic purposes, involving the steeping of parts in hot liquid. Millettia barteri can cause adverse reactions such as gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, or allergic hypersensitivity in sensitive individuals. Ingesting preparations of the plant without standardized dosing carries a risk of unpredictable systemic effects. Some studies suggest that concentrated extracts might interfere with certain metabolic processes if used improperly. Therefore, caution is necessary when handling or consuming any part of this species.
