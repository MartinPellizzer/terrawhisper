What to know about Galipea officinalis (angelica) before using it medicinally

Galipea officinalis, commonly known as angelica, is a plant-rich in medicinal properties that have been shown to improve overall health by reducing inflammation and alleviating symptoms of various ailments.

The Galipea officinalis herb is relatively easy to cultivate, making it a popular choice among gardeners, and can be grown in a variety of environments, from tropical to temperate climates. From a botanical standpoint, Galipea officinalis is a member of the Rutaceae family and is known for its distinctive, aromatic foliage and fragrant flowers.

Historically, Galipea officinalis has been referenced in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties, and was even mentioned in the works of the Greek physician Dioscorides in the 1st century AD.

This article explains the medicinal, horticultural, botanical, and historical aspects of Galipea officinalis.

What are the medicinal properties of Galipea officinalis?

Galipea officinalis helps with digestive issues, respiratory problems, and skin irritations due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It also has antioxidant and antifungal effects, making it a versatile medicinal plant.

The active constituents of Galipea officinalis include flavonoids, alkaloids, and glycosides, which are responsible for its medicinal properties. These compounds have been identified as galipeine, galipine, and angelic acid, among others, and contribute to its therapeutic effects.

The parts of Galipea officinalis most used for medicinal purposes are the leaves, stems, and roots. The leaves are used to treat skin conditions, while the stems and roots are used to treat digestive issues and respiratory problems.

Possible side effects of Galipea officinalis include allergic reactions, gastrointestinal upset, and interactions with other medications. Improper use can lead to excessive drowsiness, headaches, and increased risk of bleeding.

Common precautions when using Galipea officinalis medicinally include pregnant women, breastfeeding women, and individuals with bleeding disorders, should consult a healthcare professional before using it. It should also be used under medical supervision and in controlled dosages to avoid adverse effects.

What are the horticulural aspects of Galipea officinalis?

Galipea officinalis grow in tropical and subtropical regions, typically at elevations of 100-1,500 meters above sea level. It thrives in rich, well-drained soils with a pH range of 5.5-7.5. Adequate sunlight, ranging from 60-80% shade, is also essential for growth.

When planting Galipea officinalis, it's recommended to use seedlings 1-2 months old, which can tolerate transplant shock. Plant them in a location with minimal wind and frost protection. Maintain a spacing of 30-60 cm between plants to allow for optimal growth and air circulation.

Galipea officinalis can be harvested 2-3 years after planting, when the plant reaches a height of 1-2 meters. The harvesting process typically takes place from July to September, depending on the region's climate. Harvesting should be done carefully to avoid damaging the stem or roots, as this can impact the plant's yield and longevity.

Galipea officinalis is susceptible to pests such as scale insects, mealybugs, and aphids, which can cause damage to the leaves and stems. Fungal diseases, including root rot and leaf spot, are also common, particularly in wet conditions. Regular monitoring and control measures are necessary to prevent infestation and disease spread.
