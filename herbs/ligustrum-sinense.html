Ligustrum sinense

What's the taxonomical classification of Ligustrum sinense?

Ligustrum sinense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Lamiales. This species is a member of the family Oleaceae and is categorized under the genus Ligustrum, specifically identified by the species name sinese.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ligustrum sinense has opposite, simple, elliptical to ovate leaves that typically measure 5 to 15 centimeters in length and possess entire margins. The foliage is characterized by a glossy, dark green upper surface and a slightly paler underside. Its woody stems are smooth and greyish, branching out to form a dense, spreading shrub or small tree. During the flowering season, the plant produces terminal panicles of small, white, tubular flowers that emit a strong fragrance. These blossoms eventually give way to small, dark purple to blackish drupes that are borne on the branches.
