What side-effects this plant can have?

Diarrhena mandshurica can cause severe gastrointestinal distress including nausea, abdominal cramping, and persistent diarrhea due to its chemical composition. These symptoms often arise if the plant is ingested in uncontrolled amounts or without proper processing. Some individuals may also experience allergic skin reactions such as redness or itching upon direct contact with the foliage. Prolonged exposure or high doses can potentially lead to dehydration resulting from the intense digestive upset. It is essential to exercise caution as the specific toxicity levels can vary depending on the plant's maturity and environment.
