What's the taxonomical classification of Osmunda vachellii?

Osmunda vachellii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae. As a member of the order Osmundales, it falls into the family Osmundaceae. Its taxonomic hierarchy concludes with its placement in the genus Osmunda, specifically identifying it as the species vachellii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Osmunda vachellii has large, bipinnate to tripinnate fronds that can reach lengths of up to 1.5 meters and consist of fertile and sterile pinnae arranged along a central rachis. The sterile leaflets are typically bright green and possess a delicate, finely divided texture. In contrast, the fertile segments are located at the tips of the branches and often appear brownish or darker in color. These fertile portions are densely packed with small, spore-producing structures known as sporangia. The overall architecture of the plant creates a lush, spreading appearance suitable for its wetland habitats.
