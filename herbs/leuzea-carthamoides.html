Leuzea (Leuzea carthamoides): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

Leuzea (Leuzea carthamoides) is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia, known for its adaptogenic properties and traditional use in supporting energy and vitality. It is primarily used to improve conditions related to fatigue, stress, and adrenal function.

What is Leuzea (Leuzea Carthamoides)?

Leuzea carthamoides, commonly known as Leuzea, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of Asia and Europe, characterized by its erect stems, large heart-shaped leaves, and clusters of yellow flowers.

Leuzea carthamoides, commonly known as Leuzea, is a flowering plant native to South America, belonging to the Asteraceae family and characterized by its annual growth habit, erect stems, and composite flower heads.
