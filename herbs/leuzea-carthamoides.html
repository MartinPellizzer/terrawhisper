Scientific and Botanical Profile

Common Leuzea, with botanical name Leuzea carthamoides, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family, commonly known as the sunflower or daisy family.

Native to regions such as Russia, Central Asia, Mongolia, China, Kazakhstan, Tajikistan, Kyrgyzstan, Uzbekistan, Turkmenistan, and other parts of the former Soviet Union, this species is also referred to by a variety of common names, including Cape Goldenrod, Common Evening Primrose, Carthamus-Like Leuzea, Fool's Parsley, Siberian Germander, Russian Tea, Golden Alexander, Russian Buttercup, Capeweed, Common Germander, Goldenrod, Common Leuzea, Yellow Weed, Yellow Starbursh, Common Thistle, Common Goldenrod, Yellow Leuzea, Lesser Thistle, Carthamus-Like Plant, Field Thistle. Morphologically, it is a biennial or perennial herb with erect, branched stems and alternate, pinnately divided leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Its yellow flowers are typically arranged in dense, terminal clusters, resembling those of the goldenrod genus, and are accompanied by a distinctive, feathery pappus that aids in seed dispersal.

Growing, Harvesting and Storage

Leuzea carthamoides grows best in well-drained, loamy soil with a slightly acidic to neutral pH, in full sun to partial shade, and requires consistent moisture without waterlogging.

It thrives in temperate climates and should be planted in early spring after the last frost, with spacing of about 30-45 cm between plants to allow for proper growth. Regular watering is essential during the growing season, but care should be taken to avoid overwatering, which can lead to root rot. To maintain healthy growth, occasional pruning of spent flowers and dead foliage encourages new blooms and prevents disease. Harvesting should occur in late summer to early autumn when the leaves and flowers are most potent, using clean shears to cut the stems just above the base, ensuring minimal damage to the plant.

For storage, the harvested material should be spread in a single layer in a well-ventilated area to dry completely, then stored in airtight glass jars or sealed containers in a cool, dark place to preserve its medicinal potency and extend its shelf life.
