Leuzea

Leuzea carthamoides

Leuzea (Leuzea carthamoides) is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia, known for its adaptogenic properties and traditional use in supporting energy and vitality. It is primarily used to improve conditions related to fatigue, stress, and adrenal function.

Botanical Identification

Leuzea carthamoides, commonly known as Leuzea, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family. It is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Brazil. The plant has palmate leaves and yellow flower heads, and is also known as "São Paulo" in some regions. Synonyms include Leuzea carthamoides and Leuzea glabra.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Magnoliopsida
  • Order: Asterales

  • Genus: Leuzea

Safety Profile

Leuzea is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation due to limited safety data. Individuals with hormone-sensitive conditions should consult a healthcare provider before use.
