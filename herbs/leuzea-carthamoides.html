Leuzea (Leuzea carthamoides): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

What is Leuzea (Leuzea Carthamoides)?

Leuzea carthamoides, commonly known as Leuzea, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, characterized by its yellow flowers and medicinal properties traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and digestive benefits.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Leuzea is Leuzea carthamoides. This plant belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Asterales, family Asteraceae, genus Leuzea, and species carthamoides. It is commonly known as the common leuzea in Europe and the Australian leuzea in Australia. The plant is also referred to as the mountain tea in some regions. Synonyms include Leuzea carthamoides, Leuzea crassifolia, and Leuzea scabiosa.

Plant Description and Morphology

Leuzea is an upright, herbaceous perennial plant that grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring a branched stem covered in fine, soft hairs.

Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped with a pointed tip, and have a waxy surface that becomes rough to the touch. The flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences at the top of the plant. The root system consists of a central taproot with several lateral roots, making it a strong, deep-rooted plant.

The plant has a mild, slightly spicy odor, with a rough, fibrous texture to its stems and leaves.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Leuzea is native to regions of southern Europe, the Mediterranean Basin, and parts of North Africa, with a primary distribution spanning from the Iberian Peninsula to the Middle East.

It thrives in temperate to subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and sunny to partially shaded environments. The plant is commonly found in rocky or gravelly areas, often along coastal regions and in mountainous terrains. Its natural range includes countries such as Spain, France, Italy, and Turkey, where it grows in dry, open habitats.

Ecological conditions favoring Leuzea include moderate rainfall, low humidity, and good air circulation.

Therapeutic Uses

Leuzea carthamoides is traditionally used in herbal medicine to support respiratory and digestive health.

It is commonly employed to alleviate symptoms of bronchitis, asthma, and other respiratory conditions due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. The plant is also used to address digestive issues such as bloating, indigestion, and stomach discomfort. Its active compounds, including flavonoids and essential oils, contribute to its therapeutic effects.

Leuzea is often incorporated into herbal formulations for its ability to promote mucus clearance and soothe gastrointestinal irritation.

The typical dosage of Leuzea carthamoides for herbal use is 1 to 2 grams of the dried herb per day, usually divided into two or three doses, and it is typically taken with meals to enhance absorption and reduce gastrointestinal discomfort.

Leuzea (Leuzea carthamoides) is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses for short periods, but it may cause mild gastrointestinal discomfort, allergic reactions, or interactions with certain medications, and should be avoided during pregnancy, breastfeeding, and by individuals with known allergies to plants in the Asteraceae family.
