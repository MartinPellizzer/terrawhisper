Polygonum tortuosum

What's the taxonomical classification of Polygonum tortuosum?

Polygonum tortuosum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving through its hierarchical classification, it falls under the order Caryophyllales and is a member of the family Polygonaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Polygonum and the species name tortuosum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polygonum tortuosum has an erect, branched herbaceous stem that typically reaches heights of 20 to 60 centimeters and features small, lanceolate leaves with entire margins. The leaves are arranged alternately along the stem and possess long, slender petioles. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in clusters within the leaf axils, often appearing white or pinkish in color. The plant is characterized by the presence of ocreae, which are membranous sheaths surrounding the nodes of the stem. Its root system consists of a slender taproot that helps anchor the plant in its native habitats.
