Viburnum punctatum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viburnum punctatum has opposite, simple leaves that are oval to oblong in shape, measuring two to four inches long, with finely serrated margins and prominent veins. The leaf surfaces often exhibit a dark green color on the upper side and a paler green hue underneath. During the flowering stage, the plant produces small, white, five-petaled blossoms arranged in flat-topped cymes. These clusters eventually transition into small, fleshy drupes that turn from green to deep blue or black as they ripen. The stems are woody and can become grayish or brownish as the shrub matures into its full size.
