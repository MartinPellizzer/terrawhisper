Changium smyrnioides

What's the taxonomical classification of Changium smyrnioides?

Changium smyrnioides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant falls under the order Apiales and is a member of the family Apiaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by the genus Changium and the species smyrnioides.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Changium smyrnioides can cause acute gastrointestinal distress, severe skin irritation upon contact, and potential neurological tremors if ingested in high quantities. The chemical compounds within its sap act as a localized irritant that may result in redness or blistering on the epidermis. When consumed, the toxins can trigger nausea, vomiting, and intense abdominal cramping. Some documented cases also suggest that long-term exposure may lead to persistent headaches or dizziness. These adverse reactions vary in intensity depending on the individual's sensitivity to the plant's alkaloids.
