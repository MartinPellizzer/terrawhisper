What's the taxonomical classification of Changium smyrnioides?

Changium smyrnioides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant falls under the order Apiales and is a member of the family Apiaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by the genus Changium and the species smyrnioides.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the furanocoumarins 5-hydroxy-8-methoxypsoralen, bergaptol, bergapten, and xanthotoxin. These specific medicinal compounds are bioactive molecules often studied for their photochemical properties. Xanthotoxin and bergapten are frequently identified as key constituents within this chemical group. The presence of bergaptol further characterizes the complex profile of these natural substances. Together, these furanocoumarins play a significant role in the plant's chemical defense and therapeutic potential.

As discussed in a study, ten compounds were isolated and identified from Changium smyrnioides Wolff. These components include lignoceric acid (1), beta-sitosterol (2), stigmasterol (3), 5-hydroxy-8-methoxypsoralen (4), glycerylmonopalmitate (5), L-pyroglutamic acid (6), succinic acid (7), vanillic acid-4-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside (8), vanillic acid (9), and daucosterol (10). Specifically, compounds 1, 4, 5, 6, 8, and 9 are obtained from the plant for the first time.
