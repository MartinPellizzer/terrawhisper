Ginkgo (Ginkgo biloba) Uses

Ginkgo biloba is used to treat memory loss, dementia, and Alzheimer's disease due to its ability to improve blood flow and protect brain cells. It may also help with tinnitus, peripheral neuropathy, and depression by reducing oxidative stress. Additionally, it is used for insomnia, dizziness, and migraines, though evidence for these uses is less robust.

Ginkgo (Ginkgo biloba) Monograph

1. Memory loss

Ginkgo biloba is used to support cognitive function and may help with memory loss. It improves blood flow to the brain and contains antioxidants that protect brain cells. Studies suggest it may enhance memory and concentration, particularly in older adults. It is often used as a complementary therapy for age-related memory decline.

2. Dementia

Ginkgo biloba is used in herbal medicine to support cognitive function and may help manage dementia symptoms. It is believed to improve blood flow to the brain and has antioxidant properties that protect brain cells. Clinical studies suggest it may slow cognitive decline, though results vary, and it should be used under professional guidance.

3. Tinnitus

Ginkgo biloba is used to support auditory health and may help alleviate tinnitus symptoms. Its ability to improve blood flow and reduce oxidative stress can enhance inner ear function. Clinical studies suggest it may reduce the frequency and intensity of tinnitus in some individuals. It is often recommended as a complementary therapy in herbal medicine for this condition.

4. Peripheral neuropathy

Ginkgo biloba is used to support nerve function in peripheral neuropathy due to its ability to improve blood flow and reduce oxidative stress. It may help alleviate symptoms like numbness and tingling by enhancing circulation to the peripheral nerves. Standardized extracts are typically recommended for consistent potency and effectiveness.

5. Depression

Ginkgo biloba is used in herbal medicine to support mental clarity and mood regulation. It may help alleviate symptoms of depression by improving blood flow to the brain and enhancing neurotransmitter activity. Some studies suggest it can complement conventional treatments for mild to moderate depression. Its antioxidant properties also contribute to its potential antidepressant effects.

6. Insomnia

Ginkgo biloba is used to address insomnia due to its ability to enhance blood flow to the brain and reduce anxiety. It contains compounds that may regulate neurotransmitters involved in sleep, promoting relaxation and improving sleep quality. However, it should be used cautiously and under professional guidance to avoid interactions with other medications.

7. Alzheimer's disease

Ginkgo biloba is used in traditional herbal medicine to support cognitive function, particularly in Alzheimer's disease. Its active compounds, such as flavonoids and terpene lactones, may improve blood flow to the brain and reduce oxidative stress, potentially slowing cognitive decline. Clinical studies suggest it may help enhance memory and mental clarity in early-stage patients.

8. Dizziness

Ginkgo biloba is used to alleviate dizziness by improving blood circulation and enhancing oxygen delivery to the brain. Its active compounds, such as flavonoids and terpene lactones, support vascular health and may reduce symptoms associated with vertigo or motion sickness. It is often recommended for age-related cognitive decline and circulatory issues contributing to dizziness.

9. Migraine

Ginkgo biloba is used to alleviate migraine symptoms by improving blood flow and reducing inflammation. Its flavonoids and terpene lactones may help prevent migraine attacks by enhancing cerebral circulation. Studies suggest it can reduce the frequency and severity of migraines when taken regularly. It is often used as a complementary therapy in herbal medicine for migraine management.

10. Memory impairment

Ginkgo biloba is used to support memory function and cognitive performance. It improves blood flow to the brain, enhances oxygen utilization, and may reduce oxidative stress. Studies suggest it may help with age-related memory decline and conditions like dementia. It is often taken as a supplement for cognitive support.
