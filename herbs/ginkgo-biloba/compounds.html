Ginkgo (Ginkgo biloba) Compounds

The primary compounds in Ginkgo biloba include flavonoids and terpenoids, which are known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Glycosides and phenolic acids contribute to its neuroprotective effects, while coumarin and tannins support its antimicrobial activity. Saponins, essential oils, and alkaloids also play roles in its therapeutic benefits. Anthocyanins provide additional antioxidant support.

Ginkgo (Ginkgo biloba) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are key active compounds in Ginkgo biloba, known for their antioxidant properties. They help protect cells from oxidative stress and may improve blood flow. These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in supporting cognitive function and circulatory health.

2. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Ginkgo biloba, known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds contribute to the plant's cognitive-enhancing effects and are responsible for many of its therapeutic benefits in traditional herbal medicine.

3. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Ginkgo biloba. It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by enhancing circulation and neuroprotection. Glycosides in Ginkgo are known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. These compounds support cognitive function and may help in treating conditions like dementia and peripheral vascular disease.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Ginkgo biloba, contributing to its antioxidant properties. It helps neutralize free radicals, reducing oxidative stress in the body. This compound supports cognitive function and may improve blood circulation, making it a valuable component in herbal medicine for neurological and cardiovascular health.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a bioactive compound found in Ginkgo biloba. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in improving circulation and cognitive function. Coumarin may also have anticoagulant properties, though its role in the overall therapeutic effects of Ginkgo remains an area of ongoing research.

6. Tannin

Tannin is a compound found in Ginkgo biloba. It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health. Tannins in Ginkgo are also believed to have antioxidant effects, though their role in the plant's traditional medicinal uses is less emphasized compared to other compounds like flavonoids.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Ginkgo biloba. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in enhancing cognitive function and improving circulation. Saponins may also support immune function and have anti-inflammatory properties. These effects are part of the broader therapeutic profile of Ginkgo biloba in herbal medicine.

8. Essential oil

Essential oil is a volatile compound found in Ginkgo biloba. It contributes to the plant's distinctive aroma and has been studied for its potential antimicrobial and antioxidant properties. While not the primary active constituent, it complements other compounds in Ginkgo's therapeutic profile.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Ginkgo biloba. It contributes to the plant's pharmacological effects, though it is less prominent than flavonoids and terpenoids. Alkaloids in Ginkgo may support cognitive function and have mild neuroprotective properties. They are often studied alongside other active compounds for their potential therapeutic benefits.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid compound found in Ginkgo biloba. It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties and may support cognitive function by improving blood flow and protecting cells from oxidative stress. This compound is part of the complex bioactive profile of Ginkgo biloba used in traditional herbal medicine.
