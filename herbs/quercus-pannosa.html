Quercus pannosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is classified under the order Fagales. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Quercus pannosa has small, coriaceous leaves that are typically oval or obovate in shape and characterized by entire or slightly serrated margins. Its bark is generally dark and develops shallow fissures as the tree matures into adulthood. The plant produces small, pedunculate acorns that are nestled within cupules covered in scales. These structural features collectively define its physical identity within the oak genus.
