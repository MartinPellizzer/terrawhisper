Quercus pannosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Quercus pannosa?

Quercus pannosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is classified under the order Fagales. This plant is a member of the family Fagaceae and falls within the genus Quercus, specifically identified by the species name pannosa.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Quercus pannosa contains high concentrations of polyphenolic compounds, specifically tannins, flavonoids, and quercetin, which provide significant antioxidant and antimicrobial properties. These bioactive molecules work together to neutralize free radicals and protect cellular structures from oxidative stress. The presence of gallotannins in the bark and leaves offers astringent qualities that can assist in reducing inflammation. Furthermore, the specific flavonoid profile contributes to the plant's ability to inhibit certain bacterial growth. Researchers study these chemical constituents to understand their potential applications in developing natural therapeutic agents.

What are the therapeutic actions of Quercus pannosa?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various inflammatory conditions, digestive disorders, and skin irritations due to its high concentration of tannins and polyphenols. Its medicinal properties are often leveraged to manage diarrhea and other gastrointestinal disturbances. The bark and leaves can be applied topically to soothe minor wounds, rashes, and infections. Researchers also study its potential for reducing oxidative stress and systemic inflammation within the body. These therapeutic effects stem from the plant's ability to act as a natural astringent and antioxidant.
