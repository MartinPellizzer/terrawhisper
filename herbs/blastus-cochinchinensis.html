Blastus cochinchinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Blastus cochinchinensis?

Blastus cochinchinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Myrtales. This plant is further categorized into the family Melastomataceae, specifically within the genus Blastus, and is identified by its specific epithet, cochinchiensis.

What traditional systems uses this plant?

This plant, known commonly as sappanwood, has been traditionally utilized across Southeast Asia as a primary source of natural red dye for textiles and food coloring.

Historically, the heartwood was boiled to extract a deep crimson liquid used to tint fabrics and color medicinal tonics. In various folk medicine traditions, the decoction of its wood was applied to treat ailments such as diarrhea, skin infections, and menstrual irregularities. Practitioners also valued its properties for reducing inflammation and promoting blood circulation within the body.
