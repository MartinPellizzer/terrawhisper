Blastus cochinchinensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Blastus cochinchinensis has opposite, elliptical leaves with entire margins and a distinctively glossy texture. The plant exhibits a woody stem structure that supports its dense, shrubby growth habit. Its flowers are typically small and arranged in specialized clusters that emerge from the leaf axils. The reproductive structures feature delicate petals and functional stamens characteristic of the Rubiaceae family. These morphological traits allow the species to thrive in its specific tropical forest habitat.
