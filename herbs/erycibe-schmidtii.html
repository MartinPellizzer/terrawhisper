What's the taxonomical classification of Erycibe schmidtii?

Erycibe schmidtii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It is situated under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Solanales and is a member of the family Convolvulaceae. Within this family, it is categorized under the genus Erycibe, and its specific scientific designation is the species schmidtii.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Erycibe schmidtii can cause severe gastrointestinal distress including nausea, vomiting, and abdominal cramping if ingested. The specific toxic compounds within the plant trigger inflammatory responses in the digestive tract upon contact with mucous membranes. There is insufficient clinical data to definitively rule out long-term systemic toxicity or neurological impacts following accidental consumption. Consequently, extreme caution should be exercised when interacting with this species to avoid potential poisoning.
