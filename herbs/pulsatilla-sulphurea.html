Pulsatilla sulphurea

Pulsatilla sulphurea, commonly known as Meadow Anemone, is a perennial plant species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is valued for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including its use in treating menstrual irregularities, menopausal symptoms, and anxiety disorders.

The therapeutic actions of Pulsatilla sulphurea are attributed to its ability to regulate hormones, calm the nervous system, and promote relaxation.

The plant's bioactive constituents include isoflavones, alkaloids, and glycosides, which contribute to its medicinal properties and potential health benefits.
