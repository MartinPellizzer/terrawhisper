What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Mazus japonicus has a low-growing, creeping perennial habit characterized by stoloniferous stems that root at the nodes to form dense mats. During the flowering season, the plant produces small, zygomorphic flowers that are typically lavender or pale blue with white markings.
