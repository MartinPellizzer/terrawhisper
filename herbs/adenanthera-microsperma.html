Adenanthera microsperma

What's the taxonomical classification of Adenanthera microsperma?

Adenanthera microsperma belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is a member of the order Fabales and the family Fabaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Adenanthera, and its unique species designation, microsperma.

Adenanthera microsperma contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, and tannins that contribute to its pharmacological properties. What are the therapeutic actions of Adenanthera microsperma?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat digestive ailments, skin infections, and inflammatory conditions. Local healers often prepare decoctions from the leaves to alleviate stomach pains and intestinal issues. The plant also possesses antimicrobial properties that help in managing various surface wounds and skin irritations. In some traditional practices, it is applied topically to reduce swelling and soothe localized inflammation. These medicinal applications highlight its role in community-based healthcare for common physical ailments.
