How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires high humidity levels above sixty percent, consistent moisture in well-draining acidic soil, and bright but indirect light to thrive.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating Parkinson's disease by mitigating dopamine depletion and reducing oxidative stress in the brain. Research suggests that its bioactive compounds can help restore neurotransmitter levels essential for motor control. By addressing these chemical imbalances, the plant helps alleviate significant locomotor deficits in affected subjects. This therapeutic effect is crucial for managing the progressive locomotor dysfunction associated with the condition. Consequently, Selaginella delicatula shows promise as a neuroprotective agent in managing neurodegenerative symptoms.

According to a study published by "Neurotoxicology", the aqueous extract of Selaginella delicatula was investigated for its potential to offset rotenone-induced oxidative dysfunctions and neurotoxicity, which has implications for Parkinson's disease. When adult flies were fed a diet enriched with 0.05, 0.1, and 0.2% of the extract along with 500 μM of rotenone for 7 consecutive days, the extract offered concentration-dependent protection against lethality ranging from 30-95%. The extract helped attenuate locomotor deficits and protected against oxidative stress, mitochondrial dysfunction, and neurotoxicity. Specifically, Selaginella delicatula helped restore elevations in ROS, protein carbonyls, and hydroperoxides, while also normalizing dopamine depletion and acetylcholinesterase activity. Furthermore, it improved the activity levels of membrane bound enzymes, including NADH-cytochrome c reductase and succinate dehydrogenase, to protect mitochondrial integrity.
