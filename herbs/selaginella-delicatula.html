Selaginella delicatula

What's the taxonomical classification of Selaginella delicatula?

Selaginella delicatula belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Lycopodiidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Selaginellales and the family Selaginellaceae, eventually reaching the genus Selaginella. Finally, the specific identification of this plant is completed at the species level as Selaginella delicatula.

What are the herbal preparations of Selaginella delicatula?

Selaginella delicatula is prepared primarily as a decoction, a concentrated liquid extract made by boiling the plant material in water to release its active compounds. This method is frequently used in traditional practices to create medicinal teas or infusions for internal consumption. Some practitioners may also process the plant into a powder through drying and grinding for use in capsules or topical applications. Additionally, the plant can be integrated into poultices where the crushed material is applied directly to the skin. These various preparation forms allow for different methods of absorption and therapeutic delivery.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Selaginella delicatula can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and respiratory discomfort if its spores or dust are inhaled or touched directly. While the plant is primarily known for its unique biological properties, physical contact with its foliage may trigger redness or itching in sensitive individuals. Some people might experience sneezing or coughing if airborne particles from the plant enter the nasal passages. It is advisable to wear gloves when handling the species to minimize these potential dermatological reactions. Monitoring your environment for any signs of sensitivity is recommended when keeping this plant in living spaces.
