What's the taxonomical classification of Selaginella delicatula?

Selaginella delicatula belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Lycopodiidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Selaginellales and the family Selaginellaceae, eventually reaching the genus Selaginella. Finally, the specific identification of this plant is completed at the species level as Selaginella delicatula.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Selaginella delicatula has small, delicate, scale-like leaves that are arranged in four distinct rows along slender, creeping stems.

These microphylls are typically greenish to brownish in color and possess a fine, intricate texture. The plant exhibits a branching growth pattern that allows it to spread across moist substrates. Its stems are thin and wiry, providing a fragile structural framework for the dense foliage.

Small, inconspicuous sporangia are produced in specialized clusters known as strobili at the ends of the branches.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Selaginella delicatula can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and respiratory discomfort if its spores or dust are inhaled or touched directly. It is advisable to wear gloves when handling the species to minimize these potential dermatological reactions.
