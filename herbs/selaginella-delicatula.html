What's the taxonomical classification of Selaginella delicatula?

Selaginella delicatula belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Lycopodiidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Selaginellales and the family Selaginellaceae, eventually reaching the genus Selaginella. Finally, the specific identification of this plant is completed at the species level as Selaginella delicatula.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Selaginella delicatula has small, delicate, scale-like leaves that are arranged in four distinct rows along slender, creeping stems. These microphylls are typically greenish to brownish in color and possess a fine, intricate texture. The plant exhibits a branching growth pattern that allows it to spread across moist substrates. Its stems are thin and wiry, providing a fragile structural framework for the dense foliage. Small, inconspicuous sporangia are produced in specialized clusters known as strobili at the ends of the branches.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds amentoflavone, robustaflavone, and robustaflavone 4'-methyl ether. These specific flavones serve as key bioactive constituents within the plant's chemical profile. Researchers study these molecules to understand their potential pharmacological effects on human health. The presence of robustaflavone and its methylated derivative suggests a complex secondary metabolism. Identifying these distinct chemical markers helps scientists categorize the plant's medicinal properties more accurately.

As discussed in a study, Selaginella delicatulatula contains four new biflavonoids identified as robustaflavone 4'-methyl ether (1), robustaflavone 7,4'-dimethyl ether (2), 2",3"-dihydrorobustaflavone 7,4'-dimethyl ether (3), and 2",3'-dihydrorobustaflavone 7,4', 7"-trimethyl ether (4). The plant also yields two known biflavonoids, robustaflavone and amentoflavone, along with 3 caffeoylquinic acids. These three caffeoylquinic acids include 3,5-di-O-caffeoylquinic acid, 3,4-di-O-caffeoylquinic acid, and 4,5-di-O-caffeoylquinic acid. Regarding cytotoxic activity, both compounds 1 and 3 significantly suppressed the growth of Raji and Calu-1 tumor cell lines.
