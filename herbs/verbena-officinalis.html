Verbenas

Verbena officinalis

Verbenas (Verbena officinalis) is a flowering plant belonging to the Verbenaceae family, known for its medicinal properties and historical use in traditional medicine to treat various ailments, including respiratory and skin conditions.

Botanical Identification

Verbenas, scientifically known as Verbena officinalis, belong to the Verbenaceae family. Commonly called blueweed or common vervain, it is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. The plant has square stems, opposite leaves, and spikes of blue or violet flowers. It has been referenced by various synonyms in historical herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Lamiales
  • Order: Lamiales
  • Family: Verbenaceae
  • Genus: Verbena
  • Species: Verbena officinalis

Safety Profile

Verbenas are generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, they may cause gastrointestinal upset in some individuals. There are no well-documented contraindications, but caution is advised during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to limited research on their effects.
