are also prevalent, infecting the plant's leaves and stems, leading to symptoms like yellowing or browning of foliage, black spots, and eventual defoliation. Additionally, Aristolochia macrophylla is prone to root rot caused by overwatering, which can lead to a range of fungal diseases including Phytophthora and Pythium spp., while nematodes like Meloidogyne spp. can also infect the plant's roots, disrupting its ability to absorb nutrients.

Effective management strategies for these pests and diseases include proper watering habits, regular pruning, and fungicide applications, as well as introducing beneficial insects that prey on the pipevine moth larvae.
