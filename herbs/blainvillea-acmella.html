Blainvillea acmella

What's the taxonomical classification of Blainvillea acmella?

Blainvillea acmella belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. This plant is a representative of the family Asteraceae, situated within the genus Blainvillea, and is specifically identified by the species name acmella.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Blainvillea acmella has woody climbing vines characterized by opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that possess entire margins and distinct pinnate venation. The foliage is typically dark green and leathery in texture, providing a dense canopy during its growth. Its inflorescences are composed of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These flowers eventually produce small, fleshy fruits that serve as a key identifying feature of the species. The stems are flexible and capable of twisting around support structures to facilitate its climbing habit.

What are the pharmacological activities of Blainvillea acmella?

This plant has anti-osteoporosis, antioxidant, and bone anabolic pharmacological activities that contribute to its therapeutic potential. Its antioxidant properties help mitigate oxidative stress, which is a key factor in cellular damage and aging. The plant also exhibits bone anabolic effects by promoting the formation and growth of new bone tissue. These combined actions work together to strengthen skeletal integrity and combat the degradation associated with bone density loss. Consequently, these biological mechanisms make the plant a significant subject for studying bone health management.

As discussed in a study, Blainvillea acmella exhibits antioxidant activities that were tested against DPPH, ABTS, and FRAP assays. The ethanol extract of Blainvillea acmella showed higher antioxidant activities with an IC50 of 476.71 µg/ml against DPPH and 56.01 ± 6.46 mg L-ascorbic acid/g against FRAP. This plant also demonstrates bone anabolic effects on MC3T3-E1 cells, which were measured using MTT at 1, 3, 5, and 7 days, Sirius-red and ALP at 7 and 14 days, and Alizarin Red S at 14 and 21 days. Anabolic activities in bone proliferation, differentiation, and mineralization were higher in the ethanol extract compared to the aqueous extract. These antioxidant and anabolic effects may be attributed to terpenoids such as α-cubebene, cryophyllene, cryophyllene oxide, and phytol, as well as flavonoids pinostrobin and apiigenin.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

These diverse chemical constituents contribute to the plant's complex therapeutic profile and biological activities. The presence of apigenin and specific flavonoids suggests significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory potential. Furthermore, the terpenoids of α-cubebene and cryophyllene provide unique structural components that influence its medicinal efficacy. Phytol also plays a crucial role in the plant's overall chemical composition and health benefits.

As discussed in a study, the ethanol extract of Blainvillea acmella contributes antioxidant activities with an IC50 of 476.71 µg/ml against DPPH and 56.01 ± 6.46 mg L-ascorbic acid/g against FRAP. Phytochemical analysis through GCMS and LCTOFMS revealed that the plant contains terpenoids such as α-cubebene, cryophyllene, cryophyllene oxide, and phytol. Additionally, the flavonoids pinostrobin and apiogenin were identified as compounds contributing to both antioxidant and anabolic effects. These medicinal compounds are linked to the total phenolic and flavonoid contents that show correlations with ABTS, FRAP, and DPPH activities. Such properties suggest that Blainvillea acmella may serve as a valuable antioxidant and anti-osteoporosis agent.
