Viburnum sargentii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viburnum sargentii has an upright, multi-stemmed shrubby habit characterized by opposite, elliptical leaves that measure 2 to 5 inches in length and possess finely serrated margins. The foliage typically exhibits a deep green color during the growing season and can transition to reddish hues in the autumn. Its flowers are arranged in flat-topped, white cymes that emerge in late spring to early summer. Following the bloom, the plant produces small, fleshy drupes that transition from green to a dark blue or black color when ripe. The bark is generally smooth on younger stems but becomes more textured as the shrub matures.
