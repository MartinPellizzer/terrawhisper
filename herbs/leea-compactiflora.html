Leea compactiflora

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Leea compactiflora has opposite, simple leaves that are elliptic to lanceolate in shape and typically measure between 5 and 12 centimeters in length. The leaf margins are mostly entire or slightly serrated, while the venation is prominent on the undersides. This shrubby plant features small, axillary cymes that bear dense clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The inflorescences are characterized by their compact arrangement, which gives the species its specific epithet. Its woody stems provide a sturdy structure for the spreading foliage and reproductive parts.
