Daphniphyllum oldhamii

What's the taxonomical classification of Daphniphyllum oldhamii?

Daphniphyllum oldhamii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Saxifragales. It is a member of the family Daphniphyllaceae, specifically falling within the genus Daphniphyllum, with its unique biological classification completed by the specific epithet oldhamii.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Daphniphyllum oldhamii contains various bioactive alkaloids, most notably daphniphylline and its related derivatives, which exhibit significant pharmacological activities. These chemical compounds are primarily studied for their potential to act as potent anti-tumor agents by inducing apoptosis in cancer cells. Researchers also investigate these alkaloids for their ability to modulate the immune system and provide anti-inflammatory effects. The complex molecular structures of these secondary metabolites make them essential subjects for drug discovery and development. Further studies focus on isolating specific analogs to improve therapeutic efficacy while minimizing toxicity.

What are the therapeutic actions of Daphniphyllum oldhamii?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various inflammatory conditions including rheumatism, arthritis, and skin ailments such as sores or eczema. In traditional medicine, the extracts are often applied or consumed to alleviate symptoms of swelling and joint pain. It is also utilized in specific regional practices to address digestive issues and certain febrile illnesses. Researchers investigate its bioactive compounds to better understand their potential roles in managing systemic inflammation. These medicinal applications rely on the plant's diverse chemical profile to target different bodily systems.
