What's the taxonomical classification of Salsola monoptera?

Salsola monoptera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the hierarchy of biological classification, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. As a member of the order Caryophyllales, it falls under the family Amaranthaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Salsola, and its unique species designation, monoptera.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Salsola monoptera has a prostrate or ascending herbaceous habit characterized by slender, succulent stems that are often branched and covered in small, scale-like leaves.

These leaves are reduced to minute, overlapping structures that serve to minimize water loss in arid environments. The plant typically exhibits a woody base as it matures, providing structural support to its sprawling form. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in clusters along the stems, eventually developing into fleshy, somewhat winged fruits.

This specialized morphology allows the species to thrive in saline and sandy desert habitats.
