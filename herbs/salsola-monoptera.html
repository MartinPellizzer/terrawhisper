Salsola monoptera has a prostrate or ascending herbaceous habit characterized by slender, succulent stems that are often branched and covered in small, scale-like leaves. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in clusters along the stems, eventually developing into fleshy, somewhat winged fruits. This specialized morphology allows the species to thrive in saline and sandy desert habitats.
