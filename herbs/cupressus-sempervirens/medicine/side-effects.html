10 Possible Side Effects Of Cupressus Sempervirens (Italian Cypress)

Cupressus sempervirens, also known as Italian cypress, has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal problems, allergic reactions, and interactions with certain medications.

These issues can be caused by ingesting large quantities, using essential oil in aromatherapy without dilution, or combining with other herbal supplements.

In severe cases, these side effects can worsen quality of life by causing chronic pain, digestive issues, and respiratory problems, impacting daily activities and overall well-being.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Cupressus sempervirens if used imporperly.

1. Increases toxicity levels

Cupressus sempervirens, also known as Italian Cypress, causes allergic reactions due to its chemical composition.

The oil extracted from its leaves and twigs contains terpenes, which are known to cause an immune system response in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as skin irritation, itching, and respiratory issues.

Additionally, the plant's pollen can also trigger allergic reactions in people with hay fever or asthma, further exacerbating their symptoms.

2. Increases toxicity levels

Cupressus sempervirens, also known as Italian Cypress, triggers respiratory issues due to its high concentration of volatile oils, particularly alpha-pinene and beta-pinene.

These oils can cause bronchodilation, leading to constriction of airways and increased mucus production, which may exacerbate conditions such as asthma or chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).

Additionally, the oils' ability to irritate mucous membranes can lead to coughing, wheezing, and shortness of breath.

3. Increases toxicity levels

Cupressus sempervirens, also known as Italian Cypress, induces gastrointestinal problems in some individuals.

This is attributed to its potential interaction with certain medications, such as anticoagulants and blood thinners, which can alter the body's absorption of nutrients and disrupt digestion.

Additionally, the essential oils present in Cupressus sempervirens may irritate the digestive system, causing symptoms like bloating, cramps, and diarrhea.

4. Increases toxicity levels

Cupressus sempervirens, also known as Italian Cypress, aggravates skin irritations due to its high concentration of terpenes and aromatic compounds.

These potent oils can cause skin reactions, such as redness, itching, and swelling, particularly in individuals with sensitive skin or pre-existing conditions like eczema or acne.

The volatile compounds released by the tree's essential oil may also trigger allergic contact dermatitis, leading to increased discomfort and distress for those who come into contact with it.

5. Increases toxicity levels

Cupressus sempervirens, also known as Italian Cypress, provokes headaches due to its high concentration of terpenes and camphene.

These volatile oils can stimulate the brain's neurotransmitters, leading to increased blood flow and tension in the scalp.

As a result, some individuals may experience mild to moderate headaches when using Cupressus sempervirens, which can be exacerbated by factors such as anxiety or stress.

6. Increases toxicity levels

Cupressus sempervirens, also known as Italian Cypress, increases blood pressure due to its ability to stimulate the cardiovascular system.

The essential oils present in the plant, such as bornyl acetate and α-pinene, can cause a rapid increase in heart rate and blood pressure, leading to hypertension in some individuals.

This is particularly concerning for people with pre-existing cardiovascular conditions or those taking medications that already affect blood pressure.

This is because the essential oils present in the plant, particularly bornyl acetate and alpha-pinene, can slow down the contraction of the heart muscles, leading to a decrease in heart rate.

Additionally, the plant's compounds may also affect the body's autonomic nervous system, further contributing to this side effect.

8. Increases toxicity levels

Cupressus sempervirens, also known as Italian cypress, interferes with medication by potentially altering the levels of certain medications in the body.

The essential oils present in the plant can inhibit the metabolism of drugs, leading to increased or decreased blood concentrations.

This interaction may occur with blood thinners, antidepressants, and anti-inflammatory agents, among others, which can significantly impact their therapeutic effects.

Cupressus sempervirens, also known as Italian Cypress, affects liver function due to its potential interaction with certain medications and supplements.

The terpenes present in the essential oil of this plant can inhibit enzymes responsible for liver detoxification, leading to changes in liver function and potentially causing damage over time.

This effect is particularly concerning for individuals taking medications metabolized by the liver or those with pre-existing liver issues.

10. Increases toxicity levels

Cupressus sempervirens, also known as Italian Cypress, disrupts hormonal balance when used due to its phytoestrogenic properties.

The essential oils and terpenes present in the plant mimic the effects of estrogen in the body, potentially altering hormone levels and disrupting the natural balance.

This may lead to a range of symptoms, including changes in menstruation, mood swings, and reproductive issues.
