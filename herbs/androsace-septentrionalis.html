What's the taxonomical classification of Androsace septentrionalis?

Androsace septentrionalis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Ericales. This plant is a member of the family Primulaceae, specifically belonging to the genus Androsace, and is identified by the species name septentrionalis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Androsace septentrionalis has a basal rosette of small, spatulate to ovate leaves that are typically hairy and reach lengths of approximately 1 to 2 centimeters.

Each stem bears small, solitary flowers that feature a tubular corolla often characterized by a pale pink or white coloration. The calyx is deeply lobed and possesses fine pubescence along its surface.

These morphological features allow the plant to thrive in the rocky, alpine environments it inhabits.
