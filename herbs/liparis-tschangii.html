What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Liparis tschangii has creeping rhizomes that produce fleshy, elliptical to oblong leaves arranged in a distichous pattern. These leaves are typically green and possess a smooth, somewhat thickened texture. The inflorescence consists of a terminal raceme bearing small, yellowish to greenish-white flowers. Each flower features a characteristic three-lobed lip that is often slightly more prominent than the other tepals.
