Sabicea venosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sabicea venosa has opposite, elliptical leaves characterized by prominent, raised venation on the surface and a distinctively coriaceous texture. The leaf margins are entire and typically terminate in an acute or acuminate apex. Its stems exhibit a woody structure common to shrubs in the Rubiaceae family, often displaying small stipules at the nodes. The inflorescence consists of small, tubular flowers that are arranged in dense clusters. These floral structures are often white or pale in color, providing a subtle contrast to the dark green foliage.
