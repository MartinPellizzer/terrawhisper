Sabicea venosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Sabicea venosa?

Sabicea venosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Gentianales and the family Rubiaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by its placement within the genus Sabicea, culminating in its unique designation as the species venosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sabicea venosa has opposite, elliptical leaves characterized by prominent, raised venation on the surface and a distinctively coriaceous texture.

The leaf margins are entire and typically terminate in an acute or acuminate apex. Its stems exhibit a woody structure common to shrubs in the Rubiaceae family, often displaying small stipules at the nodes. The inflorescence consists of small, tubular flowers that are arranged in dense clusters.

These floral structures are often white or pale in color, providing a subtle contrast to the dark green foliage.
