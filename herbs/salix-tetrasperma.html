What's the taxonomical classification of Salix tetrasperma?

Salix tetrasperma belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Malpighiales. This plant is a member of the family Salicaceae, falling specifically within the genus Salix, and is identified by the species name tetrasperma.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Salix tetrasperma has narrow, linear leaves measuring up to 7 centimeters in length that are typically bright green and possess entire margins. The plant grows as a small, low-lying shrub with slender, upright branches that often emerge from a creeping rhizome. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous catkins that appear before or alongside the new foliage. Each catkin contains numerous tiny, individual flowers that are specialized for wind pollination. The overall structure is delicate and compact, allowing it to thrive in moist, marshy environments.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause gastrointestinal distress, allergic skin reactions, and potential interference with blood clotting mechanisms due to its salicylate content. Individuals consuming the plant may experience nausea, stomach pain, or diarrhea as the body reacts to its chemical compounds. Contact with the foliage or bark can trigger localized dermatitis or itching in sensitive individuals. Because it contains compounds similar to aspirin, it may increase the risk of bleeding or interact poorly with anticoagulant medications. People with aspirin allergies or those scheduled for surgery should exercise extreme caution.
