Salix tetrasperma

What's the taxonomical classification of Salix tetrasperma?

Salix tetrasperma belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Malpighiales. This plant is a member of the family Salicaceae, falling specifically within the genus Salix, and is identified by the species name tetrasperma.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Salix tetrasperma contains salicin, various phenolic glycosides, and flavonoids that serve as its primary medicinal compounds. These chemical constituents function as precursors to salicylic acid, which provides significant analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties. The presence of tannins in the plant bark also contributes to its astringent effects during traditional medicinal applications. Researchers study these specific phytochemicals to understand their potential for treating pain and fever. Such bioactive compounds make the plant a subject of interest in ethnobotanical studies regarding natural pain relief.

What are the therapeutic actions of Salix tetrasperma?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat inflammatory conditions, fevers, and various types of physical pain. It contains salicin, a precursor to salicylic acid which acts as a natural analgesic and antipyretic. Historically, extracts from the Salix genus have been applied to alleviate symptoms of rheumatism and arthritis. The chemical properties of the plant help to reduce swelling and lower body temperature during illness. These medicinal qualities laid the foundational groundwork for the development of modern aspirin.
