Poppy (Papaver rhoeas)

Poppy (Papaver rhoeas) is a member of the Papaveraceae family, native to Europe, West Asia, and North Africa. Traditionally, its flowers, seeds, and resin have been used for infusions, decoctions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its bitter, anti-inflammatory, and antispasmodic actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and traditional chinese medicine.

Annual herbaceous plant.

Leaves
Broad leaves with upper surface glaucous green and lower surface pale green, bearing distinct stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Inflorescence composed of solitary flowers with actinomorphic symmetry, petals five in number arranged in a radial pattern, flower color ranging from pale yellow to orange-red, calyx consisting of two whorls of sepals, stamens ten in number, ovary superior with two styles.
Stems
Elongated, erect growth habit, opposite branching, glabrous surface, ribbed structure, and presence of latex vessels.
