Wild Parsnip (Pastinaca Sativa Subsp. Sylvestris)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Pastinaca sativa subsp. sylvestris.

Wild parsnip, scientifically known as Pastinaca sativa subsp. sylvestris, is a wild-growing medicinal herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, valued for its potential health benefits and unique properties. It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and digestive properties, making it a valuable adaptogen that supports the body's natural resilience. Historically, it was employed in folk remedies to treat ailments such as skin conditions, respiratory issues, and digestive discomfort, while its distinctive, peppery flavor made it a sought-after spice in ancient cuisines. In modern wellness practices, wild parsnip is increasingly used in herbal formulations to support immune function and reduce inflammation, often in combination with other medicinal plants. One of its unique features is the presence of compounds like psoralens, which have phototoxic properties, and its historical use in ancient Roman and Greek medicine highlights its long-standing role in traditional healing.
