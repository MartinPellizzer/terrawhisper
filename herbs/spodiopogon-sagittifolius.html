Spodiopogon sagittifolius

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Spodiopogon sagittifolius has lanceolate to arrow-shaped leaves with prominent central veins and serrated margins that emerge from a central stem. The foliage displays a distinct sagittal morphology, characterized by two pointed lobes at the base of each leaf blade. Its stems are typically slender and upright, supporting a dense arrangement of these specialized leaves. The texture of the leaf surface varies from smooth to slightly rugose depending on the maturity of the plant. Small, inconspicuous reproductive structures often appear at the nodes or terminal ends of the stems.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Spodiopogon sagittifolius can cause severe skin irritation, localized swelling, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Contact with the plant's sap often results in contact dermatitis characterized by redness and itching. If the leaves are consumed, individuals may experience nausea, abdominal cramping, or vomiting. Some sensitive people might also report allergic reactions such as hives or respiratory discomfort. It is crucial to avoid direct skin contact and ensure no part of the plant enters the digestive system.
