Gladiolus quartinianus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gladiolus quartinianus has slender, sword-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a fan-like pattern and emerge from an underground corm. The plant produces a tall, erect inflorescence featuring several funnel-shaped flowers that vary in color from pale pink to deep rose. Each individual blossom is characterized by six tepals and a prominent, protruding style that aids in pollination. The stem is sturdy enough to support the weight of the blooming spike throughout its growing season. These morphological features are well-adapted to the specific environmental conditions of its native habitat.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Gladiolus quartinianus can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if any part of the plant is ingested. The bulbs contain compounds that may act as irritants to sensitive skin upon direct contact. If swallowed, the plant can lead to nausea, vomiting, or stomach pain in humans and animals. While specific toxicity levels are not extensively documented, caution is advised due to the general irritant properties found in many gladiolus species. Always wear gloves when handling the plant to minimize the risk of a reaction.
