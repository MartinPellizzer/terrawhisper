What's the taxonomical classification of Gladiolus quartinianus?

Gladiolus quartinianus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It further falls under the order Asparagales and is a member of the family Iridaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Gladiolus, specifically identified by its species name, quartinianus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

What are the pharmacological activities of Gladiolus quartinianus?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological potential through its proven anticancer and cytotoxic properties. Research indicates that specific extracts from Gladiolus quartinianus can effectively inhibit the growth of various malignant cell lines. These cytotoxic effects occur as the plant's bioactive compounds interfere with essential cellular processes in cancer cells. Scientists continue to investigate the exact chemical constituents responsible for driving this therapeutic response. Understanding these mechanisms could eventually lead to the development of new treatments derived from this species.

According to a study published by "Evidence-based complementary and alternative medicine : eCAM", the methanol extracts of Gladiolus quartinianus (GQW) exhibited significant cytotoxicity against cancer cell lines. At 40 g/mL, GQW showed a growth of CCRF-CEM leukemia cells by only 25.69%. This plant achieved the lowest IC50 values below 30 μg/mL against 7/9 tested cancer cell lines. Specifically, GQW demonstrated an IC50 value of 10.57 μg/mL against CCRF-CEM cells. Furthermore, GQW induced cell cycle arrest between the G0/G1 and S phases.
