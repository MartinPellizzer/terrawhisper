Nardostachys Jatamansi (Nardostachys jatamansi) Compounds

Nardostachys jatamansi contains several primary active compounds, including flavonoids, terpenoids, and essential oils. These compounds contribute to its medicinal properties, such as anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Coumarin and phenolic acids also play roles in its therapeutic benefits.

Nardostachys Jatamansi (Nardostachys jatamansi) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are key active compounds in Nardostachys jatamansi, contributing to its therapeutic properties. These compounds exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting the plant's traditional use in treating neurological and cardiovascular conditions. They are also responsible for the plant's aromatic profile and potential pharmacological benefits.

2. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a major class of active compounds in Nardostachys jatamansi. They contribute to the plant's therapeutic properties, including anti-inflammatory and sedative effects. These compounds are responsible for the aromatic qualities and medicinal value of the plant.

3. Essential oil

The essential oil of Nardostachys jatamansi contains bioactive components like nardosinone and jatamansone. These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating anxiety and depression. The oil is extracted through steam distillation and is valued for its aromatic and therapeutic properties.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Nardostachys jatamansi. It contributes to the plant's therapeutic properties, including anti-inflammatory and sedative effects. Coumarin is also responsible for the plant's characteristic fragrance and is used in traditional herbal medicine for its calming and pain-relieving properties.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Nardostachys jatamansi. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating anxiety and insomnia. This compound is responsible for the plant's calming effects and is often extracted for herbal formulations. Glycoside's pharmacological properties support its role in Ayurvedic and herbal medicine.

6. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in Nardostachys jatamansi. It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound supports the traditional use of Nardostachys jatamansi in treating neurological and inflammatory conditions. 7. Tannin

Tannin in Nardostachys jatamansi contributes to its medicinal properties by exhibiting astringent and antimicrobial effects. It helps in reducing inflammation and promoting wound healing. This compound is also associated with the plant's traditional use in treating digestive and respiratory ailments.

8. Alkaloid

Nardostachys jatamansi contains the alkaloid jatamansine, which is known for its sedative and calming effects. This compound contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating anxiety and insomnia. Jatamansine is one of the key active components responsible for the therapeutic properties of Nardostachys jatamansi.

9. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Nardostachys jatamansi. It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Saponin helps in enhancing the therapeutic value of Nardostachys jatamansi in traditional herbal medicine.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Nardostachys jatamansi. It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in promoting mental clarity and reducing inflammation. This compound plays a key role in the therapeutic effects attributed to Nardostachys jatamansi in herbal medicine.
