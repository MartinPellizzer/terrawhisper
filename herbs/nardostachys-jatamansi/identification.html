Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of the plant is Nardostachys jatamansi, a member of the Caprifoliaceae family. It was first described by the botanist Nathaniel Wallich in 1832. The name reflects its traditional use in Ayurvedic medicine, particularly for its aromatic and therapeutic properties.

Morphological Characteristics

Nardostachys jatamansi is a perennial herb with a compact growth habit. It has a fibrous root system and slender, ascending stems. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and hairy. The inflorescence is a raceme, bearing small, purple flowers. The fruit is a capsule containing small, black seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Nardostachys jatamansi can be distinguished by its slender, branched stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves with serrated edges. It produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers in early summer. The roots are aromatic and have a distinctive woody texture, setting it apart from similar species like Nardostachys pauciflora.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Nardostachys jatamansi is native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan. It thrives in temperate climates with cool summers and cold winters. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils and is typically found at altitudes between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Nardostachys jatamansi blooms from June to July, producing purple to violet flowers. Seed formation occurs in late summer, typically between August and September. For identification purposes, the plant is harvested during the early flowering stage, usually in June, to ensure accurate botanical recognition.
