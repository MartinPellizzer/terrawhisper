Scrofella chinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Scrofella chinensis?

Scrofella chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized under the order Lamiales. This plant is a representative of the family Plantaginaceae, falling specifically into the genus Scrofella, with its unique identity defined by the species chinensis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Scrofela chinensis contains high concentrations of iridoid glycosides, specifically aucubin and catalpol, which serve as its primary bioactive constituents. These compounds are recognized for their significant anti-inflammatory and neuroprotective properties in pharmacological studies. The plant also possesses various flavonoids and phenolic acids that contribute to its antioxidant capacity. Additionally, the presence of specific alkaloids assists in regulating biological pathways related to immune response. These diverse chemical profiles collectively support the plant's traditional use in treating various inflammatory conditions.

What are the therapeutic actions of Scrofella chinensis?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat swellings of the lymph nodes, skin abscesses, boils, and various types of inflammatory lumps or tumors. It is frequently applied in traditional medicine to reduce swelling and promote the healing of localized infections. The plant possesses properties that help dissipate nodules and clear toxins from the body. Practitioners often utilize it to address conditions where heat and swelling are present in the subcutaneous tissues. This herbal remedy serves as a topical or internal agent for managing diverse dermatological and lymphatic issues.
