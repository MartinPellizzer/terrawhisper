Euonymus salicifolius

What's the taxonomical classification of Euonymus salicifolius?

Euonymus salicifolius belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further falls into the order Celastrales and is a member of the family Celastraceae. Finally, its taxonomic position is completed within the genus Euonymus, specifically identified by the species name salicifolius.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Euonymus salicifolius has opposite, lanceolate to narrowly elliptic leaves that measure between 3 and 10 centimeters in length and feature serrated margins.

The foliage is characterized by a dark green upper surface and a paler underside, often resembling the leaves of a willow tree. Its stems are woody and can become somewhat quadrangular in younger growth before maturing into a spreading shrub. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that emerge in clusters from the leaf axils.

Following flowering, it develops distinctive small, four-lobed capsules that split open to reveal bright orange or red seeds.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Euonymus salicifolius can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, including vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal pain, if ingested by humans or animals. The plant contains various alkaloids and glycosides that act as toxic irritants to the digestive tract. Skin contact with the leaves or berries may also lead to localized dermatitis or painful rashes in sensitive individuals. Because the toxicity levels can vary depending on the part of the plant consumed, accidental ingestion often requires immediate medical attention. Careful avoidance of all plant parts is necessary to prevent these adverse physiological reactions.
