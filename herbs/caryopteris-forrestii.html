Caryopteris forrestii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Caryopteris forrestii has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically 2 to 5 centimeters long and possess finely serrated margins. The foliage is characterized by a soft, grayish-green hue due to a fine covering of hairs on both the upper and lower surfaces. During the blooming season, the plant produces dense, terminal clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale blue to lavender. These inflorescences are supported by woody stems that develop a shrubby, multi-branched growth habit over time. The overall structure of the plant remains compact, making it a distinctive specimen in garden landscapes.
