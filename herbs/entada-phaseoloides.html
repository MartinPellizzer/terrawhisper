Entada phaseoloides

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Entada phaseoloides has woody climbing vines that develop large, pinnately compound leaves and thick, woody pods containing massive seeds. The stems are robust lianas that can reach significant lengths as they scramble through forest canopies. Its leaves are composed of several pairs of leaflets that are typically elliptical or oblong in shape. The flowers are generally small and arranged in clusters, often appearing in shades of white or pale yellow. Most notably, the plant produces elongated, woody legume pods that can grow to impressive sizes. These pods eventually split to reveal large, dark seeds that are characteristic of the genus.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical regions of West and Central Africa, extending from Senegal in the west to Angola in the south and stretching north toward Sudan. It primarily thrives in lowland rainforest environments and secondary forests throughout the Congo Basin. The species is frequently found in disturbed areas and forest edges where sunlight is more accessible. Its range is strictly limited to the humid, equatorial climates of the African continent. This specific distribution pattern reflects its dependence on stable tropical moisture and warm temperatures.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Entada phaseoloides targets specific enzymes and signaling proteins including cyclooxygenase, lipoxygenase, and various inflammatory cytokines to exert its medicinal effects. The plant contains bioactive compounds like alkaloids and saponins that interfere with the arachidonic acid metabolism pathway. By inhibiting cyclooxygenase enzymes, these molecules effectively reduce the production of pro-inflammatory prostaglandins. Furthermore, the phytochemicals interact with cytokine-regulating proteins to dampen systemic inflammatory responses. These specific protein interactions underpin the plant's traditional use in treating swelling and pain.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Entada phaseoloides can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, including abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, if any part of the plant is ingested improperly. The presence of certain alkaloids and glycosides within the seeds and pods may lead to toxic reactions in sensitive individuals. While some traditional practices use parts of the plant medicinally, incorrect dosage or preparation can result in systemic toxicity. There is also limited documentation regarding potential skin irritation or allergic contact dermatitis from handling the plant material. Caution is advised because the chemical composition varies significantly between different stages of plant maturity.
