Illigera grandiflora has woody climbing vines characterized by opposite leaves that are broadly ovate or elliptic in shape with entire margins and acute apices. These fruits are often reddish or yellowish when mature and contain seeds essential for reproduction. The stems are flexible, allowing the plant to scramble through forest canopies effectively.
