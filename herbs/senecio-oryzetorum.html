Senecio oryzetorum

What's the taxonomical classification of Senecio oryzetorum?

Seneciio oryzetorum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Asterales. This plant is a specific representative of the family Asteraceae, characterized by its distinctive floral structures. Within this family, it is classified under the genus Senecio, and its unique biological identity is defined by its specific epithet, oryzetorum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Seneciio oryzetorum has an erect, herbaceous stem that produces small, lanceolate to linear leaves arranged alternately along its length.

The foliage is typically characterized by a somewhat fleshy texture and can vary in size depending on the plant's stage of development. Its inflorescences consist of small, yellow flower heads that are grouped in terminal corymbs. Each flower head contains numerous tiny tubular florets that give the plant its characteristic golden appearance when in full bloom.

The root system is relatively shallow, supporting the plant's rapid growth in disturbed soils.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Senecio oryzetorum can cause severe liver damage and potential death if ingested due to the presence of toxic pyrrolizidine alkaloids. These chemical compounds lead to hepatic veno-occlusive disease, which obstructs the small veins in the liver. Chronic exposure to the plant can result in progressive liver fibrosis and permanent organ scarring. Symptoms of poisoning often include vomiting, abdominal pain, and jaundice as the liver fails. Because these toxins accumulate in the body, even small amounts consumed over time can be fatal.
