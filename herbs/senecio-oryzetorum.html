Senecio oryzetorum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Seneciio oryzetorum has an erect, herbaceous stem that produces small, lanceolate to linear leaves arranged alternately along its length. The foliage is typically characterized by a somewhat fleshy texture and can vary in size depending on the plant's stage of development. Its inflorescences consist of small, yellow flower heads that are grouped in terminal corymbs. Each flower head contains numerous tiny tubular florets that give the plant its characteristic golden appearance when in full bloom. The root system is relatively shallow, supporting the plant's rapid growth in disturbed soils.

        What side effects this plant can have?

        This plant can cause severe nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain, and significant liver toxicity due to the presence of toxic pyrrolizidine alkaloids. Once ingested, these chemical compounds can lead to acute gastrointestinal distress characterized by intense stomach cramping and frequent bowel movements. The most dangerous consequence is the potential for lasting damage to the liver, which may manifest as jaundice or organ failure. Prolonged exposure to the plant's toxins can exacerbate these systemic issues and lead to chronic health complications. Monitoring for these specific symptoms is vital if accidental consumption occurs.
