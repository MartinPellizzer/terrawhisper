Torenia asiatica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Torenia asiatica has spreading or decumbent stems that typically reach 15 to 30 centimeters in length and feature small, ovate, bright green leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is characterized by a soft, pubescent texture caused by fine hairs covering the leaf surfaces. Its most striking morphological feature is the zygomorphic flowers, which are bright yellow with dark nectar guides located on the throat. These tubular blossoms are approximately 2 to 3 centimeters wide and possess five lobes arranged in an asymmetrical pattern. The plant's overall structure is compact and herbaceous, making it suitable for ground cover in shaded environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of South and Southeast Asia, specifically spanning across India, China, Thailand, and parts of the Malay Peninsula. Its natural range is largely defined by humid, forested environments where moisture is consistently available. Populations can often be found growing in the understory of broadleaf evergreen forests. Because it thrives in warm climates, its distribution is limited by frost and extreme aridity. Many varieties have been introduced to other tropical zones globally through cultivation for ornamental purposes.
