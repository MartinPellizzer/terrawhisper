What's the taxonomical classification of Smithia conferta?

Smithia conferta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further organized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Fabales. As a member of the family Fabaceae, this plant is placed within the genus Smithia, eventually reaching its specific taxonomic designation as the species conferta.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Smithia conferta has small, prostrate, or ascending herbs characterized by trifoliate leaves, linear to lanceolate leaflets, and small pea-like flowers typically colored yellow or white.

The stems are often slender and may trail along the ground or rise slightly above the soil surface. Its leaves consist of three distinct leaflets that are arranged symmetrically along the petiole. The inflorescence usually features solitary or few-flowered axillary racemes emerging from the leaf axils.

Small, narrow pods develop from the flowers and eventually split to release tiny seeds.
