What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Smithia conferta has small, prostrate, or ascending herbs characterized by trifoliate leaves, linear to lanceolate leaflets, and small pea-like flowers typically colored yellow or white. The stems are often slender and may trail along the ground or rise slightly above the soil surface. Its leaves consist of three distinct leaflets that are arranged symmetrically along the petiole. The inflorescence usually features solitary or few-flowered axillary racemes emerging from the leaf axils. Small, narrow pods develop from the flowers and eventually split to release tiny seeds.
