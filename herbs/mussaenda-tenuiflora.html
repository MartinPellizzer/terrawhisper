Mussaenda tenuiflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Mussaenda tenuiflora?

Mussaenda tenuiflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It further falls under the order Gentianaales and is a member of the family Rubiaceae. Within this family, the plant is categorized under the genus Mussaenda, with its specific designation being the species tenuiflora.

      What are the herbal preparations of Mussaenda tenuiflora?

      Mussaenda tenuiflora is prepared as a decoction or an infusion using the plant's leaves and flowers to extract its medicinal properties. These liquid preparations are commonly consumed orally to address various ailments such as fever or digestive issues.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Mussaenda tenuiflora can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or localized swelling if the plant's sap or leaves come into direct contact with sensitive skin. While comprehensive clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, many species within this genus contain secondary metabolites that may trigger adverse reactions. Individuals with known sensitivities to tropical flora should exercise caution when handling the foliage. Ingesting parts of the plant could potentially lead to gastrointestinal distress or other systemic complications. Always consult a medical professional if an accidental exposure occurs.
