What's the taxonomical classification of Lilium sargentiae?

Lilium sargentiae belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the hierarchy of classification, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Liliales, this plant is a member of the family Liliaceae. Specifically, it falls under the genus Lilium, and its precise biological identity is defined by its species name, sargentiae.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lilium sargentiae has upright, lanceolate leaves that grow in whorls or spirals along a stem reaching up to one meter in height. The plant produces nodding, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically bright orange or reddish-orange with dark spots on the inner tepals. These blooms often feature recurved petals and prominent stamens that extend outward from the center. The bulbous root system provides the necessary energy for its perennial growth cycle. Small, ovate capsules develop from the ovary after successful pollination to house the seeds.
