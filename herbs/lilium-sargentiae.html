Lilium sargentiae

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lilium sargentiae has upright, lanceolate leaves that grow in whorls or spirals along a stem reaching up to one meter in height. The plant produces nodding, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically bright orange or reddish-orange with dark spots on the inner tepals. These blooms often feature recurved petals and prominent stamens that extend outward from the center. The bulbous root system provides the necessary energy for its perennial growth cycle. Small, ovate capsules develop from the ovary after successful pollination to house the seeds.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of western China, specifically within the provinces of Sichuan, Yunnan, and Tibet. It typically flourishes in mountainous terrains characterized by cool, moist climates. These lilies are often found growing in alpine meadows or along the edges of subalpine forests. The species prefers specific elevations where the soil is well-drained and organic matter is plentiful. Such specialized habitat requirements limit its natural range to these distinct eastern Himalayan corridors.

        What side effects this plant can have?

Ingestion of its components may lead to acute renal failure, particularly in sensitive species like cats. Symptoms of toxicity often include vomiting, diarrhea, and a significant loss of appetite. Topical contact with the plant's fluids can result in redness, itching, or localized dermatitis. These adverse reactions highlight the necessity of handling the specimen with extreme caution.
