Lemna minor

What's the taxonomical classification of Lemna minor?

Lemna minor belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Alismatales and into the family Araceae. Finally, the plant is situated within the genus Lemna, specifically identified by the species name minor.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Lemna minor contains high concentrations of bioactive compounds including phenolic acids, flavonoids, carotenoids, and essential fatty acids that contribute to its medicinal potential. These phytochemicals provide significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties when extracted for therapeutic use. Research suggests that the plant's protein content and mineral profile can also support nutritional interventions. Some studies explore its application in developing bioactive peptides for digestive health. Additionally, its antimicrobial characteristics are being investigated for potential topical and systemic applications.

What are the therapeutic actions of Lemna minor?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various digestive ailments including gastritis, stomach ulcers, and diarrhea in both humans and animals. Due to its high nutritional content, it serves as a functional food that supports gastrointestinal health and promotes regular bowel movements. Some traditional medicinal practices also utilize its extracts to help mitigate inflammation within the digestive tract. Additionally, its bioactive compounds are studied for their potential to combat oxidative stress related to metabolic disorders. Research continues into how its specific proteins and minerals can assist in overall systemic recovery.
