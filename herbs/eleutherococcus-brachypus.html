Eleutherococcus brachypus

What's the taxonomical classification of Eleutherococcus brachypus?

Eleutherococcus brachypus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic progression continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, eventually reaching the order Apiales. As a member of the family Araliaceae, it is further categorized under the genus Eleutherococcus, specifically identified by its unique species name, brachypus.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Eleutherococcus brachypus contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including eleutherosides, polysaccharides, sesquiterpene lactones, and various phenolic compounds. These specific eleutherosides are believed to act as adaptogens by helping the body manage physiological stress. The presence of polysaccharides contributes to the plant's potential immune-modulating properties. Additionally, the phenolic components provide antioxidant activities that may protect cells from oxidative damage. These diverse chemical constituents work together to support overall metabolic stability and energy levels.

What are the therapeutic actions of Eleutherococcus brachypus?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat fatigue, physical exhaustion, reduced immunity, and various stress-related conditions. It functions as an adaptogen to help the body resist the effects of environmental and biological stressors. Practitioners often utilize its extracts to boost metabolic processes and improve overall stamina during recovery from illness. The plant is also applied to support nervous system regulation and enhance cognitive function during periods of intense mental strain.
