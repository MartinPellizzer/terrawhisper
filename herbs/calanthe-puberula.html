What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Calanthe puberula has a terrestrial growth habit characterized by creeping rhizomes, fleshy roots, and upright stems that bear several lanceolate to ovate leaves. The foliage is typically bright green and emerges from the base of the plant to support the flowering spike. Its inflorescence consists of a terminal raceme that produces numerous small, delicate flowers. These blooms often exhibit pale colors with fine spotting or subtle patterns on the labellum. The floral structure includes a distinct spur and specialized lip segments common to the orchid family.

      What are the herbal preparations of Calanthe puberula?

      Calanthe puberula is prepared primarily as a decoction of the dried rhizomes or as a topical poultice made from crushed fresh leaves. The rhizomes are typically simmered in water to extract active alkaloids for internal use in traditional medicine.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Calanthe puberula can cause localized skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and potential gastrointestinal distress if ingested. While specific clinical studies on this particular orchid are limited, many members of the Orchidaceae family contain alkaloids or organic compounds that trigger hypersensitivity in sensitive individuals. Contact with the plant's sap or foliage may result in redness, itching, or a burning sensation on the skin. If accidental ingestion occurs, symptoms might include nausea, vomiting, or abdominal cramping. It is essential to exercise caution and avoid direct contact to prevent these adverse reactions.
