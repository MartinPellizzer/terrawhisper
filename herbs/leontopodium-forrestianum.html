Leontopodium forrestianum

What's the taxonomical classification of Leontopodium forrestianum?

Leontopodium forrestianum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. This plant is a constituent of the family Asteraceae, and its specific taxonomic hierarchy concludes with the genus Leontopodium and the species forrestianum.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat respiratory ailments such as coughs, asthma, and bronchitis, as well as digestive issues like stomach aches. Its medicinal properties are primarily utilized to alleviate inflammation and soothe irritated airways. Local healers often prepare extracts from the leaves to manage various infections and febrile conditions. The plant serves as a traditional remedy for stabilizing internal bodily functions during illness. This botanical resource plays a significant role in managing symptoms related to both systemic and localized discomfort.
