Hoodia Minor (Hoodia Currorii Var. Minor)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Hoodia currorii var. minor.

Hoodia minor, scientifically known as Hoodia currorii var. minor, is a medicinal herb native to the arid regions of southern Africa, particularly Namibia and South Africa. This rare plant is renowned for its unique ability to suppress appetite, making it a popular subject in research for weight management and metabolic health. Traditionally used by indigenous San people for centuries, Hoodia minor was consumed as a thirst quencher and to endure long periods without food or water during hunts. In modern wellness applications, it is often marketed as a natural supplement to support fat loss and enhance energy levels, though its efficacy remains a topic of scientific debate. One of its most distinctive features is the presence of the rare active compound P52, which is believed to influence appetite-regulating hormones, making it a subject of interest in both traditional and contemporary health practices.
