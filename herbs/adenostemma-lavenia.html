What's the taxonomical classification of Adenostemma lavenia?

Adenostemma lavenia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. This plant is a representative of the family Asteraceae, which is further narrowed down to the genus Adenostemma, eventually reaching its specific identification as the species lavenia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Adenostemma lavenia has a perennial woody base with opposite, sessile leaves that are linear to lanceolate in shape and possess entire margins. The foliage is typically grayish-green due to a fine covering of pubescence on the leaf surfaces. During its flowering period, the plant produces small, tubular heads arranged in terminal clusters. These inflorescences are characterized by their compact structure and are often surrounded by bracts. The overall growth habit is that of a low-growing, spreading subshrub adapted to rocky environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

These populations are typically found at elevations ranging from approximately 9,000 to over 12,000 feet. The species prefers open, well-drained habitats such as talus slopes and granite outcrops. Additionally, the anti-oxidative capacity allows the plant to neutralize free radicals that cause cellular damage. These combined actions support its potential use in developing therapeutic agents for dermatological health.
