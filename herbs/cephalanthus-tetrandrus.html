Cephalanthus tetrandrus

What's the taxonomical classification of Cephalanthus tetrandrus?

Cephalanthus tetrandrus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Gentianales. This plant is further categorized into the family Rubiaceae, which is a prominent group of flowering plants. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Cephalanthus, and its specific biological identity is defined by its species designation, tetrandrus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cephalanthus tetrandrus has an upright, woody shrub habit characterized by slender, branching stems and small, opposite, lanceolate leaves that are typically dark green and leathery.

The foliage is densely arranged along the branches, often appearing crowded near the tips of the stems. Its most distinctive feature is the terminal inflorescence, which consists of small, tubular, four-lobed flowers that are white to pale pink in color. These flowers are clustered into compact, head-like structures that emerge from the ends of the branches.

The plant also produces small, dry fruits that follow the flowering period.
