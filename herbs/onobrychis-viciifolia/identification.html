Botanical Identification of Onobrychis Viciifolia (Onobrychis viciifolia)

To identify Onobrychis Viciifolia (Onobrychis viciifolia) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Onobrychis viciifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Fabales, order Fabales, family Fabaceae, genus Onobrychis, and species viciifolia. This classification places it within the legume family, known for its nitrogen-fixing properties and medicinal uses in herbal medicine.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of the plant is Onobrychis viciifolia, first described by the botanist Johann Georg August von Bunge in 1825. It is also known by several synonyms, including Astragalus viciifolius and Onobrychis viciifolia subsp. viciifolia. Common names such as "bitter vetch" and "pea vetch" are used in various regions, reflecting its widespread recognition in herbal traditions.

Morphological Characteristics

Onobrychis viciifolia is a perennial herb with a deep taproot system, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions. It has upright, branched stems that are hairy. The leaves are pinnately compound, with 10-20 pairs of oval, hairy leaflets. The inflorescence is a raceme of yellow flowers, each with a distinctive spurred petal. The fruit is a flattened pod containing several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Onobrychis viciifolia can be distinguished by its trifoliate leaves with a long, slender central leaflet, and its pea-like, yellowish-white flowers. It differs from similar species like Astragalus by its leaf structure and the absence of spines on the stems. The plant also has a distinctive, rounded root system that helps in identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Onobrychis viciifolia is native to southern Europe and western Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with mild winters and warm summers. The plant prefers well-drained soils, such as loamy or sandy soils. It typically grows at altitudes between 500 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Onobrychis viciifolia blooms from May to July, producing yellow flowers in racemes. Seed formation occurs from late summer to early autumn. For identification purposes, the plant is typically harvested in late summer when the seeds are mature and the plant is in its peak growth stage.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Onobrychis viciifolia is closely related to species within the Genus Onobrychis, particularly those in the tribe Astragalideae. It shares key family traits with legumes, including pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. Its phylogenetic placement highlights its evolutionary ties to other vetch-like plants.
