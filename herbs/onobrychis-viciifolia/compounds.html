Onobrychis Viciifolia (Onobrychis viciifolia) Compounds

Onobrychis viciifolia contains several key compounds, including flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarins and glycosides may support digestive health, while saponins and terpenoids have potential detoxifying effects. Essential oils, alkaloids, and anthocyanins further enhance its therapeutic value in herbal medicine.

Onobrychis Viciifolia (Onobrychis viciifolia) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are key active compounds in Onobrychis viciifolia, known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for treating ailments like inflammation and digestive issues. They are also believed to support cardiovascular health.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Onobrychis viciifolia. It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Tannins in this plant may support digestive health and wound healing.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Onobrychis viciifolia, contributing to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. It helps modulate immune responses and protects cells from oxidative stress, supporting the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for treating inflammatory conditions.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Onobrychis viciifolia. It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, including antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory effects. Coumarin is also known for its ability to inhibit blood clotting, supporting its use in traditional herbal medicine.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Onobrychis viciifolia. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating inflammatory conditions and respiratory issues. Glycosides in this plant are known for their anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. They are often extracted and used in herbal formulations.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Onobrychis viciifolia. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine, particularly for its anti-inflammatory and digestive properties. Saponins in this plant may support liver function and help in treating conditions like jaundice. Their presence enhances the plant's therapeutic value in herbal formulations.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Onobrychis viciifolia contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. They support the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments. Terpenoids are key to the plant's therapeutic value in herbal medicine.

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of Onobrychis viciifolia contains volatile compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. These components are valuable in herbal medicine for treating infections and reducing inflammation.

9. Alkaloid

Onobrychis viciifolia contains the alkaloid onobrychisin. This compound is known for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. It contributes to the plant's use in traditional herbal medicine for treating pain and inflammation. Onobrychisin is one of the key active components responsible for the therapeutic effects of Onobrychis viciifolia.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanins are bioactive compounds found in Onobrychis viciifolia. They contribute to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. These compounds are also associated with anti-inflammatory and cardioprotective effects, making them important in the therapeutic potential of this medicinal plant.
