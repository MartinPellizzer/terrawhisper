Sagraea umbrosa

Sagraea umbrosa, also known as the forest sagraea or umbrosa sagraea, is a plant species native to Central and South America, characterized by its small, white flowers and heart-shaped leaves.

Traditionally, Sagraea umbrosa has been used for its medicinal properties, particularly in treating various health issues such as fever, rheumatism, and gastrointestinal problems.

The plant's leaves and roots are rich in flavonoids and other bioactive compounds, which are responsible for its health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties.

Sagraea umbrosa is often prepared as a tea or tincture, and can also be used in infusions and decoctions to harness its medicinal and health-promoting effects.
