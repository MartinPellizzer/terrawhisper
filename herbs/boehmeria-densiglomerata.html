Boehmeria densiglomerata

What's the taxonomical classification of Boehmeria densiglomerata?

Boehmeria densiglomerata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Rosales. This plant is a member of the family Urticaceae and is specifically identified within the genus Boehmeria, reaching its final taxonomic designation as the species densiglomerata.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin conditions such as eczema, psoriasis, and various types of dermatitis. Its medicinal properties are primarily utilized for their ability to soothe inflammation and alleviate itching on the surface of the body. Some traditional practices also apply the plant to assist with wound healing and minor skin irritations. Researchers continue to investigate the specific bioactive compounds that contribute to these dermatological benefits. These applications focus on managing the symptoms of chronic skin ailments through topical administration.
