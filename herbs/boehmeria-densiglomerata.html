Boehmeria densiglomerata

What's the taxonomical classification of Boehmeria densiglomerata?

Boehmeria densiglomerata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Rosales. This plant is a member of the family Urticaceae and is specifically identified within the genus Boehmeria, reaching its final taxonomic designation as the species densiglomerata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Boehmeria densiglomerata has an erect, herbaceous habit characterized by opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins and dense, axillary clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers.

The stems are typically slender and may exhibit varying degrees of pubescence depending on the specific environmental conditions. The leaf venation is prominent, often displaying a pinnate pattern that extends toward the finely toothed edges. Its inflorescences are notably compact, forming the tight glomerules that give the species its name.

These dense flowering structures are arranged along the nodes, providing a distinct textural appearance to the plant's upper architecture.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Boehmeria densiglomerata can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or digestive distress if it is ingested or touched without protection. While specific toxicological studies on this exact species are limited, many plants in the Urticaceae family contain stinging hairs or chemical compounds that trigger contact dermatitis. It is essential to use gloves when handling the plant to prevent these adverse physical reactions.
