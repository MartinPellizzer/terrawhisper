Boehmeria densiglomerata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Boehmeria densiglomerata has an erect, herbaceous habit characterized by opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins and dense, axillary clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers. The stems are typically slender and may exhibit varying degrees of pubescence depending on the specific environmental conditions. The leaf venation is prominent, often displaying a pinnate pattern that extends toward the finely toothed edges. Its inflorescences are notably compact, forming the tight glomerules that give the species its name. These dense flowering structures are arranged along the nodes, providing a distinct textural appearance to the plant's upper architecture.
