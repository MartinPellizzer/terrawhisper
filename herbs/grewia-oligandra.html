Grewia oligandra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic progression continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, eventually reaching the order Malvales. As a member of the family Malvaceae, it is specifically categorized under the genus Grewia, with its unique biological identity defined by the species name oligandra.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Grewia oligandra has a woody, deciduous growth habit characterized by slender branches and small, simple, lanceolate leaves that are typically arranged alternately.

The leaves possess entire margins and acute apices, often displaying a smooth or slightly leathery texture. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that are produced in relatively sparse clusters. These flowers eventually develop into small, fleshy drupes which serve as the plant's fruit.

The overall architecture of the plant is relatively compact compared to other members of its genus.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Grewia oligandra can cause severe skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if any part of the plant is ingested or touched without protection. The sap contains chemical compounds that may act as irritants to the mucous membranes and sensitive skin tissues. Ingestion of the plant material can result in nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain due to its potentially toxic properties.
