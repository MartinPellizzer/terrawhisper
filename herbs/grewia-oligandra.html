Grewia oligandra

What's the taxonomical classification of Grewia oligandra?

Grewia oligandra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic progression continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, eventually reaching the order Malvales. As a member of the family Malvaceae, it is specifically categorized under the genus Grewia, with its unique biological identity defined by the species name oligandra.

    What are the molecular targets of this plant?

    Grewia oligandra targets cyclooxygenase enzymes, lipoxygenase, and various pro-inflammatory cytokines to exert its pharmacological effects. The plant contains bioactive phytochemicals that inhibit the arachidonic acid cascade, thereby reducing the production of prostaglandins and leukotrienes. These interactions suppress the activation of nuclear factor kappa B, which is a key regulator of the immune response and inflammation. Additionally, certain secondary metabolites in the plant may modulate antioxidant enzymes to mitigate oxidative stress at the cellular level. By interfering with these specific protein pathways, the plant demonstrates significant anti-inflammatory and potentially antimicrobial properties.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Grewia oligandra can cause severe skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if any part of the plant is ingested or touched without protection. The sap contains chemical compounds that may act as irritants to the mucous membranes and sensitive skin tissues. Contact with the foliage or bark often leads to redness, itching, or localized swelling in susceptible individuals. Ingestion of the plant material can result in nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain due to its potentially toxic properties. It is essential to handle this species with gloves to mitigate the risk of adverse physical reactions.
