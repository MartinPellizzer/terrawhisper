Neanotis wightiana has pendulous, creeping stems that produce fleshy, elliptical leaves arranged in a somewhat distichous manner. These leaves are typically thick and somewhat succulent, providing a structural base for the plant's growth habit. The root system is adapted to cling to its host or substrate, facilitating its epiphytic or lithophytic lifestyle.
