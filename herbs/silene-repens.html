Silene repens

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Silene repens has a perennial growth habit characterized by a creeping rhizomatous root system, lanceolate to ovate leaves with serrated margins, and five-petaled pink to purplish flowers. The stems are typically erect and can reach heights of up to thirty centimeters. Its leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem and possess a slightly hairy texture. The calyx is tubular and often features distinct longitudinal veins. Each flower is supported by a slender pedicel and produces small, capsule-like fruits upon maturity.
