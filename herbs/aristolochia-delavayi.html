Aristolochia delaavay belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Piperales. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the family Aristolochiaceae and the genus Aristolochia, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species delaayi.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains aristolochic acids, which are potent nitrogenous compounds categorized as protoanemonin-type alkaloids. These specific chemicals serve as the primary bioactive constituents within the Aristolochia delaavayi species. Beyond these major alkaloids, the plant also houses various isoquinoline alkaloids and several organic acids. Research indicates that these medicinal compounds possess significant biological activities, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause severe nephrotoxicity, carcinogenicity, uropathy, renal failure, and extensive genitourinary tract damage due to the presence of aristolochic acids. These toxic compounds are known to damage the delicate structures of the kidneys, often leading to progressive renal failure. Furthermore, the substances possess significant carcinogenicity, which is linked to the development of upper urinary tract cancers. This process frequently results in uropathy, characterized by structural and functional changes within the urinary system. Consequently, the genitourinary tract damage caused by this species can be irreversible and life-threatening.
