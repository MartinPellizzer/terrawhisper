What's the taxonomical classification of Halogeton glomeratus?

Halogeton glomeratus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, the plant is ordered within the Caryophyllales and falls under the family Amaranthaecae. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Halogeton and is identified as the specific species glomeratus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Halogeton glomeratus has cylindrical, unbranched stems that reach up to 50 centimeters in height and are covered in densely packed, linear, succulent leaves. These leaves are arranged in whorls along the stem and possess a pointed tip. The plant lacks a complex root system, instead utilizing a simplified rhizome for anchorage in its aquatic habitat. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically borne in dense, axillary clusters known as glomerules. The entire structure is characterized by a fleshy, water-storing morphology adapted for submerged life.
