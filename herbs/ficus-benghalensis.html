Ficus Benghalensis

Ficus benghalensis, commonly known as Banyan tree or Vata, is a species of fig tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

This herb has been used in Ayurvedic medicine for its various health benefits, including reducing inflammation, improving cardiovascular health, and aiding in digestion.

The therapeutic actions of Ficus benghalensis include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which are attributed to its bioactive constituents such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids.

Regular consumption of Ficus benghalensis is believed to help in managing conditions like diabetes, hypertension, and cancer due to its potential to modulate metabolic and immune responses.

According to "Journal of ethnopharmacology", Ficus benghalensis is one of the species used in the treatment of diarrhea, stomach complaints, and diabetes, among other health conditions.

Possible Side Effects of ficus benghalensis

Ficus benghalensis can have side effects if used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, due to its high concentration of saponins.

It may also cause allergic reactions, such as skin rashes, itching, and respiratory problems, in sensitive individuals.

Additionally, excessive consumption of Ficus benghalensis can lead to kidney damage and liver toxicity, especially when combined with other medications.

In severe cases, it can even cause cardiovascular problems, including high blood pressure and irregular heartbeats.
