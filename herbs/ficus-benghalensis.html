Possible Side Effects of ficus benghalensis

Ficus benghalensis can have side effects if used improperly, such as inducing nausea, vomiting, and dizziness in some individuals.

In rare cases, excessive consumption may lead to allergic reactions, skin irritation, and respiratory problems.

Additionally, using ficus benghalensis without medical supervision can interact with other medications, causing adverse effects like increased blood pressure and decreased blood sugar levels.

Pregnant or breastfeeding women should exercise extreme caution when using this herb, as it may cause uterine contractions or affect fetal development.
