What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Microlepia striatos has large, triangular to lanceolate fronds composed of pinnate or bipinnate segments that are densely covered in fine, spreading hairs. Each pinna is arranged along a central rachis that often displays a brownish or reddish tint. The margins of the segments are frequently serrated or irregularly lobed, contributing to a rugged appearance.       What are the herbal preparations of Microlepia strigosa?

      Microlepia strigosa is prepared primarily as a decoction or a topical paste derived from its dried or fresh fronds. This fern is frequently boiled in water to create a liquid extract used for internal medicinal purposes. The crushed leaves can also be applied directly to the skin as a poultice to treat localized inflammation or wounds.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Microlepia strigosa can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and localized rashes upon direct physical contact with its foliage. The presence of fine hairs or specific chemical compounds on the surface of the plant may trigger itching or redness in sensitive individuals. If the plant material is ingested, it may lead to gastrointestinal distress such as nausea or stomach pain. Individuals with known hypersensitivity to ferns should exercise extreme caution when handling this species. Proper protective equipment like gloves should be used to minimize the risk of adverse dermatological reactions.
