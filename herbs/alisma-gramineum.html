What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Alisma gramineum has slender, creeping rhizomes and narrow, linear leaves that typically measure between 2 and 5 millimeters in width.

The foliage is dark green and grows in dense, tufted mats that can spread extensively through aquatic environments. Its flowering stems rise above the leaf canopy, bearing small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in terminal spikes. The plant features a fine-textured, grass-like appearance that allows it to weave through surrounding vegetation.

These morphological adaptations enable the species to thrive in both submerged and emergent zones of wetlands.

      What are the herbal preparations of Alisma gramineum?

      Alisma gramineum is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical wash using the dried or fresh aerial parts of the plant.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Alisma gramineum can cause allergic skin reactions such as contact dermatitis or localized itching when handled by sensitive individuals. While there is little documented evidence regarding systemic toxicity, skin irritation remains the primary concern for those with plant allergies. It is generally advisable to wear gloves when planting or maintaining this species to prevent these dermatological issues.
