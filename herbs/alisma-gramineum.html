What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Alisma gramineum has slender, creeping rhizomes and narrow, linear leaves that typically measure between 2 and 5 millimeters in width. The foliage is dark green and grows in dense, tufted mats that can spread extensively through aquatic environments. Its flowering stems rise above the leaf canopy, bearing small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in terminal spikes. The plant features a fine-textured, grass-like appearance that allows it to weave through surrounding vegetation. These morphological adaptations enable the species to thrive in both submerged and emergent zones of wetlands.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds 11-deoxyalisol C, 16beta-hydroxyalisol B acetate, 16beta-methoxyalisol B acetate, and alisol F. These specific chemical constituents are classified as sesquiterpenoids that contribute to the plant's unique biological profile. Researchers study these molecules to understand how they might interact with various physiological systems. The presence of such diverse secondary metabolites highlights the complex chemical makeup of Alisma gramineum. Such detailed phytochemical analysis is essential for exploring the plant's potential applications in pharmacology.

According to a study published by "Zhongguo Zhong yao za zhi = Zhongguo zhongyao zazhi = China journal of Chinese materia medica", researchers investigated the tritrepeneid chemical constituents in the rhizome of Alisma gramineum. Using silicon gel chromatography and HPLC techniques, 5 protosane-type tetracyclic triterpeneoids were isolated and identified. These compounds are 16,23-oxidoaalisol B(1), 11-deoxyalisol C(2), alisol F(3), 16beta-methoxyalisol B acetate (4), and 16beta-hydroxyalisol B acetate (5). All of these 5 compounds were found from Alisma gramineum for the first time.
