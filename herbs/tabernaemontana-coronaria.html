Tabernaemontana coronaria

What's the taxonomical classification of Tabernaemontana coronaria?

Tabernaemontana coronaria belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Gentianaales. This species is a member of the family Apocynaceae, falling under the genus Tabernaemontana, and is specifically identified by its species name, coronaaria.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tabernaemontana coronaria has an erect, woody shrub habit characterized by opposite, elliptical, dark green leaves with entire margins and an acute apex. The foliage typically features a glossy texture and a prominent midrib that distinguishes its leaf structure. Its most striking morphological feature is the salverform white flowers that emerge from terminal clusters. These blossoms possess a long, slender corolla tube that expands into five spreading lobes. The plant also produces small, globose fruits that develop from the fertilized ovaries.

What are the pharmacological activities of Tabernaemontana coronaria?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological activities including potent anticancer and antituberculosis properties through its bioactive secondary metabolites. Research indicates that specific alkaloids within the species can inhibit the proliferation of various malignant cell lines. Additionally, its chemical constituents exhibit inhibitory effects against Mycobacterium tuberculosis, making it a subject of interest for infectious disease studies. These medicinal qualities are primarily attributed to the presence of unique indole alkaloids found in its leaves and bark. Further investigation into these mechanisms continues to support its potential role in future drug development.

As discussed in a study, Tabernaemontana coronaaria contains the bioactive alkaloid Jerantinine A which shows potential for cancer treatment. This compound was isolated via column chromatography and characterized using UV-visible spectroscopy, FTIR, and NMR. Molecular docking studies assessed its binding affinities with 6 critical protein targets. These targets include PTEN and androgen receptor for prostate cancer, CXCR4 and HER2 for ovarian cancer, and CDK1 and NEK2 for breast cancer. Jerantinine A exhibits strong binding affinities with all 6 protein targets, suggesting it can inhibit cancer cell proliferation.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

These specific alkaloids are secondary metabolites that contribute to the biological activity of the species. Researchers study these substances to understand their potential therapeutic properties in pharmacology. The complex chemical profile of the plant serves as a foundation for developing new medicinal treatments. Each of these compounds plays a unique role in the plant's overall chemical defense and medicinal value.

As discussed in a study, chemical investigation of the stem bark of Tabernaemontana coronaria led to the isolation of several medicinal compounds. These include 3 carbazole alkaloids, 2 furoquinoline alkaloids, 1 acridone alkaloid, 3 monomeric indole alkaloids, and 2 dimeric indole alkaloids. Specifically, the monomeric indole alkaloids found are coronaridine, 10-methoxy coronaridine, and tabernaemontanine. The study also identified the dimeric indole alkaloids voacamine and tabernaelegantine B. These extracts exhibited significant antimutagenic activity against NPD and sodium azide for Salmonella typhimurium TA98 and TA100 strains.
