Tabernaemontana coronaria

What's the taxonomical classification of Tabernaemontana coronaria?

Tabernaemontana coronaria belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Gentianaales. This species is a member of the family Apocynaceae, falling under the genus Tabernaemontana, and is specifically identified by its species name, coronaaria.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Tabernaemontana coronaeria contains bioactive compounds in its leaves, bark, and roots that are utilized for their diverse medicinal properties. The leaves are frequently prepared as a decoction or poultice to treat skin inflammations and various topical ailments. Extracts from the bark are often employed to alleviate digestive issues and manage pain. Additionally, the roots are sometimes used in traditional remedies to address febrile conditions and systemic infections. These various parts provide a rich source of alkaloids that contribute to the plant's therapeutic efficacy.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse range of bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, terpenoids, steroids, and phenolic compounds.

These chemical constituents contribute to the plant's significant pharmacological potential and traditional medicinal uses. Specifically, the presence of various alkaloids is often linked to its neuroprotective and analgesic properties. Flavonoids and phenolic compounds provide essential antioxidant activities that help mitigate oxidative stress in biological systems.

Furthermore, the terpenoids and steroids found within the tissues play crucial roles in its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial actions.

The primary medicinal compounds of this plant are shown in the list below.

  • Alkaloids
  • Flavonoids
  • Terpenoids
  • Steroids
  • Phenolics

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Tabernaemontana coronaria targets various neuroreceptors, enzymes, and signaling proteins, specifically acting as an agonist or antagonist at opioid receptors, muscarinic acetylcholine receptors, and monoamine oxidase enzymes. The plant contains indole alkaloids that interact with the central nervous system to modulate neurotransmitter levels and pain signaling pathways. These bioactive compounds often exhibit inhibitory effects on acetylcholinesterase, which can enhance cholinergic transmission in the brain. Additionally, some constituents engage with inflammatory mediators like cyclooxygenase enzymes to produce antinociceptive and anti-inflammatory responses. Through these diverse protein interactions, the plant exerts its documented sedative, analgesic, and antimicrobial activities.

What are the therapeutic actions of Tabernaemontana coronaria?

Tabernaemontana coronaeria has potent antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and analgesic properties derived from its bioactive alkaloids and terpenoids. The plant's extracts are frequently used in traditional medicine to treat skin infections, wounds, and various inflammatory conditions. Some studies suggest that its chemical constituents can inhibit the growth of specific pathogenic bacteria and fungi. Additionally, the plant is known to provide relief from pain when applied topically or administered in controlled traditional preparations. These therapeutic actions make it a significant subject for pharmacological research into natural healing agents.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including skin infections, wounds, fever, and inflammation. The leaves are often crushed and applied topically to manage skin conditions and promote healing in open cuts. In some traditional medicinal practices, preparations made from the plant are ingested to help alleviate systemic symptoms like fever. It is also utilized in certain regions to address gastrointestinal distress and minor digestive issues. The specific application depends heavily on the local botanical knowledge and the part of the plant being utilized.

What are the herbal preparations of Tabernaemontana coronaria?

Tabernaemontana coronaria is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, a poultice, or a topical paste depending on the intended therapeutic use. The leaves and stems are frequently boiled in water to create a medicinal tea used for internal consumption. For external ailments such as skin irritations or wounds, the fresh plant material is crushed into a thick paste and applied directly to the affected area. Some traditional practices involve soaking the plant parts in oils or spirits to create concentrated tinctures. These various forms allow practitioners to target specific symptoms through either systemic or localized administration.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Tabernaemontana coronaaria can cause skin irritation, contact dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested due to the presence of toxic alkaloids in its sap and tissues. The milky latex released from the leaves and stems often acts as a local irritant that may lead to redness or itching upon contact with human skin. If the plant is consumed accidentally, symptoms might include nausea, vomiting, or more severe abdominal pain. There is also a risk of mucosal irritation if the sap comes into contact with the eyes or mouth. Caution is necessary when handling this species to avoid these adverse physical reactions.
