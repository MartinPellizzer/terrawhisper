What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hibiscus vitifolius has woody, climbing stems with trifoliate leaves consisting of three distinct leaflets that are typically ovate or heart-shaped with serrated margins. Its foliage is characterized by a somewhat rough texture and a deep green coloration on the upper surfaces. The flowers are large and showy, featuring five delicate petals that often display shades of pink, red, or white. As discussed in a study, Hibiscus vitifolius root extracts show potent protective action against anti-tubercular drug induced hepatotoxicity. All petroleum ether, chloroform, methanol, and aqueous extracts of the roots were found to be safe up to a dose of 2000mg/kg. The oral administration of the methanol extract at 400mg/kg showed a significant (P<0.001) reduction in levels of serum aspartate amino transaminase, alanine amino transferase, alkaline phosphatase, lactate dehydrogenase, total bilirubin, and direct bilirubin. This treatment caused a significant increase in levels of total protein, phospholipids, glycogen, catalase, and superoxide dismutase, while reducing levels of total lipids, triglycerides, cholesterol, and thiobarbituric acid reactive substances. Furthermore, histology showed a significant reduction of necrosis and fatty formation when compared to control specimens.
