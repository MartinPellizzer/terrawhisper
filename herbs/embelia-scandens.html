Embelia scandens

What's the taxonomical classification of Embelia scandens?

Embelia scandens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Ericales. This plant is further categorized into the family Primulaceae, where it is placed within the genus Embelia, ultimately reaching its specific identification as the species scandens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Embelia scandens has a woody climbing shrub habit characterized by slender, twining stems and simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to lanceolate in shape. The leaf blades possess entire margins and acute tips, often displaying a smooth, somewhat leathery texture. Small, inconspicuous flowers typically emerge from the leaf axils, presenting a structure suited for specialized pollinators. The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that develops following successful pollination. These morphological features allow the plant to efficiently navigate through forest undergrowth to reach sunlight.
