Cirsium monocephalum

What's the taxonomical classification of Cirsium monocephalum?

Cirsium monocephalum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae, falling under the genus Cirsium, and is specifically identified by the species name monocephalum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cirsium monocephalum has an erect, unbranched stem that typically reaches heights of 30 to 100 centimeters and is covered in small, white, woolly hairs. The leaves are deeply lobed or pinnatifid with spiny margins and are arranged alternately along the stem. A distinguishing feature is the single, terminal flower head which is surrounded by an involucre of overlapping, spiny bracts. The flower head consists of numerous small, tubular, purplish-pink florets that emerge from a central receptacle. Below the flower head, the stem often features small, scale-like leaves that clasp the stalk.
