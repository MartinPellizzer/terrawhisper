What's the taxonomical classification of Briggsia longipes?

Briggsia longipes belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the taxonomic lineage of the order Lamiales. This plant is further categorized under the family Gesneriaceae, placing it within the genus Briggsia, with its specific identification defined by the species longipes.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Briggsia longipes has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by long-petioled leaves and tubular, bilabiate flowers that typically emerge in shades of purple or violet.

The leaves are generally lanceolate to ovate in shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. Its most distinctive feature is the elongated corolla tube which supports a specialized pollination mechanism. The plant often develops from a creeping rhizome that allows it to spread through the forest undergrowth.

Small calyx lobes support the base of the floral structure during the blooming period.
