Rapistrum × ramosissimum

Rapistrum × ramosissimum, commonly known as False Rapiste, is a plant species in the Brassicaceae family, resulting from the hybridization of Rapistrum rugosum and Brassica ramosissima.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering health benefits such as anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects.

The therapeutic actions of Rapistrum × ramosissimum include treating various conditions like fever, rheumatism, and skin diseases, making it a valuable resource in traditional medicine.

Its bioactive constituents, including glucosinolates, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, contribute to its therapeutic potential, warranting further research into its full range of benefits.
