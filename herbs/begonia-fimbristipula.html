Begonia fimbristipula

What's the taxonomical classification of Begonia fimbristipula?

Begonia fimbristipula belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Cucurbitaleales. This species is a representative of the family Begoniaceae, situated within the expansive genus Begonia, and is specifically identified by its unique species designation, fimbristipula.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Begonia fimbristipula can cause skin irritation, mouth sores, and stomach upset if ingested or touched. The plant contains calcium oxalate crystals that act as a physical irritant to mucous membranes. When these tiny crystals come into contact with the skin, they may cause redness, itching, or a burning sensation. If a person eats any part of the plant, the crystals can cause significant swelling of the lips, tongue, and throat. This swelling can lead to difficulty swallowing or breathing in severe cases.
