What is Pimpinella procumbens?

Pimpinella procumbens is a perennial herbaceous plant that belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes plants with umbrella-like inflorescences and often aromatic foliage.

The leaves are pinnate or bipinnate, with 3-5 pairs of leaflets that are 1-2 cm long and 0.5-1.5 cm wide, with a petiole that is 1-5 cm long. The inflorescence is a compound umbel, with 5-20 rays, each bearing 10-20 small, white or pinkish flowers, 2-3 mm in diameter, and surrounded by a calyx of 5 triangular teeth.

The fruit is a schizocarp, consisting of 2-4 mericarps, each 4-5 mm long and 2-3 mm wide, with a ridged surface and a prominent beak.
