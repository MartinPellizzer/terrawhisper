Bulbophyllum cylindraceum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bulbophyllum cylindraceum has a creeping rhizome with small, egg-shaped pseudobulbs that are densely clustered and topped by a single, narrow, cylindrical leaf. The inflorescence emerges from the base of the pseudobulb and produces a long, slender rachis bearing numerous small, yellowish-white flowers. Each flower features a distinctively shaped labellum that is often mobile and sensitive to touch. The sepals are elongated and slightly fleshy, contributing to the overall delicate appearance of the bloom. These morphological traits allow the plant to adapt to its specific epiphytic niche within the forest canopy.
