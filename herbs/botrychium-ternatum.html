What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Botrychium ternatum has a dimorphic leaf structure consisting of a sterile, three-lobed basal portion and a fertile, spike-like segment that emerges from the center. The sterile portion features leaflets that are typically arranged in a single plane and often exhibit a distinct bluish-green hue. These leaflets are broadly ovate to lanceolate and are attached to a common petiole. Rising from the center of this base, the fertile spike holds small, yellowish-green spores arranged in a dense, elongated cluster. The entire plant is supported by a single stalk that emerges from a creeping, underground rhizome.
