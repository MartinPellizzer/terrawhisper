Asparagus (Asparagus officinalis)

Asparagus (Asparagus officinalis) is a member of the Asparagaceae family, native to Mediteranean Region, Middle East, and Central Asia. Traditionally, its shoots, leaves, and stems have been used for decoctions, infusions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its diuretic, tonic, and anti-inflammatory actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and japanese kampo medicine.

Leaves
Bisexual inflorescences with spadices and staminate catkins.
Flowers
Inflorescence consisting of a dense spike of small white to greenish-yellow flowers with six tepals arranged in two whorls, actinomorphic symmetry, and a single stamen with a bifid filament.
Stems
Climbing, with nodes, terete, usually glabrous, sometimes hairy, branching dichotomously, with leaf scars and axillary buds.
