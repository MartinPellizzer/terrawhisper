What is Iphiona mucronata?

Iphiona mucronata is a species of plant in the family Asteraceae, native to tropical Africa.

It is an annual herb with a single stem, typically 10-30 cm tall, and a woody base. The leaves are ovate, 5-10 cm long, and have a pointed tip, with a ciliate margin and a petiole that is 1-2 cm long. The inflorescence is a capitulum, 1-2 cm in diameter, with a ring of 5-10 phyllaries surrounding the disc, and 10-20 florets that are tubular, yellow or orange in color.

The plant produces achenes that are 4-5 mm long, with a pappus of 10-15 bristles.
