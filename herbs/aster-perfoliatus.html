Perfoliate Aster (Aster Perfoliatus)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Aster perfoliatus.

Perfoliate aster, scientifically known as Aster perfoliatus, is a medicinal herb native to the eastern United States, valued for its adaptogenic properties and traditional healing applications. This plant is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and its unique ability to form a dense mat, with leaves that often appear to be "perforated" by the stems, giving it its common name. Historically, Indigenous peoples such as the Cherokee and Iroquois used it to treat respiratory ailments, digestive issues, and skin conditions, valuing its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. In modern wellness practices, Perfoliate aster is used in herbal teas and tinctures to support immune function, reduce stress, and promote overall vitality. Its rare active compound, known as perfolatin, is believed to contribute to its adaptogenic effects, making it a unique and culturally significant herb in both traditional and contemporary herbal medicine.
