Ranunculus polii

What's the taxonomical classification of Ranunculus polii?

Ranunculus polii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its evolutionary placement continues through the order Ranunculales and the family Ranunculaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Ranunculus, and its specific species designation, polii.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Ranunculus polii contains various protoanemonin and ranunculin compounds that serve as its primary bioactive constituents. These chemical components undergo enzymatic hydrolysis to produce protoanemonin, a highly reactive and toxic lactone. This specific substance is known for its irritant properties and its ability to cause skin blistering upon contact. While these compounds exhibit biological activity, they are generally considered toxic rather than therapeutic in human applications. Consequently, the plant is primarily studied for its chemical toxicity rather than its medicinal utility.

What are the therapeutic actions of Ranunculus polii?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin conditions such as warts, corns, and various types of fungal infections through its caustic properties. The active compounds in the plant act as a local irritant that can help slough off abnormal skin growths. Historically, preparations of the plant have been applied topically to target localized ailments. However, because the sap is highly irritating, extreme caution must be taken to avoid chemical burns on healthy tissue. Its use is largely limited to specific dermatological applications due to this high level of toxicity.
