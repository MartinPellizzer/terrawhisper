Ranunculus polii

What's the taxonomical classification of Ranunculus polii?

Ranunculus polii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its evolutionary placement continues through the order Ranunculales and the family Ranunculaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Ranunculus, and its specific species designation, polii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ranunculus polii has an herbaceous growth habit characterized by basal leaves that are primarily kidney-shaped or reniform with shallowly lobed margins and smaller, less divided cauline leaves. The stems are typically slender and can be glabrous or slightly pubescent depending on the specific specimen. Its flowers feature five bright yellow petals surrounding a central cluster of numerous stamens and carpels. The fruit consists of small, smooth, or slightly textured achenes that are often slightly compressed. This plant generally reaches a modest height suited for its specific ecological niche.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains various protoanemonin compounds, which are highly irritating sesquiterpene lactones produced when the plant tissues are damaged. These chemical constituents act as a defense mechanism by causing blistering and inflammation upon contact with skin or mucous membranes. In addition to protoanemonin, the species contains ranunculin, a glycoside that undergoes enzymatic conversion into the toxic lactone. These bioactive compounds are responsible for the plant's characteristic toxicity and its ability to induce intense local irritation. Consequently, these medicinal components are studied for their potential biological activity despite their inherent danger to human tissue.
