Clerodendrum splendens

What's the taxonomical classification of Clerodendrum splendens?

Clerodendrum splendens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic classification continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Lamiales. As a member of the family Lamiaceae, this plant is further defined by its membership in the genus Clerodendrum, eventually reaching its specific identification at the species level as splendens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clerodendrum splendens has opposite, elliptical leaves with serrated margins and woody, upright stems that typically reach heights of up to two meters. The foliage is characterized by a bright green color and a somewhat rough texture on the upper surface. Small, white, tubular flowers emerge in dense terminal clusters, often appearing throughout the growing season. These blossoms transition into striking, bright red berries that provide a sharp visual contrast to the greenery. The plant's overall structure is bushy and can become quite dense as the woody branches mature.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, specifically spanning across countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. It thrives primarily within the humid understory of rainforest ecosystems where moisture levels remain consistently high. While its primary range is concentrated in these equatorial zones, certain species within the Clerodendrum genus have been introduced to other tropical climates worldwide. The distribution is heavily influenced by the availability of warm temperatures and shaded, fertile soil. Consequently, its presence is most dense in areas characterized by high annual rainfall and stable tropical weather patterns.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires bright, indirect sunlight and well-draining, moist soil to thrive in tropical or subtropical environments.

You should maintain consistent humidity levels by misting the leaves or using a pebble tray. Regular watering is necessary to keep the soil damp, but you must avoid waterlogging the roots to prevent decay. During the growing season, applying a balanced liquid fertilizer every few weeks will encourage more vibrant blooms.

Pruning the stems occasionally helps maintain a compact shape and promotes bushier growth.

What are the pharmacological activities of Clerodendrum splendens?

This plant has anti-hyperglycaemic, antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant pharmacological activities. Its specific ability to regulate blood glucose levels makes it a subject of interest for managing diabetes. Beyond glucose control, the extracts demonstrate a capacity to inhibit the growth of various pathogenic microorganisms. The presence of bioactive compounds also helps neutralize free radicals and reduce systemic inflammation. These diverse medicinal properties highlight the therapeutic potential of Clerodendrum splendens in traditional and modern medicine.

According to a study published by "Frontiers in bioscience (Scholar edition)", Clerodendrum splendens possesses anti-hyperglycaemic effects when ethanol extracts are administered at 250 mg/kg body weight alongside glucose at 18 mmoL/kg body weight. This administration ameliorated glucose tolerance with p < 0.05-0.01. The extracts also improved gut motility at a dosage of 250 mg/kg with p < 0.05-0.001. Furthermore, the plant reduced food intake during the feeding test at 250 mg/kg with p < 0.05-0.001. Phytochemical screening of the plant depicted the presence of flavonoids, alkaloids, tannins, saponins, steroids, and reducing sugars.
