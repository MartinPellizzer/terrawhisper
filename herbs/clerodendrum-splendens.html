Clerodendrum splendens

What's the taxonomical classification of Clerodendrum splendens?

Clerodendrum splendens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic classification continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Lamiales. As a member of the family Lamiaceae, this plant is further defined by its membership in the genus Clerodendrum, eventually reaching its specific identification at the species level as splendens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clerodendrum splendens has opposite, elliptical leaves with serrated margins and woody, upright stems that typically reach heights of up to two meters. The foliage is characterized by a bright green color and a somewhat rough texture on the upper surface. Small, white, tubular flowers emerge in dense terminal clusters, often appearing throughout the growing season. These blossoms transition into striking, bright red berries that provide a sharp visual contrast to the greenery. The plant's overall structure is bushy and can become quite dense as the woody branches mature.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, specifically spanning across countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. It thrives primarily within the humid understory of rainforest ecosystems where moisture levels remain consistently high. While its primary range is concentrated in these equatorial zones, certain species within the Clerodendrum genus have been introduced to other tropical climates worldwide. The distribution is heavily influenced by the availability of warm temperatures and shaded, fertile soil. Consequently, its presence is most dense in areas characterized by high annual rainfall and stable tropical weather patterns.

What are the pharmacological activities of Clerodendrum splendens?

This plant has significant pharmacological activities including anti-hyperglycaemic, antimicrobial, and anti-inflammatory properties. The anti-hyperglycaemic effect is particularly notable for its ability to assist in regulating blood glucose levels. Various studies suggest that the bioactive compounds within the plant may inhibit enzymes involved in carbohydrate metabolism. Additionally, its medicinal potential extends to fighting bacterial infections and reducing bodily inflammation. These diverse therapeutic actions make the species a subject of interest in pharmacological research.

As discussed in a study, the ethanol extracts of Clerodendrum splendens demonstrate significant pharmacological activities. Oral administration of the extract at 250 mg/kg body weight ameliorated glucose tolerance when combined with glucose at 18 mmoL/kg body weight (p < 0.05-0.01). Furthermore, the 250 mg/kg dose improved gut motility with a significance level of p < 0.05-0.001. This same dosage of 250 mg/kg also reduced food intake during the feeding test (p < 0.05-0.001). Phytochemical screening of the plant depicted the presence of flavonoids, alkaloids, tannins, saponins, steroids, and reducing sugars.
