Dracocephalum isabellae

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dracocephalum isabellae has an erect, herbaceous habit characterized by square stems, lanceolate to ovate leaves with serrated margins, and dense terminal verticillasters of hooded, violet-blue labiate flowers. The foliage is typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stem and often possesses a slightly pubescent texture. These flowering heads consist of multiple small blossoms clustered tightly at the ends of the branches. The individual flowers feature a prominent upper lip that protects the reproductive organs, a common trait among the Lamiaceae family. This morphological structure allows the plant to efficiently attract specific insect pollinators through its concentrated floral displays.
