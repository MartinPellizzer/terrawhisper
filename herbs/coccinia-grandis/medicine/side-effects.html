10 Possible Side Effects Of Coccinia Grandis (Ivy Gourd)

Coccinia grandis has some side effects when used improperly, such as diarrhea and stomach upset due to excessive consumption of its juice or extract.

This can be caused by an allergic reaction or hypersensitivity to the herb's saponins, alkaloids, and glycosides.

Prolonged diarrhea can lead to dehydration, electrolyte imbalance, and even kidney damage, significantly impacting daily life by limiting activities and requiring hospitalization.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Coccinia grandis if used imporperly.

The plant's active compounds may stimulate the production of gastric acid and bile secretion, leading to heartburn, bloating, and discomfort.

Additionally, Coccinia grandis may interfere with normal digestion, causing an imbalance of gut flora and contributing to stomach upset in susceptible individuals.
