African Eggplant (Coccinia grandis) Compounds

The primary compounds in African Eggplant include flavonoids, which act as antioxidants, and phenolic acids, known for their anti-inflammatory properties. Tannins contribute to its astringent effects, while saponins may support immune function. These compounds collectively provide the plant's medicinal value in traditional herbal practices.

African Eggplant (Coccinia grandis) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in African Eggplant (Coccinia grandis) contribute to its medicinal properties by exhibiting antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. These compounds help protect cells from damage and may support immune function, making them valuable in traditional herbal medicine practices.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid in African Eggplant (Coccinia grandis) contributes to its antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals. This compound supports the plant's traditional use in treating inflammation and oxidative stress-related conditions. It is a key component in the plant's bioactive profile, enhancing its therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

Tannin in African Eggplant (Coccinia grandis) contributes to its astringent properties, helping to reduce inflammation and promote wound healing.
