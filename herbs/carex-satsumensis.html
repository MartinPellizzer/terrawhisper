What's the taxonomical classification of Carex satsumensis?

Carex satsumensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Poales. Within this order, the plant is situated in the family Cyperaceae. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Carex, with its specific designation being the species satsumensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Carex satsumensis has a perennial growth habit characterized by dense, tufted clumps of long, narrow, bright green leaves that often reach lengths of up to 60 centimeters. The leaf blades are typically linear with slightly scabrous margins and a prominent central midrib. During its flowering period, the plant produces slender, upright spikes that emerge from the center of the foliage. These inflorescences consist of small, inconspicuous perigynia that hold the seeds. The overall structure is compact and robust, making it well-suited for stabilizing soil in its natural habitats.
