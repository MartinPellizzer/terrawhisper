Saxifraga montana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saxifraga montana has a basal rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are dark green with slightly serrated margins and a thickened texture. These leaves grow densely together to form a low-lying mound, which helps the plant conserve moisture in alpine environments. From the center of this rosette, a slender, leafless stem emerges to support a cluster of small flowers. Each flower typically features five white petals and five small, yellowish stamens arranged around a central carpel. The plant often develops a woody base as it ages, allowing it to form long-lasting tufts in rocky crevices.
