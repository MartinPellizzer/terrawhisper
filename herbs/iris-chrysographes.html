Iris chrysographes

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Iris chrysographes has sword-shaped, ensiform leaves that emerge from a rhizomatous base and produce flowers characterized by falls with intricate gold-colored markings on a darker background. The plant features a robust rhizome system that anchors it firmly in the soil while facilitating vegetative spread. Each bloom consists of three upright standards and three drooping falls, which are heavily decorated with golden veins and spots. The reproductive structures are protected within the perianth tube, supporting the plant's complex pollination strategy. This morphological combination of sturdy foliage and highly patterned petals distinguishes the species within its genus.
