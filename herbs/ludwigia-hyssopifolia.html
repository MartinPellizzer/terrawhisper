Ludwigia hyssopifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Myrtales and the family Onagraceae, which places it within the genus Ludwigia. Ultimately, its unique biological identity is defined by its specific species designation, hyssopiifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ludwigia hyssopifolia has an erect or ascending herbaceous habit characterized by slender, often branched stems and small, lanceolate to linear leaves arranged oppositely along the axis. Its floral structure consists of small, yellowish flowers that emerge from the leaf axils, often featuring four petals and numerous stamens. The reproductive organs are centered within a capsule-like fruit that develops after successful pollination.
