Hemsleya dolichocarpa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hemsleya dolichocarpa has large, tripinnate leaves that are deeply divided into narrow, lanceolate segments with serrated margins. The plant grows as a climbing fern, utilizing its specialized rhizomes to ascend through forest vegetation. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous sori located on the undersides of the pinnules. The species is characterized by its elongated, slender fruit-like structures which define its specific epithet. These morphological traits allow the fern to thrive in shaded, humid understory environments.
