Pittosporum leptosepalum

What's the taxonomical classification of Pittosporum leptosepalum?

Pittosporum leptosepalum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Apiales and falls within the family Pittosporaceae. Ultimately, it is identified by the genus Pittosporum and is specifically designated by its species name, leptosepalum.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Pittosporum leptosepalum can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. If parts of the plant are accidentally consumed, symptoms might include nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. While specific toxicity levels are not extensively documented for this exact species, many plants in the Pittosporum genus contain saponins that can be harmful. It is always advisable to use gloves when pruning and to keep the plant away from pets and small children.
