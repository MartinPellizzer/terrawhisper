What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saxifraga przewalskii has a dense, low-growing rosette of thick, fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are dark green and often covered in fine hairs. These leaves are arranged in a compact basal cluster to conserve moisture and resist harsh alpine environments. During the blooming period, the plant produces several upright flowering stalks that rise above the foliage. Each stalk bears small, star-shaped flowers characterized by five white petals and prominent central stamens. The overall structure is typical of high-altitude perennials designed to withstand wind and cold.
