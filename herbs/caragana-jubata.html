According to a study published by "BMC cancer", Caragana jubaata contains Texasin, which serves as a potential anti-tumor agent. This compound inhibits the proliferation, migration, and invasion of lung adenocarcinoma cells without harming normal cells. Texasin promotes cell senescence, arrests the cell cycle in the G1 phase, and upregulates both β-galactosidase and RB protein expression. It also induces protective autophagy, a process that can be converted to apoptosis through chloroquine co-treatment. Furthermore, Texasin inhibits the proliferation of lung adenocarcinoma cells in vivo, which is evidenced by an increase in autophagy levels within the tumors.
