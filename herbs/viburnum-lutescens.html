Viburnum lutescens

What's the taxonomical classification of Viburnum lutescens?

Viburnum lutescens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Dipsacales and the family Viburnaceaae. Finally, the plant is situated within the genus Viburnum, specifically identified as the species lutescens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viburnum lutecesns has opposite, simple leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptical in shape, featuring serrated margins and distinct pinnate venation. The leaf surfaces often display a slightly textured feel with a dull green color on the upper side and a paler underside. During the flowering stage, the plant produces flat-topped cymes composed of small, creamy white blossoms. These inflorescences transition into fleshy, yellowish-to-reddish drupes that serve as a key identifying feature. The stems are woody and can vary in thickness depending on the age of the shrub.
