Polygonum pinetorum

What's the taxonomical classification of Polygonum pinetorum?

Polygonum pinetorum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Caryophyllales. The plant is further categorized into the family Polygonaceae and the genus Polygonum, eventually reaching its specific identification as the species pinetorum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polygonum pinetorum has erect, slender stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 50 centimeters and feature alternate, lanceolate to ovate leaves with entire margins. The leaves are characterized by prominent longitudinal veins and a smooth surface texture. Small, white, or pinkish flowers are arranged in dense, terminal clusters known as racemes or panicles. Each individual flower possesses five petals and is supported by a distinctively swollen perianth. This perennial herb often develops a rhizomatous root system that facilitates its growth in montane environments.
