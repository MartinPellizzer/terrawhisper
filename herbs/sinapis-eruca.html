What is Sinapis eruca?

Sinapis eruca is an annual or biennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae, native to Europe, Western Asia, and North Africa.

It grows up to 100 cm in height, with a taproot and a branching stem, bearing alternate, simple, and entire leaves that are 5-20 cm long and 2-8 cm wide. The plant produces white, yellow, or purple flowers in racemes, 2-10 cm long, with 4-6 petals, from March to July, followed by siliques 6-15 cm long and 2-5 cm wide, containing 10-50 seeds each. The leaves, stems, and roots of Sinapis eruca contain glucosinolates, particularly glucoraphanin and gluconasturtiin, which are responsible for its toxic properties.

Sinapis eruca is a close relative of Brassica eruca, also known as garden rocket, and is considered a weed in many agricultural fields.

Additionally, Sinapis eruca has been used to treat digestive issues like diarrhea, a condition where the body loses fluids and electrolytes due to excess water in the stool. Sinapis eruca has also been used to treat wounds, cuts, and abrasions by promoting tissue repair and reducing the risk of infection.

What are the therapeutic properties this herb?

The main therapeutic properties of Sinapis eruca are anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antiseptic.

The bioactive compounds, including flavonoids, saponins, and volatile oils, such as erucin, erucamide, and selenocyanate, are responsible for its anti-inflammatory action, which reduces swelling and pain in conditions like arthritis. The analgesic properties of Sinapis eruca are attributed to its ability to inhibit the production of pain mediators, providing relief from discomfort and pain. The antiseptic properties of this plant are due to its bioactive compounds, including saponins and volatile oils, which have antimicrobial activity, helping to prevent infections and promote wound healing.

The antioxidant properties of Sinapis eruca, mediated by its flavonoids and other bioactive compounds, protect against oxidative stress and cell damage, contributing to its therapeutic effects.

What are the medicinal constituents this herb?

The most important healing constituents of Sinapis eruca are isothiocyanates, specifically allyl isothiocyanate, glucosinolates, and erucic acid.

Isothiocyanates have been shown to have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, reducing pain and inflammation, while also exhibiting antioxidant and anticancer effects. Glucosinolates contribute to the plant's antimicrobial and antifungal properties, and have been found to have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. Erucic acid has been used as a topical agent to treat skin conditions, including eczema and acne, due to its emollient and anti-inflammatory properties.

These constituents work together to provide Sinapis eruca with its medicinal properties, making it a useful treatment for various skin conditions and infections.

What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

The main herbal preparations of Sinapis eruca are mustard oil, mustard paste, mustard powder, mustard seeds, and salve.

Mustard oil is extracted from the seeds and used for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, treating conditions like arthritis and skin infections. Mustard paste is prepared by mixing the ground seeds with water or other ingredients and is used as a topical application for relieving pain and reducing swelling. Mustard powder is made by grinding the seeds into a fine powder and is used in cooking as a condiment, but also has medicinal uses such as treating respiratory issues like bronchitis.

Mustard seeds and salve are used in traditional medicine to treat various conditions including digestive issues and skin conditions due to their antifungal and antibacterial properties.
