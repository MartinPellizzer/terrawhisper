What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Balanophora laxiflora has a holoparasitic morphology characterized by a lack of chlorophyll, a reduced lack of leaves, and a specialized root system that attaches to the roots of host plants. The plant lacks a traditional stem and leaves, instead presenting as fleshy, scale-like structures that emerge from the soil. Its reproductive structures consist of small, sparsely arranged flowers that are typical of the genus. These flowers are often reddish or brownish in color, reflecting the absence of photosynthetic pigments.
