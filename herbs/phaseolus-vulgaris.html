The bioactive constituents in Phaseolus vulgaris include alkaloids, flavonoids, and saponins, which contribute to its therapeutic actions.

Bioactive Constituents

Phaseolus vulgaris carbohydrates primarily consist of complex carbohydrates, including starch and various oligosaccharides, which contribute to the plant's energy content and serve as a dietary source for both humans and animals.

In addition to carbohydrates, Phaseolus vulgaris is rich in dietary fiber, which supports digestive health by promoting regular bowel movements and enhancing gut microbiota diversity.

The fiber content also aids in reducing cholesterol levels and improving overall cardiovascular health. Another important medicinal constituent is stachyose, a type of oligosaccharide that acts as a prebiotic, stimulating the growth of beneficial gut bacteria and enhancing immune function. Protein is also present in Phaseolus vulgaris, offering essential amino acids that support muscle repair and growth.

These components collectively contribute to the plant's nutritional and medicinal value, making it a valuable addition to a balanced diet and potential therapeutic use in various health conditions.

The leaves are often used in herbal remedies for their diuretic and antidiabetic properties, while the fruits, though less commonly used, may offer similar benefits.
