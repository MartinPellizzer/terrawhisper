What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cystacanthus pyramidalis has a pyramidal growth habit characterized by woody stems that branch into dense, upright clusters. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axillary regions of the stems throughout the growing season. These floral structures are often accompanied by specialized bracts that provide structural support and protection.       What are the herbal preparations of Cystacanthus pyramidalis?

      Cystacanthus pyramidalis is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a concentrated tincture derived from its dried aerial parts.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Cystacanthus pyramidalis can cause localized skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Contact with the plant's sap or foliage may lead to redness, itching, or swelling on the skin of sensitive individuals. If the plant is swallowed, users might experience nausea, stomach cramps, or vomiting. Some people may also react with respiratory irritation if the pollen or fine particles are inhaled. It is important to handle this species with caution to avoid these adverse physiological reactions.
