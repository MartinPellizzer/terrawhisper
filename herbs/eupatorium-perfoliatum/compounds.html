Boneset (Eupatorium perfoliatum) Compounds

Boneset contains several primary compounds, including flavonoids, which have anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarin contributes to its bitter taste and may have anticoagulant effects. Saponins and glycosides support its traditional use in treating fevers and respiratory issues. These compounds work together to give Boneset its medicinal profile.

Boneset (Eupatorium perfoliatum) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Boneset (Eupatorium perfoliatum). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in treating fevers and respiratory conditions. These compounds help modulate immune responses and may aid in reducing swelling and mucus production.

2. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Boneset (Eupatorium perfoliatum). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating fevers and digestive issues. Coumarin has antispasmodic and mild anticoagulant properties, supporting its role in herbal medicine. It is also responsible for the plant's characteristic sweet aroma.

Saponin is a key active compound in Boneset (Eupatorium perfoliatum). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating fevers and digestive issues. Saponins have mild antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting the plant's role in herbal medicine.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Boneset (Eupatorium perfoliatum). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Found in significant amounts in the leaves and stems, it supports the plant's traditional use in treating fevers and respiratory conditions.

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Boneset (Eupatorium perfoliatum). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antipyretic properties. Terpenoids like alpha-pinene and camphor are responsible for some of its medicinal effects. These compounds are often used in herbal remedies to support respiratory and immune health.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Boneset (Eupatorium perfoliatum). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating fevers and digestive issues. 7. Tannin

Tannin in Boneset (Eupatorium perfoliatum) contributes to its astringent properties, helping to reduce inflammation and soothe mucous membranes. It is found in the leaves and stems, supporting the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments.

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of Boneset (Eupatorium perfoliatum) contains volatile compounds like camphor and pinene. These oils are used in traditional medicine for their antispasmodic and expectorant properties. They contribute to the plant's reputation for treating respiratory and digestive ailments.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Boneset (Eupatorium perfoliatum). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, though its specific role and concentration may vary. Alkaloids are often associated with the plant's potential effects on the immune system and respiratory health.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid compound found in Boneset (Eupatorium perfoliatum). It contributes to the plant's dark purple color and may have antioxidant properties. This compound is believed to support the plant's traditional use in treating fevers and respiratory ailments.
