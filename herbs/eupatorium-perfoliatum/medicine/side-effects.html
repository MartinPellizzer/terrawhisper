10 Possible Side Effects Of Eupatorium Perfoliatum

Eupatorium perfoliatum has some side effects when used improperly, such as allergic reactions, skin irritation, and digestive issues.

These reactions can occur due to individual sensitivities or excessive consumption.

If left unaddressed, they can worsen to cause difficulty breathing, increased heart rate, or severe stomach pain, significantly impacting daily life, productivity, and overall well-being.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Eupatorium perfoliatum causes allergic reactions in some individuals due to the presence of sesquiterpene lactones, a type of compound found in its essential oil.

These allergens can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and inflammation.

Additionally, cross-reactivity with other plants may occur, exacerbating the reaction.

2. Triggers asthma attacks

Eupatorium perfoliatum triggers asthma attacks due to its potential to cause allergic reactions and irritation in some individuals.

The plant's volatile oils can stimulate the release of histamine, a chemical that constricts airways and increases mucus production, leading to bronchospasm and wheezing.

Additionally, the presence of polyacetylenes, a type of compound found in joe pye weed, may further exacerbate respiratory issues by increasing inflammation and oxidative stress in the lungs.

3. Increases blood pressure

Eupatorium perfoliatum increases blood pressure due to its ability to stimulate the cardiovascular system.

The compound nepetalactone in the herb causes a direct effect on the blood vessels, leading to increased heart rate and blood pressure.

Additionally, the bitter constituents of Eupatorium perfoliatum may also contribute to its hypertensive effects by stimulating the release of adrenaline and increasing peripheral vascular resistance.

4. Lowers blood sugar

Eupatorium perfoliatum lowers blood sugar due to its ability to stimulate the pancreas and increase insulin production.

This property makes it a potential herb for managing type 2 diabetes, but individuals with hypoglycemia may need to monitor their levels closely.

The triterpenoid saponins present in joe pye weed are responsible for this effect, as they have been shown to enhance glucose uptake in the body's cells, leading to a decrease in blood sugar levels.

5. Induces dizziness

Eupatorium perfoliatum induces dizziness due to its ability to affect the central nervous system and alter brain chemistry.

The plant's active compounds, such as sesquiterpenes and flavonoids, may cause a temporary imbalance in neurotransmitters like serotonin and GABA, leading to feelings of lightheadedness and disorientation.

6. Causes gastrointestinal upset

Eupatorium perfoliatum causes gastrointestinal upset due to its ability to stimulate the digestive system and increase bile production.

The herb's bitter properties can irritate the stomach lining, leading to nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea in some individuals.

Additionally, Eupatorium perfoliatum may cause an increase in gut motility, which can exacerbate symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome (IBS) or other gastrointestinal disorders.

7. Produces respiratory distress

Eupatorium perfoliatum produces respiratory distress due to its ability to stimulate the lungs and airways, causing constriction and increased mucus production.

The volatile oils present in the plant can trigger an allergic response, leading to inflammation and irritation of the mucous membranes, which can further exacerbate symptoms such as wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.

This reaction is often seen when taking high doses or using the herb for extended periods.

8. Affects liver function

Eupatorium perfoliatum affects liver function due to its ability to alter the way the body processes and eliminates substances.

The compound eudesmanolide, a sesquiterpene found in the plant, is believed to be responsible for this effect by influencing cytochrome P450 enzymes and inducing the production of glutathione, a key antioxidant in liver detoxification pathways.

This altered liver function can lead to changes in the metabolism and elimination of other substances in the body.

9. Increases heart rate

Eupatorium perfoliatum increases heart rate due to its ability to stimulate the cardiovascular system.

The plant's bioactive compounds, including sesquiterpenes and phenolic acids, can bind to beta-adrenergic receptors in the heart, leading to an increase in cardiac output and heart rate.

This stimulatory effect may be more pronounced in individuals with pre-existing cardiac conditions or those taking medications that also affect the heart.

10. Causes nervousness

Eupatorium perfoliatum causes nervousness due to its ability to stimulate the central nervous system and alter brain chemistry.

The herb's active compounds, such as volatile oils and flavonoids, can increase alertness and energy levels, leading to feelings of restlessness and anxiety in some individuals.

This response is often more pronounced when taking high doses or combining Eupatorium perfoliatum with other stimulants.
