Botanical Identification of Boneset (Eupatorium perfoliatum)

To identify Boneset (Eupatorium perfoliatum) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Boneset (Eupatorium perfoliatum) Monograph

Scientific Classification

Boneset, or Eupatorium perfoliatum, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Asterales, family Asteraceae, genus Eupatorium, and species Eupatorium perfoliatum. This classification places it within the flowering plants and the sunflower family.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Eupatorium perfoliatum is the scientific name for Boneset. It was first described by Carl von Linné in 1753. Synonyms include Eupatorium rugosum and Eupatorium capillifolium. Common names include Boneset, thoroughwort, and tansy. Regional names vary, with some areas calling it Indian plantain. Historically, it was known as the "herb of the bone."

Morphological Characteristics

Boneset is an upright, branching herb with a taproot system. Its stems are square and hairy, often reddish in color. Leaves are opposite, pinnately compound, and have serrated edges. The inflorescence is a dense cluster of small, white to pink flowers. Each flower has five petals and produces a dry, three-lobed fruit containing tiny seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Boneset can be distinguished by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and purple-tipped white flowers in clustered umbels. It has a distinctive, aromatic scent and hollow stems with a characteristic "perfoliate" leaf arrangement, where leaves appear to pass through the stem. These features help differentiate it from similar species like Eupatorium rugosum.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Boneset (Eupatorium perfoliatum) is native to eastern North America, particularly the eastern United States and southern Canada. It thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers moist, rich soils and is commonly found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks. It grows at elevations ranging from lowlands to about 1,500 feet.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Boneset blooms in late summer to early fall, typically from August to October. Seed formation occurs in late fall, with seeds dispersing by winter. For identification, the plant is most identifiable during its flowering period, making late summer to early fall the optimal time for accurate botanical recognition.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Boneset (Eupatorium perfoliatum) is closely related to species like E. cannabinum and E. rugosum. It belongs to the genus Eupatorium, which is part of the family Asteraceae. The family traits include composite flowers and opposite leaf arrangements, shared with other members of the aster family.
