Impatiens longialaata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific placement within the genus Impatiens, reaching its unique biological identification at the species level as Impatiens longialaata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Impatiens longialata has succulent, lanceolate leaves with serrated margins and a distinctive reddish-purple coloration on the undersides. Its inflorescence consists of zygomorphic flowers that feature elongated nectar spurs and delicate, petaloid sepals. The plant structure is characterized by an upright, herbaceous growth habit that reaches moderate heights in moist environments.
