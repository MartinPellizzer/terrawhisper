Craterispermum laurinum

What's the taxonomical classification of Craterispermum laurinum?

Craterispermum laurinum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its hierarchical descent, it is placed in the order Gentianaales and falls within the family Rubiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Craterispermum, and its unique species designation, laurinum.

Craterispermum lauurinum contains bioactive phytochemicals such as alkaloids, saponins, tannins, and flavonoids that contribute to its pharmacological activities. What are the therapeutic actions of Craterispermum laurinum?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various inflammatory conditions, skin infections, and digestive disorders in traditional medicinal practices. Its bioactive compounds are often utilized to alleviate symptoms of eczema and other topical dermatological issues. Some practitioners also apply its extracts to combat bacterial infections through their antimicrobial properties. Additionally, the plant has been documented for its potential role in managing gastrointestinal discomfort. These therapeutic applications rely on the specific phytochemical profile found within its leaves and stems.

What are the herbal preparations of Craterispermum laurinum?

Craterispermum laurinum is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical ointment for various medicinal uses. The bark and leaves are often boiled in water to create a concentrated liquid extract used for internal consumption. This liquid preparation can also be applied externally to treat skin conditions or wounds. Some practitioners utilize the plant in the form of a poultice by mashing the fresh material directly onto the affected area. These different methods allow for the controlled extraction of active compounds depending on the desired therapeutic effect.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Craterispermum laurinum can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, and potential toxicity if ingested due to its chemical composition. The plant contains various alkaloids and secondary metabolites that may trigger nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain in humans. Contact with the foliage or stems can lead to localized dermatitis or allergic reactions on sensitive skin. Because its specific toxicological profile is not fully mapped in clinical studies, any consumption carries a significant risk of systemic poisoning. It is essential to avoid direct handling or ingestion to prevent these unpredictable adverse reactions.
