Chinese Cabbage (Brassica juncea)

Chinese Cabbage (Brassica juncea) is a member of the Brassicaceae family, native to South Asia, Eastern Europe, and Central Asia. Traditionally, its leaves, seeds, and stems have been used for decoctions, infusions, and culinary uses.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, diuretic, and expectorant actions, and has a long history of use in ayurvedic medicine, traditional chinese medicine, and european herbal medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Annual herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 30 to 100 centimeters.
Leaves
Broad leaves with upper surface glaucous green and lower surface pale green, bearing prominent stomatal bands.
Flowers
Inflorescence composed of solitary flowers arranged in a raceme, each flower having four sepals and four petals arranged in a cross shape, with white petals and yellow stamens, actinomorphic symmetry, and a single pistil with two stigmatic lobes.
Stems
Cylindrical, herbaceous, glabrous, with a solid, fleshy cross-section, and exhibiting an alternate branching pattern.
