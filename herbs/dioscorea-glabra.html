Dioscorea glabra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dioscorea glabra has a climbing habit characterized by slender, twining stems, cordate to sagittate leaves with smooth margins, and small, inconspicuous greenish flowers. Its root system consists of fleshy tubers that serve as underground storage organs for the plant. The stems can reach significant lengths as they scramble over surrounding vegetation to reach sunlight.
