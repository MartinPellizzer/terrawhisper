What is Oxalis confertifolia?

Oxalis confertifolia is a species of flowering plant in the family Oxalidaceae.

It is a perennial herb with a sprawling habit, typically growing up to 30 cm in height, and characterized by trifoliate leaves with three leaflets that are 2-6 cm long and 1-3 cm wide. The leaves are petiolate and have a cleft tip, while the inflorescence is a terminal raceme bearing 3-5 flowers with five petals, typically 1-2 cm in diameter, and a yellow to orange color. Oxalis confertifolia is native to southern South America, specifically in the Andean regions of Argentina, Chile, and Peru.

The plant produces small, globose capsules, 1-1.5 cm in diameter, containing several seeds.
