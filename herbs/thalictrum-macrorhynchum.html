Thalictrum macrorhynchum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Thalictrum macrorhynchum has erect stems that typically reach heights of 20 to 60 centimeters and bear compound, ternately divided leaves characterized by large, ovate leaflets with serrated margins. The inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous flowers that lack true petals and instead feature prominent, elongated stamens. These reproductive structures are often yellowish or greenish in color and are arranged in loose, terminal clusters. The plant develops a branched root system that anchors its herbaceous structure within moist, montane habitats. Fine hairs may be present on the stems or leaf surfaces depending on the specific environmental conditions of its growth site.
