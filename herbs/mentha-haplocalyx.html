Mentha haplocalyx

What's the taxonomical classification of Mentha haplocalyx?

Mentha haplocalyx belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Lamiales. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the family Lamiaceae, which places it within the genus Mentha, ultimately identifying it as the specific species Mentha haplocalyx.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Mentha haplocalyx has an erect, herbaceous structure characterized by square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers arranged in dense terminal verticillasters. The leaves are typically ovate or lanceolate with serrated margins and a distinct aromatic quality. Its calyx is notably small and possesses a single-lobed appearance, which distinguishes it from many other mint species. The inflorescence consists of compact clusters that emerge from the axils or the tip of the stem. Tiny, bilabiate flowers emerge from these clusters to facilitate pollination.

What are the pharmacological activities of Mentha haplocalyx?

This plant has anti-inflammatory properties characterized by the reduction of IL-6 and TNF-α production, the suppression of CCL2 and TNF-α production, the suppression of nitric oxide production, and α-glucosidase inhibitory activity. These diverse pharmacological activities suggest that the plant can effectively modulate complex immune responses. By decreasing the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines like IL-6 and TNF-α, it helps mitigate systemic inflammation. Additionally, its ability to suppress CCL2 and nitric oxide production further aids in controlling leukocyte recruitment and oxidative stress. Finally, the observed α-glucosidase inhibitory activity highlights its potential role in managing glucose metabolism.

According to a study published by "Phytomedicine : international journal of phytotherapy and phytopharmacology", Mentha haplocalyx Briq. (Herba Menthae) has shown beneficial effects on atopic dermatitis, a disease affecting 10-20% of the population worldwide. In vitro tests using concentrations of 0.125-1 mg/ml showed that Herbا Menthae significantly reduced the production of IL-6 and tumor necrosis factor (TNF)-α in HMC-1 cells. Additionally, this plant suppressed the lipopolysaccharide (LPS)-induced nitric oxide (NO) production in RAW 264.7 cells. Herbа Menthae also significantly suppressed CCL2 and TNF-α production in LPS-induced RAW 264.7 cells. These pharmacological activities contribute to the overall anti-inflammatory effects observed in the study.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds menthol, menthone, menthyl acetate, piperitone, and pulgone. These volatile essential oils contribute to the distinct aromatic profile and therapeutic properties of Mentha haplocalyx. Menthol provides a cooling sensation, while menthone and menthyl acetate influence the overall scent and biological activity. The presence of piperitone and pulgone further defines the chemical complexity of the species. Researchers study these specific constituents to understand the plant's potential applications in pharmacology.

According to a study published by "Chemistry & biodiversity", the essential oil from the aerial parts of Mentha haplocalyx contains 23 identified components representing 92.88% of the total oil composition. The main compounds within the oil are menthol at 59.71%, menthyl acetate at 7.83%, limonene at 6.98%, and menthone at 4.44%. Through bioassay-guided fractionation, three compounds were isolated, including menthol, menthyl acetate, and limonene. These substances exhibited LD50 values of 16.5, 7.91, 5.96, and 13.7 μg/adult. At 2 h after exposure, menthol showed significantly stronger repellency than the positive control at lower concentrations tested.
