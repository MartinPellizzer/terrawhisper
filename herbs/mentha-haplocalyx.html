Mentha haplocalyx

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Mentha haplocalyx has an erect, herbaceous structure characterized by square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers arranged in dense terminal verticillasters. The leaves are typically ovate or lanceolate with serrated margins and a distinct aromatic quality. Its calyx is notably small and possesses a single-lobed appearance, which distinguishes it from many other mint species. The inflorescence consists of compact clusters that emerge from the axils or the tip of the stem. Tiny, bilabiate flowers emerge from these clusters to facilitate pollination.
