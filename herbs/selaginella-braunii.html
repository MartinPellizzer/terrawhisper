Selaginella braunii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Selaginella braunii has a creeping rhizome structure with small, scale-like leaves arranged in four rows along its slender, branching stems. The plant exhibits a distinct dimorphism in its leaf structure, featuring smaller dorsal leaves and larger lateral leaves that overlap to protect the stem. These leaves are typically dark green or brownish and possess a fine, textured surface characteristic of many lycophytes. The overall growth habit is compact and spreading, allowing it to form dense, moss-like mats in moist environments. Its morphology is specifically adapted to facilitate water retention through its tightly packed foliage.

      What are the herbal preparations of Selaginella braunii?

      Selaginella braunii is prepared as a decoction, a concentrated liquid extract made by boiling the plant material in water to release its active compounds.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Selaginella braunii can cause skin irritation or allergic contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals who come into direct contact with its foliage. Some people may experience redness, itching, or a mild rash upon touching the plant's surface. While the plant is primarily used for ornamental purposes, inhaling fine spores during reproduction might trigger respiratory discomfort or sneezing in certain users. It is important to handle the plant with caution if you have known hypersensitivity to tropical fern species. Monitoring for any unusual skin reactions is recommended when incorporating this species into a home environment.
