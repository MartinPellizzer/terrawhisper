What is European Sanicle (Sanicula Europaea)?

European Sanicle (Sanicula europaea) is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small greenish-yellow flowers, commonly used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Botanical Identity and Classification

The European Sanicle (Sanicula europaea) is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its biennial or perennial growth habit, palmate leaves, and small yellowish-green flowers that typically bloom in early spring.

Common Names and Synonyms

European Sanicle (Sanicula europaea) is commonly known by various names including Sanicle, European Sanicle, and in some regions, it may be referred to as Sanicula or by local vernacular terms depending on the cultural and linguistic context.

Plant Description and Morphology

European Sanicle (Sanicula europaea) is a small, herbaceous perennial plant characterized by a slender, branched stem that grows up to 30 cm tall, with trifoliate leaves consisting of three ovate to lanceolate leaflets, and small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in loose, umbellate clusters.

Native Habitat and Distribution

The European Sanicle (Sanicula europaea) is native to Europe, specifically found in temperate regions across the continent, including parts of the UK, France, Germany, and Scandinavia, typically growing in moist, shaded woodlands and along stream banks.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally
