What's the taxonomical classification of Marsdenia oreophila?

Marsdenia oreophila belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Gentianaales. This plant is a member of the family Apocynaceae, falling under the genus Marsdenia, and is specifically identified by its species name, oreophila.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Marsdenia oreophila has climbing woody stems, opposite ovate to elliptical leaves with entire margins, and small, tubular, five-lobed flowers that are typically white or pale cream.

The leaves are characterized by a somewhat leathery texture and can vary in size depending on the specific environmental conditions of its habitat. Its inflorescences are often arranged in small clusters or cymes that emerge from the leaf axils. The reproductive structures include a specialized corolla designed to facilitate pollination by specific insect vectors.
