Ilex micrococca

What's the taxonomical classification of Ilex micrococca?

Ilex micrococca belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized under the order Aquifoliales and the family Aquifoliaceae. Finally, it is placed within the genus Ilex, specifically identified by the species name micrococca.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ilex micrococca has small, elliptical, dark green leaves that are coriaceous in texture and possess serrated margins.

The foliage typically grows in an opposite or sub-opposite arrangement along the woody stems. These leaves are characterized by a somewhat leathery surface and a distinctively narrow shape compared to other species in the genus. The plant also produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by tiny, globose drupes.

These morphological traits collectively define the compact and shrubby growth habit of the species.

By regulating these protein pathways, the plant exhibits significant anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties. These molecular interactions collectively contribute to its traditional use in treating skin conditions and inflammatory ailments.
