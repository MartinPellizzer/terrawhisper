Laggera oblonga

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Laggera oblonga has woody, perennial stems that produce lanceolate to oblong leaves with entire margins and an acute apex. These leaves are arranged in an opposite pattern and exhibit a smooth, somewhat leathery texture on their surface. The plant features terminal clusters of flowers that are typically white or pale pink with a prominent yellow center. Each blossom consists of tubular corollas that flare into five spreading lobes. The overall structure of the shrub is compact and dense, supporting its colorful floral displays.

This plant is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, specifically occurring in countries like Indonesia, Malaysia, and Thailand. The species thrives in areas characterized by high rainfall and consistent warmth throughout the year.
