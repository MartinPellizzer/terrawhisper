Caucasian Sideritis

Sideritis congesta

Caucasian Sideritis (Sideritis congesta) is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region, known for its traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments, particularly in alleviating symptoms of bronchitis and stomach inflammation.

Botanical Identification

Caucasian Sideritis, scientifically known as Sideritis congesta, belongs to the Lamiaceae family. It is also called Caucasian woundwort and is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. The plant has square stems, hairy leaves, and clustered purple flowers. It is occasionally referenced in herbal texts under synonyms like Sideritis caucasica.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Lamiales
  • Order: Lamiales
  • Family: Lamiaceae
  • Genus: Sideritis
  • Species: Sideritis congesta

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory ailments such as coughs and colds.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and as a general tonic.

Extract: Used for inflammation and pain relief.

Safety Profile

Caucasian Sideritis (Sideritis congesta) is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal upset in some individuals. Consult a healthcare provider before use, especially if you have existing health conditions or are taking other medications.
