Caucasian Sideritis (Sideritis congesta)

Caucasian Sideritis (Sideritis congesta) is a member of the Lamiaceae family, native to Greece, Balkans, and Turkey. Traditionally, its leaves, flowers, and stems have been used for infusions, decoctions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its tonic, bitter, and diuretic actions, and has a long history of use in mediterranean herbal traditions, european herbal medicine, and tibetan medicine.

Broad leaves with upper surface gray-green and lower surface white-pubescent, featuring prominent stomatal bands.
Flowers
Flowers are actinomorphic, with five white to pale pink petals arranged in a dense spike, and two stamens with filaments shorter than the anthers.
Stems
Elongated, herbaceous stems with opposite branching, smooth surface, and prominently ridged longitudinal channels.
