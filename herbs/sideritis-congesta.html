Caucasian Sideritis

Sideritis congesta

Caucasian Sideritis (Sideritis congesta) is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region, known for its traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments, particularly in alleviating symptoms of bronchitis and stomach inflammation.

Morphology

Caucasian Sideritis (Sideritis congesta) is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, branched stems that can grow up to 60 cm in height, opposite and ovate to lanceolate leaves with hairy surfaces, and small, clustered pink to purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stems.

Distribution

Caucasian Sideritis (Sideritis congesta) is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus, specifically found in countries such as Georgia, Russia, and Armenia, where it thrives in temperate and alpine habitats.
