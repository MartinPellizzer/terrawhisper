Vigna minima

What's the taxonomical classification of Vigna minima?

Vigna minima belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized under the order Fabales and falls within the family Fabaceae. Ultimately, it is identified by its genus, Vigna, and its specific species designation, minima.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vigna minima has prostrate, trailing stems that form dense mats, trifoliate leaves with small ovate leaflets, and small yellow pea-like flowers. The stems are typically slender and can grow several decimeters in length to cover the ground surface. Each leaf consists of three leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs, providing a slightly textured feel. The inflorescence produces delicate blossoms that transition from yellow to a pale hue as they age. These plants also develop small, cylindrical legume pods that contain tiny seeds essential for reproduction.
