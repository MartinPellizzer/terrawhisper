Lindernia micrantha

What's the taxonomical classification of Lindernia micrantha?

Lindernia micrantha belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further organized under the order Lamiales. It is a representative of the family Linderniaceae and is specifically identified within the genus Lindernia, ultimately falling under the species designation micrantha.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lindernia micrantha has small, opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with entire or slightly serrated margins and a herbaceous, upright stem.

The plant typically reaches a height of 10 to 30 centimeters and features a branched growth habit. Its inflorescence consists of tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are often tucked into the leaf axils. These flowers possess a tubular corolla, which is characteristic of the Linderniaceae family.

The reproductive structures include small capsules that contain numerous minute seeds for dispersal.
