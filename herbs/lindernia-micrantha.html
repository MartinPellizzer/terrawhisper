Lindernia micrantha

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lindernia micrantha has small, opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with entire or slightly serrated margins and a herbaceous, upright stem. The plant typically reaches a height of 10 to 30 centimeters and features a branched growth habit. Its inflorescence consists of tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are often tucked into the leaf axils. These flowers possess a tubular corolla, which is characteristic of the Linderniaceae family. The reproductive structures include small capsules that contain numerous minute seeds for dispersal.
