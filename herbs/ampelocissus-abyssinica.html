What's the taxonomical classification of Ampelocissus abyssinica?

Ampelociscus abyssinica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical structure of its lineage, it falls under the order Vitales and the family Vitaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement within the genus Ampelocissus, with the specific epithet abyssinica.

What are the herbal preparations of Ampelocissus abyssinica?

Ampelocisssus abyssinicat is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste depending on the intended therapeutic use. Some preparations involve macerating the plant in oils or fats to facilitate transdermal absorption of its active compounds. These various methods allow for the targeted delivery of the plant's medicinal properties to different parts of the body.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Ampelociscus abyssinicica can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, and potential neurological toxicity if ingested or touched. The plant contains bioactive compounds that may trigger intense nausea, vomiting, and abdominal cramping upon consumption. Direct contact with the plant's surfaces often results in localized dermatitis or painful allergic skin reactions. There is also evidence suggesting that certain alkaloids within the species could impact the central nervous system. Due to these unpredictable physiological responses, careful handling and avoidance of ingestion are strictly necessary.
