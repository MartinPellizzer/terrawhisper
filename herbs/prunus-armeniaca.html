Prunus Armeniaca: Medicinal Uses And Benefits

Prunus armeniaca, commonly known as the apricot, is a fruit-bearing shrub or small tree native to Asia and widely cultivated for its edible fruits.

It is rich in bioactive constituents such as vitamins A and C, beta-carotene, antioxidants, and dietary fiber.

The herb offers numerous health benefits, including supporting immune function, promoting skin health, and aiding digestion.

Its therapeutic actions include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, making it valuable in traditional and modern herbal medicine.
