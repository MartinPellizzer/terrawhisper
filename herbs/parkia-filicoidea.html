Parkia filicoidea

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Parkia filicoidea has bipinnate leaves with numerous small, oblong leaflets and woody stems that support a spreading canopy. The foliage is characterized by a delicate, fern-like texture due to the intricate arrangement of its leaf segments. Its flowers are typically small and clustered into dense, globose heads that often possess long, protruding stamens. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain the seeds within a protective fibrous structure. These morphological traits are adapted to its specific ecological niche within tropical forest environments.

      What are the herbal preparations of Parkia filicoidea?

      Parkia filicoidea is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a poultice depending on the specific ailment being addressed.
