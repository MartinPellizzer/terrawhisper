What's the taxonomical classification of Berberis pubescens?

Berberis pubevescens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the order Ranunculales and the family Berberidaceae. Ultimately, its specific identity is defined by its placement within the genus Berberis, with its unique designation being the species pubevescens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Berberis pubescens has small, elliptical to obovate leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs on both the upper and lower surfaces. These leaves are typically arranged in clusters along woody, spine-tipped stems that serve as a primary defensive feature. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that emerge from the axils of the leaves during its blooming period. Following pollination, it develops dark, fleshy berries that are often used by local wildlife. The overall structure of the shrub is dense and compact, characterized by its distinctly pubescent foliage.
