Zanthoxylum ailanthoides

What's the taxonomical classification of Zanthoxylum ailanthoides?

Zanthoxylum ailanthoides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Sapindales. This plant is a representative of the family Rutaceae, specifically placed within the genus Zanthoxylum, and is identified by its unique species designation, ailanthoides.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Zanthoxylum ailanthoides has pinnately compound leaves that typically consist of 11 to 27 leaflets arranged symmetrically along a central rachis. The individual leaflets are lanceolate or elliptical in shape with serrated margins and acute tips. This deciduous tree features a bark that becomes deeply fissured and rugged as the specimen matures. Its reproductive structures include small, greenish-yellow flowers that cluster into panicles during the blooming season. The resulting fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to reveal single seeds.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds ailanthamide, ailanthoidol, alkaloids of benzophenanthridine type, decarine, and xanthyletin. These specific chemical constituents are primarily responsible for the biological activities observed in the species. The alkaloids of benzophenanthridine type are particularly noted for their potent pharmacological properties. Additionally, compounds like decarine and xanthyletin contribute to the plant's complex chemical profile. The presence of ailanthamide and ailanthoidol further characterizes the unique medicinal potential of these extracts.

As discussed in a study, 5 new compounds and 17 known compounds were isolated from the stem bark of Zanthoxylum ailanthoides. These include ailanthamide (1), N-(4-methoxyphenethyl)-N-methylbenzamide (2), (2E,4E)-N-isobutyl-6-oxohepta-2,4-dienamide (3), 4-(4'-hydroxy-3'-methylbutoxy)benzaldehyde (4), and (E)-methyl 4-[4-(3-hydroxypropyl)phenoxy]-2-methylbut-2-enoate (5). Compounds 1, 3, xanthyletin, decarine, (+)-episesamin, (-)-hinokinin, and evofolin-B showed inhibition of superoxide anion generation with IC50 values less than or equal to 5.34 microg/mL. Additionally, compounds 1, xanthyletin, decarine, and (+)-episesamin inhibited elastase release with IC50 values less than or equal to 5.53 microg/mL.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating hepatic tumor cells, cervical tumor cells, colon tumor cells, and stomach tumor cells while also preventing tumor promotion. Researchers have identified specific bioactive compounds within the plant that demonstrate significant anti-cancer properties. These substances work by inhibiting the growth and spread of various malignant cells throughout the digestive and reproductive systems. By interfering with cellular processes, the plant helps to suppress the mechanisms responsible for tumor promotion. Ongoing studies continue to evaluate how these natural extracts can effectively combat different types of oncological developments.

As discussed in a study, Zanthoxylum ailanthoides contains 15 unique peaks within its alkaloid profile. The benzophenanthridine type alkaloids are commonly present in this species with a high relative content of 60.52%. When testing crude alkaloid extracts at a concentration of 200 μg/mL, inhibitory rates against various tumor cells, including Hep G2 hepatic tumor cells, ranged from 60.71 to 93.63%. The study utilized an HPLC-UV/ESI-MS/MS method to analyze these Zanthoxylum alkaloids. This research suggests that the potential anti-cancer activity is attributed to the quaternary alkaloids found in these species.
