Ixeris denticulata

What's the taxonomical classification of Ixeris denticulata?

Ixeris denticulata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Asterales and the family Asteraceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by the genus Ixeris and the species denticulata.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Ixeris denticulata contains a complex profile of bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, tannins, and triterpenoids. These chemical constituents contribute to the plant's documented pharmacological activities such as antioxidant and antimicrobial properties. The presence of specific phenolic compounds helps mitigate oxidative stress within biological systems. Furthermore, certain glycosides found in the leaves and stems are believed to support wound healing processes. These diverse medicinal compounds make the plant a significant subject for ethnobotanical and biochemical research.

What are the therapeutic actions of Ixeris denticulata?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, inflammation, and gastrointestinal issues such as diarrhea. Its leaves contain bioactive compounds that provide antimicrobial and wound-healing properties for various dermatological ailments. Local healers often apply crushed portions of the plant topically to reduce swelling and soothe irritations. When ingested in specific preparations, it serves as a traditional remedy for calming digestive distress. These medicinal applications rely on the plant's ability to combat pathogens and reduce localized pain.
