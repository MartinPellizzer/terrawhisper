Dryopteris labordei

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dryopteris labordei has large, tripinnate-pinnatifid fronds that emerge from a short, stout rhizome covered in dark scales. The stipes are typically dark brown or blackish and are densely clothed in lanceolate scales near the base. Each pinna is subdivided into smaller segments that possess serrated or lobed margins, contributing to a delicate, feathery appearance. The texture of the foliage is relatively thin and membranous, often displaying a deep green color on the upper surface. Sori are arranged in two rows on the underside of the pinnules, protected by characteristic kidney-shaped indusia.
