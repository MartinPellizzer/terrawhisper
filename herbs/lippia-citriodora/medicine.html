What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Lippia Citriodora (Lemon Verbena)?

Lippia citriodora, also known as lemon verbena, has health benefits such as reducing anxiety and insomnia due to its calming effects.

Its essential oil is rich in citral, limonene, and beta-pinene, which are known for their anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. Medicinal preparations of Lippia citriodora include teas, infusions, and essential oil blends used for aromatherapy. However, excessive consumption may cause gastrointestinal upset, nausea, and headaches.

Precautions include avoiding use during pregnancy and breastfeeding, and consulting a healthcare professional before using it for extended periods or in high doses.

This article explains the health benefits, active constituents, medicinal preparations, possible side effects, and precautions related to Lippia citriodora .

What are the possible side effect of using Lippia citriodora improperly?

Improper use of Lippia citriodora, also known as lemon verbena, increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as digestive issues, nausea, and dizziness.

The essential oil extracted from the plant can also cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and respiratory problems in some individuals. Moreover, high doses of Lippia citriodora may interact with medications, worsen underlying health conditions, and lead to an increased risk of bleeding.

It's essential to consult a healthcare professional before using the plant for medicinal purposes.

What precautions to take when using Lippia citriodora medicinally?

Before using Lippia citriodora, also known as lemon verbena, for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as consulting a qualified healthcare practitioner, especially if you have any underlying medical conditions or allergies.
