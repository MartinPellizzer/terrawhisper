What is Stillingia annua?

Stillingia annua is a plant species belonging to the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is an annual herb that grows up to 1.5 meters tall, with a hairy stem and alternate, simple leaves that are 5-15 cm long and 1-4 cm wide. The leaves are elliptical in shape and have a pointed tip, with a dark green color and a hairy margin. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in a terminal corymb, and has a cylindrical, 3-4 cm long capsule that contains 3-4 seeds.

The seeds are ovate in shape and have a brown color.

What are the therapeutic properties this herb?

The main therapeutic properties of Stillingia annua are anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiviral.

Its anti-inflammatory properties are attributed to the presence of flavonoids and saponins, which inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines. Additionally, the antiviral properties of Stillingia annua are due to the bioactive compounds such as alkaloids and glycosides, which exhibit inhibitory activity against certain viruses. Furthermore, its antimicrobial properties are associated with the presence of volatile oils and phenolic compounds, which exhibit antibacterial and antifungal activities.

Stillingia annua's antioxidant properties, on the other hand, are attributed to the presence of flavonoids, which scavenge free radicals and protect against oxidative stress.

Stillingia annua's high levels of anthraquinones may also lead to the development of diarrhea, further exacerbating dehydration and electrolyte imbalances.
