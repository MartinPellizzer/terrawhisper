Mexican Sage (Salvia divinorum)

Mexican Sage (Salvia divinorum) is a member of the Lamiaceae family, native to Mexico, Southeastern United States, and Central America. Traditionally, its leaves, stems, and roots have been used for infusions, decoctions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its sedative, antispasmodic, and stimulant actions, and has a long history of use in native american herbal medicine, african traditional medicine, and european herbal medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 30 to 100 centimeters.
Leaves
Ovate leaves with upper surface glabrous and pale green, lower surface hairy and dark green, bearing distinct stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Bilaterally symmetrical flowers with violet to purple petals, arranged in clustered inflorescences, featuring two lip-like lobes and four lateral petals, with a prominent staminode.
Stems
Erect growth habit with opposite branching, smooth surface, and presence of glandular dots along the stem.
