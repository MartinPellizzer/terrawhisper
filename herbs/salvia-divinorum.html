Bioactive Constituents

While salvinorin a is the most well-known and studied compound, other related compounds such as salvinorin b and salvinorin c also exist within the plant, though they are less understood and have not been extensively researched for their pharmacological properties. Rosmarinic acid, another compound found in Salvia divinorum, is a polyphenolic antioxidant that may contribute to the plant's potential anti-inflammatory and neuroprotective effects. These compounds collectively play a role in the plant's traditional use in spiritual and ceremonial contexts, as well as its modern interest in scientific research.

However, the full therapeutic potential and mechanisms of action of these constituents remain areas of ongoing investigation, highlighting the complex biochemical profile of Salvia divinorum.
