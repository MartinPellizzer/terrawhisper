Abies nephrolepis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Abies nephrolepis has needle-like leaves that are relatively short, flattened, and arranged spirally or two-ranked along the branches. The foliage typically exhibits a dark green color on the upper surface while the underside may show distinct stomatal bands. This species is characterized by its woody stem structure and a conical growth habit common to many fir trees. Its reproductive structures consist of upright, cylindrical cones that contain numerous scales protecting the developing seeds. The overall morphology is adapted to withstand specific environmental pressures found within its native mountain habitats.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of central and southern China, specifically within the mountainous provinces of Sichuan, Yunnan, Guizhou, and Tibet. It thrives primarily in montane forests located at elevations ranging from 2,000 to 3,500 meters above sea level. These habitats are characterized by cool, moist climates and acidic soils found on steep slopes. The species is often found growing alongside other temperate coniferous trees in shaded environments.
