Possible Side Effects of juniperus communis

Juniperus communis can have side effects if used improperly, such as causing kidney damage and liver issues in individuals who consume excessive amounts of juniper berries or extracts.

It may also interact with certain medications, such as diuretics, and worsen conditions like high blood pressure, kidney stones, and gout.

Additionally, juniperus communis can cause allergic reactions, including skin rashes and respiratory issues, in some individuals, especially when used topically or in aromatherapy.

Furthermore, juniper berries can also stimulate uterine contractions, which can be problematic for pregnant women.
