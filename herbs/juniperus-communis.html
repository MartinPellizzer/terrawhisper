Medicinal Parts of Juniperus communis

Juniperus communis has many medicinal parts, such as the berries, leaves, and roots, which have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

The berries, in particular, are rich in flavonoids, including kaempferol and quercetin, as well as terpenes like alpha-pinene and beta-pinene, which possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

The leaves contain sesquiterpenes like borneol and camphor, which have been traditionally used to treat respiratory issues such as bronchitis and coughs.

The roots, on the other hand, contain lignans like podophyllotoxin and aryltetralin lignans, which have been used in traditional medicine to treat various gastrointestinal issues.
