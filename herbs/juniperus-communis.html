Common juniper

Juniperus communis

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family

Botanical Identification

Juniperus communis is the scientific name for the Common Juniper, belonging to the Cupressaceae family. It is also known as Juniper bush or white juniper in various regions. A perennial shrub, it is native to the Northern Hemisphere and has needle-like leaves, small inconspicuous flowers, and red berries. It has been referred to by several synonyms in historical herbal texts.

Active Compounds

The Common Juniper contains essential oils, terpenoids, and flavonoids as its main bioactive compounds. These compounds work together to exert anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. They may also interact synergistically to enhance therapeutic benefits in herbal medicine.

  • Essential oil
  • Terpenoid
  • Flavonoid

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory conditions.

Infusion: Used for respiratory ailments like coughs and colds.

Essential oil: Used for skin conditions and as a stimulant.

Safety Profile

Common Juniper is generally considered safe when used in moderation. However, it should not be consumed in large quantities or by pregnant women due to potential uterine stimulation. Long-term use may lead to kidney damage, so caution is advised for prolonged consumption.
