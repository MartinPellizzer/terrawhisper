Nervilia fordii

What's the taxonomical classification of Nervilia fordii?

Nervilia fordii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and falls into the order Asparagales. This plant is a member of the family Orchidaceae, specifically situated within the genus Nervilia, and is identified by its unique species name, fordire.

What are the therapeutic actions of Nervilia fordii?

Nervilia fordii has medicinal properties primarily used in traditional practices to treat gastrointestinal issues, skin inflammations, and various types of fever. The rhizomes of this orchid are often prepared into decoctions or pastes to alleviate digestive discomfort and stomach aches. When applied topically, the plant extract can help reduce swelling and soothe irritated skin surfaces. Some ethnobotanical studies suggest its bioactive compounds may contribute to mild analgesic effects in local healing rituals. These therapeutic applications highlight the plant's importance within specific regional herbal medicine systems.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including dysentery, stomach pains, and various types of skin infections. These applications vary depending on the specific region and the preparation method used.
