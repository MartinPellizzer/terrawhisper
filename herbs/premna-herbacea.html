Premna herbacea

What's the taxonomical classification of Premna herbacea?

Premna herbacea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Lamiales. It is further categorized under the family Lamiaceae, which places it within the genus Premna, eventually reaching its specific identification as the species herbacea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Premna herbacea has an herbaceous growth habit characterized by slender, green stems and opposite, ovate leaves with entire margins and acute apices. The foliage typically displays a smooth texture and prominent venation that distinguishes its surface pattern. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in axillary clusters, often appearing in shades of white or pale green. These flowers eventually develop into small, fleshy drupes that serve as the plant's primary reproductive structures. The overall structure remains relatively low to the ground compared to its woody relatives in the Lamiaceae family.

What are the pharmacological activities of Premna herbacea?

This plant has anti-diabetic, anti-hyperglycemic, anti-inflammatory, antinociceptive, and antipyretic pharmacological activities. These properties allow the herb to assist in managing blood glucose levels by reducing high sugar concentrations in the body. It also functions effectively to reduce swelling and alleviate pain through its potent anti-inflammatory and antinociceptive actions. Furthermore, the plant exhibits antipyretic qualities that help in lowering body temperatures during a fever. These diverse biological effects make Premna herbacea a significant subject in medicinal research.

As discussed in a study, the methanolic extract of Premna herbacea (MEPH) shows therapeutic potential against type 2 diabetes mellitus (T2DM) and acts as an effective anti-hyperglycemic agent. The plant contains major bioactive phytochemicals including Verbascoide and Isoverbascoside (ISOVER), with ISOVER identified for the first time as the most bioactive phytochemical from the Premna genus. MEPH enhances glucose uptake in FFA-induced insulin resistant (IR) L6 muscle cells and decreases elevated blood glucose levels in HF-HFD fed rats. ISOVER attenuates IR by decreasing the phosphorylation of p38 mitogen-activated protein kinase (p38MAPK) and c-Jun N-terminal kinase (JNK) while increasing the phosphorylation of mammalian target of rapamycin (mTOR). Molecular docking further reveals that ISOVER increases insulin sensitivity by targeting the JNK1 kinase as a competitive inhibitor. These functional properties include the inhibition of ROS through the JNK-AKT/mTOR signaling cascade.

These specific compounds include Verbascoide and Isoverbascoside (ISOVER). Isoverbascoside was identified as the most bioactive phytochemical for the first time from the plant in the Premna genus. The study utilized an XFe24 analyzer to validate the bioenergetics profile and anti-hyperglycemic potential of ISOVER. This compound functions by targeting the JNK1 kinase as a competitive inhibitor to increase insulin sensitivity. Through the JNK-AKT/mTOR signaling cascade, ISOVER helps in attenuating insulin resistance.
