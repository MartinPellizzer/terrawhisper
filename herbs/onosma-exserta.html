What's the taxonomical classification of Onosma exserta?

Onosma exserta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Boraginales and the family Boraginaceae. Finally, its taxonomic identity is completed by its placement within the genus Onosma, specifically as the species exserta.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Onosma exserta has erect, glabrous stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and feature narrow, lanceolate leaves arranged alternately along the axis.

The foliage consists of linear to lanceolate leaf blades that are often sessile and taper toward the base. Its inflorescence is a terminal raceme composed of numerous small, funnel-shaped flowers that are predominantly pink to purple in color. Each individual flower possesses a long, protruding style that extends significantly beyond the corolla tube, giving the plant its specific epithet.

The calyx is tubular and composed of several distinct, narrow lobes that surround the base of the floral structure.
