Impatiens apalophylla

What's the taxonomical classification of Impatiens apalophylla?

Impatiens apalophylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Ericales. This plant is a member of the family Balsaminaceae and is specifically identified by its genus, Impatiens, and its unique species designation, apalophylla.

Impatiens apalophylla has medicinal properties primarily used for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects on skin irritations. The plant contains bioactive compounds that help reduce swelling and soothe localized redness when applied topically. Traditional applications often involve using the plant's extracts to treat minor wounds or fungal infections. These therapeutic actions make it a valuable resource in regional ethnobotanical practices for managing surface ailments.
