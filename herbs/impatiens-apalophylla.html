Impatiens apalophylla

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Impatiens apalophylla has a stem that is succulent and glandular, leaves that are lanceolate to ovate with serrated margins, and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically exhibits a decumbent or ascending growth habit, often appearing in shaded or rocky environments. Its leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem and possess distinctively thin, translucent textures. The inflorescence consists of small, often pink or white blossoms that lack the showy petals found in many other members of the genus. These morphological traits allow the species to adapt to its specific ecological niche.
