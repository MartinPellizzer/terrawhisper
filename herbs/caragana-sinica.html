Caragana sinica

Caragana sinica, commonly known as Northern Pea Tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China and Mongolia.

It is valued for its medicinal properties, providing relief from various health issues such as fever, cough, and digestive problems.

The plant's health benefits are attributed to its ability to promote sweating, reduce inflammation, and act as an expectorant, making it a popular treatment for respiratory issues.

The bioactive constituents of Caragana sinica include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which contribute to its therapeutic actions in treating various health conditions.
