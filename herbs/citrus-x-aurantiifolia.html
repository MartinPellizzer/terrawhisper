Citrus × aurantiifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Citrus × aurantiifolia has an evergreen growth habit characterized by small, spreading trees that reach heights of 2 to 5 meters and feature dark green, ovate leaves with slightly serrated margins. The foliage often displays a glossy texture and may include small thorns along the branches. Its flowers are typically white, fragrant, and pentamerous, emerging from the axils of the leaves. These blossoms develop into small, globose, yellowish-green fruits known as limes that possess a thin, highly aromatic rind. The internal pulp consists of numerous juice vesicles that provide a sharp, acidic flavor profile.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to parts of Southeast Asia and South Asia but is now widely cultivated throughout tropical and subtropical regions across the globe. It thrives in warm climates where frost is rare, making it a common sight in gardens and orchards from Florida to Brazil. Many commercial plantations are established in countries like Mexico, Thailand, and various island nations within the Pacific. Because of its ability to adapt to diverse tropical landscapes, it has become a globally distributed ornamental and fruit-bearing species. Its presence is most dense in areas characterized by high humidity and consistent warmth.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Citrus × aurantiifolia contains medicinal compounds primarily located in its fruit juice, the rind, and the leaves. The juice is frequently utilized for its high vitamin C content to support immune function and treat respiratory ailments. Essential oils extracted from the peel are applied topically or inhaled to reduce anxiety and alleviate digestive discomfort. Additionally, the leaves are often brewed into teas to help lower fever and reduce inflammation. These diverse parts provide a versatile range of therapeutic applications in traditional medicine.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Citrus × aurantiifolia contains a complex mixture of bioactive phytochemicals including limonoids, flavonoids, coumarins, and various essential oils such as limonene. These compounds work together to provide significant antioxidant and antimicrobial properties that can combat oxidative stress in cells. Specifically, the presence of hesperidin and naringin contributes to anti-inflammatory effects within the body. Many of these secondary metabolites also exhibit potential anticonvulsant and hypoglycemic activities in pharmacological studies. This diverse chemical profile makes the plant a subject of interest for developing natural therapeutic agents.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Citrus × aurantiifolia targets various enzymes and receptors including cyclooxygenase-2, lipoxygenase, and acetylcholinesterase to exert its therapeutic effects. Its bioactive compounds, such as flavonoids and limonoids, act as potent inhibitors of inflammatory mediators by modulating the arachidonic acid metabolism pathway. These phytochemicals also interact with neurotransmitter systems to influence cognitive function and neuroprotection through the modulation of cholinergic signaling. Furthermore, certain constituents interfere with the activity of specific cytokines and nuclear factor kappa B pathways to reduce systemic inflammation. Such diverse molecular interactions allow the plant to exhibit antimicrobial, antioxidant, and anti-inflammatory properties.

What are the therapeutic actions of Citrus × aurantiifolia?

Citrus × aurauntiifolia has carminative, digestive stimulant, antiseptic, astringent, and antispasmodic properties that support gastrointestinal and dermatological health. Its carminative action helps to expel gas from the digestive tract, reducing bloating and discomfort. As a digestive stimulant, the plant helps to increase the secretion of gastric juices to improve overall digestion. The antiseptic and astringent qualities allow it to combat microorganisms and tighten body tissues effectively. Additionally, its antispasmodic effects assist in relaxing smooth muscle contractions to alleviate cramping.

What are the herbal preparations of this plant?

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause photosensitivity, heartburn, stomach upset, nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea in some individuals. Exposure to the juices of Citrus x aurantiifolia can lead to skin irritation or burns when the skin is subsequently exposed to sunlight. Ingesting the fruit may also trigger significant gastrointestinal distress, such as nausea and vomiting. Some people might experience more persistent issues like stomach upset or diarrhea after consumption. Heartburn is another common digestive complaint associated with the acidity of this species.

  • Photosensitivity

  • Stomach upset

  • Diarrhea
