Citrus × aurantiifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Citrus × aurantiifolia?

Citrus × aurantiifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant follows the biological order Sapindales. It is further categorized under the family Rutaceae, which is characteristic of many citrus species. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Citrus, with its specific designation being the species aurantifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Citrus × aurantiifolia has an evergreen growth habit characterized by small, spreading trees that reach heights of 2 to 5 meters and feature dark green, ovate leaves with slightly serrated margins. The foliage often displays a glossy texture and may include small thorns along the branches. Its flowers are typically white, fragrant, and pentamerous, emerging from the axils of the leaves. These blossoms develop into small, globose, yellowish-green fruits known as limes that possess a thin, highly aromatic rind. The internal pulp consists of numerous juice vesicles that provide a sharp, acidic flavor profile.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to parts of Southeast Asia and South Asia but is now widely cultivated throughout tropical and subtropical regions across the globe. It thrives in warm climates where frost is rare, making it a common sight in gardens and orchards from Florida to Brazil. Many commercial plantations are established in countries like Mexico, Thailand, and various island nations within the Pacific. Because of its ability to adapt to diverse tropical landscapes, it has become a globally distributed ornamental and fruit-bearing species. Its presence is most dense in areas characterized by high humidity and consistent warmth.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Citrus × aurantiifolia contains medicinal compounds primarily located in its fruit juice, the rind, and the leaves. The juice is frequently utilized for its high vitamin C content to support immune function and treat respiratory ailments. Essential oils extracted from the peel are applied topically or inhaled to reduce anxiety and alleviate digestive discomfort. Additionally, the leaves are often brewed into teas to help lower fever and reduce inflammation. These diverse parts provide a versatile range of therapeutic applications in traditional medicine.

What are the pharmacological activities of Citrus × aurantiifolia?

This plant has antimicrobial, antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antidiabetic pharmacological activities driven by its rich concentration of flavonoids and essential oils. The presence of limonene and citral provides significant antibacterial properties against various pathogenic bacteria and fungi. Furthermore, its high phenolic content allows it to scavenge free radicals, effectively reducing oxidative stress in biological systems. Studies also indicate that extracts from the fruit can modulate glucose metabolism and suppress inflammatory cytokines. These diverse therapeutic potentials make the species a valuable subject for natural drug development.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

The presence of vitamin C further enhances its ability to support immune function and skin health. Additionally, certain alkaloids within the fruit have been studied for their potential sedative and analgesic effects. What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause photosensitivity, heartburn, stomach upset, nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea in some individuals. Exposure to the juices of Citrus x aurantiifolia can lead to skin irritation or burns when the skin is subsequently exposed to sunlight. Ingesting the fruit may also trigger significant gastrointestinal distress, such as nausea and vomiting. Some people might experience more persistent issues like stomach upset or diarrhea after consumption. Heartburn is another common digestive complaint associated with the acidity of this species.
