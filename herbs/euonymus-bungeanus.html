Euonymus bungeanus has an upright, deciduous shrub habit characterized by slender, arching branches and oppositely arranged, ovate to elliptical leaves that measure approximately 2 to 5 centimeters in length. During late spring, the plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually give way to striking, bright pink or red capsules. These four-lobed fruit capsules split open to reveal fleshy, orange-colored arils that surround the seeds.
