Olax latifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Olax latifolia?

Olax latifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Santalales. This plant is a member of the family Olacaceae and is specifically identified by the genus Olax, with its unique biological identity defined by the species latifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Olax latifolia has elliptical to oblong leaves that are characterized by a coriaceous texture, an acute apex, and an entire margin. The foliage is typically arranged in an opposite phyllotaxy along the woody stems of the shrub or small tree. Its flowers are small and cream-colored, blooming in axillary clusters that emerge from the leaf axils. The fruit is a fleshy drupe that transitions in color as it reaches maturity. These morphological features distinguish it from other species within the Oleaceae family.
