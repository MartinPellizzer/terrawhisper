Viburnum henryi

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viburnum henryi has opposite, simple leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape, measuring 5 to 10 centimeters in length, with finely serrated margins and a glossy, dark green upper surface. The leaf bases are typically cuneate, transitioning into long petioles that support the foliage. During the blooming season, the plant produces flat-topped cymes of small, creamy white flowers that emerge from terminal clusters. These blossoms eventually give way to small, fleshy drupes that transition from green to a deep red or black color upon ripening. The stems exhibit a woody structure with a somewhat spreading growth habit that defines its overall shrubby silhouette.

      What side-effects this plant can have?
