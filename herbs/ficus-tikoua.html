Ficus tikoua

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ficus tikoua has an arborescent growth habit characterized by woody stems, large elliptic to oblong leaves with entire margins, and small, inconspicuous pale green flowers. The leaves typically feature a coriaceous texture and a prominent midrib that extends toward the apex. Its bark is generally smooth and grayish, providing a stark contrast to the dense, dark green foliage. As a member of the Moraceae family, the plant produces small syconia that function as specialized inflorescences. These structures are essential for the plant's reproductive cycle and are often found tucked within the leaf axils.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the tropical forests of the Tikoua region within the Department of Chaco in Paraguay. It grows specifically in localized pockets where the soil conditions and humidity levels meet its unique biological requirements. Its range is highly restricted, meaning it is not found in surrounding South American countries or even other parts of Paraguay. Because of this narrow distribution, the species is considered geographically rare. Conservation efforts often focus on these specific habitats to prevent local extinction.
