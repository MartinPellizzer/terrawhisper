Aster senecioides

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aster senecioides has erect, unbranched stems that typically reach heights of 20 to 60 centimeters and feature leaves that are lanceolate to linear in shape with serrated margins. The foliage is primarily basal, forming a rosette of leaves, while the stem leaves are smaller and alternate in arrangement. Its flowering heads are composed of numerous small, yellow ray and disk florets clustered in terminal corymbs. Each individual flower head is surrounded by overlapping phyllaries that form a distinct involucre. The plant often develops a woody base as it matures, providing structural support for its seasonal blooms.

    What are the herbal preparations of Aster senecioides?

    Aster senecioides is prepared as a decoction, infusion, or tincture to utilize its active phytochemical constituents. Herbalists typically simmer the dried leaves and flowering tops in water to create a potent decoction for internal use. Infusions are often made by steeping the softer plant parts in hot water to extract more delicate compounds.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Aster senecioides can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Contact with the plant's sap may lead to contact dermatitis or localized redness on sensitive skin. If the plant is consumed accidentally, it can trigger nausea, vomiting, or stomach cramping. Some individuals may also experience respiratory irritation if they inhale pollen or dust from the flowers. These adverse effects are generally linked to the plant's chemical composition and varying individual sensitivities.
