Aster senecioides

What's the taxonomical classification of Aster senecioides?

Aster senecioides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Asterales, this plant is a member of the family Asteraceae. Taxonomically, it is placed within the genus Aster, and its specific biological identity is defined by the species name senecioides.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aster senecioides has erect, unbranched stems that typically reach heights of 20 to 60 centimeters and feature leaves that are lanceolate to linear in shape with serrated margins. The foliage is primarily basal, forming a rosette of leaves, while the stem leaves are smaller and alternate in arrangement. Its flowering heads are composed of numerous small, yellow ray and disk florets clustered in terminal corymbs. Each individual flower head is surrounded by overlapping phyllaries that form a distinct involucre. The plant often develops a woody base as it matures, providing structural support for its seasonal blooms.
