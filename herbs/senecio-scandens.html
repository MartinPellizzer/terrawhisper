Senecio scandens

What's the taxonomical classification of Senecio scandens?

Senecio scandens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. This plant is further categorized into the family Asteraceae, which is a prominent group of flowering plants. Within this family, it is defined by the genus Senecio and is specifically identified by its species name, scandens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Senecio scandens has a climbing or trailing growth habit characterized by slender, herbaceous stems that often possess a slightly pubescent or glabrous texture. The leaves are typically arranged in an alternate pattern along the stem and can vary from lanceolate to ovate in shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. Small, tubular flowers are produced in terminal or axillary clusters, featuring characteristic rayless heads typical of many species in the Asteraceae family. The reproductive structures include numerous small florets that emerge from a common receptacle surrounded by involucral bracts. These morphological traits collectively enable the plant to scramble over other vegetation to reach sunlight.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of the tropical Andes, specifically within the mountain ranges of Ecuador, Peru, and Colombia. It thrives primarily in montane forests and páramo ecosystems where moisture levels remain consistently high. These specific habitats are often characterized by cool temperatures and frequent cloud cover. The species is restricted to these narrow elevational bands rather than being widespread across entire continents. Consequently, its distribution is highly fragmented due to the isolated nature of Andean mountain peaks.

What are the pharmacological activities of Senecio scandens?

This plant has analgesic, anti-inflammatory, anti-tumor, antibacterial, and antimicrobial pharmacological activities. Its chemical constituents work to reduce pain and swelling by modulating various biological pathways within the body. Research indicates that the plant also possesses significant properties that can inhibit the growth of malignant cells. Furthermore, its ability to combat diverse pathogens makes it an effective agent for managing bacterial and microbial infections. These multifaceted therapeutic effects highlight its potential importance in developing new medicinal treatments.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", Senecio scandens Buch.-Ham. has a long history in traditional Chinese medicine for treating ailments like bacterial diarrhea, enteritis, conjunctivitis, and respiratory tract infections. Modern pharmacological studies have demonstrated that extracts and compounds from the plant exhibit a wide spectrum of activities, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, anti-leptospirosis, hepatoprotective, anti-infusorial, antioxidant, antiviral, antitumoral, analgesic, mutagenic, and toxicological activities. These pharmacological activities can be attributed to the presence of various flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids. The Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2010 does not have well-defined ranking criteria for the toxicity of the pyrrolizidine alkaloids (PAs) found in Senecio scandens. Because of the toxicity of these PAs, regulations for the plant vary among different countries and regions.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several specific pyrrolizidine alkaloids, including the compounds senkirkine, senecionine, seneciphylliine, and jacozine N-oxide. These medicinal compounds are secondary metabolites that serve as the primary chemical constituents of Senecio scandens. The presence of these alkaloids characterizes the chemical profile of the species and influences its biological activity. Researchers often study these particular molecules to understand their potential pharmacological properties. Understanding the concentrations of these substances is essential for identifying the plant's medicinal potential.

According to a study published by "Zhong yao cai = Zhongyaocai = Journal of Chinese medicinal materials", the alkaloids of Senecio scandens growing in Guangdong were analyzed using rapid resolution liquid chromatography-electrospray ionization mass spectrometry (RRLC-ESI-MS/MS). Four alkaloids were identified in the plant, which are senkirkine, dehydrosenkirkine, monocrotaline, and adonifoline. Senkirkine was isolated and purified by silica gel column chromatography. This study notes that senkirkine was firstly isolated from Senecio scandens growing in Guangdong. Senkirkine serves as the main component of Senecio scandens growing in Guangdong.
