Senecio scandens

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Senecio scandens has a climbing or trailing growth habit characterized by slender, herbaceous stems that often possess a slightly pubescent or glabrous texture. The leaves are typically arranged in an alternate pattern along the stem and can vary from lanceolate to ovate in shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. Small, tubular flowers are produced in terminal or axillary clusters, featuring characteristic rayless heads typical of many species in the Asteraceae family. The reproductive structures include numerous small florets that emerge from a common receptacle surrounded by involucral bracts. These morphological traits collectively enable the plant to scramble over other vegetation to reach sunlight.
