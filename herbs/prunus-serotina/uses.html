Black Cherry (Prunus serotina) Uses

Black Cherry is used to treat respiratory infections, bronchitis, and coughs, including those associated with influenza and tonsillitis. It helps alleviate lung congestion and supports wound healing, reducing skin irritation and infections. Its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties make it effective for various respiratory and skin conditions.

Black Cherry (Prunus serotina) Monograph

1. Bronchitis

Black Cherry (Prunus serotina) has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in cases of bronchitis. Its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties help reduce bronchial irritation and loosen mucus, aiding in easier breathing. A tea made from the bark or leaves may be used to alleviate symptoms and soothe coughing.

2. Respiratory infections

Black Cherry is used to support respiratory health by reducing inflammation and soothing coughs. Its antimicrobial properties may help combat infections, while its expectorant action aids in clearing mucus. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture for respiratory support during colds or bronchitis.

3. Cough

Black Cherry bark is used to soothe coughs due to its expectorant properties, helping to loosen mucus and reduce irritation in the respiratory tract. It also contains compounds that may ease inflammation and promote healing in the throat and lungs.

4. Influenza

Black Cherry (Prunus serotina) may support immune function and reduce fever associated with influenza. Its compounds, such as anthocyanins and vitamin C, have antiviral and anti-inflammatory properties. It is often used in herbal formulas to alleviate symptoms and aid recovery during flu outbreaks.

5. Tonsillitis

Black Cherry (Prunus serotina) has been traditionally used to support the treatment of tonsillitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The leaves and bark contain compounds that may help reduce throat inflammation and combat bacterial infections. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to soothe sore throats and alleviate symptoms associated with tonsillitis.

6. Cough and bronchitis

Black Cherry bark contains compounds that soothe respiratory inflammation and loosen mucus, making it effective for coughs and bronchitis. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to reduce irritation and ease breathing. Its antiseptic properties help prevent secondary infections during prolonged respiratory issues.

7. Lung congestion

Black Cherry is used to address lung congestion by promoting expectoration and reducing mucus buildup. Its anti-inflammatory properties help soothe irritated airways, while its mild diuretic effect aids in clearing excess fluids from the respiratory system. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to support respiratory health.

8. Skin infections

Black Cherry bark contains antimicrobial compounds that help treat skin infections. It reduces inflammation and promotes healing. Traditionally used for conditions like eczema and fungal infections. Its astringent properties aid in drying excess moisture from infected areas.

9. Skin irritation

Black Cherry bark contains compounds with anti-inflammatory and astringent properties that can help soothe skin irritation. It is traditionally used to treat conditions like eczema and psoriasis. The tannins in the bark help reduce redness and promote healing. It is often prepared as a topical salve or infused oil for direct application.

10. Wound healing

Black Cherry (Prunus serotina) supports wound healing through its astringent and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce swelling and promote tissue repair. The bark and leaves contain compounds that may enhance cellular regeneration. It is often used topically to clean and protect injured skin.
