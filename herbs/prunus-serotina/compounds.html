Black Cherry (Prunus serotina) Compounds

Coumarin is a key compound known for its anticoagulant properties. Tannins contribute to astringency and may support digestive health. Flavonoids and phenolic acids act as antioxidants, protecting cells from damage. Glycosides, saponins, and terpenoids offer various therapeutic benefits, while essential oils and alkaloids provide additional pharmacological activity. Anthocyanins are responsible for the plant's vibrant color and have antioxidant properties.

Black Cherry (Prunus serotina) Monograph

1. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Black Cherry (Prunus serotina). It contributes to the plant's anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory properties. Found in the bark and leaves, coumarin is used in herbal medicine to support circulation and reduce inflammation. Its presence is a significant factor in the plant's therapeutic applications.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Black Cherry (Prunus serotina). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health. Tannins also have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects, making them valuable in herbal medicine.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Black Cherry (Prunus serotina) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting immune function and reducing oxidative stress. They are found in the bark, leaves, and fruits of the plant. Flavonoids may also aid in cardiovascular health and tissue repair.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Black Cherry (Prunus serotina). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports the plant's traditional use in promoting digestive health and reducing inflammation.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Black Cherry (Prunus serotina). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, such as anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Glycosides in Black Cherry are often used in herbal preparations to support digestive and respiratory health. These compounds are extracted and utilized in traditional herbal medicine for their therapeutic benefits.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Black Cherry (Prunus serotina). It exhibits mild detergent properties and may support digestive health. Found in the bark and leaves, saponin contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine. Its role in promoting liver function and reducing inflammation is well-documented in herbal practice.

7. Essential oil

The essential oil of Black Cherry (Prunus serotina) contains volatile compounds like limonene and myrcene. These components contribute to its aromatic profile and may support respiratory and digestive health. It is often used in aromatherapy and as a complementary therapy in herbal medicine.

8. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Black Cherry (Prunus serotina) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, supporting the plant's traditional use in treating ailments like fever and digestive issues. They also play a role in the plant's defense mechanisms and may influence its therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloids in Black Cherry (Prunus serotina) are naturally occurring nitrogen-containing compounds with potential medicinal properties. These alkaloids may contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating ailments like inflammation and digestive issues. However, their specific therapeutic effects and safety profiles require further scientific investigation.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin, found in Black Cherry (Prunus serotina), is a flavonoid responsible for the plant's deep red and purple pigments. It exhibits strong antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress in the body. This compound supports overall health by protecting cells from damage and may contribute to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.
