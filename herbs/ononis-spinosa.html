Ononis Spinosa

Ononis spinosa

Ononis Spinosa (Ononis spinosa) is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, known for its small yellow flowers and its traditional use in herbal medicine to support urinary health and alleviate symptoms of bladder disorders.

Botanical Identification

Ononis spinosa, commonly known as goat's rue, is a perennial herb in the Fabaceae family. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it has regional names like "bitter vetch" and "wild peavine." The plant features slender stems, trifoliate leaves, and small purple flowers. It is also referred to as "Ononis spinosa" in various herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Fabales
  • Order: Fabales
  • Family: Fabaceae
  • Genus: Ononis
  • Species: Ononis spinosa

Safety Profile

Ononis spinosa is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to limited safety data. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
