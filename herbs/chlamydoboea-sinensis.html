Chlamydoboea sinensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Chlamydoboea sinensis has small, elliptical to lanceolate leaves that are arranged oppositely along a woody, climbing stem. The foliage typically exhibits a deep green coloration with a smooth, somewhat leathery texture. Its reproductive structures consist of inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the axils of the leaves. The plant develops small, fleshy fruits that follow the flowering period. These morphological traits are characteristic of its growth habit as a climbing shrub or vine.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Chlamydoboea sinensis can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and allergic reactions if ingested or touched directly. Specific dermatological responses may include redness, itching, or localized swelling at the site of contact. When consumed, individuals might experience symptoms such as nausea, stomach cramps, or diarrhea. Some people may also develop more severe systemic allergic responses like hives or respiratory discomfort. Due to these unpredictable risks, careful handling and avoidance of ingestion are highly recommended.
