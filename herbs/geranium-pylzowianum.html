What's the taxonomical classification of Geranium pylzowianum?

Geranium pylzowianum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Geraniales, it falls under the family Geraniaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Geranium, and its specific species designation, pylzowianum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Geranium pylzowianum has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by deeply palmately lobed leaves and five-petaled flowers that typically feature light pink to lavender hues. The foliage is arranged in a basal rosette and possesses distinctively serrated margins and long petioles. Its stems are slender and can become slightly hairy as the plant matures during the growing season. The inflorescence consists of small clusters of flowers that emerge from the axils of the leaves. Each flower displays delicate venation on the petals and a central reproductive structure typical of the Geraniaceae family.
