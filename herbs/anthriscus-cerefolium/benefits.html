It commonly affects older adults, individuals with a family history of cardiovascular disease, and those with obesity or diabetes.

Anthriscus cerefolium, also known as cow parsley, has been studied for its potential to lower blood pressure due to its rich content of antioxidants and bioactive compounds.

These components may help relax blood vessels and improve circulation, offering a natural support for managing hypertension.

How this herb helps with lowers blood pressure?

Anthriscus cerefolium lowers blood pressure because it contains bioactive constituents such as flavonoids and alkaloids.

These compounds help relax blood vessels and improve blood flow. The flavonoids in the plant act as natural antioxidants, reducing oxidative stress in the cardiovascular system. Alkaloids may contribute by modulating nerve signals that regulate blood pressure.

Regular consumption of Anthriscus cerefolium may support overall heart health and aid in maintaining normal blood pressure levels.
