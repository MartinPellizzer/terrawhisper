1. Causes allergic reactions

Anthriscus cerefolium causes allergic reactions due to its inherent allergenic properties.

The plant contains a variety of compounds that can stimulate an immune response, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing in some individuals. These allergens may be present in the plant's leaves, stems, or flowers, and can be released into the air or ingested through consumption.

As a result, some people may experience adverse reactions when exposed to chervil, whether through skin contact, inhalation, or ingestion.

2. Triggers asthma attacks

Anthriscus cerefolium triggers asthma attacks due to its allergenic properties.

The plant contains volatile oils that can cause an allergic reaction in some individuals, leading to constriction of the airways and a sudden onset of asthmatic symptoms. These oils may also irritate the mucous membranes, further exacerbating respiratory issues.

As a result, people with asthma may experience severe wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath upon exposure to chervil or its extracts.

3. Induces vomiting

Anthriscus cerefolium induces vomiting in some individuals due to its potential toxicity.

The plant contains a compound called furanocoumarins, which can cause gastrointestinal disturbances, including nausea and vomiting, when ingested.

This is thought to be triggered by the interaction of these compounds with certain enzymes in the body, leading to an imbalance that affects digestion and nutrient absorption.

4. Increases blood pressure

Anthriscus cerefolium increases blood pressure due to its ability to stimulate the sympathetic nervous system and cause a rise in vascular resistance.

This is attributed to the presence of alkaloids such as apiin, which can bind to alpha-adrenergic receptors, leading to increased heart rate and cardiac output.

As a result, blood vessels constrict, increasing peripheral resistance and ultimately causing blood pressure to surge.

5. Reduces appetite significantly

Anthriscus cerefolium reduces appetite significantly.

This side effect may occur due to the presence of sesquiterpenes, a type of compound found in the herb that can interact with the body's hormonal system and suppress hunger. Additionally, the high levels of vitamin K in chervil may contribute to reduced appetite by altering the body's metabolic processes and regulating fat storage.

The precise mechanisms underlying this effect are not yet fully understood, but it is clear that Anthriscus cerefolium can have a significant impact on an individual's eating habits.

6. Worsens diarrhea

Anthriscus cerefolium worsens diarrhea by interfering with the body's ability to absorb nutrients and fluids effectively.

The herb's saponin content can cause an imbalance in gut bacteria, leading to an increased frequency and severity of loose stools.

Additionally, chervil's bitter compounds may stimulate the release of stomach acids, further exacerbating digestive issues and contributing to diarrhea.

7. Interferes with iron absorption

Anthriscus cerefolium interferes with iron absorption due to its high content of flavonoids and phenolic acids.

These compounds can bind to iron in the digestive tract, preventing it from being absorbed into the bloodstream.

As a result, individuals who consume Anthriscus cerefolium may experience reduced levels of iron in their blood, which can lead to fatigue, weakness, and other symptoms associated with iron deficiency.

8. Lowers white blood count

Anthriscus cerefolium lowers white blood count due to its ability to suppress the bone marrow's production of white blood cells.

This is primarily caused by its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can inhibit the normal functioning of the bone marrow stem cells.

Additionally, the herb's bioactive compounds, such as flavonoids and phenolic acids, may also contribute to this effect by altering the immune system's response.

9. Causes kidney damage

Anthriscus cerefolium causes kidney damage due to its high content of pyridine alkaloids, specifically sesquiterpene lactones.

These compounds can accumulate in the kidneys and potentially lead to damage and scarring over time.

The severity of kidney damage is often dependent on the individual's exposure level and duration of use, with long-term or excessive consumption increasing the risk of adverse effects on kidney function.

10. Disrupts menstrual cycles

Anthriscus cerefolium disrupts menstrual cycles due to its potential estrogenic properties.

The herb's phytochemical compounds, such as flavonoids and terpenes, can mimic the effects of estrogen in the body, leading to hormonal imbalances that affect ovulation and menstruation.

This may result in irregular periods, prolonged or shortened cycles, or even cessation of menstruation altogether.
