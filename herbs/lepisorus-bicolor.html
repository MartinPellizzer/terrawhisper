What's the taxonomical classification of Lepisorus bicolor?

Lepisorus bicolor belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Polypodiales and the family Polypodiaceae, eventually reaching its specific classification within the genus Lepisorus and the species bicolor.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lepisorus bicolor has pinnate fronds characterized by a distinct bicolor appearance where the upper surface of the pinnules is green and the underside is covered in dense, silvery-white scales or hairs. These scales are most prominent on the abaxial surface, providing a striking contrast to the verdant dorsal side. The stipes are typically dark and slender, supporting the delicate, spreading foliage. Small sori are located on the underside of the segments, often tucked near the midvein or margins. This combination of scale density and color variation is a primary diagnostic feature for identifying the species.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires high humidity, consistent moisture, and dappled or indirect sunlight to thrive in a cultivation setting.

You should place it in a container with well-draining soil that retains moisture without becoming waterlogged.
