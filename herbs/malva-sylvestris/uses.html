Wild Mallow (Malva sylvestris) Uses

Wild Mallow is used to treat respiratory conditions such as cough, bronchitis, and sinusitis. It helps reduce inflammation in the lungs and mucous membranes, alleviating symptoms like sore throat and respiratory congestion. Its properties also support clearing mucous buildup and may ease asthma symptoms.

Wild Mallow (Malva sylvestris) Monograph

1. Cough

Wild Mallow is used to soothe coughs due to its mucilage content, which coats the throat and reduces irritation. It is often prepared as a tea to ease dry or productive coughs. Its anti-inflammatory properties may also help reduce bronchial inflammation. It is typically used in herbal remedies for respiratory discomfort.

2. Respiratory infections

Wild Mallow is used to support respiratory health due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. It helps soothe coughs, reduce mucus buildup, and ease symptoms of colds and bronchitis. Its mucilage content coats the throat, providing relief during respiratory infections. It is often prepared as a tea or gargle for direct application.

3. Bronchitis

Wild Mallow has been traditionally used to soothe symptoms of bronchitis due to its mucilage content, which helps lubricate the airways and reduce inflammation. It may also act as an expectorant, aiding in the removal of mucus. Its calming properties support respiratory comfort during bouts of bronchial irritation.

4. Sore throat

Wild Mallow is used to soothe sore throats due to its anti-inflammatory and demulcent properties. It forms a thick, protective layer over the throat, reducing irritation. Often prepared as a tea, it provides relief from coughing and inflammation. Its mild nature makes it suitable for long-term use in throat care.

5. Sinusitis

Wild Mallow (Malva sylvestris) is used in herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms of sinusitis due to its anti-inflammatory and mucolytic properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion and soothe irritated mucous membranes. Typically prepared as a tea or steam inhalation, it supports respiratory comfort during sinus infections.

6. Respiratory congestion

Wild Mallow is used to alleviate respiratory congestion due to its mucilage content, which soothes irritated airways. It helps reduce inflammation and mucus buildup in the respiratory tract. Typically prepared as a tea, it supports easier breathing and may ease symptoms of coughs and bronchitis.

7. Inflamed lungs

Wild Mallow is used to soothe inflamed lungs due to its mucilage content, which forms a protective layer over irritated tissues. It helps reduce coughing and inflammation, making it beneficial for conditions like bronchitis. Its anti-inflammatory and demulcent properties support respiratory health. It is typically prepared as a tea or syrup.

8. Mucous buildup

Wild Mallow is used to address mucous buildup due to its demulcent properties. The plant contains mucilage that coats and soothes irritated mucous membranes. It helps to loosen and expel excess mucus from the respiratory tract. This makes it beneficial for conditions like bronchitis and sinus congestion.

9. Asthma

Wild Mallow (Malva sylvestris) has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in managing asthma symptoms. Its mucilage content helps soothe airway inflammation and reduce bronchial irritation. It may also help loosen mucus, making it easier to expel. This makes it a valuable herb in herbal medicine for respiratory support.

10. Mucous membrane inflammation

Wild Mallow is used to soothe mucous membrane inflammation due to its demulcent properties. It forms a protective layer over irritated tissues, reducing friction and promoting healing. Commonly applied as a poultice or infusion, it is effective for conditions like sore throats, coughs, and digestive tract irritations. Its mild nature makes it suitable for long-term use in herbal treatments.
