Wild Mallow (Malva sylvestris) Compounds

Wild Mallow contains several key active compounds, including tannins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its astringent and anti-inflammatory properties. Glycosides and coumarin support its mild diuretic and antispasmodic effects. Saponins, terpenoids, and essential oils add to its detoxifying and antimicrobial qualities. Anthocyanins and alkaloids further enhance its antioxidant and therapeutic potential.

Wild Mallow (Malva sylvestris) Monograph

1. Tannin

Tannin in Wild Mallow (Malva sylvestris) contributes to its astringent properties. It helps reduce inflammation and soothe mucous membranes. Tannins also support wound healing and may aid in digestive health. This compound is a key component in the plant's traditional medicinal uses.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Wild Mallow (Malva sylvestris) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help reduce swelling and support immune function. They are found in the leaves and flowers, making them valuable in herbal remedies for respiratory and skin conditions.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid in Wild Mallow (Malva sylvestris) contributes to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. It helps reduce oxidative stress and supports the plant's traditional use in treating skin conditions and digestive issues. This compound is present in significant amounts in the leaves and flowers of the plant.

4. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Wild Mallow (Malva sylvestris). It contributes to the plant's soothing and anti-inflammatory properties, often used in herbal remedies for respiratory and digestive ailments. Glycosides in Wild Mallow support expectoration and help reduce irritation in the respiratory tract.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Wild Mallow (Malva sylvestris). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. Coumarin also has mild sedative effects and may support digestive health. Its presence enhances the therapeutic value of Wild Mallow in herbal medicine.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Wild Mallow (Malva sylvestris). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and demulcent properties, making it useful for soothing irritated mucous membranes. Saponins also exhibit mild cleansing and emulsifying actions, supporting digestive and respiratory health.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Wild Mallow (Malva sylvestris) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. These compounds help reduce swelling and combat infections, supporting the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and skin conditions. They are part of the plant's broader biochemical profile that underpins its therapeutic applications.

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of Wild Mallow (Malva sylvestris) contains volatile compounds like palmitic acid and linoleic acid. These components contribute to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties, making it valuable in herbal medicine for skin and respiratory conditions.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Wild Mallow (Malva sylvestris). It contributes to the plant's vibrant color and has antioxidant properties that support skin health and reduce inflammation. Found in the flowers and leaves, anthocyanin is valued in herbal medicine for its potential therapeutic benefits.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Wild Mallow (Malva sylvestris). It contributes to the plant's therapeutic properties, potentially supporting respiratory and digestive health. Alkaloids in this plant are often studied for their mild sedative and anti-inflammatory effects. Their presence enhances the plant's value in herbal medicine practices.
