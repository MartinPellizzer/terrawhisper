Pistacia Lentiscus

Pistacia lentiscus, commonly known as mastic tree, is a small evergreen tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a resinous herb with a rich history of use in traditional medicine, valued for its numerous health benefits.

The therapeutic actions of Pistacia lentiscus include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which contribute to its effectiveness in treating various health conditions such as digestive issues, wounds, and skin problems.

The bioactive constituents of this herb include sesquiterpenes, terpenoids, and flavonoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Possible Side Effects of pistacia lentiscus

Pistacia lentiscus can have side effects if used improperly, such as allergic reactions, skin irritation, and respiratory problems.

Excessive consumption of the herb can cause dizziness, nausea, and stomach upset.

In rare cases, it may interact with other medications, leading to complications like low blood pressure or kidney damage.

Additionally, using the herb during pregnancy or breastfeeding may pose risks to the unborn child or fetus.
