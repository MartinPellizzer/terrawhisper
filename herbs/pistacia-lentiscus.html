Lentisk

Pistacia lentiscus

Lentisk (Pistacia lentiscus) is a member of the Anacardiaceae family, native to the Mediterranean region and parts of the Middle East. Traditionally, its leaves, bark, and resin have been used for treating skin diseases, as an anti-inflammatory agent, and for their antiseptic properties. The plant has a long history of cultural and medicinal use in various traditional systems.

Botanical Identification

Lentisk, scientifically known as Pistacia lentiscus, belongs to the Anacardiaceae family. It is also called mastic tree or mastic tree in various regions. A perennial shrub or small tree, it is native to the Mediterranean region. It has dense, aromatic foliage and produces small yellow flowers. Historically referred to as "lentisk" in herbal texts, it has several synonyms in traditional medicine.

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory ailments.

Infusion: Used for skin conditions and as a general tonic.

Essential oil: Used for skin infections and as an antiseptic.

Safety Profile

Lentisk (Pistacia lentiscus) is generally considered safe when used in moderate amounts. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to potential risks to the fetus or infant. Always consult a healthcare professional before use.
