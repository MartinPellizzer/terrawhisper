Lentisk (Pistacia lentiscus): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

What is Lentisk (Pistacia Lentiscus)?

Lentisk, or Pistacia lentiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, commonly used in herbal medicine for its resin, known as mastic, which has antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and digestive properties.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Lentisk is Pistacia lentiscus. This plant belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Sapindales, family Anacardiaceae, genus Pistacia, and species Pistacia lentiscus. It is known as lentisk in the Mediterranean region and as mastic tree in the eastern Mediterranean. Common names also include mastic tree, mastic tree, and mastic tree in various regional contexts. Synonyms for this plant include Pistacia lentiscus var. lentiscus, Pistacia lentiscus subsp. lentiscus, and regional names such as mastic tree and mastic tree.

Plant Description and Morphology

Lentisk is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 5 meters, with a dense, rounded crown and thorny branches.

Its leaves are simple, alternate, and oval to elliptic in shape, with a leathery texture and a bluish-green upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, appearing in late spring to early summer. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor, rocky soils.

The bark is dark brown to black, with a rough, fissured texture, while the leaves have a strong, aromatic scent when crushed.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Lentisk (Pistacia lentiscus) is native to the Mediterranean region, including southern Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East, and is commonly found in coastal areas, rocky slopes, and dry woodlands.
