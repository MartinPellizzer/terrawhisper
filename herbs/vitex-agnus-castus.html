What to know about Vitex agnus-castus (chaste tree) before using it medicinally

Vitex agnus-castus, commonly known as chaste tree, is a revered herb prized for its multifaceted health benefits, which encompass menstrual regulation, stress relief, and mood stabilization, thus promoting overall well-being.

Cultivated for its attractive flowers and berries, the chaste tree is a popular ornamental plant, often grown in gardens and landscapes, particularly in warm climates. Botanically, Vitex agnus-castus is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, four-angled twigs and small, lilac-colored flowers.

Historically, the chaste tree has been used for centuries in traditional medicine, with references dating back to ancient Greek and Roman civilizations, including those of Hippocrates and Galen.

What are the medicinal properties of Vitex agnus-castus?

Vitex agnus-castus helps with menstrual irregularities, premenstrual syndrome (PMS), and menopausal symptoms due to its ability to regulate estrogen and progesterone levels. It also shows potential in managing anxiety, insomnia, and mood disorders.

The plant's medicinal properties are attributed to its active constituents, including iridoids, flavonoids, and terpenoids. The most notable iridoid is aucubin, responsible for the plant's estrogenic and anti-inflammatory effects.

The most commonly used parts of the plant for medicinal purposes are the leaves and berries. The leaves are typically dried and prepared as a tea, while the berries are crushed and used in supplements.

Improper use of Vitex agnus-castus can cause side effects, including mild gastrointestinal upset, headaches, and dizziness. Concomitant use with hormonal medications can exacerbate estrogen-related side effects.

When using Vitex agnus-castus medicinally, it is essential to follow proper dosing and precautions, particularly for individuals with hormonal imbalances or taking estrogen-related medications. Consultation with a healthcare professional is recommended to avoid potential interactions or adverse effects.
