Chaste Tree (Vitex agnus-castus): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

What is Chaste Tree (Vitex Agnus-Castus)?

Chaste Tree, or Vitex agnus-castus, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region, known for its use in traditional herbal medicine for its potential hormonal and menstrual regulatory properties.

Botanical Identity and Classification

Chaste Tree, or Vitex agnus-castus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, belonging to the genus Vitex in the family Verbenaceae.

Common Names and Synonyms

The Chaste Tree, scientifically known as Vitex agnus-castus, is also referred to by common names such as Monk's Pepper, Verveine, and Agnus Castus, and has been known by various regional names throughout history.

Plant Description and Morphology

The Chaste Tree (Vitex agnus-castus) is a shrub or small tree with opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually hairy on the underside, and small, clustered, fragrant flowers that range from purple to white, blooming in summer.

Native Habitat and Distribution

The Chaste Tree (Vitex agnus-castus) is native to the Mediterranean region, including southern Europe, North Africa, and western Asia, and has been historically cultivated in temperate climates for its medicinal and ornamental properties.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The dried flowers, berries, and sometimes leaves of the Chaste Tree (Vitex agnus-castus) are used medicinally in herbal medicine.

Phytochemical Composition

The phytochemical composition of Chaste Tree (Vitex agnus-castus) includes essential oils, flavonoids, iridoids, and lignans, with key compounds such as vitexin, isovitexin, and aucubin contributing to its medicinal properties.

Pharmacological Properties

The Chaste Tree (Vitex agnus-castus) contains compounds such as flavonoids, iridoids, and essential oils that exhibit estrogenic, anti-inflammatory, and antispasmodic properties, which contribute to its traditional use in regulating menstrual cycles and alleviating symptoms of premenstrual syndrome.

Mechanisms of Action

The mechanisms of action of Chaste Tree (Vitex agnus-castus) involve its ability to modulate hormonal activity, particularly through its influence on luteinizing hormone (LH) and prolactin levels, and its interaction with estrogen receptors, which may support reproductive and menstrual health.

Therapeutic Uses and Indications

Chaste Tree is used to treat menstrual irregularities, premenstrual syndrome, infertility, menopausal symptoms, and hormonal imbalances.

Preparation Methods and Forms

The preparation methods and forms of Chaste Tree (Vitex agnus-castus) include standardized extracts, tinctures, teas, capsules, and essential oils, with the most common form being standardized aqueous or alcohol-based extracts containing 0.2-0.5% vitexin and 0.2-0.5% aucubin.

Dosage and Administration

The typical dosage of Chaste Tree (Vitex agnus-castus) for herbal use ranges from 20 to 40 mg of standardized extract per day, divided into two or three doses, with the exact amount depending on the concentration of active compounds and the intended therapeutic effect.

Safety, Side Effects, and Contraindications

While Chaste Tree is generally well-tolerated, it may cause side effects such as nausea, headaches, digestive upset, or hormonal imbalances, particularly with prolonged use or high doses.

Drug Interactions

Chaste tree (Vitex agnus-castus) may interact with medications such as birth control pills, hormone replacement therapies, and certain antidepressants due to its potential effects on hormone regulation, though specific interactions are not well-documented in clinical studies.

Toxicity and Precautions

While Chaste Tree (Vitex agnus-castus) is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses, it may cause mild side effects such as gastrointestinal discomfort, headaches, or skin irritation, and it should be avoided during pregnancy and by individuals with hormone-sensitive conditions due to its potential phytoestrogenic effects.

Cultivation and Harvesting

Chaste tree is typically cultivated in well-drained soil in full sun, requiring minimal water and care once established, with pruning recommended in early spring to promote healthy growth and flowering.

Processing and Extraction

Processing Vitex agnus-castus involves harvesting the ripe berries and dried flowers, typically in late summer to early autumn, followed by drying the plant material in a shaded, well-ventilated area to preserve its active compounds.

Storage and Shelf Life

Chaste Tree (Vitex agnus-castus) should be stored in a cool, dry, and dark place in an airtight container to maintain potency and prevent moisture and light exposure, with a typical shelf life of 2 to 3 years when properly stored.

Scientific Research and Evidence

Scientific research on Vitex agnus-castus has explored its potential effects on hormonal balance, menstrual regulation, and mood disorders, with some studies suggesting possible benefits for women's reproductive health, though results remain mixed and further rigorous clinical trials are needed.

Traditional and Historical Uses

Chaste Tree (Vitex agnus-castus) has been traditionally used for centuries in herbal medicine to support hormonal balance, particularly in women, with historical applications including the treatment of menstrual disorders, menopausal symptoms, and emotional well-being.

Regulatory Status

The Chaste Tree (Vitex agnus-castus) is not approved as a drug by the FDA, but it is classified as a dietary supplement under the Dietary Supplement Health and Education Act of 1994, allowing it to be sold without prior approval for specific therapeutic claims.
