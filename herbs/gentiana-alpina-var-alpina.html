Alpine Gentian (Gentiana Alpina Var. Alpina)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Gentiana alpina var. alpina.

Alpine gentian, scientifically known as Gentiana alpina var. alpina, is a medicinal herb native to the alpine regions of Europe, prized for its vibrant blue flowers and potent therapeutic properties. This adaptogen is traditionally used to support digestive health, enhance vitality, and alleviate fatigue due to its high concentration of bitter compounds like gentiopicroside. In traditional European medicine, it was revered as a remedy for digestive disorders, liver support, and as a general tonic to improve overall well-being. Modern wellness applications include its use in herbal formulations to boost metabolism, reduce inflammation, and support detoxification processes. Its unique feature is its striking blue color and a distinctive bitter flavor, which has historically been associated with its ability to stimulate appetite and improve digestion, making it a valued herb in both historical and contemporary herbal practices.
