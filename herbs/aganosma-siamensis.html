Aganosma siamensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Aganosma siamensis?

Aganosma siamensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Gentianaales and is a member of the family Apocynaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Aganosma, and its specific species epithet, siamensis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Aganosma siamensis contains a variety of bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, and terpenoids. These specific chemical constituents are responsible for the plant's documented pharmacological activities such as antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. Research suggests that the presence of specific phenolic compounds contributes significantly to its ability to mitigate oxidative stress in biological systems. Furthermore, the diverse chemical profile supports its traditional use in folk medicine for treating various ailments. These medicinal properties make the plant a subject of interest for developing new natural therapeutic agents.

What are the therapeutic actions of Aganosma siamensis?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat digestive ailments, fever, malaria, and various types of inflammation. Its medicinal properties are frequently utilized to address stomach discomfort and gastrointestinal issues. Traditional practitioners often employ its leaves and roots to reduce high body temperatures. Additionally, it is applied in folk medicine to alleviate pain associated with inflammatory conditions. These diverse applications make it a significant botanical resource in regional healthcare practices.
