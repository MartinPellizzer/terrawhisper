Gnetum luofuense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gnetum luofuense has woody, scrambling stems that branch extensively and bear small, scale-like leaves arranged in opposite pairs along the branches. The plant is characterized by its photosynthetic stems which often take on a greenish hue to assist in energy production. Its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape and possess a relatively thin texture. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in clusters, eventually developing into fleshy, berry-like seeds. These structural adaptations allow the species to thrive in its specific montane habitat.
