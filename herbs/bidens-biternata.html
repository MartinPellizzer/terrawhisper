What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bidens biternaata has pinnately compound leaves that feature distinctively double-serrated margins and a dark green, textured surface. The foliage is arranged oppositely along a sturdy, upright stem that can exhibit slight reddish pigmentation. Each leaflet is lanceolate in shape, tapering to a sharp point at the apex. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, typically presenting in clusters. The overall structure of the plant is herbaceous, supported by a root system designed for stability in its native habitat.
