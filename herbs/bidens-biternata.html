Bidens biternata

What's the taxonomical classification of Bidens biternata?

Bidens bitertana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it falls into the order Asterales and is a member of the family Asteraceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific genus, Bidens, and its unique species designation, bitertana.

Bidens biternaata has potent antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties that serve to mitigate various infectious and inflammatory conditions. What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, and various inflammatory conditions. The extracts are often applied topically to accelerate the healing process of minor cuts and abrasions. Some studies suggest the plant possesses antimicrobial properties that help combat bacterial pathogens on the skin. It is also utilized in traditional medicine to reduce swelling and alleviate localized pain. These therapeutic applications rely on the specific bioactive compounds found within its leaves and stems.

What are the herbal preparations of Bidens biternata?

Bidens bipinnata is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice to utilize its medicinal properties. Practitioners often simmer the dried roots or leaves in water to create a potent decoction for internal use. Alternatively, steeping the fresh plant parts in hot water produces a milder infusion suitable for various ailments. For skin conditions or localized pain, the crushed plant material is applied directly as a poultice. These diverse methods allow for the effective extraction of the plant's bioactive compounds.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Bidens biternaata can cause severe skin irritation, localized swelling, and intense itching upon direct physical contact with the leaves. The caustic oils within the plant often lead to red, inflamed rashes that may persist for several days. If the sap enters the eyes, it can trigger stinging sensations and temporary blurred vision. Ingesting any part of this vegetation may result in nausea, abdominal cramping, or more serious gastrointestinal distress. It is essential to wash the affected areas immediately with soap and water to minimize these reactions.
