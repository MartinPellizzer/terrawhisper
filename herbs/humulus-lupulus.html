Humulus lupulus

What's the taxonomical classification of Humulus lupulus?

Humulus lupulus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical structure, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Rosales, it is a member of the family Cannabaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by the genus Humulus and is identified specifically as the species lupulus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Humulus lupulus has climbing stems known as bines that utilize twining mechanisms to ascend support structures, characterized by an alternate arrangement of deeply lobed, ovate leaves with serrated margins. These perennial herbaceous plants develop a vigorous rhizomatous root system that allows for underground spreading and long-term survival. The foliage typically features a dark green color on the upper surface and a paler, pubescent underside. During the reproductive stage, the plant produces cone-like strobiles which are the specialized female flowers used for lupulin production. These structures are often accompanied by small, inconspicuous male flowers produced on separate plants.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, spanning across much of Europe, northern Asia, and North America. It thrives in various climates ranging from maritime to continental, provided there is sufficient moisture and sunlight. While its natural range is limited to these northern latitudes, it has been widely introduced to other parts of the world for commercial hop production. Cultivation is particularly concentrated in regions like Germany, the Czech Republic, and the Pacific Northwest of the United States. Because it can spread via rhizomes, it often establishes itself in wild habitats near riverbanks and woodlands far from its original center of origin.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Humulus lupulus contains medicinal compounds primarily located within the dried female flowers, commonly known as strobiles or hops. These flowering heads are harvested and processed to extract essential oils and bitter resins used for their sedative properties. The lupulin glands found inside these strobiles provide the concentrated chemical constituents necessary for treating insomnia and anxiety. While the leaves and stems are sometimes used in traditional preparations, the flowers remain the most potent and widely utilized part of the plant. This specific reliance on the reproductive structures ensures a high concentration of active phytochemicals.

What are the pharmacological activities of Humulus lupulus?

This plant has diverse pharmacological activities including anxiolytic, sedative, hypnotic, anti-inflammatory, and phytoestrogenic properties. Its active compounds work to reduce anxiety and promote relaxation in the central nervous system. These constituents also function as sleep aids by inducing hypnotic effects to improve sleep quality. Additionally, the plant contains substances that mitigate inflammation throughout the body. The presence of phytoestrogens allows it to mimic certain hormonal functions within biological systems.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds 6-prenylnaringenin (6-PN), 8-prenylnarringenin (8-PN), alpha-acids, hop bitter acids, and xanthohumol. These specific constituents are primarily responsible for the characteristic bitter flavor and various pharmacological properties of the species. The prenylated flavonoids 6-PN and 8-PN are noted for their potent estrogenic activities within biological systems. Additionally, the alpha-acids and hop bitter acids contribute to the preservative qualities and flavor profile of brewed beverages. Xanthohumol serves as another significant bioactive molecule that has been studied for its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory potential.

As discussed in a study, Humulus lupulus contains several secondary plant metabolites including the prenylated chalcone xanthohumol (XN). Other major derivatives from hops include isoxanthohumol (IXN), 6-prenylnaringenin (6-PN), and 8-prenylnaringenin (8-PN). These hop-derived compounds inhibit SARS-CoV-2 PLpro with half maximal inhibitory concentration (IC50) values in the range of 59-162 µM. Specifically, XN and 6-PN impede viral replication with IC50 values of 3.3 µM and 7.3 µM, respectively. These findings support the possibility of developing new hop-derived antiviral drugs.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for managing insomnia, anxiety, stress, sleep disturbances, and menopause symptoms. Its sedative properties help calm the nervous system to promote deeper rest during the night. Many people rely on it to alleviate the restlessness associated with chronic sleep disturbances. Additionally, it can be effective in reducing the physiological symptoms of anxiety and daily stress. It is also frequently utilized to mitigate the various uncomfortable menopause symptoms that women experience.

What are the herbal preparations of this plant?

This plant can be prepared as an infusion, tincture, essential oil, extract, or decoction to utilize its sedative and bitter properties. An infusion of the dried flowers is commonly used to make a calming tea for sleep support. For more concentrated therapeutic effects, a tincture or a standardized extract is often prepared using alcohol as a solvent. The essential oil can be extracted through steam distillation to be used in aromatherapy or topical applications. A decoction may be made by simmering the tougher parts of the plant to release its medicinal compounds into a liquid base.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause adverse reactions including drowsiness, sedation, decreased alertness, dizziness, dry mouth, nausea, confusion, and depression. Users may experience a significant reduction in cognitive focus or feel lightheaded shortly after consumption. Physical discomfort such as a parched throat or stomach upset is also a common occurrence. Some individuals might struggle with mental fog or unexpected shifts in mood. These varied side effects can impact daily activities and overall well-being.
