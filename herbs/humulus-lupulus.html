Humulus lupulus

Humulus lupulus, commonly known as hops, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It has been traditionally used for its numerous health benefits, including its ability to aid in sleep, reduce anxiety and stress, and exhibit anti-inflammatory properties.

The therapeutic actions of hops include sedative, anxiolytic, and anti-spasmodic effects, which are attributed to its bioactive constituents such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and essential oils.

The bioactive constituents of hops have been shown to possess antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial activities, making it a valuable plant in traditional medicine.
