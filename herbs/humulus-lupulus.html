Hops

Humulus lupulus

Hops (Humulus lupulus) is a flowering plant native to North America and Europe, commonly known for its use in brewing beer, but also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in improving sleep disorders and reducing anxiety.

Botanical Identification

Hops (Humulus lupulus) is a perennial flowering plant in the Cannabaceae family. Commonly known as hop cone or humulus, it is native to Europe, Asia, and North America. The plant has hollow stems, palmately lobed leaves, and cone-like flowers called strobiles. It is also referred to as "hopping" in some regional herbal texts.

Active Compounds

Hops contain key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, coumarins, and terpenoids. These compounds work together to exert their effects, with flavonoids providing anti-inflammatory properties and terpenoids contributing to sedative and calming actions. Coumarins may also support digestive health and muscle relaxation.

Hops Uses.

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and as a diuretic.

Infusion: Used for respiratory issues like coughs and colds.

Tincture: Used for anxiety, insomnia, and as a sedative.

Safety Profile

Hops are generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, they may cause drowsiness and should be avoided by individuals with epilepsy or those taking sedative medications. Prolonged use may lead to dependency, so it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before use.
