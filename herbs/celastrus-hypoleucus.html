Celastrus hypoleucus

What's the taxonomical classification of Celastrus hypoleucus?

Celastrus hypoleucus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows a specific evolutionary lineage that leads to its placement in the order Celastrales. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the family Celastraceae, within the genus Celastrus, ultimately identifying it as the specific species hypoleucus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Celastrus hypoleucus has opposite, simple leaves that are elliptic to oblong in shape, measuring between 2 and 5 centimeters in length, with an acute apex and a finely serrated margin. The leaf surfaces are typically a deep green, while the undersides often feature a paler, slightly pubescent texture. Its woody stems are characterized by a smooth to slightly lenticellate bark that transitions to a more textured surface as the plant matures. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that cluster in cymes, eventually giving way to distinctive, fleshy drupes. These fruits are typically bright red or orange and are encased in a thin, papery capsule that splits upon maturity.

What are the pharmacological activities of Celastrus hypoleucus?

This plant has anti-proliferation, anti-tumor, and apoptosis-inducing pharmacological activities that target various cancer cell lines. These biological properties allow the plant's extracts to effectively inhibit the rapid growth and division of malignant cells. By triggering apoptosis-inducing mechanisms, the phytochemicals promote programmed cell death within diseased tissues. The demonstrated anti-tumor effects suggest that the species contains potent bioactive compounds capable of disrupting cancer progression. Consequently, these anti-proliferation capabilities make the plant a significant subject for further oncological research.

According to a study published by "Zhongguo Zhong yao za zhi = Zhongguo zhongyao zazhi = China journal of Chinese materia medica", the novel triterpene (12-oleanene-3beta, 6alpha-diol) from Celastrus hypoleucus significantly inhibits the proliferation of RK0 cells in a dose-dependent and time-dependent manner. The IC50 for this inhibition was (12.20 +/- 0.79) microg x mL(-1) at 48 h. DNA ladders were detected on agarose gels at concentrations ranging from 10 microg x mL(-1) to 20 microg x mL(-1) at 48 h. After 48 h of treatment, apoptotic rates in the RK0 cell line increased from (2.93 +/- 0.84) % to (50.79 +/- 6.61) % at concentrations from 2.5 microg x mL(-1) to 20 microg x mL(-1). Furthermore, the number of cells was obviously reduced during the G0-G1 phase and G2-M phase after 48 hours of treatment, though not during the S phase.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for the treatment of various types of cancer, specifically targeting colorectal cancer and various other malignant tumors. Researchers have identified that the plant contains bioactive compounds that can inhibit the growth of cancer cells. These chemical properties allow the plant to interfere with the cellular processes that drive tumor development. Studies suggest that its extracts may induce programmed cell death in certain cancerous tissues. This makes the species a significant subject of interest in pharmacological research for oncology.

According to a study published by "Bioorganic & medicinal chemistry letters", Celastrus hypoleucus contains novel skeleton terpenes that demonstrate anti-tumor activities. The stalks of Celastrus hypoleucus (Oliv.) Warb. provided the isolation of Celahypodiol 1, which is an unusual 17-membered carbon diterpenoid. Additionally, the plant yielded a new triterpenoid 12-oleanene-3beta,6alpha-diol 2. The study also identified four known compounds from the species, specifically furreginol 3, suigol 4, 20(30)-lupene-3beta, 29-diol 5, and 20(29)-lupene-1beta,3beta-diol 6. These new compounds exhibited anti-tumor activities against a panel of human tumor cell lines.
