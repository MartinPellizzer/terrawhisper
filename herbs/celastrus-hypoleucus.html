Celastrus hypoleucus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Celastrus hypoleucus has opposite, simple leaves that are elliptic to oblong in shape, measuring between 2 and 5 centimeters in length, with an acute apex and a finely serrated margin. The leaf surfaces are typically a deep green, while the undersides often feature a paler, slightly pubescent texture. Its woody stems are characterized by a smooth to slightly lenticellate bark that transitions to a more textured surface as the plant matures. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that cluster in cymes, eventually giving way to distinctive, fleshy drupes. These fruits are typically bright red or orange and are encased in a thin, papery capsule that splits upon maturity.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of the Caribbean, including the Greater Antilles and the Lesser Antilles. It is frequently found in coastal areas and scrublands where it can thrive in limestone-based soils. Beyond the Caribbean, the species is also recorded in parts of Central America.
