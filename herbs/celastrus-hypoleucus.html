Celastrus hypoleucus

What's the taxonomical classification of Celastrus hypoleucus?

Celastrus hypoleucus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows a specific evolutionary lineage that leads to its placement in the order Celastrales. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the family Celastraceae, within the genus Celastrus, ultimately identifying it as the specific species hypoleucus.

    What are the herbal preparations of Celastrus hypoleucus?

    Celastrus hypoleucus is prepared as decoctions, infusions, and topical poultices to utilize its bioactive compounds for medicinal purposes. The bark and leaves are most frequently boiled in water to create concentrated decoctions for internal use. Infusions are also commonly made by steeping the plant parts in hot water to extract milder therapeutic properties. For external applications, the crushed plant material is applied directly to the skin as a poultice to treat localized inflammation. These various methods allow for the targeted delivery of the plant's alkaloids and other chemical constituents.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Celastrus hypoleucus can cause severe gastrointestinal distress including vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea if ingested. The plant contains toxic compounds that may lead to more systemic complications if consumed in significant amounts. Individuals who touch the plant may also experience skin irritation or localized dermatitis. There is a risk of more serious neurological symptoms if the toxins enter the bloodstream. Due to these potential hazards, it is important to avoid all contact and ingestion of the species.
