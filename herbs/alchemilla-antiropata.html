Lady'S Mantle (Alchemilla Antiropata)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Alchemilla antiropata.

Lady's mantle, scientifically known as Alchemilla antirrhonata, is a medicinal herb valued for its adaptogenic properties and historical use in traditional herbal medicine. This low-growing plant, native to Europe and parts of Asia, is renowned for its ability to support women's health, particularly in regulating menstrual cycles and alleviating symptoms of menopause. Traditionally, it has been used to treat uterine issues, excessive bleeding, and skin conditions, and was believed to have the power to "turn lead into gold," hence its name. In modern wellness practices, Lady's mantle is used in herbal formulations for hormonal balance and as a natural remedy for inflammation and digestive support. Its unique feature is the presence of a rare compound called aucubin, which contributes to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, making it a valuable herb in both historical and contemporary herbal medicine.
