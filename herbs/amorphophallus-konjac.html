Konjac (Amorphophallus konjac)

Konjac (Amorphophallus konjac) is a member of the Araceae family, native to Southeast Asia, East Asia, and South Asia. Traditionally, its rhizomes, tubers, and roots have been used for culinary uses, decoctions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, tonic, and astringent actions, and has a long history of use in japanese kampo medicine, traditional chinese medicine, and korean traditional medicine.
