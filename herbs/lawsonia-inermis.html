What to know about Lawsonia inermis before using it medicinally

Lawsonia inermis, commonly known as henna, is a medicinal herb with a range of applications in traditional medicine.

The main medicinal uses of Lawsonia inermis are in treating skin conditions such as eczema, psoriasis, and dermatitis, as well as aiding in the management of diabetes and digestive issues. The herb is known for its health benefits, including its antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. Lawsonia inermis is also attributed to its therapeutic properties, which include being a natural dye, a skin protectant, and an anti-haemorrhagic agent.

The herb's bioactive compounds include lawsone, a key component responsible for its therapeutic effects, and other flavonoids and phenolic acids.

Featured Study:

According to a study published by "Pharmacology", the crude ethanolic extract of Lawsonia inermis produced significant and dose-dependent anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antipyretic effects in rats. The butanol and chloroform fractions of Lawsonia inermis were found to have more potent anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antipyretic effects than the crude extract, with the butanolic extract being the most effective in the analgesic test. Additionally, the isolated compound 2-hydroxy-1,4-naphthaquinone (lawsone) from Lawsonia inermis was found to possess significant anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antipyretic activity, similar to the reference drug phenylbutazone.
