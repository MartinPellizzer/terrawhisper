Lawsonia inermis, commonly known as henna, is a plant-based dye obtained from the leaves of the Lawsonia inermis herb.

This herb has been used for centuries in traditional medicine due to its numerous health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties.

The therapeutic actions of henna include wound healing, skin protection, and hair care, attributed to its bioactive constituents such as lawsone, flavonoids, and phenolic acids.

Possible Side Effects of lawsonia inermis

Lawsonia inermis can have side effects if used improperly, such as allergic reactions, skin irritation, and gastrointestinal problems.

Ingesting large quantities of the herb can lead to nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea due to its high tannin content.

Excessive use or prolonged exposure to the herb can also cause liver damage and increased risk of bleeding due to its potential interaction with blood-thinning medications.

Furthermore, applying Lawsonia inermis directly to the skin without proper dilution can cause redness, itching, and rashes in sensitive individuals.
