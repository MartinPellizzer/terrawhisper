Phlegmariurus carinatus

What's the taxonomical classification of Phlegmariurus carinatus?

Phlegmariurus carinatus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further organized into the subclass Lycopodiidae and the order Lycopodiales. This plant is a member of the family Lycopodiaceae, falling specifically under the genus Phlegmariurus, and is identified by its unique species designation, carinatus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phlegmariurus carinatus has pendulous, creeping stems characterized by small, closely overlapping, scale-like leaves that are distinctly asymmetric and often feature a prominent keel or ridge. These leaves are arranged in a spiral pattern around the slender axes, creating a dense, brush-like appearance. The morphology is defined by the rigid, somewhat flattened structure of the microphylls which provides a textured surface. Spore-producing structures called strobili are located at the terminal ends of the branches. This specific leaf shape and stem habit distinguish the species from other members of the Lycopodiaceae family.
