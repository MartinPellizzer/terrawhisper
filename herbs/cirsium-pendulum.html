Cirsium pendulum

What's the taxonomical classification of Cirsium pendulum?

Cirsium pendulum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae, falling specifically within the genus Cirsium, with its unique biological identity defined by the species name pendulum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cirsium pendulum has pendulous, nodding flower heads that are surrounded by spreading, spreading, or recurved involucral bracts. The plant features long, slender stems that are typically hairy and can reach heights of up to one meter. Its leaves are deeply lobed and possess sharp, prickly margins characteristic of the thistle family. The foliage often exhibits a spreading habit, providing a textured base for the drooping inflorescences. These distinctive hanging flower heads distinguish it from many other species within the genus.
