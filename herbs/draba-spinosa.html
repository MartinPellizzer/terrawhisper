Draba spinosa

Draba spinosa, also known as prickly draba or prickly whitlow grass, is a perennial plant species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, particularly for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, which have been attributed to the presence of compounds such as flavonoids and phenolic acids.

The health benefits of Draba spinosa include its potential to reduce inflammation, improve cardiovascular health, and exhibit antimicrobial properties, making it a valuable herb in traditional medicine.

The plant is typically prepared in herbal teas, infusions, or dried extracts, and is often used to treat various conditions, including respiratory issues, digestive problems, and skin irritations.
