Mandarin Orange (Citrus reticulata) Compounds

The primary compounds of Mandarin Orange include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and essential oils, known for their aromatic and therapeutic effects. Phenolic acids and terpenoids contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial benefits. Tannins and coumarin offer astringent and antispasmodic actions, while glycosides and saponins support digestive health. Anthocyanins and alkaloids add to its diverse pharmacological profile.

Mandarin Orange (Citrus reticulata) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Mandarin Orange (Citrus reticulata). They exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, contributing to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for respiratory and digestive health. These compounds help protect cells from oxidative damage and support immune function.

2. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound in Mandarin Orange (Citrus reticulata). It contains volatile aromatic compounds like limonene and linalool, which contribute to its antimicrobial and calming properties. This oil is widely used in herbal medicine for its therapeutic and aromatic benefits.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in Mandarin Orange (Citrus reticulata). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress in the body. This compound supports various therapeutic effects associated with Mandarin Orange in traditional herbal medicine.

4. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Mandarin Orange (Citrus reticulata). They contribute to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial effects. These compounds are often responsible for the therapeutic benefits associated with citrus fruits in herbal medicine.

5. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Mandarin Orange (Citrus reticulata). It exhibits astringent properties and is used in traditional herbal medicine to treat diarrhea and skin conditions. Tannins also contribute to the plant's antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a bioactive compound found in Mandarin Orange (Citrus reticulata). It exhibits anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory properties, contributing to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for promoting blood circulation and reducing swelling.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Mandarin Orange (Citrus reticulata). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by supporting digestive health and reducing inflammation. Glycosides in Mandarin Orange are often used in herbal medicine to promote liver function and ease gastrointestinal discomfort.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Mandarin Orange (Citrus reticulata). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Saponin is commonly used in traditional herbal medicine to support digestive health and reduce respiratory infections.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Mandarin Orange (Citrus reticulata). It contributes to the fruit's vibrant color and possesses antioxidant properties. In herbal medicine, anthocyanin supports cardiovascular health and may reduce inflammation. Its presence in Mandarin Orange highlights the plant's therapeutic potential.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Mandarin Orange (Citrus reticulata). It contributes to the plant's pharmacological properties, though its specific role and concentration may vary. Alkaloids in Mandarin Orange are often studied for their potential therapeutic effects in traditional herbal medicine.
