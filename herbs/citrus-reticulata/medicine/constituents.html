10 Medicinal Constituents Of Citrus Reticulata (Mandarin)

Citrus reticulata has active constituents such as vitamin C, flavonoids, and limonoids.

These compounds possess antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. The flavonoids, in particular, have been shown to have anti-cancer and cardiovascular protective effects.

For example, the flavonoid nobiletin has been found to inhibit the growth of cancer cells and reduce inflammation in the body, potentially improving the quality of life for individuals with certain diseases.

1. Limonene

Citrus reticulata limonene is a naturally occurring compound found in the peels and leaves of citrus fruits.

It is a monoterpene hydrocarbon that is responsible for the characteristic aroma and flavor of oranges, lemons, and other citrus varieties.

Limonene has been shown to have various biological effects, including antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, and is often used as a natural remedy for digestive issues and other health concerns.

2. Hesperidin

Citrus reticulata hesperidin is a bioflavonoid compound found in the peel and pulp of citrus fruits.

It is a potent antioxidant that has been shown to have numerous health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties. Hesperidin has been studied for its potential role in reducing the risk of cardiovascular disease, improving blood vessel function, and alleviating symptoms of depression and anxiety.

Its bioavailability and safety make it a promising ingredient for dietary supplements and functional foods.

3. Nobiletin

Citrus reticulata nobiletin is a naturally occurring flavonoid compound found in the peel of citrus fruits.

It has been extensively studied for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Nobiletin has been shown to have neuroprotective effects, may help prevent cardiovascular disease, and even exhibit antimicrobial activity against certain pathogens.

4. Polymethoxyflavones (pmfs)

Citrus reticulata polymethoxyflavones (PMFs) is a group of flavonoids extracted from the peel of this citrus fruit.

PMFs have been extensively studied for their potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties.

They have also been shown to exhibit anti-cancer activity and may help to improve cardiovascular health by reducing triglyceride levels and improving lipid profiles.

5. Auraptene

Citrus reticulata auraptene is a bioactive limonoid that has been isolated from the peel of Citrus reticulata.

It exhibits various pharmacological activities, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. Auraptene has also shown potential in treating diseases such as cancer, cardiovascular disorders, and neurodegenerative diseases.

Its unique chemical structure and biological properties make it a valuable compound for further research and development.

6. Citral

Citrus reticulata citral is a naturally occurring essential oil extracted from the peel of this citrus fruit.

It has a sweet and citrusy aroma, often used in aromatherapy to uplift and rejuvenate the mind and body.

Citral is also a key component of lemon and orange oils, and is valued for its antimicrobial and antioxidant properties, making it a popular ingredient in skincare products.

7. Furocoumarins

Citrus reticulata furocoumarins is a type of coumarin, a class of natural compounds found in the peel and leaves of citrus plants.

These furocoumarins have been shown to exhibit various biological activities, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties. Additionally, they have been used as traditional Chinese medicine for centuries, particularly for their potential to treat skin diseases and cancers.

Further research is needed to fully understand the extent of their benefits and potential applications.

8. Limonoid glycosides

Citrus reticulata limonoid glycosides is a type of secondary metabolite found in its leaves and stems.

These compounds are formed by combining limonoids with sugars to create glycosides. Limonoids have been shown to possess antifeedant and insecticide properties, providing protection against pests and diseases.

The limonoid glycosides present in Citrus reticulata have been reported to exhibit antimicrobial and antioxidant activities, making them potential natural products for use in agriculture, medicine, and food preservation.

9. Hydroxycitronellal

Citrus reticulata hydroxycitronellal is a natural fragrance ingredient derived from the peel of the citrus fruit.

It possesses a sweet, floral, and slightly citrusy aroma, which is often used in perfumes, fragrances, and aromatherapy products. Hydroxycitronellal has been found to have calming and uplifting properties, making it a popular choice for stress relief and mood enhancement.

Its unique scent also adds a subtle twist to skincare products, leaving skin feeling refreshed and revitalized.

10. Geraniol

Citrus reticulata geraniol is a natural compound extracted from the essential oil of the citrus peel.

It is a sesquiterpene alcohol with a sweet, floral, and slightly minty aroma. Geraniol has been traditionally used in aromatherapy to promote relaxation, reduce anxiety and stress, and improve mood.

It also exhibits antimicrobial properties, making it effective against skin infections and wounds.
