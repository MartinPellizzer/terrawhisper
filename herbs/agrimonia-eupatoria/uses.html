St. John'S Wort (Agrimonia eupatoria) Uses

St. John's Wort is used to treat respiratory conditions such as bronchitis, cough, and asthma, as well as cold and flu symptoms. It also helps with sore throat and skin infections, supporting wound healing and boosting immune response against respiratory tract infections.

St. John'S Wort (Agrimonia eupatoria) Monograph

1. Bronchitis

St. John's Wort (Agrimonia eupatoria) is used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health. It may help reduce inflammation in the airways and ease symptoms of bronchitis by promoting expectoration and soothing irritation. Its mild antiseptic properties also aid in clearing mucus and preventing secondary infections.

2. Sore throat

St. John's Wort (Agrimonia eupatoria) has been traditionally used to soothe sore throats due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It can be prepared as a tea or gargle to reduce irritation and infection. Its mild astringent action helps ease pain and promote healing in the throat lining.

3. Influenza

St. John's Wort (Agrimonia eupatoria) may support immune function and help alleviate symptoms of influenza due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It is often used in herbal formulations to reduce fever and soothe respiratory discomfort during flu outbreaks. However, it should be used with caution and under professional guidance, especially when combined with other medications.

4. Respiratory infections

St. John's Wort (Agrimonia eupatoria) has been traditionally used to support respiratory health. It is often employed to alleviate symptoms of colds, coughs, and bronchitis due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Its expectorant effects help loosen mucus, making it easier to expel. It is typically prepared as a tea or tincture for respiratory support.

5. Cough

St. John's Wort (Agrimonia eupatoria) is used to support respiratory health, particularly for coughs. It has mild expectorant properties that help loosen mucus and soothe irritated airways. Traditionally, it is prepared as a tea or tincture to alleviate persistent or dry coughs. Its anti-inflammatory effects may reduce throat irritation and ease breathing.

6. Respiratory tract infections

St. John's Wort (Agrimonia eupatoria) has been traditionally used to support the treatment of respiratory tract infections. It possesses antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that may help alleviate symptoms such as coughing and congestion. Its use is often combined with other herbs in respiratory formulas. Always consult a qualified herbalist for proper guidance.

7. Asthma

St. John's Wort (Agrimonia eupatoria) has been traditionally used to support respiratory health. It may help reduce inflammation in the airways and ease symptoms of asthma. Its antispasmodic properties can soothe bronchial constriction, aiding in easier breathing. However, it should be used under professional guidance, especially when combined with other treatments.

8. Cold symptoms

St. John's Wort (Agrimonia eupatoria) is used traditionally to alleviate cold symptoms such as congestion and sore throat. Its anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties help reduce inflammation in the respiratory tract and may shorten the duration of colds. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture for internal use.

9. Skin infections

St. John's Wort has been traditionally used to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce bacterial growth and soothe irritated skin, making it effective for conditions like eczema and minor wounds. Its use is often combined with other herbs in topical preparations for enhanced healing.

10. Wound healing

St. John's Wort, or Agrimony, has been traditionally used to support wound healing. Its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties help reduce swelling and promote tissue repair. It is often applied topically as a poultice or infused oil to cleanse and protect wounds. Its ability to stimulate healing makes it a valued herb in herbal medicine for minor cuts and skin injuries.
