      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Cotoneaster franchetii can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, including vomiting and diarrhea, if the berries are ingested by humans or pets. The small red fruits contain cyanogenic glycosides which release toxic hydrogen cyanide when broken down in the body. While the plant is generally considered non-toxic to touch, some individuals may experience mild skin irritation or allergic contact dermatitis after handling the foliage. It is important to keep this shrub away from curious children and animals to prevent accidental poisoning. Monitoring for signs of lethargy or stomach upset is essential if exposure is suspected.
