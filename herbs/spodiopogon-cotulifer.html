Spodiopogon cotulifer

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Spodiopogon cotulifer has a growth habit characterized by a basal rosette of leaves and a slender, upright flowering stem topped with small, inconspicuous inflorescences. The leaves are typically broad and somewhat heart-shaped or lobed, resembling the foliage of a coltsfoot. Along the stem, the plant features smaller, bract-like leaves that decrease in size as they ascend toward the flower head. The reproductive structures consist of numerous tiny florets that are clustered tightly together. These morphological traits help distinguish the species within its specific ecological niche.
