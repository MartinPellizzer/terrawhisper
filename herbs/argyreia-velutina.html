Argyreia velutina

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Argyreia velutina has large, heart-shaped leaves that are characterized by a soft, velvety texture due to a dense covering of fine hairs on both the upper and lower surfaces. These leaves are typically dark green and can grow quite substantial in size, often providing lush ground cover. The plant produces clusters of pale pink to white flowers that emerge from the foliage during its blooming season. The stems are climbing or trailing in nature, allowing the plant to spread across surfaces using its tendrils. Small, woody seed pods may eventually develop following successful pollination of the blooms.

      Argyreia velutina can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and digestive distress if ingested.
