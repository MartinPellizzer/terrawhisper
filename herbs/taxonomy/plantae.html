The Plantae Kingdom

The Plantae kingdom, also known as the Plantae, is a taxonomical rank that comprises all eukaryotic, photosynthetic organisms commonly known as plants.

This kingdom includes a diverse range of plant species, from flowering plants to mosses and ferns, that are capable of photosynthesis and typically have cell walls made of cellulose.

Examples of divisions within the Plantae kingdom include Angiospermophyta (flowering plants), Pteridophyta (ferns), Marchantiophyta (liverworts), Spermatophyta (seed-producing plants), Lycopodiophyta (club mosses), Pinophyta (conifers), Chlorophyta (green algae), Bryophyta (mosses), and Ascomycota is actually a division of the fungus kingdom, not the plant kingdom.
