The Ascomycota Division

The Ascomycota division is a taxonomical rank that encompasses a group of fungi characterized by the production of ascospores, typically in a fruiting structure called an ascocarp.

This division includes a wide variety of fungi, such as molds, yeasts, and lichens.

The Ascomycota division is a significant group within the fungal kingdom, with examples of divisions including Monocotyledonae, which is actually a division of flowering plants, but Ascomycota is not the same, and examples such as Magnoliopsida and Polypodiopsida which are also divisions of flowering and fern plants respectively.

Pezizomycetes

Pezizomycetes is a class of fungi that includes ascomycetes with peziza-like ascocarps, commonly known as cup fungi or earthstars.
