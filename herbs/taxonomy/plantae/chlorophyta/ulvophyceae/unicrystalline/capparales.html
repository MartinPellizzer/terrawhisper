The Capparales Orders

The capparales rank, also known as the Brassicales order, is a taxonomic group of flowering plants that are characterized by having a distinct type of fruit, typically a silique or silicles, and often exhibit four- or five-petaled flowers.

This order includes a diverse range of species, from the common mustard family (Brassicaceae) to the capper family (Capparaceae). The capparales order also comprises families such as Orchidaceae, which are known for their unique and often showy flowers, Euphorbiaceae, which are characterized by their milky sap and diverse range of species, and Cyperaceae, a family of sedge plants.

These families, among others, are united by their shared characteristics and are classified within the capparales order.
