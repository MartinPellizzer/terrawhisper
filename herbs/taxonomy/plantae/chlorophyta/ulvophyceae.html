The Ulvophyceae Class

Ulvophyceae is a division of green algae that includes species with chlorophyll a and b and are primarily found in freshwater and marine environments.

This division is characterized by its simple thallus structure and the presence of two types of chloroplasts. Ulvophyceae is distinct from other divisions such as Commelinidae, which comprises flowering plants and their allies, and Rosidae and Asteridae, which are also groups of flowering plants.

The diverse range of species within Ulvophyceae includes genera such as Ulva, Enteromorpha, and Cladophora.
