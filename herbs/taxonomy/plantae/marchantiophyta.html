The Marchantiophyta Division

The Marchantiophyta division is a taxonomical rank that includes a group of non-vascular plants commonly known as liverworts.

This division is characterized by its simplicity in structure, often consisting of a flat leaf-like body called a thallus. Marchantiophyta includes approximately 9,000 species, which are primarily found in moist environments worldwide.

For comparison, other plant divisions include Monocotyledonae, which contains flowering plants with a single cotyledon, and Polypodiopsida, a group of ferns, while Magnoliopsida is the division that includes flowering plants with a double cotyledon.
