The Pinidae Subclass

The rank Pinidae is a subgroup or a clade within the plant kingdom, referring to a group of plants that share certain characteristics and are often closely related.

It is often used as an intermediate taxonomic rank between the order and family levels. Examples of orders within the Pinidae rank include the Asparagales, Malpighiales, and Poales, which are groups of flowering plants that exhibit distinct features and relationships.

The Pinidae rank is not universally recognized and its use may vary depending on the plant classification system or source.
