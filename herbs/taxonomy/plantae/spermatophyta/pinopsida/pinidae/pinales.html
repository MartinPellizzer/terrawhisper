The Pinales Orders

The pinales rank is an order of plants that consists of coniferous trees and shrubs, characterized by needle-like or scale-like leaves, and cone-like reproductive structures.

The pinales order includes families such as Pinaceae, Podocarpaceae, and Taxaceae. However, other examples of families that belong to the pinales order include Taxodiaceae, Araucariaceae, and Cephalotaxaceae, but not orchidaceae, euphorbiaceae, cyperaceae which are part of other orders: asparagales, sapindales and poales respectively.

The pinales order is a distinct group within the division Pinophyta, and is often considered to be one of the oldest groups of land plants.
