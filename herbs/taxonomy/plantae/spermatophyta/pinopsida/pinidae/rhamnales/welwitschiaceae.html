The Welwitschiaceae Family

The Welwitschiaceae rank is a family of plants.

It is a taxonomical rank that groups together related genera of flowering plants. The Welwitschiaceae family is primarily composed of conifer-like plants, often referred to as "living fossils" due to their unique characteristics and ability to thrive in specific environments.

The Welwitschiaceae family is relatively small, consisting of a limited number of species, but its members are of significant interest to botanists and scientists due to their evolutionary significance.
