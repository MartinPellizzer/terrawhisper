The Pinopsida Class

The rank Pinopsida refers to a division of coniferous plants that includes species such as pine, spruce, and fir trees, characterized by needle-like leaves and cone-like reproductive structures.

Pinopsida plants are typically evergreen, meaning they retain their leaves year-round, and often have adapted to survive in harsh, temperate environments. This division is distinct from others such as Commelinidae, which includes plants like bamboo and palms, and Rosidae, which encompasses a wide range of flowering plants including roses and apples.

In contrast, Asteridae is a division that includes plants like sunflowers and daisies, all of which are characterized by their showy, flower-like reproductive structures.
