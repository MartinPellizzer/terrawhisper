The Psilotaceae Family

The Psilotaceae rank is a family of vascular plants, commonly known as the whisk ferns.

This family consists of two genera: Psilotum and Tmesipteris, which are characterized by their unique, fern-like but non-vascular leaves. The Psilotaceae family is primarily found in tropical and subtropical regions around the world, and its members are often epiphytic or terrestrial plants.

The plants within this family are known for their ability to thrive in a variety of environments, from shaded forests to rocky outcrops.
