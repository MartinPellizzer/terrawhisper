The Gleicheniales Orders

The Gleicheniales is an order of plants within the division Pteridophyta, comprising ferns that are typically epiphytic or terrestrial and have free-veined leaves.

This order is characterized by its unique fern species that exhibit distinctive growth habits and leaf structures. Some examples of families within the Gleicheniales include the Dipteridaceae, Matoniaceae, and the Gleicheniaceae, however the ones you provided, orchidaceae, euphorbiaceae, cyperaceae, are not part of this group.

These families often display diverse morphologies, including variations in leaf venation and sporangial distribution.

Gleicheniaceae

Gleicheniaceae is a family of ferns belonging to the order Gleicheniales, characterized by simple or forked rhizomes and usually leathery or chartaceous leaves.
