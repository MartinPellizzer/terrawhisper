The Polypodiales Orders

The Polypodiales rank is an order of ferns, a group of vascular plants characterized by their vascular tissue being surrounded by a thick layer of cells called the "ground tissue" and having an unbranched stem with a central vascular bundle.

This order comprises approximately 8,500 species, including many commonly known ferns such as the Polypodium and Blechnum genera. The Polypodiales order includes various families, such as the Dryopteridaceae, Aspleniaceae, and Onocleaceae, in addition to some families not traditionally classified here like the Thelypteridaceae, but also others such as: the orchidaceae which is known for their flowers and are one of the most diverse plant families in the world, the euphorbiaceae which includes the poinsettia and the rubber tree, and the cyperaceae which comprises plants known for their sedge-like appearance.

The Polypodiales order is a significant and ecologically diverse group of plants, playing vital roles in many different ecosystems.
