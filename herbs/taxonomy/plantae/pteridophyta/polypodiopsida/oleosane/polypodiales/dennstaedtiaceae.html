The Dennstaedtiaceae Family

The Dennstaedtiaceae is a family of plants that consists of approximately 1,300 species of ferns.

This family is characterized by its members having simple, often lobed or pinnate leaves and a variety of growth habits, including terrestrial, epiphytic, and epilithic forms. Dennstaedtiaceae ferns can be found in a wide range of environments, from tropical forests to arctic tundras, and are commonly known for their ability to thrive in shaded, humid conditions.

The Dennstaedtiaceae family is one of the largest and most diverse groups of ferns, with species found on every continent except Antarctica.
