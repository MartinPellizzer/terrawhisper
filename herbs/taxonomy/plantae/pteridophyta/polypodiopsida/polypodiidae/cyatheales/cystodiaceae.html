The Cystodiaceae Family

Cystodiaceae is a family rank in the taxonomy of plants, specifically referring to a group of liverworts that belong to the order Marchantiophyta.

This family is characterized by a unique combination of morphological and anatomical features, including the presence of perianth and sporophyte production. Cystodiaceae comprises several genera of liverworts, which are generally found in wet environments such as bogs, marshes, and forests.

The family Cystodiaceae is notable for its diversity and widespread distribution across the world, with species found in various continents and habitats.
