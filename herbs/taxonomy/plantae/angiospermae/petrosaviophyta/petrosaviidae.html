The Petrosaviidae Subclass

Petrosaviidae is a clade, a taxonomic rank that groups together a set of plant families that share a common ancestor.

It is considered a distinct group within the plant kingdom, consisting of two plant families: Petrosaviaceae and Blandfordiaceae. The orders within the petrosaviidae clade include the Asparagales, a diverse group of plants that includes asparagus and orchids, the Malpighiales, a group of plants that includes mangroves and coffee, and the Poales, a group of plants that includes grasses and bamboo.

These orders, along with others, are thought to have evolved within the petrosaviidae clade, highlighting its importance in plant phylogeny.
