The Santalales Orders

The rank santalales is an order of flowering plants, consisting of families with distinctive leaf and stem characteristics, often featuring aromatic foliage.

This order includes families such as Santalaceae, which is the type family, as well as Olacaceae and Loranthaceae. Santalales also encompasses the family Grubbiaceae, which is part of a distinct group of flowering plants known as the Santalales.

Other examples of families within the order santalales are the Thymelaeaceae and the Icacinaceae and, of course, the aforementioned Santalaceae.

Ericaceae is a family of flowering plants commonly known as heath or heather plants, which includes over 4,000 species of shrubs and small trees found in heathlands, moors, and rocky areas worldwide.
