The Caryophyllales Orders

The caryophyllales rank is an order of flowering plants, characterized by the presence of a specific type of floral structure known as a perigynous ovary, where the ovary is positioned above a ring of tissue called the receptacle.

This order is one of the largest and most diverse in the plant kingdom, comprising over 33,000 species. Examples of families within the caryophyllales order include the Orchidaceae, which includes orchids, and the Euphorbiaceae, which includes spurge and poinsettia.

Another example of a family within this order is the Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and rushes, but the family cyperaceae does not belong to caryophyllales.
