The Ranunculales Orders

The rank Ranunculales is an order of flowering plants that consists of approximately 210 genera and 4,000 species, characterized by their often showy flowers and varying growth habits.

This order encompasses a diverse range of families, including the Ranunculaceae family, which comprises buttercups and their relatives. Other notable families within the Ranunculales order include the Berberidaceae family, which includes barberries and Oregon grape, and the Menispermaceae family, which includes the moonseed vine.

Notable exceptions to the Ranunculales order include the Orchidaceae family, the Euphorbiaceae family, and the Cyperaceae family, which are found in different orders, such as Asparagales, Malpighiales, and Poales, respectively.
