The Magnolales Orders

The rank Magnolales is a taxonomic order of flowering plants, characterized by the presence of distinct sepals and usually three or more petals, often with showy, fragrant flowers.

This order includes a diverse range of plant families, such as the orchid family (Orchidaceae) with over 30,000 species, the spurge family (Euphorbiaceae) with its unique latex-producing stems, and the sedge family (Cyperaceae) with its distinctive, grass-like vegetation. Many members of the Magnolales order are known for their striking, often fragrant flowers, which attract a wide variety of pollinators.

With over 1,000 species spread across these and other families, the Magnolales order is a significant component of many ecosystems around the world.
