The Paracryphiaceae Family

The Paracryphiaceae is a family of flowering plants, specifically a group of species commonly known as the 'Kauri family'.

It is a family of plants native to the subtropical and tropical regions of the Pacific Islands, primarily found in New Zealand, Australia, and Southeast Asia. The Paracryphiaceae family consists of 5-7 genera, including Paracryphia, Laurelia, and Daphnandra.

These plants are known for their distinctive foliage and resinous bark, with many species valued for their timber and ornamental value.
