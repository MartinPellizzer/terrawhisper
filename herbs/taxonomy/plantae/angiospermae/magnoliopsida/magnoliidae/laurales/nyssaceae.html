The Nyssaceae Family

The taxonomical rank of Nyssaceae is a family of plants.

It is a group of flowering plants commonly known as the tupelo or blackgum family, characterized by having four or five sepals and four or five petals in their flowers. The Nyssaceae family is primarily found in eastern North America and is composed of about 10 to 15 species.

They are often deciduous trees or shrubs that thrive in wet environments, such as swamps and riverbanks.
