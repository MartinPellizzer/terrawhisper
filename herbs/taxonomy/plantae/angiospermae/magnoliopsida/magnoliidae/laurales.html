The Laurales Orders

The rank Laurales is an order of flowering plants, characterized by their complex, often aromatic flowers and typically woody growth habit.

This order includes a diverse range of plant families, such as the Lauraceae, which is the largest family in the order, consisting of laurels and cinnamon trees. Other notable families within the Laurales order are the Chloranthaceae, but also the other families you have mentioned like the Orchidaceae, Euphorbiaceae and Cyperaceae are not part of the Laurales order.

The Laurales order is notable for its unique combination of characteristics, such as the presence of aromatic oils and the often-leathery leaves of its members.
