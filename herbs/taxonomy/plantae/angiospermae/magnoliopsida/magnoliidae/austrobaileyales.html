The Austrobaileyales Orders

The austrobaileyales is an order of flowering plants, characterized by having a unique combination of morphological and molecular characteristics.

This order consists of approximately 120 families, including the Orchidaceae, Euphorbiaceae, and Cyperaceae, among others. The austrobaileyales order is generally considered to be one of the most ancient lineages of flowering plants, with fossil records dating back to the Early Cretaceous period.

As a result, the austrobaileyales order plays an important role in understanding the evolution and diversification of flowering plants.
