The Rosidae Subclass

The rank Rosidae, also known as Rosids, is a clade of flowering plants that share a common ancestor and distinct characteristics.

It is one of the two main groups of flowering plants, the other being the Asteridae. Examples of orders within the Rosidae clade include Asparagales, Malpighiales, and Poales, which encompass a diverse range of plants such as orchids, willows, and grasses.

The Rosidae clade is characterized by the presence of a specific type of fruit, known as an aggregate fruit, and a variety of growth habits.
