The Asteridae Subclass

The asteridae rank refers to a clade of flowering plants that includes over 80,000 species, characterized by the presence of a distinct type of flower morphology and reproductive system.

This clade is a large and diverse group, encompassing a wide range of plant families. Examples of orders within the asteridae clade include Asparagales, such as the asparagus and lilies; Malpighiales, a group including plants like willows and mulberries; and Poales, which includes grasses, sedges, and rushes.

The asteridae clade is a significant and complex part of the plant kingdom, with many different families and orders working together to create a diverse array of ecosystems.
