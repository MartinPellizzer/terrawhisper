The Urticales Orders

The rank urticales is an order of flowering plants that includes nettle-like species with stinging hairs.

This order is characterized by its unique combination of characteristics, such as the presence of stinging trichomes and the typical arrangement of flowers in catkins. The urticales order includes several families, including the Urticaceae family, which is the most representative of this group, as well as the Celastraceae and Cornaceae families.

Other notable families within the urticales order include the Euphorbiaceae, Orchidaceae, and Cyperaceae, which are found in various habitats around the world.
