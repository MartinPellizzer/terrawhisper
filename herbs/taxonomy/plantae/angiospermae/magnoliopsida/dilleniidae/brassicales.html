The Brassicales Orders

The taxonomic rank of Brassicales is an order of flowering plants, characterized by having four- or five-merous flowers and seeds that are often winged or ribbed.

This order includes a diverse group of plants, such as the mustard family (Brassicaceae), which is one of its most prominent members. Other notable families within the Brassicales order include the Capparaceae, Tovariaceae, and Akaniaceae, as well as the aforementioned Brassicaceae, but examples that aren't part of the Brassicales order are the orchid family (Orchidaceae), the spurge family (Euphorbiaceae), and the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

The Brassicales order is found in temperate and tropical regions worldwide and includes around 1,500 to 2,000 species.
