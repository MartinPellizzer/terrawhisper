The Myrtales Orders

The rank myrtales refers to a taxonomic order of flowering plants, characterized by the presence of showy flowers and often, fragrant scents.

It is a diverse order that comprises around 16,000 species, spanning over 160 families. Myrtales includes families such as the Myrtaceae, which includes eucalyptus and myrtle, as well as the Thymelaeaceae, the Lythraceae, and the Oliniaceae.

Other examples of families within the myrtales order include the Onagraceae, the Penaeaceae, and the Melastomataceae, as well as the Clusiaceae, the Melianthaceae, the Penaeaceae, and the Rhizophoraceae.
