The Theales Orders

The Theales rank is an order of flowering plants that includes a diverse group of species, primarily found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This order is characterized by a variety of growth forms, including trees, shrubs, and herbs, and is often used as a diagnostic feature in plant identification. Some examples of families within the Theales order include the Orchidaceae, which comprises over 30,000 species of orchids, the Euphorbiaceae, which includes the poinsettia and the rubber tree, and the Cyperaceae, which is a large family of sedge plants.

The Theales order is a key component of the plant kingdom, and its diverse members play important roles in ecosystems around the world.
