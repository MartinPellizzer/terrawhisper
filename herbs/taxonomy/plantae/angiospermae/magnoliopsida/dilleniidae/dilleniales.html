The Dilleniales Orders

The Dilleniales rank is an order of flowering plants, characterized by having a distinct type of flower arrangement and often featuring showy, five-petaled flowers.

This order includes a variety of plant families, such as the Dilleniaceae family, which contains species like Tetracera and Hibbertia. However, it's worth noting that the Dilleniales order is not as well-represented as other orders, and some sources may group its families under other orders.

Examples of families sometimes placed within the Dilleniales order include the Cercidiphyllaceae, Hoplestigmataceae, and Paracryphiaceae families are sometimes replaced with other families like the orchidaceae (which contains species like Phalaenopsis and Dendrobium), the euphorbiaceae (which contains species like Euphorbia and Ricinus), and the cyperaceae (which contains species like Carex and Cyperus).
