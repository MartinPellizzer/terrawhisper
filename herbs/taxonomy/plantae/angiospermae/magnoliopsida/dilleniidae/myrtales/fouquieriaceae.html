The Fouquieriaceae Family

Fouquieriaceae is a family of flowering plants that consists of approximately 15 species of trees and shrubs, primarily found in the deserts of the Americas.

This family is characterized by its unique adaptations to arid environments, including small leaves and waxy stems to conserve water. The name Fouquieriaceae comes from the genus Fouquieria, which refers to a specific group of plants within this family.

The Fouquieriaceae family is also commonly known as the ocotillo or boojum tree family due to the presence of these distinctive plant species within it.
