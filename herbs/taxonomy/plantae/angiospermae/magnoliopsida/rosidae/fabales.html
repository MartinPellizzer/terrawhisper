The Fabales Orders

The fabales rank is an order of flowering plants, specifically a clade of legumes that includes a diverse group of plants.

It is characterized by having flowers with a distinctive structure and often producing seeds in pods. Some examples of families within the fabales order include Fabaceae, which includes beans and peas, but also the families Orchidaceae (orchids), Euphorbiaceae (spurges and poinsettias), and Cyperaceae (sedges and rushes), although these latter three are not actually part of the legume order.

The fabales order is a distinct and well-defined group within the plant kingdom, containing a wide range of species that have adapted to various environments.
