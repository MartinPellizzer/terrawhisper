The Rosales Orders

The rosales rank is an order of flowering plants, characterized by a wide range of growth habits and flower structures, and is often associated with fruiting structures such as pomes and aggregates.

This order includes a diverse group of plant families, including the Orchidaceae, which comprises orchids, the Euphorbiaceae, which comprises spurge and euphorbia plants, and the Cyperaceae, which comprises sedges and rushes. Other examples of families within the rosales order include Rosaceae, Fabaceae, and Vitaceae.

The diversity of plant forms and growth habits within the rosales order reflects its broad range of adaptations to different environments.
