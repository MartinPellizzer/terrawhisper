The Sapindales Orders

The Sapindales order is a taxonomical rank that consists of a group of flowering plants that are typically characterized by having a distinct arrangement of flowers and fruits.

This order includes a diverse range of plant families, such as the Anacardiaceae, Rutaceae, and Sapindaceae families, which are commonly known for producing fruits like mangoes, oranges, and lychees. Other notable families within the Sapindales order include the Simaroubaceae, Meliaceae, and Burseraceae, which are known for their unique characteristics and adaptations.

Additionally, some families like Orchidaceae, Euphorbiaceae, and Cyperaceae are not part of the Sapindales order, but are actually found in different taxonomic groups such as the Asparagales, Malpighiales, and Poales orders respectively.
