The Malpighiales Orders

The Malpighiales rank is an order of flowering plants that includes a diverse array of species, characterized by their complex flowers and varied growth habits.

This order encompasses over 16,000 species, spread across more than 1,000 genera, making it one of the most species-rich groups of flowering plants. Malpighiales includes families such as the Orchidaceae, known for their unique and often showy flowers, the Euphorbiaceae, which includes species like the poinsettia and the rubber tree, and the Clusiaceae, which is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The Malpighiales order is a significant component of many ecosystems, providing essential food and shelter for a wide range of animals.
