The Carpofyllales Orders

The rank Carpofoyllales is an order of flowering plants, defined by the presence of carpels, which are the female reproductive organs, and the production of nectar, typically in the form of flowers.

The Carpofoyllales order contains a diverse range of plant families, including the Orchidaceae family, commonly known as orchids, which comprises over 30,000 species. The Euphorbiaceae family, known for its diverse range of plants such as the poinsettia, also belongs to this order.

Other examples of families within the Carpofoyllales order include the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and rushes.

Elatinaceae

Elatinaceae is a family of small, herbaceous plants, commonly known as waterworts, that are native to Northern Hemisphere wetlands.
