The Oxalidales Orders

The rank of Oxalidales is a botanical order, which is a taxonomic rank used to group plants with shared characteristics and evolutionary relationships.

It is a level of classification that groups related families together. Oxalidales includes several plant families, such as Orchidaceae, which comprises orchids, and Euphorbiaceae, which includes spurge and poinsettia.

Other examples of families within the Oxalidales order include the Cunoniaceae, Oxalidaceae and the Staphylaceae, and the family Cyperaceae, which comprises sedge and rushes.
