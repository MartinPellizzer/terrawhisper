The Penthoraceae Family

The Penthoraceae rank is a family of flowering plants, consisting of a group of closely related genera that share common characteristics.

This family includes a limited number of species, primarily found in North America, and is characterized by their unique flower and leaf morphology. The Penthoraceae family is often referred to as the "beak-rush" or "hog-penwort" family, due to the distinctive shape of their flowers and leaves.

These plants are relatively rare and can be found in wetlands and moist areas.
