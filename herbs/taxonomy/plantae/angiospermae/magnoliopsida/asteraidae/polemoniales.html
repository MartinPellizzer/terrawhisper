The Polemoniales Orders

The taxonomical rank of Polemoniales is an order of flowering plants, characterized by a specific set of morphological and reproductive features.

It is a distinct grouping that comprises families with similar characteristics, such as flowers with distinct features and a specific arrangement of reproductive organs. Families within the Polemoniales order include Polemoniaceae, the family that gives it its name, as well as other plant families such as Orchidaceae, which includes orchids, and Euphorbiaceae, the spurge family, which comprises plants like the poinsettia.

Another example of a family within the Polemoniales order is Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and rushes.
