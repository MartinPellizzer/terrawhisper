The Dipsacales Orders

The taxonomical rank of Dipsacales is an order of flowering plants, characterized by having four- or five-parted flowers, often with a whorled arrangement of leaves and a characteristic shape of the calyx.

This order includes a diverse range of plant families, such as Adoxaceae, Caprifoliaceae, and Valerianaceae. Some notable examples of families that belong to the Dipsacales order include Dipsacaceae, which includes teasel and teasel-like plants, and Valerianaceae, which includes the fragrant valerian and valerian-like plants.

Notably, not all plant families associated with Dipsacales are part of this order, for example the Orchidaceae (orchids), Euphorbiaceae (spurge family), and Cyperaceae (sedge family) are all part of different orders.
