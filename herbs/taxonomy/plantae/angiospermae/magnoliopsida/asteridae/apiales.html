The Apiales Orders

The rank Apiales is an order of flowering plants, which is a division of plants based on their evolutionary relationships and shared characteristics.

This order is characterized by plants that typically have five-petaled flowers, often with a distinctive arrangement of the stamens and carpels. Apiales includes a diverse range of families, such as the carrot family Apiaceae, the Pittosporaceae and Myodocarpaceae families, and others.

Notable families within this order include Apiaceae, Araliaceae, and Pittosporaceae, and some notable exceptions like the following families not belonging to this order: orchidaceae, euphorbiaceae, and cyperaceae.

Apiaceae

Apiaceae is a family of flowering plants commonly known as the carrot or parsley family, characterized by their umbrella-like inflorescences and often pungent or aromatic foliage.
