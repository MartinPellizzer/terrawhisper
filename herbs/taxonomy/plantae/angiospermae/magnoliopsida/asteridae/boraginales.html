The Boraginales Orders

The Boraginales rank, also known as the Boraginales order, is a taxonomic division of flowering plants characterized by its diverse range of species and distinctive floral features.

This order includes approximately 2,000 species of plants, with examples of families such as Boraginaceae, representing the borage and forget-me-not families. Other notable families within this order include Ehretiaceae and Heliotropiaceae.

The Boraginales order is also home to the Lamiaceae family, commonly known as mint and basil, and the Hydrophyllaceae family, which includes the waterleaf and the brooklines.
