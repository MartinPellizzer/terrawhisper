The Gentianales Orders

The rank gentianales is an order of flowering plants that includes families with diverse characteristics, such as tubular flowers and often woody stems.

This order comprises approximately 1,145 genera and 17,000 species, with examples including the orchid family (Orchidaceae), the spurge family (Euphorbiaceae), and the sedge family (Cyperaceae). The gentianales order is primarily found in tropical and subtropical regions, but some families have a more widespread distribution.

The unique characteristics and adaptations of gentianales plants have allowed them to thrive in a variety of environments, from wetlands to dry forests.
