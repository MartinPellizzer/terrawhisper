The Asterales Orders

The rank asterales is a taxonomic order that consists of flowering plants, specifically those that are part of the larger clade known as the asterids.

This order encompasses a diverse group of plants, including the asteraceae family, which is commonly known as the daisy or sunflower family. In contrast, other families such as orchidaceae (orchids), euphorbiaceae (spurges), and cyperaceae (sedges) belong to different orders and are not part of the asterales.

The plants within the asterales order often display distinct features such as composite flowers, where multiple tiny flowers are clustered together.
