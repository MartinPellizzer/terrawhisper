The Lamiales Orders

The Lamiales rank is an order of flowering plants, commonly referred to as the lip flower order, which comprises approximately 24,000 species.

This order is characterized by flowers with a distinct lower lip, often zygomorphic in shape, and is found in a wide range of habitats worldwide. The Lamiales order includes several notable families such as Orchidaceae, which contains over 30,000 species of orchids, and Euphorbiaceae, the spurge family.

Additionally, the Lamiales order also encompasses the family Cyperaceae, commonly known as the sedge family, which comprises over 5,000 species.
