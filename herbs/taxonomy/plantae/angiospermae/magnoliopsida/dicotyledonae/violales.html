The Violales Orders

The taxonomical rank violales, also known as the order of violets, is a division of flowering plants that contains approximately 31 families.

Some examples of families within the violales order include the Orchidaceae, which comprises orchids, the Euphorbiaceae, which includes spurge and poinsettia, and the Celastraceae is not mentioned but the Cyperaceae, which consists of sedge plants. The violales order is characterized by plants with five-merous flowers and a distinctive arrangement of their reproductive parts.

This order is found in both temperate and tropical regions around the world, with many species adapted to a variety of habitats.
