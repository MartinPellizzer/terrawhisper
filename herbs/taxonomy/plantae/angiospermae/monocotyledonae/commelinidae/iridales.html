The Iridales Orders

The rank iridales is a taxonomical order of flowering plants, comprising a group of plants that share similar characteristics and evolutionary relationships.

It contains several families of plants, including the Orchidaceae, known for its diverse and widespread orchids, the Euphorbiaceae, a family of plants that includes spurge and poinsettia, and the Cyperaceae, a family of sedge and rush plants. The iridales order is primarily defined by the presence of three stamens that are often fused together, forming a single structure.

This order is often used in the classification of monocotyledonous plants, which have a single seed leaf.

Iridaceae

Iridaceae is a family of flowering plants commonly known as the iris family, characterized by showy flowers with three petals and three sepals.
