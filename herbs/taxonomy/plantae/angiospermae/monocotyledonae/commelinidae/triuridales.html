The Triuridales Orders

The Triuridales is an order of flowering plants characterized by the presence of small, usually underground flowers and a unique pollination mechanism involving fungi.

This order includes families such as Triuridaceae, which is the only family in the order. Other examples of families that are not part of the Triuridales include the Orchidaceae, known for their showy flowers and diverse species, the Euphorbiaceae with their diverse range of succulent plants, and the Cyperaceae, which comprises the sedge family.

The Triuridales order is a relatively small group of plants, but its unique characteristics make it an interesting area of study in botany.

Triuridaceae

Triuridaceae is a family of flowering plants, commonly known as the bootlace vines or triuridaceae, characterized by their unique, small, and often epiphytic habit.
