The Nymphaeidae Subclass

The rank Nymphaeidae is a superfamily of aquatic plants, characterized by its unique water lily-like flowers and submerged or emergent leaves.

It is a distinct grouping within the plant kingdom that encompasses a variety of species adapted to different aquatic environments. The Nymphaeidae superfamily includes several orders, such as Nymphales, which comprises the water lilies, and Hydatellales, a smaller order of aquatic plants.

Other examples of orders within the Nymphaeidae superfamily include the cabombales order, as well as the more diverse orders such as asparagales, malpighiales, and poales.

Nymphaeales

Nymphaeales are an order of flowering plants that belong to the clade of magnoliids, which is a group within the broader class of angiosperms.
