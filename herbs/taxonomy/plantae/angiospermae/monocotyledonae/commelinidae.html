The Commelinidae Subclass

The commelinidae rank is a clade or a group of plants that includes various families of flowering plants, commonly known as the Commelinids.

This group is characterized by the presence of a distinct type of floral anatomy and is often considered a more advanced group within the monocots.

The Commelinidae rank includes a diverse range of plants, such as the grasses and sedges in the order Poales, the water lilies and cattails in the order Alismatales, and the asparagus and orchids in the order Asparagales, as well as the willow and mangrove families in the order Malpighiales is not part of this group but is an example of a group of plants that is not part of Commelinidae.
