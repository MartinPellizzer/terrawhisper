The Cabombaceae Family

The cabombaceae rank is a family of flowering plants, specifically a group of aquatic plants commonly found in shallow, freshwater environments.

This family is characterized by its unique water lily-like appearance and the presence of submerged and emergent leaves. Cabombaceae is a relatively small family, consisting of a limited number of genera, primarily cabomba and shawia.

The plants within this family are known for their ability to thrive in a variety of aquatic habitats, including ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams.
