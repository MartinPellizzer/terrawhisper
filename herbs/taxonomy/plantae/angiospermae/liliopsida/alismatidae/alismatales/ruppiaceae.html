The Ruppiaceae Family

The Ruppiaceae rank is a family of aquatic plants, specifically belonging to the division Pteridophyta or Spermatophyta, but more commonly being found in the division Pteridophyta.

Ruppiaceae includes species of aquatic plants that are typically found in shallow, brackish water environments and are often characterized by their ability to thrive in conditions with high water movement. This family is primarily composed of rye-grass-like plants, with the majority of species being found in Europe and Asia.

Ruppiaceae plants are often used as indicators of water quality and can provide valuable insights into the ecological health of aquatic ecosystems.
