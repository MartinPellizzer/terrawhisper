The Alismatales Orders

The taxonomical rank alismatales is an order of flowering plants, specifically aquatic or semi-aquatic species, characterized by their submerged or emergent leaves and often, showy flowers.

This order includes a diverse group of plants, such as water lilies and bur reeds. Examples of families within the alismatales order include the Alismataceae family, which includes water plantains, and the Hydrocharitaceae family, which includes water wisterias.

Other examples include the Aponogetonaceae and the Posidoniaceae families.
