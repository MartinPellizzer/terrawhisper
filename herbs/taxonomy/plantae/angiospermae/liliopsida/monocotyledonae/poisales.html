The Poisales Orders

The Poales rank is a taxonomic order of monocotyledonous flowering plants, characterized by typically long stems, narrow leaves, and a three-angled flower structure.

This order includes a diverse range of plants, such as grasses, sedges, and bromeliads. The Poales order comprises numerous families, including Orchidaceae, which contains orchids, Euphorbiaceae, which contains spurge and poinsettia, and Cyperaceae, which contains sedges.

These families showcase the adaptability and variety found within the Poales order.

Smilacaceae

Smilacaceae is a family of flowering plants commonly known as the sarsaparilla or greenbrier family, characterized by their woody stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers.
