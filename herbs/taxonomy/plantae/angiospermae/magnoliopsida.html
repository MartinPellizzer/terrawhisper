The Magnoliopsida Class

The taxonomical rank of magnoliopsida refers to a subclass of flowering plants that contain about 64,000 species, making it one of the largest and most diverse groups of plants.

It includes a wide range of plant families such as roses, sunflowers, and daisies. The magnoliopsida subclass is further divided into several smaller divisions, including the Commelinidae, which comprises plants like the spiderworts and dayflowers, the Rosidae, which includes plants like roses and apples, and the Asteridae, which includes plants like sunflowers and daisies.

The plants classified under magnoliopsida are typically characterized by having two cotyledons, or seed leaves, during germination.
