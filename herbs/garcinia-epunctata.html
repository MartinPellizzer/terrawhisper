Garcinia epunctata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Garcinia epunctata has woody, evergreen branches characterized by simple, entire leaves that are typically elliptic to oblong in shape with smooth margins. The foliage is often leathery in texture and possesses a glossy, dark green upper surface. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves, displaying a structure typical of the Clusiaceae family. The plant produces fleshy, somewhat globose fruits that transition in color as they reach maturity. These morphological traits collectively distinguish the species within its tropical forest habitat.
