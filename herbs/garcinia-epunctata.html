What's the taxonomical classification of Garcinia epunctata?

Garcinia epunctata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic progression continues through the order Malpighiales and the family Clusiaceae, eventually reaching its specific identification within the genus Garcinia. Finally, the plant is distinguished as the species epunctata.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds epunctanone and taxifolin 6-C-glucoside. These specific chemical constituents contribute to the plant's diverse biological activities and therapeutic potential. Epunctanone is known for its unique role within the plant's chemical profile. Meanwhile, taxifolin 6-C-glucoside is a flavonoid derivative that often provides antioxidant properties. Researchers study these substances to better understand how they interact with human physiological systems.

As discussed in a study, Garcinia epunctata contains the phytochemical epunctanone, which is identified as compound 5. This compound displayed cytotoxic effects across 9 studied cancer cell lines with IC50 values below 70 µM. Specifically, epunctanone showed IC50 values ranging from 4.84 µM in HCT116 (p53-/-) cells to 13.12 µM in HepG2 cells. The study found that compound 5 induced apoptosis in CCRF-CEM cells by increasing ROS production and altering MMP. Furthermore, ferroptosis was identified as another mode of cell death induced by epunctanone.
