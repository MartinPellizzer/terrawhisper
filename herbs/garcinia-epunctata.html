Garcinia epunctata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic progression continues through the order Malpighiales and the family Clusiaceae, eventually reaching its specific identification within the genus Garcinia. Finally, the plant is distinguished as the species epunctata.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains various medicinal compounds including epunctanone and taxifolin 6-C-glucoside. These specific bioactive substances are part of a complex profile of phytochemicals found within the species. The presence of these molecules contributes to the traditional therapeutic uses documented for the plant. Researchers study these individual constituents to better understand their potential pharmacological activities. Such chemical analysis helps identify the specific drivers behind the plant's biological effects.

According to a study published by "Phytomedicine : international journal of phytotherapy and phytopharmacology", the medicinal plant Garcinia epunctata contains the phytochemical epunctanone, which is identified as compound 5. This compound displayed cytotoxic effects in 9 studied cancer cell lines with IC50 values ranging from 4.84 µM in HCT116 (p53-/-) cells to 13.12 µM in HepG2 cells. Epunctanone induced apoptosis in CCRF-CEM cells through the alteration of MMP and an increase in ROS production. Additionally, ferroptosis was identified as another mode of cell death induced by this compound. These phytochemicals are considered valuable for combating MDR cancer cells.
