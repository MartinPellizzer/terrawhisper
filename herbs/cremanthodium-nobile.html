Cremanthodium nobile

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cremanthodium nobile has an erect, perennial herb morphology characterized by slender, branched stems and oppositely arranged, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The leaves are typically thin and membranous, often featuring distinct venation patterns that extend to the leaf edges. Its inflorescence consists of small, tubular flowers arranged in terminal heads or clusters. These flowers are generally white or pale in color and emerge from the upper nodes of the stems. The root system consists of a shallow rhizomatous structure that aids in its vegetative spread.
