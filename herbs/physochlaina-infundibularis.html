What's the taxonomical classification of Physochlaina infundibularis?

Physochlaina infundibularis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further categorized under the order Solanales. It falls within the family Solanaceae, specifically belonging to the genus Physochlaina, with its unique identity defined by its species name, infundibularis.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Physochlaina infundibularis targets inflammatory signaling proteins such as cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2), inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS), and various pro-inflammatory cytokines like tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-alpha) and interleukin-6 (IL-6). The plant's bioactive compounds, particularly its diverse polyphenols and flavonoids, inhibit the activation of nuclear factor kappa B (NF-κB), which is a central regulator of the immune response. By modulating these protein pathways, the plant exerts significant anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects within cellular environments. These interactions help reduce the production of reactive oxygen species and mitigate oxidative stress at the molecular level. Such targeted inhibition of enzymatic and transcriptional proteins provides the mechanistic basis for its traditional medicinal applications in treating inflammation.
