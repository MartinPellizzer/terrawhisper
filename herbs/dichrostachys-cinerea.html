Dichrostachys cinerea

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dichrostachys cinerea has pinnate leaves consisting of numerous small, oblong leaflets arranged along a central axis and small, woody, bipinnate flowers that produce long, silky, yellowish-brown stamens. The plant typically grows as a spreading shrub or small tree with dark, fissured bark. Its leaves are arranged alternately on the stems and provide a dense, feathery texture to the canopy. The characteristic flowers are arranged in globular heads that appear at the ends of branches. After flowering, the plant develops distinctive, flattened, woody seed pods that are often curved or twisted in shape.

    What are the herbal preparations of Dichrostachys cinerea?

    Dichrostachys cinerea is prepared as a decoction from the bark, a leaf infusion, or a topical paste derived from the crushed leaves. The bark is frequently boiled in water to create a medicinal liquid used to treat various internal ailments. Leaf extracts are often steeped to produce teas that serve as traditional remedies for digestive or inflammatory issues.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Dichrostachys cinerea can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and potential gastrointestinal distress if ingested in significant quantities. The plant contains various secondary metabolites that may trigger localized inflammatory responses upon direct contact with the skin. Individuals with sensitive constitutions might experience redness, itching, or swelling after handling the foliage or bark. While specific toxicological studies are limited, traditional use suggests caution regarding its internal consumption due to unpredictable systemic reactions. Proper protective equipment should be used when working with this species to mitigate these risks.
