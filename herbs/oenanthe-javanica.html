Oenanthe javanica

What's the taxonomical classification of Oenanthe javanica?

Oenanthe javanica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Apiales and places it within the family Apiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by the genus Oenanthe and is specifically designated as the species javanica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Oenanthe javanica has an erect, herbaceous stem that typically grows between 20 and 60 centimeters tall and features a branched structure with lanceolate to linear leaves. The leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem and possess entire margins with acute tips. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils, displaying a delicate pink to purple coloration. These blossoms are characterized by a long calyx and a distinctively shaped corolla that is typical of the Onagraceae family. The plant also develops a slender, creeping rhizome that facilitates its growth in moist or wetland environments.
