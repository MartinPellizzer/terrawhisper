Oenanthe javanica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Oenanthe javanica has an erect, herbaceous stem that typically grows between 20 and 60 centimeters tall and features a branched structure with lanceolate to linear leaves. The leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem and possess entire margins with acute tips. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils, displaying a delicate pink to purple coloration. These blossoms are characterized by a long calyx and a distinctively shaped corolla that is typical of the Onagraceae family. The plant also develops a slender, creeping rhizome that facilitates its growth in moist or wetland environments.

      What are the herbal preparations of Oenanthe javanica?

      Oenanthe javanica is prepared as an aqueous decoction or an infusion using the dried aerial parts of the plant to extract its bioactive compounds. These liquid preparations are the primary methods used to harness the botanical's properties in traditional medicinal practices.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Oenanthe javanica can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, including intense abdominal pain, vomiting, and diarrhea, if ingested. This plant contains toxic compounds that act as potent irritants to the digestive tract. Beyond stomach issues, consumption may lead to more systemic neurological symptoms or significant dehydration due to fluid loss. Because of these dangerous effects, the plant is considered highly toxic and should never be consumed. Careful identification is necessary to avoid accidental poisoning from this species.
