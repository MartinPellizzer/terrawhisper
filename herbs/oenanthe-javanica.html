Oenanthe javanica

What's the taxonomical classification of Oenanthe javanica?

Oenanthe javanica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Apiales and places it within the family Apiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by the genus Oenanthe and is specifically designated as the species javanica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Oenanthe javanica has an erect, herbaceous stem that typically grows between 20 and 60 centimeters tall and features a branched structure with lanceolate to linear leaves. The leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem and possess entire margins with acute tips. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils, displaying a delicate pink to purple coloration. These blossoms are characterized by a long calyx and a distinctively shaped corolla that is typical of the Onagraceae family. The plant also develops a slender, creeping rhizome that facilitates its growth in moist or wetland environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the temperate regions of East Asia, specifically found throughout parts of China, Japan, Korea, and Taiwan. It typically inhabits moist or semi-aquatic environments such as the margins of ponds, streams, and marshes. Its presence is often concentrated in lowland areas where water availability remains consistent throughout the growing season. Within these habitats, it thrives in silty or muddy substrates that provide necessary nutrients. How is this plant cultivated?

This plant, known as Java waterdropwort, is cultivated in shallow, slow-moving freshwater or boggy environments where it can root in soft, organic mud.

It requires consistent moisture and prefers waterlogged soil to support its submerged growth habit. Cultivators must ensure the substrate is rich in nutrients to promote the development of its edible stems. Because it thrives in damp conditions, it is often grown in specialized aquatic garden beds or managed wetlands.

Regular monitoring of water levels is essential to prevent the plant from drying out.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating Parkinson's disease, alcohol hangovers, duck hepatitis B virus infection, human hepatitis B virus infection, and general hepatitis. Researchers have investigated its potential to mitigate the neurological symptoms associated with Parkinson's disease. The plant also shows promise in managing the physiological distress caused by alcohol hangovers. Furthermore, its antiviral properties are studied for their effectiveness against both duck hepatitis B virus infection and human hepatitis B virus infection. These medicinal applications highlight its diverse impact on various forms of hepatitis.

According to a study published by "Phytomedicine : international journal of phytotherapy and phytopharmacology", Oenanthe javanica (Blume) DC. is used to protect human cells from MPP+ damage. This plant helps address conditions related to FBXO7 mutations, which have been identified in families with early-onset parkinsonism and pyramidal tract signs designated as PARK15. Such mutations are also found in typical and young onset Parkinson's disease (PD). The extracts of Oenanthe javanica improve the cell viability of both MPP+ treated HEK-293 and SH-SY5Y cells. These effects include rescuing proteasome activity in MPP+ treated HEK-293 cells and restoring mitochondrial membrane potential in MPP+ treated SH-SY5Y cells. This protection acts through enhancing FBXO7 and decreasing TRAF2 expression, likely mediated by GATA2 induction.
