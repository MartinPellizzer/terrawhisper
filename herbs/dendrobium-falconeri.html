Dendrobium falconeri

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dendrobium falconeri has pendulous, elongated pseudobulbs that can reach lengths of up to 60 centimeters and are characterized by a ridged, slightly compressed structure. These fleshy stems serve as the primary storage organs for water and nutrients during periods of environmental stress. The leaves are relatively small, lanceolate in shape, and are typically distributed along the upper portions of the pseudobulbs. During its blooming period, the plant produces numerous small, fragrant flowers that emerge from the nodes of the stems. The floral structure features delicate petals and a distinctive labellum that attracts specific pollinators in its natural habitat.
