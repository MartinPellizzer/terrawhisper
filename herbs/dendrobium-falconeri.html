Dendrobium falconeri

What's the taxonomical classification of Dendrobium falconeri?

Dendrobium falconeri belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Asparagales. This plant is a member of the Orchidaceae family, specifically falling under the genus Dendrobium, with its unique biological identity defined by its species name, falconeri.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dendrobium falconeri has pendulous, elongated pseudobulbs that can reach lengths of up to 60 centimeters and are characterized by a ridged, slightly compressed structure. The leaves are relatively small, lanceolate in shape, and are typically distributed along the upper portions of the pseudobulbs. During its blooming period, the plant produces numerous small, fragrant flowers that emerge from the nodes of the stems. How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires bright, filtered light and high humidity levels between 60% and 80% to thrive in a warm, tropical environment.

It is best grown mounted on cork bark or in slatted wooden baskets to ensure the roots remain well-ventilated and dry between waterings. Frequent misting and a consistent supply of pure water are essential to mimic its natural rainforest habitat. According to a study published by "Rapid communications in mass spectrometry : RCM", Dendrobium falconeri contains specific medicinal compounds.
