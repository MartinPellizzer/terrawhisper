Ribes komarovii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ribes komarovii has a deciduous shrub habit characterized by slender, woody stems and oppositely arranged, palmately lobed leaves with serrated margins. The foliage typically exhibits three to five distinct lobes and a somewhat pubescent texture on the underside. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves during the blooming season. These flowers are arranged in loose clusters and possess a tubular structure. The plant eventually produces small, fleshy berries that serve as a primary feature of its reproductive morphology.
