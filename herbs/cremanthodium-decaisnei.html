Cremanthodium decaisnei

What's the taxonomical classification of Cremanthodium decaisnei?

Cremanthodium decaisnei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized under the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae, falling into the specific genus Cremanthodium, and is identified by the species name decaisnei.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cremanthodium decaisnei has erect, herbaceous stems that typically reach heights between 30 and 100 centimeters with leaves arranged in opposite pairs.

The leaf blades are ovate to lanceolate in shape and possess serrated margins. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the terminal inflorescences, often displaying a pale yellow or white coloration. The plant's root system is comprised of slender rhizomes that allow for gradual spreading through the soil.

These morphological traits distinguish the species within its specific ecological niche.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Cremanthodium decaisnei can cause severe poisoning if ingested, leading to symptoms such as intense abdominal pain, vomiting, and diarrhea. The plant contains toxic compounds that may trigger significant gastrointestinal distress in humans or animals. There is also a risk of more systemic reactions depending on the amount consumed.
