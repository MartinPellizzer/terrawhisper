What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Enydra fluctuans has woody, perennial stems that reach heights of up to 2 meters and bear trifoliate leaves with elliptical to oblong leaflets. Small, fragrant, white or pale cream-colored flowers emerge from axillary racemes during the blooming season. These flowers feature five petals and are characterized by their relatively small size compared to the overall foliage.
