Enydra fluctuans

What's the taxonomical classification of Enydra fluctuans?

Enydra fluctuans belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic descent, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Asterales, this plant is a member of the family Asteraceae. It is specifically identified under the genus Enydra, with its unique biological designation being the species fluctuans.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Enydra fluctuans has woody, perennial stems that reach heights of up to 2 meters and bear trifoliate leaves with elliptical to oblong leaflets. The leaflets are typically 3 to 6 centimeters long and possess entire margins with a smooth, somewhat leathery texture. Small, fragrant, white or pale cream-colored flowers emerge from axillary racemes during the blooming season. These flowers feature five petals and are characterized by their relatively small size compared to the overall foliage. The plant also produces distinctive, somewhat flattened legume pods that eventually turn brown as they mature.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, specifically spanning across West Africa, Central Africa, and parts of East Africa. It typically thrives in lowland areas where seasonal rainfall supports dense vegetation. Many populations are found in disturbed habitats, such as roadsides, forest edges, and abandoned agricultural land. The species is often distributed along riverbanks and moist savannas where soil moisture is consistent. Its presence is most common in countries including Nigeria, Cameroon, and various nations within the Congo Basin.

What are the pharmacological activities of Enydra fluctuans?
