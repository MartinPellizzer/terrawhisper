Trollius farreri

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Trollius farreri has perennial herbaceous stems that typically reach heights of 20 to 50 centimeters and support deeply lobed, palmately divided leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is characterized by a lush green color and a texture that varies depending on the specific microhabitat. Its most distinctive feature is the globular, multi-petaled flower which often exhibits a yellowish or pale orange hue. These complex blossoms are held atop sturdy pedicels that emerge from the upper leaf axils. The plant's root system is generally composed of a thick, fleshy rhizome that aids in its perennial survival.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of the eastern Himalayas, specifically within parts of China, Bhutan, and Nepal. It typically thrives in alpine or subalpine meadows located at elevations ranging from 3,000 to 4,500 meters. These populations are often found in moist, cool environments such as mountain slopes and near stream banks. The species is restricted to these specific montane ecosystems due to its reliance on high-elevation climates. Its distribution is largely defined by the rugged topography and temperate conditions of the Himalayan mountain range.

      What are the herbal preparations of Trollius farreri?

      Trollius farreri is prepared as decoctions, powders, or tinctures to utilize its medicinal properties in traditional practices. The dried roots are frequently boiled in water to create a concentrated liquid extract. These root powders can also be mixed with honey or other substances to facilitate ingestion.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Trollius farreri can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if ingested in significant quantities. The plant contains protoanemonin, a chemical compound that acts as a powerful irritant to mucous membranes and sensitive tissues. Contact with the leaves or stems can result in redness, swelling, or blistering on the skin. If swallowed, individuals may experience nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. Because of these unpredictable reactions, careful handling and avoidance of consumption are strongly recommended.
