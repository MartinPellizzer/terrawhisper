Trollius farreri

What's the taxonomical classification of Trollius farreri?

Trollius farreri belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Ranunculales. This plant is further classified under the family Ranunculaceae, which places it within the genus Trollius, ultimately reaching its specific identification at the species level as farreri.

What are the herbal preparations of Trollius farreri?

Trollius farreri is prepared as decoctions, powders, or tinctures to utilize its medicinal properties in traditional practices. The dried roots are frequently boiled in water to create a concentrated liquid extract. These root powders can also be mixed with honey or other substances to facilitate ingestion. Some practitioners prefer using alcohol-based tinctures to ensure a long shelf life and higher potency. Each preparation method is chosen based on the specific therapeutic goal required for the patient.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Trollius farreri can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if ingested in significant quantities. The plant contains protoanemonin, a chemical compound that acts as a powerful irritant to mucous membranes and sensitive tissues. Contact with the leaves or stems can result in redness, swelling, or blistering on the skin. If swallowed, individuals may experience nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. Because of these unpredictable reactions, careful handling and avoidance of consumption are strongly recommended.
