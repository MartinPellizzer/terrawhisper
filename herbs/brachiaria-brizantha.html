Brocchiaria brizantha belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it falls under the order Poales and is a member of the family Poaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Brocchiaria, and its unique species designation, brizantha.

    What are the herbal preparations of Brachiaria brizantha?

    Brachiaria brizantha is prepared as decoctions, infusions, poultices, or topical washes to utilize its medicinal properties. Practitioners often boil the plant material in water to create a concentrated liquid used for internal consumption. For external skin conditions, the crushed leaves are applied directly as a warm mash to soothe inflammation. Infusions are also common, where hot water is poured over dried parts to extract delicate compounds. These various methods allow the plant's bioactive components to be delivered effectively depending on the specific ailment being treated.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Brachiaria brizantha can cause severe skin irritation, mechanical dermatitis, and respiratory distress due to its sharp, silica-rich leaves and high pollen production. The stiff, abrasive texture of the blades often creates tiny lacerations on the skin when contact occurs. These physical wounds can lead to localized inflammation, redness, and persistent itching. Additionally, inhaling the fine pollen or dust from the plant may trigger allergic reactions or aggravate asthma in sensitive individuals. Contact with livestock can also result in physical injury to their mouths or digestive tracts.
