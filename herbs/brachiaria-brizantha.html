Brachiaria brizantha

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Brachiaria brizantha has an upright or decumbent growth habit characterized by tufted, perennial culms that typically reach heights between 30 and 150 centimeters. The leaves are linear and lanceolate with a prominent midrib and often feature a short, blunt tip. Each leaf sheath is typically hairy or scabrous, providing a rough texture to the plant surface. The inflorescence is a compound panicle consisting of several racemes that are often digitately arranged at the top of the stem. The spikelets are small and arranged in pairs, which is a distinguishing feature of this grass species.

        What side effects this plant can have?

        This plant can cause severe skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and respiratory distress through its sharp, silica-rich leaves and airborne pollen. When the serrated edges of the leaves brush against human skin, they create microscopic lacerations that lead to redness, itching, and painful inflammation. Individuals with sensitive respiratory systems may experience sneezing, coughing, or asthma exacerbations if they inhale the dust or pollen released by the grass. Prolonged exposure to the plant can result in persistent dermatological issues or heightened seasonal allergies. Careful avoidance is necessary for those with known sensitivities to various grass species.
