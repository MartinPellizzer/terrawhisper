Whiteweed (Monardella Villosa Subsp. Euvillosa)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Monardella villosa subsp. euvillosa.

Whiteweed, scientifically known as Monardella villosa subsp. euvillosa, is a medicinal herb native to the western United States, valued for its adaptogenic properties and historical use in traditional herbal medicine. This perennial plant, characterized by its white-tinged flowers and fuzzy leaves, has been traditionally used by Indigenous communities such as the Kalapuya and Klamath for its calming and digestive benefits. The primary benefits of Whiteweed include its ability to reduce anxiety, support respiratory health, and promote digestion, making it a sought-after herb in both traditional and modern wellness practices. In contemporary herbalism, Whiteweed is used as a natural remedy for stress relief, insomnia, and gastrointestinal discomfort, often prepared as a tea or tincture. Its unique feature is the presence of a rare active compound called monardellin, which contributes to its soothing effects, and it has a distinctive, slightly sweet and minty flavor that sets it apart from other herbs.
