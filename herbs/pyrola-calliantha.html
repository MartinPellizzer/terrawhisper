Pyrola calliantha

What's the taxonomical classification of Pyrola calliantha?

Pyrola calliantha belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Ericales. It is a member of the family Ericaceae, which places it within the genus Pyrola, specifically identified by the species name calliantha.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pyrola calliantha has a perennial rhizomatous rootstock that produces upright, unbranched stems reaching heights of 10 to 30 centimeters, topped by several bell-shaped, pink to purplish flowers. The leaves are lanceolate to narrowly elliptic, arranged oppositely along the stem, and possess finely serrated margins. Each flower features a tubular, five-lobed corolla with distinctively spreading or recurved lobes. The calyx is deeply divided into narrow, linear segments that extend beyond the base of the petals. These plants often exhibit a delicate, herbaceous texture throughout their foliage and floral structures.
