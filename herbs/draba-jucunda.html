Draba jucunda

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Draba jucunda has a low-growing, rosulate habit characterized by small, ovate to lanceolate leaves and tiny, four-petaled white flowers arranged in compact racemes. The basal leaves are typically more pronounced and persistent, while the cauline leaves decrease in size as they ascend the stem. Its stems are relatively short and often remain close to the ground, supporting the delicate inflorescences. The siliques, or seed pods, are slender and emerge from the flower stalks after pollination. These morphological features allow the plant to thrive in its specific alpine or subalpine ecological niche.
