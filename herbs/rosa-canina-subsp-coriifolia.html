Rosa canina subsp. coriifolia

Rosa canina subsp.

coriifolia, also known as the wild rose or dog rose, is a subspecies of the Rosa canina species, a type of rose native to Europe and Western Asia.

The medicinal properties of Rosa canina subsp.

coriifolia include its high content of vitamin C, flavonoids, and tannins, which have anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects.

The health benefits of consuming Rosa canina subsp.

coriifolia include its ability to boost the immune system, reduce inflammation, and improve heart health, as well as its potential to prevent certain diseases such as cancer and diabetes.
