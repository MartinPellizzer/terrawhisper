What is Schmidtottia sessilifolia?

Schmidtottia sessilifolia is a species of flowering plant in the family Rubiaceae.

It is an evergreen shrub that reaches heights of up to 1.5 meters, with leaves that are opposite, sessile, elliptic, and glabrous, measuring 4-12 cm long and 1.5-3.5 cm wide. The inflorescence is a terminal spike with white flowers, which are sessile, 4-merous, and glabrous. Each flower is 5-6 mm in diameter and consists of a calyx with 4 lobes and a corolla with 4 lobes.

The plant is endemic to Cuba and is typically found in humid forests at elevations of 500-1000 meters.
