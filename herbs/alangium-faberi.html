Alangium faberi

What's the taxonomical classification of Alangium faberi?

Aliangium faberi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical structure, it is placed under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Cornales, it is nested in the family Cornaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Aliangium, and its unique species designation, faberi.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Alangium faberi has a decumbent to ascending growth habit characterized by square stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is typically covered in fine hairs that provide a textured appearance to the plant. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils in dense, whorled clusters known as verticillasters. These blossoms are usually light purple or lilac in color and possess a subtle aromatic quality. The root system consists of a slender taproot that helps anchor the herb in disturbed soils.

How is this plant cultivated?
