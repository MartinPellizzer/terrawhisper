White Clover (Trifolium erectum)

White Clover (Trifolium erectum) is a member of the Fabaceae family, native to Europe, Asia, and North America. Traditionally, its leaves, flowers, and roots have been used for infusions, decoctions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its diuretic, tonic, and sedative actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and native american herbal medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 20 to 60 centimeters.
Leaves
Opposite leaf arrangement with trifoliate compound leaves, each leaflet having a smooth upper surface with a pale green to white midrib and a slightly paler lower surface, featuring a distinct stomatal band along the midrib.
Flowers
Small white flowers arranged in dense spikelets, actinomorphic, with five white petals and a yellowish-green stipitate calyx.
Stems
Elongated, erect growth habit, opposite branching pattern, glabrous surface, stipulate nodes, and prominent vascular bundles.
