Crassocephalum crepidioides

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Crassocephalum crepidioides has an erect, herbaceous habit characterized by stem diameters of up to 10 millimeters and leaves that are primarily lanceolate to linear in shape. The foliage is typically arranged in an opposite pattern and often features serrated margins with a slightly hairy texture on both surfaces. Its inflorescence consists of small, discoid flower heads clustered in terminal panicles that appear yellowish or whitish. Each individual head contains numerous tubular florets that lack ray petals, a distinguishing feature of the genus. The plant also produces small, ribbed achenes that are frequently topped by a pappus of fine bristles to facilitate wind dispersal.
