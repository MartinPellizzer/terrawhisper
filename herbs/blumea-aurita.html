Blumea aurita

What's the taxonomical classification of Blumea aurita?

Blumea aurita belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it falls under the order Asterales and is a member of the family Asteraceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Blumea, and its unique species designation, aurita.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Blumea aurita has an erect, perennial herb structure characterized by opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins and a distinctly hairy or pubescent surface. The stems are often woody at the base and covered in fine, soft hairs that give the plant a velvety texture. Its inflorescence consists of small, yellowish flower heads arranged in dense, terminal clusters known as capitula. These flower heads are supported by involucral bracts that protect the developing florets. The entire plant exhibits a characteristic aromatic quality due to the presence of specialized glandular hairs.
