Drynaria propinqua

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Drynaria propinqua has a woody climbing habit characterized by twining stems, pinnately compound leaves with multiple leaflets, and small, yellowish-to-cream colored flowers arranged in axillary racemes. The foliage typically features elliptical or lanceolate leaflets that possess smooth margins and a distinct midrib. Its stems are flexible enough to scramble over surrounding vegetation to reach sunlight. The inflorescences consist of small, fragrant blossoms that emerge from the leaf axils. The plant also develops small, woody pods that contain the seeds necessary for reproduction.
