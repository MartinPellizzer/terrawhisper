Pimpinella coriacea

What's the taxonomical classification of Pimpinella coriacea?

Pimpinella coriacea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Apiales, this plant is a member of the family Apiaceae. Its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Pimpinella and the species coriacea.

Its medicinal properties are primarily utilized for their expectorant effects to help clear mucus from the lungs. It is also frequently applied in traditional remedies to alleviate symptoms of the common cold and influenza. The bioactive compounds within the plant help soothe inflamed airways during acute infections. Consequently, it serves as a natural aid for managing several ailments related to the bronchial system.
