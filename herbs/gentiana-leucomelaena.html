Gentiana leucomelaena

What's the taxonomical classification of Gentiana leucomelaena?

Gentiana leucomelaena belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is positioned in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Gentianaales. The plant is a member of the family Gentianaaceae, which places it within the genus Gentiana, specifically identified as the species leucomelaena.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gentiana leucomelaena has a perennial growth habit characterized by upright stems, lanceolate to ovate leaves, and white to pale lilac flowers with distinctively pale or white corolla lobes. The foliage typically presents a smooth texture and is arranged oppositely along the stem. Its floral structure features a tubular corolla that flares into five spreading lobes, often displaying subtle markings near the throat. The reproductive parts are tucked within the bell-shaped blossom to facilitate specific pollination mechanisms. Small, inconspicuous bracts may be present at the base of the flower clusters.
