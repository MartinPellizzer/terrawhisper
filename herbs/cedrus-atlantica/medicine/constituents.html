10 Medicinal Constituents Of Cedrus Atlantica (Atlas Cedar)

Cedrus atlantica has active constituents such as cedrol, cedrenes, and cedrol acetate.

These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antioxidant properties, which contribute to the plant's medicinal benefits. Atlas cedar oil, for instance, can alleviate pain and reduce stress, improving overall well-being.

Additionally, its antimicrobial properties can help combat skin infections, promoting healthy skin and wound healing.

This article explains in details the 10 best active constituents of Cedrus atlantica.

1. Cembrene

Cedrus atlantica, also known as Atlas Cedar, cembrene is a unique chemical compound found in the wood of this majestic tree.

Cembrene is a type of sesquiterpene, a class of organic compounds that are commonly found in plants and have been shown to possess various biological activities, including antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

The presence of cembrene in Atlas Cedar wood is thought to contribute to its durability and resistance to decay.

2. Atlantone

Cedrus atlantica, also known as Atlas Cedar, atlantone is a natural essential oil extracted from the wood and leaves of this evergreen tree native to the Atlas Mountains in Morocco.

This oil is prized for its unique fragrance, which combines sweet, woody, and slightly spicy notes.

It has been traditionally used in aromatherapy to promote relaxation, reduce stress and anxiety, and improve sleep quality.

3. Cedrol

Cedrus atlantica, also known as Atlas Cedar, cedrol is a natural essential oil extracted from the wood of this majestic tree.

This fragrant oil has been used for centuries in aromatherapy and traditional medicine to promote relaxation, reduce anxiety and stress, and improve sleep quality.

Cedrol's sweet, woody, and slightly spicy scent is often used in perfumery and incense blends, making it a popular ingredient in the world of essential oils.

4. Bornyl acetate

Cedrus atlantica, also known as Atlas Cedar, bornyl acetate is a naturally occurring essential oil component found in the wood and foliage of this evergreen conifer.

Bornyl acetate has been shown to possess antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties, making it a popular ingredient in aromatherapy and traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

5. Limonene

Cedrus atlantica, also known as Atlas Cedar, limonene is a monoterpene compound found in its essential oil.

It has a citrus-like aroma and is known for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Limonene is often used as a natural preservative, disinfectant, and insect repellent.

Additionally, it has been shown to have therapeutic benefits, such as reducing anxiety and stress levels when inhaled.

Cedrus atlantica sabinol is a vital component of this coniferous tree.

8. Thujone

Cedrus atlantica, also known as Atlas Cedar, thujone is a chemical compound found in the essential oil of this evergreen tree species native to the Atlas Mountains.

Cedrus atlantica, also known as Atlas Cedar, α-pinene is a type of terpene that is naturally found in the wood and leaves of this species.

It is a colorless liquid with a fresh, piney aroma, which is often used as a fragrance ingredient in perfumes, soaps, and aromatherapy products.

α-Pinene has also been reported to have various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

10. Camphor

Cedrus atlantica, also known as Atlas Cedar, Camphor is a fragrant and aromatic wood extracted from the Atlas Cedar tree (Cedrus atlantica).

It is highly valued for its medicinal properties, being used to relieve anxiety, stress, and insomnia.

The camphor oil derived from this wood has antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, making it a popular ingredient in aromatherapy, perfumery, and traditional medicine.
