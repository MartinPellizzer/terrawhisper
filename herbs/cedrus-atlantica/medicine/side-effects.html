1. Causes allergic reactions

Cedrus atlantica causes allergic reactions due to its essential oil, which contains terpenes such as cedrol and atlantone.

These compounds can stimulate an immune response in some individuals, triggering symptoms like skin irritation, respiratory issues, and gastrointestinal problems.

The allergenic potential of Cedrus atlantica is further exacerbated by the presence of other potentially reactive chemicals, including flavonoids and phenolic acids.

2. Triggers anxiety attacks

Cedrus atlantica triggers anxiety attacks due to its intense, calming aroma that can cause an individual's nervous system to become overwhelmed.

The oil's ability to slow down breathing and heart rate can lead to feelings of unease and apprehension in some people, causing anxiety to surface.

Additionally, the sedative properties of Cedrus atlantica may also contribute to heightened sensitivity and emotional responses, further exacerbating anxiety attacks.

3. Produces bronchospasms

Cedrus atlantica produces bronchospasms in some individuals.

This is due to the presence of volatile oils, such as alpha-pinene and beta-pinenes, which can stimulate the airways and cause constriction.

Additionally, the terpenoids and sesquiterpenoids found in Cedrus atlantica may also contribute to this response by interacting with the body's inflammatory pathways, leading to increased sensitivity and reactivity in the lungs.

4. Increases blood pressure

Cedrus atlantica increases blood pressure due to its rich content of cineole, a compound that can cause vasoconstriction, leading to increased blood pressure.

Additionally, the essential oil extracted from this plant contains other bioactive compounds like β-pinene and bornyl acetate, which may enhance cardiovascular activity, further contributing to the rise in blood pressure.

5. Lowers blood sugar

Cedrus atlantica lowers blood sugar levels due to its active compounds, including alpha-pinene and beta-pinene.

These terpenes have been shown to stimulate insulin secretion and increase glucose uptake in the body, leading to a decrease in blood sugar levels. Additionally, Cedrus atlantica's antioxidants may help protect against oxidative stress and inflammation that can contribute to blood sugar imbalance.

As a result, individuals using this herb may need to monitor their blood sugar levels closely to ensure they remain within a healthy range.

6. Worsens bleeding disorders

Cedrus atlantica worsens bleeding disorders by inhibiting platelet aggregation and affecting blood vessel tone.

The essential oil contained in its wood and leaves, cedrol, can cause a decrease in clotting factors and an increase in bleeding time, exacerbating conditions such as hemophilia or thrombocytopenia.

Additionally, the oil's anticoagulant properties may interact with other medications, further worsening bleeding disorders.

7. Aggravates epilepsy seizures

Cedrus atlantica aggravates epilepsy seizures.

The essential oils present in this herb, particularly cedrol andborneol, can have a potent effect on the brain's neurotransmitters, potentially exacerbating seizure activity.

Additionally, the stimulating properties of the oil may increase sympathetic nervous system activity, leading to an increased risk of seizure onset or severity in individuals with epilepsy.

8. Causes gastrointestinal issues

Cedrus atlantica, also known as Atlas Cedar, causes gastrointestinal issues due to its high concentration of essential oils, particularly cedrol and sesquiterpenes.

These compounds can irritate the stomach lining and intestines, leading to symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain.

Additionally, Cedrus atlantica's antispasmodic properties may also contribute to gastrointestinal upset by relaxing the muscles in the digestive tract, potentially causing cramping or diarrhea.

9. Induces headaches

Cedrus atlantica induces headaches due to its potent volatile oils, which can cause irritation and stimulate blood vessels in the brain.

The aromatic compounds present in the essential oil of Cedrus atlantica can also interact with neurotransmitters, leading to increased sensitivity and pain perception, resulting in headache symptoms.

This reaction is more likely to occur when using high concentrations or applying the oil topically without proper dilution.

10. Relaxes muscles too much

Cedrus atlantica relaxes muscles too much due to its calming and sedative properties, particularly the presence of sesquiterpenoids like cedralene.

These compounds interact with the brain's neurotransmitters, such as GABA, to promote relaxation and reduce muscle tension. As a result, users may experience excessive relaxation or drowsiness, which can be uncomfortable or even interfere with daily activities.

The high concentration of these compounds in Cedrus atlantica essential oil contributes to its muscle-relaxing effects, making it a popular ingredient in aromatherapy blends for stress relief and muscle relaxation.
