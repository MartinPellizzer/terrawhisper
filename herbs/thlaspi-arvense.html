Thlaspi arvense

What's the taxonomical classification of Thlaspi arvense?

Thlaspi arvense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Brassicales. Furthermore, its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the family Brassicaceae and the genus Thlaspi, eventually reaching its specific designation as the species arvense.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Thlaspi arvense contains various bioactive compounds including glucosinolates, flavonoids, phenolic acids, and essential oils. These chemical constituents contribute to the plant's documented antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Glucosinolates specifically undergo enzymatic hydrolysis to produce isothiocyanates, which are studied for their potential anticancer activities. The presence of diverse phenolic acids provides the plant with significant radical scavenging capabilities. Such metabolic profiles suggest that the plant may play a role in traditional medicinal applications targeting oxidative stress.

What are the therapeutic actions of Thlaspi arvense?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including respiratory conditions, urinary tract infections, and digestive issues. It is often employed in traditional herbalism to help clear mucus from the lungs and soothe coughs. Some practitioners utilize its properties to act as a diuretic to assist with kidney function and bladder discomfort. Additionally, its bitter components are believed to stimulate digestion and improve appetite. While used for these purposes, it is important to consult a professional before using it for medicinal treatment.
