Morphologically, it features a single, large, showy flower with a pouch-like lip that is typically pink or yellow, surrounded by a velvety, greenish-brown or reddish-brown calyx with prominent veins. The plant has a rhizomatous root system and produces a single leaf that is broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in boreal and subarctic habitats, making it a rare and ecologically significant species.

Growing, Harvesting and Storage

Cypripedium pubescens grows in cool, moist environments with partial shade, typically in forested areas with rich, well-drained soil high in organic matter.

It prefers a slightly acidic to neutral pH and requires consistent moisture without waterlogging, making it suitable for regions with moderate rainfall or regular irrigation. To ensure optimal growth, it should be planted in a location with dappled sunlight and protected from strong winds, with mulching recommended to retain soil moisture and suppress weeds. Care involves regular monitoring for pests and diseases, as well as pruning dead foliage in early spring to promote new growth.

Harvesting should occur in late summer or early autumn when the flowers are fully open but before pollination, using a gentle technique to avoid damaging the plant, and the harvested material should be dried in a cool, dark place and stored in airtight containers to preserve its medicinal potency.
