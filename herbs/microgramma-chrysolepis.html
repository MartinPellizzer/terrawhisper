What is Microgramma chrysolepis?

Microgramma chrysolepis is a species of fern in the family Polypodiaceae, native to Brazil.

It is a terrestrial fern with fronds 1-3 meters long, pinnate and bipinnate, with 20-40 pairs of pinnae and 5-7 pairs of pinnules, and a rhizome with scales. The frond is green on top, glabrous, and has a dark brown or reddish-brown rachis. The plant reproduces via spores, produced in sori on the underside of the frond, and the spores are brown to reddish-brown and globose.

The fronds and rhizome are covered with a thick, brown, and scaly indumentum.

The main herbal preparations of Microgramma chrysolepis are tea, infusion, tincture, salve, and infused oil.
