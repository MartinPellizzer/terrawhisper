Parathelypteris glanduligera

What's the taxonomical classification of Parathelypteris glanduligera?

Parathelypteris glanduligera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae. It further falls under the order Polypodiales and is a member of the family Aspleniaceae. Within this family, the plant is identified by the genus Parathelypteris, and its specific scientific designation is the species glanduligera.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Parathelypteris glanduligera can cause severe skin irritation, localized swelling, and respiratory distress if the plant's glandular secretions are inhaled or touched. These reactions occur due to the high concentration of caustic oils present in its specialized glands. Contact with the epidermis often results in painful redness and blistering that may last for several days. If the toxins enter the bloodstream through an open wound, systemic symptoms such as nausea or dizziness may follow. Careful avoidance is necessary to prevent these acute physiological responses.
