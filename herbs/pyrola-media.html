What's the taxonomical classification of Pyrola media?

Pyrola media belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the order Ericales and the family Ericaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Pyrola, and its unique species designation, media.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pyrola media has an erect, slender stem that reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with entire or slightly serrated margins. The plant typically displays a reddish or purplish tint along its stems and leaf veins. Its flowers are bell-shaped and characterized by a distinctively enlarged, petaloid calyx that is often pink or purplish in color. These blossoms hang from the upper portion of the stem and possess small, inconspicuous petals. The overall structure is delicate, reflecting its preference for moist, shaded forest environments.
