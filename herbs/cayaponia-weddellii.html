What is Cayaponia weddellii?

Cayaponia weddellii is a species of flowering plant in the family Cucurbitaceae, native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine with a woody stem, pubescent with trichomes, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate-lanceolate in shape, with 7-9 lobes and a cordate base. The plant produces yellow flowers in axillary clusters, which are followed by green, edible, and ovate fruits that are 4-6 cm in length and contain several seeds. The fruit is surrounded by a fleshy, edible pericarp, and the plant's stem and leaves are also used as food.

Cayaponia weddellii is a perennial plant that grows in the understory of tropical forests and along streams in its native habitat.
