10 Possible Side Effects Of Coptis Trifolia (Golden Thread)

Coptis trifolia has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal upset, dizziness, and skin irritation.

These side effects can occur if the herb is ingested in large quantities or used for an extended period.

In severe cases, liver damage and kidney problems can arise, impacting a person's ability to work or perform daily tasks, further exacerbating stress and anxiety.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Coptis trifolia if used imporperly.

1. Leads to insomnia

Coptis trifolia causes allergic reactions due to the presence of alkaloids such as berberine and coptisine.

These compounds can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms like hives, itching, and swelling. Additionally, some individuals may experience respiratory issues, including wheezing and congestion, upon exposure to Coptis trifolia.

The allergic reactions are thought to be triggered by the chemical makeup of the plant's essential oils and other bioactive compounds.

This may lead to symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, or abdominal cramps in some individuals who consume the plant.

The bitter taste of Coptis trifolia's constituents can also irritate the stomach lining, further contributing to gastrointestinal discomfort.
