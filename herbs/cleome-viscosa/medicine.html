What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Cleome Viscosa (Spider Flower)?

Cleome viscosa, also known as spider flower, has health benefits such as treating skin diseases and infections.

Its medicinal constituents include alkaloids, glycosides, and phenolic compounds, which contribute to its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties. Traditionally, the herb is prepared into a poultice or infusion for topical application.

However, excessive use can cause skin irritation, and individuals with sensitive skin should be cautious when using this herb, and it is recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before using it as a remedy.

This article explains the health benefits, active constituents, medicinal preparations, possible side effects, and precautions related to Cleome viscosa.

What are the health benefits of Cleome viscosa?

Cleome viscosa has health benefits such as treating respiratory issues like bronchitis and asthma due to its expectorant properties.

Its leaves and stems have anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, making it effective in curing wounds, skin infections, and fever.

Additionally, the plant has been traditionally used to treat digestive problems, diarrhea, and menstrual disorders, showcasing its versatility and potential as a natural remedy for various health issues.

What are the active constituents of Cleome viscosa?

The health benefits of Cleome viscosa comes from its active constituents, such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins.

These compounds have been shown to exhibit antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, which can help to alleviate various health issues including respiratory problems, skin conditions, and digestive disorders.

Additionally, the plant's extracts have also been found to possess immunomodulatory and antitumor activities, making it a promising natural remedy for various diseases.

What are the medicinal preparations of Cleome viscosa?

Cleome viscosa has different medicinal preparations, such as decoction, infusion, poultice, and essential oil.

The decoction is used to treat fever, cough, and respiratory issues, while the infusion is employed for treating digestive problems like diarrhea and dysentery.

The poultice is applied topically to relieve skin conditions like eczema and wounds, whereas the essential oil is used in aromatherapy to calm anxiety and promote relaxation.

What are the possible side effect of using Cleome viscosa improperly?

Improper use of Cleome viscosa increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as allergic reactions, skin irritation, and contact dermatitis.

It may also cause gastrointestinal issues like diarrhea, stomach cramps, and nausea. Additionally, excessive consumption can lead to headaches, dizziness, and fatigue. In severe cases, it may trigger respiratory problems, such as bronchial constriction, asthma attacks, or even anaphylaxis.

Furthermore, spider flower may interact with medications, resulting in reduced efficacy or increased risk of adverse reactions.

What precautions to take when using Cleome viscosa medicinally?

Before using Cleome viscosa for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as consulting a healthcare professional or qualified herbalist to determine the appropriate dosage and preparation method.

It is also essential to ensure that the plant material is free from contaminants and adulterants, and to use fresh and high-quality extracts or dried herbs.

Additionally, pregnant or breastfeeding women should exercise caution when using Cleome viscosa due to its potential effects on the reproductive system.
