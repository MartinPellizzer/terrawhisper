What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Cleome Viscosa (Spider Flower)?

Cleome viscosa, also known as spider flower, has health benefits such as reducing inflammation and alleviating respiratory issues.

Its medicinal constituents include flavonoids, saponins, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties. These constituents can be extracted through decoction or infusion, and used as a remedy for coughs, fever, and skin infections. However, excessive consumption can lead to gastrointestinal issues, such as diarrhea and nausea.

When using Cleome viscosa, it is essential to consult a healthcare professional, especially for pregnant or breastfeeding women, to avoid potential harm.

This article explains the health benefits, active constituents, medicinal preparations, possible side effects, and precautions related to Cleome viscosa.

What are the health benefits of Cleome viscosa?

Cleome viscosa, also known as spider flower, has health benefits such as reducing inflammation and alleviating respiratory issues.

The plant's leaves and flowers have been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine to treat conditions like bronchitis, asthma, and coughs. Its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties help soothe skin irritations, wounds, and fever.

Additionally, the plant has been found to have antimicrobial properties, making it a potential natural remedy for various health issues.

What are the active constituents of Cleome viscosa?

Cleome viscosa, also known as spider flower, has active constituents such as flavonoids, alkaloids, saponins, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The flavonoids in Cleome viscosa have been shown to possess antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial activities, making it a potential treatment for various ailments, including fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions.

Its saponins also exhibit immunomodulatory effects, which can boost the body's immune system.

What are the medicinal preparations of Cleome viscosa?

Cleome viscosa, also known as spider flower, has medicinal preparations such as its leaves being used in traditional medicine to treat fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions.

The roots and leaves are believed to possess antibacterial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties.

In folk medicine, it is also used to treat various ailments like eczema, ulcers, and wounds.

What are the possible side effect of using Cleome viscosa improperly?

Improper use of Cleome viscosa, also known as spider flower, increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as skin irritation, allergic reactions, and digestive issues.

Ingesting excessive amounts can cause nausea, vomiting, and dizziness. Additionally, Cleome viscosa may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes medications, potentially leading to severe complications.

It is essential to consult a medical professional before using this plant for medicinal purposes.

What precautions to take when using Cleome viscosa medicinally?

Before using Cleome viscosa, also known as spider flower, for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as consulting a qualified practitioner to determine the appropriate dosage and ensure safe usage, especially if pregnant or breastfeeding.

Additionally, it is essential to identify the plant correctly to avoid misidentification with toxic plants.

Proper preparation and storage of the plant parts are also crucial to prevent contamination and ensure efficacy.
