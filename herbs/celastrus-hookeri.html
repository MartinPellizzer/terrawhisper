Celastrus hookeri

What's the taxonomical classification of Celastrus hookeri?

Celastrus hookeri belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Celastrales and the family Celastraceae, eventually reaching its specific classification within the genus Celastrus, with the distinct species designation hookeri.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Celastrus hookeri targets several key enzymes and proteins, most notably cyclooxygenase (COX) enzymes, lipoxygenase (LOX), and various proinflammatory cytokines such as tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-alpha) and interleukin-6 (IL-6). These molecular interactions are primarily driven by bioactive secondary metabolites like triterpenoids and flavonoids found within the plant's extracts. By inhibiting COX and LOX pathways, the plant effectively modulates the arachidonic acid metabolism that drives inflammation. Additionally, its ability to suppress cytokine signaling pathways suggests a significant role in regulating the systemic immune response. These specific protein-ligand interactions underlie the plant's documented anti-inflammatory and analgesic medicinal properties.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin diseases, various types of internal inflammation, and digestive ailments. The bark and roots are traditionally employed to manage conditions like eczema or localized skin irritations. It also possesses medicinal properties that help soothe intestinal discomfort and general abdominal swelling. What are the herbal preparations of Celastrus hookeri?

Celastrus hookeri is prepared as a decoction, a powder, or a topical paste depending on the intended therapeutic application. The dried bark and leaves are most commonly boiled in water to create a potent liquid infusion used for internal consumption. For external ailments like skin irritation, the plant material is often crushed into a fine paste and applied directly to the affected area. Some practitioners also grind the dried components into a fine powder that can be mixed with honey or other carriers. These various methods allow for the controlled extraction of the plant's active medicinal compounds.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Celastrus hookeri can cause severe gastrointestinal distress including nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea if ingested. The plant contains toxic compounds that may lead to significant irritation of the digestive tract. Beyond internal issues, contact with the plant can result in skin irritation or allergic dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Some reports suggest that excessive consumption might impact the central nervous system. It is essential to avoid all parts of this plant to prevent these adverse reactions.
