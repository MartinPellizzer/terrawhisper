Celastrus hookeri

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Celastrus hookeri has opposite, simple, entire-margined leaves that are elliptical to oblong in shape and typically measure between 5 and 12 centimeters in length. The plant features woody stems with a finely textured bark and often exhibits a shrubby or small tree-like growth habit. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge in clusters, typically possessing four to five petals. The most distinctive morphological feature is the fruit, which consists of a capsule that splits open to reveal brightly colored, fleshy arils surrounding the seeds. These structural elements collectively define the species' identification within its natural habitat.

    Celastrus hookeri can cause severe gastrointestinal distress including nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea if ingested. Some reports suggest that excessive consumption might impact the central nervous system. It is essential to avoid all parts of this plant to prevent these adverse reactions.
