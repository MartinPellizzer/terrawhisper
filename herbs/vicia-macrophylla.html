Vicia macrophylla

Vicia macrophylla, also known as the large-leaved vetch or vetchling, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects, which can help to soothe digestive issues, reduce fever, and promote wound healing.

The plant is rich in vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants, making it a valuable source of health benefits, including reducing the risk of heart disease, improving cognitive function, and supporting immune system function.

Vicia macrophylla can be prepared in various herbal forms, including teas, infusions, and tinctures, which can be used to support overall health and well-being.
