Pedicularis oliveriana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pedicularis oliveriana has an erect stem that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and is adorned with narrow, lanceolate leaves that are arranged oppositely along the length of the plant. The foliage is characterized by serrated margins and a slightly pubescent texture on both the upper and lower surfaces. Its inflorescence consists of a dense terminal spike containing zygomorphic flowers with a distinctively enlarged upper lip. These blossoms exhibit a pale pink to purplish coloration and feature a tubular corolla structure adapted for specific pollinators. The plant also develops a fibrous root system that helps anchor it within its subalpine or alpine habitats.
