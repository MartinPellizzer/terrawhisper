Cyperus nutans

What's the taxonomical classification of Cyperus nutans?

Cyperus nutans belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent through the order Poales, it is placed within the family Cyperaceae. Ultimately, the plant is identified by the genus Cyperus, with its specific designation being the species nutans.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cyperus nutans has triangular stems that reach heights of up to 50 centimeters and bear terminal clusters of drooping, brownish inflorescences. The leaves are long, linear, and grass-like, emerging from the base of the plant to form a dense rosette. Each flower spike consists of multiple small spikelets that hang downward due to their lightweight structure. The plant also features a creeping rhizomatous root system that helps it spread through wetland habitats. Tiny, scale-like bracts surround the individual flowers within the drooping clusters.
