Dipsacus atropurpureus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dipsacus atropurpureus has upright, square-shaped stems that reach heights of up to 60 centimeters and are covered in fine, spreading hairs. The leaves are primarily arranged in opposite pairs along the stem, displaying an oblong to lanceolate shape with serrated margins. Its most striking feature is the inflorescence, which consists of dense, terminal heads of tubular flowers. These individual florets exhibit a deep, dark purple to almost black coloration, giving the plant its distinctive name. The calyx is typically divided into narrow, linear lobes that support the developing seeds.
