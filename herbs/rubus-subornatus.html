Rubus subornatus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rubus subornatus has woody stems characterized by a dense covering of sharp, recurved prickles and leaves that are typically palmately lobed with serrated margins. The plant displays a sprawling or arching growth habit common to many members of the Rosaceae family. Its leaves often consist of three to five leaflets that possess a distinctively rugose texture and prominent venation. Small, actinomorphic flowers emerge from the axils, featuring white petals and a central cluster of numerous stamens. These floral structures eventually give way to aggregate fruits that transition from green to a deep red or black color upon ripening.
