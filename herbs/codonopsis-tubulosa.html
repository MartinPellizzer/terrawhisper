Codonopsis tubulosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Codonopsis tubulosa?

Codonopsis tubulosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic descent, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. As a member of the order Asterales, it falls under the family Campanulaceae. At the most specific levels of its classification, the plant is identified by the genus Codonopsis and the species tubulosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Codonoopsis tubulosa has tubular, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow with reddish-brown longitudinal stripes and dark spots.

These blossoms emerge from a basal rosette of long, lanceolate leaves that feature prominent parallel venation. The plant's stem is relatively short, supporting an inflorescence that can hold multiple individual flowers. Its underground structure consists of fleshy rhizomes which allow the plant to persist in its natural habitat.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Codonopsis tubulosa can cause skin irritation or allergic reactions if the plant's sap comes into direct contact with human skin. While there is limited clinical data on human toxicity, accidental ingestion could lead to gastrointestinal discomfort or nausea. Always wash your hands thoroughly after any contact with the plant to minimize these potential side effects.
