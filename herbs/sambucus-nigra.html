Sambucus nigra

What's the taxonomical classification of Sambucus nigra?

Sambucus nigra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized into the order Dipsacales. It is a member of the family Viburnaceae, falling under the genus Sambucus, and is specifically identified by the species name nigra.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Sambucus nigra contains high concentrations of anthocyanins, quercetin, and vitamin C within its dark berries. These bioactive flavonoids function as potent antioxidants that help neutralize free radicals in the body. The plant also features various phenolic acids which contribute to its significant anti-inflammatory properties. Additionally, certain compounds in the flowers and berries exhibit antiviral activity that may support immune function during respiratory infections. Regular consumption of these constituents is often linked to improved cardiovascular health and reduced oxidative stress.

What are the therapeutic actions of Sambucus nigra?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat upper respiratory tract infections, common cold symptoms, influenza, and seasonal allergies. Its berries and flowers contain bioactive compounds that help reduce inflammation and support the immune system. Many people utilize elderberry syrups to decrease the duration and severity of viral symptoms. The plant also possesses diuretic and laxative properties that can assist with digestive issues. Additionally, its antioxidant content helps mitigate oxidative stress associated with various inflammatory conditions.

What are the herbal preparations of Sambucus nigra?

Sambucus nigra is prepared as syrups, teas, tinctures, wines, jams, and elderberry extracts derived from its flowers and berries. The flowers are most commonly used to create fragrant infusions and syrups that support respiratory health. The dark berries are frequently boiled down into concentrated syrups or cooked into jams to provide a high dose of antioxidants. Tinctures made from the plant offer a more potent, alcohol-based way to consume its bioactive compounds. These various forms allow for diverse applications ranging from soothing throat drinks to medicinal dietary supplements.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Sambucus nigra can cause severe nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea if the raw berries, leaves, stems, or bark are consumed because they contain cyanogenic glycosides. These compounds release cyanide into the body, which can lead to significant toxicity. While cooked berries are generally considered safe, consuming unprepared parts of the plant poses a serious health risk. Individuals with autoimmune diseases should also exercise caution, as the plant may stimulate the immune system and worsen certain conditions. Always ensure that all plant material is properly heated to neutralize these harmful substances before use.
