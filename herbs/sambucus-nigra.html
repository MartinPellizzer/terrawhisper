Black Elderberry (Sambucus nigra)

Black Elderberry (Sambucus nigra) is a member of the Caprifoliaceae family, native to Europe, Western Asia, and North Africa. Traditionally, its fruits, leaves, and bark have been used for decoctions, infusions, and tinctures.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, diuretic, and astringent actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and japanese kampo medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 4 to 10 meters.
Leaves
Simple leaves with entire margins, upper surface dark green, lower surface pale green, and prominent stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Flowers are small, white, actinomorphic, arranged in clusters of 5 to 10, with five ovate petals and five distinct sepals.
Stems
Elongated, woody stems with opposite branching, covered in short, glandular hairs, exhibiting a cylindrical cross-section and bearing persistent, woody buds at the nodes.
