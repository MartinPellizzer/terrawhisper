Sambucus Nigra

The therapeutic actions of Sambucus nigra include expectorant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, which help to loosen and clear mucus from the lungs, reduce swelling and pain, and prevent the growth of pathogens.

The bioactive constituents of Sambucus nigra include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and anthocyanins, which contribute to its medicinal properties and make it a valuable natural remedy for various health conditions.

Possible Side Effects of sambucus nigra

Sambucus nigra can have side effects if used improperly, such as causing allergic reactions, particularly in individuals with ragweed allergy, as it belongs to the same plant family.

Additionally, excessive consumption of sambucus nigra may lead to gastrointestinal issues, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea.

In rare cases, it may also interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners, and decrease their effectiveness.

Furthermore, sambucus nigra may cause uterine contractions, making it contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding.
