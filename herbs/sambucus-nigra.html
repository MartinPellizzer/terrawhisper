Sambucus nigra

What's the taxonomical classification of Sambucus nigra?

Sambucus nigra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized into the order Dipsacales. It is a member of the family Viburnaceae, falling under the genus Sambucus, and is specifically identified by the species name nigra.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sambucus nigra has opposite, pinnate leaves consisting of five to seven serrated, ovate leaflets arranged along a central rachis. The plant typically develops as a deciduous shrub or small tree reaching heights of three to seven meters. Its woody stems are characterized by a soft, pithy center and a bark that becomes furrowed with age. During the flowering season, it produces large, flat-topped cymes of small, creamy-white flowers that emit a sweet fragrance. These blossoms eventually transition into clusters of small, dark purple to black fleshy drupes.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to a vast range stretching from Western Europe and the Mediterranean through much of temperate Asia and into parts of North Africa. It thrives across diverse landscapes ranging from woodland edges to riverbanks where moisture is abundant. While its primary origin is the Eurasian continent, it has been widely introduced to other regions like North America and Oceania for ornamental and medicinal purposes. The species can withstand various climates provided they remain within temperate zones. Its wide distribution is supported by its ability to adapt to a variety of soil types and elevations.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Sambucus nigra contains medicinal compounds in its flowers, berries, and bark that are used to treat various ailments. The elderflowers are frequently prepared as teas or syrups to alleviate respiratory symptoms like coughs and congestion. The dark purple berries are rich in antioxidants and are commonly processed into extracts to support immune system function during viral infections. While the flowers and fruit are widely used, the bark and roots must be handled with extreme caution because they contain cyanogenic glycosides that can be toxic. Historically, these different components have been integrated into traditional herbal remedies for their anti-inflammatory and diuretic properties.

What are the pharmacological activities of Sambucus nigra?

This plant has potent anti-inflammatory, antiviral, antioxidant, antimicrobial, and immunostimulatory pharmacological activities. Its high concentration of anthocyanins and flavonoids provides significant antioxidant properties that neutralize free radicals in the body. The extract also demonstrates strong antiviral capabilities, particularly in inhibiting the replication of influenza viruses. Furthermore, its antimicrobial effects help combat various pathogenic bacteria and fungi. These combined properties also serve an immunostimulatory role by enhancing the body's natural immune response.

As discussed in a study, Sambucus nigra fruit and flower extracts exhibit anti-inflammatory and antinociceptive activities. The flower extract, SNFlE, contains flavonoids and phenolic acids, while the fruit extract, SNFrE, contains anthocyanins as main components. In the cotton pellet-induced granuloma test, SNFrE doses of 10, 20, and 50 mg/kg BW reduced granuloma weight in rats by 20.3%, 20.5%, and 28.4%, respectively. At the 50 mg/kg BW dose, SNFrE showed significant (p < 0.01) anti-inflammatory activity comparable to diclofenac at 10 mg/kg BW. Furthermore, both extracts at 50 mg/kg BW significantly inhibited abdominal contractions in the acetic-acid-induced writhing test compared to the control group (p < 0.01).

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds catechin, rutin, kaempferol, quercetin, and cyanidin. These specific polyphenols function as potent antioxidants that help neutralize free radicals within the body. Rutin and quercetin are well-known for their ability to support cardiovascular health and reduce inflammation. The presence of kaempferol and catechin provides additional protective benefits for cellular structures. Furthermore, the anthocyanin known as cyanidin contributes to the plant's vibrant pigmentation and offers neuroprotective properties.

As discussed in a study, Sambucus nigra contains specific medicinal compounds such as catechin, rutin, and quercetin. Rutin was identified as the most abundant compound, especially in the flowers of Sambucus nigra at a concentration of 9.39 mg/g DW. The study also highlights that phenolic compounds like rutin inhibit cholinesterases. These findings contribute to understanding the neuroprotective potential of medicinal plants.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating the common cold, flu, influenza, respiratory viral infections, and various other viral infections. Its active compounds are frequently utilized to reduce the duration and severity of symptoms associated with these illnesses. Many people rely on its properties to support the immune system during seasonal outbreaks of the flu. It is particularly valued for its potential to inhibit the replication of viruses that target the respiratory tract. Regular use of extracts can help manage the discomfort caused by a wide range of viral infections.

As discussed in a study, Sambucus nigra L. was one of 11 important medicinal plants studied for its mineral contents to alleviate common cold symptoms. Researchers identified 11 minor and major elements, including essential, non-essential, and toxic elements, within the plant. The study employed flame atomic absorption and emission spectrometry along with electro-thermal atomic absorption spectrometry to analyze the specimen. A microwave digestion procedure for total concentration was applied under optimized conditions for the dissolution of the medicinal plant. Plant based biological certified reference materials served as standards for the quantification of these elements.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause severe gastrointestinal distress including nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and stomach cramps if the raw berries, leaves, or bark are ingested. These unpleasant symptoms typically occur because the uncooked parts of the plant contain cyanogenic glycosides that release cyanide in the body. Some individuals may also experience more systemic issues such as dizziness during a reaction. Beyond digestive upset, certain people might suffer from various allergic reactions after contact or consumption. It is essential to ensure all parts of the plant are properly cooked to mitigate these risks.

The main side effects this plant can have are shown in the list below.

  • Nausea
  • Vomiting
  • Diarrhea
  • Stomach cramps
  • Allergic reactions
  • Dizziness
