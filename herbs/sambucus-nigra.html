Sambucus nigra

What's the taxonomical classification of Sambucus nigra?

Sambucus nigra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized into the order Dipsacales. It is a member of the family Viburnaceae, falling under the genus Sambucus, and is specifically identified by the species name nigra.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Sambucus nigra contains medicinal compounds in its flowers, berries, and bark that are used to treat various ailments. The elderflowers are frequently prepared as teas or syrups to alleviate respiratory symptoms like coughs and congestion. The dark purple berries are rich in antioxidants and are commonly processed into extracts to support immune system function during viral infections. While the flowers and fruit are widely used, the bark and roots must be handled with extreme caution because they contain cyanogenic glycosides that can be toxic. Historically, these different components have been integrated into traditional herbal remedies for their anti-inflammatory and diuretic properties.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a complex profile of bioactive substances including flavonoids, anthocyanins, phenolic acids, alkaloids, terpenoids, tannins, and cyanogenic glycosides.

The high concentration of flavonoids and anthocyanins provides potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties often utilized to support immune function. Phenolic acids contribute to the plant's overall protective effects against oxidative stress within the body. While terpenoids and tannins offer various secondary metabolic benefits, the presence of cyanogenic glycosides necessitates careful processing to ensure safety.

These diverse chemical constituents work together to define the medicinal potential of Sambucus nigra.

The primary medicinal compounds of this plant are shown in the list below.

  • Flavonoids
  • Anthocyanins
  • Phenolic acids
  • Alkaloids
  • Terpenoids
  • Tannins
  • Cyanogenic glycosides

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Sambucus nigra targets viral proteins such as neuraminidase, pro-inflammatory cytokines including TNF-alpha and IL-1 beta, and various enzymes involved in the oxidative stress pathway. The anthocyanins and polyphenols present in the plant bind to viral surface proteins to inhibit the release of new virions from infected cells. These bioactive compounds also modulate the activity of cyclooxygenase-2 and lipoxygenase to reduce systemic inflammation. Additionally, the plant's secondary metabolites interact with signaling proteins in the NF-kappaB pathway to suppress excessive immune responses. By acting on these specific molecular junctions, the extract exerts its characteristic antiviral and immunomodulatory effects.

What are the therapeutic actions of Sambucus nigra?

Sambucus nigra has potent antiviral, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties that primarily function to reduce the duration and severity of upper respiratory tract infections. The berries contain high concentrations of anthocyanins, which help modulate the immune response and protect cells from oxidative stress. These bioactive compounds may inhibit the replication of influenza viruses by preventing them from attaching to host cell membranes. Additionally, the plant acts as a diaphoretic, helping to induce sweating and manage fevers during illness. Its ability to support mucosal health makes it a widely utilized natural remedy for combating the common cold and seasonal flu.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat upper respiratory tract infections, common cold symptoms, influenza, and seasonal allergies. Its berries and flowers contain bioactive compounds that help reduce inflammation and support the immune system. Many people utilize elderberry syrups to decrease the duration and severity of viral symptoms. The plant also possesses diuretic and laxative properties that can assist with digestive issues. Additionally, its antioxidant content helps mitigate oxidative stress associated with various inflammatory conditions.

What are the herbal preparations of Sambucus nigra?

Sambucus nigra is prepared as syrups, teas, tinctures, wines, jams, and elderberry extracts derived from its flowers and berries. The flowers are most commonly used to create fragrant infusions and syrups that support respiratory health. The dark berries are frequently boiled down into concentrated syrups or cooked into jams to provide a high dose of antioxidants. Tinctures made from the plant offer a more potent, alcohol-based way to consume its bioactive compounds. These various forms allow for diverse applications ranging from soothing throat drinks to medicinal dietary supplements.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Sambucus nigra can cause severe nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea if the raw berries, leaves, stems, or bark are consumed because they contain cyanogenic glycosides. These compounds release cyanide into the body, which can lead to significant toxicity. While cooked berries are generally considered safe, consuming unprepared parts of the plant poses a serious health risk. Individuals with autoimmune diseases should also exercise caution, as the plant may stimulate the immune system and worsen certain conditions. Always ensure that all plant material is properly heated to neutralize these harmful substances before use.
