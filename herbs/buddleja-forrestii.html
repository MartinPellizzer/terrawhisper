Buddleja forrestii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Buddlejia forrestii has an erect, woody shrub habit characterized by lanceolate leaves that are oppositely arranged on the stems and possess finely serrated margins. The foliage is typically a deep green color with a somewhat textured surface. Its inflorescences consist of dense, terminal panicles containing numerous small, tubular flowers. These blossoms often display a pale lilac or white coloration and are held in compact clusters. The plant's overall structure is defined by these elongated flowering spikes and sturdy, branching stems.
