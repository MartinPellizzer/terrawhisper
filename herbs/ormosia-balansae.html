Ormosia balansae

What's the taxonomical classification of Ormosia balansae?

Ormosia balansae belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Fabales. It is further categorized under the family Fabaceae, which identifies it as a legume. Finally, the plant is placed within the genus Ormosia, specifically designated as the species balansae.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Ormosia balansae contains various alkaloids, glycosides, and tannins that serve as the primary bioactive constituents for its medicinal applications. These complex chemical compounds are often extracted from the seeds or bark of the plant to harness their biological activities. Research suggests that these substances may possess antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties useful in traditional pharmacology. The specific concentration of these molecules can vary significantly depending on the plant's geographic origin and the season of harvest. Understanding these chemical profiles is essential for isolating pure compounds for future pharmaceutical development.

What are the therapeutic actions of Ormosia balansae?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, and various types of inflammation. The extracts from its seeds and bark are applied topically to promote healing in damaged tissue. It is also utilized by traditional practitioners to address gastrointestinal issues and stomach aches. Some local medicinal practices involve using the plant to manage minor ailments related to the respiratory system. The therapeutic application of the species remains deeply rooted in regional folk medicine.

What are the herbal preparations of Ormosia balansae?

Ormosia balansae is prepared as a decoction or an infusion by boiling or soaking the plant parts in water for medicinal use. These aqueous extracts are commonly derived from the seeds or bark to isolate specific phytochemicals. Some practitioners also utilize the powdered form of the dried plant material for topical applications or mixed into other substances. The method of preparation often depends on the specific ailment being targeted by the user. These traditional liquid formulations remain the primary way this species is processed in herbal medicine.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Ormosia balansae can cause severe poisoning through the ingestion of its seeds, which contain toxic alkaloids known as toxalbumins. These chemical compounds act similarly to ricin by inhibiting protein synthesis within human cells. Once consumed, the toxins can lead to gastrointestinal distress, including intense vomiting and abdominal pain. In more extreme cases, the systemic toxicity may result in organ failure or death. Because of these potent effects, the seeds should never be handled or eaten by humans or animals.
