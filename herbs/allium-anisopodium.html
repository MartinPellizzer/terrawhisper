What's the taxonomical classification of Allium anisopodium?

Allium anisopodium belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asparagales. The plant is a prominent representative of the family Amaryllidaceae, which is further classified into the genus Allium. Ultimately, its unique biological identity is defined by its specific species designation, Allium anisopodium.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Allium anisopodium has a bulbous structure characterized by narrow, linear leaves and an umbel-shaped inflorescence consisting of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant features slender, hollow leaves that emerge directly from the underground bulb to facilitate photosynthesis. Its flowering stalks, or scapes, are typically erect and bear multiple individual florets arranged in a dense cluster at the apex. The individual flowers possess six tepals and small stamens that are central to the reproductive process. What is the geographical distribution of this plant?
