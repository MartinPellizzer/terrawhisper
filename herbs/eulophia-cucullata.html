Eulophia cucullata

What's the taxonomical classification of Eulophia cucullata?

Eulophia cucullaata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the evolutionary hierarchy further, it falls under the order Asparagales and is a member of the family Orchidaceae. At the most specific taxonomic levels, this plant is identified by the genus Eulophia and is designated as the species cucullata.

    What are the herbal preparations of Eulophia cucullata?

    Eulophia cucullata is prepared primarily as a decoction or a powder derived from its dried tubers to treat various ailments. The tubers are often cleaned, sliced, and sun-dried before being processed into a fine medicinal dust. These powders are frequently mixed with water or honey to facilitate easier ingestion during traditional treatments. In some regional practices, the plant material is also boiled to create a concentrated liquid extract. This liquid form is used to administer the active components of the orchid to patients.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Eulophia cucullata can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or gastrointestinal distress if it is ingested or touched directly. While specific toxicological studies on humans are limited, many terrestrial orchids contain alkaloids or sap that may trigger localized sensitivity. Individuals with sensitive skin might experience redness, itching, or a rash upon contact with the plant tissues. If accidentally swallowed, the chemical components could lead to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal discomfort. It is always advisable to exercise caution and avoid direct contact with any unknown wild plant species.
