Eulophia cucullata

What's the taxonomical classification of Eulophia cucullata?

Eulophia cucullaata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the evolutionary hierarchy further, it falls under the order Asparagales and is a member of the family Orchidaceae. At the most specific taxonomic levels, this plant is identified by the genus Eulophia and is designated as the species cucullata.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Eulophia cucullata contains bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, and various glycosides within its tuberous roots. These chemical constituents are primarily utilized in traditional medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Researchers have investigated these compounds to understand their efficacy in treating skin ailments and digestive issues. Some studies suggest that the plant extracts may exhibit antioxidant activities that help mitigate oxidative stress in cells. Further pharmacological analysis continues to explore how these specific molecules interact with human biological systems.

What are the therapeutic actions of Eulophia cucullata?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat digestive ailments, respiratory issues, and various types of inflammation. Its tubers are often prepared into medicinal decoctions to soothe stomach pains and dysentery. In some traditional practices, the plant is also applied to alleviate symptoms of coughs and bronchitis. Additionally, it may be used to reduce swelling and pain associated with physical injuries. The specific application depends heavily on the local traditional knowledge and the preparation method used.
