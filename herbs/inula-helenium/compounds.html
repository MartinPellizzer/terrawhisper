Helenium (Inula helenium) Compounds

The primary compounds of Helenium include essential oil, terpenoids, flavonoids, and glycosides. These compounds contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Phenolic acids and coumarin support its digestive and circulatory benefits. Saponins, tannins, and alkaloids also play roles in its medicinal effects.

Helenium (Inula helenium) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Inula helenium contains bioactive compounds like sesquiterpene lactones and flavonoids. It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. This oil is commonly used in herbal medicine for respiratory and digestive ailments. Its therapeutic effects are attributed to its complex chemical composition.

2. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a key class of active compounds in Inula helenium, commonly known as elecampane. These compounds contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties, making it valuable in herbal medicine for respiratory and digestive ailments.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Inula helenium. They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds may support immune function and help reduce oxidative stress in the body. Flavonoids are among the key constituents responsible for the therapeutic effects of Helenium in traditional herbal medicine.

4. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Inula helenium, commonly known as elecampane. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in respiratory and digestive health. Glycosides in elecampane may support expectoration and have anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds are often extracted and used in herbal formulations.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Helenium (Inula helenium), contributing to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. It helps support immune function and may aid in reducing symptoms of respiratory and digestive conditions associated with this traditional herbal remedy.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Inula helenium, commonly known as elecampane. It contributes to the plant's expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting respiratory health. Coumarin also exhibits antispasmodic effects, enhancing the plant's traditional use in treating coughs and bronchial issues.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Inula helenium, commonly known as elecampane. It contributes to the plant's expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties, aiding in respiratory conditions by helping to loosen mucus and reduce inflammation in the airways.

8. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Inula helenium, known for its astringent properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in reducing inflammation and supporting digestive health. Tannins also help preserve the plant's medicinal qualities by stabilizing its other active constituents.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloids in Inula helenium are bioactive compounds known for their anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. They contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments. These alkaloids may support immune function and reduce oxidative stress. Their presence enhances the therapeutic potential of the herb in herbal medicine.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a pigment found in Helenium (Inula helenium) that contributes to its vibrant flower color. It is believed to possess antioxidant properties, supporting the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and immune-enhancing effects.
