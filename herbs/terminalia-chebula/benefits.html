1. Fights cancer cells

Cancer is a group of diseases characterized by the uncontrolled growth and spread of abnormal cells.

This condition, also known as malignancy or neoplasia, occurs when genetic mutations disrupt normal cell regulation, leading to the formation of tumors.

It can affect various organs and tissues, and is commonly experienced by individuals across all age groups, though it is more prevalent in older adults.

Terminalia chebula, also known as the Indian gooseberry, has been studied for its potential to fight cancer cells due to its rich content of antioxidants and bioactive compounds.

These properties may help inhibit the growth of cancerous cells and support the body's natural defense mechanisms against malignant transformation.

How this herb helps with fights cancer cells?

Terminalia chebula fights cancer cells because it contains bioactive constituents such as alkaloids and flavonoids, which exhibit potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress, which are linked to the development of cancer. Alkaloids like chebulinic acid and flavonoids such as quercetin have been shown to inhibit the growth of cancerous cells by inducing apoptosis and suppressing tumor proliferation. Additionally, they may interfere with signaling pathways that promote cell survival and metastasis.

Overall, the synergistic action of these bioactive compounds contributes to the plant's potential as a natural anticancer agent.

Scientific Research

According to "Pharmacognosy research", Terminalia chebula fights cancer cells by inducing cytotoxic effects in various cell lines, including MCF-7 (human mammary gland adenocarcinoma) and A-549 (human lung cancer) cell lines, with an IC50 value of 305.18 ± 1.7 μg/mL and 208.16 ± 3.7 μ/mL, respectively.

2. Reduces oxidative stress

Oxidative stress is a condition characterized by an imbalance between free radicals and the body's ability to neutralize them, leading to cellular damage.

This condition, also known as oxidative damage or reactive oxygen species overload, occurs when there is an excess of harmful molecules that overwhelm the body's antioxidant defenses.

It is commonly experienced by individuals exposed to environmental toxins, smokers, and those with chronic diseases such as diabetes or cardiovascular conditions.

The terminalia chebula plant has been shown to reduce oxidative stress by enhancing the body's antioxidant capacity and neutralizing free radicals.

This health benefit makes it a valuable natural remedy for mitigating cellular damage and supporting overall well-being.

How this herb helps with reduces oxidative stress?

Terminalia chebula reduces oxidative stress because it contains bioactive constituents such as alkaloids and flavonoids.

These compounds act as potent antioxidants by scavenging free radicals in the body. The alkaloids, including chebulinic acid and chebulogonic acid, help neutralize harmful reactive oxygen species. Flavonoids in the plant also contribute to its antioxidant properties by enhancing the body's defense mechanisms.

Together, these constituents support cellular health and reduce the risk of oxidative damage.

Scientific Research

According to "Journal of ethnopharmacology", Terminalia chebula reduces oxidative stress by inhibiting lipid peroxide formation, scavenging hydroxyl and superoxide radicals in vitro.
