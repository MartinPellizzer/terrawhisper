Botanical Identification of Chebuli (Terminalia chebula)

To identify Chebuli (Terminalia chebula) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Chebuli, or Terminalia chebula, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Sapindales, family Combretaceae, genus Terminalia, and species Terminalia chebula. This classification places it within the flowering plants and provides a structured understanding of its biological relationships.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Terminalia chebula is the scientific name of Chebuli, first described by the botanist Robert Ellis in 1793. It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is also known by several synonyms, including Terminalia chebula Retz. and Terminalia chebula Gaertn. Common names such as Chebuli, Harad, and Indian chebuli are widely used across different regions.

Morphological Characteristics

Terminalia chebula is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree with a dense, rounded crown. It has a deep taproot system, which helps it survive in dry conditions. The stem is smooth, greyish-brown, and becomes fissured with age. Leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic to ovate, with a leathery texture and entire margins. Inflorescences are axillary, bearing small, greenish-white flowers. The fruit is a globular, yellowish drupe that turns red when mature, containing one large, kidney-shaped seed.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Terminalia chebula can be distinguished by its smooth, dark brown bark with longitudinal fissures, elliptical leaves with prominent veins, and small yellowish-green flowers. The fruit is a small, round drupe with a thick, leathery pericarp. These features help differentiate it from closely related Terminalia species.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Terminalia chebula, or Chebuli, is native to regions in India, Southeast Asia, and parts of Africa. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity. The plant prefers well-drained, loamy soils and is typically found at altitudes between 300 and 1,200 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Chebuli (Terminalia chebula) blooms from March to April, producing small greenish-white flowers. Seed formation occurs from May to July, with the fruit ripening from September to December. For identification purposes, the fruit is typically harvested between September and December when it turns dark red to black.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Terminalia chebula belongs to the genus Terminalia, which is part of the family Combretaceae. It is closely related to species like Terminalia bellerica and Terminalia tomentosa. These species share similar morphological and genetic traits, reflecting their close phylogenetic relationship within the genus.
