Chebuli (Terminalia chebula) Compounds

Terminalia chebula contains several primary active compounds, including tannins, which have astringent properties, and flavonoids, known for their antioxidant effects. Phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory actions, while saponins and glycosides support digestive health. Terpenoids and coumarin offer antimicrobial benefits, and alkaloids enhance its therapeutic potential.

Chebuli (Terminalia chebula) Monograph

1. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Chebuli (Terminalia chebula), known for its astringent properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating digestive disorders and respiratory conditions. Tannin also acts as an antioxidant and has antimicrobial effects. Its presence enhances the therapeutic value of Chebuli in herbal medicine.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Chebuli (Terminalia chebula). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds support digestive health and may enhance immune function. Flavonoids are key to the therapeutic value of Chebuli in traditional herbal medicine.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in Chebuli (Terminalia chebula). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. This compound helps in protecting cells from oxidative damage and supports digestive health. Its presence enhances the therapeutic value of Chebuli in traditional herbal medicine.

4. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Chebuli (Terminalia chebula). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant effects. Saponin helps in treating respiratory and digestive disorders, making it a vital component in traditional herbal medicine.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Chebuli (Terminalia chebula). It contributes to the plant's astringent and antioxidant properties. Glycosides in Chebuli support digestive health and are used in traditional medicine for their therapeutic effects. These compounds are essential for the plant's pharmacological profile.

6. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a major class of active compounds in Chebuli (Terminalia chebula). They contribute to the plant's astringent properties and are involved in its traditional use for digestive and respiratory ailments. Specific terpenoids like chebulinic acid and chebulogonic acid are key to its therapeutic effects.

7. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Chebuli (Terminalia chebula). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Coumarin is also known for its potential role in digestive health and has been studied for its therapeutic applications in traditional medicine.

8. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a key active compound found in Chebuli (Terminalia chebula). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly in traditional uses for digestive and respiratory health. Alkaloids in Chebuli are known for their astringent and antimicrobial effects.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Chebuli (Terminalia chebula). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is responsible for its deep red color. Found in the fruit's outer layer, anthocyanin has antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting its traditional use in digestive and respiratory health.

10. Essential oil

Essential oil is a volatile compound found in Terminalia chebula. It contributes to the plant's aromatic properties and has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. The oil is extracted through steam distillation and is used in traditional medicine for its therapeutic benefits.
