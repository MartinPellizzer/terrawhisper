Chebuli (Terminalia chebula) Uses

Chebuli is used to treat respiratory conditions such as cough, cold, asthma, and tuberculosis. It also helps with throat infections like tonsillitis and digestive issues including diarrhea and inflammatory bowel disease. Its astringent properties make it effective for gastrointestinal disorders.

Chebuli (Terminalia chebula) Monograph

1. Cough

Terminalia chebula is used to treat coughs due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps loosen mucus and reduce irritation in the respiratory tract. It is often combined with other herbs in traditional formulations to enhance its effectiveness for chronic or persistent coughs.

2. Respiratory infections

Terminalia chebula is used in traditional systems to support respiratory health. Its astringent properties help reduce mucus and soothe irritated airways. It is commonly applied for conditions like coughs, colds, and bronchitis. Its antiviral and anti-inflammatory effects may aid in treating respiratory infections.

3. Asthma

Terminalia chebula is used in traditional medicine to alleviate asthma symptoms by reducing inflammation and mucus production in the airways. Its expectorant and bronchodilatory properties help clear respiratory congestion and improve breathing. It is often prepared as a decoction or powder and taken with honey or water.

4. Cold

Terminalia chebula, or Chebuli, is used in traditional medicine to treat cold symptoms. It helps reduce fever, relieve congestion, and soothe throat irritation. Its astringent properties may help clear mucus and support respiratory health during colds. It is often combined with other herbs in formulations for enhanced efficacy.

5. Common cold

Terminalia chebula is used in traditional medicine to alleviate symptoms of the common cold. It helps reduce nasal congestion and soothe throat irritation due to its antiviral and anti-inflammatory properties. Its mucilage content also acts as a demulcent, providing relief from coughing and sore throat.

6. Tuberculosis

Terminalia chebula has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine for its potential anti-tubercular properties. It contains tannins and chebulic acid, which may help in reducing inflammation and supporting lung health. While not a substitute for modern treatments, it is sometimes used as an adjunct in managing tuberculosis symptoms.

7. Tonsillitis

Terminalia chebula is used in traditional medicine to treat tonsillitis due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce swelling and soothe throat irritation. The bark and fruit are often prepared as decoctions or pastes to alleviate symptoms effectively.

8. Diarrhea

Terminalia chebula is used to treat diarrhea due to its astringent and antimicrobial properties. It helps firm stools and reduce intestinal inflammation. Often prepared as a powder or decoction, it is effective in managing acute and chronic cases of diarrhea. Its high tannin content contributes to its therapeutic action.

9. Gastrointestinal disorders

Terminalia chebula is used to treat gastrointestinal disorders due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It helps reduce inflammation in the digestive tract and can alleviate symptoms of gastritis and ulcers. The fruit's high tannin content also aids in soothing irritation and promoting healing in the stomach lining.

10. Inflammatory bowel disease

Terminalia chebula has shown potential in managing inflammatory bowel disease due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. It helps reduce intestinal inflammation and oxidative stress, which are key factors in the condition. Preliminary studies suggest it may support gut health and symptom relief, though more research is needed for clinical application.
