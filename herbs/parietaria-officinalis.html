Parietaria officinalis

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Parietaria officinalis, commonly known as burnet or nettle weed, is a perennial herb in the family Urticaceae. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it has various regional names. The plant has heart-shaped leaves, hairy stems, and small greenish flowers. It is also referred to by several synonyms in historical herbal texts.

Active Compounds

Parietaria officinalis contains key active compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids. These compounds work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Their combined action supports the herb's traditional use in herbal medicine for respiratory and skin conditions.

  • Flavonoid
  • Tannin
  • Phenolic acid

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory conditions and skin irritations.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and inflammatory conditions.

Extract: Used for allergic reactions and respiratory ailments.

Safety Profile

Parietaria officinalis is contraindicated during pregnancy and lactation due to potential toxicity. It may cause skin irritation and allergic reactions in sensitive individuals. Avoid use if you have a known allergy to plants in the Asteraceae family.
