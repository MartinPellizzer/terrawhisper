What is Bael Tree (Aegle Marmelos)?

The Bael Tree (Aegle marmelos) is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent, known for its medicinal properties and the use of its fruit, leaves, bark, and seeds in traditional herbal medicine.

Botanical Identity and Classification

The Bael Tree, scientifically known as Aegle marmelos, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent and belongs to the Rutaceae family, commonly known as the rue or citrus family.

Common Names and Synonyms

The Bael Tree, scientifically known as Aegle marmelos, is commonly referred to by various regional names such as Bael, Bahera, Bel, and Karpas in different parts of South and Southeast Asia.

Plant Description and Morphology

The Bael Tree (Aegle marmelos) is a deciduous tree characterized by its rough, fissured bark, elliptical leaves with a serrated margin, and a dense cluster of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by its distinctive oval-shaped, yellowish fruit that ripens in summer.

Native Habitat and Distribution

The Bael Tree (Aegle marmelos) is native to the Indian subcontinent, primarily found in regions of India, Nepal, Bhutan, and Sri Lanka, growing in tropical and subtropical climates along riverbanks, moist forests, and open woodlands.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The bark, leaves, fruits, and roots of the Bael Tree (Aegle marmelos) are used medicinally, with the bark and roots being most commonly utilized for their therapeutic properties.

Phytochemical Composition

The Bael Tree (Aegle marmelos) contains a variety of bioactive compounds including tannins, alkaloids, flavonoids, glycosides, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties and therapeutic applications in herbal medicine.

Pharmacological Properties

The Bael Tree (Aegle marmelos) exhibits a range of pharmacological properties including antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and hypoglycemic effects, supported by both traditional use and modern scientific studies.

Mechanisms of Action

The Bael Tree (Aegle marmelos) exerts its therapeutic effects through a combination of phytochemical compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, alkaloids, and glycosides, which exhibit antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and gastroprotective properties by interacting with cellular pathways and enzyme systems in the body.

Therapeutic Uses and Indications

Bael Tree is used to treat digestive disorders, diarrhea, constipation, dysentery, respiratory infections, skin conditions, diabetes, and inflammation.

Preparation Methods and Forms
