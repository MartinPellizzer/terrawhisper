Bael Tree

Aegle marmelos

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

The Bael Tree, scientifically known as Aegle marmelos, belongs to the family Rutaceae. It is also called Khariballika in India and is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent. The tree has thick, aromatic leaves and yellowish flowers, with a distinctive fruit resembling a lemon. It has been referred to by various synonyms in traditional herbal texts.

Active Compounds

The Bael Tree contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and coumarins, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic value in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Tannin
  • Coumarin

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action
Integumentary Acne, skin infections, wounds, fungal infections Antimicrobial, antiseptic, antifungal, antioxidant, anti-inflammatory
Digestive Diarrhea, gastritis, gastrointestinal discomfort, gastrointestinal disorders, gastrointestinal infection Antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, antacid, antispasmodic
Respiratory Cough, tonsillitis, respiratory infections, asthma Expectorant, antimicrobial, antiviral, anti-inflammatory

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory conditions.

Infusion: Used for colds, coughs, and skin ailments.

Poultice: Used for skin infections and wounds.

Safety Profile

The Bael Tree (Aegle marmelos) is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and lactation due to potential adverse effects on fetal and infant development. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
