What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Berberis purdomii has small, leathery, evergreen leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. Tiny, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves, usually appearing in small clusters. These blossoms eventually give way to small, fleshy berries that change color as they ripen. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Berberis purdomii contains isoquinoline alkaloids, most notably berberine, which function as the primary bioactive medicinal compounds. These alkaloids are known for their significant antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties. They act by disrupting the cellular processes of various pathogens, making them useful in treating infectious conditions. Additionally, these compounds may assist in regulating metabolic functions and glucose levels within the body.
