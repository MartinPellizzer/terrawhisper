Jiaogulan

Gynostemma pentaphyllum

Jiaogulan (Gynostemma pentaphyllum) is a climbing vine native to China, known for its adaptogenic properties and traditional use in promoting health and longevity. It is primarily used to support cardiovascular health, reduce stress, and improve energy levels by enhancing the body's ability to cope with physical and mental fatigue.

Taxonomy

Jiaogulan, or Gynostemma pentaphyllum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cucurbitaceae, belonging to the genus Gynostemma within the order Cucurbitales, class Liliopsida, division Magnoliophyta, and kingdom Plantae.

Morphology

Jiaogulan is a climbing herb with heart-shaped leaves, opposite or whorled, and yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, with five petals and five sepals.

Distribution

Jiaogulan (Gynostemma pentaphyllum) is native to the mountainous regions of southern China, particularly in provinces such as Guangdong, Guizhou, Hubei, and Yunnan, and is also found in parts of northern Vietnam, eastern India, and northern Thailand.

Traditional Uses

Jiaogulan has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine for its purported ability to enhance vitality, support immune function, and promote longevity, often prepared as a tea or tonic.

Medicinal Systems

Jiaogulan has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine, traditional Korean medicine, and traditional Vietnamese medicine for its purported health benefits.

Phytochemistry

Jiaogulan contains a variety of bioactive compounds, including gypenosides, saponins, flavonoids, and polysaccharides, which contribute to its pharmacological activities and health benefits.

Pharmacology

Jiaogulan exhibits a range of pharmacological properties including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, immunomodulatory, and adaptogenic effects, which contribute to its traditional use in supporting overall health and resilience.

Medicinal Uses

Jiaogulan is used to support immune function, reduce stress and anxiety, enhance physical endurance, improve respiratory health, alleviate symptoms of fatigue, support cardiovascular health, and may help with weight management.

Preparations

Jiaogulan is used to prepare various herbal formulations, including teas, tinctures, capsules, and powders, which are commonly utilized for their purported adaptogenic, antioxidant, and immune-supporting properties.

Safety

While Jiaogulan is generally considered safe when consumed in moderate amounts, some individuals may experience mild side effects such as gastrointestinal discomfort, nausea, or allergic reactions, particularly when taken in high doses or by those with existing sensitivities.

Evidence

Clinical evidence suggests that Jiaogulan may support cardiovascular health, reduce oxidative stress, and enhance immune function, with some studies indicating potential benefits for metabolic syndrome and cognitive performance.

Cultivation

Jiaogulan is typically cultivated in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil, often in shaded environments to mimic its natural habitat, and requires regular watering and organic fertilization to thrive.

Processing

Jiaogulan is typically processed by drying the fresh plant material in a controlled environment to preserve its active compounds, followed by grinding into a fine powder or encapsulating for convenient use.

Identification

Jiaogulan, or Gynostemma pentaphyllum, is a climbing herb native to China, characterized by its pinnately compound leaves with five leaflets and small yellow-green flowers, typically found in humid mountainous regions.

Sustainability

Jiaogulan's sustainability is supported by its status as a wild-harvested plant with a long history of traditional use, which encourages responsible harvesting practices to preserve its natural habitat and ensure long-term availability.

Related

Jiaogulan is closely related to other species within the Cucurbitaceae family, including Gynostemma pentaphyllum var. pubescens and Gynostemma pentaphyllum var. sinense, which share similar morphological and chemical characteristics.
