Impatiens textorii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Impatiens textorii has succulent, lanceolate leaves with serrated margins and a decumbent stem structure that facilitates rapid ground cover. The foliage typically displays a vibrant green hue with subtle longitudinal veining visible under direct light. Small, zygomorphic flowers emerge from the axils, characterized by a distinct nectar spur at the rear of the corolla. These blossoms are often delicately colored, ranging from pale pink to soft white, to attract specific pollinators. The plant's overall growth habit remains low-lying, allowing it to thrive in moist, shaded microhabitats.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of Yunnan Province in southwestern China. It grows specifically within the subtropical montane forests and moist mountain slopes of this particular area. Its habitat is typically characterized by shaded, damp environments found at significant elevations. Because it is endemic to this localized territory, its occurrence is restricted to these specific mountain ranges.
