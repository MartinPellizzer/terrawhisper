Baphicacanthus cusia

What's the taxonomical classification of Baphicacanthus cusia?

Baphicacanthus cusia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Acanthaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Baphicacanthus, and its species name, cusia.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

These interactions significantly decrease the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines like tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-α) and interleukin-6 (IL-6). By modulating these specific protein pathways, the plant demonstrates potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant activities in biological systems. This multi-target approach allows the plant extracts to mitigate oxidative stress and cellular damage.

If ingested, the plant could potentially lead to gastrointestinal distress or more severe systemic toxicity depending on the dosage. Careful handling is necessary to avoid accidental exposure to the leaves or stems. Always consult a medical professional if symptoms arise after touching or consuming any part of this species.
