Eragrostis geniculata

Eragrostis geniculata, also known as finger grass, is a perennial grass native to Australia and New Zealand, characterized by its distinctive finger-shaped panicles and clumping habit.

The plant is used in traditional medicine due to its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory and antioxidant activities.

Eragrostis geniculata has been traditionally used to treat various health conditions, including fever, respiratory issues, and skin problems, offering benefits such as reduced inflammation, improved immune function, and enhanced wound healing.
