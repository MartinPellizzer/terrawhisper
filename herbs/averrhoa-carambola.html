Averrhoa carambola

What's the taxonomical classification of Averrhoa carambola?

Averrhoa carambola belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Oxalidales. This plant is further categorized into the family Oxalidaceae, within the genus Averrhoa, and is specifically identified by the species name carambola.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Averrhoa carambola contains high concentrations of vitamin C, flavonoids, tannins, and various alkaloids that provide significant therapeutic potential. The fruit is particularly rich in antioxidant compounds which help neutralize free radicals in the body. Its chemical profile includes organic acids that can assist in digestive regulation and metabolic functions. Additionally, certain extracts from the plant exhibit antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties used in traditional medicine. These bioactive substances work together to support overall immune health and cellular protection.

What are the therapeutic actions of Averrhoa carambola?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat ailments such as fever, diarrhea, dysentery, cough, asthma, and various skin infections. Its juice and fruit extracts are frequently employed in traditional medicine to manage digestive irregularities and respiratory distress. The plant also possesses properties that help alleviate inflammation and soothe throat irritation. Additionally, some practitioners utilize its leaves or bark to address symptoms related to diabetes and minor wounds. These medicinal applications rely on the plant's natural antimicrobial and antioxidant characteristics.
