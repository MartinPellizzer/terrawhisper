Starfruit

Botanical Identification

Starfruit, or Averrhoa carambola, is a tropical fruit-bearing shrub native to Southeast Asia. Belonging to the Oxalidaceae family, it is also known as carambola or star apple. The plant is a perennial shrub with oval leaves, yellow flowers, and a distinctive star-shaped fruit. It has been referred to by various regional names in herbal traditions.

Active Compounds

The main active compounds of Starfruit include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and tannins, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to provide antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial effects. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Phenolic acid
  • Tannin

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for digestive issues and respiratory conditions.

Extract: Used for inflammation and antioxidant benefits.

Culinary use: Used for general nutrition and digestive health.

Safety Profile

Starfruit contains oxalic acid and can cause kidney damage, especially in individuals with pre-existing renal conditions. It should be avoided by those with kidney disease or a history of kidney stones. Consumption in large quantities may also lead to hypokalemia and other metabolic disturbances.
