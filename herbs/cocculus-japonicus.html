Cocculus japonicus

Cocculus japonicus, commonly known as the Japanese snail herb or Japanese dodder, is a climbing plant native to East Asia.

This plant is valued for its potential health benefits, which include its use in treating fever, diarrhea, and respiratory issues, as well as its ability to help with weight loss and improve digestion.

The therapeutic actions of Cocculus japonicus include its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antispasmodic properties, which may help alleviate symptoms of various health conditions.

The bioactive constituents of this plant include alkaloids, glycosides, and flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.
