Bitterleaf

Vernonia amygdalina

Bitterleaf (Vernonia amygdalina) is a medicinal plant native to Africa, commonly used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, including the treatment of ailments such as malaria, fever, and digestive issues.

Botanical Identification

Bitterleaf, scientifically known as Vernonia amygdalina, belongs to the family Asteraceae. Commonly called African bitterleaf or bitterleaf, it is a perennial shrub native to Africa. The plant has serrated leaves, yellow flowers, and a woody root system. It is also known by various regional names and has been referenced in traditional herbal literature.

Bitterleaf Uses.

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory infections and malaria due to its antimalarial and anti-inflammatory properties.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and as an antispasmodic due to its high concentration of active compounds.

Extract: Used for skin conditions and as an antihelmintic due to its potent bioactive compounds.

Safety Profile

Bitterleaf contains toxic compounds that can cause gastrointestinal upset, liver damage, and neurological effects. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to potential harm to the fetus or infant. Use under professional supervision is strongly recommended.
