Possible Side Effects of vernonia amygdalina

Vernonia amygdalina can have side effects if used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, nausea, and vomiting, particularly when consumed in excessive amounts.

Prolonged use or high doses of the herb may also lead to kidney and liver damage, as well as electrolyte imbalances.

In some cases, Vernonia amygdalina has been associated with allergic reactions, including skin rashes and respiratory problems.

If not used under the guidance of a qualified healthcare professional, Vernonia amygdalina may cause more harm than good, making it essential to exercise caution and follow proper dosage guidelines.
