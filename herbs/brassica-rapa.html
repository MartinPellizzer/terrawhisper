Brassica Rapa: Medicinal Uses And Benefits

Brassica rapa, commonly known as the turnip, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its edible root and leafy greens.

This herb is rich in nutrients and has been used for centuries in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, including supporting digestive health and boosting the immune system.

Therapeutically, it exhibits anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and detoxifying properties.

The bioactive constituents of Brassica rapa include glucosinolates, vitamins C and K, dietary fiber, and minerals such as calcium and iron.
