Ligularia xanthotricha

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ligularia xanthotricha has large, cordate leaves characterized by serrated margins and a dense covering of golden-yellow hairs on their undersides. These prominent trichomes provide a distinct color contrast against the green upper surfaces of the foliage. The plant features thick, succulent stems that support robust, upright growth habits. Its inflorescences consist of bright yellow flower heads arranged in terminal clusters typical of the Asteraceae family. The overall morphology is defined by these striking hairy textures and broad leaf structures.

      Ligularia xanthotricha is prepared primarily as a decoction through boiling the dried aerial parts in water or as a topical poultice made from crushed fresh leaves.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Ligularia xanthotricha can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or digestive distress if ingested by humans or animals. Contact with the foliage or stems may trigger redness, itching, or localized swelling in sensitive individuals. Some people might experience more severe hypersensitivity reactions such as rashes or hives upon exposure to the plant's toxins. If consumed, the plant may lead to nausea, abdominal pain, or vomiting. Careful handling is recommended to avoid these potential physiological reactions.
