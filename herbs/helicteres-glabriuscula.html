Helicteres glabriuscula

What's the taxonomical classification of Helicteres glabriuscula?

Helicteres glabriuscula belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Malvales. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the family Malvaceae, specifically within the genus Helicteres, which defines its species designation as Helicteres glabriuscula.

Helicteres glabriuscula contains diverse bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, tannins, and terpenoids that exhibit various therapeutic properties. What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, dysentery, fever, and various types of stomach ailments. The leaves and bark are frequently employed in traditional medicine to address inflammatory skin conditions and wounds. Decoctions made from the plant are often consumed to alleviate digestive distress and intestinal issues. Its properties are also utilized in local practices to help reduce high body temperatures. This botanical species remains a significant component of herbal remedies for managing these specific ailments.
