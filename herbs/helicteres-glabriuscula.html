What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Helicteres glabriuscula has a woody shrub or small tree habit characterized by elliptic to oblong leaves that are glabrous on both surfaces and arranged in an opposite pattern along the stems. The leaf margins are typically entire or slightly revolute, and the venation is distinct with prominent secondary veins. Its inflorescences are composed of small, tubular flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. The fruit is a characteristic schizocarpic capsule that splits into several one-seeded cocci upon maturity. These morphological traits distinguish the species within its genus by the absence of pubescence on the vegetative organs.
