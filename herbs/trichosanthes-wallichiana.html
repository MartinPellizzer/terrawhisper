Trichosanthes wallichiana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Trichosanthes wallichiana has a prostrate, herbaceous climbing habit characterized by slender, twinning stems and pentagonal cross-sections. The leaves are simple, alternate, and typically possess a cordate or hastate shape with serrated margins. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that are often arranged in axillary clusters. The fruit develops into a small, globose berry that typically turns red or yellowish upon maturity. These morphological traits allow the plant to effectively scramble over surrounding vegetation in its natural habitat.

What side-effects this plant can have?

There is also limited scientific data regarding its potential toxicity during long-term usage or its effects on pregnant women. Users should exercise caution and consult medical professionals before integrating this botanical into a regular health regimen.
