Trichosanthes wallichiana

What's the taxonomical classification of Trichosanthes wallichiana?

Trichosanthes wallichiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. As a member of the order Cucurbitales, it falls into the family Cucurbitaceae, which is a group known for its diverse climbing plants. More specifically, it is identified by the genus Trichosanthes and is distinguished as the species wallichiana.

Trichosanthes wallichiana is prepared as decoctions, infusions, powders, pastes, and juice extracts derived from its various plant parts including the roots, leaves, and fruits. What side-effects this plant can have?

Trichosanthes wallichiana can cause gastrointestinal distress including nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea if consumed in improper doses or without careful preparation. While often used in traditional medicine, the plant contains various bioactive compounds that may trigger allergic reactions in sensitive individuals. Excessive intake might lead to unexpected changes in blood sugar levels or blood pressure due to its pharmacological properties. There is also limited scientific data regarding its potential toxicity during long-term usage or its effects on pregnant women. Users should exercise caution and consult medical professionals before integrating this botanical into a regular health regimen.
