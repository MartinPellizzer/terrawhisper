Osmanthus heterophyllus

What's the taxonomical classification of Osmanthus heterophyllus?

Osmanthus heterophyllus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Lamiales. It is a prominent member of the family Oleaceae, which places it alongside other well-known fragrant shrubs. Specifically, it is categorized under the genus Osmanthus, with its unique botanical identity defined by the specific epithet heterophyllus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Osmanthus heterophyllus has leaves that exhibit heterophylly, meaning they vary in shape between juvenile and mature stages. Young plants often produce serrated or lobed leaves that resemble holly, while mature specimens develop smooth-edged, elliptical leaves. The foliage is typically evergreen, characterized by a leathery texture and a deep green color. Small, fragrant, white tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils during the growing season. The plant's woody structure supports these diverse leaf forms on sturdy, spreading branches.
