Lemon Myrtle (Backhousia citriodora) Actions

Lemon Myrtle exhibits several key therapeutic actions including anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and expectorant properties. It also acts as a carminative, tonic, and diuretic, while providing a mild sedative and stimulating effect. Its astringent and bitter qualities support digestive health and respiratory function.

Lemon Myrtle (Backhousia citriodora) Monograph

1. Anti-inflammatory

Lemon Myrtle has an anti-inflammatory action, primarily due to its high content of citral and other terpenes. These compounds inhibit inflammatory pathways, reducing swelling and pain. It is commonly used for conditions like arthritis and skin inflammation. Its effects are supported by both traditional use and preliminary scientific research.

2. Carminative

Lemon Myrtle has a carminative action, helping to relieve gas and bloating by stimulating the digestive system and promoting the expulsion of trapped air. It is commonly used in herbal medicine to support digestive health and ease gastrointestinal discomfort.

3. Antispasmodic

Lemon Myrtle has an antispasmodic action, helping to relieve muscle spasms and cramps by relaxing smooth muscle tissue. It is commonly used for digestive issues like colic and intestinal spasms. Its compounds interact with the nervous system to reduce involuntary muscle contractions. This makes it valuable in herbal medicine for gastrointestinal and respiratory conditions.

4. Expectorant

Lemon Myrtle has an expectorant action, helping to loosen and expel mucus from the respiratory tract. It is commonly used to alleviate symptoms of coughs and congestion. Its essential oils contain compounds that stimulate the production of bronchial secretions. This makes it beneficial for respiratory conditions involving excess mucus.

5. Tonic

Lemon Myrtle has a tonic action, supporting overall vitality and immune function. It stimulates digestion and helps balance bodily systems. Its aromatic properties enhance mental clarity and energy levels. Regular use can promote resilience and well-being.

6. Bitter

Lemon Myrtle has a bitter action, which stimulates digestion and increases appetite. It helps to clear mucus and supports liver function. This action is particularly useful for digestive discomfort and respiratory congestion. The bitter taste also promotes a sense of clarity and vitality.

7. Stimulant

Lemon Myrtle has a stimulant action, enhancing mental alertness and energy levels. It works by supporting the nervous system and improving circulation, which can help with fatigue and sluggishness. This effect makes it useful in herbal formulations for boosting vitality and cognitive function.

8. Diuretic

Lemon Myrtle has a diuretic action, promoting the elimination of excess fluids from the body. This effect supports kidney function and can help reduce swelling. It is commonly used in herbal medicine to address fluid retention and support urinary health.

9. Astringent

Lemon Myrtle has an astringent action, which helps reduce excess moisture and tighten tissues. It is particularly effective for conditions like diarrhea and skin irritations. This property is due to its high concentration of polyphenols and flavonoids. It supports healing by promoting tissue repair and reducing inflammation.

10. Sedative

Lemon Myrtle has a sedative action, promoting relaxation and reducing anxiety. It may help calm the nervous system, making it useful for stress relief. Its calming properties support restful sleep and emotional balance. This effect is attributed to its aromatic compounds and mild antispasmodic properties.
