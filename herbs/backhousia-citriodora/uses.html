Lemon Myrtle (Backhousia citriodora) Uses

Lemon myrtle is used to treat respiratory infections, including colds, flu, bronchitis, and sinusitis. It helps reduce inflammation and relieve mucous congestion in the upper respiratory tract. It may also support recovery from tonsillitis and ear infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

Lemon Myrtle (Backhousia citriodora) Monograph

1. Respiratory infections

Lemon Myrtle contains high levels of citral, which has antimicrobial properties. It may help reduce symptoms of respiratory infections by inhibiting bacterial and viral growth. Traditionally used in herbal medicine, it supports immune function and can be consumed as a tea or extract. Its potent aroma also acts as a natural decongestant.

2. Inflammation

Lemon Myrtle contains high levels of cineole, which has anti-inflammatory properties. It can help reduce inflammation in the respiratory tract and may support conditions like bronchitis. Its essential oils are often used in aromatherapy to ease inflammation and promote respiratory health.

3. Mucous congestion

Lemon Myrtle is used to help alleviate mucous congestion due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It can soothe irritated respiratory passages and reduce excess mucus production. Often used in steam inhalations or as a herbal tea, it supports clear breathing and respiratory comfort.

4. Upper respiratory tract infections

Lemon Myrtle contains high levels of citral, which has antimicrobial properties. It can help reduce symptoms of upper respiratory tract infections by fighting bacteria and viruses. Its essential oil is often used in inhalation therapies to support respiratory health. It is valued in herbal medicine for its soothing and cleansing effects on the respiratory system.

5. Sinusitis

Lemon Myrtle has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly for sinusitis. Its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties help reduce congestion and soothe sinus inflammation. It can be used in steam inhalations or as a herbal infusion to relieve symptoms.

6. Bronchitis

Lemon Myrtle has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in cases of bronchitis. Its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties help reduce mucus production and soothe irritated airways. It may be used in steam inhalations or as a herbal tea to ease breathing and alleviate symptoms.

7. Cold

Lemon Myrtle is used to support the body's response to colds due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It may help alleviate symptoms like congestion and sore throat when consumed as a tea or added to remedies. Its high vitamin C content also boosts immune function during colds. Regular use can aid in reducing the duration and severity of cold symptoms.

8. Influenza

Lemon Myrtle contains high levels of citral, which exhibits antiviral properties. Studies suggest it may help reduce the duration and severity of influenza symptoms. It supports immune function and can be used as a complementary remedy during flu season. Its essential oil is often diluted and applied topically or inhaled for respiratory support.

9. Tonsillitis

Lemon Myrtle has been traditionally used to support the treatment of tonsillitis due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Its essential oils, particularly citral, help reduce throat inflammation and combat bacterial infections. It can be used in steam inhalations or as a gargle to alleviate symptoms and promote healing.

10. Ear infections

Lemon Myrtle has been used traditionally to support ear health due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Its essential oils, particularly citral, may help reduce infections and inflammation in the ear canal. It is often used in ear drops or as a supportive remedy alongside conventional treatments for ear infections.
