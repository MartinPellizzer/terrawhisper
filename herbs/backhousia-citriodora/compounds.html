Lemon Myrtle (Backhousia citriodora) Compounds

The primary compounds of Lemon Myrtle include essential oil, which gives it its distinctive citrus scent, and terpenoids, which contribute to its antimicrobial properties. Flavonoids and phenolic acids provide antioxidant benefits, while coumarin adds anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins and glycosides support digestive health, and saponins may aid in detoxification.
