Horse Radish (Cnidium monnieri)

Horse Radish (Cnidium monnieri) is a member of the Apiaceae family, native to Asia, China, and East Asia. Traditionally, its roots, rhizomes, and stems have been used for decoctions, powders, and infusions.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, tonic, and stimulant actions, and has a long history of use in traditional chinese medicine, japanese kampo medicine, and korean traditional medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 30 to 100 centimeters.
Leaves
Sessile, pinnately compound leaves with dark green upper surfaces and lighter green lower surfaces, featuring prominent stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Inflorescences are racemose with small, yellowish-green flowers that are actinomorphic, having four sepals and four petals, with two stamens and a single pistil.
Stems
Smooth, cylindrical, somewhat ridged, glabrous, branched in an alternate pattern, with a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.
