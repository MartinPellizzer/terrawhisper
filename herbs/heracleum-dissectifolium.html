Heracleum dissectifolium

What's the taxonomical classification of Heracleum dissectifolium?

Heracleum dissectifolium belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and falls within the order Apiales. The plant is a member of the family Apiaceae, which is further narrowed down to the genus Heracleum, ultimately identifying it as the specific species dissectifolium.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat respiratory ailments such as coughs, bronchitis, and asthma, as well as digestive issues like indigestion and stomach pain. The plant contains various bioactive compounds that possess anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. These characteristics help soothe irritated mucous membranes in the lungs and throat. Additionally, its extracts are sometimes applied to aid in the relief of skin inflammations. Local traditional medicine often relies on its roots and seeds to address these specific physiological conditions.
