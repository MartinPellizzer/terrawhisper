Andrographis paniculata causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

This is due to its high content of andrografin, a compound that can stimulate the immune system, leading to an overactive response in people with sensitive skin or allergies.

The allergic reaction may manifest as hives, itching, redness, swelling, or even difficulty breathing in severe cases.
