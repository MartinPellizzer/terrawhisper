Kalmegh (Andrographis paniculata) Compounds

The primary compounds in Kalmegh include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and terpenoids, known for their anti-inflammatory effects. Glycosides and saponins contribute to immune support, while alkaloids and phenolic acids offer antimicrobial benefits. Tannins and coumarin provide additional therapeutic properties, and essential oils and anthocyanins enhance the plant's overall medicinal profile.

Kalmegh (Andrographis paniculata) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of compounds found in Kalmegh (Andrographis paniculata) that contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These bioactive components support the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory infections and immune support. Flavonoids enhance the overall therapeutic potential of Kalmegh by modulating cellular responses and reducing oxidative stress.

2. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a key class of bioactive compounds in Kalmegh (Andrographis paniculata). They contribute to the plant's antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Specific terpenoids like andrographolide are responsible for many of its therapeutic effects. These compounds are central to the traditional use of Kalmegh in herbal medicine.

3. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Kalmegh (Andrographis paniculata). It contributes to the plant's immunomodulatory and anti-inflammatory effects. Glycosides in Kalmegh are known to enhance the body's natural defenses and support respiratory health.

4. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Kalmegh (Andrographis paniculata). It contributes to the plant's bitter taste and has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Saponins may support immune function and are part of the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

5. Alkaloid

Andrographolide is a key alkaloid in Kalmegh (Andrographis paniculata), known for its potent anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory effects. It is responsible for many of the plant's traditional uses in treating infections and respiratory conditions. This compound is widely studied for its therapeutic potential in modern herbal medicine.

6. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Kalmegh (Andrographis paniculata), known for its potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating infections and boosting immune function. This compound is also associated with its ability to modulate cellular signaling pathways.

7. Tannin

Tannin is a compound found in Kalmegh (Andrographis paniculata) that contributes to its astringent properties. It helps in reducing inflammation and may support digestive health by binding to proteins and inhibiting microbial growth. Tannins also play a role in the plant's traditional use for treating infections and respiratory conditions.

8. Coumarin

Coumarin is a bioactive compound found in Kalmegh (Andrographis paniculata). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Coumarin may support immune function and has been studied for its potential therapeutic effects. Its presence enhances the overall efficacy of Kalmegh in traditional herbal medicine.

9. Essential oil

The essential oil of Kalmegh (Andrographis paniculata) contains volatile compounds like limonene and eucalyptol. These components contribute to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The essential oil is often used in aromatherapy and as a supportive treatment for respiratory and digestive issues.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Kalmegh (Andrographis paniculata). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress in the body. This compound supports the plant's traditional use in promoting immune health and reducing inflammation.
