Kalmegh (Andrographis paniculata) Uses

Kalmegh is commonly used to treat the common cold, bronchitis, and upper respiratory infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce fever and alleviate symptoms of cough and asthma. It is also used for influenza, lung infections, and allergic rhinitis, supporting immune function and respiratory health.

Kalmegh (Andrographis paniculata) Monograph

1. Common cold

Kalmegh is traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of the common cold due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce fever, sore throat, and congestion. Studies suggest it may shorten the duration of cold symptoms. It is often taken as a tonic during cold season to boost immune response.

2. Bronchitis

Kalmegh is used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health, particularly in cases of bronchitis. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties help reduce throat irritation and mucus production. It may also enhance immune function, aiding recovery from respiratory infections. Traditionally, it is prepared as a tea or tincture to alleviate symptoms.

3. Influenza

Kalmegh supports immune function and may help alleviate symptoms of influenza due to its antimicrobial and antiviral properties. It is often used in herbal formulations to reduce fever, sore throat, and respiratory congestion associated with flu. Its active compound, andrographolide, enhances the body's defenses against viral infections.

4. Tuberculosis

Kalmegh has shown potential in supporting tuberculosis treatment by enhancing immune response and reducing inflammation. Its active compounds, such as andrographolide, may help combat Mycobacterium tuberculosis. Studies suggest it can complement conventional therapies, though it should not replace prescribed medications. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.

5. Lung infections

Kalmegh is used in traditional systems to support the treatment of lung infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce symptoms like coughing and fever, and may enhance the body's immune response. Its active compounds, such as andrographolide, are believed to target pathogens and soothe respiratory inflammation.

6. Allergic rhinitis

Kalmegh (Andrographis paniculata) is used in herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis. Its active compounds, such as andrographolide, possess anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory properties that help reduce nasal congestion, sneezing, and runny nose. Clinical studies suggest it can be effective as a supportive treatment for seasonal allergies.

7. Cough

Kalmegh is used to alleviate coughs due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce mucus production and soothe irritated airways. Traditionally, it is combined with other herbs to enhance its effectiveness in treating respiratory infections and persistent coughs. Its action supports the body's natural defenses against respiratory ailments.

8. Upper respiratory infection

Kalmegh is traditionally used to support the body's response to upper respiratory infections. Its active compounds, such as andrographolides, have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It may help reduce symptoms like sore throat and congestion. It is often used as a supportive therapy in herbal medicine for respiratory health.

9. Asthma

Kalmegh is used in traditional medicine to support respiratory health. It contains andrographolide, which may help reduce inflammation in the airways. This makes it a potential aid for managing asthma symptoms. However, it should be used under professional guidance as part of a comprehensive treatment plan.

10. Fever

Kalmegh is used to reduce fever due to its strong antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps lower body temperature by supporting the immune system and reducing infection-related inflammation. Traditionally, it is prepared as a decoction or tincture for fever relief. It is particularly effective for fevers caused by viral or bacterial infections.
