Tephroseris kirilowii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tephroseris kirilowii has large, deeply lobed leaves with a coarse texture and a woody, perennial structure. The foliage is characterized by prominent venation and a somewhat hairy surface that provides protection. Its inflorescence consists of numerous small flower heads arranged in complex clusters typical of the Asteraceae family. These flower heads feature ray florets that surround a central disk, often displaying shades of yellow. The plant typically develops a robust stem system to support its significant leafy growth.
