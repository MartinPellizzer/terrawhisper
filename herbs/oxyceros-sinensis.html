Oxyceros sinensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Oxyceros sinensis has a woody, scrambling shrub habit characterized by slender, branched stems that often form dense thickets. The leaves are simple, evergreen, and typically possess an ovate to elliptical shape with entire margins. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in axillary clusters, which eventually develop into fleshy, berry-like fruits. The texture of the foliage is often leathery, helping the plant withstand various environmental conditions. This morphology allows the species to effectively navigate and climb through surrounding vegetation in its natural habitat.
