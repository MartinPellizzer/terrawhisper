Hydrangea striosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the order Cornales and the family Hydrangeaceae. Finally, its specific identity is defined by its placement within the genus Hydrangea, with its unique biological classification completed at the species level as Hydrangea striosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hydrangea strigosa has woody, deciduous stems that support large, oppositely arranged, ovate leaves featuring serrated margins and distinctively rough, scabrid surfaces. The foliage is characterized by prominent venation and a textured feel caused by minute, stiff hairs on the leaf blades. During the blooming season, the plant produces terminal corymbs composed of numerous small, white or pale pink flowers. These inflorescences are clustered at the ends of the branches, creating a dense display of floral structures. The overall architecture of the shrub is bushy and spreading, reaching moderate heights in its natural habitat.
