Hydrangea strigosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hydrangea strigosa has woody, deciduous stems that support large, oppositely arranged, ovate leaves featuring serrated margins and distinctively rough, scabrid surfaces. The foliage is characterized by prominent venation and a textured feel caused by minute, stiff hairs on the leaf blades. During the blooming season, the plant produces terminal corymbs composed of numerous small, white or pale pink flowers. These inflorescences are clustered at the ends of the branches, creating a dense display of floral structures. The overall architecture of the shrub is bushy and spreading, reaching moderate heights in its natural habitat.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountain forests and rocky slopes of China, specifically found in provinces such as Hubei, Hunan, Sichuan, and Guizhou. It thrives in temperate montane environments where it can access shaded, moist habitats. The species is typically distributed across various elevations within these central and southern highland regions. Local populations often grow in close proximity to streams or in damp, well-drained woodland settings. This specific ecological niche limits its natural occurrence to these particular mountainous territories in East Asia.

      Hydrangea striosa is prepared primarily as a decoction of the dried roots or as a concentrated herbal tincture to extract its active alkaloids.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Hydrangea striosa can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if the plant is ingested by humans or pets. The leaves and stems contain cyanogenic glycosides which may release cyanide when chewed or swallowed. Contact with the plant's foliage can lead to dermatitis or a localized rash in sensitive individuals. If consumed in significant quantities, symptoms often include nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain. It is important to keep this species away from curious children and animals to prevent accidental poisoning.
