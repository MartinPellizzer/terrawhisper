Aristolochia tagala

What's the taxonomical classification of Aristolochia tagala?

Aristolochia tagala belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Piperales and is a member of the family Aristolochiaceae. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Aristolochia and the species tagala.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aristolochia tagala has climbing stems that reach several meters in length, cordate leaves with entire margins, and tubular, two-lipped flowers that are typically yellowish-green or brownish. The foliage is characterized by a glossy texture and prominent venation on the leaf surface. Its inflorescences are often solitary or in small clusters arising from the leaf axils. The flowers possess a complex structure featuring a perianth tube that houses a specialized chamber for pollination. This plant species is distinguished by its vigorous twining habit which allows it to scramble over surrounding vegetation.

As discussed in a study, Aristolochia tagala is of interest to researchers because of its traditional uses for treating fever. The anti-inflammatory activity of the petroleum ether, ethyl acetate, and ethanol extracts of A. tagala roots were investigated. All extracts exhibited significant anti-inflammatory effects at 200 and 400 mg/kg, p.o, in rats tested with p < 0.001. The ethyl acetate extract inhibited the induction of PGE(2) with an IC50 = 39.1 mg mL(-1) and LTB(4) with an IC50 = 29.5 mg mL(-1). These findings demonstrate that the A. tagala roots have excellent anti-inflammatory activity.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause severe nephrotoxicity, uropathy, renal failure, carcinogenicity, nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain. The presence of aristolochic acid leads to significant damage to the urinary tract and kidneys. This toxic substance is known for its high carcinogenicity, which can result in the development of upper urinary tract cancers. Patients consuming this plant often experience immediate gastrointestinal distress including nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain. Over time, the cumulative damage to the renal system can progress to permanent renal failure.
