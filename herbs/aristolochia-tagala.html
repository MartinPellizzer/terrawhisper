Aristolochia tagala

What's the taxonomical classification of Aristolochia tagala?

Aristolochia tagala belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Piperales and is a member of the family Aristolochiaceae. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Aristolochia and the species tagala.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Aristolochia tagala contains various bioactive compounds including aristolochic acids, alkaloids, flavonoids, and terpenoids. These chemical constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's pharmacological activities such as anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Specific alkaloids found within the roots and leaves contribute to its traditional use in treating fever and skin ailments. The presence of flavonoids also provides antioxidant properties that help mitigate oxidative stress in cellular models. Researchers continue to study these specific molecules to understand their potential therapeutic applications in modern medicine.

What are the therapeutic actions of Aristolochia tagala?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various inflammatory conditions, skin ailments, and gastrointestinal disorders such as diarrhea or dysentery. It is frequently applied topically to soothe skin irritations, wounds, and fungal infections. In traditional medicine, the roots and leaves are often decocted to address respiratory issues and fever. Some practitioners also utilize it to alleviate pain associated with rheumatism and joint swelling. The plant's medicinal properties are primarily derived from its specific chemical compounds found within the stems and roots.
