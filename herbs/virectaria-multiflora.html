Virectaria multiflora

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Virectaria multiflora has a woody, perennial stem structure characterized by dense, branching clusters of small, actinomorphic flowers with multiple petals. The leaves are typically lanceolate in shape with entire margins and a slightly leathery texture. Each inflorescence consists of numerous individual florets arranged in compact cymes to maximize pollination potential. The root system is fibrous and deep, providing stability in its native soil types. Small, globular fruit develops following successful fertilization of the many flowers.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, spanning across parts of northern India, Nepal, and Bhutan. It typically thrives in montane forests and subalpine meadows situated between 2,500 and 4,000 meters above sea level. The species prefers well-drained soils found along rocky slopes and mountain edges. Seasonal snow cover plays a significant role in its habitat by providing essential moisture during the growing season. Due to its specific elevation requirements, its distribution remains restricted to these distinct alpine zones.
