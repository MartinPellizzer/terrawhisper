10 Possible Side Effects Of Nymphaea Alba

Nymphaea alba has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, skin irritation, and allergic reactions.

These side effects can occur due to improper dosing, allergic sensitivity, or interactions with other medications.

For instance, individuals with pre-existing kidney or liver conditions may experience worsened symptoms, while those with sensitive skin may experience redness and itching, potentially interfering with daily activities and causing discomfort.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Nymphaea alba causes allergic reactions.

This is due to the presence of allergenic compounds such as sesquiterpenoids and alkaloids in its extracts.

The contact with these substances can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms like redness, itching, and swelling of the skin, as well as respiratory issues such as coughing and sneezing.

2. Triggers stomach upset

Nymphaea alba triggers stomach upset due to its ability to alter digestive processes and potentially irritate the gastric lining.

The saponins present in this plant can cause an increase in gut motility, leading to nausea and discomfort. Additionally, Nymphaea alba's flavonoids may interact with the body's natural pH levels, further contributing to stomach upset.

These compounds work together to disrupt normal digestive functions, resulting in uncomfortable symptoms for some individuals using the herb.

3. Induces drowsiness suddenly

Nymphaea alba induces drowsiness suddenly due to its sedative properties.

The plant contains alkaloids, such as nuciferine and apotheinoside, which have been shown to have a calming effect on the nervous system. These compounds can cause relaxation and reduced mental alertness, leading to feelings of sleepiness or fatigue.

As a result, users may experience sudden onset drowsiness, particularly when taking high doses or combining it with other sedative substances.

4. Increases blood pressure rapidly

Nymphaea alba increases blood pressure rapidly due to its ability to stimulate the sympathetic nervous system.

This is thought to be caused by the presence of alkaloids such as nupharine and nux vomica, which can induce a rise in heart rate and blood pressure.

Additionally, the herb's beta-adrenergic properties may also contribute to its hypertensive effects, making it essential for individuals with pre-existing hypertension or cardiovascular conditions to exercise caution when using Nymphaea alba.

5. Provokes respiratory problems

Nymphaea alba provokes respiratory problems due to its containing alkaloids that can cause allergic reactions and irritation in the mucous membranes of the respiratory tract.

The consumption or use of Nymphaea alba may lead to symptoms such as coughing, wheezing, shortness of breath, and constriction of the airways.

These issues are attributed to the plant's ability to stimulate an immune response, resulting in inflammation and increased mucus production in the lungs.

6. Affects menstrual cycles irregularly

Nymphaea alba affects menstrual cycles irregularly.

This is due to its ability to influence hormone levels in the body, particularly estrogen and progesterone. The plant's active compounds, such as alkaloids and flavonoids, can bind to hormone receptors, disrupting the normal balance of hormones that regulate menstruation.

This disruption can lead to changes in menstrual frequency, duration, or consistency, causing irregularities in the menstrual cycle.

7. Interferes with liver function

Nymphaea alba interferes with liver function due to its potential to cause damage to liver cells and disrupt normal metabolic processes.

The plant's active compounds, including alkaloids and glycosides, may affect the liver's ability to detoxify substances, leading to an accumulation of toxic metabolites that can harm liver tissue.

This interference with liver function can result in a range of adverse effects, including nausea, fatigue, and abdominal pain.

8. Reduces bone density severely

Nymphaea alba reduces bone density severely due to its high content of phytoestrogens, which can mimic the effects of estrogen in the body.

This can lead to a decrease in the production of bone-forming cells and an increase in the breakdown of existing bone tissue, potentially resulting in osteoporosis or other bone-related issues.

Additionally, Nymphaea alba has been shown to inhibit the activity of enzymes involved in bone metabolism, further contributing to its negative impact on bone health.

9. Disrupts thyroid hormone production

Nymphaea alba disrupts thyroid hormone production due to its potential interaction with certain enzymes involved in thyroid metabolism.

The plant's bioactive compounds, such as flavonoids and alkaloids, may inhibit the activity of these enzymes, leading to altered levels of thyroid hormones in the body.

This disruption can have a range of effects on the individual, from mild symptoms such as fatigue and weight changes to more severe conditions like hypothyroidism or hyperthyroidism.

10. Causes skin rash appearance

Nymphaea alba causes skin rash appearance.

This is often due to an allergic reaction to its chemical compounds, particularly alkaloids and glycosides, which can cause an inflammatory response in some individuals.

Additionally, the plant's saponins may contribute to this effect by disrupting the skin's natural barrier function, leading to increased permeability and the development of a rash.
