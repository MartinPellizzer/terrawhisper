White Water Lily (Nymphaea alba) Compounds

The primary compounds in White Water Lily include flavonoids, which act as antioxidants, and tannins, which have astringent properties. Phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory effects, while coumarin may have anticoagulant properties. Glycosides and saponins support detoxification, and anthocyanins provide color and antioxidant benefits. Terpenoids, essential oils, and alkaloids also contribute to its therapeutic potential.

Flavonoids in White Water Lily (Nymphaea alba) contribute to its medicinal properties.
