Seriphidium terrae-albae has a woody, perennial shrubby habit characterized by erect stems, small lanceolate leaves, and clusters of small white flowers. Its flowering structures consist of compact inflorescences that emerge from the axils of the branches. The plant also develops a robust root system designed to anchor itself in saline or sandy soils.
