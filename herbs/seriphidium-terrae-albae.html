Seriphidium terrae-albae belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. This plant is further categorized into the family Asteraceae, which is a large and diverse group of flowering plants. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Seriphiodium, and its specific identification is completed by its species name, terrae-albae.

Such studies aim to further validate the therapeutic potential of the species in modern medicine.

According to a study published by "Fitoterapia", three new monoterpene compounds, (5S, 8S)-5-(2E-butenyl)-8-methyl propionate-cyclopentanone (1), 1-Oxy, 10-keto-α-myrcene hydroxide (2), and (3R,4R)-3-hydroxy-4-isobutenyl-cyclopentyl ester (3), were isolated from Seriphiidum terrae-albae. Along with these, eleven known small molecular compounds including monoterpene (1-7, 14), coumarin (10), and other small molecular compounds (8, 9, 11-13) were also isolated. Testing showed that 9 compounds inhibited NO secretion by mouse macrophage Raw 264.7. Specifically, compound 1 had an IC50 value of 9.56 ± 0.66 μM, which was relatively close to the positive control drug Andrographolide (AG) at 2.70 ± 0.39 μM. These compounds exerted anti-inflamatory effects through the JAK/STAT and NF-κB signaling pathways.
