Seriphidium terrae-albae

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Seriphidium terrae-albae has a woody, perennial shrubby habit characterized by erect stems, small lanceolate leaves, and clusters of small white flowers. The foliage is typically sparse and adapted to arid environments, featuring narrow leaf blades that minimize water loss. Its flowering structures consist of compact inflorescences that emerge from the axils of the branches. The plant also develops a robust root system designed to anchor itself in saline or sandy soils. These morphological adaptations allow the species to thrive in its specific ecological niche.
