Seriphidium terrae-albae

What's the taxonomical classification of Seriphidium terrae-albae?

Seriphidium terrae-albae belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. This plant is further categorized into the family Asteraceae, which is a large and diverse group of flowering plants. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Seriphiodium, and its specific identification is completed by its species name, terrae-albae.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Seriphidium terrae-albae can cause gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, and potential allergic reactions if handled or ingested improperly. Users may experience nausea, stomach cramping, or diarrhea following consumption. Topical contact with the plant's juices can lead to redness, itching, or localized dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Some people might also exhibit respiratory irritation if the pollen or fine particles are inhaled. It is essential to monitor for any adverse physiological responses when interacting with this species.
