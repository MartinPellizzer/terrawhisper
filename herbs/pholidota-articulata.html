Pholidota articulata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pholidota articulata has a creeping rhizome with small, scale-like leaves and a cluster of fleshy, cylindrical, yellowish-brown to brownish fruits. The plant typically produces small, translucent, or pale yellowish mushrooms with a cap that is often convex or flattened. The gills are closely spaced and pale, while the stem is relatively thin and often displays a slightly fibrous texture. These fungi are often found growing on decaying wood in moist, shaded forest environments. Its overall structure is compact, reflecting its specialized niche in forest ecosystems.
