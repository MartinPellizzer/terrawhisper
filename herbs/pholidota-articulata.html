What's the taxonomical classification of Pholidota articulata?

Pholidota articulata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized under the order Asparagales. This plant is a member of the family Orchidaceae, placing it within the diverse group of orchids, and is specifically identified by its genus, Pholidota, and its species name, articulata.

The gills are closely spaced and pale, while the stem is relatively thin and often displays a slightly fibrous texture. What are the pharmacological activities of Pholidota articulata?

This plant has significant fracture healing properties and potent osteogenic activity that promotes the formation and differentiation of bone cells. Research indicates that its chemical constituents stimulate the proliferation of osteoblasts to accelerate the repair of skeletal injuries. These biological mechanisms facilitate the mineralization of bone tissue, which is essential for restoring structural integrity after a break. By modulating specific signaling pathways, the plant enhances the overall regenerative capacity of the bone environment. Such pharmacological effects make it a subject of interest for developing natural treatments for bone-related disorders.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", the ethanolic extract of Pholidota articulata Lindley (PA) exhibits significant restoration of trabecular micro-architecture in both femoral and tibial bones. In ovariectomized estrogen deficient mice, treatment with the PA extract led to better bone quality and was devoid of any uterine estrogenicity. The isolated compound oxoflavidin from the plant enhanced alkaline phosphatase (ALP) activity, mineral nodule formation, and mRNA levels of osteogenic markers including BMP-2, Type 1 Collagen, RUNX-2, and osteocalcin. These findings suggest that the ethanolic extract of PA and its pure compound oxoflavidin possess fracture healing properties. Consequently, the extract and oxoflavidin show a strong therapeutic potential for the treatment and management of postmenopausal osteoporosis.
