Lovoa trichilioides

What's the taxonomical classification of Lovoa trichilioides?

Lovoa trichilioides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed under the order Sapindales. This plant is a representative of the family Meliaceae, falling under the genus Lovoa, with the specific species designation trichilioides.

What are the therapeutic actions of Lovoa trichilioides?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat stomach aches, intestinal worms, skin infections, and various types of fever. The leaves and bark are often prepared as decoctions or infusions to alleviate gastrointestinal distress and expel parasites from the body. When applied topically, its extracts can help soothe inflammation and clear up minor skin irritations or sores. Local medicinal practices also rely on the plant to help reduce body temperature during febrile episodes. These diverse applications demonstrate the plant's importance in traditional healing systems within its native regions.
