Ficus sarmentosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Ficus sarmentosa?

Ficus sarmentosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant falls under the order Rosales and is a member of the family Moraceae. Ultimately, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement in the genus Ficus and its designation as the species sarmentosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ficus sarmentoosa has a woody, climbing or scrambling growth habit characterized by slender, twining stems and elliptical, leathery leaves with entire margins. The foliage typically displays a dark green color on the upper surface and a slightly paler underside. Its flowers are inconspicuous and produced in the axils of the leaves, eventually developing into small, globose figs. These fruits transition from green to a ripe color, containing numerous tiny seeds. The overall structure allows the plant to weave through surrounding vegetation in its natural forest habitat.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, specifically occurring in countries such as Thailand, Vietnam, Malaysia, and Indonesia. It thrives primarily in lowland rainforest environments and seasonal monsoon forests where humidity remains high. The species is often found growing as an epiphyte or hemiepiphyte on larger trees within these dense jungle canopies. Its distribution is closely tied to the presence of warm climates and significant annual rainfall patterns across the Indomalayan realm.
