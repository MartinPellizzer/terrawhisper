What to know about Sambucus ebulus (dwarf elder) before using it medicinally

Dwarf elder, scientifically known as Sambucus ebulus, is a herb that has been revered for its impressive health properties, boasting anti-inflammatory and antioxidant qualities that can help to alleviate symptoms of digestive issues, respiratory problems, and even skin conditions.

In addition to its medicinal uses, dwarf elder is also a popular ingredient in many culinary traditions, often used to add flavor and depth to soups, stews, and salads. As an ornamental plant, dwarf elder is also prized for its compact growth habit and delicate white flowers, making it a favorite among gardeners and horticulturists. Botanically speaking, dwarf elder belongs to the Adoxaceae family and features a unique, sprawling habit with small, fragrant leaves and berries that are highly toxic if ingested.

With ancient roots in traditional European folklore, dwarf elder has been documented in historical records as a symbol of purification, protection, and spiritual growth, further solidifying its place in the world of herbalism and botany.

What are the side effects of Sambucus ebulus when used improperly?

When used improperly, Sambucus ebulus increases the chances of experiencing side effects, such as allergic reactions, respiratory issues, and gastrointestinal problems.

Improper use can lead to an overexposure to the plant's active compounds, particularly its saponins and flavonoids, which can cause symptoms like hives, itching, and rashes. Additionally, the improper preparation or consumption of Sambucus ebulus may also lead to respiratory issues, such as bronchospasm, coughing, and shortness of breath, especially in individuals with pre-existing conditions like asthma. Furthermore, gastrointestinal side effects can occur, including nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and stomach cramps, potentially leading to dehydration and electrolyte imbalances.

In extreme cases, improper use of Sambucus ebulus can also cause anaphylaxis, a severe and life-threatening allergic reaction that requires immediate medical attention.
