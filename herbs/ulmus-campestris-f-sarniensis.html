Ulmus campestris f. sarniensis

Ulmus campestris f. sarniensis, also known as the Jersey Elm or Sarnian Elm, is a subspecies of the common elm tree (Ulmus campestris).

It is a deciduous tree native to the Channel Islands, particularly Jersey, where it is highly valued for its medicinal properties.

The bark and leaves of Ulmus campestris f. sarniensis contain tannins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which exhibit antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties.
