What's the taxonomical classification of Onosma paniculata?

Onosma paniculata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent further, the plant is placed in the order Boraginales and the family Boraginaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Onosma, and its species designation, paniculata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Onosma paniculata has erect, unbranched or sparsely branched stems that reach heights of 20 to 50 centimeters and are covered in fine, spreading hairs. The leaves are primarily basal and lanceolate in shape, while the cauline leaves are smaller, opposite, and more narrowly linear. Its inflorescence is a dense, terminal panicle composed of numerous small, funnel-shaped flowers. These flowers typically display a vibrant pink to purplish hue and possess long, protruding stamens. The plant also features a woody taproot that helps it anchor firmly in its preferred rocky habitats.
