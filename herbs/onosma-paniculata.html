Onosma paniculata

What's the taxonomical classification of Onosma paniculata?

Onosma paniculata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent further, the plant is placed in the order Boraginales and the family Boraginaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Onosma, and its species designation, paniculata.

What are the herbal preparations of Onosma paniculata?

Onosma paniculata is prepared primarily as a dried herb for infusions, decoctions, or tinctures to utilize its medicinal properties. The dried aerial parts of the plant are steeped in hot water to create a therapeutic tea. For a more concentrated dose, the plant material can be macerated in alcohol to produce a potent tincture. Some traditional applications also involve using the powdered form of the dried plant in topical preparations. These various methods allow for the extraction of the specific phytochemicals contained within the species.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Onosma paniculata can cause skin irritation or allergic contact dermatitis when the plant comes into direct contact with sensitive human tissue. Some individuals may experience redness, itching, or a localized rash upon touching the leaves or stems. While specific systemic toxicity is not widely documented in human clinical studies, caution is advised regarding ingestion. Allergic reactions might also occur if the pollen is inhaled by hypersensitive people. It is always best to handle the plant with gloves to minimize any potential dermatological discomfort.
