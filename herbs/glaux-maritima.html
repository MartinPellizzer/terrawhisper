Glaux maritima

What's the taxonomical classification of Glaux maritima?

Glaux maritima belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Ericales and the family Primulaceae, eventually reaching its specific designation within the genus Glaux and the species maritima.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Glaux maritima contains significant concentrations of vitamin C, vitamin A, and various essential fatty acids that contribute to its nutritional and medicinal value. The plant is a rich source of antioxidants which help mitigate oxidative stress within the human body. It also provides minerals such as calcium and magnesium that support overall physiological health. Additionally, the presence of specific phytochemicals suggests potential anti-inflammatory properties. These bioactive compounds make the sea purslane a functional food with therapeutic benefits.

What are the therapeutic actions of Glaux maritima?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin ailments such as eczema, psoriasis, and various inflammatory dermatological conditions. The leaves contain specific medicinal compounds that possess soothing and healing properties for irritated tissues. Historically, herbalists applied the plant to soothe burns and reduce swelling on the skin's surface. These therapeutic effects are attributed to its unique chemical makeup found in coastal environments. It remains a subject of interest for those studying traditional maritime botanical remedies.
