What is Launaea sarmentosa?

Launaea sarmentosa is a species of flowering plant that belongs to the family Asteraceae.

It is an annual herb with a slender stem, typically 10-60 cm in height, and a spread of 10-30 cm, bearing linear or linear-lanceolate leaves that are 2-10 cm long and 0.5-2 cm wide, with a pilose or villous base. The plant produces numerous small, yellow or yellowish flowers in a capitulum, which is a dense cluster of small flowers, and the capitulum is surrounded by a ring of bracts. Launaea sarmentosa is native to Africa and the Middle East, and it grows in well-drained soils in arid or semi-arid regions.

The plant's fruits are small, dry achenes, each containing a single seed, and they are dispersed by wind.
