10 Possible Side Effects Of Peucedanum Ostruthium

Peucedanum ostruthium has some side effects when used improperly, such as allergic reactions, digestive issues, and interactions with medications.

These side effects can occur due to excessive dosages, individual sensitivities, or concurrent use with other substances.

For instance, an allergic reaction can cause itching, hives, and difficulty breathing, significantly impacting daily life, while interactions with medications can lead to adverse reactions, potentially worsening symptoms of underlying health conditions.

1. Irritates skin strongly

Peucedanum ostruthium irritates skin strongly.

This is due to its high concentration of sesquiterpene lactones and other bioactive compounds, which can cause allergic reactions, contact dermatitis, or even phototoxicity when applied topically.

These irritating compounds can penetrate the skin, triggering an inflammatory response and resulting in symptoms such as redness, itching, and blistering.

2. Causes allergic reactions

Peucedanum ostruthium causes allergic reactions due to the presence of volatile oils and alkaloids in its composition.

These compounds can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling, especially in individuals with a history of allergies or sensitive skin.

The allergenic potential of Peucedanum ostruthium is attributed to its unique chemical makeup, which can interact with the body's natural defenses, causing adverse reactions in susceptible individuals.

3. Triggers seizures suddenly

Peucedanum ostruthium triggers seizures suddenly due to its high concentration of falcarinol and other sesquiterpenes.

These compounds have been shown to affect the brain's neurotransmitters, leading to changes in electrical activity that can precipitate a seizure.

Additionally, the herb's stimulant properties may further contribute to this risk, particularly in individuals with a history of seizure disorders or those taking medications that lower the seizure threshold.

4. Induces respiratory distress

Peucedanum ostruthium induces respiratory distress due to its expectorant properties, which can stimulate the lungs and airways.

This may cause symptoms such as coughing, wheezing, and shortness of breath.

The herb's active compounds, including apiol and falcarinol, may also irritate the mucous membranes in the respiratory tract, leading to inflammation and constriction of the bronchial tubes.

5. Increases blood pressure

Peucedanum ostruthium increases blood pressure due to its inherent properties of being a stimulant and vasopressor.

The herb contains compounds such as sesquiterpenes and flavonoids that can cause blood vessels to constrict, leading to an increase in blood pressure.

Additionally, the stimulant effects of masterwort may also contribute to the rise in blood pressure by increasing the body's overall stress response, which can further elevate blood pressure levels.

6. Worsens kidney function

Peucedanum ostruthium worsens kidney function due to its ability to inhibit the activity of certain enzymes responsible for filtering waste products in the kidneys.

The herb's bioactive compounds, such as falcarinol and sesquiterpenes, may interfere with normal kidney function, leading to a reduction in glomerular filtration rate and increased risk of kidney damage over time.

This side effect is thought to occur due to the herb's ability to alter the structure and function of kidney cells, potentially compromising their ability to effectively remove waste products from the blood.

7. Interferes with thyroid hormones

Peucedanum ostruthium interferes with thyroid hormones by containing a compound called falcarinol, which can disrupt the body's natural thyroid function.

This is because falcarinol has been shown to inhibit the activity of enzymes involved in thyroid hormone synthesis, leading to reduced levels of thyroxine (T4) and triiodothyronine (T3).

As a result, individuals may experience symptoms such as fatigue, weight gain, dry skin, and hair loss.

8. Affects liver enzymes

Peucedanum ostruthium affects liver enzymes by inhibiting their activity, which can lead to changes in the metabolism and elimination of substances from the body.

This is due to the presence of furano-coumarins, a class of compounds found in the plant that interact with the cytochrome P450 enzyme system in the liver.

As a result, people using Peucedanum ostruthium may experience altered liver function, potentially impacting the efficacy or toxicity of concomitantly administered medications.

9. Reduces bone density

Peucedanum ostruthium reduces bone density due to its high levels of flavonoids and phenolic compounds.

These bioactive compounds may interact with calcium and vitamin D, essential for bone health, thereby inhibiting their absorption and leading to reduced bone mass and density over time.

This can increase the risk of osteoporosis, particularly in individuals who already have a predisposition to bone loss.

10. Enhances bleeding risk

Peucedanum ostruthium enhances bleeding risk due to its potential to interact with anticoagulant and antiplatelet medications, which can increase the likelihood of uncontrolled bleeding.

This is because Peucedanum ostruthium contains compounds that may amplify the effects of these medications, thereby increasing the risk of excessive bleeding or hemorrhaging.
