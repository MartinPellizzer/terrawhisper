Dendrobium chrysanthum

What's the taxonomical classification of Dendrobium chrysanthum?

Dendrobium chrysanthum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further organized into the order Asparagales. As a member of the Orchidaceae family, it is specifically categorized under the genus Dendrobium, with its unique scientific identity defined by the species name chrysanthum.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Dendrobium chrysanthum contains bioactive alkaloids, polysaccharides, steroids, and various organic acids that provide its therapeutic properties. These complex carbohydrates are particularly valued for their ability to modulate the immune system and reduce inflammation. The specific alkaloid profile of the plant contributes to its traditional use in treating fever and improving respiratory function. Additionally, the presence of phenolic compounds offers significant antioxidant protection against cellular oxidative stress. These combined chemical constituents make the orchid a subject of interest for developing modern pharmacological treatments.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat coughs, fever, inflammation, and various types of digestive disorders. Practitioners of traditional medicine often utilize the orchid to alleviate symptoms of respiratory congestion and lung heat. It is frequently prescribed to reduce swelling and calm internal inflammation within the body. Additionally, the plant serves as a remedy for clearing heat and promoting fluid production to combat dehydration. These medicinal properties make it a significant component in various herbal formulations.
