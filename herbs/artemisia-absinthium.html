What to know about wormwood before using it medicinally

Wormwood, scientifically known as Artemisia absinthium, is a herb with numerous health benefits.

It has been used in traditional medicine for centuries to improve digestion, alleviate fever, and reduce inflammation. In addition to its medicinal uses, wormwood is also used in culinary applications. It is commonly used as a flavoring agent in alcoholic beverages, such as absinthe, and is also used to add a bitter flavor to some dishes.

From a horticultural perspective, wormwood is a hardy perennial herb that is easy to grow and requires minimal maintenance. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and it can be propagated through division or cuttings. Botanically, wormwood is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-green leaves and small yellow flowers.

Historically, wormwood has been referenced in literature and folklore for its psychoactive properties and has been associated with everything from witchcraft to absinthe-induced hallucinations.
