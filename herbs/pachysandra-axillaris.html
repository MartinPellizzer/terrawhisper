    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Pachysandra axillaris can cause skin irritation, digestive upset, and potential toxicity if ingested by humans or pets. Contact with the plant's foliage or sap may trigger contact dermatitis, resulting in redness, itching, or a localized rash on sensitive skin. If any part of the plant is swallowed, individuals might experience nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, the presence of certain alkaloids in similar species suggests caution is necessary. It is important to handle the plant with gloves and keep it out of reach of curious animals to prevent accidental poisoning.
