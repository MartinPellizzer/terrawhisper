Engelhardia serrata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Engelhardia serrata has deciduous, alternate, simple leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptical in shape with sharply serrated margins and prominent pinnate venation. The leaf blades typically measure between five and fifteen centimeters in length and possess a long, slender petiole. Its woody structure consists of a sturdy trunk with grayish-brown bark that often becomes fissured with age. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in catkin-like structures that hang from the branches. These catkins eventually develop into winged samaras which facilitate wind dispersal of the seeds.
