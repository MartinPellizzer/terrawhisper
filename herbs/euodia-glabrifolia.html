Euodia glabrifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Euodia glabrifolia?

Euodia glabrifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further organized into the order Sapindales. It is a member of the family Rutaceae, specifically falling under the genus Euodia, and is identified by its unique species name, glabrifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Euodia glabrifolia has opposite, simple, elliptical to lanceolate leaves that measure between 5 and 15 centimeters in length and possess entire margins with glabrous surfaces. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub with smooth, greyish bark and a spreading crown. Its reproductive structures consist of small, fragrant white flowers that emerge in axillary cymes. These flowers develop into small, fleshy drupes that transition from green to a reddish or orange hue upon maturity. The overall architecture of the plant is characterized by its compact branching pattern and dense foliage.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, specifically spanning across parts of China, Vietnam, Laos, Thailand, and Malaysia. It thrives primarily in moist, lowland forests and along riverbanks where humidity is consistently high. Within these habitats, it is frequently found at elevations ranging from sea level up to several hundred meters. Its distribution is closely tied to the seasonal monsoon patterns that characterize these equatorial and sub-tropical climates. Consequently, the species is most abundant in areas that provide stable, warm temperatures throughout the year.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and localized swelling if its oils or sap come into direct contact with sensitive human tissue. Individuals with pre-existing skin sensitivities may experience redness, itching, or a burning sensation upon exposure to the leaves. While systemic toxicity in humans is not extensively documented in clinical literature, ingestion of concentrated extracts should be avoided to prevent potential gastrointestinal distress. Some users might also experience respiratory irritation if the volatile oils are inhaled in high concentrations. Always conduct a patch test before using any part of the plant for topical applications to ensure safety.
