Derris marginata

What's the taxonomical classification of Derris marginata?

Derris marginata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Fabales. It is situated within the family Fabaceae and is identified under the genus Derris, specifically designated as the species marginata.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Derris marginata contains high concentrations of rotenone, a potent non-protein amino acid used historically as an insecticide and piscicide. This specific compound works by inhibiting the mitochondrial electron transport chain, effectively disrupting cellular respiration in target organisms. Beyond its insecticidal properties, the plant's roots and extracts have been studied for their potential bioactive secondary metabolites. Some research explores the pharmacological implications of these compounds in relation to neurotoxicity and metabolic regulation. These chemical constituents make the plant a significant subject in both traditional pest control and modern biochemical study.

What are the therapeutic actions of Derris marginata?

Derris marginata has potent insecticidal and antiparasitic properties primarily due to the presence of rotenone in its roots. This natural compound acts by inhibiting the mitochondrial electron transport chain, effectively starving cells of energy and causing death in targeted organisms. It is traditionally used to control various pests in agricultural settings and to treat intestinal worms in humans and livestock. Because of its high toxicity to aquatic life, its application must be carefully managed to prevent environmental damage. The plant serves as a critical biological tool for managing infestations while offering a botanical alternative to synthetic chemicals.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat infestations of lice, fleas, and other ectoparasites through its active chemical compound known as rotenone. This natural insecticide works by inhibiting the cellular respiration of various insects and small organisms. Historically, extracts from the roots have been applied to combat skin parasites and manage agricultural pests. However, the toxic nature of the substance means it must be used with extreme caution to avoid harming humans or beneficial wildlife. While effective against specific pests, its use is increasingly regulated due to potential environmental impacts.

What are the herbal preparations of Derris marginata?

Derris marginata is prepared primarily as a concentrated liquid extract or a fine powder derived from its dried roots to utilize its rotenone content. The most common application involves creating a topical solution used for controlling insect infestations and treating parasitic skin conditions. In many traditional practices, the crushed root is infused into water or oil to create a potent botanical pesticide. Some preparations also involve boiling the plant material to produce a decoction for specific medicinal or agricultural uses. These diverse forms allow the plant's bioactive compounds to be applied effectively to surfaces or biological targets.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Derris marginata can cause severe neurotoxicity, respiratory distress, and death if the rotenone extracted from its roots is ingested or absorbed through the skin. This plant contains high concentrations of rotenone, a potent chemical that disrupts cellular energy production by inhibiting mitochondrial function. In humans, accidental exposure can lead to tremors, loss of muscle coordination, and significant neurological impairment. The toxicity is particularly dangerous because the substance can be absorbed through mucous membranes or broken skin. Careful handling is essential to prevent systemic poisoning from this botanical source.
