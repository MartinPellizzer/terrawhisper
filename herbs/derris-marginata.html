Derris marginata

What's the taxonomical classification of Derris marginata?

Derris marginata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Fabales. It is situated within the family Fabaceae and is identified under the genus Derris, specifically designated as the species marginata.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Derris marginata contains high concentrations of rotenone, a potent non-protein amino acid used historically as an insecticide and piscicide. This specific compound works by inhibiting the mitochondrial electron transport chain, effectively disrupting cellular respiration in target organisms. Beyond its insecticidal properties, the plant's roots and extracts have been studied for their potential bioactive secondary metabolites. Some research explores the pharmacological implications of these compounds in relation to neurotoxicity and metabolic regulation. These chemical constituents make the plant a significant subject in both traditional pest control and modern biochemical study.

What are the therapeutic actions of Derris marginata?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat infestations of lice, fleas, and other ectoparasites through its active chemical compound known as rotenone. This natural insecticide works by inhibiting the cellular respiration of various insects and small organisms. Historically, extracts from the roots have been applied to combat skin parasites and manage agricultural pests. However, the toxic nature of the substance means it must be used with extreme caution to avoid harming humans or beneficial wildlife. While effective against specific pests, its use is increasingly regulated due to potential environmental impacts.
