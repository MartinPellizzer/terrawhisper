Dryopteris barbigera

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dryopteris barbigera has bipinnate-pinnatifid fronds that typically measure between 30 and 70 centimeters in length, characterized by a distinctively hairy or bristly texture on the underside of the pinnae. The stipe is stout and covered in brownish scales, providing a sturdy base for the spreading foliage. Each leaflet features finely serrated margins and a prominent midrib that contributes to its intricate, feathery appearance. The presence of these fine, hair-like structures along the veins and margins is a defining morphological trait of the species. These complex architectural features allow the fern to thrive in shaded, moist forest environments.
