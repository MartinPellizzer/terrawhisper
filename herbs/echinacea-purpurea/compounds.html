Echinacea (Echinacea purpurea) Compounds

Echinacea contains several key compounds, including flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties. Phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory effects. Saponins and glycosides may enhance immune response. Tannins and terpenoids provide additional antimicrobial benefits. Coumarin, essential oils, and anthocyanins also play roles in its therapeutic actions.

Echinacea (Echinacea purpurea) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of compounds found in Echinacea purpurea. They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help support immune function and may reduce oxidative stress in the body. Flavonoids are part of the complex mixture of active components in Echinacea.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Echinacea purpurea, known for its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. It contributes to the plant's immune-modulating effects and is believed to enhance the body's defense mechanisms. This compound is commonly found in the roots and flowers of Echinacea purpurea.

3. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Echinacea purpurea, known for its immune-boosting properties. It exhibits mild antimicrobial effects and may help reduce the duration of colds. Saponin contributes to the plant's traditional use in supporting respiratory and immune health.

4. Glycoside

Glycosides are a class of compounds found in Echinacea purpurea, known for their immune-stimulating properties. These compounds, including echinacoside and others, contribute to the plant's traditional use in supporting immune function and reducing the duration of colds. They work by modulating inflammatory responses and enhancing white blood cell activity.

5. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Echinacea purpurea, contributing to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Found in the roots and leaves, tannins help reduce swelling and support immune function. They also aid in the healing of wounds and may enhance the absorption of other beneficial compounds in Echinacea.

6. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Echinacea purpurea, contributing to its immune-supporting properties. These compounds, including alkamides and glycoproteins, help modulate the immune system and may reduce the duration of colds. They are responsible for many of the plant's therapeutic effects in herbal medicine.

7. Coumarin

Coumarin is a compound found in Echinacea purpurea. It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and anticoagulant properties. Coumarin may support immune function and has been studied for its potential role in reducing oxidative stress. Its presence in Echinacea enhances the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

8. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound in Echinacea purpurea, contributing to its immunostimulant properties. Extracted from the plant's flowers and roots, it contains volatile aromatic compounds that may enhance immune function and reduce inflammation.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Echinacea purpurea. It contributes to the plant's immune-modulating properties. While alkaloids are less prominent than other compounds in Echinacea, they play a role in its traditional use for colds and infections. Their presence supports the plant's overall therapeutic profile.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Echinacea purpurea. It contributes to the plant's vibrant color and possesses antioxidant properties. This compound supports immune function and may reduce inflammation, making it a key component in the plant's traditional use for respiratory and immune support.
