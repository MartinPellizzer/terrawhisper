10 Possible Side Effects Of Echinacea Purpurea (Purple Coneflower)

Echinacea purpurea has some side effects when used improperly, such as allergic reactions, digestive issues, and interactions with other medications.

These side effects are often caused by taking high doses or for extended periods. For example, severe allergic reactions can lead to anaphylaxis, a potentially life-threatening condition.

Additionally, digestive issues like diarrhea, stomach cramps, and nausea can interfere with daily activities and worsen quality of life.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Echinacea purpurea if used imporperly.

1. Aggravates cardiovascular issues

Echinacea purpurea causes allergic reactions in some individuals due to its chemical composition.

The plant contains compounds called alkamides and polyenes, which can stimulate an immune response and trigger an allergic reaction in people sensitive to these substances.

This sensitivity can lead to symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing, although rare and usually mild cases.
