Picea smithiana

What's the taxonomical classification of Picea smithiana?

Picea smithiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its hierarchical lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Pinidae, falling under the order Pinales. Within the family Pinaceae, this plant is situated in the genus Picea, and it is specifically identified by its species name, smithiana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Picea smithiana has a narrow, conical crown with spreading branches and needles that are typically 10 to 25 millimeters long, spreading, and dark green. The needles are relatively soft and possess a distinctively blunt or rounded tip compared to other spruce species. Its bark is thin and scaly, transitioning from reddish-brown to gray as the tree matures. The cones are pendulous, cylindrical, and measure approximately 3 to 6 centimeters in length. These specialized morphological traits allow the tree to thrive in high-altitude, subalpine environments.
