10 Medicinal Constituents Of Ficus Benghalensis (Banyan)

Ficus benghalensis has active constituents such as flavonoids, tannins, and alkaloids.

These constituents are responsible for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects. The flavonoids in banyan help reduce fever and alleviate respiratory issues, while the tannins exhibit astringent properties, treating diarrhea and bleeding disorders.

The alkaloids in banyan have been shown to improve cardiovascular health and reduce stress levels, leading to a better quality of life.

1. Alphitolic acid

Ficus benghalensis alphitolic acid is a bioactive compound extracted from its bark and leaves.

This acidic triterpenoid has been studied for its various pharmacological properties, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and anticancer activities.

Alphitolic acid has been shown to exhibit protective effects against oxidative stress, neurodegenerative diseases, and cardiovascular disorders, making it a promising natural product for the development of new therapeutic agents.

2. Mangiferin

Ficus benghalensis mangiferin is a bioactive compound derived from the bark of this fig tree species.

It has been found to possess antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, making it a potential therapeutic agent for various diseases. Mangiferin has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine to treat conditions such as fever, inflammation, and digestive disorders.

Its unique bioactive profile has sparked significant interest in its applications in modern medicine.

3. Quercetin

Ficus benghalensis quercetin is a bioflavonoid compound found in its leaves.

Quercetin has been shown to exhibit numerous pharmacological activities, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. It has been reported to possess potential therapeutic effects on various diseases, such as cardiovascular disorders, diabetes, and certain types of cancer.

Additionally, quercetin has been found to have antimicrobial and antiviral activity, making it a promising compound for the development of novel medicines.

4. Kaempferol

Ficus benghalensis kaempferol is a flavonoid compound isolated from the leaves of the plant.

It has been reported to possess various biological activities, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antiviral properties. Kaempferol has also been found to exhibit cytotoxicity against certain cancer cell lines, making it a potential candidate for cancer therapy.

Additionally, it has been shown to have protective effects on cardiovascular health by inhibiting lipid peroxidation and improving blood vessel function.

5. Fisetin

Ficus benghalensis fisetin is a unique compound extracted from its leaves.

It has been extensively studied for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties. Fisetin has shown promise in preventing age-related diseases such as Alzheimer's and cancer, as well as protecting against neurodegeneration and oxidative stress.

Its potential uses are vast, making it a valuable compound for further research and development.

6. Ursolic acid

Ficus benghalensis ursolic acid is a triterpenoid compound extracted from its leaves.

It has been studied for its potential medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant activities. Ursolic acid has been shown to have inhibitory effects on cancer cell growth and induce apoptosis (cell death) in various types of cancer cells.

Additionally, it may help protect against neurodegenerative diseases such as Alzheimer's and Parkinson's.

7. Oleanolic acid

Ficus benghalensis oleanolic acid is a triterpenoid compound extracted from the bark and leaves of this tree species.

It has been extensively studied for its potential therapeutic properties, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antibacterial activities.

Oleanolic acid has been shown to exhibit cytotoxic effects against various types of cancer cells, making it a promising candidate for the development of new anticancer drugs.

8. Β-sitosterol

Ficus benghalensis β-sitosterol is a phytoconstituent derived from its bark and leaves.

This bioactive compound has been extensively researched for its numerous health benefits. β-Sitosterol has been shown to possess anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, making it effective in treating various ailments such as arthritis, high cholesterol, and infections.

Its potential uses extend beyond medicine, with applications in cosmetics and pharmaceutical industries due to its moisturizing, skin-soothing, and wound-healing properties.

9. Gallic acid

Ficus benghalensis gallic acid is a naturally occurring compound extracted from the bark of this fig species.

It has been extensively studied for its potential medicinal and biological properties, including antioxidant and antimicrobial activities. Gallic acid has been reported to exhibit anti-inflammatory effects, inhibit cancer cell growth, and show promise in treating various diseases such as diabetes, cardiovascular disorders, and neurodegenerative conditions.

Its unique chemical structure makes it an attractive candidate for further research and development.

10. Protocatechuic acid

Ficus benghalensis protocatechuic acid is a potent antioxidant compound isolated from its leaves.

This flavonoid has been found to exhibit numerous biological activities, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, as well as antioxidant potential.

Its presence has been linked to the plant's ability to protect against oxidative stress and may contribute to its traditional use in Ayurvedic medicine for treating various diseases.
