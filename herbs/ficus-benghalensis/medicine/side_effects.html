1. Causes allergic reactions

Ficus benghalensis causes allergic reactions due to the presence of certain compounds like chitinase and ficin in its sap.

When these compounds come into contact with human skin or mucous membranes, they can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms such as redness, itching, and swelling.

The high concentration of these allergens in Ficus benghalensis sap makes it a common allergen among people who handle the plant or touch its leaves.

2. Triggers asthma attacks

Ficus benghalensis triggers asthma attacks due to its high pollen count and the presence of certain proteins that can cause allergic reactions in susceptible individuals.

The pollen from Ficus benghalensis is released into the air during flowering, which typically occurs between February and April, and can exacerbate existing respiratory issues or trigger new episodes of asthma.

This reaction can lead to wheezing, coughing, shortness of breath, and chest tightness.

3. Induces nausea and vomiting

Ficus benghalensis induces nausea and vomiting due to its unique chemical composition, which includes compounds like ficin and saponins.

These chemicals can stimulate the stomach lining, leading to an imbalance in gut flora and altering digestion patterns, resulting in unpleasant symptoms such as nausea and vomiting.

The plant's natural defense mechanisms may also contribute to these adverse effects, making it essential for individuals to be aware of potential reactions when using Ficus benghalensis.

4. Increases heart rate rapidly

Ficus benghalensis increases heart rate rapidly due to its high concentration of caffeine-like compounds, specifically epinephrine and norepinephrine.

These stimulants can bind to adrenergic receptors in the body, causing a rapid increase in heart rate and blood pressure.

This response is often seen when the plant's sap or extracts are ingested or used topically, leading to an intense physical sensation that may be uncomfortable for some individuals.

5. Lowers blood pressure severely

Ficus benghalensis lowers blood pressure severely.

This is due to its high content of flavonoids and phenolic acids, which are potent vasodilators that relax blood vessels, reducing peripheral resistance and cardiac output.

Additionally, the plant's extracts have been shown to inhibit angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE), a key enzyme involved in blood pressure regulation, leading to a further decline in blood pressure.

6. Interferes with medications

Ficus benghalensis interferes with medications by altering the way they are metabolized in the body.

The plant's active compounds, such as flavonoids and phenolic acids, can bind to proteins in the liver and kidneys, reducing the effectiveness of certain medications.

Additionally, Ficus benghalensis may stimulate enzymes that break down medications too quickly, leading to reduced concentrations in the blood and decreased therapeutic effects.

7. Affects fertility significantly

Ficus benghalensis affects fertility significantly due to its high concentration of alkaloids and flavonoids.

These compounds can interfere with hormone production and disrupt the balance of reproductive hormones in the body, leading to issues such as decreased sperm count, low testosterone levels, and irregular menstrual cycles.

Additionally, Ficus benghalensis may also stimulate the release of stress hormones, which can further complicate fertility issues by causing hormonal imbalances and disruptions in the body's natural rhythms.

8. Disrupts hormone balance

Ficus benghalensis disrupts hormone balance due to its phytoestrogens and estrogenic compounds.

These chemicals mimic the effects of estrogen in the body, potentially altering the production of hormones such as testosterone and thyroid hormones. This may lead to changes in mood, sleep patterns, and reproductive function.

As a result, individuals using Ficus benghalensis supplements or consuming large amounts of its fruit may experience hormonal imbalances, which can have long-term effects on overall health.

9. Causes skin rashes frequently

Ficus benghalensis causes skin rashes frequently due to its high concentration of sap and latex.

The sap contains allergenic compounds that can trigger an immune response in some individuals, leading to skin irritation and rash formation.

Additionally, the latex present in the plant's sap can cause contact dermatitis, a common allergic reaction that results in itchy, red, and blistered skin.
