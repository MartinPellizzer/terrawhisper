Khaya

Khaya senegalensis

Khaya (Khaya senegalensis) is a tropical tree native to Africa, known for its various medicinal properties and traditional use in treating ailments such as inflammation, skin conditions, and digestive disorders.

Taxonomy

Khaya senegalensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Sapindales, family Meliaceae, genus Khaya, and species Khaya senegalensis.

Morphology

Khaya senegalensis is a deciduous tree characterized by its straight, cylindrical trunk, smooth bark that is light grey to brown in color, and large, pinnate leaves composed of 12-26 pairs of lanceolate to elliptic leaflets.

Distribution

Khaya senegalensis is native to tropical Africa, primarily found in countries such as Senegal, Gambia, Guinea, Mali, Burkina Faso, and Nigeria, with its range extending into parts of the Caribbean and southern Florida.

Traditional Uses

Khaya senegalensis has been traditionally used in various forms of African herbal medicine to treat ailments such as fever, inflammation, digestive issues, and skin conditions, with different parts of the tree—such as the bark, leaves, and roots—being employed for their respective therapeutic properties.

Medicinal Systems

Khaya senegalensis has been traditionally used in African traditional medicine systems, including those of the Yoruba, Igbo, and Fulani peoples, for treating ailments such as inflammation, fever, and skin conditions.

Phytochemistry

Khaya senegalensis contains a variety of bioactive compounds such as alkaloids, flavonoids, tannins, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties and therapeutic applications in traditional herbal medicine.

Pharmacology

Khaya senegalensis exhibits pharmacological properties including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, antispasmodic, and analgesic effects, making it valuable in traditional herbal medicine for treating conditions such as pain, infections, and gastrointestinal disorders.

Botanical Identification

Khaya senegalensis, commonly known as African shea or mukua, is a deciduous tree native to West Africa. Belonging to the Fabaceae family, it is a perennial species with a distinctive trunk and large, palmate leaves. Its flowers are yellow and clustered, while its fruit is a flat pod containing edible seeds. Synonyms include "shea tree" and "mukua" in various regional dialects.

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for treating digestive issues and respiratory infections.

Infusion: Used for treating mild fevers and as a general tonic.

Poultice: Used for treating skin infections and wounds.

Safety Profile

Khaya senegalensis is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. Long-term use or high doses should be avoided, and it is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to potential adverse effects on the fetus or infant.
