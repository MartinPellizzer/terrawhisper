Khaya

Khaya senegalensis

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Khaya senegalensis, commonly known as African shea or mukua, is a deciduous tree native to West Africa. Belonging to the Fabaceae family, it is a perennial species with a distinctive trunk and large, palmate leaves. Its flowers are yellow and clustered, while its fruit is a flat pod containing edible seeds. Synonyms include "shea tree" and "mukua" in various regional dialects.

Active Compounds

Khaya senegalensis contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids. These compounds work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Tannin
  • Phenolic acid

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action
Respiratory Respiratory infection, influenza, respiratory infections, pulmonary inflammation, cough Antimicrobial, antiviral, anti-inflammatory, expectorant
Integumentary Skin infection, wound healing, fungal infection, skin infections, skin irritation Antimicrobial, antioxidant, antifungal, anti-inflammatory
Musculoskeletal Inflammation, arthritis, joint inflammation, muscle pain, joint pain Anti-inflammatory

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for treating digestive issues and respiratory infections.

Infusion: Used for treating mild fevers and as a general tonic.

Poultice: Used for treating skin infections and wounds.

Safety Profile

Khaya senegalensis is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. Long-term use or high doses should be avoided, and it is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to potential adverse effects on the fetus or infant.
