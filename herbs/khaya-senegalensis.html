Khaya (Khaya senegalensis)

Khaya (Khaya senegalensis) is a member of the Meliaceae family, native to West Africa, Sahelian Regions, and Tropical Africa. Traditionally, its bark, leaves, and wood have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, astringent, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in african traditional medicine, european herbal medicine, and mediterranean herbal traditions.
