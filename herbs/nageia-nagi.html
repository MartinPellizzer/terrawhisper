Nageia nagi

What's the taxonomical classification of Nageia nagi?

Nageia nagi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Pinidae, following the order Pinales. As a member of the family Podocarpaceae, it is placed within the genus Nageia, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species nagi.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Nageia nagi contains a variety of bioactive secondary metabolites, most notably complex diterpenes, triterpenoids, and specific phenolic compounds. These chemical constituents are primarily concentrated within the plant's bark and leaf tissues. Research indicates that these organic compounds possess significant antimicrobial and antioxidant properties. Some studies suggest that the specific terpenoid profiles of the species may offer potential anti-inflammatory effects. These medicinal characteristics make the plant a subject of interest for pharmacological investigation into natural defense molecules.

What are the therapeutic actions of Nageia nagi?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin conditions such as eczema, psoriasis, and various dermatological inflammations through its topical application. The needles and bark contain bioactive compounds that possess significant antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. These chemical constituents help reduce redness and swelling when applied to irritated patches of skin. Some traditional practices also suggest its use in managing minor wounds to prevent infection. Research continues into how its specific phytochemical profile interacts with human cellular responses to healing.
