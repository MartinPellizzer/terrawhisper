Nageia nagi

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Nageia nagi has evergreen, linear leaves that are typically 10 to 25 centimeters long, leathery in texture, and possess a distinctively sharp, pungent tip. The foliage is arranged spirally around the woody stems, often appearing densely packed to create a lush canopy. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous male cones and larger, more prominent female cones that develop from ovulate scales. These female cones gradually mature into woody, somewhat globose structures that protect the developing seeds. The overall growth habit is that of a slow-growing tree or large shrub with a conical or irregular crown.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the temperate forests of Japan, specifically found across the islands of Honshu, Shikoku, Kyushu, and Hokkaido. It typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as mountain slopes and ravines. These populations are often located in cool, humid climates that support dense evergreen broadleaf forests. The species remains relatively localized within these specific East Asian regions rather than spreading globally. Its distribution is closely tied to the availability of well-drained, organic-rich soils found in these mountainous habitats.
