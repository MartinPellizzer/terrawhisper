Pseudostellaria heterantha

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pseudostellaria heterantha has a prostrate growth habit characterized by creeping, woody stems that form dense mats near the soil surface. The leaves are small, elliptical to lanceolate in shape, and possess a slightly thickened texture with entire margins. These leaves are arranged oppositely along the stems, often appearing crowded or overlapping in certain sections. The reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that typically emerge from the leaf axils. This morphology allows the plant to effectively colonize open, rocky environments where space is limited.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the coastal regions of the central California coast, ranging from Monterey County southward to San Luis Obispo County. It primarily inhabits maritime scrub and coastal prairie ecosystems where fog provides essential moisture. Populations are often concentrated along specific sandstone outcrops and stabilized dune systems. These localized habitats restrict its spread to narrow strips of land adjacent to the Pacific Ocean.       What are the herbal preparations of Pseudostellaria heterantha?

      Pseudostellaria heterantha is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice derived from its dried aerial parts. The leaves and flowering stems are typically steeped in hot water to create a medicinal tea used for internal consumption. For localized skin irritations, the plant material can be crushed into a moist paste and applied directly to the affected area.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Pseudostellaria heterantha can cause severe contact dermatitis, acute gastrointestinal distress, and mild neurotoxicity if ingested. Skin contact with the plant's sap often leads to intense redness, itching, and blistering that may persist for several days. If swallowed, individuals frequently experience nausea, abdominal cramping, and vomiting shortly after exposure. Some users have reported temporary dizziness or lightheadedness due to the plant's alkaloid content. It is essential to wash any affected skin immediately with soap and water to minimize these reactions.
