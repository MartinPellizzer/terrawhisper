Pseudostellaria heterantha

What's the taxonomical classification of Pseudostellaria heterantha?

Pseudostellaria heterantha belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Caryophyllales. This plant is further categorized into the family Caryophyllaceae, which contains the genus Pseudostellaria, and is specifically identified by the species epithet heterantha.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pseudostellaria heterantha has a prostrate growth habit characterized by creeping, woody stems that form dense mats near the soil surface.

The leaves are small, elliptical to lanceolate in shape, and possess a slightly thickened texture with entire margins. These leaves are arranged oppositely along the stems, often appearing crowded or overlapping in certain sections. The reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that typically emerge from the leaf axils.

This morphology allows the plant to effectively colonize open, rocky environments where space is limited.

        What are the herbal preparations of Pseudostellaria heterantha?

        Pseudostellaria heterantha is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice derived from its dried aerial parts. The leaves and flowering stems are typically steeped in hot water to create a medicinal tea used for internal consumption. For localized skin irritations, the plant material can be crushed into a moist paste and applied directly to the affected area. Some practitioners also distill the essence of the plant to create concentrated tinctures for long-term storage. These various methods allow for the extraction of specific bioactive compounds depending on the intended therapeutic use.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Pseudostellaria heterantha can cause severe contact dermatitis, acute gastrointestinal distress, and mild neurotoxicity if ingested. Skin contact with the plant's sap often leads to intense redness, itching, and blistering that may persist for several days. If swallowed, individuals frequently experience nausea, abdominal cramping, and vomiting shortly after exposure. Some users have reported temporary dizziness or lightheadedness due to the plant's alkaloid content. It is essential to wash any affected skin immediately with soap and water to minimize these reactions.
