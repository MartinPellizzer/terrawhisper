What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stixis suaveolens has woody, climbing stems that form dense, tangled thickets through the use of twining growth habits. The leaves are typically simple, alternate, and possess an elliptical to ovate shape with smooth margins. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves, often presenting in clusters. The reproductive structures eventually produce small, fleshy fruits that serve as a primary dispersal mechanism.
