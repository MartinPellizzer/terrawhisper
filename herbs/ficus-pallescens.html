Possible Side Effects of Ficus pallescens

Ficus pallescens can have side effects if used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, including diarrhea and nausea, due to the presence of toxic compounds like ficin and aglycones.

Ingestion of large amounts of ficus pallescens can also cause allergic reactions, such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.

Prolonged exposure to the plant's sap or leaves may lead to skin irritation and dermatitis, especially in individuals with sensitive skin.

In extreme cases, improper use of ficus pallescens can trigger anaphylaxis, a life-threatening allergic reaction that requires immediate medical attention.
