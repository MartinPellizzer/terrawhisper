What's the taxonomical classification of Juncus castaneus?

Juncus castaneus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Poales and is a member of the family Juncaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Juncus and the species castaneus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Juncus castaneus has cylindrical, unbranched stems that reach heights of up to 30 centimeters and are characterized by a chestnut-brown color. The leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures located at the base of the stems. Each stem terminates in a single, inconspicuous flower cluster that lacks showy petals. These inflorescences consist of small, brownish flowers arranged in a compact manner. The overall structure of the plant is slender and adapted for growth in marshy or damp environments.
