Artemisia macrocephala

What's the taxonomical classification of Artemisia macrocephala?

Artemisia macrocephala belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is placed in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae and is specifically categorized within the genus Artemisia, with its unique biological identity defined by the species name macrocephala.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Artemisia macrocephala has a perennial, woody-based habit characterized by large, broadly ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically grey-green and covered in fine, silvery pubescence. The foliage is often deeply lobed or pinnatifid, providing a textured appearance to the plant's canopy. Its stems are erect and can reach significant heights, often displaying a slightly woody texture at the base as the plant matures. The inflorescences consist of dense, terminal flower heads that emerge from the leafy branches. These capitula are typically yellowish or brownish and are surrounded by specialized bracts.

What are the therapeutic actions of Artemisia macrocephala?

Artemisia macrocephala has therapeutic actions that include being bitter, carminative, stomachic, and anthelmintic. Its bitter properties stimulate the secretion of digestive juices to improve appetite and digestion. As a carminative, the plant helps to relieve intestinal gas and soothe abdominal discomfort. Its stomachic qualities further support the overall function of the digestive system by promoting healthy gastric activity. Additionally, the plant acts as an anthelmintic to help expel parasitic worms from the intestinal tract.
