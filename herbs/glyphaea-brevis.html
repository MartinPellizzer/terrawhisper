Glyphaea brevis

What's the taxonomical classification of Glyphaea brevis?

Glyphaea brevis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Malvales and the family Malvaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Glyphaea, and its unique species designation, brevis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Glyphaea brevis has large, simple, oblong-lanceolate leaves that can reach up to 60 centimeters in length and are characterized by prominent parallel venation. The plant is a large tree that typically grows to heights of 30 meters or more, featuring a straight trunk with smooth, greyish bark. Its inflorescence is a large, terminal panicle that bears small, creamy-white flowers. The fruit is a woody, capsule-like structure that eventually splits to release seeds. This species is easily recognized in its native habitats by its distinct foliage and robust growth habit.
