Botanical Identification of Catigua (Trichilia catigua)

To identify Catigua (Trichilia catigua) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Catigua, or Trichilia catigua, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Sapindales, family Meliaceae, genus Trichilia, and species Trichilia catigua. These classifications help identify its botanical relationships and place it within the broader plant kingdom.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Trichilia catigua is the scientific name for Catigua, first described by the botanist Carl Friedrich von Forster in 1833. It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is also known by several synonyms, including Pseudolmedia catigua and Trichilia glabra. Common names such as Catigua, Caiapó, and Catigua-do-campo are widely used in Brazil and other regions.

Morphological Characteristics

Catigua is a small to medium-sized tree with a straight trunk and a dense, rounded crown. Its root system is fibrous and shallow, adapting well to various soil types. The stem is smooth and grayish-brown, with a sturdy structure. Leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate with a pointed tip, usually measuring 5-10 cm in length. The inflorescence is a panicle, bearing small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered closely together. The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, flat seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Trichilia catigua can be distinguished by its elliptical leaves with smooth margins, yellowish-green flowers clustered in panicles, and fruits that are flat, oblong capsules splitting into three valves. It is often confused with Trichilia emetica, but it has a more aromatic scent and smaller, more pointed leaf tips.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Catigua (Trichilia catigua) is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Paraguay, and Argentina. It thrives in warm, humid climates with abundant rainfall. The plant prefers well-drained, fertile soils and is typically found at altitudes between 300 and 1,200 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Catigua blooms from September to November, producing yellow flowers that attract pollinators. Seed formation occurs from December to February, with the fruits ripening to a dark red color. Harvesting is typically done when the fruits are fully ripe, usually in late winter to early spring, to ensure optimal potency and identification accuracy.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Trichilia catigua belongs to the genus Trichilia, which is part of the family Meliaceae. Its closest relatives include species like Trichilia emetica and Trichilia pallida. These species share similar morphological and genetic traits, such as leaf structure and fruit morphology, which help in their classification within the genus.
