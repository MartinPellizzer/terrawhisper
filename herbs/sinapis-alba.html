White Mustard (Sinapis alba)

White Mustard (Sinapis alba) is a member of the Brassicaceae family, native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. Traditionally, its seeds, leaves, and flowers have been used for infusions, decoctions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its diuretic, expectorant, and bitter actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and traditional chinese medicine.

Botanical Description

Annual herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters.
Leaves
Broad leaves with upper surface glaucous green and lower surface pale green, bearing distinct stomatal bands along midrib.
Flowers
Flowers are yellow with four petals arranged in a cross, actinomorphic, and have four stamens with distinct anthers and a single pistil with a capitate stigma.
Stems
Erect growth habit with alternate branching, glabrous surface, and pith present.
