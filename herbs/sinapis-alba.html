White Mustard

Sinapis alba

White Mustard (Sinapis alba) is a flowering plant native to Europe and Western Asia, commonly used in traditional medicine for its potential to support respiratory health and alleviate symptoms of conditions such as asthma and bronchitis.

Botanical Identification

White Mustard (Sinapis alba) is an annual herb belonging to the Brassicaceae family. Commonly known as white mustard or yellow rocket, it is native to Europe and western Asia. It has slender stems, lobed leaves, and yellow flowers. Synonyms include Sinapis arvensis and Brassica alba in some herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Spermatophyta
  • Class: caryophyllopsida
  • Order: Brassicales
  • Family: Brassicaceae
  • Genus: Sinapis
  • Species: Sinapis alba

Active Compounds

White Mustard contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and coumarins, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to exert anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Phenolic acid
  • Coumarin

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for digestive issues and respiratory ailments.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and to extract medicinal compounds from tougher plant parts.

Powder: Used for respiratory conditions and as a dietary supplement.

Safety Profile

White Mustard (Sinapis alba) should not be used by individuals with known allergies to the mustard family. It may cause skin irritation and gastrointestinal upset in sensitive individuals. Consult a healthcare professional before use, especially during pregnancy or with existing medical conditions.
