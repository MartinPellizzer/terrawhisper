White Mustard

Sinapis alba

White Mustard (Sinapis alba) is a flowering plant native to Europe and Western Asia, commonly used in traditional medicine for its potential to support respiratory health and alleviate symptoms of conditions such as asthma and bronchitis.

Taxonomy

White Mustard (Sinapis alba) is a flowering plant in the mustard family Brassicaceae, classified within the kingdom Plantae, division Spermatophyta, class Caryophyllopsida, order Brassicales, genus Sinapis, and species Sinapis alba.

Morphology

White Mustard (Sinapis alba) is an annual herb with a slender, branched stem that grows up to 60 cm tall, featuring alternate, pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges and small yellow flowers arranged in racemes.

Distribution

White Mustard (Sinapis alba) is native to Europe and western Asia, with a wide distribution across temperate regions, including parts of North Africa, the Middle East, and the Mediterranean basin.

Traditional Uses

White Mustard (Sinapis alba) has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its digestive, diuretic, and expectorant properties, often prepared as a poultice, tea, or tincture to treat respiratory conditions, inflammation, and skin disorders.

Medicinal Systems

In traditional medicinal systems such as Ayurveda, Unani, and traditional Chinese medicine, White Mustard (Sinapis alba) has been utilized for its purported therapeutic properties.

Phytochemistry

White Mustard (Sinapis alba) contains bioactive compounds such as glucosinolates, flavonoids, alkaloids, and essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties and therapeutic applications in herbal medicine.

Pharmacology

White Mustard (Sinapis alba) exhibits various pharmacological properties including antispasmodic, expectorant, and mild analgesic effects, which contribute to its traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive conditions.

Botanical Identification

White Mustard (Sinapis alba) is an annual herb belonging to the Brassicaceae family. Commonly known as white mustard or yellow rocket, it is native to Europe and western Asia. It has slender stems, lobed leaves, and yellow flowers. Synonyms include Sinapis arvensis and Brassica alba in some herbal texts.

Safety Profile

White Mustard (Sinapis alba) should not be used by individuals with known allergies to the mustard family. It may cause skin irritation and gastrointestinal upset in sensitive individuals. Consult a healthcare professional before use, especially during pregnancy or with existing medical conditions.
