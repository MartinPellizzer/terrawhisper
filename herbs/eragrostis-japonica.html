Eragrostis japonica

What's the taxonomical classification of Eragrostis japonica?

Eragrostis japonica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its placement in the order Poales, the plant is categorized under the family Poaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Eragrostis and the species japonica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eragrostis japonica has an annual growth habit characterized by slender, creeping stems that form dense mats through the production of many fine, branched rhizomes. The leaves are linear and narrow, typically measuring only a few millimeters in width with a smooth or slightly scabrous texture. Its inflorescence consists of small, compact panicles that emerge from the leaf axils, often appearing somewhat spike-like. The spikelets are minute and contain single florets with inconspicuous, small glumes. These morphological traits allow the plant to spread rapidly across moist soil surfaces in wetland habitats.
