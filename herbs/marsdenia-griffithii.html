Marsdenia griffithii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Marsdenia griffithii has woody, twining stems that form climbing vines with opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves featuring entire margins and acute tips. The foliage is typically characterized by a smooth, somewhat leathery texture and a glossy green surface. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils in clusters, often displaying a pale color profile. These inflorescences support specialized reproductive structures typical of the Apocynaceae family. The plant may also produce small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds equipped with tufts of hair for wind dispersal.
