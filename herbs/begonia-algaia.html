Begonia algaia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Begonia algaia has succulent, asymmetrical leaves that are broadly ovate to cordate in shape, featuring a distinctively reddish-tinted underside and asymmetrical bases. The leaf margins are often irregularly serrated or wavy, contributing to its complex silhouette. The plant displays a rhizomatous growth habit, with thick, fleshy stems that emerge from a creeping underground structure. Small, delicate flowers typically emerge from the leaf axils, presenting a contrast to the heavy foliage. The overall morphology is defined by these fleshy, water-storing tissues and the characteristic uneven leaf geometry typical of the genus.
