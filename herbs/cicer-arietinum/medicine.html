What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Cicer Arietinum (Chickpea)?

Cicer arietinum, also known as chickpea, has health benefits such as lowering cholesterol and blood sugar levels, as well as reducing the risk of heart disease and certain cancers.

The medicinal constituents of chickpea include isoflavones, saponins, and flavonoids, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Chickpea can be prepared in various forms, including tablets, powders, and tea infusions. However, excessive consumption of chickpea can cause gastrointestinal side effects, and individuals with allergies or sensitivities should exercise caution.

Precautions should be taken to avoid overuse, especially in individuals with kidney or digestive issues.

This article explains the health benefits, active constituents, medicinal preparations, possible side effects, and precautions related to Cicer arietinum.

What are the health benefits of Cicer arietinum?

Cicer arietinum has health benefits such as reducing the risk of heart disease and diabetes due to its high fiber and protein content.

Chickpeas are also rich in antioxidants, vitamins, and minerals that help to lower cholesterol levels and improve blood sugar control.

Additionally, they have anti-inflammatory properties that may help alleviate symptoms of arthritis and other inflammatory diseases.

What are the active constituents of Cicer arietinum?

The health benefits of Cicer arietinum come from its active constituents, such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins.

These bioactive compounds are responsible for its antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, which have been linked to reducing the risk of chronic diseases like cardiovascular disease, diabetes, and certain cancers.

Additionally, chickpea's fiber content and prebiotic potential support digestive health and gut microbiota balance.

What are the medicinal preparations of Cicer arietinum?

Cicer arietinum has different medicinal preparations, such as chickpea flour extract, which is used to treat digestive issues and diarrhea.

Chickpea seed oil is used in traditional medicine to reduce inflammation and treat skin conditions. Chickpea leaf extract is used to manage diabetes by lowering blood sugar levels.

Additionally, chickpea sprout extract is used as a natural remedy for anxiety and stress relief.

What are the possible side effect of using Cicer arietinum improperly?

Improper use of Cicer arietinum increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as digestive issues like bloating, gas, and stomach discomfort.

It can also lead to allergic reactions, including hives, itching, and difficulty breathing. In some cases, excessive consumption may cause kidney stones, interact with medications, or worsen existing conditions like gout or irritable bowel syndrome (IBS).

Additionally, improper preparation or storage of chickpeas can result in foodborne illnesses, such as E. coli or Salmonella infections.

What precautions to take when using Cicer arietinum medicinally?

Before using Cicer arietinum for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as consulting with a healthcare professional to determine the appropriate dosage and potential interactions with other medications.

Additionally, it is essential to choose high-quality products that are free from contaminants and adulterants.

Furthermore, individuals with allergies or sensitivities should exercise caution when consuming chickpeas or its derivatives.
