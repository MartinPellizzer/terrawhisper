10 Possible Side Effects Of Cicer Arietinum (Chickpea)

Cicer arietinum has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal discomfort, diarrhea, and allergic reactions.

The causes of these side effects are often due to individual sensitivities or excessive consumption. For instance, digestive issues can disrupt daily routines, leading to fatigue, decreased productivity, and straining relationships.

Furthermore, severe allergic reactions can trigger anaphylactic shock, a life-threatening condition requiring immediate medical attention.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Cicer arietinum if used imporperly.

1. Disrupts hormonal balance

Cicer arietinum causes allergic reactions.

This is due to the presence of specific proteins in the legume that can trigger an immune response in some individuals. The most common allergenic protein found in chickpeas is Ara h 1, which is responsible for causing symptoms such as hives, itching, and respiratory problems.

Additionally, other proteins like Ara h 2 and Gly m Bd 28K have also been identified as potential allergens, further increasing the risk of allergic reactions in susceptible individuals.

2. Disrupts hormonal balance

Cicer arietinum triggers digestive issues in some individuals.

This is attributed to its high content of raffinose, a complex sugar that can be difficult for the body to digest, leading to uncomfortable symptoms such as bloating, gas, and abdominal discomfort.

Additionally, the phytic acid present in chickpeas may also contribute to digestive issues by binding to minerals like iron, zinc, and calcium, reducing their absorption and potentially causing gastrointestinal upset.

Cicer arietinum increases blood pressure due to its high sodium content and the potential for individuals to consume excessive amounts of this legume.

The natural occurrence of phytoestrogens in chickpeas may also contribute to increased blood pressure by altering hormonal balances within the body.

Additionally, the potassium-to-sodium ratio in chickpeas can lead to fluid retention, further elevating blood pressure readings.

4. Disrupts hormonal balance

Cicer arietinum induces kidney stones due to its high concentration of oxalates and purines.

These compounds can increase urine's acidity, making it more conducive for the formation of kidney stones.

Additionally, the high fiber content in chickpeas may not be fully digested, leading to an increased amount of undigested oxalates entering the bloodstream and potentially causing issues with calcium metabolism, further contributing to the development of kidney stones.

5. Disrupts hormonal balance

Cicer arietinum causes thyroid problems due to its high content of phytoestrogens, specifically genistein and daidzein.

These compounds can mimic the effects of estrogen in the body, potentially disrupting the normal functioning of the thyroid gland and leading to issues such as hypothyroidism or hyperthyroidism.

The exact mechanism by which this occurs is not fully understood, but it is believed that the phytoestrogens bind to estrogen receptors, altering the balance of hormones in the body.

As a result, individuals consuming Cicer arietinum may experience alterations in their gut health.
