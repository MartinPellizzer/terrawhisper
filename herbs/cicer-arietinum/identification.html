Botanical Identification of Chickpea (Cicer arietinum)

To identify Chickpea (Cicer arietinum) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Chickpea (Cicer arietinum) Monograph

Scientific Classification

Chickpea, or Cicer arietinum, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Fabales, order Fabales, family Fabaceae, genus Cicer, and species Cicer arietinum. This classification places it within the legume family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability and medicinal potential.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Cicer arietinum is the scientific name for chickpea, first described by the botanist Carl von Linné. It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is also known by several synonyms, including Cicer hariotanum and Cicer crenatum. Common names such as garbanzo bean and Egyptian bean are widely used, while regional and historical names reflect its long cultivation history across different cultures.

Morphological Characteristics

Chickpea (Cicer arietinum) is an annual herb with a taproot system. Its stem is erect and branched, reaching up to 30 cm in height. The leaves are pinnately compound with 4-6 pairs of leaflets. Inflorescence is a raceme with small, yellow flowers. The fruit is a dehiscent pod containing 2-3 kidney-shaped seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Chickpea can be distinguished by its trifoliate leaves with a heart-shaped leaflet, yellow flowers with a distinctive black spot, and pods that are typically green to brown. The seeds are kidney-shaped, smooth, and range in color from white to brown, setting it apart from similar legumes.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Chickpea, or Cicer arietinum, is native to the Mediterranean region, the Middle East, and parts of South and Central Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained loamy soils and is typically found at altitudes between 200 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Chickpea (Cicer arietinum) blooms 60-90 days after planting, typically in late spring to early summer. Seed formation occurs 30-45 days after flowering, usually in mid to late summer. Harvest timing is when pods turn yellow and dry, generally in late summer to early autumn, depending on the climate.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Chickpea (Cicer arietinum) belongs to the genus Cicer, which is closely related to species like Cicer reticulatum and Cicer pinnatum. It is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, sharing traits such as pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities with other members of the family. Its phylogenetic position reflects its evolutionary ties to other legumes and closely related genera.
