Oxalis corniculata

What's the taxonomical classification of Oxalis corniculata?

Oxalis corniculata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and follows the order Oxalidales. This plant is a member of the family Oxalidaceae, falling under the genus Oxalis, with its specific designation being the species corniculata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Oxalis corniculata has trifoliate leaves composed of three heart-shaped leaflets, small five-petaled flowers that range from white to pale pink, and slender, erect stems arising from underground rhizomes. The foliage often displays a delicate texture with leaflets that fold inward during the night or under stress. Its inflorescence typically consists of solitary flowers or small clusters situated atop long pedicels. The reproductive structures include a central ovary surrounded by five stamens and five petals that protect the internal nectar. These morphological features allow the plant to thrive in shaded, moist environments where it spreads via its creeping root systems.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant thrives in well-draining, humus-rich soil and requires consistent moisture alongside partial shade to prevent leaf scorch.

Gardeners should maintain a temperature range between 60 and 75 degrees Fahrenheit for optimal growth. Propagation is most effectively achieved by dividing the underground bulbils during the early spring or autumn months.     What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause kidney stones, electrolyte imbalance, nausea, and abdominal pain due to its high concentration of oxalic acid. When consumed in large quantities, the acid can bind with calcium in the body to form calcium oxalate crystals. These crystals are a primary component of kidney stones and can lead to significant physical distress. The resulting disruption of mineral levels may also trigger a dangerous electrolyte imbalance. Individuals experiencing these issues often report acute symptoms such as nausea and sharp abdominal pain.
