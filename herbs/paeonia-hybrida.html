Paeonia hybrida

What's the taxonomical classification of Paeonia hybrida?

Paeonia hybrida belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic progression, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized under the order Saxifragales and falls within the family Paeoniaceae. Finally, it is identified by its genus, Paeonia, and its specific species designation, hybrida.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Paeonia hybrida has large, herbaceous perennials characterized by thick, tuberous roots, deeply lobed basal leaves, and massive multi-petaled flowers that typically range from deep red to soft pink. The foliage consists of complex, compound leaves with smooth margins that provide a lush, dark green canopy during the growing season. Its stems are sturdy and erect, capable of supporting heavy floral heads that bloom in late spring or early summer. The reproductive structures include a central carpel surrounded by numerous stamens, which eventually develop into woody seed pods. These morphological features allow the plant to thrive in temperate climates with well-draining soil.
