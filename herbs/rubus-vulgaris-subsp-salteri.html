Possible Side Effects of Rubus vulgaris subsp. salteri

Rubus vulgaris subsp.

salteri can have side effects if used improperly, such as allergic reactions, including hives, itching, and difficulty breathing, as well as gastrointestinal issues like nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea.

In some cases, excessive consumption of the plant's berries or leaves can also lead to kidney or liver damage.

Additionally, the plant's high levels of salicylic acid can cause stomach ulcers or interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners.

It's essential to consult a medical professional before using Rubus vulgaris subsp.

salteri for any health-related purposes.
