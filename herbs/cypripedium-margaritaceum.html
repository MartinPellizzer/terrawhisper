Cypripedium margaritaceum

What's the taxonomical classification of Cypripedium margaritaceum?

Cypripedium margaritaceum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. As a member of the order Asparagales, this plant is situated within the family Orchidaceae. Its taxonomic hierarchy concludes with its placement in the genus Cypripedium, specifically identifying it as the species Cypripedium margaritaeceum.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat respiratory ailments such as coughs, asthma, and bronchitis, as well as various skin inflammations. Traditional medicinal practitioners often utilize the plant's extracts to soothe irritated lungs and clear congestion. Some cultures also apply the crushed botanical material topically to reduce swelling and calm skin irritations. The plant contains specific bioactive compounds believed to provide these soothing effects. Its application in folk medicine varies significantly depending on the specific region and preparation method used.
