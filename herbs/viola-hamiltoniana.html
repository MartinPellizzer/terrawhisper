Viola hamiltoniana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viola hamiltoniana has ovate to lanceolate leaves with crenate margins, a creeping rhizomatous rootstock, and zygomorphic flowers featuring five petals with prominent nectar spurs. The foliage typically exhibits a slightly hairy or glabrous texture depending on the specific habitat and age of the plant. Its delicate blossoms are characterized by a violet or pale blue coloration with distinct darker veins guiding pollinators toward the center. The reproductive structures include a central pistil surrounded by five stamens that are fused to the corolla base. Small, capsule-like fruits develop following pollination to facilitate seed dispersal across the forest floor.
