Latouchea fokienensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Latouchea fokienensis has small, woody, perennial stems that bear simple, opposite, elliptical leaves with entire margins and acute apices. The plant's foliage is characterized by a relatively thin texture and a smooth surface area. Small, inconspicuous flowers typically emerge from the leaf axils in a manner consistent with its genus. The reproductive structures include tiny fruits that develop from the fertilized ovaries of these flowers. These morphological traits collectively distinguish the species within its specific ecological niche.
