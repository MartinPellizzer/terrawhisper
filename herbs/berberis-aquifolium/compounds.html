Oregon Grape (Berberis aquifolium) Compounds

The primary compounds in Oregon Grape include alkaloids like berberine, known for its antimicrobial properties. Flavonoids and anthocyanins contribute to its antioxidant effects. Tannins and phenolic acids provide astringency and additional health benefits. These compounds work synergistically to support the plant's medicinal uses.

Oregon Grape (Berberis aquifolium) Monograph

1. Alkaloid

The alkaloid berberine is found in Oregon Grape (Berberis aquifolium). It has antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and digestive properties. Berberine supports gut health and may help with conditions like diarrhea and skin infections. It is a key component in the plant's traditional medicinal use.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Oregon Grape (Berberis aquifolium) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help support digestive health and may enhance the plant's overall therapeutic effects when used in herbal formulations.

3. Tannin

Tannin in Oregon Grape (Berberis aquifolium) contributes to its astringent properties and may support digestive health. It works synergistically with berberine to enhance the plant's antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins also help preserve the plant's active compounds during preparation.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Oregon Grape (Berberis aquifolium). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting cellular health and reducing oxidative stress. This compound is also involved in the plant's traditional use for digestive and skin-related conditions.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Oregon Grape (Berberis aquifolium). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, often working synergistically with berberine to support digestive and immune health. Glycosides are known for their anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects in herbal medicine.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a compound found in Oregon Grape (Berberis aquifolium). It contributes to the plant's bitter taste and has mild anticoagulant properties. Coumarin may support cardiovascular health and is often used in herbal formulations for its potential blood-thinning effects. However, it should be used cautiously, as high doses can be toxic.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Oregon Grape (Berberis aquifolium). It contributes to the plant's bitter taste and has mild cleansing properties. Saponin may support digestive health and has antimicrobial effects, though its role in traditional herbal medicine is less prominent than that of berberine.

8. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Oregon Grape (Berberis aquifolium) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine. They are found in the roots and berries, enhancing the plant's therapeutic potential.

9. Essential oil

The essential oil of Oregon Grape (Berberis aquifolium) contains volatile compounds like monoterpene hydrocarbons and oxygenated derivatives. It contributes to the plant's aromatic profile and may support antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects when used in herbal formulations.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanins are pigments found in Oregon Grape (Berberis aquifolium) that contribute to its vibrant color and offer antioxidant properties. These compounds support overall health by neutralizing free radicals and may enhance the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and inflammatory support.
