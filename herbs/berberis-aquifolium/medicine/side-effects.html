10 Possible Side Effects Of Berberis Aquifolium (Oregon Grape)

Berberis aquifolium has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, allergic reactions, and liver damage.

These side effects are often caused by consuming excessive amounts or using the herb for extended periods without medical guidance.

For instance, a person with an allergy to Berberis aquifolium may experience life-threatening anaphylaxis, while a person with pre-existing liver conditions may experience worsened symptoms or even liver failure, leading to significant disability and reduced quality of life.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Berberis aquifolium if used imporperly.

1. Exacerbates existing medical conditions

Berberis aquifolium causes allergic reactions due to its active compounds, berberine and alkaloids.

These substances can trigger an immune response in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, flushing, and swelling. The exact mechanisms behind these reactions are not fully understood, but it is thought that the body mistakenly identifies these compounds as foreign invaders, triggering an allergic response.

This can occur even at small doses, making individual tolerance unpredictable.

2. Exacerbates existing medical conditions

Berberis aquifolium triggers skin irritation due to its high concentration of berberine, a compound that can cause allergic reactions and skin inflammation.

The plant's sap, leaves, and roots contain this alkaloid, which can penetrate the skin and trigger an immune response, leading to redness, itching, and swelling.

Additionally, the presence of other compounds like caryophyllene and isoferulic acid may also contribute to skin irritation.
