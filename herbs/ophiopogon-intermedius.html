Ophiopogon intermedius

What's the taxonomical classification of Ophiopogon intermedius?

Ophiopogon intermedius belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It further falls under the order Asparagales and is a member of the family Asparagaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Ophiopogon, and its unique species designation, intermedius.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ophiopogon intermedius has narrow, linear, dark green leaves that typically measure between 2 and 5 millimeters in width and grow in dense, tufted clumps. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale lavender in color. Following the bloom, it develops small, spherical berries that transition from green to a deep blue or black hue when ripe. How is this plant cultivated?

This plant, commonly known as intermediate mondo grass, thrives in partial to full shade and requires well-draining, moist, organic-rich soil to grow effectively.
