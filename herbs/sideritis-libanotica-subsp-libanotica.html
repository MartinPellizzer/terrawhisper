Libanotis Sideritis (Sideritis Libanotica Subsp. Libanotica)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

Libanotis sideritis, scientifically known as Sideritis libanotica subsp. libanotica, is a medicinal herb native to the Mediterranean region, valued for its adaptogenic properties and rich phytochemical profile. This plant is traditionally used to support respiratory health, reduce inflammation, and promote emotional balance, with its primary benefits attributed to compounds like flavonoids and essential oils. In ancient Greek and Mediterranean cultures, it was brewed as a tea to alleviate stress, improve digestion, and enhance vitality, reflecting its long-standing role in traditional medicine. Modern wellness applications include its use in herbal supplements for anxiety relief and immune support, as well as in culinary contexts where its distinctive licorice-like flavor adds depth to teas and infusions. Notably, its historical significance dates back to the time of Hippocrates, and its unique combination of bioactive compounds makes it a rare and valuable herb in both traditional and contemporary holistic practices.
