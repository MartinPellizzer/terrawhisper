What's the taxonomical classification of Euonymus carnosus?

Euonymus carnosus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical structure, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized under the order Celastrales and the family Celastraceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its membership in the genus Euonymus, with its unique designation being the species carnosus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Euonymus carnosus has opposite, simple, ovate to elliptical leaves that measure between 2 and 8 centimeters in length and possess entire margins with entire or slightly serrated edges. The plant is a deciduous shrub characterized by its multi-stemmed growth habit and slender, woody branches. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically greenish-white and are borne in small clusters along the stems. During the fruiting season, the shrub produces distinctive four-lobed capsules that split open to reveal bright red or orange arils surrounding the seeds.
