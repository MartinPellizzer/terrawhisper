Malus mandshurica

What's the taxonomical classification of Malus mandshurica?

Malus mandshurica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further classified under the order Rosales. It is a member of the family Rosaceae, which places it within the genus Malus, and it is specifically identified by its species name, mandshurica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Malus mandshurica has a deciduous, spreading tree habit reaching heights of up to 10 meters, characterized by single or multi-stemmed trunks and finely serrated, ovate leaves. The foliage features elliptical blades with acute tips and finely toothed margins that transition from bright green to autumnal hues. Small, white, five-petaled flowers emerge in clusters during the spring, eventually developing into small, globose pomes that turn reddish or yellowish upon ripening. The bark on mature specimens becomes fissured and dark grey, providing a rugged texture to the main stems. Its branching pattern is typically irregular, contributing to a dense and rounded crown.
