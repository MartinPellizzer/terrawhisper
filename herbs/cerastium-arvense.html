Cerastium arvense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cerastium arvense has linear to lanceolate leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs along a stem characterized by a basal rosette of more rounded foliage. The plant typically reaches heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and features small, white, five-petaled flowers held in terminal cymes. Its stems are often reddish and may be slightly hairy or glabrous depending on the specific variety. The fruit consists of small, ovoid capsules that split open to release numerous tiny seeds. These morphological traits help distinguish this species from other members of the Caryophyllaceae family.
