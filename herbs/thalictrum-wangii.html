Thalictrum wangii

What's the taxonomical classification of Thalictrum wangii?

Thalictrum wangii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Ranunculales. This plant is a representative of the family Ranunculaceae and is specifically categorized under the genus Thalictrum, with its unique designation being the species wangii.

    What are the molecular targets of this plant?

    Thalictrum wangii targets various enzymes and signaling proteins, most notably through the interaction of its isoquinoline alkaloids with topoisomerases and specific neurotransmitter receptors. These bioactive compounds act as potent inhibitors of topoisomerase I and II, which disrupts DNA replication in rapidly dividing cells. Additionally, certain alkaloids found in the plant exhibit affinity for GABA receptors and various protein kinases involved in inflammatory cascades. By modulating these specific protein functions, the plant exerts its observed pharmacological effects on cellular proliferation and neurological signaling. These interactions provide a molecular basis for its potential applications in oncology and neuropharmacology.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Thalictrum wangii can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested or handled improperly. Many species within the Ranunculaceae family contain protoanemonin, a chemical that reacts with moisture to cause blistering upon contact with the skin. This substance can lead to redness, itching, and painful swelling in sensitive individuals. Internal consumption of the plant may result in severe nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. Due to these potential toxicities, caution is necessary when interacting with the plant in its natural habitat.
