Thalictrum wangii

What's the taxonomical classification of Thalictrum wangii?

Thalictrum wangii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Ranunculales. This plant is a representative of the family Ranunculaceae and is specifically categorized under the genus Thalictrum, with its unique designation being the species wangii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Thalictrum wangii has herbaceous stems that arise from a rhizomatous base, bearing compound leaves characterized by multiple leaflets with serrated margins. What are the pharmacological activities of Thalictrum wangii?

This plant has anti-proliferative, antitumor, and immunosuppressive pharmacological activities. These biological properties are primarily attributed to the presence of specific alkaloids within its chemical profile. Research indicates that these compounds can inhibit the rapid growth of cancer cells to exert their antitumor effects. Furthermore, the plant demonstrates a capacity to modulate the immune system through its immunosuppressive actions. Understanding these diverse mechanisms is essential for exploring its potential applications in clinical pharmacology.

As discussed in a study, eight previously undescribed benzylisoquinoline alkaloid dimers, neothalfines A-H (1-8), were isolated from the roots of Thalictrum wangii B. Boivin. Evaluation of the identified alkaloids revealed that twelve dimers exhibited anti-proliferative activity against three human cancer cell lines, A549, MHCC97H, and U-87MG, with IC50 values ranging from 2.1 to 38.1 μM. Dimer 8 proved particularly potent, displaying IC50 values of 3.5, 2.1, and 2.8 μM against these respective cell lines. This dimer induces mitochondrial dysfunction, ROS-mediated apoptosis, and inhibits colony formation. Additionally, network pharmacology and molecular docking identified SRC, EGFR, and GSK3β as core targets for its mechanism.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds berbberine, magnoflorine, aporphine, proaporphine, and oxoaporphine. These specific alkaloids contribute to the diverse pharmacological profile of the species. Berreberine is well known for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The presence of magnoflorine often provides neuroprotective or sedative effects. Additionally, the various aporphine derivatives like aporphine, proaporphine, and oxoaporphine play significant roles in its biological activity.

As discussed in a study, the roots of Thalictrum wangii contain the novel tetrahydroprotoberberiine-aporphine dimeric alkaloid (-) -thalibealine (1). This specific plant was also found to contain three other alkaloids. These isolated compounds include the benzyltetrahydroisoquinoline-aporphine dimer (+) -thalmelatidine, the apo-rhine (+) -magnoflorine, and the protoberberine berbine. This research marks the first reported isolation of constituents from Thalictrum wangii.
