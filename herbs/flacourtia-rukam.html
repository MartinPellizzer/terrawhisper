What's the taxonomical classification of Flacourtia rukam?

Flacourtia rukam belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Malpighiales and the family Salicaceae. Finally, the plant is placed within the genus Flacourtia, with its specific identity defined by the species name rukam.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Flacourtia rukam can cause allergic skin reactions such as dermatitis or itching if the sap or fruit makes direct contact with sensitive skin. While many people consume the fruit safely, individual sensitivities may lead to gastrointestinal discomfort or mild stomach upset. There is limited scientific data regarding its long-term toxicity in humans, so caution is advised when using it medicinally. Pregnant or breastfeeding women should avoid significant consumption until more clinical research is available to ensure safety.
