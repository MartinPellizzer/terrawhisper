Flacourtia rukam

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Flacourtia rukam has an evergreen tree structure characterized by a trunk covered in smooth, greyish bark and a dense, spreading canopy of elliptical, leathery leaves. The foliage consists of dark green, glossy leaves that are typically arranged in an alternate pattern along the branches. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often appearing in clusters to facilitate pollination. These flowers eventually develop into fleshy, globose drupes that transition from green to a bright red or orange color when ripe. The overall morphology reflects its adaptation to tropical forest environments where dense foliage is essential for light capture.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Flacourtia rukam can cause allergic skin reactions such as dermatitis or itching if the sap or fruit makes direct contact with sensitive skin. There is limited scientific data regarding its long-term toxicity in humans, so caution is advised when using it medicinally. Pregnant or breastfeeding women should avoid significant consumption until more clinical research is available to ensure safety.
