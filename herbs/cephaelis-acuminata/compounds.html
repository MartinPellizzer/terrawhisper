Cephaelis Blue Poppy (Cephaelis acuminata) Compounds

The primary compounds of Cephaelis acuminata include alkaloids, which have potential pharmacological effects, and flavonoids, known for their antioxidant properties. Tannins and phenolic acids contribute to its astringent qualities, while coumarin and saponins may support digestive health. Glycosides, terpenoids, and essential oils provide additional therapeutic benefits, and anthocyanins contribute to its vibrant blue color.

Cephaelis Blue Poppy (Cephaelis acuminata) Monograph

1. Alkaloid

The alkaloid berberine is found in Cephaelis acuminata, commonly known as the Blue Poppy. It is known for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, contributing to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and respiratory ailments.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Cephaelis acuminata are bioactive compounds known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. They contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and skin conditions. These compounds help modulate immune responses and may support wound healing. Their presence enhances the therapeutic potential of the Blue Poppy in herbal medicine.

3. Tannin

Tannin in Cephaelis acuminata contributes to its astringent properties, aiding in the treatment of skin conditions and digestive issues. This compound helps reduce inflammation and may support wound healing, making it a key component in traditional herbal remedies derived from the Blue Poppy.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Cephaelis acuminata, known for its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments. This compound supports the plant's role in herbal medicine by enhancing its therapeutic potential.

Coumarin is a key active compound in Cephaelis acuminata, known for its antispasmodic and analgesic properties. 6. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Cephaelis acuminata, known for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
