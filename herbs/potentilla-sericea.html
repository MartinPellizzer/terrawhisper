What's the taxonomical classification of Potentilla sericea?

Potentiilla sericea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the order Rosales and the family Rosaceae. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Potentiella, with its specific designation being the species sericea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Potentiella sericea has a low-growing, stoloniferous growth habit characterized by woody caudices and leaves that are densely covered in fine, silvery-white hairs. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers with five distinct petals arranged around a central cluster of numerous stamens and pistils. Its stems are often creeping along the ground, allowing it to form dense mats in its natural habitat.
