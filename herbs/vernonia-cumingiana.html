Vernonia cumingiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. This plant is a specific representative of the family Asteraceae, a diverse group of flowering plants, and is categorized under the genus Vernonia. Finally, its unique biological identity is defined by its specific epithet, making its full taxonomic designation Vernonia cumingiana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vernonia cumingiana has an erect, herbaceous growth habit characterized by slender, branching stems and lanceolate to oblong leaves with serrated margins. Its inflorescence consists of small, discoid flower heads clustered in terminal cymes, which are characteristic of the Asteraceae family. These flowers are generally purplish or blue in color and emerge from involucral bracts that surround the base of each head.
