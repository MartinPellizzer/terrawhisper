Shallon Berry (Gaultheria shallon) Compounds

The primary compounds in Shallon Berry include flavonoids, which act as antioxidants, and tannins, which contribute to its astringent properties. Phenolic acids and anthocyanins provide antioxidant and anti-inflammatory benefits. Coumarin and essential oils offer antimicrobial effects, while glycosides, saponins, and terpenoids support various therapeutic actions. Alkaloids may also contribute to its medicinal properties.

Shallon Berry (Gaultheria shallon) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Shallon Berry (Gaultheria shallon) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help protect cells from oxidative stress and support overall health. They are naturally occurring in the berries and are part of the plant's traditional medicinal uses.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Shallon Berry (Gaultheria shallon). It contributes to the berry's astringent properties and is used in herbal medicine for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins help in reducing excess mucus and supporting digestive health.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid in Shallon Berry (Gaultheria shallon) contributes to its antioxidant properties. It helps neutralize free radicals, supporting cellular health. This compound is part of the berry's complex phytochemical profile that underpins its traditional medicinal uses.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Shallon Berry (Gaultheria shallon). It contributes to the plant's mild sedative and antispasmodic properties. Found naturally in the berries, coumarin supports digestive health and may aid in muscle relaxation. Its presence enhances the therapeutic potential of Shallon Berry in herbal medicine.

5. Glycoside

The glycoside in Shallon Berry (Gaultheria shallon) contributes to its medicinal properties. It is a sugar-bound compound that may support cardiovascular health and reduce inflammation. Glycosides in this plant are often responsible for its tonic and circulatory benefits. They are typically extracted and used in herbal formulations.

6. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Shallon Berry (Gaultheria shallon) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine. They help protect cells from damage and may aid in treating respiratory and digestive ailments.

7. Essential oil

The essential oil of Shallon Berry (Gaultheria shallon) contains aromatic compounds like methyl salicylate and other phenolic substances. These contribute to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, making it useful in herbal medicine for relieving muscle pain and reducing inflammation.

8. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Shallon Berry (Gaultheria shallon). It contributes to the berry's deep color and has antioxidant properties. Anthocyanin supports cardiovascular health and may reduce inflammation, making it valuable in herbal medicine for its therapeutic potential.

9. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Shallon Berry (Gaultheria shallon). It contributes to the berry's traditional use in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponin helps support the plant's role in supporting digestive and immune health.

10. Alkaloid

Shallon Berry (Gaultheria shallon) contains the alkaloid gaultherin. This compound is known for its analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties. It is commonly used in herbal medicine to relieve pain and reduce inflammation, particularly in topical applications.
