Botanical Identification of Shallon Berry (Gaultheria shallon)

To identify Shallon Berry (Gaultheria shallon) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Shallon Berry, or Gaultheria shallon, belongs to the plant kingdom, specifically the flowering plants. It is classified under the order Ericales, family Ericaceae, and genus Gaultheria, with the species name shallon. This classification places it among the heath and related plants commonly found in temperate regions.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The botanical name of Shallon Berry is Gaultheria shallon, first described by Linnaeus in 1771. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is also known by several synonyms, including Gaultheria fragrans and Gaultheria leucocarpa. Common names include shallon, salmon berry, and red salmonberry, with regional variations such as "red salmonberry" in British Columbia. Historically, it was referred to as "salmon berry" by Indigenous peoples.

Morphological Characteristics

Shallon berry (Gaultheria shallon) is a low-growing evergreen shrub with a dense root system that anchors it firmly in soil. Its stems are woody and sprawling, often forming mats. Leaves are oval, glossy, and dark green, with a leathery texture. Inflorescences are clusters of small white flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is a dark red, shiny berry containing several small seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Shallon berry can be distinguished by its oval, dark red to black berries with a waxy coating, and its alternate, elliptical leaves with serrated edges. It grows in moist, shady areas and has a fragrant, spicy aroma. It is commonly found in western North America, particularly in the Pacific Northwest.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Shallon Berry (Gaultheria shallon) is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, particularly regions of British Columbia and Oregon. It thrives in cool, temperate climates with high humidity. It prefers acidic, well-drained soils and is commonly found at elevations between 300 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Shallon Berry blooms in late spring to early summer, typically between May and June. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early autumn, around August to September. For identification purposes, the berries are most visible and identifiable from late summer through early autumn.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Shallon berry (Gaultheria shallon) belongs to the Ericaceae family, sharing close relations with other genus members like Gaultheria and Vaccinium. It is most closely related to species such as Gaultheria shallon and Gaultheria shallon var. obovata. These species exhibit similar morphological and chemical traits, including the presence of aromatic compounds and evergreen foliage.
