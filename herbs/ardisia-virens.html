Ardisia virens

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ardisia virens has simple, evergreen leaves that are elliptical or oblong in shape, possessing a glossy texture and a dark green upper surface with paler undersides. The plant typically grows as a woody shrub or small tree, characterized by its dense, spreading canopy. Its flowers are small and tubular, often appearing in clusters among the foliage. The fruit develops into fleshy, globose berries that change color as they mature, usually transitioning to a bright red or orange hue. These morphological features collectively help the species thrive in the humid, shaded environments of tropical forests.
