10 Precautions To Take When Using Anacardium Occidentale (Cashew)

Anacardium occidentale has some precautions to consider before using it medicinally, such as potential skin and eye irritation.

The cashew nut shell liquid, a key component, contains anacardic acids that can cause allergic reactions, skin burns, and eye damage. If not taken properly, using this herb may result in severe skin reactions, such as redness, itching, and blistering, or even eye infections if not protected.

It's crucial to exercise caution when handling this herb to avoid unintended side effects.

This article explains in details the 10 most important precautions to take when using Anacardium occidentale medicinally.

1. Follow recommended dosing instructions

When using Anacardium occidentale medicinally, it's important to wash hands before handling.

This precaution is crucial because the sap of the plant contains a toxic compound called urushiol, which can cause skin irritation and allergic reactions. If not properly handled, this sap can transfer to the practitioner's hands, potentially contaminating them and causing harm to others.

By washing hands thoroughly before handling cashew products, practitioners can minimize the risk of exposure and ensure safe and effective use of this medicinal plant.
