1. Irritates skin

Anacardium occidentale irritates skin due to the presence of a chemical called urushiol in its resin.

This compound is similar to that found in poison ivy and causes an allergic reaction when it comes into contact with skin, resulting in inflammation, redness, and itching.

The severity of the irritation can vary depending on the individual's sensitivity to urushiol and the amount of exposure.

2. Causes allergic reactions

Anacardium occidentale causes allergic reactions.

The allergenic compound urushiol present in its nutshell and oil can trigger an immune response, resulting in symptoms such as itching, hives, and inflammation.

Additionally, the protein-rich pulp of the cashew nut is also a common allergen that can cause respiratory issues, gastrointestinal problems, and skin irritation.

3. Induces vomiting

Anacardium occidentale induces vomiting due to its high content of urushiol, a potent allergen and skin irritant.

When ingested, urushiol can cause the body to react violently, leading to nausea, abdominal cramps, and eventually, vomiting.

This side effect is typically experienced by individuals who consume raw or unripe cashews, as the urushiol content is highest in these forms of the nut.

4. Triggers seizures

Anacardium occidentale triggers seizures due to its toxic properties.

The primary culprit is urushiol, a chemical compound present in the nut's resinous sap, which can cause neurotoxicity and disrupt normal brain activity. This disruption can lead to episodes of convulsion, resulting in seizures.

Additionally, the high levels of anacardic acids in cashew may also contribute to its seizure-inducing properties.

6. Causes respiratory distress

Anacardium occidentale causes respiratory distress due to the presence of a toxic compound called urushiol in its shell and leaves.

When ingested or inhaled, this chemical can cause irritation to the lungs and airways, leading to symptoms such as coughing, wheezing, and shortness of breath.

As the body tries to expel the toxin, it can trigger an allergic reaction, further exacerbating respiratory issues and potentially even triggering asthma attacks.

7. Activates liver toxicity

Anacardium occidentale activates liver toxicity due to the presence of a toxic compound called urushiol in its shell and pulp.

When ingested or comes into contact with skin, urushiol can cause allergic reactions, inflammation, and damage to the liver cells, leading to impaired detoxification and potentially even organ failure.

The severity of liver toxicity depends on the amount and duration of exposure to the toxin.

8. Produces neurological symptoms

Anacardium occidentale produces neurological symptoms in some individuals due to its potential to cause an allergic reaction or irritation when ingested.

The compounds anacardic acid and cardol present in the seed can induce a range of adverse effects, including headache, dizziness, confusion, and even seizures in severe cases.

These reactions are thought to be triggered by the toxin content within the seed, which can be released into the body during processing or consumption.

9. Erodes tooth enamel

Anacardium occidentale erodes tooth enamel due to its high acidity and abrasive nature.

The sap of the cashew tree contains urushiol, a compound that can dissolve the minerals in tooth enamel, leading to erosion. Additionally, the texture of the nut itself can cause wear and tear on tooth surfaces, further contributing to the erosion of enamel.

This can result in sensitive teeth and potentially lead to cavities if left untreated.

10. Irritates mucous membranes

Anacardium occidentale irritates mucous membranes due to its toxic compound urushiol, which is responsible for causing allergic reactions and inflammation.

When ingested or absorbed through the skin, urushiol can trigger a range of symptoms including itching, burning, and redness in the mouth, throat, and eyes.

This can lead to discomfort and distress, particularly in individuals who are sensitive to this compound.
