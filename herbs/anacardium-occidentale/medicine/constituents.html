10 Medicinal Constituents Of Anacardium Occidentale (Cashew)

Anacardium occidentale has active constituents such as anacardic acid, cardol, and cardanol.

These compounds possess antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antifungal properties, which can help alleviate symptoms of skin conditions like acne, eczema, and psoriasis. They can also improve wound healing and reduce inflammation, thus enhancing overall skin health.

These properties can significantly improve the quality of life for individuals suffering from skin-related disorders.

This article explains in details the 10 best active constituents of Anacardium occidentale .

1. Cardol

Anacardium occidentale cardol is a bioactive compound extracted from the seed of the cashew tree.

It is a complex mixture of lipids, primarily composed of anacardic acids and cardanol, which have been shown to possess various biological activities, including antimicrobial, antifungal, and anticancer properties.

Cardol has also been reported to exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, making it a potential candidate for the treatment of certain diseases.

2. Anacardic acid

Anacardium occidentale anacardic acid is a phenolic compound found in the nut shell of the tree.

This acid is responsible for the toxic and allergenic properties of the cashew shell, causing skin irritation and allergic reactions. Anacardic acid has been used in traditional medicine for centuries to treat various ailments, including arthritis, rheumatism, and fever.

Its antifungal and antibacterial properties have also made it a popular ingredient in natural remedies.

3. Oleic acid

Anacardium occidentale oleic acid is a naturally occurring fatty acid found in its seeds.

It is a significant component of cashew oil, accounting for approximately 40-50% of the total fatty acids present.

Oleic acid has been shown to have various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may contribute to its potential role in preventing chronic diseases such as cardiovascular disease and cancer.

4. Linoleic acid

Anacardium occidentale linoleic acid is a polyunsaturated fatty acid found in its seeds.

It is a major component of the seed's oil, which accounts for approximately 35-40% of its total composition. Linoleic acid has been recognized for its potential health benefits, including reducing inflammation and improving cardiovascular health.

It also plays a crucial role in maintaining skin elasticity and hydration due to its ability to penetrate deep into the skin layers.

5. Stearic acid

Anacardium occidentale stearic acid is a saturated fatty acid found in its nut shells and leaves.

It is an important component of the natural wax coating on the surface of the cashew seeds and is responsible for their water-repellent properties.

Stearic acid has been traditionally used in various industrial applications, including the production of soap, cosmetics, and pharmaceuticals.

6. Palmitic acid

Anacardium occidentale palmitic acid is a type of saturated fatty acid that is found in its nuts.

It is an essential component of the lipids present in the cashew kernel and is responsible for the unique flavor and aroma of the nut.

Palmitic acid plays a crucial role in the nutritional value and bioactivity of cashews, making them a popular ingredient in various cuisines around the world.

7. Saturated fatty acids

Anacardium occidentale saturated fatty acids is a unique characteristic of its oil.

The oil extracted from the nut contains a significant amount of saturated fatty acids, primarily stearic acid and palmitic acid.

These saturated fats are relatively stable and have a high melting point, making them useful in various industrial applications such as cosmetics, lubricants, and biofuels.

8. Vitamin e

Anacardium occidentale vitamin E is a potent antioxidant compound found in the nuts and seeds of the tree.

This essential nutrient plays a crucial role in protecting cells from damage caused by free radicals, which can contribute to various health issues such as cancer, heart disease, and aging.

Vitamin E also helps maintain healthy skin, hair, and mucous membranes, while supporting immune function and eye health.

9. Flavonoids

Anacardium occidentale flavonoids is a type of phytochemical found in its leaves and stems.

These flavonoids are responsible for the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which have been shown to possess therapeutic potential.

Cashew flavonoids have been found to exhibit antimicrobial, antiviral, and anticancer activities, making them a promising area of research in the development of novel pharmaceuticals and nutraceuticals.

10. Phenolic acids

Anacardium occidentale phenolic acids is a class of compounds found in various parts of the plant, including its seeds, leaves, and bark.

These phenolic acids have been shown to possess significant antioxidant, antimicrobial, and anti-inflammatory activities, making them valuable for their potential health benefits.

The main types of phenolic acids present in cashew include gallic acid, ellagic acid, and chlorogenic acid.
