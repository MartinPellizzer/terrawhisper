Cashew (Anacardium occidentale) Compounds

Cashew contains several primary active compounds, including tannins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarin and saponins also play roles in its medicinal effects, while essential oils and terpenoids provide aromatic and potential therapeutic benefits. Glycosides, anthocyanins, and alkaloids further enhance its pharmacological profile.

Cashew (Anacardium occidentale) Monograph

1. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Cashew (Anacardium occidentale). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins in cashew may support digestive health and skin healing, making it valuable in herbal medicine.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Cashew (Anacardium occidentale) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting their use in traditional herbal medicine. They may help in reducing oxidative stress and promoting overall health. Flavonoids are found in various parts of the plant, including the nuts and leaves.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Cashew (Anacardium occidentale). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound is also associated with anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects in traditional herbal medicine.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Cashew (Anacardium occidentale). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, including anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory effects. Coumarin is also responsible for the plant's characteristic fragrance and has been studied for its potential therapeutic applications.

5. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Cashew (Anacardium occidentale). It exhibits antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, contributing to the plant's traditional use in treating skin infections and digestive issues. Saponin's ability to disrupt cell membranes makes it valuable in herbal medicine for its therapeutic potential.

6. Essential oil

The essential oil of Cashew (Anacardium occidentale) contains volatile compounds like anacardic acid and cardol, which have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It is used in traditional herbal medicine for treating skin infections and respiratory issues due to its potent therapeutic effects.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Cashew (Anacardium occidentale) are a class of bioactive compounds known for their diverse pharmacological properties. These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating inflammation and skin conditions. They also exhibit antimicrobial and antioxidant activities, supporting their role in herbal medicine.

8. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Cashew (Anacardium occidentale). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal medicine. Glycosides in cashew may support cardiovascular health and have anti-inflammatory effects, though further research is needed to confirm their therapeutic applications.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Cashew (Anacardium occidentale). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports the therapeutic potential of Cashew in promoting health and preventing chronic diseases.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Cashew (Anacardium occidentale). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, though its specific therapeutic uses are not well-documented. Alkaloids in cashew may influence various physiological processes, but further research is needed to fully understand their role in herbal medicine.
