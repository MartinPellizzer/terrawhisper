Dryopteris cycadina

What's the taxonomical classification of Dryopteris cycadina?

Dryopteris cycadina belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. Taxonomically, it is situated in the family Polypodiaceae, within the genus Dryopteris, and is specifically identified by its species name, cycadina.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Dryopteris cycadina contains various secondary metabolites including flavonoids, tannins, and potentially glycosides that contribute to its chemical profile. These bioactive compounds are often studied for their antioxidant and antimicrobial properties within the fern genus. Specific alkaloids may also be present, which can influence the plant's biological interactions with microorganisms. The concentration of these medicinal substances can vary significantly depending on the plant's environmental conditions and growth stage. Understanding these constituents is essential for exploring any potential pharmacological applications of the species.

What are the therapeutic actions of Dryopteris cycadina?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various parasitic infections of the intestinal tract, specifically those caused by tapeworms and other helminths. Historically, extracts from this fern species served as an anthelmintic agent to expel worms from the digestive system. The biological activity of the plant relies on specific chemical compounds that disrupt the life cycles of these internal parasites. It was frequently included in traditional medicinal preparations designed to cleanse the bowels of infestations. While its use has diminished with modern pharmacology, it remains a significant subject in the study of historical botanical treatments.
