Dryopteris cycadina

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dryopteris cycadina has large, pinnately compound fronds that exhibit a distinctively arching growth habit and a texture reminiscent of ancient cycads. The individual pinnae are broadly lanceolate with deeply lobed or serrated margins that give the foliage a textured appearance. Stipes are typically sturdy and clothed in pale, brownish scales that persist along the base of the leaf structure. The arrangement of the leaflets creates a lush, spreading canopy that maximizes surface area for light absorption. These morphological features collectively define the plant's prehistoric aesthetic and structural resilience.
