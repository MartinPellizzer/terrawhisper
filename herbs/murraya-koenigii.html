Murraya koenigii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Murraya koenigii has pinnately compound leaves consisting of 11 to 21 small, lanceolate leaflets that are dark green, glossy, and arranged in opposite pairs along a central rachis. The leaflets typically measure between 1 and 3 centimeters in length and feature entire margins with acute tips. The plant is a perennial shrub or small tree that can reach heights of up to 4 meters in optimal conditions. Its woody stems are often branched and can exhibit a slightly grayish bark texture as the plant matures. Small, white, fragrant flowers emerge from the axils, eventually giving way to small, black, fleshy drupes.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the Indian subcontinent, specifically spanning across India, Sri Lanka, and parts of Southeast Asia. It thrives primarily in tropical and subtropical regions where the climate remains warm and humid throughout the year. In its natural habitat, it is often found growing in well-drained soils along the edges of forests or in open scrublands. While its origins are centered in South Asia, it has been widely introduced to other tropical regions around the world for culinary and medicinal purposes. Because of its adaptability, it can be found cultivated in many gardens and agricultural zones globally.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Murraya koenigii contains a diverse array of bioactive phytochemicals including carbazole alkaloids such as mahanimbine, koenimbine, and girinimbine, along with essential oils like carvone and caryophyllene. These specific alkaloids are recognized for their potent antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. The plant also provides significant amounts of vitamins A and C, as well as various minerals like calcium and iron. Research indicates that these medicinal compounds can help regulate blood glucose levels and protect against oxidative stress in human cells. Consequently, the leaves are frequently utilized in traditional medicine to treat ailments ranging from digestive issues to skin infections.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Murraya koenigii targets various enzymes and signaling proteins, including cyclooxygenase-2, lipoxygenase, acetylcholinesterase, and alpha-glucosidase, to exert its medicinal effects. Its bioactive carbazole alkaloids and terpenoids act as potent inhibitors of cyclooxygenase-2 to reduce inflammation through the suppression of prostaglandin synthesis. The plant also modulates neurotransmitter levels by inhibiting acetylcholinesterase, which suggests a mechanism for enhancing cognitive function. Additionally, the inhibition of alpha-glucosidase helps regulate blood glucose levels by slowing down carbohydrate digestion in the gut. These interactions with specific protein pathways allow the plant to address diverse conditions ranging from oxidative stress to metabolic disorders.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause hypoglycemia, nausea, gastrointestinal distress, and dizziness in some individuals. Consuming excessive amounts may lead to a significant drop in blood sugar levels, which is particularly concerning for diabetics. Some people also report experiencing stomach upset or various forms of gastrointestinal distress after ingestion. Nausea is another common reaction that may occur following consumption. Additionally, certain users have experienced bouts of dizziness that could impair their coordination.
