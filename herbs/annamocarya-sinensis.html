Annamocarya sinensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Annamocarya sinensis has a deciduous tree form characterized by pinnately compound leaves, small yellowish-green flowers, and winged nutlets. The leaves typically feature an odd number of leaflets that are lanceolate in shape with serrated margins. During the flowering stage, the small blossoms emerge in dense panicles from the axils of the branches. The fruit consists of nutlets equipped with elongated, papery wings that facilitate wind dispersal. Mature specimens develop rugged, fissured bark that provides a protective layer for the woody trunk.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to specific mountainous regions within central and southern China, primarily occurring in provinces such as Hubei, Hunan, and Guizhou. It typically inhabits subtropical monsoon climates where it grows in shaded areas of broad-leaved forests. These populations are often found at moderate elevations ranging from several hundred to over one thousand meters above sea level. The species thrives in moist, well-drained soils characteristic of hilly or montane terrain. Localized distributions make it a specialized component of the regional flora in these temperate to subtropical zones.

    What are the herbal preparations of Annamocarya sinensis?

    Annamocarya sinensis is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a powder derived from its dried fruits. The most common traditional method involves boiling the dried fruit in water to extract its medicinal components for oral consumption. Some practitioners also utilize the fruit in the form of a tincture by soaking it in alcohol to concentrate the active compounds. Additionally, the dried fruit can be ground into a fine powder to be used in various medicinal mixtures. These diverse preparation methods allow for different levels of potency and absorption depending on the intended therapeutic use.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Annamocarya sinensis can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, including nausea, vomiting, and abdominal cramping, if the plant material is ingested improperly. The presence of certain bioactive compounds may lead to unpredictable systemic reactions in sensitive individuals. Some reports suggest that excessive consumption could potentially interfere with metabolic processes or cause localized irritation of the digestive tract. It is essential to exercise caution because the specific toxicity levels can vary depending on the plant part used or the concentration of its chemical constituents.
