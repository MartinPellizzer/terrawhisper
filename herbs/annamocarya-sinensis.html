Annamocarya sinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Annamocarya sinensis?

Annamocarya sinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further categorized under the order Fagales. It falls within the family Juglandaceae, which places it in the genus Annamocarya, eventually reaching its specific designation as the species sinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Annamocarya sinensis has a deciduous tree form characterized by pinnately compound leaves, small yellowish-green flowers, and winged nutlets. During the flowering stage, the small blossoms emerge in dense panicles from the axils of the branches. The fruit consists of nutlets equipped with elongated, papery wings that facilitate wind dispersal. Mature specimens develop rugged, fissured bark that provides a protective layer for the woody trunk.
