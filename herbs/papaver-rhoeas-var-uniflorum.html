Common Poppy (Papaver Rhoeas Var. Uniflorum)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Papaver rhoeas var. uniflorum.

*Papaver rhoeas var. uniflorum*, a variant of the common poppy, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential therapeutic properties. While primarily known for its ornamental value, this variety contains alkaloids similar to those found in other poppy species, which may have analgesic and sedative effects. Some historical uses suggest it was employed to alleviate pain and promote relaxation, though scientific research on its medicinal efficacy remains limited. Due to its close relation to the opium poppy, its use in modern medicine is restricted and regulated. Nonetheless, it continues to be studied for its potential role in alternative and complementary medicine.
