What's the taxonomical classification of Alafia lucida?

Alafia lucida belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. As a member of the order Gentianales, it falls into the family Apocynaceae. At the most specific levels of its classification, the plant is identified by the genus Alafia and the species lucida.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Alafia lucida has lanceolate, glossy green leaves with entire margins and a distinctively prominent central vein that extends to the apex. The foliage is arranged in an opposite pattern along a semi-woody stem characterized by a smooth, light brown epidermis. Small, actinomorphic flowers emerge from the leaf axils, featuring five white petals and a central cluster of yellow stamens. The reproductive structures are supported by a slender pedicel that allows the blooms to dangle slightly below the canopy. Below the surface, a fibrous root system provides stability and efficient nutrient absorption from the surrounding soil.
