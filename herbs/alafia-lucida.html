Alafia lucida

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Alafia lucida has lanceolate, glossy green leaves with entire margins and a distinctively prominent central vein that extends to the apex. The foliage is arranged in an opposite pattern along a semi-woody stem characterized by a smooth, light brown epidermis. Small, actinomorphic flowers emerge from the leaf axils, featuring five white petals and a central cluster of yellow stamens. The reproductive structures are supported by a slender pedicel that allows the blooms to dangle slightly below the canopy. Below the surface, a fibrous root system provides stability and efficient nutrient absorption from the surrounding soil.

      What are the herbal preparations of Alafia lucida?

      Alafia lucida is prepared as a decoction of the dried leaves, a topical poultice made from crushed fresh foliage, or an infused oil for skin application. The decoction method involves simmering the plant material in water to extract potent medicinal compounds for internal consumption. When used topically, the macerated leaves are applied directly to the skin to soothe inflammation or localized discomfort.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Alafia lucida can cause severe skin irritation, localized swelling, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested in significant quantities. Contact with the plant's sap often results in contact dermatitis characterized by redness and itching. Some individuals may experience more systemic reactions such as nausea or abdominal cramping after accidental consumption. It is also possible for the plant to trigger allergic respiratory responses in highly sensitive people. Careful avoidance of direct skin contact and ingestion is necessary to prevent these adverse reactions.
