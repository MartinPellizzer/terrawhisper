Cremanthodium helianthus

What's the taxonomical classification of Cremanthodium helianthus?

Cremanthodium helianthus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical structure of biological classification, it falls under the order Asterales and is a member of the family Asteraceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Cremanthodium, and its unique species name, helianthus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cremanthodium helianthus has an erect, herbaceous stem that typically reaches heights between 30 and 100 centimeters. The leaves are primarily basal or alternate, featuring deeply lobed or pinnatifid margins and a somewhat coarse texture. Small, bright yellow flower heads are arranged in terminal corymbose cymes, characteristic of the Asteraceae family. Each composite flower head consists of numerous disc florets surrounded by narrow ray florets. The plant also possesses a branched root system that helps anchor it in its preferred wetland habitats.
