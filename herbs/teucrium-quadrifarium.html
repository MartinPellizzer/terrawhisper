What's the taxonomical classification of Teucrium quadrifarium?

Teucrium quadrifarium belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its hierarchical descent, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Lamiales, it falls under the family Lamiaceae, which is a group known for its diverse aromatic characteristics. The plant is further categorized into the genus Teucrium, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species quadrifarium.

What are the pharmacological activities of Teucrium quadrifarium?

This plant has significant pharmacological activities including anti-ferroptosis, antioxidant, and anti-inflammatory properties. Research indicates that its bioactive compounds effectively mitigate iron-dependent programmed cell death, which is a critical mechanism in various neurodegenerative and metabolic diseases. The phytochemical profile of Teucrium quadrifarium contributes to its ability to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress within cellular environments. Furthermore, its ability to modulate inflammatory pathways suggests potential therapeutic applications in managing chronic inflammatory conditions. These combined medicinal qualities highlight the plant's importance in pharmacological studies focused on cell protection and disease prevention.

As discussed in a study, Teucrium quadrifarium yielded four previously unreported neo-clerodane-type diterpenoids named teucrifarides A-D (1-4) along with sixteen known analogs (5-20). Among these components, compound 1 and compound 12 demonstrated significant inhibitory effects against ferroptosis in HT-22 cells induced by RSL3. Specifically, compound 1 exhibited an EC50 value of 11.8 ± 1.0 μM. Compound 12 showed a more potent inhibitory effect with an EC50 value of 4.52 ± 1.24 μM. Additionally, the treatment of HT22 cells with compound 1 resulted in observed ROS accumulation.
