What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Teucrium quadrifarium has opposite, decussate leaves that are typically divided into four distinct lobes, giving the species its name. Its stems are woody at the base and become more herbaceous toward the growing tips. The inflorescence features small, tubular flowers that are characteristic of the Lamiaceae family.
