White Peony (Paeonia Lactiflora Var. Trichocarpa)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Paeonia lactiflora var. trichocarpa.

White peony, scientifically known as Paeonia lactiflora var. trichocarpa, is a medicinal herb widely recognized for its adaptogenic properties and use in traditional Chinese medicine. It is prized for its ability to support cardiovascular health, alleviate stress, and promote emotional balance, primarily due to its active compounds like paeoniflorin and benzoylpethidine. Historically, white peony has been used in Chinese herbal practices for centuries to treat pain, inflammation, and menstrual disorders, and it remains a key ingredient in formulations for anxiety and insomnia. In modern wellness, it is increasingly incorporated into supplements and aromatherapy products for its calming effects and potential to enhance mental resilience. Notably, its distinctive floral aroma and rare active compounds have made it a unique and valued component in both traditional and contemporary holistic health approaches.
