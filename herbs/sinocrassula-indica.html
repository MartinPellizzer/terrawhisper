Sinocrassula indica

What's the taxonomical classification of Sinocrassula indica?

Sinocrassula indica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Saxifragales. This plant is further categorized into the family Crassulaceae, within the genus Sinocrassula, and is specifically identified by the species name indica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sinocrassula indica has fleshy, succulent leaves that are arranged in dense, overlapping rosettes on short, upright stems. These leaves are typically small, ovate to lanceolate in shape, and possess a smooth, slightly glaucous surface texture. The plant displays a compact growth habit, often forming tight mats of greenery in its natural rocky habitats. During its flowering period, small, star-shaped blossoms emerge from the center of the leaf rosettes. The coloration of the foliage can range from a bright green to reddish hues depending on environmental stress and light exposure.
