Serrated Agathosma

Agathosma serratifolia

Serrated Agathosma (Agathosma serratifolia) is a small, aromatic shrub native to South Africa, known for its use in traditional medicine to support respiratory health and alleviate symptoms of asthma and bronchitis.

Taxonomy

Serrated Agathosma (Agathosma serratifolia) is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae, belonging to the genus Agathosma within the order Asterales.

Morphology

Serrated Agathosma (Agathosma serratifolia) is a perennial herb with upright, branching stems that are hairy and often reddish in color, bearing opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and a strong, aromatic scent.

Distribution

Serrated Agathosma (Agathosma serratifolia) is native to the coastal regions of the Western Cape Province in South Africa, specifically growing in sandy and gravelly soils along the Atlantic seaboard.

Traditional Uses

Serrated Agathosma (Agathosma serratifolia) has been traditionally used in South African herbal medicine to treat respiratory conditions such as coughs, colds, and bronchitis, as well as to alleviate symptoms of asthma and other inflammatory lung disorders.

Medicinal Systems

Serrated Agathosma (Agathosma serratifolia) has been traditionally used in African herbal medicine, particularly by the San and Khoi peoples, for its purported anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and wound-healing properties.

Phytochemistry

The phytochemistry of Serrated Agathosma (Agathosma serratifolia) is characterized by the presence of essential oils, flavonoids, and iridoids, with key compounds including thymol, carvacrol, and agathosmin, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Pharmacology

Serrated Agathosma (Agathosma serratifolia) exhibits a range of pharmacological properties including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and analgesic effects, making it valuable in the treatment of inflammatory conditions, infections, and pain-related disorders.

Botanical Identification

Serrated Agathosma, scientifically known as Agathosma serratifolia, belongs to the Asteraceae family. It is also called "Cape Sage" in South Africa and is a perennial shrub native to the Western Cape. The plant has serrated leaves, yellow flowers, and a fibrous root system. It is sometimes referred to as "Agathosma dentata" in older herbal texts.
