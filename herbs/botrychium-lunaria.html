Botrychium lunaria

What's the taxonomical classification of Botrychium lunaria?

Botrychium lunaria belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Ophioglossidae and the order Ophioglossales. Further taxonomic refinement places it in the family Ophioglossaceae, specifically within the genus Botrychium, eventually reaching its unique designation as the species lunaria.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Botrychium lunaria contains no documented medicinal compounds or specific pharmacological properties in current scientific literature. As a member of the moonwort family, its chemical constituents remain largely unstudied compared to more common medicinal herbs. Most botanical research regarding this species focuses on its ecological role and taxonomic classification rather than its therapeutic potential. There is a lack of clinical evidence or traditional use records to support any specific health benefits derived from the plant. Consequently, its biochemical makeup is not yet characterized for pharmaceutical applications.
