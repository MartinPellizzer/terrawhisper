What's the taxonomical classification of Caesalpinia decapetala?

Caesalpinia decapetala belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Fabales. The plant is a member of the family Fabaceae and is specifically categorized within the genus Caesalpinia, ultimately reaching its specific identification as the species decapetala.

    Caesalpinia decapetalha can cause skin irritation and allergic contact dermatitis when the plant or its sap comes into direct contact with human skin. While direct toxicity to internal organs is not widely documented, ingestion of various parts of the plant could potentially lead to gastrointestinal distress. Caution is advised when handling the shrub to prevent accidental contact with the eyes or mucous membranes. Careful monitoring is necessary for anyone developing an unexpected reaction after exposure to this species.
