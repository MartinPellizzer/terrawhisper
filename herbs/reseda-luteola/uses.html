Common Reseda (Reseda luteola) Uses

Common Reseda is used to treat skin infections, wound healing, and inflammatory skin disorders. It helps with fungal infections, skin irritation, and itching, making it effective for conditions like mouth ulcers and skin conditions. Its anti-inflammatory and antifungal properties support its use in addressing skin inflammation and itchiness.

Common Reseda (Reseda luteola) Monograph

1. Skin infections

Common Reseda (Reseda luteola) is used topically to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce bacterial growth and soothe irritated skin, making it effective for conditions like eczema and minor fungal infections. Its astringent qualities also aid in drying out wounds and preventing secondary infections.

2. Wound healing

Common Reseda (Reseda luteola) is used in herbal medicine to support wound healing due to its astringent and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce swelling, prevent infection, and promote tissue repair. Traditionally applied as a poultice or infused oil, it is valued for its ability to clean and close wounds more effectively.

3. Skin inflammation

Common Reseda (Reseda luteola) has been traditionally used to treat skin inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and irritation, making it useful for conditions like eczema and rashes. Its mild action makes it suitable for sensitive skin when prepared properly.

4. Mouth ulcers

Common Reseda (Reseda luteola) is used topically to treat mouth ulcers due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. A decoction of the leaves can be applied directly to the affected area to reduce pain and promote healing. It helps soothe irritation and speed up the recovery process.

5. Itchiness

Common Reseda (Reseda luteola) is used externally to relieve itchiness, particularly in skin conditions like eczema and psoriasis. Its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties help reduce irritation and promote healing. A decoction or infusion can be applied as a wash or compress for localized relief.

6. Fungal infections

Common Reseda (Reseda luteola) has been traditionally used to treat fungal infections due to its antifungal properties. The plant contains compounds that inhibit the growth of fungi, making it effective for conditions like athlete's foot and ringworm. It is often prepared as a topical salve or infusion to apply directly to affected areas.

7. Skin irritation

Common Reseda (Reseda luteola) is used to alleviate skin irritation due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It can help reduce redness, swelling, and itching associated with minor skin conditions. Traditionally applied as a poultice or infusion, it provides soothing relief for sensitive or inflamed skin.

8. Skin conditions

Common Reseda (Reseda luteola) has been traditionally used to treat skin conditions such as eczema and psoriasis due to its anti-inflammatory and antifungal properties. It helps soothe irritated skin and reduce redness, making it a valuable herb in herbal medicine for dermatological care.

9. Inflammatory skin disorders

Common Reseda (Reseda luteola) has been traditionally used to address inflammatory skin disorders due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and irritation, making it useful for conditions like eczema and dermatitis. Its topical application can soothe and protect the skin, supporting healing in inflamed areas.

10. Itching

Common Reseda (Reseda luteola) has been traditionally used to alleviate itching, particularly in skin conditions such as eczema and psoriasis. Its antihistaminic and anti-inflammatory properties help reduce irritation and soothe the skin. External applications of its extracts or infusions are commonly recommended for localized relief.
