Common Reseda (Reseda luteola) Compounds

The primary active compounds in Common Reseda include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and tannins, which contribute to astringent effects. Essential oils and coumarin provide aromatic and anti-inflammatory benefits, while phenolic acids and saponins support detoxification and skin health. Glycosides, terpenoids, and alkaloids also play roles in therapeutic actions, and anthocyanins contribute to its vibrant color and potential antioxidant activity.

Common Reseda (Reseda luteola) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Common Reseda (Reseda luteola) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds possess antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting the plant's traditional use in treating skin conditions and respiratory issues. They help protect cells from oxidative stress and may modulate immune responses.

2. Tannin

Tannin in Common Reseda (Reseda luteola) contributes to its astringent properties. It helps reduce inflammation and can aid in treating skin conditions. Tannins also support wound healing and may have antimicrobial effects. This compound is a key component in the plant's traditional medicinal use.

3. Essential oil

The essential oil of Common Reseda (Reseda luteola) contains volatile compounds like linalool and geraniol. These contribute to its aromatic properties and may have mild antiseptic and soothing effects. The oil is often used in traditional herbal preparations for its calming and skin-nourishing qualities.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Common Reseda (Reseda luteola). It contributes to the plant's mild sedative and antispasmodic properties. Coumarin also has anticoagulant effects, making it relevant in herbal formulations for circulation and nervous system support.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid in Common Reseda (Reseda luteola) contributes to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. It helps in reducing oxidative stress and supporting skin health. This compound is part of the plant's broader therapeutic profile in traditional herbal medicine.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Common Reseda (Reseda luteola). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and skin conditions. Saponin exhibits mild expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. It is often used in herbal formulations for its soothing effects.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Common Reseda (Reseda luteola). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and skin conditions. Glycosides in Reseda luteola are known for their mild sedative and anti-inflammatory properties. They are often extracted for herbal formulations.

8. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Common Reseda (Reseda luteola) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects, supporting the plant's traditional use in treating skin conditions and respiratory ailments. They are among the key bioactive components responsible for the plant's therapeutic value.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid in Common Reseda (Reseda luteola) is a naturally occurring nitrogen-containing compound with potential pharmacological effects. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine, though specific therapeutic applications require further research.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a pigment found in Common Reseda (Reseda luteola). It contributes to the plant's vibrant flower color and may have antioxidant properties. While not the primary medicinal component, anthocyanin supports the plant's overall therapeutic profile in traditional herbal use.
