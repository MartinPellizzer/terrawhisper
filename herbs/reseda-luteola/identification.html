Botanical Identification of Common Reseda (Reseda luteola)

To identify Common Reseda (Reseda luteola) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Common Reseda (Reseda luteola) belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Spermatophyta, class Caryophyllopsida, order Brassicales, family Brassicaceae, genus Reseda, and species luteola. This classification places it within the flowering plants and the mustard family, which includes many economically important species.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Common Reseda is Reseda luteola, first described by the botanist Jean-Baptiste Lamarck in 1785. It is also known by several synonyms, including Reseda officinalis and Reseda lutea, reflecting its historical classification and regional usage. Common names such as Yellow Reseda and Garden Reseda highlight its botanical and medicinal significance.

Morphological Characteristics

Common Reseda (Reseda luteola) is an annual herb with a taproot system. It has erect, branching stems that are hairy and reddish at the base. Leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, with hairy surfaces and prominent veins. The inflorescence is a dense spike of yellow flowers, each with five petals and numerous stamens. The fruit is a slender capsule containing small, brown seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Reseda luteola can be distinguished by its bright yellow flowers and slender, branching stems. It has alternate, linear leaves with a hairy texture. It lacks the glandular dots found on the leaves of Reseda odorata. Its flowers are smaller and more numerous compared to other similar species.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Common Reseda (Reseda luteola) is native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East. It thrives in temperate climates with mild summers and cool winters. It prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils. It is typically found at altitudes between 0 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Common Reseda (Reseda luteola) blooms from late spring to early autumn, typically between May and September. Seed formation occurs from late summer to early fall, around August to October. For identification purposes, the plant is best harvested during its flowering period, from late spring through early autumn.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Reseda luteola belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae, sharing traits with other members like Malva and Dianthus. It is closely related to species such as Reseda alba and Reseda odorata. These relationships are evident in their morphological and genetic similarities, which aid in accurate identification within the genus.
