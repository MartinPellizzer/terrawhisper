Campsis radicans

What's the taxonomical classification of Campsis radicans?

Campsis radicans belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized under the order Lamiales and falls within the family Bignoniaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Campsis and the species radicans.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Campsis radicans has woody, twining stems that produce large, pinnately compound leaves consisting of seven to eleven leaflets with serrated margins. The plant features trumpet-shaped, bright orange or red flowers that grow in terminal clusters. These blossoms are characterized by a long, tubular corolla designed to attract pollinators like hummingbirds. The leaves are arranged oppositely along the vines, which can climb significantly using aerial rootlets. The foliage remains mostly green, though it may turn reddish in autumn.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant, commonly known as trumpet vine, requires full sun to bloom profusely and thrives in well-draining, fertile soil.

It is a vigorous climber that uses aerial rootlets to scale fences, trellises, or large trees. Because it grows extremely rapidly, regular pruning is necessary to prevent it from overtaking nearby vegetation or structures. While it is quite drought-tolerant once established, consistent moisture during the first growing season helps ensure healthy development.
