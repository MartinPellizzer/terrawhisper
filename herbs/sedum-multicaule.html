Sedum multicaule

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sedum multicaule has succulent, cylindrical to slightly flattened leaves that grow in dense, fleshy rosettes along creeping or ascending stems. These leaves are typically light green to glaucous in color and measure only a few millimeters in width. The plant often forms low-growing mats or cushions composed of multiple stems that emerge from a common root system. During the flowering stage, it produces small, star-shaped flowers that are usually pink, white, or yellowish. These blossoms are clustered in terminal cymes atop the fleshy stems.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of central and eastern Asia, specifically spanning parts of China, Tibet, and Mongolia. It typically thrives in alpine and subalpine environments located on rocky slopes or scree. Its range is primarily concentrated within the mountainous territories of the Qinghai-Tibetan Plateau. These specific habitats provide the cold, well-drained conditions necessary for its survival. Because it is adapted to extreme elevations, its distribution remains limited to these specialized mountain ecosystems.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Sedum multicaule can cause skin irritation or allergic dermatitis if the plant's sap comes into direct contact with sensitive skin. There is limited clinical data regarding the internal effects of ingestion, but consuming any unknown plant part can lead to gastrointestinal distress. It is best to use gloves when gardening with this species to prevent unexpected dermatological reactions.
