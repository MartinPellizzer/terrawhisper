Finally, the plant is defined by its specific classification under the genus Cinnamomum and the species iners.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cinnamomum iners has simple, evergreen leaves that are elliptical or lanceolate in shape, measuring approximately 5 to 15 centimeters in length, with an entire margin and an acuminate tip. Its stems are woody and can grow into a small tree or shrub, often characterized by a smooth to slightly fissured bark.
