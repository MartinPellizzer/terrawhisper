What's the taxonomical classification of Vaccinium henryi?

Vaccinium henryi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. As a member of the order Ericales, it falls within the family Ericaceae. Specifically, this plant is part of the genus Vaccinium, and its unique taxonomic identity is defined by its species name, henryi.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vaccinium henryi has deciduous, opposite leaves that are typically elliptic to ovate in shape, measuring between 2 and 5 centimeters in length with finely serrated margins and acute tips. Its flowers are small, urceolate, and white, emerging from the axils of the leaves during the blooming season. Following pollination, the plant produces small, globose berries that transition from green to a dark, glaucous blue color when mature.
