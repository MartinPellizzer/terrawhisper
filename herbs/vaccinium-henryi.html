Vaccinium henryi

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vaccinium henryi has deciduous, opposite leaves that are typically elliptic to ovate in shape, measuring between 2 and 5 centimeters in length with finely serrated margins and acute tips. The plant grows as a low-spreading subshrub characterized by slender, woody stems that can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its flowers are small, urceolate, and white, emerging from the axils of the leaves during the blooming season. Following pollination, the plant produces small, globose berries that transition from green to a dark, glaucous blue color when mature. The overall structure is compact, often forming dense mats across its natural forest floor habitat.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountain ranges of central and southern China, specifically occurring in provinces such as Sichuan, Yunnan, and Guizhou. It is typically found growing at high elevations within montane forests or shrublands. The species thrives in cool, moist environments often associated with temperate woodland ecosystems. Its presence is closely tied to the rugged terrain and specific climatic conditions of these East Asian highland regions. Due to these specialized habitat requirements, its distribution remains localized to these specific mountainous corridors.

      What are the herbal preparations of Vaccinium henryi?

      Vaccinium henryi is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a concentrated tincture to extract its medicinal properties.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Vaccinium henryi can cause allergic reactions such as skin irritation, swelling, or digestive upset if consumed in large quantities or by sensitive individuals. While many species in the Vaccinium genus are considered safe, specific botanical compounds may trigger sensitivities in certain people. Symptoms might include nausea, stomach cramps, or mild rashes upon contact with the plant. It is important to note that there is limited clinical research regarding the long-term toxicity of this specific species in humans. Therefore, caution should be exercised when handling or ingesting any part of the plant to avoid unexpected adverse effects.
