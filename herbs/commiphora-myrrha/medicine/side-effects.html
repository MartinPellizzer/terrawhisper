10 Possible Side Effects Of Commiphora Myrrha

Commiphora myrrha has some side effects when used improperly, such as stomach upset, diarrhea, and allergic reactions.

These side effects can be caused by excessive consumption or sensitization to the herb's volatile oils.

In severe cases, myrrh overdose can lead to liver damage and exacerbate underlying health conditions like hypertension, heart disease, and kidney problems, significantly affecting individuals' quality of life and overall well-being.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Commiphora myrrha causes allergic reactions due to its high concentration of sesquiterpenes and terpenoids.

These compounds can stimulate an immune response, leading to symptoms such as skin irritation, itching, and redness in some individuals.

Additionally, the aromatic oils present in myrrh may cause respiratory issues like congestion and coughing, particularly in people with pre-existing respiratory conditions or sensitivities.

2. Triggers digestive issues

Commiphora myrrha triggers digestive issues due to its bitter and astringent properties.

The resinous extract can cause gastrointestinal disturbances such as nausea, vomiting, and stomach pain, potentially leading to diarrhea or constipation. This is because the compound beta-eudesmol present in Commiphora myrrha can irritate the mucous membranes of the digestive tract, disrupting normal gut function.

As a result, individuals may experience uncomfortable symptoms when using this herb.

3. Induces anxiety attacks

Commiphora myrrha induces anxiety attacks due to its high concentration of sesquiterpenes, particularly beta-bisabolene and alpha-caryophyllene.

These compounds can stimulate the brain's limbic system, leading to increased activity in areas responsible for emotional processing. This may result in feelings of unease, apprehension, and panic, as well as rapid heartbeat and sweating.

The intense aroma of myrrh can also overwhelm the senses, exacerbating anxiety symptoms.

4. Produces dizziness symptoms

Commiphora myrrha produces dizziness symptoms due to its high concentration of sesquiterpenes and terpenoids.

These compounds can cause a stimulant effect on the central nervous system, leading to feelings of lightheadedness and dizziness.

Additionally, myrrh's ability to stimulate circulation may also contribute to this side effect by increasing blood flow to the brain, which can further exacerbate symptoms.

5. Enhances bleeding risk

Commiphora myrrha enhances bleeding risk due to its ability to thin the blood and disrupt platelet function.

The resin's anticoagulant properties can interfere with blood clotting, increasing the likelihood of excessive bleeding following injury or surgery. Additionally, the compound curcumin, present in myrrh, has been shown to inhibit platelet aggregation, further contributing to this effect.

This increased bleeding risk is a potential side effect that should be considered when using Commiphora myrrha.

6. Lowers blood pressure

Commiphora myrrha lowers blood pressure by dilating blood vessels and improving vasodilation.

The essential oil present in myrrh contains compounds such as boswellic acid and terpenes, which have a direct impact on the cardiovascular system, leading to a decrease in blood pressure. This effect is due to the stimulation of alpha-adrenergic receptors, causing peripheral resistance to drop and cardiac output to increase.

As a result, users may experience a decline in their resting blood pressure.

7. Increases heart rate

Commiphora myrrha increases heart rate due to its stimulant properties.

The essential oil's ability to dilate blood vessels and increase cardiac output can lead to a rapid pulse. Additionally, the oil's beta-eudesmol and sesquiterpene compounds may further contribute to this effect by stimulating the nervous system and increasing sympathetic activity.

This increased heart rate is a common side effect when using Commiphora myrrha, particularly in high doses or through prolonged use.

8. Affects kidney function

Commiphora myrrha affects kidney function by interfering with the normal filtration process of waste and excess substances.

This may occur due to its ability to slow down blood flow through the kidneys, which can lead to a decrease in urine production and an accumulation of toxins. Additionally, myrrh's anticoagulant properties may cause bleeding in the kidneys, further compromising their function.

These effects can manifest as frequent urination, dark-colored urine, or even kidney damage over time.

9. Interferes with thyroid hormones

Commiphora myrrha interferes with thyroid hormones by inhibiting the conversion of thyroxine (T4) to triiodothyronine (T3), which is essential for proper thyroid function.

The resinous extract from the plant contains compounds that compete with iodine in the body, disrupting the normal functioning of the thyroid gland and potentially leading to hypothyroidism or hyperthyroidism symptoms.

10. Stimulates uterine contractions

Commiphora myrrha stimulates uterine contractions due to its ability to cause the uterus to contract and tighten.

This is likely caused by the presence of cymene, a compound found in myrrh that can stimulate the smooth muscle of the uterus, leading to increased uterine tone and contractions.

As a result, pregnant women should use caution when using Commiphora myrrha as it may cause miscarriage or premature labor.
