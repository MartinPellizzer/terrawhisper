Morphology

Aspalathus linearis is a small, evergreen shrub characterized by its upright growth habit, narrow linear leaves arranged in opposite pairs, and small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to red in color, blooming in clusters along the stems.

Distribution

Aspalathus linearis is native to the Western Cape region of South Africa, specifically growing in the fynbos biome along the coastal areas and mountainous regions.

Traditional Uses

Aspalathus linearis, commonly known as rooibos, has been traditionally used in South African herbal medicine for its soothing properties, particularly in treating digestive issues, respiratory conditions, and as a mild sedative.

Aspalathus linearis, commonly known as rooibos, has been traditionally used in African herbal medicine systems, particularly among the Khoisan people of South Africa, for its soothing and therapeutic properties.

Safety Profile

Aspalathus linearis is generally safe when consumed in recommended doses. However, it may cause mild gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. There are no well-documented contraindications, but pregnant and breastfeeding women should consult a healthcare provider before use.
