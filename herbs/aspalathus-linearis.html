Aspalathus Linearis (Aspalathus linearis)

Aspalathus Linearis (Aspalathus linearis) is a member of the Fabaceae family, native to Cape Province, South Africa, Western Cape, South Africa, Eastern Cape, and South Africa. Traditionally, its leaves, flowers, and stems have been used for infusions, decoctions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, diuretic, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in african traditional medicine, european herbal medicine, and mediterranean herbal traditions.

Leaves
Lanceolate leaves with upper surface a glossy dark green and lower surface a lighter, often glaucous green, featuring distinct stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Inflorescence consisting of solitary flowers with actinomorphic symmetry, each flower having five white petals with a yellowish glandular spot at the base and five green sepals.
Stems
Erect, unbranched, woody, with smooth, ridged surface and persistent stipules.
