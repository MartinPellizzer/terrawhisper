Aspalathus linearis

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Aspalathus linearis, commonly known as rooibos, is a leguminous shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa. It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its fine, linear leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is perennial and has a deep root system, with synonyms including "red bush" and "red tea" in herbal contexts.

Active Compounds

Aspalathus linearis contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and tannins. These compounds work synergistically to provide its therapeutic effects, primarily through antioxidant and anti-inflammatory mechanisms. Their combined action enhances the herb's efficacy in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Phenolic acid
  • Tannin

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action
Respiratory Inflammation, cough, mucosal inflammation Anti-inflammatory, expectorant, antimicrobial
Immune Allergies, immune suppression, oxidative stress, allergic reactions Antihistaminic, immunostimulant, antioxidant
Integumentary Skin irritation, skin infections, eczema, inflammatory skin conditions, skin aging Antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, antioxidant

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for digestive issues and as a mild diuretic.

Extract: Used for its antioxidant properties and to support heart health.

Decoction: Used for respiratory conditions and to reduce inflammation.

Safety Profile

Aspalathus linearis is generally safe when consumed in recommended doses. However, it may cause mild gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. There are no well-documented contraindications, but pregnant and breastfeeding women should consult a healthcare provider before use.
