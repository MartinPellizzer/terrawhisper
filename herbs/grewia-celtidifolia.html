Grewia celtidifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Grewia celtidifolia?

Grewia celtidifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Malvales. This plant is a member of the family Malvaceae, which falls under the genus Grewia, and is specifically identified by the species name celtidifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Grewia celtidifolia has small, ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated margins, woody stems, and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in axillary clusters.

The foliage is typically characterized by a slightly leathery texture and a dark green color on the upper surface. Its branching pattern is often irregular, contributing to a somewhat shrubby or small tree-like growth habit. The bark on older specimens tends to become textured and brownish as the plant matures.

These morphological traits help distinguish it from other members of the Grewiaceae family.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Grewia celtidifolia contains medicinal properties found primarily in its leaves, bark, and roots. The leaves are often prepared as infusions to treat various inflammatory conditions and digestive ailments. Extracts from the bark are utilized in traditional practices to address skin irritations and systemic infections. Some indigenous applications also involve using the root decoctions to manage pain or fever. These different parts work together to provide a diverse range of therapeutic benefits in folk medicine.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Grewia celtidifolia targets inflammatory mediators and oxidative stress enzymes, specifically inhibiting cyclooxygenase (COX) enzymes and reducing pro-inflammatory cytokines. The plant contains bioactive secondary metabolites that modulate the activity of various protein kinases involved in signaling pathways. These phytochemicals act by interfering with the expression of nitric oxide synthase, which helps regulate vascular inflammation. Additionally, certain compounds in the plant interact with antioxidant enzymes like superoxide dismutase to protect cells from damage. This multi-target approach contributes to its documented antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory therapeutic potential.

      What are the herbal preparations of Grewia celtidifolia?

      Grewia celtidifolia is prepared as decoctions, infusions, or topical poultices derived from its leaves and bark. These liquid extracts are most commonly used to harness the plant's medicinal properties through boiling or steeping in hot water. The resulting decoctions are often consumed to address various internal ailments according to traditional practices. For external applications, crushed plant matter is applied directly to the skin as a poultice to treat localized issues. This versatility allows practitioners to utilize different parts of the plant depending on the specific health requirement.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Grewia celtidifolia can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential allergic reactions if ingested or touched directly. The plant contains chemical compounds that may act as irritants to sensitive dermal tissues. Contact with the foliage or stems can result in redness, itching, or localized swelling. If the plant material is consumed, it may trigger nausea, vomiting, or abdominal discomfort. Individuals with known hypersensitivities should exercise extreme caution when handling this species to avoid systemic responses.
