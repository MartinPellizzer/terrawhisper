Grewia celtidifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Grewia celtidifolia?

Grewia celtidifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Malvales. This plant is a member of the family Malvaceae, which falls under the genus Grewia, and is specifically identified by the species name celtidifolia.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Grewia celtidifolia contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, and triterpenoids that contribute to its pharmacological potential. These chemical constituents are often studied for their ability to exhibit antioxidant and antimicrobial activities. Specific studies suggest that the extracts from this plant species may possess anti-inflammatory properties useful in traditional medicine. The concentration of these medicinal compounds can vary depending on the geographical location and environmental stressors affecting the plant. Researchers continue to investigate these specific molecular structures to better understand their therapeutic applications in modern pharmacology.

What are the therapeutic actions of Grewia celtidifolia?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, gastrointestinal issues, and various inflammatory conditions. The bark and leaves are frequently prepared into decoctions or poultices to soothe wounds and abrasions. In some traditional practices, it is applied topically to address fungal growths on the skin. It is also utilized in oral preparations to manage stomach ailments and digestive discomfort. These medicinal applications highlight its role in managing both external and internal ailments.
