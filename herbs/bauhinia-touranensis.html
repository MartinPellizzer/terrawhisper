Bauhinia touranensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Bauhinia touranensis?

Bauhinia touranenensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Fabales. It is a prominent representative of the family Fabaceae and is further categorized under the genus Bauhinia, specifically identified by the species name touranenensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bauhinia touranensis has trifoliate leaves with distinctively long petioles, woody stems, and pentamerous flowers that typically feature a pale pink or white corolla.

The foliage is characterized by its unique structure, where the leaflets are often deeply lobed or shaped to resemble a butterfly. Its flowers emerge from terminal or axillary buds, showcasing a delicate arrangement of petals and prominent stamens. The plant's bark tends to be relatively smooth on younger branches but becomes more textured as the woody structure matures.

These morphological traits help distinguish this specific species within the broader Bauhinia genus.
