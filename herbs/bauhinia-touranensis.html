What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bauhinia touranensis has trifoliate leaves with distinctively long petioles, woody stems, and pentamerous flowers that typically feature a pale pink or white corolla. Its flowers emerge from terminal or axillary buds, showcasing a delicate arrangement of petals and prominent stamens. The plant's bark tends to be relatively smooth on younger branches but becomes more textured as the woody structure matures.
