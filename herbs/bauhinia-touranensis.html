Bauhinia touranensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bauhinia touranensis has trifoliate leaves with distinctively long petioles, woody stems, and pentamerous flowers that typically feature a pale pink or white corolla. The foliage is characterized by its unique structure, where the leaflets are often deeply lobed or shaped to resemble a butterfly. Its flowers emerge from terminal or axillary buds, showcasing a delicate arrangement of petals and prominent stamens. The plant's bark tends to be relatively smooth on younger branches but becomes more textured as the woody structure matures. These morphological traits help distinguish this specific species within the broader Bauhinia genus.
