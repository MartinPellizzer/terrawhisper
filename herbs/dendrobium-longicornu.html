Dendrobium longicornu

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dendrobium longicornu has elongated, cane-like pseudobulbs that are slender, cylindrical, and typically reach lengths of several dozen centimeters. These stems are characterized by closely spaced, lanceolate leaves that emerge from the nodes along the upper portions of the plant. The flowers are relatively small and feature distinctively long, tapering sepals and petals that give the species its name. Each bloom exhibits a complex structure with a prominent labellum that often displays intricate patterns. The overall growth habit is epiphytic, allowing the plant to cling to tree bark in its natural habitat.

    What are the herbal preparations of Dendrobium longicornu?

    Dendrobium longicornu is prepared primarily as a decoction through boiling the dried stems in water, as a powdered supplement, or as a concentrated tincture.
