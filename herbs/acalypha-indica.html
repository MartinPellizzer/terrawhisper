Acalypha indica

What's the taxonomical classification of Acalypha indica?

Acalypha indica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it falls into the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Malpighiales and the family Euphorbiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Acalypha, and its species designation, indica.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Acalypha indica contains alkaloids, flavonoids, tannins, saponins, and steroids that provide its therapeutic properties. These bioactive compounds work together to exhibit significant antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant activities. Specifically, the presence of tannins and saponins contributes to its ability to treat skin infections and wound healing. The plant's chemical profile also supports its traditional use in managing gastrointestinal issues and respiratory ailments. Research continues to investigate how these specific secondary metabolites can be utilized for modern pharmacological applications.

What are the therapeutic actions of Acalypha indica?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin diseases such as eczema, psoriasis, and various fungal infections, as well as respiratory ailments like asthma and bronchitis. Its medicinal properties are often leveraged to alleviate gastrointestinal issues, including dysentery and diarrhea. The leaves and roots are frequently applied topically to reduce inflammation and soothe itchy rashes. Additionally, practitioners sometimes use decoctions of the plant to help manage urinary tract infections and kidney-related discomforts.
