Galium uliginosum

What's the taxonomical classification of Galium uliginosum?

Galium uliginosum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Magnoliidae and is further categorized into the order Gentianales. This plant is a member of the family Rubiaceae and is specifically identified within the genus Galium, with its unique classification completed at the species level as uliginosum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Galium uliginosum has an erect, branched stem that typically reaches heights of 10 to 50 centimeters and is characterized by whorls of narrow, linear leaves. These leaves are arranged in sets of six to eight at each node along the stem. The plant produces small, white, four-petaled flowers that bloom in dense clusters at the ends of the branches. Its fruit is a small, cylindrical drupe that is covered in hooked hairs to facilitate dispersal by animals.
