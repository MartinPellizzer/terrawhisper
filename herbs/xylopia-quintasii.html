Xylopia quintasii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Xylopia quintasii has an arborescent growth habit characterized by a woody trunk, alternate simple leaves with entire margins, and small yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. The foliage typically consists of coriaceous leaves that are lanceolate to elliptic in shape, often featuring a distinct midrib and fine venation. Its reproductive structures include small, inconspicuous petals and numerous stamens that eventually develop into fleshy, often colorful, monocarps. The bark is generally smooth or slightly fissured depending on the age of the specimen. These morphological traits are consistent with other members of the Annonaceae family found in tropical forest ecosystems.
