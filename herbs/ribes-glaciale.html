Ribes glaciale

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ribes glaciale has woody, deciduous stems that produce palmately lobed leaves with three to five shallowly cut lobes and serrated margins. The leaf surfaces are typically smooth or slightly hairy, depending on the specific specimen's maturity. Small, greenish to yellowish flowers emerge in clusters from the axils of the leaves during the growing season. These blossoms transition into small, fleshy berries that change color as they ripen. The overall growth habit is that of a low-growing, spreading shrub adapted to alpine or arctic environments.
