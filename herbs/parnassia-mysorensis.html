Parnassia mysorensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Parnassia mysorensis has a basal rosette of long-petioled, ovate-lanceolate leaves characterized by prominent venation and slightly serrated margins. The plant produces a single, erect flowering stalk that rises from the center of the foliage to bear solitary, pale greenish-white flowers. These flowers feature five distinct petals with intricate nectariferous markings and a central structure composed of specialized staminodes. The arrangement of the floral parts is highly symmetrical, providing a complex morphological structure typical of the Parnassiaceae family. The root system is typically fibrous, supporting the perennial growth habit within its native montane habitats.
