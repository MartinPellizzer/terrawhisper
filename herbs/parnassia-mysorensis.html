Parnasssia mysorensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Celastrales and the family Celastraceae. Finally, the plant is placed within the genus Parnasssia, with its specific designation being the species mysorensis.

    What are the herbal preparations of Parnassia mysorensis?

    Parnassia mysorensis is prepared as a decoction, a paste, or a juice using various parts of the plant such as the leaves and roots. The juice extracted from the fresh leaves is often applied topically to treat skin ailments or ingested for internal relief. For more intensive treatments, the roots are boiled in water to create a decoction that serves as a traditional remedy. A paste can also be fashioned from the crushed plant matter to be spread directly onto affected areas of the body. These different preparations allow the medicinal properties of the herb to be utilized for both external and internal health purposes.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Parnassia mysorensis can cause adverse reactions such as skin irritation, localized itching, or allergic dermatitis if the plant comes into direct contact with sensitive tissues. While specific toxicological studies on humans are limited, many species within the Parnassia genus contain phytochemicals that may trigger hypersensitivity in certain individuals. Potential side effects of accidental ingestion could include gastrointestinal distress or nausea depending on the concentration of active compounds. Users should exercise caution and avoid prolonged exposure to the plant's sap to prevent dermatological issues. Monitoring for any signs of swelling or redness is essential when handling this botanical specimen.
