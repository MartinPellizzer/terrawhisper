Common Reseda

Reseda luteola

Common Reseda (Reseda luteola) is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, known for its yellow flowers and historical use in traditional medicine to treat respiratory and skin conditions.

Botanical Identification

Reseda luteola, commonly known as Common Reseda, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae. It is an annual herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia. The plant has yellow flowers, narrow leaves, and a taproot, and is also known as Madder or Yellow Bedstraw in some regions.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Spermatophyta
  • Class: caryophyllopsida
  • Order: Brassicales
  • Family: Brassicaceae
  • Genus: Reseda
  • Species: Reseda luteola

Common Reseda Identification.

Active Compounds

Common Reseda contains flavonoids, tannins, and essential oils as its main active compounds. These compounds work together to provide its therapeutic effects, with flavonoids offering antioxidant support and tannins contributing to astringent properties. The essential oils may enhance the overall bioavailability and efficacy of the herb.

  • Flavonoid
  • Tannin
  • Essential oil

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory ailments such as coughs and colds.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and as a tonic for overall health.

Oil infusion: Used for skin conditions and as a topical treatment for wounds.

Safety Profile

Common Reseda (Reseda luteola) is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation due to potential risks. Always consult a healthcare provider before use, especially for individuals with sensitive constitutions or chronic conditions.
