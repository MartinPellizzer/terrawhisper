Prunus cerasus subsp. caproniana

Prunus cerasus subsp.

caproniana, also known as the wild cherry or morello cherry, is a subspecies of the Prunus cerasus species, which is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly its fruit, which contains anthocyanins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, and has been traditionally used to treat various health conditions.

The health benefits of Prunus cerasus subsp.

caproniana include its potential to reduce inflammation, improve cardiovascular health, and exhibit antimicrobial and antiviral activities.
