Angelica laxifoliata

What's the taxonomical classification of Angelica laxifoliata?

Angelica laxifoliata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is situated within the order Apiales. It is further categorized under the family Apiaceae, which is characterized by its distinctive umbel inflorescences. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Angelica, and its specific species name, laxifoliata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Angelica laxifoliata has hollow, ribbed stems that support large, compound leaves characterized by broad, lanceolate leaflets with serrated margins. The plant typically exhibits a basal rosette of foliage that transitions into more divided leaf structures as the stem ascends. Its inflorescence consists of large, flat-topped umbels composed of numerous small, white flowers. The root system is robust and fleshy, providing structural stability and nutrient storage for the perennial herb. These morphological traits collectively distinguish it within the Apiaceae family.

The distribution is heavily influenced by the cool, temperate climate and the presence of nutrient-rich, well-drained soils.
