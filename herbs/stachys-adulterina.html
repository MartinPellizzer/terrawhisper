Stachys adulterina

What's the taxonomical classification of Stachys adulterina?

Stachyis adulterina belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the evolutionary hierarchy, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Stachys, and its unique species designation, adulterina.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stachys adulterina has a basal rosette of ovate to lanceolate leaves that are characterized by a densely pubescent, grayish-green surface and crenate margins.

The stem is erect and typically unbranched, bearing several pairs of opposite leaves that become smaller as they ascend toward the inflorescence. Its flowering structure consists of dense, terminal verticillasters that encircle the stem in a spike-like arrangement. The individual flowers are bilabiate and possess a tubular corolla, often displaying subtle color variations within the labia.

Small, fine hairs cover most vegetative parts, providing a textured appearance to the entire plant.
