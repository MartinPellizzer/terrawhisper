Stachys adulterina

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stachys adulterina has a basal rosette of ovate to lanceolate leaves that are characterized by a densely pubescent, grayish-green surface and crenate margins. The stem is erect and typically unbranched, bearing several pairs of opposite leaves that become smaller as they ascend toward the inflorescence. Its flowering structure consists of dense, terminal verticillasters that encircle the stem in a spike-like arrangement. The individual flowers are bilabiate and possess a tubular corolla, often displaying subtle color variations within the labia. Small, fine hairs cover most vegetative parts, providing a textured appearance to the entire plant.
