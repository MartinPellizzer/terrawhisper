What is Acmella repens?

Acmella repens is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is a low-growing, perennial herb that typically grows up to 10 cm tall, with a spreading habit. The leaves are oppositely arranged, simple, and lanceolate, measuring 1-3 cm long and 0.5-1.5 cm wide. Acmella repens produces small, yellow flowers in axillary clusters, with 4-5 florets per head, and a prominent ring of scales at the base of the corolla tube.

The plant is native to South America, specifically in the tropical regions of Brazil, Paraguay, and Uruguay.
