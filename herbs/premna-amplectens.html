What's the taxonomical classification of Premna amplectens?

Premna amplectens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further organized into the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Its specific biological identification is defined by the genus Premna, with its unique designation being the species amplectens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Premna amplectens has woody, shrub-like stems with leaves that are oppositely arranged, elliptical to oblong in shape, and characterized by bases that partially clasp the stem.

The leaf margins are typically entire, and the surface often displays a slightly textured or rugose appearance. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in clusters, often emerging from the axils of the leaves. The plant eventually develops small, fleshy drupes that serve as its primary fruit structure.

These morphological traits collectively distinguish the species within its genus.
