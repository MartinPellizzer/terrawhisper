Botanical Identification of Chinese Magnolia (Magnolia officinalis)

To identify Chinese Magnolia (Magnolia officinalis) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Chinese Magnolia (Magnolia officinalis) Monograph

Scientific Classification

Chinese Magnolia, or Magnolia officinalis, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Magnoliales, family Magnoliaceae, genus Magnolia, and species Magnolia officinalis. This classification places it within the flowering plants, specifically in the order that includes ancient and primitive plant groups.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Chinese Magnolia is Magnolia officinalis, first described by Linnaeus in 1789. It is also known by several synonyms, including Magnolia denudata and Magnolia luchuana. Common names include Chinese Magnolia, Great Magnolia, and Lion's Mouth. It is widely recognized in traditional Chinese medicine for its medicinal properties.

Morphological Characteristics

Chinese Magnolia (Magnolia officinalis) is a deciduous tree or shrub with a taproot system. Its stem is smooth and cylindrical, typically reaching 5-10 meters in height. Leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic with a pointed tip. Inflorescence is a terminal panicle, bearing large, fragrant, white or pink flowers with numerous stamens. The fruit is a woody capsule splitting into three parts, containing numerous seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Magnolia officinalis can be distinguished by its thick, rough bark with longitudinal fissures, and its large, showy flowers with pinkish-white petals. It differs from similar species like Magnolia denudata by having a more robust stem and a stronger, more pungent odor. The leaves are also broader and more ovate compared to other Magnolia species.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Chinese Magnolia (Magnolia officinalis) is native to southern China, particularly in Yunnan, Sichuan, and Guangxi. It thrives in warm, humid climates with mild winters. It prefers well-drained, fertile soils and is typically found at altitudes between 500 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Chinese Magnolia (Magnolia officinalis) blooms in late winter to early spring, typically between February and April, depending on local climate. Seed formation occurs after pollination, usually in late summer to early autumn. Harvest timing for identification purposes is typically during the flowering period when leaves and flowers are most distinct.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Magnolia officinalis is closely related to species like Magnolia denudata and Magnolia lilies. It belongs to the genus Magnolia, which is part of the Magnoliaceae family. The family is characterized by showy flowers and is closely related to other primitive angiosperms.
