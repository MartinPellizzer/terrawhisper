What's the taxonomical classification of Ancistrocladus tectorius?

Ancistrocladus tectorius belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized under the order Caryophyllales. This plant is a representative of the family Ancistrocladaceae, falling specifically into the genus Ancistrocladus, with the specific epithet tectorius identifying the species.

What are the pharmacological activities of Ancistrocladus tectorius?

This plant has potent anti-plasmodial, antibacterial, and antifungal pharmacological activities derived from its diverse phytochemical constituents. Research indicates that extracts from Ancistrocladus tectorius can effectively inhibit the growth of various pathogenic bacteria and fungi. Its anti-plasmodial properties suggest a significant potential for treating malaria caused by Plasmodium parasites. These biological effects are largely attributed to the presence of specific secondary metabolites like alkaloids and flavonoids within the plant tissues. Such multifaceted medicinal actions position this species as a valuable subject for future drug development.

As discussed in a study, extracts containing naphthylisoquinoline alkaloids from Ancistrocladus tectorius display pronounced in vitro activities against exoerythrocytic stages of Plasmodium berghei (Anka) developing in Hep G2 human hepatoma cells. The highest activities were obtained using a CH2Cl2 leaf extract at a concentration of 10 micrograms ml-1. The degrees of growth inhibition for these extracts ranged within 27.7-70.0%. For comparison, the commercially available drug primaquine diphosphate at 25 micrograms ml-1 caused a comparable effect of 62.1% in the same test system.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse array of medicinal compounds including naphthyldihydroisoquinoline alkaloids, various terpenoids, and specific phenolic acids. These naphthyldihydroisoquinoline alkaloids are particularly significant due to their potent biological activities and potential therapeutic applications in treating neurological disorders. In addition to these alkaloids, the plant's chemical profile includes antioxidant compounds that help combat oxidative stress in cellular environments. Researchers are currently studying how these combined substances interact to produce synergistic effects during medicinal use. Understanding the precise concentration of these constituents is essential for developing standardized herbal extracts.

As discussed in a study, three new 5--1'-linked naphthyldihydroisoquinoline alkaloids, identified as 1-3, have been isolated from the organic extract of Ancistrocladus tectorius. The gross structures of these 3 compounds were established through the use of 1D and 2D NMR spectroscopy along with difference NOE experiments. The absolute stereochemistry of compounds 1, 2, and 3 was determined using chemical degradation and CD spectral comparison. Evidence shows that 2 of these compounds exist exclusively in the keto form at C-8 of the isoquinoline system, specifically compounds 2b and 3b.
