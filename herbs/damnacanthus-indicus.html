Damnacanthus indicus

What's the taxonomical classification of Damnacanthus indicus?

Damnacanthus indicus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Gentianaales and is a member of the family Rubiaceae. At the most specific taxonomic levels, it is identified by the genus Damnacanthus, with its unique species designation being indicus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Damnacanthus indicus has lanceolate to ovate leaves that are arranged oppositely on sturdy, upright stems. The foliage typically exhibits a deep green color with smooth margins and distinct venation patterns. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils in clusters, often displaying shades of white or pale cream. The root system is characterized by a thick, fleshy rhizome that serves as a primary storage organ. These morphological traits collectively allow the plant to thrive in its native tropical habitats.

There is also a risk of systemic toxicity that could affect internal organs if high doses are consumed.
