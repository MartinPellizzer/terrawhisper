What's the taxonomical classification of Adenophora trachelioides?

Adenophora trachelioides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the order Asterales and belongs to the family Campanulaceae. Finally, it is defined by the genus Adenophora and is specifically identified as the species trachelioides.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Adenophora trachelioides has an erect, perennial herbaceous habit characterized by slender, cylindrical stems and oppositely arranged, lanceolate to ovate leaves with entire or slightly serrated margins. The foliage is typically green and lacks significant pubescence, providing a smooth texture to the vegetative structure. During the flowering season, the plant produces small, tubular, bell-shaped corollas that emerge from the leaf axils. These flowers are often clustered in terminal inflorescences, displaying colors that range from pale lavender to light purple. The root system consists of a persistent rhizome that allows the plant to spread slowly through its habitat.
