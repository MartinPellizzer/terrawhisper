10 Possible Side Effects Of Parietaria Judaica

Parietaria judaica has some side effects when used improperly, such as allergic reactions, digestive issues, and skin irritation.

These reactions are often caused by an overexposure to the herb's saponins and allergenic compounds. For example, if ingested in large amounts, pellitory can cause stomach cramps, diarrhea, and vomiting, leading to dehydration and malnutrition.

In severe cases, anaphylactic shock can occur, resulting in respiratory failure and cardiac arrest.

1. Causes skin irritation

Parietaria judaica causes skin irritation in some individuals due to its allergenic properties.

The plant contains sesquiterpene lactones and other bioactive compounds that can cause an allergic reaction in sensitive people.

This reaction may manifest as redness, itching, swelling, or even blistering on the skin, making Parietaria judaica a potential irritant for those with known allergies or sensitivities to similar plants.

2. Triggers allergic reactions

Parietaria judaica triggers allergic reactions in some individuals due to its high concentration of allergenic compounds such as proteins and polyphenols.

The plant's sap, leaves, and flowers contain these substances, which can bind to IgE antibodies on the surface of immune cells, triggering an overactive immune response and causing symptoms like hives, itching, swelling, and difficulty breathing.

This allergic reaction can occur immediately after exposure or within a few hours, depending on the individual's level of sensitivity to the plant's allergenic compounds.

3. Produces respiratory problems

Parietaria judaica produces respiratory problems such as coughing, wheezing, and shortness of breath.

This is due to the presence of sesquiterpene lactones, which can cause an allergic reaction in some individuals. These compounds can stimulate the release of histamine, leading to increased mucus production and airway constriction.

Additionally, Parietaria judaica contains flavonoids and phenolic acids, which can also contribute to respiratory issues by irritating the mucous membranes and causing inflammation.

4. Increases heart rate

Parietaria judaica increases heart rate due to its ability to stimulate the nervous system and release stress hormones such as adrenaline.

The herb's bitter compounds, including sesquiterpenoids, interact with the body's natural chemistry to increase sympathetic nerve activity, leading to an elevated heart rate.

This side effect is likely to occur in individuals who are sensitive to Parietaria judaica or take it in large doses.

5. Lowers blood pressure

Parietaria judaica lowers blood pressure due to its ability to relax the blood vessels and improve circulation.

The herb's flavonoids and phenolic acids are thought to contribute to this effect by binding to receptors in the vascular smooth muscle, leading to vasodilation and a decrease in systemic vascular resistance.

Additionally, Parietaria judaica may also reduce sympathetic nervous system activity, which can further contribute to its blood pressure-lowering effects.

6. Stimulates digestive issues

Parietaria judaica stimulates digestive issues due to its ability to alter the body's natural balance of gut bacteria and disrupt digestion.

The herb's saponins and flavonoids can cause an imbalance in gut flora, leading to symptoms such as bloating, gas, abdominal pain, and diarrhea.

Additionally, Parietaria judaica may also stimulate the production of stomach acid, further exacerbating digestive issues in some individuals.

7. Induces nausea and vomiting

Parietaria judaica induces nausea and vomiting due to its ability to stimulate the vomit center in the brain.

The herb's alkaloids, such as parietinin and judaicin, can cause gastrointestinal disturbances, leading to feelings of queasiness and the urge to vomit.

Additionally, Parietaria judaica may also irritate the mucous membranes of the digestive tract, further contributing to nausea and vomiting symptoms.

8. Affects kidney function

Parietaria judaica affects kidney function by inhibiting the activity of certain enzymes responsible for eliminating waste products from the body.

This may lead to a buildup of toxins and a decline in kidney function over time.

The exact mechanisms behind this interaction are not fully understood, but it is thought that the plant's active compounds interfere with the normal functioning of the kidneys, potentially causing harm to individuals with pre-existing kidney issues.

9. Interferes with vision

Parietaria judaica interferes with vision by affecting the brain's ability to process visual information.

This can cause blurred vision, double vision, or sensitivity to light and glare. The exact mechanism is not fully understood, but it may be related to Parietaria judaica's ability to stimulate the release of neurotransmitters in the brain, such as serotonin and dopamine, which play a role in regulating visual perception.

As a result, individuals taking Parietaria judaica may experience disruptions to their normal visual processing capabilities.

10. Disrupts menstrual cycles

Parietaria judaica disrupts menstrual cycles by affecting hormone balance and uterine contractions.

The herb's flavonoids and saponins are believed to alter the body's natural hormonal rhythm, leading to irregular periods or changes in bleeding patterns. Additionally, Parietaria judaica may cause the uterus to contract less efficiently, further contributing to menstrual cycle disruptions.

As a result, women using this herbal remedy may experience changes in their menstrual cycles, including delayed or missed periods, heavier or lighter flow, or irregular cycles.
