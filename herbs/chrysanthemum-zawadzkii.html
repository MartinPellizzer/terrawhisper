Chrysanthemum zawadzkii has perennial herbaceous stems that bear deeply lobed or pinnatifid leaves with serrated margins and distinctively shaped florets. Its inflorescences consist of composite flower heads featuring both central disc florets and outer ray florets. The petals often exhibit specific pigmentation patterns that distinguish this species from other members of the genus.
