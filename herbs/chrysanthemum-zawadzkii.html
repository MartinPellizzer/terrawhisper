Chrysanthemum zawadzkii

What's the taxonomical classification of Chrysanthemum zawadzkii?

Chrysanthemum zawadzkii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. This plant is a representative of the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its complex flower heads. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Chrysanthemum, and its specific biological identity is defined by its species name, zawadzkii.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various inflammatory conditions, respiratory ailments, and skin disorders. Its medicinal properties are often harnessed to alleviate symptoms of asthma and bronchitis through its soothing effects on the lungs. Additionally, the plant is applied topically to soothe skin irritations and manage localized swelling. Some traditional practices also utilize it to help mitigate the effects of fever and common colds. These applications rely on the specific chemical compounds found within its petals and leaves.
