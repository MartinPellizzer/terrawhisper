Peregrine Marrubium

Marrubium peregrinum

Peregrine Marrubium (Marrubium peregrinum) is a member of the Fabaceae family and has been traditionally used for respiratory disorders, digestive issues, and skin conditions. Native to certain regions of Europe, the herb's leaves and flowers are commonly utilized in herbal preparations.

Botanical Identification

Peregrine Marrubium, scientifically known as Marrubium peregrinum, belongs to the Boraginaceae family. Commonly referred to as "wild horehound" in some regions, it is a perennial herb native to Mediterranean climates. The plant features hairy stems, oval leaves, and violet flowers, with historical synonyms including "Pulverulentum" in ancient herbal texts.

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory conditions such as coughs and colds.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and to soothe stomach discomfort.

Powder: Used for topical applications to reduce inflammation and irritation.

Safety Profile

Peregrine Marrubium is generally safe when used as directed. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation due to potential risks. Consult a healthcare professional before use, especially if you have existing health conditions or are on medication.
