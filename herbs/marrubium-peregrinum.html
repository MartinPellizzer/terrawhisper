Peregrine Marrubium

Marrubium peregrinum

Peregrine Marrubium (Marrubium peregrinum) is a medicinal plant native to certain regions of Europe, known for its historical use in treating respiratory conditions such as coughs and bronchitis.

Peregrine Marrubium (Marrubium peregrinum) is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its erect, branched stems, lanceolate leaves with rough, hairy surfaces, and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Distribution

Peregrine Marrubium (Marrubium peregrinum) is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, including parts of Spain, Italy, and the Balkans, and is also found in some isolated regions of North Africa.

Traditional Uses

Peregrine Marrubium (Marrubium peregrinum) has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its antispasmodic, expectorant, and mild sedative properties, primarily to treat respiratory conditions such as coughs, bronchitis, and asthma, as well as to alleviate digestive spasms and promote relaxation.
