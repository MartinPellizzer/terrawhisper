Peregrine Marrubium (Marrubium peregrinum)

Peregrine Marrubium (Marrubium peregrinum) is a member of the Fabaceae family, native to Southern Europe, North Africa, and Middle East. Traditionally, its leaves, stems, and roots have been used for infusions, decoctions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its bitter, anti-inflammatory, and carminative actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and ayurvedic medicine.
