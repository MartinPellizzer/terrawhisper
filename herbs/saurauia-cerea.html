Saurauia cerea

What's the taxonomical classification of Saurauia cerea?

Saurauia cerea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Ericales and the family Actinidiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Saurauia, and its species designation, cerea.

    What are the molecular targets of this plant?

    Saurauia cerea targets cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2), inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS), and various pro-inflammatory cytokines to exert its therapeutic effects. The plant contains bioactive phytochemicals, such as flavonoids and polyphenols, that inhibit the enzyme COX-2 to reduce prostaglandin synthesis. These compounds also modulate the expression of iNOS, thereby limiting the production of nitric oxide associated with inflammation. Furthermore, the extracts interfere with the signaling pathways of tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-alpha) and interleukin-6 (IL-6) to suppress systemic inflammatory responses. Through these multi-target interactions, the plant demonstrates significant potential as an anti-inflammatory and analgesic agent.

      What are the herbal preparations of Saurauia cerea?

      Saurauia cerea is prepared as a decoction, a tea, or a topical infusion using its leaves and bark to treat various ailments. The plant material is typically boiled in water to extract its active medicinal compounds for oral consumption. In some traditional practices, the crushed leaves are applied directly to the skin as a poultice to soothe inflammation.       What side-effects this plant can have?

Ingesting parts of the plant without proper preparation might trigger nausea or abdominal discomfort. Users should exercise extreme caution because the specific chemical compounds responsible for these effects are not fully understood.
