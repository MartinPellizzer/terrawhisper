Saurauia cerea

What's the taxonomical classification of Saurauia cerea?

Saurauia cerea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Ericales and the family Actinidiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Saurauia, and its species designation, cerea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saurauia cerea has large, ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated margins, woody stems, and small, actinomorphic flowers arranged in panicle inflorescences.

The foliage is characterized by prominent venation and a somewhat textured surface that helps distinguish it from similar species. Its woody architecture allows the plant to develop into a substantial shrub or small tree within its tropical habitat. The flowers are typically white or cream-colored and emerge from axillary positions along the branches.

These reproductive structures eventually develop into fleshy fruits that serve as a primary feature of the plant's life cycle.
