Embelia

Embelia ribes

Embelia (Embelia ribes) is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Asia, known for its traditional use in treating digestive disorders and respiratory conditions.

Botanical Identification

Embelia ribes is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae. Commonly known as "Bhuiya" in India and "Kumari" in Nepal, it is a perennial shrub native to Southeast Asia. The plant has small, elliptical leaves and pinkish-white flowers, with a distinctive red, peppery fruit. It is also referred to as "Embelia" in some herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Eudicots
  • Order: Sapindales
  • Family: Myrtaceae
  • Genus: Embelia
  • Species: Embelia ribes

Safety Profile

Embelia ribes is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to its potential to cause uterine contractions and harm fetal development. It should also be avoided by individuals with known allergies to the plant or its compounds. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
