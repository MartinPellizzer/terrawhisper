Embelia ribes

What's the taxonomical classification of Embelia ribes?

Embelia ribes belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Ericales. The plant is a member of the family Primulaceae, which falls under the genus Embelia, and is specifically identified by its species name, ribes.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Embeliaribes contains embelin, chebulic acid, and various polyphenols that function as its primary bioactive constituents. Embelin serves as the most significant component due to its potent antioxidant and anti-tumor properties. The plant also possesses a diverse profile of tannins and flavonoids that contribute to its antimicrobial effects. These chemical compounds work together to provide anti-inflammatory and gastrointestinal benefits in traditional medicine. Such phytochemical diversity makes the plant a subject of interest for modern pharmacological research.

What are the therapeutic actions of Embelia ribes?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat intestinal worms, digestive disorders, respiratory ailments, and skin conditions. It contains embelin, which acts as an anthelmintic to expel parasites like roundworms and pinworms from the body. In traditional medicine, it is frequently applied to soothe coughs and manage symptoms of asthma or bronchitis. The plant also possesses antimicrobial properties that help address various fungal and bacterial infections. Additionally, it is sometimes used to regulate menstrual cycles and improve overall digestive function.
