Strophanthus hispidus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Strophanthus hispidus has an erect, perennial shrub habit characterized by woody stems covered in stiff, bristly hairs and lanceolate leaves that are dark green and distinctly serrated. The foliage typically exhibits a leathery texture with prominent venation on the undersides. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often appearing in clusters. These blossoms eventually develop into small, fleshy drupes that serve as the plant's fruit. The overall structure is defined by its rough, hispid pubescence which gives the species its specific name.

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, specifically spanning from West Africa through Central Africa to parts of East Africa. It is frequently found in savanna ecosystems and open woodlands where it can thrive in seasonally dry climates. Within these habitats, the species often populates disturbed areas, forest edges, and coastal regions. Its range extends through diverse countries including Nigeria, Cameroon, and Kenya. The distribution follows specific environmental patterns dictated by tropical moisture and temperature gradients.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Strophantus hispidus contains medicinal compounds primarily located in its seeds, leaves, and bark. The seeds are the most potent part of the plant and are frequently used in traditional medicine for their purgative and emetic properties. Extracts from the leaves are sometimes applied topically or used in decoctions to treat various skin ailments. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Strophantus hispidus contains cardiac glycosides, primarily strophantin, which act as potent agents to increase the force of heart muscle contractions. These chemical compounds function by inhibiting the sodium-potassium pump in cardiac cells, thereby altering electrolyte concentrations. Beyond its cardiovascular effects, the plant produces various alkaloids and saponins that contribute to its overall pharmacological profile. Researchers study these bioactive molecules for their potential applications in treating specific heart conditions and regulating rhythm. Careful dosage is critical due to the inherent toxicity associated with these powerful glycosides.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Strophantus hispidus targets cardiac glycosides such as strophantin and strophanthin which primarily act as potent inhibitors of the Na+/K+-ATPase enzyme. By binding to this transmembrane protein, these molecules disrupt the sodium-potassium pump, leading to an increase in intracellular sodium concentrations. This ionic shift indirectly promotes the activity of the sodium-calcium exchanger, resulting in elevated levels of intracellular calcium within myocardial cells. The accumulation of calcium enhances the force of cardiac muscle contractions through improved excitation-contraction coupling. These specific protein interactions underpin the plant's primary medicinal applications in treating congestive heart failure and regulating cardiac rhythm.

What are the therapeutic actions of Strophanthus hispidus?

Strophanthus hispidus has potent medicinal properties that function primarily as a powerful cardiac stimulant and cardiotonic agent. The plant contains glycosides that increase the force of heart contractions to treat various forms of cardiac insufficiency. Due to its intense chemical profile, the substance also acts as a strong emetic to induce vomiting. It is characterized by a very bitter taste that can trigger physiological responses in the digestive system. These combined actions make it a highly potent botanical used specifically for managing cardiovascular irregularities.

This plant is used to treat cardiac conditions such as congestive heart failure, atrial fibrillation, and various arrhythmias by increasing the force of heart contractions. What are the herbal preparations of Strophanthus hispidus?

Strophantus hispidus is prepared primarily as an aqueous infusion, a decoction, or a topical paste derived from its seeds and leaves. The seeds contain potent cardiac glycosides that require careful extraction through boiling or steeping in water to create medicinal liquids. These liquid preparations are often administered in controlled, minute doses due to the plant's high toxicity. Additionally, the crushed leaves can be applied externally as a poultice to treat skin ailments or localized inflammation. Because of the risk of overdose, these herbal forms must be handled with extreme caution in clinical or traditional settings.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Strophanthus hispidus can cause severe cardiac toxicity, including life-threatening arrhythmias, bradycardia, and sudden heart failure due to its high concentration of cardiac glycosides. These potent compounds work by inhibiting the sodium-potassium pump, which can lead to dangerous electrolyte imbalances in the body. If ingested, the plant may also trigger intense gastrointestinal distress characterized by nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain. Neurological symptoms such as confusion, tremors, or even seizures may occur as the toxins affect the central nervous system. Because the therapeutic window is extremely narrow, even small doses can quickly become lethal to a human.
