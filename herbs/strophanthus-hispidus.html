Strophanthus hispidus

What's the taxonomical classification of Strophanthus hispidus?

Strophanthus hispidus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Gentianales and is a member of the family Apocynaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by the genus Strophanthus and the species name hispidus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Strophanthus hispidus has an erect, perennial shrub habit characterized by woody stems covered in stiff, bristly hairs and lanceolate leaves that are dark green and distinctly serrated. These blossoms eventually develop into small, fleshy drupes that serve as the plant's fruit. The overall structure is defined by its rough, hispid pubescence which gives the species its specific name.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Strophantus hispidus contains cardiac glycosides, primarily strophantin, which act as potent agents to increase the force of heart muscle contractions. These chemical compounds function by inhibiting the sodium-potassium pump in cardiac cells, thereby altering electrolyte concentrations. Beyond its cardiovascular effects, the plant produces various alkaloids and saponins that contribute to its overall pharmacological profile. What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Strophantus hispidus targets cardiac glycosides such as strophantin and strophanthin which primarily act as potent inhibitors of the Na+/K+-ATPase enzyme. By binding to this transmembrane protein, these molecules disrupt the sodium-potassium pump, leading to an increase in intracellular sodium concentrations. This ionic shift indirectly promotes the activity of the sodium-calcium exchanger, resulting in elevated levels of intracellular calcium within myocardial cells. The accumulation of calcium enhances the force of cardiac muscle contractions through improved excitation-contraction coupling. These specific protein interactions underpin the plant's primary medicinal applications in treating congestive heart failure and regulating cardiac rhythm.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause severe toxicity characterized by cardiac arrhythmias, bradycardia, convulsions, respiratory depression, dizziness, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal pain. The presence of cardiac glycosides in the plant often leads to dangerous irregularities in heart rhythm and a significant slowing of the heart rate. Gastrointestinal distress including nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and intense abdominal pain frequently follows ingestion. Neurological and systemic complications such as dizziness, convulsions, and life-threatening respiratory depression may also occur as the toxins affect the central nervous system.
