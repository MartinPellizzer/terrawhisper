Strophanthus hispidus

What's the taxonomical classification of Strophanthus hispidus?

Strophanthus hispidus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Gentianales and is a member of the family Apocynaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by the genus Strophanthus and the species name hispidus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Strophanthus hispidus has an erect, perennial shrub habit characterized by woody stems covered in stiff, bristly hairs and lanceolate leaves that are dark green and distinctly serrated. The foliage typically exhibits a leathery texture with prominent venation on the undersides. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often appearing in clusters. These blossoms eventually develop into small, fleshy drupes that serve as the plant's fruit. The overall structure is defined by its rough, hispid pubescence which gives the species its specific name.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause severe toxicity characterized by cardiac arrhythmias, bradycardia, convulsions, respiratory depression, dizziness, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal pain. The presence of cardiac glycosides in the plant often leads to dangerous irregularities in heart rhythm and a significant slowing of the heart rate. Gastrointestinal distress including nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and intense abdominal pain frequently follows ingestion. Neurological and systemic complications such as dizziness, convulsions, and life-threatening respiratory depression may also occur as the toxins affect the central nervous system.
