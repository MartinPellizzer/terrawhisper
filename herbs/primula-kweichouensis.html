Primula kweichouensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Primula kweichouensis?

Primula kweichouensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Ericales and the family Primulaceae. Ultimately, this plant is identified by its genus, Primula, and its specific species designation, kweichouensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Primula kweichouensis has a basal rosette of oblong to lanceolate leaves characterized by wrinkled or rugose surfaces and finely serrated margins. The foliage is typically dark green and possesses a somewhat fleshy texture. From the center of this rosette, a long, slender scape emerges to support a terminal umbel of flowers. Each flower features a salverform corolla with five petals that often display a delicate pink or pale violet hue. The reproductive structures are centrally located within the floral tube, surrounded by a ring of small scales at the base of the limb.
