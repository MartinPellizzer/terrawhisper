1. Results in withdrawal

Erythroxylum coca causes dizziness due to its high levels of alkaloids, particularly cocaine and ecgonine.

These compounds can bind to neurotransmitters in the brain, disrupting normal functioning and leading to feelings of lightheadedness or vertigo.

Additionally, the stimulant effects of Erythroxylum coca on the nervous system can also contribute to dizziness by increasing heart rate and blood pressure, further exacerbating symptoms.

2. Results in withdrawal

Erythroxylum coca induces nausea due to its high concentration of cocaine and other alkaloids.

These chemicals can stimulate the vomiting center in the brain, leading to feelings of queasiness and potentially triggering bouts of nausea.

The potency and duration of these symptoms vary depending on the individual's sensitivity to the active ingredients and the method of consumption.
