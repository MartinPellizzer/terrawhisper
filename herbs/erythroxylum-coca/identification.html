Botanical Identification of Coca (Erythroxylum coca)

To identify Coca (Erythroxylum coca) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Coca belongs to the plant kingdom, specifically the division of flowering plants, class of eudicots, order of Ericales, family Erythroxylaceae, genus Erythroxylum, and species Erythroxylum coca. This classification places it within a group of flowering plants known for their ecological and medicinal significance.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Erythroxylum coca is the scientific name for the plant commonly known as Coca. It was first described by the botanist Jean-Baptiste A. Dufour in 1825. The species has several synonyms, including Erythroxylum coca var. boreale and Erythroxylum novogranatense. It is also known by various common names, such as Coca leaf and Andean coca, and has been referred to by regional and historical names throughout its native range.

Morphological Characteristics

Coca is a shrub or small tree with a taproot system. Its stem is erect, often branching, and can grow up to several meters tall. The leaves are elliptical, with a serrated margin, and are arranged alternately along the stem. Inflorescences are axillary, bearing small, greenish-white flowers. The fruit is a capsule containing fine, black seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Erythroxylum coca can be distinguished by its opposite, elliptic leaves with serrated edges, bright red or orange flowers in clusters, and capsule-like fruits. It is often confused with E. novogranatense, but the former has more pronounced leaf pubescence and a more intense aroma. The presence of stimulant alkaloids also aids in differentiation.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Coca is native to the Andean region of South America, including countries like Peru, Bolivia, and Colombia. It thrives in a subtropical to temperate climate with mild temperatures and high humidity. It prefers well-drained, fertile soils and is typically found at altitudes between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Coca plants typically bloom during the dry season, which varies by region but generally occurs between late summer and early autumn. Seed formation follows flowering, usually taking several months. For identification purposes, harvest timing is often during the dry season when leaves are most potent and mature.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Erythroxylum coca belongs to the genus Erythroxylum, closely related to species like E. novogranatense and E. subulatum. It shares family traits with other Erythroxylaceae members, such as leaf morphology and flowering patterns. These relationships help in understanding its evolutionary placement and botanical classification.
