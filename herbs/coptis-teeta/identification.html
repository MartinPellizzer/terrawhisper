Botanical Identification of Coptis Teeta (Coptis teeta)

To identify Coptis Teeta (Coptis teeta) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Coptis teeta belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Ranunculales, family Ranunculaceae, genus Coptis, and species Coptis teeta. These classifications place it within the broader group of flowering plants and the Ranunculaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental plants.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Coptis teeta is the scientific name for the plant, with the author citation being S. C. Buch. The species is also known by several synonyms, including Coptis chinensis and Coptis franchetii. It has various common names such as yellow thread, gold thread, and Indian goldthread, and is referred to by different regional and historical names in traditional systems of medicine.

Morphological Characteristics

Coptis teeta is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous root system. It has erect, branched stems that are smooth and hollow. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a prominent midrib. The inflorescence is a raceme of yellow flowers with prominent yellow stamens. The fruit is a capsule containing small, flat seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Coptis teeta can be distinguished by its distinctive yellow rhizomes with a yellowish-brown outer layer, dark green, doubly-serrated leaves, and yellow flowers with orange stamens. It differs from similar species like Coptis chinensis through its smaller leaves and less aromatic rhizomes. The presence of a bitter taste and specific alkaloid content also aids in identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Coptis teeta is native to the Himalayan regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan. It thrives in temperate climates with cool, moist conditions. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils and is typically found at altitudes between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Coptis teeta blooms from April to June, producing bright yellow flowers. Seed formation occurs from July to September. For identification purposes, the plant is typically harvested in early spring before flowering for the highest concentration of alkaloids.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Coptis teeta is closely related to species like Coptis chinensis and Coptis delavayi, all belonging to the genus Coptis. It shares morphological and genetic traits with other members of the Ranunculaceae family, such as the presence of protoindolizidines and similar leaf structures. These relationships aid in accurate identification and understanding of its medicinal properties.
