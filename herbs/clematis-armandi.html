Clematis armandi

What's the taxonomical classification of Clematis armandi?

Clematis armandi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Ranunculales. This plant is a distinguished member of the family Ranunculaceae, falling under the genus Clematis, and is specifically identified by its species name, armandi.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Clematis armandi contains medicinal compounds primarily located within its roots and leaves. The root systems are often harvested for their ability to treat various inflammatory conditions and digestive ailments. Extracts derived from the foliage are sometimes utilized in traditional practices to manage fever or skin irritations. These specific plant parts are processed carefully to isolate the active chemical constituents required for therapeutic use. Careful dosage is essential when employing these components due to the potential toxicity of the species.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

The alkaloids present in Clematis armandi are often studied for their potential effects on the central nervous system and inflammatory pathways. What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Clematis armandi targets inflammatory signaling proteins such as cyclooxygenase-2, tumor necrosis factor-alpha, and various interleukins to exert its pharmacological effects. The plant contains bioactive compounds like alkaloids and terpenoids that interfere with the arachidonic acid metabolism pathway. These phytochemicals act as inhibitors to reduce the production of pro-inflammatory mediators within the cellular environment. By modulating these specific protein activities, the plant demonstrates potential for treating swelling and pain. Such interactions with enzyme pathways and cytokine signaling provide the biochemical basis for its traditional medicinal uses.

What are the therapeutic actions of Clematis armandi?

Clematis armandi has therapeutic actions primarily focused on relieving symptoms of nervous tension, anxiety, and emotional instability through its affinity for the nervous system. What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including skin infections, inflammation, respiratory issues, and digestive problems. Historically, its leaves and roots have been applied topically to soothe skin irritations and heal wounds. When used internally, it is often prepared as a decoction to address symptoms of coughs and congestion. Some traditional practices also involve using the plant to alleviate stomach discomfort and general internal swelling. Caution is always necessary as specific preparation methods are required to ensure safety.

What are the herbal preparations of Clematis armandi?

Clematis armandi is prepared primarily as a decoction of the dried roots, an infusion of the leaves, or a topical poultice applied directly to the skin. These traditional methods extract the active compounds to address various ailments through oral consumption or external application. When used as a decoction, the roots are boiled in water to create a concentrated liquid for internal use. Leaf infusions provide a milder tea-like preparation suitable for gentler therapeutic purposes. The poultice form involves crushing the fresh plant material to facilitate direct contact with affected areas.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Clematis armandi can cause skin irritation, contact dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if any part of the plant is ingested. The plant contains protoanemonin, a toxic chemical that triggers redness, itching, and blistering upon contact with the skin. Even minor brushing against the foliage can result in painful inflammatory reactions for sensitive individuals. If the stems or leaves are consumed, it may lead to severe stomach pain, nausea, or vomiting. It is essential to wear protective clothing when handling this climber to prevent these adverse physical reactions.
