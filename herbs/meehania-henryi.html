Meehania henryi

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Meehania henryi has an erect, perennial herbaceous habit characterized by oppositely arranged, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins and a distinctly pubescent texture. The plant typically reaches heights of up to 60 centimeters, featuring stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of small, tubular, bilabiate flowers that are predominantly pale blue or lavender in color. These flowers are borne in dense clusters, often appearing in the leaf axils or at the terminal ends of the stems. The reproductive structures are tucked within the corolla, reflecting its placement within the Lamiaceae family.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical rainforest regions of West Africa, specifically within countries like Cameroon and Gabon. These specific habitats provide the consistent moisture and shade necessary for its survival. The species is primarily distributed across the Guineo-Congolian regional center of endemism.
