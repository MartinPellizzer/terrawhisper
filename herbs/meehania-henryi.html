Meehania henryi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Lamiales. It is a member of the family Lamiaceae, specifically falling within the genus Meehania, with its unique designation as the species henryi.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Its inflorescence consists of small, tubular, bilabiate flowers that are predominantly pale blue or lavender in color. These flowers are borne in dense clusters, often appearing in the leaf axils or at the terminal ends of the stems.
