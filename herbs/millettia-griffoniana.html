Millettia griffoniana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Millettia griffoniania has pinnately compound leaves, woody stems, and small, inconspicuous flowers typically arranged in axillary clusters. The leaflets are arranged oppositely along the rachis and possess an elliptical to oblong shape with entire margins. The tree structure is characterized by a robust trunk and a spreading canopy that provides dense shade. Its reproductive organs consist of small, yellowish or cream-colored blossoms that eventually develop into woody, dehiscent capsules. These capsules contain numerous small seeds which are dispersed through various natural mechanisms.
