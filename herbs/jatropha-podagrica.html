    What are the herbal preparations of Jatropha podagrica?

    Jatropha podagrica is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice using its leaves, roots, or sap depending on the intended medicinal application. The leaves are often boiled in water to create a tea used for internal cleansing or digestive support.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Jatrophia podagrica can cause severe skin irritation, blistering, and localized inflammation if the plant's sap comes into contact with human tissue. The milky latex contains various diterpene esters that act as potent irritants to the eyes and mucous membranes. If ingested, the plant may induce vomiting, abdominal pain, and significant gastrointestinal distress. Some individuals may also experience allergic dermatitis or delayed hypersensitivity reactions after handling the stems or leaves. Careful avoidance of all plant fluids is necessary to prevent these physical complications.
