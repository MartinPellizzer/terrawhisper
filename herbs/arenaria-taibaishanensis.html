Arenaria taibaishanensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Arenaria taibaishanensis has perennial herbs characterized by erect or ascending stems that typically reach a height of 5 to 15 centimeters. The leaves are arranged in opposite pairs and are mostly small, ovate to lanceolate in shape, with entire margins. Its flowering structures consist of small, white petals that emerge from terminal cymes. The calyx is composed of five fused sepals that protect the developing reproductive organs. The plant often develops a compact growth habit suited to its specific alpine or rocky habitat.
