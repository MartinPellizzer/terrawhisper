Arenaria taibaishanensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Arenaria taibaishanensis?

Arenaria taibaishanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further organized into the order Caryophyllales. It is a member of the family Caryophyllaceae, falling under the genus Arenaaria, and is specifically identified by the species name taibaishanensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Arenaria taibaishanensis has perennial herbs characterized by erect or ascending stems that typically reach a height of 5 to 15 centimeters.

The leaves are arranged in opposite pairs and are mostly small, ovate to lanceolate in shape, with entire margins. Its flowering structures consist of small, white petals that emerge from terminal cymes. The calyx is composed of five fused sepals that protect the developing reproductive organs.

The plant often develops a compact growth habit suited to its specific alpine or rocky habitat.

        What are the herbal preparations of Arenaria taibaishanensis?

        Arenaria taibaishanensis is prepared primarily as a decoction through boiling the dried plant material in water to extract its active medicinal components.         What side-effects this plant can have?

Because the specific pharmacological profile of this plant is not comprehensively documented in clinical literature, users should be aware of the risk of unknown chemical interactions.
