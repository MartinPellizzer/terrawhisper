Smilax cocculoides

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Smilax cocculoides has woody, climbing stems equipped with axillary spines and alternate, leathery leaves that typically feature three distinct leaflets. The foliage is characterized by an ovate shape and a glossy texture, often displaying prominent venation on the underside. Its inflorescence consists of small, greenish-white flowers that emerge from the leaf axils during the growing season. These flowers eventually develop into small, fleshy red berries that serve as a primary reproductive feature. The plant's overall structure relies on its ability to scramble over other vegetation using its thorny stems for support.
