Lindera glauca

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lindera glauca has deciduous, lanceolate to ovate leaves that are typically 2 to 5 centimeters long and possess smooth or slightly serrated margins. The foliage displays a distinct glaucous coating, giving the undersides of the leaves a pale or bluish-green appearance. During the spring, the plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that emerge in clusters from the woody stems. These blossoms eventually give way to small, fleshy red drupes that persist on the branches through the winter. The bark is generally grayish and becomes increasingly furrowed as the shrub matures into a larger woody form.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the southwest corner of Western Australia, specifically distributed within the kwongan heathlands of the Esperance and Albany regions. It typically occupies coastal and near-coastal environments characterized by sandy soils. Within these habitats, the species thrives in diverse shrubland communities ranging from the southern coast to inland areas. Its presence is often limited to specific ecological niches where nutrient-poor soils support its specialized growth patterns. This restricted range makes it an endemic species to this particular Mediterranean-type climate zone.
