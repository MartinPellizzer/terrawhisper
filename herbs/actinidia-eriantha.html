Actinidia eriantha

What's the taxonomical classification of Actinidia eriantha?

Actinidia eriantha belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Ericales and into the family Actinidiaceae. Finally, it is placed within the genus Actinidia, specifically identifying as the species eriantha.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Actinidia eriantha targets enzymes such as cyclooxygenase, lipoxygenase, and various inflammatory cytokines to mediate its pharmacological activities. The plant contains bioactive compounds like polyphenols and flavonoids that inhibit these protein mediators to reduce systemic inflammation. These molecules specifically interfere with the arachidonic acid metabolism pathway to alleviate pain and swelling. Furthermore, certain constituents interact with nitric oxide synthase to regulate vascular responses and oxidative stress. Such interactions with intracellular signaling proteins contribute to the plant's observed antioxidant and neuroprotective properties.

What are the therapeutic actions of Actinidia eriantha?

Actinidia eriantha has pharmacological properties that primarily involve its antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and digestive-promoting effects due to its high concentration of organic acids, vitamins, and phenolic compounds. Furthermore, its nutritional profile supports immune function and helps reduce systemic inflammation. What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat gastrointestinal disorders such as diarrhea, dysentery, and abdominal pain. Its medicinal properties are primarily utilized to regulate digestive functions and soothe inflammation within the gut. Researchers focus on its bioactive compounds to understand how it assists in managing various intestinal ailments. Traditional practices often incorporate the plant to alleviate discomfort associated with digestive tract infections. These applications highlight its role in supporting overall digestive health through natural means.

What are the herbal preparations of Actinidia eriantha?

Actinidia eriantha is prepared primarily as a decoction of its dried fruit or as a powder derived from the dried plant parts. The fruit is often boiled in water to extract its active components for medicinal use. In some traditional practices, the dried material is also infused into liquid preparations to create concentrated extracts. These various forms allow for the systematic administration of the plant's properties in herbal medicine. The specific method of preparation typically depends on the desired therapeutic effect and the part of the plant being utilized.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Actinidia eriantha can cause allergic reactions such as skin rashes, itching, or swelling if the plant comes into contact with sensitive skin. Some individuals may experience oral irritation or swelling of the mouth when consuming parts of the plant. Because it belongs to the Actinidiaceae family, cross-reactivity with other plants could trigger more severe sensitivities in certain people. It is important to monitor for any signs of redness or discomfort after handling the foliage or fruit. Careful observation is necessary to ensure no adverse reactions occur during use.
