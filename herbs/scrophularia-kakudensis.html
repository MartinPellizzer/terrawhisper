What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Scrophularia kakudensis has upright, herbaceous stems that typically reach heights of 20 to 50 centimeters and feature opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the upper leaf axils, displaying a distinct yellowish-brown or reddish coloration. These blossoms are arranged in dense, terminal racemes that appear during the flowering season. The plant's overall structure is slender and compact, allowing it to thrive in specific shaded or semi-shaded habitats.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Scrophularia kakudensis can cause skin irritation, digestive upset, and potential allergic reactions in sensitive individuals. Contact with the plant's sap may lead to localized redness or itching on the skin. Ingesting any part of the herb might result in nausea, stomach cramps, or diarrhea. Some people might experience more severe hypersensitivity symptoms like swelling or rashes if they are allergic to the species. Monitoring for these specific physical responses is essential when handling or consuming the plant.
