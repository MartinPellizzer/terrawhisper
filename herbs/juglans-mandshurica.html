Juglans mandshurica

What's the taxonomical classification of Juglans mandshurica?

Juglans mandshurica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the biological hierarchy, it is placed in the order Fagales and the family Juglandaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by the genus Juglans and is specifically distinguished as the species mandshurica.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Juglans mandshurica contains high concentrations of juglone, various phenolic acids, flavonoids, and essential fatty acids within its kernels and hulls. These bioactive compounds work together to provide potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The presence of juglone specifically exhibits antimicrobial and antifungal activities that can inhibit certain pathogens. Additionally, the plant's rich lipid profile contributes to cardiovascular health benefits through the provision of essential nutrients. Researchers also study these phytochemicals for their potential roles in managing oxidative stress in cellular models.

What are the therapeutic actions of Juglans mandshurica?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various inflammatory conditions, digestive disorders, skin diseases, and respiratory ailments. Its medicinal properties are often applied to alleviate symptoms of asthma and bronchitis through its expectorant qualities. The bark and leaves are frequently utilized in topical applications to manage skin irritations such as eczema or fungal infections. Additionally, some traditional practices employ its components to address gastrointestinal issues like constipation or stomach pain. These therapeutic uses are primarily attributed to the presence of specific tannins and essential oils within the plant.
