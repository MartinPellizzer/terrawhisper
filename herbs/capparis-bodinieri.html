Capparis bodinieri

What's the taxonomical classification of Capparis bodinieri?

Capparis bodinieri belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Brassicales. Further taxonomic classification places it in the family Capparaceae, specifically within the genus Capparis, where it is identified by its unique species name, bodinieri.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Capparis bodinieri has woody, scrambling stems with alternate, simple leaves that are typically elliptic to oblong in shape.

The foliage is characterized by a leathery texture and a somewhat glaucous or dull green surface. Small, white to cream-colored flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often featuring prominent stamens that provide a delicate appearance. The inflorescences are generally solitary or occur in small groups.

Following fertilization, the plant produces distinct fleshy fruits that serve as a key identifying feature of the species.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

It is advisable to avoid any consumption or skin contact until comprehensive safety profiles are established.
