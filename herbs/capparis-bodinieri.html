Capparis bodinieri

What's the taxonomical classification of Capparis bodinieri?

Capparis bodinieri belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Brassicales. Further taxonomic classification places it in the family Capparaceae, specifically within the genus Capparis, where it is identified by its unique species name, bodinieri.

    Capparis bodinieri is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice derived from its various plant parts. Alternatively, fresh plant material can be crushed to form a paste applied directly to the skin. This topical application is typically used to soothe local inflammation or skin irritations. Some traditional methods also involve steeping the flowers to capture their specific aromatic and medicinal properties.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Capparis bodinieri can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and allergic reactions if ingested or touched. While specific toxicological data is limited, members of the Capparaceae family often contain glucosinolates which may act as irritants. Direct contact with the plant tissues might lead to contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Ingestion of unstudied plant parts carries a risk of nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. It is advisable to avoid any consumption or skin contact until comprehensive safety profiles are established.
