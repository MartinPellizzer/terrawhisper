Passion Vine (Passiflora Brevifila)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Passiflora brevifila.

Passion vine, scientifically known as Passiflora brevifila, is a climbing vine native to South America, valued for its medicinal properties and used in traditional and modern wellness practices. It is considered a medicinal herb with adaptogenic qualities, helping the body manage stress and promote emotional balance. The primary benefits of Passion vine include its calming effects, ability to reduce anxiety, and support for sleep quality, making it a popular remedy for nervous system health. Traditionally, indigenous communities in the Amazon have used it to treat insomnia, anxiety, and digestive issues, while modern wellness applications include its use in herbal teas, supplements, and aromatherapy for relaxation. This unique plant is also noted for its distinctive floral fragrance and rare active compounds like harmine and harmaline, which contribute to its therapeutic potential.
