Liparis fargesii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Liparis fargesii has an elongated, terrestrial growth habit characterized by slender, creeping rhizomes that produce small, ovate to lanceolate leaves arranged in a distichous manner. These leaves are typically thin and fleshy with a smooth margin and a bright green color. The inflorescence emerges from the base of the plant, featuring a raceme of small, yellowish to greenish-white flowers. Each individual flower displays a complex structure with a distinctively shaped labellum that is often slightly larger than the other perianth segments. The overall architecture of the plant is diminutive and adapted for life in shaded, moist forest floor environments.
