Liparis fargesii

What's the taxonomical classification of Liparis fargesii?

Liparis fargesii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further categorized under the order Asparagales. As a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, it is placed within the genus Liparis, ultimately reaching its specific identification at the species level as fargesii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Liparis fargesii has an elongated, terrestrial growth habit characterized by slender, creeping rhizomes that produce small, ovate to lanceolate leaves arranged in a distichous manner.

These leaves are typically thin and fleshy with a smooth margin and a bright green color. The inflorescence emerges from the base of the plant, featuring a raceme of small, yellowish to greenish-white flowers. Each individual flower displays a complex structure with a distinctively shaped labellum that is often slightly larger than the other perianth segments.

The overall architecture of the plant is diminutive and adapted for life in shaded, moist forest floor environments.
