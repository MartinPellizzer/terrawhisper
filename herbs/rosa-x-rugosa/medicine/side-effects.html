10 Possible Side Effects Of Rosa × Rugosa

Rosa × rugosa has some side effects when used improperly, such as allergic reactions, skin irritation, and digestive issues.

These side effects can be caused by its high concentrations of anthocyanins and phenolic acids.

For instance, excessive consumption can worsen conditions like eczema, dermatitis, and acid reflux, leading to discomfort, pain, and decreased quality of life.

1. Causes skin irritation

Rosa × rugosa causes skin irritation due to its high concentration of fragrant essential oils and phenolic compounds.

These potent chemicals can penetrate the skin's natural barrier, causing discomfort, redness, and itching.

Additionally, the plant's sap can contain irritant compounds that may trigger allergic reactions or skin inflammation in some individuals, leading to an unpleasant experience when handling Rosa × rugosa.

2. Triggers allergic reactions

Rosa × rugosa triggers allergic reactions due to its high content of lipids and terpenes, which can cause an immune system response in some individuals.

These allergenic compounds are typically present on the surface of the plant's leaves and stems, making contact with the skin or mucous membranes possible.

This sensitivity can lead to a range of symptoms, including redness, itching, and inflammation.

3. Induces nausea and vomiting

Rosa × rugosa induces nausea and vomiting due to its high concentration of fragarine, a chemical compound that can cause gastrointestinal irritation.

The plant's essential oils, particularly geraniol and linalool, may also contribute to this effect by stimulating the vomit center in the brain.

Furthermore, the plant's flavonoids and phenolic acids can interfere with digestive enzymes, leading to nausea and vomiting.

4. Increases blood pressure

Rosa × rugosa increases blood pressure.

This is due to its high concentration of flavonoids and phenolic acids, which can stimulate the body's vascular system and cause an increase in blood pressure.

Additionally, the plant's bioactive compounds may interact with certain medications, further exacerbating this side effect.

5. Stimulates uterine contractions

Rosa × rugosa stimulates uterine contractions.

This is attributed to its high concentration of fragarine and beta-phellandrene, which have been shown to mimic the action of oxytocin in the body. Oxytocin is a hormone that helps stimulate contractions during childbirth and lactation.

As a result, women who consume Rosa × rugosa or apply it topically may experience increased uterine activity, potentially leading to more frequent or intense menstrual cramps, preterm labor, or even miscarriage.

6. Interferes with insulin production

Rosa × rugosa interferes with insulin production by stimulating an increase in blood glucose levels.

This is due to its high concentration of polyphenolic compounds, such as flavonoids and phenolic acids, which can inhibit the activity of insulin receptors, thereby disrupting glucose metabolism.

Additionally, Rosa × rugosa contains saponins that may also contribute to this effect by altering the gut microbiome and reducing the body's ability to respond to insulin.

7. Activates digestive enzymes

Rosa × rugosa activates digestive enzymes, which may lead to side effects in some individuals.

This is due to its high content of bioactive compounds, including flavonoids and phenolic acids, that can stimulate the production of digestive enzymes in the gut, potentially causing increased gas, bloating, or stomach discomfort in susceptible people.

8. Inhibits platelet aggregation

Rosa × rugosa inhibits platelet aggregation by releasing salicylic acid, a compound similar to aspirin.

This acid binds to platelets in the blood, preventing them from sticking together and forming clots.

As a result, people using Rosa × rugosa may experience increased bleeding risk or bruising due to reduced platelet function.

9. Disrupts hormone regulation

Rosa × rugosa disrupts hormone regulation due to its potential interaction with estrogen receptors.

The herb contains phytoestrogens, which are plant-based compounds that mimic the effects of estrogen in the body.

When ingested, these compounds can bind to estrogen receptors, altering the balance of hormones and potentially leading to changes in menstrual cycles, breast tenderness, or mood swings.

10. Produces gastrointestinal upset

Rosa × rugosa produces gastrointestinal upset due to its high concentration of flavonoids and phenolic acids, which can irritate the mucous membranes in the digestive tract.

These compounds may also alter the balance of gut bacteria, leading to changes in digestion and absorption patterns.

Additionally, some individuals may be sensitive to the allergenic properties of Rosa × rugosa, exacerbating symptoms such as nausea and stomach discomfort.
