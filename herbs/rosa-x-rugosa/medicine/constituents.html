10 Medicinal Constituents Of Rosa × Rugosa (Beach Rose)

Rosa × rugosa has active constituents such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenes.

These bioactive compounds possess anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties. The medicinal properties of beach rose can help alleviate symptoms of menstrual cramps, improve skin health by reducing inflammation and oxidative stress, and support immune function by combating bacterial infections.

For example, the flavonoids present in beach rose have been shown to reduce prostaglandin production, providing relief from menstrual pain, while its antimicrobial properties can aid in wound healing and skin infection treatment.

1. Salidroside

Rosa × rugosa salidroside is a bioactive compound derived from the fruit of the Rosa rugosa plant.

This compound has been extensively studied for its potential medicinal properties and antioxidant activities. Salidroside has been shown to have anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and anticancer effects, making it a promising candidate for the treatment of various diseases.

Additionally, it has also been found to possess neuroprotective and cardioprotective properties, further highlighting its therapeutic potential.

2. Betulinic acid

Rosa × rugosa betulinic acid is a unique bioactive compound extracted from the flowers and seeds of this plant species.

It has been found to possess antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, making it a promising candidate for various biomedical applications.

Additionally, betulinic acid has been shown to exhibit antitumor activity, inhibiting the growth of cancer cells and inducing apoptosis.

3. Epigallocatechin gallate (egcg)

Rosa × rugosa epigallocatechin gallate (EGCG) is a polyphenolic compound extracted from the leaves and stems of this species.

EGCG has been extensively studied for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and anti-cancer properties.

It is also believed to have a role in improving cardiovascular health and cognitive function, as well as showing promise in treating certain diseases such as Alzheimer's and Parkinson's.

4. Kaempferol

Rosa × rugosa kaempferol is a flavonoid compound found in the flowers and leaves of this species.

It has been shown to possess various biological activities, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. Kaempferol has also been reported to have potential therapeutic effects on cardiovascular disease, cancer, and neurodegenerative disorders.

Additionally, it may play a role in protecting against environmental stressors and oxidative damage.

5. Quercetin

Rosa × rugosa quercetin is a bioflavonoid compound found in the flowers and leaves of this species.

It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various health conditions, including inflammation, allergies, and cardiovascular diseases. Quercetin exhibits antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a potential therapeutic agent for preventing chronic diseases.

Its bioavailability is enhanced when consumed as part of Rosa × rugosa tea or supplements.

6. Rosavin

Rosa × rugosa rosavin is a natural compound extracted from the roots of Rosa rugosa, a species of rose native to Eastern Asia.

It has been used in traditional medicine for centuries, particularly in China and Japan, to treat various ailments such as cardiovascular disease, diabetes, and inflammatory conditions.

7. Gallic acid

Rosa × rugosa gallic acid is a type of phenolic compound found in the plant's leaves and stems.

It has been studied for its potential antioxidant and antimicrobial properties, as well as its ability to inhibit the growth of certain cancer cells.

Gallic acid has also been shown to have anti-inflammatory effects and may be useful in treating conditions such as arthritis and other inflammatory diseases.

8. Caffeic acid

Rosa × rugosa caffeic acid is a type of phenolic compound found in its leaves and stems.

This natural antioxidant has been shown to possess anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, making it a potential therapeutic agent for various diseases.

Additionally, caffeic acid has been demonstrated to exhibit antiviral activity against certain viruses, highlighting its value as a natural defense mechanism against pathogens.

9. Ellagic acid

Rosa × rugosa ellagic acid is a naturally occurring phenolic compound found in the fruit and leaves of this species.

It has been studied for its potential health benefits, including antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, as well as its ability to inhibit cancer cell growth and induce apoptosis.

Additionally, it may help protect against oxidative stress and DNA damage.

10. Ferulic acid

Rosa × rugosa ferulic acid is a bioactive compound extracted from the flowers of this species.

It has been found to possess various biological activities, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties.

Ferulic acid has also shown potential in preventing cancer cell proliferation and inducing apoptosis, making it a promising natural agent for cancer treatment.
