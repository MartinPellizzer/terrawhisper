Garden nasturtium

Tropaeolum majus

Garden Nasturtium (Tropaeolum majus) is a flowering plant known for its bright, colorful flowers and edible leaves, commonly used in herbal medicine for its potential to improve respiratory conditions such as bronchitis and coughs.

The Garden Nasturtium, scientifically known as Tropaeolum majus, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Spermatophyta, class Caryophyllopsida, order Tropaeolales, family Tropaeolaceae, genus Tropaeolum, and species Tropaeolum majus.

Morphology

The Garden Nasturtium (Tropaeolum majus) is a flowering plant characterized by its heart-shaped, slightly lobed leaves that are typically bright green with a slightly hairy texture, and its vibrant, rounded flowers that range in color from yellow to red, often with a distinctive orange or yellow center.

Distribution

The Garden Nasturtium (Tropaeolum majus) is native to South America, specifically the Andean region, and has been cultivated in temperate regions worldwide for centuries.

Traditional Uses

In traditional herbal medicine, Garden Nasturtium (Tropaeolum majus) has been used for its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and digestive properties, often applied topically to treat wounds, skin infections, and insect bites, while its leaves and seeds have been consumed to support digestion and respiratory health.

In traditional medicinal systems such as Ayurveda, Traditional Chinese Medicine, and Native American herbal practices, Garden Nasturtium (Tropaeolum majus) has been utilized for its purported anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and digestive properties.

Garden Nasturtium (Tropaeolum majus) possesses antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties that support its traditional use in herbal medicine for respiratory and digestive health.

Safety Profile

Garden Nasturtium is generally safe when used in culinary amounts. However, high doses may cause gastrointestinal upset. It is contraindicated in individuals with kidney disease due to its potential to increase urinary calcium excretion. Always consult a healthcare provider before use, especially for prolonged periods or in medicinal quantities.
