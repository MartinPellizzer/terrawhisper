Garden nasturtium

Tropaeolum majus

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Tropaeolum majus, commonly known as garden nasturtium, belongs to the Tropaeolaceae family. It is an annual plant native to South America. Its bright flowers and edible leaves are widely used in herbal medicine. Synonyms include Indian cress and summer cress, reflecting its historical use in various regions.

Active Compounds

The Garden Nasturtium contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, coumarins, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects, supporting its use in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Coumarin
  • Phenolic acid

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action
Respiratory Respiratory infections, bronchial congestion, cough Antimicrobial, expectorant, antiviral
Integumentary Skin infections, wound healing, skin irritation Antimicrobial, antioxidant, antifungal, anti-inflammatory
Immune Inflammation, allergies, allergic reactions Anti-inflammatory, antihistaminic, antimalarial, antipyretic

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory infections.

Infusion: Used for mild digestive issues and as a calming tea.

Poultice: Used for skin irritations and wounds.

Safety Profile

Garden Nasturtium is generally safe when used in culinary amounts. However, high doses may cause gastrointestinal upset. It is contraindicated in individuals with kidney disease due to its potential to increase urinary calcium excretion. Always consult a healthcare provider before use, especially for prolonged periods or in medicinal quantities.
