Garden nasturtium

Tropaeolum majus

Garden Nasturtium (Tropaeolum majus) is a flowering plant known for its bright, colorful flowers and edible leaves, commonly used in herbal medicine for its potential to improve respiratory conditions such as bronchitis and coughs.

Botanical Identification

Tropaeolum majus, commonly known as garden nasturtium, belongs to the Tropaeolaceae family. It is an annual plant native to South America. Its bright flowers and edible leaves are widely used in herbal medicine. Synonyms include Indian cress and summer cress, reflecting its historical use in various regions.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Spermatophyta
  • Class: caryophyllopsida
  • Order: Tropaeolales
  • Family: Tropaeolaceae
  • Genus: Tropaeolum
  • Species: Tropaeolum majus

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory infections.

Infusion: Used for mild digestive issues and as a calming tea.

Poultice: Used for skin irritations and wounds.

Safety Profile

Garden Nasturtium is generally safe when used in culinary amounts. However, high doses may cause gastrointestinal upset. It is contraindicated in individuals with kidney disease due to its potential to increase urinary calcium excretion. Always consult a healthcare provider before use, especially for prolonged periods or in medicinal quantities.
