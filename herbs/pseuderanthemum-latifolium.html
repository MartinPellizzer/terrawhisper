Pseuderanthemum latifolium has broad, ovate leaves with entire margins, a woody stem structure, and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in compact inflorescences. Its stems exhibit a branching pattern that supports the spreading habit of the shrub. The floral structures are typically characterized by a reduced corolla and a functional reproductive system suited for specific pollinators.       What are the herbal preparations of Pseuderanthemum latifolium?

      Pseuderanthemum latifolium is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice depending on the intended therapeutic application. The leaves and stems are frequently simmered in water to create a concentrated decoction for internal use. Alternatively, steeping the dried plant material in hot water allows for a gentler infusion that preserves delicate volatile compounds.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Pseuderanthemum latifolium can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, including intense abdominal cramping, persistent nausea, and acute diarrhea if ingested. These symptoms often arise from the plant's high concentration of unidentified alkaloids that irritate the digestive lining. Some individuals may also experience localized skin irritation or contact dermatitis after touching the leaves. In higher doses, the toxins may lead to neurological symptoms such as dizziness or lightheadedness. It is crucial to avoid all contact with this species to prevent these adverse reactions.
