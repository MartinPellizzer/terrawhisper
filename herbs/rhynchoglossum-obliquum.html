Rhynchoglossum obliquum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhynchoglossum obliquum has elongated, elliptical leaves that emerge from pseudobulbs and produce fragrant, star-shaped flowers with prominent, colorful lip markings. The leaves are typically leathery in texture and arranged in a way that supports the plant's epiphytic growth habit. Its pseudobulbs serve as essential water storage organs to sustain the plant during periods of low humidity. The flowers feature a distinctively shaped labellum that often displays intricate patterns used to attract specific pollinators. These blossoms are characterized by their long, spreading petals and sepals that create a wide, open floral structure.
