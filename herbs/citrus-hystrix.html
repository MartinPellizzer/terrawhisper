Citrus hystrix

What's the taxonomical classification of Citrus hystrix?

Citrus hystrix belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Sapindales. This plant is a member of the family Rutaceae and is specifically categorized within the genus Citrus, ultimately reaching its scientific designation as the species hystrix.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Citrus hystrix contains high concentrations of bioactive compounds including citronellal, limonene, and various flavonoids such as hesperidin and naringin. The essential oil derived from its leaves and rind is primarily composed of citronellal, which provides potent antimicrobial and insecticidal properties. These phytochemicals work together to exhibit significant antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antifungal activities in pharmacological studies. Additionally, the presence of polyphenols contributes to the plant's ability to mitigate oxidative stress within biological systems. Such diverse chemical constituents make the plant a subject of interest for developing natural therapeutic agents.

What are the therapeutic actions of Citrus hystrix?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, digestive issues, respiratory ailments, and inflammatory conditions. The essential oils extracted from its leaves and rind possess potent antimicrobial properties that help combat fungal and bacterial skin disorders. In traditional medicine, decoctions are often consumed to alleviate symptoms of indigestion, stomach aches, and bloating. It is also applied topically or inhaled to provide relief from coughs, colds, and various respiratory congestions. Furthermore, its antioxidant compounds assist in reducing systemic inflammation throughout the body.
