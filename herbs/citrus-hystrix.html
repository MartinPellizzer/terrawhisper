Citrus hystrix

What's the taxonomical classification of Citrus hystrix?

Citrus hystrix belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Sapindales. This plant is a member of the family Rutaceae and is specifically categorized within the genus Citrus, ultimately reaching its scientific designation as the species hystrix.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Citrus hystrix has dark green, glossy leaves characterized by a unique double-leaf structure where a smaller leaflet is fused to the base of a larger one, creating a distinctively notched or kidney-shaped appearance. This morphological feature gives the foliage a bi-lobed look that resembles a miniature figure-eight. The tree typically develops a spreading canopy with woody branches and small, serrated margins along the leaf edges. Its flowers are white and fragrant, emerging in small clusters from the axils of the leaves. The fruit, commonly known as kaffir lime, is a small, green, highly irregular sphere with a deeply wrinkled and bumpy rind.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, including countries such as Indonesia, Malaysia, Thailand, and Vietnam. It thrives in warm, humid climates where rainfall is abundant throughout the year. While its origins are centered in these specific Asian territories, it has been introduced to many other tropical zones worldwide for culinary and medicinal purposes. Cultivation is widespread in many island nations throughout the Indo-Pacific region due to its adaptability to moist soils. Because of its popularity in Southeast Asian cuisine, it can now be found in many botanical gardens and home gardens across the globe.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Citrus hystrix contains medicinal compounds primarily located in its aromatic leaves, thick rind, and essential oils extracted from the fruit. The leaves are frequently used in traditional medicine to treat digestive issues, fever, and skin ailments due to their antibacterial properties. The rind and juice are valued for their high concentration of citral, which serves as a powerful antiseptic and anti-inflammatory agent. Additionally, the essential oils extracted from the entire plant are applied topically to alleviate muscle aches and respiratory congestion. These various components work together to provide a wide range of therapeutic benefits in many Southeast Asian cultures.

What are the pharmacological activities of Citrus hystrix?

This plant has antibacterial, antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimelanogenic pharmacological activities. Its chemical constituents allow it to inhibit the growth of various pathogenic microorganisms effectively. The presence of bioactive compounds also helps reduce oxidative stress and mitigate inflammatory responses within biological systems. Furthermore, its specific properties can suppress melanin production, which provides significant antimelanogenic benefits. These diverse therapeutic actions make Citrus hystrix a valuable subject for pharmaceutical research.

As discussed in a study, the antibacterial activity of the volatile oil extracted from kaffir lime (Citrus hystrix DC.) was investigated. This research focused on the activity of 10 volatile oils against four standard strains of Staphylococcus aureus, Escherichia coli, Pseudomonas aeruginosa, and Acinetobacter baumannii. The study also examined the effects against 30 clinical isolates of multidrug-resistant A. baumannii. Methods used to determine the bactericidal activity included agar diffusion, minimum inhibitory concentration, and minimum bactericidal concentration.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds citral, citronellal, eucalyptol, eugenol, and sabinene. These bioactive constituents contribute to the plant's diverse therapeutic properties, including antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Citral and citronellal are often recognized for their potent insect-repelling and antiseptic qualities. Meanwhile, eucalyptol provides significant respiratory benefits, while eugenol acts as a powerful analgesic and anesthetic. The presence of sabinene further enhances the plant's overall chemical complexity and biological activity.

As discussed in a study, the volatile constituents of Citrus hystrix leaves were analyzed by gas chromatography coupled with mass spectrometer. These compounds mainly consist of eucalyptol, citronellal, and citral. When used in a combination extract at a 1:2:1 ratio, the water extract form demonstrated medicinal effects. In rats fed the extract for 14 days, the treatment resulted in significantly less severe hepatitis compared to simvastatin-treated rats. The extract also corrected blood cholesterol, LDL, HDL, and triglyceride levels.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating acne vulgaris, Candida albicans, Escherichia coli, Staphylococcus aureus, and gingivitis due to its potent antimicrobial properties. The essential oils extracted from its leaves and rind exhibit significant inhibitory effects against various pathogenic bacteria and fungal infections. Researchers have observed that its bioactive compounds can effectively combat skin inflammatory conditions like acne vulgaris. Furthermore, the plant demonstrates efficacy in controlling gastrointestinal pathogens such as Escherichia coli and common skin infections caused by Staphylococcus aureus. It also serves as a natural remedy for oral health issues, specifically targeting the inflammation associated with gingivitis.

What are the herbal preparations of this plant?

This plant can be prepared as an essential oil, infusion, decoction, tincture, or extract for various therapeutic uses. The essential oil is primarily extracted from the leaves to provide a potent aroma and antimicrobial properties. An infusion of the leaves is often used to create soothing teas, while a decoction involves boiling tougher parts of the plant to draw out medicinal compounds. For more concentrated applications, a tincture can be made by soaking the plant material in alcohol. Finally, an extract provides a standardized way to utilize the plant's bioactive constituents in different formulations.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause an allergic reaction, nausea, dizziness, and stomach upset in sensitive individuals. Some people may experience skin irritation or rashes if the essential oils come into direct contact with the body. Ingesting large quantities can lead to significant stomach upset and gastrointestinal discomfort. Additionally, certain users have reported feeling dizzy or lightheaded after consuming its derivatives. It is important to use this plant with caution to avoid these adverse physical reactions.

The main side effects this plant can have are shown in the list below.

  • Nausea
