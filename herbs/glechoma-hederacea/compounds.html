Ground Ivy (Glechoma hederacea) Compounds

The primary compounds in Ground Ivy include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and phenolic acids, known for their anti-inflammatory effects. Coumarin contributes to the plant's mild sedative qualities, while tannins provide astringent properties. Glycosides and terpenoids support its traditional use in respiratory and skin treatments.

Ground Ivy (Glechoma hederacea) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Ground Ivy (Glechoma hederacea) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help reduce oxidative stress and support immune function. They are found in various parts of the plant, including leaves and stems. Flavonoids are key to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Ground Ivy (Glechoma hederacea). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and support cellular health. This compound is commonly found in the leaves and is valued for its potential therapeutic effects in herbal medicine.

3. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Ground Ivy (Glechoma hederacea). It contributes to the plant's mild sedative and anti-inflammatory properties. Found in the leaves and stems, coumarin is used in herbal medicine for its calming effects and potential to support respiratory health.

4. Tannin

Tannin in Ground Ivy (Glechoma hederacea) contributes to its astringent properties. It helps reduce inflammation and soothe irritated tissues. Tannins are found in the leaves and are used in traditional herbal medicine for their healing effects.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Ground Ivy (Glechoma hederacea). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly in respiratory and skin conditions. Glycosides in Ground Ivy are known for their expectorant and anti-inflammatory effects. These compounds are often extracted and used in herbal preparations.

6. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Ground Ivy (Glechoma hederacea) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. They are responsible for some of the plant's traditional uses in treating respiratory and skin conditions. Terpenoids are commonly found in the leaves and aerial parts of the plant.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Ground Ivy (Glechoma hederacea). It contributes to the plant's mild expectorant and antimicrobial properties, supporting respiratory and skin health. Saponins may help reduce inflammation and enhance the immune response when used in herbal remedies.

8. Essential oil

Essential oil from Ground Ivy (Glechoma hederacea) contains volatile compounds like thymol and carvacrol. These components contribute to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The essential oil is often used in herbal medicine for respiratory and digestive support. It is extracted through steam distillation from the plant's leaves and flowers.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Ground Ivy (Glechoma hederacea). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, though its specific therapeutic effects are less well-documented compared to other constituents. Alkaloids in Ground Ivy are often associated with its traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Ground Ivy (Glechoma hederacea). It contributes to the plant's deep purple color and has antioxidant properties. This compound may support cardiovascular health and reduce inflammation, making it a valuable component in traditional herbal medicine.
