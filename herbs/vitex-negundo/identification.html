Botanical Identification of Negundo Vitex (Vitex negundo)

To identify Negundo Vitex (Vitex negundo) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Negundo Vitex, or Vitex negundo, belongs to the plant kingdom, specifically the division of flowering plants, class Lamiales, order Lamiales, family Verbenaceae, genus Vitex, and species Vitex negundo. This classification places it within the flowering plant group, highlighting its botanical relationships and characteristics.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Negundo Vitex is Vitex negundo, first described by the botanist Antoine Laurent de Jussieu in the 18th century. It is also known by several synonyms, including Vitex negundo var. lindenii and Vitex lindenii, reflecting historical taxonomic revisions. Common names such as "Negundo Vitex" and "Chamomile Vitex" are used in various regions, while historical names often reflect its traditional medicinal uses across cultures.

Morphological Characteristics

Negundo Vitex is a shrub or small tree with a branched root system. Its stem is upright, often with thorns, and has a rough, grayish bark. Leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy surface. The inflorescence is a panicle, bearing small, clustered flowers. Flowers are bisexual, with blue or purple petals and yellow stamens. The fruit is a 3-lobed capsule containing three seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Vitex negundo can be distinguished by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a hairy stem. It produces small, clustered purple flowers followed by smooth, dark purple berries. The leaves often have a faint aromatic scent when crushed, helping in identification. It is commonly found near water sources, which differentiates it from similar species growing in drier conditions.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Negundo Vitex is native to parts of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with warm, humid conditions. It prefers well-drained soils and is commonly found at low to mid-altitudes in forested areas.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Vitex negundo blooms in late spring to early summer, producing fragrant blue or purple flowers. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early fall. For identification purposes, harvesting is best done during the flowering period to ensure accurate recognition of the plant.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Vitex negundo belongs to the genus Vitex, which is part of the family Lamiaceae. Its closest relatives include other Vitex species such as Vitex agnus-castus. The genus shares characteristic features like opposite leaves and showy flowers, typical of the Lamiaceae family.
