Negundo Vitex (Vitex negundo) Compounds

The primary compounds of Vitex negundo include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and tannins, known for their astringent effects. Phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory actions, while coumarin may have anticoagulant properties. Essential oils and saponins also play roles in its medicinal uses.

Negundo Vitex (Vitex negundo) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Vitex negundo are bioactive compounds known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. They contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments. These compounds help modulate immune responses and may support overall health. Their presence enhances the therapeutic potential of Vitex negundo in herbal medicine.

2. Tannin

Tannin in Vitex negundo contributes to its astringent properties, aiding in reducing inflammation and bleeding. It is found in the leaves and flowers, supporting traditional uses for digestive and skin conditions. Tannins also help preserve the plant's medicinal potency during preparation.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Vitex negundo, contributing to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound supports the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive issues. It helps modulate immune responses and protect cells from oxidative damage. Its presence enhances the therapeutic potential of Vitex negundo in herbal medicine.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Vitex negundo, known for its antispasmodic and sedative properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating menstrual disorders and nervous system conditions. Coumarin also exhibits mild anticoagulant effects, supporting its role in herbal medicine for circulatory and hormonal health.

5. Essential oil

The essential oil of Vitex negundo contains volatile compounds like limonene and pinene, which contribute to its aromatic properties. These oils are commonly extracted through steam distillation and are used in traditional medicine for their potential calming and antispasmodic effects.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Vitex negundo, known for its ability to enhance circulation and support hormonal balance. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating menstrual disorders and digestive issues. Saponin's cleansing and anti-inflammatory properties make it a valuable component in herbal formulations.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Vitex negundo contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects. They are involved in the plant's traditional use for treating menstrual disorders and headaches. Terpenoids also show potential in antimicrobial applications.

8. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Vitex negundo, a traditional medicinal plant used in herbal medicine. It contributes to the plant's therapeutic properties, including anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects. Glycosides in Vitex negundo are often responsible for its use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a bioactive compound found in Vitex negundo, a traditional medicinal plant used in herbal medicine. 10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Vitex negundo, known for its antioxidant properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating inflammation and digestive issues. This pigment also supports skin health and may aid in reducing oxidative stress in the body.
