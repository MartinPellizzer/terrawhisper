What is Dalea grayi?

Dalea grayi is a perennial shrub that belongs to the family Fabaceae, commonly known as the pea family.

It has a woody base and a branching habit, with an average height of 0.5-1.5 meters. The plant's stems are covered with fine, silky hairs and bear leaflets with 2-3 pairs of pinnae, each with 6-10 leaflets. The leaflets are elliptical in shape, 1-3 centimeters long, and have a leathery texture.

The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, followed by flat, brown seed pods 2-3 centimeters long.
