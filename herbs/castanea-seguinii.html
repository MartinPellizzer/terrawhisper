Castanea seguinii

What's the taxonomical classification of Castanea seguinii?

Castanea seguinii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further organized into the order Fagales. It is a member of the family Fagaceae, falling under the genus Castanea, and is specifically identified by its species name, seguinii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Castanea seguinii has lanceolate to oblong leaves that are typically 5 to 15 centimeters long, featuring serrated margins and a distinctively acuminate apex. The foliage is characterized by a dark green upper surface and a paler underside, often displaying a somewhat leathery texture. Its reproductive structures consist of catkins that produce small, inconspicuous flowers within a spiny, globose cupule. This protective husk eventually splits to reveal the mature, nut-like fruit contained inside. The overall growth habit is that of a deciduous tree with a spreading crown and rough, grayish bark.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

The species is often found in forests where it can access well-drained soil and moderate humidity levels.
