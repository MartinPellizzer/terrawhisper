Lannea coromandelica

What's the taxonomical classification of Lannea coromandelica?

Lannea coromandelica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Sapindales and the family Anacardiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Lannea, and its unique species designation, coromandelica.

Lannea coroandelicam contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including tannins, saponins, flavonoids, and terpenoids. These specific chemical constituents contribute to its diverse pharmacological profile used in traditional medicine. Additionally, the extracts are often utilized for their antimicrobial and wound-healing capabilities. Researchers study these specific molecules to understand their potential in treating various physiological ailments.

What are the therapeutic actions of Lannea coromandelica?

Lannea coroemandelica has medicinal properties that primarily involve its use as an antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and wound-healing agent in traditional medicine. The bark and leaves contain bioactive compounds such as tannins and polyphenols that help combat various bacterial infections. These chemical constituents work to reduce swelling and soothe irritation when applied topically to damaged skin. Additionally, decoctions made from the plant parts are sometimes used to treat gastrointestinal distress and digestive ailments. The plant serves as a vital natural resource for treating localized inflammation and promoting tissue regeneration in specific cultural practices.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including skin infections, stomach pains, and fever. Local traditional practices often utilize its components to manage gastrointestinal distress. It is also applied topically to address dermatological issues such as sores or rashes. The medicinal properties are frequently sought for their potential to alleviate general systemic discomfort. These applications highlight the plant's importance within specific regional ethnobotanical knowledge.

What are the herbal preparations of Lannea coromandelica?

Lannea coroandelicata is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste derived from its bark, leaves, and roots. These liquid extractions are frequently used in traditional medicine to treat various inflammatory conditions and digestive issues. The leaves can also be crushed into a poultice to be applied directly to the skin for wound healing. Additionally, some preparations involve boiling the woody parts to create a concentrated medicinal tea. These diverse forms allow the plant's bioactive compounds to be utilized for both internal and external therapeutic purposes.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Lannea coroemandelica can cause gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, or allergic reactions if ingested or applied topically without proper preparation. While many parts of the tree are used in traditional medicine, consuming concentrated extracts may lead to nausea or stomach cramps. Some individuals might experience localized itching or redness upon contact with the plant's bark or leaves. It is essential to exercise caution because the chemical composition of certain species in this genus can vary significantly. Always consult a professional before attempting to use this plant for medicinal purposes.
