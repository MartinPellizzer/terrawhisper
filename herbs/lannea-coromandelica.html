Lannea coromandelica

What's the taxonomical classification of Lannea coromandelica?

Lannea coromandelica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Sapindales and the family Anacardiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Lannea, and its unique species designation, coromandelica.

What are the pharmacological activities of Lannea coromandelica?

This plant has significant antioxidant pharmacological activities driven by its high concentration of bioactive phenolic compounds. These phytochemicals work by neutralizing free radicals and reducing oxidative stress within biological systems. Research indicates that the extracts from Lannea coroandemlica can effectively inhibit lipid peroxidation and protect cells from oxidative damage. According to a study published by "African journal of traditional, complementary, and alternative medicines : AJTCAM", the plant Lannea coromandelica is one of 12 medicinal plants used by the Marakh sect of the Garo tribe for the treatment of diabetes. A review of the scientific literature demonstrated that almost all the plants used by these tribal practitioners, including Lannea coromandelica, have reported antidiabetic and/or antioxidant properties. These plants have enormous potential for the possible development of new and efficacious antidiabetic drugs. This research was conducted in the Mymensingh district of Bangladesh where practitioners use these formulations to manage the disease.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains caffeic acid, catechin, chlorogenic acid, gallic acid, and (-)-epigallocatechin-3-gallate as its primary medicinal compounds. These specific phytochemicals are recognized for their significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The presence of gallic acid and chlorogenic acid contributes to the plant's ability to combat oxidative stress within biological systems. Additionally, the inclusion of catechin and (-)-epigallocatechin-3-gallate enhances its potential therapeutic value in various health studies. These diverse bioactive substances work together to provide a complex profile of medicinal benefits.

According to a study published by "International journal of molecular sciences", the bark extract of Lannea coromandelica contains key polyphenolic compounds including gallic acid, (-) -epigallocatechin-3-gallate, catechin, chlorogenic acid, and caffeic acid. These compounds were detected through high-pressure liquid chromatography (HPLC) analysis. The extract was tested using RAW 264.7 cells to evaluate its antioxidant effects. Through the upregulation of the nuclear factor erythroid 2-related factor 2 (NRF2) mediated pathway via the phosphorylation of p38 kinase and c-Jun N-terminal kinase (JNK), the extract enhances the expression of heme oxygenase 1. This process allows the plant extract to combat oxidative stress and mitigate cellular reactive oxygen species (ROS) generation.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating bacterial infections, dysentery, painful conditions, pains of the body, and ulcers. Its medicinal properties allow it to combat various internal ailments and skin issues. Practitioners often apply its derivatives to soothe intense pains of the body and manage discomfort from chronic conditions. It also serves as an effective remedy for digestive distress such as dysentery. Furthermore, the plant's components are utilized to heal open ulcers and clear up bacterial infections.

According to a study published by "Molecules (Basel, Switzerland)", Lannea coroandelica was identified among species shown to have significant antimicrobial activity. This plant demonstrated anti-infective activity against both Gram-positive and Gram-negative bacteria. The screening process involved testing 662 plant extracts from 222 plant species across 82 families and 177 genera. While the study notes that methanolic extracts of several plants showed zones of inhibition ≥ 12 mm, it highlights Lannea coroandelica as having significant activity against a broad spectrum of bacterial species. These findings contribute to the search for antibacterial agents to combat the high global burden of bacterial infections.
