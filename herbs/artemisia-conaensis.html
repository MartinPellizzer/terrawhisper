What's the taxonomical classification of Artemisia conaensis?

Artemisia conaensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its hierarchical lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it firmly within the order Asterales. As a member of the family Asteraceae, it is part of a diverse group of flowering plants, specifically categorized under the genus Artemisia. Ultimately, its unique biological identity is defined by its specific designation as the species conaensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Artemisia conaensis has a perennial growth habit characterized by woody, erect stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. The foliage consists of deeply lobed or pinnatifid leaves that exhibit a silvery-grey appearance due to a dense covering of fine hairs. These leaves are arranged alternately along the stem and possess narrow, linear segments. The inflorescence is composed of small, discoid flower heads clustered in terminal panicles. Each flower head is relatively inconspicuous and lacks prominent petals, following the typical structure of the Asteraceae family.
