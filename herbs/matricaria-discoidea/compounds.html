German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea) Compounds

German Chamomile contains essential oil, which includes compounds like bisabolol and chamazulene. Flavonoids, such as apigenin, contribute to its anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarin is known for its antispasmodic effects, while terpenoids and phenolic acids support its calming and antiseptic actions.

German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea) is the primary active compound, containing compounds like bisabolol and chamazulene. These compounds contribute to its anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and calming properties, making it a key component in herbal treatments for digestive and skin conditions.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are key active compounds in German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds support the plant's traditional use in soothing digestive issues and reducing inflammation. Flavonoids are also responsible for the characteristic mild, calming aroma of chamomile.

3. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea). It contributes to the plant's calming and anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarin is responsible for the herb's characteristic scent and is used in traditional medicine for its soothing effects on the nervous system.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects, making it useful for digestive and skin conditions. Its presence supports the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

5. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a major class of active compounds in German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and sedative properties. Key terpenoids like bisabolol and chamazulene are responsible for its calming and analgesic effects. These compounds are extracted through steam distillation and are widely used in herbal medicine for their therapeutic benefits.

6. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health by reducing inflammation and aiding in the healing of mucous membranes. Tannins are also believed to enhance the absorption of other active constituents in the herb.

7. Glycoside

German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea) contains the glycoside chamazulene, which is responsible for its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. This compound contributes to the plant's effectiveness in treating digestive issues and respiratory conditions.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and mild sedative effects. Saponins also aid in the plant's ability to deter pests and support its traditional use in herbal medicine.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce inflammation. This compound supports the plant's therapeutic use in calming digestive issues and promoting skin health.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloids in German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea) are present in smaller amounts compared to other compounds. They contribute to the plant's mild sedative and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting its use in traditional herbal medicine for calming and digestive support.
