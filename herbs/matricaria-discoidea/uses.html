German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea) Uses

German Chamomile is used to treat allergic rhinitis, hay fever, and nasal congestion due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties. It also helps with mild asthma symptoms and respiratory infections. Additionally, it is applied for skin irritation, eczema, and allergic conjunctivitis because of its soothing and calming effects.

German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea) Monograph

1. Allergic rhinitis

German Chamomile has been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion, sneezing, and runny nose by soothing the respiratory tract. Studies suggest its essential oils may inhibit histamine release, offering a natural alternative for managing seasonal allergies.

2. Respiratory infection

German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea) is used to support respiratory health by reducing inflammation and soothing irritation in the throat and airways. It may help alleviate symptoms of mild respiratory infections such as coughs and sore throats. Its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties make it a valuable herbal remedy in respiratory care.

3. Hay fever

German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea) is used to alleviate hay fever symptoms due to its antihistamine properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion, itching, and sneezing by calming allergic reactions. Often consumed as a tea, it supports respiratory comfort during seasonal allergies. Its mild, soothing effects make it a popular remedy in herbal medicine for managing hay fever.

4. Nasal congestion

German Chamomile is used to alleviate nasal congestion due to its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. It helps reduce swelling and mucus production in the nasal passages. Often used in steam inhalations or as a nasal spray, it provides relief for conditions like sinusitis and allergies. Its mild action makes it suitable for long-term use under professional guidance.

5. Respiratory tract infections

German Chamomile is used to support respiratory health by reducing inflammation and soothing irritation in the airways. It may help alleviate symptoms of coughs, bronchitis, and other respiratory infections due to its antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties. Its essential oil is often used in steam inhalations or as a topical application to ease breathing.

6. Respiratory infections

German Chamomile is used to support respiratory health by reducing inflammation and soothing irritation in the airways. It may help alleviate symptoms of coughs, bronchitis, and sore throats. Its antiseptic properties can aid in clearing mucus and preventing infection. It is often prepared as a steam inhalation or herbal tea for respiratory support.

7. Mild asthma symptoms

German Chamomile may help alleviate mild asthma symptoms due to its antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties. It can soothe airway irritation and reduce bronchial spasms. Inhalation of its steam or consumption as a tea may provide relief. However, it should not replace prescribed asthma treatments.

8. Allergic conjunctivitis

German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea) is used in herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms of allergic conjunctivitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistaminic properties. It helps reduce eye redness, itching, and swelling, often through locally applied infusions or eye drops. Its mild nature makes it a preferred option for sensitive individuals seeking natural relief.

9. Skin irritation

German Chamomile is effective for skin irritation due to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. It can be applied topically as a compress or infused oil to reduce redness, swelling, and itching. Its essential oils contain compounds like bisabolol that promote healing and calm sensitive skin. It is commonly used for eczema, rashes, and insect bites.

10. Eczema

German Chamomile is used topically for eczema due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistaminic properties. It helps reduce skin irritation and redness. A chamomile infusion applied as a compress can soothe inflamed skin. It is often recommended as a natural alternative to synthetic treatments in herbal medicine.
