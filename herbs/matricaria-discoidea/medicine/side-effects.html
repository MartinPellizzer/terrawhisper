10 Possible Side Effects Of Matricaria Discoidea

Matricaria discoidea has some side effects when used improperly, such as skin irritation, allergic reactions, and digestive issues.

Causes of these side effects include prolonged use, excessive consumption, and individual sensitivities.

For example, skin irritation can lead to discomfort and affect daily activities, while digestive issues can cause nausea, diarrhea, and affect one's work or studies.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Matricaria discoidea causes allergic reactions in some individuals due to its high concentration of sesquiterpene lactones and flavonoids.

These compounds can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, redness, and swelling at the site of contact or inhalation.

Additionally, the plant's pollen and resin can also cause allergic reactions in those with sensitive skin or respiratory systems.

2. Triggers asthma attacks

Matricaria discoidea triggers asthma attacks due to its potent allergenic and irritant properties.

The plant's tiny flowers and leaves contain sesquiterpene lactones, which can stimulate the release of histamine in susceptible individuals, leading to bronchospasm and wheezing.

Additionally, Matricaria discoidea contains a significant amount of pollen, which can exacerbate respiratory issues and trigger allergic reactions in people with asthma, causing severe symptoms such as shortness of breath, coughing, and chest tightness.

3. Increases heart rate

Matricaria discoidea increases heart rate due to its stimulatory effects on the cardiovascular system.

This is caused by the presence of sesquiterpenes and flavonoids in the plant, which can stimulate the heart and blood vessels, leading to an increase in cardiac output and blood pressure.

As a result, users may experience a rapid heartbeat or palpitations as a side effect of consuming Matricaria discoidea.

4. Lowers blood pressure

Matricaria discoidea lowers blood pressure due to its flavonoid and saponin compounds.

These bioactive molecules interact with the body's cardiovascular system, causing a decline in blood pressure by dilating blood vessels and reducing peripheral resistance.

This effect is thought to be a result of Matricaria discoidea's ability to relax smooth muscle cells in blood vessel walls, allowing for increased blood flow and reduced blood pressure.

5. Causes skin irritation

Matricaria discoidea causes skin irritation due to its oily resinous coating on its leaves and stems.

The presence of this coating can cause an allergic reaction in some individuals, leading to skin redness, itching, and burning sensations.

Additionally, the plant's sap contains a chemical compound that can trigger contact dermatitis, further exacerbating the skin irritation.

6. Induces sleepiness

Matricaria discoidea induces sleepiness due to its sedative properties.

The herb contains sesquiterpene lactones, which have a calming effect on the nervous system, leading to relaxation and drowsiness. Additionally, Matricaria's flavonoids, such as apigenin, interact with GABA receptors in the brain, further enhancing its sleep-inducing effects.

As a result, users may experience increased sleepiness or fatigue after consuming Matricaria discoidea, making it unsuitable for those who require alertness and focus.

7. Interferes with digestion

Matricaria discoidea interferes with digestion by stimulating the release of stomach acid and digestive enzymes, which can lead to discomfort, bloating, and cramping.

The herb's potent volatile oils, particularly camphor and borneol, may irritate the digestive tract, causing symptoms such as nausea, diarrhea, or stomach pain.

As Matricaria discoidea increases blood flow to the digestive system, it may also disrupt normal digestion and absorption of nutrients.

8. Affects cognitive function

Matricaria discoidea affects cognitive function due to its ability to interact with neurotransmitters and hormones in the brain.

The plant's constituents, such as flavonoids and terpenes, can bind to receptors responsible for regulating memory and attention, potentially leading to impaired focus, confusion, and difficulty concentrating.

As a result, individuals using Matricaria discoidea may experience altered mental clarity and processing speed.

9. Causes stomach upset

Matricaria discoidea causes stomach upset when ingested due to its volatile oil content.

The essential oils present in the plant can irritate the mucous membranes of the stomach and intestines, leading to symptoms such as nausea, bloating, and cramps.

Additionally, the plant's flavonoids and phenolic acids may also contribute to gastric distress by altering gut bacteria and increasing digestive enzyme production, resulting in uncomfortable gastrointestinal effects.

10. Increases saliva production

Matricaria discoidea increases saliva production due to its natural ability to stimulate the salivary glands.

This is likely attributed to the presence of sesquiterpene lactones, a type of bioactive compound found in the plant's essential oil.

As these compounds interact with the body's physiology, they can cause an increase in saliva production as a natural response, potentially leading to a sensation of excessive drooling or mouth watering.
