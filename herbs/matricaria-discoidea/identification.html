Botanical Identification of German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea)

To identify German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

German Chamomile, or Matricaria discoidea, belongs to the plant kingdom, specifically the division of flowering plants, class Magnoliopsida, order Asterales, family Asteraceae, genus Matricaria, and species Matricaria discoidea. This classification reflects its position within the broader plant taxonomy and its relationships to other flowering plants.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of German Chamomile is Matricaria discoidea, first described by Carl von Linné in 1753. It is also known by several synonyms, including Chamomilla recutita, which reflects its traditional use in herbal medicine. Common names include German Chamomile, while regional and historical names vary, often linked to its widespread use in folk remedies across Europe.

Morphological Characteristics

German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea) is an annual herb with a branched root system. Its stems are upright and hairy, typically growing up to 30 cm tall. The leaves are alternate, spoon-shaped, and have a serrated edge. The inflorescence consists of a single flower head, with white petals and yellow centers. The fruit is a small, dry achene with a pappus for wind dispersal.

Diagnostic Identification Features

German Chamomile can be distinguished by its daisy-like flower heads with white petals and a yellow center, and its finely divided, serrated leaves. It has a strong, sweet aroma and is often found in dry, open areas. It is different from other chamomiles like Roman Chamomile, which has smaller flowers and a more compact growth habit.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

German Chamomile is native to southern Europe and western Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with mild summers and cool winters. It prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils. It typically grows at elevations between 500 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

German Chamomile blooms from June to September, with flower heads opening in the morning. Seed formation occurs in late summer, typically August to September. For identification, the plant is most easily recognized during its flowering stage, which is key for accurate botanical identification.

Phylogenetic Relationships

German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea) is closely related to species within the genus Matricaria, particularly Matricaria chamomilla. It shares characteristics with plants in the Asteraceae family, such as composite flower heads and alternate leaf arrangements. Its phylogenetic placement reflects its evolutionary ties to other chamomile and related flowering plants.
