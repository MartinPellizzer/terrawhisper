Virginia Water-Hemlock (Lycopus Virginicus Var. Macrophyllus)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

*Lycopus virginicus* var. *macrophyllus*, commonly known as large-leaved bugleweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States, often found in moist woodlands and stream banks. Traditionally used in herbal medicine, it has been valued for its potential cardiovascular benefits, including the regulation of heart rhythm and reduction of high blood pressure. The plant contains compounds such as flavonoids and iridoids, which may contribute to its therapeutic effects. Historically, it was used by Native American tribes for treating heart conditions and as a sedative. Today, it is still used in some herbal formulations, though scientific research on its efficacy and safety continues to be explored.
