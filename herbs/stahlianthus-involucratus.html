Stahlianthus involucratus

What's the taxonomical classification of Stahlianthus involucratus?

Stahlianthus involucratus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is placed in the order Zingiberales. It is a member of the family Zingiberaceae, falling under the genus Stahlianthus, and is specifically identified by the species name involucratus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stahlianthus involucratus has lanceolate to ovate leaves that are arranged oppositely on a stem and characterized by prominent longitudinal veins and a distinctively serrated margin.

The plant produces large, showy inflorescences that are protected by multiple layers of colorful, leaf-like bracts. These bracts typically display vibrant hues such as red or pink, which serve to attract pollinators to the small, tubular flowers nestled within. The flowers themselves are zygomorphic and emerge from the dense bract clusters during the blooming season.

The overall architecture of the plant is robust, often reaching a significant height depending on the environmental conditions of its tropical habitat.
