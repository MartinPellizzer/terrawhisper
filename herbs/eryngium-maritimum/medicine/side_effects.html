1. Causes allergic reactions

Eryngium maritimum causes allergic reactions in some individuals due to its unique combination of compounds, including sesquiterpene lactones and flavonoids.

These chemicals can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms such as skin irritation, itching, redness, and swelling.

The exact mechanism is unclear, but it's thought that the body misinterprets these compounds as foreign invaders, causing an allergic reaction.

2. Triggers bronchial spasms

Eryngium maritimum triggers bronchial spasms due to its potential to cause an allergic reaction or irritation in the respiratory system.

The plant contains sesquiterpene lactones, a class of compounds that can stimulate the airways and trigger coughing fits or wheezing in susceptible individuals.

Additionally, some people may be particularly sensitive to the plant's pollen or other particles, which can further exacerbate bronchial spasms and respiratory issues.

3. Induces respiratory distress

Eryngium maritimum induces respiratory distress due to its ability to release irritating compounds when ingested or inhaled.

The plant contains sesquiterpenes and phenolic acids, which can cause constriction of the airways and inflammation of the lungs.

This reaction can lead to symptoms such as coughing, wheezing, and shortness of breath, particularly in individuals with pre-existing respiratory conditions.

4. Enhances toxicity levels

Eryngium maritimum enhances toxicity levels in some individuals due to its saponin and alkaloid content.

These compounds can cause an increase in the body's natural detoxification processes, leading to symptoms such as nausea, dizziness, and headaches. Additionally, the plant's bitter properties may irritate the stomach lining, further exacerbating digestive issues.

As a result, users of Eryngium maritimum may experience adverse reactions, including gastrointestinal disturbances and increased sensitivity to other substances.

5. Reduces fertility rates

Eryngium maritimum reduces fertility rates by affecting the reproductive system.

The plant's bioactive compounds, including sesquiterpenes and lignans, can alter hormone levels and disrupt ovulation, leading to decreased sperm count and motility in males. Additionally, Eryngium maritimum may cause uterine contractions, reducing the likelihood of successful implantation of a fertilized egg.

As a result, individuals using this herbal remedy for medicinal purposes should be aware of its potential impact on fertility.

6. Disrupts digestive systems

Eryngium maritimum disrupts digestive systems due to its ability to alter the balance of gut flora and interfere with nutrient absorption.

The saponins present in the plant may cause inflammation and irritation in the digestive tract, leading to symptoms such as bloating, cramps, and diarrhea.

Additionally, the flavonoids and alkaloids in Eryngium maritimum can slow down gastric emptying and reduce gut motility, further exacerbating gastrointestinal discomfort.

7. Causes nausea episodes

Eryngium maritimum causes nausea episodes due to its ability to stimulate digestive processes and increase gut motility.

This can lead to feelings of discomfort, queasiness, and stomach upset in some individuals.

The plant's high concentration of essential oils, such as camphor and borneol, may also contribute to these gastrointestinal side effects by altering the balance of gut bacteria and irritating the lining of the stomach and intestines.

8. Provokes anxiety attacks

Eryngium maritimum provokes anxiety attacks by interacting with the body's neurotransmitters, particularly serotonin and GABA.

The compound eringalin in Eryngium maritimum can bind to serotonin receptors, leading to increased levels of stress hormones like cortisol and adrenaline, causing feelings of panic and unease.

Additionally, the plant's saponins may also contribute to the development of anxiety attacks by affecting the brain's neurotransmitter balance, resulting in symptoms such as rapid heartbeat, sweating, and trembling.

9. Increases blood pressure

Eryngium maritimum increases blood pressure due to its high content of saponins and flavonoids.

These compounds can stimulate the cardiovascular system, causing an increase in blood pressure and heart rate. Additionally, Eryngium maritimum may interact with certain medications, such as beta-blockers and diuretics, which can exacerbate the effect on blood pressure.

As a result, users of this herb should be aware of its potential impact on their cardiovascular system.

10. Triggers cardiac arrhythmias

Eryngium maritimum triggers cardiac arrhythmias due to its ability to affect the body's potassium levels.

The plant contains saponins, which can interfere with the normal functioning of the heart by altering the balance of potassium and other electrolytes in the blood.

This disruption can lead to irregular heartbeat rhythms, including tachycardia and bradycardia.
