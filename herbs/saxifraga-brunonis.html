What's the taxonomical classification of Saxifraga brunonis?

Saxifraga brunonis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is placed in the order Saxifrages, specifically within the family Saxifragaecae. It is a member of the genus Saxifraga, and its specific identification is defined by the species name brunonis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saxifraga brunonis has a perennial growth habit characterized by a basal rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with slightly serrated margins.

These leaves are arranged in a compact cluster to conserve moisture and protect the central growing point. From this rosette, a slender, leafless flowering stalk emerges, reaching several centimeters in height. The inflorescence consists of small, five-petaled flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color.
