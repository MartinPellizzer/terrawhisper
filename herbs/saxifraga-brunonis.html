Saxifraga brunonis has a perennial growth habit characterized by a basal rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with slightly serrated margins. The inflorescence consists of small, five-petaled flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. Each individual blossom is supported by a fine stem and features a central cluster of stamens.
