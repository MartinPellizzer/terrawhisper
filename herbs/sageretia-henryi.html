What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sageretia henryi has opposite, decussate leaves that are elliptical to lanceolate in shape, featuring serrated margins and a distinctively textured, somewhat rugose surface. The plant typically presents as a woody shrub with slender, branching stems that can reach moderate heights. Its inflorescences consist of small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in terminal clusters. These flowers often possess delicate petals and reproductive structures characteristic of the Rubiaceae family.
