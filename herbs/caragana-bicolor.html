Caragana bicolor

What's the taxonomical classification of Caragana bicolor?

Caragana bicolor belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Magnoliidae and is a member of the order Fabales. This plant is further categorized within the family Fabaceae, specifically residing in the genus Caragana, with its unique designation as the species bicolor.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Caragana bicolor has pinnately compound leaves consisting of multiple pairs of small, oblong leaflets and bright red, papilionaceous flowers that emerge from clusters of axillary racemes. The plant grows as a large shrub or small tree characterized by a woody trunk and a spreading canopy. Its stems are typically smooth or slightly lenticellate, providing structural support for the dense foliage. The flowers are notable for their intense pigmentation and distinct keel, wing, and standard petal structure. During the fruiting stage, the plant produces flat, brown legume pods that contain several small seeds.
