The therapeutic actions of Ficus rigida are attributed to its ability to act as an antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial agent, which may help in alleviating symptoms of various diseases.

The bioactive constituents of Ficus rigida include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and other compounds that contribute to its medicinal properties.

Ficus rigida has many active constituents, such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, alkaloids, and terpenoids, which possess various medicinal properties.

Medicinal Parts of Ficus rigida

Ficus rigida has many medicinal parts, such as its leaves, roots, and latex.

The leaves of Ficus rigida are rich in flavonoids, tannins, and saponins, which are known for their anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The roots of the plant are said to contain glycosides and alkaloids, which have been traditionally used to treat various health issues.

The latex of Ficus rigida is known to contain mucilage, which has been used to soothe and protect the mucous membranes in the body.

Possible Side Effects of Ficus rigida

Ficus rigida can have side effects if used improperly, such as causing skin irritation, allergic reactions, or respiratory issues in individuals with sensitive skin or respiratory problems.

In rare cases, ingesting the plant's sap or leaves can lead to gastrointestinal problems, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea.

Long-term exposure to the plant's volatile compounds may also contribute to headaches, fatigue, and dizziness.

It is essential to handle and use the ficus rigida plant with caution and under the guidance of a medical professional or experienced horticulturist.
