The therapeutic actions of Bellis cordifolia include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which are attributed to its bioactive constituents including sesquiterpenoids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids.

Possible Side Effects of Bellis cordifolia

Bellis cordifolia can have side effects if used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, including nausea and diarrhea, due to its high saponin content.

Additionally, excessive consumption of the plant may lead to cardiovascular problems, such as high blood pressure and arrhythmias.

In some cases, improper use of bellis cordifolia can also cause allergic reactions, including skin rashes and itching.

Furthermore, the plant may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes medications, resulting in adverse health effects.
