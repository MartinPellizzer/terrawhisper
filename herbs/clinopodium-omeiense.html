Clinopodium omeiense

What's the taxonomical classification of Clinopodium omeiense?

Clinopodium omeiense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is categorized under the order Lamiales. It further belongs to the family Lamiaceae, specifically within the genus Clinopodium, and is identified by the species name omeiense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clinopodium omeiense has an erect, perennial herb habit characterized by square stems, lanceolate to ovate leaves with serrated margins, and small, tubular, bilabiate flowers arranged in dense terminal clusters. The leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem and feature distinct venation and a slightly pubescent texture. Its inflorescence consists of compact cymes that typically display shades of pink, purple, or white. The calyx is persistent and often becomes enlarged or inflated during the fruiting stage. These morphological traits are characteristic of the Lamiaceae family and assist in identifying the species within its specific habitat.
