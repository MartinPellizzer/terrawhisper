Thyme (Thymus serpyllum) Compounds

The primary compounds in thyme include essential oils, which contain terpenoids like thymol and carvacrol. Flavonoids and phenolic acids contribute to its antioxidant properties. Tannins and saponins provide astringent effects, while coumarin and glycosides add to its medicinal profile. Alkaloids and anthocyanins further support its therapeutic uses.

Thyme (Thymus serpyllum) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Thyme (Thymus serpyllum) contains thymol and carvacrol, which are potent antimicrobial agents. These compounds contribute to Thyme's use in treating respiratory infections and digestive issues. The oil is also valued for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. It is commonly used in aromatherapy and herbal remedies.

2. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Thyme (Thymus serpyllum). They contribute to the plant's antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Common terpenoids in thyme include thymol and carvacrol, which are responsible for its strong aroma and therapeutic effects. These compounds are widely used in herbal medicine for respiratory and digestive support.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Thyme (Thymus serpyllum) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help reduce oxidative stress and support immune function. They are found in the essential oil and aerial parts of the plant. Flavonoids enhance the therapeutic value of Thyme in herbal medicine.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Thyme (Thymus serpyllum). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. This compound supports respiratory health and may aid in reducing microbial infections. Its presence enhances the therapeutic value of Thyme in herbal medicine.

5. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Thyme (Thymus serpyllum). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins help preserve the plant's medicinal qualities and support its traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Thyme (Thymus serpyllum). It contributes to the plant's distinctive aroma and has antispasmodic and mild sedative properties. Coumarin also acts as a natural anticoagulant, supporting cardiovascular health. Its presence enhances the therapeutic profile of Thyme in herbal medicine.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Thyme (Thymus serpyllum). It contributes to the plant's antimicrobial and antioxidant properties. Glycosides are often responsible for the therapeutic effects of thyme in herbal medicine. They help in reducing inflammation and supporting respiratory health.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Thyme (Thymus serpyllum). It contributes to the plant's antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Saponins help enhance the immune system and may support digestive health. They are also responsible for some of Thyme's characteristic effects in traditional herbal medicine.

9. Alkaloid

Thyme (Thymus serpyllum) contains the alkaloid thymol. Thymol is a potent antimicrobial and antifungal compound that contributes to thyme's traditional use in treating respiratory infections and digestive issues. It also enhances the plant's aromatic properties and supports its role in herbal medicine.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Thyme (Thymus serpyllum). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports the plant's traditional use in respiratory and digestive health. Anthocyanin also enhances the overall therapeutic profile of Thyme in herbal medicine.
