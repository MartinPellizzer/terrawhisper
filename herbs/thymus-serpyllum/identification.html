Botanical Identification of Thyme (Thymus serpyllum)

To identify Thyme (Thymus serpyllum) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Thyme (Thymus serpyllum) Monograph

Scientific Classification

Thyme, or Thymus serpyllum, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Lamiales, order Lamiales, family Lamiaceae, genus Thymus, and species Thymus serpyllum. This classification reflects its position within the plant kingdom and its relationships to other aromatic herbs.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Thyme is Thymus serpyllum, first described by Carl von Linne in 1753. It has several synonyms, including Thymus vulgaris and Thymus mastichina, though T. serpyllum is the accepted modern name. Common names vary, with "true thyme" and "wild thyme" being widely recognized. Regional and historical names reflect its long use in herbal medicine across cultures.

Morphological Characteristics

Thyme (Thymus serpyllum) is a low-growing perennial herb with a compact, mat-like growth habit. It has a fibrous root system that anchors it firmly in the soil. The stems are square and branching, often forming dense clusters. Leaves are small, oval, and aromatic, while the inflorescence consists of clusters of tiny flowers. The flowers are typically pink or lavender, and the fruit is a small, dry capsule containing several seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Thyme (Thymus serpyllum) can be distinguished by its small, oval leaves with a slightly serrated edge, and its pink to purple flowers that grow in dense clusters. It has a strong, aromatic scent and is typically found in dry, rocky soils. It is often confused with other thymes, but its leaf shape and flower arrangement are key differentiating features.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Thyme (Thymus serpyllum) is native to southern Europe, North Africa, and parts of western Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with full sun. It prefers well-drained, sandy or rocky soils. It is commonly found at low to moderate altitudes, typically between 0 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Thyme (Thymus serpyllum) typically blooms from late spring to early summer, producing small pink or white flowers. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early autumn. For identification purposes, harvesting is best done just before flowering, when the leaves are most aromatic and the plant is most identifiable.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Thymus serpyllum belongs to the genus Thymus, which is part of the family Lamiaceae. Its closest relatives include other thyme species like Thymus vulgaris. The genus Thymus is closely related to other Lamiaceae members such as Salvia and Mentha, sharing similar morphological and chemical traits.
