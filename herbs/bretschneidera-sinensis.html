Bretschneidera sinensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bretschneidera sinensis has large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach up to 1.5 meters in length and consist of several pairs of oblong leaflets with entire margins. The tree typically grows to a height of 15 to 25 meters, characterized by a straight trunk and a spreading crown. Its bark is relatively smooth and greyish in color, providing a stark contrast to the lush green foliage. The reproductive structures include small, inconspicuous flowers that are borne in clusters. Once pollination occurs, the plant develops woody, globose fruits that contain a single large seed.
