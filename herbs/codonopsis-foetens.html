Codonopsis foetens

What's the taxonomical classification of Codonopsis foetens?

Codonopsis foetens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. This plant is specifically nested within the family Campanulaceae, which is the taxonomic group to which its genus, Codonopsis, is assigned. Ultimately, its unique biological identity is defined by its specific designation as the species foetens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Codonopsis foetens has long, lanceolate leaves that emerge from a creeping rhizome and produce terminal inflorescences of small, fragrant, white or pale lilac flowers.

The foliage is characterized by a smooth texture and a deep green color, often displaying subtle longitudinal veining. Each flower consists of six tepals and possesses a delicate, tubular structure designed to attract specific pollinators. The plant's growth habit is primarily spreading, as the underground rhizomes allow it to colonize shaded forest floors effectively.

Small, inconspicuous bracts often surround the base of the individual flower stalks within the cluster.
