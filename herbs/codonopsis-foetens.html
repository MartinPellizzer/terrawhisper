Codonopsis foetens

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Codonopsis foetens has long, lanceolate leaves that emerge from a creeping rhizome and produce terminal inflorescences of small, fragrant, white or pale lilac flowers. The foliage is characterized by a smooth texture and a deep green color, often displaying subtle longitudinal veining. Each flower consists of six tepals and possesses a delicate, tubular structure designed to attract specific pollinators. The plant's growth habit is primarily spreading, as the underground rhizomes allow it to colonize shaded forest floors effectively. Small, inconspicuous bracts often surround the base of the individual flower stalks within the cluster.
