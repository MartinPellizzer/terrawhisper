Alchemilla japonica

What's the taxonomical classification of Alchemilla japonica?

Alchemilla japonica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, the plant is placed in the order Rosales and the family Rosaceae. Ultimately, it is defined by its genus, Alchemilla, and its specific species designation, japonica.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Alchemiella japonica contains medicinal properties primarily located within its aerial parts, specifically the leaves and stems. These harvested components are often dried and prepared as decoctions or infusions to extract active compounds. The leaves are valued for their potential astringent and anti-inflammatory effects in traditional herbal practices. Phytochemical studies suggest that the presence of tannins and flavonoids in these tissues contributes to its therapeutic utility. Consequently, practitioners focus on the entire above-ground portion of the plant to ensure a concentrated dose of these bioactive substances.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse array of bioactive secondary metabolites including flavonoids, phenolic acids, and triterpenoids that contribute to its medicinal properties.

The presence of various flavonoids provides significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities within the organism. Phenolic acids serve as essential compounds that help mitigate oxidative stress and protect cells from damage. Furthermore, the triterpenoids found in the plant are known for their potential antimicrobial and cytotoxic effects.

Together, these chemical constituents form the basis for the traditional and pharmacological applications of the species.

The primary medicinal compounds of this plant are shown in the list below.

  • Flavonoids
  • Phenolic acids
  • Triterpenoids

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Alchemilla japonica targets several key enzymes and inflammatory signaling proteins, most notably cyclooxygenase (COX) enzymes, lipoxygenase (LOX), and various pro-inflammatory cytokines. Its rich concentration of hydrolyzable tannins and flavonoids allows these compounds to inhibit the arachidonic acid metabolism pathway, effectively reducing the production of prostaglandins and leukotrienes. By modulating the activity of tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-alpha) and interleukin-6 (IL-6), the plant exerts potent anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory effects. Additionally, certain polyphenolic constituents interact with oxidative stress-related proteins to scavenge free radicals and protect cellular integrity. These multi-target interactions contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating skin inflammation and gastrointestinal disorders.

What are the therapeutic actions of Alchemilla japonica?

Alchemiella japonica has astringent, diuretic, and anti-inflammatory properties that primarily function by tightening body tissues and promoting fluid excretion through the kidneys. The plant contains high concentrations of tannins which help to reduce swelling and constrict mucous membranes in various bodily systems. These bioactive compounds also aid in treating gastrointestinal issues by decreasing excessive secretions and soothing inflamed linings. When applied topically, its extracts can assist in the healing of minor skin irritations and wounds. Additionally, its mild diuretic effect supports detoxification by encouraging the elimination of metabolic waste via urine.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various gastrointestinal ailments such as diarrhea, dysentery, and stomach pains, as well as urinary tract infections and inflammatory conditions. Its high concentration of tannins provides potent astringent properties that help tighten tissues and reduce fluid loss. These medicinal qualities make it effective for managing excessive bleeding or bleeding gums. Additionally, its anti-inflammatory effects can assist in soothing minor skin irritations or internal swellings. Traditional practitioners often rely on its ability to calm digestive distress through these chemical compounds.

What are the herbal preparations of Alchemilla japonica?

Alchemilla japonica is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a concentrated tincture to extract its active phytochemical components. The dried aerial parts of the plant are often boiled in water to create a potent decoction used for internal therapeutic purposes. Alternatively, steeping the herb in hot water for a shorter period produces a milder infusion suitable for daily consumption. For more concentrated medicinal applications, the plant material is soaked in alcohol to create a tincture that provides a long shelf life. These various forms allow practitioners to tailor the dosage and strength based on the specific health objective being addressed.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Alchemilla japonica can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or gastrointestinal distress if ingested or applied incorrectly. While the plant is often used in traditional practices, individual sensitivity to its chemical compounds remains a primary concern for users. Contact with the foliage may lead to localized redness or itching for those with sensitive skin. There is limited clinical data regarding the long-term toxicity of this species in humans. Therefore, caution is advised when using any part of the plant for topical or internal purposes.
