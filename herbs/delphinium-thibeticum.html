What's the taxonomical classification of Delphinium thibeticum?

Delphinium thiibetcum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its hierarchical lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Ranunculales. As a member of the family Ranunculaceae, this plant is further categorized into the genus Delphinium, with the specific identification falling under the species thibeticum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Delphinium thibeticum has erect, perennial stems that typically reach heights between 0.5 and 1.5 meters and are covered in fine, pubescent hairs. The leaves are deeply palmately lobed or divided into several narrow segments, often appearing in basal clusters. During the flowering season, the plant produces dense, terminal racemes filled with zygomorphic flowers that exhibit a characteristic spurred shape. These blossoms usually display shades of blue, violet, or pale lavender, depending on the specific variety or environmental conditions. The overall structure is robust and upright, supported by a well-developed root system that anchors the plant in its native habitat.
