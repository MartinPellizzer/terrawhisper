Smilax ocreata

What's the taxonomical classification of Smilax ocreata?

Smilax ocreata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Liliales. This plant is a member of the family Smilacaceae, falling into the genus Smilax, and is specifically identified by the species name ocreata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Smilax ocreata has woody, climbing stems characterized by sharp, recurved prickles and leaves that feature prominent stipular sheaths at the base of each petiole. These leaves are typically alternate and can range from ovate to lanceolate in shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in axillary umbels that eventually develop into fleshy, dark red or black berries. Its climbing habit is supported by these prickles which allow the vines to scramble through dense vegetation. The overall structure of the plant is robust and adapted for life in shaded forest understories.
