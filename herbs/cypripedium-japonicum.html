What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cypripedium japonicum has a perennial life cycle characterized by basal leaves that are elliptical to lanceolate in shape and a single, terminal, showy flower held atop a slender stem. The most distinctive morphological feature is the pouch-like labellum, which is typically creamy white with reddish-brown or purple markings. Surrounding this pouch are two large, spreading lateral petals that often feature intricate veining and delicate coloring. The sepals are similarly elongated and provide a structural backdrop to the central reproductive organs. This orchid species relies on these complex floral structures to attract specific pollinators through visual cues and scent.
