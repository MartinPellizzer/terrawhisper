What's the taxonomical classification of Polystichum shensiense?

Polystichum shensiense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Polypodiales and the family Polypodiaceae, eventually reaching its specific designation within the genus Polystichum. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its unique species name, shensiense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polystichum shensiense has pinnate fronds that typically measure between 30 and 50 centimeters in length with individual pinnae that are lanceolate in shape and possess serrated margins. The texture of the foliage is distinctly leathery, providing a structural rigidity to each leaf blade. Small, brownish scales cover the rhizome and the stipes, offering a protective layer for the plant's base. The sori are arranged in two rows on the underside of the pinnules, tucked neatly beneath the protective flaps of the indusia. These morphological characteristics allow the fern to thrive within its specific montane habitat.
