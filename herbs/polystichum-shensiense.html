Polystichum shensiense

What's the taxonomical classification of Polystichum shensiense?

Polystichum shensiense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Polypodiales and the family Polypodiaceae, eventually reaching its specific designation within the genus Polystichum. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its unique species name, shensiense.

Polystichum shensiense is prepared primarily as a decoction through boiling the dried rhizomes or fronds in water to extract its bioactive components. Some preparations may also involve infusing the plant material in alcohol to create a tincture for long-term storage. What side-effects this plant can have?

Polystichum shensiense can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested or handled without protection. Some individuals may experience redness or itching upon direct contact with the plant's foliage or sap. If the plant is consumed, it might lead to nausea, stomach cramps, or vomiting in sensitive subjects. These adverse reactions are often linked to specific chemical compounds found within the plant's tissues. It is recommended to use gloves when working with this species to avoid unexpected physical sensitivities.
