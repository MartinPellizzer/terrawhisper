Merremia boisiana

What's the taxonomical classification of Merremia boisiana?

Merremia boisiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is situated under the order Solanales. It is further categorized into the family Convolvulaceae, falling under the genus Merremia, and is specifically identified by its species name, boisiana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Merremia boisiana has slender, twining stems that produce small, ovate to elliptical leaves with entire margins and acute tips.

The foliage is typically arranged in an alternate pattern along the climbing vines. Small, fragrant flowers emerge from the axils, often featuring delicate petals that range in color from white to pale yellow. The reproductive structures include a simple ovary that develops into a small, fleshy fruit upon maturation.

These morphological traits characterize its growth habit as a creeping or climbing herbaceous perennial.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Merremia boisiana can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and potential gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Contact with the plant's sap or foliage may trigger localized redness, itching, or swelling on sensitive areas of the skin. Some individuals may experience more severe hypersensitivity reactions depending on their personal chemical sensitivities. If accidental consumption occurs, it could lead to nausea, stomach cramps, or vomiting. Monitoring for these symptoms is essential when handling or being in close proximity to the species.
