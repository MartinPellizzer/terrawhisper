Maesa perlarius

What's the taxonomical classification of Maesa perlarius?

Maesa perlarius belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed under the order Ericales. This plant is further categorized into the family Primulaceae, falling under the genus Maesa, and is specifically identified by its species name, perlarius.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Maesa perlarius has elongated, lanceolate leaves with serrated margins and a deep emerald pigmentation. Small, bell-shaped flowers emerge from the axillary nodes in clusters of three to five. These blossoms feature a pale cream coloration and a delicate, waxy surface. The root system is extensive and tuberous, allowing the plant to anchor firmly in nutrient-poor soil.
