Engelhardia roxburghiana

What's the taxonomical classification of Engelhardia roxburghiana?

Engelhardia roxburghiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae, which places it in the order Fagales. The plant is a member of the family Juglandaceae and is categorized under the genus Engelhardia, eventually reaching its specific identification as the species roxburghiana.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds engeletin, isoengeletin, kaempferol, naringenin, and quercitrin. These specific phytochemicals are primarily classified as flavonoids found within the species. The presence of these substances contributes to the diverse biological activities associated with the plant's medicinal uses. Research often focuses on how these compounds interact with cellular processes to provide therapeutic benefits. Understanding the concentration of these molecules helps scientists evaluate the plant's overall pharmacological potential.

According to a study published by "Chemistry & biodiversity", chemical analysis of the total flavonoids of Engelhardia roxburgiana leaves (TFER) revealed 10 components. Out of these, 7 acted as active ingredients, which included naringenin, kaempferol, quercetin, isoengeletin, engeletin, astilbin, and quercitrin. These components were screened using oral bioavailability (OB) and drug-likeness (DL) indices to clarify their active roles. The study found that TFER exhibited regulatory effects on blood glucose and lipids, making the leaves widely used for diabetes mellitus and hyperlipidemia. Furthermore, the treatment down-regulated NF-κB signaling by decreasing the mRNA level of the NF-κB p65 subunit, as well as TNF-α, VCAM-1, and IL-1β expressions in the thoracic aorta.
