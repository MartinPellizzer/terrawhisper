Engelhardia roxburghiana

What's the taxonomical classification of Engelhardia roxburghiana?

Engelhardia roxburghiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae, which places it in the order Fagales. The plant is a member of the family Juglandaceae and is categorized under the genus Engelhardia, eventually reaching its specific identification as the species roxburghiana.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Engelhardia roxburghiana targets cyclooxygenase-2, lipoxygenase, and various pro-inflammatory cytokines such as tumor necrosis factor-alpha to exert its therapeutic effects. The plant contains bioactive compounds like triterpenoids and flavonoids that inhibit these specific enzymatic pathways to reduce inflammation. These interactions modulate the arachidonic acid metabolism, which prevents the synthesis of prostaglandins and leukotrienes. Furthermore, the phytochemicals influence signaling proteins involved in oxidative stress pathways to protect cellular structures. Such molecular interactions explain the plant's traditional use in treating inflammatory conditions and pain.

What are the therapeutic actions of Engelhardia roxburghiana?

Engelhardia roxburghiana has medicinal properties primarily utilized for its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and analgesic effects in traditional herbal practices. What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin diseases, rheumatism, dysentery, and various types of abdominal pains. The bark and leaves are often prepared into decoctions or pastes to soothe inflammatory conditions. It is frequently applied topically to address wounds or skin irritations found in traditional medicinal practices. Additionally, the plant's properties are utilized to alleviate digestive distress and internal discomfort. These applications demonstrate its importance in managing both topical and systemic ailments.

What are the herbal preparations of Engelhardia roxburghiana?

Engelhardia roxburghiana is prepared as a decoction, a powder, or a topical paste to treat various ailments. The bark is frequently boiled in water to create a medicinal tea used for treating digestive issues. When processed into a fine powder, the plant material is often mixed with other substances to facilitate ingestion. Additionally, the crushed parts of the plant can be applied directly to the skin as a paste for external healing. These diverse methods allow for both internal and external therapeutic applications depending on the specific condition being addressed.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Engelhardia roxburghiana can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or gastrointestinal distress if handled improperly or ingested without medicinal guidance. While the plant is used in traditional medicine, its chemical constituents have not been fully evaluated for human toxicity in clinical settings. Contact with the bark or leaves may lead to localized redness or itching in sensitive individuals. Ingesting preparations made from this species could potentially trigger nausea or stomach cramping. Because specific toxicological profiles are limited, caution is necessary when using any part of the plant.
