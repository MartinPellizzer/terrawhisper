Parsnip (Pastinaca sativa)

Parsnip (Pastinaca sativa) is a member of the Apiaceae family, native to Europe, Western Asia, and North Africa. Traditionally, its roots, leaves, and stems have been used for decoctions, infusions, and culinary uses.

This herb is particularly valued for its diuretic, anti-inflammatory, and bitter actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and japanese kampo medicine.

Flowers

Stems
Cylindrical, hollow, ridged, and tuberous with a woody core, the stems are perennial, herbaceous, and exhibit a branching pattern of opposite and alternate leaves.
