Pastinaca Sativa: Medicinal Uses And Benefits

Pastinaca sativa, commonly known as wild parsnip, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, belonging to the carrot family (Apiaceae).

It is valued for its medicinal properties and contains bioactive constituents such as sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and coumarins.

The herb is known for its health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, and is used therapeutically to support skin health and treat conditions like eczema.

Its therapeutic actions also include detoxification support and immune system stimulation.
