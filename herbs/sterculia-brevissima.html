Sterculia brevissima

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sterculia brevissima has a woody, spreading canopy characterized by trifoliate leaves with large, leathery leaflets and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters. The tree typically features a trunk with grayish, fissured bark that provides structural support for its wide-reaching branches. Each leaf consists of three distinct leaflets that are often elliptical or ovate in shape with smooth margins. Its reproductive structures produce woody follicles that eventually split open to reveal bright seeds. These seeds are often encased in a fleshy aril which serves to attract dispersers in its natural habitat.
