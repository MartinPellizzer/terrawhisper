Hypericum hookerianum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hypericum hookerianum has opposite, elliptical to lanceolate leaves that are typically 3 to 7 centimeters long with entire margins and prominent punctate glands. The stems are woody at the base and often display a branching habit that supports terminal clusters of flowers. Its bright yellow blossoms feature five distinct petals and numerous long, protruding stamens that create a fuzzy appearance. Small, black glandular dots are often visible on the leaf surfaces and petals under magnification. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruit that eventually dehisce to release tiny seeds.
