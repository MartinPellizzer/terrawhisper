What's the taxonomical classification of Leontopodium longifolium?

Leontopodium longifolium belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Asterales. This plant is a specific representative of the family Asteraceae, characterized by its distinctive floral structures. Within this family, it is categorized under the genus Leontopodium, and its specific identity is defined by the species epithet longifolium.

        Leontopodium longifolium is prepared as a decoction, a concentrated tincture, or a topical poultice depending on the intended therapeutic application.         What side-effects this plant can have?

        Leontopodium longifoliuum can cause adverse reactions including skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential allergic responses in sensitive individuals. Some people may experience localized swelling or redness if the foliage touches their skin. It is important to monitor for more severe systemic reactions such as nausea or dizziness. Consulting a medical professional is necessary if any unexpected symptoms occur after exposure.
