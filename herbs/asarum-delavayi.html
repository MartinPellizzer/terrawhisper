What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Asarum delavayi has kidney-shaped, leathery leaves that typically measure between five and fifteen centimeters in length and possess entire or slightly wavy margins. Its stems are creeping or rhizomatous, allowing the plant to spread across the forest floor as a groundcover. The reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the base of the plant near the soil level.
