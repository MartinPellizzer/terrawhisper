Fritillaria Lily (Fritillaria Skorpili)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Fritillaria skorpili.

Fritillaria lily, scientifically known as Fritillaria skorpili, is a rare medicinal herb native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia, valued for its unique therapeutic properties. This plant is traditionally used as an adaptogen to support respiratory health, reduce inflammation, and enhance immune function, with its bulb containing bioactive compounds like alkaloids and saponins. In ancient Persian and Arabic medicine, it was revered for treating coughs, bronchitis, and respiratory infections, and it remains a key ingredient in traditional remedies for clearing mucus and easing breathing. Modern wellness applications include its use in herbal supplements for respiratory support and as a natural anti-inflammatory agent, with some studies suggesting potential benefits for chronic lung conditions. Notably, Fritillaria skorpili is distinguished by its distinctive bitter taste and the presence of unique alkaloids, making it a rare and historically significant plant in both traditional and contemporary herbal medicine.
