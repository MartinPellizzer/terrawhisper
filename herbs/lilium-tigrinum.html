Lilium tigrinum

What's the taxonomical classification of Lilium tigrinum?

Lilium tigrinum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the order Liliales and belongs to the family Liliaceae. Finally, it is defined by the genus Lilium and is identified by its specific species name, tigrinum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lilium tigrinum has upright, slender stems that reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters and are topped by several nodding, trumpet-shaped flowers with bright orange petals marked by dark spots. The leaves are lanceolate in shape, arranged in whorls or spirals along the stem, and possess a smooth, waxy texture. Each flower features six prominent tepals that curve outward to reveal a central cluster of long, protruding stamens. The base of the flower often transitions into a deeper reddish-orange hue, creating a striking contrast against the speckled petal surfaces. These morphological traits collectively distinguish this species within the genus Lilium.
