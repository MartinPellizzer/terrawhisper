What's the taxonomical classification of Mallotus tiliifolius?

Mallotus tiliifolius belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its hierarchical progression, the plant is placed in the order Malpighiales and the family Euphorbiaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its membership in the genus Mallotus, with its unique designation as the species tiliifolius.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Mallotus tiliifolius has simple, alternate, ovate to broadly elliptic leaves that measure approximately 5 to 15 centimeters in length and feature serrated margins and an acute apex. The leaf surface is often characterized by a somewhat scabrous or rough texture due to the presence of small hairs. Its stems are woody and can develop into medium-sized trees or shrubs depending on the environment. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in axillary racemes. The fruit is a small, globose capsule that splits open to reveal seeds often covered in a fleshy aril.
