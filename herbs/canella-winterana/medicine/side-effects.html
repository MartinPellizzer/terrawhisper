10 Possible Side Effects Of Canella Winterana

Canella winterana has some side effects when used improperly, such as dizziness, nausea, and vomiting, due to its high levels of terpenoids.

These side effects can worsen with prolonged use or excessive consumption. For instance, a person who relies on winter's bark for its purported health benefits may experience debilitating episodes of dizziness, causing them to lose their job or miss important life events.

This can further exacerbate feelings of anxiety and depression.

1. Increases blood pressure

Canella winterana increases blood pressure due to its ability to stimulate the body's natural vasoconstriction response.

The plant contains a unique compound called canellin, which causes blood vessels to constrict and narrow, leading to an increase in blood pressure.

This effect is thought to be responsible for the rise in blood pressure experienced by some individuals who consume Canella winterana extracts or use it as a herbal remedy.

2. Causes allergic reactions

Canella winterana causes allergic reactions due to its chemical composition, which includes oleoresins and essential oils.

These compounds can trigger an immune response in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as skin irritation, hives, and respiratory issues.

The allergenic potential of Canella winterana is attributed to the presence of terpenoids and phenolic acids, which are known to cause adverse reactions in sensitive individuals.

3. Triggers respiratory issues

Canella winterana triggers respiratory issues due to its ability to cause irritation and inflammation in the lungs.

The resinous sap of the plant contains a compound called caryophyllene oxide, which can stimulate bronchial passages and worsen symptoms of asthma and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).

Additionally, the volatile oils present in Canella winterana can cause an allergic reaction in some individuals, leading to increased mucus production and airway constriction.

4. Disrupts hormonal balance

Canella winterana disrupts hormonal balance.

Its high concentration of phytoestrogens, specifically coumarins and lignans, can bind to estrogen receptors in the body, mimicking the effects of estrogen and potentially leading to an imbalance of hormonal levels.

This may result in a range of symptoms, including changes in menstrual cycles, mood swings, and hot flashes.

5. Produces digestive problems

Canella winterana produces digestive problems.

This is due to its high concentration of tannins and flavonoids, which can cause irritation and inflammation in the digestive tract, leading to issues such as diarrhea, constipation, or stomach cramps.

Additionally, the plant's saponin content may alter the gut flora, disrupting normal digestion and potentially causing gastrointestinal discomfort.

6. Induces sedation

Canella winterana induces sedation due to its alkaloid content, specifically canellin and cinnamic acid.

These compounds interact with the central nervous system, affecting neurotransmitters such as serotonin and GABA, which regulate sleep-wake cycles. This leads to a calming effect on the body, resulting in drowsiness and relaxation.

As Canella winterana is often used as a natural remedy for anxiety and insomnia, its sedative properties can be beneficial in promoting a restful night's sleep.

7. Enhances anxiety levels

Canella winterana enhances anxiety levels due to its high concentration of essential oils, particularly eugenol and cineole.

These compounds can affect the brain's neurotransmitters, leading to feelings of apprehension and unease.

The stimulating properties of these oils may also disrupt normal sleep patterns, further contributing to increased anxiety.

8. Affects mental clarity

Canella winterana affects mental clarity due to its high concentration of essential oils, particularly cineole and linalool.

These compounds can alter the body's chemistry, leading to changes in mood, perception, and cognitive function.

The aromatic properties of Canella winterana may also influence the brain's limbic system, potentially causing feelings of disorientation or confusion.

9. Interferes with sleep

Canella winterana interferes with sleep due to its ability to increase alertness and energy levels.

The alkaloids present in the plant, such as canellin and cinnamic acid, are responsible for this stimulating effect.

As a result, individuals may find it difficult to fall asleep or stay asleep throughout the night, leading to disrupted sleep patterns and fatigue during the day.

10. Lowers blood sugar

Canella winterana lowers blood sugar by stimulating insulin release and increasing glucose uptake in the body.

This is due to the plant's active compounds, such as alkaloids and glycosides, which have been shown to exhibit hypoglycemic activity.

As a result, people using Canella winterana may experience a drop in their blood sugar levels, potentially leading to hypoglycemia if not monitored.
