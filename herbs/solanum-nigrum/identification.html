Botanical Identification of Black Nightshade (Solanum nigrum)

To identify Black Nightshade (Solanum nigrum) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Black Nightshade (Solanum nigrum) Monograph

Scientific Classification

Black Nightshade, Solanum nigrum, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Spermatophyta, class Caryophyllopsida, order Solanales, family Solanaceae, genus Solanum, and species Solanum nigrum. These classifications reflect its evolutionary relationships and botanical characteristics within the plant kingdom.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The botanical name for Black Nightshade is Solanum nigrum, first described by Linnaeus in 1753. It is also known by various synonyms, including Solanum melongena and Solanum nigrum L., and is referred to by common names such as Black Nightshade, Bitter Nightshade, and Wild Tomato. Regional and historical names vary, reflecting its widespread use in traditional herbal medicine.

Morphological Characteristics

Black Nightshade is an annual herb with a fibrous root system. It has upright, branched stems that are hairy and often reddish near the base. The leaves are alternate, ovate to heart-shaped, with a serrated margin and a rough texture. The inflorescence is a raceme of small, greenish-white flowers. The fruit is a globose berry that turns from green to black when mature, containing numerous small seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Black Nightshade can be distinguished by its dark purple berries with white warts, spiny calyx that remains attached, and alternate, oval-shaped leaves with slightly serrated edges. It differs from similar species like Bitter Nightshade by its smaller berries and from Common Nightshade by its more compact growth habit and distinct calyx structure.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Black Nightshade is native to Europe, Asia, and Africa. It thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils. It is commonly found at low to mid-altitude regions, often in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Black Nightshade blooms from late spring to early summer, typically between May and July. Seed formation occurs from late summer to early fall, around August to September. For identification purposes, the plant is most easily recognized during its flowering and fruiting stages, which are key for accurate botanical identification.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Black Nightshade (Solanum nigrum) belongs to the genus Solanum in the nightshade family, Solanaceae. It is closely related to species like Common Nightshade (Solanum dulcamara) and Eggplant (Solanum melongena). These species share similar morphological and genetic traits, including berry fruits and alkaloid production.
