Black Nightshade (Solanum nigrum) Compounds

The primary compounds of Black Nightshade include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and tannins, known for their astringent effects. Phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory qualities, while alkaloids may influence nerve function. Saponins and glycosides are also present, offering potential antimicrobial and digestive benefits.

Black Nightshade (Solanum nigrum) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Black Nightshade (Solanum nigrum) contribute to its medicinal properties by exhibiting antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. These compounds help neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress, supporting the plant's traditional use in treating ailments like inflammation and skin conditions.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Black Nightshade (Solanum nigrum). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and has been traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Tannins may also support wound healing and digestive health when used appropriately.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Black Nightshade (Solanum nigrum). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress in the body. This compound is often studied for its potential role in supporting immune function and inflammatory conditions.

4. Alkaloid

Alkaloids in Black Nightshade (Solanum nigrum) are bioactive compounds with potential medicinal properties. These alkaloids may contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating inflammation and pain. However, they can also be toxic in high doses, necessitating careful handling and preparation. Their presence underscores the dual nature of this plant as both a medicinal and potentially harmful substance.

5. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Black Nightshade (Solanum nigrum). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Saponin is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits. Its presence supports the plant's role in various therapeutic applications.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Black Nightshade (Solanum nigrum). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, though its specific effects require further research. Glycosides in Solanum nigrum may have potential therapeutic applications, but they are also associated with toxicity, necessitating careful use.

7. Coumarin

Coumarin is an active compound found in Black Nightshade (Solanum nigrum). It contributes to the plant's potential medicinal properties, including antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory effects. However, coumarin can be toxic in high doses and may interact with certain medications. Its presence highlights the dual nature of the plant's bioactive components.

8. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Black Nightshade (Solanum nigrum) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, supporting the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and skin conditions. They also play a role in the plant's defense mechanisms against pests and pathogens.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin, found in Black Nightshade (Solanum nigrum), is a pigment with antioxidant properties. It contributes to the plant's dark color and may support cellular health. Its presence in the plant highlights its potential therapeutic value in herbal medicine.

10. Essential oil

The essential oil of Black Nightshade (Solanum nigrum) contains volatile compounds like limonene and cineole. These oils are used topically for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties. However, they should be used with caution due to the plant's overall toxicity.
