Strobilanthes japonica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Strobilanthes japonica has an erect, herbaceous habit characterized by opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with entire margins and a distinctly pubescent surface. The stem is typically quadrangular and can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters in well-developed specimens. Its inflorescence consists of dense, terminal clusters of tubular, bilabiate flowers that often exhibit a vibrant violet or bluish hue. The calyx is persistent and frequently possesses fine hairs, providing a structural base for the delicate corollas. These morphological features allow the plant to thrive in the moist, shaded environments of its native habitat.

Within these habitats, the species thrives in areas with well-drained soil and moderate humidity. Its range is largely determined by the availability of consistent moisture and specific temperature gradients found in these mountainous or coastal zones.
