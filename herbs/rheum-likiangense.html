Rheum likiangense

What's the taxonomical classification of Rheum likiangense?

Rheum likiangense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Caryophyllales. The plant is further categorized into the family Polygonaceae, within the genus Rheum, and is specifically identified by its species name, likiangense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rheum likiangense has a perennial growth habit characterized by large, basal, cordate to ovate leaves that feature prominent longitudinal veins and wavy or dentate margins. The leaf blades are typically thick and fleshy, often displaying a rugose texture on their upper surfaces. These leaves are supported by long, sturdy petioles that emerge directly from a thickened rhizome. During its flowering stage, the plant produces upright, branched inflorescences that bear small, inconspicuous flowers. The overall structure allows the plant to thrive in moist, high-altitude environments where its robust foliage can capture significant sunlight.
