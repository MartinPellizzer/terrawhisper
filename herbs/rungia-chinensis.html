What's the taxonomical classification of Rungia chinensis?

Rungia chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, the plant falls under the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Acanthaceae. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Rungia and the species chinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rungia chinensis has perennial, creeping rhizomes and lanceolate to ovate leaves that are dark green, leathery, and possess finely serrated margins. The foliage typically grows in dense, low-lying clumps with a somewhat glossy texture. During its flowering period, the plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually purple or violet in color. These blossoms emerge from the leaf axils and can create a colorful carpet when in full bloom. The overall structure remains compact and ground-covering, making it suitable for shaded garden environments.
