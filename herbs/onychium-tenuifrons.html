What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Onychium tenuifrons has narrow, linear leaves that are typically only a few millimeters wide and arranged in dense, overlapping clusters. These hairs provide a silvery or grayish appearance to the foliage, which helps the plant adapt to arid environments. The leaf margins are often slightly revolute, meaning they curl downward toward the underside. During its flowering stage, it produces small, inconspicuous blooms that emerge from the center of the leaf rosettes.
