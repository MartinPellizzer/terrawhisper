Helwingia chinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Helwingia chinensis?

Helwingia chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and falls into the order Aquifoliales. The plant is a member of the family Helwingiaceae and is situated within the genus Helwingia, with its specific designation being the species chinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Helwingia chinensis has opposite, simple, ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated margins and a distinctive tendency to grow in a single plane along the stem. The foliage typically measures between 3 and 8 centimeters in length and features a glossy, dark green upper surface. Its woody stems are slender and can develop a somewhat spreading or climbing habit as the plant matures. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils and eventually develop into small, fleshy, red or black drupes. These berries are the most recognizable fruit characteristic of the species during the fruiting season.

To discriminate between the species, plant metabolomics were performed in 48 batches of samples. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds alpha-amyrin, cinnamic acid, friedelin, kult-5-en-3beta-ol, and luteolin-7-O-beta-D-glucoside. These diverse phytochemicals contribute to the plant's complex biological profile and potential therapeutic applications. Specifically, the presence of triterpenoids like alpha-amyrin and friedelin often suggests anti-inflammatory properties. Cinnamic acid serves as a key building block for various metabolic processes within the organism. Furthermore, the flavonoid luteolin-7-O-beta-D-glucoside provides antioxidant benefits that support cellular health. Together, these molecules define the chemical essence of the plant's medicinal value.

As discussed in a study, six compounds were isolated and identified from Helwingia chinensis. These medicinal compounds include cinnamic acid (1), gulit-5-en-3beta-ol (2), friedelin (3), alpha-amyrin (4), luteolin-7-O-beta-D-glucoside (5), and 4, 5-dimethoxy-1, 2-benzoquinone (6). All 6 of these compounds were obtained from H. chinensis for the first time. The isolation process utilized silica gel, Sephadex LH-20, and polyamide chromatography to identify the constituents.
