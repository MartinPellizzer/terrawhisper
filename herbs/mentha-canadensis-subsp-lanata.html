The therapeutic actions of woolly mint include its antispasmodic, anti-inflammatory, and expectorant properties, making it useful for treating various health conditions.

Possible Side Effects of Mentha canadensis subsp. lanata

Mentha canadensis subsp.

lanata can have side effects if used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, allergic reactions, and skin irritation.

Ingesting large amounts of the plant can lead to stomach upset, nausea, and diarrhea.

Topical application of the plant's essential oil can cause skin redness, itching, and rashes in some individuals.

Additionally, pregnant women and breastfeeding mothers should exercise caution when using products containing Mentha canadensis subsp.

lanata, as it may stimulate the uterus or affect milk production.
