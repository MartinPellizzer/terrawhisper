Corydalis mucronifera

What's the taxonomical classification of Corydalis mucronifera?

Corydalis mucronifera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Ranunculales and is a member of the family Papaveraceae. Finally, it is identified by its genus, Corydalis, and its specific species name, mucronifera.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Corydalis mucronifera has deeply lobed, bluish-green leaves that feature distinctively pointed or mucronate tips on the individual segments. The plant produces zygomorphic flowers that exhibit a characteristic spurred structure common to the Papaveraceae family. These delicate blooms typically display a pale coloration with subtle markings that guide pollinators toward the nectar. The stem is relatively slender and supports the foliage in a spreading or upright growth habit. Small, elongated seed pods develop following successful pollination to facilitate the next generation.
