Pausinystalia johimbe

What's the taxonomical classification of Pausinystalia johimbe?

Pausinystalia johimbe belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant falls under the order Gentianales and is a member of the family Rubiaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Pausinystalia, and its unique species designation, johimbe.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Pausinystalia johimbe targets alpha-2 adrenergic receptors and centrally located imidazoline receptors to produce its physiological effects. The primary active alkaloid, yohimbine, acts as a potent and selective antagonist at these alpha-2 receptors, preventing them from inhibiting the release of norepinephrine. This antagonism leads to increased sympathetic nervous system activity and higher levels of circulating catecholamines. Additionally, the plant interacts with certain serotonin receptors and can influence nitric oxide pathways to impact vascular tone. These complex interactions with various neuroreceptors and enzymes underlie its traditional use for improving sexual function and blood flow.

Pausinystalia johimbe has medicinal properties that include acting as a stimulant, an aphrodisiac, a vasodilator, an antihypertensive, and an analgesic. Its ability to function as an antihypertensive agent assists in managing blood pressure levels through these vascular effects. Additionally, the plant provides analgesic relief to mitigate pain while simultaneously stimulating sexual desire.
