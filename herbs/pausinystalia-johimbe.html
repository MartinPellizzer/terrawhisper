What's the taxonomical classification of Pausinystalia johimbe?

Pausinystalia johimbe belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant falls under the order Gentianales and is a member of the family Rubiaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Pausinystalia, and its unique species designation, johimbe.

What traditional systems uses this plant?

This plant, known commonly as Yohimbe, was historically utilized by West African ethnic groups such as the Baule and Ashanti for its potent stimulant properties and as a traditional remedy for sexual dysfunction.

Local healers prepared decoctions from the bark to treat various ailments including pain, fever, and digestive issues. Beyond physical health, the bark was often integrated into ritualistic practices and used to enhance vigor during social gatherings. These traditional applications relied on the alkaloid yohimbine found within the tree to influence the central nervous system.

This long history of ethnobotanical use eventually led to the plant's introduction into Western medicine for similar physiological purposes.
