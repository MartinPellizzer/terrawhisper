Pausinystalia johimbe

What's the taxonomical classification of Pausinystalia johimbe?

Pausinystalia johimbe belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant falls under the order Gentianales and is a member of the family Rubiaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Pausinystalia, and its unique species designation, johimbe.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Pausinystalia johimbe contains the alkaloid yohimbine, which acts as a potent alpha-2 adrenergic receptor antagonist. This primary compound is primarily utilized for its ability to increase blood flow and stimulate the central nervous system. In pharmaceutical applications, the extracts are often studied for their potential to treat erectile dysfunction and assist with weight loss. The plant also possesses other minor alkaloids that contribute to its complex chemical profile. These substances work together to influence various physiological processes within the human body.

What are the therapeutic actions of Pausinystalia johimbe?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat erectile dysfunction, hypertension, and certain neurological conditions. The active alkaloids within the bark act as alpha-adrenergic receptor antagonists to stimulate blood flow. These chemical properties specifically target the vascular system to improve circulation during sexual arousal. Historically, clinicians also utilized extracts from this species to manage various forms of circulatory insufficiency. Modern pharmacological research continues to study its potential effects on blood pressure regulation and mental alertness.
