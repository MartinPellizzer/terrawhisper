Fordia cauliflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Fordia cauliflora?

Fordia cauliflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, the plant is situated in the order Fabales and the family Fabaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by its placement within the genus Fordia, resulting in its unique species name, Fordia cauliflora.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Fordia cauliflora has a woody, perennial shrub habit characterized by erect stems, lanceolate to elliptical leaves, and the distinctive ability to produce flowers directly from its bark. The leaves are typically arranged alternately along the branches and possess entire margins with acute tips. Its most striking morphological feature is cauliflory, where floral buds emerge from the older woody tissue of the main stems rather than just the terminal shoots. The flowers are tubular in shape and often present in dense clusters or racemes that erupt from the trunk. This unique reproductive structure allows the plant to utilize its entire stem surface for flowering.

What are the pharmacological activities of Fordia cauliflora?

This plant has pharmacological activities including the treatment of bruises, the treatment of dementia in children, and the treatment of rheumatism. Its medicinal properties are utilized in various traditional practices to alleviate physical discomfort and cognitive issues. The extracts from the plant are specifically applied to soothe inflammation associated with joint pain and rheumatic conditions. Research also explores its application in supporting neurological health for pediatric dementia cases.

According to a study published by "Chemistry Central journal", the root of Fordia cauliflora has long been used in southern China for the treatment of rheumatism, bruises, dementia in children, and valetudinarianism. The root is the traditionally used medicinal part and yields the highest flavonoid content in Fordia cauliflora. A method was developed for the simultaneous determination of 5 flavonoids in different parts of the plant. All calibration curves for the study showed good linearity with R2>0.99 within test ranges. The overall LOD and LOQ were lower than 2.5 ng/mL and 5.0 ng/mL, respectively. Recovery studies for the quantified compounds were found to be within the range of 93.6-99.8% with RSD less than 5.73%.
