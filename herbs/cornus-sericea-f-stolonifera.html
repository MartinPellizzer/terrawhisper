Cornus sericea f. stolonifera

Cornus sericea f. stolonifera, also known as red-osier dogwood, is a shrub or small tree native to North America, distinguished by its red or yellowish stems.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which may contribute to its medicinal value.

The health benefits associated with Cornus sericea f. stolonifera include reducing inflammation, improving cardiovascular health, and supporting immune function.

Herbal preparations of the plant, such as teas, infusions, and tinctures, can be made from its bark, leaves, and roots, but more research is needed to confirm its efficacy and safety.
