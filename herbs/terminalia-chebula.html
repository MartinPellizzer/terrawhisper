Possible Side Effects of terminalia chebula

Terminalia chebula can have side effects if used improperly, such as diarrhea, stomach cramps, and nausea due to its high content of tannins and other compounds that can irritate the digestive system.

Prolonged use or excessive consumption of Terminalia chebula can lead to kidney damage and interact with certain medications, including blood thinners and diabetes medications.

In some cases, it may also cause allergic reactions, such as skin rashes and itching, in individuals who are sensitive to its compounds.

Additionally, pregnant or breastfeeding women should consult a healthcare professional before using Terminalia chebula, as it may affect the fetus or baby's development.
