Terminalia chebula

Terminalia chebula, commonly known as Haritaki, is a deciduous tree that belongs to the Combretaceae family.

The plant has been used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine for centuries, where it is valued for its numerous health benefits, including its potential to aid digestion, boost the immune system, and act as an antioxidant.

The therapeutic actions of Haritaki are attributed to its ability to reduce inflammation, exhibit antimicrobial properties, and support liver function.

The bioactive constituents of the plant include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Possible Side Effects of Terminalia chebula

Terminalia chebula can have side effects if used improperly, such as allergic reactions, skin irritation, and gastrointestinal problems.

Consuming high doses of Terminalia chebula may cause stomach upset, diarrhea, and nausea, while prolonged use can lead to liver damage and kidney problems.

Additionally, individuals with bleeding disorders or taking anticoagulant medications should avoid using Terminalia chebula, as it may exacerbate bleeding tendencies.

If not used under the guidance of a healthcare professional, Terminalia chebula can interact with other medications and worsen pre-existing health conditions.
