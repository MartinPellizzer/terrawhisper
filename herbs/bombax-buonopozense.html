Bombax buonopozense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bombax buonopozense has a large deciduous tree habit characterized by a thick trunk often covered in conical prickles and spreading branches that terminate in large, fleshy, cup-shaped flowers. The leaves are imparipinnate with several pairs of leaflets that possess an elliptical shape and smooth margins. Its flowers are typically large, solitary, and colored in shades of red or pink, emerging prominently from the woody stems. The fruit develops into a large, woody capsule that eventually splits to release numerous seeds embedded in a soft, fibrous pulp. These morphological features allow the species to adapt effectively to its tropical forest habitat.

This plant is native to the tropical regions of West and Central Africa, specifically spanning across countries like Cameroon, Gabon, Congo, and the Democratic Republic of the Congo. The species is frequently found in dense equatorial forests where the soil remains nutrient-rich and well-drained. Its distribution is closely tied to the presence of stable tropical climates that support large canopy trees.
