What's the taxonomical classification of Sinacalia davidii?

Sinacalia davidii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its evolutionary lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, eventually placing it in the order Asterales. As a member of the family Asteraceae, this plant is categorized under the genus Sinacalia, with its specific identity defined by the species name davidii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sinacalia davidii has succulent, cylindrical stems that can reach lengths of up to 1.5 meters and are characterized by a bright green color and a slightly waxy texture.

The leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures that are sparsely distributed along the stem to minimize water loss. During its flowering period, the plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that typically range in color from pale pink to deep red. These inflorescences emerge from the stem nodes and are often accompanied by tiny, inconspicuous bracts.

The root system is fibrous and shallow, designed to quickly absorb moisture from brief rainfall events in its arid habitat.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Sinacalia davidii can cause severe skin irritation, blistering, and potential allergic reactions if the plant's sap comes into direct contact with human tissue. The milky latex found within the stems and leaves contains irritants that may trigger redness or swelling upon contact. If ingested, the plant may lead to gastrointestinal distress, including nausea or vomiting. While specific systemic toxicity levels are not extensively documented in all medical literature, caution is advised to prevent accidental exposure.
