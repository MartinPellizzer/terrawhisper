What's the taxonomical classification of Sinacalia davidii?

Sinacalia davidii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its evolutionary lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, eventually placing it in the order Asterales. As a member of the family Asteraceae, this plant is categorized under the genus Sinacalia, with its specific identity defined by the species name davidii.

      What are the herbal preparations of Sinacalia davidii?

      Sinacalia davidii is prepared as a decoction by boiling the dried stems or roots in water to create a medicinal liquid. This aqueous extract is the most common method used to harness the plant's active components for internal consumption. Some practitioners may also process the plant into a powder for easier storage or specific dosing requirements.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Sinacalia davidii can cause severe skin irritation, blistering, and potential allergic reactions if the plant's sap comes into direct contact with human tissue. The milky latex found within the stems and leaves contains irritants that may trigger redness or swelling upon contact. If ingested, the plant may lead to gastrointestinal distress, including nausea or vomiting. While specific systemic toxicity levels are not extensively documented in all medical literature, caution is advised to prevent accidental exposure. It is important to avoid touching the plant with bare skin or consuming any part of it.
