Mentha dahurica

What's the taxonomical classification of Mentha dahurica?

Mentha dahurica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Finally, it is situated within the genus Mentha, with its specific designation being the species dahurica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Mentha dahurica has square stems, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins, and small, tubular, pale purple or white flowers arranged in dense terminal spikes. The plant typically grows from a creeping rhizome that allows it to form dense mats in moist habitats. Its leaves are oppositely arranged along the stem and often possess a distinct aromatic scent when crushed. The inflorescence consists of many-flowered verticillasters that create a compact flowering head at the tips of the branches. These morphological features are characteristic of the Lamiaceae family and assist in its identification among other mint species.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires moist, well-draining soil with a slightly acidic to neutral pH and partial shade to thrive in temperate climates.

It is typically propagated through division of the rhizomes or by planting stem cuttings during the spring growing season. Because it spreads aggressively through underground runners, it is best contained within dedicated garden beds or large containers to prevent it from overtaking other species. Regular watering is essential to maintain consistent soil moisture, especially during periods of high heat.

Periodic harvesting of the leaves encourages bushier growth and prevents the stems from becoming overly woody.
