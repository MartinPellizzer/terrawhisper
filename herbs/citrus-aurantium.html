Citron (Citrus aurantium)

Citron (Citrus aurantium) is a member of the Rutaceae family, native to Southeast Asia, China, and Indonesia. Traditionally, its fruits, bark, and leaves have been used for infusions, oil infusions, and decoctions.

This herb is particularly valued for its bitter, antispasmodic, and carminative actions, and has a long history of use in traditional chinese medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and european herbal medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters.
Leaves
Simple leaves with upper surface pale green and lower surface darker green, featuring prominent stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Flowers are zygomorphic with five petals, two of which are larger and show a distinct sinus, and five stamens arranged in two whorls, with a superior ovary and bilateral symmetry
Stems
Erect, woody, branched, with opposite arrangement, and covered in short, pubescent hairs.
