What to know about Citrus aurantium (bitter orange) before using it medicinally

Bitter orange, scientifically known as Citrus aurantium, is a herb that has been revered for its numerous health benefits, boasting properties that can help alleviate digestive issues, reduce inflammation, and even combat certain diseases.

In the culinary world, bitter orange is a staple ingredient in many recipes, used to add depth and complexity to dishes such as marmalades, preserves, and marinades. From a horticultural perspective, bitter orange is a popular choice for home gardeners due to its relatively low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in a variety of climates. Botanically speaking, the herb's name is derived from its distinctive bitter flavor, which is attributed to the presence of limonoids and other compounds found within its peel, leaves, and fruit.

With a history dating back thousands of years, bitter orange has been referenced in ancient texts and used in traditional medicine practices around the world, making it a fascinating herb worth exploring in more detail.

What are the pest and diseases tips of Citrus aurantium?

One of the most common pests affecting C. aurantium is the citrus leafminer (Phyllocnistis citrella), which lays eggs on the leaves causing significant damage and reducing fruit quality. Diseases affecting C. aurantium include root rot caused by Fusarium oxysporum, leaf spot caused by Colletotrichum gloeosporioides, and canker caused by Botryosphaeria dothidea.

These pests and diseases can be managed through integrated pest management (IPM) strategies that combine chemical control methods with biological control agents, cultural practices, and resistant cultivars.
