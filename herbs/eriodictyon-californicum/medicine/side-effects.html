1. Causes allergic reactions

Eriodictyon californicum causes allergic reactions.

The plant's sap and leaves contain quinovic acid glycosides, which may trigger an immune response in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling.

Additionally, the plant's flavonoids and terpenes can cause respiratory issues like congestion and coughing in people with pre-existing allergies or sensitivities.

2. Triggers asthma attacks

Eriodictyon californicum triggers asthma attacks due to its ability to stimulate the respiratory system and increase bronchial secretions.

This can exacerbate existing asthmatic conditions or trigger episodes in individuals with mild asthma.

As a result, individuals may experience symptoms such as wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.

3. Increases blood pressure

Eriodictyon californicum increases blood pressure due to its ability to stimulate the nervous system and increase sympathetic activity.

The herb's alkaloid content, particularly eriodictyol and isoveridotin, can cause vasoconstriction, leading to increased blood pressure.

Additionally, yerba santa may also enhance the effects of other herbs or supplements that have a similar mechanism of action, further contributing to its hypertensive effect.

4. Lowers blood sugar

Eriodictyon californicum lowers blood sugar levels due to its ability to stimulate insulin secretion and increase glucose uptake in muscle cells.

This is primarily attributed to the herb's saponin content, which has been shown to enhance insulin sensitivity and reduce glucose absorption in the gut.

As a result, individuals taking Eriodictyon californicum may experience hypoglycemia (low blood sugar) if they are not monitoring their blood sugar levels or adjusting their medication accordingly.

5. Stimulates excessive sweating

Eriodictyon californicum stimulates excessive sweating due to its ability to increase heart rate and blood flow.

This is a result of the herb's stimulatory effect on the nervous system, which can cause an increase in sweat production as the body tries to cool itself down.

Additionally, yerba santa contains compounds that have been shown to dilate blood vessels, further contributing to increased sweating.

6. Causes stomach upset

Eriodictyon californicum causes stomach upset due to its bitter and astringent properties that can irritate the mucous membranes in the digestive tract.

The alkaloids present in this herb, such as eriodictyol and loganin, may also contribute to stomach discomfort by altering gut motility and increasing the production of gastric acid.

This irritation can lead to symptoms like nausea, bloating, and cramps in some individuals who ingest Eriodictyon californicum.

7. Induces nausea and vomiting

Eriodictyon californicum induces nausea and vomiting.

This side effect is likely due to the plant's alkaloid content, particularly eriodictyol and its derivatives, which can stimulate the vagus nerve and cause gastrointestinal irritation.

Additionally, the saponins present in Eriodictyon californicum may also contribute to these adverse effects by altering gut motility and increasing gastric acid secretion.

8. Affects heart rate irregularly

Eriodictyon californicum affects heart rate irregularly.

This is due to its ability to stimulate the heart and increase cardiac output, which can lead to an irregular heartbeat or palpitations. The herb contains flavonoids and terpenes that can cause a rapid increase in blood pressure, further contributing to heart rate irregularities.

Additionally, yerba santa's stimulatory effects on the nervous system may also play a role in its ability to alter heart rhythms.

9. Inhibits bone growth

Eriodictyon californicum inhibits bone growth by disrupting the normal process of osteoblast activity.

The compound's ability to bind to estrogen receptors and activate certain signaling pathways can lead to a decrease in bone formation and an increase in bone resorption, ultimately resulting in impaired bone growth and development.

This side effect may be particularly concerning for individuals who are already at risk for osteoporosis or other bone-related disorders.

10. Interferes with hormone levels

Eriodictyon californicum interferes with hormone levels due to its estrogen-like properties.

The plant's isoflavones, such as eriodictyol and saponins, bind to estrogen receptors in the body, potentially altering normal hormonal balance.

This can lead to changes in menstruation, fertility, and other endocrine-related functions.
