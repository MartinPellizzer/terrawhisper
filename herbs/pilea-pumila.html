Pilea pumila

What's the taxonomical classification of Pilea pumila?

Pilea pumila belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Rosales and is a member of the family Urticaceae. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Pilea and the species pumila.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pilea pumila has small, opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are typically 1 to 3 centimeters long with serrated margins and a slightly textured, matte surface. The plant features thin, delicate stems that grow in a creeping or spreading habit, often forming dense mats of low-growing foliage. Its flowers are inconspicuous and tiny, appearing in clusters tucked within the leaf axils. The overall structure is characterized by a compact, herbaceous form that stays close to the substrate. These morphological traits allow the species to thrive in moist, shaded environments where it can efficiently cover the soil surface.
