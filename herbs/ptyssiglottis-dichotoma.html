What is Ptyssiglottis dichotoma?

Ptyssiglottis dichotoma is a species of flowering plant in the family Acanthaceae.

It is a climber with a woody stem, up to 10 cm in diameter, and a dichotomous branching pattern. The leaves are elliptic to lanceolate, 5-10 cm long and 2-4 cm wide, with a petiole up to 1 cm long. The inflorescence is a terminal spike, 10-20 cm long, with pinkish-white flowers that are 2-3 cm long and have a 2-lipped corolla.

The plant is endemic to New Guinea, Papua New Guinea, and the Solomon Islands.
