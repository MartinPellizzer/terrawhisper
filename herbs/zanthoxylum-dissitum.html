Zanthoxylum dissitum

What's the taxonomical classification of Zanthoxylum dissitum?

Zanthoxylum dissitum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized under the order Sapindales and falls within the family Rutaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement in the genus Zanthoxylum, with its unique designation being the species dissitum.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including gastrointestinal disorders, respiratory infections, skin diseases, and inflammatory conditions. Its medicinal properties are frequently applied to manage stomach aches, indigestion, and intestinal parasites. Practitioners also utilize its components to alleviate symptoms of fever and coughs. Furthermore, the plant is often employed in traditional medicine to reduce swelling and treat external skin lesions. These therapeutic applications rely on the plant's bioactive compounds to target different physiological systems.
