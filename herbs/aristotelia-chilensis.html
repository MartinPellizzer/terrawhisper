Chilean Wineberry (Aristotelia chilensis)

Chilean Wineberry (Aristotelia chilensis) is a member of the Elaeocarpaceae family, native to South America, Chile, and Patagonia. Traditionally, its leaves, bark, and stems have been used for decoctions, infusions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its tonic, anti-inflammatory, and astringent actions, and has a long history of use in native american herbal medicine, european herbal medicine, and african traditional medicine.
