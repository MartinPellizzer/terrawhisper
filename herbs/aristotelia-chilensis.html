Chilean Wineberry

Aristotelia chilensis

Chilean Wineberry (Aristotelia chilensis) is a small shrub native to southern Chile and is known for its small, dark red berries that are rich in antioxidants and have been traditionally used to improve symptoms of diabetes and other metabolic disorders.

Botanical Identification

Aristotelia chilensis, commonly known as Chilean Wineberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Elaeocarpaceae family. Native to southern Chile, it is a perennial shrub with red berries and elliptical leaves. It is also referred to as "Chilean wineberry" or "Araucaria berry" in herbal literature.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Magnoliopsida
  • Order: Saxifragales
  • Family: Elaeocarpaceae
  • Genus: Aristotelia
  • Species: Aristotelia chilensis

The Chilean Wineberry contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and tannins, which contribute to its medicinal properties. Safety Profile

The Chilean Wineberry is generally considered safe when consumed in typical food amounts. However, it may cause mild gastrointestinal upset in some individuals. There are no well-documented contraindications, but caution is advised for those with allergies to related plants.
