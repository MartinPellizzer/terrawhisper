Botanical Identification of Argan Tree (Argania spinosa)

To identify Argan Tree (Argania spinosa) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

The Argan Tree, Argania spinosa, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Santalales, family Santalaceae, genus Argania, and species Argania spinosa. These classifications place it within the group of flowering plants and highlight its evolutionary relationships within the Santalaceae family.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The Argan Tree is scientifically known as Argania spinosa, a name first cited by the French botanist Jean-Baptiste Lamarck in 1785. It has several synonyms, including Argania spinosa (Lam.) A. DC. and Argania spinosa var. spp. Common names include Argan tree, Argan wood, and Argan fruit tree, while regional and historical names vary across North African cultures.

Morphological Characteristics

The Argan Tree is a small, slow-growing tree with a dense, gnarled root system that anchors it firmly in arid soils. Its stem is short and thick, often forming a bushy crown. Leaves are pinnately compound, with small, leathery leaflets. The inflorescence is a panicle, bearing yellowish-green flowers. The fruit is a drupe, containing one or two seeds enclosed in a fibrous outer layer.

Diagnostic Identification Features

The Argan Tree can be distinguished by its distinctive spinose leaves with sharp spines, rough bark, and small yellow flowers. It is often confused with other species like the common tamarisk, but its unique leaf structure and habitat in arid regions help in accurate identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

The Argan Tree is native to the semi-arid regions of North Africa, particularly Morocco. It thrives in hot, dry climates with low rainfall. It prefers well-drained, sandy or clay soils. It is typically found at altitudes between 300 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

The Argan tree blooms from March to May, producing yellow-green flowers. Seed formation occurs between July and September. Harvesting of fruits typically happens in October, when the fruits are fully mature and ready for collection.

Phylogenetic Relationships

The Argan Tree (Argania spinosa) is closely related to species within the genus Argania, which belongs to the family Sapotaceae. It shares key family traits such as latex production and fleshy fruits, and its phylogenetic position places it among other tropical trees with similar morphological and genetic characteristics.
