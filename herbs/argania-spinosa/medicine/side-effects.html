10 Possible Side Effects Of Argania Spinosa

Argania spinosa has some side effects when used improperly, such as allergic reactions, digestive issues, and skin irritation.

These side effects can occur due to individual sensitivities or excessive consumption.

In severe cases, argan oil can cause anaphylaxis, leading to respiratory distress, or interact with medications, potentially worsening health conditions like diabetes or high blood pressure.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Argania spinosa causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

This is due to the presence of oleic acid and linoleic acid, which are essential fatty acids found in its oil. These fatty acids can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms such as redness, itching, swelling, and blistering. Additionally, the antioxidant properties of argan oil may also contribute to allergic reactions by causing histamine release and increasing blood flow to affected areas.

As a result, people with sensitive skin or allergies may experience adverse reactions when using Argania spinosa products.

2. Triggers asthma attacks

Argania spinosa triggers asthma attacks due to its high content of oleic acid and linoleic acid.

These polyunsaturated fatty acids can stimulate the production of prostaglandins, which are potent bronchoconstrictors that can exacerbate respiratory symptoms in individuals with asthma.

Additionally, the oil extracted from Argania spinosa seeds contains allergenic proteins that may trigger an allergic response in susceptible individuals, further contributing to the development of asthmatic symptoms.

3. Induces gastrointestinal upset

Argania spinosa induces gastrointestinal upset in some individuals.

This is attributed to the high concentration of linoleic acid and oleic acid present in its oil, which can cause stomach discomfort, nausea, and diarrhea due to their ability to alter gut flora and increase prostaglandins production, leading to increased gastric motility and secretion.

4. Produces dizziness spells

Argania spinosa produces dizziness spells due to its high concentration of oleic acid and linoleic acid.

These essential fatty acids can cause a sudden drop in blood pressure, leading to feelings of lightheadedness and dizziness.

Additionally, the herb's ability to dilate blood vessels may further contribute to this effect, as it allows for increased blood flow and potentially rapid changes in blood pressure.

5. Enhances bleeding risk

Argania spinosa enhances bleeding risk due to its ability to inhibit platelet aggregation and prolong bleeding time.

This is attributed to its high content of unsaturated fatty acids, particularly oleic acid, which can interfere with blood clotting mechanisms.

Additionally, the presence of bioactive compounds like flavonoids and phenolic acids may further contribute to this effect by disrupting normal hemostasis.

6. Increases heart rate

Argania spinosa increases heart rate due to its high levels of oleic acid and linoleic acid.

These polyunsaturated fatty acids can stimulate the cardiovascular system, leading to an increase in heart rate.

Additionally, the antioxidant properties of argan oil may also contribute to this effect by reducing inflammation and improving blood circulation, further elevating heart rate.

7. Lowers blood pressure

Argania spinosa lowers blood pressure by inhibiting the production of angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE), a key player in the renin-angiotensin-aldosterone system that regulates blood pressure.

This property makes it a potential therapeutic agent for hypertension treatment.

Additionally, the polyunsaturated fatty acids and antioxidants present in argan oil may also contribute to its hypotensive effects by relaxing blood vessels and improving cardiovascular health.

8. Interferes with medication

Argania spinosa interferes with medication by altering its absorption rate and potency due to its high fatty acid content.

The omega-9 fatty acids in argan oil can bind to certain medications, reducing their efficacy or increasing the risk of adverse reactions.

Additionally, argan's antioxidant properties may enhance the elimination of certain medications from the body, leading to reduced blood levels and potentially decreased therapeutic effects.

9. Affects fertility rates

Argania spinosa affects fertility rates due to its potential interaction with hormonal imbalances.

The oil's rich content of antioxidants and essential fatty acids may disrupt the body's natural hormone production, leading to irregular menstrual cycles, polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS), and decreased sperm count.

These changes can negatively impact fertility rates in both men and women.

10. Increases urine output

Argania spinosa increases urine output due to its natural diuretic properties.

The plant's extract contains compounds like terpenoids and saponins that stimulate the kidneys to excrete excess fluids, leading to increased urination.

This is a common side effect of using argan oil or other products containing Argania spinosa extracts, as they can affect the body's fluid balance and electrolyte levels.
