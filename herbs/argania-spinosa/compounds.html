Argan Tree (Argania spinosa) Compounds

The primary compounds of Argan Tree include phenolic acids, which act as antioxidants. Flavonoids and tannins also contribute to antioxidant properties. Terpenoids and coumarin provide anti-inflammatory effects. Saponins and glycosides support immune function, while essential oils and alkaloids have antimicrobial properties. Anthocyanins add to the plant's antioxidant capacity.

Argan Tree (Argania spinosa) Monograph

1. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in the Argan Tree (Argania spinosa). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound is widely used in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and skin-protective effects.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in the Argan Tree (Argania spinosa). They contribute to the tree's antioxidant properties, supporting skin health and reducing inflammation. These compounds are valued in herbal medicine for their potential therapeutic benefits.

3. Tannin

Tannin, found in the active compounds of Argan Tree (Argania spinosa), contributes to its astringent properties. It is used in traditional herbal medicine to treat digestive issues and skin conditions. Tannin also helps in preserving the plant's medicinal qualities. Its presence supports the therapeutic applications of Argan Tree in herbal treatments.

4. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in the Argan Tree (Argania spinosa) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds are found in the tree's oil and are known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. They support skin health and are used in traditional herbal remedies for their therapeutic benefits.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in the Argan Tree (Argania spinosa). It contributes to the plant's therapeutic properties, including anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory effects. Coumarin is commonly used in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits. Its presence in Argan Tree oil supports its traditional use in treating various ailments.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound found in the Argan Tree (Argania spinosa). It contributes to the tree's traditional use in herbal medicine due to its cleansing and emulsifying properties. Saponin helps in the preparation of argan oil, supporting its applications in skin care and as a natural remedy.

7. Glycoside

Glycosides in the Argan Tree (Argania spinosa) are naturally occurring compounds with potential therapeutic benefits. They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, making them valuable in traditional herbal medicine for treating skin conditions and digestive issues. These compounds are often extracted from the tree's leaves and fruits.

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of Argan Tree (Argania spinosa) contains bioactive compounds like limonene and geraniol. These components contribute to its antimicrobial and antioxidant properties, making it valuable in traditional herbal medicine for skin care and digestive health.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a bioactive compound found in the Argan Tree (Argania spinosa). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by interacting with biological systems to potentially support skin health and reduce inflammation. Alkaloids in Argan Tree are valued for their role in traditional herbal medicine practices.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a pigment found in the Argan Tree (Argania spinosa), contributing to its antioxidant properties. It helps neutralize free radicals, supporting cellular health and reducing oxidative stress. This compound is particularly valued in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and protective effects on the body.
