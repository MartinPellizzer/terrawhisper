What's the taxonomical classification of Pyrostegia ignea?

Pyrostegia ignea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Bignoniaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Pyrostegia, and its unique species designation, ignea.

      What are the herbal preparations of Pyrostegia ignea?

      Pyrostegia ignea is prepared as a concentrated decoction, a topical infused oil, and a dried powdered extract for medicinal use. The decoction is typically made by boiling the woody stems in water to extract deep-seated alkaloids. For dermatological applications, the leaves are macerated in a carrier oil to create a soothing ointment. The dried plant material can also be finely ground into a powder to be encapsulated or mixed into herbal tinctures. These various forms allow for both internal consumption and external therapeutic application.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Pyrostegia ignea can cause severe chemical burns on the skin, acute respiratory distress if inhaled, and intense ocular inflammation upon direct contact. The plant contains caustic sap that reacts immediately with moisture to irritate epithelial tissues. Individuals exposed to its pollen may experience rapid swelling of the throat and difficulty breathing. If the crushed leaves are handled without protection, the resulting dermatological reaction often leads to blistering and long-term scarring. Prompt medical intervention is necessary to mitigate these toxicological effects.
