Kaempferia elegans

What's the taxonomical classification of Kaempferia elegans?

Kaempferia elegans belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and placed in the order Zingiberales. As a member of the family Zingiberaceae, this plant is specifically identified by its genus, Kaempferia, and its species designation, elegans.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Kaempferia elegans has elliptical to lanceolate leaves that emerge directly from a rhizome and form a low-growing rosette pattern.

These leaves typically measure several decimeters in length and possess smooth margins with distinct parallel venation. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that bloom near the ground level among the foliage. These blossoms often feature white or pale petals with intricate colored markings in the center.

The underground rhizome serves as the primary storage organ for the plant's nutrients and water.
