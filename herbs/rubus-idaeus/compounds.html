Raspberry (Rubus idaeus) Compounds

Raspberry contains several key active compounds, including flavonoids, which act as antioxidants. Phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory properties. Tannins and anthocyanins provide astringent and pigment effects. Coumarin and glycosides have mild sedative and digestive benefits. Saponins, essential oils, terpenoids, and alkaloids also play roles in its medicinal and aromatic qualities.

Raspberry (Rubus idaeus) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Raspberry (Rubus idaeus). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting anti-inflammatory and immune-modulating effects. These compounds are often responsible for the plant's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in Raspberry (Rubus idaeus). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports the plant's traditional use in promoting digestive health and reducing inflammation.

3. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Raspberry (Rubus idaeus). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health by reducing inflammation and promoting tissue repair. Tannins also act as antioxidants, helping to neutralize harmful free radicals in the body.

4. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Raspberry (Rubus idaeus). It contributes to the fruit's vibrant color and possesses antioxidant properties. These compounds help protect cells from oxidative stress and may support cardiovascular and inflammatory health. Anthocyanin is also associated with improved urinary tract health.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Raspberry (Rubus idaeus). It contributes to the plant's aromatic properties and has potential anti-inflammatory and anticoagulant effects. Coumarin is often used in herbal medicine for its mild therapeutic benefits.

6. Glycoside

Glycosides in Raspberry (Rubus idaeus) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds, such as raspberry ketones, have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. They support digestive health and may aid in skin conditions. Glycosides are found in the fruit's tissues and are part of the plant's natural defense mechanisms.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Raspberry (Rubus idaeus). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponins may support digestive health and immune function. They are also known for their potential role in reducing cholesterol levels.

8. Essential oil

Essential oil is a volatile compound found in Raspberry (Rubus idaeus). It contributes to the plant's aroma and has antimicrobial properties. The oil is extracted through steam distillation and is used in herbal medicine for its potential therapeutic benefits.

9. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Raspberry (Rubus idaeus). They contribute to the plant's aromatic properties and have demonstrated antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. These compounds may support digestive health and immune function, making them relevant in herbal medicine.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloids are a class of nitrogen-containing compounds found in Raspberry (Rubus idaeus). While Raspberries contain minimal alkaloids compared to other plants, trace amounts may contribute to its mild medicinal effects. These compounds are typically associated with more potent pharmacological activities in other herbs, but their role in Raspberry is less prominent.
