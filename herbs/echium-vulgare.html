What is Common Viper'S Bugloss (Echium Vulgare)?

Common Viper's Bugloss (Echium vulgare) is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its spiky purple flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves, commonly used in herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and mild sedative properties.

Common Viper's Bugloss (Echium vulgare) is native to southern Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, and is commonly found in dry, sunny grasslands, rocky slopes, and along roadsides.
