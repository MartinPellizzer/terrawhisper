It can also cause stomach upset and gastrointestinal discomfort, resulting in bloating, cramping, and diarrhea due to its high mucilage content. The herb may lead to nausea and induce fatigue, as the body processes its compounds, which can be taxing on the digestive and energy systems.

Additionally, echium vulgare may cause dizziness and headaches, possibly due to its mild sedative effects or interactions with other substances. Some users report skin irritation or throat irritation, which could be attributed to the herb's resinous properties or individual sensitivity.

While these side effects are not universal, they highlight the importance of consulting a healthcare professional before using echium vulgare, especially for those with pre-existing conditions or taking other medications.
