Pennyroyal (Levisticum officinale) Compounds

Pennyroyal contains essential oil as its primary volatile compound, known for its strong aromatic properties. Coumarin contributes to its scent and has potential anticoagulant effects. Flavonoids and phenolic acids provide antioxidant benefits. Glycosides, terpenoids, and saponins support various medicinal properties, while tannins and alkaloids contribute to its astringent and stimulant effects.

Pennyroyal (Levisticum officinale) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Pennyroyal (Levisticum officinale) is a key active compound, containing compounds like methyl carvilate and carvone. It contributes to the plant's aromatic properties and is used in herbal medicine for its potential digestive and antiseptic benefits.

2. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Pennyroyal (Levisticum officinale). It contributes to the plant's antispasmodic and sedative properties. Coumarin also has mild anticoagulant effects, though it should be used with caution. Its presence influences the overall therapeutic profile of Pennyroyal in herbal medicine.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Pennyroyal (Levisticum officinale). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and respiratory support. These compounds help protect cells from oxidative damage and may enhance the plant's therapeutic effects.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Pennyroyal (Levisticum officinale). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties and may support digestive health. This compound is often found in higher concentrations in the leaves and roots of the plant. Its presence plays a role in the traditional use of Pennyroyal in herbal medicine.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Pennyroyal (Levisticum officinale). It contributes to the plant's aromatic properties and is believed to support digestive and respiratory health. Glycosides in Pennyroyal are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their mild stimulant and carminative effects.

6. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Pennyroyal (Levisticum officinale), contributing to its aromatic profile and medicinal properties. These compounds are known for their antimicrobial and antifungal effects, making them valuable in herbal medicine for treating infections and supporting respiratory health.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Pennyroyal (Levisticum officinale). It contributes to the plant's mild detoxifying properties and supports digestive health by promoting bile secretion. Saponins also have mild antimicrobial effects, enhancing Pennyroyal's traditional use in herbal medicine.

8. Tannin

Tannin in Pennyroyal (Levisticum officinale) contributes to its astringent properties, helping to reduce inflammation and soothe mucous membranes. It also supports the plant's traditional use in digestive and skin health applications.

9. Alkaloid

Pennyroyal (Levisticum officinale) contains the alkaloid apiol. Apiol is known for its potential antispasmodic and carminative properties. It may support digestive health and help relieve gastrointestinal discomfort. However, its use requires caution due to potential toxicity.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Pennyroyal (Levisticum officinale). It contributes to the plant's vibrant color and has antioxidant properties. This compound may support overall health by neutralizing free radicals and reducing oxidative stress. Its presence in Pennyroyal enhances the plant's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.
