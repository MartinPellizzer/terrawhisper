10 Possible Side Effects Of Levisticum Officinale

Levisticum officinale has some side effects when used improperly, such as nausea, vomiting, and dizziness.

These side effects can be caused by excessive consumption, interactions with medications, or sensitivity to the herb's compounds.

For instance, severe nausea can lead to dehydration, exacerbate underlying health conditions, and disrupt daily activities, causing work absenteeism and strained relationships.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Levisticum officinale causes allergic reactions due to its ability to stimulate the immune system and cause an overactive response.

This can lead to symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.

The allergenic compounds present in lovage, including flavonoids and terpenes, may trigger this reaction, making it essential to be aware of potential allergies before using this herb.

2. Induces gastrointestinal upset

Levisticum officinale induces gastrointestinal upset due to its high concentration of furanocoumarins and other compounds that can irritate the digestive tract.

These bioactive molecules can cause nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and stomach cramps in some individuals who consume this herb, potentially leading to discomfort and distress.

The potency of Levisticum officinale's active ingredients may vary depending on factors such as preparation method, dosage, and individual sensitivity.

3. Triggers respiratory distress

Levisticum officinale triggers respiratory distress due to its volatile oil composition and potential allergenic properties.

The herb contains a high concentration of sesquiterpene lactones, which can cause bronchial constriction and inflammation in some individuals.

Additionally, the presence of histamine-releasing compounds may exacerbate existing respiratory conditions such as asthma or allergies, leading to increased wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.

4. Elicits skin irritation

Levisticum officinale elicits skin irritation due to its high concentration of volatile oils and sesquiterpenes, which can cause allergic reactions or sensitivity in some individuals.

The oils may penetrate the skin's surface, triggering an inflammatory response and leading to redness, itching, and discomfort.

Additionally, the plant's aromatic compounds may also be absorbed through the skin, potentially contributing to this adverse effect.

5. Promotes headaches

Levisticum officinale promotes headaches due to its high content of coumarins and furanocoumarins.

These compounds can cause a stimulation of the central nervous system, leading to increased blood flow and pressure in the head, which may result in headache pain.

Additionally, the volatile oils present in Levisticum officinale can also contribute to this effect by stimulating the brain's trigeminovascular system, further exacerbating headaches.

6. Exacerbates insomnia

Levisticum officinale exacerbates insomnia due to its stimulating properties that can increase alertness and energy.

The herb's essential oils, particularly falcarinol, have been shown to affect the nervous system, leading to difficulty falling asleep or staying asleep. This is because levisticum officinale can stimulate the brain and body, making it challenging for individuals to relax and wind down before bedtime.

As a result, people may experience fragmented sleep patterns and wake up feeling groggy and fatigued.

7. Increases heart rate

Levisticum officinale increases heart rate due to its stimulant properties, which can cause a rise in blood pressure and cardiac activity.

This is likely attributed to the presence of alkaloids such as caryophylline and ligustilide, which have been shown to stimulate the cardiovascular system.

The increased heart rate can also be a result of lovage's ability to increase the production of adrenaline, leading to a rapid heartbeat.

8. Lowers blood pressure

Levisticum officinale lowers blood pressure due to its vasodilatory properties, which cause blood vessels to relax and dilate.

The compound umbelliferone, present in the herb, is responsible for this effect, as it inhibits the activity of angiotensin-converting enzyme, a key regulator of blood pressure.

Additionally, lovage's potassium-rich content can also contribute to its hypotensive properties by counteracting the effects of sodium and promoting vasodilation.

9. Stimulates sweating excessively

Levisticum officinale stimulates sweating excessively.

This is due to its diaphoretic properties, which cause the body's sweat glands to produce more sweat in an effort to cool down and eliminate toxins from the body.

As a result, users may experience an increase in perspiration, which can be particularly noticeable on hot days or during physical activity.

10. Produces dizziness

Levisticum officinale produces dizziness due to its ability to stimulate the nervous system and alter brain activity.

The volatile oils present in this herb can cause an increase in heart rate and blood pressure, leading to feelings of lightheadedness or dizziness, especially when taken in large doses or combined with other stimulants.

Additionally, lovage's potential interactions with certain medications may also contribute to this side effect.
