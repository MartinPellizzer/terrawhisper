Pennyroyal (Levisticum officinale) Uses

Pennyroyal is traditionally used for respiratory conditions like cough, bronchitis, and asthma. It may also help with allergic rhinitis, the common cold, and sinusitis. For skin issues, it's applied to treat infections, eczema, and irritation. Its effects are primarily attributed to its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Pennyroyal (Levisticum officinale) Monograph

1. Cough

Pennyroyal is traditionally used to soothe coughs due to its expectorant and antispasmodic properties. It helps loosen mucus and reduce bronchial irritation, making it effective for both dry and productive coughs. Its aromatic compounds also have a calming effect on the respiratory system. Used internally as a tea or tincture, it supports respiratory health in mild cases.

2. Bronchitis

Pennyroyal is used to support respiratory health in bronchitis by helping to clear mucus and reduce inflammation. It may ease coughing and soothe irritated airways. Typically, it is prepared as a tea or tincture. Its antiseptic properties may help prevent secondary infections.

4. Allergic rhinitis

Pennyroyal (Levisticum officinale) may help alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistaminic properties. It can reduce nasal congestion and irritation, though it should be used cautiously and under professional guidance to avoid adverse effects.

5. Common cold

Pennyroyal is traditionally used to support respiratory health and may help alleviate symptoms of the common cold due to its antimicrobial and expectorant properties. It is often prepared as a tea to soothe congestion and reduce fever, though it should be used with caution and under professional guidance.

6. Sinusitis

Pennyroyal (Levisticum officinale) is traditionally used to support respiratory health, including sinusitis. Its anti-inflammatory and decongestant properties may help alleviate nasal congestion and reduce sinus inflammation. It is often prepared as a steam inhalation or tea to ease symptoms. However, it should be used with caution and under professional guidance.

7. Skin infection

Pennyroyal is used topically to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial properties. It can help reduce bacterial growth and inflammation on the skin. However, it should be diluted before application to avoid irritation. Always consult a qualified herbalist before use.

8. Eczema

Pennyroyal is traditionally used for eczema due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. It helps reduce skin irritation and promote healing. However, it should be used with caution, as it may cause skin sensitivity in some individuals. Always consult a qualified herbalist before use.

9. Skin infections

Pennyroyal is used topically to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial and antifungal properties. It helps reduce bacterial and fungal growth on the skin, making it effective for conditions like athlete's foot and minor bacterial infections. However, it should be diluted before applying to avoid skin irritation. Always consult a qualified herbalist before use.

10. Skin irritation

Pennyroyal is traditionally used to address skin irritation, such as rashes and eczema, due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. It can be applied topically as a compress or infused oil to soothe inflamed skin and promote healing. However, it should be used with caution, as it may cause sensitivity in some individuals.
