Habenaria tibetica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Habenaria tibetica has slender, erect stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and bear several lanceolate leaves arranged in a basal rosette or along the stem. The inflorescence consists of a terminal raceme featuring small, white to greenish-white flowers with complex, spurred labella. Each flower exhibits a distinct nectar spur that extends backward from the base of the lip. The vegetative structures are adapted to high-altitude environments, often showing a compact growth habit. Its roots consist of fleshy tubers that allow for nutrient storage during dormant periods.
