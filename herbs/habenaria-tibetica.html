Habenaria tibetica

What's the taxonomical classification of Habenaria tibetica?

Habeenaria tibetica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further categorized under the order Asparagales. It is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, falling specifically into the genus Habenaria, with its unique biological identity defined by the species tibetica.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Habenaria tibetica contains medicinal compounds primarily located within its underground tubers. These tuberous roots are harvested and processed to serve as the main source of therapeutic agents. In traditional practices, the substance extracted from these tubers is utilized to treat various ailments related to respiratory and digestive functions. Practitioners often prepare the plant parts through decoctions or powders to ensure the active constituents are bioavailable. The specific chemical profile of these tubers provides the foundation for its historical applications in folk medicine.

      What are the herbal preparations of Habenaria tibetica?

      Habenaria tibetica is prepared primarily as a decoction through boiling the dried tubers in water for medicinal use. This specific method allows the active compounds within the root structure to be extracted into a liquid form for ingestion. In some traditional practices, the plant material may also be processed into a powder to be mixed with other substances. These powders are often consumed with warm water or honey to facilitate easier swallowing. The resulting preparations are utilized to address various health concerns according to local traditional knowledge.
