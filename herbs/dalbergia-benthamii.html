Dalbergia benthamii

What's the taxonomical classification of Dalbergia benthamii?

Dalbergia benthamii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the biological order Fabales. The plant is further categorized into the family Fabaceae, which is known for its diverse range of legumes. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Dalbergia, and its specific biological identity is defined by the species name benthamii.

Its flowers are small and arranged in axillary or terminal panicles, often displaying a pale yellow or creamy color. The fruit is a characteristic legume, which is typically a flattened pod that contains several small seeds. What are the pharmacological activities of Dalbergia benthamii?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological activities including cytotoxicity and topoisomerase I inhibitory activity. Research into its chemical constituents has revealed that specific bioactive compounds can effectively induce cell death in various cancer cell lines. These extracts work by interfering with essential biological processes within malignant cells. Specifically, the plant exhibits potent topoisomerase I inhibitory activity, which disrupts DNA replication and transcription. This mechanism of action makes it a subject of interest for the development of new anticancer therapeutic agents.

As discussed in a study, robustic acid is a bioactive compound isolated from the medicinal plant Dalbergia benthamii Prain. Ten alkyl and benzyl derivatives, labeled 2a-2j, were designed and synthesized from this robustic acid. Among these, the 4-O-phenylpropyl substituted compound 2g displayed potent topoisomerase I inhibitory activity. This specific compound 2g also demonstrated cytotoxicity against the SMMC-7721, HepG2, and HeLa cell lines. Further biological testing suggests that compound 2g acted mainly by an arrest of the tumor cells in the G1 phase of the cell cycle and suppressed cell proliferation by inducing apoptosis.
