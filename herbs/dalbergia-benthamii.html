Dalbergia benthamii

What's the taxonomical classification of Dalbergia benthamii?

Dalbergia benthamii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the biological order Fabales. The plant is further categorized into the family Fabaceae, which is known for its diverse range of legumes. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Dalbergia, and its specific biological identity is defined by the species name benthamii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dalbergia benthamii has pinnately compound leaves featuring small, ovate to elliptical leaflets with entire margins and an acute apex.

The plant typically presents as a shrub or a small tree with bark that becomes fissured with age. Its flowers are small and arranged in axillary or terminal panicles, often displaying a pale yellow or creamy color. The fruit is a characteristic legume, which is typically a flattened pod that contains several small seeds.

These seeds are often enclosed within the pod to facilitate dispersal in its native habitats.
