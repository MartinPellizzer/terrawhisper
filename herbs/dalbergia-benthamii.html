What are the pharmacological activities of Dalbergia benthamii?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological potential through its potent cytotoxicity and topoisomerase I inhibitory activity. Researchers have identified specific bioactive compounds within Dalbergia benthamii that can effectively trigger cell death in various cancer cell lines. These chemical constituents function by interfering with the enzyme topoisomerase I, which is essential for DNA replication and transcription. By stabilizing the DNA-enzyme complex, the plant extracts prevent the repair of DNA strands, ultimately leading to apoptosis. Such targeted molecular mechanisms suggest that Dalbergia benthamii could serve as a valuable source for developing new chemotherapeutic agents.

According to a study published by "Chemistry & biodiversity", the medicinal plant Dalbergia benthamii Prain is the source of the bioactive compound robustic acid. Ten alkyl and benzyl derivatives, labeled 2a-2j, were designed and synthesized from this compound based on molecular docking approaches. Specifically, the 4-O-phenylpropyl substituted compound 2g showed potent topoisomerase I inhibitory activity. This derivative also demonstrated cytotoxicity against 3 cell lines, which were SMMC-7721, HepG2, and HeLa. Further testing showed that compound 2g acted by arresting tumor cells in the G1 phase of the cell cycle and suppressing proliferation through apoptosis induction.
