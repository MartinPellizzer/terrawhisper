What is Microchirita sahyadriensis?

Microchirita sahyadriensis is a species of flowering plant that belongs to the family Gesneriaceae.

It is an evergreen, terrestrial herb with short, stout stems that are 5-10 cm in length, and leaves that are ovate, elliptical, or lanceolate in shape, measuring 2-6 cm in length, and have a crenulate or dentate margin. The plant produces axillary inflorescences that are 5-7 cm in length, bearing 1-2 flowers that are tubular, greenish-white, and 1.5-2 cm in length, with a 5-lobed corolla and 2 fertile stamens. The calyx is tubular, 5-7 mm in length, and has 5 triangular teeth.

The plant is endemic to the Western Ghats of India, where it grows in wet forests at elevations of 1,000-1,500 meters.
