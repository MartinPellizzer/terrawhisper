1. Causes allergic reactions

Erythraea centaurium causes allergic reactions due to the presence of bioactive compounds such as flavonoids and saponins.

These compounds can trigger an immune response in some individuals, leading to symptoms like hives, itching, and difficulty breathing. Additionally, the herb's high concentration of volatile oils may cause sensitivity or intolerance in people who are prone to allergies, further exacerbating the allergic reaction.

As a result, individuals with known allergies should exercise caution when using Erythraea centaurium.

2. Triggers anxiety attacks

Erythraea centaurium triggers anxiety attacks due to its bitter taste and strong stimulating properties.

The herb's ability to stimulate the nervous system can cause an increase in heart rate and blood pressure, leading to feelings of panic and unease.

Additionally, the caffeine-like effects of centaury may exacerbate existing anxiety disorders or trigger new episodes, resulting in heightened levels of stress and apprehension.

3. Produces dizziness spells

Erythraea centaurium produces dizziness spells due to its ability to stimulate the central nervous system and alter blood pressure.

The herb's bitter compounds, such as gentianine, can cause a sudden increase in heart rate and blood flow, leading to feelings of lightheadedness and dizziness.

Additionally, centaury may also affect the balance centers in the brain, further contributing to its sedative and dizzying effects on users.

4. Increases heart rate

Erythraea centaurium increases heart rate due to its ability to stimulate the nervous system and increase blood circulation.

The herb's bioactive compounds, such as flavonoids and iridoid glycosides, interact with the body's natural mechanisms to produce a cardiac stimulating effect, leading to an elevation in heart rate.

This response is often attributed to the herb's potential to increase norepinephrine levels, a neurotransmitter involved in regulating heart rate and blood pressure.

5. Lowers blood pressure

Erythraea centaurium lowers blood pressure by affecting the body's natural balance of fluids and electrolytes.

The herb is believed to reduce blood vessel constriction, allowing for increased blood flow and decreased peripheral resistance, which subsequently leads to a drop in blood pressure.

Additionally, its bitter compounds may slow down digestion, reducing the absorption of nutrients and increasing the workload on the heart, further contributing to hypotension.

6. Causes stomach cramps

Erythraea centaurium causes stomach cramps due to its bitter and astringent properties.

The plant's active compounds, including flavonoids and iridoid glycosides, can irritate the digestive tract and reduce muscle contractions in the stomach, leading to discomfort, pain, and spasms.

This side effect is often mild and temporary, but it may be uncomfortable for some individuals taking the herb.

7. Induces nausea vomiting

Erythraea centaurium induces nausea vomiting.

This side effect is believed to be caused by the plant's bitter compounds, which can stimulate the stomach and increase gastric contractions, leading to discomfort and nausea.

Additionally, centaury's volatile oils may contribute to its emetic properties, making it a potential trigger for vomiting in some individuals who ingest it.

8. Relaxes too much muscle

Erythraea centaurium relaxes too much muscle due to its ability to interact with the nervous system.

The herb's alkaloids, particularly amarogentin and catmintin, are believed to influence the activity of neurotransmitters such as GABA and serotonin, which can lead to a decrease in muscle tone and an increase in relaxation.

This relaxing effect is thought to be responsible for the herb's traditional use in treating anxiety and insomnia, although it may also cause excessive drowsiness or lethargy in some individuals.

9. Weakens immune system

Erythraea centaurium weakens immune system by suppressing its activity.

This is because it contains alkaloids that inhibit the production of interferon, a vital protein that helps fight off viruses and other foreign substances in the body.

As a result, users may experience increased susceptibility to infections and illnesses, making it essential to be aware of this potential side effect when considering the use of Erythraea centaurium.

10. Disrupts menstrual cycle

Erythraea centaurium disrupts menstrual cycle by affecting hormone levels and uterine contractions.

The plant's phytoestrogens can mimic the effects of estrogen in the body, leading to changes in menstruation patterns and potentially causing irregular periods or amenorrhea. Additionally, its bitter compounds may stimulate the uterus, leading to increased muscle tone and potentially inducing menstrual cramps or heavy bleeding.

These effects are thought to be dose-dependent and may vary from person to person.
