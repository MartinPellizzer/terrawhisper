Botanical Identification of Red Centaury (Erythraea centaurium)

To identify Red Centaury (Erythraea centaurium) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Red Centaury, Erythraea centaurium, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Asterales, family Asteraceae, genus Erythraea, and species Erythraea centaurium. These classifications place it within the flowering plants and the daisy family, reflecting its botanical relationships and characteristics.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Red Centaury is Erythraea centaurium, first described by Carl von Linné in 1753. It has several synonyms, including Centaurium erythraea and Centaurium centaurium, reflecting historical taxonomic changes. Common names include Red Centaury, Blood Centaury, and Centaury, while regional and historical names vary across cultures and periods.

Morphological Characteristics

Red Centaury is a low-growing perennial herb with a dense root system that anchors it firmly. Its stems are slender and branching, often lying close to the ground. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and hairy, with a slightly serrated edge. The inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of flowers, each with five petals and a yellow center. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dark seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Red Centaury can be distinguished by its bright red flowers with five lobes, opposite leaves with serrated edges, and a hairy stem. It differs from similar species like Erythraea capitata by having smaller, more clustered flowers and a more branched growth habit. Its unique color and leaf structure make it easily identifiable in the field.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Red Centaury (Erythraea centaurium) is native to southern Europe and western Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with mild summers and cool winters. It prefers well-drained, calcareous soils. It is typically found at altitudes between 300 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Red Centaury blooms from May to July, producing vibrant red flowers. Seed formation occurs in late summer, typically August to September. For identification purposes, the plant is best harvested during its flowering period to ensure accurate species recognition.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Red Centaury (Erythraea centaurium) belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is closely related to species within the genus Erythraea. It shares key family traits such as opposite leaves and spurred flowers, and is most closely related to other centaury species in the same genus. Its phylogenetic placement reflects its evolutionary ties to other Mediterranean plants with similar morphological and ecological characteristics.
