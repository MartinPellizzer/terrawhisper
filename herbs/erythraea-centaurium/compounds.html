Red Centaury (Erythraea centaurium) Compounds

Red Centaury contains several key compounds, including flavonoids, tannins, and coumarins, which contribute to its medicinal properties. Phenolic acids and glycosides provide antioxidant effects, while alkaloids and saponins support anti-inflammatory actions. Terpenoids, anthocyanins, and essential oils add to its diverse therapeutic potential.

Red Centaury (Erythraea centaurium) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Red Centaury (Erythraea centaurium) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine. They help protect cells from oxidative stress and may enhance overall health benefits.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Red Centaury (Erythraea centaurium). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Tannins may support digestive health and aid in wound healing, though more research is needed to confirm these benefits.

3. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Red Centaury (Erythraea centaurium). It contributes to the plant's antispasmodic and sedative effects. Found in the roots and leaves, coumarin also exhibits mild anticoagulant properties. Its presence supports the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and nervous system support.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Red Centaury (Erythraea centaurium). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and inflammatory conditions. Its presence enhances the plant's therapeutic potential.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Red Centaury (Erythraea centaurium). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly its use in traditional herbal medicine. Glycosides in Red Centaury are known for their potential effects on the cardiovascular and nervous systems.

6. Alkaloid

Alkaloids in Red Centaury (Erythraea centaurium) are bioactive compounds with potential therapeutic effects. These alkaloids may contribute to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. They are typically extracted from the plant's roots and leaves. Their chemical structure and pharmacological activity make them a focus of research in herbal pharmacology.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Red Centaury (Erythraea centaurium). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Saponin also aids in the plant's traditional use for digestive and respiratory support. Its presence enhances the overall efficacy of Red Centaury in herbal formulations.

8. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Red Centaury (Erythraea centaurium) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, supporting the plant's use in traditional herbal medicine for digestive and respiratory conditions. They are key to the plant's therapeutic profile and bioactivity.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanins are flavonoid compounds found in Red Centaury (Erythraea centaurium). They contribute to the plant's vibrant red color and possess antioxidant properties. These compounds may support cardiovascular health and reduce oxidative stress, making them important in the plant's medicinal profile.

10. Essential oil

The essential oil of Red Centaury (Erythraea centaurium) contains volatile compounds like thujone and camphor. These contribute to its aromatic properties and may support digestive and antimicrobial effects. The oil is typically extracted through steam distillation from the plant's flowering tops.
