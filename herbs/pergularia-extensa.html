What is Pergularia Extensa (Pergularia Extensa)?

Pergularia extensa, also known as Pergularia Extensa, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia, commonly used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Pergularia extensa is Pergularia extensa. This plant belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Liliopsida, order Asphodelales, family Acanthaceae, genus Pergularia, and species Pergularia extensa. It is commonly known as "Serpentine grass" in South Africa and "Serpent's tongue" in the Caribbean. The plant is also known by the synonyms Pergularia serpens and Pergularia caribaea.

Pergularia extensa is a low-growing perennial plant that typically reaches a height of 10-30 centimeters, with a sprawling or trailing growth habit.

Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a pointed tip and a slightly serrated margin, measuring approximately 2-5 centimeters in length. The flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, featuring five white to pale pink petals and a yellowish center, blooming from late spring to early summer. The root system consists of a fibrous network that spreads horizontally just beneath the soil surface, making it adaptable to various soil types.

The plant has a mild, slightly earthy scent, with leaves that are smooth and slightly hairy on the underside, and stems that are rigid yet flexible.
