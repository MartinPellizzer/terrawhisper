What is Pergularia Extensa (Pergularia Extensa)?

Pergularia Extensa, also known as Pergularia extensa, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa, commonly used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Pergularia extensa is a herbaceous plant characterized by its sprawling growth habit, with slender, branched stems that often root at the nodes, and small, opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

Pergularia extensa is native to the tropical regions of Africa, particularly found in countries such as Kenya, Tanzania, and Mozambique, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments like forests and along riverbanks.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The leaves, roots, and flowers of Pergularia extensa are used medicinally, with the leaves primarily applied topically for their anti-inflammatory properties and the roots prepared as a decoction to address digestive issues.

Pharmacological Properties

Therapeutic Uses and Indications

Preparation Methods and Forms
