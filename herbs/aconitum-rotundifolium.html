Aconitum rotundifolium

What's the taxonomical classification of Aconitum rotundifolium?

Aconitum rotundifolium belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, falling into the order Ranunculales. Within this order, the plant is a member of the family Ranunculaceae. Its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Aconitum and the species rotundifolium.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aconitum rotundifoliums has palmately lobed, circular leaves that are divided into several deep segments, typically measuring between 5 and 15 centimeters in diameter. The plant features a robust, erect stem that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters in optimal growing conditions. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of helmet-shaped flowers that vary in color from deep blue to violet. These zygomorphic flowers possess a distinctive hood-like uppermost sepal that protects the inner reproductive organs. The root system is characterized by a thick, tuberous rhizome that stores nutrients for the perennial plant.

What side effects this plant can have?

If the toxic compounds are not addressed immediately, the ultimate consequence of exposure is death.
