Callerya speciosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Callerya speciosa?

Callerya speciosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is situated under the order Fabales. It further falls into the family Fabaceae, which is characterized by its diverse group of flowering plants. Within this family, it is categorized under the genus Callerya, with its specific identification being the species speciosa.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Callerya speciosa contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, tannins, and terpenoids that contribute to its potential therapeutic properties. What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, and various inflammatory conditions through its medicinal properties. The leaves and flowers are often applied topically to soothe irritation or promote the healing of minor abrasions. Some traditional practices utilize extracts to combat bacterial or fungal issues affecting the skin. Beyond surface treatments, it is occasionally valued for its potential to reduce swelling and pain in localized areas. These applications rely on the plant's natural bioactive compounds to provide relief.
