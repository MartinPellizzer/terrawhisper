Sageretia pycnophylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is positioned under Equisetopsida, falling into the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Rosales and the family Rhamnaceae. Ultimately, this plant is identified by its genus, Sageretia, and its specific species designation, pycnophyllia.

Sageretia pycnophylla is prepared primarily as a decoction or an infusion made from its dried leaves to extract medicinal compounds. This paste is applied directly to the skin to soothe localized irritation. Some practitioners also utilize the plant in concentrated tinctures for more potent long-term storage.

Sageretia pycnophylla can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or gastrointestinal distress if ingested due to its concentrated secondary metabolites. While specific clinical studies on humans are limited, many plants in similar genera contain compounds that act as irritants to sensitive mucous membranes. Contact with the leaves or sap may lead to localized redness or itching in individuals with topical sensitivities. If swallowed, the plant could potentially trigger nausea or abdominal discomfort. It is essential to handle the specimen with caution to avoid accidental exposure to the skin or eyes.
