Castanopsis hystrix

What's the taxonomical classification of Castanopsis hystrix?

Castanopsis hystrix belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Fagales. As a member of the family Fagaceae, this plant is specifically identified within the genus Castanopsis, with its unique biological designation being the species hystrix.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Castanopsis hystrix has large, coriaceous, elliptical leaves that are arranged oppositely along the woody stems and feature prominently serrated margins. The foliage is characterized by a dark green, lustrous upper surface and a slightly paler underside. These leaves are typically densely packed, contributing to a lush and compact canopy structure. The plant also produces small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into distinct woody fruits. Its overall growth habit presents a robust, shrub-like appearance with sturdy branching patterns.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical regions of West Africa, specifically found in countries such as Nigeria, Cameroon, Gabon, and the Democratic Republic of the Congo. The species prefers shaded environments under the dense forest canopy rather than open grasslands. Its distribution is largely restricted to these humid equatorial belts due to its specific moisture requirements.
