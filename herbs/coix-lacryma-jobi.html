Coix lacryma-jobi

What's the taxonomical classification of Coix lacryma-jobi?

Coix lacryma-jobi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Poales. As a member of the family Poaceae, this plant is further categorized into the genus Coix, with its specific designation being the species lacrymama-jobi.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Coix lacrymata-jobi contains bioactive compounds such as coixol, coixolide, and various polysaccharides that provide anti-inflammatory, diuretic, and immunomodulatory effects. These medicinal properties are primarily attributed to the presence of unique triterpenoids and specialized carbohydrates found within the seeds. Researchers have identified these constituents as potential agents for managing metabolic disorders and supporting immune function. Additionally, the plant's phytosterols contribute to its ability to regulate cholesterol levels in certain biological models. These diverse chemical profiles make the plant a significant subject in traditional and modern pharmacological studies.

What are the therapeutic actions of Coix lacryma-jobi?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat inflammation, skin disorders, urinary tract infections, and various digestive issues. Its seeds possess diuretic properties that help alleviate swelling and kidney-related discomfort. In traditional medicine, the plant is frequently applied topically to manage skin conditions such as eczema or acne. Some practitioners also use it to help reduce fever and address respiratory ailments. Regular consumption may assist in managing metabolic functions and supporting overall kidney health.
