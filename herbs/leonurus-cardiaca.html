Bioactive Constituents

Leonurus cardiaca glycosides are a group of bioactive compounds found in the herb commonly known as heart herb or red clover.

These glycosides, including cardiac glycosides and cardiotonic glycosides, are known for their potent effects on the cardiovascular system. Cardiac glycosides, in particular, work by inhibiting the sodium-potassium ATPase pump in cardiac muscle cells, leading to increased intracellular sodium and calcium levels, which enhance myocardial contractility.

This makes them valuable in the treatment of heart failure and arrhythmias. In addition to glycosides, Leonurus cardiaca also contains other beneficial constituents such as glutamic acid, an amino acid that plays a role in nerve function and energy production. Together, these compounds contribute to the herb's reputation as a traditional remedy for cardiovascular health.

The combination of glycosides and other nutrients in Leonurus cardiaca makes it a subject of ongoing research for its potential therapeutic applications in modern medicine.

Side Effects of leonurus cardiaca

Leonurus cardiaca induces vomiting, which can be a severe and distressing side effect, particularly when consumed in high doses or by individuals with sensitive stomachs.

This herb is also known to cause skin irritation, manifesting as redness, itching, or rashes upon contact, making it potentially hazardous for those with sensitive skin. Additionally, Leonurus cardiaca may lead to heart palpitations and induce a rapid heartbeat, which can be alarming and may require medical attention, especially for individuals with pre-existing cardiovascular conditions.

The herb can also result in nausea and fatigue, contributing to a general feeling of weakness and discomfort. Furthermore, it may cause breathing difficulty, sweating, and diarrhea, all of which can be serious symptoms that necessitate immediate medical evaluation.

These side effects highlight the importance of using Leonurus cardiaca under professional supervision and cautioning against its use without proper knowledge of its potential risks.
