What to know about Leonurus cardiaca before using it medicinally

Leonurus cardiaca, commonly known as motherwort, is a perennial herb used primarily in the treatment of anxiety and stress-related disorders, as well as in managing symptoms of menopausal hot flashes and irregular heartbeats.

The herb is valued for its adaptogenic properties, which enable it to help the body adapt to stress and promote overall well-being. The therapeutic properties of Leonurus cardiaca include its ability to act as a sedative, a nervine tonic, and an antispasmodic agent, making it beneficial for individuals experiencing insomnia, restlessness, and muscle spasms. The bioactive compounds present in this herb include iridoid glycosides, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

A popular herbal preparation made with Leonurus cardiaca is a tea or tincture, often used as a calming agent to alleviate symptoms of anxiety and promote a restful night's sleep.
