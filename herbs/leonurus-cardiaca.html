Heartworts

Leonurus cardiaca

Heartworts (Leonurus cardiaca) is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, known for its historical use in traditional medicine to support cardiovascular health and improve circulation.

Botanical Identification

Leonurus cardiaca, commonly known as heartworts, is a perennial herb in the Lamiaceae family. Native to Europe and Asia, it has regional names like "devil's bit" and "heart herb." The plant features heart-shaped leaves, purple flowers, and a fibrous root system. It is also referred to as "cardiaca" in some herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Lamiales
  • Order: Lamiales
  • Family: Lamiaceae
  • Genus: Leonurus
  • Species: Leonurus cardiaca

Active Compounds

Heartworts contain key active compounds such as flavonoids, glycosides, and coumarins, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to support various therapeutic effects, including anti-inflammatory and circulatory benefits. Their combined action enhances the herb's efficacy in traditional herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Glycoside
  • Coumarin

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory conditions.

Infusion: Used for mild calming effects and sleep disorders.

Tincture: Used for nervous system support and anxiety relief.

Safety Profile

Heartworts, or Leonurus cardiaca, are generally considered safe when used in moderate amounts. However, they should be avoided during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to potential harm to the fetus or infant. Individuals with known allergies to related plants should also exercise caution.
