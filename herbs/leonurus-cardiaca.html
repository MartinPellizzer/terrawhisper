What to know about Leonurus cardiaca (motherwort) before using it medicinally

Motherwort, scientifically known as Leonurus cardiaca, is an herb that has been revered for its impressive health properties, which include reducing inflammation and improving cardiovascular function.

In culinary traditions, motherwort is often used in soups, teas, and salads to add a slightly bitter flavor and aroma. As a horticultural gem, motherwort is a hardy perennial that can thrive in moist environments with partial shade. Botanically speaking, motherwort belongs to the mint family Lamiaceae, characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and tubular flowers.

With ancient references dating back to traditional Chinese medicine and European herbalism, motherwort has been a prized ingredient for centuries, used to treat a range of ailments from anxiety to menstrual cramps.
