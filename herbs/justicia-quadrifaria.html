Justicia quadrifaria

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Justicia quadrifaria has opposite, decussate leaves that are lanceolate to oblong in shape with entire margins and a distinctively short petiole. The foliage typically displays a vibrant green color with prominent pinnate venation extending to the edges. Its stems are herbaceous and square in cross-section, which is a characteristic feature of the Acanthaceae family. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that emerge from the leaf axils in a structured arrangement. These inflorescences are often supported by specialized bracts that protect the developing floral buds.
