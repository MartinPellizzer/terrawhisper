What's the taxonomical classification of Anemone rivularis?

Anemone rivularis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Ranunculales. Furthermore, it is a member of the family Ranunculaceae, falling under the genus Anemone, and is specifically identified by the species name rivularis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Anemone rivularis has basal leaves that are deeply divided into three to five narrow, linear segments and terminal flowers featuring five to seven white petals with yellow centers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 25 centimeters, supported by a slender, hairy stem. Its foliage consists of both basal leaves and smaller, less complex leaves located further up the stem. The flowers are characterized by their delicate, star-like appearance and occur during the late spring or early summer months. This species often develops from a creeping rhizome that allows it to spread through moist, riparian habitats.
