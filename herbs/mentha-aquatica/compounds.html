Water Mint (Mentha aquatica) Compounds

The primary compounds in Water Mint include essential oil, which contains menthol and menthone, and flavonoids like quercetin. Phenolic acids such as rosmarinic acid and terpenoids contribute to its aroma and medicinal properties. Tannins and coumarin provide astringent and antispasmodic effects, while glycosides and saponins support digestive health. Alkaloids and anthocyanins add additional therapeutic benefits.

Water Mint (Mentha aquatica) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Water Mint (Mentha aquatica) contains volatile compounds like menthol and menthone. These compounds contribute to its characteristic cooling effect and are used in traditional medicine for digestive and respiratory support. The oil is extracted through steam distillation and is valued for its therapeutic properties in herbal formulations.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Water Mint (Mentha aquatica) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help reduce oxidative stress and support immune function. They are present in the leaves and stems, enhancing the plant's therapeutic value in herbal medicine. Flavonoids also aid in protecting cells from damage caused by free radicals.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Water Mint (Mentha aquatica). It contributes to the plant's antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. This compound is often found in high concentrations in the leaves and stems. Phenolic acid supports the plant's role in traditional herbal medicine for digestive and respiratory ailments.

4. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Water Mint (Mentha aquatica). They contribute to the plant's aromatic properties and have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. These compounds are often responsible for the therapeutic benefits associated with Water Mint in herbal medicine.

5. Tannin

Tannin in Water Mint (Mentha aquatica) contributes to its astringent properties, aiding in digestive health and reducing inflammation. It also supports wound healing and may help in managing symptoms of gastrointestinal disorders. Tannins are found in various parts of the plant, including leaves and stems.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Water Mint (Mentha aquatica). It contributes to the plant's characteristic aroma and has antispasmodic and mild sedative properties. Coumarin may help ease digestive discomfort and promote relaxation, making it valuable in herbal remedies.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Water Mint (Mentha aquatica). It contributes to the plant's therapeutic effects, such as anti-inflammatory and mild sedative properties. Glycosides are often responsible for the plant's aromatic qualities and are commonly used in herbal formulations.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Water Mint (Mentha aquatica). It contributes to the plant's antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Saponins help in reducing swelling and fighting infections, making Water Mint useful in traditional herbal remedies.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Water Mint (Mentha aquatica). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, though its specific therapeutic effects are less studied compared to other constituents like menthol. Alkaloids in Water Mint may support digestive and respiratory health, though more research is needed to confirm their role.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a pigment found in Water Mint (Mentha aquatica) that contributes to its antioxidant properties. It helps neutralize free radicals, supporting overall health. This compound is particularly valued in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and protective effects.
