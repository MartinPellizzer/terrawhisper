10 Health Benefits Of Mentha Aquatica (Water Mint)

Mentha aquatica, also known as water mint, has health benefits such as reducing inflammation, relieving digestive issues, and alleviating respiratory problems.

The plant's medicinal properties, including its essential oils and flavonoids, contribute to these benefits. For example, water mint can ease bloating and nausea, allowing individuals to manage symptoms of IBS and enjoy a better quality of life.

Its anti-inflammatory effects also help soothe skin irritations and relieve headaches, promoting overall well-being.

This article explains in details the 10 best health benefits of Mentha aquatica.

1. Reduces inflammation

Mentha aquatica reduces inflammation because of its potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

The essential oils present in water mint, particularly menthol and menthone, have been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines and enzymes, thereby reducing inflammation.

Additionally, water mint's flavonoids and rosmarinic acid have been found to have anti-inflammatory effects, making it a valuable natural remedy for alleviating symptoms associated with conditions such as arthritis, gout, and other inflammatory disorders.

2. Relieves indigestion

Mentha aquatica relieves indigestion because of its natural antispasmodic and carminative properties.

The menthol and menthone compounds present in the leaves and stems of water mint help to relax the muscles in the digestive tract, reducing spasms and cramps that can cause indigestion.

Additionally, water mint's carminative properties help to ease flatulence and bloating, further alleviating discomfort associated with indigestion.

3. Soothes stomachaches

Mentha aquatica soothes stomachaches because of its calming and anti-inflammatory properties.

The menthol content in water mint helps to ease digestive discomfort and reduce inflammation in the stomach, providing relief from symptoms like bloating, cramps, and nausea.

Its natural anti-spasmodic properties also help to relax the stomach muscles, further reducing discomfort and promoting a sense of calm.

4. Combats nausea

Mentha aquatica combats nausea because of its unique combination of chemical compounds.

The essential oils present in water mint, particularly menthol and menthone, have natural anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties that help to soothe the stomach and alleviate nausea.

Additionally, the aromatic properties of water mint can help to calm the digestive system, reducing symptoms of nausea and vomiting.

5. Eases migraines

Mentha aquatica eases migraines because of its unique combination of bioactive compounds, including rosmarinic acid, flavonoids, and terpenes.

These molecules work synergistically to reduce inflammation, relax blood vessels, and calm the nervous system, thereby mitigating migraine symptoms.

Water mint's analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties also help to ease headache pain, while its calming effects promote relaxation and reduce stress, two common migraine triggers.

6. Treats fever

Mentha aquatica treats fever because of its inherent antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

The leaves and stems of the plant contain essential oils, including menthol and menthone, which have been shown to have antipyretic effects.

When consumed as a tea or used topically, these compounds help to reduce fever by lowering the body's temperature and alleviating symptoms such as sweating and chills.

7. Relaxes muscles

Mentha aquatica relaxes muscles because of its unique combination of bioactive compounds, particularly flavonoids and phenolic acids.

These compounds have been shown to possess potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help to reduce muscle tension and spasms.

When ingested or applied topically, water mint's bioactives can also help to calm the nervous system, promoting a sense of relaxation and reducing muscle cramps and fatigue.

8. Sedates anxiety

Mentha aquatica sedates anxiety because it contains a unique combination of compounds that work synergistically to calm the nervous system.

The herb's essential oils, particularly linalool, have been shown to have a sedative effect on the brain, reducing the production of stress hormones like cortisol and adrenaline.

Additionally, water mint's cooling and calming properties can soothe the digestive system, further reducing anxiety and promoting overall relaxation.

9. Lowers blood pressure

Mentha aquatica lowers blood pressure because of its unique combination of bioactive compounds, including rosmarinic acid and flavonoids.

These compounds have been shown to relax blood vessels, improve blood flow, and reduce systemic vascular resistance, all of which contribute to a decrease in blood pressure.

Additionally, water mint's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties may also help to protect against hypertension-related damage to blood vessels.

10. Inhibits bacterial growth

Mentha aquatica inhibits bacterial growth because of its unique chemical composition.

The leaves of this plant contain a high concentration of phenolic compounds, which have been shown to exhibit antibacterial properties. Specifically, the flavonoids and phenolic acids present in water mint have been found to disrupt the bacterial cell membrane, preventing the growth and multiplication of bacteria.

This natural inhibitory effect makes water mint a valuable resource for natural remedies and sustainable pest control.
