What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Curcuma Aeruginosa (Indian Turmeric)?

Curcuma aeruginosa, also known as Indian turmeric, has health benefits such as reducing inflammation and treating various skin conditions.

Its medicinal constituents include curcumenol, curdione, and geraniin, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties. Indian turmeric is prepared in various medicinal preparations, including powders, capsules, and teas, which can be used to treat arthritis, wounds, and other ailments. However, long-term use may cause gastrointestinal side effects such as nausea and diarrhea.

Pregnant and breastfeeding women should consult a healthcare professional before using Indian turmeric, as its effects on fetal development and infant health are unknown.

What are the active constituents of Curcuma aeruginosa?

Curcuma aeruginosa, also known as Indian turmeric, has active constituents such as curcumin, demethoxycurcumin, and bisdemethoxycurcumin, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Curcumin, in particular, has been extensively studied for its potential health benefits, including reducing inflammation and improving symptoms of various diseases, making it a valuable ingredient in traditional medicine.

What are the possible side effect of using Curcuma aeruginosa improperly?

Improper use of Curcuma aeruginosa, also known as indian turmeric, increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as liver damage, stomach upset, and bleeding disorders.

High doses can lead to interactions with medications, including blood thinners, and may worsen conditions like gallstones, kidney stones, and certain types of cancer.

Additionally, some individuals may experience allergic reactions, such as skin rashes and itching.
