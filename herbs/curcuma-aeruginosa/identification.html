Botanical Identification of Blue Ginger (Curcuma aeruginosa)

To identify Blue Ginger (Curcuma aeruginosa) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Blue Ginger (Curcuma aeruginosa) Monograph

Scientific Classification

Blue Ginger, or Curcuma aeruginosa, belongs to the plant kingdom, specifically within the division of flowering plants, class of monocots, order of Zingiberales, family of ginger family, genus Curcuma, and species Curcuma aeruginosa. This classification places it among other economically important plants like turmeric and ginger.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Blue Ginger is Curcuma aeruginosa, first described by the botanist Rumphius in 1750. It is also known by several synonyms, including Curcuma zanthorrhiza and Curcuma heyneana. Common names include Blue Ginger, Blue Turmeric, and Purple Yam. It is widely recognized across various regions and has historical significance in traditional medicine.

Morphological Characteristics

Blue Ginger, or Curcuma aeruginosa, is a rhizomatous plant with a compact growth habit. Its root system consists of thick, fleshy rhizomes that store nutrients. The stem is short and underground, supporting the plant's leafy structure. Leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a smooth, waxy texture. The inflorescence is a spike with clustered, pale blue flowers. The fruit is a capsule containing numerous small, dark seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Blue Ginger, or Curcuma aeruginosa, can be distinguished by its distinctive blue or purple rhizomes, which contrast with the yellow rhizomes of turmeric. It has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy underside and pale yellow flowers. These features help differentiate it from similar Curcuma species. Its unique coloration and leaf characteristics are key identifiers in botanical recognition.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Blue Ginger (Curcuma aeruginosa) is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and India. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and warmth. It prefers well-drained, fertile soils and is typically found at low to moderate altitudes, usually below 1,000 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Blue Ginger (Curcuma aeruginosa) typically blooms from April to June, producing purple flowers. Seed formation occurs in the subsequent months, from July to September. For identification purposes, the plant is harvested when the leaves begin to yellow, usually between October and November.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Curcuma aeruginosa belongs to the genus Curcuma within the ginger family, Zingiberaceae. It is closely related to species like Curcuma longa and Curcuma aromatica. These species share similar morphological and chemical traits, including the presence of curcuminoids.
