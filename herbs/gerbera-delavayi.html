Gerbera delavayi

What's the taxonomical classification of Gerbera delavayi?

Gerbera delavayi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical structure of botanical classification, it falls under the order Asterales and is a member of the family Asteraceae. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its genus, Gerbera, and its specific species designation, delavayi.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gerbera delavayi has a rosette-forming growth habit characterized by long, slender leaf petioles and large, deeply lobed, pinnatisect leaves that emerge from a central base. The foliage is typically dark green and possesses a slightly hairy or pubescent texture on both the upper and lower surfaces. From this basal rosette, tall, leafless flowering stalks called scapes rise to support the inflorescences. Each flower head is a composite structure consisting of numerous bright ray florets surrounding a central cluster of disc florets. These scape stems are often covered in fine hairs, providing structural support for the heavy terminal blooms.
