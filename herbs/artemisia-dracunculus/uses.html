Tarragon (Artemisia dracunculus) Uses

Tarragon is used to treat headaches, migraines, and motion sickness due to its calming and antispasmodic properties. It helps relieve gastrointestinal gas, cramps, and indigestion by promoting digestion and reducing inflammation. It may also assist in expelling intestinal parasites and easing gastrointestinal distress.

Tarragon (Artemisia dracunculus) Monograph

1. Headache

Tarragon is used to alleviate headaches due to its mild analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties. It can be brewed into a tea or applied topically as a compress. The herb helps ease tension and reduce pain, making it a simple remedy for mild headaches in herbal medicine practices.

2. Migraine

Tarragon is used to alleviate migraine symptoms due to its antispasmodic and analgesic properties. It helps reduce tension and inflammation in blood vessels, easing headache intensity. Often prepared as a tea, it supports digestive health, which can indirectly manage migraine triggers. Its calming effects may also help reduce stress-related migraines.

3. Migraines

Tarragon has been traditionally used to alleviate migraine symptoms due to its calming and anti-inflammatory properties. It may help reduce headache intensity and frequency when consumed as a tea or tincture. Its essential oils are believed to support vascular health, which can contribute to migraine prevention. Always consult a healthcare professional before using tarragon for medicinal purposes.

4. Tinnitus

Tarragon has been traditionally used to support ear health and may help alleviate symptoms of tinnitus. Its anti-inflammatory and circulatory properties are believed to reduce inner ear stress and improve blood flow, potentially easing ringing or buzzing sounds. It is often used in herbal formulations for auditory support.

5. Motion sickness

Tarragon is used to alleviate motion sickness due to its calming effects on the digestive system. It helps reduce nausea and vomiting by regulating stomach activity. Often prepared as a tea, it is recommended for travel or sea sickness. Its essential oils also support overall digestive health.

6. Gastrointestinal gas

Tarragon is used to alleviate gastrointestinal gas by promoting digestion and reducing bloating. Its volatile oils help relax digestive muscles, easing discomfort. It is often recommended for mild digestive issues and can be consumed as a tea or added to meals.

7. Gastrointestinal cramps

Tarragon contains compounds that help relax gastrointestinal muscles, easing cramps. It is often used in herbal remedies for digestive discomfort. Its anti-spasmodic properties make it effective for relieving stomach cramps and bloating. It can be consumed as a tea or tincture for targeted relief.

8. Intestinal parasites

Tarragon has been traditionally used to expel intestinal parasites due to its aromatic compounds that disrupt parasitic life cycles. It is often included in herbal formulations for digestive health and parasite removal. Its antiparasitic properties support cleansing the gastrointestinal tract naturally.

9. Gastrointestinal distress

Tarragon is used to alleviate gastrointestinal distress by reducing inflammation and soothing the digestive tract. Its compounds help ease spasms and promote digestion, making it effective for conditions like indigestion and colic. It is typically prepared as a tea or tincture for internal use.

10. Indigestion

Tarragon is used to alleviate indigestion by stimulating digestive enzymes and reducing bloating. Its aromatic compounds help ease stomach discomfort and promote smoother digestion. It is often recommended as a herbal remedy for mild digestive upset and can be consumed as a tea or added to meals.
