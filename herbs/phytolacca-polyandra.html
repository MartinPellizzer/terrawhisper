Phytolacca polyandra

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phytolacca polyandra has erect, herbaceous stems that reach heights of up to one meter and bear alternate, lanceolate to oblong leaves with entire margins. The foliage is typically green and lacks petioles, attaching directly to the stem. Its inflorescence consists of many-flowered racemes that emerge from the leaf axils. Each flower is small and possesses several stamens, which is a defining characteristic of the species. The plant eventually produces dark, fleshy berries that grow in dense clusters along the stem.

    What are the herbal preparations of Phytolacca polyandra?

    Phytolacca polyandra is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice using the roots and leaves of the plant. The roots are often boiled in water to create a concentrated decoction for internal use in traditional medicine. Leaves may be steeped in hot water to form an infusion for milder applications. For external ailments, the plant material is crushed into a paste to serve as a poultice applied directly to the skin. These diverse preparation methods allow the plant's active compounds to be delivered in various therapeutic concentrations.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Phytolacca polyandra can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, and potential systemic toxicity if ingested or touched. The plant contains various saponins and toxic alkaloids that act as potent irritants to the human body. Ingesting even small amounts may lead to intense vomiting, abdominal cramping, and diarrhea. Direct contact with the sap can result in redness, swelling, or blistering of the skin. Because the chemical composition is highly reactive, careful handling is necessary to avoid accidental poisoning.
