Phyllagathis longiradiosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phyllagathis longiradiosa has a prostrate growth habit characterized by small, creeping stems that form dense, mat-like cushions. The leaves are tiny, ovate to elliptic in shape, and are closely imbricated along the stems. These leaves possess a distinctively long petiole or base structure that distinguishes it from similar species. The plant's foliage typically exhibits a deep green color with subtle textures on the surface. This compact morphology allows the plant to thrive in specialized ecological niches such as rocky crevices or mossy substrates.
