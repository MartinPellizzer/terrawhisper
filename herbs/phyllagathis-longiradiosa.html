What's the taxonomical classification of Phyllagathis longiradiosa?

Phyllagathis longiradiosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Myrtales. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the family Melastommataceae, which contains the genus Phyllagathis, ultimately identifying the specific plant as the species longiradiosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phyllagathis longiradiosa has a prostrate growth habit characterized by small, creeping stems that form dense, mat-like cushions.

The leaves are tiny, ovate to elliptic in shape, and are closely imbricated along the stems. These leaves possess a distinctively long petiole or base structure that distinguishes it from similar species. The plant's foliage typically exhibits a deep green color with subtle textures on the surface.

This compact morphology allows the plant to thrive in specialized ecological niches such as rocky crevices or mossy substrates.
