What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Ageratum Conyzoides (Blueweed)?

Ageratum conyzoides, also known as blueweed, has health benefits such as reducing inflammation and fever.

The herb contains medicinal constituents like flavonoids, saponins, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties. The dried leaves and flowers can be prepared as a tea or infusion for medicinal use. However, excessive consumption may cause stomach irritation and allergic reactions in some individuals.

It is essential to consult a healthcare professional before using blueweed for medicinal purposes, especially for pregnant or breastfeeding women and individuals with allergies or underlying medical conditions.

What are the possible side effect of using Ageratum conyzoides improperly?

Improper use of Ageratum conyzoides, also known as blueweed, increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal pain due to its toxic alkaloids.

In severe cases, it can cause headaches, dizziness, and allergic reactions, particularly skin irritation and respiratory problems. Long-term consumption may also lead to liver and kidney damage.

It is essential to handle this plant with care and use it only under the guidance of a medical professional.
