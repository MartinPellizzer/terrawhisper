Begonia handelii

What's the taxonomical classification of Begonia handelii?

Begonia handelii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Cucurbitalees. It is a member of the family Begoniaceae, falling specifically into the genus Begonia, with the formal species designation being handelii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Begonia handelii has asymmetrical, ovate leaves that are typically bright green with prominent, reddish-tinted veins and slightly serrated margins. The plant features a succulent, fleshy stem that supports its spreading growth habit. Its inflorescences consist of numerous small, delicate flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep rose. These blossoms emerge from the leaf axils on slender, reddish pedicels. The overall structure is characterized by its rhizomatous base which allows for efficient nutrient storage and spreading.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires bright, indirect sunlight and well-draining, organic-rich soil kept consistently moist but never waterlogged to thrive.

You should maintain a warm environment with temperatures between 60 and 75 degrees Fahrenheit for optimal growth. High humidity is essential, so placing the pot on a pebble tray or using a humidifier helps prevent leaf crisping. Avoid overhead watering to prevent fungal issues on the delicate foliage.

During the active growing season, provide a balanced liquid fertilizer every few weeks to encourage lush growth.
