10 Possible Side Effects Of Carthamus Tinctorius (Safflower)

Carthamus tinctorius has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, allergic reactions, and skin irritation.

These side effects are usually caused by an excessive intake of the herb or its individual components.

For example, consuming high amounts of its essential oil can lead to digestive problems, such as diarrhea or stomach pain, which can severely limit daily activities.

Carthamus tinctorius causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

This is due to the presence of sesquiterpene lactones, a type of compound found in its flowers and leaves. These compounds can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and redness. Additionally, some people may experience respiratory issues like coughing or sneezing due to the plant's pollen.

The allergic reactions can range from mild to severe, depending on the individual's sensitivity to the compound.
