Musa coccinea

What's the taxonomical classification of Musa coccinea?

Musa coccinea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Zingiberales and is a member of the family Musaceae. Ultimately, this plant is defined by its genus, Musa, and its specific species designation, coccinea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Musa coccinea has large, oblong, bright green leaves that emerge from a thick, fleshy pseudostem composed of tightly overlapping leaf bases. The plant features a robust, upright growth habit typical of the Musaceae family, with a central stem that can reach significant heights. Its most distinctive morphological trait is the inflorescence, which produces vibrant, deep red or scarlet bracts that protect the developing flowers. These large, waxy bracts eventually peel back to reveal small, pale blossoms arranged in dense clusters. The root system consists of a thick underground rhizome that provides stability and stores nutrients for the plant.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, specifically concentrated in the Malay Peninsula, Sumatra, and parts of Borneo. Its range is limited to these specific equatorial zones due to its reliance on stable, warm temperatures.
