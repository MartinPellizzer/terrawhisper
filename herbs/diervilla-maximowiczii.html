What is Diervilla maximowiczii?

Diervilla maximowiczii is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia, belonging to the family Caprifoliaceae.

It grows 1-3 meters tall, with a spread of 1-2 meters, and has a multi-stemmed, woody habit. The leaves are lance-shaped, 3-12 centimeters long, and 1-4 centimeters wide, with serrated margins and a cuneate base. The flowers are yellow, 2-3 centimeters in diameter, and arranged in corymbose panicles; they bloom in late spring to early summer.

Diervilla maximowiczii is cultivated for its ornamental value and tolerance of moist soils.
