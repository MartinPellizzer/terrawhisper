Lagerstroemia speciosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Lagerstroemia speciosa?

Lagerstroemia speciosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Myrtales. This plant is a member of the family Lythraceae, falling within the genus Lagerstroemia, and is specifically identified by its species name, speciosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lagerstroemia speciosa has a large deciduous tree structure characterized by a spreading crown, smooth grayish bark that peels in thin flakes, and large elliptical leaves measuring up to 30 centimeters in length. The foliage is typically dark green and glossy, often turning shades of red or orange before shedding during the dry season. Its most striking morphological feature is the large terminal panicles of showy flowers that emerge in vibrant shades of pink, purple, or lavender. Each individual flower possesses six to eight crinkled petals and prominent yellow stamens that provide a sharp visual contrast. The fruit consists of woody, ovoid capsules that split open to release numerous small, winged seeds designed for wind dispersal.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, including countries such as Thailand, Vietnam, Malaysia, Indonesia, and the Philippines. It thrives primarily in moist, lowland environments where temperatures remain consistently warm throughout the year. While its natural range is concentrated in these Asian territories, it has been widely introduced to other tropical zones around the world for ornamental purposes. In many of these new locations, it flourishes in gardens and parks due to its ability to adapt to humid climates. Its distribution is strictly limited by its inability to survive in regions that experience freezing temperatures.

What are the pharmacological activities of Lagerstroemia speciosa?

This plant has anti-diabetic, anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, hypoglycaemic, and α-glucosidase inhibitory activities. Its potent hypoglycaemic properties help regulate blood glucose levels, making it a significant subject for managing diabetes. The plant specifically exhibits α-glucosidase inhibitory activity, which prevents the breakdown of carbohydrates into sugar. Furthermore, its antioxidant capacity protects cells from oxidative stress caused by free radicals. These combined effects are complemented by strong anti-inflammatory actions that reduce systemic inflammation.

As discussed in a study, the methanolic extract of Lagerstroemia speciosa is used as medicinal food for the treatment of diabetes and other ailments. The extract was evaluated for its effect on insulin stimulated glucose uptake in 3T3-L1 cells, erythrocyte sorbitol accumulation, and protein glycation. When combined with Allium sativum extract in a 1:1 ratio, the formulation produced a synergistic and dose dependent increase in glucose uptake in 3T3 adipocyte cell lines. A similar effect was observed in the inhibition of sorbitol accumulation and protein glycation tests. The study concluded that the formulation possessing the Lagerstroemia speciosa extract has potent anti-diabetic activity and anti-oxidant effects.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds corosolic acid, berberine, betulinic acid, oleanolic acid, and ursolic acid. These specific phytochemicals contribute to a diverse range of biological activities within the human body. Berberine is often recognized for its potential to regulate glucose metabolism and support cardiovascular health. Meanwhile, the triterpenoids including betulinic acid, oleanolic acid, and ursolic acid are studied for their anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Corosolic acid further enhances the plant's therapeutic profile by assisting in blood sugar management. Together, these substances make the plant a significant subject in pharmacological research.

As discussed in a study, corosolic acid (CRS) is a naturally occurring pentacyclic triterpenoid primarily isolated from Lagerstroemia speciosa. This compound has attracted growing interest because of its promising anticancer properties. The literature search for this review covered studies published up to April 2025 across major scientific databases. CRS exerts broad-spectrum anticancer activity by inducing apoptosis, autophagy, ferroptosis, and cytotoxicity. It also works by inhibiting tumor cell proliferation, metastasis, and survival signaling pathways such as PI3K/Akt/mTOR, NF-κB, STAT3, and YAP/TAZ.

What side effects this plant can have?

It is essential to exercise caution because these side effects can vary in intensity depending on the dosage used.
