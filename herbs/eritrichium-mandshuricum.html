Eritrichium mandshuricum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eritrichium mandshuricum has a low-growing, cushion-forming habit characterized by dense, compact mats of small, hairy leaves and bright blue, funnel-shaped flowers. The foliage consists of tiny, ovate to elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery pubescence to conserve moisture. These leaves are tightly packed along short, woody stems that form a sturdy, ground-hugging mound. The inflorescences feature small, five-lobed corollas that emerge from the leaf axils, typically displaying a vibrant sky-blue hue. This compact morphology is an evolutionary adaptation to the harsh, high-altitude rocky environments where the species typically thrives.
