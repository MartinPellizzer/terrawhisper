Scutellaria orthocalyx

What's the taxonomical classification of Scutellaria orthocalyx?

Scutelariia orthocalyx belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Lamiales and is a member of the family Lamiaceae. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Scutellaria and the species orthocalyx.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Scutellaria orthocalyx has a woody, perennial habit characterized by erect stems and opposite, decussate leaves that are typically lanceolate to elliptic in shape.

The foliage often features entire or slightly serrated margins and a texture that can range from glabrous to pubescent depending on the specific environment. Its reproductive structure consists of zygomorphic flowers housed within a tubular calyx that is notably straight or orthotropic. The corolla is bilabiate, providing a distinct platform for pollinators, while the stamens are positioned according to the characteristic arrangement of the Lamiaceae family.

These morphological traits collectively define its identity within its native habitat.
