What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Scutellaria orthocalyx has a woody, perennial habit characterized by erect stems and opposite, decussate leaves that are typically lanceolate to elliptic in shape. Its reproductive structure consists of zygomorphic flowers housed within a tubular calyx that is notably straight or orthotropic. The corolla is bilabiate, providing a distinct platform for pollinators, while the stamens are positioned according to the characteristic arrangement of the Lamiaceae family.
