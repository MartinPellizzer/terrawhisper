Medicinal Parts

Hydrastis canadensis root, commonly known as goldenseal, has been historically valued for its medicinal properties.

The root contains alkaloids such as berberine, which contribute to its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and astringent effects. Traditionally, it has been used to treat digestive issues, including indigestion and diarrhea, due to its ability to stimulate bile production. In modern herbal medicine, the root is often employed for its potential to support the immune system and promote healing of mucous membranes.

However, its use is sometimes limited due to concerns about long-term safety and potential interactions with certain medications.
