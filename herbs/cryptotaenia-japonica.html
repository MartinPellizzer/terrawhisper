Cryptotaenia japonica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cryptotaenia japonica has a perennial growth habit characterized by a rhizomatous root system and compound leaves consisting of multiple leaflets. The foliage features small, ovate to elliptical leaflets with serrated margins and a distinctively textured surface. These leaves are arranged in an alternate pattern along the stems, which remain relatively low to the ground. During its flowering stage, the plant produces small, inconspicuous blossoms that emerge from the leaf axils. The overall structure is compact, making it well-suited for shaded forest floor environments.

      Cryptotaenia japonica can cause severe allergic reactions, skin irritation, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested or touched. It is essential to handle this species with caution to avoid these adverse physiological responses.
