What to know about borage before using it medicinally

Borage, scientifically known as Borago officinalis, is a versatile herb that offers numerous health benefits.

It is rich in essential fatty acids, particularly gamma-linolenic acid (GLA), which has been shown to reduce inflammation and improve health conditions such as rheumatoid arthritis and eczema. In addition to its health benefits, borage is also a popular ingredient in culinary applications. Its delicate, cucumber-like flavor makes it a great addition to salads, soups, and beverages.

In horticulture, borage is known for its attractive, bright blue flowers and hairy leaves. It is easy to grow and thrives in well-drained soil, often self-seeding and returning year after year. Botanically, borage is a member of the Boraginaceae family and is native to the Mediterranean region. Historically, borage has been used for medicinal purposes for centuries, with references to its use dating back to ancient Greece.

It was believed to have cooling and soothing properties, and was used to treat a variety of ailments, including fever and depression.

What are the side effects of borage when used improperly?

When used improperly, borage increases the chances of experiencing side effects, such as digestive issues, skin irritation, and potential liver damage.

Consuming borage in large amounts or over an extended period can lead to stomach discomfort, nausea, and diarrhea. Topical application of borage oil or extract may cause skin irritation and rashes, particularly in individuals with sensitive skin.

Moreover, borage contains pyrrolizidine alkaloids (PAs), which can cause liver damage and other health problems when ingested in high concentrations or over time. PAs can also negatively interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and anticonvulsants, increasing the risk of adverse effects. Pregnant women should avoid using borage, as it may stimulate menstruation and potentially lead to miscarriage.

Therefore, it is crucial to consult a healthcare professional before incorporating borage into a medicinal regimen and strictly follow recommended dosages to minimize the risk of side effects.
