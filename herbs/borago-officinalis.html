Borage

Borago officinalis

Borage (Borago officinalis) is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia, known for its vibrant blue flowers and historical use in traditional medicine to support respiratory and skin health.

Botanical Identification

Borage, scientifically known as Borago officinalis, belongs to the Boraginaceae family. Common names include honeyflower and bugloss, with regional variations. It is an annual herb native to Europe and western Asia. The plant features hairy leaves and blue or violet star-shaped flowers. Synonyms in herbal literature include "Borago" and "Boraginaceae."

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Lamiales
  • Order: Lamiales
  • Family: Lamiaceae
  • Genus: Borago
  • Species: Borago officinalis

Active Compounds

Borage contains essential oil, flavonoids, and phenolic acids as its main active compounds. These compounds work together to provide anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. The essential oil contributes to its calming properties, while flavonoids and phenolic acids support overall health benefits.

  • Essential oil
  • Flavonoid
  • Phenolic acid

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory ailments.

Infusion: Used for mild digestive issues and as a calming tea.

Oil infusion: Used for skin conditions and muscle pain.

Safety Profile

Borage is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to potential toxicity. It should be avoided by individuals with known allergies to the Asteraceae family. High doses may cause gastrointestinal distress and central nervous system depression.
