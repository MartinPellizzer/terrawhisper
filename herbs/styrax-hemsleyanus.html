Styrax hemsleyanus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Styrax hemsleyanus has deciduous, opposite leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical with finely serrated margins and a smooth, glabrous surface. The plant grows as a small tree or shrub, often reaching heights of several meters with slender, brownish bark. During the flowering season, it produces small, fragrant white flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These blossoms consist of five delicate petals and are central to the plant's reproductive structure. After pollination, the plant develops small, fleshy drupes that transition in color as they mature.
