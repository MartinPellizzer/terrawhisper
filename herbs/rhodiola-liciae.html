Rhodiola Rosea (Rhodiola Liciae)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Rhodiola liciae.

Rhodiola rosea, scientifically known as Rhodiola liciae, is a perennial flowering plant native to cold, arctic regions and high altitudes, renowned as a medicinal herb and adaptogen for its ability to help the body resist stress. It is traditionally used in traditional Chinese medicine and Russian folk remedies to enhance physical endurance, improve mood, and support mental clarity. The primary benefits of Rhodiola rosea include boosting energy levels, reducing symptoms of fatigue, and enhancing cognitive function, making it a popular supplement for individuals seeking natural support for stress and mental performance. In modern wellness practices, it is widely used as a nootropic and adaptogen to combat anxiety, depression, and burnout, particularly among athletes and professionals. One of its unique features is the presence of rare active compounds called rosavins and salidrosides, which contribute to its adaptogenic properties, and it has a long history of use in Siberian cultures for enhancing vitality and resilience.
