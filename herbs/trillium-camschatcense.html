Trillium camschatcense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Trillium camschatense has three large, ovate to elliptical leaves that emerge from a single stem and three white, petaloid tepals that form a single whorl of flowers. These leaves are typically arranged in a single whorl at the top of the stem, creating a distinct three-part symmetry characteristic of its genus. The flowers bloom in the spring and feature upright pedicels that support the delicate blossoms. Each flower consists of six tepals that may vary slightly in shape but generally maintain a consistent white coloration. The plant's root system consists of a thick, fleshy rhizome that allows it to persist in forest floor environments.
