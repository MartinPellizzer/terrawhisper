Tilia japonica

What's the taxonomical classification of Tilia japonica?

Tilia japonica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Malvales. It is further categorized into the family Malvaceae, which places it within the genus Tilia. Ultimately, the specific identity of this plant is defined by its species name, japonica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tilia japonica has heart-shaped leaves with serrated margins, small yellowish-white flowers, and woody stems that support a broad, spreading canopy. The foliage typically exhibits a smooth texture and prominent venation that radiates from the base of each leaf. During the flowering season, the plant produces fragrant blossoms clustered on specialized bracts that facilitate seed dispersal. The bark on mature specimens becomes deeply fissured and develops a rugged, grayish appearance. Its overall structure forms a dense, rounded crown that provides significant shade.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the temperate regions of Japan, where it is primarily found on the islands of Honshu, Shikoku, and Kyushu. It is often integrated into urban landscapes and parks throughout its indigenous range due to its ornamental value.
