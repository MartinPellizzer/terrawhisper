White Cinquefoil (Potentilla anserina) Uses

White Cinquefoil is used to treat various skin conditions such as inflammation, infections, rashes, eczema, and irritations. It supports wound healing and may alleviate symptoms of dermatitis. Its antimicrobial properties help with skin infections and promote overall skin health. It is also used for respiratory infections due to its anti-inflammatory and immune-supporting effects.

White Cinquefoil (Potentilla anserina) Monograph

1. Skin inflammation

White Cinquefoil is used to treat skin inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and irritation. Often prepared as a poultice or infused oil, it is applied topically for conditions like eczema and psoriasis. Its mild action makes it suitable for sensitive skin.

2. Wound healing

White Cinquefoil supports wound healing through its astringent and anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce swelling and prevent infection. It promotes tissue repair and can be applied topically as a poultice or infused oil. Its traditional use in herbal medicine highlights its role in accelerating recovery and enhancing skin regeneration.

3. Skin infections

White Cinquefoil has been traditionally used to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and bacterial growth on the skin. Often applied topically as a poultice or infused oil, it supports healing in conditions like eczema and minor wounds. Its astringent qualities also aid in tightening and soothing irritated skin.

4. Skin rash

White Cinquefoil is used topically to treat skin rashes due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and irritation associated with various types of rashes. A poultice or infused oil made from the leaves and flowers can be applied directly to the affected area for relief.

5. Skin irritation

White Cinquefoil is used to treat skin irritation due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It can help soothe rashes, eczema, and minor burns when applied topically. Its high tannin content reduces swelling and promotes healing. It is often prepared as a poultice or infused oil for direct application.

6. Dermatitis

White Cinquefoil has been traditionally used to treat dermatitis due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It helps soothe skin irritation and reduce redness. Its high tannin content supports healing and may prevent further skin damage. It is often applied topically in herbal salves or infusions.

7. Skin wounds

White Cinquefoil has been traditionally used to treat skin wounds due to its astringent and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps in reducing swelling, preventing infection, and promoting tissue repair. The herb is often applied topically as a poultice or infused oil to support healing in minor cuts, abrasions, and skin irritations.

8. Skin irritations

White Cinquefoil is used topically to treat skin irritations due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It helps soothe redness, reduce swelling, and promote healing. Often applied as a poultice or infused oil, it is valued for its gentle yet effective action on sensitive skin.

9. Skin eczema

White Cinquefoil has been traditionally used to treat skin eczema due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It helps soothe irritated skin and reduce redness. Preparations like decoctions or salves can be applied topically to affected areas. Its mild nature makes it suitable for sensitive skin conditions.

10. Respiratory infections

White Cinquefoil is traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly for coughs and mild respiratory infections. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties help reduce throat irritation and clear mucus. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to soothe symptoms and support the body's natural healing processes.
