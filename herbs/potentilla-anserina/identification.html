Botanical Identification of White Cinquefoil (Potentilla anserina)

To identify White Cinquefoil (Potentilla anserina) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

White Cinquefoil (Potentilla anserina) belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Rosales, family Rosaceae, genus Potentilla, and species Potentilla anserina. These classifications help identify its botanical relationships and place it within the broader plant kingdom.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of White Cinquefoil is Potentilla anserina, first described by Linnaeus in 1753. It is also known by several synonyms, including Potentilla alpina and Potentilla glabra. Common names such as White Cinquefoil, Fiveleaf Cinquefoil, and Alpine Cinquefoil are widely used. Regional and historical names vary, reflecting its presence in different ecosystems and cultural contexts.

Morphological Characteristics

White Cinquefoil is a low-growing perennial with a fibrous root system. Its stems are prostrate, often rooting at the nodes. Leaves are palmately compound, with five oval leaflets. The inflorescence is a single flower head, surrounded by bracts. Flowers are yellow with five petals, and the fruit is a schizocarp with three nutlets.

Diagnostic Identification Features

White Cinquefoil can be distinguished by its white-petaled flowers with five pointed lobes, contrasting with the yellow flowers of other Potentilla species. Its leaves are doubly pinnate with fine, serrated margins, and it grows in moist meadows, unlike similar species that prefer drier soils. The presence of white flowers and specific leaf structure are key diagnostic features.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

White Cinquefoil is native to Europe and parts of Asia, thriving in temperate climates. It prefers well-drained soils, often found in meadows and along riverbanks. This plant grows at altitudes between 300 and 1,500 meters, commonly in moist, sunny environments.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

White Cinquefoil blooms from May to July, with seed formation occurring from July to September. Harvesting for identification purposes is best done during the flowering period, typically between May and July, when the plant's characteristic features are most visible.

Phylogenetic Relationships

White Cinquefoil (Potentilla anserina) belongs to the genus Potentilla, which is part of the family Rosaceae. It is closely related to other species within the genus, such as Potentilla fruticosa. Its family traits include simple leaves and flowers with five petals, typical of the Rosaceae family.
