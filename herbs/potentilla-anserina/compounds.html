White Cinquefoil (Potentilla anserina) Compounds

The primary compounds in White Cinquefoil include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and tannins, which contribute to its astringent effects. Phenolic acids and glycosides also play roles in its medicinal value, while coumarin and terpenoids may support its anti-inflammatory actions. Saponins, anthocyanins, essential oils, and alkaloids further contribute to its diverse therapeutic potential.

White Cinquefoil (Potentilla anserina) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in White Cinquefoil (Potentilla anserina) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help reduce oxidative stress and support immune function. They are found in the plant's leaves and roots, making it valuable in herbal medicine for treating inflammatory conditions.

2. Tannin

Tannin in White Cinquefoil (Potentilla anserina) contributes to its astringent properties, aiding in reducing inflammation and promoting wound healing. It also supports digestive health by binding to proteins and reducing intestinal permeability. This compound is a key component in the plant's traditional use for treating skin conditions and gastrointestinal issues.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid in White Cinquefoil (Potentilla anserina) contributes to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound helps reduce oxidative stress and supports the plant's traditional use in treating skin conditions and digestive issues. It is a key component in the plant's therapeutic profile.

4. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in White Cinquefoil (Potentilla anserina). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Glycosides in this species are often responsible for its traditional use in treating digestive and skin conditions. Their presence supports the plant's role in herbal medicine.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in White Cinquefoil (Potentilla anserina). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. Coumarin also acts as a natural anticoagulant, supporting cardiovascular health. Its presence enhances the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

6. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in White Cinquefoil (Potentilla anserina) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help reduce swelling and oxidative stress, supporting the plant's traditional use in treating skin conditions and digestive issues. They are part of the plant's complex chemical profile that underpins its therapeutic effects.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in White Cinquefoil (Potentilla anserina). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Saponin supports the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and skin-related ailments.

8. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in White Cinquefoil (Potentilla anserina). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in treating digestive and respiratory ailments. Anthocyanin also aids in protecting cells from oxidative stress.

9. Essential oil

The essential oil of White Cinquefoil (Potentilla anserina) contains volatile compounds like limonene and cineole. These components contribute to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, making it valuable in herbal medicine for treating skin infections and respiratory issues.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid in White Cinquefoil (Potentilla anserina) contributes to its traditional use in treating digestive issues and inflammation. It is believed to possess mild sedative and antispasmodic properties. While research is limited, its presence supports the plant's historical role in herbal medicine.
