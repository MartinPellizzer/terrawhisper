Pogostemon (Pogostemon cablin) Uses

Pogostemon cablin is used to treat respiratory infections, including the common cold, bronchitis, and asthma. It helps alleviate cough and cold symptoms. It also addresses skin infections and inflammatory skin conditions due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

Pogostemon (Pogostemon cablin) Monograph

1. Respiratory infection

Pogostemon cablin is used in traditional medicine to treat respiratory infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps alleviate symptoms such as coughing, fever, and bronchitis by reducing inflammation and inhibiting pathogenic bacteria in the respiratory tract. Its essential oils are often steam-distilled and used in inhalations or as a topical application.

2. Respiratory infections

Pogostemon cablin is used to treat respiratory infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps alleviate symptoms like coughing and congestion. Traditionally, it is prepared as a decoction or steam inhalation. Its essential oils are particularly effective in reducing mucus and soothing the respiratory tract.

3. Common cold

Pogostemon cablin is used in traditional medicine to alleviate symptoms of the common cold. It helps reduce fever, relieve nasal congestion, and soothe sore throat due to its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties. It is often prepared as a decoction or steam inhalation for respiratory relief.

4. Bronchitis

Pogostemon cablin is used in traditional medicine to treat bronchitis due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. It helps reduce bronchial inflammation and loosens mucus, easing coughing and improving respiratory function. Often prepared as a decoction or steam inhalation, it supports clearing airways and relieving symptoms associated with bronchial irritation.

5. Asthma

Pogostemon cablin is used in traditional medicine to alleviate asthma symptoms. Its anti-inflammatory and bronchodilatory properties help reduce airway inflammation and improve breathing. It is often prepared as a decoction or steam inhalation to ease respiratory discomfort.

6. Cold symptoms

Pogostemon cablin is used to alleviate cold symptoms such as fever, headache, and nasal congestion. Its cooling properties help reduce inflammation and soothe respiratory discomfort. It is often prepared as a tea or steam inhalation for immediate relief. It is valued for its antipyretic and decongestant effects in traditional herbal medicine.

7. Cough

Pogostemon cablin is used to treat coughs due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps loosen mucus and reduce throat irritation. It is often prepared as a decoction or steam inhalation. Its aromatic compounds soothe respiratory discomfort and enhance respiratory function.

8. Skin infection

Pogostemon cablin is used topically to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce bacterial growth and soothe inflamed skin, making it effective for conditions like eczema and fungal infections. Its essential oils are often applied directly or diluted in carrier oils for localized treatment.

9. Skin infections

Pogostemon cablin is used to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce bacterial growth and soothe irritated skin. Topical applications are common, often in the form of ointments or poultices. It is particularly effective against fungal and bacterial infections of the skin.

10. Inflammatory skin conditions

Pogostemon cablin is used topically to treat inflammatory skin conditions due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and irritation associated with eczema, psoriasis, and fungal infections. The essential oil is often diluted and applied directly to the affected area for localized relief.
