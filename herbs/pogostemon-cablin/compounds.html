Pogostemon (Pogostemon cablin) Compounds

The primary compounds of Pogostemon cablin include essential oil, which contains aromatic components like limonene and pinene. Flavonoids, such as apigenin and luteolin, contribute to its anti-inflammatory properties. Terpenoids, including pogostol, are responsible for its characteristic scent and medicinal effects. These compounds work synergistically to provide the plant's therapeutic benefits.

Pogostemon (Pogostemon cablin) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Pogostemon cablin is a key active compound, rich in sesquiterpenes and methyl pogostenate. It is widely used in traditional medicine for its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and analgesic properties. The oil is extracted through steam distillation and is a major component of oil of patchouli.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Pogostemon cablin. They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds support the plant's traditional use in treating digestive and respiratory conditions. Flavonoids also enhance the overall therapeutic profile of Pogostemon.

3. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a key group of active compounds in Pogostemon cablin, commonly known as patchouli. These compounds contribute to the plant's distinctive aroma and are responsible for its medicinal properties, including antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Terpenoids are widely studied for their potential therapeutic applications in herbal medicine.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a bioactive compound found in Pogostemon cablin. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating digestive and respiratory ailments. Coumarin exhibits antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting its role in herbal medicine. It is also valued for its aromatic profile in essential oils.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in Pogostemon cablin. It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. This compound is involved in the plant's ability to treat digestive issues and skin conditions. Its presence enhances the therapeutic value of Pogostemon in herbal medicine.

6. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Pogostemon cablin, known for its astringent properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating inflammation and digestive issues. Tannins in Pogostemon help reduce bleeding and promote wound healing. They are also believed to support the plant's antimicrobial effects.

Alkaloids in Pogostemon cablin, also known as patchouli, are naturally occurring nitrogen-containing compounds. Glycoside is a key active compound found in Pogostemon cablin. It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Glycosides in Pogostemon are often derived from its essential oils and are used in traditional herbal medicine for their therapeutic effects.

9. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Pogostemon cablin, known for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating skin infections and digestive issues. 10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a pigment found in Pogostemon cablin, contributing to its distinctive color. It possesses antioxidant properties that may support skin health and reduce inflammation. This compound is part of the plant's bioactive profile, aiding in its traditional use for therapeutic purposes.
