Grewia nervosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Grewia nervosa?

Grewia nervosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical order of Malvales, the plant is a member of the family Malvaceae. Its specific taxonomic placement is defined by the genus Grewia, with the distinct species name nervosa.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Grewia nervosa can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal distress if the plant or its components are ingested or handled improperly. While some parts of the plant are studied for medicinal potential, direct contact with the leaves or bark may trigger localized dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Ingestion of unrefined parts could lead to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal discomfort due to unidentified secondary metabolites. There is currently limited clinical data regarding the long-term systemic toxicity of this species in humans. Therefore, caution should be exercised when using any part of the plant for topical or internal purposes.
