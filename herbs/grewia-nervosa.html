Grewia nervosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Grewia nervosa has small, simple, lanceolate to oblong leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the woody stems. The foliage often features prominent pinnate venation and can exhibit a slightly leathery texture. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters that emerge from the axils of the leaves. The plant eventually produces small, woody, or fleshy fruits that contain seeds essential for its reproduction. This deciduous shrub or small tree maintains a branching habit that defines its overall structural form.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Grewia nervosa can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal distress if the plant or its components are ingested or handled improperly. There is currently limited clinical data regarding the long-term systemic toxicity of this species in humans. Therefore, caution should be exercised when using any part of the plant for topical or internal purposes.
