Thalictrum rutifolium

What's the taxonomical classification of Thalictrum rutifolium?

Thalictrum rutifolium belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is classified under the order Ranunculales. This plant is a member of the family Ranunculaceae, falling into the genus Thalictrum, and is specifically identified by its species name, rutifolium.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Thalictrum rutifoliuum targets various signaling proteins and enzymes, most notably through its isoquinoline alkaloids which inhibit acetylcholinesterase and modulate various neurotransmitter receptors. These bioactive compounds interact with specific protein kinases to regulate inflammatory signaling pathways, such as the inhibition of cyclooxygenase enzymes. The alkaloids also demonstrate an ability to bind to tubulin proteins, which can influence cellular division and antiproliferative processes. Furthermore, certain constituents exhibit interactions with calcium channels and other ion transport proteins within the cellular membrane. These diverse molecular interactions underpin the plant's potential applications in neuroprotection and anti-inflammatory therapies.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Thalictrum rutifoliuum can cause skin irritation, redness, and blistering if the plant comes into direct contact with sensitive skin. There is also a risk of allergic reactions in people with high sensitivity to the Ranunculaceae family. Caution should always be exercised when handling this plant to avoid these adverse physical responses.
