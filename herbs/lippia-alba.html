Side Effects of lippia alba

Lippia alba induces nausea in some individuals, particularly when consumed in high quantities or in its raw form.

This herb may also trigger skin rash or cause skin irritation, leading to redness, itching, or hives in sensitive individuals. Additionally, Lippia alba can cause eye irritation, resulting in redness, burning, or excessive tearing.

Throat and mouth irritation are also possible side effects, with some users reporting a sore throat or a burning sensation in the mouth. Furthermore, the herb may induce fatigue, especially in those who are not accustomed to its strong flavor or medicinal properties. These side effects highlight the importance of consulting a healthcare professional before using Lippia alba, especially for individuals with known allergies or sensitivities.

While the herb is generally considered safe in moderate amounts, its potential to cause adverse reactions underscores the need for caution and proper usage.
