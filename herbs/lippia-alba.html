Lippia Alba Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Medicinal Parts of lippia alba

Lippia alba leaf is commonly used in traditional medicine for its aromatic and therapeutic properties, often harvested during the flowering season for maximum potency.

The leaf contains essential oils, flavonoids, and other bioactive compounds that contribute to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and digestive benefits.

The main herbal preparations made from the leaf include infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, which are used to treat respiratory infections, digestive issues, and skin conditions.

Similarly, the seeds of Lippia alba are also utilized, typically dried and ground into powders or used in spice blends, though their medicinal use is less extensive compared to the leaves.
