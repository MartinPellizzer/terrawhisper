What to know about Lippia alba (lemon verbena) before using it medicinally

Lemon verbena, scientifically known as Lippia alba, is a herb that has garnered attention for its impressive health properties, boasting antioxidant and anti-inflammatory compounds that can help alleviate symptoms of anxiety, stress, and even skin conditions.

In the culinary world, lemon verbena is often used to add a citrusy twist to teas, desserts, and savory dishes, particularly in Mediterranean and Latin American cuisines. As an ornamental plant, lemon verbena is prized for its delicate, fern-like foliage and small white flowers that attract butterflies and hummingbirds. Botanically speaking, lemon verbena belongs to the Verbenaceae family, with its leaves featuring a distinctive fragrance when touched or bruised.

With records of use dating back to ancient Greece and Rome, lemon verbena has been a staple in traditional medicine and cooking for centuries, earning it a reputation as a versatile and valuable herb.

What are the side effects of Lippia alba when used improperly?

When used improperly, Lippia alba increases the chances of experiencing side effects, such as gastrointestinal disturbances, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea.

This is because the herb can interact with other medications, such as blood thinners, diabetes medications, and blood pressure medications, leading to adverse reactions. Additionally, taking large doses or using it for extended periods without proper monitoring can cause liver damage or kidney problems due to its potential toxicity.

Furthermore, allergic reactions, including hives, itching, and difficulty breathing, may occur in some individuals who are sensitive to Lippia alba. In rare cases, anaphylaxis, a life-threatening allergic reaction, has been reported. Moreover, improper use of Lippia alba during pregnancy or breastfeeding can increase the risk of birth defects or interact with hormones, potentially leading to changes in menstrual cycles or fertility issues.

It is crucial to consult with a healthcare professional before using Lippia alba for medicinal purposes and to follow proper guidelines for dosing and administration to minimize the risk of side effects.
