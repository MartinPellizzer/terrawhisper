Illicium angustisepalum

What's the taxonomical classification of Illicium angustisepalum?

Illicium angustisepalum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Austrobaileyales. Taxonomically, it is a member of the family Schisandraceae and is placed within the genus Illicium, ultimately identified by its specific species designation, angustisepalum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Illicium angustisepalum has elliptical to lanceolate leaves, narrow sepals, and star-shaped aggregate fruits consisting of several woody follicles.

The foliage is typically evergreen with a glossy texture and prominent venation visible on the undersides. Its characteristic sepals are notably slender and elongated compared to other species within the genus. The woody follicles contain aromatic seeds and are arranged in a distinctive radial pattern around a central receptacle.

These morphological traits allow the plant to thrive in specific forest understory environments.
