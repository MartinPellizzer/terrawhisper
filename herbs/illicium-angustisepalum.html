Illicium angustisepalum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Illicium angustisepalum has elliptical to lanceolate leaves, narrow sepals, and star-shaped aggregate fruits consisting of several woody follicles. The foliage is typically evergreen with a glossy texture and prominent venation visible on the undersides. Its characteristic sepals are notably slender and elongated compared to other species within the genus. The woody follicles contain aromatic seeds and are arranged in a distinctive radial pattern around a central receptacle. These morphological traits allow the plant to thrive in specific forest understory environments.
