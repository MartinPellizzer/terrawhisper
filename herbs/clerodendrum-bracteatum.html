What's the taxonomical classification of Clerodendrum bracteatum?

Clerodendrum bracteatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Lamiales. This species is a member of the family Lamiaceae, falling under the genus Clerodendrum, with the specific designation of species bracteatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clerodendrum bracteatum has large, spreading, ovate leaves with entire margins and prominent venation, alongside showy, bright red, petal-like bracts that surround small, tubular, white flowers. The true flowers emerge from the center of these colorful structures, typically exhibiting a long corolla tube. The plant develops into a woody shrub characterized by an upright growth habit and branching stems.
