Common Teucrium (Teucrium marum) Actions

Common Teucrium exhibits several key therapeutic actions including anti-inflammatory, diuretic, and antispasmodic properties. It acts as a bitter tonic and carminative, aiding digestion and relieving gas. Additionally, it has astringent and stimulant effects, supporting mucous membranes and boosting vitality. Its sedative and expectorant qualities help calm the nervous system and loosen respiratory secretions.

Common Teucrium (Teucrium marum) Monograph

1. Anti-inflammatory

Common Teucrium has an anti-inflammatory action, primarily due to its high concentration of essential oils and flavonoids. These compounds help reduce inflammation by inhibiting pro-inflammatory enzymes and modulating immune responses. It is commonly used to alleviate symptoms of inflammatory conditions like arthritis and skin irritations.

2. Bitter

Common Teucrium has a bitter action, which stimulates digestive secretions and enhances appetite. This action supports the liver and gallbladder by promoting bile flow and aiding in the digestion of fats. It is particularly effective in cases of sluggish digestion and poor nutrient absorption.

3. Diuretic

Common Teucrium has a diuretic action, promoting the excretion of urine and helping to reduce fluid retention. It supports kidney function by encouraging the removal of excess water and toxins from the body. This action can be beneficial for conditions involving edema or mild fluid overload.

4. Tonic

Common Teucrium has a tonic action, stimulating digestion and improving overall vitality. It supports the nervous system and enhances energy levels. Traditionally used to boost strength and combat fatigue, it is valued for its revitalizing properties.

5. Carminative

Common Teucrium has a carminative action, helping to relieve gas and bloating in the digestive system. It promotes the expulsion of trapped air, reducing discomfort and supporting healthy digestion. This action makes it useful for addressing digestive disturbances associated with excess gas.

6. Astringent

Common Teucrium has an astringent action, which helps reduce secretion and tighten tissues. It is used to treat conditions like diarrhea and excessive sweating. This property is due to its high tannin content. It supports healing by promoting tissue repair and reducing inflammation.

7. Stimulant

Common Teucrium has a stimulant action, enhancing mental clarity and alertness. It is traditionally used to invigorate the nervous system and improve circulation. This effect supports its historical use in treating fatigue and low energy. Its stimulating properties are believed to result from its aromatic compounds and mild tonic effects.

8. Antispasmodic

Common Teucrium has an antispasmodic action, helping to relieve muscle spasms and cramps. It works by relaxing smooth muscle tissue, making it useful for digestive issues and menstrual discomfort. Its calming effect on muscles supports its traditional use in easing tension and pain.

9. Expectorant

Common Teucrium has an expectorant action, helping to loosen and expel mucus from the respiratory tract. It is commonly used to relieve congestion and support clear breathing in conditions like bronchitis or colds. Its properties aid in reducing phlegm and improving airflow.

10. Sedative

Common Teucrium has a sedative action, helping to calm the nervous system and reduce anxiety. It is often used in herbal medicine to promote relaxation and improve sleep quality. Its mild calming effects make it suitable for stress-related conditions.
