10 Health Benefits Of Teucrium Marum (Wall Marjoram)

Teucrium marum, also known as wall marjoram, has health benefits such as anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, making it a natural remedy for soothing digestive issues and reducing fever.

Its medicinal properties also exhibit antioxidant and antiseptic actions, which can help alleviate symptoms of colds and respiratory problems.

By consuming wall marjoram, individuals can experience relief from coughs and sore throats, leading to improved quality of life and enhanced overall well-being.

This article explains in details the 10 best health benefits of Teucrium marum.

1. Alleviates digestive issues

Teucrium marum, also known as wall marjorum, alleviates digestive issues because of its carminative and anti-inflammatory properties.

The herb's volatile oils, such as borneol and bornyl acetate, help to relax the muscles in the digestive tract, reducing symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome (IBS) like bloating, cramps, and diarrhea.

Additionally, wall marjoram's anti-inflammatory compounds may help soothe and protect the mucous membranes lining the gut, further alleviating discomfort and inflammation associated with gastrointestinal disorders.

2. Reduces fever

Teucrium marum reduces fever because of its unique combination of bioactive compounds.

The essential oils present in the plant, particularly thymol and carvacrol, have been shown to possess potent antipyretic properties, which help to reduce body temperature by inhibiting the production of prostaglandins, a key mediator of fever.

Additionally, wall marjoram's flavonoids and phenolic acids exhibit anti-inflammatory effects, further contributing to its ability to alleviate febrile symptoms.

3. Eases respiratory symptoms

Teucrium marum eases respiratory symptoms because of its rich concentration of volatile oils and flavonoids.

These bioactive compounds have been shown to possess expectorant properties, helping to loosen and clear mucus from the lungs, making it easier for the body to expel.

Additionally, the herb's anti-inflammatory effects can reduce swelling in the airways, alleviating congestion and making breathing easier.

4. Acts as a diuretic

Teucrium marum acts as a diuretic because it contains certain compounds that increase urine production by stimulating the kidneys and bladder.

The herb's essential oils, including camphor and bornyl acetate, have been shown to relax the muscles in the urinary tract, allowing for increased urine flow and reduced pressure on the bladder.

This natural diuretic property makes wall marjoram a potential treatment option for conditions such as edema, hypertension, and urinary retention.

5. Improves liver function

Teucrium marum improves liver function because of its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

Its extracts have been shown to reduce oxidative stress and inflammation in the liver, which can help protect against liver damage caused by toxins and other environmental factors.

Additionally, wall marjoram has been found to increase glutathione production, a powerful antioxidant that helps detoxify the liver and promote overall liver health.

6. Soothes skin conditions

Teucrium marum soothes skin conditions because of its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The essential oils present in the herb have been shown to reduce redness and swelling associated with conditions such as acne, eczema, and psoriasis.

Additionally, its antimicrobial properties help to prevent bacterial and fungal infections that can exacerbate these conditions, promoting a calming and healing effect on the skin.

7. Possesses anti-inflammatory properties

Teucrium marum possesses anti-inflammatory properties because it contains a range of bioactive compounds, including flavonoids, phenolic acids, and diterpenes.

These compounds have been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes and cytokines, which helps to reduce inflammation and alleviate symptoms associated with conditions such as arthritis, gout, and other inflammatory disorders.

The anti-inflammatory effects of wall marjoram may also help to protect against oxidative stress and cell damage.

8. Exhibits antimicrobial effects

Teucrium marum exhibits antimicrobial effects because of its rich composition of bioactive compounds, primarily rosmarinic acid and flavonoids.

These secondary metabolites have been shown to inhibit the growth of a wide range of microorganisms, including bacteria, fungi, and protozoa.

The antimicrobial properties of T. marum are thought to be due to its ability to disrupt microbial cell membranes and inhibit enzyme activity, making it a promising natural remedy for the treatment of various infectious diseases.

9. Regulates menstrual cycle

Teucrium marum regulates menstrual cycle because of its ability to balance hormones and calm the uterus.

The herb contains compounds that have been shown to inhibit the production of prostaglandins, which are hormone-like substances that can cause uterine contractions and menstrual cramps.

By reducing prostaglandin levels, Teucrium marum helps to regulate the menstrual cycle, alleviating symptoms such as irregular periods and heavy bleeding.

10. Helps control blood sugar levels

Teucrium marum helps control blood sugar levels because of its ability to slow down carbohydrate digestion and absorption in the gut.

The herb's flavonoids, particularly quercetin, have been shown to inhibit alpha-glucosidase enzymes, thereby reducing glucose uptake into the bloodstream.

This delayed glucose release allows for improved insulin sensitivity and reduced postprandial blood sugar spikes, making it a natural adjunct therapy for managing type 2 diabetes.
