Botanical Identification of Common Teucrium (Teucrium marum)

To identify Common Teucrium (Teucrium marum) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Common Teucrium, or Teucrium marum, belongs to the kingdom Plantae. Its classification places it in the family Labiatae, genus Teucrium, and species marum. The division, class, and order are not specified in the provided data.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Teucrium marum is the scientific name for Common Teucrium. It was first described by Linnaeus in 1753. Synonyms include Teucrium polium and Teucrium montanum. Common names include creeping teucrium and common germander. It is also known as mountain teucrium in some regions.

Morphological Characteristics

Common Teucrium (Teucrium marum) is a perennial herb with a spreading growth habit. It has a fibrous root system that anchors it firmly. The stems are upright and slightly hairy, often branching near the base. Leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a rough, slightly hairy surface. The inflorescence is a dense spike of small blue or purple flowers. The fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several tiny seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Teucrium marum can be distinguished by its opposite, oval leaves with glandular dots, and blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters. It lacks the hairy stems and broader leaves of Teucrium chamaedrys. The leaves are also more elongated and less toothed compared to similar species.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Common Teucrium (Teucrium marum) is native to Europe, North Africa, and Western Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained soils, such as sandy or loamy types. It typically grows at low to mid-altitudes, between 0 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Common Teucrium (Teucrium marum) blooms from late spring to early summer, typically between May and July. Seed formation occurs in late summer, around August to September. For identification purposes, the best time to harvest is during the flowering period, when the leaves and flowers are most distinct.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Common Teucrium (Teucrium marum) is closely related to species within the same genus, Teucrium. It shares family traits with the Lamiaceae family, including square stems and aromatic leaves. Its phylogenetic placement reflects its close ties to other Mediterranean herbs, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine.
