Galearis spathulata

What's the taxonomical classification of Galearis spathulata?

Galearis spathulata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Asparagales. This plant is a member of the family Orchidaceae, falling specifically within the genus Galearis, and is identified by its unique species name, spatulata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Galearis spathulata has an orchid morphology characterized by a single, broadly spatulate leaf that emerges from a fleshy rhizome. This solitary leaf is typically lanceolate to ovate in shape with an entire margin and a distinctively widened tip. The plant produces an erect, slender flowering stem that bears several small, greenish-white or yellowish flowers. Each flower features a prominent, three-lobed lip that is often slightly fringed or wavy along the edges. The vegetative structure is relatively compact, focusing its energy into the singular leaf and the subsequent reproductive spike.
