Saussurea graminea

What's the taxonomical classification of Saussurea graminea?

Saussurea graminea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this classification, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Asterales and is a member of the family Asteraceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Saussurea, and its species designation, graminea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea graminea has a perennial growth habit characterized by a deep taproot, basal leaves that are lanceolate or linear, and upright flowering stems topped with discoid flower heads. The leaves are typically arranged in a rosette at the base and feature serrated or entire margins depending on the specific variety. Its inflorescence consists of small, yellowish-to-white flower heads that are often clustered in dense terminal panicles. The stems are slender and can reach significant heights to support the flowering structures. Each individual floret within the head is relatively small, contributing to the overall delicate appearance of the plant.

What health problems are treated using this plant?

This plant is used for treating hepatitis and sepsis-associated liver injury through its potent anti-inflammatory and hepatoprotective properties. Research indicates that its bioactive compounds help mitigate oxidative stress and prevent cellular damage in the liver. By regulating immune responses, the plant assists in reducing the systemic inflammation often triggered by severe infections. It also plays a role in stabilizing liver enzymes and promoting tissue regeneration following toxic insults. These therapeutic actions make it a significant subject in the study of traditional medicine for liver recovery.

As discussed in a study, Saussurea graminea Dunn is a traditional Chinese medicinal herb used in the treatment of inflammatory diseases such as hepatitis. This herb effectively counteracts sepsis-associated liver injury by inhibiting the cytokine storm. Through transcriptomics and metabolomics, it was revealed that the plant regulates injury via mitochondrial/TNF and metabolic pathways, specifically involving arachidonic acid metabolism. HPLC-MS identified 6 main components of Saussurea graminea as chlorogenic acid, syringin, scopoletin, rutin, isochlorogenic acid, and narcissin. These 6 compounds were confirmed as potential active components in the RAW264.7 inflammation model. Ultimately, Saussurea graminea and its active ingredients alleviate injury by reducing the cytokine storm through mtDNA/TNF/arachidonic acid metabolism.
