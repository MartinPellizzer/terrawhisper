Saussurea graminea

What's the taxonomical classification of Saussurea graminea?

Saussurea graminea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this classification, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Asterales and is a member of the family Asteraceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Saussurea, and its species designation, graminea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea graminea has a perennial growth habit characterized by a deep taproot, basal leaves that are lanceolate or linear, and upright flowering stems topped with discoid flower heads. The leaves are typically arranged in a rosette at the base and feature serrated or entire margins depending on the specific variety. Its inflorescence consists of small, yellowish-to-white flower heads that are often clustered in dense terminal panicles. The stems are slender and can reach significant heights to support the flowering structures. Each individual floret within the head is relatively small, contributing to the overall delicate appearance of the plant.

What are the pharmacological activities of Saussurea graminea?

This plant has anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, antitumor, immunomodulatory, and antiviral pharmacological activities. These diverse biological properties are attributed to the complex bioactive compounds found within its roots and aerial parts. Research suggests that its antioxidant effects help mitigate oxidative stress by neutralizing free radicals in the body. Furthermore, its ability to modulate the immune system and inhibit tumor growth makes it a subject of significant interest in medicinal studies. The plant also demonstrates potential in combating viral infections through its unique phytochemical profile.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", Saussurea graminea Dunn effectively counteracts sepsis-associated liver injury by inhibiting the cytokine storm. The plant regulates this injury through mitochondrial/TNF and metabolic pathways, specifically involving arachidonic acid metabolism. HPLC-MS identified 6 main components of the plant, which are chlorogenic acid, syringin, scopoletin, rutin, isochlorogenic acid, and narcissin. These 6 compounds were confirmed as potential active components in the RAW264.7 inflammation model. Ultimately, Saussurea graminea and its active ingredients alleviate sepsis-associated liver injury by reducing the cytokine storm through mtDNA/TNF/arachidonic acid metabolism.
