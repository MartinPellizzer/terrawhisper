What's the taxonomical classification of Piper semiimmersum?

Piper semiimmersum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it falls under the order Piperales and is a member of the family Piperaceae. Specifically, this plant is placed within the genus Piper, where it is identified by the species name semiimmersum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Piper semiimmersum has opposite or subopposite leaves that are ovate to elliptical with an acute apex and an entire or slightly undulate margin. The leaf texture is typically coriaceous, meaning it feels somewhat leathery to the touch. The plant features a woody stem that supports its climbing or scrambling growth habit. Its inflorescences consist of slender spikes that emerge from the leaf axils. These spikes bear small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into tiny drupes.
