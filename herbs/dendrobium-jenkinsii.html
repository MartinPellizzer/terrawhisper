Dendrobium jenkinsii

What's the taxonomical classification of Dendrobium jenkinsii?

Dendrobium jenkinsii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Asparagales. It is a member of the diverse family Orchidaceae, specifically falling within the genus Dendrobium, and is identified by its unique species designation, jenkinsii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dendrobium jenkinsii has elongated, pendulous pseudobulbs that are distinctly compressed and often possess a slightly wavy or irregular surface. These stems are typically slender and can reach significant lengths, acting as the primary storage organs for the plant. At the base of each pseudobulb, small, coriaceous leaves emerge in a clustered fashion. The inflorescence is characterized by small, delicate flowers that often feature a pale coloration with subtle markings. These floral structures are produced on short peduncles that arise from the upper nodes of the maturing stems.
