Sinosenecio dryas

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sinoseneciio dryas has perennial, creeping rhizomes that produce basal rosettes of simple, entire leaves with long petioles. These leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and possess a smooth, somewhat leathery texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-white flower heads arranged in terminal panicles. Each capitulum consists of numerous tubular disc florets that lack prominent ray florets. The overall structure is compact and adapted to rocky or alpine environments.
