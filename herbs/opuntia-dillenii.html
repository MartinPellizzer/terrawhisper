Opuntia dillenii

What's the taxonomical classification of Opuntia dillenii?

Opuntiia dillenii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its progression through the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Caryophyllales and is a member of the family Cactaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Opuntia, and its specific species designation, dillenii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Opuntiia dillenii has flattened, oval-shaped photosynthetic cladodes that are typically covered in fine, white glochids and larger, sharp spines. These succulent stem segments serve as the primary site for water storage and nutrient production within the plant. The surface of the pads often exhibits a waxy cuticle to minimize moisture loss in arid environments. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the edges of the pads, eventually developing into fleshy fruits. The root system is generally shallow and wide-spreading to capture surface moisture quickly during infrequent rainfall.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern and eastern Africa, specifically spanning across parts of South Africa, Namibia, and Botswana. It typically inhabits rocky outcrops and dry scrublands where drainage is excellent. The species is well-adapted to the subtropical climates found within these specific territories. Its distribution is largely dictated by the availability of well-drained soil and intense sunlight. Because it thrives in these harsh environments, it remains concentrated in these continental regions rather than spreading globally.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant thrives in well-draining, sandy soils under full sun to partial shade and requires infrequent, deep watering once the substrate has completely dried.

Because it is a succulent, it is highly susceptible to root rot if left in waterlogged conditions. It is best grown in containers or rock gardens where drainage can be easily managed. During the growing season, occasional fertilization with a low-nitrogen cactus formula can promote vigorous growth.

Winter care involves reducing moisture significantly to prevent damage from cold and dampness.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds Opuntia dillenii polysaccharides, opuntiol, betalains, betanin, and opuntioside. The polysaccharides within the plant are known for their potential antioxidant and immunomodulatory properties. Opuntiol and opuntioside serve as important bioactive constituents that contribute to the plant's therapeutic profile. Additionally, the plant is a rich source of betalains, which include the specific pigment betanin. These combined compounds work together to provide various health benefits through their chemical activity.

According to a study published by "Zhongguo Zhong yao za zhi = Zhongguo zhongyao zazhi = China journal of Chinese materia medica", the chemical composition of Opuntiia dillenii was investigated using an 80% ethanolic extract of its stems. Through chromatography, NMR, and MS spectral analysis, one new compound and five known compounds were isolated. The new compound was characterized as opuntioside. Additionally, four compounds obtained for the first time from the genus Opuntiia were identified as daucosterol, p-hydroxybenzoicacid, L-(-)-malic acid, and (E)-ferulic acid. Opuntiol was also separated for the first time from the plant.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for managing diabetes mellitus, hyperglycemia, hyperlipidemia, hypertension, and inflammation. Its bioactive compounds work to regulate blood glucose levels and reduce high sugar concentrations in the bloodstream. Additionally, the plant assists in lowering lipid profiles to combat elevated cholesterol and fats. It also supports cardiovascular health by helping to control high blood pressure. Finally, its natural properties are utilized to reduce systemic inflammation within the body.

According to a study published by "Molecules (Basel, Switzerland)", Opuntia dillenii is a medicinal plant used for the prevention and treatment of diabetes mellitus (DM) in Morocco. The seed oil of this plant, referred to as ODSO, significantly attenuates postprandial hyperglycemia in both normal and STZ-diabetic rats. Research shows that ODSO significantly blocks the SGLT1 with an IC50 of 60.24 µg/mL. It also induces significant inhibition of intestinal α-glucosidase with an IC50 of 278 ± 0.01 µg/mL and pancreatic α-amylase with an IC50 of 0.81 ± 0.09 mg/mL. Furthermore, no toxic effect was observed in ODSO-treated mice up to a dose of 7 mL/kg.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause skin irritation, localized swelling, and potential digestive distress if consumed in large quantities. The presence of tiny, hair-like spines called glochids can embed themselves in the skin, leading to persistent itching and inflammation. If these spines are accidentally ingested, they may cause physical irritation to the mucosal linings of the mouth, throat, or stomach. Some individuals might also experience allergic reactions such as rashes or hives upon direct contact with the plant's juices or surface. Therefore, careful handling is necessary to avoid both dermatological and internal discomfort.
