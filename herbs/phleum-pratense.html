Timothy Grass (Phleum pratense)

Timothy Grass (Phleum pratense) is a member of the Poaceae family, native to Europe, Asia, and North America. Traditionally, its leaves, stems, and shoots have been used for decoctions, infusions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its diuretic, tonic, and anti-inflammatory actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and traditional chinese medicine.

Stems
Culms are erect, unbranched, cylindrical, and terete, with nodes that are smooth and glabrous, and leaf sheaths that are leafy and partially overlapping.
