Timothy grass

Phleum pratense

Timothy Grass (Phleum pratense) is a member of the Poaceae family, commonly found in temperate regions. Traditionally, it has been used for respiratory ailments, as a diuretic, and for digestive issues. The plant parts, including the leaves and flowers, are typically utilized in herbal preparations.

Botanical Identification

Timothy grass, scientifically known as Phleum pratense, belongs to the Poaceae family. It is a perennial grass native to Europe and Asia, commonly found in meadows and pastures. The plant has tufted leaves and spikelets with two to three florets. It is also known as meadow grass or Timothy, and has been used in herbal medicine for its mild sedative properties.

Active Compounds

Timothy grass contains key active compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its therapeutic properties. These compounds work synergistically to exert anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, supporting its use in herbal medicine.

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory ailments such as coughs and colds.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and as a general tonic.

Powder: Used for digestive issues and as a mild laxative.

Safety Profile

Timothy grass is contraindicated for individuals with a known allergy to grass pollens. It should be avoided during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to limited safety data. Always consult a healthcare professional before use.
