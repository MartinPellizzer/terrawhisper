Field cress

Lepidium sativum

Field Cress (Lepidium sativum) is a small, leafy plant native to Europe and Asia, commonly used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, particularly in improving digestive health and reducing inflammation.

Botanical Identification

Lepidium sativum, commonly known as field cress, is an annual plant in the Brassicaceae family. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, with regional names including "cress" and "wild mustard." The plant has small white or yellow flowers, alternate leaves, and a taproot system. It is also referred to as "sawroot" in some herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Spermatophyta
  • Class: caryophyllopsida
  • Order: Brassicales
  • Family: Brassicaceae
  • Genus: Lepidium
  • Species: Lepidium sativum

Field Cress contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and tannins. These compounds work synergistically to provide antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. Their combined action supports the herb's traditional use in herbal medicine.

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for digestive issues and respiratory conditions.

Decoction: Used for treating gastrointestinal ailments and as a diuretic.

Powder: Used for digestive problems and as a general tonic.

Safety Profile

Field Cress is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal upset in some individuals. Long-term use or high doses could lead to toxicity, so it should be used with caution and under professional guidance.
