Field Cress (Lepidium sativum)

Field Cress (Lepidium sativum) is a member of the Brassicaceae family, native to South America, and Europe. Traditionally, its seeds, leaves, and shoots have been used for infusions, decoctions, and culinary uses.

This herb is particularly valued for its diuretic, anti-inflammatory, and carminative actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and ayurvedic medicine.

Cylindrical, hollow, ribbed, and glabrous, the stems are erect, simple, and exhibit opposite branching.
