What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Microtoena delaavay has small, prostrate herbs characterized by creeping stems, ovate to elliptic leaves with entire margins, and tiny, white to cream-colored flower heads arranged in terminal clusters. The plant typically grows close to the ground, forming dense mats through its sprawling vegetative structure. Each flower head contains minute tubular florets that are characteristic of the Asteraceae family. The leaves are often slightly hairy or glabrous depending on the specific environmental conditions of its habitat. These morphological traits allow the species to thrive in rocky or sandy substrates where low-lying growth is advantageous.
