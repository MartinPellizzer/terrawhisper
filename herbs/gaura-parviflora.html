Gaura parviflora

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gaura parviflora has slender, wiry stems that branch out to bear small, four-petaled flowers ranging in color from white to pale pink. The leaves are typically lanceolate or linear in shape, often appearing in opposite pairs along the stem with finely serrated margins. Its inflorescences are airy and loosely arranged, creating a delicate, cloud-like appearance when in full bloom. The plant possesses a woody base that develops over time, allowing it to form small, perennial subshrubs. These botanical features collectively contribute to its graceful and ethereal aesthetic in garden settings.
