Gaura parviflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Gaura parviflora?

Gaura parviflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic lineage further, it falls under the order Myrtales and the family Onagraceae. Ultimately, the plant is identified by the genus Gauras and is specifically designated as the species parviflora.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gaura parviflora has slender, wiry stems that branch out to bear small, four-petaled flowers ranging in color from white to pale pink.

The leaves are typically lanceolate or linear in shape, often appearing in opposite pairs along the stem with finely serrated margins. Its inflorescences are airy and loosely arranged, creating a delicate, cloud-like appearance when in full bloom. The plant possesses a woody base that develops over time, allowing it to form small, perennial subshrubs.

These botanical features collectively contribute to its graceful and ethereal aesthetic in garden settings.
