What's the taxonomical classification of Gastrochilus distichus?

Gastrochilus distichus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down through its hierarchical classification, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Asparagales, this plant is a member of the family Orchidaceae. Specifically, it is identified by the genus Gastrochilus, with its unique biological designation being the species distichus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gastrochilus distichus has a monopodial growth habit characterized by short, creeping rhizomes and fleshy, succulent leaves that are arranged in a distichous or two-ranked pattern.

The leaves are typically elliptical or oblong in shape with an entire margin and a somewhat leathery texture. Its inflorescence emerges from the base of the plant, bearing small, fragrant flowers that are often white or pale pink with distinct spotting. The most defining feature of the flower is the labellum, which possesses a fleshy, papillose callus on its surface.
