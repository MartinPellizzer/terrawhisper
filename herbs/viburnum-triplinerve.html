Viburnum triplinerve

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viburnum triplinerve has simple, opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves characterized by three prominent longitudinal veins originating from the base of the leaf blade. These leaves typically measure between 5 and 15 centimeters in length and feature finely serrated margins. The leaf texture is often somewhat leathery, and the surface may appear slightly rugose due to the deep vein structure. The plant grows as a woody shrub with smooth to slightly fissured bark depending on its age. Small, white, five-petaled flowers cluster in terminal cymes during the blooming season.
