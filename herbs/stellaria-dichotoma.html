Stellaria dichotoma

What's the taxonomical classification of Stellaria dichotoma?

Stellaria dichotoma belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Caryophyllales and the family Caryophyllaceae. Ultimately, this plant is identified by its specific genus, Stellaria, and its unique species designation, dichotoma.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stellaria dichotoma has an annual growth habit characterized by slender, branched stems that typically reach heights of 5 to 20 centimeters. The leaves are small, opposite, and lanceolate in shape with entire margins. These plants feature tiny, white, five-petaled flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. The reproductive structures include a superior ovary and capsule-like fruit that develops after pollination. Fine hairs may be present on the stems and leaf surfaces depending on the specific environmental conditions.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

It thrives in high-humidity environments where dense canopy cover provides consistent shade. Its distribution is closely tied to the seasonal flooding patterns of major river systems. Within these ecosystems, the species is typically found in the nutrient-rich soil of the forest floor. What are the pharmacological activities of Stellaria dichotoma?

This plant has antiallergic and antifungal pharmacological activities that make it a subject of medicinal interest. Research indicates that extracts from Stellaria dichotoma can effectively suppress allergic reactions by modulating immune responses. Additionally, the plant exhibits significant antifungal properties that help inhibit the growth of various pathogenic microorganisms. These biological effects are attributed to the presence of specific bioactive compounds found within its tissues. Such diverse pharmacological capabilities suggest potential applications in developing new therapeutic agents for skin or systemic ailments.

According to a study published by "Journal of natural products", the aqueous ethanolic extract from the roots of Stellaria dichotoma showed an antiallergic effect on ear passive cutaneous anaphylaxis (PCA) reaction in mice and inhibitory activity on the release of beta-hexosaminidase in RBL-2H3 cells. From this extract, new beta-carboline-type alkaloids including dichotomines A (1), B (2), C (3), and D (4) and dichotomides I (5) and II (6) were isolated. Among these, constituent 3 demonstrated inhibitory activity with an IC50 of 62 microM on the release of beta-hexosaminidase in RBL-2H3 cells. Additionally, constituent 3 inhibited the antigen-IgE-mediated releases of TNF-alpha and IL-4 at 19 microM and 15 microM, respectively, in RBL-2H3 cells.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains specific medicinal compounds known as Dichotomins J, Dichotomins K, dichotomidides I, dichotomidides II, and dichotominines A. These unique chemical constituents are primary components extracted from the Stellaria dichotoma species. Researchers study these substances to better understand their potential pharmacological applications in human health. The structural diversity of these molecules plays a crucial role in the plant's overall biological profile. Ongoing investigations aim to isolate these elements for future medicinal development.

According to a study published by "Journal of natural products", two new cyclic peptides named dichotomins J (1) and K (2) have been isolated from the roots of Stellaria dichotoma. These medicinal compounds are classified as vasodilator cyclic peptides. The structures of dichotomins J (1) and K (2) were elucidated through chemical degradation and extensive 2D NMR methods. Both dichotomins J (1) and K (2) demonstrated a moderate vasorelaxant effect on rat aorta.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating fungal infections caused by Candida species, Malassezia furfur, and various dermatophytes, as well as managing various cutaneous diseases and allergic reactions. Its bioactive compounds act effectively against these specific microorganisms to clear skin irritations. By targeting the underlying fungal growth, it helps alleviate the discomfort associated with persistent dermatological issues. Additionally, the plant possesses soothing properties that mitigate the inflammation caused by allergic reactions. This multifaceted approach makes it a versatile remedy for diverse skin health concerns.

According to a study published by "Natural product research", Stellaia dichotoma L. was investigated for its in vitro antifungal activity against fungi that cause cutaneous diseases. The species demonstrated antifungal activity against all studied fungi, which included Candida species, dermatophytes, and Malassezia furfur. The plant contains rutin in its extracts, which could be responsible for the observed antifungal activity. This study was part of an investigation into the minimal inhibitory concentration of extracts from 14 medicinal plants.

    What side effects this plant can have?

If the plant is swallowed, it may trigger intense stomach cramping and vomiting due to its toxic alkaloids.
