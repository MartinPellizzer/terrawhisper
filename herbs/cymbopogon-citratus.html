Cymbopogon citratus

What's the taxonomical classification of Cymbopogon citratus?

Cymbopogon citratus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Poales, the plant is a member of the family Poaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by its placement within the genus Cymbopogon and its designation as the species citratus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cymbopogon citratus has perennial, tufted, graminoid leaves that grow from a dense rhizomatous base and reach heights of up to one meter. The leaf blades are long, linear, and possess a distinctively sharp, serrated edge along their margins. These blades are characterized by a bright green color and a strong, citrus-like aroma released when the foliage is crushed. The plant produces small, inconspicuous panicles that emerge on slender stalks during its flowering period. Its root system is fibrous and compact, which helps the plant form thick, grassy clumps in its natural habitat.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, specifically spanning across countries such as Thailand, Vietnam, Laos, and parts of southern China. It is commonly found growing in moist, disturbed habitats including forest edges, riverbanks, and grassy openings. The species thrives in environments with high humidity and consistent warmth characteristic of its regional climate. Its distribution is largely tied to the seasonal monsoon patterns that provide necessary moisture for its growth. Localized populations may also occur in similar tropical climates throughout the Indochina peninsula.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Cymbopogon citratus contains bioactive compounds primarily located in its aromatic leaves and essential oils extracted from the entire aerial portion of the plant. These leaves are frequently dried or used fresh to prepare infusions and decoctions for various therapeutic purposes. The essential oil, rich in citral, is often applied topically or inhaled to alleviate digestive issues and respiratory congestion. Additionally, the plant extracts are utilized for their antimicrobial and antifungal properties in traditional medicine. Many cultures also employ the leaf extracts to reduce fever and soothe inflammation through oral consumption.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the primary medicinal monoterpenes citral, geraniol, and citronellal, alongside bioactive flavonoids such as luteolin-7-O-neohesperidoside. These specific chemical constituents contribute to the plant's significant antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties. Citral is the dominant component responsible for the characteristic lemony scent and much of the botanical's biological activity. The presence of geraniol and citronellal further enhances its ability to combat various pathogens. Additionally, the flavonoids like luteolin-7-O-neohesperidoside provide specialized cellular protection against oxidative stress. Together, these diverse compounds form a complex chemical profile used in various therapeutic applications.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Cymbopogon citratus targets various inflammatory mediators, including cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2), inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS), and pro-inflammatory cytokines such as tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-alpha) and interleukin-6 (IL-6). These interactions primarily occur through its bioactive citral components, which inhibit the enzymatic pathways responsible for prostaglandin synthesis. By modulating these protein signals, the plant exerts significant anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties within the body. Additionally, certain compounds in the lemongrass extract interact with antioxidant enzymes like superoxide dismutase (SOD) and catalase to mitigate oxidative stress. These molecular mechanisms collectively support its traditional use in treating fever, inflammation, and gastrointestinal distress.

What are the therapeutic actions of Cymbopogon citratus?

Cymbopogon citratus has antimicrobial, antispasmodic, carminative, diaporetic, diuretic, antipyretic, and mild sedative therapeutic actions. Its essential oils help inhibit the growth of various pathogens while simultaneously relaxing smooth muscle tissue to relieve cramping. The plant promotes digestion by reducing intestinal gas and supports the body in breaking a fever through sweating. It also increases urine output to assist in fluid regulation and detoxification. Furthermore, its calming properties provide a mild sedative effect that helps soothe the nervous system.

What health problems are treated using this plant?

This plant is used for treating gastric ulcers, inflammation, malaria, respiratory tract diseases, and urinary tract infections. Its bioactive compounds act as natural agents to soothe digestive linings and reduce bodily swelling. Medicinal preparations of the leaves are frequently utilized to alleviate symptoms associated with malaria and fever. Additionally, infusions help clear congestion in respiratory tract diseases and flush out bacteria causing urinary tract infections. These diverse therapeutic applications make it a significant resource in traditional medicine.

What are the herbal preparations of this plant?

This plant can be processed into various herbal preparations including essential oil, infusion, tincture, decoction, and distillate. The essential oil is extracted through steam distillation to capture the concentrated aromatic compounds of the leaves. An infusion made from hot water serves as a common tea for digestive support, while a decoction involves boiling the tougher plant material to extract more robust properties. A tincture prepared with alcohol allows for a long-lasting shelf life and potent medicinal applications. Finally, the distillate produced during the steam process provides a purified liquid form of the plant's essence.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause allergic reactions, nausea, dizziness, and stomach upset in some individuals. People with sensitivities to lemongrass may experience skin irritation or respiratory issues upon contact. Consuming large amounts might lead to digestive discomfort or a feeling of nausea. Some users have also reported bouts of dizziness after ingestion. These side effects highlight the importance of using the plant with caution.

The main side effects this plant can have are shown in the list below.

  • Allergic reactions
  • Nausea
  • Dizziness
  • Stomach upset
