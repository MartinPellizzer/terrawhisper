Cymbopogon citratus

What's the taxonomical classification of Cymbopogon citratus?

Cymbopogon citratus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Poales, the plant is a member of the family Poaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by its placement within the genus Cymbopogon and its designation as the species citratus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cymbopogon citratus has perennial, tufted, graminoid leaves that grow from a dense rhizomatous base and reach heights of up to one meter.

The leaf blades are long, linear, and possess a distinctively sharp, serrated edge along their margins. These blades are characterized by a bright green color and a strong, citrus-like aroma released when the foliage is crushed. The plant produces small, inconspicuous panicles that emerge on slender stalks during its flowering period.

Its root system is fibrous and compact, which helps the plant form thick, grassy clumps in its natural habitat.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Cymbopogon citratus contains bioactive compounds primarily located in its aromatic leaves and essential oils extracted from the entire aerial portion of the plant. These leaves are frequently dried or used fresh to prepare infusions and decoctions for various therapeutic purposes. The essential oil, rich in citral, is often applied topically or inhaled to alleviate digestive issues and respiratory congestion. Additionally, the plant extracts are utilized for their antimicrobial and antifungal properties in traditional medicine. Many cultures also employ the leaf extracts to reduce fever and soothe inflammation through oral consumption.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a complex mixture of bioactive substances including citral, which is composed of its geometric isomers geranial and neral, as well as geraniol, linalool, myrcene, various terpenoids, flavonoids, and phenolic compounds.

These specific constituents contribute to the plant's significant antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties. The high concentration of terpenoids and phenolic compounds provides a robust defense mechanism against oxidative stress in biological systems. Furthermore, the presence of flavonoids enhances the overall therapeutic potential of the essential oil extracted from the leaves.

Together, these diverse chemical structures work synergistically to promote various medicinal benefits.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Cymbopogon citratus targets various inflammatory mediators, including cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2), inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS), and pro-inflammatory cytokines such as tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-alpha) and interleukin-6 (IL-6). These interactions primarily occur through its bioactive citral components, which inhibit the enzymatic pathways responsible for prostaglandin synthesis. By modulating these protein signals, the plant exerts significant anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties within the body. Additionally, certain compounds in the lemongrass extract interact with antioxidant enzymes like superoxide dismutase (SOD) and catalase to mitigate oxidative stress. These molecular mechanisms collectively support its traditional use in treating fever, inflammation, and gastrointestinal distress.

What are the therapeutic actions of Cymbopogon citratus?

Cymbopogon citratus has antimicrobial, antispasmodic, carminative, diaporetic, diuretic, antipyretic, and mild sedative therapeutic actions.

Its essential oils help inhibit the growth of various pathogens while simultaneously relaxing smooth muscle tissue to relieve cramping. The plant promotes digestion by reducing intestinal gas and supports the body in breaking a fever through sweating. It also increases urine output to assist in fluid regulation and detoxification.

Furthermore, its calming properties provide a mild sedative effect that helps soothe the nervous system.

What common ailments are treated using this plant?

This plant is used to treat fever, digestive issues, inflammation, fungal infections, stress and anxiety.

Its essential oils possess antimicrobial properties that help combat various fungal infections on the skin. The herbal infusion is often consumed to soothe digestive issues such as bloating or stomach cramps. Additionally, the plant acts as an antipyretic to reduce fever and contains compounds that mitigate bodily inflammation.

Many people also utilize its aromatic qualities to alleviate symptoms of stress and anxiety.

What are the herbal preparations of Cymbopogon citratus?

Cymbopogon citratus is prepared as a liquid infusion or decoction by steeping the dried or fresh leaves in hot water, as a concentrated essential oil through steam distillation, or as a topical tincture. The most common traditional method involves boiling the fragrant leaves to create a tea that is consumed for its digestive and antipyretic properties. In modern aromatherapy, the extracted essential oil is frequently used in diffusers or diluted in carrier oils for topical application. Additionally, the plant can be processed into powders or dried herb blends that are added to culinary dishes or medicinal capsules. These diverse forms allow the plant's citronella compounds to be utilized for both internal wellness and external therapeutic purposes.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Cymbopogon citratus can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and digestive upset when ingested in large quantities. The essential oils found in the lemongrass plant are potent and may trigger redness or itching upon topical application to sensitive skin. Some individuals may experience hypersensitivity reactions if they are allergic to members of the Poaceae family. When consumed as a tea or supplement, excessive amounts might lead to nausea or stomach discomfort in certain people. Pregnant or breastfeeding women should exercise caution to avoid potential complications.
