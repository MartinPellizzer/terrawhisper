What's the taxonomical classification of Argyreia osyrensis?

Argyreiia osyrensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Solanales, this plant is further categorized into the family Convolvulaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by the genus Argyreiia and the species osyrensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Argyreia osyrensis has a climbing habit characterized by twining stems that support large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent venation.

The foliage consists of broad, cordate blades that often exhibit a velvety texture on their surfaces. Small, fragrant flowers emerge from the axils, typically displaying a pale pink or white coloration. These blooms are arranged in clusters that attract various pollinators throughout the growing season.

        What are the herbal preparations of Argyreia osyrensis?

        Argyreia osyrensis is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice using the plant's leaves and roots.         What side-effects this plant can have?

        Argyreia osyrensis can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Contact with the plant's leaves or stems may trigger redness, itching, or a burning sensation on sensitive skin. Some individuals might experience localized swelling or hives following direct exposure to the foliage. If any part of the plant is consumed, it may lead to nausea, vomiting, or stomach cramps. Monitoring for these symptoms is recommended when handling the species in a garden setting.
