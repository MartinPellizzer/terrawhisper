Argyreia osyrensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Argyreia osyrensis has a climbing habit characterized by twining stems that support large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent venation. The foliage consists of broad, cordate blades that often exhibit a velvety texture on their surfaces. Small, fragrant flowers emerge from the axils, typically displaying a pale pink or white coloration. These blooms are arranged in clusters that attract various pollinators throughout the growing season.
