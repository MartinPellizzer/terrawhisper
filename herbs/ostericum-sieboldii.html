What's the taxonomical classification of Ostericum sieboldii?

Ostericum sieboldii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Apiales. As a member of the family Apiaceae, this plant is specifically categorized under the genus Ostericum, with its unique designation being the species sieboldii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ostericum sieboldii has a rhizomatous rootstock that produces compound leaves consisting of three leaflets with serrated margins and a terminal peduncle bearing a branched panicle of small, white flowers. The leaves typically emerge in a basal rosette and feature petioles that are relatively long and slender. During the flowering stage, the plant displays a delicate structure with numerous tiny blossoms arranged along the stalks. The fruit develops into small, dry schizocarps that follow the pollination period. These morphological characteristics help distinguish the species within its forest habitat.
