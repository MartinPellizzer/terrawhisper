Picea schrenkiana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Picea schrenkiana has a conical to narrowly pyramidal growth habit with a trunk that typically reaches heights of 15 to 30 meters. The branches are often spreading or slightly pendulous, contributing to a dense canopy. Its needles are short, stiff, and four-angled, arranged spirally around the twigs. The cones are cylindrical in shape and transition from a purplish color to brown as they mature. Small, woody scales cover the seeds, which are released once the cones dry.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of the Southern Alps, specifically concentrated in parts of Italy, Slovenia, and Austria. It typically grows in subalpine and alpine zones where the terrain is rugged and rocky. Its distribution is often limited to specific mountain ranges such as the Dolomites and the Julian Alps. These trees favor cold, moist environments found at elevations where they can avoid competition from more dominant forest species. Consequently, its range remains fragmented across isolated mountain peaks throughout its primary territory.

Picea schreenkiana targets various enzymes and inflammatory mediators, specifically inhibiting cyclooxygenase (COX) and lipoxygenase (LOX) proteins to reduce prostaglandin and leukotriene synthesis. The plant's bioactive terpenoids and phenolic compounds interact with these specific protein pathways to mitigate oxidative stress within cellular environments. By binding to these molecular sites, the phytochemicals exert potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. This biochemical engagement provides the foundation for the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and inflammatory ailments.

Picea schreenkiana has antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and expectorant properties that are primarily utilized through its essential oils and resinous extracts.       What side effects this plant can have?

      This plant can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and respiratory discomfort if its needles or resin are mishandled. Contact with the sticky resin may lead to localized redness, itching, or a stinging sensation on the skin. Some individuals might experience an allergic reaction characterized by swelling or a rash upon touching the foliage. Inhaling concentrated particles or volatile compounds from the tree could potentially trigger coughing or throat irritation in sensitive people. Careful handling is recommended to avoid these physiological responses.
