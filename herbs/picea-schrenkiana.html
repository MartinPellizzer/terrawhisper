What's the taxonomical classification of Picea schrenkiana?

Picea schreenkiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Pinidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Pinales and the family Pinaceae, ultimately reaching its specific classification within the genus Picea and the species schreenkiana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Picea schrenkiana has a conical to narrowly pyramidal growth habit with a trunk that typically reaches heights of 15 to 30 meters. The branches are often spreading or slightly pendulous, contributing to a dense canopy. Its needles are short, stiff, and four-angled, arranged spirally around the twigs. The cones are cylindrical in shape and transition from a purplish color to brown as they mature. Small, woody scales cover the seeds, which are released once the cones dry.
