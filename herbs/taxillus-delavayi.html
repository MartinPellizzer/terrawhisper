Taxillus delavayi

What's the taxonomical classification of Taxillus delavayi?

Taxillus delavayi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Santalales and the family Loranthaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Taxillus, and its species designation, delaayi.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various skin conditions, including eczema, psoriasis, and fungal infections. Its medicinal properties are primarily attributed to its potent antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. When applied topically, it helps reduce redness, itching, and swelling associated with chronic skin inflammation. Some traditional practices also utilize extracts from the plant to manage minor wounds and superficial abrasions. These therapeutic applications rely on the plant's ability to inhibit the growth of harmful pathogens on the skin surface.
