Sweet Gale (Myrica gale) Compounds

The primary compounds in Sweet Gale include essential oil, tannins, phenolic acids, and flavonoids. These compounds contribute to its aromatic properties and potential medicinal effects, such as antioxidant and anti-inflammatory actions. Terpenoids and coumarin add to its therapeutic profile, while glycosides, saponins, and alkaloids may support its use in traditional herbal medicine. Anthocyanins contribute to its color and may have additional health benefits.

Sweet Gale (Myrica gale) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Sweet Gale (Myrica gale) contains volatile compounds like methyl salicylate and monoterpenes. These contribute to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The oil is used in traditional medicine for respiratory and skin conditions. It is extracted through steam distillation from the plant's leaves and stems.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Sweet Gale (Myrica gale). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health. Tannins also have antioxidant and antimicrobial effects, making them valuable in herbal medicine.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Sweet Gale (Myrica gale). It contributes to the plant's antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. This compound helps support the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and skin-related conditions. Its presence enhances the overall therapeutic potential of Sweet Gale.

4. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Sweet Gale (Myrica gale) contribute to its antioxidant properties. These compounds help neutralize free radicals, supporting cellular health. They may also influence inflammatory responses and skin conditions. Flavonoids are found in various parts of the plant, including the leaves and bark.

5. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Sweet Gale (Myrica gale) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. They are responsible for the plant's aromatic profile and may support digestive and respiratory health. Terpenoids are commonly extracted for use in herbal remedies.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Sweet Gale (Myrica gale). It contributes to the plant's aromatic profile and has mild anticoagulant properties. Coumarin is also known for its potential to support urinary health and aid in the elimination of toxins from the body.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Sweet Gale (Myrica gale). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Glycosides in Sweet Gale are often used in herbal remedies to support digestive and respiratory health.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Sweet Gale (Myrica gale). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponins may support digestive health and immune function. They are found in the leaves, berries, and roots of this medicinal plant.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Sweet Gale (Myrica gale). It contributes to the plant's potential medicinal properties, though research on its specific effects is limited. Alkaloids in Myrica gale may support traditional uses in herbal medicine, though their therapeutic applications require further scientific validation.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Sweet Gale (Myrica gale). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting skin health and reducing inflammation. Found in the berries, anthocyanin may also aid in detoxification and promote overall cellular health.
