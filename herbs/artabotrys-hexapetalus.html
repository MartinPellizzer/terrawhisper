Artabotrys hexapetalus

What's the taxonomical classification of Artabotrys hexapetalus?

Artabotrys hexapetalus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Magnoliales and into the family Annonaceae. Ultimately, this plant is identified by the genus Artabotrys and is specifically designated as the species hexapetalus.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Artabotrys hexapetalus contains bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, and terpenoids that provide its therapeutic properties. These chemical constituents contribute to the plant's documented antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory activities. What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin diseases, wounds, stomach aches, and various forms of fever. The leaves and stems are often prepared into topical applications to soothe skin irritations and promote the healing of open cuts. When consumed as a decoction, the plant components are believed to alleviate internal digestive distress and abdominal pain. Traditional practitioners also utilize the botanical extracts to help regulate body temperature during febrile episodes. These medicinal applications rely on the specific phytochemical properties found within the plant's various parts.
