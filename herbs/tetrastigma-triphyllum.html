Tetrastigma triphyllum

What's the taxonomical classification of Tetrastigma triphyllum?

Tetrastigma triphyllum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is positioned in the order Vitales. This plant is a member of the family Vitaceae, which further narrows its classification down to the genus Tetrastigma. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by its species name, triphyllum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tetrastigma triphyllum has woody vines characterized by trifoliate leaves, a thick stem with prominent lenticels, and small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers.

The leaves consist of three distinct leaflets that are typically elliptical or oblong in shape with entire margins. Its climbing habit relies on strong, twisting stems that can reach significant heights in forest canopies. The plant also features a specialized sap that is often milky or translucent depending on the specific tissue.

These morphological traits allow the species to thrive as a liana within tropical ecosystems.
