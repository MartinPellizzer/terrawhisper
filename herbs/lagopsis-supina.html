What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lagopsis supina has a prostrate, creeping growth habit characterized by slender, woody stems that spread horizontally along the ground. Tiny, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, typically displaying a structure consistent with the Asteraceae family. The plant's root system is relatively shallow, allowing it to anchor itself firmly in sandy or rocky substrates.
