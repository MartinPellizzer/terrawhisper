Lagopsis supina

What's the taxonomical classification of Lagopsis supina?

Lagopsis supina belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed under the order Lamiales. Within this order, the plant is a member of the family Lamiaceae, falling specifically under the genus Lagopsis. Ultimately, its unique biological identity is defined by its specific designation as the species supina.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat urinary tract infections, bladder inflammation, and various kidney-related ailments. It possesses diuretic properties that help flush out toxins and bacteria from the renal system. The plant is often utilized to alleviate symptoms of cystitis and manage kidney stones. By increasing urine production, it assists in clearing the urinary passages of infectious agents. These therapeutic applications are rooted in its traditional use for promoting renal health.

What are the herbal preparations of Lagopsis supina?

Lagopsis supina is prepared as a decoction, infusion, or topical poultice using the dried or fresh aerial parts of the plant. These herbal preparations are primarily used to extract bioactive compounds for medicinal purposes. Infusions are often brewed in hot water to create a tea for internal consumption. For external ailments, the plant material can be crushed into a poultice to be applied directly to the skin. Additionally, some practitioners utilize tinctures made with alcohol to ensure a more concentrated dose of the plant's properties.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Lagopsis supina can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and potential gastrointestinal distress if ingested. The plant contains chemical compounds that may trigger redness, itching, or swelling upon direct contact with sensitive skin. Some individuals might experience a localized rash or hives after handling the foliage or stems. If consumed accidentally, the plant could lead to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. It is important to use caution and avoid skin contact to prevent these adverse reactions.
