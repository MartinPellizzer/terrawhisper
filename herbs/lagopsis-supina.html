What's the taxonomical classification of Lagopsis supina?

Lagopsis supina belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed under the order Lamiales. Within this order, the plant is a member of the family Lamiaceae, falling specifically under the genus Lagopsis. Ultimately, its unique biological identity is defined by its specific designation as the species supina.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lagopsis supina has a prostrate, creeping growth habit characterized by slender, woody stems that spread horizontally along the ground. The leaves are small, elliptical to lanceolate in shape, and possess entire margins with a somewhat leathery texture. Tiny, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, typically displaying a structure consistent with the Asteraceae family. The plant's root system is relatively shallow, allowing it to anchor itself firmly in sandy or rocky substrates. According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", Lagopsis supina has been used in folk medicine for anti-inflammatory, diuretic, and blood circulation promoting activities. Modern pharmacological investigation has shown that the plant possesses antioxidative, myocardioprotective, and improvements in blood and lymphatic microcirculation. In a study involving 30 male Sprague-Dawley rats divided into 3 groups of 10 animals, an oral administration of 460 mg/kg of LS once daily for 7 successive days was tested. This treatment resulted in a significant increase in total urine output (p < 0.01) and a decrease in the expression levels of AQP-1, 2 and 3 proteins. Furthermore, LS treatment decreased levels of Ang II, ADH and ALD (p < 0.05 or p < 0.01) while increasing ANP levels (p < 0.05).
