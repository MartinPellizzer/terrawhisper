Vaccinium sprengelii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vaccinium sprengelii has low-growing, creeping stems that form dense mats, characterized by small, ovate to elliptical leaves with finely serrated margins and a glabrous surface. The foliage is typically dark green on the upper side and often appears paler underneath. Its flowers are small and urceolate, hanging from delicate pedicels during the blooming season. These blossoms eventually develop into small, fleshy berries that serve as the plant's fruit. The overall architecture of the plant allows it to colonize rocky or acidic ground through its spreading rhizomatous growth.
