Dioscorea persimilis

What's the taxonomical classification of Dioscorea persimilis?

Dioscorea persimilis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Dioscoreales and the family Dioscoreaceae, placing it within the genus Dioscorea, from which its specific epithet, persimilis, is derived.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dioscorea persimilis has climbing stems that produce cordate to sagittate leaves with entire margins and acute apices. These vines utilize twining stems to ascend through surrounding vegetation in search of light. The leaf surfaces often exhibit a smooth texture with distinct venation patterns extending from the base. Small, inconspicuous flowers typically emerge from the axils of the leaves during the growing season. The plant may also develop tuberous underground structures used for nutrient storage and survival.
