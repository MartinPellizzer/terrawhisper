Pteris vittata

What's the taxonomical classification of Pteris vittata?

Pteris vittata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. Further taxonomic refinement places this plant in the family Pteridaceae, within the genus Pteris, and specifically identifies it as the species vittata.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Pteris vittata contains various phytochemicals including flavonoids, tannins, phenols, and alkaloids that contribute to its biological activity. These specific compounds are often studied for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties in traditional medicinal practices. Some research also suggests that the plant possesses antimicrobial qualities due to its unique chemical profile. Furthermore, the presence of certain organic acids may influence how the plant interacts with biological systems. These chemical constituents collectively define its potential role in various therapeutic applications.

What are the therapeutic actions of Pteris vittata?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat intestinal parasites including tapeworms, roundworms, and various other helminths. It contains specific compounds that act as anthelmintics to expel worms from the digestive tract. Traditional medicinal practices often utilize its fern fronds to address stomach ailments caused by parasitic infections. Some studies suggest its efficacy is linked to the presence of certain phytochemicals that disrupt the life cycles of these organisms. Careful dosage is necessary because the plant can be toxic if consumed in large quantities.
