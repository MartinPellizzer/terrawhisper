What is Senna hilariana?

Senna hilariana is a perennial shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, commonly known as legumes. This species has bipinnate leaves, with 7-15 pairs of leaflets, that are dark green in color and 6-12 mm long. Senna hilariana produces small, yellow flowers in axillary racemes, with calyxes that are 5-8 mm long and corollas that are 5-7 mm long.

The plant grows to a height of 1.5-3 meters, with woody stems and a spread of 1.5-2.5 meters.
