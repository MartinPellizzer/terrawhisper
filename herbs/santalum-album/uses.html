Sandalwood (Santalum album) Uses

Sandalwood is used to treat respiratory infections, asthma, and cough due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It also helps with skin infections, inflammatory conditions, and wound healing. Its applications extend to psoriasis, fungal infections, and gastrointestinal issues like ulcers, showcasing its broad therapeutic value.

Sandalwood (Santalum album) Monograph

1. Respiratory infections

Sandalwood is used in traditional herbal medicine to treat respiratory infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps alleviate symptoms such as coughing, congestion, and inflammation in the airways. Often prepared as an inhalant or topical application, it supports respiratory health and aids in clearing mucus. Its soothing effects make it a valued remedy in managing mild to moderate respiratory conditions.

2. Asthma

Sandalwood is used in traditional herbal medicine to help alleviate asthma symptoms. It is believed to have bronchodilatory effects, which may help open airways and reduce inflammation. Its essential oil is often used in inhalation therapies to ease breathing and promote respiratory health.

3. Cough

Sandalwood is used in traditional herbal medicine to soothe coughs due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps clear mucus from the respiratory tract and reduces irritation in the throat. Often prepared as a paste or inhalation, it provides relief for both dry and productive coughs.

4. Skin infections

Sandalwood is used topically to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce inflammation and prevent bacterial growth. Its soothing effects promote healing and can alleviate symptoms of fungal and bacterial infections. It is often applied as an oil or in herbal formulations.

5. Inflammatory skin conditions

Sandalwood is used in herbal medicine to treat inflammatory skin conditions due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and infections in conditions like eczema and psoriasis. Topical application of sandalwood oil or paste is common for its soothing and healing effects on the skin.

6. Skin wound healing

Sandalwood oil is applied topically to promote skin wound healing due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps prevent infections and reduces inflammation, accelerating the repair process. The compound santalol in sandalwood also stimulates cell regeneration, aiding in faster tissue recovery.

7. Psoriasis

Sandalwood is traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat psoriasis due to its anti-inflammatory and skin-soothing properties. It helps reduce redness, scaling, and itching associated with the condition. Its essential oil is often applied topically to calm skin irritation and promote healing. Regular use may support long-term management of psoriasis symptoms.

8. Fungal infections

Sandalwood is used in traditional systems to treat fungal infections due to its antimicrobial properties. It contains compounds like santalol and sesquiterpenes that inhibit fungal growth. It is often applied topically as an oil or in formulations to combat skin infections like ringworm and athlete's foot. Its effectiveness is attributed to its ability to disrupt fungal cell membranes.

9. Gastrointestinal infections

Sandalwood exhibits antimicrobial properties that help combat gastrointestinal infections. It inhibits the growth of harmful bacteria like Escherichia coli and Salmonella. Used in herbal formulations, it supports digestive health and reduces inflammation in the gut. Its essential oils are often incorporated into remedies for dysentery and food poisoning.

10. Stomach ulcers

Sandalwood is used in traditional herbal medicine to treat stomach ulcers due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce gastric acid secretion and soothe the lining of the stomach. Preparations often include sandalwood oil or powder, taken orally to promote healing and alleviate symptoms.
