Sandalwood (Santalum album) Actions

Sandalwood exhibits several primary therapeutic actions including anti-inflammatory, tonic, and sedative properties. It also acts as an astringent, carminative, and stimulant, while possessing bitter and antispasmodic effects. Additionally, it is known for its diuretic and expectorant qualities, supporting various health conditions through these actions.

Sandalwood (Santalum album) Monograph

1. Anti-inflammatory

Sandalwood has an anti-inflammatory action, primarily due to its high content of sesquiterpene compounds like santalol and santalbic acid. These compounds inhibit inflammatory pathways by reducing the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines and mediators. This makes it effective in treating conditions like arthritis and skin inflammation.

2. Tonic

Sandalwood has a tonic action, enhancing vitality and supporting overall bodily function. It is used to invigorate the body, improve digestion, and promote mental clarity. Its warming properties help balance energy levels, making it beneficial for those experiencing fatigue or weakness.

3. Sedative

Sandalwood has a sedative action, promoting calmness and reducing anxiety. It works by influencing the central nervous system, helping to ease restlessness and improve sleep quality. This effect makes it useful in formulations for stress relief and insomnia.

4. Astringent

Sandalwood has an astringent action, which helps to tone and tighten tissues. It is particularly effective for conditions involving excess mucus or inflammation. This property makes it useful in respiratory and digestive disorders. It also aids in reducing swelling and promoting healing.

5. Carminative

Sandalwood has a carminative action, helping to relieve gas and bloating in the digestive system. It stimulates the movement of the gastrointestinal tract, promoting the expulsion of trapped air and reducing discomfort. This property makes it beneficial for digestive disorders and overall gut health.

6. Stimulant

Sandalwood has a stimulant action, enhancing mental alertness and circulation. It is used to invigorate the body and mind, promoting clarity and energy. This effect is attributed to its essential oils and resinous compounds. It supports cognitive function and may help alleviate fatigue.

7. Bitter

Sandalwood has a bitter action, which stimulates digestion and enhances nutrient absorption. This property helps to clear mucous and improve liver function. It also has a drying effect, making it useful for conditions involving excess dampness or phlegm. The bitter taste activates the body's natural detoxification processes.

8. Antispasmodic

Sandalwood has an antispasmodic action, which helps relieve muscle spasms and cramps. It works by relaxing smooth muscle tissue, making it effective for conditions like colic, menstrual cramps, and gastrointestinal spasms. This property makes it a valuable herb in herbal medicine for managing spasmodic disorders.

9. Diuretic

Sandalwood has a diuretic action, promoting the production and excretion of urine. This helps in eliminating excess fluids and toxins from the body. It supports kidney function and can be beneficial in conditions involving fluid retention. Its mild nature makes it suitable for regular use under guidance.

10. Expectorant

Sandalwood has an expectorant action, helping to loosen and expel mucus from the respiratory tract. It is used to alleviate symptoms of coughs and congestion by promoting the clearance of phlegm. This property makes it beneficial in treating respiratory conditions characterized by excess mucus production.
