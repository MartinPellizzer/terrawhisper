10 Medicinal Constituents Of Santalum Album (Sandalwood)

Santalum album has active constituents such as alpha-santalol, beta-santalol, and santalol.

These compounds possess anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiseptic properties, which can help soothe skin irritations, wounds, and infections. They can also improve respiratory health by reducing inflammation and opening airways, providing relief for individuals with asthma and other respiratory conditions.

This can significantly improve the quality of life for those affected, promoting better breathing and overall well-being.

1. Sesquiterpenes

Santalum album sesquiterpenes is a class of natural compounds responsible for its distinctive aroma and therapeutic properties.

These volatile molecules are found in the heartwood of the tree and are released during distillation, giving sandalwood essential oil its characteristic woody, sweet scent.

Sesquiterpenes have been shown to possess anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial activities, making them valuable for various applications in perfumery, cosmetics, and traditional medicine.
