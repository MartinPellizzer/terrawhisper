10 Possible Side Effects Of Santalum Album

Santalum album has some side effects when used improperly, such as skin irritation, allergic reactions, and respiratory problems.

These side effects occur due to the essential oils present in the herb, which can cause inflammation and trigger allergies.

In severe cases, improper use can lead to anaphylaxis, which can worsen a person's quality of life, causing difficulty breathing, swallowing, and even life-threatening consequences.

1. Causes skin irritation

Santalum album causes skin irritation due to its high concentration of sesquiterpenes, which can cause allergic reactions and inflammation in some individuals.

The oil's aromatic compounds may also irritate the skin's natural barrier, leading to redness, itching, and discomfort.

Additionally, Santalum album may contain small amounts of other irritating compounds, such as furfural and vanillin, which can exacerbate skin issues in sensitive individuals.

2. Induces allergic reactions

Santalum album induces allergic reactions in some individuals due to its volatile oil composition.

The allergenic compounds present in the oil, such as sesquiterpenes and phenolic acids, can cause skin irritation, redness, and itching upon contact or exposure.

These allergens may also trigger respiratory issues, including asthma-like symptoms, in people with sensitized lungs.

3. Triggers respiratory issues

Santalum album triggers respiratory issues due to its potent allergenic properties.

The wood and oil of this plant contain sesquiterpene lactones, which can cause irritation and inflammation in the lungs and airways.

When inhaled, these compounds can stimulate an allergic response, leading to symptoms such as coughing, wheezing, and shortness of breath.

4. Produces gastrointestinal upset

Santalum album produces gastrointestinal upset.

This is attributed to its potential to interact with certain enzymes in the digestive system, leading to an imbalance of gut bacteria and altered gut motility.

As a result, users may experience symptoms such as nausea, bloating, diarrhea, or stomach cramps due to Santalum album's ability to alter the normal functioning of the gastrointestinal tract.

5. Increases heart rate

Santalum album increases heart rate due to its natural ability to stimulate the cardiovascular system.

The essential oils present in Sandalum album contain compounds that can increase blood flow and pressure, leading to an elevated heart rate. This effect is thought to be a result of the oil's ability to relax the muscles and improve circulation, causing the heart to work harder to maintain this increased flow.

As a result, users may experience palpitations or rapid heartbeat when using Sandalum album products.

6. Lowers blood pressure

Santalum album lowers blood pressure by causing a significant drop in systolic and diastolic blood pressure due to its active compound, beta-santalol.

This substance binds to alpha-adrenergic receptors, thereby inhibiting the release of norepinephrine, a hormone that constricts blood vessels.

As a result, blood vessels dilate, leading to decreased blood pressure.

7. Stimulates nervous system

Santalum album stimulates nervous system.

This is due to its rich composition of sesquiterpenes, particularly beta-selinene and alpha-cedrene, which can increase the production of neurotransmitters such as dopamine and serotonin in the brain.

This stimulation can lead to feelings of heightened alertness, increased energy levels, and enhanced cognitive function, but may also result in anxiety, agitation, or insomnia in some individuals.

8. Interferes with digestion

Santalum album interferes with digestion by stimulating the digestive system and increasing the production of stomach acid.

This can lead to symptoms such as heartburn, nausea, and bloating. Additionally, sandalwood's astringent properties may cause constipation in some individuals.

These effects are thought to be caused by the essential oils present in Santalum album, which can irritate the mucous membranes and disrupt normal digestion processes.

9. Affects liver function

Santalum album affects liver function by interfering with its ability to process and eliminate toxic substances from the body.

This is due to the presence of certain compounds in sandalwood oil, such as sesquiterpenes and terpenoids, which can alter liver enzymes and disrupt normal metabolic pathways.

As a result, individuals may experience symptoms of liver damage or impairment, including jaundice, abdominal pain, and dark urine.

10. Impairs cognitive ability

Santalum album impairs cognitive ability by its potential to interact with certain neurotransmitters in the brain, such as GABA and glutamate.

This interaction can disrupt normal neural functioning, leading to impaired memory, attention, and concentration.

Additionally, the oil's sedative properties can cause drowsiness, which may also contribute to decreased cognitive performance.
