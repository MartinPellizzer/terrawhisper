What's the taxonomical classification of Lagotis ramalana?

Lagotis ramalana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Lamiales. This plant is specifically situated within the family Plantaginaceae, which places it in the genus Lagotis, with its unique identification as the species ramanalana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lagotis ramalana has a creeping rhizome that produces small, ovate to lanceolate leaves arranged in a distichous pattern along slender, branching stems. The foliage typically measures only a few millimeters in length and lacks a distinct petiole. These plants often form dense, moss-like mats as the stems sprawl across damp substrates. The reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous sori located on the underside of the leaf segments. This diminutive morphology allows the species to occupy niche microhabitats in tropical forest environments.
