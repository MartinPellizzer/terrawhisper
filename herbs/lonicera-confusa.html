Lonicera confusa

What's the taxonomical classification of Lonicera confusa?

Lonicera confusa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Dipsacales. This plant is further categorized within the family Caprifoliaceae, specifically nested under the genus Lonicera, with its unique identity defined by the species name confusa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lonicera confusa has woody, sprawling stems that form dense, arching shrubs reaching heights of up to two meters. The leaves are simple, opposite, and elliptic to ovate in shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils, typically presenting in pairs or solitary clusters. These blossoms are often white or pale yellow and transition to a deeper hue as they age. The plant eventually produces small, fleshy berries that range in color from red to black when fully ripe.
