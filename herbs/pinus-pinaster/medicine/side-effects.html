10 Possible Side Effects Of Pinus Pinaster (Maritime Pine)

Pinus pinaster has some side effects when used improperly, such as allergic reactions, respiratory issues, and digestive problems.

These side effects can be caused by consuming excessive amounts, interacting with other medications, or being sensitive to certain compounds.

For example, an allergic reaction can lead to difficulty breathing, hives, or swelling, which can severely impact daily activities and overall well-being.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Pinus pinaster if used imporperly.

1. Exacerbates existing conditions

Pinus pinaster causes allergic reactions due to the presence of oleoresin in its needles and cones.

This sticky sap can cause skin irritation, itching, and redness in some individuals, while others may experience respiratory issues such as sneezing, coughing, and shortness of breath when exposed to airborne pollen from the trees' reproductive structures.

Additionally, people with pre-existing allergies or sensitivities may react strongly to the tree's resinous sap.

2. Exacerbates existing conditions

Pinus pinaster triggers respiratory issues due to its potent allergenic properties.

The resin and sap of the plant contain oleoresin, which can cause inflammation and irritation in the airways, leading to symptoms such as coughing, wheezing, and shortness of breath.

Additionally, the pollen of Pinus pinaster is a common allergen that can exacerbate respiratory conditions like asthma and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).

3. Exacerbates existing conditions

Pinus pinaster increases heart rate rapidly due to its stimulatory properties that can cause an adrenaline rush in some individuals.

The terpenes and flavonoids present in the extract of this tree species may contribute to this effect by interacting with the body's sympathetic nervous system, leading to increased cardiac activity and rapid heartbeat.

This side effect is often temporary but can be uncomfortable for those experiencing it.

4. Exacerbates existing conditions

Pinus pinaster produces excessive sweating in some individuals due to its unique chemical composition.

The bark and leaves of the plant contain a compound called terpinen-4-ol, which can stimulate the sweat glands and cause an increase in perspiration.

Additionally, the aromatic oils present in Pinus pinaster may also contribute to this side effect by irritating the skin and triggering a response that leads to excessive sweating.

5. Exacerbates existing conditions

Pinus pinaster induces nausea and vomiting in some individuals due to its volatile oils, particularly pinosylvin and terpinen-4-ol.

These compounds can stimulate the vomiting center in the brain, leading to gastrointestinal upset.

Additionally, the beta-pinene present in Pinus pinaster may also contribute to this adverse effect by altering gut motility and secretion.

6. Exacerbates existing conditions

Pinus pinaster disrupts hormonal balance by containing phytoestrogens, which mimic the effects of estrogen in the body.

These compounds can bind to estrogen receptors, altering the normal functioning of hormones and leading to changes in reproductive function, metabolism, and other bodily processes.

This disruption can lead to a range of symptoms, including changes in menstrual cycles, mood swings, and altered libido.

7. Exacerbates existing conditions

Pinus pinaster lowers blood pressure significantly.

This is due to its rich content of oleuropein and terpinen-4-ol, which are known for their ability to relax blood vessels and reduce peripheral resistance. Additionally, Pinus pinaster has been found to inhibit the activity of angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE), a key player in regulating blood pressure.

These compounds likely contribute to the hypotensive effects observed when using Pinus pinaster.

8. Exacerbates existing conditions

Pinus pinaster affects kidney function negatively.

The compound terpinen-4-ol present in its essential oil can reduce blood flow to the kidneys, leading to a decrease in their ability to filter waste and excess fluids from the body. This may cause an accumulation of toxic substances, potentially resulting in kidney damage or dysfunction.

Additionally, the high levels of antioxidants in Pinus pinaster can also disrupt normal kidney functioning by altering the way the kidneys respond to stress signals.

9. Exacerbates existing conditions

Pinus pinaster interacts with medications.

When taken in combination with certain prescription drugs, Pinus pinaster may cause an increase in bleeding risk due to its ability to inhibit platelet aggregation and prolong bleeding time.

This is because the extract's flavonoids and terpenoids can enhance the effects of anticoagulant medications, leading to potentially serious adverse reactions.

Pinus pinaster causes skin irritation in some individuals due to its volatile oils and resinous compounds.

These substances can be absorbed through the skin, triggering an allergic reaction or irritant contact dermatitis.

The severity of the reaction may vary depending on the individual's sensitivity and exposure level.
