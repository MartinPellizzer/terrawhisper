Sabina coahuilensis

Sabina coahuilensis, commonly known as Coahuila Juniper or Coahuila Savin, is a type of plant that belongs to the cypress family (Cupressaceae).

It is a shrub-like evergreen plant native to the southwestern region of North America, and has been traditionally used in folk medicine for various health benefits.

The health benefits of Sabina coahuilensis include its potential to act as an anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antifungal agent, which can help to alleviate symptoms of digestive issues, respiratory problems, and skin conditions.

The bioactive constituents of Sabina coahuilensis include terpenes, such as alpha-pinene and beta-pinene, as well as flavonoids and phenolic acids, which contribute to its therapeutic actions, including its ability to reduce inflammation, improve immune function, and exhibit antioxidant properties.

Possible Side Effects of Sabina coahuilensis

Sabina coahuilensis can have side effects if used improperly, such as allergic reactions, skin irritation, and gastrointestinal issues.

In rare cases, overuse of the plant has been linked to more severe side effects, including seizures, respiratory failure, and liver damage.

The plant's active compounds can also interact with certain medications, exacerbating conditions such as high blood pressure and diabetes.

Furthermore, improper preparation and dosing can lead to an overdose, resulting in symptoms like dizziness, nausea, and headaches.
