What's the taxonomical classification of Thymus altaicus?

Thymus altaicus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Lamiales. This plant is further categorized into the family Lamiaceae, which is characteristic of many aromatic herbs. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Thymus, and its specific designation is the species altaicus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Thymus altaicus has woody, perennial stems with small, elliptical, opposite leaves that are typically 3 to 6 millimeters long and characterized by entire or slightly serrated margins. The plant forms dense, low-growing mats or cushions that spread horizontally across the ground. Its flowers are small, tubular, and pink to purplish in color, emerging from axillary clusters during the summer months. The calyx is typically two-lipped and covered in fine hairs, while the corolla is bilabiate. This species is distinguished from similar thyme varieties by its specific leaf shape and the structure of its reproductive organs.

These plants typically inhabit rocky slopes and dry, sunny grasslands where well-drained soil is available.
