What is Koeleria subalpestris?

Koeleria subalpestris is a species of perennial grass in the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, glabrous leaf sheaths, and ligules that are shorter than 2 mm. The leaves are narrow, linear, and 2-10 mm wide, with a blade that is 10-40 cm long and a membranous to ciliate margin. The inflorescence is a spike-like panicle, 5-20 cm long, with spikelets that are 1-2 cm long and have 3-5 florets.

The species is typically found in moist to mesic conditions in alpine to subalpine habitats.
