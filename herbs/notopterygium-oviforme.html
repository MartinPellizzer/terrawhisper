Notopterygium oviforme

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Notopterygium oviforme has large, egg-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and feature prominent, wing-like margins. The leaf blades are typically pinnately divided into several distinct segments with serrated edges. These leaves are supported by long, sturdy petioles that emerge from a central rhizome. The plant also develops upright flowering stalks that bear small, clustered blossoms. The overall structure is characterized by a lush, herbaceous growth habit.
