Passion Fruit (Passiflora edulis)

Passion Fruit (Passiflora edulis) is a member of the Solanaceae family, native to Brazil, Amazon Basin, and South America. Traditionally, its fruits, leaves, and flowers have been used for infusions, culinary uses, and decoctions.

This herb is particularly valued for its sedative, antispasmodic, and anti-inflammatory actions, and has a long history of use in native american herbal medicine, european herbal medicine, and african traditional medicine.
