Callicarpa membranacea

What's the taxonomical classification of Callicarpa membranacea?

Callicarpa membranacea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Callicarpa, and its specific species epithet, membranacea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Callicarpa membranacea has opposite, simple, ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated margins and a thin, membranous texture.

The plant typically exhibits a shrubby growth habit with slender, woody stems. Its foliage is characterized by prominent venation that extends from the midrib toward the leaf edges. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in axillary clusters, eventually giving way to small, globose drupes.

These fruits often appear in dense bunches and change color as they mature.
