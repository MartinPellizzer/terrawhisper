Callicarpa membranacea

What's the taxonomical classification of Callicarpa membranacea?

Callicarpa membranacea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Callicarpa, and its specific species epithet, membranacea.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Callicarpa membranacea contains bioactive phytochemicals including triterpenoids, flavonoids, and phenolic compounds that exhibit significant pharmacological activities. These specific chemical constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's documented antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Researchers have identified various terpenoid derivatives within the leaves and stems that may contribute to antimicrobial effects. Additionally, the presence of polyphenols suggests a potential role in protecting cells against oxidative stress. These medicinal properties make the species a subject of interest for developing new natural therapeutic agents.

What are the therapeutic actions of Callicarpa membranacea?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, stomach pains, and various inflammatory conditions. Its leaves are often prepared into medicinal decoctions or topical applications to soothe irritated skin. The plant also contains bioactive compounds that help alleviate gastrointestinal distress. Traditional healers utilize its properties to manage minor ailments and promote healing. Such applications are based on long-standing ethnobotanical practices in specific regions.
