Goodyera schlechtendaliana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It is further categorized within the family Orchidaceae, which places it among the diverse group of orchids. Within this family, it is identified under the genus Goodyera, with its specific classification being the species schlechtendaliana.

Additionally, the phytosterol β-sitosterol helps regulate cholesterol levels and supports overall cellular health. Together, these substances form a complex chemical profile that justifies the plant's use in traditional medicine.

As discussed in a study, seven compounds were obtained from a 95% ethanol extract of Goodyera schlechtendaliana. These identified structures include syringaldehyde (I), 5-hydroxymethylfurfural (II), alloimperatorin (III), vanillic acid (IV), ferulic acid (V), glyceroyl monopalmitate (VI), and β-sitosterol (VII). While compounds II-VII are reported from this genus, compounds I-VII are reported from this plant for the first time.
