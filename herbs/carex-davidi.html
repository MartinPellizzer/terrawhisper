Carex davidi

What's the taxonomical classification of Carex davidi?

Carex davidi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls into the subclass Magnoliidae and is further categorized under the order Poales. This plant is a member of the family Cyperaceae, situated within the genus Carex, and is specifically identified by its species name, davidi.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Carex davidi has slender, upright culms that typically reach heights of 20 to 40 centimeters and are topped by inconspicuous, yellowish-green inflorescences.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often slightly channeled and taper to a fine point at the apex. Its reproductive structures consist of small, spike-like clusters that emerge from the upper portions of the stems. The perigynia, which enclose the tiny achenes, are characterized by their specific shape and texture unique to this species.

These morphological traits allow the plant to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

        What are the herbal preparations of Carex davidi?

        Carex davidi is prepared as a decoction by boiling the rhizomes or as a topical paste applied directly to the skin. These traditional medicinal preparations are primarily utilized to address inflammatory conditions and promote wound healing.         What side-effects this plant can have?

        Carex davidi can cause mild skin irritation or allergic contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals through direct contact with its foliage. While generally considered non-toxic to humans, some people may experience itching or redness upon touching the plant. There is limited clinical research regarding its systemic toxicity, but caution is advised around children and pets. It is best to wear gloves when handling the plant to prevent any unexpected dermatological reactions.
