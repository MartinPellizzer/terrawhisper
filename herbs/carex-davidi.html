Carex davidi

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Carex davidi has slender, upright culms that typically reach heights of 20 to 40 centimeters and are topped by inconspicuous, yellowish-green inflorescences. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often slightly channeled and taper to a fine point at the apex. Its reproductive structures consist of small, spike-like clusters that emerge from the upper portions of the stems. The perigynia, which enclose the tiny achenes, are characterized by their specific shape and texture unique to this species. These morphological traits allow the plant to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

      Carex davidi can cause mild skin irritation or allergic contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals through direct contact with its foliage. There is limited clinical research regarding its systemic toxicity, but caution is advised around children and pets. It is best to wear gloves when handling the plant to prevent any unexpected dermatological reactions.
