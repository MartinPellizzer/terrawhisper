What is Astragalus palenae?

Astragalus palenae is an annual or biennial legume native to Mexico, belonging to the family Fabaceae.

It has a taproot, a rosette of basal leaves, and a flowering stem that reaches up to 30 cm in height. The leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are elliptic to ovate in shape, 2-4 cm long, and have a finely serrated margin. The inflorescence is a spike or a raceme of 5-10 flowers, each 7-10 mm long, with a pink or purple corolla.

The fruit is a dehiscent pod, 2-4 cm long, containing 2-4 seeds.

What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

The main herbal preparations of Astragalus palenae are tincture, tea, infusion, capsules, and decoction.

Tincture is a concentrated liquid extract of the plant, usually made with a solvent such as ethanol, which is then diluted with water to create a medicinal solution. Tea and infusion are both made by steeping the dried plant material in hot water to release its active compounds, with tea being a more concentrated form of infusion. Capsules contain dried and powdered Astragalus palenae, which is often standardized to contain a specific amount of active compounds such as flavonoids and polysaccharides.

Decoction involves boiling the plant material in water for a longer period to release its active compounds, particularly the starches and fibers, making it a more traditional method of preparation.
