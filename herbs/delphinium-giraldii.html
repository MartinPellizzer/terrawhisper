Delphinium giraldii

What's the taxonomical classification of Delphinium giraldii?

Delphinium giraldii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Ranunculales. This species is a representative of the family Ranunculaceae, specifically situated within the genus Delphinium, and is identified by its unique species designation, giraldii.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Delphinium giraldii can cause severe poisoning through the ingestion of its toxic alkaloids, which may lead to symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and irregular heart rhythms. These dangerous compounds can affect the central nervous system, potentially causing muscle weakness or paralysis in extreme cases. Contact with the plant may also trigger skin irritation or allergic reactions in sensitive individuals. Because the toxicity levels can be quite high, accidental consumption requires immediate medical intervention to prevent life-threatening complications. It is crucial to handle this species with care to avoid both oral and topical exposure.
