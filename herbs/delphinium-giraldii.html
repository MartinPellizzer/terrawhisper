Delphinium giraldii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Delphinium giraldii has erect, herbaceous stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters and bear deeply palmately lobed leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is typically dark green and provides a dense base for the plant's large, terminal racemes. These inflorescences consist of numerous zygomorphic flowers that display a distinct spur at the back of each blossom. The petals often exhibit shades of blue or violet, sometimes accented by contrasting colors on the sepals. Small, glandular hairs may be present on the stems and calyx, adding a fine texture to the overall structure.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Delphinium giraldii can cause severe poisoning through the ingestion of its toxic alkaloids, which may lead to symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and irregular heart rhythms. These dangerous compounds can affect the central nervous system, potentially causing muscle weakness or paralysis in extreme cases. Contact with the plant may also trigger skin irritation or allergic reactions in sensitive individuals. Because the toxicity levels can be quite high, accidental consumption requires immediate medical intervention to prevent life-threatening complications. It is crucial to handle this species with care to avoid both oral and topical exposure.
