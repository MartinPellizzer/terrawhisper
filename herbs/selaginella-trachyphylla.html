What's the taxonomical classification of Selaginella trachyphylla?

Selaginella trachyphylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is classified in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Lycopodiidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Selaginellales and the family Selaginellaceae. Finally, the plant is situated within the genus Selaginella, specifically identified as the species trachyphylla.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Selaginella trachyphylla has small, scale-like leaves that are arranged in four distinct rows along its slender, creeping stems.

These leaves are characterized by their thin, almost translucent texture and a somewhat lanceolate shape. The plant typically exhibits a delicate, branching growth habit that allows it to spread across moist substrates. Each leaf is closely appressed to the stem, creating a dense, textured appearance.

This morphological structure is specialized for maximizing surface area in humid, shaded environments.
