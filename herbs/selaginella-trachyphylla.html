Selaginella trachyphylla

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Selaginella trachyphylla has small, scale-like leaves that are arranged in four distinct rows along its slender, creeping stems. These leaves are characterized by their thin, almost translucent texture and a somewhat lanceolate shape. The plant typically exhibits a delicate, branching growth habit that allows it to spread across moist substrates. Each leaf is closely appressed to the stem, creating a dense, textured appearance. This morphological structure is specialized for maximizing surface area in humid, shaded environments.
