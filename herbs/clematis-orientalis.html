Clematis orientalis

What's the taxonomical classification of Clematis orientalis?

Clematis orientalis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It further falls into the order Ranunculales and is a member of the family Ranunculaceae. Ultimately, its specific biological identity is defined by its placement within the genus Clematis, with its unique classification designated as the species orientalis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Clematis orientalis contains various phytochemicals including protoanemonin, flavonoids, and various alkaloids that contribute to its chemical profile. The presence of protoanemonin is particularly significant because this compound can act as a skin irritant when the plant is handled. While some species within the Clematis genus have been researched for potential sedative or analgesic properties, the specific medicinal applications for this variety remain largely undocumented in modern clinical practice. Historical uses of similar plants often centered on their ability to act as purgatives or emetics. Due to the potential toxicity of its chemical constituents, the plant is generally regarded as caution-inducing rather than therapeutic.

What are the therapeutic actions of Clematis orientalis?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat respiratory conditions such as cough, asthma, and bronchitis, as well as skin irritations and digestive issues. Historically, practitioners utilized its roots and leaves to help clear mucus from the lungs and soothe inflamed airways. It was also applied topically to address minor skin ailments through various traditional preparations. While some cultures employed it for its potential to calm the nervous system, its use remains largely rooted in folk medicine. Modern medical use is limited due to the plant's potential toxicity if not prepared correctly.
