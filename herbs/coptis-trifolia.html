Possible Side Effects of coptis trifolia

Coptis trifolia can have side effects if used improperly, such as gastrointestinal problems, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, due to its high concentration of berberine.

Overuse of the herb can also lead to headaches, dizziness, and fatigue in some individuals.

In rare cases, high doses of coptis trifolia may cause allergic reactions, such as skin rashes and itching.

Prolonged use or excessive consumption can also interact with certain medications, exacerbating conditions like low blood pressure and bleeding disorders.
