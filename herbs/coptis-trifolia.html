Trifoliate Coptis

Coptis trifolia

Trifoliate Coptis (Coptis trifolia) is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia, known for its medicinal properties and traditional use in treating digestive and inflammatory conditions.

Botanical Identification

Trifoliate Coptis, or Coptis trifolia, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is also known as Three-leaf Goldthread in some regions. This perennial herb is native to eastern Asia, particularly China. It features trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers, with a rhizomatous root system. Synonyms include Coptis chinensis and Coptis delavayi in traditional herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Magnoliopsida
  • Order: Ranunculales
  • Family: Ranunculaceae
  • Genus: Coptis
  • Species: Coptis trifolia

Trifoliate Coptis Uses.

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and inflammation due to its alkaloid content.

Infusion: Used for respiratory conditions and mild digestive complaints.

Tincture: Used for pain relief and as an antiseptic due to its active alkaloids.

Safety Profile

Trifoliate Coptis is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. Long-term use or high doses should be avoided, and it is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to potential adverse effects on fetal or infant development.
