Trifoliate Coptis

Coptis trifolia

Trifoliate Coptis (Coptis trifolia) is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia, known for its medicinal properties and traditional use in treating digestive and inflammatory conditions.

Trifoliate Coptis (Coptis trifolia) is a species in the genus Coptis, belonging to the family Ranunculaceae, and is classified within the order Ranunculales, class Magnoliopsida, division Magnoliophyta, and kingdom Plantae.

Morphology

Trifoliate Coptis (Coptis trifolia) is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous root system, trifoliate leaves composed of three ovate to lanceolate leaflets, and yellow flowers arranged in terminal clusters.

Distribution

Trifoliate Coptis (Coptis trifolia) is native to the mountainous regions of southern China, particularly in provinces such as Sichuan, Yunnan, and Guizhou, where it thrives in humid, temperate climates with well-drained soils.

Traditional Uses

Trifoliate Coptis has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and analgesic properties, particularly in treating conditions such as digestive disorders, skin infections, and fever.

Medicinal Systems
