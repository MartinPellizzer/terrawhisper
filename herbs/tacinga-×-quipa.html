What is Tacinga × quipa?

Tacinga × quipa is a hybrid cactus species in the family Cactaceae, resulting from the cross between Tacinga inermis and Tacinga wendlanii.

It has a columnar stem with a diameter of up to 15 cm and a height of approximately 3 meters, bearing 2-4 central spines and 6-8 radial spines. The plant's stems are covered with fine, yellowish-brown spines, and its flowers are yellow, funnel-shaped, and nocturnal, measuring 6-7.5 cm in diameter. The flowers are followed by red or yellow fruits, which are edible and contain 3-4 seeds.

The plant's growth is relatively fast, and it is found in the wild in Brazil, in the states of Minas Gerais and Bahia.

These compounds have been identified as responsible for the antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties of Tacinga × quipa. What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

The main herbal preparations of Tacinga × quipa are infusion, tea, tincture, decoction, and salve.

Infusion and tea are prepared by steeping the leaves in hot water, typically consumed for its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Tincture is a concentrated liquid extract, often used to treat respiratory issues such as bronchitis and asthma, due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. Decoction is a longer steeping process, used to extract the roots and stems, which are consumed for its potential anti-diarrheal and antimicrobial properties.

The salve preparation involves mixing the plant extract with a carrier oil, used topically to treat skin conditions such as wounds and eczema.

The improper use of Tacinga × quipa can also cause diarrhea, as the saponins and alkaloids can irritate the intestinal tract and lead to gastrointestinal upset.
