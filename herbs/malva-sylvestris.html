Malva Sylvestris

Malva sylvestris, commonly known as the mallow, is a perennial herb belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its numerous health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which help to soothe digestive issues, reduce inflammation, and protect against infections.

The therapeutic actions of Malva sylvestris include demulcent, anti-inflammatory, and expectorant properties, making it effective in treating conditions such as sore throats, coughs, and skin irritations.

The bioactive constituents of Malva sylvestris include mucilages, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

According to "Journal of ethnopharmacology", Malva sylvestris is one of the most frequently used plants in veterinary medicine, typically used to treat digestive troubles, wounds, dermatological problems, and respiratory affections.

Possible Side Effects of malva sylvestris

Malva sylvestris can have side effects if used improperly, such as allergic reactions, skin irritation, and increased sensitivity to sunlight, which may result in sunburn or photosensitivity.

Prolonged use or excessive consumption of Malva sylvestris can also lead to gastrointestinal issues, including nausea, diarrhea, and stomach cramps.

Additionally, individuals with kidney or liver problems may experience worsening symptoms or interactions with medications when using Malva sylvestris.

It is essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using Malva sylvestris to ensure safe and effective use.
