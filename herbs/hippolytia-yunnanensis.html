Hippolytia yunnanensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Hippolytia yunnanensis?

Hippolytia yunnanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down its taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is organized under the order Asterales and is a member of the family Asteraceae. Finally, it is specifically identified by the genus Hippolytia and the species yunnanensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hippolytia yunnanensis has woody, perennial stems with small, lanceolate leaves that are typically arranged in an opposite or sub-opposite pattern.

The leaf margins are generally entire or slightly serrated, and the texture often feels somewhat leathery to the touch. Its flowers are small and arranged in dense inflorescences, appearing in shades of white or pale cream. The reproductive structures include fine filaments and a central ovary that eventually develops into a small fruit.

These morphological traits allow the plant to adapt effectively to its specific montane habitat.

    Hippolytia yunnanensis is prepared primarily as a decoction made by boiling the dried leaves and stems in water to extract its active medicinal compounds.
