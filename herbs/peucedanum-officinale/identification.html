Botanical Identification of Cow Parsnip (Peucedanum officinale)

To identify Cow Parsnip (Peucedanum officinale) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Cow Parsnip (Peucedanum officinale) Monograph

Scientific Classification

Cow Parsnip, or Peucedanum officinale, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Apiales, family Apiaceae, genus Peucedanum, and species Peucedanum officinale. This classification places it within the carrot family, known for its aromatic and often medicinal plants.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Cow Parsnip is Peucedanum officinale, first described by Carl von Linné in 1753. It is also known by several synonyms, including P. terebinthaceum and P. palustre. Common names include Cow Parsley, Cow Parsnip, and Wild Carrot, while regional names vary, with some areas calling it Queen Anne's Lace. Historically, it was referred to as "Lamb's Parsley" and "Wild Celery."

Morphological Characteristics

Cow Parsnip (Peucedanum officinale) is a biennial herb with a taproot system. It has a smooth, hollow, and ridged stem that is often reddish at the base. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and deeply lobed, with a distinctive white underside. The plant produces an umbrella-like inflorescence of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The fruits are oval, dry, and contain two small seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Cow Parsnip can be distinguished by its large, umbrella-like compound leaves with jagged edges and a hairy stem. It has yellow flowers in clustered umbels and a hollow, ridged stem. Unlike similar species, it lacks the strong aromatic scent of other parsley family members and has a distinct, bitter taste.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Cow Parsnip (Peucedanum officinale) is native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia. It thrives in moderate climates with cool summers and mild winters. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found at elevations between 500 and 2,000 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Cow Parsnip blooms from June to August, with seed formation occurring from July to September. Harvest timing for identification purposes is typically during the flowering stage, from mid-June to early August, when the plant is most visually distinct.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Cow Parsnip (Peucedanum officinale) is closely related to species within the genus Peucedanum, which belongs to the Apiaceae family. It shares family traits such as compound leaves and umbellate flowers with other members of the carrot family. Its phylogenetic position reflects its evolutionary ties to other aromatic herbs used in herbal medicine.
