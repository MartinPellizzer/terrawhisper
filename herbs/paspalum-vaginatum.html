Paspalum vaginatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Paspalum vaginatum?

Paspalum vaginatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Poales, it is a member of the family Poaceae. The taxonomic hierarchy is completed by its placement within the genus Paspalum, identifying it specifically as the species vaginatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Paspalum vaginatum has slender, upright culms that typically reach heights between 20 and 60 centimeters and feature compressed, somewhat keeled stems.

The leaves are narrow and linear with a characteristic scabrous texture on both the upper and lower surfaces. Inflorescences consist of compact, spike-like panicles that are often arranged in clusters or small groups at the top of the stem. The spikelets are small and possess distinct glumes that tightly enclose the reproductive parts.

These morphological traits help distinguish the species from other members of the Paspalum genus found in similar habitats.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Paspalum vaginatum can cause localized skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and physical abrasions if handled without protection. The sharp, serrated edges of the leaf margins are known to inflict small cuts or scratches on the skin during contact. Individuals with sensitive skin may experience redness, itching, or swelling upon exposure to the plant's surfaces. Some people might also develop a hypersensitivity reaction characterized by a mild rash. These physical and dermatological reactions are the primary risks associated with direct human interaction with this species.
