Paspalum vaginatum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Paspalum vaginatum has slender, upright culms that typically reach heights between 20 and 60 centimeters and feature compressed, somewhat keeled stems. The leaves are narrow and linear with a characteristic scabrous texture on both the upper and lower surfaces. Inflorescences consist of compact, spike-like panicles that are often arranged in clusters or small groups at the top of the stem. The spikelets are small and possess distinct glumes that tightly enclose the reproductive parts. These morphological traits help distinguish the species from other members of the Paspalum genus found in similar habitats.
