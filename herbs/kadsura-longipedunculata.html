Kadsura longipedunculata

What's the taxonomical classification of Kadsura longipedunculata?

Kadsura longipedunculata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Austrobaileyales. Within this order, the plant is a member of the family Schisandraceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Kadsura, and its unique species designation, longipedunculata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Kadsura longipedunculata has an erect, woody shrub habit characterized by elongated peduncles that support its small, inconspicuous flowers. Its stems exhibit a somewhat slender structure, often displaying a slightly pubescent texture depending on the specific growth stage. The reproductive organs are held aloft on these extended stalks, which distinguishes the species from closely related taxa. What are the pharmacological activities of Kadsura longipedunculata?

This plant has anti-HBV, anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, antioxidant, and hepatoprotective pharmacological activities. Its diverse chemical profile allows it to combat viral infections specifically targeting the hepatitis B virus. The presence of bioactive compounds also provides significant protection for liver cells against oxidative damage and toxin-induced injury. Furthermore, the plant exhibits potent antimicrobial properties that can inhibit the growth of various pathogenic microorganisms. These combined effects make it a valuable subject for research into natural therapeutic agents.

According to a study published by "Biochimica et biophysica acta. General subjects", the main bioactive constituents of Kadsura longipedunculata comprise highly oxygenated triterpenoids. Schiartane-type nortriterpenoids from this plant show anti-HIV, anti-HBV, and cytotoxic bioactivities. Three new highly oxygenated tritriterpenoids were isolated, specifically micrandilactone I (1), micrandilactone J (2), and 22,23-di-epi-micrandilactone J (3). Micrandilactone I (1) and 22,23-di-epi-micrandilactone J (3) exhibited moderate hepatoprotective activity against APAP-induced toxicity in HepG2 cells. These compounds showed cell survival rates of 53.0% and 50.2% at 10μM, compared to bicyclol at 49.0%. However, micrandilactone J (2) was found to be inactive.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several bioactive sesquiterpene lactones including kadlongilactonones A, C-F, and D, as well as micrandilactonone I and micrandilactone J. These specific chemical constituents are isolated from the plant to study their potential pharmacological properties. The presence of these various kadlongilactonone isomers characterizes the unique chemical profile of Kadsura longipedunculata. Researchers analyze these compounds to understand how they interact with biological systems. Detailed structural studies of micrandilactonone I and J further expand the knowledge of this species' medicinal value.

According to a study published by "Journal of natural products", four new triterpenoids named kadlongilactones C-F (2-5) were isolated from the leaves and stems of Kadsura longipedunculata. These compounds contain a consecutive hexacyclic [7,7,5,6,6,6] ring system. The structures of compounds 2-5 were established through detailed spectroscopic analysis and DFT computational methods. Specifically, DFT computational methods were used for the structural validation of compounds 3 and 5. Compounds 1-4 exhibited significant cytotoxicity against A549, HT-29, and K562 cell lines. These inhibitory effects were measured with IC50 values ranging from 0.49-3.61 microM in vitro.

According to a study published by "Chemistry & biodiversity", Kadsura longipedunculata contains compounds evaluated for their cytotoxicity and anti-inflammatory activity. Compounds 4 and 5 can inhibit nitric oxide production with 50% inhibitory concentration (IC50) values of 36.45 and 28.38 µM. Additionally, compound 4 demonstrates concentration-dependent antiproliferative effects against HeLa cervical cancer cells. This specific compound achieved 68.99% growth inhibition at 40 µM. The IC50 value for compound 4 against these cells was determined to be 21.40 µM.
