Kadsura longipedunculata

What's the taxonomical classification of Kadsura longipedunculata?

Kadsura longipedunculata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Austrobaileyales. Within this order, the plant is a member of the family Schisandraceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Kadsura, and its unique species designation, longipedunculata.

What are the pharmacological activities of Kadsura longipedunculata?

This plant has anti-HBV, anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, antioxidant, and hepatoprotective pharmacological activities. Its chemical constituents work to inhibit the replication of the hepatitis B virus to prevent chronic infection. The plant also functions by neutralizing free radicals and reducing oxidative stress within cellular structures. Additionally, its bioactive compounds combat various pathogens and suppress inflammatory responses in the body. These diverse properties collectively contribute to its significant potential in protecting liver function from damage.

As discussed in a study, the roots and stems of Kadsura longipedunculata are traditionally used to treat blood diseases and pain. The main bioactive constituents of the plant comprise highly oxygenated triterpenoids, including schiaratane-type nortriterpenoids that showed anti-HIV, anti-HBV, and cytotoxic bioactivities. Three new highly oxygenated tritriterpenoids were isolated from the ethanol extract: micrandilactone I (1), micrandilactone J (2), and 22,23-di-epi-micrandilactone J (3). Micrandilactone I (1) and 22,23-di-epi-micrandilactone J (3) demonstrated moderate hepatoprotective activity against APAP-induced toxicity in HepG2 cells. Specifically, these compounds showed cell survival rates of 53.0% and 50.2% respectively at 10μM, compared to bicyclol at 49.0%. Micrandilactone J (2) was found to be inactive in these tests.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds borneol, camphene, cubenol, delta-cadinol, and kadlongilactonones A. These specific chemical constituents contribute to the unique therapeutic profile and aromatic properties of the species. Borneol and camphene are often recognized for their roles in traditional medicine and fragrance applications. The presence of cubenol and delta-cadinol provides additional biochemical complexity to the plant's essential oils. Furthermore, the identification of kadlongilactonones A highlights the specialized secondary metabolites found within its tissues.

As discussed in a study, the essential oil of Kadsura longipedunculata contains 50 compounds representing 97.63% of the total oil. Major components include delta-Cadinene at 21.79%, camphene at 7.27%, borneol at 6.05%, cubenol at 5.12%, and delta-cadinol at 5.11%. The oil demonstrated radical scavenging activity with an IC50 value of 3.06 +/- 0.79 mg/ml and 5-Lipoxygenase inhibition with an IC50 of 38.58 microg/ml. It showed trypanocida l activity in Trypano soma b. brucei with an IC50 value of 50.52 +/- 0.029 microg/ml and prostaglandin E(2) production inhibition of 28.82% at 25 microg/ml. For certain bacteria like Streptococcus pyogenes and S. aga l l actiae, the minimal inhibitory concentration was 60 microg/ml. Additionally, the oil exhibited cytotoxic activity against MIA PaCa-2, HepG-2, and SW-480 cell lines with IC50 values of 133.53, 136.96, and 136.62 microg/ml, respectively.
