Crocus Sativa: Medicinal Uses And Benefits

Crocus sativa, commonly known as saffron, is a flowering plant whose stigmas are harvested and dried to produce the spice saffron.

This herb is renowned for its vibrant color, distinct aroma, and numerous health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and mood-enhancing properties.

Therapeutically, it is used to support cognitive function, alleviate symptoms of depression, and aid in the treatment of certain neurological conditions.

The bioactive constituents of Crocus sativa include crocins, crocetin, safranal, and kaempferol, which contribute to its medicinal value.
