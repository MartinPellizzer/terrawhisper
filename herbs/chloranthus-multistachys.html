Chloranthus multistachys

What's the taxonomical classification of Chloranthus multistachys?

Chloranthus multistachys belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized under the order Chloranthales and the family Chloranthaceae. Finally, its taxonomic identity is completed by its placement within the genus Chloranthus, with the specific designation of the species multistachys.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Chloranthus multistachys targets several key proteins involved in inflammatory and neurological pathways, specifically inhibiting cyclooxygenase-2, inducible nitric oxide synthase, and various pro-inflammatory cytokines. Furthermore, the plant's constituents may influence MAP kinase signaling, helping to regulate cellular responses to oxidative stress. These diverse molecular interactions underpin its traditional medicinal uses for treating pain, inflammation, and certain neurological conditions.
