Chloranthus multistachys

What's the taxonomical classification of Chloranthus multistachys?

Chloranthus multistachys belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized under the order Chloranthales and the family Chloranthaceae. Finally, its taxonomic identity is completed by its placement within the genus Chloranthus, with the specific designation of the species multistachys.

What are the pharmacological activities of Chloranthus multistachys?

This plant has anti-inflammatory, anti-leukemic, anti-tumor, and antibacterial pharmacological activities. Research indicates that its bioactive compounds can effectively inhibit the growth of various cancer cell lines. These substances also demonstrate a significant capacity to reduce inflammation in biological tissues. Furthermore, the plant contains secondary metabolites that provide potent protection against diverse bacterial pathogens. Such multifaceted properties make it a subject of intense interest in pharmaceutical development.

According to a study published by "Medical oncology (Northwood, London, England)", Chloranthus multistachys is a plant that possesses anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and anti-tumor effects. A compound extracted from the plant, named SGP-17-S, was evaluated for its anti-leukemic effects and demonstrated the ability to inhibit the growth of leukemia cells in a time- and dose-dependent manner. Specifically, SGP-17-S blocked HEL cells in the G2 phase and induced apoptosis by decreasing Bcl-2 and caspase-8 protein expression while increasing Bax and caspase-3 expression. Through the use of CETSA, it was revealed that PARP1 is an important target gene for the inhibition of HEL cell growth via SGP-17-S. This study suggests that the compound acts on leukemia cells by targeting PARP1.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the specific medicinal diterpenoids Chloramultiol A, Chloramultiol B, Chloramultiol C, Chloramultiol D, and Chloramultiol E. These unique chemical constituents are primarily isolated from the species Chloranthus multistachys. Researchers study these compounds to understand their potential biological activities and therapeutic properties. The structural diversity of these five molecules provides a foundation for further pharmacological investigation. Such detailed chemical profiling helps identify how the plant may interact with various physiological systems.

According to a study published by "Journal of natural products", six new highly complex lindenane-type sesquiterpenoid dimers known as chloramultiols A-F (1-6) were isolated from the whole plant of Chloranthus multistachys. These six new compounds were identified alongside six known analogues. The structures of compounds 1-6 were elucidated using mass spectrometry (MS) and 1D and 2D NMR spectroscopic analysis. Within this group, compounds 1 and 6 specifically contain a unique 18-membered macrocyclic triester ring. All isolated compounds were evaluated for their ability to inhibit the growth of 5 tumor cell lines.
