Mukia maderaspatana

What's the taxonomical classification of Mukia maderaspatana?

Mukia maderaspatanana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Cucurbitales. This plant is further categorized into the family Cucurbitaceae, which places it among the gourd and melon relatives. Within this family, it is specifically identified by the genus Mukia and is defined by the species name maderae-spatanana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Mukia maderaspatanana has woody, climbing stems that form a dense liana with trifoliate leaves composed of three leaflets that are ovate to elliptical in shape. The leaf margins are typically entire, and the surface can range from glabrous to slightly pubescent depending on the specimen. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in axillary racemes or panicles. The fruit is a characteristic legume, appearing as a woody, somewhat flattened pod that eventually dehisces to release the seeds. These seeds are typically dark and somewhat compressed within the structure of the pod.

As discussed in a study, Mukia maderaspatanas contains phenolic compounds such as quercetin and phloroglucinol. The methanol extract of the dried whole plant was studied at concentrations of 0.25 and 0.5 mg/ml. At 0.25 mg/ml, Mukia inhibited gluconeogenesis by 45% to 0.65 mg/g/h, and with insulin, this inhibition increased to 50% at 0.59 mg/g/h. At 0.5 mg/ml, Mukia inhibited glucose production by 89% with insulin to 0.13 mg/g/h. Mukia also potentiated insulin mediated glucose uptake to 152.82 ± 13.30 mg/dl/g/30 min compared to the insulin control of 112.41 ± 9.14 mg/dl/g/30 min.
