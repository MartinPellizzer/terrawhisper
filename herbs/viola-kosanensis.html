Viola kosanensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viola kosanensis has a perennial herbaceous growth habit characterized by basal leaves that are broadly ovate to cordate with crenate margins and flowers that feature five light purple to violet petals. The leaves are typically long-petioled and possess a smooth texture with distinct veining patterns. Its flowering stems are relatively short, elevating the blossoms slightly above the foliage. The petals often display subtle nectar guides near the throat to attract pollinators. Each plant produces small, capsule-like seed pods that split open upon maturity to disperse its progeny.
