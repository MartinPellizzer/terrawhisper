What is Parinari brasiliensis?

Parinari brasiliensis is a species of plant in the family Chrysobalanaceae.

It is a large tree with a straight bole, measuring up to 60 meters in height, and a broad, rounded crown. The bark is greyish-brown, smooth, and fluted, while the leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, measuring up to 35 centimeters in length. The inflorescence is a panicle, with small, greenish-yellow flowers, and the fruit is a drupe, measuring up to 5 centimeters in length, with a single seed.

The plant is native to the Amazon rainforest in South America, where it grows in well-drained soils along rivers and streams.
