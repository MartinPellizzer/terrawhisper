Salix wilsonii

What's the taxonomical classification of Salix wilsonii?

Salix wilsonii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Malpighiales. The plant is a member of the family Salicaceae and is specifically situated within the genus Salix, ultimately reaching its binomial designation at the species level as wilsonii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Salix wilsonii has narrow, lanceolate leaves that measure approximately 2 to 5 centimeters in length and feature finely serrated margins with a smooth, slightly leathery texture. The foliage is typically a bright green on the upper surface and often displays a paler, more matte appearance on the underside. Its stems are slender and woody, transitioning from a flexible green in young growth to a brownish hue as they mature. The plant produces catkins that appear as cylindrical flowering structures during its reproductive phase. These morphological traits collectively define its structure as a small, deciduous shrub.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of central and western China, specifically within provinces such as Sichuan, Yunnan, and Tibet. It typically thrives in montane environments located at elevations ranging from 2,500 to 4,000 meters above sea level. Within these mountainous habitats, the species is most frequently found growing near the edges of streams, damp meadows, and alpine marshes. The distribution is closely tied to the availability of consistent moisture and cool, temperate climates characteristic of the Qinghai-Tibetan Plateau fringes. Because it relies on these specific microclimates, its occurrence is often fragmented across isolated mountain ranges rather than being continuous.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause gastrointestinal distress, allergic skin reactions, and potential salicylate toxicity if consumed in improper quantities. People with known allergies to salicylates should avoid contact or ingestion to prevent hives or swelling.
