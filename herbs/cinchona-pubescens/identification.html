Botanical Identification of Cinchona Tree (Cinchona pubescens)

To identify Cinchona Tree (Cinchona pubescens) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

The Cinchona Tree, Cinchona pubescens, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Rubiales, family Rubiaceae, genus Cinchona, and species Cinchona pubescens. These classifications outline its taxonomic position in the plant kingdom.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The botanical name of the Cinchona Tree is Cinchona pubescens, a species within the Rubiaceae family. It was first described by the botanist Nikolaus Joseph von Jacquin in 1760. This name is widely accepted and used in scientific literature, though it has several historical and regional synonyms.

Morphological Characteristics

Cinchona pubescens is a deciduous tree with a straight trunk and a rounded crown. It has a fibrous root system that anchors it firmly in the soil. The stem is smooth and grayish-brown, with prominent lenticels. Leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a leathery texture with prominent veins. The inflorescence is a panicle, bearing small, clustered flowers. Flowers are white with yellow stamens and a red calyx. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, elliptical seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Cinchona pubescens can be distinguished by its reddish-brown bark, elliptical leaves with a prominent midrib, and clusters of red-orange flowers. It differs from similar species like C. officinalis through its pubescent stems and smaller, more elongated leaves. The presence of crimson berries and the distinctive scent of its leaves also aid in identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Cinchona pubescens is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in countries like Peru and Ecuador. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and moderate temperatures. The tree prefers well-drained, fertile soils and is typically found at altitudes between 1,000 and 2,000 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Cinchona pubescens blooms from April to June, producing clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. Seed formation occurs from July to September. Harvesting of leaves for identification purposes is typically done during the growing season, from spring to early autumn.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Cinchona pubescens is closely related to other species within the Cinchona genus. It shares key genetic traits with species like Cinchona officinalis and Cinchona ledgeriana. Its phylogenetic position reflects broader relationships within the Rubiaceae family, characterized by similar morphological and biochemical features. These relationships are crucial for understanding its role in traditional and modern herbal medicine.
