Rhamnus cathartica

What's the taxonomical classification of Rhamnus cathartica?

Rhamnus catharticica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Rosales and is a member of the family Rhamnaceae. At the most specific level of its classification, it is identified by the genus Rhamnus and the species cathartica.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Rhamnus cathartica contains anthraquinone glycosides, specifically emodin and aloin, which serve as its primary medicinal components. These chemical compounds act as potent stimulant laxatives by increasing intestinal motility and water secretion. In addition to these glycosides, the plant possesses tannins that provide astringent properties to its various extracts. Some studies also suggest the presence of flavonoids which may offer secondary antioxidant benefits. These bioactive substances collectively drive the plant's traditional use in treating constipation and digestive ailments.

What are the therapeutic actions of Rhamnus cathartica?

Rhamnus cathartica has potent laxative and purgative properties due to the presence of anthraquinone glycosides in its bark and fruit. These compounds stimulate the peristaltic movements of the intestinal muscles to facilitate bowel evacuation. When ingested, the plant acts primarily on the large intestine to induce rapid evacuation of feces. This therapeutic action is frequently utilized in traditional medicine to treat acute constipation and intestinal congestion. However, careful dosage is essential because excessive consumption can lead to severe abdominal cramping and electrolyte imbalances.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat constipation, intestinal worms, and various digestive disorders through its potent purgative properties. The active components in the plant act as a strong laxative to stimulate bowel movements. It has also been historically applied in traditional medicine to expel parasites from the gastrointestinal tract. However, because the substance is highly irritating to the mucous membranes, it must be used with extreme caution. Overuse or improper preparation can lead to severe abdominal cramping and dehydration.
