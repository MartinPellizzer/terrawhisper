What to know about Rhamnus cathartica (buckthorn) before using it medicinally

Buckthorn, scientifically known as Rhamnus cathartica, is a plant with numerous health benefits, possessing antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties that can help to soothe digestive issues and alleviate symptoms of hemorrhoids.

In culinary contexts, buckthorn's tart and slightly bitter flavor makes it a popular ingredient in jams, preserves, and chutneys, particularly in European cuisine. Horticulturally, buckthorn is often used as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and vibrant berries. From a botanical perspective, buckthorn is a deciduous shrub or small tree with dark green leaves and clusters of yellowish-white flowers.

Historical records show that buckthorn has been used for centuries in traditional medicine, particularly in ancient Greece and Rome, where it was valued for its purgative properties.

What are the side effects of Rhamnus cathartica when used improperly?

When used improperly, Rhamnus cathartica increases the chances of experiencing side effects, such as gastrointestinal disturbances, allergic reactions, and even severe health complications.

If not prepared correctly or taken in excessive doses, the plant's anthraquinone glycosides can cause stomach cramps, diarrhea, nausea, and vomiting. Additionally, some individuals may be allergic to the plant's sap or resin, leading to contact dermatitis, hives, or anaphylaxis.

Furthermore, improper use of Rhamnus cathartica can lead to kidney damage or failure due to its toxic compounds' ability to concentrate in the kidneys. In severe cases, using the plant as a laxative can cause electrolyte imbalances, dehydration, and even heart problems. Moreover, pregnant or breastfeeding women may experience adverse effects on their fetuses or babies, potentially leading to miscarriage, premature birth, or developmental delays.

Therefore, it is crucial to consult with a healthcare professional before using Rhamnus cathartica for medicinal purposes and follow proper preparation and dosage guidelines to minimize the risk of experiencing these unpleasant side effects.
