Rhamnus Cathartica Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Rhamnus cathartica, commonly known as buckthorn, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia, known for its medicinal properties.

This herb is used for its laxative effects and has been traditionally employed to treat constipation and digestive disorders.

The bioactive constituents responsible for its therapeutic actions include anthraquinones, such as emodin and chrysophanol, which have purgative and anti-inflammatory properties.

Herbal preparations of Rhamnus cathartica can be made as infusions, decoctions, or tinctures, though it is important to use it with caution due to its potent effects on the gastrointestinal system.
