Rhamnus Cathartica Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Rhamnus cathartica, commonly known as buckthorn, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia, known for its medicinal properties.

This herb is valued for its laxative effects and has been traditionally used to treat constipation and digestive disorders.

The bioactive constituents responsible for its therapeutic actions include anthraquinones, which have mild purgative properties, and tannins that contribute to its astringent effects.

Herbal preparations such as infusions, decoctions, and tinctures can be made from the leaves and bark of Rhamnus cathartica.
