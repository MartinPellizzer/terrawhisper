What's the taxonomical classification of Callerya cinerea?

Callerya cinerea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Fabales, placing it within the family Fabaceae. Ultimately, this plant is identified by the genus Callerya and the specific epithet cinerea, forming its complete scientific name.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Callerya cinerea has large, leathery, elliptical leaves that are dark green with a distinctively ash-colored or glaucous underside.

The plant produces massive, pendulous inflorescences that can reach lengths of up to one meter. Each flower features thick, waxy petals and sepals that are typically cream or pale yellow in color. The labellum is notably large and decorated with intricate dark red or brownish markings.

These robust orchid structures are designed to support heavy blooms that hang gracefully from the stem.
