What's the taxonomical classification of Bulbophyllum pectinatum?

Bulbophyllum pectinatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Asparagales. This plant is a member of the family Orchidaceae, falling under the specific genus Bulbophyllum, and is identified by its unique species name, pectinatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bulbophyllum pectinatum has creeping rhizomes that produce small, ovate to elliptical pseudobulbs topped with a single, leathery, lanceolate leaf. These specialized floral structures are designed to attract specific pollinators through their unique shape and texture. How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires a warm and humid environment with bright, filtered light and constant air movement to thrive.

Growers should use a well-draining medium such as coarse bark or mounted on cork to mimic its natural epiphytic habitat. Because it is a tropical species, maintaining temperatures between 65 and 85 degrees Fahrenheit is essential for healthy growth.
