Bulbophyllum pectinatum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bulbophyllum pectinatum has creeping rhizomes that produce small, ovate to elliptical pseudobulbs topped with a single, leathery, lanceolate leaf. The inflorescence is a long, pendulous raceme that carries numerous small, yellowish-green flowers. Each flower features a highly modified, comb-like labellum that is characteristic of the species. These specialized floral structures are designed to attract specific pollinators through their unique shape and texture. The overall growth habit is compact, allowing the plant to cling effectively to mossy tree branches in its natural habitat.
