Triclisia dictyophylla

What's the taxonomical classification of Triclisia dictyophylla?

Triclisia dictyophylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, positioning it within the order Ranunculales. At the family level, this plant is a member of Menispermaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identification is defined by its placement in the genus Triclisia, with the specific epithet dictyophylla completing its scientific name.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tricliisia dictyophyllas has compound leaves characterized by a reticulate or net-like vein pattern, which is reflected in its specific epithet.

The foliage typically consists of multiple leaflets arranged along a central rachis. These leaves are often small to medium in size and exhibit a distinct architectural structure due to the intricate venation. The stems and branching patterns support the weight of this dense, textured canopy.

This morphological complexity helps distinguish the species from other members of its genus.
