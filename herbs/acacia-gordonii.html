What is Acacia gordonii?

Acacia gordonii is a shrub or small tree species belonging to the family Fabaceae.

It is a member of the Acacia genus, characterized by its bipinnate or pinnate leaves, which are composed of 2-5 pairs of pinnae. The leaves are 5-15 cm in length, with 1-3 mm long leaflets. Acacia gordonii produces yellow or cream-colored flowers in axillary spikes, followed by flat, 2-4 cm long pods containing 2-4 seeds.

The plant is native to the deserts and grasslands of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.
