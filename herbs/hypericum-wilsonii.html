What's the taxonomical classification of Hypericum wilsonii?

Hypericum wilsonii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Malpighiales. This plant is a representative of the family Hypericaceae, which is further categorized into the genus Hypericum, ultimately leading to its specific designation as the species wilsonii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hypericum wilsonii has woody, perennial stems that support opposite, elliptical to lanceolate leaves with entire margins and revolute edges. During its flowering stage, the plant produces small, bright yellow flowers with five distinct petals and numerous prominent stamens. These blossoms emerge from terminal clusters or axillary cymes located at the ends of the branches. What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating hepatic ischemia-reperfusion injury. This specific condition occurs when blood supply returns to the liver after a period of restricted blood flow. Such an event can trigger significant oxidative stress and cellular damage within the liver tissue. Research into Hypericum wilsonii suggests its potential to mitigate the inflammatory responses associated with this injury. Protecting the liver from these metabolic disruptions is a primary focus of its medicinal application.
