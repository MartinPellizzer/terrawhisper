Rhododendron mucronatum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhododendron mucronaatum has small, leathery, evergreen leaves that are elliptical in shape and terminate in a distinct, mucronate point. These leaves are typically arranged in whorls or clusters at the ends of the branches. The plant features woody stems that develop a rough texture as they mature. Its flowers are characterized by a tubular structure and are often found in small, terminal clusters. The overall structure of the plant is that of a compact, spreading shrub.

        What side effects this plant can have?

        This plant can cause severe toxic reactions including nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, dizziness, confusion, arrhythmia, hypotension, and seizures. Ingesting its tissues may lead to significant gastrointestinal distress such as intense stomach cramping and frequent emesis. The toxins can also disrupt the central nervous system, resulting in mental disorientation and loss of consciousness. Cardiovascular complications like irregular heartbeats and a dangerous drop in blood pressure may occur following ingestion. In extreme cases, the physiological impact can progress to life-threatening neurological episodes like seizures.
