Chinese Leek (Allium chinense) Uses

Chinese Leek is used to treat respiratory infections, cough, sinusitis, common cold, tonsillitis, bronchitis, and asthma. It also helps with skin and fungal infections, as well as promotes wound healing due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

Chinese Leek (Allium chinense) Monograph

1. Respiratory infections

Chinese leek is used in traditional medicine to treat respiratory infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps alleviate symptoms like coughing and congestion by supporting immune function and clearing mucus. Often prepared as a decoction or steam inhalation, it is valued for its ability to reduce inflammation in the respiratory tract.

2. Cough

Chinese Leek is used to alleviate coughs by reducing inflammation in the respiratory tract and loosening phlegm. It is often prepared as a decoction or soup to soothe irritated airways and ease breathing. Its expectorant properties make it a common remedy in traditional Chinese medicine for bronchitis and persistent coughs.

3. Sinusitis

Chinese Leek (Allium chinense) is used in traditional herbal medicine to alleviate sinusitis symptoms due to its anti-inflammatory and decongestant properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion and inflammation, promoting clearer breathing. Often prepared as a decoction or steam inhalation, it supports respiratory health in sinus infections.

4. Common cold

Chinese leek is used in traditional Chinese medicine to alleviate symptoms of the common cold due to its expectorant and antipyretic properties. It helps reduce fever, clear phlegm, and relieve congestion, making it a common ingredient in herbal remedies for colds. Its warming nature supports the body's recovery by enhancing circulation and respiratory function.

5. Tonsillitis

Chinese leek is used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat tonsillitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce throat swelling and combat bacterial infections. Typically prepared as a decoction, it is consumed to alleviate symptoms and promote healing. Its cooling nature also helps soothe irritations associated with inflammation.

6. Bronchitis

Allium chinense is used in traditional Chinese medicine to alleviate symptoms of bronchitis due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps clear mucus from the respiratory tract and reduces inflammation in the bronchial tubes, supporting respiratory health. It is often prepared as a decoction or incorporated into herbal formulas for chronic or acute bronchial conditions.

7. Asthma

Chinese Leek (Allium chinense) is used in traditional Chinese medicine to alleviate asthma symptoms by promoting respiratory clarity and reducing inflammation. It is often prepared as a decoction and combined with other herbs to enhance its expectorant and bronchodilator effects. Its properties are believed to help ease breathing and support lung function in asthmatic conditions.

8. Skin infections

Chinese leek is used topically to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce inflammation and promotes healing. The bulb is often crushed and applied directly to the affected area. Its sulfur compounds contribute to its therapeutic effects on infected skin.

9. Fungal infections

Chinese Leek (Allium chinense) has antimicrobial properties that make it effective in treating fungal infections. Its active compounds inhibit the growth of fungi, supporting its use in traditional herbal medicine for conditions like ringworm and athlete's foot. It is often applied topically or consumed internally as part of herbal formulations.

10. Wound healing

Chinese Leek, or Allium chinense, is used in traditional medicine to promote wound healing due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce infection risk and accelerates tissue repair. The bulb is often applied topically or used in herbal formulations to support the body's natural healing processes.
