Chinese Leek (Allium chinense) Compounds

The primary compounds in Chinese leek include flavonoids, which act as antioxidants, and phenolic acids, known for their anti-inflammatory properties. Saponins and tannins contribute to its bitter taste and potential health benefits. Essential oils and terpenoids provide aromatic qualities, while glycosides and coumarin may support various therapeutic effects. Alkaloids and anthocyanins add to its complex chemical profile and medicinal value.

Chinese Leek (Allium chinense) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Allium chinense, a traditional Chinese medicinal plant. They contribute to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting its use in treating respiratory and cardiovascular conditions. These compounds help modulate immune responses and protect cells from oxidative damage.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Chinese Leek (Allium chinense), contributing to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. This compound is commonly found in the leaves and bulbs of the plant, making it a valuable component in traditional herbal medicine.
