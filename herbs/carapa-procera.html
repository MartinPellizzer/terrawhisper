Carapa procera

What's the taxonomical classification of Carapa procera?

Carapa procera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, the plant is organized under the order Sapindales and falls within the family Meliaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Carapa, and its species designation, procera.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Carapa procera contains high concentrations of limonoids, specifically gedunin, which serve as the plant's primary bioactive constituents. These specialized triterpenoids are recognized for their potent anti-inflammatory and anti-plasmodial properties. Research indicates that these compounds can effectively inhibit the growth of certain cancer cell lines in laboratory settings. Additionally, the plant contains various flavonoids and polyphenols that contribute to its antioxidant capabilities. These chemical elements work together to provide significant therapeutic potential for treating infectious diseases and inflammatory conditions.

What are the therapeutic actions of Carapa procera?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat gastrointestinal infections, skin inflammation, wounds, and various bacterial or fungal ailments. The bark and leaves contain bioactive compounds that exhibit significant antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Researchers often investigate its potential for managing intestinal parasites and treating stomach ulcers. Some traditional applications also include using extracts to soothe skin irritation and accelerate the healing of topical lesions. Its complex chemical profile makes it a subject of interest for developing new treatments for infectious diseases.
