Craibiodendron henryi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Ericales and is a member of the family Ericaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Craibiodendron, and its unique species designation, henryi.

    As discussed in a study, twelve compounds were isolated from the ethyl acetate soluble fraction of the 95% ethanolic extract of Craibiodendron henryi roots. These identified medicinal compounds include quercetin (1), quercetin-3-O-rhamnicoside (2), quercetin-3-O-arabinofuranoside (3), (-)-epicatechin (4), proanthocyanidin A-2 (5), and procyaniidin B-2 (6). The isolation process also yielded (-)-isolariciresinol-2a-O-beta-D-xylopyranoside (7), lyoniside (8), sito-ster-yl-3beta-glucoside-6'-O-palmitate (9), beta-sitosterol (10), daucosterol (11), and octacosanoic acid (12). Compounds 1 through 12 were isolated from this plant for the first time.
