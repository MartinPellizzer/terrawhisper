What's the taxonomical classification of Craibiodendron henryi?

Craibiodendron henryi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Ericales and is a member of the family Ericaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Craibiodendron, and its unique species designation, henryi.

What are the pharmacological activities of Craibiodendron henryi?

This plant has antioxidant and vasodilator pharmacological activities that contribute to its therapeutic potential. Its antioxidant properties help neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress within biological systems. Additionally, the plant demonstrates vasodilator effects by relaxing smooth muscle tissues to increase blood flow. These combined actions suggest that the species may play a role in managing cardiovascular health and cellular protection. Further research into these specific mechanisms could reveal more about its medicinal applications.

According to a study published by "Journal of natural products", Craibiodendron henryi roots yielded four new iridoid glycosides identified as 10-O-trans-p-coumaroylscandoside (1), 10-O-cis-p-coumaroylscandoside (2), 10-O-trans-p-coumaroyldesacetyl asperulosidic acid (3), and 10-O-cis-p-coumaroyldesacetyl asperulosidic acid (4). Additionally, two new grayanane diterpenoids, 14beta-O-(2S,3S-nilyl)-2alpha,3beta,5beta,6beta,16alpha-pentahydroxygrayanane (5) and 14beta-O-(2S,3S-nilyl)-2alpha,3beta,5beta,6beta,16alpha-pentahydroxygrayan-10(20)-ene (6), were isolated from the plant. The antioxidant activities of these specific compounds were assessed. The study also evaluated the vasodilator effects of these isolated substances.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds (-)-epicatechin, beta-sitosterol, daucosterol, quercetin, and proanthocyanidin A-2. These specific phytochemicals contribute to the diverse biological activities observed in Craibiodendron henryi extracts. The presence of flavonoids like quercetin and (-)-epicatechin suggests significant antioxidant potential within the plant material. Additionally, the phytosterols beta-sitosterol and daucosterol are known for their role in various physiological processes. These various chemical constituents work together to define the medicinal profile of the species.

According to a study published by "Zhongguo Zhong yao za zhi = Zhongguo zhongyao zazhi = China journal of Chinese materia medica", twelve compounds were isolated from the ethyl acetate soluble fraction of the 95% ethanolic extract of the roots of Craibiodendron henryi. These compounds, which were isolated from this plant for the first time, include quercetin (1), quercetin-3-O-rhamnicoside (2), quercetin-3-O-arabinofuranoside (3), (-)-epicatechin (4), proanthocyanidin A-2 (5), and procyanidin B-2 (6). The research also identified (-)-isolaresinol-2a-O-beta-D-xylopyranoside (7), lyoniside (8), sitoster-yl-3beta-glucoside-6'-O-palmitate (9), beta-sitosterol (10), daucosterol (11), and octacosa noiic acid (12). All 12 compounds had their structures elucidated through chemical evidence and spectral methods.
