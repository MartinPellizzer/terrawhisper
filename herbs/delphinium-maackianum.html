Delphinium maackianum

What's the taxonomical classification of Delphinium maackianum?

Delphinium maackianum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further organized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Ranunculales. As a member of the family Ranunculaceae, this plant is placed within the genus Delphinium, with its specific identity defined by the species designation maackianum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Delphinium maackianum has erect, herbaceous stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters and feature deeply palmately lobed leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is typically dark green and clustered towards the base or middle of the stem. Its inflorescence consists of dense, terminal racemes bearing numerous zygomorphic flowers characterized by a prominent nectar-producing spur. These blossoms display a distinct color palette ranging from deep blue to violet with lighter accents on the petals. The reproductive structures include a central cluster of numerous stamens and carpels typical of the Ranunculaceae family.
