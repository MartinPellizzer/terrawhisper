Delphinium maackianum

What's the taxonomical classification of Delphinium maackianum?

Delphinium maackianum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further organized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Ranunculales. As a member of the family Ranunculaceae, this plant is placed within the genus Delphinium, with its specific identity defined by the species designation maackianum.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Delphinium maackianum can cause severe poisoning characterized by gastrointestinal distress, neurological impairment, and potential cardiac arrest if ingested. The plant contains potent alkaloids that disrupt the nervous system and can lead to symptoms like vomiting, abdominal pain, and muscle weakness. In more serious cases, individuals may experience respiratory failure or irregular heart rhythms due to the toxicity of its chemical compounds. Because the concentration of these toxins can vary, even small amounts may pose a significant risk to humans and animals. Prompt medical intervention is necessary if any part of the plant is consumed.
