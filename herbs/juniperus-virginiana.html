Common Juniper (Juniperus virginiana)

Common Juniper (Juniperus virginiana) is a member of the Cupressaceae family, native to Eastern United States, Southeastern United States, and Southwestern United States. Traditionally, its fruits, bark, and resin have been used for decoctions, infusions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its diuretic, astringent, and antispasmodic actions, and has a long history of use in native american herbal medicine, european herbal medicine, and traditional chinese medicine.

Perennial woody shrub.

Leaves
Bifacial leaves with upper surface dark green and lower surface pale green, featuring distinct stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Small, clustered flowers with blue-gray or white petals, actinomorphic, with four to five sepals and four to five stamens, bearing a single pistil with two ovules.
Stems
Erect growth habit with a branched, woody structure, bearing opposite or alternate phyllotaxis, and characterized by a rough, fissured surface with persistent, scale-like bark.
