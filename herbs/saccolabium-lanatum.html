What is Saccolabium lanatum?

Saccolabium lanatum is an epiphytic orchid species belonging to the family Orchidaceae.

It has a terete, green, and hairy stem, measuring up to 60 cm in length, with a few, subulate bracts. The leaves are elliptical in shape, measuring 10-15 cm in length and 3-4 cm in width, with a leathery texture and a cuneate base. The inflorescence is a raceme, measuring up to 20 cm in length, bearing 10-15 flowers with a greenish-white, campanulate shape, 2-3 cm in length and 1.5-2 cm in width, and a yellowish, labellum-like structure with a triangular shape, measuring 1-1.5 cm in length.

The flowers are pollinated by wasps and bees, which are attracted to the yellowish color of the labellum.
