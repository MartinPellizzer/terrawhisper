Sparganium subglobosum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sparganium subglobosum has a subglobose to irregular spore sac that typically measures between 1 and 3 millimeters in diameter and is supported by a short, slender stipe. The peridium is thin and fragile, often splitting irregularly to reveal the internal spore mass. This spore mass is characterized by a yellowish to brownish color and contains globose spores that are typically smooth and hyaline. The capillitium is present within the sac, consisting of branched filaments that help maintain the structure of the spore mass. These morphological features collectively distinguish this species from other members of the genus.
