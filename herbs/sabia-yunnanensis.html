Sabia yunnanensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sabia yunnanensis has woody, perennial stems that support opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with entire or slightly serrated margins. The foliage is typically bright green and displays prominent venation across the leaf surface. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils in clusters. These blossoms are characterized by a tubular structure and are often pale in color. The plant develops small, fleshy fruits that follow the flowering period.

While its primary concentration is in Yunnan, its range is defined by the specific climatic conditions found in these southeastern Asian highlands.
