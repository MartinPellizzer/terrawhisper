White Mustard (Brassica alba) Compounds

White Mustard contains several key compounds, including flavonoids, phenolic acids, and tannins, which contribute to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarin and glycosides are also present, offering potential benefits in traditional medicine. Saponins and terpenoids support its detoxifying effects, while essential oils and anthocyanins enhance its aromatic and protective qualities. Alkaloids contribute to its medicinal and therapeutic uses.

White Mustard (Brassica alba) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in White Mustard (Brassica alba) are plant compounds with antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. They contribute to the plant's medicinal value by supporting immune function and reducing oxidative stress. These compounds are commonly found in the seeds and leaves of the plant.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid in White Mustard (Brassica alba) contributes to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. It helps reduce oxidative stress and may support immune function. This compound is part of the plant's broader therapeutic profile in herbal medicine.

3. Tannin

Tannin in White Mustard (Brassica alba) contributes to its astringent properties. It helps in reducing inflammation and can aid in treating digestive issues. Tannins also support wound healing by promoting tissue repair. This compound is commonly used in herbal remedies for its soothing and protective effects.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in White Mustard (Brassica alba). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. Coumarin may also support digestive health and has been studied for its mild sedative effects.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in White Mustard (Brassica alba). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly in expectorant and anti-inflammatory effects. Glycosides in White Mustard support respiratory health and are traditionally used in herbal medicine for treating coughs and bronchial conditions.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in White Mustard (Brassica alba). It exhibits detoxifying properties and supports liver function. Saponins also contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. These actions make saponin a valuable component in herbal medicine.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in White Mustard (Brassica alba) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. They are found in the seeds and leaves. Terpenoids support the plant's use in traditional herbal medicine.

8. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound in White Mustard (Brassica alba). It contains volatile compounds like glucosinolates and allyl isothiocyanate, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds are responsible for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Essential oil is commonly used in herbal medicine to support respiratory and digestive health.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a pigment found in White Mustard (Brassica alba) that contributes to its medicinal properties. It acts as an antioxidant, helping to neutralize free radicals in the body. This compound may support cardiovascular health and reduce inflammation, making it a valuable component in herbal medicine.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a key compound found in White Mustard (Brassica alba). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by influencing various physiological processes. Alkaloids in White Mustard are used in traditional herbal medicine for their potential therapeutic effects.
