White Mustard (Brassica alba) Uses

White Mustard is used to treat respiratory conditions such as cough, bronchitis, and asthma. It helps reduce inflammation in the upper respiratory tract and mucous membranes, making it effective for cold symptoms, allergic rhinitis, and influenza. Its properties soothe mucosal irritation and support the body's response to infections.

White Mustard (Brassica alba) Monograph

1. Cough

White Mustard (Brassica alba) is used to alleviate coughs due to its expectorant properties. It helps loosen mucus and reduce irritation in the respiratory tract. The seeds are often prepared as a poultice or infused oil to target chest congestion. It is particularly effective for dry or productive coughs associated with respiratory infections.

2. Respiratory infections

White Mustard (Brassica alba) is used to support respiratory health by reducing inflammation and clearing mucus. Its essential oil, containing compounds like thymol and carvacrol, helps alleviate symptoms of colds, bronchitis, and other respiratory infections. It is often used in steam inhalations or as a topical application to ease breathing and relieve congestion.

3. Mucous membrane irritation

White Mustard (Brassica alba) can cause mucous membrane irritation due to its volatile oils, particularly erucic acid. This property is utilized in herbal medicine to stimulate secretions and clear congestion. However, it should be used cautiously to avoid excessive irritation.

4. Upper respiratory tract inflammation

White Mustard (Brassica alba) is used to alleviate upper respiratory tract inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. It helps reduce swelling and mucus buildup in the throat and bronchial passages. Traditionally, it has been applied as a poultice or in steam inhalations to ease symptoms of coughs and congestion.

5. Bronchitis

White Mustard (Brassica alba) has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in treating bronchitis. Its expectorant properties help loosen mucus and reduce congestion in the airways. The seeds and leaves are often prepared as teas or poultices to alleviate symptoms and promote clearer breathing.

6. Allergic rhinitis

White Mustard (Brassica alba) is used in herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis due to its antihistamine and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion and inflammation, supporting respiratory relief. Traditionally, it is prepared as a tincture or poultice for local application. Its efficacy is supported by historical use and some modern studies on its bioactive compounds.

7. Cold symptoms

White Mustard (Brassica alba) is used to alleviate cold symptoms due to its expectorant and mild antispasmodic properties. It helps loosen mucus and reduce congestion, making it useful for coughs and sore throats. Often prepared as a poultice or tea, it supports respiratory comfort during colds.

8. Mucosal inflammation

White Mustard (Brassica alba) is used to support mucosal inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. It helps soothe irritated tissues in the respiratory and digestive tracts. The plant's active compounds, including essential oils and glucosinolates, contribute to its effectiveness in reducing mucosal irritation and promoting healing.

9. Asthma

White Mustard (Brassica alba) has been traditionally used to support respiratory health. Its seeds contain compounds that may help reduce bronchial inflammation and ease breathing in asthma patients. However, it should be used under professional guidance due to potential interactions and side effects.

10. Influenza

White Mustard (Brassica alba) has been traditionally used to support respiratory health during influenza. Its expectorant properties help loosen mucus, ease coughing, and reduce congestion. It may also have antiviral effects that support the body's response to viral infections.
