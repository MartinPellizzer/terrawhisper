White Mustard (Brassica alba) Actions

White Mustard exhibits several key therapeutic actions, including anti-inflammatory, diuretic, and carminative effects. It also acts as a bitter tonic and expectorant, helping to clear mucus. Its astringent and stimulant properties support digestion, while antispasmodic and sedative effects aid in calming the body and relieving spasms.

White Mustard (Brassica alba) Monograph

1. Anti-inflammatory

White mustard has an anti-inflammatory action, primarily due to its active compound, sinigrin, which inhibits inflammatory pathways. It helps reduce swelling and pain associated with conditions like arthritis and skin inflammation. This effect is supported by traditional use and preliminary scientific studies.

2. Diuretic

White mustard has a diuretic action, promoting the elimination of excess fluid from the body. This effect is due to its compounds that support kidney function and increase urine production. It is commonly used to alleviate water retention and support urinary health.

3. Carminative

White mustard has a carminative action, helping to relieve gas and bloating by stimulating the digestive system. It promotes the movement of gas through the intestines, reducing discomfort. This effect is due to its volatile oils and compounds that support healthy digestion.

4. Bitter

White mustard has a bitter action, which stimulates digestion and increases metabolic activity. This action helps to clear mucus and reduce congestion, making it beneficial for respiratory and digestive health. It also supports liver function and can aid in detoxification processes.

5. Tonic

White mustard has a tonic action, stimulating the body's natural processes to enhance vitality and strength. It supports digestion, improves nutrient absorption, and helps restore energy levels in cases of fatigue or weakness. Its warming properties make it particularly effective in strengthening the digestive and respiratory systems.

6. Expectorant

White mustard has an expectorant action, helping to loosen and expel mucus from the respiratory tract. It stimulates the secretion of bronchial mucus, making it easier to cough up. This makes it useful for conditions like bronchitis or congestion. It is typically used in steam inhalations or as a poultice.

7. Astringent

White mustard has an astringent action, which helps reduce secretions and tighten tissues. It is particularly effective for conditions involving excessive mucus or inflammation. This property supports its use in respiratory and digestive ailments. It works by constricting blood vessels and reducing fluid loss.

8. Stimulant

White mustard has a stimulant action, enhancing metabolic processes and increasing energy levels. It works by stimulating the nervous system and promoting circulation. This effect can help with fatigue and improve overall vitality when used appropriately.

9. Antispasmodic

White mustard has an antispasmodic action, helping to relieve muscle spasms and cramps by relaxing smooth muscle tissue. It is traditionally used for digestive issues like colic and intestinal cramping. Its active compounds, such as essential oils and alkaloids, contribute to its ability to ease involuntary muscle contractions.

10. Sedative

White mustard has a sedative action, promoting relaxation and reducing anxiety. It works by calming the nervous system and easing tension. This effect makes it useful in managing stress and insomnia when used appropriately. Its mild nature allows for gentle support without causing drowsiness.
