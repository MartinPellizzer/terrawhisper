Selaginella rolandi-principis

What's the taxonomical classification of Selaginella rolandi-principis?

Selaginella rolandi-principis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Lycopodiidae. This plant is further categorized under the order Selaginellales and the family Selaginellaceae. Finally, it is placed within the genus Selaginella, with its specific designation being the species rolandi-principis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Selaginella rolandi-principis has a creeping rhizomatous habit with small, scale-like microphylls that are arranged in four rows along the flattened, branched stems.

The leaves are densely overlapping and possess a distinctively triangular or lanceolate shape. Each leaf is characterized by a central midrib that extends toward the narrow tip. These diminutive leaves are often dark green or brownish in color, reflecting the plant's specialized surface structure.

The overall architecture of the plant creates a compact, moss-like appearance suitable for shaded environments.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Selaginella rolandi-principis can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and potential respiratory distress if its spores are inhaled. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, members of the Selaginella genus often contain secondary metabolites that may react with sensitive skin. Individuals with pre-existing allergies should avoid direct contact to prevent localized redness or itching. Exposure to dust or fine particles from the plant could also trigger coughing or sneezing in sensitive individuals. Always exercise caution when handling this species in an indoor environment.
