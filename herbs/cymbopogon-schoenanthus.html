From a botanical perspective, lemon grass belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its long, slender leaves and fragrant essential oils.

With a history dating back thousands of years to ancient Indian and Chinese cultures, lemon grass has been revered for its medicinal and culinary properties across generations.
