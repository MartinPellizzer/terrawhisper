Cymbopogon Grass

Cymbopogon schoenanthus

Cymbopogon Grass (Cymbopogon schoenanthus) is a type of grass native to tropical regions, known for its aromatic properties and traditional use in treating digestive issues and respiratory conditions.

Cymbopogon schoenanthus is a species of grass belonging to the genus Cymbopogon in the family Poaceae, classified under the order Poales, class Liliopsida, division Magnoliophyta, and kingdom Plantae.

Morphology

Cymbopogon schoenanthus is a perennial grass characterized by its erect, slender stems that grow up to 1.5 meters in height, narrow linear leaves with a rough, hairy texture, and dense clusters of small, pale blue to whitish flowers at the top of the stems.

Distribution

Cymbopogon schoenanthus is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly found in countries such as Kenya, Tanzania, and Uganda, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and forest edges.

Cymbopogon schoenanthus has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and digestive properties, often prepared as a tea or applied topically to treat infections, skin conditions, and gastrointestinal issues.
