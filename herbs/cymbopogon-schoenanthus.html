Cymbopogon Grass

Cymbopogon schoenanthus

Cymbopogon Grass (Cymbopogon schoenanthus) is a type of grass native to tropical regions, known for its aromatic properties and traditional use in treating digestive issues and respiratory conditions.

Botanical Identification

Cymbopogon schoenanthus, commonly known as narrowleaf lemon grass, is a perennial grass native to Australia. It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its narrow leaves and aromatic, lemon-scented oils. Synonyms in herbal literature include Cymbopogon citratus and Andropogon citratus.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Liliopsida
  • Order: Poales
  • Family: Poaceae
  • Genus: Cymbopogon
  • Species: Cymbopogon schoenanthus

Active Compounds

Cymbopogon schoenanthus contains essential oil, flavonoids, and phenolic acids as its main bioactive compounds. These compounds work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. The essential oil, in particular, contributes to its medicinal properties through its volatile components.

  • Essential oil
  • Flavonoid
  • Phenolic acid

Cymbopogon Grass Uses.

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory ailments.

Infusion: Used for colds, flu, and as a general tonic.

Essential oil: Used for skin conditions and as a natural insect repellent.

Safety Profile

Cymbopogon schoenanthus is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause allergic reactions in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to limited safety data. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
