Anagallis Arvensis: Medicinal Uses And Benefits

Anagallis arvensis, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a small flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found growing in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

This herb is known for its mild diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties, making it beneficial for supporting urinary health and reducing inflammation in the body.

Therapeutically, it is used to alleviate symptoms of respiratory conditions and promote skin health.

The bioactive constituents of Anagallis arvensis include flavonoids, tannins, and essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal effects.
