What are the therapeutic actions of Anagallis arvensis?

Anagallis arvensis has therapeutic properties that function as a bitter, diuretic, aperient, and cholagogue to support digestive and urinary health. Its bitter components stimulate the appetite and prepare the gastrointestinal tract for digestion by increasing gastric secretions. As a cholagogue, the plant promotes the flow and discharge of bile from the gallbladder into the small intestine. The diuretic action helps to increase urine production, which can assist in flushing the urinary system. Additionally, its aperient qualities act as a mild laxative to facilitate regular bowel movements.
