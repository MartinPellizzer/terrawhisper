Anagallis arvensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Anagallis arvensis?

Anagallis arvensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Ericales and the family Primulaceae. The plant is specifically identified by its genus, Anagallis, and its species, arvensis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Anagallis arvensis contains various secondary metabolites including flavonoids, phenolic acids, and potentially terpenoids that contribute to its biological activity. These chemical constituents are primarily concentrated within the plant's leaves and stems. Research suggests that these compounds may possess antioxidant and antimicrobial properties useful in traditional applications. While specific isolated molecules are still being studied, the presence of these bioactive groups indicates a potential for medicinal utility. Detailed pharmacological profiling of this specific species remains an area of ongoing botanical investigation.

This plant is used to treat various ailments including respiratory issues, digestive disturbances, and inflammatory skin conditions. Historical herbal practices suggest its versatility in addressing multiple internal and external health concerns.
