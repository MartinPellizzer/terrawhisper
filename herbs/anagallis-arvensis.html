Side Effects of anagallis arvensis

This herb may also provoke allergic reactions in susceptible individuals, leading to symptoms such as sneezing, runny nose, and even asthma-like symptoms. Skin irritation and rashes are common side effects, with contact dermatitis occurring due to the presence of irritant alkaloids and other phytochemicals.

Ingestion or prolonged exposure can result in throat and mouth irritation, causing a burning sensation and difficulty swallowing. Additionally, Anagallis arvensis may lead to headaches, nausea, dizziness, and fatigue, likely due to its impact on the central nervous system and gastrointestinal tract.

These adverse effects highlight the importance of proper handling and caution when using this herb, especially for those with pre-existing sensitivities or medical conditions.
