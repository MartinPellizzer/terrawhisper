Antrophyum obovatum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Antrophyum obovatum has obovate to elliptical fronds that measure approximately 10 to 25 centimeters in length and feature entire margins with a leathery texture. The leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern and possess a distinctively thickened, dark brown or blackish stipe. The underside of the foliage is characterized by large, continuous, and somewhat irregular sori that lack an indusium. These reproductive structures follow the veins of the leaf, creating a net-like appearance across the fertile surface. The venation is free and dichotomously branched, contributing to the overall structural complexity of the blade.
