What's the taxonomical classification of Phlogacanthus curviflorus?

Phlogacanthus curviflorus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized into the order Lamiales. It is a member of the family Acanthaceae, specifically falling under the genus Phlogacanthus, and is identified by its unique species designation, curviflorus.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several unique medicinal compounds including phlogacantosides A, phlogacantosides B, phlogacantosides C, phlogaecantholides B, and phlogaecantholides C. According to a study published by "Journal of natural products", two diterpenes and three diterpene glucosides were identified within the plant. The roots of Phlogacanthus curviflorus yielded two new diterpene lactones, phlogacantholides B (1) and C (2), along with three new diterpene lactone glucosides, phlogacanthosides A (3), B (4), and C (5). The structure and relative configuration of phlogacantholide B (1) were confirmed using X-ray crystallographic analysis of its diacetate (6).
