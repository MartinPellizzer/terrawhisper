Phlogacanthus curviflorus

What's the taxonomical classification of Phlogacanthus curviflorus?

Phlogacanthus curviflorus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized into the order Lamiales. It is a member of the family Acanthaceae, specifically falling under the genus Phlogacanthus, and is identified by its unique species designation, curviflorus.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Phlogacanthus curviflorus can cause skin irritation, localized redness, and mild allergic reactions if the plant comes into direct contact with sensitive areas of the body. Individuals with pre-existing dermatological conditions may experience more intense swelling or itching upon exposure to its sap. Some reports suggest that accidental ingestion could lead to gastrointestinal distress such as nausea or stomach cramping. There is limited scientific data regarding the long-term systemic effects of this species on human health. It is essential to exercise caution and avoid touching the foliage to prevent these potential adverse reactions.
