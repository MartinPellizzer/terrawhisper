What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains tropane alkaloids, specifically hyoscyamine, scopolamine, and atropine, which act as potent anticholinergic agents. These medicinal compounds function by inhibiting the action of acetylcholine at muscarinic receptors in the nervous system. Due to these chemical properties, extracts from the plant are traditionally studied for their ability to relieve muscle spasms and reduce secretions. However, the high toxicity of these alkaloids requires extremely precise dosing to avoid dangerous side effects. Scientists continue to investigate these specific molecules for potential applications in treating gastrointestinal disorders and certain neurological conditions.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause severe anticholinergic poisoning characterized by nausea, vomiting, dizziness, tachycardia, mydriasis, dry mouth, blurred vision, confusion, hallucinations, delirium, hyperthermia, and urinary retention. Ingesting the toxic alkaloids leads to an elevated heart rate and significantly dilated pupils that impair sight. The central nervous system becomes highly disturbed, resulting in intense mental disorientation and frightening visual or auditory perceptions. Physiological disruptions also include an inability to urinate and a dangerous rise in body temperature. These combined symptoms represent a life-threatening medical emergency requiring immediate intervention.
