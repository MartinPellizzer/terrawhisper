What's the taxonomical classification of Berberis soulieana?

Berberis soulieana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is classified under the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Ranunculales. This plant is a member of the family Berberidaceae, specifically situated within the genus Berberis, and is identified by the unique species epithet soulieana.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Berberis soulieana contains high concentrations of isoquinoline alkaloids, specifically berberine, palmatine, and jatrorrhizine, which serve as its primary bioactive constituents. These specific chemical compounds are known for possessing significant antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties. Researchers often investigate these alkaloids for their potential to regulate glucose metabolism and manage lipid levels in the bloodstream. The plant's roots and stems are the most concentrated sources of these therapeutic substances.
