Lobelia zeylanica

What's the taxonomical classification of Lobelia zeylanica?

Lobelia zeylanica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant falls under the order Asterales and is a member of the family Campanulaceae. Finally, it is placed within the genus Lobelia, with its specific identity defined by the species name zeylanica.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Lobelia zeylanica contains various bioactive alkaloids, specifically lobeline and related nitrogenous compounds, which serve as its primary medicinal constituents. These alkaloids act primarily on the nervous system by mimicking the action of nicotine at nicotinic acetylcholine receptors. Beyond these nitrogenous bases, the plant is thought to possess diverse secondary metabolites including flavonoids and terpenoids that contribute to its pharmacological profile. Research suggests these chemical properties may offer potential applications in treating respiratory ailments and regulating certain neurological functions. The specific concentration and synergy of these compounds dictate the plant's overall therapeutic potential in traditional medicine.

What are the therapeutic actions of Lobelia zeylanica?

Lobelia zeylanica has potent emetic, expectorant, and anti-inflammatory properties used primarily to induce vomiting and clear respiratory congestion. The plant contains various alkaloids that stimulate the gastrointestinal tract to expel toxins through emesis. When applied to respiratory issues, its active compounds help loosen mucus and soothe irritated airways. Local traditional practitioners also utilize the plant to reduce swelling and alleviate pain in various bodily tissues. These therapeutic actions make it a significant component in ethnobotanical treatments for digestive and pulmonary ailments.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat respiratory ailments such as asthma, bronchitis, and various forms of cough. It serves as an expectorant to help clear mucus from the lungs and soothe inflamed airways. Local practitioners often utilize its properties to alleviate symptoms of congestion and shortness of breath. The plant's medicinal value is particularly noted for its ability to relax the bronchial muscles during respiratory distress.
