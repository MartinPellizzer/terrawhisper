In addition to sesamolin, Sesamum orientale contains stigmasterol glycosides, which are plant sterols that support cholesterol regulation and may contribute to heart health.

The herb is also rich in sesamin, a lignan with strong antioxidant and anti-cancer properties that can protect cells from damage and inhibit tumor growth. Furthermore, Sesamum orientale is a good source of various fatty acids, including unsaturated fats like linoleic acid, which are essential for maintaining healthy skin, brain function, and overall metabolic processes.

Together, these constituents make Sesamum orientale a valuable herb in traditional and modern medicine for promoting holistic wellness and preventing chronic diseases.

Medicinal Parts

Sesamum orientale seed is one of the most widely used medicinal parts of this plant, known for its rich content of essential oils, lignans, and antioxidants.

The seed oil is commonly employed in traditional medicine to treat ailments such as inflammation, digestive issues, and skin conditions due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Additionally, the seeds are valued for their ability to support cardiovascular health by helping to lower cholesterol levels and improve blood circulation.

The leaves of Sesamum orientale are also utilized in herbal remedies, often prepared as a decoction to alleviate respiratory problems and reduce fever. The root and fruit of the plant are less frequently used but have been traditionally employed in some cultures for their potential diuretic and detoxifying effects.

Overall, various parts of Sesamum orientale contribute to its reputation as a versatile medicinal herb with a wide range of therapeutic applications.
