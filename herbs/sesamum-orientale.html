Indian Sesame (Sesamum orientale)

Indian Sesame (Sesamum orientale) is a member of the Pedaliaceae family, native to Southern Africa, Eastern Africa, and Madagascar. Traditionally, its seeds, leaves, and bark have been used for oil infusions, decoctions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, tonic, and carminative actions, and has a long history of use in african traditional medicine, ayurvedic medicine, and european herbal medicine.

Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters.
Leaves
Sessile, ovate leaves with upper surface pale green and lower surface glossy dark green, bearing prominent stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Flowers are small, yellow, actinomorphic, with five petals and a superior ovary, arranged in dense spikes.
Stems
Erect, branched, woody, with opposite, sessile, lanceolate leaves and smooth, glabrous surfaces.
