Indian sesame

Sesamum orientale

Indian Sesame (Sesamum orientale) is a traditional medicinal plant native to South and Southeast Asia, known for its therapeutic properties and historical use in Ayurvedic and traditional medicine systems. It is primarily used to improve conditions such as digestive disorders, skin ailments, and respiratory issues due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Botanical Identification

Sesamum orientale, also known as Indian sesame, is a member of the Pedaliaceae family. It is an annual herb native to South and Southeast Asia. The plant has alternate leaves, yellow flowers, and produces capsule fruits containing small, oil-rich seeds. Common regional names include sesamum and til. Synonyms in herbal literature include Sesamum indicum, though it is distinct from the commonly used sesame.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Liliopsida
  • Order: Pedaliales
  • Family: Pedaliaceae
  • Genus: Sesamum
  • Species: Sesamum orientale

Active Compounds

Indian Sesame contains essential oil, phenolic acid, and terpenoids as its primary bioactive compounds. These compounds work together to provide its medicinal properties, with essential oils contributing to anti-inflammatory effects and phenolic acids offering antioxidant benefits. The synergistic interaction between these compounds enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

  • Essential oil
  • Phenolic acid
  • Terpenoid

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory conditions and digestive issues.

Safety Profile

Indian Sesame, or Sesamum orientale, is generally considered safe for most people when used in traditional herbal medicine. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and should be avoided by those with known allergies to the sesame family. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
