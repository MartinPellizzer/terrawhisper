Anaphalis nepalensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Anaphalis nepalensis has perennial herbaceous stems that support pinnately compound leaves and terminal, discoid flower heads surrounded by white, petal-like involucral bracts. The leaves are typically arranged alternately and feature multiple pairs of leaflets with serrated margins. These plants produce small, yellowish disc florets clustered tightly within the center of the flower head. The involucral bracts are arranged in several rows, providing a bright appearance that mimics true petals to attract pollinators. The overall structure is adapted to the high-altitude environments where this species is commonly found.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the Himalayan mountain range, spanning across Nepal, Bhutan, northern India, and parts of Tibet. It typically thrives in high-altitude alpine and subalpine zones located between 2,500 and 4,500 meters above sea level. The species prefers open rocky slopes, meadows, and scree areas where sunlight is abundant. Within these montane ecosystems, it is often found growing alongside other specialized high-elevation flora. Its distribution is strictly limited by the specific climatic conditions and temperature gradients found in these mountain regions.

What are the herbal preparations of Anaphalis nepalensis?

Anaphalis nepalensis is prepared as decoctions, infusions, powders, and pastes to treat various medicinal conditions. What side-effects this plant can have?

Anaphalis nepalensis can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if handled or ingested improperly. While used in traditional medicine, the specific chemical compounds within the plant may trigger hypersensitivity reactions in sensitive individuals. Direct contact with the plant material can lead to redness or itching on the skin surface. If consumed in uncontrolled doses, it might result in nausea or stomach discomfort. Users should exercise caution and consult a professional before using this species for medicinal purposes.
