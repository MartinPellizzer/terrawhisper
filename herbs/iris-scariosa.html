Iris scariosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Iris scariosa has sword-shaped, ensiform leaves that are typically grayish-green and emerge from a rhizomatous base. The plant features slender, erect stems that support delicate, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pale blue or violet. These blossoms are characterized by thin, spreading tepals and a prominent, yellowish signal on the falls. The rhizomes are stout and creeping, allowing the plant to form dense colonies in its natural habitat. Small, green seed capsules develop following successful pollination to ensure future dispersal.
