What's the taxonomical classification of Lithospermum zollingeri?

Lithospermum zolliingeri belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant's taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Boraginales and the family Boraginaceae, ultimately placing it within the genus Lithospermum, with the specific designation of the species zollingeri.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains naphthoquinones, specifically shikonin and its derivatives, which serve as the primary bioactive components.

These chemical compounds are known for their potent antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The presence of these pigments often results in the characteristic reddish color found within the root tissues. Researchers study these molecules for their potential applications in wound healing and skin treatments.

Additionally, the chemical profile of the plant may include various fatty acids and other secondary metabolites that contribute to its biological activity.

    What are the herbal preparations of Lithospermum zollingeri?

    Lithospermum zolliingeri is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical oil derived from its roots. The dried roots are often simmered in water for extended periods to extract the active alkaloids and pigments. These liquid preparations can be ingested to address internal issues or applied externally to the skin. Some practitioners also incorporate the plant into tinctures using alcohol as a solvent for long-term storage. This versatility allows the plant to be used for both systemic and localized medicinal purposes.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Lithospermum zoLLIngeri can cause gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, or potential toxicity if ingested due to the presence of pyrrolizidine alkaloids. These specific chemical compounds are known to cause cumulative liver damage when consumed in significant amounts over time. Some individuals may also experience localized allergic reactions or dermatitis if the plant makes direct contact with sensitive skin. Because the specific safety profile for this particular species is not extensively documented in human clinical trials, caution is required.
