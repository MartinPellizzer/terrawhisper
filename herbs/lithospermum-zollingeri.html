What's the taxonomical classification of Lithospermum zollingeri?

Lithospermum zolliingeri belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant's taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Boraginales and the family Boraginaceae, ultimately placing it within the genus Lithospermum, with the specific designation of the species zollingeri.

These red-pigmented compounds are known for their potent antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties. In traditional botanical applications, these chemical structures are utilized to promote skin healing and reduce tissue irritation. The specific molecular arrangement of these naphthoquinones allows them to interact effectively with cellular processes involved in wound repair. Researchers continue to study these specific compounds to understand their potential in modern dermatological pharmacology.

Because the specific safety profile for this particular species is not extensively documented in human clinical trials, caution is required.
