Allium condensatum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Allium condensatum has a bulbous structure supporting narrow, linear leaves and a dense, globose umbel composed of numerous small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically exhibits a compact growth habit with leaves that are often shorter than the flowering scape. Each individual flower possesses six tepals that surround a central reproductive structure. The inflorescence is notably crowded, giving the flower head a tightly packed appearance compared to related species. These morphological features allow the plant to thrive in its specific ecological niche.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Turkey, specifically within the Taurus Mountains. It primarily inhabits rocky slopes and limestone outcrops at varying elevations throughout the Anatolian peninsula. Localized populations are often found in scrubland environments where well-drained soil is abundant. The species maintains a restricted range that is characteristic of many endemic flora in this Mediterranean climate zone. These specific topographical requirements limit its spread to certain high-altitude habitats.
