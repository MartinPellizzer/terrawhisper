Allium condensatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Allium condensatum?

Allium condensatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving through its hierarchical structure, the plant falls under the order Asparagales and is a member of the family Amaryllidaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement within the genus Allium, with its unique designation being the species condensatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Allium condensatum has a bulbous structure supporting narrow, linear leaves and a dense, globose umbel composed of numerous small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically exhibits a compact growth habit with leaves that are often shorter than the flowering scape. Each individual flower possesses six tepals that surround a central reproductive structure. The inflorescence is notably crowded, giving the flower head a tightly packed appearance compared to related species. These morphological features allow the plant to thrive in its specific ecological niche.

Localized populations are often found in scrubland environments where well-drained soil is abundant.
