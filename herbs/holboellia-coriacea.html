Holboellia coriacea

What's the taxonomical classification of Holboellia coriacea?

Holboellia coriacea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized under the order Ranunculales and is a member of the family Lardizabalaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identification is defined by the genus Holboellia and the species coriacea.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat digestive ailments, skin infections, and respiratory issues. Its medicinal properties are often applied to soothe stomach discomfort and promote healing in the gastrointestinal tract. When used topically, the plant can assist in managing various dermatological conditions and wounds. Some traditional practices also utilize its extracts to alleviate symptoms related to breathing difficulties. These diverse applications highlight the plant's importance in folk medicine for managing several physical ailments.

What are the herbal preparations of Holboellia coriacea?

Holboellia coriacea is prepared primarily as a decoction, a concentrated liquid extract made by boiling the plant parts in water to release medicinal compounds. This method is commonly used to create therapeutic teas or medicinal broths from the leaves and stems. In some traditional practices, the plant may also be processed into a topical paste for external application on the skin. Some practitioners utilize the dried material to make infusions, which involves steeping the plant in hot water without boiling. These various preparations allow for the extraction of different chemical profiles depending on the desired medicinal effect.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Holboellia coriacea can cause severe skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential allergic reactions if ingested or touched. Contact with the plant's sap may lead to contact dermatitis, characterized by redness, itching, and blistering on the affected area. If consumed accidentally, individuals might experience nausea, stomach cramps, or vomiting due to unknown toxic compounds. Some people may also exhibit systemic symptoms such as dizziness or headaches following exposure. Careful handling and avoidance of ingestion are necessary to prevent these adverse physiological responses.
