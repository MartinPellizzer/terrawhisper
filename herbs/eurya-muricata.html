Eurya muricata

What's the taxonomical classification of Eurya muricata?

Eurya muricata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Ericales and is a member of the family Pentaphylacaceae. Ultimately, this plant is identified by its genus, Eurya, and its specific species designation, muricata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eurya muriata has opposite, elliptical to obovate leaves that are typically 2 to 5 centimeters long and possess serrated margins with a leathery texture. The leaf surfaces often appear glossy and dark green, while the undersides may show a slightly paler coloration. Its stems are woody and can become somewhat branched as the shrub matures. Small, white, actinomorphic flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often appearing in clusters. These flowers eventually give way to small, fleshy berries that facilitate seed dispersal.
