Diplazium donianum

What's the taxonomical classification of Diplazium donianum?

Diplazium donianum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae, it falls under the order Polypodiales. This fern is a representative of the family Aspleniaceae, specifically categorized under the genus Diplazium, with its specific designation being the species donianum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Diplazium donianum has long-creeping rhizomes that produce large, pinnate fronds characterized by a dark, shiny texture and a distinctively narrow shape. The stipes are typically dark brown or blackish and possess a smooth, polished appearance. Each leaf consists of multiple pairs of pinnae that are arranged symmetrically along the central rachis. The pinnules are lanceolate with slightly serrated or wavy margins that give the foliage a delicate look. Small, circular sori are located on the underside of the leaflets to facilitate spore production.
