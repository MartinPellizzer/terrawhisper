What's the taxonomical classification of Carex brunnea?

Carex brunnea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Poales and into the family Cyperaceae. Finally, the plant is identified within the genus Carex, reaching its specific designation at the species level as Carex brunnea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Carex brunnea has a rhizomatous growth habit characterized by slender, brownish stems and linear, grass-like leaves that typically reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters.

The foliage is relatively narrow and often displays a subtle yellowish-green hue compared to other sedge species. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous spikes that emerge from the leaf axils. The perigynia, which enclose the seeds, are typically brownish and possess a distinct shape that aids in identification.

These morphological features collectively allow the plant to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
