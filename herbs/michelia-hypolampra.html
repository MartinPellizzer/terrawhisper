Michelia hypolampra

What's the taxonomical classification of Michelia hypolampra?

Michelia hypolampra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Magnoliales. Furthermore, it is a member of the family Magnoliaceae and is categorized under the genus Michelia, specifically identified by the species name hypolampra.

What are the herbal preparations of Michelia hypolampra?

Michelia hypolampra is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste derived from its dried leaves and bark. These herbal preparations are frequently utilized in traditional medicine to address specific inflammatory conditions. The concentrated liquid extracts are often ingested to manage internal ailments or systemic discomfort. Additionally, the plant material can be ground into a fine powder to create poultices for external application on the skin. This versatility in preparation allows the plant's bioactive compounds to be delivered through various therapeutic routes.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Michelia hypolampra can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested or handled improperly. The plant contains various chemical compounds that may trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals upon skin contact. If any part of the plant is swallowed, it can lead to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. Some people may also experience respiratory irritation if they inhale concentrated pollen or particles from the foliage. Proper precautions like wearing gloves and avoiding ingestion are recommended when working with this species.
