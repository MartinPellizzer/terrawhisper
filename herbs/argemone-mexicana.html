What to know about Argemone mexicana (prickly poppy) before using it medicinally

Prickly poppy, scientifically known as Argemone mexicana, is a herb that has been revered for its impressive array of health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can help alleviate symptoms of arthritis, improve digestion, and even provide natural relief from anxiety.

Beyond its medicinal uses, prickly poppy is also prized for its culinary applications, with its seeds often used as a spice to add depth and warmth to dishes ranging from soups to salads. As an ornamental plant, prickly poppy boasts delicate white flowers and striking, velvety leaves that make it a popular choice among gardeners and landscapers. From a botanical perspective, prickly poppy is characterized by its distinctive, waxy coating that protects it from drought and pests, as well as its unique ability to grow in poor soil conditions.

With historical records dating back to the ancient Aztecs, who used it for medicinal purposes, prickly poppy has been a beloved and versatile herb for centuries.
