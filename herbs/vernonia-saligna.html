Vernonia saligna

What's the taxonomical classification of Vernonia saligna?

Vernonia saliigna belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Asterales. As a member of the family Asteraceae, this plant is placed in the genus Vernonia, with its specific designation being the species saliigna.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vernonia saligna has an erect, perennial herbaceous habit characterized by slender, cylindrical stems that typically reach heights between 30 and 80 centimeters. The leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem and exhibit an elliptical to lanceolate shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. Small, tubular florets are clustered into compact, discoid heads that appear in terminal cymes. These flower heads are often pale lilac or white in color and possess a distinctive involucre of overlapping bracts. The plant's root system is composed of a woody rhizome that allows it to persist in its native grassland habitats.
