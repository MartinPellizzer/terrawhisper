Callicarpa bodinieri

What's the taxonomical classification of Callicarpa bodinieri?

Callicarpa bodinieri belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. This specific plant is further categorized into the genus Callicarpa, ultimately reaching its unique designation at the species level as bodinieri.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Callicarpa bodinieri has opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape with serrated margins and an acute apex. The leaf surfaces are typically pubescent, providing a soft texture to the foliage. Its woody stems support small, axillary clusters of flowers that eventually develop into distinct drupes. These small, spherical fruits are notable for their vibrant color and clustered arrangement along the branches. The overall structure of the plant is characterized by its shrubby growth habit and multi-stemmed form.

As discussed in a study, investigation of the leaves and twigs of Callicarpa bodinieri resulted in the isolation of nine new abietane diterpenoids, bodinieric acids A-I (1-9), and six known compounds (10-15). To explore potential therapeutic targets for immune-mediated disease, the inhibitory activities of the isolates were determined against 13 kinase enzymes. Eight compounds exhibited moderate inhibitory effects on spleen tyrosine kinase (SYK). Specifically, the IC50 values of compounds 2 and 6 were 7.2 and 10.7 μM, respectively. A preliminary structure-activity relationship of this scaffold was analyzed using both molecular docking and a 3D-QSAR pharmacophore model.

As discussed in a study, seven compounds were isolated from the leaves of Callicarpa bodinieri. These constituents include 5-hydroxy-4',3,6,7-tetramethoxy flavone (I), luteolin-7-O-glucoside (II), chrysoeriol-4'-O-glucoside (III), luteolin-4'-O-glucoside (IV), beta-sitosterol (V), ursolic acid (VI), and betulinic acid (VII). All 7 of these compounds were isolated from this plant for the first time. Specifically, compounds I, III, and IV were isolated from the Callicarpa genus for the first time.
