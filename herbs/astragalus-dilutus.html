Astragalus dilutus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Astragalus dilutus has a low-growing, spreading growth habit characterized by slender, procumbent stems that often form dense mats or cushions. The leaves are pinnately compound, typically consisting of several small, oval to oblong leaflets arranged along a central axis. Each leaflet is relatively smooth with an entire margin and lacks significant pubescence. The inflorescence features small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale lavender or pink. These blossoms are borne on short stalks emerging from the leaf axils, often appearing in small clusters.
