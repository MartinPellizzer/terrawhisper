Alternanthera sessilis

What's the taxonomical classification of Alternanthera sessilis?

Alternanthera sessilis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Caryophyllales. This plant is a member of the family Amaranthaceae, falling under the genus Alternanthera, and is specifically identified by its species name, sessilis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Alternanthethera sessilis has prostrate, creeping stems that branch extensively from a central root system and possess small, elliptical to lanceolate leaves measuring roughly 1 to 3 centimeters in length. The leaves are typically arranged oppositely along the stems and exhibit a smooth or slightly hairy texture on their surfaces. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in dense, axillary clusters that lack prominent petals. These plants often develop a reddish or purplish tint on their foliage and stems depending on light exposure. The root system is shallow but efficient at anchoring the plant as it spreads across the soil surface.

What are the pharmacological activities of Alternanthera sessilis?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological activities including analgesic, anti-inflammatory, anti-microbial, anti-oxidant, and anti-cancer properties. Its ability to reduce pain and swelling makes it a valuable subject for studying natural anti-inflammatory and analgesic agents. Researchers have also identified its potent anti-microbial effects, which help in combating various pathogenic microorganisms. Furthermore, the high concentration of bioactive compounds provides strong anti-oxidant defense against oxidative stress. Recent studies are even exploring its potential anti-cancer mechanisms to inhibit the growth of malignant cells.

As discussed in a study, the methanol extract of the aerial parts of Alternanthera sessilis demonstrates antihyperglycemic activity by causing dose-dependent reductions in blood glucose levels. At doses of 50, 100, 200, and 400 mg per kg body weight, the extract reduced blood sugar levels by 22.9, 30.7, 45.4, and 46.1%, respectively. Regarding analgesic activity, the extract administered at these same four doses reduced the number of abdominal writhings by 27.6, 37.9, 41.4, and 44.8%, respectively. These findings validate the use of the plant to alleviate severe pain and suggest it may be a potential source for blood sugar lowering drugs.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

These specific substances contribute to the plant's diverse pharmacological profile and potential therapeutic benefits. The presence of flavonoids like quercetin and rutin provides significant antioxidant properties to the organism. Additionally, the combination of (+) -catechin and ellagic acid supports cellular health through complex biochemical interactions. Finally, the inclusion of alkaloids adds a layer of biological activity that can influence various physiological processes.

As discussed in a study, the ethanol extract of the Alternanthera sessilis whole plant was tested at 250 and 500 mg/kg body weight dose levels. HPLC analysis for polyphenols in the extract detected (+) -catechin, rutin, ellagic acid, and quercetin at concentrations of 117.72, 490.74, 3007.26, and 13.85 mg/100 g of dry extract, respectively. The study found that the extract significantly (p < 0.001) increased the onset of sleep and decreased the duration of sleep in the sleeping time test. Furthermore, the extract significantly (p < 0.001) increased movements in open field and hole cross tests, inhibited the writhed reflex in the writhed test, and raised the pain threshold in the hot plate test. These results indicate that Alternanthera sessilis can be a potential source of bioactive molecules.
