Tetrastigma cauliflorum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tetrastigma cauliflorum has woody, climbing lianas characterized by thick, succulent stems that produce flowers directly from the main trunk and older branches. The leaves are typically large, elliptical or ovate, and arranged in an alternate pattern along the climbing vines. Its specialized cauliflorous flowering habit allows blossoms to emerge from the woody bark rather than from new green shoots. These woody stems often feature prominent lenticels and can grow to significant diameters as they ascend the forest canopy. The overall structure supports a heavy, sprawling growth habit typical of tropical forest climbers.
