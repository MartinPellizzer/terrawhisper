Pleione hookeriana

What's the taxonomical classification of Pleione hookeriana?

Pleione hookeriana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Asparagales. It is a member of the family Orchidaceae, falling specifically within the genus Pleione, with its unique biological identity defined by the species hookerianana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pleione hookeriana has a terrestrial growth habit characterized by small, fleshy pseudobulbs that are partially covered by the bases of old leaf sheaths.

Each pseudobulb typically produces one to two elliptical, bright green leaves that emerge during the active growing season. The inflorescence is a terminal raceme that can bear several fragrant, zygomorphic flowers ranging in color from white to soft pink with distinct purple spotting. These flowers feature a prominent labellum and are supported by slender, arching pedicels.

The plant's overall structure is compact, making it well-suited for life in montane forest environments.
