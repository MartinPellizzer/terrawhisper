Panax ginseng

Panax Ginseng (Panax ginseng) is a member of the Araliaceae family, traditionally used in Chinese medicine and other Asian cultures. The root of the plant is most commonly harvested and utilized for its purported effects on energy, mental clarity, and immune support.

Botanical Identification

Panax ginseng, also known as Korean ginseng, is a perennial plant native to East Asia. It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy roots, lance-shaped leaves, and yellowish flowers. Common names include red ginseng and Chinese ginseng, with synonyms like "Jin Shen" in traditional Chinese herbal texts.

Active Compounds

Panax ginseng contains key active compounds such as saponins, glycosides, and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to influence various physiological processes, enhancing the herb's therapeutic effects in traditional and modern herbal medicine.

Safety Profile

Panax ginseng is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners, and should be avoided by individuals with hypertension or specific mental health conditions. Consult a healthcare provider before use, especially for prolonged periods or in high doses.
