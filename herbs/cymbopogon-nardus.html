Cymbopogon nardus

Cymbopogon nardus, commonly known as East Indian sandalwood grass or West Indian lemongrass, is a tropical plant native to India and Southeast Asia.

It has been traditionally used for its medicinal and therapeutic properties, providing health benefits such as reducing fever, alleviating digestive issues, and relieving stress and anxiety.

The therapeutic actions of Cymbopogon nardus include its analgesic, anti-inflammatory, and antiseptic properties, which help to soothe skin irritations, reduce pain, and prevent the growth of microorganisms.

The plant's bioactive constituents include sesquiterpenes, such as citral and geraniol, which contribute to its therapeutic effects and fragrance.
