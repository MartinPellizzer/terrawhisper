Licaria brasiliensis

Licaria brasiliensis, commonly known as Licaria, is a plant species in the family Lauraceae.

It is a small, evergreen tree or shrub native to the Amazon rainforest, valued for its medicinal and therapeutic properties.

The Licaria plant has been traditionally used to treat various health issues, offering health benefits such as anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which may help alleviate symptoms of conditions such as fever, pain, and respiratory problems.

The bioactive constituents of Licaria brasiliensis include compounds like terpenes, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its therapeutic actions of sedation, analgesia, and antipyretic effects.
