Barleria cristata

What's the taxonomical classification of Barleria cristata?

Barleria cristata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Lamiales. This plant is a representative of the family Acanthaceae, specifically situated within the genus Barleria, and is identified by its unique species name, cristata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Barleria cristata has an erect, perennial shrubby habit characterized by opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with entire margins and a glabrous texture. The stems are woody at the base and often exhibit a slightly decumbent growth pattern before turning upright. Its inflorescence consists of terminal or axillary racemes bearing bright orange or reddish flowers with distinct tubular corollas. The floral structure features prominent stamens and a spreading limb that attracts various pollinators. These morphological traits are typical of the Acanthaceae family, aiding in the plant's identification in tropical habitats.

Together, these bioactive substances define the medicinal profile of the species.

As discussed in a study, Barleria cristata contains the key metabolites barlerin and verbascoside. These extracts demonstrate significant anti-proliferative activity against PC-3 cells by inducing DNA damage, enhancing apoptosis, and obstructing the cell cycle. Importantly, these extracts did not exert cytotoxic effects on PBMCs, HPrEC, or THLE-3 cells. The treatment with these extracts activates PBMCs and leads to the upregulation of several cytokine genes including IL-2, IL-10, IL-12, IL-15, IL-21, and IFN-γ. These immune cell activations ultimately promote cytotoxicity for PC-3 cells.
