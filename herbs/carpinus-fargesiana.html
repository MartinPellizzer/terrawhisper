Carpinus fargesiana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Carpinus fargesiana has ovate to elliptical leaves that measure up to 15 centimeters long, featuring doubly serrated margins and prominent, parallel secondary veins. The leaf texture is typically coriaceous, providing a leathery feel to the foliage. Its branches often exhibit a greyish bark that becomes more fissured as the tree matures. The reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers followed by nutlets attached to three-lobed, papery bracts. These specialized bracts facilitate wind dispersal of the seeds from the canopy.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

These trees are most commonly found growing along the edges of moist ravines and within thick montane woodlands. The distribution is often tied to specific microclimates that provide consistent humidity and cool temperatures.
