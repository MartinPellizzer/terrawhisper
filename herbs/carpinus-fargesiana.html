Carpinus fargesiana

What's the taxonomical classification of Carpinus fargesiana?

Carpinus fargesiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, eventually falling under the order Fagales. This species is a member of the family Betulaceae, specifically within the genus Carpinus, and is identified by its unique species name, fargesiana.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Carpinus fargesiana can cause skin irritation or allergic contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals upon direct contact with its leaves or bark. While extensive clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, many members of the Carpinus genus contain saponins which may trigger localized reactions. Some users might experience redness, itching, or a mild rash if the plant's sap touches the skin. It is generally advisable to use gloves when handling the specimen to prevent these potential dermatological issues. Careful observation is recommended for those with known sensitivities to woody plant extracts.
