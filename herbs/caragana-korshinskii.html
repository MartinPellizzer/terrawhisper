Caragana korshinskii

What's the taxonomical classification of Caragana korshinskii?

Caragana korshinskii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Fabales. It is further categorized under the family Fabaceae, a group well-known for its diverse range of legumes. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Caragana, specifically identified as the species korshinskii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Caragana korshinskii has a deciduous shrub morphology characterized by upright, spreading branches and pinnately compound leaves composed of several pairs of small, oval leaflets. The plant typically reaches a height of one to two meters, developing a dense and bushy structure as it matures. During the flowering season, it produces clusters of bright red or pink pea-like flowers that emerge from the terminal ends of the stems. The foliage remains a vibrant green throughout the growing season before transitioning in color during the autumn months. Its woody stems are relatively slender and provide the structural support for its profuse seasonal blooms.
