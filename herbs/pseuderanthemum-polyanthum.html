Pseuderanthemum polyanthum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pseuderanthemum polyanthum has a herbaceous structure characterized by slender, erect stems and oppositely arranged, lanceolate to ovate leaves with entire or slightly serrated margins. The foliage typically displays a smooth texture and a vibrant green coloration. Its most distinguishing feature is the inflorescence, which consists of numerous small, tubular flowers clustered at the terminal ends of the branches. These flowers are often arranged in dense cymes or racemes, providing a prolific blooming appearance. The root system is generally fibrous, allowing the plant to anchor effectively in its native soil.
