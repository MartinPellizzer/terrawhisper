Pseuderanthemum polyanthum

What's the taxonomical classification of Pseuderanthemum polyanthum?

Pseuderanthemum polyanthum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the order Lamiales and the family Acanthaceae. Finally, the specific identity of the plant is established within the genus Pseuderanthemum, with its unique designation being the species polyanthum.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Pseuderanthemum polyanthum can cause severe skin irritation, respiratory distress, and gastrointestinal upset if ingested or touched. Contact with the plant's sap often leads to localized dermatitis characterized by redness and itching. Inhaling airborne particles from the flowers may trigger allergic reactions or asthma-like symptoms in sensitive individuals. If accidentally swallowed, the plant's toxins can result in nausea, vomiting, and abdominal cramping. Immediate medical attention is recommended if any significant symptoms emerge after exposure.
