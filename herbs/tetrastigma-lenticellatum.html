Tetrastigma lenticellatum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tetrastigma lenticellatum has woody vines characterized by thick stems covered in numerous prominent lenticels. These small, porous openings allow for gas exchange across the bark of the climbing plant. The leaves are typically large and elliptical with entire margins and distinct venation patterns. Its climbing habit is supported by strong adventitious roots that anchor the plant to host trees. The overall structure is adapted to thrive within the dense, humid canopies of tropical forest environments.
