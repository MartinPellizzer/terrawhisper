Tetrastigma lenticellatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Tetrastigma lenticellatum?

Tetrastigma lenticellatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Vitales. As a member of the family Vitaceae, it is placed within the genus Tetrastigma, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species lenticellatum.

    What are the herbal preparations of Tetrastigma lenticellatum?

This aqueous extract is often consumed directly to address various ailments according to traditional practices.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Tetrastigma lenticellatum can cause adverse reactions including gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, and potential neurological impairment if ingested in improper doses. While specific human clinical trials are limited, traditional medicinal use suggests that concentrated extracts may lead to nausea or abdominal cramping. Some researchers warn that the chemical constituents could trigger allergic contact dermatitis when the plant material touches sensitive skin. There is also a theoretical risk of toxicity affecting the central nervous system due to unknown alkaloid concentrations. Users should exercise extreme caution to avoid these physiological complications.
