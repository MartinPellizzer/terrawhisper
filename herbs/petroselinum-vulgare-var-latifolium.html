Parsley (Petroselinum Vulgare Var. Latifolium)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Petroselinum vulgare var. latifolium.

Parsley, scientifically known as Petroselinum vulgare var. latifolium, is a widely used medicinal herb, spice, and adaptogen valued for its health benefits and distinct flavor. It is rich in vitamins, antioxidants, and compounds like apiol and limonene, which contribute to its primary benefits such as supporting digestion, reducing inflammation, and promoting detoxification. Traditionally, parsley has been used in various cultures for its diuretic properties, as a remedy for respiratory ailments, and to freshen breath, with ancient Greeks and Romans incorporating it into their medicine and cuisine. In modern wellness practices, parsley is popular in herbal teas, dietary supplements, and as a natural remedy for kidney health and detox support. Its unique combination of a fresh, peppery flavor and a rare active compound, apiol, makes it both a culinary staple and a powerful medicinal plant with a long history of use.
