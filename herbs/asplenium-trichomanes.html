What's the taxonomical classification of Asplenium trichomanes?

Asplenium trichomanes belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae. Its evolutionary path continues through the order Polypodiales, placing it in the family Aspleniaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Asplenium, with its specific designation being the species trichomanes.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Asplenium trichomanes can cause skin irritation or allergic contact dermatitis in individuals with sensitive skin upon direct contact. Some people might experience mild dermatological reactions if they handle the fronds without protection. There is currently limited scientific data regarding systemic toxicity or internal side effects from ingestion. Therefore, it is best to avoid direct contact if you have a history of plant-based allergies.
