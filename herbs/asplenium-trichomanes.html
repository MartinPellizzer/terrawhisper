Asplenium trichomanes

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Asplenium trichomanes has a creeping rhizome covered in dark, shiny scales and produces small, simple fronds that are typically 5 to 15 centimeters long. Each frond consists of a single, undivided blade that is lanceolate or linear in shape. The leaf margins are entire, meaning they lack teeth or lobes, and the venation is free and dichotomous. A distinguishing characteristic is the presence of fine hairs on the rachis and the underside of the veins. The sori are arranged in a single row along the underside of the pinnules, protected by small, kidney-shaped indusia.
