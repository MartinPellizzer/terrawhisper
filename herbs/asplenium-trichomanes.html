Asplenium trichomanes

What's the taxonomical classification of Asplenium trichomanes?

Asplenium trichomanes belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae. Its evolutionary path continues through the order Polypodiales, placing it in the family Aspleniaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Asplenium, with its specific designation being the species trichomanes.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Asplenium trichomanes has a creeping rhizome covered in dark, shiny scales and produces small, simple fronds that are typically 5 to 15 centimeters long.

Each frond consists of a single, undivided blade that is lanceolate or linear in shape. The leaf margins are entire, meaning they lack teeth or lobes, and the venation is free and dichotomous. A distinguishing characteristic is the presence of fine hairs on the rachis and the underside of the veins.

The sori are arranged in a single row along the underside of the pinnules, protected by small, kidney-shaped indusia.
