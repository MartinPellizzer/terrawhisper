Bertiera racemosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bertiera racemosa has opposite, oblong to lanceolate leaves with entire margins and a terminal, racemose inflorescence consisting of small, tubular flowers. The stems are typically quadrangular and characterized by a woody structure as the plant matures. The leaves feature prominent longitudinal veins that run parallel along the surface. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged along a central axis in a continuous spike-like cluster. The plant generally exhibits a shrubby growth habit with a compact branching pattern.
