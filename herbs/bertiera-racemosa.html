Bertiera racemosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Bertiera racemosa?

Bertiera racemosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical structure of biological classification, the plant is organized under the order Gentianaales and is a member of the family Rubiaceae. Ultimately, its specific taxonomic identification is defined by the genus Bertiera and the species name racemosa.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Bertiera racemosa contains various secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, and triterpenoid saponins that contribute to its biological activity. These chemical constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's reported antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Researchers focus on these specific compounds to understand how the plant might be used in traditional medicine for treating skin infections or localized swelling. The presence of glycosides within the plant tissue also suggests potential applications in modulating physiological functions. Detailed phytochemical screening remains necessary to fully isolate and quantify each individual bioactive molecule.

What are the therapeutic actions of Bertiera racemosa?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat intestinal worms, stomach aches, fever, and various skin infections. The leaves and roots are often prepared into decoctions or poultices to address these ailments. In many traditional medicinal practices, it serves as a remedy for digestive disturbances and internal parasites. Practitioners also apply parts of the plant topically to soothe inflammatory skin conditions. These applications vary depending on the specific symptoms being targeted by the patient.
