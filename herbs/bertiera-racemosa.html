What's the taxonomical classification of Bertiera racemosa?

Bertiera racemosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical structure of biological classification, the plant is organized under the order Gentianaales and is a member of the family Rubiaceae. Ultimately, its specific taxonomic identification is defined by the genus Bertiera and the species name racemosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bertiera racemosa has opposite, oblong to lanceolate leaves with entire margins and a terminal, racemose inflorescence consisting of small, tubular flowers.

The stems are typically quadrangular and characterized by a woody structure as the plant matures. The leaves feature prominent longitudinal veins that run parallel along the surface. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged along a central axis in a continuous spike-like cluster.

The plant generally exhibits a shrubby growth habit with a compact branching pattern.

    Bertiera racemosa has therapeutic actions as a bitter, diuretic, febrifuge, and emmenagogue.
