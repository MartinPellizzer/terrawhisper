Pterocephalus hookeri

What's the taxonomical classification of Pterocephalus hookeri?

Pterocephalus hookeri belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Dipsacales. Within this hierarchy, it is a member of the family Caprifoliaceae and is specifically identified by the genus Pterocephalus, with its unique species designation being hookeri.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Pterocephalus hookeri can cause skin irritation, localized allergic reactions, or gastrointestinal distress if ingested in large quantities. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, contact with the plant's tissues may trigger dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Ingesting parts of the plant might lead to stomach aches, nausea, or vomiting due to unverified chemical compounds. It is essential to avoid direct skin contact and ingestion to prevent these adverse physiological responses. Always consult a medical professional if symptoms occur after exposure to this species.
