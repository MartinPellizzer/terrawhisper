Ogden'S Hawthorn (Crataegus Ogdensburgensis)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Crataegus ogdensburgensis.

Ogden's hawthorn, scientifically known as Crataegus ogdensburgensis, is a rare medicinal herb native to the northeastern United States, particularly found in New York and Vermont. This small, thorny shrub is valued for its heart-healthy properties and has been traditionally used as an adaptogen to support cardiovascular function and reduce stress. Its primary benefits include improving circulation, lowering blood pressure, and enhancing mood through its flavonoid content, which acts as a natural antioxidant. Historically, Native American tribes and early European settlers used Ogden's hawthorn to treat ailments such as heart palpitations, anxiety, and digestive issues. Today, it is gaining recognition in modern wellness practices for its potential role in holistic heart care and as a natural alternative to pharmaceuticals, with its unique blend of bitter compounds and floral notes making it a distinctive ingredient in herbal teas and tinctures.
