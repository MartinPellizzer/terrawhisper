Setaria geniculata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Setaria geniculata has an annual growth habit characterized by erect, slender culms that typically reach heights of 20 to 60 centimeters. The leaf blades are linear and often possess a distinctively hairy or scabrous texture along the margins and surfaces. Its inflorescence consists of a contracted, cylindrical panicle that is densely packed with bristly spikelets. These bristles are often longer than the spikelets themselves and serve to aid in seed dispersal. The plant's structure is fine and delicate compared to other members of the Setaria genus.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of West and Central Africa, specifically spanning across countries such as Nigeria, Cameroon, and Ghana. It typically thrives in humid, lowland environments characterized by high rainfall and warm temperatures. Within these habitats, it is often found in disturbed areas, forest edges, and along riverbanks where soil moisture is consistent. The species is not widely documented in other natural continental regions outside of its primary African range.
