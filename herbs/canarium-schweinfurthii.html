What are the pharmacological activities of Canarium schweinfurthii?

This plant has antihyperlipidemic, antioxidant, antiproliferative, antiradical, and cytotoxic pharmacological activities. Its antioxidant and antiradical properties allow it to neutralize harmful free radicals within biological systems. The plant also demonstrates significant antiproliferative effects, which can inhibit the rapid growth of certain cells. Furthermore, its cytotoxic nature suggests a potential ability to target and destroy specific diseased cell lines. These combined therapeutic actions highlight the medicinal potential of Canarium schweinfurtii in various pharmacological applications.

According to a study published by "Food science & nutrition", Canarium schweinfurthii pulp oil demonstrates antioxidant and antihyperlipidemic potential. The oil contains 39.68% palmitic, 28.13% oleic, and 28.19% linoleic acids, with an EC50 value of 11.81 μg/mL. In a study involving 36 rats, a 2 mL/kg dose significantly reduced TAG from 93.83 ± 1.69 to 75.44 ± 4.57 mg/dL in males and total cholesterol from 72.89 ± 3.59 to 62.69 ± 6.24 mg/dL in females. This 2 mL/kg dose also reduced LDL from 15.52 ± 5.72 to 20.27 ± 4.92 mg/dL, VLDL from 18.76 ± 0.91 to 15.08 ± 1.08 mg/dL, total lipids from 268.24 ± 7.70 to 226.13 ± 18.95 mg/dL, and the atherosclerosis index from 1.88 ± 0.17 to 2.29 ± 0.27. Furthermore, the oil increased HDL levels to 38.61 ± 1.63 mg/dL in males and 27.33 ± 3.28 mg/dL in females, while boosting SOD activity to 85.00% ± 17.32% and catalase activity to 10.67 ± 0.36 μM/min/mL for males and 91.66% ± 2.88% and 10.85 ± 0.47 μM/min/mL for females.

According to a study published by "SpringerPlus", Canarium schweinfurthii is used in the treatment of bacterial infections including multidrug resistant phenotypes. The methanol extracts of the plant were tested against a panel of 28 multidrug resistant Gram-negative bacterial strains. The bark extract of Canarium schweinfurthii recorded MIC values ranging from 32 to 1024 µg/mL against 85.7 % of the tested bacteria. Specifically, the lowest MIC value of 32 µg/mL was obtained with the Canarium schweinfurthii bark extract against the Klebsiella pneumoniae KP63 strain.
