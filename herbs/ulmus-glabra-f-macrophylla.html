Ulmus glabra f. macrophylla

Ulmus glabra f. macrophylla, also known as the Wych Elm, is a large tree species characterized by its broad, vase-shaped crown and long, serrated leaves.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antiviral properties, which can be attributed to its high concentration of tannins and flavonoids.

The health benefits of Ulmus glabra f. macrophylla include its ability to reduce fever, alleviate respiratory issues, and support the immune system, making it a valuable remedy for various health conditions.

The plant can be prepared as a decoction, infusion, or tincture, and is often used in combination with other herbs to enhance its medicinal properties.
