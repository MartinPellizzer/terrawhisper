Chinese mahogany

Phellodendron chinense

Chinese Mahogany (Phellodendron chinense) is a deciduous tree native to China, known for its bark and roots that have been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries to treat skin conditions, inflammation, and infections.

Taxonomy

Chinese Mahogany (Phellodendron chinense) is a species belonging to the genus Phellodendron in the family Rutaceae, classified under the order Sapindales, class Eudicots, division Magnoliophyta, and kingdom Plantae.

Morphology

Chinese Mahogany (Phellodendron chinense) is a deciduous tree characterized by its rough, fissured bark that is dark brown to black in color, with a distinctive yellowish-white inner bark, and it has opposite, simple, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually 5-12 cm long.

Distribution

Chinese Mahogany (Phellodendron chinense) is native to eastern China, specifically found in regions such as Zhejiang, Jiangsu, Anhui, and Fujian, with its natural habitat primarily in temperate forest areas and mountainous regions.

Traditional Uses

Chinese Mahogany (Phellodendron chinense) has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine to clear heat, detoxify the body, and treat conditions such as fever, skin infections, and inflammatory disorders.

Medicinal Systems

Chinese Mahogany (Phellodendron chinense) has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine, Ayurveda, and indigenous Southeast Asian healing practices for its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and detoxifying properties.

Phytochemistry

The phytochemistry of Chinese Mahogany (Phellodendron chinense) is characterized by the presence of alkaloids, particularly berberine, which is known for its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antidiabetic properties, as well as other bioactive compounds such as phellodendrin, phellodendronin, and various flavonoids.

Pharmacology

Chinese Mahogany (Phellodendron chinense) exhibits pharmacological properties characterized by anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antipyretic effects, primarily due to its ability to modulate immune responses and inhibit the proliferation of pathogenic microorganisms.

Botanical Identification

Chinese Mahogany, scientifically known as Phellodendron chinense, belongs to the family Rutaceae. It is also called Huang Huang in Chinese and is a deciduous shrub or small tree. Native to eastern Asia, it has yellow bark, compound leaves, and yellow flowers. It is sometimes referred to as Phellodendron amurense in older herbal texts.

Decoction: Used for treating fever, inflammation, and digestive issues.

Safety Profile

Chinese Mahogany is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. Long-term use or high doses could lead to liver toxicity, so caution is advised, especially for those with pre-existing liver conditions.
