Chinese mahogany

Phellodendron chinense

Chinese Mahogany (Phellodendron chinense) is a deciduous tree native to China, known for its bark and roots that have been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries to treat skin conditions, inflammation, and infections.

Taxonomy

Chinese Mahogany (Phellodendron chinense) is a species belonging to the genus Phellodendron in the family Rutaceae, classified under the order Sapindales, class Eudicots, division Magnoliophyta, and kingdom Plantae.

Morphology

Chinese Mahogany (Phellodendron chinense) is a deciduous tree characterized by its rough, fissured bark that is dark brown to black in color, with a distinctive yellowish-white inner bark, and it has opposite, simple, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually 5-12 cm long.

Distribution

Chinese Mahogany (Phellodendron chinense) is native to eastern China, specifically found in regions such as Zhejiang, Jiangsu, Anhui, and Fujian, with its natural habitat primarily in temperate forest areas and mountainous regions.

Traditional Uses

Chinese Mahogany (Phellodendron chinense) has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine to clear heat, detoxify the body, and treat conditions such as fever, skin infections, and inflammatory disorders.

Medicinal Systems

Chinese Mahogany (Phellodendron chinense) has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine, Ayurveda, and indigenous Southeast Asian healing practices for its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and detoxifying properties.

Phytochemistry

The phytochemistry of Chinese Mahogany (Phellodendron chinense) is characterized by the presence of alkaloids, particularly berberine, which is known for its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antidiabetic properties, as well as other bioactive compounds such as phellodendrin, phellodendronin, and various flavonoids.

Pharmacology

Chinese Mahogany (Phellodendron chinense) exhibits pharmacological properties characterized by anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antipyretic effects, primarily due to its ability to modulate immune responses and inhibit the proliferation of pathogenic microorganisms.

Medicinal Uses

Chinese mahogany is used to treat conditions such as fever, inflammation, skin infections, diarrhea, and digestive disorders.

Preparations

Chinese mahogany is used to prepare decoctions, tinctures, and powders from its bark and roots, which are then utilized in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

Safety

When used in excess or improperly, Chinese Mahogany (Phellodendron chinense) may cause gastrointestinal discomfort, such as nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, and prolonged use may lead to liver toxicity.

Evidence

Clinical evidence for Chinese Mahogany (Phellodendron chinense) primarily supports its use in traditional Chinese medicine for conditions such as inflammation, skin disorders, and diabetes, with some studies suggesting anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and hypoglycemic effects, though more rigorous clinical trials are needed to confirm these findings.

Cultivation

Chinese Mahogany (Phellodendron chinense) is typically cultivated in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil, often in shaded or semi-shaded conditions, and requires regular watering and pruning to promote healthy growth and bark production.

Processing

Processing Chinese Mahogany involves soaking the dried bark in water for several hours to soften it, followed by slicing it into thin strips and drying it in the shade to preserve its medicinal properties.

Identification

Chinese Mahogany (Phellodendron chinense) is a deciduous tree native to China, characterized by its smooth, light grey bark that peels in thin, flaky layers, dark green elliptical leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside, and yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months.

Sustainability

The sustainability of Chinese Mahogany (Phellodendron chinense) as a resource for herbal medicine is a critical concern due to its slow growth rate, limited natural distribution, and increasing demand, which has led to overharvesting and habitat degradation in many regions.

Related

Phellodendron chinense is closely related to other species within the genus Phellodendron, including Phellodendron amurense, Phellodendron trichocarpum, and Phellodendron hexandrum, which share similar morphological and chemical characteristics commonly used in traditional herbal medicine.
