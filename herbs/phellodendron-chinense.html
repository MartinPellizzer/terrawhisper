Chinese Mahogany (Phellodendron chinense)

Chinese Mahogany (Phellodendron chinense) is a member of the Rutaceae family, native to Southeast Asia, China, and Indonesia. Traditionally, its bark, roots, and rhizomes have been used for decoctions, powders, and infusions.

This herb is particularly valued for its bitter, anti-inflammatory, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in traditional chinese medicine, japanese kampo medicine, and korean traditional medicine.

Perennial herbaceous plant.

Leaves
Lateral veins form prominent reticulate patterns on the upper surface, while the lower surface exhibits a distinct stomatal band along the midrib.
Flowers
Flowers are yellowish-green with red staminodes, arranged in racemes, actinomorphic, and have four petals.
Stems
Elongated, woody stems with a smooth surface, exhibiting a branching pattern of opposite and alternate shoots, characterized by the presence of prominent lenticels and a distinctive bark texture with fissures and ridges.
