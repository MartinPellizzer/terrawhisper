Phellodendron chinense

What's the taxonomical classification of Phellodendron chinense?

Phelloidenron chinense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Sapindales and the family Rutaaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by the genus Phelloidenron and the species chinense.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Phelodenron chinense contains various bioactive alkaloids and polyphenolic compounds, most notably those belonging to the isoquinoline class, which provide its primary therapeutic effects. These specific chemical constituents are studied for their potent antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Some researchers focus on the plant's ability to inhibit certain enzyme activities through these natural molecules. Additionally, the presence of diverse secondary metabolites suggests potential applications in treating infectious diseases. Such chemical complexity makes the plant a significant subject in ethnopharmacological research.
