10 Possible Side Effects Of Asclepias Syriaca

Asclepias syriaca has some side effects when used improperly, such as allergic reactions, skin irritation, and gastrointestinal issues.

These symptoms can occur due to excessive dosage, individual sensitivities, or incorrect preparation.

In severe cases, they can cause discomfort, fever, and even anaphylaxis, potentially leading to missed work, social isolation, and decreased quality of life.

1. Irritates skin causing dermatitis

Asclepias syriaca irritates skin causing dermatitis.

This is due to its milky sap and alkaloids, which can cause an allergic reaction in some individuals. The sap contains irritating compounds that can penetrate the skin, leading to inflammation, redness, and itching. Additionally, the plant's hairs and fibers can cause mechanical irritation, exacerbating the dermatitis.

As a result, people may experience discomfort, swelling, and potentially even blistering or rashes after coming into contact with Asclepias syriaca.

2. Causes allergic reactions

Asclepias syriaca causes allergic reactions.

The milky sap of the plant contains toxic compounds, including cardiac glycosides and latex, which can trigger an immune response in some individuals.

These substances can cause skin irritation, hives, and even life-threatening anaphylaxis in severe cases.

3. Triggers respiratory distress

Asclepias syriaca triggers respiratory distress due to its high concentration of cardiac glycosides and saponins.

These compounds can cause constriction of the airways, leading to bronchospasm and increased mucus production, which may exacerbate conditions such as asthma or chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).

Additionally, the plant's sap has been shown to contain allergenic proteins that can stimulate an allergic response in some individuals.

4. Induces nausea and vomiting

Asclepias syriaca induces nausea and vomiting.

The compound cardenolides present in the plant are responsible for this side effect, which can be attributed to their ability to interact with the digestive system and disrupt normal gut function. As the body attempts to eliminate these toxic compounds, it can lead to feelings of discomfort, nausea, and vomiting.

The severity of these symptoms varies depending on the individual's sensitivity and dose of Asclepias syriaca consumed.

5. Enhances cardiac arrhythmia

Asclepias syriaca enhances cardiac arrhythmia due to its ability to stimulate the autonomic nervous system.

The plant's cardiac glycosides, specifically cardenolides and bufodienolides, increase the heart's electrical activity by altering potassium channels and prolonging the QT interval.

This can lead to irregular heartbeat patterns, including tachycardia and bradycardia, which may be exacerbated in individuals with pre-existing cardiac conditions or electrolyte imbalances.

6. Increases blood pressure

Asclepias syriaca increases blood pressure due to its high concentration of glycosides and cardenolides, which can stimulate the heart and cardiovascular system.

The plant's alkaloids, such as asclepin and syringin, may also contribute to this effect by influencing blood vessel tone and cardiac output.

As a result, individuals using Asclepias syriaca may experience an increase in their blood pressure levels.

7. Aggravates existing allergies

Asclepias syriaca aggravates existing allergies due to its high content of latex and alkaloids.

These compounds can trigger an allergic reaction in individuals with pre-existing sensitivities, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.

Additionally, the plant's volatile oils may cause irritation and inflammation in the respiratory system, further exacerbating allergy symptoms.

8. Inflames mucous membranes

Asclepias syriaca inflames mucous membranes due to its irritant properties and the presence of cardenolides, a type of glycoside that can stimulate mucus production.

The sap of the plant contains these compounds, which can cause irritation and inflammation in the eyes, nose, throat, and lungs when ingested or inhaled, leading to discomfort and potentially serious health issues.

9. Causes photosensitivity rash

Asclepias syriaca causes photosensitivity rash in some individuals.

This is due to the presence of a chemical called cardenolides, which is responsible for the plant's toxic properties. When ingested or applied topically, these compounds can cause skin irritation and increased sensitivity to sunlight, leading to rashes and other skin problems.

The severity of the reaction varies depending on individual tolerance and exposure levels.

10. Produces gastrointestinal upset

Asclepias syriaca produces gastrointestinal upset.

The sap of this plant contains a toxic compound called cardenolides, which can cause nausea and vomiting when ingested. Additionally, the plant's milky latex can irritate the mucous membranes in the digestive tract, leading to stomach pain, diarrhea, and bloating.

These symptoms may occur due to the body's attempt to eliminate the toxic compounds from the system, resulting in discomfort and distress.
