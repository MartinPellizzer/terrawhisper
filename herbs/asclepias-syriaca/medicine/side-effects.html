10 Possible Side Effects Of Asclepias Syriaca (Milkweed)

Asclepias syriaca has some side effects when used improperly, such as allergic reactions, skin irritation, and gastrointestinal issues.

These symptoms can occur due to excessive dosage, individual sensitivities, or incorrect preparation.

In severe cases, they can cause discomfort, fever, and even anaphylaxis, potentially leading to missed work, social isolation, and decreased quality of life.

1. Worsens inflammatory conditions

Asclepias syriaca irritates skin causing dermatitis.

This is due to its milky sap and alkaloids, which can cause an allergic reaction in some individuals. The sap contains irritating compounds that can penetrate the skin, leading to inflammation, redness, and itching. Additionally, the plant's hairs and fibers can cause mechanical irritation, exacerbating the dermatitis.

As a result, people may experience discomfort, swelling, and potentially even blistering or rashes after coming into contact with Asclepias syriaca.
