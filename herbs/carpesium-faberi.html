What's the taxonomical classification of Carpesium faberi?

Carpesium faberi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. This plant is a representative of the family Asteraceae, and its specific taxonomic designation is defined by the genus Carpesium and the species faberi.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Carpesium faberi has an upright, herbaceous stem structure characterized by elongated, lanceolate leaves with serrated margins and a prominent central vein. Its reproductive organs consist of small, actinomorphic flowers that emerge from the axillary nodes along the primary stem. The root system is fibrous and shallow, allowing for efficient nutrient uptake in its native soil environments.     What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

    This plant contains a group of bioactive sesquiterpene lactones known as carpespenes, which specifically include carpespene A and the related carpespene A-J series. These specialized medicinal compounds are primarily identified within the chemical profile of the plant's various extracts. Research suggests that the diverse structures within the carpespene A-J group may contribute to unique pharmacological properties. Scientists study these specific molecules to better understand their potential therapeutic applications in medicine. Each compound in the series offers a slightly different chemical framework for biochemical analysis.

    As discussed in a study, Carpesium faberi contains ten previously undescribed sesquiterpenes named carpespenes A-J (1-10) and eight known compounds (11-18). Carpespene A (1) is a eudesmanolide-type sesquiterpene lactone that demonstrated significant cytotoxic effects against four cancer cell lines. This compound showed IC50 values ranging from 8.20 to 18.45 μM. Mechanistically, compound 1 induced apoptosis in HepG2 cells by triggering excessive ROS accumulation. The study also found that simultaneous autophagy inhibition with compound 1 treatment augmented its cytotoxic effects on HepG2 cells.
