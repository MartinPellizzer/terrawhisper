Dracocephalum tanguticum

What's the taxonomical classification of Dracocephalum tanguticum?

Dracocephalum tanguticum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized under the order Lamiales. This plant is a member of the family Lamiaceae, which places it within the genus Dracocephalum, specifically identified as the species tanguticum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dracocephalum tangutium has a perennial growth habit characterized by erect, square-shaped stems that reach heights of approximately 20 to 40 centimeters. The leaves are oppositely arranged, appearing primarily lanceolate to linear in shape with finely serrated margins. Small, tubular flowers emerge from dense, terminal spikes, typically displaying colors ranging from pale blue to deep violet. These blossoms are nestled within bracts that often feature distinct purplish pigmentation. The overall structure of the plant is compact, making it well-suited for rocky or alpine environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of central and western China, specifically within the provinces of Gansu, Qinghai, Sichuan, and Tibet. It thrives primarily in alpine meadows and rocky slopes located at elevations ranging from 2,500 to 4,000 meters. The species is adapted to the harsh, cool climates characteristic of the Tibetan Plateau and surrounding mountainous terrains. Its distribution is restricted to these specific montane ecosystems where moisture and soil composition meet its specialized needs. Consequently, it is not found in lowland or tropical environments due to its evolutionary reliance on high-altitude habitats.

    What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

    This plant contains the medicinal compounds betulon, betulinic acid, oleanolic acid, rosmarinic acid, and ursolic acid. These specific pentacyclic triterpenoids and phenolic acids contribute to a wide range of biological activities. Betulin and betulinic acid are often recognized for their potential anti-inflammatory and anticancer properties. The presence of oleanolic acid and ursolic acid further enhances the plant's therapeutic profile through various metabolic pathways. Additionally, rosmarinic acid provides potent antioxidant benefits that protect cells from oxidative stress.

    As discussed in a study, Dracocephalum tanguticum contains 7 active components that can be simultaneously determined. Samples from Huangzhong county, Qinghai province, showed the highest content of p-hydroxybenzoic acid, cosmosiin, rosmarinic acid, oleanolic acid, and ursolic acid at 9.29, 12.14, 6.02, 3.11, and 17.67 mg·g-1 respectively.
