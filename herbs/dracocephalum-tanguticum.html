Dracocephalum tanguticum

What's the taxonomical classification of Dracocephalum tanguticum?

Dracocephalum tanguticum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized under the order Lamiales. This plant is a member of the family Lamiaceae, which places it within the genus Dracocephalum, specifically identified as the species tanguticum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dracocephalum tangutium has a perennial growth habit characterized by erect, square-shaped stems that reach heights of approximately 20 to 40 centimeters. The leaves are oppositely arranged, appearing primarily lanceolate to linear in shape with finely serrated margins. Small, tubular flowers emerge from dense, terminal spikes, typically displaying colors ranging from pale blue to deep violet. These blossoms are nestled within bracts that often feature distinct purplish pigmentation. The overall structure of the plant is compact, making it well-suited for rocky or alpine environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of central and western China, specifically within the provinces of Gansu, Qinghai, Sichuan, and Tibet. It thrives primarily in alpine meadows and rocky slopes located at elevations ranging from 2,500 to 4,000 meters. The species is adapted to the harsh, cool climates characteristic of the Tibetan Plateau and surrounding mountainous terrains. Its distribution is restricted to these specific montane ecosystems where moisture and soil composition meet its specialized needs. Consequently, it is not found in lowland or tropical environments due to its evolutionary reliance on high-altitude habitats.

What are the pharmacological activities of Dracocephalum tanguticum?

This plant has anti-fungal, anti-hyperglycemic, and hepatoprotective pharmacological activities that contribute to its medicinal value. The anti-fungal properties allow it to inhibit the growth of various pathogenic fungal strains. Its anti-hyperglycemic effects assist in regulating blood glucose levels, making it a subject of interest for diabetes research. Furthermore, the plant exhibits hepatoprotective qualities that help shield liver cells from chemical or toxic damage. These diverse therapeutic actions highlight the potential of Dracocephalum tanguticum in modern drug development.

According to a study published by "Natural product research", three new phenylacetamide glycosides (1-3) were isolated from whole plants of Dracocephalum tanguticum. These compounds (1-3) were evaluated for their anti-hyperglycemic and anti-fungal activities. The results revealed that all three compounds showed moderate anti-hyperglycemic activity. Specifically, at a final concentration of 25 μM, the 3T3-L1 adipocytes glucose consumption rates were 20.80 ± 1.47%, 21.48 ± 2.44%, and 21.57 ± 1.35%, respectively. However, none of the compounds showed obvious inhibitory activity against Candida albicans.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds betulinic acid, rosmarinic acid, ursolic acid, oleanolic acid, and cosmosiin. These bioactive molecules are known for providing significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties to the organism. Specifically, the triterpenoids like oleanolic and ursolic acid are studied for their potential roles in cellular protection. Additionally, rosmarinic acid contributes to the plant's ability to mitigate oxidative stress. The presence of cosmosiin further enhances the diverse pharmacological profile of this species.

According to a study published by "Zhongguo Zhong yao za zhi = Zhongguo zhongyao zazhi = China journal of Chinese materia medica", Dracocephalum tanguticum contains 7 active components. Samples from Huangzhong county, Qinghai province, contained 9.29 mg/g of p-hydroxybenzoic acid, 12.14 mg/g of cosmosiin, 6.02 mg/g of rosmarinic acid, 3.11 mg/g of oleanolic acid, and 17.67 mg/g of ursolic acid. Samples from Chaya county, Tibet autonomous region, contained 15.53 mg/g of betulin and 7.17 mg/g of betulinic acid. These functional components vary in content across different producing areas.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating autoimmune hepatitis, immune liver damage, and immune liver injury. It contains specific bioactive compounds that help regulate the body's inflammatory responses. These properties assist in protecting liver cells from being attacked by the host's own immune system. By modulating immune activity, the plant may help reduce the severity of chronic hepatic inflammation. Researchers study these effects to understand how the herb can mitigate progressive liver dysfunction.

According to a study published by "Food science & nutrition", Dracocephalum tanguticum is a medicinal and edible plant used for its hepatoprotective effects. The plant's crude polysaccharides, specifically DrTP80, are studied for their ability to alleviate immune liver damage and autoimmune hepatitis (AIH) induced by ConA. DrTP80 can significantly reduce AST and ALT levels in injured livers and ALM12 cells while improving hepatopathological changes. The mechanism involves regulating TNF and IL-17 signaling pathways, which include 13 differentially expressed genes such as Vcam1, Atf6b, Akt1, Irf1, Map2k3, Lcn2, Hsp90ab1, Anapc5, Traf4, Fosl1, Jun, Cxcl5, and Nfκbia. Consequently, DrTP80 could be used as a healthy food supplement for the treatment of immune liver injury.
