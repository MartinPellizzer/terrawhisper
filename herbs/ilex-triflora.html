Ilex triflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Ilex triflora?

Ilex triflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and belongs to the order Aquifoliales. This plant is a member of the family Aquifoliaceae, which contains the genus Ilex, and is specifically identified as the species triflora.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ilex triflora has evergreen, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical or lanceolate in shape with finely serrated margins and a glossy dark green upper surface. The plant develops small, inconspicuous white flowers that emerge in clusters from the leaf axils. Its woody stems transition from green in younger growth to a brownish bark as the shrub matures. The fruit consists of small, globose drupes that change color from green to a dark red or black upon ripening. These structural features collectively define its appearance as a dense, compact shrub.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Ilex triflora targets inflammatory enzymes such as cyclooxygenase-2 and lipoxygenase, alongside various pro-inflammatory cytokines and signaling proteins involved in oxidative stress pathways. What are the therapeutic actions of Ilex triflora?

Ilex triflora has medicinal properties that act as an astringent, diuretic, hemostatic, anti-inflammatory, and antipyretic agent to treat various ailments. Its astringent qualities allow it to tighten tissues and reduce secretions in inflamed areas. The plant also functions as a diuretic to promote fluid excretion and as a hemostatic to help control bleeding. Furthermore, its anti-inflammatory effects work to reduce swelling and pain throughout the body. Finally, its antipyretic action provides relief by effectively lowering elevated body temperatures during a fever.
