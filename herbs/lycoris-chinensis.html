What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lycoris chinensis has strap-shaped, bright green leaves that emerge from underground bulbs and slender, leafless flower stalks that can reach heights of approximately 30 to 40 centimeters. The leaves are linear in shape and typically appear during a different growing season than the flowering period. Its most striking feature is the terminal cluster of funnel-shaped flowers, which are often pink or white and possess long, protruding stamens. These blooms emerge on scape-like stems that rise directly from the bulb without supporting foliage. The plant's root system consists of fleshy, tunicated bulbs that store nutrients for the plant's seasonal life cycle.
