Senna montana

Senna montana, commonly known as Bitter Bush or Wild Senna, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and Mexico, belonging to the legume family.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering various health benefits such as treating digestive issues, including constipation and diarrhea, as well as its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The therapeutic actions of Senna montana include its ability to stimulate digestion, reduce fever, and soothe skin irritations, making it a valuable remedy in traditional medicine.

The bioactive constituents of the plant include anthraquinones, glycosides, and flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties and potential health benefits.
