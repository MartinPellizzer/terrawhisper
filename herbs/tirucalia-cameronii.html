Tirucalia cameronii

Tirucalia cameronii is a plant species native to Southeast Asia, commonly known as the "Snake Vine" or "Snake Root", which belongs to the family Apocynaceae.

This plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine, and its medicinal properties include anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The health benefits of Tirucalia cameronii include the potential treatment of various conditions such as fever, rheumatism, and snake bites, due to its analgesic and antivenom properties.

Traditionally, the plant is prepared as a decoction, tincture, or infusion for herbal medicine, and its extracts are also used in modern pharmacological studies.
