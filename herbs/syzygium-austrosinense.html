What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Syzygium austrosinense has small, elliptical leaves that are oppositely arranged on woody stems, typically measuring between 2 and 5 centimeters in length. Following pollination, the plant produces small, fleshy drupes that transition in color as they reach maturity.
