Flueggea acicularis

What's the taxonomical classification of Flueggea acicularis?

Flueggea acicularis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows a distinct evolutionary lineage within the order Malpighiales. This species is further categorized under the family Phyllanthaceae, which places it in the genus Flueggea, eventually reaching its specific taxonomic designation as the species acicularis.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Flueggea acicularis can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or gastrointestinal distress if handled improperly or ingested. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, the plant contains chemical compounds that may trigger localized dermatitis upon contact. Some individuals might experience redness, itching, or swelling if the sap touches sensitive areas of the body. Accidental ingestion could lead to more severe symptoms such as nausea or stomach cramping. It is essential to use caution and avoid direct contact with the plant to prevent these adverse reactions.
