What is Eulophia picta?

Eulophia picta is a species of orchid that belongs to the family Orchidaceae.

It is a terrestrial orchid with pseudobulbs, which are fleshy, underground stems that store water and nutrients. The pseudobulbs are crowned with a single, apical leaf that is ovate in shape and has a distinctive, red-striped pattern. The inflorescence, or flowering stem, is a raceme that bears 2-4 flowers with a yellow-green and purple-brown coloration, and a labellum that is similar in shape to the petals but has a distinctive, wavy edge.

The plant is native to tropical Africa and is typically found in shaded, humid areas with rich soil.
