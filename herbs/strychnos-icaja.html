Strychnos icaja

What's the taxonomical classification of Strychnos icaja?

Strychnos icaja belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Gentianaales. The plant is a representative of the family Loganiaceae and is specifically categorized under the genus Strychnos, with its unique designation being the species icaja.

What are the pharmacological activities of Strychnos icaja?

This plant has anti-malarial, anti-plasmodial, and cytotoxic pharmacological activities derived from its complex alkaloid profile. The presence of specific indole alkaloids allows the species to combat various strains of malaria-causing parasites. These anti-plasmodial properties make it a significant subject for studying treatments against Plasmodium falciparum. Furthermore, the plant exhibits potent cytotoxic effects that can inhibit the growth of certain malignant cell lines. Researchers continue to investigate these bioactive compounds to develop new therapeutic agents for infectious and neoplastic diseases.

As discussed in a study, Strychnos icaja roots bark displays very high in vitro antiplasmodial activity. The methanolic (MeOH) and dichloromethane (CH2Cl2) extracts of this plant demonstrated an IC50 value of less than 3 microg/ml. This specific plant is recognized as having a very interesting antiplasmodial activity. The study utilized the measurement of lactate dehydrogenase activity to evaluate growth inhibition of Plasmodium falciparum strains, including the 3D7 and W2 strains.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds 18-hydroxy-isosungucine, 18-hydroxy-sungucine, isosungucine, sungucine, and vomicine. These specific alkaloids are often studied for their various biological activities and potential therapeutic properties. The chemical structure of these molecules plays a crucial role in how they interact with human cellular systems. Researchers analyze these constituents to better understand the plant's overall pharmacological profile. Such detailed chemical identification is essential for developing new treatments based on natural sources.

As discussed in a study, reinvestigation of Strychnos icaja Baillon resulted in the isolation of vomicine, isostrychnine, and 3 new sungucine derivatives named isosungucine (8), 18-hydroxy-sungucine (9), and 18-hydroxy-isosungucine (10). These compounds were identified using detailed spectroscopic methods, including a complete 1H- and 13C-NMR study of sungucine. Some of these medicinal compounds were highly active against Plasmodium falciparum in vitro, specifically against the chloroquine-resistant strain. Compound 10 demonstrated selective antiplasmodial activity with > 100-fold greater toxicity towards Plasmodium falciparum compared to cultured human cancer cells such as KB and HeLa lines or WI38 fibroblasts.
