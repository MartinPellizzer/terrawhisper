What's the taxonomical classification of Strychnos icaja?

Strychnos icaja belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Gentianaales. The plant is a representative of the family Loganiaceae and is specifically categorized under the genus Strychnos, with its unique designation being the species icaja.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds 18-hydroxy-isosungucine, 18-hydroxy-sungucine, isosungucine, sungucine, and vomicine. These specific chemical constituents are categorized as neolignans and are often studied for their biological activities. The presence of these molecules contributes to the overall therapeutic profile of the species. Researchers examine these compounds to understand their potential roles in pharmacology. Each substance possesses unique structural properties that may influence its interaction with biological systems.

As discussed in a study, reinvestigation of Strychnos icaja Baillon resulted in the isolation of vomicine, isostrychnine, and three new sungucine derivatives named isosungucine (8), 18-hydroxy-sungucine (9), and 18-hydroxy-isosungucine (10). Detailed spectroscopic methods and a complete 1H- and 13C-NMR study of sungucine were used to identify these compounds. Some of these substances were highly active against Plasmodium falciparum in vitro, specifically targeting the chloroquine-resistant strain. Compound 10 demonstrated selective antiplasmodial activity with > 100-fold greater toxicity towards Plasmodium falciparum relative to cultured human cancer cells such as KB and HeLa lines or WI38 fibroblasts.
