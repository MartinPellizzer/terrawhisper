What's the taxonomical classification of Phyllanthus ussuriensis?

Phyllanthus ussuriensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Malpighiales and the family Phyllanthaceae, which eventually leads to its specific identification within the genus Phyllanthus. Ultimately, the plant is defined at the species level as Phyllanthus ussuriensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phyllanthus ussuriensis has woody stems with small, elliptical leaves that are arranged in a distichous pattern along the branches to mimic pinnate compound leaves. The foliage is typically bright green and features entire margins with acute or obtuse apices. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often appearing in clusters or singly. These flowers eventually produce small, globose capsules that contain several tiny seeds. The overall growth habit is that of a spreading shrub or small tree with a fine, textured appearance.
