Bletilla sinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Bletilla sinensis?

Blettilla sinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is organized under the order Asparagales and is a member of the family Orchidaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Blettilla, and its unique species designation, sinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bletilla sinensis has an underground rhizome with lanceolate, leathery leaves that grow in a basal rosette and flowers consisting of six tepals with a distinct central blotch. The foliage is typically dark green and displays a parallel venation pattern characteristic of monocots. Each flowering stem rises from the center of the leaves to support a cluster of blossoms. The petals are often marked with intricate patterns of yellow, white, or reddish-brown to attract pollinators. Small, capsule-like fruits eventually develop from the withered flowers to contain the plant's seeds.
