Kummerowia striata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. What are the pharmacological activities of Kummerowia striata?

This plant has pharmacological activities that include acting as an IL-5 inhibitor to modulate immune responses. By suppressing the production or activity of interleukin-5, the plant can potentially reduce eosinophil activation in the body. This mechanism makes it a subject of interest for treating inflammatory conditions such as asthma and allergic diseases. Research into its chemical constituents aims to identify the specific molecules responsible for these biological effects. Further studies are necessary to fully understand the therapeutic potential of its bioactive compounds.

According to a study published by "Planta medica", the plant Kummerowia striata Thunb. contains luteolin 4'-O-glucopyranoside, which acts as an IL-5 inhibitor. This flavone compound shows a dose-dependent inhibitory effect on IL-5 bioactivity with 95% inhibition at 30 microM, 79% at 15 microM, 60% at 7.5 microM, 54% at 3.8 microM, and 29% at 1.9 microM. The 50% inhibition (IC50) value for this compound was recorded at a concentration of 3.7 microM. These results demonstrate the specific pharmacological potency of the identified inhibitor from Kummerowia striata.
