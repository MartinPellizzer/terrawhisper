Crotalaria retusa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Crotalaria retusa has upright, branched stems with alternate, oblong to lanceolate leaves that feature distinctively shallowly lobed or retuse margins. The foliage is typically covered in fine, soft hairs that give the surface a slightly pubescent texture. Its inflorescences consist of small, bright yellow flowers that emerge from the leaf axils in clusters. The fruit is a characteristic legume, appearing as a compressed, bladder-like pod that rattles when dried. These pods contain several small, hard seeds that are essential for the plant's reproductive cycle.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Crotalaria retusa targets various inflammatory enzymes including cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2), lipoxygenase (LOX), and inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS) to mediate its biological activities. These interactions primarily inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory mediators like prostaglandins and nitric oxide, which helps reduce systemic inflammation. The plant also contains pyrrolizidine alkaloids that can interact with hepatic proteins and cytochrome P450 enzymes during metabolic processes. Furthermore, certain bioactive compounds within the species show potential for modulating cytokine signaling pathways and oxidative stress markers. These multi-target protein interactions underlie the plant's documented pharmacological potential in treating inflammatory and oxidative conditions.

Crotalaria retusa has therapeutic actions that include diuretic, emetic, purgative, anti-inflammatory, and astringent properties. What are the herbal preparations of Crotalaria retusa?

Crotalaria retusa is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste using its various plant parts such as leaves, roots, and seeds. The leaves and roots are often boiled in water to create a medicinal tea for internal consumption. When used for skin ailments, the fresh plant material is crushed into a fine paste and applied directly to the affected area. Some traditional practices also involve soaking the dried seeds to extract bioactive compounds. These diverse preparations allow the plant to be utilized for both systemic and localized treatments.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Crotalaria retusa can cause severe liver damage and neurological impairment due to the presence of pyrrolizidine alkaloids in its tissues. These toxic compounds are known to induce hepatic veno-occlusive disease, which restricts blood flow in the liver. Ingesting the plant can lead to progressive liver fibrosis and potentially fatal organ failure. Additionally, the alkaloids may cause systemic toxicity that affects the central nervous system. Symptoms of poisoning often include vomiting, abdominal pain, and jaundice.
