What's the taxonomical classification of Hydrangea hypoglauca?

Hydrangea hypoglauca belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is classified under the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Cornales. This plant is a member of the family Hydrangeaceae and is specifically identified within the genus Hydrangea, ultimately reaching its unique classification at the species level as Hydrangea hypoglauca.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hydrangea hypoglauca has elliptical to ovate leaves that are characterized by a distinct lack of glaucousness on their undersides compared to similar species.

These leaves are arranged oppositely along woody stems that support the plant's structural framework. The plant's overall morphology supports the production of large, clustered inflorescences composed of numerous small flowers.
