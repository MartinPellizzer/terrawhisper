Calocedrus macrolepis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Calocedrus macrolepis has a woody, shrub-like habit characterized by small, scale-like leaves that are densely arranged along its spreading, multi-stemmed branches. The foliage is typically greyish-green in color and possesses a fine, textured appearance due to the overlapping nature of the individual leaf scales. These plants often exhibit a highly branched architecture that creates a compact and bushy growth form. The stems are somewhat rigid and can become quite woody as the plant matures over time. What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the high-altitude regions of the Western Ghats in southwestern India. It is specifically found in the shola ecosystems located within the Nilgiri Hills and the Anaimalai Hills. These populations are restricted to montane grasslands and stunted evergreen forests situated at elevations typically exceeding 1,500 meters. The species occupies a very narrow ecological niche defined by cool, moist climates and unique soil conditions. Because of this limited range, the plant is considered a rare specialist of the Indian subcontinent's southern mountain ranges.
