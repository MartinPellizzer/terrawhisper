Calocedrus macrolepis

What's the taxonomical classification of Calocedrus macrolepis?

Calocedrus macrolepis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Pinidae and the order Pinales. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the family Cupressaceae, which contains the genus Calocedrus, eventually reaching its specific designation as the species macrolepis.

    What diseases this plant treats?

    This plant is used to treat skin infections, topical wounds, and various inflammatory dermatological conditions. The extract derived from its bark and leaves contains bioactive compounds that possess significant antimicrobial properties. These chemical constituents help combat bacterial and fungal pathogens that colonize the skin. When applied topically, the plant aids in the acceleration of wound healing processes. Research suggests its efficacy in reducing localized swelling and irritation caused by environmental stressors.

    What are the herbal preparations of Calocedrus macrolepis?

    Calocedrus macrolepis is prepared primarily as a decoction or an infusion through the boiling or steeping of its dried fungal fruiting bodies in water. This method extracts the bioactive compounds necessary for its traditional medicinal applications. Some practitioners may also utilize the plant in the form of a concentrated tincture by soaking the material in alcohol. Additionally, topical applications such as poultices can be fashioned from the crushed fungal tissue to treat skin conditions. These diverse preparations allow for both internal consumption and external medicinal use.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Calocedrus macrolepis can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or respiratory distress if its dust or oils come into contact with sensitive areas. While extensive clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, contact with the plant's foliage may trigger redness or itching in some individuals. Inhalation of fine particles during pruning or handling could potentially irritate the lungs or nasal passages. It is advisable to wear gloves and protective eyewear when working with this species to minimize accidental exposure. Always consult a medical professional if an adverse reaction occurs after touching the plant.
