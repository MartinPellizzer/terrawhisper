Swertia bifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Swertia bifolia?

Swertia bifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further classified under the order Gentianaales. It is a member of the family Gentianaceae, specifically falling within the genus Swertia, and is identified by its unique species designation, bifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Swertia bifolia has opposite, decussate leaves that are typically elliptical to oblong in shape, measuring approximately 2 to 5 centimeters in length with entire margins. The leaf texture is often slightly coriaceous, and the apex can range from acute to obtuse. The plant features small, inconspicuous flowers that are borne in axillary clusters. Its stems are woody and can exhibit a somewhat quadrangular structure in younger growth. The overall habit of the shrub is characterized by its spreading branches and relatively compact foliage arrangement.
