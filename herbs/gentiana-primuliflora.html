Gentiana primuliflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Gentiana primuliflora?

Gentiana primuliflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical structure of botanical classification, it falls under the order Gentianaales and the family Gentianaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Gentiana, and its specific species designation, primuliflora.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gentiana primuliflora has lanceolate to ovate leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs along a stem, bearing deep blue, trumpet-shaped flowers with five fused petals. The foliage often exhibits a slightly hairy texture and is typically concentrated toward the base of the plant. Each flower features a prominent throat and long, slender corolla lobes that spread outward when fully bloomed. Small, rounded seed capsules develop following pollination to facilitate reproduction. This perennial herb maintains a relatively compact growth habit suitable for its alpine environment.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude alpine regions of the Rocky Mountains, spanning from southern Alberta in Canada down through the mountain ranges of Montana, Wyoming, Colorado, and New Mexico. It typically grows in subalpine and alpine tundra environments where rocky soil and moisture are abundant. Populations are often found in meadows or near mountain streams at elevations ranging from 8,000 to over 13,000 feet. Because it is restricted to these specific high-elevation niches, its distribution is highly fragmented across different mountain peaks. This narrow ecological requirement makes the species a specialist of the North American montane landscape.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?
