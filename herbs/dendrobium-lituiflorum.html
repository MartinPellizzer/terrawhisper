Dendrobium lituiflorum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dendrobium lituiflorum has pseudobulbs that are cylindrical, fleshy, and typically grow in an upright or arching manner from a creeping rhizome. These storage organs are covered by several thick, leathery leaves that are lanceolate in shape and arranged alternately along the stem. The inflorescence consists of small, fragrant flowers that emerge from the nodes of the mature pseudobulbs. Each flower features a complex labellum and delicate petals that are often pale in color with subtle markings. The overall structure is characteristic of epiphytic orchids adapted to forest environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, specifically spanning across parts of Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. It thrives primarily within humid, lowland rainforest environments where moisture levels remain consistently high. These orchids are typically found growing as epiphytes on the branches of trees in shaded forest canopies. Their distribution is closely tied to specific elevations and microclimates that support their delicate growth requirements. Because they rely on stable tropical weather patterns, their range is restricted to these equatorial zones.

      Dendrobium lituiflorum is prepared primarily as a dried decoction where the stems are boiled in water to extract active medicinal compounds.
