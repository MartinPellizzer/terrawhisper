Stephania kuinanensis has a woody, climbing habit characterized by a prominent, tuberous caudex that serves as a water storage organ. This central tuberous structure supports slender, twining stems that can extend significantly to reach light sources. The leaves are typically arranged in a way that maximizes surface area for photosynthesis within its forest habitat. Small, delicate flowers emerge from the foliage, often displaying subtle colors typical of the Menispermaceae family.
