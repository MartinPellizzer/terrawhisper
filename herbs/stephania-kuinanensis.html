Stephania kuinanensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Stephania kuinanensis?

Stephania kuinanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy places it in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, falling under the order Ranunculales. Within this lineage, the plant is a member of the family Menispermaceae and is situated in the genus Stephania, specifically identified as the species kuinanenensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stephania kuinanensis has a woody, climbing habit characterized by a prominent, tuberous caudex that serves as a water storage organ.

This central tuberous structure supports slender, twining stems that can extend significantly to reach light sources. The leaves are typically arranged in a way that maximizes surface area for photosynthesis within its forest habitat. Small, delicate flowers emerge from the foliage, often displaying subtle colors typical of the Menispermaceae family.
