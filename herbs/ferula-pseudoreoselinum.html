What is Ferula pseudoreoselinum?

Ferula pseudoreoselinum is a perennial plant in the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by a robust, woody base and a leafy stem that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, with feathery, compound leaves divided into linear leaflets. The inflorescence is a large, rounded umbel, composed of numerous small, yellowish flowers. Ferula pseudoreoselinum is endemic to the western part of the Himalayas, specifically in the Kashmir region.

Its rhizome, like many other members of the Apiaceae family, is used in traditional medicine for its resinous sap, which is rich in ferulic acid and other bioactive compounds.
