Halerpestes sarmentosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Halerpestes sarmentosa?

Halerpestes sarmentoosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Ranunculales and is a member of the family Ranunculaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Halerpestes and the species name sarmentoosa.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, stomach aches, and fever. Its leaves and stems contain bioactive compounds that possess antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Local communities often apply crushed parts of the plant directly to skin lesions to accelerate healing. Additionally, decoctions made from the plant are ingested to alleviate gastrointestinal distress and reduce body temperature. These traditional medicinal practices rely on the plant's natural chemical profile to manage various ailments.
