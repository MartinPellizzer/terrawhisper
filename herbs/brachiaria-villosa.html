Brachiaria villosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Brachiaria villosa?

Brachiaría villosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Poales. As a member of the family Poaceae, it is further defined by its genus, Brachiaría, and is specifically identified by its species name, villosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Brachiaria villosa has a perennial growth habit characterized by tufted, erect, or decumbent culms that typically reach heights of 0.5 to 1.5 meters. The leaves are linear to lanceolate with a prominent midrib and often possess a fine, pubescent texture along the margins and sheaths. Its inflorescence is a terminal panicle composed of several racemes that are generally digitately arranged or somewhat contracted. Each spikelet is small and contains multiple florets protected by specialized glumes and lemmas. The plant is further distinguished by its fine hairs, which provide a villous appearance to various vegetative structures.
