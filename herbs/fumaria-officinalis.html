Fumaria officinalis

What's the taxonomical classification of Fumaria officinalis?

Fumaria officinalis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical structure of biological classification, it falls under the order Ranunculales and is a member of the family Papaveraceae. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its genus, Fumaria, and its specific species name, officinalis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Fumaria officiinalis contains alkaloids such as protopine, somamine, and ficine, along with various flavonoids, tannins, and organic acids. These specific alkaloids are primarily responsible for the plant's pharmacological effects on the nervous and digestive systems. The presence of flavonoids contributes to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Additionally, the plant contains high amounts of vitamin C and essential minerals that support overall metabolic health. These diverse chemical constituents make it a subject of interest for traditional medicinal applications.

What are the therapeutic actions of Fumaria officinalis?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin conditions like eczema, psoriasis, and acne, as well as liver and gallbladder disorders such as jaundice and hepatitis. Its components are believed to support hepatic function by promoting bile flow. In traditional herbalism, it is also applied to soothe various dermatological inflammations and rashes. Some practitioners use it to address digestive issues related to bile secretion. These therapeutic properties stem from the plant's specific alkaloids and flavonoids.
