Pursh's buckthorn

Frangula purshiana

Pursh's Buckthorn (Frangula purshiana) is a deciduous shrub native to North America, known for its use in traditional medicine to treat digestive issues such as constipation and bowel irregularities.

Taxonomy

Pursh's Buckthorn (Frangula purshiana) is a species belonging to the genus Frangula in the family Rhamnaceae, which is part of the order Rhamnales, class Eudicots, division Magnoliophyta, and kingdom Plantae.

Morphology

Pursh's Buckthorn (Frangula purshiana) is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its smooth, dark brown to black bark, elliptical to ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into narrow, ribbed, yellowish-orange berries.

Distribution

Pursh's Buckthorn (Frangula purshiana) is native to the western United States, specifically found in regions spanning from British Columbia and Alberta in Canada down through the Pacific Northwest, including parts of Washington, Oregon, and California, and extending into northern Mexico.

Traditional Uses

Traditionally used in herbal medicine for its laxative properties, Pursh's Buckthorn (Frangula purshiana) has been employed to treat constipation and digestive ailments among Indigenous communities and early settlers in North America.

Medicinal Systems

In traditional medicinal systems such as Native American medicine, Pursh's Buckthorn (Frangula purshiana) has been historically used for its laxative properties and as a treatment for digestive ailments.

Phytochemistry

Pursh's Buckthorn (Frangula purshiana) contains a variety of bioactive compounds, including anthraquinones, lignans, and flavonoids, which contribute to its traditional use in herbal medicine.

Pharmacology

Pursh's Buckthorn (Frangula purshiana) exhibits purgative, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, making it traditionally used for digestive disorders, skin conditions, and infections.

Botanical Identification

Pursh's Buckthorn is scientifically known as Frangula purshiana, belonging to the Rutaceae family. It is also called Oregon grape or California buckthorn, and is a deciduous shrub native to western North America. The plant has yellow flowers, dark green leaves, and distinctive orange berries. It is sometimes referred to as "Oregon grape" in herbal texts.

Safety Profile

Pursh's Buckthorn is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to potential uterine stimulation and toxicity risks. It should be avoided by individuals with gastrointestinal ulcers or severe constipation. Long-term use may lead to electrolyte imbalances and should be monitored by a healthcare provider.
