Acer fabri

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Acer fabri has opposite, palmately lobed leaves with three to five distinct lobes and serrated margins. The foliage typically exhibits a deep green color during the growing season and can turn vibrant shades in autumn. Its woody stems are characterized by a greyish bark that becomes more textured as the tree matures. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into winged samaras used for seed dispersal. These specialized fruit structures allow the seeds to spin through the air to reach new locations.
