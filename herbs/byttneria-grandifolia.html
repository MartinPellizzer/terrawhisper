What's the taxonomical classification of Byttneria grandifolia?

Byttneria grandifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further organized under the order Malvales. It is a constituent of the family Malvaceae, falling under the genus Byttneria, and is specifically identified by its species name, grandifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Byttneria grandifolia has large, broadly ovate to elliptical leaves that typically measure between 10 and 25 centimeters in length and are characterized by an entire or slightly wavy margin. The leaf surfaces are often glabrous or sparsely pubescent, exhibiting prominent venation that extends from the central midrib to the edges. Its stems are woody and can develop a somewhat scrambling or climbing habit depending on the surrounding support. The inflorescences consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the axils of the leaves. These reproductive structures are typically arranged in clusters to facilitate pollination.
