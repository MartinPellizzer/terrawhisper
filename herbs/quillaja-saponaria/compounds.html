Soapbark Tree (Quillaja saponaria) Compounds

The primary compounds of Soapbark Tree include saponins, which are known for their foaming properties and potential anti-inflammatory effects. Flavonoids contribute to antioxidant activity, while terpenoids may have antimicrobial properties. Tannins and phenolic acids provide astringent and protective effects. Coumarin, glycosides, essential oils, alkaloids, and anthocyanins also play roles in the tree's medicinal and therapeutic properties.

Soapbark Tree (Quillaja saponaria) Monograph

Saponin is a key active compound in the Soapbark Tree (Quillaja saponaria).
