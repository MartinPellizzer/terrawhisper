Microlepia hancei

What's the taxonomical classification of Microlepia hancei?

Microlepia hancei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Polypodiinae and is organized within the order Polypodiales. Further taxonomic refinement places this plant in the family Dennstaedtiaceae, specifically within the genus Microlepia, which culminates in its unique designation as the species hancei.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Microlepia hancei has pinnate fronds composed of multiple pairs of narrow, lanceolate pinnae that are arranged along a central stipe.

The pinnules are typically small and densely packed, exhibiting a distinct texture due to the presence of fine hairs or scales. The margins of the segments are often entire or slightly serrated, contributing to the delicate appearance of the foliage. On the underside of the leaves, the sori are positioned in discrete rows near the margins, often protected by a specialized indusium.

These reproductive structures are characteristic of the genus and are essential for identifying the species within its habitat.
