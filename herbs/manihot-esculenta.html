Cassava (Manihot esculenta)

Cassava (Manihot esculenta) is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, native to Amazon Basin, South America, Central America, and Caribbean Region. Traditionally, its leaves, stems, and roots have been used for culinary uses, decoctions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, diuretic, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in african traditional medicine, traditional chinese medicine, and european herbal medicine.

Leaves
Simple leaves with upper surface glaucous green and lower surface dull green, bearing prominent stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Inflorescences composed of small yellowish-green flowers arranged in a spike, actinomorphic, with five sepals and five petals, each petal with a distinct midvein and a narrow apex.
Stems
Erect, woody, branching pattern opposite, surface characteristics hairy, presence of lenticels.
