Cassava

Manihot esculenta

Cassava (Manihot esculenta) is a tropical plant native to South America, widely cultivated for its starchy root, which is a staple food in many parts of the world. It is also used in traditional medicine to treat various ailments, including diabetes, inflammation, and digestive issues.

Botanical Identification

Cassava, scientifically known as Manihot esculenta, belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family. Commonly called manioc or yuca, it is a tropical perennial shrub native to South America. The plant features a tuberous root, alternate leaves, and small yellow flowers. It is also known by various regional names and has been referenced in herbal texts for its medicinal properties.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Magnoliopsida
  • Order: Malpighiales
  • Family: Euphorbiaceae
  • Genus: Manihot
  • Species: Manihot esculenta

Active Compounds

Cassava contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, saponins, and phenolic acids. These compounds work synergistically to provide its medicinal properties, influencing cellular processes and inflammatory responses. Their combined action enhances the herb's efficacy in traditional herbal medicine.

Cassava Uses.

Preparation Methods

Culinary use: Used for providing dietary carbohydrates and as a staple food in many cultures.

Decoction: Used for treating digestive issues and as a diuretic.

Extract: Used for managing diabetes due to its potential hypoglycemic properties.

Safety Profile

Cassava contains cyanogenic glycosides, which can release cyanide when consumed improperly. Proper processing is essential to remove toxins. It should be avoided by individuals with kidney disease or those on certain medications due to potential interactions.
