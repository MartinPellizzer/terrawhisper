It offers health benefits including supporting digestion, reducing inflammation, and potentially aiding in weight management.

Medicinal Parts

Manihot esculenta leaf is one of the most widely used medicinal parts of the cassava plant, known for its rich content of bioactive compounds such as polyphenols, flavonoids, and alkaloids, which exhibit antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties.

The leaves are commonly used in traditional medicine to treat ailments such as diabetes, hypertension, and gastrointestinal disorders due to their hypoglycemic and hepatoprotective effects. They are also valued for their ability to reduce fever and combat infections, making them a valuable resource in rural healthcare settings. In addition to their therapeutic uses, the leaves are often consumed as a vegetable, providing essential nutrients like vitamin C, iron, and dietary fiber.

While the root (cassava) is more renowned for its starchy content, the leaf remains a vital component in both medicinal and nutritional contexts, highlighting the versatility of Manihot esculenta as a multipurpose plant.
