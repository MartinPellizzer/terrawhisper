Manihot Esculenta Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Manihot esculenta, commonly known as cassava, is a tropical plant whose roots are widely used as a staple food crop.

It is rich in carbohydrates and contains various bioactive constituents such as saponins, alkaloids, and phenolic compounds, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

The herb has been associated with health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects.

It can be prepared in various forms such as infusions, decoctions, or poultices to harness its medicinal properties.

Health Benefits of manihot esculenta

Manihot esculenta has many health benefits, such as reducing blood sugar due to its high content of dietary fiber and polyphenols, which help improve insulin sensitivity and slow the absorption of glucose into the bloodstream.

It boosts heart health by containing antioxidants that reduce oxidative stress and inflammation, which are major contributors to cardiovascular diseases.

Manihot esculenta supports weight loss because it is low in calories and high in fiber, promoting a feeling of fullness and reducing overall calorie intake.

It lowers cholesterol by increasing the excretion of bile acids and reducing the absorption of dietary cholesterol in the intestines.
