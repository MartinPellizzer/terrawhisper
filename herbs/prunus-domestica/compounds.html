European Plum (Prunus domestica) Compounds

European Plum contains several key compounds, including flavonoids, which act as antioxidants, and tannins, which have astringent properties. Phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory effects, while glycosides and saponins support digestive health. Coumarin and anthocyanin provide additional antioxidant and anti-inflammatory benefits, and essential oils contribute to its aromatic profile.

European Plum (Prunus domestica) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in European Plum (Prunus domestica). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant properties and may support cardiovascular and inflammatory health. These compounds are often present in the fruit's skin and pulp, enhancing its therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

2. Tannin

Tannin in European Plum (Prunus domestica) acts as a mild astringent, helping to reduce inflammation and soothe digestive issues. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating diarrhea and skin conditions due to its ability to bind proteins and reduce fluid secretion. Tannins also support the plant's antimicrobial properties.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in European Plum (Prunus domestica). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports the plant's traditional use in promoting digestive health and reducing inflammation.

4. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in European Plum (Prunus domestica). It contributes to the plant's therapeutic properties, often acting as a precursor to bioactive sugars. Glycosides in European Plum are known for their mild laxative effects and potential anti-inflammatory benefits. They are typically present in the fruit's pulp and can be extracted for use in herbal preparations.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in European Plum (Prunus domestica). It contributes to the plant's mild sedative and antispasmodic effects. Found in the fruit and seeds, coumarin also has anticoagulant properties. It is used in herbal medicine to support digestive health and ease muscle tension.

6. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin, found in European Plum (Prunus domestica), is a powerful antioxidant that supports cardiovascular health and reduces inflammation. It contributes to the fruit's deep color and is associated with improved metabolic function and potential cancer prevention benefits.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in European Plum (Prunus domestica). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine, particularly for digestive support and as a mild detoxifying agent. Saponins help emulsify fats and may aid in nutrient absorption. Their presence supports the plant's role in supporting gut health and metabolic function.

8. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in European Plum (Prunus domestica). They contribute to the plant's medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. These compounds are often responsible for the plant's therapeutic benefits in herbal medicine.

9. Essential oil

The essential oil of European Plum (Prunus domestica) contains volatile compounds like limonene and myrcene. It is used in herbal medicine for its calming and antiseptic properties. The oil may support respiratory health and aid in digestive issues. Its aromatic profile makes it a valuable component in aromatherapy and traditional remedies.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloids in European Plum (Prunus domestica) are less prominent compared to other compounds like flavonoids and phenolic acids. While some alkaloids may be present, they are not the primary active constituents responsible for the plant's medicinal properties. Their role in herbal medicine from this plant is limited and not well-documented.
