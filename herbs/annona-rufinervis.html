Red-Veined Anona (Annona Rufinervis)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Annona rufinervis.

Annona rufinervis, commonly known as the red-veined soursop, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant that has been traditionally used in various medicinal practices across its native regions. The fruit's pulp and seeds contain bioactive compounds such as alkaloids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which have shown potential anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties. In traditional medicine, extracts from the plant have been used to treat ailments such as digestive disorders, skin infections, and even as a remedy for parasitic infestations. Recent scientific studies suggest that Annona rufinervis may also possess antitumor activities, making it a subject of interest for modern pharmacological research. However, further clinical trials are needed to fully validate its therapeutic applications and ensure its safe use in medicinal formulations.
