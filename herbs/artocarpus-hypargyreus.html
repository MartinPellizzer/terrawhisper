Artocarpus hypargyreus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Artocarpus hypargyreuus has large, leathery leaves characterized by a distinct silvery pubescence on their undersides and an entire or slightly serrated margin. The foliage typically grows in an alternate arrangement along sturdy, woody stems. Its reproductive structures consist of specialized inflorescences that eventually develop into aggregate fruits. These fruits are often globose or oval in shape and possess a textured exocarp. The overall architecture of the plant reflects its identity as a woody tree within the Moraceae family.
