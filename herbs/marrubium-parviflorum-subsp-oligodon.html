Small-Flowered Horehound (Marrubium Parviflorum Subsp. Oligodon)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Marrubium parviflorum subsp. oligodon.

Small-flowered horehound, scientifically known as Marrubium parviflorum subsp. oligodon, is a rare medicinal herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Caucasus. It is valued for its adaptogenic properties and has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, reduce inflammation, and promote overall vitality. Historically, it was employed in ancient Persian and Arabic medicine to treat coughs, bronchitis, and digestive issues, and its unique bitter flavor was believed to enhance digestion and clear the mind. In modern wellness practices, it is used as a natural remedy for respiratory ailments and as a complementary therapy in holistic health regimens. Notably, it contains a rare alkaloid called horehoundin, which contributes to its medicinal potency and distinguishes it from other members of the Marrubium genus.
