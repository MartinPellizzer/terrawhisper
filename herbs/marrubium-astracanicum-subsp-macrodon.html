Astracan Horehound (Marrubium Astracanicum Subsp. Macrodon)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Marrubium astracanicum subsp. macrodon.

Astracan horehound, scientifically known as Marrubium astracanicum subsp. macrodon, is a rare medicinal herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus, prized for its adaptogenic properties and historical use in traditional medicine. This herb is valued for its potential to support respiratory health, reduce inflammation, and enhance immune function due to its high concentration of mucilage and flavonoids. Traditionally, it has been used in folk remedies to treat coughs, bronchitis, and digestive issues, particularly in the Caucasus and parts of Eastern Europe. In modern wellness practices, it is increasingly incorporated into herbal teas and supplements for its soothing and anti-inflammatory effects. Its unique combination of a bitter, licorice-like flavor and the presence of rare alkaloids make it a distinctive and valuable plant in both historical and contemporary herbal medicine.
