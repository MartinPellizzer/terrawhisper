Holostemma ada-kodien

What's the taxonomical classification of Holostemma ada-kodien?

Holostemma ada-kodien belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further organized under the order Gentianales. It is a representative of the family Apocynaceae, falling under the genus Holostemma, with its specific designation being the species ada-kodien.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the alkaloids codeine, noscapine, and papaverine which serve as its primary medicinal compounds.

These specific chemical constituents are known for their significant pharmacological activities in human medicine. Codeine acts as a potent analgesic and antitussive to relieve pain and coughing. Noscapine provides additional sedative and antitussive effects while lacking the narcotic properties of other alkaloids. Papaverine functions as a smooth muscle relaxant to treat spasms and improve blood flow.

Together, these compounds form the basis for various therapeutic applications derived from the species.

      What are the herbal preparations of Holostemma ada-kodien?

      Holostemma ada-kodien is prepared primarily as a decoction or infusion using the dried leaves and aerial parts of the plant to extract its bioactive compounds. This liquid preparation is often consumed orally to access its medicinal properties. Some practitioners may also process the plant material into topical poultices for external application on the skin. These various forms allow for different methods of administration depending on the intended therapeutic use.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Holostemma ada-kodien can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, acute allergic reactions, and potential neurological impairment if ingested. Individuals may experience nausea, vomiting, and intense abdominal cramping shortly after consumption. Skin contact with the plant's sap might lead to localized dermatitis or painful blistering in sensitive subjects. Some cases have reported dizziness and a loss of motor coordination following exposure. It is essential to avoid all contact with this species due to these unpredictable and harmful physiological responses.
