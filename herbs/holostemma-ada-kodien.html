Holostemma ada-kodien

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Holostemma ada-kodien has a woody, perennial habit characterized by slender, erect stems that support clusters of small, tubular flowers. The leaves are typically arranged in an opposite pattern along the stem and possess an elliptical shape with entire margins. Each inflorescence consists of multiple densely packed florets that emerge from the axillary positions of the foliage. The floral structure features a narrow corolla tube that expands slightly toward the apex to reveal delicate petal lobes. Fine pubescence often covers the stems and leaf surfaces, providing a subtle texture to the plant's exterior.
