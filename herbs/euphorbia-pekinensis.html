What to know about Euphorbia pekinensis (chinese milkweed) before using it medicinally

Chinese milkweed, scientifically known as Euphorbia pekinensis, is a highly valued herb in traditional Chinese medicine, prized for its ability to calm irritability and improve overall well-being through its anti-inflammatory properties.

In addition to its medicinal uses, chinese milkweed is also employed as a culinary ingredient, with its young leaves and stems often used in stir-fries and soups due to their mild flavor and crunchy texture. From a horticultural perspective, chinese milkweed is an easy-to-grow perennial plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners looking to add some Asian flair to their landscaping. Botanically speaking, Euphorbia pekinensis belongs to the spurge family and features clusters of small, white flowers on long stalks.

With records dating back to the Ming dynasty, chinese milkweed has been an integral part of traditional Chinese herbalism for centuries, with its versatility and effectiveness earning it a revered place in the country's rich botanical heritage.
