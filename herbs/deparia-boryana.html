Deparia boryana

What's the taxonomical classification of Deparia boryana?

Deparia boryana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. This fern is a member of the family Aspleniaceae, falling under the genus Deparia, and is specifically identified by its species name, boryana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Deparia boryana has a creeping rhizome and pinnate fronds that typically reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. The individual pinnae are narrowly lanceolate with serrated margins and acute tips. The stipe is often clothed in brownish scales near the base. Sori are located on the underside of the pinnules and are protected by kidney-shaped indusia. The overall texture of the foliage is somewhat thin and herbaceous.
