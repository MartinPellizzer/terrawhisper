Phoebe forrestii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phoebe forrestii has lanceolate to elliptic leaves that are typically 5 to 15 centimeters long, featuring an entire margin and a coriaceous texture. The foliage is arranged alternately along the branches and displays a glossy green upper surface with a paler underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in axillary panicles. These blooms eventually develop into small, globose drupes which transition from green to a dark color upon maturity. The woody stems exhibit a smooth, greyish bark that characterizes the mature tree structure.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the subtropical evergreen forests of southern China, specifically occurring in the provinces of Guangdong, Guangxi, Fujian, and Hainan. It primarily thrives in montane environments at elevations ranging from several hundred to over one thousand meters above sea level. Within these regions, the species is often found in well-drained soils along hillsides and forest margins. The distribution is characterized by a preference for warm, humid climates typical of the subtropical zone. Localized populations are often scattered across specific mountain ranges rather than being uniformly spread across the entire landscape.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Phoebe forrestii can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and localized swelling if the plant's sap or leaves come into direct contact with human skin. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, many species within the Lauraceae family contain essential oils that act as potent allergens. Exposure may result in redness, itching, or even blistering in individuals with sensitive skin or existing botanical allergies. Ingestion of various parts of the plant should be avoided as the physiological impact on the digestive system has not been fully established. Always use caution and protective gloves when handling this species to prevent unexpected dermatological reactions.
