What's the taxonomical classification of Phoebe forrestii?

Phoebe forrestii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving through its hierarchical structure, the plant is placed in the order Laurales and the family Lauraceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its membership in the genus Phoebe, with its unique designation being the species forrestii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phoebe forrestii has lanceolate to elliptic leaves that are typically 5 to 15 centimeters long, featuring an entire margin and a coriaceous texture. The foliage is arranged alternately along the branches and displays a glossy green upper surface with a paler underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in axillary panicles. These blooms eventually develop into small, globose drupes which transition from green to a dark color upon maturity. The woody stems exhibit a smooth, greyish bark that characterizes the mature tree structure.
