Lysimachia longipes

What's the taxonomical classification of Lysimachia longipes?

Lysimachia longipes belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized under the order Ericales. This plant is a member of the family Primulaceae, falling under the genus Lysimachia, and is specifically identified by its species name, longipes.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lysimachia longipes has erect, slender stems that typically reach heights of 20 to 50 centimeters and bear opposite, lanceolate leaves with entire margins.

The foliage is characterized by smooth surfaces and a relatively small leaf size compared to other members of the genus. Its flowers are actinomorphic and yellow, featuring five distinct petals that emerge from long, thin pedicels. These pedicels extend significantly from the leaf axils, giving the plant its characteristic long-stalked appearance.

The reproductive structures include central stamens that are prominent within the floral cup.

        What are the herbal preparations of Lysimachia longipes?

        Lysimachia longipes is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical wash using the dried or fresh aerial parts of the plant. It is important to exercise caution and avoid direct contact to prevent these adverse physiological responses.
