Lysimachia longipes

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lysimachia longipes has erect, slender stems that typically reach heights of 20 to 50 centimeters and bear opposite, lanceolate leaves with entire margins. The foliage is characterized by smooth surfaces and a relatively small leaf size compared to other members of the genus. Its flowers are actinomorphic and yellow, featuring five distinct petals that emerge from long, thin pedicels. These pedicels extend significantly from the leaf axils, giving the plant its characteristic long-stalked appearance. The reproductive structures include central stamens that are prominent within the floral cup.
