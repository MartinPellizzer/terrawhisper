Pedicularis cranolopha

What's the taxonomical classification of Pedicularis cranolopha?

Pedicularis cranolopha belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical classification, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It further falls under the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Orobanchaceae. Finally, its taxonomic identity is completed by its placement within the genus Pedicularis, specifically identifying it as the species cranolopha.

What are the therapeutic actions of Pedicularis cranolopha?

Pedicularis cranolopha has traditionally been utilized in herbal medicine to treat nervous system disorders, reduce anxiety, and alleviate symptoms of depression. This plant contains various bioactive compounds that act as mild sedatives to calm an overactive mind. Practitioners often use it to address insomnia by promoting a more restful sleep cycle through its calming properties. It is also frequently applied to manage physical tension and emotional distress associated with chronic stress. These therapeutic actions make it a significant component in various traditional botanical formulations.
