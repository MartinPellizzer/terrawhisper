Pedicularis cranolopha

What's the taxonomical classification of Pedicularis cranolopha?

Pedicularis cranolopha belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical classification, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It further falls under the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Orobanchaceae. Finally, its taxonomic identity is completed by its placement within the genus Pedicularis, specifically identifying it as the species cranolopha.

These preparations are sometimes combined with other botanical elements to balance their specific medicinal properties. What side-effects this plant can have?

Pedicularis cranolopha can cause adverse reactions such as nausea, dizziness, or stomach upset if ingested by humans or animals. While specific clinical studies on its toxicity are limited, many species within the Pedicularis genus contain iridoid glycosides that may irritate the digestive tract. These chemical compounds can trigger mild allergic reactions or skin irritation in sensitive individuals upon direct contact. Consumption of the plant should be avoided due to the unpredictable nature of its bioactive constituents. Always consult a medical professional before handling or using wild flora for any purpose.
