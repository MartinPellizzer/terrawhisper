Pedicularis cranolopha

What's the taxonomical classification of Pedicularis cranolopha?

Pedicularis cranolopha belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical classification, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It further falls under the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Orobanchaceae. Finally, its taxonomic identity is completed by its placement within the genus Pedicularis, specifically identifying it as the species cranolopha.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pedicularis cranolopha has an erect, unbranched stem that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and is characterized by small, lanceolate leaves arranged oppositely along the stalk.

The foliage consists of narrow, entire leaf margins that taper toward the base. Its most distinctive feature is the dense, terminal inflorescence composed of hooded, tubular flowers that vary in color from pink to purplish-red. Each individual flower displays a prominent upper lip that curves over the reproductive organs, a hallmark of the genus.

The plant often grows in compact tufts within subalpine or alpine meadow environments.
