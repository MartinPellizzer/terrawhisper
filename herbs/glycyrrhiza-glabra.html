The main medicinal uses of this herb include treating respiratory conditions such as bronchitis and asthma, as well as gastrointestinal issues like ulcers and gastritis. The herb's therapeutic properties are attributed to its main bioactive compounds, including glycyrrhizin, flavonoids, and saponins, which have been shown to have immunomodulatory and antiviral effects.

In traditional medicine, people often prepare a herbal tea by infusing the roots of Glycyrrhiza glabra in hot water, which is then consumed to alleviate symptoms of various health conditions.
