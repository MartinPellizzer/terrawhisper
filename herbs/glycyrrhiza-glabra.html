Glycyrrhiza Glabra Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Glycyrrhiza glabra, commonly known as licorice, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, western Asia, and parts of northern Africa.

It is widely used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and expectorant properties.

The herb contains bioactive constituents such as glycyrrhizin, flavonoids, and saponins, which contribute to its therapeutic actions.

Glycyrrhiza glabra can be prepared as an infusion, tincture, or syrup, and is often used to support respiratory health and digestive function.

Side Effects of glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra induces potassium loss because the same mechanism that retains sodium also increases the excretion of potassium through the kidneys, leading to hypokalemia.

Glycyrrhiza glabra leads to high blood pressure because the sodium retention and potassium loss disrupt the body's fluid balance, increasing blood volume and vascular resistance.

Glycyrrhiza glabra causes stomach ulcers because its active compounds can irritate the gastric mucosa and reduce the protective mucus layer, making the stomach lining more susceptible to acid damage.
