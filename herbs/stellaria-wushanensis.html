Stellaria wushanensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Stellaria wushanensis?

Stellaria wushanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further classified into the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant falls under the order Caryophyllales and is a member of the family Caryophyllaceae. Specifically, it is identified within the genus Stellaria, with its unique designation being the species wushanensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stellaria wushanensis has small, annual herbaceous stems that grow upright with finely divided, linear leaves and tiny white flowers featuring five petals.

The foliage is characterized by its delicate, thread-like segments that give the plant a feathery appearance. Its flowers are relatively inconspicuous, often tucked near the leaf axils or at the ends of the stems. The root system is typically shallow, supporting its rapid growth cycle in moist environments.

        What are the herbal preparations of Stellaria wushanensis?

        Stellaria wushanensis is prepared as a decoction, a powder, or a topical wash depending on the intended medicinal application. The most common method involves boiling the dried aerial parts in water to create a concentrated tea for internal consumption. For more localized skin treatments, the plant material is often ground into a fine powder to be mixed with oils or water. Some practitioners also utilize the crushed herb directly as a poultice to treat external ailments. These various forms allow for the effective administration of the plant's bioactive compounds across different therapeutic needs.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Stellaeria wushanensis can cause skin irritation, localized swelling, and digestive distress if ingested. Contact with the plant's sap often leads to contact dermatitis characterized by redness and itching. Some individuals may also experience nausea or abdominal cramping following accidental consumption. These adverse reactions are typically mild but can be exacerbated by sensitive skin or pre-existing allergies. Careful handling is recommended to avoid any direct contact with the foliage.
