What's the taxonomical classification of Embelia parviflora?

Embelia parviflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it resides in the order Ericales and the family Primulaceae. Ultimately, this specific plant is identified by the genus Embelia and the species parviflora.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Embelia parviflora has a woody, climbing shrub habit characterized by small, elliptical, dark green leaves and clusters of tiny, white, bell-shaped flowers. The leaves are oppositely arranged on the stems and possess entire margins with slightly acute tips. Its inflorescence consists of axillary cymes that bear numerous small blossoms. The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that typically turns dark or black upon ripening. These structural features allow the plant to effectively navigate through dense vegetation to reach sunlight.
