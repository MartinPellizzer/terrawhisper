Chenopodium × hybridum

What's the taxonomical classification of Chenopodium × hybridum?

Chenopodium × hybridum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic journey continues through the order Caryophyllales and the family Amaranthaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Chenopodium, and its species designation, hybridum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Chenopodium × hybridum has an indeterminate growth habit characterized by erect, branched stems that often exhibit reddish or purplish pigmentation. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate with irregular or wavy margins and a mealy, farinose texture on both surfaces. Small, inconspicuous green flowers are arranged in dense, terminal or axillary panicles consisting of many-seeded glomerules. The fruit consists of a single small nutlet enclosed within the perianth, which often develops a powdery coating as the plant matures. These morphological traits reflect its hybrid origin between distinct species within the Chenopodiaceae family.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause kidney stones, calcium deficiency, digestive upset, and nausea due to its high oxalate content. The presence of oxalates in the plant can lead to the formation of painful kidney stones if consumed in large quantities. These same oxalates can bind to calcium in the body, potentially resulting in a calcium deficiency. In addition to these systemic issues, eating the plant may cause significant digestive upset. Many individuals also report experiencing nausea after ingestion.
