What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Chenopodium × hybridum has an indeterminate growth habit characterized by erect, branched stems that often exhibit reddish or purplish pigmentation. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate with irregular or wavy margins and a mealy, farinose texture on both surfaces. Small, inconspicuous green flowers are arranged in dense, terminal or axillary panicles consisting of many-seeded glomerules. The fruit consists of a single small nutlet enclosed within the perianth, which often develops a powdery coating as the plant matures.
