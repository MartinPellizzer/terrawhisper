What's the taxonomical classification of Ilex dasyphylla?

Ilex dasyphylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Aquifoliales and the family Aquifoliaceae. Finally, it is placed within the genus Ilex, with its specific identification being the species dasyphylla.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ilex dasyphylla has an evergreen shrub or small tree habit characterized by densely packed, leathery leaves that are elliptical to lanceolate in shape and covered in fine, bristly hairs on both surfaces. The foliage typically measures between 2 and 5 centimeters in length and possesses a distinctly dull, grayish-green appearance due to this pubescence. Its branches are woody and often spread in a compact, rounded form. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, eventually giving way to small, dark berries. The overall structure is rugged and adapted to withstand various environmental stresses.
