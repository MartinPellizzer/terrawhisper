Ilex dasyphylla

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ilex dasyphylla has an evergreen shrub or small tree habit characterized by densely packed, leathery leaves that are elliptical to lanceolate in shape and covered in fine, bristly hairs on both surfaces. The foliage typically measures between 2 and 5 centimeters in length and possesses a distinctly dull, grayish-green appearance due to this pubescence. Its branches are woody and often spread in a compact, rounded form. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, eventually giving way to small, dark berries. The overall structure is rugged and adapted to withstand various environmental stresses.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes Mountains, spanning across parts of Peru, Bolivia, and northern Argentina. It specifically thrives in the montane forests and puna grasslands found at elevations between 2,500 and 4,000 meters. The distribution is characterized by its presence in moist, temperate environments within these mountainous corridors. Climatic patterns in these specific zones allow the species to maintain its specialized habitat requirements. Because it is tied to these high-elevation ecosystems, its range is geographically fragmented by the rugged topography of the South American continent.
