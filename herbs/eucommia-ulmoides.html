What is Eucommia (Eucommia Ulmoides)?

Eucommia (Eucommia ulmoides) is a deciduous tree native to China, commonly known as the Chinese emu tree, and is widely used in traditional Chinese medicine for its bark, which contains compounds believed to support cardiovascular health and blood pressure regulation.

Botanical Identity and Classification

Eucommia ulmoides, commonly known as the Chinese empress tree, is a deciduous flowering plant belonging to the family Ebenaceae, native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and is widely used in traditional Chinese medicine for its bark, which contains compounds like iridoid glycosides and flavonoids.

Common Names and Synonyms

Eucommia ulmoides is commonly known as the Chinese rubber tree, Chinese cork tree, and in traditional Chinese medicine, it is referred to as 'Du Zhong' or 'Du Zhong'.

Plant Description and Morphology

Eucommia ulmoides is a deciduous tree characterized by its thick, bark-like trunk that can reach up to 2 meters in diameter, smooth gray bark with longitudinal fissures, and alternate simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape with a leathery texture and dark green upper surface.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Eucommia ulmoides is native to China, particularly in the southern and western regions, including provinces such as Sichuan, Hubei, Guizhou, and Yunnan, where it thrives in humid, subtropical climates with well-drained soils.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The bark, leaves, and fruits of Eucommia ulmoides are used medicinally, with the bark being the most commonly utilized part due to its high concentration of bioactive compounds such as eucommia lignans and flavonoids.

Phytochemical Composition

Eucommia ulmoides contains a diverse array of bioactive compounds, including iridoid glycosides such as aucubin and loganin, lignans like secoisolariciresinol, and various phenolic acids and flavonoids that contribute to its medicinal properties.

Pharmacological Properties

Eucommia ulmoides exhibits pharmacological properties including antihypertensive, anti-inflammatory, and antidiabetic effects, primarily due to its active compounds such as iridoid glycosides, alkaloids, and flavonoids.

Mechanisms of Action

Eucommia ulmoides exerts its therapeutic effects primarily through the modulation of vascular tone, regulation of blood pressure, and enhancement of endothelial function via the release of bioactive compounds such as alkaloids, iridoids, and flavonoids.

Therapeutic Uses and Indications

Eucommia is used to treat hypertension, hyperlipidemia, osteoporosis, asthma, diabetes, and cerebral hemorrhage.

Preparation Methods and Forms

Eucommia is typically prepared as a dried bark, which is then used to make powders, decoctions, or tinctures for medicinal purposes.

Dosage and Administration

The typical dosage of Eucommia ulmoides for herbal use ranges from 3 to 10 grams per day, usually prepared as a decoction or powder, and it is often combined with other herbs in traditional formulas depending on the specific condition being treated.

Safety, Side Effects, and Contraindications

While Eucommia ulmoides is generally considered safe when used in appropriate amounts, it may cause side effects such as gastrointestinal discomfort, dizziness, and allergic reactions in some individuals, particularly when taken in high doses or over prolonged periods.

Drug Interactions
