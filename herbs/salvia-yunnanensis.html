Salvia yunnanensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Salvia yunnanensis?

Salvia yunnanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Lamiales. As a member of the family Lamiaceae, this plant is categorized within the genus Salvia, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species yunnanensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Salvia yunnanensis has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by erect, square stems and oppositely arranged, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The foliage typically exhibits a rough, pubescent texture and a deep green coloration. Its inflorescence consists of verticillasters arranged along a terminal spike, bearing tubular, bilabiate flowers. These blossoms often present in shades of blue, purple, or violet, featuring a distinct upper lip and a larger lower lip. The plant's reproductive structures are adapted for pollination by specific insect vectors within its native montane habitats.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains rosmarinic acid, tanshinones, salvianolic acid A, methyl salvianolate A, and lithospermic acid which serve as its primary medicinal compounds. These bioactive molecules contribute to the diverse pharmacological profile of the species. Specifically, the presence of tanshinones and salvianolic acid A is often linked to potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Methyl salvianolate A and rosmarinic acid further enhance its biological activity in various physiological systems. Additionally, lithospermic acid plays a significant role in the plant's unique chemical composition. Together, these substances make the plant a subject of intense study in natural product chemistry.

As discussed in a study, twelve compounds were isolated and identified from the root of Salvia yunnanensis. These specific medicinal compounds include protocatechaldehyde (1), caffeic acid (2), ferulic acid (3), rosmarinic acid (4), salvianolic acid A (5), salvianolic acid C (6), lithospermicacid (7), lithospermicacid B (8), 9'-methyl lithospermate B (9), 9''-methyl lithospermate B (10), 9',9'''-dimethyl lithospermate B (11), and 9'-ethyl lithospermate B (12). The compounds 1, 2, 3, 5, 6, 9, 10, 11, and 12 were first isolated from S. yunnanensis. Isolation and purification of these substances were achieved using Diaion HP20, Sephadex LH-20, and ODS chromatography.
