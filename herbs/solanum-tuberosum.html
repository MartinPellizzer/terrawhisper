Potato (Solanum tuberosum)

Potato (Solanum tuberosum) is a member of the Solanaceae family, native to South America, Central America, and North America. Traditionally, its tubers, leaves, and stems have been used for culinary uses, decoctions, and infusions.

This herb is particularly valued for its tonic, anti-inflammatory, and stimulant actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and traditional chinese medicine.

Perennial herbaceous plant.

Leaves
Simple leaves with upper surface glabrous and green, lower surface slightly pubescent and lighter green, featuring prominent stomatal bands.
Flowers
Flowers are actinomorphic, yellow with purple stigmatic lobes, arranged in solitary umbels, typically having five stamens and a three-lobed ovary.
Stems
Cylindrical, herbaceous, glabrous, and tuberous with nodes and internodes, exhibiting an erect growth habit and alternate branching pattern.
