Viburnum hainanense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viburnum hainanense has opposite, elliptical to lanceolate leaves that measure approximately 5 to 12 centimeters in length and feature finely serrated margins. The leaf surfaces exhibit a distinct texture, often appearing slightly coriaceous with prominent venation on the underside. Its flowering structures consist of small, creamy white blossoms arranged in dense, flat-topped cymes. These inflorescences emerge from woody stems that display a characteristic branching pattern typical of the Adoxaceae family. Following pollination, the plant produces small, fleshy drupes that transition in color as they mature.
