    What is Pfaffia paniculata?

    Pfaffia paniculata is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

    It is characterized by its erect stem, which can reach up to 1 meter in height, and its compound leaves, with leaflets arranged oppositely and having a lanceolate shape. The flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and arranged in axillary spikes, while the fruit is a small capsule containing several seeds. This plant is native to South America, specifically in Brazil, Paraguay, and Argentina, and is commonly found in grasslands and savannas.

    Pfaffia paniculata has been used in folk medicine for its purported health benefits, particularly in the treatment of inflammation and oxidative stress.
