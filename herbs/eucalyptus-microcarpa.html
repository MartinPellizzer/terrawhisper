Eucalyptus microcarpa

Eucalyptus microcarpa, commonly known as the silver-leaved mountain gum, is a species of tree in the myrtle family (Myrtaceae).

The leaves of this plant have been used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and expectorant properties, which provide health benefits such as relieving respiratory issues and reducing pain.

The therapeutic actions of Eucalyptus microcarpa include its ability to act as a bronchodilator, reducing the severity of asthma and other respiratory conditions.

The bioactive constituents of the plant include eucalyptol, a compound with antimicrobial properties, as well as other volatile oils and flavonoids with antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects.
