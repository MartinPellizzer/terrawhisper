Clematis rubifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Clematis rubifolia?

Clematis rubifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Ranunculales. Furthermore, it is a member of the family Ranunculaceae, specifically categorized under the genus Clematis, with the species designation rubifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clematis rubifolia has opposite, simple, or trifoliate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. The foliage often displays a reddish or purplish tint, particularly on the underside or when exposed to high light levels. Its stems are slender and climbing, utilizing specialized petioles to wrap around surrounding vegetation for support. The flowers are relatively small and characterized by four to six petal-like sepals that range in color from pale pink to deep rose. Following bloom, the plant produces distinctive feathery, achene-bearing seed heads that aid in wind dispersal.
