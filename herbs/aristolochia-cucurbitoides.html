Aristolochia cucurbitoides

What's the taxonomical classification of Aristolochia cucurbitoides?

Aristolochia cucurbitoides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Piperales. This plant is a representative of the family Aristolochiaceae, falling under the genus Aristolochia, and is specifically identified by its species name, cucurbitoides.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aristolochia cucurbitoides has climbing, herbaceous stems that produce heart-shaped, ovate leaves with smooth margins and an entire base. These leaves are typically arranged in an opposite pattern along the vines and possess a soft, velvety texture. The plant is most notable for its unique, tube-shaped flowers that feature a mottled brownish-purple coloration. These blooms consist of a long, slender tube that expands into a wider, chambered structure designed to trap specific insect pollinators. The overall growth habit is characterized by its ability to scramble over surrounding vegetation using its twining stems.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause severe nephrotoxicity leading to permanent kidney damage, nephropathy, and life-threatening renal failure. The consumption of its tissues is linked to uropathy, which affects the urinary tract and its functions. Many individuals who ingest the plant develop painful kidney stones as a direct consequence of its toxic components. Furthermore, the species exhibits significant carcinogenicity, increasing the risk of developing various types of cancer. These cumulative effects pose a grave danger to human health through systemic organ destruction.
