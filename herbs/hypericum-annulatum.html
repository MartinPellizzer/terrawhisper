Annual St. John's Wort

Hypericum annulatum

Annual St. John's Wort (Hypericum annulatum) is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, known for its bright yellow flowers and historical use in traditional medicine to support mood and mental well-being.

Taxonomy

Annual St. John's Wort, scientifically known as Hypericum annulatum, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Garryales, family Clusiaceae, genus Hypericum, and species Hypericum annulatum.

Morphology

Annual St. John's Wort (Hypericum annulatum) is a low-growing herb with opposite, oval to elliptic leaves that are typically reddish or purple underneath, and yellow flowers with five petals and prominent yellow stamens.

Distribution

Annual St. John's Wort (Hypericum annulatum) is native to the Mediterranean region, including parts of southern Europe, North Africa, and western Asia, and is typically found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils, grasslands, and coastal areas.

Traditional Uses

Annual St. John's Wort (Hypericum annulatum) has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to support mood regulation, alleviate mild depressive symptoms, and promote skin healing through its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Medicinal Systems

In traditional medicinal systems such as Ayurveda, Traditional Chinese Medicine, and Native American herbal practices, Annual St. John's Wort has been historically used for its purported calming and healing properties.

Phytochemistry

The phytochemistry of Annual St. John's Wort (Hypericum annulatum) includes bioactive compounds such as hypericin, hyperforin, flavonoids, and essential oils, which contribute to its traditional use in herbal medicine for mood regulation and anti-inflammatory effects.

Pharmacology

Annual St. John's Wort (Hypericum annulatum) exhibits pharmacological properties characterized by antidepressant, anti-inflammatory, and mild analgesic effects, primarily through its influence on neurotransmitter activity and modulation of inflammatory pathways.

Botanical Identification

Annual St. John's Wort, scientifically known as Hypericum annulatum, belongs to the family Hypericaceae. It is also called the lesser St. John's Wort and is an annual herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant has yellow flowers with red veins and small, yellowish leaves. It is sometimes referred to as Hypericum humifusum in older herbal texts.

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for digestive issues and mild depression due to its calming properties.

Decoction: Used for respiratory conditions and inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory effects.

Extract: Used for anxiety and depression due to its potential mood-enhancing properties.

Safety Profile

Annual St. John's Wort is generally considered safe when used short-term, but it may interact with certain medications and should be avoided during pregnancy or breastfeeding. It is contraindicated for individuals taking antidepressants or anticoagulants due to potential drug interactions. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
