Desmodium renifolium

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Desmodium renifoliuom has trifoliate leaves characterized by kidney-shaped or reniform leaflets that are typically smaller and more rounded than those of related species. Its stems are slender and can be either erect or slightly decumbent depending on the growing environment. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually clustered in axillary racemes. The fruit is a distinctive legume, often forming a hairy pod that facilitates seed dispersal through attachment to passing animals.
