What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Clitoria Ternatea (Butterfly Pea)?

Clitoria ternatea, also known as butterfly pea, has health benefits such as improving cognitive function and memory.

Its medicinal constituents include flavonoids, alkaloids, and glycosides, which are responsible for its neuroprotective and anti-inflammatory effects. The herb is traditionally prepared as a tea or supplement, and is also used in Ayurvedic and traditional medicine to enhance mental clarity and reduce stress. However, potential side effects include allergic reactions and interactions with certain medications.

When using Clitoria ternatea, individuals should consult with a healthcare professional to ensure safe dosages and monitor any adverse effects.

This article explains the health benefits, active constituents, medicinal preparations, possible side effects, and precautions related to Clitoria ternatea.

What are the health benefits of Clitoria ternatea?

Clitoria ternatea, also known as butterfly pea, has health benefits such as reducing stress and anxiety due to its adaptogenic properties.

Its antioxidant and flavonoid content also have anti-inflammatory effects, which can help to protect against cell damage and oxidative stress.

Additionally, Clitoria ternatea is believed to improve cognitive function and memory, making it a popular ingredient in traditional Ayurvedic and herbal remedies.

What are the active constituents of Clitoria ternatea?

Clitoria ternatea, also known as butterfly pea, has active constituents such as flavonoids, particularly 3,4',7-trihydroxyflavone, kaempferol and its glycosides, and isoflavones like genistein and daidzein.

These compounds have been reported to possess antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and neuroprotective properties.

The flavonoids and isoflavones in Clitoria ternatea are also believed to have a potential role in improving cognitive function and memory.

What are the medicinal preparations of Clitoria ternatea?

Clitoria ternatea, also known as butterfly pea, has medicinal preparations such as extracts, capsules, and tea that are used to promote cognitive function and memory.

The plant is rich in antioxidants, flavonoids, and alkaloids, which are believed to have neuroprotective properties. These preparations are also used to alleviate stress and anxiety, improve sleep quality, and boost mood.

Traditionally, the plant is used in Ayurvedic and Unani medicine to enhance mental clarity and focus.

What are the possible side effect of using Clitoria ternatea improperly?

Improper use of Clitoria ternatea, also known as butterfly pea, increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as stomach upset, nausea, and diarrhea due to its high concentration of flavonoids and alkaloids.

Prolonged consumption may also lead to allergic reactions, such as skin rashes and itching, in some individuals.

Additionally, it may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners, and exacerbate existing health conditions.

What precautions to take when using Clitoria ternatea medicinally?

Before using Clitoria ternatea, also known as butterfly pea, for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as consulting a healthcare professional to determine a safe dosage, as excessive consumption can lead to adverse effects like diarrhea, vomiting, and allergic reactions.

Additionally, pregnant and breastfeeding women should avoid using it due to limited research on its safety during these periods.
