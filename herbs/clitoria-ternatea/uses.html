Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea) Uses

Butterfly pea is used to treat skin infections, wounds, and inflammation, including dermatitis. It helps reduce joint pain and may alleviate respiratory infections. It also shows potential in managing anxiety and inflammatory conditions.

Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea) Monograph

1. Skin infection

Butterfly pea has been traditionally used to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and bacterial growth on the skin. Applications such as poultices or topical extracts can aid in healing minor infections and wounds. Its natural compounds support skin recovery and prevent further infection.

2. Wound healing

Butterfly pea has been traditionally used to promote wound healing due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce inflammation, prevent infection, and accelerate tissue repair. Extracts from the plant are applied topically to wounds to enhance recovery and minimize scarring. Its use in herbal medicine supports natural approaches to skin injury treatment.

3. Skin inflammation

Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea) has been traditionally used to treat skin inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. The plant's extracts help reduce redness, swelling, and irritation, making it effective for conditions like eczema and psoriasis. Its natural compounds support skin healing and soothe inflammatory responses.

4. Dermatitis

Butterfly pea has been traditionally used to treat dermatitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce skin irritation and redness, often applied as a topical remedy. Its ability to soothe inflamed skin makes it a valued herb in herbal medicine for skin conditions.

5. Skin infections

Butterfly pea has antimicrobial properties that help treat skin infections. It reduces inflammation and promotes healing. Traditionally used for fungal and bacterial infections, it can be applied topically as a poultice or infusion. Its active compounds inhibit harmful pathogens on the skin.

6. Inflammation

Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea) has anti-inflammatory properties due to its flavonoid content, which helps reduce swelling and pain. It is used traditionally to treat inflammatory conditions like arthritis and skin infections. Its active compounds inhibit inflammatory pathways in the body. Clinical studies support its potential as a natural remedy for inflammation.

7. Joint pain

Butterfly pea has been traditionally used to alleviate joint pain due to its anti-inflammatory properties. The plant contains compounds that may reduce swelling and pain in conditions like arthritis. It is often prepared as a tea or extract to support joint health. Its use aligns with holistic approaches in herbal medicine for managing chronic pain.

8. Respiratory infection

Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea) is used in traditional herbal medicine to support respiratory health. It helps reduce inflammation and soothe symptoms of respiratory infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It is often prepared as a tea to ease coughing and clear congestion.

9. Respiratory infections

Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea) has been traditionally used to support respiratory health. It contains compounds that may help reduce inflammation and alleviate symptoms of respiratory infections. Its antimicrobial properties are believed to aid in clearing infections. It is often used in herbal formulations for coughs, bronchitis, and other respiratory ailments.

10. Anxiety

Butterfly pea has been traditionally used to alleviate anxiety due to its calming properties. It contains compounds that may help regulate mood and reduce stress. Its use in herbal medicine often involves teas or extracts to support emotional balance. It is valued for its gentle yet effective approach to managing anxiety naturally.
