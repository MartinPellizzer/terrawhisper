Botanical Identification of Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea)

To identify Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea) Monograph

Scientific Classification

Butterfly Pea, or Clitoria ternatea, belongs to the plant kingdom, specifically the division of flowering plants. It is classified under the class Fabales, order Fabales, and family Fabaceae, which includes legumes. The genus is Clitoria, and the species is Clitoria ternatea, named for its pea-like flowers and origin from the Ternate region.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The botanical name of Butterfly Pea is Clitoria ternatea, first described by Linnaeus in 1758. It is also known by several synonyms, including Clitoria major and Clitoria hirsuta. Common names such as "Butterfly Pea," "Blue Pea," and "Kumbu" are widely used globally. Regional and historical names reflect its traditional use in various cultures.

Morphological Characteristics

Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea) is an erect, herbaceous annual plant. It has a fibrous root system that anchors it to the soil. The stem is cylindrical, slender, and slightly hairy. The leaves are pinnately trifoliate, with ovate leaflets and a short petiole. The inflorescence consists of racemes of bright blue or purple flowers. Each flower has a spurred corolla, five petals, and a prominent standard petal. The fruit is a slender, curved pod that splits open to release seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea) can be distinguished by its distinctive butterfly-shaped purple flowers, trifoliate leaves, and cylindrical seed pods. It differs from similar species like Clitoria javanica by its more vibrant flower color and leaf structure. The presence of prominent, curved calyx lobes also helps in identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea) is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas. It thrives in warm climates with high humidity and well-drained, loamy soils. It is commonly found at altitudes ranging from sea level to 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Butterfly Pea blooms from May to September, producing vibrant purple flowers. Seed formation occurs from October to December. For identification purposes, the best time to harvest is during the flowering period when the plant is most identifiable.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea) is closely related to species within the genus Clitoria. It shares genetic and morphological traits with other legumes, particularly those in the family Fabaceae. Its phylogenetic placement reflects its close ties to other climbing legumes, highlighting its evolutionary relationships within the broader legume family.
