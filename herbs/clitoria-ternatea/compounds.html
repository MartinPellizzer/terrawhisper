Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea) Compounds

The primary active compounds in Butterfly Pea include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and anthocyanins, responsible for its vibrant color and potential health benefits. Tannins and saponins contribute to its astringent effects, while alkaloids and coumarin may influence its medicinal applications. These compounds work together to provide the plant's therapeutic value.

Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are key active compounds in Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea). They contribute to the plant's medicinal properties, including antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. These compounds are found in the flowers and are used in traditional herbal medicine for their therapeutic benefits.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is used in traditional medicine for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins in Butterfly Pea may support digestive health and skin conditions. They are also valued for their potential role in reducing oxidative stress.

3. Alkaloid

Alkaloids in Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea) are nitrogen-containing compounds with potential pharmacological effects. These alkaloids may contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating various ailments, though research on their specific roles and mechanisms is still emerging.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating inflammation and digestive issues. Coumarin's antispasmodic and mild sedative properties support its role in herbal medicine. It is also noted for its potential antioxidant effects.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, which support immune function and may help reduce inflammation. This compound is commonly found in the flowers and leaves of the plant, making it a valuable component in traditional herbal remedies.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, including its use in traditional herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Glycosides in Butterfly Pea may support digestive health and aid in managing conditions like ulcers.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Saponin helps support digestive health and may aid in managing conditions like hypertension and diabetes. Its presence enhances the therapeutic value of Butterfly Pea in traditional herbal medicine.

8. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin, a pigment found in Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea), contributes to its medicinal properties. This compound is known for its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting the plant's use in traditional herbal medicine. It helps protect cells from oxidative damage and may aid in treating conditions like diabetes and inflammation.

9. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea). They contribute to the plant's medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. These compounds are often responsible for the plant's therapeutic applications in traditional herbal medicine.

10. Essential oil

Essential oil from Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea) contains volatile compounds like limonene and geraniol. These compounds contribute to its aromatic properties and may support digestive and anti-inflammatory effects. The essential oil is often used in aromatherapy and traditional herbal medicine for its calming and therapeutic benefits.
