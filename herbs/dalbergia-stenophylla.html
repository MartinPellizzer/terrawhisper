Dalbergia stenophylla

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dalbergia stenophylla has pinnately compound leaves characterized by narrow, linear leaflets that are significantly thinner than those of related species. The plant typically grows as a woody shrub or small tree with a branched architecture. Its leaflets are arranged oppositely or sub-oppositely along the rachis, contributing to a delicate foliage texture. The bark is generally smooth, while the flowers are small and clustered in axillary panicles. The fruit consists of small, indehiscent pods that facilitate seed dispersal within its native habitat.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Dalbergia stenophylla can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and potential respiratory sensitivities due to the presence of bioactive quinones and phenolic compounds. Individuals frequently report redness, itching, or localized swelling upon direct contact with the plant's sap or processed wood. Some users may experience hypersensitivity reactions if the dust from the timber is inhaled during woodworking activities. While extensive clinical studies are limited, the chemical profile suggests a risk of systemic toxicity if ingested in significant quantities. Careful handling and protective equipment are recommended to mitigate these physiological risks.
