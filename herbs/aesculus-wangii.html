Aesculus wangii

What's the taxonomical classification of Aesculus wangii?

Aesculus wangii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical structure, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant is further categorized under the order Sapindales and the family Sapindaceae. Finally, it is situated within the genus Aesculus, with its specific designation being the species wangii.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various inflammatory conditions, specifically targeting chronic venous insufficiency and hemorrhoidal swelling. The medicinal properties are primarily utilized to reduce edema and improve circulation within the vascular system. By strengthening capillary walls, the extracts help alleviate the discomfort associated with varicose veins. Additionally, it serves as a natural remedy for reducing localized swelling in the lower extremities. Practitioners often rely on its bioactive compounds to manage symptoms of vascular congestion.
