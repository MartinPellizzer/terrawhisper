Aesculus wangii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aesculus wangii has palmately compound leaves typically consisting of five to seven leaflets with serrated margins and an elliptical to obovate shape. The leaflets are arranged around a central point on a long petiole, creating a distinct hand-like appearance. The plant features woody stems and branches that support terminal panicles of fragrant, creamy white flowers. These floral clusters are composed of numerous individual blossoms that attract various pollinators during the blooming season. Small, globose fruits develop from the flowers and eventually mature into capsules containing large seeds.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Aesculus wangii can cause gastrointestinal distress including nausea, vomiting, stomach cramps, and diarrhea due to the presence of toxic saponins. These compounds can irritate the lining of the digestive tract if the plant material is ingested. In higher doses, the toxins may lead to more severe systemic issues such as dizziness or irregular heart rhythms. It is essential to avoid consuming any part of this species to prevent these adverse reactions. Individuals with sensitive stomachs should be particularly cautious around this plant.
