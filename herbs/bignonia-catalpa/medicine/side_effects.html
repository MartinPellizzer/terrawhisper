1. Increases blood pressure

Bignonia catalpa increases blood pressure by stimulating the body's natural production of adrenaline, a hormone that constricts blood vessels and causes an increase in heart rate.

This stimulatory effect can lead to a rise in blood pressure, which may be problematic for individuals with pre-existing hypertension or cardiovascular conditions.

The exact mechanisms behind this effect are attributed to the presence of certain compounds in catalpa, such as alkaloids and glycosides, which interact with the body's physiological systems to produce this response.

Additionally, the high concentration of alkaloids in Bignonia catalpa can cause a sudden drop in blood pressure, further contributing to dizziness and lightheadedness.

6. Disrupts digestive system

Bignonia catalpa disrupts digestive system when used, potentially causing stomach upset, nausea, and diarrhea.

This is due to its saponin content, which can irritate the mucous membranes in the gastrointestinal tract, leading to inflammation and altered gut motility.

Additionally, the plant's bitter compounds may also contribute to digestive discomfort by slowing down gastric emptying and reducing appetite.

7. Raises heart rate significantly

Bignonia catalpa raises heart rate significantly due to its stimulatory effects on the body's autonomic nervous system.

The compound catalpol, found in the plant's leaves and stems, has been shown to increase sympathetic activity, leading to a heightened state of alertness and increased blood pressure.

This can cause an individual's heart rate to rise significantly, potentially impacting cardiovascular health if not properly managed.

8. Leads to respiratory issues

Bignonia catalpa leads to respiratory issues due to its high pollen count and allergenic properties.

When inhaled, these particles can irritate the nasal passages, eyes, and throat, triggering symptoms such as congestion, sneezing, and coughing. The potent allergens released by the plant's flowers and leaves can also exacerbate pre-existing conditions like asthma, making it challenging for individuals to breathe properly.

As a result, individuals exposed to Bignonia catalpa may experience respiratory discomfort and distress.

9. Interacts with medications badly

Bignonia catalpa interacts with medications badly due to its potential to alter the way certain medicines are absorbed by the body.

This is because catalpa contains compounds that can inhibit or induce enzymes in the liver and intestines, leading to changes in medication levels and potentially causing adverse reactions.

Additionally, catalpa's ability to stimulate the digestive system may also contribute to interactions with medications.

10. Increases anxiety levels

Bignonia catalpa increases anxiety levels due to its stimulating properties that can affect the nervous system.

The plant's high concentration of alkaloids and glycosides can cause an overstimulation of the brain, leading to feelings of restlessness, jitteriness, and heightened senses of alertness.

This can result in increased anxiety levels as the body struggles to cope with the overwhelming stimulation.
