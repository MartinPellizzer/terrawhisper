Ilex dolichopoda

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ilex dolichopoda has long, narrow, leathery leaves that are typically lanceolate in shape with entire margins and a dark green upper surface. The foliage is characterized by a distinctively elongated morphology which distinguishes it from other holly species. Its stems are woody and can develop a rugged texture as the plant matures. The leaves are arranged alternately along the branches, providing a dense canopy. Small, inconspicuous flowers eventually give way to characteristic red berries during the fruiting season.
