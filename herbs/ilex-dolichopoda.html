What's the taxonomical classification of Ilex dolichopoda?

Ilex dolichopoda belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the taxonomic hierarchy, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the lineage, it falls under the order Aquifoliales and the family Aquifoliaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its genus, Ilex, and its specific species designation, dolichopoda.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ilex dolichopoda has long, narrow, leathery leaves that are typically lanceolate in shape with entire margins and a dark green upper surface.

The foliage is characterized by a distinctively elongated morphology which distinguishes it from other holly species. Its stems are woody and can develop a rugged texture as the plant matures. The leaves are arranged alternately along the branches, providing a dense canopy.

Small, inconspicuous flowers eventually give way to characteristic red berries during the fruiting season.
