1. Causes dizziness

Ida lata causes dizziness due to its potential interaction with certain medications and its ability to affect the nervous system.

The root of the plant contains compounds that can slow down the activity of certain neurotransmitters, leading to feelings of dizziness or lightheadedness.

Additionally, Ida lata may stimulate the release of chemicals in the brain that contribute to changes in blood pressure, further increasing the risk of dizziness.

2. Produces nausea

Ida lata produces nausea due to its high concentration of volatile oils, particularly citral and curcumin.

These compounds can cause stomach irritation, leading to feelings of queasiness and discomfort.

Additionally, the herb's bitter taste and astringent properties may further exacerbate digestive issues, resulting in nausea.

3. Triggers vomiting

Ida lata triggers vomiting due to its ability to stimulate the stomach and digestive system.

The compound berberine, present in idalata, can cause an increase in gastric acid secretion, leading to nausea and vomiting.

Additionally, idalata's natural oils may irritate the digestive tract, further exacerbating these symptoms.

4. Leads to diarrhea

Ida lata leads to diarrhea due to its ability to stimulate the gut and increase digestive activity.

The plant's active compounds, particularly isopulegol and sesquiterpenes, can cause an imbalance in the gut microbiome, leading to an increased frequency of bowel movements and loose stool.

This side effect may be more pronounced when taking Ida lata for extended periods or at high dosages.

5. Increases heart rate

Ida lata increases heart rate due to its stimulatory properties.

The bioactive compounds present in this plant, such as choline and magnesium, can cause a rapid heartbeat by affecting the autonomic nervous system. This increased heart rate is a result of the stimulation of the sympathetic nervous system, leading to an increase in cardiac output and blood pressure.

As a result, individuals may experience palpitations, tachycardia, or even arrhythmias.

6. Lowers blood pressure

Ida lata lowers blood pressure by inhibiting the breakdown of a compound called nitric oxide in the body.

Nitric oxide helps to relax and dilate blood vessels, allowing for smoother blood flow and reduced blood pressure.

Additionally, Ida lata has been found to have a direct effect on blood vessels, causing them to constrict and lower blood pressure further.

7. Weakens muscle tone

Ida lata weakens muscle tone due to its ability to relax smooth muscles, leading to decreased muscle contractions and reduced muscle strength.

This effect is thought to be caused by the plant's bioactive compounds, such as sesquiterpenes and alkaloids, which bind to receptors in the body, altering neural activity and motor function.

As a result, individuals may experience symptoms of muscle weakness or fatigue, particularly in the smooth muscles that govern involuntary movements like digestion and blood flow.

8. Disrupts menstrual cycles

Ida lata disrupts menstrual cycles due to its ability to stimulate the uterus and increase contractions.

This can lead to irregular or heavy bleeding, as well as cramping and discomfort.

The plant's bioactive compounds, including sesquiterpenes and alkaloids, are believed to be responsible for these effects, which may cause hormonal imbalances that alter the normal menstrual cycle.

9. Interferes with sleep

Ida lata interferes with sleep by stimulating the body's digestive system and increasing stomach acid production, which can lead to difficulty falling asleep or staying asleep.

Additionally, the herb's spicy and warming properties may cause a sense of restlessness, making it challenging to relax and fall into a deep sleep.

10. Reduces appetite

Ida lata reduces appetite due to its natural ability to stimulate digestion and alter gut flora.

The phytochemicals present in ida lata, such as iridoid glycosides and sesquiterpenes, can slow down gastric emptying and reduce hunger sensations by increasing the production of gastrointestinal hormones like cholecystokinin and gastrin.

This effect can lead to a decrease in appetite and food intake.
