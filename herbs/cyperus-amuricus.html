Cyperus amuricus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cyperus amuricus has a perennial growth habit characterized by triangular, compressed stems that emerge from creeping rhizomes to form dense tufts. The leaves are linear, grass-like, and arranged in a basal manner, often displaying a bright green coloration. Inflorescences consist of compact, terminal clusters of spikelets that are typically arranged in a head-like structure. These spikelets contain small, inconspicuous flowers that are adapted for wind pollination within wetland environments. The overall architecture of the plant is optimized for stability in saturated soil conditions.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to specific wetland ecosystems within the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, primarily documented in countries such as Thailand and Malaysia. Its distribution is closely tied to low-lying marshes and seasonally flooded areas where stagnant or slow-moving water is abundant. Because it relies on high humidity and consistent moisture, the species does not spread into arid or mountainous inland territories. Local populations are often fragmented and restricted to specific hydrological niches within these tropical landscapes. This limited range makes the plant highly dependent on the preservation of local wetland habitats.
