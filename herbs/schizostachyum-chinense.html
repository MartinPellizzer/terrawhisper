What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Schizostachyum chinense has woody stems, elliptical to lanceolate leaves with entire margins, and small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers arranged in axillary racemes. The leaves are typically arranged alternately along the branches and possess a coriaceous texture. Its inflorescence consists of multiple small flowers that lack showy petals, characteristic of many members in the family Schisandraceae. The fruit develops into a cluster of follicles that eventually dehisce to reveal bright red seeds. These morphological features help distinguish the species within its natural forest habitats.
