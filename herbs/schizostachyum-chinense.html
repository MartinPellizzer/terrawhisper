What's the taxonomical classification of Schizostachyum chinense?

Schizostachyum chinense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Poales. This plant is a member of the family Poaceae, which places it within the genus Schizostachyum, and its specific biological identity is defined by the species designation chinense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Schizostachyum chinense has woody stems, elliptical to lanceolate leaves with entire margins, and small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers arranged in axillary racemes.

The leaves are typically arranged alternately along the branches and possess a coriaceous texture. Its inflorescence consists of multiple small flowers that lack showy petals, characteristic of many members in the family Schisandraceae. The fruit develops into a cluster of follicles that eventually dehisce to reveal bright red seeds.
