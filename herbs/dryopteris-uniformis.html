What's the taxonomical classification of Dryopteris uniformis?

Dryopteris uniformis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. The plant is a member of the family Polypodiaceae and falls under the genus Dryopteris, ultimately being identified by its specific species name, uniformis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dryopteris uniformis has large, bipinnate to tripinnate fronds that can reach up to 1.5 meters in length, featuring dark brown to blackish stipes and bright green pinnae. The fronds are arranged in a dense, upright rosette that emerges from a short, stout rhizome covered in pale scales. Each leaflet is typically lanceolate in shape with finely serrated margins that contribute to a delicate, feathery texture. The underside of the mature fronds displays prominent sori arranged in two rows along the veins of the pinnules. These morphological characteristics allow the plant to thrive in moist, shaded woodland environments.
