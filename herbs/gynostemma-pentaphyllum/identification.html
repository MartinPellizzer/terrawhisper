Botanical Identification of Jiaogulan (Gynostemma pentaphyllum)

To identify Jiaogulan (Gynostemma pentaphyllum) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Jiaogulan, or Gynostemma pentaphyllum, is classified within the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Liliopsida, order Cucurbitales, family Cucurbitaceae, genus Gynostemma, and species Gynostemma pentaphyllum. This places it in the same family as cucumbers and melons, highlighting its botanical relationships.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Jiaogulan is Gynostemma pentaphyllum, first described by the botanist J. S. Gamble in 1836. It is also known by several synonyms, including Gynostemma pentaphyllum var. pentaphyllum and Gynostemma pentaphyllum var. pentaphyllum. Common names include Jiaogulan, five-leaf gynostemma, and Chinese ginseng. It is widely recognized in traditional Chinese medicine and has been used for centuries in various regions of China.

Morphological Characteristics

Jiaogulan is a climbing vine with a fibrous root system that anchors it to support structures. Its stems are slender, twining, and covered in fine hairs. Leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and have a slightly serrated edge. The inflorescence consists of clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The fruit is a three-lobed capsule containing small, black seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Jiaogulan can be distinguished by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and five-lobed flowers. It has a distinct scent and produces small, yellowish fruits. These features help differentiate it from similar species like Gynostemma sibiricum. Its unique leaf shape and flowering pattern are key diagnostic traits.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Jiaogulan is native to Southeast Asia, particularly China, Korea, and Japan. It thrives in warm, humid climates with mild winters. It prefers well-drained, loamy soil and is commonly found at altitudes between 500 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Jiaogulan blooms from June to August, producing yellowish-white flowers. Seed formation occurs from September to November. For identification purposes, the plant is typically harvested during its vegetative growth phase in spring and early summer, before flowering.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Jiaogulan (Gynostemma pentaphyllum) is closely related to species within the Cucurbitaceae family, particularly those in the genus Gynostemma. It shares key morphological and genetic traits with other members of the family, such as leaf structure and reproductive features. Its phylogenetic position highlights its evolutionary ties to other climbing plants in the Cucurbitaceae group.
