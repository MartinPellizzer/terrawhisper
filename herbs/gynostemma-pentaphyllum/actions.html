Jiaogulan (Gynostemma pentaphyllum) Actions

Jiaogulan exhibits several therapeutic actions including tonic, anti-inflammatory, and diuretic properties. It acts as a stimulant and carminative, aiding digestion and reducing gas. The herb also functions as an expectorant and sedative, helping with respiratory issues and calming the nervous system. Its antispasmodic and astringent effects support muscle relaxation and tissue tightening.

Jiaogulan (Gynostemma pentaphyllum) Monograph

1. Tonic

Jiaogulan has a tonic action, enhancing energy and vitality by supporting the body's overall function. It is used to strengthen the immune system and improve physical endurance. This effect is attributed to its rich content of saponins and other bioactive compounds. It is commonly used in traditional herbal medicine to promote general well-being.

2. Anti-inflammatory

Jiaogulan has an anti-inflammatory action, which helps reduce inflammation in the body by modulating inflammatory pathways. It contains compounds like saponins and flavonoids that inhibit pro-inflammatory cytokines. This property supports its use in managing conditions like arthritis and inflammatory bowel disease.

3. Bitter

Jiaogulan has a bitter action, which supports digestion and detoxification by stimulating gastric secretions and enhancing metabolic processes. This property helps in clearing heat and reducing inflammation, making it beneficial for digestive and liver health.

4. Stimulant

Jiaogulan has a stimulant action, enhancing energy and alertness by increasing metabolic rate and improving circulation. It supports mental clarity and physical endurance, making it useful for fatigue and low energy. This effect is attributed to its bioactive compounds, including saponins and polysaccharides.

5. Diuretic

Jiaogulan has a diuretic action, promoting the elimination of excess water and salts through urine. This effect helps reduce fluid retention and supports kidney function. It is commonly used in traditional medicine to manage edema and hypertension. The diuretic properties are attributed to its bioactive compounds.

6. Carminative

Jiaogulan has a carminative action, meaning it helps to relieve gas and bloating in the digestive system. It promotes the expulsion of trapped air, reducing discomfort and improving digestion. This effect is attributed to its bioactive compounds that support healthy gastrointestinal function.

7. Expectorant

Jiaogulan has an expectorant action, helping to loosen and expel mucus from the respiratory tract. It stimulates the secretion of bronchial fluids, making it easier to cough up phlegm. This property makes it beneficial for conditions involving excess mucus production, such as bronchitis or asthma.

8. Sedative

Jiaogulan has a sedative action, helping to reduce anxiety and promote relaxation. It works by modulating the nervous system, possibly through its bioactive compounds like sesquiterpene lactones. This effect supports its traditional use in calming the mind and improving sleep quality.

9. Antispasmodic

Jiaogulan has an antispasmodic action, helping to relieve muscle spasms by relaxing smooth muscle tissues. This effect is attributed to its bioactive compounds, which may modulate nerve-muscle interactions. It is commonly used in traditional medicine to address conditions involving involuntary muscle contractions.

10. Astringent

Jiaogulan has an astringent action, which helps reduce secretion and tighten tissues. This property is useful in treating conditions like diarrhea and excessive sweating. It also supports wound healing by promoting tissue repair and reducing inflammation.
