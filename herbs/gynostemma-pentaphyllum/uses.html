Jiaogulan (Gynostemma pentaphyllum) Uses

Jiaogulan is used to treat fatigue and chronic fatigue syndrome by boosting energy levels. It helps with insomnia by promoting relaxation and improving sleep quality. The herb also supports cardiovascular health by reducing high blood pressure and cholesterol. Additionally, it may aid in managing diabetes and metabolic syndrome by improving insulin sensitivity and reducing inflammation.

Jiaogulan (Gynostemma pentaphyllum) Monograph

1. Fatigue

Jiaogulan is traditionally used to combat fatigue due to its adaptogenic properties. It helps enhance energy levels and reduce mental and physical exhaustion. The herb supports the body's ability to cope with stress, making it a valuable remedy for persistent tiredness. Its effects are attributed to compounds like gypenosides, which may improve endurance and overall vitality.

2. Insomnia

Jiaogulan is used to alleviate insomnia due to its adaptogenic properties that reduce stress and promote relaxation. It helps regulate the nervous system, improving sleep quality. Traditionally, it is brewed as a tea or taken as a supplement to support restful sleep. Its calming effects make it a natural remedy for sleep disorders.

3. Chronic fatigue syndrome

Jiaogulan is used to support energy levels in chronic fatigue syndrome due to its adaptogenic properties. It helps reduce fatigue and enhance stamina by balancing the body's stress response. Clinical studies suggest it may improve physical and mental endurance in individuals with prolonged fatigue. Its effects are attributed to compounds like ginsenosides and polysaccharides.

4. High blood pressure

Jiaogulan has been traditionally used to support cardiovascular health. It contains compounds that may help regulate blood pressure by promoting relaxation of blood vessels. Studies suggest it may reduce both systolic and diastolic readings. Its adaptogenic properties contribute to its hypotensive effects.

5. High cholesterol

Jiaogulan has been studied for its potential to lower high cholesterol by reducing LDL (bad cholesterol) and increasing HDL (good cholesterol). Its saponins and other bioactive compounds may enhance liver function and promote cholesterol metabolism, supporting cardiovascular health. Clinical research suggests it may be a complementary therapy for managing cholesterol levels when used alongside conventional treatments.

6. Hypertension

Jiaogulan has been studied for its potential to support blood pressure regulation. Its bioactive compounds, including gypenosides, may help relax blood vessels and reduce vascular resistance. Some research suggests it may lower systolic and diastolic blood pressure, though more clinical trials are needed to confirm its efficacy in treating hypertension.

7. Diabetes

Jiaogulan has been studied for its potential to support blood sugar regulation. It contains compounds that may enhance insulin sensitivity and reduce glucose absorption. Some research suggests it may help lower fasting blood sugar levels. However, it should not replace prescribed diabetes treatments without medical supervision.

8. Metabolic syndrome

Jiaogulan has been studied for its potential to support metabolic health. It may help regulate blood sugar, reduce cholesterol, and improve insulin sensitivity, which are key factors in metabolic syndrome. Its bioactive compounds, including gypenosides, are believed to contribute to these effects. Clinical research suggests it may aid in managing weight and reducing inflammation associated with the condition.

9. Hypothyroidism

Jiaogulan has been studied for its potential to support thyroid function. It contains compounds that may help regulate thyroid hormone production. Preliminary research suggests it may assist in managing hypothyroidism by enhancing metabolic activity. However, more clinical trials are needed to confirm its efficacy.

10. Inflammation

Jiaogulan exhibits anti-inflammatory properties due to its rich content of saponins and polyphenols. These compounds help reduce oxidative stress and inhibit inflammatory pathways. It is used traditionally to alleviate symptoms of chronic inflammation and support immune function. Clinical studies suggest it may aid in managing conditions like arthritis and inflammatory bowel disease.
