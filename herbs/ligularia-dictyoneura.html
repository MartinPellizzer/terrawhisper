Ligularia dictyoneura

What's the taxonomical classification of Ligularia dictyoneura?

Ligularia dictyoneura belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Asterales. As a member of the family Asteraceae, it is categorized under the genus Ligularia, with its specific designation being the species dictyoneura.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ligularia dictyoneara has large, ovate to broadly lanceolate leaves characterized by prominent, reticulate venation and serrated margins. These leaves are typically held on long, sturdy petioles that emerge from a central stem. The leaf surfaces can vary in texture, sometimes appearing slightly rugose or textured due to the deep vein patterns. During the flowering stage, the plant produces terminal clusters of bright yellow ray florets that form composite heads. The overall structure of the plant is robust, often reaching significant heights in its native montane habitats.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

These populations are often located at elevations ranging from 2,500 to 4,000 meters above sea level. The species thrives in the cool, moist climates characteristic of these mountainous ecosystems.
