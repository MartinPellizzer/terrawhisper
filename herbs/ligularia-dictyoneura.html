Ligularia dictyoneura

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ligularia dictyoneara has large, ovate to broadly lanceolate leaves characterized by prominent, reticulate venation and serrated margins. These leaves are typically held on long, sturdy petioles that emerge from a central stem. The leaf surfaces can vary in texture, sometimes appearing slightly rugose or textured due to the deep vein patterns. During the flowering stage, the plant produces terminal clusters of bright yellow ray florets that form composite heads. The overall structure of the plant is robust, often reaching significant heights in its native montane habitats.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of central and western China, specifically occurring within the provinces of Sichuan, Yunnan, and Tibet. It is typically found growing in alpine or subalpine environments where the terrain is rocky and well-drained. These populations are often located at elevations ranging from 2,500 to 4,000 meters above sea level. The species thrives in the cool, moist climates characteristic of these mountainous ecosystems. Due to its specific habitat requirements, its range is quite restricted to these particular montane zones.
