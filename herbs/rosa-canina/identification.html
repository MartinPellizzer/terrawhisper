Botanical Identification of Dog Rose (Rosa canina)

To identify Dog Rose (Rosa canina) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

The botanical identification of Dog Rose (Rosa canina) places it in the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Rosales, family Rosaceae, genus Rosa, and species Rosa canina. This classification system helps in understanding its evolutionary relationships and biological characteristics within the plant kingdom.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Dog Rose is Rosa canina, first described by Carl Linnaeus in his 1753 publication Species Plantarum. It has several synonyms, including Rosa sylvestris and Rosa fœcunda, reflecting historical classification variations. Common names include Dog Rose, Wild Rose, and Scotch Rose, while regional names vary across Europe. Historically, it was also known as the "Beggar's Rose" in some areas.

Morphological Characteristics

Dog Rose (Rosa canina) is a shrub with a sprawling growth habit, often forming thickets. Its root system is fibrous and extensive, aiding in soil stabilization. The stems are thorny and reddish-brown, with a woody structure. Leaves are pinnately compound, with 5-7 leaflets, and are serrated along the edges. The inflorescence consists of solitary flowers at the ends of branches. Flowers are pink to red, with five petals and numerous stamens. The fruit is a red, hairy rose hip, containing numerous achenes surrounded by a fleshy pulp.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Dog Rose (Rosa canina) can be distinguished by its pink to white flowers with five petals, red stems with thorns, and oval, hairy leaves with serrated edges. It produces red-orange hips in autumn, which are distinct from the similar Rosa rugosa, which has darker hips and more robust growth.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Dog Rose (Rosa canina) is native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa. It thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils. It typically grows at elevations between 0 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Dog Rose blooms from late spring to early summer, typically between May and July. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early autumn, around August to September. For identification purposes, the most accurate harvest timing is during the flowering period when the plant is most distinctive.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Rosa canina belongs to the genus Rosa, which is part of the family Rosaceae. It is closely related to other species like Rosa rugosa and Rosa gallica. These species share similar morphological and genetic traits, including thorny stems, compound leaves, and five-petaled flowers.
