Dog Rose (Rosa canina) Uses

Dog Rose is used to treat respiratory conditions such as cough, cold, bronchitis, and asthma. It helps reduce inflammation in the lungs and soothe sore throats. It is also beneficial for managing symptoms of influenza and common colds. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties support recovery from respiratory infections.

Dog Rose (Rosa canina) Monograph

1. Cough

Rosa canina is used to support respiratory health, particularly for coughs. Its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties help loosen mucus and reduce irritation in the throat. It is often prepared as a syrup or tea to soothe persistent or dry coughs. Regular use may aid in clearing respiratory passages and easing breathing.

2. Cold

Dog Rose is used to treat colds due to its high vitamin C content, which supports immune function. It helps reduce symptoms like sore throat and congestion. Infusions or tinctures are commonly prepared for internal use. Its mild nature makes it suitable for both adults and children.

3. Inflamed lungs

Rosa canina has been traditionally used to support lung health, particularly in cases of inflammation. Its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties help reduce irritation and ease respiratory discomfort. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to soothe inflamed lungs and promote healing.

4. Asthma

Rosa canina has been traditionally used to support respiratory health. Its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties may help alleviate asthma symptoms by reducing bronchial inflammation and promoting mucus clearance. It is often used in herbal formulations for chronic respiratory conditions. However, it should complement, not replace, conventional asthma treatments.

5. Influenza

Rosa canina, or Dog Rose, is used in herbal medicine to support the immune system during influenza. It contains vitamins and antioxidants that may help reduce fever and soothe respiratory symptoms. Its anti-inflammatory properties can ease congestion and support recovery. It is often prepared as a tea or syrup to aid in managing flu symptoms.

6. Cold symptoms

Rosa canina is used to alleviate cold symptoms due to its antiviral and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce fever, soothe sore throats, and ease congestion. Often prepared as a tea, it supports the immune system and promotes respiratory health during colds. Its mild nature makes it suitable for regular use in cold season.

7. Bronchitis

Rosa canina is used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health, particularly for bronchitis. It helps soothe inflamed airways and reduce mucus production. Its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties may ease coughing and improve breathing. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture.

8. Sore throat

Rosa canina is used to soothe sore throats due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce swelling and discomfort in the throat. Often prepared as a tea, it can be consumed regularly to support respiratory health and ease symptoms of throat irritation.

9. Common cold

Rosa canina is traditionally used to support the immune system and alleviate symptoms of the common cold. It contains antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds that may reduce throat irritation and congestion. Its use is often combined with other herbs in herbal formulations for cold relief. It is typically prepared as a tea or tincture.

10. Respiratory infections

Dog Rose is used to support respiratory health by reducing inflammation and easing congestion. Its high vitamin C content boosts immune function, helping the body fight off infections. It is often made into teas or syrups to soothe coughs and bronchial irritation. Regular use may help prevent and alleviate symptoms of colds and flu.
