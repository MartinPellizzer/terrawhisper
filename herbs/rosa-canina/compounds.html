Dog Rose (Rosa canina) Compounds

The primary compounds in Dog Rose include flavonoids, which act as antioxidants, and tannins, which have astringent properties. Phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory effects, while glycosides and saponins support immune function. Coumarin and terpenoids offer anticoagulant and antimicrobial benefits, and anthocyanins provide vibrant color and additional antioxidant support.

Dog Rose (Rosa canina) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Rosa canina. They contribute to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to reduce oxidative stress and inflammation. These compounds support the immune system and may aid in cardiovascular health. Their presence enhances the therapeutic potential of Dog Rose in herbal medicine.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Dog Rose (Rosa canina). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins in Dog Rose are primarily found in the fruit and leaves, supporting its traditional use in digestive and skin-related ailments.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Dog Rose (Rosa canina). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports the plant's traditional use in supporting immune function and cardiovascular health.

4. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Dog Rose (Rosa canina). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Glycosides in Rosa canina support immune function and may aid in wound healing. These compounds are typically found in the flowers and hips of the plant.

5. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Dog Rose (Rosa canina). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and immune-modulating properties. Saponin also aids in the emulsification of fats, supporting digestive health. This compound plays a significant role in the therapeutic applications of Dog Rose in herbal medicine.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Dog Rose (Rosa canina). It contributes to the plant's anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarin is also responsible for the characteristic scent of the plant. Its presence supports the traditional use of Dog Rose in supporting cardiovascular health.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Dog Rose (Rosa canina) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help support immune function and may aid in skin health. They are commonly found in the plant's flowers and hips, which are used in traditional herbal remedies. Terpenoids play a key role in the plant's therapeutic effects.

8. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Rosa canina, known for its antioxidant properties. It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and immune-supporting effects. This compound helps protect cells from oxidative stress and supports overall health. Anthocyanin is also responsible for the red and purple pigments in the rose hips.

9. Essential oil

The essential oil of Dog Rose (Rosa canina) contains volatile aromatic compounds like geraniol and citral. These contribute to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It is used in herbal medicine for respiratory support and skin health. The oil is extracted through steam distillation from the flowers and leaves.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Dog Rose (Rosa canina). While alkaloids are more commonly associated with other medicinal plants, Dog Rose contains trace amounts of alkaloids that contribute to its overall therapeutic profile. These compounds may play a role in the plant's anti-inflammatory and immune-modulating effects.
