1. Fight inflammation

Inflammation is a bodily response to injury or infection, often characterized by redness, swelling, and pain.

It can be chronic or acute, and may result from prolonged exposure to irritants or autoimmune reactions.

Chronic inflammation is linked to a range of diseases, including arthritis, cardiovascular issues, and metabolic disorders.

Rosa canina, also known as dog rose, contains bioactive compounds that may help reduce inflammatory markers in the body.

This plant is particularly beneficial for individuals suffering from long-term inflammatory conditions, offering a natural approach to managing symptoms.

How this herb helps with fight inflammation?

Rosa canina fight inflammation because it contains bioactive constituents such as flavonoids and alkaloids.

These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory properties by inhibiting pro-inflammatory enzymes like COX-1 and COX-2. Flavonoids in Rosa canina help reduce oxidative stress, which is closely linked to inflammatory processes. Alkaloids contribute to the plant's ability to modulate immune responses and suppress inflammation.

Overall, these natural compounds make Rosa canina a valuable source for supporting inflammatory conditions.

Scientific Research

According to "International journal of molecular sciences", Rosa canina has been found to possess properties that may help fight inflammation in a positive way.

Regular consumption of rosa canina may support overall health and reduce the risk of this deficiency-related illness.

How this herb helps with prevent scurvy?

Rosa canina prevent scurvy because it contains high levels of vitamin C, which is essential for the synthesis of collagen and the maintenance of healthy skin, blood vessels, and immune function.

These compounds help neutralize free radicals and support the body's natural defenses against oxidative stress. Additionally, the presence of these phytochemicals contributes to the plant's overall therapeutic value in preventing and treating vitamin C deficiency.

Rosa canina is therefore a valuable natural source of nutrients that support overall health and prevent scurvy.
