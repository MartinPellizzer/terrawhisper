What is Urera baccifera?

Urera baccifera is a plant species in the family Urticaceae, commonly known as the wild spinach or wild bittercress.

It is a perennial herb with a woody root system, growing up to 1 meter in height, and has heart-shaped, pointed leaves that are 5-12 cm long and 3-6 cm wide, with a green to dark green color. The leaves are arranged oppositely on the stem and are covered with fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in panicles, which are 2-4 cm long, and are pollinated by bees and other insects.

Urera baccifera is native to the Caribbean and Central and South America, and is often used as a food source in some regions.
