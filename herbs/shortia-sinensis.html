What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Shortia sinensis has small, evergreen leaves that are typically oval or elliptical in shape, possessing serrated margins and a glossy, dark green upper surface. The foliage is densely arranged along woody stems that form a low-growing, mounded shrub habit. During the blooming season, the plant produces bell-shaped flowers that vary in color from pure white to pale pink. These blossoms emerge from the leaf axils and feature prominent, contrasting stamens at the center. The root system is relatively shallow, which is characteristic of many species within the Ericaceae family.
