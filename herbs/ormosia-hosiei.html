What's the taxonomical classification of Ormosia hosiei?

Ormosia hosiei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic lineage further, it resides in the order Fabales and the family Fabaceae. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Ormosia and the species hosiei.

    What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

    This plant contains a diverse range of medicinal compounds including cytisine-like and angustifoline type alkaloids, cytisine derivatives, and specific glycosides known as hosimosine A-E. These cytisine-like alkaloids and cytisine derivatives often demonstrate significant pharmacological activities within the human body. The presence of angustifoline type alkaloids further contributes to the complex chemical profile of the species. Additionally, the unique hosimosine A-E compounds provide specialized metabolic characteristics to the plant's overall composition. These various chemical constituents work together to define the plant's medicinal potential.

    As discussed in a study, the seeds of Ormosia hosiei Hemsl. et Wils contain cytisine-like and angustifoline type alkaloids. Researchers isolated 4 new angustifoline type alkaloids (1-4) and 1 new cytisine-like alkaloid (5), named hosimosine A-E, along with 13 known analogues (6-18). These compounds were used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat conditions such as hernia, abdominal pain, blood stasis, and amenorrhea. Certain isolates from the plant exhibited moderate cytotoxic effects against 3 specific cell lines, namely HepG2, A2780, and MCF-7. Compounds 1-4 were identified as 2 pairs of epimers through the use of various spectroscopic methods and calculations.

        What side effects this plant can have?

        This plant can cause severe poisoning through the ingestion of abrins-like toxalbumins contained within its seeds. Once consumed, these toxic proteins trigger immediate gastrointestinal distress including nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and intense abdominal pain. If a significant amount is ingested, the toxins can lead to systemic complications and extensive organ damage. Such physiological damage often targets the liver, kidneys, and other vital systems. Monitoring medical symptoms is essential due to the potentially lethal nature of these substances.
