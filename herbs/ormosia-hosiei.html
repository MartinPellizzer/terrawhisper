What's the taxonomical classification of Ormosia hosiei?

Ormosia hosiei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic lineage further, it resides in the order Fabales and the family Fabaceae. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Ormosia and the species hosiei.

Ormosia hosiei is prepared primarily as a decoction through boiling the dried seeds or bark in water to extract its medicinal components. In some regional practices, the plant material is ground into a fine powder to be mixed into capsules or swallowed directly with liquid. Some preparations involve creating a topical paste by crushing the plant matter with a small amount of water for external application. These diverse methods allow practitioners to utilize the specific bioactive compounds found within the species.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Ormosia hosiei can cause severe poisoning if the seeds are ingested due to the presence of toxic glycosides known as toxalbumins. These chemical compounds can lead to serious symptoms such as vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea. In high doses, the ingestion of these seeds may result in more critical complications like respiratory failure or death. While the plant is primarily dangerous through its seeds, handling the plant requires caution to prevent accidental exposure. It is essential to avoid all consumption of any part of this species to ensure safety.
