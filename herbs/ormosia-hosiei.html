Ormosia hosiei

What's the taxonomical classification of Ormosia hosiei?

Ormosia hosiei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic lineage further, it resides in the order Fabales and the family Fabaceae. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Ormosia and the species hosiei.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ormosia hosiei has woody stems, pinnately compound leaves with multiple pairs of leaflets, and distinctive bright red seeds encased in dark pods. The leaves are typically arranged alternately along the branches and consist of relatively small, oval-shaped leaflets. Each leaflet possesses a smooth margin and a pointed tip, contributing to the overall delicate texture of the foliage. The most striking morphological feature remains the seed pods, which eventually split to reveal vibrant, glossy seeds. These seeds serve as a key identifying characteristic of the species within its natural habitat.

What are the pharmacological activities of Ormosia hosiei?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological activities including analgesic, anti-neuroinflammatory, anti-tyrosinase, anti-alpha-glucosidase, and antioxidant properties. Its antioxidant capabilities help mitigate oxidative stress within biological systems by neutralizing free radicals. The anti-neuroinflammatory and analgesic effects suggest potential applications in managing pain and protecting the nervous system from inflammation. Furthermore, the inhibition of tyrosinase and alpha-glucosidase indicates its utility in skin lightening and glycemic control research. These diverse bioactive properties highlight the medicinal potential of Ormosia hosiei extracts.

According to a study published by "Fitoterapia", Ormosia hosiei Hemsl. et Wils. is an arbor species endemic to China whose seeds are used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat hernia, abdominal pain, blood stasis, and amenorrhea. The seeds contain main components identified as cytisine-like and angustifoline type alkaloids. Researchers isolated 4 new angustifoline type alkaloids (1-4), 1 new cytisine-like alkaloid (5) named hosimosine A-E, and 13 known analogues (6-18). Among these, compounds 1-4 were identified as two pairs of epimers. Furthermore, some of the isolates exhibited moderate cytotoxic effects against HepG2, A2780, and MCF-7 cells.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause severe poisoning through the ingestion of abrins-like toxalbumins contained within its seeds. Once consumed, these toxic proteins trigger immediate gastrointestinal distress including nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and intense abdominal pain. If a significant amount is ingested, the toxins can lead to systemic complications and extensive organ damage. Such physiological damage often targets the liver, kidneys, and other vital systems. Monitoring medical symptoms is essential due to the potentially lethal nature of these substances.
