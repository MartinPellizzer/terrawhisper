Malus pumila

What's the taxonomical classification of Malus pumila?

Malus pumila belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Rosales. This plant is a member of the family Rosaceae, which places it within the genus Malus, and it is specifically identified by the species name pumila.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Malus pumila contains various bioactive compounds including quercetin, chlorogenic acid, procyanidins, and various dietary fibers that provide significant health benefits. These polyphenols function as potent antioxidants that help neutralize free radicals within the body. The presence of pectin serves as a soluble fiber that can assist in regulating cholesterol levels and improving digestive health. Additionally, the plant provides essential vitamins such as vitamin C and various minerals like potassium. These combined elements contribute to anti-inflammatory and cardioprotective properties in human nutrition.

What are the therapeutic actions of Malus pumila?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat digestive issues, respiratory ailments, and certain cardiovascular conditions through its bioactive compounds. The high pectin content in the fruit helps regulate bowel movements and can assist in managing cholesterol levels. Flavonoids and polyphenols present in the plant also provide antioxidant support that may mitigate oxidative stress in the lungs. Some traditional practices utilize the fruit to soothe minor inflammatory responses in the throat. Additionally, its nutritional profile contributes to overall metabolic health and immune system maintenance.
