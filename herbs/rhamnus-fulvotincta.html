What's the taxonomical classification of Rhamnus fulvotincta?

Rhamnus fulvotincta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Rosales. This plant is a member of the family Rhamnaceae, which contains the genus Rhamnus, and is specifically identified by its unique species name, fulvotincta.

Rhamnus fulvotincta targets various enzymes and signaling proteins, most notably cyclooxygenase-2, lipoxygenase, and pro-inflammatory cytokines like tumor necrosis factor-alpha. Rhamnus fulvotincta has therapeutic properties that function as a bitter, aperient, cholagogue, and stomachic agent to support digestive health. Furthermore, the herb acts as a cholagogue by promoting the flow of bile from the gallbladder into the small intestine. These combined actions help regulate the digestive processes and support liver function.
