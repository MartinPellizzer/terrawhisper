Rhamnus fulvotincta

What's the taxonomical classification of Rhamnus fulvotincta?

Rhamnus fulvotincta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Rosales. This plant is a member of the family Rhamnaceae, which contains the genus Rhamnus, and is specifically identified by its unique species name, fulvotincta.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhamnus fulvotincta has an erect shrub habit characterized by slender, grayish-brown branches and simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical with finely serrated margins. The leaf texture is often leathery, and the venation remains prominent on the undersides. Small, inconspicuous greenish flowers emerge in clusters from the leaf axils during the blooming season. These flowers eventually develop into small, fleshy drupes that transition from green to a dark reddish or blackish hue when fully ripe. The overall architecture of the plant provides a dense, twiggy appearance common to many members of the Rhamnaceae family.
