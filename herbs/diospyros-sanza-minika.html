Diospyros sanza-minika

What's the taxonomical classification of Diospyros sanza-minika?

Diospyros sanza-minika belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving through its higher taxonomic ranks, it falls under the order Ericales and is a member of the family Ebenaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Diospyros and is specifically designated as the species sanza-minika.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Diospyros sanza-minika contains bioactive compounds primarily located in its leaves, bark, and roots that are utilized for various therapeutic purposes. These different plant components work together to provide a broad range of medicinal applications.

    This plant is used to treat malaria, skin infections, stomach aches, and various types of inflammation. Local communities often prepare decoctions from the leaves or bark to combat parasitic infections like malaria. The plant's extracts are also applied topically to soothe skin irritations and promote healing of wounds. When ingested in controlled amounts, it serves as a traditional remedy for gastrointestinal distress and abdominal pain.     Diospyros sanza-minika is prepared as a decoction made from its leaves, a decoction from its bark, or as a fresh juice extracted from the fruit. These various preparations are utilized by local populations to treat a range of ailments including stomach pains and skin issues. Each specific part of the plant yields a different formulation depending on the intended therapeutic effect.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Diospyros sanza-minika can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, including intense nausea, vomiting, and abdominal cramping, if the unripe fruit or certain parts of the plant are ingested. These adverse reactions are often linked to the presence of glycosides that disrupt digestive processes. Some individuals may also experience localized skin irritation or allergic dermatitis upon direct contact with the plant's sap or foliage. Prolonged exposure to these elements can lead to swelling or itching in sensitive individuals. Monitoring for these symptoms is essential when interacting with any part of this species.
