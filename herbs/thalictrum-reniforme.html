What's the taxonomical classification of Thalictrum reniforme?

Thalictrum reniforme belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Ranunculales. This plant is a member of the family Ranunculaceae, which places it within the genus Thalictrum, and it is specifically identified by its species name, reniforme.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Thalictrum reniforme has kidney-shaped to orbicular leaves that are deeply lobed and characterized by a prominent, palmate venation pattern. These basal leaves are typically large and fleshy, providing a distinct silhouette compared to other species in the genus. The stems are slender and erect, supporting clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers that lack true petals. Instead, the plant features showy, petal-like sepals that are often pale yellow or greenish in color. The reproductive structures consist of numerous stamens surrounding a central group of carpels that eventually develop into small achenes.
