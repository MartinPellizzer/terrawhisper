Senna smithiana

Senna smithiana, commonly known as Snow Queen senna, is a plant species belonging to the Fabaceae family, native to Mexico and Central America.

It has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, particularly in treating gastrointestinal issues such as constipation, diarrhea, and irritable bowel syndrome due to its health benefits.

The therapeutic actions of Senna smithiana include its laxative properties, which are attributed to its bioactive constituents, including anthraquinone glycosides, such as sennosides A and B.

These bioactive constituents are responsible for the plant's ability to stimulate bowel movements, relax the muscles in the intestines, and reduce inflammation in the digestive tract.
