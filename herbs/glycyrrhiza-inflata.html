Glycyrrhiza inflata

What's the taxonomical classification of Glycyrrhiza inflata?

Glycyrrhiza inflata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Fabales and into the family Fabaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by the genus Glycyrrhiza and is specifically designated by the species name inflata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Glycyrrhiza inflata has a perennial, woody-based herbaceous habit characterized by erect, unbranched stems that reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The leaves are pinnately compound, featuring numerous small, oblong leaflets that are arranged oppositely along the rachis. Its distinctive inflated pods are oblong-ellipsoid, often displaying a slightly swollen appearance due to their internal structure. The flowers are typically small, pea-like, and range in color from white to pale violet. These structures are supported by a robust, deep-reaching taproot system that serves as a primary storage organ for the plant.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, spanning from southern Utah and Arizona through New Mexico and into Texas. It thrives primarily in arid environments such as desert scrublands, canyons, and rocky slopes. Within these regions, it is frequently found in alkaline soils and well-drained areas typical of the Mojave and Sonoran deserts. Its distribution follows specific elevation gradients where seasonal moisture allows for its biennial growth cycle. This species remains localized to these subtropical and semi-arid western territories.

What are the pharmacological activities of Glycyrrhiza inflata?

This plant has anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, antioxidant, antiviral, and hepatoprotective pharmacological activities. Its bioactive compounds work to suppress inflammatory responses by inhibiting specific chemical mediators in the body. The plant also exhibits potent antimicrobial properties that help combat various bacterial and fungal pathogens. Additionally, its antioxidant capacity neutralizes free radicals, while its antiviral effects provide a defense against certain viral infections. Finally, the medicinal properties of Glycyrrhiza inflata contribute to hepatoprotective functions by shielding liver cells from toxic damage.

As discussed in a study, licochalcone A, which is 3-a,a-dimethylallyl-4,4'-dihydroxy-6-methoxychalcone, was isolated from the root of Glycyrrhiza inflata Beta. This substance showed anti-inflammatory action towards mouse ear edema induced by arachidonic acid (AA) and 12-O-tetradecanoylphorbol 13-acetate (TPA) through topical application. An anti-tumour promoting action was also observed in vivo for mouse skin papilloma initiated by dimethylbenz[a]anthracene (DMBA) and promoted by TPA. Furthermore, it inhibited in vitro 32Pi-incorporation to phospholipids in HeLa cells promoted by TPA. A competitive interaction of licochalcone A with the TPA-receptors in the cell membrane has been suggested.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds glycyrrhizin, liquiritigenin, glabridin, licochalcone A, and isoliquiritigenin. Glycyrrhizin is the primary component responsible for the intense sweetness of the root. Liquiritigenin and isoliquiritigenin are flavonoids that contribute to its anti-inflammatory properties. Glabridin acts as a potent antioxidant and is frequently studied for its skin-lightening effects. Additionally, licochalcone A provides significant antimicrobial activity against various pathogens.

As discussed in a study, Glycyrrhiza inflata contains important bioactive compounds including glycyrrhizin and the characteristic flavonoid licochalcone A (LCA). The high-quality genomic sequence data for this species has a size of 447 Mb. Researchers identified key transcription factors GibHLH9, GibHLH53, and GibHLH174 that promote glycyrrhizin by transactivating the expression of GiCSyGT and GiUGT73P12. Furthermore, the function of GiOMT17 was confirmed in vivo and in vitro regarding its role in the biosynthesis of LCA. These findings offer valuable gene resources for the production of high-yield active ingredients.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause significant physiological disturbances such as hypertension, hypokalemia, edema, nausea, and dizziness. Consuming high amounts of its compounds can lead to an imbalance of electrolytes, specifically resulting in dangerously low potassium levels known as hypokalemia. This electrolyte depletion often triggers secondary symptoms like dizziness and muscle weakness. Furthermore, the plant is known to promote sodium and water retention, which manifests as edema and elevated blood pressure or hypertension. These adverse reactions can be severe if the plant is ingested in large quantities over a prolonged period.

  • Dizziness
  • Hypertension
  • Hypokalemia
