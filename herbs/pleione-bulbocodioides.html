Pleione bulbocodioides

What's the taxonomical classification of Pleione bulbocodioides?

Pleione bulbocoidioides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Asparagales. This plant is a member of the family Orchidaceae, falling under the genus Pleione, and is specifically identified by its species name, bulbocoidioides.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pleione bulbocoedioides has a pseudobulbous structure characterized by an ovoid fleshy base, two to three lanceolate leaves, and a single terminal inflorescence bearing multiple fragrant pink or white flowers with distinct purple spots. The pseudobulbs serve as essential storage organs for nutrients and water during periods of dormancy. Each leaf is typically bright green and features parallel venation common to monocots. The flowering spike emerges from the center of the foliage, showcasing zygomorphic blossoms that are adapted for specific pollinators. Below the soil surface, a creeping rhizome helps the plant spread through its natural woodland habitat.

What are the pharmacological activities of Pleione bulbocodioides?

This plant has anti-inflammatory, anti-lung-cancer, anti-tumor, clearing heat, and detoxification pharmacological activities. Its bioactive compounds work to suppress abnormal cell proliferation, making it a subject of interest for treating various types of tumors. According to a study published by "Molecular immunology", Pleione bulbocoedioides is a CITES Appendix II-listed species used in dermatological therapies. Extracts from artificially cultivated P. bulbocoedioides significantly suppressed the TNF-α/IFN-γ-induced expression of IL-6, IL-1β, CCL5, CCL8, CXCL8, CXCL3, and TMEM173 (STING) genes in HaCaT cells and NHEKs. Phytochemical characterization identified militariine and batatasin III as the principal bioactive constituents responsible for inhibiting the STING/NF-κB signaling pathway. The P. bulbocoedioides extract demonstrated comparable anti-inflammatory efficacy in LPS-induced RAW 264.7 macrophages and SLS-irritated 3D reconstructed human epidermis. These findings suggest the potential of the extract as a natural anti-inflammatory therapeutic or skincare ingredient.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several bioactive medicinal compounds including batatasin III, dactylorhin A, militarine, and militariine. These specific chemical constituents are primarily categorized as sesquiterpenoid glycosides and alkaloids. Researchers study these substances due to their potential pharmacological activities such as anti-inflammatory and cytotoxic properties. The presence of these unique molecules makes Pleione bulbocoidioides a significant subject in ethnopharmacology. Understanding the structure of each compound helps scientists develop more effective natural therapies.

According to a study published by "Chemistry & biodiversity", the medicinal orchid Pleione bulbocoedioides contains three active compounds which are dactylohrin A, militariine and batatasin III. The accumulation of these three active compounds is significantly influenced by light intensity and irrigation frequency. High light levels of 65% of full sunlight or drought stress from a 14-day irrigation interval increased active compound accumulation. The highest total content of these three active compounds was observed in plants subjected to a 3-day irrigation interval with high light levels. To balance quality and quantity, it is recommended to use 30 to 65% of full sunlight while avoiding drought stress.
