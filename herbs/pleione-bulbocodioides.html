Pleione bulbocodioides

What's the taxonomical classification of Pleione bulbocodioides?

Pleione bulbocoidioides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Asparagales. This plant is a member of the family Orchidaceae, falling under the genus Pleione, and is specifically identified by its species name, bulbocoidioides.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pleione bulbocoedioides has a pseudobulbous structure characterized by an ovoid fleshy base, two to three lanceolate leaves, and a single terminal inflorescence bearing multiple fragrant pink or white flowers with distinct purple spots.

The pseudobulbs serve as essential storage organs for nutrients and water during periods of dormancy. Each leaf is typically bright green and features parallel venation common to monocots. The flowering spike emerges from the center of the foliage, showcasing zygomorphic blossoms that are adapted for specific pollinators.

Below the soil surface, a creeping rhizome helps the plant spread through its natural woodland habitat.
