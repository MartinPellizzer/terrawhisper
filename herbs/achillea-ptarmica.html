Common Yarrow

Achillea ptarmica

Common Yarrow (Achillea ptarmica) is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, known for its feathery leaves and clusters of small, white or yellow flowers, and it has been traditionally used for its various medicinal properties, particularly in treating digestive issues and skin conditions.

Botanical Identification

Common Yarrow is scientifically known as Achillea ptarmica and belongs to the Asteraceae family. It is also called "Goat's Rue" in some regions and is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant has feathery leaves and small white or yellow flowers. It has been referred to by various synonyms in historical herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Magnoliopsida
  • Order: Asterales
  • Family: Asteraceae
  • Genus: Achillea
  • Species: Achillea ptarmica

Active Compounds

Common Yarrow contains essential oil, flavonoids, and coumarin as its main bioactive compounds. These compounds work together to exert anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects. The synergistic interaction between these components enhances its therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

  • Essential oil
  • Flavonoid
  • Coumarin

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory issues and digestive problems.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and as a general tonic.

Poultice: Used for skin irritations and wounds.

Safety Profile

Common Yarrow is generally safe when used in recommended doses, but it may cause mild side effects like stomach upset or allergic reactions. It should be avoided during pregnancy and by individuals with bleeding disorders due to its potential to increase bleeding risk. Always consult a healthcare provider before use, especially if taking blood-thinning medications.
