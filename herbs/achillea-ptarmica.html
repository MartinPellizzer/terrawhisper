Common Yarrow (Achillea Ptarmica)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Achillea ptarmica.

Common Yarrow, scientifically known as Achillea ptarmica, is a flowering perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, valued for its medicinal properties and historical significance. It is traditionally used as a medicinal herb and adaptogen, known for its ability to support the body's response to stress and promote overall wellness. The plant contains essential oils, flavonoids, and sesquiterpene lactones, which contribute to its primary benefits such as reducing inflammation, aiding digestion, and supporting respiratory health. In traditional medicine, it has been used to treat wounds, fevers, and digestive issues, while modern wellness applications include its use in herbal teas, topical salves, and as a natural remedy for colds and menstrual discomfort. Its distinctive fern-like leaves and clusters of small white or pale yellow flowers, along with its historical use by ancient Greeks and Romans, make it a unique and enduring plant in both cultural and medicinal contexts.
