Pedicularis rhinanthoides

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pedicularis rhinanthoides has erect stems reaching heights of 10 to 40 centimeters, characterized by linear to lanceolate leaves that are primarily basal or arranged in whorls along the stem. The leaves often possess serrated margins and a somewhat fleshy texture. Its inflorescence consists of dense, terminal spikes of tubular, irregular flowers that are typically pink to purplish in color. Each flower features a prominent upper lip that is hooded and a smaller lower lip that serves as a landing platform for pollinators. The plant's root system is specialized, often forming associations with fungi to facilitate nutrient uptake in its subalpine habitats.
