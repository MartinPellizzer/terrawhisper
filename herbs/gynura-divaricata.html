Gynura divaricata

What's the taxonomical classification of Gynura divaricata?

Gyunura divaricata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Asterales. As a member of the family Asteraceae, this plant is specifically identified by the genus Gyunura and the species divaricata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gynura divaricata has deeply lobed, spreading leaves that are typically trifoliate and characterized by prominent, irregular serrations along their margins. The plant exhibits a low-growing, herbaceous habit with stems that often branch out from a central base. Its foliage presents a textured appearance due to the variable width of the leaf segments and the presence of fine veins. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves to complete its reproductive cycle. The overall structure reflects a sprawling growth pattern suited for its specific ecological niche.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the eastern and central United States, specifically ranging from southern New England through the Appalachian Mountains and into the Midwest. It is commonly found inhabiting moist woodlands, stream banks, and shaded forest edges across these regions. Its distribution follows a pattern where it thrives in areas with rich, well-drained soils and consistent moisture. What are the pharmacological activities of Gynura divaricata?

This plant has anti-diabetic, anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, hypoglycaemic, and α-glucosidase inhibition activities. Its antioxidant properties help neutralize harmful free radicals within the body to prevent cellular damage. The plant demonstrates significant hypoglycaemic effects by lowering blood glucose levels through various biological mechanisms. Specifically, its α-glucosidase inhibition activity aids in managing diabetes by slowing down the digestion of carbohydrates. Furthermore, the anti-inflammatory actions of Gynura divaricata assist in reducing systemic swelling and pain.

According to a study published by "Phytomedicine : international journal of phytotherapy and phytopharmacology", Gynura divaricata oligopeptides significantly ameliorated hyperglycemia, dyslipidemia, and insulin resistance in diabetic mice. A total of 37 bioactive peptides were identified through LC-ESI-MS/MS, with molecular docking confirming strong binding between these GD oligopeptides and AKT. These peptides enhanced hepatic glycogen synthesis and suppressed gluconeogenesis by activating the AKT/FoxO1 pathway. Furthermore, they reduced pancreatic apoptosis by modulating Bcl-2/Bax expression and restored intestinal barrier integrity. The oligopeptides also enriched SCFA-producing Lachnospiraceae and promoted GPR43-dependent GLP-1 secretion to normalize energy homeostasis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains high concentrations of several bioactive hydroxycinnamic acid derivatives, specifically O-caffeoylquinic acid, O-feruloylquinic acid, O-p-coumaroylquinic acid, chlorogenic acid, and dicaffeoylquinic acid. These specific medicinal compounds are primarily classified as polyphenols which contribute to the plant's significant antioxidant properties. The presence of chlorogenic acid and its related esters suggests that the plant may possess various anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities. Research into these chemical constituents helps scientists understand the therapeutic potential of the species in pharmacological applications. Such diverse chemical profiles are characteristic of the complex secondary metabolites found within the Gynura genus.

According to a study published by "Chemistry & biodiversity", the aerial parts of Gynura divaricata collected from 5 different Chinese locations were studied for their phenolic secondary metabolites. Using UPLC/MS/MS analysis, a total of 53 phenolics were either identified or tentatively characterized. These compounds include kaempferol, quercetin oligoglycosides, and various derivatives of benzoic acid, hydroxycinnamic acid, and caffeic acid. The research also qualitatively characterized positional isomers of O-caffeoylquinic acid, O-p-coumaroylquinic acid, O-feruloylquinic acid, and dicaffeoylquinic acid along with their methyl esters. The study evaluated the total phenolic content and in vitro antioxidant activity of these fractions for medicinal development.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for managing Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus, general diabetes, hyperglycemia, insulin resistance, and dyslipidemia. Its bioactive compounds help regulate blood glucose levels to prevent dangerous spikes in sugar. By improving the body's response to insulin, it addresses the underlying issues of insulin resistance. Additionally, the plant assists in correcting lipid profiles to combat dyslipidemia. These combined effects make it a significant subject in metabolic health research.

According to a study published by "Evidence-based complementary and alternative medicine : eCAM", Gynura divaricata (GD) is used to improve glucose and lipid metabolism and ameliorate Type 2 diabetes mellitus (T2DM). The study utilized an obesity animal model induced by a high-fat and high-sugar diet combined with a low dose of streptozocin and an insulin-resistant HepG2 cell model induced by dexamethasone. The GD extract A significantly reduced fasting serum glucose, reversed dyslipidemia and pancreatic damage, and regulated the body weight of mice. In insulin-resistant HepG2 cells, GD extract A may increase glucose consumption and decrease impairment in insulin pathway signaling molecules such as IRS-1, AKT, and GLUT1. Overall, GD extract A is considered a promising candidate for the prevention and treatment of T2DM.

The main health conditions this plant is used for are shown in the list below.

  • Hyperglycemia

  • Dyslipidemia
  • Glucose metabolism
