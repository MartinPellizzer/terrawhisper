10 Precautions To Take When Using Fabiana Imbricata (Chilean Violet)

Fabiana imbricata, also known as Chilean violet, has some precautions to consider before using it medicinally, such as excessive consumption of its extracts or infusions, which can cause gastrointestinal irritation and stomach upset if taken in high doses.

Taking this precaution is crucial as it may lead to nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, potentially resulting in dehydration and electrolyte imbalance.

It is essential to consult with a healthcare professional for guidance on safe dosages to avoid adverse effects.

This article explains in details the 10 most important precautions to take when using Fabiana imbricata medicinally.

1. Avoid mixing with other medications

When using Fabiana imbricata, also known as Chilean Violet, medicinally, it's important to take under medical supervision.

This precaution is crucial because Fabiana imbricata contains saponins and flavonoids that can interact with medications or exacerbate underlying health conditions. Additionally, its use may not be suitable for individuals with certain allergies or sensitivities.

Without proper guidance, improper dosage or combination with other treatments could lead to adverse effects, making it essential to consult a healthcare professional before using Fabiana imbricata medicinally.
