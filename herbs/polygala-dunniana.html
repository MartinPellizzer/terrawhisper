What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polygala dunniana has a perennial growth habit characterized by erect, slender stems that typically reach heights of up to 30 centimeters. The leaves are small, linear to narrowly lanceolate, and arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are distinctive, featuring two large, petal-like sepals that are often pink or purple in color. The floral structure includes a keel petal that is typically crested or fringed at the apex. These plants often grow in sandy or rocky substrates where their compact form provides an advantage.
