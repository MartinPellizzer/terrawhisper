What's the taxonomical classification of Sinopodophyllum hexandrum?

Sinopodophyllum hexandrum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Ranunculales. The plant is a member of the family Berberidaceae and is specifically categorized under the genus Sinopodophyllum, with the specific epithet hexaandrum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sinopodophyllum hexandrum has a perennial rhizomatous habit characterized by large, solitary, deeply three-lobed leaves and bright red, oblong fleshy fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring a creeping underground stem that facilitates spreading. Its leaves are broad and often show distinct venation patterns that radiate from the center of the lobes. During the flowering stage, small white flowers emerge on short pedicels, usually appearing before or alongside the foliage. The most striking morphological feature is the ripening fruit, which transitions from green to a vivid scarlet color as it matures.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the potent aryltetralin lignan podophyllotoxin, along with other related compounds such as picropodophyllin and epipodophyllotoxin. These medicinal substances are primarily extracted from the rhizomes of the plant for their significant biological activities. Podophyllotoxin serves as a crucial precursor in the chemical synthesis of semi-synthetic derivatives like etoposide and teniposide. These derived medications are widely used in chemotherapy to treat various forms of cancer by inhibiting cell division. The unique chemical structure of these compounds allows them to interfere with microtubule formation in rapidly dividing cells.

What side effects this plant can have?

More serious systemic risks involve the significant suppression of bone marrow function. This suppression can lead to dangerous drops in blood cell counts, specifically leukopenia and thrombocytopenia. Patients may also experience neurological symptoms such as dizziness during use.
