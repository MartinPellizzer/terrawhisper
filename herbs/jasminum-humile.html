Jasminum humile

What's the taxonomical classification of Jasminum humile?

Jasminum humile belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant falls under the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Oleaceae. Finally, its taxonomic identity is completed within the genus Jasminum, specifically identified as the species humile.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Jasminum humile has a sprawling, woody growth habit characterized by slender, arching stems and small, elliptical, dark green leaves arranged in opposite pairs. The foliage typically consists of simple, entire-margined leaflets that provide a dense, bushy appearance to the shrub. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often displaying a pale white or yellowish hue during their bloom period. These delicate blossoms are frequently accompanied by small, fleshy fruits that develop following successful pollination. The overall structure of the plant allows it to form low-growing mats or climbing thickets depending on the available support.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, specifically spanning across countries such as Thailand, Vietnam, and parts of Southern China. It thrives predominantly in humid, low-altitude environments where warm temperatures are consistent throughout the year. While its primary natural range is confined to these Asian territories, it has been widely introduced to other tropical zones by horticulturalists. In these non-native regions, it is often cultivated in gardens and urban landscapes for its fragrant blooms. Consequently, its distribution has expanded from a specific indigenous niche to a broader global presence in warm climates.

What are the pharmacological activities of Jasminum humile?

This plant has anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, apoptosis inducing, cytotoxic, and anti-breast cancer pharmacological activities. Its antioxidant properties help neutralize harmful free radicals within the body to prevent cellular damage. The plant also exhibits significant anti-inflammatory effects by reducing swelling and pain in biological systems. Furthermore, its cytotoxic nature allows it to target and destroy abnormal cells through apoptosis inducing mechanisms. These specific combined actions demonstrate its potential effectiveness in anti-breast cancer research and therapeutic development.

As discussed in a study, Jasminum humile is used for skin diseases via its pulp and leaf decoction, while its root juice treats ringworm. The methanol extract of the plant contains 89.71 ± 2.79 mg RE/g of total flavonoids and 124.77 ± 2.41 mg GAE/g of total phenolic content. In experiments involving nine groups of male rats with six rats/group, the plant demonstrated non-toxicity even at higher doses. Jasminum humile ameliorates CCl4-induced oxidative stress in the liver by regulating ER stress, inflammatory, and fibrosis markers. Co-administration of the plant with CCl4 resulted in normal levels of serum markers and antioxidant enzymes, showing significant (P < 0.05) downregulation in the mRNA expression of stress and inflammatory markers.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds 1-methoxyjasmigenin, 1-methyl-9-aldojasmigenin, jasmoside, isojasminin, and various total flavonoids. These specific chemical constituents are responsible for the plant's unique therapeutic properties and biological activities. Research indicates that these jasmo-type compounds contribute significantly to the overall phytochemical profile. The presence of total flavonoids further enhances the antioxidant potential found within the plant tissue. Studying these individual molecules helps scientists understand how the plant interacts with different biological systems.

As discussed in a study, the 95% ethanol extracts of the aerial parts of Jasminum humile were investigated for cytotoxicity against HepG-2, MCF-7, and THP-1 cell lines. Chromatographic workup of ethyl acetate and n-butanol fractions of the plant yielded two new compounds, 1-methoxyjasmigenin (1) and 1-methyl-9-aldojasmigenin (2), alongside five known compounds (3-7). Jasmoside (5) was identified as the most potent anticancer compound, displaying IC50 values of 66.47, 41.32, and 27.59 µg/mL against the HepG-2, MCF-7, and THP-1 cell lines, respectively. Additionally, isojasminin (4) exhibited IC50 values of 33.49, 43.12, and 51.07 µg/mL against those same cell lines. The study highlights jasmoside (5) as a lead anticancer drug due to its high selectivity index towards MCF-7 and THP-1.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested or touched. Sensitive individuals may experience redness, itching, or swelling upon direct contact with the foliage or flowers. Ingestion of the plant parts may lead to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal discomfort in certain people. While generally considered safe for ornamental use, caution is necessary for those with known allergies to the jasmine family. Always consult a medical professional if symptoms persist after exposure.
