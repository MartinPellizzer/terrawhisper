What's the taxonomical classification of Ilex kaushue?

Ilex kaushue belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic progression, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It further belongs to the order Aquifoliales and the family Aquifoliaceae. Ultimately, this plant is categorized under the genus Ilex, specifically identified by its species name, kaushue.

What are the pharmacological activities of Ilex kaushue?

This plant has potent pharmacological activities including anti-EV-A71, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties. Its chemical constituents, particularly various triterpenoids and flavonoids, play a crucial role in inhibiting the replication of the Enterovirus A71. Beyond its antiviral capacity, the plant demonstrates the ability to mitigate oxidative stress and reduce inflammatory responses in biological models. These diverse medicinal actions suggest that its extracts could serve as valuable candidates for developing new therapeutic agents. Research continues to explore the specific mechanisms by which these bioactive compounds interact with cellular targets.

As discussed in a study, 3,4-dicaffeoylquinic acid (3,4-DCQA) from Ilex kaushue extracts exerts significant anti-EV-A71 activity with a broad inhibitory spectrum against different genotypes. This compound affects the initial entry step of EV-A71 infection by targeting the 5-fold axis located within the VP1 structural protein. Specifically, 3,4-DCQA targets the glutamic acid residue at position 98 (E98) and the proline residue at position 246 (P246) to disrupt viral attachment. The substance specifically inhibited the attachment of EV-A71 to the host receptor heparan sulfate (HS), while showing no effect on the SCARB2 or PSGL1 receptors. Through molecular docking, 3,4-DCQA was shown to form a stable structure with the E98 and P246 residues. Furthermore, the inhibition of HS binding was successful even in viruses carrying the K242A or K244A mutations in the 5-fold axis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds randialic acid B and tomentosolic acid. These specific chemical constituents are responsible for many of the plant's therapeutic properties. Researchers study these molecules to understand their potential impact on various biological processes. The presence of such unique compounds makes the species a subject of significant pharmacological interest. Further investigation into these substances may lead to new developments in drug discovery.

As discussed in a study, the traditional medicinal plant Ilex kaushue contains two triterpenoids known as randialic acid B (RAB), which is 3β-hydroxyurs-12,18-dien-28-oic acid, and tomentosolic acid (TA), which is 3β-hydroxyurs-12,19-dien-28-oic acid. These compounds act as FPR1 inhibitors in human neutrophils and can suppress psoriatic symptoms in mice. Both RAB and TA selectively inhibit reactive oxygen species production, elastase release, and CD11b expression in human neutrophils activated by FPR1. These substances also competitively inhibit FPR1-downstream signaling, including Ca2+ mobilisation and the activation of Akt and MAPK. Through these mechanisms, RAB and TA reduce imiquimod-induced psoriasis-like symptoms such as epidermal hyperplasia and transepidermal water loss.
