Rhamnus davurica

What's the taxonomical classification of Rhamnus davurica?

Rhamnus davurica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It is situated under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, following the biological lineage of the order Rosales. This plant is a member of the family Rhamnaceae, falling under the genus Rhamnus, and is specifically identified by its species name, davurica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhamnus davurica has deciduous, opposite leaves that are typically elliptic to obovate in shape, measuring between 3 and 7 centimeters in length, with finely serrated margins and a slightly leathery texture. The plant features slender, greyish-brown branches that often develop small, sharp spines at the nodes. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and arranged in dense clusters known as cymes that emerge in late spring. During the autumn, the foliage transitions into vibrant shades of red or orange before falling. The fruit consists of small, fleshy drupes that turn from green to a dark purple or black color upon ripening.

What are the pharmacological activities of Rhamnus davurica?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological activities including anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, anticancer, antioxidant, and antiproliferative properties. Research indicates that its bioactive compounds effectively neutralize free radicals to reduce oxidative stress within biological systems. The plant also exhibits strong antibacterial capabilities by inhibiting the growth of various pathogenic microorganisms. Furthermore, its anticancer effects are characterized by potent antiproliferative actions that can suppress the rapid division of malignant cells. These diverse therapeutic potential suggest that Rhamnus davurica could serve as a valuable source for developing new medicinal agents.

According to a study published by "Molecules (Basel, Switzerland)", Rhamnus davurica is a rich source of flavonoids that exhibits anti-inflammation, anticancer, antibacterial, and antioxidant activities. Using HPLC-ESI-MS/MS, 32 peaks were detected and identified, including 14 peaks found in R. davurica for the first time and 23 peaks resolved as flavonoids. The antiproliferative activities against human cancer cells HT-29 and SGC-7901 showed IC50 values of 24.96 ± 0.74 and 89.53 ± 4.11 μg/mL, respectively. General toxicity against L-O2 cells displayed a much higher IC50 at 229.19 ± 8.52 μg/mL, suggesting very low or no toxicity on hepatic cell viability.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a variety of bioactive medicinal compounds including flavonoids, apiogenin, and quercetin. These chemical constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Within the broader category of flavonoids, specific molecules like quercetin play a crucial role in protecting cells from oxidative stress. The presence of apiogenin further enhances the therapeutic potential of the species through its unique biological activities. Together, these compounds contribute to the overall pharmacological profile used in traditional medicinal applications.

According to a study published by "Frontiers in plant science", the R.d-60 fraction from ethyl acetate extracts of Rhamnus davuricica showed higher inhibitory rates against SGC-7901 and HT-29 compared with the R.d-30 fraction in vitro. Using a method based on bio-affinity ultrafiltration and HPLC-ESI-MS/MS, eight ligands were identified from this fraction. Among these, six ligands with higher enrichment factors showed more potential antitumor activity. Two representative candidates, apigenin and quercetin, were used in antiproliferative assays to determine IC50 values against SGC-7901, HT-29, and Hep G2 cells. Structure-activity relationships revealed that flavones containing a C2-C3 double bond of the C ring exhibited higher bio-affinities to Topo I.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause severe gastrointestinal distress including nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain, and intestinal cramps. These intense symptoms are primarily due to the presence of anthraquinone glycosides which act as potent laxatives. Prolonged or excessive consumption of the plant can lead to a significant electrolyte imbalance in the body. This loss of essential minerals often results in dangerous dehydration if not addressed promptly. Careful avoidance is necessary to prevent these debilitating physical reactions.
