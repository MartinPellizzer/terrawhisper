Abutilon theophrasti

What's the taxonomical classification of Abutilon theophrasti?

Abutilon theophrasti belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, the plant is situated in the order Malvales and the family Malvacaceae. It is specifically identified within the genus Abutilon, with its unique classification finalized at the species level as theophrasti.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Abutilon theophrasti contains various secondary metabolites including flavonoids, tannins, saponins, and alkaloids that contribute to its therapeutic properties. These bioactive compounds are primarily concentrated in the leaves and roots of the plant. Researchers have identified specific antioxidant and antimicrobial activities linked to these chemical constituents. The presence of these substances suggests potential applications in treating inflammatory conditions and skin ailments. Traditional medicinal practices often utilize these compounds to manage various systemic health issues.

What are the therapeutic actions of Abutilon theophrasti?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin diseases, wounds, respiratory ailments, and digestive disorders. Its leaves are frequently applied topically to soothe skin irritations and promote the healing of open sores. When used for respiratory issues, preparations of the plant can help alleviate symptoms like coughing or inflammation. Some traditional practices also involve using the plant to address stomach-related problems and gastrointestinal distress. These medicinal applications are rooted in various regional herbal traditions.
