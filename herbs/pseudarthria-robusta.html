Pseudarthria robusta, commonly known as the "Robust Pseudarthria", is a type of flowering plant belonging to the family Fabaceae.

It is traditionally used in various cultures for its medicinal properties, which include reducing inflammation, treating fever, and relieving respiratory issues such as coughs and colds.

The tea made from Pseudarthria robusta is used to treat fever, rheumatism, and other inflammatory conditions, while the tincture is used to treat skin infections and wounds.

Possible Side Effects of Pseudarthria robusta

Pseudarthria robusta can have side effects if used improperly, such as causing gastrointestinal problems, including diarrhea, nausea, and vomiting.

Prolonged or excessive use of the plant may also lead to kidney damage and increased risk of bleeding due to its high alkaloid content.

Additionally, improper use of Pseudarthria robusta may result in allergic reactions, including skin rashes and respiratory issues.

In severe cases, it can cause cardiac problems and even death, especially in individuals with pre-existing medical conditions or those who are taking medications.
