Crotalaria mairei

What's the taxonomical classification of Crotalaria mairei?

Crotalaria mairei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is positioned within the order Fabales. This plant is a representative of the family Fabaceae, which places it among the legumes, and it is specifically categorized under the genus Crotalaria. Ultimately, its unique biological identity is defined by its specific designation as the species mairei.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Crotalaria mairei has an erect, herbaceous growth habit characterized by slender, branched stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three ovate to elliptical leaflets. Its inflorescence consists of small, pea-like flowers that are frequently yellow in color and arranged in racemes. The plant produces distinctive legume pods that are inflated and contain several small seeds. These structural features are typical of many members within the Fabaceae family.
