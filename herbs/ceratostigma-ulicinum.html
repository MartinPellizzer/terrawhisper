What's the taxonomical classification of Ceratostigma ulicinum?

Ceratostigma ulicinum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Caryophyllales. This plant is a member of the family Plumbaginaceae and is specifically categorized within the genus Ceratostigma, with its specific designation being the species ulicinum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ceratostigma ulicinum has woody, spreading stems that produce small, ovate to lanceolate leaves with finely serrated margins.

The foliage typically presents a deep green color during the growing season but transitions to brilliant shades of red and purple in autumn. Tiny, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils, displaying a striking bright blue or violet hue. These blossoms are supported by slender pedicels and often appear in clusters throughout the late summer.

The overall structure of the plant forms a low-growing, mat-like groundcover that spreads via underground rhizomes.
