Jasminum officinale

What's the taxonomical classification of Jasminum officinale?

Jasminum officinale belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It further falls under the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Oleaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Jasminum, with its specific designation being the species officinale.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Jasminum officinale has woody, climbing stems that produce opposite, pinnate leaves consisting of five to nine elliptical leaflets. The foliage is typically dark green and possesses a smooth, somewhat leathery texture. Small, fragrant white flowers emerge in clusters from the leaf axils during the flowering season. Each blossom features five to seven spreading lobes that create a star-like shape. These plants often develop sprawling vines that can reach significant lengths as they scramble over supporting structures.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the temperate regions of southern Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa. It thrives in diverse climates ranging from Mediterranean environments to more continental zones. Due to its fragrant flowers, it has been widely introduced and naturalized in many other parts of the world, including parts of Asia and North America. It often colonizes disturbed areas and woodland edges where conditions are favorable. This extensive spread is largely attributed to human cultivation for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

What are the therapeutic actions of Jasminum officinale?

Jasminum officinale has therapeutic actions as a nervine, carminative, antispasmodic, sedative, anxiolytic, emmenagogue, and antidepressant. Its primary function involves soothing the nervous system to alleviate symptoms of anxiety and depression. The plant also acts as a sedative to promote relaxation and better sleep quality. For digestive issues, it serves as a carminative and antispasmodic to reduce gas and cramping. Additionally, it may function as an emmenagogue to help stimulate menstrual flow.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several potent medicinal compounds including flavonoids, gallic acid, kaempferol, quercetin, and rutin. These bioactive substances are known for their significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Gallic acid provides essential protective benefits against oxidative stress in various biological systems. The specific flavonoids such as kaempferol, quercetin, and rutin contribute to cellular health and immune support. Together, these chemical constituents make the plant a valuable subject for pharmacological research.

As discussed in a study, Jasminum officinale contains flavonoids, saponins, phenols, and tannins. The ethyl acetate fraction of the plant yielded 36.4% and contained a total phenol content of 103.01 ± 1.1 mg GAE/g and a flavonoid content of 80.29 ± 1.03 mg QUE/g. HPTLC analysis identified kaempferol, gallic acid, quercetin, and rutin, while LC-M/MS analysis detected cirsiliol, kaempferol, and 2-tridecanone at the greatest concentrations. The ethyl acetate fraction demonstrated a DPPH scavenging activity with an IC50 of 33.845 ± 1.09 μg/mL and a FRAP assay IC50 of 15.14 ± 0.25 μM Fe equivalents. Additionally, the leaf extract showed alpha-amylase deactivating action from 13.25% to 74.51% with an IC50 of 47.40 ± 0.29 μg/mL. Finally, the extract exhibited an anti-inflammatory IC50 of 66.00 ± 1.84 and a lipid peroxidation IC50 of 34.67 ± 1.69.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause an allergic skin reaction, nausea, dizziness, digestive upset, vomiting, and headache in some individuals. Contact with the skin may trigger redness or irritation for those with sensitivities. Ingesting certain parts of the plant can lead to significant digestive upset or vomiting. Some people might also experience systemic symptoms like dizziness or a persistent headache. It is important to be cautious when handling or consuming any part of this species.
