Jasminum officinale

What's the taxonomical classification of Jasminum officinale?

Jasminum officinale belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It further falls under the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Oleaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Jasminum, with its specific designation being the species officinale.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Jasminum officinale contains a complex profile of bioactive compounds including jasmonates, essential oils like linalool and benzyl acetate, flavonoids, and various terpenoids. These phytochemicals work synergistically to provide significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Linalool specifically contributes to the plant's well-known sedative and anxiolytic effects when used in aromatherapy. The presence of specific phenolic compounds also supports its traditional use in treating skin irritations and promoting wound healing. These diverse chemical constituents make the plant a valuable subject in both traditional medicine and modern pharmacological research.

What are the therapeutic actions of Jasminum officinale?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat respiratory ailments like asthma and bronchitis, skin irritations, digestive issues, and nervous system disorders such as anxiety or insomnia. Its essential oils possess antimicrobial properties that help combat various bacterial and fungal infections. In traditional medicine, the flowers are often brewed into teas to soothe coughs and reduce inflammation in the throat. Additionally, the plant is utilized in topical applications to calm skin rashes and minor wounds. The soothing aroma also serves as a natural remedy for alleviating stress and promoting relaxation.
