Field Cress (Lepidium sativum) Uses

Field Cress is used to treat respiratory infections, cough, and inflamed airways. It helps with bronchitis and mucosal irritation, and may alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis and asthma. It also supports wound healing and can address skin infections due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Field Cress (Lepidium sativum) Monograph

1. Respiratory infections

Field Cress is used to support respiratory health by reducing inflammation and clearing mucus. Its expectorant properties help alleviate symptoms of colds, bronchitis, and other respiratory infections. It can be prepared as a tea or tincture to soothe coughs and improve breathing. Regular use may enhance immune function and aid recovery from respiratory illnesses.

2. Cough

Field Cress is used to soothe coughs due to its expectorant properties. It helps loosen mucus and reduce throat irritation. Traditionally prepared as a tea, it supports respiratory health. Its mild nature makes it suitable for frequent use.

3. Inflamed airways

Field Cress has been traditionally used to soothe inflamed airways due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. It helps reduce mucus buildup and irritation, making it beneficial for respiratory conditions like bronchitis and asthma. Its essential oils and leaves are often prepared as teas or tinctures to support respiratory health.

4. Bronchitis

Field Cress is used in herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms of bronchitis due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps loosen mucus and reduce airway inflammation, making it beneficial for respiratory relief. Traditionally, it is prepared as a tea or tincture to support clearing congestion and easing breathing in bronchial infections.

5. Mucosal irritation

Field Cress is used to alleviate mucosal irritation due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It helps soothe inflamed mucous membranes in the throat and digestive tract. Traditionally prepared as a poultice or infusion, it provides localized relief and reduces discomfort associated with irritation.

6. Allergic rhinitis

Field Cress has shown potential in alleviating allergic rhinitis symptoms due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistaminic properties. Studies suggest it may reduce nasal congestion and sneezing by modulating immune responses. It is often used in herbal formulations to support respiratory health during seasonal allergies. Its mild nature makes it a safe alternative for those seeking natural relief.

7. Asthma

Field Cress has been traditionally used to support respiratory health. It contains compounds that may help reduce inflammation in the airways, potentially easing asthma symptoms. Some studies suggest it may act as a bronchodilator, improving airflow. However, it should not replace prescribed asthma treatments.

8. Mucous membrane inflammation

Field Cress is used to soothe mucous membrane inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It helps reduce swelling and irritation in the respiratory and digestive tracts. Traditionally, it has been applied as a poultice or brewed into a tea to alleviate symptoms. Its mild nature makes it suitable for internal and external use in such conditions.

9. Skin infections

Field Cress has been traditionally used to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial properties. The plant contains compounds that inhibit bacterial growth, making it effective for conditions like eczema and fungal infections. Its application as a poultice or topical treatment helps reduce inflammation and promote healing.

10. Wound healing

Field Cress has been traditionally used to promote wound healing due to its high vitamin C content, which supports collagen synthesis. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties help reduce infection risk and accelerate tissue repair. It is often applied topically as a poultice or infused oil to treat minor cuts, burns, and skin irritations.
