Field Cress (Lepidium sativum) Compounds

Field Cress contains several primary compounds, including flavonoids, phenolic acids, and tannins, which contribute to its antioxidant properties. Coumarin and glycosides are also present, offering anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Essential oils, saponins, and terpenoids support its use in herbal medicine for digestive and respiratory support. Alkaloids and anthocyanins further enhance its therapeutic potential.

Field Cress (Lepidium sativum) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Field Cress (Lepidium sativum) are bioactive compounds known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in supporting digestive and respiratory health. They help neutralize free radicals and may enhance the plant's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Field Cress (Lepidium sativum). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.

3. Tannin

Tannin in Field Cress (Lepidium sativum) contributes to its astringent properties. It helps in reducing inflammation and may support digestive health. Tannins also act as antioxidants, potentially protecting cells from damage. This compound is commonly found in the leaves and seeds of the plant.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Field Cress (Lepidium sativum). It contributes to the plant's mild antispasmodic and diuretic properties. Coumarin may also support cardiovascular health by promoting healthy blood flow. Its presence enhances the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Field Cress (Lepidium sativum). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly in supporting digestive health and reducing inflammation. Glycosides in Field Cress are known for their mild laxative effects and potential role in detoxification processes.

6. Essential oil

The essential oil of Field Cress (Lepidium sativum) contains volatile compounds like monoterpenes and sesquiterpenes. It exhibits antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, making it valuable in herbal medicine for treating infections and reducing inflammation.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound found in Field Cress (Lepidium sativum). It exhibits antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, contributing to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine. Saponin also aids in detoxification and may support digestive health.

8. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Field Cress (Lepidium sativum) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. They are found in various parts of the plant, including leaves and seeds. Terpenoids support the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a bioactive compound found in Field Cress (Lepidium sativum). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, including potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Alkaloids in this plant may support traditional uses in herbal medicine for digestive and respiratory ailments.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Field Cress (Lepidium sativum). It contributes to the plant's vibrant color and has antioxidant properties that support cellular health. This compound is valued in herbal medicine for its potential to reduce oxidative stress and enhance overall well-being.
