Loxogramme chinensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Loxogramme chinensis has erect, woody stems that branch frequently and bear leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate with entire or slightly wavy margins. The foliage often displays a glossy texture and can range in color from bright green to deeper shades depending on light exposure. Its flowers are relatively small and emerge from the leaf axils, characterized by a tubular structure and delicate petals. The reproductive parts include a capsule that splits to release tiny, lightweight seeds for dispersal. The overall growth habit is that of a compact shrub or subshrub suited for various tropical or subtropical environments.

Within these regions, the species is frequently found inhabiting forest edges, thickets, and shaded areas near water sources. Its distribution is closely tied to the warm, monsoon-influenced climates characteristic of the South China coast.
