Brassica nigra

What's the taxonomical classification of Brassica nigra?

Brassica nigra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is situated in the order Brassicales and the family Brassicaceae. Finally, it is defined by the genus Brassica and the specific species nigra.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Brassica nigra contains glucosinolates, specifically sinigrin, which break down into allyl isothiocyanate when the plant tissue is damaged. This specific sulfur-containing compound is responsible for the pungent aroma and serves as the primary bioactive agent in the plant. These isothiocyanates exhibit significant antimicrobial, antifungal, and anti-inflammatory properties in various biological studies. Some research also suggests that these compounds may possess antioxidant effects and potential anti-carcinogenic activities. Consequently, extracts from the plant are often studied for their therapeutic applications in managing inflammation and infection.

What are the therapeutic actions of Brassica nigra?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various inflammatory conditions and respiratory ailments including rheumatism, arthritis, sciatica, and bronchitis. The mustard seeds contain glucosinolates that react to form mustard oil when crushed and mixed with water. This chemical reaction produces a warming sensation that helps stimulate blood flow to affected joints and muscles. When applied topically as a poultice, the plant serves as a counterirritant to relieve localized pain. Additionally, it has historically been utilized in traditional medicine to help clear congestion in the lungs.
