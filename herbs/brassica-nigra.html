Black Mustard (Brassica nigra)

Black Mustard (Brassica nigra) is a member of the Brassicaceae family, native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. Traditionally, its seeds, fruits, and leaves have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its bitter, anti-inflammatory, and carminative actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and traditional chinese medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 30 to 100 centimeters.
Leaves
Broad leaves with upper surface dark green and lower surface pale green, featuring prominent stomatal bands.
Flowers
Flowers are yellow with four petals arranged in a cross shape, exhibiting actinomorphic symmetry, and have four stamens and a single pistil with a slender style.
Stems
Cylindrical, hollow, ridged, and terete, the stems are herbaceous, erect, and branched in a dichotomous pattern, bearing opposite phyllotaxis and covered with glandular trichomes.
