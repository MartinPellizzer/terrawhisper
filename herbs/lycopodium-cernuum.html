Lycopodium cernuum

What's the taxonomical classification of Lycopodium cernuum?

Lycopodium cernuum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it is further organized into the subclass Lycopodiidae and falls under the order Lycopodiales. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the family Lycopodiaceae, placing it within the genus Lycopodium, and it is ultimately identified by the specific species epithet cernuum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lycopodium cernuum has a creeping, rhizomatous stem structure with small, scale-like leaves called microphylls that are densely arranged along the branches. The plant typically grows in spreading mats where the stems are branched and can reach several centimeters in length. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, providing a lush, fern-like appearance to the overall foliage. During the reproductive stage, it produces small, yellowish, club-shaped strobili at the tips of the branches. These cones contain numerous sporangia that release fine spores for dispersal.
