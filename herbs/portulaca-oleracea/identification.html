Botanical Identification of Portulaca (Portulaca oleracea)

To identify Portulaca (Portulaca oleracea) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Portulaca oleracea belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Spermatophyta, class Caryophyllopsida, order Caryophyllales, family Portulacaceae, genus Portulaca, and species Portulaca oleracea. This classification reflects its position within the plant kingdom and its botanical relationships.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Portulaca oleracea is the scientific name for the common purslane. It was first described by Linnaeus in 1753. Synonyms include Portulaca oleracea L. and Portulaca grandiflora. It is known by various common names such as pusley, mouse tails, and liveforever.

Morphological Characteristics

Portulaca oleracea is an annual herb with a shallow, fibrous root system. It has prostrate or ascending stems that are smooth and succulent. The leaves are fleshy, opposite, and oval-shaped with smooth margins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are yellow with five petals, and the fruit is a capsule containing numerous tiny seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Portulaca oleracea can be distinguished by its fleshy, swollen stems and leaves that are typically oval or rounded with smooth edges. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the sun, and its seeds are round and often found in clusters. It lacks the spines or bracts found in some related species, making it easier to identify in the field.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Portulaca oleracea is native to Africa, southern Europe, and western Asia. It thrives in warm, arid climates and prefers well-drained sandy or rocky soils. It commonly grows at low altitudes, typically below 1,000 meters, in disturbed areas like roadsides and fields.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Portulaca oleracea blooms from late spring to early autumn, typically from June to September, depending on climate. Seed formation occurs from late summer to early fall, around August to October. For identification purposes, the plant is most easily recognized during its flowering stage, which is also the optimal time for harvest.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Portulaca oleracea belongs to the family Portulacaceae and is closely related to other species within the genus Portulaca. It shares key characteristics with its relatives, such as fleshy leaves and flowers, but differs in growth habits and geographic distribution. Its phylogenetic placement reflects its evolutionary ties to other members of the family.
