Eurya loquaiana

What's the taxonomical classification of Eurya loquaiana?

Eurya loquaiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant follows the lineage of the order Ericales. It is specifically situated within the family Pentaphylacaeae, falling under the genus Eurya, and is identified by its unique specific epithet, loquaiana.

Through these specific protein-ligand interactions, the plant exerts its diverse therapeutic influence on various biological systems.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, stomachaches, and intestinal parasites. Local communities utilize the leaves to create topical applications for healing cuts and sores. When consumed as a decoction, the plant serves as a remedy for various digestive ailments and abdominal pains. It also possesses properties believed to help expel worms from the digestive tract. These traditional medicinal applications highlight the plant's role in primary healthcare within its native regions.

What are the herbal preparations of Eurya loquaiana?

Eurya loquaiana is prepared primarily as a decoction by boiling the plant leaves in water to extract its bioactive compounds. This aqueous extract is commonly consumed as a medicinal tea to address various traditional ailments. In some regional practices, the plant material may also be used in topical applications or infused into oils. The potency of these preparations often depends on the specific part of the plant used and the duration of the heating process. These different methods allow practitioners to tailor the delivery of the herb to the specific needs of the patient.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Eurya loquaiana can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and potential gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Because the specific toxicological profile of this plant is not fully documented in clinical studies, researchers suggest exercising extreme caution when handling it. Direct contact with the leaves or stems may trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. If the plant is consumed, it might lead to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. Always consult a medical professional or a toxicologist if you suspect poisoning from this species.
