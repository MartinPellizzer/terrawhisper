Salvia himmelbaurii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Salvia himimmelbaurii has upright, square-shaped stems that reach heights of approximately 30 to 60 centimeters and support opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is characterized by a textured, rugose surface and a grayish-green hue that provides a dense base for the flowering spikes. Long, slender inflorescences emerge from the upper nodes, bearing whorls of tubular, two-lipped flowers. These blossoms typically exhibit a deep violet or purple coloration with pale accents on the lower lip to attract pollinators. The plant's structure is herbaceous and perennial, allowing it to form compact, bushy clumps over time.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of the eastern Himalayas, specifically spanning across parts of Bhutan, northern India, and Nepal. These populations are often found at elevations ranging from 2,500 to 4,000 meters above sea level.
