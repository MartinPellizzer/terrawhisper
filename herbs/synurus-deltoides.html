What's the taxonomical classification of Synurus deltoides?

Synurus deltoides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Asterales, this plant is a member of the family Asteraceae. It is further distinguished by its specific placement in the genus Synurus, with its unique identity defined by the species name deltoides.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Synurus deltoides has a perennial rootstock that produces erect, unbranched stems reaching heights of 10 to 40 centimeters, topped with terminal clusters of small, white, five-petaled flowers. Stem leaves are fewer in number and significantly smaller than the basal foliage, often appearing in opposite pairs. The plant's inflorescence consists of a compact cyme that displays a delicate, airy structure. Each flower features prominent stamens that contribute to the visual texture of the blooming heads.
