10 Possible Side Effects Of Pueraria Lobata (Kudzu)

Pueraria lobata has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, headaches, and dizziness.

These side effects can occur due to excessive consumption or interacting with certain medications. For instance, individuals with kidney problems may experience worsening symptoms, such as nausea and vomiting, which can lead to dehydration and electrolyte imbalances, further compromising their health.

In severe cases, these side effects can be debilitating and interfere with daily activities, affecting quality of life.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Pueraria lobata if used imporperly.

Pueraria lobata causes allergic reactions in some individuals due to its unique chemical composition.

The plant contains a group of isoflavones called daidzein and genistein, which are responsible for triggering an allergic response in certain people.

This reaction can manifest as skin rashes, hives, or itching, and in rare cases, anaphylaxis may occur.
