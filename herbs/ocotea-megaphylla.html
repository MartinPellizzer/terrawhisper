What is Ocotea megaphylla?

Ocotea megaphylla is a species of plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is a tree with a straight trunk, smooth, gray bark, and a rounded or conical crown. Leaves are large, 15-25 cm long, elliptic, with a pointed tip, and have a leathery texture; they are arranged opposite, with 2-4 pairs of secondary veins. The plant is native to the Andes mountains in South America, specifically in Colombia and Ecuador.

Flowers are small, yellowish-green, and arranged in clusters, with 4-6 tepals, and are dioecious, meaning they have separate male and female plants.
