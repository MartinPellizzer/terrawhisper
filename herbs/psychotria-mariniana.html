Psychotria mariniana

Psychotria mariniana, also known as Chaliponga, is a species of plant in the Rubiaceae family native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It has been used for its medicinal properties, particularly for its antimalarial and anti-inflammatory effects.

The plant is believed to have various health benefits, including reducing fever, alleviating pain, and treating respiratory issues, due to its high concentration of alkaloids such as harmine and tetrahydroharmine.

Traditionally, the plant is prepared as a tea or infused in hot water to be consumed, or sometimes used in traditional medicine rituals in South America.
