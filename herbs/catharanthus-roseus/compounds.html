Madagascar Periwinkle (Catharanthus roseus) Compounds

The primary compounds of Madagascar Periwinkle include alkaloids, terpenoids, flavonoids, and glycosides. Alkaloids like vinblastine and vincristine are notable for their medicinal properties. Terpenoids contribute to the plant's chemical complexity, while flavonoids and glycosides offer antioxidant and anti-inflammatory benefits.

Madagascar Periwinkle (Catharanthus roseus) Monograph

1. Alkaloid

Alkaloids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Catharanthus roseus, also known as Madagascar Periwinkle. These compounds have significant medicinal value, particularly in cancer treatment, due to their ability to inhibit cell division and induce apoptosis.

2. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Madagascar Periwinkle (Catharanthus roseus). These compounds contribute to the plant's medicinal properties, including its use in cancer treatments. Specific terpenoids like vinblastine and vincristine are alkaloids with potent anti-cancer effects. They are extracted from the plant's leaves and have become important in pharmaceuticals.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Madagascar Periwinkle (Catharanthus roseus) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting the plant's use in traditional herbal medicine. They may aid in treating conditions like cancer and cardiovascular diseases. Flavonoids work synergistically with other alkaloids present in the plant.

4. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Madagascar Periwinkle (Catharanthus roseus). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly in the treatment of cancer and diabetes. Glycosides in this plant are known for their ability to inhibit tumor growth and regulate blood sugar levels. These compounds are extracted and used in pharmaceutical formulations.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a compound found in Madagascar Periwinkle (Catharanthus roseus). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine, though its therapeutic role is less prominent compared to other alkaloids like vinblastine and vincristine. Coumarin is also noted for its mild anticoagulant properties.

6. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a compound found in Madagascar Periwinkle (Catharanthus roseus). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, which may support various therapeutic applications in herbal medicine. While not as well-studied as alkaloids, phenolic acids are part of the plant's broader biochemical profile with potential health benefits.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Madagascar Periwinkle (Catharanthus roseus). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. While not as well-studied as other compounds in the plant, saponin supports the overall therapeutic potential of Catharanthus roseus in traditional herbal medicine.

8. Tannin

Tannin is a compound found in Madagascar Periwinkle (Catharanthus roseus). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support wound healing and inflammation reduction. While not the primary active compound, tannin plays a role in the plant's traditional medicinal uses.

9. Essential oil

The essential oil of Madagascar Periwinkle (Catharanthus roseus) contains bioactive compounds like alkaloids and terpenoids. It is used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The oil's complex composition contributes to the plant's therapeutic applications.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid compound found in Catharanthus roseus, commonly known as Madagascar Periwinkle. It contributes to the plant's vibrant flower colors and has antioxidant properties that may support cellular health. While not the primary medicinal compound, anthocyanin complements the plant's overall therapeutic potential.
