Botanical Identification of Madagascar Periwinkle (Catharanthus roseus)

To identify Madagascar Periwinkle (Catharanthus roseus) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

The Madagascar Periwinkle, Catharanthus roseus, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Liliopsida, order Apocynales, family Apocynaceae, genus Catharanthus, and species Catharanthus roseus. This classification places it within the flowering plants and the milkweed family, highlighting its botanical relationships and characteristics.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The botanical name of Madagascar Periwinkle is Catharanthus roseus, first described by the French botanist Antoine Laurent de Jussieu in 1789. It is also known by several synonyms, including Vinca rosea and Apocynum roseum, reflecting its historical classification within the Apocynaceae family.

Morphological Characteristics

Madagascar Periwinkle is an evergreen shrub with a compact growth habit. It has a fibrous root system that anchors it firmly. The stems are erect, slender, and often branched. Leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy. The inflorescence is a raceme with small, tubular flowers. Flowers are pink to purple, five-petaled, and fragrant. The fruit is a capsule that releases seeds when mature.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Madagascar Periwinkle can be distinguished by its opposite, ovate leaves with wavy margins, and tubular, pink to purple flowers. It has a square stem and produces violet-blue berries. These features differentiate it from similar species like Vinca major and Vincapulchra.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Madagascar Periwinkle is native to Madagascar and parts of southern Africa. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained, loamy soil. It typically grows at altitudes between 300 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Catharanthus roseus blooms from late spring to early autumn, typically between May and September. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early fall, around August to October. For identification purposes, the best time to harvest is during the flowering period when the plant is most identifiable.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Catharanthus roseus belongs to the Apocynaceae family, closely related to species like Vinca and Strophorrhynchos. It shares morphological and genetic traits with these genera, including milky sap and opposite leaf arrangement. Its phylogenetic placement reflects its evolutionary ties to other apocynaceae plants, highlighting its unique yet related botanical identity.
