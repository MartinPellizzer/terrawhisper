    What are the herbal preparations of Rabdosia enanderiana?

    Rabdosia enanderiana is prepared primarily as a decoction, a concentrated liquid extract made by boiling the dried aerial parts of the plant in water. This method is used to release the active alkaloids and other medicinal compounds into the solution for consumption.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Rabdosia enanderiana can cause gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, and potential neurotoxicity if ingested in improper quantities. Some individuals may experience localized allergic reactions such as redness or itching upon direct contact with the foliage. Excessive consumption poses a risk to the central nervous system due to the presence of specific bioactive compounds. It is essential to exercise extreme caution as the specific toxicological threshold for humans is not fully documented.
