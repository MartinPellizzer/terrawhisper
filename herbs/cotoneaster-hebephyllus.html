These blossoms eventually transition into small, globose red berries that remain on the plant through the winter.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Cotoneaster hebephyllus can cause gastrointestinal distress, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, if the berries are ingested by humans or pets. The fruit contains cyanogenic glycosides which may release toxic cyanide into the bloodstream upon consumption. While the plant is generally considered non-toxic to touch, some individuals might experience mild skin irritation or allergic contact dermatitis. It is important to keep these small red berries away from young children who might mistake them for edible snacks. Monitoring pets is also necessary since ingestion can lead to significant illness.
