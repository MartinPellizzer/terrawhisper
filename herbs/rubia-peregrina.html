Wild Madder (Rubia peregrina)

Wild Madder (Rubia peregrina) is a member of the Rubiaceae family, native to Southern Europe, North Africa, and Middle East. Traditionally, its roots, stems, and leaves have been used for decoctions, infusions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its astringent, diuretic, and anti-inflammatory actions, and has a long history of use in mediterranean herbal traditions, european herbal medicine, and unani medicine.
