10 Precautions To Take When Using Conyza Canadensis (Canadian Fleabane)

Conyza canadensis, also known as Canadian fleabane, has some precautions to consider before using it medicinally, such as being cautious with allergic reactions and interactions with medications.

Failure to do so may result in skin irritation, itching, or allergic rhinitis. It may also interact with blood thinners, increasing the risk of bleeding.

Additionally, using it without proper guidance may lead to over-dosage, exacerbating existing conditions like asthma or cardiovascular issues.

10. Use only dried or fresh

When using Conyza canadensis, also known as Canadian Fleabane, medicinally, it's important to store in a cool place only.

This precaution is crucial because Conyza canadensis is sensitive to temperature fluctuations, which can affect its potency and stability. Storing it in a cool environment prevents degradation of its active compounds, ensuring the consistency and effectiveness of its medicinal properties.
