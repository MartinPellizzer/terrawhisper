10 Possible Side Effects Of Conyza Canadensis

Conyza canadensis, also known as Canadian fleabane, has some side effects when used improperly, such as allergic reactions, skin irritation, and digestive problems.

These reactions can be caused by an individual's sensitivity to the plant's bioactive compounds.

For example, severe allergic reactions can worsen respiratory problems, such as asthma, and digestive issues can lead to malnutrition and electrolyte imbalance, which can further exacerbate underlying health conditions.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Conyza canadensis, also known as Canadian fleabane, causes allergic reactions when its plant parts come into contact with human skin or mucous membranes.

The allergenic compounds present in the plant, including sesquiterpene lactones and flavonoids, trigger an immune response that leads to symptoms such as itching, redness, swelling, and blistering.

This sensitivity is often due to individual differences in tolerance and genetic predispositions, making some people more susceptible to these allergic reactions.

2. Induces liver damage

Conyza canadensis, also known as Canadian fleabane, induces liver damage due to its high concentration of sesquiterpene lactones, which are potent hepatic toxins.

These toxins can cause inflammation and oxidative stress in the liver, leading to cellular damage and potentially resulting in chronic liver disease or even acute liver failure.

Additionally, the plant's pyrrolizidine alkaloids have been shown to be metabolized into toxic compounds that can accumulate in the liver and cause progressive fibrosis and cirrhosis.

3. Triggers respiratory issues

Conyza canadensis, also known as Canadian fleabane, triggers respiratory issues in some individuals due to its allergenic properties.

The plant's tiny flowers and leaves contain potent allergens that can cause an immune response, leading to symptoms such as wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.

This is often triggered by inhaling the plant's pollen or touching its leaves, which can release irritating chemicals into the air.

4. Increases blood pressure

Conyza canadensis, also known as Canadian fleabane, increases blood pressure by stimulating the sympathetic nervous system and releasing adrenaline, leading to a rapid heartbeat and elevated blood pressure.

This is due to the plant's ability to interact with the body's natural hormonal balance, specifically the adrenal glands.

The increased levels of adrenaline cause vasoconstriction, or the narrowing of blood vessels, which further contributes to the rise in blood pressure.

5. Worsens asthma symptoms

Conyza canadensis, also known as Canadian fleabane, worsens asthma symptoms due to its potent allergenic properties.

The plant's tiny flowers and leaves contain a high concentration of bioactive compounds that can trigger an allergic response in susceptible individuals, leading to increased bronchial constriction and inflammation.

This can exacerbate existing asthma conditions and cause more frequent and severe attacks.

6. Provokes skin rashes

Conyza canadensis, also known as Canadian fleabane, provokes skin rashes due to its allergenic properties and ability to trigger an immune response.

The plant's sap contains a number of compounds that can cause irritation and inflammation on the skin, leading to redness, itching, and blistering.

In some cases, these reactions can be severe and widespread, causing discomfort and distress for those who come into contact with the plant.

7. Reduces platelet count

Conyza canadensis, also known as Canadian fleabane, reduces platelet count due to its ability to cause gastrointestinal irritation and inflammation.

The plant's saponins and flavonoids can alter the gut microbiome, leading to a decrease in platelet production. Additionally, Conyza canadensis contains quercetin, which has been shown to inhibit platelet aggregation and reduce blood clotting factors.

As a result, individuals using Conyza canadensis may experience decreased platelet count, potentially increasing their risk of bleeding complications.

8. Affects kidney function

Conyza canadensis, also known as Canadian fleabane, affects kidney function by inhibiting the excretion of creatinine and other waste products in the urine.

This is due to its ability to alter the structure and function of renal tubular cells, leading to reduced glomerular filtration rate and increased blood urea levels.

As a result, individuals who use Conyza canadensis may experience decreased urine output, dark-colored urine, or an increase in blood pressure.

9. Enhances bleeding risk

Conyza canadensis, also known as Canadian fleabane, enhances bleeding risk due to its ability to inhibit platelet aggregation and prolong bleeding time.

This is attributed to the presence of certain compounds in the plant, including caffeic acid and rosmarinic acid, which have been shown to interfere with blood clotting mechanisms.

As a result, individuals using Conyza canadensis may experience prolonged or increased bleeding following injury or surgery.

10. Disrupts hormonal balance

Conyza canadensis, also known as Canadian fleabane, disrupts hormonal balance due to its high concentration of phytoestrogens, which mimic the effects of estrogen in the body.

This can lead to an imbalance of the body's natural hormonal levels, potentially causing issues such as changes in menstrual cycles, mood swings, and breast tenderness.

Additionally, the plant's ability to bind to estrogen receptors can also interfere with thyroid function, leading to a range of symptoms including fatigue, weight gain, and depression.
