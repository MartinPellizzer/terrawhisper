Eurya hainanensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Eurya hainanensis?

Eurya hainanenensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic classification continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Ericales. Within this order, the plant is a member of the family Pentaphylacaceae, specifically falling under the genus Eurya, with the unique species designation hainanenensis.

Eurya hainanenensis has antibacterial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties that are primarily attributed to its high concentration of bioactive secondary metabolites like flavonoids and polyphenols. Additionally, its ability to modulate inflammatory pathways suggests potential applications in treating skin irritations or systemic swelling. Such diverse pharmacological activities highlight its importance in traditional medicine and modern phytotherapy studies.

What side-effects this plant can have?
