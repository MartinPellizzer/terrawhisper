Symplocarpus foetidus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Symplocarpus foetidus has an evergreen, woody habit characterized by simple, alternate, oblong-elliptic leaves that are coriaceous and possess an entire margin. The leaf surfaces are typically glabrous and exhibit a distinctively leathery texture. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in axillary clusters and emit a strong, unpleasant odor that defines the species. These flowers develop into small, globose drupes which transition in color as they reach maturity. The overall structure of the plant provides a dense, bushy appearance common to many members of the Salicaceae family.

What side-effects this plant can have?

SympLocarpus foetidus can cause severe skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal distress if its parts are touched or ingested. The plant is notorious for its incredibly foul odor, which is produced by volatile organic compounds that can trigger nausea or headaches in sensitive individuals. Direct contact with the foliage or sap may result in contact dermatitis, manifesting as redness, itching, or swelling on the skin. Furthermore, consuming any part of the plant without proper processing may lead to vomiting or stomach cramps. Caution is advised when handling this species to avoid these unpredictable physiological responses.
