What's the taxonomical classification of Jasminanthes chunii?

Jasminanthes chunii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the order Gentianales and the family Apocynaceae. Finally, its specific identity is defined by its classification within the genus Jasminanthes, culminating in the species name chunii.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Jasminanthes chunii can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and digestive distress if ingested. If any part of the plant is accidentally swallowed, it can lead to nausea, abdominal pain, or vomiting. Because clinical data on this specific species is limited, extreme caution should always be exercised when handling it.
