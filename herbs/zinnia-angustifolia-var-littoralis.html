Zinnia angustifolia var. littoralis

Zinnia angustifolia var. littoralis, commonly known as Littoral Zinnia or Narrow-leaved Coast Zinnia, is a plant species within the daisy family and is native to the coastal regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

This plant has been traditionally used to treat various health conditions, including fever, wounds, and skin infections, due to its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antiseptic properties.

The bioactive constituents present in Littoral Zinnia include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its therapeutic actions, such as reducing inflammation, fighting infections, and promoting wound healing.

The health benefits of Littoral Zinnia include its ability to boost the immune system, reduce fever, and promote skin health, making it a valuable medicinal plant for treating various health conditions.
