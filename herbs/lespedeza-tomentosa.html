Lespedeza tomentosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Lespedeza tomentosa?

Lespedeza tomentosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized under the order Fabales. This plant is a member of the family Fabaceae and falls within the genus Lespedeza, eventually reaching its specific identification at the species level as tomentoza.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lespedeza tomentosa has trifoliate leaves with densely pubescent surfaces and small, bright yellow pea-like flowers arranged in axillary racemes.

The stems are woody at the base and covered in fine, soft hairs that give the plant a fuzzy texture. Each leaflet is typically oval or elliptical in shape with entire margins and a distinctively felted underside. The inflorescence consists of several small blossoms that emerge from the leaf axils on slender stalks.

These morphological traits, particularly the thick pubescence, help the plant retain moisture in its native habitats.
