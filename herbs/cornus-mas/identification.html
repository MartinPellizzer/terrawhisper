Botanical Identification of Cornus Mas (Cornus mas)

To identify Cornus Mas (Cornus mas) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Cornus mas belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Saxifragales, family Cornaceae, genus Cornus, and species Cornus mas. This classification places it within the flowering plants and the dogwood family, highlighting its botanical relationships and characteristics.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Cornus Mas is Cornus mas, which was first described by the botanist Carl von Linné in 1753. It is also known by several synonyms, including Cornus sanguinea and Cornus sericea, though these are now considered different species. Common names include common dogwood, European dogwood, and sour cherry, reflecting its historical use in herbal medicine.

Morphological Characteristics

Cornus mas is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3-6 meters. It has a bushy growth habit with multiple stems arising from the base. The root system is shallow and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils. The stems are slender, often reddish-brown, and bear a distinctive flaky bark.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Cornus mas can be distinguished by its opposite, oval leaves with serrated edges, red stems with swollen nodes, and white flowers in clusters. It produces red, fleshy berries in late summer. It differs from Cornus sanguinea by having more rounded leaves and smaller berries.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Cornus mas is native to southern Europe, North Africa, and western Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with mild winters and warm summers. It prefers well-drained soils, such as loam or sandy soils, and is typically found at low to moderate altitudes, ranging from 0 to 1,000 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Cornus mas blooms in early spring, typically between March and May, depending on the region. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early autumn, around July to September. For identification purposes, the best time to harvest the plant is during the flowering period, when characteristics are most distinct.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Cornus mas belongs to the genus Cornus, which is part of the family Cornaceae. Its closest relatives include species like Cornus sanguinea and Cornus canadensis. These species share similar morphological and genetic traits, such as opposite leaf arrangement and drupes, which help in distinguishing them within the genus.
