Cornus Mas (Cornus mas) Uses

Cornus mas is used to treat arthritis, gout, and osteoporosis due to its anti-inflammatory and bone-strengthening properties. It also aids in digestive issues, including diarrhea, dyspepsia, and gastritis, by supporting liver function and gut health. The plant is applied externally for skin infections and wound healing, thanks to its antimicrobial and astringent qualities.

Cornus Mas (Cornus mas) Monograph

1. Arthritis

Cornus Mas has been traditionally used to support joint health and alleviate arthritis symptoms. Its anti-inflammatory properties help reduce swelling and pain, while its ability to improve circulation may enhance joint mobility. It is often prepared as a tincture or decoction for internal use in herbal medicine practices.

2. Gout

Cornus mas has been traditionally used to support joint health and reduce inflammation, making it beneficial for gout sufferers. Its anti-inflammatory properties help alleviate pain and swelling associated with gouty arthritis. The plant's compounds may aid in lowering uric acid levels, a key factor in gout management. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture for internal use.

3. Osteoporosis

Cornus mas has been traditionally used to support bone health due to its high content of polyphenols and flavonoids, which may help reduce oxidative stress and inflammation. Studies suggest it may aid in preventing bone loss and improving bone density, making it a potential supportive remedy for osteoporosis.

4. Diarrhea

Cornus mas has been traditionally used to treat diarrhea due to its astringent properties. The fruit's high tannin content helps reduce intestinal inflammation and firm stools. It is often prepared as a decoction or infusion to soothe digestive discomfort. Its use aligns with historical herbal practices for gastrointestinal support.

5. Dyspepsia

Cornus mas has been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of dyspepsia, such as bloating and indigestion. Its astringent properties help reduce excess mucus and soothe the stomach lining. The fruit and bark contain tannins that may aid in digestion and relieve gastrointestinal discomfort. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture for this purpose.

6. Gastritis

Cornus mas has been traditionally used to support digestive health, particularly in cases of gastritis. Its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties help reduce stomach irritation and excess mucus. It is often prepared as a tincture or infusion to soothe the gastrointestinal lining and promote healing.

7. Digestive issues

Cornus mas has been traditionally used to support digestive health. Its astringent properties help reduce excess mucus and soothe inflamed digestive tracts. It is often used for diarrhea and to strengthen the stomach lining. It may also aid in the digestion of food and the absorption of nutrients.

8. Liver dysfunction

Cornus mas has been traditionally used to support liver function due to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps in detoxification and may aid in conditions like hepatitis and cirrhosis. The fruit and bark contain compounds that promote liver cell regeneration and reduce oxidative stress.

9. Skin infections

Cornus mas has been traditionally used to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The bark and leaves contain compounds that help reduce bacterial growth and soothe inflamed skin. It is often prepared as a topical salve or infusion to address conditions like eczema and fungal infections. Its effectiveness is supported by historical use in herbal medicine.

10. Wounds

Cornus mas has been traditionally used to treat wounds due to its astringent and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps in reducing swelling, promoting tissue repair, and preventing infection. The tincture or poultice made from its leaves and berries can be applied directly to wounds to accelerate healing and reduce scarring.
