Cornus Mas (Cornus mas) Compounds

Cornus Mas contains several primary compounds, including flavonoids, which act as antioxidants. Tannins contribute to its astringent properties. Phenolic acids provide anti-inflammatory effects. Coumarin has antispasmodic and anticoagulant properties. Glycosides and terpenoids support its medicinal uses, while saponins enhance absorption. Anthocyanins, essential oils, and alkaloids also play roles in its therapeutic benefits.

Cornus Mas (Cornus mas) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Cornus Mas. They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds support the therapeutic uses of Cornus Mas in traditional herbal medicine. Flavonoids help in reducing oxidative stress and enhancing immune function.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Cornus Mas, known for its astringent properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating digestive issues and skin conditions. Tannins also provide antioxidant and antimicrobial benefits, supporting its role in herbal medicine.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Cornus Mas. It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports its traditional use in promoting urinary health and managing inflammation. Phenolic acid is also involved in the plant's ability to support kidney function and reduce symptoms of urinary tract infections.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Cornus Mas. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating digestive issues and inflammation. Coumarin also has anticoagulant properties, making it relevant in herbal medicine for its therapeutic potential.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Cornus Mas. It contributes to the plant's therapeutic effects, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Glycosides in Cornus Mas are often found in its fruits and roots, supporting its use in traditional herbal medicine.

6. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Cornus Mas contribute to its medicinal properties by exhibiting anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. These compounds support the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments. They also enhance the plant's potential in modern herbal formulations for chronic conditions.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Cornus Mas. It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponin helps support immune function and may aid in digestive health. Its presence enhances the therapeutic potential of Cornus Mas in herbal medicine.

8. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Cornus Mas, known for its potent antioxidant properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating inflammation and digestive issues. This compound helps protect cells from oxidative stress and supports overall health. Its presence in Cornus Mas highlights the plant's value in herbal medicine.

9. Essential oil

Essential oil from Cornus Mas contains volatile compounds like limonene and caryophyllene. These compounds contribute to the plant's aromatic profile and may have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. They are often extracted through distillation and used in traditional herbal remedies.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Cornus Mas. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating inflammatory conditions. Alkaloids in Cornus Mas may have antispasmodic and analgesic properties. They are often extracted for their medicinal effects in herbal formulations.
