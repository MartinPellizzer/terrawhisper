Cabbage (Brassica oleracea) Compounds

Cabbage contains several key compounds, including flavonoids, which act as antioxidants. Phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory properties. Glycosides and tannins support digestive health. Saponins and coumarins have detoxifying effects. Terpenoids and essential oils provide antimicrobial benefits. Anthocyanins and alkaloids add to its medicinal versatility.

Cabbage (Brassica oleracea) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Cabbage (Brassica oleracea) that contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting immune function and potentially reducing oxidative stress. They are naturally present in the plant's leaves and stems, enhancing its therapeutic value in herbal medicine.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in cabbage (Brassica oleracea). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports overall health by enhancing immune function and promoting cellular repair. Its presence in cabbage makes it valuable in herbal medicine for its therapeutic benefits.

3. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Cabbage (Brassica oleracea). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by supporting digestive health and acting as a mild laxative. Glycosides in cabbage also have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects, making the plant valuable in herbal medicine.

4. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in cabbage (Brassica oleracea). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health. Tannins in cabbage also have antioxidant effects, potentially reducing inflammation and aiding in wound healing.

5. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Cabbage (Brassica oleracea). It contributes to the plant's natural defense mechanisms and has mild detergent properties. Saponin may support digestive health and has potential anti-inflammatory effects. Its presence in cabbage highlights the plant's role in traditional herbal medicine.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a bioactive compound found in cabbage (Brassica oleracea). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antithrombotic properties. Coumarin may support cardiovascular health and help reduce oxidative stress. Its presence in cabbage highlights the plant's role in traditional herbal medicine.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in cabbage (Brassica oleracea) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. They support digestive health and may help reduce oxidative stress. Terpenoids are naturally occurring in many plants, including cabbage.

8. Essential oil

Essential oil is a volatile compound found in Cabbage (Brassica oleracea). It contributes to the plant's characteristic aroma and has antimicrobial properties. The oil is extracted through distillation and is used in herbal medicine for its potential digestive and anti-inflammatory benefits.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Cabbage (Brassica oleracea). It contributes to the plant's vibrant color and has antioxidant properties. This compound may support immune function and reduce inflammation, making it a valuable component in herbal medicine.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in the medicinal plant Brassica oleracea, commonly known as cabbage. While cabbage is not traditionally classified as an alkaloid-containing plant, certain varieties may contain trace alkaloids that contribute to its medicinal properties, such as anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. These compounds are part of the broader biochemical profile of cabbage, supporting its use in herbal medicine.
