Botanical Identification of Cabbage (Brassica oleracea)

To identify Cabbage (Brassica oleracea) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Cabbage (Brassica oleracea) Monograph

Scientific Classification

Cabbage belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Spermatophyta, class Caryophyllopsida, order Brassicales, family Brassicaceae, genus Brassica, and species Brassica oleracea. These classifications define its botanical identity and place it within the broader plant kingdom.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Cabbage is Brassica oleracea, a member of the Brassicaceae family. It was first described by Carl von Linne in 1753. Common synonyms include Brassica capitata and Brassica botrytis. It is widely known by regional names such as Kohlrabi and Kale in various parts of the world.

Morphological Characteristics

Cabbage (Brassica oleracea) is a biennial plant typically grown as an annual. It has a taproot system with fibrous roots. The stem is thick and fleshy, often forming a compact head. Leaves are large, thick, and arranged in a rosette. The inflorescence is a terminal head of small yellow flowers. The fruit is a siliqua containing numerous seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Cabbage can be distinguished by its tightly packed, dark green, fleshy leaves forming a compact head. It lacks the mustard-like odor of related species and has a smooth, waxy surface. The stalk is short and stout, and the leaves are broadly oval with a pointed tip. It does not produce flowers or fruits under normal growing conditions.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Cabbage (Brassica oleracea) is native to the eastern Mediterranean and Central Europe. It thrives in temperate climates with cool summers and moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils. It is typically found at altitudes between 0 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Cabbage (Brassica oleracea) blooms from late spring to early summer, typically between May and July, depending on the climate. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early fall, around August to September. For identification purposes, the plant is usually harvested in late summer to early fall, before flowering, when the head is fully developed.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Cabbage belongs to the genus Brassica within the family Brassicaceae. Its closest relatives include Brassica rapa and Brassica napus. These species share similar morphological and genetic traits, such as cruciform flowers and segmented pods. The family Brassicaceae is characterized by its four-petaled flowers and siliqua fruits.
