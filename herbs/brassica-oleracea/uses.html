Cabbage (Brassica oleracea) Uses

Cabbage has been traditionally used to treat respiratory infections, including cough, bronchitis, and sinusitis. It may help reduce inflammation and ease symptoms of conditions like otitis media and allergic rhinitis. Its mucilage content can soothe mucous membrane irritation and aid in clearing lung congestion associated with colds and respiratory allergies.

Cabbage (Brassica oleracea) Monograph

1. Respiratory infections

Cabbage contains compounds with antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that can help alleviate symptoms of respiratory infections. Its high vitamin C content supports immune function, while steam from cooked cabbage may ease congestion. Traditionally used in herbal medicine, cabbage is a mild, accessible remedy for common colds and bronchitis.

2. Otitis media

Cabbage, specifically red or green varieties, has been used traditionally to treat otitis media due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Its high vitamin C content supports immune function, while its sulfur compounds may help reduce ear inflammation and infection. Some studies suggest it may aid in clearing mucus and reducing ear pressure.

3. Cough

Cabbage is used to soothe coughs due to its high vitamin C content, which supports immune function. Its anti-inflammatory properties may help reduce throat irritation. A cabbage leaf poultice or brewed tea can provide relief. It is often recommended as a natural remedy for mild respiratory symptoms.

4. Bronchitis

Cabbage contains compounds like sulforaphane that help reduce inflammation in the airways. It may support respiratory health by soothing irritated bronchial passages. Traditionally used in herbal medicine, cabbage is sometimes consumed raw or brewed into teas to alleviate bronchitis symptoms. Its mild expectorant properties can aid in clearing mucus from the lungs.

5. Sinusitis

Cabbage contains compounds with anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that can help alleviate symptoms of sinusitis. Its high vitamin C content supports immune function, while steam from cooked cabbage may help clear nasal passages. Traditionally used in herbal medicine, cabbage is a natural remedy for reducing congestion and fighting infection in the sinuses.

6. Allergic rhinitis

Cabbage contains compounds like glucosinolates and quercetin that may help reduce inflammation and histamine response, common in allergic rhinitis. It is traditionally used to support respiratory health and may alleviate symptoms when consumed regularly as part of a holistic approach.

7. Common cold

Cabbage contains compounds like sulfur-containing phytochemicals and vitamin C that may support immune function. Some traditional uses suggest it can help alleviate cold symptoms due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. However, scientific evidence for its effectiveness in treating the common cold remains limited.

8. Mucous membrane irritation

Cabbage contains sulfur compounds that can soothe mucous membrane irritation. Its anti-inflammatory properties help reduce swelling and discomfort. Chewing raw cabbage or applying cabbage juice can provide localized relief. It is particularly effective for conditions like gastritis and sore throats.

9. Lung congestion

Cabbage has been traditionally used to alleviate lung congestion due to its mucilage content, which helps soothe irritated airways. It also acts as a mild expectorant, aiding in the removal of mucus from the respiratory tract. Rich in antioxidants and vitamins, cabbage supports overall respiratory health and may reduce inflammation in the lungs.

10. Respiratory allergies

Cabbage contains compounds that may help reduce inflammation and support immune function, potentially alleviating symptoms of respiratory allergies. It is rich in antioxidants and sulfur compounds, which can help neutralize allergens and reduce histamine response. Some traditional remedies use cabbage juice to soothe allergic reactions in the respiratory tract.
