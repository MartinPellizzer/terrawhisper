What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Blyxa echinosperma has slender, submerged leaves that are linear to narrowly lanceolate, typically measuring between 1 and 5 centimeters in length.

These leaves grow in dense, rosette-like clusters from a creeping rhizome. The plant features small, inconspicuous flowers that develop on short stalks near the leaf axils. Its reproductive structures eventually produce small, nutlet-like fruits that are characteristic of the genus.

The overall morphology is adapted for life in aquatic environments where its fine foliage can efficiently interact with water currents.
