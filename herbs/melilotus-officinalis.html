Medicinal Parts of melilotus officinalis

Melilotus officinalis has many medicinal parts, such as its leaves, flowers, and roots, which are utilized for their therapeutic properties.

The leaves of Melilotus officinalis are rich in flavonoids, including kaempferol and quercetin, which have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects.

The flowers of this herb contain coumarin, a compound known for its anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory properties.

The roots of Melilotus officinalis are a good source of isoflavones, such as genistein and daidzein, which have been shown to have estrogenic and antioxidant activities.

Possible Side Effects of melilotus officinalis

Melilotus officinalis can have side effects if used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, including nausea, diarrhea, and stomach cramps.

Prolonged use or high doses of this herb may also lead to kidney problems and liver damage due to its coumarin and furanocoumarin content.

Additionally, allergic reactions such as skin rashes, itching, and respiratory issues may occur in sensitive individuals.

Excessive consumption of Melilotus officinalis may also interact with blood-thinning medications, increasing the risk of bleeding complications.
