What to know about Ambrosia arborescens (golden ragweed) before using it medicinally

Golden ragweed, scientifically known as Ambrosia arborescens, is a versatile and ancient herb that has been prized for its numerous health benefits.

Its leaves, stems, and flowers have been used to treat various ailments such as inflammation, fever, and respiratory issues due to their anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. In culinary traditions, golden ragweed is often used to add flavor and texture to soups, salads, and teas, while its young leaves can be sautéed or added to stir-fries for a nutritious boost. Horticulturally, golden ragweed is known for its ease of growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a popular choice among gardeners and landscapers.

From a botanical standpoint, golden ragweed belongs to the aster family and features bright yellow flowers that are rich in nutrients and antioxidants, while historical records dating back to ancient Egypt and China reveal its use for medicinal and spiritual purposes.
