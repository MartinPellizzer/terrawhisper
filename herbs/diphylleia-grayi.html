What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Diphylleia grayi has large, opposite, ovate leaves that are deeply three-lobed and arranged in two distinct whorls along the stem. Each leaf measures several inches in length and features serrated margins with prominent venation. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that hang from the leaf axils in late spring. These blossoms are followed by fleshy, dark blue to black berries that develop from the ovaries. The stems are somewhat woody at the base and can reach a height of approximately two feet.
