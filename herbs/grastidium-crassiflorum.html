Grastidium crassiflorum

Grastidium crassiflorum, commonly known as the tree daisy, is a flowering plant species in the family Thymelaeaceae.

It is a shrub that has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its various health benefits, including its ability to treat fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions, due to its anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antifungal properties.

The therapeutic actions of Grastidium crassiflorum include its use as an antipyretic, analgesic, and anti-inflammatory agent, which is attributed to its bioactive constituents, including flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids.

These bioactive compounds have been found to have antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial activities, which contribute to the plant's therapeutic properties.
