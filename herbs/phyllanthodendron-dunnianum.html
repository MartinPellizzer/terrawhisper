Phyllanthodendron dunnianum

What's the taxonomical classification of Phyllanthodendron dunnianum?

Phyllanthodendron dunnianum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Malpighiales. This plant is further categorized into the family Phyllanthaceae, which contains the genus Phyllanthodendron, and is specifically identified by its species name, dunnianum.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Phyllanthodendron dunnianum can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested or handled improperly. Contact with the plant's sap or foliage may result in localized redness, itching, or a burning sensation on the skin. Some individuals might experience more severe hypersensitivity symptoms like swelling or rashes depending on their sensitivity levels. If any part of the plant is consumed, it could lead to nausea, vomiting, or stomach pain. It is important to use protective gear when handling this species to mitigate these potential adverse effects.
