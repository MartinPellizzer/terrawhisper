Cynanchum giraldii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cynanchum giraldii has climbing, twining stems with opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that possess entire margins and acute apices. The foliage is typically smooth and displays a bright green color during the peak growing season. Small, tubular flowers emerge in clusters, often characterized by a pale or creamy hue. These blossoms are accompanied by specialized structures known as pollinia which are characteristic of the Apocynaceae family. The plant's overall structure allows it to scramble over surrounding vegetation to reach sunlight.
