Additionally, the cotton aphid (Aphis gossypii) can transmit viruses such as cotton leaf curl virus and yellow mosaic virus to G. herbaceum, leading to stunted growth, distorted leaves, and reduced fertility. Fungal diseases like powdery mildew (Erysiphe cichoracearum) and root rot (Fusarium oxysporum f. sp.

vasinfectum) are also prevalent in G. herbaceum, causing defoliation, leaf spotting, and reduced plant vigor. Bacterial diseases like bacterial blight (Xanthomonas campestris pv. malvacearum) can cause lesions on leaves and stems, leading to defoliation and plant death. These pests and diseases can significantly impact the growth and productivity of G. herbaceum, making it essential to implement integrated pest management strategies to minimize their effects.
