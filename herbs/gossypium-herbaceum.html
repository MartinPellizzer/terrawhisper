Gossypium herbaceum seed, commonly known as cottonseed, is a vital part of the plant with significant medicinal value.

The herb can also cause skin rashes and irritation, particularly when it comes into contact with the skin, resulting in redness, itching, or a burning sensation. Prolonged exposure may lead to eye irritation, including redness, watering, and a gritty feeling, which can be uncomfortable and affect vision.

In more severe cases, the herb may cause digestive upset, such as nausea, stomach pain, or vomiting, especially if ingested in large amounts. Additionally, Gossypium herbaceum may induce respiratory issues, including coughing, throat irritation, and breathing difficulty, which can be particularly dangerous for individuals with pre-existing respiratory conditions.

These side effects highlight the importance of caution when handling or using this herb, especially for those with known sensitivities or allergies.
