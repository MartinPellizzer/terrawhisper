Gossypium herbaceum

What's the taxonomical classification of Gossypium herbaceum?

Gossypium herbaceum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Malvales and the family Malvaceae, eventually reaching its specific classification within the genus Gossypium. Finally, the plant is identified at the species level as herbaceum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gossypium herbaceum has a perennial, shrubby habit characterized by erect, woody stems that often possess stellate hairs and occasional spines. The leaves are typically palmately lobed with three to five lobes and feature serrated margins. Its flowers are solitary and cream-colored, transitioning to a pinkish hue as they age. The fruit is a dehiscent capsule that contains multiple seeds covered in fine, white lint. These seeds are eventually released from the capsule to facilitate reproduction in its natural habitat.

What are the pharmacological activities of Gossypium herbaceum?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological potential through its anti-diabetic, antimicrobial, antimutagenic, antiviral, and hypolipidemic activities. Its phytochemical constituents work to lower blood glucose levels and reduce lipid profiles in the bloodstream. The herb also exhibits a strong ability to inhibit the growth of various pathogens and counteract viral replication. Furthermore, its antimutagenic properties help protect cells from genetic damage caused by environmental stressors. These diverse medicinal effects suggest that Gossypium herbaceum serves as a valuable source for developing new therapeutic agents.

As discussed in a study, Gossypium herbaceum powder and its aqueous and ethanol extracts significantly (P<0.05) reduced normoglycemia, serum cholesterol, triglyceride, and urea in a dose dependent order of 200 to 300 mg/kg of body weight in normal rabbits. GH and GHE completely ameliorated the Alloxan effect on serum levels of glucose, cholesterol, triglyceride, creatinine, and urea in Alloxan-induced diabetic rabbits, while GHA partially blocked these effects. Phytochemical analysis of GH and its extracts revealed the presence of flavonoids and phenolic compounds. Histopathological examination demonstrated that GH, GHA, and GHE provide a protective effect against the Alloxan-induced destruction of pancreatic β-cells. Furthermore, GH, GHA, and GHE did not cause any change in food or water intakes or in the liver, spleen, kidney, lung, and heart of treated rabbits. These data indicate that GH and its aqueous and ethanol extracts have promising anti-diabetic and hypolipidemic effects.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds isoquercitrin, gossypol, and hyperoside. Isoquercitrin is a flavonoid known for its potential antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties in various biological systems. Gossypol serves as a complex polyphenolic compound that can influence cellular processes and reproductive health. Hyperoside acts as another important glycoside that contributes to the plant's overall pharmacological profile. These substances work together to define the chemical complexity and therapeutic potential of the species.

As discussed in a study, the crude extraction of cotton flower contains cis-diol flavonoid glycosides such as isoquercitrin (ISO) and hyperoside (HYP). Using a poly(VPBA-co-EDMA) polymer for solid phase extraction, high purity ISO and HYP exceeding 99.96% can be obtained. The recovery rates for these medicinal compounds are 83.7% for ISO and 78.6% for HYP. The study utilized a specific polymerization condition involving an M/C ratio of 7:3 and a PS concentration of 40 mg/ml.
