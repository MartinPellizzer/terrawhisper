Ligularia rumicifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Ligularia rumicifolia?

Ligularia rumicifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic classification continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Asterales. As a member of the family Asteraceae, it is specifically identified by the genus Ligularia, with its unique designation being the species rumicifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ligularia rumicifolia has large, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically 10 to 30 centimeters long with serrated margins and a distinctively rugose texture. The leaf blades feature prominent venation and a deep green color that provides a dense ground cover. Its stems are erect and can reach significant heights, supporting large inflorescences composed of numerous bright yellow ray florets. These flower heads are arranged in terminal clusters, creating a striking visual display during the blooming season. The overall structure of the plant is robust, characterized by its herbaceous habit and substantial foliage mass.
