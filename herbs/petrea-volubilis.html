Petrea volubiilis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Petrea volubiis has woody, twining stems that produce opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated margins and a rough texture. The foliage remains evergreen in tropical climates, providing a dense backdrop for its distinctive floral displays. Its inflorescences consist of small, pale lavender fertile flowers surrounded by larger, spreading sterile bracts that shift from white to deep purple. These clusters form dense, pendulous panicles that drape heavily from the climbing vines. The plant eventually develops small, fleshy drupes that serve as its primary method of seed dispersal.
