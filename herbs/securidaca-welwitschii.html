What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Securidaca welwitschii has a woody, shrubby or small tree-like growth habit characterized by pinnate leaves and distinctive, fleshy, club-shaped roots. The leaves are composed of several pairs of leaflets that are typically elliptical or oblong in shape. Its inflorescences consist of dense, drooping racemes containing small, pale or cream-colored flowers. The fruit is a fleshy, somewhat indehiscent drupe that often remains attached to the plant. These morphological features help the plant adapt to its specific ecological niche within the African landscape.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Securidaca welwitschii can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, and potential systemic toxicity if ingested or applied improperly. Ingesting concentrated doses may lead to nausea, vomiting, or more serious internal complications. Therefore, extreme caution is required when handling any part of this species.
