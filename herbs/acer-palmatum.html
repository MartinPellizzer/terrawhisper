Acer palmatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Acer palmatum?

Acer palmatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Sapindales, it falls into the family Sapindaceae, which contains a diverse array of woody plants. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its genus, Acer, and its specific species, palmatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Acer palmatum has small, opposite, palmately lobed leaves that typically feature five to seven pointed lobes and serrated margins. These leaves vary in color from bright green to deep red depending on the specific cultivar and seasonal changes. The plant grows as a deciduous shrub or small tree with a delicate, spreading branching structure. Its bark is relatively smooth and often transitions to a grayish tone as the specimen matures. During the flowering season, it produces small, inconspicuous red or purple flowers that emerge from the leaf axils.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Japan, where it thrives in temperate climates. What are the pharmacological activities of Acer palmatum?

This plant has anti-apoptotic, anti-inflammatory, hepatic disorder treatment, retinal degeneration protection, and hemostasis pharmacological activities. Its bioactive compounds function to inhibit programmed cell death and reduce systemic inflammation in various tissues. The plant also demonstrates significant therapeutic potential in managing liver dysfunction and protecting the eyes from retinal degeneration. Additionally, its properties contribute to effective hemostasis by assisting in the regulation of blood clotting processes. These diverse medicinal qualities make Acer palmatum a subject of interest for multiple therapeutic applications.

As discussed in a study, the hot-water extract of Acer palmatum leaves, known as KIOM-2015EW, exhibits anti-inflammatory and anti-apoptotic effects in human corneal epithelial cells. When cells are exposed to hyperosmolar medium, KIOM-2015EW treatment significantly reduces the mRNA and protein expression of pro-inflammatory mediators such as TNF-α, IL-1β, and IL-6. The extract also inhibits the activation of MAPK signaling, specifically targeting ERK, p38, and c-JUN N-terminal kinase (JNk). Furthermore, KIOM-2015EW attenuates the increase in NF-κB phosphorylation induced by hyperosmolar stress. These results demonstrate that KIOM-2015EW protects the ocular surface by suppressing inflammation.
