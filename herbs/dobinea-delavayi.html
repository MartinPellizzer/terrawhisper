Dobinea delaavayi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further categorized under the order Sapindales. It is a representative of the family Anacardiaceae, falling under the genus Dobinea, with its specific designation being the species delaayi.

What are the pharmacological activities of Dobinea delavayi?

This plant has significant pharmacological activities including anti-malarial, antitumor, and cytotoxic properties. Research indicates that the chemical constituents found within Dobinea delaavayi can effectively inhibit the growth of malaria parasites. The plant also demonstrates notable antitumor activity by interfering with the progression of certain cancer cell lines. Furthermore, its inherent cytotoxicity allows it to induce cell death in various targeted biological systems. These diverse medicinal capabilities highlight the therapeutic potential of its specialized secondary metabolites.

According to a study published by "Planta medica", three new sesquiterpenes named dobinins A-C (1-3) were isolated from the root of Dobinea delavayi along with five known compounds. These three new compounds were screened for antitumor activity in vitro and demonstrated definite cytotoxic activity against the human tumor cell line HL-60. The cytotoxic activity of compounds 1-3 was measured with IC50 levels of 8.0 x 10-5, 4.7 x 10-5, and 5.1 x 10-5 M, respectively.

These various medicinal compounds collectively define the pharmacological potential of Dobinea delavayi.

According to a study published by "Bioorganic chemistry", five dimeric eudesmane sesquiterpenoid dimers named Dodelates A-E (1-5) were isolated from the roots of Dobinea delavayi. These 5 angeloylated eudesmane sesquiterpenoid dimers had their structures elucidated through extensive spectroscopic data and single-crystal X-ray diffraction analyses. A possible biosynthetic pathway for these 5 compounds was also proposed. Among these, compounds 1 and 3 exhibited moderate antimalarial activities against Plasmodium yoelii BY265RFP.

Some traditional practices also suggest its application in supporting the body during oncological struggles. Beyond these major uses, it is frequently employed to soothe digestive issues and skin irritations. This versatile botanical serves as a cornerstone in several regional medicinal traditions.

According to a study published by "Natural products and bioprospecting", the peeled roots of Dobinea delavayi were used to isolate the previously undescribed angeloylated noreudesmane sesquiterpenoid, dobinin O (1). This specific compound (1) exhibited moderate antimalarial activity against Plasmodium yoelii BY265RFP. At a dose of 30 mg/kg/day, the inhibition ratio was measured at 17.8 ± 13.3%. In addition to compound 1, four known euudesmane sesquiterpenoids (2-5) were also isolated from the plant.
