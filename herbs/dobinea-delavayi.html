Dobinea delavayi

What's the taxonomical classification of Dobinea delavayi?

Dobinea delaavayi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further categorized under the order Sapindales. It is a representative of the family Anacardiaceae, falling under the genus Dobinea, with its specific designation being the species delaayi.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Dobinea delavayi targets various inflammatory mediators and signaling enzymes, specifically inhibiting cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2) and inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS) to reduce oxidative stress. The plant contains bioactive alkaloids and flavonoids that modulate the expression of pro-inflammatory cytokines such as tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-alpha) and interleukin-6 (IL-6). These phytochemicals also interact with the nuclear factor kappa B (NF-kB) pathway to suppress the transcription of genes involved in the inflammatory cascade. By regulating these specific protein pathways, the plant exerts its potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory medicinal effects. This targeted modulation of enzyme activity helps stabilize cellular homeostasis during physiological stress.

What are the therapeutic actions of Dobinea delavayi?

Dobinea delavayi has significant therapeutic potential as an anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial agent used primarily to treat skin conditions and digestive ailments. What are the herbal preparations of Dobinea delavayi?

Dobinea delavayi is prepared as a decoction of the roots, a dried plant powder, or a concentrated liquid extract for medicinal use. What side-effects this plant can have?

Dobinea delavayi can cause severe gastrointestinal distress including vomiting, intense abdominal pain, and diarrhea if ingested. The plant contains toxic compounds that may lead to significant irritation of the digestive tract. Such symptoms often manifest shortly after consumption as the body reacts to the chemical constituents. In some instances, the toxicity may lead to more systemic issues depending on the amount swallowed. It is essential to avoid contact with the plant to prevent these adverse reactions.
