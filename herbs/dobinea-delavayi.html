Dobinea delavayi

What's the taxonomical classification of Dobinea delavayi?

Dobinea delaavayi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further categorized under the order Sapindales. It is a representative of the family Anacardiaceae, falling under the genus Dobinea, with its specific designation being the species delaayi.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dobinea delavayi has a woody, perennial structure characterized by a sprawling or erect growth habit and small, ovate to lanceolate leaves with entire margins.

The foliage typically presents a smooth texture and a deep green coloration that contrasts with the stem. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the axils of the leaves. The plant often develops a complex root system that assists in its stabilization within rocky or mountainous terrains.

These morphological features collectively allow the species to adapt to its specific ecological niche.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Dobinea delavayi can cause severe gastrointestinal distress including vomiting, intense abdominal pain, and diarrhea if ingested. The plant contains toxic compounds that may lead to significant irritation of the digestive tract. Such symptoms often manifest shortly after consumption as the body reacts to the chemical constituents. In some instances, the toxicity may lead to more systemic issues depending on the amount swallowed. It is essential to avoid contact with the plant to prevent these adverse reactions.
