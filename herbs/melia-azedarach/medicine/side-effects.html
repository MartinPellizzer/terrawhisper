10 Possible Side Effects Of Melia Azedarach (Chinaberry)

Melia azedarach has some side effects when used improperly, such as nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea.

These side effects can occur if the herb is consumed in excessive amounts, contaminated with heavy metals, or taken with certain medications.

For example, excessive consumption can worsen conditions like irritable bowel syndrome, and contamination can increase the risk of mercury poisoning.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Melia azedarach if used imporperly.

Melia azedarach causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

This is due to the presence of toxic compounds like amygdalin and linamarin in its leaves, seeds, bark, and fruit, which can trigger an immune response. Additionally, the plant's essential oils, including melianol and azedarachin, may also contribute to these adverse effects.

As a result, people exposed to Melia azedarach may experience symptoms such as skin irritation, respiratory problems, and gastrointestinal disturbances.
