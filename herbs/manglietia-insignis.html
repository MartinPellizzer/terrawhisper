Manglietia insignis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Manglietia insignis has large, elliptical leaves that are approximately 15 to 25 centimeters long, featuring a smooth texture and a deep green color. The woody branches exhibit a spreading growth habit that supports a dense canopy of foliage. Its flowers are prominent, characterized by numerous thick, creamy-white petals that emerge from large, rounded buds. These blossoms often possess a subtle fragrance and are positioned at the ends of the stems. The reproductive structures include a central cluster of carpels surrounded by a ring of stamens.
