Crotalaria cylindrocarpa

What's the taxonomical classification of Crotalaria cylindrocarpa?

Crotalaria cylindrocarpa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Fabales. The plant is further categorized within the family Fabaceae, a group known for its diverse legumes. Specifically, it is identified under the genus Crotalaria, with the specific epithet being cylindrocarpa.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Crotalaria cylindrocarpa contains various pyrrolizidine alkaloids, flavonoids, and sterols that serve as its primary bioactive components. These chemical constituents are often studied for their potential pharmacological properties in traditional medicine. Specifically, the alkaloids within the genus are known to interact with biological systems, though they can also pose toxicity risks if not managed. The presence of flavonoids may contribute to antioxidant or anti-inflammatory activities during medicinal application. Researchers continue to investigate these specific compounds to understand their full therapeutic potential.

What are the therapeutic actions of Crotalaria cylindrocarpa?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, and various types of inflammatory conditions. Local healers often apply the crushed leaves or a prepared paste directly to affected areas to promote healing. It is frequently utilized in traditional medicine to address skin eruptions and minor cuts. Some practitioners also use the plant to alleviate symptoms associated with internal ailments through specific decoctions. The medicinal application of this species focuses primarily on its topical antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
