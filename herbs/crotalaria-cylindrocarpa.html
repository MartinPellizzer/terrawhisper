Crotalaria cylindrocarpa

What's the taxonomical classification of Crotalaria cylindrocarpa?

Crotalaria cylindrocarpa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Fabales. The plant is further categorized within the family Fabaceae, a group known for its diverse legumes. Specifically, it is identified under the genus Crotalaria, with the specific epithet being cylindrocarpa.

What are the herbal preparations of Crotalaria cylindrocarpa?

Crotalaria cylindrocarpa is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste depending on the intended therapeutic application. The dried roots and leaves are frequently boiled in water to create a concentrated liquid used for internal consumption. When treating skin conditions, the plant material is often crushed into a fine paste to be applied directly to the affected area. Some practitioners also utilize alcohol-based tinctures to extract the active alkaloids for long-term storage and standardized dosing. These various methods allow for the efficient delivery of the plant's medicinal properties to different systems in the body.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Crotalaria cylindrocarpa can cause severe liver damage and neurological issues due to the presence of toxic pyrrolizidine alkaloids within its tissues. These alkaloids are metabolized in the body into reactive pyrroles that directly attack hepatic cells. Ingestion can lead to hepatic veno-occlusive disease, a condition where small veins in the liver become blocked. Chronic exposure may result in permanent scarring of the liver, known as cirrhosis, or even fatal liver failure. Furthermore, some species in this genus have been linked to neurological complications if the toxins affect the central nervous system.
