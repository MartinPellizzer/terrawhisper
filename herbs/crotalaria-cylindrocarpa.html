Crotalaria cylindrocarpa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Crotalaria cylindrocarpa has an erect, herbaceous growth habit characterized by cylindrical, woody pods that can reach up to 10 centimeters in length. The stems are typically branched and covered in fine, soft hairs that provide a distinct texture. Its leaves are alternate, trifoliate, and possess elliptical leaflets with smooth margins. The inflorescence consists of bright yellow flowers that emerge from the leaf axils in small clusters. Each pod contains several small, hard seeds that rattle when the dried fruit is shaken.
