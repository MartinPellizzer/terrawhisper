Crotalaria cylindrocarpa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Crotalaria cylindrocarpa has an erect, herbaceous growth habit characterized by cylindrical, woody pods that can reach up to 10 centimeters in length. The stems are typically branched and covered in fine, soft hairs that provide a distinct texture. Its leaves are alternate, trifoliate, and possess elliptical leaflets with smooth margins. The inflorescence consists of bright yellow flowers that emerge from the leaf axils in small clusters. Each pod contains several small, hard seeds that rattle when the dried fruit is shaken.

      What are the herbal preparations of Crotalaria cylindrocarpa?

      Crotalaria cylindrocarpa is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste depending on the intended therapeutic application. The dried roots and leaves are frequently boiled in water to create a concentrated liquid used for internal consumption. When treating skin conditions, the plant material is often crushed into a fine paste to be applied directly to the affected area.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Crotalaria cylindrocarpa can cause severe liver damage and neurological issues due to the presence of toxic pyrrolizidine alkaloids within its tissues. These alkaloids are metabolized in the body into reactive pyrroles that directly attack hepatic cells. Ingestion can lead to hepatic veno-occlusive disease, a condition where small veins in the liver become blocked. Chronic exposure may result in permanent scarring of the liver, known as cirrhosis, or even fatal liver failure. Furthermore, some species in this genus have been linked to neurological complications if the toxins affect the central nervous system.
