Gynocardia odorata

What's the taxonomical classification of Gynocardia odorata?

Gynocardia odorata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Malpighiales and is a member of the family Achariaceae. At the most specific levels of its classification, it is identified by the genus Gynocardia and the species odorata.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Gynocardia odorata contains bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, tannins, and terpenoids that provide its therapeutic properties. These chemical constituents contribute to the plant's documented antimicrobial, antioxidant, and anti-inflammatory activities. Specific studies suggest that its flavonoid profile may help mitigate oxidative stress within biological systems. Furthermore, the presence of saponins and tannins supports its traditional use in treating various skin conditions and digestive ailments. These complex compounds work synergistically to enhance the plant's overall medicinal potential.

What are the therapeutic actions of Gynocardia odorata?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat digestive ailments such as stomach aches, dysentery, and diarrhea, as well as respiratory issues like coughs and asthma. Its medicinal properties are often applied to soothe gastrointestinal inflammation and restore normal bowel function. In addition to digestive support, practitioners use the plant to alleviate symptoms of various skin infections and minor wounds. Some traditional applications also involve using its extracts to manage fever and other febrile conditions. These diverse therapeutic uses stem from the plant's natural antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory compounds.
