What's the taxonomical classification of Dicranopteris pedata?

Dicranopteris pedata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae, this plant follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Gleicheniales. It is uniquely situated within the family Gleicheniaceae and is distinguished by its specific classification under the genus Dicranopteris, ultimately reaching its species designation as Dicranopteris pedata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dicranopteris pedata has large, deeply lobed, pedate fronds that spread out in a shape resembling a human foot. Each fertile frond features pinnate segments that are significantly more divided than the sterile leaves. The stipes are long, dark, and often possess a distinctive reddish or brownish coloration at the base. Small, hair-like scales are typically found along the rachis and the edges of the pinnae. These morphological traits distinguish it from other members of the Osmundaceae family.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for inhibiting the growth of MCF-7, SMMC-7721, and SW480 cancer cell lines. Research indicates that extracts from Dicranopteris pedata exhibit significant cytotoxic activity against these specific types of human cancer cells. Specifically, the plant contains bioactive compounds that interfere with the proliferation of breast, liver, and colon cancer cells. These studies suggest that the phytochemicals within the plant may possess potential anti-tumor properties. Further investigation is necessary to fully understand the molecular mechanisms behind its effectiveness against these particular cell lines.

According to a study published by "Natural products and bioprospecting", the plant Dicranopteris pedata contains three new clerodan-type diterpene glycosides labeled as 1, 2, and 3. These include (5R,6S,8R,9S,10R)-6-O-[β-D-glucopyranosyl-(1→4)-α-L-rhamnopyranosyl]cleroda-3,13(16),14-diene, (5R,6S,8R,9S,10R,13S)-6-O-[β-D-glucopyranosyl-(1→4)-α-L-rhamnopyranosyl]-2-ox-oneocleroda-3,13-dien-15-ol, and (5R,6S,8R,9S,10R)-6-O-[β-D-glucopyranosyl-(1→4)-α-L-rhamnopyranosyl]-(13E)-2-oxoneocleroda-3,14-dien-13-ol. Two known compounds, 4 and 5, were also isolated from the plant. Regarding biological activity, compound 1 exhibited weak inhibitory activities against the SMMC-7721, MCF-7, and SW480 cell lines.
