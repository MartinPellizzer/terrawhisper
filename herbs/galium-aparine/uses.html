Cleavers (Galium aparine) Uses

Cleavers is commonly used for skin conditions such as irritation, rashes, and minor abrasions. It supports wound healing and can alleviate symptoms of skin allergies and itching. It is also used for mild dermatitis, abscesses, and infections, as well as to relieve insect bite reactions.

Cleavers (Galium aparine) Monograph

1. Skin irritation

Cleavers is traditionally used to treat skin irritation due to its mild cleansing and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps soothe rashes, eczema, and minor wounds when applied topically. A decoction or infusion can be used as a wash or compress for localized skin issues. Its gentle nature makes it suitable for sensitive skin in herbal medicine practices.

2. Wound healing

Cleavers is valued for its wound-healing properties due to its high content of silica and mucilage. It promotes tissue repair, reduces inflammation, and supports the regeneration of skin and mucous membranes. Traditionally used for minor cuts, abrasions, and eczema, it is often prepared as a poultice or infused oil. Its gentle action makes it suitable for sensitive skin and chronic wounds.

3. Skin allergies

Cleavers is used to alleviate skin allergies due to its anti-inflammatory and detoxifying properties. It helps reduce redness, itching, and irritation associated with allergic reactions. Often prepared as a tea or poultice, it supports the body's natural healing processes and promotes skin clarity.

4. Minor skin abrasions

Cleavers is effective for minor skin abrasions due to its mild astringent and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce swelling and promote healing when applied topically. A poultice or compress made from fresh leaves can soothe irritation and prevent infection. It is particularly useful for small cuts, scrapes, and insect bites.

5. Skin rashes

Cleavers is used to treat skin rashes due to its anti-inflammatory and detoxifying properties. It helps reduce redness, itching, and swelling associated with eczema and contact dermatitis. A topical infusion or compress can be applied to affected areas for relief. It is often combined with other herbs like chickweed for enhanced effectiveness.

6. Itching

Cleavers is used to alleviate skin itching, particularly in conditions like eczema and urticaria. Its anti-inflammatory and detoxifying properties help reduce irritation and promote skin healing. It is often prepared as a tea or topical application for targeted relief.

7. Mild dermatitis

Cleavers is used externally for mild dermatitis due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It helps soothe irritated skin and reduce redness. A decoction or infused oil can be applied topically to affected areas. It is particularly effective for conditions like eczema or minor rashes.

8. Skin abscesses

Cleavers is used topically to treat skin abscesses due to its anti-inflammatory and detoxifying properties. It helps draw out infection and reduce swelling. A poultice or compress made from fresh leaves can be applied directly to the affected area. It is often combined with other herbs like echinacea or goldenseal for enhanced effectiveness.

9. Skin infections

Cleavers is used to treat skin infections due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and pus in conditions like boils and eczema. A poultice or compress made from fresh leaves can be applied directly to the affected area for localized relief.

10. Insect bites

Cleavers are traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of insect bites by reducing swelling and inflammation. The plant's cooling properties help soothe the skin, and its mild diuretic effect may aid in eliminating toxins. It is often applied topically as a poultice or infused oil to promote healing and prevent infection.
