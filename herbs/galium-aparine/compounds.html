Cleavers (Galium aparine) Compounds

Cleavers contains several primary compounds, including tannins, flavonoids, and coumarin. These compounds contribute to its astringent properties and potential anti-inflammatory effects. Other notable components are phenolic acids, glycosides, and saponins, which may support detoxification and skin health.

Cleavers (Galium aparine) Monograph

1. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Cleavers (Galium aparine). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support wound healing and skin conditions. Tannins also help in reducing inflammation and can aid in digestive health.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Cleavers (Galium aparine) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help support skin health and may aid in reducing swelling. They are found in the plant's seeds and stems, enhancing its traditional use in herbal medicine.

3. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Cleavers (Galium aparine). It contributes to the plant's mild diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarin also acts as a natural anticoagulant, supporting circulation and reducing blood viscosity. Its presence enhances the therapeutic profile of Cleavers in herbal medicine.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Cleavers (Galium aparine). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound supports the plant's traditional use in urinary tract health and skin conditions. Its presence enhances the overall therapeutic profile of Cleavers in herbal medicine.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Cleavers (Galium aparine). It contributes to the plant's diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties. Glycosides in Cleavers support kidney function and may aid in the treatment of urinary tract issues. These compounds are found in the seeds and stems of the plant.

6. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Cleavers (Galium aparine) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, supporting the plant's traditional use in treating skin conditions and urinary tract issues. They also enhance the plant's overall therapeutic profile in herbal medicine.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Cleavers (Galium aparine). It contributes to the plant's mild diuretic properties and helps support urinary tract health. Saponins also have mild anti-inflammatory effects, making them valuable in traditional herbal medicine for cleansing and detoxifying the body.

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of Cleavers (Galium aparine) contains volatile compounds like methyl salicylate and limonene. These contribute to its mild anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. It is often used in topical applications to support skin health and reduce minor irritations.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Cleavers (Galium aparine). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly in supporting urinary health. Alkaloids in Cleavers may help in reducing inflammation and promoting diuresis, making the plant useful in herbal treatments for kidney and bladder support.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Cleavers (Galium aparine). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting skin health and wound healing. Anthocyanins also help reduce inflammation and may aid in detoxification processes.
