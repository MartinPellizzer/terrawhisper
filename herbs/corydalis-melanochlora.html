Corydalis melanochlora

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Corydalis melanochlora has a herbaceous structure characterized by deeply lobed, bluish-green leaves and tubular, nectar-bearing flowers that feature a distinctively long, downward-curving spur. The plant typically grows from a tuberous rhizome and reaches heights consistent with other members of the Papaveraceae family. Its foliage is dissected and displays a characteristic glaucous texture that provides a subtle sheen to the stem and leaves. The floral morphology is specialized for specific pollinators, with petals that often exhibit delicate color gradations. During its reproductive stage, the plant develops elongated seed capsules that eventually split to release numerous small seeds.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?
