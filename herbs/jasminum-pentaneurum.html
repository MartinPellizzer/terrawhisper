Jasminum pentaneurum

What's the taxonomical classification of Jasminum pentaneurum?

Jasminum pentaneurum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Lamiales. This flowering plant is a member of the family Oleaceae and is specifically classified within the genus Jasminum, with its unique identity defined by the species name pentaneurum.

What are the therapeutic actions of Jasminum pentaneurum?

Jasminum pentaneurum has medicinal properties that are primarily utilized for their antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and wound-healing capabilities. The plant contains bioactive compounds that effectively inhibit the growth of various pathogenic bacteria and fungi. These phytochemicals work to reduce swelling and alleviate pain in affected tissues when applied topically. Furthermore, its extracts promote cellular regeneration, which accelerates the healing process for skin lesions and minor injuries. This species is often valued in traditional medicine for treating diverse dermatological conditions.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, and various types of inflammation. The leaves and stems are often prepared into decoctions or topical applications to address localized irritations. Local traditional practitioners frequently utilize its properties to soothe dermatological issues and promote healing in broken skin. Beyond skin ailments, some uses involve managing internal discomfort through controlled preparations. This botanical specimen remains a significant resource in specific regional medicinal practices for managing minor physical ailments.
