Jasminum pentaneurum

What's the taxonomical classification of Jasminum pentaneurum?

Jasminum pentaneurum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Lamiales. This flowering plant is a member of the family Oleaceae and is specifically classified within the genus Jasminum, with its unique identity defined by the species name pentaneurum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Jasminum pentaneurum has woody, climbing stems with opposite, ovate leaves that typically measure between 3 and 7 centimeters in length.

The foliage is characterized by entire margins and a smooth texture, often arranged in pairs along the vine. Its inflorescence consists of small, white, tubular flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These blossoms feature five distinct lobes and produce a fragrant scent to attract pollinators.

The plant's overall structure is defined by its sprawling, twining habit which allows it to ascend surrounding vegetation.

      What are the herbal preparations of Jasminum pentaneurum?

      Jasminum pentaneurum is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a tincture derived from its leaves and stems.
