Elaeagnus Magna

Elaeagnus magna, commonly known as the sandthorn or silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia and the Americas, valued for its medicinal properties.

It is traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat ailments such as respiratory infections, skin conditions, and digestive disorders due to its high content of flavonoids, tannins, and essential oils. The leaves and fruits of Elaeagnus magna are often prepared as decoctions or poultices to reduce inflammation and promote wound healing. Its ability to thrive in harsh environments also makes it a useful plant for soil stabilization and ecological restoration.

Overall, Elaeagnus magna holds significant potential in both traditional and modern medicinal applications.
