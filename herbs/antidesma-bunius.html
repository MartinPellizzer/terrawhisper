What are the pharmacological activities of Antidesma bunius?

This plant has anti-diabetic, antioxidant, hypoglycaemic, hypolipidemic, and α-glucosidase inhibitory activities. Its antioxidant properties help neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress within the body. The plant also demonstrates significant hypoglycaemic effects by lowering blood glucose levels. Furthermore, it exhibits α-glucosidase inhibitory activity, which prevents the breakdown of carbohydrates into sugar. These combined mechanisms also contribute to its hypolipidemic action by helping to manage lipid profiles.

As discussed in a study, Antidesma bunius extract administered orally at a daily dose of 250 mg/kg body weight for 28 days showed significant hypoglycaemic activities. The extract revealed an 80.5% reduction in blood glucose levels along with a 134% increase in serum insulin. Furthermore, it caused an increase in lipase by 90.7% and liver glycogen levels by 160%. Conversely, the study observed a decrease in amylase activity by 28.2%, TC levels by 40.2%, and TG levels by 28.8% compared to diabetic control rats. These results suggest that Antidesma bunius possesses anti-diabetic activity through the enhancement of hepatic glycogen storage and regeneration of the islet of Langerhans.

These medicinal applications make it a significant resource for addressing various metabolic and systemic conditions.

As discussed in a study, the ethanol seed extract from Antidesma bunius was administered to streptozotocin-induced diabetic rats at a dose of 250 mg kg-1 daily for 6 weeks. In acute toxicity studies, the extract was employed at doses of 500, 1000, 1500, and 2000 mg kg-1 without producing symptoms of mortality. The extract significantly (p<0.05) reduced blood glucose levels, increased body weight, and slightly increased serum insulin. Regarding antioxidant activity, the extract possessed an IC50 of 2174±14.24 mg mL-1. While the extract increased WBC and HDL, it reduced CHOL, LDL, and TG in both normal and diabetic rats.
