What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ophioglossum reticulatum has a single, undivided, lanceolate leaf that is fundamentally split into a sterile, basal vegetative portion and a fertile, spore-bearing spike. The sterile part consists of a fleshy, unbranched frond that lacks complex divisions or pinnation. Rising from this base is a distinct, erect stipe that supports the fertile segment where the sporangia are clustered. These sporangia are arranged in a characteristic reticulate or net-like pattern along the axis of the spike. The overall architecture is relatively simple compared to other fern species due to this lack of leaf dissection.
