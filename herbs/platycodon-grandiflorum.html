Balloon Flower (Platycodon grandiflorum)

Balloon Flower (Platycodon grandiflorum) is a member of the Campanulaceae family, native to East Asia, Korean Peninsula, and China. Traditionally, its roots, flowers, and stems have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its expectorant, anti-inflammatory, and bitter actions, and has a long history of use in traditional chinese medicine, korean traditional medicine, and japanese kampo medicine.

Flowers
Radially symmetrical flowers with blue to purple petals arranged in a single whorl, each petal having a narrow, elongated base and a broad, rounded apex, with a distinctive white throat and five lobes.
Stems
Cylindrical, herbaceous, hollow, glabrous, erect, branching in a dichotomous pattern, with nodes distinctly marked.
