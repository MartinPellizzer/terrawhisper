Sinosenecio eriopodus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sinoseneciio eriopodus has perennial herbaceous stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and are characterized by a dense covering of white, woolly hairs. The leaves are predominantly basal and exhibit a cordate to ovate shape with serrated margins and a noticeably pubescent texture. This pubescence extends to the underside of the foliage, providing a soft, felt-like appearance to the plant. The inflorescence consists of small, capitate flower heads that are arranged in terminal clusters. These floral structures are often surrounded by involucral bracts that match the hairy morphology of the vegetative parts.
