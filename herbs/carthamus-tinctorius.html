Possible Side Effects of carthamus tinctorius

Carthamus tinctorius can have side effects if used improperly, such as allergic reactions, skin irritation, and gastrointestinal issues.

Prolonged consumption of the herb can lead to stomach ulcers, diarrhea, and vomiting due to its high saponin content.

Additionally, excessive intake of carthamus tinctorius can cause an imbalance of blood sugar levels, interact with medications, and exacerbate conditions such as high blood pressure and kidney problems.

It is essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using carthamus tinctorius, especially if taken in large quantities or combined with other medications.
