Safflower (Carthamus tinctorius)

Safflower (Carthamus tinctorius) is a member of the Compositae family, native to Southern Europe, Western Asia, and North Africa. Traditionally, its seeds, flowers, and resin have been used for oil infusions, decoctions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, bitter, and diuretic actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, traditional chinese medicine, and mediterranean herbal traditions.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Annual herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters.
Leaves
Simple, broad leaves with upper surface glabrous and green, lower surface pale green and glaucous, bearing distinct stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Flowers are yellow, actinomorphic, with five petals and a prominent central disk composed of numerous small florets arranged in a radiate pattern.
Stems
Elongated, herbaceous stems with opposite branching, glabrous surfaces, and ribbed texture.
