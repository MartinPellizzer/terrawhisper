Ficus hispida

What's the taxonomical classification of Ficus hispida?

Ficus hispida belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Rosales, the plant is placed in the family Moraceae, which is the group to which its genus, Ficus, belongs. Finally, it is identified at the specific level by its species name, hispida.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Ficus hispida contains diverse phytochemicals including flavonoids, tannins, saponins, alkaloids, and triterpenoids that contribute to its pharmacological activities. These bioactive compounds are primarily responsible for the plant's documented antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Studies indicate that the extracts can help mitigate oxidative stress within biological systems. Furthermore, the presence of specific alkaloids and tannins suggests potential applications in antimicrobial and wound-healing treatments. Researchers continue to investigate these chemical constituents to understand their full therapeutic potential in modern medicine.

What are the therapeutic actions of Ficus hispida?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin conditions like eczema and psoriasis, gastrointestinal issues such as diarrhea, and various inflammatory ailments. Its medicinal properties are often derived from its leaves and bark, which contain bioactive compounds with antimicrobial effects. Practitioners sometimes apply topical preparations to manage wounds or fungal infections on the skin. In some traditional practices, decoctions are ingested to soothe digestive tract irritation. The plant's efficacy in these areas is a subject of ongoing ethnobotanical interest.
