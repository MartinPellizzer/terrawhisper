Beesia calthifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Beesia calthifolia?

Beesia calthifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Ranunculales and belongs to the family Ranunculaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus Beesia and its specific species designation, calthifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Beesia calthifolia has small, elliptical to lanceolate leaves that are arranged oppositely along a slender, upright stem. The foliage typically features a smooth texture and a bright green coloration. Its inflorescence consists of tiny, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These flowers are often solitary or arranged in small clusters. The plant's overall structure is herbaceous and delicate in appearance.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the specific medicinal compounds beesiosiide A, beesiosiide I, beesiosiides G, beesiosiides H, and beesiosiides J-N. These specialized chemical constituents are unique to the Beesia calthifolia species. Researchers study these diverse glycosides to understand their potential therapeutic applications in medicine. The presence of multiple variations within the beesiosiide group suggests a complex phytochemical profile. Each of these distinct compounds may contribute differently to the overall biological activity of the plant.

According to a study published by "Journal of natural products", seven new cycloartane glycosides (1-7) were isolated from the rhizomes of Beesia calthifolia. These compounds include beesiosiides G, H, and J-N, as well as beesioside I (8) and beesioside A. The identified structures include 20xi(1),24xi(2)-epoxy-9,19-cyclolanostane-3beta,16beta,18,25-tetraol-3-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside (1) and 20xi(1),24xi(2)-epoxy-9,19-cyclolanostane-3beta,16beta,18,25-tetraol-3-O-[beta-D-glucopyranosyl-(1-->6)]-beta-D-glucopyranoside (2). Other specific compounds found are (20S,24R)-15alpha,16beta-diacetoxy-20,24-epoxy-9,19-cyclolanostane-3beta,18,25-triol-3-O-beta-D-xylopyranoside (3), (20S,24S)-16beta-acetoxy-18,24;20,24-diepoxy-9,19-cyclanosstane-3beta,15beta,25-triol-3-O-beta-D-xylopyranoside (4), (20S,24S)-16beta-acetoxy-18,24;20,24-diepoxy-9,19-cyclanosstane-3beta,25-diol-3-O-beta-D-xylopyranoside (5), 20xi(1),24xi(2)-epoxy-15alpha-acetoxy-9,19-cyclolanostane-3beta,16beta,25-triol-3-O-beta-D-xylopyranoside (6), and 20xi(1),24xi(2)-epoxy-9,19-cyclolanostane-3beta,12alpha,15alpha,16beta,25-pentaol-3-O-beta-D-xylopyranoside (7).

    What side effects this plant can have?

Internal consumption should be approached with extreme caution due to the risk of stomach upset or nausea.
