What's the taxonomical classification of Rubus idaeopsis?

Rubus idaeopsis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Rosales. As a member of the family Rosaceae, it is further categorized into the genus Rubus, with its specific identification defined by the species name idaeopsis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rubus idaeopsis has woody, arching canes that are densely covered in fine, reddish-brown hairs and sharp, recurved prickles. Small, fragrant white flowers emerge from the terminal ends of the stems, featuring five distinct petals and numerous stamens. These blossoms eventually give way to aggregate fruits that transition from green to a deep red or black color when ripe. The overall structure of the plant allows it to form dense, scrambling thickets in its natural habitat.
