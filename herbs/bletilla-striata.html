Bletilla striata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bletilla striata has linear, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green with distinct longitudinal white or pale stripes running along their length. These leaves grow from a central base and can reach several inches in height depending on the maturity of the plant. The plant produces upright flower spikes that emerge from the foliage to display small, bell-shaped blossoms. Each flower usually features delicate petals colored in shades of pale pink, lavender, or white. These blooms are often accompanied by small, inconspicuous bracts located along the stem. The overall structure is compact and tufted, making it a common sight in boggy or wetland environments.
