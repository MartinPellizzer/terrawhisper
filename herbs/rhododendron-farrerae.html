Rhododendron farrerae

What's the taxonomical classification of Rhododendron farrerae?

Rhododendron farrerae belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Ericales and falls within the family Ericaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic placement is defined by the genus Rhododendron and the species farrerae.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Rhododendron farrerae can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, neurological impairment, and potential respiratory issues if any part of the plant is ingested due to its high concentration of grayanotoxins. These toxins work by binding to sodium channels in cell membranes, which prevents them to close properly. This interference can lead to symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea. In more serious cases, individuals may experience dizziness, blurred vision, or irregular heart rhythms. Careful avoidance of the plant is necessary to prevent these toxic reactions.
