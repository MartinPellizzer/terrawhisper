Malus baccata

What's the taxonomical classification of Malus baccata?

Malus baccata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it is placed in the order Rosales and falls within the family Rosaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus Malus and its specific species designation, baccata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Malus baccata has woody stems, simple alternate leaves with serrated margins, and small pink to white flowers that develop into fleshy pomes. The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate in shape and feature a distinct petiole. Its flowers emerge in clusters from short spurs during the spring blooming season. The resulting fruits are small, globose, and change color as they reach maturity. These morphological traits are characteristic of the Rosaceae family.

As discussed in a study, Malus baccata provides a precursor natural product used for the production of the drug etoposide. This plant is the source of the precursor needed for the semisynthetic method of etoposide production. The study focuses on the biosynthesis of (-) -deoxypodophyllotoxin, which is a late-stage biosynthetic precursor to the etoposide aglycone. Through the transfer of 16 genes, researchers achieved accumulation levels of up to 4.3 mg/g dry plant weight of (-) -deoxypodophyllotoxin. After chromatography, high-purity levels of up to 0.71 mg/g dry plant weight were isolated.
