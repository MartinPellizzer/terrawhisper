What's the taxonomical classification of Rhododendron intricatum?

Rhododendron intricatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the evolutionary path of the order Ericales, placing it within the family Ericaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Rhododendron and is specifically known by its species name, intricatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhododendron intricatum has a low-growing, spreading growth habit characterized by densely branched, woody stems that form thick mats or shrubs up to 0.5 meters in height. The leaves are small, evergreen, and elliptical in shape, typically measuring between 1 and 3 centimeters in length. These leaves possess a leathery texture and are arranged alternately along the intricate, twiggy branches. During the flowering season, the plant produces small, bell-shaped blossoms that are usually pale pink to white. The overall structure of the plant is defined by its tangled, interlacing branch pattern which gives it its specific name.
