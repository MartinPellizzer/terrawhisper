Botanical Identification of Annual St. John's Wort (Hypericum annulatum)

To identify Annual St. John's Wort (Hypericum annulatum) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Annual St. John's Wort, Hypericum annulatum, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Garryales, family Clusiaceae, genus Hypericum, and species Hypericum annulatum. This classification places it within a group of flowering plants characterized by specific leaf and flower structures.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Annual St. John's Wort is Hypericum annulatum, a member of the Hypericaceae family. It was first described by Carl von Linné in 1753. Synonyms include Hypericum hirtum and Hypericum annulatum var. hirtum. Common names include Annual St. John's Wort and Yellow St. John's Wort.

Morphological Characteristics

Annual St. John's Wort (Hypericum annulatum) is an upright, herbaceous annual plant with a taproot system. Its stems are slender, branched, and covered in fine hairs. Leaves are opposite, oval, and have a slightly serrated edge. The inflorescence is a terminal cluster of yellow flowers with prominent orange stamens. Each flower produces a small capsule fruit containing numerous tiny seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Annual St. John's Wort can be distinguished by its upright, branched stems with glandular dots, opposite, oval leaves with smooth margins, and bright yellow flowers with five petals and prominent stamens. It differs from similar species like Hypericum perforatum by its lack of leaf dots and smaller, more clustered flowers.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Annual St. John's Wort is native to southern Europe and western Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with full sun. It prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils. It is commonly found at low to moderate altitudes, typically between 0 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Annual St. John's Wort blooms from June to August, producing bright yellow flowers with orange-red stamens. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early autumn, typically between August and October. For identification purposes, the plant is most easily recognized during its flowering period, which is crucial for accurate botanical identification.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Hypericum annulatum is closely related to other Hypericum species, particularly those in the Hypericaceae family. It shares key genus traits like yellow flowers and orange berries, but differs in leaf and stem structure. Its phylogenetic placement highlights its evolutionary ties to other St. John's Wort species within the family.
