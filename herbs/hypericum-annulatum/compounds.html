Annual St. John'S Wort (Hypericum annulatum) Compounds

The primary compounds in Annual St. John's Wort include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and phenolic acids, known for their anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins and terpenoids contribute to its astringent and aromatic qualities, while essential oils provide its characteristic scent. Glycosides and saponins support its medicinal uses, and compounds like coumarin and anthocyanin add to its therapeutic profile.

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Annual St. John's Wort (Hypericum annulatum). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for mood support and skin health.

Tannin is a key compound in Annual St. John's Wort (Hypericum annulatum), contributing to its astringent properties. It helps in reducing inflammation and may support wound healing. Tannins also act as antioxidants, enhancing the plant's overall therapeutic potential.
