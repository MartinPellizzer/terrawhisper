What's the taxonomical classification of Lycoris anhuiensis?

Lycoris anhuiensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Asparagales and the family Amaryllidaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Lycoris, and its unique species designation, anhuiensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lycoris anhuiensis has narrow, linear leaves that emerge from a bulbous base and several funnel-shaped flowers with six tepals that typically display a white or pale pink coloration. The foliage is relatively slender and emerges during the rainy season to support the plant's photosynthetic needs. Each flower is supported by a long, slender scape that elevates the blooms well above the leaf surface. The tepals often feature a delicate texture and may transition in color as they age.
