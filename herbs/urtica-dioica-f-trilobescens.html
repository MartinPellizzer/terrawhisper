Stinging Nettle (Urtica Dioica F. Trilobescens)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Urtica dioica f. trilobescens.

*Urtica dioica f. trilobescens*, a rare variant of stinging nettle, has been explored for its potential medicinal properties due to its high concentration of bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, polyphenols, and minerals. This subspecies is traditionally used in herbal medicine to support urinary health, reduce inflammation, and alleviate symptoms of arthritis due to its diuretic and anti-inflammatory effects. Studies suggest that its leaves may also possess antioxidant properties that contribute to cellular protection and immune support. While research on this specific form is limited compared to the common stinging nettle, its unique genetic makeup may offer novel therapeutic applications. As a result, *Urtica dioica f. trilobescens* continues to be of interest in both traditional and modern pharmacological research.
