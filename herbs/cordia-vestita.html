Cordia vestita

Cordia vestita, commonly known as Indian cherry or Wild Crape Myrtle, is a small tree or shrub native to the tropical regions of Asia and Australia.

The plant has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues, due to its reported health benefits of reducing inflammation, improving digestion, and boosting the immune system.

The therapeutic actions of Cordia vestita are attributed to its antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antibacterial properties, which contribute to its ability to soothe and protect the body.

The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Cordia vestita can have side effects if used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, allergic reactions, and interactions with certain medications.
