Cordia vestita

Cordia vestita, also known as the Tree of Life or Silver Buttonwood, is a tropical plant species native to the Caribbean and Central America, characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of small white flowers.

The plant has been used for centuries in traditional medicine due to its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiseptic properties.

The health benefits of Cordia vestita include its ability to treat fever, sore throats, and respiratory issues, as well as its use as an antiseptic to treat wounds and skin infections.

In herbal preparations, the plant is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat various ailments and promote overall health and well-being.
