Cymbopogon goeringii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cymbopogon goeringii has perennial, tufted, perennial grass-like foliage characterized by slender, linear leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length and feature slightly serrated margins. The leaf blades are typically light green and possess a somewhat rigid, upright growth habit. Its stems are short, forming dense clumps or tussocks that serve as the foundation for the plant's structure. During the flowering season, it produces small, inconspicuous panicles that rise above the foliage. These inflorescences consist of numerous tiny spikelets arranged in a branched manner. The overall morphology allows it to thrive in various meadow and grassland environments.

Ingesting parts of the plant could potentially lead to stomach aches, nausea, or vomiting.
