Licaria nayaritensis

Licaria nayaritensis, commonly known as the Mexican stinkwood, is a plant species in the family Lauraceae, native to Mexico.

Possible Side Effects of Licaria nayaritensis

Licaria nayaritensis can have side effects if used improperly, such as causing gastrointestinal issues, including nausea, diarrhea, and stomach pain, due to its high saponin content.

Prolonged consumption may also lead to kidney damage and impaired liver function.

Additionally, excessive use of the plant may trigger allergic reactions, resulting in skin irritation, itching, and respiratory problems.

In severe cases, improper use of Licaria nayaritensis may even cause seizures and other neurological complications.
