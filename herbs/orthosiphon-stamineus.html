Java tea

Orthosiphon stamineus

Java Tea (Orthosiphon stamineus) is a medicinal plant native to Southeast Asia, commonly used for its diuretic properties and its ability to support kidney health by helping to reduce urinary tract infections and improve bladder function.

Botanical Identification

Java Tea, scientifically known as Orthosiphon stamineus, belongs to the family Lamiaceae. Commonly called Java tea or Katuk in parts of Southeast Asia, it is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and small white flowers, with roots used in traditional medicine. It is also known by various regional names in herbal literature.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Lamiales
  • Order: Lamiales
  • Family: Lamiaceae
  • Genus: Orthosiphon
  • Species: Orthosiphon stamineus

Java Tea contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to support various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in traditional herbal medicine.

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for kidney health and as a diuretic to support urinary system functions.

Extract: Used for managing hypertension and kidney disorders due to its bioactive compounds.

Safety Profile

Java Tea, also known as Orthosiphon stamineus, is generally considered safe when consumed in moderate amounts. However, it may interact with certain medications, particularly those affecting the kidneys or blood pressure. Individuals with kidney disease or those on diuretics should consult a healthcare provider before use.
