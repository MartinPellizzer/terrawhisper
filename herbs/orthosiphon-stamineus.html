Java Tea, scientifically known as Orthosiphon stamineus, is a medicinal herb native to Southeast Asia, widely used for its health-promoting properties.

It is often referred to as a natural diuretic and is valued for its adaptogenic qualities, helping the body manage stress and maintain balance. Scientific and Botanical Profile

Java Tea, with botanical name Orthosiphon stamineus, is a medicinal plant belonging to the family Lamiaceae, commonly known as the mint family.

It is also referred to by various common names such as Pennywort, Kaphi, Siam Weed, Horse Leaf, Kalmegh, and Mint Weed, among others. Native to Southeast Asia, including countries like India, Indonesia, and Malaysia, this plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Morphologically, it is a herbaceous perennial with square stems, opposite leaves that are often hairy, and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in whorls along the stem.

It is also known by names such as Coleus Forskohlii, Kopor, Karela, Mint Bush, Kapha Parnam, Bog Orchid, and Kapha Kudam, reflecting its widespread use in traditional medicine across different cultures.

History and Cultural Relevance

Java Tea was used for centuries in Southeast Asian cultures, particularly in Indonesia, Malaysia, and Singapore, as a traditional herbal remedy with a wide range of health benefits.

Known as Orthosiphon stamineus, it has been integral to local medicinal systems such as Traditional Chinese Medicine and Ayurveda, where it was valued for its diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties. In Indonesia, Java Tea is often incorporated into daily rituals, such as being brewed and consumed during family gatherings or as part of ceremonial offerings to ancestors. Its traditional use in treating urinary tract infections, kidney stones, and hypertension remains relevant today, with modern studies supporting its efficacy in these areas.

As a symbol of cultural heritage, Java Tea continues to be a staple in both traditional and contemporary wellness practices across the region.

It is also rich in essential nutrients such as vitamins B complex, vitamin C, and minerals like potassium, calcium, and magnesium. These compounds work synergistically to support kidney function by promoting diuresis and reducing uric acid levels, making it beneficial for urinary health.

Additionally, the essential oils and terpenes may have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects, enhancing its overall health benefits.

Orthosiphon stamineus has been widely recognized for its diuretic, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties, making it beneficial for supporting kidney function, urinary tract health, and reducing inflammation.

It is particularly effective in aiding the excretion of excess fluids and toxins, which can help manage conditions like hypertension and edema. Unlike other diuretic herbs such as parsley or dandelion, orthosiphon stamineus offers a more balanced approach by reducing the risk of electrolyte imbalance due to its mild and consistent action. Its unique combination of compounds, including flavonoids and saponins, enhances its potency in treating urinary disorders compared to similar herbs.

Forms, Preparation and Usage

Orthosiphon stamineus has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with various forms available including fresh leaves, dried leaves, tinctures, powders, essential oils, and capsules.

It can be prepared as a tea by steeping dried leaves in hot water, or as a decoction by boiling the leaves for a longer period, while an infusion involves a shorter steeping time. Topical applications include using the leaves as a poultice or applying essential oil diluted with a carrier oil. For adults, a typical dosage is 1-2 grams of dried leaves per cup of tea, taken 2-3 times daily, while children should only use it under medical supervision.

The duration of use should be conservative, generally not exceeding two weeks unless advised by a healthcare professional.

Safety, Side Effects and Contraindications

Orthosiphon stamineus can be used as a medicinal plant for its potential diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties, but it should be used with caution due to its potential side effects and interactions.

Common side effects may include gastrointestinal discomfort, such as nausea or stomach upset, and in some cases, it may cause allergic reactions. It may interact with certain medications, including diuretics and blood pressure medications, potentially leading to excessive fluid loss or hypotension. Special populations, such as pregnant or breastfeeding women, should avoid using orthosiphon stamineus due to insufficient safety data, and individuals with chronic illnesses like diabetes or kidney disease should consult a healthcare provider before use.

To ensure safe use, it is recommended to consult a healthcare professional, start with a low dose, and monitor for any adverse reactions.

Harvesting is most effective during the dry season, typically between May and September, when the leaves are most potent; the leaves should be cut just above the node using clean, sharp shears to avoid damaging the plant.

After harvesting, the leaves should be shade-dried in a well-ventilated area to preserve their medicinal properties, then stored in airtight containers in a cool, dark place or refrigerated to maintain potency and extend shelf life.
