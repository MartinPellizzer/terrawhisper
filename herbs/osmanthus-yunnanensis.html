Osmanthus yunnanensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Osmanthus yunnanensis has an evergreen shrub or small tree habit characterized by opposite, leathery leaves that are elliptical to lanceolate in shape with entire margins and a glossy dark green upper surface. The foliage typically measures several centimeters in length and possesses a distinctively smooth texture. Small, fragrant, white to cream-colored flowers emerge in clusters from the leaf axils during the blooming season. These flowers are actinomorphic and possess four petals, which is a defining trait of the genus. The plant also produces small, fleshy drupes that transition in color as they mature.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of southwest China, specifically within the Yunnan, Sichuan, and Guizhou provinces. It thrives primarily in high-altitude habitats where temperate conditions prevail. The species is often found growing in the understory of broad-leaved forests or along shaded slopes. Its distribution is closely tied to specific moisture and soil requirements found in these subtropical mountain ecosystems. Localized populations are frequently scattered throughout the rugged terrain of the Hengduan Mountains.
