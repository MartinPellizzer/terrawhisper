Alstonia rostrata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Alstonia rostrata has small, elliptical leaves with entire margins, a woody stem, and distinctive beak-like floral structures. The foliage is typically arranged in an opposite or sub-opposite pattern along the branches. Its flowers are characterized by a specialized rostrum that gives the species its specific epithet. The plant often develops a compact, shrubby growth habit depending on its environmental conditions. These morphological traits are essential for identifying the species within its native habitat.
