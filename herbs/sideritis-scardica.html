Scardic Sideritis (Sideritis scardica) is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans, known for its traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments.

Scardic Sideritis (Sideritis scardica) is a shrub characterized by its upright, branched stems that are typically covered in short, stiff hairs, with small, narrow leaves arranged in opposite pairs and small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in spikes at the tips of the stems.

Distribution

Scardic Sideritis (Sideritis scardica) is native to the mountainous regions of southern Greece, particularly the Peloponnese and the islands of Crete and Rhodes, where it thrives in dry, rocky habitats.

Scardic Sideritis has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat respiratory conditions such as coughs, bronchitis, and asthma, as well as to alleviate symptoms of inflammation and fever.

Scardic Sideritis (Sideritis scardica) has been traditionally used in Greek and Balkan herbal medicine systems for its purported calming and digestive properties.
