Scardic Sideritis

Sideritis scardica

Scardic Sideritis (Sideritis scardica) is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans, known for its traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments.

Botanical Identification

Scardic Sideritis, scientifically known as Sideritis scardica, belongs to the Lamiaceae family. It is also called Scardic ironweed and is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. The plant has hairy stems, gray-green leaves, and pinkish-white flowers. It is sometimes referred to as Sideritis scardica in herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Lamiales
  • Order: Lamiales
  • Family: Lamiaceae
  • Genus: Sideritis
  • Species: Sideritis scardica

Safety Profile

Scardic Sideritis is generally safe when used as directed. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to limited safety data. Consult a healthcare provider if you have a history of allergies or are taking other medications.
