Scardic Sideritis (Sideritis scardica): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

Scardic Sideritis, scientifically known as Sideritis scardica, is a rare medicinal plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, particularly found in Greece and parts of the Balkans, and is valued for its traditional use in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.

What is Scardic Sideritis (Sideritis Scardica)?

Scardic Sideritis, or Sideritis scardica, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans, known for its use in traditional herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and calming properties.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Scardic Sideritis is Sideritis scardica. This plant belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Lamiales, order Lamiales, family Lamiaceae, genus Sideritis, and species Sideritis scardica. It is known as Scardic Sideritis in the Balkans and as Greek Sideritis in Greece. Common names also include Ironwort and Mountain Tea in various regional contexts. The synonyms for this plant include Sideritis scardica subsp. scardica and Sideritis scardica var. scardica, with regional names such as Sideritis kritikos in Greece.
