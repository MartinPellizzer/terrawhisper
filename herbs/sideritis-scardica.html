What to know about Sideritis scardica (ironwort) before using it medicinally

Ironwort, scientifically known as Sideritis scardica, is a herb that has been revered for its impressive health properties, which have been shown to improve respiratory issues and boost immune function.

In the culinary world, ironwort is often used to add depth and warmth to soups, stews, and salads, while its delicate flavor also makes it a popular choice for teas and infusions. From an horticultural perspective, ironwort is relatively easy to grow, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. Botanically speaking, ironwort belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its small white flowers and lance-shaped leaves.

With historical roots dating back to ancient Greece and Rome, where it was used for a variety of ailments, including fever and inflammation, ironwort has long been prized for its medicinal and culinary applications.
