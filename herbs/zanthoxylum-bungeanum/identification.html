Botanical Identification of Chinese Prickly Ash (Zanthoxylum bungeanum)

To identify Chinese Prickly Ash (Zanthoxylum bungeanum) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Chinese Prickly Ash, or Zanthoxylum bungeanum, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Sapindales, family Rutaceae, genus Zanthoxylum, and species Zanthoxylum bungeanum. This classification places it within the flowering plants and highlights its botanical relationships within the Rutaceae family.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The botanical name of Chinese Prickly Ash is Zanthoxylum bungeanum, a species within the Rutaceae family. It was first described by the botanist Johann Friedrich von Bunge. This name is widely accepted in modern botanical literature and is used as the standard scientific designation for this plant.

Morphological Characteristics

Zanthoxylum bungeanum is a deciduous shrub or small tree. It has a taproot system with lateral roots, providing strong anchorage. The stem is straight, often branching, and has rough, fissured bark. Leaves are alternate, pinnately compound with 5-7 leaflets, and are serrated along the edges. The inflorescence is a raceme of yellowish-green flowers. Each flower has five sepals and five petals. The fruit is a red, spiny drupe that splits into two valves, containing one to three seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Zanthoxylum bungeanum can be distinguished by its compound leaves with 9-15 leaflets, each with a distinct serrated margin. The fruit is a yellow-orange drupe with a single seed, and the plant exudes a strong, spicy scent when crushed. These features help differentiate it from similar species like Zanthoxylum schinifolium.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Zanthoxylum bungeanum is native to regions of China, particularly in Sichuan, Hubei, and Shaanxi provinces. It thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils and is commonly found at elevations between 500 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Zanthoxylum bungeanum blooms in late spring to early summer, typically between May and June. Seed formation occurs from late summer to early autumn, around August to September. For identification purposes, the plant is harvested during its fruiting stage, usually in late summer to early autumn.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Zanthoxylum bungeanum belongs to the Rutaceae family, closely related to other Zanthoxylum species. It shares key morphological and genetic traits with its genus, such as thorny stems and pinnate leaves. Its phylogenetic placement highlights its evolutionary ties to other citrus-related plants.
