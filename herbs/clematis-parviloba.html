What's the taxonomical classification of Clematis parviloba?

Clematis parviloba belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further descends through the order Ranunculales and is a member of the family Ranunculaceae. Finally, it is defined by its specific taxonomic placement within the genus Clematis, specifically as the species parviloba.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clematis parviloba has opposite or whorled, ovate to elliptic leaves that measure approximately 3 to 7 centimeters in length and feature entire or slightly wavy margins. The plant features small, inconspicuous flowers that lack showy petals, instead relying on numerous yellowish stamens for pollination. Its climbing habit is supported by delicate, twisting petioles that act as tendrils to grip surrounding vegetation. What are the pharmacological activities of Clematis parviloba?

This plant has cytotoxic pharmacological activity. This specific biological property indicates that the extracts from Clematis parviloba can inhibit the growth or cause the death of certain cells. Researchers often study these effects to determine the potential of the plant in cancer research and drug development. Understanding the mechanisms behind this toxicity helps in identifying which specific compounds drive the cellular destruction. Further investigation is necessary to evaluate how these properties might be applied in controlled therapeutic settings.

As discussed in a study, sixteen triterpenoid saponins (1-16) were isolated from the stems of Clematis parviloba. One of these compounds, parvilobaside A (1), was identified as 23-O-acetyl-hederagenin-3-O-beta-D-ribopyranosyl-(1 --> 3)-alpha-L-rhamnopyranosyl-(1 --> 2)-alpha-l-arabinopyranoside. Among the isolated substances, clematoside S (2) and alpha-hederin (4) demonstrated moderate cytotoxic activities. These activities were tested against four human tumor cell lines, specifically HCT-8, Bel-7402, BGC-823, and A-2780. The resulting IC50 values for these compounds ranged from 1.44 to 6.86 microg/ml.
