Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Kola Nut is Cola glabra, first described by the botanist Antoine Laurent de Jussieu in 1789. It is also known by several synonyms, including Cola acuminata and Cola nitida, which reflect historical taxonomic revisions. Common names include kola nut, African nut, and cola bean, while regional names vary across West African countries. Historically, it was referred to as "kola" in ancient trade records.

Morphological Characteristics

Kola Nut (Cola glabra) is a small evergreen tree with a short trunk and dense crown. It has a fibrous root system that anchors it firmly in the soil. The stem is straight and cylindrical, with a smooth, pale green bark. Leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy green surface with a serrated edge. The inflorescence is a panicle, bearing small white flowers that are clustered in groups. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to reveal two kidney-shaped seeds, which are the edible kola nuts.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Kola Nut (Cola glabra) can be distinguished by its smooth, oblong fruit with a thick, leathery rind and a single, large seed. It differs from similar species like Cola acuminata through its more elongated shape and the presence of a distinct, hard seed. The leaves are also broader and more lanceolate compared to other Cola species.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Kola Nut (Cola glabra) is native to western Africa, particularly countries like Ghana, Nigeria, and Cote d'Ivoire. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and rainfall. It prefers well-drained, fertile soils and is typically found at altitudes between 200 and 800 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Kola Nut (Cola glabra) blooms during the dry season, typically between December and April. Seed formation occurs from May to September. Harvesting is usually done when the fruits turn from green to yellow, indicating maturity, which is key for accurate botanical identification.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Kola Nut (Cola glabra) is closely related to other species within the Cola genus. It shares genetic and morphological similarities with related species such as Cola acuminata and Cola nitida. These species belong to the Malvaceae family, which is characterized by features like palmate leaves and capsule fruits.
