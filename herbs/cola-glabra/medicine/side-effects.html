10 Possible Side Effects Of Cola Glabra (Bitter Kola)

Cola glabra has some side effects when used improperly, such as allergic reactions, skin irritation, and gastrointestinal problems.

These side effects can be caused by individual sensitivities to its alkaloids, or by consuming excessive amounts.

For instance, gastrointestinal problems can worsen for people with pre-existing digestive conditions, causing discomfort and interrupting daily activities, while skin irritation can lead to painful rashes and infections that may affect social interactions and overall well-being.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Cola glabra if used imporperly.

Cola glabra increases blood pressure due to its high concentration of caffeine and saponins.

The caffeine in Cola glabra stimulates the nervous system, causing an increase in heart rate and blood pressure. Additionally, the saponins present in the plant can constrict blood vessels, further contributing to elevated blood pressure levels.

As a result, regular consumption of Cola glabra may lead to hypertension and potentially increase the risk of cardiovascular disease.

Cola glabra causes allergic reactions in some individuals due to its unique chemical composition.

The pod's high concentration of flavonoids, terpenes, and phenolic acids can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing. Additionally, the presence of volatile oils in the plant can cause skin irritation and allergic contact dermatitis.

These compounds can also exacerbate existing allergies or sensitivities.
