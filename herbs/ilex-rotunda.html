Ilex rotunda

What's the taxonomical classification of Ilex rotunda?

Ilex rotunda belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its hierarchical classification, it falls under the order Aquifoliales and the family Aquifoliaceae. Finally, the plant is placed within the genus Ilex, identifying its specific biological identity as the species rotunda.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ilex rotunda has evergreen, leathery leaves that are typically oval or elliptical in shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. These leaves possess a glossy dark green upper surface and a paler underside, often feeling somewhat thick to the touch. The plant features small, white, fragrant flowers that emerge in clusters from the leaf axils during the spring. Following flowering, it produces small, fleshy drupes that transition from green to a deep black color when fully ripe. The woody stems are sturdy and can develop a grayish bark as the shrub or small tree matures.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the Mediterranean Basin, specifically distributed across coastal regions of Southern Europe, North Africa, and Western Asia. It thrives primarily in maquis and garrigue shrublands located near the sea. Its range includes countries such as Spain, France, Italy, Greece, and various nations along the North African coastline. The species favors rocky, calcareous soils and can be found in habitats ranging from sea level up to moderate elevations. Localized populations are often concentrated in areas with mild, wet winters and hot, dry summers.

What are the pharmacological activities of Ilex rotunda?

This plant has analgesic, anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, hepatoprotective, and antibacterial pharmacological activities. Its bioactive compounds work to reduce pain perception and diminish swelling caused by inflammatory processes. The presence of natural antioxidants helps neutralize free radicals, thereby preventing cellular damage from oxidative stress. Furthermore, the extracts demonstrate a protective effect on liver cells against chemical toxins and harmful substances. The plant also exhibits significant inhibitory effects against various pathogenic bacteria, making it a candidate for antimicrobial research.

As discussed in a study, the plant Ilex rotunda is a component of Trilex which demonstrated antipyretic effects by decreasing rat body temperature about 0.8 degree C on yeast induced hyperthermia. This mixture also enhanced the threshold of hot plate induced pain in mice, where the maximum threshold increased 43.8%. Additionally, Trilex could inhibit acetic acid induced inflammatory ooze in the mice abdominal cavity with an inhibitory rate of 34.8%. These results show that the combination containing Ilex rotunda possesses antipyretic, analgesic, and anti-inflammatory effects. Consequently, it could be used to treat acute and chronic pharyngitis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse array of medicinal compounds including syringin, neochlorogenic acid, phenylpropanoids, triterpenoids, and verbascoide. These specific chemical constituents are responsible for many of the plant's therapeutic properties. The phenylpropanoids and verbascoide contribute significantly to its antioxidant capabilities. Furthermore, the presence of triterpenoids often supports anti-inflammatory activities within the organism. Together, these various compounds form a complex phytochemical profile that defines the plant's medicinal utility.

As discussed in a study, Ilex rotunda contains the phenylpropanoid glycoside known as syringin. This compound serves as a main ingredient in numerous medicines, health products, and foods. It provides medicinal effects such as immunomodulatory, anti-tumor, antihyperglycemic, and antihyperlipidemic actions. Syringin is a key component in many Chinese herbal medicines and holds significant research value because of its unique sinapyl alcohol structure. The compound also demonstrates neuroprotection, liver protection, radiation protection, cardioprotection, and bone protection.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause severe gastrointestinal distress including vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea if any part of it is ingested. The presence of toxic saponins within the berries and leaves is the primary reason for these adverse physical reactions. Consuming even small amounts of the fruit may lead to significant irritation of the digestive tract. Beyond stomach issues, accidental ingestion could potentially cause more systemic complications depending on the quantity consumed. It is important to handle the plant with caution to avoid unintended consumption by humans or pets.
