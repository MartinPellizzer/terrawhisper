Ilex rotunda

What's the taxonomical classification of Ilex rotunda?

Ilex rotunda belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its hierarchical classification, it falls under the order Aquifoliales and the family Aquifoliaceae. Finally, the plant is placed within the genus Ilex, identifying its specific biological identity as the species rotunda.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ilex rotunda has evergreen, leathery leaves that are typically oval or elliptical in shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. These leaves possess a glossy dark green upper surface and a paler underside, often feeling somewhat thick to the touch. The plant features small, white, fragrant flowers that emerge in clusters from the leaf axils during the spring. Following flowering, it produces small, fleshy drupes that transition from green to a deep black color when fully ripe. The woody stems are sturdy and can develop a grayish bark as the shrub or small tree matures.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the Mediterranean Basin, specifically distributed across coastal regions of Southern Europe, North Africa, and Western Asia. It thrives primarily in maquis and garrigue shrublands located near the sea. Its range includes countries such as Spain, France, Italy, Greece, and various nations along the North African coastline. The species favors rocky, calcareous soils and can be found in habitats ranging from sea level up to moderate elevations. Localized populations are often concentrated in areas with mild, wet winters and hot, dry summers.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires well-drained, moist soil and a temperate climate with moderate sunlight to thrive during its cultivation process.

Growers typically propagate the species through seeds or stem cuttings during the spring months. Regular watering is essential to maintain consistent soil moisture, especially during the warmer growing seasons. Providing organic mulch around the base of the plant helps regulate soil temperature and suppress competing weeds.

Once established, the plant is relatively hardy and can tolerate various soil types if drainage remains adequate.

What are the pharmacological activities of Ilex rotunda?

This plant has analgesic, anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, hepatoprotective, and lipid-lowering pharmacological activities. Its bioactive compounds work together to reduce pain and mitigate inflammatory responses in biological systems. The antioxidant properties help neutralize free radicals, thereby protecting cells from oxidative stress. Additionally, the plant exhibits significant hepatoprotection by shielding liver tissues from various forms of damage. It also demonstrates a capacity for lipid-lowering, which assists in managing cholesterol levels within the body.

According to a study published by "Zhong yao cai = Zhongyaocai = Journal of Chinese medicinal materials", the plant Ilex rotunda is part of the Trilex composition which exhibits antipyretic, analgesic, and anti-inflammatory effects. This formulation decreased the rat body temperature by about 0.8 degree C during yeast induced hyperthermia. In mice, the maximum threshold of hot plate induced pain increased by 43.8%. Additionally, Trilex inhibited acetic acid induced inflammatory ooze in the mice abdominal cavity at an inhibitory rate of 34.8%. These pharmacological activities suggest it could be used to treat acute and chronic pharyngitis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse profile of bioactive substances including phenylpropanoids, triterpenoids, syringin, neochlorogenic acid, and verboascoside. These specific medicinal compounds are responsible for many of the plant's pharmacological properties. The presence of phenylpropanoids and syringin often contributes to significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. Additionally, the triterpenoids found within the leaves and stems provide various biological activities. Verbascoside and neochlorogenic acid further enhance the therapeutic potential of the species through their unique chemical structures.

According to a study published by "Frontiers in pharmacology", the plant Ilex rotundata Thumb. contains the phenylpropanoid glycoside syringin. This compound serves as a main ingredient in numerous medicines, health products, and foods. Syringin provides immunomodulatory, anti-tumor, antihyperglycemic, and antihyperpipidemic effects. It also offers neuroprotection, liver protection, radiation protection, cardioprotection, and bone protection. Syringin is a key component in many Chinese herbal medicines and holds significant research value due to its unique sinapyl alcohol structure.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating acute gastroenteritis, acute pharyngitis, bacterial infection, gastric ulcer, and inflammation. Its medicinal properties are often utilized to soothe the digestive tract and reduce internal swelling. Specifically, the plant helps alleviate the painful symptoms associated with stomach ulcers and intestinal distress. It also serves as an effective remedy for calming throat irritation during cases of pharyngitis. By combating various bacterial infections, it supports the body's natural healing processes.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", the dried bark of Ilex rotunda is used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat conditions such as tonsillitis, acute gastroenteritis, gastric and duodenal ulcer, rheumatism, and traumatic injury. The plant is also utilized for clearing heat and removing toxin, draining dampness, and relieving pain. Modern pharmacological studies indicate that the plant has activities for protecting the cardiovascular system, lowering lipids, and protecting the liver. Additionally, it has been found to possess anti-inflammatory, anti-tumor, and antibacterial properties. The literature reviewed for these findings covered the period from 1956 to January 2022.     What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause severe gastrointestinal distress including vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea if any part of it is ingested. The presence of toxic saponins within the berries and leaves is the primary reason for these adverse physical reactions. Consuming even small amounts of the fruit may lead to significant irritation of the digestive tract. Beyond stomach issues, accidental ingestion could potentially cause more systemic complications depending on the quantity consumed. It is important to handle the plant with caution to avoid unintended consumption by humans or pets.
