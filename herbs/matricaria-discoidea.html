German Chamomile

Matricaria discoidea

German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea) is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, commonly used for its calming and anti-inflammatory properties, particularly in treating digestive issues and promoting relaxation.

Taxonomy

German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea) is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae, belonging to the genus Matricaria within the order Asterales.

Morphology

German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea) is an annual herb with a prostrate to ascending growth habit, featuring slender, branching stems covered in fine white hairs, and linear, aromatic leaves that are alternate and often recurved at the edges.

Distribution

German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea) is native to southern Europe, particularly the Mediterranean region, and is also found in parts of northern Africa and western Asia.

Traditional Uses

German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea) has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its calming effects on the digestive system, to relieve mild inflammation, and as a sedative to promote relaxation and sleep.

Medicinal Systems

In traditional medicinal systems such as Ayurveda, Traditional Chinese Medicine, and European herbalism, German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea) has been widely used for its calming, anti-inflammatory, and digestive properties.

Phytochemistry

German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea) contains a variety of bioactive compounds, including essential oils such as bisabolol and chamazulene, flavonoids like apigenin and quercetin, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Pharmacology

German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea) exhibits a range of pharmacological properties including anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, anxiolytic, and sedative effects, making it effective in the treatment of digestive disorders, insomnia, and mild anxiety.

Botanical Identification

German Chamomile, scientifically known as Matricaria discoidea, is a member of the Asteraceae family. It is also called Wild Chamomile and is an annual herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant has finely divided leaves and small yellow flowers with white centers, and is sometimes referred to as Chamomilla in older herbal texts.

Active Compounds

German Chamomile contains essential oil, flavonoids, and coumarin as its main bioactive compounds. These compounds work together to provide anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and calming effects. Flavonoids and coumarin may enhance the overall therapeutic potential of the essential oil.

Safety Profile

German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea) is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation due to potential risks. Individuals with allergies to related plants should also exercise caution.
