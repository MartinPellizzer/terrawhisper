What is German Chamomile (Matricaria Discoidea)?

German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea) is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, commonly used in herbal medicine for its calming, anti-inflammatory, and digestive properties.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of German Chamomile is Matricaria discoidea. This plant belongs to the family Asteraceae, order Asterales, class Magnoliopsida, division Magnoliophyta, and kingdom Plantae. It is commonly known as German chamomile in Europe and as wild chamomile in North America. The plant is also referred to by synonyms such as Matricaria chamomilla and Matricaria recutita in botanical and regional contexts.

Plant Description and Morphology

German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea) is a short-lived herbaceous plant with a low-growing, bushy habit, characterized by finely divided, linear to lanceolate leaves and yellow-orange flower heads composed of tiny tubular florets surrounded by a single row of white to pale yellow ray florets.

Native Habitat and Distribution

German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea) is native to southern Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa, with a historical presence in regions such as the Mediterranean, the Middle East, and parts of southern Russia.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The aerial parts of German Chamomile, including the flowers and leaves, are primarily used medicinally due to their high concentration of active compounds such as essential oils, flavonoids, and terpenoids.

Phytochemical Composition

German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea) contains a variety of bioactive compounds, including essential oils such as bisabolol and chamazulene, flavonoids like apigenin and quercetin, and other phytochemicals that contribute to its therapeutic properties in herbal medicine.

Pharmacological Properties

German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea) exhibits notable pharmacological properties, including anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and anxiolytic effects, primarily attributed to its active compounds such as bisabolol and chamazulene.

Mechanisms of Action

German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea) exerts its therapeutic effects primarily through the activation of GABA receptors, modulation of the central nervous system, and the release of anti-inflammatory and antioxidant compounds such as bisabolol and chamazulene.

Therapeutic Uses and Indications

German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea) is primarily used in herbal medicine for its calming effects on the nervous system, digestive support, and mild anti-inflammatory properties.

Preparation Methods and Forms

German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea) is typically prepared as a tea, tincture, or essential oil, with the dried flowers being the primary part used for infusion or extraction.

Dosage and Administration

The typical dosage of German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea) for herbal use ranges from 200 to 400 mg of dried herb per day, divided into two or three doses, though specific formulations may vary depending on the intended therapeutic effect.

Safety, Side Effects, and Contraindications

German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea) is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses, with minimal side effects, but may cause allergic reactions in some individuals, particularly those allergic to ragweed or other plants in the Asteraceae family.

Drug Interactions

German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea) may interact with medications such as anticoagulants, sedatives, and antidiabetic drugs due to its effects on blood clotting, central nervous system depression, and glucose metabolism.

Toxicity and Precautions

German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea) is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses, but it may cause allergic reactions in some individuals, particularly those sensitive to plants in the Asteraceae family, and should be avoided during pregnancy and by people with certain medical conditions such as asthma or gastrointestinal issues.

Cultivation and Harvesting

German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea) is typically cultivated in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure, requiring careful timing of harvest to ensure optimal essential oil content and potency.

Processing and Extraction

German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea) is typically processed by steam distillation of its flowering heads to extract the essential oils, which contain the primary active compounds such as alpha-bisabolol and chamazulene.

Storage and Shelf Life

German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea) should be stored in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture to maintain its potency and shelf life, which is typically 1 to 2 years when properly preserved.

Scientific Research and Evidence

Scientific research has shown that German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea) contains bioactive compounds such as flavonoids and terpenoids, which contribute to its traditional use in treating inflammation, digestive issues, and mild anxiety.

Traditional and Historical Uses

German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea) has been traditionally used for its calming and anti-inflammatory properties in herbal medicine for centuries, particularly in Germany and other European countries.

Regulatory Status

German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea) is generally recognized as safe (GRAS) by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) for use in food, beverages, and dietary supplements, but it is not approved as a drug by the FDA and is subject to specific regulations under the Dietary Supplement Health and Education Act (DSHEA).
