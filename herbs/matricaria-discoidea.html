German Chamomile

Matricaria discoidea

German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea) is a member of the Asteraceae family, traditionally used for treating skin irritations, relieving digestive issues, and being incorporated into teas for cold symptoms. The flower heads are most commonly used in herbal preparations.

Botanical Identification

German Chamomile, scientifically known as Matricaria discoidea, is a member of the Asteraceae family. It is also called Wild Chamomile and is an annual herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant has finely divided leaves and small yellow flowers with white centers, and is sometimes referred to as Chamomilla in older herbal texts.

German Chamomile Identification.

Active Compounds

German Chamomile contains essential oil, flavonoids, and coumarin as its main bioactive compounds. These compounds work together to provide anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and calming effects. Flavonoids and coumarin may enhance the overall therapeutic potential of the essential oil.

  • Essential oil
  • Flavonoid
  • Coumarin

German Chamomile Actions.

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for digestive issues and mild respiratory complaints.

Decoction: Used for gastrointestinal issues and pain relief.

Essential oil: Used for aromatherapy and skin conditions.

Safety Profile

German Chamomile (Matricaria discoidea) is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation due to potential risks. Individuals with allergies to related plants should also exercise caution.
