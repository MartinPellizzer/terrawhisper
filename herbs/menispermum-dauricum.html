What's the taxonomical classification of Menispermum dauricum?

Menispermum dauricum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the diverse order Ranunculales. Specifically, this plant is a member of the family Menispermaceae and is categorized under the genus Menispermum, ultimately reaching its specific identification as the species dauricum.

What are the pharmacological activities of Menispermum dauricum?

This plant has anti-allergic, anti-arrhythmic, anti-inflammatory, anti-platelet aggregation, and anti-thrombotic pharmacological activities. These diverse biological properties suggest that the plant contains specific bioactive compounds capable of modulating various physiological processes. Its anti-inflammatory and anti-allergic effects indicate potential utility in treating hypersensitivity and immune-related disorders. Furthermore, the plant demonstrates cardiovascular benefits through its ability to regulate heart rhythms and inhibit blood clot formation. By preventing platelet aggregation and providing anti-thrombotic actions, it may help reduce the risk of vascular complications.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", the roots of Menispermum dauricum have been used for the treatment of inflammation, allergy and arrhythmia in China. The bisbenzylisoquinline alkaloid dauricine from Menispermum dauricum contributes to anti-arrhythmic effects by prolonging the action potential duration (APD) through the modulation of Ca(2+) and several K(+) channels. Research shows that dauricine inhibits the HERG current (I(HERG)) in a concentration-dependent manner with an IC50 of 3.5 μM. This inhibition of HERG encoded potassium channels shows significant voltage and time dependence and occurs in the open and inactivated states, but not in the closed states. This inhibitory action might serve as a molecular mechanism for the previously reported APD prolongation.
