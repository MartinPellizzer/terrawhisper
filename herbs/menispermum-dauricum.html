Menispermum dauricum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the diverse order Ranunculales. Specifically, this plant is a member of the family Menispermaceae and is categorized under the genus Menispermum, ultimately reaching its specific identification as the species dauricum.

What are the pharmacological activities of Menispermum dauricum?

Its chemical constituents work to reduce systemic inflammation and modulate immune responses to combat allergic reactions. The plant also demonstrates significant cardiovascular benefits by helping to regulate heart rhythms and prevent irregular beats. Furthermore, it inhibits the clumping of blood cells to prevent the formation of dangerous clots. These combined properties make Menispermum dauricum a subject of interest for treating various circulatory and inflammatory conditions.

As discussed in a study, the roots of Menispermum dauricum have long been used in China to treat inflammation, allergy, and arrhythmia. The bisbenzylisoquinline alkaloid dauriciine, derived from Menispermum dauricum, contributes to anti-arrhythmic effects by prolonging the action potential duration through the modulation of Ca(2+) and several K(+) channels. Research using the whole-cell patch-clamp technique in HEK293 cells found that dauriciine inhibits I(HERG) in a concentration-dependent manner with an IC50 of 3.5 μM. This inhibitory action is contingent on channel gating and shows significant voltage and time dependence. Specifically, dauriciine inhibits I(HERG) in the open and inactivated states but not in the closed states. This inhibition of HERG encoded potassium channels may serve as the molecular mechanism for the reported action potential duration prolongation.
