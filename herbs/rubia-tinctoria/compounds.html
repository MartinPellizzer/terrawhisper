Madder (Rubia tinctoria) Compounds

The primary compounds of Madder include tannins, which contribute to its astringent properties, and flavonoids, known for their antioxidant effects. Phenolic acids and coumarin also play roles in its medicinal and coloring properties. Glycosides and saponins support its traditional use in treating skin conditions, while anthocyanins provide its characteristic red color.

Madder (Rubia tinctoria) Monograph

1. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Madder (Rubia tinctoria). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects. Tannins may also support skin health and are commonly found in herbal preparations derived from Madder roots.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Madder (Rubia tinctoria) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting skin health and wound healing. They are found in the roots and are used traditionally in herbal medicine for their therapeutic benefits.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in Madder (Rubia tinctoria). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating skin conditions and inflammation. This compound is responsible for some of the plant's antimicrobial and astringent properties. Its presence supports Madder's role in herbal medicine for wound healing and digestive health.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Madder (Rubia tinctoria). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating skin conditions and as a mild sedative. Coumarin also has anticoagulant properties, though its role in herbal medicine is less emphasized compared to other compounds in Madder.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Madder (Rubia tinctoria). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating skin conditions and as a mild astringent. Glycosides in Madder are also responsible for its characteristic red dye and may have anti-inflammatory properties.

6. Essential oil

The essential oil of Madder (Rubia tinctoria) contains volatile compounds like limonene and geraniol. It is used in herbal medicine for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The oil is often extracted through steam distillation from the plant's roots and leaves. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating skin conditions and infections.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Madder (Rubia tinctoria) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds, such as rubiadin and rubiadinone, are responsible for the plant's antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects. They also play a role in the plant's traditional use for skin conditions and as a dye. Terpenoids enhance the overall therapeutic profile of Madder in herbal medicine.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Madder (Rubia tinctoria). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine, particularly for its potential anti-inflammatory and detoxifying properties. Saponins may support liver function and aid in the elimination of toxins from the body.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Madder (Rubia tinctoria). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, though it is less prominent than other constituents like anthraquinones. Alkaloids in Madder are generally present in smaller amounts and are not the primary active components used in traditional herbal preparations.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Madder (Rubia tinctoria). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in dyeing and has antioxidant properties. In herbal medicine, anthocyanin may support circulatory health and skin vitality. Its presence highlights Madder's historical and therapeutic significance.
