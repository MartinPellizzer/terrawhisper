Euonymus dielsianus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further falls into the order Celastrales and is a member of the family Celastraceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement within the genus Euonymus and its designation as the species dielsianus.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Euonymus dielsianus can cause severe gastrointestinal distress including vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal pain if any part of the plant is ingested. The berries and leaves contain toxic alkaloids that may lead to more serious systemic issues such as increased heart rate or tremors. Skin contact with the plant can trigger localized allergic reactions like redness, itching, or dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Because of these potential risks, children and pets should be kept away from the plant to prevent accidental poisoning. Proper precautions are necessary when pruning or handling this species to avoid contact with its irritating compounds.
