Microsorum punctatum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Microsorum punctatum has creeping, slender rhizomes that are dark brown to black and covered with closely appressed, lanceolate scales. The fronds are pinnate or bipinnate, featuring elongated, leathery leaflets that are typically elliptic or oblong in shape. These leaflets possess entire margins and are arranged symmetrically along the central rachis. Small, circular sori are located on the undersides of the pinnules, often appearing in distinct rows. The overall structure of the plant allows it to thrive in humid, shaded environments by anchoring itself to rocky or woody substrates.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, including countries such as Thailand, Malaysia, Indonesia, and parts of southern China. Its distribution is heavily concentrated in areas characterized by high annual rainfall and consistent warmth. Because it thrives in moisture-rich habitats, it is often found growing on damp rocks or decaying wood in these specific zones.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Microsorum punctatum can cause skin irritation, contact dermatitis, or allergic reactions in individuals sensitive to its organic compounds. While it is often used in various traditional contexts, direct contact with the sap or foliage may lead to redness and itching. Some people might experience localized swelling if the plant touches sensitive areas of the body. There is limited scientific data regarding systemic toxicity, so ingestion should be strictly avoided to prevent potential digestive distress. Always exercise caution when handling this species to minimize the risk of a dermatological response.
