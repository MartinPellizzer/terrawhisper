Coral tree

Saraca asoca

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Saraca asoca, commonly known as Coral Tree, belongs to the Fabaceae family. It is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent. The plant features bright orange flowers, compound leaves, and a woody root system. It is also referred to as Indian Laburnum and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for centuries.

Active Compounds

The Coral Tree contains key active compounds such as alkaloids, flavonoids, and glycosides. These compounds work together to provide its medicinal effects, with flavonoids contributing to anti-inflammatory properties and glycosides supporting cardiovascular health. Alkaloids may enhance the overall therapeutic potential of the plant.

  • Alkaloid
  • Flavonoid
  • Glycoside

System Condidtion Action

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory infections.

Powder: Used for general health support and as a dietary supplement.

Extract: Used for managing diabetes and improving cardiovascular health.

Safety Profile

The coral tree (Saraca asoca) has a generally safe profile when used as a herbal remedy. However, it is contraindicated during pregnancy and lactation due to potential risks to fetal and infant development. Caution is also advised for individuals with kidney disorders, as the herb may affect renal function.
