Coral Tree (Saraca asoca)

Coral Tree (Saraca asoca) is a member of the Bignoniaceae family, native to India, Nepal, and Bhutan. Traditionally, its bark, roots, and flowers have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, tonic, and astringent actions, and has a long history of use in ayurvedic medicine, traditional chinese medicine, and unani medicine.

Perennial herbaceous plant.

Leaves
Broad leaves with upper surface dark green and lower surface lighter green, featuring prominent stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Inflorescences are racemose clusters of zygomorphic flowers with five petals, one of which is enlarged and colored red or pink, while the others are yellow or white, and the calyx is five-lobed with hairy margins.
Stems
Woody, erect, branched, with smooth, cylindrical, persistent, and slightly furrowed surface.
