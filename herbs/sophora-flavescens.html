Yellow Sophora (Sophora flavescens): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

Yellow Sophora (Sophora flavescens) is a flowering plant native to East Asia, known for its use in traditional medicine to treat conditions such as hepatitis, jaundice, and digestive disorders.

What is Yellow Sophora (Sophora Flavescens)?

Yellow Sophora, or Sophora flavescens, is a traditional Chinese medicinal herb native to East Asia, known for its root as the primary medicinal part, which contains active compounds like flavonoids and alkaloids used to treat various health conditions.

Botanical Identity and Classification

Yellow Sophora (Sophora flavescens) is a perennial leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family, native to regions of East Asia including China, Korea, and Japan.

Common Names and Synonyms

Yellow Sophora, scientifically known as Sophora flavescens, is commonly referred to by several names including Chinese Pagoda Tree, Sophora Tree, and Yellow Sophora, and is also known by its Latin name Sophora flavescens.

Plant Description and Morphology

The Yellow Sophora (Sophora flavescens) is a perennial herb with a slender, upright stem that can grow up to 1 to 2 meters in height, bearing alternate, pinnately compound leaves with 15 to 30 pairs of small, ovate to elliptic leaflets.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Yellow Sophora (Sophora flavescens) is native to East Asia, particularly China, Japan, and Korea, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils in woodland and mountainous regions.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The roots and seeds of Yellow Sophora (Sophora flavescens) are primarily used medicinally.

Phytochemical Composition

Yellow Sophora (Sophora flavescens) contains a variety of bioactive compounds, including flavonoids such as rutin, quercetin, and kaempferol, as well as alkaloids like oxysophorine and matrine, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Pharmacological Properties

Yellow Sophora (Sophora flavescens) possesses a range of pharmacological properties, including antibacterial, antifungal, anti-inflammatory, and antitumor effects, primarily due to its active compounds such as flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins.

Mechanisms of Action

The mechanisms of action of Yellow Sophora (Sophora flavescens) involve the bioactive compounds such as matrine and oxymatrine, which exhibit antitumor, anti-inflammatory, and neuroprotective effects by modulating various signaling pathways including apoptosis, cell cycle regulation, and neurotransmitter activity.

Therapeutic Uses and Indications

Yellow Sophora is used to treat conditions such as diarrhea, dysentery, cough, asthma, skin infections, and inflammation.

Preparation Methods and Forms

Yellow Sophora is typically prepared as a decoction, powder, or tincture, with the most common method involving boiling the dried root or seed in water to extract its active compounds.

Dosage and Administration

The typical dosage of Yellow Sophora (Sophora flavescens) for herbal use ranges from 3 to 10 grams per day, usually prepared as a decoction, with the exact amount depending on the specific condition being treated and the practitioner's recommendation.

Safety, Side Effects, and Contraindications

While Yellow Sophora is generally considered safe when used appropriately, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort, such as nausea, vomiting, or diarrhea, and prolonged use may lead to liver toxicity in some individuals.

Drug Interactions

Yellow Sophora (Sophora flavescens) may interact with certain medications due to its potential to affect liver enzymes and blood clotting, including anticoagulants, antiplatelet agents, and drugs metabolized by the CYP enzyme system.

Toxicity and Precautions

Yellow Sophora (Sophora flavescens) contains toxic alkaloids such as sophorine and matrine, which can cause gastrointestinal distress, central nervous system depression, and in high doses, may lead to respiratory failure or cardiac arrest, necessitating caution in its use and proper dosing under professional guidance.

Cultivation and Harvesting

Yellow Sophora (Sophora flavescens) is typically cultivated in well-drained soil with full sun exposure, requiring moderate watering and minimal fertilization to thrive.

Processing and Extraction

The processing of Yellow Sophora involves drying the mature seed pods under the sun or using low heat to preserve its active compounds, ensuring the herb retains its medicinal properties for use in traditional formulations.

Storage and Shelf Life

Yellow Sophora (Sophora flavescens) should be stored in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture, and its shelf life is typically 2 to 3 years when properly preserved.

Scientific Research and Evidence

Scientific research and evidence on Yellow Sophora (Sophora flavescens) have primarily focused on its bioactive compounds, such as sophorae flavescens extract, which have demonstrated potential anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antitumor properties in various in vitro and animal studies.

Traditional and Historical Uses

Yellow Sophora (Sophora flavescens) has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine for centuries to treat a variety of ailments, including inflammation, skin conditions, and digestive disorders, with its active compounds believed to possess anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antitumor properties.

Regulatory Status

The Yellow Sophora (Sophora flavescens) is classified as a traditional herbal medicine in China and is regulated under the Chinese Pharmacopoeia, but it is not approved for sale as a food supplement or drug in the United States or the European Union due to insufficient safety data and potential toxicity.
