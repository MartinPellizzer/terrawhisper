What to know about Sophora flavescens (ku shen) before using it medicinally

Ku Shen, scientifically known as Sophora flavescens, is a revered herb in traditional Chinese medicine that boasts numerous health properties to improve overall well-being.

In culinary practices, Ku Shen is often used to add depth and complexity to soups, stews, and sauces, particularly in Sichuan cuisine. Horticulturally, Ku Shen is a hardy perennial plant native to China's mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils with full sun. Botanically, the herb's unique properties are attributed to its rich concentration of flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids.

Historically, Ku Shen has been referenced in ancient Chinese medical texts, including the Compendium of Materia Medica by Li Shizhen, where it is praised for its ability to calm the mind and soothe the spirit.
