Alnus trabeculosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Alnus trabeculosa?

Alnus trabecuclosas belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Fagales and the family Betulaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Alnus and its specific species designation, trabecuclosas.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Alnus trabecuulos contains bioactive secondary metabolites including tannins, flavonoids, and various phenolic compounds that provide its medicinal properties. These chemical constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities. Specific phenolic acids found within the bark and leaves help neutralize free radicals in biological systems. Additionally, the presence of tannins contributes to astringent effects that can assist in wound healing processes. Researchers study these specific compounds to understand their potential therapeutic applications in modern pharmacology.

What are the therapeutic actions of Alnus trabeculosa?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various inflammatory conditions such as arthritis, skin irritations, and respiratory ailments. The bark of the alder tree contains tannins and salicylic acid derivatives that help reduce swelling and pain in joint tissues. When applied topically, its extracts can soothe eczema and other dermatological inflammations. Some traditional practices also utilize the plant to alleviate symptoms of bronchial congestion and coughs. These medicinal properties stem from the complex chemical compounds found within its woody structure.
