Cowpea (Canavalia ensiformis) Compounds

The primary compounds in Cowpea include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and saponins, which exhibit antimicrobial effects. Tannins and phenolic acids contribute to its astringent qualities and antioxidant activity. Coumarin and glycosides are also present, offering potential anti-inflammatory benefits. Terpenoids, alkaloids, essential oils, and anthocyanins further enhance its medicinal properties.

Cowpea (Canavalia ensiformis) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Cowpea (Canavalia ensiformis). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for conditions like inflammation and oxidative stress.

Saponin is a key active compound in Cowpea (Canavalia ensiformis). It exhibits antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, contributing to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine. Saponin helps in treating infections and reducing inflammation, making it valuable in herbal formulations.

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Cowpea (Canavalia ensiformis). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Glycosides in Cowpea are often used in traditional herbal remedies to support digestive and immune health.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Cowpea (Canavalia ensiformis). It acts as a powerful antioxidant, helping to neutralize free radicals in the body. This compound contributes to the plant's traditional use in supporting cardiovascular health and reducing inflammation.
