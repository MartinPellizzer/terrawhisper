Wood Betony (Stachys sylvatica) Uses

Wood Betony is used to treat respiratory infections, coughs, and mucous congestion. It may help with bacterial infections and skin inflammation, including infections and irritation. It is also applied to support wound healing and reduce skin irritation.

Wood Betony (Stachys sylvatica) Monograph

1. Respiratory infection

Wood Betony is used to support respiratory health by reducing inflammation and easing coughing. It may help alleviate symptoms of bronchitis and other respiratory infections. Its expectorant properties aid in clearing mucus, while its antimicrobial effects may combat infection. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture for respiratory support.

2. Cough

Wood Betony is used to soothe coughs by calming the respiratory system and reducing irritation. It is often prepared as a tincture or infusion to ease persistent or dry coughs. Its bitter compounds may help clear mucus, making it a valued herb in herbal cough remedies.

3. Respiratory infections

Wood Betony is used to support respiratory health by reducing inflammation and soothing irritated mucous membranes. It may help alleviate symptoms of coughs, colds, and bronchitis. Its expectorant properties aid in clearing mucus, while its antimicrobial effects may help combat infection. It is often used in herbal teas or tinctures for respiratory support.

4. Mucous congestion

Wood Betony is used to address mucous congestion due to its expectorant properties. It helps loosen and expel mucus from the respiratory tract, easing breathing in conditions like bronchitis or sinusitis. Its mild stimulation supports healthy mucus production without irritating the mucous membranes.

5. Bacterial infections

Wood Betony is used to support the treatment of bacterial infections due to its antimicrobial properties. It contains compounds that inhibit the growth of certain bacteria, making it a valuable herb in herbal medicine for addressing infections. Its use is often combined with other antimicrobial herbs to enhance effectiveness.

6. Skin inflammation

Wood Betony is used topically to reduce skin inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It helps soothe irritated skin and promote healing. Typically applied as a compress or infused oil, it is effective for conditions like eczema and minor rashes. Its soothing effects make it a valuable remedy in herbal skincare.

7. Skin infection

Wood Betony is used topically to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and bacterial growth on the skin. Traditionally applied as a poultice or infused oil, it supports healing in cases of minor infections and wounds.

8. Wound healing

Wood Betony is traditionally used to support wound healing due to its astringent and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce bleeding, prevent infection, and promote tissue repair. Applications include topical use on minor cuts and abrasions, often in salves or infused oils. Its historical use in herbal medicine highlights its role in traditional first aid practices.

9. Skin infections

Wood Betony is used topically to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and infection in conditions like eczema and fungal infections. Its astringent qualities also promote healing and prevent further irritation.

10. Skin irritation

Wood Betony is used topically to soothe skin irritation due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It helps reduce redness, itching, and inflammation associated with minor skin conditions. A poultice or infused oil made from the plant can be applied directly to affected areas for relief. It is particularly effective for eczema, rashes, and insect bites.
