Botanical Identification of Wood Betony (Stachys sylvatica)

To identify Wood Betony (Stachys sylvatica) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Wood Betony (Stachys sylvatica) Monograph

Scientific Classification

Wood Betony, Stachys sylvatica, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Lamiales, order Lamiales, family Lamiaceae, genus Stachys, and species sylvatica. This classification places it within the flowering plants and the mint family, highlighting its botanical relationships and characteristics.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Wood Betony is Stachys sylvatica, first described by Carl von Linné in 1753. It belongs to the plant family Lamiaceae, known for its aromatic herbs. The name "sylvatica" refers to its natural habitat in wooded areas. This nomenclature reflects its classification and historical usage in herbal medicine.

Morphological Characteristics

Wood Betony is a perennial herb with a fibrous root system. Its stems are square and upright, often branching near the base. Leaves are opposite, heart-shaped with hairy surfaces, and have a mint-like scent. The inflorescence is a whorled cluster of spikes, bearing small, pink or purple flowers. The fruit is a dry capsule containing numerous tiny seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Wood Betony can be distinguished by its square stems, opposite leaves with scalloped edges, and violet-blue flowers in whorls. It differs from related species like Narrow-leaved Betony by having broader leaves and a more robust stem. The presence of a distinct central vein in the leaves is another key identifier.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Wood Betony is native to Europe and parts of Asia, thriving in temperate climates. It prefers well-drained soils and is commonly found in woodland edges, meadows, and rocky areas. It grows at elevations between 200 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Wood Betony blooms from June to August, producing spikes of pink to purple flowers. Seed formation occurs in late summer, typically from July to September. For identification purposes, the plant is best harvested in early summer before flowering for fresh leaves and roots.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Wood Betony, Stachys sylvatica, belongs to the genus Stachys within the family Lamiaceae. It is closely related to species like Stachys palustris and Stachys arvensis. The genus shares key family traits such as square stems and opposite leaves, which aid in its identification.
