Clematis brevicaudata

What's the taxonomical classification of Clematis brevicaudata?

Clematis brevicaudata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Ranunculales and the family Ranunculaceae, placing it within the genus Clematis. Ultimately, its specific scientific designation is defined by the species epithet brevicaudata.

What are the herbal preparations of Clematis brevicaudata?

Clematis brevicaudata is prepared primarily as an aqueous infusion, a decoction, or a tincture involving the use of alcohol as a solvent. These liquid preparations allow for the extraction of specific bioactive compounds from the plant's tissues. Practitioners often utilize dried aerial parts to ensure a consistent concentration of medicinal properties during the brewing process. Tinctures are particularly valued for their long shelf life and ability to deliver concentrated doses in small volumes. Infusions are typically preferred for gentler applications where a milder effect is desired.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Clematis brevicaudata can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if ingested due to the presence of protoanemonin. Contact with the plant's sap may lead to contact dermatitis, characterized by redness, itching, or blistering on the skin. If someone consumes any part of the plant, they might experience symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, or diarrhea. These adverse reactions are common among many species within the Clematis genus because of their chemical composition. It is essential to avoid direct contact and ingestion to prevent these physiological complications.
