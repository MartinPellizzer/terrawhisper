Clematis brevicaudata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clematis brevicaudata has slender, woody stems that are often twining and produce opposite or whorled leaves composed of three to five ovate or lanceolate leaflets. The leaf margins are typically entire or slightly serrated, while the leaf surfaces may appear glabrous or possess fine pubescence. Its inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous flowers that lack large, showy petals, instead featuring prominent sepals. The reproductive structures include numerous stamens that surround a central cluster of pistils. Following flowering, the plant develops characteristic feathery achenes that facilitate wind dispersal.
