What's the taxonomical classification of Zanthoxylum esquirolii?

Zanthoxylum esquirolii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It further belongs to the order Sapindales and is a member of the family Rutaeeae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Zanthoxylum, with its specific designation being the species esquirolii.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Zanthoxylum esquirolii contains bioactive alkaloids, terpenoids, and lignans that exhibit significant pharmacological properties including antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant activities. The plant is particularly rich in specialized metabolites such as xanthoxylin and various limonoids which contribute to its therapeutic potential. These chemical constituents work by inhibiting specific pathogens and modulating biological pathways related to oxidative stress. Research suggests that these compounds may also possess neuroprotective and anti-tumor qualities in certain experimental models. Consequently, the chemical profile of this species makes it a valuable subject for drug development and traditional medicinal applications.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including skin diseases, intestinal worms, gastrointestinal infections, and inflammation. The bark and roots are frequently utilized in traditional medicine to address digestive issues and parasitic infestations. It also serves as a remedy for topical conditions such as fungal infections or skin irritations. Some practitioners apply extracts to manage localized pain and reduce swelling in different parts of the body. These medicinal applications rely heavily on the plant's bioactive compounds to combat pathogens.
