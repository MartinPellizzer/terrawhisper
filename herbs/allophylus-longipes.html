Allophylus longipes

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Allophylus longipes has woody stems, elliptical to oblong leaves with entire margins, and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in axillary panicles. The foliage typically exhibits a lustrous texture and prominent venation on the underside of the blades. Its long-pedicelled structure is a defining characteristic that distinguishes it from closely related species within the Combretaceae family. The plant also produces small, fleshy drupes that serve as its primary fruit type. These morphological features collectively define its vegetative and reproductive identity in tropical forest ecosystems.
