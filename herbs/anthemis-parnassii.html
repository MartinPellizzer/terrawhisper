Parnassian Buttonweed (Anthemis Parnassii)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Anthemis parnassii.

Parnassian buttonweed, scientifically known as Anthemis parnassii, is a rare medicinal herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and Central Asia, valued for its adaptogenic and anti-inflammatory properties. This plant is traditionally used in folk medicine to treat respiratory ailments, digestive issues, and skin conditions, with its essential oils and aerial parts being the primary sources of its therapeutic compounds. Historically, it has been revered in Tibetan and Ayurvedic traditions for its ability to enhance vitality and resilience against environmental stressors. In modern wellness practices, it is gaining attention for its potential role in natural anti-aging formulations and as a support for immune and nervous system health. One of its unique features is the presence of sesquiterpene lactones, which contribute to its distinctive bitter taste and may offer novel therapeutic applications in phytotherapy.
