Fortunella margarita

What's the taxonomical classification of Fortunella margarita?

Fortunella margarita belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Sapindales and the family Rutaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Fortunella, and its species designation, margarita.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires bright, indirect sunlight and well-draining, sandy soil to prevent root rot caused by excess moisture.

You should allow the top inch of soil to dry completely before watering again to mimic its natural habitat. Maintaining a warm temperature between 65 and 80 degrees Fahrenheit ensures steady growth throughout the season. High humidity levels are also beneficial, so frequent misting or a pebble tray can help maintain the necessary moisture in the air.

Avoid placing the plant in direct midday sun, as the intense heat can scorch its delicate leaves.
