Fortunella margarita

What's the taxonomical classification of Fortunella margarita?

Fortunella margarita belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Sapindales and the family Rutaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Fortunella, and its species designation, margarita.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Fortunella margarita has small, elliptical to lanceolate leaves that are typically bright green and arranged in dense, spreading clusters along its woody stems. Its stems are slender and branching, forming a low-growing, shrub-like habit that stays close to the ground. Tiny, inconspicuous flowers occasionally emerge from the axils of the leaves during its blooming period. The overall structure of the plant is compact and mounded, making it suitable for groundcover applications.
