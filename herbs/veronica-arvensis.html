Veronica arvensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Veronica arvensis?

Veronica arvensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage that leads to the order Lamiales. Within this order, the plant is situated in the family Plantaginaceae, where it is categorized under the genus Veronica. Finally, its specific biological identity is completed by its species designation, arvensis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Veronica arvensis contains iridoid glycosides such as aucubin and verbascoside, along with various phenylethanoid glycosides and flavonoids. These specific chemical constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's biological activity and potential therapeutic properties. The presence of aucubin is particularly noted for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial characteristics in pharmacological studies. Furthermore, the antioxidant capacity of the species is attributed to its complex profile of polyphenolic compounds. These medicinal elements suggest that the plant may have applications in treating oxidative stress or minor infections.

What are the therapeutic actions of Veronica arvensis?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including respiratory conditions like coughs, bronchitis, and asthma, as well as digestive issues and inflammatory skin conditions. Historically, its properties have been applied to soothe irritated mucous membranes through traditional herbal remedies. It is often utilized in folk medicine to address symptoms of common colds and seasonal congestion. Some practitioners also suggest its use for managing minor gastrointestinal discomfort. Research into its specific therapeutic applications remains limited in modern clinical settings.
