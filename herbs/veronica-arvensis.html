Veronica arvensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Veronica arvensis?

Veronica arvensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage that leads to the order Lamiales. Within this order, the plant is situated in the family Plantaginaceae, where it is categorized under the genus Veronica. Finally, its specific biological identity is completed by its species designation, arvensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Veronica arvensis has an erect, branched stem typically reaching 10 to 30 centimeters in height with small, ovate to elliptical leaves that feature serrated margins. The leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem and possess short petioles. Its flowers are small and blue to pale violet, characterized by four petals where the lower petal is notably larger than the others. These blossoms are borne in terminal racemes and contain two prominent stamens. The plant produces small, rounded seed capsules that split open to release numerous tiny seeds upon maturity.
