1. Causes allergic reactions

Tetrapanax papyrifer, also known as Chinese Aralia, causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

This is often due to its high content of sapogenins and sesquiterpenes, which can trigger immune responses in sensitive people. Additionally, the plant's natural defense mechanisms involve producing chemical compounds that deter herbivores, which can also cause adverse reactions in humans.

These compounds can be released through skin contact or ingestion.

2. Triggers skin irritation

Tetrapanax papyrifer, also known as Chinese aralia, triggers skin irritation in some individuals.

This is often due to the plant's sap or latex, which contains allergenic compounds that can cause an allergic reaction when it comes into contact with skin.

The sap can also cause skin irritation due to its high alkaloid content, which can lead to redness, itchiness, and inflammation in sensitive individuals.

3. Induces nausea symptoms

4. Promotes stomach upset

Tetrapanax papyrifer, also known as Chinese Aralia, promotes stomach upset due to its saponin content, which is believed to irritate the digestive tract and cause gastrointestinal disturbances.
