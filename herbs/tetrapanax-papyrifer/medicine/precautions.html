10 Precautions To Take When Using Tetrapanax Papyrifer (Chinese Aralia)

Tetrapanax papyrifer, also known as Chinese aralia, has some precautions to consider before using it medicinally, such as avoiding its use in pregnancy and breastfeeding due to its potential to stimulate uterine contractions.

Excessive consumption can also cause gastrointestinal issues, such as diarrhea and vomiting. For example, taking high doses of Tetrapanax papyrifer may lead to dehydration and electrolyte imbalances.

It's essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using this herb to minimize the risk of adverse effects.
