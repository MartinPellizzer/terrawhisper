What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Tetrapanax Papyrifer (Chinese Aralia)?

Tetrapanax papyrifer, also known as Chinese Aralia, has health benefits such as treating fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions due to its anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties.

Its medicinal constituents include flavonoids, terpenes, and saponins, which contribute to its therapeutic effects. Traditionally, the plant is prepared into teas, infusions, or ointments to be applied externally. However, excessive consumption or prolonged use may cause gastrointestinal upset or allergic reactions.

It is essential to consult a healthcare professional before using Chinese Aralia, especially for pregnant or breastfeeding women.

What are the possible side effect of using Tetrapanax papyrifer improperly?

Improper use of Tetrapanax papyrifer, also known as chinese aralia, increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as gastrointestinal issues, including nausea and vomiting, due to its high saponin content.

Additionally, it may cause allergic reactions, skin irritation, and respiratory problems in some individuals. Prolonged consumption of excessive amounts can lead to liver and kidney damage.

Pregnant or breastfeeding women should exercise caution when using chinese aralia.
