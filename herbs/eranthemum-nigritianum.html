Eranthemum nigritianum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eranthemum nigritianum has upright, woody stems that support clusters of small, lanceolate leaves with entire margins and acute tips. The foliage is typically dark green and possesses a leathery texture that aids in moisture retention. Its inflorescence consists of dense, terminal spikes featuring tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are often cream or pale yellow in color. The plant structure is characterized by a compact, shrubby habit that allows it to thrive in rocky environments. These morphological traits collectively define its specialized adaptation to its native habitat.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical regions of West and Central Africa, specifically occurring in countries such as Nigeria, Cameroon, and Gabon. It thrives within the humid environments characteristic of the Guineo-Congolian rainforest belt. Its distribution is closely tied to these moist, low-to-mid altitude forest ecosystems where shade and consistent moisture are available. Because it prefers these specific tropical conditions, it is rarely found outside of these equatorial woodland and jungle habitats. This localized range makes it a distinct component of the African tropical flora.
