Common Hawthorn (Crataegus Excelsa)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Crataegus excelsa.

Common hawthorn, scientifically known as Crataegus excelsa, is a small ornamental shrub valued for its medicinal properties and long history of use in traditional herbal medicine. This deciduous plant, native to Europe and parts of Asia, is primarily recognized as a medicinal herb and adaptogen, known for its ability to support cardiovascular health and reduce stress. Its primary benefits include improving heart function, lowering blood pressure, and promoting relaxation due to its high content of flavonoids and antioxidants. Traditionally, it has been used in European folk medicine to treat heart conditions, anxiety, and insomnia, while in modern wellness practices, it is often incorporated into herbal supplements and teas for its calming and circulatory benefits. One unique feature of Crataegus excelsa is its distinctive floral scent and the presence of a rare active compound called vitexin, which contributes to its therapeutic potential.
