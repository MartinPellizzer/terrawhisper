Asystasia calycina

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Asystasia calycina has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins and terminal clusters of small, tubular, violet-blue flowers supported by prominent, leafy calyx lobes. The stems are typically hairy and grow in an upright, herbaceous habit reaching heights of about 30 centimeters. Each flower is characterized by a distinct corolla tube that flares into five lobes at the opening. The calyx is particularly notable because it is enlarged and leaf-like, often extending significantly beyond the corolla. These morphological features collectively help identify the species within the Lamiaceae family.
