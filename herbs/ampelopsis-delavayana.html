What's the taxonomical classification of Ampelopsis delavayana?

Ampelopsis delavayana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It falls under the order Vitales and is a member of the family Vitaceae. At the most specific levels of its classification, this plant is identified by the genus Ampelopsis and the species name delavayana.

      Ampelopsis delavayana can cause skin irritation and allergic contact dermatitis when its tissues come into direct contact with human skin. While systemic toxicity is less commonly documented, careful avoidance of skin contact is recommended for those with sensitive skin.
