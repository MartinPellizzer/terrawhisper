Rhodiola kirilowii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhodiola kirilowii has a thick, fleshy rhizome that serves as its primary storage organ and grows horizontally beneath the soil surface. This perennial herb features basal leaves that are typically lanceolate or elliptical in shape with smooth margins. The leaves are arranged in dense rosettes and exhibit a succulent texture characteristic of high-altitude species. During its flowering stage, the plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered on terminal inflorescences. These floral structures often transition into small, dried capsules that facilitate seed dispersal across its alpine habitat.
