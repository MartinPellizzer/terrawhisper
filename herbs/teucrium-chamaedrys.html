Teucrium Chamaedrys Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Teucrium chamaedrys, commonly known as germander, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its various health benefits, including aiding digestion, reducing inflammation, and supporting respiratory health.

The therapeutic actions of this herb are attributed to its bioactive constituents such as flavonoids, iridoids, and essential oils, which exhibit antimicrobial, antioxidant, and anti-inflammatory effects.

Herbal preparations of Teucrium chamaedrys can be made as infusions, tinctures, or decoctions, often used to make teas or topical applications for skin conditions.
