Germander

Teucrium chamaedrys

Germander (Teucrium chamaedrys) is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, known for its aromatic leaves and medicinal properties. It has been traditionally used to support digestive health and alleviate symptoms of ailments such as indigestion, stomach cramps, and mild infections.

Botanical Identification

Teucrium chamaedrys, commonly known as germander, is a perennial herb native to Europe and western Asia. Belonging to the Lamiaceae family, it features small blue or violet flowers and aromatic, hairy leaves. It is also known as "German carnation" and has been used in herbal medicine for centuries.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Lamiales
  • Order: Lamiales
  • Family: Lamiaceae
  • Genus: Teucrium
  • Species: Teucrium chamaedrys

Safety Profile

Germander is contraindicated during pregnancy and lactation due to potential risks to fetal and infant development. It should also be avoided by individuals with a known allergy to plants in the Lamiaceae family. Long-term use may lead to toxicity, so it is important to follow recommended dosage guidelines.
