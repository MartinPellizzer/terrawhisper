Botanical Identification of Chile Ironweed (Bidens chilensis)

To identify Chile Ironweed (Bidens chilensis) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Chile Ironweed, or Bidens chilensis, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Asterales, family Asteraceae, genus Bidens, and species Bidens chilensis. These classifications place it within the flowering plants and the daisy family, highlighting its botanical relationships and ecological role.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Bidens chilensis is the scientific name for Chile Ironweed, first described by the botanist Linnaeus in 1753. It is also known by several synonyms, including Bidens chilensis L. and Bidens chilensis Linnaeus. Common names include Chile Ironweed, and it is referred to by regional and historical names in various parts of its native range.

Morphological Characteristics

Chile Ironweed is an upright annual herb with a branching stem that grows up to 1.5 meters tall. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces a cluster of yellow flowers with five petals, followed by three-lobed seeds enclosed in a bristly pappus.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Bidens chilensis can be distinguished by its yellow disk flowers, narrow linear leaves, and rough, hairy stems. It differs from similar species like Bidens pilosa by having fewer flower heads and shorter, more pointed leaf tips. Its habitat in coastal and disturbed areas also aids in identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Bidens chilensis is native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina. It thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained soils and is commonly found in open fields and disturbed areas. It typically grows at altitudes between 0 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Chile Ironweed blooms from late spring to early summer, typically between May and July. Seed formation occurs from late summer to early fall, around August to October. For identification purposes, the most accurate harvest time is during the flowering period when the plant is most identifiable.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Bidens chilensis is closely related to other species in the genus Bidens, which belongs to the family Asteraceae. It shares key morphological and genetic traits with related species, such as its composite flower heads and biennial growth habit. These relationships help classify it within the broader context of the Asteraceae family.
