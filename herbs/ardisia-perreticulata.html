Ardisia perreticulata

What's the taxonomical classification of Ardisia perreticulata?

Ardisia perreticulata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is situated in the order Ericales. This plant is a member of the family Primulaceae, falling under the genus Ardisia, and is specifically identified by the species name perreticulata.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Ardisia perreticulata contains various secondary metabolites including alkaloids, triterpenoids, and polyphenolic compounds that exhibit potential pharmacological activities. These chemical constituents are primarily concentrated within the leaves and roots of the plant. Research suggests that the specific alkaloid profile may contribute to antimicrobial and antioxidant properties. Additionally, the triterpenoids found in the species are often studied for their anti-inflammatory effects in traditional medicinal applications. These bioactive compounds serve as the basis for investigating the plant's therapeutic potential in modern pharmacology.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, and various types of digestive ailments. The leaves and bark are often prepared as a decoction or a topical paste to promote healing. This traditional medicine is particularly effective at addressing inflammation and localized discomfort. Some practitioners also utilize the plant's components to manage stomach-related issues. Its medicinal properties are deeply rooted in local botanical knowledge.
