Capparis urophylla

What's the taxonomical classification of Capparis urophylla?

Capparis urophyllia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is situated in the order Brassicales. The plant is a member of the family Capparaceae, specifically falling within the genus Capparis, with its unique identity defined by its specific epithet, urophylla.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Capparis urophyllia has large, broadly ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically 10 to 20 centimeters long with an entire margin and a prominent central vein.

These leaves are arranged alternately along woody, climbing stems that serve as a sprawling shrub or liana. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that emerge from the axils of the foliage. Its fruit consists of small, fleshy berries that develop from the fertilized ovaries of the blossoms.

The overall structure is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and lush, green canopy.
