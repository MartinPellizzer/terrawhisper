Yellow Wood (Maclura pomifera) Monograph

1. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Yellow Wood (Maclura pomifera). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. Coumarin also exhibits mild sedative effects, supporting its role in treating conditions like muscle spasms and digestive issues.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Yellow Wood (Maclura pomifera) contribute to its medicinal properties by exhibiting antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. These compounds help protect cells from damage and may support immune function. They are found in the fruit and bark of the plant, making them valuable in herbal medicine for their therapeutic potential.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in Yellow Wood (Maclura pomifera). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and skin-related ailments. This compound plays a significant role in the therapeutic effects attributed to Yellow Wood.

4. Tannin

Tannin in Yellow Wood (Maclura pomifera) contributes to its astringent properties. It helps reduce inflammation and may support wound healing. Tannins are also believed to aid in digestive health by firming stools. This compound is a key component in traditional herbal preparations of the plant.

5. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Yellow Wood (Maclura pomifera). They contribute to the plant's medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects. These compounds are often responsible for the plant's therapeutic applications in traditional herbal medicine.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Yellow Wood (Maclura pomifera). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects. Glycosides in Yellow Wood are often used in traditional herbal medicine to support joint health and reduce pain.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Yellow Wood (Maclura pomifera). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and detoxifying properties. Saponins may support liver function and aid in the removal of toxins from the body.

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of Yellow Wood (Maclura pomifera) contains volatile compounds like limonene and alpha-pinene. These compounds contribute to its aromatic properties and may support respiratory and digestive health. The oil is often used in aromatherapy and as a natural remedy for inflammation and stress relief.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid compound found in Yellow Wood (Maclura pomifera). It contributes to the plant's vibrant fruit color and has antioxidant properties. These compounds help neutralize free radicals, supporting cellular health. Anthocyanin may also contribute to the plant's traditional use in promoting skin and joint health.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid compounds in Yellow Wood (Maclura pomifera) have been studied for their potential medicinal properties. These alkaloids may contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating inflammation and pain. However, research on their specific effects and safety is still limited.
