Botanical Identification of Yellow Wood (Maclura pomifera)

To identify Yellow Wood (Maclura pomifera) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Yellow Wood, or Maclura pomifera, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Spermatophyta, class Caryophyllopsida, order Urticales, family Moraceae, genus Maclura, and species Maclura pomifera. These classifications help identify its botanical relationships and place it within the broader plant kingdom.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Yellow Wood is Maclura pomifera, first described by Carl Peter Thunberg in 1794. It has several synonyms, including Maclura tinctoria and Maclura aurantiaca, reflecting historical taxonomic changes. Common names include Yellow Wood, Green Ash, and Yellow Monkey Puzzle, while regional and historical names vary across cultures and regions.

Morphological Characteristics

Yellow Wood, or Maclura pomifera, is a deciduous tree with a straight, cylindrical trunk and a rounded crown. Its roots are deep and taproot-like, providing stability. The bark is thick, fissured, and dark brown, while the leaves are compound, odd-pinnate, and alternate. The inflorescence is a panicle, with small yellow-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The fruit is a spiny, orange-hued berry that matures in late summer, containing several seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Yellow Wood can be distinguished by its thick, fissured bark that peels in vertical strips, its alternate leaves with three to five lobes, and its distinctive yellowish-ochre fruit. It differs from similar species like Osage orange by its more rounded leaf shape and the presence of a short, blunt stem on its fruit.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Yellow Wood (Maclura pomifera) is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in areas of Arkansas, Missouri, and Tennessee. It thrives in temperate climates with warm summers and mild winters. The tree prefers well-drained soils, such as loamy or sandy soils, and is typically found at elevations between 100 and 600 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Yellow Wood blooms in late spring to early summer, producing yellowish-green flowers. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early fall, with fruits ripening by late fall. For identification purposes, the best time to observe key features is during late summer when the fruit is mature and distinctive.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Yellow Wood (Maclura pomifera) belongs to the Moraceae family, sharing traits with other members like figs and mulberries. It is closely related to species such as Ficus and Morus, differing mainly in fruit structure and leaf morphology. Its genus, Maclura, is distinct within the family but shares evolutionary traits with its closest relatives.
