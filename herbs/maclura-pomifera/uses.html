Yellow Wood (Maclura pomifera) Uses

Yellow Wood is used to treat respiratory conditions such as bronchitis, asthma, and respiratory infections. It helps alleviate coughs and reduce mucous membrane irritation. The plant also supports wound healing and is applied for skin infections and irritation. Its anti-inflammatory properties make it useful for managing respiratory allergies.

Yellow Wood (Maclura pomifera) Monograph

1. Bronchitis

Yellow Wood has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in cases of bronchitis. Its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties help reduce mucus buildup and soothe irritated airways. It may aid in alleviating symptoms such as coughing and congestion, though it should be used under professional guidance.

2. Respiratory allergies

Yellow Wood has been traditionally used to alleviate respiratory allergies due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties. It helps reduce symptoms like sneezing, itching, and nasal congestion by modulating immune responses. Its bioactive compounds support bronchial health and may reduce allergic reactions when used as a herbal remedy.

3. Cough

Yellow Wood is used to treat coughs due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps loosen mucus and reduce throat irritation. Traditionally, it is prepared as a tea or tincture to soothe respiratory discomfort. Its efficacy supports its role in herbal remedies for respiratory conditions.

4. Respiratory infections

Yellow Wood has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in treating coughs and inflammation associated with respiratory infections. Its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties may help reduce symptoms and promote healing in the respiratory tract. It is often prepared as a tincture or decoction for internal use.

5. Asthma

Yellow Wood has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in managing asthma symptoms. Its anti-inflammatory and bronchodilating properties may help reduce airway swelling and improve breathing. It is often prepared as a tincture or decoction to harness its therapeutic effects.

6. Respiratory tract infections

Yellow Wood is used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health. It helps alleviate symptoms of respiratory tract infections by reducing inflammation and promoting mucus clearance. Its antimicrobial properties may help combat bacterial and viral infections. It is often used in formulations for coughs, bronchitis, and other respiratory ailments.

7. Mucous membrane irritation

Yellow Wood can cause irritation to mucous membranes due to its high resin content. This irritation is typically observed in the mouth, throat, and gastrointestinal tract when consumed in large quantities. It is important to use Yellow Wood with caution and in controlled amounts to avoid adverse effects.

8. Skin infections

Yellow Wood has been used traditionally to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial properties. The resin from the fruit contains compounds that inhibit bacterial and fungal growth. It is often applied topically as a poultice or ointment to reduce inflammation and promote healing. Its effectiveness makes it a valuable remedy in herbal medicine for various skin conditions.

9. Wound healing

Yellow Wood, or Maclura pomifera, has been traditionally used to promote wound healing due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. Its resinous sap helps disinfect and seal wounds, reducing infection risk and accelerating tissue repair. The plant's compounds may stimulate cellular regeneration, making it a valued remedy in herbal medicine for treating cuts, sores, and skin injuries.

10. Skin irritation

Yellow Wood has been traditionally used to treat skin irritation due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. The sap, when applied topically, can soothe rashes, eczema, and minor burns. It helps reduce redness and promote skin healing. Its natural compounds make it a valuable remedy in herbal medicine for various skin conditions.
