Lepisorus eilophyllus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lepisorus eilophyllus has small, thin, light green fronds that typically measure between 5 and 15 centimeters in length and consist of pinnate or bipinnate divisions. The blades are characterized by their delicate, almost hair-like segments which give the plant a fine texture. These narrow pinnae are arranged along a central rachis that is often dark or brownish in color. The underside of the leaves contains small, circular sori that are protected by a specialized membrane called an indusium. This morphological structure allows the fern to thrive in shaded, moist environments where its compact size is an advantage.
