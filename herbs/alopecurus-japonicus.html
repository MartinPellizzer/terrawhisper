Alopecurus japonicus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Alopecucus japonicus has densely tufted, perennial growth habits featuring upright, slender culms that typically reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. The foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often folded or involute in cross-section. Its inflorescence is a compact, cylindrical panicle that displays a distinctive greenish to purplish hue during maturation. These flowering spikes are characterized by a soft, bristly texture due to the presence of fine hairs on the spikelets. The plant often forms thick mats through its spreading rhizomatous root system.
