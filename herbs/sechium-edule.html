Sechium edule

What's the taxonomical classification of Sechium edule?

Sechium edule belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Cucurbitales and is a member of the family Cucurbitaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by the genus Sechium and the specific species edule.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Sechium edule contains significant concentrations of bioactive compounds including phenolic acids, flavonoids, and various essential minerals such as magnesium, calcium, and iron. These phytochemicals contribute to the plant's potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The presence of specific dietary fibers also aids in regulating blood glucose levels and supporting gastrointestinal health. Furthermore, the nutritional profile provides essential amino acids that promote overall metabolic function. Regular consumption may assist in reducing oxidative stress within the body.

This plant is used to treat various ailments including digestive disorders, skin infections, inflammatory conditions, and respiratory issues. The seeds and roots are often utilized in traditional medicine to alleviate symptoms of stomach discomfort and indigestion. Its natural properties are also applied topically to soothe skin irritations and promote healing. Additionally, preparations of the plant are sometimes used to help reduce coughing and other respiratory distress.
