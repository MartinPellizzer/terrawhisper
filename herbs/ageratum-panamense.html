What is Ageratum panamense?

Ageratum panamense is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae, native to Central and South America.

It is an annual herb with a slender stem, 10-60 cm tall, and ovate to lanceolate leaves, 2-10 cm long and 1-5 cm wide, with ciliate margins. The plant bears small, white or pinkish-purple flowers in axillary and terminal clusters, 1-2 cm in diameter, with 5-7 petals and a prominent disc of yellow florets. The flowers are arranged in a capitulum, a characteristic of the Asteraceae family.

The plant produces achenes, 2-4 mm long, with a pappus of 5-10 white bristles.
