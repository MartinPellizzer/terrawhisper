Cyrtomium yamamotoi

What's the taxonomical classification of Cyrtomium yamamotoi?

Cyrtomium yamamotoi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Polypodiales and the family Polypodiaceae. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its genus, Cyrtomium, and its specific species designation, yamamotoi.

What are the herbal preparations of Cyrtomium yamamotoi?

Cyrtomium yamamotoi is prepared as a decoction, a dried powder, or a topical poultice for medicinal use. The rhizomes are most commonly dried and boiled in water to create a concentrated liquid extract. This liquid can also be processed into a fine powder that is easily ingested or mixed with other substances. For external applications, the fresh plant material is crushed into a paste to be applied directly to the skin. These various forms allow the plant's bioactive compounds to be utilized for both internal and external treatments.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Cyrtomium yamamotoi can cause skin irritation, contact dermatitis, or allergic reactions in sensitive individuals when the plant comes into direct contact with the skin. While specific toxicological studies on humans are limited, many fern species contain compounds that may trigger redness or itching. Individuals should exercise caution when handling the foliage to avoid potential dermatological discomfort. It is generally recommended to wear gloves during planting or pruning to minimize these risks. Monitoring for any localized swelling or rashes is a prudent safety measure for gardeners.
