Cyrtomium yamamotoi

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cyrtomium yamamotoi has large, pinnate fronds that typically measure 30 to 60 centimeters in length and consist of numerous small, oblong to lanceolate pinnules arranged along a central rachis. The texture of these leaflets is generally thin and membranous, often exhibiting a slightly wavy or undulate margin. The stipe, or leaf stalk, is relatively long and can appear somewhat hairy or scaly near the base. Underneath the fertile fronds, the sori are located along the margins of the pinnules and are protected by a specialized structure called a false indusium. This morphological arrangement allows the plant to thrive in shaded, moist forest environments.
