What to know about Adhatoda vasica (malabar nut) before using it medicinally

Malabar nut, scientifically known as Adhatoda vasica, is a revered herb that has been touted for its impressive health benefits, which include lowering blood pressure, soothing respiratory issues, and even exhibiting antidiabetic properties.

In the kitchen, malabar nut is commonly used to add a unique flavor and aroma to various dishes, particularly in traditional Indian cuisine. From a horticultural perspective, malabar nut is a relatively easy-to-grow plant that thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade. Botanically speaking, malabar nut belongs to the family Acanthaceae and features small white flowers and oval-shaped leaves.

With a rich history dating back thousands of years, malabar nut has been mentioned in ancient Ayurvedic texts and is still widely used today in traditional medicine and culinary practices around the world.

What are the side effects of Adhatoda vasica when used improperly?

When used improperly, Adhatoda vasica increases the chances of experiencing side effects, such as allergic reactions, digestive issues, and cardiovascular problems.

If taken in high doses or for extended periods, this herb can cause an allergic reaction, manifesting as hives, itching, swelling, and difficulty breathing. Additionally, improper use can lead to gastrointestinal disturbances, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea.

Moreover, Adhatoda vasica may interact with blood-thinning medications and exacerbate bleeding issues, potentially leading to internal hemorrhaging or prolonged bleeding after an injury or surgery. Furthermore, its stimulatory properties can cause increased heart rate, palpitations, and even arrhythmias in susceptible individuals.

In rare cases, high doses of Adhatoda vasica have been linked to severe liver damage and kidney failure, making it crucial for patients to follow the recommended dosage and consult with a healthcare professional before using this herb as a treatment option.
