Kalmegh

Adhatoda vasica

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Kalmegh, scientifically known as Adhatoda vasica, belongs to the family Acanthaceae. It is also called Malabar nut or Indian bignonia. A perennial herb native to southern Asia, it features elliptical leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers. Common synonyms include Adhatoda asiatica and Kalmegh.

Active Compounds

Kalmegh contains key bioactive compounds such as alkaloids, tannins, and flavonoids. These compounds work synergistically to enhance its therapeutic effects, supporting its traditional use in respiratory and inflammatory conditions. The alkaloids are primarily responsible for its bronchodilatory and anti-inflammatory actions.

  • Alkaloid
  • Tannin
  • Flavonoid

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action
Respiratory Bronchitis, asthma, tuberculosis, allergic rhinitis Expectorant, bronchodilator, antimicrobial, antihistaminic
Integumentary Skin infections, inflammatory skin conditions, dermatitis Antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, antifungal, antioxidant
Digestive Gastrointestinal infections, oral infections, gastritis, peptic ulcer Antimicrobial, antacid, anti-ulcer, astringent, antidiarrheal

Preparation Methods

Extract: Used for respiratory conditions such as asthma and bronchitis.

Infusion: Used for treating coughs and respiratory infections.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and as a tonic.

Safety Profile

Kalmegh is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation due to limited safety data. Long-term use may cause gastrointestinal discomfort, and individuals with sensitive stomachs should use it cautiously.
