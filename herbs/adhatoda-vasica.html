Adhatoda vasica

What's the taxonomical classification of Adhatoda vasica?

Adhatoda vasica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and falls into the order Lamiales. The plant is a member of the family Acanthaceae, specifically situated within the genus Adhatoda, and is identified by the species name vasica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Adhatoda vasica has a woody shrub habit characterized by erect, quadrangular stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The leaves are simple, opposite, and lanceolate in shape with entire margins and acute tips. These leaves exhibit a distinctively serrated edge when young and possess a prominent midrib. The inflorescence consists of small, white, tubular flowers that emerge from the leaf axils in a cyme arrangement. The plant's overall structure is defined by its sturdy, branched stems and dense foliage.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several bioactive quinazoline alkaloids including vasicine, deoxyvasicine, L-vasicinone, vasicinol, and vasicinolone. These specific medicinal compounds are primarily responsible for the plant's pharmacological activities, such as bronchodilation and expectorant effects. Vasicine acts as a central constituent that provides significant therapeutic potential for respiratory ailments. Other derivatives like deoxyvasicine and vasicinone contribute to the overall chemical complexity and medicinal efficacy of the species. Together, these alkaloids form a potent chemical profile used in traditional herbal medicine.

According to a study published by "Photosynthetica", Adhatoda vasica contains the medicinally important secondary metabolite vasicine. This specific compound was found to increase under the studied treatments. The study ultimately revealed that Adhatoda vasica shows variable responses to these environmental factors.
