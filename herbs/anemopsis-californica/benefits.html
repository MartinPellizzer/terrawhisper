The plant's presence in riparian zones can help prevent erosion and maintain water quality by filtering runoff. However, due to its high toxicity, it is not recommended for use in gardens or agricultural settings.

Overall, while it offers some ecological advantages, its health risks far outweigh any potential benefits for human or animal consumption.
