Artemisia tainingensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Artemisia tainingensis?

Artemisia tainingenensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the family Asteraceae, placing it within the genus Artemisia, and it is specifically identified by its species name, tainingenensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Artemisia tainingenensis has perennial herbaceous stems that reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters and bear deeply pinnatifid leaves with silver-gray tomentose undersides. The leaf segments are narrow and often display a lobed or serrated margin. Small, numerous flower heads are arranged in dense, terminal panicles that emerge from the upper branches. These capitula contain tubular florets that typically exhibit a yellowish or brownish coloration. The entire plant is characterized by a fine coating of hairs that provides a distinct grayish appearance to the foliage.
