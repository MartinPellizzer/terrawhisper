Cremanthodium sagittifolium

What's the taxonomical classification of Cremanthodium sagittifolium?

Cremanthodium sagittifolium belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further classified under the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae, falling specifically within the genus Cremanthodium, and is identified by its unique species name, sagittifoliuim.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cremanthodium sagittifoliuum has lanceolate to sagittate leaves that are typically deeply lobed with acute tips and an entire or slightly wavy margin.

The plant features a decumbent or ascending growth habit with slender, often reddish stems. Its inflorescence consists of small, tubular flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters or panicles. These flowers are generally pale yellow or cream in color and possess a characteristic five-lobed corolla.

The root system is fibrous, allowing the plant to establish itself in moist, disturbed soil environments.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Cremanthodium sagittifoliuum can cause severe toxicity, including vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain, and potential neurological impairments if ingested. These adverse reactions typically stem from the presence of toxic alkaloids and glycosides found within the plant's tissues. Excessive consumption may lead to more serious systemic issues such as cardiovascular instability or gastrointestinal distress. Because the exact dosage required to trigger these symptoms can vary, careful avoidance is necessary. Users should be aware that any accidental ingestion requires immediate medical attention to manage these physiological disruptions.
