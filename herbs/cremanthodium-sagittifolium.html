Cremanthodium sagittifolium

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cremanthodium sagittifoliuum has lanceolate to sagittate leaves that are typically deeply lobed with acute tips and an entire or slightly wavy margin. The plant features a decumbent or ascending growth habit with slender, often reddish stems. Its inflorescence consists of small, tubular flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters or panicles. These flowers are generally pale yellow or cream in color and possess a characteristic five-lobed corolla. The root system is fibrous, allowing the plant to establish itself in moist, disturbed soil environments.
