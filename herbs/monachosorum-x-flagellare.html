What's the taxonomical classification of Monachosorum × flagellare?

Monachosorum × flagellare belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic classification continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae, placing it under the order Polypodiales. Furthermore, this plant is a member of the family Dennstaedtiaceae, specifically falling under the genus Monachosorum, with the species designation flagellare.

      What are the herbal preparations of Monachosorum × flagellare?

      Monachosorum × flagellare is prepared primarily as a concentrated liquid extract, a fine topical powder, or a decoction of its dried aerial parts. The liquid extract is often processed through alcohol infusion to stabilize the active botanical compounds for systemic use. Practitioners frequently utilize the powder form for external applications to treat localized skin irritations. When used as a decoction, the plant material is simmered in water to release water-soluble constituents. These various preparations allow for different modes of administration depending on the intended therapeutic effect.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Monachosorum × flagellare can cause skin irritation, localized inflammation, and allergic contact dermatitis upon physical contact with its tissues. The plant likely contains unidentified secondary metabolites that act as irritants to human dermal layers. Individuals with sensitive skin may experience redness, itching, or a stinging sensation following exposure. Symptoms can escalate if the plant's sap is accidentally transferred to the eyes or mucous membranes. It is advisable to wear gloves when handling this species to prevent these adverse reactions.
