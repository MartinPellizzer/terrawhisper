Ligularia achyrotricha

What's the taxonomical classification of Ligularia achyrotricha?

Ligularia achyrotricha belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. This plant is a representative of the family Asteraceae, which is one of the most diverse families of flowering plants. Within this family, it is specifically categorized under the genus Ligularia, with its unique identity defined by its species name, achyrotricha.

    Ligularia achyrotricha can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or digestive upset if ingested or handled improperly. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, many plants in the Asteraceae family contain compounds that may trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Direct contact with the foliage or sap could lead to redness, itching, or localized swelling on the skin. If the plant is consumed, it may cause nausea, vomiting, or abdominal discomfort due to its chemical composition. Always exercise caution and consult a professional before any potential exposure to this species.
