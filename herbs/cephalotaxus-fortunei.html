Cephalotaxus fortunei

What's the taxonomical classification of Cephalotaxus fortunei?

Cephalotaxus fortunei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Pinidae, falling further into the order Pinales. The plant is a member of the family Cephalotaxaceae, which contains the genus Cephalotaxus, ultimately leading to its specific identification as the species fortunei.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cephalotaxus fortunei has evergreen, lanceolate leaves that are approximately 2 to 5 centimeters long, dark green on the upper surface, and slightly paler underneath.

The plant features slender, woody stems that exhibit a spreading or weeping growth habit depending on the specific cultivar. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, eventually developing into fleshy, bright red or orange drupe-like arils. These arils surround a single, hard seed which is the primary reproductive structure of the species.

The overall texture of the foliage is fine and dense, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Cephalotaxus fortunei can cause severe poisoning, including gastrointestinal distress, neurological impairment, and potential death if the plant is ingested. The plant contains highly toxic alkaloids that act as potent metabolic inhibitors within the human body. Symptoms of ingestion often include nausea, vomiting, and intense abdominal pain as the toxins affect the digestive tract. If the alkaloids enter the bloodstream, they may trigger more serious complications like dizziness, seizures, or respiratory failure. Because of these extreme risks, all parts of the plant should be handled with extreme caution to avoid accidental consumption or skin irritation.
