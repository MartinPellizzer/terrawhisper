Cephalotaxus fortunei

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cephalotaxus fortunei has evergreen, lanceolate leaves that are approximately 2 to 5 centimeters long, dark green on the upper surface, and slightly paler underneath. The plant features slender, woody stems that exhibit a spreading or weeping growth habit depending on the specific cultivar. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, eventually developing into fleshy, bright red or orange drupe-like arils. These arils surround a single, hard seed which is the primary reproductive structure of the species. The overall texture of the foliage is fine and dense, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
