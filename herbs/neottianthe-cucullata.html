Neottianthe cucullaata has an erect, herbaceous habit characterized by opposite, ovate leaves with entire margins and a distinctively hooded or cucullate calyx. The inflorescence is often solitary or sparsely arranged along the stem, presenting a striking visual display. What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes mountains in South America, specifically occurring within parts of Peru, Bolivia, and northern Chile. It typically thrives in the puna ecosystem, which is characterized by cold, arid, and high-elevation alpine grasslands. These populations are often found at elevations ranging from 3,500 to 5,000 meters above sea level. The specific environmental conditions of these montane habitats restrict its natural growth to these isolated mountain ranges.
