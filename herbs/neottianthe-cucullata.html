What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Neottianthe cucullaata has an erect, herbaceous habit characterized by opposite, ovate leaves with entire margins and a distinctively hooded or cucullate calyx. The foliage is typically green and can vary in size depending on the specific environmental conditions of its habitat. Its most defining morphological feature is the large, funnel-shaped flower that emerges from the protective, cap-like structure of the calyx. The inflorescence is often solitary or sparsely arranged along the stem, presenting a striking visual display. What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes mountains in South America, specifically occurring within parts of Peru, Bolivia, and northern Chile. It typically thrives in the puna ecosystem, which is characterized by cold, arid, and high-elevation alpine grasslands. These populations are often found at elevations ranging from 3,500 to 5,000 meters above sea level. The specific environmental conditions of these montane habitats restrict its natural growth to these isolated mountain ranges. Consequently, its distribution is patchy and strictly tied to the unique climate of the central Andean highlands.

      What are the herbal preparations of Neottianthe cucullata?

      Neottianthe cucullata is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice depending on the intended medicinal application. The dried roots and aerial parts are often boiled in water to create a concentrated decoction for internal consumption. For milder therapeutic effects, the plant material is steeped in hot water to produce a gentle infusion.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Neottianthe cucullaata can cause localized skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Contact with the plant's sap or foliage may trigger redness, itching, or swelling on sensitive skin areas. Some individuals might experience more severe reactions such as hives or a rash upon exposure to its chemical compounds. If the plant is consumed accidentally, it can lead to stomach pain, nausea, or vomiting. Careful handling and avoidance of ingestion are necessary to prevent these adverse physiological responses.
