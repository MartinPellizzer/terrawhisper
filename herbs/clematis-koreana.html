Clematis koreana

What's the taxonomical classification of Clematis koreana?

Clematis koreana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is situated in the order Ranunculales. This plant is a member of the family Ranunculaceae and is categorized under the genus Clematis, specifically identified by the species name koreanas.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clematis koreana has woody, climbing stems with opposite, trifoliate leaves composed of three ovate to lanceolate leaflets. The foliage typically features serrated margins and a smooth, somewhat leathery texture. Its most distinctive feature is the large, bell-shaped flowers that hang downward from the axils. These blooms are usually pale lavender to white and possess four to six prominent, spreading tepals. The plant also produces small, hairy achenes that develop into feathery seed heads following pollination.
