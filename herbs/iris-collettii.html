Iris collettii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Iris collettii has rhizomes that spread underground to form dense clumps, supporting upright, sword-shaped leaves that are narrow, glaucous, and reach heights of up to 40 centimeters. The foliage is characterized by its grayish-green color and a distinct ensiform shape typical of many iris species. During its blooming period, the plant produces elegant flowers that feature pale violet to lavender petals with subtle yellow markings on the falls. These blossoms are supported by sturdy, upright stems that emerge from the center of the leafy basal rosette. The overall structure is compact and hardy, allowing the plant to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
