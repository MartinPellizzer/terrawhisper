Bupleurum komarovianum

What's the taxonomical classification of Bupleurum komarovianum?

Bupleurum komarovianum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is situated under the order Apiales. It is further categorized into the family Apiaceae, which is characterized by its distinct floral structures. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Bupleurum, and its specific taxonomic designation is completed by its species name, komarovianum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bupleurum komarovianum has a perennial growth habit characterized by narrow, linear to lanceolate leaves that are arranged spirally along the stems. These inflorescences are supported by sturdy stalks that rise above the basal foliage. The plant's overall architecture is relatively compact, making it well-suited for rocky or alpine environments.
