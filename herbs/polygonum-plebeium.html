Polygonum plebeium

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polygonum plebeium has slender, erect stems that typically reach heights of 20 to 60 centimeters and feature distinct, membranous ocreae surrounding the nodes. The leaves are lanceolate to narrowly elliptic in shape, possessing smooth margins and an alternate arrangement along the stem. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in dense, terminal clusters or axillary racemes, usually appearing in shades of white or pale pink. The fruit is a small, dry achene that is often reddish or brownish in color upon maturity. These morphological traits collectively allow the plant to thrive in moist, disturbed habitats.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to East Asia, specifically ranging across China, Japan, Korea, and parts of Taiwan. It is commonly found in various habitats throughout these temperate regions where it thrives in moist soils. Some populations have become naturalized in other parts of the world due to human activity and environmental adaptability. Its presence is often noted in disturbed sites or near water sources within its primary range. The species remains largely concentrated in the eastern longitudinal zones of the Asian continent.
