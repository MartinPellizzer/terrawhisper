What to know about Ruta graveolens before using it medicinally

Ruta graveolens, commonly known as common rue, is a herb with a long history of medicinal use, primarily for the treatment of various health conditions such as rheumatism, arthritis, and gout.

The herb has been found to have several health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which contribute to its therapeutic potential. The main therapeutic properties of Ruta graveolens include its ability to reduce inflammation and pain, as well as its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. The herb is rich in bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Ruta graveolens is often used to make a herbal preparation called "rue tea," which is traditionally consumed to relieve joint pain and inflammation.

Featured Study:

According to a study published by "Chemistry & biodiversity", Ruta graveolens was found to be rich in phenolic compounds, which contributed to its antioxidant properties, with a total phenol content of 41.63±0.394 mg GAE/gE and a total flavonoid content of 13.97±0.33 mg EQ/gE. The extract of Ruta graveolens also exhibited good anti-inflammatory activity, with a 50.61% inhibition of albumin denaturation at a concentration of 200 μg/mL. Additionally, the extract showed antimicrobial properties, with Staphylococcus aureus being the most sensitive bacteria, having an inhibition zone of 14.37 mm and a MIC value of 0.625 mg/mL.
