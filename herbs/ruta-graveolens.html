What to know about Ruta graveolens (rue) before using it medicinally

Rue, scientifically known as Ruta graveolens, is a herb that has been revered for its impressive health properties, which include anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects that can help alleviate symptoms of arthritis, improve digestion, and boost the immune system.

In culinary circles, rue is prized for its pungent flavor and aroma, often used to add depth and complexity to sauces, salads, and soups. As a horticultural plant, rue requires careful cultivation due to its sensitivity to temperature and moisture fluctuations, making it a challenging yet rewarding crop for gardeners. From a botanical standpoint, rue is an evergreen perennial with needle-like leaves and yellow flowers that are rich in essential oils, giving the herb its distinctive aroma and flavor.

With roots dating back thousands of years, rue has been mentioned in ancient texts from Greece to China, solidifying its place as one of history's most revered and enduring herbs.
