Lecanthus peduncularis

What's the taxonomical classification of Lecanthus peduncularis?

Lecanthus peduncularis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic progression continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, eventually falling under the order Rosales. Within this lineage, the plant is a member of the family Urticaceae, which contains the genus Lecanthus, and is specifically identified by the species epithet peduncularis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lecanthus peduncularis has opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically 5 to 15 centimeters long with entire margins and an acute apex. The plant is characterized by its woody, perennial shrubby habit and smooth to slightly textured bark. Its reproductive structures consist of small, tubular flowers that are borne on elongated, slender pedicels. These flowers are often white or pale lilac and emerge from axillary positions along the stems. The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that develops following successful pollination.
