What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lecanthus peduncularis has opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically 5 to 15 centimeters long with entire margins and an acute apex. Its reproductive structures consist of small, tubular flowers that are borne on elongated, slender pedicels. These flowers are often white or pale lilac and emerge from axillary positions along the stems. The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that develops following successful pollination.
