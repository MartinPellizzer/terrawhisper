What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ranunculus yunnanensis has perennial herbaceous stems that reach heights of approximately 10 to 40 centimeters, supporting deeply lobed, palmate leaves and bright yellow actinomorphic flowers. The foliage consists of basal and cauline leaves that feature serrated margins and distinct venation patterns. Each flower typically displays five to nine shiny, waxy petals arranged around a central cluster of numerous stamens and carpels. The plant often develops a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread through its alpine or montane habitat. Small, globose achenes form the fruit following successful pollination.
