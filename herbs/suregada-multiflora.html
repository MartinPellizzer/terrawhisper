Suregada multiflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Suregada multiflora?

Suregada multiflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized under the order Malpighiales. This plant is a member of the family Euphorbiaceae, specifically falling within the genus Suregada, with its unique biological identity defined by the species name multiflora.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Suregada multiflora has pinnately compound leaves with 7 to 15 leaflets that are elliptical to lanceolate in shape and possess serrated margins. These leaflets are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the rachis, terminating with a single apical leaflet. The plant produces small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered into dense, terminal panicles. Its woody stems exhibit a somewhat spreading growth habit, often reaching several meters in height. The bark is generally smooth on younger branches but becomes more textured as the specimen matures.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds Bannaringaolide A, Suremulide A, suregadolide A, suregadolide B, and suregadolide C. These specific bioactive molecules are primarily classified as terpenoids within the plant's chemical profile. Researchers study these substances to determine their potential therapeutic applications in pharmacology. Each of these compounds possesses a unique molecular structure that may influence biological pathways. Further investigation into these constituents helps clarify the medicinal value of the species.

As discussed in a study, six new diterpenoids were isolated from a CH(2)Cl(2)-MeOH extract of the bark of Suregada multiflora. Two compounds, suregadolide C (1) and D (2), were identified as new diterpene lactones containing a cyclopropane ring bridging C-3 and C-4 of the basic abietane skeleton. The study also identified suremulide A (3) as a new abietene diterpene lactone. Additionally, bannaringaolide A (4), a diterpene lactone based on a novel carbon skeleton with a seven-membered ring, was isolated. Finally, two new metabolites identified were the kaurane triol, suremulol A (5), and the kaurane diol, suremulol B (6).
