Suregada multiflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Suregada multiflora?

Suregada multiflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized under the order Malpighiales. This plant is a member of the family Euphorbiaceae, specifically falling within the genus Suregada, with its unique biological identity defined by the species name multiflora.

Suregada multiflora contains bioactive compounds primarily located within its leaves, bark, and roots that are utilized for various therapeutic purposes. The roots are also harvested to prepare decoctions believed to possess antimicrobial and antioxidant properties. These diverse botanical components work together to provide a wide range of medicinal applications in regional healing practices.

    What are the herbal preparations of Suregada multiflora?

    Suregada multiflora is prepared as a decoction, a powder, or a topical paste using its leaves, bark, and roots. Practitioners often boil the plant material in water to extract active compounds for oral consumption. The dried leaves can also be ground into a fine powder that is mixed with honey or water. For external applications, the fresh leaves are crushed into a paste to treat skin conditions or inflammation. These various methods allow the plant's medicinal properties to be utilized for both internal and external healing.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Suregada multiflora can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. The plant contains various chemical compounds that may trigger contact dermatitis when the sap touches sensitive skin. Individuals with known allergies to similar botanical species may experience heightened respiratory or cutaneous responses. If swallowed, the plant's components can lead to nausea, vomiting, or stomach cramping. It is essential to handle this species with caution to avoid these adverse physical reactions.
