Suregada multiflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Suregada multiflora?

Suregada multiflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized under the order Malpighiales. This plant is a member of the family Euphorbiaceae, specifically falling within the genus Suregada, with its unique biological identity defined by the species name multiflora.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Suregada multiflora contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, and terpenoids that provide its therapeutic potential. These chemical constituents are often utilized in traditional medicine to treat ailments such as inflammation, fever, and skin infections. Research indicates that the plant's antioxidant properties may help neutralize free radicals within biological systems. Some studies also suggest that specific extracts possess antimicrobial capabilities against certain bacterial strains. The precise pharmacological mechanisms of these compounds are still being actively investigated in contemporary biochemical studies.

What are the therapeutic actions of Suregada multiflora?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin diseases such as eczema, psoriasis, and various fungal infections. The leaves and bark contain bioactive compounds that act as natural antimicrobial agents to combat surface infections. Local healers often apply crushed extracts directly to lesions to reduce inflammation and itching. These botanical applications are particularly common in traditional medicine systems across Southeast Asia. By targeting specific pathogens, the plant helps soothe irritated skin and promote faster healing of wounds.
