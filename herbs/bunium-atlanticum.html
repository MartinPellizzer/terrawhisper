What is Bunium atlanticum?

Bunium atlanticum is a perennial plant species in the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its umbel inflorescence, which consists of a central stem bearing multiple, small, white or pinkish flowers. The leaves are alternate, pinnate, and have a sheathing petiole, with 3-5 pairs of pinnae and a terminal leaflet. The plant grows up to 30-50 cm in height and has a stout, subterranean rootstock, which produces a bulbous, edible root.

The rootstock of Bunium atlanticum is used as a spice and is native to the Iberian Peninsula in southwestern Europe.
