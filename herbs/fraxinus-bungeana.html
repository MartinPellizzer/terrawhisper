Fraxinus bungeana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Fraxinus bungeanay has pinnately compound leaves composed of seven to thirteen lanceolate leaflets with serrated margins and a glossy green upper surface. The tree features a spreading canopy and smooth, greyish bark that becomes fissured as the specimen matures. Small, fragrant white flowers emerge in clusters from the axils of the leaves during the spring season. These blossoms eventually develop into winged samaras that are held upright on the branches to facilitate wind dispersal. The overall structure of the plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and spreading branch architecture.
