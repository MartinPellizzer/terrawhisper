Didymocarpus mengtze

What's the taxonomical classification of Didymocarpus mengtze?

Didymocarpus mengtze belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving through its higher taxonomic ranks, the plant is organized under the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Gesneriaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Didymocarpus and the specific species epithet mengtze.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Didymocarpus mengtze has small, lanceolate leaves arranged in opposite pairs along a slender, woody stem.

The leaf margins are entire and possess a slightly thickened texture that aids in moisture retention. Each leaf features a single, prominent central vein running from the base to the apex. Tiny, inconspicuous white flowers emerge from the axils located between the leaf nodes.

The root system is fibrous and relatively shallow, anchoring the plant firmly into rocky or well-drained soil.
