Didymocarpus mengtze

What's the taxonomical classification of Didymocarpus mengtze?

Didymocarpus mengtze belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving through its higher taxonomic ranks, the plant is organized under the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Gesneriaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Didymocarpus and the specific species epithet mengtze.

    What are the herbal preparations of Didymocarpus mengtze?

    Didymocarpus mengtze is prepared as a decoction, a dried powder, or a topical paste depending on the intended therapeutic application. For external ailments affecting the skin, the plant is crushed into a thick paste and applied directly to the affected area.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Didymocarpus mengtze can cause acute contact dermatitis, localized swelling, and mild gastrointestinal distress if ingested. The plant contains specific irritants that react with human skin upon physical contact. Users may experience redness or itchy rashes if the sap touches sensitive areas. Ingestion of any part of the organism typically leads to nausea or stomach cramping. These reactions are generally temporary but require immediate washing or medical attention to prevent further irritation.
