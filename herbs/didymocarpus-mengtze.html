Didymocarpus mengtze

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Didymocarpus mengtze has small, lanceolate leaves arranged in opposite pairs along a slender, woody stem. The leaf margins are entire and possess a slightly thickened texture that aids in moisture retention. Each leaf features a single, prominent central vein running from the base to the apex. Tiny, inconspicuous white flowers emerge from the axils located between the leaf nodes. The root system is fibrous and relatively shallow, anchoring the plant firmly into rocky or well-drained soil.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the Yunnan province of southwestern China, specifically concentrated around the Mengzi region. It thrives within the unique montane ecosystems characterized by the subtropical highland climate of this area. Local populations are typically found in specific soil types and elevations that define its narrow ecological niche. Because its range is so restricted to these particular mountainous terrains, the species is considered geographically isolated.
