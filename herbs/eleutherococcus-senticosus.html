What to know about Eleutherococcus senticosus (siberian ginseng) before using it medicinally

Siberian ginseng, scientifically known as Eleutherococcus senticosus, is a herb that has been revered for its adaptogenic properties, which enable it to improve overall health by boosting the body's ability to resist stress and disease.

While it can be used in various culinary dishes, such as soups and stews, siberian ginseng is most commonly consumed as a supplement or tea due to its subtle flavor. Horticulturally, siberian ginseng is relatively low-maintenance, thriving in cool, well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. From a botanical standpoint, the herb features small, yellowish-green flowers and small, fleshy roots that contain its medicinal properties.

With ancient roots tracing back to Chinese medicine, Siberian ginseng has been used for centuries to promote vitality and longevity, earning it a reputation as one of the most revered herbs in traditional Eastern medicine.
