According to a study published by "Fitoterapia", three new indole alkaloid derivatives known as fissindoalka A-C (1-3) and one known biogeentically related polysubstituted indole alkaloid (4) were isolated from the roots of Fissistigma oldhamii. These compounds 1-4 were evaluated in vitro using mouse macrophage RAW264.7 cells to determine inhibitory activities against nitric oxide (NO) production induced by lipopolysaccharide (LPS). Compounds 2 and 3 demonstrated potent inhibitory activities on NO production with IC50 values of 2.52 ± 0.18 μM and 2.33 ± 0.16 μM, respectively.
