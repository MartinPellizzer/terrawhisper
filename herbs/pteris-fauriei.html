Pteris fauriei

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pteris fauriei has a creeping rhizome that produces pinnate fronds characterized by narrow, oblong to lanceolate pinnae with entire or slightly wavy margins. The stipe is typically slender and can vary in color from light green to a brownish hue. Each leaf segment is arranged symmetrically along the central rachis, contributing to a delicate, airy appearance. The underside of the fronds features small, discrete sori located near the margins, which are a defining feature of this fern species. These morphological traits collectively distinguish the plant within its genus.

These localized populations typically thrive in moist, shaded environments characteristic of the Indian Ocean coastal belt.       What are the herbal preparations of Pteris fauriei?

      Pteris fauriei is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice depending on the intended therapeutic use. The dried fronds are typically boiled in water to create a concentrated liquid used for internal consumption.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Pteris fauriei can cause skin irritation, contact dermatitis, and potential respiratory issues if its spores are inhaled. The plant contains chemical compounds that may act as allergens when they come into direct contact with human skin. Individuals sensitive to ferns might experience redness, itching, or a localized rash after touching the foliage. Furthermore, the fine spores released during reproduction can irritate the mucous membranes of the nose and throat. Caution is advised when handling this species to prevent accidental exposure to these irritants.
