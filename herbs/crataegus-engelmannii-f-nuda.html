Engelmann'S Hawthorn (Crataegus Engelmannii F. Nuda)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Crataegus engelmannii f. nuda.

Engelmann's hawthorn, scientifically known as Crataegus engelmannii f. nuda, is a medicinal herb native to the western United States, valued for its traditional and modern therapeutic applications. It is commonly used as an adaptogen and herbal remedy, particularly for supporting cardiovascular health due to its high concentration of flavonoids and oligomeric proanthocyanidins. Historically, Indigenous peoples and early settlers used it to treat heart conditions, digestive issues, and as a calming tonic, while its berries were also used as a spice in local cuisines. In modern wellness practices, it is increasingly incorporated into supplements and teas to promote circulation, reduce stress, and enhance overall vitality. One unique feature of Engelmann's hawthorn is its distinctive tart flavor and the presence of rare compounds like hyperoside, which contribute to its potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.
