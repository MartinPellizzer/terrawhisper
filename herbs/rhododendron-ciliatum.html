Rhododendron ciliatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Rhododendron ciliatum?

Rhododendron ciliatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is situated in the order Ericales. It is further categorized into the family Ericaceae, which places it within the genus Rhododendron, eventually reaching its specific identification as the species ciliatum.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause severe physiological distress including nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, dizziness, confusion, arrhythmia, and hypotension due to the presence of toxic grayanotoxins. These toxins interfere with the sodium channels in cell membranes, which can lead to dangerous cardiovascular issues like an irregular heartbeat or a sudden drop in blood pressure. Gastrointestinal upset often occurs shortly after ingestion, manifesting as intense stomach cramping and fluid loss. Neurological symptoms such as disorientation and lightheadedness may also impair a person's ability to function safely. If these symptoms are not managed quickly, the combination of cardiac and neurological instability can become life-threatening.
