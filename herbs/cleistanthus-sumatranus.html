Cleistanthus sumatranus

What's the taxonomical classification of Cleistanthus sumatranus?

Cleistanthus sumatranus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Malpighiales. This plant is a member of the family Phyllanthaceae, specifically categorized within the genus Cleistanthus, and is identified by its specific species name, sumatranus.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Cleistanthus sumatranus contains bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, tannins, and saponins that contribute to its therapeutic properties. These chemical constituents work synergistically to provide significant antioxidant and antimicrobial activities. Research indicates that the plant's extracts are often utilized in traditional medicine for treating inflammatory conditions and skin ailments. The specific concentration of these compounds can vary depending on the geographical location and the part of the plant harvested. Such phytochemical diversity supports its potential application in developing new pharmacological treatments.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, gastrointestinal issues, and various inflammatory conditions. Local healers often apply the crushed leaves directly to the skin to accelerate the healing of cuts and sores. It is also frequently decocted into a liquid used to soothe stomach aches and digestive discomfort. Some traditional practices utilize the plant to manage symptoms associated with fever and internal aches. These medicinal applications rely on the plant's specific chemical properties to combat pathogens and reduce swelling.
