Clematis loureiroana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clematis loureiroana has opposite, simple, or trifoliate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. The stems are woody and climbing, utilizing petioles to scramble over surrounding vegetation. Its flowers are characterized by relatively small, actinomorphic petals that often exhibit pale or creamy hues. The reproductive structures include numerous stamens that are prominent within the floral center. The plant produces small, achene-type fruits that are often topped by persistent, feathery styles for wind dispersal.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the high-altitude regions of the Sierra Madre Occidental mountain range in western Mexico. It is primarily found growing within the temperate coniferous forests located in the states of Chihuahua and Durango. The species typically inhabits rocky slopes and forest edges where it can access specific moisture levels provided by mountain mists. Because it is restricted to these particular montane ecosystems, its range is quite limited compared to more widespread clematis varieties. This specialized distribution makes the plant a unique component of the local high-elevation biodiversity.

      What are the herbal preparations of Clematis loureiroana?

      Clematis loureiroana is prepared as an aqueous decoction, an infusion, or a macerated tincture using its dried aerial parts. These liquid preparations are the primary methods used to extract the bioactive compounds from the plant material. For decoctions, the roots or stems are boiled in water to ensure the release of tougher constituents. Infusions are typically used for more delicate leaves to prevent the degradation of sensitive phytochemicals. Tinctures allow for long-term storage and more concentrated dosages when dissolved in an alcohol base.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Clematis loureiroana can cause severe skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential neurological issues if ingested or handled improperly. The plant contains protoanemonin, a chemical compound that acts as a potent irritant to mucous membranes and skin. Contact with the foliage often results in redness, swelling, or blistering known as contact dermatitis. If the plant is consumed, it may trigger vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea. In more serious cases of ingestion, the toxins can lead to more significant systemic reactions.
