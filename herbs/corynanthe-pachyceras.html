Corynanthe pachyceras

What's the taxonomical classification of Corynanthe pachyceras?

Corynanthe pachyceras belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized under the order Gentianaales. This plant is a member of the family Rubiaceae, which is situated within the genus Corynanthe, and is specifically identified by the species name pachyceras.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Corynanthe pachyceras has a woody, arborescent habit characterized by thick, succulent-like stems and a crown of large, elliptical leaves. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the axils of the foliage, often presenting in clusters. What are the pharmacological activities of Corynanthe pachyceras?

This plant has leishmanicidal, antiplasmodial, aphrodisiac, cytotoxic, and antioxidant pharmacological activities. Its leishmanicidal and antiplasmodial properties suggest significant potential for treating parasitic infections such as leishmaniasis and malaria. The cytotoxic effects indicate that certain extracts may possess the ability to inhibit the growth of various cancer cell lines. Additionally, the plant exhibits antioxidant capabilities that help neutralize harmful free radicals within biological systems. Research also highlights its traditional use as an aphrodisiac to enhance sexual function.

As discussed in a study, five indole alkaloids, including corynantheidine, corynantheine, dihydrocorynantheine, alpha-yohimbine, and corynanthine, were isolated from the bark of Corynanthe pachyceras K. Schum. (Rubiaceae). These alkaloids showed pronounced leishmanicidal activity against Leishmania major promastigotes with an IC50 at the micromolar level. However, the plant extracts showed no significant in vitro antiplasmodial activity against chloroquine-sensitive Plasmodium falciparum. Cytotoxicity was assessed using drug sensitive KB-3-1 and multidrug-resistant KB-V1 cell lines, where the activity was found to be low. Additionally, the alkaloids are apparently not substrates for the P-glycoprotein (P-170) efflux pump.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for the treatment of age-induced hypogonadism, infertility, and male sexual dysfunction. It contains bioactive compounds that help regulate hormonal balances necessary for reproductive health. By addressing hormonal deficiencies, the plant supports the restoration of natural testosterone levels in aging men. These physiological improvements can lead to enhanced fertility and improved sexual performance. Consequently, it serves as a traditional remedy for various reproductive and endocrine issues in males.

As discussed in a study, Corynanthe pachyceras was administered to 36 aged male Wistar rats aged 17-19 months in doses of 8 mg/kg or 50 mg/kg of aqueous or ethanolic extracts over 2 weeks. Treatment with these extracts resulted in a significant increase in sperm count and motility (P < .01-.001), LH (P < .01), FSH (P < .01-.001), testosterone (P < .01), and NO (P < .05) levels. The extracts also improved antioxidant status by reducing MDA levels and increasing SOD (P < .05-.01) and glutathione (P < .05-.01). Additionally, Corynanthe pachyceras caused a significant decrease in motivation parameters and an increase in ejaculation frequency. These findings demonstrate that Corynanthe pachyceras can boost reproductive conditions in age-induced hypoagonadism rats through its androgenic, aphrodisiac, and antioxidant potentials.
