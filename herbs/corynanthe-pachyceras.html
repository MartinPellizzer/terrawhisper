Corynanthe pachyceras

What's the taxonomical classification of Corynanthe pachyceras?

Corynanthe pachyceras belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized under the order Gentianaales. This plant is a member of the family Rubiaceae, which is situated within the genus Corynanthe, and is specifically identified by the species name pachyceras.

What are the pharmacological activities of Corynanthe pachyceras?

This plant has leishmanicidal, antiplasmodial, antioxidant, cytotoxic, and aphrodisiac pharmacological activities. Its leishmanicidal and antiplasmodial properties allow it to combat specific protozoan parasites that cause serious human diseases. The antioxidant capacity of the plant helps neutralize harmful free radicals within biological systems. Furthermore, its cytotoxic effects suggest potential utility in targeting specialized cell types, such as those found in cancer research. Finally, the plant is recognized for its aphrodisiac qualities which impact reproductive health.

According to a study published by "Planta medica", five indole alkaloids including corynantheidine, corynantheine, dihydrocorynantheine, alpha-yohimbine and corynanthine were isolated from the bark of Corynanthe pachyceras K. Schum. (Rubiaceae). These alkaloids showed pronounced leishmanicidal activity against Leishmania major promastigotes with an IC50 at the micromolar level. However, the plant extracts showed no significant in vitro antiplasmodial activity against chloroquine-sensitive Plasmodium falciparum. Cytotoxicity assessment using drug sensitive KB-3-1 and multidrug-resistant KB-V1 cell lines resulted in low values. Additionally, the alkaloids are apparently not substrates for the P-glycoprotein (P-170) efflux pump.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating age-induced hypogonadism, male sexual dysfunctions, reproductive performance, semen quality, and sexual performance. It functions by helping to restore hormonal balance in men experiencing declining testosterone levels due to aging. The botanical extracts are often utilized to improve various aspects of male reproductive performance and overall fertility. Research suggests it can significantly enhance semen quality by optimizing sperm concentration and motility. Additionally, it is applied to boost sexual performance and address common issues related to male sexual dysfunctions.

According to a study published by "Sexual medicine", Corynanthe pachyceras is used to address age-induced hypoandrogenism, a condition where the body does not produce enough testosterone. This plant is studied for its potential to improve reproductive performance and sexual health in androgen-deficient subjects. In a study involving 36 aged male Wistar rats aged 17-19 months, extracts were administered at doses of 8 mg/kg or 50 mg/kg. The plant was shown to increase sperm count and motility (P < .01-.001), LH (P < .01), FSH (P < .01-.001), testosterone (P < .01), and NO (P < .05) levels. Additionally, it improved antioxidant status (P < .05-.01) by reducing MDA and increasing SOD (P < .05-.01) and glutathione.
