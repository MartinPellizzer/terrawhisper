Corynanthe pachyceras

What's the taxonomical classification of Corynanthe pachyceras?

Corynanthe pachyceras belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized under the order Gentianaales. This plant is a member of the family Rubiaceae, which is situated within the genus Corynanthe, and is specifically identified by the species name pachyceras.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Corynanthe pachyceras contains various indole alkaloids, such as corynantheine and various related alkaloids, that exhibit potential pharmacological activities. These specialized secondary metabolites are primarily concentrated within the bark and roots of the plant. Research suggests that these specific chemical structures may possess neuroprotective or antimicrobial properties. Scientists study these compounds to understand their interaction with biological receptors in the human body. Ongoing biochemical investigations aim to isolate these molecules for potential therapeutic applications in modern medicine.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including fever, malaria, stomachaches, and inflammatory conditions. In traditional medicinal practices, different parts of the plant are prepared to address digestive disturbances and abdominal pain. It is also utilized by local communities to help manage symptoms associated with infectious diseases. The pharmacological potential of its extracts is often explored for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. These applications highlight its significance in regional herbal medicine for managing systemic discomfort.
