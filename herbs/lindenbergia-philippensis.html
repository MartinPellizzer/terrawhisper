What's the taxonomical classification of Lindenbergia philippensis?

Lindenbergia philippensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Lamiales and the family Orobanchaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific placement within the genus Lindenbergia, with its unique identity established by the species name philippensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lindenbergia philippensis has an erect, herbaceous habit characterized by slender, quadrangular stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves with entire or slightly serrated margins.

The foliage typically presents a bright green color and is arranged in a decussate pattern along the stem. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the axils, often displaying a pale color and a distinct corolla shape typical of the Verbenaceae family. The plant also produces minute seeds that are contained within small, dry fruits.

These morphological features allow the species to thrive in its specific tropical habitats.
