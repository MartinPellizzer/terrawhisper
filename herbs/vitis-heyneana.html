Vitis heyneana

What's the taxonomical classification of Vitis heyneana?

Vitis heyneana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It follows the taxonomic order Vitales and is a member of the family Vitaceae. Finally, it is defined by its genus, Vitis, and its specific species designation, heyneana.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Vitis heyneana contains various bioactive phytochemicals including tannins, flavonoids, saponins, and alkaloids that contribute to its therapeutic potential. These specific secondary metabolites are often utilized in traditional medicinal practices to address inflammatory conditions and digestive issues. Researchers have studied these chemical constituents to understand their antioxidant properties and their ability to combat oxidative stress in biological systems. The presence of these organic compounds suggests that the plant may possess antimicrobial and analgesic qualities. Further investigation into these precise molecular structures is necessary to fully characterize their pharmacological applications.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat stomach aches, intestinal worms, and various skin infections. The medicinal properties of the species are often utilized by local communities to manage digestive ailments. It is also applied topically to address dermatological issues and promote healing. Traditional practitioners rely on the plant's extracts to alleviate internal discomfort. These applications highlight the plant's diverse role in ethnobotanical medicine.
