Maclura tricuspidata

What's the taxonomical classification of Maclura tricuspidata?

Maclurura tricuspidata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Rosales and is a member of the family Moraceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Maclurura, and its unique species designation, tricuspidata.

    What are the herbal preparations of Maclura tricuspidata?

    Maclura tricuspidata is prepared as decoctions, infusions, or topical pastes using the plant's leaves, bark, or fruit. The bark is often boiled in water to create a concentrated decoction for internal use. Leaves may be steeped in hot water to produce a mild infusion. These various methods allow for the extraction of specific phytochemicals depending on the desired therapeutic effect.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Maclurura tricuspidata can cause severe skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if any part of the plant is ingested. The milky white sap contains irritants that may lead to redness, swelling, or blistering upon contact with human skin. If these juices enter the eyes, they can cause significant inflammation or painful stinging sensations. Ingesting the fruit or other plant tissues may result in nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. Due to these risks, careful handling is necessary to avoid accidental exposure to the sap.
