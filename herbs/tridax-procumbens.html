Tridax Procumbens: What To Know Before Using It For Medicinal Purposes

In traditional medicine, Tridax procumbens has been employed to treat ailments such as inflammation, skin infections, and digestive disorders due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Overall, Tridax procumbens continues to be a subject of interest in pharmacological research for its diverse medicinal applications.

Side Effects

Tridax procumbens can have some side effects, such as gastrointestinal disturbances including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, which may occur due to its irritant properties.

Prolonged use or high doses might lead to liver damage, as some compounds in the plant have been shown to be hepatotoxic. Skin irritation or allergic reactions may also develop upon contact with the plant's leaves or sap, causing redness, itching, or rash. In rare cases, it may cause respiratory issues if the plant is inhaled in powdered form, leading to coughing or difficulty breathing.

Therefore, it is important to use Tridax procumbens with caution and under the guidance of a healthcare professional.
