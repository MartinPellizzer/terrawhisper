Phalaris arundinacea

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phalaris arundinacea has erect, perennial culms that typically reach heights between 30 and 100 centimeters with slender, cylindrical stems and linear, lanceolate leaves. The foliage consists of flat, green blades that often possess a slightly involute margin and a prominent midrib. Its inflorescence is a dense, spike-like panicle composed of numerous small, ovoid spikelets arranged tightly along the central axis. These spikelets feature short, membranous glumes and contain single florets that are often slightly pubescent. The overall structure is characteristic of robust, clump-forming grasses adapted to wetland environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the western coastal regions of North America, specifically spanning from British Columbia in Canada down through Washington, Oregon, and California. It is most frequently encountered in moist habitats such as riparian zones, marshes, and along the edges of wetlands. Within these ecosystems, the species thrives in areas where soil moisture is consistently high. Its distribution is largely influenced by the availability of water and the temperate climate of the Pacific coast. Consequently, it remains concentrated in these specific ecological niches rather than spreading into more arid inland environments.

      Phalaris arundinacea is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice derived from its dried leaves and stems.
