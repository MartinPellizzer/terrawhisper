What's the taxonomical classification of Phalaris arundinacea?

Phalaris arundiacacea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it falls under the order Poales and is a member of the family Poaceae. Specifically, this plant is identified by the genus Phalaris, and its precise biological designation is the species arundiacacea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phalaris arundinacea has erect, perennial culms that typically reach heights between 30 and 100 centimeters with slender, cylindrical stems and linear, lanceolate leaves. The foliage consists of flat, green blades that often possess a slightly involute margin and a prominent midrib. Its inflorescence is a dense, spike-like panicle composed of numerous small, ovoid spikelets arranged tightly along the central axis. These spikelets feature short, membranous glumes and contain single florets that are often slightly pubescent. The overall structure is characteristic of robust, clump-forming grasses adapted to wetland environments.

        What side effects this plant can have?

        This plant can cause severe toxicosis in livestock including respiratory distress, neurological impairment, and death due to the presence of ergot alkaloids. These alkaloids interfere with the central nervous system and can lead to symptoms such as tremors, seizures, and loss of coordination. Grazing animals may also experience vasoconstriction, which results in gangrene or the loss of extremities like ears and tails. The severity of these side effects often depends on the concentration of alkaloids present in the specific plant varieties being consumed. Monitoring pasture health is essential to prevent accidental poisoning in cattle, sheep, and horses.
