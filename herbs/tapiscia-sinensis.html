Tapiscia sinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Tapiscia sinensis?

Tapiscia sinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Magnoliidae and is organized within the order Huerteales. This plant is the type specimen for its specific family, Tapisciaceae, and is further categorized into the genus Tapiscia, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species sinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tapiscia sinensis has small, lanceolate to ovate leaves that are arranged oppositely along its slender, herbaceous stems. The foliage typically displays a bright green color and possesses smooth margins. Its inflorescence consists of small, delicate flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. The plant maintains a low-growing, bushy habit with a fine texture. These morphological traits contribute to its aesthetic value in ornamental horticulture.
