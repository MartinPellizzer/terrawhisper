Clitoria mariana

What's the taxonomical classification of Clitoria mariana?

Clitoria mariana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical structure of botanical classification, it falls under the order Fabales and is a member of the family Fabaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Clitoria, and its unique species designation, mariaana.

Clitoria mariana contains medicinal compounds primarily located in its leaves and flowers. This plant serves as a vital resource in regional folk medicine for its diverse healing potential.

    What are the molecular targets of this plant?

    Clitoria mariana targets various enzymes and receptors involved in inflammatory and oxidative stress pathways, specifically interacting with cyclooxygenase-2 and various antioxidant enzymes. The plant contains bioactive phytochemicals that modulate the expression of pro-inflammatory cytokines to reduce swelling and pain. These compounds often act by inhibiting the activity of nitric oxide synthase to regulate vascular responses. Additionally, its secondary metabolites influence the signaling pathways of nuclear factor erythroid 2-related factor 2 to enhance cellular defense mechanisms. This multifaceted interaction with molecular signaling proteins underpins its traditional medicinal uses for managing inflammation.

    What are the therapeutic actions of Clitoria mariana?

    Clitoria mariana has diuretic, emmenagogue, and abortifacient properties used primarily to stimulate menstruation and induce uterine contractions. This plant is traditionally utilized in various herbal practices to regulate menstrual cycles and manage certain reproductive health issues. By increasing blood flow to the pelvic region, it can help alleviate symptoms related to irregular periods. However, its ability to stimulate the uterus means it carries significant risks during pregnancy. Due to these potent effects, it should be used with extreme caution and under professional guidance.

    What diseases this plant treats?

    This plant is used to treat various ailments including fever, stomach aches, dysentery, and skin infections. Its medicinal properties are frequently applied to soothe internal inflammation and digestive distress. In some traditional practices, the plant is also utilized to manage respiratory issues and minor wounds. Local practitioners often prepare decoctions or poultices to target these specific physiological symptoms. The botanical components serve as a natural remedy for stabilizing bodily functions during illness.

    What are the herbal preparations of Clitoria mariana?

    Clitoria mariana is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a tincture to extract its active botanical properties. Practitioners often boil the dried plant material in water to create a concentrated decoction for therapeutic use. Alternatively, steeping the leaves or flowers in hot water produces a milder infusion suitable for daily consumption. Making a tincture involves soaking the plant in alcohol to ensure a long shelf life and high potency. These various liquid forms allow for precise dosing and efficient absorption of the plant's constituents.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Clitoria mariana can cause adverse reactions such as nausea, dizziness, stomach cramps, and potential allergic skin irritations upon contact. While specific clinical data on human toxicity remains limited, the presence of certain alkaloids may trigger gastrointestinal distress if ingested in improper doses. Some users have reported mild headaches or lightheadedness following consumption. Because its chemical profile is not fully documented for human safety, there is a risk of unpredictable systemic effects. It is essential to exercise caution and consult medical professionals before any internal use.
