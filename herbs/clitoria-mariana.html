Clitoria mariana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clitoria mariana has a prostrate or ascending growth habit characterized by slender, creeping stems that produce trifoliate leaves with small, ovate leaflets. The plant features delicate, zygomorphic flowers that typically exhibit a pale blue or violet hue with intricate markings on the standard petal. These blossoms are borne on long, thin pedicels that extend from the leaf axils. The reproductive structures are housed within a specialized floral architecture designed for specific insect pollination. Small, cylindrical seed pods develop following fertilization, eventually splitting to release tiny seeds into the surrounding substrate.

What are the therapeutic actions of Clitoria mariana?

Clitoria mariana has therapeutic properties that function as an emmenagogue, abortifacient, uterine stimulant, diaphoric, and alterative. This plant is primarily utilized to stimulate uterine contractions and regulate menstrual cycles. Due to its ability to induce these contractions, it is also classified as a potent abortifacient in traditional medicine. Furthermore, it acts as a diaphoric to promote sweating and help balance bodily functions. Finally, its role as an alterative helps to purify the blood and restore overall systemic health.

What are the herbal preparations of Clitoria mariana?

Clitoria mariana is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a tincture to extract its active botanical properties. Practitioners often boil the dried plant material in water to create a concentrated decoction for therapeutic use. Alternatively, steeping the leaves or flowers in hot water produces a milder infusion suitable for daily consumption. Making a tincture involves soaking the plant in alcohol to ensure a long shelf life and high potency. These various liquid forms allow for precise dosing and efficient absorption of the plant's constituents.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Clitoria mariana can cause adverse reactions such as nausea, dizziness, stomach cramps, and potential allergic skin irritations upon contact. While specific clinical data on human toxicity remains limited, the presence of certain alkaloids may trigger gastrointestinal distress if ingested in improper doses. Some users have reported mild headaches or lightheadedness following consumption. Because its chemical profile is not fully documented for human safety, there is a risk of unpredictable systemic effects. It is essential to exercise caution and consult medical professionals before any internal use.
