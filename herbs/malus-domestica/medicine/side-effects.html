10 Possible Side Effects Of Malus Domestica (Apple)

Malus domestica has some side effects when used improperly, such as allergic reactions, digestive issues, and skin irritation.

Causes of these side effects include consuming large amounts, having sensitive skin or allergies, or not cooking the apple properly.

For example, severe digestive issues can lead to malnutrition, while allergic reactions can cause life-threatening anaphylaxis, significantly impacting one's quality of life.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Malus domestica if used imporperly.

Malus domestica causes allergic reactions when the body's immune system overreacts to certain proteins found in its fruit, leaves, and pollen.

This hypersensitivity can trigger symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling of the skin, respiratory issues like congestion and asthma, and even gastrointestinal problems like diarrhea and stomach cramps.

The severity of these reactions varies widely among individuals, but can be severe enough to require medical attention in some cases.

Malus domestica triggers asthma attacks due to its allergenic properties.

The proteins present in apples can cause an immune response, leading to the release of histamine and other inflammatory chemicals in the body. This reaction can lead to narrowing of airways, making breathing difficult, and triggering asthmatic symptoms such as wheezing and coughing.

Additionally, the high levels of polyphenols and flavonoids in apples may also contribute to the exacerbation of asthma symptoms.
