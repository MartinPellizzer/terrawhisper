Dysosma tsayuensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dysosma tsayuensis has herbaceous stems that reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters, bearing leaves that are oppositely arranged and primarily ovate or elliptical in shape with serrated margins. The leaf blades feature distinct venation and are attached to the stem via short petioles. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, typically presenting in small clusters or solitary arrangements. These floral structures consist of five petals that are often white or pale in color, surrounding a central reproductive core. The plant develops a root system consisting of slender rhizomes that facilitate its growth in alpine environments.
