Stachyurus yunnanensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Stachyurus yunnanensis?

Stachyurus yunnanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it occupies a specific evolutionary lineage defined by the order Crossosomatales. The plant is further categorized under the family Stachyuraceae, which contains its parent genus, Stachyurus. Finally, it is identified at the species level as yunnanensis, completing its formal taxonomic hierarchy.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stachyurus yunnanensis has woody stems, simple lanceolate to elliptic leaves, and small cream-colored flowers arranged in drooping racemes.

The leaves are typically arranged alternately along the branches and possess serrated margins. Its flowers emerge from the leaf axils, characterized by five distinct petals and numerous stamens. The plant develops small, woody capsules that split open to release its seeds.

These structural features are characteristic of its classification within the Stachyuraceae family.
