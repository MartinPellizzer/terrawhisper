What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stachyurus yunnanensis has woody stems, simple lanceolate to elliptic leaves, and small cream-colored flowers arranged in drooping racemes. The leaves are typically arranged alternately along the branches and possess serrated margins. Its flowers emerge from the leaf axils, characterized by five distinct petals and numerous stamens. The plant develops small, woody capsules that split open to release its seeds. These structural features are characteristic of its classification within the Stachyuraceae family.
