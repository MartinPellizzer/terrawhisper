Blue Ginger (Curcuma aeruginosa)

Blue Ginger (Curcuma aeruginosa) is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, native to Southeast Asia, Indonesia, and India. Traditionally, its rhizomes, roots, and leaves have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, carminative, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in ayurvedic medicine, traditional chinese medicine, and japanese kampo medicine.

Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height

Leaves
Broad leaves with upper surface exhibiting a bluish-green hue and lower surface displaying a darker, often purplish tone, featuring prominent stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Inflorescences consist of dense clusters of zygomorphic flowers with violet to blue petals, three sepals, and a prominent three-lobed staminode.
Stems
Climbing, cylindrical, woody, with opposite branching, covered in short, stiff hairs, and exhibiting a blue-green hue due to the presence of anthocyanin pigments.
