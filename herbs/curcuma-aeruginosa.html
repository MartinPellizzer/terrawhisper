Blue ginger

Curcuma aeruginosa

Blue Ginger (Curcuma aeruginosa) is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, native to Southeast Asia. Traditionally, its rhizomes have been used for treating skin diseases, anti-inflammatory purposes, and as a digestive aid. The plant is valued in various cultural practices for its medicinal properties.

Botanical Identification

Blue Ginger, or Curcuma aeruginosa, is a species of the Zingiberaceae family. Commonly known as Blue Java or Blue Curcuma, it is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia. The plant features rhizomes, lanceolate leaves, and pale blue flowers. It is also referred to as "Blue Ginger" in some herbal texts.

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for digestive issues and inflammation.

Decoction: Used for respiratory and digestive ailments.

Powder: Used for digestive disorders and as a dietary supplement.

Safety Profile

Blue Ginger (Curcuma aeruginosa) is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and should be avoided by people with gallbladder issues due to its potential to stimulate bile secretion.
