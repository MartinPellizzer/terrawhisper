Rhododendron primuliflorum

What's the taxonomical classification of Rhododendron primuliflorum?

Rhododendron primuliflorum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is part of the order Ericales and falls within the family Ericaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Rhododendron and is specifically designated as the species primuliflorum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhododendron primuliflorum has small, elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and often feature a brownish, scaly underside. Its flowers are funnel-shaped and range in color from pale pink to deep rose, emerging in clusters from the terminal ends of branches. The foliage remains evergreen, providing a consistent texture throughout the year. These distinct leaf scales and delicate floral structures are key identifiers for the species.

      What side effects this plant can have?

      This plant can cause severe toxic reactions including nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, dizziness, confusion, arrhythmia, hypotension, seizures, and respiratory depression. These dangerous symptoms result from the presence of grayanotoxins which interfere with the sodium channels in cell membranes. Ingestion of the plant can lead to a rapid drop in blood pressure and irregular heart rhythms that may become life-threatening. Neurological complications such as intense disorientation or loss of consciousness are also common during poisoning episodes. Severe cases may escalate to include convulsive seizures or a critical slowing of breathing.
