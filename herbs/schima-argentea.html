Schima argentea

What's the taxonomical classification of Schima argentea?

Schima argentea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. As a member of the order Ericales, it falls under the family Theaceae. This specific plant is identified by the genus Schima and is distinguished at the species level as argentea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Schima argentea has evergreen, lanceolate leaves that are dark green and glossy on the upper surface while possessing a distinct silvery-white underside.

The plant typically grows as a medium to large tree with a straight trunk and a spreading crown. Its flowers are white and actinomorphic, featuring five petals and numerous prominent stamens that create a brush-like appearance. The fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

These morphological features allow the species to thrive in diverse forest ecosystems.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Schima argentea can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or gastrointestinal distress if ingested or applied improperly. While the plant is widely used in traditional medicine, there is limited scientific data regarding its potential toxicity in humans. Some individuals may experience contact dermatitis after touching the leaves or bark. If consumed in large quantities, it might lead to stomach upset or nausea.
