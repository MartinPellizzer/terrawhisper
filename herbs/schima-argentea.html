Schima argentea

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Schima argentea has evergreen, lanceolate leaves that are dark green and glossy on the upper surface while possessing a distinct silvery-white underside. The plant typically grows as a medium to large tree with a straight trunk and a spreading crown. Its flowers are white and actinomorphic, featuring five petals and numerous prominent stamens that create a brush-like appearance. The fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds. These morphological features allow the species to thrive in diverse forest ecosystems.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the subtropical and tropical montane forests of southern China, specifically found in provinces such as Guangdong, Guangxi, Fujian, Hunan, and Jiangxi. It thrives in hilly and mountainous regions where the elevation typically ranges from middle to high altitudes. Within these habitats, it is often integrated into mixed evergreen broad-leaved forests. The species prefers humid environments with well-drained soils characteristic of these specific southeastern Asian landscapes. Its range is geographically concentrated within these warm, moist mountain belts.
