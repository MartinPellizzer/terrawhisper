Pueraria stricta

What's the taxonomical classification of Pueraria stricta?

Pueraria stricta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Fabales and into the family Fabaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Pueraria, and its unique species designation, stricta.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pueraria stricta has a climbing or scrambling woody habit characterized by trifoliate leaves with ovate to elliptic leaflets and small, papilionaceous flowers that are typically pale purple or white. The stems are often twining and can reach significant lengths as they ascend through surrounding vegetation. Each leaf consists of three leaflets that possess smooth margins and distinctively pointed tips. The inflorescence is arranged in racemes, bearing numerous individual flowers that bloom in clusters. Additionally, the plant produces legume-type pods that eventually dry and split to release its seeds.
