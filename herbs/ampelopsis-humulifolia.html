What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ampelopsis humulifolia has climbing stems that reach several meters in length, trifoliate leaves with serrated margins, and small, inconspicuous greenish flowers. Its woody vines utilize tendrils to anchor themselves to surrounding vegetation for vertical growth. The plant typically produces small, fleshy drupes that change color as they reach maturity. These morphological traits allow the species to thrive in shaded, moist forest environments.
