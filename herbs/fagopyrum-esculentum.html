Side Effects of fagopyrum esculentum

Fagopyrum esculentum induces nausea, which can manifest as a persistent feeling of queasiness or an urge to vomit, particularly when consumed in large quantities or by individuals with heightened sensitivity.

This herb is also known to trigger allergic reactions in some people, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and even more severe responses like throat swelling, which can be life-threatening in extreme cases. Additionally, it may cause skin rashes and irritation, often appearing as red, itchy patches or blisters on the skin upon contact or ingestion. The herb's potential to result in vomiting further underscores its gastrointestinal impact, making it unsuitable for those with sensitive digestive systems.

Due to these various side effects, it is crucial to exercise caution when using Fagopyrum esculentum and to consult with a healthcare professional before incorporating it into one's diet or medicinal regimen.
