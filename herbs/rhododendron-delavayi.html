Rhododendron delavayi

What's the taxonomical classification of Rhododendron delavayi?

Rhododendron delaavay belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is a member of the order Ericales and falls within the family Ericaceae. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its genus, Rhododendron, and its specific species designation, delaayi.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhododendron delavayi has leathery, elliptical leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and often display a fine, brownish indumentum on the undersides. The plant grows as an evergreen shrub with woody stems that can reach heights of several meters in its natural habitat. Its inflorescences consist of clusters of funnel-shaped flowers that typically exhibit a vibrant pink or rose-colored hue. Each individual blossom features five fused petals and prominent stamens that emerge from the center of the corolla. The texture of the foliage remains consistent throughout the seasons, providing a dense and structural appearance to the shrub.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, specifically distributed across parts of northern India, Nepal, Bhutan, and southern Tibet. It thrives primarily in montane forests and subalpine shrublands located between 2,500 and 4,000 meters above sea level. The species prefers the cool, moist environments provided by temperate mountain slopes and rocky crevices. Within these ranges, its presence is often tied to specific microclimates characterized by high humidity and seasonal snow cover. This plant contains the medicinal compounds catechin, epicatechin, and quercetiin-3-O-beta-D-arabinoopyranoside. These specific phytochemicals are part of a diverse profile of polyphenols found within the species. Such compounds are often studied for their potential antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties in biological systems. Researchers analyze these molecules to understand how they might interact with human cellular pathways.

According to a study published by "Zhong yao cai = Zhongyaocai = Journal of Chinese medicinal materials", eight compounds were isolated and elucidated from the leaves of Rhododendron delavayi. These identified compounds include 3',4',7-trihydroxy-3,5-dimethoxyflavone (1), 3',4',5,7-tetrahydroxy-3-methoxyflavone (2), quercetin-3-O-beta-D-arabinopyranoxide (3), catechin (4), epicatechin (5), epicatechin-(2beta-->O-->7,4beta-->8)-ent-epicatechin (6), (2S)-4-(3,4-dihydroxyphenyl)-2-butanol (7), and (3,4-dihydroxyphenyl)-2-ethanol (8). Within this group, compounds 2, 3, 6, 7, and 8 were isolated from this plant for the first time. The chemical constituents were purified using silica gel and Sephadex LH-20 column chromatography.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause severe poisoning through the ingestion of grayanotoxins, leading to symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea. Once these toxins enter the system, they can disrupt the central nervous system to cause dizziness and confusion. More serious cardiovascular complications may arise, specifically manifesting as life-threatening arrhythmia. In extreme cases of toxicity, a person might suffer from seizures and dangerous respiratory depression. These combined effects necessitate immediate medical intervention to prevent fatal outcomes.
