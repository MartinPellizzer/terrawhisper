Malus leiocalyca

What's the taxonomical classification of Malus leiocalyca?

Malus leiocalyca belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its hierarchical descent, the plant is placed in the order Rosales and falls under the family Rosaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Malus and is specifically designated by the species name leiocalyca.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Malus leiocalyca has deciduous, simple leaves that are ovate or elliptical in shape, featuring finely serrated margins and a glabrous or sparsely pubescent surface.

The plant is characterized by woody stems that develop a rough, lenticellate bark as it matures. Its inflorescence typically consists of small, white or pale pink flowers emerging from distinct calyces. The fruit produced is a pome, which varies in size and color depending on the specific environmental conditions.

These morphological features help distinguish this species from other members of the Malus genus.
