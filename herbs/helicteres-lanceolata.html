Helicteres lanceolata

What's the taxonomical classification of Helicteres lanceolata?

Helicteres lanceolata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Malvales. This plant is a member of the family Malvaceae, falling under the genus Helicteres, and is specifically identified by its species name, lanceolata.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Helicteres lanceolata contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including flavonoids, tannins, saponins, and terpenoids that facilitate its medicinal properties. These chemical constituents contribute to the plant's documented antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial activities. Traditional healers often utilize the bark and leaves to treat ailments such as skin infections, digestive issues, and fever. Research suggests that the presence of specific polyphenolic compounds may play a significant role in protecting cells from oxidative stress. The diverse profile of these medicinal compounds makes the species a subject of interest for pharmacological studies.

What are the therapeutic actions of Helicteres lanceolata?

Helicteres lanceolata has medicinal properties primarily used to treat skin infections, fever, and gastrointestinal ailments through its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory compounds. The leaves and bark are frequently decocted to serve as an antiseptic wash for various dermatological conditions. In traditional medicine, preparations from the plant are also utilized to reduce body temperature during febrile episodes. Some practitioners use the extracts to alleviate stomach discomfort and aid digestion. These therapeutic applications rely on the diverse phytochemical profile found within the different parts of the shrub.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, digestive disorders, fever, and various inflammatory conditions. The decoction of its leaves and bark serves as a traditional remedy for managing gastrointestinal issues like diarrhea. Its medicinal properties are also applied topically to soothe skin irritations and promote healing in localized infections. Additionally, practitioners use preparations from the plant to help reduce body temperature during febrile episodes. These diverse applications highlight the plant's significant role in traditional herbal medicine.
