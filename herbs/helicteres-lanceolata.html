Helicteres lanceolata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Helicteres lanceolata has lanceolate to elliptic leaves that are typically 5 to 15 centimeters long, arranged oppositely on woody stems, and possess entire margins with acute apices. The foliage often exhibits a slightly leathery texture and a distinctively green color on the upper surface. Its reproductive structures consist of small, tubular flowers that frequently emerge in axillary clusters. These flowers transition into globose, woody capsules that split open to reveal numerous seeds. The overall architecture of the plant is that of a spreading shrub or small tree with a dense branching pattern.
