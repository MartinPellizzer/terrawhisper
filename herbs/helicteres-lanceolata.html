What's the taxonomical classification of Helicteres lanceolata?

Helicteres lanceolata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Malvales. This plant is a member of the family Malvaceae, falling under the genus Helicteres, and is specifically identified by its species name, lanceolata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Helicteres lanceolata has lanceolate to elliptic leaves that are typically 5 to 15 centimeters long, arranged oppositely on woody stems, and possess entire margins with acute apices. Its reproductive structures consist of small, tubular flowers that frequently emerge in axillary clusters. These flowers transition into globose, woody capsules that split open to reveal numerous seeds.
