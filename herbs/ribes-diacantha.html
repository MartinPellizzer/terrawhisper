Ribes diacantha

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ribes diacantha has woody, spreading shrubs that typically reach heights of 0.5 to 1.5 meters and feature leaves with three to five shallowly lobed segments and serrated margins. The foliage is characterized by a somewhat pubescent texture, providing a fine, hairy appearance to the surfaces. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves, often displaying colors ranging from pale pink to light purple. The plant's stems are often slightly angled and can be covered in fine hairs or glandular structures. Developing berries are small, fleshy, and typically transition from green to a darker hue as they mature.
