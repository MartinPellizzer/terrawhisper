Ribes diacantha

What's the taxonomical classification of Ribes diacantha?

Ribes diacantha belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Saxifragales. It is further categorized under the family Grossulariaceae, which defines its specific botanical characteristics. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Ribes, and its specific identity is defined by its species name, diacantha.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Ribes diacantha can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or gastrointestinal distress if ingested or touched without caution. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, many species within the Ribes genus contain compounds that may trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Accidental consumption of unprocessed parts might lead to stomach upset or nausea. It is essential to avoid direct contact with the plant if you have a known sensitivity to similar berry-producing shrubs. Always consult a medical professional if symptoms arise after exposure.
