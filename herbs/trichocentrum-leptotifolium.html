What is Trichocentrum leptotifolium?

Trichocentrum leptotifolium is a species of orchid in the family Orchidaceae.

It is an epiphytic herb with a short stem and 2-4 linear, lanceolate leaves, 15-35 cm long and 0.5-1.5 cm wide, that are mostly unifacial, with a keeled midrib and entire margins. The inflorescence is a dense, cylindrical spike, 5-20 cm long, bearing 10-20 small, fleshy flowers, each 1.5-2 cm long, with sepals and petals that are white or yellowish-green with a reddish-brown keel, and a labellum that is white with yellow or reddish-brown markings. The flowers are slightly fragrant and open in the evening.

Trichocentrum leptotifolium is native to tropical forests in Central and South America.
