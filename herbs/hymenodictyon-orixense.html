What's the taxonomical classification of Hymenodictyon orixense?

Hymenodictyon orixense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds hymexelsin, loganin, scopeletin, scocoleptin 7-O-beta-D-xylopyranosyl-(1 to 6)-beta-D-glucopyranoside, and scopolin. These specific phytochemicals contribute to the diverse pharmacological profile of the species. Loganin and hymexelsin are known to play roles in various biological signaling pathways. The presence of scopeletin and its glycosylated derivative, scocoleptin 7-O-beta-D-xylopyranosyl-(1 to 6)-beta-D-glucopyranoside, often provides antioxidant or anti-inflammatory properties. Additionally, the compound scopolin serves as another significant chemical constituent within the plant's matrix.

As discussed in a study, the methanol extract of Hymenodictyon orixense bark provided five medicinal compounds. These include one iridoid glycoside identified as loganin (1) and four coumarins consisting of scopoletin (2), scopolin (3), hymexelsin (4), and scopoletin 7-O-β-D-xylopyranosyl-(1→6)-β-D-glucopyranoside (5). Compounds 1-5 demonstrated acetylcholinesterase (AChE) inhibitory activity within the range of 13.92-34.18% when used at a concentration of 100 μg/mL. Furthermore, compounds 1 and 5 are reported for the first time from this genus.
