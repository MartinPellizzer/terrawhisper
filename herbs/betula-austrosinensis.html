Betula austrosinensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Betula austrosinensis has an erect growth habit characterized by slender, grayish-white bark and ovate-shaped leaves with serrated margins. The foliage typically exhibits fine venation and a pointed apex, reflecting its adaptation to temperate montane environments. Its branches are relatively delicate and often display a slightly pendulous quality as they mature. The reproductive structures consist of small, catkin-like inflorescences that emerge from the woody stems. These morphological features collectively define the species' distinct identity within the birch family.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the high-altitude regions of the eastern Himalayas, specifically within the mountain ranges of central and western China. It is primarily found growing in temperate montane forests at elevations ranging from 2,500 to 4,000 meters. These populations are often restricted to rocky slopes and forest edges where moisture levels remain consistent. The species occupies specific ecological niches that require cool climates and well-drained soils. Consequently, its natural range remains highly fragmented across distinct mountain peaks.
