Fraxinus malacophylla

What's the taxonomical classification of Fraxinus malacophylla?

Fraxinus malacophylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Lamiales and is a member of the family Oleaceae. Specifically, it is identified under the genus Fraxinus, with its unique classification completed at the species level as malacophylla.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Fraxinus malacophylla has pinnately compound leaves consisting of 7 to 13 lanceolate leaflets with entire or slightly serrated margins and a soft, thin texture. The leaflets are arranged oppositely along a central rachis that typically exhibits a light green hue. The tree develops woody stems and branches that support a spreading canopy structure. Its reproductive structures include small flowers that eventually develop into winged samaras for wind dispersal. The bark matures into a textured surface that provides protection for the inner vascular tissues.
