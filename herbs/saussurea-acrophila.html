Saussurea acrophila

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea acrophila has a rosette-forming growth habit characterized by dense, basal leaves that are lanceolate to oblong in shape with entire or slightly dentate margins. The plant produces a tall, erect flowering scape that emerges from the center of the leafy rosette. Its inflorescence consists of a compact, many-flowered capitulum typically surrounded by specialized, colorful involucral bracts. The individual florets are tubular and adapted for high-altitude pollination environments. These morphological features allow the species to thrive in the harsh, rocky terrains of its alpine habitat.
