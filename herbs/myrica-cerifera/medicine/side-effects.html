10 Possible Side Effects Of Myrica Cerifera

Myrica cerifera has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal irritation, allergic reactions, and interactions with certain medications.

These side effects can be caused by excessive consumption or allergic sensitivities. For instance, gastrointestinal irritation may lead to stomach pain, nausea, and diarrhea, making everyday activities like eating and socializing uncomfortable.

In severe cases, allergic reactions can cause anaphylaxis, a life-threatening condition.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Myrica cerifera causes allergic reactions due to the presence of certain compounds such as terpenes and flavonoids in its bark, leaves, and berries.

These chemicals can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms like hives, itching, redness, and swelling. Additionally, some people may experience respiratory issues like congestion or coughing when exposed to the plant's pollen or resin.

As a result, individuals with known allergies or sensitivities should exercise caution when handling or using products derived from Myrica cerifera.

2. Triggers asthma attacks

Myrica cerifera triggers asthma attacks due to its potential allergenic properties and the presence of volatile organic compounds (VOCs) that can exacerbate respiratory issues.

The berry's sap, bark, and leaves contain terpenes and polyphenolic compounds that may stimulate an immune response in individuals with asthma, leading to bronchial constriction and increased mucus production, making it difficult for airways to function properly.

3. Induces skin rashes

Myrica cerifera induces skin rashes due to its potential allergenic properties.

The essential oils present in this plant, particularly the sesquiterpenes and triterpenoids, can cause an immune response that leads to allergic reactions on the skin, resulting in the formation of rashes.

This sensitivity is thought to be triggered by the plant's unique chemical composition, which can vary depending on factors such as soil quality, climate, and harvesting methods.

4. Causes stomach cramps

Myrica cerifera causes stomach cramps due to its high concentration of tannins and saponins.

These compounds can irritate the digestive tract, leading to spasms and contractions in the abdominal muscles.

Additionally, bayberry's bitter taste may stimulate the production of stomach acid, further exacerbating discomfort and pain.

5. Produces dizziness spells

Myrica cerifera produces dizziness spells due to its ability to interact with certain medications and alter blood pressure.

The plant's volatile oils can stimulate the nervous system, leading to lightheadedness and dizziness, especially when taken in large doses or combined with other herbs or supplements that have similar effects.

This side effect is more likely to occur in individuals who are sensitive to its compounds or have pre-existing conditions such as hypertension.

6. Leads to headache pain

Myrica cerifera leads to headache pain due to its high concentration of limonoids and saponins.

These compounds can cause increased blood flow and stimulate the release of neurotransmitters in the brain, leading to discomfort and pain.

Additionally, the flavonoids present in Myrica cerifera may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners, which can further contribute to headache development.

7. Causes nausea episodes

Myrica cerifera causes nausea episodes due to its ability to stimulate the digestive system and increase stomach acid production.

This can lead to feelings of queasiness and discomfort in some individuals.

Additionally, the plant's anthraquinone glycosides may also contribute to this side effect by irritating the mucous membranes lining the stomach and intestines, further exacerbating nausea episodes.

8. Increases blood pressure

Myrica cerifera increases blood pressure due to its high concentration of flavonoids and terpenes.

These compounds can stimulate the sympathetic nervous system, leading to increased heart rate and blood vessel constriction, which elevates blood pressure.

Additionally, myricetin, a key flavonoid in Myrica cerifera, can also enhance vasoconstriction and increase peripheral resistance, further contributing to hypertension.

9. Suppresses appetite sharply

Myrica cerifera suppresses appetite sharply due to its ability to slow down gastric emptying and reduce hunger hormones in the body.

The berry's extract has been shown to inhibit the release of ghrelin, a hormone that stimulates appetite, while increasing the production of peptide YY, which helps regulate satiety.

As a result, individuals taking Myrica cerifera may experience a significant reduction in their desire for food.

10. Creates anxiety attacks

Myrica cerifera creates anxiety attacks in some individuals due to its high concentration of alkaloids and other bioactive compounds.

These compounds can affect the body's nervous system, leading to feelings of apprehension, tension, and panic.

The exact mechanism is not fully understood, but it is believed that the interaction between these compounds and neurotransmitters such as serotonin and GABA can disrupt normal brain function and lead to anxiety symptoms.
