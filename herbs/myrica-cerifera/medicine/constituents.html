10 Medicinal Constituents Of Myrica Cerifera (Bayberry)

Myrica cerifera has active constituents such as myricetin and salicylic acid.

These constituents have anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, making bayberry effective in treating various health issues. It can help alleviate digestive issues, reduce fever, and soothe skin irritations.

Additionally, the constituents in bayberry may also contribute to improved cardiovascular health by reducing blood pressure and cholesterol levels, ultimately enhancing overall well-being.

This article explains in details the 10 best active constituents of Myrica cerifera.

1. Tannins

Myrica cerifera tannins is a type of polyphenolic compound found in its bark and leaves.

These tannins are responsible for the plant's astringent and bitter taste, which makes it unpalatable to humans and animals.

The tannins in Myrica cerifera have been shown to possess antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, making them of interest for potential therapeutic applications.

2. Gallic acid

Myrica cerifera gallic acid is a potent phenolic compound extracted from its leaves and stems.

This naturally occurring antioxidant has been widely studied for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antiviral properties. Gallic acid has also been shown to possess anti-cancer properties, inhibit oxidative stress, and exhibit antimicrobial activities against various microorganisms.

Its unique composition makes it a valuable component in traditional medicine and pharmaceutical applications.

3. Ellagic acid

Myrica cerifera ellagic acid is a potent polyphenolic compound extracted from its fruit.

This antioxidant-rich extract has been studied for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and anticancer properties. Ellagic acid has also been shown to exhibit antimicrobial and antiviral activities, making it a promising natural ingredient for food, cosmetics, and pharmaceutical applications.

Its unique combination of bioactive compounds makes Myrica cerifera a valuable species in traditional medicine and modern research.

4. Quercetin

Myrica cerifera quercetin is a bioactive flavonoid compound isolated from the plant's leaves and bark.

It exhibits numerous biological activities, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. Quercetin has been shown to inhibit enzymes involved in allergic reactions and may help reduce symptoms of asthma and other respiratory diseases.

Additionally, its potential anti-cancer effects have also been studied, suggesting that it may play a role in the prevention and treatment of various types of cancer.

5. Kaempferol

Myrica cerifera kaempferol is a flavonoid compound found in its berries.

It has been extensively studied for its potential health benefits and pharmacological activities. Kaempferol has been reported to possess antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, making it a promising natural remedy for various diseases and conditions.

Its bioactivity has also been linked to improved cardiovascular health and reduced risk of certain cancers.

6. Isoorientin

Myrica cerifera isoorientin is a bioactive flavonoid compound isolated from the fruit of this plant.

It has been found to possess various pharmacological activities, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. Isoorientin has been reported to exhibit potent inhibitory effects on platelet aggregation, making it a potential agent for the prevention and treatment of cardiovascular diseases.

Additionally, its antioxidant activity may contribute to its protective effects against oxidative stress and related disorders.

7. Orientalin

Myrica cerifera orientalin is a subspecies of wax myrtle that is native to Asia.

It is an evergreen shrub or small tree that is characterized by its glossy leaves and aromatic fruit.

The fruit of M. cerifera orientalin contains a waxy coating that has been used for centuries in traditional medicine and as a natural remedy for skin conditions.

8. Isorhapontigenin

Myrica cerifera isorhapontigenin is a bioactive compound isolated from the seeds of this plant.

It has been found to possess various pharmacological activities, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. Studies have shown that isorhapontigenin can inhibit the growth of certain cancer cells and protect against oxidative damage in vivo.

Its potential therapeutic applications are being explored, particularly in the treatment of inflammatory diseases and cancer.

9. Betulinic acid

Myrica cerifera betulinic acid is a triterpenoid compound extracted from its fruit.

It has been traditionally used in medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Betulinic acid has been shown to exhibit significant antioxidant activity, inhibiting the growth of cancer cells and inducing apoptosis (programmed cell death).

Its potential therapeutic applications are being explored for various diseases, including cancer, cardiovascular disorders, and skin infections.

10. Oleanolic acid

Myrica cerifera oleanolic acid is a triterpenoid saponin isolated from the bark of the plant.

It has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine to treat various diseases such as inflammation, fever, and cancer. Oleanolic acid has been shown to possess anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and anticancer properties, making it a potential candidate for the prevention and treatment of various diseases.

Its mechanisms of action include inhibiting pro-inflammatory enzymes and inducing apoptosis in cancer cells.
