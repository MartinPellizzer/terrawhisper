Maranthes glabra

What's the taxonomical classification of Maranthes glabra?

Maranthes glabra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is positioned under the order Malpighiales. It is further categorized into the family Chrysobalanaceae and is specifically identified within the genus Maranthes, with its unique designation being the species glabra.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Maranthes glabra contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, and tannins that provide its therapeutic potential. These chemical constituents are believed to contribute to the plant's antimicrobial and antioxidant properties. Researchers often study these compounds to understand how they can combat oxidative stress and infectious pathogens. The specific profile of these phytochemicals varies depending on the plant part used and the extraction method applied. Understanding these medicinal components is essential for developing future pharmacological applications.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including skin infections, digestive issues, and inflammatory conditions. The leaves and roots are frequently applied topically to soothe skin irritations and heal wounds. Some traditional practices involve using the plant to alleviate gastrointestinal discomfort and stomach aches. It is also believed to possess properties that help manage systemic inflammation within the body. These medicinal applications are rooted in long-standing ethnobotanical knowledge of the species.
