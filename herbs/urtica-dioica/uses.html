Stinging Nettle (Urtica dioica) Uses

Stinging Nettle is used to treat psoriasis, eczema, and skin infections due to its anti-inflammatory properties. It helps alleviate urticaria and skin irritation, supporting wound healing and reducing symptoms of skin rashes and ulcers. It is also used for hay fever and bronchitis to ease allergic reactions and respiratory inflammation.

Stinging Nettle (Urtica dioica) Monograph

1. Psoriasis

Stinging Nettle has been traditionally used to support skin health, including in the management of psoriasis. Its anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties may help reduce redness, itching, and plaque formation. Topical applications of nettle extracts or infusions can soothe irritated skin and promote healing.

2. Eczema

Stinging Nettle has been traditionally used to treat eczema due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties. Topical applications of nettle leaves or extracts can soothe irritated skin and reduce redness. It is often used in herbal formulations to support skin health and alleviate symptoms associated with eczema.

3. Skin infections

Stinging Nettle has been used traditionally to treat skin infections due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The plant contains compounds that help reduce bacterial growth and soothe irritated skin. Topical applications of nettle extracts or poultices can aid in healing minor infections and reducing redness. It is often used in herbal medicine for its natural disinfectant qualities.

4. Urticaria

Stinging Nettle has been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of urticaria, or hives. Its anti-inflammatory properties help reduce redness, swelling, and itching. External applications of nettle-based remedies can soothe skin irritation, while internal use may support systemic inflammation reduction.

5. Skin irritation

Stinging Nettle is used topically to soothe skin irritation. Its anti-inflammatory properties help reduce redness and swelling. It can be applied as a poultice or infusion. It is particularly effective for eczema, psoriasis, and insect bites.

6. Wound healing

Stinging Nettle has been traditionally used to support wound healing due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It helps reduce swelling, prevent infection, and promote tissue repair. Topical applications of prepared nettle can accelerate healing and soothe irritated skin. Its rich content of minerals and antioxidants further enhances its healing potential.

7. Skin rash

Stinging nettle can be used topically to alleviate skin rash symptoms. Its anti-inflammatory properties help reduce redness and irritation. A nettle leaf poultice or infusion can soothe the skin and promote healing. It is particularly effective for eczema and hives when applied externally.

8. Skin ulcers

Stinging Nettle has been traditionally used to treat skin ulcers due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It helps reduce inflammation, promote tissue repair, and prevent infection. Topical applications of nettle infusions or poultices can soothe ulcers and accelerate healing. Its rich nutrient profile also supports skin regeneration.

9. Hay fever

Stinging Nettle has been traditionally used to alleviate hay fever symptoms due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion, sneezing, and eye irritation by supporting the immune system and regulating allergic responses. Herbal preparations often include dried leaves or tinctures, taken regularly during pollen seasons for best results.

10. Bronchitis

Stinging Nettle has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, including bronchitis. Its anti-inflammatory properties may help reduce bronchial swelling and ease coughing. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to soothe irritation and support mucus clearance. Regular use may help manage symptoms and promote healing in chronic respiratory conditions.
