Stinging Nettle (Urtica dioica) Compounds

Stinging Nettle contains several primary compounds, including flavonoids, which act as antioxidants. Tannins contribute to its astringent properties. Phenolic acids provide anti-inflammatory effects. Coumarin has anticoagulant properties, while saponins support immune function. Glycosides, terpenoids, essential oils, anthocyanins, and alkaloids also contribute to its therapeutic profile.

Stinging Nettle (Urtica dioica) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Stinging Nettle (Urtica dioica) are plant compounds known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. They contribute to the plant's therapeutic potential by supporting immune function and reducing oxidative stress. These compounds are found in various parts of the plant, including leaves and roots, and are often extracted for use in herbal remedies.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Stinging Nettle (Urtica dioica). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and has anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Tannins in Stinging Nettle support digestive health and may help reduce symptoms of urinary tract infections. They also play a role in the plant's traditional use for skin conditions and wound healing.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Stinging Nettle (Urtica dioica), contributing to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. It helps reduce inflammation and supports immune function, making it valuable in herbal medicine for conditions like arthritis and skin irritation.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a compound found in Stinging Nettle (Urtica dioica). It contributes to the plant's mild sedative and antispasmodic effects. Coumarin may also support urinary tract health and help reduce inflammation. Its presence enhances the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

5. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Stinging Nettle (Urtica dioica). It contributes to the plant's natural defense mechanisms and has mild detergent properties. Saponin may support immune function and has been studied for its potential anti-inflammatory effects. It is often used in herbal preparations for its overall tonic and supportive properties.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Stinging Nettle (Urtica dioica). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by supporting anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects. Glycosides in Stinging Nettle are often associated with its traditional use in treating conditions like arthritis and muscle pain.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Stinging Nettle (Urtica dioica) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. These compounds help reduce swelling and pain, supporting the plant's traditional use in treating arthritis and muscle inflammation. They also exhibit antioxidant activity, enhancing the plant's overall therapeutic potential.

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of Stinging Nettle (Urtica dioica) contains volatile compounds like methyl salicylate and limonene. These contribute to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. It is used topically for muscle pain and skin irritation. The oil is extracted through steam distillation from fresh or dried plant material.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a pigment found in Stinging Nettle (Urtica dioica) that contributes to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. It supports the plant's traditional use in reducing swelling and promoting urinary health. Anthocyanin also enhances the plant's overall therapeutic profile in herbal medicine.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloids in Stinging Nettle (Urtica dioica) are a class of nitrogen-containing compounds with potential therapeutic effects. While not the primary active constituents, certain alkaloids may contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, though research on their specific roles in herbal medicine remains limited.
