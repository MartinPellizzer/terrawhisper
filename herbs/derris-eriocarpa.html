Derris eriocarpa

What's the taxonomical classification of Derris eriocarpa?

Derris eriocarpa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic lineage of the order Fabales and is a member of the family Fabaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Derris, and its unique species designation, eriocarpa.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Derris eriocarpa contains bioactive rotenoids, most notably rotenone, which functions as a potent natural pesticide and piscicide. These specific compounds act by inhibiting the mitochondrial electron transport chain in target organisms. Due to this mechanism, the plant's extracts are traditionally used to treat parasitic infections and certain skin ailments. Research suggests that these chemical constituents also exhibit significant insecticidal properties against various agricultural pests. The presence of these specialized secondary metabolites makes the species a subject of interest for pharmacological development.

What are the therapeutic actions of Derris eriocarpa?

Derris eriocarpa has potent insecticidal properties derived from its high concentration of rotenone, which acts as a natural mitochondrial inhibitor to paralyze and kill various pests. This bioactive compound works by interfering with the electron transport chain in the cells of insects, leading to a rapid loss of energy and death. Because it disrupts cellular respiration, it is frequently utilized in organic gardening and traditional pest control methods to manage infestations. The plant's ability to target specific metabolic pathways makes it a significant subject in studies regarding botanical pesticides. Researchers continue to investigate its chemical structure to develop safer, more effective biological control agents for agriculture.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various infestations of ectoparasites including lice, fleas, ticks, and other skin-dwelling insects. The active chemical compound rotenone acts as a potent neurotoxin that disrupts the nervous systems of these pests upon contact. Historically, extracts from the roots have been applied to skin or clothing to eliminate parasitic burdens. It is also utilized in agricultural settings to control various insect species that damage crops. Because of its high toxicity, its application is carefully controlled to prevent harm to non-target organisms.
