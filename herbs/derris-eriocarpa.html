Derris eriocarpa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Derris eriocarpa has a woody climbing habit characterized by pinnate leaves with several pairs of leaflets and small, densely hairy flowers. The leaves are typically composed of an odd number of leaflets that exhibit a soft, pubescent texture on both surfaces. Its stems act as twining vines that allow the plant to scramble through forest undergrowth to reach sunlight. The inflorescence consists of small, clustered blossoms that are often white or pale in color. These flowers are distinguished by their fine coating of hairs, which is a key identifying feature of the species.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Derris eriocarpa contains bioactive rotenoids, most notably rotenone, which functions as a potent natural pesticide and piscicide. These specific compounds act by inhibiting the mitochondrial electron transport chain in target organisms. Due to this mechanism, the plant's extracts are traditionally used to treat parasitic infections and certain skin ailments. Research suggests that these chemical constituents also exhibit significant insecticidal properties against various agricultural pests. The presence of these specialized secondary metabolites makes the species a subject of interest for pharmacological development.

Derris eriocarpa is prepared primarily as a liquid extract or a concentrated decoction derived from its roots to utilize its insecticidal properties. What side-effects this plant can have?

Derris eriocarpa can cause severe skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and potential neurological toxicity if absorbed in significant quantities through the skin or ingested. The plant contains rotenone, a potent natural insecticide that can interfere with cellular respiration in humans. Exposure to its concentrated extracts may lead to symptoms such as dizziness, nausea, or headaches. Prolonged contact with the crushed roots can result in localized redness, itching, or blistering on the skin. Because of these toxic properties, careful handling is necessary to prevent accidental poisoning or systemic effects.
