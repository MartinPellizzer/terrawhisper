Buxus sinica

Buxus sinica, commonly known as Chinese boxwood or variegated boxwood, is a type of evergreen shrub native to China and Japan.

It has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for its health benefits, which include reducing inflammation, improving digestion, and alleviating symptoms of bronchitis and other respiratory issues.

The plant's therapeutic actions are attributed to its ability to calm the mind, reduce stress, and promote a good night's sleep.

The bioactive constituents of Buxus sinica include alkaloids, flavonoids, and terpenoids, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal properties.
