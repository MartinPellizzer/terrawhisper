What's the taxonomical classification of Calathodes polycarpa?

Calathodes polycarpa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Ranunculales. As a member of the family Ranunculaceae, this plant is further categorized into the genus Calathodes, with the specific identifier of the species polycarpa.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Calathea polycarpa can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or gastrointestinal distress if the plant is ingested or touched without protection. While it is primarily an ornamental species, the presence of certain alkaloids may trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Symptoms of skin contact typically include redness, itching, or a mild stinging sensation. If swallowed, the plant might cause nausea, vomiting, or abdominal discomfort. It is best to wear gloves when handling the foliage and to keep the plant away from pets and small children.
