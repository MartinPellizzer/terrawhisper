Purple Foxglove

Digitalis purpurea

Purple Foxglove (Digitalis purpurea) is a flowering plant native to Europe, known for its bell-shaped purple flowers and its historical use in treating heart conditions.

Taxonomy

Purple Foxglove, or Digitalis purpurea, is a flowering plant belonging to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Liliopsida, order Plantaginales, family Plantaginaceae, genus Digitalis, and species Digitalis purpurea.

Morphology

Purple Foxglove (Digitalis purpurea) is a biennial plant characterized by its tall, erect stem, sparsely hairy leaves that are broadly ovate to heart-shaped, and striking violet to purple spurred flowers that bloom in terminal racemes.

Distribution

Purple Foxglove (Digitalis purpurea) is native to southern and eastern Europe, western Asia, and parts of North Africa, with its natural range extending into the British Isles and northern regions of the European continent.

Traditional Uses

Purple Foxglove, or Digitalis purpurea, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its cardiac tonifying properties, particularly in treating heart conditions such as arrhythmias and congestive heart failure, though it is important to note that its use requires careful preparation and dosage due to its potent and potentially toxic nature.

Medicinal Systems

In traditional medicinal systems such as Traditional Chinese Medicine, Ayurveda, and European folk medicine, Purple Foxglove has been historically utilized for its purported cardiovascular and therapeutic properties.

Phytochemistry

Purple Foxglove (Digitalis purpurea) contains cardiac glycosides such as digitoxin and digoxin, which are responsible for its medicinal effects and are primarily found in the dried leaves and flowers.

Pharmacology

Purple Foxglove (Digitalis purpurea) exhibits pharmacological properties characterized by positive inotropic effects, which enhance cardiac contractility, and chronotropic effects that regulate heart rate, making it valuable in treating cardiovascular conditions.

Botanical Identification

Digitalis purpurea, commonly known as Purple Foxglove, is a biennial plant native to Europe. Belonging to the Plantaginaceae family, it is characterized by its tall spikes of purple flowers and hairy leaves. It is also referred to as Fairy Gloves or Woolly Foxglove in various regions.

Infusion: Used for treating heart conditions and hypertension due to its cardiotonic properties.

Safety Profile

Purple Foxglove, or Digitalis purpurea, contains cardiac glycosides that are highly toxic even in small amounts. It should never be used during pregnancy or by individuals with heart block, kidney failure, or severe hypertension. Always consult a qualified herbalist or healthcare provider before use.
