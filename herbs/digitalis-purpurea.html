What to know about Digitalis purpurea before using it medicinally

Digitalis purpurea, commonly known as foxglove, is a herb that has been used to treat various health conditions, including heart failure, atrial fibrillation, and certain cases of irregular heartbeat.

The main medicinal uses of Digitalis purpurea are attributed to its ability to increase the force of heart contractions and slow the heart rate, thereby improving cardiac output and reducing symptoms of heart failure. The health benefits of Digitalis purpurea include improved cardiovascular function, increased urine production, and reduced swelling in the body. The main therapeutic properties of Digitalis purpurea include cardiotonic, diuretic, and vasodilatory effects, which contribute to its ability to improve heart function and alleviate symptoms of heart failure.

The bioactive compounds responsible for the medicinal properties of Digitalis purpurea include glycosides, specifically digoxin and digitoxin, which are extracted from the leaves and used to prepare digitalis preparations, commonly in the form of tinctures or tablets.
