10 Possible Side Effects Of Paeonia Suffruticosa

Paeonia suffruticosa has some side effects when used improperly, such as allergic reactions, digestive issues, and skin problems.

These issues can arise due to excessive consumption, poor quality, or adulteration of the herb. For instance, if left untreated, skin problems can lead to scarring, infection, or even chronic conditions like eczema.

Similarly, digestive issues can cause discomfort, nausea, or diarrhea, affecting daily activities and quality of life.

1. Causes dizziness

Paeonia suffruticosa causes dizziness due to its ability to interact with certain neurotransmitters in the brain.

The herb contains alkaloids and glycosides that can affect the body's serotonin levels, leading to feelings of lightheadedness or dizziness.

Additionally, Paeonia suffruticosa may also cause a decrease in blood pressure, which can contribute to its dizziness-inducing effects on the body.

2. Produces stomach upset

Paeonia suffruticosa produces stomach upset due to its ability to inhibit gastrointestinal motility and delay gastric emptying.

This can lead to nausea, vomiting, and abdominal discomfort in some individuals.

The saponins present in the plant may also cause irritation of the mucous membranes lining the stomach, exacerbating digestive issues and leading to stomach upset.

3. Increases blood pressure

Paeonia suffruticosa increases blood pressure due to its high content of flavonoids and saponins.

These bioactive compounds can stimulate the body's sympathetic nervous system, leading to increased cardiac output and peripheral vascular resistance, resulting in elevated blood pressure.

Additionally, Paeonia suffruticosa has been shown to inhibit the activity of enzymes involved in blood vessel relaxation, further contributing to the rise in blood pressure.

4. Triggers allergic reactions

Paeonia suffruticosa triggers allergic reactions.

This is due to the plant's high concentration of allergenic compounds such as sesquiterpenes and flavonoids, which can cause skin irritation, itchiness, and hives in some individuals.

Additionally, the sap and milky latex of the plant contain histamine-releasing agents that can lead to allergic responses, including anaphylaxis in severe cases.

5. Interferes with digestion

Paeonia suffruticosa interferes with digestion due to its saponin and anthraquinone compounds, which can slow down the movement of food through the digestive system.

This may lead to symptoms such as bloating, gas, and stomach discomfort.

Additionally, the flavonoids present in Paeonia suffruticosa can also alter the gut microbiome, further disrupting the normal digestion process.

6. Affects menstrual cycles

Paeonia suffruticosa affects menstrual cycles by influencing the body's hormonal balance.

The plant's active compounds, including flavonoids and saponins, may interact with estrogen receptors, altering the normal functioning of the reproductive system. This can lead to changes in menstruation patterns, such as irregular or heavy bleeding, or even amenorrhea.

As a result, women who use Paeonia suffruticosa may experience disruptions to their menstrual cycles.

7. Induces headache

Paeonia suffruticosa induces headache due to its high concentration of coumarins, a class of compounds that can cause vasodilation and increased blood flow.

This can lead to a range of unpleasant symptoms including throbbing or pounding headaches, which may be exacerbated by the plant's ability to stimulate the release of histamine in the body.

The exact mechanisms behind this side effect are not fully understood, but it is believed to be related to the plant's interactions with the nervous system and its effects on blood vessel tone.

8. Causes nausea and vomiting

Paeonia suffruticosa causes nausea and vomiting due to its potential interaction with certain medications, such as antidepressants and antihistamines, which can affect the stomach's ability to digest food properly.

Additionally, the plant's compounds may irritate the gastrointestinal tract, leading to nausea and vomiting in some individuals.

The exact mechanism behind this side effect is still unclear, but it is thought to be related to the plant's saponins and flavonoids, which have been shown to have a negative impact on gut health.

10. Raises blood sugar levels

Paeonia suffruticosa raises blood sugar levels.

This is attributed to its active compounds, including paeoniflorin and paeonol, which can stimulate the release of glucose from stored energy sources. Additionally, Paeonia suffruticosa may enhance the activity of enzymes involved in glucose metabolism, leading to increased glucose production and elevated blood sugar levels.

The exact mechanism is still not fully understood, but it is believed that these compounds interact with insulin receptors and affect glucose uptake by cells.
