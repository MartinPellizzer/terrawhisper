Alkanna (Alkanna tinctoria)

Alkanna (Alkanna tinctoria) is a member of the Fabaceae family, native to Middle East, Central Asia, and Southwest Asia. Traditionally, its roots, rhizomes, and stems have been used for decoctions, powders, and infusions.

This herb is particularly valued for its astringent, anti-inflammatory, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, african traditional medicine, and ayurvedic medicine.
