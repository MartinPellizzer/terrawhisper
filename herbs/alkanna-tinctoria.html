Alkanna Tinctoria Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Alkanna tinctoria, commonly known as Indian lac or red sanders, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent, valued for its deep red to orange-colored resinous gum.

This herb is known for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which may support digestive and skin health.

The bioactive constituents responsible for its therapeutic actions include tannins, flavonoids, and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

Herbal preparations such as infusions, decoctions, and topical applications can be made from Alkanna tinctoria to harness its traditional healing properties.
