Basella alba

What's the taxonomical classification of Basella alba?

Basella alba belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is situated in the order Caryophyllales and is a member of the family Basellaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by the genus Basella and the species alba.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Basella alba has a woody, perennial stem structure characterized by small, opposite leaves and inconspicuous white flowers. The foliage consists of elliptical or lanceolate leaflets that are typically arranged in an alternating pattern along the branches. These leaves feature smooth margins and a slightly waxy texture on the upper surface. The reproductive organs are small and clustered, often appearing in axillary positions along the stem. The overall growth habit is compact, allowing the plant to maintain a dense and shrub-like appearance.

Its presence is often tied to specific soil compositions found within these dense equatorial forest zones. What are the pharmacological activities of Basella alba?

This plant has potent pharmacological activities including anti-inflammatory, anticancer, antimicrobial, antioxidant, and antiproliferative properties. These biological actions are driven by a diverse range of bioactive secondary metabolites found within its tissues. The antioxidant capacity helps neutralize harmful free radicals, while the antimicrobial effects provide defense against various pathogens. Furthermore, its anticancer and antiproliferative qualities suggest a significant potential for inhibiting the growth and spread of malignant cells. These combined therapeutic attributes make the species a subject of interest in modern medicinal research.

As discussed in a study, Basella alba produces dark-colored fruits rich in betacyanins, including rare 6-glycosylated derivatives called gomphrenins. Both the crude extract and the enriched gomphrenin fraction exhibited significant anti-inflamatory and antioxidant effects in vitro. These substances inhibited pro-inflamatory cytokines IL-1β and IL-6, while also reducing oxidative stress markers such as ROS and NO. Additionally, both fractions decreased lipid peroxidation and inhibited NF-κB and COX activity. The enriched gomphrenin fraction showed stronger antioxidant and anti-inflamatory effects, particularly in the reduction of ROS and NO levels.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse range of bioactive flavonoid compounds, specifically including apigenin, gomphreniins, luteolin, and naringin. These specific chemical constituents contribute to the medicinal profile by providing various antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The presence of these flavonoids is essential for the plant's biological activity and therapeutic potential. Researchers often study these specific molecules to understand how they interact with human cellular pathways. Together, these compounds form a complex chemical fingerprint that characterizes the plant's medicinal value.

As discussed in a study, Basella alba leaf extract showed the highest inhibitory effect at about 74% during the screening of 25 medicinal plant methanol extracts. Analysis using gas chromatography with tandem mass spectrometry and reversed phase high-performance liquid chromatography revealed several phytochemical components. These identified compounds include phenol 2,6-bis(1,1-dimethylethyl), 1-heptatriacoetanol, oleic acid, eicosyl ester, naringin, apiigenin, luteolin, ascorbic acid, and α-tocopherol. These specific components have been reported to possess antihypercholesterolemic effects.

The plant also contains potent antioxidants that mitigate the harmful effects of oxidative stress within the body. What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, stomach cramps, allergic reactions, and dizziness in individuals who consume it. Digestive distress is a common manifestation of toxicity, often presenting as severe abdominal pain or liquid stools. Some people may also experience systemic issues such as lightheadedness or a spinning sensation known as dizziness. Furthermore, sensitive individuals might develop skin rashes or respiratory issues due to allergic reactions. It is essential to avoid ingestion to prevent these various adverse physical responses.
