Saussurea ussuriensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Saussurea ussuriensis?

Saussurea ussuriensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Asterales. It is further categorized under the family Asteraceae, which places it among the diverse group of composite flowers. Within this family, the plant is situated in the genus Saussurea, and its specific classification is completed by the species name ussuriensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea ussuriensis has perennial herbaceous stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 50 centimeters and feature leaves that are lanceolate to oblong in shape with serrated margins. The plant develops basal rosettes of leaves that are often densely hairy to protect against harsh alpine environments. Its inflorescence consists of a compact, head-like capitulum composed of numerous small, yellowish-white florets. Each flower head is surrounded by several layers of overlapping involucral bracts that provide structural support and protection. The overall morphology is adapted specifically for survival in high-altitude rocky terrains.
