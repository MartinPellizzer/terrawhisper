Polypogon fugax

What's the taxonomical classification of Polypogon fugax?

Polypogon fugax belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Poales, it is placed in the family Poaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Polypogon, with its specific identification being the species fugax.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Polypogon fugax contains no documented medicinal compounds or therapeutic properties within established pharmacological literature. This grass species is primarily recognized as a component of natural ecosystems rather than a source of bioactive substances. Scientific studies typically focus on its ecological role and its ability to colonize disturbed soils. Because it lacks chemical analysis for pharmaceutical use, it is not currently utilized in traditional or modern medicine. Researchers have not identified specific alkaloids, flavonoids, or glycosides in this plant that offer healing benefits.
