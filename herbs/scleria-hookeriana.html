Scleria hookeriana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Scleria hookerianana has an erect, tufted growth habit characterized by slender, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters. The leaves are typically basal, linear, and possess a somewhat rigid or coriaceous texture. Its inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous spikelets arranged in clusters along the upper portion of the stem. The seeds are small and hard, reflecting the characteristic toughness associated with many members of the Cyperaceae family. These structural features allow the plant to thrive in various grassy or wetland habitats.
