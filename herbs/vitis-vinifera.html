What to know about Vitis vinifera before using it medicinally

Vitis vinifera, commonly known as grapevine, is a plant with a rich history of medicinal use in traditional medicine.

The main medicinal uses of this herb include its ability to help alleviate symptoms associated with anxiety disorders, insomnia, and certain types of cancer. The health benefits of Vitis vinifera include its potential to act as an anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial agent, which may contribute to its ability to reduce inflammation, protect against cell damage, and combat infections. The therapeutic properties of Vitis vinifera are attributed to its flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which possess anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and anti-cancer properties.

The bioactive compounds present in Vitis vinifera include resveratrol, quercetin, and anthocyanins, which are commonly prepared into wine, grape extracts, and grape seed oil.

According to a study published by "Biomedicine & pharmacotherapy = Biomedecine & pharmacotherapie", the hydroalcoholic leaf extract of Vitis vinifera (EVV) was found to possess significant anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antipyretic effects. The extract exhibited dose-dependent inhibition of carrageenan-induced paw edema and acetic acid-induced vascular permeability in mice, indicating its potential in reducing inflammation and pain. Additionally, EVV showed marked dose-dependent analgesic activity in the formalin test and caused a significant reduction in rectal temperatures, suggesting its effectiveness in treating various inflammatory and pain diseases.

Improper use of Vitis vinifera can lead to gastrointestinal distress, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, as well as experiencing increased bleeding due to its anticoagulant properties. Furthermore, it can interact with medications, such as blood thinners, and lead to kidney damage, nausea and vomiting, develop liver damage and even experience hypotension.
