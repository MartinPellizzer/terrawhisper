Common grape

Vitis vinifera

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family

Botanical Identification

Vitis vinifera is the scientific name for the Common Grape, belonging to the Vitaceae family. It is a perennial vine native to the Mediterranean region. The plant has tendrils, heart-shaped leaves, and clusters of small green flowers. Common names include European grape and table grape, while synonyms in herbal literature include Vitis labrusca and Vitis riparia.

Active Compounds

The Common Grape contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and anthocyanins. These compounds work synergistically to provide antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. Their combined action supports the herb's traditional use in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Phenolic acid
  • Anthocyanin

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for digestive issues and as a mild sedative.

Decoction: Used for respiratory conditions and as an antioxidant.

Tincture: Used for pain relief and as an antiseptic.

Safety Profile

Common Grape (Vitis vinifera) is generally safe when consumed in typical food amounts. However, it may cause allergic reactions in some individuals. Large quantities or concentrated forms can lead to gastrointestinal discomfort or interact with certain medications.
