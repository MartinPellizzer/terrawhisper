10 Possible Side Effects Of Asparagus Racemosus (Shatavari)

Asparagus racemosus has some side effects when used improperly, such as nausea, diarrhea, and stomach discomfort.

These side effects can occur due to the herb's high saponin content, which can irritate the digestive system. In severe cases, these symptoms can worsen, leading to dehydration, electrolyte imbalances, and even kidney damage.

For individuals with pre-existing conditions, such as gastritis or ulcers, using shatavari can exacerbate their symptoms, affecting their daily activities and overall well-being.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Asparagus racemosus if used imporperly.
