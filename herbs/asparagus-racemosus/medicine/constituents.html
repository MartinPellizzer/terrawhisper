10 Medicinal Constituents Of Asparagus Racemosus (Shatavari)

Asparagus racemosus has active constituents such as saponins, flavonoids, and glycosides.

These compounds exhibit adaptogenic, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties. The saponins help to regulate hormonal imbalances, while flavonoids soothe digestive issues and alleviate stress.

As a result, shatavari is used to enhance female fertility, alleviate menopausal symptoms, and support overall hormonal balance, thereby improving the quality of life for women experiencing menstrual and reproductive issues.

1. Shatavarin iv

Asparagus racemosus, also known as Shatavari, Shatavarin IV is a well-known medicinal herb used in Ayurvedic medicine.

It is believed to possess various bioactive compounds like stachyose, verbascose, and raffinose, which are responsible for its medicinal properties. The plant has been traditionally used to treat various health issues such as menopausal symptoms, stress, anxiety, and digestive problems.

Its extracts have also been found to exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant activities.
