Cratoxylum formosum

What's the taxonomical classification of Cratoxylum formosum?

Cratoxylum formosum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Malpighiales. This plant is further categorized into the family Hypericaceae, specifically within the genus Cratoxylum, and is identified by its unique species name, formosum.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Cratoxylum formosum contains various bioactive alkaloids, triterpenoids, and phenolic compounds that exhibit significant pharmacological properties. The presence of specific alkaloids provides the plant with potent antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory activities. Many of its extracts demonstrate antioxidant potential, which helps neutralize free radicals in biological systems. Research suggests that these chemical constituents may also possess cytotoxic effects useful in studying cancer cell inhibition. These diverse chemical profiles make the plant a subject of interest for developing new therapeutic agents.

What are the therapeutic actions of Cratoxylum formosum?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, stomach pains, and various types of fever. The bark of the species contains bioactive compounds that exhibit antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Indigenous communities often apply crushed bark to open sores to accelerate the healing process. It is also prepared as a decoction to alleviate gastrointestinal distress and internal discomfort. These traditional medicinal practices highlight the plant's role in managing localized and systemic ailments.

What are the herbal preparations of Cratoxylum formosum?

Cratoxylum formosum is prepared as a decoction of the bark or a liquid infusion of the leaves to treat various ailments. Traditional practitioners often boil the woody parts of the plant to extract potent medicinal compounds for internal use. The resulting liquid is frequently consumed to address digestive issues or to alleviate inflammatory conditions. In some regional practices, the plant materials are also processed into topical applications for skin concerns. These various forms allow the therapeutic properties of the species to be administered effectively depending on the specific health need.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Cratoxylum formosum can cause severe skin irritation, localized inflammation, and potential allergic reactions upon direct contact with its sap or tissues. The plant contains chemical compounds that act as irritants to human epithelial cells. Individuals may experience redness, itching, or a burning sensation where the plant has touched the skin. In some cases, prolonged exposure might lead to more significant dermatological distress or swelling. Caution is necessary when handling this species to avoid these adverse physiological responses.
