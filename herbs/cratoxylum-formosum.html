Cratoxylum formosum

What's the taxonomical classification of Cratoxylum formosum?

Cratoxylum formosum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Malpighiales. This plant is further categorized into the family Hypericaceae, specifically within the genus Cratoxylum, and is identified by its unique species name, formosum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cratoxylum formosum has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical to oblong in shape with entire margins and acute tips. The foliage typically displays a glossy texture on the upper surface and can range from bright green to deeper shades depending on maturity. Its woody stems exhibit a smooth bark that often develops characteristic lenticels as the tree ages. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in terminal panicles or clusters. These floral structures eventually give way to fleshy fruits that serve as a key morphological identifier for the species.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical rainforests and coastal sclerophyll forests of eastern Australia, specifically ranging from the Wet Tropics in Queensland down to New South Wales. It is most commonly found inhabiting sub-tropical and temperate rainforest margins where moisture is abundant. Within these regions, it thrives in diverse habitats including vine thickets and moist gullies. The species is frequently distributed along the coastal strip where rainfall is reliable and soil is nutrient-rich. What are the pharmacological activities of Cratoxylum formosum?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological potential through its potent anti-cancer, antibacterial, antimicrobial, antioxidant, and cytotoxic activities. Research indicates that the bioactive compounds within Cratoxylum formosum can effectively inhibit the growth of various pathogenic microorganisms. Its strong antioxidant properties help neutralize free radicals, thereby reducing oxidative stress within biological systems. Furthermore, the plant exhibits notable cytotoxic effects that allow it to target and destroy specific malignant cell lines. These diverse biological actions suggest that the species holds considerable promise for future therapeutic developments in medicine.

As discussed in a study, Cratoxylum formosum is a medicinal plant rich in phenolic compounds. The water crude extract of C. formosum leaves was used to synthesize zinc oxide nanoparticles with an average electronic band gap of approximately 3 eV. These synthesized samples demonstrated growth inhibition of Gram-positive bacteria Bacillus subtilis and Staphylococcus epidermidis, as well as the Gram-negative bacterium Escherichia coli. The resulting ZnO demonstrated potent anti-cancer activity against A431 non-melanoma skin cancer cells and HaCaT intermediary cancerous keratinocytes without affecting Vero normal cell lines. Specifically, the ZnO nanosheet offered stronger cytotoxicity effects against A431 cells than spherical shaped particles. These findings suggest the potential use of green-synthesized ZnO nanosheets from C. formosum extract for developing antibacterial substances or anti-cancer drugs.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds (E)-caryophyllene, (E)-β-ocimene, anthraqquinones, formoxanthone A-C, and xanthones. These specific chemical constituents contribute to the plant's diverse biological activities and therapeutic potential. The presence of sesquiterpenes like (E)-caryophyllene and (E)-β-ocimene often relates to anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Furthermore, the anthraqquinones and xanthones are known for their significant pharmacological roles in various medicinal applications. The unique formoxanthone A-C compounds add to the complex phytochemical profile of the species.

As discussed in a study, the essential oil from the leaves of Cratoxylum formosum contains 35 identified compounds. The major constituents include (E)-caryophyllene at 39.30%, α-humulene at 12.90%, and (E)-β-ocimene at 11.85%. This oil shows antimicrobial activity with minimum inhibitory concentration values ranging from 16 to 64 µg/mL and IC50 values between 7.55 and 20.56 µg/mL. Furthermore, it demonstrates larvicidal potential with LC50 values ranging from 44.27 to 77.71 µg/mL and LC90 values from 57.74 to 102.61 µg/mL after 24 and 48 h of exposure.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating cardiovascular diseases, non-melanoma skin cancer, oxidative stress, vascular dysfunction, and infections caused by Staphylococcus aureus. Its bioactive compounds provide potent antioxidant properties that help the body combat oxidative stress. These chemical constituents also play a critical role in regulating blood flow to prevent vascular dysfunction and associated heart conditions. Furthermore, the plant exhibits significant medicinal potential in protecting skin cells from non-melanoma skin cancer. It also demonstrates effective antibacterial activity against pathogens like Staphylococcus aureus.

As discussed in a study, aqueous extracts of Cratoxylum formosum were investigated for antioxidant and vascular protective activities using in vitro and in vivo models. Male Sprague-Dawley rats were orally administered the plant extract at a dose of 1 g/kg/d for 6 d. While phenylhydrazine induced severe hemolysis and hemodynamic disturbances, treatment with the extract significantly improved hemodynamic status. The plant extract largely restored vascular responses to bradykinin, acetylcholine, and phenylephrine that were impaired in PHZ-control rats. Additionally, the extract prevented the loss of blood reduced glutathione and suppressed the formation of plasma malondialdehyde, plasma NO metabolites, and blood superoxide anion. These findings suggest that the plant possesses antioxidant properties and potential roles in protecting against vascular dysfunction.
