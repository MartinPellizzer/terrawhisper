Indigofera sensitiva

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Indigofera sensitiva has an erect, herbaceous growth habit characterized by slender, branched stems and pinnately compound leaves consisting of multiple small, oblong leaflets. The leaves exhibit a sensitive response to touch, where the leaflets fold inward or droop when disturbed. Small, fragrant, pea-like flowers emerge in axillary racemes, typically displaying a pale yellow or cream coloration. The plant develops small, cylindrical legume pods that eventually dehisce to release tiny seeds. This species often features a fine pubescence along its stems and leaf surfaces, providing a soft texture to the foliage.

What are the herbal preparations of Indigofera sensitiva?

Indigofera sensitiva is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste derived from its dried or fresh leaves and stems. Practitioners often boil the plant material in water to create a liquid extract used for internal consumption. The leaves can also be crushed into a fine pulp to serve as a poultice for treating skin ailments. Some traditional methods involve using the plant in powdered form for topical applications or mixing it with oils. What side-effects this plant can have?

Indigofera sensitiva can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and potential gastrointestinal distress if ingested due to the presence of alkaloids and tannins. Contact with the raw plant material often results in redness, itching, or localized swelling on the skin. Some individuals may experience more severe hypersensitivity reactions depending on their level of exposure. If consumed, the chemical components may lead to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal discomfort. Careful handling is necessary to avoid these adverse reactions during use.
