Pedicularis nasturtiifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Pedicularis nasturtiifolia?

Pedicularis nasturtiifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Lamiales. It is a member of the family Orobanchaceae and is further categorized into the genus Pedicularis, eventually reaching its specific designation as the species nasturtiifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pedicularis nasturtiifolia has pinnately divided leaves with rounded to blunt lobes and dense, tubular flowers arranged in terminal spikes.

The foliage is characterized by a basal rosette of leaves that transition into smaller, more deeply lobed structures along the stem. Its flowers are typically pink or purplish and feature a prominent upper lip that covers the reproductive organs. The stems are upright and can vary in height depending on the specific alpine environment.

Small hairs often cover the stem and leaf surfaces, providing a textured appearance to the plant.
