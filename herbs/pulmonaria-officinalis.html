Lungwort

Pulmonaria officinalis

Lungwort (Pulmonaria officinalis) is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, known for its medicinal properties and traditional use in treating respiratory conditions such as coughs and bronchitis.

Botanical Identification

Pulmonaria officinalis, commonly known as lungwort, belongs to the Violaceae family. It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant has basal rosettes of lanceolate leaves and violet flowers. Synonyms include lung plant and heartworts, reflecting its historical use in treating respiratory ailments.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Spermatophyta
  • Class: caryophyllopsida
  • Order: Geraniales
  • Family: Geraniaceae
  • Genus: Pulmonaria
  • Species: Pulmonaria officinalis

Active Compounds

Pulmonaria officinalis contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids. These compounds work synergistically to support respiratory health by reducing inflammation and enhancing mucus clearance. Their combined action makes the herb effective in traditional herbal medicine for respiratory conditions.

  • Flavonoid
  • Tannin
  • Phenolic acid

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory ailments such as coughs and bronchitis.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and as a general tonic.

Poultice: Applied externally for skin irritations and wounds.

Safety Profile

Lungwort is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to potential risks. There is limited research on its long-term safety, so it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before use.
