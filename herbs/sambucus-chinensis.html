Sambucus chinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Sambucus chinensis?

Sambucus chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, the plant is placed in the order Dipsacales and belongs to the family Viburnaceae. Finally, it is identified under the genus Sambucus, with its specific designation being the species chinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sambucus chinensis has decussate, pinnately compound leaves consisting of five to seven lanceolate leaflets with serrated margins and a deciduous nature. The plant typically grows as a large shrub or small tree reaching heights of up to six meters. Its stems are woody and often feature lenticels, while the inflorescences appear as large, flat-topped cymes of small, creamy-white flowers. These blossoms eventually give way to clusters of dark purple to blackish drupes that are roughly 5 to 8 millimeters in diameter. The overall structure is characterized by its spreading habit and robust branching pattern.

Its distribution is closely tied to specific elevations and soil moisture levels found in these East Asian ecosystems. What are the pharmacological activities of Sambucus chinensis?

This plant has hepatoprotective, antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial pharmacological activities. The bioactive compounds found within Sambucus chinensis work to protect liver cells from oxidative damage and chemical toxicity. Research indicates that its phytochemical constituents can effectively reduce inflammation in various biological systems. Additionally, the plant demonstrates significant potential in inhibiting the growth of certain pathogenic microorganisms. These diverse therapeutic properties make it a subject of interest for developing new medicinal treatments.

According to a study published by "Zhong yao cai = Zhongyaocai = Journal of Chinese medicinal materials", the extraction of Sambucus chinensis by 75% alcohol showed very significantly protective effects on mice acute hepatic injury induced by CCl4. This 75% alcohol extraction process proved to be better than other extraction processes. The active parts of Sambucus chinensis against hepatitis include the extraction eluted by 30% alcohol after purification with macroporous adsorption resin and the extraction with EtOAc. Both of these active parts showed significantly protective effects on mice acute hepatic injury induced by CCl4. Among these, the effects of the extraction eluted by 30% alcohol after purification with macroporous adsorption resin were better than the extraction with EtOAc.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating acute hepatic injury, acute liver-injury, chemical liver injuries, hepatitis, and immunological liver injuries. These various conditions involve significant damage or inflammation to the liver tissues. Specifically, it may be applied to mitigate damage caused by toxic chemicals or sudden inflammatory responses. The plant's properties are also studied for their potential to manage hepatitis and complex immunological liver injuries. Researchers often look at these applications to understand how the species supports liver function during recovery.

According to a study published by "Zhong yao cai = Zhongyaocai = Journal of Chinese medicinal materials", the granule of Sambucus chinensis Lindl is used to provide protective effects against acute hepatic injury. This plant is studied for its ability to protect mice from acute hepatic injury induced by CCl4, which involves antagonizing lipoperoxidation and improving the energy of Ca2+-ATPase of theca mitochondria and microsome. It also shows significantly protective effects on acute hepatic injury in mice or rats induced by D-GalN or Con A. Ultimately, the granule of Sambucus chinensis Lindl demonstrates significant protection against many kinds of chemical and immunological liver injuries.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause significant gastrointestinal distress including nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal pain if consumed improperly. These symptoms often arise because certain parts of the plant contain cyanogenic glycosides that may release toxic compounds during digestion. Some individuals might also experience dizziness as a secondary neurological reaction to these toxins. It is crucial to ensure all parts of the plant are thoroughly cooked or processed to mitigate these adverse effects. Always exercise extreme caution when handling or ingesting any part of this species.
