Cymbopogon distans

What's the taxonomical classification of Cymbopogon distans?

Cymbopogon distans belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Poaales. This plant is a member of the family Poaceae, which places it within the genus Cymbopogon, and is specifically identified by its species name, distans.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cymbopogon distans has a perennial, tufted growth habit characterized by long, slender leaves that are linear in shape and possess a distinctively involute or rolled margin. The leaf blades are typically bright green and can reach significant lengths, tapering gradually toward a fine point. Its inflorescence consists of terminal panicles that are relatively loose and spreading, often displaying a delicate, airy structure. The individual spikelets are small and arranged along thin branches, contributing to the plant's overall light texture. These morphological traits allow the grass to effectively navigate and thrive in open, sunny habitats.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds citral, geranial, geraniol, geranyl acetate, and neral. These specific monoterpene constituents are primarily responsible for the aromatic profile and therapeutic potential of the species. Geranial and neral are isomers that together make up the citral component, often providing antimicrobial properties. Geraniol and geranyl acetate contribute to the plant's insecticidal and anti-inflammatory capabilities. The synergistic interaction of these chemical compounds enhances the overall biological activity of the plant extracts.

As discussed in a study, the essential oil hydro-distilled from Cymboogon distans, coded as CDA-01, contains 54 identified compounds. Among these, the most abundant medicinal compounds are citral at 34.3%, geranyl acetate at 21.2%, and geraniol at 16.4%. This oil demonstrated the ability to lower pro-inflammatory cytokine levels, specifically IL-6 and TNF-α, in HaCaT cells. In vivo investigations also showed that CDA-01 reduced pro-inflammatory mediators including TNF-α, IL-6, and NO. These findings suggest that the plant could serve as an effective treatment for skin inflammation disorders.
