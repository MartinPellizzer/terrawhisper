Photinia villosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Photinia villosa?

Photinia villosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent through the order Rosales and the family Rosaceae, it is specifically identified within the genus Photinia, ultimately reaching its unique designation as the species villosa.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat respiratory ailments like cough, asthma, and bronchitis, as well as digestive issues and inflammation. It contains bioactive compounds that act as natural expectorants to help clear mucus from the lungs. Traditional medicinal practices often utilize its extracts to soothe sore throats and reduce systemic swelling. Some cultures also apply the plant to manage skin irritations and minor wounds due to its antimicrobial properties. These diverse therapeutic uses stem from the high concentration of flavonoids and polyphenols found within its tissues.
