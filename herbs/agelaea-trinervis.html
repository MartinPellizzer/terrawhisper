Agelaea trinervis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Agelaea trinervis has simple, opposite leaves characterized by three prominent longitudinal veins originating from the base of the blade. The leaf margins are typically entire, and the texture varies from slightly coriaceous to chartaceous depending on the specimen's maturity. Its woody stems exhibit a branching pattern that supports a small to medium-sized shrubby growth habit. The reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into fleshy fruits.
