Camellia fraterna

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Camellia fraterna has evergreen, elliptical leaves that measure approximately 5 to 10 centimeters in length and possess a glossy, dark green surface with slightly serrated margins. The foliage is densely arranged along woody, upright stems that form a compact shrubby habit. During its blooming period, the plant produces solitary, rose-like flowers that typically exhibit a creamy white or pale pink coloration. These blossoms feature multiple layers of delicate petals surrounding a central cluster of prominent yellow stamens. The overall structure of the plant is characterized by its fine texture and robust, leathery leaf consistency.
