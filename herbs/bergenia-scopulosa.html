What's the taxonomical classification of Bergenia scopulosa?

Bergenia scopulosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Saxifragales and is a member of the family Saxifragaceae. Specifically, it is nested within the genus Bergenia, with its unique biological identity defined by the species name scopulosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bergenia scopulosa has thick, leathery, evergreen leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptical in shape and arranged in basal rosettes. These leaves typically feature serrated or dentate margins and a glossy, dark green upper surface. The plant develops long, creeping rhizomes that allow it to spread across the forest floor. During the flowering season, it produces upright, leafless stalks that bear clusters of pink to purplish flowers. These inflorescences consist of numerous small, five-petaled blossoms that emerge from the center of the rosette.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Bergenia scopulosa contains medicinal compounds primarily located in its thick, fleshy rhizomes and large, leathery leaves. The rhizomes are most frequently harvested because they possess a high concentration of tannins and various glycosides.
