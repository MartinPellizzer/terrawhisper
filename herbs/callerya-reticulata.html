Callerya reticulata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Callerya reticulata has large, broadly elliptic to ovate leaves that grow from a sympodial rhizome and produce striking, large white flowers with a distinct yellow and reddish-brown reticulated pattern on the labellum. The plant is an epiphytic orchid characterized by its robust, thick pseudobulbs which serve as water storage organs. These pseudobulbs are typically elongated and support the growth of several leathery, dark green leaves. During its blooming period, the orchid produces long, arching inflorescences that carry multiple fragrant blossoms. The intricate net-like markings on the lip of the flower provide its specific taxonomic distinction.
