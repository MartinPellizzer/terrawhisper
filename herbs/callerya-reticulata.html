What's the taxonomical classification of Callerya reticulata?

Callerya reticulaata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the taxonomic hierarchy, it is organized under the order Fabales and is a member of the family Fabaceae. Specifically, this plant is identified by the genus Callerya and the species name reticulata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Callerya reticulata has large, broadly elliptic to ovate leaves that grow from a sympodial rhizome and produce striking, large white flowers with a distinct yellow and reddish-brown reticulated pattern on the labellum. The plant is an epiphytic orchid characterized by its robust, thick pseudobulbs which serve as water storage organs. These pseudobulbs are typically elongated and support the growth of several leathery, dark green leaves. During its blooming period, the orchid produces long, arching inflorescences that carry multiple fragrant blossoms.
