What's the taxonomical classification of Lespedeza virgata?

Lespedeza virgata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Fabales. The plant is a member of the family Fabaceae, which is further refined into the genus Lespedeza, ultimately reaching its specific identification as the species virgata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lespedeza virgata has trifoliate leaves with elliptical to oblong leaflets and small, pea-like yellow flowers arranged in terminal racemes. Each leaflet possesses a smooth margin and a pointed tip, contributing to a delicate foliage appearance. The inflorescence consists of numerous tiny blossoms that bloom sequentially along a central stalk. Furthermore, the unique structures of lespedezacoumestan, lespedezavirgatal, and lespedezavirgatol are key chemical markers for this plant. The presence of 2-phenylbenzofurans also adds to the complex chemical makeup used in therapeutic research.

As discussed in a study, the aerial parts of Lespedeza virgata contain two 2-phenylbenzofurans known as lespedezavirgatol (1) and lespedezavirgatal (2) along with a coumestan identified as lespedezacoumesstan (3). These three new compounds were isolated alongside nine known flavonoids. The three new compounds demonstrated strong oxygen radical absorbance capacity. Furthermore, they showed potent inhibition toward lipid peroxidation in both plasma and kidney homogenate of renal failure rats.
