What's the taxonomical classification of Lespedeza virgata?

Lespedeza virgata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Fabales. The plant is a member of the family Fabaceae, which is further refined into the genus Lespedeza, ultimately reaching its specific identification as the species virgata.

Each leaflet possesses a smooth margin and a pointed tip, contributing to a delicate foliage appearance. What are the pharmacological activities of Lespedeza virgata?

This plant has antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and hepatoprotective pharmacological activities. These diverse biological effects make it a subject of significant interest in medicinal plant studies.

As discussed in a study, the aerial parts of Lespedeza virgata yielded two 2-phenylbenzofurans, lespedezavirgatol (1) and lespedezavirgatal (2), and a coumestan, lespedezacoumestan (3). These 3 new compounds were isolated alongside 9 known flavonoids. The 3 new compounds showed strong oxygen radical absorbance capacity. Additionally, they demonstrated potent inhibition toward lipid peroxidation in both plasma and kidney homogenate of renal failure rats.
