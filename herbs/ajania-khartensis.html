Ajania khartensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Ajania khartensis?

Ajania khartensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical classification, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant is a member of the order Asterales and falls under the family Asteraceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic designation is defined by the genus Ajania and the species khartensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ajania khartensis has woody, perennial stems that support small, ovate to lanceolate leaves arranged oppositely along the branches. The leaf margins are typically serrated or finely toothed, while the surfaces often exhibit a slightly pubescent texture. During its flowering stage, the plant produces small, disciform flower heads that are clustered in terminal corymbose cymes. These inflorescences consist of numerous tiny florets that lack prominent ray petals. The overall growth habit is characterized by a low-growing, shrubby structure suited to its specific habitat.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of western China, specifically within the provinces of Gansu, Qinghai, Sichuan, and Tibet. It typically thrives in high-altitude environments characterized by rocky crevices and alpine meadows. The species is often found at elevations ranging from 2,500 to 4,000 meters above sea level. These specific habitats provide the cool, well-drained conditions necessary for its survival. Its distribution remains concentrated in these central Asian highland zones.
