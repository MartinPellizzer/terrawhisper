Pimpineilla candolleana has a perennial growth habit characterized by erect stems that typically reach heights of 20 to 50 centimeters and possess finely divided, pinnate leaves with serrated margins. Its inflorescence consists of compound umbels bearing small, white flowers clustered in flat-topped arrangements. The individual florets are surrounded by bracts that vary in shape and size depending on the specific subspecies.
