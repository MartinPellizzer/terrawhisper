Curcuma yunnanensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Curcuma yunnanensis?

Curcuma yunnanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further categorized under the order Zingiberales. It is a member of the family Zingiberaceae, a group known for its diverse aromatic plants, and falls specifically under the genus Curcumma, with its unique designation as the species yunnanensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Curcuma yunnanensis has long, lanceolate leaves that emerge from a rhizomatous base and large, spreading inflorescences bearing bright yellow flowers. The plant's underground rhizomes are thick and fleshy, serving as the primary storage organ for nutrients. Each leaf typically features a prominent midrib and parallel venation characteristic of the Zingiberaceae family. The floral structure consists of showy labella that attract pollinators through their vivid coloration. During the flowering stage, the bracts provide additional structural support and visual prominence to the cluster.
