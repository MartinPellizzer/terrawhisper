Dregea sinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Dregea sinensis?

Dregea sinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, the plant is organized under the order Gentianaales and falls within the family Apocynaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by the genus Dregea and the species sinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dregea sinensis has woody, climbing vines characterized by trifoliate leaves with ovate leaflets and small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers arranged in axillary panicles. The stems are flexible and often utilize twining mechanisms to ascend through surrounding vegetation. Each leaf typically consists of three distinct leaflets that feature serrated margins and a slightly pubescent texture on the underside. The plant produces small, globose fruits that eventually transition to a darker color upon maturity. These morphological traits allow the species to thrive within dense subtropical forest environments.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains polyoxypregnane di- and triglycosides, polyoxypregnane glycosides, and polyoxypregnane tetraglycosides which serve as its primary medicinal compounds. These specific chemical structures belong to a class of bioactive molecules known for their diverse pharmacological properties. Researchers study these glycosides to understand how they interact with human biological systems. The varying number of sugar moieties in the di-, tri-, and tetra- forms can significantly influence the compound's solubility and potency. Understanding these complex molecular profiles is essential for developing targeted therapeutic applications.

As discussed in a study, Dregea sinensis is an important herbal medicine used in the Dai nationality of China. Research on an ethyl acetate fraction of the plant led to the characterization of twenty-five previously undescribed polyoxypregnane tetraglycosides using HPLC-ESI-MSn analysis. Among these compounds, eight structures were specifically isolated and confirmed through 1D and 2D NMR methods. When evaluating the cytotoxicity of the isolates on several human cell lines, most showed little effect. However, slight inhibitory effects were detected from four polyoxypregnane glycosides when applied at 10 μM against the human leukemia cell line HL-60.
