What's the taxonomical classification of Dregea sinensis?

Dregea sinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, the plant is organized under the order Gentianaales and falls within the family Apocynaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by the genus Dregea and the species sinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dregea sinensis has woody, climbing vines characterized by trifoliate leaves with ovate leaflets and small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers arranged in axillary panicles. The stems are flexible and often utilize twining mechanisms to ascend through surrounding vegetation. Each leaf typically consists of three distinct leaflets that feature serrated margins and a slightly pubescent texture on the underside. The plant produces small, globose fruits that eventually transition to a darker color upon maturity. These morphological traits allow the species to thrive within dense subtropical forest environments.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains various medicinal compounds including polyoxypregnane di- and triglycosides, polyoxypregnane glycosides, and polyoxypregnane tetraglycosides. These specific secondary metabolites are primarily concentrated within the roots and stems of the species. Research indicates that these chemical constituents play a significant role in the plant's overall pharmacological profile. The presence of these diverse glycoside forms contributes to the complex biological activities observed in Dregea sinensis. Such detailed chemical composition makes the plant a subject of interest for further medicinal studies.

According to a study published by "Phytochemistry", twenty-five previously undescribed polyoxypregnane tetraglycosides were characterized from an ethyl acetate fraction of Dregea sinensis using HPLC-ESI-MSn analysis in positive mode. The research aimed to identify these related tetraglycosides by building upon previous studies of the polyoxypregnane di- and triglycosides found in the plant. To test structure predictions, eight of these glycosides were isolated and their structures were elucidated through 1D and 2D NMR methods. Regarding cytotoxicity, the isolates generally showed little effect on several human cell lines. However, slight inhibitory effects were detected from four polyoxypregnane glycosides at a concentration of 10 μM against the human leukemia cell line HL-60.
