Selaginella picta

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Selagineella picta has a creeping rhizomatous growth habit with small, scale-like leaves that are arranged in four distinct rows along the stems. The individual leaves are microphyllous, meaning they lack true vascular tissue, and possess a characteristic patterned appearance due to varying pigmentation. These diminutive leaves often display contrasting colors, such as green with reddish or brownish markings, which give the species its specific epithet. The plant typically forms dense, spreading mats as the stems branch frequently to colonize moist substrates. Fine, hair-like structures may also be present on the stem surface to assist with moisture retention in its natural habitat.
