Corylus heterophylla

What's the taxonomical classification of Corylus heterophylla?

Corylus heterophyllla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized into the order Fagales. It is a member of the family Betulaceae, which places it within the genus Corylus, and is ultimately identified by its specific species name, heterophylla.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Corylus heterophylla contains various secondary metabolites including polyphenols, flavonoids, and tannins that possess potential antioxidant and antimicrobial properties. These bioactive compounds are primarily concentrated within the leaves and the nut hulls of the plant. Studies suggest that the specific phenolic profile of this species can help neutralize free radicals in biological systems. While much research focuses on other members of the Corylus genus, the chemical complexity of this species offers significant interest for pharmacological exploration. Further investigation into its specific glycosides may reveal more therapeutic applications for human health.

What are the therapeutic actions of Corylus heterophylla?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, inflammatory conditions, and gastrointestinal distress. The leaves contain bioactive compounds that possess antimicrobial and antioxidant properties helpful in managing topical ailments. When applied externally, decoctions of the foliage can soothe irritated skin surfaces and reduce swelling. Some traditional practices also involve using the plant to address internal digestive imbalances. These medicinal applications rely on the specific chemical profile found within the species.
