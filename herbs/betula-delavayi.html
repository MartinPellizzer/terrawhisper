Betula delavayi

What's the taxonomical classification of Betula delavayi?

Betula delaavay belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is placed in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Fagales. As a member of the family Betulaceae, it is further categorized into the genus Betula, eventually reaching its specific designation as the species delaayi.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Betula delavayi contains bioactive compounds such as betulin, betulinic acid, lupeol, and various flavonoids that provide significant therapeutic potential. These triterpenoids are known for their potent anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and anticancer properties in pharmacological studies. The presence of phenolic compounds also contributes to the plant's ability to combat oxidative stress within biological systems. Additionally, the bark and leaves yield essential oils and tannins that may exhibit antimicrobial activity against certain pathogens. Researchers continue to investigate these chemical constituents to develop new treatments for inflammatory diseases and cellular malfunctions.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, inflammatory conditions, digestive ailments, and various respiratory issues. The bark and leaves contain bioactive compounds that help reduce swelling and soothe irritated skin surfaces. Traditional medicinal practices often utilize its extracts to alleviate symptoms of stomach discomfort or indigestion. Furthermore, it acts as an antimicrobial agent to combat localized infections. Its properties are also leveraged to provide relief from persistent coughs and congestion.
