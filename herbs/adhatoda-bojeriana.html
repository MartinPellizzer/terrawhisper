White Adhatoda (Adhatoda Bojeriana)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Adhatoda bojeriana.

White adhatoda, scientifically known as Adhatoda bojeriana, is a medicinal herb native to the Indian subcontinent and widely used in Ayurvedic and traditional medicine systems. It is valued for its potent bronchodilatory and expectorant properties, making it particularly effective in treating respiratory conditions such as asthma, bronchitis, and cough. Traditionally, it has been used in India and other South Asian countries to relieve breathing difficulties and as a digestive aid, often prepared as a powder or decoction. In modern wellness practices, it is increasingly incorporated into herbal formulations for respiratory support and as a natural remedy for inflammation. One of its unique features is the presence of the rare alkaloid vasicine, which contributes to its therapeutic effects, and it has a distinctive bitter taste that is often masked in formulations.
