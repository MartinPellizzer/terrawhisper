Microsorum membranaceum

What's the taxonomical classification of Microsorum membranaceum?

Microsorum membranaceum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae. Following its taxonomic descent, it falls into the order Polypodiales and the family Polypodiaceae. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Microsorum and the species membranaceum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Microsorum membranaceum has creeping, slender, and branched rhizomes that are typically dark brown or black and serve as the primary structural base for the plant. The fronds are pinnate in shape and feature a distinctively thin, membranous texture that gives the species its name. Each leaflet is lanceolate or narrowly elliptical with entire margins and a glossy, dark green surface. The venation is fine and reticulate, providing a delicate appearance to the foliage. Small, dark sori are located on the underside of the leaves, arranged in rows near the margins to facilitate spore production.
