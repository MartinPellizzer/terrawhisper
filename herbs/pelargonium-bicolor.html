What is Pelargonium bicolor?

Pelargonium bicolor is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is characterized by its white and pink bi-colored flowers, which are composed of five petals and are arranged in terminal inflorescences. The leaves are opposite and simple, with an ovate shape and serrated margins, and are typically 5-10 cm long. The plant is an annual or short-lived perennial, with a woody base and a branching stem that grows to a height of 30-60 cm.

The species is native to southern Africa, specifically in the western and southern parts of the continent.
