Chinese Peony (Paeonia lactiflora): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

The Chinese Peony, or Paeonia lactiflora, is a perennial herb native to China, widely cultivated for its medicinal properties and ornamental value in traditional Chinese medicine.

What is Chinese Peony (Paeonia Lactiflora)?

The Chinese Peony (Paeonia lactiflora) is a perennial herb native to China, widely used in traditional Chinese medicine for its roots, which contain compounds with anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and cardiovascular benefits.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Chinese peony is Paeonia lactiflora. This plant belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Paeoniales, family Paeoniaceae, genus Paeonia, and species Paeonia lactiflora. It is commonly known as Chinese peony in China, and as garden peony in North America. It is also known as the tree peony in some regions of China. The synonyms for this plant include Paeonia officinalis and Paeonia suffruticosa.

Plant Description and Morphology

Chinese peony is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with a compact, bushy structure and multiple stems.

Its leaves are compound, consisting of 3 to 5 oval-shaped leaflets arranged in an alternate pattern along the stems. The flowers are large, showy, and borne singly at the ends of the stems, with petals that can be single, semi-double, or double, depending on the cultivar. The root system is thick, fleshy, and tuberous, often forming a dense network beneath the soil.

The flowers come in a range of colors including red, pink, white, and yellow, with a subtle, sweet fragrance and a velvety, soft texture.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Chinese peony is native to the mountainous regions of northwestern China, particularly in provinces such as Gansu, Sichuan, and Shaanxi, where it thrives in temperate climates.

Its geographic distribution extends to parts of Mongolia and northern Korea, though it is most commonly found in the Yangtze River basin and the Yellow River region. The plant prefers cool, moist environments with well-drained soil and partial to full sun exposure. It grows best in areas with moderate temperatures, typically between 15°C and 25°C, and requires a period of cold dormancy to bloom successfully.

Ecological conditions such as soil pH, humidity levels, and seasonal rainfall patterns significantly influence its growth and survival.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The roots, leaves, and flowers of the Chinese Peony (Paeonia lactiflora) are primarily used medicinally in traditional Chinese medicine.

Phytochemical Composition

The phytochemical composition of Chinese Peony (Paeonia lactiflora) includes a variety of bioactive compounds such as paeoniflorin, paeonol, flavonoids, alkaloids, and essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties and therapeutic applications in traditional Chinese medicine.

Pharmacological Properties

Chinese Peony (Paeonia lactiflora) possesses significant pharmacological properties including anti-inflammatory, analgesic, antispasmodic, and hemostatic effects, primarily due to its active compounds such as paeoniflorin, benzoylpaeoniflorin, and paeonol, which interact with various physiological pathways to modulate immune responses and cellular functions.

Mechanisms of Action

The Chinese Peony (Paeonia lactiflora) exerts its pharmacological effects primarily through the modulation of inflammatory pathways, antioxidant activity, and neuroprotective mechanisms, with its active constituents such as paeoniflorin and paeonol interacting with various cellular targets to reduce oxidative stress, inhibit pro-inflammatory cytokines, and regulate neuronal signaling.

Therapeutic Uses and Indications

Chinese Peony (Paeonia lactiflora) is traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat conditions such as menstrual disorders, inflammation, pain, and cardiovascular issues due to its anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antispasmodic properties.

Preparation Methods and Forms

The Chinese Peony (Paeonia lactiflora) is typically prepared as dried roots, root pieces, or powdered form, often used in decoctions, tinctures, or as part of traditional herbal formulations.

Dosage and Administration

The recommended dosage of Chinese Peony root for herbal use typically ranges from 6 to 12 grams per day, prepared as a decoction or powder, and should be administered under the guidance of a qualified practitioner to ensure safety and efficacy.

Safety, Side Effects, and Contraindications

When used appropriately, Chinese Peony (Paeonia lactiflora) is generally considered safe, but it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort, allergic reactions, or interact with certain medications, particularly anticoagulants, and should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation.

Drug Interactions

Chinese Peony (Paeonia lactiflora) may interact with anticoagulant medications due to its potential blood-thinning effects, and it should be used cautiously with drugs that affect blood clotting or the central nervous system.

Toxicity and Precautions

Chinese Peony (Paeonia lactiflora) is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses, but it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort, and long-term or excessive use could lead to toxicity, particularly affecting the liver and kidneys, so it should be used with caution under professional guidance.

Cultivation and Harvesting

Chinese Peony (Paeonia lactiflora) is typically cultivated in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sunlight, requiring a cold winter dormancy period to ensure optimal flowering and medicinal compound development.

Processing and Extraction

The processing and extraction of Chinese Peony involve drying the petals and roots, followed by methods such as decoction, maceration, or solvent extraction to obtain active compounds like paeoniflorin and flavonoids.

Storage and Shelf Life

Chinese Peony (Paeonia lactiflora) should be stored in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture, and its shelf life is typically 2 to 3 years when properly preserved.

Scientific Research and Evidence

Scientific research has demonstrated that Chinese Peony contains various bioactive compounds, including paeoniflorin and oxypaeoniflorin, which exhibit anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine.

Traditional and Historical Uses

Chinese Peony (Paeonia lactiflora) has been traditionally used in Chinese herbal medicine for centuries to treat conditions such as pain, inflammation, and digestive disorders, with its roots and flowers being the primary parts utilized for their medicinal properties.

Regulatory Status

The Chinese Peony (Paeonia lactiflora) is classified as a Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) herb and is regulated under the Chinese Pharmacopoeia, with specific guidelines for its cultivation, processing, and use in medicinal formulations.
