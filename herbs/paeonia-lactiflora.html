The herb's botanical properties include its ability to be dried and processed into teas, capsules, and other supplements, making it easily accessible to those seeking its benefits.

According to historical records, Chinese peony has been revered for centuries in traditional Chinese medicine, with references dating back to the Tang Dynasty (618-907 CE), where it was used to treat a range of ailments from fever to respiratory issues.

What are the planting tips of Paeonia lactiflora?

Paeonia lactiflora, also known as Chinese peony, is a popular and showy perennial flower that requires proper planting techniques to thrive.

When planting Paeonia lactiflora, it's essential to choose a location with full sun to partial shade and well-draining soil rich in organic matter. The ideal time for planting is in the fall, about 8-10 weeks before the first frost, or in early spring as soon as the soil can be worked.

Dig a hole that is twice as wide and just as deep as the root ball of the peony, and add a layer of compost or well-rotted manure to improve drainage and fertility. Gently remove the plant from its container and place it in the hole, spreading the roots outwards and downwards, making sure they are not tangled. Fill the hole with soil, gently firming it around the roots as you go, and water thoroughly to settle the soil.

Finally, mulch around the base of the plant to retain moisture and suppress weeds, which will help the peony establish a strong root system and produce abundant, fragrant blooms in late spring.
