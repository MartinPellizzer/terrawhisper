Myriactis nepalensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Myriactis nepalensis has small, lanceolate, bright green leaves that are arranged oppositely on slender, woody stems. These leaves are typically thin with entire margins and a smooth texture. The plant features small, inconspicuous flowers that often appear in axillary clusters. As the plant matures, it develops a spreading habit with branching woody stems. The overall structure of the shrub is characterized by its delicate foliage and compact growth form.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Myriactis nepalensis can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Some individuals may experience localized redness or itching upon direct contact with the plant's foliage. There is limited scientific documentation regarding systemic toxicity, yet caution is advised during handling. Allergic reactions can vary in intensity depending on the sensitivity of the person. It is important to avoid consuming any part of this species to prevent potential nausea or stomach pain.
