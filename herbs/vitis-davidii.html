Vitis davidii

What's the taxonomical classification of Vitis davidii?

Vitis davidii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further belongs to the order Vitales and is a member of the family Vitaceae. Specifically, it is identified by its genus, Vitis, and its species name, davidii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vitis davidii has woody, climbing vines characterized by large, palmately lobed leaves with three to five distinct lobes and serrated margins. The foliage typically exhibits a deep green color and can reach significant sizes to maximize light absorption in shaded environments. Its stems are equipped with specialized tendrils that allow the plant to coil around supports for vertical growth. During the flowering stage, it produces small, inconspicuous clusters of greenish flowers. The mature fruit consists of small, globose berries that change color as they ripen.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the central and southern regions of China, specifically within provinces such as Hubei, Hunan, and Guizhou. It typically thrives in the moist, temperate climates found in mountainous or hilly terrains. These wild grapevines are often discovered growing in the understory of deciduous forests or along the edges of shaded ravines. The species prefers well-drained soils that remain consistently humid throughout the growing season. Its specific range is largely defined by these localized ecological niches within the subtropical landscape.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds (+)-epsilon-viniferin, ampeolopsin E, amurensin B, amurensin G, and resveratrol. These specific polyphenolic substances are classified as stilbenoids, which are known for their potent biological activities. Resveratrol serves as a primary component often studied for its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The presence of amurensin B and amurensin G suggests significant potential for pharmacological applications in human health. Additionally, the combination of (+)-epsilon-viniferin and ampeolopsin E contributes to the complex chemical profile of Vitis davidii.

According to a study published by "Zhongguo Zhong yao za zhi = Zhongguo zhongyao zazhi = China journal of Chinese materia medica", seven compounds were isolated from Vitis davidii. These medicinal oligosstilbene compounds include resveratrol, heyneanol A, ampelopsin E, amurensin B, (+)-epsilon-viniferin, vitisin A, and amurensin G. The isolation process utilized polystyrene resin RA, silica gel, and C18 column chromatography. All 7 of these specific compounds were obtained from this plant for the first time.
