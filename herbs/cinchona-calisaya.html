Calisaya Cinchona

Cinchona calisaya

Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya) is a medicinal plant native to the Andean regions of South America, known for its use in treating malaria and other fevers due to the presence of quinine.

Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya) is a species belonging to the genus Cinchona in the family Rubiaceae, classified within the order Rubiales, class Eudicots, division Magnoliophyta, and kingdom Plantae.

Morphology

Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya) is a shrub or small tree characterized by its upright, branched stems, dark green, elliptical leaves with a slightly waxy texture, and small, clustered yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the early summer.

Distribution

Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya) is native to the humid montane forests of northern Peru and southern Ecuador, specifically within the Andean regions of these countries.

Traditional Uses

Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya) has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its antimalarial properties, primarily due to the presence of quinine, which has historically been employed to treat malaria and other fevers.

Medicinal Systems

Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya) has been traditionally used in Andean medicine, particularly by Quechua and Aymara communities in the Peruvian highlands, for its antimalarial, antipyretic, and digestive properties.

Phytochemistry

The phytochemistry of Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya) is characterized by the presence of alkaloids, particularly quinine and quinidine, which are responsible for its traditional use in herbal medicine.

Pharmacology

Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya) exhibits pharmacological properties characterized by antipyretic, anti-inflammatory, and mild sedative effects, primarily due to its bioactive constituents that modulate fever, reduce inflammation, and promote relaxation of smooth muscles.

Medicinal Uses

Calisaya Cinchona is used to treat fever, malaria, digestive issues, respiratory infections, skin conditions, and as a tonic for overall health improvement.

Preparations

Calisaya Cinchona is used to prepare various medicinal formulations, including tinctures, teas, and extracts, which are traditionally employed to treat fever, inflammation, and digestive ailments.

Safety

While Calisaya Cinchona is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort, dizziness, and in rare cases, liver toxicity, particularly with prolonged use or high dosages.

Evidence

There is limited clinical evidence supporting the use of Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya) in modern medicine, with most studies focusing on its traditional applications and potential pharmacological properties such as antipyretic, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial effects.

Cultivation

Cinchona calisaya is typically cultivated in the humid, shaded environments of tropical highlands, often in regions with well-drained soils and consistent moisture, requiring careful management of temperature and humidity to ensure optimal growth and alkaloid production.

Processing

The processing of Calisaya Cinchona involves harvesting the bark of the plant, drying it under controlled conditions, and then grinding it into a fine powder to extract its active compounds, primarily quinidine and quinine, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Identification

Sustainability

The sustainability of Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya) is a critical concern due to its role in traditional medicine and the potential threats posed by overharvesting, habitat loss, and climate change.

Related

Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya) is closely related to other species within the Cinchona genus, including Cinchona officinalis and Cinchona reticulata, which are also used in traditional medicine for their quinine content and other bioactive compounds.
