What parts of Cinchona calisaya are used for medicinal purposes?

The most commonly used parts of Cinchona calisaya for medicinal purposes are the bark and leaves, which contain a unique set of alkaloids known as quinines.

The bark is rich in cinchonine, cinchonidine, and quinine, which are responsible for its anti-inflammatory, antipyretic, and antimalarial properties. These compounds have been used to treat various febrile conditions, including malaria, rheumatoid arthritis, and menstrual cramps. The leaves, on the other hand, contain a higher concentration of quinic acid and caffeine, which are believed to possess antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities.

What are the side effects of Cinchona calisaya when used improperly?

When used improperly, Cinchona calisaya increases the chances of experiencing side effects, such as gastrointestinal disturbances, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, due to its high alkaloid content.

Furthermore, Cinchona calisaya may also cause allergic reactions, including skin rashes, itching, and respiratory distress, particularly in individuals with a history of hypersensitivity to plants in the same family. In severe cases, improper use of Cinchona calisaya can even lead to more serious complications, such as liver or kidney damage, as well as an increased risk of bleeding and bruising due to its anticoagulant properties.

Therefore, it is essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using Cinchona calisaya for medicinal purposes to ensure safe and effective treatment.
