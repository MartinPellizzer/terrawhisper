Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya) is a medicinal plant native to the Peruvian Amazon, traditionally used in herbal medicine for its antimalarial properties due to the presence of alkaloids such as quinine.

What is Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona Calisaya)?

Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya) is a species of tree native to the Andean regions of South America, known for its medicinal properties and historical use in the production of quinine, a compound traditionally used to treat malaria.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Calisaya Cinchona is Cinchona calisaya. This plant belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Rubiales, family Rubiaceae, genus Cinchona, and species Cinchona calisaya. It is commonly known as Calisaya Cinchona in the Amazon region and Yagé in parts of the Andes. The synonyms for this plant include Cinchona calisaya var. calisaya and Cinchona calisaya subsp. calisaya.

Plant Description and Morphology

Calisaya Cinchona is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters tall, with a straight, cylindrical trunk and a dense crown.

Its leaves are elliptical to ovate in shape, with a pointed tip and a smooth, glossy surface that is dark green on top and lighter underneath. The flowers are small, clustered in terminal panicles, and consist of white to pale yellow petals with five lobes, emitting a faint, sweet fragrance. The root system is fibrous and extensive, anchoring the plant firmly in the soil and contributing to its resilience in various soil types.

The bark is rough and cracked, with a reddish-brown color, while the leaves have a slightly leathery texture and a mild, aromatic scent when crushed.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Calisaya Cinchona is native to the highland regions of eastern Peru and western Bolivia, specifically within the Amazonian rainforest areas.

Its geographic distribution is limited to elevations between 1,200 and 2,500 meters above sea level. The plant thrives in a tropical climate with high humidity and consistent temperatures ranging from 18°C to 25°C. It requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soils and ample rainfall throughout the year.

Calisaya Cinchona grows best in shaded, moist environments under the canopy of dense forest vegetation.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The plant parts used medicinally in Calisaya Cinchona include the bark, leaves, and roots.

The bark is the most commonly utilized part, as it contains the highest concentration of alkaloids. Leaves are often used in teas and poultices for their therapeutic properties. Roots are harvested for their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects.

Each part is processed differently depending on the intended medicinal application.

Phytochemical Composition

Calisaya Cinchona contains a variety of active compounds, primarily alkaloids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Among these, quinine and quinidine are the most notable alkaloids known for their antimalarial effects. Secondary metabolites such as flavonoids and terpenoids contribute to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities. Chemical markers like berberine and cinchonine are also present and are used to authenticate the plant's identity and potency.

These compounds collectively define the pharmacological profile of Calisaya Cinchona.

Pharmacological Properties

Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya) exhibits pharmacological properties primarily due to its alkaloid content, which includes quinine and other related compounds.

Its therapeutic actions are mainly antipyretic, anti-inflammatory, and antimalarial, making it valuable in traditional herbal medicine. System-level effects include reduced fever, decreased inflammation, and potential antiparasitic effects in the bloodstream.

Therapeutic Uses

Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya) is primarily used in herbal medicine for its therapeutic properties, particularly its antimalarial and antipyretic effects.

This plant contains alkaloids such as quinine, which are effective in treating malaria by targeting the Plasmodium parasite. It is also used to reduce fever and inflammation due to its ability to lower body temperature and soothe inflammatory responses. Common ailments it addresses include fever, chills, and symptoms associated with malaria.

Its traditional use extends to treating digestive issues and respiratory infections in various herbal practices.

Preparation Methods and Forms

The preparation methods of Calisaya Cinchona involve various forms in which the plant is utilized.

The most common forms include dried bark, powdered bark, and tinctures. Dried bark is often used to make teas or decoctions by boiling it in water. Powdered bark can be taken directly or mixed with other herbal ingredients.

Tinctures are prepared by soaking the bark in alcohol to extract its active compounds.

Dosage and Administration

The standard dose of Calisaya Cinchona is typically 200 to 400 mg of the dried bark per day.

This dosage is based on traditional use and is considered safe when taken as directed. The extract concentration should not exceed 10% alkaloid content to ensure safety and efficacy. It is recommended to take the dose once or twice daily, depending on the individual's response and the condition being treated.

The duration of treatment should not exceed four weeks without consulting a healthcare professional.

Safety, Side Effects, and Contraindications

When used appropriately, Calisaya Cinchona is generally considered safe, but it may cause side effects such as nausea, vomiting, and dizziness in some individuals.

Prolonged or excessive use can lead to more severe effects, including gastrointestinal upset and potential toxicity. It is contraindicated in individuals with known allergies to plants in the Rubiaceae family, as it may trigger allergic reactions. Due to limited research, its safety during pregnancy has not been fully established, and it is advisable to consult a healthcare provider before use.

Always follow recommended dosages and consult a professional for personalized guidance.

Cultivation and Harvesting

Calisaya Cinchona requires well-drained, slightly acidic soil rich in organic matter for optimal growth.

It thrives in full sunlight but can tolerate partial shade during its early growth stages. Regular watering is necessary, especially during dry periods, but the soil should not remain waterlogged. Propagation is typically done through cuttings or seeds, with cuttings offering faster results. The plant reaches maturity in 3 to 5 years and is best harvested during the dry season when alkaloid content is highest.

Harvesting involves carefully stripping the leaves and stems to avoid damaging the plant.

Processing and Extraction

The processing and extraction of Calisaya Cinchona involve several key steps to isolate its active compounds.

Drying is the first step, where the leaves and bark are sun-dried or mechanically dried to reduce moisture content and preserve the chemical integrity. Once dried, the plant material is ground into a fine powder to increase surface area for more efficient extraction. Extraction is typically performed using solvents such as ethanol or water to dissolve the alkaloids and other bioactive compounds.

Distillation may be employed to concentrate the extracted compounds and remove residual solvents, ensuring a pure final product.

Storage and Shelf Life

Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya) should be stored in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight to maintain its potency.
