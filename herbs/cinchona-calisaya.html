Calisaya Cinchona

Cinchona calisaya

Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya) is a medicinal plant native to the Andean regions of South America, known for its use in treating malaria and other fevers due to the presence of quinine.

Botanical Identification

Calisaya Cinchona, scientifically known as Cinchona calisaya, belongs to the Rubiaceae family. It is also called "Calisaya Quina" in some regions. This evergreen shrub is native to the Amazon basin and is characterized by its red flowers and yellow leaves. It is sometimes referred to as "Peruvian Bark" in herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Eudicots
  • Order: Rubiales
  • Family: Rubiaceae
  • Genus: Cinchona
  • Species: Cinchona calisaya

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory conditions and digestive issues.

Decoction: Used for treating fever and inflammation.

Safety Profile

Calisaya Cinchona is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation due to potential risks. Long-term use may lead to toxicity, so it is important to follow professional guidance.
