Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya)

Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya) is a member of the Rubiaceae family, native to Bolivia, Peru, and Ecuador. Traditionally, its bark, leaves, and fruits have been used for infusions, decoctions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its bitter, tonic, and stimulant actions, and has a long history of use in traditional chinese medicine, european herbal medicine, and ayurvedic medicine.

Perennial herbaceous plant.

Leaves
Broad leaves with upper surface dark green and lower surface pale green, featuring prominent stomatal bands along the midvein.
Flowers
Inflorescences are terminal racemes with zygomorphic flowers having five fused petals, two lip-like lobes, and four stamens, with pale yellow to orange-red corolla and a distinct yellowish glandular appendage at the base of the ovary.
Stems
Cinching stems with erect growth habit, opposite branching pattern, and glabrous surface.
