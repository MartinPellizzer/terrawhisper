Calisaya Cinchona

Cinchona calisaya

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Calisaya Cinchona, scientifically known as Cinchona calisaya, belongs to the Rubiaceae family. It is also called "Calisaya Quina" in some regions. This evergreen shrub is native to the Amazon basin and is characterized by its red flowers and yellow leaves. It is sometimes referred to as "Peruvian Bark" in herbal texts.

Active Compounds

Calisaya Cinchona contains key bioactive compounds such as alkaloids, terpenoids, and flavonoids. These compounds work synergistically to provide therapeutic effects, with alkaloids playing a central role in its medicinal properties. The presence of multiple active constituents enhances its potency and versatility in herbal medicine.

  • Alkaloid
  • Terpenoid
  • Flavonoid

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action
Immune Malaria, fever, allergic reactions Antimalarial, antipyretic, antihistaminic, antimicrobial
Respiratory Influenza, respiratory infections, tuberculosis, cough, asthma Antiviral, antimicrobial, expectorant, bronchodilator
Integumentary Skin infections Antimicrobial

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory conditions and digestive issues.

Decoction: Used for treating fever and inflammation.

Extract: Used for treating malaria due to quinine content.

Safety Profile

Calisaya Cinchona is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation due to potential risks. Long-term use may lead to toxicity, so it is important to follow professional guidance.
