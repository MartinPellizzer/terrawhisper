Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya) is a medicinal plant native to the Andean regions of South America, known for its use in treating malaria and other fevers due to the presence of quinine.

What is Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona Calisaya)?

Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya) is a medicinal plant native to the highlands of Peru, known for its use in traditional herbal medicine to treat fever, malaria, and digestive ailments.

Botanical Identity and Classification

Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya) is a species of the genus Cinchona, belonging to the family Rubiaceae, and is native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in Peru.

Common Names and Synonyms

Calisaya Cinchona, also known as Cinchona calisaya, is a species of plant native to the Amazon rainforest and is commonly referred to by various regional names such as Calisaya, Cinchona de Calisaya, and sometimes as a type of quina or quinine tree.

Plant Description and Morphology

Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya) is a shrub or small tree characterized by its upright, branched stems, dark green, elliptical leaves with a slightly waxy texture, and small, clustered yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the early summer.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya) is native to the cloud forests of the eastern Andes in northern Bolivia and southern Peru, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments at elevations between 1,200 and 2,000 meters.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The leaves, bark, and roots of Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya) are primarily used medicinally, with the bark being the most significant part due to its high concentration of alkaloids such as quinine.

Phytochemical Composition

The phytochemical composition of Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya) includes alkaloids such as quinine, cinchonine, and cinchonidine, along with flavonoids, tannins, and essential oils, which contribute to its traditional use in herbal medicine.

Pharmacological Properties

Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya) possesses pharmacological properties primarily attributed to its alkaloid content, including quinine and other related compounds, which exhibit antimalarial, antipyretic, and anti-inflammatory effects.

Mechanisms of Action

Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya) exerts its medicinal effects primarily through the alkaloids it contains, such as quinine and quinidine, which interact with parasitic organisms and human cells to disrupt cellular functions and inhibit the growth of pathogens.

Therapeutic Uses and Indications

Calisaya Cinchona is used to treat fever, malaria, digestive issues, respiratory infections, skin conditions, and as a tonic for overall health improvement.

Preparation Methods and Forms

Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya) is typically prepared as a tincture, powder, or decoction, with the most common form being a tincture made by soaking the dried bark in alcohol to extract its active compounds.

Dosage and Administration

The recommended dosage of Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya) for herbal use typically ranges from 200 to 400 mg of dried bark per day, divided into two or three doses, though specific dosing may vary based on individual health conditions and professional guidance.

Safety, Side Effects, and Contraindications

While Calisaya Cinchona is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort, dizziness, and in rare cases, liver toxicity, particularly with prolonged use or high dosages.

Drug Interactions

Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya) may interact with medications that affect the liver, such as anticoagulants, antidiabetics, and other drugs metabolized by the liver, potentially increasing the risk of toxicity or reducing the effectiveness of these medications.

Toxicity and Precautions

Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya) may cause gastrointestinal upset, dizziness, and skin irritation in some individuals, and should be used with caution, especially during pregnancy, lactation, or by individuals with pre-existing health conditions.

Cultivation and Harvesting

Cinchona calisaya is typically cultivated in the humid, shaded environments of tropical highlands, often in regions with well-drained soils and consistent moisture, requiring careful management of temperature and humidity to ensure optimal growth and alkaloid production.

Processing and Extraction

The processing of Calisaya Cinchona involves harvesting the bark of the plant, drying it under controlled conditions, and then grinding it into a fine powder to extract its active compounds, primarily quinidine and quinine, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Storage and Shelf Life

Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya) should be stored in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture to maintain its potency and effectiveness over time.

Scientific Research and Evidence

Scientific research on Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya) has primarily focused on its alkaloid content, particularly quinine, which has been historically significant for its antimalarial properties, though contemporary studies are limited and often emphasize its traditional use in herbal medicine.

Traditional and Historical Uses

Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya) has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its antimalarial properties, derived from the alkaloids present in its bark, which have historically been employed to treat fever and malaria.
