According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", the fruit of Thladiantha dubia is used in Chinese mancchu nationality folk medicine to treat rheumatic pain, lumbocrucal pain, and dysmenorrhea. When administered at a dose of 200mg/kg in mice, the Thladiantha dubia fruit crude polysaccharide fraction significantly reduced acetic acid-induced writhes and stretchings. This fraction also significantly inhibited xylene-induced ear oedema in mice and inhibited joint swelling in rats induced by CFA. The administration of this fraction had no effect on the body weight of rats. Histo-pathological analysis indicated that the fraction alleviated the degree of rheumatoid arthritis and preserved a nearly normal histological architecture of the joint.
