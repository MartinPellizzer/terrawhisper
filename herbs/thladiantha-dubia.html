Consequently, its multi-targeted approach provides a holistic mechanism for treating symptoms associated with systemic inflammation.

As discussed in a study, the fruit of Thladiantha dubia is used in Chinese manchu nationality folk medicine to treat various pains including rheumatic pain, lumbocrucal pain, and dysmenorrhea. The crude polysaccharide fraction of the fruit, TF-P, was tested at a dose of 200mg/kg and significantly reduced writhes and stretchings induced by acetic acid in mice. This TF-P fraction also significantly inhibited xylene-induced ear oedema in mice and inhibited both inoculated and non-inoculated joint swellings in rats induced by CFA. While TF-P had no effect on body weight in rats, histopathological analysis suggested it obviously alleviated the degree of rheumatoid arthritis and preserved a nearly normal histological architecture of the joint. These results confirm the traditional use of Thladiantha dubia for painful and inflammatory conditions and suggest therapeutic effects on RA rats induced by CFA.
