Botanical Identification of English Lavender (Lavandula angustifolia)

To identify English Lavender (Lavandula angustifolia) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

English Lavender, Lavandula angustifolia, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Lamiales, order Lamiales, family Lamiaceae, genus Lavandula, and species Lavandula angustifolia. These classifications reflect its position within the plant kingdom and its botanical relationships.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of English Lavender is Lavandula angustifolia, first described by Linnaeus in 1753. It is also known by several synonyms, including Lavandula officinalis and Lavandula hirta. Common names such as English lavender, true lavender, and garden lavender are widely used. Regional and historical names reflect its long use in herbal medicine and perfumery.

Morphological Characteristics

English Lavender (Lavandula angustifolia) is a perennial shrub with a compact, upright growth habit. It has a fibrous root system that anchors it firmly in soil. The stems are square and woody, often with a slight bluish-gray hue. Leaves are linear, aromatic, and gray-green with a fuzzy texture. The inflorescence is a spike of dense, spike-like flower clusters. Each flower has showy purple-lavender petals and a fragrant, narrow calyx. The fruit is a small, dry capsule containing numerous tiny seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Lavandula angustifolia can be distinguished by its narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescence with purple flowers. It has a strong aromatic scent and a square, hairy stem. It differs from Lavandula x intermedia by having smaller, more pointed leaves and a more compact growth habit.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Lavandula angustifolia is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly southern Europe and western Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with mild, wet winters and warm, dry summers. It prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils. It typically grows at altitudes between 200 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Lavandula angustifolia blooms from June to August, producing spikes of purple flowers. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early autumn. For identification purposes, harvesting is typically done when flowers are in full bloom, usually between June and July.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Lavandula angustifolia belongs to the genus Lavandula in the family Lamiaceae. It is closely related to species like Lavandula stoechas and Lavandula x intermedia. These species share similar morphological and chemical characteristics, reflecting their close phylogenetic ties within the genus.
