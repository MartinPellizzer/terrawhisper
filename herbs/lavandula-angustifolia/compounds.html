English Lavender (Lavandula angustifolia) Compounds

The primary active compounds in English lavender include terpenoids, which give it its aromatic properties, and flavonoids, known for their antioxidant effects. Phenolic acids and tannins contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Coumarin and anthocyanin also play roles in its therapeutic benefits.

English Lavender (Lavandula angustifolia) Monograph

1. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound of English Lavender (Lavandula angustifolia). It contains volatile aromatic compounds like linalool and linalyl acetate, which contribute to its calming and anti-inflammatory properties. The oil is widely used in herbal medicine for its soothing effects on the mind and skin.

2. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a major class of active compounds in English Lavender (Lavandula angustifolia). They contribute to the plant's aromatic profile and have therapeutic properties such as sedative, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial effects. These compounds are responsible for many of the plant's traditional and modern medicinal uses.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in English Lavender (Lavandula angustifolia). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in calming and soothing applications. These compounds help enhance the therapeutic effects of the plant in herbal medicine.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in English Lavender (Lavandula angustifolia). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in calming and soothing applications. This compound plays a vital role in the therapeutic effects of lavender in herbal medicine.

5. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in English Lavender (Lavandula angustifolia). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support skin health and wound healing. Tannins also help preserve the plant's medicinal qualities by stabilizing its volatile compounds.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a compound found in English Lavender (Lavandula angustifolia). It contributes to the plant's aromatic profile and has mild anticoagulant properties. In herbal medicine, coumarin may support circulatory health and is often used in formulations for its calming and soothing effects.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in English Lavender (Lavandula angustifolia). It contributes to the plant's calming and anti-inflammatory properties. Glycosides are often responsible for the therapeutic effects of lavender in herbal medicine. They may support stress relief and skin healing when used in essential oils or infusions.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in English Lavender (Lavandula angustifolia). It contributes to the plant's mild cleansing properties and may support skin health. Saponins can also aid in reducing inflammation and promoting respiratory wellness when used in herbal preparations.

9. Alkaloid

English Lavender (Lavandula angustifolia) contains alkaloids, which are nitrogen-containing compounds. While alkaloids are more commonly found in other plant families, they contribute to the plant's pharmacological profile. These compounds may influence the plant's therapeutic effects, though their role in English Lavender is less prominent compared to other active constituents.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in English Lavender (Lavandula angustifolia). It contributes to the plant's deep purple color and may have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Anthocyanin supports the plant's role in traditional herbal medicine by potentially aiding in skin healing and reducing oxidative stress.
