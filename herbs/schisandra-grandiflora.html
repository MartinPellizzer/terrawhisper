What's the taxonomical classification of Schisandra grandiflora?

Schisandra grandiflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Austrobaileyales. It is a member of the family Schisandraceae, falling specifically within the genus Schisandra, and is identified by its unique species designation, grandiflora.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds gomisin M1, gomisin B, schisanndrin, deoxyschizanndrin, and gamma-schizanndrin. These specific lignans are secondary metabolites that contribute to the plant's diverse pharmacological activities. Research suggests that these substances play significant roles in neuroprotection and antioxidant processes within the body. The presence of both gomisin derivatives and schisanndrin variants creates a complex chemical profile used in traditional medicine. Scientists continue to study how these individual molecules interact to provide therapeutic benefits.

As discussed in a study, Gomisin B (1) was isolated as the major phytochemical lead from Schisandra grandiflora. This plant is traditionally used in different Asian systems of medicine. Researchers synthesized a series of 1,2,3-triazole derivatives (5a-5q) at the C-7' position of the gomisin B core. Among these, compound 5b exhibited the best cytotoxicity against the SIHA cell with an IC50 of 0.24 μM. This activity was more than the standard drug doxorubicin.
