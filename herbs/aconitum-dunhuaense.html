What's the taxonomical classification of Aconitum dunhuaense?

Aconitum dunhuaense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent, it falls under the order Ranunculales and is a member of the family Ranunculaceae. Specifically, this plant is identified by the genus Aconitum, with its unique designation being the species dunhuaense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aconitum dunhuaense has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by upright stems, deeply palmately lobed leaves, and distinctive zygomorphic flowers featuring a prominent, helmet-shaped upper sepal. Its inflorescence consists of a dense raceme where individual flowers display a range of colors, often including shades of blue, purple, or white. The reproductive structures are tucked within the protective hood of the sepal, a common trait among the Ranunculaceae family. What side effects this plant can have?

These toxins primarily target the cardiovascular and nervous systems, leading to dangerous heart rhythm irregularities. As the poison progresses, it can trigger intense neurological symptoms and eventual systemic shutdown. If left untreated, the physiological distress often culminates in fatal respiratory or cardiac events.
