Dichaeantahera africana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. The plant is further categorized into the family Melastomataceae, falling under the genus Dichaeantahera, with its specific designation being the species africana.

Dichaetanthera africana is prepared primarily as a decoction made by boiling the roots or leaves in water to create a medicinal tea. What side-effects this plant can have?

Dichaetanthera africana can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or digestive distress if ingested or handled without protection. The plant contains bioactive compounds that may trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Contact with the sap can lead to redness, itching, or localized swelling on the skin. If the plant is consumed accidentally, it may result in stomach pain or nausea. Always use gloves when pruning this species to avoid potential dermatological issues.
