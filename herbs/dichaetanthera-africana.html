What's the taxonomical classification of Dichaetanthera africana?

Dichaeantahera africana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is positioned under the order Myrtales. The plant is further categorized into the family Melastomataceae, falling under the genus Dichaeantahera, with its specific designation being the species africana.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including skin infections, stomach pains, and respiratory issues. Traditional practitioners often utilize the plant to manage symptoms of intestinal parasites and digestive discomfort. The leaves or roots may be prepared as decoctions to help alleviate persistent coughs and congestion. Additionally, topical applications are sometimes employed to soothe skin irritations or treat minor wounds. These medicinal uses are deeply rooted in local traditional knowledge regarding the plant's chemical properties.
