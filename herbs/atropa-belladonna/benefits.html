10 Best Atropa Belladonna Health Benefits

Atropa belladonna, commonly known as deadly nightshade, contains potent alkaloids such as atropine, scopolamine, and hyoscyamine, which have been historically used in medicine for their anticholinergic properties.

These compounds can help alleviate symptoms of gastrointestinal disorders, such as irritable bowel syndrome, by reducing muscle spasms and slowing intestinal contractions. In ophthalmology, belladonna has been used to dilate the pupil for examining the eye's interior, although its use is now limited due to its toxicity. Despite its medicinal applications, the plant is highly poisonous, and ingestion can lead to severe side effects including hallucinations, respiratory failure, and even death.

Due to its dangerous nature, Atropa belladonna is typically only handled under strict medical supervision.
