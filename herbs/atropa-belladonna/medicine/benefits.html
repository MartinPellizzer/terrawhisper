1. Reduces saliva production

Atropa belladonna reduces saliva production because of its anticholinergic properties.

Specifically, the plant's alkaloids, such as atropine and scopolamine, bind to muscarinic receptors in the salivary glands, blocking the action of acetylcholine, a neurotransmitter that stimulates saliva secretion.

This blockade leads to decreased saliva production, resulting in dry mouth, also known as xerostomia.

2. Relaxes bronchial smooth muscles

Atropa belladonna relaxes bronchial smooth muscles because it contains a unique alkaloid compound called scopolamine.

This compound binds to muscarinic receptors on the surface of airway smooth muscle cells, causing them to relax and decrease in size. As a result, bronchial constriction is alleviated, allowing for easier breathing and improved lung function.

This property has made Atropa belladonna an important ingredient in traditional remedies for respiratory conditions such as asthma and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).

3. Accelerates heart rate

Atropa belladonna accelerates heart rate because it contains a group of toxic compounds called tropane alkaloids.

These alkaloids, particularly atropine and scopolamine, bind to certain receptors in the autonomic nervous system, leading to an increase in sympathetic activity and a subsequent acceleration of the heart rate.

This can result in tachycardia, or rapid heartbeat, which can be life-threatening if left untreated.

4. Dilates pupils

Atropa belladonna dilates pupils because it contains a potent alkaloid called scopolamine.

When ingested or administered topically, scopolamine binds to muscarinic receptors in the eye's parasympathetic nervous system, leading to an increase in sympathetic tone and pupil dilation. This effect is often used therapeutically to treat conditions such as mydriasis, where a doctor needs to dilate the pupils to examine the back of the eye.

However, the drug's potency and potential for toxicity make it unsuitable for recreational use.

5. Inhibits intestinal peristalsis

Atropa belladonna inhibits intestinal peristalsis because of its potent anticholinergic properties.

The alkaloids present in the plant, such as atropine and scopolamine, bind to muscarinic receptors in the smooth muscle of the intestine, reducing the contractions that normally facilitate the movement of food through the digestive tract.

This results in slowed or halted intestinal transit, which can lead to severe gastrointestinal symptoms including constipation, abdominal pain, and nausea.

6. Reduces stomach acid secretion

Atropa belladonna reduces stomach acid secretion because it inhibits the production of histamine, a neurotransmitter that stimulates the release of gastric acid.

The plant's active compounds, including scopolamine and hyoscyamine, bind to histamine receptors in the stomach, thereby reducing the stimulation of parietal cells to secrete hydrochloric acid.

This action helps alleviate symptoms of gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD) and peptic ulcers by decreasing gastric acidity.

7. Relaxes urinary bladder smooth muscle

Atropa belladonna relaxes urinary bladder smooth muscle because it contains tropane alkaloids such as atropine and scopolamine.

These compounds act on muscarinic receptors to reduce acetylcholine's contractile effects, leading to a decrease in smooth muscle tone.

This relaxation of the bladder smooth muscle helps to alleviate symptoms of overactive bladder, such as urinary frequency and urgency, making it a potential therapeutic target for treating lower urinary tract disorders.

8. Relieves bronchospasm

Atropa belladonna relieves bronchospasm because of its unique ability to relax smooth muscle tissue in the airways.

The plant's active compounds, particularly scopoletin and atropine, work by inhibiting the release of acetylcholine, a neurotransmitter that causes airway constriction. As a result, Atropa belladonna helps to open up constricted airways, reducing bronchospasm and alleviating symptoms such as wheezing and shortness of breath.

This property has led to its use in traditional medicine for the treatment of respiratory conditions.

9. Reduces respiratory tract secretions

Atropa belladonna reduces respiratory tract secretions because of its unique chemical composition.

The plant contains tropane alkaloids, particularly atropine and scopolamine, which have anticholinergic properties. These compounds bind to muscarinic receptors in the respiratory tract, thereby decreasing the production of mucus and other secretions.

This reduction in secretion helps to alleviate symptoms such as coughing, wheezing, and shortness of breath associated with conditions like bronchitis and asthma.

10. Slows gastric emptying

Atropa belladonna slows gastric emptying because it binds to muscarinic receptors in the smooth muscle of the stomach and intestines.

This binding action decreases acetylcholine's ability to stimulate gastric contractions, leading to delayed gastric emptying.

As a result, food remains in the stomach for a longer period, which can cause symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain.
