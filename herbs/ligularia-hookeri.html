Ligularia hookeri

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ligularia hookeri has large, broadly ovate to heart-shaped leaves that feature coarsely serrated margins and a distinctly cordate base. The leaf blades are often deeply veined, creating a textured surface that can be dark green on the upper side and paler underneath. These leaves are supported by long, sturdy petioles that arise from a rhizomatous root system. During the flowering stage, the plant produces terminal clusters of bright yellow capitula held atop tall, erect scapes. The overall structure is characterized by its lush, herbaceous foliage and upright reproductive stems.

The specific environmental requirements of the species limit its presence to these particular ecological niches within its regional range. What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Ligularia hookeri targets various inflammatory and apoptotic signaling proteins, including cyclooxygenase-2, tumor necrosis factor-alpha, and various caspases. These interactions occur primarily through bioactive compounds like sesquiterpene lactones and polyphenols present in the plant tissue. What are the therapeutic actions of Ligularia hookeri?

Ligularia hookeri has therapeutic properties that function as a diuretic, anti-inflammatory, hemostatic, and antipyretic agent.
