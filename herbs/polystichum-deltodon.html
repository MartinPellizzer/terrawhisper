Polystichum deltodon

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polystichum deltodon has a distinct morphology characterized by delta-shaped leaves, a robust central stem, and a complex branching pattern of lateral shoots. The foliage consists of thick, fleshy leaves that taper to a sharp point at the apex. These leaves are arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem to maximize sunlight absorption. The stem structure is woody at the base but becomes increasingly herbaceous toward the growing tips. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the upper leaves during the blooming season.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Polystichum deltodon can cause severe contact dermatitis, acute gastrointestinal distress, and localized neurological numbness upon exposure. These adverse reactions can escalate quickly if the plant is not handled with protective gear.
