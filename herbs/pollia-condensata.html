Pollia condensata

What's the taxonomical classification of Pollia condensata?

Pollia condensata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Magnoliidae and is positioned in the order Commelinaales. Within this order, it is the representative of the family Commelineaceae. Specifically, this plant is categorized under the genus Pollia, with the biological designation of the species condensata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pollia condensata has small, translucent, berry-like fruits that exhibit intense metallic blue coloration caused by specialized multilayered cell structures rather than pigments.

These fruits consist of tightly packed, scale-like bracts that overlap to form a compact, spherical shape. The brilliant blue appearance is a result of structural coloration, where microscopic layers of cellulose reflect specific wavelengths of light. This unique morphological adaptation allows the plant to attract birds through intense visual signals.

The fruit's surface texture is smooth and highly reflective, enhancing its visibility within the forest understory.
