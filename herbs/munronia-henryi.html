What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds Munroniin O, Munroinin V, limonoids, prieurianiin-type limonoids, and compound 1. These bioactive substances are primarily responsible for the various pharmacological properties observed in the species. Researchers have identified these specific chemical constituents through rigorous phytochemical analysis. The presence of prieurianiin-type limonoids and other limonoids suggests potential therapeutic applications for the plant. Detailed studies continue to investigate how compound 1 and the Munroonin series interact with biological systems.

According to a study published by "Pesesticide biochemistry and physiology", a series of limonoids 1-8 were isolated from the whole plant of Munronia henryi. Compound 1, known as Munronin O, showed the best protective activity with a 50% effective concentration value of 91.5 μg/mL. Compounds 7 and 8 demonstrated significant inactivating, protective, and curative activities against tobacco mosaic virus. Compound 1 also exhibited excellent induction activities for systemic acquired resistance and could inhibit the expression of TMV CP.
