Munronia henryi

What's the taxonomical classification of Munronia henryi?

Munronia henryi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical structure of botanical classification, it falls under the order Sapindales and is a member of the family Meliaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized within the genus Munronia, specifically identified as the species henryi.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including digestive issues, skin infections, and respiratory problems. Local communities often prepare decoctions from the leaves to alleviate stomach pains and dysentery. It is also frequently applied topically to soothe skin irritations or wounds. Some traditional practices involve using the plant to manage symptoms associated with common colds and coughs. These medicinal applications rely on the plant's bioactive properties to promote healing.
