Duhaldea eupatorioides

What's the taxonomical classification of Duhaldea eupatorioides?

Duhaldea eupatorioides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is positioned in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae, which is further refined into the genus Duhaldea, eventually reaching its specific designation as the species eupatorioides.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Duhaldea eupatorioides contains various bioactive phytochemicals including sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and essential oils that provide its therapeutic properties. These specific sesquiterpene lactones are primarily responsible for the plant's significant anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities. The presence of diverse flavonoids contributes to its potent antioxidant capacity, helping to neutralize free radicals within biological systems. Additionally, the essential oil components offer mild analgesic effects when used in traditional medicinal applications. Researchers continue to study these complex chemical profiles to better understand their potential in modern pharmacological developments.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various digestive ailments, respiratory issues, and inflammatory conditions through its medicinal properties. It is frequently applied to alleviate symptoms of gastrointestinal distress, such as stomach aches and indigestion. Practitioners also utilize its extracts to manage upper respiratory tract infections and coughs. Additionally, its anti-inflammatory qualities make it a candidate for addressing skin irritations and minor wounds. These therapeutic uses are primarily based on its traditional applications in herbal medicine.
