Duhaldea eupatorioides

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Duhaldea eupatorioides has an erect, perennial herbaceous habit characterized by slender, branching stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The foliage typically presents a grayish-green hue and possesses a slightly pubescent texture on both surfaces. Its inflorescence consists of small, terminal discoid flower heads arranged in loose, spreading clusters. These flowers are generally white or pale pink and emerge from involucres composed of multiple overlapping bracts. The plant's overall structure is delicate yet upright, reflecting its adaptation to open, rocky environments.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Duhaldea eupatorioides targets various inflammatory mediators and signaling enzymes, most notably inhibiting cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2), inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS), and various pro-inflammatory cytokines. What are the therapeutic actions of Duhaldea eupatorioides?

Duhaldea eupatorioides has therapeutic properties as a bitter, diuretic, cholagogue, stomachic, and aperient agent. It also functions as a stomachic to strengthen digestive processes and a diuretic to increase urine production. Furthermore, its aperient qualities help to gently stimulate bowel movements and relieve constipation.
