What's the taxonomical classification of Duhaldea eupatorioides?

Duhaldea eupatorioides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is positioned in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae, which is further refined into the genus Duhaldea, eventually reaching its specific designation as the species eupatorioides.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Duhaldea eupatorioides has an erect, perennial herbaceous habit characterized by slender, branching stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. Its inflorescence consists of small, terminal discoid flower heads arranged in loose, spreading clusters. These flowers are generally white or pale pink and emerge from involucres composed of multiple overlapping bracts. The plant's overall structure is delicate yet upright, reflecting its adaptation to open, rocky environments.
