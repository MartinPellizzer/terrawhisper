Geranium sinense

What's the taxonomical classification of Geranium sinense?

Geranium sinense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Geraniales and the family Geraniaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Geranium, and its unique species designation, sinense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Geranium sinense has upright, herbaceous stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters and feature deeply lobed, palmate leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is often covered in fine hairs, providing a soft texture to the plant's dense green canopy. Its flowers are actinomorphic and consist of five distinct petals that vary in color from soft pinks to vibrant purples. These blooms are supported by slender pedicels that emerge from the leaf axils during the peak growing season. The reproductive structures include a central column of stamens and a prominent ovary that eventually develops into a characteristic schizocarp fruit.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the subtropical and tropical regions of China, specifically distributed across provinces such as Yunnan, Guizhou, and Guangdong. It thrives primarily in moist, shaded environments found within these southern territories. Beyond its core native range, some specimens have been introduced to other parts of East Asia due to cultivation. The species prefers the warm, humid climates characteristic of these specific latitudes. Its presence is often tied to the seasonal monsoon patterns that define its natural habitat.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain, dizziness, tachycardia, and hypotension in individuals who consume it. These gastrointestinal issues are among the most frequently reported adverse reactions to the herb. Some users may also experience neurological or cardiovascular symptoms such as dizziness or an irregular heart rate. Changes in blood pressure, specifically hypotension, have also been noted in certain cases. It is important to monitor these physical responses closely when using this botanical substance.
