Geranium sinense

What's the taxonomical classification of Geranium sinense?

Geranium sinense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Geraniales and the family Geraniaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Geranium, and its unique species designation, sinense.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Geranium sinense contains various bioactive compounds including geraniin, gallic acid, ellagic acid, and several types of tannins. These chemical constituents provide the plant with significant antioxidant and antimicrobial properties used in traditional medicine. The high concentration of hydrolyzable tannins is particularly noted for its ability to act as an astringent. Such compounds are often utilized to treat gastrointestinal issues and inflammatory conditions. Research suggests that these specific phytochemicals may also play a role in protecting cells from oxidative stress.

What are the therapeutic actions of Geranium sinense?

Geranium sinense has potent antimicrobial, antifungal, and anti-inflammatory properties that allow it to combat various bacterial and fungal infections. The plant contains essential oils and bioactive compounds that help inhibit the growth of pathogens such as Staphylococcus aureus and various Candida species. It is frequently utilized in traditional medicine to reduce inflammation and soothe skin irritations. These therapeutic actions are often leveraged in topical applications to treat wounds and dermatological conditions. Furthermore, its phytochemical profile suggests potential benefits in supporting the immune system against oxidative stress.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various gastrointestinal disorders, specifically diarrhea, dysentery, and intestinal amoebiasis. It contains active compounds that help reduce inflammation and combat parasitic infections within the digestive tract. Beyond stomach issues, practitioners sometimes employ it to address respiratory ailments and certain types of fever. The herb is traditionally valued for its ability to clear heat and toxins from the body. Its medicinal application remains centered on its potent antimicrobial and antidiarrheal properties.

What are the herbal preparations of Geranium sinense?

Geranium sinense is prepared primarily as a dried powder, a concentrated liquid extract, or a decoction made by boiling the plant material in water. The dried herb is often finely ground to be encapsulated or mixed into other medicinal formulas. Liquid extracts are frequently produced through alcohol-based tinctures to ensure a high concentration of active constituents. Decoctions are traditional methods where the dried roots or leaves are simmered to release their medicinal properties into a drinkable liquid. These various forms allow for different speeds of absorption and varying dosages depending on the specific therapeutic requirement.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Geranium sinense can cause nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain, and dizziness when consumed in large amounts or by sensitive individuals. These gastrointestinal disturbances are the most common reactions reported by users of the plant. Some people may also experience allergic skin reactions or rashes if the plant comes into direct contact with the skin. Because the plant contains potent compounds, there is also a risk of liver toxicity if it is ingested excessively or over a long period. It is important to monitor for these symptoms to avoid more serious health complications.
