Neottopteris nidus

What's the taxonomical classification of Neottopteris nidus?

Neottopteris nidus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae. Following its taxonomic descent through the order Polypodiales, the plant is placed within the family Aspleniaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by the genus Neottopteris and the species nidus.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Neottopteris nidus contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including flavonoids, tannins, and saponins that contribute to its therapeutic properties. These specific chemical constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's documented antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. Research suggests that the phenolic compounds within the leaves can help neutralize free radicals in biological systems. Additionally, the presence of certain glycosides may provide antimicrobial benefits when used in traditional medicinal preparations. The complex synergy between these organic compounds forms the basis for its potential applications in pharmacology.
