Corydalis scaberula

What's the taxonomical classification of Corydalis scaberula?

Corydalis scaberula belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Ranunculales. As a member of the family Papaveraceae, it is further categorized into the genus Corydalis, and its specific biological identity is defined by its species name, scaberula.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Corydalis scaberula has slender, herbaceous stems that support deeply lobed, bluish-green leaves and zygomorphic flowers with long, nectar-filled spurs. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Corydalis scaberula contains several bioactive isoquinoline alkaloids, most notably protopine, corydaline, and scaberina, which serve as its primary medicinal constituents. These specific alkaloids are known to exert significant effects on the central nervous system and can influence pain perception. Research indicates that these compounds may possess sedative, analgesic, and antitussive properties when utilized in traditional medicinal applications. The chemical structure of these alkaloids allows them to interact with various neurotransmitter receptors in the body. What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Corydalis scaberula targets various neuroreceptors and enzymes through its primary bioactive isoquinoline alkaloids, specifically acting as potent ligands for GABA-A receptors and dopamine transporters. This complex interaction with diverse protein targets underlies the plant's multifaceted pharmacological profile in treating neurological discomfort.
