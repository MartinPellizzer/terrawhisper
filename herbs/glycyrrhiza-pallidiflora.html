Glycyrrhiza pallidiflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Glycyrrhiza pallidiflora?

Glycyrrhiza pallidiflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Fabales. This plant is a member of the family Fabaceae and is specifically categorized within the genus Glycyrrhiza, with the distinct species designation of pallidiflora.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Glycyrrhiza pallidiflora has a perennial, woody-based herbaceous habit characterized by pinnately compound leaves, small pale flowers, and elongated, cylindrical legume pods. The foliage consists of several leaflets that are typically lanceolate or oblong in shape with smooth margins. Its inflorescence features delicate, pale-colored blossoms that emerge from the axils of the leaves. The fruit develops into slender pods that contain numerous small seeds essential for its reproduction. These structural features are characteristic of the legume family and help distinguish it from closely related species.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It primarily inhabits desert scrublands and dry alkaline basins throughout states like Arizona, New Mexico, and Texas. Its range extends southward into the Mexican states of Sonora and Chihuahua where it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. How is this plant cultivated?

This plant Glycyrrhiza pallidiflora is cultivated in well-drained, sandy loam soils within temperate or subtropical climates that provide full sun exposure.

Growers must ensure consistent moisture levels throughout the growing season to support robust root development. What are the pharmacological activities of Glycyrrhiza pallidiflora?

This plant has potent anticancer and cytotoxic activities that demonstrate its therapeutic potential in oncology research. Specific bioactive compounds isolated from its roots trigger programmed cell death in various malignant cell lines. These phytochemicals disrupt the cellular machinery of cancer cells, leading to significant growth inhibition. Experimental studies confirm that its cytotoxic properties are effective against several types of human tumor cells. Continued investigation into these pharmacological mechanisms may lead to the development of new cancer treatments.

According to a study published by "Natural product research", twenty-one phenolic compounds including dihydrocinnamic acid, isoflavonoids, flavonoids, coumestans, pterocarpans, chalcones, isoflavan and isoflaven were isolated from the roots of Glycyrrhiza pallidiflora Maxim. Compounds 2-4, 10, 11, 19 and 21 were evaluated for cytotoxic activity against model cancer cell lines CEM-13, MT-4, and U-937. The isoflavonoid calycosin (4) demonstrated the best potency against human T-cell leukaeamia cells MT-4 with a CTD50 of 2.9 μM. Pterocarpans medicarpin (10) and homopterocarpin (11) exhibit anticancer activity in the micromolar range with selectivity on human monocyte cells U-937. Additionally, the isoflavan (3R)-vestitol (16) was highly selective on lymphoblastoid leukaeamia cells CEM-13 and proved more active than the drug doxorubicin.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds licoaagroside D, licoagroside E, licoagroside F, calycosin 7-O-glucoside, and 2'-hydroxyformononetin 7-O-glucoside. These specific phytochemicals are characteristic of the Glycyrrhiza pallidiflora species. The presence of these glycosides and neolignans contributes to the plant's complex chemical profile. Researchers often study these molecules to understand their potential therapeutic properties. Such detailed analysis helps identify the biological activities associated with this particular legume.

According to a study published by "Phytochemistry", three flavonoids named licoaagrosides D, E and F were isolated from Glycyrrhiza pallidiflora hairy root cultures. Along with these, four known flavonoids including medicarpin 3-O-glucoside, calycosin 7-O-glucoside, formononetin 7-O-(6"-malonylglucoside) and 2'-hydroxyformononetin 7-O-glucoside were also identified. Licoaagroside E represents the first example of a 6a-hydroxypterocarpan glycoside. Additionally, licoaagroside F is the first example of an alpha-O-glycosidic alpha-hydroxydihydrochalcone found in the cultures.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause hypertension, edema, hypokalemia, and muscle weakness due to the presence of glycyrrhizin which mimics the effects of aldosterone. Excessive consumption may lead to significant imbalances in electrolytes, particularly a dangerous drop in potassium levels. Such physiological changes can trigger heart palpitations or irregular rhythms in sensitive individuals. Prolonged use is also associated with increased sodium retention and water weight gain. These symptoms necessitate caution for those with pre-existing kidney or cardiovascular conditions.
