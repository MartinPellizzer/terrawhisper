10 Possible Side Effects Of Sida Cordifolia

Sida cordifolia has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal problems, including diarrhea, nausea, and abdominal pain.

These side effects can occur due to the herb's high saponin content, which can irritate the stomach lining.

In severe cases, these issues can worsen to the point of dehydration, weight loss, and fatigue, significantly impacting a person's daily activities and quality of life.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Sida cordifolia causes allergic reactions due to its high content of saponins and alkaloids.

These compounds can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and skin rashes in some individuals. Additionally, the plant's proteins may cause an allergic reaction in people with pre-existing sensitivities, resulting in respiratory issues like coughing and wheezing.

This hypersensitivity is thought to be caused by the similarity between Sida cordifolia's protein structures and those found in common allergens.

2. Triggers asthma attacks

Sida cordifolia triggers asthma attacks due to its ability to stimulate the respiratory system and cause inflammation in the airways.

This can lead to increased production of mucus, constriction of airway passages, and subsequent bronchospasm, which can trigger severe asthmatic symptoms.

The plant's phytochemicals, such as flavonoids and glycosides, may also exacerbate existing allergies and hypersensitivity reactions in individuals with asthma, further increasing the risk of attacks.

3. Induces vomiting

Sida cordifolia induces vomiting due to its strong antitussive and expectorant properties.

The plant's extracts have been found to stimulate the vagus nerve, which can cause a sudden contraction of the abdominal muscles, leading to nausea and vomiting.

Additionally, the presence of alkaloids in Sida cordifolia may also contribute to this side effect by irritating the stomach lining and triggering an adverse reaction.

4. Increases blood pressure

Sida cordifolia increases blood pressure due to its ability to stimulate the body's natural production of adrenaline.

This increase in adrenaline causes blood vessels to constrict, leading to a rise in blood pressure.

Additionally, Sida cordifolia can also cause an increase in heart rate and cardiac output, further contributing to elevated blood pressure levels.

5. Lowers blood sugar levels

Sida cordifolia lowers blood sugar levels due to its ability to inhibit alpha-glucosidase enzymes in the small intestine, thereby reducing glucose absorption.

Additionally, it may stimulate insulin secretion and increase pancreatic beta-cell function, further contributing to its hypoglycemic effect.

These mechanisms can lead to a decrease in blood sugar concentrations, potentially posing a risk for individuals with diabetes or hypoglycemia.

6. Interferes with fertility

Sida cordifolia interferes with fertility by affecting hormonal balance and altering ovulation patterns.

The plant's phytoestrogens mimic the effects of estrogen in the body, potentially disrupting the delicate balance of reproductive hormones necessary for healthy ovulation and fertilization. Additionally, Sida cordifolia's saponins may also bind to sperm cells, reducing their motility and fertility.

This interference can result in irregular menstrual cycles, reduced libido, or even infertility.

7. Affects liver function

Sida cordifolia affects liver function due to its ability to increase liver enzymes and alter bile production.

The bioactive compounds present in the herb, such as cardenolides and glycosides, may cause hepatotoxicity by damaging liver cells and disrupting normal liver function. This can lead to symptoms like jaundice, fatigue, and abdominal pain.

As Sida cordifolia interacts with the liver's natural detoxification processes, it may exacerbate existing liver issues or increase the risk of developing liver damage in susceptible individuals.

8. Disrupts digestive system

Sida cordifolia disrupts digestive system due to its ability to alter the gut microbiome and slow down digestion.

The plant's alkaloids can cause constipation or diarrhea by affecting the motility of the digestive tract and altering the balance of beneficial bacteria in the gut.

Additionally, Sida cordifolia has been known to increase the production of stomach acid, leading to heartburn and indigestion in some individuals.

9. Provokes kidney damage

Sida cordifolia provokes kidney damage due to its potential to cause inflammation and scarring in the kidneys.

The plant's high concentration of flavonoids and triterpenoids may lead to oxidative stress, which can damage the delicate tubular structures in the kidneys.

Additionally, Sida cordifolia's ability to alter gut microbiota may also contribute to kidney damage by disrupting the normal balance of beneficial bacteria that play a crucial role in maintaining renal health.

10. Enhances sedative effects

Sida cordifolia enhances sedative effects by interacting with GABA (gamma-aminobutyric acid), a neurotransmitter that promotes relaxation and calmness in the brain.

The plant's alkaloids, particularly saponins and flavonoids, are believed to bind to GABA receptors, increasing the inhibitory activity of GABA and leading to sedative-like effects.

This interaction can amplify the relaxing properties of Sida cordifolia, potentially causing drowsiness or lethargy in some individuals.
