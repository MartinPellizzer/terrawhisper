Echinops ritro

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Echinops ritro has spiny, globular flower heads that measure approximately 2 to 5 centimeters in diameter and are supported by stiff, silver-gray stems. These spherical inflorescences are composed of numerous small, tubular blue or violet florets arranged in a dense pattern. The foliage consists of deeply lobed, grayish-green leaves that possess a prickly texture along their margins. As the plant matures, the stems can reach heights of up to 1 meter, providing structural support for the heavy flower heads. The overall morphology is characterized by a metallic sheen and a rugged, architectural appearance.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Echinops ritro can cause allergic skin reactions, such as contact dermatitis, in individuals who are sensitive to plants in the Asteraceae family. People with existing allergies to ragweed, daisies, or marigolds may experience heightened sensitivity when handling this species. Contact with the foliage or flowers can lead to redness, itching, or localized swelling on the skin. While systemic toxicity is not widely documented, caution is advised for those with seasonal hay fever or pollen sensitivities. It is generally considered safe for ornamental use, but direct physical contact should be avoided by those with known plant allergies.
