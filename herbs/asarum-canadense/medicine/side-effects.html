10 Possible Side Effects Of Asarum Canadense (Canadian Snakeroot)

Asarum canadense, also known as Canadian snakeroot, has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal irritation, nausea, and diarrhea.

These symptoms are caused by the herb's saponin and iridoid compounds, which can irritate the digestive system.

In severe cases, these side effects can worsen lives by leading to dehydration, electrolyte imbalances, and malnutrition, ultimately affecting one's daily routine and overall well-being.
