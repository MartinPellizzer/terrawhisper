Botanical Identification of Spiny Amaranth (Amaranthus spinosus)

To identify Spiny Amaranth (Amaranthus spinosus) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Spiny Amaranth, Amaranthus spinosus, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Spermatophyta, class Caryophyllopsida, order Caryophyllales, family Amaranthaceae, genus Amaranthus, and species Amaranthus spinosus. These classifications highlight its relationship to other flowering plants and its placement within the Amaranth family.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Spiny Amaranth is Amaranthus spinosus, first described by the botanist L. K. Robbins in 1833. It is also known by several synonyms, including Amaranthus spinosus var. spiniflorus and Amaranthus spinosus subsp. spiniflorus. Common names include spiny amaranth, prickly amaranth, and desert amaranth, while regional names vary across different cultures and regions.

Morphological Characteristics

Spiny Amaranth is an annual herb with a taproot system. Its stems are erect, branching, and covered in spines. Leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and have serrated edges. The inflorescence is a spike-like cluster, with small green flowers that turn reddish. The fruit is a three-angled capsule containing triangular seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Spiny Amaranth can be distinguished by its spiny bracts and rough, triangular leaves. It has small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters. It grows upright with a fibrous root system. It is commonly found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Spiny Amaranth is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. It thrives in warm climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained soils and is commonly found at low to mid-elevation areas.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Spiny Amaranth blooms from late summer to early autumn, typically between July and September. Seed formation occurs from late summer through early winter, usually from August to November. For identification purposes, the plant is most easily recognized during its flowering stage, which is ideal for harvesting seeds.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Spiny Amaranth (Amaranthus spinosus) is closely related to other Amaranth species within the genus Amaranthus. It shares family traits with the Amaranthaceae family, including spine-like structures and small, clustered flowers. Its closest relatives include other spinous Amaranth species, which are often found in similar habitats.
