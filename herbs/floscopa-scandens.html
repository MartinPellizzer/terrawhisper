What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Floscopa scandens has slender, twining stems that emerge from a woody base and support small, lanceolate leaves arranged oppositely along the vines. The foliage typically features smooth margins and a vibrant green coloration that changes slightly with the age of the leaf. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils in clusters, displaying a delicate structure designed for specific pollinators. These blossoms are often accompanied by tiny, inconspicuous buds that develop sequentially throughout the growing season. The overall growth habit allows the plant to climb rapidly over surrounding vegetation using its flexible stems for support.

      What are the herbal preparations of Floscopa scandens?

      Floscopa scandens is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice depending on the specific therapeutic goal. Practitioners often boil the woody stems and roots to create a potent decoction for internal use. For more delicate applications, the leaves are steeped in hot water to form a mild infusion.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Floscopa scandens can cause severe contact dermatitis, localized swelling, and intense skin irritation upon physical contact with its sap or foliage. These dermatological reactions often manifest as red, itchy rashes that may blister if the exposure is prolonged. Some individuals might also experience mild systemic symptoms such as nausea or headaches if the plant is accidentally ingested. It is important to avoid touching the stems and leaves to prevent these adverse effects. Proper protective gear should always be worn when working in areas where this climber is present.
