Oxytropis bicolor

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Oxytropis bicolor has a perennial growth habit characterized by erect stems that reach heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and are covered in fine, silvery-white hairs. The leaves are pinnately compound, typically featuring 7 to 15 narrow, linear leaflets that contribute to a soft, pubescent appearance. Its most distinctive morphological feature is the bicolor flower, which displays a deep purple or violet standard petal contrasted against lighter, often whitish or pale lavender wing and keel petals. The calyx is notably hairy and serves to protect the developing reproductive structures within the legume family. Small, light-colored pods follow the blooming period, completing the plant's reproductive morphology.
