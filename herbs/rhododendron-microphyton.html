Rhododendron microphyton

What's the taxonomical classification of Rhododendron microphyton?

Rhododendron microphyton belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its progression through the order Ericales, it is placed within the family Ericaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by the genus Rhododendron and is specifically designated by the species name microphyton.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhododendron microphyton has small, elliptical to obovate leaves that typically measure between 5 and 15 millimeters in length and are characterized by a coriaceous texture. These leaves are often densely packed along the woody stems, appearing somewhat crowded and spreading. The foliage is generally leathery and can exhibit a range of green hues depending on the specific environmental conditions. Its flowering structure consists of small, bell-shaped blossoms that emerge from the terminal ends of the branches. The plant maintains a compact, shrubby growth habit suitable for its high-altitude habitat.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the high-altitude regions of the eastern Himalayas, specifically spanning parts of Bhutan, northeast India, and southern Tibet. It thrives primarily in montane shrublands and subalpine zones located at elevations ranging from 3,000 to 4,500 meters. The species prefers acidic soils found along rocky slopes and forest edges where moisture is consistent. Due to its specialized habitat requirements, its population is restricted to these specific mountain corridors. How is this plant cultivated?

It thrives in partial shade where humidity remains consistently high and temperatures stay cool. Regular misting or the use of a humidity tray helps prevent the delicate foliage from drying out. Watering should be frequent enough to keep the root zone moist but never saturated to avoid fungal rot.

Proper drainage is essential to ensure the root system receives adequate oxygen while staying hydrated.
