Senna petersiana

Senna petersiana is a plant species native to tropical Africa, belonging to the legume family (Fabaceae), and is commonly known as the 'naked senna' due to its leafless stem.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, particularly in treating constipation and digestive issues, due to its high content of anthraquinone glycosides, which have a laxative effect.

The health benefits of senna petersiana include relief from symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome, improved gut health, and enhanced bowel movements.

Senna petersiana can be prepared as a herbal tea by infusing the leaves and pods in hot water, or as a powder by drying and grinding the plant material, although its use should be limited and under medical supervision due to potential side effects.
