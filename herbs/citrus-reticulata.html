Citrus reticulata

Mandarin Orange (Citrus reticulata) is a small citrus fruit native to Southeast Asia, known for its vibrant orange peel and sweet, segmented fruit. It is widely used in traditional medicine for its potential to improve digestion and support respiratory health.

Botanical Identification

Mandarin Orange is scientifically known as Citrus reticulata and belongs to the Rutaceae family. Common names include mandarin and kumquat, with regional variations. It is a perennial plant native to East Asia. The tree has a fibrous root system, elliptical leaves, and fragrant white flowers. Synonyms in herbal literature include Citrus nobilis and Citrus sinensis.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Eudicots
  • Order: Sapindales
  • Family: Rutaceae
  • Genus: Citrus
  • Species: Citrus reticulata

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory conditions such as coughs and colds.

Essential oil: Used for skin conditions and as a natural disinfectant.

Capsule: Used for general health support and digestion.

Safety Profile

Mandarin orange is generally safe when used in culinary or medicinal doses. However, it may cause allergic reactions in some individuals. It is contraindicated for those with known citrus allergies or hypersensitivity. Always consult a healthcare provider before use, especially during pregnancy or with chronic conditions.
