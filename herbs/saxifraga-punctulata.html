Saxifraga punctulata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Saxifragales and the family Saxifragaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Saxifraga, with its specific identification being the species punctulata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saxifraga punctulata has a perennial growth habit characterized by a basal rosette of small, spoon-shaped leaves that are dark green with distinct reddish-purple spots. From the center of the rosette, a slender, leafless stem rises to support a small cluster of flowers. The blossoms are typically white or pale pink with five petals and are marked by tiny dark spots on the petal surfaces.
