Scorzonera divaricata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Scorzonera divaricata has a perennial growth habit characterized by a woody base, deeply pinnatisect leaves, and bright yellow flower heads. The leaves are typically divided into narrow, linear segments that grow in a basal rosette or along ascending stems. Its flowering stems are often branched and bear heads composed of numerous yellow ligulate florets. The plant frequently develops a tufted appearance due to its dense, spreading foliage. Small, cylindrical achenes serve as the primary means of seed dispersal.
