Scorzonera divaricata

What's the taxonomical classification of Scorzonera divaricata?

Scorzonera divaricata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Asterales. This plant is a representative of the family Asteraceae, a large group of flowering plants, and is specifically categorized under the genus Scorzonera. Its unique botanical identity is completed by its specific designation as the species divaricata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Scorzonera divaricata has a perennial growth habit characterized by a woody base, deeply pinnatisect leaves, and bright yellow flower heads.

The leaves are typically divided into narrow, linear segments that grow in a basal rosette or along ascending stems. Its flowering stems are often branched and bear heads composed of numerous yellow ligulate florets. The plant frequently develops a tufted appearance due to its dense, spreading foliage.

Small, cylindrical achenes serve as the primary means of seed dispersal.
