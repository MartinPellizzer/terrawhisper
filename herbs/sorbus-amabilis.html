Sorbus amabilis

What's the taxonomical classification of Sorbus amabilis?

Sorbus amabilis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is a member of the order Rosales and the family Rosaceae. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Sorbus, with its specific designation being the species amabilis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sorbus amabilis has pinnate leaves consisting of several pairs of elliptic to ovate leaflets with serrated margins and a woody structure characterized by a spreading crown. The foliage typically displays a bright green color during the growing season and may transition to deeper tones in autumn. Small, creamy white flowers emerge in dense, flat-topped clusters known as corymbs during the spring flowering period. These blossoms eventually give way to clusters of bright orange or red pomes that serve as a primary feature of the tree. The bark of the specimen becomes textured and fissured as the plant matures into a small to medium-sized tree.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?
