Nanocnide japonica

What's the taxonomical classification of Nanocnide japonica?

Nanocnide japonica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is situated under the order Rosales. Its taxonomic lineage continues into the family Urticaceae, where it is specifically categorized under the genus Nanocnide, with the species designation japonica.

      What are the herbal preparations of Nanocnide japonica?

      Nanocnide japonica is prepared as a decoction, a powder, or a topical paste to facilitate its medicinal use. The dried roots and stems are most commonly boiled in water to create a concentrated liquid extract known as a decoction. For long-term storage or easier administration, the plant material is ground into a fine powder that can be mixed with warm water. Additionally, the plant can be crushed into a thick paste and applied directly to the skin for external treatments. These various forms allow practitioners to tailor the dosage and delivery method to specific therapeutic needs.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Nanocnide japonica can cause severe poisoning through the ingestion of its cyanogenic glycosides, which release toxic hydrogen cyanide into the bloodstream. These toxins interfere with the body's ability to utilize oxygen at a cellular level. Symptoms of exposure often include headache, dizziness, nausea, and rapid breathing. In more extreme cases, individuals may experience convulsions, loss of consciousness, or even death. Immediate medical attention is necessary if any part of the plant is consumed.
