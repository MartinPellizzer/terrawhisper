Botanical Identification of Butterfly Bush (Buddleja officinalis)

To identify Butterfly Bush (Buddleja officinalis) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

The Butterfly Bush, Buddleja officinalis, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Oleales, family Oleaceae, genus Buddleja, and species Buddleja officinalis. This classification places it within the flowering plants and highlights its botanical relationships and characteristics.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The botanical name Buddleja officinalis was first described by the Swedish botanist Peter Forsskål in 1775. It is also known by several synonyms, including Buddleja asiatica and Buddleja asiatica var. officinalis. Common names include butterfly bush, blue bush, and blue ball. It is widely recognized in herbal medicine for its therapeutic properties.

Morphological Characteristics

Butterfly Bush (Buddleja officinalis) is a shrub with a woody stem and a bushy growth habit. It has a fibrous root system that anchors it firmly. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have serrated edges. The inflorescence is a panicle of clustered flowers, which are tubular and fragrant, attracting butterflies. The fruit is a capsule containing small, flat seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Butterfly Bush (Buddleja officinalis) can be distinguished by its elongated, cylindrical flower clusters that hang in spikes, typically pink to purple in color. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and slightly hairy. It lacks the fuzzy, glandular hairs found on the stems of similar species, making it easier to identify in the field. The plant also has a distinctive, aromatic scent when crushed.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Butterfly Bush (Buddleja officinalis) is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils. It is typically found at altitudes between 500 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Butterfly Bush (Buddleja officinalis) blooms from late spring to early autumn, typically between May and September. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early fall, around August to October. For identification purposes, harvest timing is best during the flowering period to ensure accurate recognition of the plant's key features.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Buddleja officinalis belongs to the genus Buddleja, closely related to species like Buddleja davidii. It shares characteristics with other members of the Buddlejaceae family, such as opposite leaves and clustered flowers. Its phylogenetic position highlights its evolutionary ties to other Buddleja species within the Scrophulariaceae family.
