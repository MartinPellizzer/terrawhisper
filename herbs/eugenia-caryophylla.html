Cloves (Eugenia caryophylla)

Cloves (Eugenia caryophylla) is a member of the Myrtaceae family, native to South East Asia, Australia, and Pacific Islands. Traditionally, its fruits, leaves, and bark have been used for infusions, decoctions, and culinary uses.

This herb is particularly valued for its astringent, carminative, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in traditional chinese medicine, japanese kampo medicine, and korean traditional medicine.
