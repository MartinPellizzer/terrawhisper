Eugenia Caryophylla: Medicinal Uses And Benefits

Eugenia caryophylla, commonly known as clove, is a flowering plant native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia, widely used for its aromatic properties.

This herb is renowned for its strong, spicy scent and is primarily valued for its medicinal and culinary applications.

Health benefits of clove include its ability to alleviate pain, reduce inflammation, and support dental health due to its antimicrobial properties.

Therapeutic actions of Eugenia caryophylla encompass antiseptic, analgesic, and carminative effects, while its bioactive constituents include eugenol, which is responsible for many of its pharmacological activities.
