Cloves

Eugenia caryophylla

Cloves (Eugenia caryophylla) is a small evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia, known for its aromatic flower buds used as a spice and traditional medicine. Its primary medicinal uses include improving digestion by stimulating the production of digestive enzymes and relieving symptoms of gastrointestinal disorders such as bloating and indigestion.

Botanical Identification

Cloves are the dried flower buds of Eugenia caryophylla, a species in the Myrtaceae family. Commonly known as clove sticks or clove buds, they are native to the Maluku Islands of Indonesia. The plant is a small evergreen tree with aromatic leaves and clusters of red flowers that develop into the spice used in herbal medicine.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Eudicots
  • Order: Sapindales
  • Family: Myrtaceae
  • Genus: Eugenia
  • Species: Eugenia caryophylla

Active Compounds

Cloves contain essential oil, flavonoids, and tannins as their main bioactive compounds. The essential oil, rich in eugenol, contributes to its medicinal properties. These compounds work together to provide anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects.

  • Essential oil
  • Flavonoid
  • Tannin

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory issues and digestive ailments.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and as an antiseptic.

Essential oil: Used for aromatherapy and to relieve headaches.

Safety Profile

Cloves are generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, they should be avoided during pregnancy and by individuals with bleeding disorders due to their potential anticoagulant effects. High doses may cause gastrointestinal irritation or allergic reactions in sensitive individuals.
