Heather (Calluna vulgaris) Compounds

Heather contains several key compounds, including flavonoids, which act as antioxidants, and tannins, which have astringent properties. Phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory effects, while glycosides and terpenoids support its overall medicinal profile. Coumarin and saponins add additional therapeutic benefits, and essential oils and anthocyanins enhance its aromatic and protective qualities.

Heather (Calluna vulgaris) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Heather (Calluna vulgaris) are bioactive compounds known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. They contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and skin conditions. These compounds help protect cells from oxidative stress and may support immune function. Their presence enhances the therapeutic potential of Heather in herbal medicine.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Heather (Calluna vulgaris). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is used in herbal medicine for its ability to reduce inflammation and support digestive health. Tannins in Heather are also believed to have antimicrobial effects.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Heather (Calluna vulgaris). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments. This compound helps reduce inflammation and may enhance the plant's therapeutic effects in herbal medicine.

4. Glycoside

Glycosides in Heather (Calluna vulgaris) are bioactive compounds with potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. They contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and skin conditions. These compounds are often extracted and used in herbal formulations. Their presence supports Heather's role in herbal medicine.

5. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a key class of active compounds in Heather (Calluna vulgaris). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for respiratory and skin conditions. These compounds are also responsible for the plant's distinctive aroma and may play a role in its adaptogenic effects.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a bioactive compound found in Heather (Calluna vulgaris). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine, particularly for its antispasmodic and mild sedative properties. Coumarin may also support cardiovascular health and has been studied for its potential anti-inflammatory effects.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Heather (Calluna vulgaris). It exhibits mild detergent properties and may support digestive health. Found in the plant's flowers and leaves, saponin contributes to Heather's traditional use in herbal remedies. Its presence enhances the plant's overall therapeutic profile.

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of Heather (Calluna vulgaris) contains aromatic compounds like pinocarvyl acetate and other terpenoids. These oils are used in herbal medicine for their calming and antiseptic properties. They are often employed in aromatherapy and topical applications to support respiratory and skin health.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Heather (Calluna vulgaris). It contributes to the plant's vibrant flower color and has antioxidant properties. This compound supports the plant's role in traditional herbal medicine, particularly for its potential anti-inflammatory and immune-boosting effects.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Heather (Calluna vulgaris). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies. Alkaloids in Heather are known for their potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects, supporting its use in herbal medicine for respiratory and skin conditions.
