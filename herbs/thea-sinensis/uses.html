Green Tea (Thea sinensis) Uses

Green tea is used to treat several respiratory and digestive conditions. It helps alleviate symptoms of acute bronchitis, asthma, and influenza by reducing inflammation and boosting the immune system. It also supports managing allergic rhinitis, cough, and respiratory infections. For digestive issues, it aids in treating gastric ulcers, stomach ulcers, gastritis, and gastroesophageal reflux disease due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Green Tea (Thea sinensis) Monograph

1. Acute bronchitis

Green tea contains catechins, particularly epigallocatechin gallate (EGCG), which exhibit anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. These compounds may help reduce mucus production and soothe respiratory tract inflammation in acute bronchitis. While not a substitute for medical treatment, green tea can support recovery when used alongside conventional care.

2. Asthma

Green tea contains bioactive compounds like EGCG and caffeine, which may help reduce airway inflammation and bronchoconstriction in asthma. Studies suggest it could enhance lung function and reduce oxidative stress, though more research is needed to confirm its efficacy as a complementary therapy.

3. Influenza

Green tea contains catechins, particularly epigallocatechin gallate (EGCG), which exhibit antiviral properties. Studies suggest it may inhibit influenza virus replication by reducing viral entry into host cells. It is often used as a supportive remedy during flu seasons to boost immunity and alleviate symptoms.

4. Allergic rhinitis

Green tea contains compounds like catechins, which may reduce inflammation and histamine release, common in allergic rhinitis. Studies suggest it can alleviate symptoms such as sneezing and nasal congestion. Its antioxidant properties support immune function, potentially reducing allergic reactions. Regular consumption may offer a natural complement to conventional treatments.

5. Cough

Green tea is used to soothe coughs due to its mild expectorant properties and antioxidant content. It helps loosen mucus and reduce throat irritation. The polyphenols in green tea may also have anti-inflammatory effects. Regular consumption can support respiratory health and ease persistent coughing.

6. Respiratory infections

Green Tea (Thea sinensis) contains catechins, particularly epigallocatechin gallate (EGCG), which exhibit antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds help reduce symptoms and duration of respiratory infections by inhibiting viral replication and supporting immune function. Regular consumption may lower the risk of upper respiratory tract infections.

7. Gastric ulcer

Green tea contains catechins, particularly epigallocatechin gallate (EGCG), which have been shown to reduce stomach acid and protect the gastric lining. Studies suggest it may aid in the healing of gastric ulcers by inhibiting harmful bacteria and promoting tissue repair. Its mild anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties support its traditional use in digestive health.

8. Stomach ulcer

Green Tea (Thea sinensis) may help in the treatment of stomach ulcers due to its high concentration of polyphenols, particularly catechins, which have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds can reduce gastric acid secretion and protect the stomach lining, promoting healing and preventing further damage.

9. Gastritis

Green tea has been traditionally used to support digestive health, particularly in managing gastritis. Its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties help reduce stomach lining irritation and combat harmful bacteria like H. pylori. Regular consumption may alleviate symptoms such as bloating and indigestion, though it should complement, not replace, medical treatment.

10. Gastroesophageal reflux disease

Green tea contains catechins, particularly epigallocatechin gallate, which reduce stomach acid and relax esophageal muscles. Studies suggest it may alleviate GERD symptoms by improving digestion and reducing inflammation. However, it should complement, not replace, medical treatment for severe cases.
