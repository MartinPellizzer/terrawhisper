Green Tea (Thea sinensis) Compounds

The primary compounds in green tea include catechins, which are a type of flavonoid known for their antioxidant properties. Flavonoids like quercetin and kaempferol also contribute to its health benefits. Phenolic acids and tannins provide additional antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. These compounds work together to support overall wellness.

Green Tea (Thea sinensis) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Green Tea (Thea sinensis). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant properties and may support cardiovascular and inflammatory health. These compounds are naturally present in the leaves and are often extracted for their therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Green Tea (Thea sinensis). It contributes to the tea's antioxidant properties by scavenging free radicals and reducing oxidative stress. This compound supports overall health by enhancing cellular protection and may aid in inflammation reduction.

3. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Green Tea (Thea sinensis). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by supporting metabolic processes and acting as a mild antioxidant. Glycosides in green tea are often associated with improved cardiovascular health and anti-inflammatory effects.

4. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound found in Green Tea (Thea sinensis). It contributes to the tea's astringent taste and has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Tannins in green tea may support digestive health and help in reducing oxidative stress. They are also believed to play a role in the tea's traditional use for detoxification and overall wellness.

5. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Green Tea (Thea sinensis). They contribute to the plant's aroma and have various biological activities, including antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. These compounds play a role in the therapeutic properties of Green Tea, supporting its use in traditional herbal medicine.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Green Tea (Thea sinensis). It contributes to the tea's bitter taste and has mild detoxifying properties. Saponins may support liver function and aid in the elimination of toxins from the body. They are part of the complex profile of bioactive compounds in green tea.

7. Coumarin

Coumarin is a compound found in Green Tea (Thea sinensis). It contributes to the plant's mild anticoagulant properties and may support cardiovascular health. However, its presence is minimal compared to other bioactive compounds like catechins. Coumarin's role in Green Tea is secondary to its more prominent constituents.

8. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Green Tea (Thea sinensis). While alkaloids are more commonly associated with other medicinal plants, Green Tea contains minimal amounts of alkaloids, primarily caffeine. These compounds contribute to the tea's stimulant effects and are part of its broader pharmacological profile.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Green Tea (Thea sinensis). It acts as an antioxidant and has anti-inflammatory properties. This compound contributes to the tea's potential health benefits, including supporting cardiovascular health and reducing oxidative stress.

10. Essential oil

Essential oil is a volatile compound found in Green Tea (Thea sinensis). It contains aromatic compounds like linalool and geraniol, which contribute to the tea's distinctive scent. While not the primary active component for health benefits, essential oil plays a role in traditional uses and may support mood and digestive health.

11. Catechin

Catechin is a key active compound found in Green Tea (Thea sinensis). It contributes to the tea's antioxidant properties and is believed to support cardiovascular health and reduce inflammation. Catechins are also linked to improved metabolic function and may aid in weight management.
