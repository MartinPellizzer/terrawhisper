Botanical Identification of Green Tea (Thea sinensis)

To identify Green Tea (Thea sinensis) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Green Tea (Thea sinensis) Monograph

Scientific Classification

Green Tea, Thea sinensis, belongs to the plant kingdom, classified under Magnoliophyta, Magnoliopsida, Theales, Theaceae family, genus Thea, and species Thea sinensis. This classification reflects its position within the broader context of flowering plants and its specific botanical lineage.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Green Tea is Thea sinensis, which was first described by the botanist Carolus Linnaeus in the 18th century. This name reflects its origin in China, where it has been cultivated for centuries. It is also known by various synonyms, common names, and regional terms that highlight its cultural and historical significance in herbal medicine.

Morphological Characteristics

Green tea (Thea sinensis) is a shrub or small tree with a fibrous root system. Its stems are erect and branched, often covered in fine hairs. Leaves are lanceolate, serrated, and have a glossy surface. The inflorescence is a cluster of small white flowers. The fruit is a spherical capsule containing several small, flat seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Green Tea (Thea sinensis) can be distinguished by its lanceolate leaves with a serrated margin, opposite leaf arrangement, and the presence of a single flower cluster with white petals. It differs from similar species like Camellia by its smaller, more delicate leaves and the absence of a hairy stem. The characteristic tea aroma when crushed also helps in identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Green Tea (Thea sinensis) is native to East Asia, particularly China, Japan, and Korea. It thrives in a subtropical to temperate climate with mild winters and warm summers. The plant prefers well-drained, slightly acidic soils and is typically grown at altitudes between 300 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Green Tea (Thea sinensis) blooms in early spring, typically between February and April, depending on the climate. Seed formation occurs after flowering, usually in late summer to early autumn. Harvesting takes place during the spring and summer months, focusing on the tender new leaves for optimal flavor and quality.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Green Tea (Thea sinensis) belongs to the genus Thea within the family Theaceae. It is closely related to species like Thea flavinervis and Thea flavida. These species share similar morphological and genetic traits, reflecting their close phylogenetic relationship within the genus.
