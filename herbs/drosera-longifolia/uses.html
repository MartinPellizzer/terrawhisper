Long-Leaved Sundew (Drosera longifolia) Uses

Long-Leaved Sundew is traditionally used to treat respiratory conditions such as bronchitis, sinusitis, and asthma. It helps reduce inflammation and mucus buildup in the respiratory tract, easing symptoms of cough and lung congestion. Its properties may also support in managing allergic rhinitis and other respiratory infections.

Long-Leaved Sundew (Drosera longifolia) Monograph

1. Bronchitis

Drosera longifolia is used in traditional herbal medicine to treat bronchitis due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. It helps reduce mucus buildup and soothe irritated airways, making it a valued remedy for respiratory conditions. The plant's active compounds support clearing congestion and easing breathing in cases of chronic bronchial inflammation.

2. Respiratory tract inflammation

Drosera longifolia is used to reduce inflammation in the respiratory tract due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps alleviate symptoms such as coughing and congestion, supporting healing in conditions like bronchitis and pneumonia. Its mucilage content soothes irritated tissues, promoting respiratory comfort and recovery.

3. Respiratory tract infections

Drosera longifolia has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in treating symptoms of respiratory tract infections. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties may help reduce mucus production and combat bacterial or viral pathogens. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to ease congestion and soothe irritated airways.

4. Sinusitis

Drosera longifolia is used in herbal medicine to treat sinusitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion and combat bacterial infections in the sinuses. Often prepared as a tincture or infusion, it supports respiratory health and alleviates symptoms associated with chronic sinus inflammation.

5. Asthma

Drosera longifolia has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health. Its expectorant properties help clear mucus from the airways, making it beneficial for managing asthma symptoms. It is often prepared as a tincture or tea to ease breathing and reduce inflammation in the lungs.

6. Cough

Drosera longifolia is used to treat coughs due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps loosen mucus and reduce irritation in the respiratory tract. Traditionally, it is prepared as a tea or tincture to alleviate persistent or chronic cough symptoms. Its action supports clearing the airways and easing breathing.

7. Allergic rhinitis

Drosera longifolia has been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistaminic properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion and sneezing by inhibiting histamine release. Its mucilage content soothes irritated mucous membranes, providing relief in respiratory inflammation.

9. Lung congestion

Drosera longifolia is used to alleviate lung congestion by promoting mucus drainage and reducing inflammatory swelling. Its expectorant properties help clear respiratory passages, making it beneficial for conditions like bronchitis and asthma. It is typically prepared as a tea or tincture for internal use.

10. Mucus buildup

Long-Leaved Sundew is used to address mucus buildup due to its demulcent and expectorant properties. It helps soothe irritated respiratory passages and promote the expulsion of excess mucus. This makes it beneficial for conditions like bronchitis and sinus congestion. Its mucilage content coats and protects the mucous membranes.
