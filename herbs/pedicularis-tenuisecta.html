What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pedicularis tenuisecta has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along a slender, erect stem. The flowering stalks can reach significant heights, supporting dense clusters of tubular, bilabiate flowers. The corollas are generally pale pink or lavender and feature a prominent upper lip that protects the reproductive organs. This overall delicate architecture allows the plant to thrive in alpine and subalpine environments.
