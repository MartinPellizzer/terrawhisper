Pedicularis tenuisecta

What's the taxonomical classification of Pedicularis tenuisecta?

Pedicularis tenuisecta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is classified under the order Lamiales. This specific plant is a part of the family Orobanchaceae and is further organized into the genus Pedicularis, ultimately reaching its unique designation as the species tenuisecta.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pedicularis tenuisecta has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along a slender, erect stem.

These leaves are characterized by their thin, almost thread-like shape and often possess small, inconspicuous teeth along the margins. The flowering stalks can reach significant heights, supporting dense clusters of tubular, bilabiate flowers. The corollas are generally pale pink or lavender and feature a prominent upper lip that protects the reproductive organs.

This overall delicate architecture allows the plant to thrive in alpine and subalpine environments.

    What are the herbal preparations of Pedicularis tenuisecta?

    Pedicularis tenuisecta is prepared primarily as a decoction made from the dried aerial parts of the plant or as an infusion using the leaves and stems. Contact with the plant may trigger localized allergic reactions or dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Ingesting even small amounts could lead to nausea, vomiting, or more severe systemic effects depending on the individual's sensitivity. Therefore, caution should always be exercised when handling or consuming this species.
