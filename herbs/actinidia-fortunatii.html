Actinidia fortunatii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Actinidia fortunatii has woody, climbing vines characterized by opposite, simple, ovate to broadly elliptic leaves with entire or slightly serrated margins and prominent venation. The foliage typically features a bright green surface, while the underside may exhibit a more matte texture. During the flowering stage, the plant produces small, inconspicuous white or yellowish flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These blossoms eventually give way to fleshy, globose to ovoid fruits that transition from green to a yellowish or brownish hue upon ripening. The overall growth habit is vigorous and sprawling, allowing the plant to cover large areas of canopy or ground cover.
