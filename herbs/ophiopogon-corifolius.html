Ophiopogon Orchid (Ophiopogon Corifolius)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Ophiopogon corifolius.

Ophiopogon orchid, scientifically known as Ophiopogon corifolius, is a medicinal herb native to East Asia, prized for its therapeutic properties in traditional medicine. This adaptogen is primarily used to support respiratory health, enhance immune function, and promote overall vitality due to its rich content of saponins and polysaccharides. In traditional Chinese medicine, it has been historically used to treat coughs, dryness, and heat-related ailments, while in Korean culture, it is often incorporated into herbal teas and tonics for its calming and restorative effects. Modern wellness applications include its use in supplements and functional foods to support stress resilience and digestive health. Its unique feature is the presence of a rare active compound called ophiopogonin, which contributes to its ability to nourish the lungs and moisten the body, making it a valuable herb in both ancient and contemporary health practices.
