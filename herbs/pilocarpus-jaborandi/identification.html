To identify Jaborandi (Pilocarpus jaborandi) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Jaborandi, scientifically known as Pilocarpus jaborandi, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Sapindales, family Rutaceae, genus Pilocarpus, and species Pilocarpus jaborandi. This classification places it within the flowering plants, specifically in the rutaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The botanical name of Jaborandi is Pilocarpus jaborandi, first described by the botanist A.R. Haw in 1832. It has several synonyms, including Pilocarpus pumila and Pilocarpus sp. Common names include Jaborandi, Devil's weed, and Pilocarpus, while regional and historical names vary across cultures and time periods.

Morphological Characteristics

Jaborandi is a small to medium-sized tree with a straight trunk and dense crown. Its root system is taproot-based, providing stability. The stem is smooth and grayish-brown, with a slightly fissured bark. Leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy green upper surface. The inflorescence is a panicle, bearing small, white flowers with a faint fragrance. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, flat seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Pilocarpus jaborandi can be distinguished by its elliptical, dark green leaves with a leathery texture and prominent veins. The plant has a distinct, aromatic scent and produces small, yellow flowers. It is often confused with Pilocarpus microphyllus, but it has larger leaves and a more robust growth habit. These features aid in accurate identification during herbal studies.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Pilocarpus jaborandi is native to the northeastern regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Paraguay, and Uruguay. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity. The plant prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils. It is typically found at altitudes ranging from 200 to 800 meters above sea level.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Jaborandi blooms between April and June, producing small greenish-white flowers. Seed formation occurs from July to September. For identification purposes, the plant is typically harvested during its flowering period to ensure accurate botanical characteristics.

Phylogenetic Relationships
