What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Crataegus hupehensis has small, serrated leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical in shape and stems characterized by sharp, woody thorns. The plant features a spreading growth habit with branches that produce small, white or pale pink flowers in dense clusters. These blossoms eventually give way to small, fleshy pomes that serve as the fruit of the species. The bark is generally grayish and becomes more textured as the shrub or small tree matures. Overall, its physical structure is defined by its compact foliage and defensive prickly appendages.
