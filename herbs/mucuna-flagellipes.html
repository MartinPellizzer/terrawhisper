Mucuna flagellipes

What's the taxonomical classification of Mucuna flagellipes?

Mucuna flagellipes belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Fabales. As a member of the family Fabaceae, this plant is categorized under the genus Mucuna, and its specific designation is defined by the species epithet flagellipes.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

The presence of these monosaccharides contributes to the overall nutritional and therapeutic profile of the plant. Researchers study these constituents to understand how they interact with biological systems. Such chemical properties make the plant a subject of interest in pharmacological investigations.

According to a study published by "Plant foods for human nutrition (Dordrecht, Netherlands)", the gums derived from Mucuna flagellipes contain D-galactose as a major monosaccharide. The presence of D-mannose and D-glucose was also indicated in Mucuna flagellipes. At a 2% concentration, the gum dispersions showed an apparent viscosity of 145.14 cps. These measurements were taken at 174/sec and 25 +/- 1 degree C with the NV sensor of a Haaké rotovisko viscometer. Emulsions of Mucuna flagellipes gum showed little response to pH changes.
