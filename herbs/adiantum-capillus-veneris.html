Adiantum Capillus-Veneris Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Adiantum capillus-veneris, commonly known as the "Maidenhair Fern," is a delicate fern species native to tropical and subtropical regions, valued for its medicinal properties.

The bioactive constituents include flavonoids, tannins, and saponins, which contribute to its therapeutic actions.

Side Effects of adiantum capillus-veneris

Prolonged exposure may lead to more severe symptoms, including photophobia and temporary vision impairment. Additionally, the herb can cause skin irritation, resulting in redness, rashes, and itching upon direct contact with the skin.

Individuals with sensitive skin may experience more pronounced reactions, including dermatitis or allergic contact dermatitis. Furthermore, Adiantum capillus-veneris may lead to digestive upset, such as nausea, vomiting, or stomach cramps, if ingested, and can also cause throat irritation, dizziness, and fatigue in some cases.

These side effects highlight the importance of handling the herb with care and avoiding ingestion or prolonged skin contact to prevent adverse health effects.
