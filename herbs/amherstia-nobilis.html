Amherstia nobilis

What's the taxonomical classification of Amherstia nobilis?

Amherstia nobili belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant follows a distinct evolutionary lineage that leads to its placement in the order Fabales. It is a representative of the family Fabaceae, commonly known as the legume family, which defines its genus as Amherstia. Ultimately, its specific biological identity is finalized under the species name nobilis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Amherstia nobilis has large, pinnate leaves composed of multiple pairs of leaflets and produces striking, pendulous racemes of bright red or orange flowers. The tree reaches significant heights and is characterized by its broad, spreading canopy that provides dense shade. Its woody stems are sturdy to support the massive weight of the blooming floral displays. The individual flowers are pea-like in shape and emerge in dense clusters from the branches. These unique morphological features make it one of the most visually distinct members of the Fabaceae family.
