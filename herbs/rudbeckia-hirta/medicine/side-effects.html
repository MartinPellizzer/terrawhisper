1. Aggravates respiratory issues

Rudbeckia hirta causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

This is due to the presence of sesquiterpene lactones, a class of compounds found in the plant's leaves and flowers. These compounds can stimulate an immune response, leading to symptoms such as skin irritation, itching, and rash when contact is made with the plant.

Additionally, inhaling pollen or other airborne particles from Rudbeckia hirta may also trigger allergic reactions in sensitive individuals.

2. Aggravates respiratory issues

Rudbeckia hirta triggers asthma attacks due to its high pollen count and volatile organic compounds (VOCs) released by its flowers.

These allergens can cause respiratory irritation, leading to wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath in individuals with asthma.

As the plant blooms profusely during the summer months, exposure to its potent allergenic particles may exacerbate asthma symptoms, making it challenging for sufferers to breathe comfortably outdoors.

3. Aggravates respiratory issues

Rudbeckia hirta induces dizziness episodes due to its potential interactions with certain medications and the release of histamine in some individuals.

The plant's active compounds may combine with antidepressants, antihistamines, or sedatives, leading to a decrease in blood pressure and increased risk of dizziness.

Additionally, Rudbeckia hirta contains sesquiterpenes, which can cause histamine release, resulting in symptoms such as lightheadedness, fainting, and dizziness.

Rudbeckia hirta elicits skin rashes in some individuals due to its ability to cause phototoxicity.

The sap of the plant contains a chemical called sesquiterpene lactones, which can react with sunlight and cause skin irritation, leading to redness, itching, and blistering upon exposure to UV rays.

This reaction is more likely to occur in people with sensitive skin or those who spend extended periods outdoors while handling the plant.
