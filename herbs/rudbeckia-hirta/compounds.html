Black-Eyed Susan (Rudbeckia hirta) Compounds

The primary compounds in Black-Eyed Susan include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and phenolic acids, known for their anti-inflammatory effects. Glycosides and terpenoids contribute to its medicinal and aromatic qualities, while coumarin and tannins provide antimicrobial benefits. Saponins, essential oils, and alkaloids also play roles in its therapeutic applications. Anthocyanins add color and may support cardiovascular health.

Black-Eyed Susan (Rudbeckia hirta) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Black-Eyed Susan (Rudbeckia hirta) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help reduce oxidative stress and support immune function. They are found in the flowers and leaves, making the plant useful in traditional herbal medicine for treating respiratory and digestive ailments.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Black-Eyed Susan (Rudbeckia hirta). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and immune support.

3. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Black-Eyed Susan (Rudbeckia hirta). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. Glycosides in this plant are often used in herbal medicine to support digestive and respiratory health. They work by interacting with the body's systems to promote healing and balance.

4. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Black-Eyed Susan (Rudbeckia hirta) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. These compounds are found in the plant's resinous secretions and are responsible for its distinctive aroma. They support the plant's use in traditional herbal medicine for treating skin conditions and digestive issues.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Black-Eyed Susan (Rudbeckia hirta). It contributes to the plant's mild sedative and antispasmodic properties. Found in the flower heads, coumarin may support digestive health and has been traditionally used to ease muscle tension.

6. Tannin

Tannin in Black-Eyed Susan (Rudbeckia hirta) contributes to its astringent properties, helping reduce inflammation and bleeding. It also supports wound healing by promoting tissue repair and acting as a mild antimicrobial. Tannins are commonly found in plant-based remedies for their ability to soothe irritated tissues.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Black-Eyed Susan (Rudbeckia hirta). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and immune-modulating properties. Saponins may support digestive health and help in the treatment of skin conditions. They are also believed to have mild antimicrobial effects.

8. Essential oil

Essential oil from Black-Eyed Susan (Rudbeckia hirta) contains compounds like sesquiterpenes and flavonoids. It is used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. The oil is often extracted through steam distillation and applied topically or in aromatherapy.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Black-Eyed Susan (Rudbeckia hirta). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, though research on its specific effects is limited. Alkaloids are known for their potent biological activities, which may support the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin, found in Black-Eyed Susan (Rudbeckia hirta), is a flavonoid responsible for the flower's vibrant color. It possesses antioxidant properties that may support immune function and reduce inflammation. These compounds are abundant in the petals and contribute to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.
