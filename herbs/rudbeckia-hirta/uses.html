Black-Eyed Susan (Rudbeckia hirta) Uses

Black-Eyed Susan is used to treat respiratory infections, including the common cold, cough, and bronchitis. It helps alleviate symptoms like congestion and inflammation. The plant also supports skin healing and is applied for wounds and infections due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Black-Eyed Susan (Rudbeckia hirta) Monograph

1. Respiratory infections

Black-Eyed Susan (Rudbeckia hirta) has been traditionally used to support respiratory health. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties may help alleviate symptoms of respiratory infections. Infusions of the leaves and flowers are commonly prepared to soothe coughs and reduce mucus production. It is often used as a complementary remedy in herbal medicine for colds and bronchitis.

2. Common cold

Black-Eyed Susan (Rudbeckia hirta) has been traditionally used to support the body's response to the common cold. Its anti-inflammatory and immune-stimulating properties may help reduce symptoms and shorten the duration of illness. Infusions of the leaves and flowers are commonly prepared to promote respiratory health during colds.

3. Cold symptoms

Black-Eyed Susan (Rudbeckia hirta) has been traditionally used to alleviate cold symptoms due to its anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties. It helps reduce congestion and soothe sore throats when brewed as a tea. Its mucilage content also supports respiratory health during colds.

4. Cough

Black-Eyed Susan is used to support respiratory health, particularly for coughs. Its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties help loosen mucus and reduce irritation. Typically prepared as a tea, it is used in herbal medicine to alleviate persistent coughs and soothe the throat.

5. Influenza

Black-Eyed Susan (Rudbeckia hirta) has been traditionally used to support respiratory health during influenza. Its anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties may help reduce fever and ease coughing. Infusions of the leaves and flowers are often prepared to alleviate symptoms and boost immunity during flu outbreaks.

6. Respiratory congestion

Black-Eyed Susan (Rudbeckia hirta) is used to alleviate respiratory congestion due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. It helps loosen mucus and reduce swelling in the airways, making it useful for conditions like bronchitis and colds. The plant's active compounds support clearing congestion and improving breathing.

7. Bronchitis

Black-Eyed Susan (Rudbeckia hirta) has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly for bronchitis. Its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties help reduce mucus buildup and soothe irritated airways. Infusions or tinctures made from the leaves and flowers are commonly prepared to ease coughing and promote easier breathing.

8. Skin inflammation

Black-Eyed Susan (Rudbeckia hirta) has been traditionally used to reduce skin inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. It may help alleviate symptoms of eczema, rashes, and minor burns when applied topically. The plant's compounds are believed to support skin healing and reduce irritation.

9. Wound healing

Black-Eyed Susan (Rudbeckia hirta) has been traditionally used to support wound healing. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties help reduce infection risk and promote tissue repair. Infusions or salves made from the leaves and flowers can be applied topically to minor cuts, bruises, and skin irritations.

10. Skin infections

Black-Eyed Susan has been traditionally used to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial properties. The plant contains compounds that help reduce bacterial growth and inflammation. It can be applied topically as a poultice or infused oil to soothe infected areas and promote healing.
