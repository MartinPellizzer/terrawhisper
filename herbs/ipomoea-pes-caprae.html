Ipomoea pes-caprae

What's the taxonomical classification of Ipomoea pes-caprae?

Ipomoea pes-caprae belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Solanales. This plant is a member of the family Convolvulaceae and falls under the genus Ipomoea, specifically identified as the species pes-caprae.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Ipomoea pes-caprae contains bioactive alkaloids, flavonoids, steroids, and phenolic compounds that contribute to its diverse therapeutic properties. The presence of specific alkaloids such as ipomeine provides significant anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial actions. Flavonoids within the plant act as potent antioxidants, helping to neutralize free radicals in cellular structures. Additionally, the various steroids and glycosides found in the leaves and roots are utilized in traditional medicine to treat skin ailments and digestive issues. These chemical constituents work synergistically to offer potential pharmacological benefits in treating various inflammatory conditions.

What are the therapeutic actions of Ipomoea pes-caprae?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, inflammatory conditions, and various digestive ailments. The leaves are often applied topically as a poultice to soothe burns and accelerate the healing of minor cuts. Some traditional medicinal practices also utilize extracts from the plant to manage symptoms of rheumatism and joint pain. Additionally, certain preparations are ingested to help alleviate stomach discomfort and parasitic infections. These diverse applications stem from the plant's natural antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.
