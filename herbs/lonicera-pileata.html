Lonicera pileata

What's the taxonomical classification of Lonicera pileata?

Lonicera pileata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it falls under the order Dipsacales and the family Caprifoliaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Lonicera, and its species designation, pileata.

What are the herbal preparations of Lonicera pileata?

Lonicera pileata is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a tincture using the dried stems and leaves of the plant. These aqueous extractions allow the soluble active compounds to be easily absorbed by the body during consumption. Tinctures are often created by soaking the plant material in high-proof alcohol to preserve its medicinal properties for long-term use. Some practitioners also utilize topical ointments or salves made from the plant to treat external skin conditions. Each preparation method is chosen based on whether the intended therapeutic effect is internal or local.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Lonicera pileata can cause skin irritation, redness, and itching if the plant comes into direct contact with sensitive skin. While the plant is not known to be highly toxic, ingestion of its berries or foliage may lead to gastrointestinal distress such as nausea or vomiting. Some individuals might also experience allergic reactions including swelling or a rash when handling the stems or leaves. It is best to wear gloves when pruning this shrub to prevent any unwanted physical discomfort. Always keep the plant away from small children and pets to avoid accidental consumption.
