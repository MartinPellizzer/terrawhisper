Viola yunnanensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Viola yunnanensis?

Viola yunnanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Magnoliidae and is further categorized within the order Malpighiales. This plant is a member of the family Violaceae, specifically placed within the genus Viola, and is identified by its unique species designation, yunnanensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viola yunnanensis has an herbaceous perennial habit characterized by slender rhizomes and basal leaves that are typically cordate or reniform in shape with crenate margins. The leaves possess long petioles and are arranged in a basal rosette. Its inflorescence consists of solitary flowers borne on pedicels that emerge from the leaf axils. Each flower features five zygomorphic petals, often displaying a distinct violet or purplish hue with varying patterns on the lower lobes. The fruit develops into a small, three-valved capsule containing numerous tiny seeds.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of southwestern China, specifically within the provinces of Yunnan, Sichuan, and Guizhou. It thrives primarily in montane environments where rocky crevices or forest edges provide suitable habitats. These populations are typically found at elevations ranging from moderate to high mountain zones. The distribution is closely tied to the temperate climates characteristic of the Hengduan Mountains and surrounding subtropical highlands.
