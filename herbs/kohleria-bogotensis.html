What is Kohleria bogotensis?

Kohleria bogotensis is an evergreen perennial plant belonging to the family Gesneriaceae.

It has elongated, succulent leaves that are 3-7 cm long and 1-2 cm wide, with entire margins and a pointed apex, and are arranged oppositely on a stout stem. The leaves are a bright green color with a reddish tint on the underside, and are slightly pubescent with short, appressed hairs. The plant produces funnel-shaped flowers that are 2-3 cm long and 1-2 cm wide, with five lobes and a reddish-pink color with yellow markings on the upper lip, and is a hermaphroditic species.

Kohleria bogotensis is native to the Andean region of Colombia, where it grows in well-drained soils at elevations of 2,500-3,500 meters.
