Heterotis rotundifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Heterotis rotundifolia has a perennial growth habit characterized by rounded, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The foliage consists of thick, succulent blades that exhibit a smooth texture and a vibrant green coloration. These leaves emerge from a central crown, spreading outward to capture maximum sunlight near the ground level. Small, inconspicuous flowers may occasionally appear atop slender stalks rising from the center of the plant. The root system is robust, allowing the species to store nutrients and survive in various environmental conditions.
