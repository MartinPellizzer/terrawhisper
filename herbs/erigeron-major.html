What is Erigeron major?

Erigeron major is a perennial plant species belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its taproot and erect stems, typically 10-40 cm tall, bearing leafy bracts and small, white to pinkish flowers in dense, rounded clusters. The leaves are usually linear to lanceolate, 1-4 cm long, and arranged oppositely on the stem. The plant reproduces through achenes, the fruit of the flowers, which are 3-4 mm long, containing a single seed and a pappus of 5-7 barbed bristles.

Erigeron major is native to Europe, Western Asia, and North Africa, and is commonly found in moist meadows and grasslands.
