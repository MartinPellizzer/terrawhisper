Maranta arundinacea

What's the taxonomical classification of Maranta arundinacea?

Marantra arundinacea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic lineage through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, the plant is placed under the order Zingiberales. It is a member of the family Marantaceae and falls under the genus Marantra, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species arundinacea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Maranta arundinacea has lanceolate leaves that grow from underground rhizomes, featuring prominent longitudinal veins and a distinctively patterned surface. These leaves can reach significant lengths and possess a texture that often feels slightly leathery to the touch. The plant structure is characterized by a spreading habit facilitated by its creeping rhizomatous stems. Small, inconspicuous flowers typically emerge from the foliage to facilitate reproduction. The overall morphology is adapted to the shaded, humid environments of the tropical forest floor.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires well-draining, organic-rich soil, high humidity, and consistent moisture with filtered sunlight to thrive in a tropical environment.

Growers should maintain temperatures between 65 and 80 degrees Fahrenheit to prevent leaf damage. Because it is a rhizomatous species, it benefits from regular fertilization during the active growing season. Providing a humid atmosphere through misting or a pebble tray is essential for its foliage health.

Periodic repotting helps manage the spreading root system and ensures continued vigor.

What traditional systems uses this plant?

This plant, commonly known as arrowroot, has been historically utilized by indigenous communities in the Caribbean and Central America as a primary source of dietary starch.

Cultivators traditionally extract the fine, white powder from the rhizomes to create easily digestible puddings and thickeners for soups.
