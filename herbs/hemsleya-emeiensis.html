Hemsleya emeiensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hemsleya emeiensis has large, deeply pinnatifid or bipinnatifid fronds that typically measure between 30 and 60 centimeters in length. The individual segments of these leaves are characterized by thin, delicate textures and serrated margins. Long, slender stipes support the foliage, which often displays a vibrant green coloration. These ferns develop through a creeping rhizome system that allows them to spread through moist forest substrates. Small, inconspicuous sporangia are arranged in sori on the underside of the pinnules to facilitate reproduction.
