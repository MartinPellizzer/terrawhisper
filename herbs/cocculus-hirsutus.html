Cocculus

Cocculus hirsutus

Cocculus (Cocculus hirsutus) is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia, known for its use in traditional medicine to support digestive health and alleviate symptoms of gastrointestinal disorders.

Botanical Identification

Cocculus hirsutus, commonly known as the horse chestnut, is a member of the Fabaceae family. It is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia. The plant has palmately compound leaves, yellowish flowers, and large, spiny fruits. It is also referred to as the European horse chestnut in some herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Eudicots
  • Order: Rhamnales
  • Family: Rhamnaceae
  • Genus: Cocculus
  • Species: Cocculus hirsutus

Active Compounds

Cocculus hirsutus contains alkaloids, tannins, and flavonoids as its primary active compounds. These substances contribute to its medicinal effects by interacting with various physiological systems. Alkaloids may influence nerve function, while tannins and flavonoids offer anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

  • Alkaloid
  • Tannin
  • Flavonoid

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory conditions.

Infusion: Used for calming effects and mild digestive support.

Powder: Used for general tonic and digestive health.

Safety Profile

Cocculus hirsutus is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal upset or drowsiness in some individuals. Consult a healthcare provider before use, especially during pregnancy or if taking other medications.
