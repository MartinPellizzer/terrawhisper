Morus nigra

What's the taxonomical classification of Morus nigra?

Morus nigra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Rosales, this plant is situated in the family Moraceae. It is further categorized under the genus Morus, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species nigra.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Morus nigra has deciduous, alternate leaves that are typically ovate or heart-shaped with serrated margins and can range from smooth to rugose textures. The foliage often displays a glossy upper surface and a lighter underside. Its woody branches produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are borne in pendulous clusters. These flowers eventually develop into aggregate fleshy fruits known as mulberries, which transition from green to deep purple or black when ripe. The bark of the mature tree is characterized by a grayish-brown color and a somewhat rough texture.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to a vast range spanning from China and Japan through the Korean Peninsula to much of Europe and Western Asia. While its primary origins are found in East Asia, it has successfully naturalized in many other temperate regions across the globe. In North America, it is widely distributed and often grows as an escaped ornamental or invasive species. The species thrives in diverse climates ranging from subtropical zones to cooler temperate environments. Its ability to adapt to various soil types allows it to spread across many different continents.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Morus nigra contains bioactive compounds in its leaves, fruits, bark, and roots that are utilized for various therapeutic purposes. The leaves are frequently processed into teas or extracts to help manage blood glucose levels and support cardiovascular health. Its dark berries are highly valued for their potent antioxidant properties and are consumed to boost the immune system. Traditional medicine often employs the bark and roots to treat inflammatory conditions and digestive issues. What are the pharmacological activities of Morus nigra?

This plant has antioxidant, anti-diabetic, anti-inflammatory, anti-cancer, and antimicrobial pharmacological activities derived from its rich profile of bioactive compounds like flavonoids and alkaloids. Its antioxidant properties help neutralize free radicals, which protects cells from oxidative damage and reduces systemic stress. The anti-diabetic effects are primarily seen through the inhibition of alpha-glucosidase, helping to regulate blood glucose levels effectively. Additionally, its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial actions provide defense against various pathogens and chronic swelling. Finally, research suggests that certain extracts possess anti-cancer potential by inhibiting the growth and proliferation of malignant cells.

As discussed in a study, Morus nigra leaves possess potent antioxidant activity with an EC50 of 42.97 µg extract/mL due to high amounts of polyphenol and flavonoid compounds. When male mice were given 500 mg/kg of APAP, the administration of Morus nigra extract at 150, 300, and 500 mg/kg for 4 consecutive days suppressed damaging effects. The extract suppressed APAP-induced genotoxicity by lowering meiotic chromosomal aberrations in spermatozytes, morphological sperm abnormalities, and % DNA damage in the comet tail of liver and kidney tissues. Furthermore, the extract significantly reversed altered levels of glutathione S transferase activity, lipid peroxidation, and liver and kidney functions. The protective activity of Morus nigra was further strengthened by restoring histo-architectural distortions and decreasing over-expression of p53 protein in the liver, kidney, and testis sections.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains 1-deoxynojirimycin, DNJ alkaloids, flavonoids, phenolic acids, and resveratrol, which serve as its primary medicinal compounds. The DNJ alkaloids, particularly 1-deoxynojirimycin, are well-known for their ability to inhibit alpha-glucosidase and help regulate blood sugar levels. Additionally, the presence of various flavonoids and phenolic acids provides significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory benefits to the body. Resveratrol contributes to these therapeutic effects by supporting cardiovascular health and cellular longevity. Together, these bioactive molecules create a complex profile of health-promoting properties.

As discussed in a study, Cortex Mori contains the main active ingredient 1-Deoxynojirimycin (DNJ), which is recognized as a potent α-glycosidase inhibitor. This herb has a long history of use for the treatment of headaches, cough, edema and diabetes. When administered at DNJ equivalent doses of 20 and 40mg/kg, the medicinal effects of the extract can be investigated. In intestinal perfusion models, the DNJ concentration was evaluated at 10µM. The study focused on how these compounds interact to control postprandial blood glucose levels.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for managing Type 2 diabetes and related metabolic complications by regulating blood glucose levels. Its bioactive compounds help combat hyperglycemia, which is a common symptom of uncontrolled sugar levels in the body. By improving glucose metabolism, it can assist in reducing the symptoms associated with diabetes mellitus. Furthermore, the extracts may help mitigate insulin resistance by enhancing how cells respond to hormonal signals. This makes the plant a significant subject of study for natural interventions in metabolic health.

As discussed in a study, Morus nigra L. presents hepatoprotective, anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, hypolipidemic, and hypoglycemic activities. These activities are shown to be advantageous in the management of obesity, dyslipidemia, Type 2 diabetes, and non-alcoholic fatty liver diseases. Recent studies on mulberry leaves extracts indicated that they could decrease palmitic acid-induced lipotoxicity. Furthermore, these extracts could increase total cholesterol and bile acid excretion, improve superoxide dismutase expression, and improve insulin resistance.

What are the herbal preparations of this plant?

This plant can be prepared as an infusion of its leaves, a decoction of its bark, a tincture of its berries, a syrup from its fruit, a juice from its ripe berries, or an extract from its various parts. Herbalists often use a hot infusion of the leaves to support metabolic health. The bark is typically simmered into a decoction to access its more robust medicinal compounds. Berries are frequently processed into a sweet syrup or a fresh juice for dietary supplementation. What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause hypoglycemia, nausea, diarrhea, and stomach discomfort in some individuals. The potential for low blood sugar occurs because certain compounds in the fruit may lower glucose levels. Digestive issues such as nausea and diarrhea are common side effects when consuming excessive amounts. Many people also report general stomach discomfort following ingestion. It is important to monitor how your body reacts to this species.
