Cudrania cochinchinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Cudrania cochinchinensis?

Cudrania cochinchinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further follows the evolutionary path of the order Rosales and is a member of the family Moraeaae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Cudrania, and its unique species designation, cochinchinenis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cudrania cochinchinensis has a woody climbing shrub morphology characterized by slender, twining stems and trifoliate leaves with ovate to elliptical leaflets. The leaves feature serrated margins and a distinctively textured surface that aids in moisture retention. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in clusters along the branches, eventually developing into fleshy, globose drupes. These fruits transition from green to a deep reddish or purplish hue when fully mature. The root system is robust, providing the necessary stability for its climbing habit within forest ecosystems.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds kaempferol, dihydrokaempferol, beta-sitosterol, vanillin, and umbelliferone. These specific phytochemicals contribute to the plant's diverse pharmacological profile and biological activities. Kaempferol and dihydrokaempferol are known flavonoids that often provide antioxidant and anti-inflammatory benefits. The presence of beta-sitosterol may assist in regulating cholesterol levels and supporting cellular health. Additionally, the aromatic compound vanillin and the coumarin umbelliferone add to the complex chemical makeup of the species.

As discussed in a study, thirteen compounds were isolated from the 70% ethanol extract of the root of Cudrania cochinchinensis. These identified compounds include beta-sitosterol (1), butyrospermol (2), butyrospermol acetate (3), (+) syringarenol (4), 1, 3, 6-trihydroxy-7-methoxy xanthone (5), 1, 3, 6, 7-tetrahydroxy-8-prenylxanthone (6), kaempferol (7), dihydrokaempferol (8), umbelliferone (9), 4-hydroxybenzyl ethyl ether (10), 2,4-dihydroxybenzaldehyde (11), 4-hydroxybenzaldehyde (12) and vanillin (13). Compounds 2 through 6 and 10 through 13 were isolated from Cudrania cochinchinensis for the first time. The isolation and purification process utilized silica gel column, sephadex LH-20 chromatography and recrystallization.
