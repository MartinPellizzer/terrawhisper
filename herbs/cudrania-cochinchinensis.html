Cudrania cochinchinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Cudrania cochinchinensis?

Cudrania cochinchinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further follows the evolutionary path of the order Rosales and is a member of the family Moraeaae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Cudrania, and its unique species designation, cochinchinenis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cudrania cochinchinensis has a woody climbing shrub morphology characterized by slender, twining stems and trifoliate leaves with ovate to elliptical leaflets.

The leaves feature serrated margins and a distinctively textured surface that aids in moisture retention. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in clusters along the branches, eventually developing into fleshy, globose drupes. These fruits transition from green to a deep reddish or purplish hue when fully mature.

The root system is robust, providing the necessary stability for its climbing habit within forest ecosystems.

What are the therapeutic actions of Cudrania cochinchinensis?

Cudrania cochinchinenensis has medicinal properties that function as an astringent, hemostatic, anti-inflammatory, antipyretic, and diuretic agent.

What are the herbal preparations of Cudrania cochinchinensis?

Cudrania cochinchinensis is prepared primarily as a decoction, a concentrated liquid extract made by boiling the bark or leaves in water to release medicinal compounds. This plant is also frequently processed into a dried powder that can be mixed into food or swallowed with water for therapeutic use. In some traditional practices, the fruit or bark is macerated in alcohol to create a tincture for more potent applications. Some regional methods involve using the plant in poultices, where the crushed raw material is applied directly to the skin. These various forms allow for different absorption rates and specific uses in folk medicine.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Cudrania cochinchinensis can cause adverse reactions such as gastrointestinal distress, allergic skin rashes, or unexpected metabolic changes if consumed in improper quantities. There is limited clinical data available regarding its long-term toxicity, meaning potential users should exercise caution. Interactions with prescription medications have not been extensively studied, which could lead to unpredictable physiological effects. Always consult a medical professional before incorporating this species into a dietary regimen to avoid these risks.
