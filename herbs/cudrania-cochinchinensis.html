Cudrania cochinchinensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cudrania cochinchinensis has a woody climbing shrub morphology characterized by slender, twining stems and trifoliate leaves with ovate to elliptical leaflets. The leaves feature serrated margins and a distinctively textured surface that aids in moisture retention. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in clusters along the branches, eventually developing into fleshy, globose drupes. These fruits transition from green to a deep reddish or purplish hue when fully mature. The root system is robust, providing the necessary stability for its climbing habit within forest ecosystems.

The species prefers warm, humid climates that characterize the subtropical and tropical zones of its range.
