Clove (Eugenia caryophyllata) Compounds

The primary compounds in clove include essential oil, which contains eugenol, a key aromatic compound. Tannins and flavonoids contribute to its astringent and antioxidant properties. Phenolic acids and terpenoids support anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Coumarin, glycosides, saponins, alkaloids, and anthocyanins also play roles in its medicinal profile.

Clove (Eugenia caryophyllata) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Clove (Eugenia caryophyllata) is a key active compound, containing eugenol as its primary component. It is used in herbal medicine for its antiseptic, analgesic, and anti-inflammatory properties, making it valuable in treating dental pain, infections, and skin conditions.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Clove (Eugenia caryophyllata). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and has antimicrobial effects. Tannins help reduce inflammation and may support digestive health. They are also responsible for the bitter taste of cloves.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Clove (Eugenia caryophyllata). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting immune function and reducing inflammation. These compounds are also responsible for some of the therapeutic effects associated with clove in traditional herbal medicine.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Clove (Eugenia caryophyllata). It contributes to the plant's antimicrobial and antioxidant properties. This compound is responsible for some of the therapeutic effects attributed to clove in traditional and modern herbal medicine.

5. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Clove (Eugenia caryophyllata). They contribute to the plant's aromatic profile and have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds are responsible for many of the therapeutic effects associated with clove in herbal medicine.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Clove (Eugenia caryophyllata). It contributes to the plant's aromatic properties and has antispasmodic and anticoagulant effects. Coumarin is also used in traditional medicine for its potential to alleviate pain and inflammation.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Clove (Eugenia caryophyllata). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly in anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects. Glycosides in cloves are often metabolized to release active aglycones, enhancing their therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Clove (Eugenia caryophyllata). It contributes to the plant's antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Saponins help enhance the absorption of other active compounds and support overall therapeutic effects in herbal treatments.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Clove (Eugenia caryophyllata). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, though it is less prominent than the essential oils. Alkaloids in clove may have mild antispasmodic and analgesic effects. They are typically present in smaller quantities compared to other bioactive components.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Clove (Eugenia caryophyllata). It contributes to the plant's red and purple pigments and may have antioxidant properties. While not the primary active compound in clove, anthocyanin supports overall plant health and may contribute to its medicinal potential.
