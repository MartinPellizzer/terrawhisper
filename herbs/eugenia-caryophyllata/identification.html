Botanical Identification of Clove (Eugenia caryophyllata)

To identify Clove (Eugenia caryophyllata) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Clove (Eugenia caryophyllata) Monograph

Scientific Classification

Clove, or Eugenia caryophyllata, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Sapindales, family Myrtaceae, genus Eugenia, and species Eugenia caryophyllata. These classifications help identify its botanical relationships and place it within the broader plant kingdom.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Clove is Eugenia caryophyllata, first described by Linnaeus in 1788. It has several synonyms, including Syzygium aromaticum, reflecting historical taxonomic changes. Common names include clove, and it is also known by regional and historical names such as kari in India and laksa in Indonesia.

Morphological Characteristics

Clove trees are evergreen shrubs or small trees with a dense, bushy growth habit. They have a fibrous root system that anchors the plant and absorbs nutrients. The stem is thick, woody, and often branched, supporting the overall structure of the plant.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Clove is distinguished by its distinctive aromatic, reddish-brown flower buds, which are harvested and dried. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin. The stem is aromatic and has a rough, bark-like texture. These features help differentiate it from other Eugenia species.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Clove trees are native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia. They thrive in tropical climates with high humidity and warm temperatures. They prefer well-drained, fertile soils and are typically found at altitudes between 200 and 800 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Clove trees bloom from April to June, producing small white flowers. Seed formation occurs in the following months, from July to September. Harvesting of flower buds, the primary part used in identification, takes place when they turn from green to red, typically in late autumn to early winter.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Clove, or Eugenia caryophyllata, is closely related to other Eugenia species, particularly those in the Myrtaceae family. It shares key morphological and genetic traits with related genera such as Syzygium and Psychotria. These relationships highlight its evolutionary placement within the Myrtaceae family, emphasizing its unique botanical characteristics.
