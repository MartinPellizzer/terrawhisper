Michelia maudiae

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Michelia maudiae has an evergreen habit characterized by large, elliptic to oblong leaves that are coriaceous in texture and possess entire margins. The foliage displays a deep green color on the upper surface with a lighter underside, often featuring prominent pinnate venation. Its inflorescence consists of fragrant, solitary flowers that emerge from the leaf axils during the blooming season. These flowers typically feature multiple layers of creamy white to pale yellow petals and numerous stamens arranged in a central cluster. The plant also develops woody, indehiscent fruits that eventually split to reveal bright red seeds.
