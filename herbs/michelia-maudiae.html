Michelia maudiae

What's the taxonomical classification of Michelia maudiae?

Michelia maudiae belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further classified into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Magnoliales. This plant is a member of the family Magnoliaceae and is specifically situated within the genus Michelia, reaching its final taxonomic designation at the species level as maudiae.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Michelia maudiae has an evergreen habit characterized by large, elliptic to oblong leaves that are coriaceous in texture and possess entire margins. Its inflorescence consists of fragrant, solitary flowers that emerge from the leaf axils during the blooming season. These flowers typically feature multiple layers of creamy white to pale yellow petals and numerous stamens arranged in a central cluster. The plant also develops woody, indehiscent fruits that eventually split to reveal bright red seeds.
