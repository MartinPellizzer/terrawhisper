10 Possible Side Effects Of Stevia Rebaudiana

Stevia rebaudiana has some side effects when used improperly, such as headaches, digestive issues, and allergic reactions.

These side effects can be caused by excessive consumption or individual sensitivities to its compounds. In severe cases, stevia can worsen conditions like high blood pressure, diabetes, or irritable bowel syndrome.

For instance, a person with high blood pressure may experience increased blood pressure spikes due to stevia's blood sugar-raising properties.

1. Causes stomach discomfort

Stevia rebaudiana causes stomach discomfort in some individuals due to its high concentration of diterpene glycosides.

These compounds can slow down gastric emptying, leading to feelings of bloating and discomfort.

Additionally, the sweetness of stevia may stimulate an increase in digestive enzymes, resulting in abdominal cramping and gas.

2. Triggers headache attacks

Stevia rebaudiana triggers headache attacks in some individuals.

This is attributed to its high concentration of diterpenoid glycosides, particularly steviol, which can cause a sudden and significant increase in blood sugar levels. This rapid spike may lead to changes in blood vessel dilation and constriction, resulting in headache episodes.

The intense sweetness of stevia may also overwhelm the taste buds, leading to an unpleasant sensation that can manifest as headaches or migraines in susceptible individuals.

3. Increases blood pressure

Stevia rebaudiana increases blood pressure due to its natural stimulatory effects on the body.

The saponins and terpenoids present in stevia can stimulate the sympathetic nervous system, leading to an increase in heart rate and cardiac output, which in turn can raise blood pressure.

Additionally, some studies suggest that stevia may also cause vasoconstriction, further contributing to the increase in blood pressure.

4. Lures digestive issues

Stevia rebaudiana lures digestive issues due to its high concentration of compounds like stevioside and rebaudioside.

These sweet-tasting molecules can cause gastrointestinal upset in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as bloating, gas, stomach cramps, and changes in bowel movements.

The exact mechanisms behind these adverse reactions are unclear, but it is thought that Stevia's intense sweetness may disrupt the body's natural gut bacteria, leading to an imbalance of gut flora.

5. Produces nausea episodes

Stevia rebaudiana produces nausea episodes due to its high concentration of mogrosides, which can cause digestive upset and irritate the stomach lining.

Additionally, stevia's sweet compounds may trigger the release of gastrin, a hormone that stimulates stomach acid production, leading to feelings of queasiness and discomfort.

The rapid absorption of these compounds into the bloodstream can also contribute to nausea episodes in some individuals.

6. Affects menstrual cycles

Stevia rebaudiana affects menstrual cycles due to its potential to disrupt hormonal balances.

The sweetener's glycosides may interact with estrogen receptors, leading to changes in menstrual flow and frequency. Additionally, Stevia's ability to stimulate the thyroid gland could contribute to irregular periods by affecting the body's natural hormonal rhythms.

As a result, some women may experience alterations in their menstrual cycles when consuming Stevia products.

7. Reduces potassium levels

Stevia rebaudiana reduces potassium levels due to its high concentration of compounds like stevioside and rebaudioside A.

These compounds inhibit the absorption of potassium in the gut and can increase urinary excretion, leading to a decrease in serum potassium levels.

As stevia is metabolized in the body, it can also stimulate the release of hormones that further reduce potassium levels, potentially causing an imbalance in electrolyte levels.

8. Interferes with insulin

Stevia rebaudiana interferes with insulin by altering glucose metabolism and potentially causing blood sugar fluctuations.

This is due to its ability to stimulate the release of glucose from liver glycogen stores, increasing serum glucose levels. Additionally, stevia may also inhibit the action of sulfonylureas, a class of diabetes medications that work by stimulating insulin secretion.

As a result, people with diabetes who consume stevia may experience changes in blood sugar control and require adjustments to their medication regimen.

9. Aggravates allergic reactions

Stevia rebaudiana aggravates allergic reactions by triggering an immune response in individuals who are already sensitized to its components.

The plant's natural compounds, including stevioside and rebaudioside A, can bind to IgE antibodies and stimulate the release of histamine, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling.

Additionally, Stevia rebaudiana may also contain other allergenic substances like pollen and leaf debris that can exacerbate existing allergies.

10. Disrupts thyroid function

Stevia rebaudiana disrupts thyroid function.

The potential disruption is attributed to its high concentration of saponins, which may interfere with the body's ability to absorb essential nutrients and hormones, including those produced by the thyroid gland. Additionally, Stevia's sweetness can stimulate insulin production, leading to a rapid increase in blood sugar levels that may put additional strain on the thyroid gland's function.

As a result, individuals taking Stevia supplements may experience changes in their metabolism, energy levels, or overall hormonal balance.
