10 Medicinal Constituents Of Stevia Rebaudiana (Stevia)

Stevia rebaudiana has active constituents such as steviol, stevioside, and rebaudioside.

These constituents have been shown to possess medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory, antidiabetic, and antihypertensive effects. By inhibiting the absorption of glucose, stevia can help improve glycemic control and reduce the risk of cardiovascular disease.

This can significantly improve the lives of individuals with diabetes, enabling them to manage their condition more effectively and reducing the need for medication.

This article explains in details the 10 best active constituents of Stevia rebaudiana.

1. Stevioside

Stevia rebaudiana stevioside is a natural sweetener derived from the leaves of the Stevia rebaudiana plant.

It is 200-300 times sweeter than sugar and contains zero calories.

Stevioside is used as a food additive, particularly in Japan and South America, where it has been consumed for centuries due to its unique properties, including being heat-stable and non-glycemic, making it an attractive alternative to traditional sweeteners.

2. Rebaudioside a

Stevia rebaudiana rebaudioside A is a sweet-tasting compound extracted from the leaves of the Stevia rebaudiana plant.

It is 200-300 times sweeter than sugar and has been used for centuries in traditional medicine in South America.

Rebaudioside A is considered one of the primary sweet compounds responsible for stevia's sweet taste, making it a popular natural alternative to artificial sweeteners and sugar substitutes.

3. Isosteviol

Stevia rebaudiana isosteviol is a diterpene glycoside compound extracted from the leaves of the Stevia rebaudiana plant.

It is responsible for the sweet taste and medicinal properties of stevia.

Isosteviol has been shown to exhibit various biological activities, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, making it a potential natural remedy for various diseases.

4. Steviolbioside

Stevia rebaudiana steviolbioside is a natural sweetener extracted from the leaves of the Stevia rebaudiana plant.

This non-caloric compound is approximately 200-300 times sweeter than sugar and has been used for centuries in traditional medicine and cooking.

Steviolbioside is responsible for the sweetness of stevia, making it an attractive alternative to artificial sweeteners and a popular choice for those with dietary restrictions or preferences.

5. D-umbelliferone

Stevia rebaudiana d-umbelliferone is a naturally occurring compound found in the leaves of the Stevia rebaudiana plant.

It belongs to a class of compounds called flavonoids and has been shown to exhibit various biological activities, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties.

D-umbelliferone is responsible for many of stevia's unique properties, including its sweet taste and potential health benefits.

6. Quercetin

Stevia rebaudiana quercetin is a natural compound found in the leaves of Stevia rebaudiana, a plant native to South America.

Quercetin is a flavonoid that has been isolated and studied for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

It is believed to play a role in the plant's unique ability to sweeten without adding calories or carbohydrates, making it an attractive alternative to sugar for those with dietary restrictions or concerns.

7. Kaempferol

Stevia rebaudiana kaempferol is a flavonoid compound extracted from the leaves of Stevia rebaudiana Bertoni.

It has been widely studied for its potential health benefits, including antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

Kaempferol has also shown to have antimicrobial and antiviral activities, making it a promising natural ingredient in the development of new medicines and food products.

8. Rhamnose

Stevia rebaudiana rhamnose is a type of sweet-tasting compound derived from the leaves of the Stevia rebaudiana plant.

It is 200-300 times sweeter than sugar and has zero calories, making it an attractive natural alternative to artificial sweeteners.

Rhamnose is responsible for the sweetness and flavor of stevia, providing a distinct aftertaste that some find pleasant and others unpleasant.

9. Galactose

Stevia rebaudiana galactose is a type of sugar substitute derived from the leaves of the Stevia rebaudiana plant.

It is 200-300 times sweeter than sugar and has been used for centuries in traditional medicine to sweeten tea and other beverages.

Galactose is a low-calorie alternative to sugar that does not raise blood sugar levels, making it an attractive option for people with diabetes or those who are watching their weight.

10. Glucose

Stevia rebaudiana glucose is a natural sweetener extracted from the leaves of the Stevia rebaudiana plant.

It is 200-300 times sweeter than sugar and has been used for centuries in traditional medicine to treat various ailments.

Stevia glucose contains zero calories and does not raise blood sugar levels, making it an attractive alternative to sugar for people with diabetes or those seeking a low-calorie sweetener.
