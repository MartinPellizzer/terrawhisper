Stevia (Stevia rebaudiana) Compounds

The primary compounds in Stevia include glycosides like stevioside and rebaudioside, which are responsible for its sweet taste. Flavonoids and phenolic acids contribute to its antioxidant properties. Terpenoids and saponins offer additional health benefits, while coumarin and tannins provide anti-inflammatory effects. Essential oils and alkaloids also play roles in its medicinal profile.

Stevia (Stevia rebaudiana) Monograph

1. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key compound in Stevia rebaudiana, known for its intense sweetness and low caloric content. It functions as a natural sweetener and has been studied for its potential health benefits, including blood sugar regulation. Glycosides in Stevia are responsible for its medicinal properties and are commonly used in herbal medicine.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Stevia rebaudiana. They contribute to the plant's antioxidant properties and may support cardiovascular and inflammatory health. These compounds are naturally present alongside stevioside and other glycosides. Flavonoids enhance the overall therapeutic potential of Stevia in herbal medicine.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Stevia rebaudiana, contributing to its antioxidant properties. It helps neutralize free radicals, supporting cellular health and reducing oxidative stress. This compound is part of the broader phytochemical profile that gives Stevia its medicinal value in herbal medicine.

4. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Stevia rebaudiana, known for their diverse biological activities. Stevia's terpenoids, such as stevioside and rebaudioside A, are responsible for its intense sweet taste and potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds are widely used as natural sweeteners and are being studied for their medicinal applications.

5. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Stevia rebaudiana. It contributes to the plant's distinctive bitter taste and has potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponins may also support cardiovascular health by influencing cholesterol levels. These effects make saponin an important component in traditional herbal medicine.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a compound found in Stevia rebaudiana. It contributes to the plant's characteristic aroma and has mild anticoagulant properties. While not the primary active component for sweetening, coumarin may support cardiovascular health and is often present in Stevia-based products.

7. Tannin

Tannin is a compound found in Stevia rebaudiana. It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health by reducing inflammation and promoting gut health. Tannins in Stevia are also believed to enhance the plant's overall medicinal profile.

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of Stevia rebaudiana contains volatile compounds like methyl esters of stearic and palmitic acid. These contribute to the plant's aroma and may have mild antimicrobial properties. It is used in aromatherapy and as a natural flavoring agent. The essential oil complements the plant's primary use as a sweetener.

9. Alkaloid

Stevia rebaudiana contains no alkaloids. Alkaloids are a class of naturally occurring compounds found in various plants, but they are not present in Stevia. This distinguishes it from other medicinal plants that may contain alkaloids as active components.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Stevia rebaudiana. It contributes to the plant's vibrant color and has antioxidant properties. This compound supports overall health by neutralizing free radicals and may enhance the plant's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.
