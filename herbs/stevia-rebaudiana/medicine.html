What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Stevia Rebaudiana (Stevia)?

Stevia rebaudiana has health benefits such as lowering blood pressure and aiding in weight management due to its zero-calorie sweetness.

The herb contains medicinal constituents like stevioside and rebaudioside, which have been shown to possess anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and antibacterial properties. Stevia is commonly prepared into various forms, including tablets, capsules, and liquid extracts, making it easily accessible for consumption. However, some individuals may experience side effects such as bloating or digestive issues when using stevia, so it's essential to start with a low dose and monitor its effects.

Additionally, pregnant women and children should consult with a healthcare professional before using stevia due to limited research on its safety in these populations.

What are the health benefits of Stevia rebaudiana?

Stevia rebaudiana has health benefits such as lowering blood pressure and cholesterol levels due to its ability to inhibit the absorption of dietary fat.

It also helps regulate blood sugar levels and may have anti-inflammatory properties that can aid in relieving pain and inflammation.

Additionally, stevia has been shown to have antioxidant effects, which can help protect against cell damage and reduce the risk of chronic diseases like heart disease and cancer.

What are the active constituents of Stevia rebaudiana?

The health benefits of Stevia rebaudiana comes from its active constituents, such as stevioside and rebaudioside A.

These glycosides are responsible for the sweet taste without the calories, while also exhibiting antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties.

Additionally, stevia contains flavonoids like kaempferol and quercetin, which contribute to its potential therapeutic effects on cardiovascular health, diabetes management, and oral health.

What are the medicinal preparations of Stevia rebaudiana?

Stevia rebaudiana has different medicinal preparations, such as tablets, capsules, powders, and teas.

The most common use of stevia is in tablet or capsule form to treat conditions like type 2 diabetes, hypertension, and hyperlipidemia due to its antidiabetic, anti-hypertensive, and lipid-lowering properties.

Stevia powder can be used as a sweetener for foods and beverages, while stevia tea is consumed for its medicinal benefits, particularly in treating digestive issues and improving oral health.

What are the possible side effect of using Stevia rebaudiana improperly?

Improper use of Stevia rebaudiana increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as headaches, dizziness, and nausea.

Prolonged consumption may lead to allergic reactions, digestive issues like bloating and gas, stomach cramps, and even diarrhea. Additionally, excessive intake can cause jitteriness, anxiety, and difficulty falling asleep due to its stimulating properties.

In rare cases, stevia has been linked to kidney damage, thyroid disorders, and an increased risk of type 2 diabetes.

What precautions to take when using Stevia rebaudiana medicinally?

Before using Stevia rebaudiana for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as consulting a healthcare professional to determine the appropriate dosage and potential interactions with other medications.

Additionally, be cautious when consuming stevia if you have a history of kidney or liver disease, as it may exacerbate existing conditions.
