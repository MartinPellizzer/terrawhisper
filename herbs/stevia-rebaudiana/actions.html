Stevia (Stevia rebaudiana) Actions

Stevia rebaudiana exhibits several therapeutic actions, including tonic, anti-inflammatory, and diuretic properties. It acts as a stimulant and carminative, aiding digestion and reducing gas. Its antispasmodic and expectorant effects help alleviate respiratory and gastrointestinal issues. Additionally, it has astringent and sedative qualities, supporting mucous membrane health and calming the nervous system.

Stevia (Stevia rebaudiana) Monograph

1. Tonic

S tea has a tonic action, stimulating the body's vital functions and enhancing energy levels. It supports digestion and can help alleviate fatigue. Stevia's tonic properties are attributed to its bioactive compounds, which promote overall vitality and balance in the body.

2. Anti-inflammatory

Stevia has an anti-inflammatory action, primarily due to its steviol glycosides, which inhibit inflammatory pathways. These compounds reduce the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines and mediators, making Stevia useful in managing conditions like arthritis and inflammatory skin disorders.

3. Bitter

Stevia has a bitter action, which stimulates digestion and enhances nutrient absorption. This bitter quality helps to clear mucus and support liver function. It is particularly useful for digestive complaints and respiratory conditions. The bitterness also aids in balancing sweet and heavy qualities in the body.

4. Stimulant

Stevia has a stimulant action, primarily through its glycosides, which can increase alertness and energy levels. This effect is mild compared to caffeine but may support mental clarity and focus. It is generally considered safe for short-term use in moderate amounts.

5. Carminative

Stevia has a carminative action, helping to relieve gas and bloating by promoting the expulsion of excess air from the digestive tract. This effect supports digestion and can alleviate discomfort associated with gastrointestinal issues. Its mild nature makes it a safe herbal option for those seeking natural digestive support.

6. Diuretic

Steaia has a diuretic action, promoting the excretion of urine through increased kidney function. This effect helps in reducing fluid retention and supporting cardiovascular health. It is considered a mild diuretic, making it suitable for those seeking natural support for urinary system function.

7. Sedative

Stevia has a sedative action, which can help calm the nervous system and reduce anxiety. It is believed to work by interacting with neurotransmitters that regulate mood and stress response. This effect makes it a useful herb in herbal medicine for promoting relaxation and improving sleep quality.

8. Antispasmodic

Sativa has an antispasmodic action, helping to relieve muscle spasms and cramps by relaxing smooth muscle tissue. This effect is attributed to its bioactive compounds, which may modulate nerve impulses and reduce excessive muscle contractions. It is commonly used in herbal medicine to support digestive and respiratory health.

9. Expectorant

Stevia has an expectorant action, helping to loosen and expel mucus from the respiratory tract. This effect is attributed to its compounds, which may stimulate the secretion of fluids in the bronchial passages. It is often used in herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms of coughs and congestion.

10. Astringent

Sativa has an astringent action, which helps reduce secretions and tighten tissues. This property makes it useful for soothing sore throats, reducing excessive saliva, and supporting oral health. The astringent effect is due to compounds like stevioside and rebaudioside. It is commonly used in herbal formulations for digestive and respiratory conditions.

11. Sweet

Stevia has a sweet action, primarily due to its steviol glycosides, which mimic the effects of sugar without contributing calories. This sweetness is non-caloric and can aid in managing blood sugar levels, making it a popular natural sweetener for those seeking alternatives to sugar.
