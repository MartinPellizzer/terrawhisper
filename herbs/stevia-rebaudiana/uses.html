Stevia (Stevia rebaudiana) Uses

Stevia rebaudiana is used to manage hypertension, high cholesterol, and hyperglycemia. It helps regulate blood sugar levels, making it beneficial for diabetes mellitus and obesity. It may also support weight management and reduce stress-related fatigue and insomnia.

Stevia (Stevia rebaudiana) Monograph

1. Hypertension

Stevia rebaudiana has been studied for its potential to help manage hypertension. Its compounds, such as stevioside, may act as vasodilators, improving blood flow and reducing blood pressure. Research suggests it may support cardiovascular health when used as part of a holistic approach to blood pressure management.

2. High cholesterol

Stevia rebaudiana has been studied for its potential to help manage high cholesterol. Preliminary research suggests it may reduce low-density lipoprotein (LDL) levels and improve lipid profiles. Its bioactive compounds may inhibit cholesterol absorption and promote cardiovascular health. However, more clinical studies are needed to confirm these effects.

3. Hyperglycemia

Stevia rebaudiana has been studied for its potential to help manage hyperglycemia. Its leaves contain stevioside, a natural sweetener that does not raise blood sugar levels. Research suggests it may improve insulin sensitivity and reduce glucose absorption. It is often used as a safe, non-caloric alternative to sugar for individuals with diabetes.

4. Diabetes mellitus

Stevia rebaudiana has shown potential in managing diabetes mellitus due to its hypoglycemic properties. It may help regulate blood sugar levels by improving insulin sensitivity and reducing glucose absorption. Clinical studies suggest it can complement traditional diabetes management when used as part of a balanced diet and lifestyle.

5. High blood sugar

Stevia rebaudiana has been studied for its potential to support blood sugar management. It contains compounds like stevioside that may enhance insulin sensitivity and reduce glucose absorption. Some research suggests it may help regulate blood glucose levels, though more studies are needed to confirm its efficacy in managing high blood sugar.

6. Obesity

Stevia rebaudiana has been studied for its potential role in managing obesity due to its zero-calorie sweetener properties. It may help reduce overall calorie intake and support weight management when used as a natural alternative to sugar. Some research suggests it may also influence metabolic processes, though more studies are needed to confirm its effectiveness in obesity treatment.

7. Weight gain

Stevia rebaudiana is not typically used for weight gain; in fact, it is often recommended as a natural sweetener for those managing their weight. However, some traditional practices suggest it may support metabolic health, though scientific evidence for weight gain is limited. Its primary role remains as a low-calorie sweetener rather than a weight-promoting herb.

8. Insomnia

Stevia rebaudiana may aid insomnia by promoting relaxation and reducing anxiety through its calming effects. It contains compounds that may support nervous system balance, helping to ease restlessness and improve sleep quality. However, individual responses may vary, and it is best used as a complementary support rather than a standalone treatment.

9. Fatigue

Stevia rebaudiana is used to combat fatigue due to its adaptogenic properties, which help the body manage stress and enhance energy levels. It supports metabolic function and may improve stamina, making it a valued herb in herbal medicine for those experiencing chronic fatigue or low vitality.

10. Stress

Stevia rebaudiana has been used in herbal medicine to help manage stress due to its adaptogenic properties. It supports the body's ability to cope with stress by regulating cortisol levels and promoting relaxation. Its natural sweetness also makes it a healthier alternative to sugar, aiding in mood stabilization.
