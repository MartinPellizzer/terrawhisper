Thalictrum flavum

What's the taxonomical classification of Thalictrum flavum?

Thalictrum flavum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its systematic position continues through the order Ranunculales and the family Ranunculaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its genus, Thalictrum, and its specific epithet, flavum.

    What are the herbal preparations of Thalictrum flavum?

    Thalictrum flavum is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a tincture using the dried aerial parts of the plant. The roots and stems are often boiled in water to create a potent liquid extract for traditional medicinal use. In some regional practices, the dried herb is also incorporated into poultices to be applied directly to the skin. Tinctures made with alcohol serve as a concentrated method for preserving the plant's active compounds for long-term storage. These various forms allow for different modes of administration depending on the specific therapeutic goal.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Thalictrum flavum can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested or handled improperly. The plant contains various alkaloids and chemical compounds that may act as irritants to the sensitive tissues of the skin. Contact with the leaves or stems can result in redness, itching, or localized dermatitis in susceptible individuals. If consumed, the botanical components may lead to symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. Due to these potential toxicological effects, careful handling and avoidance of ingestion are highly recommended.
