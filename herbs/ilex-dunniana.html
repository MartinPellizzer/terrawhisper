What's the taxonomical classification of Ilex dunniana?

Ilex dunniana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Aquifoliales and the family Aquifoliaceae. Finally, the plant is placed within the genus Ilex, specifically identifying as the species dunniana.

    What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

    This plant contains the specific medicinal compounds ilexdunnoside A and ilexdunnoside B. These bioactive molecules belong to a class of compounds that researchers study for their potential therapeutic properties. Scientists often analyze these substances to understand how they interact with biological systems at a molecular level. Preliminary studies suggest that such chemical constituents may play a significant role in the plant's overall pharmacological profile. Further investigation into these specific glycosides could lead to new advancements in drug development.

    As discussed in a study, the roots of Ilex dunniana Levl. contain a new triterpene glycoside ilexdunnoside A (1) and a new sulfated triterpene derivative ilexdunnoside B (2), along with five known analogues (3-7). The 75% ethanol and n-butanol extracts of the plant displayed anti-inflammatory activities against ear edema in mice with inhibition rates of 23.5% and 37.5% at a dose of 50 mg/kg. Compounds 1, 2, and 3 exhibited moderate indirect inhibitory effects on lipopolysaccharide-induced NO production in BV2 microglial cells in vitro. These specific compounds showed IC50 values of 11.60, 12.30, and 9.70 μM, respectively.
