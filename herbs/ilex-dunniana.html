Ilex dunniana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ilex dunniana has small, evergreen leaves that are typically elliptic to ovate in shape, possessing a leathery texture and an entire margin. The foliage is characterized by a dark green upper surface and a paler underside, which helps it withstand environmental stressors. Its stems exhibit a woody structure common to the holly genus, providing a sturdy framework for the plant's growth. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils, eventually giving way to dark, spherical drupes. These berries serve as a primary morphological feature during the fruiting season.
