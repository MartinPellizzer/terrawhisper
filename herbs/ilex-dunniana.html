Ilex dunniana

What's the taxonomical classification of Ilex dunniana?

Ilex dunniana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Aquifoliales and the family Aquifoliaceae. Finally, the plant is placed within the genus Ilex, specifically identifying as the species dunniana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ilex dunniana has small, evergreen leaves that are typically elliptic to ovate in shape, possessing a leathery texture and an entire margin. The foliage is characterized by a dark green upper surface and a paler underside, which helps it withstand environmental stressors. Its stems exhibit a woody structure common to the holly genus, providing a sturdy framework for the plant's growth. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils, eventually giving way to dark, spherical drupes. These berries serve as a primary morphological feature during the fruiting season.

What are the pharmacological activities of Ilex dunniana?

This plant has anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial pharmacological activities derived from its diverse bioactive secondary metabolites. Researchers have identified that the extracts of Ilex dunniana effectively reduce swelling and inhibit inflammatory mediators in biological models. Beyond its ability to combat inflammation, the plant exhibits significant potential in neutralizing harmful free radicals through its high phenolic content. These antioxidant properties work alongside its antimicrobial effects to protect cells from oxidative stress and pathogen invasion. Consequently, the chemical profile of Ilex dunniana makes it a promising candidate for future therapeutic drug development.

According to a study published by "Molecules (Basel, Switzerland)", 75% ethanol and n-butanol extracts of the roots of Ilex dunniana Levl. displayed anti-inflammatory activities against ear edema in mice. At a dose of 50 mg/kg, these extracts showed inhibition rates of 23.5% and 37.5%, respectively. Additionally, compounds 1, 2, and 3 from the plant exhibited moderate indirect inhibitory effects on lipopolysaccharide-induced NO production in BV2 microglial cells in vitro. These specific compounds demonstrated IC50 values of 11.60, 12.30, and 9.70 μM, respectively.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the unique medicinal compounds ilexdunnoside A and ilexdunnoside B. These specific iridoid glycosides are identified as the primary bioactive constituents within the species. Researchers have isolated these molecules to study their potential pharmacological properties and chemical structures. The discovery of these compounds highlights the plant's importance in phytochemical research. Further investigation into these glycosides may reveal new therapeutic applications for medicinal chemistry.
