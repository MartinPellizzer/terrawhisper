What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pteris dissitifolia has pinnate fronds characterized by widely spaced, oblong to lanceolate pinnae that are arranged alternately along a central rachis. The segments of the leaves are typically entire or slightly lobed with smooth margins and lack the deep indentations found in many other fern species. These fronds emerge from a creeping rhizome and are supported by a slender, often brownish stipe. Small, inconspicuous sori are located on the underside of the pinnae, usually positioned near the margins to facilitate spore dispersal. The overall structure of the plant presents a delicate, airy appearance due to the significant gaps between its individual leaf components.
