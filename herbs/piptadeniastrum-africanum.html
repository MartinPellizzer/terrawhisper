Piptadeniastrum africanum

What's the taxonomical classification of Piptadeniastrum africanum?

Piptadenia strum africanum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the order Fabales and the family Fabaceae. Finally, its specific identity is defined by its classification within the genus Piptadenia strum and its unique species designation, africanum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Piptadeniastrum africanum has an upright, woody growth habit characterized by simple, opposite leaves that are elliptic to oblong in shape with entire margins and prominent pinnate venation. The foliage typically displays a dark green color on the upper surface and a lighter shade underneath. Small, fragrant flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often appearing in clusters or solitary arrangements. These blossoms lead to the development of small, fleshy fruits that are essential to the plant's reproductive cycle. The bark of the stem is generally smooth and serves as a protective layer for the internal vascular tissues.

What are the pharmacological activities of Piptadeniastrum africanum?

This plant has anticancer, antioxidant, cytotoxic, neuromodulatory, and tyrosinase inhibitory activities. The presence of bioactive compounds allows it to combat oxidative stress and prevent cellular damage. Its cytotoxic properties enable it to inhibit the proliferation of various malignant cell lines. Furthermore, the plant exhibits neuromodulatory effects that may influence nervous system functions. It also demonstrates tyrosinase inhibitory activity, which is useful for managing skin pigmentation.

As discussed in a study, the Piptadeniastrum africanum stembark extract demonstrated the highest cytotoxicity on brine shrimps with an LC50 of 1.58 μg/mL compared to vincristine sulphate which had an LC50 of 2.24 μg/mL. All extracts, including Piptadeniastrum africanum, produced significant growth inhibitory effects against all tested cell lines in a dose-dependent manner with a p < 0.05. The Piptadeniastrum africanum stembark extract exhibited 2-fold selectivity toward MCF-7 cancer cells relative to normal lung fibroblast (MRC-5). This extract also significantly inhibited cell migration and colony formation in MCF-7-treated cells in dose-dependent manners. These findings advocate for the continued development of Piptadeniastrum africanum stembark as a potential chemotherapeutic agent.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds apiagenin, liquiritigenin, luteolin, naringenin, and oleanolic acid. These specific phytochemicals are recognized for their diverse biological activities and potential therapeutic benefits. Many of these flavonoids contribute to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Additionally, the presence of oleanolic acid provides significant triterpenoid benefits for cellular health. Together, these substances form a complex chemical profile that supports various medicinal applications.

As discussed in a study, the methanol extract of Piptadeniastrum africanum stem bark contains 867.42 mg quillaja equivalent/g of saponin and 33.81 mg catechin equivalent/g of tannin. This plant contains multiple isomers including dihydroxy-trimethoxy(iso)flavone, loliolide, eriodictyol, naringenin, luteolin, chrysoeriol, apigenin, and liquiritigenin. The methanol extract demonstrated inhibitory activity against acetylcholinesterase at 4.88 mg galantaamine equivalent (GALAE)/g extract, butyrylcholinesterase at 5.37 mg GALAE/g extract, and tyrosinase at 154.86 mg kojic acid equivalent/g extract. Furthermore, the ethyl acetate extract inhibited α-glucosidase at 15.22 mmol acarbose equivalent/g extract. Antioxidant properties in the methanol extract were measured at 493.87 mg Trolox equivalent/g for DPPH, 818.12 mg for ABTS, 953.07 mg for CUPRAC, and 732.19 mg for FRAP.
