Scientific and Botanical Profile

Lomatium Dissectum, with botanical name Lomatium dissectum, is a member of the Apiaceae family, commonly known as the carrot or parsley family.

Native to the western United States, including regions of Washington, Oregon, and California, it thrives in open habitats such as prairies, meadows, and rocky slopes. Morphologically, it is a perennial herb characterized by a thick, short rhizome, with basal leaves that are dissected into fern-like segments, giving it the common name Fernleaf Lomatium. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, followed by oval, ribbed fruits.

Common names for this species include Lomatium Dissectum, Common Camas, Lomatium Camas, Fawn Lomatium, and Common Sneezeweed, among others, reflecting its diverse regional and cultural significance.

For safe use, it is recommended to consult a healthcare professional, start with low doses, and monitor for adverse reactions, especially when using it for extended periods.

Growing, Harvesting and Storage

Lomatium dissectum grows in well-drained, sandy or loamy soils in full sun to partial shade, preferring cooler climates with moderate moisture levels.

It thrives in USDA hardiness zones 4 to 7, where it can be planted in the spring after the last frost, with spacing of about 12 to 18 inches between plants to allow for root development. Regular watering is necessary during the growing season, but the plant is drought-tolerant once established, so overwatering should be avoided to prevent root rot. To ensure optimal growth, mulching around the base of the plant helps retain soil moisture and suppress weeds, while regular inspection for pests and diseases is recommended. Harvesting is best done in late summer to early fall when the leaves and roots are most potent, using a garden knife to carefully dig up the entire plant, ensuring minimal damage to the roots.

After harvesting, the roots should be cleaned, cut into small pieces, and dried in a well-ventilated area or using a dehydrator to preserve their potency, then stored in airtight containers in a cool, dark place to maintain their medicinal properties.
