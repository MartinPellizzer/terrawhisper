Lomatium dissectum

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Lomatium dissectum, commonly known as dissected lomatium, is a perennial herb native to western North America. It belongs to the Apiaceae family and is characterized by its finely dissected leaves and yellow flowers. Synonyms include "bishop's weed" and "mountain parsley."

Active Compounds

Lomatium dissectum contains key active compounds such as flavonoids, terpenoids, and coumarins. These compounds work synergistically to support immune function and reduce inflammation. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Terpenoid
  • Coumarin

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action
Respiratory Respiratory infections, bronchitis, cough, mucus buildup Antimicrobial, expectorant, anti-inflammatory, immunostimulant
Integumentary Skin infections, wound healing, inflammatory skin conditions, fungal infections, skin inflammation Antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, antifungal
Immune Allergic reactions, immune support, allergies Anti-inflammatory, immunostimulant

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for respiratory and digestive ailments due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties.

Infusion: Used for mild respiratory issues and as a general tonic due to its mild stimulating effects.

Poultice: Applied externally for skin irritations and wounds due to its anti-inflammatory and healing properties.

Safety Profile

Lomatium dissectum is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation due to potential risks. Individuals with allergies to related plants should also exercise caution.
