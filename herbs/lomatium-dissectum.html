What is Lomatium Dissectum (Lomatium Dissectum)?

Lomatium dissectum, also known as dissected lomatium, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada, characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small yellow flowers.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Lomatium dissectum is Lomatium dissectum. This plant belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Apiales, family Araliaceae, genus Lomatium, and species Lomatium dissectum. It is commonly known as the dissected lomatium in North America and the Pacific Northwest. In European regions, it is referred to as the mountain parsley or the western cowparsley. The synonyms for this plant include Lomatium dissectum, Lomatium campestre, and Lomatium triternatum.
