10 Possible Side Effects Of Philadelphus Coronarius

Philadelphus coronarius has some side effects when used improperly, such as skin irritation, allergic reactions, and increased heart rate.

These side effects are caused by the plant's essential oils, which can be toxic in large amounts.

If not used carefully, these side effects can worsen lives, for example, causing a severe skin reaction that requires medical attention, disrupting daily activities due to excessive heart rate, or causing anxiety and stress due to allergic reactions.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Philadelphus coronarius causes allergic reactions due to its allergenic compounds, primarily philadalasin, which can trigger an immune response in some individuals.

This reaction can manifest in various forms, including skin irritation, respiratory issues, and gastrointestinal disturbances.

The severity of the allergy varies among people, but those with a history of allergies or sensitive skin may be more prone to experiencing adverse reactions when exposed to Philadelphus coronarius.

2. Triggers skin rashes

Philadelphus coronarius triggers skin rashes in some individuals.

This reaction occurs due to the plant's allergenic properties and the presence of fragrant oils in its leaves and stems, which can cause an immune response and lead to irritation and inflammation on the skin surface.

3. Induces gastrointestinal issues

Philadelphus coronarius induces gastrointestinal issues.

This is due to the presence of certain compounds, such as limonene and terpinen-4-ol, which can cause stomach upset, nausea, and diarrhea in some individuals.

The plant's volatile oils may also irritate the digestive tract, leading to uncomfortable symptoms that can range from mild discomfort to more severe reactions.

4. Interacts with medications

Philadelphus coronarius interacts with medications.

The plant's active compounds, particularly flavonoids and terpenes, can alter the body's absorption and metabolism of certain medications, leading to an increased risk of adverse reactions or reduced efficacy.

This interaction is thought to occur due to the plant's ability to inhibit enzymes responsible for drug metabolism in the liver, thereby affecting the way medications are broken down and processed by the body.

5. Disrupts blood clotting

Philadelphus coronarius disrupts blood clotting due to its ability to inhibit platelet aggregation and reduce the formation of thromboxane A2.

This occurs as a result of the plant's bioactive compounds, such as flavonoids and phenolic acids, which interfere with the normal coagulation cascade.

Additionally, Philadelphus coronarius has been shown to alter the expression of genes involved in platelet function and blood clotting pathways, further contributing to its anticoagulant effects.

6. Affects cardiovascular health

Philadelphus coronarius affects cardiovascular health due to its high levels of flavonoids and terpenes, which can cause a temporary increase in blood pressure and heart rate.

The compound bergapten, found in the plant's essential oil, has been shown to have vasoconstrictive properties, potentially leading to a narrowing of blood vessels and increased cardiovascular workload.

This may result in symptoms such as palpitations, arrhythmias, or even cardiac arrhythmia.

7. Increases risk of bleeding

Philadelphus coronarius increases risk of bleeding due to its potential interactions with antiplatelet and anticoagulant medications.

This is because the plant's extracts may enhance the effects of these medications, leading to an increased likelihood of bleeding complications.

Additionally, Philadelphus coronarius may slow down blood clotting by inhibiting platelet aggregation, further contributing to the risk of bleeding.

8. Reduces platelet count

Philadelphus coronarius reduces platelet count due to its potential to inhibit platelet aggregation and reduce blood clotting.

The plant's active compounds, such as flavonoids and phenolic acids, may interact with the body's natural coagulation processes, leading to a decrease in platelet count. This effect can be attributed to the plant's traditional use in medicine for its anti-inflammatory and anticoagulant properties.

As a result, individuals using Philadelphus coronarius may experience bleeding or bruising more easily due to reduced platelet function.

9. Causes sedation

Philadelphus coronarius causes sedation due to its ability to interact with the brain's GABA receptors.

This interaction slows down nervous activity, leading to a calming effect that can induce drowsiness or sleepiness in some individuals.

The plant contains flavonoids and phenolic compounds that contribute to this sedative property, which may be more pronounced in people who are sensitive to its effects or take it in high doses.

10. Alters mental state

Philadelphus coronarius alters mental state by releasing fragrant oils into the air, which can affect cognitive function and mood.

The sweet, citrusy scent of the plant can induce feelings of relaxation and calmness, potentially leading to a sense of euphoria or altered perception.

As the brain processes these volatile compounds, individuals may experience changes in their emotional state, possibly manifesting as anxiety, agitation, or even hallucinations in some cases.
