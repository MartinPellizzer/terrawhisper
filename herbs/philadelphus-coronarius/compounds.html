Coronary Philadelphus (Philadelphus coronarius) Compounds

The primary compounds in Coronary Philadelphus include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and coumarin, known for its anticoagulant effects. Phenolic acids and tannins contribute to its astringent qualities, while glycosides and terpenoids support its medicinal uses. Essential oils, saponins, alkaloids, and anthocyanins further enhance its therapeutic potential.

Coronary Philadelphus (Philadelphus coronarius) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Coronary Philadelphus (Philadelphus coronarius) are bioactive compounds known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating cardiovascular conditions and respiratory ailments. They help protect cells from oxidative stress and may support overall cardiovascular health.

2. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Coronary Philadelphus (Philadelphus coronarius). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly in promoting circulation and reducing inflammation. Coumarin is also known for its potential anticoagulant effects, supporting cardiovascular health.

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Coronary Philadelphus (Philadelphus coronarius).
