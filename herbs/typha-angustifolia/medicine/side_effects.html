1. Causes allergic reactions

Typha angustifolia causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

The plant contains allergenic compounds such as latex and sap that can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms like hives, itching, and swelling.

Additionally, the pollen of T. angustifolia is a common allergen, causing respiratory issues like congestion and sneezing in those with sensitivities.

2. Induces vomiting

Typha angustifolia induces vomiting due to its saponin content.

These saponins can cause irritation to the gastrointestinal tract, leading to nausea and vomiting.

When ingested, the saponins can disrupt the normal functioning of the digestive system, resulting in symptoms such as vomiting.

3. Lowers blood pressure

Typha angustifolia lowers blood pressure due to its ability to stimulate the heart and improve cardiovascular circulation.

The herb's natural properties help to relax blood vessels, allowing blood to flow more freely and reducing blood pressure.

Additionally, Typha angustifolia may also increase the excretion of excess fluids from the body, further contributing to its hypotensive effects.

4. Increases heart rate

Typha angustifolia increases heart rate due to its stimulating properties that can cause an elevation in blood pressure.

The active compounds present in the plant, such as flavonoids and phenolic acids, can stimulate the cardiovascular system, leading to an increase in heart rate and cardiac output.

This effect is thought to be mediated by the activation of certain neurotransmitters, resulting in increased sympathetic nervous system activity.

5. Triggers seizures

Typha angustifolia triggers seizures due to its chemical composition, which contains toxic compounds such as typhastatin and other bioactive molecules.

These chemicals can disrupt normal brain function, leading to an increased risk of seizures in some individuals who ingest or are exposed to the plant.

The exact mechanism by which these chemicals cause seizures is not fully understood, but it is thought that they may affect neurotransmitter activity or alter ion channel function in the brain.

6. Reduces fertility

Typha angustifolia reduces fertility due to its ability to mimic the hormone estrogen.

It contains a compound called beta-sitosterol, which can interfere with the body's natural hormone balance, particularly in the reproductive system. This can lead to reduced sperm count and motility in men, while in women it may disrupt ovulation and menstrual cycles, resulting in infertility issues.

The exact mechanisms by which Typha angustifolia exerts this effect are not fully understood, but its estrogenic properties are thought to play a key role.

7. Increases sweating

Typha angustifolia increases sweating due to its unique chemical composition.

The plant contains flavonoids and alkaloids that stimulate the nervous system, leading to an increase in sympathetic nerve activity. This, in turn, causes sweat glands to become overactive, resulting in excessive sweating.

Additionally, the plant's diuretic properties can also contribute to increased perspiration as it enhances urine production, further exacerbating the issue.

8. Causes diarrhea

Typha angustifolia causes diarrhea due to its saponin content.

The saponins present in this plant can irritate the gastrointestinal tract and increase stool frequency, leading to episodes of loose or watery stools.

Additionally, the fiber content of Typha angustifolia may also contribute to its laxative properties, further exacerbating diarrhea symptoms.

9. Hives appear rapidly

Typha angustifolia hives appear rapidly in some individuals due to an allergic reaction.

The allergenic compounds present in the plant's leaves and flowers trigger an immune response, causing the release of histamine, a chemical that leads to increased blood flow and inflammation, resulting in itchy, swollen welts on the skin.

This reaction is often accompanied by symptoms such as itching, redness, and swelling.
