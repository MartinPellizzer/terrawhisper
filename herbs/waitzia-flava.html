What is Waitzia flava?

Waitzia flava is a flowering plant endemic to Western Australia, belonging to the family Iridaceae.

It is a perennial herb with linear to lance-shaped leaves, typically 15-30 cm long, and a stout stem. The plant produces yellow flowers, 3-4 cm long, with three outer tepals and three inner tepals, the outer ones being more or less equal in size to the inner ones. The flowers have six stamens and a three-lobed style.

The plant reproduces via seeds, which are contained in a capsule.
