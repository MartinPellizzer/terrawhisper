Gentiana triflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Gentiana triflora?

Gentiana triflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Gentianaales. This plant is a member of the family Gentianaceae, falling within the genus Gentiana, and is specifically identified by the species epithet triflora.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gentiana triflora has upright, unbranched stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and are adorned with opposite, lanceolate leaves. The foliage is characterized by a smooth texture and distinct parallel venation that runs along the length of each leaf. At the terminal end of each stem, the plant produces three distinct, bell-shaped flowers that are deep blue or violet in color. These blossoms feature prominent longitudinal stripes and possess five fused petals forming a tubular corolla. The root system consists of a sturdy, woody caudex from which the seasonal growth emerges each spring.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the montane and subalpine regions of western North America, ranging from southern British Columbia through the Rocky Mountains to New Mexico. It primarily inhabits high-elevation environments such as meadows, alpine tundra, and rocky slopes. Within these habitats, it thrives in moist soils often found near melting snowbanks or in subalpine fir forests. The species is frequently encountered in the mountain ranges of Idaho, Montana, Wyoming, and Colorado. Its distribution is strictly tied to these cool, high-altitude ecosystems across the western United States and Canada.
