Tectaria subpedata

What's the taxonomical classification of Tectaria subpedata?

Tecartia subpedata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the family Polypodiaceae, which contains the genus Tecartia, eventually reaching its specific designation as the species subpedata.

Some traditional practices also involve using extracts from the plant to soothe localized swelling and reduce irritation. These therapeutic actions make it a significant component in various ethnobotanical remedies for dermatological care.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Tectaria subpedata can cause severe skin irritation, localized swelling, and painful rashes upon direct contact with the human epidermis. These dermatological reactions often manifest as intense itching or redness in the areas where the plant's sap or foliage touched the skin. Some individuals may experience more systemic symptoms such as mild nausea or dizziness if the plant material is accidentally ingested. Prolonged exposure to the plant's chemical compounds can lead to more persistent allergic contact dermatitis. Monitoring the site of contact for increased inflammation is recommended for anyone handling the specimen.
