Tectaria subpedata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tectaria subpedata has narrow, linear leaves that are approximately 2 to 4 centimeters wide and characterized by an entire margin with slightly undulate edges. The leaf bases are typically tapered, while the apex often concludes in a short, blunt point. The foliage displays a deep green coloration and a smooth, somewhat leathery texture that helps it retain moisture. Stems are relatively slender and can grow in dense clusters, providing structural support for the spreading leaves. These morphological traits collectively allow the plant to adapt to its specific ecological niche.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Tectaria subpedata can cause severe skin irritation, localized swelling, and painful rashes upon direct contact with the human epidermis. These dermatological reactions often manifest as intense itching or redness in the areas where the plant's sap or foliage touched the skin. Some individuals may experience more systemic symptoms such as mild nausea or dizziness if the plant material is accidentally ingested. Prolonged exposure to the plant's chemical compounds can lead to more persistent allergic contact dermatitis. Monitoring the site of contact for increased inflammation is recommended for anyone handling the specimen.
