Dolichos lablab belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds dolichin, flosdolilabflavonosides A1-A5, flosdolilabflavonosides B (1-6), raffinose, and stachyose. These specific chemical constituents contribute to the complex phytochemical profile of the species. The flosdolilabflavonosides are categorized into distinct A and B series groups within the plant tissue. Additionally, the presence of the oligosaccharides raffinose and stachyose adds to its nutritional and bioactive composition. These various elements work together to define the plant's unique pharmacological properties.

As discussed in a study, the seeds of the field bean Dolicho s lablab contain an antifungal protein designated dolichin. This purified protein possesses a molecular weight of 28 kDa and has an N-terminal sequence resembling chitinases. Dolichiin exhibited antifungal activity against the fungi Fusarium oxysporum, Rhizoctonia solani, and Coprinus comaatus. Furthermore, the protein was capable of inhibiting human immuno deficiency virus (HIV) reverse transcriptase and alpha- and beta-glucosidases. It also demonstrated very low ribonuclease and cell-free translation-inhibitory activities.
