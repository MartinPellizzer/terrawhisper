Dolichos lablab

What's the taxonomical classification of Dolichos lablab?

Dolichos lablab belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant falls under the order Fabales and is a member of the family Fabaceae. Finally, its specific biological classification is defined by the genus Dolichos and the species lablab.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dolichos lablab has trifoliate leaves consisting of three ovate to lanceolate leaflets, climbing stems that utilize twining tendrils, and papilionaceous flowers that transition from white to purple.

The plant is a vigorous perennial or annual climber that can reach significant heights through its rapid vegetative growth. Its flowers are typically arranged in axillary racemes and possess a distinct structure characteristic of the Fabaceae family. The resulting pods are elongated, flattened, and contain multiple seeds that vary in color and shape depending on the cultivar.

These morphological traits allow the species to effectively compete for sunlight in tropical and subtropical environments.

Dolicho s lablab contains medicinal properties found in its seeds, leaves, stems, and roots. What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Dolicho lablab targets various enzymes and signaling proteins including cyclooxygenase, alpha-glucosidase, and various proinflammatory cytokines to exert its medicinal effects. Its bioactive compounds, particularly flavonoids and polyphenols, act as competitive inhibitors of alpha-glucosidase to help manage glycemic levels in diabetic models. These phytochemicals also modulate the arachidonic acid metabolism pathway by inhibiting cyclooxygenase enzymes, which effectively reduces systemic inflammation. Furthermore, the plant's extracts interact with nuclear factor-kappa B signaling pathways to suppress the production of inflammatory mediators like tumor necrosis factor-alpha. Through these diverse molecular interactions, the plant demonstrates significant antioxidant, antidiabetic, and anti-inflammatory properties.

What are the herbal preparations of Dolichos lablab?

Dolicho's lablab is prepared as a decoction of the dried seeds, a powder made from crushed beans, or a concentrated juice extracted from the fresh leaves. These various forms are used to harness the plant's nutritional and medicinal properties for different therapeutic goals. The powdered seeds are often incorporated into dietary supplements to increase protein and fiber intake. In traditional practices, a decoction is simmered in water to extract soluble compounds for drinking. Additionally, topical applications of leaf extracts are sometimes utilized in regional folk medicine.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Dolicho s lablab can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, including nausea, vomiting, bloating, and abdominal pain, if the seeds or immature pods are consumed before they are properly cooked. These adverse reactions are primarily due to the presence of high concentrations of cyanogenic glycosides and lectins found in the raw plant material. If ingested improperly, the toxins can lead to more serious symptoms such as dizziness or even cyanide poisoning. To mitigate these risks, the plant must undergo thorough boiling or heat treatment to neutralize the harmful compounds. Careful preparation is essential to ensure the edible parts are safe for human consumption.
