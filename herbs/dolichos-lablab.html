Dolichos lablab belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant falls under the order Fabales and is a member of the family Fabaceae. Finally, its specific biological classification is defined by the genus Dolichos and the species lablab.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dolichos lablab has trifoliate leaves consisting of three ovate to lanceolate leaflets, climbing stems that utilize twining tendrils, and papilionaceous flowers that transition from white to purple. The plant is a vigorous perennial or annual climber that can reach significant heights through its rapid vegetative growth. Its flowers are typically arranged in axillary racemes and possess a distinct structure characteristic of the Fabaceae family. The resulting pods are elongated, flattened, and contain multiple seeds that vary in color and shape depending on the cultivar. These morphological traits allow the species to effectively compete for sunlight in tropical and subtropical environments.

As discussed in a study, the flowers of Dolicho lablab L. possess anti-inflammatory activity that might work by suppressing the activation of the NLRP3 inflammasome. During the exploration of these flowers, fourteen compounds numbered 1-14 were found, which included seven previously unreported ones named flosdolilabnitrogenousols A-D (1-4) and flosdolilabsaponins A-C (5-7). One specific ingredient, flosdolilabsaponin A (5), is an exceptionally rare tetracyclic lactone oleane-type saponin. When evaluated using BMDMs stimulated with LPS/Nigericin, compounds 1, 8, 9, and 11-14 exhibited significant inhibition of IL-1β release at a concentration of 30 μM. This inhibitory activity occurred without cytotoxicity.

As discussed in a study, the seeds of the field bean Dolicho s lablab contain a new chitinase-like antifungal protein known as dolichi n. This purified protein possesses a molecular weight of 28 kDa and an N-terminal sequence that resembles chitinases. Dolichi n exhibits antifungal activity against the fungi Fusarium oxysporum, Rhizoctonia solani, and Coprinus coma tus. Additionally, the protein is capable of inhibiting human immuno defi ciency virus (HIV) reverse transcriptase as well as alpha- and beta-glucosidase s. What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause severe gastrointestinal distress and toxicity if the seeds are consumed without being boiled first. Consuming unboiled seeds introduces natural toxins into the digestive system that trigger various adverse reactions. Common symptoms of this poisoning include intense nausea and vomiting shortly after ingestion. Patients may also experience significant abdominal pain and persistent diarrhea. Additionally, the consumption of raw seeds can lead to uncomfortable flatulence.
