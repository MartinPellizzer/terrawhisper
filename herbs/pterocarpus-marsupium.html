Pterocarpus Marsupium Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Pterocarpus marsupium, commonly known as Indian Kino or Banyan bark, is a medicinal herb native to India, valued for its therapeutic properties.

It is derived from the bark of the Pterocarpus marsupium tree and has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine for its health benefits.

This herb is known to support blood sugar regulation, reduce inflammation, and promote cardiovascular health due to its bioactive constituents such as tannins, flavonoids, and alkaloids.

It can be prepared as an infusion, decoction, or powder for internal use, and is also used in topical applications for skin conditions.

Health Benefits of pterocarpus marsupium

Pterocarpus marsupium has many health benefits, such as reducing blood sugar due to its active compounds, like isoquercetin and pterosin, which enhance insulin sensitivity and promote glucose uptake by cells.

It manages diabetes symptoms by improving pancreatic function and reducing oxidative stress, which helps in maintaining stable blood glucose levels over time.

Pterocarpus marsupium supports heart health by lowering inflammation and improving vascular function, which reduces the risk of cardiovascular diseases.

It lowers cholesterol by inhibiting the absorption of low-density lipoprotein (LDL) and promoting the metabolism of fats, thereby preventing atherosclerosis and other lipid-related disorders.
