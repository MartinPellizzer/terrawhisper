Pterocarpus Marsupium

Pterocarpus marsupium, commonly known as kino, is a tree native to the tropical forests of India and Southeast Asia, and its heartwood is used to produce the medicinal herb.

This herb has been traditionally used to treat various health conditions, including diarrhea, dysentery, and fever, due to its numerous health benefits such as anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties.

The therapeutic actions of Pterocarpus marsupium include its ability to reduce inflammation, fight off infections, and protect against cell damage, making it a valuable remedy in traditional medicine.
