Pterocarpus

Pterocarpus marsupium

Pterocarpus (Pterocarpus marsupium) is a flowering plant native to India, commonly known as Indian Kino or Pau d'arco, and is widely used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, particularly in improving diabetes management and supporting kidney function.

Botanical Identification

Pterocarpus marsupium, commonly known as Indian satinwood, belongs to the Fabaceae family. It is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent. Morphologically, it has dark bark, compound leaves, and yellow flowers. Synonyms include Pterocarpus indicus and P. erinaceus in traditional herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Fabales
  • Order: Fabales
  • Family: Fabaceae
  • Genus: Pterocarpus
  • Species: Pterocarpus marsupium

Active Compounds

Pterocarpus marsupium contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids. These compounds work synergistically to provide its therapeutic effects, influencing anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and hypoglycemic pathways. Their combined action enhances the herb's medicinal value in traditional and modern herbal medicine.

Safety Profile

Pterocarpus marsupium is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and lactation due to limited safety data. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
