Pterocarpus (Pterocarpus marsupium)

Pterocarpus (Pterocarpus marsupium) is a member of the Fabaceae family, native to India, Sri Lanka, and Indonesia. Traditionally, its bark, roots, and leaves have been used for decoctions, powders, and infusions.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, diuretic, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in ayurvedic medicine, traditional chinese medicine, and korean traditional medicine.

Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 10 to 20 meters.
Leaves
Simple leaves with dorsiventral coloration, upper surface dark brown to black, lower surface pale yellow to pale brown, featuring distinct stomatal bands.
Flowers
Flowers are actinomorphic, with five white to pale yellow petals arranged in a solitary cluster, and possess five stamens and a single pistil with a capitate stigma.
Stems
Woody, cylindrical, smooth, with opposite branching, and bearing distinctive thorn-like protuberances along the surface.
