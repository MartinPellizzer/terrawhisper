Medicinal Parts

Pterocarpus marsupium root is one of the most extensively studied medicinal parts of this plant, known for its rich content of bioactive compounds such as tannins, flavonoids, and alkaloids.

These compounds contribute to its traditional use in treating diabetes, as the root has been shown to enhance insulin sensitivity and reduce blood glucose levels. The root is also valued for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may help in managing conditions like arthritis and cardiovascular diseases. In Ayurvedic medicine, it is often used as a rejuvenating tonic to improve overall health and longevity.

Additionally, the root's ability to promote wound healing and its potential role in liver protection have made it a subject of ongoing scientific research.
