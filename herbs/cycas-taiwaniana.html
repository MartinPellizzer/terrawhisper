Cycas taiwaniana

What's the taxonomical classification of Cycas taiwaniana?

Cycas taiwaniana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Cycadidae. The plant is further categorized under the order Cycadales and the family Cycadaceae. Finally, it is situated within the genus Cycas, where it is specifically identified by its species name, taiwaniana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cycas taiwaniana has a stout, cylindrical trunk that reaches up to 2 meters in height, topped by a crown of pinnate leaves that can extend 1 to 2 meters in length. The leaflets are dark green, coriaceous, and possess a distinctively narrow, linear shape with slightly revolute margins. These leaves emerge from a central rosette and are arranged in a spiral pattern around the apex of the stem. The plant is dioecious, meaning individual specimens are either male or female, and the reproductive structures consist of large, cone-like strobili. The base of the trunk often features a thick, woody texture formed by persistent leaf bases from previous growth cycles.
