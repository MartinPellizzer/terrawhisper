Grewia eriocarpa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Grewia eriocarpa has a woody, deciduous shrub habit characterized by small, lanceolate leaves that are typically 1 to 3 centimeters long and possess finely serrated margins. The foliage is often dark green and leathery, providing a dense texture to the plant's spreading branches. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters that eventually develop into distinctive, woolly fruits. These fruits are covered in dense, white, hair-like structures which give the species its name. The overall structure of the plant is rugged and adapted to survive in arid, rocky environments.
