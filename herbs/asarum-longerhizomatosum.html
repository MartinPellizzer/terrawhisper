Asarum longerhizomatosum

What's the taxonomical classification of Asarum longerhizomatosum?

Asarum longerhizomatosum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down through its hierarchical structure, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It further falls within the order Piperales and the family Aristolochiaceae. Finally, its taxonomic identity is completed by its designation under the genus Asarum and the specific species epithet longerhizomatosum.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Asarum longerhizomatosum can cause significant toxicity including severe gastrointestinal distress, neurological impairment, and potential organ damage due to the presence of aristolochic acids. These specific compounds are known to be nephrotoxic, meaning they can lead to serious kidney injury or failure if ingested. In addition to renal issues, the plant may trigger intense vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea. Some users might also experience central nervous system effects such as dizziness or convulsions. Because of these high risks, the plant should be handled with extreme caution and avoided for medicinal use.
