Adenia gracilis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Adenia gracilis has a large, tuberous caudex that serves as a primary water storage organ and supports slender, climbing stems. These stems are relatively thin and can reach significant lengths as they scramble through surrounding vegetation. The leaves are typically trifoliate or simple, displaying a delicate texture that contrasts with the robust base. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the plant, often appearing in clusters. The overall structure is specialized for survival in arid environments where moisture must be sequestered underground.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Adenia gracilis can cause severe skin irritation, chemical burns, or allergic reactions if the plant's sap comes into direct contact with human skin. The milky latex contains toxic compounds that may trigger redness, swelling, or intense itching upon exposure. If this sap accidentally enters the eyes, it can lead to painful inflammation or potential damage to the corneal tissue. Ingesting any part of the plant is also dangerous and may result in systemic toxicity or gastrointestinal distress. Proper protective gear should always be worn when handling this species to prevent accidental contact with its caustic fluids.
