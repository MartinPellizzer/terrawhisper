Adenia gracilis

What's the taxonomical classification of Adenia gracilis?

Adenia gracilis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized as Equisetopsida, falling under the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant is further organized into the order Malpighiales and is a member of the family Passifloraceae. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Adenia, and its specific biological identity is defined by its species name, gracilis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Adenia gracilis contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, saponins, and terpenoids that exhibit significant pharmacological potential. These specific chemical constituents are often studied for their ability to demonstrate antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Researchers have focused on the plant's unique glycosides which may play a role in regulating biological processes within cellular environments. Some studies also suggest that the presence of certain phenolic compounds contributes to its antioxidant capabilities. This diverse chemical profile makes the species a subject of interest for developing new natural therapeutic agents.

What are the therapeutic actions of Adenia gracilis?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, intestinal worms, and various inflammatory conditions. The various parts of the plant, including the roots and stems, are often prepared as decoctions or poultices by local healers. These medicinal preparations are applied topically to soothe skin ailments or ingested to combat parasitic infestations. Some traditional practices also involve using the plant to manage internal discomfort and fever. Its specific chemical properties are believed to provide the necessary relief for these diverse health issues.
