Adenia gracilis

What's the taxonomical classification of Adenia gracilis?

Adenia gracilis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized as Equisetopsida, falling under the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant is further organized into the order Malpighiales and is a member of the family Passifloraceae. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Adenia, and its specific biological identity is defined by its species name, gracilis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Adenia gracilis has a large, tuberous caudex that serves as a primary water storage organ and supports slender, climbing stems. The leaves are typically trifoliate or simple, displaying a delicate texture that contrasts with the robust base. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the plant, often appearing in clusters. How is this plant cultivated?

To ensure healthy growth, you should provide consistent warmth during the active growing season while avoiding waterlogged soil that could lead to caudex rot. During the dormant months, withhold almost all moisture to mimic its natural arid environment.
