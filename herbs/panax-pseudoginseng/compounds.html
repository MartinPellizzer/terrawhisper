American Ginseng (Panax pseudoginseng) Compounds

American Ginseng contains several key compounds, including saponins, which have anti-inflammatory properties, and flavonoids, known for their antioxidant effects. Glycosides and terpenoids also contribute to its medicinal value, while phenolic acids and tannins support digestive health. Coumarin and alkaloids offer additional therapeutic benefits, and essential oils and anthocyanins enhance its overall pharmacological profile.

American Ginseng (Panax pseudoginseng) Monograph

1. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in American Ginseng (Panax pseudoginseng). It contributes to the plant's adaptogenic properties, supporting immune function and energy metabolism. Saponins also have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, making them important for the plant's traditional medicinal uses.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in American Ginseng (Panax pseudoginseng). These compounds contribute to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting immune function and cellular health. They are responsible for some of the therapeutic effects associated with this traditional herbal remedy.

3. Glycoside

American Ginseng (Panax pseudoginseng) contains glycosides, which are complex sugar molecules that contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds are believed to support immune function and cognitive health, making them key components in traditional herbal medicine. Glycosides in American Ginseng are often associated with adaptogenic effects, helping the body manage stress and maintain balance.

4. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in American Ginseng (Panax pseudoginseng). They contribute to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and immune-modulating effects. Specific terpenoids like ginsenosides are key to the herb's traditional use in enhancing vitality and supporting overall health.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in American Ginseng (Panax pseudoginseng). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties and may support immune function and reduce inflammation. This compound is part of the complex phytochemical profile that gives American Ginseng its medicinal value.

6. Tannin

Tannin is a compound found in American Ginseng (Panax pseudoginseng). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health. Tannins also act as antioxidants, helping to neutralize free radicals in the body. Their presence enhances the overall therapeutic profile of the herb.

7. Coumarin

Coumarin is a compound found in American Ginseng (Panax pseudoginseng). It contributes to the plant's characteristic aroma and has mild anticoagulant properties. While not the primary active component, coumarin may support overall cardiovascular health when consumed as part of the herb.

8. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in American Ginseng (Panax pseudoginseng). It contributes to the plant's pharmacological effects, including potential anti-inflammatory and neuroprotective properties. Alkaloids in this species are part of the broader group of bioactive compounds responsible for its traditional use in herbal medicine.

9. Essential oil

Essential oil is a volatile compound found in American Ginseng (Panax pseudoginseng). It contributes to the plant's aromatic profile and may have antimicrobial properties. The essential oil is typically extracted through steam distillation and is used in traditional herbal formulations. Its presence supports the plant's role in herbal medicine.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in American Ginseng (Panax pseudoginseng). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting immune function and reducing oxidative stress. This compound plays a role in the plant's traditional use for enhancing vitality and overall health.
