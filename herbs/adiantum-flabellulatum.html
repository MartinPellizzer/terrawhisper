Adiantum flabellulatum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Adiantum flabellulatum has delicate, fan-shaped pinnules that are typically bright green, deeply lobed, and attached to slender, dark, wiry stipes. The fronds are characterized by their intricate, lace-like appearance and a distinctively thin, papery texture. Each leaflet is finely segmented, often displaying a rounded or slightly irregular margin that contributes to its delicate silhouette. The plant's dark, almost black stems provide a sharp visual contrast to the light, translucent foliage. These morphological traits are essential for distinguishing this species from other members of the adiantum genus.
