Eremochloa muricata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eremochloa muricata has a tufted perennial growth habit characterized by slender, erect culms that typically reach heights of 10 to 40 centimeters. The leaves are narrow and linear with a scabrous texture, often featuring a rolled or involute margin. Its inflorescence consists of compact, spike-like panicles that are frequently branched and possess a somewhat spiciform appearance. The spikelets are relatively small and are arranged in a way that allows them to withstand arid environments effectively. Each component of the plant is adapted to minimize moisture loss while maintaining structural integrity in sandy soils.
