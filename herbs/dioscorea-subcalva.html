Dioscorea subcalva

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dioscorea subcalva has climbing stems with cordate leaves that possess entire margins and a distinctively subglabrous surface texture. The foliage typically features prominent venation and a somewhat fleshy consistency compared to other species in the genus. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. The plant develops tuberous rhizomes underground which serve as its primary storage organs. These morphological characteristics are essential for distinguishing it from closely related Dioscorea species within its habitat.
