Dioscorea subcalva

What's the taxonomical classification of Dioscorea subcalva?

Dioscorea subcalva belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Dioscoreales. The plant is further categorized into the family Dioscoreaceae, which contains the genus Dioscorea, eventually reaching its specific identification as the species Dioscorea subcalva.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dioscorea subcalva has climbing stems with cordate leaves that possess entire margins and a distinctively subglabrous surface texture.

The foliage typically features prominent venation and a somewhat fleshy consistency compared to other species in the genus. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. The plant develops tuberous rhizomes underground which serve as its primary storage organs.

These morphological characteristics are essential for distinguishing it from closely related Dioscorea species within its habitat.

        What are the herbal preparations of Dioscorea subcalva?

        Dioscorea subcalva is prepared as a decoction, a powder, or a tincture for medicinal use. The roots are most commonly dried and ground into a fine powder to be mixed with water or honey.
