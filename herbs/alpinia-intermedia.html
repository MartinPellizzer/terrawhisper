Alpinia intermedia

What's the taxonomical classification of Alpinia intermedia?

Alpinia intermedia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized into the order Zingiberales. This plant is a member of the family Zingiberaceae, specifically falling under the genus Alpinia, with its distinct designation as the species intermedia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Alpinia intermedia has a rhizomatous growth habit characterized by large, lanceolate leaves that can reach up to 60 centimeters in length and emerge from thick, fleshy underground stems. The foliage is typically bright green with prominent longitudinal veins and a smooth, waxy texture. During its flowering stage, the plant produces striking inflorescences featuring tubular, pale pink or white flowers that emerge from large, colorful bracts. These bracts often exhibit reddish or purplish hues to attract specific pollinators in its natural habitat. The overall structure of the plant is robust and upright, often forming dense clumps in shaded, moist environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical rainforest regions of Southeast Asia, specifically spanning across parts of Indonesia, Malaysia, and Thailand. It thrives primarily in the humid, shaded understory of low-altitude equatorial forests. Within these habitats, the species is often found growing in moist, organic soils near riverbanks or in areas with high rainfall. Its distribution is limited by its requirement for consistent tropical temperatures and high moisture levels. Consequently, it does not occur in any temperate or arid geographical zones.

How is this plant cultivated?
