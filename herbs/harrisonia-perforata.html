Harrisonia perforata

What's the taxonomical classification of Harrisonia perforata?

Harrisonia perforata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is classified in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Sapindales and the family Rutaaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by the genus Harrisonia and is specifically designated as the species perforata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Harrisonia perforata has small, elliptical to obovate leaves that are characterized by numerous tiny, circular holes or perforations across their surface. This unique leaf structure gives the plant its specific epithet and a distinct, lace-like appearance. The plant typically grows as a small, woody shrub with slender, branching stems. Its flowers are minute and often inconspicuous, appearing in small clusters along the branches. The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that serves as a key identifying feature of the species.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical regions of West and Central Africa, spanning across countries such as Nigeria, Cameroon, Gabon, and the Democratic Republic of the Congo. It primarily thrives in the humid environments of lowland rainforests and secondary forests. The species is often found growing in shaded undergrowth or along the margins of dense vegetation where moisture is consistent. Its distribution follows the belt of the African equatorial zone where high temperatures and rainfall support its growth cycle. What are the pharmacological activities of Harrisonia perforata?

This plant has anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, antiplasmodial, antipyretic, and bactericidal pharmacological activities. Its chemical constituents work to reduce swelling and fever through various biological mechanisms. The extract demonstrates significant antimicrobial properties that help inhibit the growth of many harmful microorganisms. Furthermore, its bactericidal action allows it to actively kill certain types of bacteria. Researchers also value its antiplasmodial potential for fighting parasitic infections.

As discussed in a study, Harrisonia perforata (Blanco) Merr. is recognized for diverse biological activities including antipyretic and anti-inflammatory effects. The study evaluated the anti-cancer attributes of ethanolic extracts obtained from Harrisonia perforata, referred to as HPEE, against the A549 lung adenocarcinoma cell lines. Results from the Sulforhodaimine B (SRB) assay revealed that HPEE showed no cytotoxicity on A549 cells. This research focused on the potential properties of the plant against non-small-cell lung cancer.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds 12-desacetylhaperforine A, Harpertrioate A, Perforalactone D, Perforalactone E, and Perforaltone G. These specific bioactive molecules are extracted from the plant's tissues for pharmacological study. Each compound possesses unique chemical structures that contribute to the plant's overall therapeutic profile. Researchers analyze these substances to understand their potential roles in developing new treatments. The presence of such a diverse array of lactones and related derivatives makes the species a significant subject in natural product chemistry.

As discussed in a study, two new rearranged limonoids named haperforine A (1) and haperforine E (2) were isolated from a sample of Harrisonia perforata leaves collected in Center Vietnam. The structures of these 2 compounds were determined by X-ray diffraction analysis. A minor compound was also established as 12-desacetylhaperforine A (3) through the process of chemical correlation. The study reports the specific NMR and mass spectroscopic properties for these identified compounds.
