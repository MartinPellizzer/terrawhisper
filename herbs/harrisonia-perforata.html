Harrisonia perforata

What's the taxonomical classification of Harrisonia perforata?

Harrisonia perforata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is classified in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Sapindales and the family Rutaaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by the genus Harrisonia and is specifically designated as the species perforata.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Harrisonia perforata contains bioactive alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, and tannins that serve as its primary medicinal compounds. These chemical constituents contribute to the plant's documented antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Researchers have specifically identified various secondary metabolites that assist in treating gastrointestinal issues and skin infections. The presence of these compounds supports the traditional use of the plant in various ethnic medicinal practices. Ongoing pharmacological studies aim to further isolate these specific molecules to develop new therapeutic agents.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including stomach aches, intestinal worms, skin infections, and inflammatory conditions. Traditional healers often utilize its leaves and roots to address digestive disturbances and gastrointestinal discomfort. It is also frequently applied topically to soothe wounds or manage skin-related irritations. Some communities rely on its properties to help manage symptoms of fever or general body aches. The medicinal application of this species varies significantly depending on the specific culture and preparation method used.
