Aleuritopteris rufa

What's the taxonomical classification of Aleuritopteris rufa?

Aleuritopteris rufa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it is situated under the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. This fern is a representative of the family Pteridaceae, falling under the genus Aleuritopteris, and is specifically identified by its species name, rufa.

Aleuritopteris rufa has anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties that are utilized in traditional medicinal practices to treat various ailments. What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, stomach aches, wounds, and various types of fever. The fern is frequently employed in traditional medicine to soothe inflammation and promote the healing of external injuries. Some practitioners apply its processed parts to dermatological issues to alleviate irritation or itching. Additionally, decoctions made from the plant are consumed to manage gastrointestinal discomfort and internal ailments. These diverse applications highlight the plant's role in managing both topical and systemic health concerns.
