Dolomiaea berardioidea

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dolomiaea berardioidea has a woody, shrubby growth habit characterized by lanceolate to elliptical leaves with entire margins and acute tips. The foliage is typically arranged in an opposite or sub-opposite pattern along the stems. Its inflorescence consists of small, tubular flowers that are often clustered in terminal or axillary cymes. The reproductive structures feature a corolla that varies in color depending on the specific population, often ranging from pale hues to deeper tones. The overall architecture of the plant reflects its adaptation to rocky, high-altitude environments.
