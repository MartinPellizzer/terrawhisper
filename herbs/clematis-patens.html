Clematis patens

What's the taxonomical classification of Clematis patens?

Clematis patens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Ranunculales. Its taxonomic journey continues through the family Ranunculaceae, which places it in the genus Clematis, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species patens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clematis patens has opposite, simple, or trifoliate leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. The plant exhibits a climbing growth habit facilitated by its specialized leaf stalks which act as tendrils to grip surrounding vegetation. Its inflorescences consist of solitary or small clusters of actinomorphic flowers that feature four to five spreading tepals. These tepals generally display a pale cream or yellowish-white coloration and are centered around a prominent cluster of numerous stamens. The fruit develops into an aggregate of achenes, each equipped with a long, feathery style that aids in wind dispersal.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant, Clematis patens, requires well-drained soil and a location that provides full sun to partial shade with feet in the shade and head in the sun.
