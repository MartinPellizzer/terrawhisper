Pyrrosia similis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pyrrosia similis has small, lanceolate leaves with serrated margins and a woody, branched stem structure. The foliage is typically arranged in an opposite pattern along the slender branches. Its flowers are diminutive and emerge from the leaf axils in small clusters. The root system consists of a fibrous network that anchors the plant firmly into the soil. These morphological traits allow the species to adapt effectively to its specific ecological niche.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the subtropical and temperate forest regions of eastern China, primarily located within provinces such as Zhejiang, Fujian, and Jiangxi. It typically thrives in moist, shaded environments found under the canopy of broadleaf evergreen forests. These specific habitats provide the high humidity and stable temperatures necessary for its survival. The distribution is often restricted to mountainous areas where well-drained, acidic soils are prevalent.
