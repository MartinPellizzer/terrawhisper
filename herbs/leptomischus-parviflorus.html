Leptomischus parviflorus

What's the taxonomical classification of Leptomischus parviflorus?

Leptomischus parviflorus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it falls under the order Gentianaales and is a member of the family Rubiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Leptomischus, and its unique species designation, parviflorus.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Leptomischus parviflorus can cause skin irritation, redness, and localized inflammation if the plant comes into direct contact with human tissue. Contact dermatitis is a common reaction for individuals sensitive to the chemical compounds present in its foliage. Some people may also experience itching or a burning sensation at the site of exposure. If ingested, the plant may lead to gastrointestinal distress or more severe systemic toxicity depending on the quantity consumed. It is important to handle this species with caution to prevent these adverse physical reactions.
