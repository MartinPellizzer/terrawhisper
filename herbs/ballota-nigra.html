What to know about Ballota nigra (black horehound) before using it medicinally

Black horehound, scientifically known as Ballota nigra, is a plant renowned for its impressive array of health benefits, which include alleviating respiratory issues such as bronchitis and coughs by acting as an expectorant, soothing digestive troubles like indigestion and diarrhea, and even exhibiting antibacterial properties.

In the kitchen, black horehound is commonly used to add a distinctive flavor and aroma to teas, soups, stews, and desserts, particularly in traditional medicine and folk remedies. Horticulturally, black horehound is an annual or biennial plant that thrives in dry, well-drained soil and full sun, making it an attractive addition to any herb garden. The botanical properties of black horehound include its small, white flowers and dark green leaves, which are often used for medicinal purposes due to their high concentration of alkaloids.

Throughout history, black horehound has been referenced in ancient texts such as the Ebers Papyrus, dating back to 1550 BCE, where it was listed as a treatment for various ailments, including respiratory issues and digestive problems.
