Saussurea amurensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea amurensis has a perennial herbaceous structure characterized by a thick, woody rhizome and basal leaves that are lanceolate to elliptical in shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. The plant typically grows in dense clumps, featuring upright flowering stems that can reach various heights depending on the environment. Each stem terminates in a compact, head-like inflorescence composed of numerous small, yellowish-green florets. The involucral bracts surrounding these flower heads are often prominent and play a key role in the plant's reproductive display. Fine hairs may cover the stems and foliage, providing protection against harsh alpine conditions.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of East Asia, specifically spanning across northeastern China, the Korean Peninsula, and parts of the Russian Far East. It typically thrives in high-altitude alpine environments where rocky crevices and subalpine meadows are abundant. Within these habitats, the species is often found in well-drained soils situated along slopes or near stony outcrops. Its distribution is closely tied to specific climatic conditions found in these temperate montane zones. These geographical patterns reflect its adaptation to cool, seasonal mountain ecosystems.
