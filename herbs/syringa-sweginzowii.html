Syringa sweginzowii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Syringa sweginzoi has opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that typically measure 5 to 12 centimeters in length and possess finely serrated margins. The foliage is characterized by a smooth, glabrous texture on the upper surface and a slightly paler underside. Its flowering structures consist of dense, terminal panicles that produce small, tubular flowers. These blossoms are often creamy white or pale lilac in color and emerge during the late spring season. The stems exhibit a woody structure typical of deciduous shrubs, often developing a grayish bark as the plant matures.
