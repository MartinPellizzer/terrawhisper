1. Causes allergic reactions

Orthosiphon aristata causes allergic reactions in some individuals due to its high concentration of flavonoids and phenolic acids.

These bioactive compounds can trigger an immune response in people with sensitive skin or allergies, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and redness.

Additionally, the presence of volatile oils in Orthosiphon aristata may also contribute to allergic reactions by irritating the skin or respiratory tract, causing discomfort and distress.

2. Induces excessive sweating

Orthosiphon aristata, also known as Java tea, induces excessive sweating due to its diuretic properties, which stimulate the kidneys to produce more urine and increase fluid release from the body.

This increased perspiration is a common side effect of taking Orthosiphon aristata, as the herb causes an increase in sweat glands' activity, leading to excessive sweating, particularly during hot weather or physical activity.

3. Leads to digestive issues

Orthosiphon aristata leads to digestive issues when consumed in large doses or for extended periods.

The compound aristolochic acid present in the herb can irritate the stomach lining and intestines, causing symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal pain.

Additionally, Orthosiphon aristata may disrupt the balance of gut bacteria, leading to further digestive problems.

4. Triggers increased urination

Orthosiphon aristata triggers increased urination due to its natural diuretic properties.

The herb contains compounds that stimulate the kidneys to release excess fluids and electrolytes, leading to a frequent need to urinate.

This side effect is often temporary and may subside once the body adjusts to the supplement's presence.

5. Produces insomnia

Orthosiphon aristata produces insomnia due to its high concentration of flavonoids and caffeic acid.

These compounds can stimulate the central nervous system, leading to an increase in alertness and energy. However, this stimulation can also disrupt the normal sleep-wake cycle, making it difficult for individuals to fall asleep or stay asleep throughout the night.

As a result, users may experience insomnia as a side effect of taking Orthosiphon aristata.

6. Increases blood pressure

Orthosiphon aristata increases blood pressure due to its ability to stimulate the heart and cardiovascular system.

The herb's active compounds, such as caffeic acid and flavonoids, can cause blood vessels to constrict and increase cardiac output, leading to a rise in blood pressure.

This effect is more pronounced in individuals with pre-existing hypertension or those who are already taking blood pressure medications.

7. Stimulates heart palpitations

Orthosiphon aristata stimulates heart palpitations due to its ability to increase heart rate and blood pressure.

The plant's active compounds, particularly aristolochic acid, may cause the heart muscle to contract more forcefully, leading to irregular heartbeat rhythms and palpitations. Additionally, Orthosiphon aristata may interact with certain medications that affect heart function, further exacerbating this side effect.

As a result, some individuals may experience rapid or fluttering heartbeat when using Orthosiphon aristata.

8. Causes stomach upset

Orthosiphon aristata, also known as Java tea, causes stomach upset in some individuals due to its ability to stimulate digestive enzymes and increase gastric acid production.

The herb's saponins and flavonoids may irritate the stomach lining, leading to nausea, bloating, and discomfort.

Additionally, Orthosiphon aristata's bitter compounds can slow down digestion and absorption of nutrients, further contributing to stomach upset in some cases.

9. Generates anxiety attacks

Orthosiphon aristata, also known as Java Tea, generates anxiety attacks due to its ability to interact with the body's neurotransmitters, particularly serotonin and GABA.

This interaction can lead to an imbalance in these neurotransmitters, resulting in feelings of unease, worry, and fear. Additionally, Java Tea's stimulating properties can increase cortisol levels, contributing to increased anxiety.

These effects can be more pronounced in individuals who are already prone to anxiety disorders or have a history of nervous system imbalances.

10. Creates respiratory problems

Orthosiphon aristata creates respiratory problems due to its potential to cause allergic reactions and inflammation in some individuals.

The plant's active compounds, particularly orthosiphol A and B, may stimulate the release of histamine, leading to symptoms such as coughing, wheezing, and shortness of breath.

Additionally, the herb may also increase mucus production, exacerbating respiratory issues like asthma or chronic bronchitis.
