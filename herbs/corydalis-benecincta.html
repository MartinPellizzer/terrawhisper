What's the taxonomical classification of Corydalis benecincta?

Corydalis benecincta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Ranunculales. As a member of the family Papaveraceae, this plant is situated within the genus Corydalis, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species benecincta.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Corydalis benecincta contains a diverse array of isoquinoline alkaloids, primarily including protopine, corydaline, and various benzylisoquinoline derivatives. Many of these alkaloids function as potent analgesics by interacting with the central nervous system to alleviate pain. Additionally, some compounds exhibit sedative and antispasmodic properties that help regulate muscle contractions.
