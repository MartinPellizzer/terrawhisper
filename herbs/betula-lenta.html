Horticulturally, yellow birch is a relatively easy-to-grow tree that can thrive in a variety of environments, making it a popular choice for gardeners. Botanically, yellow birch belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow-green leaves and papery bark.

Historically, yellow birch has been referenced in traditional medicine practices, with indigenous communities utilizing its extracts to treat various ailments.
