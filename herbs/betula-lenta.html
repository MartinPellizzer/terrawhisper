Betula Lenta

Betula lenta, commonly known as sweet birch, has been used for medicinal purposes for centuries due to its various bioactive compounds.

The bark and sap of the tree contain high levels of methyl salicylate, which is responsible for its analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties. Traditionally, it has been used to relieve pain, reduce fever, and treat skin conditions such as eczema and psoriasis. However, due to its potential toxicity and the risk of allergic reactions, its use in modern medicine is limited.

Despite these concerns, some herbal preparations still incorporate Betula lenta for its therapeutic effects.
