Spanish needle, scientifically known as Bidens pilosa, is a plant that has been revered for its numerous health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that have been shown to improve overall well-being.

In the culinary world, Spanish needle is often used as an aromatic herb in soups, salads, and stews, adding a subtle tanginess and freshness to dishes. From a horticultural perspective, Spanish needle is a low-maintenance annual plant that thrives in warm climates, making it a popular choice for gardeners and landscapers alike. Botanically speaking, Spanish needle belongs to the Asteraceae family and features delicate white or yellow flowers with a distinctive "needle-like" seedhead.

With ancient references dating back to the Aztecs and Mayans, Spanish needle has a rich history as a medicinal and spiritual herb in traditional cultures around the world.
