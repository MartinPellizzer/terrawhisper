Beggarticks

Bidens pilosa

Beggarticks (Bidens pilosa) is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions, known for its distinctive appearance and wide range of traditional medicinal uses, particularly for improving skin conditions and treating inflammation.

Botanical Identification

Beggarticks, scientifically known as Bidens pilosa, is a member of the Asteraceae family. Commonly called beggar's ticks or Spanish needle, it is an annual herb native to the Americas. The plant has hairy, branching stems, narrow leaves, and small yellow flowers with distinctive bracts. It is also known by various regional names and has been referenced in traditional herbal literature.

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for treating fever and digestive issues.

Decoction: Used for treating skin infections and respiratory conditions.

Powder: Used for treating intestinal worms and as a general tonic.

Safety Profile

Beggarticks (Bidens pilosa) should be avoided during pregnancy due to potential uterine stimulant effects. It may also cause gastrointestinal irritation in some individuals. Always consult a healthcare provider before use, especially if you have a history of allergies or digestive issues.
