Bidens pilosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bidens pilosa has an erect, herbaceous stem that typically reaches heights of 30 to 100 centimeters and is characterized by a dense covering of spreading, bristly hairs. The leaves are oppositely arranged, trifoliate or pinnately lobed, and possess serrated margins with a rough, pubescent texture. Small, white or yellow flower heads are found in terminal clusters, consisting of ray florets that surround a central disc of yellow tubular florets. The plant is most distinctive for its achene fruits, which are black, elongated, and equipped with two to four barbed awns designed for animal dispersal. These specialized bristles allow the seeds to cling easily to fur or clothing.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, ranging from the southern United States through Central America and into much of South America. It is frequently found in disturbed areas, roadsides, and open fields where sunlight is abundant. Its distribution is particularly widespread in countries like Brazil, Colombia, and Mexico. Because it thrives in various warm climates, it has become naturalized in several other tropical zones across the globe. The species maintains a presence in both coastal and inland habitats within its primary range.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Bidens pilosa contains a diverse array of bioactive secondary metabolites including sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, polyphenols, and various triterpenoids. These chemical constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's documented antibacterial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties. Specifically, the presence of caffeic acid derivatives and quercetin helps mitigate oxidative stress in biological systems. Research also indicates that the alkaloids and tannins within the plant contribute to its potential wound-healing and antimicrobial activities. What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Bidens pilosa targets various inflammatory mediators including cyclooxygenase-2, inducible nitric oxide synthase, and tumor necrosis factor-alpha to exert its medicinal properties. The plant contains bioactive phytochemicals like polyphenols and flavonoids that inhibit the expression of these specific proteins involved in the inflammatory cascade. By suppressing cyclooxygenase-2, the extracts effectively reduce the production of pro-inflammatory prostaglandins. Additionally, the interaction with tumor necrosis factor-alpha helps modulate the immune response and provides anti-inflammatory benefits. These molecular interactions also extend to the inhibition of certain enzymes that contribute to oxidative stress within cells.

Bidens pilosa has diverse therapeutic actions including bitter, diuretic, antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, hemostatic, hypoglycemic, and immunomodulator properties. What are the herbal preparations of Bidens pilosa?

Bidens pilosa is prepared as a decoction, infusion, tea, topical poultice, or tincture to utilize its bioactive compounds. The most common method involves simmering the dried leaves and flowers in water to create a therapeutic tea. For external applications, the crushed fresh plant material is applied directly to the skin as a poultice to treat wounds or inflammation. Concentrated tinctures can also be made by soaking the plant parts in alcohol for several weeks to extract specific phytochemicals. These diverse preparation methods allow the plant to be used for both internal consumption and localized skin treatments.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Bidens pilosa can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested improperly. Some individuals may experience localized redness or itching where the plant makes direct contact with the skin. If consumed, it might lead to nausea, vomiting, or stomach cramps depending on the dosage and concentration. There is also a potential for more severe systemic reactions in people with known sensitivities to similar botanical species. It is important to exercise caution as specific toxicological data for this exact plant may be limited.
