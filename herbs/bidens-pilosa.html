What is Beggarticks (Bidens Pilosa)?

Beggarticks, scientifically known as Bidens pilosa, is a common flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions, widely recognized in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and wound-healing properties.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Beggarticks is Bidens pilosa. This plant belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Asterales, family Asteraceae, genus Bidens, and species Bidens pilosa. It is commonly known as Beggarticks in North America and as Sticktight in parts of Europe. It is also referred to as Common Beggarweed in some regions. Synonyms for this plant include Bidens frondosa, Bidens tripartita, and Bidens subalternans.

Its root system is fibrous and shallow, allowing it to spread rapidly in disturbed soils.

The plant has a distinct green color, a mild grassy smell, and a rough, hairy texture on its stems and leaves.
