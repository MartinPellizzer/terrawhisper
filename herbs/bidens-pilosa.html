Bidens pilosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bidens pilosa has an erect, herbaceous stem that typically reaches heights of 30 to 100 centimeters and is characterized by a dense covering of spreading, bristly hairs. The leaves are oppositely arranged, trifoliate or pinnately lobed, and possess serrated margins with a rough, pubescent texture. Small, white or yellow flower heads are found in terminal clusters, consisting of ray florets that surround a central disc of yellow tubular florets. The plant is most distinctive for its achene fruits, which are black, elongated, and equipped with two to four barbed awns designed for animal dispersal. These specialized bristles allow the seeds to cling easily to fur or clothing.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, ranging from the southern United States through Central America and into much of South America. It is frequently found in disturbed areas, roadsides, and open fields where sunlight is abundant. Its distribution is particularly widespread in countries like Brazil, Colombia, and Mexico. Because it thrives in various warm climates, it has become naturalized in several other tropical zones across the globe. The species maintains a presence in both coastal and inland habitats within its primary range.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Bidens pilosa contains a diverse array of bioactive secondary metabolites including sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, polyphenols, and various triterpenoids. These chemical constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's documented antibacterial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties. Specifically, the presence of caffeic acid derivatives and quercetin helps mitigate oxidative stress in biological systems. Research also indicates that the alkaloids and tannins within the plant contribute to its potential wound-healing and antimicrobial activities. These complex organic compounds work synergistically to provide the medicinal benefits observed in traditional herbal practices.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Bidens pilosa targets various inflammatory mediators including cyclooxygenase-2, inducible nitric oxide synthase, and tumor necrosis factor-alpha to exert its medicinal properties. The plant contains bioactive phytochemicals like polyphenols and flavonoids that inhibit the expression of these specific proteins involved in the inflammatory cascade. By suppressing cyclooxygenase-2, the extracts effectively reduce the production of pro-inflammatory prostaglandins. Additionally, the interaction with tumor necrosis factor-alpha helps modulate the immune response and provides anti-inflammatory benefits. These molecular interactions also extend to the inhibition of certain enzymes that contribute to oxidative stress within cells.

What are the therapeutic actions of Bidens pilosa?

Bidens pilosa has diverse therapeutic actions including bitter, diuretic, antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, hemostatic, hypoglycemic, and immunomodulator properties. Its bitter compounds help stimulate digestion and bile production, while its diuretic effects assist in fluid regulation. The plant provides significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory support to reduce oxidative stress and swelling throughout the body. It also functions as an antimicrobial agent to fight infections and a hemostatic agent to help control bleeding. Furthermore, it serves as a hypoglycemic tool for managing blood sugar levels and acts as an immunomodulator to balance the immune system.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause nausea, dizziness, diarrhea, vomiting, and stomach pain if ingested. These gastrointestinal issues often arise shortly after consuming the botanical material. Some individuals may also experience a sense of lightheadedness or vertigo. Severe cases might involve persistent abdominal discomfort or frequent bouts of sickness. Monitoring these symptoms is essential when encountering this specific species.
