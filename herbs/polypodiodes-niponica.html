What's the taxonomical classification of Polypodiodes niponica?

Polypodioides niponica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae. Following its taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Polypodiales and the family Polypodiaceae. Ultimately, this specific fern is defined by its genus, Polypodiodes, and its species designation, niponica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polypodioides niponica has creeping rhizomes that are scaly and produce pinnate fronds that typically measure between 10 and 30 centimeters in length. The pinnules are arranged in a single plane and exhibit a somewhat leathery texture with slightly wavy margins. Each leaflet is oblong to lanceolate in shape and possesses a dull green color on both the upper and lower surfaces. The underside of the fronds features small, circular sori arranged in rows near the margins of the pinnules. These reproductive structures are often protected by a thin indusium to ensure proper spore development.
