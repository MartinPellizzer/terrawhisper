What's the taxonomical classification of Tirpitzia sinensis?

Tirpitzia sinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant falls under the order Malpighiales. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the family Linaceae, which contains the genus Tirpitzia, ultimately defining the specific species as Tirpitzia sinensis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds tirpitzin, tirpitziol, and tirpitzoside. These specific chemical constituents are responsible for much of the therapeutic potential observed in the species. Researchers study tirpitzin to understand its unique biochemical interactions within biological systems. Meanwhile, tirpitziol and tirpitzoside are being investigated for their potential roles in various pharmacological applications. Understanding the synergy between these three substances is crucial for future medicinal developments.

As discussed in a study, Tirpitzia sinensis contains three new compounds including the lignan tirpitzin (1), the flavonoid tirpitzoside (2), and the furan-glycoside tirpitziol (3). These three new compounds were isolated from the aerial part along with 5 known compounds. In an in vitro assay using lipopolysaccharide-induced BV-2 cells, all 3 new compounds displayed moderate nitric oxide inhibitory activity. The IC50 values for these compounds were 14.97 ± 0.87, 26.63 ± 1.32, and 17.09 ± 2.3 μM, respectively. Potential targets for these compounds identified through analysis include ERBB2, IRAK4, LCK, JAK2, MAPK14, and MMP-12.
