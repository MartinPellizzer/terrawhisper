What are the pharmacological activities of Tirpitzia sinensis?

This plant has anti-inflammatory, analgesic, wound-healing, anti-edematous, and circulatory-enhancing pharmacological activities. Its chemical constituents work together to alleviate pain and reduce systemic inflammation in various tissues. By promoting blood circulation, the plant helps deliver essential nutrients to injured areas to facilitate the wound-healing process. These diverse medicinal properties make Tirpitzia sinensis a significant subject for pharmacological research.

According to a study published by "BMC chemistry", Tirpitzia sinensis is an ethnomedicinal plant from Southwest China used to stop bleeding, invigorate blood circulation, and treat inflammation and wounds. Three new compounds were isolated from the aerial part of the species, including the lignan tirpitzin (1), the flavonoid tirpitzoside (2), and the furan-glycoside tirpitziol (3). In silico pharmacophore-based analysis suggested these compounds may target ERBB2, IRAK4, LCK, JAK2, MAPK14, and MMP-12, which could be involved with wound-healing and/or inflammation. In vitro assays using lipopolysaccharide-induced BV-2 cells showed that all three new compounds displayed moderate nitric oxide inhibitory activity. The specific IC50 values for these compounds were 14.97 ± 0.87, 26.63 ± 1.32, and 17.09 ± 2.3 μM, respectively.
