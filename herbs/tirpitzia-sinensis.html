Tirpitzia sinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant falls under the order Malpighiales. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the family Linaceae, which contains the genus Tirpitzia, ultimately defining the specific species as Tirpitzia sinensis.

As discussed in a study, Tirpitzia sinensis is an ethnomedicinal plant from Southwest China used to stop bleeding, invigorate blood circulation, and treat inflammation and wounds. From the aerial part of this species, three new compounds were isolated: the lignan tirpitzin (1), the flavonoid tirpitzoside (2), and the furan-glycoside tirpitziol (3), along with 5 known compounds. In silico analysis identified potential targets for these new compounds, including ERBB2, IRAK4, LCK, JAK2, MAPK14, and MMP-12. These findings suggested roles in wound-healing and/or inflammation, which were tested through in vitro nitric oxide inhibition assays with lipopolysaccharide-induced BV-2 cells. All 3 new compounds displayed moderate nitric oxide inhibitory activity with IC50 values of 14.97 ± 0.87, 26.63 ± 1.32, and 17.09 ± 2.3 μM, respectively.
