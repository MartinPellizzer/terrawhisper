Deparia okuboana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Deparia okuboana has a rhizomatous growth habit with pinnate fronds that typically reach heights of 20 to 40 centimeters. The stipe is dark brown and features small scales concentrated near the base. Each leaf is divided into multiple pinnae that possess serrated or lobed margins. The pinnules are arranged symmetrically along the rachis and exhibit a slightly thickened texture. Small, inconspicuous sori are located on the underside of the fertile segments.
