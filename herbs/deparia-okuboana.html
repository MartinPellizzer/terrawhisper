Deparia okuboana

What's the taxonomical classification of Deparia okuboana?

Deparia okuboaana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae. Following the order Polypodiales, the plant is placed within the family Aspleniaceae. Its taxonomic hierarchy concludes with its designation in the genus Deparia, specifically as the species Deparia okuboaana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Deparia okuboana has a rhizomatous growth habit with pinnate fronds that typically reach heights of 20 to 40 centimeters.

The stipe is dark brown and features small scales concentrated near the base. Each leaf is divided into multiple pinnae that possess serrated or lobed margins. The pinnules are arranged symmetrically along the rachis and exhibit a slightly thickened texture.

Small, inconspicuous sori are located on the underside of the fertile segments.

This liquid extract is often consumed warm to facilitate absorption of the plant's active constituents.
