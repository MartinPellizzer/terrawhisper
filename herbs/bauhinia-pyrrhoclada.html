What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bauhinia pyrrhoclada has a woody, deciduous habit characterized by spreading branches and distinctively bilobed leaves that resemble a butterfly or a camel's foot. The leaves are typically large and composed of two rounded lobes meeting at a single point at the apex. Its flowers are striking, featuring bright red to deep pink petals that serve as a primary identifier for the species. The plant often develops a dense canopy, providing significant shade through its broad foliage. Small, woody pods form from the flowers and eventually dry to release the seeds.
