Artemisia orientalihengduangensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Artemisia orientalihengduangensis has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by erect stems and deeply pinnatifid leaves that exhibit a silvery-gray pubescence. The foliage is divided into narrow, linear lobes that provide a fine texture to the plant's overall appearance. Small, discoid flower heads are arranged in dense terminal clusters or panicles. These inflorescences are typically accompanied by inconspicuous bracts that protect the developing florets. The plant's morphological features are adapted to its specific high-altitude habitat within the Hengduan Mountains.

This plant is endemic to the Hengduan Mountains region within the southwestern provinces of China. It is primarily documented growing in the high-altitude habitats of Sichuan and Yunnan. Its specific distribution is tied to the unique montane ecosystems found along the steep slopes of this mountain range.
