What's the taxonomical classification of Eberhardtia aurata?

Eberhardtia auraata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. As a member of the order Ericales, it falls specifically within the family Sapotaeceae. The plant's precise taxonomic placement is defined by its membership in the genus Eberhardtia, with its specific designation being the species auraata.

      What are the herbal preparations of Eberhardtia aurata?

      Eberhardtia auraata is prepared as a decoction made by boiling the plant's leaves and stems in water to extract its medicinal properties. This liquid infusion is traditionally consumed orally to treat various ailments related to the digestive and reproductive systems. Some practitioners also utilize the plant in the form of topical poultices by crushing the fresh foliage into a paste. These pastes are applied directly to the skin to soothe localized inflammation or infections. The versatility of these preparation methods allows for both internal and external therapeutic applications.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Eberhardtia auraata can cause localized skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or stinging sensations if the sap comes into contact with human tissue. This plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is frequently known for producing caustic or irritating latex. Individuals with sensitive skin may experience redness, itching, or small blisters following accidental exposure. It is advisable to wear protective gloves when handling the plant to prevent these dermatological reactions. Contact with the eyes should be strictly avoided to prevent more severe chemical irritation.
