What's the taxonomical classification of Portulaca quadrifida?

Portulaca quadrifidida belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized as Equisetopsida, falling under the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Caryophyllales and belongs to the family Portulacacae. Within this family, it is situated in the genus Portulaca, and its specific biological identity is defined by its species name, quadrifida.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Portulaca quadrifida has succulent, cylindrical leaves that are approximately 1 to 2 centimeters long and terminate in a slightly pointed tip. These fleshy leaves are arranged in an alternate pattern along the stems, which can grow in a spreading or upright manner. The plant typically produces small, bright yellow flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. Its root system is fibrous and helps the plant stabilize itself in sandy or rocky soils. The overall structure allows the species to store significant amounts of moisture within its specialized tissues.
