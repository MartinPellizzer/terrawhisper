Dog Rose (Rosa Canina Subvar. Oxyodonta)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Rosa canina subvar. oxyodonta.

Dog rose, scientifically known as Rosa canina subvar. oxyodonta, is a medicinal herb widely valued for its therapeutic properties and adaptogenic qualities. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and its hips, which are the fruit of the plant, are rich in vitamin C, antioxidants, and other essential nutrients. Traditionally, Dog rose has been used in herbal medicine to support immune function, reduce inflammation, and aid digestion, with historical records dating back to ancient Greek and Roman times. In modern wellness practices, it is commonly used in supplements, teas, and topical preparations to promote skin health, respiratory support, and overall vitality. One of its unique features is the high concentration of bioflavonoids, which enhance the absorption of vitamin C and contribute to its reputation as a powerful natural remedy.
