10 Medicinal Constituents Of Celtis Australis (Wild Pear)

Celtis australis has active constituents such as flavonoids, terpenoids, and saponins.

These constituents exhibit anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which can help alleviate symptoms of various health issues. Flavonoids can improve cardiovascular health and reduce the risk of heart disease. Terpenoids can aid in wound healing and reduce inflammation.

Saponins can help manage diabetes by regulating blood sugar levels.

This article explains in details the 10 best active constituents of Celtis australis .

1. Triterpenoids

Celtis australis triterpenoids is a class of compounds isolated from its fruit and bark.

These triterpenoids have been found to possess various bioactive properties, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial activities.

The chemical composition of these compounds includes ursane-type triterpenoids, which are known for their potential in the prevention of cardiovascular diseases and cancer.

2. Flavonoids

Celtis australis flavonoids is a type of bioactive compound found in its leaves and bark.

These flavonoids have been extensively studied for their potential therapeutic properties, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial activities.

The flavonoids present in C. australis, such as quercetin and kaempferol, have been shown to exhibit protective effects against oxidative stress, cellular damage, and microbial infections, making it a promising natural remedy for various diseases.

3. Quercetin

Celtis australis quercetin is a flavonoid compound that has been isolated from its bark and leaves.

It has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Quercetin has been shown to possess numerous health benefits, including reducing the risk of cardiovascular disease, improving immune function, and exhibiting antiviral and antibacterial activities.

Its bioactive compounds also have potential applications in pharmaceuticals, food supplements, and cosmetics.

4. Kaempferol

Celtis australis kaempferol is a flavonoid compound that has been isolated from the leaves and stems of this plant species.

It has been found to exhibit significant antioxidant activity, inhibiting the oxidation of low-density lipoprotein (LDL) cholesterol in human blood serum.

Additionally, kaempferol has been shown to have anti-inflammatory properties, making it a potential natural remedy for various diseases associated with inflammation.

5. Oleanolic acid

Celtis australis oleanolic acid is a bioactive compound extracted from its leaves and fruits.

This triterpenoid saponin has been extensively studied for its pharmacological properties, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial activities.

It has been shown to exhibit potential therapeutic effects in treating various diseases, such as cardiovascular disorders, cancer, and viral infections, making it a promising natural product for medicinal applications.

6. Ursolic acid

Celtis australis ursolic acid is a type of triterpenoid compound found in its leaves and fruits.

It has been extensively studied for its various bioactivities, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties.

Ursolic acid has also been shown to exhibit anti-cancer and anti-diabetic effects, making it a potential natural therapeutic agent for the treatment of several diseases.

7. Tannins

Celtis australis tannins is a type of polyphenolic compound present in its bark, leaves, and fruit.

These tannins are responsible for the plant's astringent properties, which can help to reduce inflammation and antibacterial activity.

They have been traditionally used in traditional medicine to treat various health issues such as fever, cough, and diarrhea, as well as skin conditions like eczema and dermatitis.

8. Phenolic acids

Celtis australis phenolic acids is a class of compounds responsible for its antioxidant and antimicrobial properties.

These acids, including caffeic acid, ferulic acid, and gallic acid, are present in the leaves, bark, and fruit of the plant.

They have been shown to exhibit anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and antibacterial activities, making them valuable components in traditional medicine and potentially useful for treating various diseases.

9. Gallic acid

Celtis australis gallic acid is a type of phenolic compound that has been isolated from its fruit.

Gallic acid has been shown to possess various biological activities, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. It has been traditionally used in traditional medicine to treat various diseases such as fever, rheumatism, and diarrhea.

Recent studies have also demonstrated its potential use in the treatment of cancer and other diseases.

10. Ellagic acid

Celtis australis ellagic acid is a polyphenolic compound that has been isolated from its fruit.

It has been shown to possess numerous biological activities, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. Ellagic acid has also been found to exhibit cytotoxic effects against various cancer cell lines, making it a potential therapeutic agent for the treatment of cancer.

Additionally, it has been reported to have beneficial effects on cardiovascular health by reducing cholesterol levels and preventing platelet aggregation.
