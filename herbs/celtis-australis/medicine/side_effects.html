1. Causes stomach upset

Celtis australis causes stomach upset due to its tannin content, which can irritate the mucous membranes in the digestive tract.

The high fiber and polyphenol levels in this plant may also contribute to gastrointestinal issues such as bloating, nausea, and abdominal discomfort.

Additionally, the bitter compounds present in Celtis australis may stimulate the release of stomach acid, leading to heartburn and indigestion.

2. Induces allergic reactions

Celtis australis induces allergic reactions due to its unique chemical composition.

The tree's sap and leaves contain allergenic proteins that can trigger an immune response in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.

Additionally, the presence of terpenes and other volatile compounds may exacerbate these reactions, causing systemic inflammation and discomfort.

3. Interacts with medications

Celtis australis interacts with medications by affecting their metabolism and absorption in the body.

This can lead to increased or decreased levels of certain medications in the bloodstream, potentially altering their effectiveness or causing adverse reactions.

The plant's flavonoids and polyphenols may compete with medications for protein binding sites, leading to changes in pharmacokinetics and increasing the risk of interactions.

4. Affects blood sugar levels

Celtis australis affects blood sugar levels due to its potential to interact with diabetes medications and alter glucose metabolism.

The extracts of this plant have been shown to lower blood sugar levels by increasing insulin sensitivity and reducing glucose absorption in the gut.

This may be beneficial for individuals with type 2 diabetes, but could have unintended consequences for those not taking diabetes medication or with normal blood sugar levels.

5. Triggers anxiety attacks

Celtis australis triggers anxiety attacks due to its ability to stimulate the nervous system and release adrenaline.

The high levels of catecholamines such as epinephrine and norepinephrine can cause an increase in heart rate, blood pressure, and respiration, leading to feelings of panic and fear.

Additionally, the plant's effects on the brain's neurotransmitters, including serotonin and dopamine, may disrupt normal mood regulation and exacerbate anxiety symptoms.

6. Increases heart rate

Celtis australis increases heart rate due to its potential to interact with certain medications and affect cardiovascular functioning.

The plant's flavonoids and glycosides can stimulate the nervous system, leading to an increased heart rate.

Additionally, Celtis australis may cause a slight increase in blood pressure, further contributing to the elevated heart rate.

7. Lowers blood pressure

Celtis australis lowers blood pressure by its ability to dilate blood vessels and reduce peripheral resistance.

This is due to its vasodilatory properties, which can lead to a decrease in blood pressure. Additionally, the herb's antihypertensive compounds may help relax blood vessels, further contributing to this effect.

As a result, individuals using Celtis australis may experience a noticeable drop in their blood pressure levels.

8. Causes insomnia

Celtis australis causes insomnia due to its ability to increase alertness and energy levels.

The plant's bioactive compounds, including flavonoids and terpenes, can stimulate the nervous system, leading to difficulty falling asleep or staying asleep.

This is because the body's natural sleep-wake cycle is disrupted by the plant's stimulating effects, making it challenging for individuals to relax and fall into a restful sleep.

9. Stimulates seizures

Celtis australis stimulates seizures due to its ability to affect the levels of certain neurotransmitters in the brain.

The plant contains compounds that can alter the activity of GABA and glutamate, leading to an imbalance that can trigger seizure activity.

Additionally, Celtis australis may interact with medications used to treat epilepsy or other neurological conditions, exacerbating symptoms.

10. Aggravates asthma symptoms

Celtis australis aggravates asthma symptoms due to its potential allergenic properties.

The plant's pollen and sap can trigger allergic reactions in some individuals, leading to an increase in inflammation and airway constriction. This can exacerbate existing asthma conditions and even induce bronchospasms, further restricting airflow.

As a result, people with pre-existing asthma may experience worsening symptoms when exposed to Celtis australis.
