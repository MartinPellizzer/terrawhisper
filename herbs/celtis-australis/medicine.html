What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Celtis Australis (Wild Pear)?

Celtis australis, also known as wild pear, has health benefits such as reducing inflammation, improving digestion, and lowering blood pressure.

The medicinal constituents of this herb include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These constituents can be extracted and formulated into teas, infusions, or tinctures, which are commonly used to treat digestive issues and cardiovascular problems. Side effects are generally mild, but excessive consumption may cause gastrointestinal upset or interact with certain medications.

Precautions include consulting with a healthcare professional, especially for pregnant or breastfeeding individuals, due to the herb's potential effects on uterine contractions and infant development.

What are the active constituents of Celtis australis ?

Celtis australis, also known as wild pear, has active constituents such as flavonoids, triterpenoids, and alkaloids.

The leaves and fruits contain flavonoids like kaempferol and quercetin, which possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Triterpenoids, including ursolic acid, have been shown to have antimicrobial and antifungal activities.

Alkaloids present in the plant have been reported to exhibit analgesic and anti-inflammatory effects, contributing to the plant's traditional use in medicine.

What are the possible side effect of using Celtis australis improperly?

Improper use of Celtis australis, also known as wild pear, increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as allergic reactions, respiratory problems, and skin irritation.

Long-term consumption of its seeds, leaves, or unripe fruits may lead to digestive issues, including diarrhea, abdominal pain, and nausea. Additionally, its high levels of saponins can interact with medications and worsen underlying health conditions.

Proper identification and preparation are essential to minimize risks.
