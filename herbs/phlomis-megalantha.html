What's the taxonomical classification of Phlomis megalantha?

Phlomis megalantha belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Lamiaceae. More specifically, it is situated within the genus Phlomis, and its unique biological identity is defined by its specific designation as the species megalantha.

The bioactive compounds within the plant, such as diterpenoids and flavonoids, work by inhibiting these specific protein pathways to reduce oxidative stress. Additionally, the phytochemicals may influence various signaling proteins involved in the regulation of the immune response. Such molecular interactions provide the basis for the plant's traditional use in treating inflammatory conditions.

      What are the herbal preparations of Phlomis megalantha?

      Phlomis megalantha is prepared primarily as a decoction or an infusion using the dried leaves and flowering tops of the plant. These liquid extracts are often consumed to utilize the phytochemical properties found within the herb.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Phlomis megalantha can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or digestive distress if ingested or handled improperly. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, many members of the Lamiaceae family contain essential oils that may trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Direct contact with the foliage could result in redness, itching, or a localized rash on the skin. If consumed, the plant may cause stomach upset or other gastrointestinal issues due to its complex chemical composition. It is generally advised to avoid direct contact and prevent ingestion to mitigate these potential risks.
