Phlomis megalantha

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phlomis megalantha has a woody, perennial habit characterized by large, ovate, rugose leaves with crenate margins and dense, velvety pubescence. The foliage is thick and felt-like, providing a soft texture to the plant's overall structure. Its most striking feature is the terminal verticillasters that hold massive, tubular, bright yellow flowers. These large corollas are arranged in distinct whorls along a sturdy, upright stem. The inflorescence is topped by a dense cluster that commands attention due to its significant scale.
