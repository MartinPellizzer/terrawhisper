Corylus yunnanensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Corylus yunnanensis?

Corylus yunnanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Fagales. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the family Betulaceae, placing it within the genus Corylus, where it is specifically identified as the species yunnanensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Corylus yunnanensis has deciduous shrubs or small trees reaching heights of up to 5 meters, characterized by simple, alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated margins and long petioles. The leaf surfaces are typically glabrous or slightly pubescent, often displaying a distinct texture on the underside. Its flowering habit involves slender, drooping catkins that emerge early in the season, with male catkins being longer and more pendulous than the female ones. Small, nut-like fruits develop within a leafy involucre, which serves as a protective structure for the developing seeds. The bark is generally smooth and grayish, providing a stark contrast to the dense green foliage during the growing season.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

The species prefers temperate climates found in shaded slopes and woodland understories. Its distribution is closely tied to the moist, montane environments characteristic of these specific subtropical mountainous zones.
