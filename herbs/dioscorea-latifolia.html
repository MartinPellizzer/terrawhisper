Dioscorea latifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Dioscorea latifolia?

Dioscorea latifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Dioscoreales. The plant is a member of the family Dioscoreaceae, which contains the genus Dioscorea, and is specifically identified by its species name, latifolia.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Dioscorea latifolia contains high concentrations of steroidal saponins, specifically diosgenin, which serves as a primary precursor for the synthesis of various steroid hormones. These chemical compounds are highly valued in pharmacology for their ability to be converted into progesterone, cortisone, and other essential medicinal steroids. The plant also possesses various alkaloids and flavonoids that contribute to its overall bioactive profile. Researchers study these specific phytochemicals to explore their potential applications in anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial treatments.
