What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Primula szechuanica has a basal rosette of oblong to lanceolate leaves that are typically crinkled or rugose with serrated margins. The foliage grows densely from a central rhizome, forming a compact mound of green greenery. From the center of this rosette, slender, upright scapes emerge to bear clusters of flowers. The blossoms are characterized by five-lobed corollas that exhibit varying shades of yellow or pale colors. Small, inconspicuous stamens are tucked within the throat of the flower, while the overall plant maintains a low-growing, herbaceous structure.
