White Clover

Trifolium repens

White Clover (Trifolium repens) is a low-growing perennial herb commonly found in lawns and pastures, known for its trifoliate leaves and small white flowers, and it has been traditionally used to support digestive health and alleviate symptoms of conditions such as irritable bowel syndrome.

Botanical Identification

White Clover, scientifically known as Trifolium repens, belongs to the Fabaceae family. Commonly called Dutch Clover or Small White Clover, it is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. The plant has trifoliate leaves, white flowers, and a fibrous root system. It is also referred to as Trefoil in herbal literature.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Fabales
  • Order: Fabales
  • Family: Fabaceae
  • Genus: Trifolium
  • Species: Trifolium repens

Active Compounds

White Clover contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and tannins, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to reduce inflammation and support digestive health. Their combined action enhances the herb's effectiveness in traditional herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Phenolic acid
  • Tannin

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for digestive issues and as a mild sedative.

Decoction: Used for respiratory ailments and as an antioxidant.

Extract: Used for skin conditions and as an anti-inflammatory.

Safety Profile

White Clover is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause mild gastrointestinal upset in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to limited safety data. Always consult a healthcare provider before use, especially for those with existing health conditions.
