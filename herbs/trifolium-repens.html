White Clover (Trifolium repens)

White Clover (Trifolium repens) is a member of the Fabaceae family, native to Europe, Asia, and North America. Traditionally, its leaves, flowers, and stems have been used for infusions, decoctions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its diuretic, anti-inflammatory, and carminative actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and traditional chinese medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous.
Height
15 to 30 centimeters tall.
Leaves
Bilaterally symmetrical leaflets with stipules, upper surface pale green to white, lower surface lighter green, margins entire, abaxial surface with prominent stomatal bands.
Flowers
Small white flowers arranged in a spike, actinomorphic, with five white petals and three leaf-like sepals, each flower having a single central yellow glandular structure.

Herbaceous,匍匐生长, 有绒毛, 节间短, 叶鞘状排列, 节上生有叶芽和分枝。
