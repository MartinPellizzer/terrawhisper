Tongoloa dunnii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tongoloa dunnii has a woody, arborescent habit characterized by a single unbranched stem that supports a dense terminal crown of long, strap-shaped leaves. These leaves are linear to lanceolate in shape and feature parallel venation typical of monocots. The leaf margins are smooth, though they may occasionally exhibit slight undulations toward the tips. The plant produces large inflorescences that emerge from the center of the leaf rosette. These flowering structures are supported by sturdy, elongated stalks that elevate the blossoms above the foliage.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical rainforest regions of West Africa, specifically found within the moist forest ecosystems of Nigeria and Cameroon. It thrives in high-humidity environments where dense canopy cover provides the necessary shade and soil moisture. Its distribution is closely tied to the specific microclimates found along the Guineo-Congolian forest belt. Localized populations are often concentrated in lowland areas that receive consistent year-round rainfall. These specific ecological requirements limit its presence to these particular equatorial forest zones.
