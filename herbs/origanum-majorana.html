Majorana (Origanum majorana)

Majorana (Origanum majorana) is a member of the Lamiaceae family, native to Mediterranean Region, Middle East, and Southwest Asia. Traditionally, its leaves, flowers, and stems have been used for infusions, decoctions, and oil infusions.

This herb is particularly valued for its carminative, antispasmodic, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and ayurvedic medicine.

Flowers
Inflorescences are terminal spikes bearing small, pale yellow to white actinomorphic flowers with four ovate petals and five elliptic sepals, arranged in a dense cluster.
Stems
Elongated, erect growth habit, opposite branching pattern, glabrous surface, and presence of pith in cross-section.
