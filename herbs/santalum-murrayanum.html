Santalum murrayanum

Santalum murrayanum, commonly known as Murray's Sandalwood, is a species of flowering plant in the sandalwood family.

It is native to southeastern Australia and is prized for its aromatic and medicinal properties.

Murray's Sandalwood has been traditionally used in Australian medicine to treat a variety of health issues, including wounds, skin irritations, and respiratory problems, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The plant contains bioactive constituents such as santalols, santalenes, and other terpenoids, which are responsible for its therapeutic actions, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects.
