What is Elymus debilis?

Elymus debilis is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender stems, typically 30-90 cm tall, bearing a panicle up to 20 cm long with a greenish-yellow inflorescence. The leaves are linear, flat, and 5-15 mm wide, with a glabrous sheath and a ligule that is ciliate or not. The plant produces seeds in a spikelet, 10-15 mm long, with a glabrous lemma and palea, and a caryopsis that is 5-7 mm long.

Elymus debilis grows in open woods, rocky outcrops, and dry slopes, often in areas with shallow soil and full sun to partial shade.
