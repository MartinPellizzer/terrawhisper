Picrasma quassioides

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Picrasma quassioides has large, pinnately compound leaves that typically consist of seven to eleven leaflets arranged along a central rachis. The leaflets are generally elliptical or lanceolate in shape with entire margins and acute tips. This woody shrub or small tree exhibits a spreading growth habit with branches that can become quite sturdy. The flowers are small, yellowish-green, and arranged in dense terminal panicles. During the fruiting stage, the plant produces small, fleshy drupes that transition in color as they mature.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Picrasma quassioides contains bitter principles such as quassinoids, specifically quassin, neoquassin, and picrasin, which serve as its primary medicinal compounds. These specific terpenoids are responsible for the plant's potent biological activities, including anti-malarial and anti-inflammatory effects. The chemical profile also includes various alkaloids and glycosides that contribute to its traditional use in treating digestive ailments. Researchers focus on these molecules to develop new treatments for parasitic infections and gastrointestinal disorders. What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Picrasma quassioides targets various inflammatory mediators and metabolic enzymes, most notably inhibiting cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2), inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS), and lipoxygenase (LOX) enzymes. These interactions effectively reduce the production of pro-inflammatory molecules like prostaglandin E2 and nitric oxide, which contributes to its potent anti-inflammatory properties. The plant's bitter quassinoids also influence glucose metabolism by modulating insulin signaling pathways and targeting enzymes involved in gluconeogenesis. Additionally, certain bioactive compounds interact with tumor-related proteins to induce apoptosis in malignant cells by disrupting cell cycle progression. These multi-target mechanisms allow the plant to exert complex biological effects across inflammatory, metabolic, and oncological pathways.

What are the therapeutic actions of Picrasma quassioides?

Picrasma quassioides has therapeutic properties that include acting as a potent bitter tonic, a mild aperient, a cholagogue, a digestive stimulant, and an anthelmintic. The intense bitter compounds within the plant stimulate the secretion of gastric juices to enhance overall digestion. As a cholagogue, it promotes the flow of bile from the gallbladder to assist in lipid metabolism. Its aperient qualities provide a gentle laxative effect to help regulate bowel movements. Additionally, the plant serves as an effective anthelmintic by helping to expel intestinal worms.
