Picrasma quassioides

What's the taxonomical classification of Picrasma quassioides?

Picrasma quassioides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Sapindales and falls within the family Simaroubaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Picrasma and the species quassioidea.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Picrasma quassioides contains bitter principles such as quassinoids, specifically quassin, neoquassin, and picrasin, which serve as its primary medicinal compounds. These specific terpenoids are responsible for the plant's potent biological activities, including anti-malarial and anti-inflammatory effects. The chemical profile also includes various alkaloids and glycosides that contribute to its traditional use in treating digestive ailments. Researchers focus on these molecules to develop new treatments for parasitic infections and gastrointestinal disorders. The concentration of these bitter compounds varies depending on the specific part of the plant being harvested.

What are the therapeutic actions of Picrasma quassioides?

Picrasma quassioides has potent bitter principles like quassinoids that act as gastrointestinal stimulants, anthelmintics, and anti-inflammatory agents to treat digestive disorders and parasitic infections. These bioactive compounds stimulate the secretion of digestive juices, which helps to improve appetite and facilitate better nutrient absorption. The plant is also utilized in traditional medicine to reduce fever and address various skin conditions due to its antimicrobial properties. Furthermore, its ability to induce bile secretion supports liver function and helps manage systemic inflammation. These combined therapeutic actions make it a significant component in treating metabolic and parasitic ailments.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat malaria, intestinal worms, fever, and various digestive disorders. The bitter principles found in the roots serve as potent anthelmintic agents to expel parasites from the body. It is also frequently applied to manage symptoms of jaundice and liver ailments. Traditional practitioners often utilize its extracts to reduce high temperatures associated with infections. These medicinal properties make it a significant resource in herbal medicine for gastrointestinal health.

What are the herbal preparations of Picrasma quassioides?

Picrasma quassioides is prepared primarily as a decoction of the dried root or bark, which is boiled in water to extract its bitter principles. This liquid extract is frequently used in traditional medicine to treat various ailments such as fever, intestinal worms, and skin infections. In some regional practices, the plant material is also processed into a fine powder that can be mixed with other substances for topical application. Some preparations involve macerating the plant parts in alcohol to create a concentrated tincture for more potent medicinal use. These diverse forms allow the bitter compounds to be administered orally or applied externally depending on the specific therapeutic goal.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Picrasma quassioides can cause severe gastrointestinal distress including intense nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea due to its high concentration of bitter principles. These potent bitter compounds act as strong purgatives that may lead to dehydration if consumed in excessive amounts. Some individuals might also experience localized irritation or allergic skin reactions upon direct contact with the plant material. Because of its intense physiological impact, careful dosage is critical to avoid systemic toxicity. Prolonged or improper use could potentially disrupt the natural electrolyte balance in the body.
