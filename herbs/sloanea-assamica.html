What is Sloanea assamica?

Sloanea assamica is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is a tree with a smooth, grey bark, and elliptical, coriaceous leaves that are 8-15 cm long and 4-7.5 cm wide, with a cordate base and a rounded apex. The inflorescence is a terminal, corymbous panicle, bearing small, greenish-white flowers, while the fruit is a drupe, 1.5-2 cm long, with a single seed, and a red or yellowish skin. Sloanea assamica is native to the eastern Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia, and is found in subtropical and tropical forests, typically at elevations between 1,000-2,000 meters.

The tree is often used as a medicinal plant, with its bark and leaves employed in traditional medicine for various purposes.
