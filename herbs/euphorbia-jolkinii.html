Euphorbia jolkinii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Euphorbia jolkinii has a perennial herbaceous growth habit characterized by upright, slender stems that reach heights of approximately 20 to 50 centimeters. The leaves are typically arranged in an alternate pattern and possess an elliptical or lanceolate shape with smooth margins. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in dense, terminal umbel-like cymes. These inflorescences are surrounded by specialized bracts that serve to protect the delicate reproductive structures. The plant also features a milky white latex that is characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Euphorbia jolkinii can cause severe skin irritation, chemical burns, and temporary blindness if its milky white latex comes into contact with the eyes or sensitive tissues. The sap contains diterpene esters that act as potent irritants to human skin upon contact. Exposure often results in redness, swelling, and painful blistering that may take time to heal. If ingested, the plant can trigger intense gastrointestinal distress, including vomiting and abdominal pain. Protecting the eyes and skin with gloves is essential when handling any part of this species.
