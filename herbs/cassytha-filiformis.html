Cassytha filiformis has narrow, linear leaves that are typically small, densely arranged along slender, wiry branches, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers. Its stems are woody and can become quite flexible, allowing the shrub to weave through surrounding vegetation. The foliage is often described as having a fine, thread-like appearance due to the extremely thin leaf shape.
