1. Causes allergic reactions

Haronga madagascariensis causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

This is due to the presence of allergenic compounds such as alkaloids and glycosides in the plant, which can trigger an immune response and lead to symptoms like hives, itching, and swelling. The exact mechanisms behind these reactions are not fully understood, but it is thought that the body's reaction to these compounds may be similar to its response to other common allergens.

As a result, some people may experience adverse effects when using Haronga madagascariensis.

2. Triggers asthma attacks

Haronga madagascariensis triggers asthma attacks due to its ability to stimulate the release of histamine and other chemical mediators in the body.

This can lead to increased bronchial constriction, inflammation, and mucus production, causing symptoms such as wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.

The alkaloids present in Haronga madagascariensis are thought to be responsible for this effect, making it a potential allergen or irritant that can exacerbate existing asthma conditions.

3. Produces diarrhea episodes

Haronga madagascariensis produces diarrhea episodes due to its high concentration of alkaloids and glycosides.

These compounds can irritate the digestive system, leading to increased motility and secretion in the intestines, resulting in frequent bowel movements and loose stools.

As a natural remedy for pain relief and inflammation, Haronga madagascariensis's gastrointestinal effects may be an unwanted consequence for some users.

4. Induces dizziness spells

Haronga madagascariensis induces dizziness spells due to its high concentration of iridoid glycosides, particularly harpagoside and cnicin.

These compounds can cause a decrease in blood pressure, leading to feelings of lightheadedness or dizziness, especially when standing up quickly or changing positions suddenly.

Additionally, the herb's potential stimulatory effects on the nervous system may contribute to this side effect, as it can alter heart rate and blood flow patterns.

5. Hikes blood pressure levels

Haronga madagascariensis hikes blood pressure levels due to its ability to increase adrenaline production in the body.

This can lead to an elevation in blood pressure, which may be a concern for individuals with pre-existing hypertension or cardiovascular conditions.

The compound harpagoside, found in Haronga madagascariensis, is responsible for this effect by stimulating the sympathetic nervous system, thereby increasing heart rate and blood pressure.

6. Lowers blood sugar levels

Haronga madagascariensis lowers blood sugar levels by stimulating the pancreas to release insulin and increasing glucose uptake in cells.

This effect may be due to the presence of iridoid glycosides, which have been shown to have hypoglycemic properties.

As a result, individuals taking Haronga madagascariensis may experience symptoms such as shakiness, dizziness, or sweating as their blood sugar levels drop.

7. Increases heart rate beats

Haronga madagascariensis increases heart rate beats due to its high concentration of alkaloids, particularly harpagoside and clematistic acid.

These bioactive compounds stimulate the cardiovascular system, leading to an increased heart rate and potentially elevated blood pressure. This side effect is attributed to the plant's ability to mimic the effects of digitalis, a cardiac glycoside commonly used to treat certain arrhythmias.

The increased heart rate is thought to be a direct result of harpagoside's action on the heart, causing it to beat more rapidly.

8. Relaxes muscles excessively

Haronga madagascariensis relaxes muscles excessively due to its unique composition of harpagosides and iridoid glycosides.

These compounds interact with the body's nervous system, causing a decrease in muscle tone and tension, which can lead to excessive relaxation.

This effect is often attributed to the herb's ability to stimulate the release of serotonin and other neurotransmitters that regulate muscle contraction and relaxation.

9. Stimulates rapid breathing

Haronga madagascariensis stimulates rapid breathing due to its alkaloid content, particularly harpagoside.

This compound is known to increase heart rate and blood pressure, leading to an accelerated respiratory response.

As a result, users may experience feelings of anxiety or panic in addition to the physical symptoms of rapid breathing, which can be uncomfortable but generally temporary.

10. Provokes insomnia cases

Haronga madagascariensis provokes insomnia cases due to its stimulating properties that can interfere with a person's ability to fall asleep.

The herb contains alkaloids that can increase alertness and energy levels, making it difficult for individuals to relax and wind down before bedtime.

Additionally, Haronga madagascariensis has been known to cause restlessness, anxiety, and irritability in some people, which can further exacerbate sleep disorders.
