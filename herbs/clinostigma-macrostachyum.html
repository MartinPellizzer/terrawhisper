Clinostigma macrostachyum

Clinostigma macrostachyum, also known as the Tahitian fan palm, is a species of palm tree native to the island of Tahiti in French Polynesia.

It is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Polynesian medicine, where it is used to treat various ailments due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The health benefits of Clinostigma macrostachyum include its potential to reduce fever, alleviate digestive issues, and soothe skin irritations, making it a sought-after herbal remedy in the region.

In herbal preparations, the plant's leaves or sap are often used to create infusions, decoctions, or topical applications, highlighting its versatility and importance in traditional medicine.
