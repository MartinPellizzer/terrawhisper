    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Elaeagnus multiflora can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. While the fruit is generally considered edible, consuming excessive amounts or unripe specimens can lead to stomach upset or nausea. Some people may experience localized itching or redness upon touching the foliage. It is advisable to handle the plant with caution to avoid these adverse physical reactions.
