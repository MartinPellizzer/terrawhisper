Elaeagnus multiflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Elaeagnus multiflora?

Elaeagnus multiflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant's taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Rosales and the family Elaeagnaceae, eventually reaching its specific designation within the genus Elaeagnus and the species multiflora.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Elaeagnuus multiflora contains high concentrations of bioactive flavonoids, phenolic acids, and tannins that provide significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These specific chemical constituents work to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress within biological systems. Research indicates that the plant's extracts may exhibit antimicrobial activities against various pathogenic bacteria. Some studies also suggest that its polyphenolic profile could support cardiovascular health by improving lipid metabolism. Consequently, these compounds make the plant a subject of interest for developing natural therapeutic agents.

What are the therapeutic actions of Elaeagnus multiflora?

Elaeagnuus multiflora has medicinal properties centered on its high concentrations of polyphenols, flavonoids, and essential fatty acids that provide antioxidant and anti-inflammatory benefits. These bioactive compounds work to neutralize free radicals within the body, reducing oxidative stress at a cellular level. The presence of vitamin C and various minerals further supports immune function and helps combat systemic inflammation. Additionally, the edible fruits contain dietary fibers that promote gastrointestinal health and regular digestion. Regular consumption of these components may assist in long-term metabolic stability and cardiovascular wellness.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including skin infections, inflammation, and digestive issues through its medicinal properties. The leaves and bark contain bioactive compounds that offer antibacterial and antifungal benefits. These constituents help reduce swelling and soothe irritated skin surfaces when applied topically. Furthermore, traditional applications involve using extracts to alleviate internal discomfort related to gastrointestinal distress. Researchers continue to study these specific chemical profiles to better understand their therapeutic potential.

What are the herbal preparations of Elaeagnus multiflora?

Elaeagnus multiflora is prepared as a decoction of the dried fruit, a tea from the leaves, or a topical paste using the bark. The berries are frequently boiled in water to create a concentrated liquid used for internal consumption. Infusions made from the leaves are also common for addressing various health concerns. Additionally, some traditions utilize the crushed bark to form a poultice applied directly to the skin. These diverse methods allow the medicinal properties of the plant to be delivered in multiple ways.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Elaeagnus multiflora can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. The small, silvery scales covering the leaves and stems may trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals through physical or chemical irritation. While the fruit is generally considered edible, consuming excessive amounts or unripe specimens can lead to stomach upset or nausea. Some people may experience localized itching or redness upon touching the foliage. It is advisable to handle the plant with caution to avoid these adverse physical reactions.
