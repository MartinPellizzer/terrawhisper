Thalictrum baicalense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Thalictrum baicalense has herbaceous stems that reach heights of approximately 30 to 60 centimeters and bear compound leaves with three to five leaflets that are broadly ovate and finely serrated. The foliage displays a distinct texture and a vibrant green color that characterizes its growth habit in alpine environments. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the terminal clusters, often featuring delicate petals and numerous stamens. These floral structures are typically yellowish or greenish in hue and are arranged in a loose panicle. The plant also develops a sturdy rhizomatous root system that helps it anchor in moist, rocky soils.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?
