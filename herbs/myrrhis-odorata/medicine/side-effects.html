10 Possible Side Effects Of Myrrhis Odorata (Sweet Cicely)

Myrrhis odorata has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal upset, allergic reactions, and skin irritation.

The causes of these side effects are often due to the herb's high concentrations of coumarin, a compound that can irritate the digestive tract and skin.

If left unaddressed, these side effects can worsen lives by causing chronic digestive problems, allergic reactions, and skin conditions that may lead to absenteeism and reduced quality of life.

This side effect is due to the high concentration of furanocoumarins, a group of compounds responsible for its phototoxic properties. When applied topically, these compounds can react with UV light, leading to inflammation and irritation of the skin.

This is due to the plant's ability to stimulate an immune response, triggering the release of histamine and other chemical mediators that cause inflammation and skin irritation.
