What to know about Verbascum thapsus before using it medicinally

Verbascum thapsus, commonly known as great mullein, is a herb with various medicinal uses, primarily in the treatment of respiratory conditions such as bronchitis, asthma, and coughs.

The main health benefits of this herb include its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties, which help to relieve congestion and promote the clearance of mucus from the airways. The herb's main therapeutic properties include its anodyne, demulcent, and antiseptic properties, which provide pain relief, soothe irritated tissues, and prevent infection. Bioactive compounds present in Verbascum thapsus include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

People often prepare Verbascum thapsus as a tea, infusion, or tincture to harness its medicinal benefits, although it can also be used as a topical application for skin conditions and wounds.
