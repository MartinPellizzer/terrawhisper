Verbascum Thapsus Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Verbascum thapsus, commonly known as Common Mullein, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its soft, velvety leaves and bright yellow flowers.

This herb has been traditionally used for its soothing and anti-inflammatory properties, offering potential benefits for respiratory and skin health.

The bioactive constituents in Verbascum thapsus include mucilage, flavonoids, saponins, and mucilaginous compounds, which contribute to its therapeutic actions.

Herbal preparations such as infusions, tinctures, and poultices can be made from the leaves and flowers to harness its medicinal properties.
