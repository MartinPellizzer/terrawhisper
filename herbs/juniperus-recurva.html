Juniperus recurva

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Juniperus recurva has scale-like, overlapping leaves arranged in whorls of three on its spreading, often pendulous branches. The foliage is typically dark green and provides a dense, textured appearance to the shrub or tree. Its woody stems can become twisted and gnarled with age, contributing to a rugged silhouette. Small, fleshy, berry-like cones develop from the female plants, turning a deep blue or purple color when mature. These cones contain the seeds and serve as a primary diagnostic feature of the species.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of the western Himalayas, spanning across northern India, Nepal, Bhutan, and parts of southern China. It primarily inhabits subalpine and alpine zones, often found growing at elevations between 2,500 and 4,500 meters above sea level. The species thrives in rocky crevices and steep mountain slopes where drainage is rapid. Its distribution is closely tied to specific climatic conditions characterized by cold temperatures and heavy seasonal snow cover. Consequently, the plant is restricted to these specific montane ecosystems across the Himalayan mountain range.
