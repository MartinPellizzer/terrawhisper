Juniperus recurva

What's the taxonomical classification of Juniperus recurva?

Juniperus recurva belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Pinidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Pinales and the family Cupressaceae. Ultimately, this plant is identified by the genus Juniperus and the specific species name recurva.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Juniperus recurva contains high concentrations of terpenoids, including alpha-pinene and limonene, along with various phenolic compounds and flavonoids. These chemical constituents are primarily found within the plant's essential oils and extracted resins. The terpenoids provide the plant with significant antimicrobial and antioxidant properties that are often studied for therapeutic potential. Additionally, the presence of specific flavonoids may contribute to anti-inflammatory effects in biological systems. Research into these bioactive molecules aims to identify their specific roles in traditional and modern medicine.

What are the therapeutic actions of Juniperus recurva?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat ailments such as urinary tract infections, digestive issues, and various forms of inflammation. Its berries contain essential oils that act as a natural diuretic to help cleanse the kidneys and bladder. Traditional applications also include using the plant to soothe menstrual cramps and alleviate symptoms of rheumatism. Some practitioners suggest its antimicrobial properties can help combat minor skin irritations and fungal infections. However, it should be used with caution due to its potential impact on kidney function.
