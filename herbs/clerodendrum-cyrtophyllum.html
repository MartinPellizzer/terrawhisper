Clerodendrum cyrtophyllum

What's the taxonomical classification of Clerodendrum cyrtophyllum?

Clerodendrum cyrtophyllum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Lamiales. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the family Lamiaceae, which places it in the genus Clerodendrum, ultimately identifying it as the specific species cyrtophyllum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clerodendrum cyrtophyllum has opposite, elliptical leaves with prominent veins and curved margins, woody stems, and tubular flowers that transition from white to pinkish hues.

The foliage is characterized by a lush, green texture that often displays a slight sheen under direct light. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, fragrant blossoms that emerge from the axils of the leaves. The plant typically develops a shrubby growth habit with branching patterns that support its heavy flowering periods.

These morphological traits combine to create a distinctive aesthetic common among members of the Verbenaceae family.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Clerodendrum cyrtophyllum can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and localized swelling if the plant material touches sensitive skin. Some individuals may experience redness or itching upon direct contact with the leaves or stems. If ingested, the plant could potentially lead to gastrointestinal distress or other toxic reactions depending on the dosage. There is also limited scientific documentation regarding the long-term systemic effects of consuming this specific species. Caution is recommended when handling the plant to avoid unexpected dermatological or internal reactions.
