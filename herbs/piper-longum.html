Piper Longum

Piper longum, commonly known as long pepper, is a tropical flowering plant native to India and has been used for centuries in traditional medicine systems such as Ayurveda and Unani.

It is valued for its aromatic, pungent berries, which contain a variety of bioactive compounds, including piperine, which is known to enhance the absorption of other nutrients and compounds in the body. In medicinal applications, Piper longum is often used to aid digestion, relieve respiratory issues, and reduce inflammation due to its warming and carminative properties. It has also been studied for its potential antioxidant and antimicrobial effects, making it a valuable herb in both traditional and modern herbal medicine.

Despite its benefits, it should be used with caution, as excessive consumption may cause gastrointestinal irritation or interact with certain medications.
