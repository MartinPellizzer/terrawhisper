Long pepper

Piper longum

Long Pepper (Piper longum) is a tropical climbing vine native to South and Southeast Asia, known for its pungent, peppery aroma and its traditional use in Ayurvedic and Chinese medicine to enhance digestion and treat respiratory and inflammatory conditions.

Botanical Identification

Long Pepper, scientifically known as Piper longum, belongs to the family Piperaceae. It is also called "Pippali" in Sanskrit and "Kali Mirch" in Hindi. A perennial vine, it originates from the Indian subcontinent and has elongated, yellowish fruits. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, while flowers are small and green. Synonyms include "Long Black Pepper" and "Vrihat Pippali" in Ayurvedic texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Magnoliopsida
  • Order: Piperales
  • Family: Piperaceae
  • Genus: Piper
  • Species: Piper longum

Safety Profile

Long pepper is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It should be avoided during pregnancy and by those with peptic ulcers due to its potential to irritate the stomach lining.
