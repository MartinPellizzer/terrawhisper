What is Bulbostylis pilosa?

Bulbostylis pilosa is a perennial plant species belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

The plant produces brownish or reddish-brown spikelets, 2-8 mm in length, and 2-4 mm in width, on a slender raceme, and the flowers are dioecious. The spikelets are composed of 2-3 fertile florets and 3-5 sterile florets, and the seeds are 0.8-1.2 mm in length and 0.4-0.6 mm in width.

The plant grows in wetlands, such as marshes, swamps, and along streams, and is commonly found in tropical and subtropical regions.
