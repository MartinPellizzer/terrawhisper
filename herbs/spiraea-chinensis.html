What's the taxonomical classification of Spiraea chinensis?

Spiraea chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, eventually reaching the order Rosales. As a member of the family Rosaceae, this plant is further categorized into the genus Spiraea, with its specific designation being the species chinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Spiraea chinensis has arching, multi-stemmed woody shrubs characterized by serrated, lanceolate leaves and dense terminal clusters of small, five-petaled pink flowers. The foliage typically appears in bright green shades and provides a textured backdrop for the blooming period. Each stem grows in a graceful, spreading habit that can reach several feet in height and width. The inflorescences consist of numerous tiny blossoms that create a vibrant, cloud-like effect across the plant. During the late spring and summer, these flower heads become the most prominent morphological feature of the shrub.
