Epiphyllum oxypetalum

What's the taxonomical classification of Epiphyllum oxypetalum?

Epiphyllum oxypetalum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further organized into the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is a member of the order Caryophyllales and falls under the family Cactaceae. Specifically, it is categorized within the genus Epiphyllum, with its unique scientific designation being the species oxypetalum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Epiphyllum oxypetalum has flattened, leaf-like stem segments that are long, slender, and possess wavy or serrated margins. These succulent stems function as the primary photosynthetic organs of the plant while also serving as storage vessels for water. The margins of these stems are typically adorned with small, pointed spines or teeth that emerge from areoles. During its blooming phase, the plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that emerge from the edges of these specialized stems. The overall structure is epiphytic, meaning it is designed to cling to and grow upon other plants or surfaces in its natural habitat.

How is this plant cultivated?

Because it is an epiphytic cactus, it thrives in high humidity and temperatures between 60 and 80 degrees Fahrenheit. You should water the soil thoroughly whenever the top inch feels dry to prevent root rot.
