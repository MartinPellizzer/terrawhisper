Egyptian Senna (Senna alexandrina) Compounds

The primary compounds in Egyptian Senna include flavonoids, which have anti-inflammatory properties, and glycosides, known for their laxative effects. Coumarin contributes to its aromatic profile, while alkaloids provide additional medicinal benefits. These compounds work together to support the plant's therapeutic uses in herbal medicine.

Egyptian Senna (Senna alexandrina) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Egyptian Senna (Senna alexandrina). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds are often responsible for the therapeutic effects associated with Senna in traditional herbal medicine. Flavonoids may also support digestive health and have potential in treating various ailments.

2. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Egyptian Senna (Senna alexandrina). It contributes to the plant's laxative properties by stimulating bowel movements. This compound is commonly found in the plant's leaves and is responsible for its traditional use in treating constipation.

3. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Egyptian Senna (Senna alexandrina). It exhibits antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties, contributing to the plant's use in treating digestive issues and respiratory conditions. Its presence enhances the therapeutic potential of Senna alexandrina in traditional herbal medicine.

Saponin is a key active compound in Egyptian Senna (Senna alexandrina). It contributes to the plant's laxative properties by stimulating bowel movements and reducing intestinal inflammation. 8. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Egyptian Senna (Senna alexandrina). These compounds contribute to the plant's laxative effects and anti-inflammatory properties. 9. Essential oil

Essential oil is a volatile compound found in Egyptian Senna (Senna alexandrina). It contributes to the plant's aromatic profile and has been studied for its potential antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The essential oil is typically extracted through steam distillation from the plant's leaves and flowers. It plays a role in the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a pigment found in Egyptian Senna (Senna alexandrina) that contributes to its potential medicinal properties. It is known for its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, which may support digestive health and reduce oxidative stress in the body.
