Swertia diluta

What's the taxonomical classification of Swertia diluta?

Swertia diluta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Gentianaales. This plant is further categorized into the family Gentianaceae, specifically within the genus Swertia, and is identified by the unique species epithet diluta.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Swertia diluta has opposite, decussate, lanceolate to oblong leaves that are typically 5 to 15 centimeters long with entire margins and acute tips. The plant grows as a perennial shrub reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters with woody stems. Small, actinomorphic flowers appear in axillary cymes and possess a pale greenish-white or yellowish corolla. The fruit is a characteristic capsule that splits open to release numerous small, winged seeds. These morphological traits are typical of the Gentianaceae family.
