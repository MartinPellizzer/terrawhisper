Whitfieldia longifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Whitfieldia longifolia has lanceolate to linear leaves that are arranged oppositely along a slender, upright stem reaching heights of up to thirty centimeters. The foliage is characterized by entire margins and a smooth texture that distinguishes it from many other members of its genus. Its small, actinomorphic flowers typically feature four to five petals that exhibit a delicate, pale coloration. These blooms are supported by thin pedicels that emerge from the leaf axils. The overall structure of the plant is herbaceous and relatively lightweight, allowing it to thrive in open, sunny environments.
