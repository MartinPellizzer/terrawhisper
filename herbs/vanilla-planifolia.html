Vanilla (Vanilla planifolia)

Vanilla (Vanilla planifolia) is a member of the Orchidaceae family, native to Polynesia, Tahiti, and French Polynesia. Traditionally, its fruits, leaves, and bark have been used for infusions, decoctions, and tinctures.

This herb is particularly valued for its tonic, antispasmodic, and sedative actions, and has a long history of use in traditional chinese medicine, european herbal medicine, and mediterranean herbal traditions.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically reaches a height of 2 to 5 meters.
Leaves
Smooth, glabrous, lanceolate blades with dorsiventral coloration, featuring prominent stomatal bands on the lower surface.
Flowers
Inflorescences consisting of clustered, greenish-yellow flowers with five sepals and five petals, actinomorphic symmetry, and a slender, tubular corona at the base of the ovary.
Stems
Erect, woody, branching in a sympodial pattern, with smooth, glabrous surfaces and adventitious root scars along the nodes.
