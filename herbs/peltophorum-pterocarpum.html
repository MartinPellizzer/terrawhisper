Peltophorum pterocarpum

What's the taxonomical classification of Peltophorum pterocarpum?

Peltophorum pterocarpum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Fabales and is a member of the family Fabaceae. Specifically, it is categorized under the genus Peltophorum, with its unique biological identity defined by the species name pterocarpum.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Peltophorum pterocarpum contains bioactive flavonoids, tannins, and polyphenolic compounds that provide significant therapeutic potential. These phytochemicals contribute to the plant's documented antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties. Researchers have identified specific alkaloids and saponins within its bark and leaves that assist in managing oxidative stress. Extracts from various parts of the tree are often utilized in traditional medicine to treat skin infections and digestive issues. The presence of these complex organic molecules makes the species a subject of interest for modern pharmacological studies.

What are the therapeutic actions of Peltophorum pterocarpum?

Peltophorum pterocarpum has significant therapeutic potential due to its high concentration of bioactive compounds like flavonoids, tannins, and saponins that exhibit strong antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. The bark and leaves are frequently utilized in traditional medicine to treat skin infections, wounds, and various inflammatory conditions. These plant extracts work by neutralizing free radicals and inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory mediators in the body. Some studies also suggest that its chemical constituents may possess hepatoprotective and antidiabetic effects. Consequently, the species serves as a valuable natural resource for developing new pharmacological agents.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, inflammation, digestive disorders, and various bacterial or fungal ailments. Its bark and leaves contain bioactive compounds that demonstrate significant antimicrobial properties. Researchers have noted its potential effectiveness in managing skin conditions like eczema or fungal rashes through topical applications. Some traditional medicinal practices also utilize extracts from the plant to soothe internal inflammation and gastrointestinal distress. These therapeutic uses stem from the presence of flavonoids and tannins within its chemical composition.
