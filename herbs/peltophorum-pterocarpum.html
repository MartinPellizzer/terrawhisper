Peltophorum pterocarpum

What's the taxonomical classification of Peltophorum pterocarpum?

Peltophorum pterocarpum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Fabales and is a member of the family Fabaceae. Specifically, it is categorized under the genus Peltophorum, with its unique biological identity defined by the species name pterocarpum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Peltophorum pterocarpum has a large spreading canopy supported by a trunk with greyish-brown bark and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oblong leaflets. The tree produces large, bright yellow flowers that grow in dense terminal panicles. Its distinctive seed pods are woody, oblong, and turn a dark reddish-brown color as they mature. The foliage typically remains evergreen or semi-deciduous depending on the local climate and water availability. These morphological features collectively allow the species to thrive in tropical environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical regions of South America, specifically spanning from Brazil through the Caribbean and into Central America. It has since been widely introduced and naturalized across various tropical and subtropical zones globally. Significant populations are now found throughout Southeast Asia, including countries like Malaysia, Thailand, and Indonesia. It also thrives in parts of Australia and several island nations in the Pacific. Because of its adaptability, it is frequently planted in urban landscapes across many different continents.

What are the pharmacological activities of Peltophorum pterocarpum?

This plant has documented anti-inflammatory, anti-proliferative, antibacterial, antioxidant, and antiulcer pharmacological activities derived from its diverse phytochemical constituents. Its potent antioxidant properties help neutralize free radicals, thereby reducing oxidative stress within biological systems. The plant also exhibits significant antibacterial capabilities that can inhibit the growth of various pathogenic microorganisms. Furthermore, its anti-inflammatory and antiulcer effects suggest potential utility in treating gastrointestinal issues and chronic swelling. Finally, the observed anti-proliferative activity indicates its possible role in managing abnormal cell growth.

As discussed in a study, the medicinal plant recipe N039 which contains Peltophorum pterocarpum (DC.) showed higher anti-proliferative activity on HeLa cell lines than the standard anti-cancer drug cisplatin by 1.29 times. The investigation of these aqueous extracts included testing for phytochemicals such as alkaloids, flavonoids, tannins and xanthones, though anthraquinones and carotenoids were not found. A positive relationship was observed between anti-proliferative activity and both MMP-2 inhibition activity and metal chelating inhibition activity. However, no relationship was found between anti-proliferative activity and apoptosis induction, free radical scavenging activity or lipid peroxidation inhibition activity.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse array of medicinal compounds including flavonoids such as quercetin and rhamnetin, as well as tannins and phenols. These specific chemical constituents contribute to the plant's potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The presence of flavonoids like quercetin and rhamnetin helps neutralize free radicals within biological systems. Additionally, the tannins provide astringent qualities that are often utilized for their healing effects. Together, these phenols and other bioactive molecules form a complex profile of therapeutic potential.

As discussed in a study, Peltophorum pterocarpum is a rich source for phenols and consists of phenols, flavonoids, and tannins which have potential antioxidant and cytoprotective activity. The methanolic extract of the leaves of Peltophorum pterocarpum (MEPP) was evaluated using doses of 100 mg/kg and 200 mg/kg on groups of six rats. During the evaluation, gastric lesions were induced by oral administration of 20 mg/kg of indomethacin. The MEPP treated groups showed a significant decrease in mean ulcer index, free acidity, and total acidity at p<0.01 compared to the control group. The study revealed that MEPP exhibited potential antiulcer activity and showed a dose dependent antiulcer effect.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating acne vulgaris, Escherichia coli O157:H7, diarrhea, methicillin resistant Staphylococcus aureus (MRSA) infection, and peptic ulcer. Its medicinal properties are often utilized to combat skin conditions like acne vulgaris through its antimicrobial effects. The plant also demonstrates significant activity against enteric pathogens such as Escherichia coli O157:H7 to help manage cases of diarrhea. Researchers have noted its potential to fight resistant bacteria including methicillin resistant Staphylococcus aureus (MRSA) infections. Additionally, its therapeutic components can assist in the management of a peptic ulcer by protecting the gastric lining.

What side effects this plant can have?
