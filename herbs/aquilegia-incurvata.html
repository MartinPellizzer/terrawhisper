Aquilegia incurvata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aquilegia incurvata has a basal rosette of lobed, grayish-green leaves and upright flowering stems that reach heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant is characterized by its nodding, bell-shaped flowers that feature distinctive nectar spurs extending backward from the petals. Each blossom displays a combination of pale blue or violet colors with yellow centers located within the inner sepals. Its stems are slender and often slightly curved, supporting the weight of the drooping floral structures. The overall architecture of the plant reflects its adaptation to rocky or shaded montane environments.

Its distribution is often fragmented due to its preference for specific microclimates found within mountain ranges. Because it relies on cool, moist environments, its presence is closely tied to the availability of snowmelt and rocky terrain.
