What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Zea Mays (Corn)?

Zea mays, also known as corn, has health benefits such as lowering cholesterol levels and alleviating digestive issues.

Its medicinal constituents include ferulic acid, zeatin, and ferulic esters, which have been shown to have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Corn can be used to prepare various medicinal preparations, including teas, infusions, and salves. However, excessive consumption of corn may lead to gastrointestinal disturbances, and individuals with gluten intolerance or diabetes should exercise caution when using corn as a medicinal agent.

Precautions include consulting a healthcare professional before using corn medicinally.

This article explains the health benefits, active constituents, medicinal preparations, possible side effects, and precautions related to Zea mays.

What are the health benefits of Zea mays?

Zea mays has health benefits such as being rich in antioxidants, fiber, and vitamins A and E. The antioxidant properties of zeaxanthin, a carotenoid found in corn, may help protect the eyes from age-related macular degeneration.

Additionally, corn is a good source of soluble fiber, which can help lower cholesterol levels and regulate blood sugar levels.

Corn also contains various minerals like potassium, magnesium, and iron, making it a nutritious addition to a balanced diet.

What are the active constituents of Zea mays?

The health benefits of Zea mays comes from its active constituents, such as beta-carotene, zeaxanthin, and lutein.

These carotenoids have antioxidant properties that help protect the eyes and skin from damage, while also supporting immune function.

Additionally, corn contains fiber, vitamins B and E, and minerals like potassium and magnesium, which can aid in digestion, energy production, and heart health.

What are the medicinal preparations of Zea mays?

Zea mays has different medicinal preparations, such as corn silk tea, which is used to treat urinary tract infections and kidney stones due to its diuretic properties.

Corn oil, extracted from the germ, is used in cosmetics for its moisturizing effects and in traditional medicine to reduce inflammation and pain. Zea mays starch, also known as cornstarch, is used as an anti-inflammatory agent and to soothe skin irritations.

Additionally, Zea mays extracts are used in pharmaceutical products to treat conditions such as diabetes and hypertension.

What are the possible side effect of using Zea mays improperly?

Improper use of Zea mays increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as digestive issues like bloating and gas, stomach cramps, and diarrhea.

Long-term consumption can lead to an increased risk of insulin resistance, blood sugar spikes, and even type 2 diabetes. Additionally, high doses or excessive intake may cause allergic reactions, skin irritation, and respiratory problems. Furthermore, some individuals may experience migraines, headaches, and fatigue as a result of corn sensitivities or intolerances.

In severe cases, improper use of corn can lead to gastrointestinal blockages, kidney damage, and even anaphylaxis in rare instances.

What precautions to take when using Zea mays medicinally?

Before using Zea mays for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as ensuring the proper identification of the plant to avoid misidentification with toxic lookalikes.

Additionally, consult with a qualified healthcare professional or licensed herbalist to determine the safe dosage and administration method.

It's also essential to monitor for allergic reactions and interactions with other medications, as well as store the corn in a cool, dry place to maintain potency and prevent contamination.
