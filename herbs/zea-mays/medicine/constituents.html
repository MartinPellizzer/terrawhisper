10 Medicinal Constituents Of Zea Mays (Corn)

Zea mays has active constituents such as ferulic acid, feruloyltyramine, and phytic acid.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which can help alleviate digestive issues, prevent cancer, and protect against oxidative stress.

The antioxidants in corn can improve heart health by reducing cholesterol levels and improving circulation, thereby reducing the risk of cardiovascular disease and improving overall well-being.

This article explains in details the 10 best active constituents of Zea mays.

1. Kaempferol

Zea mays kaempferol is a flavonoid compound that has been identified in the plant.

This bioactive molecule has been found to possess various pharmacological properties, including antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities. Kaempferol has been shown to protect against oxidative stress and DNA damage, making it a potential natural agent for disease prevention and treatment.

Additionally, its anti-inflammatory effects may help alleviate symptoms associated with chronic diseases.

2. Quercetin

Zea mays quercetin is a flavonoid compound derived from its leaves.

This bioactive compound has been extensively studied for its potential health benefits, including antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Zea mays quercetin has been shown to exhibit antimicrobial activity, protect against oxidative stress, and have cardioprotective effects.

Its bioavailability is improved when consumed as a supplement or added to food products, making it a promising natural compound for human health applications.

3. Ferulic acid

Zea mays ferulic acid is a naturally occurring compound found in the plant's pericarp and germ.

It has been identified as a potent antioxidant with various biological activities, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Ferulic acid has also been shown to have potential therapeutic benefits, such as protecting against cardiovascular disease and certain types of cancer.

4. Oleanolic acid

Zea mays oleanolic acid is a type of pentacyclic triterpenoid that is extracted from the plant.

It has been studied for its various biological activities, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and anticancer properties. Oleanolic acid has been shown to inhibit the growth of cancer cells, reduce inflammation, and protect against oxidative damage.

Its potential therapeutic applications include treating a range of diseases, including cardiovascular disease and diabetes.

5. Saponins

Zea mays saponins is a type of natural compound found in the plant's leaves and other parts.

These compounds are known for their ability to reduce blood pressure, lower cholesterol levels, and have anti-inflammatory properties.

Zea mays saponins have been studied for their potential health benefits, including reducing the risk of cardiovascular disease, diabetes, and certain types of cancer.

6. Zeaxanthin

Zea mays zeaxanthin is a carotenoid pigment found in the endosperm of certain varieties of this grain.

This yellow-orange pigment plays a crucial role in protecting the eyes and skin from damage caused by UV light and blue light emitted from digital devices.

Zeaxanthin has been shown to reduce the risk of age-related macular degeneration, cataracts, and other eye diseases, making it an important component of many nutritional supplements and functional foods.

7. Lutein

Zea mays lutein is a naturally occurring carotenoid pigment that is responsible for the yellow or golden color of the plant.

It is found in high concentrations in the kernels and cob of the corn plant, and is also present in smaller amounts in other parts of the plant such as the leaves and husks.

Lutein has been shown to have several health benefits, including protecting against age-related macular degeneration and cataracts.

8. Beta-caryophyllene

Zea mays beta-caryophyllene is a sesquiterpene compound that is naturally present in certain varieties of corn.

It has been found to possess various biological activities, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Additionally, beta-caryophyllene has been shown to have a potential role in the prevention and treatment of certain diseases, such as cancer, diabetes, and neurological disorders.

9. Alpha-linolenic acid (ala)

Zea mays alpha-linolenic acid (ALA) is a type of omega-3 fatty acid found in certain varieties of maize.

ALA is an essential fatty acid that plays a crucial role in heart health by reducing inflammation and improving lipid profiles.

It is also important for brain function, eye health, and may help to prevent chronic diseases such as cardiovascular disease and cancer.

10. Pinoresinol

Zea mays pinoresinol is a lignan compound found in its pericarp and germ.

This phytochemical has been extensively studied for its potential health benefits, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. Pinoresinol has been linked to improved glucose metabolism, reduced cholesterol levels, and protection against certain diseases.

Additionally, it may have applications in the development of new pharmaceuticals and food additives.
