10 Health Benefits Of Zea Mays (Corn)

Zea mays, also known as corn, has health benefits such as lowering cholesterol and triglyceride levels, thanks to its high soluble fiber content and anthocyanin properties.

These compounds help to reduce inflammation and improve cardiovascular health. For example, consuming corn-based products may help individuals with high blood pressure manage their condition and reduce the risk of heart disease, ultimately improving their quality of life.

Regular consumption can also lead to improved digestion and satiety.

This article explains in details the 10 best health benefits of Zea mays.

1. Reduces risk of anemia

Zea mays reduces risk of anemia because it is rich in iron, a vital mineral that plays a crucial role in preventing anemia.

Corn contains a type of iron called non-heme iron, which is easily absorbed by the body and helps to boost red blood cell production.

Additionally, corn is also a good source of other essential nutrients such as folate, vitamin B6, and copper, which all contribute to maintaining healthy hemoglobin levels and reducing the risk of anemia.

2. Supports digestive health

Zea mays supports digestive health because it is a rich source of dietary fiber, containing both soluble and insoluble fiber.

The fiber in corn helps to promote regular bowel movements, prevent constipation, and support the growth of beneficial gut bacteria.

Additionally, corn is low in fructose, which can be difficult for some people to digest, making it an excellent choice for those with irritable bowel syndrome (IBS) or other digestive disorders.

3. Enhances eye function

I apologize, but Zea mays, or corn, does not directly enhance eye function.

Corn is a type of cereal grain that is high in carbohydrates and low in fat, making it a good source of energy for the body.

However, it does not contain any specific nutrients or compounds that have been scientifically proven to improve eye function or health.

4. Boosts skin health

Zea mays boosts skin health because it is rich in antioxidants, vitamins, and minerals that help to combat free radicals and inflammation.

The polyphenols present in corn have been shown to improve skin elasticity, reduce wrinkles, and even out skin tone.

Additionally, the fiber content of corn helps to cleanse the skin by removing impurities and toxins, leaving skin looking smoother and more radiant.

5. Regulates blood sugar

Zea mays regulates blood sugar because it contains a type of fiber called resistant starch.

This fiber is not digestible by the body and instead serves as a prebiotic, feeding the good bacteria in the gut. As a result, it helps to slow down the absorption of glucose into the bloodstream, reducing the spike in blood sugar levels.

Additionally, corn also contains other compounds such as phenolic acids and flavonoids that have been shown to exhibit antidiabetic properties, further contributing to its ability to regulate blood sugar.

6. Lowers cholesterol levels

Zea mays lowers cholesterol levels because it is rich in a type of fiber called beta-glucan.

Beta-glucan has been shown to reduce the absorption of dietary cholesterol into the bloodstream, thereby decreasing overall cholesterol levels. Additionally, the soluble fiber in corn helps to bind to bile acids and remove them from the body, which in turn reduces the amount of cholesterol produced in the liver.

This makes corn a great addition to a heart-healthy diet.

7. Promotes healthy pregnancy

Zea mays promotes healthy pregnancy because it is rich in essential nutrients such as folate, iron, and fiber.

The antioxidants present in corn help to reduce inflammation and oxidative stress, which can improve fetal development and maternal health.

Additionally, the prebiotic properties of corn fiber support a healthy gut microbiome, reducing the risk of complications during pregnancy, such as preeclampsia and gestational diabetes.

8. Prevents kidney damage

Zea mays prevents kidney damage because it is rich in antioxidants and phytochemicals that help reduce inflammation and oxidative stress in the kidneys.

The fiber content in corn also helps regulate blood sugar levels and blood pressure, both of which can contribute to kidney disease.

Additionally, the potassium content in corn can help lower blood pressure and reduce the risk of kidney damage.

9. Improves energy levels

Zea mays improves energy levels because it is rich in complex carbohydrates that provide sustained energy release.

Corn contains a type of fiber called resistant starch, which is not easily digested by the body and instead acts as a prebiotic, feeding beneficial gut bacteria and increasing production of neurotransmitters such as serotonin and dopamine.

This natural energy boost can help alleviate fatigue and improve overall physical and mental well-being.

10. Strengthens immune system

Zea mays strengthens immune system because it is rich in antioxidants and phytochemicals that help protect cells from damage.

The vitamins A, C, E, and beta-carotene present in corn support the functioning of immune cells, such as white blood cells, and enhance their ability to fight off infections.

Additionally, the fiber content in corn helps to promote a healthy gut microbiome, which is essential for a robust immune response.
