Mentha pascuorum

Mentha pascuorum, commonly known as sheeps parsley or wild mint, is a perennial herb belonging to the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is used in traditional medicine for its numerous health benefits, including its ability to aid in digestion, reduce inflammation, and alleviate symptoms of respiratory issues such as bronchitis and coughs.

The therapeutic actions of Mentha pascuorum can be attributed to its antispasmodic, expectorant, and anti-inflammatory properties, which also contribute to its use in treating gastrointestinal issues and as a natural breath freshener.

The bioactive constituents of Mentha pascuorum include menthone, menthyl acetate, and limonene, which are responsible for its medicinal properties and aromatic flavor.
