Sacoglottis gabonensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sacoglottis gabonenensis has woody stems with simple, alternate leaves that feature entire margins and prominent pinnate venation. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in axillary clusters, often appearing in shades of pale yellow or cream. These blooms are followed by the development of fleshy, globose fruits that contain several seeds. The overall architecture of the plant is characterized by a spreading habit that allows it to occupy specific niches within the tropical rainforest ecosystem.
