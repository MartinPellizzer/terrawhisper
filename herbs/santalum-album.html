Sandalwood

Santalum album

Sandalwood (Santalum album) is a small evergreen tree native to India, known for its fragrant heartwood and various traditional medicinal applications, including the treatment of skin disorders and respiratory conditions.

Taxonomy

Sandalwood (Santalum album) is a species belonging to the genus Santalum in the family Santalaceae, classified within the order Santalales, class Eudicots, division Magnoliophyta, and kingdom Plantae.

Morphology

Sandalwood (Santalum album) is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with a straight, slender trunk, dense, dark green foliage, and fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

Distribution

Sandalwood (Santalum album) is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of southern and eastern India, particularly the Western Ghats, and is also found in parts of Australia, Madagascar, and the islands of the Indonesian archipelago.

Traditional Uses

Sandalwood has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its calming properties, respiratory support, and as a natural remedy for skin conditions such as inflammation and acne.

Medicinal Systems

Sandalwood has been traditionally used in Ayurveda, Traditional Chinese Medicine, and Indigenous Australian medicine for its aromatic, anti-inflammatory, and calming properties.

Phytochemistry

Sandalwood (Santalum album) contains a variety of bioactive compounds, including sesquiterpenes, monoterpenes, phenolic compounds, and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties and therapeutic applications in traditional herbal medicine.

Pharmacology

Sandalwood exhibits anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and sedative effects, making it effective in treating conditions such as rheumatism, headaches, and insomnia.

Botanical Identification

Sandalwood is scientifically known as Santalum album and belongs to the family Santalaceae. It is also called Chandan in India and is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. The plant has a deep root system, elliptical leaves, and small yellow flowers. It is often referred to by various regional names in traditional herbal texts.

Preparation Methods

Extract: Used for respiratory and digestive disorders due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

Oil infusion: Applied topically for skin conditions and muscle pain due to its soothing and healing properties.

Essential oil: Inhaling or applying for respiratory issues and mood enhancement due to its aromatic and calming properties.

Safety Profile

Sandalwood is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause skin irritation in some individuals. Prolonged use or high doses may lead to adverse effects, and it should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
