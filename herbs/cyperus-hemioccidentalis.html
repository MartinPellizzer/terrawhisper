What is Cyperus hemioccidentalis?

Cyperus hemioccidentalis is a species of sedge within the family Cyperaceae.

The inflorescence is a terminal spike of 3-15 cm, with spikelets bearing 3-5 flowers in a whorl, and the fruit is a trigonous nut 3-4 mm long and 2-3 mm wide.

Cyperus hemioccidentalis is native to the Americas, specifically in the tropical and subtropical regions of Central and South America.
