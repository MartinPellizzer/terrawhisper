Celastrus stylosus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Celastrus stylosus has opposite, simple leaves that are elliptical to lanceolate in shape, featuring entire margins and acute to acuminate apices. The leaf texture is typically coriaceous, providing a sturdy feel to the foliage. Its woody stems exhibit a branching pattern that supports small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters. The reproductive structures include distinctive styles that are characteristic of the species name. Small, fleshy fruits eventually develop from these flowers to aid in seed dispersal.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Celastrus stylosus can cause severe gastrointestinal distress including vomiting, diarrhea, and intense abdominal pain if ingested. The plant contains various toxic alkaloids that irritate the mucous membranes of the digestive tract. Skin contact with the plant material may also trigger localized irritation or allergic dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Symptoms of poisoning often manifest shortly after consumption due to the rapid absorption of its chemical constituents. Proper identification is essential to avoid accidental exposure to these harmful biological compounds.
