Melastoma sanguineum has opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves characterized by prominent longitudinal veins that curve toward the leaf apex, along with bright red, five-petaled flowers and woody stems. Its reproductive structures feature long, protruding stamens that are a defining feature of the Melastomataceae family. The floral color provides a striking contrast against the deep green of the vegetative growth.
