Caryopteris aureoglandulosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Caryopteris aureoglandulosa has opposite, simple, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically 2 to 5 centimeters long and characterized by a densely pubescent surface covered in fine hairs. The plant exhibits a woody, shrubby growth habit with slender, branching stems that can reach various heights depending on the environment. Its inflorescence consists of terminal panicles bearing small, tubular flowers that often display subtle color variations. The foliage often maintains a textured appearance due to the presence of trichomes on both the upper and lower leaf surfaces. These morphological traits distinguish it from other species within the Verbenaceae family.
