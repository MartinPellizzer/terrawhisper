Caryopteris aureoglandulosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Caryopteris aureoglandulosa?

Caryopteris aureoglanduosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. It is specifically categorized under the genus Caryopteris, with its unique identification finalized at the species level as aureoglanduosa.

      What are the herbal preparations of Caryopteris aureoglandulosa?

      Caryopteris aureoglandulosa is prepared primarily as an aqueous infusion, a decoction, or a tincture derived from its dried leaves and flowering tops. These botanical extracts are often used in traditional medicine to harness the plant's concentrated phytochemical properties. Infusions are typically made by steeping the plant material in hot water to extract delicate volatile oils. For more robust components, a decoction involves boiling the plant parts in water for an extended period. Tinctures utilize ethanol as a solvent to ensure a longer shelf life and higher potency of the active compounds.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Caryopteris aureoglanduosa can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or mild gastrointestinal distress if ingested by humans or animals. While the plant is generally considered non-toxic, direct contact with its foliage may trigger redness or itching in individuals with sensitive skin. Some people might experience localized swelling or a rash after touching the stems or leaves. There is also a possibility of digestive upset if any part of the plant is consumed accidentally. Monitoring for these reactions is recommended when handling the plant in a garden setting.
