What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hydrilla verticillata has slender, cylindrical stems that can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters and feature whorls of small, lanceolate leaves arranged in multiples of four. These leaves typically measure between 1 and 3 centimeters in length and possess serrated margins along their edges. The plant lacks true roots, instead utilizing adventitious roots that emerge from the stem nodes to anchor itself in the substrate. Small, white, four-petaled flowers may emerge above the water surface during reproductive stages. This submerged aquatic species is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to form dense, tangled mats.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?
