Vangueriopsis rubiginosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Vangueriopsis rubiginosa?

Vangueriopsis rubiginosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is situated within the order Gentianaales. It is a representative of the family Rubiaceae, falling under the specific genus Vangueriopsis, and is identified by its unique species designation, rubiginosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vangueriopsis rubiginosa has small, elliptical to oblong leaves that are densely covered in reddish-brown ferruginous hairs on both the upper and lower surfaces.

These leaves are typically arranged in an opposite pattern along the stems of the woody shrub. The plant features small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the axils of the foliage. The presence of these distinct rusty scales or hairs is a key diagnostic feature for identifying this specific species.

Its overall structure reflects its adaptation to tropical forest environments where such pubescence can play a role in moisture retention.
