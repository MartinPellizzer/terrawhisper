Vangueriopsis rubiginosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Vangueriopsis rubiginosa?

Vangueriopsis rubiginosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is situated within the order Gentianaales. It is a representative of the family Rubiaceae, falling under the specific genus Vangueriopsis, and is identified by its unique species designation, rubiginosa.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Vangueriopsis rubiginosa can cause localized skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and potential gastrointestinal distress if ingested. The specific chemical compounds within the plant may trigger redness or itching upon direct contact with sensitive dermal tissues. Some individuals might experience swelling or an inflammatory response if the sap touches the skin. Ingestion of unknown quantities could lead to nausea or more severe digestive complications. Due to the lack of extensive clinical studies, the full spectrum of toxicity remains uncertain.
