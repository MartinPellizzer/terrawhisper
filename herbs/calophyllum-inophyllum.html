Calophyllum inophyllum

What's the taxonomical classification of Calophyllum inophyllum?

Calophyllum inophyllum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further classified into the subclass Magnoliidae and placed in the order Malpighiales. The plant is a member of the family Calophyllaceae, falling under the genus Calophyllum, and is specifically identified by the species name inophyllum.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Calophyllum inophyllum contains bioactive medicinal compounds located primarily in its seeds, bark, leaves, and resin. The oil extracted from the seeds is the most widely utilized component for treating skin conditions, inflammation, and wound healing. Its bark is often decocted to create infusions used to manage digestive issues or fever. Additionally, the leaves and resin provide secondary therapeutic benefits through their antimicrobial and antioxidant properties. These various parts are integrated into traditional medicine systems to address a range of topical and internal ailments.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse profile of bioactive secondary metabolites including flavonoids, terpenoids, alkaloids, coumarins, phenolics, and steroids.

These chemical constituents are distributed throughout the leaves, bark, and fruit of the species. The presence of phenolics and flavonoids contributes significant antioxidant properties to the plant's medicinal extracts. Furthermore, the terpenoids and steroids often provide anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities essential to its traditional uses.

Alkaloids and coumarins also play critical roles in the complex pharmacological interactions of the plant.

  • Flavonoids
  • Terpenoids
  • Alkaloids
  • Coumarins
  • Phenolics
  • Steroids

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Calophyllum inophyllum targets several key inflammatory enzymes including cyclooxygenase-2, lipoxygenase, and various pro-inflammatory cytokines to exert its medicinal effects. The plant's bioactive xanthones and coumarins work by inhibiting the expression of inducible nitric oxide synthase, which reduces oxidative stress in tissues. These secondary metabolites also interact with the NF-kappaB signaling pathway to suppress the production of inflammatory mediators. Furthermore, certain compounds within the plant exhibit inhibitory activity against protein kinases that regulate cell growth and survival. This multi-targeted approach allows the plant to provide potent anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial therapeutic benefits.

What are the therapeutic actions of Calophyllum inophyllum?

Calophyllum inophyllum has potent anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and wound-healing properties primarily derived from its bioactive phytochemicals like coumarins and terpenoids. The oil extracted from its seeds is frequently applied topically to soothe skin irritations, reduce swelling, and promote the regeneration of damaged tissue. These compounds work by inhibiting specific inflammatory mediators in the body, making the plant effective against various dermatological conditions. Additionally, its antibacterial characteristics help prevent infections in open wounds or skin lesions. Research suggests that these therapeutic actions are driven by the synergistic effect of its complex chemical constituents.

What common ailments are treated using this plant?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, inflammation, and various skin diseases.

Its extracts possess potent antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that promote healing in damaged tissues. Local communities often apply the plant to open sores and abrasions to prevent bacterial growth. It is also effective in reducing swelling and discomfort associated with dermatological conditions.

By targeting underlying irritation, the plant serves as a versatile remedy for maintaining skin health.

The main common ailment treated using this plant are shown in the list below.

  • Skin infections
  • Wounds
  • Inflammation
  • Skin diseases

What are the herbal preparations of Calophyllum inophyllum?

Calophyllum inophyllum is prepared as a medicinal oil extracted from its seeds, a decoction made from its bark, and various topical ointments or poultices derived from its leaves. The seed oil, often called tamanu oil, serves as the primary liquid medium for treating skin conditions and inflammation. Bark decoctions are frequently consumed orally to address internal issues like digestive ailments or urinary tract infections. Leaf extracts can be applied directly to wounds or processed into topical salves to promote healing. These diverse preparations allow the plant's bioactive compounds to be utilized for both systemic and localized therapeutic purposes.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Calophyllum inophyllum can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or localized dermatitis when its extracts are applied topically to sensitive areas. While many studies focus on its medicinal properties, the concentration of bioactive compounds may trigger redness or itching in certain individuals. Some users might experience mild gastrointestinal distress if the plant parts are ingested without proper processing or dosage control. Because clinical data on human toxicity remains limited, there is a risk of unforeseen hypersensitivity during long-term use. It is essential to perform a patch test before applying any derived oils or ointments to the body.
