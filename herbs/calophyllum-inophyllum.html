Calophyllum inophyllum

What's the taxonomical classification of Calophyllum inophyllum?

Calophyllum inophyllum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further classified into the subclass Magnoliidae and placed in the order Malpighiales. The plant is a member of the family Calophyllaceae, falling under the genus Calophyllum, and is specifically identified by the species name inophyllum.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Calophyllum inophyllum contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including coumarins, xanthones, triterpenoids, and various fatty acids. These chemical constituents contribute to the plant's significant pharmacological potential in treating inflammatory and oxidative stress conditions. Specifically, the presence of xanthones provides potent antioxidant and antimicrobial properties that are useful in traditional medicine. Research also suggests that its triterpenoid compounds may exhibit anti-tumor and anti-inflammatory activities. These diverse medicinal compounds make the plant a subject of interest for developing new therapeutic agents.

What are the therapeutic actions of Calophyllum inophyllum?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various inflammatory skin conditions, fungal infections, bacterial ailments, and wound-related issues. Its medicinal properties are frequently applied to combat eczema, psoriasis, and other dermatological irritations. The bioactive compounds within the plant also serve as effective agents against topical infections and minor cuts. Many traditional practices utilize its extracts to soothe skin inflammation and promote the healing of damaged tissue. These diverse therapeutic applications make it a significant resource in ethnobotanical medicine.
