What's the taxonomical classification of Dendrocalamus hamiltonii?

Dendrocalamus hamiltonii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Poales. Furthermore, this plant is a member of the family Poaceae, specifically categorized within the genus Dendrocalamus, and is identified by its unique species name, hamiltonii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dendrocalamus hamiltonii has a robust, clumping growth habit characterized by large, woody culms that can reach heights of up to 25 meters and diameters exceeding 10 centimeters. Each leaf consists of a long, slender petiole supporting an elliptical or lanceolate blade with prominent parallel venation. The branching pattern is typically concentrated in the upper portions of the culms, creating a dense, overlapping canopy.
