Artemisia atrovirens

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Artemisia atrovirens has a perennial growth habit characterized by erect, woody stems and deeply pinnatifid leaves that exhibit a distinct dark green to grayish-green coloration. The foliage is highly dissected, often featuring narrow, linear lobes that give the plant a feathery appearance. Its leaves are typically covered in fine hairs, which contributes to a subtle pubescence across the surface. During the flowering season, the plant produces small, inconspicuous flower heads arranged in dense, terminal clusters. These inflorescences are characteristic of the genus and are often surrounded by specialized bracts.

      What are the herbal preparations of Artemisia atrovirens?

      Artemisia atrovirens is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical tincture to extract its active phytochemical constituents.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Artemisia atrovirens can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested or handled improperly. Contact with the plant's foliage may lead to redness, itching, or localized swelling in sensitive individuals. Some users might experience nausea or stomach cramping when consuming preparations derived from its components. Because the specific toxicity levels are not fully documented in clinical studies, caution is necessary to avoid unpredictable reactions. It is advisable to perform a patch test before any significant exposure to the plant.
