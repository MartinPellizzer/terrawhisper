Hedysarum sikkimense

What's the taxonomical classification of Hedysarum sikkimense?

Hedysarum sikkimense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the classification of land plants, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the evolutionary lineage further, it falls under the order Fabales and is a member of the family Fabaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by its placement within the genus Hedysarum, with its unique species designation being sikkimense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hedysarum sikkimense has pinnately compound leaves with several pairs of small, ovate to elliptical leaflets and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense axillary racemes.

The plant typically exhibits a woody, climbing, or scrambling growth habit characterized by slender stems. The leaflets often feature entire margins and a slightly pubescent texture on their surfaces. Its reproductive structures consist of small, pea-like legumes that develop from the flowers into flattened pods.

These pods eventually split to release seeds, which are essential for the plant's dispersal within its natural habitat.
