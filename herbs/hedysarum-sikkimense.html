Hedysarum sikkimense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hedysarum sikkimense has pinnately compound leaves with several pairs of small, ovate to elliptical leaflets and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense axillary racemes. The plant typically exhibits a woody, climbing, or scrambling growth habit characterized by slender stems. The leaflets often feature entire margins and a slightly pubescent texture on their surfaces. Its reproductive structures consist of small, pea-like legumes that develop from the flowers into flattened pods. These pods eventually split to release seeds, which are essential for the plant's dispersal within its natural habitat.
