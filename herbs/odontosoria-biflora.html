Odontosoria biflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Odontosoria biflora?

Odontosoria biflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae, falling under the order Polypodiales. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the family Lindsaeaceae and the genus Odontosoria, ultimately identifying it as the specific species biflora.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Odontosoria biflora contains bioactive phytochemicals including triterpenoids, flavonoids, and various polyphenolic compounds that contribute to its medicinal properties. These chemical constituents are believed to provide significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities when utilized in traditional treatments. Research into the plant's chemical profile suggests that its secondary metabolites may assist in regulating cellular responses to oxidative stress. The specific concentration of these compounds varies depending on the plant's ecological environment and the part of the plant harvested. Such medicinal potential makes it a subject of interest for pharmacological studies focused on natural therapeutic agents.

What are the therapeutic actions of Odontosoria biflora?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including skin diseases, fever, and digestive issues. Traditional practitioners often apply preparations of the plant to manage dermatological conditions such as eczema or sores. It is also frequently utilized in folk medicine to reduce high body temperatures associated with infectious illnesses. Some studies suggest its components may help alleviate stomach pains and other gastrointestinal discomforts. These medicinal applications rely on the specific bioactive compounds found within its leaves and stems.
