Fenugreek (Trigonella foenum-graecum) Compounds

Fenugreek contains several primary active compounds, including flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties. Phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory effects. Coumarins are known for their anticoagulant properties. Tannins and glycosides support digestive health. Saponins and terpenoids offer additional therapeutic benefits. Essential oils, alkaloids, and anthocyanins also play roles in its medicinal properties.

Fenugreek (Trigonella foenum-graecum) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Fenugreek (Trigonella foenum-graecum). They contribute to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and hypoglycemic effects. These compounds support the plant's traditional use in managing diabetes and digestive health.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Fenugreek (Trigonella foenum-graecum). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting its use in traditional herbal medicine for digestive and metabolic health.

3. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Fenugreek (Trigonella foenum-graecum). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects. Coumarin also plays a role in the plant's use for digestive support and hormonal balance. Its presence enhances the therapeutic potential of Fenugreek in herbal medicine.

4. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Fenugreek (Trigonella foenum-graecum). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins in Fenugreek are primarily found in the seeds and are used in traditional medicine for digestive and skin conditions.

5. Glycoside

Fenugreek (Trigonella foenum-graecum) contains the glycoside trigonelline, which contributes to its medicinal properties. This compound is known for its potential role in regulating blood sugar and supporting digestive health. Trigonelline is also associated with antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects in traditional herbal medicine.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Fenugreek (Trigonella foenum-graecum). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and cholesterol-lowering effects. Saponins are also responsible for the characteristic foaming action when the plant is prepared as a decoction.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Fenugreek (Trigonella foenum-graecum). They contribute to the plant's medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. These compounds are often responsible for the plant's therapeutic benefits in herbal medicine.

8. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound of Fenugreek (Trigonella foenum-graecum). It contains volatile compounds like anethole and limonene, which contribute to its aromatic properties and potential therapeutic effects, including anti-inflammatory and digestive benefits.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloids in Fenugreek (Trigonella foenum-graecum) are bioactive compounds that contribute to its medicinal properties. They have been shown to support hormonal balance and digestive health. These alkaloids are often studied for their potential therapeutic effects in herbal medicine. Their presence enhances the plant's traditional use in various therapeutic applications.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Fenugreek (Trigonella foenum-graecum). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports the plant's traditional use in promoting digestive health and managing blood sugar levels.
