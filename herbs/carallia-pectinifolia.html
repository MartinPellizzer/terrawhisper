Carallia pectinifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Carallia pectinifolia?

Caralliia pectinifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed under the order Malpighiales. This plant is further categorized into the family Rhizophoraceae, falling under the genus Carailliia, and is specifically identified by its species name, pectinifolia.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Carallia pectinifolia contains a diverse profile of secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, tannins, and saponins that contribute to its pharmacological potential. These bioactive compounds work synergistically to provide antioxidant and antimicrobial properties within the plant tissues. Research suggests that the specific flavonoid concentrations may assist in reducing oxidative stress in cellular models. Additionally, the presence of tannins contributes to the plant's astringent qualities, which are often studied for wound healing applications. The complex chemical makeup makes it a subject of interest for developing new phytotherapeutic agents.

What are the therapeutic actions of Carallia pectinifolia?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat ailments such as skin infections, digestive issues, and various inflammatory conditions. Its medicinal properties are often applied topically to soothe irritations or combat fungal growth on the surface of the body. Some traditional practices involve consuming preparations of the plant to alleviate internal discomfort or stomach distress. The bioactive compounds within the plant serve as the primary agents for addressing these diverse health concerns. Researchers continue to study these specific chemical components to better understand their therapeutic potential.
