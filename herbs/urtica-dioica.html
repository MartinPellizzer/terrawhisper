Urtica Dioica Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Urtica dioica, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is known for its stinging hairs that cause a skin irritation when touched.

Side Effects of urtica dioica

Urtica dioica triggers allergic reactions due to the presence of histamine and other allergens in its stinging hairs, which can cause symptoms like sneezing, runny nose, and in severe cases, anaphylaxis.

Urtica dioica causes skin irritation because its hairs release chemicals such as acetylcholine and serotonin when touched, leading to a burning or stinging sensation. Urtica dioica induces skin rashes as the body's immune response to the irritants results in inflammation and redness on the skin.

Urtica dioica induces skin itching because the release of histamine and other inflammatory mediators stimulates nerve endings, triggering an itch response.
