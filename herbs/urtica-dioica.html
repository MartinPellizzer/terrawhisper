Stinging Nettle (Urtica dioica)

Stinging Nettle (Urtica dioica) is a member of the Urticaceae family, native to Europe, Asia, and North America. Traditionally, its leaves, stems, and shoots have been used for decoctions, poultices, and infusions.

This herb is particularly valued for its diuretic, anti-inflammatory, and stimulant actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, traditional chinese medicine, and mediterranean herbal traditions.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically reaches a height of 1 to 2 meters.
Leaves
Lanceolate leaves with serrated margins, upper surface dull green, lower surface pale green with prominent stomatal bands.
Flowers
Inflorescence consists of small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, with actinomorphic symmetry, each flower having four sepals and four petals, and staminodes present in two whorls.
Stems
Climbing, hollow, cylindrical, ridged, pubescent, with opposite branching, and possessing stinging hairs along the surface.
