What's the taxonomical classification of Trapa manshurica?

Trapa manshurica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Myrtales and is a member of the family Lythraceae. Finally, the plant is placed within the genus Trapa, specifically identified by the species name manshuriica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Trapa manshurica has floating, rosette-shaped leaves that are typically kidney-shaped or heart-shaped with deeply notched bases and serrated edges. These leaves can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter and feature a leathery texture designed for aquatic life. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that emerge from the center of the leaf rosettes. Its most distinctive feature is the woody, nut-like fruit which is encased in a hard shell adorned with sharp, defensive spines. These fruits often contain multiple seeds and serve as the primary method for the species' dispersal in freshwater habitats.
