What's the taxonomical classification of Anaphalis souliei?

Anaphalis souliei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. This plant is a representative of the family Asteraceae, a large group of flowering plants, and is specifically categorized under the genus Anaphalis, with the unique species designation souliei.

This multifaceted biochemical approach allows the plant to function as a potent anti-inflammatory and analgesic agent in traditional medicine.

      Anaphalis souliei is prepared as a decoction, a poultice, or an infusion depending on the specific ailment being treated.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Anaphalis souliei can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and digestive distress if consumed or applied improperly. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, many plants in the Asteraceae family contain sesquiterpene lactones which are known allergens. These compounds often trigger contact dermatitis or localized redness when they touch sensitive skin. Ingestion of unidentified wild plants may also lead to nausea or stomach cramps in sensitive individuals. It is essential to consult a medical professional before using this species for any medicinal purpose.
