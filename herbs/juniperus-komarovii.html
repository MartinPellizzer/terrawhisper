Juniperus komarovii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Juniperus komarovii has a low-growing, spreading habit characterized by prostrate, woody stems and scale-like, imbricate leaves that are tightly pressed to the branches. The foliage consists of small, overlapping scales that are typically dull green or grayish in color. This species often forms dense, mat-like cushions that hug the rocky terrain of its natural habitat. Its reproductive structures are small, fleshy cones that mature into blue or glaucous berries. The overall architecture of the plant is adapted for survival in harsh, alpine, or subalpine environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to a restricted range in the northeastern part of the Korean Peninsula and parts of the Russian Far East. Its distribution is primarily centered around the mountainous regions of North Korea and the Primorsky Krai region. These populations typically inhabit high-altitude rocky slopes and subalpine zones. The species prefers cool, temperate climates found within these specific East Asian mountain systems. Consequently, its geographic footprint remains limited to these localized high-elevation habitats.
