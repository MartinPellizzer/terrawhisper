Alangium chinense

What's the taxonomical classification of Alangium chinense?

Aliangium chinense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Cornales and is a member of the family Cornaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific classification within the genus Aliangium and the species chinense.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Alangium chinense contains high concentrations of essential oils composed of limonene, linalool, and various terpene alcohols that provide its therapeutic properties. These volatile compounds exhibit significant antimicrobial, antifungal, and anti-inflammatory effects when applied topically or ingested in controlled amounts. The presence of specific phenolic compounds further contributes to its antioxidant capacity, helping to neutralize free radicals in the body. Traditional medicinal practices often utilize these chemical constituents to treat digestive issues and skin irritations. Research suggests that the synergistic interaction of these molecules supports its use in various holistic healing applications.

What are the therapeutic actions of Alangium chinense?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat digestive disorders, respiratory infections, skin ailments, and inflammatory conditions. It is frequently utilized in traditional medicine to alleviate symptoms of indigestion, bloating, and stomach cramps. The aromatic properties of the plant are often applied to soothe coughs, colds, and congestion. Additionally, its antimicrobial qualities help manage various skin infections and topical irritations. These therapeutic applications rely on the plant's natural bioactive compounds to promote healing.
