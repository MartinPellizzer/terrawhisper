Petunia violacea

What's the taxonomical classification of Petunia violacea?

Petunia violacea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Solanales. This plant is further classified under the family Solanaceae, which places it within the genus Petunia, ultimately identifying its specific biological designation as the species violacea.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various skin infections, respiratory ailments, and digestive issues. Its medicinal properties are often applied topically to soothe inflammation and manage fungal outbreaks. When prepared in specific traditional formulas, it can help alleviate symptoms of persistent coughing or congestion. Some practitioners also utilize its extracts to assist with gastrointestinal discomfort. The effectiveness of these treatments relies heavily on the concentration of active compounds within the leaves.
