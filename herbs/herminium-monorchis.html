Herminium monorchis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Herminium monorchis has a small, creeping rhizome and slender, erect stems that reach heights of up to 15 centimeters. The leaves are typically few in number, narrow, and lanceolate in shape, often appearing near the base of the stem. Its flowers are minute and white or pale greenish, characterized by a single, prominent lip that distinguishes the species. These tiny blossoms are arranged in a loose, terminal spike that emerges during the late spring or early summer. The overall structure is delicate and inconspicuous, reflecting its specialized habitat in moist, shaded environments.
