Catalpa fargesii

What's the taxonomical classification of Catalpa fargesii?

Catalpa fargesii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, the plant is placed in the order Lamiales and belongs to the family Bignoniaceae. Finally, it is defined by its specific genus, Catalpa, and its unique species designation, fargesii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Catalpa fargesii has large, broadly ovate to cordate leaves that measure up to 25 centimeters in length and possess entire margins with prominent, pinnate venation.

These leaves are arranged in an opposite pattern along the woody, grayish-brown branches of the tree. The plant produces showy, white, trumpet-shaped flowers that emerge from terminal panicles during the summer months. Following the flowering stage, long, cylindrical, pendulous seed pods develop that can reach considerable lengths.

The overall structure of the tree is characterized by a spreading canopy and a sturdy trunk suitable for temperate environments.
