What's the taxonomical classification of Clematis rehderiana?

Clematis rehderiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Ranunculales and into the family Ranunculaceae. Finally, it is placed within the genus Clematis, specifically identified by its species name, rehderiana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clematis rehderiana has woody, climbing stems that produce large, bell-shaped, creamy-white to pale yellow flowers with four distinct tepals. The foliage consists of opposite, trifoliate leaves featuring ovate to lanceolate leaflets with entire or slightly wavy margins. Each flower is typically accompanied by several prominent, pale stamens that extend from the center of the bloom. The plant develops small, hairy achenes that form into decorative, feathery seed heads once the flowering period concludes. These structural features allow the vine to scale vertical surfaces effectively in its natural habitat.
