This herb also leads to stomach upset, often accompanied by discomfort, bloating, or an acidic taste in the mouth, which can further exacerbate nausea. In addition to gastrointestinal effects, Grindelia camporum causes skin irritation, resulting in redness, itching, or a burning sensation upon contact with the skin.

Eye irritation is another potential side effect, with symptoms such as redness, watering, or a gritty feeling in the eyes, especially if the herb comes into contact with the face. Furthermore, the herb triggers sneezing and throat irritation, which may lead to a dry throat or even coughing, making it unsuitable for individuals with respiratory sensitivities.
