What is Athyrium flabellulatum?

Athyrium flabellulatum is a fern species within the family Aspleniaceae.

It is a rhizomatous perennial with fronds that are bipinnate and have a pinnatifid rachis, with pinnae being 1-2 pairs of pinnules. The fronds typically range in size from 20-70 cm in length and 10-20 cm in width. The plant reproduces via spores produced on the underside of the fronds in a sori pattern, with each sporangium releasing 64 spores.

Athyrium flabellulatum is native to China and is often found in shaded, humid areas at elevations ranging from 2,500 to 4,500 meters above sea level.
