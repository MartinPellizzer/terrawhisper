St. John'S Wort (Hypericum perforatum) Compounds

St. John's Wort contains several primary compounds, including flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and hypericin, a unique terpenoid with potential antidepressant effects. Phenolic acids and tannins contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial actions. Other compounds like glycosides and essential oils also play roles in its therapeutic effects.

St. John'S Wort (Hypericum perforatum) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of compounds found in St. John's Wort, a medicinal plant used traditionally for its antidepressant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds contribute to the plant's therapeutic effects by modulating neurotransmitter activity and reducing oxidative stress.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in St. John's Wort (Hypericum perforatum). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound supports the herb's traditional use in treating mild depression and anxiety. It works synergistically with other compounds in the plant to enhance its therapeutic effects.

3. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in St. John's Wort. They contribute to the plant's antidepressant and anti-inflammatory effects. These compounds, including hyperforin and hypericin, are responsible for many of the therapeutic properties attributed to St. John's Wort in herbal medicine.

4. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in St. John's Wort (Hypericum perforatum). It contributes to the plant's antidepressant and anti-inflammatory effects. Glycosides in St. John's Wort are often broken down into bioactive flavonoids and hypericin. These compounds work synergistically to support mood regulation and reduce inflammation.

5. Tannin

Tannin is a compound found in St. John's Wort (Hypericum perforatum). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support wound healing by reducing inflammation and promoting tissue repair. Tannins also help preserve the plant's active components during storage.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a compound found in St. John's Wort (Hypericum perforatum). It contributes to the plant's mild sedative and antispasmodic properties. Coumarin also acts as a natural sunscreen, protecting the plant from UV damage. Its presence supports the plant's traditional use in treating mild depression and anxiety.

7. Essential oil

The essential oil of St. John's Wort contains compounds like hypericin and hyperforin, which are believed to contribute to its antidepressant and anti-inflammatory effects. These compounds interact with neurotransmitters and may help regulate mood, making the essential oil a key component in the plant's traditional and modern medicinal use.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in St. John's Wort (Hypericum perforatum). It contributes to the plant's bitter taste and may have mild antimicrobial properties. While not the primary active component, saponins may support the overall efficacy of the herb in traditional uses.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloids in St. John's Wort (Hypericum perforatum) are a class of nitrogen-containing compounds with potential therapeutic effects. These alkaloids, such as hypericin and hyperforin, contribute to the plant's antidepressant and anti-inflammatory properties, playing a key role in its traditional and modern use in herbal medicine.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a pigment found in St. John's Wort (Hypericum perforatum). It contributes to the plant's characteristic red and yellow hues. While not the primary active compound, anthocyanin may support overall plant health and has antioxidant properties that could contribute to the plant's medicinal effects.
