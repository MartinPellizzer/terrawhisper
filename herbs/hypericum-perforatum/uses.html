St. John'S Wort (Hypericum perforatum) Uses

St. John's Wort is primarily used to treat depression and mood disorders. It also addresses anxiety, insomnia, and stress-related conditions. Additionally, it supports neurological health by reducing inflammation and neuralgia. Chronic fatigue and seasonal affective disorder are also commonly managed with this herb.

St. John'S Wort (Hypericum perforatum) Monograph

1. Depression

St. John's Wort has been traditionally used to support mental health, particularly in mild to moderate depression. Studies suggest it may increase serotonin and other neurotransmitters, improving mood. However, it can interact with medications, so professional guidance is recommended.

2. Insomnia

St. John's Wort has been traditionally used to support sleep and alleviate insomnia. It is believed to enhance serotonin levels, promoting relaxation and improving sleep quality. However, its effectiveness for insomnia varies, and it should be used cautiously due to potential interactions with other medications.

3. Neurological disorders

St. John's Wort has been traditionally used to support mood regulation and may help with symptoms of mild to moderate depression. It contains compounds like hyperforin and hypericin that influence neurotransmitters, though its efficacy for severe neurological disorders remains less supported by clinical research.

4. Anxiety

St. John's Wort is commonly used to alleviate symptoms of anxiety due to its ability to modulate neurotransmitters like serotonin and dopamine. It may help reduce feelings of restlessness and tension, though it should be used with caution due to potential interactions with other medications.

5. Mood disorders

St. John's Wort has been traditionally used to support mood regulation, particularly in mild to moderate depression. It contains compounds like hypericin and hyperforin that may influence neurotransmitter levels. Clinical studies suggest it can be effective for certain mood disorders, though it should be used with caution due to potential interactions with other medications.

6. Neuralgia

St. John's Wort has been traditionally used to alleviate neuralgia, a type of nerve pain. It is believed to work by reducing inflammation and calming nerve activity. While modern research supports its potential in managing neuropathic pain, it should be used with caution due to possible interactions with other medications.

7. Chronic fatigue

St. John's Wort has been traditionally used to support energy levels and alleviate symptoms of chronic fatigue. It is believed to enhance mood and reduce mental exhaustion through its active compounds, such as hypericin and hyperforin. However, it should be used with caution due to potential interactions with other medications.

8. Seasonal affective disorder

St. John's Wort is used to treat seasonal affective disorder due to its ability to regulate neurotransmitters like serotonin. It is typically taken as a standardized extract, with doses ranging from 300 to 900 mg daily. While effective for some, it may interact with medications and should be used under professional guidance.

9. Neurological inflammation

St. John's Wort has been studied for its potential to reduce neurological inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant compounds. These properties may support brain health by mitigating oxidative stress and inflammatory markers. Research suggests it may aid in conditions like neurodegenerative diseases and mood disorders. However, it should be used with caution due to possible interactions with medications.

10. Stress-related disorders

St. John's Wort has been traditionally used to support mental health, particularly in managing symptoms of stress-related disorders. It is believed to influence neurotransmitters like serotonin, which can help alleviate anxiety and mild depression. Studies suggest it may be effective for mild to moderate cases, though it should be used under professional guidance due to potential interactions with other medications.
