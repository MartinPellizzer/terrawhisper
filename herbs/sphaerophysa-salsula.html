Furthermore, the structural variety of these molecules, such as the specialized isorhamnetin derivative, highlights the plant's phytochemical richness. Detailed analysis of these constituents helps researchers understand the plant's therapeutic potential.

    As discussed in a study, phytochemical studies on the whole herb of Sphaerophysa salsula resulted in the discovery of one new 8-isopentenyl isoflavone derivative named sphaerosin s2 (3-(8-(2-hydroxypropan-2-yl)-3,4-dihydro-2H-furo[2,3-h]chromen-3-yl)-2,6-dimethoxyphenol) (1). This discovery occurred alongside four known 8-isopentenyl isoflavone derivatives labeled 2-5. Specifically, compounds 2, 4 and 5 were isolated for the first time from this species.
