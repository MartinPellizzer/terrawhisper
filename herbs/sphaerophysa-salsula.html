Researchers often focus on these compounds to understand their potential therapeutic properties. Such metabolic diversity highlights the complex chemical makeup inherent to Sphaerophysa salsula.

According to a study published by "Journal of Asian natural products research", two new 9,10-seco-cycloartanes, named sphaerophyside SC (1) and sphaerophyside SD (2), were obtained from the ethanol extract of the seeds of Sphaerophysa salsula. These 2 new compounds were found together with 4 known compounds (3-6). The structures of these compounds were elucidated on the basis of spectral and chemical evidences. Additionally, compounds 3-6 were isolated from the plant for the first time.
