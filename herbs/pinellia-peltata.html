What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pinellia peltata has peltate leaves characterized by a central point where the petiole attaches to the underside of the leaf blade rather than at the margin. These leaves are typically circular or orbicular in shape and possess entire margins. The leaf texture is relatively thin, often appearing somewhat succulent or fleshy depending on the environmental conditions. The venation pattern radiates outward from the central attachment point toward the leaf edges.
