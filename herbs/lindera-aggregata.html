Lindera aggregata

What's the taxonomical classification of Lindera aggregata?

Lindera aggregata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant falls under the order Laurales. It is further categorized into the family Lauraceae, which places it within the genus Lindera, eventually reaching its specific designation as the species aggregata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lindera aggregata has deciduous, lanceolate to ovate leaves that are typically arranged alternately along slender, reddish-brown woody stems. The leaf margins are finely serrated, and the foliage often turns a vibrant red or orange during the autumn months. Small, inconspicuous yellowish flowers emerge in early spring before the leaves fully expand, appearing in small clusters. The plant produces small, bright red drupes that serve as a key identifying feature during the later growing seasons. These berries are held on thin stalks and provide a distinct visual contrast against the green summer canopy.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the southwestern corner of Western Australia, specifically ranging from the coastal areas near Perth to the Esperance region. It typically thrives within the kwongan heathlands and shrublands characteristic of this Mediterranean climate zone. Its distribution is largely confined to sandy or lateritic soils found in these nutrient-poor coastal and inland environments. Populations are often scattered throughout various protected reserves and national parks across the South West Botanical Province. The species remains restricted to this specific corner of the continent due to its specialized ecological requirements.

What are the pharmacological activities of Lindera aggregata?

This plant has anti-inflammatory, anti-tumor, anticancer, antibacterial, and anthelmintic pharmacological activities. Research indicates that its chemical constituents can effectively inhibit the growth of various harmful pathogens and parasitic worms. The plant also demonstrates significant potential in fighting cellular abnormalities by exhibiting both anticancer and anti-tumor properties. Additionally, its ability to reduce inflammation makes it a subject of interest for managing swelling and pain. These diverse biological effects highlight the medicinal importance of Lindera aggregata in various therapeutic contexts.

As discussed in a study, the leaves of Lindera aggregata were investigated to study antibacterial and anti-inflammatory constituents. Through column chromatography, 6 compounds were isolated and identified using spectroscopic methods. These identified compounds include a mixture of 6-Acetyllindenanolide B-1 and B-2(I), dehydrolindestrenolide (II), hydroxylinderstrenolide (III), linderalactone (IV), kameofero (V), and beta-sitosterol (VI). These 6 specific compounds were obtained from the leaves of Lindera aggregata for the first time.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds kaempferol, quercetin, beta-sitosterol, dihydrokaempferol-3-O-L-rhamnoside, and sesquitepenoids. These specific flavonoids and sterols contribute to the overall therapeutic profile of the species. The presence of dihydrokaempferol-3-O-L-rhamnoside provides unique antioxidant properties within the plant's chemical makeup. Additionally, the sesquitepenoids play a significant role in the biological activity of the organic extracts. Together, these diverse phytochemicals work to provide various health-promoting benefits.

As discussed in a study, the leaves of Lindera aggregata contain 8 isolated compounds. These chemical constituents were identified using 1H-NMR and 13C-NMR spectral analysis. The specific compounds identified are quercetin-3-O-L-rhamnoside(1), kaempferol-3-O-L-rhamnoside(2), kaempferol(3), dihydrokaempferol-3-O-L-rhamnoside(4), quercetin(5), quercetin-3-O-alpha-D-arabinofuranoside(6), quercetin-3-O-alpha-D-glucopyranoside(7), and kaempferol-3-O-D-glucopyranoside(8). Compounds 2, 4, and 8 were obtained from Lindera aggregata for the first time.
