Spicebush (Lindera aggregata)

Spicebush (Lindera aggregata) is a member of the Oleaceae family, native to Southeastern United States, Eastern Asia, and China. Traditionally, its leaves, roots, and bark have been used for decoctions, infusions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its bitter, anti-inflammatory, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in japanese kampo medicine, traditional chinese medicine, and native american herbal medicine.
