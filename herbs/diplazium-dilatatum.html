Diplazium dilatatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Diplazium dilatatum?

Diplazium dilatatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is categorized under the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. This fern is a member of the family Aspleniaceae, specifically falling within the genus Diplazium, with its scientific designation completed as the species dilatatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Diplazium dilatatum has pinnate fronds that reach up to 60 centimeters in length, characterized by spreading, lanceolate pinnae and a distinctively dilated or widened rachis near the base. The stipe is typically dark brown to blackish and possesses a smooth, somewhat shiny surface. Each pinna is subdivided into smaller, oblong segments that are arranged in a regular pattern along the central axis. The underside of the leaflets features prominent sori, which are circular clusters of sporangia often protected by a kidney-shaped indusium. These morphological traits collectively distinguish this fern from other species within the Diplaziaceae family.

Researchers continue to study these toxicological effects to better understand the long-term risks of exposure.
