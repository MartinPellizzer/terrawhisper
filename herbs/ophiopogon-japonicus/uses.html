Ophiopogon (Ophiopogon japonicus) Uses

Ophiopogon japonicus is used to treat dryness of the throat, dry cough, and sore throat. It helps alleviate throat irritation and bronchitis by moistening the lungs and reducing inflammation. It is also applied for skin dryness, rashes, and conditions like thrush, promoting skin healing and reducing irritation.

Ophiopogon (Ophiopogon japonicus) Monograph

1. Dryness of the throat

Ophiopogon japonicus is used to relieve dryness of the throat by moistening lung dryness and generating body fluids. It is commonly prescribed in formulas for dry cough, throat irritation, and soreness caused by prolonged dryness or heat. Its moistening properties help soothe the throat and restore balance to the body's fluid levels.

2. Dry cough

Ophiopogon japonicus is used to treat dry cough by moistening the lungs and reducing irritation. It is particularly effective for chronic, dry, and irritating coughs that lack phlegm. This herb helps to soothe the respiratory tract and improve lung function. It is often combined with other herbs in traditional formulas for enhanced efficacy.

3. Asthma

Ophiopogon japonicus is used in traditional Chinese medicine to alleviate asthma symptoms by moistening the lungs and reducing phlegm. It is often combined with other herbs to enhance respiratory function and ease breathing difficulties in asthma patients. Its properties help to clear airways and support lung health in chronic respiratory conditions.

4. Throat irritation

Ophiopogon japonicus is used to soothe throat irritation by moistening lung tissue and reducing inflammation. It is often prescribed for dry, sore throats and chronic coughs. Its properties help to lubricate the throat and alleviate discomfort caused by dryness or excessive heat. It is commonly combined with other herbs in traditional formulas for respiratory conditions.

5. Sore throat

Ophiopogon japonicus is used to soothe sore throats due to its moistening and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce irritation and inflammation in the throat, often used in herbal formulations for coughs and throat discomfort. It is typically combined with other herbs to enhance its therapeutic effects.

6. Bronchitis

Ophiopogon japonicus is used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat bronchitis due to its moistening and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps soothe irritated airways and reduce coughing. Often combined with other herbs, it supports respiratory health and alleviates symptoms associated with chronic bronchial inflammation.

7. Skin irritation

Ophiopogon japonicus is used in traditional herbal medicine to alleviate skin irritation. It is applied topically as a poultice or ointment to reduce redness, itching, and inflammation. Its soothing properties help promote skin healing and may also prevent further irritation from external factors.

8. Thrush

Ophiopogon japonicus is used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat thrush by nourishing the lungs and reducing inflammation. It helps soothe throat irritation and promote healing in cases of fungal infections. Its mucilage content provides a protective coating, aiding in symptom relief.

9. Skin rashes

Ophiopogon japonicus is used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat skin rashes by reducing inflammation and promoting skin healing. It is often combined with other herbs to enhance its soothing effects on irritated or inflamed skin conditions. Its moistening properties help alleviate dryness and redness associated with various dermatological issues.

10. Skin dryness

Ophiopogon japonicus is used to treat skin dryness due to its moistening properties. It helps hydrate and nourish the skin, improving dryness and flakiness. Often combined with other herbs, it supports skin health in traditional herbal formulations.
