10 Possible Side Effects Of Ophiopogon Japonicus (Mondo Grass)

Ophiopogon japonicus has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal upset, diarrhea, and nausea, caused by its emetic and purgative properties.

Consuming excessive amounts can worsen digestive issues, leading to malnutrition and fatigue.

In rare cases, it can also cause allergic reactions, such as skin rashes and itching.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Ophiopogon japonicus if used imporperly.

Ophiopogon japonicus causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

This is due to the presence of allergenic proteins and glycoproteins in the plant's leaves, stems, and roots. These compounds can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms such as itching, redness, and swelling. The allergens are thought to be released into the environment through contact with the plant or inhalation of its pollen.

As a result, people who come into contact with Ophiopogon japonicus may experience uncomfortable and potentially severe allergic reactions.
