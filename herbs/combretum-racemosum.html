Combretum racemosum

What's the taxonomical classification of Combretum racemosum?

Combretum racemosum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized under the order Myrtales. The plant is a member of the family Combretaceae and falls within the genus Combretum, specifically identified by the species name racemosum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Combretum racemosum has woody stems and leaves that are typically arranged in an opposite or whorled pattern along the branches. The foliage consists of relatively small, elliptical to oblong leaflets that often possess a slightly leathery texture. Its most distinguishing morphological feature is the long, slender racemes that bear numerous small, tubular flowers. These inflorescences are often densely packed and extend outward from the stem to attract specific pollinators. The overall growth habit of the plant reflects its adaptation to seasonally dry environments.

What are the pharmacological activities of Combretum racemosum?

This plant has anti-inflammatory, antidiabetic, antimicrobial, antioxidant, and antiproliferative pharmacological activities. These diverse biological properties are attributed to the rich concentration of secondary metabolites like tannins and flavonoids found in its extracts. The antioxidant effects help neutralize free radicals, while the antimicrobial capabilities allow it to combat various pathogenic microorganisms. Additionally, its antidiabetic potential assists in regulating blood glucose levels through specific metabolic pathways. Research also indicates that its antiproliferative action may play a role in inhibiting the growth of certain cancer cells.

As discussed in a study, the methanol extract of Combretum racemosum leaf, known as MECR, was partitioned into n-hexane, ethyl acetate, n-butanol, and aqueous fractions. These samples demonstrated radical scavenging abilities for DPPH, ABTS, and LPO that were above 50%. While the MECR contained significant amounts of total phenol, total flavonoid was abundant in the n-HFCR, EACR, n-BFCR, and AFCR fractions. Only the fractions showed significant inhibition with p < 0.05 of amylase, glucosidase, and sucrase. Furthermore, the AFCR possessed better anti-inflammatory properties compared to the other samples. Molecular docking of the most abundant phytocompound, kaempferol, revealed strong binding interactions with human amylase, SGLT-1, SGLT-2, IL-6R, and trypsin.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds Casuarictin, Digalloyl-glucoside, Pedunculagin, Vescalagin, and kaempferol. These specific phytochemicals are primarily classified as hydrolyzable tannins and flavonoids. The presence of these polyphenolic substances often contributes to the plant's antioxidant and antimicrobial properties. Researchers study these molecules to understand their potential roles in managing oxidative stress within biological systems. Such complex chemical profiles make the plant a significant subject for pharmacological investigation.

As discussed in a study, the leaves and roots of Combretum racemosum contain significant polyphenolic compounds. Specifically, the leaves are abundant in Pedunculagin, Vescalagin, Casuarictin, and Digalloyl-glucoside. In the roots, Vescalagin was found to be especially predominant. These extracts, including dichloromethane and water-ethanol versions, exhibit antimicrobial, antioxidant, and anti-inflammatory activities. The extracts also show antiproliferative effects against the A375 human melanoma cell line in a dose-dependent manner.
