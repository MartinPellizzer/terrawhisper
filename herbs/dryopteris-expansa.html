Dryopteris expansa

What's the taxonomical classification of Dryopteris expansa?

Dryopteris expansa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Polypodiinae and falls under the order Polypodiales. Its taxonomic placement continues through the family Polypodiaceae, leading to its assignment to the genus Dryopteris, and it is specifically identified by its species name, expansa.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Dryopteris expansa contains various secondary metabolites including flavonoids, tannins, saponins, and glycosides that contribute to its biological activities. These chemical constituents are thought to provide the plant with significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Some studies suggest that extracts from the plant may exhibit antimicrobial effects against specific pathogens. Researchers have also investigated its potential role in traditional medicine for treating various ailments due to these complex organic compounds. However, the precise therapeutic mechanisms of these specific compounds require further clinical validation.

What are the therapeutic actions of Dryopteris expansa?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat intestinal helminthiasis, specifically infections caused by tapeworms and roundworms. Historically, extracts from the rhizomes have been employed as anthelmintic agents to expel various parasitic worms from the human digestive tract. The active compounds within the plant work by paralyzing the parasites, allowing them to be passed through the bowels. Because of the potential for high toxicity and severe side effects, its medicinal application has largely been superseded by safer modern pharmaceuticals. Careful dosage was traditionally required to avoid harming the patient while targeting the infection.
