What's the taxonomical classification of Aristolochia howii?

Aristolochia howii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It further falls into the order Piperales and is a member of the family Aristolochiaceae. At the most specific levels of its classification, this plant is situated within the genus Aristolochia, with its specific designation being the species howii.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains aristolochic acids, which are the primary bioactive compounds found within its roots and stems. These nitrogen-containing compounds are known for their potent pharmacological properties but are also highly toxic to human cells. Researchers have identified that these acids can cause significant damage to the kidneys and are linked to the development of urothelial cancers. Beyond these toxic elements, the plant may also host various alkaloids and flavonoids that contribute to its traditional medicinal uses.
