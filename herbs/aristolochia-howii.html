Aristolochia howii

What's the taxonomical classification of Aristolochia howii?

Aristolochia howii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It further falls into the order Piperales and is a member of the family Aristolochiaceae. At the most specific levels of its classification, this plant is situated within the genus Aristolochia, with its specific designation being the species howii.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause severe nephrotoxicity, carcinogenicities, and uropathy, along with gastrointestinal issues like nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea. The presence of aristolochic acids in the plant leads to progressive kidney damage and the development of malignant tumors. These toxic compounds are also known to cause permanent damage to the urinary tract through uropathy. Beyond these serious systemic risks, individuals consuming the plant often experience acute digestive distress. Symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea frequently occur during initial exposure.
