10 Possible Side Effects Of Echium Vulgare (Vipers Bugloss)

Echium vulgare has some side effects when used improperly, such as allergic reactions, digestive issues, and skin irritation.

These side effects can be caused by excessive consumption, interactions with other medications, or individual sensitivities.

In severe cases, anaphylaxis or gastrointestinal bleeding may occur, severely impacting daily life, causing discomfort, anxiety, and even life-threatening complications.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Echium vulgare if used imporperly.

1. Produces anaphylaxis symptoms

Echium vulgare, also known as viper's bugloss, causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

This is due to the presence of sesquiterpene lactones, a group of compounds found in its leaves and flowers, which can trigger an immune response in susceptible people. Additionally, Echium vulgare contains flavonoids and alkaloids that may also contribute to allergic reactions.

These allergens can cause symptoms ranging from mild discomfort to severe reactions, depending on the individual's sensitivity.

2. Produces anaphylaxis symptoms

Echium vulgare, also known as viper's bugloss, triggers respiratory issues in some individuals.

The plant contains pyrrolizidine alkaloids, which can be toxic when ingested and cause damage to the lungs and liver. These alkaloids are released into the air when the plant is crushed or dried, exacerbating breathing difficulties such as asthma-like symptoms.

Additionally, the plant's pollen and volatile compounds may also contribute to respiratory issues in sensitive individuals.

3. Produces anaphylaxis symptoms

Echium vulgare induces liver toxicity due to its high concentration of pyrrolizidine alkaloids.

These compounds are toxic to the liver and can cause damage to its cells and tissues.

The liver's inability to process these alkaloids leads to the accumulation of toxins, which can result in inflammation, scarring, and potentially even liver failure.

Echium vulgare, also known as viper's bugloss, disrupts digestive tracts due to its saponin content, which can cause gastrointestinal upset in some individuals.

The plant's alkaloids and flavonoids may also contribute to this effect by altering the gut's natural flora, leading to changes in bowel habits and potentially causing symptoms such as bloating, cramping, and diarrhea.

The plant contains sesquiterpene lactones, which can cause an allergic reaction in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as redness, itching, and inflammation on the skin.

Additionally, Echium vulgare may also contain other compounds that can cause contact dermatitis, a type of skin rash, in those who are sensitive to it.

The plant's active compounds, such as echinacein and echiumolides, may increase the risk of irregular heartbeat and cardiac arrhythmias in some individuals.

Additionally, the herb's stimulatory effects on the cardiovascular system can further contribute to this side effect.

Echium vulgare, also known as viper's bugloss, enhances bleeding risks due to its ability to inhibit platelet aggregation and prolong bleeding time.

The plant contains bioactive compounds such as echinocystic acid and flavonoids that interfere with blood clotting mechanisms, making it more challenging for wounds to heal.

Additionally, Echium vulgare may also increase the risk of bleeding when combined with antiplatelet or anticoagulant medications.
