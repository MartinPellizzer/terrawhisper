Elaeocarpus apiculatus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Elaeocarpus apiculatus has lanceolate to narrowly elliptic leaves that are typically 5 to 12 centimeters long, featuring entire margins and an acute apex. The foliage is characterized by a coriaceous texture and a distinctively glossy upper surface. Its stems are woody and can develop a somewhat spreading habit depending on the environmental conditions. The reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually produce woody capsules. These capsules are typically dehiscent and contain seeds often surrounded by an aril.
