Butterfly Bush

Buddleja officinalis

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Buddleja officinalis, commonly known as the butterfly bush, belongs to the family Oleaceae. It is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. The plant features opposite leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers, often used in traditional herbal medicine. Synonyms include Buddleja asiatica and Buddleja madagascariensis.

Active Compounds

The Butterfly Bush contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and tannins, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, supporting its use in traditional herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Phenolic acid
  • Tannin

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action
Respiratory Bronchitis, allergic rhinitis, respiratory tract infections Expectorant, anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, antiseptic
Integumentary Skin infections, wound healing, skin inflammation Antimicrobial, antiseptic, anti-inflammatory
Urinary Urinary tract infections Antimicrobial

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for respiratory conditions and digestive issues.

Infusion: Used for mild digestive issues and as a calming tea.

Poultice: Used for skin irritations and wounds.

Safety Profile

Buddleja officinalis is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause mild gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to potential adverse effects on fetal or infant development. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
