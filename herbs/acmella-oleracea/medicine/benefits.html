10 Health Benefits Of Acmella Oleracea (Toothache Plant)

Acmella oleracea, also known as toothache plant, has health benefits such as pain relief and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a natural remedy for toothaches and other oral health issues.

Its spilanthol content has analgesic and antimicrobial effects, reducing inflammation and preventing infection.

For instance, in traditional medicine, Acmella oleracea is used to ease toothache pain and gum inflammation, improving overall oral health and reducing the need for pharmaceutical pain relievers.

This article explains in details the 10 best health benefits of Acmella oleracea.

1. Reduces toothache pain

Acmella oleracea reduces toothache pain because it contains a unique compound called spilanthol, which has potent analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties.

When the leaves of the toothache plant are chewed, the spilanthol is released and can quickly alleviate toothache pain by blocking the production of a chemical called bradykinin, which is involved in pain transmission.

This natural pain reliever can provide fast and effective relief from toothache discomfort.

2. Eases migraines

Acmella oleracea eases migraines because it contains a unique compound called alkaloids, which have been shown to have a potent anti-inflammatory effect.

This compound is capable of reducing the inflammation and swelling associated with migraines, providing relief from the throbbing pain.

Additionally, Acmella oleracea has been found to have a sedative effect, helping to calm the nervous system and alleviate migraine symptoms.

3. Treats acne

Acmella oleracea treats acne because of its unique compounds that have anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties.

The plant's extract has been shown to reduce inflammation and prevent the growth of Propionibacterium acnes, a common bacteria that contributes to acne.

Additionally, the plant's antioxidant properties help to protect the skin from oxidative damage, promoting a clear and healthy complexion.

4. Fades scar tissue

Acmella oleracea fades scar tissue because of its unique compounds, particularly the sesquiterpene lactones.

When applied topically, these compounds inhibit the production of collagen, the main protein responsible for scar formation.

This reduction in collagen production leads to a decrease in scar tissue, ultimately resulting in a more even and natural-looking skin tone.

5. Relieves stress

Acmella oleracea relieves stress because of its unique compound, parthenolide.

This natural substance has been shown to possess anxiolytic properties, which means it can help calm the mind and body. When consumed, parthenolide interacts with the brain's GABA receptors, promoting relaxation and reducing feelings of anxiety and tension.

As a result, the toothache plant has become a popular natural remedy for managing stress and promoting a sense of well-being.

6. Lowers blood sugar

Acmella oleracea lowers blood sugar because it contains a unique compound called caryophyllene oxide.

This compound has been shown to have anti-diabetic properties, which can help regulate blood sugar levels by increasing insulin sensitivity and improving glucose metabolism.

Additionally, the plant's flavonoid and phenolic compounds have been found to possess antioxidant properties, which can help protect against oxidative stress and inflammation that can contribute to insulin resistance and diabetes.

7. Kills bacteria

Acmella oleracea kills bacteria because of its unique chemical composition.

The plant contains sesquiterpene lactones, which have been shown to have potent antimicrobial properties. These compounds bind to the bacterial cell membrane, causing damage and ultimately leading to the death of the bacteria.

This ability to kill bacteria has led to the plant being used traditionally to treat various oral infections, including toothaches, and has also been studied for its potential use in modern medicine.

8. Cures fungal infections

Acmella oleracea cures fungal infections because of its unique composition of compounds, particularly the sesquiterpene lactones, which exhibit potent antifungal properties.

The plant's extracts have been shown to inhibit the growth of various fungal species, including Candida albicans, Aspergillus niger, and Fusarium oxysporum, thereby preventing the spread of fungal infections and promoting recovery.

This natural remedy offers a promising alternative to conventional treatments for fungal infections.

9. Soothes sunburns

Acmella oleracea soothes sunburns because of its unique compounds.

The plant's leaves and stems contain a natural analgesic, allantoin, which has anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

When applied topically to sunburned skin, allantoin helps to reduce redness, swelling, and discomfort, promoting faster healing and relief from pain.

10. Heals wounds

Acmella oleracea heals wounds because of its unique compound, allantoin.

This natural ingredient has been shown to accelerate the wound healing process by stimulating the production of collagen and improving tissue elasticity. Allantoin also exhibits anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which help to reduce swelling, prevent infection, and promote a healthy environment for healing.

This makes Acmella oleracea a valuable natural remedy for wound care.
