Cucurbita pepo

What's the taxonomical classification of Cucurbita pepo?

Cucurbita pepo belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Cucurbitales. This plant is a member of the family Cucurbitaceae, which is further narrowed down to the genus Cucurbita, and is ultimately identified by its specific species name, pepo.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Cucurbita pepo contains bioactive compounds including cucurbitacins, phytosterols, fatty acids, and various phenolic acids that provide significant therapeutic benefits. The cucurbitacins serve as potent anti-inflammatory and anti-tumor agents through their ability to inhibit specific cellular signaling pathways. Phytosterols found in the seeds and oil are highly effective at reducing cholesterol levels and managing prostate health in men. Additionally, the plant is rich in antioxidants like carotenoids and vitamin E, which help mitigate oxidative stress throughout the body. These diverse chemical constituents work synergistically to support immune function and digestive wellness.

What are the therapeutic actions of Cucurbita pepo?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat intestinal parasitic infections, specifically those caused by various species of tapeworms and roundworms. The seeds contain a compound called cucurbitacin that helps paralyze parasites within the digestive tract. Once immobilized, these organisms are more easily expelled from the body through natural bowel movements. In addition to parasitic issues, the plant is sometimes studied for its potential role in managing metabolic conditions like high blood sugar. Its medicinal application primarily focuses on the active properties found in the raw seeds.
