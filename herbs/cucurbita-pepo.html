Pumpkin (Cucurbita pepo)

Pumpkin (Cucurbita pepo) is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, native to North America, Central America, and South America. Traditionally, its fruits, seeds, and leaves have been used for culinary uses, decoctions, and infusions.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, diuretic, and bitter actions, and has a long history of use in native american herbal medicine, european herbal medicine, and mediterranean herbal traditions.

Flowers are yellow with five lobed petals, arranged in solitary inflorescences, actinomorphic, featuring five stamens and a superior ovary.
Stems
Climbing, herbaceous, cylindrical, with opposite, simple branching, and covered in short, pubescent hairs.
