Aphanamixis polystachya

What's the taxonomical classification of Aphanamixis polystachya?

Aphanamixis polystachya belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Sapindales and is a member of the family Meliaceae. Finally, it is placed within the genus Aphanamixis, with its specific designation being the species polystachya.

Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils, often leading to the development of distinctive fleshy fruits. What are the pharmacological activities of Aphanamixis polystachya?

This plant has anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, antiproliferative, cytotoxic, and hepatoprotective pharmacological activities. Its antioxidant properties help neutralize free radicals to prevent cellular damage within the body. The plant also demonstrates significant antiproliferative and cytotoxic effects, which suggest a potential role in inhibiting the growth of cancer cells. Furthermore, its anti-inflammatory actions work to reduce swelling and pain associated with various physiological conditions. Finally, the hepatoprotective qualities of Aphanamixis polysachya provide a protective mechanism for liver cells against chemical or toxic injury.

As discussed in a study, nineteen triterpenoids were isolated from the fruits of Aphanamixis polystachya, consisting of five previously unknown compounds and fourteen known triterpenoids. These isolates included four triucallane-type derivatives and one highly oxidized A, B-seco limonoid. All isolated compounds demonstrated the ability to inhibit LPS-induced nitric oxide production in RAW264.7 macrophages. The IC50 values for these inhibitory activities ranged from 95 to 1332 uM. Notably, compound 6 exhibited obvious anti-inflammatory activity comparable to the positive control with an IC50 value of 94.96 uM.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds aphanaperoxiides E-H, caffeic acid, p-coumaric acid, syringic acid, and various triterpenoids. The presence of phenolic acids like caffeic, p-coumaric, and syringic acid suggests potent antioxidant properties. Furthermore, the aphanaperoxiides E-H and triterpenoids play significant roles in the plant's biological activity. Together, these molecules form a complex chemical makeup used in various therapeutic studies.

As discussed in a study, four new diterpenes were isolated from the stem bark of Aphanamixis polystachya. These four novel diterpenes, named aphanaperoxides E-H (1-4), possess a rare five-membered peroxide ring. The structures and absolute configurations of these 1-4 compounds were elucidated using spectroscopic data and CD analysis. Additionally, the cytotoxicities of these isolated compounds were evaluated against a panel of human cancer cell lines.
