What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Alchornea davidii has an upright, woody shrub habit characterized by slender, greyish-brown stems and lanceolate, finely serrated leaves that are arranged oppositely along the branches. The foliage typically displays a bright green color with distinct venation and a slightly textured surface. Small, tubular flowers emerge in dense, terminal clusters, often presenting in shades of white or pale cream. These inflorescences are supported by thin pedicels that allow the blossoms to sway easily in the breeze.
