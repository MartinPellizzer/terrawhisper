Alchornea davidii

What's the taxonomical classification of Alchornea davidii?

Alchornea davidii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Malpighiales and into the family Euphorbiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Alchornea, and its species designation, davidii.

    This plant is used to treat inflammatory conditions, digestive disorders, skin infections, and various respiratory ailments.     What are the herbal preparations of Alchornea davidii?

    Alchornea davidii is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice made from its dried leaves and stems. The leaves are often steeped in hot water to create a medicinal tea that is consumed for its systemic effects. For external ailments, the crushed plant material can be applied directly to the skin as a poultice to soothe inflammation. Some practitioners also extract the active components through alcohol-based tinctures to ensure long-term stability and potency. These various methods allow for both oral ingestion and localized application depending on the specific health concern.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Alchornea davidii can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Contact with the plant's sap or leaves may trigger contact dermatitis, leading to redness, itching, or swelling on the skin. Some individuals might experience more severe hypersensitivity reactions if they are allergic to its specific chemical compounds. If swallowed, the plant can cause nausea, vomiting, or stomach pain due to its potential toxicity. It is important to avoid direct skin contact and prevent consumption to minimize these health risks.
