Alchornea davidii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Alchornea davidii has an upright, woody shrub habit characterized by slender, greyish-brown stems and lanceolate, finely serrated leaves that are arranged oppositely along the branches. The foliage typically displays a bright green color with distinct venation and a slightly textured surface. Small, tubular flowers emerge in dense, terminal clusters, often presenting in shades of white or pale cream. These inflorescences are supported by thin pedicels that allow the blossoms to sway easily in the breeze. The overall structure of the plant is somewhat airy and spreading, allowing it to integrate well into various landscape settings.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Alchornea davidii can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Contact with the plant's sap or leaves may trigger contact dermatitis, leading to redness, itching, or swelling on the skin. Some individuals might experience more severe hypersensitivity reactions if they are allergic to its specific chemical compounds. If swallowed, the plant can cause nausea, vomiting, or stomach pain due to its potential toxicity. It is important to avoid direct skin contact and prevent consumption to minimize these health risks.
