Safety, Side Effects and Contraindications

Growing, Harvesting and Storage

Geranium thunbergii grows best in well-drained soil with a slightly acidic to neutral pH, thriving in full sun to partial shade and requiring consistent moisture without becoming waterlogged. It benefits from regular watering during dry periods and mulching to retain soil moisture and suppress weeds. To maintain its health, prune back spent flowers to encourage new growth and remove any dead or diseased foliage throughout the growing season. The best time to harvest Geranium thunbergii is in late summer to early autumn when the leaves and flowers are most potent, using clean shears to cut the stems just above a node. For storage, dried leaves and flowers should be placed in airtight containers in a cool, dark place, or refrigerated to preserve potency and extend shelf life.
