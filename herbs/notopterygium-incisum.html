Notopterygium incisum

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Notopterygium incisum, commonly known as Chinese angelica or qin jiao, is a perennial herb native to East Asia. It belongs to the Apiaceae family and is characterized by its deeply lobed leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is widely used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.

Active Compounds

Notopterygium incisum contains key active compounds such as flavonoids, terpenoids, and alkaloids. These compounds work together to exert their therapeutic effects, with flavonoids providing anti-inflammatory properties and terpenoids contributing to immune modulation. Alkaloids may enhance the overall efficacy of the herb by supporting its anti-rheumatic actions.

  • Flavonoid
  • Terpenoid
  • Alkaloid

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action
Musculoskeletal Rheumatoid arthritis, joint pain, muscle pain, rheumatism Anti-inflammatory, analgesic
Respiratory Cold symptoms, respiratory infections, bronchitis Expectorant, antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory
Integumentary Skin infections, skin inflammation, eczema, fungal infections, skin rashes Antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, antifungal

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for treating rheumatic pains and inflammatory conditions.

Infusion: Used for respiratory conditions and to reduce fever.

Tincture: Used for muscle pain and to enhance circulation.

Safety Profile

Notopterygium incisum is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation due to potential risks. Long-term use may cause gastrointestinal discomfort, so it is advisable to consult a healthcare provider before use.
