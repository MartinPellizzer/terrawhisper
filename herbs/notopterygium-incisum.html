Scientific and Botanical Profile

Notopterygium Incisum, with botanical name notopterygium incisum, is a member of the Apiaceae family, commonly known as the carrot or parsley family.

Native to the mountainous regions of China, Korea, and Japan, this plant has been historically used in traditional Chinese medicine and is also found in parts of the Himalayas. Morphologically, it is a biennial herb characterized by its finely dissected, fern-like leaves that are often described as cut-leaved or incised, giving rise to its common names such as Incised Notopterygium and Notopterygium Fern. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in umbels, and its roots are the primary part used medicinally, though it is also sometimes referred to as Chinese Angelica or Chinese Yam.

Notopterygium incisum can be used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, but it is important to consider its safety profile.

There is limited research on its interactions with other drugs or herbs, but caution is advised when combining it with anticoagulants or anti-inflammatory medications due to potential additive effects. To ensure safe use, always follow recommended dosages, consult with a qualified healthcare professional, and discontinue use if adverse reactions occur.

Growing, Harvesting and Storage

Notopterygium incisum grows in temperate climates, preferring well-drained, loamy soil with a pH between 6.

0 and 7. 5, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It requires consistent moisture during the growing season but should not be overwatered, as it is susceptible to root rot. Regular weeding and mulching help maintain soil moisture and suppress weeds, while periodic pruning encourages bushier growth and better air circulation. The best time to harvest is in late summer to early autumn, when the leaves and stems are most potent, using sharp scissors or shears to cut the plant just above the base.

After harvesting, the plant material should be dried in a cool, shaded area with good airflow to preserve its medicinal properties, then stored in airtight containers in a cool, dark place or refrigerated to maintain potency and extend shelf life.
