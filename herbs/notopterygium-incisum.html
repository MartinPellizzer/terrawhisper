Notopterygium incisum

Notopterygium Incisum (Notopterygium incisum) is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China, known for its traditional use in treating respiratory and musculoskeletal conditions.

Taxonomy

Notopterygium incisum is a species within the genus Notopterygium, belonging to the family Rutaceae, order Sapindales, class Eudicots, division Magnoliophyta, and kingdom Plantae.

Morphology

Notopterygium incisum is a perennial herb with a straight, cylindrical rootstock, trifoliate leaves with serrated margins, and small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in loose panicles.

Distribution

Notopterygium incisum is native to the mountainous regions of East Asia, including countries such as China, Japan, and Korea, where it thrives in temperate climates and rocky terrain.

Traditional Uses

In traditional Chinese medicine, Notopterygium incisum has been used to treat rheumatic disorders, arthritis, and other inflammatory conditions due to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Medicinal Systems

In traditional Chinese medicine and other traditional medicinal systems, Notopterygium incisum has been historically used for its therapeutic properties, particularly in treating conditions related to wind-cold patterns and rheumatic disorders.

Phytochemistry

The phytochemical profile of Notopterygium incisum includes a variety of bioactive compounds such as alkaloids, flavonoids, terpenoids, and essential oils, which contribute to its traditional use in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Pharmacology

Notopterygium incisum is known for its anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antipyretic effects, primarily attributed to its ability to regulate immune responses and reduce pain and fever through its bioactive constituents.
