Jaborandi

Pilocarpus jaborandi

Jaborandi (Pilocarpus jaborandi) is a member of the Rutaceae family, traditionally used for stimulating saliva production, treating glaucoma, and relieving nasal congestion. The leaves and bark of this plant are commonly harvested for its medicinal applications, particularly in regions of South America where it has been historically utilized in traditional medicine.

Botanical Identification

Jaborandi, scientifically known as Pilocarpus jaborandi, is a flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family. It is also called jaborandi tree or pilocarpus in some regions. A small evergreen shrub, it is native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a distinct aromatic scent. The plant is sometimes confused with Pilocarpus microphyllus, but they are distinct species.

Preparation Methods

Extract: Used for treating glaucoma and other eye conditions due to its miotic effects.

Tincture: Used for treating colds, nasal congestion, and eye inflammation.

Decoction: Used for respiratory ailments and as a stimulant.

Safety Profile

Jaborandi is contraindicated in patients with asthma, hypertension, or peptic ulcers due to its potential to cause bronchospasm, lower blood pressure, and increase gastric acid secretion. It should be used cautiously in individuals with cardiac conditions or those taking anticholinergic medications. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
