Soaproot (Saponaria officinalis) Uses

Soaproot is used to treat respiratory conditions such as bronchitis, sinusitis, and pharyngitis. It helps alleviate coughs, sore throats, and mucus congestion by reducing inflammation and supporting respiratory health. Its saponins may help loosen mucus and ease symptoms of colds and other respiratory infections.

Soaproot (Saponaria officinalis) Monograph

1. Bronchitis

Soaproot, or Saponaria officinalis, has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in cases of bronchitis. Its saponins help loosen mucus and reduce inflammation in the airways, making it useful for easing coughing and clearing congestion. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to aid in respiratory relief.
