Salomonia cantoniensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Salomonia cantoniensis?

Salomonia cantoniensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy places it under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the lineage, the plant is categorized under the order Fabales and the family Polygalaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Salomonia, with its specific designation being the species cantoniensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Salomonia cantoniensis has small, submerged, filamentous green leaves that are highly dissected and lack a distinct midrib or stem. These delicate, thread-like structures emerge from thin, branching stems that allow the plant to float freely in slow-moving freshwater. The plant body is characterized by a complex, bushy architecture formed by numerous fine branches that increase its surface area for nutrient absorption. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically produced underwater. This morphological simplicity enables the species to thrive in various aquatic environments by maximizing light capture.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Salomonia cantoniensis targets various inflammatory mediators including cyclooxygenase enzymes, lipoxygenase, and pro-inflammatory cytokines such as tumor necrosis factor-alpha and interleukin-6. What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause severe skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and painful physical abrasions due to the presence of microscopic, hook-like hairs on its stems and leaves. These tiny structures, known as trichomes, easily penetrate the surface of human skin upon contact. Once embedded, they can trigger localized redness, intense itching, and swelling in sensitive individuals. The mechanical irritation often leads to small wounds that may become inflamed or infected if not properly cleaned. Some people may also experience systemic allergic reactions if the irritants are widespread across large areas of the body.
