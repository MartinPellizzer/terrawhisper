Genista Stipulacea

Genista stipulacea, commonly known as the leafy broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe, often used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits.

The plant contains bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and alkaloids, which contribute to its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and analgesic properties. In traditional herbal practices, it has been used to treat respiratory conditions, skin infections, and digestive ailments due to its soothing and healing effects. Recent scientific studies have begun to validate some of these traditional uses, showing potential in managing inflammation and oxidative stress.

However, more research is needed to fully understand its pharmacological potential and ensure safe usage in modern medicinal applications.
