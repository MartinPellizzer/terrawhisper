Clerodendranthus spicatus

What's the taxonomical classification of Clerodendranthus spicatus?

Clerodendranthus spicatus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Lamiales. Furthermore, it is situated within the family Lamiaceae, specifically categorized under the genus Clerodendranthus, with its unique designation as the species spicatus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clerodendrantus spicatus has woody, perennial stems with simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical to oblong in shape and possess entire margins. The foliage is characterized by a smooth texture and prominent pinnate venation on the undersides. Small, tubular white flowers are arranged in dense, terminal panicles that extend from the branch tips. These inflorescences are supported by slender pedicels and produce small, fleshy berries upon maturation. The overall structure of the plant reflects a shrubby growth habit common to many members of the Verbenaceae family.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical regions of West Africa, specifically spanning across countries such as Ghana, Nigeria, Cameroon, and Gabon. It typically thrives in moist lowland forests and coastal vegetation zones where humidity remains high throughout the year. The species is often found growing in shaded understory environments or along riverbanks within these equatorial ecosystems. Its distribution is closely tied to the specific climatic patterns of the Guineo-Congolian rainforest belt. Localized populations may also occur in disturbed forest edges where sunlight and moisture levels are optimal for growth.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds cleroden A, cleroden B, cleroden C, cleroden D, and rosmarinic acid. These specific clerodane diterpenoids are primary bioactive constituents responsible for much of the species' biological activity. Rosmarinic acid further contributes to the plant's profile by providing significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Researchers study these various chemical components to understand how they interact with human physiological systems. The unique combination of these diterpenes and phenolic acids makes the plant a subject of interest in pharmacological development.

According to a study published by "Fitoterapia", four new phenolic acids named clerodens A-D (1-4) were isolated from the whole plants of Clerodendranthus spicatus. These four compounds possess an unusual bicycle [2.2.2] octane moiety. All isolates were evaluated for anti-inflammatory activities on lipopolysaccharide (LPG)-induced nitric oxide (NO) production in RAW 264.7 cells. Among the isolates, compound 4 showed significant inhibitory activities. This specific activity was recorded with an IC50 value of 6.8 μM.
