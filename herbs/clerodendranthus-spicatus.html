Clerodendranthus spicatus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clerodendrantus spicatus has woody, perennial stems with simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical to oblong in shape and possess entire margins. The foliage is characterized by a smooth texture and prominent pinnate venation on the undersides. Small, tubular white flowers are arranged in dense, terminal panicles that extend from the branch tips. These inflorescences are supported by slender pedicels and produce small, fleshy berries upon maturation. The overall structure of the plant reflects a shrubby growth habit common to many members of the Verbenaceae family.

This plant is native to the tropical regions of West Africa, specifically spanning across countries such as Ghana, Nigeria, Cameroon, and Gabon. The species is often found growing in shaded understory environments or along riverbanks within these equatorial ecosystems. Its distribution is closely tied to the specific climatic patterns of the Guineo-Congolian rainforest belt. What are the molecular targets of this plant?

These specific molecular interactions collectively contribute to its traditional uses in treating wound healing, skin infections, and inflammatory disorders.

What side effects this plant can have?

The chemical compounds within the leaves may trigger redness or itching upon direct contact with sensitive skin. Individuals who consume parts of the plant might experience nausea, vomiting, or abdominal cramping. Some reports suggest that systemic toxicity could occur if large quantities are swallowed.
