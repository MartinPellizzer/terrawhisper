Tragopogon pratensis

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Botanical Identification

Tragopogon pratensis, commonly known as Common Cudweed, belongs to the Asteraceae family. It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. Characterized by its slender stems, hairy leaves, and yellow flowers, it has been referenced in herbal texts under various synonyms like Cudweed and Goat's beard.

Active Compounds

Common Cudweed contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and coumarins. These compounds work synergistically to support its medicinal properties, potentially reducing inflammation and oxidative stress. Their combined action enhances the herb's efficacy in traditional herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Phenolic acid
  • Coumarin

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory issues and as a general tonic.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and to extract bitter compounds.

Poultice: Used for skin conditions and inflammation.

Safety Profile

Common Cudweed is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause mild gastrointestinal upset in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to limited safety data. Always consult a healthcare provider before use, especially for those with preexisting conditions.
