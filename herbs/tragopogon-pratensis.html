Common Cudweed (Tragopogon pratensis)

Common Cudweed (Tragopogon pratensis) is a member of the Asteraceae family, native to Europe, West Asia, and North Africa. Traditionally, its leaves, flowers, and seeds have been used for infusions, decoctions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its bitter, tonic, and diuretic actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and native american herbal medicine.

Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 30 to 80 centimeters.
Leaves
Laminae with a single prominent midrib, upper surface glabrous and pale green, lower surface with a distinct stomatal band and pubescent.
Flowers
Radially symmetrical flowers with yellow petals and a central tuft of bristles, arranged in a solitary head with 12 to 18 reflexed petals and a ligulate corolla.
Stems
Calyx-like structures arise from the base of the inflorescence, forming a persistent, bract-like sheath that encloses the flower head.
