Rubus leucanthus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further follows the taxonomic order of Rosales and is a member of the family Rosaeaceace. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rubus leucanthus has arching, woody stems that are densely covered in fine, white, woolly hairs and armed with small, sharp prickles. The leaves are typically trifoliate or five-foliate with serrated margins and a distinctively fuzzy underside. These leaves possess a dark green upper surface that contrasts with the pale, pubescent texture of the leaf undersides. During the flowering stage, the plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that emerge from the hairy axils. The resulting fruit consists of aggregate drupelets that transition in color as they mature.
