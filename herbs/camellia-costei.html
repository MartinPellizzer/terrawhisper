Camellia costei

What's the taxonomical classification of Camellia costei?

Camellia costei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows a lineage within the order Ericales. It is further categorized under the family Theaceae, which places it in the genus Camellia, and is specifically identified by its species name, costei.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Camellia costei has elliptical to oblong dark green leaves that are leathery in texture with serrated margins and a glossy upper surface. These evergreen leaves are arranged alternately along the woody stems of the shrub. The plant produces fragrant white flowers that typically feature multiple layers of delicate petals and prominent yellow stamens. Small, woody capsules develop from the flowers to protect the developing seeds. The overall growth habit forms a dense, compact shrub suitable for shaded environments.
