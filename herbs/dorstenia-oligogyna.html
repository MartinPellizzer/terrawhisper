What's the taxonomical classification of Dorstenia oligogyna?

Dorstenia oligogyna belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it falls under the order Rosales and is a member of the family Moraceae. Finally, it is placed within the genus Dorstenia, specifically identified as the species oligogyna.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dorstenia oligogyna has a succulent, creeping rhizome that produces small, basal leaves with a distinctively lobed or cordate shape and a leathery texture. Its reproductive structures consist of specialized, flattened inflorescences known as cyathia that lack petals and instead display a fleshy, disc-like receptacle. These unique structures house tiny, inconspicuous flowers that eventually produce small, hard seeds. The plant's overall morphology is characterized by its compact, ground-hugging growth habit and minimal vertical stem development.
