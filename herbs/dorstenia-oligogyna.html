Dorstenia oligogyna

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dorstenia oligogyna has a succulent, creeping rhizome that produces small, basal leaves with a distinctively lobed or cordate shape and a leathery texture. The leaves typically feature three to five shallow lobes and are arranged in a somewhat rosette-like pattern near the soil surface. Its reproductive structures consist of specialized, flattened inflorescences known as cyathia that lack petals and instead display a fleshy, disc-like receptacle. These unique structures house tiny, inconspicuous flowers that eventually produce small, hard seeds. The plant's overall morphology is characterized by its compact, ground-hugging growth habit and minimal vertical stem development.
