What's the taxonomical classification of Vitex canescens?

Vitex canescens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic descent, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. As a member of the order Lamiales, it falls under the family Lamiaceae, which is characterized by specific floral structures. Within this family, the plant is identified by the genus Vitex and is specifically designated as the species canescens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vitex canescens has opposite, decussate leaves that are lanceolate to oblong in shape, measuring approximately 5 to 15 centimeters in length with finely serrated margins and a pubescent underside. The woody stems exhibit a somewhat quadrangular cross-section when young and develop a roughened bark texture as the plant matures. Small, tubular flowers are produced in dense, terminal panicles, displaying a pale lavender to blue coloration. These inflorescences eventually give way to small, fleshy drupes that transition from green to a dark purple or black hue upon ripening. The overall structure of the plant is that of a spreading shrub characterized by a dense, branched canopy.
