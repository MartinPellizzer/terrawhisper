Khat Tree (Catha Buxifolia)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Catha buxifolia.

Khat tree, scientifically known as Catha buxifolia, is a shrub native to the Horn of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula, renowned for its medicinal and stimulant properties. This plant is traditionally used as a medicinal herb and adaptogen, valued for its ability to enhance alertness and reduce fatigue. The primary benefits of Khat include its effects on the central nervous system, which can improve concentration, elevate mood, and provide a sense of well-being. In traditional and cultural contexts, Khat has been chewed for centuries in social and ritual settings, particularly in Ethiopia, Somalia, and Yemen, where it plays a significant role in community gatherings and ceremonies. Modern wellness applications include its potential use in managing stress and enhancing cognitive performance, although its use remains controversial due to its psychoactive effects and potential for abuse. The plant is also notable for containing cathinone and cathine, rare active compounds that contribute to its stimulating effects, and it has a distinctive, bitter flavor that is both unique and culturally significant.
