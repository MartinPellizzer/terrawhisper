Corylus mandshurica

What's the taxonomical classification of Corylus mandshurica?

Corylus mandshurica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized under the order Fagales. The plant is a member of the family Betulaceae, falling within the genus Corylus, and is specifically identified by the species name mandshurica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Corylus mandshurica has deciduous shrubs or small trees characterized by simple, alternate, ovate leaves with doubly serrated margins and a distinctively long, pointed tip. Its inflorescence consists of pendulous, yellowish catkins that appear in early spring before the leaves fully expand. The fruit is a small, nut-like achene enclosed within a leafy, bladder-like involucre that expands during development. These morphological traits distinguish it from other members of the hazelnut genus found in similar temperate forest habitats.
