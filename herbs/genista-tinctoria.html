Botanical Identification

Genista tinctoria, commonly known as Yellow Broom, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. Belonging to the Fabaceae family, it is also referred to as Spanish Broom or Genista. The plant features yellow flowers, trifoliate leaves, and a woody root system, with historical use in herbal medicine for its dyeing and medicinal properties.

Active Compounds

Yellow Broom contains key active compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids. These compounds contribute to its medicinal properties by exhibiting antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. They work synergistically to support overall health and therapeutic outcomes.

  • Flavonoid
  • Tannin
  • Phenolic acid

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory ailments.

Infusion: Used for mild respiratory and digestive complaints.

Tincture: Used for skin conditions and as an antiseptic.

Safety Profile

Yellow Broom (Genista tinctoria) should not be used by individuals with hypersensitivity to legumes or those with a history of allergic reactions to related plants. It may cause gastrointestinal upset and skin irritation in sensitive individuals. Consult a healthcare provider before use, especially during pregnancy or breastfeeding.
