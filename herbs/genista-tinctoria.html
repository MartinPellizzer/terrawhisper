Side Effects of genista tinctoria

Genista tinctoria may cause allergic reaction in individuals who are sensitive to the plant's compounds, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, or even anaphylaxis in severe cases.

The herb is also known to cause skin irritation, which can manifest as redness, burning, or a tingling sensation upon contact with the skin. Additionally, Genista tinctoria may induce dizziness, particularly when ingested in large quantities, which can impair coordination and judgment.

It can also cause eye irritation, resulting in redness, tearing, or a sensation of grit in the eyes. Furthermore, the herb has been reported to trigger skin rash and cause mouth irritation, with some users experiencing a burning or numbness sensation in the oral cavity. These side effects highlight the importance of caution when using Genista tinctoria, especially for individuals with a history of allergies or sensitivities.

It is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before incorporating this herb into one's regimen to minimize the risk of adverse reactions.
