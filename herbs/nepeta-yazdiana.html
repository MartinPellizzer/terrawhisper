Nepeta yazdiana

Nepeta yazdiana, also known as Persian catmint, is a perennial plant species native to the mountainous regions of Iran and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its fragrant, grayish-green foliage and vibrant blue flowers.

The medicinal properties of Nepeta yazdiana include its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant effects, which have been traditionally used to treat various health issues.

The herbal preparations of Nepeta yazdiana include teas, infusions, and extracts, which are often used to alleviate symptoms of digestive issues, anxiety, and insomnia, as well as to promote relaxation and improve overall well-being.
