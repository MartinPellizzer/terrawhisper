European Plum (Prunus avium)

European Plum (Prunus avium) is a member of the Rosaceae family, native to Mediterranean Region, Central Asia, and Southwest Asia. Traditionally, its fruits, bark, and leaves have been used for decoctions, infusions, and culinary uses.

This herb is particularly valued for its tonic, astringent, and anti-inflammatory actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and traditional chinese medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial deciduous tree.
Height
It typically reaches a height of 5 to 12 meters.
Leaves
Lateral, alternate, broad, ovate, with a glossy upper surface and a paler, often pubescent lower surface, featuring distinct stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Inflorescences are racemose clusters of zygomorphic flowers with five petals, each bearing a distinct claw and a prominent keel, and sepals that are fused at the base.
Stems
Elongated, erect growth habit, opposite branching pattern, glabrous surface, woody texture, presence of lenticels.
