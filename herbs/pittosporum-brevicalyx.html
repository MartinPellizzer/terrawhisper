Pittosporum brevicalyx

What's the taxonomical classification of Pittosporum brevicalyx?

Pittosporum brevicalyx belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized under the order Apiales. This specific plant is a part of the family Pittosporaceae, situated within the genus Pittosporum, and is identified by its unique species designation, brevicalyx.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pittosporum brevicalyx has small, simple, entire-margined leaves that are typically elliptic to obovate in shape and arranged alternately along its woody stems.

The plant features a distinctively short calyx, which gives the species its specific name, surrounding small flowers that are often yellowish or white. These flowers are generally solitary or arranged in small clusters at the leaf axils. The fruit develops into a fleshy, woody capsule that eventually splits open to reveal brightly colored seeds.

The overall growth habit is that of a dense, evergreen shrub with a compact branching structure.
