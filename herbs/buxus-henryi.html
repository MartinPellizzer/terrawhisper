What's the taxonomical classification of Buxus henryi?

Buxus henryi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Buxales. It is further nested within the family Buxaceae and is characterized by its placement in the genus Buxus, specifically identified by the species name henryi.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Buxus henryi has small, elliptic to obovate leaves that are dark green, leathery, and arranged oppositely along its woody stems. The leaf margins are entire and often possess a slightly revolute edge that adds to their dense appearance. This evergreen shrub produces inconspicuous, yellowish-green flowers that emerge from the leaf axils during the spring season. Small, globose fruits follow the flowering period, typically developing into single-seeded capsules. The overall growth habit is compact and slow-growing, forming a dense thicket of fine-textured foliage.
