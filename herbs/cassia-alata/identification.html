Botanical Identification of Candle Bush (Cassia alata)

To identify Candle Bush (Cassia alata) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Candle Bush, or Cassia alata, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Fabales, order Fabales, family Fabaceae, genus Cassia, and species Cassia alata. This classification places it within the legume family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities and medicinal uses.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The botanical name of Candle Bush is Cassia alata, a species within the legume family. It was first described by Carl von Linné in 1753. The name is derived from the Latin "candela," referring to its candle-like appearance. It is also known by various common names such as candle bush, yellow candle, and flame tree.

Morphological Characteristics

Candle Bush (Cassia alata) is a shrub or small tree with a taproot system. Its stems are straight, woody, and often branched. Leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture. The inflorescence is a raceme, bearing bright yellow flowers. The fruit is a slender, flat pod containing several seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Candle Bush (Cassia alata) can be distinguished by its bright yellow, candle-like flower clusters and bipinnate leaves with small, oval leaflets. It differs from similar species like Cassia abbreviata by having more elongated leaflets and a more pronounced floral arrangement. The bark is also smoother and less fissured compared to related Cassia species.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Candle Bush (Cassia alata) is native to the Americas, particularly Central and South America. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It prefers well-drained soils and is commonly found at low to mid-altitudes.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Candle Bush (Cassia alata) blooms from late spring to early summer, producing bright yellow inflorescences. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early fall. For herbal use, the bark and leaves are harvested in early spring before flowering, while seeds are collected after maturity in late summer.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Candle Bush (Cassia alata) is closely related to other Cassia species within the genus Cassia. It shares familial traits with the legume family Fabaceae, characterized by pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. Its phylogenetic placement reflects its close ties to other tropical shrubs in the same family.
