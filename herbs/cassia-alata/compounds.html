Candle Bush (Cassia alata) Compounds

The primary compounds in Candle Bush include flavonoids, which have anti-inflammatory properties, and coumarin, known for its anticoagulant effects. Tannins and phenolic acids contribute to antioxidant activity, while terpenoids and saponins support immune function. Glycosides and alkaloids may aid in digestive health, and anthocyanins provide protective benefits against oxidative stress.

Candle Bush (Cassia alata) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Cassia alata, known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating skin conditions and infections. They also support immune function and may aid in wound healing. Flavonoids are key to the therapeutic potential of Cassia alata in herbal medicine.

2. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Cassia alata, known for its antifungal and anti-inflammatory properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating skin infections and inflammatory conditions. Coumarin also exhibits phototoxic effects, which must be considered in its application. Its presence enhances the therapeutic potential of Cassia alata in herbal medicine.

3. Tannin

Tannin in Cassia alata contributes to its astringent properties, helping to reduce inflammation and promote wound healing. It also acts as an antioxidant, supporting the plant's traditional use in treating skin conditions and digestive issues. Tannins may enhance the plant's efficacy in herbal formulations for their therapeutic benefits.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in Cassia alata, the Candle Bush. It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which are utilized in traditional herbal medicine for treating skin conditions and digestive issues. This compound plays a significant role in the therapeutic effects of Cassia alata.

5. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Cassia alata contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. They are responsible for the plant's traditional use in treating infections and skin conditions. Terpenoids enhance the therapeutic potential of Cassia alata in herbal medicine.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Cassia alata, commonly known as Candle Bush. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating inflammation and skin conditions. Glycosides in Cassia alata are known for their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Cassia alata, known for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating skin conditions and infections. Saponin's ability to disrupt cell membranes may enhance its therapeutic effects in herbal medicine.

8. Essential oil

Essential oil from Cassia alata is a key active compound extracted through distillation. It contains volatile compounds like cineole and limonene, known for their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. This oil is used in traditional medicine to treat skin infections and digestive issues. Its aromatic profile also supports its use in aromatherapy.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a bioactive compound found in Cassia alata, commonly known as Candle Bush. It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by interacting with biological systems to produce therapeutic effects. Alkaloids in Cassia alata are often associated with anti-inflammatory and antipyretic activities.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin, found in Cassia alata, is a flavonoid pigment responsible for the plant's vibrant red and purple hues. It exhibits antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, contributing to the plant's traditional use in treating skin conditions and digestive issues. Its presence supports the therapeutic potential of Cassia alata in herbal medicine.
