Cynoglossum divaricatum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cynoglossum divaricatum has erect, branched stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 80 centimeters and possess simple or slightly serrated leaves. The leaves are generally lanceolate or oblong in shape with entire margins and are arranged alternately along the stem. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils and are often white or pale in color. The plant produces small, nut-like fruits that are characteristic of the Boraginaceae family. These morphological features help distinguish the species from other closely related members of its genus.

Its distribution is primarily restricted to these subtropical and tropical seaside environments where salt spray is frequent.
