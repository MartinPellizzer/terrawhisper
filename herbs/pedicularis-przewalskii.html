Pedicularis przewalskii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pedicularis przewalskii has a perennial herbaceous structure characterized by erect stems that reach heights of up to 40 centimeters, bearing densely packed, tubular, zygomorphic flowers in shades of pink or purple. The leaves are typically pinnately lobed or deeply divided into narrow, linear segments that emerge from the base and along the stem. The inflorescence consists of a dense spike or raceme where the upper bracts often mimic the color and shape of the corolla. Each flower features a prominent upper lip that forms a hood-like structure over the reproductive organs. The root system is composed of a stout rhizome that allows the plant to persist in its alpine or subalpine habitats.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude alpine regions of Central Asia, specifically distributed across the mountain ranges of Kyrgyzstan, Kazakhstan, and parts of western China. It grows predominantly in subalpine and alpine meadows where the terrain is rugged and rocky. These populations are typically found at elevations ranging from 2,500 to 4,000 meters above sea level. The species thrives in the cold, moist environments characteristic of these montane ecosystems. Its distribution is limited by its strict requirement for high-elevation habitats and specific climatic conditions.
