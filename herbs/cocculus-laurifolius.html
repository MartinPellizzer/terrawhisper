Cocculus laurifolius

What's the taxonomical classification of Cocculus laurifolius?

Cocculus laurifolius belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is placed in the subclass Magnoliidae and follows the order Ranunculales. This specific plant is a member of the family Menispermaceae and is categorized under the genus Cocculus, with its unique identity defined by the species laurifolius.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cocculus laurifolius has woody climbing vines characterized by trifoliate leaves with dark green, leathery leaflets that typically possess an entire or slightly serrated margin. The plant exhibits a twining growth habit, using its stems to ascend surrounding vegetation in its natural habitat. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually arranged in axillary clusters and are often yellowish or greenish in color. Following pollination, the plant produces small, fleshy drupes that change color as they mature. The overall structure is adapted for forest undergrowth, where it relies on its climbing mechanism to reach sunlight.
