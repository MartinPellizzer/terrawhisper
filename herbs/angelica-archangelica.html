What to know about Angelica archangelica (angelica) before using it medicinally

Angelica, scientifically known as Angelica archangelica, is a highly prized herb renowned for its impressive array of health benefits.

The plant's properties have been shown to alleviate digestive issues, reduce inflammation, and even exhibit antiseptic and antiviral properties, making it a valuable addition to many natural remedies. In the culinary world, angelica is often used as a garnish or added to soups, stews, and salads for its sweet, slightly spicy flavor. As a horticultural curiosity, angelica is prized for its unique, towering growth habit and delicate white blooms.

With roots dating back to ancient Greece, where it was believed to possess sacred properties, angelica has been a staple of traditional medicine and folklore for centuries.

What are the side effects of Angelica archangelica when used improperly?

When used improperly, Angelica archangelica increases the chances of experiencing side effects, such as allergic reactions, digestive issues, and interactions with other medications.

If consumed in large quantities or without proper preparation, the plant's essential oils can cause skin irritation, hives, or anaphylaxis in susceptible individuals. Furthermore, the anthelmintic properties of Angelica archangelica may lead to stomach upset, nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea if not balanced with other herbs that calm the digestive system.

Additionally, the plant may interact with blood thinners, diabetes medications, and other prescription drugs, potentially leading to adverse effects or decreased efficacy. Moreover, improper use can also cause photoallergic reactions, where exposure to sunlight triggers an allergic response, resulting in skin rash, blisters, or even Stevens-Johnson syndrome in severe cases.

It is essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using Angelica archangelica for medicinal purposes to minimize the risk of side effects and ensure safe and effective treatment.

Fertilize lightly, as the plant is sensitive to excessive nutrients. What are the harvesting tips of Angelica archangelica?

Angelica archangelica, also known as wild celery or archangel, is a perennial herb that requires careful consideration when it comes to harvesting.

The optimal time for harvesting is during its flowering stage, typically in mid-to-late summer, when the plant has reached maturity and produced its distinctive umbels of small white flowers. Additionally, handling the plant gently and avoiding excessive handling will help to minimize bruising and promote healthy regrowth for future harvests.

What are the pest and diseases tips of Angelica archangelica?

Angelica archangelica, a popular herbaceous perennial plant, is susceptible to several pests and diseases that can affect its growth and yield.

One common pest affecting Angelica archangelica is the aphid, which feeds on the plant's sap and can cause curled or distorted leaves. Additionally, the caterpillar of the small white moth (Opisthophora semialba) can chew holes in the leaves and stems, while spider mites can infest the undersides of leaves, causing yellowing and bronzing. As for diseases, Angelica archangelica is prone to root rot caused by overwatering or poor drainage, which can lead to blackened roots and a general decline in plant health.

Fungal diseases such as powdery mildew (Erysiphe sp.). and leaf spot (Septoria angelicae) can also infect the plant, causing white powder-like growths on leaves and stems, respectively. Furthermore, damping-off, a soil-borne disease caused by fungi such as Pythium or Rhizoctonia, can affect young seedlings, causing them to collapse or rot at the base.

Effective management of these pests and diseases requires integrated pest management techniques, including crop rotation, proper watering, and targeted control measures.
