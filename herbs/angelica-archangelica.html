Angelica Archangelica

Angelica archangelica, commonly known as angelica, is a perennial herb native to Europe and Asia, belonging to the carrot family (Apiaceae).

This herb has been used for centuries in traditional medicine for its numerous health benefits, which include its antiviral, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties, helping to alleviate respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and coughs, and supporting immune function.

The therapeutic actions of angelica include stimulating digestion, reducing fever, and relieving menstrual cramps, while its bioactive constituents include volatile oils, phenolic acids, and coumarins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Overall, angelica archangelica is a valuable herb that has been used in folk medicine to promote health and well-being.

Possible Side Effects of angelica archangelica

Angelica archangelica can have side effects if used improperly, such as gastrointestinal upset, diarrhea, and stomach cramps due to its high content of furanocoumarins.

Overuse of the herb may also cause allergic reactions, such as skin rashes, itching, and hives in some individuals.

In rare cases, angelica archangelica may interact with blood thinners and other medications, leading to increased bleeding risk or other adverse effects.

Furthermore, pregnant or breastfeeding women should avoid using angelica archangelica due to its potential to stimulate uterine contractions and affect fetal development.
