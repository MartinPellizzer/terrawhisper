Archangelica

Angelica archangelica

Archangelica (Angelica archangelica) is a member of the Apiaceae family, traditionally used for digestive issues, respiratory remedies, and support during colds and flu. Native to northern regions, it has been historically valued for its aromatic leaves, roots, and seeds, which are often utilized in herbal preparations.

Botanical Identification

Archangelica, or Angelica archangelica, is a member of the Apiaceae family. Common names include angelica and hog fennel. It is a biennial herb native to northern Europe and Asia. The plant has large, fern-like leaves and yellowish-green flowers. Synonyms in herbal literature include garden angelica and wild angelica.

Archangelica Identification.

Active Compounds

Archangelica contains essential oils, coumarin, and flavonoids as its primary active compounds. These work synergistically to support digestive and respiratory health. The essential oils provide antimicrobial properties, while coumarin and flavonoids contribute to anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

  • Essential oil
  • Coumarin
  • Flavonoid

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory conditions.

Infusion: Used for colds, coughs, and respiratory infections.

Tincture: Used for digestive disorders and nervous system support.

Safety Profile

Archangelica is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and should be used with caution by those with kidney disease. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
