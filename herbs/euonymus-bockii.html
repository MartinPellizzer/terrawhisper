Euonymus bockii

What's the taxonomical classification of Euonymus bockii?

Euonymus bockii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Celastrales. It is further categorized under the family Celastraceae, falling into the genus Euonymus, and is specifically identified by its species name, bockii.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Euonymus bockii can cause skin irritation, nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and potential neurological issues if ingested. Contact with the leaves or stems can trigger contact dermatitis, resulting in redness or itching on the skin. Ingestion of the berries or foliage is particularly dangerous and may lead to severe gastrointestinal distress. Careful avoidance of all parts of this plant is necessary to prevent accidental poisoning.
