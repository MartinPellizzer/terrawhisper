Euonymus bockii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Euonymus bockii has opposite, simple, ovate to elliptic leaves with entire margins and acute tips that grow on woody, deciduous stems. The foliage typically displays a smooth texture and can range in color from bright green to darker shades depending on light exposure. During the flowering season, small, inconspicuous greenish flowers appear in clusters. The plant produces distinctive four-lobed capsules that split open to reveal bright orange or red fleshy arils surrounding the seeds. These colorful fruits serve as a primary identifying feature of the species in the field.
