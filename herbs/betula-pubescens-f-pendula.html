Betula pubescens f. pendula

Betula pubescens f. pendula, also known as weeping birch, is a subspecies of the white birch tree (Betula pubescens) characterized by its drooping branches and slender stems.

The plant is used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antiviral properties.

The bark of the weeping birch contains salicin, a compound similar to aspirin, which provides pain relief and reduces fever, offering health benefits such as reducing inflammation and alleviating symptoms of colds and flu.

Herbal preparations of weeping birch include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which can be used to treat various ailments, including skin conditions, wounds, and respiratory issues.
