What is Keckiella cordifolia?

Keckiella cordifolia is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, belonging to the family Plantaginaceae.

It grows to a height of 3-6 feet, with a woody stem that is hairy and glandular. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and cordate, typically 2-4 inches long and 1-2 inches wide, with a hairy and glandular surface. The flowers are tubular, bell-shaped, and white, occurring in clusters at the end of the stem, and are pollinated by hummingbirds.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous small seeds.
