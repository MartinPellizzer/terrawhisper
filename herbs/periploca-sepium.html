Periploca sepium

What's the taxonomical classification of Periploca sepium?

Periploca sepium belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Gentianaales. Within this order, it is situated in the family Apocynaceae, specifically falling under the genus Periploca, with the formal species designation being sepium.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Periploca sepium has opposite, simple, elliptical leaves that measure 5 to 15 centimeters in length and possess entire margins with distinctively prominent pinnate venation. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically reaches heights of up to five meters. Its bark is smooth and grayish, often becoming slightly fissured as the specimen matures. During the summer, it produces small, inconspicuous, creamy-white flowers that are arranged in axillary panicles. The resulting fruit consists of a small, fleshy, dark berry that contains several tiny seeds.

What are the pharmacological activities of Periploca sepium?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological potential through its anti-proliferative, anticancer, and cytotoxic activities against various malignant cell lines. Research indicates that specific bioactive compounds within its extracts can induce direct cytotoxic activity, leading to programmed cell death in cancer cells. These substances work primarily by exerting growth inhibition, which prevents the rapid multiplication of tumor cells. By disrupting essential cellular processes, the plant acts as a potent agent in suppressing the progression of certain diseases. Such multifaceted biological actions highlight its importance in ongoing oncological studies.

As discussed in a study, extracts of the root bark of Periploca sepium Bunge showed strong cytotoxic activity during a screening of Chinese plants used for cancer treatment. The study isolated the cardiac glycosides periplocin, glucosyl divostroside, periplogenin, periplocymarin, and periplocoside M. Among these, periplocin exhibited the lowest IC50 value against leukemia and liposarcoma cells. Specifically, periplocin kills liposarcoma cells by death receptor mediated apoptosis. In SW-872 cells, periplocin leads to growth inhibition and apoptosis induction by changing death receptor expression and inducing DNA double strand breaks.
