What's the taxonomical classification of Arisaema hunanense?

Arisaema hunanense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Alismatales. This species is a member of the family Araceae, which is the group to which its genus, Arisaema, is assigned. Finally, the specific biological identity of this plant is concluded by its species designation, hunanense.

      What are the herbal preparations of Arisaema hunanense?

      Arisaema hunanense is prepared as a decoction, a topical paste, or a processed powder to mitigate the presence of irritating calcium oxalate crystals. Because the plant contains potent needle-like crystals, the raw material must undergo heat treatment or specialized processing to reduce toxicity before human use. In decoctions, the plant parts are boiled in water to extract active compounds while simultaneously neutralizing certain irritants. When used as a topical paste, the preparation is applied directly to the skin for localized therapeutic effects. Powders are often derived from dried, processed tubers to ensure a consistent dosage for oral administration.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Arisaema hunanense can cause severe irritation to the skin, eyes, and mucous membranes due to the presence of insoluble calcium oxalate crystals. When these needle-like crystals come into contact with sensitive tissues, they penetrate the cells and trigger an intense burning sensation. Ingesting any part of the plant can lead to significant swelling of the mouth, throat, and tongue, potentially obstructing the airway. This localized inflammation is often accompanied by intense pain and nausea. Because of these hazardous properties, the plant should be handled with extreme caution to avoid accidental exposure.
