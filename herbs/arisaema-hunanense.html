      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Arisaema hunanense can cause severe irritation to the skin, eyes, and mucous membranes due to the presence of insoluble calcium oxalate crystals. When these needle-like crystals come into contact with sensitive tissues, they penetrate the cells and trigger an intense burning sensation. Ingesting any part of the plant can lead to significant swelling of the mouth, throat, and tongue, potentially obstructing the airway. This localized inflammation is often accompanied by intense pain and nausea. Because of these hazardous properties, the plant should be handled with extreme caution to avoid accidental exposure.
