Spiraea cantoniensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Spiraea cantoniensis?

Spiraea cantoniensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order of Rosales and is a member of the family Rosaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Spiraea and is specifically designated by the species name cantoniensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Spiraea cantoniensis has deciduous, arching stems that support opposite, finely serrated leaves measuring 1 to 3 centimeters in length. The foliage is typically bright green and transitions to reddish hues during autumn. Small, five-petaled white flowers emerge in dense, flat-topped clusters known as corymbs during the summer months. These blossoms are supported by slender pedicels that create a delicate, airy appearance across the shrub. The plant generally maintains a spreading, mounded growth habit that can reach several feet in width.
