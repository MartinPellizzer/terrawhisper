Terminthia paniculata

What's the taxonomical classification of Terminthia paniculata?

Terminthia paniculata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Sapindales and the family Anacardiaceae, eventually reaching its specific designation within the genus Terminthia. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its unique species name, paniculata.

    What are the herbal preparations of Terminthia paniculata?

    Terminthia paniculata is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste for medicinal use. The dried leaves and stems are often boiled in water to extract bioactive compounds for internal consumption. The plant contains bioactive compounds that may trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. When applied topically, it might lead to redness, itching, or localized swelling. Ingestion of the plant material poses a risk of stomach pain or nausea due to its chemical constituents. Proper precautions should be taken to avoid accidental exposure to its parts.
