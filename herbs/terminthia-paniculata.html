What's the taxonomical classification of Terminthia paniculata?

Terminthia paniculata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Sapindales and the family Anacardiaceae, eventually reaching its specific designation within the genus Terminthia. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its unique species name, paniculata.

The termipaniculatones represent a specific class of bioactive molecules that contribute to the plant's unique chemical profile. Additionally, the presence of chalcone-flavonone heterodimers suggests significant potential for pharmacological investigation. Together, these constituents define the complex medicinal composition of the species.

    As discussed in a study, Terminthia paniculata contains six chalcone-flavonone heterodimers known as termipaniculatones A-F. Termipaniculatones A and E exhibited xanthine oxidase inhibitory activity with IC50 values of 55.6 and 89.5 μM, respectively. Termipaniculatone A interacts with Glu802, Arg880, Thr1010, and Val1011 residues within the active site of xanthine oxidase. At a concentration of 20 mg/kg, termipaniculatone A demonstrated anti-hyperuricemic effects and anti-inflammatory activity by alleviating paw swelling in mice. These findings reveal the anti-hyperuricemic and anti-acute gouty arthritis potency of Terminthia paniculata through its characteristic biflavonoids.
