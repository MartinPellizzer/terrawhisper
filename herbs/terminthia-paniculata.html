What's the taxonomical classification of Terminthia paniculata?

Terminthia paniculata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Sapindales and the family Anacardiaceae, eventually reaching its specific designation within the genus Terminthia. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its unique species name, paniculata.

What are the pharmacological activities of Terminthia paniculata?

This plant has anti-acute gouty arthritis, anti-hyperuricemic, anti-inflammatory, anti-rheumatic arthritis, and xanthine oxidase inhibitory activities. These pharmacological properties allow it to effectively manage conditions related to high uric acid levels in the body. By demonstrating xanthine oxidase inhibitory activity, the plant helps prevent the overproduction of uric acid. Its anti-inflammatory and anti-rheumatic effects further assist in reducing the pain and swelling associated with joint disorders. Consequently, these combined actions make it a significant subject for treating various forms of inflammatory arthritis.

According to a study published by "Phytochemistry", Terminthia paniculata is used for treating inflammation and rheumatic arthritis, showing xanthine oxidase inhibitory activity in vitro and anti-hyperuricemic effects in vivo. The plant contains six chalcone-flavonone heterodimers known as termipaniculatones A-F. Termipaniculatones A and E demonstrated obvious xanthine oxidase inhibitory activity with IC50 values of 55.6 and 89.5 μM, respectively. Termipaniculatone A showed anti-hyperuricemic effects by decreasing serum uric acid levels and inhibited xanthine oxidase activity in serum and liver. Additionally, termipaniculatone A demonstrated anti-inflammatory activity by alleviating paw swelling in monosodium urate-induced mice at a concentration of 20 mg/kg.
