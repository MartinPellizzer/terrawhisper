What's the taxonomical classification of Morinda cochinchinensis?

Morinda cochinchinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy places it in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Gentianales, the plant is a member of the family Rubiaceae. Within this family, it is categorized under the genus Morinda, and its specific identification is defined by the species epithet cochinchinenis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Morinda cochinchiensis has woody stems with opposite leaves that are elliptical to oblong in shape, featuring entire margins and an acute apex. The foliage is typically dark green and possesses a somewhat leathery texture. Small, white, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils in clusters. These flowers eventually develop into small, fleshy, globose fruits that are often used for dye production. The plant also exhibits a characteristic branching pattern typical of the Rubiaceae family.
