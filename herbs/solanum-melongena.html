Eggplant (Solanum melongena)

Eggplant (Solanum melongena) is a member of the Solanaceae family, native to South Asia, Southeast Asia, and Indo-Malayan Region. Traditionally, its fruits, leaves, and seeds have been used for culinary uses, decoctions, and infusions.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, astringent, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in ayurvedic medicine, traditional chinese medicine, and european herbal medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters.
Leaves
Broad leaves with upper surface glabrous and lower surface pubescent, exhibiting a single prominent stomatal band along the midrib.
Flowers
Flowers are actinomorphic, solitary, with violet to purple coloration, five fused petals forming a funnel shape, and five stamens arranged in two whorls.
Stems
Erect growth habit with opposite branching, glabrous surface, and woody texture.
