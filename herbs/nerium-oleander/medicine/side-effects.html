3. Triggers anaphylactic shock

Nerium oleander triggers allergic reactions due to its toxic sap, which contains compounds such as oleandrin and neriine.

These potent chemicals can cause skin irritation, itching, redness, and swelling in some individuals who come into contact with the plant or use products containing its extracts.

The severity of these reactions can vary widely depending on individual sensitivities and exposure levels.

6. Triggers anaphylactic shock

Nerium oleander causes gastrointestinal upset in some individuals due to its high concentration of iridoid glycosides, such as oleandrin and neriine.

These compounds can cause nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and stomach cramps when ingested or absorbed through the skin. Additionally, oleander contains cardiac glycosides that may alter bowel function and contribute to gastrointestinal disturbances.

The exact mechanisms by which these compounds induce gut upset are not fully understood, but it is thought to be related to their ability to affect the gut lining and alter water and electrolyte balance in the body.

7. Triggers anaphylactic shock

Nerium oleander increases heart rate due to its cardiac glycosides content, which can affect the body's regulation of electrolytes such as potassium and calcium.

This can lead to an increased sympathetic tone, causing the heart to beat faster in order to compensate for the imbalance.

Additionally, oleander's beta-blocker-like properties may also contribute to this effect, further amplifying its impact on the cardiovascular system.
