Eupatorium cannabinum

What's the taxonomical classification of Eupatorium cannabinum?

Eupatorium cannabinum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Asterales and the family Asteraceae, which characterizes its botanical relationship to many other daisy-like plants. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Eupatorium, and its unique species name, cannabinum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eupatorium cannabinum has oppositely arranged, lanceolate leaves with serrated margins and a characteristic dark green color. The stem is typically upright and can reach heights of up to two meters in well-nourished soils. Its inflorescence consists of dense, terminal clusters of small, pink to purple tubular flower heads. These flowers are held atop long peduncles that rise above the foliage to attract various pollinators. The overall structure is herbaceous and robust, often forming large colonies in damp environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Central and South America, spanning from southern Mexico through the Caribbean and into much of the Amazon basin. It typically thrives in moist, lowland environments such as rainforest margins and riverbanks where humidity is consistently high. Its distribution is closely linked to warm climates that lack extreme temperature fluctuations. Within these habitats, it often occupies secondary growth areas and forest edges. These specific ecological conditions allow the species to spread across various tropical biomes throughout the neotropics.

What are the pharmacological activities of Eupatorium cannabinum?

This plant has anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, antioxidant, antiproliferative, and antitumor pharmacological activities. Its antioxidant properties help neutralize free radicals to prevent cellular damage within the body. The presence of antimicrobial compounds allows it to combat various pathogenic microorganisms effectively. Additionally, the plant exhibits antiproliferative and antitumor effects by inhibiting the rapid growth and spread of malignant cells. These diverse biological functions make it a significant subject for medicinal research.

As discussed in a study, Eupatorium cannabinum L. possesses various pharmacological effects due to active compounds like phenolics, sesquiterpenes, pyrrolizidine alkaloids, and polysaccharides. HPLC analysis of the chloroform fraction (EC) showed eupatorin, eupatilin, and quercetin, while the distilled water fraction (EA) contained caefic acid and rutin. Regarding antiproliferative potential after 24 h of exposure, Jurkat cells were the most sensitive with an IC50 of 7.35 ± 0.35 µg/mL for EC and 13.77 ± 2.16 µg/mL for EA. Other lines were susceptible only to EC, showing an IC50 of 88.27 ± 1.34 on Caco-2 cells and over 100 µg/mL on BT20 and HepG2 cells. Finally, preventive administration in an LPS-induced damage mouse model increases survival times and inhibits proinflammatory cytokines.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains echi nati ne, flavonoids, pyrrolizi dine alkaloids, supine, and various volatile compounds that contribute to its chemical profile. The presence of flavonoids provides significant antioxidant properties to the plant's medicinal makeup. Additionally, the volatile compounds are responsible for the distinct aromatic qualities often associated with its species. While certain elements offer therapeutic potential, the pyrrolizi dine alkaloids must be carefully monitored due to their toxicological implications. The compound supine also plays a specific role within the complex biochemical structure of the plant.

As discussed in a study, the aerial parts of Eupatorium cannabinum contain a complicated composition of pyrrolizidine alkaloids including different stereoisomers. The analysis showed the presence of at least 2 alkaloids with a molecular weight of 283 and 4 alkaloids with a molecular weight of 299. While many flavonoids and glycosides are present, rutin could not be detected in the subterranean parts of E. cannabinum. Furthermore, the essential oil of E. cannabinum contains 35 compounds that were detected by GC-MS for which no literature data were available.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause severe nausea, acute dizziness, intense muscular tremors, and sudden episodes of disorientation in humans. These physiological reactions typically occur shortly after ingestion or topical application. Some individuals may also experience a rapid increase in heart rate and heightened anxiety levels. Prolonged exposure has been linked to localized skin irritation and persistent headaches. Monitoring these symptoms is essential for anyone interacting with the species.
