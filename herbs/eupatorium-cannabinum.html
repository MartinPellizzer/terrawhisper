Hemp agrimony, scientifically known as Eupatorium cannabinum, is a herb rich in health-improving properties, boasting anti-inflammatory and antioxidant compounds that can help soothe skin irritations, reduce joint pain, and boost immunity.

While its culinary uses are often overlooked, hemp agrimony adds a unique flavor and texture to salads, soups, and teas, making it a versatile ingredient for creative cooks. Horticulturally, this hardy perennial thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, requiring minimal maintenance while producing an abundance of stems and leaves. From a botanical perspective, hemp agrimony is part of the aster family, featuring delicate white flowers that resemble tiny daisies.

With its long history of use dating back to ancient Greek and Roman medicine, hemp agrimony has been a trusted remedy for centuries, with references in the works of renowned herbalists such as Pliny the Elder.
