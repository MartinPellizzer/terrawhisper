Common Boneset

Eupatorium cannabinum

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Common Boneset, scientifically known as Eupatorium cannabinum, belongs to the Asteraceae family. It is also called Hemp Agaric and is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant has hairy stems, opposite leaves, and small greenish-white flowers. It is sometimes referred to as "hemp" due to its similar appearance to Cannabis species.

Active Compounds

Common Boneset contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, coumarins, and phenolic acids. These compounds work synergistically to support its medicinal properties, likely through anti-inflammatory and antioxidant mechanisms. Their combined action enhances the herb's efficacy in traditional herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Coumarin
  • Phenolic acid

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for respiratory and digestive ailments.

Infusion: Used for colds and flu symptoms.

Extract: Used for skin conditions and inflammation.

Safety Profile

Common Boneset is generally safe when used in recommended doses, but it may cause gastrointestinal upset in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to potential risks to the fetus or infant. Avoid use if you have a known allergy to plants in the Asteraceae family.
