Eupatorium cannabinum

What's the taxonomical classification of Eupatorium cannabinum?

Eupatorium cannabinum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Asterales and the family Asteraceae, which characterizes its botanical relationship to many other daisy-like plants. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Eupatorium, and its unique species name, cannabinum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eupatorium cannabinum has oppositely arranged, lanceolate leaves with serrated margins and a characteristic dark green color. The stem is typically upright and can reach heights of up to two meters in well-nourished soils. Its inflorescence consists of dense, terminal clusters of small, pink to purple tubular flower heads. These flowers are held atop long peduncles that rise above the foliage to attract various pollinators. The overall structure is herbaceous and robust, often forming large colonies in damp environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Central and South America, spanning from southern Mexico through the Caribbean and into much of the Amazon basin. It typically thrives in moist, lowland environments such as rainforest margins and riverbanks where humidity is consistently high. Its distribution is closely linked to warm climates that lack extreme temperature fluctuations. Within these habitats, it often occupies secondary growth areas and forest edges. These specific ecological conditions allow the species to spread across various tropical biomes throughout the neotropics.

What are the therapeutic actions of Eupatorium cannabinum?

Eupatorium cannabinum has therapeutic properties as a nervine, sedative, anxiolytic, and antispasmodic agent used to soothe the nervous system and reduce physical tension. It works primarily to calm the central nervous system, making it effective for alleviating symptoms of anxiety and restlessness. The plant's sedative qualities help induce relaxation and promote better sleep patterns in agitated individuals. Additionally, its antispasmodic action assists in relaxing smooth muscles to mitigate various forms of cramping or involuntary contractions. This combination of actions makes the herb a versatile tool for managing stress-related physical and mental discomfort.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains echiantine, flavonoids, pyrrolizidine alkaloids, supine, and various volatile compounds that serve as its primary medicinal constituents. The flavonoids within the plant are recognized for their significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. While the volatile compounds contribute to the plant's distinct aroma and therapeutic essence, the presence of pyrrolizidine alkaloids requires careful monitoring due to potential toxicity. Additionally, the specific chemical structures of echiantine and supine play specialized roles in the plant's overall biological activity. Together, these diverse compounds form a complex chemical profile used in various medicinal applications.

As discussed in a study, the aerial parts of Eupatorium cannabinum contain a complicated composition of pyrrolizidine alkaloids consisting of different stereoisomers. The analysis showed the presence of at least 2 alkaloids with a molecular weight of 283 and 4 alkaloids with a molecular weight of 299. While many flavonoids and glycosides exist in the species, rutin could not be detected in the subterranean parts of E. cannabinum. The essential oil of E. cannabinum contains volatile compounds where 35 compounds were detected by means of a GC-MS analysis about which no literature data were available.
