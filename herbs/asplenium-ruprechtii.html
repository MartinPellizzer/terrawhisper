What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Asplenium ruprechtii has large, dark green, pinnate fronds that can reach up to 1 meter in length with individual pinnae that are lanceolate and possess slightly wavy margins. The plant features a creeping rhizome that is typically covered in brownish scales, providing a sturdy base for the emerging foliage. Each leaflet is arranged along a central rachis that is often smooth or slightly textured depending on the specimen's maturity. On the underside of the mature fronds, small, linear sori are arranged in two neat rows along the veins to facilitate spore production. The overall structure of the fern is robust, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded forest environments.
