What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea chetchozensis has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by basal leaves that are lanceolate to oblong with entire or slightly serrated margins and a dense, woolly indumentum on both surfaces. The plant features a robust, erect flowering stem that typically rises from a deep taproot system. The inflorescence consists of a compact, terminal capitulum enclosed by several layers of overlapping, papery involucral bracts. These bracts often exhibit distinct coloration and provide protection to the numerous small, tubular florets within the flower head. The overall morphology is highly adapted to the harsh, high-altitude environments where it grows.
