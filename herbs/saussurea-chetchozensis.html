Saussurea chetchozensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Saussurea chetchozensis?

Saussurea chetchozensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it falls under the order Asterales and is a member of the family Asteraceae. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Saussurea and the species cretchozensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea chetchozensis has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by basal leaves that are lanceolate to oblong with entire or slightly serrated margins and a dense, woolly indumentum on both surfaces.

The plant features a robust, erect flowering stem that typically rises from a deep taproot system. The inflorescence consists of a compact, terminal capitulum enclosed by several layers of overlapping, papery involucral bracts. These bracts often exhibit distinct coloration and provide protection to the numerous small, tubular florets within the flower head.

The overall morphology is highly adapted to the harsh, high-altitude environments where it grows.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Saussurea chetchozensis can cause adverse reactions such as skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential allergic responses in sensitive individuals. Scientific studies on its specific toxicological profile are limited, meaning any use should be approached with extreme caution.
