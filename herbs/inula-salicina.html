Inula salicina

What's the taxonomical classification of Inula salicina?

Inula salicina belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the order Asterales and the family Asteraceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Inula, and its unique species name, salicina.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Inula salicina has lanceolate to narrowly elliptic leaves that are typically 5 to 15 centimeters long, arranged oppositely along a sturdy, upright stem. The leaf margins are often finely serrated or entire, featuring a texture that can range from smooth to slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of bright yellow, composite flower heads characterized by prominent central disc florets surrounded by a ring of ray florets. The stems are often woody at the base and may exhibit a pubescent coating depending on the specific environmental conditions. These morphological traits distinguish the species within its genus and allow it to thrive in specific montane habitats.
