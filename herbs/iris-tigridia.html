What's the taxonomical classification of Iris tigridia?

Iris tigridia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Asparagales. It is a member of the family Iridaeceae, falling under the genus Iris, and is specifically identified by its species name, tigridia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Iris tigridia has upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and grow in a basal clump. These leaves feature a distinct longitudinal vein and a smooth, somewhat leathery texture. The plant produces slender, branching stems that rise above the foliage to support its colorful flowers. Each bloom consists of three large outer petals and three smaller inner petals, often decorated with intricate spotted or striped patterns. The root system is composed of thick, fleshy rhizomes that store nutrients for future growth.
