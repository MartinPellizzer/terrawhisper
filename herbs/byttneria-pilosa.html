What's the taxonomical classification of Byttneria pilosa?

Byttneria pilosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Malvales and the family Malvaceae. Finally, this specific plant is identified by the genus Byttneria and the species epithet pilosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Byttneria pilosa has a woody, perennial habit characterized by stems that are densely covered in fine, soft hairs. The leaves are typically simple, arranged oppositely, and feature ovate to elliptic shapes with serrated margins. These leaves possess a pubescent underside, which provides a distinct textured appearance compared to the upper surface. The inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. Each flower is supported by a hairy pedicel, consistent with the pilose nature of the entire plant.
