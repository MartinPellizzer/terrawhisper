What's the taxonomical classification of Piper yunnanense?

Piper yunnanense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is organized under the order Piperales and the family Piperaceae. Finally, it is placed within the genus Piper, specifically identifying as the species yunnanense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Piper yunnanense has opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with entire or slightly wavy margins and a distinctively acuminate apex. The leaf surface typically exhibits a smooth texture with prominent pinnate venation that creates a slightly textured appearance. The stems are woody at the base and characterized by a slender, somewhat flexible growth habit. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences that emerge from the leaf axils. These spikes eventually produce small, fleshy fruits that serve as a key identifying feature of the species.

The species is restricted to these specific East Asian habitats due to its preference for shaded, well-drained terrain.
