Jasminum duclouxii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Jasminum duclouxii has woody, scrambling stems that feature small, elliptical, dark green leaves arranged in opposite pairs. The foliage typically consists of three to five leaflets per leaf, which possess smooth margins and a slightly glossy texture. During its flowering period, the plant produces fragrant, tubular white blossoms that emerge from the axils of the leaves. These flowers often feature five spreading lobes that create a delicate star-like appearance. The overall growth habit allows the plant to climb or spread across surfaces using its flexible branches.

      What are the herbal preparations of Jasminum duclouxii?

      Jasminum duclouxii is prepared as decoctions, infusions, or topical pastes using its dried leaves and flowers to extract active medicinal compounds. The flowers are frequently steeped in hot water to create aromatic teas that are consumed for their soothing properties. Leaves can be crushed into a fine pulp to form a poultice applied directly to the skin for local treatments.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Jasminum duclouxii can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, or mild digestive upset if ingested accidentally. While not widely documented as highly toxic, individual sensitivities to its essential oils or sap may result in redness and itching upon contact with the skin. People with known allergies to the jasmine family should exercise caution when handling the plant to avoid respiratory or dermal reactions. There is currently limited clinical data regarding the long-term systemic effects of its consumption in humans. Always consult a medical professional if any adverse reaction occurs after exposure.
