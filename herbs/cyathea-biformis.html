    What is Cyathea biformis?

    Cyathea biformis is a species of tree fern native to New Caledonia.

    It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae, a group of ferns characterized by the presence of a sterile crown of fronds and a fertile crown with sporangia-bearing fronds. Cyathea biformis has a stout trunk covered with old leaf bases, and a crown of bipinnate fronds, 1-2 meters long, with leaflets that are lanceolate to linear in shape and have a serrated margin. The species is epiphytic, but can also grow as a terrestrial fern.

    The fronds of Cyathea biformis are attached to the trunk and branches by a stipe, which is covered with scales and has a black, waxy texture.
