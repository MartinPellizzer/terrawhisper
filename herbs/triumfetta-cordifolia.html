Triumfetta cordifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Triumfetta cordifolia?

Triumfetta cordifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is part of the order Malvales and falls within the family Malvaceae. It is further defined by its genus, Triumfetta, and is specifically identified by its species name, cordifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Triumfetta cordifolia has an erect or sprawling shrubby habit characterized by cordate-shaped leaves with serrated margins and small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense axillary clusters. Its stems are often woody at the base and can exhibit a branching pattern that helps the plant spread through undergrowth. The inflorescence consists of small, tubular corollas that emerge from compact, head-like structures. These botanical features collectively define its physical presence within tropical forest ecosystems.
