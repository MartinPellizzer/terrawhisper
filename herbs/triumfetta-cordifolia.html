Triumfetta cordifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Triumfetta cordifolia?

Triumfetta cordifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is part of the order Malvales and falls within the family Malvaceae. It is further defined by its genus, Triumfetta, and is specifically identified by its species name, cordifolia.

Its stems are often woody at the base and can exhibit a branching pattern that helps the plant spread through undergrowth. The inflorescence consists of small, tubular corollas that emerge from compact, head-like structures. What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for the treatment of malaria and the reduction of malaria parasitemia. Researchers have identified that its bioactive compounds possess significant antimalarial properties that target the infection. Studies indicate that extracts from the leaves can effectively lower the density of parasites in the bloodstream. Beyond malaria, it is also traditionally applied to manage various inflammatory and febrile conditions. These therapeutic applications make it a subject of interest in pharmacological research for tropical diseases.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", Triumfetta cordifolia is used for the treatment of malaria. The leaf extract displayed moderate in vitro antiplasmodial activity with an IC50 of 48.09 μg/ml. This plant extract was found to be non-toxic to HEK293 cells and erythrocytes. At a dose of 400 mg/kg, T. cordifolia significantly (p<0.001) suppressed parasitemia and significantly (p<0.001) inhibited RBC depletion. It also prolonged survival in infected mice within the study.
