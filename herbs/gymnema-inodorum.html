Gymnema inodorum

What's the taxonomical classification of Gymnema inodorum?

Gymnema inodorum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further follows the order Gentianaales and is a member of the family Apocynaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Gymnema and the species inodorum.

These specific chemical constituents are primarily known for their ability to interact with glucose receptors in the intestinal tract. By inhibiting the absorption of sugars, these compounds help in regulating blood glucose levels in the body. Researchers often study these bioactive molecules to understand their potential role in managing diabetes and metabolic health. The concentration of these substances can vary significantly depending on the growing conditions and the part of the plant being harvested.
