Loquat (Eriobotrya japonica) Compounds

The primary active compounds in Loquat include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and phenolic acids, known for their anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins and saponins contribute to its astringent and digestive benefits, while essential oils provide aromatic and antimicrobial properties.

Loquat (Eriobotrya japonica) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Loquat (Eriobotrya japonica). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting its traditional use in respiratory and digestive health. These compounds may help reduce oxidative stress and modulate immune responses in the body.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Loquat (Eriobotrya japonica). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports the therapeutic effects of Loquat in treating respiratory and digestive conditions.

3. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Loquat (Eriobotrya japonica). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins in Loquat are primarily found in the fruit's skin and leaves, supporting its traditional use in digestive and respiratory health.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Loquat (Eriobotrya japonica). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects. Found in the leaves and fruit, coumarin supports traditional uses of Loquat in respiratory and digestive health. Its presence enhances the plant's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Loquat (Eriobotrya japonica). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, such as expectorant and antitussive effects. Glycosides are often responsible for the therapeutic actions of herbal remedies derived from Loquat. They are commonly found in the fruit and leaves of the plant.

6. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Loquat (Eriobotrya japonica). They contribute to the plant's medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. These compounds are often responsible for the therapeutic benefits attributed to Loquat in traditional herbal medicine.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound found in Loquat (Eriobotrya japonica). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in respiratory and digestive health. Saponins exhibit antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting its role in herbal medicine.

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of Loquat (Eriobotrya japonica) contains volatile compounds like limonene and cineole. These oils are used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties. They are often extracted through distillation and applied in respiratory treatments. The oil's aromatic profile also supports its use in aromatherapy.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Loquat (Eriobotrya japonica). It contributes to the fruit's vibrant color and has antioxidant properties. Found in the skin and pulp, anthocyanin supports respiratory and digestive health in traditional herbal medicine. It is used to soothe coughs and reduce inflammation.

10. Alkaloid

Loquat (Eriobotrya japonica) contains the alkaloid pseudoephedrine. This compound is known for its decongestant properties and is used in traditional herbal medicine to relieve nasal congestion. Pseudoephedrine is derived from the plant's leaves and is often combined with other compounds for enhanced therapeutic effects.
