Loquat (Eriobotrya japonica) Uses

Loquat is used to treat cough, bronchitis, and respiratory infections by soothing mucous membrane irritation. It may also help with asthma and allergic rhinitis due to its expectorant properties. The fruit and its derivatives are commonly used to alleviate cold symptoms and support recovery from influenza or tonsillitis.

Loquat (Eriobotrya japonica) Monograph

1. Cough

Loquat is used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat coughs, particularly dry, irritating coughs. Its expectorant properties help loosen mucus, while its anti-inflammatory effects soothe the respiratory tract. It is often prepared as a syrup or decoction to ease persistent coughing and improve breathing.

2. Bronchitis

Loquat fruit is used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat bronchitis due to its expectorant properties. It helps loosen mucus and reduce coughing. The fruit's high content of vitamin C and antioxidants supports respiratory health. It is often prepared as a syrup or decoction for therapeutic use.

3. Common cold

Loquat is used in traditional Chinese medicine to help alleviate symptoms of the common cold. It is believed to clear heat and reduce phlegm, making it effective for coughs and sore throats associated with colds. The fruit and its extracts are often prepared as teas or syrups to support respiratory health during colds.

4. Cold symptoms

Loquat is used in traditional herbal medicine to alleviate cold symptoms such as coughing, sore throat, and fever. Its expectorant properties help loosen mucus, while its antipyretic effects reduce fever. It is often prepared as a syrup or decoction to support respiratory health during colds.

5. Respiratory infections

Loquat is used to treat respiratory infections due to its antiviral and antibacterial properties. It helps reduce inflammation in the respiratory tract and soothe coughing. Traditionally, it is prepared as a syrup or decoction to alleviate symptoms of colds, bronchitis, and sore throat. Its expectorant effects aid in clearing mucus from the lungs.

6. Mucous membrane irritation

Loquat is used to soothe mucous membrane irritation due to its demulcent properties. The fruit's mucilage forms a protective layer over inflamed tissues, reducing discomfort. It is commonly employed for sore throats, coughs, and irritated respiratory tracts. Its mild nature makes it suitable for long-term use in herbal remedies.

7. Asthma

Loquat fruit has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to help alleviate asthma symptoms. It is believed to act as an expectorant, helping to loosen mucus in the airways. The fruit's anti-inflammatory properties may reduce bronchial irritation, supporting respiratory health. It is often prepared as a syrup or tea to ease breathing in individuals with asthma.

8. Allergic rhinitis

Loquat leaf and fruit are used in traditional Chinese medicine to alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis. They help reduce nasal congestion and inflammation due to their anti-inflammatory and antihistaminic properties. Loquat is often prepared as a decoction or syrup to support respiratory health during allergy seasons.

9. Influenza

Loquat leaves and fruit are used in traditional Chinese medicine to help alleviate symptoms of influenza. They are valued for their antiviral and expectorant properties, which can reduce fever, soothe coughing, and ease respiratory discomfort. Loquat is often prepared as a tea or decoction to support the body's recovery during viral infections.

10. Tonsillitis

Loquat is used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat tonsillitis due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce swelling and soothe throat pain. The leaves and fruits are commonly prepared as decoctions or teas. It is often combined with other herbs to enhance its therapeutic effects.
