Loquat (Eriobotrya japonica) Actions

Loquat is a tonic that strengthens the body and supports overall health. It has anti-inflammatory properties that reduce swelling and irritation. As a carminative, it helps relieve gas and bloating. It also acts as an expectorant, aiding in the removal of mucus from the respiratory tract.

Loquat (Eriobotrya japonica) Monograph

1. Tonic

Loquat has a tonic action, enhancing vitality and supporting the digestive system. It is used to strengthen the spleen and lungs, aiding in digestion and respiratory health. Its tonic properties help restore energy and balance in the body. It is commonly used in traditional formulas to boost overall health and immunity.

2. Anti-inflammatory

Loquat has an anti-inflammatory action, primarily due to its high content of bioactive compounds like triterpenoids and flavonoids. These compounds inhibit inflammatory pathways and reduce the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines. This makes loquat useful in managing conditions like respiratory inflammation and skin disorders.

3. Carminative

Loquat has a carminative action, helping to relieve gas and bloating in the digestive system. It promotes smooth muscle movement in the intestines, aiding in the expulsion of trapped air and reducing discomfort. This property makes it beneficial for digestive ailments characterized by excessive gas.

4. Expectorant

Loquat has an expectorant action, helping to loosen and expel mucus from the respiratory tract. It is commonly used for conditions like coughs and bronchitis. Its active compounds stimulate the secretion of bronchial mucus, making it easier to cough up. This effect supports respiratory health and aids in clearing congestion.

5. Bitter

Loquat has a bitter action, which stimulates digestion and clears heat from the body. This property helps to reduce inflammation and promote the elimination of toxins. It is particularly effective in treating conditions like coughs and sore throats. The bitter taste also aids in improving appetite and metabolic function.

6. Sedative

Loquat has a sedative action, promoting relaxation and reducing anxiety. It is used to calm the nervous system, aiding sleep and easing restlessness. Its mild calming effect makes it suitable for mild stress and insomnia support.

7. Antispasmodic

Loquat has an antispasmodic action, helping to relieve smooth muscle spasms in the respiratory and gastrointestinal tracts. This effect is attributed to its bioactive compounds, which reduce excessive nerve stimulation and muscle contractions. It is commonly used in traditional medicine to treat coughing and bronchial spasms.

8. Stimulant

Loquat has a stimulant action, enhancing mental alertness and physical energy. It is used to invigorate the body and mind, particularly during periods of fatigue or lethargy. This effect is attributed to its ability to promote circulation and metabolic activity. It is often recommended in traditional medicine for its revitalizing properties.

9. Diuretic

Loquat has a diuretic action, promoting the elimination of excess fluid from the body through increased urine production. This effect helps reduce swelling and supports kidney function. It is commonly used in traditional medicine to address conditions involving fluid retention. The diuretic properties are attributed to its bioactive compounds.

10. Astringent

Loquat has an astringent action, which helps reduce secretions and tighten tissues. This property makes it useful for treating respiratory conditions like coughs and excessive mucus. It also supports digestive health by firming up stools. Its astringent effect is due to compounds like tannins present in the fruit.
