10 Possible Side Effects Of Eriobotrya Japonica

Eriobotrya japonica has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal upset, nausea, and diarrhea due to its high fiber and saponin content.

If consumed in excess, it can also cause stomach pain, bloating, and gas.

These side effects can worsen the quality of life for individuals, particularly those with pre-existing gastrointestinal conditions, by restricting their diet and causing discomfort during daily activities.

1. Causes stomach upset

Eriobotrya japonica causes stomach upset in some individuals.

This is due to its high concentration of anthraquinones, which can stimulate the bowel movements and increase stool frequency, leading to loose stools or diarrhea.

Additionally, the plant's flavonoids may also contribute to stomach discomfort by altering the gut flora and increasing the production of gastrointestinal enzymes, resulting in nausea and bloating.

2. Increases blood pressure

Eriobotrya japonica increases blood pressure due to its rich source of antioxidants and flavonoids.

These compounds can stimulate the production of nitric oxide in the body, causing blood vessels to constrict and increasing blood pressure.

Additionally, loquat's high levels of potassium and magnesium can further exacerbate this effect by altering electrolyte balances in the body.

3. Triggers allergic reactions

Eriobotrya japonica triggers allergic reactions in some individuals.

The allergenic properties are attributed to the presence of urushiol, a chemical compound found in the plant's sap, leaves, and fruit.

When introduced into the body, this substance can cause an immune response, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and respiratory issues.

4. Induces vomiting

Eriobotrya japonica induces vomiting due to its high concentration of limonoids and flavonoids, which can stimulate the digestive system and increase stomach contractions.

The plant's volatile oils may also irritate the mucous membranes in the mouth, throat, and stomach, leading to nausea and vomiting.

Additionally, some individuals may be allergic to certain compounds found in Eriobotrya japonica, exacerbating these gastrointestinal symptoms.

5. Affects liver function

Eriobotrya japonica affects liver function due to its high content of flavonoids and phenolic acids.

These compounds can interact with the liver's detoxification processes, potentially leading to changes in liver enzyme activity and altered liver function.

The exact mechanisms are not fully understood, but it is thought that the phytochemicals in Eriobotrya japonica may induce a stress response in the liver cells, resulting in altered expression of genes involved in detoxification pathways.

6. Interferes with medications

Eriobotrya japonica interferes with medications by altering their absorption and metabolism.

This can occur due to its high levels of polyphenolic compounds, flavonoids, and glycosides, which may bind to proteins in the liver and intestine, affecting drug clearance.

Additionally, loquat's ability to increase gastric emptying and alter gut motility may also contribute to its potential to interact with medications.

7. Produces skin rashes

Eriobotrya japonica produces skin rashes due to its allergenic properties.

The plant contains flavonoids and terpenes that can cause an allergic reaction in some individuals, leading to the development of skin rashes. Additionally, the presence of urushiol, a potent allergen found in the fruit and leaves of the plant, can exacerbate this reaction.

As a result, people may experience skin irritation, redness, and inflammation after coming into contact with Eriobotrya japonica or using its products.

8. Enhances bleeding risk

Eriobotrya japonica enhances bleeding risk due to its ability to inhibit platelet aggregation and prolong blood clotting time.

The flavonoids present in the fruit and leaves of Eriobotrya japonica have been shown to reduce platelet activity, thereby increasing the likelihood of bleeding episodes.

Additionally, the herb's potential to decrease blood pressure and alter blood vessel function may also contribute to its effect on coagulation, making it essential to monitor for signs of bleeding when using Eriobotrya japonica.

9. Raises glucose levels

Eriobotrya japonica raises glucose levels due to its high content of fructose and sorbitol, which are naturally occurring sugars.

These sugars are not fully absorbed by the body, causing an increase in blood sugar levels.

Additionally, Eriobotrya japonica contains a compound called epicatechin, which has been shown to inhibit the activity of enzymes that break down glucose, further contributing to elevated glucose levels.

10. Stimulates heart palpitations

Eriobotrya japonica stimulates heart palpitations.

This side effect is attributed to the presence of certain compounds, such as quercetin and kaempferol, which are responsible for its ability to stimulate cardiac activity. These flavonoids can increase heart rate and blood pressure, leading to an irregular heartbeat or palpitations in some individuals.

Additionally, loquat's potential interaction with medications that also affect the cardiovascular system may exacerbate this effect.
