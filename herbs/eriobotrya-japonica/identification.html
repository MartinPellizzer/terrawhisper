Botanical Identification of Loquat (Eriobotrya japonica)

To identify Loquat (Eriobotrya japonica) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Loquat, or Eriobotrya japonica, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Rosales, family Rosaceae, genus Eriobotrya, and species Eriobotrya japonica. These classifications help identify its botanical relationships and place it within the broader context of plant life.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Loquat is Eriobotrya japonica, first described by Linnaeus in 1761. It has several synonyms, including Pyrus japonica and Eriobotrya malifolia. Common names include Japanese white plum and Chinese white plum, while regional names vary across cultures. Historically, it was known as the "Japanese plum" in early botanical literature.

Morphological Characteristics

Loquat is a small to medium-sized tree with a rounded crown. Its root system is shallow and fibrous, adapting to various soil types. The stem is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming more woody with age. Leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical with a pointed tip. Inflorescences are axillary clusters, bearing small, white, five-petaled flowers. The fruit is a yellow or orange berry with a rough skin, containing one or two seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Loquat can be distinguished by its smooth, reddish bark on older stems, opposite leaves with a waxy upper surface, and yellow-orange flowers with five petals. The fruit is typically oval, yellow with a red blush, and has a sweet-sour flavor. It differs from similar species like white loquat by its more pronounced fruit color and distinct leaf texture.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Loquat is native to China, Japan, and Korea. It thrives in temperate climates with mild winters and warm summers. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils. It is commonly found at altitudes between 200 and 800 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Loquat trees typically bloom in late winter to early spring, around February to March in temperate regions. Seed formation occurs in the summer following pollination, usually between June and August. Harvesting takes place in late autumn, from October to November, when the fruit is fully ripe and ready for identification and use in herbal medicine.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Loquat belongs to the genus Eriobotrya within the family Rosaceae. Its closest relatives include Eriobotrya deflexa and Eriobotrya lycioides. It shares key family traits such as simple leaves and aggregate fruits, distinguishing it from other genera in the Rosaceae family.
