Corydalis parviflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Corydalis parviflora?

Corydalis parviflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further categorized under the order Ranunculales. It is a member of the family Papaveraceae, specifically situated within the genus Corydalis, and is identified by the specific epithet parviflora.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Corydalis parviflora contains a diverse array of isoquinoline alkaloids, most notably protopine, corydaline, and various derivatives of benzylisoquinoline. These bioactive compounds are primarily concentrated within the rhizomes of the plant. Many of these specific alkaloids exhibit significant pharmacological properties, including analgesic, sedative, and antitussive effects. Researchers study these chemical constituents to understand their potential for treating neurological disorders and inflammatory conditions. The precise chemical structure of these alkaloids allows them to interact with specific neurotransmitter receptors in the human body.

What are the therapeutic actions of Corydalis parviflora?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various conditions including menstrual irregularities, abdominal pain, and various forms of stagnation in the blood or qi. It is frequently utilized in traditional medicine to alleviate symptoms associated with gynecological disorders and uterine issues. The plant's properties are often applied to address digestive ailments characterized by sharp or spasmodic pain. Additionally, practitioners use it to help resolve localized swelling and discomfort caused by trauma or circulatory blockages.
