Gueldenstaedtia multiflora

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gueldenstaedtia multiflora has woody, deciduous shrubs that typically grow to a height of 1 to 3 meters and feature oppositely arranged, ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is characterized by a bright green color and a texture that remains relatively smooth throughout the growing season. During its blooming period, the plant produces dense, terminal clusters of small, white, five-petaled flowers that emerge from the branch tips. These fragrant blossoms are often accompanied by small, globose fruit capsules that turn brown as they mature. The overall structure of the plant is characterized by an upright and somewhat spreading growth habit.
