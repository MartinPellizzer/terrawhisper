What's the taxonomical classification of Gueldenstaedtia multiflora?

Gueldenstaedtia multiflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Fabales and is a member of the family Fabaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identification is defined by the genus Gueldenstaedtia and the species multiflora.

Gueldenstaedtia multiflora has woody, deciduous shrubs that typically grow to a height of 1 to 3 meters and feature oppositely arranged, ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated margins. During its blooming period, the plant produces dense, terminal clusters of small, white, five-petaled flowers that emerge from the branch tips. These fragrant blossoms are often accompanied by small, globose fruit capsules that turn brown as they mature.
