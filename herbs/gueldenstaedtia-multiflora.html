What's the taxonomical classification of Gueldenstaedtia multiflora?

Gueldenstaedtia multiflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Fabales and is a member of the family Fabaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identification is defined by the genus Gueldenstaedtia and the species multiflora.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Gueldenstaedtia multiflora can cause allergic skin reactions such as contact dermatitis, redness, and itching if an individual has a hypersensitivity to its pollen or sap. While the plant is primarily used for ornamental purposes, its heavy fragrance can trigger respiratory irritation or headaches in sensitive people. Some individuals may experience sneezing or watery eyes due to the airborne particles released during blooming periods. There is limited clinical research regarding the internal toxicity of this species, so ingestion should be strictly avoided to prevent unknown gastrointestinal distress. Always exercise caution when handling the plant if you have a history of environmental allergies.
