What's the taxonomical classification of Berchemia huana?

Berchemia huaana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, eventually placing it in the order Rosales. As a member of the family Rhamnaceae, it is further categorized under the genus Berchemia, with the specific designation of the species huaana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Berchemia huana has deciduous, lanceolate to ovate leaves with serrated margins, woody stems, and small, inconspicuous flowers that produce fleshy, globose drupes. Its woody structure consists of slender branches that can develop a slightly rough texture as the plant matures. The flowers are small and often appear in clusters, transitioning into small fruits that change color during ripening.
