Jasminum nervosum

What's the taxonomical classification of Jasminum nervosum?

Jasminum nervosum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Lamiales. This plant is a member of the family Oleaceae and is specifically categorized within the genus Jasminum, ultimately reaching its unique designation at the species level as Jasminum nervosum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Jasminum nervosum has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that feature prominent, deeply impressed pinnate venation and entire margins. The foliage typically grows in a decussate arrangement along slender, woody stems. Small, tubular white flowers emerge from the axils, often possessing a delicate fragrance. These blossoms are characterized by a five-lobed corolla that transitions into a narrow tube. The plant structure remains primarily shrubby or climbing, depending on the environmental support available.
