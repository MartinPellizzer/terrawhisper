Stevia (Stevia rebaudiana)

Stevia (Stevia rebaudiana) is a member of the Asteraceae family, native to Paraguay, Brazil, and Argentina. Traditionally, its leaves, stems, and flowers have been used for infusions, culinary uses, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its tonic, anti-inflammatory, and bitter actions, and has a long history of use in japanese kampo medicine, traditional chinese medicine, and ayurvedic medicine.
