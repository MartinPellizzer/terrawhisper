Stevia Rebaudiana Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Stevia rebaudiana, commonly known as the sweet leaf plant, is a perennial shrub native to Paraguay and Brazil, belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is renowned for its intense natural sweetness, derived from its bioactive constituents, such as stevioside and rebaudioside A, which are glycosides responsible for its sweet taste and potential health benefits.

Stevia rebaudiana is considered a natural sweetener and has been used for centuries for its hypoglycemic, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties.

It can be prepared as an infusion, tincture, or powder, making it versatile for use in teas, cooking, and herbal remedies.

Medicinal Parts

Stevia rebaudiana leaf is the primary medicinal part of the plant, known for its intense sweetness and various health benefits.

The main herbal preparations made from the leaves include stevia extract, which is commonly used as a natural sweetener, and herbal teas made by steeping the dried leaves in hot water.
