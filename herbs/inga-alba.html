White inga

Inga alba

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

White Inga, scientifically known as Inga alba, belongs to the Fabaceae family. It is also called "White Bean Tree" in some regions and is a fast-growing perennial shrub native to Central and South America. The plant has compound leaves, white flowers, and a shallow root system. It is sometimes referred to as "Inga alba" or "White Inga" in herbal texts.

Active Compounds

The main active compounds of White Inga include flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to provide antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting its use in herbal medicine. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential.

  • Flavonoid
  • Tannin
  • Phenolic acid

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action
Respiratory Respiratory infections, inflammation, cough, bronchitis Antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, expectorant, bronchodilator, antiviral
Integumentary Skin infections, wound healing, skin irritation, wounds, fungal infection Antimicrobial, antioxidant, antiseptic, antifungal, anti-inflammatory
Immune Allergies, fever, malaria Antihistaminic, antipyretic, antimicrobial

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory conditions.

Infusion: Used for mild respiratory and digestive complaints.

Powder: Used for digestive health and as a general tonic.

Safety Profile

White Inga is generally considered safe when used as directed, but it may cause mild gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to limited safety data. Caution is advised for people with allergies to legumes or related plants.
