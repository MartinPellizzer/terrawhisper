White Inga (Inga alba)

White Inga (Inga alba) is a member of the Fabaceae family, native to Amazon Basin, Central America, and Northern South America. Traditionally, its leaves, bark, and stems have been used for decoctions, infusions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, diuretic, and bitter actions, and has a long history of use in african traditional medicine, native american herbal medicine, and traditional chinese medicine.
