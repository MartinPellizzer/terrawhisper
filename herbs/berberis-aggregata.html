Berberis aggregata

What's the taxonomical classification of Berberis aggregata?

Berberis aggregata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Ranunculales. This plant is a representative of the family Berberidaceae, specifically situated within the genus Berberis, and is identified by its unique species designation, aggregata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Berberis aggregata has a deciduous shrub habit characterized by arching branches, small ovate leaves with serrated margins, and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant typically features spinescent branchlets that protect its dense foliage. Each leaf is arranged alternately along the stems and possesses a distinctively textured surface. During the flowering season, the small, petaloid structures emerge in tight bunches from the leaf axils. Following pollination, the plant develops small, fleshy berries that serve as a key identification feature.
