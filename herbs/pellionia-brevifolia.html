What's the taxonomical classification of Pellionia brevifolia?

Pellioniia brevifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Rosales. The plant is a member of the family Urticaceae, specifically categorized within the genus Pellioniia, with the species designation brevilifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pellioniia brevifolia has small, opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are typically only a few centimeters in length with entire margins.

The plant exhibits a creeping or prostrate growth habit, often forming dense mats across the forest floor. Its stems are slender and herbaceous, sometimes rooting at the nodes to facilitate spreading. The flowers are relatively inconspicuous and emerge from the leaf axils in a characteristic arrangement.

This species is distinguished from its relatives by the notably reduced size of its leaf blades.
