Denseflower Lousewort

Pedicularis densiflora

Denseflower Lousewort (Pedicularis densiflora) is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia, known for its traditional use in treating respiratory conditions such as asthma and bronchitis.

Botanical Identification

Pedicularis densiflora, commonly known as Denseflower Lousewort, belongs to the family Orobanchaceae. It is a perennial herb native to alpine regions of western North America. The plant has fleshy roots, oval leaves, and dense clusters of purple flowers. It is also referred to as "Lousewort" in some herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Lamiales
  • Order: Scrophulariales
  • Family: Scrophulariaceae
  • Genus: Pedicularis
  • Species: Pedicularis densiflora

Safety Profile

Denseflower Lousewort is contraindicated during pregnancy and lactation due to potential toxic effects on the fetus and infant. It should be avoided by individuals with hypersensitivity to plants in the Orobanchaceae family. Always consult a healthcare professional before use.
