Denseflower Lousewort (Pedicularis densiflora)

Denseflower Lousewort (Pedicularis densiflora) is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family, native to China, Russia, and Central Asia. Traditionally, its leaves, flowers, and stems have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its bitter, tonic, and anti-inflammatory actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, traditional chinese medicine, and japanese kampo medicine.

Flowers
Inflorescence consisting of dense clusters of small zygomorphic flowers with five fused petals, two of which are bilabiate, and stamen numbers varying but typically four, with a single superior ovary.
Stems
Erect, unbranched, hairy, with a square cross-section and prominent ridges.
