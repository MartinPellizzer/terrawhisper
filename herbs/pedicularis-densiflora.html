What is Denseflower Lousewort (Pedicularis Densiflora)?

Denseflower Lousewort (Pedicularis densiflora) is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern China, characterized by its dense clusters of pink to purple flowers and its use in traditional Chinese herbal medicine for its purported ability to clear heat, detoxify the body, and promote circulation.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Denseflower Lousewort is Pedicularis densiflora. This plant belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Lamiales, order Scrophulariales, family Scrophulariaceae, genus Pedicularis, and species Pedicularis densiflora. It is commonly known as Denseflower Lousewort in North America and as Denseflower Betony in parts of Europe. Synonyms for this plant include Pedicularis densiflora var. densiflora and regional names such as Lousewort in some areas of the British Isles.

The root system consists of a thick, fleshy rhizome that branches into numerous fibrous roots.
