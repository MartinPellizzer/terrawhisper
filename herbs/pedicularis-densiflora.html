Denseflower Lousewort (Pedicularis densiflora) is a perennial herb with a slender, branched stem that grows up to 30 cm tall, bearing dense clusters of small, tubular flowers with bright red petals and yellow anthers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

Distribution

Denseflower Lousewort (Pedicularis densiflora) is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China, including provinces such as Sichuan, Yunnan, and Guizhou, where it thrives in moist, shaded habitats at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,500 meters.

Denseflower Lousewort has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat respiratory ailments, including coughs and bronchitis, due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties.
