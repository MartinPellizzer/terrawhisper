Amaranthus lividus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized as Equisetopsida, which falls under the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Caryophyllales and the family Amaranthaceae. Finally, the plant is placed within the genus Amaranthus, specifically identifying as the species lividus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Amaranthus lividus has an erect, branched herbaceous stem that typically reaches heights of 30 to 60 centimeters and features ovate to lanceolate leaves with entire margins. The foliage consists of green leaves that often exhibit reddish or purplish tints on the undersides or stems. Its inflorescence is composed of dense, terminal clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or slightly reddish in color. These flowers are arranged in panicles and lack prominent petals, which is characteristic of the Amaranthaceae family. The plant also produces numerous small, shiny black seeds that are dispersed upon maturity.

Consequently, Amaranthus lividus serves as a valuable natural source for developing therapeutic agents.

As discussed in a study, A. lividus possesses medicinal properties including a neuroprotective effect against toxicity caused by advanced glycation end-products (AGEs). Preincubation of SH-SY5Y cells with petroleum ether, dichloromethane, or methanol extracts of A. lividus dose-dependently attenuated the neuron toxicity induced by AGEs treatment. The extracts of A. lividus significantly downregulated the gene expression of the pro-inflammatory cytokines TNF-α, IL-1, and IL-6 genes in AGEs-induced cells. These findings demonstrate that A. lividus has anti-inflammatory activity by reducing the expression of these specific cytokine genes. This suggests that the plant may be useful for treating chronic inflammation associated with neurodegenerative disorders.
