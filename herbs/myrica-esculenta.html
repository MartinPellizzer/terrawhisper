Blackthorn (Myrica esculenta)

Blackthorn (Myrica esculenta) is a member of the Myricaceae family, native to Southeast Asia, East Asia, and South Asia. Traditionally, its fruits, leaves, and bark have been used for decoctions, infusions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, astringent, and diuretic actions, and has a long history of use in ayurvedic medicine, african traditional medicine, and traditional chinese medicine.

Perennial herbaceous plant.

Leaves
Lateral leaves with acute tips, dark green upper surface, pale green lower surface, and distinct stomatal bands along midribs.
Flowers
Small, solitary flowers with five white petals, five yellow stamens, and a superior ovary with two styles, arranged in an umbel, actinomorphic symmetry, with no distinct markings or specialized structures.
Stems
Woody, erect, branched, with smooth, thorn-bearing surface and opposite leaf scars.
