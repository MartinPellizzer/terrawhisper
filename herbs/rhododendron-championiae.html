Rhododendron championiae

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhododendron championiae has elliptical to oblong leaves that are dark green, leathery, and typically measure between five and twelve centimeters in length. The leaf margins are entire and the undersides often exhibit a slightly paler coloration compared to the upper surface. Its flowers are funnel-shaped and range in color from pale pink to deep rose, often featuring distinctive darker spots within the throat. The plant develops as a woody shrub with a spreading growth habit and sturdy branching structures. These floral clusters emerge from terminal buds during the blooming season to create dense displays.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the high-altitude regions of the Eastern Himalayas, specifically found within the state of Sikkim in India. It typically grows in the temperate forest zones located at elevations between 2,500 and 3,500 meters. The species thrives in moist, acidic soils often found along rocky slopes and near mountain streams. Its distribution is limited to these specific montane environments where cool temperatures and heavy seasonal rainfall occur. Consequently, this plant occupies a very narrow ecological niche within its mountainous habitat.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Rhododendron championiae can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, neurological impairment, and potentially fatal cardiac arrhythmias if ingested due to the presence of toxic grayanotoxins. These toxins act by binding to voltage-gated sodium channels in cell membranes, preventing them from closing properly. This interference disrupts normal nerve and muscle function throughout the body. Common symptoms often include nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and low blood pressure. In more extreme cases, patients may experience dizziness, confusion, or irregular heartbeats that require immediate medical intervention.
