What's the taxonomical classification of Rhododendron championiae?

Rhododendron championiae belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent, it falls under the order Ericales and is a member of the family Ericaceae. Finally, it is categorized within the genus Rhododendron, specifically identified as the species championiae.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhododendron championiae has elliptical to oblong leaves that are dark green, leathery, and typically measure between five and twelve centimeters in length. The leaf margins are entire and the undersides often exhibit a slightly paler coloration compared to the upper surface. Its flowers are funnel-shaped and range in color from pale pink to deep rose, often featuring distinctive darker spots within the throat. The plant develops as a woody shrub with a spreading growth habit and sturdy branching structures. These floral clusters emerge from terminal buds during the blooming season to create dense displays.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the high-altitude regions of the Eastern Himalayas, specifically found within the state of Sikkim in India. The species thrives in moist, acidic soils often found along rocky slopes and near mountain streams.       What side effects this plant can have?

      This plant can cause severe toxicity involving nausea, vomiting, dizziness, cardiac arrhythmias, confusion, and seizures due to the presence of grayanotoxins. Once ingested, these toxins interfere with sodium channels in cell membranes, leading to dangerous disruptions in heart rhythm. Gastrointestinal distress such as nausea and vomiting often occurs shortly after consumption. Neurological symptoms like confusion and dizziness may follow as the toxins affect the central nervous system. In extreme cases, the physiological impact can escalate to life-threatening seizures.
