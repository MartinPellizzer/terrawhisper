What's the taxonomical classification of Veratrum schindleri?

Veratrum schindleri belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, falling under the order Liliales. It is a member of the family Melanthiaceae and is specifically categorized under the genus Veratrum, with its unique species designation being schindleri.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Veratrum schindleri has rhizomatous roots, erect stems reaching up to 1 meter in height, and large, lanceolate leaves with prominent parallel venation. The foliage is typically dark green and grows in a basal rosette before extending upward along the stem. Its inflorescence consists of a terminal panicle bearing numerous small, greenish-white to cream-colored flowers. These flowers feature six tepals and distinct stamens characteristic of the Melanthiaceae family. The plant's overall structure is robust and herbaceous, suited for its specific montane habitat.
