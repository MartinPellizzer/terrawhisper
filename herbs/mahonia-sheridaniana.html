Mahonia sheridaniana

What's the taxonomical classification of Mahonia sheridaniana?

Mahonia sheridaniana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Ranunculales. This plant is a member of the family Berberidaceae, which contains the genus Mahonia, and is specifically identified by its species name, sheridaniana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Mahonia sheridaniana has large, leathery, pinnately compound leaves composed of several pairs of stiff, spiny-toothed leaflets that typically measure several inches in length. These evergreen leaves exhibit a deep green color and a glossy texture, often appearing in dense, shrubby clusters. During the flowering season, the plant produces bright yellow, fragrant blossoms that emerge from the terminal ends of the branches. Following the bloom, small, dark blue to purple berries develop, providing a distinct visual contrast to the foliage. The overall growth habit is upright and spreading, forming a robust architectural presence in a landscape.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the coastal regions of the Pacific Northwest, specifically ranging from southern British Columbia in Canada through Washington and Oregon to northern California in the United States.
