Pilea longicaulis

What's the taxonomical classification of Pilea longicaulis?

Pilea longicaulis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Rosales. This plant is a constituent of the family Urticaceae, which is further refined into the genus Pilea, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species longicaulis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pilea longicaulis has slender, creeping stems that produce ovate to elliptical leaves with finely serrated margins and prominent venation. The foliage typically displays a soft, slightly textured surface and can range in color from bright green to deeper shades depending on light exposure. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced on delicate inflorescences that emerge from the leaf axils. The plant's growth habit is characterized by its trailing or spreading stems, which allow it to cover ground effectively. This morphology makes it well-suited for growing in shaded, humid environments where its delicate structure can thrive.
