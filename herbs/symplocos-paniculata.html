Symplocos paniculata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is part of the order Ericales and falls under the family Sympclocaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Symplocos and the specific species epithet paniculata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Symplocos paniculata has simple, alternate, elliptical to oblong leaves that are typically 5 to 15 centimeters long with entire margins and an acute apex.

The foliage is characterized by a coriaceous texture and a shiny green upper surface. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in terminal panicles, which serve as the namesake for the species. These flowers are followed by small, globose drupes that transition in color as they mature.

The plant overall exhibits a woody structure consistent with its classification in the Symplocaceae family.
