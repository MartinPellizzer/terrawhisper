Callicarpa longifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Callicarpa longifolia?

Callicarpa longifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows a lineage that places it under the order Lamiales. This specific plant is a part of the family Lamiaceae, and its taxonomic identity is further defined by its placement in the genus Callicarpa, with its unique designation as the species longifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Callicarpa longifolia has elongated, lanceolate leaves that measure between 10 and 25 centimeters in length and are arranged in opposite pairs along its woody stems. The leaf margins are finely serrated, while the upper surfaces exhibit a smooth, lustrous texture. Small, tubular flowers emerge in dense, axillary clusters that form around the stem during the blooming season. These floral structures eventually give way to distinctive, spherical drupes that turn a vibrant purple color as they ripen. The overall architecture of the plant consists of upright, branching shrubs that can reach significant heights in their natural habitat.
