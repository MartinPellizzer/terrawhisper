Jacobaea raphanifolia

Jacobaea raphanifolia, also known as Jacobaea or Wild Cabbage, is a plant species within the daisy family, Asteraceae, native to South Africa.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which can help to soothe skin irritations and promote wound healing.

The plant's leaves and stems can be used to treat various health issues, including fever reduction, digestive problems, and respiratory infections, thanks to its antiviral and antifungal properties.

Jacobaea raphanifolia can be prepared into herbal teas, infusions, or tinctures, making it a versatile and accessible remedy for various health benefits.
