Oberonia ensiformis

What's the taxonomical classification of Oberonia ensiformis?

Oberonia ensiformis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further classified under the order Asparagales. It is a member of the family Orchidaceae, falling within the genus Oberonia, and is specifically identified by the species name ensiformis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

What are the pharmacological activities of Oberonia ensiformis?

This plant has antimicrobial, antioxidant, and α-glucosidase-inhibitory activities that demonstrate its significant therapeutic potential. The presence of bioactive compounds allows it to effectively combat various pathogenic microorganisms through its antimicrobial properties. Furthermore, its antioxidant capacity helps neutralize free radicals, thereby protecting cells from oxidative stress. The plant also exhibits α-glucosidase-inhibitory activity, which suggests its utility in managing blood glucose levels. These diverse pharmacological profiles highlight the importance of investigating this species for medicinal development.

As discussed in a study, seven previously undescribed prenylated p-hydroxybenzoic acid derivatives known as oberoniaensiformisins A-G were isolated from an EtOH extract of the whole plant Oberonia ensiformis. These isolated compounds were tested for their antimicrobial, antioxidant, and α-glucosidase-inhibitory activity. Among these derivatives, compounds 6 and 12 exhibited potential antioxidant activity. Furthermore, compounds 5, 6, 12, 13, and 15 showed varying degrees of α-glucosidase-inhibitory activity. The IC50 values for these specific compounds ranged from 34.03 to 106.10 μg/mL.
