Oberonia ensiformis

What's the taxonomical classification of Oberonia ensiformis?

Oberonia ensiformis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further classified under the order Asparagales. It is a member of the family Orchidaceae, falling within the genus Oberonia, and is specifically identified by the species name ensiformis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Oberonia ensiformis has small, succulent, linear leaves that are arranged in a dense, distichous manner along a creeping stem.

These leaves are typically narrow and pointed, resembling small blades. The plant produces numerous tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered on long, slender inflorescences. Each individual flower is characterized by minute petals and a complex lip structure typical of the genus.

The overall morphology is adapted for an epiphytic lifestyle, allowing it to cling to bark and absorb moisture efficiently.
