Citrus hongheensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Citrus hongheensis?

Citrus hongheenensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further categorized into the order Sapindales. It is a member of the family Rutaceae, falling under the genus Citrus, and is specifically identified by its species name, hongheenensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Citrus hongheenensis has small, ovate leaves with serrated margins, woody stems, and white flowers that produce small, globose orange fruits. The foliage is typically dark green and glossy, providing a dense canopy for the shrub. Its branches are somewhat spreading and can develop small thorns depending on the age of the specimen. The flowers are fragrant and emerge in clusters during the spring season. Once the fruit matures, it reaches a diameter of approximately two centimeters and possesses a thin rind.

These areas offer the humid, frost-free environments necessary for its survival in a temperate zone.
