Hypericum seniawinii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hypericum seniawinii has erect, woody stems that branch out to support small, opposite, elliptical leaves with entire margins. The foliage is typically characterized by a dense arrangement of these leaves, which often feature translucent glandular dots on their surfaces. Small, yellow flowers emerge from the terminal ends of the branches, showcasing five distinct petals and numerous prominent stamens. These floral structures are surrounded by inconspicuous sepals that protect the developing buds. The overall architecture of the plant reflects a compact, shrubby growth habit common to many species within its genus.
