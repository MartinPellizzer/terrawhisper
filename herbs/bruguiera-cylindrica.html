Bruguiera cylindrica

What's the taxonomical classification of Bruguiera cylindrica?

Bruguiera cylindrica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Malpighiales. The plant is further categorized under the family Rhizophoraceae, which places it within the genus Bruguiera. Ultimately, its specific identity is defined by its species designation, cylindrica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bruguiera cylindrica has cylindrical, woody, and elongated fruit capsules that measure approximately 10 to 15 centimeters in length and possess a distinct longitudinal shape. These fruits are characterized by their thick, leathery walls and often feature a brownish or yellowish exterior texture. The plant itself is a mangrove shrub or small tree with elliptical to oblong leaves that are typically arranged in an opposite pattern. Its root system often includes specialized structures typical of mangrove species to facilitate stability in tidal environments. The overall morphology is adapted specifically to thrive in intertidal zones and saline conditions.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical coastal regions of West and Central Africa, ranging from Senegal to Angola and extending into parts of East Africa. It primarily inhabits mangrove swamps and estuarine environments along the Atlantic and Indian Ocean coastlines. The species thrives in brackish water zones where tidal influences are constant. Its distribution is closely tied to the availability of specific intertidal mudflats. These habitats allow it to colonize the edges of river deltas and sheltered coastal lagoons.
