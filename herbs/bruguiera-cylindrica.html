Bruguiera cylindrica

What's the taxonomical classification of Bruguiera cylindrica?

Bruguiera cylindrica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Malpighiales. The plant is further categorized under the family Rhizophoraceae, which places it within the genus Bruguiera. Ultimately, its specific identity is defined by its species designation, cylindrica.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Bruguiera cylindrica contains various phytochemicals including alkaloids, flavonoids, tannins, and saponins that contribute to its biological activities. These secondary metabolites are often studied for their potential antimicrobial and antioxidant properties. Researchers have focused on the plant's ability to combat oxidative stress through its rich phenolic content. Additionally, certain extracts from the bark and leaves show promise in traditional medicinal practices for treating inflammation. The specific chemical profile of this species makes it a subject of interest for developing new natural therapeutic agents.

What are the therapeutic actions of Bruguiera cylindrica?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, stomach aches, and various inflammatory conditions. Local communities often utilize the bark or leaves to soothe painful swellings and minor cuts. Some traditional practices incorporate the plant to alleviate digestive discomfort and gastrointestinal issues. It is also applied topically to address fungal irritations or rashes on the skin. The medicinal application of this species varies depending on the specific part of the plant harvested.
