Ipomoea involucrata

What's the taxonomical classification of Ipomoea involucrata?

Ipomoea involucrata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Solanales and is a member of the family Convolvulaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Ipomoea and the species involucrata.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Ipomoea involucrata contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, tannins, and terpenoids that contribute to its therapeutic properties. These chemical constituents provide the plant with significant antioxidant and antimicrobial activities used in traditional medicine. Research suggests that certain extracts from the leaves and roots can exhibit anti-inflammatory effects. Some studies also indicate potential analgesic properties due to the specific interaction of these compounds with biological pathways. The diverse chemical profile makes it a subject of interest for developing new pharmacological treatments.

What are the therapeutic actions of Ipomoea involucrata?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, fever, and digestive ailments such as diarrhea or stomach pain. The leaves and roots are often prepared into topical poultices to soothe inflammation or applied as decoctions for internal issues. In various traditional medicine practices, it serves as an agent to reduce body temperature during febrile illnesses. It is also valued for its potential antimicrobial properties that help combat localized bacterial infections. Practitioners frequently rely on these specific botanical parts to manage minor injuries and promote healing.
