Embelia sessiliflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Embelia sessiliflora has small, sessile, greenish-white flowers that lack stalks and grow directly from the leaf axils of its woody, shrubby stems. The leaves are typically elliptical or oblong in shape with entire margins and an acute apex. These leaves are arranged oppositely along the branches, providing a dense canopy for the plant. The plant often exhibits a somewhat spreading growth habit with slender, brownish stems. The inflorescence is characterized by its lack of a pedicel, which is the defining feature of its sessile floral structure.

The use of this plant-based formulation helps restore AChE activity and attenuate oxidative stress.
