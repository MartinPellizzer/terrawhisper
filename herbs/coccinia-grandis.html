African Eggplant (Coccinia grandis)

African Eggplant (Coccinia grandis) is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, native to Southern Africa, Eastern Africa, and Madagascar. Traditionally, its fruits, leaves, and seeds have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, tonic, and diuretic actions, and has a long history of use in african traditional medicine, ayurvedic medicine, and traditional chinese medicine.
