Asarum maximum

What's the taxonomical classification of Asarum maximum?

Asarum maximum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Piperales. The plant is a member of the family Aristolochiaceae, falling under the genus Asarum, with its specific designation being the species maximum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Asarum maximum has a low-growing, rhizomatous perennial habit characterized by thick, creeping underground stems and heart-shaped, evergreen leaves that grow in basal clusters. The leaves are typically dark green with prominent venation and possess a smooth or slightly hairy texture depending on the specific variety. Its flowers are inconspicuous, small, and brownish, emerging from the leaf axils near the ground surface. These unique blooms are often partially hidden by the foliage, which serves to protect the reproductive structures. The plant's overall structure remains compact, rarely exceeding a few inches in height above the soil.

What are the therapeutic actions of Asarum maximum?

Asarum maximum has therapeutic actions that include acting as an expectorant, anthelmintic, stimulant, carminative, emmenagogue, diaphoretic, and bitter. What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, dizziness, seizures, convulsions, hallucinations, confusion, respiratory depression, and liver toxicity. Ingesting the plant may lead to severe gastrointestinal distress through intense stomach cramping and emesis. Neurological complications are a significant risk, potentially manifesting as altered mental states or violent physical spasms. The substance is also capable of depressing the central nervous system to a dangerous degree, affecting breathing patterns. Furthermore, chronic or acute exposure can result in permanent damage to the liver.
