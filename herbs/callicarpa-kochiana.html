Callicarpa kochiana

What's the taxonomical classification of Callicarpa kochiana?

Callicarpa kochiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Lamiales. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the family Lamiaceae, placing it within the genus Callicarpa, and it is specifically identified by the species name kochiana.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, inflammation, and various digestive ailments. Its medicinal properties are derived from the chemical compounds found within its leaves and stems. Local practitioners often apply crushed parts of the plant topically to soothe itching or reduce swelling in skin lesions. When prepared as a decoction, it can be ingested to address internal discomfort or gastrointestinal issues. These traditional uses rely on the plant's natural antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory characteristics.

What are the herbal preparations of Callicarpa kochiana?

Callicarpa kochiana is prepared primarily as a decoction of the leaves, a concentrated infusion of the bark, or a topical poultice made from crushed plant material. These traditional preparations are often used to address various ailments depending on the specific part of the plant utilized. For instance, liquid extracts derived from the bark may be administered orally to manage systemic issues. The leaf infusions are frequently consumed as teas to provide therapeutic benefits through ingestion. Additionally, applying the plant directly to the skin in paste form helps treat localized external conditions.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Callicarpa kochiana can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or digestive distress if the plant is ingested or handled without protection. While many species in the Callicarpa genus are used traditionally, contact with the leaves or berries may trigger dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Ingesting parts of the plant could lead to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. There is also a potential for systemic toxicity if large quantities are consumed by humans or animals. Always consult a professional before using any part of this plant for medicinal purposes.
