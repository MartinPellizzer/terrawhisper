What's the taxonomical classification of Callicarpa kochiana?

Callicarpa kochiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Lamiales. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the family Lamiaceae, placing it within the genus Callicarpa, and it is specifically identified by the species name kochiana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Callicarpa kochiana has oppositely arranged, ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated margins and a distinctly pubescent underside. The stems are woody and often display a fine covering of hairs that can persist into maturity. Small, axillary clusters of flowers emerge from the leaf axils, eventually developing into spherical, fleshy drupes. These fruits typically transition from green to a vibrant purple or red hue as they ripen. The overall architecture of the plant is characterized by a shrubby growth habit with spreading branches.
