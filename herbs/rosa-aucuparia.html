Dog Rose (Rosa Aucuparia)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Rosa aucuparia.

Dog rose, scientifically known as Rosa aucuparia, is a wild shrub native to Europe and western Asia, valued for its medicinal properties and historical significance. It is a traditional medicinal herb, often used as an adaptogen to support the body's resilience against stress and fatigue. The primary benefits of Dog rose include its high vitamin C content, which boosts the immune system, and its ability to aid in digestive health and skin conditions. Historically, it has been used in traditional herbal medicine for centuries, particularly in European cultures, to treat colds, coughs, and respiratory issues. In modern wellness, Dog rose is incorporated into herbal teas, supplements, and skincare products, and its distinctive tart flavor and rare compound, ellagic acid, make it a unique and valuable plant in both traditional and contemporary health practices.
