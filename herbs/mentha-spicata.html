Mentha spicata

What's the taxonomical classification of Mentha spicata?

Mentha spicata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is classified under the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Mentha and the specific species epithet spicata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Mentha spicata has square stems, opposite lanceolate leaves with serrated margins, and terminal spikes of small, bilabiate purple or white flowers. The leaves are typically arranged in pairs along the stem and possess a textured, rugose surface with prominent venation. Its stem is distinctly quadrangular, which is a characteristic trait of the Lamiaceae family. The inflorescence consists of dense, elongated clusters of flowers that emerge from the upper nodes. Below the flower spikes, the plant often features a creeping rhizomatous root system that allows for vegetative spreading.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to much of Europe, western Asia, and north-western Africa, where it grows naturally in damp environments. Its range extends through temperate regions that provide the moisture necessary for its survival. While widespread across the Eurasian landmass, it can also be found in parts of North America due to human introduction. It thrives specifically in marshy areas, riverbanks, and wet meadows across these diverse continents. This broad distribution allows it to colonize many different types of wetland habitats.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Mentha spicata contains therapeutic compounds primarily located within its aerial parts, which consist of the leaves, stems, and flowering tops. The essential oils concentrated in these green tissues are the main source of medicinal properties used in herbal remedies. Practitioners often harvest the leaves to prepare infusions, tinctures, or steam inhalations for digestive and respiratory support. These specific parts are rich in menthol and carvone, which provide the plant's characteristic aroma and calming effects. Using the dried or fresh foliage allows for the extraction of bioactive molecules that assist in soothing gastrointestinal discomfort.

What are the pharmacological activities of Mentha spicata?

This plant has antibacterial, antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and carminative pharmacological activities. Its essential oils contain compounds that effectively inhibit the growth of various pathogenic microorganisms. The antioxidant properties help neutralize free radicals, which reduces oxidative stress within biological systems. Additionally, the plant serves as an anti-inflammatory agent by modulating immune responses to decrease swelling and pain. Its carminative effects also aid digestion by helping to expel gas from the gastrointestinal tract.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a complex profile of medicinal compounds including menthol, carvacrol, thymol, rosmarinic acid, and various flavonoids. The presence of menthol provides a cooling sensation often utilized for its soothing properties. Carvacrol and thymol act as potent antimicrobial agents that help combat various pathogens. Rosmarinic acid contributes significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory benefits to the plant's therapeutic potential. Additionally, the diverse array of flavonoids offers further protection against oxidative stress within the body.

As discussed in a study, Mentha spicata contains various chiral terpenoids including beta-pinene, sabiene, limonene, isomenthone, alpha-terpineol, carvone, menthone, terpinen-4-ol, menthol, and piperitone. The essential oils of Mentha spicata contain (+)-isomenthone, (-)-menthone, (1R,2S,5R)-(-)-menthol, and (4S)-(+)-piperitone as single enantiomers with more than 99% excess. Specifically, (R)-(+)-limonene and (S)-(+)-carvone enantiomers have been proven to exist in Mentha spicata essential oils. These compounds can be separated with specific enantioselectivity values such as Es 1.038 for beta-pinene, Es 1.051 for sabiene, Es 1.045 for limonene, Es 1.029 for isomenthone, Es 1.014 for alpha-terpineol, and Es 1.006 for carvone.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for the relief of gastrointestinal issues including Irritable Bowel Syndrome, bloating, flatulence, indigestion, and stomach aches. Mentha spicata contains menthol, which helps relax the smooth muscles of the digestive tract to ease discomfort. By reducing muscle spasms, it effectively manages the cramping associated with Irritable Bowel Syndrome. It also assists in the expulsion of gas to alleviate bloating and flatulence. Furthermore, its soothing properties help settle the stomach to remedy indigestion and general stomach aches.

As discussed in a study, Mentha spicata is a cool and spicy plant with astringent and digestive properties recommended for the treatment of flatulence. The essential oil of Mentha spicata contains carvone as its main component and possesses potency for treating flatulence related to indigestion, cesarean section, and dysmenorrhea. This essential oil can also reduce pain severity during colonoscopy or dysmenorrheal conditions. Mentha spicata essential oil can be applied to other intestinal complaints involving pain and flatulence, such as Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS). While no hazardous effects were reported for the essential oil when used in proper dosages, more deep clinical trials are needed to demonstrate its potential for IBS.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause a variety of adverse reactions including heartburn, acid reflux aggravation, nausea, abdominal pain, diarrhea, allergic reaction, and dizziness. Some individuals may experience significant gastrointestinal discomfort such as stomach aches or loose stools after consumption. Those prone to digestive issues might notice that the mint increases the frequency of acid reflux episodes. Sensitivities to the plant can also manifest as skin rashes or other forms of an allergic reaction. Additionally, some users report feeling lightheaded or experiencing dizziness following ingestion.
