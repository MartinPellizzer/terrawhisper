Ilex anguulata has small, coriaceous, evergreen leaves that are characterized by distinctively angular or undulated margins and an elliptical to oblong shape. The plant's structural framework consists of dense, shrubby branches that support these specialized foliage structures.     Ilex angulata is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a tincture derived from its leaves and bark.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Ilex angulata can cause severe skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if handled or ingested improperly. The plant contains various bioactive compounds that may trigger localized swelling or redness upon contact with sensitive skin. If the leaves or berries are consumed, individuals might experience nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. Some people may also exhibit systemic allergic reactions such as itching or hives. It is essential to exercise caution when working with this species to avoid these adverse physical responses.
