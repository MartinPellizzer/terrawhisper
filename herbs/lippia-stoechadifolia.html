What is Lippia stoechadifolia?

Lippia stoechadifolia is a shrub native to South Africa, belonging to the family Verbenaceae.

Its leaves are opposite, elliptical to lanceolate, 1.5-4 cm long, with 3-5 pairs of secondary veins and a cuneate base. The flowers are small, in axillary corymbose cymes, with a calyx of 5 lobes and a corolla of 4-5 lobes, blue-purple in color. The fruits are small, dry, and schizocarpic, consisting of 4-5 mericarps.

The plant grows up to 1-2 meters in height, with a woody stem and a hairy leaf surface.
