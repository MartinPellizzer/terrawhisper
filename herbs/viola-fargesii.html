Viola fargesii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viola fargesiii has cordate to reniform leaves with crenate margins and a basal rosette growth habit, complemented by small, zygomorphic flowers that typically feature violet or pale lavender petals. The leaves are characterized by long petioles and a texture that can range from smooth to slightly pubescent depending on the specific environment. Each flower possesses five petals, with the lower petal often displaying distinct nectar guides or darker veining. The plant produces delicate, upright peduncles that elevate the blossoms above the foliage for pollinators. Following pollination, the plant develops small, three-valved capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Its distribution is closely tied to the moist, temperate climates found within these specific subtropical montane zones.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Viola fargesii can cause skin irritation, digestive upset, or allergic reactions if consumed or applied improperly. There is also a potential for hypersensitivity in people with known allergies to the Violaceae family.
