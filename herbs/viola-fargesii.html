Viola fargesii

What's the taxonomical classification of Viola fargesii?

Viola fargesii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Malpighiales. This plant is a member of the family Violaceae, which falls under the genus Viola, and is specifically identified by the species epithet fargesii.

For external ailments such as skin irritations or inflammations, the liquid is used as a wash to soothe the affected area.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Viola fargesii can cause skin irritation, digestive upset, or allergic reactions if consumed or applied improperly. While specific clinical data on human toxicity is limited, many plants in the Viola genus contain saponins that may trigger gastrointestinal distress. Some individuals might experience contact dermatitis or localized redness when the plant touches sensitive skin. There is also a potential for hypersensitivity in people with known allergies to the Violaceae family. Careful observation is necessary when interacting with this species to avoid adverse biological responses.
