Cotoneaster harrovianus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cotoneaster harrovianus has prostrate, spreading woody stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy on the upper surface. The foliage typically measures between 10 and 20 millimeters in length and features finely serrated margins. During the flowering season, tiny white blossoms emerge from the leaf axils to attract pollinators. These flowers eventually develop into small, round red berries that persist through the winter months. The plant's compact, mat-forming growth habit makes it well-suited for groundcover applications.
