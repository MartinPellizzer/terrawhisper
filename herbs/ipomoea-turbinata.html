Ipomoea turbinata

What's the taxonomical classification of Ipomoea turbinata?

Ipomoea turbinata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Solanales. It is a member of the family Convolvulaceae and is further categorized within the genus Ipomoea, ultimately reaching its specific identification as the species turbinata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ipomoea turbinata has twining, herbaceous stems that support cordate to sagittate leaves and funnel-shaped, solitary flowers. The foliage typically features prominent venation and a smooth or slightly pubescent texture on the leaf surfaces. Its inflorescences consist of large, trumpet-like corollas that often exhibit pale lavender or white coloration with a deeper throat. The reproductive structures include a central style and stamens tucked within the floral tube. Small, globose capsules serve as the fruit, containing numerous dark seeds that facilitate dispersal.
