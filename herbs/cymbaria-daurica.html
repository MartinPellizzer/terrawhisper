Cymbaria daurica

What's the taxonomical classification of Cymbaria daurica?

Cymbaria daurica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Lamiales. This specific plant is a representative of the family Orobanchaceae, situated within the genus Cymbaria, and is identified by the specific epithet daurica.

What are the pharmacological activities of Cymbaria daurica?

This plant has anti-diabetic, anti-inflammatory, anti-itching, anti-swelling, and α-glucosidase-inhibitory pharmacological activities. Its ability to inhibit α-glucosidase plays a crucial role in managing blood glucose levels by slowing down carbohydrate digestion. Furthermore, the specimen exhibits significant anti-inflammatory properties that effectively reduce bodily swelling. These bioactive compounds also provide relief from skin irritation through potent anti-itching effects. Together, these diverse therapeutic actions highlight the plant's medicinal potential for treating metabolic and inflammatory conditions.

As discussed in a study, Cymbaria daurica is used in traditional Mongolian medicine to treat psoriasis, pruritus, diabetes, impetigo, and fetotoxiicity. The n-butanol and ethyl acetate extracts of the plant showed significant (p < 0.05) anti-inflammatory activities by inhibiting nitric oxide production in RAW 264.7 cells. The ethyl acetate extract significantly inhibited alpha-glucosidase activity with a potency equivalent to acarbose (p > 0.05), while the n-butanol extract had the second highest inhibitory activity. From the potent extracts, 10 compounds were separated and identified, including seven compounds isolated for the first time. Additionally, 30 compounds were identified from the n-butanol and ethyl acetate extracts using UHPLC-Q-Exactive. This research also utilized a novel HPLC method for the simultaneous identification of 5 compounds within those specific extracts.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains medicinal compounds including echinacoside, isoacteoside, phenylethanoid glycosides, tricin, and verbascoside. These specific chemical constituents contribute to the diverse therapeutic potential of the species. Phenylethanoid glycosides serve as a major class of bioactive molecules found throughout the plant tissues. Within this group, echinacoside and verbascoside act as significant components with notable biological activities. Additionally, the presence of isoacteoside and the flavone tricin provides further medicinal complexity to the plant's profile.

As discussed in a study, Cymbaria daurica extracts were analyzed for their chemical composition and bioactivity. Researchers separated and identified 10 compounds from the n-butanol and ethyl acetate extracts of the plant. Among these, vanillic acid, cistanoside F, echinacoside, arenarioside, verbascoside, isoacteoside, and tricin were isolated for the first time. Using UHPLC-Q-Exactive, a total of 30 compounds from the n-butanol and ethyl acetate extracts were identified. The study also demonstrated for the first time that Cymbaria daurica contains phenylethanoid glycosides. Finally, a novel HPLC method allowed for the simultaneous identification of 5 compounds in the n-butanol and ethyl acetate extracts.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating various skin diseases, including eczema, impetigo, pruritus, and psoriasis. Its medicinal properties are often applied topically to soothe irritated or inflamed areas of the body. By addressing these conditions, the plant helps manage intense itching and uncomfortable skin eruptions. It serves as a traditional remedy for stabilizing the skin barrier during flare-ups. This botanical application provides relief from the discomfort associated with these specific dermatological ailments.

As discussed in a study, Cymbaria daurica is used in Mongolia as a traditional medicine to treat eczema due to its anti-swelling, anti-inflammatory, anti-hemorrhagic, and anti-itching properties. In experiments using DNCB-induced eczema rat models, the extract was administered at dosages of 10, 50, and 250 mg/mL per day. An analysis of the 173 targets included in the protein-protein interaction network identified TNF and IL-2 as key targets associated with the natural killer cell-mediated cytotoxicity pathway. The results showed that Cymbaria daurica significantly reduced IL-2 and TNF-α serum levels in the eczema rat models with a P < 0.0001. Furthermore, the extract was shown to inhibit the expression of SHP-1, Vav, and Grb2 in the skin of the model rats.
