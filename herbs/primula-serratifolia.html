Primula serratifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Primula serratifolia?

Primula serratifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and placed in the order Ericales. This plant is a member of the family Primulaceae, falling under the genus Primula, and is specifically identified by its species name, serratifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Primula serratifolia has basal, long-petioled leaves that are elliptical to oblong in shape and feature distinctively serrated or saw-toothed margins. The leaf surfaces often display a slightly rugose texture with prominent venation patterns extending from the midrib to the edges. These leaves form a dense rosette at the ground level, which serves as the structural foundation for the plant. From the center of this rosette, slender, leafless stalks called scapes emerge to support the inflorescence. The flowers are typically borne in umbels at the top of these scapes, displaying a range of colors depending on the specific variety.
