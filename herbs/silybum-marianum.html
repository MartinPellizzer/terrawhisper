Medicinal Parts of silybum marianum

Silybum marianum has many medicinal parts, such as the seeds, leaves, and flowers, which are used to create medicinal products.

Possible Side Effects of silybum marianum

Silybum marianum can have side effects if used improperly, such as gastrointestinal upset, nausea, and diarrhea due to its potent saponin and flavonoid content.

In rare cases, excessive consumption may lead to liver damage and increased risk of bleeding, particularly when taken in combination with blood-thinning medications.

Additionally, silymarin, the primary active compound in Silybum marianum, may interact with certain medications, such as antacids and blood pressure regulators, resulting in adverse effects.

If not used as directed, Silybum marianum may also cause allergic reactions, including skin rashes and itching.
