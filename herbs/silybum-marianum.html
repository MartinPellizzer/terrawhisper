What to know about Silybum marianum before using it medicinally

Silybum marianum, commonly known as milk thistle, is a herb that has been traditionally used to treat various health conditions, including liver and gallbladder disorders, such as cirrhosis, hepatitis, and jaundice.

The main health benefits of milk thistle include its ability to protect the liver from damage, promote liver regeneration, and reduce inflammation. The main therapeutic properties of this herb include its antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and hepatoprotective effects. The bioactive compounds present in milk thistle include silymarin, a complex mixture of flavonoids, and silibinin, which have been shown to have potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities.

Milk thistle is often prepared as a herbal supplement or tea, with the most common preparation being a standardized extract of silymarin, which is used to support liver health and improve overall well-being.
