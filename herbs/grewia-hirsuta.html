Grewia hirsuta

What's the taxonomical classification of Grewia hirsuta?

Grewia hirsuta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent through the order Malvales, it is placed within the family Malvaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Grewia, and its unique species name, hirsuta.

Grewia hirsuta is prepared as decoctions, infusions, or topical poultices derived from its various plant parts to treat different ailments. What side-effects this plant can have?

Grewia hirsuta can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and potential gastrointestinal distress if ingested. The plant contains secondary metabolites that may act as irritants when they come into contact with sensitive human tissue. Contact with the leaves or stems often results in localized redness, itching, or swelling of the skin. If the plant parts are consumed, individuals might experience nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. Because specific toxicological studies are limited, caution is necessary when handling this species in its natural habitat.
