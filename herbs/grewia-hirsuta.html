What's the taxonomical classification of Grewia hirsuta?

Grewia hirsuta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent through the order Malvales, it is placed within the family Malvaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Grewia, and its unique species name, hirsuta.

According to a study published by "BMC complementary and alternative medicine", Grewia hirsuta is a traditional herbal medicinal plant reported to possess a variety of pharmacological actions. The compound (4Z, 12Z)-cyclopentadeca-4, 12-dienone was isolated from the methanol extract of the leaves of Grewia hirsuta. This ligand was evaluated through an in silico docking approach against 7 different target proteins related to diabetes mellitus. These specific molecular targets included aldose reductase, glucokinase, pyruvate dehydrogenase kinase isoform 2, peroxisome proliferator-activated receptor-gamma, glycogen synthase kinase-3, 11β-Hydroxysteroid dehydrogenase, and glutamine: fructose-6-phosphate amidotransferase. The study showed that (4Z, 12Z)-cyclopentadeca-4, 12-dienone is a good inhibitor that docks well with these targets. Consequently, the leaves of Grewia hirsuta contain a potential inhibitor for diabetes.
