Grewia hirsuta

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Grewia hirsuta has a woody, perennial structure characterized by spreading branches covered in dense, stiff, hair-like bristles. The leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical to lanceolate in shape, often possessing a rough texture due to pubescence. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves, typically appearing in clusters. The fruit develops into a small, dry drupe that is often enclosed by the persistent, hairy sepals. This bristly morphology serves as a distinctive defensive mechanism against herbivores in its natural habitat.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the semi-arid and arid regions of South America, specifically spanning across parts of northern Argentina, Bolivia, and western Paraguay. It thrives primarily within the Gran Chaco biome, an expansive lowland region characterized by seasonal moisture fluctuations. Within these habitats, the species is typically found in dry forests and scrublands where soil drainage is efficient. Its distribution is closely tied to specific environmental conditions that favor its drought-tolerant nature. Consequently, the species remains localized to these distinct subtropical corridors.
