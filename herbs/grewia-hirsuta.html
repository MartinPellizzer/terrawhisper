Grewia hirsuta

What's the taxonomical classification of Grewia hirsuta?

Grewia hirsuta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent through the order Malvales, it is placed within the family Malvaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Grewia, and its unique species name, hirsuta.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Grewia hirsuta contains bioactive phytochemicals including saponins, tannins, flavonoids, and various alkaloids that contribute to its therapeutic properties. These specific secondary metabolites are believed to provide significant antioxidant and antimicrobial activities when applied in traditional medicine. Research indicates that extracts from the plant can be used to treat skin ailments and inflammatory conditions due to these chemical constituents. The presence of triterpenoid saponins specifically may also play a role in modulating biological responses within the body. Consequently, the chemical profile of this species supports its long-standing use in ethnobotanical practices for healing diverse ailments.

What are the therapeutic actions of Grewia hirsuta?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, stomach ailments, and various inflammatory conditions. The bark and leaves are frequently prepared into decoctions or topical applications to address dermatological issues like rashes or sores. In some traditional medicinal practices, the plant components are utilized to alleviate gastrointestinal distress and digestive discomfort. It is also believed to possess properties that help manage pain and swelling in different parts of the body. These medicinal uses vary significantly depending on the specific region and the part of the plant being utilized.
