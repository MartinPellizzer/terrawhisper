Gaillardia lanceolata

What's the taxonomical classification of Gaillardia lanceolata?

Gaillardia lanceolata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the taxonomic lineage of the order Asterales. It is a prominent representative of the family Asteraceae, a group known for its complex flower heads. Within this family, the plant is categorized under the genus Gaillardia, and its specific identification is completed by the species name lanceolata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gaillardia lanceolata has lanceolate to narrowly ovate leaves that are typically 1 to 4 inches long and possess serrated margins. The plant features a perennial root system that often forms woody bases or rhizomes. Its inflorescences consist of composite flower heads characterized by ray florets with bright red or orange centers and yellowish outer tips. The central disk florets are tubular and clustered together to form a prominent golden core. Stems are generally erect and can reach heights of approximately 12 to 24 inches depending on the environment.
