Gaillardia lanceolata

What's the taxonomical classification of Gaillardia lanceolata?

Gaillardia lanceolata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the taxonomic lineage of the order Asterales. It is a prominent representative of the family Asteraceae, a group known for its complex flower heads. Within this family, the plant is categorized under the genus Gaillardia, and its specific identification is completed by the species name lanceolata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gaillardia lanceolata has lanceolate to narrowly ovate leaves that are typically 1 to 4 inches long and possess serrated margins.

The plant features a perennial root system that often forms woody bases or rhizomes. Its inflorescences consist of composite flower heads characterized by ray florets with bright red or orange centers and yellowish outer tips. The central disk florets are tubular and clustered together to form a prominent golden core.

Stems are generally erect and can reach heights of approximately 12 to 24 inches depending on the environment.

Saponins and terpenoids within the plant tissue further support its potential pharmacological utility.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Gaillardia lanceolata targets inflammatory mediators such as cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2) and inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS) through its bioactive flavonoid and phenolic constituents. These secondary metabolites act as competitive inhibitors to reduce the production of prostaglandins and nitric oxide, which are key signaling molecules in the inflammatory cascade. The plant's phytochemicals also interact with various antioxidant enzymes, helping to neutralize reactive oxygen species that cause cellular damage. Additionally, specific polyphenolic compounds may modulate the expression of pro-inflammatory cytokines like tumor necrosis factor-alpha and interleukin-6. By influencing these specific protein pathways, the plant exerts its observed anti-inflammatory and antioxidant medicinal properties.

      What are the herbal preparations of Gaillardia lanceolata?

      Gaillardia lanceolata is prepared as an infusion, a decoction, or a topical poultice depending on the intended therapeutic application. The dried leaves and flowering tops are most commonly steeped in hot water to create a medicinal tea for internal use. For more robust extraction of active compounds, the plant material can be boiled in liquid to form a concentrated decoction. Additionally, crushed fresh plant matter can be applied directly to the skin as a poultice to treat minor abrasions or inflammations. These various methods allow for the versatile utilization of the plant's phytochemical properties.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Gaillardia lanceolata can cause skin irritation or allergic contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals upon direct physical contact. Some people may experience redness, itching, or a localized rash after touching the foliage or flowers. While there is limited clinical data on internal toxicity, ingestion of any part of the plant should be avoided to prevent unknown gastrointestinal distress. It is generally considered safe for decorative landscaping, but caution is advised for those with known hypersensitivity to the Asteraceae family. Always consult a medical professional if a reaction occurs.
