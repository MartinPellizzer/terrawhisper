Hedychium venustum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hedychium venustum has upright, rhizomatous stems that reach significant heights, complemented by long, lanceolate leaves with parallel venation. The plant features large, showy flowers that emerge from terminal inflorescences, typically displaying vibrant colors and complex petal structures. Its floral morphology includes a prominent labellum and delicate stamens that are characteristic of the ginger family. The root system consists of thick, fleshy rhizomes that store nutrients and facilitate vegetative spreading. These structural adaptations allow the plant to thrive in moist, shaded forest environments.
