    What is Elymus laxiflorus?

    Elymus laxiflorus is a perennial grass species belonging to the family Poaceae.

    It is characterized by its lax inflorescence, bearing spikelets that are 10-15 mm long, with a reddish-brown glume and a yellowish-brown lemma. The glume is 7-8 mm long, with an acute apex, and the lemma is 8-10 mm long, with a cuneate base. This species has a tufted habit, with erect stems 40-100 cm tall, and linear-lanceolate leaves that are 5-10 cm long and 0.5-1.5 mm wide.

    Elymus laxiflorus is native to East Asia, specifically China, Japan, and the Korean Peninsula.
