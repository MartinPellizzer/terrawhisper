Wendlandia uvariifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Wendlandiia uvariifolia has woody, climbing vines characterized by opposite, ovate leaves with entire margins and prominent venation. The foliage typically displays a smooth texture and a deep green coloration on the upper surface. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils to facilitate reproduction through specific pollinators. The plant often develops aerial roots that assist in its climbing habit through forest canopies. These structural features allow the species to effectively compete for light in tropical environments.
