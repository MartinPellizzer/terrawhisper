Calanthe tricarinata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Calanthe tricarinaata has upright, slender stems that bear several lanceolate leaves with distinct parallel venation and a slightly wavy margin. The inflorescence is a terminal raceme featuring multiple delicate, long-lasting flowers that exhibit a pale pink to white coloration. Each orchid flower possesses a complex labellum that is often marked with deeper rose hues and intricate patterns. The sepals and petals are narrow and spreading, contributing to the airy appearance of the blooming spike. This species is characterized by its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments where its foliage remains lush.
