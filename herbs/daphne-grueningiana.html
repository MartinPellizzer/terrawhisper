Daphne grueningiana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Daphne grueningiana has an erect, woody shrub habit characterized by slender, smooth stems and small, opposite, lanceolate leaves that are typically dark green and leathery. The foliage remains evergreen, providing a consistent texture throughout the growing season. During its flowering period, the plant produces small, fragrant, tubular flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These blossoms are often pale pink or white and sit closely against the stems. The overall structure of the plant is compact, making it well-suited for rocky or alpine environments.
