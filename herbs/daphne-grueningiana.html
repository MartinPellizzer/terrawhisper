What's the taxonomical classification of Daphne grueningiana?

Daphne grueningiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic journey continues through the order Malvales and the family Thymelaeaceae. Ultimately, this plant is identified by its specific genus, Daphne, and its unique species designation, grueningiana.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Daphne grueningiana can cause severe skin irritation, chemical burns, and gastrointestinal distress if any part of the plant is ingested or touched. The plant contains toxic compounds that trigger inflammatory responses upon contact with human tissue. If the sap touches the skin, it may lead to redness, blistering, or painful swelling. Ingestion of the berries or leaves can result in nausea, vomiting, and more serious systemic poisoning. Careful avoidance is necessary to prevent these adverse physical reactions.
