Daphne grueningiana

What's the taxonomical classification of Daphne grueningiana?

Daphne grueningiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic journey continues through the order Malvales and the family Thymelaeaceae. Ultimately, this plant is identified by its specific genus, Daphne, and its unique species designation, grueningiana.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Daphne grueningiana contains various diterpenoids and phenolic compounds that serve as the primary bioactive constituents of the plant. These chemical structures are characteristic of the Thymelaeaceae family and often exhibit significant pharmacological activity. Specific compounds such as mezerein-type diterpene esters are frequently found within this genus. These substances can demonstrate potent biological effects, including antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. However, the high toxicity of many daphne alkaloids means these compounds must be studied with extreme caution.
