Swertia erythrosticta

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Swertia erythrosticta has opposite, elliptical to lanceolate leaves that are characterized by a distinct reddish-brown pigmentation on their undersides and stems. The foliage is typically arranged in decussate pairs along woody, upright stems. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often exhibiting subtle coloring consistent with the plant's overall aesthetic. The overall architecture of the shrub is defined by its slender branching pattern and textured bark. These morphological traits collectively distinguish this species within its genus.
