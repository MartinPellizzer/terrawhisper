Juncus przewalskii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Juncus przewalskii has cylindrical, unbranched stems that reach heights of 10 to 60 centimeters and are characterized by a smooth, bright green surface. These stems are typically solid and lack the distinct ridges or ribs found in many other rush species. The leaves are reduced to tiny, scale-like structures located at the base of the stems, making the plant appear largely leafless. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in compact clusters near the tips of the stems. The plant's reproductive structures eventually develop into small, brownish capsules containing numerous tiny seeds.
