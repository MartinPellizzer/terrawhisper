Saussurea neofranchetii

What's the taxonomical classification of Saussurea neofranchetii?

Saussurea neofranchetii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae, which places it within the genus Saussurea, ultimately identifying it by its specific species name, neofranchetii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea neofranchetii has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by basal leaves that are lanceolate to narrowly ovate with entire or slightly serrated margins.

The plant features a distinct rosette of leaves that grow close to the ground to withstand high-altitude environments. Its flowering stalks are erect and terminate in a dense, head-like inflorescence surrounded by specialized involucral bracts. These bracts often exhibit unique coloration or textures that serve to protect the developing florets.
