Golden St. John'S Wort (Hypericum calycinum) Compounds

The primary compounds in Golden St. John's Wort include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and hypericin, a unique compound with potential antidepressant effects. Other key components are phenolic acids, terpenoids, and glycosides, which contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities.

Golden St. John'S Wort (Hypericum calycinum) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are key active compounds in Golden St. John's Wort (Hypericum calycinum). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds support skin health and may aid in wound healing. Flavonoids are found in the flowers and leaves of the plant.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Golden St. John's Wort (Hypericum calycinum). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties and may support skin health and wound healing. This compound is part of the plant's broader therapeutic profile in herbal medicine.

3. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Golden St. John's Wort. These compounds contribute to the plant's medicinal properties, including its antidepressant and anti-inflammatory effects. They are responsible for many of the therapeutic benefits attributed to this herb in traditional and modern herbal medicine.

4. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Golden St. John's Wort (Hypericum calycinum). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating mild depression and anxiety. Glycosides in this species are part of the hypericin family, which may influence mood regulation. These compounds are often found in the flowers and leaves of the plant.

5. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Golden St. John's Wort (Hypericum calycinum). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support wound healing and inflammation reduction. Tannins also help preserve the plant's medicinal potency by stabilizing other active constituents.

6. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound in Golden St. John's Wort (Hypericum calycinum). It contains bioactive components like flavonoids and hypericins that contribute to its traditional uses for mood regulation and skin health. The oil is extracted through steam distillation and is valued for its therapeutic properties in herbal medicine.

7. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Golden St. John's Wort (Hypericum calycinum). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. Coumarin also acts as a mild sedative and may support cardiovascular health. Its presence enhances the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Golden St. John's Wort (Hypericum calycinum). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponins may support skin health and help in treating mild inflammatory conditions. They are part of the plant's natural defense mechanisms.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid compound found in Golden St. John's Wort (Hypericum calycinum). It contributes to the plant's vibrant color and has antioxidant properties that may support skin health and reduce inflammation. Its presence in the plant highlights the diverse bioactive components that contribute to its traditional medicinal use.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Golden St. John's Wort (Hypericum calycinum). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, though its role is less prominent than hypericin and hyperforin. Alkaloids in this plant are part of the complex mixture responsible for its traditional use in herbal medicine.
