10 Possible Side Effects Of Hypericum Calycinum

Hypericum calycinum has some side effects when used improperly, such as nausea, dizziness, and allergic skin reactions.

These side effects can be caused by excessive consumption or interaction with other medications. In severe cases, these reactions can worsen people's lives, causing digestive issues that lead to dehydration, fatigue, and difficulty concentrating.

This can impact daily routines and productivity, making it challenging to maintain a normal life.

1. Causes dizziness

Hypericum calycinum, also known as St John's Wort, causes dizziness in some individuals.

This side effect is attributed to the herb's ability to interact with certain medications, such as sedatives and antidepressants, which can lead to dizziness and lightheadedness. Additionally, Hypericum calycinum may cause a decrease in blood pressure, further contributing to dizziness.

The exact mechanism of how it affects blood pressure is not fully understood, but it is thought to be related to the herb's ability to dilate blood vessels.

2. Triggers allergic reactions

Hypericum calycinum, also known as St John's Wort, triggers allergic reactions in some individuals.

This is due to the presence of hyperforin and hypericin, two main active compounds in the plant, which can cause an immune response in sensitive people. These compounds may stimulate the release of histamine, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.

Additionally, Hypericum calycinum contains other allergenic proteins that can trigger an allergic response in some individuals.

3. Induces vomiting

Hypericum calycinum, also known as St John's Wort, induces vomiting due to its ability to stimulate the gut and increase digestive secretions.

The plant's active compounds, particularly hypericin and hyperforin, can irritate the stomach lining and small intestine, leading to nausea and vomiting.

Additionally, Hypericum calycinum may also slow down digestion, allowing toxins and undigested food particles to accumulate in the gut and further exacerbate vomiting symptoms.

4. Exacerbates depression

Hypericum calycinum, also known as St John's Wort, exacerbates depression due to its ability to bind to serotonin receptors in the brain, potentially interfering with the neurotransmitter's natural functioning.

This may lead to a worsening of depressive symptoms and mood disorders.

The plant's chemical composition, which includes hyperforin and hypericin, is thought to contribute to this effect, as it can alter the balance of serotonin levels and disrupt normal brain activity.

5. Increases bleeding risk

Hypericum calycinum, also known as St John's Wort, increases bleeding risk due to its ability to inhibit platelet aggregation and antagonize platelet activating factor.

This can lead to decreased blood clotting, making it easier for individuals to bruise or bleed excessively, particularly when combining it with anticoagulant medications or undergoing surgical procedures.

The herb's effect on platelets is attributed to its bioactive compounds, including hyperforin and hypericin, which interact with platelet receptors to impair their function.

6. Enhances sedation

Hypericum calycinum, also known as St John's Wort, enhances sedation due to its active compounds, including hyperforin and hypericin.

These chemicals interact with the brain's neurotransmitters, such as serotonin and melatonin, which regulate sleep and wakefulness.

As a result, Hypericum calycinum can increase drowsiness and relaxation, making it easier for individuals to fall asleep.

7. Reduces blood pressure

Hypericum calycinum, also known as St John's Wort, reduces blood pressure by interfering with the body's ability to process certain medications, including blood pressure medications.

It does this by activating an enzyme in the liver that breaks down these drugs too quickly, reducing their effectiveness and leading to decreased blood pressure levels.

As a result, individuals taking Hypericum calycinum may experience dizziness or lightheadedness due to hypotension.

8. Impairs cognitive function

Hypericum calycinum, also known as St John's Wort, impairs cognitive function by affecting the brain's neurotransmitters, such as serotonin and dopamine.

Its active compounds, like hyperforin and hypericin, can bind to serotonin receptors, reducing the levels of this neurotransmitter in the brain.

This disruption can lead to impaired concentration, memory loss, and confusion, making it difficult to perform daily tasks and think clearly.

9. Increases photosensitivity

Hypericum calycinum, also known as St John's Wort, increases photosensitivity in some individuals.

This is due to its active compounds, hyperforin and hypericin, which are responsible for its phototoxic properties. When exposed to sunlight or UV light, these compounds can react with the skin, causing adverse reactions such as redness, itching, and blistering.

This increased sensitivity can occur even with brief exposure to sunlight, making it essential for individuals using Hypericum calycinum to take extra caution when spending time outdoors.
