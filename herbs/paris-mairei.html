Paris mairei

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Paris mairei has a rhizomatous perennial habit characterized by a single, terminal, solitary flower surrounded by a whorl of four to six large, ovate, bright green leaves. The leaves are typically petiolate and possess distinct venation that creates a textured surface. The central flower features dark, purplish-black tepals that are often fleshy and lack true petals or sepals. A prominent, central ring of stamens is situated in the middle of the floral structure, surrounding a single pistil. This unique arrangement creates a striking contrast between the dark reproductive parts and the vibrant foliage.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the subtropical and temperate forest regions of central and southern China, specifically occurring in provinces such as Hubei, Hunan, Jiangxi, Zhejiang, and Fujian. It thrives primarily in mountainous areas where it can find suitable shade and moisture. The species is often found growing in the understory of broad-leaved forests. Its distribution is relatively localized compared to more widespread plant species. Environmental conditions in these specific East Asian habitats dictate its natural range.

      What are the herbal preparations of Paris mairei?

      Paris mairei is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste using its dried rhizomes and roots. The plant contains potent alkaloids that require careful processing to manage its biological activity. Traditional practitioners often boil the crushed root material in water to extract the active medicinal compounds. These liquid extracts can also be formulated into tinctures through alcohol-based extraction methods. In some regional applications, the plant matter is ground into a fine powder for direct topical application to the skin.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Paris mairei can cause severe irritation to the skin, eyes, and mucous membranes if the plant comes into direct contact with the body. The presence of toxic compounds within its tissues means that touching the leaves or stems may result in redness, itching, or painful dermatitis. If any part of the plant is accidentally ingested, it can lead to gastrointestinal distress, including vomiting and abdominal pain. There is also a risk of systemic toxicity if the chemical components are absorbed into the bloodstream through an open wound. Because the specific potency of these toxins can vary, immediate medical attention is recommended following any exposure.
