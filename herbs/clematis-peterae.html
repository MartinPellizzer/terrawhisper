Clematis peterae

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clematis peterae has opposite, trifoliate leaves consisting of three ovate to elliptical leaflets with entire or slightly wavy margins and obtuse tips. The plant exhibits a climbing growth habit supported by slender, twisting petioles that act as tendrils. Its flowers are typically solitary and feature four to five pale to creamy white tepals. The reproductive structures include numerous stamens that surround a central cluster of pistils. Small, feathery achenes develop from the flowers once pollination is successful.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Clematis peterae can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Contact with the plant's sap may lead to dermatitis or redness on sensitive areas of the skin. If swallowed, the toxins within the plant can trigger nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. Some individuals may also experience respiratory irritation if the pollen or fine hairs are inhaled. It is important to handle this species with care to avoid these adverse physical responses.
