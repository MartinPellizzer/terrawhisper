Clematis peterae

What's the taxonomical classification of Clematis peterae?

Clematis peterae belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it in the order Ranunculales. As a member of the family Ranunculaceae, this plant is categorized under the genus Clematis, with its specific designation being the species peterae.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Clematis peterae contains various protoanemonin-type glycosides and small amounts of alkaloids that act as the primary bioactive components. These chemical structures are responsible for the plant's potential irritant and therapeutic properties when processed correctly. The presence of these compounds often necessitates careful preparation to avoid toxicity during medicinal application. Historical studies suggest these elements may influence localized physiological responses in traditional remedies. Researchers continue to examine the specific molecular interactions of these compounds to understand their broader pharmacological potential.

What are the therapeutic actions of Clematis peterae?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat respiratory conditions including coughs, bronchitis, and asthma, as well as various neurological issues like neuralgia and facial paralysis. In traditional herbalism, it is often applied to soothe inflammation in the throat and lungs. Some practitioners also utilize it to address skin irritations and localized nerve pain. The plant's applications frequently extend to calming nervous system disorders and managing sudden spasms. Careful dosage is essential when using this botanical for these medicinal purposes.
