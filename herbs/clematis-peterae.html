What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clematis peterae has opposite, trifoliate leaves consisting of three ovate to elliptical leaflets with entire or slightly wavy margins and obtuse tips. The plant exhibits a climbing growth habit supported by slender, twisting petioles that act as tendrils. Its flowers are typically solitary and feature four to five pale to creamy white tepals. The reproductive structures include numerous stamens that surround a central cluster of pistils. Small, feathery achenes develop from the flowers once pollination is successful.
