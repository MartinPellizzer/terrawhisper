Cassia hirsuta

What's the taxonomical classification of Cassia hirsuta?

Cassia hirsuta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Fabales. It is a member of the family Fabaceae, which places it within the well-known legume group. Specifically, its taxonomic hierarchy concludes with its placement in the genus Cassia, with its unique biological identity defined by the species name hirsuta.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cassia hirsuta has pinnately compound leaves, small yellow flowers, and hairy, linear seed pods. The leaflets are typically small and arranged symmetrically along a central rachis. Its stems and calyx are covered in fine, spreading hairs that give the plant a textured appearance. The flowers feature five petals and are often found in small clusters or solitary arrangements. Long, slender legumes develop from the flowers and contain the seeds as they mature.
