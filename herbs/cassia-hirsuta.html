Cassia hirsuta

What's the taxonomical classification of Cassia hirsuta?

Cassia hirsuta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Fabales. It is a member of the family Fabaceae, which places it within the well-known legume group. Specifically, its taxonomic hierarchy concludes with its placement in the genus Cassia, with its unique biological identity defined by the species name hirsuta.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cassia hirsuta has pinnately compound leaves, small yellow flowers, and hairy, linear seed pods. The leaflets are typically small and arranged symmetrically along a central rachis. Its stems and calyx are covered in fine, spreading hairs that give the plant a textured appearance. The flowers feature five petals and are often found in small clusters or solitary arrangements. Long, slender legumes develop from the flowers and contain the seeds as they mature.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compound L-DOPA, along with lectins, tannins, and a significant concentration of total free phenolics. L-DOPA serves as a critical precursor for dopamine synthesis, which is often utilized in treating neurological conditions. The presence of lectins can influence biological signaling and immune responses within a host organism. Tannins contribute to the plant's chemical profile by providing astringent properties and antioxidant benefits. Furthermore, the total free phenolics act as potent bioactive agents that help mitigate oxidative stress.

As discussed in a study, Cassia hirsuta L. seeds contain various nutritional components and anti-nutritional factors including total free phenolics, tannins, L-DOPA, and lectins. The seeds possess a crude protein content ranging from 15.52 to 20.74% and crude lipid levels between 3.77% and 7.04%. They also contain crude fiber from 4.68 to 6.92%, ash from 3.98 to 6.42%, and carbohydrates from 62.45 to 70.16%. The energy values of these seeds are measured at 1549-1634 kJ/100 g (DM). Regarding mineral profiles, potassium is the most abundant at 1029-1786 mg/100 g, while manganese is low at 2.1-2.2 mg/100 g.
