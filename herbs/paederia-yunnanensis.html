Paederia yunnanensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Paederia yunnanensis has a climbing or sprawling habit characterized by slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with entire margins. The foliage typically features a smooth surface and prominent venation that extends toward the leaf edges. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the axils, often displaying a pale color and arranged in compact clusters. The plant's reproductive structures include small fruits that develop from the fertilized ovaries. These morphological traits help distinguish it from other members of the Rubiaceae family.
