Dysosma pleiantha

What's the taxonomical classification of Dysosma pleiantha?

Dysosmoma pleiantha belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Ranunculales. This plant is a specific representative of the family Berberidaceae, situated within the genus Dysosmoma, and is identified by its unique species epithet, pleiantha.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dysosma pleiantha has erect stems that reach heights of 10 to 60 centimeters and bear leaves that are primarily basal or arranged in whorls of three to six along the stem.

The leaves are typically ovate or lanceolate in shape with entire or slightly wavy margins. Small, bell-shaped flowers appear in terminal clusters, displaying a pale yellow or greenish-yellow coloration. The plant also features a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread through forest floors.

Each reproductive structure is supported by slender pedicels that emerge from the leaf axils.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Dysosma pleiantha can cause gastrointestinal distress, dizziness, nausea, and potential neurological disturbances if consumed in improper amounts. While historical uses suggest medicinal properties, the plant contains alkaloids that may trigger adverse reactions in sensitive individuals. Excessive intake might lead to more severe symptoms such as disorientation or physical instability. Because specific toxicological data is limited, cautious handling and avoidance of ingestion are recommended for safety.
