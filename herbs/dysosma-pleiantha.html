Dysosma pleiantha

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dysosma pleiantha has erect stems that reach heights of 10 to 60 centimeters and bear leaves that are primarily basal or arranged in whorls of three to six along the stem. The leaves are typically ovate or lanceolate in shape with entire or slightly wavy margins. Small, bell-shaped flowers appear in terminal clusters, displaying a pale yellow or greenish-yellow coloration. The plant also features a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread through forest floors. Each reproductive structure is supported by slender pedicels that emerge from the leaf axils.
