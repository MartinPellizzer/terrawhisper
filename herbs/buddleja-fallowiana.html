Buddleja fallowiana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Buddlejia fallowiaana has opposite, lanceolate to ovate leaves that are typically 5 to 15 centimeters long with serrated margins and a finely pubescent texture. The plant exhibits a woody, shrubby growth habit with slender, upright branches. Its inflorescences consist of dense, terminal panicles composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. These individual blossoms often display a pale lavender to white coloration with distinct nectar guides. The foliage maintains a soft, grayish-green appearance due to the presence of fine hairs on both the upper and lower surfaces.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of western China, specifically within the provinces of Yunnan, Sichuan, and Tibet. Within these mountainous landscapes, the species is commonly found growing along rocky slopes and the edges of forest margins. The specific microclimates of these regions provide the cool, well-drained conditions necessary for its survival. Its distribution remains localized to these specific eastern Himalayan foothills.
