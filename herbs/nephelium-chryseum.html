Nephelium chryseum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Nephelium chryseum has an upright, woody growth habit characterized by a single main trunk and a spreading canopy of elliptic to oblong leaves. The foliage consists of dark green, leathery leaves that are arranged spirally along the branches. Small, inconspicuous flowers typically emerge in panicles from the leaf axils. The plant produces distinctive fruit that is covered in soft, fleshy protuberances rather than hard spines. These fruits eventually mature into a golden or yellowish hue, which is reflected in its specific epithet.

      What are the herbal preparations of Nephelium chryseum?

      Nephelium chryseum is prepared as decoctions, infusions, or topical poultices depending on whether the leaves, bark, or fruit pulp is being utilized.
