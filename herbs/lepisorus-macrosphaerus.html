What's the taxonomical classification of Lepisorus macrosphaerus?

Lepisorus macrosphaerus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Polypodiales and the family Polypodiaceae, eventually placing it within the genus Lepisorus. Finally, the specific identification of this plant is completed by its species name, macrosphaerus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lepisorus macrosphaerus has small, thin, lanceolate to elliptic fronds that are characterized by entire margins and a distinctively glossy, dark green surface.

The leaves are arranged in a somewhat spreading habit from a creeping rhizome that is covered in scales. Each individual pinna is relatively small and lacks the deep lobing seen in many other fern species. The sori are typically located on the underside of the pinnules, often appearing as rounded clusters near the margins.

These morphological traits are essential for distinguishing this specific fern from its close relatives in the Hymenophyllaceae family.
