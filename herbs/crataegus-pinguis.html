Fat-Fruited Hawthorn (Crataegus Pinguis)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Crataegus pinguis.

Fat-fruited hawthorn, scientifically known as Crataegus pinguis, is a lesser-known medicinal herb belonging to the Rosaceae family, valued for its potential health benefits and historical significance. This shrub is traditionally used for its cardiovascular support properties, with its fruits and leaves containing compounds that may help regulate blood pressure and improve heart function. In traditional Chinese medicine and European herbalism, it has been employed to treat ailments such as hypertension, anxiety, and digestive issues, often prepared as teas or tinctures. Modern wellness applications include its use in herbal formulations aimed at enhancing circulatory health and reducing stress, making it a popular adaptogen in holistic health practices. Notably, Crataegus pinguis is distinguished by its unusually large, fleshy fruits and the presence of flavonoids, which are believed to contribute to its therapeutic effects.
