Pleurospermum cristatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Pleurospermum cristatum?

Pleurospermum cristatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Apiales. This plant is further categorized into the family Apiaceae, specifically within the genus Pleurospermum, and is identified by its specific species name, cristatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pleurospermum cristatum has woody, scrambling stems that produce small, simple, opposite leaves with entire margins and obtuse apices.

The foliage is typically characterized by a bright green color and a smooth texture. Its reproductive structures consist of tiny, inconspicuous flowers that lack showy petals. These flowers are arranged in small clusters along the axils of the leaves.

The plant's growth habit allows it to climb or spread across forest floors using its flexible branches.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Pleurospermum cristatum can cause skin irritation, contact dermatitis, and allergic reactions upon direct contact with its tissues. If the sap or foliage comes into contact with the eyes, it may trigger redness, inflammation, or temporary discomfort. While extensive toxicological data on human ingestion is limited, many species in this family possess chemical compounds that could lead to gastrointestinal distress if consumed.
