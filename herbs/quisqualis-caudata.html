Quisqualis caudata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Quisqualis caudata has long, slender, creeping rhizomes that produce upright, unbranched stems reaching heights of up to 15 centimeters. The leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical to lanceolate in shape, often featuring smooth margins. Each leaf is supported by a distinct petiole that connects the blade to the central stem. The reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These plants typically develop a dense, mat-like growth habit due to their spreading underground root systems.
