Platycarya strobilacea has a deciduous woody habit characterized by spreading branches, elliptical to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins, and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in axillary catkins. Its reproductive structures consist of small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into small, nut-like fruits.
