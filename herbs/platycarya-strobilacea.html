What's the taxonomical classification of Platycarya strobilacea?

Platycarya strobilacea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further categorized under the order Fagales. It is a representative of the family Juglandaceae, falling specifically into the genus Platycarya, with its unique biological identity defined by the species name strobilacea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Its reproductive structures consist of small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into small, nut-like fruits. What are the pharmacological activities of Platycarya strobilacea?

This plant has significant antimicrobial and antioxidant pharmacological activities that contribute to its therapeutic potential. Its chemical constituents allow it to inhibit the growth of various pathogenic microorganisms effectively. Additionally, the plant contains bioactive compounds that scavenge free radicals to prevent oxidative stress in biological systems. These dual properties make it a subject of interest for developing new natural medicines. Further studies continue to explore how these specific mechanisms can be utilized in clinical applications.

According to a study published by "Pakistan journal of pharmaceutical sciences", the ethyl acetate extract of Platycarya strobilacea infructescence demonstrated the best radical-scavenging activity, ferric reducing power, and antimicrobial activity in vitro. To investigate protective effects against oxidative stress in vivo, extracts were administered to rats for analysis of the serum, liver, and heart. The water extract and tannin polymers significantly decreased malonyldialdehyde (MDA) concentrations in the serum, liver, and heart compared to the control group (P<0.01). Additionally, the administration of all extracts increased the activities of superoxide dismutase (SOD) and glutathione per oxidase (GSOH-Px). These findings suggest that Platycarya strobilacea has potential for medicinal uses and food preservation.
