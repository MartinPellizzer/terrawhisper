Daphne altaica

What's the taxonomical classification of Daphne altaica?

Daphne altaica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is positioned in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic journey continues through the order Malvales and into the family Thymelaeaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Daphne, specifically identified by its unique species name, altaica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Daphne altaica has deciduous, opposite, lanceolate leaves that measure between 2 and 5 centimeters in length and slender, woody stems characterized by a spreading, shrubby growth habit. The plant typically reaches a height of 0.5 to 1 meter and develops a multi-stemmed structure. During its flowering period, it produces small, tubular, yellowish-white blossoms that are fragrant and emerge from the leaf axils. These flowers are often accompanied by small, fleshy drupes that turn red as they ripen. The bark on older stems becomes somewhat textured and grayish over time.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires well-drained, slightly alkaline soil and a location that provides full sun to partial shade to thrive.

It is a hardy shrub that prefers moist but not waterlogged growing conditions. When planting, ensure the root zone remains cool by applying a layer of organic mulch during the warmer months. Regular watering is necessary during establishment, though the mature plant can tolerate moderate periods of dryness.

Pruning should be done sparingly after the spring flowering period to maintain its natural shape.
