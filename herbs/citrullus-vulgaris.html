Watermelon (Citrullus vulgaris)

Watermelon (Citrullus vulgaris) is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, native to Southern Africa, Northern Africa, and Middle East. Traditionally, its fruits, seeds, and leaves have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its diuretic, anti-inflammatory, and expectorant actions, and has a long history of use in african traditional medicine, european herbal medicine, and mediterranean herbal traditions.
