What is Didymosperma tremulum?

Didymosperma tremulum is an evergreen palm species belonging to the family Arecaceae.

It has a slender stem with a height of up to 5 meters and a crown diameter of 2 meters. The leaves are pinnate with a length of 1-2 meters, bearing 100-200 leaflets that are 20-40 cm long and 1-2 cm wide. The inflorescence is a branched panicle up to 1 meter long, producing white flowers in a terminal cluster.

The fruit is a berry-like syncarp with a length of 5-7 cm, containing a single seed.

Additionally, Didymosperma tremulum has been found to exhibit analgesic and antipyretic effects, which can be attributed to the bioactive compound coumarin.

These constituents contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating various health conditions, including skin infections, wounds, and respiratory issues.

The main herbal preparations of Didymosperma tremulum are infusion, tincture, salve, tea, and decoction.
