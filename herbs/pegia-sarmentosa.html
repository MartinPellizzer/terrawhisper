Pegia sarmentosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pegia sarmentoosa has a climbing or scandent habit characterized by slender, woody stems and elliptical to oblong leaves with entire margins and acute apices. The foliage is typically arranged in an opposite pattern along the stems. Its flowers are small and often exhibit a pale coloration, emerging from the leaf axils. The reproductive structures include a capsule that serves as the primary fruit type for this species. These morphological features collectively allow the plant to navigate through dense tropical vegetation.
