Cassia (Cinnamomum Zeylanicum Var. Cassia)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Cinnamomum zeylanicum var. cassia.

Cassia, scientifically known as Cinnamomum zeylanicum var. cassia, is a medicinal herb and spice widely used for its therapeutic properties. It is prized for its rich content of essential oils, particularly coumarin and eugenol, which contribute to its distinct sweet and spicy flavor and numerous health benefits. Traditionally, Cassia has been used in Ayurvedic and Chinese medicine to support digestion, reduce inflammation, and promote mental clarity. In modern wellness practices, it is valued for its antioxidant and antimicrobial properties, often incorporated into teas, supplements, and topical remedies. With a long history dating back thousands of years, Cassia remains a versatile plant with unique cultural significance and a growing presence in holistic health approaches.
