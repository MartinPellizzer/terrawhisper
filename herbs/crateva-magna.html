Cape gooseberry

Crateva magna

Cape Gooseberry (Crateva magna) is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa and Asia, known for its oval-shaped, red-orange berries that are used in traditional medicine to treat various ailments, including digestive issues and skin conditions.

Botanical Identification

Cape Gooseberry, scientifically known as Crateva magna, belongs to the family Malvaceae. It is also called Soursop or Indian gooseberry in some regions. The plant is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia. It has hairy stems, heart-shaped leaves, and yellow flowers. Synonyms in herbal literature include Bael and Kaphal.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Eudicots
  • Order: Sapindales
  • Family: Sapindaceae
  • Genus: Crateva
  • Species: Crateva magna

Active Compounds

Cape Gooseberry contains key bioactive compounds such as tannins, flavonoids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in traditional herbal medicine.

Safety Profile

Cape Gooseberry is generally considered safe when consumed in typical food amounts. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. There is limited research on its safety during pregnancy and lactation, so caution is advised.
