What is Cape Gooseberry (Crateva Magna)?

Cape Gooseberry, or Crateva magna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas, commonly used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.

Botanical Identity and Classification

Plant Description and Morphology

Cape Gooseberry (Crateva magna) is a small, yellow or orange-fruited plant with a distinctive, thick, spiny calyx that encases the fleshy, edible berries, which are typically 1-2 cm in diameter and contain multiple seeds.

Cape Gooseberry (Crateva magna) is native to the tropical regions of Africa, particularly South Africa, and is also found in parts of Madagascar, India, and Southeast Asia.
