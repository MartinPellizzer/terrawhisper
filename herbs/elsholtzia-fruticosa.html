Elsholtzia fruticosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Elsholtzia fruticosa has pinnately compound leaves consisting of 7 to 15 small, oval, serrated leaflets arranged along a central rachis. The tree typically reaches a height of 5 to 10 meters and features a spreading, rounded crown with grayish-brown bark. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are borne in drooping yellowish-green panicles during the spring season. Following flowering, the plant produces small, winged samaras that are characteristic of the soapberry family. The overall structure is often multi-stemmed or shrub-like, especially in its natural woodland habitats.

    Elsholtzia fruticosa is prepared as decoctions, infusions, or tinctures primarily using its bark, leaves, or fruit.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Elsholtzia fruticosa can cause allergic skin reactions such as contact dermatitis, itching, or redness if the plant's sap or leaves touch sensitive skin. While the plant is generally considered non-toxic to humans, some individuals may experience respiratory irritation if they inhale excessive pollen during the flowering season. There is limited clinical data regarding its internal effects, so ingestion should be avoided to prevent potential gastrointestinal distress. Pet owners should also exercise caution as the long-term effects on animals have not been extensively studied. Always consult a medical professional if an unexpected reaction occurs after contact with the species.
