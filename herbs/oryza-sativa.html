Rice (Oryza sativa)

Rice (Oryza sativa) is a member of the Poaceae family, native to Asia, East Asia, and South Asia. Traditionally, its seeds, leaves, and stems have been used for decoctions, culinary uses, and infusions.

This herb is particularly valued for its tonic, diuretic, and anti-inflammatory actions, and has a long history of use in traditional chinese medicine, japanese kampo medicine, and korean traditional medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Annual herbaceous plant.
Height
Typical height ranges from 100 to 150 centimeters.
Leaves
Broad leaves with upper surface green and lower surface pale green, featuring prominent stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Inflorescence composed of spikelets arranged in a panicle, each spikelet bearing two sessile lemmae with a single floret, the floret enclosed in two glumes, the flower having three stamens and a single pistil, with actinomorphic symmetry and palea and lemma with distinct veins.
Stems
Cylindrical, hollow, node-based, with internodes of uniform thickness, possessing a distinct vascular bundle arrangement and a fibrous sheath at the base of each leaf attachment.
