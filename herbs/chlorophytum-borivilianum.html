Growing, Harvesting and Storage

Chlorophytum borivilianum grows best in well-drained, loamy soil that is rich in organic matter, with a pH range of 6.

0 to 7. 5, and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. It thrives in warm, humid climates and needs regular watering to maintain moist but not waterlogged soil, especially during the growing season. Regular weeding, mulching, and occasional fertilization with a balanced organic fertilizer will help promote healthy growth and maximize medicinal properties. Harvesting is best done during the dry season when the plant has matured, typically after 6-8 months, by cutting the entire plant at the base using a clean, sharp tool.

After harvesting, the roots should be washed, cut into small pieces, and dried in a cool, well-ventilated area or using a dehydrator to preserve potency, then stored in airtight containers in a cool, dark place to maintain their medicinal efficacy.
