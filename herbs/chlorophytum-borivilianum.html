Chlorophytum borivilianum

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Chlorophytum borivilianum, commonly known as Shatavari, belongs to the family Asparagaceae. It is a perennial herb native to the Indian subcontinent. The plant features tuberous roots, lanceolate leaves, and small white flowers. It is also known by regional names such as Shatavari and Dhat Pichhur.

Active Compounds

Chlorophytum borivilianum contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, saponins, and alkaloids. These compounds work synergistically to enhance its medicinal properties, supporting its use in traditional herbal medicine for various health benefits.

  • Flavonoid
  • Saponin
  • Alkaloid

Preparation Methods

Extract: Used for enhancing vitality and sexual health due to its adaptogenic properties.

Powder: Used for general health support and to improve physical endurance.

Capsule: Used for convenience in daily supplementation for energy and immunity.

Safety Profile

Chlorophytum borivilianum is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause mild gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and lactation due to limited safety data. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
