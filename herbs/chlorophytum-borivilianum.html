Chlorophytum borivilianum

Chlorophytum Borivilianum (Chlorophytum borivilianum) is a medicinal plant native to the Indian subcontinent, known for its potential health benefits and traditional use in Ayurvedic medicine. It is primarily used to improve male reproductive health and enhance vitality, particularly in conditions related to infertility and low sperm count.

Botanical Identification

Chlorophytum borivilianum, commonly known as Shatavari, belongs to the family Asparagaceae. It is a perennial herb native to the Indian subcontinent. The plant features tuberous roots, lanceolate leaves, and small white flowers. It is also known by regional names such as Shatavari and Dhat Pichhur.

Safety Profile

Chlorophytum borivilianum is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause mild gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and lactation due to limited safety data. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
