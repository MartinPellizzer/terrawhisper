Pleione yunnanensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Pleione yunnanensis?

Pleione yunnanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized into the order Asparagales. This plant is a member of the Orchidaceae family, specifically falling under the genus Pleione, with its specific designation being the species yunnanensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pleione yunnanensis has a pseudobulb that is ovoid in shape, producing a single, lanceolate, bright green leaf that typically reaches lengths of 10 to 20 centimeters.

The inflorescence emerges from the top of the pseudobulb, bearing a raceme of several fragrant, orchid-like flowers. These blossoms exhibit a white or pale pink base color, often accented with distinctive purple or magenta spots and streaks on the labellum. The petals and sepals are delicate and slightly spreading, creating a graceful appearance during the flowering season.

Small, fleshy roots extend from the base of the pseudobulb to anchor the plant into its organic substrate.

        What are the herbal preparations of Pleione yunnanensis?

        Pleione yunnanensis is prepared primarily as a decoction or a concentrated liquid extract derived from its underground tubers. Practitioners typically boil the dried tuberous parts in water to extract the active medicinal compounds for oral consumption.         What side-effects this plant can have?

        Pleione yunnanensis can cause localized skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, or mild digestive upset if ingested by humans or animals. While the plant is primarily grown for its ornamental beauty, the specific chemical composition of its tissues remains insufficiently studied for human consumption. Some individuals may experience redness, itching, or swelling upon direct contact with the foliage or roots. Because detailed toxicological data is limited, it is best to handle the plant with gloves to avoid potential sensitivities. Caution should always be exercised when keeping this species in environments where pets or small children might access it.
