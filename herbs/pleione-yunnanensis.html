Pleione yunnanensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Pleione yunnanensis?

Pleione yunnanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized into the order Asparagales. This plant is a member of the Orchidaceae family, specifically falling under the genus Pleione, with its specific designation being the species yunnanensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pleione yunnanensis has a pseudobulb that is ovoid in shape, producing a single, lanceolate, bright green leaf that typically reaches lengths of 10 to 20 centimeters. The inflorescence emerges from the top of the pseudobulb, bearing a raceme of several fragrant, orchid-like flowers. These blossoms exhibit a white or pale pink base color, often accented with distinctive purple or magenta spots and streaks on the labellum. The petals and sepals are delicate and slightly spreading, creating a graceful appearance during the flowering season. Small, fleshy roots extend from the base of the pseudobulb to anchor the plant into its organic substrate.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of Yunnan Province in southwestern China. It grows specifically within montane forests and rocky slopes where moisture levels are consistently high. The species is typically found at elevations ranging from 2000 to 3500 meters above sea level. These specific habitats provide the cool, temperate conditions necessary for its seasonal growth cycles. Consequently, its range is restricted to these specialized ecological niches within the Himalayan foothills.

What are the pharmacological activities of Pleione yunnanensis?

This plant has anti-lung-cancer, anti-tumor, clearing heat, detoxification, and dispersing nodules pharmacological activities. These medicinal properties allow the species to combat malignant cell growth and suppress various types of tumors. Its ability to clear heat and facilitate detoxification helps the body eliminate internal toxins and restore physiological balance. Additionally, the plant serves to disperse nodules, which is essential for reducing swelling and mass formation in affected tissues. These diverse therapeutic effects make Pleione yunnanensis a significant subject in traditional and modern pharmacological studies.

According to a study published by "Pharmaceuticaals (Basel, Switzerland)", Pleione yunnanensis is used as a component of Cremastrae Pseudobulbus Pleiones Pseudobulbus (CPPCPP) which has properties of clearing heat, detoxification, resolving phlegm, and dispersing nodules. This substance is frequently used in clinical practice for the treatment of various malignant tumors, especially lung cancer. The study successfully screened 11 predominant anti-lung-cancer active ingredients from CPPP. These effective ingredient combinations demonstrated anti-tumor effects equivalent to the original materials both in vitro and in vivo. Furthermore, research showed that BQZC can inhibit lung cancer cell growth and induce protective autophagy and apoptosis by activating the AMPK-mTOR-ULK1/BMF signaling pathway.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds bibenzyls, dihydrophenanthrenes, pleionesins A-C, shanchigusin E, and shanchigusin F. These diverse secondary metabolites are primarily classified as stilbenoid derivatives. The pleionesins A-C group represents a unique set of structurally complex molecules found specifically within this species. Additionally, the presence of shanchigusin E and shanchigusin F contributes to the plant's overall chemical profile. These compounds are often studied for their potential biological activities in pharmacological research.

According to a study published by "Chemical & pharmaceutical bulletin", the tubers of Pleione yunnanensis contain four new bibenzyl derivatives known as shancigusins A-D (1-4). In addition to these new compounds, five known bibenzyls (5-9) were also isolated from the plant. The structures of these 9 compounds were determined through extensive analyses of their spectroscopic data.
