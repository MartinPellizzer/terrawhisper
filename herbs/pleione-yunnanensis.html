Pleione yunnanensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pleione yunnanensis has a pseudobulb that is ovoid in shape, producing a single, lanceolate, bright green leaf that typically reaches lengths of 10 to 20 centimeters. The inflorescence emerges from the top of the pseudobulb, bearing a raceme of several fragrant, orchid-like flowers. These blossoms exhibit a white or pale pink base color, often accented with distinctive purple or magenta spots and streaks on the labellum. The petals and sepals are delicate and slightly spreading, creating a graceful appearance during the flowering season. Small, fleshy roots extend from the base of the pseudobulb to anchor the plant into its organic substrate.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of Yunnan Province in southwestern China. It grows specifically within montane forests and rocky slopes where moisture levels are consistently high. The species is typically found at elevations ranging from 2000 to 3500 meters above sea level. These specific habitats provide the cool, temperate conditions necessary for its seasonal growth cycles.
