Rubus amphidasys

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rubus amphidasys has woody, arching stems that are densely covered in curved, reddish-brown prickles and possess small, serrated leaves arranged in an alternate pattern. The foliage typically consists of trifoliate or palmately lobed leaflets with distinctively dentate margins and a slightly pubescent underside. Its flowers are characterized by numerous white petals surrounding a central cluster of stamens and pistils. Once pollinated, the plant develops small, aggregate fruits that transition from green to a deep red or black color upon ripening. The overall architecture of the plant reflects a sprawling, bramble-like growth habit common to the Rubus genus.
