Dabelowa Hawthorn (Crataegus × Dabelowa)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Crataegus × dabelowa.

Dabelowa hawthorn, scientifically known as Crataegus × dabelowa, is a hybrid shrub native to Central Europe, prized for its medicinal properties and adaptogenic qualities. This plant is traditionally used as a medicinal herb to support cardiovascular health, reduce anxiety, and promote relaxation due to its rich content of flavonoids and oligomeric proanthocyanidins. In traditional European herbal medicine, it has been valued for centuries to treat heart conditions, insomnia, and nervous disorders, often prepared as tinctures or teas. Modern wellness applications include its use in supplements for stress management and circulatory support, with research highlighting its potential to improve blood flow and lower blood pressure. Unique to this species is its distinctive floral aroma and the presence of rare compounds like rutin, which contribute to its reputation as a botanical with both historical and contemporary therapeutic significance.
