Nephelium lappaceum

What's the taxonomical classification of Nephelium lappaceum?

Nephelium lappaceum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its classification through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Sapindales. The plant is a member of the family Sapindaceae, falling under the genus Nephelium, and is specifically identified by its species name, lappaceum.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Nephelium lappaceum contains high concentrations of bioactive compounds including polyphenols, flavonoids, tannins, saponins, and various vitamins such as vitamin C. These chemical constituents provide significant antioxidant properties that help neutralize free radicals within the body. Specifically, the presence of ellagic acid and gallic acid supports anti-inflammatory and anti-diabetic activities. Some studies also suggest that the extracts possess antimicrobial and anticancer potential due to these complex phytochemical profiles. The diverse array of secondary metabolites makes the fruit and peel valuable for both nutritional supplementation and pharmacological research.

What are the therapeutic actions of Nephelium lappaceum?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various inflammatory conditions, digestive issues, skin infections, and respiratory ailments. Its medicinal properties are often applied to soothe stomach pain and resolve gastrointestinal distress. The plant extracts are also utilized to manage skin irritations and promote healing in minor wounds. Additionally, it serves as a traditional remedy for calming coughs and addressing bronchial discomfort. These therapeutic uses are rooted in its diverse chemical composition.

What are the herbal preparations of Nephelium lappaceum?

Nephelium lappaceum is prepared as decoctions, infusions, juices, or dried powders derived from its fruit, seeds, and leaves. The juice extracted from the fresh pulp is frequently consumed directly for its nutritional and hydrating properties. Dried leaf extracts are often steeped in hot water to create medicinal teas used in traditional practices. Additionally, the seeds can be processed into oils or ground into fine powders for topical or internal applications. These various preparations allow for the extraction of different bioactive compounds depending on the intended therapeutic use.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Nephelium lappaceum can cause gastrointestinal distress, allergic skin reactions, and potential complications for individuals with kidney issues due to its high potassium content. Excessive consumption of the fruit may lead to stomach upset, bloating, or diarrhea in sensitive individuals. Some people might experience contact dermatitis or itchy rashes if they have an underlying allergy to the plant's compounds. High intake of the fruit is particularly risky for those managing chronic kidney disease because the potassium load can strain renal function. It is also important to monitor consumption for individuals prone to blood sugar fluctuations due to the natural sugars present in the flesh.
