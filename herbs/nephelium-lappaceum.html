Nephelium lappaceum

What's the taxonomical classification of Nephelium lappaceum?

Nephelium lappaceum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its classification through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Sapindales. The plant is a member of the family Sapindaceae, falling under the genus Nephelium, and is specifically identified by its species name, lappaceum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Nephelium lappaceum has a woody tree structure reaching up to 30 meters in height, characterized by a dense, spreading crown and a trunk covered in brownish-grey bark.

The leaves are pinnate and consist of numerous small, glossy, dark green leaflets arranged along a central rachis. Its flowers are small, creamy-white, and grow in branched panicles emerging from the leaf axils. The most distinctive morphological feature is the fruit, which is a globose drupe covered in soft, fleshy red spines.

Inside the protective rind, a translucent, white aril surrounds a single seed.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Nephelium lappaceum contains bioactive compounds in its fruit pericarp, seeds, leaves, and bark that are utilized for their various therapeutic properties. The dried peel of the fruit is frequently prepared as a decoction to treat ailments such as diarrhea, dysentery, and skin infections. Extracts from the leaves are often applied topically or ingested to help manage inflammation and fever. The seeds are sometimes processed to address specific digestive issues or used in traditional topical applications. Additionally, the bark possesses astringent qualities that contribute to its use in treating various gastrointestinal disorders.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Nephelium lappaceum targets cyclooxygenase-2 enzymes, various inflammatory cytokines like TNF-alpha, and specific antioxidant enzymes to exert its medicinal effects. The plant's bioactive compounds, particularly polyphenols and flavonoids, act as potent inhibitors of the COX-2 protein to reduce systemic inflammation. These phytochemicals also modulate the expression of pro-inflammatory signaling pathways, which helps mitigate oxidative stress at a cellular level. Furthermore, certain extracts interact with glucose metabolism regulators to assist in managing blood sugar levels. This multi-target mechanism allows the plant to provide diverse therapeutic benefits ranging from anti-inflammatory to neuroprotective actions.

      What are the herbal preparations of Nephelium lappaceum?

      Nephelium lappaceum is prepared as decoctions, infusions, juices, or dried powders derived from its fruit, seeds, and leaves. The juice extracted from the fresh pulp is frequently consumed directly for its nutritional and hydrating properties. Dried leaf extracts are often steeped in hot water to create medicinal teas used in traditional practices. Additionally, the seeds can be processed into oils or ground into fine powders for topical or internal applications. These various preparations allow for the extraction of different bioactive compounds depending on the intended therapeutic use.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Nephelium lappaceum can cause gastrointestinal distress, allergic skin reactions, and potential complications for individuals with kidney issues due to its high potassium content. Excessive consumption of the fruit may lead to stomach upset, bloating, or diarrhea in sensitive individuals. Some people might experience contact dermatitis or itchy rashes if they have an underlying allergy to the plant's compounds. High intake of the fruit is particularly risky for those managing chronic kidney disease because the potassium load can strain renal function. It is also important to monitor consumption for individuals prone to blood sugar fluctuations due to the natural sugars present in the flesh.
