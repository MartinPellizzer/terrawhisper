Nephelium lappaceum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Nephelium lappaceum has a woody tree structure reaching up to 30 meters in height, characterized by a dense, spreading crown and a trunk covered in brownish-grey bark. The leaves are pinnate and consist of numerous small, glossy, dark green leaflets arranged along a central rachis. Its flowers are small, creamy-white, and grow in branched panicles emerging from the leaf axils. The most distinctive morphological feature is the fruit, which is a globose drupe covered in soft, fleshy red spines. Inside the protective rind, a translucent, white aril surrounds a single seed.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical rainforests of the Sundaland region, specifically spanning across Peninsular Malaysia, Sumatra, Borneo, and parts of the Malay Archipelago. It thrives primarily in lowland dipterocarp forests where high humidity and consistent rainfall are present. Within these ecosystems, the species is often found in shaded understory environments or along riverbanks. Its distribution is closely tied to the specific soil compositions and moisture levels found in these equatorial maritime climates. Consequently, its range is restricted to these highly specialized tropical habitats.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Nephelium lappaceum contains bioactive compounds in its fruit pericarp, seeds, leaves, and bark that are utilized for their various therapeutic properties. Extracts from the leaves are often applied topically or ingested to help manage inflammation and fever. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Nephelium lappaceum contains high concentrations of bioactive compounds including polyphenols, flavonoids, tannins, saponins, and various vitamins such as vitamin C. Specifically, the presence of ellagic acid and gallic acid supports anti-inflammatory and anti-diabetic activities. Some studies also suggest that the extracts possess antimicrobial and anticancer potential due to these complex phytochemical profiles. The diverse array of secondary metabolites makes the fruit and peel valuable for both nutritional supplementation and pharmacological research.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

The plant's bioactive compounds, particularly polyphenols and flavonoids, act as potent inhibitors of the COX-2 protein to reduce systemic inflammation.       What are the herbal preparations of Nephelium lappaceum?

      Nephelium lappaceum is prepared as decoctions, infusions, juices, or dried powders derived from its fruit, seeds, and leaves. The juice extracted from the fresh pulp is frequently consumed directly for its nutritional and hydrating properties. Dried leaf extracts are often steeped in hot water to create medicinal teas used in traditional practices. Additionally, the seeds can be processed into oils or ground into fine powders for topical or internal applications. These various preparations allow for the extraction of different bioactive compounds depending on the intended therapeutic use.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Nephelium lappaceum can cause gastrointestinal distress, allergic skin reactions, and potential complications for individuals with kidney issues due to its high potassium content. Excessive consumption of the fruit may lead to stomach upset, bloating, or diarrhea in sensitive individuals. Some people might experience contact dermatitis or itchy rashes if they have an underlying allergy to the plant's compounds. High intake of the fruit is particularly risky for those managing chronic kidney disease because the potassium load can strain renal function. It is also important to monitor consumption for individuals prone to blood sugar fluctuations due to the natural sugars present in the flesh.
