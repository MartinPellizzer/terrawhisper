Draba oreades

What's the taxonomical classification of Draba oreades?

Draba oreades belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is positioned within the order Brassicales. It further falls under the family Brassicaceae, which defines its botanical relationships. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is established through the genus Draba and the species oreades.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Draba oreades has a low-growing, tufted perennial habit characterized by densely packed basal rosettes of spoon-shaped to spatulate leaves that often feature ciliate margins.

The leaves are typically grayish-green due to a fine covering of hairs that help the plant withstand harsh alpine environments. During the flowering stage, the plant produces slender, erect scapes that bear small, four-petaled white flowers arranged in racemes. The siliques, or seed pods, are elongated and slightly compressed, developing along the stem as the plant matures.

These structural features collectively allow the species to thrive in the rocky, high-altitude habitats it inhabits.
