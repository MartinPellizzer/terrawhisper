What is Commiphora leptophloeos?

Commiphora leptophloeos is a species of tree in the family Burseraceae, native to tropical Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that grows up to 10 meters in height, with a straight trunk and a rounded crown. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, measuring 4-7 cm long and 1.5-3 cm wide, with a leathery texture and an entire margin. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in axillary racemes, which are followed by reddish-brown, 3-4 cm long fruit capsules containing 2-3 seeds.

The bark is reddish-brown, smooth, and exfoliating in small scales, with a distinctive odor.
