Chinese Angelica (Ligusticum Integrifolium)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Ligusticum integrifolium.

Chinese angelica, scientifically known as Ligusticum integrifolium, is a traditional medicinal herb widely used in Chinese medicine for its adaptogenic and therapeutic properties. This plant, often referred to as "Chinese celery," is valued for its ability to support the immune system, reduce inflammation, and promote circulation, making it a key ingredient in many herbal formulations. Historically, it has been used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat conditions such as colds, headaches, and digestive issues, and it holds a special place in Chinese culinary and medicinal culture. In modern wellness practices, Chinese angelica is incorporated into supplements and teas to enhance vitality and support respiratory health. Notably, it contains unique compounds like ligustilide, which contribute to its distinct aromatic flavor and potent medicinal effects, and it has been cultivated for centuries in China, reflecting its deep-rooted significance in both traditional and contemporary health practices.
