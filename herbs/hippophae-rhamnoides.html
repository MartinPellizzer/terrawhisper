Sea Buckthorn (Hippophae Rhamnoides)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Hippophae rhamnoides.

Sea Buckthorn, scientifically known as Hippophae rhamnoides, is a hardy shrub native to Asia and Europe, renowned for its medicinal properties and rich nutritional profile. It is often referred to as a medicinal herb and adaptogen, valued for its ability to support overall health and resilience. The fruit of the plant is particularly rich in vitamins, antioxidants, and essential fatty acids, offering primary benefits such as improved skin health, reduced inflammation, and enhanced immune function. Traditionally used in Ayurvedic and Chinese medicine, Sea Buckthorn has been employed to treat wounds, digestive issues, and respiratory ailments. In modern wellness, it is increasingly used in skincare products, dietary supplements, and functional foods, with its distinctive tart flavor and rare omega-7 compounds making it a unique and valuable botanical.
