Ulmus laciniata

What's the taxonomical classification of Ulmus laciniata?

Ulmus laciniata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within its taxonomic hierarchy, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Rosales, this plant is a member of the family Ulmaceae, which places it within the genus Ulmus. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by its species name, lacinia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ulmus laciniata has elliptical to lanceolate leaves with deeply serrated or lobed margins and asymmetrical bases. The leaf texture is often somewhat leathery, providing a sturdy structure to the foliage. Its branches tend to exhibit a spreading growth habit, creating a dense canopy of greenery. The bark of the tree typically becomes rough and fissured as the specimen matures. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge during the spring season prior to the full expansion of the leaves.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for bladder irritation, cystitis, interstitial cystitis, and kidney dysfunction. Its medicinal properties are often applied to soothe the lining of the urinary tract to alleviate discomfort. Practitioners use it to manage the chronic inflammation associated with interstitial cystitis. Additionally, it helps regulate functions related to kidney dysfunction to support overall renal health. This botanical application focuses primarily on calming the entire urinary system.

According to a study published by "The American journal of Chinese medicine", Ulmus laciniata, included as Ulmi radicis cortex in modified yukmijihwangtang, is evaluated for its therapeutic effects on bladder function and interstitial cystitis. In a rat model of chronic cystitis, the modified extract was administered in doses of 100, 200, or 500 mg/kg. This treatment notably improved bladder histological changes and suppressed IL-6/TNF α production after eight days. Furthermore, the extract inhibited LPS-induced NO production and IκB phosphorylation in a human uroepithelial cell line of T24 cells. The study suggests these findings regarding the modified formulation may support its use for treating interstitial cystitis.
