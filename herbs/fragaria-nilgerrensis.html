Fragaria nilgerrensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Fragaria nilgerrensis?

Fragaria nilgerrensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its placement in the order Rosales, the plant is a member of the family Rosaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Fragaria and the species nilgerrensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Fragaria nilgerrensis has a perennial growth habit characterized by stoloniferous spreading, trifoliate leaves with serrated margins, and small white flowers with five petals.

The leaves typically feature long petioles and a distinctively textured surface with prominent venation. During the flowering stage, the plant produces numerous delicate blossoms that emerge from the base of the foliage. These flowers eventually transition into small, aggregate fruits that develop from the receptacle.

The overall structure allows the plant to colonize ground space effectively through its creeping runners.
