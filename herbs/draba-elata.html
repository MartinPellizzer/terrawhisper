What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Draba elata has an erect, perennial growth habit characterized by a woody caudex and stems that typically reach heights of 5 to 25 centimeters. The leaves are mostly basal, forming a rosette of small, spatulate to linear shapes with entire or slightly dentate margins. As the plant ascends, it produces smaller, alternate cauline leaves that diminish in size toward the flowering tip. The inflorescence consists of small, four-petaled white flowers arranged in racemes. After flowering, the plant develops short, spreading siliques that contain numerous tiny seeds.
