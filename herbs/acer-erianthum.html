Acer erianthum

What's the taxonomical classification of Acer erianthum?

Acer erianthum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it is placed in the order Sapindales and the family Sapindaceae. Ultimately, this plant is identified by the genus Acer and the specific species name erianthum.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Acer erianthum contains bioactive secondary metabolites including various flavonoids, phenolic acids, and triterpenoids that contribute to its pharmacological potential. These chemical constituents are often studied for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Specifically, the presence of polyphenolic compounds helps neutralize free radicals within biological systems. Researchers investigate these specific extracts to determine their efficacy in managing oxidative stress. Such medicinal interest highlights the potential applications of the plant in natural therapeutic development.

What are the therapeutic actions of Acer erianthum?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various inflammatory conditions, digestive ailments, and skin disorders within traditional medicinal practices. Practitioners often employ its bark or leaves to address issues related to the gastrointestinal tract, such as stomach pain or indigestion. It is also utilized to mitigate swelling and soothe skin irritations through topical applications. Some herbal traditions suggest its efficacy in managing mild fevers and systemic discomfort. The specific therapeutic applications often depend on the preparation method used by the healer.
