What's the taxonomical classification of Trachyspermum roxburghianum?

Trachyspermum roxburghianum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Apiales. As a member of the family Apiaceae, it is represented by the genus Trachyspermum, with the specific epithet roxburghianum identifying the individual species.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", Trachyspermum roxburghianum is identified as one of the most commonly used plants for managing ailments in captive Asian elephants. The study, which was carried out between June 2015 and February 2016, involved a questionnaire survey among 50 selected informants. This research documented 53 plant species belonging to 29 families used to treat 23 types of ailments. Among the reported diseases, constipation was the most common ailment at 14.6%, followed by bloating at 8.7% and flatulence at 8.7%. Trachyspermum roxburghianum is utilized either independently or in combination to address these conditions.
