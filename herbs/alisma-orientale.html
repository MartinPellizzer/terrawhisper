Alisma orientale

What's the taxonomical classification of Alisma orientale?

Alisma orientale belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is situated in the order Alismatales and the family Alismataceae. It is a member of the genus Alisma, and its specific identification is defined by its species name, orientale.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Alisma orientale has slender, lanceolate leaves that measure up to 10 centimeters in length and terminal spikes of small, pink to purple flowers. The stems are typically smooth and can grow several feet tall in wetland environments. Each flower possesses a tubular corolla with distinctively shaped petals that create a delicate appearance. The plant also produces small, rounded seed capsules that develop after successful pollination. These morphological features allow the species to thrive in moist or saturated soil conditions.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, extending from the southeastern United States through Central America and into parts of South America. It is frequently found in wetland habitats across Florida, Texas, and various Caribbean islands. As an opportunistic species, it has expanded its range into many warm coastal areas where moisture is abundant. Its distribution is often limited by colder climates, as it requires consistent warmth to thrive. Consequently, it remains most prevalent in low-lying, humid environments across these specific latitudes.

What are the pharmacological activities of Alisma orientale?

This plant has anti-diabetic, anti-inflammatory, diuretic, hypoglycemic, and hypolipidemic pharmacological activities. Its hypoglycemic and anti-diabetic properties allow it to effectively regulate blood glucose levels in the body. The plant also functions as a diuretic to help manage fluid balance and excretion. Additionally, its anti-inflammatory effects assist in reducing swelling and pain throughout various tissues. Finally, the hypolipidemic activity contributes to the management of lipid profiles by lowering cholesterol and fat levels in the blood.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds alisol A, alisol B, alismoxide, spathulenol, and α-bisabolol. These specific sesquiterpenes are often studied for their potential biological activities and therapeutic properties. The presence of alismoxide and spathulenol suggests unique chemical characteristics that distinguish this species. Furthermore, the inclusion of α-bisabolol and the alisols provides a complex profile of bioactive molecules. Researchers analyze these components to understand how they contribute to the plant's overall medicinal value.

As discussed in a study, a quantitative method by high performance liquid chromatography was developed for ten triterpene constituents found in Alisma oriental. These specific medicinal compounds include alisols A, A monoacetate, B, B monoacetate, E 23-acetate, F, and G, as well as 13,17-epoxyalisol A, 11-deoxyalisols B, and B 23-acetate. The study used this HPLC method to examine the contents of these triterpene constituents in fresh rhizoma of Alisma oriental JUZEPC originated from China, Taiwan, and Japan. Investigations into the drying process of the Alisma oriental rhizoma revealed chemical changes in the constituents. Specifically, the bioactive triterpenes of Alismatis Rhizoma, such as alisol A and alisol A monoacetate, were found to be artificially formed during this drying process.
