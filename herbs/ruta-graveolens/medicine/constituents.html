10 Medicinal Constituents Of Ruta Graveolens (Rue)

Ruta graveolens has active constituents such as flavonoids, alkaloids, and terpenes.

These constituents possess anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which make rue a valuable herb in traditional medicine.

Rue's medicinal properties can improve people's lives by reducing inflammation and pain, promoting wound healing, and providing relief from respiratory issues like bronchitis and asthma.

1. Limonene

Ruta graveolens limonene is a natural compound extracted from the herb of the same name.

Ruta graveolens bergapten is a bioflavonoid compound found in the leaves and seeds of the Ruta graveolens plant.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, and has been shown to have potential therapeutic effects on various diseases, including cancer, cardiovascular disorders, and neurodegenerative diseases.

Bergapten is also a natural preservative and has been used as a food additive in some countries.

4. Rutamarin

Ruta graveolens rutamarin is a bioactive compound extracted from the herb Ruta graveolens.

Ruta graveolens flavonoids is a class of bioactive compounds found in the plant's leaves and stems.

Ruta graveolens phenolic acids is a class of bioactive compounds found in various parts of the plant.

7. Volatile oil

Ruta graveolens volatile oil is a bioactive compound extracted from the leaves and stems of the plant.

8. Furocoumarins

Ruta graveolens furocoumarins is a group of bioactive compounds found in its leaves and stems.

These potent chemicals are responsible for the plant's medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antibacterial effects.

Ruta graveolens coumarin derivatives is a class of bioactive compounds that have been isolated from various parts of the plant.

10. Quercetin

Ruta graveolens quercetin is a bioactive compound extracted from the leaves of the Ruta graveolens plant.

Quercetin has been found to exhibit numerous health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. It has been traditionally used to treat various health issues such as respiratory problems, allergies, and digestive disorders. Additionally, quercetin has shown potential in inhibiting cancer cell growth and reducing the risk of cardiovascular disease.

Its bioavailability is enhanced when consumed with food, making it a valuable natural remedy for overall well-being.
