Impatiens balsamina

What's the taxonomical classification of Impatiens balsamina?

Impatiens balsamina belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Ericales, the plant is placed in the family Balsaminaeaceace, which contains its specific genus, Impatiens. Finally, its unique biological identity is defined by its species designation, balsamina.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Impatiens balsamina has succulent, fleshy stems that branch out to support lanceolate leaves with serrated margins and a bright green color. The foliage is characterized by a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface that helps retain moisture. Its flowers are zygomorphic and emerge in clusters, displaying a wide range of vibrant colors including pink, red, and white. These blooms feature a prominent nectar spur at the back of the petals, which is a defining structural feature of the species. The plant also produces distinctive capsule-shaped seed pods that explode when touched to disperse seeds.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including countries such as India, China, and various islands throughout the Malay Archipelago. While its biological origins are confined to these warm, humid climates, it has been widely naturalized in many other parts of the world through cultivation. In temperate zones, it is commonly grown as an ornamental bedding plant in gardens and parks across North America and Europe. Because it thrives in moist, shaded environments, its distribution is often concentrated in areas with consistent rainfall or artificial irrigation. It remains primarily a cultivated species rather than a widespread wild invasive in most non-tropical territories.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Impatiens balsamina contains medicinal compounds primarily found in its succulent leaves and stems. These parts of the plant are often harvested to create topical applications for treating skin irritations and minor inflammations. The plant's juices can be used to soothe itching or to assist in the healing of small abrasions. Some traditional practices also involve using the crushed foliage to reduce swelling in localized areas. What are the pharmacological activities of Impatiens balsamina?

This plant has anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, antioxidant, antitumor, and anticancer pharmacological activities derived from its diverse phytochemical constituents. The presence of flavonoids and phenolic compounds contributes significantly to its ability to neutralize free radicals through antioxidant mechanisms. These bioactive molecules also help mitigate swelling and pain by inhibiting specific inflammatory pathways in the body. Furthermore, the plant demonstrates potent antimicrobial properties that allow it to combat various bacterial and fungal pathogens. Research also suggests that its chemical profile provides inhibitory effects against malignant cell growth, highlighting its potential antitumor and anticancer capabilities.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds hosenkols, kaempferol, lawsone, flavonoids, and naphthoquinones. These specific chemical constituents contribute to the plant's diverse therapeutic properties and biological activities. Within this profile, the naphthoquinone group includes the bioactive molecule lawsone, which is known for its antimicrobial effects. The presence of various flavonoids, such as kaempferol, provides significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory benefits to the organism. Together, these diverse chemical structures define the complex pharmacological potential of the species.

As discussed in a study, the leaves and stems of Impatiens balsamina contain various metabolite groups including phenolic, organic, and amino acids. The plant contains several secondary metabolites from the groups of benzopyrones, benzofuranones, naphthoquinones, alkaloids, and flavonoids. Bioactive components detected in this study include kaempferol, quercetin and their glycosylated forms, and lawsone and its derivatives. Analysis showed that aqueous methanol extracts flavonoids better than water, with flavonoids mostly detected in leaf samples. The score plots revealed a minor variance in metabolite profiles between water and aqueous methanolic extracts, showing 21.5% of the total variance for the first principal component in positive ion mode and 30.5% in negative ion mode.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for the treatment of dermatitis, atopic dermatitis, and the relief of pruritus. Its medicinal properties help to soothe inflamed skin and reduce intense itching sensations. The plant contains compounds that can assist in managing infections caused by bacteria such as Staphylococcus aureus. It also shows potential in addressing complications involving Escherichia coli. By applying these extracts, patients may find relief from various inflammatory skin conditions.

As discussed in a study, a 35% ethanol extract (IB) from the petals of Impatiens balsamina L. was examined for its effects on pruritus and dermatitis in NC mice. IB at 100 mg/kg significantly inhibited serious scratching behaviour when administered i.v. 1 h before or p.o. 24 h before the measurement. A 10 microg/kg dose of kaempferol 3-rutinoside and 2-hydroxy-1,4-naphthoquinone (lawsone) isolated from IB also inhibited scratching behaviour. When 4-week-old NC mice were administered orally 100 mg/kg/day of IB until 13 weeks of age, protection was noted against scratching behaviour during the development of dermatitis. IB was effective for the prevention and treatment of atopic dermatitis.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause skin irritation upon contact and severe gastrointestinal distress if any part of it is ingested. People who consume the plant may experience nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and intense stomach pain. These adverse reactions typically occur when the plant's internal compounds interact with the digestive system or sensitive tissues. It is important to avoid touching the leaves or stems to prevent redness or itching on the skin. Always wash your hands thoroughly if you accidentally come into contact with this species.
