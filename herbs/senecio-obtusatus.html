Senecio obtusatus

What's the taxonomical classification of Senecio obtusatus?

Seneciio obtusatus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further categorized under the order Asterales. It is a member of the prominent family Asteraceae, which places it within the genus Senecio, and is specifically identified by its species name, obtusatus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Senecio obtusatus has fleshy, succulent, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and arranged in dense, basal rosettes. These leaves possess blunt, rounded tips and a smooth texture designed to store moisture. The plant produces small, yellow, daisy-like flower heads that sit atop slender, leafless stalks. Each flower head consists of numerous tiny disc florets surrounded by a ring of ray florets. As the plant matures, the foliage often develops a slightly glaucous coating that gives it a subtle waxy appearance.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the coastal regions of southern and eastern Africa, specifically found within South Africa, Namibia, and Mozambique. It primarily inhabits rocky shorelines and sandy coastal dunes where it can access salt spray and maritime air. The species is most frequently documented along the temperate shores of the Cape provinces. Its distribution is closely tied to the presence of maritime influences and specific coastal soil compositions. Because it thrives in these specialized seaside niches, it is rarely found deep within inland terrestrial ecosystems.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires well-draining sandy or gritty soil and full sunlight to thrive in a warm, Mediterranean-style climate.

Because it is a succulent, you must allow the substrate to dry out completely between watering sessions to prevent root rot. It performs best when planted in containers or rock gardens where drainage is easily managed. During the winter months, reduce irrigation significantly to accommodate its dormant period.

Regular maintenance involves removing dead foliage to encourage fresh growth.
