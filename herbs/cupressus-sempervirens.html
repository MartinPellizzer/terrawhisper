Italian Cypress

Cupressus sempervirens

Italian Cypress (Cupressus sempervirens) is a tall, evergreen coniferous tree native to the Mediterranean region, known for its aromatic wood and historical use in traditional medicine. It has been traditionally used to support respiratory health and improve symptoms of conditions such as asthma and bronchitis.

Botanical Identification

The Italian Cypress, scientifically known as Cupressus sempervirens, belongs to the family Cupressaceae. It is also called the Italian cypress or Mediterranean cypress. A perennial evergreen tree native to the Mediterranean region, it has narrow, scale-like leaves and a tall, conical shape. It is commonly referenced in herbal texts under various synonyms, including cypress and cypress tree.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Coniferophyta
  • Class: Pinopsida
  • Order: Pinales
  • Family: Cupressaceae
  • Genus: Cupressus
  • Species: Cupressus sempervirens

Active Compounds

The Italian Cypress contains essential oil, terpenoids, and tannins as its main bioactive compounds. These compounds work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

  • Essential oil
  • Terpenoid
  • Tannin

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for respiratory and digestive issues due to its warming and antiseptic properties.

Infusion: Used for mild respiratory ailments and as a calming herbal tea.

Essential oil: Used for skin conditions and respiratory support through aromatherapy.

Safety Profile

The Italian Cypress is generally safe when used in moderate amounts. However, it may cause skin irritation in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and should be avoided by people with hypersensitivity to cypress or related plants. Always consult a healthcare professional before use.
