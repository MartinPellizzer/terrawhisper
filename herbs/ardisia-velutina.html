What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ardisia velutinina has elliptical to oblong leaves that are characterized by a distinct velvety texture on their undersides due to a dense covering of fine hairs. The foliage is typically dark green and glossy on the upper surface, while the lower surface displays a much paler, pubescent appearance. Its stems are woody and can develop into small trees or shrubs depending on the environment. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually give way to fleshy berries.
