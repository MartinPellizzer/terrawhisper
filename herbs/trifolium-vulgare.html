White Clover (Trifolium vulgare)

White Clover (Trifolium vulgare) is a member of the Fabaceae family, native to Europe, North America, and Asia. Traditionally, its leaves, flowers, and seeds have been used for infusions, decoctions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its diuretic, anti-inflammatory, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, traditional chinese medicine, and mediterranean herbal traditions.

Botanical Description

Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 20 to 50 centimeters.
Leaves
Laminae trifoliate with stipitate bases, upper surface glabrous and pale green, lower surface pubescent and lighter green.
Flowers
Inflorescence consists of a dense spike of small white zygomorphic flowers with five sepals, two lateral petals, one enlarged lip petal, and ten stamens arranged in two whorls.
Stems
Cylindrical, hollow, four-angled, glabrous, ascending, opposite branching.
