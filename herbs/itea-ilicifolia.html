Itea ilicifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Itea ilicifolia has small, leathery, evergreen leaves that are elliptical to oblong in shape and possess serrated margins reminiscent of holly. The foliage typically exhibits a dark green color on the upper surface with a slightly paler underside. These leaves are arranged alternately along woody, slender stems that form a dense, shrubby habit. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that emerge from the axils of the leaves during its blooming period. Following flowering, it develops small, fleshy red berries that serve as a key identifying feature of the species.

This plant is native to the eastern United States, ranging from southern New England through the Appalachian Mountains and down to the Gulf Coast. While it thrives in these humid areas, it can also be found in scattered populations across the mid-Atlantic states.
