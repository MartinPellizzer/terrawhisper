What's the taxonomical classification of Itea ilicifolia?

Itea ilicifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Saxifragales. This plant is specifically nested within the family Iteaceae and the genus Itea, ultimately reaching its unique taxonomic designation at the species level as iliçifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Itea ilicifolia has small, leathery, evergreen leaves that are elliptical to oblong in shape and possess serrated margins reminiscent of holly. These leaves are arranged alternately along woody, slender stems that form a dense, shrubby habit. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that emerge from the axils of the leaves during its blooming period.
