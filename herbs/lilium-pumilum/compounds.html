Little Lily (Lilium pumilum) Compounds

Little Lily contains several primary active compounds, including flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties. Glycosides and phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory effects. Terpenoids and tannins support digestive health, while coumarin and saponins may aid in circulation. Essential oils and anthocyanins provide additional therapeutic benefits, and alkaloids contribute to its medicinal potency.

Little Lily (Lilium pumilum) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Little Lily (Lilium pumilum) are bioactive compounds known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. They contribute to the plant's traditional use in supporting respiratory and circulatory health. These compounds are often extracted to harness their therapeutic benefits in herbal formulations.

2. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Little Lily (Lilium pumilum). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by supporting respiratory and cardiovascular health. Glycosides in Little Lily are known for their soothing and anti-inflammatory effects. They are typically extracted from the plant's roots and flowers.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid in Little Lily (Lilium pumilum) contributes to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound helps protect cells from oxidative damage and may support immune function. It is found in the bulbs and is commonly used in traditional herbal medicine for its therapeutic effects.

4. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Little Lily (Lilium pumilum) are a group of bioactive compounds known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and skin conditions. They are extracted from the bulbs and have shown potential in modern herbal medicine research.

5. Tannin

Tannin in Little Lily (Lilium pumilum) contributes to its astringent properties. It helps in reducing inflammation and supporting wound healing. Tannins also act as antioxidants, protecting cells from damage. This compound is commonly used in traditional herbal medicine for its therapeutic effects.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Little Lily (Lilium pumilum). It contributes to the plant's mild sedative and anti-inflammatory properties. Found in the roots and rhizomes, coumarin supports traditional uses in herbal medicine for calming and pain relief.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Little Lily (Lilium pumilum). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponin helps support respiratory and digestive health, making it valuable in herbal medicine. Its presence enhances the therapeutic potential of Little Lily in traditional treatments.

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of Little Lily (Lilium pumilum) contains bioactive compounds with anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It is traditionally used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health and reduce skin irritation. The oil is extracted through steam distillation, preserving its therapeutic potency.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Little Lily (Lilium pumilum). It contributes to the plant's vibrant colors and has potent antioxidant properties. Found in the petals and bulbs, anthocyanin supports anti-inflammatory and immune-boosting effects in traditional herbal medicine.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid in Little Lily (Lilium pumilum) contributes to its medicinal properties. It is known for its potential effects on the nervous system and is often studied for its therapeutic applications.
