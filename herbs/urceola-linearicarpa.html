Urceola linearicarpa

What's the taxonomical classification of Urceola linearicarpa?

Urceola linearicarpa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is categorized under the order Gentianales and the family Apocynaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Urceola, and its specific epithet, linearicarpa.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, stomach aches, coughs, and various types of inflammation. Local healers often prepare decoctions from its leaves to address respiratory issues and digestive distress. It is also frequently applied topically to soothe skin irritations or wounds. Some traditional practices utilize the plant to manage fever and general body pains. These medicinal applications are central to its use in specific regional folk medicine.
