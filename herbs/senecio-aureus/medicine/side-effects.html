1. Leads to coma

Senecio aureus causes allergic reactions when its sap comes into contact with skin.

The allergenic compound senkirkine in the plant's sap triggers an immune response, leading to symptoms such as redness, itching, and swelling.

This reaction can occur even at low concentrations of the sap, making it important for individuals to avoid handling or touching the plant unnecessarily.

2. Leads to coma

Senecio aureus triggers asthma attacks due to the presence of alkaloids, particularly senegin and retrorsine, which can cause airway constriction and inflammation.

These compounds can stimulate the release of histamine, a chemical mediator that exacerbates bronchospasm and further constricts airways.

The resulting symptoms include wheezing, shortness of breath, and coughing, which can be severe in individuals with pre-existing asthma or respiratory conditions.

3. Leads to coma

Senecio aureus induces vomiting due to its high concentration of pyrrolizidine alkaloids.

These toxic compounds are responsible for disrupting the liver and kidneys' normal functioning, leading to nausea and vomiting in some individuals who consume the plant.

The severity of this side effect can vary greatly depending on the individual's sensitivity to these toxins and the amount of Senecio aureus consumed.

4. Leads to coma

Senecio aureus affects liver function due to its ability to cause pyrrolizidine alkaloids (PAs) to accumulate in the body.

These PAs can bind to liver tissue, leading to damage and potentially causing chronic liver disease, cirrhosis, or even liver cancer.

The toxic effects of Senecio aureus on the liver are thought to result from the bioaccumulation of PAs over time, making it essential for individuals using this herb to be aware of these potential risks.

5. Leads to coma

Senecio aureus increases bleeding risk due to its ability to slow blood clotting.

This is attributed to its containing pyrrolizidine alkaloids, which are toxic compounds that can damage the liver and cause vitamin K deficiency.

As a result, people taking Senecio aureus may experience easy bruising or prolonged bleeding from cuts or wounds, increasing their risk of hemorrhage.

6. Leads to coma

Senecio aureus disrupts digestion due to its content of pyrrolizidine alkaloids, which can cause irritation and inflammation in the gastrointestinal tract.

These compounds can alter the normal balance of gut flora and lead to changes in bowel habits, nausea, and abdominal pain.

The disruption can be attributed to the way these alkaloids interact with enzymes involved in digestion, ultimately affecting the body's ability to break down nutrients properly.

7. Leads to coma

Senecio aureus provokes skin irritation due to its high concentration of sesquiterpene lactones and alkaloids.

These compounds can cause an allergic reaction in some individuals, leading to redness, itching, and inflammation on the skin. As these compounds are absorbed through the skin, they can trigger a chemical response that results in uncomfortable symptoms.

The severity of the irritation may vary depending on individual sensitivities and exposure levels.

8. Leads to coma

Senecio aureus lowers blood pressure.

This is due to its ability to relax the muscles in the blood vessel walls, thereby increasing blood flow and reducing peripheral vascular resistance.

The plant's flavonoids, particularly quercetin, are responsible for this effect, which can lead to a decrease in blood pressure.

9. Leads to coma

Senecio aureus interacts with medications due to its ability to stimulate liver enzymes.

This can lead to reduced effectiveness of certain medicines, such as anticoagulants and blood thinners, and may cause increased levels of toxic substances in the body.

The interaction is thought to occur because Senecio aureus contains alkaloids that affect the metabolism of these medications.

10. Leads to coma

Senecio aureus worsens respiratory issues due to its content of pyrrolizidine alkaloids, which can accumulate in the lungs and cause inflammation, bronchitis, and even pulmonary fibrosis.

As these alkaloids are absorbed into the bloodstream, they can also affect the heart and liver, further exacerbating respiratory problems.

This toxicity can lead to chronic respiratory issues, making breathing more difficult and increasing the risk of serious health complications.
