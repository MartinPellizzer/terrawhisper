Scientific and Botanical Profile

Calea, with botanical name Calea sp.

C, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Acanthaceae, commonly known as the acanthus family. This diverse group includes numerous species such as Calea zonata, Calea zacatechica, and Calea zanzibarensis, which are collectively referred to as Calea, Calea Sp., or Calea Species. Native to regions across Mexico, Central America, the Caribbean, and parts of South America, including Brazil, these plants are found in areas ranging from Northern Mexico to the West Indies. Morphologically, Calea is characterized by its whorled arrangement of leaves, often with a bluish or silver hue, and its showy, blue or purple flower clusters that resemble mist or bells, giving rise to common names like Blue Mistflower, Bluebell Flower, and Blue Mist Flower.

Additionally, species such as Whorled False Dandelion, Whorled False Geranium, and Whorled Milkweed reflect the plant's unique leaf structure, while Mexican Calea and Common Calea highlight its regional and general significance.

Growing, Harvesting and Storage

Calea sp.

c grows in well-drained, loamy soil that is rich in organic matter, preferring a slightly acidic to neutral pH. Regular watering is essential, especially during dry periods, but care should be taken to avoid overwatering, which can lead to root rot.
