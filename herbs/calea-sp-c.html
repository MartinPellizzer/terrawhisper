Calea Zonata (Calea sp. c)

Calea Zonata (Calea sp. c) is a member of the Acanthaceae family, native to Central America, and Southern United States. Traditionally, its leaves, stems, and roots have been used for infusions, decoctions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its bitter, anti-inflammatory, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in african traditional medicine, native american herbal medicine, and european herbal medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically reaches a height of 30 to 80 centimeters.
Leaves
Smooth, elliptic leaves with upper surface glabrous and pale green, lower surface glaucous and covered with stomatal bands.
Flowers
Radial symmetry, white to pale yellow petals arranged in a solitary flower with five lobes, central disk with prominent stamens and a single pistil, stipitate base, and marginal staminodes.
Stems
Cylindrical, erect, woody stems with opposite branching, smooth surface, and nodes bearing leaf scars.
