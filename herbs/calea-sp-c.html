What to know about Calea sp. c (dream herb) before using it medicinally

Calea sp.

c, commonly known as dream herb, is a plant with a long history of use in traditional medicine for its ability to promote relaxation and reduce anxiety. This annual herb is relatively easy to cultivate, requiring minimal care and maintenance, and is often grown in warm and humid climates.

Botanically, Calea sp. c is classified as a member of the Compositae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and broad, ovate leaves. References to the use of Calea sp.

c in traditional rituals and ceremonies can be found in the ancient cultures of the Americas, where it was highly valued for its psychoactive properties.

This article explains the medicinal, horticultural, botanical, and historical aspects of Calea sp. c.

What are the medicinal properties of Calea sp. c?

Calea sp. c helps with anxiety, insomnia, and restlessness due to its sedative and relaxing properties. It is also used to treat depression, headaches, and migraines. Its calming effects promote relaxation and reduce stress.

The active constituents of Calea sp. c include flavonoids, terpenoids, and sesquiterpenes, which are responsible for its medicinal properties. These compounds have been shown to have anxiolytic and sedative effects, as well as anti-inflammatory properties.

The aerial parts of Calea sp. c, particularly the leaves and flowers, are most commonly used for medicinal purposes. These parts are rich in essential oils and contain a high concentration of active constituents. The leaves and flowers are typically dried and used in tea form or as an extract.

Improper use of Calea sp. c can lead to drowsiness, dizziness, and headaches. High doses may also cause stomach upset and nausea. Prolonged use can result in dependence and withdrawal symptoms upon cessation.

Precautions include pregnant or breastfeeding women, children under 12, and individuals with liver or kidney disease. Calea sp. c should also be avoided with other sedatives, tranquilizers, or medications. Consult a healthcare professional before using it for medicinal purposes.

What are the horticulural aspects of Calea sp. c?

Calea sp. c grow in full sun to partial shade with warm temperatures between 18-30°C. It requires well-draining soil with a pH range of 6.0-7.0. Adequate watering is necessary, but the soil should not be waterlogged.

Proper planting involves selecting a spot with adequate air circulation, as dream herb is sensitive to fungal diseases. The seeds should be planted 1-2 cm deep, 30-60 cm apart, and in rows 60-90 cm apart. Water the soil gently but thoroughly after planting.

Dream herb is ready for harvesting 6-8 months after planting, when the flowers are in full bloom. Pinch or cut off the flower stalks to encourage new growth and prevent seed production. Harvest the leaves and flowers in the morning, after the dew has evaporated.

Common pests affecting dream herb include aphids, whiteflies, and spider mites. Fungal diseases such as leaf spot, powdery mildew, and root rot can also occur. Regular monitoring and maintenance can help prevent infestations and diseases, and prevent the spread of disease to other plants.
