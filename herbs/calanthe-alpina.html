Calanthe alpina

What's the taxonomical classification of Calanthe alpina?

Calanthe alpina belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further categorized under the order Asparagales. It is a member of the family Orchidaceae, specifically situated within the genus Calanthe, and is identified by its unique species name, alpina.

      What are the herbal preparations of Calanthe alpina?

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Calanthe alpina can cause skin irritation, digestive upset, or allergic reactions in sensitive individuals. Ingestion of any part of the orchid could lead to nausea, stomach cramping, or vomiting. Allergic responses might also manifest as respiratory discomfort or itching in those with high sensitivity to botanical alkaloids.
