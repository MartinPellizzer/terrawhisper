Calanthe alpina

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Calanthe alpina has an upright growth habit characterized by slender, lanceolate leaves that emerge from a creeping rhizome and small, elliptical, white to pale pink flowers arranged in a terminal raceme. The leaves are typically smooth and taper to a fine point, providing a lush green backdrop for the delicate blooms. Each flower features a complex structure with a prominent, often colored, labellum that serves to attract specific pollinators. The stems are relatively thin and flexible, allowing the plant to navigate through montane forest debris. This orchid's overall structure is refined and adapted to its high-altitude environment.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

These populations are typically restricted to elevations ranging from 3,000 to 4,500 meters above sea level. Due to its preference for cold, montane environments, its distribution is closely tied to specific microclimates within these mountain ranges.
