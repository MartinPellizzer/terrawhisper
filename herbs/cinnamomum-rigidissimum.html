What's the taxonomical classification of Cinnamomum rigidissimum?

Cinnamomum rigidissimum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Laurales. Furthermore, this plant is a member of the family Lauraceae, specifically falling under the genus Cinnamomum, with the specific epithet rigidissimum identifying the individual species.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cinnamomum rigidissimum has coriaceous, lanceolate leaves that are characterized by an entire margin and a distinctly stiff, leathery texture. These evergreen leaves typically feature an acute apex and a cuneate base, often displaying a deep green color on the upper surface. The arrangement of the foliage is alternate along the woody stems of the shrub or small tree. Smaller morphological details include a prominent midrib and subtle secondary venation that provides structural integrity to the foliage.
