Urtica mairei

What's the taxonomical classification of Urtica mairei?

Urtica mairei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized into the order Rosales. It is further categorized under the family Urticaceae, falling within the genus Urtica, and is ultimately identified by its specific species name, mairei.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Urtica mairei has ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins and a dense covering of stinging trichomes along its stems and foliage. The plant typically exhibits a perennial growth habit with upright, herbaceous stems that can reach various heights depending on its environment. Its leaf arrangement is opposite, featuring prominent venation that extends from the central midrib to the edges. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in clusters, characteristic of the Urticaceae family. The overall structure is defined by its bristly texture which serves as a primary defensive mechanism.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

These populations are often restricted to specific microclimates such as humid ravines or laurel forests. Because of its limited range, the species is highly localized to these unique Macaronesian habitats.
