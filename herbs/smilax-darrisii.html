What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Smilax darrisii has climbing stems equipped with axillary prickles, simple or trifoliate leaves with prominent veins, and small greenish-white flowers arranged in axillary clusters. The leaves typically exhibit an ovate to lanceolate shape with entire or slightly wavy margins. These woody vines utilize their sharp thorns to scramble through dense forest undergrowth. Small, fleshy berries develop from the flowers and serve as a primary fruit characteristic of the species. The plant's overall structure is defined by its ability to weave through surrounding vegetation using its climbing habit.
