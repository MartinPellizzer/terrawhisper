Hops (Humulus lupulus) Compounds

The primary compounds in hops include flavonoids, which contribute to its bitter flavor and antioxidant properties. Coumarin is a fragrant compound with potential anti-inflammatory effects. Terpenoids, especially humulone, are responsible for the characteristic bitterness. Essential oils provide aromatic qualities, while phenolic acids and glycosides contribute to its medicinal and antimicrobial properties.

Hops (Humulus lupulus) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Hops (Humulus lupulus). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting digestive health and calming effects. These compounds are also responsible for the bitter taste and some of the therapeutic benefits associated with hop usage in herbal medicine.

2. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in hops (Humulus lupulus), known for its antispasmodic and sedative effects. It contributes to the calming properties of hops, often used in herbal remedies for anxiety and insomnia. Coumarin also has mild anticoagulant properties, though its use is limited due to potential toxicity at high doses.

3. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in hops, Humulus lupulus, and contribute to its medicinal properties. They play a key role in the plant's traditional use for calming effects and may support digestive and nervous system health. Specific terpenoids like humulene and myrcene are known for their potential anti-inflammatory and sedative actions.

4. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound in hops (Humulus lupulus), contributing to its use in herbal medicine. It contains volatile aromatic compounds like myrcene and humulene, which have sedative, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. This oil is commonly used in formulations for relaxation, sleep support, and digestive health.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in hops (Humulus lupulus), contributing to its medicinal properties. It possesses anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, supporting digestive health and calming nervous system activity. This compound plays a vital role in the therapeutic applications of hops in herbal medicine.

6. Glycoside

Glycosides are a class of compounds found in hops (Humulus lupulus), contributing to their medicinal properties. These compounds, such as humulone and lupulone, have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. They play a key role in the traditional use of hops for digestive and calming purposes.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in hops (Humulus lupulus). It contributes to the plant's bitterness and has potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponin may support digestive health and is used in traditional herbal medicine for its detoxifying effects.

8. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in hops (Humulus lupulus). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and plays a role in reducing inflammation and supporting digestive health. Tannins also help preserve the plant's medicinal qualities and are used in traditional herbal formulations.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloids are a class of naturally occurring compounds found in Hops (Humulus lupulus). They contribute to the plant's medicinal properties, including sedative and anti-inflammatory effects. Specific alkaloids in hops, such as lupulins, are responsible for its calming and sleep-inducing qualities. These compounds are often used in herbal medicine to support relaxation and sleep.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a pigment found in Hops (Humulus lupulus). It contributes to the plant's color and has antioxidant properties. In herbal medicine, anthocyanin supports anti-inflammatory and circulatory benefits. It is often used in formulations for its potential to improve vascular health.
