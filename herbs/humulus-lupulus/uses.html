Hops (Humulus lupulus) Uses

Hops are used to treat various gastrointestinal issues such as inflammatory bowel disease, gastroesophageal reflux disease, and stomach ulcers. They help reduce inflammation and soothe digestive discomfort, cramps, and gas. Hops may also alleviate gastrointestinal spasms and promote overall digestive health.

Hops (Humulus lupulus) Monograph

1. Inflammatory bowel disease

Hops have shown potential in managing inflammatory bowel disease due to their anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Compounds like humulene and lupulone may reduce intestinal inflammation and support gut health. Clinical studies suggest hop extracts could aid in symptom relief, though more research is needed for standardized therapeutic use.

2. Gastroesophageal reflux disease

Hops are used in herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms of gastroesophageal reflux disease due to their anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. They help reduce stomach acid production and soothe esophageal irritation, making them a supportive remedy for digestive discomfort and heartburn.

3. Gastrointestinal cramps

Hops have been traditionally used to alleviate gastrointestinal cramps due to their antispasmodic properties. The compounds in hops, such as humulene and lupuline, help relax smooth muscles in the digestive tract. This makes hops effective in reducing cramping and discomfort associated with conditions like irritable bowel syndrome.

4. Gastrointestinal spasms

Hops contain compounds that help relax gastrointestinal muscles, alleviating spasms. They reduce cramping and inflammation in the digestive tract. Used in herbal formulations, hops support digestive comfort and ease symptoms of conditions like irritable bowel syndrome.

5. Stomach ulcers

Hops contain compounds that may help reduce stomach acid and protect the gastric lining. They have been traditionally used to soothe ulcers by promoting healing and reducing inflammation. However, their effectiveness for ulcers is still being studied, and they should not replace medical treatment.

6. Digestive upset

Hops are used to alleviate digestive upset by calming the nervous system and reducing intestinal spasms. They may help with symptoms like bloating, gas, and cramping. Typically prepared as a tea, hops can soothe the digestive tract and promote relaxation of the gastrointestinal muscles.

7. Gastrointestinal discomfort

Hops are used to alleviate gastrointestinal discomfort by reducing inflammation and soothing the digestive tract. They may help relieve symptoms such as bloating, gas, and mild stomach cramps. Traditionally, hops have been used in herbal teas and formulations to support digestive health. Their calming effects on the gut may also ease nausea and promote digestion.

8. Gastrointestinal gas

Hops are used to alleviate gastrointestinal gas by relaxing smooth muscle in the digestive tract. Their sedative and antispasmodic properties reduce bloating and discomfort. Traditionally, they are combined with other herbs like peppermint or ginger for enhanced effectiveness. This use is supported by historical herbal practices and some modern studies.

9. Stomach cramps

Hops are used to relieve stomach cramps due to their antispasmodic properties. They help relax the smooth muscles of the digestive tract, reducing pain and discomfort. This effect is often utilized in herbal remedies for digestive issues. Hops are typically combined with other herbs like ginger or peppermint for enhanced relief.

10. Digestive disorders

Hops are used to support digestive health by reducing inflammation and soothing the gastrointestinal tract. They may help alleviate symptoms of indigestion, bloating, and irritable bowel syndrome. Their antimicrobial properties also aid in preventing harmful bacterial overgrowth in the gut.
