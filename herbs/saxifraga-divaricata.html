Saxifraga divaricata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saxifraga divaricata has a basal rosette of leaves that are typically spoon-shaped or spatulate with slightly toothed margins and a fleshy texture. These leaves grow in a tight cluster near the ground to conserve moisture and stabilize the plant. From this central rosette, a slender, upright flowering stem emerges that can reach heights of several inches. The stem eventually produces small, five-petaled white flowers that are often arranged in loose clusters. These delicate blossoms feature distinctively divided or notched petals, which gives the species its specific epithet.
