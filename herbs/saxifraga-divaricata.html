What's the taxonomical classification of Saxifraga divaricata?

Saxifraga divaricata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Saxifragales and the family Saxifragaceae, eventually reaching the genus Saxifraga. Finally, the specific identity of this plant is established by its species designation, divaricata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saxifraga divaricata has a basal rosette of leaves that are typically spoon-shaped or spatulate with slightly toothed margins and a fleshy texture.

These leaves grow in a tight cluster near the ground to conserve moisture and stabilize the plant. From this central rosette, a slender, upright flowering stem emerges that can reach heights of several inches. The stem eventually produces small, five-petaled white flowers that are often arranged in loose clusters.

These delicate blossoms feature distinctively divided or notched petals, which gives the species its specific epithet.
