Panax valdiviensis

It has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, which include reducing inflammation, improving cognitive function, and enhancing immune system function.

The therapeutic actions of Panax valdiviensis are attributed to its adaptogenic and antioxidant properties, which may also help to reduce stress and anxiety.

The bioactive constituents of Panax valdiviensis include saponins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Medicinal Parts of Panax valdiviensis

Panax valdiviensis has many medicinal parts, such as the rhizome, roots, and leaves.

The leaves, on the other hand, are rich in flavonoids like isoflavonoids and phenolic acids, which may have anti-oxidative and anti-inflammatory effects.

Additionally, the plant's roots and rhizome also contain saponins, which can help to lower cholesterol levels and have anti-cancer properties.
