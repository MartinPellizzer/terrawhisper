Typhonium trilobatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Typhonium trilobatum?

Typhonium trilobatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and falls within the order Alismatales. This plant is a member of the family Araceae, positioned within the genus Typhonium, and is specifically identified by its species name, trilobatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Typhonium trilobatum has a single basal leaf characterized by three distinct lobes and a prominent sheath-like petiole.     What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

    This plant contains the medicinal compounds linoleic acid and palmitic acid within its lipid profile. Linoleic acid acts as an essential fatty acid that supports skin barrier function and reduces systemic inflammation. Palmitic acid serves as a primary saturated fatty acid that plays a critical role in cellular signaling and energy metabolism. Together, these compounds contribute to the overall therapeutic potential of the plant's chemical composition. These bioactive lipids are frequently studied for their impact on human physiological health.

    As discussed in a study, the methanolic extract of the Typhonium trilobatum tuber contains bioactive molecules identified as Linoleic acid and Palmitic acid. The IC50 value of Linoleic acid was found to be 6.09 ± 0.78 μg/ml after 24 h and 4.27 ± 0.63 μg/ml after 48 h. For Palmitic acid, the IC50 value was 12.35 ± 1.09 μg/ml after 24 h and 8.79 ± 0.94 μg/ml after 48 h. These values were compared to the standard drug Ivectin, which had an IC50 of 11.88 ± 1.07 μg/ml in 24 h and 2.74 ± 0.43 μg/ml in 48 h. Additionally, the compounds showed better results compared to DEC, which had IC50 values of 194.2 ± 2.28 μg/ml in 24 h and 101.8 ± 2.06 μg/ml in 48 h.
