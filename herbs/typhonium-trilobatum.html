Typhonium trilobatum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Typhonium trilobatum has a single basal leaf characterized by three distinct lobes and a prominent sheath-like petiole. The leaf surface often exhibits a slightly textured or rugose appearance with visible venation patterns. Its reproductive structure consists of a spadix enclosed within a specialized bract known as a spathe. The plant typically grows from a fleshy underground tuber that serves as a storage organ. These morphological features collectively distinguish it from other members of the Araceae family.

      What are the herbal preparations of Typhonium trilobatum?

      Typhonium trilobatum is prepared primarily as a decoction, a powder, or a topical paste using its dried rhizomes and leaves. The plant parts are often boiled in water to extract active compounds for internal consumption. Alternatively, the dried material can be ground into a fine powder and mixed with honey or warm water.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Typhonium trilobatum can cause severe irritation, burning, and swelling of the mouth, throat, and digestive tract due to the presence of calcium oxalate crystals. These needle-like structures penetrate soft tissues immediately upon contact with mucous membranes. Ingestion often leads to intense pain, difficulty swallowing, and localized edema. If the plant is handled without protection, it may also trigger skin irritation or dermatitis. In severe cases, the resulting swelling can potentially obstruct the airway.
