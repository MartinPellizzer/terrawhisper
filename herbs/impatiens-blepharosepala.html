Impatiens blepharosepala

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Impatiens blepharosepala has succulent, erect stems and lanceolate leaves with serrated margins that grow in an opposite arrangement. The plant is characterized by its unique floral structure, which features sepals with distinct, ciliate margins that give the species its name. Small, zygomorphic flowers emerge from the axils, displaying a delicate color palette often ranging from pale pink to white. The fruit is a typical explosive capsule that releases seeds upon maturity to facilitate dispersal. This morphology is adapted to the humid, shaded forest understories where the species is typically found.
