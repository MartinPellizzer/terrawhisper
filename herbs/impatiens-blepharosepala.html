What's the taxonomical classification of Impatiens blepharosepala?

Impatiens blepharosepala belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Ericales. This plant is a member of the family Balsaminaceae, which places it within the genus Impatiens, and it is specifically identified by its species name, blepharosepala.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Impatiens blepharosepala has succulent, erect stems and lanceolate leaves with serrated margins that grow in an opposite arrangement.

The plant is characterized by its unique floral structure, which features sepals with distinct, ciliate margins that give the species its name. Small, zygomorphic flowers emerge from the axils, displaying a delicate color palette often ranging from pale pink to white. The fruit is a typical explosive capsule that releases seeds upon maturity to facilitate dispersal.

This morphology is adapted to the humid, shaded forest understories where the species is typically found.

        What side-effects this plant can have?
