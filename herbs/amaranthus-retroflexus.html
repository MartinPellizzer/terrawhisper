Amaranthus retroflexus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Amaranthus retroflexus has erect, branching stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 150 centimeters and feature alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with entire margins. The leaf bases are often clasping or decurrent, running down the stem to form small wings. Its inflorescences consist of dense, terminal and axillary panicles of small, inconspicuous green flowers. These flowers lack petals and are characterized by prominent, spreading sepals that give the clusters a somewhat bristly appearance. The plant produces numerous tiny, smooth, blackish seeds that are distributed within the dried floral structures.

      What are the herbal preparations of Amaranthus retroflexus?

      Amaranthus retroflexus is prepared as an infusion, decoction, tincture, or topical poultice depending on the specific therapeutic application intended. The leaves and stems are commonly boiled in water to create a decoction used for internal cleansing or nutritional supplementation.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Amaranthus retroflexus can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and potential toxicity if ingested in large quantities due to the presence of oxalates. These oxalates are organic acids that can interfere with calcium absorption and contribute to the formation of kidney stones in humans. Contact with the plant's leaves or stems may result in dermatitis or itching for individuals with sensitive skin. While not considered highly lethal, consuming significant amounts of the plant can lead to gastrointestinal distress. Caution is advised when handling or consuming any part of this species to avoid these adverse reactions.
