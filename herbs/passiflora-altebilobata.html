Passiflora altebilobata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Passiflora altebilobata has a climbing habit characterized by long, slender stems that utilize specialized tendrils to ascend surrounding vegetation. The leaves are typically large and deeply lobed, often exhibiting three to five distinct segments with serrated margins. Its flowers are structurally complex, featuring prominent corona filaments and a central column consisting of the fused stamens and pistil. The fruit develops from the ovary into a fleshy berry that varies in color as it reaches maturity. These morphological features allow the plant to thrive in dense, tropical forest environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical regions of South America, specifically found in countries such as Brazil, Peru, and Colombia. It thrives within the moist, humid environments of lowland rainforests and subtropical forest edges. The species is primarily distributed across the Amazon basin where high precipitation levels support its climbing growth habit. Localized populations can also be found in specific mountainous foothills where the climate remains consistently warm. This concentrated range makes it a characteristic element of the Neotropical flora.

Passiflora altebilobata contains various bioactive compounds including alkaloids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids that provide its therapeutic properties. What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Passiflora altebilobatata targets gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA) receptors, specifically the GABA-A receptor subtype, to exert its primary sedative and anxiolytic effects. The bioactive flavonoids and alkaloids within the plant act as positive allosteric modulators that enhance the inhibitory neurotransmission mediated by these receptors. Additionally, certain compounds in the plant demonstrate the ability to interact with adenosine receptors, which contributes to its sleep-inducing properties. Research also suggests potential modulation of monoamine oxidase enzymes, which can influence the levels of neurotransmitters like serotonin and dopamine in the brain. These diverse protein interactions collectively facilitate the plant's medicinal role in treating insomnia and anxiety disorders.

What are the therapeutic actions of Passiflora altebilobata?

Passiflora altebilobatata has sedative, anxiolytic, hypnotic, nervine, and antispasmodic properties that primarily target the central nervous system to alleviate various forms of mental and physical distress. Its anxiolytic effects work to reduce feelings of anxiety and promote a sense of emotional calm. When used to address sleep disturbances, its hypnotic qualities facilitate easier transitions into restful slumber. Additionally, its antispasmodic action assists in relaxing smooth muscle tissue to mitigate physical tension.
