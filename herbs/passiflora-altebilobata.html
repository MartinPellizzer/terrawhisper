Passiflora altebilobata

What's the taxonomical classification of Passiflora altebilobata?

Passiflora altebilobata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It falls under the order Malpighiales and is a member of the family Passifloraceae. Within this family, the plant is placed in the genus Passiflora, and its specific identification is completed by its species name, altebilobata.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Passiflora altebilobatata contains medicinal compounds primarily located in its leaves, stems, and fruit. The leaves are most frequently harvested to create infusions or extracts used for their sedative properties. These vegetative parts contain flavonoids and alkaloids that help alleviate anxiety and promote sleep. Additionally, the fruit can be consumed for nutritional value and certain therapeutic benefits. Some traditional practices also utilize the whole plant to address various nervous system ailments.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse profile of medicinal compounds including alkaloids such as harmine, harmaline, and harmane, alongside flavonoids like vitexin and isovitexin, cyanogenic glycosides, phenolic acids, and terpenoids.

The presence of harmala alkaloids provides the species with significant psychoactive and enzyme-inhibiting properties. Flavonoids and phenolic acids contribute to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory capabilities. Additionally, the concentration of cyanogenic glycosides and various terpenoids plays a role in the plant's complex chemical defense and therapeutic potential.

Together, these bioactive constituents form the basis for its traditional and pharmacological applications.

  • Flavonoids (notably vitexin and isovitexin)
  • Cyanogenic glycosides
  • Phenolic acids
  • Terpenoids

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Passiflora altebilobatata targets gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA) receptors, specifically the GABA-A receptor subtype, to exert its primary sedative and anxiolytic effects. The bioactive flavonoids and alkaloids within the plant act as positive allosteric modulators that enhance the inhibitory neurotransmission mediated by these receptors. Additionally, certain compounds in the plant demonstrate the ability to interact with adenosine receptors, which contributes to its sleep-inducing properties. Research also suggests potential modulation of monoamine oxidase enzymes, which can influence the levels of neurotransmitters like serotonin and dopamine in the brain. These diverse protein interactions collectively facilitate the plant's medicinal role in treating insomnia and anxiety disorders.

What are the therapeutic actions of Passiflora altebilobata?

Passiflora altebilobata has sedative, anxiolytic, and anticonvulsant properties that primarily target the central nervous system to reduce anxiety and promote sleep. The plant contains bioactive compounds like flavonoids and alkaloids that interact with GABA receptors in the brain to inhibit overactive nerve signaling. By modulating these neurochemical pathways, it helps to calm emotional distress and lower physiological tension. These therapeutic actions make it a useful natural remedy for treating insomnia and various forms of mild nervous agitation. Regular use can assist in stabilizing mood and improving the overall quality of rest for individuals experiencing stress.

What common ailments are treated using this plant?

This plant is used to treat insomnia, anxiety, nervousness, and irritability through its natural sedative properties.

Many people rely on its extracts to calm a restless mind and promote a sense of relaxation. By acting on the central nervous system, it helps reduce the physiological symptoms associated with daily stress. Regular use can assist individuals in falling asleep more easily and staying asleep throughout the night.

These therapeutic effects make it a popular herbal remedy for managing various emotional imbalances.

The main common ailment treated using this plant are shown in the list below.

  • Insomnia

  • Irritability

What are the herbal preparations of Passiflora altebilobata?

Passiflora altebilobatata is prepared as dried herb infusions, decoctions, tinctures, or standardized extracts for therapeutic use. The dried aerial parts are commonly steeped in hot water to create a calming tea that targets insomnia and anxiety. Tinctures prepared with ethanol allow for a more concentrated dose and a longer shelf life for consistent administration. Some medicinal preparations utilize concentrated extracts to ensure a specific level of passionflower alkaloids. These various forms allow patients to tailor the dosage and delivery method to their specific neurological needs.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Passiflora altebilobata can cause drowsiness, dizziness, and gastrointestinal disturbances such as nausea or stomach upset in some individuals. These sedative effects occur because the plant contains alkaloids and flavonoids that interact with the central nervous system. Because of this potential for sedation, users should avoid operating heavy machinery or driving after consumption. There is also a risk of interactions with prescription medications, particularly those used for anxiety or sleep disorders. Pregnant or breastfeeding women should exercise extra caution as the long-term safety profile for these groups is not well established.
