What's the taxonomical classification of Picris japonica?

Picris japonica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Asterales. This plant is a representative of the family Asteraceae, a large group of flowering plants, and is specifically categorized under the genus Picris. Its complete scientific binomial designation is Picris japonica, identifying it as a distinct species within its genus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Picris japonica has an erect, herbaceous habit characterized by a stem that typically reaches heights of 30 to 100 centimeters and features basal leaves that are oblong to lanceolate with dentate margins. The upper leaves are smaller and more deeply lobed or pinnatifid as they ascend the stem. It produces terminal clusters of small, yellow, ligulate flower heads that lack true disc florets. Each flower head is surrounded by multiple series of involucral bracts that are often graduated in length. The resulting seeds are small achenes equipped with a pappus of fine, white bristles to facilitate wind dispersal.
