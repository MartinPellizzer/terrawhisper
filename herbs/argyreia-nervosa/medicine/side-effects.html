10 Possible Side Effects Of Argyreia Nervosa

Argyreia nervosa has some side effects when used improperly, such as hallucinations, anxiety, and increased heart rate.

These side effects can occur due to the high content of alkaloids, specifically argyretone. Prolonged use or excessive consumption can exacerbate these symptoms, leading to difficulties in daily functioning and strained relationships.

In severe cases, it can even lead to dependency, worsening a person's overall quality of life.

1. Causes anxiety attacks

Argyreia nervosa causes anxiety attacks when used.

This is due to the presence of alkaloids and glycosides in its leaves, which can stimulate the nervous system and disrupt normal brain activity. As a result, individuals may experience increased heart rate, sweating, and feelings of panic or fear.

The effects are believed to be caused by the interaction of these compounds with neurotransmitters in the brain, leading to an exaggerated stress response.

2. Triggers palpitations rapidly

Argyreia nervosa triggers palpitations rapidly due to its ability to increase heart rate and blood pressure.

The stimulating effects of the herb's alkaloids, particularly harmine, can cause a surge in adrenaline levels, leading to rapid heartbeat and palpitations.

This increased cardiac activity is believed to be a result of Argyreia nervosa's affinity for binding to serotonin receptors in the brain, which can amplify its stimulatory effects on the cardiovascular system.

3. Induces insomnia heavily

Argyreia nervosa induces insomnia heavily due to its high content of alkaloids, particularly argyrophylline and lignans.

These compounds can cause an increase in brain activity, leading to difficulty falling asleep and staying asleep. Additionally, the stimulant properties of Argyreia nervosa can disrupt the body's natural sleep-wake cycle, making it challenging for individuals to get a restful night's sleep.

As a result, users may experience insomnia or other sleep-related issues.

4. Increases blood pressure sharply

Argyreia nervosa increases blood pressure sharply due to its high concentration of bioactive compounds such as reserpine and ajmaline.

These alkaloids have been shown to stimulate the sympathetic nervous system, leading to increased heart rate and blood pressure. Additionally, the herb's vasopressor properties cause blood vessels to constrict, further exacerbating hypertension.

This sudden and significant increase in blood pressure can be a concerning side effect of using Argyreia nervosa, particularly for individuals with pre-existing hypertension or cardiovascular conditions.

5. Disrupts digestive rhythms

Argyreia nervosa disrupts digestive rhythms.

This may occur due to its ability to slow down gastric emptying and alter gut motility, leading to changes in the natural rhythm of digestion and bowel movements. Additionally, Argyreia nervosa's interaction with the serotonin system, a key player in regulating gut function, can also contribute to disruptions in digestive patterns.

As a result, users may experience irregular bowel habits, bloating, or discomfort after consuming the plant.

6. Produces allergic reactions

Argyreia nervosa produces allergic reactions due to its high concentration of latex and alkaloids.

These allergenic compounds can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, redness, and swelling.

Additionally, the vine's sap may cause contact dermatitis in some individuals, further contributing to the risk of allergic reactions when handling or ingesting Argyreia nervosa.

7. Increases heart rate excessively

Argyreia nervosa increases heart rate excessively due to its effects on the nervous system.

The plant's compounds, including aristolochic acid and alkaloids, can stimulate the sympathetic nervous system, leading to an increase in heart rate and cardiac output. This is because they mimic the actions of epinephrine and norepinephrine, neurotransmitters that play a key role in regulating heart rate and blood pressure.

As a result, individuals using Argyreia nervosa may experience a rapid or irregular heartbeat, which can be a sign of its potent physiological effects.

8. Affects cognitive functions

Argyreia nervosa affects cognitive functions by altering brain chemistry and neurotransmitter regulation.

The active compounds in Argyreia nervosa, including alkaloids and glycosides, can bind to serotonin receptors, disrupting the normal flow of neural signals and affecting attention, memory, and decision-making abilities.

This alteration can lead to impaired concentration, difficulty processing information, and changes in mood, potentially impacting daily life and performance.

9. Elevates body temperature

Argyreia nervosa elevates body temperature due to its ability to stimulate the nervous system and increase sympathetic activity.

This can lead to an increased heart rate and blood flow, causing a rise in body temperature.

Additionally, Argyreia nervosa contains compounds that may interact with the body's natural thermoregulation mechanisms, further contributing to the elevation of body temperature.

10. Weakens immune response

Argyreia nervosa weakens immune response by suppressing the activity of certain immune cells, such as macrophages and T-lymphocytes.

This is thought to be due to its alkaloid compounds, which can bind to specific receptors on immune cells, altering their function and leading to a decrease in immune response.

As a result, individuals using Argyreia nervosa may experience increased susceptibility to infections and diseases.
