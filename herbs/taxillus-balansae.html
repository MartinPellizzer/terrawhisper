The reproductive structures eventually produce small fruits that contain seeds essential for the plant's dispersal. These bioactive compounds primarily act by inhibiting the activation of nuclear factor-kappa B, which is a central regulator of the immune response. By suppressing this signaling pathway, the plant effectively reduces the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines and prostaglandins. Additionally, its components interact with nitric oxide synthase to modulate vascular tone and systemic inflammation. These multi-target interactions contribute to the plant's documented analgesic and anti-inflammatory therapeutic properties.
