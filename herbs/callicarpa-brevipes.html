Callicarpa brevipes

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Callicarpa brevipes has opposite, simple leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape with serrated margins and a glabrous surface. The plant exhibits a woody structure characterized by slender stems that support its spreading growth habit. Small, axillary clusters of flowers emerge from the leaf axils, eventually developing into distinctive, globose drupes. These berries are typically bright purple or violet and hang in dense, compact bunches. The overall morphology is defined by these small, clustered fruits and the fine texture of its foliage.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Callicarpa brevipes can cause localized skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Individuals with sensitive skin may experience redness, itching, or swelling upon contact with the plant's sap or foliage. If swallowed, the plant may lead to nausea, stomach cramps, or vomiting due to its chemical composition. There is also a risk of hypersensitivity reactions in people who have pre-existing allergies to members of the Verbenaceae family. Careful handling is advised to prevent accidental exposure to the skin or mucous membranes.
