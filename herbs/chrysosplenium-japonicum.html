Chrysosplenium japonicum

What's the taxonomical classification of Chrysosplenium japonicum?

Chrysospeltiun japonicum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Saxifragales. It is further categorized into the family Saxifragaceae, which contains its specific genus, Chrysospeltiun, and its unique species designation, japonicum.

    This plant is used to treat gastrointestinal issues, including stomach pain, indigestion, and diarrhea. Practitioners often prepare decoctions from its components to address these specific internal ailments. Its application is primarily focused on restoring balance to the digestive system.

    Chrysospelnium japonicum is prepared primarily as a decoction made by boiling the dried aerial parts of the plant in water to extract its medicinal compounds. In some regional practices, the plant material is also processed into a tincture using alcohol as a solvent for better preservation and potency. Additionally, topical applications involving poultices or infused oils may be used to address external skin conditions. These various methods allow for the flexible administration of the plant's active constituents depending on the specific ailment being treated.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Chrysosplenium japonicum can cause localized skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Contact with the plant's sap or leaves may trigger redness, itching, or swelling in sensitive individuals. If consumed accidentally, people might experience nausea, abdominal cramping, or vomiting. These reactions vary depending on the individual's level of sensitivity and the amount of exposure. It is advisable to use protective gloves when handling the plant to minimize these risks.
