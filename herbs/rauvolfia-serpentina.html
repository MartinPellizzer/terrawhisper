Rauvolfia serpentina reserpine is a potent alkaloid known for its significant hypotensive effects, primarily due to its ability to inhibit the reuptake of neurotransmitters such as norepinephrine and serotonin, thereby reducing sympathetic nervous system activity and lowering blood pressure.

Rauvolfia serpentina tetracyclic alkaloids, which include reserpine and its derivatives, are characterized by their complex four-ring structure and are responsible for the plant's traditional use in treating hypertension and psychiatric disorders.

Rauvolfia serpentina yohimbine is a selective alpha-2 adrenergic receptor antagonist that has been historically used for its ability to enhance libido and as a vasodilator, though its clinical use has been limited due to side effects.

Rauvolfia serpentina rescinnamine is a minor alkaloid with structural similarities to reserpine, and while its pharmacological effects are less well-studied, it is believed to contribute to the overall hypotensive and sedative properties of the plant.
