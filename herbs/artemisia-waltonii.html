Artemisia waltonii

What's the taxonomical classification of Artemisia waltonii?

Artemisia waltonii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. The plant is a representative of the family Asteraceae, a diverse group of flowering plants, and is specifically categorized under the genus Artemisia. Its unique botanical identity is finalized by its specific designation as the species waltonii.

These interactions occur at the molecular level to disrupt cellular homeostasis within pathogens and modulate immune responses in host tissues.       What are the herbal preparations of Artemisia waltonii?

      Artemisia waltonii is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical tincture to utilize its bioactive compounds. The leaves and stems are often dried and steeped in hot water to create a bitter medicinal tea. When a more concentrated dose is required, the plant material is soaked in high-proof alcohol to produce a tincture. This liquid extract can be administered orally or diluted for external applications on the skin. These diverse preparation methods allow for the extraction of specific volatile oils and tannins present in the herb.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Artemisia waltonii can cause allergic skin reactions such as contact dermatitis, redness, and itching when the plant comes into direct contact with sensitive skin. Some individuals may experience respiratory irritation or sneezing if they inhale fine particles or pollen from the plant. Ingestion of the plant is generally discouraged as it may lead to gastrointestinal distress or nausea. Because specific toxicological studies on this exact species are limited, potential systemic reactions remain a concern for those with known sensitivities to the Asteraceae family. Always consult a medical professional if you suspect an adverse reaction has occurred.
