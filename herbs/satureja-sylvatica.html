Satureja sylvatica

Satureja sylvatica, commonly known as the wood savory, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe, particularly in the Mediterranean region.

It is valued for its health benefits, which include its antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, and is often used to support digestive health and alleviate respiratory issues.

The therapeutic actions of wood savory are attributed to its bioactive constituents, including carvacrol, thymol, and borneol, which have been shown to exhibit antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties.

These bioactive compounds are thought to contribute to the plant's potential therapeutic applications in wound healing, pain relief, and the management of various infections.
