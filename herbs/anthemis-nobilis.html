Pyrethrum

Anthemis nobilis

Pyrethrum (Anthemis nobilis) is a member of the Compositae family, traditionally used for various purposes. The plant's flowers have been utilized in traditional medicine for treatment of digestive issues, relief of anxiety and insomnia, and topical application for skin conditions. The dried flowers are the primary part used in herbal preparations.

Botanical Identification

Pyrethrum, scientifically known as Anthemis nobilis, belongs to the Asteraceae family. It is also called tansy or golden buttons in various regions. This perennial plant is native to southern Europe and western Asia. It has yellow flowers, aromatic leaves, and a taproot. It is sometimes referred to as "Pyrethrum" in herbal texts, though this name is also used for other species.

Pyrethrum Identification.

Active Compounds

Pyrethrum contains essential oils, flavonoids, and terpenoids as its primary active compounds. These components work together to disrupt nerve function in insects, making the herb effective in pest control. Flavonoids and terpenoids may enhance the potency of the essential oils through synergistic interactions.

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for digestive issues and as a calming drink.

Decoction: Used for respiratory conditions and to relieve coughs.

Essential oil: Used for skin conditions and to relieve stress and anxiety.

Safety Profile

Pyrethrum, derived from Anthemis nobilis, is generally safe when used as directed. However, it may cause skin irritation or allergic reactions in some individuals. It should be avoided during pregnancy and by those with known allergies to related plants.
