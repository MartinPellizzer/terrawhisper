What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Anchusa ovata has erect, hairy stems that reach up to 50 centimeters in height and bear lanceolate, sessile leaves with a rough, bristly texture. Its inflorescence consists of dense, coiled cymes that produce small, funnel-shaped flowers ranging in color from pale blue to deep violet. The calyx is characterized by prominent, stiffly hairy lobes that protect the developing floral structures.
