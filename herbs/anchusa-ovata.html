Anchusa ovata

What's the taxonomical classification of Anchusa ovata?

Anchusa ovata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic classification continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Boraginales, it is placed in the family Boraginaceae, which contains the genus Anchusa. Finally, the specific identity of this plant is defined by its species name, ovata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Anchusa ovata has erect, hairy stems that reach up to 50 centimeters in height and bear lanceolate, sessile leaves with a rough, bristly texture. The foliage is primarily basal, forming a rosette of leaves that gradually transition into smaller, more spaced-out leaves along the upper stem. Its inflorescence consists of dense, coiled cymes that produce small, funnel-shaped flowers ranging in color from pale blue to deep violet. The calyx is characterized by prominent, stiffly hairy lobes that protect the developing floral structures. Additionally, the plant features a woody taproot that provides stability and nutrient storage within its native rocky habitats.
