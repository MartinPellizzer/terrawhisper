Erysimum flavum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Erysimum flavum has a perennial growth habit characterized by basal rosettes of oblong to lanceolate leaves that transition into smaller, alternate leaves along its upright, branching stems. The foliage typically presents a hairy or pubescent texture, which helps the plant retain moisture. Its most striking feature is the clusters of bright yellow, four-petaled flowers that emerge from the terminal ends of the stems. These blossoms are arranged in racemes and are supported by slender pedicels. Once pollination occurs, the plant produces elongated, narrow seed pods that stand upright or slightly spreading.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe, specifically spanning from the Alps through the Balkans and into parts of the Mediterranean. It typically thrives in rocky limestone habitats and alpine meadows found at varying elevations. Within these territories, populations are often scattered across rugged terrain where soil is well-drained. The species is frequently documented in countries such as Austria, Italy, Slovenia, and Greece. Its distribution is closely linked to specific climatic conditions and geological formations characteristic of these mountain ranges.

      Erysimum flavum is prepared primarily as an infusion, decoction, or tincture to extract its bioactive compounds for medicinal use.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Erysimum flavum can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or digestive upset if ingested or touched directly. There is limited clinical research available to fully document the specific toxicity levels in humans. Some individuals may experience contact dermatitis characterized by redness or itching upon contact with the plant material. If consumed accidentally, it might lead to gastrointestinal discomfort such as nausea or stomach aches. It is generally recommended to avoid contact with the plant to prevent any unpredictable sensitivities.
