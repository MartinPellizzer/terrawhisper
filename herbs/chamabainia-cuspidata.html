Chamabainia cuspidata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Chamabainia cuspidata has a holoparasitic morphology characterized by a lack of chlorophyll, scale-like leaves, and a specialized root system known as a haustorium. This plant does not possess functional leaves for photosynthesis, instead appearing as small, brownish, scale-like structures along its stem. The stem is relatively slender and grows by attaching itself to the roots of host plants to extract water and nutrients. Its flowers are small and typically emerge from the leaf axils in a somewhat crowded arrangement. The overall structure is highly reduced due to its reliance on a host for survival.
