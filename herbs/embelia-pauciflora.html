Embelia pauciflora

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Embelia pauciflora has small, opposite, elliptical leaves with entire margins and a glabrous surface, paired with inconspicuous, sparsely distributed, axillary flowers. The plant typically exhibits a woody or semi-woody habit with slender, branching stems. Its inflorescences are characterized by a low number of flowers, which is the defining feature reflected in its specific epithet. The flowers are often small and pale in color, appearing tucked within the leaf axils. These morphological traits distinguish it from other members of the Primulaceae family.
