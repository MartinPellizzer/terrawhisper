Passiflora quadrangularis

What's the taxonomical classification of Passiflora quadrangularis?

Passiflora quadrangularis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further falls into the order Malpighiales and is a member of the family Passifloraceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Passiflora and is specifically designated as the species quadrangularis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Passiflora quadrangularis has woody, climbing vines characterized by thick, four-angled stems and large, deeply three-lobed leaves. The foliage consists of bright green, ovate leaves that can reach significant sizes to support the plant's vigorous growth habit. Its most striking morphological feature is the large, complex flower, which displays white petals and a prominent, dark corona consisting of many fine filaments. Following successful pollination, the plant produces large, oblong, green fruits that eventually turn yellow and contain numerous small seeds. These structures allow the vine to climb aggressively through surrounding vegetation using specialized tendrils.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant Passiflora quadrangularis thrives in tropical and subtropical environments with well-draining soil, consistent moisture, and full sunlight exposure.

To ensure vigorous growth, it requires a sturdy trellis or support structure because it is a heavy climbing vine. Frequent watering is necessary to prevent the soil from drying out completely, though the roots must not sit in stagnant water. Providing organic mulch helps maintain the humidity and soil temperature that this large-flowered species prefers.

What are the pharmacological activities of Passiflora quadrangularis?

This plant has potent anxiolytic and sedative pharmacological activities that make it useful for managing various neurological conditions. The bioactive compounds within the plant work to modulate the central nervous system to reduce feelings of anxiety and promote relaxation. These effects are often attributed to the interaction of specific flavonoids with neurotransmitter receptors in the brain. Clinical applications of its extracts frequently focus on improving sleep quality and lowering overall stress levels. Such properties position the species as a significant natural resource in the development of herbal tranquilizers.

According to a study published by "Central nervous system agents in medicinal chemistry", Passiflora quadrangularis was one of five investigated Passiflora species native to Brazil. This species induced a decrease in the locomotor activity of mice during behavioral testing. The results indicated that Passiflora quadrangularis reduced the general activity of mice and conferred a calmative/sedative potential. Oral administration of the extracts evoked no severe signs of toxicity, even at higher doses. None of the species tested, including Passiflora quadrangularis, interfered with motor coordination.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause sedation, nausea, dizziness, confusion, and hypotension in individuals who consume it. These adverse reactions often stem from the plant's potent sedative properties which can impact the central nervous system. Users might experience a significant drop in blood pressure, leading to the symptom of hypotension. Furthermore, the physiological effects may result in mental disorientation or feelings of confusion. Physical discomfort such as nausea and lightheadedness from dizziness are also frequently reported side effects.
