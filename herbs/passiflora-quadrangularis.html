Passiflora quadrangularis

What's the taxonomical classification of Passiflora quadrangularis?

Passiflora quadrangularis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further falls into the order Malpighiales and is a member of the family Passifloraceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Passiflora and is specifically designated as the species quadrangularis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Passiflora quadrangularis has woody, climbing vines characterized by thick, four-angled stems and large, deeply three-lobed leaves.

The foliage consists of bright green, ovate leaves that can reach significant sizes to support the plant's vigorous growth habit. Its most striking morphological feature is the large, complex flower, which displays white petals and a prominent, dark corona consisting of many fine filaments. Following successful pollination, the plant produces large, oblong, green fruits that eventually turn yellow and contain numerous small seeds.

These structures allow the vine to climb aggressively through surrounding vegetation using specialized tendrils.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Passiflora quadrangularis contains medicinal compounds primarily located in its leaves, flowers, and fruit pulp. The leaves are frequently harvested for their sedative properties and are used to treat anxiety or insomnia. The fruit provides a fleshy pulp that is often consumed for its nutritional value and potential digestive benefits. Additionally, the flowers may be utilized in traditional remedies to help calm the nervous system. Various parts of the plant are processed into extracts or teas to harness these therapeutic effects.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse array of bioactive constituents including alkaloids, flavonoids, cyanogenic glycosides, terpenoids, and phenolic compounds.

The presence of specific alkaloids often contributes to the plant's sedative and neuroprotective properties. Flavonoids and phenolic compounds work together to provide significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities within biological systems. Additionally, the terpenoids found in the tissues may offer antimicrobial benefits.

Researchers also monitor the cyanogenic glycosides, which play a role in the plant's chemical defense mechanisms.

  • Cyanogenic glycosides
  • Terpenoids

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Passiflora quadrangularis targets GABA-A receptors, various inflammatory cytokines, and specific enzymes involved in oxidative stress pathways to produce its medicinal effects. The plant's alkaloids and flavonoids act as modulators of the gamma-aminobutyric acid system, which facilitates its sedative and anxiolytic properties. Additionally, bioactive compounds interact with cyclooxygenase enzymes to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory mediators. These interactions also extend to the modulation of antioxidant enzymes like superoxide dismutase to combat cellular damage. By influencing these diverse protein pathways, the plant exerts significant neuroprotective and anti-inflammatory actions.

What are the therapeutic actions of Passiflora quadrangularis?

Passiflora quadrangularis has sedative, anxiolytic, hypnotic, nervine, and carminative therapeutic actions used to treat various neurological and digestive conditions.

As a potent nervine, it helps to soothe and support a strained nervous system. Its anxiolytic properties allow it to effectively reduce feelings of anxiety and restlessness. The plant also functions as a hypnotic to promote deeper and more restful sleep patterns.

Additionally, its carminative effects assist in relieving intestinal gas and digestive discomfort.

What are the herbal preparations of Passiflora quadrangularis?

Passiflora quadrangularis is prepared primarily as a dried powder, an aqueous infusion, or a concentrated decoction derived from its leaves and stems. These botanical materials are often steeped in hot water to create soothing teas used for relaxation. Some practitioners also process the plant into tinctures using alcohol to ensure a more stable and potent extract. Additionally, the dried plant matter can be encapsulated for standardized dosage in dietary supplements. These various forms allow for different absorption rates and ease of consumption depending on the intended use.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Passiflora quadrangularis can cause gastrointestinal distress such as nausea, abdominal cramping, diarrhea, and dizziness when consumed. These side effects often arise because the plant contains various alkaloids and glycosides that may irritate the digestive lining. Some individuals might also experience drowsiness or a reduction in alertness due to its sedative properties. It is important to note that excessive consumption could potentially lead to more significant electrolyte imbalances. Due to these risks, caution should be exercised when using this plant for medicinal purposes.
