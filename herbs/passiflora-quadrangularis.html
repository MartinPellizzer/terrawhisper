Passiflora quadrangularis

What's the taxonomical classification of Passiflora quadrangularis?

Passiflora quadrangularis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further falls into the order Malpighiales and is a member of the family Passifloraceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Passiflora and is specifically designated as the species quadrangularis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Passiflora quadrangularis contains alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, and tannins that provide its primary therapeutic properties. The presence of specific alkaloids like harman and harmine contributes to its known sedative and anxiolytic effects. Flavonoids within the plant act as powerful antioxidants that help reduce oxidative stress in the body. Additionally, the saponin content may support immune function and exhibit anti-inflammatory activities. These diverse secondary metabolites work together to justify its traditional use in treating various ailments.

What are the therapeutic actions of Passiflora quadrangularis?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat insomnia, anxiety, restlessness, and various forms of nervous tension. Its sedative properties help calm the central nervous system to promote better sleep cycles. People also utilize it as a remedy for digestive issues and mild spasms. The active compounds in the plant act as natural tranquilizers to reduce emotional stress. These therapeutic applications make it a valuable component in herbal medicine for managing mental and physical agitation.
