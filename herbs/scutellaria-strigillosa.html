Scutellaria strigillosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Scutellaria strigillosa?

Scutelariia strigillosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Lamiales. Its taxonomic progression continues through the family Lamiaceae, leading to its placement in the genus Scutellaria, and finally to its specific designation as the species strigillosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Scutelariia strigillosa has an erect, herbaceous habit characterized by slender, quadrangular stems and oppositely arranged, ovate to lanceolate leaves with distinctively striate or wrinkled surfaces. The foliage is typically green and displays prominent venation that contributes to its textured appearance. Its inflorescence consists of small, tubular flowers that often emerge from the leaf axils. These blossoms are typically blue or violet in hue and possess a two-lipped structure characteristic of the Lamiaceae family. According to a study published by "Frontiers in plant science", the chloroplast genome of Scutelariia strigillosa was assembled and measured 152,533 bp.
