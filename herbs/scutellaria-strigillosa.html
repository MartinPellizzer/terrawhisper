Scutellaria strigillosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Scutelariia strigillosa has an erect, herbaceous habit characterized by slender, quadrangular stems and oppositely arranged, ovate to lanceolate leaves with distinctively striate or wrinkled surfaces. The foliage is typically green and displays prominent venation that contributes to its textured appearance. Its inflorescence consists of small, tubular flowers that often emerge from the leaf axils. These blossoms are typically blue or violet in hue and possess a two-lipped structure characteristic of the Lamiaceae family. The plant's overall structure is delicate yet organized, reflecting its specialized adaptation to specific ecological niches.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China, specifically within the provinces of Yunnan, Sichuan, and Guizhou. It typically inhabits high-altitude environments such as forest margins, rocky slopes, and alpine meadows. The distribution is largely concentrated in the Hengduan Mountains where specific climatic conditions support its growth. Within these rugged landscapes, it thrives in well-drained soils often found at elevations ranging from moderate to high levels. This localized range makes it a characteristic component of the subtropical highland flora in this specific part of Asia.
