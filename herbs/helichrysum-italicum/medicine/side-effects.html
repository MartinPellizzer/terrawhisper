10 Possible Side Effects Of Helichrysum Italicum (Curry Plant)

Helichrysum italicum has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, skin irritation, and allergic reactions.

These side effects can be caused by the essential oils, saponins, and flavonoids present in the plant.

If not used correctly, these side effects can worsen, leading to nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, skin rashes, and even anaphylaxis, which can be life-threatening.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Helichrysum italicum if used imporperly.

Helichrysum italicum causes allergic reactions in some individuals due to its high concentration of volatile compounds such as sesquiterpenes and monoterpene aldehydes.

These bioactive molecules can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms like skin rashes, hives, itching, and even respiratory issues.

The allergic reaction is thought to be caused by the plant's ability to stimulate the release of histamine from mast cells, a common mechanism behind many allergic responses.
