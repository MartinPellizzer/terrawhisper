Ceriops tagal

What's the taxonomical classification of Ceriops tagal?

Ceriops tagal belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic progression continues through the order Malpighiales and the family Rhizophoraceae, ultimately placing the plant within the genus Ceriops. At the most specific level of classification, it is identified as the species tagal.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Ceriops tagal contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including tannins, flavonoids, saponins, and alkaloids that provide its medicinal properties. These chemical constituents contribute to the plant's documented antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory activities. Researchers often focus on the high concentration of tannins within the bark for their astringent qualities. Some traditional practices utilize these compounds to treat skin irritations and promote wound healing. The presence of these specific phytochemicals supports the plant's role in ethnobotanical medicine.

What are the therapeutic actions of Ceriops tagal?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, stomachaches, and various digestive ailments. The sap or extracts from the plant are frequently applied topically to promote healing in skin lesions and minor cuts. When taken internally, it serves as a traditional remedy for addressing gastrointestinal discomfort and abdominal pain. Local medicinal practices often rely on its bioactive properties to manage symptoms of internal inflammation. These applications demonstrate its significant role in traditional herbal medicine for both external and internal ailments.
