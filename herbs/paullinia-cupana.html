Guaraná

Paullinia cupana

Guaraná (Paullinia cupana) is a climbing plant native to the Amazon basin, known for its stimulating properties due to its high concentration of caffeine and other alkaloids. It has been traditionally used to enhance energy, improve mental focus, and support physical performance.

Botanical Identification

Guaraná, scientifically known as Paullinia cupana, belongs to the Sapindaceae family. It is also called "Brazil nut tree" in some regions and is a perennial shrub native to the Amazon basin. The plant features bright red berries with yellow seeds, and its leaves are elliptical with a smooth margin. Synonyms include "Brazilian pepper" and "cupuaçu" in herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Eudicots
  • Order: Sapindales
  • Family: Sapindaceae
  • Genus: Paullinia
  • Species: Paullinia cupana

Active Compounds

Guaraná contains key bioactive compounds such as alkaloids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids. These compounds work synergistically to enhance cognitive function and provide antioxidant benefits. The alkaloid caffeine, in particular, contributes to its stimulating effects.

Safety Profile

Guaraná contains caffeine and should be used with caution. It may interact with stimulants and affect heart rate. Consult a healthcare professional before use, especially for individuals with cardiovascular issues or those taking medications.
