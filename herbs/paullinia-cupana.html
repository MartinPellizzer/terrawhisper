Medicinal Parts

Paullinia cupana seed is the primary medicinal part of the plant, commonly known as the Brazilian guarana.

The seeds contain high concentrations of caffeine, theobromine, and theophylline, which are alkaloids known for their stimulant properties. These compounds are responsible for enhancing alertness, improving physical performance, and increasing metabolic rate. In traditional medicine, the seeds are often used to treat fatigue, depression, and respiratory issues.

However, due to the high caffeine content, excessive consumption can lead to side effects such as insomnia, anxiety, and gastrointestinal discomfort. The seeds are typically processed into powders, capsules, or extracts for use in dietary supplements and energy drinks. While the fruit and leaves of the plant also contain some beneficial compounds, the seeds remain the most studied and utilized part for their medicinal properties.

The use of Paullinia cupana seed continues to be a subject of scientific research, particularly in the fields of pharmacology and neuroscience.
