Guaraná (Paullinia cupana)

Guaraná (Paullinia cupana) is a member of the Sapindaceae family, native to Brazil, Amazon Basin, and South America. Traditionally, its fruits, seeds, and leaves have been used for infusions, decoctions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its stimulant, tonic, and anti-inflammatory actions, and has a long history of use in native american herbal medicine, traditional chinese medicine, and ayurvedic medicine.
