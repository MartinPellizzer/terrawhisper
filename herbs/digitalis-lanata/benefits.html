1. Slow heart rate

Bradycardia, or an abnormally slow heart rate, is a condition where the heart beats fewer than 60 times per minute.

This can result from various factors, including natural aging, certain medications, or underlying health issues like hypothyroidism.

It is often experienced by individuals with heart disease, athletes, or those with neurological disorders.

Digitalis lanata, also known as foxglove, has been traditionally used to support heart function and may help regulate heart rate in certain cases.

However, its use should be carefully managed under medical supervision due to its potent effects on the cardiovascular system.

How this herb helps with slow heart rate?

Digitalis lanata slow heart rate because it contains bioactive constituents such as cardiac glycosides, including digoxin and digitoxin.

These compounds work by inhibiting the sodium-potassium ATPase pump in cardiac muscle cells. This inhibition leads to an increase in intracellular sodium, which in turn affects calcium levels and enhances myocardial contractility. As a result, the heart beats more forcefully and at a slower rate.

These effects make Digitalis lanata useful in treating certain heart conditions, such as atrial fibrillation.
