10 Health Benefits Of Digitalis Lanata (Foxglove)

Digitalis lanata, also known as foxglove, has health benefits such as lowering heart rate and blood pressure.

Its medicinal properties include digoxin and digitoxin, cardiac glycosides that enhance the heart's pumping ability and reduce fluid accumulation. This can alleviate symptoms of heart failure, improving patients' quality of life by increasing energy levels and reducing shortness of breath.

For example, a study showed that treatment with digitalis reduced hospitalizations in heart failure patients by 30%.

This article explains in details the 10 best health benefits of Digitalis lanata.

1. Slows heart rate

Digitalis lanata slows heart rate because it contains a complex of cardiac glycosides that bind to sodium-potassium ATPase pumps in the heart muscle.

This binding action increases the amount of calcium ions entering the heart cells, causing an increase in the force of contraction and a decrease in the heart rate.

As a result, the heart beats more slowly and efficiently, increasing blood flow to the body's tissues.

2. Treats heart failure

Digitalis lanata treats heart failure because of its unique ability to increase the force of contractions in the heart.

The plant contains a group of compounds called glycosides, particularly digoxin and digitoxin, which are used to treat atrial fibrillation and chronic heart failure.

These glycosides help regulate the heartbeat by increasing the contraction strength of the ventricles, allowing more blood to be pumped out with each beat, ultimately improving cardiac output and reducing symptoms of heart failure.

3. Increases cardiac output

Digitalis lanata increases cardiac output because it enhances the contractility of the heart muscle by increasing the intracellular concentration of calcium ions.

This occurs through the inhibition of the sodium-potassium pump and the stimulation of the sarcoplasmic reticulum, resulting in a prolonged action potential duration.

As a result, the heart pumps more blood with each beat, leading to an increase in cardiac output and a decrease in peripheral resistance.

4. Relaxes coronary blood vessels

Digitalis lanata relaxes coronary blood vessels because of its active constituent digoxin.

Digoxin binds to cardiac sodium-potassium ATPase pump receptors in the coronary smooth muscle cells, leading to a decrease in calcium ion influx and subsequent relaxation of the vascular smooth muscle.

This results in dilation of the coronary arteries, increasing blood flow to the heart muscle and reducing oxygen demand.

5. Enlarges heart chambers

Digitalis lanata enlarges heart chambers because of its active compound digoxin.

Digoxin slows down the heart rate by inhibiting the Na+/K+-ATPase pump, leading to increased calcium levels in the heart cells. This increases the contractility and force of heart muscle contraction, resulting in an increase in cardiac output.

As a compensatory mechanism, the heart chambers dilate to accommodate the increased workload, allowing for more blood to be pumped into circulation.

6. Improves heart's pumping efficiency

Digitalis lanata improves heart's pumping efficiency because it contains the cardiac glycoside digoxin.

Digoxin increases the contraction force of the heart muscle, allowing for more effective blood ejection and improved circulation. It achieves this by inhibiting the action of an enzyme that breaks down sodium-potassium pumps, resulting in increased calcium levels within the heart cells.

This increase in calcium ions enhances contractility, leading to enhanced cardiac pumping efficiency and reduced symptoms of heart failure.

7. Reduces heart's workload

Digitalis lanata reduces heart's workload because its active compound digoxin slows down the heart rate by inhibiting the electrical conduction system.

This decreases the force and frequency of contractions, resulting in a reduction of blood pressure and cardiac output.

By doing so, the heart does not have to work as hard, leading to improved cardiac function and reduced symptoms of conditions such as atrial fibrillation and congestive heart failure.

8. Treats atrial arrhythmias

Digitalis lanata treats atrial arrhythmias because its cardiac glycosides, particularly digoxin, slow the rapid heartbeat and regulate the electrical activity of the heart.

By increasing the threshold for cardiac depolarization, digitalis reduces the firing rate of abnormal pacemaker cells in the atria, allowing the normal sinus node to take over and restore a regular heartbeat.

This effective treatment option has been used for centuries to manage atrial fibrillation and other supraventricular arrhythmias.

9. Treats ventricular arrhythmias

Digitalis lanata treats ventricular arrhythmias because it contains a cardiac glycoside called digoxin.

Digoxin works by slowing the heart rate and increasing the contraction force of the atria, which helps to regulate the electrical conduction system in the heart.

Specifically, it inhibits the Na+/K+-ATPase pump, leading to an increase in the level of intracellular calcium ions, which can help to terminate abnormal ventricular rhythms such as atrial fibrillation and supraventricular tachycardia.

10. Regulates electrical activity of heart

Digitalis lanata regulates electrical activity of heart because it contains a cardenolide compound called digoxin.

Digoxin binds to specific sites on the sodium-potassium pump in cardiac cells, slowing down the transport of ions and thereby regulating the heart's natural pacemaker function.

This results in a decrease in heart rate and an increase in the force of contraction, improving the heart's efficiency in pumping blood.
