Ficus solomonensis

Ficus solomonensis, commonly known as Solomon Islands fig, is a species of fig tree native to the Solomon Islands.

This plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering various health benefits such as wound healing, antimicrobial, and anti-inflammatory activities.

The therapeutic actions of Ficus solomonensis include antioxidant, anti-diabetic, and anti-hypertensive effects, making it a promising natural remedy for various ailments.

The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, alkaloids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Possible Side Effects of Ficus solomonensis

Ficus solomonensis can have side effects if used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, allergic reactions, and skin irritation.

In some cases, excessive consumption of the plant's sap or leaves can cause dizziness, nausea, and stomach pain.

Furthermore, improper handling of the plant can lead to skin contact dermatitis, characterized by redness, itching, and blistering.

If ingested in large quantities or without proper medical supervision, Ficus solomonensis can cause liver and kidney damage.
