What's the taxonomical classification of Alchornea floribunda?

Alchornnea floribunda belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Malpighiales. This plant is a member of the family Euphorbiaceae, specifically situated within the genus Alchornea, with its unique designation being the species floribunda.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Alchornea floribunda has a woody, perennial growth habit characterized by erect stems and opposite, simple leaves that are typically elliptic to oblong in shape. Its inflorescences consist of small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in terminal or axillary clusters. The bark tends to be relatively smooth when young but becomes more textured as the plant matures.     What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

    This plant contains flavonoid glycosides, specifically the flavonol glycoside AFF1, alongside long chain saturated and unsaturated hydrocarbons and various oxygenated derivatives. These complex chemical structures serve as the primary bioactive constituents responsible for the plant's therapeutic potential. The presence of flavonoid glycosides contributes to significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties within the organism. Additionally, the long chain saturated and unsaturated hydrocarbons provide essential lipid components that influence membrane stability. The oxygenated derivatives further expand the chemical profile, playing critical roles in the plant's metabolic and medicinal functions.

    As discussed in a study, the leaves of Alchornea floribunda are used in ethnomedicine for managing rheumatism, arthritis, and toothache. Chromatographic purification of the methanol leaf extract led to the isolation of 7 flavonoid glycosides. When splenocytes were treated with graded concentrations of these 7 compounds ranging from 6.25-25 μg/mL and incubated for 24 h, they caused an up-regulation of activated CD8(+) T-lymphocytes to 57.85-72.45% compared to 57.85% for the untreated control. These compounds also increased activated CD4(+) T-lymphocytes to 3.21-7.21% versus 2.75% for the untreated control. Stimulation with all the compounds increased the proportion of CD8(+)/IFNγ(+) and CD4(+)/IFNγ(+) T lymphocytes up to 2-fold when compared with untreated control wells.

      As discussed in a study, Alchornea floribunda leaves are widely used in African ethnomedicine for the management of acute and chronic inflammatory disorders. Bioactivity-guided fractionation of the ethyl acetate fraction of the methanol leaf extract led to the isolation of a new flavonol glycoside named AFF1. The anti-inflammatory activity of AFF1 was evaluated using an egg albumen-induced rat paw oedema model. At a dose of 50 mg kg(-1), the activity of AFF1 was higher than that of the standard drug aspirin at 100 mg kg(-1). Additionally, the compound significantly (p < 0.001) inhibited heat-induced haemolysis of human erythrocytes in vitro.
