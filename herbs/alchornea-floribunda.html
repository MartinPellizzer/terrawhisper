Alchornea floribunda

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Alchornea floribunda has a woody, perennial growth habit characterized by erect stems and opposite, simple leaves that are typically elliptic to oblong in shape. The leaf margins are often entire or slightly serrated, possessing a leathery texture that aids in moisture retention. Its inflorescences consist of small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in terminal or axillary clusters. The bark tends to be relatively smooth when young but becomes more textured as the plant matures. These morphological features collectively define its structural identity within its native habitat.

      What are the herbal preparations of Alchornea floribunda?

      Alchornea floribunda is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice using the plant's leaves and stems.
