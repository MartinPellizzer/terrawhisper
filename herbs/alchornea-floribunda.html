Alchornea floribunda

What's the taxonomical classification of Alchornea floribunda?

Alchornnea floribunda belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Malpighiales. This plant is a member of the family Euphorbiaceae, specifically situated within the genus Alchornea, with its unique designation being the species floribunda.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Alchornea floribunda has a woody, perennial growth habit characterized by erect stems and opposite, simple leaves that are typically elliptic to oblong in shape. The leaf margins are often entire or slightly serrated, possessing a leathery texture that aids in moisture retention. Its inflorescences consist of small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in terminal or axillary clusters. The bark tends to be relatively smooth when young but becomes more textured as the plant matures. These morphological features collectively define its structural identity within its native habitat.

What are the pharmacological activities of Alchornea floribunda?

This plant has diverse pharmacological activities including analgesic, anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, anticancer, and anti-arthritic properties. Researchers have identified specific phytochemical constituents within the leaves and bark that contribute to these medicinal effects. These bioactive compounds work by modulating biological pathways to reduce pain sensation and suppress systemic inflammation. Furthermore, its potent anti-inflammatory mechanisms provide significant relief for conditions associated with chronic arthritis.

As discussed in a study, Alchornea floribunda leaves are used in ethnomedicine for managing rheumatism, arthritis, and toothache. Seven flavonoid glycosides isolated from the methanol leaf extract were tested at graded concentrations of 6.25-25 μg/mL. These compounds caused the up-regulation of activated CD8(+) T-lymphocytes to 57.85-72.45% compared to 57.85% for the untreated control. They also increased activated CD4(+) T-lymphocytes to 3.21-7.21% versus 2.75% for the untreated control. Stimulation with all compounds increased the proportion of CD8(+)/IFNγ(+) and CD4(+)/IFNγ(+) T lymphocytes up to two-fold relative to the untreated control. While these T-lymphocyte-specific immune-modulatory properties were recorded, intracellular IL-2 secretion by treated T cells was not detected.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating acute inflammatory disorders, arthritis, rheumatism, inflammation, and respiratory tract ailments. Its medicinal properties help reduce systemic inflammation throughout the body. People often utilize it to manage the painful symptoms associated with arthritis and rheumatism. Additionally, the plant serves as a remedy for various respiratory tract ailments. These diverse applications make it a valuable resource for managing several different health conditions.

As discussed in a study, Alchornea floribunda leaves are widely used in African ethnomedicine for the management of acute and chronic inflammatory disorders. Bioactivity-guided fractionation of the ethyl acetate fraction of the methanol leaf extract led to the isolation of a new flavonol glycoside named AFF1. The anti-inflammatory activity of AFF1 was evaluated using an egg albumen-induced rat paw oedema model. At a dose of 50 mg kg(-1), the activity of AFF1 was higher than that of the standard drug aspirin at 100 mg kg(-1). Additionally, the compound significantly (p < 0.001) inhibited heat-induced haemolysis of human erythrocytes in vitro.
