Alchornea floribunda

What's the taxonomical classification of Alchornea floribunda?

Alchornnea floribunda belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Malpighiales. This plant is a member of the family Euphorbiaceae, specifically situated within the genus Alchornea, with its unique designation being the species floribunda.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Alchornea floribunda has a woody, perennial growth habit characterized by erect stems and opposite, simple leaves that are typically elliptic to oblong in shape. The leaf margins are often entire or slightly serrated, possessing a leathery texture that aids in moisture retention. Its inflorescences consist of small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in terminal or axillary clusters. The bark tends to be relatively smooth when young but becomes more textured as the plant matures. These morphological features collectively define its structural identity within its native habitat.

What are the pharmacological activities of Alchornea floribunda?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological activities including analgesic, anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, anti-arthritic, and anti-rheumatic properties. Scientific investigations into its various extracts reveal a potent ability to alleviate pain and reduce swelling in biological systems. The presence of specific bioactive compounds allows it to combat harmful bacterial strains effectively. Furthermore, its medicinal potential extends to managing chronic joint conditions by suppressing inflammatory responses. These multifaceted therapeutic effects make the species a subject of interest for developing new pharmaceutical treatments.

According to a study published by "Pharmaceutical biology", the leaves of Alchornea floribunda are used in ethnomedicine for rheumatism, arthritis and toothache. Seven flavonoid glycosides were isolated from the methanol leaf extract to test their effects at graded concentrations of 6.25-25 μg/mL. These compounds caused the up-regulation of activated CD8(+) T-lymphocytes to 57.85-72.45% compared to 57.85% for the untreated control. They also increased activated CD4(+) T-lymphocytes to 3.21-7.21% compared to 2.75% for the untreated control. Stimulation with all compounds increased the proportion of CD8(+)/IFNγ(+) and CD4(+)/IFNγ(+) T lymphocytes up to two-fold relative to the untreated control. While these compounds modulated IFNγ, intracellular IL-2 secretion by treated T cells was not detected.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse range of medicinal compounds including flavonoid glycosides, a specific flavonol glycoside known as AFF1, long chain saturated and unsaturated hydrocarbons, and various oxygenated derivatives. These phytochemicals contribute to the plant's complex chemical profile and potential therapeutic properties. The presence of long chain hydrocarbons suggests significant lipid-soluble components within the plant tissues. Furthermore, the oxygenated derivatives and specific glycosides play crucial roles in its biological activity. Together, these chemical constituents define the medicinal utility of Alc hornea floribunda.

According to a study published by "Pharmaceuticaal biology", the ethyl acetate fraction of the methanol leaf extract of Alchornea floribunda led to the isolation of seven flavonoid glycosides (1-7). These compounds were tested at graded concentrations of 6.25-25 μg/mL on splenocytes incubated for 24 h. The compounds 1-7 caused the up-regulation of activated CD8(+) T-lymphocytes to 57.85-72.45% compared to 57.85% for the untreated control. Additionally, they increased activated CD4(+) T-lymphocytes to 3.21-7.21% versus 2.75% for the untreated control. Stimulation with all the compounds increased the proportion of CD8(+)/IFNγ(+) and CD4(+)/IFNγ(+) T lymphocytes up to two-fold when compared with untreated control wells.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating acute inflammatory disorders, chronic inflammatory disorders, general inflammation, respiratory tract ailments, and respiratory tract infections. Its medicinal properties are primarily leveraged to reduce swelling and soothe irritated tissues throughout the body. In addition to managing systemic inflammation, it is frequently applied to alleviate symptoms associated with various respiratory tract ailments. This includes providing relief for several types of respiratory tract infections that affect the lungs and airways. By addressing these diverse ailments, the plant serves as a versatile natural remedy for both sudden and long-term health issues.

According to a study published by "Natural product research", Alchornnea floribunda leaves are widely used in African ethnomedicine for the management of acute and chronic inflammatory disorders. A novel flavonol glycoside named AFF1 was isolated from the methanol leaf extract to evaluate its anti-inflammatory activity. In a rat paw oedema model, the activity of AFF1 at 50 mg kg(-1) was higher than the standard drug aspirin at 100 mg kg(-1). The compound also significantly (p < 0.001) inhibited heat-induced haemolysis of human erythrocytes in vitro. This compound, identified as 3,5,7,3'-tetrahydroxyflavone-3-O-alpha-L-rhamnoside, accounts for part of the anti-inflammatory effect of the leaves.
