Bearberry (Uva-Ursi Auriculata)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Uva-ursi auriculata.

Bearberry, scientifically known as Uva-ursi auriculata, is a small evergreen shrub native to the northern regions of North America and Eurasia, valued for its medicinal properties. It is primarily recognized as a medicinal herb, traditionally used for its diuretic and antimicrobial effects, particularly in supporting urinary tract health. Historically, indigenous communities have utilized bearberry to treat infections, kidney stones, and as a general tonic for vitality. In modern wellness practices, it is often incorporated into herbal supplements aimed at promoting kidney function and reducing inflammation. Additionally, bearberry contains a unique compound called arbutin, which is responsible for its therapeutic effects, and its leaves have a distinctive, slightly tart flavor that has also made it a rare spice in traditional cuisine.
