Astragalus sinicus

What's the taxonomical classification of Astragalus sinicus?

Astragalus sinicus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Fabales. As a member of the family Fabaceae, it is categorized under the genus Astragalus, eventually reaching its specific designation as the species sinicus.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Astragalus sinicus contains various bioactive compounds including astragalosides, flavonoids, polysaccharides, and saponins. These specific chemical constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's ability to modulate immune responses and reduce inflammation. Polysaccharides found in the roots are often studied for their potential to enhance white blood cell activity. Additionally, the presence of unique triterpenoid saponins contributes to its adaptogenic properties. Research suggests these compounds work together to provide antioxidant effects and support overall cellular protection.

What are the therapeutic actions of Astragalus sinicus?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various inflammatory conditions, immune system disorders, viral infections, and certain types of cancer. It is frequently employed in traditional medicine to boost the body's natural defense mechanisms against pathogens. Practitioners often use its extracts to manage chronic fatigue and improve overall metabolic function. Additionally, its components are studied for their ability to reduce swelling and mitigate the symptoms of autoimmune diseases. The plant's medicinal properties are believed to stem from its complex polysaccharides and flavonoids.
