Cimicifuga simplex

What's the taxonomical classification of Cimicifuga simplex?

Cimicifuga simplex belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is situated under the order Ranunculales. It is further classified within the family Ranunculaceae, specifically falling under the genus Cimicifuga, with its unique identification as the species simplex.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cimicifuga simplex has slender, upright stems that typically reach heights of thirty to sixty centimeters and bear clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers. Each flower consists of five distinct petals and a central arrangement of numerous stamens that give the bloom a delicate appearance. The plant often develops a tuberous root system that allows it to store energy underground. These morphological features help distinguish it from other species within the Cimicifuga genus.
