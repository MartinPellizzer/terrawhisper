Citronella

Aloysia citrodora

Citronella (Aloysia citrodora) is a fragrant shrub native to the Mediterranean region, known for its lemon-scented leaves and flowers, and it has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, particularly in improving symptoms of digestive disorders and respiratory conditions.

Botanical Identification

Citronella, scientifically known as Aloysia citrodora, belongs to the Verbenaceae family. Commonly called lemon verbena, it is a perennial shrub native to South America. The plant has fragrant, lemon-scented leaves and yellow flowers, with historical use in herbal medicine for its calming and digestive properties. Synonyms include Vitex citrodora and Lemon verbena.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Lamiales
  • Order: Lamiales
  • Family: Verbenaceae
  • Genus: Aloysia
  • Species: Aloysia citrodora

Active Compounds

Citronella contains essential oil, flavonoids, and coumarin as its main bioactive compounds. These compounds work synergistically to provide its therapeutic effects, with essential oil being the primary contributor to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Flavonoids and coumarin support these actions by enhancing cellular protection and reducing oxidative stress.

  • Essential oil
  • Flavonoid
  • Coumarin

Infusion: Used for respiratory issues such as coughs and colds.

Safety Profile

Citronella, or Aloysia citrodora, is generally safe when used in moderate amounts. However, it may cause allergic reactions in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to potential risks to the fetus or infant. Always consult a healthcare provider before use, especially for prolonged periods or in high doses.
