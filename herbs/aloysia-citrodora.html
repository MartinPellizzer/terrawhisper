Citronella (Aloysia citrodora)

Citronella (Aloysia citrodora) is a member of the Verbenaceae family, native to South America, Madagascar, and Comoros. Traditionally, its leaves, flowers, and bark have been used for infusions, decoctions, and tinctures.

This herb is particularly valued for its bitter, anti-inflammatory, and carminative actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and native american herbal medicine.

Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters.
Leaves
Broad leaves with upper surface glaucous green and lower surface silver-white, bearing prominent stomatal bands.
Flowers
Inflorescences are racemose with actinomorphic flowers having five white petals and five yellow stamens, arranged in a single whorl around a central pistil.
Stems
Elongated, erect growth habit with opposite branching, glabrous surface, and presence of aromatic glands.
