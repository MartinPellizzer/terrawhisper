In the culinary world, lemon verbena is commonly used to add a subtle citrus flavor to desserts, teas, and sauces, while its leaves can be used fresh in salads or as a garnish. As a horticultural gem, lemon verbena is relatively low-maintenance, requiring minimal care and thriving in warm climates with well-drained soil.

Botanically speaking, lemon verbena is an evergreen shrub native to South America, characterized by its fragrant leaves and small white flowers, which have been revered for centuries in traditional medicine and folk remedies, dating back to the ancient Greeks and Romans who referred to it as "herba citrina".
