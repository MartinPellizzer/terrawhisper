What's the taxonomical classification of Piper umbellatum?

Piper umbellatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. As a member of the order Piperales, it falls under the family Piperaceae. The plant is further categorized into the genus Piper, ultimately reaching its specific identification at the species level as Piper umbellatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Piper umbellatum has simple, ovate to elliptical leaves with an entire or slightly wavy margin and an acute apex, growing on woody stems that exhibit a scrambling or climbing habit. The foliage is characterized by prominent pinnate venation and a smooth, somewhat leathery texture. Its inflorescence consists of small, spike-like structures that emerge from the leaf axils. These flowering spikes eventually develop into small, fleshy drupes that are typically red or dark in color when mature. The overall growth form is that of a perennial shrub or vine, often found in tropical understory environments.
