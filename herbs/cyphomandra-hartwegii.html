Cyphomandra Hartwegii

Cyphomandra hartwegii, commonly known as the horned nightshade or the bitter apple, is a species of nightshade plant native to South America.

While it is often considered a weed, it has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties. The plant contains various bioactive compounds, including alkaloids and glycosides, which may contribute to its medicinal effects. In some indigenous cultures, it has been utilized to treat ailments such as inflammation and digestive issues.

However, due to its toxic nature, caution is necessary when using it for medicinal purposes, and it should only be handled by trained professionals.
