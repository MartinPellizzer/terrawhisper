Pedicularis szetschuanica

What's the taxonomical classification of Pedicularis szetschuanica?

Pedicularis szetschuanica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Lamiales. This plant is a member of the family Orobanchaceae and is specifically categorized within the genus Pedicularis, eventually reaching its unique species designation as Pedicularis szetschuanica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pedicularis szetschuanica has upright, unbranched stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and are adorned with lanceolate to linear leaves that are often deeply lobed or serrated.

The inflorescence consists of a dense, terminal spike of tubular, bilabiate flowers that display colors ranging from pink to deep purple. Each individual flower features a prominent, hooded upper lip that protects the reproductive organs from environmental elements. The lower lip of the corolla is typically three-lobed and serves as a landing platform for pollinators.

Small, inconspicuous bracts are positioned at the base of each floral structure along the stem.
