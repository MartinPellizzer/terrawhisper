Pedicularis szetschuanica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pedicularis szetschuanica has upright, unbranched stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and are adorned with lanceolate to linear leaves that are often deeply lobed or serrated. The inflorescence consists of a dense, terminal spike of tubular, bilabiate flowers that display colors ranging from pink to deep purple. Each individual flower features a prominent, hooded upper lip that protects the reproductive organs from environmental elements. The lower lip of the corolla is typically three-lobed and serves as a landing platform for pollinators. Small, inconspicuous bracts are positioned at the base of each floral structure along the stem.
