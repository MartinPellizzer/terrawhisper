What's the taxonomical classification of Fritillaria usuriensis?

Fritillaria usuriensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical lineage further, it falls under the order Liliales and is a member of the family Liliaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Fritillaria and is specifically designated as the species usuriensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Fritillaria usuriensis has bulbous structures with several lanceolate leaves and nodding, bell-shaped flowers that feature greenish-yellow tepals marked with dark purple or brownish spots. The plant typically grows from a small, tunicated bulb that supports a stem reaching moderate heights. Its leaves are arranged in a whorl or are sparsely distributed along the upper portion of the stem. The drooping blossoms are characterized by a distinct curvature and a patterned interior that aids in pollination. These morphological traits are characteristic of the Liliales order within its specific highland habitat.
