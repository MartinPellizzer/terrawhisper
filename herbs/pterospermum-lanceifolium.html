Pterospermum lanceifolium

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pterospermum lanceifolium has lanceolate leaves with entire margins, a woody stem structure, and small, inconspicuous flowers. The foliage is characterized by elongated, pointed blades that taper to a fine tip at the apex. Its stems exhibit a branched growth habit typical of many woody shrubs or small trees within its genus. The leaf arrangement often follows an alternate pattern along the length of the branches. These morphological features collectively distinguish the species from other members of the Pterospermum family.

    What are the herbal preparations of Pterospermum lanceifolium?

    Pterospermum lanceifolium is prepared as a decoction, infusion, or topical paste using its various plant parts including the leaves, bark, and flowers. The leaves are often boiled in water to create a liquid extract used for internal consumption or medicinal baths. Bark extracts are frequently processed into concentrated decoctions to treat specific inflammatory conditions. These diverse preparation methods allow the plant's bioactive compounds to be administered according to the specific therapeutic need.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Pterospermum lanceifolium can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or localized redness upon direct contact with its sap or foliage. The plant contains secondary metabolites that may trigger hypersensitivity reactions in individuals with sensitive skin. Contact with the leaves can lead to itching or small rashes if the person is not immune to its chemical compounds. There is limited clinical data regarding systemic toxicity, but caution is advised against ingestion. Always use gloves when handling the plant to prevent unintended dermatological issues.
