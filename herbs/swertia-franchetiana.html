As discussed in a study, Swertifrancheside [1] is a new flavonone-xanthone glucoside isolated from Swertia franchetiana. This compound was found to be a potent inhibitor of the DNA polymerase activity of human immunodeficiency virus-1 reverse transcriptase (HIV-1 RT) with a 50% inhibitory dose (IC50 value) of 43 microM. It was not cytotoxic with cultured mammalian cells. Swertifrancheside [1] bound to DNA and was shown to be a competitive inhibitor with respect to template-primer, but a mixed-type competitive inhibitor with respect to TTP. The compound inhibits enzyme activity by binding to the template-primer.
