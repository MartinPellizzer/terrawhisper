Chirita parvifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Chirita parvifolia?

Chirita parvifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic descent, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized under the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Gesneriaceae. Within this family, it is identified by the genus Chirita, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species parvifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Chirita parvifolia has small, lanceolate leaves that are oppositely arranged on a slender stem and tubular, two-lipped flowers that typically feature a pale coloring.

The foliage is characterized by its diminutive size and relatively smooth margins, which distinguish it from larger-leaved species in its genus. The floral structure consists of a long corolla tube that expands into distinct upper and lower lobes to facilitate pollination. These delicate blossoms often emerge from the leaf axils along the upright flowering stalks.

The overall morphology reflects an adaptation to specific microhabitats where compact growth provides a survival advantage.
