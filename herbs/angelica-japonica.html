Japanese Angelica (Angelica japonica)

Japanese Angelica (Angelica japonica) is a member of the Apiaceae family, native to Japan, Korea, and Eastern China. Traditionally, its roots, stems, and leaves have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in japanese kampo medicine, korean traditional medicine, and traditional chinese medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically reaches a height of 1 to 2 meters.
Leaves
Bipinnate compound leaves with upper surface dark green and lower surface pale green, featuring prominent stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Inflorescence consists of dense, clustered umbels with white flowers, each flower having four petals, five sepals, and a superior ovary with two carpels, exhibiting actinomorphic symmetry.
Stems
Elongated, erect growth habit, opposite branching pattern, smooth surface, hollow internodes, presence of prominent ridges and grooves along the stem.
