Japanese angelica

Angelica japonica

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Japanese Angelica, scientifically known as Angelica japonica, belongs to the family Apiaceae. It is also called Japanese angelica or mountain angelica. A perennial herb native to East Asia, it has a thick, aromatic root, pinnately lobed leaves, and small white flowers. Common synonyms include Angelica acutiloba and Angelica sparsifolia.

Active Compounds

Japanese Angelica contains key bioactive compounds such as coumarin, flavonoids, and essential oils. These compounds work together to provide its medicinal effects, with coumarin contributing to anti-inflammatory properties and flavonoids supporting antioxidant activity. The synergistic interaction between these components enhances its therapeutic potential in traditional herbal medicine.

  • Coumarin
  • Flavonoid
  • Essential oil

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action
Respiratory Common cold, asthma, cough, respiratory infections, nasal congestion Expectorant, bronchodilator, antimicrobial
Integumentary Skin infections, skin inflammation, skin allergies Antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, antihistaminic
Musculoskeletal Rheumatoid arthritis, rheumatism Anti-inflammatory

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory issues and digestive problems.

Decoction: Used for treating colds and flu symptoms.

Extract: Used for improving circulation and reducing inflammation.

Safety Profile

Japanese Angelica (Angelica japonica) is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause allergic reactions in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and should be avoided by those with a history of allergies to related plants.
