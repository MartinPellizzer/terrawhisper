Japanese Angelica (Angelica japonica): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

Japanese Angelica (Angelica japonica) is a perennial herb native to Japan, known for its medicinal properties and traditional use in treating conditions such as arthritis, inflammation, and digestive issues.

What is Japanese Angelica (Angelica Japonica)?

Japanese Angelica (Angelica japonica) is a perennial herb native to Japan, characterized by its tall, slender stem, large compound leaves, and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers, commonly used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.

Botanical Identity and Classification

Japanese Angelica (Angelica japonica) is a biennial or perennial herb native to East Asia, belonging to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its tall stem, compound leaves, and umbel-shaped flowers.

Common Names and Synonyms

Japanese Angelica, or Angelica japonica, is commonly known by various regional names such as Shōbu-ō, Shōbu-ōsō, and Shōbu-ōsō-kyō, reflecting its traditional use in Japanese herbal medicine.

Plant Description and Morphology

Japanese Angelica (Angelica japonica) is a biennial herb characterized by a thick, branched root system, large pinnately compound leaves with a serrated margin, and yellow-green flowers arranged in clustered umbels.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Japanese Angelica (Angelica japonica) is native to the mountainous regions of Japan, particularly in the northern and central areas, and is also found in parts of Korea and eastern China.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The roots, leaves, and seeds of Japanese Angelica (Angelica japonica) are the primary plant parts used medicinally in traditional herbal practices.

Phytochemical Composition

Japanese Angelica (Angelica japonica) contains a variety of bioactive compounds, including volatile oils, lignans, flavonoids, and alkaloids, which contribute to its pharmacological properties and traditional use in herbal medicine.

Pharmacological Properties

Japanese Angelica (Angelica japonica) exhibits a range of pharmacological properties including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and hepatoprotective effects, primarily attributed to its rich content of lignans, coumarins, and essential oils.

Mechanisms of Action

Japanese Angelica (Angelica japonica) exerts its pharmacological effects primarily through the activation of antioxidant pathways, modulation of inflammatory responses via suppression of pro-inflammatory cytokines, and enhancement of immune function through stimulation of T-cell and B-cell activity.

Therapeutic Uses and Indications

Japanese angelica is used to treat respiratory infections, coughs, bronchitis, digestive issues, headaches, and as a general tonic to improve circulation and overall vitality.

Preparation Methods and Forms

Japanese Angelica (Angelica japonica) is typically prepared as a dried root or root bark, often processed into powders, decoctions, or tinctures for use in traditional herbal medicine.

Dosage and Administration

The typical dosage of Japanese Angelica (Angelica japonica) for herbal use ranges from 2 to 5 grams per day, typically prepared as a decoction or tincture, and should be taken under the guidance of a qualified herbalist or healthcare professional.

Safety, Side Effects, and Contraindications

When used in excess or over a prolonged period, Japanese Angelica (Angelica japonica) may cause gastrointestinal discomfort, such as nausea, vomiting, or diarrhea, and in some cases, may lead to allergic reactions or skin irritation.

Drug Interactions

Japanese Angelica (Angelica japonica) may interact with anticoagulant medications due to its potential to increase bleeding risk, and it may also interact with hypotensive drugs as it can lower blood pressure.

Toxicity and Precautions

While Japanese Angelica (Angelica japonica) is generally considered safe when used in moderate amounts, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals and should be avoided during pregnancy due to potential uterine stimulant effects.

Cultivation and Harvesting

Japanese Angelica (Angelica japonica) is typically cultivated in shaded, moist environments with well-drained soil, often in mountainous regions of Japan, and requires careful management of temperature and humidity to thrive.

Processing and Extraction

The processing of Japanese Angelica (Angelica japonica) typically involves drying the roots and rhizomes in a shaded, well-ventilated area to preserve their active compounds, followed by grinding into a powder or slicing for use in herbal formulations.

Storage and Shelf Life

Japanese Angelica (Angelica japonica) should be stored in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture, and its shelf life is typically 2 to 3 years when properly preserved.

Scientific Research and Evidence

Scientific research on Japanese Angelica (Angelica japonica) has shown potential pharmacological activities, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, with some studies suggesting its use in traditional medicine for digestive and respiratory health.

Traditional and Historical Uses

Japanese Angelica (Angelica japonica) has been traditionally used in Japanese herbal medicine for its purported effects on digestion, respiratory health, and as a general tonic to support vitality and immunity.

Regulatory Status

In Japan, Japanese Angelica (Angelica japonica) is classified as a specific medicinal plant under the Pharmaceutical and Medical Device Act, and its use in traditional herbal medicine is regulated by the Ministry of Health, Labour and Welfare.
