What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ophiopogon tonkinensis has tufted, evergreen perennial foliage consisting of narrow, linear leaves that measure approximately 2 to 5 millimeters in width and reach lengths of 10 to 25 centimeters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white to pale lavender flowers that emerge from the base of the leaf clusters. Following flowering, it develops small, globose, dark blue to black berries that ripen in late summer or autumn. The rhizomes are slender and creeping, allowing the plant to form dense, low-growing groundcover mats.
