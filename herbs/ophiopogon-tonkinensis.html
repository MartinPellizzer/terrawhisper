Ophiopogon tonkinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Ophiopogon tonkinensis?

Ophiopogon tonkinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its hierarchical lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Asparagales. Within this order, the plant is a member of the family Asparagaceae. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Ophiopogon, with its specific designation being the species tonkinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ophiopogon tonkinensis has tufted, evergreen perennial foliage consisting of narrow, linear leaves that measure approximately 2 to 5 millimeters in width and reach lengths of 10 to 25 centimeters. These leaves are typically dark green with a slightly arching growth habit and fine parallel venation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white to pale lavender flowers that emerge from the base of the leaf clusters. Following flowering, it develops small, globose, dark blue to black berries that ripen in late summer or autumn. The rhizomes are slender and creeping, allowing the plant to form dense, low-growing groundcover mats.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the temperate forests of Honshu, Shikoku, and Kyushu in Japan. It grows primarily in mountainous regions where it occupies shaded, moist habitats under forest canopies. These populations are often found in well-drained soils along slopes or near woodland edges. The species maintains a relatively localized distribution within these specific Japanese islands. Its presence is closely tied to the unique microclimates provided by the subtropical and temperate vegetation of its home range.
