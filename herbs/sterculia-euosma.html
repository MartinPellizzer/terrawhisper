What's the taxonomical classification of Sterculia euosma?

Sterculia euosma belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Malvales. The plant is further categorized under the family Malvacaceae and sits within the genus Sterculia, eventually reaching its specific designation as the species euosma.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sterculia euosma has a woody tree structure characterized by alternate, trifoliate leaves with elliptical to obovate leaflets that possess entire margins and acute tips.

The bark is typically smooth or slightly fissured, providing a protective layer for the trunk and branches. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils, often appearing in clusters or solitary arrangements. These blooms lead to the development of woody, capsule-like fruits that split open to reveal seeds.
