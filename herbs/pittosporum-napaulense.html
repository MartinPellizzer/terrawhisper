Pittosporum napaulense

What's the taxonomical classification of Pittosporum napaulense?

Pittosporum napaulense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is placed in the order Apiales and belongs to the family Pittosporaceae. Finally, it is identified within the genus Pittosporum, specifically as the species napaulense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pittosporum napaulense has elliptical to lanceolate leaves that are leathery in texture, glossy dark green on the upper surface, and often feature a lighter underside. The plant typically develops as a small tree or large shrub with spreading branches and a dense canopy. Its flowers are small, cream-colored, and produced in clusters that emit a distinct fragrance to attract pollinators. After flowering, the plant produces woody capsules that split open to reveal bright orange or red seeds.
