Pittosporum napaulense

What's the taxonomical classification of Pittosporum napaulense?

Pittosporum napaulense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is placed in the order Apiales and belongs to the family Pittosporaceae. Finally, it is identified within the genus Pittosporum, specifically as the species napaulense.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Pittosporum napaulense can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and potential digestive distress if any part of the plant is ingested. The sap or juices from the leaves and stems may lead to redness, itching, or a burning sensation upon contact with sensitive skin. Inhaling dust or pollen from the plant might trigger respiratory reactions such as sneezing or coughing in susceptible individuals. While specific systemic toxicity data is limited, it is generally advised to handle the plant with gloves to prevent dermatological issues. Careful observation is necessary if accidental ingestion occurs to manage any gastrointestinal upset.
