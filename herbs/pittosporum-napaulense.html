Pittosporum napaulense

What's the taxonomical classification of Pittosporum napaulense?

Pittosporum napaulense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is placed in the order Apiales and belongs to the family Pittosporaceae. Finally, it is identified within the genus Pittosporum, specifically as the species napaulense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pittosporum napaulense has elliptical to lanceolate leaves that are leathery in texture, glossy dark green on the upper surface, and often feature a lighter underside. After flowering, the plant produces woody capsules that split open to reveal bright orange or red seeds. What are the pharmacological activities of Pittosporum napaulense?

This plant has antibacterial, antibiofilm, and antioxidant pharmacological activities derived from its diverse secondary metabolites. The antibacterial properties allow the extracts to inhibit the growth of various pathogenic microorganisms. Furthermore, the plant demonstrates significant antibiofilm activity by preventing bacteria from forming protective layers that resist treatment. Its antioxidant capacity helps neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress within biological systems. These combined therapeutic effects suggest that the species holds great potential for developing new medicinal applications.

According to a study published by "Natural product research", Pittosporum napaulense (DC.) Rehder & E. H. Wilson is an important medicinal plant with widespread pharmacological importance. The ethanolic extract of the plant demonstrated antioxidant potential through DPPH and ABTS radical scavenging activity with an IC50 of 5.58 µg/ml and 5.28 µg/ml, respectively. Additionally, the plant showed antibacterial and antibiofilm properties against multidrug-resistant bacterial strains, specifically Staphylococcus aureus and Shigella sp. The biological activities of the P. napaulense (DC.) bark are attributed to phytoconstituents like Malabaricone C, Borapetoside B, and Kanzonol R, as shown by UPLC-Q-TOF-MS analysis. Based on these bioactivities, the plant could be explored for developing potential therapeutic drug candidates against severe bacterial diseases.
