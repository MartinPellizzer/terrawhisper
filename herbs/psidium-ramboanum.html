What is Psidium ramboanum?

Psidium ramboanum is a species of plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is a tree or shrub with a height range of 5-10 meters and a trunk diameter of up to 20 cm. The leaves are elliptical, alternate, and 4-8 cm long, with a 1-2 cm petiole and a cuneate base. The flowers are white, in axillary corymbose panicles, with 4-6 mm long sepals and 8-10 mm long petals, and are pollinated by bees and butterflies.

The fruit is a berry, 2-3 cm long, red or yellow when ripe, and contains 2-4 seeds.
