Eriophorum comosum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eriophorum comosum has linear, grass-like leaves that grow from a rhizomatous base and bear small, solitary flower heads atop long, slender stalks. These leaves are typically narrow and lack the prominent midrib found in many related species. The inflorescence consists of a compact cluster of small flowers surrounded by white, silky bristles that give the plant a cotton-like appearance. These bristles act as a dispersal mechanism for the seeds when they reach maturity. The overall structure is delicate and adapted to the damp, boggy environments where it thrives.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude alpine and subalpine regions of the Andes mountains in South America. It is primarily located within the puna ecosystem across countries such as Peru, Bolivia, and northern Chile. These populations thrive in specific microclimates characterized by moist, peat-rich soils and cold temperatures. The distribution is often restricted to isolated mountain peaks where specialized wetland conditions exist. Because of these niche habitat requirements, the species remains localized rather than widespread.
