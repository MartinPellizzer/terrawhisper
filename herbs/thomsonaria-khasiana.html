What is Thomsonaria khasiana?

Thomsonaria khasiana is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is a shrub-like plant with deciduous leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape, 5-10 cm long, and have 3-5 pairs of lateral veins. The flowers are small, white to pinkish, and have 5 petals, while the fruit is a pome, 1-2 cm in diameter, with a leathery pericarp. The plant is found in the Himalayan region of India and Pakistan, typically growing in moist, humid areas at elevations of 1,800-3,200 meters.

Thomsonaria khasiana is a member of the genus Thomsonaria, which is a group of plants closely related to the genus Pyrus.
