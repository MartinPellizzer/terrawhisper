Botanical Identification of Urena (Urena lobata)

To identify Urena (Urena lobata) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Urena lobata belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Malvales, family Malvaceae, genus Urena, and species Urena lobata. These classifications help identify its botanical relationships and place it within the broader plant kingdom.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of the plant is Urena lobata, first described by the botanist Carl von Linné. It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is also known by several synonyms, including Abelmoschus manihot and Urena lobata var. lobata. Common names include velvetleaf and cotton bush, while regional names vary, with some areas referring to it as jungle cotton or wild cotton. Historically, it has been known by names such as Abelmoschus and Urena.

Morphological Characteristics

Urena lobata is an annual herb with a branched root system. It has erect, hairy stems that can grow up to 1 meter tall. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture. The inflorescence is a cluster of small, yellowish flowers. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Urena lobata can be distinguished by its opposite, oval leaves with hairy surfaces, clusters of small white flowers, and cylindrical, hairy seed pods. It differs from similar species like Sida and Marsilea through leaf shape, flower arrangement, and distinct seed pod structure. These features aid in accurate identification during herbal medicine studies.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Urena lobata is native to the Americas, particularly tropical and subtropical regions. It thrives in warm climates with high humidity. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils. It is commonly found at low to moderate altitudes, typically below 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Urena lobata blooms from late spring to early summer, producing small, fluffy white flowers. Seed formation occurs in late summer, with mature seeds dispersing by autumn. For identification purposes, harvest timing is best during late summer to early fall when the plant is most mature and seeds are fully developed.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Urena lobata belongs to the Malvaceae family, closely related to species like Abutilon and Sida. It shares characteristics such as palmate leaves and capsule fruits with its genus. Its phylogenetic position highlights its evolutionary ties to other flowering plants in the mallow family.
