Cumin (Trachyspermum ammi)

Cumin (Trachyspermum ammi) is a member of the Apiaceae family, native to Southwest Asia, Middle East, and North Africa. Traditionally, its seeds, fruits, and leaves have been used for powders, decoctions, and infusions.

This herb is particularly valued for its antispasmodic, carminative, and diuretic actions, and has a long history of use in ayurvedic medicine, european herbal medicine, and mediterranean herbal traditions.

Annual herbaceous plant.

Leaves
Lanceolate leaves with upper surface dull green and lower surface pale green, bearing distinct stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Umbellate inflorescences with white to pale yellow flowers, actinomorphic, having four ovate petals with a narrow claw, and two stamens with distinct anthers.
Stems
Erect, branched, hairy, with opposite leaves and a pungent aroma.
