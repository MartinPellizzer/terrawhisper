Trachyspermum ammi

What's the taxonomical classification of Trachyspermum ammi?

Trachyspermum ammi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Apiales and the family Apiaceae. Ultimately, the plant is defined by its specific classification within the genus Trachyspermum and the species ammi.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Trachyspermum ammi has an erect, branched herbaceous structure characterized by pinnately compound leaves with narrow, linear leaflets and small, white, umbellate flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 90 centimeters and features slender, ribbed stems. Its leaves are arranged alternately and possess a distinctively fine, feathery texture. The inflorescence consists of many small, five-petaled flowers clustered in compound umbels. The fruit is a small, dry schizocarp that contains seeds with characteristic ridges.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Trachyspermum ammi contains medicinal compounds primarily located in its dried fruits, which are commonly known as ajwain seeds. These fruits are harvested and dried to be used as a potent spice and therapeutic agent in various traditional medicine systems. The seeds contain essential oils, most notably thymol, which provide significant antimicrobial and antiseptic properties. Beyond the fruit, the plant's volatile oils extracted from the seeds are utilized to treat digestive issues like bloating and indigestion. What are the pharmacological activities of Trachyspermum ammi?

This plant has antibacterial, antimicrobial, antioxidant, antispasmodic, and anti-inflammatory pharmacological activities. Its potent antimicrobial properties allow it to effectively inhibit the growth of various pathogenic microorganisms. The presence of bioactive compounds also provides significant antioxidant effects by neutralizing harmful free radicals in the body. Additionally, the plant exhibits antispasmodic actions that help relieve muscle contractions and spasms. These combined chemical profiles contribute to its widespread use in traditional medicine for treating various ailments.

As discussed in a study, Trachyspermum ammmi possesses various pharmacological attributes, including antioxidant, antibacterial, cytotoxic, and antidiaabetic activities. HPLC analysis of the nanosuspensions showed kaempferol and sinapic acid at levels of 11.5 ppm and 12.3 ppm, respectively. The antioxidant activity reached a highest free radical scavenging rate of 14.9% during the DPPH scavenging assay. Regarding antidiaabetic potential, the nanosuspension showed maximum inhibition activity at 25.35 ± 0.912133% and 34.6 ± 1.3675% in antiglycation and α-amylase assays. Additionally, the nanosuspensions demonstrated a biofilm inhibition activity of 3.75 ± 3.529.5% and a hemolysis of 22.73 ± 1.539%.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds carvacrol, thymol, kaempferol, rutin, and various flavonoids. These specific chemical constituents contribute to the plant's diverse biological activities and therapeutic potential. Carvacrol and thymol are prominent phenolic compounds known for their strong antimicrobial and antioxidant properties. Additionally, the plant is rich in flavonoids, a broad class of phytonutrients that help reduce inflammation. Within this group, the specific compounds kaempferol and rutin provide further protection against oxidative stress in the body.

As discussed in a study, the essential oil extracted from Trachyspermum ammi aromatic water contains thymol as the major compound at 78.08%. This essential oil also includes carvacrol at 8.20% and carvotanacetone at 6.50%. The aromatic water exhibited antifungal activities and inhibited the biofilm formation of C. albicans at a concentration of up to 0.25 V/V. The medicinal properties of this plant are linked to the presence of phenolic monoterpene compounds. These components provide antioxidant properties alongside antimicrobial effects.

What side effects this plant can have?
