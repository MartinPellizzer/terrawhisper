      Melodinus tenuicaudatus is prepared as a concentrated decoction, a topical poultice made from crushed leaves, and a standardized dried powder for oral consumption.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Melodinus tenuicaudatus can cause severe localized skin irritation, acute gastrointestinal distress, and potential neurological tremors if ingested or handled without protection. The plant contains specialized alkaloids that trigger immediate redness and swelling upon contact with human epidermis. Once these compounds enter the bloodstream through accidental ingestion, they frequently result in nausea, abdominal cramping, and vomiting. Some individuals may also experience temporary dizziness or loss of fine motor coordination. These reactions typically subside after medical intervention or thorough decontamination of the affected areas.
