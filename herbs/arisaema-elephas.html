Arisaema elephas

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Arisaema elephas has a large, fleshy tuberous rhizome and a single, broadly ovate leaf that can reach significant dimensions. The leaf structure features a long, thick petiole that supports a wide blade often marked with distinct variegation. During the flowering stage, it produces a spadix enclosed by a prominent, greenish-purple spathe. This inflorescence emerges from the base of the leaf stalk to facilitate pollination. The plant's overall architecture is characterized by robust, succulent textures typical of the Araceae family.

    What are the herbal preparations of Arisaema elephas?

    Arisaema elephas is prepared primarily as a decoction of its processed corms, a powder derived from dried tubers, or as a topical paste for external applications. To mitigate the toxicity caused by calcium oxalate crystals, the plant material must undergo specific processing techniques like steaming or boiling before consumption. When used in decoctions, the active compounds are extracted into a liquid form for more rapid absorption by the body. The powdered form is often used in traditional formulations where precise dosing is required for systemic effects. Additionally, the paste is applied directly to the skin to treat localized swellings or inflammatory conditions.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Arisaema elephas can cause severe skin irritation, intense oral burning, and swelling of the mucous membranes due to the presence of insoluble calcium oxalate crystals. When the plant touches the skin, these needle-like crystals penetrate the tissue to cause redness and itching. If ingested, the crystals trigger immediate pain in the mouth, throat, and digestive tract. This irritation can lead to significant swelling that may temporarily interfere with swallowing or breathing. Prolonged contact or accidental ingestion requires careful monitoring to manage these inflammatory responses.
