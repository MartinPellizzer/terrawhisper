What's the taxonomical classification of Arisaema elephas?

Arisaema elephas belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Alismatales and the family Araceae, eventually reaching its specific designation within the genus Arisaema. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its unique species name, elephas.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause severe oral irritation, a burning sensation, swelling of the mouth or throat, difficulty swallowing, nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and dyspnea. Contact with the plant's calcium oxalate crystals often triggers immediate pain and inflammation in the mucous membranes. If the plant is ingested, the resulting swelling can lead to significant difficulty swallowing and respiratory distress known as dyspnea. Gastrointestinal distress follows ingestion, frequently manifesting as intense nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain. These systemic and localized reactions require immediate medical attention to manage potential airway obstruction.
