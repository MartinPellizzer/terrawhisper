Forsythia (Forsythia suspensa)

Forsythia (Forsythia suspensa) is a member of the Oleaceae family, native to China, Korea, and Japan. Traditionally, its flowers, leaves, and stems have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, expectorant, and bitter actions, and has a long history of use in traditional chinese medicine, japanese kampo medicine, and korean traditional medicine.

Flowers
Inflorescence composed of clustered yellow flowers with four petals each, actinomorphic symmetry, and five stamens arranged in two whorls.
Stems
Elongated, woody stems with opposite branching, covered in short, glandular hairs, exhibiting a bushy growth habit.
