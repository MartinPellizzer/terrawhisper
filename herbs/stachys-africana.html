What is Stachys africana?

Stachys africana is a perennial plant with a woody base, belonging to the family Lamiaceae.

Its leaves are opposite, sessile, elliptic to ovate, and gland-dotted, measuring 2-6 cm in length, with a cuneate base and an acute apex. The inflorescence is a terminal spike, with flowers in shades of purple or pink, in the axils of the upper leaves. Each flower is zygomorphic, with a 4-lobed calyx and a 2-lipped corolla, comprising a 2-lobed upper lip and a 3-lobed lower lip.

The plant grows to a height of 50-100 cm, in moist, rocky areas at high elevations.
