Lindsaea ensifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lindsaea ensifolia has once-pinnate fronds consisting of narrow, sword-shaped segments that are deeply lobed and taper to a sharp point. The stipe is typically dark and robust, supporting a lamina that exhibits a distinctively linear or ensiform geometry. Each segment features small, inconspicuous sori located near the margins, which are protected by specialized indusia. The texture of the foliage is relatively thin, often appearing somewhat leathery to the touch. These morphological characteristics allow the plant to thrive in the shaded, humid environments of its natural forest habitat.
