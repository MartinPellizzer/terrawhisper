Common Chickweed (Capsella Bursa-Pastoris Trevirorum)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Capsella bursa-pastoris trevirorum.

Common chickweed, scientifically known as Capsella bursa-pastoris trevirorum, is a low-growing medicinal herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in lawns, gardens, and moist soils. It has been traditionally used for its soothing properties, offering benefits such as reducing inflammation, easing skin irritations, and supporting respiratory health. In ancient times, it was valued by herbalists and cultures like the Romans and Greeks for its ability to calm the body and mind, often used in poultices and teas. Today, it is incorporated into modern wellness practices as a natural remedy for allergies, eczema, and as a mild adaptogen to reduce stress. Its unique feature includes a distinctive, slightly sweet and peppery flavor, along with compounds like aucubin that contribute to its therapeutic effects.
