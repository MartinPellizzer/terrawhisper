Pedicularis semitorta

What's the taxonomical classification of Pedicularis semitorta?

Pedicularis semitorta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its placement in the order Lamiales, the plant is a member of the family Orobanchaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its membership in the genus Pedicularis, with its unique designation being the species semitorta.

The plant contains bioactive secondary metabolites that interact with gamma-aminobutyric acid receptors to produce sedative and anxiolytic effects. These compounds may also influence ion channels within the central nervous system to regulate neuronal excitability. Through these complex protein-ligand interactions, the plant exerts its systemic physiological influence.

While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, many species within the Pedicularis genus contain iridoid glycosides which may trigger sensitivities. Contact with the plant could lead to localized redness or itching for individuals with sensitive skin. Ingestion of unknown wild plants often carries a risk of nausea or stomach cramping. It is essential to exercise caution and avoid consumption unless the safety profile is fully established.
