Pedicularis semitorta

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pedicularis semitorta has a perennial rootstock that produces upright stems reaching heights of 10 to 40 centimeters, topped by dense, tubular flowers with a distinct galea and a protruding lower lip. The leaves are typically pinnately divided into narrow, linear segments that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its inflorescence consists of a compact spike or raceme where the individual flowers exhibit colors ranging from pale pink to deep reddish-purple. The calyx is deeply cleft into narrow lobes, while the corolla structure is specialized to facilitate pollination by specific insects. These morphological features allow the plant to thrive in its native subalpine and alpine habitats.
