Tadehagi pseudotriquetrum

What's the taxonomical classification of Tadehagi pseudotriquetrum?

Tadehagi pseudotriquetrum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Fabales. The plant is further categorized into the family Fabaceae, which places it among the legumes. Within this family, it is identified by the genus Tadehagi, with its specific designation being the species pseudotriquetrum.

What are the therapeutic actions of Tadehagi pseudotriquetrum?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, digestive disorders, and respiratory ailments. Its medicinal properties are often harnessed to address inflammation and promote the healing of external injuries. In traditional practices, decoctions or pastes made from the plant are applied to soothe skin irritations and fungal issues. Some practitioners also utilize its extracts to alleviate gastrointestinal discomfort and manage coughs. The specific application depends on the part of the plant used and the method of preparation.

What are the herbal preparations of Tadehagi pseudotriquetrum?

Tadehagi pseudotriquetrum is prepared as a decoction, a medicinal tea, or a topical poultice to treat various ailments. The dried roots and stems are typically boiled in water to extract active compounds for oral consumption. These aqueous extracts are often consumed to address internal issues such as inflammation or digestive discomfort. For external skin conditions, the plant material can be crushed and applied directly to the affected area as a paste. Some traditional practices also involve using the plant in fermented liquids or infused oils for topical application.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Tadehagi pseudotriquetrum can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and localized swelling upon direct contact with its tissues. The plant contains various bioactive compounds that may trigger hypersensitivity reactions in sensitive individuals. When handled without protection, the sap or leaves can lead to redness and itching on the skin. Some users may also experience gastrointestinal distress if any part of the plant is accidentally ingested. It is important to exercise caution when working near this species to avoid these adverse reactions.
