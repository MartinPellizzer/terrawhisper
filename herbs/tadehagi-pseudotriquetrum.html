Tadehagi pseudotriquetrum

What's the taxonomical classification of Tadehagi pseudotriquetrum?

Tadehagi pseudotriquetrum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Fabales. The plant is further categorized into the family Fabaceae, which places it among the legumes. Within this family, it is identified by the genus Tadehagi, with its specific designation being the species pseudotriquetrum.

Additionally, certain extracts demonstrate an ability to modulate matrix metalloproteinases (MMPs) which are crucial in managing tissue degradation. By interacting with these specific protein families, the plant exerts its potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant medicinal effects.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Tadehagi pseudotriquetrum can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and localized swelling upon direct contact with its tissues. The plant contains various bioactive compounds that may trigger hypersensitivity reactions in sensitive individuals. When handled without protection, the sap or leaves can lead to redness and itching on the skin. Some users may also experience gastrointestinal distress if any part of the plant is accidentally ingested. It is important to exercise caution when working near this species to avoid these adverse reactions.
