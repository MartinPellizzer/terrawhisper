Saussurea licentiana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea licentiana has a rosette-forming habit characterized by numerous lanceolate leaves with entire or slightly serrated margins and a dense covering of fine, silvery hairs. The flowering stem is typically erect and slender, terminating in a compact, many-flowered capitulum. Each individual floret within the head displays the characteristic tubular structure and yellowish-white coloration typical of the genus. The involucral bracts are arranged in several overlapping series, providing protective coverage for the developing florets. This specialized morphology allows the plant to thrive in the high-altitude, rocky environments of its native habitat.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the high-altitude alpine regions of the Sichuan province in southwestern China. It is primarily found growing in rocky crevices and scree slopes within the mountainous terrain of the Hengduan Mountains. These specific habitats are characterized by cold, moist environments typical of high elevation ecosystems. Its range is quite restricted, making it a localized species tied to very particular geological and climatic conditions.
