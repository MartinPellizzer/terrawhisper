Helwingia himalaica

What's the taxonomical classification of Helwingia himalaica?

Helwingia himalaica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is positioned in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Aquifoliales. This plant is a member of the family Helwingiaceae, specifically situated within the genus Helwingia, and is identified by the specific epithet himalayica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Helwingia himalaica has woody, deciduous shrubs characterized by slender, grayish-brown branches and simple, alternate, ovate-to-elliptic leaves with serrated margins.

The leaf blades are typically thin and possess distinct pinnate venation that is visible on the undersides. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in clusters, often appearing before or alongside the emerging foliage. These flowers are typically unisexual and are borne on short lateral branches.

The resulting fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that transitions in color as it matures.
