1. Increases heart rate rapidly

Atropa bella-donna increases heart rate rapidly due to its direct stimulation of the autonomic nervous system.

The plant's alkaloids, particularly scopolamine and atropine, bind to muscarinic receptors in the heart, leading to an increased contraction force and heart rate.

This can cause a rapid and intense increase in heart rate, potentially resulting in cardiac arrhythmias and other cardiovascular disturbances.

2. Causes delirium immediately

Atropa bella-donna causes delirium immediately due to its potent effects on the central nervous system.

The plant's active compounds, including atropine and scopolamine, bind to receptors in the brain, leading to a disruption of normal neurotransmitter function. This results in altered perception, confusion, and hallucinations, which can progress to delirium.

The rapid onset of these symptoms is attributed to the high concentration of these alkaloids in the plant, making it essential to exercise caution when handling or ingesting Atropa bella-donna.

3. Produces hallucinations vividly

Atropa bella-donna produces hallucinations vividly due to its unique chemical composition.

The plant contains tropane alkaloids, specifically atropine and scopolamine, which have a profound effect on the central nervous system. When ingested, these compounds can alter perception, causing users to experience altered states of consciousness, distorted visual and auditory sensations, and a sense of detachment from reality.

As a result, individuals may exhibit hallucinations, often with intense vividness and detail.

4. Induces coma deeply

Atropa bella-donna induces coma deeply due to its potent neurotoxic properties.

When ingested, atropine and scopolamine, the active compounds found in the plant, bind to specific receptors in the brain and nervous system, disrupting normal functioning.

This can lead to a range of severe symptoms, including confusion, hallucinations, and ultimately, loss of consciousness, potentially inducing a deep coma.

5. Raises blood pressure sharply

Atropa bella-donna raises blood pressure sharply due to its stimulation of the sympathetic nervous system.

This causes an increase in heart rate and cardiac output, leading to a rapid rise in blood pressure.

Additionally, Atropa bella-donna's vasoconstrictive properties further contribute to this effect by constricting blood vessels and reducing blood flow to various organs.

6. Slows down breathing significantly

Atropa bella-donna slows down breathing significantly.

This side effect occurs due to the plant's ability to affect the autonomic nervous system, specifically the parasympathetic branch, which is responsible for regulating respiration.

The plant's alkaloids, particularly atropine and scopolamine, bind to muscarinic receptors in the brain and lungs, leading to a decrease in respiratory rate and depth.

7. Causes confusion severely

Atropa bella-donna causes confusion severely due to its ability to affect the brain's neurotransmitters, particularly acetylcholine.

This can lead to disorientation, hallucinations, and an altered state of consciousness.

The plant's alkaloids, such as scopolamine and atropine, bind to muscarinic receptors in the brain, disrupting normal cognitive function and causing confusion and dizziness.

8. Disrupts vision intensely

Atropa bella-donna disrupts vision intensely due to its ability to alter the brain's processing of visual information.

The alkaloids present in this plant, such as atropine and scopolamine, bind to receptors in the retina, causing a disruption in the normal functioning of the eye's visual pathways.

This can lead to distorted or blurred vision, as well as sensitivity to light, making it difficult for individuals to perceive their surroundings accurately.

9. Affects hearing profoundly

Atropa bella-donna affects hearing profoundly.

This occurs due to the plant's toxic compounds, particularly atropine and scopolamine, which can bind to receptors in the inner ear, disrupting normal auditory function.

The hallucinogenic properties of these compounds may also contribute to altered perception and distorted sound processing, further compromising hearing abilities.

10. Increases body temperature suddenly

Atropa bella-donna increases body temperature suddenly.

This is due to its ability to stimulate the sympathetic nervous system, causing an increase in heart rate and blood pressure.

The alkaloids present in Atropa bella-donna, such as atropine, scopolamine, and hyoscyamine, bind to acetylcholine receptors in the brain, leading to a sudden rise in body temperature.
