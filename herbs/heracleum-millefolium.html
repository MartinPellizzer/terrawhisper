What's the taxonomical classification of Heracleum millefolium?

Heracleum millefolium belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Magnoliidae and is further organized into the order Apiales. As a member of the family Apiaceae, it is categorized under the genus Heracleum, ultimately reaching its specific identification at the species level as millefolium.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Heracleum millefolium has large, deeply divided compound leaves characterized by numerous small, lanceolate leaflets that create a feathery or fern-like appearance. These leaves are arranged alternately along a thick, hollow, and often purplish-spotted stem that grows upright. The plant produces large, flat-topped umbels composed of many small, white, five-petaled flowers. The root system consists of a sturdy, woody taproot that allows the plant to persist in various soil types. Each individual leaf can reach a significant size, contributing to the plant's overall lush and spreading growth habit.
