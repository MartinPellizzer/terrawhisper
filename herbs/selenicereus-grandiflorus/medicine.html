What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Selenicereus Grandiflorus (Queen Of The Night)?

Selenicereus grandiflorus, also known as queen of the night, has health benefits such as reducing inflammation and promoting relaxation.

The herb's medicinal constituents include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its therapeutic properties. Traditionally, Selenicereus grandiflorus is used in teas, infusions, and tinctures to treat various ailments. However, excessive consumption may lead to gastrointestinal issues, such as nausea and diarrhea.

Precautions include consulting a healthcare professional before using the herb, especially for individuals with sensitive stomachs or allergies.

This article explains the health benefits, active constituents, medicinal preparations, possible side effects, and precautions related to Selenicereus grandiflorus.

What are the health benefits of Selenicereus grandiflorus?

Selenicereus grandiflorus has health benefits such as reducing stress and anxiety due to its calming effects.

Its flavonoids and saponins have been shown to possess antioxidant properties, which can help protect against cell damage and inflammation.

Additionally, the plant's leaves and stems contain phenolic compounds that may aid in treating respiratory issues, making it a valuable asset for traditional medicine and natural remedies.

What are the medicinal preparations of Selenicereus grandiflorus?

Selenicereus grandiflorus has different medicinal preparations, such as extracts, teas, and infusions.

The flower extract is used to treat digestive issues like diarrhea and dysentery, while the leaf extract is used to treat wounds, skin conditions, and inflammation. The tea made from the flowers and leaves is used to alleviate respiratory problems, fever, and coughs.

Meanwhile, the infusion of the plant's pulp is used to treat urinary tract infections and kidney stones.

What are the possible side effect of using Selenicereus grandiflorus improperly?

Improper use of Selenicereus grandiflorus increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as allergic reactions, skin irritation, respiratory issues, gastrointestinal disturbances, headaches, dizziness, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, stomach pain, and increased heart rate.

In severe cases, it may also lead to kidney or liver damage, interact with medications, cause hormonal imbalances, worsen pre-existing medical conditions, trigger seizures, and even result in coma or death.

Before using Selenicereus grandiflorus for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as consulting with a qualified healthcare professional to determine the appropriate dosage and potential interactions with other medications.

Additionally, ensure that the plant material is properly identified and free from contaminants or adulterants.
