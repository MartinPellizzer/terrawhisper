Red Onion (Allium Haemanthoides F. Majus)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Allium haemanthoides f. majus.

Red onion, scientifically known as Allium haemanthoides f. majus, is a medicinal herb widely recognized for its adaptogenic and nutritional properties. It is valued for its ability to support immune function, reduce inflammation, and promote cardiovascular health due to its high concentration of sulfur compounds and antioxidants. Traditionally used in various cultures for treating respiratory ailments, digestive issues, and as a natural remedy for colds and flu, it has a long history in herbal medicine. In modern wellness practices, it is incorporated into diets for its potential to lower blood pressure and improve gut health. Its distinctive sharp flavor and rare active compound, quercetin, make it a unique and versatile ingredient in both culinary and therapeutic applications.
