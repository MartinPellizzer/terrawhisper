Dioscorea hispida

What's the taxonomical classification of Dioscorea hispida?

Dioscorea hispida belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is placed in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Dioscoreales. Further classification identifies it as a member of the family Dioscoreaceae, specifically within the genus Dioscorea, with its unique identification as the species hispida.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dioscorea hispida has climbing stems that are cylindrical, fleshy, and covered in coarse, spreading hairs. These vines utilize twining stems to ascend through surrounding vegetation. The leaves are typically cordate or sagittate in shape and possess a distinctively rough, pubescent texture on their surfaces. Small, greenish or yellowish flowers emerge from the leaf axils in clusters. The plant produces tubers that serve as underground storage organs for nutrients.
