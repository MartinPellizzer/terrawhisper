What's the taxonomical classification of Fibraurea recisa?

Fibraurea recisa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and falls within the order Ranunculales. This plant is a member of the family Menispermaceae, specifically situated within the genus Fibraurea, and is identified by the species name recisa.

Fibraurea recisa has small, elliptical leaves that are arranged oppositely along a slender, woody stem.

These reproductive structures are small and often blend into the overall canopy of the shrub.

        What are the herbal preparations of Fibraurea recisa?

        Fibraurea recisa is prepared primarily as a concentrated liquid extract, a dried powdered decoction, or a topical ointment for medicinal application. The liquid extract is often processed through alcohol-based tinctures to ensure the stability of its active alkaloids.         What side-effects this plant can have?

        Fibraurea recisa can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, including nausea, vomiting, and acute abdominal cramping, due to its concentrated alkaloid content. These systemic reactions often occur shortly after accidental ingestion of the plant material. Some individuals may also experience localized skin irritation or contact dermatitis if the sap touches unprotected flesh. In high concentrations, the chemical compounds may lead to more serious neurological symptoms such as dizziness or disorientation. Careful avoidance of all plant parts is necessary to prevent these adverse physiological responses.
