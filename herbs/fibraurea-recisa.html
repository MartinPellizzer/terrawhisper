What are the pharmacological activities of Fibraurea recisa?

This plant has potent anti-apoptosis, antioxidant, antitumor, and cognitive-enhancing pharmacological activities. The antioxidant properties of Fibraurea recisa help neutralize harmful free radicals within the body. Its antitumor activity suggests a potential role in inhibiting the progression of various cancerous cells. Additionally, the plant exhibits anti-apoptosis effects that may protect healthy cells from premature programmed death. Finally, its cognitive-enhancing capabilities offer promising therapeutic possibilities for improving neurological functions.

As discussed in a study, the main active ingredient in the rattan stems of Fibraurea recisa Pierre is fibrauretine. This substance exhibits pharmacological activities related to antioxidation and anti-apoptosis in models of Alzheimer's disease induced by Aβ1-42. When combined with ginsenosides, it enhances spatial learning and memory abilities as measured by the Morris water maze test. The treatment reverses the increases in AChE, p-Tau, NO, MDA, and NT-proBNP while significantly increasing levels of CAT, SOD, ChAT, and GSH-Px. Furthermore, the combined treatment decreases the expression levels of apoptosis markers including Bax, NF-κBp65, cleaved caspase-3, and cleaved caspase-9, while increasing Bcl-2 expression in the hippocampus.

As discussed in a study, Fibraurea recisa contains a novel triterpenoid known as Fibrarecisin (1). This compound was isolated from the chloroform extract of the stem bark of Fibraurea recisa Pierre. Fibrarecisin (1) exhibited significant antitumoer activity in vitro against A-549 cancer cell. This specific activity was observed at 10 microM using both SRB and MTT methods.
