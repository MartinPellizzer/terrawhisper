Christia constricta

What's the taxonomical classification of Christia constricta?

Christia constricta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is situated in the order Fabales. It further falls under the family Fabaceae, which is a large group of flowering plants commonly known as legumes. Within this family, the plant is categorized under the genus Christia, with its specific identification being the species constricta.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Christia constricta can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested by humans or animals. The plant contains chemical compounds that may trigger contact dermatitis, resulting in redness, itching, or swelling upon skin contact. Some individuals may experience more severe hypersensitivity reactions depending on their personal sensitivity to the plant's sap. If consumed, the foliage might lead to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain due to its inherent toxicity. It is essential to handle this species with care to avoid these adverse physical reactions.
