Christia constricta

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Christia constricta has pinnate leaves composed of several pairs of small, ovate to elliptical leaflets that are often slightly asymmetrical at the base. The foliage exhibits a delicate, fern-like appearance due to the thin texture and intricate arrangement of these leaflets. The stems are slender and woody at the base, providing a structural framework for the spreading growth habit. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, typically appearing in shades of pink or white. The overall morphology is characterized by a fine-textured, bushy silhouette that makes it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

It thrives primarily in subtropical forest margins and coastal thickets where humidity is relatively high. The species is often found in well-drained soils along the edge of dense vegetation.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Christia constricta can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested by humans or animals. The plant contains chemical compounds that may trigger contact dermatitis, resulting in redness, itching, or swelling upon skin contact. Some individuals may experience more severe hypersensitivity reactions depending on their personal sensitivity to the plant's sap. If consumed, the foliage might lead to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain due to its inherent toxicity. It is essential to handle this species with care to avoid these adverse physical reactions.
