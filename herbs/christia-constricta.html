Christia constricta

What's the taxonomical classification of Christia constricta?

Christia constricta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is situated in the order Fabales. It further falls under the family Fabaceae, which is a large group of flowering plants commonly known as legumes. Within this family, the plant is categorized under the genus Christia, with its specific identification being the species constricta.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, and various inflammatory conditions. Local healers often prepare decoctions or poultices from its leaves to soothe irritation on the skin. It is frequently applied to minor cuts and abrasions to promote faster healing processes. Some traditional practices also utilize the plant to manage digestive discomforts or internal ailments. The specific application often depends on the preparation method and the severity of the symptom.
