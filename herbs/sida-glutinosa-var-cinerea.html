Velvetleaf (Sida Glutinosa Var. Cinerea)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Sida glutinosa var. cinerea.

Velvetleaf, scientifically known as Sida glutinosa var. cinerea, is a medicinal herb native to tropical regions, valued for its adaptogenic and anti-inflammatory properties. It is traditionally used to support immune function, reduce fever, and alleviate respiratory and digestive ailments due to its rich content of bioactive compounds like flavonoids and tannins. In traditional African and South Asian medicine, Velvetleaf has been employed to treat infections, wounds, and skin conditions, often prepared as a decoction or poultice. Modern wellness applications include its use in herbal formulations for stress relief and as a natural remedy for cold and flu symptoms. Notably, Velvetleaf is distinguished by its mucilaginous texture and earthy, slightly bitter flavor, and it has a long history of use in indigenous healing practices, making it a rare and valuable plant in both traditional and contemporary herbal medicine.
