Aspilia kotschyi

What's the taxonomical classification of Aspilia kotschyi?

Aspilia kotschyi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae and is specifically identified within the genus Aspilia, ultimately reaching its unique species designation as Aspilia kotschyi.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Aspilia kotschyi contains bioactive secondary metabolites such as flavonoids, tannins, saponins, and alkaloids that contribute to its medicinal properties. These chemical constituents are primarily utilized in traditional medicine to treat various ailments including skin infections and wounds. The presence of these compounds provides the plant with significant antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory activities. Researchers often study these specific phytochemicals to understand their potential for developing new therapeutic drugs. The efficacy of the plant's healing properties is directly linked to the concentration and synergy of these organic compounds.

What are the therapeutic actions of Aspilia kotschyi?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, stomach aches, and various inflammatory conditions. The leaves are frequently applied topically to promote the healing of cuts and sores due to their antimicrobial properties. When prepared as a decoction, it can be consumed to alleviate digestive discomfort and abdominal pain. Traditional practitioners also utilize the plant to manage symptoms associated with fever and malaria. Its medicinal value lies in its ability to combat bacterial and fungal pathogens.
