Aspilia kotschyi

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aspilia kotschyi has opposite, elliptical to oblong leaves with serrated margins and a pubescent surface texture. The stems are woody at the base and often feature a fine covering of hairs. Its inflorescences consist of small, yellow flower heads arranged in terminal or axillary clusters. The individual florets are tubular and lack ray flowers, which is characteristic of many species in its genus. These morphological traits help distinguish it from similar members of the Asteraceae family.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical regions of West and Central Africa, specifically occurring in countries such as Nigeria, Cameroon, and Gabon. It thrives primarily within the humid lowland forests and transitional woodland savannas of these equatorial zones. The species is often found in disturbed sites or along forest edges where sunlight can penetrate the canopy. Its distribution is closely tied to the specific rainfall patterns and soil compositions characteristic of the Guineo-Congolian phytogeographical region. What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause localized skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and potential gastrointestinal distress if ingested in significant quantities. While it is often used in traditional medicine for wound healing, the chemical compounds within its leaves may trigger hypersensitivity reactions in sensitive individuals. Contact with the sap or crushed foliage can lead to redness, itching, or swelling at the site of application. Furthermore, there is insufficient clinical data to rule out systemic toxicity if the plant is consumed improperly. Users should exercise caution and avoid direct skin contact unless they have first confirmed their tolerance to the species.
