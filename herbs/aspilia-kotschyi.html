What's the taxonomical classification of Aspilia kotschyi?

Aspilia kotschyi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae and is specifically identified within the genus Aspilia, ultimately reaching its unique species designation as Aspilia kotschyi.

What are the herbal preparations of Aspilia kotschyi?

Aspilia kotschyi is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a poultice depending on the specific ailment being treated. What side-effects this plant can have?

Aspilia kotschyi can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and potential gastrointestinal distress if ingested improperly. While often used in traditional medicine, the plant contains bioactive compounds that may trigger localized dermatitis upon contact with sensitive skin. Allergic individuals might experience redness, itching, or swelling in the areas where the plant touched them. Furthermore, consuming parts of the plant without precise dosage or preparation could lead to nausea or stomach discomfort. Careful handling is necessary to mitigate these adverse physiological responses.
