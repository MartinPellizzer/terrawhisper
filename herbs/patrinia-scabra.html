What's the taxonomical classification of Patrinia scabra?

Patriina scabra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic lineage through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, the plant is placed under the order Dipsacales. It is a member of the family Caprifoliaceae, specifically falling under the genus Patriina, with the specific epithet scabra designating its species identity.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Patriña scabra has a perennial growth habit characterized by erect stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 100 centimeters and feature a distinctly scabrous, or rough, texture. The leaves are primarily basal and alternate, displaying a cordate to ovate shape with serrated margins. As the plant matures, it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in terminal cymes. These blossoms are typically white or pale pink and possess five petals. The fruit consists of small, dry achenes that are characteristic of the Apiaceae family.

What are the pharmacological activities of Patrinia scabra?

This plant has analgesic, anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, antitumor, and antiviral pharmacological activities. Scientific studies demonstrate that its extracts can effectively reduce pain and mitigate swelling caused by inflammatory processes. The bioactive compounds within the plant also exhibit significant properties that inhibit the growth of various bacteria and viruses. Furthermore, research indicates that Patrinia scabra possesses specialized mechanisms capable of suppressing tumor cell proliferation. These diverse therapeutic qualities make it a subject of great interest in pharmacological research and drug development.

According to a study published by "BMC complementary medicine and therapies", Patrinia scabra Bunge is an herbal medicine used for the treatment of inflammatory diseases due to the significant role of iridoid glycosides. Through ultrasonic-microwave synergistic extraction, the content of total iridoid glycosides (PSI) can reach 81.42 ± 0.31 mg/g using 52% ethanol, a 1:18 g/mL material-to-liquid ratio, 610 W microwave power, and 45 min of extraction time. After macroporous resin column chromatography, the PSI content in 30% and 50% ethanol eluates increased to 497.65 mg/g and 506.90 mg/g, respectively. These 30% and 50% ethanol elution fractions were shown to relieve pain centrally and peripherally. Consequently, the plant exhibits significant analgesic and anti-inflammatory activities.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds patrisca brin F, isopatriscabrol, patriscabrol, villosol, and laricirisinol. These specific chemical constituents are primarily classified as neolignans found within the species. Researchers study these substances to understand their potential biological activities and therapeutic properties. The presence of these unique molecules distinguishes the chemical profile of Patrinia scabra from other related plants. Detailed analysis of these compounds helps in developing new pharmacological treatments.

According to a study published by "Phytomedicine : international journal of phytotherapy and phytopharmacology", the roots of Patrinia scabra have been used as a medicinal herb in Asia. The compound patriscabrin F is an active component from the roots of P. scabra that demonstrates potent inhibitory effects on LPS-induced nitric oxide production compared to other isolated iridoids. This compound inhibits the LPS-induced production of NO, PGE2, TNF-α, IL-1β, and IL-6 in both BMDMs and RAW 264.7 macrophages. Patriscabrin F downregulates LPS-induced iNOS, COX-2, TNF-α, IL-1β, and IL-6 at the transcriptional level. It further suppresses LPS-induced inflammation by decreasing p65 nuclear translocation, IκBα phosphorylation, and IKKα/β phosphorylation. Additionally, patriscabrin F attenuates AP-1 activity by inhibiting c-Fos phosphorylation and suppresses the phosphorylation of IRF3, JAK1/JAK2, and STAT1/STAT3.
