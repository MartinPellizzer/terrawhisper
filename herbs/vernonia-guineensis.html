Vernonia guineensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Vernonia guineensis?

Vernonia guineensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Asterales. It is a member of the family Asteraceae, which places it within the genus Vernonia, and is specifically identified by its species name, guineensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vernonia guineensis has an erect, perennial herbaceous structure characterized by woody stems, lanceolate leaves with serrated margins, and terminal heads of small, tubular purple or blue florets. The leaves are typically arranged oppositely along the stem and possess a distinctively textured surface. Its flowering heads are composed of numerous individual disc florets that emerge from a common involucral base. The plant often develops a branched architecture as it matures, increasing its surface area for floral display. Fine pubescence may be present on the stems and leaf undersides depending on the specific environmental conditions.
