What's the taxonomical classification of Vernonia chingiana?

Vernonia chingiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, the plant falls under the order Asterales and is a member of the family Asteraceae. Ultimately, its specific biological identity is defined by its placement within the genus Vernonia, with its unique species designation being chingiana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vernonia chingiana has erect, herbaceous stems that reach heights of up to 2 meters and are typically branched toward the upper portions. The leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem and feature an elliptical to lanceolate shape with serrated margins. Each leaf possesses a distinct petiole and a prominent midrib that facilitates nutrient transport throughout the foliage. The inflorescence consists of numerous small, tubular heads arranged in terminal corymbose cymes. These flower heads are characterized by a purplish or lavender color and are supported by an involucre of graduated bracts.
