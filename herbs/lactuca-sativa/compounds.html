Lettuce (Lactuca sativa) Compounds

Lettuce contains several primary active compounds, including flavonoids, which act as antioxidants, and phenolic acids, which have anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarin and tannins contribute to its bitter taste and potential health benefits. Essential oils and glycosides add to its medicinal profile, while terpenoids, saponins, alkaloids, and anthocyanins provide additional therapeutic effects.

Lettuce (Lactuca sativa) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are naturally occurring compounds found in Lettuce (Lactuca sativa). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant properties and may support cardiovascular health. These bioactive substances are present in various parts of the plant, including leaves and stems. Their presence enhances the medicinal potential of lettuce in herbal applications.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Lettuce (Lactuca sativa). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, which may support detoxification and reduce inflammation. This compound is naturally present in various parts of the lettuce plant, particularly in the leaves. Its presence plays a role in the traditional use of lettuce in herbal medicine.

3. Coumarin

Coumarin is a bioactive compound found in Lettuce (Lactuca sativa). It contributes to the plant's mild sedative and antispasmodic properties. Coumarin may support digestive health and has been studied for its potential calming effects. It is present in various parts of the lettuce plant.

4. Tannin

Tannin is a compound found in Lettuce (Lactuca sativa). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health. Tannins in lettuce are primarily derived from its leaves and are known for their potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

5. Essential oil

Essential oil from Lettuce (Lactuca sativa) contains volatile compounds like lactucin and lactucopicrin. These compounds have sedative and antispasmodic properties. They are used in herbal medicine to treat anxiety and digestive issues. The essential oil is extracted through steam distillation from fresh lettuce leaves.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Lettuce (Lactuca sativa). It contributes to the plant's mild sedative and anti-inflammatory properties. Glycosides in lettuce may support digestive health and reduce irritation. These compounds are naturally occurring and are part of the plant's therapeutic profile in herbal medicine.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Lettuce (Lactuca sativa). They contribute to the plant's medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects. These compounds are often responsible for the plant's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Lettuce (Lactuca sativa). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and detoxifying properties. Saponins may support digestive health and immune function when consumed as part of a balanced herbal regimen.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Lettuce (Lactuca sativa). It has been studied for its potential sedative and antispasmodic properties. While not the primary active component in traditional herbal use, alkaloids contribute to the plant's overall pharmacological profile. Their presence supports the plant's use in mild medicinal applications.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Lettuce (Lactuca sativa). It contributes to the plant's vibrant color and possesses antioxidant properties. In herbal medicine, anthocyanin supports detoxification and may aid in reducing inflammation and oxidative stress. Its presence in lettuce highlights the plant's potential therapeutic value.
