Morinda scabrifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Morinda scabrifolia?

Morinda scabrifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Gentianaales. This plant is a representative of the family Rubiaceae, a diverse group of flowering plants, and is specifically categorized under the genus Morinda. Ultimately, its unique biological identity is defined by its specific epithet, making its full scientific name Morinda scabrifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Morinda scabrifolia has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptic to oblong in shape and characterized by a distinctively scabrous, or rough, texture on both surfaces. The leaf margins are entire, and the venation is typically prominent, reflecting the plant's rugged appearance. Its stems are woody and often possess small prickles or bumps that contribute to its coarse feel. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into fleshy, multi-locular fruits. These fruits are often clustered and serve as a key morphological feature for identifying the species within the Rubiaceae family.
