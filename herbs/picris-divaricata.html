Picris divaricata

What's the taxonomical classification of Picris divaricata?

Picris divaricata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Asterales. It is further categorized under the family Asteraceae, which is a large group of flowering plants. Within this family, the plant is assigned to the genus Picris, and its specific identification is completed by the species name divaricata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Picris divaricata has erect, herbaceous stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 100 centimeters and feature basal leaves that are long, lanceolate, and often deeply lobed. The plant produces a branched inflorescence composed of multiple yellowish-white flower heads arranged in a corymbose manner. Each individual flower head contains numerous small, ligulate florets that are characteristic of the Asteraceae family. The leaves transition from large, spreading basal forms to smaller, sessile leaves along the upper portions of the stem. Fine, spreading hairs often cover the stems and foliage, providing a textured surface to the plant's anatomy.
