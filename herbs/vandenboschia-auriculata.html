Vandenboschia auriculata

What's the taxonomical classification of Vandenboschia auriculata?

Vandenboschia auriculata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further classified into the subclass Polypodiinae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Hymenophyllales and the family Hymenophyllaceae. Finally, the plant is placed within the genus Vandenboschia, with its specific designation being the species auriculata.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, inflammation, and various digestive ailments. The leaves and bark are frequently prepared into medicinal decoctions or poultices to soothe irritated skin surfaces. Some traditional practices employ the plant to address internal discomforts by managing stomach-related issues. Its chemical properties are believed to assist in the healing process of open cuts and abrasions. This species serves as a vital component in local herbal remedies for managing diverse physical maladies.
