Vandenboschia auriculata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vandenboschia auriculata has woody stems, simple leaves with distinct auriculate bases, and small, pale yellow to cream-colored flowers. The leaves are typically arranged oppositely along the branches and possess a leathery texture. Each leaf blade ends in an acute or obtuse apex and features a small lobe at the base where it meets the petiole. The inflorescences are often found in the leaf axils, displaying a delicate structure characteristic of the family. These morphological traits collectively allow the plant to thrive in its specific ecological niche.

      What are the herbal preparations of Vandenboschia auriculata?

      Vandenboschia auriculata is prepared as a decoction of the dried roots or as a topical poultice of crushed leaves. The root decoction is typically ingested to address internal ailments such as digestive distress or fever. For external skin conditions, the fresh leaves are masticated or crushed into a paste and applied directly to the affected area.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Vandenboschia auriculata can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and localized swelling if the plant comes into direct contact with human tissue. The chemical compounds present in its leaves and stems may trigger redness or itching upon contact. Some individuals might experience more severe systemic reactions if the sap is ingested accidentally. Monitoring for signs of hypersensitivity is important when handling this species in a garden setting. Protective clothing should be worn to mitigate the risk of accidental exposure.
