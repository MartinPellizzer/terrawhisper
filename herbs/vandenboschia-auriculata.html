Vandenboschia auriculata

What's the taxonomical classification of Vandenboschia auriculata?

Vandenboschia auriculata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further classified into the subclass Polypodiinae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Hymenophyllales and the family Hymenophyllaceae. Finally, the plant is placed within the genus Vandenboschia, with its specific designation being the species auriculata.

      What are the herbal preparations of Vandenboschia auriculata?

      Vandenboschia auriculata is prepared as a decoction of the dried roots or as a topical poultice of crushed leaves. The root decoction is typically ingested to address internal ailments such as digestive distress or fever. For external skin conditions, the fresh leaves are masticated or crushed into a paste and applied directly to the affected area. Some traditional practitioners also use a concentrated infusion of the plant to serve as a medicinal wash. These diverse methods allow the bioactive compounds to be utilized for both systemic and localized healing.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Vandenboschia auriculata can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and localized swelling if the plant comes into direct contact with human tissue. The chemical compounds present in its leaves and stems may trigger redness or itching upon contact. Some individuals might experience more severe systemic reactions if the sap is ingested accidentally. Monitoring for signs of hypersensitivity is important when handling this species in a garden setting. Protective clothing should be worn to mitigate the risk of accidental exposure.
