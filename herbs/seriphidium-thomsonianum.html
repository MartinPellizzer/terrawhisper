Seriphidium thomsonianum

What's the taxonomical classification of Seriphidium thomsonianum?

Seriphidium thomsonianum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae, which places it within the genus Seriphidium, and is specifically identified by the species name thomsonianum.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant, commonly known as the Polka Dot Plant, requires bright, indirect sunlight, consistently moist well-draining soil, and high humidity levels to thrive.

It grows best in a temperature range between 65 and 75 degrees Fahrenheit. If the soil dries out completely, the foliage may wilt quickly, though it often recovers after thorough watering. Providing a pebble tray or a humidifier helps maintain the moisture necessary for its colorful leaves.

Overwatering should be avoided to prevent root rot in its delicate root system.
