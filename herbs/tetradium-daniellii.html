Tetradium daniellii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tetradium daniellii has opposite, elliptic to ovate leaves that are typically 3 to 7 centimeters long and possess serrated margins with acute tips. The plant features a woody stem structure characteristic of small shrubs or subshrubs found in montane environments. Its flowers are small and relatively inconspicuous, often emerging from the leaf axils in clusters. The reproductive organs consist of delicate stamens and a central pistil that facilitate pollination in its specific ecological niche.
