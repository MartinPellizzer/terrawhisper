Rubus xanthocarpus

What's the taxonomical classification of Rubus xanthocarpus?

Rubus xanthocarpus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Rosales and the family Rosaceae, ultimately placing it within the genus Rubus. The specific identification of this plant is completed by its species name, xanthocarpus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rubus xanthocarpus has woody, arching stems armed with sharp, recurved prickles and leaves that are typically odd-pinnate with serrated margins. The plant exhibits a sprawling or climbing habit facilitated by its robust, thorny canes. Each leaf consists of several leaflets that vary in shape from ovate to elliptical with distinctively pointed tips. The flowers are generally small and white, emerging from the leaf axils during the blooming season. Eventually, these flowers develop into aggregate fruits known as drupelets, which change color as they ripen.
