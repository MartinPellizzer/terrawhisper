What's the taxonomical classification of Cogniauxia podolaena?

Cogniauxia podolaena belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It is a representative of the family Cucurbitaceae, falling under the genus Cogniauxia, with the specific species designation podolaena.

What are the pharmacological activities of Cogniauxia podolaena?

This plant has demonstrated potent antimalarial and antiplasmodial pharmacological activities through its bioactive secondary metabolites. Researchers have identified specific chemical constituents within Cogniauxia podolaena that effectively inhibit the growth of Plasmodium parasites. These properties make the species a significant subject of interest for developing new treatments against tropical blood diseases. Studies suggest that the plant's extract works by disrupting the lifecycle of various protozoan pathogens. Consequently, its medicinal potential remains a key focus in the search for natural antiprotozoal agents.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", the ethanolic and dichloromethane extracts of the Cogniauxia podolaena root were found to be moderately active. This activity was measured with IC(50) values ranging between 10 microg/ml and 50 microg/ml. These results provide scientific support for the traditional use of the plant as an antimalarial agent. The investigation was part of a study involving 66 extracts from 18 plants used in Congo Brazzaville traditional medicine.
