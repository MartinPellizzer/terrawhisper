What is Ericameria linearis?

Ericameria linearis is a species of flowering plant that belongs to the family Asteraceae.

It is a shrub or subshrub that grows up to 2 meters in height, with a woody base and green, linear or lance-shaped leaves that are 1-5 cm in length. The plant produces yellow or orange, daisy-like flowers with 10-20 ray florets and a cluster of disk florets in the center, which are 1-2 cm in diameter and have a prominent yellow or orange disc. The flowers are borne in a terminal, branched inflorescence, and the plant produces small, achenes that are 3-4 mm in length and have a pappus of 50-60 white, hairy scales.

The plant is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

The main herbal preparations of Ericameria linearis are tea, tincture, infusion, infused oil, and salve.

The tea is prepared by steeping the leaves and flowers in hot water to make a decoction, which is traditionally used to treat colds and flu. The tincture is created by soaking the plant in a solvent, usually ethanol or glycerin, to extract the bioactive compounds, primarily responsible for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Infused oil is produced by steeping the plant in a carrier oil, such as olive or coconut oil, to create a topical treatment for skin issues like wounds and eczema.

The infused oil and tincture are then combined with a base, such as beeswax, to create a salve that can be applied directly to affected areas.
