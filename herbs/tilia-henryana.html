Tilia henryana

What's the taxonomical classification of Tilia henryana?

Tilia henryana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the hierarchy, the plant is situated in the order Malvales and the family Malvaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Tilia and the specific species designation henryana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tilia henryana has heart-shaped leaves that are characterized by their distinctly asymmetrical bases and deeply serrated margins. The foliage typically displays a deep green color on the upper surface while the underside remains a lighter shade. Its woody stems develop a rugged bark texture as the tree reaches maturity. During the flowering season, the plant produces clusters of pale yellowish-white blossoms that emit a sweet fragrance. These flowers are accompanied by specialized leaf-like bracts that facilitate wind dispersal of the seeds.
