Tilia henryana

What's the taxonomical classification of Tilia henryana?

Tilia henryana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the hierarchy, the plant is situated in the order Malvales and the family Malvaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Tilia and the specific species designation henryana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tilia henryana has heart-shaped leaves that are characterized by their distinctly asymmetrical bases and deeply serrated margins.

The foliage typically displays a deep green color on the upper surface while the underside remains a lighter shade. Its woody stems develop a rugged bark texture as the tree reaches maturity. During the flowering season, the plant produces clusters of pale yellowish-white blossoms that emit a sweet fragrance.

These flowers are accompanied by specialized leaf-like bracts that facilitate wind dispersal of the seeds.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Tilia henryana contains medicinal properties primarily concentrated in its fragrant flowers and the surrounding leafy bracts. These floral parts are harvested to prepare infusions that act as mild sedatives and antispasmodics. The chemical compounds found within the blossoms help to soothe the nervous system and reduce anxiety. Additionally, the tea brewed from these components is frequently used to alleviate symptoms of common colds and fevers. By utilizing these specific botanical structures, practitioners can access the plant's natural calming and restorative effects.

    What are the molecular targets of this plant?

    Tilia henryana targets GABAergic receptors, cyclooxygenase enzymes, and various muscarinic acetylcholine receptors to produce its sedative and anti-inflammatory effects. The presence of bioactive flavonoids and essential oils within the plant allows these compounds to modulate neurotransmitter activity in the central nervous system. By binding to GABA receptors, the plant's constituents can induce a calming effect that reduces anxiety and promotes sleep. Additionally, the inhibition of cyclooxygenase enzymes helps suppress the production of pro-inflammatory mediators like prostaglandins. These multi-target interactions contribute to the plant's overall profile as a therapeutic agent for stress and inflammation.

        What are the herbal preparations of Tilia henryana?

        Tilia henryana is prepared as an infusion, decoction, tincture, or topical salve using the dried flowers and leaves. The most common method involves steeping the fragrant blossoms in hot water to create a soothing tea. For concentrated therapeutic benefits, the plant material can be macerated in alcohol to produce a potent tincture. These liquid extracts are often diluted in water or mixed into other beverages for consumption. Additionally, the essential oils or infused oils from the plant can be incorporated into ointments for external application.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Tilia henryana can cause allergic reactions such as skin rashes, itching, or respiratory distress in individuals sensitive to its pollen or chemical compounds. While most interactions are mild, some people may experience gastrointestinal upset if ingested in significant quantities. It is important to monitor for any signs of contact dermatitis after handling the leaves or flowers. People with known allergies to the Linden tree family should exercise extreme caution when near this species. Always consult a medical professional if any adverse physical symptoms occur following exposure.
