What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Potentiella fragarioides has a creeping rhizomatous habit with trifoliate leaves that consist of three leaflets, each bearing three to five serrated sublobes. The leaf blades are typically dark green and possess a texture that is somewhat rugose or wrinkled on the upper surface. These leaves are supported by long petioles that emerge from a central creeping stem. Small, five-petaled yellow flowers grow on upright stalks and emerge during the blooming season. The plant also produces small, aggregate fruits that develop from the floral structure after pollination.
