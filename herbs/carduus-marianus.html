Carduus marianus

What's the taxonomical classification of Carduus marianus?

Carduus marianus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Asterales and falls within the family Asteraceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its genus, Carduus, and its specific species designation, marianus.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Carduus marianus contains silymarin, a complex mixture of flavonolignans that serves as its primary therapeutic agent. The most significant component within this group is silybin, which possesses potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds work by stabilizing hepatocyte membranes to prevent toxins from entering liver cells. Additionally, silymarin stimulates protein synthesis to facilitate the regeneration of damaged liver tissue. This unique chemical profile makes the plant a widely studied natural treatment for various liver pathologies.

What are the therapeutic actions of Carduus marianus?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat chronic liver diseases, including cirrhosis, hepatitis, and fatty liver disease, by utilizing its active compound silymarin. Silymarin works by protecting liver cells from toxins and promoting the regeneration of damaged tissue. It is also frequently employed to manage complications from jaundice and alcohol-induced liver damage. Some clinical studies suggest it may help regulate blood sugar levels in individuals with metabolic issues. Practitioners often prescribe it to support detoxification processes within the digestive system.
