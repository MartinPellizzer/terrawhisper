What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Oplopanax elatus targets inflammatory signaling proteins such as cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2), inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS), and various tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-alpha) pathways. These interactions occur primarily through its bioactive sesquiterpene lactones and polysaccharides which inhibit the nuclear factor-kappa B (NF-κB) signaling cascade. By suppressing these specific enzyme activities, the plant extracts effectively reduce the production of pro-inflammatory mediators like prostaglandins and nitric oxide. Additionally, certain components demonstrate affinity for apoptotic regulatory proteins, which helps modulate programmed cell death in cancer cell lines. This multi-target approach underlies its traditional uses in treating inflammation and various neoplastic conditions.

What are the herbal preparations of Oplopanax elatus?

Oplopanax elaatus is prepared primarily as a decoction made by boiling the dried rhizomes in water or as a medicinal tincture using alcohol as a solvent. Because specific toxicological profiles are not fully established, caution is necessary when using this species.
