Oplopanax elatus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The specific classification of this plant is defined by the genus Oplopanax and the species elatus.

What are the pharmacological activities of Oplopanax elatus?

This plant has antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, anti-tumor, and anti-diabetic pharmacological activities. Its high concentration of bioactive compounds like sesquiterpene lactones contributes significantly to its ability to scavenge free radicals. These chemical properties help reduce oxidative stress and protect cells from damage within biological systems. Research also suggests that its extracts can modulate immune responses and inhibit the growth of certain cancer cells. Furthermore, its ability to regulate blood glucose levels makes it a subject of interest for managing metabolic disorders.

According to a study published by "Journal of biotechnology", Oplopanax elatus adventitious roots exhibit high antioxidant activity when treated with 100 μM salicylic acid for 4 days starting on day 35. Under these conditions, total flavonoid and phenolic contents reached 387 rutin mg/g DW and 128 gallic acid mg/g DW, respectively, which were significantly (p < 0.05) higher than the control. The treatment also greatly increased DPPH scavenging, ABTS+ scavenging, and Fe2+ chelating rates. The EC50 values for these activities were 0.0117, 0.61, and 3.34 mg/L, respectively. These results demonstrate the plant's increased antioxidant enzyme activity and metabolite synthesis through elicitation.

These specific chemical constituents are primarily categorized as triterpenoids and glycosides found within the species.
