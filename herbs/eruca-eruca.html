What is Eruca eruca?

Eruca eruca is a species of plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is an annual or biennial herb, native to the Mediterranean region. The plant has a taproot and a rosette of leaves, which are pinnately lobed and have a long petiole. The leaves are 10-30 cm long, with 1-3 pairs of lateral leaflets, and are covered in small, glandular hairs.

The inflorescence is a terminal raceme, bearing white or pale purple flowers with 4 petals and 6 stamens.

What are the therapeutic properties this herb?

The main therapeutic properties of Eruca eruca are anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, antimicrobial, and anticancer.

The anti-inflammatory properties of Eruca eruca are attributed to the presence of isothiocyanates, a type of glucosinolate that has been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes. Additionally, the antimicrobial properties of Eruca eruca are attributed to the presence of volatile oils, particularly allyl isothiocyanate, which has been shown to inhibit the growth of various microorganisms.

The anticancer properties of this plant are thought to be due to the presence of saponins, which have been found to induce apoptosis and inhibit the proliferation of cancer cells.
