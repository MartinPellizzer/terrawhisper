What to know about Tinospora cordifolia (giloy) before using it medicinally

Giloy, scientifically known as Tinospora cordifolia, is a revered herb in Ayurvedic medicine that has been extensively researched for its impressive health benefits.

Its unique combination of anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antibacterial properties makes it an effective natural remedy for alleviating various health issues, from digestive problems to respiratory disorders. In culinary practices, giloy is often used as a spice or ingredient in traditional Indian dishes, adding flavor and aroma to curries, soups, and stews. As a horticultural plant, giloy is relatively easy to grow and maintain, requiring moderate temperatures and well-drained soil.

With its botanical name dating back to the 18th century, giloy has been mentioned in ancient Hindu scriptures, such as the Charaka Samhita, where it is described as an effective treatment for various ailments.
