What to know about Tinospora cordifolia before using it medicinally

Tinospora cordifolia, commonly known as amrita, is a medicinal herb extensively used in traditional Indian and Ayurvedic medicine to treat a range of health conditions, including diabetes, liver and kidney disorders, fever, and inflammation.

The herb has been found to possess several health benefits, including anti-diabetic, anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and immunomodulatory effects. Its therapeutic properties include anti-bacterial, anti-fungal, and anti-viral activities, which contribute to its ability to combat infections and diseases. Bioactive compounds present in Tinospora cordifolia include alkaloids, glycosides, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The herb is often prepared as a decoction or a powder, and is commonly used in traditional medicine to make a drink called "amrit," which is believed to promote overall health and well-being.
