Botanical Identification of Tansy (Tanacetum vulgare)

To identify Tansy (Tanacetum vulgare) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Tansy, or Tanacetum vulgare, belongs to the kingdom Plantae. Its family is Compositae, also known as Asteraceae. The genus is Tanacetum, and the species is Tanacetum vulgare. The division, class, and order are not specified in the provided data.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Tansy is Tanacetum vulgare, first described by Carl von Linné in 1753. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is also known by several synonyms, including Chrysanthemum vulgare and Tanacetum bessarabicum. Common names like Tansy, Golden Button, and Common Tansy reflect its widespread recognition across different regions.

Morphological Characteristics

Tansy is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively. It has erect, branching stems that are hairy and often reddish-brown. The leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and have a strong, camphor-like scent. The inflorescence consists of dense, yellow button-like flower heads, each containing small, tubular florets. The fruit is a small, dry achene with a pappus, enabling wind dispersal.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Tansy can be distinguished by its distinctive yellow button-like flower head surrounded by yellow ray florets, and its finely divided, fern-like leaves. It has a strong aromatic scent and is often found in moist, sunny areas. It is commonly confused with other yellow composites but lacks the fuzzy stems and different leaf shapes of similar species.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Tansy is native to Europe and western Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with full sun. It prefers moist, well-drained soils. It typically grows at low to mid-elevation areas.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Tansy blooms from June to August, producing yellow button-like flowers. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early autumn. For identification purposes, harvest timing is critical during the flowering period when the plant is most easily recognized.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Tansy (Tanacetum vulgare) is closely related to other members of the Compositae family, particularly species within the genus Tanacetum. It shares key morphological and genetic traits with other members of its family, such as daisy and chamomile, which are also part of the Asteraceae family. Its phylogenetic position reflects its evolutionary ties to these related plants.
