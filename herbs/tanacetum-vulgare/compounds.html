Tansy (Tanacetum vulgare) Compounds

Tansy contains several primary compounds, including essential oil, flavonoids, and coumarin. These compounds contribute to its medicinal properties, such as anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Other notable constituents include terpenoids, phenolic acids, and tannins, which support its use in herbal medicine.

Tansy (Tanacetum vulgare) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Tansy (Tanacetum vulgare) is a key active compound, containing compounds like thujone and camphor. It has potent insecticidal and antifungal properties, making it historically used in herbal medicine for pest control and treating fungal infections.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Tansy (Tanacetum vulgare) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help reduce oxidative stress and support immune function. They are found in various parts of the plant, including leaves and flowers. Flavonoids may also enhance the plant's traditional use in treating digestive and respiratory ailments.

3. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Tansy (Tanacetum vulgare). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, including antispasmodic and anticoagulant effects. Coumarin is also responsible for the characteristic aroma of Tansy and plays a role in its traditional use for digestive and respiratory ailments.

4. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a key group of active compounds in Tansy (Tanacetum vulgare). They contribute to the plant's bitter taste and have antimicrobial and antifungal properties. These compounds are responsible for many of Tansy's traditional medicinal uses, including treating digestive issues and parasitic infections.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Tansy (Tanacetum vulgare). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antifungal properties. This compound is commonly found in the essential oils and extracts of Tansy. It plays a role in the plant's traditional use for treating digestive and skin conditions.

6. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Tansy (Tanacetum vulgare). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health. Tannins in Tansy also have antimicrobial effects, making the plant useful in traditional herbal medicine for treating infections and inflammation.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Tansy (Tanacetum vulgare). It contributes to the plant's bitter taste and has potential antimicrobial properties. Glycosides in Tansy may support digestive health and have traditionally been used in herbal medicine for their cleansing effects.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Tansy (Tanacetum vulgare). It contributes to the plant's bitter taste and has mild detoxifying properties. Saponins may support liver function and aid in the elimination of toxins from the body.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloids in Tansy (Tanacetum vulgare) are a class of nitrogen-containing compounds with potential pharmacological effects. These alkaloids may contribute to the plant's traditional use in digestive and nervous system support. However, their specific roles and mechanisms are less well understood compared to other active constituents in Tansy.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a pigment found in Tansy (Tanacetum vulgare). It contributes to the plant's characteristic blue and purple hues. While not the primary medicinal component, anthocyanins may support antioxidant activity in the plant. Their presence adds to the complex biochemical profile of Tansy.
