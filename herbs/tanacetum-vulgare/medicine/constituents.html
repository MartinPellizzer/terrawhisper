10 Medicinal Constituents Of Tanacetum Vulgare (Tansy)

Tanacetum vulgare has active constituents such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and volatile oils.

These compounds contribute to its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antifungal activities. The constituents in tansy can help alleviate symptoms of digestive issues like bloating and flatulence by reducing gas production and soothing the stomach lining. Additionally, the antimicrobial properties can aid in wound healing and infections, making tansy a natural remedy for various health concerns.

By incorporating tansy into one's diet or using it as an herbal supplement, individuals may experience improved digestion and overall well-being.

1. Lactucarium

Tanacetum vulgare lactucarium is a natural resin extracted from the flowering heads of Tanacetum vulgare plants.

It has been used in traditional medicine for centuries to treat various ailments such as insomnia, anxiety, and indigestion.

The lactucarium contains sesquiterpene lactones, which are believed to have sedative and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a popular ingredient in herbal remedies and teas.

2. Azulene

Tanacetum vulgare azulene is a natural compound derived from the plant Tansy (Tanacetum vulgare).

Azulene has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Its name comes from the blue color it exhibits when isolated, which is rare in nature.

This unique property makes azulene a promising ingredient in skincare products, with potential benefits including reduced fine lines, improved skin elasticity, and enhanced overall skin health.

3. Cinerol

Tanacetum vulgare cinerol is a type of bioactive compound extracted from the leaves and flowers of the common tansy plant (Tanacetum vulgare).
