What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Tanacetum Vulgare (Tansy)?

Tanacetum vulgare, also known as tansy, has health benefits such as reducing fever and relieving anxiety.

Its medicinal constituents include the terpene camphor, sesquiterpene lactones, and flavonoids, which contribute to its therapeutic properties. Infusions, teas, and essential oils are the primary preparations of this herb. However, excessive consumption of tansy can lead to side effects such as vomiting, diarrhea, and seizures.

Pregnant women should use this herb under medical supervision due to its potential to cause uterine contractions.

This article explains the health benefits, active constituents, medicinal preparations, possible side effects, and precautions related to Tanacetum vulgare.

What are the health benefits of Tanacetum vulgare?

Tanacetum vulgare has health benefits such as anti-inflammatory properties, which can help alleviate symptoms of arthritis and other inflammatory conditions.

Its essential oils have been shown to possess antioxidant activity, protecting the body from free radical damage and potentially reducing the risk of chronic diseases like cancer and cardiovascular disease.

Additionally, tansy has been used traditionally to treat digestive issues, such as indigestion and flatulence, due to its carminative properties.

What are the active constituents of Tanacetum vulgare?

The health benefits of Tanacetum vulgare comes from its active constituents, such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenes.

Flavonoids like quercetin and kaempferol exhibit anti-inflammatory properties, while phenolic acids like caffeic acid have antimicrobial and antioxidant effects. Terpenes, including camphor and borneol, possess analgesic and anti-inflammatory activities.

These constituents work together to provide relief from various health issues, including digestive problems, respiratory issues, and skin conditions.

What are the medicinal preparations of Tanacetum vulgare?

Tanacetum vulgare has different medicinal preparations, such as dried flowers and leaves, essential oil, and infusions.

The dried flowers and leaves are used to make teas, which help to alleviate symptoms of indigestion, flatulence, and menstrual cramps. The essential oil is employed in aromatherapy to treat digestive issues, relieve stress, and repel insects. Infusions made from the plant's parts can be applied topically to treat skin irritations, wounds, and minor burns.

Additionally, tansy extracts are used as a natural insecticide and repellent.

What are the possible side effect of using Tanacetum vulgare improperly?

Improper use of Tanacetum vulgare increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as stomach upset, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal pain.

It may also cause dizziness, headache, and fatigue. In severe cases, it can lead to liver damage, kidney problems, seizures, and even coma.

Additionally, tansy use has been linked to allergic reactions, skin rashes, and eye irritation. In rare instances, it may cause anaphylaxis, a life-threatening allergic reaction.

Furthermore, excessive consumption of tansy tea may result in insomnia, anxiety, and irritability.

What precautions to take when using Tanacetum vulgare medicinally?

Before using Tanacetum vulgare for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as consulting with a qualified healthcare professional to determine the appropriate dosage and method of preparation.

Additionally, pregnant women, breastfeeding mothers, and individuals with liver or kidney issues should avoid using tansy altogether, as it can be toxic in large quantities.

Furthermore, tansy may interact with certain medications, including anticoagulants, diabetes medications, and blood thinners, so caution is advised when combining it with other therapies.
