Tansy (Tanacetum vulgare) Uses

Tansy is traditionally used to treat respiratory conditions such as cough, asthma, and bronchitis. It may help alleviate symptoms of sinusitis, colds, and influenza by reducing inflammation and supporting immune function. It is also used for allergic rhinitis and tonsillitis, though its effectiveness for these conditions requires further scientific validation.

Tansy (Tanacetum vulgare) Monograph

1. Cough

Tansy has been traditionally used to alleviate coughs due to its expectorant properties. It helps loosen mucus and soothe irritated airways. Often prepared as a tea, it is valued for its mild antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects. However, it should be used cautiously due to its potential toxicity in high doses.

2. Asthma

Tansy has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in cases of asthma. It is believed to help reduce bronchial inflammation and ease breathing through its antispasmodic and expectorant properties. However, it should be used with caution and under professional guidance due to its potent nature.

3. Sinusitis

Tansy has been traditionally used to treat sinusitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion and clear mucus, supporting respiratory health. Often prepared as a steam inhalation or nasal spray, it provides relief from sinus pressure and infection symptoms.

4. Cold symptoms

Tansy is traditionally used to alleviate cold symptoms due to its antispasmodic and expectorant properties. It helps reduce congestion and soothe respiratory discomfort. Often prepared as a tea, it supports clearing mucus and easing coughing. Its warming effects may also help relieve chest tightness associated with colds.

5. Respiratory infections

Tansy is used to support the treatment of respiratory infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce symptoms like coughing and congestion, often prepared as a tea or tincture. Its active compounds may inhibit bacterial and viral growth in the respiratory tract. However, it should be used with caution due to potential toxicity.

6. Tonsillitis

Tansy has been traditionally used to treat tonsillitis due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce throat pain and swelling, often through gargling with its infused water. Its use is supported by historical herbal practices and is considered safe when prepared properly.

7. Common cold

Tansy has been traditionally used to support the treatment of the common cold due to its antimicrobial and antiseptic properties. It may help alleviate symptoms such as congestion and sore throat when used as a tea or gargle. However, it should not replace professional medical advice, especially for severe or persistent cases.

8. Bronchitis

Tansy has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in cases of bronchitis. Its expectorant properties help loosen mucus, while its anti-inflammatory effects may reduce bronchial irritation. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to alleviate coughing and ease breathing. However, it should be used with caution due to its potential toxicity if misused.

9. Influenza

Tansy has been historically used to support respiratory health, particularly in cases of influenza. Its antimicrobial and expectorant properties may help alleviate symptoms such as coughing and congestion. However, it is important to note that it should not replace conventional medical treatment for influenza. Always consult a healthcare professional before use.

10. Allergic rhinitis

Tansy has been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis due to its antihistamine properties. It may help reduce nasal congestion and inflammation, though its efficacy varies. Consult a qualified herbalist before use, as it can be potent and interact with medications.
