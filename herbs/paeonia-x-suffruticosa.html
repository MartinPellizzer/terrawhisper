Paeonia × suffruticosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Paeonia × suffruticosa?

Paeonia × suffruticosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. It is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, following the biological order Saxifragales. Within this hierarchy, the plant is a member of the family Paeoniaceae and falls under the genus Paeonia, specifically identified by the species name suffruticosa.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Paeonia × suffruticosa contains high concentrations of monoterpene glycosides, specifically paeoniflorin, which provides its primary therapeutic properties. These bioactive compounds are often studied for their ability to reduce inflammation and modulate the immune system. The plant also features various phenolic compounds and flavonoids that contribute to its antioxidant capabilities. Some research suggests that these chemical constituents may offer neuroprotective and analgesic effects in pharmacological applications. These diverse secondary metabolites make the species a subject of interest in traditional and modern herbal medicine.

What are the therapeutic actions of Paeonia × suffruticosa?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat menstrual disorders, dysmenorrhea, abdominal pain, and various inflammatory conditions. Its medicinal properties are primarily derived from the bioactive compounds found in the roots. Practitioners often utilize it to regulate blood flow and alleviate severe cramping during cycles. Additionally, the plant serves as a natural remedy for managing pain associated with internal organ dysfunction. These therapeutic effects are attributed to its ability to calm the nervous system and reduce swelling.
