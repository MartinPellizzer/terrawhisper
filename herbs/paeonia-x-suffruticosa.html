Tree Peony (Paeonia x suffruticosa)

Tree Peony (Paeonia x suffruticosa) is a member of the Rosaceae family, native to China, East Asia, and Mongolia. Traditionally, its roots, rhizomes, and flowers have been used for decoctions, infusions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, tonic, and sedative actions, and has a long history of use in traditional chinese medicine, european herbal medicine, and japanese kampo medicine.

Perennial herbaceous plant.

Leaves
Ovate to elliptic leaves with upper surface glabrous and dark green, lower surface pale green with prominent midvein and stomatal bands.
Flowers
Inflorescences consisting of solitary flowers with actinomorphic symmetry, five to seven overlapping petals in shades of pink, red, and white, and a central disk of yellow stamens surrounded by a ring of yellow to orange carpels.
Stems
Terracotta-colored bark, erect growth habit, woody structure, alternate branching pattern, glabrous surface, pith chamber present.
