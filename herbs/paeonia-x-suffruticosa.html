Paeonia × suffruticosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Paeonia × suffruticosa?

Paeonia × suffruticosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. It is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, following the biological order Saxifragales. Within this hierarchy, the plant is a member of the family Paeoniaceae and falls under the genus Paeonia, specifically identified by the species name suffruticosa.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Paeonia × suffruticosa contains medicinal compounds primarily concentrated in the dried roots and rhizomes of the plant. These underground structures are harvested and processed to extract active substances like paeoniflorin, which is valued for its pharmacological properties. While the roots are the main focus for therapeutic use, the seeds may also be utilized in specific traditional applications. Practitioners often focus on the root bark or the fleshy core of the rhizome to achieve desired chemical profiles. These components are frequently studied for their potential effects on inflammation and various physiological processes.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse array of medicinal compounds including paeoniflorin, paeonol, montanine, albiflorin, flavonoids, terpenoids, and phenolic acids.

These chemical constituents contribute to its significant biological activities such as anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. The presence of specific monoterpene glycosides like paeoniflorin is particularly noted for its neuroprotective properties. Additionally, the combination of various flavonoids and phenolic acids supports the plant's overall therapeutic potential in traditional medicine.

Through the interaction of these terpenoids and other secondary metabolites, the plant exhibits complex pharmacological profiles.

The primary medicinal compounds of this plant are shown in the list below.

  • Paeoniflorin
  • Paeonol
  • Montanine
  • Albiflorin
  • Flavonoids
  • Terpenoids
  • Phenolic acids

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Paeonia × suffruticosa targets various inflammatory signaling proteins including cyclooxygenase-2, inducible nitric oxide synthase, and tumor necrosis factor-alpha to exert its medicinal effects. The plant's bioactive compounds, such as paeoniflorin, modulate the activity of nuclear factor-kappa B to suppress excessive immune responses. These constituents also interact with various mitogen-activated protein kinases to regulate cellular stress and apoptosis pathways. Furthermore, the extracts influence the expression of cytokines and chemokines to reduce systemic inflammation in various physiological models. This multi-target approach allows the plant to provide broad pharmacological benefits ranging from neuroprotection to anti-inflammatory action.

What are the therapeutic actions of Paeonia × suffruticosa?

Paeonia × suffruticosa has therapeutic properties primarily derived from its high concentration of paeoniflorin, which functions as a potent anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antispasmodic agent. This compound works by modulating neurotransmitters and reducing oxidative stress within cellular structures. Its pharmacological effects extend to regulating menstrual disorders and alleviating symptoms of uterine contractions through smooth muscle relaxation. Additionally, the plant exhibits neuroprotective qualities that may help mitigate damage caused by inflammatory processes in the central nervous system. Research also suggests its potential utility in stabilizing cardiovascular functions and managing mild sedative needs.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat menstrual disorders, dysmenorrhea, abdominal pain, and various inflammatory conditions. Its medicinal properties are primarily derived from the bioactive compounds found in the roots. Practitioners often utilize it to regulate blood flow and alleviate severe cramping during cycles. Additionally, the plant serves as a natural remedy for managing pain associated with internal organ dysfunction. These therapeutic effects are attributed to its ability to calm the nervous system and reduce swelling.

What are the herbal preparations of Paeonia × suffruticosa?

Paeonia × suffruticosa is prepared as decoctions, tinctures, powders, or topical pastes using the dried roots of the plant. The roots are typically sliced and simmered in water to create a concentrated medicinal tea. Tinctures are often made by soaking the root material in alcohol to extract specific active compounds. In some traditional practices, the pulverized root is mixed with oils or water to form a paste for external application to the skin. These various methods allow for different rates of absorption and therapeutic uses depending on the intended ailment.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Paeoonia × suffruticosa can cause allergic skin reactions such as contact dermatitis, itching, or redness when the plant material touches the skin. Some individuals may also experience respiratory irritation or sneezing if they inhale pollen from the flowers. Digestive upset can occur if any part of the plant is ingested accidentally. While these effects are generally localized, sensitivity levels vary significantly between different people. It is important to handle the plant with caution to avoid these adverse reactions.
