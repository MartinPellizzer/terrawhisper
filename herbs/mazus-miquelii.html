Mazus miquelii

What's the taxonomical classification of Mazus miquelii?

Mazus miquelii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is placed under the order Lamiales. It is further classified within the family Mazaceae and is identified under the genus Mazus, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species miquelii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Mazus miquelii has a low-growing, creeping growth habit characterized by prostrate stems that spread via stolons and small, opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated margins. The foliage typically features a bright green color and can develop reddish tints under specific environmental conditions. Its reproductive structures consist of small, zygomorphic flowers that are usually pale purple, lavender, or white with distinct spotting on the lower lip. These flowers emerge from the leaf axils on short pedicels and typically measure only a few millimeters in diameter. The overall plant structure forms a dense, mat-like groundcover that remains relatively low to the soil surface.
