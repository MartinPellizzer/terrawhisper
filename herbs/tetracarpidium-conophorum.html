Tetracarpidium conophorum

What's the taxonomical classification of Tetracarpidium conophorum?

Tetracarpidium conophorum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic journey continues through the order Malpighiales and the family Euphorbiaceae. Finally, the plant is placed within the genus Tetracarpidium, specifically identified by its species name, conophorum.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds betulinic acid and ricinine. Betulinic acid is a triterpenoid known for its potent anti-tumor and anti-inflammatory properties. Ricinine is an alkaloid found within the plant that exhibits sedative and insecticidal activities. These specific chemical constituents contribute to the complex pharmacological profile of the species. Researchers study these substances to develop new therapeutic treatments for various diseases.

As discussed in a study, phytochemical investigation on the hexane fraction of Tetracarpidium conophorum seeds led to the isolation of two compounds, Betulinic acid (1) and Ricinine (2). Betulinic acid inhibited HepG2 cells with a percentage inhibition of 54% compared with the standard doxorubicin at 64%. Molecular docking revealed binding energies of -11.2 kcal/mol for betulinic acid and -5.4 kcal/mol for ricinine regarding interactions with Hepatitis B Virus DNA polymerase. The study showed that these compounds possessed hepatoprotective and cytotoxic activities. Specifically, ricinine exhibited hepatoprotection in CCl4-induced liver damage.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating male infertility, oxidative reproductive toxicity, reproductive cellular damage, and liver damage. Research indicates that its bioactive compounds help mitigate the effects of oxidative stress on the reproductive system. By reducing cellular damage, it supports the preservation of healthy sperm function and overall reproductive integrity. Furthermore, the plant demonstrates protective properties that help prevent various forms of liver damage. These medicinal properties make it a significant subject in the study of natural treatments for reproductive and hepatic issues.

As discussed in a study, Tetracarpidium conophorum is a medicinal plant used in folklore as a male fertility enhancer. Adult male rats were administered the aqueous leaf extract orally at doses of 50, 500, and 1000 mg/kg body weight for 21 consecutive days. At the highest concentration of 1000 mg/kg, the highest percentage decreases of MDA levels were 66.35% in the testis, 42.68% in the epididymis, 62.50% in the seminal vesicle, and 63.36% in the prostate gland. These recorded values were two-fold greater than the values obtained for the standard drug, clomiphene citrate, which was administered at 1.04 mg/kg body weight. The results showed that the extract significantly increased the activities of superoxide dismutase, catalase, glutathione peroxidase, glutathione-S-transferase, and the levels of GSH, vitamin C, and total protein. Consequently, the extract showed a capacity to reduce lipid peroxidation in reproductive organs and accessory glands.
