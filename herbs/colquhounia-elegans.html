What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the unique medicinal compounds colqueleganoide A and colqueleganoide B. These specific diterpenoids are the primary bioactive constituents identified within the species Colquhounia elegans. Researchers have focused on these substances due to their potential pharmacological properties and distinct chemical structures. Studying these molecules provides insight into the therapeutic possibilities inherent in the plant's secondary metabolites. Such investigations aim to further understand how these compounds might interact with biological systems.

According to a study published by "Organic letters", two novel triterpenoid saponins, colqueleganoids A (1) and B (2), were isolated from the root of Colquhounia elegans. These compounds possess a methyl-30 incorporated 6/6/6/6-cyclized carbon skeleton known as colquelegane. Both compounds significantly enhanced TNF-α production in RAW264.7 macrophages stimulated with lipopolysaccharide (LPS). Additionally, compound 1 also increased the IL-6 production in these same macrophages. These findings suggest the potential application of these compounds as immunostimulants in immunotherapy and vaccination.
