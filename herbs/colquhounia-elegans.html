What's the taxonomical classification of Colquhounia elegans?

Colquhounia elegans belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Colquhounia, and its unique species designation, elegans.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Colquhounia elegans has erect, woody stems that reach heights of up to 1.5 meters and feature lanceolate, serrated leaves with prominent veins.

The foliage is dark green and possesses a slightly rugose texture on the upper surface. During the blooming season, the plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink. These blossoms grow in dense, terminal clusters at the ends of the branches.

The overall structure of the plant is bushy and spreading, allowing it to form thickets in its natural habitat.

        Colquhounia elegans is prepared primarily as a decoction or a tea made by boiling the dried leaves and stems in water.         What side-effects this plant can have?

        Colquhounia elegans can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or digestive distress if ingested or touched. While much of the plant's toxicity is not extensively documented in medical literature, contact with the foliage may trigger localized dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Some people might experience redness or itching upon physical contact with the leaves. If accidentally consumed, it could potentially lead to nausea or stomach upset. It is always advisable to handle this plant with gloves to avoid unexpected bodily reactions.
