Colquhounia elegans

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Colquhounia elegans has erect, woody stems that reach heights of up to 1.5 meters and feature lanceolate, serrated leaves with prominent veins. The foliage is dark green and possesses a slightly rugose texture on the upper surface. During the blooming season, the plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink. These blossoms grow in dense, terminal clusters at the ends of the branches. The overall structure of the plant is bushy and spreading, allowing it to form thickets in its natural habitat.
