Polygonum molliiforme

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polygonum molliiforme has soft, pubescent leaves and stems that are covered in fine, downy hairs. These hairs typically extend from the base of the plant up through the foliage, giving the entire structure a velvety texture. The leaves are generally lanceolate or elliptical in shape with entire margins. Its stems are slender and often exhibit a slightly reddish tint under certain light conditions. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils during its growing season.
