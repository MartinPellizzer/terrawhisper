What's the taxonomical classification of Clematis pogonandra?

Clematis pogonandra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Ranunculales. It is a distinctive representative of the family Ranunculaceae, falling under the genus Clematis, and is specifically identified by its species name, pogonandra.

Clematis pogonanra has opposite, simple, or trifoliate leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate with entire or slightly serrated margins.

        What are the herbal preparations of Clematis pogonandra?

        Clematis pogonanra is prepared primarily as a decoction, a tea, or a poultice using the plant's roots and stems. Practitioners often simmer the tough woody parts in water to extract its active constituents for internal consumption. When used topically, the crushed plant material is applied directly to the skin as a warm mash to soothe localized irritations.         What side-effects this plant can have?

        Clematis pogonandra can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if ingested due to the presence of protoanemonin. This chemical compound is a known irritant that reacts when plant tissues are crushed or damaged. Contact with the sap may result in redness, itching, or blistering on the skin of sensitive individuals. If the plant is eaten, it can lead to nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain. While less documented than other Clematis species, cautious handling is advised to avoid these adverse reactions.
