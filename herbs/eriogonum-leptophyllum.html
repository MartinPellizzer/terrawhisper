What is Eriogonum leptophyllum?

Eriogonum leptophyllum is a perennial herbaceous plant that belongs to the family Polygonaceae.

It has a taproot system and erect stems that are 10-50 cm in height and 0.5-2 mm in diameter, with simple, alternate leaves that are linear to lanceolate in shape, and 1-10 cm in length. The leaves have a glabrous or puberulent surface, and the margins are entire or ciliate. The inflorescence is a corymbose or paniculate cyme, and the flowers are small, 2-4 mm in diameter, with 5 petals that are pink to red in color.

The plant produces small, ovoid to ellipsoidal seeds, 0.5-1.5 mm in length, that are glabrous and have a reticulate surface.
