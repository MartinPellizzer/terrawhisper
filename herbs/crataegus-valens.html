Red Hawthorn (Crataegus Valens)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Crataegus valens.

Red hawthorn, scientifically known as Crataegus valens, is a small, deciduous shrub native to the southern United States, prized for its medicinal and culinary uses. As a medicinal herb, it is valued for its ability to support cardiovascular health, reduce stress, and promote emotional well-being, making it a versatile adaptogen. Traditionally, Native American tribes used red hawthorn to treat heart conditions, anxiety, and digestive issues, while European herbalists incorporated it into remedies for circulatory and nervous system support. In modern wellness practices, it is often used in herbal formulations for stress relief, sleep support, and as a natural alternative to synthetic sedatives. One of its unique features is the presence of flavonoids and oligomeric procyanidins, which contribute to its heart-protective properties, and its berries have a tart, slightly sweet flavor that makes them a popular ingredient in teas and culinary applications.
