    What is Celtis aetnensis?

    Celtis aetnensis is a species of flowering plant in the family Cannabaceae.

    It is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically growing to 5-10 meters in height, with a broad, rounded crown and a short, stout trunk. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, 2-6 centimeters in length, with a short petiole and a serrated margin. The inflorescence is a catkin, bearing small, greenish-yellow flowers in spring, followed by small, drupaceous fruits in autumn.

    The plant is native to the Mediterranean region, specifically in Sicily, and is known for its ability to tolerate poor soil and dry conditions.
