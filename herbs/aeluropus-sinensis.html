Aeluropus sinensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aeluropus sinensis has a perennial growth habit characterized by slender, erect stems that typically reach heights of 20 to 60 centimeters. The leaves are arranged alternately along the stem and feature linear to lanceolate shapes with entire margins. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in terminal clusters or panicles, often displaying a pale coloration. The root system consists of a creeping rhizome that allows the plant to spread effectively through its habitat. Fine hairs may be present on the stem or leaf surfaces depending on the specific environmental conditions.
