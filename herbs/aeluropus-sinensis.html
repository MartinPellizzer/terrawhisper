Aeluropus sinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Aeluropus sinensis?

Aeluropus sinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further falls under the order Poales and is a member of the family Poaceae. Taxonomically, it is identified by the genus Aeluropus, with the specific epithet sinensis designating its unique species status.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aeluropus sinensis has a perennial growth habit characterized by slender, erect stems that typically reach heights of 20 to 60 centimeters. The root system consists of a creeping rhizome that allows the plant to spread effectively through its habitat.
