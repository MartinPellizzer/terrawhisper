Rhododendron thymifolium

What's the taxonomical classification of Rhododendron thymifolium?

Rhododendron thymifoliuim belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized under the order Ericales. This plant is a member of the family Ericaceae, falling under the genus Rhododendron, and is specifically identified by its species name, thymifolium.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhododendron thymifoliuum has small, elliptical, evergreen leaves that are typically 5 to 15 millimeters long and possess a revolute margin.

These leaves are characterized by a leathery texture and are often densely clustered along the woody stems. The plant features tiny, tubular flowers that emerge from terminal clusters during its blooming period. Its overall growth habit is that of a low-growing, spreading subshrub adapted to rocky or alpine environments.

The foliage often displays a dark green color on the upper surface with a lighter underside.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains grayanotoxins, flavonoids, and triterpenoids that contribute to its complex chemical profile.

The grayanotoxins found in the foliage are potent neurotoxins that can affect sodium channels in cell membranes. Flavonoids within the plant provide antioxidant properties that are common among many species in the Rhododendron genus. Triterpenoids are also present and may contribute to various biological activities.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Rhododendron thymifoliuum can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, neurological impairment, and potential cardiac issues due to the presence of toxic grayanotoxins within its tissues. Ingestion of these toxins disrupts the sodium channels in cell membranes, leading to symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain. Victims may also experience dizziness, confusion, and muscle weakness as the toxins affect the central nervous system. More serious cases can involve irregular heartbeats or a significant drop in blood pressure that requires medical intervention. Because of these risks, the plant should never be consumed by humans or animals.
