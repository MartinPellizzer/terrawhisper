Pyrus betulifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pyrus betulifolia has an upright, deciduous growth habit characterized by simple, alternate leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptical with finely serrated margins. The leaf surfaces often exhibit a slightly rugose texture and are typically dark green on the upper side. Young twigs and petioles frequently display a reddish or purplish tint, contrasting with the more mature, grayish bark of the main stem. Small, pubescent flowers emerge from the branches, eventually developing into small, rounded pome fruits. These botanical features distinguish the species within its specific ecological niche.

The species is most frequently found in coniferous forests where it thrives in well-drained soils.
