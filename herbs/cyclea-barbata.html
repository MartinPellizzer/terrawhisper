What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse array of bioactive substances including azafluoranthene alkaloids and phytoecdysone. These specific chemical constituents are responsible for the various biological activities observed in the species. The presence of these alkaloids contributes significantly to the plant's complex chemical profile. Additionally, the phytoecdysone acts as a potent steroidal compound within its structure. Such medicinal properties make the plant a subject of interest for pharmacological research.

According to a study published by "Journal of Asian natural products research", two new azafluoranthene alkaloids (1 and 2) and a new phytoecdysone (3) were isolated from the stems of Cyclea barbata Miers. These were found alongside six known compounds (4-9). This research represents the first report of azafluoranthene alkaloids (1 and 2) and phytoecdysones (3, 8, and 9) from the Cyclea genus. In in vitro bioassay, four isolates (3, 5, 6, and 9) demonstrated moderate hepatoprotective activity against N-acetyl-p-aminophenol (APAP)-induced toxicity in HepG2 cells.
