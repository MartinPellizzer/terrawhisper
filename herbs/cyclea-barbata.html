Cyclea barbaata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and follows the order Ranunculales. This plant is a member of the family Menispermaceae, specifically falling under the genus Cyclea, with its unique identity defined by the species barbata.

The reproductive structures produce small, fleshy fruits that change color as they mature. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains azafluoranthene alkaloids and phytoecdysone which serve as its primary medicinal compounds. These specific alkaloids are known for their potential bioactive properties in various pharmacological studies. Phytoecdysone acts as a plant-derived steroid that can influence hormonal processes in biological systems. Researchers study these chemical constituents to understand their therapeutic applications in modern medicine. The synergy between these substances defines the plant's unique chemical profile.

As discussed in a study, the stems of Cyclea barbata Miers contain two new azafluoranthene alkaloids labeled 1 and 2, alongside a new phytoecdysone identified as 3. The plant also yielded six known compounds numbered 4 through 9. This research represents the first report of azafluoranthene alkaloids 1 and 2 and phytoecdysones 3, 8, and 9 from the Cyclea genus. In an in vitro bioassay, four specific isolates, which are 3, 5, 6, and 9, demonstrated moderate hepatoprotective activity against N-acetyl-p-aminophenol (APAP)-induced toxicity in HepG2 cells.
