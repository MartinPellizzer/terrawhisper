What's the taxonomical classification of Schoenorchis gemmata?

Schoenorchis gemmata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Asparagales and is a member of the Orchidaceae family. Finally, this specific plant is identified by its genus, Schoenorchis, and its unique species designation, gemmata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Schoenorchis gemmata has small, succulent, bright green leaves that are arranged in a dense, compact rosette atop a very short stem.

The plant grows as a miniature epiphyte, often staying only a few centimeters in size. Its inflorescence consists of a slender, erect spike that carries numerous tiny, jewel-like white flowers. These flowers feature delicate, intricate petals and a prominent lip that can sometimes display subtle color variations.

The entire structure is designed for extreme miniaturization, allowing it to thrive in specific niches of tropical forest environments.
