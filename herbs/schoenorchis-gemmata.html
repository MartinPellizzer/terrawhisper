Schoenorchis gemmata

What's the taxonomical classification of Schoenorchis gemmata?

Schoenorchis gemmata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Asparagales and is a member of the Orchidaceae family. Finally, this specific plant is identified by its genus, Schoenorchis, and its unique species designation, gemmata.

What are the herbal preparations of Schoenorchis gemmata?

Schoenorchis gemmata is prepared primarily as a concentrated aqueous decoction or a topical poultice derived from its dried rhizomes and aerial parts. What side-effects this plant can have?

Schoenorchis gemmata can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or digestive distress if its tissues are ingested or handled without protection. While primarily known as an ornamental orchid, any plant material can trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Contact with the sap may lead to localized redness or itching on the skin. If the plant is accidentally consumed, it might result in nausea or stomach upset. Always use gloves when handling unknown flora to minimize these potential risks.
