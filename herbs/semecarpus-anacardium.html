What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin diseases like eczema, fungal infections, wounds, and inflammatory conditions. The extracts from the bark and leaves possess significant antimicrobial and antioxidant properties that aid in healing. These medicinal qualities help reduce swelling and soothe irritation on the skin surface. Traditional healers often apply the plant to manage various dermatological ailments. It is also studied for its potential role in addressing internal inflammatory responses.

What are the herbal preparations of Semecarpus anacardium?

Semecaarpus anacardium is prepared as decoctions, infusions, powders, and topical pastes derived from its leaves, bark, and fruit. The leaves are frequently boiled in water to create medicinal teas used for various systemic ailments. Bark extracts are often processed into concentrated decoctions to address specific inflammatory conditions. For external applications, the plant material is crushed into a fine paste to treat skin irritations or wounds. Additionally, dried parts of the plant may be ground into a powder for oral consumption or topical dusting.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Semecaarpus anacardium can cause severe skin irritation, blistering, and swelling if the raw sap or unprocessed seeds come into contact with human tissue. This reaction is primarily due to the presence of caustic phenolic compounds and urushiol-like substances found within the plant's chemical makeup. When applied to the skin, these irritants trigger an inflammatory response that often leads to painful dermatological lesions. Ingesting untreated parts of the plant may also cause significant gastrointestinal distress or mucosal damage. Consequently, careful handling and proper processing are essential to mitigate these toxicological risks.
