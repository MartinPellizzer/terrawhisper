What side-effects this plant can have?

Semecaarpus anacardium can cause severe skin irritation, blistering, and swelling if the raw sap or unprocessed seeds come into contact with human tissue. This reaction is primarily due to the presence of caustic phenolic compounds and urushiol-like substances found within the plant's chemical makeup. When applied to the skin, these irritants trigger an inflammatory response that often leads to painful dermatological lesions. Ingesting untreated parts of the plant may also cause significant gastrointestinal distress or mucosal damage. Consequently, careful handling and proper processing are essential to mitigate these toxicological risks.
