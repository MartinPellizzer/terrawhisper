Bambusa oldhamii

What's the taxonomical classification of Bambusa oldhamii?

Bambusa oldhamii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is part of the order Poales and the family Poaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by its membership in the genus Bambusa, with its unique designation being the species oldhamii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bambusa oldhamii has culms that reach heights of up to 20 meters, featuring a diameter of 8 to 12 centimeters and a distinctive yellowish-green color with prominent nodes. The plant is characterized by its sympodial or clumping growth habit, which allows it to form dense, stable thickets. Each culm possesses a smooth, hard surface with internodes that typically measure between 30 and 60 centimeters in length. The leaves are lanceolate in shape, displaying a bright green hue and reaching lengths of up to 30 centimeters. These architectural features contribute to the plant's structural integrity and rapid biomass production.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the subtropical and tropical regions of Southern China, specifically spanning provinces such as Guangdong, Guangxi, Hainan, and Fujian. It thrives in moist, warm environments common to these East Asian coastal and inland areas. Due to its popularity in landscaping and timber production, it has been introduced to various other tropical territories worldwide. Many cultivation efforts are now found in Southeast Asian countries and parts of the Pacific Islands. Its natural range is largely defined by specific humidity and temperature requirements that favor these specific latitudes.

    What side effects this plant can have?
