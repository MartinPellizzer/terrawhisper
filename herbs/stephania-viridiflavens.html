Stephania viridiflavens

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stephania viridiflavens has a tuberous rootstock that supports slender, climbing stems and opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with entire margins. The foliage typically presents a glossy texture and a vibrant green coloration that reflects its specific epithet. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often displaying subtle yellowish or greenish tones. The climbing habit is facilitated by the flexible nature of its stems as they seek support in tropical environments. This morphology allows the plant to thrive in shaded forest understories by maximizing light interception through its leaf arrangement.
