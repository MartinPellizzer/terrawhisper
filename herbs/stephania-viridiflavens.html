Stephania viridiflavens

What's the taxonomical classification of Stephania viridiflavens?

Stephania viridiflavens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, situated within the order Ranunculales. This plant is a member of the family Menispermaceae, specifically categorized under the genus Stephania, with the specific epithet viridiflavens defining its unique species identity.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds (+) -N-methyl-laurotetanine, (+) -stepharine, and aporpine alkaloids. These specific chemical constituents are classified as indole alkaloids and play a significant role in the plant's biological activity. Researchers study these molecules to understand their potential pharmacological applications in human medicine. The presence of aporpine alkaloids, in particular, suggests complex interactions with various physiological systems. Ongoing chemical analysis continues to reveal how these diverse compounds contribute to the overall medicinal profile of the species.

According to a study published by "Natural product research", investigation into the phytochemistry of Stephania viridiflavens led to the isolation of a new naturally occurring aporpine alkaloid, (+)-6R, 6aS-isocorydine N(beta)-oxide (1). This study also identified three known aporpine alkaloids within the plant: (+)-6R, 6aS-corydine N(beta)-oxide (2), (+)-N-methyl-laurotetanine (3), and (+)-stepharine (4). The structure and stereochemistry of compound 1 were determined through spectroscopic methods and confirmed by synthesis. Notably, alkaloids 2, 3, and 4 were isolated for the first time from this specific species.
