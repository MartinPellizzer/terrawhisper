Ceylon Trichopus (Trichopus zeylanicus) Uses

Ceylon Trichopus is traditionally used to treat respiratory conditions such as bronchitis, asthma, and cough. It helps alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis, common cold, and respiratory tract infections by reducing mucous membrane irritation and easing cold symptoms. Its expectorant properties support clearing the airways and reducing inflammation.

Ceylon Trichopus (Trichopus zeylanicus) Monograph

1. Bronchitis

Ceylon Trichopus is traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of bronchitis due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. It helps reduce mucus buildup and soothe irritated airways, making it a valued herb in herbal medicine for respiratory support.

2. Asthma

Ceylon Trichopus is traditionally used in herbal medicine to alleviate asthma symptoms. It is believed to help reduce inflammation in the airways and ease breathing. Its expectorant properties may help clear mucus, supporting respiratory function. It is often prepared as a decoction or powder for internal use.

3. Cough

Ceylon Trichopus is traditionally used to alleviate cough symptoms due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps loosen mucus and reduce irritation in the respiratory tract. Often prepared as a decoction, it is valued in herbal medicine for its soothing effect on the throat and bronchial passages.

4. Allergic rhinitis

Ceylon Trichopus (Trichopus zeylanicus) is used in traditional herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis. It is believed to reduce nasal inflammation and congestion through its anti-inflammatory and antihistaminic properties. Often prepared as a decoction, it supports respiratory health and is valued for its soothing effects on the nasal passages.

5. Common cold

Ceylon Trichopus is traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of the common cold due to its anti-inflammatory and immune-boosting properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion and soothe sore throats, supporting the body's natural recovery process. Often prepared as a decoction, it is valued for its gentle yet effective action in easing cold-related discomfort.

6. Respiratory tract infections

Ceylon Trichopus is traditionally used to support respiratory health by reducing inflammation and clearing mucus. It is commonly applied for conditions like bronchitis and common colds. Its antimicrobial properties may help combat infections, though it should complement, not replace, professional medical advice.

7. Respiratory infection

Ceylon Trichopus is traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in treating symptoms of respiratory infections. It is believed to help reduce inflammation and clear mucus, making it a valued herb in herbal medicine for alleviating coughs and congestion. Its antiseptic properties may also aid in fighting bacterial infections in the respiratory tract.

8. Respiratory infections

Ceylon Trichopus is used in traditional medicine to treat respiratory infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps alleviate symptoms like coughing and congestion by supporting the body's natural defenses. Its active compounds may reduce infection severity and promote faster recovery.

9. Mucous membrane irritation

Ceylon Trichopus is used to alleviate mucous membrane irritation due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It helps reduce swelling and soothe inflamed tissues in the respiratory and digestive tracts. Traditionally applied as a poultice or decoction, it provides relief from conditions like sore throat and gastritis. Its efficacy is attributed to compounds that neutralize irritants and promote healing.

10. Cold symptoms

Ceylon Trichopus is traditionally used to alleviate cold symptoms such as congestion, coughing, and nasal inflammation. It is believed to support respiratory health by reducing mucus production and easing breathing. Its anti-inflammatory properties may help soothe sore throats and reduce fever associated with colds.
