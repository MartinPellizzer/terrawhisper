10 Precautions To Take When Using Trichopus Zeylanicus (Sarcococca)

Trichopus zeylanicus has some precautions to consider before using it medicinally, such as pregnancy and lactation warnings.

Taking this herb during pregnancy or breastfeeding may cause harm to the fetus or infant due to its unknown effects on developing organs. Without proper precautions, it may lead to birth defects or other complications.

Similarly, individuals with kidney or liver issues should consult their healthcare provider before using Trichopus zeylanicus to avoid potential interactions with medications.

This article explains in details the 10 most important precautions to take when using Trichopus zeylanicus medicinally.

When using Trichopus zeylanicus medicinally, it's important to avoid combining with MAOIs.

This is crucial because MAOIs can increase the levels of certain neurotransmitters in the body, which may enhance the effects of Trichopus zeylanicus and lead to adverse reactions or even serotonin syndrome, a potentially life-threatening condition.

Combining the two can also increase the risk of side effects such as nausea, headaches, and dizziness, making it essential to monitor patients closely and avoid co-administration whenever possible.
