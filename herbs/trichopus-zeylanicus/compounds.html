Ceylon Trichopus (Trichopus zeylanicus) Compounds

Ceylon Trichopus contains several primary active compounds, including flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its antioxidant properties. Coumarins and saponins may support anti-inflammatory effects, while glycosides and terpenoids are associated with digestive and cardiovascular benefits. Alkaloids, essential oils, and anthocyanins also play roles in its therapeutic potential.

Ceylon Trichopus (Trichopus zeylanicus) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Ceylon Trichopus (Trichopus zeylanicus) are bioactive compounds known for their antioxidant properties. They contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating inflammation and digestive issues. These compounds help neutralize free radicals, supporting overall health. Their presence enhances the plant's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

2. Tannin

Tannin in Ceylon Trichopus (Trichopus zeylanicus) contributes to its astringent properties and may support digestive health. It helps in reducing inflammation and has antimicrobial effects, making it a valuable component in traditional herbal remedies.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Ceylon Trichopus (Trichopus zeylanicus). It contributes to the plant's antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, making it valuable in traditional herbal medicine for treating infections and inflammatory conditions.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Ceylon Trichopus (Trichopus zeylanicus). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly its antispasmodic and analgesic effects. Coumarin is also noted for its potential role in supporting digestive health and reducing inflammation.

5. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Ceylon Trichopus (Trichopus zeylanicus). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine, particularly for its potential anti-inflammatory and digestive properties. Saponins are known for their ability to interact with cell membranes, which may support the plant's therapeutic applications.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Ceylon Trichopus (Trichopus zeylanicus). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. 7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Ceylon Trichopus (Trichopus zeylanicus) are bioactive compounds known for their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. 8. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a key active compound found in Ceylon Trichopus (Trichopus zeylanicus). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal medicine. 9. Essential oil

The essential oil of Ceylon Trichopus (Trichopus zeylanicus) contains bioactive compounds with antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. 10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Ceylon Trichopus (Trichopus zeylanicus). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. This compound is particularly valued in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
