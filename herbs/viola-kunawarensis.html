Viola kunawarensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viola kunawarensis has a creeping rhizomatous habit with basal leaves that are reniform to cordate in shape and possess crenate margins. The leaf blades typically feature long petioles and a distinctively textured surface. Its inflorescences consist of solitary flowers emerging from the leaf axils on slender peduncles. The corolla displays a characteristic violet hue with subtle nectar guides on the lower petals. Small, ovoid capsules develop from the fertilized flowers to facilitate seed dispersal.
