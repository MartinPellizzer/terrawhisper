Herpetospermum pedunculosum

What's the taxonomical classification of Herpetospermum pedunculosum?

Herpetospermum pedunculosum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic hierarchy further, the plant is organized under the order Cucurbitales and falls within the family Cucurbitaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Herpetospermum, and its unique species designation, pedunculosum.

What are the pharmacological activities of Herpetospermum pedunculosum?

This plant has anti-cholestaic, anti-inflammatory, anti-proliferative, antioxidant, and hepatoprotective pharmacological activities. Its chemical constituents work to reduce oxidative stress and protect liver cells from various forms of damage. The plant also exhibits properties that help mitigate inflammation and regulate bile flow within the biliary system. Furthermore, its anti-proliferative effects suggest a potential role in inhibiting the uncontrolled growth of certain cells. These diverse biological actions highlight the medicinal potential of Herpetospermum pedunculosum in treating metabolic and inflammatory conditions.

As discussed in a study, the water extract from the seeds of Herpetospermum pedunculosum (HPWE) possesses hepatoprotective effects against acetaminophen-induced liver injury in both BRL-3A cells and mice. In vitro experiments demonstrated that HPWE treatment significantly promoted cell viability, decreased ALT/AST levels, and inhibited ROS accumulation. HPWE and Fer-1 also alleviated erastin-induced cell ferroptosis by upregulating GPX4 and SLC7A11 expression and reducing lipid peroxide production. In vivo, pretreatment with HPWE in APAP-treated mice significantly alleviated serum ALT/AST levels, decreased necrotic cells and inflammatory cell infiltration, and upregulated GPX4 and SLC7A11 expression. Furthermore, HPWE treatment downregulated Nrf2 and its downstream target genes HO-1 and NQO1 at the mRNA and protein levels while inhibiting the activation of NF-κB p65 and downregulating its target genes TNF-α and IL-1β. Through these mechanisms, HPWE relieves oxidative stress and ferroptosis by activating the Nrf2 signaling pathway and inhibiting the NF-κB mediated pathway.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds (+)1-pinoresinol, cucurbitacin B, dihydrocucurbitacin E, herpetosin A, and herpetosin B. These specific chemical constituents are often studied for their diverse biological activities and potential therapeutic applications. The presence of cucurbitacins, such as cucurbitacin B and dihydrocucurbitacin E, is frequently linked to significant cytotoxic properties. Additionally, the lignan (+)1-pinoresinol and the herpetosins contribute to the unique phytochemical profile of the species. Researchers analyze these molecules to better understand how they interact with various cellular pathways.

As discussed in a study, Herpetospermum pedunculatum synthesizes lignans with significant medicinal value. Its mature dried seeds are widely used as a primary source for various therapeutic compounds. The study identified twenty-two HpDIR genes within the plant and classified them into 3 subfamilies labeled a, b/d, and e. Specifically, the proteins HpDIR16 and HpDIR17 catalyze the conversion of coniferyl alcohol to (+)-pinoresinol. These findings highlight the role of DIR proteins in the biosynthesis of these medicinal lignans.
