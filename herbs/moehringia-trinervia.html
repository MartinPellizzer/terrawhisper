Moehringia Trinervia

Moehringia trinervia, also known as three-veined mouse-ear, is a small, low-growing flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its purported diuretic and antiseptic properties. The plant contains various bioactive compounds, including flavonoids and phenolic acids, which may contribute to its medicinal potential. Some studies suggest that it might have anti-inflammatory effects, although more research is needed to confirm these claims.

Due to its hardy nature and potential health benefits, Moehringia trinervia is sometimes cultivated for use in herbal remedies, though its medicinal applications remain largely underexplored.
