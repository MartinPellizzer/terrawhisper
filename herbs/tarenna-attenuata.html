Tarenna attenuata

What's the taxonomical classification of Tarenna attenuata?

Tarenna attenuata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it is part of the order Gentianaales and falls under the family Rubiaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized within the genus Tarenna, with its specific designation being the species attenuata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tarenna attenuata has a woody, climbing shrub habit characterized by slender, twining stems and relatively small, lanceolate to elliptic leaves with entire margins. The foliage typically displays an acute apex and an obtuse base, often appearing somewhat leathery in texture. Its inflorescences are arranged in small, axillary clusters that emerge from the leaf axils. The flowers are generally inconspicuous and possess a delicate structure suitable for specialized pollinators. Small, fleshy fruits develop following fertilization, serving as a primary dispersal mechanism for the species.
