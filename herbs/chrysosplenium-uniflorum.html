Chrysosplenium uniflorum

What's the taxonomical classification of Chrysosplenium uniflorum?

Chrysospeltiun uniflorum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is placed in the order Saxifragales and the family Saxifragaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Chrysospeltiun, and its species designation, uniflorum.

      What are the herbal preparations of Chrysosplenium uniflorum?

      Chrysosplenium uniflorum is prepared primarily as a decoction of the dried roots, a concentrated infusion of the flowering tops, or a topical tincture applied to the skin. These liquid preparations allow for the efficient extraction of active phytochemicals into a water or alcohol base. When used as a tea, the plant is simmered to release dense compounds into the liquid for internal consumption. Tinctures provide a more shelf-stable method for long-term medicinal use and precise dosing. Some practitioners also incorporate the plant into poultices to utilize its properties through direct contact with the body.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Chrysosplenium uniflorum can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, acute skin irritation, and potential neurological impairment if ingested or touched. Individuals may experience nausea, vomiting, and intense abdominal cramping shortly after exposure. Contact with the plant's sap often leads to localized redness, blistering, and persistent itching on the affected areas. Some reports also suggest that high concentrations of its chemical compounds may induce dizziness or lightheadedness. It is essential to handle this species with protective gear to avoid these adverse reactions.
