What's the taxonomical classification of Chrysosplenium uniflorum?

Chrysospeltiun uniflorum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is placed in the order Saxifragales and the family Saxifragaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Chrysospeltiun, and its species designation, uniflorum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Chrysospelnium uniflorum has a single, terminal, bright yellow capitulum supported by a slender, erect stem that typically reaches heights of twenty to forty centimeters. The leaves are primarily basal and lanceolate in shape, possessing entire margins and a smooth texture. Stems are often sparsely clothed in fine, puberulent hairs that provide a subtle tactile quality. The involucral bracts surrounding the flower head are arranged in multiple overlapping rows, protecting the developing florets. Each individual flower within the head is actinomorphic and displays a consistent golden coloration.
