Aster trinervius

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aster trinervius has linear to lanceolate leaves characterized by three prominent longitudinal veins running from the base to the apex. The foliage typically exhibits a slightly serrated margin and a textured surface that helps distinguish it from similar species. Its stems are slender and upright, supporting small heads that are arranged in various configurations depending on the specific variety. The flower heads consist of numerous tiny ray florets surrounding a central cluster of disc florets. These floral components are generally white or pale in color, providing a delicate appearance to the overall structure.
