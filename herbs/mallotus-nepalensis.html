Mallotus nepalensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Mallotus nepalensis?

Mallotus nepalensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Malpighiales. This plant is a member of the family Euphorbiaceae, which places it within the genus Mallotus, and it is specifically identified by its species name, nepalesis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Mallotus nepalensis has ovate to elliptical leaves that are typically three to seven centimeters long, featuring an acute apex, a cuneate base, and a serrated margin. The leaf surfaces are often covered in fine, stellate hairs that give the foliage a slightly textured appearance. The plant grows as a small tree or shrub with branches that may exhibit reddish or brownish tints. Its flowers are small and unisexual, often appearing in dense clusters or panicles. The fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that contains seeds often surrounded by a fleshy, brightly colored aril.
