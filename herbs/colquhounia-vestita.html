Colquhounia vestita

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Colquhounia vestita has woody, perennial stems that are densely covered in fine, spreading hairs and bear lanceolate to ovate leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is characterized by a distinct pubescence that gives the plant a soft, textured appearance. These leaves are arranged oppositely along the branches and possess prominent veins. During its flowering stage, the plant produces small, tubular flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. The overall structure is that of a sprawling or upright shrub, often found in moist, montane environments.
