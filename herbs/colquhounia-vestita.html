What's the taxonomical classification of Colquhounia vestita?

Colquhounia vestita belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is situated under the order Lamiales. It is further categorized into the family Lamiaceae, which defines its botanical lineage. Within this family, the plant is placed in the genus Colquhounia, with its specific classification being the species vestita.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds E-communic acid, clerodane 5-epi-hardwickiic acid, and polyalthic acid. These specific bioactive constituents are classified as diterpenoids and are primarily found within the chemical profile of the species. Research into these substances aims to understand their potential pharmacological properties and biological activities. The presence of such unique organic acids distinguishes the chemical makeup of Colquhounia vestita from other related flora. Understanding these molecular structures is essential for future medicinal applications.

According to a study published by "Phytochemistry", the peltate glandular trichomes of Colquhounia vestita harbor 3 diterpenoid acids. These compounds include 1 clerodane 5-epi-hardwickiic acid and 2 labdanes, specifically polyalthic acid and E-communic acid. Through laser microdissection coupled with UPLC-MS/MS, researchers identified these specialized metabolites within the plant's dense peltate and capitae glandular trichomes. The major component, polyalthic acid, helps the plant adapt to UV radiation and cold stresses. This specific acid is linked to the expression of 1 calmodulin binding transcription activator gene CAMTA3 and 2 downstream cold-responsive genes, CBF3 and RD29A.
