    As discussed in a study, Colquhounia vestita contains dense peltate and capitae glandular trichomes on its leaf and stem surfaces. Through laser microdissection coupled with UPLC-MS/MS, three diterpenoid acids were identified from the peltate glandular trichomes of the plant. These medicinal compounds include 1 clerodane 5-epi-hardwickiic acid and 2 labdanes, specifically polyalthic acid and E-communic acid. The major component, polyalthic acid, was found to increase the biomass of Arabidopsis thaliana seedlings and decrease their malondialdehyde content under UV radiation and cold stresses.
