Thespesia populnea

What's the taxonomical classification of Thespesia populnea?

Thespesia populnea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it resides in the order Malvales and the family Malvaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Thespesia, and its specific species name, populnea.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Thespesia populnea contains a diverse range of bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, saponins, flavonoids, tannins, and terpenoids. These chemical constituents contribute to the plant's significant pharmacological activities such as antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties. Specifically, the presence of triterpenoids and certain flavonoids supports its traditional use in treating skin diseases and inflammation. Research also indicates that the various polyphenolic compounds within the bark and leaves help combat oxidative stress in cellular models. These complex biochemical profiles make the plant a subject of interest for developing new therapeutic agents.

What are the therapeutic actions of Thespesia populnea?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin diseases, digestive issues, respiratory ailments, and inflammatory conditions. Its leaves and bark contain bioactive compounds that help alleviate symptoms of eczema and various skin infections. When applied topically or ingested in specific preparations, it can soothe gastrointestinal distress and stomach aches. The plant also serves as a remedy for coughs and asthma due to its traditional use in managing respiratory congestion. These medicinal properties make it a significant component in various folk medicine practices.
