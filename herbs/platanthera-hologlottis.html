Platanthera hologlottis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Platanthera hologlottis has a slender, unbranched stem that typically reaches heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and bears a terminal spike of numerous white, fragrant flowers. The leaves are primarily basal and lanceolate in shape, though a few smaller, narrower leaves may emerge along the stem. Each individual flower features a long, nectar-filled spur extending from the back of the lip. The labellum is notably narrow and often curves slightly, providing a landing platform for specific moth pollinators. These delicate structures are supported by a rhizomatous root system that allows the plant to persist in moist, shaded habitats.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical regions of West and Central Africa, spanning from Senegal and Guinea in the west to Uganda and Kenya in the east. It thrives within the moist, lowland rainforest habitats that characterize much of the Guineo-Congolian regional center of endemism. Its distribution is closely tied to areas with high annual rainfall and consistent humidity levels. Within these forest ecosystems, the species is often found in dense undergrowth or along riverine corridors. Populations are most concentrated in the Congo Basin, which serves as a primary stronghold for the species.
