Platanthera hologlottis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Platanthera hologlottis has a slender, unbranched stem that typically reaches heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and bears a terminal spike of numerous white, fragrant flowers. Each individual flower features a long, nectar-filled spur extending from the back of the lip. The labellum is notably narrow and often curves slightly, providing a landing platform for specific moth pollinators.
