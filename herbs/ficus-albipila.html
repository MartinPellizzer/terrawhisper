Ficus albipila

Ficus albipila, also known as the white-footed fig, is a plant species native to the tropical forests of Southeast Asia, characterized by its unique white-colored foot and distinctive leaf shape.

Traditionally, Ficus albipila has been used in herbal medicine for its medicinal properties, including antibacterial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant effects.

The plant has been associated with various health benefits, such as reducing fever, alleviating respiratory issues, and promoting wound healing.

In herbal preparations, Ficus albipila is often used in teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as being incorporated into traditional remedies and supplements.
