Mentha pulegium, commonly known as pennyroyal, is a flowering plant belonging to the mint family, known for its strong, camphor-like aroma.

This herb is traditionally used for its digestive and antimicrobial properties, offering benefits such as aiding digestion, reducing nausea, and supporting respiratory health.

The therapeutic actions of pennyroyal are attributed to its bioactive constituents, including menthone, pulegone, and camphor, which exhibit antispasmodic and antifungal effects.

Medicinal Parts

Mentha pulegium leaf is a key component of this herb, known for its aromatic and medicinal properties.

The leaves contain essential oils rich in compounds like pulegone and menthone, which contribute to its distinctive minty scent and therapeutic effects. These oils are often extracted through distillation to produce essential oils used in aromatherapy and topical applications.

The leaves are also utilized in traditional medicine for their ability to alleviate digestive issues, such as bloating and indigestion, due to their carminative properties. Additionally, the leaf's anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects make it beneficial in treating respiratory conditions like asthma and bronchitis.

The versatility of the Mentha pulegium leaf in both culinary and medicinal contexts highlights its importance in herbal remedies and natural health practices.
