Mexican Angelica (Ligusticum porteri) Compounds

Mexican Angelica contains essential oil, which includes compounds like coumarin and terpenoids. It also has flavonoids, phenolic acids, and glycosides. Tannins, saponins, and alkaloids contribute to its medicinal properties. Anthocyanins provide antioxidant benefits.

Mexican Angelica (Ligusticum porteri) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Mexican Angelica (Ligusticum porteri) contains bioactive compounds like ligustilide and butylidenephthalide. These compounds contribute to its traditional use in treating inflammation and digestive issues. The oil is often extracted through steam distillation and is valued for its aromatic and therapeutic properties.

2. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Mexican Angelica (Ligusticum porteri). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating ailments like rheumatism and inflammation. Coumarin's anticoagulant properties may support its role in herbal medicine. However, it can also have phototoxic effects, requiring caution in use.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Mexican Angelica (Ligusticum porteri) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help reduce oxidative stress and support immune function. They are naturally occurring in the plant's aerial parts and are often studied for their potential therapeutic benefits in herbal medicine.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid in Mexican Angelica (Ligusticum porteri) contributes to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound helps reduce oxidative stress and supports the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments. It plays a key role in the plant's therapeutic effects.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Mexican Angelica (Ligusticum porteri). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects. Glycosides are often responsible for the therapeutic actions observed in herbal remedies derived from this plant.

6. Tannin

Tannin in Mexican Angelica (Ligusticum porteri) contributes to its astringent properties, aiding in wound healing and reducing inflammation. It also supports digestive health by strengthening mucous membranes and may help in managing symptoms of gastrointestinal disorders.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Mexican Angelica (Ligusticum porteri) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, supporting the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments. They are key to the plant's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Mexican Angelica (Ligusticum porteri). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponins help support digestive health and may enhance the plant's therapeutic effects in herbal medicine.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Mexican Angelica (Ligusticum porteri). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects. Alkaloids in this species are part of the complex chemical profile that supports its use in traditional herbal medicine.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Mexican Angelica (Ligusticum porteri). It contributes to the plant's vibrant color and has antioxidant properties that support cellular health. This compound may aid in reducing oxidative stress and inflammation, making it relevant in herbal medicine for its potential therapeutic benefits.
