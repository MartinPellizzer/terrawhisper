Rhamnus esquirolii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhamnus esquirolii has elliptical to lanceolate leaves that are typically arranged oppositely or in subopposite patterns along its woody stems. The leaf margins are often finely serrated or entire, while the texture varies from smooth to slightly leathery. Its branching structure is characterized by slender, somewhat irregular woody twigs that support a spreading canopy. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils, leading to the development of small, fleshy drupes. These fruits typically change color as they mature, serving as a primary morphological feature for identification.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Rhamnus esquirolii can cause gastrointestinal distress including abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea if consumed in improper amounts. These adverse reactions are often linked to the presence of anthraquinone glycosides within the plant tissues. Such compounds act as potent stimulant laxatives that may lead to electrolyte imbalances or dehydration with frequent use. Individuals with sensitive digestive systems should exercise extreme caution when handling or ingesting this species. Prolonged exposure or excessive dosage can further irritate the intestinal lining and disrupt normal bowel functions.
