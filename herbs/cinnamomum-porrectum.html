Cinnamomum porrectum

What's the taxonomical classification of Cinnamomum porrectum?

Cinnamomum porrectum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression through the order Laurales, it is situated within the family Lauraceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its genus, Cinnamomum, and its specific epithet, porrectum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cinnamomum porrectum has small, simple, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical or lanceolate in shape with entire margins and an entire or slightly wavy edge. The foliage is characterized by a leathery texture and a deep green color on the upper surface, often featuring a paler underside. The plant exhibits a woody structure with slender branches that can grow in an upright or spreading manner depending on the environment. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in axillary clusters, which eventually develop into small drupes. These fruits are typically ovoid and change color as they reach maturity.

What are the pharmacological activities of Cinnamomum porrectum?

This plant has anti-inflammatory, antifungal, carminative, febrifuge, and tonic pharmacological activities. Its anti-inflammatory properties help reduce swelling and pain in various tissues. The antifungal elements allow the plant to combat different types of fungal infections effectively. It functions as a carminative to relieve intestinal gas and aids as a febrifuge to lower high body temperatures. Finally, its use as a tonic helps restore overall strength and vigor to the body.

According to a study published by "Journal of the Medical Association of Thailand = Chotmaihet thangphaet", Cinnamomum porrectum is traditionally used as a spice, vegetable, carminative, tonic, febrifuge, and for postpartum care due to its anti-inflammation properties. The 50% ethanolic extract of the plant showed high anti-inflammatory activity with an IC50 value of 19.28±2.43 μg/ml. The 95% ethanolic extract also demonstrated high anti-inflammatory activity, yielding an IC50 value of 13.78±3.76 μg/ml. In contrast, the water extract of the plant showed low anti-inflammatory activity with IC50 values >100 μg/ml. While the ethanolic extracts showed strong activity, the oily extracts of Cinnamomum porrectum showed cytotoxicity.
