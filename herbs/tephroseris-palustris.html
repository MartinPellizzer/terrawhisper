What's the taxonomical classification of Tephroseris palustris?

Tephroseris palustris belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Asterales. As a member of the family Asteraceae, it is specifically categorized within the genus Tephroseris, with the species designation palustris.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tephroseris palustris has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by erect, unbranched stems that reach heights of 10 to 50 centimeters. The leaves are primarily basal and alternate, featuring an oblong to lanceolate shape with finely serrated margins and entire upper portions. Flower heads are arranged in terminal clusters, consisting of small, yellow ray and disc florets typical of the Asteraceae family. Each involucral bract is arranged in several overlapping series, providing structural support to the developing seed heads. The plant eventually produces small, wind-dispersed achenes equipped with a fine pappus for propagation.
