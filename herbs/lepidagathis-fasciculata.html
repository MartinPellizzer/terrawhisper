What's the taxonomical classification of Lepidagathis fasciculata?

Lepidagathis fasciculata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further organized under the order Lamiales. It falls within the family Acanthaceae, which characterizes its botanical lineage, and is specifically identified under the genus Lepidagathis, with its unique designation as the species fasciulata.

    What are the herbal preparations of Lepidagathis fasciculata?

    Lepidagathis fasciculata is prepared as a decoction, a concentrated liquid extract made by boiling the plant material in water to extract its active compounds. Once the liquid has cooled, it is often administered orally to patients seeking its medicinal properties. In some traditional applications, the plant matter may also be crushed into a fine paste for topical application on the skin. This paste provides a direct way to apply the plant's essence to localized areas of concern.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Lepidagathis fasciculata can cause severe skin irritation, localized swelling, and intense burning sensations upon direct contact with the epidermis. The chemical compounds present in the plant trigger an immediate inflammatory response in sensitive individuals. Some users may experience systemic symptoms such as nausea or dizziness if the toxins are accidentally ingested. Prolonged exposure to the plant's sap can lead to blistering or long-term dermatological scarring. It is essential to wash any affected areas immediately with soap and water to minimize these adverse reactions.
