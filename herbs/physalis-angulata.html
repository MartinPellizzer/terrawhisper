Starfruit (Physalis Angulata)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Physalis angulata.

Starfruit, scientifically known as Physalis angulata, is a medicinal herb native to tropical and subtropical regions, valued for its therapeutic properties and adaptogenic qualities. It is commonly used in traditional medicine for its ability to support digestive health, reduce inflammation, and enhance immune function. In Ayurveda and traditional Chinese medicine, starfruit has been employed to treat ailments such as fever, respiratory infections, and skin conditions, while its leaves and fruits are also used as a spice in local cuisines. Modern wellness applications include its potential as a natural remedy for metabolic disorders and stress relief, thanks to its rich content of antioxidants and bioactive compounds. Notably, starfruit is known for its distinctive tart-sweet flavor and contains a rare compound called physalin, which has shown promise in various pharmacological studies.
