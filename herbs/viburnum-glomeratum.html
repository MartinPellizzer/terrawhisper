What's the taxonomical classification of Viburnum glomeratum?

Viburnum glomeratum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed under the order Dipsacales. The plant is a member of the family Viburnaceae and falls under the genus Viburnum, specifically identified as the species glomeratum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viburnum glomeratum has oppositely arranged, ovate to elliptical leaves that measure approximately 2 to 5 centimeters in length and possess serrated margins and acute tips. Its inflorescences consist of dense, terminal cymes where small, creamy-white flowers are clustered tightly together. Following flowering, the plant produces small, fleshy drupes that transition in color as they ripen.
