Angiopteris yunnanensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Angiopteris yunnanensis?

Angiopteris yunnanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. This plant is a member of the class Equisetopsida and falls under the subclass Marattiidae. Following its taxonomic lineage through the order Marattiales, it is situated within the family Marattiaceae. Ultimately, its specific identity is defined by the genus Angiopteris and the species epithet yunnanensis.

      What are the herbal preparations of Angiopteris yunnanensis?

      Angiopteris yunnanensis is prepared primarily as a decoction where the dried rhizomes are boiled in water to extract their active medicinal components. This traditional method allows the soluble compounds to be concentrated into a potent liquid tea for ingestion. In some clinical practices, the plant material may also be processed into powders that are mixed with warm water or honey. Occasionally, extracts from the plant are used in concentrated liquid forms to ensure precise dosing. These various preparations are essential for utilizing the plant's therapeutic properties in traditional medicine.

Detailed toxicological studies on this specific species are limited, which increases the risk of unknown long-term complications.
