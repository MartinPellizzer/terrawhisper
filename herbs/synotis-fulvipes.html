What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Synotis fulvipes has ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins and distinctively fulvous, or tawny-colored, pubescence on the petioles and leaf undersides. The plant typically exhibits a herbaceous growth habit with stems that are covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of small flowers that emerge from the leaf axils in a clustered arrangement. The floral structures often feature delicate petals that contrast with the textured, hairy surfaces of the vegetative organs.
