Pleurospermum uralense has perennial, creeping rhizomes with small, opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically entire and lack petioles. The plant exhibits a decumbent growth habit, often forming dense mats across forest floors or rocky terrain. Its inflorescences consist of small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in axillary clusters or short racemes. The flowers are characterized by a lack of petals and possess a distinct calyx structure typical of the family Podostemaceae.
