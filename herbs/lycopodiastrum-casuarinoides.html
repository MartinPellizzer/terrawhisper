Lycopodiastrum casuarinoides

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lycopodiastrum casuarinoides has a creeping rhizome that produces slender, dichotomously branched stems resembling the green, needle-like branchlets of Casuarina trees. The leaves are small, scale-like, and closely appressed to the stem, arranged in a spiral pattern. These leaves are typically lanceolate or narrowly triangular in shape. The plant reproduces via specialized strobili that develop at the termini of the branches. This unique morphology allows the plant to mimic the appearance of other vegetation within its forest habitat.
