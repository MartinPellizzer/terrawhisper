Paulownia fortunei

What's the taxonomical classification of Paulownia fortunei?

Paulownia fortunei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic progression, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Lamiales, this plant is situated in the family Paulowniaceae. Specifically, it is identified under the genus Paulownia and is designated by the species name fortunei.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Paulownia fortunei has large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length and feature serrated margins and a velvety texture. The tree typically grows to a height of 10 to 15 meters, characterized by a straight, greyish trunk with relatively smooth bark. During the blooming season, it produces clusters of trumpet-shaped, pale violet or lavender flowers that emerge from the terminal branches. These blossoms are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous small, winged seeds designed for wind dispersal. The overall architecture of the plant is spreading, creating a wide canopy of dense foliage.

The species' range is restricted by its preference for specific environmental conditions found within these subtropical zones. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

These specific phytochemicals are primarily classified as flavonoids and phenolic glycosides known for their diverse biological activities. Research suggests that these constituents may provide significant antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. The presence of arbutin specifically contributes to the plant's potential use in skin whitening and dermatological applications. According to a study published by "Zhong yao cai = Zhongyaocai = Journal of Chinese medicinal materials", the chemical constituents of the flower of Paulownia fortunei were isolated by column chromatography and identified through spectroscopic analysis. These compounds include apigenin (I), luteolin (II), Hesperetin (III), Naringenin-7-O-beta-D-glucoside (IV), Arbutin (V), 4-hydroxybenzyl-beta-D-glucoside (VI), abscisic acid (VII), and 1-acetoxy-3-hydroxypropan-2-yl-3-hydroxypentanoate (VIII). All 8 of these specific compounds are obtained from the flower of this plant for the first time.
