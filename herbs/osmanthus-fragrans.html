Osmanthus fragrans

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Osmanthus fragrans has an evergreen shrub or small tree structure characterized by opposite, leathery, elliptical leaves that measure approximately 5 to 15 centimeters in length and feature entire margins and a glossy dark green upper surface. The foliage remains dense throughout the year, providing a thick canopy of foliage. Small, tubular, four-lobed flowers emerge in clusters from the leaf axils, typically appearing in late autumn or early spring. These tiny blossoms are creamy white or pale yellow and are renowned for their intense, sweet fragrance. The plant also produces small, ovoid drupes that transition from green to a dark purple or black color upon ripening.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Osmanthus fragrans targets inflammatory mediators such as cyclooxygenase-2, inducible nitric oxide synthase, and various pro-inflammatory cytokines like TNF-alpha and IL-6. These interactions occur primarily through its rich concentration of bioactive compounds, including oleanolic acid, ursolic acid, and various flavonoids. What are the therapeutic actions of Osmanthus fragrans?

Osmanthus fragrans has therapeutic properties that include being aromatic, carminative, antispasmodic, and astringent. The astringent nature of the plant further supports tissue health and helps to tone various mucous membranes.

What are the herbal preparations of this plant?

This plant can be prepared as a tea or infusion by steeping the dried flowers in hot water to create a fragrant, calming beverage.
