Hyptis rhomboidea

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hyptis rhomboidea has rhomboid-shaped leaves that are typically opposite, sessile, and possess entire or slightly serrated margins. The plant exhibits a woody or subshrubby growth habit with stems that may show varying degrees of branching. Its inflorescences consist of small, yellow capitula arranged in terminal clusters. The individual florets are characteristic of the Asteraceae family, featuring tubular disc florets. The overall foliage texture is often somewhat thickened or leathery depending on the specific environmental conditions.
