Hyptis rhomboidea

What's the taxonomical classification of Hyptis rhomboidea?

Hyptis rhomboidea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant follows the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Lamiaceae. Its taxonomic hierarchy is completed by its placement within the genus Hyptis, specifically identified by the species name rhomboidea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hyptis rhomboidea has rhomboid-shaped leaves that are typically opposite, sessile, and possess entire or slightly serrated margins. The plant exhibits a woody or subshrubby growth habit with stems that may show varying degrees of branching. Its inflorescences consist of small, yellow capitula arranged in terminal clusters. The individual florets are characteristic of the Asteraceae family, featuring tubular disc florets. The overall foliage texture is often somewhat thickened or leathery depending on the specific environmental conditions.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds campesterool, kaempferol, quercetin, rosmarinic acid, and squalene. These specific bioactive molecules work together to provide various therapeutic benefits to the human body. For instance, the flavonoids kaempferol and quercetin are well-known for their potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Rosmarinic acid contributes additional protection against oxidative stress, while squalene supports skin health and immune function. Additionally, the presence of campesterool further enhances the plant's complex chemical profile for medicinal use.

As discussed in a study, Hyptis rhomboidea is used in South China to treat liver disease, ulcers, and edema. The 95% ethanol extract of the plant showed inhibitory effects on 5 human cancer cell lines, including HL-60, A549, SMMC-7721, MDA-MB-231, and SW480. These inhibitory effects resulted in IC50 values ranging from 15.8 to 40.0 μg/mL. Analysis of the crude extract identified a total of 64 compounds using UPLC-QTOF-MS and GC-MS methods. Among these, kaempferol, quercetin, rosmarinic acid, squalene, and campesteroL were found to be abundant metabolites.
