Cynanchum bungei

What's the taxonomical classification of Cynanchum bungei?

Cynanchum bungei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Gentianaales. The plant is further categorized into the family Apocynaceae, which places it within the genus Cynanchum, ultimately identifying it as the specific species Cynanchum bungei.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains medicinal compounds including cynbungenins A-K, cynbungenins L-O, baishouwubenzophenone, and pregnane C21-steroids. These specific chemical constituents contribute to the various therapeutic properties attributed to the species. The presence of cynbungenins A-K highlights a diverse group of bioactive molecules within the plant tissue. Additionally, the inclusion of cynbungenins L-O and baishouwubenzophenone further defines its unique chemical profile. The pregnane C21-steroids also play a significant role in the plant's pharmacological potential.

As discussed in a study, the acid hydrolysatet of Cynanchum bungei roots led to the isolation of eleven undescribed steroids, specifically cynbungenins A-K (1-11), and seven previously described analogues (12-18). Within these compounds, 1 and 2 represent the first androstane-type steroids found in the genus, while 3-6 and 12 are characterized as pregnane-type steroids with a rare 8,14-seco-steroid core. In cytotoxic activity assays, compound 16 showed the strongest effect against MCF-7, HCT-116, HeLa, and HepG2 cancer cell lines. This specific compound yielded IC50 values ranging from 9.98-16.42 μM. Furthermore, research indicated that compound 16 induced apoptosis and cell cycle arrest in the G0/G1 phase toward HepG2 cells in a dose-dependent manner.
