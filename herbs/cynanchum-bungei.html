What side-effects this plant can have?

Cynanchum bungei can cause severe organ failure, including acute kidney injury, liver damage, and death due to its high concentration of cardiac glycosides. These toxic compounds interfere with the sodium-potassium pump in cells, leading to dangerous electrolyte imbalances. Such disruptions can trigger life-threatening cardiac arrhythmias and sudden heart failure. Patients may also experience gastrointestinal distress, such as vomiting and diarrhea, as the body reacts to the toxins. Because the toxicity is often rapid and profound, medical emergencies frequently arise shortly after ingestion.
