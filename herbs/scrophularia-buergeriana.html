Buerger's Scrophularia

Scrophularia buergeriana

Buerger's Scrophularia (Scrophularia buergeriana) is a perennial herb native to East Asia, known for its traditional use in treating conditions such as diabetes and cardiovascular disorders.

Botanical Identification

Buerger's Scrophularia, scientifically known as Scrophularia buergeriana, belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae. It is also called Buerger's weed in some regions. This perennial herb is native to East Asia, particularly China. It has a hairy stem, lance-shaped leaves, and violet flowers. It is sometimes referred to as Scrophularia japonica in older herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Lamiales
  • Order: Scrophulariales
  • Family: Scrophulariaceae
  • Genus: Scrophularia
  • Species: Scrophularia buergeriana

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory conditions and inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory properties.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and as a general tonic due to its bitter compounds.

Extract: Used for skin conditions and as an antimicrobial agent due to its concentrated active compounds.

Safety Profile

Buerger's Scrophularia is generally considered safe when used appropriately. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to limited safety data. Individuals with known allergies to plants in the Scrophulariaceae family should also avoid its use.
