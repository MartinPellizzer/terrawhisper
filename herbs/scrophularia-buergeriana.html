Scientific and Botanical Profile

Buerger's Scrophularia, with botanical name Scrophularia buergeriana, is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family and is known by various common names including Chinese Scrophularia, Chinese Foxglove, and Buerger's Foxglove.

Native to regions such as China, Korea, Japan, Mongolia, the Russian Far East, Tibet, Central Asia, and Southeast Asia, this plant is commonly referred to as Buerger's Root, Buerger's Bugloss, and Buerger's Weed. Morphologically, it is a perennial herb with erect, hairy stems that bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces clusters of tubular, pink or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months.

It is also known as Buerger's Violet, Common Scrophularia, and Kobushi, and is valued in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.

Buerger's Scrophularia was used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries to treat a variety of ailments, particularly those related to inflammation, fever, and skin conditions.

Its roots and leaves were often prepared as decoctions or poultices to alleviate symptoms of respiratory and digestive disorders, reflecting its deep integration into ancient healing practices. Safety, Side Effects and Contraindications

Scrophularia buergeriana can be used as a medicinal plant with potential therapeutic benefits, but its safety profile requires careful consideration.

While generally considered safe when used in recommended doses, it may cause side effects such as gastrointestinal discomfort, nausea, and allergic reactions in some individuals. It is important to note that scrophularia buergeriana may interact with certain medications, particularly those affecting the cardiovascular or nervous systems, and should be avoided with anticoagulants or antiplatelet drugs due to potential increased bleeding risk. Special populations, including pregnant and breastfeeding women, should avoid its use due to insufficient safety data, and individuals with chronic illnesses should consult a healthcare provider before use.

To ensure safe use, it is advisable to consult a qualified healthcare professional, follow recommended dosages, and monitor for any adverse reactions during treatment.

Growing, Harvesting and Storage

Scrophularia buergeriana grows best in well-drained, loamy soil with a slightly acidic to neutral pH, in full sun to partial shade, and requires consistent moisture without becoming waterlogged.

It thrives in temperate climates and benefits from regular watering during dry periods, though it can tolerate short periods of drought once established. To ensure optimal growth, it should be planted in early spring after the last frost, and mulching around the base helps retain soil moisture and suppress weeds. Harvesting is best done in late summer to early autumn when the leaves are fully developed and the roots are most potent, using a trowel to gently dig up the plant while avoiding damage to the roots.

After harvesting, the roots should be cleaned, cut into small pieces, and dried in a cool, dark, well-ventilated area before storing in airtight containers to preserve their medicinal potency and extend their shelf life.
