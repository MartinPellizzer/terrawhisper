Scientific and Botanical Profile

Buerger's Scrophularia, with botanical name Scrophularia buergeriana, is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family, commonly known as the figwort family.

Native to East Asia, particularly China, Japan, and Korea, this plant is also referred to as Chinese Scrophularia, Chinese Foxglove, Japanese Scrophularia, Scrophularia Buergeriana, Buerger's Foxglove, Buerger's Root, and Buerger's Weed. Morphologically, it is an herbaceous perennial with upright, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often gland-dotted.

The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in clusters, typically in shades of violet or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

Growing, Harvesting and Storage

Scrophularia buergeriana grows best in well-drained, loamy soil with a slightly acidic to neutral pH, in full sun to partial shade, and requires consistent moisture without becoming waterlogged.

It thrives in temperate climates and should be planted in early spring after the last frost, with spacing of about 30 cm between plants to allow for proper air circulation. Regular watering during the growing season is essential, but the soil should be allowed to dry slightly between waterings to prevent root rot. For optimal care, mulching around the base of the plant helps retain moisture and suppress weeds, while pruning dead or damaged foliage in early spring promotes healthy growth. Harvesting should occur in late summer to early fall when the leaves are fully developed and the roots are mature, using a spade or trowel to carefully dig up the plant, ensuring minimal damage to the roots.

After harvesting, the roots should be thoroughly washed, cut into small pieces, and dried in a cool, dark, well-ventilated area to preserve potency, or stored in airtight containers in a refrigerator for longer shelf life.
