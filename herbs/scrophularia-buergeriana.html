What is Buerger'S Scrophularia (Scrophularia Buergeriana)?

Buerger's Scrophularia, also known as Scrophularia buergeriana, is a perennial herb native to East Asia, characterized by its tall, erect stems, lance-shaped leaves, and clusters of pale purple to white flowers, commonly used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Buerger's Scrophularia is Scrophularia buergeriana. This plant belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Lamiales, order Scrophulariales, family Scrophulariaceae, genus Scrophularia, and species Scrophularia buergeriana. It is commonly known as Buerger's Scrophularia in China and as Japanese Scrophularia in Japan. The synonyms for this plant include Scrophularia japonica and Scrophularia buergeriana var. japonica.

Plant Description and Morphology

Buerger's Scrophularia is a perennial herb that grows to a height of 30-80 cm, with a slender, upright stem that is often branched towards the top.

Its leaves are lanceolate to ovate, with a serrated margin and a pointed tip, arranged alternately along the stem. The flowers are clustered in spikes at the ends of the branches, featuring tubular corollas with five lobes, typically in shades of pink to purple. The root system consists of a thick, fleshy rhizome that is often branched and covered in a corky outer layer.

The plant has a mild, slightly bitter scent, with rough, hairy stems and leaves that are slightly woolly in texture.
