      What are the herbal preparations of Ampelopsis megalophylla?

      Ampelopsis megalophylla is prepared as decoctions, infusions, and topical poultices using the plant's leaves and stems. These liquid preparations are typically extracted by boiling the dried botanical material in water to release its active compounds. Infusions are often used for milder therapeutic applications where a shorter steeping time is required.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Ampelopsis megalophylla can cause skin irritation, localized swelling, and potential allergic reactions upon direct contact with the plant's tissues. The sap or foliage may trigger contact dermatitis, characterized by redness and itching in sensitive individuals. Some people might experience a stinging sensation if the plant is handled without protective gear. There is also a risk of mild inflammation if the juices come into contact with the eyes or mucous membranes. Careful avoidance of skin contact is recommended to prevent these adverse physical responses.
