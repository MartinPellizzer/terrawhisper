What is Pavonia brasiliensis?

Pavonia brasiliensis is a species of flowering plant in the family Malvaceae, native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or herbaceous plant that grows up to 1 meter in height, with ovate to lanceolate leaves that are 2-5 centimeters long and have a cuneate base. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that are 2-5 centimeters in diameter, with five petals and a prominent staminal column.

The fruits are schizocarps, consisting of 5-10 achenes, each with a 1-2 centimeter long, hairy, and winged cypsela.
