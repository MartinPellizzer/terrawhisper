Trollius altaicus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Trollius altaicus has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by basal leaves that are deeply palmately lobed and stem leaves that are smaller and lanceolate. The plant features erect, unbranched stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. Its most distinctive morphological feature is the globular, cup-shaped flower which is composed of numerous bright yellow, petaloid sepals. These inflorescences are often borne singly or in small groups at the terminal ends of the stems. The root system consists of a stout, creeping rhizome that allows the plant to spread within its montane habitat.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central and East Asia, specifically spanning across Siberia, Mongolia, Kazakhstan, and northern China. It primarily inhabits high-altitude alpine and subalpine meadows where soil remains moist. Its distribution is closely tied to the Altai and Sayan mountain systems. Within these habitats, the species thrives in rocky slopes and damp grasslands. The plant's presence is often limited to specific elevations that support its specialized environmental requirements.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Trollius altaicus can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if ingested in large amounts due to its chemical constituents. The plant contains protoanemonin, a substance known to cause blistering and inflammation upon contact with sensitive skin. Ingestion may lead to symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. Because the concentration of these irritants can vary, even small doses might provoke adverse reactions in some individuals. It is generally advised to avoid direct contact and consumption of this species to prevent these complications.
