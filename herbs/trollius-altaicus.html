Trollius altaicus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Ranunculales and into the family Ranunculaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Trollius, and its unique species designation, altaicus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Trollius altaicus has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by basal leaves that are deeply palmately lobed and stem leaves that are smaller and lanceolate. Its most distinctive morphological feature is the globular, cup-shaped flower which is composed of numerous bright yellow, petaloid sepals. These inflorescences are often borne singly or in small groups at the terminal ends of the stems.
