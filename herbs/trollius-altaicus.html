Trollius altaicus

What's the taxonomical classification of Trollius altaicus?

Trollius altaicus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Ranunculales and into the family Ranunculaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Trollius, and its unique species designation, altaicus.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Trollius altaicus contains various bioactive compounds including tannins, flavonoids, and saponins that contribute to its pharmacological potential. What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various inflammatory conditions, skin irritations, and digestive ailments through its traditional medicinal applications. It is frequently employed in folk medicine to alleviate symptoms of rheumatism and joint discomfort. Some practitioners also utilize its properties to address dermatological issues such as eczema or minor skin wounds. Additionally, extracts from the plant have been historically linked to managing internal issues like gastrointestinal distress. These uses rely on the specific chemical compounds found within its roots and foliage.
