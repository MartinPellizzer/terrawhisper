Aralia chinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Aralia chinensis?

Aralia chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Apiales and into the family Araliaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Aralia, and its unique species designation, chinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aralia chinensis has a woody, perennial habit characterized by erect stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters and bear large, tripinnate compound leaves. Each leaf consists of multiple leaflets that are typically ovate or lanceolate with serrated margins. The plant produces terminal panicles of small, creamy-white flowers that emerge in clusters during the summer months. These blossoms eventually transition into small, fleshy, spherical drupes that turn from green to a deep red or black when ripe. The stem structure often features a slightly woody texture with distinct nodes where the foliage emerges.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to East Asia, specifically spanning across China, Japan, Korea, Taiwan, and parts of Southeast Asia. It thrives primarily in subtropical and temperate climates where moisture is relatively consistent. In its natural habitats, it is frequently found growing in forest edges, thickets, and shaded undergrowth. Due to its hardiness, it has also been introduced to other regions where it can persist in similar ecological niches. Its distribution is largely dictated by the availability of filtered sunlight and well-drained soil environments.

What are the pharmacological activities of Aralia chinensis?

This plant has diverse pharmacological activities including anti-oxidative, antivirus, cardioprotective, cell-protective, and immunity boosting properties. Its antioxidant components help neutralize harmful free radicals to prevent oxidative stress within the body. The specimen also demonstrates significant antiviral capabilities that can assist in inhibiting the replication of certain pathogens. Furthermore, its ability to provide cardioprotection and cell-protective effects helps maintain the integrity of vital organs and cellular structures. These combined actions ultimately work to support the body through effective immunity boosting mechanisms.

As discussed in a study, Elatoside C (EsC) is a natural saponin isolated from Longya Aralia chinensis L. that possesses anti-oxidative activity. This compound protects against ox-LDL-induced HUVECs injury by increasing the number of autophagosomes and modulating the expression of autophagy related proteins. The mechanism involves EsC significantly increasing the nuclear FoxO1 expression level to induce autophagy. Due to these pharmacological activities, EsC is considered a potential drug for the treatment of atherosclerosis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains total saponins, congmujingnosiides B-G, and oleanolic acid. These specific medicinal compounds contribute to the plant's diverse pharmacological profile. The total saponins are known for their ability to influence various biological processes within the body. Additionally, the presence of congmujingnosiides B-G provides unique chemical properties often studied for therapeutic potential. Oleanolic acid further enhances the medicinal value through its recognized biological activities.

As discussed in a study, the Chinese traditional herb Longya Aralia chinensis L. is used in Asian countries for treating numerous diseases, enhancing energy and boosting immunity. The main components extracted from this plant are the total saponins of Aralia elata (Miq.) Seem., referred to as TAS. These TAS components exert cytoprotective effects by inhibiting apoptosis and mitochondrial membrane potential depolarisation. They also regulate inflammatory factors including IL-6, MCP-1, and VCAM-1 while suppressing NF-κB transcription. This process is related to the activation of the phosphoinositiide 3-kinase (PI3K)/Akt signalling pathway.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating cardiovascular diseases, specifically addressing conditions like angina pectoris and atherosclerosis. Research indicates that its extracts can mitigate the effects of H2O2-induced myocardial cell injury in laboratory settings. It also shows potential in protecting the heart against myocardial ischemia/reperfusion injury. By targeting these specific issues, the plant provides a multifaceted approach to managing heart health.
