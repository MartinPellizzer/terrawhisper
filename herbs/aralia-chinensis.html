Aralia chinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Aralia chinensis?

Aralia chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Apiales and into the family Araliaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Aralia, and its unique species designation, chinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aralia chinensis has a woody, perennial habit characterized by erect stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters and bear large, tripinnate compound leaves. Each leaf consists of multiple leaflets that are typically ovate or lanceolate with serrated margins. The plant produces terminal panicles of small, creamy-white flowers that emerge in clusters during the summer months. These blossoms eventually transition into small, fleshy, spherical drupes that turn from green to a deep red or black when ripe. The stem structure often features a slightly woody texture with distinct nodes where the foliage emerges.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to East Asia, specifically spanning across China, Japan, Korea, Taiwan, and parts of Southeast Asia. It thrives primarily in subtropical and temperate climates where moisture is relatively consistent. In its natural habitats, it is frequently found growing in forest edges, thickets, and shaded undergrowth. Due to its hardiness, it has also been introduced to other regions where it can persist in similar ecological niches. Its distribution is largely dictated by the availability of filtered sunlight and well-drained soil environments.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires well-draining, humus-rich soil and a location that provides bright, indirect sunlight to thrive.

It flourishes best in humid environments, so frequent misting or a pebble tray can help maintain necessary moisture levels. Regular watering is essential to keep the soil consistently moist without allowing it to become waterlogged. During the growing season, applying a balanced liquid fertilizer once a month will encourage lush, green foliage.

Monitoring for pests like spider mites is important to ensure the plant remains healthy and vibrant.

What are the pharmacological activities of Aralia chinensis?

This plant has diverse pharmacological activities including anti-oxidative, antivirus, cardioprotection, cell-protective effects, and immunity boosting capabilities. Its chemical constituents work to neutralize free radicals, thereby providing significant anti-oxidative benefits to the body. The plant also demonstrates potent antivirus properties that help defend against various viral pathogens. Furthermore, its ability to offer cardioprotection and cell-protective effects assists in maintaining organ health and structural integrity. Finally, these combined mechanisms contribute to immunity boosting by strengthening the overall biological defense system.

According to a study published by "Biochimica et biophysica acta. Molecular basis of disease", the natural saponin Elatoside C (EsC) isolated from Longya Aralia chinensis L. possesses anti-oxidative activity. EsC pretreatment reduced ox-LDL-induced HUVECs oxidative injury and increased the number of autophagosomes. This substance modulated the expression of autophagy related proteins and significantly increased nuclear FoxO1 expression level. By inducing autophagy via increasing FoxO1 expression level, EsC attenuated ox-LDL-induced HUVECs injury. Consequently, EsC is considered a potential drug for the treatment of atherosclerosis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains medicinal compounds including total saponins, congmujingsinosides B-G, and oleanolic acid. These bioactive constituents are primarily responsible for the various pharmacological activities observed in the species. The total saponins contribute to the plant's diverse biological profile, which is often studied for therapeutic potential. Specifically, the presence of congmujingsinosides B-G offers unique chemical properties that researchers analyze for drug development. Additionally, the oleanolic acid serves as a significant triterpenoid component within its chemical structure.

According to a study published by "International journal of molecular sciences", the total saponins of Aralia chinensis L. are the main components extracted from the Chinese traditional herb Longya. This plant is used as a folk medicine in Asian countries for treating numerous diseases, enhancing energy, and boosting immunity. The total saponins exert cytoprotective effects by inhibiting TNF-α-triggered human umbilical vein endothelial cell (HUVEC) apoptosis and mitochondrial membrane potential depolarisation. These compounds also regulate inflammatory factors including IL-6, MCP-1, and VCAM-1 while suppressing NF-κB transcription. The protective effects of these saponins are related to the activation of the phosphoinositiide 3-kinase (PI3K)/Akt signalling pathway.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating angina pectoris, atherosclerosis, cardiovascular diseases, coronary heart disease, and rheumatism. It is frequently utilized in traditional medicine to improve blood circulation and support heart function. The medicinal properties of the plant help manage various complications related to the vascular system. Additionally, it is applied to alleviate the painful symptoms associated with inflammatory joint conditions like rheumatism. By targeting these diverse ailments, the plant serves as a versatile remedy for both circulatory and musculoskeletal issues.

According to a study published by "Frontiers in pharmacology", Aralia chinensis L. is used in clinical preparations to treat patients with coronary heart disease, angina pectoris, and other cardiovascular diseases. The plant contains saponins which are the main active substances used for cardioprotection. Specifically, the highly active saponin components of Aralia chinensis L. are oleanolic acid structures. These components possess significant regulatory effects on calcium homeostasis. Such medicinal applications aim to provide a broad prospect for developing low-toxicity anti-MIRI drugs.
