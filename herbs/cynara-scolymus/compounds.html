Artichoke (Cynara scolymus) Compounds

The primary compounds in artichoke include flavonoids, which act as antioxidants, and phenolic acids, known for their anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarin contributes to the plant's aromatic qualities, while tannins provide astringent effects. Terpenoids and saponins support digestive health, and glycosides, along with essential oils, contribute to its therapeutic profile. Anthocyanins and alkaloids add to its complex pharmacological activity.

Artichoke (Cynara scolymus) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Artichoke (Cynara scolymus). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting digestive health and reducing inflammation. These compounds are responsible for the plant's therapeutic effects in herbal medicine.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Artichoke (Cynara scolymus). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting digestive health and reducing inflammation. This compound is found in high concentrations in the leaves and heart of the artichoke.

3. Coumarin

Coumarin is a bioactive compound found in Artichoke (Cynara scolymus). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antithrombotic properties. Coumarin may also support cardiovascular health by influencing blood clotting mechanisms. Its presence in Artichoke highlights the plant's potential therapeutic applications in herbal medicine.

4. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Artichoke (Cynara scolymus). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health by reducing inflammation and strengthening mucous membranes. Tannins in artichoke are also associated with antioxidant and antimicrobial effects.

5. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Artichoke (Cynara scolymus). They contribute to the plant's medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. These compounds are often responsible for the plant's therapeutic benefits in herbal medicine.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Artichoke (Cynara scolymus). It contributes to the plant's digestive and anti-inflammatory properties by enhancing bile secretion and reducing cholesterol levels. Saponin also supports liver function and may aid in detoxification processes.

7. Glycoside

Glycosides in Artichoke (Cynara scolymus) are naturally occurring compounds that contribute to its medicinal properties. They are often associated with digestive support and may help in reducing inflammation. These compounds are part of the plant's complex phytochemical profile, making it valuable in herbal medicine.

8. Essential oil

Essential oil from Artichoke (Cynara scolymus) contains volatile compounds like sesquiterpenes and monoterpene hydrocarbons. These compounds contribute to the plant's aromatic profile and may possess antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Artichoke (Cynara scolymus). It acts as an antioxidant, helping to neutralize free radicals in the body. This compound contributes to the plant's potential health benefits, including supporting cardiovascular health and reducing inflammation.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Artichoke (Cynara scolymus). It contributes to the plant's pharmacological properties, though its specific role and concentration vary. Alkaloids in artichoke may support digestive health and have mild anti-inflammatory effects.
