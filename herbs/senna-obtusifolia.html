Senna

Senna obtusifolia

Senna (Senna obtusifolia) is a flowering plant native to Africa and widely used in traditional medicine for its laxative properties, particularly in treating constipation and promoting digestive health.

Botanical Identification

Senna obtusifolia is a species of flowering plant in the Fabaceae family. It is also known as broad-leaved senna, Indian senna, or Kudzu in some regions. An annual herb native to tropical and subtropical regions, it has palmate leaves and yellow flowers. Synonyms include Senna tora and Senna occidentalis in some herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Fabales
  • Order: Fabales
  • Family: Fabaceae
  • Genus: Senna
  • Species: Senna obtusifolia

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for digestive issues and as a mild laxative.

Decoction: Used for treating skin conditions and as a detoxifying agent.

Poultice: Used for topical application to reduce inflammation and treat wounds.

Safety Profile

Senna (Senna obtusifolia) is generally safe when used in recommended doses for short-term relief of constipation. However, long-term use may lead to dependency or electrolyte imbalances. It is contraindicated in individuals with intestinal obstruction or a history of chronic constipation.
