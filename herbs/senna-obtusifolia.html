Senna (Senna obtusifolia)

Senna (Senna obtusifolia) is a member of the Fabaceae family, native to Africa, Asia, and Australia. Traditionally, its leaves, seeds, and fruits have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its bitter, diuretic, and anti-inflammatory actions, and has a long history of use in african traditional medicine, european herbal medicine, and ayurvedic medicine.

Flowers
Flowers yellow, arranged in racemes, actinomorphic, with five petals, two of which are larger and adorned with dark veins.
Stems
Erect, branched, hairy, with opposite, leafy nodes and persistent stipules.
