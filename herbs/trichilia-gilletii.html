Trichilia gilletii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Trichilia gilletii has pinnately compound leaves with several pairs of oblong to elliptical leaflets that possess entire margins and acute tips. The foliage is characterized by a lustrous, dark green upper surface and a slightly paler underside. Its stems exhibit a woody texture typical of tropical trees, often displaying a smooth to slightly fissured bark. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles that eventually develop into fleshy fruits. These fruits are typically capsules that split to reveal seeds often encased in a colorful aril.

Within these habitats, it typically thrives in dense vegetation where moisture levels remain consistently high. What are the therapeutic actions of Trichilia gilletii?

Trichilia gilletii has diverse medicinal properties including anthelmintic, antipyretic, antimalarial, anti-inflammatory, astringent, and bitter therapeutic actions. What side-effects this plant can have?

Trichilia gilletii can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or gastrointestinal distress if the plant parts are used improperly or consumed in unsafe quantities. While much of its use is rooted in traditional medicine, the specific toxicological profile of its chemical constituents is not fully mapped for human consumption. Some users may experience localized dermatitis or redness upon contact with the sap or crushed leaves. Internal ingestion without controlled preparation could potentially lead to stomach cramps or nausea. Careful attention must be paid to dosage and preparation methods to avoid these adverse biological responses.
