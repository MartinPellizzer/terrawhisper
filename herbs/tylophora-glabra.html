Tylophora glabra

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tylophora glabra has climbing stems that are slender, twining, and characterized by a glabrous surface. The leaves are oppositely arranged, ovate to lanceolate in shape, and possess entire margins with acute tips. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils and typically feature a pentamerous structure. The plant produces slender, linear follicles that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds equipped with a tuft of hairs. These morphological features collectively define its identity as a perennial herbaceous climber.
