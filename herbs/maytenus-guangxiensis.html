Sha contains two new triterpene compounds identified as 3β, 7β-dihydroxyolean-12-en-11-one (1) and 3β-O-acetyl-7β-hydroxyolean-12-en-11-one (2). The stems and leaves of the plant also yielded four known triterpene compounds, specifically 3β-hydroxyolean-12-en-11-one (3), β-amyrin (4), lup-20(29)-ene-1β, 3β-diol (5), and 1β, 3β-dihydroxy-urs-9(11)-12-diene (6). Compounds 3-6 were isolated from Maytenus guangxiensis for the first time. All compounds 1-6 were evaluated for antiproliferative activities against 4 human cancer cell lines, including Eca-109, PANC-1, EJ, and HeLa. The results indicated that compounds 1-6 displayed a certain degree of inhibitory effects against the proliferation of these various cell lines.
