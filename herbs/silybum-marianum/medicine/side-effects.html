1. Increases risk of rash

Silybum marianum causes allergic reactions.

This may occur due to the presence of sesquiterpene lactones and flavonoids in the plant, which can trigger an immune response and lead to symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.

Additionally, some individuals may be sensitive to the plant's saponins, which can cause an allergic reaction.

4. Increases risk of rash

Silybum marianum interferes with warfarin by altering its metabolism in the liver.

The flavonoids and saponins present in Silybum marianum are known to inhibit the activity of cytochrome P450 enzymes, which are responsible for breaking down warfarin. This can lead to an increase in warfarin levels in the blood, potentially causing excessive bleeding or bruising.

As a result, individuals taking warfarin should avoid using Silybum marianum concurrently.
