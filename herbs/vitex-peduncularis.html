Vitex peduncularis

What's the taxonomical classification of Vitex peduncularis?

Vitex peduncularis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Lamiales. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the family Lamiaceae, where it is placed within the genus Vitex, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species peduncularis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vitex peduncularis has opposite, decussate leaves that are typically lanceolate to oblong in shape with entire or slightly serrated margins and a pubescent underside. The plant exhibits a woody structure characterized by slender stems and a branching habit common to many shrubs in the Lamiaceae family. Its reproductive morphology features small, tubular flowers that are borne on long, slender pedicels, which gives the species its specific epithet. These inflorescences are often arranged in terminal panicles or clusters that emerge from the axils of the foliage. The resulting fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that serves as a primary component of its morphological profile.
