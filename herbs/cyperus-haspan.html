Cyperus haspan

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cyperus haspan has a perennial growth habit characterized by a spreading rhizomatous root system and a tufted arrangement of long, linear, bright green leaves that emerge from a central base. The foliage is typically narrow and can reach considerable lengths, often tapering to a fine point at the apex. Its inflorescence consists of dense, terminal umbel-like clusters composed of multiple spikelets that change color as they mature. These spikelets are supported by slender, triangular stems which are a diagnostic feature of the Cyperaceae family. The overall structure allows the plant to form thick, stabilizing mats in wetland or moist soil environments.
