Trichosanthes villosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Trichosanthes villosa has a climbing or prostrate herbaceous habit characterized by slender, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets. The leaves feature prominent venation and a dense covering of soft, villous hairs on both the upper and lower surfaces. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that typically appear in clusters along the stem. The fruit is a fleshy berry that develops from the ovary after successful pollination. These morphological traits, particularly the hairy texture, serve as defining characteristics for distinguishing this species within its genus.
