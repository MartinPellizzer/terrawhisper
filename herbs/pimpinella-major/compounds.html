Wild Angelica (Pimpinella major) Compounds

Wild Angelica contains several key compounds, including coumarin, which has anticoagulant properties, and flavonoids, known for their antioxidant effects. Phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory properties, while essential oils provide aromatic and antimicrobial benefits. These compounds work together to support its traditional uses in herbal medicine.

Wild Angelica (Pimpinella major) Monograph

1. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Wild Angelica (Pimpinella major). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly its anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory effects. Coumarin is also responsible for the plant's characteristic aroma and has been studied for its potential therapeutic applications.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Wild Angelica (Pimpinella major) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help reduce oxidative stress and support immune function. They are commonly found in the plant's root and stem. Flavonoids may enhance the plant's traditional uses in herbal medicine.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid in Wild Angelica (Pimpinella major) contributes to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound helps reduce oxidative stress and supports the plant's traditional use in treating digestive and respiratory conditions. It also plays a role in the plant's overall therapeutic potential.

4. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound in Wild Angelica (Pimpinella major). It contains volatile aromatic compounds like limonene and pinene, which contribute to its medicinal properties. This oil is used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and digestive benefits.

5. Tannin

Tannin in Wild Angelica (Pimpinella major) contributes to its astringent properties and aids in reducing inflammation. It supports the plant's traditional use in treating digestive issues and skin conditions. Tannins also help preserve the plant's active components during preparation.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Wild Angelica (Pimpinella major). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by supporting digestive and anti-inflammatory effects. Glycosides in Wild Angelica are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their therapeutic potential.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Wild Angelica (Pimpinella major) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. They support the plant's traditional use in treating digestive and respiratory ailments. Terpenoids are key to the plant's therapeutic profile.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Wild Angelica (Pimpinella major). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. Saponins help in cleansing the respiratory system and may support immune function. They are commonly used in herbal remedies for respiratory conditions.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Wild Angelica (Pimpinella major). It contributes to the plant's pharmacological properties, though its specific role and effects require further research. Alkaloids are typically nitrogen-containing compounds that can have various therapeutic or toxic effects.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Wild Angelica (Pimpinella major). It contributes to the plant's vibrant color and has antioxidant properties that may support overall health. This compound is part of the complex mixture of bioactive substances in Wild Angelica that contribute to its traditional medicinal uses.
