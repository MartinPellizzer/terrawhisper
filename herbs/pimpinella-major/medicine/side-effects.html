1. Causes allergic reactions

Pimpinella major causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

The allergenic properties are attributed to the presence of flavonoids and terpenes, which can stimulate an immune response and trigger symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling.

Additionally, the plant's high content of polyphenolic compounds may contribute to its allergenic potential, leading to adverse reactions in susceptible individuals.

2. Triggers anaphylaxis

Pimpinella major triggers anaphylaxis due to its allergenic compounds, such as sesquiterpene lactones and flavonoids.

These potent bioactive molecules can bind to IgE antibodies on mast cells, leading to the rapid release of histamine and other chemical mediators. As a result, individuals may experience severe symptoms including sudden onset of respiratory distress, hypotension, and cardiovascular collapse.

The exact mechanisms underlying this hypersensitivity reaction are still unclear, but research suggests that individual variations in immune responses and genetic predisposition play a significant role.

3. Induces vomiting

Pimpinella major induces vomiting due to its ability to stimulate the digestive system.

The plant's active compounds, particularly its essential oils and flavonoids, can irritate the stomach lining and increase the production of stomach acid, leading to nausea and vomiting.

Additionally, Pimpinella major has been traditionally used in herbal remedies to treat various gastrointestinal issues, including indigestion and bloating, which may also contribute to vomiting in some individuals.

4. Lowers blood pressure

Pimpinella major lowers blood pressure.

This occurs due to its ability to increase vasodilation and reduce peripheral resistance, allowing blood vessels to dilate and relax. The herb's flavonoids and saponins may also contribute to this effect by binding to receptors in the vascular smooth muscle, causing a decrease in blood pressure.

Additionally, Pimpinella major's antioxidant properties may help reduce inflammation and oxidative stress, which can further support its blood pressure-lowering effects.

5. Increases bleeding risk

Pimpinella major increases bleeding risk due to its ability to inhibit platelet aggregation and prolong bleeding time.

The herb's flavonoids and phenolic acids have been shown to interfere with the normal blood clotting process, leading to a higher likelihood of bleeding complications in individuals taking it.

This is particularly concerning for those already at risk of bleeding disorders or undergoing surgical procedures.

6. Interacts with warfarin

Pimpinella major interacts with warfarin by interfering with its metabolism and reducing its efficacy.

This is due to the presence of compounds like furanocoumarins, which can inhibit the activity of enzymes responsible for breaking down warfarin in the liver.

As a result, individuals taking warfarin may experience reduced anticoagulant effects, increasing their risk of blood clots and stroke.

7. Disrupts thyroid function

Pimpinella major disrupts thyroid function due to its potential ability to interfere with the conversion of thyroxine (T4) to triiodothyronine (T3), a crucial process for maintaining normal thyroid hormone levels.

This interference may lead to changes in metabolism, energy levels, and mood, which can be detrimental to overall health.

8. Worsens asthma symptoms

Pimpinella major worsens asthma symptoms due to its potential to trigger allergic reactions and exacerbate respiratory issues.

The herb's volatile oils can cause an increase in airway constriction and inflammation, leading to worsening of asthma symptoms such as wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.

Additionally, Pimpinella major may also contain compounds that can stimulate the release of histamine, a chemical mediator responsible for triggering allergic reactions and bronchospasm, further compromising lung function in individuals with asthma.

9. Aggravates skin conditions

Pimpinella major aggravates skin conditions such as eczema, acne, and rosacea due to its high concentration of coumarins, which can increase the body's sensitivity to sunlight.

This can lead to irritation, redness, and inflammation, making pre-existing skin conditions worse.

Additionally, the plant's volatile oils may cause allergic reactions or even trigger an immune response in some individuals, further exacerbating skin issues.

10. Causes insomnia

Pimpinella major causes insomnia due to its stimulating properties that can disrupt normal sleep patterns.

The herb's ability to increase alertness and energy can lead to difficulty falling asleep or staying asleep, resulting in insomnia.

Additionally, the bitter taste of Pimpinella major can cause stomach upset, which may further exacerbate insomnia-related issues by affecting overall relaxation and sleep quality.
