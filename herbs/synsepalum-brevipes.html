Synsepalum brevipes

What's the taxonomical classification of Synsepalum brevipes?

Synsepalum brevipes belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Ericales. This plant is further categorized into the family Sapotaeciae, within which it is placed in the genus Synsepalum. Finally, its specific identification is completed by its species name, brevipes.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Synsepalum brevipes contains bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, saponins, tannins, and various glycosides. What are the therapeutic actions of Synsepalum brevipes?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, inflammatory conditions, and various digestive ailments. The extracts from its leaves and bark are often applied topically to soothe irritation and promote healing of the skin. In some traditional practices, decoctions of the plant are ingested to alleviate stomach discomfort and gastrointestinal distress. These medicinal applications rely on the phytochemical properties inherent in the plant's chemical structure. Local healers frequently utilize these specific parts of the species to manage a range of minor physical ailments.
