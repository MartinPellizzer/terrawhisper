Cynanchum chinense

What's the taxonomical classification of Cynanchum chinense?

Cynanchum chinense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Gentianaales. This plant is a member of the family Apocynaceae, falling under the genus Cynanchum, with the specific designation of the species chinense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cynanchum chinense has twining, herbaceous stems that produce ovate to lanceolate leaves with entire margins and a smooth surface texture. The foliage typically exhibits a bright green color and is arranged oppositely along the climbing vines. Small, fragrant flowers emerge in umbel-like cymes, often displaying a pale greenish or yellowish hue. These blossoms possess a complex structure with a characteristic corona that distinguishes the Apocynaceae family. The plant eventually produces elongated, slender follicles that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds cynanchoside A, cynanchoside B, and cynanchoside C. These specific glycosides are known to possess significant pharmacological activities within traditional herbal medicine. Researchers study these molecules to understand their potential effects on cellular processes and overall health. The presence of these compounds contributes to the complex chemical profile that characterizes the species. Understanding these constituents helps scientists explore new therapeutic possibilities for drug development.

As discussed in a study, the aerial part of Cynanchum chinense was investigated to identify its chemical constituents using solvent extraction and column chromatography. The spectral methods identified 3 specific structures within the plant. These identified compounds are 7-O-alpha-L-rhamnopy-ranosyl-kaempferol-3-O-alpha-L-rhamnopyranoside, 7-O-alpha-L-rhamnopy-ranosyl-kaempferol-3-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside, and 7-O-alpha-L-rhamnopyr-anosylkaempferol-3-O-beta-D-glucopyranosyl(1-->2)- beta-D-glucopyranoside. All 3 of these compounds were separated from Cynanchum chinense for the first time.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause adverse reactions including nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, stomach pain, dizziness, and hypotension. Individuals consuming it may experience significant gastrointestinal distress such as cramping or upset stomach. Neurological symptoms like dizziness can also occur during or after use. Furthermore, the chemical constituents may lead to a drop in blood pressure, known as hypotension. Monitoring these physical responses is essential when encountering this species.
