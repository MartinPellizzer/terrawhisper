Duabanga grandiflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Duabanga grandiflora?

Duabanga grandiflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is positioned under the order Myrtales. It is further categorized into the family Lythraceae, specifically falling under the genus Duabanga, with the species name grandiflora.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Duabanga grandiflora contains various bioactive phytochemicals including alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, and tannins that contribute to its therapeutic potential. These organic compounds are primarily studied for their antioxidant and antimicrobial properties in traditional medicine. Research suggests that the plant extracts may exhibit significant anti-inflammatory activity by modulating specific cellular pathways. Additionally, certain secondary metabolites within the plant show promise in pharmacological studies for their potential neuroprotective effects. The complex chemical profile makes this species a subject of interest for developing new natural drug candidates.

What are the therapeutic actions of Duabanga grandiflora?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, stomach aches, and various inflammatory conditions. The leaves and bark contain bioactive compounds that act as antimicrobial agents against common bacteria. People often apply crushed plant parts topically to promote healing in open sores and rashes. Additionally, traditional medicine practitioners prepare infusions from the plant to soothe digestive distress and internal discomfort. These therapeutic properties make it a significant resource in regional ethnobotanical practices.
