Lycium barbarum

What's the taxonomical classification of Lycium barbarum?

Lycium barbarum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this botanical lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its hierarchical descent, the plant is organized under the order Solanales and falls within the family Solanaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Lycium and the species barbarum.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Lycium barbarum contains high concentrations of polysaccharides, specifically beta-glucans, carotenoids like zeaxanthin, and essential vitamins such as A and C. These bioactive compounds are primarily responsible for the plant's potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The presence of zeaxanthin is particularly significant for supporting ocular health and protecting retinal cells from oxidative stress. Additionally, the complex polysaccharides are believed to modulate the immune system and improve metabolic functions. Research also suggests that these chemical constituents may play a role in regulating blood glucose levels and enhancing cellular longevity.

What are the therapeutic actions of Lycium barbarum?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat vision impairment, chronic liver disease, diabetes, and various forms of inflammation. Its high antioxidant content helps mitigate oxidative stress associated with age-related macular degeneration and retinal damage. The berries are frequently utilized in traditional medicine to support immune function and stabilize blood glucose levels in diabetic patients. Additionally, the plant's bioactive compounds may assist in reducing systemic inflammation and protecting hepatic cells from toxin-induced injury. These therapeutic properties make it a significant component in managing metabolic and degenerative health conditions.
