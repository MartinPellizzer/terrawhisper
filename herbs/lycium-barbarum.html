Wolfberry (Lycium barbarum)

Wolfberry (Lycium barbarum) is a member of the Solanaceae family, native to China, Central Asia, and Eastern Europe. Traditionally, its fruits, leaves, and bark have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its tonic, anti-inflammatory, and astringent actions, and has a long history of use in traditional chinese medicine, japanese kampo medicine, and korean traditional medicine.

Perennial herbaceous plant.

Leaves

Flowers
Flowers are actinomorphic, typically yellow to orange in color, arranged in solitary or clustered inflorescences, with five petals and five stamens, and a superior ovary with two locules.
Stems
Woody, branched, with opposite arrangement, and covered in glandular hairs.
