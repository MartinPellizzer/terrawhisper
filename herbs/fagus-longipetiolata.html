Fagus longipetiolata

What's the taxonomical classification of Fagus longipetiolata?

Fagus longipetiolata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant is further categorized under the order Fagales and the family Fagaceae. Finally, it is identified by its genus, Fagus, and its specific species epithet, longipetiolatata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Fagus longipetiolata has elliptical to oblong leaves characterized by long petioles and serrated margins.

These leaves are arranged alternately along the branches and possess distinct pinnate venation. The plant features a woody stem structure typical of the beech family with a developing bark texture. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually mature into nutlets contained within woody cupules.

The overall growth habit reflects a deciduous tree form with spreading canopy architecture.
