Yellow Gentian

Gentiana lutea

Yellow Gentian (Gentiana lutea) is a perennial herb native to alpine regions of Europe, known for its bright yellow flowers and historical use in traditional medicine to support digestive health and improve appetite.

Botanical Identification

Yellow Gentian, scientifically known as Gentiana lutea, is a perennial herb belonging to the Gentianaceae family. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it is commonly called the yellow gentian or gentian. The plant features bright yellow flowers, lance-shaped leaves, and a tuberous root system. It has also been referred to as the "king of gentians" in some herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Liliopsida
  • Order: Gentianales
  • Family: Gentianaceae
  • Genus: Gentiana
  • Species: Gentiana lutea

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory conditions.

Tincture: Used for treating anxiety and respiratory infections.

Infusion: Used for digestive issues and as a general tonic.

Safety Profile

Yellow Gentian is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal upset in some individuals. Long-term use or high doses should be avoided, and it is contraindicated during pregnancy and lactation due to limited safety data.
