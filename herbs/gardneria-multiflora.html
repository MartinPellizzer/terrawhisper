Gardneria multiflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Gardneria multiflora?

Gardneria multiflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Gentianales. This plant is a representative of the family Loganiaceae, specifically situated within the genus Gardneria, and is identified by its unique species name, multiflora.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gardneria multiflora has a woody, perennial growth habit characterized by erect, unbranched stems that reach heights of up to two meters. The leaves are lanceolate to narrowly elliptical, featuring entire margins and an alternate arrangement along the slender branches. Small, white, tubular flowers emerge in dense, terminal clusters or panicles, which give the species its distinctive name. Each flower possesses a long corolla tube that transitions into five spreading lobes. The plant also produces small, globose fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds for dispersal.
