What is Andropogon medius?

Andropogon medius is a perennial, tufted grass that belongs to the family Poaceae.

It has a short rhizome and culms that are 50-100 cm tall, with leaf blades that are flat, linear, and 5-15 mm wide. The ligule is a ciliate membrane and the inflorescence is a raceme or a panicle, 10-30 cm long, with spikelets that are 8-10 mm long and consist of 2-3 fertile florets. The glumes are one-nerved and 6-8 mm long, while the lemma is 3-nerved and 8-10 mm long, with 3 awns that are 2-3 mm long.

The plant is found in tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.
