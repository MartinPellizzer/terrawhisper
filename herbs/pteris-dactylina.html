Pteris dactylina

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pteris dactylina has pinnate fronds characterized by narrow, linear pinnules that are arranged in a somewhat irregular, finger-like fashion along the rachis. The plant typically features a creeping rhizome that serves as the base for its upright or ascending stipes. Each individual leaflet is relatively small and possesses entire or slightly lobed margins. The texture of the foliage is generally thin and papery, reflecting its delicate structural composition. These morphological features collectively distinguish the species from other members of the Pteridaceae family.
