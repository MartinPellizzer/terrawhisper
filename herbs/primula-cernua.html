What's the taxonomical classification of Primula cernua?

Primula cernua belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the order Ericales and the family Primulaceae. Finally, it is situated within the genus Primula, with its specific designation being the species cernua.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Primula cernua has a basal rosette of oblong to lanceolate leaves characterized by crenate margins and a slightly wrinkled surface texture. The stems are relatively short, elevating the inflorescence which consists of a cluster of flowers held atop a central scape. Each individual flower features a salverform corolla with five distinct petals that typically exhibit a yellowish or pale hue. The reproductive structures are centered within the floral tube, surrounded by a ring of delicate lobes. This morphological structure is characteristic of the primrose family, facilitating specific pollinator interactions.
