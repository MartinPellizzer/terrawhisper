Tora Senna

Senna tora

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Tora Senna, scientifically known as Senna tora, is an annual herb belonging to the Fabaceae family. It is also called Indian Senna or Yellow Senna in various regions. Native to tropical and subtropical areas, it features yellow flowers, trifoliate leaves, and a taproot system. It is often confused with other Senna species in herbal texts.

Active Compounds

Tora Senna contains key active compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and coumarins. These compounds work synergistically to provide its medicinal effects, influencing inflammation, oxidative stress, and gut motility. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Tannin
  • Coumarin

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory ailments.

Extract: Used for treating skin infections and inflammation.

Powder: Used for constipation and as a laxative.

Safety Profile

Tora Senna is contraindicated for individuals with gastrointestinal disorders, ulcers, or those taking anticoagulants. It may cause severe diarrhea and electrolyte imbalances. Prolonged use can lead to dependency and toxicity, necessitating strict dosage control and professional supervision.
