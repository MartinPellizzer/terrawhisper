    What is Deparia lasiopteris?

    Deparia lasiopteris is a perennial fern species belonging to the family Aspleniaceae, native to East Asia and Europe.

    It has rhizomes and erect or ascending fronds, with lanceolate to ovate leaflets that are 5-20 cm long and 2-7 cm wide, bearing a serrated margin and an acute tip. The leaves are pinnate, with 6-20 pairs of leaflets, and have a glabrous surface. The sporangia are linear, 2-3 mm long, and are arranged in four rows along the rachis, with a sori pattern of 2-3 pairs per leaflet.

    The species typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in woodland or mountainous areas.
