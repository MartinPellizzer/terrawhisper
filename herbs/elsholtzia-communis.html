Elsholtzia communis

What's the taxonomical classification of Elsholtzia communis?

Elsholtzia communis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Lamiales. The plant is a member of the family Lamiaceae and is specifically categorized under the genus Elsholtzia, with its designation completed by the species epithet communis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Elsholtzia communis has pinnately compound leaves consisting of 7 to 15 pairs of small, oval leaflets that are arranged alternately along the stem.

The tree typically develops a spreading crown with a textured, greyish-brown bark that becomes deeply furrowed as the specimen ages. Small, inconspicuous greenish flowers emerge in clusters during the spring, eventually transitioning into winged samaras used for seed dispersal. These distinctive winged fruits are held on long pedicels and often turn a brownish color upon maturity.

The overall architecture of the plant is characterized by a deciduous habit and a fine-textured foliage profile.
