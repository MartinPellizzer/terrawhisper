Sphagneticola calendulacea

What's the taxonomical classification of Sphagneticola calendulacea?

Sphagneticola calendulacea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Asterales. It is a member of the family Asteraceae, which is further refined into the genus Sphagneticola, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species calendulacea.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Sphagneticola calendulacea can cause contact dermatitis, skin irritation, and allergic reactions in sensitive individuals due to its chemical composition. When the plant's sap or foliage comes into direct contact with human skin, it may trigger redness, itching, or swelling. Some people may experience more severe dermatological issues if they have a pre-existing sensitivity to its compounds. While primarily known as an invasive weed, accidental ingestion could potentially lead to gastrointestinal distress in certain organisms. It is important to handle the plant with care to avoid these adverse physical reactions.
