Sphagneticola calendulacea

What's the taxonomical classification of Sphagneticola calendulacea?

Sphagneticola calendulacea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Asterales. It is a member of the family Asteraceae, which is further refined into the genus Sphagneticola, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species calendulacea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sphagneticola calendulacea has prostrate, creeping stems that form dense mats, characterized by opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated margins and a slightly hairy texture. The foliage typically measures between three and seven centimeters in length and possesses a bright green color. Small, yellow flower heads emerge from the leaf axils, consisting of ray florets surrounding a central disc of tubular florets. These inflorescences are part of the Asteraceae family and are approximately one to two centimeters in diameter. The plant's root system allows it to spread rapidly across the ground via stolons.

What are the pharmacological activities of Sphagneticola calendulacea?

This plant has antibacterial, antineoplastic, and antioxidant pharmacological activities that make it a significant subject for medicinal research. Its antioxidant properties allow it to scavenge free radicals and reduce oxidative stress within biological systems. The presence of bioactive compounds also provides potent antibacterial effects against various pathogenic microorganisms. Furthermore, the plant exhibits antineoplastic potential by demonstrating the ability to inhibit the growth and proliferation of cancer cells. These diverse therapeutic actions suggest that its chemical constituents could serve as templates for developing new pharmaceutical agents.

As discussed in a study, Sphagneticola calendulacea was evaluated for its antibacterial activity against Urinary Tract Infection causing pathogens including Staphylococcus spp., Proteus spp., Pseudomonas spp., Escherichia coli and Enterobacter spp. The research utilized the solvent extraction method, conventional cell culture technique, minimum inhibitory concentration (MIC) assay, and disc diffusion method to analyze these activities. While ethanol and hot water extracts exhibited minimum inhibitory effects, the methanol extract of the plant showed very potent antibacterial activity against most bacteria with an inhibitory zone diameter up to 14 mm. When combined with commercially available antibiotics, the zone diameter increased up to 26 mm. This significant improvement suggests that combining a natural agent with a synthetic one is more efficient than single monotherapy.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse array of medicinal compounds including flavonoids, total phenolic compounds, alkaloids, and terpenoids. These bioactive constituents work synergistically to provide potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory benefits to the human body. The high concentration of flavonoid molecules specifically helps in neutralizing free radicals and protecting cellular structures from oxidative stress. Furthermore, the total phenolic content plays a critical role in supporting cardiovascular health and regulating metabolic processes. Such chemical profiles make the plant a valuable subject for pharmacological research and natural medicine applications.

As discussed in a study, the methanol extract of S. calendulacea (MESL) contains specific medicinal compounds including total phenolic and total flavonoid contents. The dry extracts of MESL exhibited total phenolic content of 32.86 mg/gm of gallic acid equivalent and total flavonoid content of 127.26 mg/gm of catechin equivalent. These compounds contribute to antioxidant properties, with the MESL showing scavenging activity with an IC50 value ≥70.5 μg/mL. Furthermore, the antineoplastic effects of MESL were observed at doses of 50 mg/kg and 100 mg/kg body weight.
