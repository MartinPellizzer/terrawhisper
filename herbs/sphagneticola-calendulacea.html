Sphagneticola calendulacea

What's the taxonomical classification of Sphagneticola calendulacea?

Sphagneticola calendulacea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Asterales. It is a member of the family Asteraceae, which is further refined into the genus Sphagneticola, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species calendulacea.

Sphagneticola calendulacea contains bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, tannins, and terpenoids that contribute to its pharmacological activities. What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, inflammatory conditions, and various gastrointestinal disorders. Its medicinal properties are frequently applied topically to manage ailments like eczema and fungal infections. The plant contains bioactive compounds that help reduce swelling and promote the healing of minor cuts or abrasions. In some traditional practices, decoctions are prepared to soothe internal digestive distress and stomach pains. Research into its chemical constituents suggests it possesses significant antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory capabilities.
