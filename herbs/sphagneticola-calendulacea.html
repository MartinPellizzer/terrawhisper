Sphagneticola calendulacea

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sphagneticola calendulacea has prostrate, creeping stems that form dense mats, characterized by opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated margins and a slightly hairy texture. The foliage typically measures between three and seven centimeters in length and possesses a bright green color. Small, yellow flower heads emerge from the leaf axils, consisting of ray florets surrounding a central disc of tubular florets. These inflorescences are part of the Asteraceae family and are approximately one to two centimeters in diameter. The plant's root system allows it to spread rapidly across the ground via stolons.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Some people may experience more severe dermatological issues if they have a pre-existing sensitivity to its compounds. While primarily known as an invasive weed, accidental ingestion could potentially lead to gastrointestinal distress in certain organisms. It is important to handle the plant with care to avoid these adverse physical reactions.
