Sphagneticola calendulacea

What's the taxonomical classification of Sphagneticola calendulacea?

Sphagneticola calendulacea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Asterales. It is a member of the family Asteraceae, which is further refined into the genus Sphagneticola, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species calendulacea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sphagneticola calendulacea has prostrate, creeping stems that form dense mats, characterized by opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated margins and a slightly hairy texture. The foliage typically measures between three and seven centimeters in length and possesses a bright green color. Small, yellow flower heads emerge from the leaf axils, consisting of ray florets surrounding a central disc of tubular florets. These inflorescences are part of the Asteraceae family and are approximately one to two centimeters in diameter. The plant's root system allows it to spread rapidly across the ground via stolons.

In many of these new territories, the species often thrives in disturbed habitats, coastal areas, and along roadsides. How is this plant cultivated?

What are the pharmacological activities of Sphagneticola calendulacea?

This plant has demonstrated significant antibacterial, antineoplastic, and antioxidant pharmacological activities through its diverse phytochemical profile. The presence of secondary metabolites allows the plant to inhibit the growth of various pathogenic bacteria. Furthermore, its antineoplastic properties suggest a potential role in suppressing the proliferation of cancer cells in laboratory studies. The antioxidant capacity of the plant helps neutralize free radicals, thereby reducing oxidative stress within biological systems. These multifaceted biological effects highlight its therapeutic potential for various medicinal applications.

According to a study published by "New microbes and new infections", Sphagneticola calendulacea displayed notable antibacterial activity against most of the tested bacteria. While ethanol and hot water extracts exhibited minimum inhibitory effects, the methanol extract showed very potent antibacterial activity with an inhibitory zone diameter up to 14 mm. When used in combination with commercially available antibiotics, the zone diameter increased up to 26 mm. This improvement represents a significant increase compared to the individual plant extracts. These results suggest that combining a natural agent with a synthetic one is more efficient than a single monotherapy.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse profile of bioactive phytochemicals including flavonoids, alkaloids, tannins, steroids, and a significant concentration of total phenolic compounds. These chemical constituents contribute to the plant's documented antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The high level of total phenolic content is often linked to its ability to scavenge free radicals effectively. Additionally, specific flavonoids within the species provide protective mechanisms against oxidative stress in various biological systems. Researchers continue to study these compounds to explore their potential applications in pharmaceutical development.

According to a study published by "Heliyon", the methanolic extract of Sphagneticola calendulacea (MESL) contains specific medicinal compounds including total phenolic and total flavonoid contents. The phytochemical analysis of MESL showed the total phenolic content was 32.86 mg/gm of gallic acid equivalent. Additionally, the total flavonoid content in the dry extracts was 127.26 mg/gm of catechin equivalent. The MESL also demonstrated scavenging activity in five different assays with an IC50 value ≥70.5 μg/mL. In the in vivo antineoplastic assay, the extract increased EAC cell death at doses of 50 mg/kg and 100 mg/kg body weight.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

According to a study published by "New microbes and new infections", Sphagneticola calendulacea is used for its antibacterial activity against Urinary Tract Infection (UTI) causing pathogens such as Staphylococcus spp., Proteus spp., Pseudmonas spp., Escherichia coli and Enterobacter spp. The research evaluated the antibacterial activity of this plant through various methods including the minimum inhibitory concentration (MIC) assay and disc diffusion method. While ethanol and hot water extracts exhibited minimum inhibitory effects, the methanol extract showed potent antibacterial activity with an inhibitory zone diameter up to 14 mm. When combined with commercially available antibiotics, the zone diameter increased up to 26 mm. This significant improvement suggests that combining this natural agent with a synthetic one is more efficient than a single monotherapy for treating multidrug-resistant bacteria.
