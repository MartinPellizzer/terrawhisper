What's the taxonomical classification of Ardisia brunnescens?

Ardisia brunnescens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is situated in the order Ericales. This plant is a member of the family Primulaceae, falling under the genus Ardisia, and is specifically identified by its species name, brunnescens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ardisia brunnescens has simple, evergreen leaves that are elliptical to oblong in shape and possess a coriaceous, or leathery, texture.

The leaf margins are entire and smooth, while the surfaces typically exhibit a glossy appearance on the upper side. The plant features small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the axils of the leaves. These flowers eventually develop into fleshy fruits that serve as a primary morphological characteristic of the species.

The overall structure of the plant is that of a woody shrub or small tree.
