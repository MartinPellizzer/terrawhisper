What is Allium × proliferum?

Allium × proliferum is a hybrid species of the genus Allium, belonging to the family Amaryllidaceae.

It is a perennial bulbous plant characterized by linear, flat, and narrow leaves, typically growing 30-60 cm in height. The plant produces a scape with a dense umbel of white to pale purple flowers, each 10-15 mm in diameter, in late spring. The flowers are hexagonal in shape, with six petals and six stamens, and are arranged in a spherical, densely packed cluster.

The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade, in well-drained soil, and is native to the Iberian Peninsula.

What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

The main herbal preparations of Allium × proliferum are commonly utilized due to its pungent flavor and potential health benefits.

Garlic powder and garlic salt are obtained through the dehydration and salting of the plant, respectively, and are used as seasonings in various dishes.

Garlic confit is a preparation of slow-cooked garlic in oil, which is used to add flavor and depth to a variety of cuisines.
