What's the taxonomical classification of Aristolochia kwangsiensis?

Aristolochia kwangsiensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant falls under the order Piperales and is a member of the family Aristolochiaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Aristolochia and is specifically designated as the species kwangsiensis.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause severe nephrotoxicity leading to kidney damage, carcinogenicity resulting in cancer, hepatotoxicity causing liver damage, and intense gastrointestinal distress. These toxic effects are primarily driven by the presence of aristolochic acids within the plant tissues. Once ingested, these compounds can trigger significant inflammation and cell death in vital organs. Chronic exposure is particularly dangerous due to its documented link to the development of urothelial carcinomas. Patients may also experience immediate physical discomfort such as nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain following consumption.
