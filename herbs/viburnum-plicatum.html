Viburnum plicatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Viburnum plicatum?

Viburnum plicatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Dipsacales. The plant is a member of the family Viburnaceaee, falling under the genus Viburnum, and is specifically identified by its species name, plicatum.

What are the herbal preparations of Viburnum plicatum?

Viburnum plicatum is prepared as decoctions, infusions, or tinctures utilizing the various parts of the plant such as the roots, leaves, or bark. These liquid preparations allow for the extraction of bioactive compounds that are more readily absorbed by the body. Infusions are often used for the softer leaves, while decoctions are necessary to break down the tougher woody material of the roots and stems. Tinctures provide a concentrated form that can be preserved for longer periods and administered in small doses. Each method is chosen based on the specific part of the plant being used and the intended therapeutic effect.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Viburnum plicatum can cause skin irritation or allergic reactions if the leaves or stems come into direct contact with sensitive skin. Some individuals may experience redness, itching, or a mild rash after touching the foliage. While the plant is primarily used for ornamental purposes, consuming any part of it could lead to digestive upset or toxicity. There is limited scientific research regarding the long-term effects of ingestion in humans. It is best to avoid eating any part of this shrub and to wash your hands after handling it.
