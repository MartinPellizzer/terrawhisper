Viburnum plicatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Viburnum plicatum?

Viburnum plicatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Dipsacales. The plant is a member of the family Viburnaceaee, falling under the genus Viburnum, and is specifically identified by its species name, plicatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viburnum plicatum has opposite, elliptic to ovate leaves that are distinctly rugose with prominent, impressed venation on the upper surface and a characteristic pleated or folded appearance. These leaves are typically dark green and possess serrated margins that add to their textured profile. The plant produces small, creamy white flowers arranged in dense, flat-topped cymes. During the fruiting stage, the plant bears small, fleshy drupes that transition from green to a dark color. Its woody stems are sturdy and contribute to its overall shrubby growth habit.
