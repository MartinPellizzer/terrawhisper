Viburnum plicatum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viburnum plicatum has opposite, elliptic to ovate leaves that are distinctly rugose with prominent, impressed venation on the upper surface and a characteristic pleated or folded appearance. These leaves are typically dark green and possess serrated margins that add to their textured profile. The plant produces small, creamy white flowers arranged in dense, flat-topped cymes. During the fruiting stage, the plant bears small, fleshy drupes that transition from green to a dark color. Its woody stems are sturdy and contribute to its overall shrubby growth habit.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China, specifically inhabiting provinces such as Sichuan, Yunnan, Guizhou, Hubei, and Gansu. It thrives primarily in temperate montane forests and scrublands at varying elevations. Within these habitats, it often colonizes forest edges and thickets where sunlight is partially available. The species is typically found in well-drained soils along sloping terrains. Its distribution remains concentrated within these specific East Asian highland ecosystems.

There is limited scientific research regarding the long-term effects of ingestion in humans. It is best to avoid eating any part of this shrub and to wash your hands after handling it.
