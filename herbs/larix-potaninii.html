What's the taxonomical classification of Larix potaninii?

Larix potaninii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Pinidae and the order Pinales. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the family Pinaceae and the genus Larix, ultimately identifying this specific organism as the species potaninii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Larix potaninii has deciduous needles arranged spirally on short lateral shoots and long pendulous branchlets, accompanied by woody cones containing several hard scales.

The tree typically develops a conical crown with branches that spread horizontally or slightly drooping as the specimen matures. Its bark is thick and reddish-brown, eventually breaking into deep longitudinal fissures on older trunks. The small, woody cones are ovoid in shape and typically measure between one and two centimeters in length.

These unique morphological traits allow the species to adapt to high-altitude mountain environments.
