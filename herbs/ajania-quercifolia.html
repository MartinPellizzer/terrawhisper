Ajania quercifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Ajania quercifolia?

Ajania quercifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is positioned in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae, and its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Ajania and the species quercifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ajania quercifolia has woody, perennial stems with small, oval-shaped leaves that feature serrated margins and a texture resembling oak foliage. The leaves are typically dark green on the upper surface and may appear lighter underneath. These plants often form dense, low-growing mats or cushions due to their spreading growth habit. Small, disc-shaped flower heads appear in clusters, typically displaying a yellowish or white coloration during the blooming season. The overall structure is compact, making it well-suited for rocky or alpine environments.

The distribution is characterized by a preference for temperate climates found in these specific continental subtropical zones. How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires full sun to partial shade and well-draining, sandy or loamy soil to prevent root rot.

For optimal growth, provide regular watering during the initial growing season but allow the soil to dry slightly between applications. It performs best when planted in areas where it can spread as a groundcover to suppress weeds.
