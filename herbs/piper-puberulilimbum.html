Piper puberulilimbum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Piper puberulilimbum has elliptical to oblong leaves that are characterized by a dense covering of fine, soft hairs on both the upper and lower surfaces. The leaf margins are entire, and the venation pattern is prominent, often displaying a distinct midrib with secondary veins that arch toward the edges. The stems are woody at the base and exhibit a slightly pubescent texture similar to the foliage. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in elongated, spike-like inflorescences that emerge from the leaf axils. These botanical features collectively distinguish the species within its genus through its specific trichome distribution and leaf shape.
