Alpinia formosana

What's the taxonomical classification of Alpinia formosana?

Alpinia formosana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is positioned within the order Zingiberales. It further falls under the family Zingiberaceae, placing it among the ginger species. Specifically, its taxonomic hierarchy concludes with the genus Alpinia and the species formosana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Alpinia formosana has erect, rhizomatous stems that typically reach heights of up to 2 meters and feature large, lanceolate leaves with prominent parallel venation. These perennial herbs grow from thick, creeping underground rhizomes that facilitate vegetative spreading. The foliage is characterized by long, leathery leaf blades that emerge from sheathing bases to form dense clumps. During the flowering season, the plant produces terminal inflorescences consisting of small, delicate flowers that often emerge from specialized bracts. The overall structure is typical of the Zingiberaceae family, combining robust stem development with lush, tropical greenery.
