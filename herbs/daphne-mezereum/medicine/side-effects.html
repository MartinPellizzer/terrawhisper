1. Causes vomiting

Daphne mezereum causes vomiting due to its toxic compounds, particularly daphnin and mezerein.

These alkaloids can stimulate the stomach muscles, leading to nausea and vomiting, especially when ingested in large quantities or accidentally consumed by children or pets.

The severity of this side effect varies depending on the individual's sensitivity to these toxins.

2. Induces seizures

Daphne mezereum induces seizures when used due to its toxic properties.

The plant contains alkaloids, such as berberine and sanguinarine, which are responsible for this adverse effect.

These compounds can affect the central nervous system, leading to abnormal electrical activity in the brain that may result in seizure-like episodes.

3. Triggers coma

Daphne mezereum triggers coma in some individuals due to its high concentration of alkaloids, particularly daphnin and mezerein.

These compounds can cause severe gastrointestinal irritation, leading to nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain.

Furthermore, the ingestion of even small amounts of Daphne mezereum can result in systemic toxicity, which may progress to central nervous system depression, ultimately inducing a coma.

4. Produces nephrotoxicity

Daphne mezereum produces nephrotoxicity due to the presence of toxic alkaloids in its plant parts.

These alkaloids can cause damage to the kidneys, leading to adverse effects on kidney function and potentially life-threatening complications.

The exact mechanisms by which Daphne mezereum exerts its nephrotoxic effects are not fully understood, but it is thought to involve interference with normal kidney function and the destruction of renal cells.

5. Creates anemia

Daphne mezereum creates anemia by disrupting the production of red blood cells.

This is due to its ability to interfere with the body's iron absorption and utilization, leading to a reduction in hemoglobin levels.

As a result, individuals who use Daphne mezereum may experience fatigue, weakness, and shortness of breath, as their bodies struggle to deliver oxygen to tissues and organs.

6. Increases blood pressure

Daphne mezereum increases blood pressure due to its potential to stimulate the sympathetic nervous system and cause vasoconstriction.

The plant contains alkaloids that can increase heart rate and cardiac output, leading to an elevation in blood pressure.

This effect is thought to be particularly pronounced when high doses of Daphne mezereum are ingested or used topically for extended periods.

7. Activates diarrhea

Daphne mezereum activates diarrhea due to its iridoid glycosides, which are capable of inhibiting the absorption of water and electrolytes in the small intestine.

This results in an increase in stool frequency and looseness, causing a sudden onset of watery diarrhea.

The iridoids in Daphne mezereum also stimulate the intestinal muscles, leading to increased peristalsis and further exacerbating the diarrheal symptoms.

8. Stimulates abdominal pain

Daphne mezereum stimulates abdominal pain due to its toxic properties.

The plant contains glycosides and alkaloids that can irritate the mucous membranes of the digestive tract, leading to discomfort, cramping, and pain in the abdominal region.

Additionally, the consumption of mayflower extracts or preparations can cause gastrointestinal disturbances, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, which may exacerbate abdominal pain symptoms.

9. Evokes dermatitis

Daphne mezereum evokes dermatitis in some individuals due to its alkaloid content, particularly mezerein and desmethoxyyangonin.

These compounds can cause skin irritation, leading to an allergic reaction that manifests as a rash or inflammation on the skin. As the body reacts to these chemicals, it triggers an immune response, resulting in the characteristic symptoms of dermatitis.

The exact mechanism of this reaction is not fully understood, but it is thought to involve the stimulation of histamine release and subsequent activation of immune cells.

10. Develops photosensitivity

Daphne mezereum develops photosensitivity in some individuals.

This is due to its high concentration of furanocoumarins, a class of compounds that are potent inhibitors of cytochrome P450 enzymes involved in vitamin D metabolism.

As a result, exposure to ultraviolet (UV) light triggers an excessive accumulation of previtamin D3, leading to phototoxic reactions such as skin reddening, itching, and blistering.
