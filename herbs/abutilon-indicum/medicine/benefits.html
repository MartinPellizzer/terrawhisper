10 Health Benefits Of Abutilon Indicum (Indian Mallow)

Abutilon indicum, also known as Indian mallow, has health benefits such as reducing inflammation and improving wound healing due to its antimicrobial and antioxidant properties.

It also exhibits anti-diabetic and anti-cancer activities, which can help manage blood sugar levels and prevent tumor growth.

By using Indian mallow, individuals with diabetes can regulate their blood sugar levels and prevent complications, while cancer patients may benefit from its potential to prevent tumor growth and improve survival rates.

This article explains in details the 10 best health benefits of Abutilon indicum .

1. Helps lower blood sugar

Abutilon indicum helps lower blood sugar because it is rich in flavonoids and phenolic acids.

These compounds have been shown to inhibit the activity of alpha-glucosidase, an enzyme that breaks down carbohydrates into glucose.

By preventing this enzyme from working, Abutilon indicum reduces the absorption of glucose into the bloodstream, resulting in lower blood sugar levels and potential management of diabetes.

2. Reduces inflammation effectively

Abutilon indicum, also known as Indian mallow, reduces inflammation effectively because of its rich storehouse of polyphenolic compounds, flavonoids, and glycosides.

These bioactive molecules exhibit potent anti-inflammatory properties, inhibiting pro-inflammatory enzymes and cytokines responsible for chronic inflammation.

Additionally, its flavonoids possess antioxidant activity, neutralizing free radicals and promoting tissue repair, thereby alleviating inflammation and related symptoms.

3. Treats diarrhea efficiently

Abutilon indicum, also known as Indian mallow, treats diarrhea efficiently because of its high mucilage content.

This mucilage, a thick, jelly-like substance, coats the stomach and intestines, providing relief from the inflammation and irritation associated with diarrhea. As a natural demulcent, it forms a protective barrier against irritants, allowing the digestive system to heal and recover.

This makes Abutilon indicum a popular natural remedy for treating acute and chronic diarrhea.

4. Relieves fever quickly

Abutilon indicum, also known as Indian mallow, relieves fever quickly because it contains various bioactive compounds, including alkaloids, glycosides, and flavonoids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory, antipyretic, and analgesic properties, which help to reduce the temperature and alleviate associated symptoms such as headache and body aches.

Additionally, its decoction has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine to treat fever, making it a popular natural remedy for quick relief from fever.

5. Fights cancer cells

Abutilon indicum, also known as Indian mallow, fights cancer cells because of its rich content of flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids.

These compounds have been shown to induce apoptosis (cell death) in cancer cells, inhibit cell proliferation, and prevent angiogenesis (formation of new blood vessels).

Studies have demonstrated the potential of A. indicum in reducing the viability of cancer cells and inducing cell cycle arrest, making it a promising candidate for cancer treatment.

6. Controls hypertension naturally

Abutilon indicum, also known as Indian mallow, controls hypertension naturally because it has been shown to possess significant vasodilatory and diuretic properties.

The plant's roots and leaves contain flavonoids and phenolic acids, which help relax blood vessel walls and improve blood circulation, thereby lowering blood pressure.

Additionally, its diuretic properties help in eliminating excess fluids from the body, further reducing blood pressure.

7. Prevents kidney damage

Abutilon indicum, also known as Indian mallow, prevents kidney damage because of its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

The leaves and roots of the plant contain flavonoids, phenolic acids, and glycosides that help to reduce oxidative stress and inflammation in the kidneys. This natural remedy has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine to treat kidney stones and other kidney-related problems, promoting overall kidney health and function.

Regular consumption may help prevent kidney damage.

8. Reduces cholesterol levels

Abutilon indicum, also known as Indian mallow, reduces cholesterol levels because of its rich content of flavonoids and saponins.

These compounds have been shown to inhibit the absorption of cholesterol in the gut and stimulate the excretion of bile, a compound that helps to remove excess cholesterol from the body.

Studies have demonstrated that consuming Indian mallow extracts can lead to significant reductions in LDL ("bad") cholesterol levels in individuals with hyperlipidemia.

9. Alleviates menstrual cramps

Abutilon indicum, also known as Indian mallow, alleviates menstrual cramps because its anthelmintic and anti-inflammatory properties help to reduce pain and discomfort associated with menstrual cramps.

The plant's flavonoids, saponins, and mucilages have been shown to have analgesic and anti-spasmodic effects, making it an effective natural remedy for relieving menstrual pain.

Its use in traditional medicine is well-documented, with many cultures incorporating Indian mallow into their treatments for menstrual cramps and other gynecological issues.

10. Soothes respiratory issues

Abutilon indicum, also known as Indian mallow, soothes respiratory issues because of its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The plant's leaves and flowers contain compounds that help to calm irritated airways and reduce congestion.

Additionally, Indian mallow has been traditionally used to treat coughs and colds, and has been shown to have bronchodilatory effects, making it a natural remedy for respiratory problems such as asthma and bronchitis.
