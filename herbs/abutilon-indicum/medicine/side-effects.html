10 Possible Side Effects Of Abutilon Indicum (Indian Mallow)

Abutilon indicum, also known as Indian mallow, has some side effects when used improperly, such as diarrhea, nausea, and vomiting.

These side effects are caused by the plant's high levels of mucilages and tannins, which can irritate the digestive system.

Prolonged use can lead to dehydration, electrolyte imbalance, and malnutrition, worsening people's lives by causing fatigue, weakness, and weight loss.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Abutilon indicum if used imporperly.

1. Interacts with medications

Abutilon indicum, also known as Indian mallow, causes allergic reactions due to the presence of latex in its leaves and stems.

This latex can trigger an immune response in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling.

The allergenic properties of Abutilon indicum are thought to be caused by the interaction between the plant's proteins and the body's immune system, resulting in an overactive response that leads to allergic reactions.

2. Interacts with medications

Abutilon indicum, also known as Indian Mallow, triggers asthma attacks due to its allergenic properties.

The plant's pollen and other particles can cause an allergic reaction in susceptible individuals, leading to constriction of the airways, inflammation, and bronchospasm.

This can result in symptoms such as wheezing, coughing, shortness of breath, and chest tightness, exacerbating existing asthma conditions or triggering attacks in those who are not diagnosed with asthma.

Abutilon indicum, also known as Indian mellow, induces diarrhea due to its ability to stimulate bowel movements and increase intestinal fluid secretion.

The saponins present in Abutilon indicum can irritate the digestive system, leading to an increased frequency of bowel movements, resulting in diarrhea.

Additionally, the plant's high fiber content may also contribute to this side effect by speeding up food movement through the gut, further exacerbating diarrhea symptoms.

6. Interacts with medications

Abutilon indicum, also known as Indian Mallow, increases blood pressure due to its high concentration of flavonoids and triterpenoid saponins.

These compounds have been shown to cause vasopressor activity, leading to increased peripheral resistance and subsequent elevation in systolic blood pressure.

Additionally, the herb's diuretic properties can contribute to this effect by reducing blood volume and further increasing blood pressure.

The plant contains alkaloids and flavonoids that can alter serotonin levels, leading to feelings of apprehension and unease. Additionally, Abutilon indicum has been shown to increase heart rate and blood pressure, which can exacerbate anxious sensations.

The plant contains alkaloids such as abutilone and isabeline, which can alter the normal functioning of these chemicals, leading to altered perceptions and sensory experiences.

This may result in vivid dreams, visual distortions, and auditory hallucinations, potentially blurring the lines between reality and fantasy.

Additionally, Abutilon indicum's potential to alter heart rate and blood pressure may also contribute to this side effect.

10. Interacts with medications

Abutilon indicum, also known as Indian Mallow, suppresses appetite due to its active compounds, including flavonoids and saponins.

These bioactive molecules are believed to interact with the body's neurotransmitters, such as serotonin and dopamine, which play a crucial role in regulating appetite and satiety.

This interaction can result in reduced food intake and altered eating habits, potentially leading to anorexia or decreased caloric consumption.
