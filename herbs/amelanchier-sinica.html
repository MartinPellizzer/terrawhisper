Amelanchier sinica

What's the taxonomical classification of Amelanchier sinica?

Amelanchier sinica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Rosales. Furthermore, this plant is a member of the family Rosaceae, specifically falling under the genus Amelanchier, with the specific epithet sinica.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Amelanchier sinica contains high concentrations of bioactive polyphenols, specifically flavonoids and phenolic acids, which serve as its primary medicinal components. These compounds act as potent antioxidants that help neutralize free radicals within cellular structures. Research suggests that these phytochemicals provide significant anti-inflammatory properties that may support overall metabolic health. Additionally, certain glycosides found within the plant matter contribute to its potential antimicrobial activity. These diverse chemical profiles make the plant a subject of interest for pharmacological studies focused on natural wellness.

What are the therapeutic actions of Amelanchier sinica?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various respiratory conditions, including chronic bronchitis, asthma, and common coughs. Its primary medicinal value lies in its ability to act as an expectorant, helping to loosen phlegm and clear the airways. Many practitioners also utilize its extracts to alleviate symptoms of inflammation within the lungs. Additionally, it is sometimes employed to soothe sore throats and reduce minor bronchial irritation. These therapeutic properties make it a significant component in many traditional herbal remedies.
