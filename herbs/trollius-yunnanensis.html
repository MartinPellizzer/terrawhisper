Trollius yunnanensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Trollius yunnanensis?

Trollius yunnanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is positioned in the order Ranunculales. The plant is a member of the family Ranunculaceae, which is further organized into the genus Trollius, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species yunnanensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Trollius yunnanensis has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by basal leaves that are palmately lobed and stems that terminate in globose, many-flowered inflorescences. The foliage typically consists of deeply divided, green leaves with serrated margins that emerge from a rhizomatous root system. Each flower is composed of multiple yellowish to orange tepals that form a cup-like structure to protect the central reproductive organs. The stems are upright and can reach varying heights depending on the specific mountain habitat. This plant's structure is adapted to thrive in the moist, alpine environments of high-altitude regions.
