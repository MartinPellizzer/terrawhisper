Begonia taiwaniana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Begonia taiwaniana has succulent, asymmetrical leaves that are broadly ovate to cordate in shape and feature distinct, irregularly serrated margins. The leaf surfaces often display a subtle texture with prominent venation patterns that radiate from the base. Its stems are fleshy and typically exhibit a reddish or brownish pigmentation depending on light exposure. The inflorescences consist of small, delicate flowers that emerge from the leaf axils in a clustered arrangement. These flowers are primarily white or pale pink and serve as a key identifying characteristic of the species.
