What's the taxonomical classification of Cypripedium fargesii?

Cypripedium fargesii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. As a member of the order Asparagales, it falls under the family Orchidaceae, which characterizes its identity as an orchid. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Cypripedium, with its specific designation being the species fargesii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cypripedium fargesii has a terrestrial growth habit characterized by two to four broad, elliptic, bright green leaves that emerge from a rhizomatous root system.

The plant produces a single, erect flowering stem that can reach heights of approximately thirty to sixty centimeters. Its complex flowers feature a large, yellow, slipper-shaped labellum and two lateral petals that are often spreading or slightly reflexed with brownish or purplish markings. The dorsal sepal is typically upright and covers the column, while the lateral sepals are fused or closely positioned.

These morphological traits distinguish it from other members of the lady's slipper orchid genus within its high-altitude habitat.
