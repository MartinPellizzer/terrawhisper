Antrophyum formosanum

What's the taxonomical classification of Antrophyum formosanum?

Antrophyum formosanum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Polypodiinae and falls under the order Polypodiales. This fern is a member of the family Pteridaceae, specifically situated within the genus Antrophyum, with its unique biological identity defined by its species name, formosanum.

Antrophyum formosanum has antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and wound-healing properties that are traditionally utilized to treat skin infections and various physical ailments. What are the herbal preparations of Antrophyum formosanum?

Antrophyum formosanum is prepared primarily as a decoction made by boiling the dried rhizomes in water to extract its medicinal compounds. This liquid extract is often consumed warm to address various ailments associated with traditional healing practices. Some practitioners may also grind the dried plant material into a fine powder to be encapsulated or mixed into topical applications. Depending on the specific therapeutic goal, the plant can be integrated into different liquid bases to enhance its absorption. These diverse methods allow for the versatile use of the fern in folk medicine.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Antrophyum formosanum can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, or localized swelling if the plant's sap comes into contact with sensitive tissue. While specific toxicological data is limited, many ferns in similar families contain compounds that may trigger dermatological reactions in sensitive individuals. Accidental ingestion could potentially lead to gastrointestinal distress or mucosal irritation depending on the concentration of bioactive compounds present. It is advisable to wear gloves when handling the plant to prevent unexpected physical reactions. Always consult a medical professional if any symptoms develop after exposure.
