Antrophyum formosanum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Antrophyum formosanum has large, undivided, leathery fronds that are typically cordate or reniform in shape with a prominent, fleshy midrib. The leaf margins are often wavy or slightly lobed, and the overall texture is quite tough to withstand humid environments. On the underside of these leaves, the sori are arranged in distinct, linear rows following the veins. These reproductive structures are protected by a specialized membrane known as an indusium, which is characteristic of the genus. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, utilizing its robust root system to anchor itself to tree bark.
