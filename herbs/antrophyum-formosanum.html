What's the taxonomical classification of Antrophyum formosanum?

Antrophyum formosanum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Polypodiinae and falls under the order Polypodiales. This fern is a member of the family Pteridaceae, specifically situated within the genus Antrophyum, with its unique biological identity defined by its species name, formosanum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Antrophyum formosanum has large, undivided, leathery fronds that are typically cordate or reniform in shape with a prominent, fleshy midrib. These reproductive structures are protected by a specialized membrane known as an indusium, which is characteristic of the genus.
