Antrophyum formosanum

What's the taxonomical classification of Antrophyum formosanum?

Antrophyum formosanum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Polypodiinae and falls under the order Polypodiales. This fern is a member of the family Pteridaceae, specifically situated within the genus Antrophyum, with its unique biological identity defined by its species name, formosanum.

What are the therapeutic actions of Antrophyum formosanum?

Antrophyum formosanum has antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and wound-healing properties that are traditionally utilized to treat skin infections and various physical ailments. The plant contains bioactive compounds that help inhibit the growth of harmful bacteria and fungi on the skin. These chemical constituents also work to reduce swelling and alleviate pain in localized areas of inflammation. When applied topically, the extracts can accelerate the regeneration of damaged tissues to facilitate faster recovery from wounds. Such therapeutic actions make it a significant subject of study for natural dermatological treatments.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin diseases, wounds, inflammatory conditions, and certain types of internal bleeding. The leaves are often applied topically as a poultice to soothe skin irritations and promote the healing of open sores. In traditional medicine, extracts from the plant are sometimes consumed to address issues related to the digestive tract or blood circulation. Researchers study its phytochemical properties to better understand how its bioactive compounds interact with various pathogens. This botanical specimen serves as a significant resource in many regional folk medicine practices.
