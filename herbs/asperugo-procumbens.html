What's the taxonomical classification of Asperugo procumbens?

Asperugo procumbens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized as Equisetopsida, falling under the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Boraginales and the family Boraginaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by the genus Asperugo, with its specific designation being the species procumbens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Asperugo procumbens has prostrate, creeping stems that form dense mats and leaves that are arranged in whorls of four to six. The leaves are small, elliptical to lanceolate, and possess serrated margins along their edges. Tiny, inconspicuous flowers are produced in axillary clusters and typically exhibit a pale greenish or white coloration. The plant develops small, dry fruits known as schizocarps that contain numerous minute seeds. Its overall structure allows it to spread horizontally across the soil surface in various habitats.
