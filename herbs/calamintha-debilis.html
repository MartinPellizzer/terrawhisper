Calamintha debilis

What's the taxonomical classification of Calamintha debilis?

Calamintha debilis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is situated in the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Calamintha, and its unique species designation, debilis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Calamintha debilis has a low-growing, perennial habit characterized by decumbent to ascending stems that often form dense, spreading mats. Its inflorescences consist of small, densely packed clusters of tubular flowers that typically emerge from the leaf axils. These flowers are usually light purple or pinkish and are supported by delicate, hairy calyxes. The overall structure of the plant is compact and woody at the base, allowing it to thrive in rocky or sandy substrates.
