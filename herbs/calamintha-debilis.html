What's the taxonomical classification of Calamintha debilis?

Calamintha debilis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is situated in the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Calamintha, and its unique species designation, debilis.

Calamintha debilis has a low-growing, perennial habit characterized by decumbent to ascending stems that often form dense, spreading mats. These flowers are usually light purple or pinkish and are supported by delicate, hairy calyxes.
