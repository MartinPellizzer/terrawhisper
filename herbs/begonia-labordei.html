Begonia labordei

What's the taxonomical classification of Begonia labordei?

Begonia labordei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Cucurbitaleales. As a member of the family Begoniaceae, it is situated within the genus Begonia, ultimately reaching its specific taxonomic designation at the species level as Begonia labordei.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the mountain forests of the Eastern Andes in Colombia, specifically within the departments of Antioquia and Caldas. It thrives in high-altitude cloud forest environments where moisture levels remain consistently high. The species is typically found growing in shaded, rocky areas or along the banks of mountain streams. Because its habitat is restricted to these specific montane regions, its overall range is quite narrow.
