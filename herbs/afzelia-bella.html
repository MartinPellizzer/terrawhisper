Afzelia bella

What's the taxonomical classification of Afzelia bella?

Afzelia bella belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further falls under the order Fabales and is a member of the family Fabaceae. Specifically, it is identified by the genus Afzelia and is designated by the species name bella.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Afzelia bella contains a variety of bioactive secondary metabolites including flavonoids, tannins, saponins, and alkaloids that contribute to its therapeutic properties. These chemical constituents are responsible for the plant's demonstrated antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial activities. Specifically, the presence of tannins and saponins aids in wound healing and provides protective effects against certain pathogens. Researchers have also investigated the potential of its extracts to mitigate oxidative stress in biological systems. The complex synergy of these compounds supports its traditional use in treating various ailments in several African regions.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, stomach ailments, malaria, and various inflammatory conditions. The bark and leaves are often prepared as decoctions to address gastrointestinal issues like diarrhea and stomach pain. In many traditional medicinal practices, the extracts are applied topically to soothe skin irritations and promote wound healing. Some communities also utilize the plant to manage fever and symptoms associated with malaria. These various applications demonstrate the plant's significant role in regional herbal medicine.
