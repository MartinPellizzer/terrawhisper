What's the taxonomical classification of Vernonia thomsoniana?

Vernonia thomsoniana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is positioned in the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae, falling under the genus Vernonia, with its specific designation being the species thomsoniana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vernonia thomsoniana has erect, woody stems that reach heights of up to two meters and bear opposite, lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is characterized by a rough, scabrous texture on both the upper and lower surfaces. Its inflorescence consists of dense, terminal clusters of discoid flower heads that display a vibrant purple or lavender coloration. Each individual floret is tubular and features long, spreading lobes that contribute to a feathery appearance. The plant typically develops a branched structure as it matures, supporting multiple flowering stalks.
