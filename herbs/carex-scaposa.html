Carex scaposa

What's the taxonomical classification of Carex scaposa?

Carex scaposa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Poales. This plant is a member of the family Cyperaceae and is specifically categorized under the genus Carex, eventually reaching its unique identification at the species level as Carex scaposa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Carex scaposa has a perennial growth habit characterized by tufted, caespitose clumps of linear, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and somewhat rigid.

The plant produces inconspicuous spikes that are often arranged in a loose, somewhat scapose manner along the culms. These culms are slender and erect, supporting small, single-flowered scales and perigynia that are crucial for species identification. The perigynia themselves are generally small and possess a distinct shape that varies slightly among individual populations.

Overall, the morphological structure is adapted for survival in specialized, often rocky or alpine habitats.

        Carex scaposa is prepared as a decoction, infusion, or topical poultice depending on the intended therapeutic application.
