Rosa bella

What's the taxonomical classification of Rosa bella?

Rosa bella belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the evolutionary hierarchy, it falls under the order Rosales and is a member of the family Rosaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Rosa and the unique species name bella.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rosa bella has woody, perennial stems characterized by a dense covering of sharp, curved prickles and compound leaves arranged in an alternate pattern. Each leaf typically consists of several oval-shaped leaflets with finely serrated margins and a glossy green texture. The plant produces solitary or small clusters of fragrant, multi-petaled flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep rose. These blossoms emerge from terminal buds and are supported by sturdy pedicels. Following pollination, the plant develops small, fleshy hips that serve as the primary seed-bearing structures.

What are the pharmacological activities of Rosa bella?

This plant has cardiotonic, diuretic, and anti-inflammatory pharmacological activities that influence various physiological systems. The presence of specific glycosides within its chemical profile allows it to exert a significant cardiotonic effect on cardiac muscle tissue. Beyond its impact on the heart, the plant demonstrates the ability to promote fluid excretion through its diuretic properties. Researchers have also observed that its extracts can mitigate swelling and pain via anti-inflammatory mechanisms. These diverse biological actions suggest that Rosa bella contains potent bioactive compounds suitable for medicinal study.

As discussed in a study, the methanol extract of the fruit of Rosa bella exhibited an obvious cardiotonic effect at concentrations of 0.4-1.0 mg.ml-1. Seven compounds including pomolic acid, tiliroside, euscaphic acid, daucosterol, quercetin, oleanolic acid, and beta-sitosterol were isolated from the extract. When administered at 400 mg.kg-1, there was no obvious effect on the TC, HDL-ac, and TG of serum in rats. These isolated compounds may serve as the active components of the plant.
