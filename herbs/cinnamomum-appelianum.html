Cinnamomum appelianum

What's the taxonomical classification of Cinnamomum appelianum?

Cinnamomum appelianum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Laurales. Further refinement leads to its placement in the family Lauraceae, where it is categorized under the genus Cinnamomum, eventually reaching its specific designation as the species Cinnamomum appelianum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cinnamomum appelianum has elliptical to lanceolate leaves that are coriaceous in texture, featuring an entire margin and an acute apex. The leaf arrangement is typically alternate along the woody stems of the shrub or small tree. The upper leaf surface is dark green and glossy, while the underside often presents a paler hue. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in axillary clusters, eventually giving way to small drupe-like fruits. These morphological traits are characteristic of many species within the Lauraceae family.
