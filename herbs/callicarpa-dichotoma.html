Callicarpa dichotoma

What's the taxonomical classification of Callicarpa dichotoma?

Callicarpa dichotoma belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving through its hierarchical classification, it falls under the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Callicarpa, and its unique species designation, dichotoma.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Callicarpa dichotoma has opposite, simple leaves that are typically ovate or lanceolate in shape with serrated margins and a dull green upper surface. Small, fragrant white flowers emerge in axillary cymes, which are clusters located where the leaf meets the stem. These blossoms eventually give way to distinctive, spherical drupes that turn a bright, metallic purple when ripe. The stems are often slightly pubescent, providing a fine texture to the overall exterior of the plant.
