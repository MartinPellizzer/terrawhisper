Diplospora dubia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Diplospora dubia has small, woody, perennial stems that bear opposite, sessile leaves with entire margins and an acute to obtuse shape. The leaves are typically elliptic or oblong in form and possess a somewhat leathery texture. These plants often feature inconspicuous flowers that arise from the leaf axils. The reproductive structures are adapted for its specific reproductive cycle within its natural habitat. Overall, its compact growth habit characterizes its physical presence in the environment.
