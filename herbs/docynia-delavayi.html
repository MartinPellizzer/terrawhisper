Docynia delavayi is prepared as a decoction of the dried roots, a juice extracted from the fresh fruit, or a topical paste made from the crushed leaves. What side-effects this plant can have?

Docynia delavayi can cause gastrointestinal distress, such as stomach cramps or diarrhea, if consumed in excessive amounts or if the fruit is not fully ripe. The presence of specific organic acids and tannins in the plant may irritate the digestive lining of sensitive individuals. Some users might also experience mild allergic reactions including skin rashes or itching upon direct contact with the plant parts. While generally considered edible, the exact toxicological profile and long-term effects on human health remain insufficiently documented in scientific literature. Therefore, caution is advised when integrating this plant into a diet without prior knowledge of one's personal tolerance.
