What are the pharmacological activities of Docynia delavayi?

This plant has antimicrobial, antioxidant, carbohydrate digestion enzyme inhibitory, and prebiotic pharmacological activities. The plant also demonstrates antimicrobial effectiveness by inhibiting the growth of various pathogenic microorganisms. According to a study published by "Ultrasonics sonochemistry", Docynia delavayi polyphenols (DDP) exhibit excellent antioxidant activity with a DPPH radical scavenging IC50 of 3.75 μg/mL. These polyphenols also demonstrate inhibitory activity against carbohydrate digestion enzymes, specifically α-Glucosidase and α-Amylase. DDP shows inhibitory effects on Escherichia coli, Bacillus subtilis, Staphylococcus aureus, and Salmonella. Furthermore, DDP promotes species associated with short-chain fatty acid synthesis while reducing pathogenic bacteria like Shigella sonnei and Klebsiella pneumoniae. The substance also modulates the intestinal metabolic profile by enriching pathways involved in Fatty acid biosynthesis, Fatty acid metabolism, and Fatty acid elongation in mitochondria.
