Bauhinia purpurea

What's the taxonomical classification of Bauhinia purpurea?

Bauhinia purpurea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is placed in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Fabales. It is a member of the family Fabaceae, specifically classified within the genus Bauhinia, and is identified by the species name purpurea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bauhinia purpurea has compound leaves that are distinctly bilobed, resembling the shape of a butterfly or a cow's hoof. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree with a spreading canopy and smooth, greyish bark. Its flowers are large and showy, featuring five petals that range in color from pale pink to deep purple. These blossoms emerge from terminal or axillary clusters and are characterized by prominent, elongated stamens. The fruit develops into long, woody, bean-like pods that contain flat, brown seeds.

What are the pharmacological activities of Bauhinia purpurea?

This plant has potent pharmacological activities including anti-diabetic, anti-inflammatory, anti-microbial, anti-oxidant, and anti-ulcer properties. Its bioactive compounds work to regulate blood glucose levels and suppress harmful inflammatory responses within the body. The plant also exhibits significant antimicrobial capabilities that help combat various pathogenic microorganisms. Additionally, its high antioxidant content protects cells from oxidative stress and damage. These combined therapeutic effects contribute to its potential use in treating various physiological ailments.

According to a study published by "Cardiovascular & hematological agents in medicinal chemistry", the methanolic extract of the Bauhinia purpurea bark was evaluated for its antioxidant and glucose-lowering effects. This investigation utilized in vitro models including the DPPH scavenging method, H2O2 scavenging assay, TBARS assay, and total antioxidant estimation. The study also assessed anti-diabetic activity through ɑ-amylase and α-glucosidase enzyme inhibition and glucose diffusion inhibitory techniques. While the poly herbal formulation containing Bauhinia purpurea showed significantly higher antioxidant and glucose-lowering effects than the individual plant concentrates, the individual plant extracts were subjected to phytochemical examination and GC-MS to distinguish bioactive factors.

According to a study published by "Complementary therapies in medicine", Bauhinia purpurea is identified as one of the most used medicinal plants among 105 persons with DM. The main informed objective for the use of this plant was blood glucose control, which was reported by 69.01% of the participants. These individuals were part of a group where 73.34% were women and 97.14% were diagnosed with type 2 DM. The study involved 46 medicinal plants, with Bauhinia being one of the most frequently mentioned.
