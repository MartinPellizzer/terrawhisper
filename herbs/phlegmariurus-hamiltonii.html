Phlegmariurus hamiltonii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phlegmariurus hamiltonii has pendulous, unbranched stems that are covered in small, scale-like leaves arranged in tight, overlapping spirals. These microphylls are lanceolate in shape and serve to protect the delicate vascular tissue of the climbing plant. The stems often exhibit a reddish or brownish pigmentation, which contrasts with the deep green of the foliage in certain light conditions. Tiny, spore-producing sporangia are located in the axils of these leaves to facilitate reproduction. As the plant matures, the stem length can extend significantly to drape over forest floor debris or tree trunks.
