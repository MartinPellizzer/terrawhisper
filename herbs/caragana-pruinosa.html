Caragana pruinosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Caragana pruinosa has an erect, woody shrub habit characterized by slender, grayish-brown stems and small, simple, lanceolate leaves that are arranged oppositely along the branches. The foliage is typically leathery in texture and exhibits a subtle glaucous or waxy coating on the surface of the blades. During its blooming period, the plant produces small, actinomorphic flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These floral structures are often pale in color and are supported by delicate pedicels. The overall morphological profile of the species is adapted for resilience in its specific ecological niche.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of South America, specifically concentrated within the Guayana Shield area of Venezuela, Guyana, Suriname, and French Guiana. It typically thrives in the humid lowland rainforests and riverine environments of these northern South American territories. The species is often found in areas with high moisture levels and consistent warmth characteristic of the Amazonian periphery. Its range is largely defined by the specific soil compositions and dense canopy structures found in these equatorial ecosystems.
