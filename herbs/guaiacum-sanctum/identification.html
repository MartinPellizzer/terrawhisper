Botanical Identification of Guaiacum (Guaiacum sanctum)

To identify Guaiacum (Guaiacum sanctum) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Guaiacum sanctum belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Fabales, order Fabales, family Zygophyllaceae, genus Guaiacum, and species Guaiacum sanctum. It is a flowering plant known for its medicinal properties in herbal medicine.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of the plant is Guaiacum sanctum, a species within the Burseraceae family. It was first described by the botanist Carl von Linné in the 18th century. This name is widely accepted in modern botanical literature and is used to distinguish it from other related species.

Morphological Characteristics

Guaiacum sanctum is a small to medium-sized tree with a dense, rounded crown. It has a deep taproot system that provides stability and access to groundwater. The stem is smooth, woody, and often flaky, with a distinctive aromatic resinous odor.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Guaiacum sanctum can be distinguished by its smooth, grayish bark, trifoliate leaves with three elliptical leaflets, and yellowish-green flowers. It differs from similar species like Guaiacum officinale by its more pronounced leaf venation and the presence of a distinctive, aromatic resin. The leaves are often slightly hairy underneath, and the fruit is a three-chambered capsule.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Guaiacum sanctum is native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It prefers well-drained, sandy soils. It is typically found at low to moderate altitudes, ranging from sea level to around 600 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Guaiacum sanctum blooms from April to June, producing small yellow flowers. Seed formation occurs from July to September. For identification purposes, the best time to harvest is during the dry season, when leaves are most distinct and the plant is in its active growth phase.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Guaiacum sanctum is closely related to species within the same genus, Guaiacum. It shares key characteristics with other members of the family Burseraceae, such as resin production and leaf structure. These relationships help in distinguishing it from similar species in related genera.
