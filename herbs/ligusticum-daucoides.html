Ligusticum daucoides

What's the taxonomical classification of Ligusticum daucoides?

Ligusticum daucoides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is positioned in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Apiales. This plant is a member of the family Apiaceae, which is further refined into the genus Ligusticum, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species daucoides.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ligusticum daucoides has an erect, herbaceous stem that typically reaches heights of 1 to 2 meters and features hollow, furrowed structures. The leaves are large, compound, and bipinnate or tripinnate, consisting of numerous small, serrated leaflets arranged along multiple axes. Its inflorescence is composed of large, flat-topped compound umbels containing numerous tiny, white flowers. The plant produces small, ribbed, oblong fruits that turn dark as they mature. The root system is characterized by a thick, fleshy rhizome that helps the plant anchor in damp soil.
