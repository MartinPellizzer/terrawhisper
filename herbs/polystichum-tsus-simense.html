Polystichum tsus-simense

What's the taxonomical classification of Polystichum tsus-simense?

Polystichum tsus-simense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It is further categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Polypodiales and the family Polypodiaceae. Finally, this specific plant is identified by its genus, Polystichum, and its unique species designation, tsus-simense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polystichum tsus-simense has pinnate fronds characterized by narrow, lanceolate leaflets with serrated margins and a distinctively leathery texture.

The stipes are typically dark and sturdy, providing strong support for the arching blades. Each pinna is arranged symmetrically along a central rachis that remains relatively stiff throughout the plant's growth. Small sori are located on the undersides of the leaflets, often protected by prominent indusia.

This morphological structure allows the fern to thrive in the humid, shaded environments of its natural habitat.
