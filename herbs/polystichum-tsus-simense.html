Polystichum tsus-simense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polystichum tsus-simense has pinnate fronds characterized by narrow, lanceolate leaflets with serrated margins and a distinctively leathery texture. The stipes are typically dark and sturdy, providing strong support for the arching blades. Each pinna is arranged symmetrically along a central rachis that remains relatively stiff throughout the plant's growth. Small sori are located on the undersides of the leaflets, often protected by prominent indusia. This morphological structure allows the fern to thrive in the humid, shaded environments of its natural habitat.
