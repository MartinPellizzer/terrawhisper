Rubia peregrina var. angustifolia

Rubia peregrina var. angustifolia, commonly known as the Wild Madder, is a plant species belonging to the Rubiaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental properties.

The health benefits of Rubia peregrina var. angustifolia include its use in traditional medicine to treat various ailments such as fever, rheumatism, and gastrointestinal issues.

The therapeutic actions of this plant are attributed to its bioactive constituents, including anthraquinones and alkaloids, which exhibit anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties.

Possible Side Effects of Rubia peregrina var. angustifolia

Rubia peregrina var. angustifolia can have side effects if used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, due to its high concentration of anthraquinones.

Prolonged or excessive consumption may lead to kidney damage or exacerbate pre-existing kidney problems.

Additionally, it can cause allergic reactions, such as skin rashes or itching, in some individuals.

Improper use of this plant may also interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners, which can increase the risk of bleeding complications.
