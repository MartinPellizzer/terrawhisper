What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phragmites australis has upright, perennial stems that reach heights of up to five meters, supported by an extensive network of underground rhizomes and creeping stolons. The leaves are long, lanceolate, and possess a prominent midrib with a papery texture. Large, feathery panicles of silvery-white flowers emerge at the terminal ends of the stalks during the summer months. Each individual stem is cylindrical and hollow, often featuring nodes that resemble small joints. This robust architecture allows the plant to form dense, monotypic stands in wetland environments.
