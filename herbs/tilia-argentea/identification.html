Botanical Identification of Silver Lime (Tilia argentea)

To identify Silver Lime (Tilia argentea) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Silver Lime, or Tilia argentea, belongs to the kingdom Plantae. Its classification places it in the family Tiliaceae, genus Tilia, and species Tilia argentea. The division, class, and order are not specified in the provided data.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Silver Lime is Tilia argentea, first described by the botanist Linnaeus in 1753. It is also known by several synonyms, including Tilia argentea subsp. argentea and Tilia argentea var. argentea. Common names include Silver Lime, Argentea Lime, and White Lime, while regional and historical names vary across different cultures and periods.

Morphological Characteristics

Silver Lime (Tilia argentea) is a small to medium-sized tree with a rounded crown and a spreading growth habit. Its root system is shallow and fibrous, supporting its moderate growth in moist, well-drained soils. The stem is straight and smooth, with a light gray bark that becomes fissured with age. Leaves are heart-shaped, silver-white on the underside, and serrated along the edges. The inflorescence consists of clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in early summer. Flowers are inconspicuous but produce small, spherical fruits containing one or two seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Silver Lime (Tilia argentea) can be distinguished by its silvery-white undersides on the leaves, which are prominently visible. It has small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, and its bark is smooth and pale. It is often confused with Tilia cordata, but the silvery leaf undersides are a key differentiating feature.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Silver Lime (Tilia argentea) is native to parts of Central and Eastern Europe. It thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained, fertile soils. It typically grows at altitudes between 200 and 800 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Silver Lime typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing small, pale yellow flowers. Seed formation occurs in late summer, with mature seeds developing by early autumn. For identification purposes, the best time to observe key features is during the flowering season, as this is when the plant's distinguishing characteristics are most apparent.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Tilia argentea is closely related to other Tilia species, sharing common traits within the Tiliaceae family. It belongs to the genus Tilia, which includes several lime tree species. Its phylogenetic position reflects shared characteristics with related genera, such as leaf structure and floral morphology.
