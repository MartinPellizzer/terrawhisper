Silver Lime (Tilia argentea) Compounds

The primary compounds in Silver Lime include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and tannins, which provide astringent effects. Phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory actions, while coumarin is known for its anticoagulant properties. Saponins and glycosides support immune function, and terpenoids contribute to its aromatic profile.

Silver Lime (Tilia argentea) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are key active compounds in Silver Lime (Tilia argentea). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds support cardiovascular health and may aid in reducing swelling and improving circulation.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Silver Lime (Tilia argentea). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and wound-healing effects. Tannins help reduce swelling and promote tissue repair, making Silver Lime valuable in traditional remedies.

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Silver Lime (Tilia argentea). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound is often found in the bark and leaves, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for respiratory and digestive health.

Coumarin is a key active compound in Silver Lime (Tilia argentea). It contributes to the plant's mild sedative and anti-inflammatory properties. Found in the bark and leaves, coumarin is used in herbal medicine for its calming effects and potential to support digestive health.
