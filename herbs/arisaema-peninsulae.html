Arisaema peninsulae

What's the taxonomical classification of Arisaema peninsulae?

Arisaema peninsulae belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving through its hierarchical structure, the plant is placed in the order Aliasmaetales and the family Araceae. Finally, it is situated within the genus Arisaema, with its specific designation being the species peninsulae.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Arisaema peninsulae contains various bioactive compounds including alkaloids, saponins, and calcium oxalate crystals. These chemical constituents contribute to the plant's specific pharmacological profile and biological activity. The presence of oxalates is particularly notable, as these crystals can cause irritation if handled without care. Researchers often study these phytochemicals to understand their potential therapeutic properties in traditional medicine. The complex mixture of organic compounds within the plant serves as a subject of interest for studying plant-based drug development.

What are the therapeutic actions of Arisaema peninsulae?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including inflammation, fever, swelling, and digestive issues. It is frequently utilized in traditional medicine to alleviate pain associated with swelling or heat in the body. Some practitioners apply it to resolve abscesses and skin irritations caused by toxins. It also serves as a remedy for certain types of coughs and respiratory discomfort. The plant's components are often processed to manage internal imbalances related to heat.
