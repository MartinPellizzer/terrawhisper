Dalbergia hupeana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dalbergia hupeana has pinnately compound leaves consisting of 7 to 15 small, oval leaflets with entire margins and an acute apex. The tree typically reaches a height of 10 to 20 meters, characterized by a spreading crown and grayish-brown bark. Its small, fragrant flowers are clustered in axillary panicles and exhibit a pale yellow or cream color. The fruit is a small, flat, indehiscent pod that contains a single seed. These pods are often brownish in color and are designed for dispersal by wind or water.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the subtropical and tropical regions of southern China, specifically within provinces such as Hunan, Hubei, Guangdong, Guangxi, and Fujian. It typically thrives in diverse habitats including mountain forests and hilly woodlands. The species is often found at various elevations where the climate provides sufficient moisture and warmth. Its distribution is closely linked to the specific soil conditions and ecological niches found in these southern territories. Localized populations may also exist in nearby Southeast Asian regions depending on specific environmental variables.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Dalbergia hupeana targets inflammatory mediators such as cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2), inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS), and various pro-inflammatory cytokines like tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-alpha) and interleukin-6 (IL-6). These interactions are primarily driven by bioactive compounds within the plant, including neoflavonoids and stilbenoids, which inhibit the signaling pathways associated with these proteins. By suppressing the expression of COX-2 and iNOS, the plant effectively reduces the production of prostaglandins and nitric oxide, which are key drivers of pain and swelling. Furthermore, the modulation of these protein targets helps regulate the NF-kappaB signaling pathway to mitigate chronic inflammatory responses. This multi-target approach allows the plant to exert significant anti-inflammatory and antioxidant medicinal effects.

    What are the herbal preparations of Dalbergia hupeana?

    Dalbergia hupeana is prepared as a decoction, a medicinal powder, or a topical liniment to harness its therapeutic properties. The dried bark and wood are most commonly boiled in water to create a concentrated liquid extract used in traditional remedies. Some practitioners grind the dried plant material into a fine powder to be encapsulated or mixed into other substances. These powdered forms allow for more controlled dosing during oral administration. Additionally, the plant can be infused into oils or alcohol to produce topical applications for skin-related issues.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Dalbergia hupeana can cause allergic contact dermatitis, skin rashes, and respiratory irritation due to the presence of sensitizing compounds in its wood and sap. Individuals handling the raw material may experience itching, redness, or swelling upon direct skin contact. Inhaling dust from the wood during sanding or cutting processes can trigger asthma-like symptoms or inflammation of the airways. Some people may also develop hypersensitivity through repeated exposure to its chemical constituents. It is essential to use protective gear to mitigate these potential health risks.
