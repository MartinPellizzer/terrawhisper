Syzygium brachythyrsum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Syzygium brachythyrsum has an erect, woody habit characterized by small, densely packed, terminal panicles of flowers and elliptic to oblong leaves with entire margins. The leaves are typically leathery in texture and possess an opposite arrangement on the branches. Its inflorescence is notably short and compact, which distinguishes it from other species within the genus. The flowers are small and white, featuring numerous protruding stamens that create a brush-like appearance. Small, fleshy fruits develop from these flowers, eventually maturing into drupes.
