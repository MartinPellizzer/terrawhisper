Indigofera scabrida

What's the taxonomical classification of Indigofera scabrida?

Indigofera scabrida belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It falls under the order Fabales and is a member of the family Fabaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its genus, Indigofera, and its specific species designation, scabrida.

      What are the herbal preparations of Indigofera scabrida?

      Indigofera scabrida is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste depending on the intended medicinal use. The leaves and stems are often boiled in water to create a liquid extract for internal consumption. For skin ailments, the plant material is crushed into a fine pulp to form a direct application. Some traditional practices also involve drying the plant to create a powder that can be mixed with oils or water. These varied methods allow the plant's active constituents to be delivered effectively to different parts of the body.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Indigofera scabrida can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and potential gastrointestinal distress if ingested. The plant contains various phytochemicals that may trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals upon topical exposure. Ingesting unprocessed parts of the plant might lead to nausea, vomiting, or stomach cramps. There is also a lack of comprehensive clinical data regarding its long-term systemic toxicity in humans. Therefore, caution is necessary when handling or consuming any part of this species.
