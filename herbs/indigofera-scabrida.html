Indigofera scabrida

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Indigofera scabrida has pinnately compound leaves consisting of several pairs of small, oblong leaflets that are characterized by a scabrid or rough texture. The stems are typically woody at the base and can exhibit a spreading or ascending growth habit. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in axillary racemes, often displaying colors ranging from white to pale pink. The fruit is a small, oblong legume that contains a limited number of seeds. These morphological features collectively help distinguish the species from other members of the Fabaceae family.

      Indigofera scabrida is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste depending on the intended medicinal use.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Indigofera scabrida can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and potential gastrointestinal distress if ingested. The plant contains various phytochemicals that may trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals upon topical exposure. Ingesting unprocessed parts of the plant might lead to nausea, vomiting, or stomach cramps. There is also a lack of comprehensive clinical data regarding its long-term systemic toxicity in humans. Therefore, caution is necessary when handling or consuming any part of this species.
