Hippeastrum rutilum

What's the taxonomical classification of Hippeastrum rutilum?

Hippeastrum rutilum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent, it falls into the order Asparagales and is a member of the family Amaryllidaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific biological classification within the genus Hippeastrum, reaching its most precise identification at the species level as rutilum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hippeastrum rutilum has large, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically a vibrant red or orange-red color with a lighter throat. The blooms emerge from thick, fleshy bulbs that are partially buried in the soil. Long, strap-like green leaves grow from the base of the plant to support photosynthesis. These leaves are succulent in texture and can reach significant lengths during the growing season. The inflorescence consists of multiple flowers held aloft on a sturdy, leafless scape.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires bright, indirect sunlight and well-draining potting soil to thrive in a controlled indoor environment.

You should water it thoroughly only when the top inch of soil feels completely dry to prevent bulb rot. Once the foliage begins to yellow, stop all watering to allow the bulb to enter its necessary dormant period.

This rest phase is essential for ensuring the plant produces strong flower stalks during the next cycle.
