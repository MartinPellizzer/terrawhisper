What is Pelargonium pinnatum?

Pelargonium pinnatum is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is an evergreen shrub with a woody base and a branching habit, typically growing 0.5-1.5 meters tall. The leaves are pinnately compound, with 3-5 pairs of leaflets, each 2-4 cm long, and the margins are crenate. The inflorescences are axillary, with 3-4 flowers, and the flowers are 4-5 cm in diameter, with a red-purple color and a yellow center, and have 5 petals and 5 sepals.

The plant is native to the southwestern part of the Western Cape Province in South Africa.
