What is Sanguisorba (Sanguisorba Officinalis)?

Sanguisorba officinalis, also known as common sanguisorb, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its reddish-purple stems and clusters of small, pinkish flowers, and is traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported diuretic and astringent properties.

The scientific name of Sanguisorba is Sanguisorba officinalis. This plant belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Rosales, family Rosaceae, genus Sanguisorba, and species Sanguisorba officinalis. It is commonly known as the bloodwort in Europe and the Old World, and as the Japanese sorrel in parts of Asia. The plant is also known by the common names red sorrel and bloodroot in some regions.
