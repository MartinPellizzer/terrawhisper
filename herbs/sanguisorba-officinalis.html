Sanguisorba officinalis

What's the taxonomical classification of Sanguisorba officinalis?

Sanguisorba officinalis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is part of the order Rosales and the family Rosaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Sanguisorba, and its species designation, officinalis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Sanguisorba officiinalis contains high concentrations of tannins, specifically proanthocyanidins and ellagitannins, along with alkaloids like berberine and various triterpenoid saponins. These astringent tannins are responsible for the plant's ability to constrict tissues and reduce inflammation in the digestive tract. The presence of alkaloids provides significant antimicrobial and antifungal properties that help combat internal infections. Additionally, the various glycosides and phenolic compounds contribute to its antioxidant activity and blood-stanching effects. These chemical constituents work synergistically to support the plant's traditional use in treating diarrhea and wound healing.

What are the therapeutic actions of Sanguisorba officinalis?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat diarrhea, dysentery, malaria, and various inflammatory conditions of the digestive tract. It contains potent alkaloids that act as astringents to tighten tissues and reduce fluid loss. Historically, practitioners applied its extracts to manage heavy menstrual bleeding and promote wound healing due to its antimicrobial properties. Some traditional medicinal practices also utilize it to help mitigate symptoms of skin infections and minor gastrointestinal distress. These therapeutic applications rely on the plant's ability to constrict blood vessels and combat pathogens.
