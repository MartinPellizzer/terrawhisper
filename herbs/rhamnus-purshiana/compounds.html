Cascara Sagrada (Rhamnus purshiana) Compounds

Cascara Sagrada contains several primary compounds, including alkaloids, tannins, and flavonoids. Alkaloids like emodin and chrysophanol have laxative properties. Tannins contribute to its astringent effects, while flavonoids provide antioxidant benefits. These compounds work together to support digestive health.

Cascara Sagrada (Rhamnus purshiana) Monograph

1. Alkaloid

Alkaloids in Cascara Sagrada (Rhamnus purshiana) are the primary active compounds responsible for its laxative effects. These compounds, including emodin and cascarrubine, stimulate intestinal motility by increasing peristalsis and reducing water absorption in the gut. They are derived from the bark of the plant and are key to its traditional use in herbal medicine.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Cascara Sagrada (Rhamnus purshiana). It acts as a mild astringent, helping to reduce inflammation and support digestive health. Tannins in Cascara Sagrada are primarily responsible for its laxative effects, though they are less potent than other compounds found in the plant.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Cascara Sagrada (Rhamnus purshiana). They contribute to the plant's therapeutic effects, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds support the plant's traditional use in digestive and inflammatory conditions.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Cascara Sagrada (Rhamnus purshiana). It contributes to the plant's laxative effects by stimulating bowel movements. This compound is found in the bark and is responsible for some of the herb's traditional uses in herbal medicine.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Cascara Sagrada (Rhamnus purshiana). It contributes to the plant's laxative effects by stimulating bowel movements. This compound is primarily responsible for the therapeutic properties of Cascara Sagrada in herbal medicine.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Cascara Sagrada (Rhamnus purshiana). It contributes to the plant's laxative effects by stimulating bowel movements. Coumarin also has mild anticoagulant properties, though its primary role in herbal medicine is as a bowel stimulant.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a key class of active compounds in Cascara Sagrada (Rhamnus purshiana). They contribute to the plant's laxative effects by stimulating bowel movements. These compounds are primarily found in the bark and are responsible for the plant's traditional use in treating constipation.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Cascara Sagrada (Rhamnus purshiana). It contributes to the plant's laxative effects by stimulating bowel movements. Saponin works by increasing intestinal motility and promoting water retention in the intestines. It is one of the primary constituents responsible for the herb's traditional use in herbal medicine.

9. Essential oil

The essential oil of Cascara Sagrada (Rhamnus purshiana) contains volatile compounds like cineole and limonene, contributing to its mild stimulant and digestive properties. It works synergistically with the plant's active alkaloids to support gastrointestinal function and mild detoxification.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Cascara Sagrada (Rhamnus purshiana). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health by reducing inflammation and enhancing gut lining integrity. Anthocyanin also acts as an antioxidant, aiding in the plant's traditional use for treating constipation and bowel issues.
