Botanical Identification of Cascara Sagrada (Rhamnus purshiana)

To identify Cascara Sagrada (Rhamnus purshiana) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Cascara Sagrada, or Rhamnus purshiana, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Rhamnales, family Rhamnaceae, genus Rhamnus, and species Rhamnus purshiana. These classifications help identify its botanical relationships and place it within the broader plant kingdom.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The botanical name for Cascara Sagrada is Rhamnus purshiana, a species of the Rhamnaceae family. It was first described by the botanist Archibald Menzies in 1792. Common names include Cascara Sagrada, which translates to "sacred bark" in Spanish, and it is also known as buckthorn in some regions.

Morphological Characteristics

Cascara Sagrada is a deciduous shrub or small tree. It has a deep taproot system and a branched stem that can grow up to 10 meters tall. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges. The inflorescence is a panicle, with small greenish-yellow flowers. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hard seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Cascara Sagrada can be distinguished by its smooth, dark reddish-brown bark that peels in strips, its alternate simple leaves with smooth margins, and its clusters of small yellow flowers followed by red, fleshy berries. It is often confused with other Rhamnus species, but its bark texture and leaf shape are key identifiers.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Cascara Sagrada is native to western North America, primarily found in the Pacific Northwest. It thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils. It typically grows at elevations between 1,000 and 2,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Cascara Sagrada blooms in early spring, producing small yellowish-green flowers. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early fall. Harvesting is typically done in late autumn when the bark is mature and the leaves are dry, aiding in accurate identification.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Cascara Sagrada belongs to the genus Rhamnus, which is part of the Rhamnaceae family. Its closest species include Rhamnus cathartica and Rhamnus alnifolia. These species share similar morphological and chemical characteristics, reflecting their close phylogenetic relationships.
