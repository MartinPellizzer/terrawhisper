Pinus Pinaster Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Pinus pinaster pinosylvin is a complex mixture of oxygenated sesquiterpene derivatives that contribute to the plant's pharmacological properties, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial activities.

Pinus pinaster β-pinene, another monoterpene, exhibits similar bioactive properties, including anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects, and may also have potential in treating respiratory conditions.

Pinus pinaster tannins are polyphenolic compounds with strong astringent properties, which can aid in wound healing, reduce inflammation, and exhibit antimicrobial and antiviral activities.
