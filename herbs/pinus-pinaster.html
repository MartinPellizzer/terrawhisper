Bioactive Constituents

Pinus pinaster pinosylvin is a key bioactive compound found in the leaves and bark of the maritime pine, known for its potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

In addition to pinosylvin, Pinus pinaster contains various terpenes, including α-pinene and β-pinene, which are monoterpene hydrocarbons with antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and bronchodilatory effects.

These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in respiratory and digestive health. Furthermore, the herb is rich in tannins, which are polyphenolic compounds that exhibit astringent, antimicrobial, and antiviral activities, making them beneficial in the management of gastrointestinal infections and skin conditions.

Together, these medicinal constituents highlight the therapeutic potential of Pinus pinaster in modern pharmacology and traditional medicine.
