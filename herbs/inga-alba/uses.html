White Inga (Inga alba) Uses

White Inga is used to treat respiratory infections, inflammation, cough, bronchitis, and asthma. It helps alleviate symptoms of influenza and allergic rhinitis, while also addressing mucosal irritation and skin infections due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

White Inga (Inga alba) Monograph

1. Respiratory infections

White Inga (Inga alba) is used traditionally to treat respiratory infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps alleviate symptoms such as coughing, congestion, and bronchitis by supporting immune function and reducing mucus production. The leaves and bark are commonly prepared as teas or decoctions for therapeutic effects.

2. Inflammation

White Inga (Inga alba) is used to reduce inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory compounds, such as flavonoids and tannins. It is applied topically for skin irritations and internally for conditions like arthritis. Its properties help alleviate swelling and pain, making it a valued remedy in traditional herbal medicine.

3. Cough

White Inga (Inga alba) is used to alleviate cough symptoms due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. The leaves and flowers are typically brewed into a tea to soothe respiratory discomfort and loosen mucus. It is traditionally employed in herbal medicine for its gentle yet effective action on the respiratory system.

4. Bronchitis

White Inga (Inga alba) is traditionally used to treat bronchitis due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. The leaves and flowers are often prepared as teas or decoctions to soothe respiratory irritation and loosen mucus. It helps reduce coughing and inflammation in the airways, providing relief for chronic bronchial conditions.

5. Respiratory tract infections

White Inga (Inga alba) is used traditionally to treat respiratory tract infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps alleviate symptoms such as coughing, congestion, and throat irritation. The leaves and flowers are often prepared as teas or decoctions to support respiratory health. Its soothing effects make it a valued remedy in herbal medicine for clearing mucus and reducing inflammation in the airways.

6. Asthma

White Inga (Inga alba) is used in traditional herbal medicine to help manage asthma symptoms. Its leaves and flowers contain compounds with bronchodilating properties that may ease airway constriction. It is often prepared as a tea or decoction to support respiratory function. However, it should be used under professional guidance to ensure safety and efficacy.

7. Influenza

White Inga (Inga alba) is used in traditional herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms of influenza due to its antipyretic and immune-stimulating properties. It helps reduce fever and supports the body's recovery by enhancing respiratory function and reducing inflammation. The leaves and flowers are commonly prepared as teas or decoctions for this purpose.

8. Allergic rhinitis

White Inga (Inga alba) is used traditionally to alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistaminic properties. The plant's leaves and flowers contain compounds that help reduce nasal congestion and sneezing. It is often prepared as a tea or infusion to support respiratory health during allergy seasons.

9. Mucosal irritation

White Inga (Inga alba) is used to alleviate mucosal irritation due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It is applied topically to soothe inflamed tissues in the mouth, throat, and digestive tract. The plant's compounds help reduce swelling and promote healing of irritated mucous membranes. It is commonly prepared as a decoction or poultice for internal and external use.

10. Skin infections

White Inga (Inga alba) is used to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial properties. The leaves and bark contain compounds that inhibit bacterial and fungal growth. It is often applied topically as a poultice or infused oil. This makes it a valuable remedy in traditional herbal medicine for conditions like eczema and fungal infections.
