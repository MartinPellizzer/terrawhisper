10 Medicinal Constituents Of Inga Alba (White Inga)

Inga alba has active constituents such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids.

These compounds possess antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, which contribute to the plant's medicinal potential. The constituents of Inga alba may help alleviate symptoms associated with various health conditions, including fever, respiratory issues, and skin infections.

For instance, its antioxidant properties can protect against oxidative stress, potentially improving cardiovascular health and reducing the risk of chronic diseases.

1. Flavonoids

Inga alba flavonoids is a class of compounds found in the plant's leaves and stems.

These flavonoids are responsible for the plant's yellow-green color and have been shown to possess numerous biological activities, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties.

The flavonoids present in Inga alba have also been reported to exhibit potential therapeutic effects against various diseases, such as diabetes, hypertension, and cancer.

2. Triterpenoids

Inga alba triterpenoids is a type of compound extracted from the bark and leaves of the plant.

These compounds have been found to possess various bioactive properties, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial activities.

Studies have also shown that Inga alba triterpenoids exhibit significant cytotoxicity against certain cancer cell lines, making them potential candidates for further research into their therapeutic applications.

3. Sterols

Inga alba sterols is a type of natural lipid molecule found in the seeds and oil of the Inga edulis tree, native to the Amazon rainforest.

These sterols have been studied for their potential health benefits, including reducing cholesterol levels, improving heart health, and exhibiting antioxidant properties.

4. Phenolic acids

Inga alba phenolic acids is a type of bioactive compound found in the plant.

These compounds are known for their antioxidant and antimicrobial properties, which may help protect against chronic diseases such as cancer, heart disease, and neurodegenerative disorders.

Inga alba phenolic acids have been shown to exhibit anti-inflammatory activity, inhibit the growth of bacteria and fungi, and scavenge free radicals.
