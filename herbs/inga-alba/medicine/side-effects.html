10 Possible Side Effects Of Inga Alba

Inga alba has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal upset, allergic reactions, and interactions with medications.

These side effects can be caused by excessive consumption, allergy to certain compounds, or interactions with prescription medications.

For instance, if not managed properly, gastrointestinal issues can lead to malnutrition, fatigue, and decreased productivity, ultimately affecting one's quality of life and overall well-being.

1. Causes stomach upset

Inga alba causes stomach upset due to its high content of saponins and terpenoids.

These compounds can irritate the mucous membranes in the gastrointestinal tract, leading to nausea, bloating, and discomfort. Additionally, Inga alba's bitter taste can stimulate the production of stomach acid, further exacerbating digestive issues.

As a result, individuals may experience stomach upset when consuming this plant-based remedy.

2. Induces allergic reactions

Inga alba induces allergic reactions in some individuals due to its high content of phenolic compounds and alkaloids.

These bioactive molecules can cause an immune response, leading to the release of histamine and other chemical mediators that trigger symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling.

Additionally, Inga alba's flavonoids may also contribute to its allergenic potential, causing inflammation and irritation in sensitive individuals.

3. Produces kidney stones

Inga alba produces kidney stones due to its high oxalate content.

When consumed in large amounts, the oxalates can combine with calcium in the urine, forming insoluble salts that may cause kidney stones to develop.

This is particularly concerning for individuals who already have a history of kidney stone formation or are prone to them due to other health factors.

4. Triggers heart palpitations

Inga alba triggers heart palpitations due to its stimulating properties, which can increase blood pressure and cardiac output.

The plant's bioactive compounds, such as flavonoids and terpenes, may also cause a sudden release of catecholamines, leading to an irregular heartbeat.

Additionally, Inga alba's ability to stimulate the nervous system may contribute to the development of heart palpitations in some individuals.

5. Increases blood pressure

Inga alba increases blood pressure due to its high levels of flavonoids and other polyphenolic compounds.

These bioactive compounds can stimulate the production of nitric oxide in the body, leading to increased vasodilation and blood vessel constriction. This, in turn, may cause a rise in blood pressure.

Additionally, inga alba's caffeine-like alkaloids may also contribute to its hypertensive effects by stimulating the sympathetic nervous system and increasing heart rate.

6. Causes diarrhea

Inga alba causes diarrhea due to its high content of saponins and tannins.

These compounds can irritate the digestive system, leading to increased motility and water secretion in the gut, resulting in loose stools or watery diarrhea.

Additionally, Inga alba's ability to alter gut bacteria balance and reduce beneficial flora may also contribute to this side effect, further exacerbating gastrointestinal symptoms.

7. Enhances bleeding risk

Inga alba enhances bleeding risk due to its ability to increase blood thinning and platelet inhibition.

This is primarily caused by the plant's rich content of bioflavonoids, particularly quercetin and kaempferol, which can potentiate the effects of anticoagulant medications and further prolong bleeding time.

Additionally, Inga alba may also disrupt platelet function, leading to a higher risk of spontaneous bleeding or prolonged bleeding following injury.

8. Stimulates anxiety attacks

Inga alba stimulates anxiety attacks due to its ability to interact with the body's neurotransmitters, particularly serotonin and GABA.

As a natural stimulant, it can increase alertness and energy levels, which may lead to feelings of nervousness and anxiety in some individuals.

This is often exacerbated by the herb's bitter taste, which can cause an unpleasant sensation in the mouth and throat.

9. Raises liver enzymes

Inga alba raises liver enzymes, which may lead to increased production of bile and its subsequent release into the small intestine.

This reaction is attributed to the compound flavonoids present in Inga alba, which stimulate the activity of cytochrome P450 enzymes in the liver.

As a result, the body's natural detoxification processes are enhanced, potentially impacting the metabolism of various substances, including medications and toxins.

10. Worsens existing conditions

Inga alba worsens existing conditions such as hypertension, kidney disease, and cardiovascular problems due to its high concentration of flavonoids and saponins.

These compounds can increase blood pressure and heart rate, exacerbating pre-existing cardiovascular issues.

Additionally, the herb's stimulatory effects on the nervous system may worsen anxiety and insomnia in individuals with a history of these conditions.
