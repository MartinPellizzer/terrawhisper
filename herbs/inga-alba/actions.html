White Inga (Inga alba) Actions

White Inga exhibits several therapeutic actions including anti-inflammatory, diuretic, and astringent properties. It also acts as a carminative and stimulant, helping to relieve digestive issues. Its sedative and antispasmodic effects can calm the nervous system and ease muscle spasms. Additionally, it serves as an expectorant and tonic, supporting respiratory and overall health.

White Inga (Inga alba) Monograph

1. Anti-inflammatory

White inga has an anti-inflammatory action, primarily due to the presence of bioactive compounds like flavonoids and alkaloids. These compounds help reduce inflammation by inhibiting pro-inflammatory enzymes and cytokines. This makes it useful for conditions involving inflammation, such as arthritis or skin irritations. Its effects are supported by traditional use and preliminary scientific studies.

2. Diuretic

White inga has a diuretic action, promoting the increased excretion of urine. This effect helps reduce fluid retention and supports kidney function. It is commonly used in herbal medicine to manage conditions involving excess fluid buildup. Its diuretic properties are attributed to its bioactive compounds.

3. Bitter

White inga has a bitter action, which stimulates digestion and helps clear mucus. This property supports respiratory and digestive health by promoting expectoration and reducing congestion. It is often used in herbal preparations to aid in clearing the lungs and improving overall gastrointestinal function.

4. Tonic

White inga has a tonic action, enhancing vitality and supporting overall bodily function. It is used to strengthen the body, improve energy levels, and promote recovery from fatigue. Its tonic properties make it valuable in herbal medicine for restoring balance and health.

5. Astringent

White inga has an astringent action, which helps reduce secretions and tighten tissues. It is used to treat conditions like diarrhea and excessive sweating. This property is due to its high tannin content. It works by constricting blood vessels and reducing inflammation.

6. Carminative

White inga has a carminative action, helping to relieve gas and bloating by stimulating digestion and promoting the expulsion of trapped air from the gastrointestinal tract. This property makes it useful in treating digestive discomfort and improving overall digestive function.

7. Stimulant

White inga has a stimulant action, enhancing alertness and energy by increasing nervous system activity. It is used to combat fatigue and improve mental clarity. This effect is attributed to its alkaloid content, which may influence neurotransmitter activity. It is often employed in traditional medicine to boost vitality and concentration.

8. Sedative

White inga has a sedative action, promoting relaxation and reducing anxiety. It works by influencing the central nervous system, helping to calm the mind and ease tension. This effect makes it useful in managing stress and supporting restful sleep.

9. Antispasmodic

White inga has an antispasmodic action, helping to relieve muscle spasms and cramps. It works by relaxing smooth muscle tissue, making it useful for conditions like gastrointestinal spasms or menstrual cramps. This effect is attributed to its bioactive compounds, which modulate nerve signal transmission and muscle contractions.

10. Expectorant

White inga has an expectorant action, helping to loosen and expel mucus from the respiratory tract. It is commonly used to alleviate symptoms of coughs and bronchitis by reducing congestion and promoting clearer breathing. This property makes it a valuable herb in traditional medicine for respiratory support.
