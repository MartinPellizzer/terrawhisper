White Inga (Inga alba) Compounds

The primary compounds of White Inga include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and tannins, known for their astringent effects. Phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory actions, while coumarin may have anticoagulant properties. Saponins and glycosides are also present, offering potential antimicrobial and cardiovascular benefits.

White Inga (Inga alba) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in White Inga (Inga alba) are bioactive compounds known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments. They help neutralize free radicals and modulate immune responses, supporting overall health. Their presence enhances the therapeutic potential of White Inga in herbal medicine.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in White Inga (Inga alba). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is used in traditional medicine for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins may also support wound healing and reduce digestive tract irritation.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in White Inga (Inga alba), contributing to its antioxidant properties. 5. Saponin
