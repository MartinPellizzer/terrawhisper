Helicia erratica

What's the taxonomical classification of Helicia erratica?

Helicia erratica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Proteales and the family Proteaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Helicia, and its unique species designation, erratica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Helicia erratica has woody stems, elliptical to oblong leaves with entire margins, and small, actinomorphic flowers arranged in axillary clusters. The foliage typically features a coriaceous texture and prominent venation on the underside of the blades. Its flowers possess a distinct pentamerous structure with five petals that are often yellowish or cream in color. The fruit develops from the ovary as a fleshy capsule or drupe-like structure depending on the specific maturity stage. These morphological traits are characteristic of members within the Helicia genus found in tropical environments.
