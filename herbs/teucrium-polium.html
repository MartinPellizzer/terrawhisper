Polium Germander

Teucrium polium

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family

Native Region

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Teucrium polium, commonly known as Polium Germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is also called Germander and Red Germander in various regions. The plant features aromatic leaves, square stems, and small blue to purple flowers. It has been referenced by several synonyms in historical herbal texts.

Active Compounds

Polium Germander contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and essential oils. These compounds work synergistically to provide its medicinal effects, likely through antioxidant and anti-inflammatory mechanisms. The presence of multiple active components enhances its therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Phenolic acid
  • Essential oil

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action
Respiratory Bronchitis, cough, asthma Antimicrobial, expectorant, anti-inflammatory, antiviral, decongestant
Integumentary Skin infections, inflammatory skin conditions, wound healing Antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, antifungal
Digestive Gastrointestinal infections, gastritis, gastrointestinal discomfort Antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, spasmolytic

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory issues such as coughs and colds.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and as an antiseptic.

Extract: Used for skin conditions and as an antispasmodic.

Safety Profile

Polium Germander is contraindicated during pregnancy and lactation due to potential harm to the fetus or infant. It should also be avoided by individuals with known allergies to plants in the Geraniaceae family. Consult a healthcare provider before use, especially for prolonged or high-dose applications.
