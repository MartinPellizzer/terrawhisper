10 Possible Side Effects Of Celastrus Paniculatus

Celastrus paniculatus has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal upset, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, which can be caused by excessive consumption or interactions with other medications.

These side effects can worsen a person's life by leading to dehydration, electrolyte imbalances, and malnutrition, ultimately affecting their physical and mental well-being.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Celastrus paniculatus causes allergic reactions due to its alkaloid compounds, specifically paniciin and celastrol.

These bioactive molecules can trigger an immune response in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as skin rashes, hives, itching, and swelling.

The allergenic potential of Celastrus paniculatus is attributed to the unique molecular structure of its constituents, which may mimic foreign substances in the body and stimulate an immune response.

2. Triggers hypertension rapidly

Celastrus paniculatus triggers hypertension rapidly due to its ability to increase heart rate and blood pressure.

This is attributed to the presence of alkaloids, such as celestamine and panaxicine, which can stimulate the sympathetic nervous system, leading to an increase in cardiac output and peripheral resistance.

Additionally, Celastrus paniculatus contains flavonoids and terpenoids that may also contribute to its hypertensive effects.

3. Induces dizziness severely

Celastrus paniculatus induces dizziness severely in some individuals.

The compound celastrin, present in the plant, is believed to contribute to this adverse effect by interacting with the body's neurotransmitters and causing an imbalance in brain chemistry.

Additionally, the stimulatory properties of Celastrus paniculatus may also lead to a temporary disruption in blood pressure regulation, exacerbating feelings of dizziness.

4. Increases heart rate significantly

Celastrus paniculatus increases heart rate significantly.

This side effect is attributed to the high concentration of alkaloids, particularly celastrol and calactin, which stimulate the cardiovascular system and enhance sympathetic nervous activity.

The increased levels of epinephrine and norepinephrine in the body contribute to the rise in heart rate, making it a common adverse reaction among individuals who use this herb for medicinal purposes.

5. Leads to stomach upset

Celastrus paniculatus leads to stomach upset due to its bitter and astringent properties that can irritate the mucous membranes in the digestive tract.

The plant's active compounds, such as alkaloids and terpenoids, may also stimulate digestion and increase gastric acid secretion, leading to discomfort, nausea, and bloating.

6. Proves toxic fatally

Celastrus paniculatus proves toxic fatally due to its high concentration of alkaloids, particularly celestine and panidone.

These compounds can cause a range of adverse effects, including nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain when ingested in large quantities or for extended periods.

The toxicity is attributed to the ability of these alkaloids to bind with various biomolecules, disrupting normal cellular function and leading to severe health complications if left unchecked.

7. Interferes with blood clotting

Celastrus paniculatus interferes with blood clotting due to its ability to inhibit platelet aggregation and prolong bleeding time.

This is attributed to the presence of bioactive compounds like flavonoids and alkaloids in the herb, which can interact with blood components and disrupt the normal coagulation process.

As a result, individuals using Celastrus paniculatus may experience excessive bleeding or bruising more easily, requiring attention to ensure proper wound healing and hemostasis.

8. Enhances sedation excessively

Celastrus paniculatus enhances sedation excessively due to its potent ability to interact with the body's neurotransmitters, particularly GABA and serotonin.

The herb's alkaloids, such as celastricine and celestamine, bind to these neurotransmitter receptors, leading to a profound calming effect that can quickly become overwhelming for some individuals.

This excessive sedation is often characterized by feelings of lethargy, drowsiness, and impaired cognitive function.

9. Causes nausea intensely

Celastrus paniculatus causes nausea intensely when consumed due to its alkaloid compounds, particularly uric acid and flavonoids.

These bioactive molecules can stimulate the digestive system, leading to an upset stomach and nausea in some individuals.

Additionally, Celastrus paniculatus may cause a rapid increase in gut motility, further exacerbating gastrointestinal discomfort.

10. Disrupts respiratory system

Celastrus paniculatus disrupts respiratory system, causing issues such as coughing, shortness of breath, and difficulty inhaling.

This occurs due to the presence of alkaloids like paniculin, which can stimulate the nervous system and cause constriction of airways. Additionally, Celastrus paniculatus contains flavonoids that may lead to increased mucus production, further exacerbating respiratory problems.

These compounds interact with the body's natural responses, resulting in an imbalance that affects breathing patterns.
