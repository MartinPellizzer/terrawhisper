Kuroa

Picrorhiza kurroa

Kuroa (Picrorhiza kurroa) is a perennial herb native to the high altitude regions of the Himalayas, known for its use in traditional Ayurvedic medicine to support liver function and treat digestive disorders.

Botanical Identification

Kuroa, scientifically known as Picrorhiza kurroa, belongs to the family Boraginaceae. It is also called Kurchak in Hindi and is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas. The plant has dark purple roots, hairy leaves, and blue-violet flowers. It is sometimes referred to as Indian Picrorhiza in traditional herbal texts.

Active Compounds

Kuroa contains key bioactive compounds such as alkaloids, terpenoids, and flavonoids. These compounds work synergistically to enhance its therapeutic effects. The alkaloids may influence neurotransmitter activity, while terpenoids and flavonoids contribute to anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and liver disorders.

Extract: Used for treating liver and gallbladder disorders.

Safety Profile

Kuroa is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and lactation due to limited safety data. Always consult a healthcare provider before use, especially for long-term or high-dose applications.
