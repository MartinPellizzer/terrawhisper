10 Health Benefits Of Sorbus Aucuparia (Rowan)

Sorbus aucuparia, also known as rowan, has health benefits such as reducing inflammation and antioxidant properties, which can alleviate symptoms of arthritis, digestive issues, and boost the immune system.

The flavonoids and phenolic acids in rowan extract have been shown to possess anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities.

For example, rowan berries can help prevent scurvy and reduce the severity of cold and flu symptoms, improving overall well-being and quality of life.

This article explains in details the 10 best health benefits of Sorbus aucuparia.

1. Reduces inflammation

Sorbus aucuparia reduces inflammation because of its rich antioxidant and flavonoid content.

The plant's extracts have been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines, while promoting the activity of anti-inflammatory enzymes.

Additionally, rowan's polyphenolic compounds have been found to scavenge free radicals, further reducing oxidative stress and inflammation.

2. Fights cancer cells

Sorbus aucuparia fights cancer cells because of its unique combination of bioactive compounds.

The plant contains a rich source of flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which have been shown to exhibit potent anti-cancer properties.

Specifically, the extract has been found to inhibit the proliferation of cancer cells, induce apoptosis, and suppress the formation of new blood vessels, thereby impeding tumor growth and metastasis.

3. Lowers blood pressure

Sorbus aucuparia lowers blood pressure because of its unique combination of flavonoids, phenolic acids, and other bioactive compounds.

These compounds have been shown to exhibit potent vasodilatory and anti-inflammatory effects, which can help to relax blood vessels and reduce blood pressure.

Additionally, rowan's flavonoids have been found to inhibit the activity of enzymes involved in the conversion of angiotensin I to angiotensin II, further contributing to its blood pressure-lowering effects.

4. Kills bacteria naturally

Sorbus aucuparia kills bacteria naturally because of its unique composition of polyphenolic compounds, particularly sorbic acid.

This natural antimicrobial agent is produced within the rowan's leaves and berries, making it a powerful tool against a wide range of bacteria.

When bacteria come into contact with the rowan's chemicals, they are effectively neutralized, ensuring the tree's defense against potential pathogens and maintaining its overall health.

5. Relieves allergy symptoms

Sorbus aucuparia relieves allergy symptoms because of its unique properties.

The fruit of the rowan tree contains quercetin, a flavonoid that has potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. This natural compound helps to reduce histamine production, a key factor in allergic reactions, thereby alleviating symptoms such as itching, sneezing, and congestion.

Additionally, rowan's extract has been shown to inhibit the release of pro-inflammatory cytokines, further supporting its role in relieving allergy symptoms.

6. Soothes skin irritations

Sorbus aucuparia soothes skin irritations because of its unique combination of flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids.

These bioactive compounds have been shown to possess anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, making rowan an effective natural remedy for calming irritated skin.

The berries, in particular, have been traditionally used to treat various skin issues, including acne, eczema, and burns, due to their ability to reduce redness, itching, and swelling.

7. Treats respiratory issues

Sorbus aucuparia treats respiratory issues because of its unique combination of bioactive compounds.

The fruit, leaves, and bark of the rowan plant contain flavonoids, terpenoids, and saponins, which have been shown to have anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties.

These compounds help to soothe and calm the respiratory tract, reducing inflammation and congestion, and making it a traditional treatment for conditions such as bronchitis, asthma, and chronic coughs.

8. Enhances immune function

Sorbus aucuparia enhances immune function because it contains a wealth of bioactive compounds, particularly polyphenols and flavonoids, which exhibit potent immunomodulatory effects.

These compounds have been shown to stimulate the production of cytokines, which play a crucial role in activating immune cells and combating infection.

Additionally, the antioxidants present in rowan may help reduce oxidative stress and inflammation, further supporting immune function and overall health.

9. Reduces fever quickly

Sorbus aucuparia reduces fever quickly because of its remarkable antipyretic properties.

The berries of the rowan tree contain a compound called sorbic acid, which has been shown to effectively lower body temperature by reducing prostaglandins, key players in the fever response.

This natural antipyretic agent works rapidly to bring down fever, providing relief from the discomfort and distress associated with high temperatures.

10. Inhibits virus replication

Sorbus aucuparia inhibits virus replication because it contains a unique set of compounds called sorbic acid and polyphenols.

These compounds have been shown to possess antiviral properties, which enable them to interfere with the replication of viruses by binding to viral proteins and preventing them from entering host cells.

This inhibitory effect can occur at multiple stages of the viral life cycle, making rowan a promising natural source of antiviral agents.
