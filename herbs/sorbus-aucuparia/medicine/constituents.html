10 Medicinal Constituents Of Sorbus Aucuparia (Rowan)

Sorbus aucuparia has active constituents such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids.

These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, making it effective in treating various ailments. For instance, the antioxidants can help protect against cell damage, reducing the risk of chronic diseases like cancer and heart disease. The antimicrobial properties can also aid in wound healing and infection prevention.

Overall, rowan's medicinal constituents have the potential to improve people's lives by providing a natural defense against diseases and promoting overall health and well-being.

1. Flavonoids

Sorbus aucuparia flavonoids is a group of plant compounds that have been identified in its berries and leaves.

These flavonoids, including quercetin, kaempferol, and isoquercetin, possess antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which may contribute to the berry's traditional use in folk medicine for treating various health issues, such as diarrhea and fever.

They also show promise in inhibiting the growth of certain cancer cells and improving cardiovascular health.

2. Quercetin

Sorbus aucuparia quercetin is a flavonoid compound that has been isolated from its berries.

It exhibits significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a valuable natural remedy for various health issues.

Quercetin has been shown to have potent antiviral and antibacterial effects, as well as the ability to inhibit the growth of cancer cells.

3. Kaempferol

Sorbus aucuparia kaempferol is a flavonoid glycoside compound that can be extracted from its berries.

Kaempferol is responsible for the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which have been linked to its traditional use in folk medicine.

The compound has been shown to exhibit potential health benefits, including cardiovascular protection, anticancer activity, and neuroprotection against oxidative stress.

4. Gallic acid

Sorbus aucuparia gallic acid is a naturally occurring compound found in the leaves of this species.

It has been studied for its potential medicinal properties and antioxidant effects.

Gallic acid has been shown to possess antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory activities, making it a promising compound for use in various applications, including wound healing and disease prevention.

5. Ellagic acid

Sorbus aucuparia ellagic acid is a type of polyphenolic compound found in its leaves and fruits.

This bioactive molecule has been extensively studied for its potential health benefits, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and anticancer properties.

Ellagic acid has been shown to exhibit significant inhibitory effects on the growth of cancer cells and may also play a role in protecting against neurodegenerative diseases such as Alzheimer's and Parkinson's.

6. Catechins

Sorbus aucuparia catechins is a class of flavonoid compounds found in its leaves and fruits.

These bioactive molecules have been reported to exhibit various pharmacological activities, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties.

Catechins are responsible for the fruit's dark coloration and have been traditionally used in traditional medicine to treat various health issues, such as diarrhea and respiratory infections.

7. Phenolic acids

Sorbus aucuparia phenolic acids is a type of secondary metabolite that plays a crucial role in the plant's defense mechanism.

These compounds are responsible for the antioxidant and antimicrobial activities exhibited by the plant.

Phenolic acids are present in the bark, leaves, and fruits of the rowan tree, providing protection against pathogens, insects, and oxidative stress.

8. Tannins

Sorbus aucuparia tannins is a type of polyphenolic compound found in its bark and leaves.

These tannins are responsible for the plant's astringent properties, which can be used to preserve food and leather products.

Sorbus aucuparia tannins have been traditionally used in various applications, including dyeing textiles, tanning hides, and as a natural preservative in food and pharmaceutical industries.

9. Aldehydes

Sorbus aucuparia aldehydes is a class of organic compounds found in its bark, leaves, and fruits.

These aldehydes are responsible for the characteristic aroma and flavor of the rowan tree. They have been shown to possess various biological activities, including antimicrobial, antioxidant, and anti-inflammatory effects.

The aldehydes present in Sorbus aucuparia have also been used in traditional medicine for centuries to treat a range of ailments.

10. Terpenes

Sorbus aucuparia terpenes is a class of organic compounds responsible for its distinctive aroma and medicinal properties.

These terpenes are found in the fruits, leaves, and bark of the plant and have been traditionally used to treat various ailments such as fever, inflammation, and digestive issues.

The most common terpenes present in Sorbus aucuparia include alpha-pinene, beta-pinene, and limonene, which possess antimicrobial and antioxidant activities.
