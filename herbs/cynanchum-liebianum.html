    What is Cynanchum liebianum?

    Cynanchum liebianum is an Asiatic plant species in the family Apocynaceae, native to China and India.

    It is an evergreen perennial climber or vine with woody base and twining stems, which are 2-6 meters long and bear opposite, simple, and entire leaves that are 5-10 cm long and 2-4 cm wide. The inflorescence is a terminal corymb of 5-10 flowers, each 2-3 cm long and 1-2 cm wide, with a greenish-yellow corolla and a greenish-white throat. The flowers are pollinated by bees and other insects, and produce a pair of follicles that split open to release numerous seeds.

    The plant contains alkaloids, including glycosides, which contribute to its medicinal properties.
