Solanum americanum

What's the taxonomical classification of Solanum americanum?

Solanum americanum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical structure, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Solanales, it is a member of the family Solanaceae. Finally, its taxonomic identity is completed by its placement in the genus Solanum, specifically as the species americanum.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Solanum americanum contains glycoalkaloids such as solasodine, tomatine, and solamargine, which serve as its primary bioactive medicinal compounds. These alkaloids are known for their potent antimicrobial, antifungal, and cytotoxic properties. Additionally, the plant contains various steroidal saponins that contribute to its diverse pharmacological profile. Research suggests these chemical constituents may play a role in regulating cellular processes and fighting infections. The presence of phenolic compounds further enhances its potential antioxidant capabilities.

What are the therapeutic actions of Solanum americanum?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat inflammatory conditions, skin infections, respiratory ailments, and various digestive disorders. Its bioactive compounds are often employed in traditional medicine to alleviate symptoms of rheumatism and joint pain. Some practitioners also utilize extracts from the plant to manage wounds and fungal skin issues due to its antimicrobial properties. Additionally, it has been documented in folk remedies for addressing coughs and other respiratory distress. These diverse applications stem from the complex chemical profile found within its leaves and fruit.
