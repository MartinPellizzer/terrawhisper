Salvia honania

What's the taxonomical classification of Salvia honania?

Salvia honania belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, the plant is part of the order Lamiales and falls under the family Lamiaceae. Ultimately, its taxonomic identity is defined by its placement within the genus Salvia, specifically as the species Salvia honania.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Salvial honania has a perennial growth habit characterized by erect, square-shaped stems that reach heights of up to 60 centimeters. The leaves are primarily basal and heart-shaped with serrated margins and a textured, rugose surface. Flowering occurs in terminal spikes where small, bilabiate flowers emerge in shades of blue or violet. These inflorescences are supported by bracts that provide structural protection for the developing buds. The root system consists of a sturdy rhizomatous base that allows the plant to persist through various seasonal changes.
