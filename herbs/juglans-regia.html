Juglans regia

What's the taxonomical classification of Juglans regia?

Juglans regia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its hierarchical descent, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized under the order Fagales and the family Juglandaceae. Ultimately, it is identified by the genus Juglans and the specific species regia.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Juglans regia contains high concentrations of polyphenols, specifically hydrolyzable tannins like juglone, alongside essential fatty acids, flavonoids, and vitamins such as E and B6. These bioactive compounds function primarily as potent antioxidants that help neutralize free radicals in the body. Juglone exhibits significant antimicrobial and antifungal properties, making it useful in treating certain skin conditions and infections. Additionally, the high levels of alpha-linolenic acid support cardiovascular health by reducing inflammation within the vascular system. Research also suggests that the plant's flavonoids may offer neuroprotective benefits by mitigating oxidative stress in brain cells.

What are the therapeutic actions of Juglans regia?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various skin conditions such as eczema, psoriasis, and acne, as well as digestive issues like intestinal parasites and dysentery. The leaves and hulls contain tannins and antimicrobial properties that help reduce inflammation on the skin surface. Some traditional practices also utilize the nut oil to soothe minor wounds or fungal infections. Additionally, extracts may be applied to combat certain types of bacterial ailments due to their high antioxidant content. This multifaceted medicinal profile makes the walnut tree a significant resource in herbal remedies.
