Juglans regia juglone is a naphthoquinone compound known for its antimicrobial, antifungal, and antitumor activities, although it can be toxic in high concentrations and is primarily found in the roots and leaves of the plant.

Juglans regia tannins, which are polyphenolic compounds, contribute to the plant's astringent properties and have been associated with antioxidant, antimicrobial, and gastroprotective effects due to their ability to bind proteins and inhibit enzymatic activity.

Juglans regia leads to nausea symptoms as its high concentration of certain alkaloids and oils can stimulate the vomiting reflex in some people.
