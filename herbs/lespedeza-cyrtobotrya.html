What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lespedeza cyrtobotrya has trifoliate leaves with elliptical to oblong leaflets, small yellow pea-like flowers, and distinctive curved, asymmetrical seed pods. The stems are typically slender and can exhibit a decumbent or ascending growth habit depending on the environment. Its leaves feature fine pubescence on the surfaces and margins, which helps distinguish it from similar species. The flowers are arranged in small clusters or racemes and possess the characteristic structure of the Fabaceae family. The most defining morphological trait is the legume, which curves significantly and contains a single seed.
