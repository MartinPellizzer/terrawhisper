Chinese Peony (Paeonia lactiflora) Compounds

The primary compounds in Chinese Peony include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and glycosides, known for their anti-inflammatory effects. Coumarins contribute to its anticoagulant properties, while phenolic acids and tannins provide antioxidant and antimicrobial benefits. Saponins and terpenoids also play roles in its therapeutic effects.

Chinese Peony (Paeonia lactiflora) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are key active compounds in Chinese Peony (Paeonia lactiflora). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds are found in the roots and flowers, and are used in traditional medicine for their therapeutic effects.

2. Glycoside

Glycosides are key active compounds in Chinese Peony (Paeonia lactiflora). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. These compounds are often responsible for the therapeutic effects observed in traditional herbal medicine. Glycosides are extracted from the roots and used in formulations for various health conditions.

3. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Chinese Peony (Paeonia lactiflora). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. Coumarin is found in the roots and has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine to treat conditions like menstrual pain and digestive disorders. Its pharmacological effects are well-documented in herbal formulations.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Chinese Peony (Paeonia lactiflora). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound is found in high concentrations in the roots, which are the primary medicinal part of the plant. Phenolic acid supports the traditional use of Chinese Peony in treating conditions like inflammation and pain.

5. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Chinese Peony (Paeonia lactiflora). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins help in reducing swelling and may support wound healing in traditional herbal applications.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Chinese Peony (Paeonia lactiflora). It exhibits anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, contributing to the plant's traditional use in treating skin conditions and respiratory infections. Saponin also aids in enhancing the bioavailability of other compounds in the plant.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Chinese Peony (Paeonia lactiflora). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, playing a key role in its traditional use for treating conditions like pain and inflammation. Specific terpenoids, such as paeoniflorin, are isolated and studied for their therapeutic potential in modern pharmacology.

8. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Chinese Peony (Paeonia lactiflora). It contributes to the plant's vibrant color and has antioxidant properties that support cardiovascular health and reduce inflammation. Found in the petals and roots, anthocyanin is valued for its therapeutic effects in traditional herbal medicine.

9. Essential oil

The essential oil of Chinese Peony (Paeonia lactiflora) contains aromatic compounds like paeoniflorin and benzoylpaeoniflorin. These compounds contribute to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, making it valuable in traditional herbal medicine for treating pain and inflammation.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Chinese Peony (Paeonia lactiflora). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, though it is less prominent than the flavonoids. Alkaloids in peony may support anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects, though their specific roles and mechanisms require further research.
