Botanical Identification of Chinese Peony (Paeonia lactiflora)

To identify Chinese Peony (Paeonia lactiflora) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

The Chinese Peony, Paeonia lactiflora, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Paeoniales, family Paeoniaceae, genus Paeonia, and species Paeonia lactiflora. This classification places it within the group of flowering plants, highlighting its botanical significance in herbal medicine.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Chinese Peony is Paeonia lactiflora, first described by Linnaeus in 1790. It is also known by several synonyms, including Paeonia officinalis and Paeonia tenuifolia. Common names such as peony, Chinese peony, and emperor's flower are widely used. In China, it is referred to as “芍药” (sháo yào), reflecting its traditional medicinal significance.

Morphological Characteristics

Chinese Peony (Paeonia lactiflora) is a deciduous shrub with a short, thick root system that stores nutrients. Its stems are woody and branched, growing up to 1-2 meters tall. Leaves are compound, alternate, and have 3-5 leaflets with serrated edges. The inflorescence is a single, terminal flower on a peduncle. Flowers are large, showy, and have five petals, typically pink or red. The fruit is a follicle containing several seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Chinese Peony (Paeonia lactiflora) can be distinguished by its large, showy pink to red flowers with a distinct yellow center, deeply lobed leaves, and thick, smooth bark. It differs from similar species like Paeonia suffruticosa by having fewer petals and a more robust, shrub-like growth habit. Its leaves are more deeply cut and have a waxy texture compared to other peonies.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Chinese Peony (Paeonia lactiflora) is native to China, particularly in the northwestern and northeastern regions. It thrives in temperate climates with cold winters and warm summers. It prefers well-drained, loamy soil and grows best at altitudes between 500 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Chinese Peony (Paeonia lactiflora) blooms in late spring to early summer, typically between May and June. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early fall, around August to September. For identification purposes, the best time to harvest is during the flowering period when the plant is most identifiable.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Paeonia lactiflora is closely related to species like Paeonia suffruticosa and Paeonia ostii. It belongs to the genus Paeonia, which is part of the family Ranunculaceae. The family is characterized by showy flowers and often toxic compounds, traits shared by Paeonia lactiflora.
