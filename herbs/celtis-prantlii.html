Celtis prantlii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Celtis pranlii has simple, alternate, ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated margins and asymmetrical bases that grow from woody branches. The plant features a deciduous habit with bark that often develops distinctive lenticels as it matures. Small, inconspicuous flowers appear in clusters, leading to the development of fleshy drupes. These fruits are typically small and round, serving as a key identifier for the species. The overall structure consists of a spreading canopy supported by a robust, woody stem system.
