What's the taxonomical classification of Celtis prantlii?

Celtis prantlii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is positioned under the order Rosales. This plant is further categorized into the family Cannabaceae, falling under the genus Celtis, and is ultimately identified by its specific species name, prantlii.

Celtis pranlttii is prepared as a decoction of the bark, an infusion of the leaves, or a topical poultice made from the crushed fruit or stem. Leaf infusions are frequently used in traditional medicine to reduce inflammation or soothe respiratory discomfort. When applied externally, the softened plant matter serves as a soothing compress for wounds or localized swelling. These various methods allow the medicinal properties of the plant to be utilized for both internal and external therapeutic purposes.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Celtris pranliti can cause localized skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, or gastrointestinal distress if ingested in significant quantities. While many parts of the plant are studied for medicinal properties, direct contact with the sap or foliage may trigger redness and itching in sensitive individuals. Some people might experience swelling or a rash if the plant touches their skin during handling. Ingestion of unprocessed parts could lead to stomach aches or nausea depending on the individual's tolerance. It is essential to exercise caution and avoid direct contact if you have a history of plant allergies.
