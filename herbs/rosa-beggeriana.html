Rosa beggeriana

What's the taxonomical classification of Rosa beggeriana?

Rosa beggeriana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Rosales. This plant is situated within the family Rosaceae, which is a large and diverse group of flowering plants. Finally, its specific taxonomic identification is defined by the genus Rosa and the unique species name beggerianiana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rosa beggeriania has pinnate leaves composed of five to seven leaflets with serrated margins and a climbing habit facilitated by recurved prickles along its stems. The plant features small, single flowers that are typically white or pale pink with five distinct petals. Its stems are slender and arching, allowing the shrub to scramble over other vegetation in its natural habitat. The foliage is often dark green and provides a dense texture to the sprawling canes. Once the flowering period concludes, the plant produces small, fleshy hips that serve as a primary dispersal mechanism.
