Machilus velutinus

What's the taxonomical classification of Machilus velutinus?

Machilus velutinus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Laurales and is a member of the family Lauraceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Machilus and the species velutina.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Machilus velutinus has simple, alternate, elliptical leaves that are characterized by a velvety, densely pubescent underside and a smooth, coriaceous upper surface.

The leaf margins are typically entire or slightly undulate, often tapering to a fine point at the apex. The plant features small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are arranged in axillary panicles. Its woody structure consists of a medium-sized tree with grayish bark and a spreading canopy.

The resulting fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that transitions in color as it matures.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Machilus velutinus can cause allergic contact dermatitis, skin irritation, or localized swelling if its sap or foliage comes into direct contact with sensitive skin. While the plant is not widely documented as highly toxic, individuals with specific botanical sensitivities may experience redness or itching upon handling the leaves. Some people might also encounter respiratory discomfort if they inhale dust or pollen released from the plant in enclosed spaces. There is currently limited clinical research regarding the systemic effects of accidental ingestion in humans. Therefore, caution is advised when interacting with the plant to avoid unpredictable dermatological reactions.
