Excoecaria agallocha

What's the taxonomical classification of Excoecaria agallocha?

Excoecaria agallocha belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is organized under the order Malpighiales and is a member of the family Euphorbiaceae. Finally, it is placed within the genus Excoecaria, specifically identifying as the species agallocha.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Excoecaria agallocha contains bioactive phytochemicals including alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, tannins, and terpenoids that exhibit significant pharmacological properties. These secondary metabolites contribute to the plant's documented antimicrobial and antioxidant activities. Specific studies have highlighted the presence of phenolic compounds which help in neutralizing free radicals within biological systems. Additionally, the alkaloids found in the plant are often investigated for their potential neuroprotective and anti-inflammatory effects. These diverse chemical constituents form the basis for its traditional use in treating various ailments.

What are the therapeutic actions of Excoecaria agallocha?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat gastrointestinal issues like diarrhea and dysentery, respiratory ailments such as coughs and asthma, and skin infections. Its medicinal properties are frequently utilized to manage fever and reduce inflammation throughout the body. In traditional medicine, various parts of the plant are prepared to address urinary tract infections and wounds. The bioactive compounds within the species contribute to its effectiveness in combating bacterial and fungal pathogens. These therapeutic applications make it a significant component in several ethnobotanical practices.
