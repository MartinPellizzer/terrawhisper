Excoecaria agallocha

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Excoecaria agallocha has woody, evergreen shrubs or small trees characterized by simple, opposite leaves that are elliptic to oblong in shape with entire margins. The foliage typically exhibits a coriaceous texture and a glossy green surface on the upper side. Its stems are often slender and can show slight branching patterns typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in axillary clusters, which eventually develop into small, globose fruits. These fruits contain seeds that are essential for the plant's reproductive cycle within its natural habitat.

The species is most frequently documented in the understory of evergreen rainforests across the Indo-Malayan realm. Its range extends through various island chains within the Malay Archipelago. What are the herbal preparations of Excoecaria agallocha?

Excoecaria agallocha is prepared as a decoction, a powder, or a paste derived from its leaves, bark, and roots to treat various ailments. The decoction is frequently used to alleviate respiratory issues and fever by boiling the plant parts in water. When applied topically, the crushed leaves are formed into a paste to heal skin infections or reduce swelling. Some traditional practitioners also utilize the powdered form to create medicinal infusions or to be mixed with other substances. These different methods ensure that the active phytochemicals are accessible for both internal and external therapeutic use.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Excoecaria agallocha can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or gastrointestinal distress if ingested or applied improperly. The plant contains various phytochemicals that may trigger localized redness or itching upon contact with sensitive skin. If consumed in uncontrolled doses, users might experience nausea, vomiting, or abdominal discomfort. There is also limited clinical data regarding the long-term systemic toxicity of its chemical constituents in humans. Caution is advised because the specific physiological impact can vary significantly depending on the individual's sensitivity.
