What's the taxonomical classification of Scirpus rosthornii?

Scirpus rosthornii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Poales. This plant is a member of the family Cyperaceae, specifically categorized within the genus Scirpus, and is identified by its unique species name, rosthornii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Scirpus rosthornii has culms that reach heights of up to 1 meter, featuring slender, triangular stems that are typically green or slightly brownish. The leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures at the base of the stems, which emphasizes the dominant appearance of the foliage-like bracts. These bracts are linear and can extend significantly beyond the inflorescence, mimicking the look of true leaves. The inflorescence consists of small, brownish flower clusters arranged in a compact or slightly loose manner near the apex. Each flower is protected by small scales and is characterized by its minute, inconspicuous structure typical of the Cyperaceae family.
