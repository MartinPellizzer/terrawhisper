Phoebe hunanensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phoebe hunanenensis has coriaceous, lanceolate to elliptic leaves that are arranged alternately along the woody stems and possess entire margins with distinct pinnate venation. The foliage is typically dark green on the upper surface and may exhibit a slightly paler underside. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves to facilitate reproduction. These plants eventually produce small, fleshy drupes that serve as the fruit type for the species. The overall architecture of the plant consists of a woody structure characteristic of the Lauraceae family.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the subtropical limestone forests of the southern tip of the Florida Keys, specifically concentrated within Monroe County. Its range is extremely limited and restricted to a few specific islands such as Big Pine Key and Cudjoe Key. Within these small pockets, it thrives in the unique microclimates provided by maritime hammocks. The species relies on the stable, high-moisture environments found in these coastal habitats to survive. Due to this highly localized presence, the plant is considered vulnerable to habitat loss and environmental changes.
