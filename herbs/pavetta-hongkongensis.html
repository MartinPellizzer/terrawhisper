Pavetta hongkongensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Pavetta hongkongensis?

Pavetta hongkongensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Gentianales. This plant is a member of the family Rubiaceae, falling under the genus Pavetta, with its specific classification being the species hongkongensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pavetta hongkongensis has opposite, elliptic to lanceolate leaves that measure between 5 and 15 centimeters in length and possess entire margins with slightly wavy edges.

The leaf texture is typically coriaceous, meaning it has a leathery feel, and the surfaces are often dark green on top with a paler underside. This shrubby plant features clusters of fragrant, white, tubular flowers that emerge from terminal cymes. These blossoms eventually develop into small, fleshy drupes that turn dark red or black when fully mature.

The stems are woody and can branch extensively to form a dense, bushy habit.
