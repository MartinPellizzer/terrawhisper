Pavetta hongkongensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pavetta hongkongensis has opposite, elliptic to lanceolate leaves that measure between 5 and 15 centimeters in length and possess entire margins with slightly wavy edges. The leaf texture is typically coriaceous, meaning it has a leathery feel, and the surfaces are often dark green on top with a paler underside. This shrubby plant features clusters of fragrant, white, tubular flowers that emerge from terminal cymes. These blossoms eventually develop into small, fleshy drupes that turn dark red or black when fully mature. The stems are woody and can branch extensively to form a dense, bushy habit.
