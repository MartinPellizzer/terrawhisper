Pavetta hongkongensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Pavetta hongkongensis?

Pavetta hongkongensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Gentianales. This plant is a member of the family Rubiaceae, falling under the genus Pavetta, with its specific classification being the species hongkongensis.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including fever, malaria, dysentery, and inflammatory conditions. The leaves and other parts of the specimen are often prepared into traditional medicinal decoctions. These preparations are primarily utilized by local practitioners to manage gastrointestinal distress and infectious symptoms. Some applications also involve using the plant to alleviate swelling or general bodily aches. This botanical species serves as a significant component in regional ethnobotanical practices for managing acute illnesses.
