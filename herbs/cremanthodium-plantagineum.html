What's the taxonomical classification of Cremanthodium plantagineum?

Cremanthodium plantagineum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further into its specific lineage, the plant is part of the order Asterales and the family Asteraceae. Finally, its unique biological identity is defined by its placement within the genus Cremanthodium, with the specific designation of the species plantagineum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cremanthodium plantagineum has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by erect stems and pinnately compound leaves that typically feature five to nine pairs of leaflets. The leaflets are generally ovate or lanceolate in shape with serrated margins and acute tips. Its inflorescence consists of small, actinomorphic flowers arranged in terminal corymbose cymes. These flowers are usually white or pale yellow and possess five distinct petals surrounding a central cluster of stamens. The root system consists of a sturdy rhizome that allows the plant to spread through its local habitat.
