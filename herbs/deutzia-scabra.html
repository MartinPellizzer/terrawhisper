Deutzia scabra

What's the taxonomical classification of Deutzia scabra?

Deutzia scabra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Cornales and the family Hydrangeaceae, eventually placing it within the genus Deutzia. Ultimately, the specific identification of this plant is defined by its species name, scabra.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Deutzia scabra contains various bioactive compounds including flavonoids, tannins, and triterpenoids that contribute to its pharmacological properties. These chemical constituents are primarily utilized in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and antioxidant activities. Researchers have identified specific phenolic acids within the plant that may assist in reducing oxidative stress in biological systems. Some studies also suggest that extracts from the leaves and stems possess antimicrobial potential against certain pathogens. This complex mixture of secondary metabolites makes the plant a subject of interest for developing new therapeutic agents.

What are the therapeutic actions of Deutzia scabra?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin conditions such as eczema, psoriasis, and various types of dermatitis. The bark and leaves contain medicinal properties that help soothe inflamed skin and reduce irritation. In traditional medicine, decoctions made from the plant are often applied topically to manage itching and swelling. Some practitioners also utilize the plant to address minor wounds or superficial skin infections. These applications are primarily focused on the plant's anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.
