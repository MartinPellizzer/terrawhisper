Deutzia scabra has opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that measure approximately 2 to 7 centimeters in length and feature finely serrated margins along with a rough, scabrous texture on the upper surface. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are white and typically appear in terminal clusters during the spring months. The woody stems exhibit a somewhat spreading habit, contributing to a rounded and bushy architectural form.
