Clematis menglaensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clematis menglaensis has opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically composed of three leaflets with serrated margins and acute tips. The petioles are slender and facilitate the climbing habit of the woody perennial vine. Its inflorescence consists of small, yellowish-green flowers that lack showy petals but feature prominent stamens. The reproductive structures include numerous pistils that eventually develop into feathery, persistent styles characteristic of the genus. These morphological traits allow the plant to thrive in the shaded understory of subtropical forests.

It typically inhabits the subtropical monsoon climate zones characterized by high humidity and seasonal rainfall. Within these specific areas, the species is found growing in forest margins and shaded rocky outcrops. Its range is restricted to these specific mountainous terrains in southern China.       Clematis menglaensis is prepared primarily as a decoction through boiling the dried plant material in water to extract its active medicinal compounds. This traditional method allows the liquid essence to be consumed as a concentrated tea for therapeutic purposes.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Clematis menglaensis can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential neurological symptoms if ingested or touched due to the presence of protoanemonin. This toxic compound is often released when the plant's tissues are crushed or damaged. Contact with the sap can lead to redness, itching, or blistering on the skin. If swallowed, individuals may experience severe stomach pain, nausea, and vomiting. In some cases, high doses may result in more serious systemic reactions.
