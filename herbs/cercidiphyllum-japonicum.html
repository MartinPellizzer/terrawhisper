Cercidiphyllum japonicum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cercidiphyllum japonicum has deciduous, small, heart-shaped leaves that feature fine, serrated margins and a bright green coloration. These leaves are arranged alternately along slender, woody branches that contribute to a spreading canopy. The tree produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers that appear in early spring before the foliage fully develops. In autumn, the foliage undergoes a dramatic transformation, turning vibrant shades of yellow, orange, and red. The bark is typically smooth and gray in young specimens but becomes deeply furrowed and textured as the tree matures.

      What are the herbal preparations of Cercidiphyllum japonicum?

      Cercidiphyllum japonicum is prepared as an infusion, a decoction, or an aromatic extract derived primarily from its leaves and bark.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Cercidiphyllum japonicum can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or digestive upset if ingested or touched directly. While the plant is generally considered non-toxic to humans, some individuals may develop contact dermatitis upon skin exposure. Inhalation of pollen during the flowering season might trigger respiratory sensitivities or seasonal allergies in susceptible people. There is currently limited clinical research regarding the long-term systemic effects of consuming any part of this species. Caution is advised when handling the plant if you have a history of hypersensitivity to woody flora.
