What's the taxonomical classification of Atropanthe sinensis?

Atropanthe sinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it falls under the order Solanales and is a member of the family Solanaceae. Finally, the specific identification of the plant is determined by its genus, Atropanthe, and its unique species designation, sinensis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains high concentrations of tropane alkaloids, specifically atropine, hyoscyamine, and scopolamine. These medicinal compounds act as competitive antagonists at muscarinic acetylcholine receptors within the nervous system. Atropine is frequently utilized in clinical settings to treat bradycardia and certain types of organophosphate poisoning. Scopolamine serves as a potent agent for managing motion sickness and reducing postoperative nausea. Because these substances are highly toxic in large doses, their application requires precise pharmacological control.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause severe anticholinergic toxicity characterized by nausea, vomiting, dizziness, tachycardia, dry mouth, blurred vision, confusion, hallucinations, delirium, constipation, urinary retention, hyperthermia, mydriasis, seizures, and coma. Ingesting its alkaloids disrupts the nervous system, leading to rapid heart rate and significant sensory disturbances. The physical impact often involves the inability to urinate and a dangerous rise in body temperature. Mental impairment progresses from simple confusion to intense hallucinations and delirium. In extreme cases of poisoning, the patient may experience violent seizures or descend into a life-threatening coma.
