Cyanthillium cinereum

What's the taxonomical classification of Cyanthillium cinereum?

Cyanthillium cinereum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. This plant is further categorized into the family Asteraceae, which is a prominent group of flowering plants. Within this family, it is identified by the genus Cyanthillium and is specifically designated as the species cinereum.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Cyanthillium cinereum contains high concentrations of bioactive alkaloids, terpenoids, and phenolic compounds that serve as its primary medicinal agents. These specific chemical constituents work synergistically to provide potent anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The presence of specialized flavonoids within the plant tissue also aids in reducing oxidative stress at a cellular level. Researchers have noted that the concentration of these compounds often fluctuates based on the plant's environmental conditions. Such diverse chemical profiles make the species a significant subject for pharmacological investigation.

What are the therapeutic actions of Cyanthillium cinereum?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including digestive disorders, inflammatory conditions, and skin infections. It contains bioactive compounds that help reduce swelling and soothe internal irritation. Some practitioners utilize its extracts to manage gastrointestinal distress by regulating bowel functions. Additionally, its antimicrobial properties make it useful for topical applications on minor wounds. The medicinal application of this species often focuses on its ability to mitigate systemic oxidative stress.
