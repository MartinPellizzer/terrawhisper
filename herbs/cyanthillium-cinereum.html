Cyanthillium cinereum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cyanthillium cinereum has lanceolate, deep green leaves with serrated margins, a woody central stem, and small, bell-shaped violet flowers. The foliage exhibits a prominent midrib and a slightly waxy texture that aids in moisture retention. Each flower is supported by a slender pedicel and possesses five fused petals that flare slightly at the rim. The plant's root system is fibrous and extends laterally to stabilize its upright growth habit. During the flowering stage, the stems often develop a reddish tint near the nodes.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Cyanthillium cinereum targets various inflammatory mediators and oxidative stress enzymes, specifically inhibiting cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2), lipoxygenase (LOX), and inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS). Additionally, the plant's bioactive compounds modulate antioxidant enzymes like superoxide dismutase (SOD) and catalase to mitigate cellular damage. By regulating these specific protein pathways, the plant exhibits significant anti-inflammatory and neuroprotective properties. These molecular mechanisms collectively contribute to its traditional use in treating swelling and oxidative-related ailments.
