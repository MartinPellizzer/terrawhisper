Cyanthillium cinereum

What's the taxonomical classification of Cyanthillium cinereum?

Cyanthillium cinereum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. This plant is further categorized into the family Asteraceae, which is a prominent group of flowering plants. Within this family, it is identified by the genus Cyanthillium and is specifically designated as the species cinereum.

    What are the molecular targets of this plant?

    Cyanthillium cinereum targets various inflammatory mediators and oxidative stress enzymes, specifically inhibiting cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2), lipoxygenase (LOX), and inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS). These interactions effectively reduce the production of pro-inflammatory prostaglandins and nitric oxide within biological systems. Additionally, the plant's bioactive compounds modulate antioxidant enzymes like superoxide dismutase (SOD) and catalase to mitigate cellular damage. By regulating these specific protein pathways, the plant exhibits significant anti-inflammatory and neuroprotective properties. These molecular mechanisms collectively contribute to its traditional use in treating swelling and oxidative-related ailments.

    What are the therapeutic actions of Cyanthillium cinereum?

    Cyanthillium cinereum has anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties that facilitate the treatment of various skin infections and inflammatory conditions. The plant contains bioactive secondary metabolites such as flavonoids and phenolic compounds that mitigate oxidative stress within cells. These chemical constituents work together to inhibit the growth of pathogenic bacteria and fungi on the skin surface. Furthermore, the soothing effects of its extracts help to reduce swelling and redness in localized tissue areas. Regular application of its derivatives may support faster wound healing by promoting cellular regeneration.

      What are the herbal preparations of Cyanthillium cinereum?

      Cyanthillium cinereum is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical ointment to utilize its bioactive compounds effectively. When making a decoction, the dried roots are boiled in water to extract tougher medicinal elements. Infusions are created by steeping the softer leaves or flowers in hot water for a shorter duration. For external applications, the plant material is often infused into a base of oils or fats to produce a soothing salve. These various methods allow for both internal consumption and localized skin treatment depending on the intended therapeutic goal.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Cyanthillium cinereum can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, acute neurotoxicity, and potential organ failure if ingested. Consuming even small amounts of the plant may lead to intense nausea, vomiting, and abdominal cramping. Some individuals might also experience neurological symptoms such as dizziness, tremors, or blurred vision shortly after exposure. In more extreme cases, the toxins within the plant can cause lasting damage to the liver and kidneys. It is critical to avoid all contact with this species due to these unpredictable and dangerous reactions.
