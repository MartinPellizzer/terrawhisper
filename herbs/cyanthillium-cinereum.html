Cyanthillium cinereum

What's the taxonomical classification of Cyanthillium cinereum?

Cyanthillium cinereum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. This plant is further categorized into the family Asteraceae, which is a prominent group of flowering plants. Within this family, it is identified by the genus Cyanthillium and is specifically designated as the species cinereum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cyanthillium cinereum has lanceolate, deep green leaves with serrated margins, a woody central stem, and small, bell-shaped violet flowers. The foliage exhibits a prominent midrib and a slightly waxy texture that aids in moisture retention. Each flower is supported by a slender pedicel and possesses five fused petals that flare slightly at the rim. The plant's root system is fibrous and extends laterally to stabilize its upright growth habit. During the flowering stage, the stems often develop a reddish tint near the nodes.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Cyanthillium cinereum targets various inflammatory mediators and oxidative stress enzymes, specifically inhibiting cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2), lipoxygenase (LOX), and inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS). By regulating these specific protein pathways, the plant exhibits significant anti-inflammatory and neuroprotective properties. These molecular mechanisms collectively contribute to its traditional use in treating swelling and oxidative-related ailments.

What are the therapeutic actions of Cyanthillium cinereum?

Cyanthillium cinereum has therapeutic properties that function as a bitter, cholagogue, choleretic, digestive stimulant, and stomachic. Its primary action involves stimulating the secretion of bile from the liver to aid in the breakdown of fats. By acting as a cholagogue, it promotes the efficient expulsion of bile into the small intestine. The plant also serves as a digestive stimulant that enhances overall gastrointestinal motility. These combined effects make it an effective stomachic for improving appetite and easing various digestive discomforts.
