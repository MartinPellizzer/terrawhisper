Konjac (Amorphophallus konjac) Uses

Konjac is used to treat gastric ulcers, constipation, and gastroesophageal reflux disease due to its high fiber content and mild mucilage. It may help manage inflammatory bowel disease and gastritis by soothing the digestive tract. Its low calorie and high glucomannan content also support diabetes management by regulating blood sugar levels.

Konjac (Amorphophallus konjac) Monograph

1. Gastric ulcer

Konjac root contains glucomannan, a soluble fiber that coats the stomach lining, reducing irritation and promoting healing in gastric ulcers. It also absorbs excess acid, alleviating symptoms. Traditional use in herbal medicine supports its role in managing ulcer-related inflammation and discomfort.

2. Constipation

Konjac, rich in glucomannan fiber, is effective for constipation by absorbing water and increasing stool bulk. It promotes regular bowel movements and supports digestive health. Its natural properties make it a gentle, plant-based remedy for mild to moderate constipation.

3. Gastroesophageal reflux disease

Konjac, or Amorphophallus konjac, is used in herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms of gastroesophageal reflux disease. Its high fiber content, particularly glucomannan, absorbs excess stomach acid and promotes healthy digestion. This natural remedy helps reduce reflux by thickening stomach contents and strengthening the lower esophageal sphincter. It is often recommended as a safe, plant-based alternative to conventional treatments.

4. Inflammatory bowel disease

Konjac has shown potential in managing inflammatory bowel disease due to its high glucomannan content, which supports gut health and reduces inflammation. It may help regulate digestion and soothe intestinal lining, though more research is needed for specific therapeutic applications.

5. Gastrointestinal disorders

Konjac has been traditionally used to address gastrointestinal issues such as constipation and indigestion. Its high dietary fiber content, particularly glucomannan, absorbs water and promotes regular bowel movements. It also forms a gel-like substance in the stomach, aiding in digestion and reducing bloating. Konjac is considered a gentle and effective natural remedy for maintaining digestive health.

6. Gastritis

Konjac, or Amorphophallus konjac, is used in herbal medicine to support digestive health, particularly in managing gastritis. Its high mucilage content forms a protective layer over the stomach lining, reducing irritation and inflammation. It also promotes healthy digestion by absorbing excess stomach acid and regulating bowel movements.

7. Acid reflux

Konjac, rich in glucomannan, can help alleviate acid reflux by forming a protective barrier in the stomach and reducing excess acid. Its soluble fiber absorbs water, promoting a feeling of fullness and slowing digestion. It is often used as a natural alternative to conventional antacids in herbal medicine.

8. Diabetes

Konjac contains glucomannan, a soluble fiber that helps regulate blood sugar levels by slowing carbohydrate absorption. It may improve insulin sensitivity and support weight management, both critical in diabetes care. Studies suggest it can lower postprandial glucose spikes, making it a beneficial supplement when used under medical guidance.

9. Hyperglycemia

Konjac contains glucomannan, a soluble fiber that helps regulate blood sugar levels by slowing glucose absorption. It improves insulin sensitivity and reduces postprandial glucose spikes. Studies suggest it may support glycemic control in people with type 2 diabetes. Regular consumption may aid in managing hyperglycemia when combined with a balanced diet and medical supervision.

10. Type 2 diabetes

Konjac, rich in glucomannan, may help regulate blood sugar levels in type 2 diabetes by slowing carbohydrate absorption. Its soluble fiber also improves insulin sensitivity and reduces post-meal glucose spikes. Studies suggest it can complement diabetes management when included in a balanced diet.
