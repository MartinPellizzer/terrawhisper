Styrax dasyanthus

What's the taxonomical classification of Styrax dasyanthus?

Styrax dasyanthus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Ericales. The plant is a member of the family Styracaceae, specifically falling under the genus Styrax, with its unique identity defined by the species name dasyanthus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Styrax dasyanthus has elliptical to lanceolate leaves that are characterized by a dense covering of fine, soft hairs on both the upper and lower surfaces.

These leaves are arranged oppositely along the woody stems of the shrub or small tree. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that emerge from axillary clusters. Its inflorescences are typically supported by pedicels that also exhibit a pubescent texture.

The fruit is a small, globose capsule that eventually turns brown upon maturity.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Styrax dasyanthus can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. The plant contains various chemical compounds that may trigger inflammatory responses upon physical contact with sensitive areas. Individuals with pre-existing skin conditions might experience redness, itching, or localized swelling after touching the foliage or bark. If any part of the plant is consumed, it could lead to nausea or abdominal discomfort. It is important to avoid direct contact to prevent these adverse reactions.
