Machilus salicinus

What's the taxonomical classification of Machilus salicinus?

Machilus salicinus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it falls under the order Laurales and the family Lauraceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Machilus, and its species name, salicinus.

These herbal preparations are often steeped for extended periods to ensure maximum potency. The resulting liquid is typically consumed warm to assist with its physiological effects.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

While specific toxicological studies are limited, many plants in the Lauraceae family contain volatile oils that may trigger sensitivities in certain individuals. Direct contact with the leaves or bark might result in redness or itching upon the skin. If consumed, the chemical constituents could potentially lead to nausea or stomach discomfort. It is generally advisable to avoid skin contact and ingestion to prevent these adverse reactions.
