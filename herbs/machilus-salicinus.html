Machilus salicinus

What's the taxonomical classification of Machilus salicinus?

Machilus salicinus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it falls under the order Laurales and the family Lauraceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Machilus, and its species name, salicinus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Machilus salicinus has simple, alternate, elliptical to oblong leaves that measure approximately 5 to 15 centimeters in length with entire margins and acute or acuminate tips.

The leaf texture is typically coriaceous, meaning it feels leathery to the touch, and the upper surface often displays a dark green hue. The undersides of the foliage frequently exhibit a paler coloration due to a fine layer of pubescence. Its branching pattern is woody and spreading, supporting small clusters of inconspicuous flowers.

The fruit consists of small, fleshy drupes that transition in color as they reach maturity.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Machilus salicinus can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or gastrointestinal distress if ingested or handled improperly. While specific toxicological studies are limited, many plants in the Lauraceae family contain volatile oils that may trigger sensitivities in certain individuals. Direct contact with the leaves or bark might result in redness or itching upon the skin. If consumed, the chemical constituents could potentially lead to nausea or stomach discomfort. It is generally advisable to avoid skin contact and ingestion to prevent these adverse reactions.
