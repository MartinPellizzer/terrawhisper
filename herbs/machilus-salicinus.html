Machilus salicinus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Machilus salicinus has simple, alternate, elliptical to oblong leaves that measure approximately 5 to 15 centimeters in length with entire margins and acute or acuminate tips. The leaf texture is typically coriaceous, meaning it feels leathery to the touch, and the upper surface often displays a dark green hue. The undersides of the foliage frequently exhibit a paler coloration due to a fine layer of pubescence. Its branching pattern is woody and spreading, supporting small clusters of inconspicuous flowers. The fruit consists of small, fleshy drupes that transition in color as they reach maturity.
