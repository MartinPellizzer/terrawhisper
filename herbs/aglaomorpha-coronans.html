Aglaomorpha coronans

What's the taxonomical classification of Aglaomorpha coronans?

Aglaomorpha coronans belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. This specific plant is a representative of the family Polypodiaceae, situated within the genus Aglaomorpha, and is identified by its unique species designation, Aglaomorpha coronans.

    Aglaomorpha coronans has therapeutic properties that primarily function to clear heat, resolve toxicity, and detoxify the blood to treat skin conditions and swellings.       What are the herbal preparations of Aglaomorpha coronans?

      Aglaomorpha coronans is prepared primarily as a decoction through boiling the dried plant material in water to extract its medicinal properties. This liquid extract is often combined with other herbs in traditional formulas to treat respiratory issues or alleviate pain. Some practitioners may also use the plant in powder form to be mixed into warm liquids or swallowed directly. In certain regional applications, the crushed leaves are applied topically as a poultice to treat skin ailments. These various methods ensure that the active compounds are effectively delivered to the body for therapeutic use.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Aglaomorpha coronans can cause skin irritation, redness, and allergic contact dermatitis upon direct physical contact with its tissues. The plant contains various secondary metabolites that may trigger localized inflammatory responses in sensitive individuals. If the sap or crushed parts of the plant touch the eyes, it can lead to stinging sensations and temporary redness. While systemic toxicity is not extensively documented in humans, any ingestion should be avoided to prevent potential gastrointestinal distress. Regular handling without protection may lead to cumulative sensitivity over time.
