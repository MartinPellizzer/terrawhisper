Saxifraga candelabrum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saxifraga candelabrum has a basal rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that grow from a thick rhizome and support a tall, branched flowering stem. The leaves are typically bright green with slightly serrated edges and a smooth, succulent texture. From the center of the rosette, a sturdy inflorescence rises, branching out into a candelabra-like structure. Each branch terminates in small, star-shaped flowers that feature five white petals and central yellow stamens. This unique architectural arrangement allows the plant to display numerous blossoms simultaneously.
