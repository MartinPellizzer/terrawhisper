Aruncus sylvester

What's the taxonomical classification of Aruncus sylvester?

Aruncus sylvester belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant falls under the order Rosales and is a member of the family Rosaceae. At the most specific levels of its classification, it is identified by the genus Aruncus and the species sylvester.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aruncus sylvester has woody, rhizomatous stems that produce large, pinnate leaves composed of numerous small, serrated leaflets. These compound leaves are arranged alternately along the branches and display a bright green color during the growing season. The plant produces dense, creamy-white panicles of small flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Each flower cluster consists of many tiny blossoms that create a feathery, plume-like appearance atop the upright stalks. The overall structure is bushy and can reach heights of up to two meters in ideal woodland conditions.
