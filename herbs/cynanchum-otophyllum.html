Cynanchum otophyllum

What's the taxonomical classification of Cynanchum otophyllum?

Cynanchum otophyllum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Gentianaales. As a member of the family Apocynaceae, it is situated within the genus Cynanchum, with its specific designation being the species otophyllum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cynanchum otophyllum has climbing stems with opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that feature smooth margins and acute tips. The foliage typically presents a glossy texture and a vibrant green color on the upper surface. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils in clusters, displaying a pale coloration. The reproductive structures consist of specialized follicles that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. These botanical characteristics define its growth habit as a herbaceous vine within the Apocynaceae family.

What are the pharmacological activities of Cynanchum otophyllum?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological activities including anti-epileptic, anti-hepatic fibrosis, anti-social defeat stress, and antidepressant properties. Research indicates that its extracts can effectively mitigate seizure activity, showcasing potent antiepilepsy effects in various models. Furthermore, the plant exhibits protective mechanisms that help reduce the progression of liver scarring through its anti-hepatic fibrosis activity. In neurological studies, it has also shown promise in alleviating symptoms related to depression and managing the psychological impact of social defeat stress. These diverse therapeutic potentials highlight the plant's importance in medicinal chemistry.

As discussed in a study, Cynanchum otophyllum is a traditional Chinese medicine used for treating epilepsy, and its essential active component is otophylloside B. In C. elegans models of Alzheimer's disease, otophylloside B extended lifespan, increased heat stress-resistance, delayed body paralysis, and increased the chemotaxis response. The component protects against Aβ toxicity by decreasing Aβ deposition through the reduction of Aβ expression at the mRNA level. This protection is mediated by increasing the activity of heat shock transcription factor by upregulating the expression of hsf-1 and its target genes, hsp-12.6, hsp-16.2, and hsp-70. Additionally, otophylloside B increased the expression of sod-3 by partially activating DAF-16, although SKN-1 was not essential for this protection.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains five specific medicinal compounds known as cynanotin A, cynanotin B, cynanotin C, cynanotin D, and cynanotin E. These unique chemical constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's diverse biological activities. Researchers study these molecules to understand their potential applications in pharmaceutical development. Each individual cynanotin variant may possess distinct therapeutic properties that contribute to the overall medicinal value of the species. Ongoing scientific investigations aim to isolate these compounds for targeted medical treatments.

As discussed in a study, eight new C21 steroidal glycosides named cynanotins A-H (1-8) and fifteen known analogues were isolated from the roots of Cynanchum otophyllum. Compounds 3-15 demonstrated moderate cytotoxic activities against HL-60 cell lines with IC50 values ranging from 11.4 to 37.9 µM. Specifically, compounds 5, 9, and 10 showed cytotoxic activities against five human tumor cell lines with IC50 values between 11.4 and 36.7 µM. Compound 11 displayed moderate cytotoxic activities against HL-60, SMMC-7721, MCF-7, and SW480 cell lines with IC50 values of 12.2-30.8 µM. Furthermore, compounds 5 and 9-11 exhibited more potential inhibitory activity against MCF-7 cells with IC50 values of 16.1-25.6 µM compared to the positive control of 35.0 µM.
