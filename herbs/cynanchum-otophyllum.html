Cynanchum otophyllum

What's the taxonomical classification of Cynanchum otophyllum?

Cynanchum otophyllum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Gentianaales. As a member of the family Apocynaceae, it is situated within the genus Cynanchum, with its specific designation being the species otophyllum.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse array of medicinal compounds including cynanchin A-G, cynanotin A-H, cynanotophyllosides A-D, cynotosides A-E, and otophylloside A-B. These specific phytochemicals are often studied for their potential bioactive properties in pharmacological research. The presence of these unique glycosides and alkaloids suggests complex chemical interactions within the plant's cellular structure. Scientists analyze these distinct molecules to understand how they contribute to the plant's overall therapeutic profile. Such detailed chemical mapping is essential for developing new treatments based on natural sources.

As discussed in a study, seven undescribed C21 steroids known as cynanchin A-G were isolated from the roots of Cynanchum otophyllum. These were found alongside thirteen known analogues within the plant. Researchers tested all of the isolates for their anti-hepatic fibrosis activity. Specifically, compounds 4-6, 10-12, and 14-17 demonstrated moderate or significant inhibitory effects regarding the proliferation of hepatic stellate cells induced by TGF-beta1 in vitro.
