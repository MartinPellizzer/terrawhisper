Metadina trichotoma

What's the taxonomical classification of Metadina trichotoma?

Metadina trichotoma belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized into the order Gentianaales. This plant is a member of the family Rubiaceae, and its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement within the genus Metadina, resulting in the binomial name Metadina trichotoma.

    What are the herbal preparations of Metadina trichotoma?

    Metadina trichotoma is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice depending on the intended medicinal application. The leaves and stems are often boiled in water to create a concentrated liquid used for internal consumption. In some traditional practices, the plant material is crushed into a paste to be applied directly to the skin for localized relief. These preparations are carefully dosed to ensure the active compounds are effectively released for therapeutic use. Practitioners may also use alcohol-based tinctures to preserve the plant's properties for longer periods.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Metadina trichotoma can cause adverse reactions such as gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, and potential neurological symptoms if ingested or applied improperly. While some traditional uses suggest medicinal properties, the concentration of bioactive compounds varies significantly between individual plants. Direct contact with the leaves or stems may trigger localized allergic dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Ingesting large quantities could lead to nausea, vomiting, or more severe systemic toxicity. Because scientific data on its human safety profile is limited, careful caution is necessary when handling this species.
