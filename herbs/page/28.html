Annual St.

John's Wort, scientifically known as Hypericum annulatum, is a medicinal herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, renowned for its adaptogenic properties and historical use in traditional medicine.

This plant is valued for its potential to support mood regulation, reduce anxiety, and alleviate symptoms of mild depression due to its active compounds like hypericin and hyperforin.

Traditionally, it has been used in herbal medicine to treat wounds, skin conditions, and nervous system disorders, with roots in ancient Greek and Roman healing practices.

In modern wellness, it is often incorporated into supplements and topical formulations for its purported anti-inflammatory and antidepressant effects.

Its unique feature includes a distinctive, slightly spicy and honey-like flavor, making it not only a medicinal herb but also a rare spice used in some traditional cuisines.
