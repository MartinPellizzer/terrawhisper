Virginia Thyme, scientifically known as Thymus virginicus, is a medicinal herb native to the southeastern United States, valued for its adaptogenic properties and aromatic qualities.

This herb is traditionally used to support respiratory health, reduce inflammation, and boost immunity due to its rich content of thymol, a potent antimicrobial compound.

Historically, Native American tribes used Virginia Thyme to treat ailments such as coughs, colds, and skin infections, while it was also incorporated into culinary practices for its distinctive, slightly minty flavor.

In modern wellness practices, it is used in herbal teas, aromatherapy, and topical applications to promote mental clarity and stress relief.

Its rare active compound, thymol, makes it a unique and valuable herb in both traditional and contemporary herbal medicine.
