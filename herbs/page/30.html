Khaya, scientifically known as Khaya senegalensis, is a medicinal tree native to Africa, valued for its diverse therapeutic properties.

It is commonly referred to as the African shea tree and is recognized as a traditional adaptogen and herbal remedy used for centuries in African cultures.

The tree's bark, leaves, and seeds are rich in bioactive compounds, offering primary benefits such as anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects.

Traditionally, it has been used to treat ailments like fever, skin infections, and digestive issues, and its oil is still employed in skincare and aromatherapy.

In modern wellness practices, Khaya senegalensis is gaining attention for its potential in natural medicine, with research highlighting its unique compounds like khayacine, which may support immune function and cardiovascular health.
