Bael Tree, scientifically known as Aegle marmelos, is a tropical fruit-bearing tree native to the Indian subcontinent, valued for its medicinal properties and cultural significance.

It is considered a medicinal herb with adaptogenic qualities, known for its ability to help the body resist stress and maintain balance.

The fruit and its seeds are rich in nutrients, offering primary benefits such as digestive support, anti-inflammatory effects, and antioxidant properties.

Traditionally used in Ayurveda and Indian folk medicine, the bael fruit has been employed to treat ailments like diarrhea, constipation, and respiratory issues.

In modern wellness practices, it is used in herbal supplements and natural remedies, with its unique compound, marmelosin, noted for its potential health benefits, making it a rare and historically significant plant in traditional and contemporary medicine.
