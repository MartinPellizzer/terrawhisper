Blueweed, scientifically known as Lycopus virginicus, is a medicinal herb native to North America, valued for its adaptogenic and anti-inflammatory properties.

This perennial plant has been traditionally used by Native American tribes for its ability to support respiratory and digestive health, as well as to alleviate anxiety and fatigue.

Its unique blue or purple flowers and aromatic leaves have made it a distinctive component in herbal remedies and teas.

In modern wellness practices, blueweed is often incorporated into supplements and aromatherapy products for stress relief and immune support.

Historically, it was also used as a spice in culinary traditions, highlighting its dual role as both a medicinal and flavorful plant.
