White Mustard, scientifically known as Brassica alba, is a medicinal herb that has been used for centuries for its therapeutic properties and adaptogenic qualities.

It is valued for its ability to support respiratory health, reduce inflammation, and aid in digestion due to its rich content of essential oils and alkaloids.

Traditionally, it has been used in Ayurvedic and Chinese medicine to treat coughs, bronchitis, and digestive ailments, while also being a common spice in various cuisines for its distinctive sharp and pungent flavor.

In modern wellness, it is increasingly utilized in herbal formulations and dietary supplements to promote immune support and respiratory function.

One unique feature of White Mustard is its high concentration of sinigrin, a sulfur-containing compound that contributes to its medicinal effects and historical significance as a key ingredient in mustard plasters and traditional remedies.
