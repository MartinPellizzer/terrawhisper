Vanilla, scientifically known as vanilla planifolia, is a tropical orchid native to Mexico and widely cultivated for its aromatic pods, which are both a spice and a medicinal herb.

It is valued for its unique flavor and for containing bioactive compounds like vanillin, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

Traditionally, vanilla has been used in various cultures for its calming effects, often incorporated into teas and remedies to reduce stress and promote relaxation.

In modern wellness practices, it is used as an adaptogen to support emotional balance and as a natural remedy for digestive issues and skin care.

Its historical significance as a prized ingredient in ancient Mesoamerican rituals further highlights its deep cultural and medicinal roots.
