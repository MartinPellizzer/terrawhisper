Pale Coneflower, scientifically known as Echinacea pallida, is a flowering plant native to North America, commonly used as a medicinal herb for its immune-boosting properties.

It is often classified as an adaptogen, helping the body resist stress and maintain balance, and has been historically used as a spice in traditional dishes due to its distinctive, slightly sweet and bitter flavor.

Traditionally, Native American tribes such as the Cherokee and Lakota used it to treat infections, wounds, and respiratory ailments, while early European settlers adopted it for its healing potential.

In modern wellness practices, it is widely used as a natural remedy to support immune health, reduce cold and flu symptoms, and promote overall vitality.

One of its unique features is the presence of alkamides, rare active compounds that may contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antiviral effects, making it a valuable component in herbal medicine.
