Adinandra nitida

What's the taxonomical classification of Adinandra nitida?

Adinandra nitida belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is positioned in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Ericales. This plant is a member of the family Pentaphylacaceae and is specifically categorized within the genus Adinandra, with its unique designation being the species nitida.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Adinandra nitida has simple, opposite, elliptical to oblong leaves with entire margins and acute apices, growing on woody stems.

The leaf texture is typically coriaceous, providing a leathery feel to the foliage. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often appearing in small clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that develop from the inferior ovary characteristic of the Rubiaceae family.

Its overall structure is that of a perennial shrub or small tree with a branching habit.
