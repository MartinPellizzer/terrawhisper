Adinandra nitida

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Adinandra nitida has simple, opposite, elliptical to oblong leaves with entire margins and acute apices, growing on woody stems. The leaf texture is typically coriaceous, providing a leathery feel to the foliage. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often appearing in small clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that develop from the inferior ovary characteristic of the Rubiaceae family. Its overall structure is that of a perennial shrub or small tree with a branching habit.

This plant is native to the tropical rainforest regions of Southeast Asia, specifically spanning across Thailand, Malaysia, Indonesia, and parts of southern China. Within these habitats, the species thrives in shaded understory environments characterized by high moisture levels and consistent warmth. The range extends across various archipelagos, including the islands of Sumatra and Borneo.
