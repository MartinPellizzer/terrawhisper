Delphinium anthriscifolium

What's the taxonomical classification of Delphinium anthriscifolium?

Delphinium anthriscifolium belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Ranunculales. Within this order, the plant is a member of the family Ranunculaceae. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Delphinium, with the specific designation of the species anthriscifolium.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Delphinium anthriscifolium has deeply divided, ternately compound leaves that resemble those of the angelica species, often displaying a dark green color and a serrated margin. The plant typically reaches heights of up to 1.5 meters and features sturdy, erect stems that support its floral structure. Its inflorescence consists of dense, terminal racemes composed of numerous zygomorphic flowers. These flowers are characterized by a prominent spurred nectar guide and often present in shades of blue, violet, or white. The overall vegetative structure is robust, facilitating its growth in moist, mountainous habitats.
