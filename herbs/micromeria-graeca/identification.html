Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of the plant is Micromeria graeca, first described by the botanist D. Don in 1825. It has several synonyms, including Hedeoma graeca and Lepidium graecum, reflecting its taxonomic history. Common names include Greek thyme and mountain thyme, while regional names vary across Mediterranean countries. Historically, it was known by various local names, often related to its aromatic properties.

Morphological Characteristics

Micromeria graeca is a small, herbaceous plant with a compact growth habit. It has a fibrous root system and erect, branched stems. The leaves are linear to lanceolate, with a hairy surface. The inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The fruit is a capsule containing numerous tiny seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Micromeria graeca can be distinguished by its narrow, linear leaves with a slightly hairy surface and pale blue to purple flowers. It has a compact growth habit and lacks the glandular dots found on similar species. These features help differentiate it from related plants in the same genus.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Micromeria graeca is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Greece and southern Turkey. It thrives in temperate climates with mild, wet winters and hot, dry summers. It prefers well-drained, sandy or rocky soils. It is typically found at altitudes between 500 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Micromeria graeca blooms from April to June, producing small white to pink flowers. Seed formation occurs between July and September. For identification purposes, the plant is best harvested during its flowering stage, typically in late spring to early summer.
