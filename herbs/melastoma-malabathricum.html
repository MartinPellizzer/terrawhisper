Melastoma malabathricum

What's the taxonomical classification of Melastoma malabathricum?

Melastoma malabathricum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Myrtales and the family Melastomataceae, eventually reaching its specific designation within the genus Melastoma. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its unique binomial name, the species malabathricum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Melastoma malabathricum has opposite, elliptic to oblong leaves with prominent longitudinal veins that curve toward the apex and are characterized by a distinctively hairy or pubescent surface.

The plant typically presents as a perennial herb or subshrub with slender, often reddish stems. Its flowers are actinomorphic and possess five petals that are usually bright pink or purplish in color. The reproductive structures include five stamens with specialized appendages and a central ovary that develops into a capsule.

These morphological traits are characteristic of the Melastomataceae family and serve to distinguish it from related species.

Melastoma malabathricum is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste depending on the specific medicinal purpose. Melastoma malabathricum can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or gastrointestinal distress if it is consumed or applied incorrectly.
