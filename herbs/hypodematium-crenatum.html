Hypodematium crenatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Hypodematium crenatum?

Hypodematium crenatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae, following the order Polypodiales. At the familial level, it is a member of the Hypodematiaceae family, which contains the genus Hypodematium. Ultimately, the specific identity of this plant is defined by its species designation, crenatum.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Hypodematium crenatum can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and localized inflammation if it comes into direct contact with the body. Individuals with sensitive skin may experience redness, itching, or a burning sensation upon touching the plant's leaves or stems. Some people might develop a rash or hives as part of a systemic hypersensitivity response. While systemic toxicity is not widely documented, caution is advised to prevent unexpected dermatological distress. It is best to avoid prolonged exposure to ensure no adverse reactions occur.
