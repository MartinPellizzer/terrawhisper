Hypodematium crenatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Hypodematium crenatum?

Hypodematium crenatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae, following the order Polypodiales. At the familial level, it is a member of the Hypodematiaceae family, which contains the genus Hypodematium. Ultimately, the specific identity of this plant is defined by its species designation, crenatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hypodematium crenatum has creeping rhizomes that produce small, pinnately divided leaves characterized by crenate or scalloped margins on the pinnules.

The plant typically features a delicate, branched architecture where the fronds are relatively thin and translucent in appearance. Its reproductive structures consist of small sori located on the underside of the leaflets, often protected by a thin indusium. These sori are typically arranged in rows along the margins or near the edges of the segments.

The overall size of the plant is diminutive, reflecting its niche in moist, shaded environments.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Hypodematium crenatum can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and localized inflammation if it comes into direct contact with the body. While systemic toxicity is not widely documented, caution is advised to prevent unexpected dermatological distress. It is best to avoid prolonged exposure to ensure no adverse reactions occur.
