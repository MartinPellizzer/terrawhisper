Dendrobium capillipes

What's the taxonomical classification of Dendrobium capillipes?

Dendrobium capillipes belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this botanical hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its classification through the order Asparagales, it is placed within the family Orchidaceae. The plant is further categorized under the genus Dendrobium, and its specific biological identity is defined by its species name, capillipes.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dendrobium capillipes has pendulous, slender, cane-like pseudobulbs that are often branched and characterized by thin, hair-like extensions.

These elongated stems are typically covered in small, distichous leaves that emerge from the upper portions of the growth. The inflorescence consists of small, delicate flowers that emerge from the nodes of the pseudobulbs. Each blossom features a labellum that is often intricately shaped and color-coded to attract specific pollinators.

This epiphytic orchid displays a sprawling growth habit that allows it to cling effectively to tree branches in its natural habitat.
