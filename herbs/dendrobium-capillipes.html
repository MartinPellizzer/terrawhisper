Dendrobium capillipes

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dendrobium capillipes has pendulous, slender, cane-like pseudobulbs that are often branched and characterized by thin, hair-like extensions. These elongated stems are typically covered in small, distichous leaves that emerge from the upper portions of the growth. The inflorescence consists of small, delicate flowers that emerge from the nodes of the pseudobulbs. Each blossom features a labellum that is often intricately shaped and color-coded to attract specific pollinators. This epiphytic orchid displays a sprawling growth habit that allows it to cling effectively to tree branches in its natural habitat.

This plant is native to the tropical rainforests and moist sclerophyll forests of eastern Australia, specifically spanning from north Queensland down through New South Wales. Within these habitats, the species tends to occupy various elevations ranging from coastal lowlands to higher montane regions.
