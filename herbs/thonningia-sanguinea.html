What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds Epipinoresinol, Eriodictyol, Betulinic Acid, Secoisolaricirisinol, and Thonningianin A. These specific phytochemicals contribute to the diverse therapeutic profile of the species. Each substance plays a unique role in providing antioxidant or anti-inflammatory properties within biological systems. Researchers study these molecules to understand how they interact with human cellular pathways. The presence of such a complex chemical makeup highlights the plant's significant potential in pharmacological development.

As discussed in a study, Thonningia sanguinea exhibits antioxidant, antibacterial, antiviral, and anticancer activities. The ethyl acetate fraction of the plant was found to be rich in epipinoresinol, eriodictyol, and secoisolariciresinol at concentrations of 2.3, 3.9, and 2.4 mg/g of dry extract, respectively. Molecular docking revealed that eriodictyol and secoisolariciresinol had the highest binding affinities for the EGFR signaling pathway. These compounds showed good binding energies of -19.93 and -16.63 Kcal/mol, respectively. They also formed good interactions with the key amino acid Met 769.
