10 Best Glycyrrhiza Glabra Health Benefits

Glycyrrhiza glabra, commonly known as licorice root, offers numerous health benefits due to its rich content of flavonoids, glycyrrhizin, and other bioactive compounds.

It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to soothe respiratory conditions such as coughs and bronchitis, thanks to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. Glycyrrhizin, a key compound in licorice, exhibits antiviral and anti-inflammatory effects, making it beneficial for conditions like hepatitis and inflammatory disorders. Additionally, licorice root may support digestive health by reducing inflammation in the gastrointestinal tract and aiding in the treatment of ulcers.

However, long-term use should be cautious due to potential side effects, including increased blood pressure, and it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before use.

1. Reduces inflammation

Glycyrrhiza glabra reduces inflammation by inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines and suppressing the activity of enzymes involved in the inflammatory response.

Its active compound, glycyrrhizin, has been shown to modulate the immune system and decrease the release of inflammatory mediators such as histamine and prostaglandins. This herb is commonly used in traditional medicine to treat conditions like bronchitis, gastritis, and rheumatoid arthritis due to its anti-inflammatory properties. Studies suggest that Glycyrrhiza glabra may also help alleviate symptoms of inflammatory skin disorders and digestive tract inflammation.

Overall, its ability to reduce inflammation makes it a valuable natural remedy in both therapeutic and preventive healthcare settings.

2. Relieves cough

Glycyrrhiza glabra relieves cough by soothing the respiratory tract and reducing inflammation.

Its active compounds, such as glycyrrhizin and flavonoids, help to suppress cough reflexes and ease irritation in the throat. This herb has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its expectorant properties, which help to loosen mucus and promote easier breathing. Studies suggest that it may also have antiviral effects, making it beneficial for coughs caused by viral infections.

Overall, Glycyrrhiza glabra is a valuable natural remedy for managing persistent or irritating coughs.

3. Improves digestion

Glycyrrhiza glabra improves digestion by soothing the gastrointestinal tract and reducing inflammation in the stomach and intestines.

Its active compounds, such as glycyrrhizin and flavonoids, help to alleviate symptoms like indigestion, bloating, and heartburn. This herb also stimulates the production of digestive enzymes, which can enhance the breakdown of food and nutrient absorption. Additionally, Glycyrrhiza glabra has antimicrobial properties that may help combat harmful bacteria in the gut.

As a result, it is often used in traditional medicine to support overall digestive health and relieve common digestive discomforts.

4. Boosts immunity

Glycyrrhiza glabra boosts immunity by stimulating the production of white blood cells, which are crucial for fighting off infections.

The plant contains compounds like glycyrrhizin and flavonoids that have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, helping to strengthen the immune system. It also supports the function of the thymus gland, which plays a key role in immune response regulation. Regular use of Glycyrrhiza glabra may enhance the body's ability to resist viral and bacterial infections.

However, it should be used with caution, as excessive consumption can lead to side effects like hypertension.

5. Eases stomach pain

Glycyrrhiza glabra eases stomach pain by soothing the lining of the gastrointestinal tract and reducing inflammation.

Its active compounds, such as glycyrrhizin and flavonoids, have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties that help alleviate discomfort. It is commonly used in traditional medicine to treat conditions like gastritis, ulcers, and irritable bowel syndrome. The herb also promotes the secretion of mucus, which protects the stomach lining from irritants.

As a result, Glycyrrhiza glabra is a valuable natural remedy for those experiencing persistent or chronic stomach pain.

6. Supports liver health

Glycyrrhiza glabra supports liver health by promoting the detoxification processes within the liver.

It contains compounds like glycyrrhizin, which have been shown to protect liver cells from damage caused by toxins and inflammation. This herb may also help reduce oxidative stress, a key contributor to liver disease. Its anti-inflammatory properties can alleviate conditions such as hepatitis and cirrhosis.

As a result, Glycyrrhiza glabra is often used in traditional medicine to support overall liver function and regeneration.

7. Treats skin issues

Glycyrrhiza glabra treats skin issues by reducing inflammation and soothing irritations due to its high concentration of glycyrrhizin, a compound with potent anti-inflammatory properties.

It is commonly used in topical formulations to address conditions such as eczema, psoriasis, and acne because it helps regulate the skin's inflammatory response. The herb also has antimicrobial effects that can help prevent infections in sensitive or broken skin. Its ability to enhance the skin's natural barrier function makes it beneficial for those with dry or sensitive skin.

Overall, Glycyrrhiza glabra is a valuable natural remedy for a wide range of skin problems.

8. Enhances respiratory health

Glycyrrhiza glabra enhances respiratory health by reducing inflammation in the airways, which can alleviate symptoms of conditions like asthma and bronchitis.

Its active compound, glycyrrhizin, possesses potent anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties that help combat respiratory infections. The herb also acts as an expectorant, helping to loosen mucus and improve breathing in individuals with congestion. Additionally, Glycyrrhiza glabra may support the immune system, making it more effective in preventing and managing respiratory illnesses.

Due to these benefits, it is often used in traditional medicine and herbal formulations to promote overall lung function and respiratory wellness.

9. Lowers blood pressure

Glycyrrhiza glabra lowers blood pressure by influencing the body's regulation of sodium and potassium levels, which are critical for maintaining normal blood pressure.

The herb contains glycyrrhizin, a compound that may inhibit the enzyme 11Î²-hydroxysteroid dehydrogenase, thereby reducing the breakdown of cortisol and indirectly affecting blood pressure regulation. Studies suggest that Glycyrrhiza glabra may help relax blood vessels, improving blood flow and reducing vascular resistance. However, long-term use of high doses can lead to increased sodium retention and elevated blood pressure, so it should be used with caution.

Overall, Glycyrrhiza glabra shows potential as a complementary therapy for managing hypertension when used appropriately under medical supervision.

10. Alleviates stress

Glycyrrhiza glabra alleviates stress by modulating the body's stress response through its active compounds, such as glycyrrhizin and flavonoids.

These compounds may influence the hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal (HPA) axis, which plays a central role in regulating stress hormones like cortisol. Research suggests that licorice root can help reduce the physiological and psychological effects of stress by promoting relaxation and improving mood. It is often used in traditional medicine to support emotional well-being during challenging times.

Additionally, its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties may contribute to its overall calming effect on the body.
