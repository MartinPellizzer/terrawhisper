Licorice (Glycyrrhiza glabra) Actions

Licorice exhibits anti-inflammatory properties, reducing swelling and irritation. It acts as an expectorant, helping to loosen mucus. As a tonic, it supports overall health and energy. It also has sedative effects, promoting calmness, and acts as a diuretic to aid fluid balance.

Licorice (Glycyrrhiza glabra) Monograph

1. Anti-inflammatory

Licorice has an anti-inflammatory action, primarily due to its high concentration of glycyrrhizin, which inhibits the production of inflammatory cytokines. It also modulates the immune system, reducing inflammation in conditions like respiratory infections and skin disorders. This effect is supported by both traditional use and modern pharmacological research.

2. Expectorant

Licorice has an expectorant action, stimulating the secretion of mucus and aiding in the removal of respiratory secretions. It helps to loosen and expel phlegm, making it useful for conditions like coughs and bronchitis. Its effects are supported by its ability to increase the production of surfactants in the lungs.

3. Tonic

Licorice has a tonic action, enhancing the body's ability to absorb nutrients and strengthen the immune system. It supports adrenal function and helps balance energy levels, making it valuable in treating fatigue and debility. Its mild warming properties also aid digestion and promote overall vitality.

4. Sedative

Licorice has a sedative action, promoting calmness and reducing anxiety. It works by modulating the nervous system and may enhance the effects of other calming herbs. This property makes it useful in formulations for stress relief and sleep support.

5. Diuretic

Licorice has a diuretic action, promoting the excretion of urine by increasing renal filtration. This effect is due to its glycyrrhizin content, which affects sodium and potassium balance. It is used in herbal formulations to support urinary health. However, prolonged use may lead to hypertension.

6. Antispasmodic

Licorice has an antispasmodic action, helping to relieve muscle spasms and cramps by relaxing smooth muscle tissue. It is commonly used in herbal medicine for conditions like asthma, gastrointestinal issues, and menstrual cramps. Its mechanism involves modulating inflammatory responses and enhancing bronchial function.

7. Carminative

Licorice has a carminative action, helping to relieve gas and bloating by stimulating digestion and reducing intestinal fermentation. It soothes the gastrointestinal tract, making it useful for digestive discomfort and flatulence. This effect is attributed to its anti-inflammatory and mild laxative properties.

8. Stimulant

Licorice has a stimulant action, increasing adrenal cortex activity and enhancing the body's response to stress. It supports energy levels and can improve respiratory function by relaxing bronchial muscles. This effect is due to its glycyrrhizin content, which influences hormone production and nerve transmission.

9. Bitter

Licorice has a bitter action, which stimulates digestion and supports liver function. It helps to clear heat and detoxify the body. This action is particularly useful in treating conditions like indigestion and respiratory infections. The bitter taste also enhances the absorption of other herbs when used in combination.

10. Astringent

Licorice has an astringent action, which helps reduce secretions and tighten tissues. This property makes it useful for conditions involving excessive mucus, such as coughs or respiratory inflammation. It also aids in healing wounds and sores by promoting tissue repair. The astringent effect is due to its high content of glycyrrhizin and other bioactive compounds.
