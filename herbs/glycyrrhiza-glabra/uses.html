Licorice (Glycyrrhiza glabra) Uses

Licorice is used to treat bronchitis, asthma, cough, and other respiratory conditions due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. It also helps with allergic rhinitis and skin infections, inflammation, and wound healing, showing its broad therapeutic value in herbal medicine.

Licorice (Glycyrrhiza glabra) Monograph

1. Bronchitis

Licorice root has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in cases of bronchitis. It helps reduce inflammation and soothe irritation in the airways, often used in herbal formulations to ease coughing and mucus production. Its expectorant properties aid in clearing the lungs, making it a valued remedy in herbal medicine for respiratory conditions.

2. Asthma

Licorice root has been traditionally used to support respiratory health in asthma. It contains compounds that may help reduce inflammation and relax airway muscles. However, long-term use can cause side effects due to its corticosteroid-like effects, so it should be used under professional guidance.

3. Cough

Licorice root is commonly used to soothe coughs due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps loosen mucus and reduce irritation in the respiratory tract. Traditional use often involves making a tea or syrup from the root. It is particularly effective for dry, persistent coughs.

4. Laryngitis

Licorice root is used to soothe laryngitis due to its anti-inflammatory and demulcent properties. It helps reduce throat irritation and swelling, easing coughing and hoarseness. It is often prepared as a tea or syrup to provide quick relief. Its mucilage coats the throat, promoting healing and comfort.

5. Respiratory infections

Licorice root is commonly used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health. It soothes irritated mucous membranes and may help reduce inflammation in the throat and airways. Its expectorant properties can aid in clearing mucus, making it useful for conditions like coughs and bronchitis. However, long-term use should be monitored due to potential side effects.

6. Allergic rhinitis

Licorice root has been used in herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistaminic properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion and inflammation, often used in formulations to support respiratory health during seasonal allergies. Its glycyrrhizin content modulates immune responses, making it a valuable complementary therapy in managing allergic symptoms.

7. Skin infections

Licorice root has been used traditionally to treat skin infections due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and bacterial growth on the skin. Topical applications can soothe conditions like eczema and acne. Its glycyrrhizin content supports immune response against pathogens.

8. Skin inflammation

Licorice root has anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce skin inflammation. It soothes irritated skin and promotes healing. Traditionally used for eczema and psoriasis, it may also alleviate symptoms of rosacea. Its compounds inhibit inflammatory pathways in the body.

9. Wound healing

Licorice root contains compounds that promote skin healing by reducing inflammation and increasing collagen production. It is used topically to treat wounds, ulcers, and skin irritations. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties support faster tissue repair and prevent infections.

10. Eczema

Licorice root has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to support skin health. It contains anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial compounds that may help reduce redness and irritation associated with eczema. Its soothing properties can promote healing and may be applied topically in salves or infusions.
