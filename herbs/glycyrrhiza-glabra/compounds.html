Licorice (Glycyrrhiza glabra) Compounds

The primary compounds in licorice include saponins, which have anti-inflammatory properties, and glycosides like glycyrrhizin, known for their sweetening and therapeutic effects. Flavonoids and phenolic acids contribute to antioxidant activity, while alkaloids and tannins support digestive and antimicrobial functions.

Licorice (Glycyrrhiza glabra) Monograph

1. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Licorice (Glycyrrhiza glabra). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. Saponins also have antimicrobial effects and may support immune function. These actions make licorice a valued herb in herbal medicine.

2. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Licorice (Glycyrrhiza glabra). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties. Glycosides in licorice help modulate immune responses and support respiratory health. They are commonly used in herbal formulations for their therapeutic effects.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Licorice (Glycyrrhiza glabra). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help modulate immune responses and support digestive health. Flavonoids are also responsible for some of Licorice's traditional uses in herbal medicine.

4. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Licorice (Glycyrrhiza glabra). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, though licorice is more renowned for its glycyrrhizin content. Alkaloids in licorice may support respiratory and digestive health, though their role is less prominent compared to other constituents.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in Licorice (Glycyrrhiza glabra). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound helps modulate immune responses and may support digestive health. Its presence enhances the therapeutic potential of Licorice in herbal medicine.

6. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Licorice (Glycyrrhiza glabra). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins in licorice may also support digestive health and reduce irritation in the gastrointestinal tract.

7. Coumarin

Coumarin is a compound found in Licorice (Glycyrrhiza glabra). It contributes to the plant's aromatic properties and has mild anticoagulant effects. Coumarin may also support skin health and is often used in formulations for its soothing properties.

8. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a major class of active compounds found in Licorice (Glycyrrhiza glabra). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties. Specific terpenoids like glycyrrhizin are known for their potent effects on the immune system and respiratory tract. These compounds are central to Licorice's traditional use in herbal medicine.

9. Essential oil

The essential oil of Licorice (Glycyrrhiza glabra) contains volatile compounds like limonene and geraniol. These contribute to its aromatic properties and may support anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. The oil is used in herbal medicine for its soothing qualities and potential therapeutic applications.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid compound found in Licorice (Glycyrrhiza glabra). It contributes to the plant's vibrant color and has antioxidant properties. Anthocyanin may support immune function and reduce inflammation, making it a valuable component in traditional herbal medicine.
