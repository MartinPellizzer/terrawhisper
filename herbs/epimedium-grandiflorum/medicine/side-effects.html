10 Possible Side Effects Of Epimedium Grandiflorum

Epimedium grandiflorum has some side effects when used improperly, such as stomach upset, dizziness, and allergic reactions.

These side effects can occur due to an overdose or allergic sensitivity to the herb's constituents, including flavonoids and alkaloids. For example, excessive consumption can exacerbate irritable bowel syndrome, leading to debilitating digestive issues and affecting daily life.

In some cases, allergic reactions can cause anaphylaxis, a life-threatening condition requiring immediate medical attention.

Epimedium grandiflorum induces allergic reactions happens when the body's immune system overreacts to its bioactive compounds, such as flavonoids and alkaloids.

Epimedium grandiflorum increases heart rate beats due to its high concentration of alkaloids and flavonoids.

Epimedium grandiflorum lowers blood pressure drops.

Epimedium grandiflorum produces skin rashes appears when taken in excess or for prolonged periods.
