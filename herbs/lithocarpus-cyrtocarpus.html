Lithocarpus cyrtocarpus

What's the taxonomical classification of Lithocarpus cyrtocarpus?

Lithocarpus cyrtocarpus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further organized into the order Fagales. It is a member of the family Fagaceae, which falls under the genus Lithocarpus, and is specifically identified by its species name, cyrtocarpus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lithocarpus cyrtocarpus has simple, coriaceous leaves that are typically elliptic to oblong in shape with entire or slightly serrated margins and an acute apex.

The foliage often exhibits a glossy texture on the upper surface while the underside may appear more dull or puberulent. Its woody stems develop into robust branches that support a dense canopy characteristic of its forest habitat. The reproductive structures include distinctive cupules that enclose the developing nuts.

These nuts are generally smooth and vary in size depending on the maturity of the specimen.
