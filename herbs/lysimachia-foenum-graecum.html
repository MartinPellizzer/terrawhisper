Lysimachia foenum-graecum

What's the taxonomical classification of Lysimachia foenum-graecum?

Lysimachia foenum-graecuum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Ericales. Within this order, the plant is a member of the family Primulaceae. It is further categorized under the genus Lysimachia, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species foenum-graecuum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lysimachia foenum-graecuum has lanceolate to linear leaves that are arranged oppositely along a stem that can reach up to 60 centimeters in height. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that grow in terminal clusters or solitary units. Each blossom typically features five distinct petals that form a star-like shape. The stem is often somewhat square in cross-section and can be glabrous or slightly hairy depending on the specific environment. Its root system is composed of a creeping rhizome that allows the species to spread through various habitats.

What are the pharmacological activities of Lysimachia foenum-graecum?

Furthermore, the plant exhibits cytotoxicity which suggests its potential role in inhibiting the growth of certain cancer cell lines. These diverse biological actions make the species a subject of interest for pharmaceutical research.

As discussed in a study, Lysimachia foenum-graecum has been used as an oriental medicine with anti-inflammafory effect. The extract, LFE, blocked the differentiation of 3T3-L1 preaadipocyte in a dose-dependent manner with an IC50 of 2.5 μg/ml. LFE suppressed the expression of lipogenic genes and increased lipolytic genes in vitro at 10 μg/ml and in vivo at 100 mg/kg/day. In high-fat diet-induced obese mice with n = 8/group, oral administration of LFE at 30, 100, and 300 mg/kg/day significantly decreased total body weight gain. LFE also reduced the weight of white adipose tissues, including abdominal subcutaneous, epididyimal, and perirenal adipose tissue, in a dose-dependent manner. Finally, LFE improved serum levels of obesity-related biomarkers such as glucose, triglycerides, leptin, adiponecetin, and resistin.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds 3-O-α-L-rhamnopyranosyl-(1 → 2)-β-d-glucopyranosyl-(1 → 4)-α-l-arabinopyranosyl-cyclamiretin A, Foegraecumoside O, ardisiacrispins A, ardisiacrispins B, and cyclaminorin. These specific bioactive molecules contribute to the diverse chemical profile found within the plant's tissues. Researchers often study these substances to understand their potential therapeutic properties and biological activities. The presence of such complex glycosides and ardisiacrispins suggests a sophisticated secondary metabolism. Detailed analysis of these constituents helps scientists identify how the plant interacts with various cellular targets.

As discussed in a study, the aerial parts of Lysimachia foenum-graecum contain eleven oleanane-type triterpenoid saponins known as foegraecumosides A-K along with eight known ones. Seven saponins featuring the aglycone cyclamiretin A showed moderate cytotoxicity against four human cancer cell lines, including NCI-H460, MGC-803, HepG2, and T24, with IC50 values ranging from 9.3-24.5 μM. The study also tested the activities of foegraecumosides A and B, lysichriside A, ardisiacrispins A and B, cyclaminorin, and 3-O-α-L-rhamnopyranosyl-(1 → 2)-β-d-glucopyranosyl-(1 → 4)-α-l-arabinopyranosyl-cyclamiretin A on drug-resistant lung cancer cell lines A549 and A549/CDDP. Among these, ardisiacrispin B exhibited moderate cytotoxicity against the A549/CDDP line with an IC50 value of 8.7 μM and a resistant factor (RF) of 0.9.
