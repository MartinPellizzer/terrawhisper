What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Althaea Officinalis (Marshmallow)?

Althaea officinalis, also known as marshmallow, has health benefits such as soothing irritated mucous membranes and reducing inflammation.

The herb contains mucilages, such as althaea polysaccharides and flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. Medicinal preparations of marshmallow include infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, often used to treat coughs and digestive issues.

Possible side effects include allergic reactions and stomach upset; to minimize risk, use marshmallow under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

This article explains the health benefits, active constituents, medicinal preparations, possible side effects, and precautions related to Althaea officinalis.

What are the health benefits of Althaea officinalis?

Althaea officinalis has health benefits such as soothing digestive issues like irritable bowel syndrome and gastroesophageal reflux disease due to its anti-inflammatory properties.

Its gel-like mucilage coating can help protect the mucous membranes in the digestive tract, reducing inflammation and discomfort.

Additionally, marshmallow has been used traditionally to calm coughs and soothe respiratory issues like bronchitis and asthma.

What are the active constituents of Althaea officinalis?

The health benefits of Althaea officinalis comes from its active constituents, such as mucilage, flavonoids, and phenolic acids.

The mucilage, a thick, gel-like substance, soothes and protects the mucous membranes in the body, making it an effective treatment for digestive issues, respiratory problems, and skin irritations.

Flavonoids, meanwhile, possess antioxidant properties that help reduce inflammation and improve cardiovascular health, while phenolic acids have antibacterial and anti-inflammatory effects.

What are the medicinal preparations of Althaea officinalis?

Althaea officinalis has different medicinal preparations, such as dried roots and leaves, which can be used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures.

The root extract is often used in the form of lozenges or cough drops to soothe sore throats and alleviate respiratory issues. Meanwhile, the leaf extract is used topically to treat skin irritations and wounds due to its anti-inflammatory properties.

Tinctures and capsules are also available, which can be consumed orally to support digestive health and reduce inflammation.

What are the possible side effect of using Althaea officinalis improperly?

Improper use of Althaea officinalis increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as digestive issues like bloating, abdominal pain, and diarrhea.

It may also cause allergic reactions like hives, itching, and swelling. In rare cases, excessive consumption can lead to interactions with blood thinners, diabetes medications, and heart medicines, potentially resulting in hypoglycemia, bleeding episodes, or arrhythmias.

Furthermore, marshmallow root may trigger respiratory problems, such as bronchospasms and shortness of breath, particularly in individuals with asthma or chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).

What precautions to take when using Althaea officinalis medicinally?

Before using Althaea officinalis for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as consulting a healthcare professional to determine the appropriate dosage and potential interactions with other medications.

Additionally, it is essential to ensure that the product is of high quality and free from contaminants or adulterants.

Pregnant or breastfeeding women should exercise caution when using marshmallow, as its effects on these groups are not well-studied.
