10 Health Benefits Of Marshmallow

1. Soothes inflamed mucous membranes

Marshmallow soothes inflamed mucous membranes due to the presence of a substance called mucilage.

Mucilage is a sticky, gel-like substance that comes from the root of the marshmallow plant. When this mucilage comes into contact with water, it forms a thick, slippery gel.

This gel can coat and soothe inflamed mucous membranes, providing a protective barrier against irritants and helping to reduce inflammation. Marshmallow has been used traditionally for thousands of years for its soothing and anti-inflammatory properties, and it is still widely used today in various forms such as teas, lozenges, and extracts.

Its ability to soothe and protect inflamed mucous membranes makes it a popular remedy for a variety of conditions, including sore throats, coughs, and digestive issues.

2. Eases sore throats

Marshmallow eases sore throats because of its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

The mucilage, a gel-like substance found in the plant's roots and leaves, coats the throat, providing a protective barrier against irritants and reducing inflammation. This helps to alleviate pain, scratchiness, and discomfort in the throat, making it easier to swallow and speak. Furthermore, marshmallow has been shown to have demulcent properties, which means it can help to moisturize and lubricate the mucous membranes, providing additional relief from sore throats.

Its soothing effects make it a popular natural remedy for treating throat irritations and other respiratory conditions.

3. Relieves coughs

Marshmallow relieves coughs because of its mucilage content, which is a thick, slippery substance that coats and soothes the throat and mouth.

This mucilage forms a protective barrier over the mucous membranes, reducing irritation and inflammation, and helping to loosen and expel mucus from the respiratory tract. Marshmallow has been used traditionally as a demulcent, an agent that soothes and protects irritated tissues, and its expectorant properties help to break up congestion and ease breathing.

Additionally, marshmallow has anti-inflammatory and immune-boosting properties, making it a valuable remedy for respiratory infections and other conditions that cause coughing.

4. Heals wounds

Marshmallow heals wounds because of its anti-inflammatory and demulcent properties.

The mucilage present in the root of the marshmallow plant creates a protective layer on the skin, which helps to reduce inflammation and promote the healing of wounds. This soothing effect also helps to alleviate pain and discomfort associated with wounds.

Additionally, marshmallow has been found to stimulate the production of new skin cells, which can help to speed up the healing process. The astringent properties of marshmallow also help to tighten and contract damaged tissue, reducing the size of wounds and promoting faster healing.

Overall, marshmallow is a valuable natural remedy for promoting wound healing and skin regeneration.

5. Reduces skin inflammation

Marshmallow reduces skin inflammation because it contains mucilage, a sticky substance that forms a soothing film over the skin.

This film helps to protect the skin from irritants and reduces inflammation by reducing the production of pro-inflammatory chemicals in the body. Additionally, marshmallow contains flavonoids and phenolic acids, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that further help to reduce redness, swelling, and irritation of the skin. Furthermore, marshmallow has been shown to have anti-histamine effects, which can help to alleviate symptoms of skin conditions such as eczema and dermatitis that are characterized by inflammation and itching.

Therefore, marshmallow is a valuable natural remedy for reducing skin inflammation and promoting healthy, calm, and clear skin.

6. Moisturizes skin

Marshmallow moisturizes skin because it contains mucilage, a sticky substance that can hold on to water.

This mucilage forms a protective layer on the skin, helping to prevent moisture loss and keeping the skin hydrated. Additionally, marshmallow extract has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help soothe and calm irritated skin. The high levels of antioxidants found in marshmallow also help to protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals, which can lead to dryness and aging.

All of these properties make marshmallow an effective natural moisturizer for the skin.

7. Alleviates stomach ulcers

Marshmallow alleviates stomach ulcers because of its anti-inflammatory and mucilage-forming properties.

The mucilage, a gel-like substance found in the plant's roots and leaves, creates a protective barrier over the stomach lining, shielding it from damaging digestive acids and irritants. This barrier helps to reduce inflammation and promote healing of existing ulcers. Additionally, the anti-inflammatory properties of marshmallow help to soothe and reduce inflammation in the digestive tract, further aiding in the healing process.

Studies have shown that marshmallow extract can inhibit the growth of Helicobacter pylori, a bacterium linked to the development of stomach ulcers, providing an additional mechanism by which marshmallow can help alleviate this condition.

8. Improves digestion

Marshmallow improves digestion because of its mucilage content, which can soothe and protect the gastrointestinal tract.

This mucilage forms a protective layer over the mucous membrane of the digestive tract, reducing inflammation and irritation. Additionally, marshmallow can increase the mucus secretion in the gut, which can help to lubricate and ease the passage of food through the digestive system. This herb is also believed to have anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties, which can help to alleviate symptoms of digestive disorders such as indigestion, heartburn, and gastritis.

Furthermore, marshmallow may help to stimulate the production of digestive enzymes, promoting efficient digestion and nutrient absorption.

9. Reduces intestinal inflammation

Marshmallow reduces intestinal inflammation because of its anti-inflammatory and mucilage properties.

The plant contains polysaccharides, including mucilage, which forms a protective layer on the mucous membrane of the digestive tract, reducing inflammation and soothing irritation. Additionally, it has been shown to reduce the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines, further reducing inflammation. Marshmallow's demulcent properties also help to ease digestive discomfort, making it a popular natural remedy for gastrointestinal issues.

Studies have suggested that marshmallow may be beneficial in the treatment of inflammatory bowel disease and other gastrointestinal disorders.

10. Acts as a diuretic

Marshmallow acts as a diuretic because it contains mucilage, a gel-like substance that can increase the amount of urine produced by the body.

This mucilage coats and soothes the urinary tract, which can help to reduce inflammation and irritation. Additionally, marshmallow is a natural anti-inflammatory, which can further support the urinary system.

The increased urine production helps to flush out toxins and waste from the body, which can help to prevent urinary tract infections and other kidney problems. Marshmallow is also a natural source of potassium, which is an essential electrolyte that helps to regulate fluid balance in the body.

This makes marshmallow a gentle and effective diuretic that can help to support overall urinary and kidney health.
