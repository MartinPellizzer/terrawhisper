These sores are typically small, round, and covered by a white or yellowish membrane, causing discomfort during eating, speaking, or swallowing.

Individuals who frequently experience them include those with weakened immune systems, people under high stress, and individuals with certain dietary habits.

The althaea officinalis plant, also known as marshmallow, has been traditionally used to soothe mouth ulcers due to its mucilage content, which forms a protective barrier and promotes healing.

These compounds help reduce pain and inflammation associated with oral ulcers. The plant's mucilage content also contributes to soothing the oral mucosa. Alkaloids may inhibit the growth of harmful bacteria in the mouth.

Flavonoids further support tissue repair and enhance the healing process.
