Marshmallow (Althaea officinalis) Compounds

The primary compounds in marshmallow include tannins, which have astringent properties, and glycosides, which contribute to its demulcent effects. Flavonoids and saponins provide anti-inflammatory and antioxidant benefits, while phenolic acids and coumarin support tissue repair and may have mild antispasmodic actions. Anthocyanins, terpenoids, essential oils, and alkaloids also contribute to its therapeutic profile.

Marshmallow (Althaea officinalis) Monograph

1. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Marshmallow (Althaea officinalis). It contributes to the plant's demulcent properties, soothing irritated mucous membranes. Tannins also aid in reducing inflammation and promoting wound healing. This compound is central to the therapeutic use of Marshmallow in herbal medicine.

2. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Marshmallow (Althaea officinalis). It contributes to the plant's demulcent properties, helping to soothe irritated mucous membranes. Glycosides in Marshmallow are known for their mild laxative and anti-inflammatory effects. These compounds are often responsible for the plant's traditional use in respiratory and digestive ailments.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of compounds found in Marshmallow (Althaea officinalis). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in soothing respiratory and digestive tract conditions. These compounds work synergistically with mucilage to enhance the plant's therapeutic effects.

4. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Marshmallow (Althaea officinalis). It contributes to the plant's demulcent properties by forming a protective film over mucous membranes. Saponin also exhibits mild anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects, supporting its traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Marshmallow (Althaea officinalis). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and demulcent properties, helping soothe irritated mucous membranes. This compound is found in the roots and leaves, supporting its traditional use in respiratory and digestive remedies.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a compound found in Marshmallow (Althaea officinalis). It contributes to the plant's mild antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarin also acts as a natural anticoagulant, supporting circulation and reducing the risk of blood clots. Its presence enhances the therapeutic potential of Marshmallow in traditional herbal medicine.

7. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Marshmallow (Althaea officinalis). It contributes to the plant's vibrant color and possesses antioxidant properties. Anthocyanin may support cardiovascular health and reduce inflammation, making it a valuable component in herbal remedies.

8. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Marshmallow (Althaea officinalis) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. These compounds help reduce irritation and support mucosal health. They are often found in the plant's roots and are used in traditional remedies for respiratory and digestive conditions.

9. Essential oil

The essential oil of Marshmallow (Althaea officinalis) contains volatile compounds like methyl salicylate and aromatic hydrocarbons. It contributes to the plant's mild anti-inflammatory and soothing properties, often used in topical preparations for skin irritation and respiratory support.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Marshmallow (Althaea officinalis). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by supporting its anti-inflammatory and soothing effects. Alkaloids in Marshmallow are often associated with its traditional use in respiratory and digestive health. These compounds are part of the plant's broader therapeutic profile in herbal medicine.
