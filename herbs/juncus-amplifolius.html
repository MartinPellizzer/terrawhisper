Juncus amplifolius

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Juncus amplifolius has cylindrical, unbranched stems that reach heights of up to 1 meter and are characterized by broad, leaf-like basal sheaths. The leaves are reduced to inconspicuous, scale-like structures located at the base of the culms. Its inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in compact clusters along the upper portion of the stem. The capsules are typically three-valved and contain numerous tiny, brown seeds. This species often displays a tufted growth habit suitable for its wetland habitats.
