Mossy Alchemilla (Alchemilla mollis): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

Mossy Alchemilla, also known as Alchemilla mollis, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, widely used in herbal medicine for its diuretic, astringent, and anti-inflammatory properties.

What is Mossy Alchemilla (Alchemilla Mollis)?

Mossy Alchemilla, also known as Alchemilla mollis, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its dense, moss-like foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Mossy Alchemilla is Alchemilla mollis. This plant belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Rosales, family Rosaceae, genus Alchemilla, and species Alchemilla mollis. It is commonly known as Mossy Alchemilla in Europe and as Alchemilla moss in North America. It is also referred to as Lady's Mantle in some regions. The synonyms for this plant include Alchemilla vulgaris and Alchemilla helvetica.

Plant Description and Morphology

Mossy Alchemilla is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10-30 centimeters, forming dense, mat-like growths.

Its leaves are basal, with a distinctive rosette structure, and are oval to lance-shaped, covered in fine, velvety hairs. The flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in compact, upright inflorescences, often appearing after the leaves have fully developed. The root system consists of a fibrous network that spreads horizontally, anchoring the plant and facilitating nutrient absorption.

The plant has a subtle, slightly sour scent, with soft, fuzzy leaves that feel slightly sticky to the touch.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Mossy Alchemilla is native to Europe, particularly the mountainous regions of the Alps and the Carpathians, with some populations extending into western Asia.

It thrives in temperate climates with cool, moist conditions and is commonly found in rocky, shaded woodland areas. The plant prefers well-drained, acidic soils and is often associated with stream banks and limestone-rich habitats. Its distribution is limited to specific microclimates that provide consistent moisture and partial shade.

Mossy Alchemilla is adapted to high-altitude environments where temperatures are moderate and humidity is high.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The plant parts used medicinally in Mossy Alchemilla include the leaves, roots, and flowering stems.

The leaves are the most commonly utilized part, often harvested during the early flowering stage for maximum potency. The roots are collected in the fall when they are richest in active compounds. Flowering stems are occasionally used, particularly for their aromatic properties.

Each part contains a variety of bioactive compounds that contribute to its medicinal value.

Secondary metabolites such as rutin, quercetin, and chlorogenic acid are particularly abundant and play a key role in its therapeutic effects. Mossy Alchemilla (Alchemilla mollis) exhibits a range of pharmacological properties that make it valuable in herbal medicine.

Its therapeutic actions include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and wound-healing effects, supported by both traditional use and modern research. The biological activity of this plant is attributed to its rich composition of flavonoids, tannins, and essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal profile. Therapeutic Uses

Mossy Alchemilla is traditionally used in herbal medicine for its therapeutic properties, particularly in supporting urinary and digestive health.

It is commonly employed to treat conditions such as urinary tract infections, kidney stones, and bladder inflammation due to its diuretic and anti-inflammatory effects. The plant is also used to alleviate symptoms of digestive disorders like indigestion and gastritis. Its astringent qualities make it beneficial for managing skin irritations and minor wounds.

Mossy Alchemilla is often included in formulations for its ability to promote detoxification and support overall urinary system function.

Preparation Methods and Forms

Mossy Alchemilla is typically prepared as a decoction, infusion, or tincture, with the leaves and roots harvested during early spring or late autumn, dried, and then used to make teas, poultices, or topical salves for its therapeutic properties.

Dosage and Administration

The recommended dosage for Mossy Alchemilla (Alchemilla mollis) typically ranges from 1 to 3 grams of dried herb per day, divided into two or three doses, and it is usually prepared as a tea by steeping in hot water for 10 to 15 minutes.

Safety, Side Effects, and Contraindications

When used appropriately, Mossy Alchemilla (Alchemilla mollis) is generally considered safe for most people, though it may cause mild gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals, and it should be avoided during pregnancy and by those with known allergies to plants in the Rosaceae family.

Drug Interactions

Mossy Alchemilla (Alchemilla mollis) may interact with medications that affect blood clotting, such as anticoagulants and antiplatelet agents, due to its potential mild anticoagulant properties, and should be used with caution under medical supervision.

Toxicity and Precautions

Cultivation and Harvesting

Mossy Alchemilla (Alchemilla mollis) is typically cultivated in moist, well-drained soil in partial shade, requiring regular watering and consistent moisture to thrive, with harvesting typically done in early summer when the leaves are fully developed and the plant is at its most potent.

Processing and Extraction

The processing and extraction of Mossy Alchemilla involve harvesting the fresh or dried plant material, typically the leaves and stems, which are then subjected to methods such as maceration, distillation, or solvent extraction to obtain active compounds like tannins, flavonoids, and mucilage.

Storage and Shelf Life

Mossy Alchemilla (Alchemilla mollis) should be stored in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight, in an airtight container to maintain its potency and prevent moisture absorption, with a shelf life of up to two years when properly stored.

Scientific Research and Evidence

Scientific research on Mossy Alchemilla (Alchemilla mollis) has shown potential anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and wound-healing properties, with some studies suggesting its use in traditional herbal medicine for skin conditions and as a diuretic.

Traditional and Historical Uses

Mossy Alchemilla has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported ability to support urinary health, reduce inflammation, and promote skin healing, with historical records dating back to medieval European apothecaries.
