Zelkova schneideriana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Zelkova schneideriana has a medium to large deciduous tree habit characterized by a spreading crown, serrated ovate leaves, and small, inconspicuous flowers. The leaves are typically 4 to 8 centimeters long with doubly serrated margins and a texture that varies from smooth to slightly hairy. Its bark is dark gray and develops shallow longitudinal fissures as the tree matures. The branches often exhibit a zig-zag growth pattern that contributes to the tree's structural complexity. Small, nut-like fruits known as samaras are produced in clusters following the flowering period.
