Zelkova schneideriana

What's the taxonomical classification of Zelkova schneideriana?

Zelkova schneideriana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within its taxonomic hierarchy, it is classified in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Rosales and is a member of the family Ulmaceae. Specifically, it is placed within the genus Zelkova, with its unique identification defined by the species name schneideriana.

    Zelkova schneideriana is prepared primarily through aqueous decoctions of its bark and leaf extracts for traditional medicinal applications.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Zelkova schneideriana can cause allergic skin reactions such as contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals due to the presence of various organic compounds in its tissues. While not typically classified as a highly toxic plant, direct contact with the bark or leaves may trigger redness, itching, or localized swelling. Some people might also experience respiratory irritation if they inhale large quantities of pollen during the flowering season. These reactions are generally mild and resolve once the person is removed from contact with the tree. It is always advisable to exercise caution when handling any ornamental species to prevent unexpected sensitivities.
