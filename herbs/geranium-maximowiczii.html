What's the taxonomical classification of Geranium maximowiczii?

Geranium maximowiczii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its hierarchical descent, the plant is placed in the order Geraniales and the family Geraniaceae. Finally, it is defined by its specific biological classification within the genus Geranium and the species maximowiczii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Geranium maximowiczii has a rhizomatous perennial habit characterized by deeply lobed, palmate leaves that are typically five to seven centimeters wide and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces terminal clusters of five-petaled flowers that vary in color from pale pink to deep rose. Each blossom features prominent stamens and a central nectar guide that directs pollinators toward the reproductive organs. The foliage remains dense and bushy throughout the growing season, providing a lush green backdrop for the blooms. Stems are often reddish in hue and provide sturdy support for the flowering racemes.
