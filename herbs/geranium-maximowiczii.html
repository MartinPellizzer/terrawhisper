Geranium maximowiczii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Following its hierarchical descent, the plant is placed in the order Geraniales and the family Geraniaceae. Finally, it is defined by its specific biological classification within the genus Geranium and the species maximowiczii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Geranium maximowiczii has a rhizomatous perennial habit characterized by deeply lobed, palmate leaves that are typically five to seven centimeters wide and covered in fine hairs. Each blossom features prominent stamens and a central nectar guide that directs pollinators toward the reproductive organs. The foliage remains dense and bushy throughout the growing season, providing a lush green backdrop for the blooms.
