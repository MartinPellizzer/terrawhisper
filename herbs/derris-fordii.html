Derris fordii

What's the taxonomical classification of Derris fordii?

Derris fordii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Fabales. It is a representative of the family Fabaceae, a group known for its diverse botanical characteristics, and is specifically categorized under the genus Derris. Ultimately, its scientific identity is defined by its specific designation as the species foridii.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Derris fordii contains rotenone, a highly potent non-proteinaceous compound used primarily as a natural insecticide and piscicide. This specific chemical structure functions by inhibiting mitochondrial respiration within the cells of targeted organisms. Beyond its insecticidal applications, various parts of the plant are studied for their potential biochemical properties in traditional medicine. Researchers examine these bioactive molecules to understand their effects on nervous system functions and metabolic processes. The concentration of these compounds varies significantly depending on the specific part of the plant being harvested.

What are the therapeutic actions of Derris fordii?

Derris fordii has potent insecticidal and antiparasitic properties primarily derived from the rotenone compounds found within its roots. These bioactive molecules act as mitochondrial respiration inhibitors, effectively paralyzing and killing various insect species and aquatic organisms. In traditional medicine, extracts from the plant are utilized to treat skin conditions and various parasitic infections in humans. The chemical action disrupts the cellular energy production of target pests, making it a highly effective natural pesticide. Scientific studies continue to investigate its potential applications in managing specific biological threats through its biochemical pathways.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various parasitic infestations including lice, scabies, and certain types of intestinal worms. The active compound rotenone found within the roots acts as a natural insecticide and molluscicide. Historically, it has been applied topically to eliminate skin parasites and used internally to expel helminths from the digestive tract. While effective against many pests, the substance must be used with caution due to its inherent toxicity to many organisms. Its primary application remains focused on controlling insect populations and managing specific parasitic skin conditions.

What are the herbal preparations of Derris fordii?

Derris fordii is prepared primarily as a liquid extract or a powdered infusion used for its insecticidal and medicinal properties. The roots are often crushed or decocted in water to create a potent solution for controlling pests in agricultural settings. In some traditional practices, the plant material is processed into topical ointments or pastes to treat skin conditions. Some preparations involve drying the roots into a fine dust that can be applied directly to surfaces. These various forms allow the plant's active compounds to be effectively utilized for both botanical protection and human health applications.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Derris fordii can cause severe neurotoxicity, including tremors, seizures, and respiratory failure, if the rotenone it contains is ingested or absorbed through the skin. This plant produces a potent compound that acts as a mitochondrial inhibitor, disrupting the ability of cells to produce energy. When humans are exposed to high concentrations, the substance can lead to neurological damage similar to that seen in Parkinson's disease. Ingestion of the roots can lead to rapid death in both humans and animals due to systemic metabolic collapse. Because of these significant risks, handling the plant requires extreme caution to prevent accidental poisoning.
