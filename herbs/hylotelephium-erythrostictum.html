What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hylotelephium erythrostictum has succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern and exhibit a distinct reddish or purplish coloration on their undersides. The leaf margins are often slightly serrated or crenate, providing a textured appearance to the foliage. These plants frequently develop thick, fleshy stems that support the dense clusters of leaves. During the flowering stage, the plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette.
