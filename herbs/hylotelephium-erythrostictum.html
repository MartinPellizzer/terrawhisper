What's the taxonomical classification of Hylotelephium erythrostictum?

Hylotelephium erythrostictum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Saxifragales. This plant is a member of the family Crassulaceae, specifically placed within the genus Hylotelephium, with its unique identity defined by the species erythrostictum.

What are the pharmacological activities of Hylotelephium erythrostictum?

This plant has antidiabetic properties through its mechanism as an alpha-glucosidase inhibitor. By targeting this specific enzyme, the plant helps regulate blood glucose levels after meals. This inhibitory action slows down the digestion of carbohydrates in the small intestine. Such biochemical activity makes the species a subject of interest for managing type 2 diabetes. Researchers continue to investigate these specific metabolic effects to understand their full medicinal potential.

According to a study published by "Bioorganic & medicinal chemistry letters", Hylotelephium erythrostictum serves as a potential source of antidiaabetic agent. The plant contains isolated active components including 2-(3',4'-dihydroxyphenyl)-2,3-dihydro-4,6-dihydroxy-2-(methoxy)- 3-benzofuranone(1), apigenin(2), diosmetin(3), kaempferol(4), kaempferide(5), rhamnocitrin(6), quercetin(7), and gallic acid(8). Specifically, the 2-methoxybenzofuranone type compound 1 exhibits a potential inhibitory effect on α-glucosidase. This compound has an IC50 value of 1.8 μM and a Ki value of 709 nM. Such data suggests its utility in the clinical development of therapeutics.
