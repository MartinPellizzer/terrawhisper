    What are the herbal preparations of Aleurites fordii?

    Aleurites fordii is prepared primarily as a pressed oil extracted from its seeds, though it is also utilized in the form of decoctions, powders, and topical ointments. The oil serves as the most common medicinal and industrial derivative due to its high lipid content. When used in traditional practices, the seeds may be ground into a fine powder to be incorporated into various herbal mixtures.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Aleurites fordii can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal distress if the plant parts are ingested or handled improperly. The sap contains various compounds that may trigger contact dermatitis or redness upon skin exposure. When consumed, the seeds and other components can lead to stomach aches or vomiting in sensitive individuals. Careful handling is necessary to avoid direct contact with the milky latex produced by the plant. Regular exposure without protection might result in long-term sensitivities for some users.
