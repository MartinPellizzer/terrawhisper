Potamogeton compressus

What's the taxonomical classification of Potamogeton compressus?

Potamogeton compressus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It further falls under the order Alismatales and is a member of the family Potamogetonaceae. Finally, its taxonomic hierarchy is completed by its placement in the genus Potamogeton, specifically identifying it as the species compressus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Potamogeton compressus has narrow, linear leaves that are distinctly compressed or flattened laterally and submerged stems that can reach up to 1 meter in length.

The foliage typically features lanceolate leaves arranged alternately along the stem to maximize light absorption underwater. Small, white or yellowish flowers emerge on slender peduncles during the blooming season. The plant also produces small, ellipsoidal fruits that develop from the tiny inconspicuous flowers.

These morphological features allow the species to thrive in various flowing freshwater habitats.
