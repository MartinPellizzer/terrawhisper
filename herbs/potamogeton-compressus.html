Potamogeton compressus

What's the taxonomical classification of Potamogeton compressus?

Potamogeton compressus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It further falls under the order Alismatales and is a member of the family Potamogetonaceae. Finally, its taxonomic hierarchy is completed by its placement in the genus Potamogeton, specifically identifying it as the species compressus.

Potamogeton compressus is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice for various therapeutic applications. These traditional methods allow for the efficient utilization of the plant's specific chemical properties.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Potamogeton compressus can cause skin irritation or allergic reactions in sensitive individuals who come into direct contact with the plant's tissues. While it is primarily an aquatic plant, handling the submerged stems may lead to contact dermatitis for some people. There is limited clinical research regarding the systemic toxicity of this species in humans. It is generally considered non-toxic to most wildlife, but caution is advised when managing large quantities of the vegetation. Always use protective gloves if you suspect a personal sensitivity to aquatic flora.
