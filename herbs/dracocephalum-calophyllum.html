Dracocephalum calophyllum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dracocephalum calophyllum has square stems, opposite leaves with serrated margins, and dense terminal flower spikes composed of small, two-lipped labiate flowers. The foliage is characterized by a textured, somewhat hairy surface that provides a distinct tactile quality to the plant. Its inflorescence features numerous tiny blossoms that are typically arranged in tight, compact clusters. The leaf shape varies from lanceolate to ovate, helping to distinguish it from similar species in the Lamiaceae family. These morphological traits collectively support the plant's structural integrity and reproductive efficiency.
