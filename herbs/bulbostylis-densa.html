What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bulbostylis densa has a tufted growth habit characterized by dense, caespitose clumps of slender, filiform leaves that arise from a short, creeping rhizome. The leaves are typically narrow and pointed, creating a compact appearance within the plant cluster. Its inflorescences consist of small, globose to ovoid spikelets that are closely packed along the terminal ends of the culms. These spikelets contain numerous minute flowers protected by overlapping glumes. The overall structure is diminutive, often remaining low to the ground in its natural habitat.
