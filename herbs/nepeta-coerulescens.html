Nepeta coerulescens

What's the taxonomical classification of Nepeta coerulescens?

Nepeta coerulescens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical structure, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic journey continues through the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae, where it is categorized under the genus Nepeta. Finally, its specific identity is established by its species name, coerulescens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Nepeta coerulescens has square stems, ovate to lanceolate leaves with crenate margins, and small, two-lipped blue to violet flowers arranged in dense terminal verticillasters. The foliage is typically soft and textured, often exhibiting a grayish-green hue due to fine pubescence on the leaf surfaces. Its woody base develops as the perennial plant matures, providing structural support for the upright flowering stalks. Each flower is held within a calyx that features distinct teeth, characteristic of the Lamiaceae family. The overall architecture of the plant is compact yet bushy, making it a common sight in Mediterranean-style gardens.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

Its distribution is often limited to specific limestone or calcareous substrates found within these high-elevation ecosystems. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?
