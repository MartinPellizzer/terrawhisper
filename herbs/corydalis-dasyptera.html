Corydalis dasyptera

What's the taxonomical classification of Corydalis dasyptera?

Corydalis dasyptera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Ranunculales and into the family Papaveraceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Corydalis, and its specific species epithet, dasyptera.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Corydalis dasyptera has deeply pinnately lobed leaves with slender, spreading segments and zygomorphic flowers characterized by long, nectar-producing spurs. The foliage is delicate and highly dissected, providing a fine texture to the plant's overall appearance. Its blossoms feature a distinctively elongated spur that curves backward, a structural adaptation for specific pollinators. The stems are relatively herbaceous and support the weight of the spreading canopy of leaves. Small, inconspicuous fruits typically follow the blooming period, developing from the fertilized ovaries.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the high-altitude regions of western China, specifically within the mountainous areas of Sichuan, Yunnan, and Gansu provinces. It thrives in temperate montane environments where moist, shaded soils are prevalent. These populations are typically found growing in forest understories or along rocky slopes at elevations ranging from 2,000 to 4,000 meters. The specific topography of these provinces provides the unique microclimates necessary for its survival.
