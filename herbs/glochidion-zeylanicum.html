Glochidion zeylanicum

What's the taxonomical classification of Glochidion zeylanicum?

Glochidion zeylanicum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and follows the order Malpighiales. This plant is a member of the family Phyllanthaceae, specifically falling under the genus Glochidion, with the species designation zeylanicum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Glochidion zeylanicum has simple, alternate, elliptical to oblong leaves with entire margins and an acute apex. The leaves are typically leathery in texture and exhibit a glabrous surface on both the upper and lower sides. Its woody stems feature a fine pubescence that distinguishes the younger growth from older, smoother bark. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often found in axillary clusters. These flowers eventually develop into characteristic capsule-like fruits that split open to reveal seeds.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of South and Southeast Asia, specifically including countries such as Sri Lanka, India, Myanmar, Thailand, and Malaysia. Its distribution is primarily concentrated within the moist evergreen and semi-evergreen forest ecosystems of these maritime and continental zones. It thrives in lowland habitats where humidity remains consistently high throughout the year. The species is often found in coastal belts as well as inland riverine environments across its range. These specific environmental conditions define the limits of its natural occurrence across the Indo-Malayan realm.

What are the pharmacological activities of Glochidion zeylanicum?

This plant has antioxidant, oxidative stress resistance, anti-aging, lifespan extending, and anti-tumor promoting pharmacological activities. Its bioactive compounds work to neutralize free radicals, which protects cells from damage caused by oxidative stress. By mitigating this cellular damage, the plant exhibits significant anti-aging properties that help maintain physiological functions. These protective mechanisms are directly linked to its ability to promote lifespan extension in biological models. Furthermore, the plant demonstrates an ability to inhibit tumor promotion, making it a subject of interest for cancer prevention research.

As discussed in a study, four known lupane-type triterpenoids, glochidonol (1), glochidiol (2), lup-20(29)-ene-1beta,3beta-diol (3) and glochidone (3), were isolated from the stem bark of Glochidion zeylanicum. These compounds were tested for their inhibitory effects on Epstein-Barr virus early antigen (EBV-EA) activation induced by 12-O-tetradecanoylphorbol 13-acetate (TPA). Among the isolates, compound 2 showed an IC50 of 290 mol ratio/32 pmol TPA, while compound 3 had an IC50 of 300. Additionally, compound 2 exhibited a strong inhibitory effect on mouse skin tumor promotion during an in vivo mouse two-stage carcinogenesis test.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse range of medicinal compounds, specifically total phenolics and various flavonoids such as glochiflavanosides A-D, isoorientin, and vitexin. These bioactive constituents contribute to the plant's overall therapeutic profile and antioxidant properties. The presence of specific glycosides like vitexin and isoorientin suggests significant potential for pharmacological applications. Furthermore, the concentration of total phenolics plays a crucial role in the plant's chemical complexity. Research into these particular glochiflavanosides may reveal additional medicinal benefits for human health.

As discussed in a study, Glochidion zeylanicum leaf extracts, specifically hexane and methanol extracts, contain rich antioxidants and various phytochemicals analyzed via GLC-MS, LC-MS, and RP-HPLC. These extracts contain total phenolics and flavonoids that were determined using DPPH and ABTS assays. The medicinal properties of Glochidion zeylanicum include lifespan extension and oxidative stress resistance through the DAF-16/FoxO and SKN-1/Nrf-2 signaling pathways. Treatment with these extracts up-regulated the expression of stress-response genes such as SOD-3 and GST-4, while HSP-16.2 was down-regulated. These leaf extracts also improved pharyngeal pumping function and attenuated autofluorescent pigment in the Caenorhabditis elegans model.
