Glochidion zeylanicum

What's the taxonomical classification of Glochidion zeylanicum?

Glochidion zeylanicum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and follows the order Malpighiales. This plant is a member of the family Phyllanthaceae, specifically falling under the genus Glochidion, with the species designation zeylanicum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Glochidion zeylanicum has simple, alternate, elliptical to oblong leaves with entire margins and an acute apex.

The leaves are typically leathery in texture and exhibit a glabrous surface on both the upper and lower sides. Its woody stems feature a fine pubescence that distinguishes the younger growth from older, smoother bark. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often found in axillary clusters.

These flowers eventually develop into characteristic capsule-like fruits that split open to reveal seeds.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Glochidion zeylanicum can cause skin irritation, localized inflammation, and allergic reactions due to the presence of irritants in its tissues. Some individuals might experience more severe dermatological responses depending on their specific sensitivity to the plant's chemical properties.
