Glochidion zeylanicum

What's the taxonomical classification of Glochidion zeylanicum?

Glochidion zeylanicum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and follows the order Malpighiales. This plant is a member of the family Phyllanthaceae, specifically falling under the genus Glochidion, with the species designation zeylanicum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Glochidion zeylanicum has simple, alternate, elliptical to oblong leaves with entire margins and an acute apex. The leaves are typically leathery in texture and exhibit a glabrous surface on both the upper and lower sides. Its woody stems feature a fine pubescence that distinguishes the younger growth from older, smoother bark. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often found in axillary clusters. These flowers eventually develop into characteristic capsule-like fruits that split open to reveal seeds.

What are the pharmacological activities of Glochidion zeylanicum?

This plant has anti-tumor promoting, antioxidant, oxidative stress resistance, anti-aging, and lifespan extending pharmacological activities. Its potent antioxidant properties allow it to neutralize free radicals and mitigate cellular damage caused by oxidative stress. By enhancing oxidative stress resistance, the plant helps protect biological systems from metabolic dysfunction. These combined mechanisms contribute to significant anti-aging effects throughout the body. Consequently, these bioactive compounds support physiological health and promote lifespan extending outcomes in various models.

According to a study published by "Planta medica", four known lupane-type triterpenoids, glochidonol (1), glochidiol (2), lup-20(29)-ene-1beta,3beta-diol (3) and glochidone (3), were isolated from the stem bark of Glochidion zeylanicum. These isolates were tested for their inhibitory effects on Epstein-Barr virus early antigen (EBV-EA) activation induced by 12-O-tetradecanoylphorbol 13-acetate (TPA). Among the compounds, glochidiol (2) showed an IC50 of 290 mol ratio/32 pmol TPAs and lup-20(29)-ene-1beta,3beta-diol (3) had an IC50 of 300. Furthermore, compound 2 exhibited a strong inhibitory effect on mouse skin tumor promotion in an in vivo mouse two-stage carcinogenesis test.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating conditions caused by oxidative stress, aging, tumors, and the accumulation of reactive oxygen species (ROS). Its bioactive compounds function as potent antioxidants that neutralize harmful free radicals within the body. By reducing ROS accumulation, the plant helps prevent cellular damage that often leads to premature aging. These protective properties also play a significant role in inhibiting the development and progression of various tumors. Consequently, the medicinal application of Glochidion zeylanicum focuses on restoring cellular balance and promoting long-term health.

According to a study published by "Phytomedicine : international journal of phytotherapy and phytopharmacology", Glochidion zeylanicum leaf extracts, specifically hexane and methanol extracts, exhibit anti-aging and oxidative stress resistance properties. These extracts protect against oxidative stress and attenuate ROS accumulation in the Caenorhabditis elegans model. The treatment leads to the up-regulation of stress-response genes such as SOD-3 and GST-4, while HSP-16.2 is down-regulated. Glochidion zeylanicum leaf extracts also improve pharyngeal pumping function and promote autofluorescent pigment attenuation. Furthermore, these extracts modulate lifespan extension through the DAF-16/FoxO and SKN-1/Nrf-2 signaling pathways.
