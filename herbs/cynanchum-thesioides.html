Cynanchum thesioides

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cynanchum thesioides has climbing or trailing herbaceous stems that produce opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with entire margins and acute tips. The foliage typically features a smooth texture and a somewhat fleshy consistency in many specimens. Small, tubular flowers emerge in umbel-like cymes, often displaying white or pale coloration. These blossoms are characterized by a complex structure involving a corona that is typical of the Apocynaceae family. The plant also produces slender, elongated follicles that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.
