Kudzu (Pueraria montana)

Kudzu (Pueraria montana) is a member of the Fabaceae family, native to Southeast Asia, East Asia, and South Asia. Traditionally, its roots, rhizomes, and stems have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, tonic, and diuretic actions, and has a long history of use in traditional chinese medicine, japanese kampo medicine, and korean traditional medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous vine.
Height
It typically reaches a height of 2 to 5 meters.
Leaves
Opposite, broad, ovate to lanceolate leaves with glossy upper surface and lighter, sometimes pubescent lower surface, featuring prominent stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Flowers are white to pale lavender, actinomorphic, arranged in racemes, with five ovate petals and a distinctive yellow nectar gland at the base of the calyx.
Stems
Elongated, erect growth habit with opposite branching, smooth, glabrous surface, and presence of adventitious roots along the stem.
