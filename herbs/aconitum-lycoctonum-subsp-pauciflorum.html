Aconitum lycoctonum subsp. pauciflorum

Aconitum lycoctonum subsp.

pauciflorum is a subspecies of the monk's hood plant, belonging to the Aconitum genus and native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

This plant contains aconitine, a toxic compound with medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat pain, rheumatism, and fever, as well as to reduce inflammation and improve circulation.

The health benefits of Aconitum lycoctonum subsp.

pauciflorum include its ability to stimulate the immune system, reduce blood pressure, and alleviate symptoms of respiratory infections.

In herbal preparations, the plant's roots and leaves are often dried, powdered, and used in tinctures, infusions, or decoctions, although caution is advised due to its toxicity and potential side effects.
