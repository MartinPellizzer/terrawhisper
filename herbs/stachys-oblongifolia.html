Stachys oblongifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Stachys oblongifolia?

Stachy's oblongifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and falls into the order Lamiales. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the family Lamiaceae, placing it within the genus Stachys, and it is specifically identified by the species name oblongifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stachy's oblongifolia has an upright, herbaceous growth habit characterized by lanceolate to oblong leaves that are densely covered in fine, white hairs. These leaves are arranged in an opposite pattern along a central stem that can reach significant heights. The foliage typically displays a soft, textured appearance due to the presence of trichomes across its surface. During the flowering stage, the plant produces compact, terminal spikes of small, tubular flowers.
