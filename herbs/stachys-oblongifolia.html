Stachys oblongifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stachy's oblongifolia has an upright, herbaceous growth habit characterized by lanceolate to oblong leaves that are densely covered in fine, white hairs. These leaves are arranged in an opposite pattern along a central stem that can reach significant heights. The foliage typically displays a soft, textured appearance due to the presence of trichomes across its surface. During the flowering stage, the plant produces compact, terminal spikes of small, tubular flowers. These floral structures are often nestled among the protective, hairy bracts that surround the inflorescence.
