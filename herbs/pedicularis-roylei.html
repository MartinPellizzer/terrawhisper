Pedicularis roylei

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pedicularis roylei has a perennial growth habit characterized by erect, unbranched stems that reach heights of up to 30 centimeters and are topped by dense, terminal racemes of bilabiate flowers. The leaves are primarily basal and deeply pinnatifid, featuring narrow, linear lobes that give them a feathery appearance. The inflorescence consists of numerous small, tubular flowers that exhibit a distinct two-lipped structure, typically colored in shades of pink or purple. Each flower possesses a prominent upper lip that arches over the reproductive organs, providing a specialized shape for pollinators. The stems are often covered in fine hairs, adding a subtle texture to the plant's overall structure.
