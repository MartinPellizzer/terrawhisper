Aster souliei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aster souliei has a perennial growth habit characterized by upright, herbaceous stems that typically reach heights of 20 to 50 centimeters. At the terminal ends of the stems, the plant produces small, solitary flower heads composed of numerous ray and disc florets. These composite flowers feature white or pale lavender ray petals surrounding a central cluster of yellow tubular disc florets. The involucral bracts located beneath the flower head are arranged in multiple overlapping series to protect the developing seeds.
