Aster souliei

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aster souliei has a perennial growth habit characterized by upright, herbaceous stems that typically reach heights of 20 to 50 centimeters. The leaves are primarily basal and alternate, presenting an oblong to lanceolate shape with finely serrated margins. At the terminal ends of the stems, the plant produces small, solitary flower heads composed of numerous ray and disc florets. These composite flowers feature white or pale lavender ray petals surrounding a central cluster of yellow tubular disc florets. The involucral bracts located beneath the flower head are arranged in multiple overlapping series to protect the developing seeds.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the island of Hawaii, specifically occurring on the slopes of Mauna Kea. Its habitat is restricted to high-elevation subalpine and alpine shrublands. Within these unique volcanic environments, it thrives in rocky substrates and open areas. The distribution is quite limited due to its specialized ecological requirements. Because of this narrow range, the species is considered a rare component of the local flora.

    What are the herbal preparations of Aster souliei?

    Aster souliei is prepared as a decoction, infusion, or topical salve depending on the desired therapeutic application. The dried flowers and leaves are frequently simmered in water to create a potent liquid extract used for internal consumption. When intended for external skin conditions, the plant material is often infused into a carrier oil to produce a soothing ointment.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Aster souliei can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, or respiratory sensitivity in individuals who are hypersensitive to the Asteraceae plant family. Contact with the plant's sap or foliage may trigger redness, itching, or swelling on the skin. Inhalation of airborne pollen or fine plant particles can lead to sneezing, coughing, or more severe allergic reactions in some people. While systemic toxicity is not widely documented, caution is advised when handling the plant to avoid these localized reactions. Individuals with known allergies to ragweed or daisies should exercise extra care when around this species.
