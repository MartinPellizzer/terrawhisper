Zataria

Zataria multiflora

Zataria (Zataria multiflora) is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Iran and the Middle East, known for its aromatic leaves and traditional use in herbal medicine. It is primarily used to improve symptoms of digestive disorders such as indigestion, bloating, and gastrointestinal discomfort.

Botanical Identification

Zataria multiflora is a perennial herb native to the Middle East and South Asia. It belongs to the Apiaceae family and is also known as Persian cumin or wild cumin. The plant has aromatic leaves and yellow flowers, with a distinctive branched root system. It is sometimes referred to as Zatar or Zatara in regional herbal literature.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

Active Compounds

Zataria multiflora contains essential oils, flavonoids, and terpenoids as its primary bioactive compounds. These compounds work synergistically to provide its therapeutic effects, with essential oils contributing to anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Flavonoids and terpenoids enhance these effects through antioxidant and immune-modulating actions.

Preparation Methods

Essential oil: Used for respiratory issues and to relieve headaches.

Safety Profile

Zataria multiflora is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation due to potential risks. Individuals with allergies to plants in the Asteraceae family should use it with caution.
