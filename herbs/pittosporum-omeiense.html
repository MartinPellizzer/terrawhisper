Pittosporum omeiense

What's the taxonomical classification of Pittosporum omeiense?

Pittosporum omeiense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, its evolutionary lineage continues through the order Apiales. The plant is further categorized under the family Pittosporaceae and the genus Pittosporum, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species omeiense.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Pittosporum omeiense can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and digestive upset if ingested. Contact with the plant's sap or foliage may lead to redness, itching, or a rash on sensitive skin. If a person accidentally consumes any part of the plant, they might experience nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. While there is limited clinical data on its toxicity to humans, caution should be exercised when handling the shrub. Pets should also be kept away from the plant to prevent potential gastrointestinal distress.
