What's the taxonomical classification of Coelogyne occultata?

Coelogyne occultata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical classification, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Asparagales, the plant is a member of the family Orchidaceae. Finally, it is identified by its genus, Coelogyne, and its specific species name, occultata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Coelogyne occultata has elongated, fleshy, monopodial stems that bear numerous small, ovate-shaped leaves arranged in a somewhat distichous pattern.

These leaves are typically thin and leathery with a bright green color that helps the plant capture light in shaded environments. The inflorescence consists of a drooping raceme that produces multiple small, fragrant flowers with delicate petals and a prominent, waxy lip. Each flower features a complex structure where the labellum is often intricately patterned to attract specific pollinators.

The overall growth habit is epiphytic, allowing the plant to cling to the bark of trees in its native tropical habitat.
