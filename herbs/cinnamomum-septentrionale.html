Cinnamomum septentrionale

What's the taxonomical classification of Cinnamomum septentrionale?

Cinnamomum septentrionale belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Laurales and the family Lauraceae, eventually reaching its specific designation within the genus Cinnamomum, with the unique species epithet septentrionale.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cinnamomum septentrionale has elliptical to lanceolate leaves that are typically arranged oppositely on woody stems and possess entire margins with distinct pinnate venation. The foliage is characterized by a coriaceous texture and a deep green color on the upper surface, often transitioning to a paler hue underneath. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils in clusters, eventually developing into small, fleshy drupes. These fruits are typically globose in shape and change color as they reach maturity. The overall structure of the plant reflects its adaptation to specific environmental conditions within its native habitat.
