Lasianthus attenuatus

What's the taxonomical classification of Lasianthus attenuatus?

Lasianthus attenuatus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Gentianaales. This plant is a specific representative of the family Rubiaceae, placing it among the diverse group of coffee-related plants. Within this family, it is categorized under the genus Lasianthus, and its unique botanical identity is defined by its specific designation as the species attenuatus.

What are the herbal preparations of Lasianthus attenuatus?

Lasianthus attenuatus is prepared as a decoction or a simple infusion using the dried leaves and stems of the plant. These herbal preparations are typically administered orally to harness the bioactive compounds found within the vegetative parts. Some practitioners may also crush the fresh foliage to create a topical paste for external application. This paste is often applied directly to the skin to address localized ailments. The method of preparation varies depending on the specific medicinal purpose intended by the user.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Lasianthus attenuatus can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or gastrointestinal distress if ingested due to its unidentified chemical compounds. There is currently very limited scientific documentation regarding its specific toxicological profile in humans. Contact with the plant's leaves or stems may lead to localized redness, itching, or contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. If accidental ingestion occurs, symptoms might include nausea, abdominal pain, or vomiting. Because much of its pharmacology remains unstudied, any exposure should be treated with caution.
