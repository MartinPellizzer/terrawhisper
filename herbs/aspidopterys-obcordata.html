What's the taxonomical classification of Aspidopterys obcordata?

Aspidopterys obcordata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further classified into the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Malpighiales and the family Malpighiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by the genus Aspidopterys, with its specific designation being the species obcordata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aspidopterys obcordata has large, deeply divided, wedge-shaped leaves that exhibit an obcordate shape with prominent, spreading lobes. These leaves are characterized by a distinctively lobed structure that gives them a complex, almost lacy appearance. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread through underground stems. The venation on the leaf surfaces is highly visible and follows the branching pattern of the lobes. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced, typically appearing in relation to the leaf structure.
