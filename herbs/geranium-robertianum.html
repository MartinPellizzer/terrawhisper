Geranium robertianum

What's the taxonomical classification of Geranium robertianum?

Geranium robertianum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further falls into the order Geraniales and is a member of the family Geraniaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic position is defined by its placement within the genus Geranium, with its unique identity designated as the species robertianum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Geranium robertianum has deeply lobed, pinnatisect leaves that are hairy and reddish-tinted, upright stems that often exhibit a purplish hue, and small five-petaled pink flowers arranged in terminal clusters. The foliage is highly divided into narrow segments, providing a delicate texture to the plant's overall appearance. Its stems are slender and can grow quite tall, frequently becoming branched as the plant matures. The flowers feature prominent central stamens and are typically supported by long pedicels. After pollination, the plant develops characteristic explosive seed capsules that scatter seeds over a wide area.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the Mediterranean region, specifically spanning across Southern Europe, North Africa, and parts of Western Asia. It is commonly found growing in rocky areas, cliffs, and woodlands throughout these territories. Due to its adaptability, it has been introduced to other temperate regions where it can thrive in disturbed soils. Its distribution is often linked to areas with moderate rainfall and well-drained substrates. In many non-native locations, it can spread easily through its tendency to colonize open spaces.

What are the pharmacological activities of Geranium robertianum?

This plant has potent anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, antibacterial, antimicrobial, and anti-cancer pharmacological activities driven by its diverse bioactive secondary metabolites. The high concentration of phenolic compounds allows the species to neutralize free radicals and mitigate oxidative stress within biological systems. Its natural chemical profile also demonstrates significant efficacy in inhibiting the growth of various pathogenic bacteria and microorganisms. Furthermore, recent studies indicate that specific extracts from the plant may possess properties capable of suppressing tumor cell proliferation. These multifaceted therapeutic effects make the species a significant subject for future medicinal drug development.

As discussed in a study, the aqueous extract from Geranium robertianum L. herb can serve as a source for the formation of urolithin A, B and C by human gut microbiota. These urolithins demonstrate significant inhibition of TNF-α production. For the most potent urolithin A, inhibition was observed at nanomolar concentrations, specifically at 0.625 μM with 29.2±6.4% of inhibition. Additionally, urolithin C was the only compound inhibiting IL-6 production at 0.625 μM with 13.9±2.2% of inhibition.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating inflammation, SARS-CoV-2 infections, herpetic keratitis, human salivary gland carcinoma, and various systemic disorders. Scientific studies suggest its bioactive compounds possess significant antiviral properties that may inhibit the replication of SARS-CoV-2. In ocular health, the extract has demonstrated potential in managing herpetic keratitis caused by viral infections of the cornea. Research also indicates that certain components can interfere with the progression of human salivary gland carcinoma cells in laboratory settings. Beyond these specific conditions, the plant's phytochemical profile offers broad therapeutic benefits for managing various systemic disorders.
