Haplopteris flexuosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Haplopteris flexuosa?

Haplopteris flexuosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. This plant is further categorized into the family Pteridaceae, specifically within the genus Haplopteris, and is identified by the species name flexuosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Haplopteris flexuosa has a rhizomatous growth habit characterized by slender, creeping rhizomes and pinnately compound fronds that typically measure between 10 and 30 centimeters in length. The individual pinnae are narrowly lanceolate with entire to slightly serrated margins and a distinctively flexible, wavy texture. The stipe is relatively thin and lacks significant scales, often appearing smooth or slightly puberulent toward the base. Sori are located on the underside of the pinnules, typically arranged in a single row near the margins. These morphological traits allow the plant to spread effectively through its immediate forest floor environment.
