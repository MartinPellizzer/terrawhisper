Zanthoxylum calcicola

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Zanthoxylum calcicola has pinnately compound leaves, woody stems, and small, inconspicuous flowers. The foliage consists of multiple leaflets arranged along a central rachis, often exhibiting a bright green color. Its bark is typically textured and provides structural support for the shrubby or small tree growth habit. The plant produces small fruits that follow the flowering stage, contributing to its reproductive cycle. These morphological features are characteristic of many species within the Rutaceae family.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the limestone-rich regions of central and southern China, specifically found in provinces such as Guizhou and Guangxi. It is highly specialized to grow within karst landscapes where calcium-rich soils are abundant. These specific environmental conditions limit its presence to narrow ecological niches within these mountainous areas. Because of this restricted range, the species is not found in any other part of the world. Its distribution is strictly governed by the availability of alkaline substrate and unique local microclimates.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Zanthoxylum calcicola can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested or applied improperly. The plant contains various bioactive compounds that may trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Direct contact with the sap or crushed leaves often results in localized redness or itching on the skin. If swallowed, the chemical constituents can lead to stomach upset or nausea. It is important to exercise caution when handling this species to avoid these adverse physiological responses.
