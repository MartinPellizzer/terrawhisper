Celastrus hindsii

What's the taxonomical classification of Celastrus hindsii?

Celastrus hindsii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, the plant is organized under the order Celastrales and the family Celastraceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Celastrus and is specifically categorized as the species hindsii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Celastrus hindsii has opposite, simple leaves that are elliptical to oblong in shape with entire margins and acute to acuminate apices. The leaves typically feature finely serrated edges and a texture that can range from glabrous to slightly pubescent depending on the specimen. The plant's woody stems are characterized by a branching habit that supports small, inconspicuous flowers. These flowers are often clustered in axillary cymes and possess a structure typical of the Celastraceae family. The resulting fruit is a small capsule that eventually splits to reveal seeds often surrounded by a fleshy aril.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the wet, montane forests of the island of Madagascar. It is primarily found within the eastern rainforest regions where high rainfall and humidity support its growth. Its distribution is restricted to specific elevational zones that provide the necessary shade and moisture levels. Researchers have documented its presence in fragmented patches of native vegetation across certain protected forest corridors. Because it is localized to these unique habitats, the plant remains highly dependent on the stability of the Madagascan ecosystem.

What are the pharmacological activities of Celastrus hindsii?

This plant has anti-inflammatory, anti-tyrosinase, anti-xanthine oxidase, and antioxidant pharmacological activities. Its antioxidant properties help neutralize free radicals to prevent cellular damage. The anti-inflammatory effects suggest potential utility in managing swelling and immune responses. Furthermore, its anti-tyrosinase activity makes it a candidate for skin lightening applications. Finally, the inhibition of xanthine oxidase provides a mechanism for managing hyperuricemia and gout.

As discussed in a study, Celastrus hindsii Benth. has been used in Northern Vietnam for the treatment of disease relating to ulcers, tumors and inflammation. In an acute toxicity study, a single oral dose of 1000, 3000, 5000 and 15000 mg kg-1 of the aqueous extract was administered to mice and observed for seven days. A single oral administration of 15000 mg kg-1 per day for white mice did not determine the LD50 dose. For the semi-chronic study, rabbits were administered 1000 and 3000 mg kg-1 of the extract daily for 35 days. At these doses for 35 days, the extract induced neither clinical symptoms nor significant changes in hematological parameters, and the quantity of aspartate transaminase (AST or GOT) and alanine transaminase (ALT or GPT) did not differ (p> 0.05).

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds rutin, α-amyrin, and β-amyrin. These specific substances contribute to the complex chemical profile found within the plant's tissues. Rutin is a well-known flavonoid that provides significant antioxidant properties to the organism. The triterpenoids α-amyrin and β-amyrin are also present in varying concentrations. These compounds work together to support the biological functions and therapeutic potential of the plant.

As discussed in a study, the ethyl acetate extract of Celastrus hindsii was analyzed through the separation of 14 fractions using column chromatography. Using a dilution of 50-70% methanol in chloroform, researchers identified high levels of total phenolics, flavonoids, and antioxidant activities including DPPH, ABTS, and beta-carotene bleaching methods. Through GC-MS and ESI-MS analyses, 15 principal compounds belonging to fatty acids, amides, flavonoids, sterols, terpenes, and phenols were identified. The maximal quantities were accounted for by alpha-amyrin, beta-amyrin, hydrazine carboxamide, hexadecanoic acid, fucosterol, (3beta)-D:C-friedours-7-en-3-ol, rutin, and 2-hydroxy-1-ethyl ester. Concentrations of the other identified constituents were less than 5%.
