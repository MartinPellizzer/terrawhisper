Pennyroyal (Hedeoma pulegioides): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

Pennyroyal, scientifically known as Hedeoma pulegioides, is a small, aromatic herb native to the Mediterranean region and has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported antiparasitic, antimicrobial, and abortifacient properties.

What is Pennyroyal (Hedeoma Pulegioides)?

Pennyroyal (Hedeoma pulegioides) is a small, aromatic herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, commonly used in herbal medicine for its antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, and digestive properties.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Pennyroyal is Hedeoma pulegioides. This plant belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Lamiales, order Lamiales, family Lamiaceae, genus Hedeoma, and species Hedeoma pulegioides. It is commonly known as pennyroyal in North America and as millepertuis in France. The plant is also referred to as wild pennyroyal in some regions and has synonyms such as Pulegium pulegioides and Hedeoma pulegioides.

Plant Description and Morphology

Pennyroyal (Hedeoma pulegioides) is an annual herbaceous plant characterized by its upright, branched stems covered in fine, glandular hairs, opposite leaves that are narrow, lance-shaped, and aromatic, and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the summer months.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Pennyroyal (Hedeoma pulegioides) is native to the temperate regions of the Mediterranean, particularly southern Europe, northern Africa, and parts of western Asia, where it thrives in dry, sunny environments such as rocky soils and grasslands.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The aerial parts, including leaves and flowers, are primarily used medicinally in traditional herbal medicine.

Phytochemical Composition

Pennyroyal (Hedeoma pulegioides) contains volatile oils as its primary phytochemical component, including monoterpenes such as pulegone, menthone, and limonene, along with smaller amounts of sesquiterpenes and phenolic compounds.

Pharmacological Properties

Pennyroyal (Hedeoma pulegioides) contains volatile oils, primarily pulegone, which exhibit antifungal, antiparasitic, and uterine stimulant properties, though its use is controversial due to potential toxicity.

Mechanisms of Action

Pennyroyal (Hedeoma pulegioides) exerts its effects primarily through the compounds pulegone and menthone, which act as muscle relaxants and antispasmodics, while also exhibiting mild antiseptic and analgesic properties.

Therapeutic Uses and Indications

Pennyroyal (Hedeoma pulegioides) is traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential antiparasitic, antiseptic, and uterine stimulant properties, with historical applications in expelling intestinal worms and aiding in menstrual regulation.

Preparation Methods and Forms

Pennyroyal is typically prepared as a tincture, infusion, or essential oil, with the most common method involving soaking the dried leaves and flowers in alcohol for several weeks to extract its active compounds.

Dosage and Administration

The recommended dosage of Pennyroyal (Hedeoma pulegioides) for external use is typically 1 to 2 drops of the essential oil diluted in 10 mL of a carrier oil, applied directly to the skin, while internal use is generally not recommended due to potential toxicity.

Safety, Side Effects, and Contraindications

Pennyroyal (Hedeoma pulegioides) is toxic and can cause serious side effects, including liver damage, seizures, and even death, particularly when used in large doses or during pregnancy.

Drug Interactions

Pennyroyal (Hedeoma pulegioides) may interact with certain medications, particularly those affecting the liver or central nervous system, due to its potential hepatotoxic effects and anticonvulsant properties.

Toxicity and Precautions

Pennyroyal (Hedeoma pulegioides) is toxic and should not be used during pregnancy, by individuals with liver disease, or in high doses, as it can cause serious adverse effects including liver damage, seizures, and even death.

Cultivation and Harvesting

Pennyroyal is typically cultivated in well-drained soil in full sun, with harvesting occurring in late summer to early autumn when the leaves and stems are most aromatic and contain the highest concentration of essential oils.

Processing and Extraction

The processing and extraction of Pennyroyal (Hedeoma pulegioides) typically involve harvesting the fresh or dried plant material, followed by solvent extraction methods such as ethanol or hexane to isolate the essential oils and active compounds, which are then purified and standardized for use in herbal formulations.

Storage and Shelf Life

Pennyroyal (Hedeoma pulegioides) should be stored in a cool, dry, and dark place in an airtight container to maintain its potency and prevent moisture absorption, with a typical shelf life of up to two years when properly preserved.

Scientific Research and Evidence

Scientific research on Pennyroyal (Hedeoma pulegioides) has primarily focused on its traditional uses in herbal medicine, including its potential antiparasitic, antifungal, and reproductive effects, though most studies are limited to in vitro or animal models with limited human evidence.

Traditional and Historical Uses

Pennyroyal has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported ability to expel parasites, particularly from the digestive system, and has also been employed in folk remedies for its antiseptic and vermifuge properties.

Regulatory Status

Pennyroyal (Hedeoma pulegioides) is not approved for sale as a dietary supplement in the United States due to its potential toxicity and lack of established safety standards.
