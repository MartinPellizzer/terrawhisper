Pennyroyal

Hedeoma pulegioides

Pennyroyal (Hedeoma pulegioides) is a member of the Lamiaceae family, traditionally used in various cultural practices. Its primary traditional uses include treatment of respiratory infections, digestive aid, and pain relief, with the leaves and essential oils being the main plant parts utilized.

Botanical Identification

Pennyroyal, scientifically known as Hedeoma pulegioides, is a member of the Lamiaceae family. Commonly called pennyroyal in the UK and wild mint in parts of the US, it is an annual herb native to Europe and Asia. The plant has opposite, narrow leaves and small, pale blue or purple flowers. It is also known as pulegón in some herbal texts.

Pennyroyal Identification.

Active Compounds

Pennyroyal contains essential oil, flavonoids, and coumarin as its primary bioactive compounds. These compounds work synergistically to exert their effects, with essential oil contributing to its antifungal and insecticidal properties. Flavonoids and coumarin support its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial actions.

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory issues such as coughs and colds.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and as an antispasmodic.

Extract: Used for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

Safety Profile

Pennyroyal is contraindicated during pregnancy due to its potential to cause uterine contractions and fetal harm. It should also be avoided by individuals with known allergies to the plant family. Caution is advised when using it alongside medications that affect the liver or hormonal balance.
