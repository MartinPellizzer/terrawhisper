What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dumasia villosa has perennial, tufted, rhizomatous grasses characterized by cylindrical, erect culms that typically reach heights of 30 to 70 centimeters. Its inflorescence consists of narrow, contracted panicles that are composed of numerous small, spikelet-like branches. The spikelets themselves are relatively small and contain several florets arranged in a compact manner.
