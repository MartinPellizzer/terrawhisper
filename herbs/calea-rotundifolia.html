Calea rotundifolia

Calea rotundifolia, commonly known as Mexican mint or Lamb's Breath, is a plant species native to Mexico and Central America.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its numerous health benefits, including the reduction of anxiety and stress, improvement of sleep quality, and relief from symptoms of depression and headaches.

The therapeutic actions of Calea rotundifolia are attributed to its sedative and anxiolytic properties, which are thought to be mediated by the bioactive constituents, including flavonoids, phenolic acids, and sesquiterpene lactones.
