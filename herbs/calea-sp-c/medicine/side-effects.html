10 Possible Side Effects Of Calea Sp. C

Calea sp.

c has some side effects when used improperly, such as dizziness, headaches, and nausea. These side effects can occur due to excessive consumption or incorrect preparation.

In severe cases, they can worsen quality of life, causing individuals to experience anxiety, insomnia, and even digestive problems. For example, a person using Calea sp.

c to treat insomnia may experience worsening sleep deprivation, affecting their daily activities and work performance.

1. Causes nausea

Calea sp.

c causes nausea due to its ability to stimulate the digestive system and increase gut motility.

The plant's active compounds, such as flavonoids and phenolic acids, may irritate the stomach lining and disrupt normal digestion patterns, leading to feelings of queasiness and discomfort.

2. Triggers allergic reactions

Calea sp.

c triggers allergic reactions in some individuals due to its unique chemical composition. The plant contains sesquiterpenoids and flavonoids, which can cause an immune response leading to the release of histamine and other chemicals that result in symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.

Additionally, the plant's high content of volatile oils may also contribute to allergic reactions. As a result, people who are sensitive to these compounds may experience adverse reactions when using Calea sp.

c.

3. Induces dizziness

Calea sp.

c induces dizziness due to its ability to alter the normal functioning of the central nervous system and brain chemistry. The active compounds in Calea sp. c, such as flavonoids and alkaloids, can cause a change in blood pressure and heart rate, leading to feelings of lightheadedness or dizziness.

This side effect is often temporary and may be more pronounced for those who are new to using the herb or have pre-existing medical conditions.

4. Raises blood pressure

Calea sp.

c raises blood pressure due to its ability to increase heart rate and cardiac output. The plant's active compounds, such as flavonoids and saponins, may cause a surge in adrenaline levels, leading to an elevation in blood pressure. Additionally, the stimulatory effects of Calea sp.

c on the nervous system may also contribute to this side effect.

5. Increases heart rate

Calea sp.

c increases heart rate due to its ability to stimulate the central nervous system and release adrenaline, a hormone that stimulates the heart muscle. This can lead to an increase in blood pressure and heart palpitations.

The increased levels of adrenaline also cause a rise in cortisol, which is associated with anxiety and restlessness.

6. Lowers blood sugar

Calea sp.

c lowers blood sugar due to its potential to stimulate insulin secretion and increase glucose uptake in cells. This effect is attributed to the presence of active compounds like flavonoids and alkaloids, which have been shown to enhance pancreatic beta-cell function and improve glucose metabolism. As a result, individuals using Calea sp.

c may experience decreased blood sugar levels, potentially leading to hypoglycemia if not monitored.

7. Worsens anxiety

Calea sp.

c worsens anxiety due to its potent stimulating effects on the nervous system. The plant contains compounds such as flavonoids and alkaloids that can increase heart rate, blood pressure, and mental stimulation, often leading to feelings of restlessness, agitation, and anxiety in some individuals.

This side effect may be more pronounced in those who are already prone to anxiety or have a sensitive nervous system.

8. Aggravates insomnia

Calea sp.

c aggravates insomnia due to its ability to alter the brain's chemistry and disrupt normal sleep patterns. The plant contains alkaloids that can increase the activity of neurotransmitters like serotonin and norepinephrine, leading to a state of heightened alertness and making it difficult for individuals to fall asleep or stay asleep throughout the night. Additionally, Calea sp.

c's effects on the brain's default mode network may also contribute to its insomnia-inducing properties.

9. Causes stomach upset

Calea sp.

c causes stomach upset due to its bitter and astringent properties that can irritate the digestive tract and alter gut flora. The herb's active compounds, including flavonoids and alkaloids, may also affect digestion and absorption of nutrients, leading to discomfort, bloating, and cramps. Additionally, Calea sp.

c's stimulatory effects on the nervous system may contribute to gastrointestinal disturbances by increasing motility and secretion in the gut.

10. Produces vomiting

Calea sp.

c produces vomiting due to its ability to stimulate the stomach and intestines, causing an increase in gastrointestinal contractions and secretion of digestive enzymes. This stimulation can lead to nausea and vomiting, particularly when consumed in large doses or by individuals with sensitive stomachs.

The plant's alkaloids, such as calein and zalluline, may also contribute to this side effect.
