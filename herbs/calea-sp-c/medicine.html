What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Calea Sp. C (Dream Herb)?

Calea sp.

c, also known as dream herb, has health benefits such as reducing anxiety, insomnia, and stress. The medicinal constituents of Calea sp. c include flavonoids, alkaloids, and sesquiterpenes, which have been shown to have sedative and anxiolytic effects.

Medicinal preparations of Calea sp. c include teas, tinctures, and infusions, which can be used to promote relaxation and improve sleep quality. Possible side effects include drowsiness, dizziness, and headaches. Precautions should be taken when using Calea sp.

c, particularly for pregnant or breastfeeding women, as it may interact with medications or exacerbate underlying health conditions.

What are the active constituents of Calea sp. c?

Calea sp.

c, also known as dream herb, has active constituents such as flavonoids, triterpenoids, and diterpenoids. Flavonoids, particularly apigenin and luteolin, are known for their sedative and anxiolytic properties, contributing to the plant's sleep-promoting effects.

Triterpenoids, like ursolic and oleanolic acid, exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant activities. The diterpenoids in Calea sp.

c may also contribute to its sedative properties, making it a traditional remedy for insomnia and other sleep disorders.

c may interact with certain medications, such as antidepressants and sedatives, potentially exacerbating side effects or worsening underlying health conditions.
