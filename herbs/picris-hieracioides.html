Picris hieracioides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae, specifically falling under the genus Picris, with its specific designation being the species hieracioides.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Picris hieracioides has a basal rosette of oblong to lanceolate leaves with dentate margins and an upright, branched flowering stem reaching up to 60 centimeters in height. The leaves transition from being relatively smooth and larger at the base to smaller, more deeply lobed or serrated versions along the stem. Its inflorescence consists of multiple yellow, ligulate flower heads that lack ray florets, giving them a solid appearance. Each flower head is surrounded by an involucre of green bracts that are often tipped with dark or reddish colors. The plant produces small, lightweight seeds equipped with a pappus of fine hairs to facilitate wind dispersal.
