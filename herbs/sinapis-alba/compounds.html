White Mustard (Sinapis alba) Compounds

The primary compounds in White Mustard include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and phenolic acids, known for their anti-inflammatory effects. Coumarin contributes to its aromatic profile and may have anticoagulant properties. Saponins and tannins provide astringent and digestive benefits, while glycosides and alkaloids support various therapeutic actions. Essential oils and terpenoids contribute to its medicinal and aromatic qualities.

White Mustard (Sinapis alba) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in White Mustard (Sinapis alba) are bioactive compounds with antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. They contribute to the plant's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine, supporting immune function and reducing oxidative stress. These compounds are commonly found in the seeds and leaves of the plant.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid in White Mustard (Sinapis alba) contributes to its antioxidant properties, supporting cellular health and reducing oxidative stress. This compound is part of the plant's broader phytochemical profile, which has been studied for its potential therapeutic benefits in traditional herbal medicine.

3. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in White Mustard (Sinapis alba). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for its anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarin may help support cardiovascular health and reduce inflammation, though it should be used cautiously due to potential side effects.

4. Saponin

Saponin is an active compound found in White Mustard (Sinapis alba). It exhibits mild detoxifying properties and may support respiratory health. Saponins in this plant are known for their ability to aid in expectoration and may help alleviate symptoms of respiratory conditions.

5. Tannin

Tannin in White Mustard (Sinapis alba) contributes to its astringent properties, aiding in the treatment of digestive issues and skin conditions. It helps reduce inflammation and can support wound healing by promoting tissue contraction. This compound is commonly found in the seeds and leaves of the plant.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is an active compound found in White Mustard (Sinapis alba). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly in respiratory and digestive support. Glycosides in White Mustard may help reduce inflammation and aid in expectoration. Their presence highlights the plant's role in traditional herbal remedies.

7. Essential oil

The essential oil of White Mustard (Sinapis alba) contains volatile compounds like alpha-pinene and limonene. These components contribute to its aromatic properties and may support respiratory and digestive health. The oil is often used in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.

8. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in White Mustard (Sinapis alba) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, supporting the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and skin conditions. They also play a role in the plant's natural defense mechanisms.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloids in White Mustard (Sinapis alba) are a class of nitrogen-containing compounds with potential pharmacological effects. They may contribute to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine, though research on their specific therapeutic applications is limited. These compounds are often associated with the plant's medicinal properties and historical usage.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in White Mustard (Sinapis alba). It contributes to the plant's vibrant color and has antioxidant properties. This compound may support cardiovascular health and reduce oxidative stress. Its presence in White Mustard highlights the plant's potential in herbal medicine.
