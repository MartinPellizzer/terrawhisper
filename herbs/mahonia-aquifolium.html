Mahonia Aquifolium Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Mahonia aquifolium, commonly known as Oregon grape, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States, valued for its medicinal properties.

This herb is known for its bitter yellow berries and has been used traditionally for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.

The health benefits of Mahonia aquifolium include supporting liver function, reducing inflammation, and aiding in the treatment of skin conditions.

Its therapeutic actions are attributed to bioactive constituents such as berberine, berberastine, and hydrastine, which have antiseptic and detoxifying properties.

Herbal preparations of Mahonia aquifolium can be made as infusions, tinctures, or decoctions, often used in herbal medicine to support digestive and skin health.
