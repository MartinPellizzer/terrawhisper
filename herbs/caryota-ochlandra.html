Caryota ochlandra

What's the taxonomical classification of Caryota ochlandra?

Caryota ochlandra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant falls under the order Arecales. It is further categorized into the family Arecaceae, which places it within the genus Caryota, and is specifically identified by its species name, ochlandra.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Caryota ochlandra can cause severe skin irritation and physical injury due to the sharp, needle-like spines covering its stems and petioles. Contact with these spines often results in painful punctures that can lead to localized swelling and inflammation. Additionally, the plant contains calcium oxalate crystals that may cause intense stinging or burning sensations if they come into contact with sensitive skin or mucous membranes. Some individuals might also experience an allergic reaction characterized by redness and itching upon contact with the plant material. It is important to handle this species with heavy gloves to avoid these dermatological complications.
