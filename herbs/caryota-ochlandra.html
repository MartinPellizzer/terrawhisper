Caryota ochlandra

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Caryota ochlandra has a solitary trunk that typically reaches heights of 10 to 15 meters and is characterized by prominent, closely spaced leaf scars. The leaves are large, bipinnate, or tripinnate, featuring long, drooping leaflets that give the canopy a pendulous appearance. Its inflorescences are branched and emerge from the trunk in a sequence that leads to the death of the individual plant. The fruit consists of small, globose drupes that transition from green to a dark color upon maturity. This species is distinguished from other members of its genus by its specific leaf arrangement and the structure of its reproductive organs.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Caryota ochlandra can cause severe skin irritation and physical injury due to the sharp, needle-like spines covering its stems and petioles.
