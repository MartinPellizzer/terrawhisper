What are the pest and diseases tips of Secale cereale?

Secale cereale, commonly known as rye, is a crop that is susceptible to various pests and diseases that can significantly impact its yield and quality.

Additionally, rye is vulnerable to fungal diseases such as Septoria leaf spot, caused by Septoria tritici, which can cause yellowing or browning of leaves and reduce yields. Other fungal pathogens that can infect rye include Tilletia caries, which causes a disease known as smut, resulting in the formation of black, powdery spores on the plant's surfaces, and Puccinia graminis, which causes a rust-like disease characterized by yellow or orange pustules on leaves.

Other pests that can affect rye include the cereal leaf beetle, which feeds on leaves and can cause significant damage, as well as the wireworm, which can feed on roots and underground stems, leading to reduced plant growth and yields.
