Rye (Secale cereale)

Rye (Secale cereale) is a member of the Poaceae family, native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. Traditionally, its seeds, leaves, and stems have been used for decoctions, infusions, and culinary uses.

This herb is particularly valued for its diuretic, tonic, and anti-inflammatory actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and japanese kampo medicine.
