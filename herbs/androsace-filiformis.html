Androsace filiformis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Androsace filiformis has a perennial growth habit characterized by thin, thread-like leaves and small, tubular flowers. The foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that emerge from a basal rosette to form a delicate, spreading appearance. Its stems are slender and often procumbent, allowing the plant to spread across rocky substrates. The flowers are typically small and pale, featuring a corolla that is fused into a distinct tube at the base. This morphology is specifically adapted for survival in the crevices of limestone cliffs and rocky environments.
