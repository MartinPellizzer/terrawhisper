What is Senna cladophylla?

Senna cladophylla is a perennial legume plant that belongs to the family Fabaceae, native to southern Africa.

It has a woody base and erect stems, up to 2 meters tall, with compound leaves divided into 10-16 pairs of leaflets, each 2-3 cm long. The inflorescence is a raceme of 1-6 pink or purple flowers, 1-1.5 cm long, with five standard petals and a short keel. The fruit is a flat, brown pod, 2-3 cm long and 1-1.5 cm wide, containing 3-5 seeds, each 2-3 mm long.

The plant grows in well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade in subtropical regions.
