Vitis bryoniifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It falls under the order Vitales and is a member of the family Vitaeceae. More specifically, the plant is positioned within the genus Vitis, with its unique biological identity defined by the species name bryoniifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vitis bryoniifolia has woody, climbing vines with leaves that are typically cordate or hastate in shape, featuring three to five distinct lobes and serrated margins. The leaf surfaces often exhibit a somewhat leathery texture with prominent venation that radiates from the base. Its stems are characterized by the presence of tendrils used for climbing and can develop a brownish, woody bark as the plant matures. The inflorescences consist of small, greenish flowers that emerge in clusters during the growing season.
