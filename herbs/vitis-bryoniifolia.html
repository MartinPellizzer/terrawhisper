Vitis bryoniifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Vitis bryoniifolia?

Vitis bryoniifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It falls under the order Vitales and is a member of the family Vitaeceae. More specifically, the plant is positioned within the genus Vitis, with its unique biological identity defined by the species name bryoniifolia.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Vitis bryoniifolia contains a diverse profile of bioactive secondary metabolites including polyphenols, flavonoids, tannins, and various organic acids. These chemical constituents are primarily concentrated within the leaf and stem structures of the plant. The presence of antioxidant flavonoids plays a critical role in neutralizing free radicals within biological systems. Additionally, the tannins found in the plant contribute to its potential antimicrobial and astringent properties. Research into these specific compounds aims to uncover their therapeutic applications in treating inflammation and oxidative stress.

What are the therapeutic actions of Vitis bryoniifolia?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various inflammatory conditions and skin infections through its medicinal properties. It contains bioactive compounds that help reduce swelling and soothe irritated tissues. Practitioners often apply extracts from the plant to manage topical ailments such as minor wounds or rashes. Some traditional practices also involve using it to address internal ailments related to metabolic imbalances. Its chemical profile suggests potential utility in managing oxidative stress within the body.
