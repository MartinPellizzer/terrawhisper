Torenia flava

What's the taxonomical classification of Torenia flava?

Torenia flava belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized as Equisetopsida, which further descends into the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant is a member of the order Lamiales and falls under the family Linderniaceae. Within this botanical hierarchy, it is specifically identified by the genus Torenia and is defined by the unique species epithet flava.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

While it is generally considered non-toxic to humans, some individuals may experience itchy rashes or swelling upon touching the foliage or flowers. There is limited clinical data regarding its systemic effects, so accidental ingestion should be avoided to prevent potential gastrointestinal discomfort. Pet owners should also exercise caution because the plant might cause mild distress to cats or dogs if eaten. Monitoring for any unexpected reactions is recommended when handling this species in a garden setting.
