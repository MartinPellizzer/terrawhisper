Torenia flava

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Torenia flava has a low-growing, spreading growth habit characterized by soft, herbaceous stems and ovate, slightly hairy leaves with serrated margins. The foliage typically presents a bright green color and provides a dense base for the plant's reproductive structures. Its most distinctive morphological feature is the bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These blooms possess a tubular corolla that flares into five distinct lobes, often featuring subtle markings near the throat to guide pollinators. The overall structure remains compact, making it an ideal groundcover in shaded environments.
