1. Causes allergic reactions

Cinnamomum camphora causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

The culprit is likely the chemical compound cineole, a primary constituent of camphor oil, which can trigger an immune response in susceptible people. This may lead to symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling, as the body's defense mechanisms attempt to eliminate the perceived threat.

Additionally, exposure to camphor may also cause respiratory issues like wheezing and coughing due to its irritating effects on the mucous membranes.

2. Triggers respiratory issues

Cinnamomum camphora triggers respiratory issues due to its ability to cause bronchospasm and increased mucus production in the lungs.

The camphene content in Cinnamomum camphora can irritate the mucous membranes, leading to coughing, wheezing, and shortness of breath.

Additionally, the terpenes present in the herb can exacerbate conditions such as asthma and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), making it important for individuals with respiratory issues to exercise caution when using Cinnamomum camphora.

3. Produces skin irritation

Cinnamomum camphora produces skin irritation due to its high concentration of oils and volatile compounds.

The alpha-pinene and beta-pinene present in the essential oil can cause allergic reactions, inflammation, and irritation when it comes into contact with human skin.

This irritation can range from mild redness and itching to severe rashes and blisters, making Cinnamomum camphora a potentially hazardous substance for individuals with sensitive skin.

4. Induces anxiety attacks

Cinnamomum camphora induces anxiety attacks in some individuals due to its stimulatory effects on the nervous system.

The oil's high concentration of camphene and beta-pinene can cause an increase in heart rate and blood pressure, leading to feelings of panic and anxiety.

Additionally, the oil's potent aroma may overwhelm the senses, exacerbating anxiety symptoms and potentially triggering episodes of hyperventilation and rapid heartbeat.

5. Lowers blood pressure

Cinnamomum camphora lowers blood pressure due to its ability to relax and dilate blood vessels.

This effect is attributed to the presence of camphor's active compounds, which interact with the body's cardiovascular system to reduce peripheral resistance and systolic blood pressure.

Additionally, camphor's sedative properties may also contribute to this decrease in blood pressure by reducing sympathetic nervous system activity and promoting parasympathetic dominance.

6. Increases heart rate

Cinnamomum camphora increases heart rate due to its stimulating properties that affect the nervous system.

The active compounds in camphor, such as borneol and bornyl acetate, can cause an increase in sympathetic nerve activity, leading to an elevated heart rate. This is because these compounds interact with the autonomic nervous system, which regulates various bodily functions including heart rate.

As a result, users may experience an increased heart rate when using Cinnamomum camphora.

7. Affects digestive system

Cinnamomum camphora affects digestive system by causing nausea and stomach discomfort.

This is due to its ability to slow down gastric emptying, reducing the movement of food through the digestive tract, and its stimulation of the vagus nerve, which can induce vomiting.

Additionally, camphor's anticholinergic properties may also contribute to decreased gut motility, leading to constipation or diarrhea in some individuals.

8. Causes dizziness spells

Cinnamomum camphora causes dizziness spells due to its ability to affect the central nervous system.

The inhalation of camphor vapors can stimulate the brain and cause a rapid increase in blood pressure, leading to episodes of lightheadedness and dizziness.

Additionally, camphor's potent chemical composition can also irritate the mucous membranes in the nose and throat, further exacerbating feelings of disorientation and dizziness.

9. Leads to headaches frequent

Cinnamomum camphora leads to headaches frequent due to its stimulating properties, which can cause increased heart rate and blood pressure.

The strong scent of camphor can also irritate the nervous system, leading to feelings of anxiety and tension that may manifest as headaches.

Additionally, some individuals may be sensitive to the aromatic compounds present in camphor, resulting in allergic reactions or irritation that can contribute to headache development.

10. Triggers muscle weakness

Cinnamomum camphora triggers muscle weakness due to its ability to affect the neuromuscular system.

The compound's potential to block sodium channels in nerve cells can lead to a disruption of normal muscle function, causing muscle weakness and fatigue. Additionally, camphor may also interact with other compounds in the body, such as potassium ions, which are important for maintaining proper muscle function.

This combination can result in a decrease in muscle strength and overall mobility.
