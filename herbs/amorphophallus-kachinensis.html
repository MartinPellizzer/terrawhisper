Amorphophallus kachinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Amorphophallus kachinensis?

Amorphophallus kacchinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further organized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Alismatales. As a member of the family Araceae, it is situated within the genus Amorphophallus, specifically identified by its species name, kacchinensis.

During its growth phase, the leaf and the flowering stem appear at different times rather than simultaneously. What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause severe gastrointestinal distress including nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea if ingested in significant quantities. Many species within the Amorphophallus genus contain calcium oxalate crystals that act as a physical irritant to mucous membranes. Upon contact, these needle-like structures can trigger intense stinging, burning sensations, and swelling in the mouth and throat. Some individuals may also experience skin irritation or allergic dermatitis when handling the raw tubers or foliage. Due to these potent irritants, careful preparation is required to mitigate the risk of poisoning.
