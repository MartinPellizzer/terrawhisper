Rubus cochinchinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Rubus cochinchinensis?

Rubus cochinchinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Rosales. Furthermore, it is a member of the family Rosaceae, specifically falling under the genus Rubus, and is identified by the species name cochinchiensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rubus cochinchinensis has arching, woody stems covered in small, sharp prickles and compound leaves consisting of three to five serrated, ovate leaflets. The foliage is typically a deep green color with a slightly textured surface on the upper side. Small, white, five-petaled flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves during the blooming season. These flowers eventually develop into aggregate fruits that are characterized by their bright red color and fleshy texture. The plant structure is overall bushy and sprawling, allowing it to spread effectively through its habitat.
