Carya tonkinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Carya tonkinensis?

Carya tonkinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Fagales. Within this order, it is situated in the family Juglandaceae, which places it among the walnut relatives. Specifically, this plant is categorized under the genus Carya, with its unique identity defined by the species tonkinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Carya tonkinensis has an upright growth habit characterized by simple, pinnately compound leaves that typically consist of seven to thirteen lanceolate leaflets with serrated margins. The bark of the mature tree is thick and deeply furrowed, often displaying a rugged texture. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing as catkins that hang from the branches during the growing season. The fruit is a small, nut-like drupe enclosed within a thin, bladder-like husk that splits open at maturity.
