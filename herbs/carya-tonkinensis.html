Carya tonkinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Carya tonkinensis?

Carya tonkinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Fagales. Within this order, it is situated in the family Juglandaceae, which places it among the walnut relatives. Specifically, this plant is categorized under the genus Carya, with its unique identity defined by the species tonkinensis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Carya tonkinensis contains various secondary metabolites including tannins, phenolic compounds, and specific terpenoids that contribute to its biological activity. These chemical constituents are primarily concentrated within the bark and nut tissues of the hickory species. The presence of tannins is particularly notable, as these substances often exhibit antimicrobial and astringent properties in botanical studies. Researchers analyze these organic compounds to understand how the plant interacts with various pathogens and environmental stressors. Such chemical profiles are essential for evaluating the potential therapeutic applications of the species in traditional or modern pharmacology.

What are the therapeutic actions of Carya tonkinensis?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat digestive ailments, skin inflammations, and various minor wounds. The bark and leaves possess medicinal properties that have been utilized by indigenous communities for centuries. Specifically, decoctions made from the plant can soothe stomach discomfort and indigestion. When applied topically, the extracts help reduce swelling and irritation on the skin. These traditional remedies serve as a natural way to manage localized physical distress.
