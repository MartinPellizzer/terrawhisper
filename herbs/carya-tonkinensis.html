Carya tonkinensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Carya tonkinensis has an upright growth habit characterized by simple, pinnately compound leaves that typically consist of seven to thirteen lanceolate leaflets with serrated margins. The bark of the mature tree is thick and deeply furrowed, often displaying a rugged texture. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing as catkins that hang from the branches during the growing season. The fruit is a small, nut-like drupe enclosed within a thin, bladder-like husk that splits open at maturity. This species is distinguished from other hickory varieties by its specific leaf arrangement and the unique structure of its nut capsule.
