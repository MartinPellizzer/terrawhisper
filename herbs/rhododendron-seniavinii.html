Rhododendron seniavinii

What's the taxonomical classification of Rhododendron seniavinii?

Rhododendron seniavinii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is part of the order Ericales and the family Ericaceae. Finally, it is situated within the genus Rhododendron, specifically identified by its species name, seniavinii.

What are the herbal preparations of Rhododendron seniavinii?

Rhododendron seniavinii is prepared primarily as a decoction by boiling the dried leaves and stems in water to extract its medicinal components. This liquid extract is often consumed in small doses to address specific traditional health concerns. Some practitioners may also crush the plant material into a fine powder to be mixed with honey or water for easier ingestion. Occasionally, the plant is infused into oils to create topical applications for external use. These various methods allow for different concentrations of the active phytochemicals to be delivered to the body.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Rhododendron seniavinii can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, neurological impairment, and potentially fatal cardiac arrhythmia due to the presence of toxic grayanotoxins. These toxins interfere with the sodium channels in cell membranes, disrupting the normal electrical signaling within the body. Ingesting even small amounts may lead to symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain. As the toxicity progresses, a person might experience dizziness, blurred vision, or a significant drop in blood pressure. In extreme cases, the physiological impact can cause irregular heartbeats that require immediate medical intervention.
