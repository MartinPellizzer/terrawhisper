Rhododendron seniavinii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhododendron seniavinii has small, leathery, elliptical leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and covered in fine, ferruginous hairs on the underside. The plant exhibits a woody, shrubby growth habit characterized by spreading branches. Its flowers are typically bell-shaped and range in color from pale pink to deep rose. The inflorescences are arranged in compact clusters at the ends of the stems. These morphological features distinguish it from other members of its genus within its specific high-altitude habitat.

        What side effects this plant can have?

        This plant can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, neurological impairment, and potential death if ingested due to its high concentration of grayanotoxins. These toxins interfere with the sodium channels in cell membranes, leading to a dangerous drop in blood pressure and heart rate. Victims often experience symptoms such as intense nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain shortly after consumption. Additionally, the chemical impact can result in dizziness, confusion, and cardiac arrhythmias. Such physiological reactions make any part of the plant extremely hazardous to human health.
