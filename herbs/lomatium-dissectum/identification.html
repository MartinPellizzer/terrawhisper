Scientific Classification

Lomatium dissectum belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Apiales, family Araliaceae, genus Lomatium, and species dissectum. These classifications help identify its botanical relationships and place it within the broader system of plant life.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of the plant is Lomatium dissectum, first described by the botanist Thomas Nuttall in 1818. It is also known by several synonyms, including Lomatium dissectum and Lomatium hirsutum, though the latter is now considered a synonym of Lomatium dissectum.

Morphological Characteristics

Lomatium dissectum is a perennial herb with a deep taproot system. It has upright, branched stems that grow up to 1-2 meters tall. Its leaves are compound, with finely dissected lobes, giving it a fern-like appearance. The plant produces a branched, terminal inflorescence of yellow flowers. The fruits are small, dry capsules containing numerous seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Lomatium dissectum can be distinguished by its finely dissected, fern-like leaves and yellow flowers with five petals. It has a hollow, branching stem and lacks the hairy stems of Lomatium camtschaticum. Its leaves are more deeply cut than those of Lomatium grayi. The root system is fibrous and distinct from other species in the genus.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Lomatium dissectum is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, including regions of Washington, Oregon, and northern California. It thrives in cool, moist climates with moderate temperatures. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils and is commonly found at elevations between 1,000 and 4,000 feet.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Lomatium dissectum blooms from late spring to early summer, typically between May and June. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early fall, around August to September. For identification purposes, the best time to observe key features is during the flowering period, which is critical for accurate botanical recognition.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Lomatium dissectum is most closely related to species within the same genus, Lomatium. It shares key characteristics with other members of the Apiaceae family, such as compound leaves and umbellate flowers. Its phylogenetic placement reflects its evolutionary ties to other biennials and perennials in the tribe Lomadeae.
