What is Annual Seseli (Seseli Annuum)?

Annual Seseli (Seseli annuum) is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its slender stems, small blue or purple flowers, and its use in traditional herbal medicine for its purported diuretic and digestive properties.

Botanical Identity and Classification

Annual Seseli (Seseli annuum) is a flowering plant species belonging to the Apiaceae family, commonly known as the carrot or parsley family, and is native to the Mediterranean region.

Plant Description and Morphology

Annual Seseli (Seseli annuum) is a biennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and tall, branching stems that bear clusters of small, blue to violet flowers.

Annual Seseli (Seseli annuum) is native to the temperate regions of Europe and western Asia, particularly thriving in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils across countries such as France, Italy, Spain, and parts of Turkey.
