Annual Seseli

Seseli annuum

Annual Seseli (Seseli annuum) is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia, belonging to the carrot family, known for its small white flowers and its use in traditional medicine to support respiratory and digestive health.

Botanical Identification

Annual Seseli, scientifically known as Seseli annuum, belongs to the Apiaceae family. Common names include Devil's Bit and Wild Caraway, though regional variations exist. It is an annual herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant has slender stems, pinnately divided leaves, and small white to pale yellow flowers. Synonyms in herbal texts often include Anthriscus annua and Selinum annuum.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory ailments such as coughs and colds.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and to extract alkaloids from the plant.

Extract: Used for its antispasmodic properties to treat muscle spasms.

Safety Profile

Annual Seseli (Seseli annuum) is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause mild gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to limited safety data. Always consult a healthcare professional before use.
