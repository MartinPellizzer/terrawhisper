Annual Seseli (Seseli annuum) is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia, belonging to the carrot family, known for its small white flowers and its use in traditional medicine to support respiratory and digestive health.

Morphology

Annual Seseli (Seseli annuum) is a biennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and tall, branching stems that bear clusters of small, blue to violet flowers.

Distribution

Annual Seseli (Seseli annuum) is native to the Mediterranean region, including parts of southern Europe, North Africa, and western Asia, and is commonly found in dry, rocky, and semi-arid habitats.

Traditional Uses

Annual Seseli (Seseli annuum) has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported ability to support digestive health, alleviate respiratory congestion, and promote skin healing through its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Medicinal Systems

In traditional medicinal systems such as Ayurveda, Traditional Chinese Medicine, and Native American herbal practices, Annual Seseli (Seseli annuum) has been used for its purported anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and digestive properties.

Phytochemistry

The phytochemistry of Annual Seseli (Seseli annuum) includes bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, alkaloids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its traditional use in herbal medicine for their anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and analgesic properties.

Pharmacology

Annual Seseli (Seseli annuum) exhibits pharmacological properties characterized by anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antimicrobial effects, primarily attributed to its bioactive constituents that modulate immune responses and cellular signaling pathways.

Botanical Identification

Annual Seseli, scientifically known as Seseli annuum, belongs to the Apiaceae family. Common names include Devil's Bit and Wild Caraway, though regional variations exist. It is an annual herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant has slender stems, pinnately divided leaves, and small white to pale yellow flowers. Synonyms in herbal texts often include Anthriscus annua and Selinum annuum.

Annual Seseli (Seseli annuum) is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause mild gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to limited safety data. Always consult a healthcare professional before use.
