Scientific and Botanical Profile

Annual Seseli, with botanical name Seseli annuum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family, commonly known as the carrot or parsley family.

It is also referred to as Common Seseli, Seseli Annual, Seseli Annua, Seseli Annuum, and Seselweed, reflecting its widespread recognition across different regions. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this annual herb thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils, fields, and along roadsides. Morphologically, Seseli annuum is a herbaceous plant with slender, upright stems, pinnately compound leaves, and small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels.

Its distinctive appearance and ecological adaptability make it a notable species within its native range.

Forms, Preparation and Usage

Seseli annuum has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, and it is available in various forms including fresh plant, dried tincture, powder, essential oil, and capsule.

To prepare seseli annuum, it can be made into a tea by steeping dried leaves in hot water, or a decoction by boiling the dried plant material for a longer period. It can also be used as an infusion or applied topically for skin conditions. The recommended dosage for adults is typically 1-2 grams of dried plant per cup of tea, while children should only use it if deemed safe by a healthcare provider.

It is advised to use seseli annuum sparingly, with a maximum duration of 2-3 weeks, to avoid potential side effects and ensure safe usage.

Safety, Side Effects and Contraindications

Seseli annuum can be used as a medicinal plant with potential therapeutic benefits, but it must be approached with caution due to its possible safety concerns.

While some studies suggest it may have anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, it is also associated with side effects such as gastrointestinal discomfort, nausea, and allergic reactions in sensitive individuals. There are limited clinical data on drug or herb interactions, but caution is advised when combining it with sedatives, anticoagulants, or other herbs that may have similar effects. Special populations, including pregnant or breastfeeding women, should avoid seseli annuum due to potential risks to fetal or infant development, and individuals with chronic illnesses should consult a healthcare provider before use.

For safe use, it is recommended to consult a qualified herbalist or healthcare professional, start with low doses, and monitor for adverse reactions.

Growing, Harvesting and Storage

Seseli annuum grows best in well-drained, loamy soil with a pH between 6.

0 and 7. 5, in full sun to partial shade, and with consistent moisture during the growing season. It thrives in temperate climates and requires regular watering, especially during dry spells, but should not be overwatered to prevent root rot. To ensure healthy growth, it is important to weed regularly, mulch around the base to retain moisture, and monitor for pests like aphids and spider mites. Harvesting should occur when the flowers are fully open, typically in late summer, using clean scissors to cut the flower heads without damaging the stems.

After harvesting, the plant material should be dried in a cool, shaded area with good air circulation, then stored in airtight containers in a cool, dark place to preserve its medicinal potency.
