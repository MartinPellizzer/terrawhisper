Botanical Identification of English Lavender (Lavandula officinalis)

To identify English Lavender (Lavandula officinalis) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

English Lavender, Lavandula officinalis, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Lamiales, order Lamiales, family Lamiaceae, genus Lavandula, and species officinalis. These classifications reflect its position within the plant kingdom and its relationship to other flowering plants.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of English Lavender is Lavandula officinalis, first described by Carl von Linné. It is also known by several synonyms, including Lavandula angustifolia and Lavandula latifolia. Common names such as true lavender and English lavender are widely used. Regional and historical names vary, reflecting its long use in herbal medicine and perfumery.

Morphological Characteristics

English Lavender (Lavandula officinalis) is a perennial shrub with a compact, upright growth habit. It has a fibrous root system that anchors the plant and absorbs moisture. The stems are square and slightly woody, with a grayish-blue hue. Leaves are linear, aromatic, and covered in fine hairs. The inflorescence consists of spikes of narrow, pale purple flowers. Each flower has five petals and is pollinated by insects. The fruit is a small capsule containing numerous tiny seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Lavandula officinalis can be distinguished by its square, hairy stems, narrow linear leaves with a silvery underside, and spike-like inflorescences of violet-blue flowers. It differs from Lavandula angustifolia by its more robust growth and slightly different flower color, and from Lavandula stoechas by the absence of the distinctive lemon-scented bracts.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

English Lavender (Lavandula officinalis) is native to the Mediterranean region, including southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with mild, wet winters and hot, dry summers. It prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils and is commonly found at elevations between 500 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

English Lavender (Lavandula officinalis) blooms from late spring to early summer, typically between May and July. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early autumn, around August to September. For identification purposes, the best time to harvest is during early to mid-summer when the flowers are in full bloom and the plant is most aromatic.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Lavandula officinalis belongs to the genus Lavandula in the family Lamiaceae. Its closest relatives include other Lavandula species such as Lavandula angustifolia and Lavandula stoechas. It shares key family traits like square stems and aromatic leaves, which are characteristic of the Lamiaceae family.
