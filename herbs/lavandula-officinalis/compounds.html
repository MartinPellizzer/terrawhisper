English Lavender (Lavandula officinalis) Compounds

The primary compounds in English lavender include essential oil, which contains terpenoids like linalool and linalyl acetate. Flavonoids such as apigenin and luteolin contribute to its antioxidant properties. Phenolic acids and tannins provide additional anti-inflammatory benefits. Coumarin and other compounds support its traditional use in herbal medicine.

English Lavender (Lavandula officinalis) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of English Lavender (Lavandula officinalis) is a key active compound, containing over 100 volatile compounds, primarily linalyl acetate and linalool. These terpenes contribute to its calming, antiseptic, and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a cornerstone in herbal medicine for treating skin conditions, anxiety, and insomnia.

2. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a major class of active compounds in English Lavender (Lavandula officinalis). They contribute to the plant's aromatic properties and have antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and calming effects. Terpenoids like linalyl acetate and linalool are key to the therapeutic uses of lavender in herbal medicine.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in English Lavender (Lavandula officinalis). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting its use in herbal medicine for calming effects and skin health. These compounds are extracted along with essential oils during distillation.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in English Lavender (Lavandula officinalis). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in treating skin irritations and stress-related conditions. This compound is also involved in the plant's aromatic profile and therapeutic effects in herbal medicine.

5. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in English Lavender (Lavandula officinalis). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support skin health and wound healing. Tannins also aid in reducing inflammation and can help in managing digestive issues.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a naturally occurring compound found in English Lavender (Lavandula officinalis). It contributes to the plant's mild antispasmodic and sedative properties, often used in herbal medicine to promote relaxation and ease muscle tension.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in English Lavender (Lavandula officinalis). It contributes to the plant's aromatic profile and may support calming effects. Glycosides are often associated with mild sedative and antispasmodic properties in herbal medicine.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in English Lavender (Lavandula officinalis). It contributes to the plant's mild cleansing properties and may support skin health. Saponins can also aid in reducing inflammation and promoting digestive comfort when used as part of herbal remedies.

9. Alkaloid

English Lavender (Lavandula officinalis) contains the alkaloid lavendustin. This compound contributes to the plant's calming properties and is associated with its use in treating anxiety and insomnia. Lavendustin is typically present in smaller quantities compared to other active compounds like linalool. Its role in the plant's therapeutic effects is less prominent than that of essential oils.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in English Lavender (Lavandula officinalis). It contributes to the plant's vibrant color and has antioxidant properties that support skin health and reduce inflammation. This compound is part of the plant's broader therapeutic profile in herbal medicine.
