Litsea auriculata

What's the taxonomical classification of Litsea auriculata?

Litsea auriculata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and falls into the order Laurales. This plant is a member of the family Lauraceae, which places it within the genus Litsea, and is specifically identified by its species name, auriculata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Litsea auriculata has simple, alternate, elliptic to oblong leaves that measure between 5 and 15 centimeters in length and possess distinctively revolute margins and entire edges. The leaf texture is typically coriaceous, meaning it feels somewhat leathery to the touch. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in axillary panicles, often appearing in clusters along the stem. The plant produces small, fleshy drupes that transition in color as they mature. Its woody stems exhibit a characteristic branching pattern typical of the Lauraceae family.
