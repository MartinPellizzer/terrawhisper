Trichodesma calycosum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Trichodesma calycosum has an erect, herbaceous stem that typically reaches heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and is covered in fine, spreading hairs. The leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem and possess an elliptical to lanceolate shape with entire margins. Its flowers are characterized by a distinct, tubular calyx that is significantly larger than the corolla, giving the plant its specific epithet. The corolla is usually small and pale blue or white, featuring five lobes that spread outward from the center. Small, cylindrical fruits develop from the base of the flower to facilitate seed dispersal.
