Trichodesma calycosum

What's the taxonomical classification of Trichodesma calycosum?

Trichodesma calycosum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Boraginales and falls within the family Boraginaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is established through its membership in the genus Trichodesma, with its unique designation being the species calycosum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Trichodesma calycosum has an erect, herbaceous stem that typically reaches heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and is covered in fine, spreading hairs.

The leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem and possess an elliptical to lanceolate shape with entire margins. Its flowers are characterized by a distinct, tubular calyx that is significantly larger than the corolla, giving the plant its specific epithet. The corolla is usually small and pale blue or white, featuring five lobes that spread outward from the center.

Small, cylindrical fruits develop from the base of the flower to facilitate seed dispersal.

      Trichodesma calycosum is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste depending on the specific medicinal purpose.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Trichodesma calycosum can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. The plant contains various secondary metabolites that may trigger localized redness or itching upon direct contact with the dermis. If a person accidentally consumes the plant, they might experience nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. Some individuals may also exhibit hypersensitivity reactions such as swelling or rashes. Careful handling is advised to avoid these potential adverse biological responses.
