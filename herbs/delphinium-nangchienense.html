What's the taxonomical classification of Delphinium nangchienense?

Delphinium nangchienense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Ranunculales, this plant is a member of the family Ranunculaceae. Specifically, it is identified by the genus Delphinium and is distinguished at the species level as nangchienense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Delphinium nangchienense has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by erect stems that typically reach heights of 20 to 50 centimeters. The leaves are primarily basal and cauline, featuring a palmate or deeply lobed structure with serrated margins. Its inflorescence consists of a dense raceme bearing zygomorphic flowers that exhibit the classic spurred shape typical of the genus. The petals are often colored in shades of blue or violet, providing a distinct contrast against the green foliage. Small, glandular hairs may be present on the stems and calyx, contributing to the plant's fine surface texture.
