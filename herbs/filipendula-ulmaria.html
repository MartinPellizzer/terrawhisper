Queen Anne'S Lace (Filipendula ulmaria)

Queen Anne'S Lace (Filipendula ulmaria) is a member of the Rosaceae family, native to Europe, and Asia. Traditionally, its leaves, flowers, and roots have been used for infusions, decoctions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its diuretic, anti-inflammatory, and carminative actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, traditional chinese medicine, and mediterranean herbal traditions.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters.
Leaves
Bipinnate leaves with upper surface pale green and lower surface dark green, bearing distinct stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Inflorescence composed of a flat, umbellate cluster with white petals and a single purple central flower, actinomorphic flowers with five ovate petals and five sepals, each petal with a distinct central vein.
Stems
Cylindrical, hollow, ridged, and terete, the stems are usually unbranched, ascending, and have a smooth surface with longitudinal veins.
