Gnetum parvifoliuum has small, elliptical to lanceolate leaves that are typically arranged in whorls or opposite pairs along its woody, branching stems. Its stems are slender and can exhibit a somewhat scrambling or shrubby growth habit depending on the specific environment. The reproductive structures consist of specialized cones that house the seeds, which are characteristic of the Gnetaceae family.
