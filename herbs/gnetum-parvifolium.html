Gnetum parvifolium

What's the taxonomical classification of Gnetum parvifolium?

Gnetum parvifolium belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it is positioned under the subclass Gnetidae and the order Gnetales. This plant is further categorized into the family Gnetaceae, falling under the genus Gnetum, with its specific designation being the species parvifolium.

What are the pharmacological activities of Gnetum parvifolium?

This plant has demonstrated diverse pharmacological activities including HIV-1 inhibitory activity, anti-cancer properties, anti-inflammatory effects, anti-obesity capabilities, and antibacterial actions. Research indicates that its bioactive compounds can effectively suppress the replication of the HIV-1 virus within host cells. Furthermore, the plant exhibits potent cytotoxicity against various cancer cell lines and helps mitigate inflammation by modulating specific biological pathways. Its metabolic influence extends to weight management through anti-obesity mechanisms that target lipid accumulation. Finally, the presence of secondary metabolites provides a defense against several bacterial pathogens.

According to a study published by "Yao xue xue bao = Acta pharmaceutica Sinica", resveratrol, isorrhapontigenin, and pinosylvin were isolated from Gnetum parvifolium. These natural products and their analogues were tested for their inhibitory activity of HIV-1. Natural product 12a and its analogues 12d, 12e, and 12g display significant inhibitory activity of HIV-1 replication. Among these, compound 12d, also known as trans-3, 4, 5, 4'-tetrahydroxystilbene, exhibits the most potent anti-HIV-1 activity. This specific compound 12d has an IC50 value of 1.84 micromol x L(-1).

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the specific medicinal compounds (±)-Gnetuparin A, isorhapontigenin, resveratrol, pinosylvin, and various stilbenoids. These bioactive molecules are classified primarily as stilbenoids, which are known for their potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Among these, resveratrol and pinosylvin serve as key components that contribute to the plant's therapeutic potential. The presence of (±)-Gnetuparin A and isorhapontigenin further enhances the chemical complexity and medicinal value of the species. Together, these diverse compounds provide a chemical foundation for investigating the plant's biological activities.

According to a study published by "Organic & biomolecular chemistry", the stems of Gnetum parvifoliuum contain a pair of stilbene enantiomers known as (±)-Gnetuparin A (1a/1b). These compounds feature a 6/5/5 tricyclic ring system and a novel skeleton based on a stilbenoid coupled with a methylcyclopentanone to form a rare cyclopenta[b]furan-6-one motif. Biological investigations demonstrated that 1a and 1b exhibit good xanthine oxidase inhibitory activities with IC50 values of 74.9 and 56.1 µM, respectively. Furthermore, enzyme kinetic analysis revealed that compound 1b acts as a noncompetitive inhibitor of xanthine oxidase with a Ki value of 54.72 μM. These findings provide a theoretical basis for developing new uric-acid-lowering drugs from Gnetum parvifolium.
