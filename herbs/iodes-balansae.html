What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Iodes balansae has a perennial life cycle characterized by an erect, slender stem that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The leaves are linear and narrow, emerging from the base of the plant to form a somewhat tufted appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a terminal spike containing small, inconspicuous flowers arranged closely along the upper portion of the stem. Each flower possesses delicate petals and reproductive structures typical of the Cyperaceae family. The plant also develops a creeping rhizome that allows it to colonize wet or marshy habitats effectively.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

There are also indications that high doses could lead to dizziness or altered states of consciousness. Monitoring for these specific symptoms is essential to avoid long-term physiological damage.
