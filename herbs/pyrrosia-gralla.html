Pyrrosia gralla

What's the taxonomical classification of Pyrrosia gralla?

Pyrrosia gralla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Polypodiinae and is further categorized within the order Polypodiales. The plant is a representative of the family Polypodiaceae, specifically situated within the genus Pyrrosia, and is identified by its unique species designation, gralla.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Pyrrosia gralla can cause gastrointestinal distress including nausea, vomiting, and abdominal cramping if ingested in significant quantities. Some individuals may also experience skin irritation or allergic dermatitis upon direct contact with the plant's foliage. There is a potential for mild neurological symptoms such as dizziness or headaches following consumption. Because its full toxicological profile is not comprehensively documented, caution is necessary when handling the specimen. Always consult a medical professional if symptoms persist after exposure.
