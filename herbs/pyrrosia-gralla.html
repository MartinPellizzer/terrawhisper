Pyrrosia gralla

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pyrrosia gralla has small, woody, perennial stems that produce opposite, sessile leaves characterized by an elliptic to lanceolate shape and entire margins. These leaves are typically leathery in texture and possess a somewhat thickened surface to withstand its alpine environment. The plant features small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils in clusters. Its reproductive structures lead to the formation of small, dry fruits that aid in seed dispersal. The overall growth habit is compact and low-growing, reflecting its adaptation to rocky, high-altitude habitats.

Because its full toxicological profile is not comprehensively documented, caution is necessary when handling the specimen. Always consult a medical professional if symptoms persist after exposure.
