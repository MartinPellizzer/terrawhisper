Pyrrosia gralla has small, woody, perennial stems that produce opposite, sessile leaves characterized by an elliptic to lanceolate shape and entire margins. The plant features small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils in clusters. Its reproductive structures lead to the formation of small, dry fruits that aid in seed dispersal.
