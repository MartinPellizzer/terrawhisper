Dolomiaea edulis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dolomiaea edulis has a perennial herbaceous growth habit characterized by thick, fleshy rhizomes and basal rosettes of leaves that are typically lanceolate or ovate in shape. The leaf margins are often slightly serrated or entire, depending on the specific environmental conditions of its high-altitude habitat. During the flowering season, the plant produces upright inflorescences that bear small, tubular flowers arranged in dense clusters. These floral structures are typically white or pale in color and are supported by sturdy, upright stems. The overall morphology is well-adapted to the rocky, alpine terrains where it is commonly found.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the high-altitude alpine regions of the southern Alps in Italy. It is specifically found within the limestone-rich terrains of the Dolomites mountains. These populations typically occupy rocky crevices and scree slopes at elevations ranging from 2,000 to 3,000 meters. Due to its restricted habitat, the species is confined to very localized and fragmented microclimates. This limited range makes the plant highly sensitive to changes in its specific mountain ecosystem.
