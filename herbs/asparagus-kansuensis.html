Asparagus kansuensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Asparagus kansuenxis has slender, arching stems adorned with small, needle-like cladodes that function as leaves and delicate, scale-like leaves at the nodes. These photosynthetic stems are typically bright green and create a fine, feathery texture throughout the foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the axils of the cladodes. Following flowering, it develops small, fleshy berries that transition from green to red or pink when mature. The root system consists of thickened, tuberous structures that allow the plant to store water and nutrients efficiently.
