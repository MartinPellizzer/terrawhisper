Dodder (Cuscuta Bracteosa)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Cuscuta bracteosa.

Dodder, scientifically known as Cuscuta bracteosa, is a parasitic flowering plant often referred to as a medicinal herb and adaptogen. It is traditionally used for its potential to support immune function and reduce inflammation, making it valuable for promoting overall wellness. In traditional Chinese medicine and Native American practices, dodder has been employed to treat ailments such as fatigue, digestive issues, and respiratory conditions. Modern wellness applications include its use in herbal formulations aimed at enhancing vitality and supporting stress resilience. This unique plant is notable for its lack of leaves and chlorophyll, relying entirely on host plants for nutrients, and contains compounds like flavonoids and alkaloids that contribute to its medicinal profile.
