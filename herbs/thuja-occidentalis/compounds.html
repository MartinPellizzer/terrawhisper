White Cedar (Thuja occidentalis) Compounds

The primary active compounds in White Cedar include essential oils, terpenoids, and flavonoids, which contribute to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Tannins and coumarin provide astringent and antispasmodic effects, while phenolic acids and glycosides support antioxidant activity. Saponins and alkaloids add additional therapeutic benefits, and anthocyanins contribute to its protective and healing properties.

White Cedar (Thuja occidentalis) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of White Cedar (Thuja occidentalis) contains bioactive compounds like thujone and alpha-thujone, which contribute to its antimicrobial and antifungal properties. These compounds are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their potential therapeutic effects on respiratory and skin conditions.

2. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in White Cedar (Thuja occidentalis). They contribute to the plant's antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Specific terpenoids like thujone and alpha-thujone are responsible for its therapeutic effects in traditional herbal medicine. These compounds are extracted from the bark and used in various medicinal preparations.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in White Cedar (Thuja occidentalis).
