10 Possible Side Effects Of Thuja Occidentalis (Arborvitae)

Thuja occidentalis has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal problems, skin irritation, and allergic reactions.

These side effects are caused by overexposure to the herb's essential oils, particularly thujone. For instance, excessive consumption of Thuja tea can lead to stomach pain, nausea, and diarrhea, which can severely impair daily activities and overall well-being.

In severe cases, skin contact with Thuja extracts can cause dermatitis, eczema, or even life-threatening anaphylaxis.
