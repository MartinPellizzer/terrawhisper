10 Precautions To Take When Using Thuja Occidentalis (Arborvitae)

Thuja occidentalis has some precautions to consider before using it medicinally, such as consulting with a healthcare professional due to its potential toxicity and interactions with other medications.

Taking this herb in excess or without proper guidance can lead to adverse effects, including skin irritation, diarrhea, and liver damage.

For instance, using thuja for extended periods may cause gastrointestinal problems, while mixing it with other herbs or medications can amplify its toxicity.

1. Monitor blood pressure regularly

When using Thuja occidentalis medicinally, it's important to take dosage carefully due to the risk of adverse effects and interactions with other medications.

High doses can cause gastrointestinal disturbances, allergic reactions, and skin irritation, while prolonged use may lead to liver damage or thyroid problems.

Additionally, Thuja occidentalis can interact with blood thinners, diabetes medications, and immunosuppressants, which is why careful dosing and monitoring are crucial to ensure safe and effective treatment.

When using Thuja occidentalis medicinally, it's important to contraindicate in pregnancy.

This is because the herb has been shown to stimulate uterine contractions and may induce miscarriage or preterm labor. Additionally, the chemical thujone found in Thuja can also cross the placenta and potentially harm the developing fetus.

Therefore, it is crucial for pregnant women to avoid using this herb medicinally to ensure a safe and healthy pregnancy.

When using Thuja occidentalis medicinally, it's important to stop use with bleeding.
