White Cedar (Thuja occidentalis) Uses

White Cedar is used to treat respiratory conditions such as the common cold, bronchitis, and sinusitis. It helps alleviate symptoms of lung infections and congestion. It may also support the immune system against tuberculosis and otitis media. Its antimicrobial properties make it useful for skin infections and allergic rhinitis.

White Cedar (Thuja occidentalis) Monograph

1. Common cold

White Cedar is traditionally used to support the immune system and alleviate symptoms of the common cold. Its antiviral and anti-inflammatory properties may help reduce congestion and soothe respiratory discomfort. It is often prepared as a tincture or steam inhalation to ease coughing and nasal blockage.

2. Bronchitis

White Cedar has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, including bronchitis. Its expectorant properties help clear mucus, while its anti-inflammatory effects may reduce bronchial irritation. Often prepared as a tincture or steam inhalation, it is valued for its soothing and cleansing action on the respiratory tract.

3. Respiratory infections

White Cedar is traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly for colds, coughs, and bronchitis. Its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties help reduce mucus production and soothe irritated airways. It is often prepared as a tincture or steam inhalation to alleviate symptoms of respiratory infections.

4. Tuberculosis

White Cedar has been historically used in traditional medicine to support respiratory health. It contains compounds that may help reduce inflammation and clear mucus, making it a potential aid in managing tuberculosis symptoms. However, it should not replace conventional medical treatment for tuberculosis.

5. Sinusitis

White Cedar (Thuja occidentalis) is used to support respiratory health, particularly for sinusitis. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties help reduce nasal congestion and combat infections. It is often prepared as a steam inhalation or nasal spray to alleviate symptoms and promote clear breathing.

6. Lung infections

White Cedar has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in cases of lung infections. Its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties may help reduce symptoms and promote healing. It is often used in steam inhalations or as a gargle to soothe irritated airways and clear congestion.

7. Otitis media

White Cedar (Thuja occidentalis) is used in herbal medicine to support the treatment of otitis media, or middle ear infection. Its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties help reduce infection and inflammation in the ear. It is often combined with other herbs in formulations aimed at clearing respiratory and ear infections.

8. Allergic rhinitis

White Cedar has been used traditionally to support respiratory health, particularly in alleviating symptoms of allergic rhinitis. Its anti-inflammatory and antihistaminic properties may help reduce nasal congestion and sneezing. It is often prepared as a tincture or steam inhalation. Regular use may provide relief for those experiencing seasonal allergies.

9. Lung congestion

White Cedar is used to support respiratory health by helping to clear lung congestion. It contains compounds that may reduce inflammation and mucus production in the airways. Traditionally, it has been applied in herbal formulas to ease breathing and promote healthy lung function. Its expectorant properties aid in removing excess mucus from the respiratory system.

10. Skin infections

White Cedar is used to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It can be applied topically as a salve or infused oil to reduce symptoms of fungal infections, eczema, and bacterial infections. Its essential oils contain compounds that inhibit the growth of pathogens on the skin. This makes it a valuable remedy in herbal medicine for addressing various skin conditions.
