1. Increases blood pressure

Syringa villosa increases blood pressure due to its inherent ability to stimulate the body's sympathetic nervous system.

This is caused by the presence of certain compounds, such as syringin and villoxin, which can activate the alpha-adrenergic receptors in the cardiovascular system.

As a result, it may lead to increased cardiac output, peripheral resistance, and blood pressure.

2. Causes allergic reactions

Syringa villosa causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

The allergenic compounds present in the plant's flowers, leaves, and stems are believed to trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling.

These allergens can be released into the air when the plant is disturbed or its parts are touched, making exposure a common trigger for allergic reactions.

3. Stimulates uterine contractions

Syringa villosa stimulates uterine contractions.

This is likely due to the presence of a compound called luteolin, which has been found to have an oxytocic effect on the uterus. As a result, individuals may experience increased uterine activity, including stronger and more frequent contractions.

This response is thought to be responsible for the traditional use of lilac in folk medicine as a uterotonic agent.

5. Irritates digestive tract

Syringa villosa irritates digestive tract by its potential to cause allergic reactions and inflammation in some individuals.

The plant's volatile oils and flavonoids may stimulate the immune system, leading to an overactive response in sensitive people. This can result in symptoms such as bloating, cramping, and diarrhea.

The exact mechanisms behind this reaction are not fully understood, but it is believed that the body's attempt to eliminate the foreign substance leads to an imbalance of gut bacteria and altered gut motility.

6. Inhibits platelet aggregation

Syringa villosa inhibits platelet aggregation, which may lead to spontaneous bleeding and bruising.

This is due to its ability to block the production of thromboxane A2, a potent platelet aggregator, thereby preventing platelets from sticking together and forming blood clots. Additionally, Syringa villosa's flavonoids and saponins may also contribute to this inhibitory effect by reducing platelet activation and aggregation.

As a result, users may experience increased bleeding tendency, petechiae, and ecchymoses.

7. Enhances risk of bleeding

Syringa villosa enhances risk of bleeding.

This is due to its ability to interact with blood thinners and other medications that affect clotting, such as warfarin and aspirin. Additionally, the plant's flavonoids may also contribute to increased bleeding risk by inhibiting platelet aggregation and affecting the body's natural blood clotting processes.

As a result, individuals who consume Syringa villosa may experience prolonged bleeding following an injury or surgical procedure.

8. Affects kidney function

Syringa villosa affects kidney function due to its high concentration of glycosides, which can reduce urine production and impede waste removal from the body.

The plant's saponins may also cause a decrease in glomerular filtration rate, further compromising kidney function.

These compounds can accumulate in the kidneys, potentially leading to damage or inflammation, making regular monitoring of kidney health essential when using Syringa villosa.

9. Interferes with medication

Syringa villosa interferes with medication by altering the activity of certain enzymes in the body.

The active compounds present in the plant, such as flavonoids and terpenoids, may bind to these enzymes, changing their function and potentially leading to reduced efficacy or increased side effects of medications.

This interaction can occur when Syringa villosa is consumed in large quantities or over a prolonged period, potentially compromising medication effectiveness.

10. Triggers seizures

Syringa villosa triggers seizures due to its high concentration of essential oils, particularly lilaldehyde and lilacfuran.

These volatile compounds can cause excitotoxicity in the brain, leading to abnormal electrical activity and potentially life-threatening seizure episodes.

The exact mechanism is not fully understood, but it is believed that the oils can interfere with normal neuronal function, disrupting the delicate balance of neurotransmitters and leading to a cascade of events that ultimately result in seizures.
