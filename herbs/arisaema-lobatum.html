Arisaema lobatum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Arisaema lobatum has a single, large, deeply divided leaf that typically exhibits several distinct lobes and a prominent petiole. The leaf surface often shows intricate venation patterns that extend through each individual lobe toward the margins. This plant produces a specialized inflorescence consisting of a spathe that wraps around a central spadix. The spadix serves as a visual and olfactory signal to attract specific insect pollinators. These morphological features are characteristic of the Araceae family and help distinguish this species from its relatives.

      Arisaema lobatum is prepared as a topical paste or a decoction after undergoing a rigorous detoxification process to neutralize its inherent calcium oxalate crystals.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Arisaema lobatum can cause severe irritation, swelling, and burning sensations to the skin, eyes, and mucous membranes due to the presence of insoluble calcium oxalate crystals. When these microscopic, needle-shaped crystals come into contact with sensitive tissues, they physically penetrate the cells and trigger an immediate inflammatory response. Ingesting any part of the plant can lead to intense pain in the mouth and throat, potentially causing swelling that restricts breathing. Contact with the sap may also result in localized dermatitis or painful rashes on the hands. Because of these caustic properties, all parts of the plant should be handled with extreme caution.
