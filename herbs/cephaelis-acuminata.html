Cephaelis Blue Poppy

Cephaelis acuminata

Cephaelis Blue Poppy (Cephaelis acuminata) is a flowering plant native to South America, known for its striking blue and violet petals and its use in traditional medicine to treat respiratory conditions such as bronchitis and asthma.

Botanical Identification

Cephaelis Blue Poppy, scientifically known as Cephaelis acuminata, belongs to the Rubiaceae family. It is also called the Blue Poppy or Blue Bitterroot in some regions. This perennial plant is native to the Andes in South America. It features blue or purple flowers, lance-shaped leaves, and a fibrous root system. Synonyms in herbal literature include Cephaelis parviflora and Cephaelis longiflora.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Fabales
  • Order: Fabales
  • Family: Fabaceae
  • Genus: Cephaelis
  • Species: Cephaelis acuminata

Active Compounds

Cephaelis acuminata contains alkaloids, flavonoids, and tannins as its primary bioactive compounds. These compounds work together to exert anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Their synergistic action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in traditional herbal medicine.

Cephaelis Blue Poppy Uses.

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory ailments such as coughs and colds.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and to relieve pain.

Tincture: Used for skin infections and as an antiseptic.

Safety Profile

Cephaelis acuminata may cause gastrointestinal upset and has shown toxicity in high doses. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and lactation due to potential fetal and neonatal risks. Caution is advised in individuals with pre-existing liver or kidney conditions.
