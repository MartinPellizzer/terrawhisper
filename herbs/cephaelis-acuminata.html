Cephaelis Blue Poppy (Cephaelis acuminata)

Cephaelis Blue Poppy (Cephaelis acuminata) is a member of the Fabaceae family, native to India, Nepal, and Bhutan. Traditionally, its leaves, stems, and roots have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its bitter, anti-inflammatory, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in ayurvedic medicine, traditional chinese medicine, and european herbal medicine.

Height

Leaves
Lateral, broadly ovate leaves with upper surface glaucous blue and lower surface pale green, bearing prominent stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Inflorescence is a spike of blue to violet actinomorphic flowers with five sepals and five petals, each petal having a distinct dark blue blotch near the base.
Stems
Cephaelis acuminata exhibits an erect growth habit with an alternate branching pattern, characterized by smooth, glabrous surfaces and prominent, woody nodes.
