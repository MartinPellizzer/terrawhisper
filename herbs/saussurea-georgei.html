Saussurea georgei

What's the taxonomical classification of Saussurea georgei?

Saussurea georgei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Asterales and falls within the family Asteraceae. Finally, its taxonomic identity is completed by its placement in the genus Saussurea, specifically as the species georgei.

Saussurea georgei has medicinal properties that primarily function to alleviate pain, reduce inflammation, and treat various skin ailments. Some practitioners also employ its extracts to address gastrointestinal discomfort and regulate internal circulation. These therapeutic effects are often attributed to the specific phytochemical profile found within its roots and leaves.
