Saussurea georgei

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea georgei has a perennial life cycle characterized by a basal rosette of leaves that are typically lanceolate or elliptical in shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. The plant features a robust stem that rises from the center of the rosette to support a terminal inflorescence composed of numerous small, yellowish or cream-colored flower heads. Each individual floret is tubular and tucked within a structure of involucral bracts that may exhibit specific color patterns or textures. The root system is typically a thick taproot designed to anchor the plant in its high-altitude rocky habitat. These morphological adaptations allow the species to withstand the harsh environmental conditions found in its natural alpine range.
