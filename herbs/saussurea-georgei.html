Saussurea georgei

What's the taxonomical classification of Saussurea georgei?

Saussurea georgei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Asterales and falls within the family Asteraceae. Finally, its taxonomic identity is completed by its placement in the genus Saussurea, specifically as the species georgei.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea georgei has a perennial life cycle characterized by a basal rosette of leaves that are typically lanceolate or elliptical in shape with entire or slightly serrated margins.

The plant features a robust stem that rises from the center of the rosette to support a terminal inflorescence composed of numerous small, yellowish or cream-colored flower heads. Each individual floret is tubular and tucked within a structure of involucral bracts that may exhibit specific color patterns or textures. The root system is typically a thick taproot designed to anchor the plant in its high-altitude rocky habitat.

These morphological adaptations allow the species to withstand the harsh environmental conditions found in its natural alpine range.
