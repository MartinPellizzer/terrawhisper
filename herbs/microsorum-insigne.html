Microsorum insigne

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Microsorum insigne has long, leathery, lanceolate fronds that emerge from a creeping, dark brown rhizome with distinctly scales. The pinnate leaves feature a central rachis that is somewhat thickened and tapers toward the apex. Each pinna is typically entire or slightly lobed with an acute tip and a smooth margin. On the underside of the mature foliage, small, circular sori are arranged in rows along the veins to facilitate spore production. The overall texture of the plant is somewhat coriaceous, providing resilience in its natural humid environment.
