Microsorum insigne

What's the taxonomical classification of Microsorum insigne?

Microsorum insigne belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the family Polypodiaceae, placing it within the genus Microsorum, and it is ultimately identified by its specific species designation, insigne.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Microsorum insigne can cause contact dermatitis, localized skin irritation, or allergic reactions in sensitive individuals upon direct physical contact. The plant contains specific chemical compounds that may trigger itching, redness, or a burning sensation on the skin. Some people might also experience respiratory irritation if the spores or dried plant matter are inhaled. While these effects are generally mild, they are most common among those with pre-existing skin allergies. It is advisable to wear gloves when handling this fern to minimize any potential dermatological response.
