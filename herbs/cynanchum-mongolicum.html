Cynanchum mongolicum

What's the taxonomical classification of Cynanchum mongolicum?

Cynanchum mongolicum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Gentianales. Within this order, the plant is situated in the family Apocynaceae. Its specific taxonomic placement is defined by the genus Cynanchum, with the individual species identified as mongolicum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cynanchum mongolicum has perennial, twining stems that produce opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with entire margins and a smooth texture. The plant features small, tubular, actinomorphic flowers that are typically white or pale greenish-yellow in color. These flowers are clustered in umbel-like cymes and possess a distinctive corona structure characteristic of the Apocynaceae family. The fruit consists of paired, slender follicles that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds equipped with fine tufts of hair. During its growth, the foliage exhibits a soft, somewhat succulent appearance depending on the specific environmental conditions.
