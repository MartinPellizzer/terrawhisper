Machilus leptophyllus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Machilus leptophyllus has lanceolate, leathery leaves that measure up to 15 centimeters in length and are arranged in an alternate pattern along its woody stems. The leaf margins are typically entire and possess a distinctively smooth texture with a glossy upper surface. Its branching structure is characterized by a spreading canopy supported by sturdy, bark-covered branches. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in clusters, eventually developing into small, fleshy drupes. These fruits often transition in color as they mature, providing a key identification feature for the species.
