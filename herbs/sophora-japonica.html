Sophora japonica

What's the taxonomical classification of Sophora japonica?

Sophora japonica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further organized under the order Fabales. It is a prominent representative of the family Fabaceae, falling specifically into the genus Sophora, with its unique designation as the species japonica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sophora japonica has a deciduous growth habit characterized by a spreading crown, pinnately compound leaves with 10 to 25 leaflets, and large, fragrant, pea-like yellow flowers. The tree typically reaches heights of 10 to 15 meters, developing a thick, gray-brown bark that becomes deeply fissured as it matures. Its leaves are arranged alternately along the branches and consist of small, oval-shaped leaflets that provide a dense canopy. During the flowering season, the clusters of blooms emerge from the terminal ends of the branches. Following pollination, the tree produces flat, brown seed pods that eventually dehisce to release small seeds.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to East Asia, specifically spanning across China, Japan, Korea, and Taiwan. It flourishes primarily in temperate regions where it can adapt to diverse soil conditions and climates. Throughout history, human cultivation has expanded its presence to various parts of Europe and North America for ornamental purposes. In these introduced habitats, it is often found in urban parks, botanical gardens, and residential landscapes. The species remains most concentrated in its original subtropical and temperate woodland environments.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Sophora japonica contains medicinal compounds primarily located in its flower buds, bark, and roots. The dried flower buds, commonly known as sophora japonica buds or huai hua, are frequently used in traditional medicine to stop bleeding and clear heat. These buds are rich in rutin, a flavonoid that helps strengthen capillary walls and improve blood circulation. The bark and roots are also utilized for their antioxidant properties and their ability to treat various inflammatory conditions. Practitioners often prepare decoctions from these specific parts to address ailments related to the cardiovascular and respiratory systems.

What are the pharmacological activities of Sophora japonica?

This plant has significant pharmacological activities including anti-cancer, anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, antioxidant, and antitumor properties. Its high concentration of flavonoids like rutin contributes to its ability to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress in the body. These chemical compounds also assist in suppressing inflammatory responses and inhibiting the growth of various harmful bacteria. Furthermore, the plant's bioactive constituents demonstrate strong potential in preventing tumor progression and targeting malignant cell development. Such diverse biological effects make Sophora japonica a subject of intense interest in modern medicinal research.

As discussed in a study, Sophora japonica L. is a medicinal herb that contains bioactive flavonoids such as sophorabioside and sophoricoside. Sophoricoside is a more bioavailable and therapeutically potent form known for anti-inflammatory, anti-cancer, and anti-osteoporotic effects. Sophorabioside is found abundantly in the fruits and small branches of the plant. When sophorabioside is converted into sophoricoside, the resulting extracts demonstrate significant lipoxygenase inhibitory activity. This process suggests enhanced anti-inflammatory potential for products derived from Sophora japonica.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds genistein, daidzein, formononetin, kaempferol, and medicarpin. These specific phytochemicals function primarily as isoflavones and flavonoids that provide various biological benefits. Genistein and daidzein are well-known phytoestrogens that can interact with estrogen receptors in the human body. Formonoetnin and kaempferol contribute significant antioxidant properties to the plant's chemical profile. Additionally, the presence of medicarpin offers specialized phytoalexin activity which helps protect the organism against pathogens.

As discussed in a study, the fruits of Sophora japonica L. contain isoflavonoids including sophoricoside, genistein, orobol, and genistin that act as IL-5 inhibitors. Sophoricoside demonstrated the highest inhibitory effect with 89% inhibition at 12.5 microM, 82% at 6.3 microM, 72% at 3.1 microM, 59% at 1.6 microM, and 24% at 0.8 microM. This compound showed a 50% inhibition (IC50) at a concentration of 1.5 microM, which is more potent than the positive control oxyphenylbutazone at 31.7 microM. Regarding the order of IC50 values, the potency was sophoricoside, followed by orobol at 9.8 microM, genistin at 10.6 microM, and genistein at 51.9 microM.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating hemorrhoids, inflammation, oxidative stress, tumors, and wound healing through its various bioactive compounds. The presence of flavonoids and rutin helps reduce swelling and manage chronic inflammatory responses in the body. Its potent antioxidant properties effectively combat oxidative stress by neutralizing harmful free radicals. Research also suggests that certain extracts may inhibit the growth of specific tumors. Additionally, the plant's antimicrobial and soothing qualities promote faster wound healing and skin repair.

As discussed in a study, Sophora japonica is a medium-sized deciduous tree used in Asia, especially in China, to treat hemorrhoids. The flower buds and fruits, known as Flos Sophorae Immaturus and Fructus Sophorae in China, are the most commonly used parts for this purpose. What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause gastrointestinal distress including nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal pain if consumed. These symptoms typically arise from the plant's chemical constituents when it is accidentally or intentionally eaten. Toxicity can occur if ingested in large amounts, potentially leading to more severe systemic issues. Some individuals may also experience dizziness following ingestion. It is important to avoid consuming any part of the plant to prevent these adverse reactions.
