What's the taxonomical classification of Laportea cuspidata?

Laportea cuspidata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Rosales. This plant is a member of the family Urticaceae, falling under the genus Laportea, and is specifically identified by its species name, cuspidata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Laportea cuspidata has large, heart-shaped leaves with serrated margins and a distinctively pointed tip, growing on long, hollow petioles from a perennial rhizome. The plant features stinging hairs called trichomes that cover the stems and leaf surfaces, acting as a defense mechanism. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically arranged in dense axillary spikes. The stems are often reddish or green and can become quite woody at the base as the plant matures. This species is characterized by its herbaceous growth habit and a sprawling architecture that allows it to colonize forest understories.
