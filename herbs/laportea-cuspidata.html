Laportea cuspidata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Laportea cuspidata has large, heart-shaped leaves with serrated margins and a distinctively pointed tip, growing on long, hollow petioles from a perennial rhizome. The plant features stinging hairs called trichomes that cover the stems and leaf surfaces, acting as a defense mechanism. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically arranged in dense axillary spikes. The stems are often reddish or green and can become quite woody at the base as the plant matures. This species is characterized by its herbaceous growth habit and a sprawling architecture that allows it to colonize forest understories.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of West and Central Africa, spanning from Guinea and Sierra Leone eastward to Uganda and southward to Angola. It thrives primarily within the humid environments of lowland rainforests and moist riverine forests. The species is most frequently found in shaded, damp habitats where high humidity and consistent rainfall are present. Its distribution follows the equatorial belt where dense canopy cover provides the necessary protection from direct sunlight. Local populations are often concentrated along forest edges and near water sources throughout these African territories.
