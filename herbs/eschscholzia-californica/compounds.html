California Poppy (Eschscholzia californica) Compounds

The primary compounds of California poppy include alkaloids, which have sedative and analgesic properties. Essential oils contribute to its calming effects. Flavonoids and phenolic acids provide antioxidant benefits. Coumarin and terpenoids may support anti-inflammatory actions. Glycosides, saponins, and anthocyanins also contribute to its medicinal profile.

California Poppy (Eschscholzia californica) Monograph

1. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a key compound found in California Poppy (Eschscholzia californica). It contributes to the plant's sedative and analgesic effects, commonly used in herbal medicine for mild pain relief and relaxation.

2. Essential oil

The essential oil of California Poppy (Eschscholzia californica) contains bioactive compounds like sesquiterpenes and alkaloids. It is used in herbal medicine for its mild sedative and analgesic properties, supporting relaxation and pain relief. The oil is typically extracted through steam distillation from the flowers and leaves.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of compounds found in California Poppy (Eschscholzia californica). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for calming effects and pain relief.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in California Poppy (Eschscholzia californica). It contributes to the plant's sedative and analgesic properties. Coumarin may also support muscle relaxation and has been studied for its potential anti-inflammatory effects. Its presence enhances the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

5. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in California Poppy (Eschscholzia californica). They contribute to the plant's therapeutic effects, including sedative and analgesic properties. These compounds are often responsible for the plant's mild calming influence and are studied for their potential in herbal medicine.

6. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in California Poppy (Eschscholzia californica). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and sedative properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for anxiety and pain relief.

7. Tannin

Tannin is a compound found in California Poppy (Eschscholzia californica). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health. Tannins also help preserve the plant's active components and enhance its therapeutic effects in herbal medicine.

8. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in California Poppy (Eschscholzia californica). It contributes to the plant's mild sedative and analgesic effects. Glycosides in the poppy may help with pain relief and relaxation, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine.

9. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in California Poppy (Eschscholzia californica). It contributes to the plant's bitter taste and has mild surfactant properties. Saponin may support digestive health and is often used in herbal formulations for its gentle cleansing effects.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in California Poppy (Eschscholzia californica). It contributes to the plant's vibrant flower colors and may possess antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These characteristics support its traditional use in herbal medicine for promoting overall health and wellness.
