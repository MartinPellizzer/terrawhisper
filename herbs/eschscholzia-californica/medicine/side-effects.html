10 Possible Side Effects Of Eschscholzia Californica

Eschscholzia californica, also known as California poppy, has some side effects when used improperly, such as drowsiness and headaches, which may be caused by excessive dosing or prolonged use.

These side effects can worsen one's life by impairing daily activities, affecting sleep patterns, and reducing productivity.

In severe cases, it can also lead to increased risk of accidents and injuries, compromising overall well-being.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Eschscholzia californica, also known as California poppy, causes allergic reactions in some individuals due to the presence of sesquiterpenoids and alkaloids such as eschcoline and chelerythrine.

These compounds can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms like hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.

The allergenic properties of Eschscholzia californica are thought to be related to its evolutionary adaptation to defend against insects and other pests, which has led to the development of these potent bioactive compounds.

2. Produces respiratory issues

Eschscholzia californica, also known as California poppy, produces respiratory issues due to its alkaloid content, particularly eschscholtzine.

This compound can cause bronchodilation, leading to increased airway resistance and wheezing in some individuals.

Additionally, the plant's volatile oils may stimulate the respiratory system, potentially inducing asthma-like symptoms or exacerbating existing conditions such as chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).

3. Triggers cardiovascular problems

Eschscholzia californica, also known as California poppy, triggers cardiovascular problems due to its ability to stimulate the heart and blood vessels.

The herb contains a number of compounds that can increase heart rate and blood pressure, which can be problematic for people with pre-existing cardiovascular conditions or those who are sensitive to these effects.

Additionally, the sedative properties of Eschscholzia californica may interact with certain medications used to treat cardiovascular issues, further increasing the risk of adverse reactions.

4. Induces gastrointestinal upset

Eschscholzia californica, also known as California poppy, induces gastrointestinal upset when used.

The plant's alkaloids, including eschscholtzine and chelerythrine, can irritate the stomach lining, causing nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea.

Additionally, the saponins present in Eschscholzia californica may disrupt the gut flora, leading to changes in bowel habits and abdominal discomfort.

5. Increases risk of overdose

Eschscholzia californica, also known as California poppy, increases risk of overdose when used without proper guidance.

This is because it has a sedative effect on the nervous system, which can lead to physical dependence and withdrawal symptoms if consumed excessively or without gradual tapering off.

Furthermore, its euphoric properties may encourage users to take more than the recommended dose, heightening the likelihood of an accidental overdose.

6. Interferes with blood clotting

Eschscholzia californica, also known as California poppy, interferes with blood clotting due to its ability to thin the blood and inhibit platelet aggregation.

This is likely caused by the presence of a compound called eschscholtzine, which has been shown to have anticoagulant properties.

As a result, individuals taking Eschscholzia californica may be at increased risk for excessive bleeding or bruising, particularly if they are already taking blood thinners or have a pre-existing clotting disorder.

7. Disrupts hormone balance

Eschscholzia californica, also known as California poppy, disrupts hormone balance in some individuals due to its potential interaction with estrogen receptors in the body.

The herb's active compounds, including isoquinoline alkaloids and flavonoids, may bind to estrogen receptors, mimicking the effects of estrogen hormones.

This can lead to an imbalance in hormonal levels, particularly in women, potentially causing a range of symptoms including mood changes, hot flashes, and changes in menstrual cycles.

8. Affects liver function tests

Eschscholzia californica, also known as California poppy, affects liver function tests.

The herb's flavonoids and alkaloids may interfere with the liver's ability to process toxins and metabolize medications, leading to changes in liver enzymes. This can result in altered laboratory results, which may be of concern for individuals undergoing liver function tests.

The exact mechanism is not fully understood, but it is thought that the herb's bioactive compounds bind to liver proteins, altering their activity and function.

9. Worsens existing medical conditions

Eschscholzia californica, also known as California Poppy, worsens existing medical conditions due to its potential interactions with certain medications.

The plant's active compounds may amplify the effects of blood thinners, diabetes medications, and sedatives, exacerbating conditions such as bleeding disorders, hypoglycemia, or respiratory depression.

Additionally, California Poppy's antispasmodic properties can worsen symptoms in individuals with irritable bowel syndrome (IBS) or other gastrointestinal conditions, leading to increased discomfort and distress.

10. Causes skin rashes

Eschscholzia californica, also known as California poppy, causes skin rashes in some individuals.

This is likely due to the plant's chemical composition, which includes alkaloids and glycosides that can stimulate an allergic response or irritate the skin.

The rash may occur due to direct contact with the plant or ingestion of its dried flowers or leaves, resulting in a reaction that can range from mild redness to more severe inflammation.
