Phedimus kamtschaticus

What's the taxonomical classification of Phedimus kamtschaticus?

Phedimus kamtschaticus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Saxifragales and belongs to the family Crassulaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by the genus Phedimus and the species kamtschaticus.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Phedimus kamtschaticus can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, allergic skin reactions, and potential neurological impairment if ingested or handled improperly. The plant contains specific alkaloids that irritate the digestive tract, leading to nausea and intense cramping. Contact with the leaves or stems may trigger contact dermatitis, resulting in redness, itching, or blistering on sensitive skin. Some individuals may also experience dizziness or lightheadedness following accidental exposure. Due to these unpredictable toxicities, careful avoidance of the plant is recommended for all users.
