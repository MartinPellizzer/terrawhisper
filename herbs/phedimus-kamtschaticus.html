Phedimus kamtschaticus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phedimus kamtschaticus has a rhizomatous perennial habit characterized by basal leaves that are long, narrow, and lanceolate in shape. The foliage typically features prominent parallel venation and smooth margins, often appearing in dense clusters near the ground. During the flowering stage, the plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge on slender, upright scapes. These inflorescences are generally arranged in compact clusters or spikes. The overall structure is relatively low-growing, allowing it to thrive in moist, shaded forest environments.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Phedimus kamtschaticus can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, allergic skin reactions, and potential neurological impairment if ingested or handled improperly. Some individuals may also experience dizziness or lightheadedness following accidental exposure. Due to these unpredictable toxicities, careful avoidance of the plant is recommended for all users.
