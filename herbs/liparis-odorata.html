Liparis odorata

What's the taxonomical classification of Liparis odorata?

Liparis odorata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Asparagales. As a member of the family Orchidaceae, this plant is categorized under the genus Liparis, eventually reaching its specific designation as the species odorata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Liparis odorata has a terrestrial habit characterized by slender, fleshy rhizomes that produce several narrow, lanceolate leaves arranged in a basal rosette.

These leaves are typically bright green and can reach lengths of several centimeters, providing a lush base for the flowering stem. A single, upright scape emerges from the center of the foliage, bearing a loose raceme of small, fragrant flowers. Each flower features a three-lobed lip that is often more pronounced than the other perianth segments.

The blossoms emit a distinct scent that helps attract specific pollinators within its native habitat.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Liparis odorata can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or digestive distress if ingested or handled improperly. While specific clinical studies on humans are limited, many plants in the orchid family contain secondary metabolites that may trigger contact dermatitis upon touch.
