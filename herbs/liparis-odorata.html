Liparis odorata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Liparis odorata has a terrestrial habit characterized by slender, fleshy rhizomes that produce several narrow, lanceolate leaves arranged in a basal rosette. These leaves are typically bright green and can reach lengths of several centimeters, providing a lush base for the flowering stem. A single, upright scape emerges from the center of the foliage, bearing a loose raceme of small, fragrant flowers. Each flower features a three-lobed lip that is often more pronounced than the other perianth segments. The blossoms emit a distinct scent that helps attract specific pollinators within its native habitat.
