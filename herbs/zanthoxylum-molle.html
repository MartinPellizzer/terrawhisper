Zanthoxylum molle

What's the taxonomical classification of Zanthoxylum molle?

Zanthoxylum molle belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Sapindales. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the family Rutaceae, placing it within the genus Zanthoxylum, ultimately identifying it as the specific species Zanthoxylum molle.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Zanthoxylum molle has pinnately compound leaves with 7 to 15 leaflets that are elliptical or lanceolate in shape, serrated margins, and an alternate arrangement on woody stems. The tree typically reaches heights of 15 to 25 meters and features a grayish, fissured bark as it matures. Small, inconspicuous flowers bloom in panicles, eventually developing into fleshy, globose drupes that turn red or black when ripe. The wood is characterized by a dense structure and a distinct aromatic quality. Its overall growth habit is upright and spreading, providing a dense canopy of foliage.
