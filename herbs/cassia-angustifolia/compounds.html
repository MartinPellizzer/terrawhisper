Ceylon Cassia (Cassia angustifolia) Compounds

Ceylon Cassia contains several primary active compounds, including flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties. Tannins contribute to its astringent effects, while coumarin is known for its anticoagulant properties. Phenolic acids and terpenoids also play roles in its medicinal effects, supporting its use in traditional herbal medicine.

Ceylon Cassia (Cassia angustifolia) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Ceylon Cassia (Cassia angustifolia). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and skin health. These compounds help modulate cellular processes and may enhance the plant's therapeutic effects.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Ceylon Cassia (Cassia angustifolia). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and wound-healing effects. Tannins in Ceylon Cassia help reduce swelling and promote tissue repair, making it valuable in herbal treatments.
