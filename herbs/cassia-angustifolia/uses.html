Ceylon Cassia (Cassia angustifolia) Uses

Ceylon Cassia is used to treat various skin conditions such as infections, itching, irritation, eczema, and acne. It helps reduce inflammation and combat fungal infections. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties make it effective for soothing and healing inflamed or infected skin.

Ceylon Cassia (Cassia angustifolia) Monograph

1. Skin infection

Ceylon Cassia is used topically to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and bacterial growth on the skin. The compound esculetin in Cassia angustifolia has been shown to inhibit pathogens causing infections. It is commonly applied as a poultice or infused oil for localized treatment.

2. Itching

Ceylon Cassia is traditionally used to alleviate itching due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. It helps soothe skin irritation and reduce allergic reactions. The bark and leaves are often prepared as a paste or decoction for topical application. It is particularly effective for conditions like eczema and insect bites.

3. Skin irritation

Ceylon Cassia is used topically to alleviate skin irritation due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and itching associated with conditions like eczema and psoriasis. Its mild nature makes it suitable for sensitive skin when applied as a poultice or infused oil.

4. Skin infections

Ceylon Cassia is used topically to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and bacterial growth on the skin. Often applied as a paste or infusion, it is effective for conditions like eczema and minor fungal infections. Its natural compounds support skin healing and prevent further infection.

5. Eczema

Ceylon Cassia is used topically for eczema due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce skin irritation and redness. The bark and leaves are often made into pastes or oils for direct application. It is valued for its soothing effect on inflamed skin.

6. Inflammatory skin conditions

Ceylon Cassia is used topically to treat inflammatory skin conditions due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and infection in conditions like eczema and psoriasis. The active compounds in the bark provide soothing and healing effects on irritated skin.

7. Skin inflammation

Ceylon Cassia is used to treat skin inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and irritation. External application of its paste or decoction can soothe conditions like eczema and dermatitis. It is valued for its natural healing effects on sensitive skin.

8. Acne

Ceylon Cassia is used topically to treat acne due to its antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties. The active compound, escin, helps reduce skin inflammation and combat acne-causing bacteria. It is often applied as a paste or extract to clear blemishes and prevent future breakouts.

9. Fungal infection

Ceylon Cassia is used to treat fungal infections due to its antimicrobial and antifungal properties. The active compounds in the plant help inhibit the growth of fungi, making it effective for conditions like athlete's foot and ringworm. It is often applied topically in the form of creams or powders to target localized infections.

10. Skin itching

Ceylon Cassia is traditionally used to relieve skin itching due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistaminic properties. It helps reduce irritation and redness associated with conditions like eczema and dermatitis. The bark and leaves are often prepared as a paste or decoction for topical application.
