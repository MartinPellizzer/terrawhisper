10 Medicinal Constituents Of Liquidambar Orientalis (Oriental Sweetgum)

Liquidambar orientalis has active constituents such as resin, triterpenoids, and flavonoids.

These compounds are credited with its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties. The constituents can improve lives by reducing pain and inflammation, preventing infections, and protecting against cell damage.

For example, the antimicrobial properties can help prevent wound infections, while the antioxidant properties can help prevent chronic diseases such as cancer and heart disease.

This article explains in details the 10 best active constituents of Liquidambar orientalis .

1. Berberine

Liquidambar orientalis berberine is a natural compound extracted from its bark.

Berberine has been used in traditional medicine for centuries, particularly in China and Europe, to treat various health issues such as high cholesterol, diabetes, and cardiovascular diseases.

It is rich in antibacterial, antiviral, and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a valuable ingredient in herbal remedies.

Liquidambar orientalis, also known as Oriental Sweetgum, Quercetin is a flavonoid compound extracted from the bark and leaves of this deciduous tree.

It is a potent antioxidant with various biological activities, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiviral properties.

Quercetin has been traditionally used in traditional medicine to treat various health conditions, such as respiratory problems, wounds, and skin diseases.

3. Kaempferol

Liquidambar orientalis kaempferol is a flavonoid glycoside isolated from the bark of Liquidambar orientalis Mill., a deciduous tree native to southwestern Asia.
