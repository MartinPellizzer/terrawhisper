Vaccinium Vitis-Idaea Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Vaccinium vitis-idaea, commonly known as whortleberry or cloudberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the northern regions of the world, known for its edible berries and medicinal properties.

This herb has been traditionally used for its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting immune function and digestive health.

The bioactive constituents responsible for its therapeutic actions include polyphenols, flavonoids, and anthocyanins, which contribute to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

Herbal preparations such as infusions, tinctures, and capsules can be made from the dried berries to harness its beneficial effects.
