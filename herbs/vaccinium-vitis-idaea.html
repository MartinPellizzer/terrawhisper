Vaccinium Vitis-Idaea Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Vaccinium vitis-idaea, commonly known as whortleberry or cowberry, is a low-growing shrub native to northern regions, known for its small, dark blue berries and medicinal properties.

This herb has been traditionally used for its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting immune function and digestive health.

The bioactive constituents include anthocyanins, flavonoids, and tannins, which contribute to its therapeutic actions.

Herbal preparations such as infusions, tinctures, and decoctions can be made from the leaves and berries to harness its beneficial properties.
