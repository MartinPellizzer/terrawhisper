Yellow Hawthorn (Crataegus Flavescens)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Crataegus flavescens.

Yellow hawthorn, scientifically known as Crataegus flavescens, is a small, flowering shrub native to the eastern United States, prized for its medicinal properties and historical significance. As a traditional medicinal herb, it has been used for centuries to support heart health, reduce anxiety, and improve sleep due to its rich content of flavonoids and antioxidants. Historically, Native American tribes utilized yellow hawthorn to treat digestive issues, respiratory conditions, and as a calming tonic, while early European settlers also recognized its therapeutic value. In modern wellness practices, it is often incorporated into herbal supplements aimed at cardiovascular support and stress relief, thanks to its unique compounds like ursolic acid and oligomeric proanthocyanidins. Its distinctive, slightly tart flavor and historical role in both indigenous and herbal medicine make it a rare and valuable plant in the realm of natural remedies.
