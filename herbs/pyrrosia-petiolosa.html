Pyrrosia petiolosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pyrrosia petiolosa has small, opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are attached to the stem by distinct, relatively long petioles. These leaves typically feature entire or slightly serrated margins and a somewhat fleshy texture. The plant is characterized by a low-growing, herbaceous habit with slender stems that often spread across the ground. Its inflorescence consists of tiny, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. The overall structure is compact, adapted to the specific ecological niches of its native habitat.
