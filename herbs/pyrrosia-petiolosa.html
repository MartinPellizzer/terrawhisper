Pyrrosia petiolosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Pyrrosia petiolosa?

Pyrrosia petiolosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. It is further categorized under the family Polypodiaceae, which contains the genus Pyrrosia, ultimately identifying this specific organism as the species Pyrrosia petiolosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pyrrosia petiolosa has small, opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are attached to the stem by distinct, relatively long petioles. The plant is characterized by a low-growing, herbaceous habit with slender stems that often spread across the ground. Its inflorescence consists of tiny, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. The overall structure is compact, adapted to the specific ecological niches of its native habitat.

What are the pharmacological activities of Pyrrosia petiolosa?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological activities including anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antioxidant properties. Scientific investigations reveal that its extracts can effectively inhibit various inflammatory mediators in biological systems. Furthermore, the plant exhibits potent antimicrobial effects against a range of pathogenic bacteria. Its high concentration of bioactive compounds also provides strong antioxidant protection by neutralizing harmful free radicals. These diverse medicinal attributes suggest that the species holds considerable potential for pharmaceutical development.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", Pyrrosia petiolosas ethanol extract and fractions demonstrate various pharmacological activities. The petroleum ether and N-butanol fractions inhibited all tested microorganisms with a maximum inhibition zone of 15.25±0.35 mm. The ethanol and aqueous fractions inhibited 8 out of 13 (61.5%) of the tested microorganisms, with MIC values ranging from 1.25 to 10.00 mg/mL. Regarding anti-inflammatory activity, the ethanol extract at 5.0 and 10.0 mg/kg showed significant results against mouse ear swelling, reaching a maximum inhibition rate as high as 67%. Furthermore, the ethanol extract and fractions exhibited non-toxic or low toxic activity against lung cancer cell lines A549 and mouse spleen cells.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the unique medicinal compound pyrropetioside along with various flavonoids, phenols, anthraquinones, and saponins. These bioactive constituents contribute to the diverse pharmacological profile of the species. The presence of phenols and flavonoids provides significant antioxidant properties to the plant material. Furthermore, the anthraquinones and saponins play critical roles in its potential therapeutic applications. These combined chemical elements make the plant a subject of interest for medicinal research.

According to a study published by "Journal of Asian natural products research", the plant Pyrrosia petiolosa contains a new flavone diglycoside named pyrropetioside, which is specifically 7-O-[6-O-(alpha-L-arabifuranosyl)-beta-D-glucopyranosyl]-gossypetin. In addition to this compound, 13 known compounds, including 3 artifacts, have been isolated from Pyrrosia petiolosa. The structures of these substances were elucidated using chemical and spectroscopic methods such as IR, MS, 1D, and 2D NMR techniques.
