Michelia macclurei

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Michelia maccluerei has evergreen, elliptical to lanceolate leaves that are dark green, leathery, and possess finely serrated margins. The foliage grows in an alternate pattern along woody stems that form a dense, spreading canopy. Its flowers are creamy white or pale yellow, featuring several large, waxy petals that emit a strong fragrance. These blossoms typically emerge from terminal positions and transition into aggregate fruits composed of many small follicles. Each follicle eventually splits open to reveal bright red or orange seeds that are often covered in a fleshy coating.

Its distribution is closely tied to warm climates that provide consistent moisture throughout the year.       What are the herbal preparations of Michelia macclurei?

      Michelia macclurei is prepared as an infused tea from its dried flowers, a decoction made from the bark, or an essential oil extracted through steam distillation.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Michelia macclurei can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. While the plant is primarily grown for its fragrant flowers, direct contact with the sap or foliage may trigger redness or itching in sensitive individuals. Ingesting any part of the plant can lead to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal discomfort. There is limited clinical data regarding long-term toxicity, so caution is advised when handling the specimen. Always wash your hands thoroughly after gardening with this species to minimize potential reactions.
