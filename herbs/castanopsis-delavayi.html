Castanopsis delavayi

What's the taxonomical classification of Castanopsis delavayi?

Castanopsis delaavay belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further follows the order Fagales and is a member of the family Fagaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Castanopsis and is specifically classified as the species delaavay.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Castanopsis delavayi contains bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, tannins, and saponins that contribute to its therapeutic properties. These chemical constituents are primarily concentrated within the leaves and bark of the plant. Scientific studies suggest that these compounds possess significant antioxidant, antimicrobial, and anti-inflammatory activities. Researchers often investigate these specific phytochemicals for their potential in developing new pharmacological treatments. The complex mixture of these organic compounds makes the species a subject of interest in ethnopharmacological research.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, and various inflammatory conditions. The extracts from its leaves and bark are often applied topically to promote healing in damaged tissues. It is also frequently utilized in traditional medicine to manage symptoms of digestive ailments and respiratory distress. Some practitioners employ the plant to alleviate localized swelling and pain caused by minor injuries. This botanical resource plays a significant role in managing diverse dermatological issues within specific regional healing practices.
