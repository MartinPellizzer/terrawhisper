Mangifera persiciforma

What's the taxonomical classification of Mangifera persiciforma?

Mangifera persiciforma belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Sapindaales. This plant is a member of the family Anacardiaceae, which places it within the genus Mangifera, and it is specifically identified by the species name persiciforma.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Mangifera persiciforma has lanceolate leaves with an acuminate apex, a woody stem with grayish bark, and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles. The foliage is typically dark green and leathery, providing a dense canopy characteristic of many tropical species. Its branching pattern is irregular, allowing for a spreading growth habit as the plant matures. The reproductive structures consist of tiny petals and stamens that emerge from the terminal ends of the branches. Small, fleshy drupes develop from the fertilized flowers, eventually ripening into the plant's primary fruit.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires well-draining sandy loam soil with a pH between 5.5 and 7.5 and consistent exposure to full sunlight for optimal fruit production.

Growers must ensure the irrigation system provides steady moisture during the flowering stage while allowing the root zone to dry slightly between waterings. Regular fertilization with a balanced nitrogen-phosphorus-potassium ratio supports healthy canopy development and heavy yields. Pruning the branches annually is necessary to maintain airflow and prevent the spread of fungal pathogens.

Careful monitoring for pests like fruit flies is essential to protect the developing harvest.
