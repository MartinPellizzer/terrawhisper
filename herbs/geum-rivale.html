Geum rivale

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Geum rivale has upright, hairy stems that reach heights of up to 60 centimeters and bear nodding, pinkish-purple flowers with five petals and yellow centers. The leaves are primarily basal and are deeply divided into several narrow, lanceolate lobes. Stem leaves are smaller and alternate, often becoming less complex as they move up the plant. The plant produces small, dry achenes that feature hooked styles designed for dispersal by animals. This herbaceous perennial typically grows in clumps within moist, shady habitats.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to much of Europe and parts of temperate Asia, extending from the British Isles through central Europe to western Siberia. Its range covers a vast expanse of the northern hemisphere where damp, shaded environments are prevalent. Within these regions, it is frequently found in moist woodlands, riverbanks, and marshy meadows. Populations are most dense in areas with consistent soil moisture and cool temperatures. This specific distribution pattern reflects the species' preference for temperate, riparian habitats.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Geum rivale targets various enzymes and receptors through its high concentrations of tannins, salicylic acid, and essential oils to exert its medicinal effects. The tannins within the plant interact with proteins in the gastrointestinal tract to precipitate them, which helps reduce inflammation and provides astringent properties. Salicylic acid derivatives act on cyclooxygenase enzymes to inhibit the synthesis of pro-inflammatory prostaglandins, thereby reducing pain and fever. Additionally, certain volatile compounds in the plant may modulate neurotransmitter pathways or interact with cellular signaling proteins to provide mild sedative effects. These diverse molecular interactions contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating digestive issues and inflammatory conditions.

What are the therapeutic actions of Geum rivale?

Geum rivale has therapeutic properties characterized by its bitter, astringent, stomachic, and diaphoretic actions. The bitter components of the plant stimulate the appetite and improve digestive secretions. Its astringent qualities help to tone tissues and reduce excessive fluid discharge. As a stomachic, it supports overall gastrointestinal function and aids in digestion. Finally, its diaphoretic effect promotes sweating to help regulate body temperature during fevers.

      What side effects this plant can have?

      This plant can cause skin irritation or allergic contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals due to its chemical constituents. While it is not considered highly toxic, direct contact with the stems or leaves may trigger redness, itching, or a rash on the skin. There is limited scientific data regarding the systemic effects of ingestion, so it should be avoided if consumed. People with known sensitivities to the Rosaceae family should exercise extra caution when handling this species.
