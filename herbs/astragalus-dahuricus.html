Astragalus dahuricus

What's the taxonomical classification of Astragalus dahuricus?

Astragalus dahuricus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, positioning it within the order Fabales. As a member of the family Fabaceae, this plant is categorized under the genus Astragalus, with its specific biological identity defined by the species name dahuricus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Astragalus dahuricus has a perennial growth habit characterized by erect, branched stems that typically reach heights of 20 to 50 centimeters. The leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets arranged along a central axis. Small, papilionaceous flowers emerge in clusters from the leaf axils, displaying shades of pale purple or pinkish hues. The plant features a deep taproot system that aids in nutrient absorption and stability within its native soil. These morphological traits allow the species to thrive in specific grassland and steppe environments.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Astragalus dahuricus targets proteins involved in inflammatory signaling, immune modulation, and antioxidant defense, most notably tumor necrosis factor-alpha, interleukin-6, and various peroxisome proliferator-activated receptors. Its bioactive compounds like astragalosides interact with the NF-kappaB pathway to suppress excessive inflammatory responses in various tissues. These molecules also modulate the expression of antioxidant enzymes such as superoxide dismutase to reduce oxidative stress at a cellular level. Furthermore, the plant affects signaling proteins in the PI3K/Akt pathway, which helps regulate cell survival and apoptosis. Through these diverse interactions, the plant exerts its multi-target therapeutic effects on the immune and cardiovascular systems.

What are the therapeutic actions of Astragalus dahuricus?

Astragalus dahuricus has diverse therapeutic actions including properties as an adaptogen, immunomodulator, tonic, diaphoretic, anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and astringent.
