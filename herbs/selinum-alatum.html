What is Selinum alatum?

Selinum alatum is an annual or biennial plant belonging to the family Apiaceae, native to Europe.

It has a taproot and erect stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, bearing compound umbels of small, white or pinkish flowers. The leaves are pinnate with 5-15 pairs of leaflets, and are alternate on the stem. The plant's fruit is a schizocarp, consisting of two mericarps that separate at maturity.

The plant's habitat includes dry to moist meadows, grasslands, and roadsides.
