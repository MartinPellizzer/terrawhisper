What's the taxonomical classification of Euphorbia sapinii?

Euphorbia sapiini belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It is further categorized under the order Malpighiales and the family Euphorbiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Euphorbia, and its specific species epithet, sapiini.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Euphorbia sapinii has upright, unbranched stems that reach heights of up to 50 centimeters and are topped by terminal clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers called cyathia. The leaves are lanceolate to linear in shape, possessing smooth margins and an alternating arrangement along the slender stalks. These leaves are typically bright green and can vary in size depending on their position on the plant. During the flowering period, the plant displays a characteristic yellowish hue from its specialized bracts. The overall structure is herbaceous and relatively delicate compared to other members of the Euphorbia genus.
