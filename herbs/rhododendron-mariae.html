What's the taxonomical classification of Rhododendron mariae?

Rhododendron mariae belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Ericales. It is further categorized under the family Ericaceae, which contains a diverse array of flowering shrubs. Within this family, the plant is situated in the genus Rhododendron, and its specific biological identity is defined by its species designation, mariae.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhododendron mariae has elliptic to oblong leaves that are coriaceous, measuring approximately 3 to 7 centimeters in length, and characterized by a revolute margin. The foliage typically exhibits a dark green upper surface and a lighter, sometimes indumentum-covered underside. This species produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or white in color, growing in terminal clusters. The stems are woody and branched, forming a compact shrubby habit. These morphological traits distinguish it from other members of its genus within its specific high-altitude habitat.

      What side effects this plant can have?

      This plant can cause severe physiological distress including nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, dizziness, confusion, arrhythmia, and seizures. These dangerous symptoms typically arise from the ingestion of grayanotoxins found within the plant tissue. Once toxic compounds enter the bloodstream, they interfere with sodium channels in cell membranes, leading to neurological and cardiovascular instability. The resulting cardiac irregularities like arrhythmia can be life-threatening if left untreated. Furthermore, the central nervous system disruption often manifests as intense confusion or violent seizures.
