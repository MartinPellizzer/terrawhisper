Impatiens clavigera

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Impatiens clavigera has succulent, glandular-pubescent stems and oppositely arranged, ovate leaves with serrated margins and distinct venation. The plant typically reaches a height of approximately 30 to 50 centimeters, characterized by its fleshy and moisture-retaining tissues. Its flowers are zygomorphic and display a complex structure with a prominent nectar spur at the rear. The floral coloration often features a mix of pink, white, or pale violet hues to attract specific pollinators. These flowers eventually develop into elongated, explosive seed capsules that scatter seeds upon contact.

What are the herbal preparations of Impatiens clavigera?

Impatiens clavigera is prepared primarily as a topical infusion, a concentrated decoction, or a diluted tincture for various therapeutic applications. The fresh or dried plant material is often steeped in hot water to create a soothing wash for skin irritations. What side-effects this plant can have?

Impatiens clavigera can cause skin irritation, such as redness, itching, or contact dermatitis, when the plant or its juices come into direct contact with sensitive skin. Some individuals may also experience gastrointestinal distress if any part of the plant is accidentally ingested. While direct toxicity is not extensively documented in humans, the presence of certain chemical compounds in the family can trigger localized allergic reactions. It is generally advised to avoid handling the plant without gloves to prevent these dermatological issues. Monitoring for any signs of swelling or rashes is recommended after exposure.
