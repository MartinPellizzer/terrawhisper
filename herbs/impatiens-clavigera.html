Impatiens clavigera

What's the taxonomical classification of Impatiens clavigera?

Impatiens clavigera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Ericales. This plant is a member of the family Balsaminaceae, specifically falling under the genus Impatiens, with its unique identity defined by the species name clavigera.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, inflammation, wounds, and various types of dermatological irritation. Its leaves and stems contain bioactive compounds that possess significant antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. These natural elements help reduce swelling and soothe painful skin conditions when applied topically. Traditional medicinal practices often utilize the plant to accelerate the healing process of minor cuts and abrasions. By suppressing localized inflammation, it serves as an effective remedy for itchy or irritated skin surfaces.
