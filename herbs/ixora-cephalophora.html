What's the taxonomical classification of Ixora cephalophora?

Ixora cephalophora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Gentianales. This plant is a representative of the family Rubiaceae, which is further categorized under the genus Ixora. Ultimately, its specific biological identity is defined by its species name, cephalophora.

Additionally, its properties are applied topically to soothe skin irritations and wounds. These applications highlight the plant's role in diverse folk medicine traditions.

What are the herbal preparations of Ixora cephalophora?

Ixora cephalophora is prepared as a decoction, a topical paste, or an infusion depending on the specific ailment being treated. The leaves and stems are frequently boiled in water to create a liquid tonic used for internal consumption. For skin conditions or localized inflammation, the plant material is crushed into a thick paste and applied directly to the affected area. Some practitioners also utilize the dried plant parts to brew mild teas meant to soothe digestive issues. These various methods allow for the extraction of active compounds in different concentrations for diverse medicinal purposes.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Ixora cephalophora can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or localized swelling if its sap comes into direct contact with human tissue. While specific toxicological studies on this particular species are limited, most plants in the Ixora genus contain compounds that may trigger sensitivity in certain individuals. Contact with the foliage or stems can lead to redness, itching, or a mild burning sensation on the skin. Ingestion of any part of the plant should be avoided as it may lead to gastrointestinal distress or unknown systemic reactions. Always use gloves when pruning this shrub to minimize the risk of accidental exposure to its natural irritants.
