Ixora cephalophora

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ixora cephalophora has woody, evergreen shrub characteristics defined by elliptic to oblong leaves that are leathery in texture and arranged in opposite pairs along its stems. The foliage typically exhibits a deep green color with smooth margins and a slightly glossy surface. Its most distinctive feature is the dense, globose inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers that cluster tightly at the terminal ends of branches. These individual flowers possess long, slender corolla tubes that flare out into small petals. The plant structure supports these heavy floral heads, which can create a rounded, pom-pom appearance when in full bloom.
