Ixora cephalophora

What's the taxonomical classification of Ixora cephalophora?

Ixora cephalophora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Gentianales. This plant is a representative of the family Rubiaceae, which is further categorized under the genus Ixora. Ultimately, its specific biological identity is defined by its species name, cephalophora.

These specific chemical constituents are studied for their potential antioxidant and antimicrobial activities in traditional medicine. What are the therapeutic actions of Ixora cephalophora?

Ixora cephalophora has potent antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties that are utilized in traditional medicine to treat various ailments. The plant contains bioactive compounds that can effectively inhibit the growth of certain pathogenic bacteria and fungi. These chemical constituents also help reduce swelling and pain in affected tissues by modulating inflammatory responses. Additionally, the high concentration of phenolic compounds assists in neutralizing harmful free radicals within the body. Such therapeutic actions make the plant a significant resource for managing skin infections and systemic oxidative stress.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including skin infections, stomach aches, and inflammatory conditions. The leaves and roots are often prepared into decoctions or poultices to address these specific health issues. In many traditional medicinal practices, it serves as a remedy for digestive upsets and gastrointestinal distress. Additionally, its properties are applied topically to soothe skin irritations and wounds. These applications highlight the plant's role in diverse folk medicine traditions.
