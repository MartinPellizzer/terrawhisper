Indian frankincense

Boswellia serrata

Indian Frankincense (Boswellia serrata) is a small tree native to the dry regions of India, Africa, and the Middle East, known for its resinous sap that has been used for centuries in traditional medicine to treat inflammatory conditions such as arthritis and asthma.

Botanical Identification

Boswellia serrata, commonly known as Indian frankincense, is a small deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent. It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic resinous sap. The plant is a perennial shrub with pinnately compound leaves and yellowish flowers. Synonyms include Salai guggul and guggul in traditional texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Eudicots
  • Order: Burserales
  • Family: Burseraceae
  • Genus: Boswellia
  • Species: Boswellia serrata

Indian Frankincense contains key active compounds such as terpenoids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids. These compounds work synergistically to exert anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects. Their combined action supports the herb's traditional use in herbal medicine.

Preparation Methods

Capsule: Used for digestive issues and inflammatory conditions.

Essential oil: Used for skin conditions and respiratory ailments.

Oil infusion: Used for topical application to reduce inflammation.

Safety Profile

Indian Frankincense (Boswellia serrata) is generally considered safe when used as directed. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It should be avoided during pregnancy and by those with known allergies to the plant. Always consult a healthcare provider before use, especially if taking other medications.
