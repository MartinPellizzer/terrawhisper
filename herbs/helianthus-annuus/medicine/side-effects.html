10 Possible Side Effects Of Helianthus Annuus (Sunflower)

Helianthus annuus has some side effects when used improperly, such as stomach upset, diarrhea, and allergic reactions.

These side effects can be caused by ingesting large amounts or using it for extended periods. For example, if left untreated, stomach upset can lead to dehydration and electrolyte imbalance, impairing daily activities and work performance.

Allergic reactions can cause anaphylaxis, a potentially life-threatening condition.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Helianthus annuus if used imporperly.

Helianthus annuus causes allergic reactions in some individuals due to the presence of sesquiterpene lactones and flavonoids.

These compounds can stimulate an immune response, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling.

Additionally, the allergenic potential is increased by the high levels of pollen and proteins present in the plant, making it a common culprit for seasonal allergies.

These compounds can cause an allergic response, leading to inflammation and discomfort on the skin's surface. Additionally, the oil extracted from Helianthus annuus contains saponins, which can further exacerbate skin irritation by disrupting the skin's natural barrier function.
