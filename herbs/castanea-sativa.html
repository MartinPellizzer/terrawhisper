Sweet Chestnut (Castanea sativa)

Sweet Chestnut (Castanea sativa) is a member of the Fagaceae family, native to Europe, Asia Minor, and North Africa. Traditionally, its fruits, bark, and leaves have been used for decoctions, infusions, and culinary uses.

This herb is particularly valued for its tonic, astringent, and anti-inflammatory actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and traditional chinese medicine.

Perennial herbaceous plant.

Leaves
Lateral veins form prominent stomatal bands on the lower surface, with upper surface gray-green and lower surface pale green.
Flowers
Inflorescences consisting of clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in spikes, with actinomorphic symmetry, each flower having five sepals and five petals, and stamens in multiples of five.
Stems
Stems are erect, woody, with opposite branching, and have a smooth, glabrous surface.
