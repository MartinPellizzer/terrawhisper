What's the taxonomical classification of Ferula fukanensis?

Ferula fukanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the evolutionary lineage of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is situated in the order Apiales. It is a member of the family Apiaceae, which places it among the carrot and parsley family, specifically within the genus Ferula. Its complete scientific designation is defined by its species name, fukanensis.

    This plant contains the specific medicinal compounds fukanedone A, fukanedone B, fukanedone C, fukanedone D, and fukanedone E. These unique chemical constituents are primarily recognized for their bioactive properties within the plant's biological system. Researchers study these specific fukanedo de-type molecules to understand their potential pharmacological applications. The presence of this diverse group of compounds contributes significantly to the plant's overall chemical profile. Detailed analysis of these substances helps scientists determine how they interact with various biological targets.

    As discussed in a study, an 80% aqueous methanol extract of the roots of Ferula fukanensis was used to isolate six medicinal compounds. These substances include five new sesquiterpene phenylpropanoid derivatives known as fukanedone A (1), fukanedone B (2), fukanedone C (3), fukanedone D (4), and fukanedone E (5). Additionally, a novel phenyl-oxo-acetate ester called fukaneketoester A (6) was identified. These compounds demonstrated the ability to inhibit nitric oxide (NO) production and inducible NO synthase (iNOS) gene expression. This inhibitory effect was observed in a murine macrophage-like cell line (RAW 264.7) activated by lipopolysaccharide (LPS) and recombinant mouse interferon-gamma (IFN-gamma).
