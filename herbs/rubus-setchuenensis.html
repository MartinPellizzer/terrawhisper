Rubus setchuenensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rubus setchuenensis has arching or erect stems characterized by a dense covering of prickles and a woody structure that supports compound leaves with three to five serrated leaflets. The leaves are typically dark green with a somewhat rugose texture on the upper surface and lighter undersides. Small, white to pale pink flowers emerge from the terminal or axillary positions, featuring five distinct petals and numerous stamens. These blossoms eventually develop into aggregate fruits that transition from green to a deep red or black color upon maturity. The plant's overall morphology is adapted for sprawling through undergrowth while utilizing its thorns for structural support.

      Rubus setchuenensis is prepared as a decoction, a tincture, or a topical poultice using its various plant parts.
