What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cassiope selaginoides has small, needle-like leaves that are densely packed in whorls of four around its woody, spreading stems. The plant grows as a low-lying, mat-forming subshrub characterized by its compact and spreading habit. During the flowering season, it produces small, bell-shaped, white flowers that hang from the branch tips. The overall structure is adapted to survive in harsh, high-altitude alpine environments.
