Common Agrimony (Agrimonia pilosa): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

What is Common Agrimony (Agrimonia Pilosa)?

Common Agrimony, or Agrimony, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, known for its use in traditional herbal medicine for its astringent, anti-inflammatory, and digestive properties.

Botanical Identity and Classification

Common Agrimony, scientifically known as Agrimony (Agrimonia pilosa), is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its yellow flowers and serrated leaves.

Common Names and Synonyms

Common Agrimony, also known as Agrimony herb, Agrimony grass, and Pilose agrimony, is a plant widely recognized in herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.

Plant Description and Morphology

Common Agrimony (Agrimonia pilosa) is a perennial herb with a branched, upright stem that is hairy, reaching up to 1 meter in height, bearing opposite, pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in terminal clusters.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Common Agrimony (Agrimonia pilosa) is native to temperate regions of Europe, western Asia, and parts of North Africa, typically growing in dry grasslands, rocky soils, and disturbed habitats.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The aerial parts, including the leaves, stems, and flowers, are primarily used medicinally in traditional herbal medicine.

Phytochemical Composition

Common Agrimony (Agrimonia pilosa) contains a variety of phytochemicals, including flavonoids such as quercetin and rutin, tannins, iridoids, and essential oils, which contribute to its traditional use in herbal medicine.

Pharmacological Properties

Common Agrimony (Agrimonia pilosa) exhibits pharmacological properties including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial activities, primarily due to the presence of flavonoids, tannins, and polyphenolic compounds.

Mechanisms of Action

Common Agrimony (Agrimonia pilosa) exerts its medicinal effects primarily through the presence of bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and iridoids, which contribute to its anti-inflammatory, astringent, and antioxidant properties.

Therapeutic Uses and Indications

Common Agrimony is used to treat digestive issues such as indigestion, bloating, and stomach cramps, as well as skin conditions like eczema and wounds, and it is also used for respiratory problems including coughs and bronchitis.

Preparation Methods and Forms

Common Agrimony (Agrimonia pilosa) is typically prepared as a dried herb for use in teas, tinctures, and capsules, with the dried leaves and flowers often harvested during the flowering season and stored in airtight containers to preserve potency.

Dosage and Administration

The typical dosage of Common Agrimony for herbal use ranges from 2 to 4 grams of dried herb per cup of water, prepared as a decoction and consumed three times daily, though specific formulations may vary depending on the intended therapeutic effect.

Safety, Side Effects, and Contraindications

Drug Interactions

Toxicity and Precautions

Processing and Extraction

Storage and Shelf Life
