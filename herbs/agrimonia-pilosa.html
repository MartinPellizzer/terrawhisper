Common Agrimony

Agrimonia pilosa

Common Agrimony (Agrimonia pilosa) is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, known for its traditional use in herbal medicine to support digestive health and alleviate symptoms of mild gastrointestinal discomfort.

Taxonomy

Common Agrimony, or Agrimonia pilosa, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family, classified within the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Rosales, genus Agrimonia, and species Agrimonia pilosa.

Morphology

Common Agrimony (Agrimonia pilosa) is a perennial herb with a branched, upright stem that is hairy, reaching up to 1 meter in height, bearing opposite, pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in terminal clusters.

Distribution

Common Agrimony (Agrimonia pilosa) is native to Europe, western Asia, and parts of northern Africa, with a range extending into the temperate regions of the United States and Canada.

Traditional Uses

Common Agrimony has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat digestive issues, including indigestion, stomach cramps, and diarrhea, due to its astringent and anti-inflammatory properties.

Medicinal Systems

Common Agrimony (Agrimonia pilosa) has been utilized in traditional medicinal systems such as Traditional Chinese Medicine, Ayurveda, and European herbalism for its purported healing properties.

Phytochemistry

The phytochemistry of Common Agrimony (Agrimonia pilosa) includes a variety of bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, phenolic acids, and iridoids, which contribute to its traditional use in herbal medicine for their anti-inflammatory, astringent, and antimicrobial properties.

Pharmacology

Common Agrimony (Agrimonia pilosa) exhibits anti-inflammatory, astringent, and mild sedative properties, making it traditionally used for digestive ailments, skin irritations, and mild anxiety.

Botanical Identification

Agrimonia pilosa, commonly known as common agrimony, belongs to the Rosaceae family. It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and yellow flowers, with a fibrous root system. It is also referred to as agrimony or lesser agrimony in various regional contexts.

Common Agrimony is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to potential risks. Long-term use may cause gastrointestinal discomfort, so it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before use.
