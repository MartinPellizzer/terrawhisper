Common Agrimony

Agrimonia pilosa

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Agrimonia pilosa, commonly known as common agrimony, belongs to the Rosaceae family. It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and yellow flowers, with a fibrous root system. It is also referred to as agrimony or lesser agrimony in various regional contexts.

Active Compounds

Common Agrimony contains key active compounds such as tannins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids. These compounds work synergistically to provide its therapeutic effects, including anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. They contribute to its traditional use in digestive and skin-related ailments.

  • Tannin
  • Flavonoid
  • Phenolic acid

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action
Digestive Diarrhea, gastrointestinal inflammation, gastrointestinal discomfort, gastrointestinal spasms Antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic
Integumentary Skin infections, wound healing, skin irritation Antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, antiseptic
Respiratory Respiratory infections, cough, respiratory tract infections, bacterial infections, mucous membrane irritation Antimicrobial, expectorant, anti-inflammatory

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for digestive issues and as a general tonic.

Decoction: Used for treating gastrointestinal disorders and inflammation.

Tincture: Used for respiratory conditions and as an antiseptic.

Safety Profile

Common Agrimony is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to potential risks. Long-term use may cause gastrointestinal discomfort, so it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before use.
