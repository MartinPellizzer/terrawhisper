Common Agrimony

Agrimonia pilosa

Common Agrimony (Agrimonia pilosa) is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, known for its traditional use in herbal medicine to support digestive health and alleviate symptoms of mild gastrointestinal discomfort.

Taxonomy

Common Agrimony, or Agrimonia pilosa, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family, classified within the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Rosales, genus Agrimonia, and species Agrimonia pilosa.

Morphology

Common Agrimony (Agrimonia pilosa) is a perennial herb with a branched, upright stem that is hairy, reaching up to 1 meter in height, bearing opposite, pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in terminal clusters.

Distribution

Common Agrimony (Agrimonia pilosa) is native to Europe, western Asia, and parts of northern Africa, with a range extending into the temperate regions of the United States and Canada.

Traditional Uses

Common Agrimony has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat digestive issues, including indigestion, stomach cramps, and diarrhea, due to its astringent and anti-inflammatory properties.

Medicinal Systems

Common Agrimony (Agrimonia pilosa) has been utilized in traditional medicinal systems such as Traditional Chinese Medicine, Ayurveda, and European herbalism for its purported healing properties.

Phytochemistry

The phytochemistry of Common Agrimony (Agrimonia pilosa) includes a variety of bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, phenolic acids, and iridoids, which contribute to its traditional use in herbal medicine for their anti-inflammatory, astringent, and antimicrobial properties.

Pharmacology

Common Agrimony (Agrimonia pilosa) exhibits anti-inflammatory, astringent, and mild sedative properties, making it traditionally used for digestive ailments, skin irritations, and mild anxiety.

Medicinal Uses

Common Agrimony is used to treat digestive issues such as indigestion, bloating, and stomach cramps, as well as skin conditions like eczema and wounds, and it is also used for respiratory problems including coughs and bronchitis.

Preparations

Common Agrimony is used to prepare tinctures, teas, and topical salves by infusing its dried leaves and flowers in alcohol, water, or oil, often to support digestive health, reduce inflammation, and alleviate skin irritations.

Safety

Common Agrimony (Agrimonia pilosa) is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses, but it may cause mild gastrointestinal discomfort, such as nausea or stomach upset, in some individuals.

Evidence

Clinical evidence for Common Agrimony (Agrimonia pilosa) is limited, with most studies focusing on its traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and anti-inflammatory purposes, though rigorous scientific validation remains lacking.

Cultivation

Common Agrimony (Agrimonia pilosa) is typically cultivated in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade, preferring temperate climates and requiring minimal water once established.

Processing

Common Agrimony (Agrimonia pilosa) is typically processed by drying the entire plant, including the stems, leaves, and flowers, to preserve its medicinal properties and enhance its potency for use in herbal remedies.

Identification

Common Agrimony (Agrimonia pilosa) is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its upright stems, serrated leaves, and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Sustainability

Common Agrimony (Agrimonia pilosa) is sustainably harvested and cultivated in various regions, with efforts to maintain ecological balance and ensure its availability for future generations.

Related

Common Agrimony (Agrimonia pilosa) is closely related to other species within the Rosaceae family, including Agrimonia eupatoria and Agrimonia sylvatica, which share similar medicinal properties and ecological roles.
