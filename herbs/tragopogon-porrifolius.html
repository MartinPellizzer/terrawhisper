Tragopogon porrifolius

What's the taxonomical classification of Tragopogon porrifolius?

Tragopogon porrifolius belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant falls under the order Asterales. It is further categorized into the family Asteraceae, which is a diverse group of flowering plants. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Tragopogon, and its specific identification is completed by its species name, porrifolius.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tragopogon porrifolius has linear, grass-like leaves that are bright green, basal in arrangement, and typically measure several millimeters in width. These leaves are continuous throughout the plant and lack distinct petioles. The inflorescence consists of large, yellow, dandelion-like flower heads that bloom on long, leafless stalks. Each flower head contains numerous individual florets that eventually develop into a cluster of many small, bristly achenes. The seeds are equipped with a pappus, which facilitates dispersal by wind.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the Mediterranean region, specifically spanning across parts of Southern Europe, North Africa, and Western Asia. It thrives predominantly in coastal areas and rocky terrains where the climate remains relatively mild. Within these habitats, it is often found growing in open grasslands, scrublands, and along the edges of olive groves. Its distribution is closely tied to the typical Mediterranean weather patterns characterized by wet winters and dry summers. This species is also frequently observed in disturbed soils and roadside environments throughout its natural range.

What are the pharmacological activities of Tragopogon porrifolius?

This plant has antimicrobial, antioxidant, cytotoxicity, α-amylase inhibition, and α-glucosidase inhibitory activities. Its antioxidant properties help mitigate oxidative stress by neutralizing free radicals within biological systems. The plant also demonstrates antimicrobial effectiveness against various pathogens and shows significant cytotoxicity that may be useful in cancer research. Furthermore, its ability to inhibit both α-amylase and α-glucosidase enzymes suggests potential applications in managing postprandial blood glucose levels. These diverse pharmacological profiles indicate that Tragopogon porrifolius contains bioactive compounds with significant therapeutic potential.

As discussed in a study, Tragopogon porrifolius extract contains 29 compounds and exhibits a high level of antioxidant activity. The plant shows higher alpha-glucosidase inhibitory activity and strong alpha-amylase inhibition activity compared to the reference drug acarbose. Its ethanolic extract demonstrates antimicrobial activity within the concentration range of 0.039-2.5 mg/ml. Additionally, Tragopogon porrifolius shows a significant anticancer effect on MDA-MB-231 breast cancer cells with an IC50 value of 0.0625 mg/mL. These findings suggest the plant has promising antioxidant, antimicrobial, cytotoxic, and enzyme inhibition activities.
