Medicinal Parts of evodia rutaecarpa

Evodia rutaecarpa fruit and seed are the primary medicinal parts used in traditional Chinese medicine, known for their warming and dispersing properties.

The fruit, which is more commonly used, is typically dried and ground into a powder or decocted in water to make a medicinal infusion.

The seed, though less frequently used, is also dried and can be incorporated into similar preparations.

The main herbal preparations made from these parts include decoctions, powders, and tinctures, often used to treat conditions such as colds, digestive issues, and liver disorders.
