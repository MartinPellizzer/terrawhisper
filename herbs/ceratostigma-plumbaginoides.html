Ceratostigma plumbaginoides

What's the taxonomical classification of Ceratostigma plumbaginoides?

Ceratostigma plumbaginoides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Caryophyllales. Further taxonomic refinement identifies it as a member of the family Plumbaginaceae, specifically within the genus Ceratostigma, and is ultimately identified by its species name, plumbaginoides.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ceratostigma plumbaginoides has low-growing, spreading stems that produce ovate, dark green leaves and small, tubular, five-lobed flowers ranging from deep blue to violet.

These perennial subshrubs typically reach a height of about 12 inches while spreading horizontally through a dense network of rhizomes. The foliage often transitions to vibrant shades of red or bronze during the autumn months. Small, bell-shaped blossoms emerge from the leaf axils during the late summer to early fall period.

The plant's structure remains compact and mounding, making it ideal for groundcover applications.
