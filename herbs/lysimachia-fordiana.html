Lysimachia fordiana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lysimachia fordiana has erect, unbranched stems that reach heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and bear opposite, lanceolate leaves with entire margins. The foliage is typically bright green and lacks petioles, attaching directly to the stem nodes. Small, yellow, star-shaped flowers emerge from the leaf axils in terminal clusters. Each blossom consists of five distinct petals that surround a central cluster of stamens. The plant develops via a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread through moist soil.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Lysimachia fordiana can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, or gastrointestinal distress if ingested. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, many plants in the Primulaceae family contain compounds that may trigger hypersensitivity in sensitive individuals. Direct contact with the leaves or stems might result in localized redness, itching, or a mild rash. If consumed accidentally, the plant could potentially lead to nausea or stomach upset. Users should exercise caution and avoid skin contact to prevent any adverse reactions.
