Taraxacum Dandelion

Taraxacum mongolicum

Taraxacum Dandelion (Taraxacum mongolicum) is a member of the Compositae family, traditionally used in various cultural and regional practices. The plant is commonly utilized for its potential roles in treating inflammation, supporting digestive health, and exhibiting diuretic properties. Parts such as the leaves, roots, and flowers are typically harvested for preparation into teas, tinctures, or extracts.

Botanical Identification

Taraxacum mongolicum is a perennial herb belonging to the Asteraceae family. Commonly known as the dandelion, it is native to Asia and has various regional names. The plant features a thick root, basal leaves, and yellow flowers. It is also referred to by several synonyms in traditional herbal texts.

Taraxacum Dandelion Identification.

Active Compounds

The core entity "Taraxacum Dandelion (Taraxacum mongolicum)" contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and coumarins. These compounds work synergistically to support detoxification, reduce inflammation, and enhance immune function. Their combined action contributes to the herb's therapeutic value in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Phenolic acid
  • Coumarin

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for respiratory and digestive issues due to its anti-inflammatory properties.

Infusion: Used for mild digestive issues and as a diuretic.

Poultice: Used for skin conditions and inflammation due to its soothing properties.

Safety Profile

Taraxacum mongolicum is generally safe when used in moderate amounts. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated for pregnant women and those with gallbladder issues due to its potential to stimulate bile production.
