Podocarpus nagi

What's the taxonomical classification of Podocarpus nagi?

Podocarpus nagi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Pinidae, placing it within the order Pinales. Further taxonomic refinement identifies it as a member of the family Podocarpaceae and the genus Podocarpus, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species nagi.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Podocarpus nagi has evergreen, lanceolate leaves that measure approximately 5 to 15 centimeters in length and exhibit a leathery texture with parallel venation.

The tree typically grows into a medium-sized woody shrub or small tree characterized by a spreading crown and dark green foliage. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous male cones and fleshy, berry-like female receptacles that turn reddish or purple when mature. The bark is generally smooth or slightly fissured depending on the age of the specimen.

These morphological features allow the species to thrive in the shaded understories of subtropical forest environments.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Podocarpus nagi can cause skin irritation and allergic reactions such as contact dermatitis if the plant's sap or foliage touches sensitive skin. Some individuals may experience redness, itching, or swelling upon physical contact with the plant. If ingested, the seeds and foliage could potentially lead to gastrointestinal distress, including nausea or vomiting. While specific toxicity levels are not extensively documented for humans, caution is advised when handling any part of this species. It is best to wear gloves to prevent any unexpected dermatological responses.
