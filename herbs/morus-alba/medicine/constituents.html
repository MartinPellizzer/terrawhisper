10 Medicinal Constituents Of Morus Alba (Mulberry)

Morus alba has active constituents such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and anthocyanins.

These constituents possess anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and cardiovascular-protective properties. They can improve cardiovascular health by reducing triglycerides and LDL cholesterol levels, thus lowering the risk of heart disease. Additionally, flavonoids can enhance cognitive function and prevent age-related memory decline.

Overall, the medicinal properties of mulberry make it a valuable plant for promoting overall well-being and improving quality of life.

1. Anthocyanins

Morus alba anthocyanins is a type of powerful antioxidant polyphenol found in the fruit and leaves of the mulberry tree.

These bioactive compounds are responsible for the red, purple, or blue pigmentation of certain fruits and vegetables.

Morus alba anthocyanins have been shown to possess numerous health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antidiabetic properties, making them a valuable natural resource for potential medicinal applications.

2. Flavonoids

Morus alba flavonoids is a class of plant compounds found in the bark, leaves, and fruit of the white mulberry tree.

These bioactive compounds have been extensively studied for their antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antibacterial properties.

Morus alba flavonoids have been shown to possess cardiovascular protective effects, improve glucose metabolism, and exhibit anticancer activities, making them a promising area of research in the field of traditional medicine and modern pharmacology.

3. Quercetin

Morus alba quercetin is a bioflavonoid compound extracted from the bark of Morus alba trees.

It has been extensively studied for its numerous health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antibacterial properties. Quercetin has been shown to possess cardioprotective, neuroprotective, and anticancer activities, making it a promising natural compound for the prevention and treatment of various diseases.

Its potent bioactive effects have also sparked interest in its potential applications as a dietary supplement or pharmaceutical agent.

4. Kaempferol

Morus alba kaempferol is a bioactive flavonoid compound isolated from the bark and leaves of Morus alba L. It has been extensively studied for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial activities.

Kaempferol has also been shown to exhibit cytotoxic effects against various types of cancer cells, making it a promising agent in the prevention and treatment of cancer.

Its unique structure and properties make it an attractive compound for further research and development.

5. Moracin m

Morus alba moracin m is a bioactive compound extracted from the bark of the white mulberry tree.

It has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine for its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Moracin M has been shown to exhibit potential health benefits, including anti-cancer activity, neuroprotection, and cardiovascular protection.

Its unique chemical structure makes it a valuable natural product for pharmaceutical research and development.

6. Isovitexin-2'-o-glucoside

Morus alba isovitexin-2'-o-glucoside is a unique flavonoid glycoside compound found in the fruit and leaves of Morus alba L. This bioactive compound has been extensively studied for its potential health benefits, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antitumor activities.

Isotexin-2'-O-glucoside has also been reported to possess cardio-protective and neuro-protective effects, making it a promising natural product for the prevention and treatment of various diseases.

7. Vitexin

Morus alba vitexin is a flavonoid glycoside that has been extensively studied for its medicinal properties.

It is isolated from the leaves of Morus alba and has been found to possess various biological activities, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antibacterial effects.

Vitexin has also been reported to exhibit neuroprotective, cardioprotective, and anticancer properties, making it a promising candidate for the development of novel therapeutic agents.

8. Oleanolic acid

Morus alba oleanolic acid is a naturally occurring compound found in the bark and leaves of the white mulberry tree.

It has been extensively studied for its potential medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antiviral activities. Oleanolic acid has also been shown to have protective effects against liver damage, cardiovascular disease, and certain types of cancer.

Additionally, it may possess immunomodulatory and anti-diabetic properties.

9. Ursolic acid

Morus alba ursolic acid is a triterpenoid compound naturally occurring in the leaves and fruits of the white mulberry tree.

This bioactive compound has been extensively studied for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and anti-cancer properties.

Ursolic acid has been shown to exhibit significant inhibitory effects on various cancer cell lines, making it a promising candidate for the development of novel chemotherapeutic agents.

10. Betulinic acid

Morus alba betulinic acid is a unique compound extracted from the white mulberry tree.

It has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine for its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Betulinic acid has been shown to exhibit potent antitumor activity, inhibiting the growth of cancer cells by inducing apoptosis (programmed cell death) and suppressing tumor angiogenesis.

Its potential uses include treating various cancers, as well as inflammation-related diseases such as arthritis and asthma.
