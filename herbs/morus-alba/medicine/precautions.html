1. Be aware of allergy risk

When using Morus alba medicinally, it's important to be aware of allergy risk.

Some individuals may develop an allergic reaction to the plant's sap or leaves, which can lead to symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling. This is particularly crucial for those with a history of allergies or atopic dermatitis, as Morus alba may trigger severe reactions.

Furthermore, it's essential to patch test any new herbal remedy to ensure individual tolerance and avoid potential adverse effects.

4. Monitor blood pressure levels

When using Morus alba medicinally, it's important to monitor blood pressure levels.

This precaution is essential because Morus alba has been shown to lower blood pressure and may interact with medications for high blood pressure, potentially causing a significant drop in BP.

When using Morus alba medicinally, it's important to avoid with kidney issues.

This precaution is crucial because Morus alba can potentially exacerbate existing kidney problems by increasing the risk of bleeding and reducing blood flow to the kidneys. Additionally, its diuretic properties may cause an increase in urine production, placing additional strain on the kidneys.

When using Morus alba medicinally, it's important to take with food only.
