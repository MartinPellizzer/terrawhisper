White Mulberry (Morus alba) Actions

White Mulberry exhibits several therapeutic actions including anti-inflammatory, tonic, and diuretic properties. It also acts as an expectorant, carminative, and antispasmodic. The herb has bitter and astringent qualities, while also serving as a sedative and stimulant. These actions support its traditional use in promoting health and balancing bodily functions.

White Mulberry (Morus alba) Monograph

1. Anti-inflammatory

White Mulberry has an anti-inflammatory action, primarily due to the presence of compounds like morin and kaempferol. These bioactive molecules inhibit inflammatory pathways, reducing swelling and redness. This effect supports its traditional use in managing conditions like arthritis and skin inflammation.

2. Tonic

White Mulberry has a tonic action, enhancing vitality and supporting overall bodily functions. It helps strengthen the spleen and lungs, aiding in digestion and energy production. This action makes it valuable in traditional herbal medicine for boosting stamina and improving health.

3. Diuretic

White Mulberry has a diuretic action, promoting increased urine production and aiding in the removal of excess fluids from the body. This effect is attributed to its compounds that support kidney function and reduce fluid retention. It is commonly used in herbal medicine to manage edema and support urinary health.

4. Expectorant

White Mulberry has an expectorant action, helping to loosen and expel mucus from the respiratory tract. It is traditionally used to alleviate congestion and support clear breathing in conditions like coughs and colds. Its active compounds stimulate the production of bronchial secretions, aiding in the removal of phlegm.

5. Carminative

White Mulberry has a carminative action, helping to relieve gas and bloating in the digestive system. It promotes smooth muscle relaxation in the gut, reducing discomfort and spasms. This effect supports overall digestive health and can alleviate symptoms of indigestion and flatulence.

6. Antispasmodic

White Mulberry has an antispasmodic action, helping to relieve muscle spasms and cramps by relaxing smooth muscle tissue. It is traditionally used to ease gastrointestinal discomfort and reduce tension in the digestive tract. This effect is attributed to its bioactive compounds, which modulate nerve impulses and muscle contractions.

7. Bitter

White Mulberry has a bitter action, which stimulates digestion and reduces excess dampness in the body. This property helps to clear heat and detoxify, supporting liver and gallbladder function. It is particularly effective in addressing digestive sluggishness and promoting metabolic balance.

8. Astringent

White Mulberry has an astringent action, which helps reduce secretions and tighten tissues. It is used to treat conditions like diarrhea, excessive sweating, and respiratory issues. This property is due to its high content of tannins, which bind to proteins and reduce fluid loss.

9. Sedative

White Mulberry has a sedative action, helping to calm the nervous system and reduce anxiety. It promotes relaxation by influencing neurotransmitter activity, making it useful for managing stress and insomnia. Its mild effects make it a gentle option for those seeking natural sleep support.

10. Stimulant

White Mulberry has a stimulant action, enhancing metabolic activity and energy levels. It supports the nervous system and may improve mental alertness. This effect is attributed to its bioactive compounds, which influence neurotransmitter activity. It is commonly used in traditional medicine to combat fatigue and boost vitality.
