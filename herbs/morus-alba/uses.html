White Mulberry (Morus alba) Uses

White Mulberry is used to treat respiratory conditions like cough, bronchitis, and asthma by reducing inflammation and soothing the lungs. It also aids in wound healing and managing skin infections, scabies, and irritations due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

White Mulberry (Morus alba) Monograph

1. Cough

White Mulberry (Morus alba) is used in traditional herbal medicine to alleviate cough symptoms. Its leaves contain mucilage, which soothes irritated airways and reduces inflammation. It is often prepared as a tea or decoction to ease dry or productive coughs. This remedy is valued for its mild and supportive action on the respiratory system.

2. Bronchitis

White Mulberry (Morus alba) is used in traditional herbal medicine to support respiratory health, particularly in treating bronchitis. Its leaves and roots contain compounds with anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties that help reduce mucus buildup and soothe bronchial irritation. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to ease coughing and improve breathing in individuals with chronic bronchitis.

3. Asthma

White Mulberry (Morus alba) may help alleviate asthma symptoms by reducing airway inflammation and improving lung function. Its anti-inflammatory and bronchodilating properties support respiratory health. Traditionally used in herbal medicine, it is often combined with other herbs to enhance its effects in managing asthma.

4. Inflamed lungs

White Mulberry (Morus alba) is used in traditional herbal medicine to soothe inflamed lungs. Its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties help reduce irritation and mucus production. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to support respiratory health. This remedy is particularly valued for its gentle yet effective action on the respiratory system.

5. Skin infections

White Mulberry (Morus alba) is used topically to treat skin infections due to its antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties. The leaves and bark contain compounds that inhibit bacterial growth and reduce redness and swelling. It is often applied as a poultice or infused oil to promote healing and prevent infection in minor wounds and dermatitis.

6. Wound healing

White Mulberry (Morus alba) is used in traditional medicine to promote wound healing due to its high content of antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds. These properties help reduce inflammation, prevent infection, and accelerate tissue repair. It is often applied topically as a poultice or ointment to support the healing process in minor cuts and skin injuries.

7. Scabies

White Mulberry (Morus alba) is used in traditional herbal medicine to treat scabies due to its antiparasitic properties. The leaves and bark contain compounds that help eliminate the mites causing scabies. It is often prepared as a topical application or infusion to soothe skin irritation and combat infestation.

8. Wounds

White Mulberry (Morus alba) is used topically to treat wounds due to its high content of mucilage and flavonoids, which promote tissue repair and reduce inflammation. The leaves can be crushed and applied directly to the affected area to aid in healing and prevent infection.

9. Skin itching

White Mulberry (Morus alba) is used to alleviate skin itching due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistaminic properties. It helps reduce irritation and redness associated with conditions like eczema and hives. The leaves and bark contain compounds that soothe the skin and promote healing. It is often prepared as a topical application or internal tea for relief.

10. Skin irritation

White Mulberry (Morus alba) is used topically to alleviate skin irritation due to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. The leaves contain compounds that help reduce redness and itching. It is often prepared as a poultice or infused oil for direct application on affected areas. Its mild nature makes it suitable for sensitive skin conditions.
