Botanical Identification of White Mulberry (Morus alba)

To identify White Mulberry (Morus alba) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

The White Mulberry, Morus alba, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Spermatophyta, class Caryophyllopsida, order Urticales, family Moraceae, genus Morus, and species Morus alba. These classifications provide a systematic framework for identifying and understanding its botanical relationships.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of White Mulberry is Morus alba, first described by Linnaeus in 1753. It is also known by several synonyms, including Morus alba L. and Morus alba subsp. alba. Common names include White Mulberry, Chinese Mulberry, and Silk Mulberry, while regional and historical names vary across cultures and periods.

Morphological Characteristics

White Mulberry (Morus alba) is a deciduous tree or shrub with a spreading growth habit. It has a deep taproot system and can develop extensive lateral roots. The stem is smooth, often with a reddish-brown bark, and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a serrated margin. The inflorescence is a catkin, with separate male and female flowers on different plants. The fruit is a small, fuzzy, white or yellowish berry containing 1-3 seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

White Mulberry (Morus alba) can be distinguished by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a hairy underside. The bark is smooth and light gray, while the fruit is a small, white or pale green berry that turns red when mature. It differs from Morus species like Morus nigra by its lighter coloration and smaller, less fleshy fruits.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

White Mulberry (Morus alba) is native to Asia, particularly China, and thrives in temperate climates. It prefers well-drained soils and is commonly found at low to mid-altitudes, often near rivers or in cultivated areas. Its adaptability allows it to grow in a variety of environments, making it a widespread species across its native range.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

White Mulberry (Morus alba) blooms in early spring, typically between March and May, depending on the region. Seed formation occurs from late summer to early autumn, with fruit ripening in late summer. For identification purposes, harvest timing is best between July and September when the fruits are fully mature and easily identifiable.

Phylogenetic Relationships

White Mulberry (Morus alba) belongs to the genus Morus in the family Moraceae. It is closely related to other mulberry species like Morus macroura and Morus nigra. Within the family, it shares traits such as opposite leaves and aggregate fruits, distinguishing it from other genera like Ficus.
