White Mulberry (Morus alba) Compounds

The primary compounds in White Mulberry include flavonoids, which act as antioxidants, and tannins, which have astringent properties. Phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory effects, while coumarin and glycosides support its medicinal uses. Saponins and terpenoids provide additional health benefits, and anthocyanins, along with essential oils and alkaloids, enhance its therapeutic profile.

White Mulberry (Morus alba) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in White Mulberry (Morus alba). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds help support cardiovascular health and may aid in blood sugar regulation. Flavonoids are a key group of phytochemicals in this medicinal plant.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in White Mulberry (Morus alba). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health. Tannins also have antioxidant effects, helping to neutralize free radicals in the body. These properties make tannin an important component in traditional herbal remedies using White Mulberry.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in White Mulberry (Morus alba). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports various therapeutic effects, including anti-inflammatory and hypoglycemic actions, making it valuable in herbal medicine.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in White Mulberry (Morus alba). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine, particularly for its anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarin is also known for its role in regulating blood sugar levels and supporting cardiovascular health.

5. Glycoside

White Mulberry (Morus alba) contains the glycoside moruside, which has been studied for its potential anti-diabetic and antioxidant properties. This compound may support blood sugar regulation and cellular protection, contributing to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in White Mulberry (Morus alba). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Saponins may support various therapeutic applications associated with White Mulberry.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in White Mulberry (Morus alba). They contribute to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and immunomodulatory effects. These compounds are often extracted and used in traditional herbal formulations to support health and treat various conditions.

8. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in White Mulberry (Morus alba). It contributes to the plant's vibrant color and possesses antioxidant properties. These properties support its traditional use in promoting eye health and managing diabetes. Anthocyanin also aids in reducing inflammation and enhancing cardiovascular health.

9. Essential oil

The essential oil of White Mulberry (Morus alba) contains volatile compounds like limonene and alpha-pinene, which contribute to its aromatic properties. These compounds may support respiratory health and have mild antimicrobial effects, though research on their specific medicinal applications is limited.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in White Mulberry (Morus alba). It contributes to the plant's pharmacological properties, though its role in traditional herbal medicine is less emphasized compared to other constituents like flavonoids and polysaccharides. Alkaloids in White Mulberry may support various therapeutic effects, though research is ongoing to fully understand their mechanisms.
