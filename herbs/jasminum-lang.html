What's the taxonomical classification of Jasminum lang?

Jasminum lang belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Oleaceae. Ultimately, it is situated within the genus Jasminum, specifically identified as the species lang.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Jasminum lang has woody, climbing or trailing stems that bear opposite, entire-margined leaves arranged in pairs along the branches. The foliage typically consists of ovate to elliptical leaflets with smooth edges and distinct venation. Its flowers are tubular or salverform in shape, emerging from the leaf axils in small clusters or solitary arrangements. These blooms feature five to seven spreading lobes and are often white or pale yellow in color. The plant produces small, fleshy drupes that transition from green to dark colors upon maturity.
