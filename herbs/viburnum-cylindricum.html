Viburnum cylindricum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viburnum cylindricum has an arching, shrubby growth habit characterized by opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins and a woody, multi-stemmed structure. The foliage typically features prominent pinnate venation and a texture that can vary from smooth to slightly rugose. During the flowering season, the plant produces dense, cylindrical clusters of small, white, five-petaled flowers. These inflorescences transition into fleshy, drupaceous fruits that change color as they ripen. The bark on older stems becomes textured and provides a sturdy framework for the spreading canopy.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of eastern China, primarily found in provinces such as Zhejiang, Anhui, Jiangsu, and Fujian. It thrives within temperate forest environments at various elevations across these southeastern provinces. The species is most commonly documented growing in shaded understory habitats and along moist slopes. Its range is relatively localized to these specific East Asian montane ecosystems. This particular distribution pattern limits its occurrence to these distinct ecological niches within its native territory.
