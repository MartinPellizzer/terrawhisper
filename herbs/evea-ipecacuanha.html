Evea ipecacuanha

Evea ipecacuanha, commonly known as ipecac, is a plant species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

Ipecac has been traditionally used for its emetic properties, helping to induce vomiting to treat various health conditions, such as poisoning, food poisoning, and gastrointestinal disorders.

The health benefits of ipecac include treating nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, as well as its use as a laxative to stimulate bowel movements.

The bioactive constituents of ipecac include alkaloids like emetine and cephaeline, which contribute to its therapeutic actions of emesis and gastrointestinal stimulation.
