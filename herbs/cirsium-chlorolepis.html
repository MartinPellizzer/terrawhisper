Cirsium chlorolepis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cirsium chlorolepis has erect, unbranched stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 100 centimeters and feature leaves that are broadly lanceolate with deeply lobed, dentate margins. The foliage is characterized by a distinctive green coloration on both surfaces, lacking the white tomentum often found in related species. Small, involucrate flower heads are arranged in terminal clusters and possess tubular florets ranging in color from pink to purple. The plant produces narrow, cylindrical achenes that are accompanied by a pappus of fine bristles to facilitate wind dispersal. These morphological traits collectively distinguish it within the thistle genus.
