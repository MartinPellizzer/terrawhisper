What's the taxonomical classification of Cirsium chlorolepis?

Cirsium chlorolepis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Asterales and is a member of the family Asteraceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Cirsium, and its specific species name, chlorolepis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cirsium chlorolepis has erect, unbranched stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 100 centimeters and feature leaves that are broadly lanceolate with deeply lobed, dentate margins. Small, involucrate flower heads are arranged in terminal clusters and possess tubular florets ranging in color from pink to purple. The plant produces narrow, cylindrical achenes that are accompanied by a pappus of fine bristles to facilitate wind dispersal.
