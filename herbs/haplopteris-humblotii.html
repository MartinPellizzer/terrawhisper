What is Haplopteris humblotii?

Haplopteris humblotii is an evergreen, terrestrial fern species in the family Pteridaceae.

It has fronds with a simple, deltoid base, a linear-rhombic to lanceolate blade, and a rachis that is glabrous or hairy. The frond's base is often cordate or auriculate, and it has a pinnate to bipinnate venation pattern. This species is endemic to Madagascar and has been found growing in humid, shaded areas.

The fertile fronds of Haplopteris humblotii have sporangia that are glabrous and borne in sori that are abaxial and paraphyllous.
