Filipendula vestita

What's the taxonomical classification of Filipendula vestita?

Filipendula vestita belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the taxonomic lineage of the order Rosales. This plant is a part of the family Rosaceae, which places it within the genus Filipendula, ultimately identifying it as the specific species Filipendula vestita.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Filipendula vestita has a perennial rhizomatous habit characterized by basal leaves that are pinnately divided with deeply serrated leaflets and stems covered in dense, fine hairs.

The foliage often appears grayish-green due to this thick pubescence which provides a distinct texture. Small, clustered flowers emerge from the terminal ends of the stems, typically displaying a pale pink to white coloration. Each blossom consists of numerous tiny petals and stamens that create a delicate, feathery appearance when in full bloom.

The plant's overall structure is compact yet bushy, allowing it to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
