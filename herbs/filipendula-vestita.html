Filipendula vestita

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Filipendula vestita has a perennial rhizomatous habit characterized by basal leaves that are pinnately divided with deeply serrated leaflets and stems covered in dense, fine hairs. The foliage often appears grayish-green due to this thick pubescence which provides a distinct texture. Small, clustered flowers emerge from the terminal ends of the stems, typically displaying a pale pink to white coloration. Each blossom consists of numerous tiny petals and stamens that create a delicate, feathery appearance when in full bloom. The plant's overall structure is compact yet bushy, allowing it to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
