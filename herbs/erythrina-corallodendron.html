Erythrina corallodendron

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Erythrina corallodendron has a woody, spreading tree habit characterized by grayish bark and trifoliate leaves consisting of three elliptical leaflets. The plant produces large, bright red, papilionaceous flowers that grow in dense, terminal racemes. These blossoms emerge from the branches, often appearing before or alongside the foliage to attract pollinators. The leaves are typically arranged alternately along the stems and provide a lush green canopy. The overall structure is robust, supporting a heavy crown of vibrant floral displays.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the coastal and lowland regions of eastern South Africa, specifically spanning the provinces of KwaZulu-Natal and Mpumalanga. Within its natural habitat, the species is often found in well-drained soils near riverbanks or in sheltered valleys. Its distribution is closely tied to the warm, humid climates characteristic of the Indian Ocean coastline.
