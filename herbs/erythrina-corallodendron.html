Erythrina corallodendron

What's the taxonomical classification of Erythrina corallodendron?

Erythrina corallodendron belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Fabales. The plant is a member of the family Fabaceae, which places it within the genus Erythrina, specifically identified by its species name, corallodendron.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains medicinal compounds including D-galactosephilic lectin, Erythrina corallodendron lectin, and various other Erythrina lectins. These specific proteins function as a type of lectin that can interact with carbohydrates on cell surfaces. Researchers study these molecules to understand their potential biological activities and therapeutic applications. The presence of such complex lectins highlights the chemical diversity found within the species. Understanding these individual components is essential for exploring the plant's pharmacological properties.

As discussed in a study, the seeds of Erythrina corallodendron contain a mitogenic D-galactosephilic lectin. This purified lectin is a glycoprotein with a molecular weight around 110 000 - 120 000. The compound exhibits D-galactosephilic activity and binds to D-galactosamine, N-acetyl-D-galactosamine, alpha- and beta-galactosides, and D-galactose. It shows considerable mitogenic activity towards human lymphocytes, which are predominantly T cells, following treatment with neuraminidase. This specific mitogenic stimulation of lymphocytes is inhibited by D-galactose and its derivatives.
