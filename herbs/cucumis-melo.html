Cantaloupe

Cucumis melo

Cantaloupe (Cucumis melo) is a widely cultivated fruit belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family, known for its sweet, yellow-orange flesh and high nutritional value, and it is commonly used for its medicinal properties to improve digestion and support overall health.

Botanical Identification

Cantaloupe, scientifically known as Cucumis melo, belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family. It is also called muskmelon and is an annual plant native to Asia. The fruit has a thick rind, netted texture, and sweet, aromatic flesh. It is often referenced in herbal texts under various regional names.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Liliopsida
  • Order: Cucurbitales
  • Family: Cucurbitaceae
  • Genus: Cucumis
  • Species: Cucumis melo

Safety Profile

Cantaloupe (Cucumis melo) is generally safe when consumed in normal food amounts. However, it should be avoided by individuals with a known allergy to cucurbitaceae plants. Large quantities may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals.
