Cantaloupe (Cucumis melo)

Cantaloupe (Cucumis melo) is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, native to Southern Europe, Western Asia, and North Africa. Traditionally, its fruits, seeds, and leaves have been used for culinary uses, infusions, and decoctions.

This herb is particularly valued for its diuretic, anti-inflammatory, and carminative actions, and has a long history of use in mediterranean herbal traditions, european herbal medicine, and traditional chinese medicine.
