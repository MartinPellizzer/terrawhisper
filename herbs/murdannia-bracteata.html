What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Murdannia bracteata has lanceolate to elliptical leaves that are arranged oppositely on succulent, creeping stems. Its most defining morphological feature is the presence of large, colorful bracts that subtend the small, tubular flowers. These bracts often possess a distinct shape and hue that contrast sharply with the surrounding greenery.
