Triadica cochinchinensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Triadica cochinchinensis has evergreen, woody branches bearing oppositely arranged, elliptical to oblong leaves that are typically coriaceous with entire margins. The foliage exhibits a glossy upper surface and often features a distinctively pale or glaucous underside. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in axillary clusters, which eventually develop into fleshy, globose fruits. These fruits change color as they mature, often transitioning to a bright red or yellowish hue to attract dispersers. The overall architecture of the plant is that of a small to medium-sized tree with a dense, spreading canopy.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Triadica cochinchinensis targets inflammatory enzymes such as cyclooxygenase-2, lipoxygenase, and various pro-inflammatory cytokines to exert its therapeutic effects. The plant contains bioactive polyphenols and tannins that inhibit the expression of inducible nitric oxide synthase, thereby reducing oxidative stress. These compounds also interact with nuclear factor kappa B signaling pathways to modulate the immune response. Additionally, certain extracts show affinity for protein kinases involved in cell proliferation and apoptosis regulation. By modulating these specific protein interactions, the plant demonstrates significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.
