What's the taxonomical classification of Aristolochia moupinensis?

Aristolochia moupinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it falls under the order Piperales and is a member of the family Aristolochiaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by the genus Aristolochia, with its specific identity established as the species moupinensis.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause severe kidney damage, liver damage, cancer, and gastrointestinal distress due to the presence of toxic aristolochic acids. These potent compounds are known to trigger nephrotoxicity, which often results in progressive renal failure. The substances also exhibit significant hepatotoxicity, leading to serious injury to the liver. Furthermore, the plant possesses strong carcinogenicity, specifically linked to the development of urothelial cancers in the urinary tract. Ingesting the plant may also cause immediate gastrointestinal distress such as nausea and vomiting.
