Lophatherum gracile

What's the taxonomical classification of Lophatherum gracile?

Lophatherum gracile belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It falls under the order Poales and is a member of the family Poaceae. Within this family, it is specifically categorized under the genus Lophatherum, with its unique designation being the species gracile.

According to a study published by "Experimental cell research", Lophatherum gracile Brongn. extract exhibits significant anti-glioma effects in both cellular and animal models. The extract contains 5 active compounds, which are chlorogenic acid, isoorientin, orientin, vitexin, and isovitexin. These compounds were found to interact with 219 drug targets out of a total of 1472 identified glioma targets. The extract significantly attenuated glioma cell viability, proliferation, and migration while promoting autophagy, apoptosis, and cell death. Molecular docking analysis suggested that dihydrofolate reductase may be a key target for these compounds.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds apiagenin, isoorientin, luteolin, luteolin-7-O-β-D-glucoside, and swertiajaponin. These bioactive constituents are primarily categorized as flavonoids and secoiridoids that contribute to the species' therapeutic potential. Research indicates that these specific chemical structures may offer significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The presence of luteolin and its glycoside derivatives suggests a complex profile of phytochemical defense mechanisms. Scientists study these compounds to better understand how Lophatherum gracile interacts with biological systems.

According to a study published by "Chinese herbal medicines", the dried stems and leaves of Lophatherum gracile contain flavonoids and phenolic acids as the main active ingredients. There are 44 flavonoids, which have flavonoid glycosides as their main structure, and 16 phenolic acids, which have hydroxybenzoic acids as their main structure. Specific flavonoid monomers found in the plant include luteolin, isoorientin, luteolin-7-O-beta-D-glucoside, and apiigenin. These chemical constituents produce diuretic, anti-inflammatory, and antipyretic effects. Modern studies also show they play important roles in cardioprotective, hepatoprotective, and hypoglycaemic effects.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for the treatment of respiratory syncytial virus infection, specifically to mitigate RSV-induced inflammation and manage symptoms associated with RSV-related diseases. It serves as a therapeutic agent to alleviate the persistent cough and high fever often triggered by the virus. By targeting the underlying inflammatory responses, the plant helps stabilize the patient's physiological state during infection. This botanical application provides a natural approach to managing the complex complications caused by the virus.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", Lophatherum gracile is traditionally used in the treatment of cough associated with lung heat and inflammation. The ethanol extract of the plant, known as DZY, was shown to inhibit respiratory syncytial virus (RSV) infection and RSV-induced inflammation. In the HEp-2 cell line, DZY inhibited RSV infection with IC50 values of 20 μg/mL against the RSV Long strain and 25 μg/mL against the RSV A2 strain. The study also utilized three-week-old BALB/c mice to evaluate how the plant reduces viral load and ameliorates lesions in lung tissue. These findings provide scientific evidence for the use of Lophatherum gracile in treating diseases potentially caused by RSV.
