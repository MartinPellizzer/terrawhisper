Lophatherum gracile

What's the taxonomical classification of Lophatherum gracile?

Lophatherum gracile belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It falls under the order Poales and is a member of the family Poaceae. Within this family, it is specifically categorized under the genus Lophatherum, with its unique designation being the species gracile.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lophatherum gracile has slender, erect stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters and support narrow, lanceolate leaves arranged oppositely along the axis. The leaf blades are characterized by a smooth texture and acute apices, often exhibiting a bright green coloration. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, presenting a delicate structure typical of its genus. The reproductive organs are minimal in appearance, often blending with the surrounding foliage. This plant's overall architecture is defined by its lightweight, airy habit and fine-textured vegetative components.

What are the pharmacological activities of Lophatherum gracile?

This plant has potent anti-cancer, anti-glioblastoma, anti-inflammatory, anti-tumor, and antitumo properties. These medicinal qualities make it a subject of significant interest in the field of oncology and pharmacology. Specifically, its ability to target glioblastoma cells suggests potential applications in treating aggressive brain tumors. Furthermore, the plant demonstrates a strong capacity to suppress inflammation within biological systems. These combined effects highlight its multifaceted role in combating various forms of cellular malignancy and swelling.

As discussed in a study, Lophatherum gracile Brongn. extract exhibits anti-cancer properties on fibrosarcoma cells and shows significant anti-glioma effects in both cellular and animal models. The extract contains 5 active compounds, which are chlorogenic acid, isoorientin, orientin, vitexin, and isovitexin. These 5 compounds act on 219 drug targets, which are linked to a total of 1472 glioma targets. By targeting dihydrofolate reductase, the extract significantly attenuates glioma cell viability, proliferation, and migration. Additionally, the treatment promotes autophagy, apoptosis, and cell death in human glioma cells such as A172, LN229, U-87 MG, and U251.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds apiagenin, isoorientin, luteolin, luteolin-7-O-beta-D-glucoside, and swertiajaponin. These specific phytochemicals contribute to the diverse biological activities associated with the species. The presence of various flavones like apiagenin and luteolin often suggests potential antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Additionally, the glycosylated forms such as isoorientin and luteolin-7-O-beta-D-glucoside influence the solubility and metabolic pathways of these substances within a biological system. The unique compound swertiajaponin further characterizes the specific chemical profile of this plant.

As discussed in a study, Lophatherum gracile contains 44 flavonoids and 16 phenolic acids as its main active ingredients. The flavonoids are primarily composed of flavonoid glycosides, while the phenolic acids consist of hydroxybenzoic acids. Specific flavonoid monomers like luteolin, isoorientin, luteolin-7-O-beta-D-glucoside, and apiigenin are noted for being more effective in exerting pharmacological effects. These compounds produce diuretic, anti-inflammatory, and antipyretic effects. Furthermore, the chemical constituents are involved in cardioprotective, hepatoprotective, and hypoglycaemic effects.
