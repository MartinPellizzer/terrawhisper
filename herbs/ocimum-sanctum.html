Ocimum sanctum

What's the taxonomical classification of Ocimum sanctum?

Ocimum sanctum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized into the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Ocimum, with its specific designation being the species sanctum.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Ocimum sanctum contains a complex profile of bioactive phytochemicals including eugenol, ursolic acid, rosmarinic acid, and various essential oils like carvacrol and linalool. These compounds work synergistically to provide potent antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. Eugenol acts as a primary therapeutic agent, contributing significantly to the plant's ability to reduce oxidative stress and inhibit bacterial growth. The presence of triterpenoids like ursolic acid further supports its role in promoting cellular health and supporting immune function. Together, these medicinal constituents make the plant a versatile tool in traditional and modern pharmacological applications.

What are the therapeutic actions of Ocimum sanctum?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat respiratory infections like asthma and bronchitis, digestive disorders, fever, inflammation, and skin ailments. Its bioactive compounds provide significant antimicrobial and antioxidant effects that help fight bacterial and viral pathogens. Many traditional practices utilize the leaves to soothe sore throats and manage symptoms of the common cold. It also plays a role in regulating blood glucose levels and reducing stress through its adaptogenic properties. Furthermore, its anti-inflammatory nature makes it effective for addressing various joint pains and inflammatory skin conditions.
