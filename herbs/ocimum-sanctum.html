Possible Side Effects of ocimum sanctum

Ocimum sanctum can have side effects if used improperly, such as skin irritation, allergic reactions, and increased heart rate due to its active compound eugenol.

In some cases, excessive consumption of Ocimum sanctum may cause stomach upset, nausea, and diarrhea.

Prolonged use of the herb without proper guidance can also lead to interactions with other medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes medications.

Additionally, individuals with certain medical conditions, such as bleeding disorders, should avoid using Ocimum sanctum as it may exacerbate their condition.
