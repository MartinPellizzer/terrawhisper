What's the taxonomical classification of Cheilanthes smithii?

Cheilanthes smithii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Polypodiinae and falls under the order Polypodiales. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues into the family Pteridaceae, where it is placed within the genus Cheilanthes, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species smithii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cheilanthes smithii has pinnatifid or deeply lobed fronds that are typically leathery in texture and exhibit a distinctively dark, almost blackish appearance.

The sori are located along the margins of the segments, often protected by a reflexed leaf margin that forms a false indusium.
