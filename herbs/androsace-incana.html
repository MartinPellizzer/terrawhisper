What's the taxonomical classification of Androsace incana?

Androsace incana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, falling into the order Ericales. Within this taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is a member of the family Primulaceae, placed in the genus Androsace, and is identified specifically as the species incana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Androsace incana has a perennial growth habit characterized by a basal rosette of grayish-green, lanceolate leaves that are densely covered in fine, silvery hairs. These leaves feature entire or slightly dentate margins and a soft, pubescent texture due to the dense trichomes. The flowering stems arise from the center of the rosette, bearing small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Each blossom is supported by a short pedicel and possesses a deeply lobed corolla. The entire plant exhibits a compact, cushion-like morphology adapted to rocky or alpine environments.
