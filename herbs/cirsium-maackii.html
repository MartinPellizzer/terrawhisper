What's the taxonomical classification of Cirsium maackii?

Cirsium maackii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. This plant is further categorized into the family Asteraceae, which is known for its composite flower heads, and is specifically identified under the genus Cirsium. Ultimately, its unique biological identity is defined by its specific epithet, making it the species Cirsium maackii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cirsium maackii has erect, unbranched stems that reach heights of 20 to 60 centimeters and are characterized by a dense covering of white, spreading hairs. The leaves are primarily basal, forming a rosette of oblong to lanceolate shapes with deeply lobed or dentate margins and prominent veins. Upper stem leaves are much smaller and more sparse, often possessing winged bases that clasp the stem. The inflorescence consists of solitary, terminal flower heads that are typically pink to purple in color. Each head features tubular disc florets surrounded by several rows of overlapping involucral bracts.
