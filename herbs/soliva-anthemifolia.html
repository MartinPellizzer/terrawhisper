What's the taxonomical classification of Soliva anthemifolia?

Soliva anthemifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is placed under the order Asterales. It is a part of the widely recognized family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. Within this family, the plant is classified under the genus Soliva, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species anthemifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Soliva anthemifolia has prostrate, spreading stems that form dense mats with small, elliptical to lanceolate leaves measuring approximately 5 to 15 millimeters in length. The foliage is typically green and lacks distinct petioles, growing closely against the ground to facilitate its creeping habit. Tiny, inconspicuous flowers are produced in axillary clusters, appearing in small amounts throughout the plant. These flowers are characteristic of the Amaranthaceae family, featuring small tepals rather than distinct petals. The overall structure of the plant is low-growing and herbaceous, allowing it to colonize disturbed soil surfaces effectively.
