Cerasus cuneata

Cerasus cuneata, commonly known as a type of apricot kernel, is a plant-based remedy that is derived from the kernel of the apricot fruit.

It is valued for its potential health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, anti-oxidative, and antimicrobial properties, which may aid in reducing stress, improving digestion, and boosting the immune system.

The therapeutic actions of Cerasus cuneata include its ability to act as an antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and immunomodulator, which may contribute to its use in traditional medicine for various purposes.

The bioactive constituents of Cerasus cuneata include amygdalin, a cyanogenic glycoside that is broken down into amygdalin and benzaldehyde, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

Possible Side Effects of Cerasus cuneata
