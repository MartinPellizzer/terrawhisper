What is Selinum rhodopetalum?

Selinum rhodopetalum is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Apiaceae.

It has a glabrous or slightly pubescent stem, 10-30 cm tall, with a glabrous or slightly pubescent leaf sheath. The leaves are 2-3 times ternate or pinnatisect, with leaflets 2-5 cm long, ovate or lanceolate, and entire or slightly lobed. The inflorescence is a compound umbel, 3-8 cm diameter, with 5-15 umbellules, each with 5-10 white or yellowish flowers.

The flowers have 5-7 mm long calyx and 3-5 mm long corolla, and are followed by a schizocarpous fruit, consisting of 2-3 mericarps, 5-7 mm long, and 3-4 mm wide.
