What's the taxonomical classification of Polygonum griffithii?

Polygonum griffithii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized as Equisetopsida, which falls under the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Caryophyllales and is a member of the family Polygonaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic placement is defined by the genus Polygonum and the species name griffithii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polygonum griffithii has erect, slender stems that are often reddish in color and possess a smooth or slightly puberulent texture. The leaves are simple, lanceolate to narrowly elliptic, and arranged alternately along the length of the stem. Each leaf features a distinct ochrea, which is a membranous sheath characteristic of the Polygonaceae family, wrapping around the nodes. The plant typically produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in axillary or terminal positions. These floral structures eventually give way to tiny, dry fruits known as achenes.
