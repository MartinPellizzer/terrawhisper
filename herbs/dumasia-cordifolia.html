What's the taxonomical classification of Dumasia cordifolia?

Dumasia cordifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the order Fabales and the family Fabaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by the genus Dumasia and the species cordifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dumasia cordifolia has cordate leaves with serrated margins, slender upright stems, and small, tubular white flowers arranged in dense terminal clusters. The leaves are characterized by their heart-shaped base and distinct venation patterns that run toward the edges. Its stems are typically herbaceous and can grow in a sprawling or ascending manner depending on the environmental support. The inflorescence consists of many tiny blossoms that emerge from the top of the plant to attract pollinators.
