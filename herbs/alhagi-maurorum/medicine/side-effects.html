10 Possible Side Effects Of Alhagi Maurorum (Persian Silk)

Alhagi maurorum, also known as Persian silk, has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, allergic reactions, and kidney damage.

These side effects can occur if the herb is consumed in excessive amounts or by individuals with pre-existing medical conditions.

In severe cases, prolonged use can lead to anemia, fatigue, and even organ failure, significantly impacting daily life and overall well-being.

1. Worsens underlying conditions

Alhagi maurorum, also known as Persian silk, causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

This is due to the presence of allergenic compounds such as flavonoids and alkaloids within its plant extracts. These substances can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms like hives, itching, and swelling.

The severity of these reactions may vary depending on individual sensitivities and exposure levels, but they can be uncomfortable and potentially life-threatening for those who experience them.

Alhagi maurorum, also known as Persian silk, triggers asthma attacks due to its potential allergenic properties.

The plant's sap and leaves contain compounds that can cause an allergic reaction in some individuals, leading to constriction of the airways and increased mucus production, which can exacerbate asthma symptoms.

Additionally, the high levels of histamine and other bioactive molecules found in Alhagi maurorum may also contribute to bronchospasm and wheezing, further triggering asthma attacks.
