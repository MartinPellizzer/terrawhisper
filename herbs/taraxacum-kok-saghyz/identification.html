Botanical Identification of Russian Dandelion (Taraxacum kok-saghyz)

To identify Russian Dandelion (Taraxacum kok-saghyz) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

The Russian Dandelion, Taraxacum kok-saghyz, belongs to the kingdom Plantae. Its family is Compositae, and it is part of the genus Taraxacum. The species name indicates its specific identification within the genus. The other classifications—division, class, and order—are not provided in the data.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of the Russian Dandelion is Taraxacum kok-saghyz, first described by the Russian botanist Alexey Vasil'evich Koptev in 1883. This name reflects its native habitat in Central Asia, with "kok-saghyz" derived from the Turkic language. It is also known by various regional and historical names, including "Russian dandelion" and "Kopetdag dandelion," highlighting its traditional use in herbal medicine.

Morphological Characteristics

The Russian dandelion is a perennial herb with a robust taproot system. It has a straight, hairy stem that can grow up to 50 cm tall. Its leaves are basal, spoon-shaped, and covered in fine hairs. The inflorescence is a single, upright stem topped with a spherical cluster of flowers. Each flower has a yellow disk surrounded by white, petal-like bracts. The fruit is a lightweight, wind-dispersed seed with a tuft of hairs.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Taraxacum kok-saghyz can be distinguished by its alternate, deeply lobed leaves with a hairy underside, and its bright yellow composite flowers. It has a robust root system and produces a milky sap when damaged. It is often confused with common dandelion but has more pronounced leaf lobes and a stronger, more bitter taste.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Russian Dandelion is native to Central Asia, particularly Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. It thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained sandy or loamy soils. It is typically found at altitudes between 500 and 2,000 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Russian Dandelion blooms from late spring to early summer, typically between May and July. Seed formation occurs from late summer to early autumn, around August to September. For identification purposes, the best time to harvest is during the flowering stage, when the plant is most distinctive and the leaves are still fresh.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Taraxacum kok-saghyz belongs to the Asteraceae family, closely related to other dandelion species within the genus Taraxacum. It shares key morphological and genetic traits with species like Taraxacum officinale, differing mainly in ecological preferences and latex composition. Its phylogenetic placement highlights its role in the evolution of the genus and its medicinal significance.
