What's the taxonomical classification of Heterosmilax yunnanensis?

Heterosmilax yunnanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is positioned in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical structure of the order Liliales, the plant is further categorized into the family Smilaacaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Heterosmilax, and its unique species designation, yunnanensis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds beta-sitosterol, diosgenin, liquiritigenin, ursolic acid, and daucosterol. These specific phytochemicals contribute to the diverse pharmacological activities observed in the species. Beta-sitosterol and daucosterol are phytosterols known for their potential to assist in cholesterol management. The presence of diosgenin provides a precursor for various steroid syntheses, while liquiritigenin offers antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Additionally, ursolic acid is recognized for its significant role in supporting cellular health and metabolic functions.

As discussed in a study, eight compounds were isolated and purified from Heterosmilax yunnanensis using chromatography. These chemical constituents include beta-sitosterol (I), glycerol monopalmitate (II), daucosterol (III), hexacosanoiic acid (IV), 5-hydroxymethyl furaldehyde (V), hergapen (VI), ursolic acid (VII), and liquiritigenin (VIII). The study notes that these substances were obtained from this plant for the first time. Specifically, compounds IV, V, VI, VII, and VIII were obtained from the plants of Heterosmilax for the first time.
