Corydalis sigmantha

What's the taxonomical classification of Corydalis sigmantha?

Corydalis sigmantha belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Ranunculales. This plant is further categorized under the family Papaveraceae, which places it among a diverse group of flowering plants. Within this family, it is identified by the genus Corydalis, and its specific designation is determined by its species name, sigmantha.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Corydalis sigmantha has a perennial herbaceous structure characterized by deeply lobed, compound leaves and elongated, nectar-bearing flowers that typically feature a prominent spurred corolla. The foliage is divided into multiple narrow segments that create a delicate, fern-like appearance around the base of the plant. Its flowering stems rise above the leaf canopy to display zygomorphic blossoms that are often colored in shades of yellow or pale cream. These flowers possess a specialized nectar spur at the rear, which is a defining morphological trait of the genus. The root system consists of thickened tubers that allow the plant to store nutrients for seasonal regrowth.
