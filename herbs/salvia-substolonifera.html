Salvia substolonifera

What's the taxonomical classification of Salvia substolonifera?

Salvia substolonifera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It falls under the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Lamiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Salvia, and its specific species designation, substolonifera.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Salvia substolonifera has a decumbent to ascending growth habit characterized by quadrangular stems that produce short, creeping stolons to facilitate clonal spreading. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate with serrated margins and a textured, rugose surface. Its inflorescence consists of verticillasters arranged along an elongated spike, bearing small, tubular flowers that are often blue or lavender in hue. The plant's root system is fibrous and supports the development of these lateral runners. This morphological structure allows the species to effectively colonize open ground through both seed production and vegetative expansion.
