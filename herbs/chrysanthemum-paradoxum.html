Chrysanthemum paradoxum

Chrysanthemum paradoxum, also known as the Chinese chrysanthemum or crown daisy, is a type of flowering plant native to China and used in traditional medicine for centuries.

It has been found to have medicinal properties, particularly in its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, which have been attributed to its flavonoid and terpenoid compounds.

The health benefits of Chrysanthemum paradoxum include reducing fever, relieving headaches and sore throats, and improving digestion, as well as having potential anti-cancer properties.

In herbal preparations, Chrysanthemum paradoxum is often used in teas, infusions, and tinctures to harness its medicinal benefits, and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine to balance the body's energy and promote overall well-being.
