Aquilegia ecalcarata

What's the taxonomical classification of Aquilegia ecalcarata?

Aquilegia ecalcara belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, following the evolutionary lineage of the order Ranunculales. Within this order, the plant is a member of the family Ranunculaceae. Specifically, it is categorized under the genus Aquilegia, with its unique biological identity defined by the species name ecalcara.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Aquilegia ecalcarata can cause severe skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Contact with the plant's sap may lead to localized redness, itching, or blistering on sensitive areas of the skin. If someone accidentally consumes the plant, they might experience nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. Some individuals may also develop systemic allergic symptoms such as swelling or respiratory discomfort. Because the specific toxicity profile is not fully documented, cautious handling is always recommended to avoid these adverse effects.
