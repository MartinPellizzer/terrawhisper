Trollius japonicus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Trollius japonicus has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by basal leaves that are palmately divided into three to five deeply lobed segments and stem leaves that are smaller and more lanceolate. The plant typically reaches a height of 20 to 50 centimeters, supporting upright stems that bear solitary or clustered flowers. These flowers feature globular, cup-shaped petals that are bright yellow and surround a central cluster of numerous stamens and pistils. The foliage often displays a slightly hairy texture on the undersides of the leaf blades. This morphological structure allows the plant to thrive in moist, alpine meadow environments.

      Trollius japonicus is prepared as a decoction, a powder, or a topical paste for various medicinal uses. The dried roots and rhizomes are typically boiled in water to create a concentrated liquid extract. These dried plant parts can also be ground into a fine powder to be mixed into other substances.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Trollius japonicus can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential allergic reactions if ingested or touched directly. The plant contains protoanemonin, a chemical compound known for its ability to trigger contact dermatitis upon skin exposure. If the plant is consumed, it may lead to symptoms such as vomiting, abdominal pain, or diarrhea. Some individuals may also experience localized swelling or redness where the plant made physical contact with the body. These adverse reactions are typically managed by avoiding contact with the plant and washing any affected areas thoroughly with soap and water.
