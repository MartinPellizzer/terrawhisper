Alpinia globosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Alpinia globosa?

Alpinia globosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Zingiberales. This plant is a representative of the family Zingiberaceae, and its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement in the genus Alpinia, with the specific epithet globosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Alpinia globosa has large, elliptical green leaves that emerge from thick, fleshy rhizomes and produce globular, bright red inflorescences. Its most distinctive feature is the spherical flower head, which contains numerous small, nectar-rich blossoms nestled within colorful bracts. These inflorescences typically rise above the foliage on sturdy, upright stalks to attract specific pollinators.
