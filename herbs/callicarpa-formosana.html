Callicarpa formosana

What's the taxonomical classification of Callicarpa formosana?

Callicarpa formosana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It further falls under the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its genus, Callicarpa, and its specific species designation, formosana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Callicarpa formosana has opposite, simple leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape with serrated margins and an acute apex. The plant develops woody stems that can reach a shrubby or small tree stature depending on its environmental conditions. Small, axillary clusters of flowers emerge from the leaf axils, eventually giving way to distinctive spherical fruit clusters. These drupes transition from green to a vibrant purple or reddish hue as they mature. The overall structure is characterized by its spreading branching pattern and dense foliage.

What are the pharmacological activities of Callicarpa formosana?

This plant has anti-hyperuricemic, anti-inflammatory, hemostatic, and hematemesis treatment pharmacological activities. The chemical constituents within Callicarpa formosana work to reduce elevated uric acid levels in the bloodstream. Its potent anti-inflammatory properties help mitigate swelling and pain associated with various physiological conditions. Additionally, the plant functions as a hemostatic agent to effectively control and stop bleeding. These combined medicinal effects make it a valuable resource for treating hematemesis and other acute circulatory issues.

According to a study published by "Natural product research", Callicarpa formosana shares similar chemical constituents with C. macrophylla. The leaf extracts of C. formosana effectively reversed body weight loss, uric acid and creatinine content, and hepatic XOOD activity in hyperuricemia mice. Furthermore, the treatment reversed kidney, liver, and ankle tissues injury and inflammation induced by potassium oxonate. This study utilized UPLC/Q-TOF-MS analysis to identify 151 compounds across species. There are 14 robust known chemical markers that enable the differentiation between the five Callicarpa plants.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse array of medicinal compounds including flavonoids, phenylpropanoids, terpenoids, and ursolic acid. Among the identified flavonoids, luteolin serves as a significant component within its chemical profile. The presence of phenylpropanoids contributes to the overall therapeutic potential of the species. Additionally, various terpenoids are distributed throughout the plant tissues. These complex secondary metabolites collectively define the biological activities of Callicarpa formosana.

According to a study published by "Journal of pharmaceuatical and biomedical analysis", Callicarpa formosanae Rolfe leaves contain phenylpropanoids, flavonoids, terpenoids, and polysaccharides as main ingredients. A total of 32 small molecules were characterized in the plant, which included 6 phenylpropanoids, 7 flavonoids, and 19 terpenoids. The study also identified specific substances such as luteolin and ursolic acid within the samples. Variations were observed in the content of polysaccharides, luteolin, ursolic acid, and the total contents of terpenoids in commercial samples. This research utilized a strategy integrating HPGPC-ELSD, HPLC-PDA, UV-VIS, and UPLC-QTOF-MS/MS to analyze these components.
