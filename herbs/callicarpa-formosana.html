Callicarpa formosana

What's the taxonomical classification of Callicarpa formosana?

Callicarpa formosana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It further falls under the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its genus, Callicarpa, and its specific species designation, formosana.

Small, axillary clusters of flowers emerge from the leaf axils, eventually giving way to distinctive spherical fruit clusters. What are the pharmacological activities of Callicarpa formosana?

This plant has anti-hyperuricemic, anti-inflammatory, hemostatic, and hematemesis treatment pharmacological activities. Its chemical constituents work to lower uric acid levels in the blood to prevent gout. The plant also demonstrates significant properties in reducing swelling and systemic inflammation. Furthermore, it functions as a hemostatic agent to stop bleeding effectively. These combined medicinal actions make it a valuable resource for managing hematemesis and other vascular issues.

As discussed in a study, Callicarpa formosana shares similar chemical constituents with C. macrophylla. The leaf extracts of Callicarpa formosana were used to treat hyperuricemia mice induced by potassium oxonate. This treatment effectively reversed body weight loss and the content of uric acid and creatinine. Furthermore, it reversed hepatic XOX activity and inflammation in the kidney, liver, and ankle tissues. These pharmacological activities were identified among the 151 compounds identified through UPLC/Q-TOF-MS analysis.
