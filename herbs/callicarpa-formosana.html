Callicarpa formosana

What's the taxonomical classification of Callicarpa formosana?

Callicarpa formosana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It further falls under the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its genus, Callicarpa, and its specific species designation, formosana.

Callicarpa formosana contains medicinal properties primarily concentrated in its leaves, bark, and fruit. The small berries or fruits also hold significance in certain herbal remedies due to their specific chemical constituents. These diverse components work together to provide a wide range of therapeutic applications in folk medicine.

These interactions prevent the activation of nuclear factor kappa B (NF-kappaB), a key transcription factor in the inflammatory response. Additionally, certain phytochemicals within the species show the ability to modulate various protein kinases involved in cellular stress and oxidative damage. By disrupting these specific protein functions, the plant helps reduce systemic inflammation and oxidative stress in biological systems.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, inflammation, wounds, and various digestive ailments. Its leaves and stems contain bioactive compounds that possess significant antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Traditional medicine practitioners often apply crushed foliage directly to sores to accelerate the healing process. Some preparations are also ingested in controlled amounts to alleviate internal discomfort or stomach issues. These applications rely on the plant's ability to combat pathogens and reduce swelling in the body.

What are the herbal preparations of Callicarpa formosana?

Callicarpa formosana is prepared primarily as a decoction, a topical infusion, or a concentrated tincture derived from its leaves and bark. Practitioners often boil the plant parts in water to create a medicinal tea used to address internal ailments. For external applications, the leaves may be crushed and applied as a poultice to soothe skin irritations. Tinctures are also made by soaking the botanical material in alcohol to extract active compounds for long-term storage. These various methods allow for the targeted delivery of the plant's bioactive properties depending on the specific health concern.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Callicarpa formosana can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested or handled improperly. While the plant is often studied for medicinal properties, direct contact with its leaves or berries may trigger redness or itching in sensitive individuals. Some people might experience more severe localized reactions such as swelling or rashes upon skin exposure. If the plant material is consumed, it could potentially lead to stomach upset or nausea. Always consult a medical professional if you suspect a reaction has occurred after contact with this species.
