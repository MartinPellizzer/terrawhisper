Callicarpa formosana

What's the taxonomical classification of Callicarpa formosana?

Callicarpa formosana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It further falls under the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its genus, Callicarpa, and its specific species designation, formosana.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Callicarpa formosana contains bioactive compounds such as terpenoids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids that contribute to its pharmacological properties. These phytochemicals are primarily concentrated in the leaves and stems of the plant. Research suggests that these constituents possess significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities. Some studies also indicate that the extracts may exhibit antimicrobial effects against specific pathogens. These medicinal components make the species a subject of interest for developing natural therapeutic agents.

What are the therapeutic actions of Callicarpa formosana?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, inflammation, wounds, and various digestive ailments. Its leaves and stems contain bioactive compounds that possess significant antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Traditional medicine practitioners often apply crushed foliage directly to sores to accelerate the healing process. Some preparations are also ingested in controlled amounts to alleviate internal discomfort or stomach issues. These applications rely on the plant's ability to combat pathogens and reduce swelling in the body.
