Dalbergia odorifera

What's the taxonomical classification of Dalbergia odorifera?

Dalbergia odorifera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent, it falls under the order Fabales and the family Fabaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Dalbergia, and its unique species designation, odorifera.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Dalbergia odorifera contains bioactive secondary metabolites such as flavonoids, polyphenols, stilbenoids, and various terpenoids that provide its therapeutic properties. These chemical constituents are primarily concentrated in the heartwood and are known for their significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities. Researchers have identified specific compounds within the plant that demonstrate antimicrobial effects against various pathogenic bacteria. Some studies also suggest that these medicinal extracts possess potential neuroprotective qualities due to their ability to reduce oxidative stress in cells. Consequently, the plant is often utilized in traditional medicine to treat inflammatory conditions and skin ailments.

What are the therapeutic actions of Dalbergia odorifera?

Dalbergia odorifera has potent antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties derived from its complex chemical constituents like flavonoids and terpenoids. These bioactive compounds work to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress within biological tissues. The plant's extracts are frequently utilized in traditional medicine to treat skin infections and soothe localized inflammation. Furthermore, its bioactive profile supports the modulation of immune responses and may aid in wound healing processes. These diverse therapeutic actions make the species a significant subject for pharmacological research into natural healing agents.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat inflammatory conditions, skin infections, digestive disorders, and various types of fever. The medicinal properties of its wood and bark are frequently applied to soothe localized swelling and reduce pain. In traditional practices, extracts from the plant are utilized to manage stomach ailments and intestinal issues. It also serves as a topical agent for addressing fungal or bacterial skin ailments. These therapeutic applications rely on the specific chemical compounds found within its organic structure.

What are the herbal preparations of Dalbergia odorifera?

Dalbergia odorifera is prepared as a decoction, tincture, or infused oil using its aromatic heartwood to extract its bioactive compounds. Practitioners typically simmer the wood fragments in water to create a concentrated medicinal tea. For topical applications, the wood is often macerated in a carrier oil to produce fragrant salves or liniments. Alcohol-based extractions are also common for creating shelf-stable tinctures that preserve the plant's essential aromatic properties. These diverse methods allow the plant's chemical constituents to be utilized for various internal and external therapeutic purposes.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Dalbergia odorifera can cause contact dermatitis, allergic skin reactions, and respiratory irritation due to the presence of sensitizing quinones and various chemical compounds in its wood and dust. Individuals who handle the raw timber or breathe in fine particulates may develop itchy rashes, redness, or swelling on the skin. Some people experience heightened sensitivity where even minimal exposure triggers an inflammatory response. Inhaling the dust can also lead to coughing or discomfort in the nasal passages and throat. These adverse effects are most common among woodworkers and craftsmen who interact closely with the material without adequate protection.
