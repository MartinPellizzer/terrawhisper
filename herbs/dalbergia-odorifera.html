Dalbergia odorifera

What's the taxonomical classification of Dalbergia odorifera?

Dalbergia odorifera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent, it falls under the order Fabales and the family Fabaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Dalbergia, and its unique species designation, odorifera.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Dalbergia odorifera contains bioactive secondary metabolites such as flavonoids, polyphenols, stilbenoids, and various terpenoids that provide its therapeutic properties. These chemical constituents are primarily concentrated in the heartwood and are known for their significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities. Researchers have identified specific compounds within the plant that demonstrate antimicrobial effects against various pathogenic bacteria. Some studies also suggest that these medicinal extracts possess potential neuroprotective qualities due to their ability to reduce oxidative stress in cells. Consequently, the plant is often utilized in traditional medicine to treat inflammatory conditions and skin ailments.

What are the therapeutic actions of Dalbergia odorifera?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat inflammatory conditions, skin infections, digestive disorders, and various types of fever. The medicinal properties of its wood and bark are frequently applied to soothe localized swelling and reduce pain. In traditional practices, extracts from the plant are utilized to manage stomach ailments and intestinal issues. It also serves as a topical agent for addressing fungal or bacterial skin ailments. These therapeutic applications rely on the specific chemical compounds found within its organic structure.
