Dalbergia odorifera

What's the taxonomical classification of Dalbergia odorifera?

Dalbergia odorifera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent, it falls under the order Fabales and the family Fabaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Dalbergia, and its unique species designation, odorifera.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dalbergia odorifera has pinnately compound leaves consisting of several pairs of oval or elliptical leaflets that are typically light green and glabrous.

The tree produces small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that grow in dense axillary panicles. Its woody stems are characterized by a dark, dense heartwood that is highly valued for its aromatic properties. The fruit is a small, flat, indehiscent legume that contains one or two seeds.

This species often develops a spreading canopy with a rugged bark texture as it matures.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Dalbergia odorifera contains medicinal properties primarily derived from its heartwood and bark. The aromatic heartwood is frequently utilized in traditional practices to treat various inflammatory conditions and skin ailments. Extracts from the bark are also employed to alleviate digestive issues and serve as a tonic for overall vitality. Many preparations involve boiling these plant parts to create decoctions used for their soothing effects. These specific components are valued for their unique chemical compounds that contribute to their therapeutic efficacy.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a complex profile of bioactive medicinal compounds including flavonoids, terpenoids, phenolics, isoflavonoids, and alkaloids.

These phytochemicals contribute to its diverse pharmacological properties, such as antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. The presence of phenolic compounds and flavonoids specifically supports cellular protection against oxidative stress. Additionally, the isoflavonoids and alkaloids play significant roles in modulating biological pathways within the body.

Terpenoids further enhance the therapeutic potential by providing antimicrobial and bioactive benefits.

The primary medicinal compounds of this plant are shown in the list below.

  • Flavonoids
  • Terpenoids
  • Phenolics
  • Isoflavonoids
  • Alkaloids

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Dalbergia odorifera targets enzymes such as cyclooxygenase-2, lipoxygenase, and various pro-inflammatory cytokines to exert its medicinal effects. The bioactive compounds within the plant, particularly neoflavonoids and stilbenoids, inhibit the expression of tumor necrosis factor-alpha and interleukin-6. These interactions effectively modulate the inflammatory cascade by suppressing the production of prostaglandins and other chemical mediators. Additionally, certain extracts demonstrate an ability to interact with protein kinases involved in cell signaling pathways, which may contribute to its potential anticancer properties. By regulating these specific protein functions, the plant provides systemic anti-inflammatory and antioxidant benefits.

What are the therapeutic actions of Dalbergia odorifera?

Dalbergia odorifera has medicinal properties that function as an astringent, tonic, carminative, and antispasmodic agent.

Its astringent qualities allow it to help contract tissues and reduce inflammation in various bodily systems. As a tonic, the plant serves to restore vigor and strengthen overall physiological functions. The carminative effects assist in relieving intestinal gas and improving digestive comfort.

Additionally, its antispasmodic actions help to alleviate involuntary muscle contractions and soothe cramping.

The primary therapeutic actions of this plant are shown in the list below.

  • Astringent
  • Tonic
  • Carminative
  • Antispasmodic

What common ailments are treated using this plant?

This plant is used to treat respiratory issues like asthma, coughs, and bronchitis, as well as digestive problems, skin inflammation, and certain types of fever.

Its medicinal properties are often derived from the bark or heartwood through various traditional preparations. Practitioners frequently utilize the plant to soothe internal inflammation and improve lung function. Additionally, topical applications are sometimes employed to address localized skin irritations and wounds.

The aromatic compounds within the wood also contribute to its use in calming nervous tension.

The main common ailment treated using this plant are shown in the list below.

  • Skin diseases
  • Inflammation
  • Wound healing
  • Digestive issues

What are the herbal preparations of Dalbergia odorifera?

Dalbergia odorifera is prepared as a decoction, tincture, or infused oil using its aromatic heartwood to extract its bioactive compounds. Practitioners typically simmer the wood fragments in water to create a concentrated medicinal tea. For topical applications, the wood is often macerated in a carrier oil to produce fragrant salves or liniments. Alcohol-based extractions are also common for creating shelf-stable tinctures that preserve the plant's essential aromatic properties. These diverse methods allow the plant's chemical constituents to be utilized for various internal and external therapeutic purposes.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Dalbergia odorifera can cause contact dermatitis, allergic skin reactions, and respiratory irritation due to the presence of sensitizing quinones and various chemical compounds in its wood and dust. Individuals who handle the raw timber or breathe in fine particulates may develop itchy rashes, redness, or swelling on the skin. Some people experience heightened sensitivity where even minimal exposure triggers an inflammatory response. Inhaling the dust can also lead to coughing or discomfort in the nasal passages and throat. These adverse effects are most common among woodworkers and craftsmen who interact closely with the material without adequate protection.
