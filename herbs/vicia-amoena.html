Vicia amoena

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vicia amoena has slender, erect to ascending stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and feature pinnate leaves composed of several pairs of narrow, linear leaflets. Each leaf is topped by a single terminal tendril used for climbing and is accompanied by stipules that are often large and leaf-like. The inflorescence consists of small, papilionaceous flowers that are generally pale purple or bluish in color. These flowers are arranged in small clusters or solitary units emerging from the leaf axils. The legume fruit is a small, compressed pod that contains a limited number of seeds.
