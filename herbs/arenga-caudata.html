Arenga caudata

What's the taxonomical classification of Arenga caudata?

Arenga caudata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Arecales. Within this order, it is situated in the family Arecaceae, specifically falling under the genus Arenga, with its unique identification as the species caudata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Arenga caudata has a solitary, erect trunk that can reach heights of up to 15 meters and is characterized by a densely fibrous surface covered in persistent leaf bases. The leaves are pinnately compound, forming a large crown of long, arching fronds that can extend several meters in length. Each leaf consists of numerous linear leaflets that are arranged symmetrically along a central rachis. The plant produces large, woody inflorescences that emerge from the leaf axils, bearing numerous small, yellowish-white flowers. These flowers eventually develop into small, globose fruits that change color as they mature.
