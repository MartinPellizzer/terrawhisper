What's the taxonomical classification of Arenga caudata?

Arenga caudata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Within this order, it is situated in the family Arecaceae, specifically falling under the genus Arenga, with its unique identification as the species caudata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Arenga caudata has a solitary, erect trunk that can reach heights of up to 15 meters and is characterized by a densely fibrous surface covered in persistent leaf bases. Each leaf consists of numerous linear leaflets that are arranged symmetrically along a central rachis. The plant produces large, woody inflorescences that emerge from the leaf axils, bearing numerous small, yellowish-white flowers.
