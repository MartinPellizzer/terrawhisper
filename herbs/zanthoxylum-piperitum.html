Chinese prickly ash

Zanthoxylum piperitum

Chinese Prickly Ash (Zanthoxylum piperitum) is a deciduous shrub native to China, known for its pungent fruit and traditional use in herbal medicine to treat conditions such as digestive disorders, pain, and inflammation.

Botanical Identification

Zanthoxylum piperitum, commonly known as Chinese prickly ash, belongs to the Rutaceae family. It is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia, characterized by its spiny stems, pinnately compound leaves, and yellowish-green flowers. It is also referred to as Chinese prickly ash or Sichuan pepper in various regional contexts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Eudicots
  • Order: Sapindales
  • Family: Rutaceae
  • Genus: Zanthoxylum
  • Species: Zanthoxylum piperitum

Active Compounds

Chinese Prickly Ash contains key active compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to exert anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antimicrobial effects. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in traditional herbal medicine.

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory conditions and digestive issues.

Decoction: Used for digestive disorders and pain relief.

Powder: Used for topical applications and as a dietary supplement.

Safety Profile

Chinese Prickly Ash contains toxic compounds and should not be used during pregnancy or by individuals with ulcers. It may cause gastrointestinal irritation and should be avoided by those with sensitive stomachs. Consult a healthcare provider before use.
