What's the taxonomical classification of Vaccinium sikkimense?

Vaccinium sikkimense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Ericales. The plant is further categorized into the family Ericaceae and is specifically identified by its genus, Vaccinium, and its species, sikkimense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vaccinium sikkimense has an erect, woody shrub habit characterized by slender, reddish-brown stems and small, elliptical to ovate leaves with finely serrated margins. Small, bell-shaped flowers emerge from the axils, often appearing in clusters to attract pollinators. These blossoms eventually develop into small, dark-colored fleshy berries that are encased in a thin skin. The overall structure of the plant is compact, allowing it to thrive in various montane environments.
