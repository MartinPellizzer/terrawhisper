What's the taxonomical classification of Ajania parviflora?

Ajania parviflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. This plant is a prominent representative of the family Asteraceae, specifically situated within the genus Ajania. Its full scientific designation is completed by its specific epithet, making the species Ajania parviflora.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ajania parviflora has a woody, perennial growth habit characterized by upright, branched stems that reach heights of up to 1 meter. The leaves are small, lanceolate to ovate, and possess serrated margins with a distinctively shiny, dark green upper surface. These leaves are arranged oppositely along the stems and often exhibit a slightly revolute margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flower heads that are clustered in terminal corymb-like cymes. The texture of the foliage is often described as leathery, providing a dense, shrubby appearance to the overall structure.
