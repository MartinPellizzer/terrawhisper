Epilobium palustre

What's the taxonomical classification of Epilobium palustre?

Epilobium palustre belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Magnoliidae and is part of the order Myrtales. This plant is further categorized into the family Onagraceae, which contains the genus Epilobium. Finally, it is identified at the species level as palustre.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Epiloium palustre has an erect, branched stem that typically reaches heights of 10 to 50 centimeters and features opposite, lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is characterized by its thin, somewhat fleshy texture and a light green color. Small, four-petaled flowers emerge from the leaf axils in a terminal raceme, displaying a pale pink to white hue. These blossoms are supported by slender pedicels and contain four prominent stamens. Once pollinated, the plant develops elongated, narrow seed capsules that split open to release numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

What are the pharmacological activities of Epilobium palustre?

This plant has antimicrobial properties and demonstrates therapeutic potential in the treatment of benign prostate hyperplasia. Various bioactive compounds within the species contribute to its ability to inhibit the growth of diverse pathogenic microorganisms. Research suggests that its extracts can help manage symptoms associated with prostate enlargement by modulating physiological responses. These pharmacological activities make the plant a subject of interest for developing new herbal medicines. Further studies are necessary to fully elucidate the specific molecular mechanisms driving these biological effects.

As discussed in a study, the ethanolic extracts of Epilobium palustre were investigated for antimicrobial activity in vitro against Gram-positive and Gram-negative bacteria, yeasts, and fungi. These extracts demonstrated antimicrobial activity within a concentration range of 10 to 650 microgm/l of dry extract. The cytotoxicity of the extracts was evaluated through the Artemia salina test. The extracts were found to be devoid of toxicity on Artemia salina within the 10 to 650 microgm/l antimicrobial concentration range. This suggests that the action of the Epilobium palustre extracts is selective on microorganisms.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains ellagic acid, gallic acid, quercetin, flavonoids, and tannins which provide its medicinal properties. These specific phytochemicals work together to offer potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory benefits to the human body. The presence of flavonoids and quercetin specifically helps to neutralize harmful free radicals at a cellular level. Additionally, the tannins and organic acids contribute to the plant's astringent qualities and overall therapeutic potential. These diverse compounds make the plant a significant subject for pharmacological research.

As discussed in a study, Epilobium palustre has been traditionally used as a medicinal plant to treat benign prostate hyperplasia. The investigation focused on the content of polyphenols, tannins, and flavonoids in the species collected from Estonia. All roots, stems, leaves, and flowers of the plant contained comparable amounts of these biologically active compounds. The study utilized UV spectroscopy and subsequent HPLC quantification of gallic acid, ellagic acid, and quercetin as marker compounds. The presence of these compounds suggests that Epilobium palustre could be used as an herb either individually or as part of a blend.
