Epilobium palustre

What's the taxonomical classification of Epilobium palustre?

Epilobium palustre belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Magnoliidae and is part of the order Myrtales. This plant is further categorized into the family Onagraceae, which contains the genus Epilobium. Finally, it is identified at the species level as palustre.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Epiloium palustre contains various secondary metabolites including phenolic acids, flavonoids, and tannins that contribute to its biological activity. These chemical constituents are known to provide antioxidant properties by neutralizing free radicals within biological systems. Some research suggests that the plant extracts may possess mild anti-inflammatory effects due to the presence of specific polyphenolic compounds. Additionally, the tannins found in the foliage can act as astringents, which is a common characteristic among many species in the Onagraceae family. These medicinal components are primarily concentrated in the leaves and stems of the plant.

What are the therapeutic actions of Epilobium palustre?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various inflammatory conditions, specifically those affecting the urinary tract and the digestive system. It contains bioactive compounds that help reduce swelling and soothe irritation in the bladder and kidneys. Practitioners also utilize its properties to manage symptoms of gastrointestinal distress and mild infections. The plant acts as a natural astringent to help stabilize internal tissues during recovery. These medicinal applications are primarily rooted in its traditional use within herbal medicine.
