Triosteum pinnatifidum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Triosteum pinnatifidum has pinnately lobed leaves that are arranged alternately along a slender, upright stem reaching heights of up to one meter. The leaf margins are deeply incised, creating distinct lobes that give the foliage its characteristic feathery appearance. Small, greenish-white flowers emerge from the leaf axils to form inconspicuous clusters. These blossoms eventually transition into bright red, fleshy berries that are roughly oval in shape. The overall structure of the plant is herbaceous and somewhat spreading at the base.
