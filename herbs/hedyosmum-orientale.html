Hedyosmum orientale

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hedyosmum orientale has evergreen, leathery leaves that are elliptical to lanceolate in shape, measuring between 5 and 15 centimeters in length with entire margins and smooth surfaces. The foliage is characterized by a deep green upper surface and a slightly paler underside, often possessing a glossy texture. Small, fragrant, white flowers emerge from the leaf axils in clusters during the spring months. These blossoms feature five petals and are typically accompanied by a yellowish-green calyx. The plant develops small, fleshy, dark berries that serve as its primary fruit structure.
