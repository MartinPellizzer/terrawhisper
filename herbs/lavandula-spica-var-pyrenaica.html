Spanish Lavender (Lavandula Spica Var. Pyrenaica)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Lavandula spica var. pyrenaica.

Spanish lavender, scientifically known as Lavandula spica var. pyrenaica, is a medicinal herb native to the Iberian Peninsula, valued for its therapeutic properties and aromatic qualities. This plant is recognized as an adaptogen, helping the body manage stress and promote resilience, while its essential oils are used as a natural spice and calming agent. Traditionally, Spanish lavender has been employed in herbal medicine to treat anxiety, insomnia, and skin conditions, and it holds cultural significance in Spanish folk remedies and rituals. In modern wellness practices, it is widely used in aromatherapy, herbal teas, and topical applications for its soothing and anti-inflammatory effects. One of its unique features is the presence of a rare active compound called lavandin, which contributes to its distinctive floral aroma and enhanced therapeutic potential.
