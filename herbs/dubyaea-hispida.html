Dubyaea hispida

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dubyaea hispida has erect, woody stems and large, palmately lobed leaves that typically feature five to seven distinct lobes with coarsely serrated margins. The foliage is characterized by a rough, hairy texture on both the upper and lower surfaces, which is reflected in its specific epithet. During the flowering season, the plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in drooping, terminal panicles. These inflorescences are accompanied by specialized bracts that help protect the developing floral structures. Following pollination, the plant develops small, fleshy drupes that serve as its primary fruit type.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Dubyaea hispida can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, including vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea, due to the presence of toxic alkaloids. These compounds may also lead to neurological symptoms such as dizziness or tremors if ingested in significant quantities. The plant contains substances that can irritate the digestive tract upon contact. Prolonged exposure or high doses may potentially lead to more serious systemic complications. Careful avoidance is necessary because the exact threshold for toxicity can vary between individuals.
