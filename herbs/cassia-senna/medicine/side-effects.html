10 Possible Side Effects Of Cassia Senna

Cassia senna has some side effects when used improperly, such as diarrhea, stomach cramps, and vomiting.

These side effects are often caused by overconsumption or prolonged use of the herb, which can lead to dehydration, electrolyte imbalance, and even kidney damage.

For example, a person who takes cassia senna in excess may experience severe diarrhea, leading to a loss of fluids and electrolytes, causing dizziness, fatigue, and worsening overall health.

1. Causes diarrhea

Cassia senna causes diarrhea due to its high content of anthraquinone glycosides, particularly sennoside A and B.

These compounds stimulate the intestinal muscles to contract, increasing bowel movement frequency and reducing water absorption in the gut, leading to loose stools or watery diarrhea. The intense stimulation can also cause a sudden urge to have a bowel movement, known as tenesmus.

As Cassia senna increases the speed of food passage through the digestive system, it may also lead to cramping, bloating, and abdominal discomfort.

2. Increases chills

Cassia senna increases chills in some individuals due to its potent laxative properties.

The active ingredients, including sennosides and anthraquinones, stimulate the gut to contract and release stool more frequently than normal. This sudden and intense bowel movement can cause a drop in body temperature, leading to feelings of chilliness or shivering.

The intense stimulation of the intestines may also lead to changes in blood flow and circulation, which can contribute to this sensation.

3. Triggers abdominal pain

Cassia senna triggers abdominal pain due to its stimulant laxative properties that increase bowel movements and disrupt the natural digestive process.

The plant's anthraquinone glycosides, particularly sensosides, are responsible for this effect by irritating the gut lining, leading to increased peristalsis and evacuation of the bowels.

This irritation can cause abdominal cramps, discomfort, and pain in some individuals who use Cassia senna as a natural remedy or supplement.

4. Experiences vomiting

Cassia senna experiences vomiting due to its high sennoside content.

These glycosides stimulate the muscles in the intestinal wall, causing them to contract and move stool through the digestive system more quickly than usual. This rapid movement can irritate the stomach lining, leading to nausea and vomiting.

Additionally, the increased water absorption from the gut may also contribute to feelings of fullness and discomfort, further exacerbating nausea and vomiting.

5. Develops headaches

Cassia senna develops headaches in some individuals due to its stimulant properties.

The plant's active compounds, such as sennosides and anthraquinones, can cause increased blood flow and stimulate the central nervous system, leading to headache symptoms like pulsating or throbbing pain.

This side effect is often associated with high doses of Cassia senna or prolonged use.

6. Reports dizziness

Cassia senna reports dizziness.

The plant's active ingredients, such as sennosides A and B, can cause a decrease in blood pressure, leading to feelings of lightheadedness or dizziness.

Additionally, the stimulation of the digestive system and increased bowel movement may lead to changes in blood flow, further contributing to this side effect.

7. Feels fatigue

Cassia senna feels fatigue due to its ability to stimulate bowel movements and increase the frequency of bowel actions.

This sudden change in digestive habits can lead to a depletion of vital energy stores, causing fatigue and lethargy.

Additionally, the increased gut activity may disrupt normal sleep patterns, further exacerbating feelings of exhaustion.

8. Exhibits nausea

Cassia senna exhibits nausea due to its high content of anthraquinone glycosides, particularly sennosides A and B.

These compounds can stimulate the intestines and increase bowel movements, leading to a sense of discomfort or queasiness in some individuals.

This side effect is typically associated with high doses or prolonged use of Cassia senna products, as they can cause rapid changes in gut motility and electrolyte levels.

9. Shows stomach cramps

Cassia senna shows stomach cramps as a common side effect.

This is due to the plant's high concentration of anthraquinone glycosides, which stimulate the muscles in the digestive tract and cause spasms. Additionally, Cassia senna can irritate the mucous membranes lining the intestines, leading to increased peristalsis and cramping sensations.

These symptoms are often temporary and may be uncomfortable, but they typically resolve on their own once the body adjusts to the active ingredients of the herb.

10. Exhibits rash

Cassia senna exhibits rash due to its high content of anthraquinone glycosides, particularly sennosides A and B.

These compounds can cause skin irritation, leading to the development of a red, itchy, or blistering rash in some individuals.

The severity of the reaction may vary depending on the individual's sensitivity and the dosage taken.
