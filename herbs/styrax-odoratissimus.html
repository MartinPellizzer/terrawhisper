Styrax odoratissimus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Styrax odoratissimus has an evergreen habit characterized by a small tree or large shrub structure with opposite, lanceolate leaves that are leathery and glossy on the upper surface. The foliage is typically dark green, though it may transition to a more yellowish hue depending on environmental conditions. During the flowering season, the plant produces clusters of fragrant, white, bell-shaped blossoms that emerge from the axils of the leaves. These flowers are followed by the development of small, fleshy, globose drupes that typically turn dark blue or black when ripe. The bark of the mature specimen is relatively smooth and can take on a greyish tone as the plant ages.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause localized skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and potential respiratory discomfort if the aromatic resins or fine hairs are inhaled. Individuals with sensitive skin may experience redness, itching, or a stinging sensation upon direct contact with the leaves or bark. Some people might develop small bumps or rashes as a hypersensitive immune response to the plant's chemical compounds. While systemic toxicity is not widely documented, caution is advised when handling the specimen to prevent accidental exposure to the eyes or mucous membranes. Always wash hands thoroughly after touching the vegetation to minimize these dermatological risks.
