What's the taxonomical classification of Athyrium strigillosum?

Athyrium strigillosum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. Its taxonomic journey continues through the family Aspleniaceae, specifically within the genus Athyrium, ultimately reaching its designation as the species strigillosum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Athyrium strigillosum has large, bipinnate to tripinnate fronds that typically measure between 30 and 80 centimeters in length with lanceolate to oblong-lanceolate leaflets. The stipe is dark brown and features a distinct groove on the upper surface. Each pinna is subdivided into smaller pinnules that possess finely serrated or slightly lobed margins. The underside of the mature blades is characterized by prominent sori located on the margins, which are protected by a thin, reflexed indusium. These fern structures emerge from a creeping rhizome that is covered in pale, brownish scales.
