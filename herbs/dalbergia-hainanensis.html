Dalbergia hainanensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dalbergia hainanenensis has pinnately compound leaves consisting of several pairs of leaflets that are typically elliptic or oblong in shape. The leaflets feature entire margins and a smooth, slightly leathery texture on their surfaces. Its stems and branches exhibit a woody structure with a bark that may appear somewhat rough or fissured depending on the age of the specimen. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are characteristic of the Fabaceae family. These flowers are arranged in clusters and eventually give rise to small, legume-style pods containing the seeds.
