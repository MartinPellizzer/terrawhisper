Trifolium hybridum

What's the taxonomical classification of Trifolium hybridum?

Trifolium hybridum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent, it falls into the order Fabales and the family Fabaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by its placement in the genus Trifolium, with the specific epithet being hybridum.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Trifolium hybridum contains high concentrations of isoflavones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids that serve as the primary bioactive components. These specific phytochemicals act as potent antioxidants by neutralizing free radicals within cellular structures. The presence of phytoestrogens allows the plant to interact with estrogen receptors, which can influence hormonal balance. Additionally, certain triterpenoids found in the species contribute to its potential anti-inflammatory properties. These combined compounds suggest a therapeutic profile focused on metabolic and oxidative stress regulation.

What are the therapeutic actions of Trifolium hybridum?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including inflammatory skin conditions, digestive irregularities, and respiratory congestion. It contains bioactive compounds that help soothe irritation on the skin surface when applied topically. In traditional herbal medicine, infusions made from the plant are consumed to aid in calming gastrointestinal distress. Some practitioners also utilize it to alleviate symptoms associated with mild coughs and phlegm buildup. These therapeutic properties are often attributed to the plant's specific phytochemical profile.
