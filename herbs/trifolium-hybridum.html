Trifolium hybridum

What's the taxonomical classification of Trifolium hybridum?

Trifolium hybridum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent, it falls into the order Fabales and the family Fabaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by its placement in the genus Trifolium, with the specific epithet being hybridum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Trifolium hybridum has trifoliate leaves consisting of three elliptical to obovate leaflets that are typically bright green with indistinct or pale markings. The stems are slender and often procumbent or ascending, frequently displaying a fine pubescence along their length. Its inflorescences are compact, globular heads containing numerous small, pinkish to purplish pea-like flowers. Each individual flower is attached to the head by a short pedicel and features a standard, wing, and keel structure characteristic of the Fabaceae family. The plant also produces small, legume-style pods that develop from the fertilized ovaries after pollination.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant, known as hybrid clover, requires well-drained soil with a pH between 6.0 and 7.0 and consistent moisture levels to ensure optimal growth.

Sowing should occur in early spring or late autumn when soil temperatures are moderate and moisture is abundant. During the cultivation process, maintaining adequate nitrogen levels is less critical than managing sunlight, as the species thrives in full sun to partial shade. Regular irrigation prevents stunted development, while periodic mowing can encourage denser foliage and more vigorous regrowth.

Careful monitoring for fungal pathogens is essential to protect the health of the stand throughout the growing season.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse array of bioactive medicinal compounds including flavonoids, isofavones, and phenolic acids. According to a study published by "Molecular and cellular biochemistry", the chemical profiles of Trifolium hybridum extracts contained three or four groups of (poly)phenolic compounds such as phenolic acids, clovamides, isoflavones, and other flavonoids. These plant extracts were evaluated at concentrations ranging from 1.5-50.0 μg/ml. The antioxidant properties of the extracts were assessed based on their efficacy to reduce damage in blood platelets exposed to 100 μM peroxynitrite-induced oxidative stress in vitro. The total phenolic concentrations of the Trifolium extracts studied varied between 11.30 and 52.55 mg/g of dry mass. The study determined antioxidant actions through biomarkers including thiol groups, 3-nitrotyrosine, lipid hydroperoxides, and thiobarbituric acid-reactive substances (TBARS).
