Hippolytia kennedyi has a woody, shrubby growth habit characterized by small, ovate leaves that are typically opposite or subopposite along its stems. Its stems are slender and often exhibit a branching pattern that creates a dense, bushy structure. During flowering periods, the plant produces small, inconspicuous blooms that are characteristic of its genus.
