Jerusalem artichoke

Helianthus tuberosus

Jerusalem Artichoke (Helianthus tuberosus) is a plant from the Asteraceae family, traditionally used for its diuretic properties and digestive benefits. The tuberous roots are the primary part utilized, and it has also been historically applied for skin conditions and as a tonic to support energy and vitality. Native to North America, it holds cultural significance in various indigenous practices.

Botanical Identification

Jerusalem artichoke, scientifically known as Helianthus tuberosus, belongs to the Asteraceae family. It is a perennial plant native to eastern North America. Commonly called sunroot or topinambour, it features tuberous roots, lance-shaped leaves, and yellow flowers. Synonyms in herbal texts include earth apple and Indian topinambour.

Active Compounds

The Jerusalem Artichoke contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to provide antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory infections.

Infusion: Used for mild digestive issues and as a general tonic.

Powder: Used for digestive health and as a dietary supplement.

Safety Profile

Jerusalem artichoke is generally safe when consumed in typical food amounts. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated for those with allergies to plants in the Asteraceae family. Always consult a healthcare provider before using it for therapeutic purposes.
