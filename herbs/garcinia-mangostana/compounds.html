Mangosteen (Garcinia mangostana) Compounds

The primary compounds in Mangosteen include flavonoids, which are known for their antioxidant properties. Phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins and glycosides provide astringent and antimicrobial benefits. Terpenoids and anthocyanins add to its medicinal value by supporting immune function and skin health.

Mangosteen (Garcinia mangostana) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Mangosteen (Garcinia mangostana). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting immune function and reducing oxidative stress. These compounds are also linked to anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects in traditional herbal medicine.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Mangosteen (Garcinia mangostana). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress in the body. This compound is believed to support overall health by enhancing cellular protection and immune function.

3. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Mangosteen (Garcinia mangostana). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is believed to have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins in Mangosteen may support digestive health and skin healing, though research on their specific therapeutic applications is ongoing.

4. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Mangosteen (Garcinia mangostana). They contribute to the fruit's characteristic flavor and have shown potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds may support various health benefits, though more research is needed to confirm their efficacy in medicinal applications.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Mangosteen (Garcinia mangostana). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. Glycosides in Mangosteen are often associated with the fruit's traditional use in supporting digestive and immune health.

6. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Mangosteen (Garcinia mangostana). It contributes to the fruit's deep purple color and possesses antioxidant properties. Anthocyanin may support cardiovascular health and reduce inflammation, making it a valuable component in traditional herbal medicine.

7. Coumarin

Coumarin is a bioactive compound found in Mangosteen (Garcinia mangostana). It exhibits anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory properties, contributing to the plant's traditional use in promoting cardiovascular health and reducing inflammation. Coumarin's presence supports the therapeutic potential of Mangosteen in herbal medicine.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Mangosteen (Garcinia mangostana). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine due to its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponins may support digestive health and immune function, making them a significant component of Mangosteen's therapeutic profile.

9. Essential oil

Essential oil is a compound found in Mangosteen (Garcinia mangostana). It contains aromatic compounds like limonene and myrcene, which may contribute to its traditional use in treating digestive issues and inflammation. The essential oil is extracted from the pericarp and is valued for its potential therapeutic properties.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Mangosteen (Garcinia mangostana). It contributes to the plant's pharmacological properties, though its role in traditional herbal medicine is less emphasized compared to other bioactive components. Alkaloids in Mangosteen may have potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects, though research is ongoing.
