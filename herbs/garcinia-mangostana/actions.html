Mangosteen (Garcinia mangostana) Actions

Mangosteen exhibits several therapeutic actions including anti-inflammatory, tonic, and astringent properties. It also acts as a bitter, diuretic, and antispasmodic agent. Additionally, it has carminative, sedative, stimulant, and expectorant effects. These actions support its use in traditional herbal medicine for various health conditions.

Mangosteen (Garcinia mangostana) Monograph

1. Anti-inflammatory

Mangosteen has an anti-inflammatory action, primarily due to the presence of xanthones, which inhibit inflammatory pathways. These compounds reduce swelling and pain by modulating cytokine production. This property makes it useful in managing conditions like arthritis and inflammatory bowel disease. Its natural origin supports its role in herbal medicine for inflammation.

2. Tonic

Mangosteen has a tonic action, supporting overall vitality and energy levels. It helps balance bodily functions and enhance physical endurance. This effect is attributed to its rich content of bioactive compounds, including xanthones. Regular consumption may contribute to improved health and well-being.

3. Astringent

Mangosteen has an astringent action, which helps reduce inflammation and tighten tissues. This property is due to its high content of tannins and other bioactive compounds. It is commonly used to treat digestive issues and skin conditions. The astringent effect can also aid in wound healing and reducing excess secretions.

4. Bitter

Mangosteen has a bitter action, which stimulates digestive enzymes and enhances nutrient absorption. This bitterness also helps to clear excess dampness and heat from the body, supporting overall digestive health and metabolic function.

5. Diuretic

Mangosteen has a diuretic action, which helps increase urine production and promote the elimination of toxins from the body. This effect is attributed to its active compounds, such as xanthones, which support kidney function and fluid balance. It is often used in herbal medicine to address mild fluid retention and urinary issues.

6. Antispasmodic

Mangosteen has an antispasmodic action, which helps alleviate muscle spasms and cramps by relaxing smooth muscle tissue. This effect is attributed to its bioactive compounds, such as xanthones, which reduce inflammation and nerve irritation. It is commonly used in herbal medicine to treat gastrointestinal and muscular discomfort.

7. Carminative

Mangosteen has a carminative action, which helps relieve gas and bloating by promoting the expulsion of trapped air from the digestive tract. This effect is attributed to its bioactive compounds, which support healthy digestion and reduce gastrointestinal discomfort.

8. Sedative

Mangosteen has a sedative action, helping to calm the nervous system and reduce anxiety. Its compounds may interact with neurotransmitters to promote relaxation. This effect supports its traditional use in managing stress and insomnia. It is often combined with other herbs for enhanced calming effects.

9. Stimulant

Mangosteen has a stimulant action, which can enhance alertness and energy levels. It contains compounds that may support metabolic processes and improve mental clarity. This effect is attributed to its bioactive compounds, such as xanthones. However, it should be used cautiously to avoid overstimulation.

10. Expectorant

Mangosteen has an expectorant action, helping to loosen and expel mucus from the respiratory tract. This property makes it beneficial for alleviating symptoms of coughs and colds by promoting clearer breathing. Its natural compounds support the body's ability to clear congestion effectively.
