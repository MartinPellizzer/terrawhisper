Mangosteen (Garcinia mangostana) Uses

Mangosteen is used to treat various gastrointestinal conditions such as gastric ulcers, gastritis, and inflammatory bowel disease. It may help reduce inflammation and improve digestion. Its properties also support liver health and alleviate symptoms of gastrointestinal discomfort and motility issues.

Mangosteen (Garcinia mangostana) Monograph

1. Gastric ulcer

Mangosteen has shown potential in treating gastric ulcers due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. The fruit's pericarp contains compounds that may protect the stomach lining and reduce acid secretion. Preliminary studies suggest it could aid in ulcer healing, though more research is needed for clinical application.

2. Inflammatory bowel disease

Mangosteen has shown potential in reducing inflammation associated with inflammatory bowel disease due to its high content of xanthones, which possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Preliminary studies suggest it may help alleviate symptoms by modulating immune responses and protecting intestinal cells from damage. However, more research is needed to confirm its efficacy and safety in clinical settings.

3. Gastritis

Mangosteen has been traditionally used to support digestive health, particularly in managing gastritis. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties help reduce stomach lining irritation and combat harmful bacteria. Extracts from its pericarp may soothe inflammation and promote healing in the gastrointestinal tract.

4. Diarrhea

Mangosteen has been traditionally used to treat diarrhea due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The fruit's pericarp contains xanthones that help reduce intestinal inflammation and combat harmful pathogens. It is often prepared as a decoction or taken in supplement form to support digestive health. Regular use may help alleviate symptoms and restore gut balance.

5. Gastrointestinal inflammation

Mangosteen contains compounds like xanthones that exhibit anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds may help reduce inflammation in the gastrointestinal tract. Studies suggest they could support healing in conditions like gastritis and ulcerative colitis. Their antioxidant effects also protect the digestive lining from oxidative stress.

6. Gastrointestinal discomfort

Mangosteen has been traditionally used to alleviate gastrointestinal discomfort due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The fruit's pericarp contains compounds that may help reduce inflammation and soothe digestive tract irritation. It is often used to address symptoms like indigestion and bloating in herbal medicine practices.

7. Gastrointestinal ulcers

Mangosteen has shown potential in treating gastrointestinal ulcers due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The fruit's pericarp contains compounds that may protect the stomach lining and reduce ulcer severity. Studies suggest it could promote healing by neutralizing harmful bacteria and reducing acid production.

8. Gastroesophageal reflux disease

Mangosteen has been studied for its potential to alleviate symptoms of gastroesophageal reflux disease due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. The fruit's pericarp contains compounds that may help reduce gastric acidity and protect the esophageal lining. Preliminary research suggests it could support digestive health, though more clinical studies are needed to confirm its efficacy.

9. Liver disease

Mangosteen has been studied for its potential benefits in supporting liver health. Its antioxidants, such as xanthones, may help reduce inflammation and oxidative stress in the liver. Some research suggests it could aid in the management of liver diseases like hepatitis and fatty liver disease. However, more clinical studies are needed to confirm these effects.

10. Gastrointestinal motility issues

Mangosteen contains compounds like xanthones that may support gastrointestinal motility by reducing inflammation and improving digestive tract function. These properties may help alleviate symptoms of slow digestion and promote regular bowel movements. Research suggests potential benefits for conditions like irritable bowel syndrome and functional dyspepsia.
