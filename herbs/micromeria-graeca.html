What is Greek Micromeria (Micromeria Graeca)?

Greek Micromeria, or Micromeria graeca, is a small, aromatic shrub native to the Mediterranean region, known for its medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal medicine.

Botanical Identity and Classification

Micromeria graeca, commonly known as Greek Micromeria, is a small annual herbaceous plant belonging to the family Apiaceae, characterized by its slender stems, small compound leaves, and clusters of tiny white to greenish-yellow flowers.

Common Names and Synonyms

Common Names and Synonyms: Greek Micromeria (Micromeria graeca) is also known as Greek thyme, mountain thyme, and narrow-leaved thyme, with additional synonyms including Thymus graecus and Thymus microphylla.

Plant Description and Morphology

Greek Micromeria (Micromeria graeca) is a small, annual herb with slender, branched stems, narrow linear leaves arranged alternately, and small, pale yellow to white flowers clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Greek Micromeria (Micromeria graeca) is native to the Mediterranean region, specifically found in southern Europe, the Middle East, and parts of North Africa, with a primary distribution in Greece, Turkey, and the Levant.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The aerial parts, including the leaves and flowers, are primarily used medicinally in Greek Micromeria.

Greek Micromeria (Micromeria graeca) contains a variety of bioactive compounds, including flavonoids, phenolic acids, and essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

Pharmacological Properties
