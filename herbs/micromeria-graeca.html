Scientific and Botanical Profile

Greek Micromeria, with botanical name Micromeria graeca, is a flowering plant belonging to the Compositae family, known for its small, delicate flowers and medicinal properties.

Native to regions such as Greece, Turkey, Cyprus, the Balkans, and parts of the Middle East including Lebanon, Syria, Jordan, Israel, Iraq, the Persian Gulf, this plant is commonly referred to by various names such as Greek Fumitory, Island Caper, and Greek Mountain Tea. Morphologically, it is an annual or perennial herb with slender, branched stems, small alternate leaves, and clustered yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is also known by names like Caper Bush, Micromeria From Greece, and Cretan Micromeria, reflecting its regional significance and botanical diversity.

This plant is sometimes called Greek Honey Flower or Greek Annual Mallow, and it is also known as Caper Spurge or Greek Broom, highlighting its varied common names and ecological roles across its native range.

Micromeria graeca has a variety of forms available, including fresh leaves, dried tincture, powder, essential oil, and capsules, allowing for flexible use depending on the desired application.

For internal use, it can be prepared as a tea by steeping dried leaves in hot water, or as a decoction by simmering the plant material for a longer period, while an infusion involves using cold or room temperature water for a gentler extraction. Topical preparations, such as salves or oils, are commonly used for skin conditions and minor ailments. The recommended dosage for adults is typically 1 to 2 grams of dried herb per cup of water, taken up to three times daily, while children should only use it under medical supervision.

Due to its potent nature, it is advised to use micromeria graeca sparingly and for short durations, starting with low doses to assess tolerance and avoid potential adverse effects.

Micromeria graeca can be used as a medicinal plant with potential benefits, but it is important to be aware of its safety profile.

To preserve its potency, the harvested material should be dried in a cool, dark, and well-ventilated area, then stored in airtight glass containers in a cool, dry place or refrigerated to maintain its medicinal properties.
