Greek micromeria

Micromeria graeca

Greek Micromeria (Micromeria graeca) is a small, aromatic shrub native to the Mediterranean region, known for its traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments.

Botanical Identification

Micromeria graeca, commonly known as Greek Micromeria, belongs to the Asteraceae family. It is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. The plant has narrow leaves and small yellow flowers, with a taproot system. It is also referred to as Mediterranean dwarf daisy in some herbal texts.

Safety Profile

Greek Micromeria, or Micromeria graeca, is generally considered safe when used in typical herbal doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. There are no well-documented contraindications, but caution is advised during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to limited research on its safety in these populations.
