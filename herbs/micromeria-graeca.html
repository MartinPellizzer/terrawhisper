In terms of cultivation, Micromeria graeca is a low-maintenance and adaptable herb that thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and indoor spaces. Botanically speaking, Micromeria graeca belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and delicate, purple flowers.

The herb has a long history of use, with references dating back to ancient Greek and Roman medicinal practices, where it was valued for its aromatherapeutic and culinary properties.
