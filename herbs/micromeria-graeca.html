What is Greek Micromeria (Micromeria Graeca)?

Greek Micromeria, or Micromeria graeca, is a small, aromatic herb native to the Mediterranean region, known for its use in traditional herbal medicine for its potential digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

Plant Description and Morphology

Greek micromeria is a small, herbaceous annual plant that typically grows to a height of 10-30 cm, with a branching stem covered in fine, soft hairs.

Its leaves are narrow and linear, often slightly curled at the edges, and arranged alternately along the stem. The flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes at the ends of the branches, with tubular corollas that transition from blue-violet to white near the tip. The root system is fibrous and shallow, adapted to absorb moisture from the soil surface.

The plant has a mild, aromatic scent, with smooth, slightly waxy leaves and dry, brittle stems.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Greek micromeria is native to the mountainous regions of southern Greece, particularly the Peloponnese and the island of Crete.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is commonly found in sunny, arid or semi-arid environments. The plant prefers a Mediterranean climate with hot, dry summers and mild, wet winters. It is often associated with limestone and schist formations, growing in open woodlands or scrublands.

Its natural range extends to parts of the Balkans and the eastern Mediterranean, where similar ecological conditions exist.
