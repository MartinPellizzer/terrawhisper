Dysoxylum hongkongense

What's the taxonomical classification of Dysoxylum hongkongense?

Dysoxylum hongkongense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further organized under the order Sapindales. It is a representative of the family Meliaceae, falling specifically under the genus Dysoxylum, with its unique biological identity defined by its species name, hongkongense.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Dysoxylum hongkongense contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, terpenoids, and polyphenolic compounds that exhibit significant pharmacological potential. Research indicates that extracts from this species possess antioxidant properties capable of neutralizing free radicals in biological systems. Some studies also suggest that specific chemical constituents may demonstrate antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory activities. These medicinal properties are often attributed to the complex mixture of organic molecules found within the plant's bark and leaves. Further investigation is required to isolate individual compounds for targeted therapeutic applications.

What are the therapeutic actions of Dysoxylum hongkongense?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, intestinal parasites, and various inflammatory conditions. The bark of the species contains bioactive compounds that possess antimicrobial properties. These medicinal qualities allow it to combat fungal and bacterial pathogens on the skin surface. It is also traditionally employed to alleviate gastrointestinal distress caused by worms or other parasites. Local practitioners utilize specific decoctions to manage pain and swelling in different parts of the body.
