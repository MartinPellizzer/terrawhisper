Dysoxylum hongkongense

What's the taxonomical classification of Dysoxylum hongkongense?

Dysoxylum hongkongense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further organized under the order Sapindales. It is a representative of the family Meliaceae, falling specifically under the genus Dysoxylum, with its unique biological identity defined by its species name, hongkongense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dysoxylum hongkongense has small, alternate, simple leaves that are elliptical to oblong in shape and possess entire margins.

The foliage typically exhibits a leathery texture with a smooth surface on both the upper and lower sides. Its reproductive structures consist of inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into small, fleshy fruits. The tree is characterized by a woody stem and a bark texture that varies depending on the age of the specimen.

      Dysoxylum hongkongense is prepared as a decoction, a powder, or a topical paste using the bark and wood of the plant. There is limited clinical data regarding long-term exposure, so cautious handling is essential to avoid unpredictable adverse effects.
