Saruma henryi

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saruma henryi has perennial herbaceous stems that are typically erect, slender, and reach heights of approximately 20 to 40 centimeters. The leaves are arranged in opposite pairs along the stem and feature an ovate to lanceolate shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. Each leaf possesses a distinct petiole and a prominent central vein that extends toward the apex. The inflorescence consists of small, tubular flowers that emerge from the leaf axils in clusters. These blossoms are generally white or pale in color and serve as a key identifying feature for the species.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

These interactions primarily occur through the bioactive phytochemicals present in the plant, which modulate the arachidonic acid metabolism pathway to reduce inflammation. Additionally, its ability to suppress iNOS helps decrease the levels of nitric oxide, a key mediator in inflammatory signaling. These molecular mechanisms collectively contribute to the plant's documented anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
