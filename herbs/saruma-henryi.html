Saruma henryi

What's the taxonomical classification of Saruma henryi?

Saruma henryi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Piperales. This plant is a part of the family Aristolochiaceae, specifically situated within the genus Saruma, with its unique designation being the species henryi.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Saruma henryi targets several key proteins including cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2), inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS), and various pro-inflammatory cytokines such as tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-alpha). These interactions primarily occur through the bioactive phytochemicals present in the plant, which modulate the arachidonic acid metabolism pathway to reduce inflammation. By inhibiting the COX-2 enzyme, the plant effectively limits the production of prostaglandins that trigger pain and swelling. Additionally, its ability to suppress iNOS helps decrease the levels of nitric oxide, a key mediator in inflammatory signaling. These molecular mechanisms collectively contribute to the plant's documented anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

    What are the herbal preparations of Saruma henryi?

    Saruma henryi is prepared primarily as a decoction by boiling the dried stems and leaves in water to extract its bioactive compounds. This traditional liquid form is often consumed as a tea to address various inflammatory conditions. In some regional practices, the plant material is also processed into a topical paste for external application on the skin.
