Violet (Viola odorata) Compounds

Violet contains several key active compounds, including flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and tannins, which contribute to its astringent effects. Phenolic acids and coumarin provide anti-inflammatory benefits, while essential oils and anthocyanins support skin health. Glycosides, saponins, terpenoids, and alkaloids also play roles in its medicinal properties.

Violet (Viola odorata) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Violet (Viola odorata) are a group of bioactive compounds known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and skin conditions. They help reduce oxidative stress and support immune function. Flavonoids are also responsible for the plant's characteristic violet color.

2. Tannin

Tannin in Violet (Viola odorata) contributes to its astringent properties. It helps reduce inflammation and can soothe irritated tissues. Tannins are found in the leaves and flowers, supporting the plant's traditional use in herbal remedies. They play a key role in the plant's medicinal applications.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Violet (Viola odorata). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound helps support immune function and may aid in reducing oxidative stress. Phenolic acid is commonly found in the leaves and flowers of Viola odorata.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Violet (Viola odorata). It contributes to the plant's mild sedative and antispasmodic effects. Coumarin also has anticoagulant properties and is used in traditional medicine for respiratory and digestive ailments. Its presence enhances the therapeutic profile of Violet in herbal formulations.

5. Essential oil

The essential oil of Violet (Viola odorata) contains volatile compounds like linalool and methyl salicylate. These compounds contribute to its aromatic properties and have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. The oil is often used in aromatherapy and topical applications for its soothing and healing benefits.

6. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Violet (Viola odorata). It acts as a natural antioxidant, helping to neutralize free radicals in the body. This compound contributes to the plant's traditional use in reducing inflammation and supporting respiratory health. Its presence also gives violet flowers their distinctive purple color.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is an active compound found in Violet (Viola odorata). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. Glycosides in Violets help reduce irritation and support respiratory health. They are commonly used in herbal remedies for conditions like coughs and skin inflammation.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Violet (Viola odorata). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponins may support respiratory and skin health. They are often used in traditional herbal remedies for their cleansing and healing effects.

9. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Violet (Viola odorata) are a group of bioactive compounds known for their anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and skin conditions. They are often found in the flowers and leaves, supporting the plant's therapeutic applications in herbal medicine.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Violet (Viola odorata). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, though its specific therapeutic effects are less well-documented compared to other constituents. Alkaloids in Violets may support anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic actions, though research on their role in herbal medicine remains limited.
