White Poplar (Populus tremula) Compounds

The primary compounds in White Poplar include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and phenolic acids, known for their anti-inflammatory effects. Coumarin contributes to its mild sedative qualities, while tannins provide astringent properties. Glycosides and terpenoids support its medicinal uses, and saponins may enhance immune function. Essential oils and anthocyanins add to its therapeutic profile, though alkaloids are present in smaller amounts.

White Poplar (Populus tremula) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in White Poplar (Populus tremula) that contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, supporting the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and skin conditions. They are also believed to enhance the plant's ability to modulate immune responses.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in White Poplar (Populus tremula). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for conditions like arthritis and respiratory infections. This compound is often extracted from the bark and leaves for therapeutic applications.

3. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in White Poplar (Populus tremula). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and anticoagulant properties. Found in the bark and leaves, coumarin supports traditional uses in herbal medicine for treating conditions like arthritis and circulatory issues. Its presence enhances the therapeutic potential of White Poplar extracts.

4. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in White Poplar (Populus tremula). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins are found in the bark and leaves, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for respiratory and digestive conditions.

5. Glycoside

The glycoside in White Poplar (Populus tremula) contributes to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. It is often used in herbal medicine to treat conditions like arthritis and muscle pain. This compound supports the plant's traditional role in soothing inflammatory symptoms.

6. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in White Poplar (Populus tremula) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving properties. These compounds, such as populin and other flavonoids, help reduce swelling and support the plant's traditional use in treating joint and muscle pain. They are key components in the preparation of herbal remedies derived from the tree.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in White Poplar (Populus tremula). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. Saponins help reduce pain and inflammation, making White Poplar useful in traditional herbal medicine for treating conditions like arthritis and muscle pain.

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of White Poplar (Populus tremula) contains volatile compounds like limonene and alpha-pinene, which contribute to its aromatic profile. These compounds are often extracted through steam distillation and are used in herbal medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin, found in White Poplar (Populus tremula), is a powerful antioxidant that supports cardiovascular health and reduces inflammation. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating ailments like arthritis and respiratory conditions. Its presence in the bark and leaves highlights its importance in herbal medicine.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in White Poplar (Populus tremula). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, though its role is less prominent compared to other compounds like flavonoids and sesquiterpenes. Alkaloids in White Poplar are primarily associated with anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
