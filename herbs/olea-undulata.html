Possible Side Effects of Olea undulata

Olea undulata can have side effects if used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, due to its high levels of oleuropein and other bioactive compounds.

Additionally, using olea undulata improperly can also interact with certain medications, like blood thinners, and exacerbate underlying health conditions, like high blood pressure and diabetes.

Prolonged or excessive use of olea undulata may also cause kidney damage and liver toxicity, highlighting the importance of proper dosage and medical supervision.
