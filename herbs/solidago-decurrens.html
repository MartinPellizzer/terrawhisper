What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Solidago decurrens has a decurrent stem morphology where the leaf bases extend downward along the stem to form prominent wings. This characteristic distinguishes it from many other goldenrod species that possess distinct, non-winged stems. The leaves themselves are typically lanceolate or linear in shape and are arranged alternately along the length of the plant. During the flowering stage, the plant produces terminal panicles of small, bright yellow flower heads. The overall architecture of the plant is characterized by a somewhat spreading or ascending habit depending on the specific environmental conditions.
