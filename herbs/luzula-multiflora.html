Luzula multiflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Luzula multiflora?

Luzula multiflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, proceeding through the order Poales. Within the family Juncaceae, it is categorized under the genus Luzula, and its specific designation is the species multiflora.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Luzula multiflora has perennial, tufted growth habits characterized by narrow, linear leaves that feature prominent white longitudinal margins due to long, hair-like cilia. The plant produces multiple compact, brownish inflorescences arranged in dense clusters at the ends of upright, slender stems. Each flower is relatively small and contains six tepals that are typically dark brown or blackish in color. The leaves are often somewhat folded or channeled, creating a textured appearance throughout the foliage. This morphology allows the species to thrive in moist, shaded forest environments.
