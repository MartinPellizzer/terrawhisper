Botanical Identification of Nutgrass (Cyperus rotundus)

To identify Nutgrass (Cyperus rotundus) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Nutgrass (Cyperus rotundus) Monograph

Scientific Classification

Nutgrass, or Cyperus rotundus, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Liliopsida, order Poales, family Cyperaceae, genus Cyperus, and species Cyperus rotundus. This classification places it within the grass-like plants, highlighting its botanical relationships and characteristics.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Nutgrass is Cyperus rotundus, first described by Linnaeus in 1753. It is also known by several synonyms, including Cyperus cymosus and Cyperus glomeratus. Common names such as nutgrass, nutsedge, and purple nutsedge are widely used. Regional and historical names vary, reflecting its global distribution and long-standing use in traditional medicine.

Morphological Characteristics

Nutgrass (Cyperus rotundus) is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous growth habit, forming dense, mat-like colonies. Its root system consists of short, rhizomatous stems that spread horizontally and produce new shoots. The stems are erect, cylindrical, and often reddish or purple in color. Leaves are narrow, grass-like, and have a prominent midrib. The inflorescence is a cylindrical cluster of spikelets. Flowers are small, three-lobed, and clustered in spikelets. The fruit is a three-sided, three-lobed nutlet, which contains one to three seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Nutgrass can be distinguished by its dense, grass-like appearance with slender, cylindrical stems and small, triangular leaves. It produces spikelets in clusters, and its rhizomes are fibrous and stoloniferous, allowing it to spread aggressively. It is often confused with other Cyperus species, but its leaf shape and growth habit are key differentiators.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Nutgrass (Cyperus rotundus) is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. It thrives in warm climates with high humidity and moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils and is commonly found at low altitudes, typically below 1,000 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Nutgrass typically blooms from late spring to early autumn, with seed formation occurring during the summer months. Seeds are mature by late summer, making late summer to early autumn the optimal time for harvest when identifying the plant. This timing is crucial for accurate botanical identification in herbal medicine contexts.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Nutgrass (Cyperus rotundus) belongs to the genus Cyperus in the family Cyperaceae. It is closely related to species like Cyperus esculentus and Cyperus stoloniferus. Within the Cyperaceae family, it shares traits such as grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth, distinguishing it from other plant families.
