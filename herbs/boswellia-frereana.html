Olibanum (Boswellia frereana)

Olibanum (Boswellia frereana) is a member of the Burseraceae family, native to Sudan, Ethiopia, and Somalia. Traditionally, its resin, bark, and wood have been used for powders, infusions, and decoctions.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, bitter, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in ayurvedic medicine, african traditional medicine, and mediterranean herbal traditions.

Flowers
Inflorescences are clustered, flowers are zygomorphic with five petals, two of which are fused, and three stamens arranged in a single whorl.
Stems
Cylindrical, woody, aromatic, branching in a dichotomous pattern, with a smooth, pale grayish-brown surface, often exuding a resinous substance when injured.
