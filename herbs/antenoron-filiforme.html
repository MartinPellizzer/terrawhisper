What's the taxonomical classification of Antenoron filiforme?

Antenoron filiforme belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Caryophyllales and is a member of the family Polygonaceae. Specifically, it is categorized under the genus Antenoron, with its specific epithet being filiforme.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds 3beta-sitosterol, hyperoside, rhamnetin, rhamnetin-3-O-beta-D-galactopyranoside, and stigmasterol. These specific phytoconstituents contribute to the diverse biological activities and therapeutic potential of the species. The presence of sterols like 3beta-sitosterol and stigmasterol is often associated with anti-inflammatory and cholesterol-lowering properties. Additionally, the flavonoids hyperoside and rhamnetin, along with its glycoside derivative, provide significant antioxidant and protective effects. Together, these chemical constituents form a complex profile that supports various medicinal applications.

As discussed in a study, eleven compounds were isolated from the ethanol extract of Antenoron filiforme. These compounds include bronane-5-hydroxy-2-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside (I), adenosine (II), bonarosiide (III), rhamnetin (IV), hyperoside (V), rhamnetin-3-O-beta-D-galactopyranoside (VI), and kaempferol-3, 7-O-bis-alpha-L-rhamnopyranoside (VII). The study also identified stigmasterol (VIII), nonacosanoic acid (IX), daucosterol (X), and 3beta-sitosterol (XI). All 11 of these compounds were obtained from Antenoron filiforme for the first time.
