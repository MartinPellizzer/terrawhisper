Antenoron filiforme

What's the taxonomical classification of Antenoron filiforme?

Antenoron filiforme belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Caryophyllales and is a member of the family Polygonaceae. Specifically, it is categorized under the genus Antenoron, with its specific epithet being filiforme.

What are the herbal preparations of Antenoron filiforme?

Antenoron filiforme is prepared primarily as a decoction or infusion made from its roots, bark, or leaves to extract medicinal compounds. Practitioners often boil the dried plant parts in water for extended periods to create a potent liquid tonic. In some traditional practices, the crushed plant material is mixed with water or alcohol to form a tincture. These liquid preparations are the most common methods used to facilitate the ingestion of its active ingredients. Some local traditions may also involve using the plant in topical applications or as part of a broader herbal mixture.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Antenoron filiforme can cause gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, and potential neurotoxicity if consumed in improper quantities or without proper processing. While some traditional uses suggest medicinal benefits, the plant contains various alkaloids that may trigger adverse reactions in sensitive individuals. Excessive ingestion might lead to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal cramping due to its potent chemical composition. Furthermore, direct contact with the plant's sap or leaves can result in localized dermatitis or allergic skin responses. Because scientific studies on human toxicity are limited, the specific threshold for dangerous side effects remains poorly defined.
