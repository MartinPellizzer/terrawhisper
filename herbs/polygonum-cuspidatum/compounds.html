Japanese Knotweed (Polygonum cuspidatum) Compounds

Japanese Knotweed contains several key active compounds, including flavonoids, which have anti-inflammatory properties, and coumarin, known for its anticoagulant effects. Phenolic acids contribute to antioxidant activity, while glycosides and tannins support digestive health. Terpenoids and saponins exhibit antimicrobial properties, and alkaloids may have neuropharmacological effects. Anthocyanins and essential oils add to the plant's diverse therapeutic potential.

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Japanese Knotweed. These compounds contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, making them valuable in herbal medicine for their potential health benefits.
