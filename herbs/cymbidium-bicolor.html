Cymbidium bicolor

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cymbidium bicolor has long, leathery, strap-shaped leaves that grow from a rhizomatous base and produce large, spreading flowers characterized by a distinct bicolor pattern of pale petals and a colorful lip. The plant features an upright growth habit with thick, fleshy roots designed to absorb moisture in its native epiphytic or lithophytic habitats. Each flower consists of three sepals and two petals that often display a creamy white or light green hue, contrasting sharply with the patterned labellum. The central lip is typically marked with vibrant yellow, orange, or reddish spots and stripes to attract pollinators. These blooms are held on sturdy, arching scapes that can elevate the flowers well above the dense foliage.
