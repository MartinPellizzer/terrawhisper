What's the taxonomical classification of Cymbidium bicolor?

Cymbidium bicolor belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic progression, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. As a member of the order Asparagales, it falls under the family Orchidaceae, a group renowned for its diverse and complex floral structures. Within this family, the plant is categorized under the genus Cymbidium, and it is specifically identified by its species name, bicolor.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cymbidium bicolor has long, leathery, strap-shaped leaves that grow from a rhizomatous base and produce large, spreading flowers characterized by a distinct bicolor pattern of pale petals and a colorful lip. The plant features an upright growth habit with thick, fleshy roots designed to absorb moisture in its native epiphytic or lithophytic habitats. Each flower consists of three sepals and two petals that often display a creamy white or light green hue, contrasting sharply with the patterned labellum. The central lip is typically marked with vibrant yellow, orange, or reddish spots and stripes to attract pollinators.
