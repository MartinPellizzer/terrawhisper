Alyxia siamensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Alyxia siamensis has woody climbing stems with opposite, elliptical, leathery leaves that measure up to 15 centimeters in length and possess entire margins. The foliage is characterized by a dark green, glossy upper surface and a lighter green underside with prominent pinnate venation. Small, fragrant white flowers emerge in clusters from the leaf axils, featuring a tubular corolla typical of the Apocynaceae family. Following pollination, the plant produces fleshy, globose drupes that transition from green to a deep reddish or black color when mature. These morphological traits facilitate its survival as a liana within tropical forest ecosystems.
