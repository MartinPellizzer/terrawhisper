Dodartia orientalis

What's the taxonomical classification of Dodartia orientalis?

Dodartia orientalis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its hierarchical classification, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Lamiales, this plant is a member of the family Mazaceae. Specifically, it is classified under the genus Dodartia, with its specific identification being the species orientalis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dodartia orientalis has an erect, annual stem that typically reaches a height of 10 to 30 centimeters and features small, opposite, lanceolate leaves with entire margins. The leaves are relatively inconspicuous and decrease in size as they ascend the stem toward the inflorescence. Its flowers are small, actinomorphic, and possess five petals that are often pale or white in color. The reproductive structures include five stamens and a superior ovary that eventually develops into a small, dry fruit. This plant is characterized by its slender, delicate structure which is well-adapted to its specific arid or semi-arid habitats.
