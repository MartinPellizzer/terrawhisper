Rosa davurica

What's the taxonomical classification of Rosa davurica?

Rosa davurica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its placement in the order Rosales, the plant falls into the family Rosaceae. Ultimately, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its membership in the genus Rosa, with its unique designation being the species davurica.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Rosa davurica contains high concentrations of phenolic acids, flavonoids, tannins, and vitamin C within its fruits and various plant parts. These bioactive compounds function primarily as potent antioxidants that help neutralize free radicals in the body. The presence of specific flavonoids provides anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that may support immune health. Additionally, the organic acids found in the plant contribute to its overall therapeutic profile for digestive and metabolic support. Research continues to explore how these complex chemical constituents interact to provide holistic health benefits.

What are the therapeutic actions of Rosa davurica?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various inflammatory conditions, skin ailments, digestive disorders, and respiratory issues. Its medicinal properties are frequently applied to soothe skin irritations and promote the healing of wounds or ulcers. In traditional practices, extracts are often utilized to manage symptoms of gastrointestinal distress and stomach pain. Additionally, the plant is believed to possess qualities that help alleviate symptoms associated with coughs and other breathing difficulties. These diverse therapeutic applications stem from the various bioactive compounds found within its roots and leaves.
