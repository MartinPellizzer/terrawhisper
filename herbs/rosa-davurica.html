Rosa davurica

What's the taxonomical classification of Rosa davurica?

Rosa davurica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its placement in the order Rosales, the plant falls into the family Rosaceae. Ultimately, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its membership in the genus Rosa, with its unique designation being the species davurica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rosa davurica has arching, thorny stems with pinnate leaves that typically consist of five to seven ovate leaflets and fragrant, single or semi-double pink flowers. The plant exhibits a sprawling or climbing growth habit, often forming dense thickets through its spreading branches. Its leaves are characterized by finely serrated margins and a deep green color that provides a lush backdrop for the blooms. The flowers produce small, fleshy red hips that serve as a primary feature during the fruiting season. These hips are often clustered and remain on the plant well into the colder months.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

The species is typically found growing in temperate climates that offer well-drained soil and sufficient sunlight.
