Anaphalis flavescens

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Anaphalis flavescens has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter. The leaves are typically lanceolate or linear in shape with serrated margins and are arranged alternately along the stem. Its inflorescence consists of small, bright yellow flower heads that are grouped into dense, terminal corymbose clusters. The individual florets are small and radiate from a central disc, creating a textured appearance within the clusters. These morphological features allow the plant to thrive in the diverse grassland ecosystems of East Africa.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the high-altitude montane regions of East Africa, specifically within the mountain ranges of Kenya, Uganda, and Tanzania. It is primarily found in afro-alpine zones where rocky outcrops and subalpine moorlands provide its required habitat. The species thrives at elevations typically ranging from 2,500 to over 4,000 meters above sea level. Within these specific ecological niches, it often grows in association with other specialized high-altitude flora. Its distribution is strictly limited by these climatic constraints, making it a localized component of the East African mountain ecosystems.

What are the herbal preparations of Anaphalis flavescens?

Anaphalis flavescens is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a poultice for various medicinal applications. What side-effects this plant can have?

Anaphalis flavescens can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and localized swelling if the plant or its juices come into direct contact with sensitive tissues. While often used in traditional medicine, improper preparation or excessive consumption may lead to gastrointestinal discomfort or unexpected systemic reactions. Individuals with known sensitivities to the Asteraceae family should exercise extreme caution when handling this species. Because clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, the precise long-term effects of its chemical constituents remain poorly understood.
