Hemisteptia lyrata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hemisteptia lyrata has an erect, herbaceous growth habit characterized by opposite, lyre-shaped leaves that are typically sessile and taper toward the base. The foliage is often covered in fine hairs, providing a textured surface to the plant's stem and leaf blades. Its inflorescence consists of small, tubular flowers arranged in dense clusters at the terminal ends of the stems. The floral structure is typical of the Lamiaceae family, featuring a distinct bilabiate shape and subtle coloration. These morphological traits collectively assist in the plant's identification within its natural habitat.

      What are the herbal preparations of Hemisteptia lyrata?

      Hemisteptia lyrata is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a concentrated tincture derived from its dried aerial parts.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Hemisteptia lyrata can cause severe dermatological irritation, localized swelling, and systemic allergic reactions if the plant's sap comes into contact with human skin or mucous membranes. The chemical compounds within the foliage often trigger intense redness and a burning sensation upon topical exposure. Individuals with sensitive skin may experience blistering or long-lasting rashes that require medical intervention. Ingestion of any part of the plant poses a significant risk of gastrointestinal distress, including nausea and abdominal cramping. Some reports suggest that prolonged contact can lead to heightened sensitivity to sunlight in the affected areas.
