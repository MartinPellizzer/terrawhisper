Aster altaicus

What's the taxonomical classification of Aster altaicus?

Aster altaicus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is positioned within the order Asterales. It further falls under the family Asteraceae, a diverse group of flowering plants, and is classified under the genus Aster. Ultimately, its specific biological identity is defined by its species name, altaicus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aster altaicus has a perennial growth habit characterized by erect, unbranched stems that typically reach heights of 20 to 50 centimeters. The leaves are primarily basal and lanceolate, often featuring serrated margins and a textured surface. Toward the top of the plant, the leaves transition into smaller, narrower cauline leaves that are alternately arranged along the stem. The inflorescence consists of solitary, radiate flower heads that display bright yellow ray florets surrounding a darker central disc. Each flowering structure is supported by a distinct involucre composed of multiple overlapping bracts.
