Juncus prismatocarpus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Juncus prismatocarpus has cylindrical, unbranched stems that are typically green, slender, and reach heights of approximately 10 to 30 centimeters. The leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures or are entirely absent, giving the plant a leafless appearance. Its inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in compact clusters at the tips of the stems. The most distinguishing feature is the capsule, which is distinctly prismatic or multi-angled in shape. These capsules contain numerous small seeds that facilitate reproduction in its wetland habitat.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Juncus prismatocarpus can cause severe skin irritation, localized swelling, and intense itching upon direct physical contact with its foliage. These dermatological reactions occur because the plant contains specific chemical compounds that act as potent irritants to human tissue. If the sap enters an open wound or is accidentally ingested, it may lead to gastrointestinal distress or systemic inflammatory responses. Some individuals might also experience allergic sensitization, where subsequent exposures trigger more significant respiratory or cutaneous symptoms. It is essential to avoid handling this species without protective gloves to prevent these adverse health outcomes.
