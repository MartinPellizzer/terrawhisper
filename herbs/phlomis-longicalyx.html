Phlomis longicalyx

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phlomis longicalyx has a perennial woody base with erect, square-shaped stems that are densely covered in soft, white, woolly hairs. The leaves are oppositely arranged, ovate to lanceolate in shape, and possess a thick, velvety texture due to their trichomes. Its most distinguishing feature is the long, tubular calyx that supports the dense, verticillate clusters of flowers. The corolla is typically yellowish or pale, emerging from the protective, elongated calyx structure. This combination of hairy surfaces and elongated floral parts defines its unique morphological profile.
