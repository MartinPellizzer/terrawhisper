Polygonum aviculare

What's the taxonomical classification of Polygonum aviculare?

Polygonum aviculare belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Caryophyllales. This plant is further categorized under the family Polygonaceae, which places it within the genus Polygonum, ultimately reaching its specific identification as the species aviculare.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polygonum aviculare has prostrate or ascending stems that are often reddish, glabrous, and characterized by a distinct longitudinal ridge or keel. The leaves are small, lanceolate to ovate in shape, and possess an entire margin with an alternate arrangement along the stem. A key identifying feature is the presence of a swollen, ocreate stipule that wraps around the stem at each node. The inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense axillary clusters. These flowers are typically pink or white and develop into small, dry, one-seeded fruits known as achenes.

What are the pharmacological activities of Polygonum aviculare?

This plant has anti-inflammatory, anti-nephrolithiatric, antioxidant, hemorrhoids treating, and vasorelaxant activities. Its diverse chemical profile allows it to combat oxidative stress and reduce systemic inflammation throughout the body. The presence of specific bioactive compounds supports its use in preventing kidney stone formation through anti-nephrolithiatric properties. Additionally, its ability to relax blood vessels contributes to significant vasorelaxant activity in various physiological systems. These combined medicinal effects also make it a traditional remedy for the treatment of hemorrhoids.

As discussed in a study, Polygonum aviculare L. is a traditional Chinese medicinal herb used to treat urinary stones. A novel rhamnogalacturonan I (RG-I) called PAPN1 was isolated from the plant to assess its anti-nephrolithiatic effects. Oral administration of PAPN1 at 10, 20, and 40 mg/kg/day significantly reduced renal calcium oxalate crystal deposition in a dose-dependent manner and improved renal function. The PAPN1 substance reshaped the gut microbiota composition and markedly increased taurine levels. Furthermore, PAPN1 suppressed TNFα expression in renal tissue to attenuate inflammatory responses and crystal adhesion.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains medicinal compounds including acteoside, rosmarinic acid, flavonoids, anthraquinones, and gallic acid. These bioactive constituents work together to provide various therapeutic properties such as antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. The presence of flavonoids and gallic acid specifically contributes to the neutralization of free radicals within the body. Additionally, the anthraquinones in the plant are often utilized for their potent laxative qualities. Together, these diverse chemical elements define the complex pharmacological profile of the species.

As discussed in a study, the aerial parts of Polygonum aviculaire contain 7 identified phenolic compounds. These chemical constituents include rosmarinic acid (1), gallic acid (2), gentisiic acid 5-O-(6'-O-galloyl)-beta-D-glucopyranoside (3), caffeic acid (4), p-coumaric acid (5), ethyl caffeate (6), and acteoside (7). Specifically, compounds 1, 3, 6, and 7 were isolated from this plant for the first time. The structures of these substances were elucidated using spectroscopic analysis including IR, MS, 1H, and 13C-NMR. These results provide theoretical evidences for further bioactive investigation on the plant.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating abnormal uterine bleeding, hemorrhage, hemorrhoids, urinary stones, and nephrolithiasis. Its astringent properties help to control excessive blood loss in various clinical scenarios. The herb is frequently employed to manage heavy menstrual cycles and other forms of internal bleeding. It also assists in reducing the discomfort associated with inflamed veins like hemorrhoids. Additionally, the plant supports the elimination of kidney stones by addressing nephrolithiasis and urinary stones.
