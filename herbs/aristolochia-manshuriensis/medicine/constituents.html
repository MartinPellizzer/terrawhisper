10 Medicinal Constituents Of Aristolochia Manshuriensis (Chinese Birthwort)

Aristolochia manshuriensis, also known as Chinese birthwort, has active constituents such as aristolochic acids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids.

These constituents have been shown to exhibit anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and anti-cancer properties. They may improve lives by reducing inflammation, protecting against cell damage, and inhibiting tumor growth, potentially leading to relief from conditions like arthritis and cancer.

Regular consumption of Chinese birthwort may also promote overall well-being by enhancing immune function and fighting oxidative stress.

2. Aristolochic acid

Aristolochia manshuriensis, also known as Chinese Birthwort, aristolochic acid is a bioactive compound extracted from its leaves and stems.

It has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries to treat various ailments such as fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions. However, recent studies have revealed that aristolochic acid can cause kidney damage and increase the risk of urothelial cancer when consumed in large doses or over an extended period.

As a result, its use has been heavily restricted in many countries due to concerns over its potential toxicity.
