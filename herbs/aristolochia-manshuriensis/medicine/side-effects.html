10 Possible Side Effects Of Aristolochia Manshuriensis

Aristolochia manshuriensis, also known as Chinese birthwort, has some side effects when used improperly, such as kidney damage and nephropathy.

These side effects can occur due to the herb's high content of aristolochic acids, which can cause inflammation and scarring in the kidneys.

If left untreated, these side effects can worsen over time, leading to end-stage renal disease, the need for dialysis, and even kidney failure, significantly impacting the quality of life for those affected.

1. Causes kidney damage

Aristolochia manshuriensis, also known as Chinese birthwort, causes kidney damage due to its high levels of aristolochic acid (AA), a toxic compound that can cause nephropathy.

Prolonged consumption of the herb may lead to chronic kidney disease, and in severe cases, acute kidney failure.

The exact mechanism is unclear, but it's believed that AA disrupts the normal functioning of kidney cells, ultimately resulting in irreversible damage.

2. Induces nephrotic syndrome

Aristolochia manshuriensis, also known as Chinese birthwort, induces nephrotic syndrome due to its ability to interfere with the body's natural detoxification processes.

The plant contains aristolochic acid, a potent toxin that accumulates in the kidneys and damages their filtration mechanisms.

As a result, individuals who consume Aristolochia manshuriensis may experience kidney damage, proteinuria, and other symptoms associated with nephrotic syndrome, potentially leading to long-term renal impairment or even kidney failure.

3. Triggers gouty arthritis

Aristolochia manshuriensis, also known as Chinese birthwort, triggers gouty arthritis by increasing uric acid levels in the body.

The plant contains aristolochic acid, a toxic compound that can inhibit xanthine oxidase, an enzyme responsible for breaking down purines into uric acid.

This leads to a buildup of uric acid in the bloodstream, causing crystals to form in joints, resulting in gouty arthritis symptoms such as joint pain and inflammation.

4. Produces anemia symptoms

Aristolochia manshuriensis, also known as Chinese birthwort, produces anemia symptoms due to its high concentration of aristolochic acid.

This toxic compound can cause damage to the kidneys and urinary tract, leading to hemolytic anemia, a condition characterized by the destruction of red blood cells.

The acid's nephrotoxic properties can also impair kidney function, further exacerbating anemia symptoms such as fatigue, weakness, and pale skin.

5. Increases blood pressure

Aristolochia manshuriensis, also known as Chinese birthwort, increases blood pressure by stimulating the release of certain hormones and neurotransmitters in the body.

This stimulation can lead to a rise in blood pressure due to increased peripheral resistance and cardiac output.

The active compounds present in Aristolochia manshuriensis, such as aristolochic acid, have been found to cause vasoconstriction and tachycardia, further contributing to the increase in blood pressure.

6. Aggravates respiratory issues

Aristolochia manshuriensis, also known as Chinese birthwort, aggravates respiratory issues due to its potential to cause bronchospasm and increased mucus production.

The plant's constituents, including aristolochic acid, can stimulate the airways, leading to constriction of airways and inflammation.

This reaction can exacerbate existing respiratory conditions such as asthma or chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), making it difficult for individuals with these conditions to breathe.

7. Causes nausea and vomiting

Aristolochia manshuriensis, also known as Chinese Birthwort, causes nausea and vomiting due to its ability to interact with certain neurotransmitters in the brain, such as serotonin and dopamine.

The plant's bioactive compounds, including aristolochic acid, can stimulate the vomiting center in the brain, leading to episodes of nausea and vomiting.

This side effect is a result of the plant's unique chemical composition, which makes it a potential irritant to the gastrointestinal tract and nervous system.

8. Triggers skin rashes

Aristolochia manshuriensis, also known as Chinese Birthwort, triggers skin rashes due to its high content of aristolochic acid.

This toxic compound can cause an allergic reaction in some individuals, leading to redness, itching, and inflammation on the skin. The exact mechanism behind this reaction is not fully understood, but it's believed that aristolochic acid binds to skin proteins, causing an immune response and subsequent irritation.

As a result, people may experience uncomfortable and potentially painful symptoms, which can range from mild to severe in intensity.

9. Develops photosensitivity reaction

Aristolochia manshuriensis, also known as Chinese birthwort, develops photosensitivity reaction in some individuals.

This is attributed to its high content of aristolochic acid, a toxic compound that can cause skin hypersensitivity and increased sensitivity to sunlight.

When exposed to UV light, this acid triggers an inflammatory response, leading to symptoms such as redness, itching, and blistering on sun-exposed areas.

10. Exacerbates liver disease

Aristolochia manshuriensis, also known as Chinese birthwort, exacerbates liver disease due to its high content of aristolochic acid, a potent nephrotoxin and hepatotoxin.

This toxic compound can cause damage to the liver cells, leading to inflammation, scarring, and impaired liver function.

The prolonged use or excessive consumption of Aristolochia manshuriensis can further exacerbate liver disease in individuals with pre-existing conditions or those who are vulnerable due to genetic factors.
