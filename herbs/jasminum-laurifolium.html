Jasminum laurifolium

What's the taxonomical classification of Jasminum laurifolium?

Jasminum laurifolium belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical structure, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized under the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Oleaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Jasminum and is specifically designated as the species laurifolium.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Jasminum laurifolium has opposite, simple, lanceolate leaves that measure approximately 5 to 15 centimeters in length and possess entire margins with acute tips.

The plant typically exhibits a woody, climbing, or sprawling growth habit characterized by slender, twining stems. Its flowers are small, white, and tubular, often appearing in clusters or solitary arrangements during the blooming season. The foliage often retains a glossy texture and a deep green coloration, reminiscent of laurel leaves.

This morphological structure allows the species to effectively navigate through dense vegetation to reach sunlight.
