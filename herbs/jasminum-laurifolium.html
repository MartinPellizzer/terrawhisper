Jasminum laurifolium

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Jasminum laurifolium has opposite, simple, lanceolate leaves that measure approximately 5 to 15 centimeters in length and possess entire margins with acute tips. The plant typically exhibits a woody, climbing, or sprawling growth habit characterized by slender, twining stems. Its flowers are small, white, and tubular, often appearing in clusters or solitary arrangements during the blooming season. The foliage often retains a glossy texture and a deep green coloration, reminiscent of laurel leaves. This morphological structure allows the species to effectively navigate through dense vegetation to reach sunlight.
