Ilex corallina

What's the taxonomical classification of Ilex corallina?

Ilex corallina belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Aquifoliales and the family Aquifoliaceae, ultimately placing it within the genus Ilex, where it is identified as the specific species corallina.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ilex corallina has small, elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and possess slightly serrated margins. The plant typically exhibits a shrubby growth habit with woody stems that provide structural support. Each leaf is arranged oppositely along the branches, creating a dense foliage pattern. The surface of the foliage often has a waxy cuticle which helps in moisture retention. Small, inconspicuous flowers may emerge from the axils of the leaves during its growing season.
