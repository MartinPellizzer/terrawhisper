Ilex corallina

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ilex corallina has small, elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and possess slightly serrated margins. The plant typically exhibits a shrubby growth habit with woody stems that provide structural support. Each leaf is arranged oppositely along the branches, creating a dense foliage pattern. The surface of the foliage often has a waxy cuticle which helps in moisture retention. Small, inconspicuous flowers may emerge from the axils of the leaves during its growing season.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the coastal regions of the western Mediterranean Sea, specifically occurring along the shores of Spain, France, and Italy. It thrives primarily in rocky maritime habitats where it can access both sunlight and salt spray. Within these coastal zones, the species is often found clinging to cliffs or growing in crevices near the water's edge. Its distribution is closely tied to the specific environmental conditions provided by the Mediterranean climate. This limited range makes it a characteristic element of the local coastal flora.

      What are the herbal preparations of Ilex corallina?

      Ilex corallina is prepared primarily as a decoction or an infusion using the dried leaves and stems to extract its bioactive compounds. These aqueous extracts are often administered orally to leverage the plant's specific phytochemical profile. In some traditional practices, a concentrated tincture made with alcohol is used for a more potent and shelf-stable medicinal application.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Ilex corallina can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested or handled improperly. Contact with the plant's surfaces may lead to redness, itching, or localized dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Some users might experience nausea or abdominal pain if parts of the plant are consumed accidentally. It is important to avoid direct skin contact to prevent these unpredictable physiological responses. Always exercise caution and consult medical professionals if any adverse symptoms occur after exposure.
