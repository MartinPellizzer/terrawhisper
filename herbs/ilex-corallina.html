Ilex corallina

What's the taxonomical classification of Ilex corallina?

Ilex corallina belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Aquifoliales and the family Aquifoliaceae, ultimately placing it within the genus Ilex, where it is identified as the specific species corallina.

    What are the molecular targets of this plant?

    Ilex corallina targets cyclooxygenase enzymes, lipoxygenase, and various pro-inflammatory cytokines to exert its medicinal effects. The bioactive compounds found within this plant act by inhibiting these specific proteins to reduce the production of prostaglandins and leukotrienes. By interfering with the arachidonic acid metabolism pathway, the plant helps mitigate systemic inflammation and pain. Additionally, certain phytochemicals in the species may interact with nitric oxide synthase to regulate vascular responses. These multi-target interactions contribute to the plant's potential applications in treating inflammatory disorders.

    What are the therapeutic actions of Ilex corallina?

    Ilex corallina has potent antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties that stem from its high concentration of bioactive phenolic compounds and flavonoids. These therapeutic actions work by neutralizing free radicals and reducing oxidative stress within biological tissues. The plant's phytochemical profile allows it to inhibit the growth of various pathogenic microorganisms, making it a candidate for natural antiseptic applications. Furthermore, its ability to modulate inflammatory pathways can help alleviate swelling and cellular damage in affected areas. Researchers continue to investigate these specific chemical constituents to better understand their potential in developing new pharmacological treatments.

    What diseases this plant treats?

    This plant is used to treat skin infections, inflammatory conditions, and various digestive ailments. Its medicinal properties are primarily attributed to its high concentration of bioactive compounds that offer antimicrobial effects. People often apply topical preparations of the plant to soothe irritations or manage minor wounds. Additionally, traditional practices involve using extracts to alleviate internal discomfort and promote overall healing. These therapeutic applications highlight the plant's versatility in folk medicine.

    What are the herbal preparations of Ilex corallina?

    Ilex corallina is prepared primarily as a decoction or an infusion using the dried leaves and stems to extract its bioactive compounds. These aqueous extracts are often administered orally to leverage the plant's specific phytochemical profile. In some traditional practices, a concentrated tincture made with alcohol is used for a more potent and shelf-stable medicinal application. Topical preparations such as poultices or washes may also be created from the crushed plant material for external use. Each preparation method is selected based on the desired speed of absorption and the specific therapeutic goal.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Ilex corallina can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested or handled improperly. Contact with the plant's surfaces may lead to redness, itching, or localized dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Some users might experience nausea or abdominal pain if parts of the plant are consumed accidentally. It is important to avoid direct skin contact to prevent these unpredictable physiological responses. Always exercise caution and consult medical professionals if any adverse symptoms occur after exposure.
