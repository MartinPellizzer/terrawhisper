Iris tectorum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Iris tectorum has a rhizomatous root system, sword-shaped leaves with parallel venation, and complex flowers characterized by three upright standards and three drooping falls. The foliage consists of narrow, ensiform leaves that grow in dense clumps from the underground rhizome. Each flower features a distinct color pattern on the falls, often decorated with intricate veining or spots that guide pollinators. The reproductive structures include a central ovary and three stamens positioned beneath the style branches. These morphological traits allow the plant to thrive in its specific ecological niche while attracting specialized insects.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude alpine and subalpine regions of the central and eastern Himalayas, specifically within parts of India, Nepal, and Bhutan. It thrives in rocky crevices and montane meadows at elevations typically ranging from 3,000 to 4,500 meters. Its distribution is strictly limited to these cold, high-elevation ecosystems where soil drainage is rapid. The species is often found on steep slopes and scree fields throughout its mountain range. These specific environmental requirements restrict its occurrence to isolated pockets of the Himalayan massif.
