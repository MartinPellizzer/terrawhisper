Astragalus aksuensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Astragalus aksuensis has a perennial growth habit characterized by ascending or erect stems that are typically covered in dense, fine hairs. The leaves are pinnately compound with numerous small, oblong leaflets that exhibit a grayish-green coloration. Its inflorescence consists of compact racemes bearing pea-like flowers that vary in color from white to pale pink. The calyx is distinctly hairy, providing protection to the developing floral structures. The fruit is a small, legume-type pod that contains several seeds essential for the plant's reproduction.
