Side Effects of betula pubescens

Betula pubescens induces allergic reaction in individuals who are sensitive to the plant, leading to symptoms such as sneezing, nasal congestion, and eye redness.

The herb can also trigger skin rash and irritation, particularly when applied topically, causing redness, itching, and inflammation. In more severe cases, exposure to Betula pubescens may result in respiratory issues, including coughing, breathing difficulty, and even asthma-like symptoms.

Some people may experience nausea, stomach ache, or fatigue as systemic responses to the herb. Additionally, it can cause eye irritation, further emphasizing the need for caution when handling or consuming this plant.

These side effects highlight the importance of proper identification and cautious use of Betula pubescens, especially for those with known allergies or sensitivities.
