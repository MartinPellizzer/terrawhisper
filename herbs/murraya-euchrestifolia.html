Murraya euchrestifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Murraya euchrestifolia?

Murraya euchrestifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further organized into the order Sapindales. It is a member of the family Rutaceae, which falls under the genus Murraya, and is specifically identified by its species name, euchrestifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Murraya euchrestifolia has a woody shrub or small tree structure characterized by glossy, dark green, trifoliate leaves that measure approximately 5 to 15 centimeters in length. Following the flowering stage, the plant develops small, fleshy drupes that transition from green to a bright red color when ripe. What are the pharmacological activities of Murraya euchrestifolia?

This plant has significant pharmacological properties including antiplatelet activity and the ability to facilitate apoptosis induction. These medicinal characteristics are primarily attributed to the presence of various bioactive secondary metabolites found within its leaves and stems. The antiplatelet effects suggest a potential role in preventing thrombosis and improving cardiovascular health. Furthermore, its capacity to induce apoptosis indicates that the plant may possess anticancer properties by triggering programmed cell death in malignant cells. Such diverse biological activities highlight the therapeutic potential of Murraya euchristifolia in modern drug development.

As discussed in a study, girinimbine isolated from Murraya euchrestifolia acts as an antiplatelet agent. In washed rabbit platelets, it inhibited arachidonic acid-, collagen-, U46619-, and platelet-activating factor-induced aggregation and ATP release with IC50 values of 9.1 +/- 1.5, 16.7 +/- 1.7, 60.0 +/- 5.1, and 71.9 +/- 5.6 microM, respectively. While it did not affect thrombin-induced aggregation even at 80 microM, it inhibited the formation of TxB2 and PGD2 caused by AA with a maximal effect at 20 microM. At 20 microM, girinimbine inhibited [3H]inositol monophosphate generation and the increase in intracellular Ca2+ stimulated by AA and collagen. Furthermore, platelet cyclic AMP levels were elevated by high concentrations of girinimbine at 20 and 80 microM.
