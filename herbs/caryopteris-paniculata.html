Caryopteris paniculata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Caryopteris paniculata has opposite, simple, lanceolate leaves that measure approximately one to three inches in length and possess serrated margins with a slightly hairy texture. The stems are woody at the base and become more herbaceous toward the tips, often displaying a grayish-green hue. During the blooming season, the plant produces terminal panicles of small, tubular, blue to lavender flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These fragrant blossoms are held on branched clusters that create a dense visual display above the foliage. The overall growth habit forms a mounding, multi-stemmed shrub that typically reaches two to three feet in height and width.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Caryopteris paniculata can cause skin irritation or allergic contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals through direct physical contact with its foliage. While generally considered non-toxic to humans, some people may experience redness, itching, or a mild rash after touching the leaves or stems. There is limited clinical data regarding its safety, so caution is advised for those with known plant allergies. Pets may also experience mild gastrointestinal upset if they ingest any part of the shrub. Always monitor for any adverse reactions when handling this plant in a garden setting.
