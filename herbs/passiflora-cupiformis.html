Passiflora cupiformis

What's the taxonomical classification of Passiflora cupiformis?

Passiflora cupiformis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Malpighiales and the family Passifloraceae, eventually reaching its specific designation within the genus Passiflora. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its unique species name, cupiformis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Passiflora cupiformis has climbing stems with axillary tendrils, deeply three-lobed leaves, and cup-shaped flowers that feature a prominent corona of filaments. The foliage consists of alternate leaves with long petioles and distinctively serrated margins. Its floral structure is characterized by a wide, shallow perianth that forms a bowl-like shape. The reproductive organs are centrally located within this cup, supported by a well-developed andisogamous structure. Once pollination occurs, the plant produces fleshy fruits that develop from the inferior ovary.

How is this plant cultivated?
