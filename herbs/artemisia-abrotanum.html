Artemisia Abrotanum Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Artemisia abrotanum, commonly known as mugwort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its aromatic leaves and yellow flowers.

This herb has been traditionally used for its calming and digestive properties, offering benefits such as aiding sleep, reducing inflammation, and supporting respiratory health.

The therapeutic actions of mugwort are attributed to bioactive constituents like flavonoids, sesquiterpene lactones, and volatile oils.

Herbal preparations such as infusions, tinctures, and essential oils can be made from mugwort to harness its medicinal properties.
