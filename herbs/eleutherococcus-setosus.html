What's the taxonomical classification of Eleutherococcus setosus?

Eleutherococcus setosus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, following the evolutionary lineage of the order Apiales. Within this framework, the plant is a member of the family Araliaceae and is specifically categorized under the genus Eleutherococcus, with its unique identity defined by the species name setosus.

Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge in clusters from the axils of the branches during the growing season.
