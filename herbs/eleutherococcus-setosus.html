Eleutherococcus setosus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eleutherococcus setosus has woody, branched stems characterized by the presence of numerous small, sharp prickles and palmate leaves divided into several distinct leaflets. The leaves are typically dark green and possess serrated margins that contribute to their textured appearance. Each leaflet is obovate or elliptical in shape and displays a slightly leathery texture on the upper surface. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge in clusters from the axils of the branches during the growing season. Following pollination, the plant produces small, fleshy berries that change color as they reach maturity.
