Maranta bicolor

What's the taxonomical classification of Maranta bicolor?

Marantra bicolor belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows a specific evolutionary lineage that places it under the order Zingiberales. Within this order, the plant is categorized into the family Marantaceae, which further narrows down to the genus Marantra. Ultimately, its unique biological identity is defined by its specific designation as the species bicolor.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Maranta bicolor has elliptical, lanceolate leaves that feature a dark green upper surface and a strikingly contrasting reddish-purple underside. The foliage typically measures between 15 and 30 centimeters in length and displays prominent longitudinal venation. These leaves are arranged in a basal rosette pattern, emerging from a central rhizome to create a lush, spreading appearance. The plant also produces small, tubular flowers that emerge from the leaf axils, often exhibiting delicate white or pale pink hues. This distinct bicolor pattern serves as a key identification feature for the species within its tropical habitat.
