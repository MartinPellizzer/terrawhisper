Pinus yunnanensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Pinus yunnanensis?

Pinus yunnanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Pinidae and the order Pinales. This plant is a member of the family Pinaceae and falls under the genus Pinus, ultimately identified by the specific species name yunnanensis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Pinus yunnanensis contains various bioactive compounds including terpenoids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids that exhibit significant pharmacological activities. These chemical constituents, particularly the essential oils and resinous components, possess notable antimicrobial and antioxidant properties. Research suggests that specific diterpenes found in the plant can aid in reducing inflammation and protecting cellular structures from oxidative stress. Additionally, certain extracts have demonstrated potential in modulating immune responses and supporting cardiovascular health in experimental models. The synergistic effect of these diverse secondary metabolites makes the plant a subject of interest for developing new therapeutic agents.

What are the therapeutic actions of Pinus yunnanensis?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat respiratory conditions including cough, asthma, and bronchitis, as well as various skin ailments and inflammatory issues. The essential oils extracted from its needles possess strong antimicrobial and antiseptic properties that help combat infections. When applied topically, it can soothe muscle aches and reduce swelling caused by joint inflammation. In traditional practices, the plant is also utilized to aid digestion and alleviate symptoms of gastrointestinal discomfort. These therapeutic effects are primarily attributed to the high concentration of terpenes found within its resin and foliage.
