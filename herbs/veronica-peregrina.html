What's the taxonomical classification of Veronica peregrina?

Veronica peregrina belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Lamiales. This plant is a member of the family Plantaginaceae, which houses the genus Veronica, and is specifically identified as the species peregrina.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Veronica peregrina has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins and a decussate arrangement on hairy, upright stems. The foliage is typically soft to the touch due to a covering of fine pubescence. Small, blue to violet flowers emerge from terminal racemes, featuring four petals and a distinct white center. These blooms are characterized by a single, prominent stamen that extends slightly beyond the corolla. The plant often develops a branched structure as it matures, spreading through both seed production and rhizomatous growth.
