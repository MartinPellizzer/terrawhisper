Duperrea pavettifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Duperrea pavettifolia?

Duperrea pavettifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Gentianales and is a member of the family Rubiaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific genus, Duperrea, and its species designation, pavettifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Duperrea pavettifolia has ovate to elliptical leaves that are typically 5 to 12 centimeters long, featuring entire margins and an acute apex.

The leaf texture is often somewhat coriaceous, providing a sturdy structure to the foliage. The venation pattern is prominent, with secondary veins branching out towards the leaf edges in a structured manner. Its stems are woody and can exhibit varying degrees of pubescence depending on the specific specimen.

The plant's overall architecture is characterized by its shrubby growth habit common to the Rubiaceae family.

        What are the herbal preparations of Duperrea pavettifolia?

        Duperrea pavettifolia is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice depending on the specific medicinal application required. The leaves are most commonly boiled in water to create a concentrated liquid extract used for internal consumption. When treating skin ailments or localized inflammation, the plant material is crushed and applied directly to the affected area as a warm compress. Some traditional practices also involve macerating the plant in alcohol or oil to produce a tincture or infused oil for long-term storage. These diverse methods allow practitioners to tailor the strength and delivery of the plant's active compounds to the patient's needs.
