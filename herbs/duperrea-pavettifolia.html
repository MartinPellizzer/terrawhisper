Duperrea pavettifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Duperrea pavettifolia has ovate to elliptical leaves that are typically 5 to 12 centimeters long, featuring entire margins and an acute apex. The leaf texture is often somewhat coriaceous, providing a sturdy structure to the foliage. The venation pattern is prominent, with secondary veins branching out towards the leaf edges in a structured manner. Its stems are woody and can exhibit varying degrees of pubescence depending on the specific specimen. The plant's overall architecture is characterized by its shrubby growth habit common to the Rubiaceae family.
