What's the taxonomical classification of Cerasus serrulata?

Cerasus serrulata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Rosales. Furthermore, it is a member of the family Rosaceae, which serves as the broader group for its specific classification under the genus Cerasus and the species serrulata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cerasus serrulata has small, oval-shaped leaves with finely serrated margins, woody stems with smooth bark, and clusters of small white or pink five-petaled flowers. The foliage typically displays a bright green hue during the growing season and may turn reddish in autumn. Its branches are slender and somewhat spreading, supporting the weight of small, fleshy drupes that develop from the blossoms. These fruits are often dark red or black when fully ripe and contain a single central pit. The overall structure of the plant is that of a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its delicate flowering habit.
