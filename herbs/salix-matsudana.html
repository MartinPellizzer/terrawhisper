Salix matsudana

What's the taxonomical classification of Salix matsudana?

Salix matsudanana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the biological order Malpighiales and is a member of the family Salicaceae. Finally, it is placed within the genus Salix, specifically identified by the species epithet matsudanana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Salix matsudanana has lanceolate leaves that are typically 3 to 8 centimeters long, featuring finely serrated margins and a smooth, glabrous surface. The leaves are characterized by a distinctively long, tapering apex and a base that may be slightly asymmetrical. The stems of this willow species are often slender and can exhibit a yellowish-green hue during active growth periods. Small, catkin-like flowers emerge from the branches, appearing as cylindrical clusters before the foliage fully develops. This morphological structure allows the plant to thrive in moist, riparian environments.

What are the pharmacological activities of Salix matsudana?

This plant has lipolytic activity that facilitates the breakdown of fats within the body. This specific pharmacological property suggests that Salix matsudanana may play a role in metabolic regulation. Researchers often investigate such biological actions to understand the potential medicinal uses of willow species. Studying these chemical interactions helps in identifying how the plant influences lipid profiles. Further exploration could lead to the development of natural compounds for weight management or metabolic health.

As discussed in a study, three constituents were isolated from the leaves of Salix matsudana for the first time. These constituents were identified as apigenin-7-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside, luteolin-7-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside, and luteolin-3'-methyl ether-7-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside. The study used rat's epididymal lipocytes to observe how these constituents influence norepinephrine-induced lipolysis. Two of these identified constituents can increase the release of free fatty acids (FFA) during the lipolysis process promoted by norepinephrine.

What are the herbal preparations of this plant?

This plant can be prepared as a decoction by boiling the bark in water to extract its medicinal salicin compounds, a tincture by soaking the plant parts in high-proof alcohol, or an infusion by steeping the leaves in hot water.
