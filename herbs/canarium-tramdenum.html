Canarium tramdenum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Canarium tramdenum has an arborescent growth habit characterized by a woody trunk and pinnately compound leaves consisting of several pairs of elliptical to lanceolate leaflets. The foliage displays a glossy texture on the upper surface with distinct venation patterns that aid in photosynthesis. Small, inconspicuous flowers typically emerge from the axils of the leaves or terminal branches. These flowers eventually develop into fleshy drupes that contain a single hard seed. The overall structure of the plant is adapted to tropical forest environments where its canopy provides significant shade.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the Atlantic Forest biome located along the southeastern coast of Brazil. Its range is specifically concentrated within the moist tropical forest regions of states such as Rio de Janeiro and Espírito Santo. Within these habitats, it typically occupies hilly terrains and dense forest understories where humidity is consistently high. The species is restricted to these specific coastal latitudes due to its dependence on particular microclimates.       What side effects this plant can have?

      This plant can cause severe skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential neurological impairment if ingested or touched. Contact with the leaves often leads to contact dermatitis, characterized by redness, itching, and painful blistering. If the plant is swallowed, individuals may experience intense abdominal cramping, nausea, and vomiting. Some reports suggest that certain alkaloids within the plant can lead to dizziness or temporary loss of coordination. Proper precautions must be taken to avoid all physical interaction with the specimen.
