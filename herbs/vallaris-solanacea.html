Vallaris solanacea

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vallaris solanacea has a climbing, woody habit characterized by slender, twining stems that often produce small, aerial roots to secure itself to surfaces. The foliage consists of simple, opposite leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical in shape with acute or obtuse tips. These leaves feature a smooth, entire margin and a glossy, dark green upper surface. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often displaying a pale yellow or cream coloration. The plant eventually produces small, globose berries that transition in color as they mature.
