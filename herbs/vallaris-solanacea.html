Vallaris solanacea

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vallaris solanacea has a climbing, woody habit characterized by slender, twining stems that often produce small, aerial roots to secure itself to surfaces. The foliage consists of simple, opposite leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical in shape with acute or obtuse tips. These leaves feature a smooth, entire margin and a glossy, dark green upper surface. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often displaying a pale yellow or cream coloration. The plant eventually produces small, globose berries that transition in color as they mature.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Vallaris solanacea can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and potential digestive distress if any part of the plant is ingested. The milky sap contained within the stems and leaves acts as a primary irritant to human skin upon contact. Individuals with sensitive skin may experience redness, itching, or localized swelling after touching the plant. If the plant is accidentally consumed, it can lead to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. Proper precautions should be taken to avoid direct contact and accidental ingestion to prevent these adverse effects.
