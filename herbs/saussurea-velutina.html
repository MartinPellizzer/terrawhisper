Saussurea velutina

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea velutina has a basal rosette of lanceolate leaves that are covered in dense, velvety, white hairs on both surfaces. These leaves feature prominent longitudinal veins and slightly serrated margins that aid in moisture retention. The flowering stem is typically upright and carries a compact inflorescence composed of numerous small, yellow flower heads. Each individual floret is surrounded by involucral bracts that exhibit a soft, felt-like texture. The overall structure of the plant is adapted to withstand harsh, high-altitude environments through its thick pubescence.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude alpine regions of the eastern Himalayas and the Hengduan Mountains within China. It typically grows in rocky crevices and scree slopes located at elevations ranging from 3,000 to 4,500 meters. Its distribution is concentrated in specific mountainous provinces such as Yunnan and Sichuan. These areas provide the cool, moist, and well-drained environments necessary for its survival. The species follows a fragmented pattern dictated by the rugged topography of the Tibetan Plateau's edges.

What are the herbal preparations of Saussurea velutina?

Saussurea velutina is prepared as a decoction, a powder, or a tincture for various medicinal applications.
