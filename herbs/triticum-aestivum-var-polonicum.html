Triticum aestivum var. polonicum

Triticum aestivum var. polonicum, also known as Polish wheat or Kamut, is a type of ancient grain that is a variant of the common wheat plant (Triticum aestivum).

It is valued for its nutritional and medicinal properties, including its ability to soothe digestive issues and reduce inflammation, making it beneficial for those with conditions such as celiac disease or irritable bowel syndrome.

The health benefits of Triticum aestivum var. polonicum include its high fiber content, which can aid in weight management and promote regular bowel movements, as well as its potential to lower cholesterol levels and improve blood sugar control.

Triticum aestivum var. polonicum can be prepared as an herbal supplement, flour, or sprouted grain, allowing for various uses in cooking and medicine.
