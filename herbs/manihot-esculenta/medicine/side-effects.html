10 Possible Side Effects Of Manihot Esculenta (Cassava)

Manihot esculenta has some side effects when used improperly, such as cyanide poisoning, which can occur due to inadequate cooking or peeling.

Prolonged consumption of cyanide-rich cassava can lead to headaches, nausea, and respiratory problems. In severe cases, it can even cause death.

For people who rely on cassava as a staple food, these side effects can worsen their quality of life, especially in communities with limited access to healthcare and alternative food sources.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Manihot esculenta if used imporperly.

1. Increases anxiety levels

Manihot esculenta causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

This is due to the presence of certain proteins and glycoproteins found in the plant's tubers, leaves, and stems. These allergenic compounds can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling.

The severity of these reactions can vary widely depending on individual sensitivity and the amount of cassava consumed.

The consumption of cassava can cause an imbalance in the gut microbiome, leading to inflammation and hypersensitivity reactions.

Additionally, cassava contains a type of lectin called Manihotin, which can stimulate the immune system and trigger allergic responses, exacerbating asthma symptoms.
