10 Health Benefits Of Manihot Esculenta (Cassava)

Manihot esculenta, also known as cassava, has health benefits such as lowering blood pressure and cholesterol levels due to its high levels of fiber, potassium, and magnesium.

Its antinflammatory and antimicrobial properties also reduce the risk of heart disease, diabetes, and certain cancers.

For example, a diet rich in cassava has been shown to improve cardiovascular health, allowing people to enjoy increased energy levels and a longer lifespan, ultimately improving their overall quality of life.

This article explains in details the 10 best health benefits of Manihot esculenta.

1. Reduces inflammation

Manihot esculenta reduces inflammation because it contains various bioactive compounds with anti-inflammatory properties.

Cassava's soluble fiber, including saponins and oligosaccharides, inhibits pro-inflammatory enzymes and cytokines, while its antioxidant flavonoids, such as quercetin and kaempferol, scavenge free radicals. Additionally, cassava's phytochemicals may modulate the gut microbiome to reduce inflammation.

This suggests cassava's potential as a natural anti-inflammatory agent in preventing or managing chronic inflammatory diseases.

2. Lowers blood sugar

Manihot esculenta lowers blood sugar because of its rich content of resistant starch, a type of fiber that is not fully digested in the small intestine.

Resistant starch acts as a prebiotic, feeding beneficial gut bacteria, which in turn produces short-chain fatty acids that inhibit the absorption of glucose from the intestines, leading to reduced blood sugar levels and improved insulin sensitivity.

This effect is beneficial for managing diabetes.

3. Boosts immune system

Manihot esculenta boosts immune system because it is rich in vitamin C, which plays a crucial role in enhancing the body's natural defense against infections.

Additionally, cassava contains fiber and antioxidants that help protect against oxidative stress and inflammation, further supporting the immune system's function.

Its nutritional profile contributes to overall well-being and may aid in preventing illnesses.

4. Kills bacteria

Manihot esculenta kills bacteria because of its high content of phytochemicals, particularly cyanogenic glycosides.

These compounds are released when the cassava is cut, damaged, or cooked, and they have antimicrobial properties that inhibit bacterial growth.

The cyanide ions produced from the breakdown of these glycosides are toxic to bacteria, making cassava a natural antibacterial agent effective against a range of microorganisms.

5. Relieves arthritis pain

Manihot esculenta relieves arthritis pain because of its anti-inflammatory properties.

The root contains compounds like cyanogenic glycosides and saponins, which have been shown to reduce inflammation and alleviate joint pain. Additionally, cassava's antioxidant properties help protect the body from oxidative stress, which can contribute to chronic pain and inflammation.

By incorporating cassava into one's diet, individuals may experience relief from arthritis symptoms.

6. Supports bone health

Manihot esculenta supports bone health because it is a rich source of calcium, vitamin K, and other essential nutrients that promote bone density and strength.

The high levels of magnesium in cassava also help to regulate mineral balance, reducing the risk of osteoporosis and fractures. This nutrient profile makes cassava a valuable addition to a diet aimed at maintaining strong bones.

Regular consumption can help mitigate bone loss and disease.

7. Treats fever

Manihot esculenta treats fever because of its antipyretic properties.

The tuberous root of the cassava plant contains compounds such as cyanogenic glycosides and flavonoids that have been shown to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, helping to reduce body temperature and alleviate symptoms associated with fever.

Traditional medicine practitioners have long used cassava to treat fever, and its effectiveness has been validated through various studies.

8. Combats parasites

Manihot esculenta combats parasites because its roots contain cyanogenic glycosides, which are toxic to various parasites, including nematodes and tapeworms.

These compounds are released when the roots are ingested, allowing them to interfere with the parasites' digestive systems and ultimately killing them.

As a result, cassava consumption has been used as a natural method to prevent and treat parasitic infections in tropical regions.

9. Relieves constipation

Manihot esculenta relieves constipation because it is a rich source of dietary fiber.

The fiber content in cassava helps to soften and move stool through the digestive tract, facilitating regular bowel movements.

Additionally, cassava contains resistant starch, which serves as a prebiotic, feeding the good bacteria in the gut and promoting a healthy gut microbiome, further supporting digestive regularity.

10. Soothes skin issues

Manihot esculenta soothes skin issues because of its high antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

The root's extract is rich in compounds like flavonoids and saponins, which help to calm irritated skin and reduce redness. Cassava's anti-inflammatory effects can also aid in soothing conditions like eczema, psoriasis, and dermatitis, while its antioxidant properties promote collagen production and improve skin elasticity.

This makes cassava a potential natural remedy for various skin problems.
