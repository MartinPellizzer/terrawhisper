1. Causes allergic reactions

Manihot esculenta causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

This is due to the presence of certain proteins and glycoproteins found in the plant's tubers, leaves, and stems. These allergenic compounds can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling.

The severity of these reactions can vary widely depending on individual sensitivity and the amount of cassava consumed.

2. Triggers asthma attacks

Manihot esculenta triggers asthma attacks due to its high content of FODMAPs (Fermentable Oligo-, Di-, Mono-saccharides, and Polyols).

The consumption of cassava can cause an imbalance in the gut microbiome, leading to inflammation and hypersensitivity reactions.

Additionally, cassava contains a type of lectin called Manihotin, which can stimulate the immune system and trigger allergic responses, exacerbating asthma symptoms.

3. Induces nausea symptoms

Manihot esculenta induces nausea symptoms due to its high concentration of toxic compounds, particularly cyanide and solanine.

These toxins are naturally present in the plant's leaves, stems, and roots and can be released during processing or cooking, causing gastrointestinal upset and discomfort.

The severity of these symptoms varies depending on individual tolerance and the method used to prepare the cassava product.

4. Increases heart rate

Manihot esculenta increases heart rate due to its stimulatory properties, which can cause an elevation in blood pressure and cardiac output.

This is attributed to the presence of certain compounds like cyanogenic glycosides, which are metabolized into cyanide, a potent sympathomimetic agent that mimics the effects of adrenaline on the cardiovascular system.

As a result, individuals consuming Manihot esculenta may experience an increase in heart rate, palpitations, and other cardiac-related symptoms.

5. Lowers blood pressure

Manihot esculenta lowers blood pressure due to its high content of flavonoids and saponins.

These bioactive compounds help relax blood vessels, reducing peripheral resistance and allowing blood to flow more easily through the circulatory system. Additionally, the fiber content in Manihot esculenta may also contribute to its hypotensive effect by slowing down digestion and absorption of nutrients, which can further reduce blood pressure levels.

As a result, regular consumption of Manihot esculenta may lead to a decrease in systolic and diastolic blood pressure.

6. Disrupts thyroid function

Manihot esculenta disrupts thyroid function due to its potential to interfere with iodine absorption.

Cassava contains a naturally occurring compound called linamarin, which can break down into cyanide when ingested.

High levels of cyanide in the body can inhibit the production of thyroxine, a hormone produced by the thyroid gland, leading to hypothyroidism and other thyroid-related disorders.

7. Interferes with iron absorption

Manihot esculenta interferes with iron absorption due to its high content of phytates and oxalates.

These compounds can bind to iron in the gut, reducing its availability for absorption and potentially leading to iron deficiency or anemia.

The interference may be more significant when consuming large amounts of cassava or combining it with other iron-poor foods in the diet.

8. Produces skin rashes

Manihot esculenta produces skin rashes due to its allergenic properties.

The sap of the plant contains a toxic compound called linamarin, which can cause an allergic reaction in some individuals, leading to redness, itching, and rashes on the skin.

Additionally, cross-reactivity with other substances may trigger an immune response, exacerbating the rash.

9. Affects liver enzymes

Manihot esculenta affects liver enzymes by altering their activity and expression.

This is due to the presence of cyanogenic glycosides, such as linamarin and lotaustralin, which are broken down into toxic compounds during digestion. These toxins can bind to liver enzymes, impairing their function and leading to changes in metabolite processing.

As a result, individuals consuming cassava may experience alterations in their liver's ability to detoxify and process medications, potentially influencing overall health and well-being.

10. Causes stomach pain

Manihot esculenta causes stomach pain due to its high content of raffinose, a complex sugar that can be difficult for the body to digest.

Additionally, the presence of cyanogenic glycosides in cassava can break down into toxic compounds like hydrogen cyanide and prussic acid when ingested, potentially irritating the digestive tract and leading to stomach discomfort.
