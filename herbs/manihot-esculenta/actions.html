Cassava (Manihot esculenta) Actions

Cassava exhibits several therapeutic actions, including anti-inflammatory, diuretic, and tonic properties. It acts as a carminative and astringent, helping to relieve gas and reduce secretions. Additionally, it has antispasmodic and expectorant effects, aiding in digestion and respiratory health. Cassava also serves as a stimulant and sedative, offering both energizing and calming benefits.

Cassava (Manihot esculenta) Monograph

1. Anti-inflammatory

Cassava has an anti-inflammatory action, primarily due to its high content of calcium and other minerals that help reduce inflammation in the body. It also contains compounds that may inhibit inflammatory pathways, supporting its traditional use in treating conditions like arthritis and skin inflammation.

2. Diuretic

Cassava has a diuretic action, promoting the elimination of excess fluids from the body. This effect is attributed to its ability to increase urine production and reduce water retention. It may support kidney function and help manage conditions like edema. However, it should be used cautiously in individuals with kidney disease.

3. Tonic

Cassava has a tonic action, stimulating the body's vital functions and enhancing overall vitality. It is used to restore strength and energy, particularly in cases of fatigue or weakness. Its properties help support the immune system and promote general well-being. Cassava is often incorporated into traditional herbal formulations for its restorative effects.

4. Carminative

Cassava has a carminative action, helping to relieve gas and bloating by stimulating digestion and reducing intestinal fermentation. It promotes the expulsion of trapped air from the digestive tract, aiding in the relief of discomfort associated with indigestion and flatulence.

5. Astringent

Cassava has an astringent action, which helps reduce secretion and inflammation in the digestive tract. It is used to treat diarrhea and other gastrointestinal issues. The compound responsible for this effect is primarily calcium oxalate. This property makes it useful in traditional herbal medicine for digestive health.

6. Antispasmodic

Cassava has an antispasmodic action, which helps relieve muscle spasms and cramps by relaxing overactive muscles. It is traditionally used to ease symptoms of digestive disorders and menstrual cramps. The compound responsible for this effect is likely due to its alkaloid content, which interacts with nerve signals to reduce spasms.

7. Expectorant

Cassava has an expectorant action, helping to loosen and expel mucus from the respiratory tract. It is used in traditional medicine to alleviate symptoms of coughs and bronchitis by promoting the clearance of phlegm. Its active compounds stimulate the secretion of fluids in the airways, aiding in respiratory relief.

8. Stimulant

Cassava has a stimulant action, increasing metabolic rate and energy levels. It contains compounds that may enhance alertness and physical activity. This effect is due to its nutrient composition and mild thermogenic properties. However, it should be used cautiously to avoid overstimulation.

9. Bitter

Cassava has a bitter action, which is associated with its ability to stimulate digestion and clear heat from the body. This property is often used in traditional medicine to treat conditions like indigestion and inflammation. The bitterness also helps in detoxifying the system and balancing internal humors.

10. Sedative

Cassava has a sedative action, which can help reduce anxiety and promote relaxation. It contains compounds that may influence the nervous system, contributing to a calming effect. This property makes it useful in traditional herbal medicine for managing stress and sleep disorders.
