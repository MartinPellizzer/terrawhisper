Cassava (Manihot esculenta) Uses

Cassava is used to treat various gastrointestinal issues including diarrhea, constipation, and inflammation. It helps manage stomach ulcers and infections by soothing the digestive tract. Its properties also support liver health and improve gut motility, making it beneficial for overall digestive wellness.

Cassava (Manihot esculenta) Monograph

1. Diarrhea

Cassava is used to treat diarrhea due to its antidiarrheal properties. The root contains compounds that help firm stools and reduce intestinal inflammation. It is often prepared as a paste or decoction. Traditional use highlights its role in alleviating digestive discomfort.

2. Constipation

Cassava root is used to treat constipation due to its high fiber content, which promotes bowel movement. It also contains enzymes that aid digestion and reduce intestinal blockage. In traditional herbal medicine, it is often prepared as a decoction or poultice to stimulate gut activity and relieve discomfort.

3. Gastrointestinal inflammation

Cassava root contains compounds that exhibit anti-inflammatory properties, making it useful for alleviating symptoms of gastrointestinal inflammation. It helps reduce mucosal irritation and promotes healing in the digestive tract. Traditionally, it has been used to soothe conditions like gastritis and ulcerative colitis. Its mild antispasmodic effects also aid in relieving digestive discomfort.

4. Poisoning

Cassava poisoning occurs from consuming inadequately processed cassava, which contains cyanogenic glycosides. These compounds release cyanide when ingested, leading to symptoms like nausea, dizziness, and in severe cases, respiratory failure. Proper processing, such as soaking and fermenting, reduces toxin levels and makes cassava safe for consumption.

5. Stomach ulcers

Cassava contains compounds that may help protect the stomach lining and reduce inflammation. It is traditionally used to support digestive health and may aid in the healing of stomach ulcers due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. However, it should be used with caution, as raw cassava can be toxic.

6. Gastrointestinal infections

Cassava has been used traditionally to treat gastrointestinal infections due to its antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce inflammation and supports digestion, making it effective for conditions like dysentery and food poisoning. Its high fiber content also aids in cleansing the digestive tract.

7. Gastrointestinal disorders

Cassava is used to treat gastrointestinal disorders due to its mild laxative properties and ability to soothe digestive tract inflammation. It helps relieve constipation and reduce symptoms of indigestion. The root's starch content also aids in absorbing excess gas and promoting regular bowel movements.

8. Gastrointestinal motility issues

Cassava has been traditionally used to support gastrointestinal motility by promoting digestion and reducing bloating. Its fiber content aids in regulating bowel movements, while its anti-inflammatory properties may ease digestive discomfort. It is often prepared as a decoction or poultice to address sluggish digestion and abdominal distension.

9. Liver disease

Cassava has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to support liver health. It contains compounds that may help detoxify the liver and reduce inflammation. Preliminary studies suggest it could aid in managing conditions like hepatitis and fatty liver disease. However, more research is needed to confirm its efficacy and safety.

10. Gastrointestinal issues

Cassava is used to treat gastrointestinal issues such as indigestion and bloating due to its mild laxative properties. It helps regulate bowel movements and soothe digestive discomfort. The root's high starch content also supports nutrient absorption and gut health. It is often prepared as a porridge or soup for digestive relief.
