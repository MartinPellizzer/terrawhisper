What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Manihot Esculenta (Cassava)?

Manihot esculenta, also known as cassava, has health benefits such as lowering blood pressure and cholesterol levels.

The medicinal constituents of cassava include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are responsible for its pharmacological properties. The medicinal preparations of cassava include root extracts used in traditional medicine for treating fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions. However, excessive consumption can cause cyanide poisoning and other side effects.

Precautions should be taken to properly prepare and consume cassava to avoid adverse reactions.

This article explains the health benefits, active constituents, medicinal preparations, possible side effects, and precautions related to Manihot esculenta .

What are the health benefits of Manihot esculenta ?

Manihot esculenta, also known as cassava, has health benefits such as providing a rich source of carbohydrates, making it an excellent energy booster.

It is also high in fiber, which can help regulate blood sugar levels and promote digestive health.

Additionally, cassava contains various minerals and antioxidants that can help protect against oxidative stress and inflammation, supporting overall cardiovascular health and well-being.

What are the active constituents of Manihot esculenta ?

Manihot esculenta, also known as cassava, has active constituents such as cyanogenic glycosides, starch, and anthocyanin.

The cyanogenic glycosides, particularly linamarin and lotaustralin, possess anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The high starch content contributes to its high energy value and makes it a staple food in many cultures.

Anthocyanin, a pigment responsible for its purple color, exhibits antioxidant activity and has potential benefits for cardiovascular health and anti-inflammatory responses.

What are the medicinal preparations of Manihot esculenta ?

Manihot esculenta, also known as cassava, has medicinal preparations such as a paste applied topically to treat skin conditions like eczema and itching.

In some cultures, cassava roots and leaves are used to make infusions and decoctions to treat fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues.

Additionally, the leaves and roots contain cyanogenic glycosides which have been researched for their potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, making them a subject of interest in traditional medicine.

What are the possible side effect of using Manihot esculenta improperly?

Improper use of Manihot esculenta, also known as cassava, increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as cyanide poisoning, which can cause headaches, dizziness, and nausea.

Long-term consumption can also lead to weight gain, digestive issues, and potentially, kidney damage due to the high levels of oxalates present in the root.

Furthermore, high doses of cassava have been linked to neurological problems and reproductive issues.

What precautions to take when using Manihot esculenta medicinally?

Before using Manihot esculenta, also known as cassava, for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as carefully preparing and processing the root to avoid high levels of cyanide, a toxic compound.

Ensure proper cooking or treatment to break down the toxic compounds, and also be aware of any potential allergic reactions or interactions with other medications.

Consult a healthcare professional for guidance on safe usage.
