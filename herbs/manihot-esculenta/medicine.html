What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Manihot Esculenta (Cassava)?

Manihot esculenta, also known as cassava, has health benefits such as lowering blood sugar levels and reducing inflammation.

The root contains various bioactive compounds, including saponins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. Cassava is used to prepare traditional medicines, such as infusions, decoctions, and poultices, to treat various ailments. However, excessive consumption of cassava can lead to cyanide poisoning.

It is essential to note that cassava should only be consumed in moderation and under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

This article explains the health benefits, active constituents, medicinal preparations, possible side effects, and precautions related to Manihot esculenta.

What are the health benefits of Manihot esculenta?

Manihot esculenta has health benefits such as being rich in fiber, vitamins, and minerals.

It is a good source of antioxidants, which help to protect the body against free radicals that can cause cell damage and contribute to chronic diseases like cancer and heart disease.

Cassava is also low on the glycemic index, making it an ideal food for people with diabetes or those who want to manage their blood sugar levels naturally.

What are the active constituents of Manihot esculenta?

The health benefits of Manihot esculenta comes from its active constituents, such as saponins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids.

These bioactive compounds have been reported to possess antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties.

Additionally, cassava contains fiber, vitamins, and minerals like potassium, magnesium, and iron, which contribute to its potential health benefits in preventing diseases such as diabetes, hypertension, and cardiovascular disorders.

What are the medicinal preparations of Manihot esculenta?

Manihot esculenta has different medicinal preparations, such as starch, flour, and juice.

The starch is used to treat diarrhea, dysentery, and other gastrointestinal disorders due to its antidiarrheal properties. Cassava flour is used in traditional medicine to treat various health issues, including skin problems, wounds, and respiratory infections. The juice of the plant has been used to treat fever, rheumatism, and as an antidote for snakebites.

Additionally, some preparations are used to alleviate symptoms of malaria, while others have anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties.

What are the possible side effect of using Manihot esculenta improperly?

Improper use of Manihot esculenta increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as nausea and stomach cramps due to cyanide toxicity.

Consuming excessive amounts can lead to headaches, vomiting, and diarrhea. Severe cases may cause neurological symptoms like numbness, weakness, and tingling in hands and feet. In extreme scenarios, cassava poisoning can result in respiratory failure, coma, and even death.

Additionally, improper processing or preparation methods can lead to allergies, skin rashes, and eye irritation.

What precautions to take when using Manihot esculenta medicinally?

Before using Manihot esculenta for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as proper identification and authentication of the plant to ensure it is not contaminated with toxic compounds.

It is also essential to follow correct preparation methods, including cooking or processing to remove toxic cyanogenic glycosides, and consult with a healthcare professional to determine the appropriate dosage and potential interactions with other medications.
