Piptanthus nepalensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Piptanthus nepalenxis has small, actinomorphic flowers characterized by a tubular corolla that flares into five distinct lobes. The plant typically exhibits a creeping or prostrate growth habit with slender, branching stems. Its leaves are usually simple, oppositely arranged, and possess an entire margin with a slightly fleshy texture. The inflorescence often emerges from the leaf axils, presenting delicate blossoms that are often white or pale in color.
