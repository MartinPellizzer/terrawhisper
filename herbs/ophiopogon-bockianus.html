Ophiopogon bockianus

Ophiopogon bockiaanus has evergreen, linear, grass-like leaves that form dense, tufted clumps and reach heights of approximately 10 to 20 centimeters. During its flowering period, the plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that typically emerge in shades of white or pale lavender. These blossoms are followed by the development of small, dark blue or black fleshy berries that hang beneath the leaves. While specific clinical data on human toxicity is limited, many plants in the Asparagaceae family may contain saponins that act as mild irritants. It is important to exercise caution and avoid direct contact if you have known plant allergies.
