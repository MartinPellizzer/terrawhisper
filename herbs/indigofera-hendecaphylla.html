What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Indigofera hendecaphylla has a perennial woody shrub habit characterized by erect stems and pinnately compound leaves consisting of small, oblong leaflets. The foliage typically presents a bright green color with a smooth texture on both the upper and lower surfaces. Small, papilionaceous flowers emerge in axillary racemes, often displaying shades of pale yellow or white. The plant produces elongated, linear pods that contain several small, rounded seeds. These structural features are adapted to its specific tropical or subtropical growing environments.

Indigofera hendecaphylla can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or gastrointestinal distress if handled or consumed improperly. Therefore, cautious handling and professional guidance are necessary when interacting with this species.
