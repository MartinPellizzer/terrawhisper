Conyza Canadensis Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Conyza canadensis, commonly known as horseweed, is an annual flowering plant native to North America, characterized by its small yellow flowers and hairy leaves.

This herb is valued for its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which contribute to its traditional use in supporting respiratory and digestive health.

The bioactive constituents in Conyza canadensis include flavonoids, terpenoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its therapeutic actions.

Herbal preparations such as infusions, tinctures, and poultices can be made from the leaves and flowers to harness its medicinal benefits.
