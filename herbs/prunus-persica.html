Peach Tree (Prunus persica)

Peach Tree (Prunus persica) is a member of the Rosaceae family, native to Middle East, Southwest Asia, and Central Asia. Traditionally, its fruits, bark, and seeds have been used for decoctions, infusions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, tonic, and diuretic actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, traditional chinese medicine, and japanese kampo medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically reaches a height of 4 to 8 meters.
Leaves
Simple leaves with acute apex, entire margin, and a prominent midvein, having a glossy dark green upper surface and a lighter, slightly hairy lower surface.
Flowers
Flowers are solitary, actinomorphic, with five pink to white petals, five sepals, and a yellow stamen cluster surrounded by a hairy calyx.
Stems
Erect growth habit with simple branching, glabrous surface, and presence of stipitate nodes.
