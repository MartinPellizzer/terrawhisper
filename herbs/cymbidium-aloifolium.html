Cymbidium aloifolium

What's the taxonomical classification of Cymbidium aloifolium?

Cymbidium aloifoliuum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Asparagales. As a member of the family Orchidaceae, it falls into the genus Cymbidium, specifically identified by its species name, aloifolium.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cymbidium aloifoliuum has elongated, lanceolate leaves that grow in a distichous arrangement and large, pendulous inflorescences bearing numerous fragrant, waxy flowers in shades of white or pale green.

The leaves are typically leathery in texture and can reach significant lengths, providing a dense base for the plant's growth. Each flower features a complex labellum and prominent sepals that contribute to its elegant appearance. The stems are sturdy enough to support the weight of the heavy, drooping flower spikes during the blooming season.

This morphology allows the plant to thrive in specific shaded forest environments where its structure can capture filtered light.
