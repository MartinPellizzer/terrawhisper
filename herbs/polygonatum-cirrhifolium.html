Polygonatum cirrhifolium

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polygonatum cirrhifolium has slender, climbing rhizomes that produce long, arching stems equipped with specialized tendril-like structures at the leaf bases for climbing. The leaves are typically lanceolate or elliptic in shape, arranged alternately along the stem, and possess entire margins. These plants feature small, bell-shaped, or tubular flowers that hang downward from the leaf axils. The inflorescence is often solitary or in small clusters, showcasing a delicate white or pale green coloration. Once pollination occurs, the plant develops small, fleshy berries that transition from green to a dark color upon maturity.
