Ribes tenue

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ribes tenue has slender, deciduous shrubs characterized by thin, woody stems and small, palmately lobed leaves that typically feature three to five distinct lobes. The foliage is often finely serrated along the margins and possesses a relatively delicate texture compared to other species in the genus. Small, bell-shaped flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves, usually appearing in clusters during the blooming season. These blossoms are typically pale or greenish in color and serve as a primary attractant for various pollinators. The plant also produces small, fleshy berries that develop from the fertilized flowers.
