Oxybaphus himalaicus

What's the taxonomical classification of Oxybaphus himalaicus?

Oxybaphus himalaicus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression through the order Caryophyllales, it is placed into the family Nyctaginaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Oxybaphus, and its unique species designation, himalaicus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Oxybaphus himalaicus has a slender, erect stem that typically reaches heights of 20 to 60 centimeters and features small, lanceolate leaves arranged alternately along the stalk. Its flowers are small and pea-like, often displaying shades of white or pale pink with distinct yellow markings on the standard petal. These blooms emerge from axillary clusters and are supported by fine, hairless pedicels. The plant also produces small, flattened legume pods that contain several tiny seeds once the flowering cycle concludes.

What are the pharmacological activities of Oxybaphus himalaicus?

This plant has anti-inflammatory and cytotoxic pharmacological activities that make it a subject of interest for medicinal research. Scientific investigations into its chemical constituents have revealed its potential to suppress inflammatory responses in biological systems. Furthermore, the presence of specific bioactive compounds allows the plant to exhibit significant cytotoxic effects against certain cancer cell lines. These dual properties suggest that extracts from the species could play a role in developing new therapeutic agents. Ongoing studies aim to further isolate the precise molecules responsible for these biological actions.

According to a study published by "Fitoterapia", Oxybaphus himalaicus alleviates diabetic kidney disease by suppressing lipid metabolism and inflammation via PPARα signaling. The ethyl acetate (EA) fraction of the plant was identified through network pharmacology as the main active component for treating this condition. In vivo and in vitro experiments using streptozotocin-induced DKD mice and high glucose-induced HK-2 cells showed that EA reduces renal lipotoxicity. This is achieved by upregulating PPARα pathway proteins to enhance fatty acid oxidation (FAO) and downregulating inflammatory factors such as TNF-α and IL-6. Molecular docking studies revealed that rotenoid compounds within the EA fraction have a high affinity for PPARα. Ultimately, Oxybaphus himalaicus mitigates disease by activating PPARα to augment FAO, abate lipid accumulation, and impede the expression of inflammatory factors.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds Himalaflavone A-E, Himalaflavones F, Himalaflavones G, Boeravinone C, and 3-O-feruloyl sucrose. These specific chemical constituents are identified as major bioactive markers within the species Oxybaphus himalaicus. The presence of these diverse flavonoids and phenolic derivatives contributes to the plant's unique pharmacological profile. Researchers study these molecules to understand their potential therapeutic applications in human health. Detailed chemical profiling helps categorize the medicinal strength of this high-altitude vegetation.

According to a study published by "Natural product research", fifteen compounds were isolated from the roots of Oxybaphus himalaicus. These included five previously undescribed flavonoids (1-5) and ten known ones (6-15). The cytotoxic activities of all isolated compounds were assayed against human lung carcinoma A549 cells and human hepatocellular carcinoma HepG2 cells in vitro. Compound 11 displayed the most powerful inhibitory effect among the isolated substances. This compound achieved IC50 values of 19.47 ± 0.28 μM against A549 and 52.6 ± 0.38 μM against HepG2.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating acute kidney injury, diabetic kidney disease, nephritis, and various forms of renal injury. It serves as a traditional remedy to support kidney function and mitigate damage caused by metabolic imbalances. Researchers often examine its properties to see how it might alleviate the progression of DKD in patients. By targeting inflammation, the plant helps manage the physiological symptoms associated with nephritic conditions. Its application in herbal medicine focuses specifically on restoring stability to impaired renal systems.

According to a study published by "Antioxidants (Basel, Switzerland)", the roots of Oxybaphus himalaicus Edgew. have long been used in Tibetan folk medicine for the treatment of nephritis. This plant extract was studied for its ability to mitigate lipopolysaccharide-induced acute kidney injury (AKI). The extract showed significant anti-inflammatory activity by reducing the release of nitric oxide and the mRNA level and protein expression of inflammatory cytokines. UPLC-MS/MS analysis tentatively identified 41 components in the Oxybaphus himalaicus extract. These results indicate that the extract alleviated LPS-induced AKI in mice by inhibiting TLR4/MD2 complex formation.
