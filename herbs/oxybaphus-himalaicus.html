Oxybaphus himalaicus

What's the taxonomical classification of Oxybaphus himalaicus?

Oxybaphus himalaicus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression through the order Caryophyllales, it is placed into the family Nyctaginaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Oxybaphus, and its unique species designation, himalaicus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Oxybaphus himalaicus has a slender, erect stem that typically reaches heights of 20 to 60 centimeters and features small, lanceolate leaves arranged alternately along the stalk. The foliage consists of narrow, linear-shaped leaflets that provide a delicate texture to the plant's overall appearance. Its flowers are small and pea-like, often displaying shades of white or pale pink with distinct yellow markings on the standard petal. These blooms emerge from axillary clusters and are supported by fine, hairless pedicels. The plant also produces small, flattened legume pods that contain several tiny seeds once the flowering cycle concludes.

What are the pharmacological activities of Oxybaphus himalaicus?

This plant has anti-inflammatory and cytotoxic pharmacological activities derived from its unique bioactive chemical constituents. Research indicates that its extracts can effectively inhibit inflammatory mediators to reduce swelling and pain in biological systems. Furthermore, the plant exhibits significant cytotoxic properties that demonstrate its potential for inhibiting the growth of certain cancer cell lines. These dual medicinal actions suggest that the species holds promise for future drug development in treating chronic inflammation and oncology. Further investigation into its molecular mechanisms is necessary to fully harness these therapeutic benefits.

As discussed in a study, Oxybaphus himalaicus alleviates diabetic kidney disease by suppressing lipid metabolism and inflammation via PPARα signaling. Network pharmacology research identified the ethyl acetate (EA) fraction as the main active component of Oxybaphus himalaicus for treating this condition. In vivo and in vitro experiments demonstrated that the EA fraction reduces renal lipotoxicity by upregulating PPARα pathway proteins and enhancing fatty acid oxidation. This fraction also downregulates inflammatory factors such as TNF-α and IL-6 to mitigate the disease. Molecular docking studies revealed that rotenoid compounds within the EA fraction have a high affinity for PPARα. These rotenoids may be the main active components that exert these pharmacological effects.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a complex profile of medicinal compounds including Himalaflavone A-E, Himalaflavones F, Himalaflavones G, various flavonoids, and phenylpropanoid derivatives. These specific chemical constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's diverse biological activities and therapeutic potential. The presence of different himalaflavone types suggests a highly specialized secondary metabolism within the species. Furthermore, the combination of flavonoids and phenylpropanoid derivatives provides a broad spectrum of antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These integrated chemical groups work together to define the plant's overall pharmacological profile.

As discussed in a study, fifteen compounds were isolated from the roots of Oxybaphus himalaicus, consisting of five new flavonoids (1-5) and ten known ones (6-15). These isolated compounds were tested for cytotoxic activities against human lung carcinoma A549 cells and human hepatocellular carcinoma HepG2 cells. Among the substances tested, compound 11 demonstrated the most powerful inhibitory effect. This compound produced IC50 values of 19.47 ± 0.28 μM against A549 cells and 52.6 ± 0.38 μM against HepG2 cells.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating acute kidney injury, nephritis, and various forms of inflammation. Its medicinal properties are primarily directed toward supporting renal function and reducing swelling in bodily tissues. By addressing systemic inflammation, the plant helps manage the underlying causes of many inflammatory conditions. Researchers often examine its potential to mitigate damage caused by nephritis in the kidneys. This botanical specimen serves as a traditional remedy for stabilizing kidney health and soothing painful inflammatory responses.

As discussed in a study, the roots of Oxybaphus himalaicus Edgew. have long been used in Tibetan folk medicine for the treatment of nephritis. UPLC-MS/MS analysis tentatively identified 41 components in the Oxybaphus himalaicus extract (OE). In LPS-induced RAW264.7 macrophages, the OE showed significant anti-inflammatory activity by dose dependently reducing the LPS-stimulated release of nitric oxide and the mRNA level and protein expression of inflammatory cytokines. The OE inhibited Toll-like receptor 4/myeloid differentiation factor 2 (TLR4/MD2) complex formation and suppressed both MyD88-dependent and TRIF-dependent cascades activation. In LPS-induced AKI mice, OE treatment mitigated renal injury and inflammatory infiltration by inhibiting TLR4/MD2 complex formation.
