10 Medicinal Constituents Of Prunus Dulcis (Almond)

Prunus dulcis has active constituents such as amygdalin, flavonoids, and phenolic acids.

These constituents possess medicinal properties including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and cardiovascular protective effects. The flavonoids in almonds can improve cardiovascular health by reducing cholesterol levels and preventing platelet aggregation, thereby reducing the risk of heart disease. The amygdalin also has antimicrobial properties, making it effective against certain bacterial and viral infections.

Regular consumption of almonds has been shown to promote overall well-being and reduce the risk of chronic diseases.

This article explains in details the 10 best active constituents of Prunus dulcis.

1. Amygdalin

Prunus dulcis amygdalin is a glycoside compound found in the fruit of the apricot kernel and other Prunus species.

It is composed of glucose, hydrocynic acid, and benzaldehyde, which are bound together by an alpha-glycosidic linkage.

Amygdalin has been studied for its potential health benefits, including antitumor and antibacterial properties, as well as its ability to inhibit the growth of certain cancer cells.

2. Quercetin

Prunus dulcis quercetin is a flavonoid compound found in the kernels of this nut tree.

This antioxidant has been extensively studied for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties.

Quercetin is believed to play a role in protecting against chronic diseases such as cardiovascular disease, cancer, and neurodegenerative disorders by neutralizing free radicals and modulating immune responses.

3. Gallic acid

Prunus dulcis gallic acid is a type of phenolic compound found in its kernels.

It has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various ailments such as inflammation, fever, and respiratory issues. Gallic acid exhibits antimicrobial and antioxidant properties, making it effective against bacteria, viruses, and fungi.

Additionally, it has been shown to have anti-cancerous and anti-inflammatory effects, which may contribute to its potential health benefits when consumed in moderate amounts.

4. Ellagic acid

Prunus dulcis ellagic acid is a polyphenolic compound found in the fruit skin and pit of the plant.

It has been extensively studied for its potential health benefits, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and anticancer properties. Ellagic acid has also been shown to inhibit the growth of certain bacteria, viruses, and fungi, making it a promising agent against infections.

Additionally, it may help protect against cardiovascular disease and neurodegenerative disorders.

5. Catechin

Prunus dulcis catechin is a type of flavonoid polyphenol compound found in its seeds and hulls.

It has been extensively studied for its potential health benefits, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties.

Catechin is believed to contribute to the nut's characteristic flavor and aroma, and may also play a role in protecting against chronic diseases such as heart disease and certain cancers.

6. Kaempferol

Prunus dulcis kaempferol is a flavonoid compound found in the fruit, leaves, and bark of the almond tree.

It has been widely studied for its potential health benefits, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and anticancer properties.

Kaempferol has been shown to have protective effects against cardiovascular disease, neurodegenerative disorders, and certain types of cancer, making it a promising area of research in the field of nutraceuticals.

7. Isorhapontigenin

Prunus dulcis isorhapontigenin is a bioactive compound isolated from the kernel of almonds.

It has been found to possess various pharmacological activities, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and anticancer properties. Isorhapontigenin has also shown potential in reducing the risk of cardiovascular disease by inhibiting platelet aggregation and LDL cholesterol oxidation.

Its unique chemical structure makes it an attractive candidate for further research into its therapeutic applications.

8. Prunin

Prunus dulcis prunin is a type of flavonoid glycoside found in the kernels and hulls of almonds.

It is responsible for giving almonds their characteristic brown color and has been linked to several potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Prunin has also been shown to have antibacterial and antifungal activities, making it a promising ingredient in food preservation and pharmaceutical applications.

9. Epicatechin

Prunus dulcis epicatechin is a flavonoid compound that has been gaining attention for its potential health benefits.

It is found in the skin and pit of almonds and has been shown to have antioxidant properties, which may help protect against cell damage and reduce inflammation.

Epicatechin has also been linked to improved cardiovascular health and may help to lower blood pressure and cholesterol levels.

10. Chlorogenic acid

Prunus dulcis chlorogenic acid is a naturally occurring phenolic compound found in high concentrations in the fruit's skin and pit.

This antioxidant-rich compound has been studied for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Chlorogenic acid has been shown to inhibit the growth of certain bacteria and viruses, making it a promising candidate for use in food preservation and medicine.
