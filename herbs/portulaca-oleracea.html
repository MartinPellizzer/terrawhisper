Portulaca

Portulaca oleracea

Portulaca (Portulaca oleracea) is a small, succulent plant commonly known as purslane, which has been used for centuries in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, particularly in improving skin conditions and reducing inflammation.

Botanical Identification

Portulaca oleracea, commonly known as purslane, belongs to the family Portulacaceae. It is an annual herb native to Europe, Asia, and Africa. The plant has fleshy, succulent leaves and yellow flowers, with a root system adapted to arid conditions. It is also referred to as mouse ear or verdant orache in various regional contexts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Spermatophyta
  • Class: caryophyllopsida
  • Order: Caryophyllales
  • Family: Portulacaceae
  • Genus: Portulaca
  • Species: Portulaca oleracea

Active Compounds

Portulaca oleracea contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and tannins, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Phenolic acid
  • Tannin

Portulaca Actions.

Preparation Methods

Culinary use: Used for digestive issues and as a general tonic due to its nutrient content.

Decoction: Used for urinary tract infections and kidney ailments due to its diuretic properties.

Infusion: Used for respiratory issues like coughs and colds due to its mild expectorant properties.

Safety Profile

Portulaca oleracea is generally safe when consumed in moderate amounts. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy due to potential uterine stimulation effects. Always consult a healthcare provider before use, especially for those with existing health conditions.
