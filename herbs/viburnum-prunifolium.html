Sweet viburnum

Viburnum prunifolium

Sweet Viburnum (Viburnum prunifolium) is a deciduous shrub native to North America, known for its clusters of fragrant white flowers and red berries, and it has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to support digestive health and alleviate symptoms of indigestion and bloating.

Botanical Identification

Viburnum prunifolium, commonly known as Sweet Viburnum, is a species in the Caprifoliaceae family. It is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of white flowers followed by red berries. It is also known as Indian plum and common viburnum in some regions.

Active Compounds

The Sweet Viburnum contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Their combined action supports the herb's traditional use in herbal medicine.

Safety Profile

Sweet Viburnum is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated in pregnancy and breastfeeding due to limited safety data. Always consult a healthcare provider before use, especially for those with chronic conditions.
