Lagerstroemia subcostata

What's the taxonomical classification of Lagerstroemia subcostata?

Lagerstroemia subcostata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant falls under the order Myrtales and is a member of the family Lythraceae. Specifically, it is categorized within the genus Lagerstroemia, with its unique identity defined by the species subcostata.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Lagerstroemia subcostata can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, or localized swelling if the plant's sap or foliage comes into direct contact with sensitive skin. While specific toxicological studies are limited, many members of the Lythraceae family possess chemical compounds that may trigger redness or itching in certain individuals. People with known hypersensitivity to similar botanical species should exercise caution when handling the plant. It is advisable to wear gloves during pruning or maintenance to prevent accidental exposure to any potential allergens. If an adverse reaction occurs, the affected area should be washed thoroughly with soap and water immediately.
