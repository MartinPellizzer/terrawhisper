Lagerstroemia subcostata

What's the taxonomical classification of Lagerstroemia subcostata?

Lagerstroemia subcostata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant falls under the order Myrtales and is a member of the family Lythraceae. Specifically, it is categorized within the genus Lagerstroemia, with its unique identity defined by the species subcostata.

What are the therapeutic actions of Lagerstroemia subcostata?

Lagerstroemia subcostata has medicinal properties that are primarily utilized for its ability to treat digestive ailments, skin inflammations, and feverish conditions. The plant contains bioactive compounds that act as natural anti-inflammatory agents when applied topically or ingested in controlled preparations. Traditional practitioners often use decoctions of the leaves and bark to soothe stomach discomfort and regulate gastrointestinal functions. Some studies suggest that the chemical constituents may also possess antimicrobial qualities that assist in healing minor skin infections. These diverse therapeutic applications make it a significant component in various regional herbal medicine practices.
