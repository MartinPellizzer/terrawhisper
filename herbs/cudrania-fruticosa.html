Cudrania fruticosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Cudrania fruticosa?

Cudrania fruticosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the taxonomic lineage of the order Rosales. The plant is further categorized under the family Moraea (Moraeceae), which leads to its specific designation within the genus Cudrania. Ultimately, its unique biological identity is finalized by its specific epithet, making its full scientific name Cudrania fruticosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cudrania fruticosa has a deciduous, spreading shrub or small tree habit characterized by arching branches that can reach several meters in height. The leaves are pinnately compound with several pairs of serrated leaflets that are typically oblong or elliptic in shape. Small, inconspicuous yellowish flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves during the blooming season. These flowers eventually develop into globose, fleshy drupes that turn from green to a dark reddish or purple color when ripe. The bark is generally grayish and becomes somewhat fissured as the plant matures.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds Cudraxanthone T and Cudraxanthone U. These specific xanthone derivatives are primary bioactive constituents found within the plant's chemical profile. Researchers study these molecules to understand their potential therapeutic effects on human health. The presence of such specialized compounds distinguishes this species from other similar flora. Detailed analysis of these substances helps scientists map out the plant's pharmacological properties.

As discussed in a study, the roots of Cudrania fruticosa Wight were investigated to identify their chemical constituents. Various column chromatographic methods such as silica gel, polyamide, sephadex LH-20, and semi-preparative HPLC were used for isolation. The research resulted in the isolation of 2 new xanthones, named Cudraxanthone T and U (1-2). Additionally, 4 known compounds (3-6) were also isolated from the roots of Cudrania fruticosa Wight. Structures were elucidated using 1D and 2D NMR techniques, mass spectrometry, and chemical methods.
