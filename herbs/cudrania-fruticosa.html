Cudrania fruticosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Cudrania fruticosa?

Cudrania fruticosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the taxonomic lineage of the order Rosales. The plant is further categorized under the family Moraea (Moraeceae), which leads to its specific designation within the genus Cudrania. Ultimately, its unique biological identity is finalized by its specific epithet, making its full scientific name Cudrania fruticosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cudrania fruticosa has a deciduous, spreading shrub or small tree habit characterized by arching branches that can reach several meters in height. The leaves are pinnately compound with several pairs of serrated leaflets that are typically oblong or elliptic in shape. Small, inconspicuous yellowish flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves during the blooming season. These flowers eventually develop into globose, fleshy drupes that turn from green to a dark reddish or purple color when ripe. The bark is generally grayish and becomes somewhat fissured as the plant matures.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several bioactive xanthones, specifically cudraxanthone T and cudraxanthone U, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These specialized secondary metabolites are often studied for their potential pharmacological activities in biological systems. Beyond these specific compounds, the plant also hosts various other chemical constituents that may influence its therapeutic effects. Researchers analyze these unique molecules to better understand how they interact with human cellular pathways. This detailed chemical profile makes the species a subject of interest in natural product chemistry.

According to a study published by "Chinese journal of natural medicines", the roots of Cudrania fruticosa Wight were investigated to identify chemical constituents. Through various column chromatographic methods including silica gel, polyamide, sephadex LH-20, and semi-preparative HPLC, several substances were identified. The investigation resulted in the isolation of 2 new xanthones, specifically Cudraxanthone T and U (1-2). Additionally, 4 known compounds (3-6) were also isolated from the roots of Cudrania fruticosa Wight. Structures were elucidated using 1D and 2D NMR techniques, mass spectrometry, and chemical methods.
