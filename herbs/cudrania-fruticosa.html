Cudrania fruticosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cudrania fruticosa has a deciduous, spreading shrub or small tree habit characterized by arching branches that can reach several meters in height. The leaves are pinnately compound with several pairs of serrated leaflets that are typically oblong or elliptic in shape. Small, inconspicuous yellowish flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves during the blooming season. These flowers eventually develop into globose, fleshy drupes that turn from green to a dark reddish or purple color when ripe. The bark is generally grayish and becomes somewhat fissured as the plant matures.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to a vast range across East and Southeast Asia, spanning from northern China and the Korean Peninsula down through various regions of Indochina and into the Indian subcontinent. It thrives in diverse environments including deciduous forests, scrublands, and rocky hillsides. Within these habitats, the species is frequently found in temperate to subtropical climates where it can colonize forest edges. Its distribution patterns often follow mountain ranges and river valleys where soil moisture is sufficient. Localized populations can also be found in parts of Japan and Taiwan.
