What's the taxonomical classification of Rhododendron augustinei?

Rhododendron augustinei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Ericales and the family Ericaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Rhododendron, and its specific species designation, augustinei.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhododendron augustinei has large, elliptical leaves that measure up to 20 centimeters in length and are characterized by a leathery texture and a dark green color. The stems are woody and robust, supporting the heavy weight of the mature shrub's spreading branches. Once consumed, these toxins interfere with sodium channels in cell membranes, leading to significant neurological and cardiovascular distress. The onset of gastrointestinal issues often precedes more life-threatening complications like heart rhythm disturbances. In extreme cases, the physiological strain can escalate to uncontrollable seizures or a dangerous slowing of breathing.
