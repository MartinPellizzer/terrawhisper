Rhododendron augustinei

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhododendron augustinei has large, elliptical leaves that measure up to 20 centimeters in length and are characterized by a leathery texture and a dark green color. The foliage is typically evergreen, providing a dense canopy that helps the plant retain moisture in its montane habitat. Its flowers are bell-shaped and bloom in shades of deep pink or purplish-red, often appearing in clusters known as trusses. The stems are woody and robust, supporting the heavy weight of the mature shrub's spreading branches. These morphological traits collectively allow the species to thrive in the humid, high-altitude environments of the Himalayas.

        What side effects this plant can have?

        This plant can cause severe toxicity characterized by nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, dizziness, confusion, arrhythmia, seizures, and respiratory depression. These symptoms typically arise from the ingestion of grayanotoxins found within the plant's tissues. Once consumed, these toxins interfere with sodium channels in cell membranes, leading to significant neurological and cardiovascular distress. The onset of gastrointestinal issues often precedes more life-threatening complications like heart rhythm disturbances. In extreme cases, the physiological strain can escalate to uncontrollable seizures or a dangerous slowing of breathing.
