What's the taxonomical classification of Turpinia montana?

Turpinia montana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Crossosomatales and the family Staphyleaceae, eventually narrowing down to the genus Turpinia. Finally, the specific identity of this plant is defined by its species name, montana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Turpinia montana has simple, opposite, elliptical to oblong leaves that measure up to 15 centimeters in length and feature entire margins with prominent pinnate venation. The foliage typically presents a glossy, dark green upper surface and a paler underside. Its woody stems are characterized by a smooth bark that matures into a greyish-brown texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in terminal panicles. These flowers eventually give way to small, fleshy drupes that serve as the primary fruit structure.
