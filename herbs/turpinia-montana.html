Turpinia montana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Turpinia montana has simple, opposite, elliptical to oblong leaves that measure up to 15 centimeters in length and feature entire margins with prominent pinnate venation. The foliage typically presents a glossy, dark green upper surface and a paler underside. Its woody stems are characterized by a smooth bark that matures into a greyish-brown texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in terminal panicles. These flowers eventually give way to small, fleshy drupes that serve as the primary fruit structure.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, spanning from southern Mexico through much of the Amazon Basin to Brazil. It typically thrives in the understory of moist lowland forests and montane rainforests. Its distribution is closely tied to high-humidity environments where dense canopy cover is present. Because it favors these specific ecological niches, it is commonly found in countries such as Colombia, Peru, and Ecuador. The species relies on the stable temperatures and heavy rainfall characteristic of these neotropical zones.
