Arnica longifolia subsp. genuina

Arnica longifolia subsp.

genuina, commonly known as mountain arnica, is a subspecies of the arnica plant, belonging to the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

It is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which may help alleviate pain, reduce swelling, and promote wound healing.

The therapeutic actions of mountain arnica are attributed to its bioactive constituents, such as sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which may interact with biological systems to produce its effects.

Further research is needed to fully understand the properties and uses of Arnica longifolia subsp.

genuina, but it has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to support skin health and alleviate pain.

Possible Side Effects of Arnica longifolia subsp. genuina

Arnica longifolia subsp.

genuina can have side effects if used improperly, such as skin irritation, allergic reactions, and increased bleeding risk due to its potent anti-inflammatory and anticoagulant properties.

If ingested, it may cause nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, while excessive topical application can lead to skin redness and burns.

Prolonged use or high doses may also result in liver damage and kidney problems in some individuals, making it crucial to consult a healthcare professional before using the plant.

Additionally, those with bleeding disorders or taking anticoagulant medications should avoid using arnica longifolia subsp.

genuina altogether.
