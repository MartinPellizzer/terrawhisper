Cyperus orthostachyus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cyperus orthostachyus has an erect, perennial growth habit characterized by triangular stems and terminal, spike-like inflorescences containing densely packed, brownish spikelets. The leaves are linear, grass-like, and emerge from the base of the plant to form a basal rosette. Each stem is smooth and lacks lateral branches, supporting a single flowering structure at the apex. The spikelets are arranged in a tight, cylindrical cluster that remains relatively straight along the axis. Small, scale-like bracts enclose the individual flowers within these compact spikes.
