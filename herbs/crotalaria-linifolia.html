Crotalaria linifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Crotalaria linifolia?

Crotalaria linifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Fabales. It is a member of the family Fabaceae and is further categorized into the genus Crotalaria, specifically identified as the species liniifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Crotalaria linifolia has linear, narrow leaves that are sparsely distributed along its slender, upright stems. The foliage is characterized by its fine, needle-like appearance which distinguishes it from other species in the genus. Small, yellow flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves to facilitate reproduction. Its seed pods are typically small and somewhat inflated, containing the seeds necessary for propagation. The overall growth habit is herbacious and delicate in structure.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, specifically ranging from West Africa through East Africa and into parts of Southern Africa. It thrives primarily in savanna ecosystems and open grasslands where seasonal rainfall supports its growth. The species is often found in disturbed habitats or along roadsides where sunlight is abundant. Its presence is most consistent in countries such as Kenya, Tanzania, and Nigeria.     What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause severe systemic toxicity due to the presence of pyrrolizidine alkaloids in its tissues. Ingesting these compounds can lead to significant liver damage through chronic exposure. This physiological distress often manifests as venous congestion within the hepatic system. Patients suffering from poisoning frequently experience acute gastrointestinal symptoms such as nausea and vomiting. Abdominal pain is also a common clinical sign of the damage caused by this species.
