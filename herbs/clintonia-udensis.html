What's the taxonomical classification of Clintonia udensis?

Clintonia udensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Liliales and the family Liliaceae, eventually reaching its specific designation within the genus Clintonia. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its unique species name, undensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clintonia udensis has woody stems with small, simple leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the branches.

The leaf blades are generally elliptic or lanceolate in shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. Its flowers are relatively small and are often found in clusters or as solitary units depending on the specific growth stage. The plant produces distinct woody fruits that develop from the fertilized ovaries of the flowers.

These morphological traits are characteristic of its classification within the Ebenaceae family.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Clintonia udensis can cause skin irritation or allergic contact dermatitis if the plant comes into direct contact with human tissue. While specific toxicological studies on this species are limited, many members of the related Euphorbiaceae family contain irritating sap. This sap may lead to redness, itching, or localized swelling upon contact. Individuals with sensitive skin should exercise extreme caution when handling any part of the plant. Avoiding contact with the leaves and stems can help prevent these unpleasant physical reactions.
