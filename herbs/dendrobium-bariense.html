What is Dendrobium bariense?

Dendrobium bariense is a species of epiphytic orchid in the family Orchidaceae.

It has thick, fleshy pseudobulbs that store water, and leaves that are leathery and oblong in shape, often with a reddish or purplish tint. The plant produces a single, terminal inflorescence with 2-5 white flowers, each with a distinctive yellow or green lip, and a long, slender flower spike that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The flowers are relatively small, measuring up to 2 centimeters in diameter, and have a short, white or yellowish spur.

Dendrobium bariense is native to the tropical forests of the Himalayas and surrounding regions in Asia.
