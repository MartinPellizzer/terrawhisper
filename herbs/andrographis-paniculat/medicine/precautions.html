10 Precautions To Take When Using Andrographis Paniculat (King Of Bitters)

Andrographis paniculat has some precautions to consider before using it medicinally, such as consulting with a healthcare professional, especially for pregnant women and children, due to its potential to stimulate uterine contractions and interact with blood thinners.

Failure to do so may lead to side effects like dizziness, nausea, and allergic reactions.

Taking the herb without proper guidance may exacerbate underlying conditions or worsen health conditions.

1. Follow recommended dosage

When using Andrographis paniculat medicinally, it's important to take with food only.

This precaution is crucial because taking this herb on an empty stomach can lead to gastrointestinal side effects such as nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea. Food helps slow down the absorption of the active compounds in Andrographis paniculat, reducing the risk of adverse reactions and ensuring a more comfortable experience for the patient.

Additionally, food may help improve bioavailability and efficacy of the herb's medicinal properties.
