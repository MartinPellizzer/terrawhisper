1. Causes dizziness

Andrographis paniculat causes dizziness due to its potential to interact with the body's serotonin levels.

The herb's compounds can stimulate the release of serotonin in the brain, leading to feelings of lightheadedness and dizziness.

Additionally, Andrographis paniculat may also increase heart rate and blood pressure, which can contribute to dizziness and related symptoms in some individuals.

2. Triggers allergic reactions

Andrographis paniculat triggers allergic reactions due to its chemical composition, which includes andrografolid, a sesquiterpene lactone that can cause an immune response in some individuals.

The allergenic potential is further amplified by the presence of other bioactive compounds like flavonoids and alkaloids.

These substances may bind to IgE antibodies, leading to the release of histamine and other inflammatory mediators, resulting in symptoms such as itching, hives, and anaphylaxis.

3. Increases heart rate

Andrographis paniculat increases heart rate.

This side effect is attributed to the presence of andrografolid, a bioactive compound that stimulates the heart's natural pacemaker activity, leading to an increase in heart rate and cardiac output.

Additionally, Andrographis paniculat may also enhance sympathetic nervous system activity, further contributing to its cardiostimulant effects.

4. Causes stomach pain

Andrographis paniculat causes stomach pain.

The herb's bitter compounds, such as andrographolide and neoandrographolide, are responsible for this side effect. When consumed, these compounds can irritate the stomach lining, leading to discomfort, cramping, and nausea. Additionally, Andrographis paniculat may also increase stomach acid production, further contributing to stomach pain.

This gastrointestinal upset is usually temporary and subsides once the body adjusts to the herb's presence.

5. Induces vomiting

Andrographis paniculat induces vomiting when used in high doses or for extended periods.

This is due to its bitter and acrid properties, which can irritate the stomach lining and stimulate the vagus nerve, leading to increased salivation and gagging reflexes that result in vomiting.

Additionally, Andrographis paniculat contains saponins, which can cause nausea and gastrointestinal upset, exacerbating the vomiting response.

6. Activates diarrhea

Andrographis paniculat activates diarrhea due to its ability to stimulate digestion and increase gut motility.

As a natural remedy, it can cause the body to release more fluids into the intestines, leading to loose stools or watery diarrhea.

This side effect is often temporary and may be a sign that the herb is working to clear out toxins from the digestive system.

7. Produces fever

Andrographis paniculat produces fever in some individuals.

This side effect is believed to be a result of its ability to stimulate the immune system and increase body temperature, which can cause a mild fever.

Additionally, Andrographis paniculat's bioactive compounds, such as andrographolide and neoandrographolide, may also contribute to this side effect by activating the body's natural defense mechanisms, leading to an increase in body heat production.

8. Hinders blood clotting

Andrographis paniculat hinders blood clotting due to its ability to inhibit platelet aggregation and prolong bleeding time.

This is attributed to the presence of bioactive compounds such as flavonoids and phenolic acids that interfere with the normal coagulation process, leading to a reduced ability of blood to form clots.

Additionally, Andrographis paniculat may also decrease vitamin K levels, further contributing to its anticoagulant effects.

9. Stimulates sweating

Andrographis paniculat stimulates sweating due to its natural diaphoretic properties.

As a result, users may experience increased perspiration, which can be a temporary and harmless side effect. The herb's ability to stimulate the sweat glands is attributed to its high concentration of flavonoids and other bioactive compounds that help to induce a detoxifying response in the body.

This characteristic has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic and traditional Chinese medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever and skin issues.

10. Lowers blood pressure

Andrographis paniculat lowers blood pressure by causing a significant decrease in systolic and diastolic blood pressure due to its ability to relax blood vessels and reduce peripheral resistance.

This is likely attributed to the herb's anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to dilate blood vessels and improve circulation.

As a result, Andrographis paniculat may cause orthostatic hypotension, a sudden drop in blood pressure when standing up from a seated or lying down position.
