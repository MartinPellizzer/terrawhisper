Pulicaria insignis

What's the taxonomical classification of Pulicaria insignis?

Pulicaria insignis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is positioned in the order Asterales. It is a member of the family Asteraceae, which is part of the genus Pulicaria, and is specifically identified by its species name, insignis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pulicaria insignis has upright, unbranched stems that typically reach heights of 20 to 60 centimeters and bear densely clustered, discoid flower heads. The leaves are lanceolate to linear in shape, featuring entire or slightly serrated margins and an alternate arrangement along the stem. Each flower head consists of numerous small, tubular florets that are usually pale pink to purplish in color. The involucral bracts surrounding the base of the flower heads are often arranged in several distinct series. Fine, soft hairs may cover the stems and leaf surfaces, contributing to the plant's overall texture.
