Libanotis seseloides

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Libanotis seseloides has an erect, annual herbaceous habit characterized by pinnately divided leaves and small, white, actinomorphic flowers arranged in terminal umbels. The foliage consists of bipinnate or tripinnate structures with narrow, linear segments that give the plant a feathery appearance. Its stems are typically slender and may exhibit slight branching as they ascend toward the inflorescence. The flowers feature five petals and are supported by pedicels that radiate from a common point within the umbel. Small, cylindrical schizocarps serve as the fruit, eventually dispersing to complete the life cycle.
