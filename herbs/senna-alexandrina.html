Egyptian Senna (Senna alexandrina)

Egyptian Senna (Senna alexandrina) is a member of the Fabaceae family, native to Egypt, Sudan, and Eastern Africa. Traditionally, its leaves, seeds, and fruits have been used for infusions, decoctions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its laxative, anti-inflammatory, and bitter actions, and has a long history of use in mediterranean herbal traditions, ayurvedic medicine, and african traditional medicine.

Flowers
Small yellow flowers arranged in racemes, actinomorphic, with five ovate petals and a prominent yellow standard petal, bearing a distinctive dark red tubular spur at the base of the keel.

Erect, branched, woody, with opposite phyllotaxis, and glabrous surface.
