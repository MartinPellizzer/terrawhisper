Adenophora stenophylla

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Adenophora stenophylla has narrow, linear leaves with entire margins and a height typically ranging from 20 to 50 centimeters. The plant features an erect, slender stem that is often sparsely hairy. Its inflorescence consists of small, tubular flowers that are usually light purple or violet in color. The leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem and decrease in size toward the top of the plant. A fibrous root system supports the herbaceous structure throughout its growth cycle.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of southwestern China, specifically within the mountainous provinces of Yunnan and Sichuan. It typically thrives in alpine or subalpine environments located at significant elevations above sea level. These specific habitats often consist of rocky slopes, forest margins, or grassy meadows where moisture is consistent. The species is restricted to these particular ecological niches within its limited regional range. Consequently, its distribution is highly localized rather than widespread across the continent.

What are the herbal preparations of Adenophora stenophylla?

Adenophora stenophylla is prepared primarily as a dried root decoction, a powdered extract, or a concentrated tincture for medicinal use. What side-effects this plant can have?

Adenophora stenophylla can cause gastrointestinal distress, allergic reactions, or potential toxicity if consumed in improper dosages or preparations. While often used in traditional medicine, the plant contains various alkaloids that may trigger adverse physiological responses in sensitive individuals. Some users have reported symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, or abdominal discomfort following ingestion. There is also a risk of skin irritation or localized allergic dermatitis if the plant comes into direct contact with the flesh. Because scientific research on its safety profile is limited, caution is necessary when using this species for medicinal purposes.
