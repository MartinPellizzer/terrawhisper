Rosa omeiensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Rosa omeiensis?

Rosa omeiensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its placement in the order Rosales, the plant is a member of the family Rosaceae. Finally, it is defined by its genus, Rosa, and its specific species designation, omeiensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rosa omeiensis has pinnately compound leaves typically consisting of five to seven leaflets with serrated margins and a woody, arching growth habit characterized by numerous fine prickles. The leaflets are often elliptic or ovate in shape, featuring acute tips and a distinctively textured surface. Its flowers are usually solitary or in small clusters, displaying pale pink to white petals that surround a central cluster of numerous stamens. The plant produces small, fleshy red hips that serve as its fruit after the flowering season concludes. These structural features collectively define the species' physical identity within its native high-altitude habitats.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?
