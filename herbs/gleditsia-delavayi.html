What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gleditsia delavayi has a deciduous growth habit characterized by bipinnately compound leaves, large spreading crowns, and formidable stipular spines that emerge from the trunk and branches. The fruit consists of long, flattened, woody pods that hang from the branches after flowering. These distinctive structures allow the species to thrive in its specific montane forest habitats.
