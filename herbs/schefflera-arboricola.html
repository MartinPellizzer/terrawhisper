What side-effects this plant can have?

Schefflera arboricola can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal upset, and potential toxicity if ingested by humans or pets. The plant contains sap that may trigger contact dermatitis or allergic reactions in sensitive individuals upon skin contact. If swallowed, it often leads to vomiting, abdominal pain, or diarrhea. Pets such as cats and dogs are particularly susceptible to these adverse effects if they chew on the leaves or stems. Constant exposure to the sap should be avoided to prevent localized swelling or redness.
