Geranium hispidissimum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Geranium hispidissimum has deeply palmately lobed leaves that are covered in dense, stiff, spreading hairs and stems that exhibit a similar bristly texture. The foliage is characterized by prominent veins and a rugged, hirsute surface that serves as a key identifying feature. Small, five-petaled flowers typically emerge in shades of pink or pale violet from the terminal ends of the stems. These blossoms are supported by slender pedicels that rise above the coarse, textured canopy of the plant. The overall morphology reflects a hardy structure adapted to withstand environmental stressors through its protective hairiness.
