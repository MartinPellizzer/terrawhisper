Bauhinia tomentosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bauhinia tomentosa has woody, perennial stems characterized by a dense covering of fine, velvety hairs that provide a soft texture. The leaves are uniquely bilobed, appearing shaped like a butterfly or a human footprint with a distinct notch at the apex. These leaf surfaces are notably pubescent, meaning they are covered in a thick layer of soft trichomes. The flowers typically feature five delicate petals that emerge from prominent buds. Small, woody pods eventually develop to house the seeds of the plant.
