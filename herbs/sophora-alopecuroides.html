Sophora alopecuroides

What's the taxonomical classification of Sophora alopecuroides?

Sophora alopecuroides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant falls under the order Fabales. It is further categorized into the family Fabaceae, which is characteristic of many nitrogen-fixing legumes. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Sophora, ultimately being identified by its specific species name, alopecuroides.

Its stems are woody and can develop a somewhat scrambling or climbing habit depending on the environment. The plant produces small, elongated legume pods that contain the seeds. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal quinolizidine alkaloids matrine, sophocarpine, oxymatrine, oxysophocarpine, and sophoridine. These specific chemical compounds are primarily responsible for the biological activities observed in the species. Research indicates that these alkaloids possess various pharmacological properties, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Scientists study these molecules to understand their potential applications in developing new therapeutic treatments. The precise concentration of these substances can vary depending on the plant's environmental conditions and growth stage.

As discussed in a study, seven alkaloids were isolated from the seed of Sophora alopecuroides. These specific compounds were identified as oxymatrin, oxysophocarpine, cytisine, matrine, sophocarpine, sophoridine, and nicotine. The study noted that nicotine was isolated from Sophora for the first time. Researchers carried out in vitro activity tests of the extracts and alkaloids against cancer, virus, dermatophytes, and bacteria.
