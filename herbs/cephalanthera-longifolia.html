Cephalanthera longifolia has elongated, lanceolate leaves that are typically 5 to 15 centimeters long and arranged oppositely along slender, woody stems. Its inflorescence consists of small, tubular flowers clustered into dense, terminal spikes that appear during the blooming season. The plant often exhibits a sprawling or climbing growth habit, utilizing its stems to weave through surrounding vegetation.
