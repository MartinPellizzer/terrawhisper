Cudrania tricuspidata

What's the taxonomical classification of Cudrania tricuspidata?

Cudrania tricuspidata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Rosales and the family Moraceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Cudrania, and its unique species designation, tricuspidata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cudrania tricuspidata has woody climbing vines characterized by trifoliate leaves with three distinctively pointed leaflets and small, globose fruits.

The foliage typically features serrated margins and a leathery texture that helps the plant thrive in forest edges. Its stems are often armed with small prickles that assist in its climbing habit through dense vegetation. The inflorescences consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into fleshy drupes.

These fruits change color as they ripen, transitioning from green to a darker shade to attract seed dispersers.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Cudrania tricuspidata targets specific inflammatory mediators, antioxidant enzymes, and various cytokines to exert its pharmacological effects. The bioactive compounds within the plant, particularly triterpenoids and flavonoids, act by modulating the expression of nitric oxide synthase and cyclooxygenase-2 proteins. These interactions help suppress the production of pro-inflammatory molecules like tumor necrosis factor-alpha and interleukin-6. Additionally, the plant's constituents can influence signaling pathways related to oxidative stress by interacting with superoxide dismutase and catalase. Such molecular interventions contribute to the plant's documented potential in treating inflammation and protecting against cellular damage.

What are the therapeutic actions of Cudrania tricuspidata?

Cudrania tricuspidata has medicinal properties that include astringent, hemostatic, anti-inflammatory, antipyretic, and diuretic actions.

Its astringent qualities make it useful for tightening tissues and managing secretions in various bodily systems. To combat fever and swelling, it utilizes its antipyretic and anti-inflammatory capabilities to soothe the body.

Additionally, the diuretic properties assist in promoting fluid excretion to support kidney function and detoxification.

Cudrania tricuspidata is prepared as decoctions, infusions, and topical pastes using its leaves, stems, and fruits. What side-effects this plant can have?

Cudrania tricuspidata can cause allergic reactions such as skin irritation, redness, or itching if the plant comes into direct contact with sensitive areas of the body. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, consuming unknown parts of the plant may lead to gastrointestinal distress or stomach upset. Some researchers suggest that the chemical compounds within the fruit and bark might interact with certain medications if ingested in large quantities. It is important to exercise caution because the precise pharmacological profile and potential long-term effects on humans remain understudied. Always consult a medical professional before using any wild plant for medicinal purposes.
