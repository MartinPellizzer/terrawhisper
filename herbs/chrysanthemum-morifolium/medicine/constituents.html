10 Medicinal Constituents Of Chrysanthemum Morifolium (Chrysanth)

Chrysanthemum morifolium has active constituents such as flavonoids, saponins, and sesquiterpenes that possess anti-inflammatory, antipyretic, and antibacterial properties.

These constituents work together to alleviate symptoms of fever, headaches, and respiratory issues, making it a popular herbal remedy in traditional Chinese medicine.

For example, the flavonoids in chrysanth help reduce inflammation, which can improve joint pain and swelling for individuals with arthritis, while its antimicrobial properties can aid in wound healing and skin infections, ultimately enhancing overall health and well-being.

1. Flavonoids

Chrysanthemum morifolium flavonoids is a type of bioactive compound found in its flowers.

These flavonoids have been extensively studied for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which may contribute to the plant's traditional uses in herbal medicine.

Specifically, Chrysanthemum morifolium flavonoids have been shown to exhibit neuroprotective effects, anti-cancer activities, and cardiovascular protective properties, making them a promising area of research in modern pharmacology.

2. Luteolin

Chrysanthemum morifolium luteolin is a type of flavonoid compound extracted from the flowers of C. morifolium.

It has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine to treat various diseases, such as fever, rheumatism, and digestive disorders.

Luteolin has also been shown to have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a potential therapeutic agent for the prevention and treatment of chronic diseases.
