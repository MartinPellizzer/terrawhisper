What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Chrysanthemum Morifolium (Chrysanth)?

Chrysanthemum morifolium, also known as chrysanth, has health benefits such as reducing fever and inflammation, as well as calming the mind.

Its medicinal constituents include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and triterpenoids, which contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Chrysanthemum morifolium is commonly prepared as a tea or infusion, as well as in capsule or tablet form. Side effects of excessive consumption may include allergic reactions or digestive issues.

When using chrysanthemum, individuals with bleeding disorders or taking anticoagulant medications should consult their healthcare provider.

This article explains the health benefits, active constituents, medicinal preparations, possible side effects, and precautions related to Chrysanthemum morifolium.

What are the health benefits of Chrysanthemum morifolium?

Chrysanthemum morifolium, also known as chrysanth, has health benefits such as reducing fever and inflammation due to its flavonoids and terpenoids content.

Its flowers have been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries to treat various conditions, including colds and flu.

Additionally, chrysanth has antioxidant and anti-bacterial properties, making it a popular ingredient in herbal teas and infusions to support overall well-being.

What are the active constituents of Chrysanthemum morifolium?

Chrysanthemum morifolium, also known as chrysanth, has active constituents such as flavonoids, triterpenoids, and phenolic acids.

These compounds have been shown to possess anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties. Specifically, quercetin and kaempferol, two flavonoids present in chrysanthemum, have been found to inhibit the activity of pro-inflammatory enzymes and cytokines.

These medicinal constituents contribute to the herb's traditional use in treating fever, headache, and other inflammatory disorders.

What are the medicinal preparations of Chrysanthemum morifolium?

Chrysanthemum morifolium, also known as chrysanth, has medicinal preparations such as infusions, decoctions, and tinctures.

The flowers are commonly used in traditional Chinese medicine to relieve fever, headaches, and digestive issues. Chrysanthemum tea, in particular, is valued for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, making it a popular remedy for soothing sore throats and relieving sinus pressure.

It is often combined with other herbs to enhance its therapeutic effects.

What are the possible side effect of using Chrysanthemum morifolium improperly?

Improper use of Chrysanthemum morifolium, also known as chrysanth, increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as dizziness, nausea, and headaches.

Consuming excessive amounts can also lead to skin irritation, diarrhea, and allergic reactions in some individuals.

Furthermore, interactions with certain medications, including blood thinners and diabetes medications, can exacerbate these side effects, highlighting the importance of proper dosage and consultation with a healthcare professional before use.
