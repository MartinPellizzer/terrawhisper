Huperzia emeiensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Huperzia emeiensis?

Huperzia emeiensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Lycopodiidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it falls under the order Lycopodiales and the family Lycopodiaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Huperzia, with its specific designation being the species emeiensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Huperzia emeiensis has a creeping rhizome that produces upright, dichotomously branched stems featuring small, lanceolate, sessile leaves arranged spirally around the axis. These leaves are typically fleshy and characterized by an acute apex and entire margins. The plant exhibits a microphyllous structure where the leaves are relatively uniform in size and shape throughout the stem. Reproductive structures consist of strobili located at the terminal ends of the branches. This morphology is typical of many lycopod species adapted to shaded, moist forest environments.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

This primary mechanism is largely attributed to the presence of the alkaloid huperzine A, which acts as a potent, reversible inhibitor of the enzyme.
