What's the taxonomical classification of Vernonia extensa?

Vernonia extensa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic placement continues through the order Asterales and the family Asteraceae, which is characteristic of many composite flowers. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific genus, Vernia, and its unique species designation, extensa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vernonia extensa has erect, woody stems that typically reach heights of 0.5 to 1.5 meters and are characterized by opposite, lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is primarily basal or distributed along the lower stem, featuring distinctively narrow and elongated leaf blades. Its inflorescences consist of small, discoid flower heads arranged in terminal clusters that appear purplish or blue in color. Each individual floret is tubular and lacks ray petals, a defining feature of the genus. The plant's overall structure is upright and slender, facilitating its growth in prairie or meadow environments.
