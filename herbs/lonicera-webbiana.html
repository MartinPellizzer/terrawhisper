Lonicera webbiana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lonicera webbiana has woody, climbing stems that feature opposite, elliptical to ovate leaves with entire margins and a somewhat leathery texture. The foliage typically presents a bright green color on the upper surface while the underside may appear slightly paler. Its inflorescences consist of tubular, fragrant flowers that emerge in small clusters from the leaf axils. These blossoms often transition from a creamy white to a pale yellow as they age. The plant eventually produces small, fleshy berries that are used for seed dispersal.

    What are the herbal preparations of Lonicera webbiana?

    Lonicera webbiana is prepared as decoctions, infusions, or topical poultices using the dried stems and leaves of the plant.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Lonicera webbiana can cause gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, and potential toxicity if the berries or leaves are ingested in large quantities. It is essential to handle this species with caution to avoid accidental poisoning or dermatological issues.
