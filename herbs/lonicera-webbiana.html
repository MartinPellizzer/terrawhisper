Lonicera webbiana

What's the taxonomical classification of Lonicera webbiana?

Lonicera webbiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Dipsacales. This plant is a representative of the family Caprifoliaceae, situated within the genus Lonicera, and is specifically identified by its species name, webbiana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lonicera webbiana has woody, climbing stems that feature opposite, elliptical to ovate leaves with entire margins and a somewhat leathery texture.

The foliage typically presents a bright green color on the upper surface while the underside may appear slightly paler. Its inflorescences consist of tubular, fragrant flowers that emerge in small clusters from the leaf axils. These blossoms often transition from a creamy white to a pale yellow as they age.

The plant eventually produces small, fleshy berries that are used for seed dispersal.

      What are the herbal preparations of Lonicera webbiana?

      Lonicera webbiana is prepared as decoctions, infusions, or topical poultices using the dried stems and leaves of the plant.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Lonicera webbiana can cause gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, and potential toxicity if the berries or leaves are ingested in large quantities. The plant contains various chemical compounds that may trigger allergic reactions when the foliage comes into direct contact with sensitive skin. Ingestion of the fruit often leads to symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. Some individuals might experience more severe neurological effects depending on the dosage consumed. It is essential to handle this species with caution to avoid accidental poisoning or dermatological issues.
