Solanum merrillianum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Solanum merrillianum has a perennial growth habit characterized by woody stems that often exhibit a spreading or scrambling form. The leaves are typically alternate, simple, and can vary from ovate to elliptical in shape with varying degrees of pubescence. Its flowers consist of five petals that are usually pale or white, arranged in a wheel-shaped corolla around a central cone of yellow anthers. The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that transitions in color as it matures from green to a dark shade. Fine hairs often cover the vegetative structures, providing a textured surface to the stems and foliage.
