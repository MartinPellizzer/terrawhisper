Mosla cavaleriei

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Mosla cavaleriei has a slender, erect stem that typically reaches a height of thirty to sixty centimeters and features small, opposite, lanceolate leaves with entire margins. The foliage is characterized by a deep green color and a smooth texture that lacks significant pubescence. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils in a somewhat crowded arrangement. These floral structures are often pale or yellowish and serve to attract specific pollinators within its native habitat. The root system is fibrous and well-developed to support its upright growth pattern.
