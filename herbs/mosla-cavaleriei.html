What's the taxonomical classification of Mosla cavaleriei?

Mosla cavaleriei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is positioned in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic journey continues through the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae, ultimately placing it within the genus Mosla. As a distinct member of its group, it is identified specifically by its species name, cavaleriei.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Mosla cavaleriei has a slender, erect stem that typically reaches a height of thirty to sixty centimeters and features small, opposite, lanceolate leaves with entire margins.

These floral structures are often pale or yellowish and serve to attract specific pollinators within its native habitat.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Mosla cavaleriei can cause severe allergic skin reactions, digestive distress, and potential neurological symptoms if ingested in large quantities. Some sensitive users might also report dizziness or lightheadedness following exposure. It is essential to handle this species with caution to avoid these adverse physiological responses.
