Chimonanthus nitens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Chimonanthus nitens can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and potential gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Individuals with sensitive skin may experience redness, itching, or swelling upon direct contact with the plant's sap or foliage. Some users might also develop a localized rash as a hypersensitivity reaction to its chemical constituents. If the plant is consumed accidentally, it may trigger nausea, vomiting, or abdominal cramping. Monitoring for these symptoms is essential when handling or being in proximity to this species.
