Blumea lanceolaria

What's the taxonomical classification of Blumea lanceolaria?

Blumea lanceolaria belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. This plant is a representative of the family Asteraceae, a large group of flowering plants, and is specifically categorized under the genus Blumea, with its unique designation as the species lanceolaria.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Blumea lanceolaria has lanceolate, serrated leaves that are densely covered in fine, white, woolly hairs on both the upper and lower surfaces. These leaves are arranged oppositely along a sturdy, upright stem that can reach significant heights. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads clustered in terminal capitula. The foliage often emits a strong, characteristic scent when crushed due to its glandular hairs. These morphological traits are typical of its adaptation to specific tropical forest environments.
