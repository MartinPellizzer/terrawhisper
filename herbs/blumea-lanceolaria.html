What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Blumea lanceolaria has lanceolate, serrated leaves that are densely covered in fine, white, woolly hairs on both the upper and lower surfaces. The foliage often emits a strong, characteristic scent when crushed due to its glandular hairs. These morphological traits are typical of its adaptation to specific tropical forest environments.
