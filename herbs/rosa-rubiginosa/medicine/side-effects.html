Rosa rubiginosa, also known as sweet brier, causes skin irritation due to its high concentration of fragrant oils and alkaloids.

These compounds can penetrate the skin, causing allergic reactions, redness, and itching in some individuals.

The oily resin and volatile compounds present on the plant's surface may also exacerbate existing skin conditions or irritate sensitive skin types, leading to discomfort and potentially even blisters or rashes.

Rosa rubiginosa triggers allergic reactions.

The plant's sap and oils contain allergenic compounds that can cause skin irritation, redness, and itching in some individuals. This reaction is often caused by the presence of urushiol, a common allergen found in many plant species, including Rosa rubiginosa.

In addition, the plant's fragrant flowers and leaves may also release small particles into the air that can trigger allergic responses in susceptible persons.

Rosa rubiginosa lowers blood pressure due to its high concentration of flavonoids and phenolic acids, which are natural vasodilators.

These compounds relax the smooth muscle in blood vessel walls, allowing them to dilate and increase blood flow. This can lead to a decrease in blood pressure, particularly for individuals with hypertension or hypotension.

As a result, users may experience a significant drop in their blood pressure, which may be a concern for those with pre-existing circulatory conditions.
