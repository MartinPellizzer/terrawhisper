10 Medicinal Constituents Of Rosa Rubiginosa (Sweet Briar)

Rosa rubiginosa has active constituents such as flavonoids, salicylic acid, and essential oils.

These constituents exhibit anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties. The flavonoids help reduce inflammation and alleviate pain, while salicylic acid soothes skin irritations. The essential oils may improve mood and reduce anxiety.

These properties can improve lives by providing relief from arthritis, eczema, and anxiety disorders.

1. Rosmarinic acid

Rosa rubiginosa rosmarinic acid is a potent polyphenolic compound found in the leaves and stems of this shrub native to Europe and Asia.

This antioxidant-rich acid has been extensively studied for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiviral properties.

It has also been shown to exhibit neuroprotective effects and may play a role in preventing cancer cell growth.

2. Gallic acid

Rosa rubiginosa gallic acid is a phenolic compound extracted from its flowers and leaves.

It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various ailments such as fever, inflammation, and skin conditions. Gallic acid exhibits potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, making it effective against microbial infections and cancer cell proliferation.

Its antimicrobial activities also make it useful for wound healing applications.

3. Quercetin

Rosa rubiginosa quercetin is a type of flavonoid that is found in the plant's flowers and leaves.

6. Naringenin
