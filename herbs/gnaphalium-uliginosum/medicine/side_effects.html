1. Causes allergic reactions

Gnaphalium uliginosum causes allergic reactions due to its ability to stimulate an immune response in some individuals.

The plant's chemical composition, which includes sesquiterpenes and triterpenoids, can trigger an allergic reaction by binding to IgE antibodies on mast cells, leading to the release of histamine and other mediators.

This may result in symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.

2. Triggers asthma attacks

Gnaphalium uliginosum triggers asthma attacks due to its allergenic properties, releasing potent antigens into the air that can stimulate an allergic reaction in susceptible individuals.

The plant's pollen and other airborne particles can cause inflammation and constriction of airways, leading to symptoms such as wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.

This allergic response can exacerbate existing asthma conditions or trigger attacks in people who are not diagnosed with the condition.

3. Induces vomiting

Gnaphalium uliginosum induces vomiting.

This side effect is attributed to the plant's ability to stimulate the digestive system, leading to increased gut contractions and a surge in gastric secretions. The anthraquinone glycosides present in Gnaphalium uliginosum are thought to be responsible for this action, which can result in nausea, vomiting, and potentially even diarrhea.

This response is an example of the plant's traditional use as a laxative and emetic in folk medicine.

4. Increases blood pressure

Gnaphalium uliginosum increases blood pressure due to its stimulating effect on the cardiovascular system.

The herb's bioactive compounds, such as flavonoids and phenolic acids, can cause constriction of blood vessels, leading to an increase in blood pressure. Additionally, Gnaphalium uliginosum may enhance adrenaline release, further contributing to elevated blood pressure levels.

As a result, individuals using this herbal remedy should be aware of this potential side effect.

5. Produces dizziness

Gnaphalium uliginosum produces dizziness due to its sedative and anxiolytic properties.

The plant's active compounds, such as flavonoids and saponins, can interact with the central nervous system, causing a calming effect on brain activity that may lead to feelings of dizziness or lightheadedness in some individuals.

6. Causes diarrhea

Gnaphalium uliginosum causes diarrhea due to its ability to stimulate the gut and increase bowel movements.

The plant's saponins, a type of bioactive compound, are responsible for this effect by irritating the mucous membranes in the digestive tract, leading to increased water absorption and rapid movement through the intestines.

This can result in loose stools and an urgent need to have a bowel movement.

7. Exacerbates bronchitis

Gnaphalium uliginosum exacerbates bronchitis due to its potential to irritate the respiratory tract and increase mucus production.

This can lead to further inflammation and congestion in individuals already suffering from bronchial issues. The plant's volatile oils may also stimulate the airways, causing a worsening of symptoms such as coughing, wheezing, and shortness of breath.

As a result, individuals with pre-existing respiratory conditions should exercise caution when using marsh cudweed products.

8. Lowers blood sugar

Gnaphalium uliginosum lowers blood sugar by stimulating insulin secretion and enhancing glucose uptake in the body's cells.

This occurs due to its ability to activate certain enzymes involved in carbohydrate metabolism, such as α-glucosidase and glycoprotein IIb/IIIa, which helps regulate blood sugar levels. Additionally, Gnaphalium uliginosum may also increase insulin sensitivity, allowing the body to respond more effectively to insulin's glucose-lowering effects.

As a result, people using this herbal remedy may experience a decrease in their blood sugar levels.

9. Enhances sedation

Gnaphalium uliginosum enhances sedation by affecting the central nervous system's neurotransmitters, such as GABA and serotonin.

This herb's active compounds, like sesquiterpenes and flavonoids, interact with these neurotransmitters to promote relaxation and reduce anxiety.

The resulting increased sedative effects can lead to feelings of calmness and reduced stress levels, making it a potential natural remedy for individuals seeking to manage their sleep or anxiety issues.

10. Worsens insomnia

Gnaphalium uliginosum worsens insomnia due to its potential stimulating effects on the nervous system.

The plant's active compounds may interfere with sleep patterns and make it difficult for individuals to fall asleep or stay asleep throughout the night.

Additionally, some people may experience increased anxiety or restlessness when using Gnaphalium uliginosum, further exacerbating insomnia symptoms.
