Astragalus tongolensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Astragalus tongolensis?

Astragalus tongolensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical structure, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized under the order Fabales and the family Fabaceae. Finally, its taxonomic identity is completed by its placement within the genus Astragalus, specifically as the species tongolensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Astragalus tongolensis has a perennial herbaceous growth habit characterized by pinnately compound leaves and typical legume-shaped flowers. The leaves consist of multiple small leaflets arranged along a central rachis, often featuring fine pubescence on the surfaces. Its inflorescence typically presents as a dense cluster of pea-like blossoms that vary in shade from pale pink to deep purple. The stems are sturdy and may show varying degrees of hairiness depending on the specific environmental exposure. Small, elongated pods develop from the flowers to facilitate seed dispersal.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the high-altitude regions of the Dolpa district in Nepal. It grows specifically within the alpine zones of the Himalayan mountain range. Its habitat is typically characterized by rocky slopes and subalpine meadows. The species is restricted to very specific environmental niches found in these remote mountainous terrains. Consequently, its entire natural range is limited to a narrow geographical area in central Nepal.
