Sorbus folgneri

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sorbus folgneri has pinnately compound leaves characterized by numerous small, serrated leaflets that are arranged oppositely along a central rachis. These leaflets typically exhibit a light green color on the upper surface and may appear slightly paler underneath. The plant features woody stems that support small, clustered inflorescences during its flowering stage. Following pollination, the species produces small, fleshy pomes that serve as its primary fruit structure. The overall growth habit of the plant is that of a small tree or large shrub with a spreading canopy.
