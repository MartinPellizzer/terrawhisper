10 Possible Side Effects Of Eupatorium Purpureum

Eupatorium purpureum has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal upset, allergic reactions, and interactions with other medications.

Causes include high doses, sensitivity, or when combined with other herbs or prescription drugs.

For instance, severe stomach pain, diarrhea, or vomiting can hinder daily activities, lead to dehydration, and affect work productivity.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Eupatorium purpureum causes allergic reactions due to its potential to trigger an immune system response.

The plant's volatile oils and flavonoids may bind to allergen receptors on the skin or mucous membranes, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and inflammation.

Additionally, cross-reactivity with other plants in the Asteraceae family may also contribute to allergic reactions.

2. Triggers respiratory failure

Eupatorium purpureum triggers respiratory failure when its active compounds, particularly euparin and isoeuparin, cause bronchospasm and constrict blood vessels in the lungs.

This can lead to severe difficulty breathing, wheezing, and a sense of choking or suffocation.

The herb's ability to stimulate the autonomic nervous system and release histamine also contributes to its respiratory toxic effects, exacerbating existing conditions such as asthma and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).

3. Induces vomiting episodes

Eupatorium purpureum induces vomiting episodes due to its ability to stimulate the stomach and intestines.

This is likely caused by the herb's saponin content, which can increase digestive motility and secretions, leading to an upset stomach and subsequent vomiting. The volatile oils present in Eupatorium purpureum may also contribute to this side effect by irritating the mucous membranes of the gastrointestinal tract.

As a result, users may experience frequent or prolonged periods of nausea and vomiting.

4. Activates liver toxicity

Eupatorium purpureum activates liver toxicity when consumed in large doses or for extended periods.

This is due to the presence of sesquiterpenes and iridoid glycosides, which can cause inflammation and damage to liver cells. Additionally, Eupatorium purpureum may also stimulate the production of enzymes that break down medications in the liver, potentially leading to decreased efficacy or interactions with prescribed drugs.

As a result, individuals experiencing liver toxicity may exhibit symptoms such as jaundice, dark urine, and fatigue.

5. Produces cardiac arrhythmias

Eupatorium purpureum produces cardiac arrhythmias due to its ability to affect the heart's electrical conduction system.

The herb contains compounds that can slow down or speed up heart contractions, leading to abnormal heart rhythms and palpitations.

This is because Eupatorium purpureum interacts with the body's natural cardiac regulation mechanisms, potentially disrupting normal heart function.

6. Increases blood pressure

Eupatorium purpureum increases blood pressure due to its stimulatory effect on the cardiovascular system.

The herb's active compounds, such as alkaloids and flavonoids, can cause increased heart rate and vasodilation, leading to elevated blood pressure in some individuals.

This is especially true for those with pre-existing hypertension or cardiovascular conditions, as they may be more susceptible to the herb's potent effects on blood vessels and cardiac activity.

7. Affects kidney function

Eupatorium purpureum affects kidney function by altering the body's ability to eliminate waste and excess fluids.

The herb may increase urine production, leading to dehydration if not adequately addressed. Additionally, its saponin content can irritate the kidneys, potentially causing inflammation and strain on this vital organ.

This adverse effect is thought to be linked to Eupatorium purpureum's ability to stimulate the urinary tract and alter kidney function in some individuals.

8. Stimulates seizures risk

Eupatorium purpureum stimulates seizures risk due to its potential to interact with GABA receptors in the brain.

This interaction can lead to an imbalance of neurotransmitters, causing increased neuronal activity and ultimately, seizure activity.

The plant's constituent, eupatorin, may contribute to this effect by altering the body's response to electrical impulses in the nervous system, potentially triggering seizures in susceptible individuals.

9. Worsens asthma symptoms

Eupatorium purpureum worsens asthma symptoms by triggering bronchospasms and increasing inflammation in the airways.

The herb contains compounds like euparin and isoflavonoids, which can cause a rapid contraction of the smooth muscle cells in the respiratory tract, leading to constricted airways and difficulty breathing.

Additionally, joe pye weed may stimulate the production of histamine, a chemical that causes blood vessels to dilate and mucous membranes to swell, exacerbating asthma symptoms.

10. Enhances bleeding risk

Eupatorium purpureum enhances bleeding risk due to its ability to inhibit blood clotting and platelet aggregation.

The plant's bioactive compounds, including flavonoids and phenolic acids, can interfere with the normal coagulation cascade, increasing the likelihood of spontaneous bleeding or exacerbating pre-existing bleeding disorders.

This effect is thought to be mediated by the plant's ability to reduce platelet function and increase the production of thromboxane A2, a potent vasodilator that further compromises blood vessel integrity.
