Everlasting

Helichrysum italicum

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Helichrysum italicum, commonly known as immortelle, belongs to the Asteraceae family. It is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. The plant features yellow flowers, aromatic leaves, and a woody root system. It is also referred to as everlastings in herbal literature.

Active Compounds

Everlasting contains essential oils, terpenoids, and flavonoids as its primary bioactive compounds. These compounds work together to support various therapeutic effects, with terpenoids contributing to its anti-inflammatory properties and flavonoids offering antioxidant benefits. The synergy between these compounds enhances its overall efficacy in herbal medicine.

  • Essential oil
  • Terpenoid
  • Flavonoid

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action
Respiratory Respiratory infections, asthma, cough, bronchitis, respiratory allergies Antimicrobial, bronchodilator, expectorant
Integumentary Skin inflammation, wound healing, skin infections, inflammatory skin conditions, eczema Anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, antimicrobial, antioxidant
Immune Allergic reactions Antihistaminic

Preparation Methods

Essential oil: Used for respiratory issues and skin conditions due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Infusion: Used for digestive issues and to support the immune system.

Tincture: Used for anxiety and stress relief due to its calming effects.

Safety Profile

Everlasting, or Helichrysum italicum, is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should not be taken during pregnancy or by individuals with known allergies to plants in the Asteraceae family. Always consult a healthcare professional before use, especially for those with chronic health conditions.
