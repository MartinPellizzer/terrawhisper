Everlasting (Helichrysum italicum): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

Everlasting (Helichrysum italicum) is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, known for its vibrant yellow flowers and strong, aromatic scent, and it has been traditionally used for its various health benefits, including improving respiratory conditions and reducing inflammation.

What is Everlasting (Helichrysum Italicum)?

Everlasting (Helichrysum italicum) is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, known for its small, golden-yellow flowers and its use in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and skin-healing properties.

Botanical Identity and Classification

Everlasting, or Helichrysum italicum, is a flowering plant in the daisy family (Asteraceae), native to the Mediterranean region and characterized by its golden-yellow, drought-resistant flowers and essential oil-rich foliage.

Common Names and Synonyms

Common Names and Synonyms include Everlasting, St. John's Wort, Golden Flower, and Italian Immortelle.

Plant Description and Morphology

Everlasting (Helichrysum italicum) is a shrub characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and dense clusters of yellow flowers that retain their color and shape even after drying.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Everlasting (Helichrysum italicum) is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly thriving in the dry, sunny climates of southern Europe, North Africa, and western Asia.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The aerial parts, including the flowers and leaves, are primarily used medicinally in Everlasting (Helichrysum italicum) due to their high concentration of volatile oils and bioactive compounds.

Phytochemical Composition

The phytochemical composition of Everlasting (Helichrysum italicum) includes a diverse array of bioactive compounds such as sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, phenolic acids, and volatile oils, which contribute to its therapeutic properties in herbal medicine.

Pharmacological Properties

Helichrysum italicum exhibits potent pharmacological properties, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, antioxidant, and wound-healing effects, supported by both traditional use and modern scientific research.

Mechanisms of Action

Everlasting (Helichrysum italicum) exerts its therapeutic effects through its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, primarily due to the presence of sesquiterpene lactones and flavonoids that modulate cellular signaling pathways and enhance immune response.

Therapeutic Uses and Indications

Everlasting is used to treat digestive issues, skin conditions, respiratory infections, anxiety, insomnia, menstrual cramps, and inflammation.

Preparation Methods and Forms

Everlasting (Helichrysum italicum) is commonly prepared as an essential oil through steam distillation of its dried flowering heads, and also used in tinctures, herbal teas, and topical salves depending on the intended application.

Dosage and Administration

The typical dosage of Everlasting (Helichrysum italicum) for herbal use ranges from 1 to 2 milliliters of essential oil diluted in 10 to 15 milliliters of a carrier oil, applied topically twice daily, with oral ingestion typically limited to 1 to 2 drops of diluted essential oil up to three times daily, though it is crucial to consult a healthcare professional for personalized guidance.

Safety, Side Effects, and Contraindications

While Everlasting (Helichrysum italicum) is generally considered safe when used in moderate amounts, some individuals may experience mild gastrointestinal discomfort, skin irritation, or allergic reactions, particularly with excessive or prolonged use.

Drug Interactions

Everlasting (Helichrysum italicum) may interact with certain medications, including anticoagulants, antiplatelet drugs, and medications affecting the liver or kidneys, potentially increasing the risk of bleeding or altering drug metabolism.

Toxicity and Precautions

While Everlasting (Helichrysum italicum) is generally considered safe when used appropriately, it may cause mild gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals, and pregnant or breastfeeding women should avoid its use due to limited safety data.

Cultivation and Harvesting

Everlasting (Helichrysum italicum) is typically cultivated in well-drained, sunny locations with a temperate climate, requiring minimal water and thriving in sandy or loamy soils.

Processing and Extraction

Processing Everlasting (Helichrysum italicum) involves drying the flower heads to preserve their essential oils and aromatic compounds, ensuring the plant's medicinal properties remain intact for use in herbal remedies and essential oil formulations.

Storage and Shelf Life

Everlasting (Helichrysum italicum) should be stored in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture, and when properly stored, it can retain its potency for up to two years.

Scientific Research and Evidence

Extensive scientific research has demonstrated that Everlasting (Helichrysum italicum) possesses significant anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and wound-healing properties, supported by both in vitro and clinical studies.

Traditional and Historical Uses

Helichrysum italicum has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and wound-healing properties, with historical records dating back to ancient Greece and Rome.

Regulatory Status
