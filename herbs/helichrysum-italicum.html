Everlasting (Helichrysum italicum): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

Everlasting (Helichrysum italicum) is a revered herb in herbal medicine known for its potent anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and wound-healing properties, often used to support skin health, digestive function, and respiratory wellness.

What is Everlasting (Helichrysum Italicum)?

Everlasting (Helichrysum italicum) is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, known for its aromatic golden-yellow flowers and its long-lasting dried form, which has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and skin-healing properties.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Everlasting is Helichrysum italicum. This plant belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Asterales, family Asteraceae, genus Helichrysum, and species italicum. It is commonly known as Everlasting in South Africa and as Immortelle in France. The plant is also known by the synonyms Helichrysum nummularium and Helichrysum orientale.

Plant Description and Morphology

Everlasting is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters, with a compact, bushy structure.

Its leaves are narrow, linear, and slightly serrated, arranged in a basal rosette with a greyish-white underside. The flowers are small, yellowish to golden, and borne in compact clusters at the ends of the stems. The root system is fibrous and shallow, allowing the plant to thrive in well-drained soils.

The plant has a distinctive honey-like aroma, with a slightly bitter taste, and its leaves and flowers have a dry, papery texture.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Everlasting is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly southern Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East.

It thrives in areas with mild, wet winters and hot, dry summers, typical of the Mediterranean climate. The plant is commonly found in rocky soils, slopes, and coastal areas where it can receive ample sunlight. It grows best in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Its wide geographic distribution is supported by its adaptability to various ecological conditions across its native range.

The primary plant parts used medicinally in Everlasting (Helichrysum italicum) are the dried flower heads and the leaves.

The flower heads are most commonly harvested and used due to their high concentration of essential oils and active compounds. Leaves are also utilized, particularly in certain traditional preparations. The flowers are typically collected when fully open and dried under controlled conditions to preserve their potency.

Both parts are often used in tinctures, oils, and infusions for their therapeutic properties.

The preparation methods for Everlasting (Helichrysum italicum) in herbal medicine primarily involve using the dried flowers, which are harvested at peak potency.

Additionally, essential oils are distilled from the plant, providing a concentrated form for aromatherapy and topical applications.

The dried flowers can also be ground into a powder and used in capsules or as a spice in culinary applications.

Safety, Side Effects, and Contraindications

When used appropriately, Everlasting (Helichrysum italicum) is generally considered safe, but it is important to be aware of potential side effects, contraindications, and allergy risks.

Common side effects may include skin irritation, gastrointestinal discomfort, or allergic reactions in sensitive individuals. It is contraindicated for people with known allergies to plants in the Asteraceae family, such as ragweed or daisies. Pregnant and breastfeeding women should avoid using Everlasting due to limited safety data and potential risks to fetal or infant development.

Always consult a qualified healthcare provider before use, especially for long-term or high-dose applications.

Everlasting (Helichrysum italicum) may interact with certain pharmaceuticals and supplements, potentially altering their effectiveness or increasing the risk of side effects.

It can interfere with blood thinners like warfarin by enhancing their anticoagulant effects, which may lead to increased bleeding risk. Combining Everlasting with supplements such as garlic or ginkgo biloba may amplify their blood-thinning properties, requiring careful monitoring. It is also important to note that Everlasting may affect liver enzymes, potentially altering the metabolism of other medications.

Always consult a healthcare provider before using Everlasting alongside any pharmaceuticals or supplements.

Cultivation and Harvesting

Everlasting (Helichrysum italicum) is typically cultivated in Mediterranean climates and requires well-drained, loamy soil with a slightly acidic to neutral pH.

It thrives in full sun, needing at least 6 to 8 hours of direct sunlight daily for optimal growth. Water should be provided sparingly, as the plant is drought-tolerant and overwatering can lead to root rot. Propagation is often done through seeds or cuttings, with cuttings being more reliable for preserving the plant's aromatic qualities. The best time to harvest is during the early morning or late afternoon, when essential oils are most concentrated.

The plant reaches maturity in about 12 to 18 months, and harvesting should be done by cutting the flower heads just before they fully open to preserve their potency and color.

Processing and Extraction

Processing and Extraction of Everlasting (Helichrysum italicum) involves several precise steps to preserve its active compounds.

The plant material is first dried under controlled conditions to reduce moisture content and prevent microbial growth. Once dried, the flowers are ground into a fine powder to increase surface area for efficient extraction. The essential oil is then obtained through steam distillation, a process that uses heat and pressure to release the volatile aromatic compounds.

This method ensures the retention of the plant's therapeutic properties in the final product.

Storage and Shelf Life

Everlasting (Helichrysum italicum) should be stored in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight to maintain its potency.

The shelf life of Everlasting is typically 1 to 2 years when stored properly. Exposure to light can degrade its essential oils, reducing its effectiveness over time. It is important to keep the herb in an airtight container to prevent moisture absorption.

High humidity can cause mold growth and spoilage, so storage in a moisture-controlled environment is essential.
