Everlasting (Helichrysum italicum)

Everlasting (Helichrysum italicum) is a member of the Asteraceae family, native to Southern Europe, Mediterranean Region, and Italy. Traditionally, its leaves, flowers, and resin have been used for infusions, oil infusions, and decoctions.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and carminative actions, and has a long history of use in mediterranean herbal traditions, european herbal medicine, and ayurvedic medicine.

Perennial herbaceous plant.

Leaves
Broad leaves with upper surface golden yellow and lower surface pale green, bearing distinct stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Inflorescences consist of dense clusters of small, golden-yellow flowers with five petals and five sepals, arranged in a compact, spherical shape, displaying actinomorphic symmetry.
Stems
Terrestrial, erect growth habit, opposite branching pattern, glabrous surface, nodes with persistent stipitate bases, hollow internodes, persistent leaf scars.
