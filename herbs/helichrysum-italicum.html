Scientific and Botanical Profile

Everlasting, with botanical name Helichrysum italicum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family, known for its striking golden-yellow flowers that retain their color even after drying.

Commonly referred to as Immortelle, Golden Everlasting, Italian Everlasting, Golden Mary, Golden Shield, Golden Heather, Golden Scotch Heather, Golden Marguerite, and Golden Geranium, this species is native to the Mediterranean region, including southern Europe, North Africa, and parts of western Asia. Morphologically, H. italicum is a low-growing shrub with narrow, aromatic leaves and dense clusters of small, papery flowers that resemble tiny sunflowers, often persisting long after the plant has dried.

It is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and has a long history of use in traditional medicine and as a natural dye.

History and Cultural Relevance

Everlasting was used by ancient civilizations such as the Greeks and Romans for its aromatic and medicinal properties, often incorporated into perfumes and as a symbol of immortality due to its ability to retain its color and form even after drying.

In traditional medicine systems like Ayurveda and Chinese medicine, it has been valued for its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and calming effects, often used to treat respiratory ailments and skin conditions. Culturally, it has played a significant role in rituals and ceremonies, particularly in Mediterranean regions, where it was used in wedding bouquets and as a symbol of love and longevity. Folk traditions also associate it with protection against evil spirits, and it has been used in various healing practices passed down through generations.

Today, its essential oil is still widely used in aromatherapy and natural remedies for anxiety, insomnia, and digestive issues, highlighting its enduring relevance in both traditional and modern wellness practices.

Chemical Composition and Nutritional Profile

Everlasting contains a rich array of bioactive compounds, including essential oils, flavonoids, terpenes, and alkaloids, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

The essential oil of Helichrysum italicum is particularly notable for its high concentration of sesquiterpene lactones, such as helichrysetin and lactucin, which are known for their anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. In terms of nutritional profile, it is a good source of vitamins A and C, as well as minerals like calcium, iron, and magnesium, which support immune function and overall cellular health. The antioxidants present in Everlasting help neutralize free radicals, reducing oxidative stress and supporting the body's natural defense mechanisms.

Its active compounds work synergistically to promote skin healing, reduce inflammation, and enhance mood, making it a valuable herb in both traditional and modern herbal medicine.

Forms, Preparation and Usage

Helichrysum italicum has a variety of forms available, including fresh and dried plant material, tinctures, powders, essential oils, and capsules, each offering different methods of preparation and usage.

It can be prepared as a tea by steeping dried leaves in hot water, or as a decoction by boiling the plant material for a longer period, while infusions involve soaking the herb in liquid for a shorter time. For topical use, it can be applied directly as an essential oil diluted with a carrier oil or as a powder mixed into creams or salves. The recommended dosage for adults is typically 1-2 teaspoons of tea three times daily, while children should only use it under medical supervision, with a lower dosage.

Due to its potent nature, it is advised to use helichrysum italicum cautiously, with short durations and limited frequency to avoid potential side effects.

Safety, Side Effects and Contraindications

Helichrysum italicum can be a beneficial medicinal plant with potential therapeutic properties, but its use requires careful consideration due to its safety profile.

While generally considered safe when used in recommended doses, it may cause side effects such as skin irritation, gastrointestinal discomfort, or allergic reactions in some individuals. It is important to note that helichrysum italicum may interact with certain medications, including anticoagulants and antidepressants, potentially increasing the risk of adverse effects. Special populations such as pregnant or breastfeeding women, as well as individuals with chronic illnesses, should avoid its use unless specifically advised by a healthcare professional.

To ensure safe use, always consult with a qualified healthcare provider before incorporating helichrysum italicum into your regimen, and follow recommended dosages to minimize risks.

Growing, Harvesting and Storage

Helichrysum italicum grows best in well-drained, loamy soil with a pH between 6.

0 and 7. 5, in full sun to partial shade, and requires moderate water during the growing season, with careful attention to avoid waterlogging. It thrives in temperate climates and is relatively drought-tolerant once established, making it suitable for Mediterranean and similar climates. Regular pruning and weed control are essential for maintaining plant health and encouraging bushy growth, while mulching helps retain moisture and suppress weeds. Harvesting should occur in the early morning when essential oils are most concentrated, using sharp scissors to cut the flower heads just before full bloom to preserve potency and aroma.

After harvesting, the plant material should be dried in a cool, dark, well-ventilated area to prevent mold, then stored in airtight glass containers in a cool, dark place or refrigerated to maintain its medicinal properties.
