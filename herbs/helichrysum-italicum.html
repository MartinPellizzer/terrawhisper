Everlasting (Helichrysum italicum): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

Everlasting (Helichrysum italicum) is a revered herb in herbal medicine known for its potent anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and wound-healing properties, often used to support skin health, digestive function, and respiratory wellness.

What is Everlasting (Helichrysum Italicum)?

Everlasting (Helichrysum italicum) is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, known for its aromatic golden-yellow flowers and its long-lasting dried form, which has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and skin-healing properties.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Everlasting is Helichrysum italicum. This plant belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Asterales, family Asteraceae, genus Helichrysum, and species italicum. It is commonly known as Everlasting in South Africa and as Immortelle in France. The plant is also known by the synonyms Helichrysum nummularium and Helichrysum orientale.

Plant Description and Morphology

Everlasting is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters, with a compact, bushy structure.

Its leaves are narrow, linear, and slightly serrated, arranged in a basal rosette with a greyish-white underside. The flowers are small, yellowish to golden, and borne in compact clusters at the ends of the stems. The root system is fibrous and shallow, allowing the plant to thrive in well-drained soils.

The plant has a distinctive honey-like aroma, with a slightly bitter taste, and its leaves and flowers have a dry, papery texture.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Native to the Mediterranean region, Everlasting (Helichrysum italicum) is primarily found in southern Europe, including countries such as Italy, Greece, and Spain, as well as parts of North Africa and the Middle East.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The aerial parts of Everlasting, including the flowers and leaves, are primarily used medicinally due to their high concentration of essential oils and bioactive compounds.

Phytochemical Composition

The phytochemical composition of Everlasting (Helichrysum italicum) includes a diverse array of bioactive compounds such as sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, essential oils, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties and therapeutic applications in herbal medicine.

Pharmacological Properties

Helichrysum italicum exhibits a range of pharmacological properties including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, antioxidant, and wound-healing effects, supported by both traditional use and modern scientific research.

Mechanisms of Action

The mechanisms of action of Everlasting (Helichrysum italicum) involve its bioactive compounds, such as sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and essential oils, which exhibit anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and analgesic properties by modulating cellular signaling pathways and reducing oxidative stress.

Therapeutic Uses and Indications

Helichrysum italicum, known as Everlasting, is widely used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and wound-healing properties, making it effective for conditions such as digestive disorders, skin ailments, and respiratory issues.

Preparation Methods and Forms

Everlasting (Helichrysum italicum) is typically prepared as an essential oil through steam distillation of its dried flowers, and also used in herbal teas, tinctures, and topical salves for its therapeutic properties.

Dosage and Administration

The typical dosage of Everlasting (Helichrysum italicum) for herbal use ranges from 200 to 500 mg per day, usually in the form of standardized extract, with the exact amount depending on the concentration of active compounds and the individual's health condition.

Safety, Side Effects, and Contraindications

When used appropriately, Everlasting (Helichrysum italicum) is generally safe, but it may cause mild side effects such as skin irritation or gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals, and it should be avoided during pregnancy, breastfeeding, and by those with known allergies to plants in the Asteraceae family.

Drug Interactions

Everlasting (Helichrysum italicum) may interact with medications that affect blood clotting, such as anticoagulants or antiplatelet drugs, potentially increasing the risk of bleeding when used concurrently.

Toxicity and Precautions

While Everlasting (Helichrysum italicum) is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses, it may cause mild gastrointestinal discomfort or allergic reactions in some individuals, and it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before use, especially during pregnancy, breastfeeding, or for those with chronic health conditions.

Cultivation and Harvesting

Helichrysum italicum is typically cultivated in Mediterranean climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure, harvested during the summer months when the flowers are fully open and the essential oils are most concentrated.

Processing and Extraction

The processing and extraction of Everlasting (Helichrysum italicum) typically involve harvesting the dried flower heads, followed by steam distillation to obtain the essential oil, which is then purified and standardized for use in herbal medicine.

Storage and Shelf Life

Everlasting (Helichrysum italicum) should be stored in an airtight container in a cool, dark, and dry place to maintain its potency, with a shelf life of up to 2-3 years when properly preserved.

Scientific Research and Evidence

Scientific research and evidence supporting the use of Everlasting (Helichrysum italicum) in herbal medicine includes studies highlighting its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, as well as its potential role in wound healing and skin regeneration.

Traditional and Historical Uses
