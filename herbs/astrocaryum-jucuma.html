Jucuma (Astrocaryum Jucuma)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Astrocaryum jucuma.

Jucuma, scientifically known as Astrocaryum jucuma, is a palm tree native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Venezuela, and Colombia. It is widely recognized as a medicinal herb and adaptogen, valued for its ability to help the body resist stress and promote overall well-being. The fruit of the jucuma tree is rich in nutrients, including antioxidants, vitamins, and minerals, and is traditionally used to support digestive health, reduce inflammation, and enhance immune function. In traditional medicine, jucuma has been used by indigenous communities to treat ailments such as digestive disorders, skin conditions, and respiratory issues, while its seeds are also used as a spice due to their distinctive nutty flavor. Modern wellness applications include its incorporation into dietary supplements and functional foods, with its rare active compound, astrocaryum acid, being studied for potential anti-inflammatory and anti-cancer properties.
