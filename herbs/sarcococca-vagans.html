What's the taxonomical classification of Sarcococca vagans?

Sarcococca vagans belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is part of the order Buxales and falls under the family Buxaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by its placement within the genus Sarcococca, specifically as the species vagans.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Sarcococca vagans can cause severe gastrointestinal distress including nausea, vomiting, and intense abdominal cramping if ingested. These symptoms often arise due to the presence of toxic alkaloids that irritate the lining of the digestive tract. Some individuals may also experience neurological disturbances such as dizziness or lightheadedness following consumption. Prolonged exposure or higher doses could potentially lead to more systemic issues like dehydration from fluid loss. It is critical to avoid all contact with the plant to prevent accidental poisoning.
