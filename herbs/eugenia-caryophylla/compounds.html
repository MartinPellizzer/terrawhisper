Cloves (Eugenia caryophylla) Compounds

The primary compounds in cloves include essential oil, which contains eugenol, and flavonoids like quercetin. Tannins and phenolic acids contribute to antioxidant properties. Coumarin and terpenoids support anti-inflammatory effects, while glycosides and saponins may aid in digestive health. Alkaloids and anthocyanins add to the plant's medicinal complexity.

Cloves (Eugenia caryophylla) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Cloves (Eugenia caryophylla) is the primary active compound, containing eugenol as its main constituent. It possesses strong antimicrobial properties and is used in traditional medicine for its analgesic and antiseptic effects.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Cloves (Eugenia caryophylla). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. These compounds may support immune function and have anti-inflammatory effects, making them important in traditional herbal medicine.

3. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Cloves (Eugenia caryophylla). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and has antimicrobial effects. Tannins help in reducing inflammation and may support digestive health. They are concentrated in the flower buds and are responsible for some of clove's traditional medicinal uses.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in Cloves (Eugenia caryophylla). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in digestive and respiratory health. This compound helps enhance the therapeutic effects of clove oil and extracts in herbal medicine.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a compound found in Cloves (Eugenia caryophylla). It contributes to the plant's aromatic properties and has potential anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory effects. Coumarin is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its soothing and digestive benefits.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Cloves (Eugenia caryophylla). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by supporting anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Glycosides in cloves are often associated with the plant's use in traditional herbal medicine for digestive and respiratory ailments.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a key class of compounds found in cloves (Eugenia caryophylla). They contribute to the plant's aromatic profile and possess antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds are responsible for many of the therapeutic effects associated with clove use in herbal medicine.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Cloves (Eugenia caryophylla). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponin helps in enhancing the absorption of certain nutrients and may support digestive health. It is one of several bioactive components in cloves that contribute to its medicinal value.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Cloves (Eugenia caryophylla). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Alkaloids in cloves are often associated with the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and respiratory support.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a pigment found in Cloves (Eugenia caryophylla). It contributes to the plant's color and may have antioxidant properties. While not the primary active compound in cloves, anthocyanin supports overall plant health and may enhance the medicinal profile of the plant.
