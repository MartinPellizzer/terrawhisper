What's the taxonomical classification of Clematis macropetala?

Clematis macropetala belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Ranunculales. This plant is a member of the family Ranunculaceae, falling under the genus Clematis, and is specifically identified by the species epithet macropetala.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

These leaves consist of three distinct leaflets that are ovate to lanceolate in shape with finely serrated margins. The stems are slender and capable of twisting around supports to facilitate upward growth. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Clematis macropetala contains protoanemonin, a toxic lactone that serves as the plant's primary chemical defense mechanism. This compound is produced in a non-toxic form called ranunculin and converts into the irritating protoanemonin when the plant tissue is damaged or crushed. When applied to the skin, these chemical constituents can cause severe dermatitis and blistering. Ingesting the plant can lead to gastrointestinal distress and more systemic toxicity due to these same irritant properties. Consequently, there are no documented medicinal uses for this specific species, as its chemical makeup is primarily characterized by its potential for irritation and toxicity.
