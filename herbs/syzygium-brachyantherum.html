Syzygium brachyantherum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Syzygium brachyantherum has oppositely arranged, elliptical leaves that measure approximately 5 to 12 centimeters in length and possess a coriaceous texture with entire margins. The foliage typically exhibits a dark green upper surface and a slightly paler underside, often featuring fine reticulate venation. Its reproductive structures consist of small, actinomorphic flowers that emerge from axillary positions. The stamens are notably short relative to other species in the genus, which gives the plant its specific epithet. Small, fleshy drupes develop from the ovaries to facilitate seed dispersal.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Syzygium brachyantherum can cause adverse reactions such as skin irritation, digestive upset, or allergic responses if consumed or applied topically without proper preparation. Because there is very little clinical research on this specific species, the exact toxicological profile remains largely unknown to the scientific community. Accidental ingestion might lead to nausea or abdominal discomfort in sensitive subjects.
