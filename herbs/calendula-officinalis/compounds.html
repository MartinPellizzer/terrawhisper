Marigold (Calendula officinalis) Compounds

The primary compounds in Marigold include flavonoids, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Essential oils contribute to its antimicrobial effects. Tannins and terpenoids also play roles in its therapeutic actions. These compounds work together to support skin health and wound healing.

Marigold (Calendula officinalis) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are key active compounds in Marigold (Calendula officinalis). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help reduce skin irritation and promote wound healing. Flavonoids are widely used in herbal medicine for their therapeutic benefits.

2. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound of Marigold (Calendula officinalis). It contains aromatic compounds like bisabolol and flavonoids, which contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. This oil is commonly used in topical applications for skin healing and wound care.

3. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Marigold (Calendula officinalis). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. Tannins help reduce skin irritation and promote wound healing. They are found in the flowers and are responsible for some of the plant's therapeutic effects.

4. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Marigold (Calendula officinalis). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Common terpenoids in marigold include alpha-tocopherol and beta-carotene, which support skin healing and immune function. These compounds are key to the plant's traditional and modern medicinal uses.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Marigold (Calendula officinalis). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound helps support skin health and wound healing, which are central to the therapeutic use of Calendula. Its presence enhances the plant's overall medicinal efficacy.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Marigold (Calendula officinalis). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. Coumarin also acts as a natural anticoagulant, supporting cardiovascular health. Its presence enhances the therapeutic value of Marigold in herbal medicine.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Marigold (Calendula officinalis). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and wound-healing properties. Glycosides in Calendula help reduce skin irritation and promote tissue regeneration. These compounds are commonly used in topical preparations for dermatological applications.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Marigold (Calendula officinalis). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponins help enhance the bioavailability of other active compounds in Marigold. They are commonly used in herbal formulations for their skin-soothing effects.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Marigold (Calendula officinalis). It contributes to the flower's vibrant color and has antioxidant properties. This compound supports skin health and may reduce inflammation, making it a valuable component in herbal medicine.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Marigold (Calendula officinalis). It contributes to the plant's pharmacological properties, though it is not the primary active constituent. Alkaloids in Marigold are less prominent compared to flavonoids and triterpenes, which are more widely studied for their therapeutic effects.
