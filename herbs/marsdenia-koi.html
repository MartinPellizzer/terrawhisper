Marsdenia koi

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Marsdenia koi has woody, climbing stems that produce ovate to elliptical leaves with entire margins and a glossy texture. The foliage typically features prominent pinnate venation and can grow in dense, overlapping patterns along the vines. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the axils, often displaying a delicate coloration characteristic of the Apocynaceae family. These blossoms are supported by slender pedicels that allow them to dangle or extend from the main stem. The plant's structural development relies on these flexible tendrils to navigate through surrounding vegetation for light.

      What are the herbal preparations of Marsdenia koi?

      Marsdenia koi is prepared as a decoction, a topical paste, or a concentrated tincture depending on the specific medicinal requirement.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Marsdenia koi can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and potential gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Contact with the plant's sap may lead to redness, itching, or localized dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Ingesting any part of the plant could result in nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain due to its chemical composition. While specific clinical studies on humans are limited, its toxicity profile suggests caution for both people and pets. Always wear gloves when handling the plant to minimize the risk of contact dermatitis.
