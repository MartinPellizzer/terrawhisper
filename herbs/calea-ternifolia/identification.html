Botanical Identification of Blue Mistflower (Calea ternifolia)

To identify Blue Mistflower (Calea ternifolia) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Blue Mistflower, or Calea ternifolia, belongs to the plant kingdom, specifically the division of flowering plants, class of monocots, order of Asphodelales, family Acanthaceae, genus Calea, and species Calea ternifolia. It is a member of the Acanthaceae family, known for its ornamental and medicinal uses.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Blue Mistflower is Calea ternifolia, first described by the botanist Carl von Linné. It is also known by several synonyms, including Calea alba and Calea micrantha, reflecting historical taxonomic revisions. Common names include Blue Mistflower, Mistflower, and Blue Mistplant, while regional and historical names vary across cultures and regions.

Morphological Characteristics

Blue Mistflower (Calea ternifolia) is an upright, herbaceous perennial with a fibrous root system. Its stems are hairy and branched, growing up to 1-2 meters tall. Leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and slightly hairy, with a distinctive blue-gray color. The inflorescence is a dense, terminal cluster of blue-violet flowers, each with a unique, mist-like appearance. The fruit is a dry capsule containing small, black seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Blue Mistflower can be distinguished by its narrow, gray-green leaves with prominent veins, opposite arrangement, and clusters of blue-violet flowers. It has a branched stem and lacks the fuzzy texture seen in similar species. The flowers are tubular with a distinctive blue mist appearance, aiding in accurate identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Blue Mistflower (Calea ternifolia) is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and the Caribbean. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils. It typically grows at low to mid-altitudes, often in shaded or semi-shaded woodland areas.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Blue Mistflower blooms from late summer to early fall, typically between August and October. Seed formation occurs in late fall, around November. For identification purposes, the plant is most easily recognized during its blooming period, when the characteristic blue flowers are visible.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Calea ternifolia is closely related to species within the genus Calea, which belongs to the Asteraceae family. It shares key traits such as capitulum structure and leaf morphology with other members of this family. These relationships help in understanding its ecological and medicinal significance within the broader context of herbal medicine.
