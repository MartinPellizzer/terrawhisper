1. Ternofoline

Calea ternifolia ternofoline is a unique alkaloid compound extracted from the plant.

It has been traditionally used in Mexican and Latin American folk medicine for its sedative and anxiolytic properties. Ternofoline is believed to have a calming effect on the nervous system, reducing anxiety and stress levels.

Its potential benefits also extend to treating insomnia, epilepsy, and other neurological disorders.

2. Isoternofoline

Calea ternifolia isoternofoline is a significant compound found in this plant species.

Isoternofoline has been studied for its potential pharmacological activities, including antimicrobial, antioxidant, and anti-inflammatory properties. Its chemical structure consists of a unique combination of indole and quinoline rings, which may contribute to its therapeutic effects.

Further research is needed to fully understand the bioactivities and potential medicinal applications of isoternofoline.

3. Zaluzanin

Calea ternifolia zaluzanin is a perennial herb native to Mexico and Central America.

Its leaves are pinnatifid with 3-5 lobes, while its flowers are bright yellow, daisy-like, and have a distinctive sweet fragrance.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for centuries to treat various ailments, including insomnia, anxiety, and depression, as well as being used as an insect repellent.

4. Ternofolinone

Calea ternifolia ternofolinone is a type of alkaloid compound extracted from the plant's leaves and stems.

This unique compound has been traditionally used in Mexican herbal medicine for centuries to induce vivid dreams, improve sleep quality, and treat anxiety and depression.

Ternofolinone is believed to interact with the brain's neurotransmitters, promoting relaxation and reducing stress levels.

5. Zaluzaninin

Calea ternifolia zaluzaninin is a sesquiterpene lactone found in the leaves of this plant.

It has been traditionally used in Mexico and Central America for its sedative and hypnotic properties, often consumed as an infusion or tea to promote relaxation and reduce anxiety.

Zaluzaninin's mechanisms of action are not fully understood, but it is believed to interact with GABA receptors in the brain, contributing to its calming effects.

6. Caleic acid

Calea ternifolia caleic acid is a bioactive compound found in the plant's leaves.

It has been traditionally used in Mexican folk medicine to treat various ailments, including insomnia, anxiety, and respiratory issues.

Caleic acid has been shown to possess sedative and anxiolytic properties, making it a potential natural remedy for stress-related disorders.

8. Sesquiterpenoids

Calea ternifolia sesquiterpenoids is a class of bioactive compounds extracted from its leaves and flowers.

9. Flavonoids

Calea ternifolia flavonoids is a class of bioactive compounds found in its leaves and stems.

10. Tannins

Calea ternifolia tannins is a type of compound found in its leaves.

Tannins are a group of polyphenolic compounds that give the plant its characteristic bitter taste and astringent properties.

In traditional medicine, Calea ternifolia tannins have been used to treat various ailments such as fever, inflammation, and respiratory issues due to their antimicrobial and antiseptic properties.
