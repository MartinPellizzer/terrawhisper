10 Possible Side Effects Of Calea Ternifolia

Calea ternifolia has some side effects when used improperly, such as dizziness, hallucinations, and confusion.

These side effects can occur when the herb is consumed in large quantities or combined with other substances, including medications and other herbs.

If left unmanaged, these side effects can worsen a person's life by impairing their ability to work or care for themselves, leading to financial and emotional distress, as well as potential accidents and injuries.

1. Causes anxiety attacks

Calea ternifolia causes anxiety attacks due to its ability to stimulate the brain's neurotransmitters, leading to increased activity and alertness.

This can result in feelings of panic, unease, and fear. Additionally, Calea ternifolia has been known to induce vivid dreams and lucid dreaming states, which can further exacerbate anxiety levels.

Its psychoactive properties also contribute to the likelihood of anxiety attacks as it interacts with the brain's neurotransmitter systems.

2. Induces insomnia episodes

Calea ternifolia induces insomnia episodes due to its ability to stimulate the central nervous system and increase alertness.

The plant's alkaloids, particularly tropine and scopine, can affect the brain's neurotransmitters, leading to difficulty falling asleep or staying asleep. Additionally, Calea ternifolia's stimulating properties may interfere with the body's natural sleep-wake cycle, causing insomnia in some individuals.

As a result, users may experience restless nights and fatigue during the day.

3. Triggers allergic reactions

Calea ternifolia triggers allergic reactions due to its high concentration of alkaloids, primarily diterpenoid alkaloids and steroidal alkaloids.

These compounds can cause an immune response in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling.

Additionally, the plant's volatile oils may exacerbate respiratory issues like asthma or bronchitis, further contributing to allergic reactions.

4. Increases blood pressure

Calea ternifolia increases blood pressure due to its ability to stimulate the sympathetic nervous system.

This stimulation causes a release of stress hormones like adrenaline, which can lead to an increase in heart rate and blood pressure.

Additionally, the herb's bioactive compounds, such as tropine and scopolamine, may contribute to this effect by activating alpha-adrenergic receptors and increasing peripheral resistance, further elevating blood pressure.

5. Lowers body temperature

Calea ternifolia lowers body temperature in some individuals.

This side effect is attributed to the presence of scopolamine and hyoscine, two alkaloids that are naturally occurring in the plant. These compounds can bind to muscarinic receptors in the brain, leading to a decrease in body temperature, often accompanied by feelings of drowsiness and lethargy.

Additionally, the plant's anticholinergic properties may contribute to this effect by slowing down the body's metabolic rate and heart function, ultimately resulting in a lower core temperature.

6. Produces stomach cramps

Calea ternifolia produces stomach cramps due to its ability to stimulate the digestive system.

The plant contains a number of bioactive compounds, including alkaloids and flavonoids, which can cause contractions in the smooth muscle of the gastrointestinal tract, leading to cramping sensations.

Additionally, Calea ternifolia may also alter gut motility, further contributing to stomach discomfort and pain.

7. Enhances hallucinations

Calea ternifolia enhances hallucinations by stimulating the brain's neurotransmitters, particularly serotonin and acetylcholine.

This results in altered perception, thought patterns, and sensory experiences, often leading to vivid and intense visuals, sounds, and emotions.

As the plant interacts with the brain's chemistry, users may experience a heightened sense of awareness, imagination, and creativity, which can be both fascinating and overwhelming.

8. Disrupts digestion process

Calea ternifolia disrupts digestion process in some individuals due to its ability to increase stomach acid secretion and slow down gastric emptying.

This can lead to feelings of nausea, bloating, and stomach discomfort.

Additionally, Calea ternifolia's stimulation of the nervous system may cause an imbalance in gut motility, further exacerbating digestive issues.

9. Raises heart rate

Calea ternifolia raises heart rate due to its ability to stimulate the cardiovascular system.

This is attributed to the presence of alkaloids such as tropine and scopolamine, which can increase cardiac output by stimulating the sympathetic nervous system. Additionally, the herb's beta-adrenergic properties may contribute to this effect by increasing heart rate and blood pressure.

As a result, users may experience palpitations or rapid heartbeat when consuming Calea ternifolia.

10. Causes dizziness spells

Calea ternifolia causes dizziness spells when ingested.

This is due to the presence of alkaloids, specifically scopolamine and hyoscyamine, which can affect the brain's neurotransmitters and lead to altered mental states.

Additionally, the herb's sedative properties can cause a reduction in blood pressure, further contributing to feelings of dizziness or lightheadedness.
