Oreocharis auricula

What's the taxonomical classification of Oreocharis auricula?

Oreocharis auricula belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the diverse order Lamiales. As a member of the family Gesneriaceae, this plant is specifically categorized under the genus Oreocharis, with its unique identity defined by the species name auricula.

Simultaneously, its anti-inflammatory action works by modulating biochemical pathways to decrease swelling and pain. Research continues to explore how these chemical constituents can be utilized for potential therapeutic applications.

As discussed in a study, seven new acylated C-glycosylflavones named oreocharioside A-G were isolated from the whole plant of Oreocharis auricula. These compounds were evaluated for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities. Compounds 1 and 2 demonstrated significant DPPH and ABTS radical scavenging activities with IC50 values ranging from 0.32 to 3.20 μg/mL. Additionally, compounds 2 and 3 exhibited the highest potency among all the new compounds regarding the reduction of TNF-α production.
