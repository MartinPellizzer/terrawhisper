Witch Hazel (Hamamelis virginiana) Compounds

The primary compounds in Witch Hazel include tannins, which have astringent properties, and flavonoids, known for their antioxidant effects. Phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory qualities, while coumarin adds mild antiseptic properties. Other compounds like glycosides, saponins, and anthocyanins support its therapeutic uses in herbal medicine.

Witch Hazel (Hamamelis virginiana) Monograph

1. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Witch Hazel (Hamamelis virginiana). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties, helping to reduce inflammation and soothe irritated skin. Tannins also support wound healing and are responsible for many of the plant's traditional uses in herbal medicine.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are key active compounds in Witch Hazel (Hamamelis virginiana). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. These compounds help reduce swelling and tighten tissues, making Witch Hazel useful for skin conditions and wound healing. Flavonoids also support the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Witch Hazel (Hamamelis virginiana). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. This compound helps reduce skin irritation and promote wound healing. Its presence supports the traditional use of Witch Hazel in herbal medicine.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Witch Hazel (Hamamelis virginiana). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. Coumarin also helps in reducing skin irritation and promoting healing. Its presence supports the traditional use of Witch Hazel in topical treatments.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Witch Hazel (Hamamelis virginiana). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. Glycosides in Witch Hazel help reduce swelling and tighten tissues, making it effective for skin conditions and wounds. These compounds are extracted from the bark and leaves during preparation.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Witch Hazel (Hamamelis virginiana). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and astringent properties, helping to soothe skin irritation and reduce excess oil. Saponins also support the plant's traditional use in treating wounds and skin conditions.

7. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Witch Hazel (Hamamelis virginiana). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting skin health and wound healing. This compound helps reduce redness and irritation, making Witch Hazel effective in topical treatments.

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of Witch Hazel (Hamamelis virginiana) contains volatile compounds like methyl salicylate and methyl esters, which contribute to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It is commonly used in topical applications for skin irritation and minor wounds.

9. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a key group of active compounds in Witch Hazel (Hamamelis virginiana). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and astringent properties, making them valuable in herbal medicine for skin care and wound healing. These compounds are responsible for many of the therapeutic effects associated with Witch Hazel extracts.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Witch Hazel (Hamamelis virginiana). While Witch Hazel is more renowned for its tannins, it does contain trace alkaloids, which may contribute to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. These compounds are less abundant compared to other constituents in the plant.
