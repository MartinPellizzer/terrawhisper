Desmos chinensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Desmos chinensis has woody climbing vines characterized by slender, twining stems and opposite, elliptical to oblong leaves with entire margins. The foliage is typically dark green and glossy, featuring distinct pinnate venation that is visible on the surface. Small, fragrant flowers emerge in clusters, displaying white or pale yellowish petals that are often funnel-shaped. The reproductive structures consist of small, globose fruits that develop into fleshy drupes. These botanical features collectively define the climbing habit and reproductive morphology of the species.
