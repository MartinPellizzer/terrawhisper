Desmos chinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Desmos chinensis?

Desmos chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is part of the order Magnoliales and the family Annonaceae. Finally, it is placed within the genus Desmos, specifically identified by the species epithet chinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Desmos chinensis has woody climbing vines characterized by slender, twining stems and opposite, elliptical to oblong leaves with entire margins. The foliage is typically dark green and glossy, featuring distinct pinnate venation that is visible on the surface. Small, fragrant flowers emerge in clusters, displaying white or pale yellowish petals that are often funnel-shaped. The reproductive structures consist of small, globose fruits that develop into fleshy drupes.
