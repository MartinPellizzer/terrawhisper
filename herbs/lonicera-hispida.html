What's the taxonomical classification of Lonicera hispida?

Lonicera hispida belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is categorized under the order Dipsacales. Furthermore, this plant is a member of the family Caprifoliaceae, falling into the genus Lonicera, and is specifically identified by the species name hispida.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lonicera hispida has arching, deciduous stems that are characterized by being densely covered in fine, spreading hairs and often possessing small, sharp prickles. The leaves are simple, opposite, and oval to oblong in shape with entire margins and a somewhat pubescent texture. During the flowering season, the plant produces small, tubular, yellowish-white flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These flowers develop into fleshy, oblong berries that transition from green to a deep purple or black color when ripe. The overall growth habit is that of a low, spreading shrub that typically reaches heights of only a few feet.
