Cyananthus formosus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cyananthus formosus has a low-growing, cushion-like growth habit characterized by dense tufts of lanceolate to ovate leaves that are typically bright green and slightly fleshy. These leaves emerge from a basal rosette and possess finely serrated margins along their upper edges. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually a vibrant blue or violet color. These blossoms are borne on short pedicels that rise slightly above the foliage during the blooming season. The overall structure allows the plant to thrive in rocky, high-altitude crevices where it forms tight mats.
