10 Possible Side Effects Of Chamomilla Recutita

Chamomilla recutita has some side effects when used improperly, such as allergic reactions, digestive issues, and increased risk of bleeding.

These side effects can be caused by consuming large amounts or using high-quality extracts, which can irritate the digestive system and exacerbate existing allergies.

For example, a severe allergic reaction can lead to difficulty breathing, while excessive bleeding can worsen conditions like hemophilia or atherosclerosis.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Chamomilla recutita causes allergic reactions in some individuals due to its allergenic compounds such as apigenin and flavonoids.

These substances can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms like hives, itching, and difficulty breathing. Additionally, the plant's volatile oils may cause contact dermatitis, characterized by redness, swelling, and blistering of the skin upon exposure.

As a result, some people may experience adverse reactions when using Chamomilla recutita for medicinal or therapeutic purposes.

2. Triggers asthma attacks

Chamomilla recutita triggers asthma attacks when consumed in large quantities or for extended periods.

This is due to its potential to cause bronchial constriction and increased mucus production, which can exacerbate asthma symptoms.

Additionally, the apigenin present in chamomile may interact with certain medications used to treat asthma, further complicating breathing difficulties.

3. Interacts with medications

Chamomilla recutita interacts with medications that have a sedative effect, such as benzodiazepines, barbiturates, and opioids.

This interaction can lead to increased drowsiness, confusion, or difficulty breathing, which may be particularly concerning for individuals taking these medications for treating anxiety, insomnia, or pain.

4. Induces drowsiness rapidly

Chamomilla recutita induces drowsiness rapidly due to its ability to increase the levels of adenosine in the brain.

Adenosine is a neurotransmitter that promotes sleep and relaxation by slowing down neural activity. As Chamomilla recutita interacts with the body's natural chemistry, it enhances the sedative effects of adenosine, leading to feelings of drowsiness and fatigue.

This side effect is particularly common when taken in large doses or consumed close to bedtime.

5. Enhances sedative effects

Chamomilla recutita enhances sedative effects by interacting with GABA receptors in the brain, increasing the activity of this neurotransmitter that promotes relaxation and reduces anxiety.

Additionally, chamomile's flavonoids and terpenoids can bind to dopamine and serotonin receptors, further contributing to its calming effects on the nervous system.

As a result, people may experience increased drowsiness, lethargy, or reduced reaction time when using Chamomilla recutita.

6. Increases liver enzymes

Chamomilla recutita increases liver enzymes.

This is due to its potential to stimulate the liver's natural detoxification processes, causing a temporary elevation in enzyme levels.

The flavonoids and terpenoids present in chamomile may contribute to this effect, as they have been shown to increase liver metabolism and excretion of toxins.

7. Decreases blood clotting

Chamomilla recutita decreases blood clotting due to its ability to inhibit platelet aggregation and prolong bleeding time.

This is attributed to the flavonoids and phenolic acids present in the herb, which have been shown to reduce the activity of vitamin K-dependent clotting factors and platelet adhesion molecules.

As a result, individuals taking chamomilla recutita may experience increased bleeding or bruising, particularly when used in combination with anticoagulant medications.

8. Stimulates uterine contractions

Chamomilla recutita stimulates uterine contractions due to its estrogenic and prostaglandin-like properties.

The plant's active compounds, apigenin and luteolin, mimic the effects of estrogen on the uterus, leading to increased muscle tone and contraction.

Additionally, chamomile contains prostaglandins, hormone-like substances that stimulate uterine contractions and can even induce labor when taken in large amounts or at certain stages of pregnancy.

9. Affects fetal development

Chamomilla recutita affects fetal development.

This is because chamomile contains apigenin and luteolin, flavonoids that can cross the placenta and potentially alter fetal development.

Furthermore, the herb's estrogenic properties may mimic the effects of human estrogen, which could impact fetal growth and development.

10. Reduces milk production

Chamomilla recutita reduces milk production in some individuals due to its ability to stimulate the uterus and cause uterine contractions.

This can lead to a decrease in prolactin hormone levels, which is essential for milk production in lactating women. As a result, mothers may experience a decline in their milk supply, making it challenging to feed their babies.

The herb's phytochemicals, particularly apigenin and luteolin, are thought to be responsible for this effect, as they can bind to estrogen receptors and influence hormonal balance.
