10 Medicinal Constituents Of Chamomilla Recutita (Chamomile)

Chamomilla recutita has active constituents such as apigenin, bisabolol, and matricine.

These compounds possess anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and soothing properties. They can alleviate symptoms of anxiety and insomnia, promoting relaxation and improved sleep quality.

Furthermore, their antimicrobial and anti-irritant effects can soothe skin irritations and reduce inflammation, providing relief from eczema and other skin conditions, thereby enhancing the overall quality of life.

1. Apigenin

Chamomilla recutita apigenin is a flavonoid compound that has been extensively studied for its potential health benefits.

It is found in the flowers of Matricaria chamomilla and has been shown to have antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antispasmodic properties. Apigenin has also been found to have a sedative effect on the nervous system, which may contribute to chamomile's calming effects.

Additionally, apigenin has been investigated for its potential role in preventing cancer cell growth and improving cardiovascular health.

2. Chamazulene

Chamomilla recutita chamazulene is a sesquiterpene lactone responsible for the blue color and numerous therapeutic properties of chamomile flowers.

It is a powerful anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and antioxidant agent that helps to soothe digestive issues, reduce anxiety and stress, and promote relaxation.

Chamazulene has also been shown to possess antimicrobial and anticancer properties, making it a valuable component in herbal medicine and skincare products.

3. Luteolin

Chamomilla recutita luteolin is a flavonoid compound found in the flowers of this medicinal plant.

It has been shown to possess various biological activities, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, which may contribute to its traditional use in treating anxiety, insomnia, and digestive issues.

Additionally, luteolin has been reported to exhibit antibacterial and antiviral properties, making it a promising agent for the treatment of respiratory infections and skin conditions.

4. Quercetin

Chamomilla recutita quercetin is a flavonoid compound found in the flowers of the plant.

It has been used for centuries to treat various ailments, including anxiety, insomnia, and inflammation. Quercetin has anti-inflammatory properties that can help alleviate symptoms such as joint pain, arthritis, and allergies.

It also exhibits antioxidant activity, which can protect against cell damage caused by free radicals.

5. Nobiletin

Chamomilla recutita nobiletin is a flavonoid glycoside compound found in the flowers of Matricaria chamomilla L. It has been extensively studied for its medicinal properties and biological activities.

Nobiletin exhibits potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, making it a promising agent for treating various diseases, including cancer, cardiovascular disorders, and neurodegenerative conditions.

Its unique molecular structure allows it to interact with multiple cellular targets, conferring its therapeutic benefits.

6. Kaempferol

Chamomilla recutita kaempferol is a flavonoid compound that has been isolated from its flowers.

This bioactive compound exhibits numerous biological activities, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties. Kaempferol has been reported to have therapeutic potential in various diseases, such as cardiovascular disorders, cancer, and neurodegenerative diseases.

Additionally, it has been shown to have a synergistic effect with other compounds present in chamomile, enhancing its overall pharmacological activity.

7. Bisabolol

Chamomilla recutita bisabolol is a naturally occurring compound extracted from the flowers of the plant.

It has been used for centuries in traditional medicine to soothe skin irritations, wounds, and inflammation.

Bisabolol exhibits anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, making it an effective ingredient in skincare products for calming and protecting the skin.

8. Alpha-bisabolol oxide a

Chamomilla recutita alpha-bisabolol oxide A is a natural compound extracted from the flowers of this herb.

It has been traditionally used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine to promote relaxation, reduce stress and anxiety, and soothe skin irritations.

Alpha-bisabolol oxide A has anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, making it an effective ingredient in skincare products for treating acne, rosacea, and other skin conditions.

9. Alpha-bisabolol oxide b

Chamomilla recutita alpha-bisabolol oxide B is a biologically active compound found in the flowers of this daisy-like plant.

This natural molecule has been extensively studied for its therapeutic potential, exhibiting anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and soothing properties.

It has been shown to promote wound healing, reduce skin irritation, and even possess antimicrobial activity, making it a valuable ingredient in various cosmetics, pharmaceuticals, and traditional medicine practices.

10. Farnesene

Chamomilla recutita farnesene is a sesquiterpene compound found in the essential oil of the plant.

It is responsible for its calming and soothing properties, making it commonly used in aromatherapy to reduce anxiety and promote relaxation. Farnesene has been shown to possess anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects, making it beneficial in treating various health issues such as insomnia, menstrual cramps, and skin irritations.

Its sweet, floral aroma is also often used in skincare products and perfumes.
