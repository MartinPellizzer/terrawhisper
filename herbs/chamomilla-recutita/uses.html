German Chamomile (Chamomilla recutita) Uses

German Chamomile is commonly used to treat colic, gastrointestinal upset, and digestive discomfort. It helps relieve gastrointestinal cramps, spasms, and discomfort, making it useful for conditions like gastric ulcers and mouth ulcers. It is also applied for dental pain and to soothe inflammation in the digestive tract.

German Chamomile (Chamomilla recutita) Monograph

1. Colic

German Chamomile is effective for treating colic due to its antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties. It soothes intestinal cramps and reduces gastrointestinal discomfort. Often used in teas, it helps relieve pain and promote digestion in infants and adults alike. Its calming effects make it a trusted remedy in herbal medicine for colic symptoms.

2. Gastrointestinal upset

German Chamomile is effective for gastrointestinal upset due to its antispasmodic and carminative properties. It helps relieve cramps, bloating, and indigestion by soothing the digestive tract. Often used as a tea, it promotes calmness and aids in digestion. Its mild nature makes it suitable for regular use in managing mild digestive discomfort.

3. Mouth ulcers

German Chamomile is effective for treating mouth ulcers due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce pain and swelling while promoting healing. A diluted chamomile solution can be used as a mouth rinse or applied directly to the affected area. Regular use can speed up recovery and prevent infection.

4. Gastrointestinal cramps

German Chamomile is used to relieve gastrointestinal cramps due to its antispasmodic properties. It soothes the muscles of the digestive tract, reducing pain and discomfort. Often consumed as a tea, it provides gentle relief for conditions like irritable bowel syndrome and indigestion. Its mild nature makes it a safe option for many people seeking natural remedies.

5. Dental pain

German Chamomile is used to alleviate dental pain due to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. It can be applied as a mouth rinse or infused into a gel for localized relief. Its mild sedative effects also help reduce anxiety associated with dental procedures. Chamomile's essential oils, particularly bisabolol, contribute to its effectiveness in soothing gum and tooth discomfort.

6. Digestive discomfort

German Chamomile is commonly used to alleviate digestive discomfort such as bloating, cramps, and indigestion. Its antispasmodic properties help relax digestive muscles, while its mild sedative effect can ease stomach distress. It is often prepared as a tea to soothe the digestive system and promote comfort.

7. Gastrointestinal spasms

German Chamomile is effective for gastrointestinal spasms due to its antispasmodic properties. It helps relax smooth muscles in the digestive tract, reducing cramping and discomfort. Often used in teas or tinctures, it provides soothing relief for conditions like irritable bowel syndrome and colic. Its mild action makes it a preferred remedy in herbal medicine for digestive issues.

8. Digestive upset

German Chamomile is used to alleviate digestive upset by soothing the gastrointestinal tract. It reduces inflammation and spasms, making it effective for indigestion, bloating, and stomach cramps. Its mild sedative properties also help ease stress-related digestive issues. It is typically consumed as a tea or tincture.

9. Gastric ulcers

German Chamomile has demonstrated efficacy in treating gastric ulcers due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce stomach acid and soothe mucous membranes, promoting healing. Clinical studies suggest it can be a valuable adjunct in ulcer management when used alongside conventional treatments.
