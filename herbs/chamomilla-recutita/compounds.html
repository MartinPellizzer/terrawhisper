German Chamomile (Chamomilla recutita) Compounds

German Chamomile's primary active compounds include essential oil, which contains compounds like bisabolol and chamazulene. Coumarin contributes to its anti-inflammatory properties. Flavonoids and terpenoids support antioxidant and anti-spasmodic effects. Phenolic acids, glycosides, and tannins also play roles in its therapeutic actions.

German Chamomile (Chamomilla recutita) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of German Chamomile (Chamomilla recutita) is a key active compound, containing compounds like chamazulene and bisabolol. It exhibits anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and anxiolytic properties, making it widely used in herbal medicine for digestive and skin-related conditions.

2. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in German Chamomile (Chamomilla recutita). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. Coumarin also acts as a mild sedative and may help with digestive issues. Its presence supports the plant's traditional use in calming the nervous system.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in German Chamomile (Chamomilla recutita). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its use in traditional herbal medicine for calming and digestive support. Specific flavonoids like apigenin and quercetin are known for their therapeutic effects.

4. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in German Chamomile (Chamomilla recutita). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and calming effects. Specific terpenoids like bisabolol and chamazulene are responsible for its therapeutic properties. These compounds are key to the plant's use in herbal medicine.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in German Chamomile (Chamomilla recutita). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound helps in reducing oxidative stress and supporting the immune system. It is commonly found in the essential oil and flower extracts of the plant.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in German Chamomile (Chamomilla recutita). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and calming effects. Glycosides are often responsible for the therapeutic properties of chamomile in herbal medicine. They are commonly found in the flower heads and are extracted for use in remedies.

7. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in German Chamomile (Chamomilla recutita). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health by reducing inflammation and protein denaturation. Tannins are also believed to enhance the absorption of certain nutrients and support the healing of mucous membranes.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in German Chamomile (Chamomilla recutita). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponins may enhance the overall therapeutic effects of chamomile in herbal medicine. They are part of the complex mix of active constituents that support its traditional uses.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in German Chamomile (Chamomilla recutita). While alkaloids are more commonly associated with plants like opium poppy or belladonna, German Chamomile contains minimal alkaloids, making it more renowned for its essential oils and flavonoids as active components.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in German Chamomile (Chamomilla recutita). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting anti-inflammatory and soothing effects. This compound helps in reducing oxidative stress and may enhance the plant's therapeutic benefits in herbal medicine.
