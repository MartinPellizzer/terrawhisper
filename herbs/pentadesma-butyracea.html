Pentadesma butyracea

What's the taxonomical classification of Pentadesma butyracea?

Pentadesma butyracea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Malpighiales. This plant is further categorized into the family Clusiaceae, specifically within the genus Pentadesma, and is identified by its unique species name, butyracea.

These fruits are often yellowish or greenish when mature and contain a single seed. The overall architecture of the plant is compact, often forming dense thickets in its natural habitat.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains butyraxanthones A-D, flavanone-flavone dimers, lupeol, and various phenolic compounds. These diverse medicinal compounds contribute to the plant's complex chemical profile and potential therapeutic properties. The presence of specific butyraxanthones suggests unique biological activities that researchers are actively investigating. Additionally, the flavanone-flavone dimers and phenolic compounds provide a foundation for antioxidant and anti-inflammatory studies. Lupeol serves as another significant triterpene component within the plant's chemical structure. Together, these constituents define the medicinal potential of the species.

As discussed in a study, four new xanthones named butyraxanthones A-D (1-4) were isolated from the stem bark of Pentadesma butyracea. These compounds were found alongside six known xanthones (5-10) and 1 triterpenoid known as lupeol. The 10 xanthones (1-10) were tested in vitro for antiplasmodial activity against 1 Plasmodium falciparum chloroquine-resistant strain. Additionally, these substances were tested for cytotoxicity against 1 human breast cancer cell line, MCF-7. Nearly all of these xanthones exhibited good antiplasmodial activity, while some also demonstrated potent cytotoxicity.
