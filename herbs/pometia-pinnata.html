Pometia pinnata

What's the taxonomical classification of Pometia pinnata?

Pometia pinnata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further descends through the order Sapindales and falls under the family Sapindaceae. Finally, its taxonomic identity is defined by its placement within the genus Pometia, resulting in its specific scientific name, Pometia pinnata.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Pometia pinnata contains bioactive secondary metabolites including triterpenoids, flavonoids, polyphenols, and various alkaloids that contribute to its therapeutic properties. These chemical constituents are primarily concentrated in the leaves and bark of the tree. Research indicates that these compounds exhibit significant antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial activities. Some studies have specifically explored their potential in managing oxidative stress and protecting cellular structures. The presence of these specific phytochemicals supports the traditional use of the plant in treating various ailments.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including skin diseases, digestive disorders, fever, and respiratory issues. The bark and leaves are frequently employed in traditional medicine to address inflammatory conditions and promote wound healing. It is also utilized to alleviate symptoms of dysentery and other stomach-related complications. Practitioners often use decoctions from the plant to reduce high fevers and manage congestion. These medicinal properties make it a significant component in various regional herbal remedies.
