Liparis stricklandiana

What's the taxonomical classification of Liparis stricklandiana?

Liparis stricklaandiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is situated in the order Asparagales. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family, falling under the genus Liparis, and is specifically identified by its species name, stricklandiana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Liparis stricklaandiana has small, fleshy, creeping rhizomes that produce distichous, lanceolate leaves which are typically bright green and relatively thin.

The inflorescence is a slender, terminal raceme that bears multiple small, non-resupinate flowers. Each flower features a characteristic three-lobed lip and a column that is fused to the base of the lip. The petals and sepals are often translucent or pale in color, providing a delicate appearance to the blooming structure.

These morphological traits distinguish the species within its genus and adapt it to its specific forest habitat.
