Aristolochia fangchi

What's the taxonomical classification of Aristolochia fangchi?

Aristolochia fangchi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further classified into the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Piperales. This plant is a member of the family Aristolochiaceae, specifically situated within the genus Aristolochia, with the specific designation of the species fangchi.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aristolochia fangchi has perennial climbing stems with opposite, cordate to ovate leaves that feature entire margins and acute apices. The foliage typically presents a smooth texture and a dark green color on the upper surface. Its inflorescence consists of tubular, brownish or yellowish flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These specialized flowers possess a complex structure designed to trap small insects for pollination. The plant often grows as a vine, using its twining stems to ascend through surrounding vegetation.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Aristolochia fangchi contains medicinal compounds primarily located in its dried roots and rhizomes. These underground structures were traditionally harvested to prepare decoctions for treating various ailments such as inflammation, coughs, and rheumatism. The plant's active constituents were historically valued in traditional Chinese medicine to promote blood circulation and alleviate pain. However, modern scientific analysis has revealed that these specific parts also harbor potent nephrotoxic and carcinogenic aristolochic acids. Consequently, the use of these medicinal parts has been strictly regulated or banned in many regions due to the risk of kidney damage and cancer.

What are the pharmacological activities of Aristolochia fangchi?

This plant has potent carcinogenic and nephrotoxic activities that cause severe damage to the renal system. The presence of aristolochic acids within the plant leads to direct nephrotoxicity by inducing tubular cell death. These compounds are also known to be highly carcinogenic, often causing DNA adducts that lead to urothelial cancers. Prolonged exposure to the plant can result in progressive renal failure through chronic nephrotoxicity. Consequently, the pharmacological profile of Aristolochia fangchi is primarily defined by these dangerous toxicological effects.

As discussed in a study, Aristolochia fangchi contains the nephrotoxic and carcinogenic aristolochic acid (AA). The plant was evaluated through 10 samples of Fangji purchased from local medicinal shops. Nine of these samples were found to be similar to Aristolochia fangchi. While aristolochic acid I (AAI) was confirmed in the nine samples using LC-MS/MS, neither tetrandrine nor fangchinoline were detected in these samples.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains aristolochic acids, aporphine alkaloid, bisbenzylisoquinoline alkaloids, morphine alkaloids, and tetrandrine. These diverse chemical constituents contribute to the complex pharmacological profile of the species. The presence of morphine alkaloids often relates to potent analgesic properties, while the bisbenzylisoquinoline alkaloids are known for their specific biological activities. Additionally, the aporphine alkaloid and tetrandrine serve as significant markers for identifying the plant's chemical makeup. However, the inclusion of aristolochic acids is a critical factor due to their known toxicity.

As discussed in a study, Aristolochia fangchi contains medicinal compounds that are secondary metabolites. These substances include (bis)-benzylisoquinoline-alkaloids, dihydroxy-diallyl-biphenyls, and aristolochic acids. These specific components possess strong pharmacological and possibly toxic actions. What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for the treatment of various ailments, but its primary component causes severe medical complications like aristolochic acid nephropathy. This toxic condition leads to significant kidney damage and often progresses to end-stage renal disease. Patients suffering from these kidney issues are also at a heightened risk of developing urothelial carcinoma. The presence of aristolochic acid in the body is directly linked to these life-threatening renal and urinary tract cancers. Consequently, the plant is now widely recognized as a dangerous nephrotoxin rather than a safe medicinal remedy.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause severe nephrotoxicity, renal failure, uropathy, renal papillary necrosis, carcinogenicty, and urothelial carcinoma. The toxic aristolochic acids found within its tissues directly damage the kidneys and urinary tract. This damage often manifests as renal papillary necrosis, which can lead to permanent kidney dysfunction. Furthermore, the plant is known for its significant carcinogenicity. Long-term exposure is specifically linked to the development of urothelial carcinoma in the bladder and ureters.
