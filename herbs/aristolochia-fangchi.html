Aristolochia fangchi

What's the taxonomical classification of Aristolochia fangchi?

Aristolochia fangchi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further classified into the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Piperales. This plant is a member of the family Aristolochiaceae, specifically situated within the genus Aristolochia, with the specific designation of the species fangchi.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Aristolochia fangchi contains aristolochic acids, which are potent nephrotoxic and carcinogenic alkaloids. These compounds are primarily responsible for the plant's historical use in traditional medicine for treating inflammation and pain. However, these specific chemical constituents are highly dangerous because they cause severe kidney damage and increase the risk of upper urinary tract cancers. Because of the high toxicity of these alkaloids, many regulatory agencies have banned the plant's use in herbal supplements. Modern pharmacological research focuses on identifying these specific molecules to prevent accidental exposure in medicinal products.

What are the therapeutic actions of Aristolochia fangchi?

Aristolochia fangchi has traditionally been used in herbal medicine to reduce inflammation, treat respiratory ailments, and alleviate pain through its purported diuretic and analgesic properties. It was historically utilized in various traditional formulations to address conditions such as edema and urinary tract issues. The plant contains various alkaloids that were believed to exert physiological effects on the body's systems. However, modern clinical research has identified significant dangers regarding its use due to the presence of aristolochic acid. This specific compound is highly nephrotoxic and carcinogenic, leading to severe kidney damage and increased cancer risks. Consequently, its therapeutic application is strictly cautioned against or prohibited in many medical contexts.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including inflammation, fever, respiratory issues, and certain types of swelling. Historically, it has been utilized in traditional Chinese medicine to address conditions such as coughs and rheumatism. Some practitioners also applied the plant to manage skin infections or localized pain. However, the use of this species is strictly regulated because it contains aristolochic acid, a potent nephrotoxin. This chemical can cause severe kidney damage and increases the risk of developing urothelial cancers.
