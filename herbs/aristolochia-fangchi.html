What to know about Aristolochia fangchi (fang chi) before using it medicinally

Fang chi, scientifically known as Aristolochia fangchi, is an herb that has been revered for its remarkable health properties, which have been shown to improve respiratory function and alleviate symptoms of bronchitis, asthma, and other respiratory ailments.

In many Asian cuisines, fang chi is used as a key ingredient in soups, stews, and stir-fries, adding depth and complexity to dishes with its slightly bitter and nutty flavor. As a plant, fang chi is an invasive climber that can grow up to 10 feet tall, making it a challenging but rewarding crop for gardeners. From a botanical standpoint, fangchi's unique properties are attributed to the presence of aristolochic acid, a chemical compound with potent medicinal and anti-inflammatory effects.

In traditional Chinese medicine, fang chi has been documented as far back as the Han dynasty (206 BCE - 220 CE), where it was used to treat a range of health conditions, including respiratory issues and digestive problems.

What parts of Aristolochia fangchi are used for medicinal purposes?

The most commonly used parts of Aristolochia fangchi for medicinal purposes are the dried leaves, stems, and roots, which contain various bioactive compounds such as aristolochic acid I (AAI), aristolochic acid II (AAII), and others.

These compounds are believed to possess anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antitumor activities, among other properties. Traditionally, A. fangchi has been used in Chinese medicine to treat various conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and respiratory issues.

However, its use is controversial due to the risk of aristolochic acid nephropathy (AAN), a serious kidney disease that can occur when these compounds are consumed in high doses or for extended periods. Despite these risks, some herbal products still contain A. fangchi, often marketed as a treatment for various health issues.

However, it is essential to note that the use of this plant should only be done under the guidance of a qualified healthcare professional and with extreme caution due to its toxicity.

What are the side effects of Aristolochia fangchi when used improperly?

When used improperly, Aristolochia fangchi increases the chances of experiencing side effects, such as nephrotoxicity, liver damage, and gastrointestinal disturbances.

This herb is known to contain high levels of aristolochic acid, a toxic compound that can cause irreversible kidney damage when taken in excess or for extended periods. Improper use can lead to acute kidney injury, characterized by symptoms such as flank pain, blood in the urine, and decreased urine output. In severe cases, it may even progress to chronic kidney disease or end-stage renal failure.

Additionally, Aristolochia fangchi has been linked to liver damage, including elevated liver enzymes and jaundice. Furthermore, its use has also been associated with gastrointestinal complaints like nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal pain. Moreover, improper use can also lead to allergic reactions, such as skin rashes, itching, and respiratory distress.

It is essential to note that Aristolochia fangchi should only be used under the supervision of a qualified healthcare professional, and its administration should be carefully monitored due to the risk of severe side effects.

What precautions to take before using Aristolochia fangchi medicinally?

The precautions to take before using Aristolochia fangchi medicinally are numerous and crucial, as this herb can cause severe adverse effects if not used properly.

First and foremost, patients with pre-existing kidney or liver disease should avoid using Aristolochia fangchi, as it has been known to exacerbate these conditions and potentially lead to irreversible damage. Additionally, individuals who are taking medications such as ACE inhibitors, diuretics, or anticoagulants should exercise extreme caution when combining them with Aristolochia fangchi, as this herb can interact with these medications and increase the risk of bleeding or kidney failure. Furthermore, women who are pregnant or breastfeeding should not use Aristolochia fangchi due to its potential teratogenic effects and unknown impacts on fetal development.

Finally, patients should start with a low dose and gradually increase it under close medical supervision, as high doses can cause gastrointestinal upset, allergic reactions, and other adverse effects.
