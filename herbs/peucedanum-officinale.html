What to know about Peucedanum officinale (cow parsley) before using it medicinally

Cow parsley, scientifically known as Peucedanum officinale, is a herb that boasts impressive health properties, possessing anti-inflammatory and antioxidant compounds that can help alleviate digestive issues and boost immunity.

In the kitchen, cow parsley is often used as a flavor enhancer in soups, stews, and salads, its subtle anise-like taste adding depth to a variety of dishes. As a horticultural gem, cow parsley is a low-maintenance perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners of all skill levels. From a botanical perspective, cow parsley belongs to the carrot family (Apiaceae) and features fern-like foliage and delicate white flowers.

With roots dating back to ancient Greek and Roman medicine, cow parsley has been a prized medicinal herb throughout history, its uses and benefits extensively documented in historical texts such as Dioscorides' "De Materia Medica".
