Cow Parsnip

Peucedanum officinale

Cow Parsnip (Peucedanum officinale) is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, known for its medicinal properties and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.

Botanical Identification

Cow Parsnip (Peucedanum officinale) is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Apiaceae family and is also known as Common Cow Parsley or Cowroot. The plant has finely divided leaves, yellowish flowers, and a taproot. It is sometimes confused with other members of the carrot family in herbal literature.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Magnoliopsida
  • Order: Apiales
  • Family: Apiaceae
  • Genus: Peucedanum
  • Species: Peucedanum officinale

Active Compounds

Cow Parsnip contains key active compounds such as coumarin, essential oil, and flavonoids. These compounds work together to provide its medicinal properties, with coumarin contributing to its anticoagulant effects and essential oils offering anti-inflammatory benefits. Flavonoids enhance the herb's antioxidant potential, supporting overall health.

  • Coumarin
  • Essential oil
  • Flavonoid

Cow Parsnip Actions.

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for respiratory and digestive ailments due to its warming and carminative properties.

Infusion: Used for respiratory issues and colds due to its expectorant and antispasmodic effects.

Extract: Used for muscle pain and inflammation due to its analgesic and anti-inflammatory compounds.

Safety Profile

Cow Parsnip (Peucedanum officinale) should be used with caution during pregnancy and lactation due to potential uterine stimulant effects. It is contraindicated in individuals with known allergies to plants in the carrot family. Long-term use may lead to liver toxicity, so it should be avoided in cases of existing liver disease.
