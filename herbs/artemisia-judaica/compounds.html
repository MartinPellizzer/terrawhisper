St. John'S Wort (Artemisia judaica) Compounds

St. John's Wort contains several primary compounds, including terpenoids and essential oils, which contribute to its aromatic properties. Flavonoids and phenolic acids provide antioxidant benefits. Coumarin and tannins have anti-inflammatory effects. Glycosides, saponins, alkaloids, and anthocyanins also play roles in its medicinal properties.

St. John'S Wort (Artemisia judaica) Monograph

1. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in St. John's Wort (Artemisia judaica), contributing to its medicinal properties. These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects, supporting the plant's use in traditional herbal medicine for treating various ailments.

2. Essential oil

The essential oil of St. John's Wort (Artemisia judaica) contains compounds like thujone and camphor, which contribute to its medicinal properties. It is used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects, though its use requires careful consideration due to potential interactions with other medications.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of compounds found in St. John's Wort (Artemisia judaica) that contribute to its medicinal properties. They act as antioxidants and may support mood regulation and inflammation reduction. These compounds are part of the plant's complex bioactive profile, contributing to its traditional use in herbal medicine.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in St. John's Wort (Artemisia judaica), contributing to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. It helps modulate cellular responses and may support the plant's traditional use in treating mild depression and anxiety.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a compound found in St. John's Wort (Artemisia judaica). It contributes to the plant's mild sedative and anti-inflammatory effects. Coumarin may also support skin health and wound healing. Its presence adds to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

6. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in St. John's Wort (Artemisia judaica). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support wound healing and inflammation reduction. Tannins also help preserve the plant's active constituents during preparation.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in St. John's Wort (Artemisia judaica). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating mild depression and anxiety. Glycosides in this herb are known for their mild sedative and antidepressant effects. They are often extracted and used in herbal formulations.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in St. John's Wort (Artemisia judaica). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponins help in reducing skin irritation and promoting wound healing. They are often used in topical preparations for their soothing effects.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a key compound found in St. John's Wort (Artemisia judaica). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly in its effects on mood and nervous system. Alkaloids in this plant are known for their potential antidepressant and anti-inflammatory actions.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in St. John's Wort (Artemisia judaica). It contributes to the plant's red and violet pigmentation and possesses antioxidant properties. These compounds help protect the plant from environmental stress and may support its medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
