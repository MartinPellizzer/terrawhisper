Botanical Identification of St. John's Wort (Artemisia judaica)

To identify St. John's Wort (Artemisia judaica) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

St. John'S Wort (Artemisia judaica) Monograph

Scientific Classification

St. John's Wort, or Artemisia judaica, belongs to the plant kingdom, specifically the division of flowering plants, class of eudicots, order of asterids, family of sunflowers, genus of Artemisia, and species Artemisia judaica. This classification places it within the broader group of flowering plants known for their aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of St. John's Wort is Artemisia judaica, first described by the botanist Johann Friedrich Gmelin in 1794. It is also known by several synonyms, including Artemisia absinthium and Artemisia vulgaris, though these refer to different species. Common names include St. John's Wort, woundwort, and sagewort, while regional and historical names vary across cultures and time periods.

Morphological Characteristics

St. John's Wort (Artemisia judaica) is a shrub with a taproot system. It has rigid, branching stems that are aromatic and covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are silvery-green, narrow, and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish flower clusters, and its fruits are tiny, dry seeds enclosed in a capsule.

Diagnostic Identification Features

St. John's Wort (Artemisia judaica) can be distinguished by its gray-green, lance-shaped leaves with white dots and a strong aromatic scent. It has narrow, gray-green stems and produces small yellow flowers in clusters. It differs from similar species like Artemisia absinthium by its leaf shape and flower structure.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

St. John's Wort (Artemisia judaica) is native to the Mediterranean region, including parts of southern Europe and western Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with mild summers and cool winters. It prefers well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is typically found at elevations between 500 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

St. John's Wort (Artemisia judaica) blooms from June to August, producing small yellow flowers. Seed formation occurs in late summer, typically between August and September. For identification purposes, the best time to harvest is during the flowering period, when the plant is most identifiable.

Phylogenetic Relationships

St. John's Wort, Hypericum perforatum, is often confused with Artemisia judaica, but they belong to different families. Artemisia is part of the Asteraceae family, while Hypericum is in the Hypericaceae family. These differences in family and genus classification help distinguish them botanically.
