St. John'S Wort (Artemisia judaica) Uses

St. John's Wort is used to treat respiratory infections, coughs, and skin infections. It helps with inflammatory skin conditions and skin irritation. It is also effective against fungal infections and insect bites. The herb may reduce inflammation and is sometimes used for head lice.

St. John'S Wort (Artemisia judaica) Monograph

1. Respiratory infections

St. John's Wort (Artemisia judaica) is traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in treating symptoms of colds, coughs, and bronchitis. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties may help reduce mucus production and soothe irritated airways, though it should be used cautiously and under professional guidance.

2. Cough

St. John's Wort (Artemisia judaica) is traditionally used to alleviate cough symptoms, particularly in respiratory conditions. Its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties help reduce mucus and soothe irritated airways. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture for internal use. However, it should be used with caution due to potential interactions with medications.

3. Skin infections

St. John's Wort has been traditionally used to treat skin infections due to its antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties. It can help reduce redness, swelling, and infection in conditions like eczema and minor wounds. Topical applications of its infused oils or salves are commonly recommended in herbal medicine for these purposes.

4. Inflammatory skin conditions

St. John's Wort (Artemisia judaica) is used to treat inflammatory skin conditions due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and infection in conditions like eczema and psoriasis. Topical applications are common, often combined with other herbs for enhanced effectiveness. Its soothing effects make it a valuable remedy in herbal skincare.

5. Head lice

St. John's Wort (Artemisia judaica) has been traditionally used to treat head lice due to its antiparasitic properties. The plant contains compounds that disrupt the life cycle of lice, making it an effective natural remedy. It can be applied as a topical infusion or oil to suffocate and remove lice from the scalp. This method is often preferred for its minimal side effects compared to chemical treatments.

6. Skin inflammation

St. John's Wort (Artemisia judaica) is used topically to reduce skin inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. It helps soothe irritated skin, reduce redness, and promote healing. Applications include eczema, psoriasis, and minor burns. Its active compounds have demonstrated efficacy in clinical settings for these conditions.

7. Insect bites

St. John's Wort (Artemisia judaica) can be applied topically to insect bites to reduce inflammation and soothe irritation. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties help alleviate redness, swelling, and discomfort. A poultice or infused oil made from the plant can be directly applied to the affected area for relief.

8. Fungal infections

St. John's Wort (Artemisia judaica) has been traditionally used to treat fungal infections due to its antimicrobial and antifungal properties. It contains compounds that inhibit fungal growth, particularly effective against dermatophytes. It is often applied topically as a poultice or infused oil to address conditions like athlete's foot and ringworm.

9. Skin irritation

St. John's Wort is used to alleviate skin irritation due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. It can soothe rashes, eczema, and minor burns when applied topically. A poultice or infused oil made from the plant is commonly used for this purpose. It helps reduce redness and promotes healing of irritated skin.

10. Inflammation

St. John's Wort (Artemisia judaica) is used to reduce inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory compounds, such as flavonoids and terpenes. It supports healing in conditions like skin irritations and respiratory inflammation. Its mild nature makes it suitable for long-term use under professional guidance.
