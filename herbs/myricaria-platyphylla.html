What's the taxonomical classification of Myricaria platyphylla?

Myricaria platyphylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Caryophyllales. Within this order, it is a member of the family Tamaricaceae, falling under the genus Myricaria, and is specifically identified by its species name, platyphylla.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Myrica platyphylla has an erect, perennial shrubby habit characterized by slender, grayish-brown woody stems and simple, alternate leaves that are lanceolate to narrowly elliptic in shape. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous, globose drupes that appear in clusters along the branches. These fruits are often surrounded by a characteristic waxy or resinous coating that distinguishes the species.
