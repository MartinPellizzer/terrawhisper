Rotala rosea

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rotala rosea has small, elliptical to ovate leaves that typically measure between five and ten millimeters in length and are arranged in opposite or whorled patterns along its slender, branching stems. The foliage exhibits a distinct pink to reddish pigmentation that intensifies under high light intensity. These leaves possess thin, delicate margins and a smooth texture that allows for efficient nutrient absorption in aquatic environments. As the plant matures, the stems can become quite elongated, creating a bushy or spreading growth habit. The overall structure is highly adaptable, often forming dense clusters in well-established aquatic ecosystems.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, specifically concentrated within the freshwater habitats of Thailand and Vietnam. It typically flourishes in slow-moving or stagnant waters where sunlight can penetrate the surface easily. The species is most frequently found in shallow marshes and the margins of lakes throughout these specific subtropical climates. Due to its popularity in the aquarium hobby, it has been introduced to various controlled aquatic environments globally.
