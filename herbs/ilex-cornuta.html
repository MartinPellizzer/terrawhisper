What's the taxonomical classification of Ilex cornuta?

Ilex cornuta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the order Aquifoliales and the family Aquifoliaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its genus, Ilex, and its specific species designation, cornuta.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ilex cornuta has small, evergreen leaves that are dark green, leathery, and characterized by sharp, spine-tipped teeth along their margins. These elliptical leaves typically grow in an opposite arrangement on woody stems that form a dense, spreading shrub or small tree. The plant produces small, white, four-petaled flowers that appear in clusters during the late spring or early summer. Following pollination, it develops small, round drupes that transition from green to a bright, glossy black upon maturity. This species often exhibits a compact growth habit, making it useful for structural hedging or ornamental landscaping.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

The species is often found growing in shaded areas under the canopy of larger trees or along rocky slopes. Its distribution is limited by its preference for warm, temperate to subtropical climates that avoid extreme freezing. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains indole-3-carbaldehyde, indole-3-carboxylic acid (3-ICA), jasmonic acid (JA), pentacyclic triterpenoids, and phenylpropanoids which function as diverse medicinal compounds. These specific chemical constituents contribute to the plant's complex pharmacological profile and biological activity. The indole derivatives like 3-ICA are often studied for their roles in metabolic regulation and cellular signaling. Additionally, the presence of jasmonic acid facilitates various physiological defense mechanisms within the organism. The pentacyclic triterpenoids and phenylpropanoids provide further therapeutic potential due to their known anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

As discussed in a study, the medicinal plant Ilex cornuta was the source from which the endophytic fungus Lasiodiplodia pseudotheobromae LPS-1 was previously isolated. The EtOAc extract of the culture filtrate of this fungus contained three specific compounds: indole-3-carbaldehyde, indole-3-carboxylic acid (3-ICA), and jasmonic acid (JA). Research into these compounds showed a significant synergistic effect between 3-ICA and JA at various ratios ranging from 1:9 to 9:1. The highest synergistic coefficient of 22.95 was achieved when the 3-ICA to JA ratio was 2:8. Furthermore, treatments using JA at 500 μg/ml or a 3-ICA:JA ratio of 2:8 at 40 μg/ml significantly inhibited certain fungal developments.
