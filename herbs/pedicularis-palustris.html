Pedicularis palustris

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pedicularis palustris has an erect, unbranched stem that typically reaches heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and is covered in fine, spreading hairs. The leaves are primarily basal and arranged in a rosette, characterized by deeply lobed or pinnatifid segments. Higher up the stem, the leaves become smaller and more bract-like in structure. The inflorescence consists of a dense terminal spike of tubular, bilabiate flowers that are predominantly pink or purplish in color. These flowers feature a prominent upper lip that serves as a landing platform for pollinators.

    What are the herbal preparations of Pedicularis palustris?

    Pedicularis palustris is prepared primarily as an infusion, decoction, or tincture derived from the dried aerial parts of the plant. The leaves and flowering stems are harvested and dried to preserve their active chemical constituents for medicinal use. When making an infusion, the dried material is steeped in hot water to extract water-soluble components. For a more concentrated dose, a tincture is created by soaking the plant matter in a mixture of water and ethanol. These various preparations allow for different methods of administration depending on the intended therapeutic application.
