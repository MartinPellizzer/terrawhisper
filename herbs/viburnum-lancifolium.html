Viburnum lancifolium

What's the taxonomical classification of Viburnum lancifolium?

Viburnum lancifolium belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Dipsacales and is a member of the family Viburnaceae. Finally, it is placed within the genus Viburnum, specifically identified as the species lancifolium.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Viburnum lancifolium contains various secondary metabolites including flavonoids, tannins, and volatile oils that provide its medicinal properties. These bioactive compounds contribute to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects when used in traditional herbal applications. The presence of phenolic acids within the leaves and bark helps to mitigate oxidative stress in biological systems. Additionally, some studies suggest that the chemical constituents may possess mild antimicrobial activities. These combined chemical profiles make the species a subject of interest for pharmacological research.

What are the therapeutic actions of Viburnum lancifolium?

Viburnum lancifolium has therapeutic properties that primarily involve its use as an anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antipyretic agent in traditional medicine. The plant contains various bioactive compounds that help reduce swelling and alleviate physical pain in affected tissues. It is frequently utilized in herbal formulations to help lower body temperature during fevers. Some practitioners also employ its extracts to soothe digestive discomfort and promote overall systemic balance. These medicinal actions are often attributed to the specific phytochemical profile found within its leaves and bark.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including fever, cough, and stomach pain. Its medicinal properties are often utilized in traditional practices to alleviate respiratory discomfort. Some practitioners also rely on its extracts to manage gastrointestinal issues and promote better digestion. The plant contains bioactive compounds that contribute to its therapeutic effects in these specific areas. Local healers frequently prepare decoctions from its leaves or bark to address these common health concerns.
