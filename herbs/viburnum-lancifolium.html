Viburnum lancifolium

What's the taxonomical classification of Viburnum lancifolium?

Viburnum lancifolium belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Dipsacales and is a member of the family Viburnaceae. Finally, it is placed within the genus Viburnum, specifically identified as the species lancifolium.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viburnum lancifoliuum has lanceolate to elliptic leaves that measure 5 to 15 centimeters in length, characterized by serrated margins and a distinctively pointed apex. The foliage typically presents a dark green color on the upper surface with a slightly paler underside. Its woody stems are slender and can grow into a large shrub or small tree reaching heights of several meters. During the flowering season, the plant produces flat-topped cymes of small, white, five-petaled flowers. These blossoms eventually give way to small, fleshy drupes that transition from green to red and finally to a dark blue or black color when ripe.
