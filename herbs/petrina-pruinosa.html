Possible Side Effects of Petrina pruinosa

Petrina pruinosa can have side effects if used improperly, such as causing skin irritation, allergic reactions, and respiratory problems in individuals who handle the plant or inhale its pollen.

If ingested, it can lead to symptoms like nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain, as well as interact with medications and worsen existing health conditions.

Prolonged exposure to the plant's pollen can also trigger asthma attacks and exacerbate conditions like bronchitis and emphysema.

In severe cases, improper use of Petrina pruinosa can cause anaphylaxis, a life-threatening allergic reaction that requires immediate medical attention.
