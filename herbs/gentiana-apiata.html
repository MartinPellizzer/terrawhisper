Gentiana apiata

What's the taxonomical classification of Gentiana apiata?

Gentiana apiata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is a member of the order Gentianaales and the family Gentianaceae. Ultimately, this plant is identified by its genus, Gentiana, and its specific species designation, apiata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gentiana apiata has an upright, perennial growth habit characterized by lanceolate, sessile leaves and tubular, pale blue to white flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. The foliage consists of opposite leaves that are relatively narrow and taper toward the tip. Each flower features a distinct corolla with five lobes and often exhibits subtle longitudinal stripes or markings on the interior. The plant typically grows in subalpine or alpine environments where its compact structure helps it withstand harsh conditions. Small, capsule-like fruits develop following pollination to facilitate seed dispersal.
