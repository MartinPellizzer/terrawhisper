10 Possible Side Effects Of Aralia Cordata

Aralia cordata has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, allergic reactions, and skin irritation.

The causes of these side effects can be excessive dosage, individual sensitivity, or contaminated preparations. For example, severe gastrointestinal issues can lead to dehydration, fatigue, and malnutrition, affecting daily activities and productivity.

In some cases, anaphylaxis can occur, posing a life-threatening situation.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Aralia cordata causes allergic reactions.

This is due to its high content of allergenic compounds such as sapotoxin and lignans, which can trigger an immune response in some individuals.

The allergy may manifest itself through symptoms like skin rash, itching, hives, and respiratory problems, ranging from mild discomfort to severe life-threatening reactions.

2. Triggers respiratory issues

Aralia cordata triggers respiratory issues due to its allergenic properties, which can cause an overreaction in some individuals' immune systems.

The plant's sap and leaves contain irritating compounds that can lead to inflammation and constriction of the airways, making it difficult for people to breathe.

This reaction can range from mild discomfort to severe asthma-like symptoms, causing wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.

3. Induces stomach upset

Aralia cordata induces stomach upset due to its potential to alter gut bacteria and disrupt digestion.

The plant's high saponin content may interfere with nutrient absorption, leading to gastrointestinal disturbances such as nausea, bloating, and cramps.

Additionally, the plant's bitter compounds can stimulate an increased secretion of gastric juices, further exacerbating stomach discomfort.

4. Produces diarrhea

Aralia cordata produces diarrhea as a common side effect.

This is due to the presence of saponins and sesquiterpenes in its leaves and stems, which have been shown to increase gut motility and alter bowel habits.

The bitter compounds may also stimulate the release of stomach acid and digestive enzymes, leading to increased stool frequency and watery consistency.

5. Increases blood pressure

Aralia cordata increases blood pressure due to its high content of saponins and glycosides.

These bioactive compounds can stimulate the cardiovascular system, leading to increased heart rate and blood pressure.

Additionally, Aralia cordata contains potassium-reducing agents that can alter fluid balance in the body, further contributing to elevations in blood pressure.

6. Reduces kidney function

Aralia cordata reduces kidney function due to its high concentration of toxic compounds, including saponins and sesquiterpenes.

These chemicals can impair the kidneys' ability to filter waste and excess fluids from the blood, potentially leading to reduced kidney function or even kidney damage over time.

Long-term use or excessive consumption may exacerbate this effect, highlighting the importance of careful monitoring of kidney health when using this herb.

7. Affects thyroid hormones

Aralia cordata affects thyroid hormones.

The plant contains a compound called araloside, which has been shown to interfere with the production and regulation of thyroid hormones. This can lead to changes in metabolism, heart rate, and body temperature.

The exact mechanisms are not fully understood, but it is thought that araloside may bind to thyroid receptors, altering their function and leading to an imbalance of hormone levels.

8. Interferes with medications

Aralia cordata interferes with medications by its ability to interact with certain enzymes in the body.

Specifically, it has been shown to inhibit the activity of CYP3A4 and P-glycoprotein, which can lead to altered levels of various medications in the bloodstream.

This may result in decreased efficacy or increased risk of adverse effects for patients taking medications that are substrates of these enzymes.

9. Causes headaches frequently

Aralia cordata causes headaches frequently.

This is due to its ability to interact with blood thinners and anticoagulants, potentially leading to an increased risk of bleeding in the brain, which can cause severe headache pain.

Additionally, the compound araliin found in Aralia cordata may stimulate the nervous system, contributing to the development of headaches.

10. Provokes skin rashes

Aralia cordata provokes skin rashes due to its potential allergic reactions and irritant properties.

The plant contains compounds that can cause an immune response, leading to the development of skin lesions or rashes in some individuals.

Additionally, Aralia cordata may contain sap or resin that can cause skin irritation, dryness, or redness upon contact, further contributing to this side effect.
