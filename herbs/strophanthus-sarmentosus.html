Strophanthus sarmentosus

What's the taxonomical classification of Strophanthus sarmentosus?

Strophanthus sarmentosus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Gentianales and the family Apocynaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Strophanthus, and its unique species designation, sarmentosus.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Strophanthus sarmentosus contains cardiac glycosides, primarily strophanthin and related glycosidic compounds, which act by inhibiting the sodium-potassium pump in cardiac muscle cells. These potent alkaloids serve as powerful cardiotonic agents that increase the force of myocardial contractions. Due to their high toxicity, these chemical constituents must be used with extreme caution in pharmaceutical applications. Historically, these compounds have been extracted for their ability to treat various heart conditions and regulate irregular rhythms. The specific chemical structure of these glycosides allows them to exert a direct and rapid effect on the heart's electrical conduction system.

What are the therapeutic actions of Strophanthus sarmentosus?

Strophantus sarmentosus has potent cardiotonic, purgative, and emetic properties derived from its glycoside content. These bioactive compounds act primarily by increasing the force of myocardial contractions to treat various cardiac irregularities. When administered in controlled amounts, the plant serves as a powerful laxative to clear the intestinal tract. However, its high toxicity means that excessive use can lead to severe gastrointestinal distress or dangerous heart palpitations. Due to these intense physiological effects, it is traditionally used with extreme caution in medicinal preparations.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including cardiac disorders, parasitic infections, and certain skin conditions. The medicinal properties of the plant are primarily derived from its cardiac glycosides which influence heart muscle contraction. In traditional medicine, extracts may be applied topically to address localized infections or dermatological issues. It is also documented in some herbal practices for managing intestinal parasites through its purgative effects. However, extreme caution is required due to the potential toxicity of its chemical constituents.
