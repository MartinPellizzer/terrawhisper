What are the pharmacological activities of Strophanthus sarmentosus?

This plant has anti-hemolytic, anti-hemorrhagic, and antivenomous pharmacological activities that make it a significant subject of medicinal study. Its anti-hemolytic properties allow it to protect red blood cells from premature destruction. Additionally, the plant exhibits anti-hemorrhagic effects that help in controlling or preventing excessive bleeding. The presence of antivenomous qualities suggests it may play a role in neutralizing certain toxins. These combined biological actions highlight its therapeutic potential in treating various blood-related and toxicological conditions.

According to a study published by "Molecules (Basel, Switzerland)", Strophantus sarmentosus is recognized for ethnomedicinal applications including treatment after snakebites. Methanol and ethylacetate extracts from the leaves and roots of S. sarmentosus demonstrated significant positive effects in anti-hemorrhagic and anti-hemolytic assays, performing equal to or better than antivenoam. These extracts strongly inhibited or even abolished the digestion of the vasoactive neuropeptide bradykinin by snake venom metalloproteinases. The plant contains bioactive substances such as flavonoids, terpenoids, tannins, saponins, and alkaloids that contribute to its protective effects. Although ouabain is only present in low amounts in S. sarmentosus compared to other cardenolides, it is one of the cardiac glycosides that can inhibit venom-induced bradykinin cleavage.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause severe toxic reactions including nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain, dizziness, bradycardia, and cardiac arrhythmias. These symptoms arise because the plant contains potent cardiac glycosides that directly affect the heart's electrical conduction. Ingestion often leads to significant gastrointestinal distress characterized by intense abdominal pain and frequent vomiting. The cardiovascular impact is particularly dangerous, as it can result in a dangerously slow heart rate known as bradycardia. Furthermore, the disruption of normal heart rhythms can lead to life-threatening cardiac arrhythmias. Such systemic toxicity requires immediate medical attention due to the potential for fatal outcomes.
