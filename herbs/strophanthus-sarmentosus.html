What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Strophanthus sarmentozus targets cardiac glycoside-sensitive sodium-potassium ATPase pumps to exert its primary pharmacological effects. These bioactive glycosides bind to the alpha subunit of the Na+/K+-ATPase enzyme, inhibiting its activity and subsequently increasing intracellular sodium levels. This ionic shift leads to a secondary rise in intracellular calcium concentrations through the sodium-calcium exchanger, which strengthens myocardial contractility. Such interactions are the fundamental mechanism behind the plant's potent cardiotonic properties. Beyond the sodium-potassium pump, the chemical constituents may also influence various ion channels and signaling pathways within the cardiovascular system.

This stimulation is often utilized in traditional medicine to manage various forms of congestive heart failure.     What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause severe toxic reactions including nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain, dizziness, bradycardia, and cardiac arrhythmias. These symptoms arise because the plant contains potent cardiac glycosides that directly affect the heart's electrical conduction. Ingestion often leads to significant gastrointestinal distress characterized by intense abdominal pain and frequent vomiting. The cardiovascular impact is particularly dangerous, as it can result in a dangerously slow heart rate known as bradycardia. Furthermore, the disruption of normal heart rhythms can lead to life-threatening cardiac arrhythmias. Such systemic toxicity requires immediate medical attention due to the potential for fatal outcomes.
