Capparis acutifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Capparis acutifolia?

Capparis acuifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is part of the order Brassicales and falls within the family Capparaceae. Finally, it is placed in the genus Capparis, with its specific identification being the species acutifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Capparis acuifolia has lanceolate to narrowly elliptical leaves that are typically 2 to 7 centimeters long and possess acute tips with entire margins. The foliage is characterized by a leathery texture and a glossy green surface that often displays a slightly paler underside. Its stems are woody and sprawling, frequently developing small prickles or thorns that assist in climbing. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that emerge from the axils of the leaves. These blossoms are accompanied by fleshy, globose fruits that transition in color as they mature.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of eastern and southern Africa, specifically spanning from Ethiopia and Somalia through Kenya and Tanzania to South Africa. It primarily inhabits dry scrublands, rocky hillsides, and thornbush savannas where drainage is well-regulated. Within these ecosystems, the species often thrives in sandy or loamy soils that are characteristic of subtropical climates. Its distribution is closely tied to regions experiencing distinct wet and dry seasonal cycles. Localized populations are frequently found in coastal hinterlands as well as inland plateau regions.
