Shorea Squamata

Shorea squamata, commonly known as the yellow meranti or white ironwood, is a valuable tree species native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and Malaysia.

It has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its purported health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. The bark and leaves of Shorea squamata contain bioactive compounds such as flavonoids and tannins, which may contribute to its medicinal effects. In traditional practices, it has been employed to treat ailments like fever, skin infections, and digestive issues.

However, further scientific research is needed to fully validate its therapeutic potential and ensure safe usage.
