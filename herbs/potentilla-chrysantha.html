Potentilla chrysantha

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Potentiella chrysantha has a perennial growth habit characterized by low-growing, spreading stems that form dense mats or tufts. The leaves are typically trifoliate or palmately divided with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture on both surfaces. Its bright yellow flowers feature five distinct petals surrounding a central cluster of numerous stamens and pistils. Small, dry achenes develop within the flower center following successful pollination. The root system consists of woody rhizomes that help the plant anchor itself in rocky or alpine environments.
