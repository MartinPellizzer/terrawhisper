Botanical Identification of Java Tea (Orthosiphon stamineus)

To identify Java Tea (Orthosiphon stamineus) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Java Tea (Orthosiphon stamineus) Monograph

Scientific Classification

Java Tea, or Orthosiphon stamineus, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Lamiales, order Lamiales, family Lamiaceae, genus Orthosiphon, and species Orthosiphon stamineus. It is a member of the mint family, known for its medicinal properties and traditional use in herbal medicine.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The botanical name of Java Tea is Orthosiphon stamineus, a binomial classification introduced by the botanist Robert Brown in 1830. It has several synonyms, including Piper stamineus and Piper lanceolatum, reflecting historical taxonomic revisions. Common names include Java Tea, Java Pennywort, and Kaphi, while regional and historical names vary across cultures and time periods.

Morphological Characteristics

Java Tea (Orthosiphon stamineus) is a perennial herb with a bushy growth habit. It has a fibrous root system, erect stems that are quadrangular and hairy. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, with serrated margins. The inflorescence is a spike, bearing small white flowers with four lobes. The fruit is a capsule containing numerous small seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Orthosiphon stamineus can be distinguished by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and its distinctive blue to purple flowers arranged in spikes. It also has a strong, aromatic scent and produces small, elliptical fruits. These features help differentiate it from similar species in the same family.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Java Tea, or Orthosiphon stamineus, is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and temperatures. The plant prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils and is commonly found at low to mid-altitude regions, typically between 0 and 1,000 meters above sea level.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Java Tea (Orthosiphon stamineus) typically blooms from May to August, producing small white to pink flowers. Seed formation occurs from September to November. Harvesting is usually done during the dry season, from March to June, when the leaves are most potent and the plant's active compounds are concentrated.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Orthosiphon stamineus belongs to the genus Orthosiphon, which is closely related to species like Orthosiphon aristatus. It shares family traits with the Lamiaceae family, such as square stems and aromatic leaves. Its phylogenetic position highlights its evolutionary ties to other members of the Lamiaceae family.
