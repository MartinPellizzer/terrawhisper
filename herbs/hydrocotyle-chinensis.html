Hydrocotyle chinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Hydrocotyle chinensis?

Hydrocotyle chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Apiales, placing it within the family Araliaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Hydrocotyle, and its specific species name, chinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hydrocotyle chinensis has creeping, slender, reddish-brown rhizomes that produce small, circular, or kidney-shaped leaves with crenate margins and long petioles. The leaves are typically one to two centimeters in diameter and exhibit a bright green color. These plants feature small, inconspicuous flowers that are borne in umbels on stalks emerging from the leaf axils. The reproductive structures are clustered together and lack showy petals, often appearing as tiny white or yellowish blooms. This creeping growth habit allows the species to form dense mats over moist soil or rock surfaces.
