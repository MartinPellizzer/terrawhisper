What is Ballota africana?

Ballota africana is a perennial herbaceous plant that belongs to the family Lamiaceae.

It has a taproot and woody base, with a height of 0.3-1.5 meters, and a branched system of stems and leaves. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and elliptical in shape, measuring 3-10 centimeters long, with a crenate margin and a length of 1-3 centimeters wide. The flowers are purple, gathered in whorls of four, and have a tubular calyx with a 2-lipped corolla.

The plant's flowers are hermaphroditic and are pollinated by insects, producing small brown seeds in a capsule.
