Helicteres viscida

What's the taxonomical classification of Helicteres viscida?

Helicteres viscida belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further falls into the order Malvales and the family Malvaceae. Finally, its taxonomic position is completed by its placement within the genus Helicteres, resulting in its specific species designation, Helicteres viscida.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Helicteres viscida contains bioactive phytochemicals including alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, tannins, and terpenoids that contribute to its therapeutic properties. These chemical constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's documented antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory activities. Researchers have identified specific phenolic compounds within the plant that act as potent antioxidants to combat oxidative stress. Some studies also suggest that its extracts possess significant wound-healing capabilities due to the presence of these complex organic molecules. The synergy between these various compounds supports the traditional use of the plant in treating various ailments.

What are the therapeutic actions of Helicteres viscida?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including skin infections, digestive disorders, respiratory issues, and inflammatory conditions. Its medicinal properties are often applied to manage wounds and soothe skin irritations through topical use. In traditional medicine, extracts from the plant are frequently employed to alleviate stomach pains and assist with gastrointestinal discomfort. It is also utilized in the treatment of coughs and other ailments affecting the respiratory tract. These therapeutic applications rely on the bioactive compounds found within the plant's tissues.
