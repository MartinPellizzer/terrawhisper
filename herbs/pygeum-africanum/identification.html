Botanical Identification of Pygeum (Pygeum africanum)

To identify Pygeum (Pygeum africanum) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Pygeum africanum belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Rubiales, family Rubiaceae, genus Pygeum, and species Pygeum africanum. This classification places it within the flowering plants, specifically in the Rubiaceae family, known for its diverse and economically important species.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of the plant is Pygeum africanum, a species native to Africa. It was first described by the botanist Carl Peter Thunberg in 1794. This name is widely accepted in modern botanical literature, though it has several historical and regional synonyms that reflect its use and discovery over time.

Morphological Characteristics

Pygeum africanum is a small to medium-sized tree with a rounded crown and a straight trunk. Its root system is shallow and fibrous, adapting to sandy soils. The stem is smooth and greyish-brown, with a distinctive flaky bark. Leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical, with a glossy upper surface and a pale underside. The inflorescence is a panicle, bearing small yellow-green flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Pygeum africanum can be distinguished by its distinctive grayish-brown bark with vertical fissures, opposite leaf arrangement, and small, clustered yellow flowers. It differs from similar species like Terminalia by its leaf shape and bark texture, and from Sclerocarya by its smaller, more delicate flowers and different fruit structure.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Pygeum africanum is native to sub-Saharan Africa, particularly South Africa, Zimbabwe, and Mozambique. It thrives in warm, tropical to subtropical climates with high humidity. It prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils. It is commonly found at altitudes between 300 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Pygeum africanum blooms in spring, typically between March and May, depending on the region. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early autumn, around August to October. For identification purposes, the best time to harvest is during the flowering period, when the leaves are fully developed and the bark is distinct.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Pygeum africanum is closely related to species within the genus Sclerocarya. It shares characteristics with other members of the Anacardiaceae family, such as the presence of stellate hairs and drupes. Its phylogenetic position highlights its evolutionary ties to other African and Asian species in the same family.
