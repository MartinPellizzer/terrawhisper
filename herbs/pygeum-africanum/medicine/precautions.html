10 Precautions To Take When Using Pygeum Africanum (African Plum)

Pygeum africanum, also known as African plum, has some precautions to consider before using it medicinally, such as monitoring blood sugar levels.

High doses of pygeum can lower blood sugar, which is concerning for diabetics. If not taken care of, it can lead to hypoglycemia, dizziness, and shakiness. It's also recommended to avoid taking pygeum with other blood-thinning medications, as it may increase the risk of bleeding.

This herb should not be used during pregnancy or breastfeeding without consulting a healthcare professional.

This article explains in details the 10 most important precautions to take when using Pygeum africanum medicinally.
