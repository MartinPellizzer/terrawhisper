Pygeum (Pygeum africanum) Compounds

The primary compounds of Pygeum africanum include flavonoids, which act as antioxidants, and tannins, which have astringent properties. Phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory effects, while coumarin provides anticoagulant benefits. Saponins and glycosides support cardiovascular health, and terpenoids, alkaloids, essential oils, and anthocyanins further enhance its medicinal profile.

Pygeum (Pygeum africanum) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Pygeum africanum are bioactive compounds that support vascular health. They help improve blood flow and reduce inflammation, contributing to the plant's traditional use in treating prostate conditions and circulatory disorders. These compounds are key to the therapeutic effects of Pygeum in herbal medicine.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Pygeum africanum, known for its astringent properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in supporting urinary and prostate health. Tannins help reduce inflammation and may improve fluid dynamics in the urinary tract. This compound is central to the therapeutic profile of Pygeum.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Pygeum africanum, commonly known as African wild apricot. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in supporting urinary health by exhibiting anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound helps reduce symptoms of benign prostatic hyperplasia by improving fluid dynamics in the prostate.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Pygeum africanum, a medicinal plant used in herbal medicine. It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and vasodilatory effects, supporting urinary health and circulation. Coumarin's presence enhances the therapeutic value of Pygeum in traditional and modern herbal formulations.

5. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Pygeum africanum, known for its ability to support urinary tract health. It works by reducing inflammation and improving the function of the prostate gland. This compound is particularly valued in herbal medicine for its natural, gentle effects on the urinary system.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Pygeum africanum. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in supporting urinary health. Glycosides in Pygeum help reduce inflammation and improve bladder function. These compounds are responsible for many of the plant's therapeutic effects.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Pygeum africanum, known for their anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in supporting prostate health and urinary function. They work by modulating hormone levels and reducing cellular inflammation.

8. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a bioactive compound found in Pygeum africanum. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in supporting urinary health. Alkaloids in Pygeum are known for their anti-inflammatory and vasodilatory effects. These properties make them relevant in herbal treatments for prostate and kidney-related conditions.

9. Essential oil

The essential oil of Pygeum africanum is a key active compound known for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. It supports prostate health and is commonly used in herbal formulations for men's wellness. The oil is extracted through steam distillation from the bark of the African wild apricot tree.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Pygeum africanum, known for its antioxidant properties. It supports vascular health by improving circulation and reducing inflammation, making it valuable in herbal medicine for conditions like prostate issues and cardiovascular support.
