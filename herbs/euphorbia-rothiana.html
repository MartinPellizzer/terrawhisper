Euphorbia rothiana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Euphorbia rothiana has a succulent, woody stem that typically reaches heights of up to 1.5 meters and features a cylindrical, branched structure with a pale green to brownish epidermis. The plant is characterized by its lack of true leaves, instead utilizing its photosynthetic stems to sustain growth. Small, scale-like leaves may appear briefly during the rainy season before quickly being shed. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers organized into cyathia, which are the reproductive structures typical of the Euphorbia genus. The stem surface often exhibits subtle longitudinal ridges or markings that distinguish its texture from other succulent species.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the dry, rocky regions of Madagascar, specifically concentrated within its southern and southwestern territories. It thrives in arid environments where succulent vegetation is common among the limestone formations. The species is typically found growing in sandy or gravelly soils that provide necessary drainage for its root system. Its distribution is closely linked to the unique microclimates of the spiny thicket ecosystems found on the island. This localized range makes it an integral component of the regional biodiversity within these specific habitats.
