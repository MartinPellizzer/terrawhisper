Euphorbia rothiana

What's the taxonomical classification of Euphorbia rothiana?

Euphorbia rothiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Malpighiales. The plant is a constituent of the family Euphorbiaceae, which is further refined into the genus Euphorbia, eventually reaching its specific designation as the species rothiana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Euphorbia rothiana has a succulent, woody stem that typically reaches heights of up to 1.5 meters and features a cylindrical, branched structure with a pale green to brownish epidermis. The plant is characterized by its lack of true leaves, instead utilizing its photosynthetic stems to sustain growth. Small, scale-like leaves may appear briefly during the rainy season before quickly being shed. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers organized into cyathia, which are the reproductive structures typical of the Euphorbia genus. The stem surface often exhibits subtle longitudinal ridges or markings that distinguish its texture from other succulent species.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires well-draining sandy soil and bright, indirect sunlight to thrive in a controlled environment.

Because it is a succulent, you must allow the substrate to dry completely between watering sessions to prevent root rot. Maintaining warm temperatures between 65 and 85 degrees Fahrenheit is essential for healthy growth during the active season. During the winter months, reduce watering significantly and keep the plant in a cooler, dry area to encourage dormancy.

Providing adequate air circulation helps protect the stems from fungal issues.
