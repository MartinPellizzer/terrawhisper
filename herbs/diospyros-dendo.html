Diospyros dendo

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Diospyros dendo has a woody structure characterized by an erect, branching habit with elliptical, leathery leaves that possess entire margins and prominent pinnate venation. The foliage is typically dark green on the upper surface and may exhibit a slightly paler underside. Its reproductive morphology includes small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in axillary clusters. These flowers eventually develop into fleshy, globose fruits containing several large seeds. The overall vegetative form is adapted to stable forest environments where its dense canopy provides significant shade.

      What are the herbal preparations of Diospyros dendo?

      Diospyros dendo is prepared as a decoction of the bark, a juice extracted from the fruit, or a poultice made from crushed leaves. The bark is often boiled in water to create a medicinal tea used to treat various ailments. For topical applications, the leaves are mashed into a paste and applied directly to the skin.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Diospyros dendo can cause gastrointestinal distress, allergic skin reactions, and potential toxicity if the seeds or unripe fruits are consumed in large quantities. The plant contains various secondary metabolites that may irritate the lining of the digestive tract. Some individuals might experience localized swelling or itching upon direct contact with the plant's sap or foliage. It is important to avoid ingesting any part of the plant without proper expert guidance to prevent unforeseen medical complications. Careful handling is recommended to mitigate the risk of adverse physical reactions.
