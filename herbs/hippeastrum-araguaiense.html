What is Hippeastrum araguaiense?

Hippeastrum araguaiense is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaryllidaceae, native to Brazil.

It is a perennial bulbous plant, characterized by strap-like leaves and a scape that supports a single, showy inflorescence of funnel-shaped flowers, typically pink or white in color. The flowers have three outer tepals and three inner tepals, with the outer tepals being larger, and are 6-8 cm long. The plant grows up to 70 cm in height and has a broad, flat base with the bulb being partially exposed.

The flowers are highly fragrant and bloom in late summer, typically between March and May.
