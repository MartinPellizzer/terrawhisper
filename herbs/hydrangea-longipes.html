Hydrangea longipes

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hydrangea longipes has woody, deciduous stems that branch into thin, elongated peduncles supporting large, terminal corymbose inflorescences. The leaves are oppositely arranged, featuring elliptical to lanceolate shapes with finely serrated margins and acute tips. Each leaf possesses distinct pinnate venation and a smooth, somewhat leathery texture on its surface. The flowers are typically small and white, clustering densely to form a broad, flat-topped floral structure. These morphological traits collectively distinguish the species from other members of the hydrangea genus.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China, specifically spreading through provinces like Hubei, Hunan, Guizhou, and Sichuan. It thrives in the temperate forest understories of these high-altitude areas. The species is primarily found in moist, shaded habitats where it can access consistent moisture from mountain mists. Localized populations often follow the topography of river valleys and rocky slopes. These specific environmental conditions limit its natural range to these distinct East Asian territories.
