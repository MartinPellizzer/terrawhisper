Hydrangea longipes

What's the taxonomical classification of Hydrangea longipes?

Hydrangea longipes belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Cornales. This plant is a representative of the family Hydrangeaceae and is specifically categorized under the genus Hydrangea, with its unique biological identity defined by the species name longipes.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hydrangea longipes has woody, deciduous stems that branch into thin, elongated peduncles supporting large, terminal corymbose inflorescences. The leaves are oppositely arranged, featuring elliptical to lanceolate shapes with finely serrated margins and acute tips. Each leaf possesses distinct pinnate venation and a smooth, somewhat leathery texture on its surface. The flowers are typically small and white, clustering densely to form a broad, flat-topped floral structure. These morphological traits collectively distinguish the species from other members of the hydrangea genus.
