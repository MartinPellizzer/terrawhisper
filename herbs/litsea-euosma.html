Litsea euosma

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Litsea euosma has simple, alternate, coriaceous leaves that are elliptical to oblong in shape with entire margins and pinnate venation. The leaf surfaces are typically lustrous and dark green on the upper side, while the underside may appear slightly paler. Its woody structure consists of a shrub or small tree that reaches moderate heights in its native habitat. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into fleshy drupes used for reproduction. These botanical characteristics are consistent with the Lauraceae family morphology.
