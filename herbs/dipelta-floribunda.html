Dipelta floribunda

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dipelta floribunda has opposite, decussate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape, measuring 5 to 15 centimeters in length with serrated margins and acute tips. The foliage is typically dark green on the upper surface and slightly paler underneath, often displaying a prominent midrib. Its inflorescence consists of dense, terminal panicles of small, creamy-white flowers that emerge from the woody stems. The plant exhibits a shrubby or small tree-like growth habit, reaching heights of several meters in its natural habitat. These morphological characteristics distinguish it within the Adoxaceae family.
