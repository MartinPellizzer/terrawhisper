Hedyotis pinifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Hedyotis pinifolia?

Hedyotis pinifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Gentianaales. This plant is a member of the family Rubiaceae, and its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement within the genus Hedyotis, culminating in its unique species designation, pinifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hedyotis pinifolia has small, needle-like leaves arranged in dense, whorled clusters along woody, upright stems. These narrow, linear leaves mimic the appearance of pine needles, which gives the species its specific name. The plant typically exhibits a compact, shrubby growth habit with dark green foliage. Its stems are relatively slender and may show slight branching depending on the environmental conditions. Tiny, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves during its blooming period.
