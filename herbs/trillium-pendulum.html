Trillium pendulum has many active constituents, such as trillin, pendulin, and trillidin, which have been found to exhibit anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiviral properties.

These constituents can help alleviate symptoms of various health conditions, including arthritis and other inflammatory diseases, by reducing pain and swelling.

Additionally, the presence of alkaloids like pendulin and trillidin can aid in the treatment of respiratory issues like bronchitis and asthma by relaxing airways and promoting easier breathing.

Furthermore, the antiviral properties of trillin can help boost the immune system, reducing the severity and duration of illnesses like the common cold and flu.

Medicinal Parts of trillium pendulum

Trillium pendulum has many medicinal parts, such as the rhizome, which contains trillin and pendulin, compounds with potential anti-inflammatory properties.

The roots of Trillium pendulum contain alkaloids like pendulin and trilline, which have been shown to have sedative and antispasmodic effects.

The flowers of the plant contain flavonoids and saponins, which may have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.
