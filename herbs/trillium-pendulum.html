Botanical Identification

Pendulous Trillium, scientifically known as Trillium pendulum, belongs to the family Melanthiaceae. Commonly called "Dutchman's breeches" in some regions, it is a perennial herb native to eastern North America. The plant features a rhizomatous root, three elliptical leaves, and a single pendulous white flower with three petals. It is also referred to as "Trillium pendulum" in various herbal texts.

Active Compounds

The Pendulous Trillium contains key active compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Tannin
  • Phenolic acid

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for respiratory and digestive ailments due to its warming and antispasmodic properties.

Tincture: Used for pain relief and as a stimulant due to its concentrated active compounds.

Powder: Used for digestive issues and as a general tonic.

Safety Profile

Pendulous Trillium (Trillium pendulum) is generally considered safe when used in moderate amounts. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and should be avoided by those with known allergies to related plants. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
