Scientific and Botanical Profile

Pendulous Trillium, with botanical name Trillium pendulum, is a member of the Melanthiaceae family and is known by various common names including Woolly Trillium, Tall Trillium, and Flowering Trillium.

It is characterized by its distinctive morphology, featuring three broad, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence, and three white or red flowers that hang downward in a pendulous arrangement, giving it the common names such as Hanging Trillium and Trillium With Pendulous Flowers. Native to Eastern Canada and the northeastern United States, this species thrives in the diverse regions of Southern Ontario, New England, the Mid-Atlantic States, the Great Lakes Region, and the Appalachian Mountains.

Found across Eastern North America, including Southern New England and the Eastern United States and Southern Canada, Pendulous Trillium is also referred to as Eastern Trillium, Red Trillium, and Flowers Of The Woods, showcasing its widespread presence and ecological significance in forested habitats.
