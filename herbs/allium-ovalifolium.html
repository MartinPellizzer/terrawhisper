Allium ovalifolium

What's the taxonomical classification of Allium ovalifolium?

Allium ovalifolium belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Asparagales and belongs to the family Amaryllidaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Allium, and its specific species name, ovalifolium.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Allium ovalifolium has elongated, oval-shaped leaves that emerge from a bulb and terminate in a blunt or slightly pointed tip. These leaves are typically fleshy and arranged in a basal manner to maximize light absorption near the ground. The plant produces an upright flowering stalk, or scape, which carries a dense, spherical umbel of small, star-shaped flowers. The individual florets often display a delicate color palette ranging from white to pale pink or lavender. Beneath the surface, the plant relies on a specialized bulb structure for nutrient storage and survival during dormant periods.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

These specific habitats provide the well-drained soil and cool temperatures necessary for its survival. How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires well-draining soil, full sun exposure, and a temperate climate to thrive during its growing season.

Growers should plant the bulbs in late autumn or early spring to ensure proper root establishment before the heat of summer arrives. Regular watering is necessary when the soil feels dry, but it is vital to avoid waterlogged conditions that cause bulb rot. Once the foliage begins to yellow and die back, the plant enters a dormant period where it should be left undisturbed.

Mulching around the base can help regulate soil temperature and retain moisture during warmer months.
