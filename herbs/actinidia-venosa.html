Actinidia venosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Actinidia venosa?

Actinidia venosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical structure of the order Ericales, it is placed in the family Actinidiaceae. Finally, it is identified within the genus Actinidia, specifically as the species venosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Actinidia venosa has woody, climbing stems with simple, opposite leaves that feature prominent, reticulate venation and ovate to broadly elliptical shapes. The foliage is characterized by its dark green color and a slightly textured surface that highlights the intricate vein patterns. Small, inconspicuous white or cream-colored flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves during the growing season. These blossoms eventually give way to small, fleshy berries that transition from green to a dark shade as they ripen.
