Actinidia venosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Actinidia venosa has woody, climbing stems with simple, opposite leaves that feature prominent, reticulate venation and ovate to broadly elliptical shapes. The foliage is characterized by its dark green color and a slightly textured surface that highlights the intricate vein patterns. Small, inconspicuous white or cream-colored flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves during the growing season. These blossoms eventually give way to small, fleshy berries that transition from green to a dark shade as they ripen. The overall structure of the plant allows it to scramble through dense forest undergrowth using its twining stems.
