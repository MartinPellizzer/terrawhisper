Onobrychis radiata

Onobrychis radiata, commonly known as sainfoin, is a perennial legume plant native to Europe and Asia, known for its yellow flowers and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine to treat various health issues, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

The therapeutic actions of Onobrychis radiata are attributed to its bioactive constituents, which include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids that exhibit anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiviral properties.
