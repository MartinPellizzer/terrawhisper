Medicinal Parts of Callisia multiflora

Callisia multiflora has many medicinal parts, such as its leaves, roots, and flowers, which contain various bioactive compounds.

The roots of the plant are rich in alkaloids, including berberine and palmatine, which have been used to treat various health conditions.

The flowers of Callisia multiflora contain terpenoids and saponins, which have been reported to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Possible Side Effects of Callisia multiflora

Callisia multiflora can have side effects if used improperly, such as causing skin irritation, allergic reactions, and digestive problems in some individuals.

Additionally, using Callisia multiflora without proper guidance can interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes medications, resulting in adverse health consequences.
