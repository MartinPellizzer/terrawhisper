Sibiraea angustata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sibiraea angustata has narrow, lanceolate leaves that are typically 1 to 3 centimeters wide and arranged alternately along its woody, deciduous stems. Its inflorescences consist of small, creamy white flowers clustered in terminal panicles. These blooms are supported by slender pedicels and emerge from the upper leaf axils during the growing season. The overall architecture of the plant is shrubby, with a spreading habit that reflects its adaptation to montane environments.
