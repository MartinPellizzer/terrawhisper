Lobelia pleotricha

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lobelia pleotricha has a perennial life cycle characterized by a low-growing, tufted habit with linear to lanceolate leaves that are often clustered at the base. The foliage typically displays a basal rosette arrangement with leaves that possess entire or slightly serrated margins. As the plant matures, it produces upright flowering stems that bear small, zygomorphic flowers. These blossoms are typically blue or violet in color and feature the characteristic two-lipped structure common to the Campanulaceae family. The inflorescence is often somewhat compact, emerging from the upper axils of the stem.
