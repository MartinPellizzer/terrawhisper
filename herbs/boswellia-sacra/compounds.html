Frankincense (Boswellia sacra) Compounds

Frankincense contains several primary compounds, including terpenoids, which are responsible for its aromatic properties. Phenolic acids and flavonoids contribute to its anti-inflammatory effects. Essential oils and coumarin provide antimicrobial benefits, while tannins and saponins support digestive health. Glycosides, alkaloids, and anthocyanins add to its diverse therapeutic potential.

Frankincense (Boswellia sacra) Monograph

1. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a key class of active compounds in Frankincense (Boswellia sacra). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. These compounds are extracted from the resinous sap of the tree and are responsible for many of its therapeutic effects in traditional herbal medicine.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in Frankincense (Boswellia sacra). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. This compound is responsible for many of the therapeutic effects associated with Frankincense in traditional and modern herbal medicine.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Boswellia sacra, known for their anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. They contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments. These compounds help modulate inflammatory responses, supporting the plant's therapeutic value in herbal medicine.

4. Essential oil

The essential oil of Frankincense (Boswellia sacra) is a volatile compound extracted from its resin. It contains bioactive monoterpenes and sesquiterpenes, which contribute to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. This oil is commonly used in herbal medicine for respiratory and skin conditions.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a compound found in Frankincense (Boswellia sacra). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and anticoagulant properties. Coumarin helps modulate enzyme activity and may support skin health. It is one of several bioactive components responsible for Frankincense's therapeutic uses.

6. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Frankincense (Boswellia sacra). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. Tannins help reduce swelling and support wound healing. They are concentrated in the resinous sap of the Boswellia sacra tree.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Frankincense (Boswellia sacra). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in reducing inflammation and supporting joint health. Saponin's presence enhances the therapeutic profile of Frankincense in herbal medicine.

8. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Frankincense (Boswellia sacra). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties by inhibiting enzymes involved in inflammatory responses. Glycosides in Boswellia sacra are often responsible for its traditional use in treating arthritis and other inflammatory conditions.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Boswellia sacra, commonly known as Frankincense. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating inflammation and pain. Alkaloids in Frankincense may help reduce inflammatory responses in the body. These compounds are part of the resinous sap that is harvested from the tree.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Frankincense (Boswellia sacra). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. While not the primary active component, anthocyanin supports the overall therapeutic effects of Frankincense in traditional herbal medicine.
