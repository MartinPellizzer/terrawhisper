Botanical Identification of Frankincense (Boswellia sacra)

To identify Frankincense (Boswellia sacra) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Frankincense (Boswellia sacra) Monograph

Scientific Classification

Frankincense, or Boswellia sacra, is classified within the plant kingdom, specifically in the division of flowering plants. It belongs to the eudicot class and the Burserales order, with its family in the Burseraceae. The genus is Boswellia, and the species is Boswellia sacra, which is the specific type used for frankincense resin.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Boswellia sacra is the scientific name for Frankincense. It was first described by the botanist Carl von Linné in 1758. The species is also known by several synonyms, including Boswellia carteri and Boswellia thurberi. Common names include frankincense, olibanum, and kohl, while regional and historical names vary across cultures and time periods.

Morphological Characteristics

Frankincense, or Boswellia sacra, is a small, thorny shrub or tree native to the Arabian Peninsula and the Horn of Africa. It has a deep taproot system, a gnarled trunk with a rough, fissured bark, and small, leathery leaves. Its inflorescence consists of clusters of yellow-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by capsule fruits containing tiny, brown seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Frankincense, or Boswellia sacra, can be distinguished from similar species by its pale yellow or reddish-brown resin, unique leaf shape with serrated edges, and distinctive, aromatic wood. It also has a unique branching pattern and produces a fragrant gum when cut. These features help differentiate it from other Boswellia species and related plants.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Frankincense, or Boswellia sacra, is native to the Arabian Peninsula and northeastern Africa. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates with well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils. It typically grows at altitudes between 300 and 1,500 meters above sea level.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Frankincense trees bloom between April and June, producing small yellow flowers. Seed formation occurs from July to September. Harvesting of the resin typically happens between November and January, when the tree's sap is most abundant, making it crucial for accurate identification and collection.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Frankincense, or Boswellia sacra, belongs to the genus Boswellia in the family Burseraceae. Its closest relatives include other Boswellia species, such as B. frereana and B. candelabra. The genus shares common traits like aromatic resin production and similar morphological features, which help in its identification and classification within the family.
