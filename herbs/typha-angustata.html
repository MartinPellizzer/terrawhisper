What's the taxonomical classification of Typha angustata?

Typha angustata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Poales, the plant is further categorized into the family Typhaceae. Its specific taxonomic hierarchy concludes with the genus Typha and the species angustata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Typha angustata has long, narrow, grass-like leaves that can reach up to 1.5 meters in length and a cylindrical flowering spike that typically measures 10 to 20 centimeters in height. The foliage is characterized by a distinctively slender and tapering shape, which distinguishes it from many other cattail species. Its reproductive structure consists of a dense, brown, velvety spike of female flowers located above a thinner section of male flowers. The plant often grows in clusters, emerging from creeping rhizomes that allow it to spread through aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.
