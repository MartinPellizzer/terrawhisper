Typha angustata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Poales, the plant is further categorized into the family Typhaceae. Its specific taxonomic hierarchy concludes with the genus Typha and the species angustata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Typha angustata has long, narrow, grass-like leaves that can reach up to 1.5 meters in length and a cylindrical flowering spike that typically measures 10 to 20 centimeters in height. The foliage is characterized by a distinctively slender and tapering shape, which distinguishes it from many other cattail species. Its reproductive structure consists of a dense, brown, velvety spike of female flowers located above a thinner section of male flowers. The plant often grows in clusters, emerging from creeping rhizomes that allow it to spread through aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats. These specialized leaves are designed to maximize surface area for photosynthesis while maintaining structural integrity in wetland environments.

What are the pharmacological activities of Typha angustata?

This plant has anthelmintic pharmacological activity which allows it to effectively expel parasitic worms from a host organism. This specific medicinal property is utilized to combat various intestinal helminths that cause disease. Researchers study these bioactive compounds to understand how they disrupt the life cycles of parasites. Such natural substances serve as important subjects for developing new treatments in veterinary and human medicine. The presence of these chemical constituents makes the species a valuable resource in traditional healing practices.

According to a study published by "Saudi journal of biological sciences", Typha angustata extract was tested for efficacy against cestodes in 30 pigeons. In the group treated with Typha angustata extract, the mean EPG count was 176 ± 4.33 before treatment and 155 ± 4.24 after treatment. The efficacy of Typha angustata at day 28 was determined to be 11.93%. Based on the EPG and efficacy data, it was concluded that Typha angustata extract had low efficacy against railletiniasis.
