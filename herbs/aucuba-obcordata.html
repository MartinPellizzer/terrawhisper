Aucuba obcordata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aucuba obcordata has elliptical to obcordate leaves that are leathery in texture, evergreen in appearance, and characterized by a distinctively blunt or inverted heart shape at the apex. The leaf margins are typically entire and smooth, while the surface often exhibits a glossy or waxy cuticle that helps retain moisture. Depending on the specific cultivar, the foliage may feature irregular yellowish variegation or remain a deep, uniform green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the axils of the leaves during the spring season. Its woody stems are relatively slender and form a dense, bushy habit as the plant matures.

      What are the herbal preparations of Aucuba obcordata?

      Aucuba obcordata is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical ointment depending on the specific therapeutic goal. The leaves are most commonly simmered in water to create a concentrated liquid extract used for internal consumption. In some traditional practices, the bark is boiled for extended periods to ensure the extraction of active alkaloids. These different methods allow for varying levels of potency and absorption into the body.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Aucuba obcordata can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if any part of the plant is ingested. The leaves and berries contain saponins, which are chemical compounds that can trigger allergic reactions or dermatitis upon contact with the skin. If a person swallows the fruit or foliage, they may experience symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea. While these effects are generally not life-threatening for adults, they can be more serious for children or pets who consume the plant. It is important to handle the plant with care to avoid accidental ingestion or skin discomfort.
