Aucuba obcordata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aucuba obcordata has elliptical to obcordate leaves that are leathery in texture, evergreen in appearance, and characterized by a distinctively blunt or inverted heart shape at the apex. The leaf margins are typically entire and smooth, while the surface often exhibits a glossy or waxy cuticle that helps retain moisture. Depending on the specific cultivar, the foliage may feature irregular yellowish variegation or remain a deep, uniform green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the axils of the leaves during the spring season. Its woody stems are relatively slender and form a dense, bushy habit as the plant matures.
