Arabis paniculata

What's the taxonomical classification of Arabis paniculata?

Arabis paniculata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant falls under the order Brassicales. It is further categorized into the family Brassicaceae, which is a group known for its distinct flowering characteristics. Within this family, the plant is placed in the genus Arabis, and its specific biological identity is defined by its species name, paniculata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Arabis paniculata has an erect, perennial growth habit characterized by basal leaves that are oblong to spatulate and stem leaves that are smaller and alternate. The stems are typically slender and can reach heights of up to 40 centimeters. Its inflorescence consists of dense, terminal panicles or racemes bearing small, four-petaled white flowers. The petals are often slightly notched at the apex and surround bright yellow centers. The fruit develops into narrow, elongated siliques that contain numerous small seeds.
