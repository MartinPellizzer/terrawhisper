Cornus hemsleyi

What's the taxonomical classification of Cornus hemsleyi?

Cornus hemsleyi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized under the order Cornales and falls within the family Cornaceae. Within this family, it is assigned to the genus Cornus, and its specific biological identity is defined by the species name hemsleyi.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cornus hemsleyi has deciduous shrub characteristics with opposite, elliptical leaves that measure approximately 3 to 7 centimeters in length and possess entire margins. The plant features slender, somewhat arching branches that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Small, white, four-petaled flowers emerge in clusters from the terminal ends of the branches during the spring season. These blossoms are followed by the development of small, fleshy drupes that transition from green to a dark blue or black color when mature. The bark on older stems typically exhibits a smooth texture with subtle lenticels visible upon close inspection.
