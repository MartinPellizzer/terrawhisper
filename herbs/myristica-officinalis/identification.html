Scientific Classification

Myristica officinalis belongs to the plant kingdom, classified under Magnoliophyta, Eudicots, Sapindales, Myristicaceae, Myristica, and finally as the species Myristica officinalis. This classification reflects its botanical relationship and evolutionary placement within the plant kingdom.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of the plant is Myristica officinalis, a species within the Myristicaceae family. It was first described by Linnaeus in 1753. Common synonyms include Myristica fragrans and M. cinnamomum. This name reflects its traditional use in herbal medicine and its association with medicinal properties.

Morphological Characteristics

Diagnostic Identification Features

Myristica officinalis can be distinguished by its unique tripartite fruit, which splits into three distinct segments. The seeds are surrounded by a reddish, aromatic aril known as mace. The plant also has lanceolate leaves with a slightly hairy underside. These features help differentiate it from similar species in the Myristica genus.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Myristica officinalis is native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and ample rainfall. It prefers well-drained, fertile soils. It is typically found at altitudes between 200 and 800 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Myristica officinalis blooms during the summer months, typically between June and August. Seed formation occurs in the following spring, around March to May. For identification purposes, the plant is harvested when the fruit is mature, usually in late summer to early autumn.

Myristica officinalis is closely related to species within the genus Myristica, sharing key morphological and genetic traits. It belongs to the Myristicaceae family, which includes other aromatic tropical trees. These relationships highlight its evolutionary ties to related species with similar chemical compositions and medicinal uses.
