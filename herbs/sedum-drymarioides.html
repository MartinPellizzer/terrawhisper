Sedum drymarioides

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sedum drymarioides has succulent, cylindrical to slightly clavate leaves that are typically grayish-green and measure approximately 3 to 6 millimeters in diameter. These leaves are arranged in dense, crowded clusters along stem segments that exhibit a decumbent or ascending growth habit. The plant often forms compact mats or small tufts, which helps it thrive in rocky, arid environments. Small, star-shaped flowers emerge from the terminal ends of the stems, usually presenting in shades of white or pale pink. This morphology allows the species to maximize water storage while minimizing surface area exposure to intense sunlight.
