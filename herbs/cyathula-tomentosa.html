Cyathula tomentosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cyathula tomentosa has woody, perennial stems with leaves that are opposite, ovate to lanceolate in shape, and densely covered in soft, velvety hairs on both surfaces. The leaf margins are typically entire or slightly serrated, providing a textured appearance to the foliage. Its inflorescences are composed of small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in heads that emerge from the leaf axils. The dense pubescence, which gives the species its name, serves as a primary distinguishing morphological feature. These hairy structures are most prominent on the young shoots and the undersides of the leaf blades.
