What's the taxonomical classification of Cyathula tomentosa?

Cyathula tomentosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its hierarchical progression, the plant is placed in the order Caryophyllales and is a member of the family Amaranthaceae. Ultimately, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement within the genus Cyathula, with the specific epithet tomentosa identifying the individual species.

What are the therapeutic actions of Cyathula tomentosa?

Cyathula tomentosa has potent purgative, emetic, and anthelmintic properties used primarily to expel intestinal parasites and treat severe constipation. The plant contains bioactive secondary metabolites that stimulate gastrointestinal motility to facilitate rapid bowel evacuation. These chemical constituents also act directly against helminths to reduce parasite loads within the digestive tract. Because of its high toxicity and intense physiological effects, it is typically administered with extreme caution in traditional medicinal practices. Proper dosage is critical to avoid severe irritation of the mucous membranes or systemic poisoning.

Cyathula tometoza is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste using its various plant parts. Some traditional practices also involve using the plant in poultices to soothe localized inflammation. What side-effects this plant can have?

Cyathula tomentoza can cause severe skin irritation, localized allergic reactions, and potential gastrointestinal distress if ingested. The plant contains bioactive compounds that may trigger contact dermatitis upon direct exposure to the skin. Some individuals might experience redness, itching, or swelling at the site of contact. If consumed accidentally, the chemical constituents could lead to nausea, vomiting, or more significant internal complications. Careful handling and avoidance of ingestion are necessary to prevent these adverse reactions.
