Kalanchoe spathulata

What's the taxonomical classification of Kalanchoe spathulata?

Kalanchoe spatulata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Saxifragales. This succulent plant is a member of the family Crassulaceae, falling specifically within the genus Kalanchoe, and is identified by its unique species name, spatulata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Kalanchoe spathulata has thick, fleshy, obovate leaves that are typically bright green and possess distinctly serrated or dentate margins. These succulent leaves are arranged in a compact rosette pattern to maximize water storage and light absorption. The leaf surfaces are smooth but can exhibit a slightly waxy cuticle to prevent moisture loss in arid environments. Small, reddish-tinted teeth often line the edges of the foliage, providing a characteristic texture. When the plant matures, it produces small, bell-shaped flowers that emerge from clusters atop elongated stems.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Madagascar, specifically within its diverse coastal and inland ecosystems. Because of its ability to store water in its fleshy leaves, it is well-adapted to the semi-arid environments found across its home island. While primarily restricted to Madagascar, it has been widely introduced to other warm climates around the world through cultivation. How is this plant cultivated?

This plant thrives in well-draining, sandy soil under bright, indirect sunlight with watering intervals that allow the substrate to dry completely between applications.

Because it is a succulent, it is highly susceptible to root rot if kept in overly moist conditions. You can propagate it easily by taking stem cuttings that root quickly in a dry environment. During the growing season, occasional fertilization with a balanced liquid formula helps support lush foliage.

Providing consistent warmth and avoiding frost will ensure the plant remains healthy throughout the year.
