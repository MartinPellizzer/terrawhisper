Kalanchoe spathulata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Kalanchoe spathulata has thick, fleshy, obovate leaves that are typically bright green and possess distinctly serrated or dentate margins. These succulent leaves are arranged in a compact rosette pattern to maximize water storage and light absorption. The leaf surfaces are smooth but can exhibit a slightly waxy cuticle to prevent moisture loss in arid environments. Small, reddish-tinted teeth often line the edges of the foliage, providing a characteristic texture. When the plant matures, it produces small, bell-shaped flowers that emerge from clusters atop elongated stems.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Madagascar, specifically within its diverse coastal and inland ecosystems. It thrives in various habitats ranging from dry rocky outcrops to more humid forested areas. Because of its ability to store water in its fleshy leaves, it is well-adapted to the semi-arid environments found across its home island. While primarily restricted to Madagascar, it has been widely introduced to other warm climates around the world through cultivation. Many people now grow it as an ornamental houseplant in temperate regions due to its resilient nature.
