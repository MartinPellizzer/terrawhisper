Impatiens tienchuanensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Impatiens tienchuanensis?

Impatiens tienchuankenis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant falls under the order Ericales. It is further categorized into the family Balsaminaceae and sits within the genus Impatiens, ultimately reaching its specific identification as the species tienchuankensis.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Impatiens tienchuanensis can cause skin irritation, localized redness, and allergic contact dermatitis when the plant comes into direct contact with sensitive tissue. Some individuals may experience itching or a burning sensation at the site of exposure. Ingestion of the plant is generally discouraged due to the potential for gastrointestinal distress or unpredictable systemic reactions. While specific clinical data on human toxicity is limited, cautious handling is recommended to prevent adverse dermatological responses.
