Impatiens tienchuanensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Impatiens tienchuanensis has succulent, lanceolate leaves with serrated margins and small, zygomorphic flowers that typically feature pale pink or white petals with darker nectar guides. The plant's stems are fleshy and translucent, allowing it to thrive in moist, shaded forest environments. Its inflorescence is often solitary or arranged in small clusters, emerging from the leaf axils. The reproductive structures include a distinctive spurred nectar sac characteristic of the genus. These morphological traits collectively help the species adapt to its specific ecological niche in high-altitude regions.
