Mallotus nudiflorus

What's the taxonomical classification of Mallotus nudiflorus?

Mallotus nudiflorus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Malpighiales and is a member of the family Euphorbiaceae. Ultimately, its specific biological identity is defined by its placement within the genus Mallotus, specifically as the species nudiflorus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Mallotus nudiflorus has ovate to elliptical leaves with acuminate tips and serrated margins that are arranged alternately on woody stems. The plant is a small tree or shrub characterized by its distinctive dioecious flowering habit where flowers appear on naked axes. Its leaves often possess small glandular dots on the underside, which is a common trait among members of the Euphorbiaceae family. The inflorescences are typically small and clustered, lacking the large showy petals found in other botanical species. Small, woody capsules serve as the fruit, containing seeds that are essential for the plant's reproductive cycle.
