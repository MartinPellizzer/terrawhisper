10 Precautions To Take When Using Populus Balsamifera (Balm-Of-Gilead)

Populus balsamifera, also known as balm-of-Gilead, has some precautions to consider before using it medicinally, such as consulting a healthcare professional due to its potential allergenic and cytotoxic properties.

Allergic reactions can cause skin irritation, respiratory issues, and anaphylaxis if not properly diagnosed and treated.

Failure to monitor dosages can lead to liver and kidney damage from the herb's sesquiterpenes and phenolic compounds.

This article explains in details the 10 most important precautions to take when using Populus balsamifera medicinally.

1. Report adverse reactions

When using Populus balsamifera medicinally, it's important to take with caution always.

This precaution is necessary because the plant contains coumarins and phenolic compounds that can interact with medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes drugs, and exacerbate existing medical conditions like kidney or liver disease.

Additionally, internal use of Populus balsamifera preparations can cause gastrointestinal upset, allergic reactions, and respiratory issues if not properly prepared and dosed.
