10 Medicinal Constituents Of Populus Balsamifera (Balm-Of-Gilead)

Populus balsamifera has active constituents such as beta-pinene, camphor, and salicin.

These compounds contribute to its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antiseptic activities. The plant's natural resin can be used to treat wounds, skin irritations, and respiratory issues by reducing inflammation and promoting healing.

Additionally, the balm-of-gilead has been traditionally used to relieve pain and reduce fever, offering relief for individuals suffering from arthritis, headaches, and other ailments.

1. Salicin

Populus balsamifera, also known as balm-of-Gilead, salicin is a chemical compound extracted from the bark of this tree species.

Salicin has been used for centuries in traditional medicine to treat various ailments such as fever, rheumatism, and respiratory issues.

It has anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, making it a natural alternative to aspirin.
