10 Possible Side Effects Of Populus Balsamifera (Balm-Of-Gilead)

Populus balsamifera has some side effects when used improperly, such as skin irritation, allergic reactions, and digestive issues.

These side effects can be caused by an allergic sensitivity or an excessive consumption of the herb.

In severe cases, these reactions can worsen people's lives by causing discomfort, fatigue, and anxiety, potentially affecting their daily activities and overall well-being.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Populus balsamifera if used imporperly.

Populus balsamifera causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

The sap of this plant contains compounds like salicin and phenolic acids, which can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling. Additionally, the resinous bark of Populus balsamifera contains terpenoids that may cause respiratory issues or skin irritation in people sensitive to these compounds.

These allergic reactions can range from mild to severe, depending on individual susceptibility and exposure levels.
