Cowpea (Vigna radiata)

Cowpea (Vigna radiata) is a member of the Fabaceae family, native to South Asia, East Africa, and Southern Europe. Traditionally, its seeds, leaves, and fruits have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its diuretic, anti-inflammatory, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in ayurvedic medicine, traditional chinese medicine, and european herbal medicine.

Annual herbaceous plant.

Leaves
Ovate leaves with upper surface glabrous and pale green, lower surface pubescent and slightly darker green, bearing prominent stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
White flowers arranged in racemes, actinomorphic, with five petals arranged in a papilionaceous corolla, featuring a prominent standard petal, two wing petals, and two fused keel petals.
Stems
Erect growth habit with opposite branching, glabrous surface, and nodes bearing stipitate fascicles of stipules.
