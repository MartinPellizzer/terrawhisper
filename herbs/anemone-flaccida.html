Anemone flaccida

What's the taxonomical classification of Anemone flaccida?

Anemone flacciida belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Ranunculales. This plant is a representative of the family Ranunculaceae, specifically situated within the genus Anemone, and is identified by its unique species designation, flacciida.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Anemone flaccida has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by a creeping rhizome and basal leaves that are deeply divided into narrow, linear segments. The foliage is typically bright green and grows in a rosette pattern near the ground. Its flowering stems are slender and upright, bearing solitary, drooping flowers that transition from a pale yellow to a more intense golden hue as they mature. Each blossom features several delicate petals and a prominent central cluster of stamens. These morphological traits allow the plant to thrive in the shaded, moist environments of forest floors.

According to a study published by "Frontiers in plant science", the rhizome of Anemone flaccida has long been used to treat arthritis as a tradition in China. The plant contains a rich source of triterpenoid saponins. Research using Illumina HiSeq 2000 sequencing and iTRAQ technique obtained 46,962 high-quality unigenes with an average sequence length of 1,310 bp and 1,473 unique proteins from Anemone flaccida. Among the transcripts, 36,617 or 77.97% showed significant similarity to known proteins with an E-value < 1e (-5). Additionally, 14,004 isoforms/unigenes were upregulated and 1,4090 were down-regulated in the rhizomes compared to the leaves. The study identified 126 putative cytochrome P450 and 32 putative UDP glycosyltransferases as candidates for modifying these saponins.

Researchers identified 4 possible cytochrome P450 genes belonging to the CYP716A subfamily from the transcriptome of Anemone flaccida. Within this group, the specific gene CYP716A254 encodes a cytochrome P450 monooxygenase that catalyzes the conversion of β-amyrin into oleanolic acid. This process involves the oxidation of β-amyrin at the C-18 position to achieve oleanolic acid production.
