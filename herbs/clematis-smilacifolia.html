What's the taxonomical classification of Clematis smilacifolia?

Clematis smilacifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the biological hierarchy further, it falls under the order Ranunculales and is a member of the family Ranunculaceae. Specifically, this plant is identified by the genus Clematis and is distinguished as the species smilacifolia.

The plant features small, bell-shaped flowers that typically transition from a creamy white to a pale yellowish hue. Its reproductive structures consist of numerous stamens that surround a central cluster of pistils. The foliage remains relatively thin and delicate throughout the growing season.
