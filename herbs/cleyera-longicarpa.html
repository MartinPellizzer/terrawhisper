What is Cleyera longicarpa?

Cleyera longicarpa is a species of flowering plant in the family Pentaphylacaceae, endemic to China.

It is a shrub or small tree that grows up to 8 meters tall, with a trunk diameter of up to 20 centimeters. The leaves are elliptical, alternate, and have a length of 7-15 centimeters, with a width of 2-4 centimeters. The flowers are white, small, and arranged in axillary cymes, with four to five sepals and a calyx of five lobes.

The fruit is a red, drupaceous berry that is 1-2 centimeters in diameter.
