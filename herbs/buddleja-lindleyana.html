Buddleja lindleyana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Buddleja lindleyana has oppositely arranged, lanceolate leaves that measure between 5 and 12 centimeters in length and feature finely serrated margins with a slightly pubescent underside. The plant typically grows as a woody shrub characterized by upright, multi-stemmed branching patterns. Its inflorescences consist of dense, terminal panicles composed of numerous small, tubular flowers that often display shades of lilac or white. Each individual blossom possesses a long corolla tube and prominent stamens that extend slightly beyond the petals. The overall texture of the foliage appears somewhat rugose due to the presence of fine hairs on both the upper and lower surfaces.

      What are the herbal preparations of Buddleja lindleyana?

      Buddleja lindleyana is prepared primarily as a decoction, a concentrated tea made by boiling the plant parts in water to extract its medicinal compounds. This method is commonly used to utilize the roots or woody stems of the plant for their specific chemical properties. Beyond decoctions, the plant can sometimes be processed into topical poultices for external application on the skin.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Buddleja lindleyana can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or respiratory distress if its pollen or foliage is mishandled. While primarily known as an ornamental shrub, direct contact with the leaves or stems may trigger localized redness or itching in sensitive individuals. Inhaling large amounts of its fine pollen can lead to sneezing, coughing, or more significant allergic reactions in people with asthma. It is also important to note that ingestion of the plant parts could potentially cause gastrointestinal discomfort. Always use caution and protective gear when pruning or working closely with this species.
