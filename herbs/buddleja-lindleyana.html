Buddleja lindleyana

What's the taxonomical classification of Buddleja lindleyana?

Buddlejia lindleyana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it falls under the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Scrophulariaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Buddleja, and its unique species designation, lindleyana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Buddleja lindleyana has oppositely arranged, lanceolate leaves that measure between 5 and 12 centimeters in length and feature finely serrated margins with a slightly pubescent underside. The plant typically grows as a woody shrub characterized by upright, multi-stemmed branching patterns. Its inflorescences consist of dense, terminal panicles composed of numerous small, tubular flowers that often display shades of lilac or white. Each individual blossom possesses a long corolla tube and prominent stamens that extend slightly beyond the petals. The overall texture of the foliage appears somewhat rugose due to the presence of fine hairs on both the upper and lower surfaces.

What are the pharmacological activities of Buddleja lindleyana?

This plant has documented neuroprotective activity, anti-inflammatory properties, and antioxidant potential due to its rich concentration of bioactive phytochemicals. These medicinal effects are primarily driven by the presence of specific flavonoids and phenolic compounds found within its extracts. Research indicates that these substances can help mitigate oxidative stress and protect nerve cells from damage. Furthermore, the plant exhibits various antimicrobial and wound-healing capabilities that contribute to its overall therapeutic value. These diverse pharmacological activities suggest that Buddleja lindleyana holds significant promise for future drug development.

According to a study published by "Zhong yao cai = Zhongyaocai = Journal of Chinese medicinal materials", the fruit of Buddleja lindleyana contains triterpenoid chemical components. Through chromatography and spectral data, 3 compounds were separated and identified as oleanane, alpha-L-msnnopyranoside derive (1), 13, 28-epoxy-3beta,23-dihydroxy-11-oleanene (2), and 3, 23, 28-trihydroxyolean-11,13 (18)-diene (3). These 3 compounds showed obviously neuroprotective activity when evaluated using MPP+ induced injury in PC12 cells. The neuroprotective activities of compounds 1, 2, and 3 are reported for the first time. Additionally, the data for compound (1) is reported for the first time.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds alpha-spinasterol-3-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside, beta-sitosterol-3-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside, betulin acid, oleanane, and stigmasterol-3-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside. These specific phytochemicals are significant for their potential bioactive properties in various pharmacological studies. The presence of sterol glycosides like stigmasterol-3-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside suggests important roles in cellular signaling and membrane stability. Additionally, triterpenoids such as betulin acid and oleanane contribute to the plant's diverse chemical profile. These constituents collectively demonstrate the complex therapeutic potential inherent in Buddleja lindleyana.

According to a study published by "Zhongguo Zhong yao za zhi = Zhongguo zhongyao zazhi = China journal of Chinese materia medica", seven compounds were isolated from Buddleja lindleyana. These medicinal compounds include vanillic acid, daidzein, octacosanoic acid, beta-sitosterol-3-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside, stigmasterol-3-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside, alpha-spinasterol-3-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside, and betulin acid. All 7 of these compounds were obtained from this plant for the first time. The separation of these substances was achieved through chromatographic methods and identification was performed using spectral analysis.
