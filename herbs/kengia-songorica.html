What is Kengia songorica?

Kengia songorica is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is a perennial herb with a tufted growth habit, consisting of a short stem and a leafy crown. The leaves are flat, linear, and lance-shaped, typically 2-4 mm wide and 10-30 cm long, with a glabrous or hairy surface. The inflorescence is a dense panicle, 10-30 cm long, with spikelets that are 2-3 cm long and contain 3-5 florets.

The species is native to China and Mongolia, and is found in dry mountainous regions.
