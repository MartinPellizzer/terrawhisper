Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The botanical name of Spineapple is Zanthoxylum clava-herculis, a member of the Rutaceae family. It was first described by the botanist Linnaeus in 1753. This species has several synonyms, including Zanthoxylum clava-herculis and Zanthoxylum hirsutum. It is also known by various common names such as prickly ash and horse chestnut.

Morphological Characteristics

Zanthoxylum clava-herculis, commonly known as spineapple, is a shrub with a woody stem and a taproot system. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated margins and a rough texture. The plant produces small yellow flowers in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Zanthoxylum clava-herculis can be distinguished by its spiny, compound leaves with three to five pairs of leaflets, red-orange to yellow fruits, and a strong, aromatic scent. It is often confused with other Zanthoxylum species, but its unique leaf structure and fruit color are key identifiers. The plant's thorny stems and clustered fruits also help in differentiation.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Zanthoxylum clava-herculis is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and the Gulf Coast. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained, sandy or loamy soils. This plant typically grows at low to mid-elevation areas, often in open woods or along stream banks.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Spineapple (Zanthoxylum clava-herculis) blooms from May to July, producing yellowish-green flowers. Seed formation occurs from June to September. For identification purposes, the plant is typically harvested in late summer to early fall when the fruit is fully mature and the spines are prominent.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Zanthoxylum clava-herculis belongs to the genus Zanthoxylum, which is part of the Rutaceae family. It shares close relationships with other species in the genus, such as Zanthoxylum annuum and Zanthoxylum pteleium. These species exhibit similar morphological and chemical characteristics, which aid in their identification and classification within the family.
