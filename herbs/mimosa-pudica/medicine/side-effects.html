10 Possible Side Effects Of Mimosa Pudica (Sensitive Plant)

Mimosa pudica has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, allergic reactions, and skin irritation.

These can be caused by excessive consumption or prolonged use. For instance, individuals with sensitive stomachs may experience nausea and vomiting, impacting their daily productivity and overall well-being.

Severe cases can even lead to life-threatening complications, such as kidney damage.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Mimosa pudica if used imporperly.

1. Reports oral ulceration

Mimosa pudica experiences allergic reactions in some individuals.

The allergenic properties of the plant are attributed to its sap, which contains a compound called lectin, a type of carbohydrate-binding protein. This substance can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms such as skin irritation, itching, and swelling.

Additionally, the plant's high levels of flavonoids and phenolic acids may also contribute to allergic reactions, causing respiratory issues, digestive problems, or even anaphylaxis in severe cases.
