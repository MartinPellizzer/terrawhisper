Botanical Identification of Sensitive Plant (Mimosa pudica)

To identify Sensitive Plant (Mimosa pudica) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

The Sensitive Plant, Mimosa pudica, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Fabales, order Fabales, family Fabaceae, genus Mimosa, and species pudica. These classifications reflect its position within the plant kingdom and its relationship to other legumes.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of the Sensitive Plant is Mimosa pudica, a species within the legume family. It was first described by the botanist L. Gaertner in 1791. This name reflects its botanical classification and is widely recognized in scientific literature.

Morphological Characteristics

Mimosa pudica is an annual herb with a shallow, fibrous root system. Its stems are erect and branching, often hairy. Leaves are bipinnately compound, closing at touch, and flowers are small, pink or purple, clustered in spherical inflorescences. The fruit is a pod that splits open to release tiny, flat seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Mimosa pudica can be distinguished by its distinctive leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait not seen in most other plants. It has pink or purple flowers and small, hairy pods. Its stems are hairy and often have a reddish tinge, setting it apart from similar species like Mimosa tenuiflora.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Mimosa pudica is native to South and Central America, with some populations in tropical Africa and India. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained, loamy soils. It commonly grows at low to moderate altitudes, typically between 0 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Mimosa pudica blooms from late spring to early summer, producing small pink or purple flowers. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early fall. For identification purposes, the plant is typically harvested during its flowering stage for accurate botanical recognition.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Mimosa pudica belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is closely related to other genera like Mimosa and Sesbania. It shares key family traits such as compound leaves and legume pods. Its closest relatives are species within the Mimosa genus, which also exhibit similar sensitive leaf responses.
