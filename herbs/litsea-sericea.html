Litsea sericea

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Litsea sericea has an evergreen tree habit characterized by simple, alternate, lanceolate leaves that typically measure between 5 and 15 centimeters in length and possess entire margins. The foliage displays a distinct coriaceous texture with a glossy upper surface and a paler underside. Its bark is generally smooth and greyish, providing a stark contrast to the lush green canopy. Small, inconspicuous flowers are borne in axillary panicles, eventually giving rise to small, fleshy drupes. These fruits are often dark in color and serve as a key identification feature for the species.
