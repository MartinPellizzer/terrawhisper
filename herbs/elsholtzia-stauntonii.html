Elsholtzia stauntonii

What's the taxonomical classification of Elsholtzia stauntonii?

Elsholtzia stauntonii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Magnoliidae and is further categorized into the order Lamiales. As a member of the family Lamiaceae, this plant is specifically identified by the genus Elsholtzia and the species stauntonii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Elsholtzia stauntonii has a small to medium-sized deciduous tree structure characterized by spreading, rounded crowns and finely textured, pinnately compound leaves.

The leaflets are typically small, elliptical, and arranged in pairs along a central rachis to create a delicate foliage appearance. Its bark matures into a grayish-brown color with shallow longitudinal fissures that provide a textured surface. The reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into distinctive samara fruits.

These winged seeds are designed for wind dispersal and are a prominent feature during the plant's seasonal cycles.
