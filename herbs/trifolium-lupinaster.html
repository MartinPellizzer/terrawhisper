Trifolium lupinaster

What's the taxonomical classification of Trifolium lupinaster?

Trifolium lupinaster belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized under the order Fabales and the family Fabaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by its placement in the genus Trifolium, with the species name lupinaster.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Trifolium lupinaster has trifoliate leaves with elliptic to obovate leaflets that are typically 10 to 25 millimeters long and feature serrated margins.

The plant structure is characterized by a low-growing, creeping habit with stolons that allow it to spread across the ground. Its inflorescences consist of small, dense heads containing pale pink to white pea-like flowers. The calyx is notably hairy, while the corolla is delicate and slightly fragrant.

These morphological traits distinguish it from other clover species found in similar habitats.
