Trifolium lupinaster

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Trifolium lupinaster has trifoliate leaves with elliptic to obovate leaflets that are typically 10 to 25 millimeters long and feature serrated margins. The plant structure is characterized by a low-growing, creeping habit with stolons that allow it to spread across the ground. Its inflorescences consist of small, dense heads containing pale pink to white pea-like flowers. The calyx is notably hairy, while the corolla is delicate and slightly fragrant. These morphological traits distinguish it from other clover species found in similar habitats.
