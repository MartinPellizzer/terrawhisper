Tylophora floribunda

What's the taxonomical classification of Tylophora floribunda?

Tylophora floribunda belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized under the order Gentianaales and falls within the family Apocynaceae. Ultimately, its specific identification is defined by its placement in the genus Tylophora, with its unique designation being the species floribunda.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tylophora floribunda has climbing, twining stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that feature entire margins and acute tips. The plant exhibits a scandent growth habit, utilizing its slender vines to ascend surrounding vegetation. Its inflorescence consists of small, fragrant flowers that typically emerge from the leaf axils in clusters. The corolla is characterized by a tubular structure with lobes that spread outward. Small, inconspicuous fruits may develop following successful pollination of the blossoms.
