Saxifraga cernua

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saxifraga cernua has a basal rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical with entire or slightly dentate margins. The foliage often exhibits a smooth texture and can range in color from bright green to reddish hues depending on environmental exposure. From the center of this rosette, a slender, upright flowering stem emerges to bear small, inconspicuous blossoms. These flowers are characterized by five petals and are often held in loose clusters at the top of the stalk. The overall structure of the plant is compact and low-growing, adapted for rocky or alpine habitats.
