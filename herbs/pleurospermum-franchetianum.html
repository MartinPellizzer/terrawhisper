Pleurospermum franchetianum has a creeping, rhizomatous habit with small, opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that typically measure between 5 and 15 millimeters in length. The reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These tiny flowers eventually develop into small, fleshy fruits used for dispersal.
