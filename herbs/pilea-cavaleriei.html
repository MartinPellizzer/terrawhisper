Pilea cavaleriei

What's the taxonomical classification of Pilea cavaleriei?

Pilea cavaleriei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Rosales. This plant is a member of the family Urticaceae and is specifically situated within the genus Pilea, ultimately defined by its unique species designation, cavaleriei.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pilea cavaleriei has small, ovate to elliptical leaves that are characterized by a deep, puckered texture and a dark, velvety green coloration. The leaf margins are finely serrated, adding a delicate edge to the compact foliage. These leaves grow on thin, trailing stems that allow the plant to form a dense, spreading mat. The plant typically maintains a low-growing, creeping habit suitable for groundcover or hanging containers. Small, inconspicuous flowers may emerge from the leaf axils during its growing season.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires bright, indirect sunlight, consistently moist but well-draining soil, and high humidity levels between 60% and 80% to thrive.

You should avoid direct afternoon sun, which can scorch the delicate, textured leaves. Water the soil when the top inch feels dry to the touch to prevent root rot. Placing the pot on a pebble tray or using a humidifier helps maintain the moist environment necessary for its growth.

Consistent fertilization during the spring and summer months will further support its lush appearance.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds cis p-coumaroyl copaborneol-type sesquiterpenoid and p-coumaroyl humulane-type sesquiterpenoids. These specific chemical constituents are categorized as specialized secondary metabolites found within the species. Researchers study these sesquiterpenoids to understand their potential pharmacological activities and biological effects. The presence of these unique molecules contributes to the complex chemical profile of Pilea cavalieriei. Such detailed phytochemical analysis helps identify the therapeutic properties inherent to the plant's structure.

According to a study published by "Natural product research", one new cis p-coumaroyl copaborneol-type sesquiterpenoid (1) was isolated from the whole plants of Pilea cavaleriei. This new compound was found together with its known trans isomer (2). Additionally, six other known p-coumaroyl humulane-type sesquiterpenoids (3-8) were isolated from the whole plants. Compounds 1-4 exhibited moderate antimycobacterial activity against Mycobacterium tuberculosis H37Rv.
