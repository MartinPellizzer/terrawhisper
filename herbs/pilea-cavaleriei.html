Pilea cavaleriei

What's the taxonomical classification of Pilea cavaleriei?

Pilea cavaleriei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Rosales. This plant is a member of the family Urticaceae and is specifically situated within the genus Pilea, ultimately defined by its unique species designation, cavaleriei.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pilea cavaleriei has small, ovate to elliptical leaves that are characterized by a deep, puckered texture and a dark, velvety green coloration. The leaf margins are finely serrated, adding a delicate edge to the compact foliage. These leaves grow on thin, trailing stems that allow the plant to form a dense, spreading mat. The plant typically maintains a low-growing, creeping habit suitable for groundcover or hanging containers. Small, inconspicuous flowers may emerge from the leaf axils during its growing season.

    What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

    This plant contains medicinal compounds such as cis p-coumaroyl copaborneol-type sesquiterpenoid and p-coumaroyl humulane-type sesquiterpenoids. These specific sesquitepene derivatives contribute to the unique chemical profile of the species. Researchers study these molecules to understand their potential therapeutic properties in human health. The presence of these p-coumaroyl derivatives often dictates the biological activity observed in plant extracts. Detailed chemical analysis helps identify how these compounds interact with various physiological targets.

    As discussed in a study, one new cis p-coumaroyl copaborneol-type sesquiterpenoid (1) was isolated from the whole plants of Pilea cavaleriei. This new compound was found alongside its known trans isomer (2) and six other known p-coumaroyl humulane-type sesquiterpenoids (3-8). The total number of isolated sesquiterpenoids from the whole plants of Pilea cavaleriei was 8. Among these, compounds 1-4 exhibited moderate antimycobacterial activity against Mycobacterium tuberculosis H37Rv.
