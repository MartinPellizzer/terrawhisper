Pilea cavaleriei

What's the taxonomical classification of Pilea cavaleriei?

Pilea cavaleriei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Rosales. This plant is a member of the family Urticaceae and is specifically situated within the genus Pilea, ultimately defined by its unique species designation, cavaleriei.

Consequently, it is not currently utilized in traditional or modern pharmacology to treat specific ailments.     What are the herbal preparations of Pilea cavaleriei?

    Pilea cavaleriei is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical tincture to utilize its bioactive compounds. The leaves are typically dried and simmered in water to extract soluble constituents for internal consumption. When used as a tincture, the plant material is macerated in high-proof alcohol to concentrate its medicinal properties. These liquid preparations are often applied externally to the skin to address localized issues. Some traditional practitioners also incorporate the plant into poultices for direct contact with affected areas.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Pilea cavaleriei can cause mild skin irritation or allergic reactions if the sap comes into direct contact with sensitive skin. While there are no documented cases of severe toxicity in humans, handling the plant without gloves may lead to localized redness or itching for some individuals. Pet owners should be cautious because ingestion by cats or dogs might result in mild gastrointestinal upset such as vomiting or diarrhea. It is best to keep this plant out of reach of small children and animals to prevent accidental consumption. Always wash your hands thoroughly after pruning or repotting the plant to minimize any potential contact with its juices.
