Pilea cavaleriei

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pilea cavaleriei has small, ovate to elliptical leaves that are characterized by a deep, puckered texture and a dark, velvety green coloration. The leaf margins are finely serrated, adding a delicate edge to the compact foliage. These leaves grow on thin, trailing stems that allow the plant to form a dense, spreading mat. The plant typically maintains a low-growing, creeping habit suitable for groundcover or hanging containers. Small, inconspicuous flowers may emerge from the leaf axils during its growing season.

      What are the herbal preparations of Pilea cavaleriei?

      Pilea cavaleriei is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical tincture to utilize its bioactive compounds.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Pilea cavaleriei can cause mild skin irritation or allergic reactions if the sap comes into direct contact with sensitive skin. While there are no documented cases of severe toxicity in humans, handling the plant without gloves may lead to localized redness or itching for some individuals. Pet owners should be cautious because ingestion by cats or dogs might result in mild gastrointestinal upset such as vomiting or diarrhea. It is best to keep this plant out of reach of small children and animals to prevent accidental consumption. Always wash your hands thoroughly after pruning or repotting the plant to minimize any potential contact with its juices.
