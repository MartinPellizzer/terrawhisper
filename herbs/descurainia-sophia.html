Descurainia sophia

What's the taxonomical classification of Descurainia sophia?

Descurainia sophia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further categorized under the order Brassicales. It falls within the family Brassicaceae, specifically nested in the genus Descurainia, with its unique scientific designation being the species sophia.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Descurainia sophia can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, including intense nausea, vomiting, and abdominal cramping, if ingested. The plant contains bioactive compounds that may disrupt normal digestive processes upon consumption. Beyond stomach issues, potential exposure might lead to systemic toxicity affecting more sensitive internal organs. There is also a risk of localized irritation or allergic skin reactions if the plant comes into direct contact with the body. Because the specific toxicological profile is not fully documented in humans, caution is necessary when handling this species.
