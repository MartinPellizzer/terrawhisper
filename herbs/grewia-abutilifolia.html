Grewia abutilifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Grewia abutilifolia?

Grewia abutilifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it falls into the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic journey continues through the order Malvales and the family Malvaceae, eventually reaching its specific designation within the genus Grewia. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its unique species name, abutilifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Grewia abutilifolia has an erect, woody shrub or small tree structure characterized by simple, alternate leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptical in shape with entire or slightly wavy margins.

The leaf surfaces often exhibit a leathery texture and can vary in color from bright green to a more muted shade depending on environmental exposure. Its stems are typically slender but become increasingly robust and bark-covered as the plant matures. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, eventually giving way to small, woody fruits.

These morphological features allow the plant to adapt effectively to its specific ecological niche.

Grewia abutilifolia has therapeutic properties including astringent, hemostatic, anti-inflammatory, and antipyretic actions that are utilized in traditional medicine.

Additionally, its anti-inflammatory effects work to reduce swelling and soothe irritated areas of the body.

Finally, its antipyretic properties are used to help lower elevated body temperatures during a fever.
