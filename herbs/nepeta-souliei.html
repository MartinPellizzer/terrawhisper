Nepeta souliei

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Nepeta souliei has decumbent to ascending stems that are quadrangular and covered in fine, spreading hairs. The leaves are primarily opposite, displaying an ovate to lanceolate shape with crenate or serrate margins and a grayish-green coloration. Small, tubular flowers emerge from verticillate clusters located within the upper leaf axils. These blossoms typically feature a distinct two-lipped corolla that is characteristic of the Lamiaceae family. The plant's overall structure remains compact, often forming dense clumps of aromatic foliage.
