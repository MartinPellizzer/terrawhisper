Roundleaf Mallow (Malva Rotundifolia)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Malva rotundifolia.

Roundleaf mallow, scientifically known as Malva rotundifolia, is a medicinal herb native to parts of Europe and Asia, prized for its therapeutic properties and adaptogenic qualities. This plant is traditionally used to support digestive health, reduce inflammation, and promote skin healing due to its rich content of mucilage and antioxidants. In traditional Chinese medicine and European herbalism, it has been employed to soothe respiratory conditions, relieve gastrointestinal discomfort, and treat skin ailments such as eczema and burns. Today, it is increasingly integrated into modern wellness practices as a natural remedy for stress relief, immune support, and digestive wellness. Notably, its unique mucilaginous texture and mild, slightly sweet flavor make it a distinctive herb, while its historical use in ancient herbal texts highlights its long-standing significance in holistic health.
