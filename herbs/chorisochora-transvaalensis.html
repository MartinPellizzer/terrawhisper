What is Chorisochora transvaalensis?

Chorisochora transvaalensis is a species of plant in the family Acanthaceae.

It is an herbaceous perennial with a woody base, producing slender stems 10-30 cm tall, and bearing linear-lanceolate leaves 5-20 cm long with serrulate margins. The leaves are arranged oppositely on the stem, and are pubescent on both surfaces. The plant produces small, white or purple flowers in axillary clusters, which are arranged in a cymose pattern and contain 4-5 lobed corolla.

The fruits are small, elliptical capsules, 1-2 cm long, containing 4-5 seeds.
