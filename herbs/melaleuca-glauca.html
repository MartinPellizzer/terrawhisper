Melaleuca glauca

Melaleuca glauca, commonly known as the Narrow-leaved Paperbark, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

This plant is rich in various health benefits, including its ability to reduce inflammation and fight against bacterial, fungal, and viral infections.

The therapeutic actions of Melaleuca glauca are attributed to its antibacterial, anti-inflammatory, and antifungal properties, which can help soothe skin irritations, wounds, and other conditions.

The bioactive constituents of Melaleuca glauca include compounds such as caryophyllene, limonene, and pinene, which contribute to its medicinal properties.
