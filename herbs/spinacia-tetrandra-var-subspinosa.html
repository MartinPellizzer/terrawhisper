Spinach (Spinacia Tetrandra Var. Subspinosa)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Spinacia tetrandra var. subspinosa.

Spinach, scientifically known as Spinacia tetrandra var. subspinosa, is a leafy green vegetable widely recognized as a medicinal herb and adaptogen, valued for its rich nutritional profile and therapeutic properties. It is renowned for its primary benefits, including high levels of iron, vitamins A, C, and K, and antioxidants such as lutein and zeaxanthin, which support immune function, eye health, and cardiovascular wellness. In traditional and cultural uses, spinach has been a staple in Mediterranean, Asian, and Middle Eastern cuisines for centuries, often used to treat anemia, digestive issues, and as a nourishing food during periods of illness or fatigue. Modern wellness practices continue to embrace spinach for its role in detoxification, anti-inflammatory effects, and as a key ingredient in smoothies, salads, and supplements. One of its unique features is the presence of oxalic acid, which can affect mineral absorption, and its historical significance as a symbol of fertility and vitality in ancient Egypt.
