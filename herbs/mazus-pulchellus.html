Mazus pulchellus

What's the taxonomical classification of Mazus pulchellus?

Mazus pulchellus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is placed in the order Lamiales and the family Mazaceae. Finally, it is identified by its genus, Mazus, and its specific species name, pulchellus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Mazus pulchellus has low-growing, creeping stems that form dense mats of ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated margins. These leaves typically measure between 10 and 30 millimeters in length and are arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually violet or pale blue with distinct white markings on the throat. These blossoms emerge from the leaf axils and feature a two-lipped corolla structure. Small, inconspicuous fruits develop following pollination to facilitate the spread of the species.
