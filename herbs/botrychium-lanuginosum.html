Botrychium lanuginosum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Botrychium lanuginosum has a dimorphic leaf structure consisting of a sterile basal portion with pinnately lobed segments and a fertile portion featuring a single, upright, spike-like cluster of spores. The sterile frond is typically covered in fine, woolly hairs that give the plant its characteristic fuzzy appearance. Each lobe of the sterile segment is deeply divided, often appearing somewhat fleshy or thickened compared to other grape ferns. The fertile spike is distinctly separated from the sterile foliage and carries numerous small, yellowish-to-brownish sporangia. This entire photosynthetic and reproductive apparatus arises from a single, creeping underground rhizome.
