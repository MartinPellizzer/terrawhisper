Impatiens recurvicornis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Impatiens recurvicornis has succulent, oppositely arranged leaves with serrated margins and distinctive recurved, horn-like floral structures. The stems are typically fleshy and translucent, facilitating water transport through the plant's delicate tissues. Small, zygomorphic flowers emerge from the axils, often displaying intricate patterns and specialized shapes for pollination. The foliage is characterized by a soft texture and a vibrant green hue that adapts to shaded forest environments. These morphological traits collectively allow the species to thrive in high-moisture habitats.
