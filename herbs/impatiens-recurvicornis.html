Impatiens recurvicornis

What's the taxonomical classification of Impatiens recurvicornis?

Impatiens recurvicornis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Ericales and is a member of the family Balsaminaceae. Ultimately, its specific classification is defined by the genus Impatiens and the species recurvicornis.

    Impatiens recurvicornis is prepared primarily as a decoction, a concentrated liquid extract made by boiling the plant parts in water. Some practitioners may also utilize the plant in the form of a topical paste for external applications. These pastes are typically applied directly to the skin to address localized issues. In certain regional traditions, the plant might also be incorporated into infused oils for massage purposes.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Impatiens recurvicornis can cause skin irritation, redness, and localized itching if the plant's sap comes into contact with human skin. Some individuals may experience allergic reactions such as swelling or a rash upon touching the stems or leaves. There is also a risk of gastrointestinal distress if any part of the plant is accidentally ingested by humans or pets. While specific toxicological studies are limited, caution is advised when handling the species to prevent dermatological discomfort. Monitoring for these symptoms is important for anyone working closely with the plant in a garden setting.
