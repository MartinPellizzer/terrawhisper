Eurya acutisepala

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eurya acutisepala has small, elliptical leaves with acute apices and distinctively sharp, narrow sepals that characterize its floral structure. The foliage is typically evergreen, featuring a glossy texture and serrated margins that run along the leaf edges. Its flowers are relatively inconspicuous, emerging from axillary positions along the woody stems. The plant develops small, fleshy fruits that follow the flowering stage to complete its reproductive cycle. These morphological traits help distinguish the species from other members within the Thymelaeaceae family.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Eurya acutisepala can cause skin irritation, localized allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested without proper processing. While certain parts of the plant are studied for medicinal potential, direct contact with the leaves or stems may lead to redness or itching in sensitive individuals. Ingesting raw extracts could potentially result in nausea, vomiting, or abdominal cramping due to unknown toxic compounds. There is currently limited clinical data regarding long-term systemic toxicity in humans. Therefore, caution is advised when handling or consuming any part of this species.
