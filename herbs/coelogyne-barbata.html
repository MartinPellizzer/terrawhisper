Coelogyne barbata

What's the taxonomical classification of Coelogyne barbata?

Coelogyne barbaata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant falls under the order Asparagales. It is a specialized member of the family Orchidaceae, specifically situated within the genus Coelogyne, and is identified by the species name barbata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Coelogyne barbaata has sympodial rhizomes that produce fleshy, elongated pseudobulbs topped by two or three ovate to lanceolate leaves.

The inflorescence is a pendulous raceme that bears multiple small, yellowish-white flowers characterized by a distinctively fringed or bearded lip. This lip features long, fine hairs along its margins which give the species its specific epithet. The flowers possess a fleshy column and a prominent, concave labellum that serves as a landing platform for pollinators.

The overall structure of the plant allows it to cling effectively to tree branches in its natural epiphytic habitat.
