What's the taxonomical classification of Prioria buchholzii?

Prioria buchholzii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant falls under the order Fabales. It is further categorized into the family Fabaceae, which is characterized by its diverse group of flowering plants. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Prioria, and its specific biological identity is defined by its species name, buchholzii.

    Prioria buchholzii is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice depending on the intended medicinal application.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Prioria buchholzii can cause skin irritation and allergic reactions such as contact dermatitis if the sap or foliage comes into direct contact with human skin. While specific toxicological studies on humans are limited, the presence of unidentified secondary metabolites suggests a risk of localized inflammation. Contact with the plant may result in redness, itching, or a burning sensation upon exposure. It is advisable to wear gloves when handling the plant to prevent accidental dermatological distress. Individuals with sensitive skin should exercise extra caution to avoid any potential adverse reactions.
