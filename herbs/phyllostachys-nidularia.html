Phyllostachys nidularia

What's the taxonomical classification of Phyllostachys nidularia?

Phyllostachys nidularia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Poales. As a member of the family Poaceae, this plant is further categorized into the genus Phyllostachys, with the specific designation of the species nidularia.

Phyllostachys nidularia is prepared primarily as a concentrated aqueous decoction, a dried powdered extract, or a topical infused oil. What side-effects this plant can have?

Phyllostachys nidularia can cause severe contact dermatitis, localized swelling, and intense skin irritation upon direct physical exposure. The plant contains specific bioactive compounds that trigger an inflammatory response in the dermal layers of most individuals. Once the skin comes into contact with the foliage, redness and itching often develop within minutes. In some sensitive cases, these reactions may escalate into blistering or prolonged dermatological discomfort. Proper protective gear should always be worn when handling this species to prevent accidental contact.
