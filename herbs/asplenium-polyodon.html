What's the taxonomical classification of Asplenium polyodon?

Asplenium polyodon belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae. Following the order Polypodiales, the plant is a member of the family Aspleniaceae. Its taxonomic hierarchy concludes with its placement in the genus Asplenium, specifically identified as the species polyodon.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Asplenium polyodon has pinnate fronds characterized by numerous narrow, lanceolate leaflets that are arranged symmetrically along a central rachis. The plant features a creeping rhizome that often produces small, scale-like hairs along its surface. Each individual pinna is typically acute at the apex and possesses slightly serrated or entire margins depending on the specimen's maturity. The texture of the foliage is relatively thin and herbaceous, providing a delicate appearance to the overall fern structure. Spores are housed in small sori located on the underside of the leaflets, which is a defining reproductive feature of the species.
