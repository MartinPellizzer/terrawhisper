What's the taxonomical classification of Lecomtedoxa nogo?

Lecomtedoxa nogo belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is situated in the order Ericales and is a member of the family Sapotaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Lecomtedoxa, and its unique species designation, nogo.

This plant is used to treat respiratory infections, digestive ailments, skin inflammations, and mild fever symptoms. Practitioners often prepare extracts from the leaves to soothe persistent coughs and bronchial congestion. For digestive issues, the plant acts as a mild sedative for the gut to alleviate stomach cramps.
