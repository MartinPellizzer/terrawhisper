What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lecomtedoxa nogo has lanceolate to ovate leaves that are arranged oppositely along a woody, branched stem characterized by a slightly rough texture. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axillary regions of the stems during the peak growing season. These reproductive structures are often surrounded by protective bracts that vary in shape depending on the maturity of the plant. The root system is extensive and fibrous, allowing the organism to anchor firmly into diverse soil compositions.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Lecomtedoxa nogo can cause severe skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential neurological impairment if ingested or handled improperly. Some individuals have also reported dizziness and confusion following accidental exposure. It is crucial to avoid all contact with this species due to these unpredictable physiological reactions.
