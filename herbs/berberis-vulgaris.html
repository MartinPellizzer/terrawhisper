Berberis vulgaris

Berberis vulgaris, commonly known as Barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe, Western Asia, and North Africa.

The plant is valued for its numerous health benefits, including its ability to stimulate the immune system, reduce inflammation, and act as an antimicrobial agent.

The therapeutic actions of Berberis vulgaris include its antioxidant, anti-diarrheal, and anti-diabetic properties, which are attributed to its bioactive constituents, such as berberine, alkaloids, and other phenolic compounds.

Possible Side Effects of Berberis vulgaris

In severe cases, prolonged use of berberis vulgaris may cause liver damage and other organ complications.
