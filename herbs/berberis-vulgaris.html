Barberry (Berberis vulgaris)

Barberry (Berberis vulgaris) is a member of the Berberidaceae family, native to Europe, West Asia, and North Africa. Traditionally, its roots, bark, and leaves have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, diuretic, and bitter actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, ayurvedic medicine, and mediterranean herbal traditions.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically reaches a height of 1 to 3 meters.
Leaves
Simple leaves with dorsiventral coloration, upper surface dark green, lower surface pale green, possessing stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Flowers are yellow with five petals arranged in a raceme, exhibit actinomorphic symmetry, and have distinctive orange-red stipules at the base of each flower.
Stems
Erect, branched, woody stems with thorn-like spines, smooth to slightly pubescent surfaces, and opposite or alternate branching patterns.
