Sargassum Fusiforme Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Sargassum fusiforme, commonly known as the bladderwrack, is a type of brown seaweed that grows in coastal waters.

It is rich in bioactive constituents such as iodine, alginates, fucoidans, and various minerals, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

The herb is known for its potential health benefits, including supporting thyroid function, aiding digestion, and promoting detoxification.

It can be prepared as an infusion, decoction, or used in topical applications for its medicinal effects.
