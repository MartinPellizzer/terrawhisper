Stipularia africana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stipularia africana has a woody, shrubby growth habit characterized by dense, fine-textured foliage and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and leathery. The plant features prominent, often stipule-like structures at the base of its leaf stalks which contribute to its distinctive appearance. Its stems are sturdy and branched, providing a robust framework for the compact canopy. The leaves are arranged alternately along the branches and possess a smooth margin. This morphology allows the species to thrive in various African environments while maintaining a resilient structure.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of West and Central Africa, spanning from Senegal in the west to Ethiopia in the east. The species thrives in areas with high humidity and consistent seasonal rainfall patterns. Populations are often concentrated along river basins where the soil remains nutrient-rich and damp.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Stipularia africana can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and potential digestive distress if ingested. The plant contains specific chemical compounds that may trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. When handled without protection, the sap or foliage can lead to redness, itching, or localized swelling on the skin. If accidental consumption occurs, it might result in nausea, abdominal pain, or other gastrointestinal complications. It is important to exercise caution and avoid direct contact to prevent these adverse reactions.
