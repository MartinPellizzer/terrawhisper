Dendrobium linawianum

What's the taxonomical classification of Dendrobium linawianum?

Dendrobium linawianum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized under the order Asparagales. As a member of the diverse family Orchidaceae, this plant is specifically identified by its genus, Dendrobium, and its unique species designation, linawianum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dendrobium linawianum has pendulous, sympodial pseudobulbs that are elongated, compressed, and typically reach lengths of up to 40 centimeters. These fleshy stems are often slightly ridged and serve as the primary storage organs for the plant. From the nodes of these pseudobulbs, thin, leathery leaves emerge in an alternate arrangement. The inflorescence consists of small, delicate flowers that are often white or pale in color with subtle markings.
