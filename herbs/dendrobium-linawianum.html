Dendrobium linawianum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dendrobium linawianum has pendulous, sympodial pseudobulbs that are elongated, compressed, and typically reach lengths of up to 40 centimeters. These fleshy stems are often slightly ridged and serve as the primary storage organs for the plant. From the nodes of these pseudobulbs, thin, leathery leaves emerge in an alternate arrangement. The inflorescence consists of small, delicate flowers that are often white or pale in color with subtle markings. These blooms are typically borne on the upper portions of the mature stems to facilitate pollination.
