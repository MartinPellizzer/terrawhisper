Peperomia dindygulensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Peperomia dindygulensis?

Peperomia dindygulensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Piperales and is a member of the family Piperaceae. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Peperomia and the unique species designation dindygulensis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds peperomin A, peperomin B, peperomin C, peperomin E, and dindyguleranone. These specific chemical constituents are responsible for the unique therapeutic properties observed in the species. Researchers have identified that the various peperomin derivatives play a crucial role in its biological activity. Furthermore, the presence of dindyguleranone adds a complex layer to its phytochemical profile. Studying these individual components helps scientists understand how the plant interacts with human physiological systems.

As discussed in a study, Peperomia dindygulensis contains five bioactive secolignans, which are peperomin A, peperomin B, peperomin C, 4″-hydroxypeperomin B, and 4″-hydroxypeperomin C. The lower limits of quantification for these compounds are 1.1ng/mL for peperomin A, 1.24ng/mL for peperomin B, 1.02ng/mL for peperomin C, 1.91ng/mL for 4″-hydroxypeperomin B, and 1.27ng/mL for 4″-hydroxypeperomin C. Analytical methods showed correlation coefficients greater than 0.9972 for all analytes. The precision (RSD%) for these compounds was within 15% and the accuracy (RE%) ranged from -11.7% to 10.3%. These five specific bioactive secolignans were successfully studied in rat plasma following oral administration of the Peperomia dindygulensis extract.
