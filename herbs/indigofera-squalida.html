Indigofera squalida

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Indigofera squalida has pinnately compound leaves consisting of multiple small, oblong leaflets and small, inconspicuous flowers typically arranged in axillary racemes. The stems are often woody at the base and exhibit a somewhat spreading or ascending growth habit. Its leaflets are characterized by a smooth or slightly textured surface with entire margins. The plant produces small, legume-type pods that contain seeds essential for its reproductive cycle. These morphological features distinguish it from other species within the Fabaceae family.
