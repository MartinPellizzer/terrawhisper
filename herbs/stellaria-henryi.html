Stellaria henryi

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stellaeria henryi has small, herbaceous stems that grow in a low-lying, spreading habit with tiny, ovate leaves and minute, white, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically features thin, delicate foliage that is often arranged oppositely along the creeping stems. Its flowering structures are inconspicuous and emerge from the leaf axils to form small clusters. The root system is shallow, allowing the plant to colonize moist soil environments effectively. These morphological traits characterize its role as a delicate groundcover in specific microhabitats.

      What are the herbal preparations of Stellaria henryi?

      Stellaria henryi is prepared as a decoction, a tea, or a topical wash depending on the intended therapeutic use. The dried aerial parts of the plant are most commonly simmered in water to create a concentrated liquid extract for internal consumption.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Stellaria henryi can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Contact with the plant's sap or leaves may lead to redness, itching, or a localized rash on sensitive skin areas. If accidentally consumed, individuals might experience nausea, vomiting, or abdominal cramping. Some people may also exhibit respiratory discomfort if the plant's pollen is inhaled in large quantities. These adverse reactions vary depending on the individual's sensitivity to the plant's chemical compounds.
