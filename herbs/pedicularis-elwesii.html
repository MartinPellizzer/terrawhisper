Pedicularis elwesii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pedicularis elwesii has erect, leafy stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and bear dense, terminal spikes of tubular, bilabiate flowers. The leaves are mostly basal and deeply pinnatifid, often divided into narrow, linear segments. Its flowers exhibit a characteristic galea, which is a hooded upper lip that protects the reproductive organs. The corolla colors can vary from pale pink to deep purple, depending on the specific population. This species features a robust root system adapted to its high-altitude alpine habitats.
