What's the taxonomical classification of Trillium tschonoskii?

Trillium tschonoskii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Liliales and into the family Melanthiaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific placement within the genus Trillium, resulting in its scientific name, Trillium tschonoskii.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds polyphylliin VI, polyphylliin VII, steroidal saponins, sesquiteprenoid glycosides, and saponin. These bioactive constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's therapeutic effects in traditional medicine. The steroidal saponins and general saponin content play critical roles in various biological activities. Furthermore, the specific glycosides such as polyphylliin VI and polyphylliin VII are noted for their unique pharmacological properties. These sesquiteprenoid glycosides contribute to the complex chemical profile that defines the plant's medicinal utility.

As discussed in a study, Trillium tschonoskii is a medicinal plant that biosynthesizes steroidal saponins. A phytochemical investigation of its rhizomes led to the isolation of 9 new furostanol saponins (1-9) and 11 known analogues (10-20). Among these, 5 new compounds have hydroxy groups at the C-5 and C-6 positions, while 2 possess a rare aglycone with carbonyl groups at the C-16 and C-22 positions and a Δ17(20) double bond. Compounds 6, 7, 9, 10, 14, 15, and 19 were found to increase the numbers of human cord blood CD34+ hematopoietic stem cells. Additionally, compounds 9, 10, 13, and 14 demonstrated an enhanced ability to increase the percentages and numbers of CD34+CD38- cells and multipotential progenitors.
