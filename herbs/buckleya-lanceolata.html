Buckleya lanceolata

What's the taxonomical classification of Buckleya lanceolata?

Buckleya lanceolata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Santalales. It is further categorized under the family Santalaceae, placing it within a group of specialized flowering plants. Within this family, it is identified by the genus Buckleya, and its specific biological designation is completed by the species name lanceolata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Buckleya lanceolata has lanceolate leaves that are typically 10 to 25 centimeters long, featuring entire margins and prominent pinnate venation.

The foliage is characterized by a coriaceous texture and a deep green color on the upper surface. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the axils of the branches. The fruit develops into a woody capsule that eventually splits to release its seeds.

This woody shrub or small tree displays a compact growth habit with branching patterns that support its dense canopy.
