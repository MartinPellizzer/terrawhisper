Codonopsis macrocalyx belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Campanulaceae, specifically falling under the genus Codonoopsis, with the species designation macrocalyx.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Codonopsis macrocalyx has long, lanceolate leaves that are dark green with prominent longitudinal veins and large, tubular flowers characterized by an exceptionally oversized calyx.

These leaves grow from a rhizomatous base and typically feature an elongated, somewhat leathery texture. The inflorescence is notable for its bright, nectar-rich blossoms that emerge from a protective, expanded floral envelope. Each flower is structurally complex, often displaying a striking contrast between the inner petals and the outer calyx structure.
