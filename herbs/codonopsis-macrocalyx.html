What's the taxonomical classification of Codonopsis macrocalyx?

Codonopsis macrocalyx belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Campanulaceae, specifically falling under the genus Codonoopsis, with the species designation macrocalyx.

    What diseases this plant treats?

    This plant is used to treat various inflammatory conditions, digestive ailments, and skin infections in traditional medicinal practices. Local healers often utilize its components to soothe stomach pains and dysentery. It is also applied topically to reduce swelling and heal wounds or minor skin irritations. Some practitioners believe the plant possesses properties that help manage feverish symptoms. These applications highlight its role in managing both internal and external physical distress.

    What are the herbal preparations of Codonopsis macrocalyx?

    Codonopsis macrocalyx is prepared primarily as a decoction through boiling the dried plant material in water to extract its active medicinal components. Some traditional applications involve using the crushed fresh leaves as a topical poultice applied directly to the skin. Additionally, fine powders derived from the dried stems and roots may be mixed into food or water for oral consumption. These various forms allow for both internal and external therapeutic uses depending on the specific ailment being addressed.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Codonopsis macrocalyx can cause skin irritation or allergic reactions if the plant's sap comes into direct contact with sensitive skin. Some individuals may experience redness, itching, or a mild rash after handling the foliage or stems. While there is limited clinical data regarding human ingestion, consuming any part of this plant could potentially lead to gastrointestinal distress. It is important to exercise caution when gardening with this species to avoid accidental contact with mucous membranes. Always wash your hands thoroughly after touching the plant to minimize these risks.
