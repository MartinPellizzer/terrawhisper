Arenaria przewalskii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Arenaria przewalskii has a perennial growth habit characterized by a caespitose form with erect stems that typically reach heights of 5 to 15 centimeters. The leaves are small, sessile, and linear to lanceolate in shape, arranged densely along the stem. Each stem terminates in a single flower supported by a short pedicel. The flowers feature five white petals that are often slightly notched at the apex. Small, rounded sepals protect the developing bud and support the base of the corolla during blooming.
