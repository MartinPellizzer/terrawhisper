Eragrostis ferruginea

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eragrostis ferruginea has a prostrate or ascending growth habit characterized by slender, rooting stems and narrow, linear leaves with a distinctively reddish or ferruginous tint. The plant typically forms dense, low-growing mats through creeping stolons that anchor into the substrate. Its leaves are relatively short and often possess a slightly revolute margin. The inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous spikelets arranged in loose clusters at the tips of the stems. These spikelets contain several florets protected by membranous glumes that contribute to the species' delicate appearance.
