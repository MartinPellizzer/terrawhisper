What's the taxonomical classification of Cypripedium henryi?

Cypripedium henryi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Asparagales. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cypripedium henryi has a perennial life cycle characterized by fleshy, tuberous roots and narrow, lanceolate leaves that grow in a basal rosette.

The inflorescence consists of a single, showy flower supported by a slender stem that reaches heights typically between ten and thirty centimeters. The most distinctive feature is the labellum, which forms a deep, pouch-like structure often colored in shades of yellow or brownish-purple. Surrounding this pouch, the sepals and petals are typically elongated and may exhibit subtle spotting or veining patterns.
