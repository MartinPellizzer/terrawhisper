Phtheirospermum tenuisectum

What's the taxonomical classification of Phtheirospermum tenuisectum?

Phtheirospermum tenuisectuum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Lamiales. It is further categorized into the family Orobanchaceae, which contains the genus Phtheirospermum, ultimately placing this specific organism in the species tenuisectuum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phtheirospermum tenuisectuum has narrow, lanceolate leaves with entire margins and a distinctively slender, elongated shape.

The foliage is typically arranged in an opposite pattern along the woody stems. Its flowers are relatively small and inconspicuous, often appearing in axillary clusters. The plant produces small, drupaceous fruits that contribute to its reproductive morphology.

These structural features are characteristic of its growth habit within tropical forest understories.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Phtheirospermum tenuisectum can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and localized inflammation upon direct contact with its tissues. Some people might experience swelling or discomfort if the plant is handled without protective clothing. It is important to avoid contact to prevent these dermatological issues.
