Phtheirospermum tenuisectum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phtheirospermum tenuisectuum has narrow, lanceolate leaves with entire margins and a distinctively slender, elongated shape. The foliage is typically arranged in an opposite pattern along the woody stems. Its flowers are relatively small and inconspicuous, often appearing in axillary clusters. The plant produces small, drupaceous fruits that contribute to its reproductive morphology. These structural features are characteristic of its growth habit within tropical forest understories.
