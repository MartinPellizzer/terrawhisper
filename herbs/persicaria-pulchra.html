Persicaria pulchra

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Persicaria pulchrha has erect, slender stems that are often reddish or pinkish in color and can reach heights of up to 50 centimeters. The leaves are lanceolate to ovate in shape with smooth or slightly wavy margins and a distinct pointed tip. Small, delicate flowers are arranged in dense terminal clusters that typically exhibit white or pale pink hues. The plant features a specialized stipule called an ocrea that wraps around the stem at each leaf node. Its root system consists of rhizomes that allow the species to spread through various soil types.
