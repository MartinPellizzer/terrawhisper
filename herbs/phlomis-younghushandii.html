Phlomis younghushandii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phlomis younghushandii has a perennial woody base with decumbent to ascending stems that are densely covered in soft, white, woolly hairs. The leaves are oppositely arranged, broadly ovate to elliptical in shape, and possess crenate margins with a felt-like texture on both surfaces. Its inflorescence consists of dense, verticillaster-like whorls of flowers situated at the upper nodes of the stem. Each flower features a two-lipped corolla that is typically yellowish or pale in color, complemented by prominent stamens. This dense pubescence serves as a key morphological adaptation to its specific environmental conditions.
