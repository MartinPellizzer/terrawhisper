Clerodendrum chinense

What's the taxonomical classification of Clerodendrum chinense?

Clerodendrum chinense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is situated in the order Lamiales. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the family Lamiaceae, specifically within the genus Clerodendrum, with its unique biological identity defined by the species name chinense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clerodendrum chinense has opposite, ovate-lanceolate leaves that measure up to 15 centimeters in length and possess serrated margins with a distinctively rugose texture. The plant is a perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of two meters and features woody stems. Its inflorescence consists of dense, terminal panicles bearing small, white, tubular flowers with prominent protruding stamens. After pollination, the plant produces fleshy, globose berries that transition from green to a dark purple or black color when mature. The entire structure is characterized by a lush, bushy habit often found in tropical or subtropical environments.
