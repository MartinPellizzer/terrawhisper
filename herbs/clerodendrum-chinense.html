Clerodendrum chinense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clerodendrum chinense has opposite, ovate-lanceolate leaves that measure up to 15 centimeters in length and possess serrated margins with a distinctively rugose texture. The plant is a perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of two meters and features woody stems. Its inflorescence consists of dense, terminal panicles bearing small, white, tubular flowers with prominent protruding stamens. After pollination, the plant produces fleshy, globose berries that transition from green to a dark purple or black color when mature. The entire structure is characterized by a lush, bushy habit often found in tropical or subtropical environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of East and Southeast Asia, specifically spanning across China, Taiwan, India, and parts of Indochina. It flourishes in warm, humid climates where seasonal rainfall is consistent. Within these regions, it is frequently found growing in dense thickets, forest edges, and along riverbanks. The species has also been introduced to other tropical zones through cultivation for ornamental purposes. Its distribution is largely limited to areas that avoid frost and extreme arid conditions.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and potential gastrointestinal distress if ingested improperly. Contact with the leaves or stems may trigger contact dermatitis, resulting in redness or itching on sensitive skin. Some individuals might experience an immune response characterized by rashes or swelling upon exposure to its oils. Internal consumption carries risks of nausea or stomach discomfort due to the presence of unstudied bioactive compounds. Because toxicological data is limited, any direct contact or ingestion should be approached with extreme caution.
