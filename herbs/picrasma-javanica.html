What's the taxonomical classification of Picrasma javanica?

Picrasma javanica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the taxonomic order Sapindales. This plant is further categorized into the family Simaroubaceae and is situated within the genus Picrasma, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species javanica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Picrasma javanica has pinnately compound leaves that typically consist of 7 to 15 leaflets arranged oppositely along a central rachis. These leaflets are elliptical or lanceolate in shape and possess entire or slightly serrated margins. The plant is a deciduous tree that produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in terminal panicles. Its fruit is a fleshy, globose drupe that transitions to a yellowish or brownish color upon maturity. The bark is generally greyish and smooth, providing a structural contrast to the dense foliage.

What are the therapeutic actions of Picrasma javanica?

Picrasma javanica has therapeutic properties that include acting as a powerful bitter, alterative, aperient, emetic, cholagogue, and anthelmintic agent. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds javaniciins A, javaniciins C, javaniciins D, (16R)-methoxyjavanicin B, and (16S)-methoxyjavanicin B. These specific chemical constituents belong to a group of bioactive molecules often studied for their biological properties. The presence of both the (16R) and (16S) stereoisomers of methoxyjavanicin B indicates a complex chemical profile within the plant tissues. Researchers analyze these diverse javaniciins to understand their potential therapeutic applications in pharmacology. Each unique compound contributes to the overall medicinal significance of the plant species.

As discussed in a study, the CHCl3 soluble portion of the MeOH extract of Picrasma javanica wood contained two new quassinoids, (16R)-methoxyjavanicin B (1) and (16S)-methoxyjavanicin B (2), along with seven known compounds (3-9). Compound 8 exhibited moderate cytotoxicity against human lung (A549), breast (MCF7), cervical (HeLa), and normal fibroblast cell lines with IC50 values ranging from 48.6 to 65.9 μM. In antibacterial assays against the Gram-positive bacterium B. subtilis, compounds 1 and 2 demonstrated the highest activities with an MIC value of 1.6 μM each. Additionally, compound 5 and compound 3 showed MIC values of 3.1 μM and 6.3 μM, respectively.
