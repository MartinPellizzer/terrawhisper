Picrasma javanica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Picrasma javanica has pinnately compound leaves that typically consist of 7 to 15 leaflets arranged oppositely along a central rachis. These leaflets are elliptical or lanceolate in shape and possess entire or slightly serrated margins. The plant is a deciduous tree that produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in terminal panicles. Its fruit is a fleshy, globose drupe that transitions to a yellowish or brownish color upon maturity. The bark is generally greyish and smooth, providing a structural contrast to the dense foliage.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, specifically found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, Malaysia, and Indonesia. It typically inhabits lowland areas and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as evergreen forests and riverbanks. The species is often distributed along the edges of tropical rainforests where it can access adequate moisture. Its range is primarily concentrated within the humid zones of the Indochina peninsula and the Malay Archipelago. This specific ecological niche helps maintain its population across various island and mainland habitats.

Picrasma javanica contains medicinal compounds primarily concentrated in its bitter roots and bark. Practitioners also employ the bark in traditional formulations to address various ailments related to liver function. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Picrasma javanica contains high concentrations of bitter quassinoids, specifically picrasin, picrason, and quassin, which serve as its primary medicinal agents. These complex triterpenoid compounds are responsible for the plant's intense bitterness and its potent biological activities. They act as effective antimalarial and anthelmintic agents by targeting specific cellular processes in parasites. Additionally, these substances possess significant antimicrobial properties that can inhibit the growth of various bacteria and fungi. The presence of these specialized metabolites makes the plant a valuable subject in pharmacological research for developing new treatments.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Picrasma javanica targets various signaling pathways through its bitter cucurbitacins, specifically inhibiting protein kinase C and modulating inflammatory mediators like nitric oxide synthase. These bioactive compounds interact with cellular membranes to disrupt signaling cascades associated with tumor cell proliferation and migration. By interfering with these specific protein functions, the plant exhibits potent anti-inflammatory and anti-tumor properties in biological models. The interaction with various enzymes also contributes to its traditional use in treating digestive ailments and managing metabolic imbalances. This multi-target approach allows the plant to exert systemic physiological effects through diverse biochemical mechanisms.

What are the therapeutic actions of Picrasma javanica?

Picrasma javanica has therapeutic properties that include acting as a powerful bitter, alterative, aperient, emetic, cholagogue, and anthelmintic agent. Its intense bitter components stimulate the appetite and improve digestive secretions through the stimulation of gastric juices. As a cholagogue, the plant promotes the discharge of bile from the gallbladder to aid in the digestion of fats. The substance functions as an emetic to induce vomiting and an aperient to facilitate bowel movements when necessary. Furthermore, its anthelmintic actions allow it to effectively expel parasitic worms from the intestinal tract. Finally, its role as an alterative helps to gradually restore healthy metabolic functions within the body.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause severe gastrointestinal distress including nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and intense abdominal pain due to its highly irritating properties. Ingesting the plant often leads to significant gastric irritation as the bitter compounds interact with the digestive lining. These intense symptoms can quickly progress to a state of dangerous dehydration if the fluid loss is not managed. Some individuals may also experience dizziness as a systemic reaction to the plant's toxins. Careful avoidance is necessary to prevent these harmful physiological responses.
