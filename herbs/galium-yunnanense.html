What's the taxonomical classification of Galium yunnanense?

Galium yunnanense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Gentianaales. This plant is a member of the family Rubiaceae and is specifically categorized under the genus Galium, with its unique designation as the species yunnanense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Galium yunnanense has an erect, herbaceous stem that typically reaches heights of 20 to 60 centimeters and features whorled leaves arranged in multiples of four or six. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils in dense clusters, displaying a white or pale coloration. The plant produces tiny, dry fruits that often feature minute hooks or hairs to facilitate seed dispersal.
