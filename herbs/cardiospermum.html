What are the side effects of Cardiospermum when used improperly?

When used improperly, Cardiospermum increases the chances of experiencing side effects, such as allergic reactions, skin irritation, and gastrointestinal disturbances.

Additionally, improper use may lead to stomach upset, diarrhea, nausea, vomiting, and dizziness due to the plant's potential interactions with other medications or its ability to stimulate the digestive system excessively.

Furthermore, Cardiospermum's high concentration of cardiac glycosides can cause cardiovascular issues such as arrhythmias, palpitations, and decreased blood pressure if not used under medical supervision. In rare cases, improper use may also lead to more severe side effects, including kidney damage, liver toxicity, and even respiratory failure.

Therefore, it is crucial to follow proper preparation methods, dosage guidelines, and consult with a healthcare professional before using Cardiospermum for medicinal purposes.
