Helicia reticulata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Helicia reticulata has simple, alternate, elliptical leaves with distinctly reticulate venation and small, yellowish-white flowers that emerge from the axils. The leaf margins are entire and the texture is often coriaceous, providing a sturdy structure to the foliage. Its woody stems exhibit a branched growth habit characteristic of its shrub or small tree form. The reproductive structures consist of small capsules that contain the plant's seeds. These morphological traits collectively define the species within its specific ecological niche.
