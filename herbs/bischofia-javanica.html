Bischofia javanica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bischofiia javanica has a medium-sized evergreen tree structure characterized by an erect trunk, spreading branches, and simple, alternate leaves that are broadly elliptic to ovate in shape. The foliage typically features an acute apex and a cuneate base with entire margins. Its flowers are small and appear in axillary panicles, while the resulting fruits are drupes that change color as they mature. The bark is generally smooth or slightly fissured depending on the age of the specimen. These morphological traits collectively distinguish it within its tropical forest habitat.

This plant is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, specifically spanning across Indonesia, Malaysia, Thailand, and parts of the Philippines. It typically thrives in the humid environments of lowland rainforests and coastal areas. Within these habitats, the species is often found growing in well-drained soils along riverbanks or in dense forest understories. Its distribution is closely tied to the warm, wet climate characteristic of the Indo-Malayan botanical realm. Due to these specific environmental requirements, the plant remains confined to these tropical latitudes.
