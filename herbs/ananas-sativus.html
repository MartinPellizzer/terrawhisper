Ananas sativus

What's the taxonomical classification of Ananas sativus?

Ananas sativus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Poales and into the family Bromeliaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by the genus Ananas and the specific epithet sativus.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Ananas sativus contains bromelain, a complex mixture of proteolytic enzymes that breaks down proteins to reduce inflammation and aid digestion. These enzymes work by inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory mediators like prostaglandins in the body. Beyond bromelain, the plant provides significant amounts of vitamin C, manganese, and various polyphenolic compounds. These antioxidants help neutralize free radicals and support overall immune function. Additionally, certain organic acids found in the fruit may assist in supporting metabolic processes.

What are the therapeutic actions of Ananas sativus?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat inflammation, digestive issues, wound healing, and respiratory congestion. Its high concentration of the enzyme bromelain helps reduce swelling and pain associated with arthritis or surgical recovery. The fruit also aids in the breakdown of proteins to alleviate indigestion and bloating. Some traditional practices utilize its extracts to soothe sore throats and clear mucus from the lungs. Additionally, its antibacterial properties support the management of minor skin abrasions and infections.
