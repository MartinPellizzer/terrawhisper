Euphorbia prolifera

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Euphorbia prolifera has a succulent, creeping growth habit characterized by cylindrical, fleshy stems that are segmented and often reddish or pale green in color. These stems are typically leafless or possess vestigial, scale-like leaves that quickly drop off as the plant matures. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in axillary cyathia, which is the characteristic inflorescence structure of the Euphorbia genus. The plant lacks thorns or spines, relying instead on its thick, water-storing tissues to survive in arid environments. Its branching pattern allows it to spread horizontally across the substrate, forming dense mats or mats of vegetation.
