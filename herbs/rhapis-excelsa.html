Rhapis excelsa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhapis excelsa has a clustering growth habit characterized by slender, cane-like stems that are covered in persistent, dark brown leaf sheaths. The leaves are large, palmate, and deeply divided into several narrow, lanceolate segments that fan out from a central point. Each leaf segment is leathery in texture and features parallel venation throughout its length. These evergreen fronds typically grow in dense, overlapping fans atop the woody stems. Small, fleshy fruits may also develop, appearing as dark berries during certain stages of the plant's life cycle.
