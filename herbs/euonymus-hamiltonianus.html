What's the taxonomical classification of Euonymus hamiltonianus?

Euonymus hamiltonianus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical structure of its biological classification, it falls under the order Celastrales and the family Celastraceae. Ultimately, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement within the genus Euonymus, resulting in its scientific name, species hamiltonianus.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Euonymus hamiltonianus can cause severe skin irritation, nausea, vomiting, and potential neurological symptoms if ingested by humans or animals. The plant contains various alkaloids and glycosides that are toxic when processed by the body. Direct contact with the foliage or berries often leads to contact dermatitis, characterized by redness and itching. If consumed, the toxins may disrupt the central nervous system, leading to dizziness or convulsions. Because of these unpredictable reactions, any accidental exposure should be treated as a medical emergency.
