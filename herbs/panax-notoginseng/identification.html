Botanical Identification of Notoginseng (Panax notoginseng)

To identify Notoginseng (Panax notoginseng) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Notoginseng, or Panax notoginseng, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Apiales, family Araliaceae, genus Panax, and species Panax notoginseng. This classification places it within the group of flowering plants and highlights its botanical relationships within the Araliaceae family.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Notoginseng is Panax notoginseng, first described by the Chinese botanist Zhao Xiu in 1624. This name is widely accepted in modern botanical taxonomy. It is also known by several synonyms, including Panax pseudoginseng and Panax quinquefolius, though these are now considered outdated. The plant is commonly referred to by its Chinese name, San Qi, and has various regional and historical names that reflect its traditional use in medicine.

Morphological Characteristics

Notoginseng is a perennial herb with a short, thick stem. Its roots are the main medicinal part, characterized by a single main root and several smaller lateral roots. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a lanceolate shape with serrated edges. The inflorescence is a cyme, with small white flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Panax notoginseng can be distinguished by its distinctive red-brown bark with longitudinal fissures, opposite leaf arrangement, and the presence of a unique, short rhizome. It differs from similar species like Panax quinquefolius by its smaller, more elongated leaves and the absence of a prominent central taproot. The plant also has a distinct, slightly bitter taste and emits a faint aromatic odor.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Notoginseng is native to the mountainous regions of southern China, particularly in Yunnan and Guizhou provinces. It thrives in a subtropical climate with high humidity and mild temperatures. It prefers well-drained, loose soil rich in organic matter. It is typically found at altitudes between 1,500 and 2,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Notoginseng typically blooms in late spring to early summer, around May to June. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early autumn, approximately August to September. Harvesting is done in late autumn to early winter, usually from October to December, when the roots are mature and the plant has completed its growth cycle.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Panax notoginseng is closely related to other Panax species such as Panax quinquefolius and Panax japonicus. It shares characteristics with the genus Panax, including its growth habit and root structure. Within the Araliaceae family, it exhibits traits like compound leaves and milky sap, which help distinguish it from other genera.
