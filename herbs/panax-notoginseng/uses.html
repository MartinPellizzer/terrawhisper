Notoginseng (Panax notoginseng) Uses

Notoginseng is used to treat hemorrhage, inflammation, and chronic pain. It helps reduce swelling and promotes healing, making it effective for arthritis and bruising. It also supports cardiovascular health by addressing high blood pressure, atherosclerosis, and cardiac arrhythmia.

Notoginseng (Panax notoginseng) Monograph

1. Hemorrhage

Panax notoginseng is used to treat hemorrhage due to its hemostatic properties. It promotes blood clotting and reduces bleeding by enhancing platelet function. It is commonly used for nosebleeds, coughing up blood, and other internal bleeding conditions. Its action is gentle, making it suitable for chronic or recurrent cases.

2. Inflammation

Panax notoginseng is used to reduce inflammation due to its saponin and flavonoid content, which inhibit inflammatory pathways. It is commonly applied for conditions like arthritis and inflammatory bowel disease. Its anti-inflammatory effects support its role in traditional Chinese medicine for healing wounds and reducing swelling.

3. Arthritis

Notoginseng is used in traditional medicine to alleviate arthritis symptoms due to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. It helps reduce joint swelling and pain by improving circulation and inhibiting inflammatory responses. It is often combined with other herbs to enhance its therapeutic effects on degenerative joint conditions.

4. Bruising

Bruising involves gently crushing Notoginseng to release its active compounds, enhancing its medicinal properties. This process increases the bioavailability of saponins and other bioactive components, making it more effective for internal use. It is commonly used to prepare tinctures, decoctions, and powders for treating injuries and inflammation. The method preserves the herb's therapeutic potency while improving its absorption in the body.

5. Chronic pain

Panax notoginseng is used to alleviate chronic pain due to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. It helps reduce inflammation and improve blood circulation, making it effective for conditions like arthritis and musculoskeletal pain. Its active compounds, such as ginsenosides, contribute to its pain-relieving effects. It is often recommended in traditional herbal medicine for long-term pain management.

6. Cardiovascular disease

Notoginseng is used in traditional Chinese medicine to support cardiovascular health. It helps improve blood circulation, reduce blood pressure, and prevent clot formation. Its active compounds, such as notoginsenosides, have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that benefit the heart and blood vessels.

7. High blood pressure

Notoginseng is used in traditional Chinese medicine to help manage high blood pressure. It contains compounds that may promote vasodilation and reduce vascular resistance. Its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties support cardiovascular health. Clinical studies suggest it may aid in lowering blood pressure when used as part of a holistic treatment approach.

8. Atherosclerosis

Notoginseng has been studied for its potential to reduce atherosclerosis by improving blood lipid profiles and inhibiting oxidative stress. Its active compounds, such as notoginsenosides, may help prevent plaque formation in arteries. Clinical research suggests it supports cardiovascular health by enhancing blood circulation and reducing inflammation.

9. Cardiac arrhythmia

Notoginseng has been traditionally used to regulate heart rhythm and stabilize cardiac arrhythmia. Its bioactive compounds, such as ginsenosides, support cardiovascular health by improving blood circulation and reducing oxidative stress. Clinical studies suggest it may help manage irregular heartbeats, though it should be used under professional guidance.

10. Hypertension

Notoginseng is used in traditional Chinese medicine to help manage hypertension by promoting blood circulation and reducing stagnation. It contains saponins that may lower blood pressure by dilating blood vessels and improving cardiac function. Its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties also support overall cardiovascular health.
