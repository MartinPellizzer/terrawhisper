Notoginseng (Panax notoginseng) Compounds

The primary compounds in Notoginseng include saponins, which have anti-inflammatory properties, and flavonoids, known for their antioxidant effects. Glycosides and tannins contribute to its hemostatic and antiplatelet activities. Terpenoids and phenolic acids also play roles in its pharmacological actions, while alkaloids, coumarin, essential oils, and anthocyanins add to its complex therapeutic profile.

Notoginseng (Panax notoginseng) Monograph

1. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Notoginseng (Panax notoginseng). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in promoting blood circulation and reducing inflammation. Saponin's unique structure enhances its bioavailability and therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are key active compounds in Notoginseng (Panax notoginseng). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds are believed to support cardiovascular health and wound healing. Their presence makes Notoginseng a valued herb in traditional medicine.

3. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Notoginseng (Panax notoginseng). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and cardiovascular benefits. Glycosides in Notoginseng are often responsible for its traditional use in promoting wound healing and reducing blood pressure.

4. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Notoginseng (Panax notoginseng). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is believed to support wound healing and anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins in Notoginseng are also associated with its traditional use in promoting cardiovascular health.

5. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Notoginseng (Panax notoginseng). These compounds contribute to the herb's anti-inflammatory and cardiovascular benefits. They are responsible for many of the therapeutic effects attributed to Notoginseng in traditional medicine.

6. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Notoginseng (Panax notoginseng). It contributes to the herb's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound helps in reducing oxidative stress and promoting wound healing. Phenolic acid is widely studied for its role in supporting cardiovascular and immune health.

7. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a key active compound in Notoginseng (Panax notoginseng). It contributes to the herb's traditional use in promoting blood circulation and reducing swelling. Alkaloids in Notoginseng are known for their potential anti-inflammatory and cardiovascular benefits. These compounds are extracted and used in various herbal formulations.

8. Coumarin

Coumarin is a bioactive compound found in Notoginseng (Panax notoginseng). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and anticoagulant properties. Coumarin helps in reducing blood viscosity and preventing clot formation, supporting cardiovascular health. It is one of the key constituents responsible for the therapeutic effects of Notoginseng.

9. Essential oil

Essential oil is a volatile compound found in Notoginseng (Panax notoginseng). It contributes to the plant's aromatic profile and has been studied for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The essential oil is extracted through steam distillation and is used in traditional medicine to support circulatory and respiratory health.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Notoginseng (Panax notoginseng). It contributes to the herb's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports the plant's traditional use in promoting cardiovascular health and reducing inflammation.
