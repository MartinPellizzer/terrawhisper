Notoginseng (Panax notoginseng) Actions

Notoginseng exhibits anti-inflammatory properties, reducing swelling and pain. It acts as a tonic to strengthen the body and improve vitality. It also has sedative effects, helping to calm the mind and reduce anxiety. Additionally, it functions as an astringent, promoting wound healing and stopping bleeding.

Notoginseng (Panax notoginseng) Monograph

1. Anti-inflammatory

Notoginseng has an anti-inflammatory action, primarily due to its saponins and flavonoids, which inhibit pro-inflammatory cytokines and enzymes like COX-2. This reduces inflammation in conditions such as arthritis and injuries, supporting tissue repair and modulating immune responses.

2. Tonic

Notoginseng has a tonic action, enhancing overall vitality and supporting the body's energy systems. It is particularly effective in replenishing blood and promoting circulation, making it valuable in traditional medicine for recovery and maintaining health. Its tonic properties help strengthen the body and improve resilience to stress and illness.

3. Sedative

Notoginseng has a sedative action, helping to calm the nervous system and reduce anxiety. It promotes relaxation by influencing neurotransmitter activity and may aid in improving sleep quality. This effect is supported by traditional use and some modern research on its calming properties.

4. Astringent

Notoginseng has an astringent action, which helps reduce excessive bleeding and promotes wound healing. It achieves this by strengthening blood vessels and enhancing coagulation. This property makes it valuable in treating conditions like hemoptysis and hemorrhoids. Its astringent effect is attributed to its active compounds such as saponins and flavonoids.

5. Antispasmodic

Notoginseng has an antispasmodic action, helping to relieve muscle spasms and cramps by relaxing smooth muscle tissue. It is traditionally used to alleviate conditions such as convulsions, menstrual cramps, and gastrointestinal spasms. Its active compounds, including ginsenosides, contribute to its ability to modulate nerve signaling and reduce muscle overactivity.

6. Diuretic

Notoginseng has a diuretic action, promoting the elimination of excess fluid from the body. This effect is attributed to its ability to enhance kidney function and regulate fluid balance. It is often used in traditional medicine to alleviate edema and support urinary health. Its diuretic properties are considered gentle and non-irritating to the kidneys.

7. Carminative

Notoginseng has a carminative action, aiding in the expulsion of gas from the gastrointestinal tract. This effect helps alleviate bloating and discomfort caused by excess gas. Its properties support digestive health by promoting smooth muscle relaxation in the gut. It is commonly used in traditional medicine for digestive ailments.

8. Expectorant

Notoginseng has an expectorant action, aiding in the removal of phlegm from the respiratory tract. It stimulates the secretion of mucus and enhances cough reflexes, making it effective for treating coughs and respiratory congestion. This property is particularly beneficial in conditions like bronchitis and asthma.

9. Stimulant

Notoginseng has a stimulant action, enhancing physical and mental energy by increasing circulation and oxygen delivery to tissues. It supports endurance and reduces fatigue, making it useful in recovery and performance enhancement. This effect is attributed to its bioactive compounds that influence nervous and cardiovascular systems.

10. Bitter

Notoginseng has a bitter action, which helps to clear heat and detoxify the body. This property is particularly effective in treating conditions like inflammation and excessive internal heat. The bitter taste also stimulates digestive function and enhances the body's ability to process toxins. It supports overall metabolic balance and strengthens the immune system.
