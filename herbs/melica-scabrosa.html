What's the taxonomical classification of Melica scabrosa?

Melica scabrosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is situated in the order Poales. It further falls under the family Poaceae, specifically within the genus Melica, and is identified by the species designation scabrosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Melica scabrosa has a perennial growth habit characterized by tufted, caespitose clumps of slender, upright leaves and branched panicle inflorescences. The leaf blades are typically narrow and linear, often featuring a scabrid or rough texture along the margins. Its flowering spikes consist of loose, open panicles with small, delicate spikelets distributed along many spreading branches. Each spikelet contains multiple florets that are often slightly unequal in size, contributing to a light and airy appearance. The reproductive structures are supported by fine pedicels that allow the inflorescence to sway easily in the wind.
