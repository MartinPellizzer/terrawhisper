Corydalis speciosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Corydalis speciosa?

Corydalis speciosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Ranunculales. This plant is a member of the family Papaveraceae, specifically falling under the genus Corydalis, with its unique biological identity defined by the species name speciosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Corydalis speciosa has a basal rosette of deeply lobed, pinnately dissected leaves that are typically dark green in color. Each individual flower features a prominent nectar spur extending backward from the base of the corolla. The reproductive structures are nestled within the floral tube, supporting the development of elongated seed capsules. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Corydalis speciosa contains high concentrations of isoquinoline alkaloids, specifically protopine, corydaline, and californidine, which serve as its primary bioactive components. Many of these alkaloids interact with the central nervous system to modulate pain perception and induce relaxation.
