Hodgsonia macrocarpa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hodgsonia macrocarpa has woody, climbing vines characterized by large, leathery, elliptical leaves and massive, woody, globose fruits that can reach significant diameters. The foliage consists of dark green, glossy surfaces with prominent venation and long petioles that support the dense canopy. During flowering, the plant produces clusters of relatively small, inconspicuous blossoms that eventually transition into their namesake large-fruited structures. These heavy fruits are encased in a thick, woody pericarp that protects the seeds contained within.
