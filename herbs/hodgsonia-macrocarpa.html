Hodgsonia macrocarpa

What's the taxonomical classification of Hodgsonia macrocarpa?

Hodgsonia macrocarpa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic path continues through the order Cucurbitales and the family Cucurbitaceae, which places it among the gourds and melons. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Hodgsonia, and its unique species designation, macrocarpa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hodgsonia macrocarpa has woody, climbing vines characterized by large, leathery, elliptical leaves and massive, woody, globose fruits that can reach significant diameters.

The foliage consists of dark green, glossy surfaces with prominent venation and long petioles that support the dense canopy. During flowering, the plant produces clusters of relatively small, inconspicuous blossoms that eventually transition into their namesake large-fruited structures. These heavy fruits are encased in a thick, woody pericarp that protects the seeds contained within.

The overall architecture of the plant is adapted for a scandent lifestyle within tropical forest environments.
