Walsura pinnata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Walsura pinnata has pinnately compound leaves with a terminal leaflet and several pairs of lateral leaflets that are oblong or elliptic in shape. The foliage typically features entire margins and an acute apex, often displaying a leathery texture. Its woody stems and branches are characterized by a smooth to slightly fissured bark depending on the age of the tree. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into fleshy, drupaceous fruits. These fruits are often greenish or yellowish and contain a single seed essential for its reproductive cycle.

The species is often found at moderate elevations where seasonal rainfall supports its growth requirements. What side-effects this plant can have?

Walsura pinnata can cause adverse physiological reactions such as skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, or allergic dermatitis if handled or ingested without proper preparation. While many parts of the plant are used in traditional medicine, the specific concentration of bioactive compounds may trigger unexpected sensitivities in certain individuals. Contact with the sap or raw plant matter might result in localized redness or itching on the skin. Furthermore, consuming unrefined extracts could lead to nausea or stomach cramping due to the presence of complex alkaloids. It is essential to consult a medical professional before using any part of this species for therapeutic purposes.
