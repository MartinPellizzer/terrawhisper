Walsura pinnata

What's the taxonomical classification of Walsura pinnata?

Walsura pinnata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Sapindales. Furthermore, this plant is a member of the family Meliaceae, falling into the genus Walsura, and is specifically identified by its species name, pinnata.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Walsura pinnata contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, terpenoids, flavonoids, and saponins that provide its therapeutic properties. These chemical constituents are primarily concentrated in the bark, leaves, and roots of the tree. The presence of these compounds allows the plant to be used in traditional medicine for treating inflammatory conditions and skin ailments. Researchers have identified specific phytochemical profiles that suggest potential antimicrobial and antioxidant activities. These medicinal components contribute to the plant's traditional role in various ethnomedical practices across its natural habitat.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including skin infections, inflammatory conditions, stomach disorders, and respiratory issues. Its medicinal properties are frequently utilized to combat fungal infections and soothe painful skin irritations. Extracts from the plant are also applied to manage digestive distress and gastrointestinal inflammation. Furthermore, it serves as a traditional remedy for relieving coughs and other bronchial discomforts. This versatile botanical resource plays a significant role in diverse herbal healing practices.
