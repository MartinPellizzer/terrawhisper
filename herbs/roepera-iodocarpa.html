What is Roepera iodocarpa?

Roepera iodocarpa is a shrub that belongs to the family Zygophyllaceae, native to Western Australia.

The plant has a woody stem, 0.3-1.2 meters in height, with a spread of 1-2 meters, and a glabrous (hairless) branchlet with a brownish-red color. The leaves are simple, alternate, and linear, measuring 1-6 centimeters in length and 1-2 millimeters in width, with a glabrous adaxial surface and a pubescent abaxial surface. The inflorescence is a corymb, bearing small, yellowish flowers with 5 petals, 6 stamens, and a globose ovary.

Roepera iodocarpa produces small, globose fruits, 5-7 millimeters in diameter, containing 1-2 seeds.
