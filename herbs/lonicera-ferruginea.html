Lonicera ferruginea

What's the taxonomical classification of Lonicera ferruginea?

Lonicera ferruginea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Dipsacales. The plant is situated within the family Caprifoliaceae, which characterizes its botanical structure. Specifically, it is categorized under the genus Lonicera, with its unique identity defined by the species ferruginea.

    What are the herbal preparations of Lonicera ferruginea?

    Lonicera ferruginea is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a tincture to extract its bioactive compounds for medicinal use. The dried stems and leaves are commonly simmered in water to create a bitter decoction used in traditional folk remedies. Alternatively, the plant parts can be steeped in hot water to produce a milder infusion suitable for soothing digestive issues. For a more concentrated extract, the botanical material is soaked in alcohol to create a tincture that allows for long-term storage and precise dosing. These various liquid preparations allow practitioners to utilize the plant's specific chemical profile for different therapeutic purposes.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Lonicera ferruginea can cause gastrointestinal distress including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea if the berries or other parts of the plant are ingested by humans or animals. These berries contain glycosides that may lead to significant stomach upset or more severe digestive reactions. While the plant is generally not considered highly toxic, consuming it in large quantities can still pose a health risk. Careful avoidance of the fruit is recommended to prevent these adverse physical responses. There is limited documentation regarding skin irritation, but direct contact should be approached with caution.
