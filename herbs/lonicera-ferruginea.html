Lonicera ferruginea

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lonicera ferruginea has opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape and possess finely serrated margins. The foliage typically exhibits a dark green color on the upper surface while the undersides may show a paler hue. Its woody stems are characterized by a distinctive rusty-colored pubescence that gives the species its specific epithet. During the flowering season, the plant produces tubular, fragrant white or pale pink corollas that emerge from the leaf axils. These flowers eventually develop into small, fleshy berries that transition from green to a deep red or black color upon maturity.

    What are the herbal preparations of Lonicera ferruginea?

    Lonicera ferruginea is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a tincture to extract its bioactive compounds for medicinal use.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Lonicera ferruginea can cause gastrointestinal distress including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea if the berries or other parts of the plant are ingested by humans or animals. These berries contain glycosides that may lead to significant stomach upset or more severe digestive reactions. While the plant is generally not considered highly toxic, consuming it in large quantities can still pose a health risk. Careful avoidance of the fruit is recommended to prevent these adverse physical responses. There is limited documentation regarding skin irritation, but direct contact should be approached with caution.
