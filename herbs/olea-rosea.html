Olea rosea

What's the taxonomical classification of Olea rosea?

Olea rosea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Lamiales and the family Oleaceae, placing it within the genus Olea. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its specific species name, rosea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Olea rosea has an evergreen, shrub-like growth habit characterized by lanceolate leaves that feature a dark green upper surface and a silvery, pubescent underside. The leaf margins are entire, and the texture is leathery to prevent excessive moisture loss. Small, axillary flowers emerge from the leaf axils, typically presenting in pale clusters. The fruit develops as small, drupaceous berries that transition in color as they reach maturity. Woody stems provide a sturdy structural framework for the dense foliage.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the Canary Islands, where it is specifically found on the islands of Tenerife and La Palma. It typically inhabits the subtropical zones of these volcanic islands at varying elevations. These populations are often concentrated within laurel forests or similar humid montane environments. The distribution is highly localized due to the unique microclimates required for its survival. Consequently, the species occupies a very narrow ecological niche within its island habitats.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires well-draining, sandy or loamy soil and full sunlight to thrive in Mediterranean-style climates.

It thrives when planted in locations that receive at least six to eight hours of direct sun daily. Regular watering is essential during the initial establishment phase, but the mature specimen is highly drought-tolerant once its root system is deep. Providing adequate space between plants prevents fungal issues by ensuring sufficient air circulation around the foliage.
