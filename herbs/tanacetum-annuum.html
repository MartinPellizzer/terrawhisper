Tansy

Tanacetum annuum

Tansy (Tanacetum annuum) is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, known for its strong scent and yellow-orange flowers, and it has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, particularly in treating digestive issues and parasitic infections.

Botanical Identification

Tansy is scientifically known as Tanacetum annuum, belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is also called golden buttons or tansy in various regions. This annual herb is native to Europe and parts of Asia. It has finely divided leaves and yellow button-like flowers. Synonyms in herbal texts include Tanacetum corymbosum and T. vulgare.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Magnoliopsida
  • Order: Asterales
  • Family: Asteraceae
  • Genus: Tanacetum
  • Species: Tanacetum annuum

Active Compounds

Tansy contains essential oils, terpenoids, and flavonoids as its main bioactive compounds. These compounds work together to exert anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. The synergistic interaction between these components enhances its therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

Tansy Uses.

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory issues such as coughs and colds.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and as an antispasmodic.

Essential oil: Used for respiratory infections and as a natural insect repellent.

Safety Profile

Tansy is contraindicated during pregnancy and lactation due to its potential uterine stimulant effects. It should also be avoided by individuals with hypersensitivity to plants in the Asteraceae family. Long-term use may lead to liver toxicity, necessitating caution and medical supervision.
