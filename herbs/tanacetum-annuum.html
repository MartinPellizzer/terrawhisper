Tansy (Tanacetum annuum)

Tansy (Tanacetum annuum) is a member of the Asteraceae family, native to Europe, and Asia. Traditionally, its flowers, resin, and leaves have been used for infusions, decoctions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its bitter, anti-inflammatory, and carminative actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and traditional chinese medicine.

Perennial herbaceous plant.

Leaves
Broad leaves with upper surface green and lower surface pale green, bearing prominent stomatal bands.
Flowers
Small yellow flowers arranged in dense flat-topped clusters, actinomorphic, with 4-5 ovate petals and 5-6 linear sepals, bearing white central disk florets.
Stems
Cylindrical, herbaceous, hairy, with opposite branching, and containing a central, solid pith.
