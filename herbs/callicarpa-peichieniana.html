Callicarpa peichieniana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Callicarpa peichienianan has opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated margins and a characteristic pubescence on the undersides. The plant typically grows as a deciduous shrub reaching heights of several meters in dense thickets. Its small, tubular flowers are arranged in dense axillary cymes that emerge during the flowering season. Following pollination, the species produces distinctive clusters of small, globose drupes that transition from green to a vibrant purple or reddish hue. These fleshy fruits are a defining feature of the shrub's reproductive morphology.
