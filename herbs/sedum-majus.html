Sedum majus

What's the taxonomical classification of Sedum majus?

Sedum majus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Saxifragales, the plant is placed into the family Crassulaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by the genus Sedum and the species majus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sedum majus has fleshy, succulent leaves that are arranged in a dense rosette pattern atop a short, compact stem. These leaves are typically elliptical or spoon-shaped with smooth margins and a thick cuticle to prevent water loss. The plant exhibits a low-growing, spreading habit that allows it to form tight groundcover mats. Small, star-shaped flowers emerge from the center of the rosettes during its blooming period. The overall structure is designed for extreme drought tolerance through its specialized water-storing tissues.
