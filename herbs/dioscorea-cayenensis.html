Dioscorea cayenensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Dioscorea cayenensis?

Dioscorea cayenensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Dioscoreales. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the family Dioscoreaceae and the genus Dioscorea, ultimately reaching its specific identification as the species cayenensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dioscorea cayenensis has a perennial climbing habit characterized by tuberous rhizomes, cordate leaves, and small, yellowish-green flowers. The stems are slender and twining, allowing the plant to ascend through surrounding vegetation via aerial creepers. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped leaves that are typically thin and smooth, arranged alternately along the vine. The inflorescence features inconspicuous, unisexual flowers that often appear in clusters. These reproductive structures are relatively small compared to the overall size of the climbing foliage.

What are the therapeutic actions of Dioscorea cayenensis?

Dioscorea cayennensis has therapeutic actions as a hormonal regulator, emmenagogue, abortifacient, and uterine stimulant. The plant contains steroidal saponins that can influence the endocrine system to balance various biological processes. When used as an emmenagogue, it helps to stimulate menstrual flow and regulate cycles. Because it functions as a uterine stimulant, it can cause contractions in the smooth muscles of the womb. These specific physiological effects mean the plant also possesses abortifacient properties that may terminate a pregnancy.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds asperin and gracillin which serve specific therapeutic functions. The presence of asperin provides significant analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties for treating various pains. Additionally, the compound gracillin contributes to the plant's complex chemical profile by offering unique biological activities. Researchers study these specific substances to understand their potential applications in modern pharmacology. These active ingredients work together to define the overall medicinal value of the species.

As discussed in a study, Dioscorea cayenensis contains steroidal saponins which are reported to be the major physiologically active constituents.
