What is Agathosma Crenulata (Agathosma Crenulata)?

Agathosma crenulata, also known as Agathosma crenulata, is a medicinal plant native to South Africa, traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and digestive properties.

Botanical Identity and Classification

Agathosma crenulata, commonly known as Agathosma crenulata, is a perennial shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa, belonging to the Asteraceae family and classified under the genus Agathosma.

Common Names and Synonyms

Common Names and Synonyms: Agathosma crenulata is also known as Cape spearmint, African spearmint, and mountain mint, with additional synonyms including Hottentot button and Agathosma crenulata subsp. capensis.

Plant Description and Morphology

Agathosma crenulata is a shrub characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Agathosma crenulata is native to the coastal regions of South Africa, specifically found in the Western and Eastern Cape provinces, where it thrives in sandy soils and coastal climates.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The leaves and stems of Agathosma crenulata are primarily used medicinally, often harvested during the cooler months to maximize their active compounds.
