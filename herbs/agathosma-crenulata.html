Scientific and Botanical Profile

Agathosma Crenulata, with botanical name Agathosma crenulata, is a flowering plant native to South Africa, specifically found in the Western and Eastern Cape provinces.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, commonly known as the sunflower or daisy family. This species is commonly referred to by various names such as Cape Geranium, Cape Sage, Cape Chamomile, and South African Chamomile, reflecting its widespread use and recognition in local communities. Morphologically, it is a low-growing, herbaceous plant with hairy stems, narrow lance-shaped leaves, and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

It is also known by names like Stinkweed, Wild Sage, and Wild Chamomile, due to its strong aromatic scent and traditional medicinal uses.

Safety, Side Effects and Contraindications

Agathosma crenulata can be used as a medicinal plant for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, but it is important to consider its safety profile.

While generally considered safe when used in moderate amounts, it may cause gastrointestinal side effects such as nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea in some individuals. There is limited research on its interactions with other drugs or herbs, but caution is advised when combining it with medications that affect the liver or central nervous system. Special populations, including pregnant and breastfeeding women, should avoid agathosma crenulata due to insufficient evidence on its safety during these periods. Individuals with chronic illnesses, particularly those affecting the liver or kidneys, should consult a healthcare provider before use.

To ensure safe use, always follow recommended dosages, consult a qualified healthcare professional, and discontinue use if adverse effects occur.

Growing, Harvesting and Storage

Agathosma crenulata grows best in well-drained, sandy or loamy soils with a pH between 6.

0 and 7. 5, in full sun to partial shade, and requires moderate water during the growing season. It thrives in warm, dry climates and benefits from regular weeding and mulching to retain moisture and suppress weeds. For optimal growth, it should be planted in early spring, and regular pruning of dead or diseased branches helps promote healthy development. Harvesting is best done in late summer to early autumn when the leaves are most potent, using clean shears to cut the stems just above the base to avoid damaging the plant.

After harvesting, the plant material should be dried in a cool, shaded area with good air circulation, then stored in airtight containers in a cool, dark place to preserve its medicinal potency and extend its shelf life.
