Paspalum notatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Paspalum notatum?

Paspalum notatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Poales and the family Poaceae. Ultimately, this plant is identified by its specific genus, Paspalum, and its unique species designation, notaum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Paspalum notatum has a perennial growth habit characterized by tufted, stoloniferous clumps with spreading rhizomes and narrow, bright green leaf blades that reach up to 30 centimeters in length.

The leaves feature a distinct midrib and a somewhat blunt or rounded tip. Its inflorescence consists of several spikelets arranged in a compact, cylindrical panicle that typically measures between 5 and 15 centimeters in height. Each spikelet is small and contains a single fertile floret protected by lemma and palea structures.

The overall structure allows the plant to form dense, creeping mats across its habitat.

      What are the herbal preparations of Paspalum notatum?

      Paspalum notatum is prepared primarily as a decoction or an infusion made from its dried leaves and stems for medicinal use.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Paspalum notatum can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and respiratory discomfort in sensitive individuals. While it is primarily considered a non-toxic forage grass, direct contact with the foliage may trigger itchy rashes or redness on the skin. Some people may experience sneezing or coughing if they inhale pollen or fine particles from the plant during maintenance. These reactions are typically localized and occur when the plant is handled frequently without protection. Most healthy individuals will not experience significant issues unless they possess a specific sensitivity to its components.
