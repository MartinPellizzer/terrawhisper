What's the taxonomical classification of Paspalum notatum?

Paspalum notatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Poales and the family Poaceae. Ultimately, this plant is identified by its specific genus, Paspalum, and its unique species designation, notaum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Paspalum notatum has a perennial growth habit characterized by tufted, stoloniferous clumps with spreading rhizomes and narrow, bright green leaf blades that reach up to 30 centimeters in length. Its inflorescence consists of several spikelets arranged in a compact, cylindrical panicle that typically measures between 5 and 15 centimeters in height. Each spikelet is small and contains a single fertile floret protected by lemma and palea structures. The overall structure allows the plant to form dense, creeping mats across its habitat.
