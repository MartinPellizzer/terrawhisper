What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Paspalum notatum has a perennial growth habit characterized by tufted, stoloniferous clumps with spreading rhizomes and narrow, bright green leaf blades that reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The leaves feature a distinct midrib and a somewhat blunt or rounded tip. Its inflorescence consists of several spikelets arranged in a compact, cylindrical panicle that typically measures between 5 and 15 centimeters in height. Each spikelet is small and contains a single fertile floret protected by lemma and palea structures. The overall structure allows the plant to form dense, creeping mats across its habitat.
