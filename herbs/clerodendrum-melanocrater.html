Clerodendrum melanocrater

What's the taxonomical classification of Clerodendrum melanocrater?

Clerodendrum melanocrater belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Lamiales. Further taxonomic refinement identifies it as a member of the family Lamiaceae, specifically within the genus Clerodendrum, with its unique designation as the species melanocrater.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Clerodendrum melanocrater contains various bioactive phytochemicals including flavonoids, alkaloids, tannins, and saponins that contribute to its therapeutic properties. These chemical constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's documented antioxidant and antimicrobial activities. Researchers have identified specific phenolic compounds within the leaves and stems that help mitigate oxidative stress in biological systems. Additionally, the presence of terpenoids may provide anti-inflammatory effects used in traditional medicinal practices. These diverse compounds work synergistically to offer a wide range of potential pharmacological benefits.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, gastrointestinal issues, respiratory ailments, and inflammatory conditions. Local practitioners often utilize the leaves to manage wounds and skin eruptions through topical applications. When prepared as an infusion, it can help alleviate stomach aches and digestive discomfort. Some traditional medicinal practices also employ the plant to soothe coughs and other bronchial symptoms. These diverse uses highlight its importance in folk medicine for managing various physical ailments.
