Clerodendrum melanocrater

What's the taxonomical classification of Clerodendrum melanocrater?

Clerodendrum melanocrater belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Lamiales. Further taxonomic refinement identifies it as a member of the family Lamiaceae, specifically within the genus Clerodendrum, with its unique designation as the species melanocrater.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clerodendrum melanocrater has opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with entire margins and a distinctively textured, somewhat rugose surface.

The plant typically grows as a woody shrub or small tree with slender, branching stems. Its inflorescences are terminal clusters composed of small, tubular white to pale pink flowers. Following pollination, the plant produces prominent, spherical drupes that turn a deep black or dark purple color when mature.

These dark fruits are often surrounded by persistent calyx lobes that provide a striking visual contrast.
