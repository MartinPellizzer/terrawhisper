Erigeron sumatrensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Erigeron sumatrensis has perennial herbaceous stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and feature leaves that are primarily basal or distributed sparsely along the stem. The leaves are generally lanceolate to linear in shape and possess serrated margins. Its inflorescence consists of solitary heads held atop long, slender peduncles. Each flower head contains numerous narrow white or pale lavender ray florets surrounding a central cluster of yellow disc florets. The involucral bracts are arranged in multiple overlapping rows to protect the developing flower head.

      What are the herbal preparations of Erigeron sumatrensis?

      Erigeron sumatrensis is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical tincture to extract its bioactive compounds. Herbalists often simmer the dried flowering tops in water to create a strong decoction for internal use. Alternatively, steeping the plant in hot water creates a gentler infusion suitable for daily tea consumption.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Erigeron sumatrensis can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Individuals with sensitivities to the Asteraceae family may experience redness, itching, or swelling upon touching the plant. If the plant is consumed, it might lead to nausea or stomach discomfort in sensitive subjects. There is also a potential for respiratory irritation if the fine hairs or pollen are inhaled in large quantities. Monitoring for these symptoms is essential for anyone handling or being near the specimen.
