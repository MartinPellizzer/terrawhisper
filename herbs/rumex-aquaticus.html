Rumex aquaticus

What's the taxonomical classification of Rumex aquaticus?

Rumex aquaticus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant is a member of the order Caryophyllales and falls under the family Polygonaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic hierarchy is completed within the genus Rumex, identifying it as the species Rumex aquaticus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rumex aquaticus has perennial, rhizomatous stems that typically reach heights of 20 to 60 centimeters and support lanceolate to ovate leaves with entire or slightly wavy margins. The basal leaves are generally larger and more elongated than the smaller, alternate leaves found along the upper stem. Its inflorescence consists of dense, terminal panicles featuring small, greenish flowers enclosed by winged valves. These valves are characteristic of the species, often displaying distinct longitudinal veins and reddish or yellowish hues during maturation. The plant's root system is composed of thick, creeping rhizomes that allow it to colonize moist or semi-aquatic habitats effectively.
