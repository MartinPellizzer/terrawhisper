Diospyros oleifera

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Diospyros oleifera has an evergreen tree structure characterized by an erect trunk, elliptical leaves with entire margins, and fleshy drupe fruits. The foliage consists of dark green, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in an opposite or sub-opposite pattern along the branches. Its flowers are small and creamy white, emerging from axillary positions during the flowering season. The fruit develops into a large, ovoid berry containing a single hard seed that is essential for its reproductive cycle. These morphological features collectively distinguish the species within the Ebenaceae family.

Populations are frequently found within the Amazon Basin and various coastal tropical zones where the soil is rich and moist. Furthermore, certain constituents appear to influence the activity of protein kinases involved in cell proliferation and apoptosis pathways. This diverse molecular interaction profile underpins the plant's broad pharmacological potential in treating metabolic and inflammatory disorders.
