Popular 100 Medicinal Herbs

Indian Frankincense, scientifically known as boswellia serrata, is a medicinal herb native to the Indian subcontinent, often referred to as the "dragon's blood tree" for its resinous sap.

This adaptogen, also used as a spice and incense, has been valued for centuries in Ayurvedic medicine for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The primary benefits of Indian Frankincense include reducing joint pain, inflammation, and supporting respiratory and digestive health due to its active compounds like boswellic acids.

Traditionally, it has been used in religious ceremonies, as a fragrance, and to treat ailments such as arthritis and skin conditions.

In modern wellness, it is popular in supplements and topical applications for its potential to enhance joint mobility and reduce chronic inflammation, with its unique, balsamic aroma and historical significance in ancient Indian healing practices.
