Tilia chinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Tilia chinensis?

Tilia chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is classified in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Malvales and is a member of the family Malvaceae. Specifically, it is identified by the genus Tilia, with its unique designation being the species chinensis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Tilia chinensis contains flavonoids such as quercetin and kaempferol, volatile oils including farnesol, and various polyphenolic compounds that provide its therapeutic benefits. These bioactive constituents work together to exert significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects within the body. The presence of mucilage in the plant material helps soothe irritated mucous membranes, making it useful for respiratory relief. Additionally, certain volatile components contribute to its mild sedative and antispasmodic properties. These chemical profiles collectively support its traditional use in calming the nervous system and reducing fever.

What are the therapeutic actions of Tilia chinensis?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat anxiety, insomnia, fever, headaches, and various digestive upsets. The flowers are often prepared as a calming tea to soothe nervous tension and promote restful sleep. Its antispasmodic properties help alleviate muscle cramps and digestive spasms. Additionally, the plant possesses mild diaphoretic effects that assist in reducing body temperature during a fever. These therapeutic qualities make it a common remedy for managing stress-related physical symptoms.
