Rhododendron hypoglaucum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhododendron hypoglaucum has elliptic to oblong leaves that are dark green and glabrous on the upper surface while displaying a distinct glaucous or waxy underside. The plant typically grows as a small shrub with woody stems that support these leathery foliage structures. Its inflorescence consists of small, bell-shaped flowers that vary in color from white to pale pink. The texture of the leaf undersides is a defining morphological characteristic used to distinguish it from similar species.
