Dracocephalum grandiflorum

What's the taxonomical classification of Dracocephalum grandiflorum?

Dracocephalum grandiflorum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Lamiales and is a representative of the family Lamiaceae. Specifically, this plant is situated within the genus Dracocephalum, with its unique biological identity defined by its species name, grandiflorum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dracocephalum grandiflorum has square stems, lanceolate to ovate leaves with serrated margins, and dense terminal flower spikes composed of large, tubular, two-lipped lavender or blue corollas. The foliage is typically arranged in opposite pairs along the upright, branching stems. Each flower head is crowded with individual blossoms that exhibit prominent stamens protruding from the throat. The leaves possess a slightly hairy texture and often feature a rugose surface pattern. This morphological structure allows the plant to effectively attract various insect pollinators within its natural habitat.
