Rubus parvifolius

What's the taxonomical classification of Rubus parvifolius?

Rubus parvifolius belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It falls under the order Rosales and is a member of the family Rosaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its genus, Rubus, and its specific species name, parvifolius.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rubus parvifolius has large, palmately lobed leaves that typically feature three to five distinct leaflets with serrated edges and a slightly hairy surface. The stems, or canes, are woody and often covered in small prickles or fine hairs. Each leaf is arranged alternately along the stem and is supported by a long petiole. During the flowering stage, the plant produces clusters of small, white five-petaled flowers. These blooms eventually transition into aggregate fruits known as blackberries, which turn from red to deep black when fully ripe.

As discussed in a study, Rubus parvifolius L. is traditionally used as an herbal medicine for rheumatism. The extract of Rubus parvifolius L. inhibits the bone-resorption activity of osteoclasts. Its constituent, sanguiin H-6, markedly inhibits RANKL-induced osteoclast differentiation and bone resorption. Sanguiin H-6 also decreases the protein levels of NFATc1, cathepsin K, and c-Src. This constituent inhibited the nuclear translocation of NFATc1, c-Fos, and NF-κB in vitro, as well as TNF-α-mediated osteoclastogenesis in vivo. These findings suggest that Rubus parvifolius L. has anti-bone resorption activity.
