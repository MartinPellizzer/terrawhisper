Thalictrum delavayi

What's the taxonomical classification of Thalictrum delavayi?

Thalictrum delavayi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Ranunculales. It is a member of the family Ranunculaceae, which is further categorized into the genus Thalictrum, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species delavayi.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the alkaloid pseudoprotopine along with a specific group of thalidelavin compounds consisting of thalidelavin A, thalidelavin B, thalidelavin C, and thalidelavin D. These secondary metabolites are characteristic of the Thalictrum genus and contribute to the chemical profile of the species. Researchers study these specific molecules to understand their potential biological activities and pharmacological properties. The presence of these diverse alkaloids makes the plant a subject of interest in phytochemical investigations. Each unique compound offers a different structural variation within the plant's chemical defense system.

According to a study published by "Planta medica", nine known isoquinoline-type alkaloids and two new protoberberine alkaloids, 2,3,9,10-dimethylenedioxy-8-oxoprotoberberine (1) and 2,3,9,10-dimethylenedioxy-1,8-dihydroxyprotoberberine (2), were isolated from the roots of Thalictrum delavayi. Among these isolated compounds, pseudoprotopine demonstrated competitive inhibition activity through a DA receptor binding assay (D1) in vitro. The measured competitive inhibitions were 87.5% at 10(-4) M and 15.6% at 10(-6) M. These results detail the specific medicinal compounds found within the plant.

Researchers study its effects to determine how it might inhibit the progression of malignant cells. By targeting these biological pathways, the plant offers potential therapeutic benefits for complex medical conditions.

According to a study published by "Fitoterapia", the whole plants of Thalictrum delavayi were investigated for their biological activities through the isolation of 5 new dimeric benzylisoquinoline alkaloids, thalidelavines A-E (1-5), and 6 known congeners (6-11). The isolated alkaloids were evaluated for their cytotoxic and immunosuppressive effects to identify potential candidates for antitumor and immunosuppressive agents. Specifically, compounds 9 and 10 displayed significant cytotoxicities against T98G cell lines with an IC50 value of 2.1 μM, which was compared against the positive CP-11 with an IC50 of 3.0 μM. Furthermore, compounds 5-7 demonstrated remarkable immunosuppressive effects.
