Salix pentandra

What's the taxonomical classification of Salix pentandra?

Salix pentandra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Magnoliidae and is situated in the order Malpighiales. This plant is a member of the family Salicaceae, specifically categorized within the genus Salix, with the unique species designation pentandra.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Salix pentandra has linear to narrowly lanceolate leaves that are typically 3 to 8 centimeters long, characterized by entire or finely serrated margins and an acute apex. The leaf surfaces often exhibit a subtle pubescence, particularly on the undersides, which can vary depending on the age of the foliage. Its stems are woody and slender, often showing a grayish or brownish bark texture as the plant matures. The reproductive structures consist of catkins that emerge from the woody branches during the spring flowering season. These catkins feature small, inconspicuous flowers that are essential for the species' pollination and dispersal.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant Salix pentandra requires consistently moist to wet soil and full sun exposure to achieve optimal growth in temperate climates.

It is best cultivated in riparian zones or areas where the water table remains high to mimic its natural wetland habitat.     What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause gastrointestinal distress, allergic skin reactions, and potential salicylate toxicity if consumed in excessive amounts. Because it belongs to the willow family, it contains salicin which acts similarly to aspirin. Individuals with a known sensitivity to salicylates may experience hives, swelling, or respiratory issues upon contact or ingestion. Overconsumption can lead to stomach irritation, nausea, or more serious complications like gastrointestinal bleeding. It is also important to avoid this plant if you are taking blood-thinning medications or have an aspirin allergy.
