Chinese prickly ash

Zanthoxylum bungeanum

Chinese Prickly Ash (Zanthoxylum bungeanum) is a member of the Rutaceae family, traditionally used in Chinese medicine for its various applications. The plant parts, including fruits, leaves, and roots, have been utilized for treatment of digestive issues, relief of pain and inflammation, and external use for skin conditions. It is commonly found in traditional practices across regions such as China and has a long history of use in herbal formulations.

Botanical Identification

Zanthoxylum bungeanum, commonly known as Chinese prickly ash, is a species in the Rutaceae family. It is a deciduous shrub native to China, with spiny branches, pinnate leaves, and yellowish flowers. It is also referred to as Sichuan prickly ash in some regions and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries.

Active Compounds

Chinese Prickly Ash contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, enhancing the herb's therapeutic value in traditional herbal medicine.

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory conditions.

Infusion: Used for colds and flu symptoms.

Extract: Used for pain relief and inflammation.

Safety Profile

Chinese Prickly Ash is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal irritation and should be avoided by individuals with ulcers or sensitive stomachs. Long-term use is not recommended due to potential toxicity. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
