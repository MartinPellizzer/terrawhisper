Zanthoxylum bungeanum

What's the taxonomical classification of Zanthoxylum bungeanum?

Zanthoxylum bungeanum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Sapindales. Furthermore, this plant is a member of the family Rutaceae, which defines its broader botanical group. Within this family, it is specifically categorized under the genus Zanthoxylum, with its unique identity defined by the species name bungeanum.

What are the pharmacological activities of Zanthoxylum bungeanum?

This plant has anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, antibacterial, antimicrobial, and neuroprotective pharmacological activities. Its chemical constituents work to neutralize free radicals, providing significant antioxidant benefits to the body. The plant also demonstrates potent antibacterial and antimicrobial properties that help inhibit the growth of various harmful pathogens. Additionally, its anti-inflammatory actions help reduce swelling and irritation in biological tissues. Research also indicates that the plant offers neuroprotective effects that may help defend against neurological damage.

According to a study published by "Evidence-based complementary and alternative medicine : eCAM", Zantho xylum bungeanum seed oil (ZBSO) has been investigated for its antitumor and anti-inflammatory effects. ZBSO inhibits the LPS-induced expression of inflammatory mediators and proinflammatory cytokines in BEAS-2B cells. Molecular docking results indicated that active components in ZBSO could successfully dock with toll-like receptor 4 (TLR4), myeloid differentiation factor 88 (MyD88), and p65. ZBSO repressed protein expression associated with the TLR4/MyD88/nuclear factor-kB (NF-kB) signaling pathway. By inhibiting the TLR4/MyD88/NF-kB signaling pathway, ZBSO reduced the inflammatory response and oxidative stress induced by LPS to suppress COPD.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds hydroxy-alpha-sanshool, hydroxy-gamma-sanshool, various sanshools, alkylamides, and nitidine chloride. These specific chemical constituents are responsible for the diverse pharmacological activities associated with the species. The presence of alkylamides and different sanshools contributes to its significant sensory and numbing effects. Furthermore, the alkaloid nitidine chloride provides potent antimicrobial and antitumor properties. Together, these bioactive molecules justify the plant's widespread use in traditional medicine.

According to a study published by "Journal of chromatography. B, Analytical technologies in the biomedical and life sciences", the fruit of Zanthoxylum bungeanum Maxim contains alkaloids such as hydroxy-alpha-sanshool and hydroxy-gamma-sanshool. The analytical method used for these compounds achieved spiked recovery accuracy ranging from 98.89% to 102.06%. Additionally, the correlation coefficients for the quantitative analysis were measured between 0.99986 and 0.99999. This study utilized a polymer-based chromatographic monolithic column prepared via in-situ radical polymerization to separate these substances.
