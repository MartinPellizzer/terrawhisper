Kadsura interior

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Kadsura interior has lanceolate leaves with serrated margins, a central woody stem reaching two meters in height, and small violet tubular flowers. The foliage is characterized by a deep emerald pigment and a waxy cuticle that helps prevent moisture loss. Its root system consists of a dense network of fibrous structures designed to anchor the plant in loose soil. The flowering stalks emerge from the axillary nodes and bear clusters of five-petaled blossoms. Each mature specimen develops a slightly roughened bark texture as the primary stem thickens over time.
