Rubus ichangensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Rubus ichangensis?

Rubus ichangenensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Rosales and is a member of the family Rosaceae. Finally, it is situated within the genus Rubus, with its specific designation being the species ichangenis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rubus ichangenensis has woody, arching stems that are densely covered in small, sharp prickles and bear compound leaves composed of five to seven serrated leaflets. The leaves exhibit a distinct texture where the upper surface is typically dark green and the underside is noticeably paler or slightly pubescent. Small, white to pale pink flowers emerge from the terminal ends of the branches during the blooming season. These blossoms consist of five petals surrounding a central cluster of numerous stamens and carpels. The plant eventually produces small, aggregate fleshy fruits that transition in color as they ripen.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the high-altitude regions of the Ichang mountain range within central China. It thrives primarily in the subalpine shrublands and moist forest margins found at elevations between 2,000 and 3,500 meters. The specific microclimates of these mountainous areas provide the necessary humidity and cool temperatures required for its survival. Due to this restricted range, the species is highly localized and does not occur naturally in other parts of the world.
