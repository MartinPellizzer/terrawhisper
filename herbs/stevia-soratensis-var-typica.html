Stevia soratensis var. typica

Stevia soratensis var. typica, commonly known as Stevia rebaudiana, is a plant species native to the regions of Paraguay and Brazil, belonging to the Asteraceae family.

This plant is renowned for its potential health benefits, including reducing blood sugar levels, lowering blood pressure, and aiding in weight management, primarily due to its low-calorie sweetening properties.

The therapeutic actions of Stevia soratensis var. typica are attributed to its ability to act as an antidiabetic, antihypertensive, and anti-inflammatory agent, as well as an antimicrobial and antioxidant.

The bioactive constituents of this plant include steviol glycosides, specifically stevioside and rebaudioside A, which are responsible for its sweet taste and medicinal properties.
