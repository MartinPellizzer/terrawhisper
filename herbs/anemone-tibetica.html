What's the taxonomical classification of Anemone tibetica?

Anemone tibetica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized as Equisetopsida, falling under the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Ranunculales and the family Ranunculaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Anemone, and its unique species designation, tibetica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Anemone tibetica has perennial herbaceous stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and support solitary, actinomorphic flowers with five to seven pale pink or white tepals. The leaves are primarily basal and divided into several narrow, linear lobes that emerge from a rhizomatous rootstock. These basal leaves are often arranged in a rosette pattern to maximize light absorption in alpine environments. The floral center is characterized by a cluster of numerous yellow stamens surrounding a central group of carpels. This morphological structure allows the plant to thrive in the high-altitude, rocky terrains of the Himalayas.
