Centifolia Rose (Rosa centifolia)

Centifolia Rose (Rosa centifolia) is a member of the Rosaceae family, native to Middle East, Central Asia, and Southwest Asia. Traditionally, its flowers, leaves, and bark have been used for infusions, decoctions, and oil infusions.

This herb is particularly valued for its astringent, anti-inflammatory, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and traditional chinese medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
1.5 to 2.5 meters tall.
Leaves
Opposite, ovate leaves with glossy upper surface and pale green lower surface, featuring prominent stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Inflorescence consists of solitary flowers with actinomorphic symmetry, five white to pink petals with a central yellow stamen cluster, and five sepals that are green and persistent.
Stems
Cylindrical, woody, simple, erect, branched, with thornless, glabrous, angular, ridged, and terete surfaces.
