Aristolochia fordiana

What's the taxonomical classification of Aristolochia fordiana?

Aristolochia fordiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is placed in the order Piperales and is a member of the family Aristolochiaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Aristolochia and is specifically designated as the species fordidiana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aristolochia fordiana has climbing stems that reach several meters in length, heart-shaped leaves with smooth margins, and tubular, brownish-purple flowers that hang downward. The foliage is typically lush and green, providing a dense canopy for the vine as it ascends through forest vegetation. Each flower features a specialized perianth designed to trap small insects for pollination purposes. The arrangement of the leaves on the stem is alternate, contributing to the plant's sprawling growth habit. These morphological traits collectively help the species thrive in its specific tropical habitat.

What are the pharmacological activities of Aristolochia fordiana?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological potential through its potent cytotoxicity against various cancer cell lines and its ability to exert nitric oxide production inhibitory activity. These biological effects are primarily attributed to the presence of specific bioactive compounds like aristolochic acids and alkaloids found within its tissues. In laboratory studies, the extract effectively triggers programmed cell death in malignant cells, making it a subject of interest for oncological research. Additionally, the reduction of nitric oxide levels suggests that the plant may possess strong anti-inflammatory properties by modulating cellular signaling pathways. Further investigation into these mechanisms is necessary to understand the full therapeutic scope of its chemical constituents.

As discussed in a study, six new benzofuran neolignans (1-6) and twelve known analogues (7-18) were isolated from the EtOAc-soluble fraction of the aerial part of Aristolochia fordiana. These isolates were evaluated for inhibitory effects on nitric oxide production in lipopolysaccaride-activated RAW264.7 macrophages and for cytotoxic activities on three human cancer cell lines. Compound 2 demonstrated significant nitric oxide production inhibitory activity with an IC50 value of 10.00 µM. Regarding cytotoxic activity, compound 16 exhibited an IC50 value of 11.9 µM against MG-63 cells. Additionally, compound 18 showed an IC50 value of 9.15 µM against HepG2 cell lines.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause severe nephrotoxicity, urothelium-damaging uropathy, acute renal failure, and significant carcinogenicity in humans. Consumption often leads to gastrointestinal distress including nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain. The damage to the urinary tract can progress to debilitating uropathy-related pain. These toxic effects are primarily driven by aristolochic acids found within the species. Long-term exposure is particularly dangerous due to the risk of developing upper urinary tract cancers.
