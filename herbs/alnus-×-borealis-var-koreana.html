Alnus × borealis var. koreana

Alnus × borealis var. koreana, also known as Korean green alder, is a hybrid plant species that is a cross between Alnus glutinosa (black alder) and Alnus incana (gray alder).

This plant has been used in traditional medicine for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, which contribute to its potential health benefits such as reducing oxidative stress and alleviating symptoms of various diseases.

The leaves, bark, and cones of Korean green alder are used in herbal preparations, including infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, to support overall health and well-being.

Traditional uses of Korean green alder include treating fever, diarrhea, and respiratory issues, as well as promoting wound healing and skin health.
