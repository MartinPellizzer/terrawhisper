Catnip (Nepeta Cordubensis)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Nepeta cordubensis.

Catnip, scientifically known as Nepeta cordubensis, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, prized for its medicinal and aromatic properties. This adaptogen and mild sedative has been used for centuries in traditional medicine for its calming effects and digestive benefits. It is particularly noted for its ability to soothe anxiety, reduce stress, and promote relaxation, making it a popular choice in herbal remedies. In ancient cultures, catnip was used to treat ailments such as headaches, insomnia, and digestive issues, while modern wellness practices incorporate it into teas, essential oils, and supplements for its calming and mood-enhancing effects. Its unique feature is the presence of nepetalactone, a compound responsible for its stimulating effect on cats and its soothing properties in humans.
