Cinnamomum burmanni

What's the taxonomical classification of Cinnamomum burmanni?

Cinnamomum burmanni belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Laurales. It is further categorized under the family Lauraceae, falling into the genus Cinnamomum, and is specifically identified by its species name, burmanni.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Cinnamomum burmanni contains high concentrations of cinnamaldehyde, eugenol, and various phenylpropanoids that provide its therapeutic properties. These bioactive compounds serve as potent antimicrobial and antifungal agents in traditional medicine. The essential oil extracted from the bark is frequently utilized to alleviate gastrointestinal distress and reduce inflammation. Additionally, the presence of cinnamic acid contributes to the plant's antioxidant capabilities. These chemical constituents make the species a significant resource for both pharmacological research and holistic treatments.

What are the therapeutic actions of Cinnamomum burmanni?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat digestive disorders, respiratory infections, rheumatism, and skin ailments. The bark contains essential oils that possess antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties beneficial for managing stomach discomfort and indigestion. When applied topically or ingested in controlled amounts, it can help alleviate pain associated with joint inflammation. Its aromatic compounds are also utilized to combat coughs and congestion in traditional medicinal practices. These therapeutic applications stem from the plant's ability to stimulate circulation and reduce systemic inflammation.
