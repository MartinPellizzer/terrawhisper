Aglaia odorata

What's the taxonomical classification of Aglaia odorata?

Aglaiya odorata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae, following the biological hierarchy through the order Sapindales. The plant is a member of the family Meliaceae, which falls under the genus Aglaiya, and is specifically identified by its species name, odorata.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Aglaiia odorata contains bioactive limonoids, such as aglaiols and aglaiaones, along with various triterpenoids and fatty acids that exhibit significant pharmacological properties. These specific chemical constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's documented anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities. Research indicates that the extracted limonoids can also demonstrate cytotoxic effects against certain cancer cell lines in laboratory settings. Furthermore, the presence of essential oils contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating skin ailments and digestive issues. These diverse compounds collectively form the basis for its potential application in developing new therapeutic agents.

What are the therapeutic actions of Aglaia odorata?

Aglaia odorata has therapeutic properties that primarily function as an antipyretic, antimicrobial, and anti-inflammatory agent used to treat fevers and skin infections. The plant contains essential oils and bioactive compounds that help reduce body temperature and suppress bacterial growth. In traditional medicine, its extracts are often applied topically to soothe skin irritations and promote healing of minor wounds. Additionally, the botanical components may help alleviate respiratory congestion when used in specific preparations. These multifaceted actions make it a versatile tool in various herbal remedy practices.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat digestive issues, respiratory ailments, and skin inflammation. Its medicinal properties are often utilized to soothe stomach discomfort and promote regular bowel movements. Practitioners also apply it to manage symptoms of the common cold and coughs due to its soothing effects. Additionally, topical applications can help alleviate minor skin irritations and swellings. These traditional uses rely on the various bioactive compounds found within its leaves and flowers.

What are the herbal preparations of Aglaia odorata?

Aglaia odorata is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical oil derived from its leaves and flowers. The dried leaves are often simmered in water to create a medicinal tea used for internal consumption. For external applications, the plant material can be macerated in carrier oils to produce soothing ointments for the skin. Some traditional practices also involve using the crushed fresh plant as a poultice applied directly to affected areas. These various methods allow for the extraction of its aromatic and therapeutic properties in different delivery formats.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Aglaia odorata can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and localized swelling if the plant's sap or leaves come into direct contact with sensitive skin. Some individuals may experience redness or itching upon touching the foliage. If the plant is accidentally ingested, it could lead to gastrointestinal distress such as nausea or stomach discomfort. There is also a possibility of respiratory irritation if the pollen or scent is inhaled in large quantities by sensitive people. Because scientific studies on human toxicity are limited, caution should be exercised when handling this species.
