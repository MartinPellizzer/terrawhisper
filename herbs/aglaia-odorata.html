Aglaia odorata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aglaia odorata has an evergreen, woody structure characterized by opposite, elliptical leaves that measure approximately 5 to 15 centimeters in length and possess a smooth, leathery texture. The foliage typically exhibits a deep green color on the upper surface with a slightly paler underside. Small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers emerge in axillary clusters from the stems. These blossoms are relatively inconspicuous but produce a potent scent that distinguishes the species. The plant often develops a dense, spreading habit as it matures into a small tree or large shrub.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, specifically spanning across countries like China, Thailand, Malaysia, Indonesia, and the Philippines. It thrives primarily in moist, lowland environments such as coastal areas and riverbanks where humidity is high. Within its natural range, the species is commonly found in secondary forests and shaded undergrowth. Some populations have been introduced to other tropical zones due to its popularity in ornamental gardening. This widespread distribution is supported by its ability to adapt to various warm, humid climates.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Aglaiia odorata contains bioactive limonoids, such as aglaiols and aglaiaones, along with various triterpenoids and fatty acids that exhibit significant pharmacological properties. These specific chemical constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's documented anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities. Research indicates that the extracted limonoids can also demonstrate cytotoxic effects against certain cancer cell lines in laboratory settings. Furthermore, the presence of essential oils contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating skin ailments and digestive issues. These diverse compounds collectively form the basis for its potential application in developing new therapeutic agents.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Aglaiia odorata targets various enzymes and inflammatory mediators including cyclooxygenase-2, inducible nitric oxide synthase, and pro-inflammatory cytokines to exert its medicinal effects. Its bioactive constituents, such as specific limonoids and triterpenoids, act by modulating the signaling pathways responsible for oxidative stress and cellular inflammation. These molecules can inhibit the activation of nuclear factor kappa B, which plays a critical role in the body's immune response. Furthermore, certain compounds within the plant interact with protein kinases to regulate cell proliferation and survival. This complex interaction with multiple protein targets allows the plant to demonstrate significant anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

What are the therapeutic actions of Aglaia odorata?

Aglaia odorata has diverse therapeutic actions including antispasmodic, carminative, diaphoric, digestive stimulant, emmenagogue, and nervine properties. The plant acts as a digestive stimulant and carminative to relieve bloating and gastrointestinal discomfort. It can be used as an emmenagogue to promote menstrual flow and as a diaphoric to induce sweating. Its nervine qualities help soothe the nervous system while its antispasmodic effects reduce muscle contractions. These combined botanical properties make it a versatile tool in traditional herbal medicine.

What are the herbal preparations of Aglaia odorata?

Aglaia odorata is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical oil derived from its leaves and flowers. The dried leaves are often simmered in water to create a medicinal tea used for internal consumption. For external applications, the plant material can be macerated in carrier oils to produce soothing ointments for the skin. Some traditional practices also involve using the crushed fresh plant as a poultice applied directly to affected areas. These various methods allow for the extraction of its aromatic and therapeutic properties in different delivery formats.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Aglaia odorata can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and localized swelling if the plant's sap or leaves come into direct contact with sensitive skin. Some individuals may experience redness or itching upon touching the foliage. If the plant is accidentally ingested, it could lead to gastrointestinal distress such as nausea or stomach discomfort. There is also a possibility of respiratory irritation if the pollen or scent is inhaled in large quantities by sensitive people. Because scientific studies on human toxicity are limited, caution should be exercised when handling this species.
