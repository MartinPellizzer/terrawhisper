Excoecaria acerifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Excoecaria acerifolia has simple, alternate, maple-like leaves characterized by three to five shallow lobes and serrated margins. The foliage typically displays a deep green color with prominent venation that extends from the base toward the lobe tips. Its stems are woody and can become quite robust as the plant matures into a shrub or small tree. The inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the axils of the leaves. These plants often feature a dense, branching structure that provides a compact growth habit.
