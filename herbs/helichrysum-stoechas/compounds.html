Stoechas Helichrysum (Helichrysum stoechas) Compounds

The primary compounds of Stoechas Helichrysum include essential oil, flavonoids, terpenoids, and coumarin. These compounds contribute to its aromatic properties and potential therapeutic effects. Phenolic acids, tannins, and glycosides also play roles in its medicinal applications. Saponins, alkaloids, and anthocyanins further enhance its biological activity.

Stoechas Helichrysum (Helichrysum stoechas) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Helichrysum stoechas contains bioactive compounds like sesquiterpene lactones and flavonoids. These compounds contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, making it valuable in herbal medicine for treating skin conditions and respiratory infections.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Stoechas Helichrysum (Helichrysum stoechas) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help reduce oxidative stress and support the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and skin conditions. They are found in the essential oils and aerial parts of the plant.

3. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a key class of active compounds in Stoechas Helichrysum. They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds are responsible for the plant's distinct aroma and its traditional use in treating skin conditions and respiratory ailments.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Stoechas Helichrysum. It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. Coumarin also acts as a mild sedative and is used in traditional medicine for digestive issues. Its presence enhances the therapeutic profile of this herbal remedy.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Stoechas Helichrysum. It contributes to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. 6. Tannin

7. Glycoside

8. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Stoechas Helichrysum. It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponins may support skin healing and immune function. Their presence enhances the therapeutic potential of this traditional herbal remedy.

9. Alkaloid

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Stoechas Helichrysum. It contributes to the plant's vibrant flower colors and has antioxidant properties. This compound may support anti-inflammatory and immune-boosting effects, making it valuable in herbal medicine.
