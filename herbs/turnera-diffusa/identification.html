Botanical Identification of Turnera Diffusa (Turnera diffusa)

To identify Turnera Diffusa (Turnera diffusa) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Turnera diffusa belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Sapindales, family Rutaceae, genus Turnera, and species Turnera diffusa. These classifications reflect its place in the plant kingdom, from broad categories like plants to its specific species identification.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of the plant is Turnera diffusa, first described by the botanist Jean-Baptiste Lamarck in 1785. It is also known by several synonyms, including Turnera ulmifolia and Turnera subulata, reflecting historical taxonomic revisions. Common names such as "Brazilian buttercup" and "yellow wood sorrel" are widely used, while regional and historical names vary across different cultures and periods.

Morphological Characteristics

Turnera diffusa is a herbaceous perennial shrub with a sprawling growth habit. It has a fibrous root system that anchors it to the soil. The stems are woody at the base and become more flexible towards the top. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a leathery texture with a serrated margin. The inflorescence consists of clusters of small yellow flowers, which are followed by capsule fruits containing tiny seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Turnera diffusa can be distinguished by its opposite, elliptic leaves with smooth margins and yellowish-green veins, and its fragrant, yellow flowers with five petals. It differs from similar species like Turnera ulmifolia by having shorter, more upright stems and smaller, more rounded leaves. The presence of a distinctive, pungent odor when crushed also aids in identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Turnera diffusa is native to Central and South America, particularly Brazil, Peru, and Colombia. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity. It prefers well-drained, fertile soils and is commonly found at low to mid-elevation areas, typically between 300 and 1,500 meters above sea level.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Turnera diffusa blooms from April to June, producing yellow flowers. Seed formation occurs from July to September. For identification purposes, the plant is typically harvested between April and September, depending on regional climate and growth stage.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Turnera diffusa belongs to the genus Turnera within the family Turneraceae. Its closest species include Turnera subulata and Turnera ulmifolia. These species share similar morphological traits, such as opposite leaves and yellow flowers, which help in distinguishing them within the genus.
