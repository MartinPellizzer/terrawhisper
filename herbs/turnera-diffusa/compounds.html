Turnera Diffusa (Turnera diffusa) Compounds

Turnera diffusa contains several active compounds, including flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties. Coumarin contributes to its anti-inflammatory effects. Phenolic acids and tannins provide astringent and antimicrobial benefits. Terpenoids and glycosides may support immune function, while saponins and essential oils contribute to its overall therapeutic profile. Alkaloids and anthocyanins also play roles in its medicinal properties.

Turnera Diffusa (Turnera diffusa) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Turnera diffusa are bioactive compounds known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. They contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating skin conditions and respiratory ailments. These compounds are often extracted from the leaves and have shown potential in supporting immune function and reducing oxidative stress.

2. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Turnera diffusa, known for its antispasmodic and sedative properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating nervous system disorders and muscle spasms. Coumarin's presence enhances the overall therapeutic profile of Turnera diffusa in herbal medicine.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Turnera diffusa, known for its antioxidant properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating inflammation and digestive issues. This compound helps support the plant's therapeutic effects in herbal medicine.

4. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Turnera diffusa, known for its astringent properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating wounds and inflammation. Tannins help reduce swelling and promote tissue healing. This compound is also valued for its antimicrobial effects in herbal medicine.

5. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Turnera diffusa, a medicinal plant used in herbal medicine. These compounds contribute to the plant's therapeutic effects, including anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. They are often isolated and used in formulations for their potent biological activities.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Turnera diffusa. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive issues. This compound is known for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Glycoside plays a significant role in the pharmacological profile of Turnera diffusa.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Turnera diffusa, known for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating pain and inflammatory conditions. Saponin's bioactive nature supports its role in herbal medicine applications.

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of Turnera diffusa contains bioactive compounds with potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It is traditionally used in herbal medicine for its soothing effects on skin irritations and respiratory conditions. The oil is extracted through steam distillation from the plant's leaves and flowers. Its therapeutic use aligns with the traditional applications of Turnera diffusa in natural health practices.

9. Alkaloid

Turnera diffusa contains the alkaloid tetrahydrocannabinol (THC). THC is known for its psychoactive properties and has been studied for its potential therapeutic effects, including pain relief and anti-inflammatory actions. It is one of the primary compounds responsible for the plant's medicinal and recreational use.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanins are pigments found in Turnera diffusa, contributing to its vibrant colors. These compounds are known for their antioxidant properties, which may support anti-inflammatory and protective effects in the body. They are part of the plant's bioactive components studied for potential therapeutic applications in herbal medicine.
