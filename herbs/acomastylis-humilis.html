What is Acomastylis humilis?

Acomastylis humilis is a plant species that belongs to the family Rosaceae.

It is a low-growing, shrubby perennial with alternate, simple, and stipulate leaves that are 1-3 cm long, 2-7 mm wide, and have a narrowly elliptic to lanceolate shape. The leaves are glabrous, or have a sparse, white pubescence, and are arranged oppositely on the stem. The plant produces solitary, 3-5 cm diameter flowers with 5 white petals, 5 stamens, and a 3-celled ovary, which is followed by a 2.5-4 cm long, red to dark red, glabrous, and berry-like fruit.

Acomastylis humilis is endemic to the southern parts of South Africa, where it grows in moist, rocky areas and woodlands at elevations up to 2,200 meters.
