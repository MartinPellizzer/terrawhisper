These compounds, such as bufotenin and related alkaloids, exhibit strong inotropic properties, enhancing cardiac contractility and making them valuable in the treatment of heart failure and arrhythmias. Additionally, they have demonstrated potential in neuropharmacological applications, including their role in modulating neurotransmitter activity and influencing mood.

Beyond cardiac glycosides, Turnera diffusa is also rich in flavonoids, which contribute to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting overall cellular health. Together, these constituents make Turnera diffusa a versatile herbal remedy with a wide range of therapeutic applications.

Medicinal Parts

Turnera diffusa root is one of the most commonly used medicinal parts of this plant, known for its rich concentration of alkaloids, particularly berberine, which has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

The root has been traditionally used in South American medicine to treat digestive issues, such as ulcers and gastritis, due to its ability to protect the stomach lining. It is also valued for its potential in managing diabetes, as berberine has been shown to improve insulin sensitivity and lower blood glucose levels. In addition to its internal uses, the root may be applied topically to treat skin infections and wounds because of its antimicrobial effects.

Overall, the root of Turnera diffusa remains a vital component in both traditional and modern herbal medicine, offering a range of therapeutic benefits that continue to be explored in scientific research.
