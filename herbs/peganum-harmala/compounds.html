Harmal (Peganum harmala) Compounds

Harmal contains several primary compounds, including alkaloids, which are known for their psychoactive properties. Terpenoids and flavonoids contribute to its antioxidant effects. Tannins and phenolic acids provide astringent qualities, while coumarin and glycosides may have anti-inflammatory actions. Saponins, essential oils, and anthocyanins also play roles in its medicinal profile.

Harmal (Peganum harmala) Monograph

1. Alkaloid

Harmal, also known as Peganum harmala, contains alkaloids as its active compounds. These alkaloids, such as harmine and harmaline, have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their psychoactive and medicinal properties. They are believed to influence neurotransmitter activity and have been studied for potential therapeutic applications.

2. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Harmal (Peganum harmala), contributing to its medicinal properties. These compounds have shown potential in traditional uses, including antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. They play a key role in the plant's therapeutic applications within herbal medicine.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Harmal (Peganum harmala). They contribute to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. These compounds may support various therapeutic applications in traditional herbal medicine.

4. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Harmal (Peganum harmala), contributing to its astringent properties. It is found in the plant's seeds and has been traditionally used for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins may also support digestive health and aid in wound healing.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in Harmal (Peganum harmala). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. This compound is particularly valued in herbal medicine for its potential therapeutic applications.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a compound found in Harmal (Peganum harmala). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for its antispasmodic and sedative properties. Coumarin may also support its role in treating nervous system disorders and digestive issues.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Harmal (Peganum harmala). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for its potential antimicrobial and antidepressant properties. Glycosides in Harmal are often found in the seeds and may influence the plant's pharmacological effects.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Harmal (Peganum harmala). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine, particularly for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponins may support digestive health and immune function, though further research is needed to confirm their efficacy.

9. Essential oil

The essential oil of Harmal (Peganum harmala) contains bioactive compounds like harmine and harmaline. These alkaloids exhibit anticholinergic and serotonergic effects, contributing to its traditional use in treating respiratory and nervous system conditions. The oil is extracted through steam distillation from the plant's seeds and stems.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a pigment found in Harmal, also known as Peganum harmala. It contributes to the plant's color and may have antioxidant properties. While not the primary active compound, anthocyanin supports the plant's medicinal profile by enhancing its overall therapeutic potential.
