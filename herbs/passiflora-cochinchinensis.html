Passiflora cochinchinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Passiflora cochinchinensis?

Passiflora cochinchinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Malpighiales. It is a prominent representative of the family Passifloraceae, specifically falling under the genus Passiflora, with its unique identity defined by the species epithet cochinchinenis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Passiflora cochinchiensis has a climbing, perennial vine morphology characterized by thin, twining stems and deeply three-lobed leaves with long petioles. The foliage is typically dark green and possesses a somewhat leathery texture that supports its sprawling growth habit. Its most distinctive feature is the large, complex flower, which displays intricate corona filaments and a prominent central structure of anthers and stigmas. The plant also produces distinctive egg-shaped fruits that develop from the ovaries after successful pollination. These botanical structures allow the vine to effectively navigate through dense vegetation to reach sunlight.
