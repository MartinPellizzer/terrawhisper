What's the taxonomical classification of Ligularia songarica?

Ligularia songarica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae, which is a large and diverse group of flowering plants. More specifically, it is categorized under the genus Ligularia, with its specific identity being defined by the species name songarica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ligularia songarica has large, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply serrated along the margins and grow from a basal rosette. The foliage features prominent veins and a textured surface that can appear slightly hairy or rugose. Its stems are erect and sturdy, typically reaching heights that support multiple flowering heads. The inflorescence consists of bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central disc, forming a composite flower head characteristic of the Asteraceae family. These yellow blooms are clustered at the top of the stems to attract pollinators during the summer months.
