Linaria burjatica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Linaria burjatica has an erect, perennial herbaceous habit characterized by slender, branched stems and small, lanceolate leaves with entire margins. The foliage typically displays a fine texture and is arranged oppositely along the length of the stem. Its inflorescence consists of zygomorphic flowers that feature a prominent, elongated spur at the base of the corolla. The floral structure is primarily composed of bright colors, often displaying yellow or pale hues with distinct nectar guides. These specialized morphological traits facilitate specific pollination mechanisms within its native habitat.
