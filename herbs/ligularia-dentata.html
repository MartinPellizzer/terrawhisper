Ligularia dentata

What's the taxonomical classification of Ligularia dentata?

Ligularia dentata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical structure, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Asterales, it is a member of the family Asteraceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Ligularia, and its unique species name, dentata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ligularia dentata has large, coarsely serrated, cordate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in width and grow on long, sturdy petioles. These prominent foliage structures are typically bright green with a leathery texture and distinctively pointed teeth along the margins. The plant produces upright, unbranched flowering stalks that emerge from the center of the basal rosette. Each stalk is topped with a cluster of bright yellow, daisy-like flower heads composed of numerous ray and disk florets. The overall growth habit is clump-forming, creating a dense mound of expansive greenery.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the temperate regions of East Asia, specifically spanning across central and eastern China, Japan, and Korea. It typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and mountain stream banks. Within these habitats, the species is most frequently found in areas with rich, organic soil and consistent moisture levels. While its natural range is confined to these Asian territories, it has been widely introduced to many other temperate parts of the world for ornamental use. Consequently, it is now commonly cultivated in gardens throughout Europe and North America.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a variety of bioactive sesquiterpene lactones, including the specific medicinal compound furanoligularenone. These chemical constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's observed biological activities, such as anti-inflammatory and cytotoxic properties. Beyond furanoligularenone, the species also possesses other secondary metabolites that contribute to its complex chemical profile. Researchers study these compounds to understand their potential applications in pharmacological developments. The presence of these unique molecules makes Ligularia dentata a significant subject in natural product chemistry.

As discussed in a study, chemical constituents from the roots of Ligularia dentata in Henan were isolated and purified using silica gel, Sephadex LH-20 column chromatography, and solvent crystallization. Four eremophilane-type sesquiterpenoids were obtained and identified as 3-oxo-8alpha-hydroxy-10alphaH-eremophilan-1,7-dien-8, 12beta-olide(1), 3-oxo-eremophil-1, 7-dien-8, 12beta-olide (2), 3-oxo-8alpha-ethoxy-10alphaH-eremophil-1,7-dien-8,12-olide(3), and furanoligularenone(4). These compounds 1-4 are isolated from this plant for the first time. HPLC analysis showed that the contents of compounds 1, 2, and 4 in the roots are high. However, compound 3 is hardly detected in the fresh EtOH extract and might be derived from other compounds during the extraction process.
