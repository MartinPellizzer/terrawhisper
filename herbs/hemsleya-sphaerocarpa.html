What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hemsleya sphaerocarpa has large, bipinnate or tripinnate leaves characterized by deeply lobed, serrated leaflets and a distinctively woody, scrambling growth habit. Its stems are stout and climbing, allowing the plant to navigate through forest undergrowth. The most defining feature is its unique spherical fruit capsules that develop from small, inconspicuous flowers. These woody structures enclose the seeds and represent a specialized adaptation for its specific ecological niche.
