Apocynum venetum

What's the taxonomical classification of Apocynum venetum?

Apocynum venetum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it falls under the order Gentianaales and is a member of the family Apocynaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Apocynum, and its specific species designation, venetum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Apocynum venetum has woody, climbing stems that produce opposite, elliptical leaves with prominent longitudinal venation. The foliage is characterized by a smooth texture and a deep green color that provides a dense canopy. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves in clustered arrangements. These blossoms typically feature a pale coloration and a delicate structure adapted for specific pollinators. The plant also develops capsule-like fruits that contain seeds equipped with tufts of hair for wind dispersal.

What are the pharmacological activities of Apocynum venetum?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological potential through its anti-hypertensive, anti-hyperlipidemic, anti-diabetic, anti-oxidant, and cardioprotective activities. Its phytochemical constituents work to regulate blood pressure and manage lipid profiles to prevent cardiovascular complications. The plant also assists in controlling blood glucose levels, making it a candidate for managing metabolic disorders. Furthermore, its potent antioxidant properties help neutralize free radicals and reduce systemic oxidative stress. These combined mechanisms provide a robust protective effect on the heart and overall vascular health.

As discussed in a study, the total flavonoid extract of Apocynum venetum leaves (AVLE) contains a total of 62 identified compounds. This chemical profile includes 41 flavonoids, 17 phenolic acids, 3 coumarins, and 1 caffeic acid ester. The AVL E demonstrated efficacy in attenuating dyslipidemia and alleviating oxidative stress in hyperlipidemic rats. Furthermore, the extract reduced lipid accumulation and reactive oxygen species (ROS) release in a free fatty acid (FFA)-induced hyperlipidemia L02 cell model. These pharmacological activities are linked to the activation of NR2F2 and AMPK pathways to mitigate oxidative stress and relieve hepatic lipid accumulation.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds (+)-catechin, (+)-gallocatechin, (-)-epicatechin, (-)-epigallocatechin, and hyperoside. These specific polyphenols and flavonoids serve as potent antioxidants within the biological system. The catechins are known for their ability to mitigate oxidative stress and reduce inflammation in various tissues. Additionally, the presence of hyperoside contributes to the plant's overall therapeutic profile through its unique chemical structure. Together, these bioactive substances provide the foundation for the plant's recognized pharmacological properties.

As discussed in a study, the leaves of Apocynum venetum contain fifteen flavonoids that act as hepatoprotective principles. These isolated compounds include (-)‐epicatechin (1), (-)‐epigallocatechin (2), isoquercetin (3), hyperin (4), (+)‐catechin (5), (+)‐gallocatechin (6), kaempferol-6'-O-acetate (7), isoquercetin-6'-O-acetate (8), catechin-[8,7-e]-4 alpha-(3,4-dihydroxpyhenyl)-dihydro-2(3H)-pyranone (9), apocynin B (10), apocynin A (11), cinchonain Ia (12), apocynin C (13), apocynin D (14), and quercetin (15). The flavonol glycosides 3, 4, 7, and 8, along with phenylpropanoid-substituted flavan-3-ols 11 and 12, showed potent inhibitory effects on TNF-alpha-induced cell death with IC50 values of 37.5, 14.5, 31.2, 55.1, 71.9, and 41.2 microM, respectively. In contrast, compounds 1, 2, 5, and 6 showed apparent activity only at 80 microM. All of these flavonoids demonstrate inhibitory effects on TNF-alpha-induced cell death with varying intensities.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating hypertension, diabetes, liver damage, depression, and anxiety. Researchers have studied its bioactive compounds for their potential to regulate blood sugar levels in diabetic patients. The plant also shows promise in managing high blood pressure through its medicinal properties. Its chemical constituents may help protect hepatic cells from damage and support liver function. Additionally, certain extracts are investigated for their ability to stabilize mood and alleviate symptoms of anxiety and depression.

As discussed in a study, Apocyni Veneti Folium (AVF) is a traditional Chinese medicinal herb used for the treatment of hypertension. Thirteen prototype constituents were characterized in serum samples, including seven dominant chemicals related to anti-hypertensve efficacy such as quercetin, kaempferol, hyperoside, isoquercitrin, chlorogenic acid, cryptochlorogenic acid, and neochlorogenic acid. Quantitative data from 14 batches of commercial AVF samples showed that the total contents of these seven marker components exhibited a 2-fold variation. The RSD values for these samples ranged from 12.15% to 75.61%. Through hierarchical cluster and heatmap analysis, these 14 batches were divided into three main groups.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause severe skin irritation, nausea, vomiting, dizziness, and potential cardiac complications due to its high concentration of toxic cardiac glycosides. Contact with the milky sap often leads to intense redness, itching, or chemical burns on the skin. If ingested, these compounds can disrupt the electrical rhythm of the heart, leading to dangerous palpitations or even death. Symptoms may escalate quickly depending on the dosage and individual sensitivity to the plant's chemical makeup. Because of these unpredictable and serious physiological reactions, extreme caution is necessary when handling any part of the specimen.
