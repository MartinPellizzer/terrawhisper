Possible Side Effects of toxicodendron toxicarium

Toxicodendron toxicarium can have side effects if used improperly, such as skin irritation, allergic reactions, and even anaphylaxis in severe cases.

Prolonged exposure to the herb's toxic oils can cause rashes, itching, and blistering, while some people may experience respiratory problems, such as coughing and shortness of breath.

Ingesting the herb can lead to nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain, while excessive consumption may result in liver and kidney damage.

If not handled carefully, Toxicodendron toxicarium can also cause eye irritation, including redness, tearing, and blurred vision.
