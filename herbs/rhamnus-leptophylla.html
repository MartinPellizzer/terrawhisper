Rhamnus leptophylla

What's the taxonomical classification of Rhamnus leptophylla?

Rhamnus leptophylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent through the order Rosales, it is placed in the family Rhamnaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Rhamnus, and its unique species designation, leptophylla.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Rhamnus leptophylla can cause gastrointestinal distress including nausea, vomiting, and abdominal cramping if its leaves or stems are ingested. The plant contains anthraquinone glycosides which act as potent laxatives when consumed in significant quantities. Excessive use of these compounds may lead to severe dehydration or electrolyte imbalances in the body. Skin contact with certain parts of the plant might also trigger localized irritation or allergic contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. It is essential to avoid all internal consumption to prevent these adverse physiological reactions.
