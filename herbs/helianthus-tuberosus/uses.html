Jerusalem Artichoke (Helianthus tuberosus) Uses

Jerusalem Artichoke is used to support respiratory health by alleviating symptoms of respiratory infections, asthma, and cough. It may also help with skin conditions such as infections, irritation, inflammation, allergies, psoriasis, and eczema due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Jerusalem Artichoke (Helianthus tuberosus) Monograph

1. Respiratory infections

Jerusalem Artichoke supports respiratory health by acting as a natural expectorant, helping to loosen mucus and ease coughing. Its high inulin content may also reduce inflammation in the airways, potentially aiding in the management of respiratory infections. It is often used in herbal preparations to support the body's natural defenses during colds and flu.

2. Asthma

Jerusalem artichoke may support respiratory health by reducing inflammation and acting as a mild bronchodilator. Its high inulin content can help thin mucus, easing breathing in asthma patients. It is often used in herbal blends to complement conventional asthma treatments.

3. Influenza

Jerusalem Artichoke supports immune function through its high inulin content, which promotes gut health and enhances the body's ability to fight off influenza. It may also help reduce inflammation and support respiratory health during flu symptoms. Its natural properties make it a valuable complementary remedy in herbal approaches to influenza management.

4. Cough

Jerusalem Artichoke has been traditionally used to soothe coughs due to its mucilage content, which coats the throat and reduces irritation. It may also support respiratory health by promoting mucus clearance and reducing inflammation. Its mild diuretic properties can help expel toxins, aiding in overall respiratory function.

5. Skin infections

Jerusalem Artichoke has antimicrobial properties that make it effective against certain skin infections. Its compounds can inhibit the growth of bacteria and fungi. Topical applications may help reduce inflammation and promote healing. It is often used in herbal remedies for conditions like eczema and fungal infections.

6. Skin irritation

Jerusalem artichoke has been traditionally used to soothe skin irritation due to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. The tuber contains inulin, which may help reduce redness and inflammation. It can be applied topically as a poultice or used in herbal salves to promote skin healing and comfort.

7. Inflammatory skin conditions

Jerusalem Artichoke contains inulin, which has anti-inflammatory properties. It can help reduce skin irritation and redness associated with inflammatory conditions like eczema and psoriasis. Its mild, soothing effect makes it a useful addition to topical treatments for sensitive skin.

8. Skin allergy

Jerusalem Artichoke has been used in herbal medicine to address skin allergies due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistaminic properties. It helps reduce redness, itching, and irritation associated with allergic reactions. Its high inulin content supports gut health, which can influence skin conditions. It is often prepared as a topical poultice or consumed internally as a tea or supplement.

9. Psoriasis

Jerusalem artichoke has shown potential in managing psoriasis due to its anti-inflammatory and immune-modulating properties. Its high inulin content supports gut health, which can influence skin conditions. Topical applications of its extracts may help reduce plaque and inflammation associated with psoriasis.

10. Eczema

Jerusalem Artichoke has been traditionally used to support skin health, including in the management of eczema. Its high inulin content may help regulate gut health, which is linked to skin conditions. The anti-inflammatory properties of the plant may also reduce irritation and promote healing in affected areas.
