Jerusalem Artichoke (Helianthus tuberosus) Compounds

The primary compounds in Jerusalem Artichoke include flavonoids, which act as antioxidants, and phenolic acids, which have anti-inflammatory properties. Terpenoids contribute to its bitter taste and may have antimicrobial effects. Glycosides and saponins support digestive health, while coumarins and anthocyanins provide additional antioxidant benefits.

Jerusalem Artichoke (Helianthus tuberosus) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Jerusalem Artichoke (Helianthus tuberosus). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting immune function and reducing inflammation. These compounds are part of the plant's natural defense mechanisms and are beneficial in herbal medicine for their health-promoting effects.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in Jerusalem Artichoke (Helianthus tuberosus). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and support overall health. This compound is particularly beneficial in reducing oxidative stress and inflammation associated with various chronic conditions.

3. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Jerusalem Artichoke (Helianthus tuberosus) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, supporting immune function and digestive health. They are naturally present in the plant's tubers and are part of its traditional use in herbal medicine.

4. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Jerusalem Artichoke (Helianthus tuberosus). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health by reducing inflammation and aiding in nutrient absorption. Tannins also provide antioxidant benefits, enhancing the plant's overall medicinal value.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Jerusalem Artichoke (Helianthus tuberosus). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by supporting digestive health and may help regulate blood sugar levels. Glycosides in this plant are part of its traditional use in herbal medicine.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a bioactive compound found in Jerusalem Artichoke (Helianthus tuberosus). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects. Coumarin may also support digestive health and has been studied for its potential role in managing conditions like irritable bowel syndrome.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Jerusalem Artichoke (Helianthus tuberosus). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting mild cleansing effects and supporting digestive health. Saponins may also aid in reducing cholesterol levels and enhancing nutrient absorption.

8. Essential oil

Essential oil from Jerusalem Artichoke (Helianthus tuberosus) contains volatile compounds like sesquiterpenes and monoterpenes. These oils are used in aromatherapy and may support digestive and respiratory health. The plant's essential oil is extracted through steam distillation, contributing to its traditional medicinal use.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Jerusalem Artichoke (Helianthus tuberosus). It contributes to the plant's vibrant color and has antioxidant properties that support cellular health. Anthocyanin may also aid in reducing inflammation and improving digestive function, making it a valuable component in herbal medicine.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloids are a class of nitrogen-containing compounds found in many plants, including Jerusalem Artichoke (Helianthus tuberosus). While Jerusalem Artichoke is not primarily known for its alkaloid content, it does contain small amounts of alkaloids that may contribute to its mild medicinal properties, though their role is less prominent compared to other compounds like inulin.
