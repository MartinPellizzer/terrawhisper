Hypericum sampsonii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hypericum sampsonii has a woody, perennial shrub habit characterized by erect, branching stems and small, elliptical to lanceolate leaves that are arranged oppositely along the branches. The foliage typically features fine glandular dots on the leaf surfaces, which is a common trait among members of the Hypericaceae family. During its flowering period, the plant produces numerous small, bright yellow flowers with five distinct petals and prominent central stamens. These blossoms are often clustered in terminal cymes that emerge from the ends of the woody stems. The overall structure of the plant provides a compact and textured appearance suitable for various garden environments.
