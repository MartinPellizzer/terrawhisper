10 Precautions To Take When Using Boronia Megastigma (Australian Rose)

Boronia megastigma, also known as Australian rose, has some precautions to consider before using it medicinally, such as pregnancy and breastfeeding warnings.

This is because the herb may stimulate uterine contractions and affect milk production, potentially leading to miscarriage or infant exposure to toxic compounds.

Examples of adverse effects include increased bleeding, premature labor, or infant distress, highlighting the importance of consulting a healthcare professional before using Boronia megastigma medicinally.

When using Boronia megastigma, also known as Australian Rose, medicinally, it's important to consult a doctor first.

This precaution is crucial because the plant's essential oil can interact with certain medications and exacerbate underlying health conditions. Moreover, its high concentration of limonene can cause skin irritation and allergic reactions in some individuals.
