10 Possible Side Effects Of Citrus × Aurantium

Citrus × aurantium has some side effects when used improperly, such as high blood pressure, rapid heart rate, and insomnia.

These side effects are often caused by the herb's stimulant properties and excessive consumption. For individuals with pre-existing hypertension, these side effects can worsen their condition, potentially leading to heart failure or stroke.

Moreover, excessive use can also lead to anxiety, irritability, and an increased risk of hemorrhaging.

1. Causes rapid heartbeat increases

Citrus × aurantium causes rapid heartbeat increases due to its high concentration of synephrine and other alkaloids.

These compounds stimulate the heart muscle, increasing blood pressure and leading to a faster heart rate.

The sympathetic nervous system is also activated, releasing catecholamines that further contribute to the increased heart activity.

2. Raises blood pressure rapidly

Citrus × aurantium raises blood pressure rapidly due to its high concentration of synephrine and octopamine, which are naturally occurring stimulants that can increase heart rate and blood pressure.

Additionally, Citrus × aurantium contains norepinephrine, a hormone that plays a key role in regulating blood pressure, contributing to its hypertensive effects.

3. Induces high blood sugar levels

Citrus × aurantium induces high blood sugar levels due to its stimulatory effect on the sympathetic nervous system.

The plant's active compounds, such as synephrine and octopamine, mimic the action of adrenaline, increasing heart rate and blood pressure, which can lead to hyperglycemia.

Additionally, bitter orange may also stimulate the release of cortisol, a hormone that promotes glucose production in the liver, further contributing to elevated blood sugar levels.

4. Produces excessive sweating excessively

Citrus × aurantium produces excessive sweating excessively due to its stimulatory effect on the sympathetic nervous system.

The plant's active compounds, such as synephrine and octopamine, can increase heart rate and blood pressure, leading to an overactive sweat response.

This excess sweating is a common side effect of using Citrus × aurantium, particularly at high doses or in combination with other stimulants.

5. Triggers severe allergic reactions

Citrus × aurantium triggers severe allergic reactions due to its high concentration of limonoids and flavonoids, which can cause an immune response in sensitive individuals.

The presence of these compounds in the plant's essential oil may lead to the release of histamine, a chemical responsible for triggering allergic symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling.

In some cases, this reaction can be severe and even life-threatening, making it essential to exercise caution when handling or consuming products containing Citrus × aurantium.

6. Leads to stomach upset frequently

Citrus × aurantium leads to stomach upset frequently.

This is attributed to its high concentration of synephrine, a stimulant that can irritate the digestive system and cause symptoms such as nausea, bloating, and abdominal cramps.

Additionally, the essential oils present in Citrus × aurantium may also contribute to stomach upset by altering the natural balance of gut bacteria and increasing gastric acid production, leading to discomfort and distress.

7. Increases risk of bleeding internally

Citrus × aurantium increases risk of bleeding internally due to its ability to inhibit platelet aggregation and activity, thus interfering with blood clotting mechanisms.

The flavonoids present in Citrus × aurantium, particularly naringenin and hesperidin, are responsible for this effect, which may lead to prolonged bleeding times and increased risk of gastrointestinal, urinary tract, or other internal bleeding episodes.

8. Stimulates irregular heartbeat patterns

Citrus × aurantium stimulates irregular heartbeat patterns due to its high content of synephrine and octopamine.

These stimulants can interact with the body's natural chemicals, including norepinephrine and epinephrine, leading to an increase in heart rate and blood pressure.

This can result in palpitations, arrhythmias, and other irregular heartbeat patterns.

9. Causes extreme nervousness immediately

Citrus × aurantium causes extreme nervousness immediately due to its high concentration of synephrine and other alkaloids.

These compounds mimic the effects of adrenaline in the body, stimulating the nervous system and leading to feelings of anxiety and jitteriness.

The sudden increase in energy and alertness can be overwhelming, causing individuals to feel extremely nervous and on edge.

10. Disrupts normal bowel movements

Citrus × aurantium disrupts normal bowel movements due to its high concentration of synephrine and octopamine, which can stimulate the gut and increase bowel contractions.

This may lead to irregular stool patterns, diarrhea, or constipation in some individuals.

The citrus-based compound's ability to slow down gastric emptying and reduce gut motility can also contribute to these digestive issues.
