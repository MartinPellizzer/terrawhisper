What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Citrus × Aurantium (Bitter Orange)?

Citrus × aurantium, also known as bitter orange, has health benefits such as aiding in weight loss, reducing inflammation, and improving digestion.

Its medicinal constituents include synephrine, naringenin, and aurantiamide, which are responsible for its stimulant and anti-inflammatory properties. Bitter orange is often prepared as a tea, capsule, or supplement, and is commonly used to boost energy and enhance athletic performance. However, its consumption can lead to side effects such as insomnia, rapid heartbeat, and dizziness.

It is essential to consult a healthcare professional before using bitter orange, especially for individuals with high blood pressure, heart conditions, or taking certain medications.

This article explains the health benefits, active constituents, medicinal preparations, possible side effects, and precautions related to Citrus × aurantium.

What are the health benefits of Citrus × aurantium?

Citrus × aurantium, also known as bitter orange, has health benefits such as reducing anxiety and stress, improving digestion, and supporting weight loss due to its high concentration of naringenin.

This flavonoid has antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that may also help lower cholesterol levels and protect against heart disease.

Additionally, the fruit's peel has been traditionally used to ease symptoms of cold and flu.

What are the active constituents of Citrus × aurantium?

Citrus × aurantium, also known as bitter orange, has active constituents such as synephrine, a phenylethanolamine amine alkaloid, and naringenin, a flavonoid glycoside, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The essential oil of bitter orange also contains limonene and β-myrcene, which have been shown to have antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant effects.

These constituents contribute to the traditional uses of bitter orange in treating fever, digestive issues, and respiratory problems.

What are the medicinal preparations of Citrus × aurantium?

Citrus × aurantium, also known as bitter orange, has medicinal preparations such as extracts and essential oils that are used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat various conditions, including nausea and indigestion.

These preparations can also be used topically to reduce inflammation and improve circulation.

However, excessive consumption of bitter orange can lead to adverse effects, including anxiety, insomnia, and increased heart rate.

What are the possible side effect of using Citrus × aurantium improperly?

Improper use of Citrus × aurantium, also known as bitter orange, increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as rapid heartbeat, palpitations, and high blood pressure.

Additionally, it may lead to liver damage, kidney stones, and anxiety due to its stimulant properties. Excessive consumption may also cause gastrointestinal issues, nausea, and headaches.

It's essential to follow the recommended dosage and consult a healthcare professional before using bitter orange for medicinal purposes.

What precautions to take when using Citrus × aurantium medicinally?

Before using Citrus × aurantium, also known as bitter orange, for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as consulting with a healthcare professional due to potential interactions with medications and health conditions.

Additionally, individuals with high blood pressure, heart problems, or anxiety should exercise caution as bitter orange can exacerbate these conditions.

Prolonged use and excessive dosages can also lead to adverse effects.
