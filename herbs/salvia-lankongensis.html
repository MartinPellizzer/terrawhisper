Salvia lankongensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Salvia lankongensis?

Salvia lankongensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical structure of its order, Lamiales, it falls into the family Lamiaceae. Finally, the plant is situated within the genus Salvia, with its specific designation being the species lankongensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Salvia lankongensis has an erect, perennial habit characterized by square stems and opposite, ovate leaves that possess serrated margins and a densely pubescent surface. The foliage is notably soft to the touch due to a thick covering of fine, white hairs on both the upper and lower surfaces. Its inflorescence consists of verticillasters arranged in long, terminal racemes that emerge from the upper axils of the stem. The flowers are tubular and bilabiate, typically displaying a pale violet or bluish hue that contrasts with the green calyces. These calyces are also covered in pubescence, providing protection to the developing floral structures.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the Lancang County region within the Yunnan Province of southwestern China. It is specifically found inhabiting the montane environments of the Hengduan Mountains. This species thrives in high-altitude habitats characterized by rocky slopes and scrublands. Its distribution is highly localized, meaning it is not found in any other global geographical locations. The specific ecological niches it occupies are dictated by the unique climatic conditions of this mountainous corridor.
