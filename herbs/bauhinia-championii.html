Bauhinia championii

What's the taxonomical classification of Bauhinia championii?

Bauhinia championii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Fabales. It is a member of the family Fabaceae, which places it within the well-known legume group. Finally, its specific taxonomic placement is defined by the genus Bauhinia and the species championii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bauhinia championii has distinctive bilobed leaves that are shaped like a butterfly wing with two symmetrical lobes meeting at a central point. The foliage is typically bright green and possesses a smooth texture on the upper surface. Its woody stems produce clusters of fragrant, five-petaled flowers that can range in color from white to soft pink. The plant also features small, elongated seed pods that develop as the flowers fade. These structural characteristics make the species easily identifiable among other members of the legume family.
