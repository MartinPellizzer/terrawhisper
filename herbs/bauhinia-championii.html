Bauhinia championii

What's the taxonomical classification of Bauhinia championii?

Bauhinia championii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Fabales. It is a member of the family Fabaceae, which places it within the well-known legume group. Finally, its specific taxonomic placement is defined by the genus Bauhinia and the species championii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bauhinia championii has distinctive bilobed leaves that are shaped like a butterfly wing with two symmetrical lobes meeting at a central point. The foliage is typically bright green and possesses a smooth texture on the upper surface. Its woody stems produce clusters of fragrant, five-petaled flowers that can range in color from white to soft pink. The plant also features small, elongated seed pods that develop as the flowers fade. These structural characteristics make the species easily identifiable among other members of the legume family.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several distinct medicinal compounds including bauchampine A, bauhichamines, bauhiniasstatins, bauhinoxepins, and various flavonoids. These specialized phytochemicals are primarily isolated from the bark and leaves of the species. The specific alkaloids like bauchampine A and the bauhichamines group contribute to its unique chemical profile. Additionally, the presence of bauhiniasstatins and bauhinoxepins suggests complex biological activities. These diverse secondary metabolites, alongside a rich array of flavonoids, provide the basis for its pharmacological potential.

According to a study published by "Natural product research", a new polyoxygenated abietane diterpenoid called bauchampine A (1) was isolated from the rattans of Bauhinia championii (Benth.) Benth. This new compound 1 was found alongside 7 known compounds, numbered 2-8. The study evaluated compound 1 for its anti-rheumatoid arthritis activity by examining its anti-proliferative effect on synoviocytes in vitro. Compound 1 exhibited an inhibitory effect on the proliferation of synoviocytes with an IC50 value comparable to that of methotrexate.
