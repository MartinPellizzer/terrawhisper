Coniogramme procera

What's the taxonomical classification of Coniogramme procera?

Coniogramme procera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae, this plant follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Polypodiales. It is specifically situated within the family Pteridaceae and falls under the genus Coniogramme, ultimately being identified by its specific species epithet, procera.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Coniogramme procera has large, bipinnate to tripinnate fronds characterized by elongated, tapering pinnules and a distinctively dark, often reddish-brown stipe.

The fertile segments are distinguished by the presence of prominent, clustered sporangia that form dense, brownish masses along the margins of the pinnules. These fronds are typically erect or ascending, growing from a creeping rhizome covered in fine, brownish scales. The texture of the foliage is relatively thin and membranous, contributing to a delicate overall appearance despite the large size of the blades.

This morphological structure allows the plant to effectively maximize spore dispersal within its shaded, humid habitat.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Coniogramme procera can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, or respiratory distress if its spores or fine hairs are inhaled or touched. The plant contains microscopic structures that may trigger itching and redness upon contact with sensitive skin. Individuals with pre-existing respiratory conditions might experience coughing or wheezing if they encounter the plant in an enclosed space. Because specific toxicological studies on this fern are limited, caution should be exercised to avoid direct contact with its foliage. Monitoring for any swelling or discomfort is recommended if accidental exposure occurs.
