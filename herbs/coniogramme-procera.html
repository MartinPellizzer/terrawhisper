Coniogramme procera

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Coniogramme procera has large, bipinnate to tripinnate fronds characterized by elongated, tapering pinnules and a distinctively dark, often reddish-brown stipe. The fertile segments are distinguished by the presence of prominent, clustered sporangia that form dense, brownish masses along the margins of the pinnules. These fronds are typically erect or ascending, growing from a creeping rhizome covered in fine, brownish scales. The texture of the foliage is relatively thin and membranous, contributing to a delicate overall appearance despite the large size of the blades. This morphological structure allows the plant to effectively maximize spore dispersal within its shaded, humid habitat.
