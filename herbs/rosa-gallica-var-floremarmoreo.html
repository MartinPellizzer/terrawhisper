Marmalade Rose (Rosa Gallica Var. Floremarmoreo)

Marmalade rose, scientifically known as Rosa gallica var. floremarmoreo, is a rare and visually striking variety of rose prized for its medicinal and aromatic properties. This ornamental plant, often cultivated for its vibrant, marmalade-colored petals, has been historically valued as a medicinal herb and adaptogen, known for its ability to support physical and emotional resilience. Its primary benefits include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and calming effects, making it useful in treating digestive issues, stress, and skin conditions. Traditionally, it has been used in European herbal medicine for its soothing properties, often incorporated into teas and tinctures to promote relaxation and internal health. In modern wellness practices, Marmalade rose is increasingly recognized for its unique floral essence, used in aromatherapy and natural skincare products, while its rare active compounds, such as flavonoids and essential oils, continue to attract scientific interest.
