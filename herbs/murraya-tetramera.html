Murraya tetramera

What's the taxonomical classification of Murraya tetramera?

Murraya tetramera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Sapindales. It is a member of the family Rutaceae and falls within the genus Murraya, specifically identified by its species name, tetramera.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Murraya tetramera has an evergreen shrub or small tree habit characterized by pinnately compound leaves, small white fragrant flowers, and fleshy quadrangular berries. The foliage consists of several pairs of leaflets that are typically elliptic to oblong in shape with acute tips. Its flowers are actinomorphic and emerge in small clusters, possessing five distinct petals and numerous stamens. The fruit develops into a distinctive four-angled berry that changes color as it matures. This plant's overall structure is often dense and bushy, making it suitable for various landscape uses.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds aegeline, crotaleschenine, murrayafoline A, tombozine, and DL-Xylose. These diverse chemical constituents contribute to the complex pharmacological profile of the species. Aegeline and crotaleschenine are indole alkaloids that play significant roles in the plant's biological activities. The presence of murrayafoline A and tombozine further expands the chemical diversity found within its tissues. Additionally, the sugar DL-Xylose serves as a structural component in various glycoside formations. Together, these substances define the specific biochemical properties used in medicinal research.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Murraya tetramera targets inflammatory mediators such as cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2), inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS), and various pro-inflammatory cytokines including tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-alpha) and interleukin-6 (IL-6). The plant's bioactive phytochemicals, particularly its alkaloids and terpenoids, interfere with the signaling pathways that trigger these protein expressions. By inhibiting COX-2, the extracts effectively reduce the production of prostaglandins that drive pain and swelling. These interactions also extend to modulating the activity of enzymes involved in oxidative stress, such as superoxide dismutase. Such molecular interactions collectively account for the plant's demonstrated anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties in pharmacological studies.

What are the therapeutic actions of Murraya tetramera?

Murraya tetramera has bitter, astringent, stomachic, digestive stimulant, and carminative therapeutic actions that primarily target the gastrointestinal system. Its astringent properties can assist in toning the mucous membranes of the gut to reduce inflammation or loosen stools. Furthermore, its carminative action helps to expel intestinal gas and relieve abdominal bloating.
