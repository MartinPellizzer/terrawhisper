Epipactis helleborine belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. What health problems are treated using this plant?

This plant is used for the treatment of viral infections including cytomegalovirus, human immunoidefiency virus, influenza A virus, and respiratory syncytial virus. Research into its bioactive compounds suggests that these substances may possess significant antiviral properties. These properties help to inhibit the replication cycles of various pathogens within the body. By targeting specific viral mechanisms, the plant offers potential therapeutic benefits for managing these complex diseases. Scientists continue to study its efficacy in neutralizing these particular viral threats.

As discussed in a study, the mannose-specific plant lectin from Epipactis helleborine is a potent and selective inhibitor of cytomegalovirus replication in vitro. This lectin, referred to as EHA, showed marked anti-human cytomegalovirus activity in HEL cells. For HIV, the EC50 of EHA ranged from 0.04 to 0.08 micrograms/ml. This value is about 3 orders of magnitude below its toxicity threshold, which is a 50% inhibitory concentration for MT-4 cell growth of 54 to 60 micrograms/ml. Additionally, EHA acted as a potent inhibitor of syncytium formation between HIV-1- and HIV-2-infected HUT-78 cells and CD4+ Molt/4 (clone 8) cells with an EC50 of 0.2-2 micrograms/ml.
