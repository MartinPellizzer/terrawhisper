What side-effects this plant can have?

Erythrophleum ivorense can cause severe toxicity including gastrointestinal distress, neurological impairment, and potential death if ingested due to its high concentration of toxic alkaloids. These chemical compounds act as potent irritants to the digestive tract, often resulting in intense vomiting or diarrhea. If the toxins enter the bloodstream, they can disrupt the central nervous system and lead to symptoms such as convulsions or paralysis. Contact with the plant's sap may also trigger localized skin irritation or allergic reactions in sensitive individuals. Because of these dangerous physiological effects, careful handling and strict avoidance of ingestion are necessary.
