Telosma cordata

What's the taxonomical classification of Telosma cordata?

Telosma cordata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Gentianaales. At the family level, it is a member of the Apocynaceae, and its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Telosma and the species cordata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Telosma cordata has heart-shaped, cordate leaves that are typically 5 to 15 centimeters long and feature smooth or slightly wavy margins. These evergreen leaves are arranged oppositely along the stems and possess a glossy, dark green upper surface. The plant produces small, tubular, greenish-white flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. Its stems are somewhat woody at the base and can climb or scramble through surrounding vegetation. The root system is fibrous, allowing the plant to establish itself in moist, shaded environments.
