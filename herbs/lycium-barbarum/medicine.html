What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Lycium Barbarum (Goji Berry)?

Lycium barbarum has health benefits such as reducing inflammation and improving cardiovascular health.

The medicinal constituents of this herb include polysaccharides, flavonoids, and antioxidants, which are responsible for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Goji berries can be consumed as a supplement in various forms, including capsules, powders, and teas. However, possible side effects may occur when consuming goji berries, such as gastrointestinal upset or allergic reactions.

To minimize risks, it is recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before using goji berries, especially for individuals with pre-existing medical conditions or taking medications.

What are the health benefits of Lycium barbarum?

Lycium barbarum has health benefits such as improving vision and reducing the risk of age-related macular degeneration.

It is also rich in antioxidants, which can help protect against cell damage and reduce inflammation.

Additionally, Goji berries may have a positive effect on blood lipid profiles and glucose levels, making them a potential natural remedy for those with diabetes or high cholesterol.

What are the active constituents of Lycium barbarum?

The health benefits of Lycium barbarum comes from its active constituents, such as carotenoids, flavonoids, and polysaccharides.

The berries contain a rich mixture of vitamins A, C, and E, as well as potassium, iron, and calcium. Additionally, they are rich in antioxidants like beta-carotene and zeaxanthin, which have been shown to exhibit anti-inflammatory and immune-modulating effects.

These constituents contribute to the berry's potential health benefits, including antioxidant and anti-aging properties, as well as support for eye health and overall wellness.

What are the medicinal preparations of Lycium barbarum?

Lycium barbarum has different medicinal preparations, such as dried fruit, capsules, extracts, and tea.

The dried fruit is used to treat various eye problems, including conjunctivitis and cataracts, while the capsule form is commonly used to support overall health and well-being, particularly for its antioxidant properties.

Extracts are often used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat conditions such as diabetes, hypertension, and cardiovascular disease, whereas the tea preparation is used to promote relaxation and reduce stress.

What are the possible side effect of using Lycium barbarum improperly?

Improper use of Lycium barbarum increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as allergic reactions, digestive issues like diarrhea or stomach pain, interact with medications for blood pressure, diabetes, and cholesterol, cause changes in thyroid function, stimulate menstrual cycles, increase risk of bleeding or bruising due to platelet inhibition, induce anaphylaxis, trigger respiratory problems like bronchitis or asthma exacerbation, lead to kidney damage, and even affect cognitive function, mood, and behavior.

What precautions to take when using Lycium barbarum medicinally?

Before using Lycium barbarum for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as consulting with a healthcare professional to determine the appropriate dosage and potential interactions with other medications.

Additionally, be cautious when consuming goji berries if you have bleeding disorders or are taking anticoagulant medications, as they may increase the risk of bleeding.
