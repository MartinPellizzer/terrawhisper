10 Precautions To Take When Using Lycium Barbarum

Lycium barbarum has some precautions to consider before using it medicinally, such as consulting with a healthcare professional, especially for pregnant or breastfeeding women.

This is crucial because excessive consumption can stimulate the uterus, leading to premature labor or other complications.

Without proper guidance, individuals may experience side effects like increased heart rate, dizziness, and stomach upset.

1. Take under medical supervision

When using Lycium barbarum medicinally, it's important to take under medical supervision.

This is crucial because the plant's active compounds may interact with certain medications, such as diabetes treatments and blood thinners, potentially leading to adverse effects or altered efficacy.

Moreover, goji berries may cause allergic reactions, stomach upset, or interact with other supplements, making medical monitoring essential to ensure safe and effective treatment.

2. Avoid with kidney disease

When using Lycium barbarum medicinally, it's important to avoid with kidney disease.

The high levels of potassium in this herb can exacerbate kidney problems and even lead to cardiac complications if the body is unable to properly eliminate excess potassium.

Additionally, individuals with kidney disease may already be at risk for hyperkalemia, a condition characterized by elevated potassium levels, making it crucial to exercise caution when consuming Lycium barbarum supplements.

3. Monitor blood sugar levels

When using Lycium barbarum medicinally, it's important to monitor blood sugar levels carefully.

As this herb has been found to lower blood sugar levels in some studies, it may interact with medications for diabetes or exacerbate hypoglycemia (low blood sugar) in individuals already experiencing these conditions.

Additionally, Lycium barbarum may also have a potent effect on blood glucose levels when consumed in large quantities, making regular monitoring essential to ensure safe and effective use.

4. Do not exceed dosage

When using Lycium barbarum medicinally, it's important to do not exceed dosage.

This precaution is crucial because taking excessive amounts can cause adverse effects such as digestive issues, headaches, and interact with medications like diabetes drugs, blood thinners, and antihypertensives. Furthermore, high doses may lead to increased levels of potassium in the bloodstream, potentially causing cardiac problems.

Sticking to recommended dosages ensures safe and effective use of this medicinal herb.

5. Contraindicated with diabetes

When using Lycium barbarum medicinally, it's important to contraindicate with diabetes as the fruit can lower blood sugar levels.

This is particularly concerning for individuals already managing diabetes as uncontrolled fluctuations in blood glucose can lead to serious complications.

Additionally, the berries' hypoglycemic effects may mask signs of undiagnosed or poorly managed diabetes, making it crucial to consult a healthcare professional before using Lycium barbarum medicinally if you have a history of diabetes or are at risk for developing it.

6. Consult before pregnancy

When using Lycium barbarum medicinally, it's important to consult before pregnancy.

This precaution is crucial as the herb may stimulate uterine contractions and affect fetal development during early stages of pregnancy. Moreover, its high iron content can increase the risk of miscarriage or preterm labor if not properly monitored.

It's essential to discuss potential risks with a healthcare provider and determine the suitable dosage and duration for safe use during this critical period.

7. Watch for allergic reactions

When using Lycium barbarum medicinally, it's important to watch for allergic reactions.

This precaution is crucial because some individuals may be sensitive to the plant's components, which can lead to an allergic response ranging from mild symptoms like hives and itching to severe reactions such as difficulty breathing or swelling of the face.

Additionally, Lycium barbarum may interact with certain medications, making it essential for patients to consult with a healthcare professional before using this herb to ensure safe and effective treatment.

8. Avoid with stomach issues

When using Lycium barbarum medicinally, it's important to avoid with stomach issues because its consumption can exacerbate digestive problems such as irritable bowel syndrome (IBS), acid reflux, and stomach ulcers.

The berries are high in fiber and antioxidants, which can irritate the stomach lining and worsen existing conditions, leading to discomfort, nausea, and vomiting.

It is crucial to consult a healthcare professional before using Lycium barbarum medicinally if you have a pre-existing stomach condition to ensure safe and effective treatment.

9. Use in moderation only

When using Lycium barbarum medicinally, it's important to use in moderation only due to its potential interactions with certain medications and its high antioxidant content.

Consuming large amounts can cause an overabundance of antioxidants in the body, which may counteract their intended benefits and lead to adverse effects such as gastrointestinal upset or allergic reactions.

10. Inform healthcare providers

When using Lycium barbarum medicinally, it's important to inform healthcare providers about any pre-existing medical conditions and medications being taken.

This is crucial because Goji berries may interact with certain drugs, such as blood thinners, diabetes medication, and blood pressure medications, which can lead to adverse effects.

Additionally, individuals with kidney or liver disease should exercise caution when consuming Goji berry supplements due to its potential to exacerbate these conditions.
