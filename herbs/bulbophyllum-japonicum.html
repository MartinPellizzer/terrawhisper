Bulbophyllum japonicum

What's the taxonomical classification of Bulbophyllum japonicum?

Bulbophyllum japonicum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asparagales. This plant is a representative of the family Orchidaceae, specifically situated within the genus Bulbophyllum, and is identified by its unique species name, japonicum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bulbophyllum japonicum has creeping rhizomes that produce small, ovate pseudobulbs spaced along the stem, each bearing a single, narrow, lanceolate leaf. These pseudobulbs act as storage organs for water and nutrients to support the plant during dry periods. The inflorescence consists of a slender scape that carries several small, yellowish-white flowers. Each flower features a distinctively shaped lip that is often mobile to attract pollinators. The delicate structure of the plant allows it to cling effectively to mossy bark in its natural habitat.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the subtropical and temperate regions of Japan, specifically found on the islands of Honshu, Shikoku, Kyushu, and Okinawa. It primarily inhabits moist, shaded environments such as deciduous forests and rocky cliffs. Within these habitats, it often grows as an epiphyte on tree branches or as a lithophyte on mossy stones. The species prefers areas with high humidity and consistent moisture levels to thrive. Its range is relatively concentrated within the Japanese archipelago compared to other members of its genus.
