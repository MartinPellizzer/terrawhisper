Juncus nepalicus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Junius nepalicus has slender, cylindrical, unbranched stems that are typically bright green and reach heights of up to 30 centimeters. These stems are characterized by a smooth texture and a lack of distinct nodes or internodes. The leaves are reduced to minute, scale-like structures that are barely visible along the length of the culms. At the tips of the stems, the plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in compact clusters. These reproductive structures eventually develop into tiny, brownish seeds contained within protective capsules.
