Trichosanthes hylonoma

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Trichosanthes hylonoma has a climbing or trailing habit characterized by slender, herbaceous stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with entire margins. The foliage typically features prominent venation and a smooth or slightly pubescent surface texture. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in axillary clusters, often exhibiting a pale or greenish coloration. The reproductive structures produce small, fleshy fruits that are characteristic of the Cucurbitaceae family.
