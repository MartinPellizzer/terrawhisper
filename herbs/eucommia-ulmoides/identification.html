To identify Eucommia (Eucommia ulmoides) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Eucommia ulmoides belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Saxifragales, family Eucommiaceae, genus Eucommia, and species Eucommia ulmoides. This classification places it within the flowering plants and highlights its unique position in the botanical hierarchy.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Eucommia is Eucommia ulmoides, first described by the French botanist Antoine Laurent de Jussieu in 1789. This name is widely accepted in botanical literature and serves as the standard for identifying the species. Synonyms include Eucommia ulmoides var. ulmoides and Eucommia ulmoides var. lanceolata, reflecting historical classification variations.

Morphological Characteristics

Eucommia ulmoides is a deciduous tree or shrub with a straight, smooth trunk and a broad crown. Its root system is extensive and fibrous, anchoring it firmly in the soil. The stem is thick, bark is dark brown and fissured, and it can grow up to 20 meters tall. Leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a leathery texture with a serrated margin. The inflorescence is a raceme of small greenish-yellow flowers. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release winged seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Eucommia ulmoides can be distinguished by its unique combination of features. It has a rough, fissured bark that peels in strips, and its leaves are ovate with serrated edges. The flowers are small and greenish-yellow, and the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release winged seeds. These characteristics help differentiate it from closely related species.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Eucommia ulmoides is native to eastern Asia, primarily China, Korea, and Japan. It thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils. It is commonly found at altitudes between 300 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Eucommia ulmoides blooms in early spring, typically between March and April. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early autumn, around August to September. For identification purposes, the best time to observe key features is during the growing season, from spring to early autumn.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Eucommia ulmoides belongs to the family Ebenaceae and is closely related to species like Diospyros and Spondias. It shares key morphological and genetic traits with these genera, including the presence of latex and specific leaf structures. Its phylogenetic position highlights its evolutionary ties within the Ebenaceae family.
