Gentiana pubigera

What's the taxonomical classification of Gentiana pubigera?

Gentiana pubigera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Gentianales and the family Gentianaceae. Ultimately, this plant is identified by its genus, Gentiana, and its specific species designation, pubigera.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gentiana pubigera has erect, perennial stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and bear opposite, lanceolate leaves with entire margins. The foliage is characterized by a smooth, green surface and a slightly fleshy texture. Its most striking feature is the funnel-shaped, bright blue flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. Each blossom possesses a distinct corolla with five lobes and often features small, pale spots inside the throat. The plant's overall structure is compact and herbaceous, suited for its alpine or subalpine habitats.

What are the herbal preparations of this plant?

This plant can be prepared as a decoction, a tincture, or an infusion to utilize its bitter properties for digestive support.
