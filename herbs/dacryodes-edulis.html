Dacryodes edulis

What's the taxonomical classification of Dacryodes edulis?

Dacryodes eduilis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Sapindales and is a member of the family Burseraceae. Specifically, it is identified by the genus Dacryodes and the species name edulis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Dacryodes edulis contains bioactive phytochemicals including alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, tannins, and terpenoids that contribute to its therapeutic properties. These specific chemical constituents are primarily concentrated in the leaves and bark of the tree. Research suggests that these compounds possess significant antioxidant, antimicrobial, and anti-inflammatory activities. In various traditional medicinal practices, extracts from the plant are utilized to treat ailments such as skin infections, digestive issues, and respiratory problems. The presence of these secondary metabolites makes the species a valuable subject for pharmacological studies regarding natural drug development.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including stomach aches, skin infections, coughs, and respiratory issues. The bark and leaves of the species are frequently employed in traditional medicine to alleviate gastrointestinal distress. Some preparations are also applied topically to heal wounds or manage skin irritations. Additionally, the plant is believed to possess properties that help soothe persistent coughs and congestion. These medicinal applications vary depending on the specific part of the plant being utilized by local healers.
