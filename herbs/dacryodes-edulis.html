Dacryodes edulis

What's the taxonomical classification of Dacryodes edulis?

Dacryodes eduilis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Sapindales and is a member of the family Burseraceae. Specifically, it is identified by the genus Dacryodes and the species name edulis.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Dacryodes eduilis contains bioactive compounds in its bark, leaves, and resin that are utilized for various medicinal purposes. The bark is frequently employed in traditional practices to treat inflammatory conditions and digestive ailments. Extracts from the leaves are often used to manage skin infections or to act as an antiseptic. Additionally, the resin can be applied topically to aid in the healing of wounds and minor abrasions. These diverse parts provide a wide range of therapeutic applications within local medicinal knowledge.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Dacryodes edulis targets inflammatory mediators such as cyclooxygenase enzymes, various cytokines, and antioxidant proteins to produce its therapeutic effects. The plant contains bioactive secondary metabolites like polyphenols and tannins that inhibit the expression of pro-inflammatory proteins. These compounds interact with the arachidonic acid metabolism pathway to reduce the synthesis of prostaglandins. Additionally, the phytochemicals engage with oxidative stress markers to enhance the activity of superoxide dismutase and catalase. By modulating these specific protein pathways, the plant exhibits significant antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

Dacryodes edulis has medicinal properties that include antibacterial, antifungal, and anti-inflammatory effects utilized primarily through its bark, leaves, and resin. What are the herbal preparations of Dacryodes edulis?

Dacryodes edulis is prepared as a decoction of leaves, bark, or roots to treat various ailments, and its fruit is often consumed fresh or processed into syrups and juices. The leaves are frequently boiled in water to create a medicinal tea used for addressing digestive issues or respiratory symptoms. Bark extracts are sometimes used in topical applications or ingested to manage inflammation. Additionally, the resin derived from the tree can be utilized in traditional remedies for its specific therapeutic properties. These diverse preparation methods allow different parts of the plant to be used for both nutritional and medicinal purposes.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Dacryodes eduilis can cause skin irritation, localized dermatitis, or allergic reactions if the milky latex from its bark or fruit comes into direct contact with sensitive skin. This sap contains various chemical compounds that may trigger redness, itching, or swelling in some individuals. While the fruit is generally considered edible, consuming excessive amounts or consuming unripe portions might lead to gastrointestinal discomfort. Some users have reported mild digestive upsets such as nausea or stomach cramps after ingestion. It is always advisable to perform a small patch test before using any part of the plant topically.
