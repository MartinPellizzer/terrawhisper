Dacryodes edulis

What's the taxonomical classification of Dacryodes edulis?

Dacryodes eduilis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Sapindales and is a member of the family Burseraceae. Specifically, it is identified by the genus Dacryodes and the species name edulis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dacryodes edulis has an evergreen tree structure characterized by an erect trunk that typically reaches heights of 15 to 25 meters and possesses a crown composed of dense, spreading branches. The leaves are simple, alternate, and coriaceous, often exhibiting a glossy texture with an elliptical to oblong shape. Its reproductive morphology includes small, inconspicuous flowers that give rise to fleshy, drupaceous fruits. These fruits are typically oval or ellipsoidal in shape and turn a dark purple or black color when fully mature. The bark is generally smooth or slightly fissured, providing a protective layer for the tree's internal vascular system.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical rainforest regions of Central and West Africa, spanning from Guinea and Sierra Leone eastward to the Democratic Republic of the Congo and south toward Angola. It thrives primarily within the humid, lowland evergreen forests that characterize these equatorial zones. Its range is strictly limited to these high-moisture environments where dense canopy cover provides necessary shade. The species is most frequently documented in the Guineo-Congolian regional center of endemism. Localized populations can be found throughout various river basins across the African continent.

What are the pharmacological activities of Dacryodes edulis?

This plant has antibacterial, antibiotic-potentiating, and antidrepanocytary activities, along with antioxidant properties and a protective effect against aminochrome-induced toxicity. Its chemical constituents demonstrate a capacity to inhibit the growth of various bacterial pathogens. Furthermore, the plant can enhance the efficacy of existing antibiotics when used in combination. It also shows promise in managing sickle cell disease through its antidrepanocytary mechanisms. Additionally, its antioxidant nature helps neutralize free radicals and shields cells from damage caused by aminochrome-induced toxicity.

According to a study published by "BMC research notes", Dacryodes eduledis was found to have significant antibacterial activity on all the S. aureus isolates. This activity was evaluated against 11 S. aureus clinical isolates using the broth microdilution method. The minimum inhibitory concentration for Dacryodes eduledis was measured at 64-256 µg/ml. These findings demonstrate that the plant possesses interesting inhibitory properties against S. aureus species. Therefore, this plant could be a good candidate to overcome infectious diseases associated with these microorganisms.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains various medicinal compounds including gallic acid, quercetin, and various tannins that provide significant therapeutic properties. The presence of gallic acid contributes strong antioxidant and antimicrobial activities to the plant extracts. Quercetin acts as a potent flavonoid that helps reduce inflammation and protects cells from oxidative stress. These bioactive molecules work together to support the plant's traditional use in treating diverse ailments. Such chemical profiles make the species a valuable subject for pharmacological research.

According to a study published by "Medicines (Basel, Switzerland)", the methanolic seed extract of Dacryodes eduledis (DEMSE) was investigated for its potential antioxidant properties. The study evaluated the free radical scavenging antioxidant ability of the extract using 2,2-Diphenyl-1-picrylhydrazyl (DPPH). While the study measured various components, the radical scavenging percentage for the methanolic fruit extract of Dacryodes eduledis (DEMFE) was 37% relative to β-tocopherol at 98%. This research focused on the antioxidant, anti-acetylcholinesterase (AChE), and total phenolic and flavonoids contents (TPC/TFC) of the plant.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for managing diabetes, type 2 diabetes, high blood glucose level, diabetes complications, and various noncommunicable diseases. Its medicinal properties help in regulating sugar levels to prevent the onset of chronic metabolic issues. By stabilizing glucose, it plays a crucial role in reducing the risk of long-term diabetes complications. This botanical resource is increasingly recognized for its ability to combat various noncommunicable diseases through its bioactive compounds. Such therapeutic applications make it a significant subject in traditional medicine for metabolic health.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", Dacryodes eduulis is used in folklore for treating and managing diabetes and its complications. The plant modulates glucose metabolism, cholinergic activities, and Nrf2 expression, while suppressing oxidative stress and dyslipidemia. In studies involving fructose-streptozotocin induced type 2 diabetic rats, the butanol fraction of the ethanol extract was administered at doses of 150 and 300 mg/kg bodyweight. These treatments, compared to a metformin dose of 200 mg/kg bodyweight, significantly (p < 0.05) reduced blood glucose levels and improved pancreatic morphology and β-cell function. The intervention lasted for 6 weeks and resulted in significant (p < 0.05) changes to serum insulin secretion and various enzyme activities.
