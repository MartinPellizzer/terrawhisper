Ilex yunnanensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Ilex yunnanensis?

Ilex yunnanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Aquifoliales and the family Aquifoliaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized within the genus Ilex, with its specific designation being the species yunnanensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ilex yunnanensis has an evergreen shrub or small tree morphology characterized by leathery, elliptical leaves that measure approximately 3 to 7 centimeters in length and possess serrated margins. The plant features a dense, branching woody structure with dark green foliage that remains consistent throughout the year. Small, inconspicuous flowers typically emerge in clusters from the leaf axils during the blooming season. These blossoms eventually give rise to small, globose drupes that transition from green to dark colors upon ripening. The overall growth habit is compact and sturdy, allowing it to thrive in mountainous subtropical environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

It typically thrives in high-altitude habitats such as evergreen broad-leaved forests and shrublands. The distribution follows the rugged terrain of the Hengduan Mountains where moderate humidity and temperate climates are prevalent. These populations are often found at elevations ranging from middle to high mountain zones.
