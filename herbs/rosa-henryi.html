Rosa henryi

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rosa henryi has pinnate leaves with 5 to 7 leaflets that are ovate or elliptical in shape and possess serrated margins. The plant typically grows as a deciduous shrub, characterized by its slender, arching stems and sharp prickles. Its flowers are usually solitary or in small clusters, featuring five delicate petals that range in color from white to pale pink. The fruit produced by this species is a small, fleshy hip that serves as a key identifier during the dormant season. These morphological features distinguish it from other members of the Rosaceae family within its native habitat.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the mountainous regions of central and southern China, specifically within the provinces of Hubei, Hunan, Sichuan, and Guizhou. It typically thrives in high-altitude environments located on rocky slopes or near the edges of dense forests. The species is often found at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,500 meters above sea level. Its specific habitat requirements restrict it to localized pockets of temperate climates within these subtropical provinces. Consequently, its range remains relatively narrow and concentrated within these specific topographical features.

      What are the herbal preparations of Rosa henryi?

      Rosa henryi is prepared primarily as a decoction, a concentrated liquid extract made by boiling the dried flowers or stems in water. This method allows the medicinal compounds within the plant tissues to be efficiently released into the solution for consumption. Practitioners may also utilize simple infusions, where hot water is poured over the plant material and allowed to steep for a shorter period. In some traditional contexts, the plant is processed into tinctures using alcohol as a solvent to preserve its bioactive components. These various liquid preparations facilitate the rapid absorption of the plant's therapeutic properties.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Rosa henryi can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or gastrointestinal distress if ingested or applied improperly. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, many species within the Rosaceae family contain compounds that may trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Direct contact with the plant's stems or leaves might result in redness, itching, or localized swelling on the skin. If any part of the plant is consumed, it could potentially lead to stomach aches or nausea. It is always advisable to exercise caution and avoid direct exposure to prevent unexpected bodily reactions.
