What is Acianthera atropurpurea?

Acianthera atropurpurea is a species of orchid in the family Orchidaceae, native to Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by a short stem, 10-20 cm in height, with a single leaf that is elliptical in shape and 10-20 cm in length. The inflorescence bears 2-5 flowers, each 1-2 cm in diameter, with a purplish-brown color and a yellow labellum with a purple apex. The flowers have a sepals that are lanceolate and a lip that is ovate, both with a yellow color.

The plant blooms from March to May, and its flowers are pollinated by bees and wasps.
