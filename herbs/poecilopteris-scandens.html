Poecilopteris Scandens

Poecilopteris scandens, a type of fossilized fern, has been studied for its potential medicinal properties due to its historical presence in ancient ecosystems.

While not a living plant, it has been used in traditional medicine for its purported ability to support respiratory health and alleviate symptoms of inflammation. Researchers have explored its chemical composition, suggesting it may contain compounds with antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. Some herbal practitioners have incorporated it into remedies for coughs and bronchial issues, though scientific validation remains limited.

Despite its historical use, further research is needed to fully understand its efficacy and safety in modern medicinal applications.
