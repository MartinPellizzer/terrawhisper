Blueberry (Vaccinium angustifolium)

Blueberry (Vaccinium angustifolium) is a member of the Ericaceae family, native to North America, Canada, and United States. Traditionally, its fruits, leaves, and bark have been used for decoctions, infusions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its astringent, tonic, and anti-inflammatory actions, and has a long history of use in native american herbal medicine, european herbal medicine, and mediterranean herbal traditions.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 0.3 to 1.5 meters.
Leaves
Laminate leaves with upper surface glaucous blue and lower surface pale green, featuring prominent stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Flowers are actinomorphic, blue to violet in color, arranged in clusters, with five petals and five sepals, and possess stamen numbers that are consistently five.
Stems
Erect, unbranched, woody, glabrous, with persistent scar-like nodes.
