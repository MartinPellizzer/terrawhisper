What's the taxonomical classification of Botrychium virginianum?

Botrychium virginianum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Ophioglossidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Ophioglossales and the family Ophioglossaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Botrychium and is specifically designated as the species virginianum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Botrychium virginianum has a dimorphic leaf structure consisting of a sterile basal rosette of small, fleshy, oblong leaves and a fertile, erect, branching stalk bearing small, kidney-shaped spores. The sterile leaves are typically clustered near the ground and lack the complex division seen in the reproductive portion. The fertile frond is much taller and features several small, somewhat fleshy segments that hold the sporangia. These sporangia are arranged in a somewhat irregular manner along the upper branches of the stalk. The entire plant emerges from a small, underground, fleshy rhizome that stores nutrients for the growing season.
