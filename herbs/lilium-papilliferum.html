Lilium papilliferum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lilium papilliferum has bulbous rhizomes with upright, linear leaves and nodding, recurved flowers characterized by prominent papillae on the tepals. The plant typically reaches a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, supporting several funnel-shaped blossoms per stem. Its tepals exhibit a distinct texture due to the presence of small, nipple-like projections that give the species its name. The floral color often transitions from a creamy yellow to a deep orange, frequently decorated with dark spots. These morphological traits distinguish it from other members of the genus within its specific habitat.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of central and western China, specifically within provinces such as Sichuan, Yunnan, Guizhou, and Gansu. It thrives in high-altitude habitats where it grows amidst forest margins and rocky slopes. The species is typically found at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters above sea level. These specific ecological niches provide the cool, moist environments necessary for its survival. Its distribution remains concentrated within these subtropical highland zones across East Asia.

      What are the herbal preparations of Lilium papilliferum?

      Lilium papilliferum is prepared primarily as a decoction, a powder, or an infusion using its dried bulbs for medicinal purposes. The bulbs are often dried and ground into a fine powder to be mixed with honey or water for ingestion. Some traditional methods involve boiling the bulb pieces in water to create a concentrated liquid extract known as a decoction. Additionally, the plant can be steeped in hot water as an infusion to extract its soluble active compounds. These various forms allow for different methods of administration depending on the intended therapeutic effect.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Lilium papilliferum can cause severe gastrointestinal distress including nausea, vomiting, and abdominal cramping if ingested. These symptoms often arise due to the presence of toxic alkaloids within the plant tissues. Prolonged exposure or high doses may lead to more serious complications such as dehydration or systemic toxicity. Contact with the plant's sap can also trigger localized skin irritation or allergic dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Careful avoidance of all parts of the plant is necessary to prevent these adverse reactions.
