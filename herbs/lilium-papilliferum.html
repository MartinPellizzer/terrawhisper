Lilium papilliferum

What's the taxonomical classification of Lilium papilliferum?

Lilium papilliferum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Liliales. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the family Liliaceae, falling under the genus Lilium, and is ultimately identified by its specific species name, papilliferum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lilium papilliferum has bulbous rhizomes with upright, linear leaves and nodding, recurved flowers characterized by prominent papillae on the tepals. The plant typically reaches a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, supporting several funnel-shaped blossoms per stem. Its tepals exhibit a distinct texture due to the presence of small, nipple-like projections that give the species its name. The floral color often transitions from a creamy yellow to a deep orange, frequently decorated with dark spots. These morphological traits distinguish it from other members of the genus within its specific habitat.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of central and western China, specifically within provinces such as Sichuan, Yunnan, Guizhou, and Gansu. It thrives in high-altitude habitats where it grows amidst forest margins and rocky slopes. The species is typically found at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters above sea level. These specific ecological niches provide the cool, moist environments necessary for its survival. Its distribution remains concentrated within these subtropical highland zones across East Asia.
