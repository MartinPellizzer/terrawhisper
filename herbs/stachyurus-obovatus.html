Stachyurus obovatus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stachyurus obovatus has obovate to elliptical leaves that are typically 2 to 5 centimeters long, featuring entire margins and an acute or obtuse apex. The foliage is characterized by a glabrous surface and a distinctively leathery texture. Its woody stems are slender and often display a slightly rough bark as the plant matures. The flowers are small, yellowish-white, and emerge from axillary clusters during the spring season. These blossoms are supported by delicate pedicels that hang gracefully from the branches.
