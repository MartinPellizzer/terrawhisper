Hexalobus crispiflorus

What's the taxonomical classification of Hexalobus crispiflorus?

Hexalobus crispiflorus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Magnoliales. It is further categorized under the family Annonaceae, which places it within the genus Hexalobus, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species crispiflorus.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Hexalobus crispiflorus contains specific bioactive secondary metabolites including triterpenoids, flavonoids, and various phenolic acids that contribute to its pharmacological properties. These chemical constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's documented antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities. Research suggests that the triterpene fractions may play a significant role in modulating cellular responses to oxidative stress. Furthermore, the presence of diverse phenolic compounds supports its potential use in traditional medicinal applications for treating inflammation. The complex synergy between these different molecular groups defines the plant's overall therapeutic profile.

What are the therapeutic actions of Hexalobus crispiflorus?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat respiratory ailments, skin infections, gastrointestinal disorders, and inflammatory conditions. It contains bioactive compounds that help alleviate symptoms of coughs and asthma through its medicinal properties. When applied topically, the extracts are often utilized to soothe skin irritations and heal minor wounds. Additionally, traditional practitioners employ it to settle stomach upsets and reduce internal swelling. These diverse therapeutic applications make it a significant resource in folk medicine.
