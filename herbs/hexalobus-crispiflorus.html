Hexalobus crispiflorus

What's the taxonomical classification of Hexalobus crispiflorus?

Hexalobus crispiflorus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Magnoliales. It is further categorized under the family Annonaceae, which places it within the genus Hexalobus, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species crispiflorus.

What are the pharmacological activities of Hexalobus crispiflorus?

This plant has anti-plasmodial pharmacological activity, which means it possesses the ability to inhibit or destroy Plasmodium parasites responsible for causing malaria. Researchers focus on these bioactive compounds to identify potential natural treatments for malaria infections. The plant contains specific phytochemicals that disrupt the life cycle of the parasite within the host. This targeted action makes it a significant subject of study in ethnopharmacology and drug development. Continued investigation into its chemical constituents could lead to the discovery of new antimalarial medications.

As discussed in a study, the essential oil extracted from Hexalobus crispiflorus was obtained with a yield of 0.1% relative to the dried material weight. This oil was evaluated regarding its anti-plasmodial activity against the W2 strain of Plasmodium falciparum. Among the oils tested, the oil of Hexalobus crispiflorus was the most effective. It demonstrated activity in culture with an IC50 of 2 microg/ml. Analysis via gas chromatography and mass spectrometry identified mainly terpenoids in the oil, such as alpha-copaene, gamma-cadinene, delta-cadinene, alpha-cadinol, spathulenol and caryophyllene oxide.
