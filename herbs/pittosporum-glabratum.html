Pittosporum glabratum

What's the taxonomical classification of Pittosporum glabratum?

Pittosporum glabratum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is identified as Equisetopsida, falling under the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Apiales and into the family Pittosporaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Pittosporum, specifically within the species glabratum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pittosporum glabratum has an evergreen shrub morphology characterized by slender, spreading branches and elliptical to lanceolate leaves that are typically glabrous and leathery in texture. The foliage is arranged in an alternate pattern along the stems, often displaying a deep green hue on the upper surface. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils, eventually giving way to fleshy, globose capsules that split open to reveal bright orange or red seeds. These seeds are often surrounded by a sticky, resinous pulp that aids in avian dispersal. The overall growth habit is compact yet irregular, allowing it to thrive in diverse understory environments.

As discussed in a study, the roots of Pittosporum glabratum Lindl. have been used as a folk medicine for the treatment of rheumatic arthritis, insomnia and hypertension. Only a few chemical or biological studies on P. glabratum have been reported. As part of ongoing phytochemical research, 4 compounds were isolated from the plant. These structures were identified as 3beta, 6beta, 19alpha, 21alpha, 24-pentahydroxy-12-en-28-oleanolic acid (1), 3-O-beta-D-glucuronopyranosyl-28-O-beta-D-glucopyranosyl siaresinolic acid (2), 3, 4, 5-trimethoxyphenyl-1-O-beta-D-(5-O-syringoyl)-apiofuranosyl-(1 --> 6)-beta-D-glucopyranoside (3) and 3, 4, 5-trimethoxyphenol-1-O-beta-D-apiofuranosyl-(1 --> 6)-beta-D-glucopyranoside (4). Among these, compound 1 is a new triterpenoid, while compounds 2-4 are isolated from the genus for the first time.
