What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Argyreia henryi has large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 20 centimeters in width, featuring prominent veining and a velvety texture. These climbing vines produce clusters of fragrant, pale lilac or white flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. The floral structure consists of five petals that form a shallow funnel shape. As the plant matures, it develops small, dark berries that serve as a primary reproductive feature. The stems are flexible and capable of twining around surrounding vegetation to support its growth habit.
