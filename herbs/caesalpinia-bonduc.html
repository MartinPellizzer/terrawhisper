What is Bonduc Tree (Caesalpinia Bonduc)?

The Bonduc Tree (Caesalpinia bonduc) is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia, commonly used in traditional herbal medicine for its medicinal properties and various therapeutic applications.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Bonduc Tree is Caesalpinia bonduc. This plant belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Fabales, order Fabales, family Fabaceae, genus Caesalpinia, and species Caesalpinia bonduc. It is commonly known as Bonduc Tree in India and as Red Bean Tree in Southeast Asia. The plant is also known by the synonyms Botanical: Caesalpinia bonduc and regional: Kudamalai in Tamil, Kudumalai in Malayalam, and Kudamalai in Telugu.

Plant Description and Morphology

Bonduc Tree is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows up to 5 meters tall, with a dense, spreading crown and a short, often branched trunk.

Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets arranged along a central axis. The flowers are bright yellow, with a distinctive red calyx, and are clustered in dense, terminal panicles. The root system is fibrous and shallow, allowing the plant to thrive in well-drained soils.

The bark is rough and dark brown, while the leaves are smooth and slightly glossy, and the seeds are encased in a hard, woody pod.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Bonduc Tree is native to tropical regions of South and Southeast Asia, including countries like India, Sri Lanka, Indonesia, and Malaysia.

It is commonly found in lowland forests, riverine areas, and secondary growth habitats. The tree thrives in warm, humid climates with well-distributed rainfall throughout the year. It prefers loose, fertile soils and can tolerate moderate drought conditions.
