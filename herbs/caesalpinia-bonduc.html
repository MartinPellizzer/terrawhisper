Caesalpinia bonduc

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Caesalpinia bonduc has pinnately compound leaves with numerous small, oblong leaflets and bright yellow flowers that emerge from woody, spreading branches. The foliage is characterized by a delicate, feathery texture that provides a lush appearance to the shrub. Its woody stems are often slightly thorny, serving as a protective mechanism for the plant. The inflorescences are typically arranged in racemes, showcasing vibrant petals that attract various pollinators. Small, legume-like pods develop following successful fertilization to facilitate seed dispersal.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Caesalpinia bonduc targets various enzymes and inflammatory mediators, most notably cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2), inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS), and various pro-inflammatory cytokines like TNF-alpha and IL-6. Its bioactive phytochemicals, such as flavonoids and tannins, inhibit these proteins to reduce oxidative stress and systemic inflammation. The plant also interacts with the arachidonic acid metabolism pathway by suppressing the activity of lipoxygenase enzymes. Through these specific molecular interactions, the plant exerts potent antioxidant, antimicrobial, and wound-healing properties. These multi-target mechanisms explain its traditional use in treating inflammatory skin conditions and infections.
