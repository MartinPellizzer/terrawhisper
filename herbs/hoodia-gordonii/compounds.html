Hoodia (Hoodia gordonii) Compounds

Hoodia gordonii contains several primary compounds, including alkaloids, terpenoids, and flavonoids. These compounds contribute to its traditional use in herbal medicine, particularly for appetite suppression and energy enhancement. Other notable components are glycosides, phenolic acids, and coumarins, which may support its medicinal properties.

Hoodia (Hoodia gordonii) Monograph

1. Alkaloid

Hoodia gordonii contains the alkaloid named P57. This compound is known for its potential appetite suppressant properties, which have been studied for weight management applications. It works by affecting the hypothalamus, reducing hunger signals to the brain. P57 is a key active compound in Hoodia, contributing to its traditional and modern medicinal uses.
