Pogonatherum paniceum

What's the taxonomical classification of Pogonatherum paniceum?

Pogonatherum paniceum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Poales and is a member of the family Poaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identification is defined by the genus Pogonatherum and the species paniceum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pogonatherum paniceum has an erect, slender stem that typically reaches heights of 20 to 50 centimeters and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves.

The foliage consists of simple, lanceolate leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its inflorescence is a terminal panicle composed of numerous small, inconspicuous flowers. These flowers are primarily white or pale in color and possess a relatively simple structure common to the family.

The plant also develops a branched root system that helps stabilize its upright growth in various soil types.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Pogonatherum paniceum can cause severe skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and potential gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Contact with the plant's sap often leads to redness, itching, and painful blistering on the skin. Some individuals may experience swelling or hives as a systemic allergic reaction to its chemical compounds. If accidentally consumed, the plant may induce nausea, vomiting, or abdominal cramping. Long-term exposure to its toxins should be avoided to prevent chronic sensitivity.
