Hemigraphis procumbens

What's the taxonomical classification of Hemigraphis procumbens?

Hemigraphis procumbens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further classified under the order Lamiales. This plant is a member of the family Acanthaceae and falls under the genus Hemigraphis, with its specific biological designation being the species procumbens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hemigraphis procumbens has decumbent to creeping stems that produce ovate, dark green leaves with distinct purple undersides and serrated margins.

These leaves are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the succulent, reddish-tinted stems. The foliage often exhibits prominent venation that contributes to its textured appearance. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils in compact inflorescences.

The plant's growth habit allows it to spread rapidly across the soil surface through stoloniferous stems.
