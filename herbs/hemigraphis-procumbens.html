Hemigraphis procumbens

What's the taxonomical classification of Hemigraphis procumbens?

Hemigraphis procumbens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further classified under the order Lamiales. This plant is a member of the family Acanthaceae and falls under the genus Hemigraphis, with its specific biological designation being the species procumbens.

Hemigraphis procumbens contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, tannins, and terpenoids. What are the therapeutic actions of Hemigraphis procumbens?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin diseases, wounds, inflammation, and various types of digestive disorders. Its medicinal properties are frequently applied topically to soothe skin irritations and accelerate the healing of physical injuries. In traditional practices, the plant is often consumed or prepared in specific ways to address internal ailments such as dysentery or stomach discomfort. The leaves contain bioactive compounds that contribute to its ability to reduce swelling and manage pain. These therapeutic applications make it a significant component in many herbal medicine traditions.
