Hemigraphis procumbens

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hemigraphis procumbens has decumbent to creeping stems that produce ovate, dark green leaves with distinct purple undersides and serrated margins. These leaves are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the succulent, reddish-tinted stems. The foliage often exhibits prominent venation that contributes to its textured appearance. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils in compact inflorescences. The plant's growth habit allows it to spread rapidly across the soil surface through stoloniferous stems.
