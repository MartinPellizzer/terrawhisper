What is Lyonia affinis?

Lyonia affinis is a shrub species in the family Ericaceae, native to East Asia.

It grows 1-2 meters tall, with a woody stem and alternate, elliptical leaves that are 2-6 cm long and 1-3 cm wide. The leaves are dark green on the upper surface and pale beneath, with a short petiole and an entire margin. The plant produces white or pinkish flowers in a terminal corymb, with 5-6 petals and 10-20 stamens, followed by red or purple berries that are 6-8 mm in diameter.

The species is typically found in forests, thickets, and mountainous areas at elevations of 800-2,400 meters.
