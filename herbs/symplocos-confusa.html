What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Symplocos confusa has an evergreen woody shrub or small tree morphology characterized by simple, alternate, and leathery leaves that are elliptical to oblong in shape. The foliage typically features entire margins and a glossy upper surface with a paler underside. Its stems are often slender and can become woody with age, supporting a dense canopy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually borne in axillary clusters. These blooms eventually give way to small, fleshy drupes that serve as the fruit of the species.
