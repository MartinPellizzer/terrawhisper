Urtica cannabina

What's the taxonomical classification of Urtica cannabina?

Urtica cannabina belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Rosales. Furthermore, it is a member of the family Urticaceae and is situated under the genus Urtica, ultimately defining its specific identity as the species cannabina.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Urtica cannabinna has an erect, perennial herbaceous habit characterized by opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins and a stem densely covered in stinging trichomes. The foliage typically displays a dark green coloration and a prominent venation pattern that extends toward the leaf edges. Its stems are relatively slender yet sturdy enough to support the plant's upright growth through various life stages. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in drooping clusters called panicles that emerge from the leaf axils. These reproductive structures are often greenish in hue and lack showy petals, relying instead on wind for pollination.
