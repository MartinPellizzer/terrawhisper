What's the taxonomical classification of Lagotis brevituba?

Lagotis brevituba belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Lamiales. This plant is a member of the family Plantaginaceae, specifically categorized within the genus Lagotis, with its unique designation as the species brevituba.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lagotis brevituba has a creeping rhizome structure supporting small, linear to lanceolate leaves that are typically dark green and somewhat fleshy. Its sporangiophores are arranged in distinct clusters along the stems to facilitate spore production. The scale-like leaves are closely imbricated, creating a tight, overlapping pattern along the axes. These morphological traits allow the species to thrive in moist, shaded environments where it can spread efficiently.

What are the pharmacological activities of Lagotis brevituba?

This plant has antioxidant pharmacological activity that helps neutralize harmful free radicals in biological systems. These antioxidant properties are essential for protecting cells from oxidative stress and subsequent cellular damage. By scavenging reactive oxygen species, the plant's chemical constituents may support overall cellular integrity. Such activity is often linked to the presence of specific secondary metabolites within its structure. This biochemical mechanism plays a key role in the plant's overall therapeutic potential.

According to a study published by "Evidence-based complementary and alternative medicine : eCAM", Lagotis brevituba Maxim has been used for more than 2000 years as a traditional Tibetan medicinal plant. In vitro antioxidant assays showed that the water extract possessed the strongest reducing power, ABTS radical, and DPPH radical scavenging activity, while the methanol extract was more potent in the beta-carotene/linoleic acid cooxidation assay. In vivo, mice exposed to 6.0 Gy 60Co gamma-ray whole-body radiation showed a dose-dependent decrease in malondialdehyde levels after administration of the plant. A total of 44 compounds were tentatively identified via LC-ESI-QTOF-MS/MS, consisting of 19 flavonoids, 14 phenols, 8 phenylethanoid glycosides, 2 iridoid glycosides, and 1 carbohydrate. This study also obtained 25 compounds from plants in the genus Lagotis for the first time.
