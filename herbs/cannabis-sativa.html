Hemp

Cannabis sativa

Hemp (Cannabis sativa) is a versatile plant with a long history of use in medicine, agriculture, and industry, known for its wide range of applications and potential therapeutic benefits.

Botanical Identification

Cannabis sativa is a species in the Cannabaceae family. Common names include hemp, marijuana, and cannabis, though these terms often overlap. It is an annual plant native to Central Asia, with tall, fibrous stalks, broad leaves, and green flowers. Synonyms in herbal literature include C. indica and C. ruderalis, though these are distinct species.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Spermatophyta
  • Class: caryophyllopsida
  • Order: Urticales
  • Family: Cannabaceae
  • Genus: Cannabis
  • Species: Cannabis sativa

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for anxiety and insomnia due to its calming properties.

Tincture: Used for pain relief and inflammation due to its active compounds.

Oil infusion: Used for topical pain relief and skin conditions.

Safety Profile

Hemp (Cannabis sativa) is generally considered safe when used appropriately. However, it may cause mild side effects such as drowsiness or dry mouth. It is contraindicated for individuals with certain mental health conditions or those taking specific medications due to potential interactions. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
