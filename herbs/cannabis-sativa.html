Hemp

Cannabis sativa

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Cannabis sativa is a species in the Cannabaceae family. Common names include hemp, marijuana, and cannabis, though these terms often overlap. It is an annual plant native to Central Asia, with tall, fibrous stalks, broad leaves, and green flowers. Synonyms in herbal literature include C. indica and C. ruderalis, though these are distinct species.

Active Compounds

Hemp contains key active compounds such as alkaloids, terpenoids, and essential oils. These bioactive components work synergistically to influence physiological processes. Their combined action enhances therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

  • Alkaloid
  • Terpenoid
  • Essential oil

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action
Nervous Chronic pain, sleep disorders, anxiety, mood disorders, seizures Analgesic, sedative, anxiolytic, mood stabilizer, anticonvulsant
Immune Inflammation, inflammatory diseases Anti-inflammatory
Gastrointestinal Nausea Antiemetic

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for anxiety and insomnia due to its calming properties.

Tincture: Used for pain relief and inflammation due to its active compounds.

Oil infusion: Used for topical pain relief and skin conditions.

Safety Profile

Hemp (Cannabis sativa) is generally considered safe when used appropriately. However, it may cause mild side effects such as drowsiness or dry mouth. It is contraindicated for individuals with certain mental health conditions or those taking specific medications due to potential interactions. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
