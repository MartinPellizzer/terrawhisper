Benzoin

Styrax benzoin

Benzoin (Styrax benzoin) is a resinous substance derived from the bark and roots of the evergreen tree Styrax benzoin, native to Southeast Asia. It has been traditionally used for its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and astringent properties, and is commonly employed to treat respiratory conditions such as coughs, colds, and bronchitis.

Benzoin (Styrax benzoin) is a species within the genus Styrax of the family Styracaceae, classified under the order Saxifragales, class Magnoliopsida, division Magnoliophyta, and kingdom Plantae.

Benzoin (Styrax benzoin) has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its antiseptic, astringent, and vulnerary properties, particularly to treat wounds, skin infections, and respiratory conditions such as coughs and colds.

In traditional medicinal systems such as Ayurveda, Traditional Chinese Medicine, and Indigenous practices in Southeast Asia, Benzoin (Styrax benzoin) has been historically used for its purported healing properties.
