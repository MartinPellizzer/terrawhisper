Trichosanthes lepiniana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Trichosanthes lepiniana has prostrate or climbing herbaceous stems that are often branched and can reach lengths of several decimeters. The leaves are typically ovate or lanceolate in shape with entire or slightly wavy margins and are arranged alternately along the stem. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in clusters, often appearing in the axils of the leaves. The plant features a specialized climbing mechanism through its tendrils which emerge from the nodes to assist in upward growth. Its reproductive structures culminate in small fruits that are characteristic of the Cucurbitaceae family.
