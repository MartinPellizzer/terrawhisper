Ligusticum brachylobum

What's the taxonomical classification of Ligusticum brachylobum?

Ligusticum brachylobum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Apiales and is a member of the family Apiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Ligusticum, and its unique species name, brachylobum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ligusticum brachylobum has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by large, compound leaves and a robust rootstock. The stems are relatively thick and can support the weight of the expansive canopy during peak growth. What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

These populations are typically found at elevations ranging from middle to high montane zones. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds anomalin, ferulic acid, nodakenin, seliniidin, and umbelliferone. These specific phytochemicals contribute to the diverse therapeutic profile of Ligusticum brachylobum. Research suggests that these bioactive molecules may offer significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The presence of ferulic acid and umbelliferone is particularly noted for their potential roles in protecting cellular health. Studying the synergy between these constituents helps scientists understand the plant's overall pharmacological efficacy.

According to a study published by "Zhongguo Zhong yao za zhi = Zhongguo zhongyao zazhi = China journal of Chinese materia medica", 5 compounds were isolated and identified from the roots of Ligusticum brachylobum. These identified medicinal compounds include umbelliferone, selinidin, anomalin, nodakenin and ferulic acid. The plant is used as a substitute for traditional Chinese medicine Radix Peucedani in the southwest of China.
