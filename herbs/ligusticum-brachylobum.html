Ligusticum brachylobum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ligusticum brachylobum has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by large, compound leaves and a robust rootstock. The foliage consists of pinnate leaves with broad, serrated leaflets that provide a lush texture to the plant. Its flowering structure features umbels that emerge from the leaf axils to attract various pollinators. The stems are relatively thick and can support the weight of the expansive canopy during peak growth. Small, inconspicuous flowers eventually develop into schizocarps that facilitate the plant's reproduction.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the mountainous regions of southern China, specifically within the provinces of Yunnan and Guizhou. These populations are typically found at elevations ranging from middle to high montane zones. The specific microclimates of these subtropical highland areas limit its natural expansion to these localized pockets.         What side effects this plant can have?

        This plant can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and digestive upset if ingested or handled improperly. Contact with the plant's oils may lead to phytophotodermatitis, a condition where skin becomes hypersensitive to sunlight and develops blisters or rashes. Some individuals might experience gastrointestinal distress such as nausea or stomach pain after consumption. It is also possible for sensitive people to develop contact dermatitis upon touching the foliage. Always exercise caution and consult a medical professional if any adverse symptoms occur.
