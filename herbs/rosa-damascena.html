Damask Rose (Rosa damascena)

Damask Rose (Rosa damascena) is a member of the Rosaceae family, native to Middle East, Southern Europe, and Central Asia. Traditionally, its flowers, leaves, and bark have been used for infusions, oil infusions, and herbal baths.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, tonic, and sedative actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and ayurvedic medicine.

Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height

Leaves
Simple leaves with upper surface pale green and lower surface darker green, bearing distinct stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Flowers are zygomorphic with five petals, two of which are lip-like and enlarged, and a single superior ovary with five stamens arranged in two whorls
Stems
Erect, woody, simple branching, glabrous surface, angular nodes, opposite phyllotaxis.
