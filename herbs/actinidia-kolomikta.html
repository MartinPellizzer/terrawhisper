Actinidia kolomikta

What's the taxonomical classification of Actinidia kolomikta?

Actinidia kolomikta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It follows the order Ericales and is a member of the family Actinidiaceae. More specifically, the plant is categorized under the genus Actinidia, with its specific designation being the species kolomikta.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Actinidia kolomikta has large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to twenty centimeters in length and possess long, drooping petioles. These deciduous vines produce striking, creamy white flowers with numerous prominent stamens that bloom in late spring. The foliage often takes on a deep bronze or reddish hue during the growing season, adding significant ornamental value. Following pollination, the plant develops small, oval-shaped berries that transition from green to a translucent yellowish color when ripe. Its woody stems are capable of climbing through surrounding vegetation using specialized twining mechanisms.

What are the pharmacological activities of Actinidia kolomikta?

This plant has antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, anti-hyperglycemic, anti-proliferative, and antitumor pharmacological activities. Its diverse bioactive compounds work to neutralize free radicals and reduce systemic inflammation within the body. These chemical properties also allow the plant to assist in regulating blood glucose levels, making it relevant for metabolic health. Furthermore, the specimen demonstrates significant potential in oncology through its ability to inhibit cell growth and combat tumor development. These combined effects highlight the plant's broad therapeutic potential in modern pharmacology.

As discussed in a study, the ethanol extract from Actinidia kolomikta root shows concentration-dependent inhibition effects on rat intestinal maltase and sucrase with IC50 values of 1.83 mg/mL and 1.03 mg/mL, respectively. This extract contains high amounts of total phenolics at 80.49 ± 0.05 mg GAE/g extract and total flavonoids at 430.69 ± 0.91 mg RE/g extract. In normal rats loaded with maltose, sucrose, and glucose, administration of the extract significantly reduced postprandial hyperglycemia in a manner similar to acarbose. These anti-hyperglycemic effects may be attributable to the high contents of phenolic and flavonoid compounds and are associated with the inhibition of α-glucosidase. The study suggests these activities might also involve the improvement of insulin release and/or insulin sensitivity.

As discussed in a study, the leaves of Actinidia kolomikta contain the medicinal compound actinoside C. The separation of this compound was performed at 25 degrees C using a ZORBAX Extend C18 column measuring 4.6 mm x 250 mm with 5 microm particles. A mobile phase consisting of a methanol and water mixture at a 51:49 ratio was used with a flow rate of 1.2 mL x min(-1) and a measurement wavelength of 267 nm. The content of actinoside C in Actinidia kolomikta varies by growth period, reaching its highest level in the middle ten days of June. After this peak, the content decreases slightly in the middle ten days of July before reaching its lowest content in the middle ten days of August. Therefore, the optimal collective date for Actinidia kolomikta is in the middle ten days of June.
