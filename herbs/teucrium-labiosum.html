Teucrium labiosum

What's the taxonomical classification of Teucrium labiosum?

Teucrium labiosum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Lamiales. Its taxonomic journey continues through the family Lamiaceae, where it is placed within the genus Teucrium, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species labiosum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Teucrium labiosum has a woody, perennial habit characterized by small, elliptical leaves with serrated margins and a dense, shrubby growth pattern. Its flowers are bilabiate, meaning they possess a distinct two-lipped structure, which is a hallmark of the Lamiaceae family. These small blossoms often emerge in clusters along the stems during the blooming season. The overall architecture of the plant is compact, making it well-adapted to rocky or arid environments.

According to a study published by "Zhong yao cai = Zhongyaocai = Journal of Chinese medicinal materials", six compounds were obtained and identified from Teucrium labiosum. These constituents include stearic acid (I), alpha-spinasterol (II), Methyl phaeophorbide a (III), friedelino (IV), lupeol (V), and stigmasta-5,22-dien-3beta-ol (VI). The isolation and purification of these components were achieved through silica gel column and Sephadex LH-20 chromatography. All 6 of these compounds are obtained from this plant for the first time.
