Teucrium labiosum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Teucrium labiosum has a woody, perennial habit characterized by small, elliptical leaves with serrated margins and a dense, shrubby growth pattern. The foliage typically exhibits a grayish-green hue and a slightly pubescent texture on the leaf surfaces. Its flowers are bilabiate, meaning they possess a distinct two-lipped structure, which is a hallmark of the Lamiaceae family. These small blossoms often emerge in clusters along the stems during the blooming season. The overall architecture of the plant is compact, making it well-adapted to rocky or arid environments.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Teucrium labiosum can cause liver toxicity, skin irritation, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested or applied improperly. Many species within the Teucrium genus contain hepatotoxic compounds that may lead to significant liver damage in humans and animals. Contact with the plant's foliage or stems can result in localized dermatitis or allergic reactions on sensitive skin. Ingestion might also trigger symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. Due to these potential risks, careful handling and avoidance of consumption are highly recommended.
