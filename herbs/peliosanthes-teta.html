What's the taxonomical classification of Peliosanthes teta?

Peliosanthes teta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Asparagales and the family Asparagaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Peliosanthes, and its unique species designation, teta.

      What are the herbal preparations of Peliosanthes teta?

      Peliosanthes teta is prepared primarily as a decoction or an infusion made from its dried leaves and whole aerial parts to extract its medicinal properties. Practitioners often boil the plant material in water to create a potent liquid extract used for internal consumption. In some traditional applications, the plant is also processed into a topical poultice by crushing the fresh leaves. This paste is applied directly to the skin to address localized inflammation or minor wounds. Some preparations may involve macerating the plant in alcohol to create a concentrated tincture for long-term storage.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Peliosanthes teta can cause skin irritation, localized redness, and allergic dermatitis upon direct contact with the plant's tissues. Some individuals may experience itching or a burning sensation if the sap touches sensitive areas of the body. While systemic toxicity is not widely documented, accidental ingestion could lead to gastrointestinal distress or nausea. It is important to handle the plant with care to avoid unpredictable reactions. Always monitor for swelling or spreading rashes after exposure to the foliage.
