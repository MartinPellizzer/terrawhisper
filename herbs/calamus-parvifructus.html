What is Calamus parvifructus?

Calamus parvifructus is a species of plant belonging to the family Arecaceae.

It has rhizomes that are stoloniferous and produce inflorescences that are panicles, each bearing small, yellowish flowers. The plant's fruit is a drupe, approximately 1-1.5 cm in length, with a single seed. Calamus parvifructus is native to Southeast Asia, and its rhizomes are used for medicinal and other purposes.

The plant's leaves are pinnate, with leaflets that are lanceolate and have a pointed apex.
