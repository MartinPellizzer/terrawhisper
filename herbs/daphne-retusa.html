Daphne retusa

What's the taxonomical classification of Daphne retusa?

Daphne retusa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Malvales and the family Thymelaeaceae. Ultimately, this plant is identified by its genus, Daphne, and its specific species designation, retusa.

What are the therapeutic actions of Daphne retusa?

Daphne retusa has medicinal properties that primarily include its use as an analgesic, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial agent in traditional herbal practices. The plant contains various bioactive secondary metabolites that interact with biological pathways to reduce pain and swelling. These chemical compounds are often utilized to treat skin irritations or minor infections when applied topically. Some traditional applications also suggest its utility in managing digestive disturbances through careful dosage. Because of its potent chemical makeup, the plant must be handled with caution to avoid toxicity.
