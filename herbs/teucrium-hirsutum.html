What is Teucrium hirsutum?

Teucrium hirsutum is a perennial herbaceous plant that belongs to the family Lamiaceae.

It has a hairy stem, typically 30-60 cm tall, with a four-angled, green, and pubescent calyx. The leaves are 3-5 cm long, elliptical, and pubescent, with serrated margins. The inflorescence is a terminal spike, bearing purple flowers that are bilabiate and 4-6 mm long, with two stamens and a 4-lobed stigma.

The plant produces small, brown, and pubescent fruits that are 2-3 mm long, containing two seeds.
