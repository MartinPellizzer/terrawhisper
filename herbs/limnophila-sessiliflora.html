Limnophila sessiliflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Limnophila sessiliflora?

Limnophila sessiliflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant falls under the order Lamiales. Its taxonomic journey continues through the family Plantaginaceae, which contains the genus Limnophila, ultimately leading to its specific designation as the species sessiliflora.

      Limnophila sessiliflora is prepared primarily as an aqueous infusion, a decoction, or a topical tincture derived from its dried vegetative parts.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Limnophilas sessiliflora can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or digestive distress if ingested by humans or animals. While primarily used as an aquatic plant in aquariums, direct contact with the plant's tissues may trigger dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Contact with the sap or broken leaves can lead to localized redness or itching on the skin. If accidentally swallowed, it may cause nausea, stomach cramps, or more severe gastrointestinal discomfort. It is important to handle the plant with caution to avoid these adverse physical responses.
