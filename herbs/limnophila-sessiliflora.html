What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Limnophila sessiliflora has a submerged, branched stem structure with small, opposite, lanceolate leaves that lack petioles. Its leaves are typically arranged in whorls or opposite pairs along the stem to maximize light absorption. The species is distinguished by its sessile flowers, which attach directly to the stem without a stalk. These morphological traits enable the plant to thrive in slow-moving or stagnant freshwater environments.
