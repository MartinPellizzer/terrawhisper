What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

The bark on mature specimens is thick and deeply fissured, providing a rough texture that contrasts with the delicate foliage. What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

What are the therapeutic actions of Salix babylonica?

Salix babylonica has analgesic, antipyretic, and anti-inflammatory properties derived primarily from the presence of salicin within its bark. This chemical compound acts as a precursor to salicylic acid, which inhibits the production of prostaglandins in the body. By modulating these inflammatory mediators, the plant effectively reduces pain and swelling in various tissues. Additionally, its ability to regulate the body's temperature control centers makes it a useful agent for reducing fevers. These combined actions make the willow species a fundamental component in the development of many modern medicinal treatments.

This plant can be prepared as a decoction by boiling the bark in water to extract salicin, a tincture by soaking the bark in alcohol, an extract through concentrated solvent processes, an infusion using hot water on softer parts, or a powder made from dried bark. What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause an increased bleeding risk, nausea, vomiting, stomach pain, gastric irritation, tinnitus, allergic reactions, dizziness, and headache. These adverse effects are primarily due to the presence of salicin, which functions similarly to aspirin in the human body. Consuming the bark may lead to significant gastric irritation and digestive distress such as stomach pain or vomiting. Some individuals might experience neurological symptoms including tinnitus, dizziness, or a persistent headache. Furthermore, people sensitive to salicylates should be cautious as they may suffer from various allergic reactions.
