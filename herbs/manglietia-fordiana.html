Manglietia fordiana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Manglietia fordiana has large, elliptical leaves that measure up to 25 centimeters in length and are characterized by a glabrous surface and prominent pinnate venation. The tree exhibits a spreading canopy with sturdy, woody branches that support its dense foliage. Its flowers are typically solitary and emerge from the terminal ends of the branches, displaying a pale yellow to cream coloration. Each blossom features thick, fleshy petals that surround a central cluster of numerous stamens. The reproductive structures are adapted to attract specific pollinators through their distinct size and aromatic properties.
