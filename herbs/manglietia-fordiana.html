Manglietia fordiana

What's the taxonomical classification of Manglietia fordiana?

Manglietia fordiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its descent through the order Magnoliales, it is placed within the family Magnoliaceae. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its genus, Manglietia, and its specific species designation, fordiana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Manglietia fordiana has large, elliptical leaves that measure up to 25 centimeters in length and are characterized by a glabrous surface and prominent pinnate venation.

The tree exhibits a spreading canopy with sturdy, woody branches that support its dense foliage. Its flowers are typically solitary and emerge from the terminal ends of the branches, displaying a pale yellow to cream coloration. Each blossom features thick, fleshy petals that surround a central cluster of numerous stamens.

The reproductive structures are adapted to attract specific pollinators through their distinct size and aromatic properties.

      What are the herbal preparations of Manglietia fordiana?

      Manglietia fordiana is prepared as a decoction by boiling the dried flower buds or bark in water to extract its medicinal compounds. Because the specific toxicological profile of this species is not extensively documented in clinical studies, users should exercise extreme caution.
