Malaxis monophyllos

What's the taxonomical classification of Malaxis monophyllos?

Malaxis monophyllos belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further categorized under the order Asparagales. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family, specifically falling within the genus Malaxis, and is identified by its unique species name, monophyllos.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Malaxis monophyllos can cause localized skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential allergic reactions if ingested or touched directly. While the plant is generally considered non-toxic to most healthy adults, contact with the leaves may trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Some people might experience redness, itching, or a mild rash upon skin exposure. Ingestion of the plant parts could lead to nausea or stomach discomfort due to unknown chemical compounds. It is always prudent to avoid handling unknown vegetation to prevent these adverse reactions.
