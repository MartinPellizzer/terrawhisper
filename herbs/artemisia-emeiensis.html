Artemisia emeiensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Artemisia emeiensis?

Artemisia emeiensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. This plant is a representative of the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its complex inflorescences. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Artemisia, and its specific biological identity is defined by the species name emeiensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Artemisia emeiensis has a perennial growth habit characterized by woody caudices and silver-gray, finely dissected leaves that are deeply lobed or pinnatifid. The foliage typically exhibits a dense covering of fine hairs, providing a characteristic felt-like texture and a muted coloration. Its flowering structures consist of small, inconspicuous heads arranged in terminal panicles or cymes. The stems are often erect and can become increasingly woody at the base as the plant matures. These morphological traits are specifically adapted to its native arid or rocky environments.
