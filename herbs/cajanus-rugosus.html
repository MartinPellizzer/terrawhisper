What is Cajanus rugosus?

Cajanus rugosus is a species of flowering plant in the family Fabaceae, native to tropical Africa.

It is an annual or perennial herb that grows up to 2 meters in height, with a woody base and branching stems. The leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are 2-5 centimeters long and have a wavy or lobed margin. The flowers are pink or red, in axillary clusters, with five petals and a standard petal that is longer than the others.

The plant produces flat, broad pods that are 3-5 centimeters long and contain 2-3 seeds.

What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

The main herbal preparations of Cajanus rugosus are decoction, infusion, tea, tincture, and cajanus tea.

Decoction involves boiling the roots and stems in water to extract its medicinal properties, particularly used for treating gastrointestinal issues and fever. Infusion is similar to decoction but uses hot water to extract the active compounds from the leaves and flowers, commonly used for its analgesic and anti-inflammatory effects. Tincture, a concentrated liquid extract, is prepared by soaking the plant material in a solvent such as ethanol or glycerin, typically used for its antiseptic and antimicrobial properties.

Cajanus tea, a specific herbal preparation made from the leaves of the plant, is traditionally used to treat digestive issues and as a natural remedy for fever reduction.
