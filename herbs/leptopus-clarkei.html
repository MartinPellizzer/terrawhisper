Leptopus clarkei

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Leptopus clarkei has woody stems, lanceolate leaves with entire margins, and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in axillary clusters. The plant typically presents as a deciduous shrub with a spreading growth habit. Its leaves are arranged oppositely along the branches and possess acute tips with smooth edges. The inflorescences consist of small, greenish or yellowish flowers that lack prominent petals. During the fruiting stage, the plant produces small berries that facilitate seed dispersal.
