Mallotus tenuifolius

What's the taxonomical classification of Mallotus tenuifolius?

Mallotus tenuifolius belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Malpighiales. This plant is further categorized into the family Euphorbiaceae, placing it within the genus Mallotus, and is specifically identified by its species name, tenuifolius.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Mallotus tenuifolius has simple, alternate, elliptical to oblong leaves characterized by finely serrated margins and a distinctively thin, coriaceous texture. The leaf bases are typically asymmetrical, while the apex varies from acute to obtuse depending on the specific specimen. Small, glandular hairs often populate the underside of the lamina, contributing to its unique tactile surface. The stems are woody and can develop a slightly pubescent coating during early growth stages. These morphological traits distinguish the species from other members of the Euphorbiaceae family found in similar habitats.
