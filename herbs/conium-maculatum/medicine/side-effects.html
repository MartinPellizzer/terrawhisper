When ingested or inhaled, this toxic compound binds to nicotinic acetylcholine receptors in the brain and spinal cord, disrupting normal neurotransmitter function and leading to abnormal electrical activity that triggers convulsions.

The severity of these seizures can range from mild tremors to full-blown grand mal seizures, depending on the dose and method of exposure.
