Lindera chunii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lindera chunii has small, lanceolate to ovate leaves that are arranged alternately along its woody stems and exhibit a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The foliage is typically a vibrant green color, though it may undergo seasonal changes in hue. Its flowering structures consist of tiny, inconspicuous blossoms that emerge from the axils of the leaves. These delicate flowers eventually give way to small, fleshy drupes that serve as the plant's fruit. The overall growth habit is that of a compact, spreading shrub with a fine-textured appearance.
