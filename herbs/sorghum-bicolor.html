Sorghum bicolor

What's the taxonomical classification of Sorghum bicolor?

Sorghum bicolor belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it falls under the order Poales and the family Poaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Sorghum, and its species name, bicolor.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Sorghum bicolor contains bioactive phenolic compounds, primarily including various types of tannins, flavonoids, and anthocyanins that offer significant therapeutic potential. These phytochemicals function as potent antioxidants that can neutralize free radicals and reduce systemic oxidative stress in the body. Specific studies suggest that these compounds possess anti-inflammatory, anti-diabetic, and antimicrobial properties. Some research even indicates that the extracts may help inhibit the growth of certain cancer cells during laboratory testing. Consequently, the plant is being investigated for its ability to mitigate chronic metabolic diseases through these natural chemical pathways.

What are the therapeutic actions of Sorghum bicolor?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various gastrointestinal conditions, including diarrhea, dysentery, and inflammatory bowel diseases. Its high concentration of bioactive phenolic compounds and antioxidants helps mitigate oxidative stress within the digestive tract. These phytochemicals work to reduce intestinal inflammation and protect the mucosal lining from damage. Additionally, extracts from the plant may assist in managing metabolic disorders by regulating blood glucose levels. The therapeutic potential of its extracts is being actively researched for its ability to combat systemic oxidative damage.
