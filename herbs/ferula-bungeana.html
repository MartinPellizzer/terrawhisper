Ferula bungeana

What's the taxonomical classification of Ferula bungeana?

Ferula bungeanana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is situated within the order Apiales. It is a prominent representative of the family Apiaceae, falling under the genus Ferula, with the specific designation of the species bungeanana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ferula bungeanana has a robust, perennial herbaceous structure characterized by thick, fleshy rhizomes and large, deeply pinnatifid leaves that emerge from a central basal rosette. The stems are erect and hollow, often reaching significant heights during the flowering stage. The leaves feature multiple pairs of leaflets that are divided into narrow, linear segments to reduce surface area. Large, compound umbels form at the apex of the stems, bearing numerous small, yellowish-green flowers. The overall morphology is adapted to arid environments through its substantial root system and specialized leaf architecture.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the mountainous regions of central and western China, specifically within provinces such as Gansu, Qinghai, Sichuan, and Shaanxi. It thrives primarily in high-altitude environments located on rocky slopes or within alpine meadows. These specific habitats provide the well-drained soil and intense sunlight necessary for its survival. The distribution is largely restricted by the rugged terrain and the unique climatic conditions of the Qinling and Qilian mountain ranges. Consequently, its occurrence is patchy rather than continuous across the landscape.

Ferula bunganea has therapeutic actions that include acting as a bitter, carminative, expectorant, emmenagogue, and stimulant.
