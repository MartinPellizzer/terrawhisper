Strychnos cathayensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Strychnos cathayensis has woody, climbing lianas characterized by smooth bark and large, simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical to oblong in shape. The foliage typically features prominent pinnate venation and a leathery texture designed for tropical environments. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often presenting in clusters with a pale coloration. These inflorescences eventually give rise to hard, globose fruits that house several seeds. The overall structure of the plant allows it to navigate forest canopies effectively using its flexible stems.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Strychnos cathayensis contains various indole alkaloids, most notably strychnine and brucine, which serve as the primary bioactive constituents. These complex chemical compounds act as potent neurotoxins by antagonizing glycine receptors in the central nervous system. Beyond these well-known toxins, the plant species often yields several structurally related alkaloids that have been studied for their unique pharmacological properties. Researchers investigate these specific molecules to understand their potential applications in neurological research and biochemical signaling. What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Strychnos cathayensis targets nicotinic acetylcholine receptors and voltage-gated sodium channels through its complex mixture of indole alkaloids. These alkaloids, such as strychnine-type derivatives, act as competitive antagonists at the neuromuscular junction to induce potent physiological responses. By binding to specific protein subunits within the postsynaptic membrane, these compounds inhibit excitatory neurotransmission. This interaction disrupts the normal signaling pathways required for muscle contraction and cellular excitability. Such precise molecular docking allows the plant's secondary metabolites to exert significant pharmacological influence on the central and peripheral nervous systems.

    What are the herbal preparations of Strychnos cathayensis?

    Strychnos cathayensis is prepared as a decoction, a powder, or a topical paste depending on the specific medicinal application. The bark and roots are often boiled in water to create a liquid extract used for internal consumption. In other instances, the plant material is dried and ground into a fine powder for easier administration. This powdered form can be mixed with water or ingested directly to manage various ailments. Additionally, the plant components may be crushed into a paste to be applied externally to the skin.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Strychnos cathayensis can cause severe neurological toxicity, muscle spasms, and respiratory failure due to the presence of potent alkaloids like strychnine. These toxic compounds act as antagonists to glycine receptors in the spinal cord and brainstem. When ingested, the alkaloids trigger uncontrolled and violent muscular contractions throughout the body. This intense physical reaction can lead to agonizing cramps and potential bone fractures. Ultimately, the physiological stress often results in death through asphyxiation because the respiratory muscles become paralyzed or exhausted.
