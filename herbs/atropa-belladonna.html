Atropa Belladonna Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Atropa belladonna, commonly known as deadly nightshade, is a toxic plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, characterized by its dark berries and bell-shaped flowers.

It contains potent bioactive constituents such as atropine, scopolamine, and hyoscyamine, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

Historically, it has been used for its anticholinergic properties to treat conditions like gastrointestinal spasms and Parkinson's disease, though it requires careful handling due to its toxicity.

Herbal preparations such as tinctures, infusions, and poultices can be made, but they should only be prepared and used under professional supervision.
