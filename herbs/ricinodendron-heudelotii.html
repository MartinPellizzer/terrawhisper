Ricinodendron heudelotii

What's the taxonomical classification of Ricinodendron heudelotii?

Ricinodendron heudelotii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Malpighiales. The plant is a specific member of the family Euphorbiaceae, falling under the genus Ricinodendron, with the species designation heudelotii.

Its branching pattern contributes to a compact shrubby or small tree-like growth habit within its forest habitat. What are the pharmacological activities of Ricinodendron heudelotii?

This plant has anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, antioxidant, and therapeutic properties used specifically for the treatment of dental abscess and toothache. Its potent antimicrobial activities allow it to combat various infectious pathogens that contribute to oral health issues. The antioxidant components help neutralize free radicals, while the anti-inflammatory effects reduce swelling and discomfort in the gums. These combined chemical properties make it a valuable traditional remedy for managing localized infections in the mouth. Consequently, the plant serves as an effective natural agent for alleviating acute dental pain and managing abscesses.

According to a study published by "Heliyon", the leaves of Ricinodendron heudelotii were extracted with ethanol and partitioned using petroleum ether, ethylacetate and butanol. The isolated amorphous tannin, corilagin, demonstrated antioxidant potential with an IC50 value of 0.003 mg/mL, which is comparable to the vitamin C standard of 0.001 mg/mL. Corilagin also showed a dose dependent scavenging effect against ABTS and DPPH radicals. Furthermore, the butanol and ethylacetate fractions exhibited significant (p < 0.05) nitric oxide inhibition of 60% and 69% respectively after treatment of RAW 264.7 macrophages with lipopolysaccharide. These findings show that the ethylacetate and butanol fractions suppress the expression of an inflammation mediator by inhibiting nitric oxide.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", medicinal plants are frequently used in popular medicine as remedies for many infectious diseases such as intestinal infection, malaria, and tuberculosis. This study screened 21 extracts from different plants, including the stem bark of Ricinodendron heudelotii, for their antimicrobial activity. The research investigated the effects of these extracts against 8 bacteria species and 1 yeast species. The bacteria species tested included Gram-positive bacteria like Staphylococcus aureus and Bacillus cereus, as well as Gram-negative bacteria such as Escherichia coli, Shigella dysenteriae, Shigella flexneri, Salmonella typhi, Pseudomonas aeruginosa, and Klebsiella pneumoniae. The yeast species tested was Candida albicans.
