What's the taxonomical classification of Ricinodendron heudelotii?

Ricinodendron heudelotii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Malpighiales. The plant is a specific member of the family Euphorbiaceae, falling under the genus Ricinodendron, with the species designation heudelotii.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compound coriaglin, which serves as a primary bioactive constituent. This specific glycoside is known for its potential therapeutic applications in various traditional medicine practices. Beyond coriaglin, the plant also houses a complex profile of secondary metabolites that contribute to its overall biological activity. These diverse chemical components work together to produce various physiological effects when processed. Researchers continue to study these individual elements to better understand their medicinal value.

As discussed in a study, the leaves of Ricinodendron heudelotii were extracted with ethanol and partitioned using petroleum ether, ethylacetate and butanol. The study isolated corilagin, an amorphous tannin, which demonstrated antioxidant potential with an IC50 value of 0.003 mg/mL compared to the vitamin C standard of 0.001 mg/mL. This isolated compound showed scavenging effects against ABTS and DPPH radicals in a dose dependent manner. Furthermore, the butanol and ethylacetate fractions of Ricinodendron heudelotii exhibited significant (p < 0.05) nitric oxide inhibition of 60% and 69% respectively. These fractions suppressed the expression of an inflammation mediator by inhibiting nitric oxide in RAW 264.7 macrophages treated with lipopolysaccharide.
