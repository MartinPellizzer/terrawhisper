What's the taxonomical classification of Zephyranthes candida?

Zephyranthes candida belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Asparagales. It is a member of the family Amaryllidaceae, which contains the genus Zephyranthes, and is specifically identified by the species name candida.

What are the pharmacological activities of Zephyranthes candida?

This plant has acetylcholinesterase inhibitory, anti-inflammatory, anti-tumoral, cytostatic, and cytotoxic pharmacological activities. These biological properties suggest that Zephyranthes candida contains potent bioactive compounds capable of modulating various physiological processes. Its acetylcholinesterase inhibitory action provides potential therapeutic value for treating neurodegenerative disorders. Furthermore, the plant demonstrates significant anti-inflammatory effects by reducing swelling and pain in biological models. The observed anti-tumoral, cytostatic, and cytotoxicity profiles indicate that its extracts can effectively inhibit the proliferation and induce death in cancer cells.

As discussed in a study, twenty-six structurally diverse alkaloids were isolated from whole plants of Zephyranthes candida, including ten undescribed compounds named zephyranine A-I and 6-O-ethylnerinine, as well as zephyranthine-6-one and 3-O-deacetyl-sternbergine. Zephyranine A, G, and H exhibited moderate acetylcholinesterase inhibitory activities with IC50 values of 8.2, 39.0, and 10.8 μM, respectively. Regarding anti-inflammatory activity on LPS-induced NO production in RAW264.7 mouse macrophages, zephyranine B, haemanthamine, haemanthidine, 11-hydroxyvittatine, and 8-demethoxy-10-O-methylhostasin exhibited potent effects. These anti-inflammatory IC50 values were 21.3, 4.6, 12.2, 5.6, and 17.4 μM, respectively. Potent acetylcholinesterase inhibitors required interactions with key Trp286 and Tyr337 residues.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds galanthamine, lycorine, plicamine, secoplicamine, and N-methylcrinasiadine. These alkaloids are primarily utilized in pharmacological research for their potential therapeutic properties. Galanthamine is particularly noted for its role in treating Alzheimer's disease due to its acetylcholinesterase inhibitory effects. Lycorine and plicamine are also studied for their various biological activities, including anti-inflammatory and antitumor potential. The presence of secoplicamine and N-methylcrinasiadine contributes to the complex chemical profile used in drug development studies.

As discussed in a study, Zephyranthes candida contains sixteen new alkaloids belonging to the galanthamine (1-6), plicamine (7-14), and secoplicamine (15 and 16) classes, as well as eight known analogues (17-24). The absolute configurations of alkaloids 1, 2, 7, 8, and 17 were confirmed via single-crystal X-ray diffraction analysis. Alkaloids 3, 12-14, and 18-21 exhibited anti-acetylcholinesterase activities with IC50 values ranging from 0.48 to 168.7 μM. Additionally, compounds 10-12, 14, and 16 showed in vitro anti-inflammatory activities with IC50 values ranging from 7.50 to 23.55 μM.
