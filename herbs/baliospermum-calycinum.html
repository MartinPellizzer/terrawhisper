Baliospermum calycinum

What's the taxonomical classification of Baliospermum calycinum?

Baliospermum calycinum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is positioned in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Malpighiales. This plant is a member of the family Euphorbiaceae, specifically within the genus Baliospermum, and is identified by its unique species name, calycinum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Baliospermum calycinum has woody, spreading stems with leaves that are pinnately compound, featuring several pairs of lanceolate leaflets arranged along a central rachis.

The foliage is characterized by a smooth texture and a bright green color that provides a dense canopy. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, typically displaying a subtle structure typical of the family. The calyx is a defining feature, often being larger or more prominent than in related species.

If ingested, the plant may lead to gastrointestinal distress such as nausea or stomach pain. There is also a potential risk of allergic reactions in individuals with specific sensitivities to its sap. It is important to handle this species with caution to avoid these adverse reactions.
