Ormosia fordiana has pinnately compound leaves with numerous small, oblong leaflets and woody stems characterized by a dense, spreading growth habit. The most distinctive morphological feature is the production of bright red, hard-coated seeds that contrast sharply against the dark woody structures.         What side effects this plant can have?

        This plant can cause severe toxicity through the ingestion of toxic proteins known as toxalbumins found within its seeds. These specialized proteins act similarly to ricin by inhibiting protein synthesis in human cells, which can lead to critical organ failure. If consumed, symptoms may include intense gastrointestinal distress such as vomiting, abdominal pain, and bloody diarrhea. In more extreme cases, the systemic effects can result in rapid dehydration, kidney damage, or even death. Because of these dangerous chemical properties, the seeds must never be eaten by humans or animals.
