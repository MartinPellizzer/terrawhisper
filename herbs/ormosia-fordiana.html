Ormosia fordiana

What's the taxonomical classification of Ormosia fordiana?

Ormosia fordiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is positioned within the order Fabales. It further falls under the family Fabaceae, a group well-known for its diverse legume members. Within this family, it is categorized under the genus Ormosia, specifically identified by its unique species name, fordiana.

      What are the herbal preparations of Ormosia fordiana?

      Ormosia fordiana is prepared as a decoction of the bark, a powder derived from the seeds, or a liquid infusion of the leaves to treat various ailments. The bark is typically boiled in water to extract its medicinal properties for topical or internal use. When the seeds are utilized, they are often ground into a fine powder and mixed with other substances to facilitate administration. Leaf infusions serve as a gentler alternative for those seeking a less concentrated preparation. These diverse forms allow for targeted application depending on the specific health concern being addressed.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Ormosia fordiana can cause severe poisoning if ingested because its seeds contain toxic alkaloids that disrupt biological functions. These chemical compounds can lead to significant gastrointestinal distress, including nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain. In more serious cases of exposure, the toxins may induce neurological symptoms or systemic toxicity. While the plant is primarily known for its ornamental value, the internal components pose a genuine risk to humans and animals. Proper handling is necessary to avoid accidental consumption of the seeds.
