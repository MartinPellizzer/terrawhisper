Ormosia fordiana

What's the taxonomical classification of Ormosia fordiana?

Ormosia fordiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is positioned within the order Fabales. It further falls under the family Fabaceae, a group well-known for its diverse legume members. Within this family, it is categorized under the genus Ormosia, specifically identified by its unique species name, fordiana.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin diseases such as leprosy, rashes, and fungal infections. The seeds are often prepared into a paste or decoction to apply directly onto the affected areas of the body. Some traditional practices also utilize the plant to address internal ailments related to digestive issues. Local healers rely on these specific medicinal properties to manage various dermatological conditions. The efficacy of the plant is documented within specific regional ethnobotanical studies.
