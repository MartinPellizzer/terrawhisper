Menyanthes trifoliata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Menyanthes trifoliata has trifoliate leaves consisting of three distinct leaflets that are lanceolate to elliptic in shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. These leaves are arranged alternately along a stem that can reach varying heights depending on the specific environmental conditions. The plant often features small, inconspicuous flowers that are characteristic of its genus. The root system is typically robust to support the structural integrity of the foliage. Its overall growth habit reflects a perennial nature suited for its native habitat.

Its distribution is closely tied to temperate climates found at elevations ranging from 2,500 to 4,000 meters above sea level.
