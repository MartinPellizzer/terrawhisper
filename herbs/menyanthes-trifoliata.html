Water pennywort

Menyanthes trifoliata

Water Pennywort (Menyanthes trifoliata) is a small flowering plant native to northern regions, known for its medicinal properties and historical use in treating respiratory and urinary tract conditions.

Botanical Identification

Water Pennywort, Menyanthes trifoliata, is a perennial herb in the family Menyanthaceae. Commonly known as water pennywort or broad-leaved water pennywort, it is native to northern temperate regions. The plant has trifoliate leaves and small white flowers, with a rhizomatous root system. It is also referred to as water sorrel in some herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Magnoliopsida
  • Order: Saxifragales
  • Family: Saxifragaceae
  • Genus: Menyanthes
  • Species: Menyanthes trifoliata

Active Compounds

Water Pennywort contains tannins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids as its main active compounds. These compounds contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. They work synergistically to support various therapeutic effects in traditional herbal medicine.

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory ailments such as coughs and bronchitis.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and as a general tonic.

Extract: Used for skin conditions and as an antiseptic.

Safety Profile

Water pennywort is generally safe when used in moderate amounts. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation due to potential risks. Long-term use may cause gastrointestinal discomfort, so it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before regular use.
