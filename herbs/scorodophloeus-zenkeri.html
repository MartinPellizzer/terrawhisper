As discussed in a study, the aqueous extract of the bark of Scorodophloeus zenkeri was administered at a dose of 300 mg/kg body weight to rats over a treatment period lasting 21 days. This extract limited weight loss and caused a reduction in blood sugar of -26.59% after the 21 days of treatment. The extract caused an increase in the activity of erythrocyte catalase and a reduction in the concentration of hepatic MDA. Furthermore, the extract caused a very marked reduction in inflammatory cells and CRP, while also causing a reduction in dyslipidemia materialized by a reduction in CRR, AC, and AIP and an increase in CPI. These findings suggest the extract contains bioactive compounds capable of reducing chronic hyperglycemia while preventing its complications.
