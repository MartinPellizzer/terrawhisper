Heterolamium debile

What's the taxonomical classification of Heterolamium debile?

Heterolamium debile belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its hierarchical lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Lamiales. Furthermore, this plant is a member of the family Lamiaceae, falling under the genus Heterolamium, and is specifically identified by its species name, debile.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Heterolamium debile has a perennial growth habit characterized by erect, slender stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 40 centimeters. The leaves are primarily basal and form a rosette, appearing lanceolate to linear in shape with entire or slightly dentate margins. Along the stem, the leaves become progressively smaller and more sparse as they ascend toward the inflorescence. The plant produces small, heads-like flower clusters arranged in terminal panicles composed of many tiny, tubular florets. These flowers are generally white or pale lilac and are supported by inconspicuous involucral bracts.
