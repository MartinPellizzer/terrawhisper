Cerasus avium f. sphenophylla

Cerasus avium f. sphenophylla, also known as the May cherry or dwarf cherry, is a variety of the wild cherry tree (Prunus avium) that has been cultivated for its medicinal and edible properties.

The plant is valued for its bark, leaves, and fruit, which have been traditionally used to treat various health conditions due to their rich content of salicylic acid, a compound similar to aspirin.

The medicinal properties of Cerasus avium f. sphenophylla include anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antipyretic effects, which have been reported to help alleviate symptoms of fever, pain, and inflammation, as well as to support digestive and cardiovascular health.

The plant can be prepared as a tea, tincture, or syrup, and is often used in herbal medicine to promote overall well-being and to address specific health concerns such as colds, flu, and digestive issues.
