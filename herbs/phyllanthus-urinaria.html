Phyllanthus urinaria

What's the taxonomical classification of Phyllanthus urinaria?

Phyllanthus urinaria belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Malpighiales and is a member of the family Phyllanthaceae. Finally, its specific biological classification is defined by the genus Phyllanthus and the species urinaria.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phyllanthus urinaria has an erect, herbaceous stem that reaches heights of 30 to 60 centimeters and features small, alternate, elliptical leaves arranged in two rows along the branches. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers that emerge from the leaf axils on short pedicels. Small, round, greenish-to-reddish fruits develop directly underneath the leaves, which is a distinguishing characteristic of this species. These fruits eventually split open to reveal small seeds once they reach maturity. The overall structure gives the plant a delicate, feathery appearance due to the dense arrangement of its foliage.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Phyllanthus urinaria contains therapeutic compounds primarily concentrated in its entire aerial portion, which includes the leaves, stems, and roots. What are the therapeutic actions of Phyllanthus urinaria?

Phyllanthus urinaria has multifaceted therapeutic actions including hepatoprotective, antiviral, anti-inflammatory, antipyretic, diuretic, cholagogue, and bitter properties. Its hepatoprotective nature helps protect the liver from toxins, while its antiviral effects can inhibit the replication of certain pathogens. The plant functions as a cholagogue by stimulating bile flow and acts as a diuretic to increase urine production. Its bitter components are often used to stimulate digestion and appetite. Additionally, it serves as an anti-inflammatory and antipyretic agent to reduce swelling and lower fevers.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds phyllanthin, corilagin, ellagitannins, gallic acid, and flavonoids. These specific phytochemicals are primarily recognized for their potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The presence of phyllanthin and corilagin often contributes to the plant's potential hepatoprotective effects. Additionally, the ellagitannins and gallic acid work to protect cells from oxidative stress and damage. Finally, the diverse array of flavonoids supports overall cellular health and immune function.

As discussed in a study, the medicinal properties of Phyllanthus urinaria L. were assessed alongside 10 other species. The research involved extracting dried leaves and stems in methanol and water to screen for hepatoprotective activity. This screening was conducted at a concentration of 50 µg/mL against tert-butyl hydroperoxide induced toxicity in HepG2 cells. The study also determined the contents of phyllanthin and hypophyllanthin within these species. While specific EC50 and IC50 values were provided for other species, the investigation focused on the overall hepatoprotective and antioxidant potential of the plant.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating HBV infection, chronic hepatitis B, various forms of hepatitis, diabetes mellitus, and kidney stones. Its medicinal properties are often harnessed to support liver function and combat viral loads associated with hepatitis. Researchers have also investigated its potential to regulate blood sugar levels in individuals managing diabetes mellitus. Furthermore, the plant acts as a diuretic and lithontriptic agent to assist in the dissolution and excretion of kidney stones. These diverse therapeutic applications make it a significant subject in ethnopharmacological studies.

As discussed in a study, a flavonoid molecule with the molecular formula C14H6O8 was isolated from Phyllanthus urinaria. This identified molecule, ellagic acid, showed unique anti-HBV functions. While it did not inhibit HBV polymerase activity, HBV replication, or block HBsAg secretion, it effectively blocks HBeAg secretion in HepG2 2.2.15 cells. The study determined that the IC50 for this blocking effect was 0.07 microg/ml. Because HBeAg is involved in immune tolerance during HBV infection, this compound from Phyllanthus urinaria may be a new candidate therapeutic against immune tolerance in HBV-infected individuals.

What are the herbal preparations of this plant?

This plant can be processed into a decoction by boiling the leaves and stems in water to extract concentrated medicinal properties for treating liver conditions. An infusion is often made by steeping the softer plant parts in hot water to create a milder herbal tea. Concentrated extracts are also produced through specialized evaporation methods to provide highly standardized doses of the plant's constituents. These various preparations allow for different methods of administration depending on the specific therapeutic goal.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause various adverse reactions including nausea, dizziness, diarrhea, stomach discomfort, hypoglycemia, and potential liver toxicity. Individuals consuming the plant may experience gastrointestinal issues like diarrhea and stomach discomfort during digestion. Some users have also reported feeling dizzy or suffering from nausea following ingestion. Because the plant may influence blood sugar levels, it can lead to hypoglycemia in certain people. Furthermore, there is a serious concern regarding its potential liver toxicity if used improperly.

The main side effects this plant can have are shown in the list below.

  • Diarrhea
  • Stomach discomfort
