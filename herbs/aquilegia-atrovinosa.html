Aquilegia atrovinosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aquilegia atrovinosa has deeply lobed, compound leaves with a bluish-green hue and distinctive spurred flowers that exhibit a deep, dark reddish-purple coloration. The foliage is characterized by delicate, divided leaflets that provide a soft texture to the plant's base. Each bloom features five petals that form a cup shape, transitioning into long, nectar-producing spurs extending backward. These dark pigmented flowers are held aloft on slender, upright stems that rise above the basal leaf rosette. The overall structure combines intricate leaf geometry with dramatic, saturated floral tones.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Aquilegia atrovinoosa can cause severe skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential neurological impairment if ingested or touched. Some reports suggest that higher levels of exposure could result in dizziness or confusion. It is important to handle this species with protective gear to avoid these adverse reactions.
