What to know about Bidens tripartita (bur marigold) before using it medicinally

Bur marigold, scientifically known as Bidens tripartita, is an herb that has been prized for its remarkable health benefits, which include reducing inflammation, soothing digestive issues, and boosting the immune system.

While it's often used in soups, stews, and salads to add flavor and nutrients, bur marigold's culinary applications also extend to making teas, sauces, and marinades. From a horticultural perspective, bur marigold is an easy-to-grow annual that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, attracting pollinators and butterflies with its bright yellow flowers. Botanically speaking, the herb belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its delicate, feathery leaves and distinctive three-petaled flowers.

With its rich history dating back to ancient Greece and Rome, where it was used as a medicinal and culinary ingredient, bur marigold remains an important herb in many traditional medicine practices around the world.
