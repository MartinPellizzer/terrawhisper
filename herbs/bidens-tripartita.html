Bidens tripartita

What's the taxonomical classification of Bidens tripartita?

Bidens tripartiata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical classification, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Asterales, it is a member of the family Asteraceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Bidens, with its specific identification being the species tripartiata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bidens tripartita has trifoliate leaves composed of three distinct leaflets, a woody stem structure, and small, inconspicuous flowers. The leaflets typically exhibit serrated margins and a lanceolate shape that tapers at both ends. The stem is characterized by a rugged texture and a brownish pigmentation that provides structural support for the foliage. Small axillary buds emerge at the junctions where the leaves meet the primary stem. These morphological features collectively assist the plant in its specific ecological niche.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse profile of bioactive medicinal compounds including flavonoids, tannins, coumarins, sterols, and triterpenes. The presence of flavonoids often contributes to significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities within the organism. Tannins provide the plant with astringent properties that can assist in healing tissues and protecting against microbial growth. Additionally, coumarins contribute to various pharmacological effects, while sterols and triterpenes play crucial roles in modulating cell membranes and supporting immune responses. Together, these phytochemicals create a complex chemical synergy that defines the plant's therapeutic potential.

As discussed in a study, the chemical composition of Bidens tripartita includes flavonoids, xanthophylls, volatile oil, acetylene, polyacetylene, sterols, aurones, chalcones, caffeine, and tannins. The alcoholic extract of Bidens tripartita contains different active principles such as tannins, anthracene derivatives, triterpenes, coumarins, and antocyanosides. Research involving a limit dose of 2000mg/kbw after intraperitoneal administration in mice helped determine the plant's toxicity. The single-dose intraperitoneal LD50 value of the alcoholic Bidens tripartita extract in mice was 4038 mg/kg. This LD50 value of 4038 mg of body weight classifies the alcoholic extract as a slightly toxic medicinal plant.
