What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bidens tripartita has trifoliate leaves composed of three distinct leaflets, a woody stem structure, and small, inconspicuous flowers. The leaflets typically exhibit serrated margins and a lanceolate shape that tapers at both ends. The stem is characterized by a rugged texture and a brownish pigmentation that provides structural support for the foliage. Small axillary buds emerge at the junctions where the leaves meet the primary stem. These morphological features collectively assist the plant in its specific ecological niche.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse profile of medicinal compounds including polyacetylene, acetylene, flavonoids, sterols, and triterpenes. The presence of polyacetylene and acetylene derivatives often contributes to the plant's potent biological activities. Additionally, flavonoids provide significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties to the chemical makeup. Sterols and triterpenes further enhance its therapeutic potential by interacting with various cellular processes. These various secondary metabolites work together to define the pharmacological utility of Bidens tripartita.

According to a study published by "Revista medico-chirurgicala a Societatii de Medici si Naturalisti din Iasi", the chemical composition of Bidens tripartita includes flavonoids, xanthophylls, volatile oil, acetylene and polyacetylene, sterols, aurones, chalcones, caffeine and tannins. In the alcoholic extract of Bidens tripartita, different active principles were identified, including tannins, anthracene derivatives, triterpene, coumarins, and antocyanosides. The acute toxicity of the alcoholic extract was assessed using a limit dose of 2000mg/kbw after intraperitoneal administration in mice. The single-dose intraperitoneal LD50 value of the alcoholic Bidens tripartita extract in mice was 4038 mg/kg. This LD50 value of 4038 mg of body weight classifies the extract as slightly toxic according to the Hodge and Sterneer toxicity scale.
