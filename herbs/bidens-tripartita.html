Tripartite Tickseed

Bidens tripartita

Tripartite Tickseed (Bidens tripartita) is a flowering plant native to North America, known for its distinct tripartite flower heads and use in traditional medicine to support digestive health and reduce inflammation.

Taxonomy

Tripartite Tickseed, scientifically classified as Bidens tripartita, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Asterales, family Asteraceae, genus Bidens, and species Bidens tripartita.

Morphology

The Tripartite Tickseed (Bidens tripartita) is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its slender, branching stems, heart-shaped to ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of small, yellow flowers with two reflexed petals and five to seven white, linear rays.

Distribution

Tripartite Tickseed (Bidens tripartita) is native to the eastern and central United States, ranging from New York and Ohio southward to Florida and Texas, and westward to Kansas and Missouri.

Traditional Uses

Traditionally, Tripartite Tickseed has been used in herbal medicine for its purported ability to reduce fever, alleviate digestive discomfort, and support respiratory health.

Medicinal Systems

In traditional medicinal systems such as Native American herbalism, Traditional Chinese Medicine, and Ayurveda, Tripartite Tickseed has been utilized for its purported therapeutic properties.

Phytochemistry

The phytochemistry of Tripartite Tickseed (Bidens tripartita) includes the presence of flavonoids, sesquiterpene lactones, and caffeic acid derivatives, which contribute to its medicinal properties and potential therapeutic applications.

Pharmacology

Tripartite Tickseed exhibits anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and wound-healing properties, making it useful in treating digestive disorders, menstrual cramps, and skin conditions.

Medicinal Uses

Tripartite Tickseed is used to treat digestive issues, skin conditions, respiratory infections, fever, and as a general tonic to boost energy and immunity.

Preparations

Tripartite Tickseed is used to prepare tinctures, decoctions, and infusions that are traditionally employed in herbal medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory, wound-healing, and antispasmodic properties.

Evidence

Clinical evidence for Tripartite Tickseed (Bidens tripartita) is limited, with most studies focusing on its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, though few human trials have been conducted to confirm its efficacy in herbal medicine applications.

Cultivation
