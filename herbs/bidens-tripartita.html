Tripartite Tickseed

Bidens tripartita

Tripartite Tickseed (Bidens tripartita) is a member of the Asteraceae family, native to various regions in North America. Traditionally, its leaves and flowers have been used to address skin infections, digestive issues, and as a remedy for fever. The plant is also valued in some cultural practices for its perceived medicinal properties.

Botanical Identification

Tripartite Tickseed, scientifically known as Bidens tripartita, is a member of the Asteraceae family. Common names include Tickseed and Three-lobed Tickseed, with regional variations. It is an annual plant native to North America, featuring hairy stems, lobed leaves, and yellow flowers with ray florets. Synonyms in herbal literature include Bidens pilosa and Bidens cernua.

Tripartite Tickseed contains key active compounds such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and tannins. These bioactives work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Their combined action supports the herb's traditional use in herbal medicine.

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for treating respiratory issues like coughs and colds

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and as a general tonic

Poultice: Used for skin conditions and wound healing

Safety Profile

Tripartite Tickseed is generally considered safe when used in moderate amounts. However, it may cause gastrointestinal upset in some individuals. Consult a healthcare provider before use, especially if you have existing health conditions or are taking other medications.
