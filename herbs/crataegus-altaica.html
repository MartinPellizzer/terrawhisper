Crataegus altaica

What's the taxonomical classification of Crataegus altaica?

Crataegus altaica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It falls under the order Rosales and is a member of the family Rosaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Crataegus, with its specific identification being the species altaica.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Crataegus altaica contains high concentrations of bioactive flavonoids, organic acids, tannins, and triterpenoid saponins that contribute to its therapeutic potential. These polyphenolic compounds function primarily as potent antioxidants, helping to neutralize free radicals within the body. The presence of specific procyanidins and quercetin derivatives suggests significant cardiovascular protective properties. Additionally, the plant's organic acids may assist in regulating metabolic processes and supporting digestive health. Such a diverse chemical profile makes the species a subject of interest for pharmacological research into heart health and inflammation.

What are the therapeutic actions of Crataegus altaica?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat cardiovascular conditions including hypertension, coronary insufficiency, cardiac arrhythmias, and congestive heart failure. Its therapeutic properties are primarily attributed to high concentrations of flavonoids and proanthocyanidins. These compounds help improve blood flow and strengthen the contraction of the heart muscle. Patients often use extracts of the plant to manage symptoms of angina pectoris and stabilize irregular heartbeats. The plant also exhibits antioxidant effects that support overall vascular health.
