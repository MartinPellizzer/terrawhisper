Maytenus confertiflora

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Maytenus confertiflora has small, elliptical leaves with entire margins and a woody, branching stem structure that supports its shrubby habit. The foliage is typically arranged in an alternate pattern along the stems, displaying a leathery texture. Its flowers are minute and clustered closely together in the axils of the leaves, which is characteristic of its species name. The fruit consists of small, fleshy capsules that eventually split open to reveal seeds. These morphological features collectively define its compact and hardy growth form in its natural habitat.
