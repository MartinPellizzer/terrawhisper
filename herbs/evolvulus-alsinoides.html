Evolvulus alsinoides

What's the taxonomical classification of Evolvulus alsinoides?

Evolvulus alsinoides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized into the order Solanales. This species is a member of the family Convolvulaceae, falling under the genus Evolvulus, and is specifically identified by its species name, alsinoides.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Evolvulus alsinoides contains bioactive phytochemicals such as alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, and tannins that contribute to its medicinal properties. These specific compounds are responsible for the plant's demonstrated antimicrobial and antioxidant activities in various biological studies. Researchers have identified that the presence of phenolic compounds helps in neutralizing free radicals within cellular structures. Furthermore, the various secondary metabolites found in the leaves and roots provide potential therapeutic benefits for treating inflammatory conditions. These chemical constituents make the species a subject of interest for developing new pharmacological treatments.

What are the therapeutic actions of Evolvulus alsinoides?

Evolvulus alsinoides has medicinal properties used primarily for its anti-inflammatory, wound-healing, and antimicrobial effects in traditional herbal practices. The plant contains bioactive compounds that help reduce swelling and soothe irritated skin surfaces when applied topically. It is often utilized in decoctions to treat minor infections and promote the regeneration of damaged tissues. Some cultures also employ its extracts to manage internal ailments due to its potential antioxidant activity. These therapeutic actions make it a valuable component in various ethnobotanical remedies for skin ailments and general inflammation.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin conditions such as inflammation, wounds, and various types of dermatitis. Its medicinal properties are frequently applied topically to soothe irritation and promote the healing of minor abrasions. Traditional practitioners often utilize its extracts to manage symptoms of localized infections and swelling. The plant contains bioactive compounds that contribute to its role in addressing dermatological ailments. These applications highlight its significance in ethnobotanical practices for managing physical discomfort related to the skin.
