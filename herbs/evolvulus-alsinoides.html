Evolvulus alsinoides

What's the taxonomical classification of Evolvulus alsinoides?

Evolvulus alsinoides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized into the order Solanales. This species is a member of the family Convolvulaceae, falling under the genus Evolvulus, and is specifically identified by its species name, alsinoides.

Evolvulus alsinoides is prepared as decoctions, infusions, powders, or topical pastes using its aerial parts to treat various ailments. Some traditional practices involve grinding the entire plant into a powder that can be mixed with honey or oil. These diverse preparations allow the plant's active compounds to be delivered effectively based on the specific medicinal need.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Evolvulus alsinoide can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, or gastrointestinal distress if ingested by humans or animals. While specific toxicological data for this species is limited, many members of the Convolvulaceae family contain alkaloids that may trigger adverse reactions. Ingestion of the plant material could lead to nausea, vomiting, or more severe digestive upsets depending on the individual's sensitivity. It is generally advised to handle the plant with care and keep it away from children and pets to avoid accidental exposure.
