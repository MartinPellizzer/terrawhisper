Michelia chapensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Michelia chapensis has elliptical to oblong leaves that are leathery in texture, dark green on the upper surface, and possess entire margins with a distinctively pale underside. The plant typically exhibits a woody structure characteristic of evergreen shrubs or small trees found in tropical forest understories. Its flowers are notable for their fragrant, creamy white or pale yellow petals that emerge from prominent buds. The reproductive organs include a central cluster of carpels surrounded by numerous stamens, which is a defining feature of the Magnoliaceae family. These morphological traits help distinguish the species within its native habitat.
