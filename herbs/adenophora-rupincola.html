Adenophora rupincola

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Adenophora rupincola has upright, herbaceous stems that reach heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and bear oppositely arranged, ovate to lanceolate leaves with entire or slightly serrated margins. The foliage is typically green and lacks significant pubescence, providing a smooth texture to the plant's surface. Its inflorescence consists of small, bell-shaped flowers that emerge from the leaf axils in a terminal cluster. These blossoms are generally pale purple or pinkish in color and possess a tubular corolla. The root system is composed of a slender, woody rhizome that allows the plant to anchor itself in rocky crevices.
