Clematis kirilowii

What's the taxonomical classification of Clematis kirilowii?

Clematis kirilowii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized under the order Ranunculales. This plant is a member of the family Ranunculaceae and is situated within the genus Clematis, specifically identified by its species name, kirilowii.

    What are the molecular targets of this plant?

    Clematis kirilowii targets various inflammatory enzymes and signaling pathways, specifically inhibiting cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2) and inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS) to reduce systemic inflammation. The plant's bioactive compounds interact with these proteins to suppress the production of pro-inflammatory mediators like prostaglandin E2 and nitric oxide. These molecular interactions are primarily mediated by triterpenoid saponins and phenolic acids found within the root extract. By modulating these enzymatic activities, the plant exerts its potent anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects on cellular processes. Such targeted inhibition contributes to its traditional use in treating swelling and pain.

    What are the therapeutic actions of Clematis kirilowii?

    Clematis kirilowii has medicinal properties that primarily act as an anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and diuretic agent to treat various ailments. Practitioners often use the plant to alleviate pain associated with rheumatism and joint inflammation. Its diuretic qualities assist in flushing toxins from the body and supporting kidney function. Additionally, the plant is sometimes utilized to soothe respiratory discomfort and reduce fever. These therapeutic actions make it a significant component in traditional herbal practices.

      What are the herbal preparations of Clematis kirilowii?

      Clematis kirilowii is prepared primarily as a decoction by boiling the dried roots in water to extract its medicinal compounds. This traditional method allows for the concentrated release of active constituents necessary for therapeutic use. In some instances, the plant material may also be infused into liquids or processed into powders for easier administration. Practitioners often adjust the boiling time and concentration depending on the specific ailment being addressed. These various preparation forms ensure that the botanical properties are effectively utilized in herbal medicine.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Clematis kirilowii can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if ingested due to the presence of protoanemonin. Contact with the plant's sap or crushed leaves often leads to contact dermatitis, manifesting as redness, itching, or blistering on the skin. If any part of the plant is swallowed, it may trigger nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain. These adverse reactions occur because the chemical compounds in the plant act as a natural irritant to biological tissues. It is important to wear protective gloves when handling this species to avoid these physiological complications.
