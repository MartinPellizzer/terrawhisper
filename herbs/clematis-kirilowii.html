Clematis kirilowii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clematis kirilowii has woody, climbing stems that produce opposite, trifoliate leaves with ovate to elliptical leaflets. The foliage features finely serrated margins and a smooth texture that helps the plant navigate its supporting structures. Its flowers are typically pale or creamy in color and emerge from the leaf axils during the growing season. The plant develops small, feathery seed heads once the blooms have faded. These botanical characteristics allow the species to thrive in shaded or semi-shaded forest environments.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Clematis kirilowii can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if ingested due to the presence of protoanemonin. Contact with the plant's sap or crushed leaves often leads to contact dermatitis, manifesting as redness, itching, or blistering on the skin. If any part of the plant is swallowed, it may trigger nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain. These adverse reactions occur because the chemical compounds in the plant act as a natural irritant to biological tissues. It is important to wear protective gloves when handling this species to avoid these physiological complications.
