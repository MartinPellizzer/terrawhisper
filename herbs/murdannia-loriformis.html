What are the pharmacological activities of Murdannia loriformis?

This plant has antimutagenic, anticancer, and chemopreventive pharmacological activities that stem from its diverse bioactive compounds. Research indicates that its extracts can effectively protect DNA from damage caused by various chemical mutagens. These properties contribute to its potential role in cancer chemopreventive strategies by inhibiting the early stages of tumor development. Furthermore, the plant demonstrates significant anticancer effects by inducing apoptosis in several malignant cell lines. Such multifaceted biological actions make it a subject of interest for developing natural therapeutic agents.

As discussed in a study, an 80% ethanol extract of Murdannia loriformis was examined for antimutagenic and cancer chemopreventive activities. In the Salmonella mutation assay, the extract reduced the mutagenicities of various compounds to 31.4-67.9% at a dose of 10 mg/plate. When testing chemopreventive activity in the colon of F344 rats, doses of 0.1-1.0 g/kg wt significantly inhibited aberrant crypt focus formation in the initiation stage by 21-51%. During the post-initiation stage, the extract tended to inhibit aberrant crypt focus formation by 12-27% and decreased the number of larger foci containing more than 3 aberrant crypts. Additionally, the extract inhibited the formation of O6-methylguanine and N7-methylguanine in the colonic mucosa and muscular layers.
