Murdannia loriformis

What's the taxonomical classification of Murdannia loriformis?

Murdannia loriformis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Commelinares and the family Commelinaeceae. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its genus, Murdannia, and its specific species designation, loriformis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Murdannia loriformis has an erect, herbaceous growth habit characterized by slender, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters. The leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem and possess a lanceolate shape with entire margins and smooth surfaces. Small, inconspicuous flowers typically emerge from the leaf axils, displaying a delicate structure often colored in shades of white or pale pink. The plant develops a fibrous root system that helps anchor its relatively small stature in moist soil environments. These morphological features collectively define its appearance as a low-growing, creeping, or upright perennial herb.

What are the pharmacological activities of Murdannia loriformis?

This plant has antimutagenic, anticancer, and chemopreventive pharmacological activities that contribute to its therapeutic potential. Research indicates that its bioactive compounds can protect DNA from damage caused by various environmental and chemical agents. By inhibiting the development of malignant cells, the plant exhibits significant properties that help suppress tumor growth. These mechanisms allow it to serve as a natural agent for cancer chemopreventive strategies in various experimental models. Furthermore, its ability to prevent genetic mutations provides a foundation for its use in long-term health protection.

According to a study published by "The journal of medical investigation : JMI", an 80% ethanol extract of Murdannia loriformis demonstrated antimutagenic activity by reducing the mutagenicities of various compounds to 31.4-67.9% at a dose of 10 mg/plate. In the Salmonella mutation assay, the extract itself showed no mutagenicity. Regarding cancer chemopreventive activity, the extract at doses of 0.1-1.0 g/kg wt significantly inhibited aberrant crypt focus (ACF) formation in the initiation stage by 21-51%. In the post-initiation stage, the extract tended to inhibit ACF formation by 12-27% and significantly decreased the number of larger ACFs containing more than 3 aberrant crypts per focus. Additionally, the extract inhibited the formation of O6-methylguanine and N7-methylguanine in the colonic mucosa and muscular layers.

According to a study published by "The journal of medical investigation : JMI", an 80% ethanol extract of Murdannia loriformis was examined for antimutagenic activity and cancer chemopreventive activity. At a dose of 10 mg/plate, the extract reduced the mutagenicities of several substances to 31.4-67.9%. The extract also tended to inhibit ACF formation by 12-27% in the post-initiation stage and decreased the number of larger ACFs containing more than 3 aberrant crypts per focus. These findings indicate that Murdannia loriformis inhibits AOM-induced ACF formation in both the initiation and post-initiation stages.
