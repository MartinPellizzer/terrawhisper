Biophytum umbraculum

What's the taxonomical classification of Biophytum umbraculum?

Biophytum umbraculum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further falls into the order Oxalidales and is a member of the family Oxalidaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic designation is defined by the genus Biophytum and the species umbraculum.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

The plant's bioactive compounds, particularly its polyphenols and flavonoids, exert their effects by inhibiting the nuclear factor kappa B signaling pathway. By modulating these protein interactions, the plant reduces the production of nitric oxide and prostaglandin E2 in cellular environments. These molecular mechanisms contribute to its documented anti-inflammatory and antioxidant medicinal properties. Such interactions with enzymatic targets help mitigate oxidative stress and cellular damage.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, inflammation, and various dermatological conditions through its application of crushed leaves. The leaves contain bioactive compounds that act as natural antiseptics and anti-inflammatory agents. Practitioners often apply the moist leaf pulp directly to sores or rashes to accelerate the healing process. It is also frequently utilized in traditional medicine to soothe localized swelling and skin irritation. This botanical remedy serves as a topical treatment rather than an internal medicine for systemic diseases.

What are the herbal preparations of Biophytum umbraculum?

Biophytum umbraculum is prepared primarily as a topical paste made from crushed fresh leaves, a decoction of the roots, or a juice extracted directly from the plant tissue. These various preparations are used to address skin ailments such as boils, carbuncles, and inflammatory swellings. When applied externally, the mashed leaf paste acts as a cooling poultice to reduce local heat and swelling. The decoction of the roots is often utilized in traditional practices to manage internal systemic issues. Each form is selected based on the specific severity and location of the ailment being treated.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Biophytum umbraculum can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested or handled improperly. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, the plant's chemical composition may trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Contact with the sap or leaves can lead to redness, itching, or localized swelling on the skin. If consumed, it might result in stomach cramps, nausea, or vomiting due to unidentified bioactive compounds. Users should exercise extreme caution and avoid direct contact to prevent these adverse reactions.
