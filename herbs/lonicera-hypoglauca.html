What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lonicera hypoglauca has opposite, simple leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical in shape and lack the waxy, bluish coating found in many other honeysuckle species. The leaf margins are generally entire, meaning they are smooth and lack teeth or lobes. The stems are woody and can grow into a sprawling shrub or a climbing vine depending on the environmental conditions. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves, often appearing in pairs. These plants produce fleshy berries that change color as they reach maturity.
