What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dumasia forrestii has a perennial growth habit characterized by tufted, caespitose clumps of slender, erect leaves that typically reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. Its inflorescence consists of compact, terminal panicles that emerge from the center of the leafy clusters. The individual spikelets are relatively small and arranged densely along the branches of the flowering stems.
