Pyrus × sorbaronia

Pyrus × sorbaronia, commonly known as Sour Cherry, is a hybrid species derived from the cross between the Pyrus communis (common pear) and Sorbus aucuparia (rowan).

This plant is valued for its health benefits, which include antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, making it useful in treating various conditions such as digestive issues and respiratory problems.

The therapeutic actions of Pyrus × sorbaronia are attributed to its bioactive constituents, including flavonoids, phenolic acids, and anthocyanins, which have been shown to possess antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial activities.

The bioactive constituents of Pyrus × sorbaronia, particularly the flavonoids and anthocyanins, may also have anti-cancer and cardio-protective effects.

Pyrus × sorbaronia has many medicinal parts, such as its bark, leaves, and roots.
