Saxifraga mengtzeana

What's the taxonomical classification of Saxifraga mengtzeana?

Saxifraga mengtzeana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Saxifragales and is a member of the family Saxifragaceae. Finally, it is categorized within the genus Saxifraga, specifically designated as the species mengtzeana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saxifraga mengtzeana has a basal rosette of spoon-shaped or spatulate leaves that are typically green with slightly serrated or entire margins.

These leaves possess long petioles and often exhibit a somewhat fleshy texture to store moisture. The plant produces an upright, slender flowering stem that emerges from the center of the leaf cluster. Small, five-petaled flowers appear at the terminal end of the stem, often displaying white or pale colors.

The overall structure is compact, allowing the plant to thrive in rocky or alpine environments.
