What are the active constituents of Ervatamia coronaria ?

Ervatamia coronaria, also known as queen of the night, has active constituents such as alkaloids, particularly coronarinine and coronaridine, which exhibit analgesic, anti-inflammatory, and antimalarial properties.

Additionally, the plant contains flavonoids, which have been shown to possess antioxidant and antimicrobial activities.

These medicinal constituents contribute to the plant's traditional uses in treating various ailments, including fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions.

What are the medicinal preparations of Ervatamia coronaria ?

Ervatamia coronaria, also known as queen of the night, has medicinal preparations such as its leaves, which are used to treat fever, rheumatism, and skin irritations.

The root extract is used to treat urinary infections and as an anti-inflammatory. In traditional medicine, it is also used to treat digestive issues, including diarrhea and dyspepsia.

Its various medicinal properties make it a sought-after plant in folk medicine.

What are the possible side effect of using Ervatamia coronaria improperly?

Improper use of Ervatamia coronaria, also known as queen of the night, increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as dizziness, nausea, and vomiting due to its high concentration of alkaloids.

It can also interact with medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes drugs, leading to adverse reactions.

Furthermore, prolonged consumption may cause kidney damage, liver toxicity, and cardiovascular problems, particularly in individuals with pre-existing medical conditions.

What precautions to take when using Ervatamia coronaria medicinally?

Before using Ervatamia coronaria, also known as queen of the night, for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as consulting with a qualified healthcare professional or expert in botany and pharmacology.

This plant is known to interact with certain medications, including blood thinners and blood pressure medications, and can cause side effects such as dizziness and nausea.

Its use should be carefully monitored, especially for pregnant or breastfeeding women, and its dosage should be strictly adhered to.
