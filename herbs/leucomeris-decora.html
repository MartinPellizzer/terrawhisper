Leucomeris decora

What's the taxonomical classification of Leucomeris decora?

Leucomeris decora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. This plant is further categorized into the family Asteraceae, which is a large and diverse group of flowering plants. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Leucomeris, and its specific identification is given by the species name decoras.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin inflammation, wounds, digestive disorders, and respiratory ailments. Its medicinal properties are often leveraged to soothe itching and reduce redness associated with various dermatological conditions. In traditional practices, preparations from the plant may be ingested to alleviate stomach discomfort or dysentery. Additionally, some applications involve using the plant to mitigate coughs and other upper respiratory issues. These various uses highlight its significance in folk medicine for managing localized and internal ailments.
