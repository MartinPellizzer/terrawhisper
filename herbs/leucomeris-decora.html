Leucomeris decora

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Leucomeris decora has an erect, perennial growth habit characterized by slender, branched stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. The leaves are primarily basal or lower-stemmed, exhibiting a lanceolate to narrowly elliptic shape with finely serrated margins. Small, tubular flowers are arranged in terminal cymes, displaying delicate white or pale pink petals that contrast with the greenery. The root system consists of a fibrous structure that supports the plant's upright posture in its natural habitat. These morphological features collectively define its compact and graceful appearance within its ecosystem.
