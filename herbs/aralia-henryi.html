Aralia henryi

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aralia henryi has a woody, shrub-like growth habit characterized by compound leaves that can reach up to 60 centimeters in length. The foliage consists of multiple large, ovate to elliptical leaflets with serrated margins and prominent venation. Its stems are typically slender and can develop a somewhat woody texture as the plant matures. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge in branched panicles, eventually producing dark, berry-like fruits. The overall structure provides a lush, tropical appearance suitable for shaded understory environments.
