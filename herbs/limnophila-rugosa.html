Limnophila rugosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Limnophila rugosa has long, slender, lanceolate leaves that are characterized by a distinctively wrinkled or rugose surface texture. The leaves are arranged in an opposite pattern along a flexible, submerged stem that allows the plant to sway with water currents. These leaves often feature wavy or slightly undulated margins which contribute to its unique appearance in aquatic environments. The foliage typically displays a deep green coloration that provides significant surface area for photosynthesis. This morphological structure enables the plant to thrive effectively in low-light conditions within various freshwater habitats.

      What are the herbal preparations of Limnophila rugosa?

      Limnophila rugosa is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice depending on the intended therapeutic application. The dried aerial parts are most commonly boiled in water to create a concentrated decoction for internal use. For gentler medicinal properties, the leaves can be steeped in hot water to form an infusion.
