What's the taxonomical classification of Ancistrocarpus densispinosus?

Ancistrocarpus densispinosus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, following the taxonomic order Malvales. This plant is a member of the family Malvaceae and is further categorized under the genus Ancistrocarpus, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species densispinosus.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Ancistrocarpus densispinosus can cause significant skin irritation, puncture wounds, and localized inflammation due to its dense, sharp spines. Contact with the plant often results in painful prick injuries that may lead to minor bleeding or swelling. If the spines become embedded deeply in the skin, they can trigger secondary infections or allergic reactions in sensitive individuals. Repeated exposure to the sharp structures may also cause persistent dermatological discomfort or small lacerations. Careful handling is necessary to avoid these physical injuries during interaction with the vegetation.
