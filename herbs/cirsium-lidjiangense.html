Cirsium lidjiangense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cirsium lidjiangense has erect, perennial stems that typically reach heights of 20 to 60 centimeters and are covered in fine, white hairs. The leaves are primarily basal, forming a rosette, and possess deeply lobed or pinnatifid margins with spreading lobes. Upper leaves are smaller and alternate along the stem, often featuring spiny teeth along their edges. The inflorescence consists of several flower heads arranged in a cluster, each surrounded by involucral bracts that are often tipped with spines. Each flower head contains numerous tubular florets that vary in color from pink to purple.
