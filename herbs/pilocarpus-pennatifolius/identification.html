Botanical Identification of Pilocarpus Tree (Pilocarpus pennatifolius)

To identify Pilocarpus Tree (Pilocarpus pennatifolius) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Pilocarpus pennatifolius belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Sapindales, family Rutaceae, genus Pilocarpus, and species Pilocarpus pennatifolius. These classifications help identify its botanical relationships and place it within the broader context of plant life.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The botanical name of the plant is Pilocarpus pennatifolius, first described by the botanist R.E. Schultes in 1926. It has several synonyms, including Pilocarpus microphyllus and Pilocarpus spicatus, reflecting historical taxonomic revisions. Common names include "Pilocarpus tree" and "Amazonian prickly ash," while regional names vary across Latin America. Historically, it was known by indigenous names such as "chamomilla" in some Amazonian communities.

Morphological Characteristics

Pilocarpus pennatifolius is a small to medium-sized tree with a compact growth habit. Its root system is fibrous and moderately deep, providing stability. The stem is straight and cylindrical, often branching near the base. Leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, with a serrated margin and a pungent scent. The inflorescence is a panicle, bearing small, greenish-white flowers. The fruit is a capsule that splits into three valves, releasing small, flat seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Pilocarpus pennatifolius can be distinguished by its alternate, pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins and clustered, small yellow flowers. It has a distinctive aromatic resinous scent and produces dark brown, elliptical pods. These features help differentiate it from similar species like Pilocarpus microphyllus.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Pilocarpus pennatifolius is native to South America, primarily found in countries like Peru, Bolivia, and Brazil. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity. The tree prefers well-drained, sandy soils and is commonly found at altitudes between 300 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Pilocarpus pennatifolius blooms from May to July, producing small greenish-yellow flowers. Seed formation occurs from August to October. For identification purposes, harvesting is best done during the dry season, when leaves are most distinct and the plant's characteristics are most apparent.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Pilocarpus pennatifolius belongs to the Rhamnaceae family, closely related to species like Pilocarpus microphyllus and Pilocarpus altitudinalis. It shares morphological and genetic traits with other members of the genus, including similar leaf structure and chemical composition. These relationships help in understanding its ecological and medicinal significance.
