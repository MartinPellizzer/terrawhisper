What's the taxonomical classification of Epimedium davidii?

Epimedium davidii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Ranunculales. This plant is a member of the family Berberidaceae, specifically categorized within the genus Epimedium, with its unique designation being the species davidii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Epimedium davidii has heart-shaped to ovate leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette and possess finely serrated margins. The foliage typically exhibits a leathery texture and can transition to reddish hues during colder temperatures. Slender, arching stems emerge from the base to support small, delicate flowers that are often pale pink or white. These blossoms feature four distinct petals and are typically arranged in loose racemes. The root system consists of creeping rhizomes that allow the plant to spread slowly through the soil.
