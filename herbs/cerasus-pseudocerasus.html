Cerasus pseudocerasus

What's the taxonomical classification of Cerasus pseudocerasus?

Cerasus pseudocerasus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is placed in the order Rosales and the family Rosaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Cerasus, and its species designation, pseudocerasus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cerasus pseudocerasus has deciduous, ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated margins, woody stems, and small, fleshy drupes that typically measure between 5 and 10 millimeters in diameter. Its branching structure is characterized by slender, spreading twigs that can support dense clusters of fruit. The flowers are generally white and emerge in small groups from the axils of the leaves.
