What's the taxonomical classification of Radermachera yunnanensis?

Radermachera yunnanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Lamiales. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the family Bignoniaceae, which contains the genus Radermachera. Finally, the plant is identified at the species level as yunnanensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Radermachera yunnanensis has evergreen, opposite leaves that are elliptical to oblong in shape, measuring approximately 5 to 15 centimeters in length with finely serrated margins and a glossy, dark green surface.

The leaf texture is somewhat leathery, providing a robust structure to the foliage. These leaves are supported by slender, woody stems that exhibit a branching growth habit typical of woody shrubs. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves, often appearing in clusters.

The plant's overall morphology reflects its adaptation to subtropical forest environments where dense canopy cover is common.
