Elaeocarpus decipiens

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Elaeocarpus decipiens has simple, alternate, elliptical to lanceolate leaves that are typically 5 to 15 centimeters long and possess entire margins. The foliage often exhibits a coriaceous or leathery texture with a glossy green upper surface and a paler underside. Its woody stems are characterized by a branching habit that forms a small to medium-sized tree or shrub. The reproductive structures include small, inconspicuous flowers that give rise to fleshy, drupaceous fruits. These fruits are often globose and serve as a key identifying feature of the species.
