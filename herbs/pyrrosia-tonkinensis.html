Pyrrosia tonkinensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pyrrosia tonkinensis has a perennial rootstock that produces upright, unbranched stems reaching heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The leaves are primarily basal and arranged in a rosette, characterized by an ovate to lanceolate shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. These foliage structures are typically dark green and possess a somewhat leathery texture. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the terminal ends of the stems, often displaying a pale pink or white coloration. The overall morphology reflects an adaptation to the specific alpine or subalpine habitats where this species is found.
