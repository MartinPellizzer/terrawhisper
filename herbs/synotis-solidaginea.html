Synotis solidaginea

What's the taxonomical classification of Synotis solidaginea?

Synotis solidaginea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed under the order Asterales. This plant is a representative of the family Asteraceae, falling into the genus Synotis, and is specifically identified by its species name, solidaginea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Synotis solidaginea has small, fleshy, lanceolate leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette with a somewhat succulent texture.

The leaf margins are generally smooth or slightly undulate, while the leaf surfaces may exhibit a subtle pubescence. Its inflorescence consists of small, delicate flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette on relatively short stalks. The reproductive structures include tiny petals and stamens that are characteristic of the Gesneriaceae family.

Synotis solidaginea targets various enzymes and receptors within the central nervous system, specifically modulating GABAergic neurotransmission and inhibiting acetylcholinesterase activity. Through these diverse protein interactions, the plant demonstrates multifaceted potential for treating neurological disorders.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Synotis solidaginea can cause adverse reactions such as gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, or allergic hypersensitivity if ingested or applied topically. Individuals with sensitive skin might experience redness, itching, or localized swelling upon contact with the foliage. There is also a risk of more severe complications if the plant is mistakenly consumed as a food source. Consult a medical professional immediately if any symptoms of poisoning or irritation occur after exposure.
