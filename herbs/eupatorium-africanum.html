What's the taxonomical classification of Eupatorium africanum?

Eupatorium africanum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. This plant is a representative of the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its complex flower heads. Within this family, it is categorized under the genus Eupatorium, with its specific designation being the species africanum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eupatorium africanum has a woody, perennial stem structure characterized by deeply fissured bark and a spreading canopy of lanceolate, leathery leaves. The foliage is typically arranged in an alternate pattern along the branches to maximize light absorption in shaded understories. Small, actinomorphic flowers emerge from axillary buds, displaying a pale yellowish hue and delicate petal arrangements. The reproductive organs are protected by a thick calyx that transitions into a fleshy, indehiscent fruit upon maturity. These fruits are often rounded in shape and serve as the primary dispersal mechanism for the species.
