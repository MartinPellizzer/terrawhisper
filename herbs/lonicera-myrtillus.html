Lonicera myrtillus

What's the taxonomical classification of Lonicera myrtillus?

Lonicera myrtillus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Dipsacales. This plant is a member of the family Caprifoliaceae, falling under the genus Lonicera, with the specific species designation being myrtillus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lonicera myrtillus has opposite, simple, elliptical to oblong leaves that measure approximately 2 to 5 centimeters in length and possess entire margins and acute tips. The plant typically exhibits a decumbent or sprawling growth habit with slender, woody stems. Small, tubular, yellowish-white flowers emerge from the leaf axils during the flowering season. These blossoms eventually develop into small, fleshy, dark blue or blackish berries that are often clustered. The overall structure of the shrub is compact and low-growing, making it well-suited for ground cover.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires acidic soil with a pH between 4.5 and 5.5, consistent moisture, and partial shade to thrive.

You should plant it in well-draining organic matter to prevent root rot while maintaining high humidity. Regular watering is essential to keep the substrate damp, though you must avoid waterlogged conditions. Applying mulch around the base helps regulate soil temperature and retains necessary moisture.

Providing protection from intense afternoon sun will prevent leaf scorch and promote healthier growth.
