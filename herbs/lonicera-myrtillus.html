Lonicera myrtillus

What's the taxonomical classification of Lonicera myrtillus?

Lonicera myrtillus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Dipsacales. This plant is a member of the family Caprifoliaceae, falling under the genus Lonicera, with the specific species designation being myrtillus.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Lonicera myrtillus contains high concentrations of bioactive flavonoids, organic acids, and various polyphenolic compounds that contribute to its therapeutic potential. These specific phytochemicals act as potent antioxidants that help neutralize free radicals within biological systems. The presence of anthocyanins provides significant anti-inflammatory properties that may assist in reducing swelling and irritation. Additionally, the plant's chemical profile suggests potential antimicrobial activities that can inhibit the growth of certain pathogens. Researchers continue to study these individual components to better understand their precise metabolic effects on human health.

Lonicera myrtillus has antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties that support immune function and cellular health. The plant contains high concentrations of polyphenols and flavonoids which help neutralize free radicals in the body. These bioactive compounds work to reduce systemic inflammation and protect tissues from oxidative stress. Additionally, its natural antimicrobial agents can assist in fighting various bacterial and fungal pathogens. What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various inflammatory conditions, skin infections, respiratory ailments, and digestive issues. Its bioactive compounds provide antimicrobial and antioxidant properties that help combat bacterial and fungal pathogens. When applied topically, extracts can soothe skin irritations, wounds, and localized swelling. In traditional medicine, decoctions are sometimes consumed to alleviate symptoms of coughs or minor gastrointestinal distress. These diverse therapeutic applications stem from the plant's complex chemical profile.
