Common Stonecrop (Sedum acre)

Common Stonecrop (Sedum acre) is a member of the Saxifragaceae family, native to Europe, and Asia. Traditionally, its leaves, stems, and flowers have been used for decoctions, infusions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its diuretic, astringent, and anti-inflammatory actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and native american herbal medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters.
Leaves
Opposite decussate leaves with acute tips, upper surface glaucous green, lower surface pale gray-green, bearing prominent stomatal bands along midribs.
Flowers
Flowers are actinomorphic, yellow with white markings, arranged in dense clusters, and typically have four petals with two lobes.
Stems
Erect, branched, woody, glabrous, with opposite leaves and adventitious root production.
