Common Stonecrop

Sedum acre

Common Stonecrop (Sedum acre) is a member of the Saxifragaceae family, traditionally used for the treatment of wounds and relief of skin irritation. The leaves and stems are typically harvested and applied topically, while the plant has also been historically used as a digestive aid in various cultural practices.

Botanical Identification

Common Stonecrop, or Sedum acre, is a member of the Crassulaceae family. It is also known as Stonecrop or Common Stonecrop, and is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It has fleshy, succulent leaves and yellow flowers. Its scientific name is often cited in herbal texts, though some older references use synonyms like Sedum album.

Active Compounds

Common Stonecrop contains flavonoids, phenolic acids, and tannins as its main active compounds. These bioactives work together to provide anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Their synergistic action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory ailments such as coughs and colds.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and as a general tonic.

Powder: Used for topical application to treat skin irritations and wounds.

Safety Profile

Common Stonecrop is generally considered safe when used as directed, but it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and lactation due to insufficient safety data. Always consult a healthcare professional before use, especially for prolonged or high-dose applications.
