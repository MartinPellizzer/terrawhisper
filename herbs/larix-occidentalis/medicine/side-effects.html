1. Increases blood pressure

Larix occidentalis increases blood pressure due to its stimulatory effects on the nervous system.

The herb contains compounds such as pinene and sesquiterpene that can cause an increase in heart rate and cardiac output, leading to elevated blood pressure levels. Additionally, Larix occidentalis may also stimulate the release of adrenaline, a hormone that increases blood pressure by constricting blood vessels and increasing cardiac contractility.

These stimulatory effects can be especially pronounced in individuals who are already hypertensive or have pre-existing cardiovascular conditions.

2. Causes allergic reactions

Larix occidentalis causes allergic reactions due to its high content of resinous compounds and oleoresins.

These chemicals can trigger an immune response in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as itching, redness, and swelling of the skin or mucous membranes.

The allergenic potential is thought to be linked to the presence of specific proteins and terpenoids in the tree's sapwood and heartwood.

3. Affects liver function

Larix occidentalis affects liver function due to its potential interaction with certain medications that are broken down by the liver.

The resin present in the plant's bark may alter the metabolism of these drugs, leading to increased or decreased levels of medication in the bloodstream.

This could result in adverse reactions or reduced efficacy, making it essential to monitor liver function closely when using Larix occidentalis.

4. Stimulates heart rate

Larix occidentalis stimulates heart rate due to its high concentration of beta-phenylethylamine, a sympathomimetic amine that mimics the effects of epinephrine in the body.

This chemical triggers an increase in heart rate and blood pressure, leading to increased cardiac activity.

Additionally, the plant's alkaloids, such as podophyllotoxin, may also contribute to its stimulatory effect on the cardiovascular system.

5. Interacts with medications

Larix occidentalis interacts with medications by altering their absorption and metabolism in the body.

This can lead to unpredictable interactions with prescription drugs, causing a range of side effects from mild to severe.

The mechanism behind these interactions is thought to be due to the herb's ability to affect the activity of certain enzymes in the liver and gut, which are responsible for breaking down medications.

6. Produces gastrointestinal issues

Larix occidentalis produces gastrointestinal issues when used.

This is due to its ability to stimulate digestive enzymes, leading to increased motility and secretion of gastric juices. As a result, individuals may experience symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and stomach pain.

The potential for gastrointestinal irritation is particularly high when consuming large doses or combining western larch with other substances that affect digestion.

7. Induces dizziness or lightheadedness

Larix occidentalis induces dizziness or lightheadedness due to its ability to lower blood pressure and affect the nervous system.

The flavonoids present in the tree's bark, needles, and seeds may contribute to this effect by causing a decrease in peripheral resistance, leading to decreased blood flow to the brain.

Additionally, the beta-amyrin triterpenoids found in western larch have been shown to exert a sedative effect on the central nervous system, further contributing to feelings of dizziness or lightheadedness.

8. Weakens immune system

Larix occidentalis weakens immune system.

This is attributed to its suppressive effects on natural killer cell activity and interleukin-2 production, which are essential components of the immune response.

Additionally, the polyphenolic compounds present in Larix occidentalis have been shown to inhibit the activation of immune cells, further contributing to the weakening of the immune system.

9. Raises blood sugar levels

Larix occidentalis raises blood sugar levels due to its ability to stimulate insulin resistance and reduce glucose uptake in the body.

This occurs when the herb's compounds, such as terpenoids and flavonoids, bind to insulin receptors, disrupting normal glucose metabolism and leading to increased blood sugar levels.

As a result, individuals with diabetes or pre-diabetic conditions may experience exacerbated symptoms.

10. Causes respiratory distress

Larix occidentalis causes respiratory distress due to its allergenic properties and potential to release volatile organic compounds (VOCs).

These VOCs can trigger an inflammatory response in the lungs, leading to symptoms such as wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.

Additionally, the plant's pollen can exacerbate existing respiratory conditions like asthma, making it essential for individuals with these conditions to take necessary precautions when handling or being around Larix occidentalis.
