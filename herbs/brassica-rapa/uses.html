Chinese Cabbage (Brassica rapa) Uses

Chinese Cabbage is used to treat respiratory infections, cough, influenza, bronchitis, and respiratory congestion. It helps soothe mucous membrane irritation and allergic rhinitis. It also addresses skin infections and inflammatory skin conditions due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

Chinese Cabbage (Brassica rapa) Monograph

1. Respiratory infections

Chinese Cabbage is used in traditional herbal medicine to treat respiratory infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps alleviate symptoms like coughing and congestion by supporting immune function and reducing mucus production. It is often prepared as a decoction or added to soups for therapeutic benefit.

2. Cough

Chinese Cabbage is used in traditional Chinese medicine to alleviate cough symptoms. It helps to moisten the lungs and reduce phlegm, making it effective for dry or productive coughs. Typically prepared as a decoction, it is often combined with other herbs to enhance its therapeutic effects.

3. Influenza

Chinese Cabbage is used in traditional Chinese medicine to alleviate influenza symptoms due to its high vitamin C content, which supports immune function. It is often prepared as a soup or decoction to reduce fever, cough, and body aches associated with the flu. Its anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties help in shortening the duration of illness.

4. Bronchitis

Chinese Cabbage is used in traditional herbal medicine to treat bronchitis due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. It helps reduce mucus buildup and soothe respiratory irritation. Often prepared as a decoction, it supports respiratory health and alleviates coughing and congestion associated with bronchial inflammation.

5. Respiratory congestion

Chinese Cabbage is used to alleviate respiratory congestion due to its expectorant properties. It helps loosen mucus and reduce inflammation in the airways. Traditionally, it is prepared as a decoction or steam infusion to ease coughing and clear phlegm. Its mild nature makes it suitable for long-term use in respiratory conditions.

6. Mucous membrane irritation

Chinese Cabbage can irritate mucous membranes due to its high sulfur content and acidic compounds. This irritation is commonly experienced when consuming raw or undercooked leaves. It may cause throat discomfort or stomach upset in sensitive individuals. Cooking reduces the risk but does not eliminate it entirely.

7. Allergic rhinitis

Chinese Cabbage contains compounds with anti-inflammatory properties that may help alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis. It is traditionally used to support respiratory health and reduce nasal congestion. Its high vitamin C content also boosts the immune system, potentially reducing the frequency and severity of allergic reactions.

8. Coughing

Chinese Cabbage is used to alleviate coughing due to its expectorant properties. It helps loosen mucus and reduce throat irritation. Typically consumed as a decoction or soup, it supports respiratory health. It is particularly effective for dry or productive coughs.

9. Skin infections

Chinese Cabbage has antimicrobial properties that help treat skin infections. It can be applied topically as a poultice to reduce inflammation and kill bacteria. Its high vitamin C content also promotes skin healing. Regular use may prevent recurring infections and support overall skin health.

10. Inflammatory skin conditions

Chinese Cabbage, or Brassica rapa, is used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat inflammatory skin conditions due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and infection in conditions like eczema and psoriasis. Its high vitamin C content also supports skin healing and immune function.
