Chinese Cabbage (Brassica rapa) Compounds

Chinese Cabbage contains several primary active compounds, including flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties. Phenolic acids and tannins contribute to its anti-inflammatory effects. Glycosides and saponins may support cardiovascular health. Coumarin and terpenoids are known for their antimicrobial properties, while essential oils and anthocyanins provide additional therapeutic benefits. Alkaloids also play a role in its medicinal value.

Chinese Cabbage (Brassica rapa) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Chinese Cabbage (Brassica rapa). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds may support immune function and help reduce oxidative stress in the body. Flavonoids are a key group of phytochemicals in this medicinal plant.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Chinese Cabbage (Brassica rapa). It exhibits antioxidant properties that help reduce oxidative stress and inflammation. This compound contributes to the plant's therapeutic potential in supporting immune function and overall health.

3. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Chinese Cabbage (Brassica rapa). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins in Chinese Cabbage may support digestive health and help reduce inflammation in the body.

4. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Chinese Cabbage (Brassica rapa). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by supporting respiratory health and reducing inflammation. Glycosides in Chinese Cabbage are known for their potential to enhance immune function and aid in detoxification processes.

5. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound found in Chinese Cabbage (Brassica rapa). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Saponin also supports digestive health and may help in reducing cholesterol levels.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a compound found in Chinese Cabbage (Brassica rapa). It exhibits anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory properties, contributing to the plant's traditional use in promoting circulation and reducing swelling. Coumarin also shows potential in supporting digestive health and may have antimicrobial effects.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of organic compounds found in Chinese Cabbage (Brassica rapa). They contribute to the plant's medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. These compounds are often responsible for the plant's distinct aroma and potential therapeutic benefits in herbal medicine.

8. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound in Chinese Cabbage (Brassica rapa). It contains volatile compounds like monoterpenes and sesquiterpenes, which contribute to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The essential oil is often extracted through steam distillation and used in traditional herbal remedies for respiratory and digestive health.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Chinese Cabbage (Brassica rapa). It contributes to the plant's vibrant color and has antioxidant properties that support immune function and reduce inflammation. This compound is valued in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Chinese Cabbage (Brassica rapa). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Alkaloids in Chinese Cabbage are studied for their potential therapeutic applications in herbal medicine.
