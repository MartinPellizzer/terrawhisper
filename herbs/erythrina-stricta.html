Erythrina stricta

What's the taxonomical classification of Erythrina stricta?

Erythrina stricta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent through the order Fabales, it is placed in the family Fabaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by the genus Erythrina and is specifically designated as the species stricta.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Erythrina stricta contains medicinal compounds primarily located in its bark, leaves, and roots. The bark is frequently harvested to prepare decoctions used for treating various ailments including gastrointestinal issues and inflammation. Extracts from the leaves are also utilized in traditional remedies to manage fever and skin conditions. Some practitioners employ the roots to address specific neurological or sedative needs due to their chemical properties. These different plant parts are processed through boiling or infusion to release their therapeutic alkaloids.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Erythrina stricta targets various neuroreceptors and enzymes, most notably acting as a modulator of GABA-A receptors and inhibiting acetylcholinesterase to influence neurotransmission. The plant contains various alkaloids that interact with nicotinic acetylcholine receptors, which contributes to its sedative and anticonvulsant properties. These bioactive compounds also show potential for interacting with cyclooxygenase enzymes, providing a mechanism for its observed anti-inflammatory effects. Additionally, some constituents may influence monoamine oxidase activity, affecting the regulation of mood-related neurotransmitters. Through these diverse protein interactions, the plant exerts its systemic medicinal effects on the central nervous and inflammatory systems.

What are the therapeutic actions of Erythrina stricta?

Erythrina stricta has medicinal properties that include acting as an analgesic, an anti-inflammatory agent, and a treatment for various skin ailments.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Erythrina stricta can cause adverse reactions such as nausea, vomiting, dizziness, and abdominal pain if ingested in significant quantities. The plant contains various alkaloids that may exert toxic effects on the central nervous system if misused. Some individuals might experience skin irritation or allergic dermatitis following direct contact with the plant's tissues. Because its chemical profile is complex, it should be treated with caution to avoid unpredictable physiological responses. Careful avoidance is recommended for anyone sensitive to botanical alkaloids.
