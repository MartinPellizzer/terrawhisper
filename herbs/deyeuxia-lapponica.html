Deyeuxia lapponica

What's the taxonomical classification of Deyeuxia lapponica?

Deyeuxia lapponica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Poales and falls into the family Poaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific genus, Deyeuxia, and its species designation, lapponica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Deyeuxia lapponica has slender, erect stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and are accompanied by narrow, lanceolate leaves with parallel venation.

The foliage is characterized by a bright green color and a texture that is relatively smooth compared to other sedges. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in compact, spike-like inflorescences located at the terminal ends of the stems. These inflorescences consist of several individual spikelets that contain the reproductive structures of the plant.

The root system consists of creeping rhizomes that allow the species to spread through its subarctic habitat.
