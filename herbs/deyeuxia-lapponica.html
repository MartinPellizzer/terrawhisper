What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Deyeuxia lapponica has slender, erect stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and are accompanied by narrow, lanceolate leaves with parallel venation. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in compact, spike-like inflorescences located at the terminal ends of the stems. These inflorescences consist of several individual spikelets that contain the reproductive structures of the plant. The root system consists of creeping rhizomes that allow the species to spread through its subarctic habitat.
