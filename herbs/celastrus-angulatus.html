Celastrus angulatus

What's the taxonomical classification of Celastrus angulatus?

Celastrus angulatus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. Its hierarchical classification continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Celastrales. Within this order, it is a member of the family Celastraceae, which contains the genus Celastrus. Ultimately, the specific identity of this plant is defined by its species designation, anguletus.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Celastrus angulatus contains various bioactive phytochemicals including alkaloids, saponins, tannins, and terpenoids that serve as its primary medicinal components. These specific chemical classes are often studied for their potential antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties in traditional medicine. The presence of triterpenoids in the plant species is particularly noted for contributing to its pharmacological activities. Researchers analyze these secondary metabolites to understand how the plant might be used to treat various ailments. Such chemical complexity allows the plant to interact with diverse biological pathways in medicinal applications.

What are the therapeutic actions of Celastrus angulatus?

Celastrus anguatus has medicinal properties used primarily to treat skin diseases, inflammatory conditions, and various internal ailments through its bioactive phytochemical constituents. The plant contains alkaloids and terpenoids that contribute to its potent antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory activities. Extracts from the bark and leaves are frequently applied topically to soothe irritations or manage fungal infections. Some traditional practices also utilize the plant to address digestive issues and fever by regulating physiological responses. These therapeutic effects are attributed to the synergistic action of its organic compounds on biological pathways.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat ailments such as malaria, fever, skin infections, and various inflammatory conditions. Practitioners often utilize the bark or roots to prepare decoctions meant to reduce high temperatures. It also holds traditional importance in managing digestive disturbances and promoting wound healing. Some local medicinal practices incorporate the plant to address localized swelling or external skin irritations. Through these various applications, the species serves as a key resource in traditional botanical medicine.
