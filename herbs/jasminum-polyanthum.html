What's the taxonomical classification of Jasminum polyanthum?

Jasminum polyanthum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Lamiales. The plant is a member of the family Oleaceae and is specifically categorized within the genus Jasminum, ultimately reaching its unique scientific designation as the species polyanthum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Jasminum polyanthum has a vigorous, twining woody growth habit characterized by slender, arching stems that can reach lengths of several meters. During the blooming season, the plant produces dense, fragrant clusters of small, star-shaped white flowers. These blossoms emerge from axillary cymes and are supported by thin, green pedicels. The overall structure allows the plant to effectively scramble over trellises or climb through dense vegetation.
