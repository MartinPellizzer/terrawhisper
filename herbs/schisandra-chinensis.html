What to know about Schisandra chinensis before using it medicinally

Schisandra chinensis, commonly known as schisandra, is a medicinal herb that has been used to treat various health conditions, including respiratory issues, digestive problems, and cardiovascular diseases.

The herb has shown to provide several health benefits, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and anti-aging properties, which contribute to its potential in reducing the risk of chronic diseases. The main therapeutic properties of schisandra include its adaptogenic, immunomodulatory, and hepatoprotective effects, making it a valuable resource for promoting overall well-being. The bioactive compounds responsible for the herb's medicinal properties include lignans, schisantherin, and gomisin, which have been shown to exhibit antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and anti-cancer activities.

In traditional Chinese medicine, schisandra is often prepared as a tea or tincture, known as wu wei zi or five-flavor berry, which is used to enhance vitality and promote longevity.
