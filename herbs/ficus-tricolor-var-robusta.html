Ficus tricolor var. robusta

Ficus tricolor var. robusta, commonly known as the three-colored fig or robust three-colored fig, is a plant species belonging to the Moraceae family.

It is valued for its potential health benefits, which include reducing inflammation, improving cardiovascular health, and acting as an antioxidant.

Studies suggest that Ficus tricolor var. robusta contains bioactive constituents such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, which contribute to its therapeutic actions like antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant activities.

The plant's leaves and roots are used in traditional medicine, and further research is needed to fully understand its medicinal properties and potential applications.

Medicinal Parts of Ficus tricolor var. robusta

Ficus tricolor var. robusta has many medicinal parts, such as leaves, roots, bark, and latex.

The leaves of Ficus tricolor var. robusta contain flavonoids, alkaloids, and glycosides, which have been traditionally used to treat fever and inflammation.

The roots of the plant contain saponins, which have been found to exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

Additionally, the latex of Ficus tricolor var. robusta contains ficin, a proteolytic enzyme with potential anti-inflammatory and wound-healing properties.

Possible Side Effects of Ficus tricolor var. robusta

Improper handling of the plant may result in respiratory issues, such as asthma-like symptoms, in individuals with pre-existing conditions.
