What is Phyllanthus pervilleanus?

Phyllanthus pervilleanus is a species of flowering plant in the family Phyllanthaceae.

It is a shrub that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, with a glabrous stem and branches. The leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, 2-6 centimeters long, and 1-3 centimeters wide, with a glabrous upper surface and a pubescent lower surface. The inflorescence is a cymose panicle, bearing small, greenish-yellow flowers, while the fruit is a 3-loculed capsule.

Phyllanthus pervilleanus is native to New Caledonia, where it grows in humid, shaded forests.
