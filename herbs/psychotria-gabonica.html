What's the taxonomical classification of Psychotria gabonica?

Psychotria gabonica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its hierarchical lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Gentianaales. This plant is a member of the Rubiaceae family, which is further defined by its membership in the genus Psychotria, ultimately leading to its specific identification as the species Psychotria gabonica.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Psychotria gabonica contains high concentrations of N,N-dimethyltryptamine, which is a potent hallucinogenic alkaloid. This compound acts as a powerful agonist at serotonin receptors in the brain to produce intense visual and auditory effects. The plant is most commonly used in traditional West African spiritual practices to induce altered states of consciousness. When combined with a monoamine oxidase inhibitor, the alkaloids become orally active and enter the bloodstream.
