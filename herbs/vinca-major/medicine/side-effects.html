10 Possible Side Effects Of Vinca Major (Periwinkle)

Vinca major has some side effects when used improperly, such as nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea.

These side effects can be caused by the plant's high concentration of vinblastine and vincristine, which can interfere with the body's ability to absorb nutrients and cause stomach irritation.

If left unattended, these symptoms can worsen, leading to weight loss, fatigue, and decreased appetite, ultimately affecting one's quality of life.

This adverse reaction is often dose-dependent, meaning it may occur more frequently at higher doses.
