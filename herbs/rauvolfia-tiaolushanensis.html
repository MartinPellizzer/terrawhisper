Rauvolfia tiaolushanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several bioactive indole alkaloids, most notably reserpine, ajmaline, and ajmalicine. Reserpine acts as a potent antipsychotic and antihypertensive agent by depleting catecholamine stores in the nervous system. Ajmaline serves as a critical compound in the treatment of cardiac arrhythmias due to its ability to affect sodium channels. Additionally, the plant produces various other alkaloids that demonstrate significant sedative and vasodilatory properties.
