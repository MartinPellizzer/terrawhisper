Apios fortunei

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Apios fortunei has climbing herbaceous stems that reach several meters in length, trifoliate leaves with ovate to elliptical leaflets, and small, fragrant, pea-like flowers arranged in axillary racemes. The plant utilizes twining stems to ascend through surrounding vegetation using its delicate foliage. Its leaves feature serrated margins and a soft texture that distinguishes it from similar climbing legumes. The inflorescences typically bear pale pink to white blossoms that attract various pollinators. Below the surface, the plant develops tuberous roots that serve as primary energy storage organs.

      What side-effects this plant can have?
