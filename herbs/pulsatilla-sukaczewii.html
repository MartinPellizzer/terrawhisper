Pulsatilla sukaczewii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pulsatilla sukaczewii has a basal rosette of deeply divided, oblong to lanceolate leaves that are covered in dense, silky white hairs on both surfaces. The flowering stems emerge from this foliage, bearing solitary, bell-shaped flowers that transition from a deep purple to a lighter shade as they mature. Each bloom is characterized by numerous long, prominent stamens and a central cluster of carpels that remain visible after petal fall. The plant also features a thick, woody taproot that assists in nutrient storage within its rocky habitat. Fine pubescence covers the stems and calyx, providing a soft texture across the entire reproductive structure.
