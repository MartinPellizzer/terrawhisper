Pulsatilla sukaczewii

What's the taxonomical classification of Pulsatilla sukaczewii?

Pulsatilla sukaczewii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, the plant falls under the order Ranunculales and is a member of the family Ranunculaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Pulsatilla, and its unique species designation, sukaczewii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pulsatilla sukaczewii has a basal rosette of deeply divided, oblong to lanceolate leaves that are covered in dense, silky white hairs on both surfaces.

The flowering stems emerge from this foliage, bearing solitary, bell-shaped flowers that transition from a deep purple to a lighter shade as they mature. Each bloom is characterized by numerous long, prominent stamens and a central cluster of carpels that remain visible after petal fall. The plant also features a thick, woody taproot that assists in nutrient storage within its rocky habitat.

Fine pubescence covers the stems and calyx, providing a soft texture across the entire reproductive structure.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Pulsatilla sukaczewii can cause severe gastrointestinal distress including nausea, vomiting, abdominal cramps, and diarrhea if ingested. The plant contains protoanemonin, a toxic chemical that acts as a potent irritant to mucous membranes. Contact with the raw plant material may lead to skin irritation, redness, or blistering in sensitive individuals. Ingesting significant quantities can result in more serious systemic issues such as convulsions or even death. Due to these high toxicity levels, the plant should never be consumed without expert pharmaceutical preparation.
