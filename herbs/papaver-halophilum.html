What is Papaver halophilum?

Papaver halophilum is a perennial plant that belongs to the family Papaveraceae, commonly known as the poppy family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and has a distinctive, upright habit, typically growing to a height of 20-40 cm. The plant's leaves are lance-shaped and gray-green in color, arranged oppositely on the stem. The flowers of Papaver halophilum are cup-shaped, with four or five petals that are typically pink or purple in color, and have a distinctive sheen.

The plant's seeds are contained within a distinctive, papery capsule that splits open to release the seeds, often after the plant has died.
