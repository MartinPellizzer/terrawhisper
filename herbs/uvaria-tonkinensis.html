Uvaria tonkinensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Uvaria tonkinensis has woody climbing vines characterized by alternate, elliptic to oblong leaves with entire margins and an obtuse apex. The foliage typically exhibits a coriaceous texture and a glossy green surface on the upper side. Its reproductive structures consist of small, yellowish flowers that emerge from the axils of the stems. These flowers develop into fleshy, somewhat globose or ovoid fruits that contain numerous seeds. The overall growth habit allows the plant to scramble through dense tropical forest canopies.
