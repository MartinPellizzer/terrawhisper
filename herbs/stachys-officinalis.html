Wood Betony

Stachys officinalis

Wood Betony (Stachys officinalis) is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, known for its historical use in traditional medicine to improve cognitive function and treat conditions such as memory loss and anxiety.

Botanical Identification

Wood Betony, or Stachys officinalis, is a perennial herb in the mint family (Lamiaceae). It is also known as betony or blue betony, and is native to Europe and western Asia. The plant has square stems, opposite leaves, and spikes of purple flowers. It has been historically used in herbal medicine for its purported calming and healing properties.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Lamiales
  • Order: Lamiales
  • Family: Lamiaceae
  • Genus: Stachys
  • Species: Stachys officinalis

Safety Profile

Wood Betony is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause mild digestive upset in some individuals. There are no well-documented contraindications, but caution is advised during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to limited research on its effects.
