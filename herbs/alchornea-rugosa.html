Alchornea rugosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Alchornea rugosa has woody stems characterized by a rough, rugose bark texture and simple, alternate leaves with distinctively wrinkled surfaces. The foliage typically displays an elliptic to lanceolate shape with serrated margins and prominent venation. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in terminal panicles, which are common for this shrubby species. The overall structure often forms a dense, spreading bush that adapts well to various scrubland habitats. These morphological features collectively help the plant withstand its specific environmental conditions.
