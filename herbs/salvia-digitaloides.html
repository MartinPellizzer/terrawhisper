Salvia digitaloides

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Salvia digitaloides has upright, square-shaped stems that support opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins and a textured, rugose surface. The foliage is typically dark green and displays prominent venation across the leaf blades. Its inflorescence consists of verticillasters arranged along the upper portions of the flowering spikes. The flowers are tubular and bilabiate, featuring a distinct color palette often ranging from pale blue to violet. These floral structures are specialized to facilitate pollination through their specific labiate morphology.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Salvias digitaloides can cause heart rhythm irregularities, nausea, dizziness, and blurred vision due to the presence of cardiac glycosides in its tissues. These potent chemical compounds mimic the effects of digitalis, which can lead to dangerous fluctuations in heart rate if ingested. Individuals experiencing toxicity might also suffer from gastrointestinal distress or confusion. Because the plant affects the electrical conduction of the heart, even small amounts can be life-threatening for sensitive populations. Careful avoidance is necessary to prevent systemic poisoning and serious medical emergencies.
