Salvia digitaloides

What's the taxonomical classification of Salvia digitaloides?

Salvia digitaloides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Lamiales. Furthermore, it is a member of the family Lamiaceae, which is characterized by its distinct morphological traits. Finally, its taxonomic hierarchy is completed by its placement within the genus Salvia, specifically identified as the species digitaloides.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Salvia digitaloides has upright, square-shaped stems that support opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins and a textured, rugose surface. The foliage is typically dark green and displays prominent venation across the leaf blades. Its inflorescence consists of verticillasters arranged along the upper portions of the flowering spikes. The flowers are tubular and bilabiate, featuring a distinct color palette often ranging from pale blue to violet. These floral structures are specialized to facilitate pollination through their specific labiate morphology.

What are the pharmacological activities of Salvia digitaloides?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological activities including potent anti-inflammatory and neuroprotective properties. These biological effects are primarily attributed to the presence of bioactive secondary metabolites such as diterpenoids and phenolic compounds. Research indicates that these constituents work to inhibit pro-inflammatory cytokines and reduce oxidative stress within cellular environments. Such mechanisms suggest that the plant could potentially serve as a natural source for treating neurodegenerative disorders. Furthermore, the ability to mitigate inflammation helps preserve neuronal integrity against various chemical insults.

According to a study published by "Phytochemistry", the 95% ethanol extract of the whole plants of Salvia digitaloides contains several bioactive compounds including two undescribed norabietane-type diterpenoids, two undescribed germacrane-type sesquiterpenoids, five undescribed guaiane-type sesquiterpenoid lactones, and two undescribed noreudesmane-type sesquiterpenoids. Regarding pharmacological activities, sadigitaloide A showed noticeable anti-inflammatoty activity at a concentration of 100.0 μM against LPS-induced TNF-α production in rat macrophage NR8383 cells. Furthermore, sadigitaloide B exhibited better protection of dopaminergic neurons than the positive control at a concentration of 60 μM in the Caenorhabditis elegans model. Some compounds also demonstrated phytotoxic activities through inhibitory effects on the growth of roots and hypocotyls of seedlings, with cis, trans-abscisic acid showing inhibition rates higher than glyphosate at concentrations ranging from 50 to 400 ppm.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds Saldigones A-C, sadigitaloides A, sadigitaloides B, sadigitaloides C, and sadigitaloides D. These specific diterpenoids are primarily isolated from the leaves and stems of the Salvia digitaloides species. Researchers study these molecules to understand their potential bioactive properties and pharmacological significance. Each unique compound within this group contributes to the complex chemical profile of the plant. Scientific analysis of these substances helps identify how they might interact with biological systems.

According to a study published by "Natural products and bioprospecting", the roots of Salvia digitaloides contain three new isoprenylated flavonoids named Saldigoones A-C (1, 3, 4) featuring flavanone, pterocarpan, and isoflavanone architectures. These isolates were identified alongside 1 known isoprenylated flavanone (2). All of the compounds were evaluated for their activities on CaV3.1 low voltage-gated Ca2+ channels (LVGCC). Among these, compound 2 was found to strongly and dose-dependently inhibit the CaV3.1 peak current.
