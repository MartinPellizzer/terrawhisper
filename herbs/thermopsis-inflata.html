Thermopsis inflata

What's the taxonomical classification of Thermopsis inflata?

Thermopsis inflata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is situated in the order Fabales. It is a member of the family Fabaceae, which places it within the well-known legume group. Finally, its specific taxonomic identification is defined by the genus Thermopsis and the species inflata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Thermopsis inflata has upright, herbaceous stems that reach heights of 0.5 to 1.5 meters and are characterized by distinctly inflated, bladder-like pods. The leaves are trifoliate with ovate to lanceolate leaflets that possess serrated margins. Small, pea-like flowers emerge in clusters, typically displaying a pale yellow to cream color. These plants often grow in rocky or montane environments where their sturdy structure can withstand varied conditions. The unique swollen shape of the seed pods serves as a primary diagnostic feature for distinguishing this species from its relatives.
