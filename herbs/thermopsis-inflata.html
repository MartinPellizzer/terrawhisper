Thermopsis inflata

What's the taxonomical classification of Thermopsis inflata?

Thermopsis inflata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is situated in the order Fabales. It is a member of the family Fabaceae, which places it within the well-known legume group. Finally, its specific taxonomic identification is defined by the genus Thermopsis and the species inflata.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Thermopsis inflata contains the potent steroid alkaloid cytisine, which acts as a nicotinic acetylcholine receptor agonist. This specific compound is known for its ability to stimulate the central nervous system and influence respiratory functions. Beyond cytisine, the plant roots house various other alkaloids that contribute to its complex chemical profile. These substances have been historically studied for their potential roles in treating respiratory ailments and as potential tools in neurological research. However, the high toxicity of these medicinal compounds requires extreme caution during any pharmacological application.

What are the therapeutic actions of Thermopsis inflata?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various conditions including chronic cough, bronchitis, asthma, and other respiratory ailments characterized by mucus buildup. Its active component, cytisine, acts as an expectorant to help loosen phlegm and clear the airways. Historically, it has also been applied in small doses to address certain digestive issues and as a mild sedative. However, the plant contains potent alkaloids that require extreme caution due to potential toxicity. Medical professionals typically limit its application to specific controlled therapeutic contexts.
