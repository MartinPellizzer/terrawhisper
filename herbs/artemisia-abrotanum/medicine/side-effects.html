10 Possible Side Effects Of Artemisia Abrotanum

Artemisia abrotanum has some side effects when used improperly, such as allergic reactions, skin irritation, and digestive issues.

These side effects can occur due to individual sensitivities or excessive consumption of the herb. For instance, skin irritation can lead to severe eczema, while allergic reactions can cause anaphylaxis.

Digestive issues can result in abdominal pain, nausea, and diarrhea, impacting daily routines and causing discomfort.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Artemisia abrotanum causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

This is due to the presence of sesquiterpene lactones and flavonoids in the plant's essential oil, which can trigger an immune response and lead to symptoms such as skin irritation, itching, and rashes.

Additionally, the plant's volatile compounds may cause respiratory issues, including congestion and coughing.

2. Triggers respiratory issues

Artemisia abrotanum triggers respiratory issues due to its high concentration of volatile oils and saponins.

These compounds can cause irritation and inflammation in the airways, leading to symptoms such as coughing, wheezing, and shortness of breath.

Additionally, the plant's aromatic properties may stimulate the lungs and trigger bronchospasm in some individuals.

3. Induces vomiting suddenly

Artemisia abrotanum induces vomiting suddenly due to its ability to stimulate the digestive system and increase gastric contractions.

The herb's volatile oils and flavonoids can irritate the stomach lining, causing sudden and intense vomiting in some individuals.

Additionally, Artemisia abrotanum has been found to slow down digestion, allowing undigested food to stagnate in the stomach, which may further contribute to the occurrence of nausea and vomiting.

4. Irritates skin severely

Artemisia abrotanum irritates skin severely.

This occurs due to its high content of volatile oils, particularly thujone and camphor, which can cause inflammation, redness, and itching upon direct contact with the skin.

The potent compounds in southernwood can also lead to dryness, cracking, and blistering, making it essential to exercise caution when handling or applying this herb topically.

5. Affects digestive systems

Artemisia abrotanum affects digestive systems in some individuals.

This is likely due to its ability to stimulate the production of stomach acid and bile, which can irritate the digestive tract and lead to symptoms such as bloating, gas, and stomach discomfort.

The herb's bitter compounds may also slow down digestion, causing food to sit in the stomach for longer periods than normal, further contributing to these issues.

6. Increases heart rate rapidly

Artemisia abrotanum increases heart rate rapidly due to its stimulating properties on the nervous system.

The volatile oils present in this herb, such as camphor and borneol, can cause an increase in sympathetic tone, leading to a rapid rise in heart rate.

This is because these oils directly stimulate the cardiovascular system, increasing both systolic and diastolic blood pressure and heart rate, resulting in a heightened state of arousal.

7. Decreases blood pressure sharply

Artemisia abrotanum decreases blood pressure sharply.

This occurs due to its ability to increase the activity of certain enzymes that help break down nitric oxide in the body, leading to vasodilation and a subsequent decrease in blood pressure. Additionally, the herb's bitter constituents can also stimulate the vagus nerve, which regulates heart rate, resulting in a further drop in blood pressure.

As a result, individuals taking Artemisia abrotanum may experience a significant decline in their blood pressure.

8. Causes kidney damage slowly

Artemisia abrotanum causes kidney damage slowly due to its ability to accumulate in the body and potentially interact with certain medications.

The plant's bioactive compounds, including sesquiterpenes and flavonoids, may contribute to this effect by altering the normal functioning of kidney cells and reducing their ability to filter waste products from the bloodstream.

Over time, this can lead to progressive kidney damage and potentially life-threatening complications.

9. Triggers anxiety attacks

Artemisia abrotanum triggers anxiety attacks due to its ability to stimulate the nervous system and increase alertness.

The plant's chemical compounds, including thujone and camphor, can cause an overstimulation of the brain, leading to feelings of unease, restlessness, and fear.

Additionally, the plant's aromatic properties may also contribute to anxiety by causing a sense of overwhelm or claustrophobia in some individuals.

10. Interacts with medications

Artemisia abrotanum interacts with medications, particularly anticoagulants and antiplatelet agents, potentially leading to increased risk of bleeding.

This is due to the plant's ability to enhance the effects of these medications, causing an excessive thinning of blood.

Additionally, Artemisia abrotanum may interact with sedatives and antidepressants, increasing the risk of drowsiness, confusion, and other central nervous system depressant effects.
