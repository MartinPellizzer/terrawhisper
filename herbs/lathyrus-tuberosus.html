Lathyrus tuberosus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lathyros tuberosus has prostrate, herbaceous stems that form dense mats, trifoliate leaves with oblong leaflets, and bright yellow pea-like flowers. The plant is characterized by its creeping growth habit, which allows it to spread across open or disturbed ground. Its leaves typically consist of three leaflets that are relatively small and often possess a slightly hairy texture. The inflorescence features solitary or paired flowers that emerge from the leaf axils to attract pollinators. Additionally, the species develops specialized tuberous root structures that serve as significant storage organs for nutrients and water.
