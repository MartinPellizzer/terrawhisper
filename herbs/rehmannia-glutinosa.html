Rehmannia glutinosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Rehmannia glutinosa?

Rehmannia glutinosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy places it under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Further classification identifies it as a member of the order Lamiales and the family Orobanchaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Rehmannia and is specifically identified by the species name glutinosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rehmannia glutinosa has an herbaceous, perennial life cycle characterized by a fleshy, tuberous rhizome that is typically dark brown or black when dried. The plant features large, ovate to broadly lanceolate leaves with serrated margins and a somewhat rugose texture. Its inflorescence consists of terminal racemes that bear small, zygomorphic flowers which range in color from pale yellow to deep purple. The stem is erect and can reach heights of up to one meter depending on the environmental conditions. These morphological features collectively distinguish the species within the Scrophulariaceae family.

What are the pharmacological activities of Rehmannia glutinosa?

This plant has anti-inflammatory, anti-diabetic, anti-oxidant, hepatoprotective, and neuroprotective pharmacological activities. Its antioxidant properties help neutralize free radicals, thereby reducing oxidative stress throughout the body. The plant demonstrates anti-diabetic potential by assisting in the regulation of blood glucose levels. It also exhibits hepatoprotective effects that safeguard liver cells from chemical or toxic damage. Furthermore, its neuroprotective capabilities suggest it may help protect the nervous system from various degenerative processes.

As discussed in a study, four novel carotenoid derivatives named rehmannins A-D (1-4) were isolated from the fresh roots of Rehmannia glutinosa. These compounds, specifically rehmannins B-D (2-4), possess a novel tricyclic ring system bearing five continuous stereocenters on the heterocyclic core. The structures of these 4 new compounds were determined through extensive spectroscopic methods and X-ray diffraction analysis. Regarding its pharmacological activities, compound 3 exhibited the inhibition of NO secretion in LPS-induced RAW264.7 cells.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds acteoside, aucubin, catalpol, geniposidic acid, and loganin. These specific iridoid glycosides are known for providing various therapeutic benefits to the human body. Aucubin and catalpol often exhibit significant anti-inflammatory and neuroprotective properties in pharmacological studies. Additionally, the presence of geniposidic acid and loganin contributes to the plant's complex chemical profile used in traditional medicine. Acteoside further enhances these biological activities through its antioxidant potential.

As discussed in a study, Rehmannia glutinosa is a medicinal herb that contains the phenolic compound acteoside. This specific compound has been shown to inhibit the non-selective herbicide paraquat. The plant utilizes a phenylalanine ammonia-lyase (PAL) enzyme, specifically the RgPAL1 cDNA clone, to regulate the phenylpropanoid pathway that produces the acteoside moiety. The deduced 712 amino acid sequence of the open reading frame contains a conserved active site and potential phosphorylation sites. Research shows that RgPAL1 mRNA levels were higher in leaves than in flowers and roots. Furthermore, the transcript level and enzyme activity increased gradually from 6 to 24 h after exposure to paraquat or jasmonic acid.
