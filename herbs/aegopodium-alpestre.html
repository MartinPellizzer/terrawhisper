Aegopodium alpestre

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aegopodium alpestre has compound leaves that are typically divided into several leaflets with serrated margins and a somewhat hairy texture. The plant grows from a creeping rhizome which allows it to form dense colonies in its alpine habitat. Its stems are upright and can reach varying heights depending on the environmental conditions of the subalpine zone. The flowers are small and arranged in terminal clusters that appear during the summer months. These botanical features help the species thrive in rocky, high-altitude terrains.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Aegopodium alpestre can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested or handled improperly. The plant contains various bioactive compounds that may trigger hypersensitivity reactions in individuals with sensitive skin. Direct contact with the foliage or stems often results in redness, itching, or localized swelling. If consumed, the chemical constituents might lead to nausea, abdominal cramping, or vomiting. People with known allergies to the Asteraceae family should exercise extreme caution when encountering this species.
