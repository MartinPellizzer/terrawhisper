What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Myrica prostata has a prostrate growth habit characterized by creeping, woody stems that spread horizontally across the ground and rooting at the nodes. These leaves possess a somewhat leathery texture with finely serrated margins and a dull green coloration. The plant produces inconspicuous flowers that lack large petals, often appearing in small clusters along the stems.
