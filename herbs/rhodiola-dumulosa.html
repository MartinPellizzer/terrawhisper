Rhodiola dumulosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Rhodiola dumulosa?

Rhodiola dumulosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Saxifragales. Within this order, the plant is a member of the family Crassulaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by its placement in the genus Rhodiola, with the species name being dumulosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhodiola dumulosa has a low-growing, woody perennial habit characterized by thick, succulent rhizomes and dense, shrubby clumps of leaves. The leaves are typically small, lanceolate or oblong in shape, and possess a fleshy texture designed for water retention. These leaves are arranged alternately along the stems and often feature slightly serrated or smooth margins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that emerge from the leaf axils during its blooming period. Its overall structure is compact, allowing it to thrive in rocky or alpine environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude alpine regions of the Tibetan Plateau and parts of western China. It grows specifically in rocky crevices and mountain slopes located within high-elevation environments. Its distribution is primarily restricted to cold, subalpine, and alpine zones where thin soils are common. The species thrives in areas characterized by intense solar radiation and seasonal snow cover. This narrow ecological niche limits its presence to specific mountain ranges across East Asia.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Rhodiola dumulosa contains medicinal compounds primarily concentrated in its rhizomes and roots. These underground structures are harvested to extract bioactive substances like salidroside and rosavin. Once collected, the roots undergo drying and processing to prepare them for various herbal formulations. The chemical constituents found within these parts are utilized to help the body adapt to physical and mental stress. Consequently, the plant's subterranean tissues serve as the essential source for its adaptogenic properties.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a complex profile of medicinal compounds including rhodionin, various polysaccharides, total flavonoids, herbacetin-7-alpha-L-rhamnoside, and kaemperol. These bioactive substances work together to provide various physiological benefits to the human body. The specific concentration of rhodionin is often studied for its potential to enhance stress resistance. Additionally, the presence of polysaccharides and flavonoids contributes to the overall antioxidant properties of the extract. These chemical constituents are essential for the plant's classification as a potent adaptogen.

According to a study published by "Zhong yao cai = Zhongyaocai = Journal of Chinese medicinal materials", the rhizoma samples of Rhodiola dumulosa were extracted by 95% EtOH and successively extracted with Et2O, EtOAc, and n-BuOH. Five compounds were obtained from the EtOAc fraction of the plant. These identified structures include beta-sitosterol glucoside (I), chrysophanol-8-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside (II), rhodionin (III), crenuloside (IV), and herbacetin-7-O-(3"-beta-D-glucopyranosyl)-alpha-L-rhamnoside (V). Both chrysophanol-8-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside and crenuloside were obtained from this plant for the first time.
