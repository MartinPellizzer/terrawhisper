Alkanna (Alkanna Tinctoria Subsp. Tinctoria)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Alkanna tinctoria subsp. tinctoria.

Alkanna, scientifically known as Alkanna tinctoria subsp. tinctoria, is a medicinal herb native to the Middle East and parts of India, prized for its rich, reddish-orange root that has been used for centuries in traditional medicine. This adaptogen and natural dye source is known for its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which support immune function and digestive health. Historically, it has been used in Ayurvedic and Unani medicine to treat ailments such as ulcers, skin conditions, and respiratory issues, while also serving as a natural dye for textiles and cosmetics. In modern wellness practices, Alkanna is incorporated into herbal formulations for its potential to reduce inflammation and support gut health, and its unique red pigment has found applications in natural food coloring and skincare products. Its rare active compounds, such as alkannin and shikonin, contribute to its distinctive color and therapeutic profile, making it a valuable plant in both traditional and contemporary holistic health.
