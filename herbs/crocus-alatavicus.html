What's the taxonomical classification of Crocus alatavicus?

Crocus alatavicus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant follows the biological lineage of the order Asparagales. It is further categorized under the family Iridaeceae, which places it within the genus Crocus, eventually reaching its specific designation as the species alatavicus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Crocus alatavicus has slender, linear leaves that are grayish-green in color and feature a distinct white central stripe running longitudinally. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that typically exhibit shades of pale purple or violet with darker veins on the tepals. These blooms emerge from underground corms that serve as the primary nutrient storage organs for the species. The reproductive structures include three prominent stamens and a central style that often branches at the tip.
