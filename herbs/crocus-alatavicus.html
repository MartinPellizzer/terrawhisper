Crocus alatavicus

What's the taxonomical classification of Crocus alatavicus?

Crocus alatavicus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant follows the biological lineage of the order Asparagales. It is further categorized under the family Iridaeceae, which places it within the genus Crocus, eventually reaching its specific designation as the species alatavicus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Crocus alatavicus has slender, linear leaves that are grayish-green in color and feature a distinct white central stripe running longitudinally.

The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that typically exhibit shades of pale purple or violet with darker veins on the tepals. These blooms emerge from underground corms that serve as the primary nutrient storage organs for the species. The reproductive structures include three prominent stamens and a central style that often branches at the tip.

Each flower is supported by a protective sheath known as a prophyll during its initial development.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Crocin and crocetin act as potent antioxidants that help neutralize free radicals within biological systems. The aromatic compound safranal contributes to the plant's potential neuroprotective and antidepressant effects.

        Crocus alatavicus is prepared as a concentrated decoction or a liquid tincture extracted from its dried floral parts and underground corm structures.         What side-effects this plant can have?

        Crocus alatavicus can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or gastrointestinal distress if ingested by humans or pets. While this plant is primarily grown for its ornamental value, contact with its tissues may trigger redness or itching in sensitive individuals. Ingestion of the plant parts can lead to nausea, vomiting, or stomach pain due to unknown chemical compounds. Small children and domestic animals should be monitored closely to prevent accidental consumption. Always consult a medical professional or veterinarian if symptoms occur after contact or ingestion.
