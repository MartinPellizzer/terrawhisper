Crocus alatavicus

What's the taxonomical classification of Crocus alatavicus?

Crocus alatavicus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant follows the biological lineage of the order Asparagales. It is further categorized under the family Iridaeceae, which places it within the genus Crocus, eventually reaching its specific designation as the species alatavicus.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

While this plant is primarily grown for its ornamental value, contact with its tissues may trigger redness or itching in sensitive individuals. Ingestion of the plant parts can lead to nausea, vomiting, or stomach pain due to unknown chemical compounds. Small children and domestic animals should be monitored closely to prevent accidental consumption. Always consult a medical professional or veterinarian if symptoms occur after contact or ingestion.
