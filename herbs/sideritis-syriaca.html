Sideritis of Syria

Sideritis syriaca

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Sideritis syriaca, also known as Syrian sainfoin, belongs to the Lamiaceae family. It is a perennial herb native to Syria and surrounding regions. The plant has square stems, opposite leaves, and pink to purple flowers. It is occasionally referred to as "sideritis" in herbal texts, though this name is more commonly associated with other species in the genus.

Active Compounds

Sideritis syriaca contains key active compounds such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids. These compounds work synergistically to provide its therapeutic effects, influencing anti-inflammatory and antioxidant pathways. Their combined action enhances the herb's efficacy in traditional herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Phenolic acid
  • Terpenoid

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action
Respiratory Cough, inflammation, sore throat Expectorant, anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, antiviral, antihistaminic

Nervous Stress, anxiety, migraine, mental exhaustion, insomnia Anxiolytic, sedative, analgesic, tonic, adaptogenic

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for respiratory and digestive issues due to its anti-inflammatory properties.

Infusion: Used for mild digestive issues and as a calming tea for anxiety.

Tincture: Used for respiratory and nervous system support due to its stimulating effects.

Safety Profile

Sideritis syriaca is generally considered safe when used as directed. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to limited safety data. Individuals with allergies to related plants should also exercise caution.
