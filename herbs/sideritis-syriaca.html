Sideritis Of Syria (Sideritis syriaca)

Sideritis Of Syria (Sideritis syriaca) is a member of the Lamiaceae family, native to Lebanon, Syria, and Turkey. Traditionally, its leaves, flowers, and stems have been used for infusions, decoctions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its tonic, anti-inflammatory, and bitter actions, and has a long history of use in mediterranean herbal traditions, european herbal medicine, and tibetan medicine.

Flowers
Inflorescences are spike-like clusters with tubular, violet-blue flowers having five lobed corolla, actinomorphic symmetry, and prominent dark purple markings on the upper lobes.
Stems
Cylindrical, terete, pubescent stems with opposite branching, characterized by persistent stipules and a hairy surface.
