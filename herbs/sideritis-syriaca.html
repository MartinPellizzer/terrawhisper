What is Sideritis Of Syria (Sideritis Syriaca)?

Sideritis syriaca, also known as Sideritis of Syria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Syria, and is widely used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Sideritis of Syria is Sideritis syriaca. This plant belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Lamiales, order Lamiales, family Lamiaceae, genus Sideritis, and species Sideritis syriaca. It is commonly known as "Syringa" in the Levant region and "Sideritis" in the Balkans. The synonyms for this plant include Sideritis syriaca, Sideritis syriaca var. syriaca, and Sideritis syriaca subsp. syriaca.

Plant Description and Morphology

Sideritis of Syria is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with a compact and upright structure.

Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly serrated edge and a dense covering of fine hairs. The flowers are small, tubular, and arranged in clusters, displaying a pale yellow to cream color with a subtle, aromatic scent. The plant has a fibrous root system that is shallow and widespread, adapting to the rocky and arid soils of its native habitat.

The stems and leaves have a rough, slightly woody texture, while the flowers are soft and delicate to the touch.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Sideritis of Syria is native to the mountainous regions of Syria, particularly in the eastern and southern parts of the country.

It is also found in adjacent areas of Lebanon and parts of Turkey. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, preferring well-drained soils and rocky slopes. It is commonly found in elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,500 meters above sea level.

Its ecological conditions favor dry summers and mild, wet winters with moderate rainfall.
