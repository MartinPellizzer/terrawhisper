Toxicodendron pumilum

Toxicodendron pumilum, also known as the dwarf sumac or dwarf poison sumac, is a plant species native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its small size and reddish-green leaves.

It possesses medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant compounds, which have been used to treat various health conditions.

The health benefits of Toxicodendron pumilum include its potential to reduce fever, alleviate pain and swelling, and support the immune system, although its use should be approached with caution due to its toxic sap.

In herbal preparations, the leaves and twigs of the plant are typically dried and used in teas, infusions, or tinctures to harness its medicinal properties, but proper identification and handling are crucial to avoid skin irritation.
