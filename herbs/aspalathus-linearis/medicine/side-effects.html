10 Possible Side Effects Of Aspalathus Linearis

Aspalathus linearis has some side effects when used improperly, such as allergic reactions, digestive issues, and interactions with medications.

Causes of these side effects include excessive consumption, sensitivity, or mixing with other herbs.

For example, digestive issues can worsen irritable bowel syndrome, leading to abdominal pain, bloating, and discomfort, while allergic reactions can cause skin rashes, itching, and breathing difficulties.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Aspalathus linearis causes allergic reactions due to the presence of anthocyanins and flavonoids, which can stimulate an immune response in some individuals.

These compounds may bind to immunoglobulin E (IgE) antibodies, triggering the release of histamine and other inflammatory chemicals.

This reaction can lead to symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing in people who are sensitive to these plant compounds.

2. Increases blood pressure

Aspalathus linearis increases blood pressure by stimulating the sympathetic nervous system, which causes the body to release stress hormones like adrenaline and noradrenaline.

These hormones constrict blood vessels and increase heart rate, leading to a rise in blood pressure.

Additionally, the caffeine-like compounds present in rooibos tea can also contribute to this effect by increasing cardiac output and peripheral resistance.

3. Induces kidney stones

Aspalathus linearis induces kidney stones due to its high concentration of oxalates and other organic compounds that increase urine's acidity.

This can lead to the formation of calcium oxalate crystals in the kidneys, which may cause pain, inflammation, or blockages if not eliminated properly.

As a result, individuals consuming large amounts of rooibos tea or supplements may be at risk for developing kidney stones.

4. Affects thyroid function

Aspalathus linearis affects thyroid function due to its potential interaction with thyroid hormones.

The plant's high levels of flavonoids and polyphenols may inhibit the conversion of thyroxine (T4) to triiodothyronine (T3), leading to changes in thyroid hormone levels.

This interference can cause a range of symptoms including fatigue, weight gain, and depression, particularly in individuals with pre-existing thyroid conditions or those taking thyroid medications.

5. Interacts with medications

Aspalathus linearis interacts with medications due to its high concentration of polyphenolic compounds and flavonoids.

These compounds may enhance the effects of certain medications, such as blood thinners, or decrease the effectiveness of others, like antibiotics. This interaction can lead to adverse reactions, including increased bleeding risk or reduced antibiotic efficacy.

The specific nature of this interaction is still being researched, but it highlights the importance of being aware of potential drug interactions when using Aspalathus linearis supplements.

6. Causes insomnia

Aspalathus linearis causes insomnia due to its caffeine-like compound called aspalathin, which can stimulate the central nervous system and affect sleep patterns.

Additionally, rooibos contains a small amount of L-theanine, an amino acid that can promote alertness and arousal, further contributing to difficulty falling asleep.

7. Triggers anxiety attacks

Aspalathus linearis triggers anxiety attacks in some individuals.

This is attributed to its high concentration of caffeine, which can stimulate the central nervous system and increase alertness, leading to feelings of jitteriness and unease.

Additionally, the flavonoids present in rooibos may interact with other substances in the body, including neurotransmitters, to exacerbate anxiety-like symptoms.

8. Leads to digestive issues

Aspalathus linearis leads to digestive issues due to its high tannin content and potential interaction with other herbal remedies or medications.

The tannins can cause stomach upset, nausea, and diarrhea in some individuals, while interactions with other substances may lead to changes in bowel movements or appetite.

9. Causes diarrhea

Aspalathus linearis causes diarrhea due to its high concentration of caffeine-like compounds called aspalathin and nothofagin.

These molecules stimulate the gut and increase bowel movements, leading to loose stools and frequent trips to the bathroom. The potent diuretic properties of rooibos also contribute to dehydration, further exacerbating the diarrhea.

As a result, individuals using Aspalathus linearis may experience unpleasant symptoms.

10. Reduces blood sugar

Aspalathus linearis reduces blood sugar levels due to its ability to inhibit the activity of enzymes that break down starch and release glucose into the bloodstream.

This effect is thought to be mediated by the flavonoids and phenolic acids present in rooibos, which can interact with insulin receptors and improve glucose uptake by cells.

As a result, individuals taking rooibos tea or supplements may experience hypoglycemia (low blood sugar) if not consuming adequate carbohydrates.
