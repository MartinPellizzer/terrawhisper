Aspalathus Linearis (Aspalathus linearis) Compounds

Aspalathus linearis contains several primary compounds, including flavonoids, which act as antioxidants. Phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory properties. Tannins and glycosides support digestive health. Coumarin and anthocyanins provide additional antioxidant benefits. Saponins, terpenoids, essential oils, and alkaloids also contribute to its medicinal profile.

Aspalathus Linearis (Aspalathus linearis) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are key active compounds in Aspalathus Linearis, commonly known as rooibos. These compounds contribute to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting immune function and reducing inflammation. They are responsible for many of the health benefits associated with rooibos tea.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Aspalathus linearis, commonly known as rooibos. It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and support overall health. This compound is particularly noted for its role in reducing inflammation and promoting cardiovascular wellness.

3. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Aspalathus Linearis, commonly known as rooibos. It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health by binding to proteins and reducing inflammation. Tannins in rooibos are also believed to have antioxidant and antimicrobial effects.

4. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Aspalathus Linearis, commonly known as African green tea. It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by supporting cardiovascular health and reducing inflammation. This compound is part of the plant's active components that make it beneficial in herbal medicine.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Aspalathus Linearis, commonly known as African green tea. It contributes to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for promoting cardiovascular health and reducing oxidative stress.

6. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Aspalathus Linearis, commonly known as rooibos. It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress in the body. This compound is responsible for the plant's distinctive red color and plays a significant role in its potential health benefits.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Aspalathus linearis, commonly known as rooibos. It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties and may support cardiovascular health by helping to reduce cholesterol levels. Saponins are also known for their ability to enhance the absorption of certain nutrients.

8. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Aspalathus Linearis, commonly known as rooibos. These compounds contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, making them important for the medicinal uses of rooibos in herbal medicine.

9. Essential oil

Essential oil from Aspalathus linearis contains volatile compounds like limonene and myrcene. These components contribute to the plant's aromatic profile and may support its traditional use in herbal medicine. The essential oil is often extracted through steam distillation and is valued for its potential therapeutic properties.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloids in Aspalathus linearis, commonly known as rooibos, are naturally occurring compounds with potential medicinal properties. These alkaloids contribute to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine, though research is ongoing to fully understand their therapeutic effects.
