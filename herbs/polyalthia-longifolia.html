Polyalthia longifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Polyalthia longifolia?

Polyalthia longifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized as Equisetopsida, falling under the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Magnoliales and the family Annonaceae. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its genus, Polyalthia, and its specific species name, longifolia.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Polyalthia longifolia contains bioactive alkaloids, acetogenins, terpenoids, and flavonoids that provide various therapeutic properties. These secondary metabolites are primarily concentrated in the leaves and bark of the tree. The presence of specific alkaloids allows the plant to exhibit significant antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory activities. Additionally, the acetogenins found within the species have been studied for their potential cytotoxic effects against certain cancer cell lines. These chemical constituents collectively support the plant's traditional use in treating skin diseases and digestive issues.

What are the therapeutic actions of Polyalthia longifolia?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin diseases, respiratory ailments, digestive disorders, and various inflammatory conditions. The bark and leaves contain phytochemicals that exhibit antimicrobial and antioxidant properties beneficial for wound healing. It is frequently employed in traditional medicine to manage symptoms of fever and malaria. Additionally, extracts from the plant are utilized to alleviate intestinal issues and promote better digestive health. These therapeutic applications rely on the diverse bioactive compounds found within its various parts.
