Buchholzia coriacea

What's the taxonomical classification of Buchholzia coriacea?

Buchholzia coriacea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Brassicales and is a member of the family Capparaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Buchholzia and the species coriacea.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Buchholzia coriacea contains various bioactive phytochemicals including tannins, saponins, flavonoids, and terpenoids that provide its therapeutic properties. These organic compounds are primarily utilized in traditional medicine to address inflammatory conditions and skin ailments. The presence of tannins specifically contributes to its astringent qualities, which can aid in wound healing and tissue contraction. Additionally, the plant's chemical profile suggests potential antimicrobial and antioxidant activities. Researchers study these specific constituents to understand their effectiveness in treating various physiological stressors.

What are the therapeutic actions of Buchholzia coriacea?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, digestive issues, and inflammatory conditions. The decoction made from its bark or leaves is often applied topically to soothe irritated skin or promote the healing of open sores. When consumed internally, it serves as a remedy for stomach ailments and intestinal discomfort. Some traditional practices also utilize the plant to manage symptoms associated with fever and general inflammation. Its medicinal properties are primarily centered around its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory characteristics.

What are the herbal preparations of Buchholzia coriacea?

Buchholzia coriacea is prepared primarily as a decoction made by boiling the bark or the leaves in water to extract its medicinal properties. This liquid infusion is commonly ingested to treat various ailments such as respiratory issues or gastrointestinal distress. Some traditional practitioners also utilize the plant material in the form of topical washes or poultices applied directly to the skin. These external applications are often used to address localized inflammation or skin irritations. The specific method of preparation frequently depends on the particular health condition being treated within local folk medicine.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Buchholzia coriacea can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested or handled improperly. The plant contains chemical compounds that may trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals upon skin exposure. When consumed, the organic constituents might lead to nausea, vomiting, or stomach cramping. There is also limited scientific documentation regarding long-term systemic toxicity in humans. Therefore, caution should be exercised when working with or consuming any part of this species.
