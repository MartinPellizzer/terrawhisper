What's the taxonomical classification of Buchholzia coriacea?

Buchholzia coriacea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Brassicales and is a member of the family Capparaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Buchholzia and the species coriacea.

The plant also produces large, compound inflorescences that bear numerous small, white flowers. This complex pharmacological profile makes it a subject of interest for medicinal study.

As discussed in a study, Buchholzia coriacea seeds are used in Eastern Nigeria to treat feverish conditions, malaria, and sleeping sickness. In an experiment using 75 mice, where 25 mice were used per experiment and each was infected with 1.0 x 10^6 trypanosomes, the seed methanol extract showed immunostimulatory activity. The extract inhibited delayed hypersensitivity reaction by 20.9% at 250 mg/kg and by 20.8% at 500 mg/kg, while at 1000 mg/kg, the paw size increased by -101.9% compared to the control. The extract elevated the antibody titre from 1.60 ± 0.40 in the control group to 8.00 ± 3.58 for the 500 mg/kg group. Additionally, the extract increased total leucocytes counts and showed no clinical signs or death within 24 h post treatment during acute toxicity tests.

What side effects this plant can have?

If the sap accidentally enters the eyes, it may lead to intense pain and potential corneal damage.
