Buchholzia coriacea

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Buchholzia coriacea has a pinnate leaf structure consisting of long, leathery leaflets arranged along a central rachis that can reach heights of several meters. The individual leaflets are linear to lanceolate in shape and possess a distinctively thick, waxy texture. These leaves emerge from a large, fleshy rhizome that allows the plant to form dense colonies in swampy environments. The plant also produces large, compound inflorescences that bear numerous small, white flowers. These flowering structures are supported by sturdy stalks that rise significantly above the surrounding foliage.
