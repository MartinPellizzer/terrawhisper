Oenothera biennis

What's the taxonomical classification of Oenothera biennis?

Oenothera biennis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic journey continues through the order Myrtales and the family Onagraceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its genus, Oenothera, and its specific species designation, biennis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Oenothera biennis has a taproot that produces a basal rosette of oblong to lanceolate leaves in its first year, followed by an upright stem reaching up to 1 meter in height with alternate, linear-lanceolate leaves. The stems are often covered in fine, spreading hairs that give them a textured appearance. Large, four-petaled flowers emerge from the leaf axils, typically displaying bright yellow petals that can reach several centimeters in length. These flowers are supported by long, slender sepals and contain numerous stamens surrounding a central stigma. The plant eventually produces elongated, narrow seed capsules that split open to release numerous small, dark seeds.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to Europe and Asia but has become widely naturalized across much of North America and other temperate regions globally. It frequently colonizes disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and waste areas where sunlight is abundant. Its ability to spread rapidly allows it to establish populations in various climates ranging from temperate to subtropical zones. In many parts of the world, it is considered an invasive species due to its extensive range. Localized distributions often depend on human activity and soil disturbance patterns.

What are the pharmacological activities of Oenothera biennis?

This plant has anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, labor stimulating, and therapeutic properties used for the treatment of atopic dermatitis and acute menopausal syndrome. Its bioactive compounds work to reduce oxidative stress and systemic inflammation throughout the body. When applied topically, the plant's constituents assist in the management of atopic dermatitis by restoring skin barrier function. The botanical extracts are also utilized to alleviate the discomfort associated with acute menopausal syndrome. Additionally, certain components of the plant are recognized for their ability to provide labor stimulation during childbirth.

As discussed in a study, the oil extracted from the seeds of Oenothera biennis is traditionally used to treat conditions with an inflammatory component, such as eczema, asthma, rheumatoid arthritis, breast problems, premenstrual, and menopausal syndrome. Long-chain fatty alcohols isolated from the non-triacylglycerol fraction of the oil, consisting of hexacosanol (C26OH: 38.65%), tetracosanol (C24OH: 31.59%), docosanol (C22OH: 11.36%), and octacosanol (C28OH: 7.64%), demonstrate in vitro anti-inflammatory effects. These alcohols significantly and dose-dependently decreased nitric oxide production (P<0.001) and inhibited the release of PLA2 and TXB2 (P<0.001). Furthermore, the LCFAs reduced cyclooxygenase-2 enzyme gene expression at all doses assayed and significantly reduced the secretion of inflammatory cytokines interleukin 1β (IL-1β) and tumour necrosis factor α (TNF-α) (P<0.001).

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for managing menopause, menopausal symptoms, premenstrual syndrome, hormonal irregularities, and mastalgia. It is primarily utilized to alleviate various physical and emotional discomforts associated with cyclical hormonal shifts. Many women use it to reduce the severity of breast pain, a condition known as mastalgia. Additionally, it helps balance endocrine functions to mitigate the impact of irregular menstrual cycles. By regulating hormonal fluctuations, the plant provides relief from common discomforts occurring throughout different stages of reproductive health.

What are the herbal preparations of this plant?
