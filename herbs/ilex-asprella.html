Ilex asprella

What's the taxonomical classification of Ilex asprella?

Ilex asprella belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized under the order Aquifoliales and the family Aquifoliaceae. Finally, its taxonomic identity is completed by its assignment to the genus Ilex and the specific species asprella.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Ilex asprella targets various enzymes and signaling proteins, most notably inhibiting cyclooxygenase (COX) enzymes, alpha-glucosidase, and acetylcholinesterase to produce its pharmacological effects. By suppressing COX-1 and COX-2, the plant's bioactive compounds reduce the production of pro-inflammatory prostaglandins. Its ability to inhibit alpha-glucosidase helps regulate postprandial blood glucose levels by slowing down carbohydrate digestion. Furthermore, the inhibition of acetylcholinesterase suggests potential neuroprotective benefits through the modulation of cholinergic neurotransmission. These diverse molecular interactions collectively contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory, antidiabetic, and neuroprotective properties.

What are the therapeutic actions of Ilex asprella?

Ilex asprella has therapeutic properties that function as an astringent, diuretic, emmenagogue, and tonic. Its astringent qualities allow it to help constrict tissues and reduce excessive secretions. The plant also acts as a diuretic to increase urine production and assist in the elimination of fluids from the body. As an emmenagogue, it can be used to stimulate or regulate menstrual flow. Finally, it serves as a tonic to support overall systemic strength and vitality.
