1. Causes allergic reactions

Desmodium adscendens causes allergic reactions due to its high concentration of alkaloids and saponins.

These compounds can stimulate an immune response in sensitive individuals, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.

In some cases, exposure to Desmodium adscendens can trigger a severe allergic reaction known as anaphylaxis, which requires immediate medical attention.

2. Induces severe drowsiness

Desmodium adscendens induces severe drowsiness due to its ability to interact with the central nervous system.

The plant's active compounds, such as flavonoids and alkaloids, can bind to neurotransmitters in the brain, leading to a decrease in alertness and an increase in sleepiness.

This effect is thought to be caused by the inhibition of enzymes involved in the synthesis of neurotransmitters, resulting in altered levels of dopamine, serotonin, and other neurotransmitters that regulate arousal and wakefulness.

3. Triggers respiratory distress

Desmodium adscendens triggers respiratory distress due to its ability to release allergenic proteins into the air.

These proteins can cause an allergic reaction in susceptible individuals, leading to inflammation and constriction of the airways. As a result, people using Desmodium adscendens may experience symptoms such as wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.

The exact mechanisms behind this reaction are not fully understood, but it is thought to be related to the plant's high levels of phenolic compounds.

4. Increases blood pressure

Desmodium adscendens increases blood pressure due to its high levels of alkaloids, which can cause vasoconstriction and increased cardiac output.

The plant's ability to stimulate the nervous system and increase heart rate contributes to this effect.

Additionally, Desmodium adscendens' potential to alter kidney function and increase sodium reabsorption may also contribute to blood pressure increases in some individuals who use it.

5. Lowers blood sugar levels

Desmodium adscendens lowers blood sugar levels.

This is attributed to its ability to stimulate insulin production and enhance glucose uptake in muscles, leading to decreased blood sugar levels.

The herb's flavonoids and saponins have been shown to possess hypoglycemic properties, which can amplify the body's natural glucose-lowering mechanisms, resulting in a decline in blood sugar concentrations.

6. Stimulates uterine contractions

Desmodium adscendens stimulates uterine contractions.

This is due to the presence of bioactive compounds, such as alkaloids and saponins, which can affect the uterine smooth muscle leading to increased contraction frequency and intensity. The stimulation of uterine contractions may be attributed to the herb's ability to modulate neurotransmitters and ion channels in the uterus, causing a cascade of events that ultimately lead to increased contractility.

As a result, individuals using Desmodium adscendens may experience heightened uterine activity, potentially impacting fetal development or pregnancy outcome.

7. Produces nausea and vomiting

Desmodium adscendens produces nausea and vomiting due to its ability to interact with the gut flora, leading to an imbalance in the stomach's natural bacteria.

This alteration can cause stomach upset, resulting in symptoms such as queasiness, vomiting, and discomfort.

The plant's chemical composition, including flavonoids and terpenes, may also contribute to these gastrointestinal side effects.

8. Affects liver function

Desmodium adscendens affects liver function due to its ability to inhibit the activity of certain liver enzymes.

This can cause an imbalance in the body's natural detoxification processes and potentially lead to liver damage or impaired liver function.

The exact mechanisms behind this effect are not fully understood, but it is believed to be related to the plant's high concentration of sesquiterpene lactones, which can interact with liver cells and disrupt their normal functioning.

9. Interferes with medication absorption

Desmodium adscendens interferes with medication absorption by reducing gastric motility and increasing intestinal secretion.

This herb's saponins and flavonoids can slow down the movement of food through the digestive system, allowing medications to remain in the stomach for a longer period.

As a result, the absorption of certain medications may be impaired, potentially affecting their efficacy.

10. Causes rapid heartbeat

Desmodium adscendens causes rapid heartbeat due to its inherent properties.

The plant's extracts contain alkaloids and glycosides that can stimulate the cardiac system, leading to an increase in heart rate and palpitations. This is because these compounds interact with the nervous system, specifically the sympathetic nervous system, which is responsible for regulating heart rate and blood pressure.

As a result, individuals using Desmodium adscendens may experience rapid heartbeat as a side effect of its use.
