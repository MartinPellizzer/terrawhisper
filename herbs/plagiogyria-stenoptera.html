Plaagiogyria stenoptera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Cyatheales and the family Cyatheaceae, eventually narrowing down to the genus Plaagiogyria. Finally, the specific designation of this plant is completed by its species name, stenoptera.

What are the therapeutic actions of Plagiogyria stenoptera?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various types of intestinal parasites, including various species of helminths and worms. It contains bioactive compounds that function as anthelmintics to expel parasitic organisms from the digestive tract. Researchers have specifically looked into its efficacy against protozoan infections and certain types of intestinal amoebiasis. The plant's medicinal properties are often utilized in traditional practices to manage gastrointestinal ailments caused by microbial imbalances. These therapeutic applications focus on reducing the worm burden and restoring gut health.
