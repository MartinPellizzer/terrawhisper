Goniothalamus donnaiensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Goniothalamus donnaiensis?

Goniothalamus donnaiensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is classified under the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Magnoliales. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the family Annonaceae, placing it within the genus Goniothalamus, with its specific identification being the species donnaiensis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Goniothalamus donnaiensis contains bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, terpenoids, and phenolic compounds that exhibit significant pharmacological potential. These chemical constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's demonstrated antimicrobial and antioxidant properties. Research suggests that certain extracts from this species possess cytotoxic activities which may be useful in anti-cancer studies. The presence of specific flavonoids further contributes to its ability to mitigate oxidative stress in biological systems. These medicinal compounds serve as the foundation for investigating the plant's therapeutic applications in traditional and modern medicine.

What are the therapeutic actions of Goniothalamus donnaiensis?

Goniothalamus donnaiensis has antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties that allow it to treat skin infections, wounds, and various inflammatory conditions. The plant contains diverse secondary metabolites such as alkaloids, flavonoids, and tannins that contribute to its medicinal effectiveness. These bioactive compounds work by neutralizing free radicals and inhibiting the growth of harmful pathogens. Local communities frequently utilize extracts from the leaves and stems to promote wound healing and reduce swelling. This botanical species serves as a significant natural resource for managing minor ailments and infections through traditional herbal medicine.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat diarrhea, stomach aches, skin infections, and various inflammatory conditions. The leaves and roots are often prepared as decoctions to alleviate gastrointestinal distress and abdominal pain. Local healers also apply topical preparations from the plant to soothe skin irritations and promote healing of minor wounds. Beyond physical ailments, it is sometimes utilized in traditional practices to manage symptoms associated with fever. These medicinal applications rely on the bioactive compounds found within the plant's various parts.
