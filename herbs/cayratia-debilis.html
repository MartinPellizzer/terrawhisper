Cayratia debilis

What's the taxonomical classification of Cayratia debilis?

Cayratiia debilis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Vitales and is a member of the family Vitaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Cayratia, and its specific species epithet, debilis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Cayratia debilis contains various bioactive phytochemicals including alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, and tannins that contribute to its therapeutic properties. These secondary metabolites are believed to provide significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects when utilized in traditional medicine. Research suggests that the presence of specific glycosides may play a role in the plant's antimicrobial activities. Local communities often employ these chemical components to treat skin ailments and digestive issues. The complex profile of these organic compounds supports the plant's reputation for diverse medicinal applications.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, gastrointestinal issues, and inflammatory conditions. The leaves and stems are often applied topically to promote healing in cuts and abrasions. In some traditional medicinal practices, decoctions of the plant are consumed to manage stomach ailments and digestive discomfort. Its properties are also believed to assist in reducing swelling and localized inflammation throughout the body. This versatile herb serves as a common remedy in various regional folk medicine traditions.
