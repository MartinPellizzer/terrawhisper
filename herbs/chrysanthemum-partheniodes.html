What is Chrysanthemum partheniodes?

Chrysanthemum partheniodes is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae, native to China, Japan, and Korea.

It is an herbaceous perennial with a rhizomatous habit, growing up to 60-120 cm in height and 30-60 cm in width. The leaves are lanceolate to elliptical, 5-12 cm long and 1-3 cm wide, with a petiole up to 5 cm long. The flower head is a capitulum, composed of 10-20 white or yellowish florets, with a diameter of up to 5 cm, and is surrounded by a ring of involucral bracts.

The plant produces achenes, 3-4 mm long, with a pappus of 2-3 mm long setae, which are dispersed by wind.
