Iphigenia indica

What's the taxonomical classification of Iphigenia indica?

Iphigenia indica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its hierarchical descent, the plant is organized under the order Liliales and is a member of the family Colchicaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Iphigenia and the species name indica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Iphigenia indica has an underground tuberous rootstock, basal leaves that are long and linear, and umbel-shaped inflorescences bearing white, actinomorphic flowers. The leaves are typically lanceolate or strap-shaped and arise from a central rosette at the base of the plant. Its stems are slender and erect, supporting multiple flower stalks that branch out to form the characteristic umbel. Each flower features six tepals that are often slightly notched at the apex and bright white in color. The reproductive structures include a central ovary surrounded by numerous stamens that are characteristic of the Amaryllidaceae family.
