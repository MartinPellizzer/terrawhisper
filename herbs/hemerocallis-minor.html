Hemerocallis minor

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hemerocallis minor has slender, arching green leaves that grow in basal clumps and produce trumpet-shaped flowers with six tepals. These blossoms typically feature delicate petals that vary in shade from pale yellow to light orange. The plant's reproductive structures consist of a central pistil surrounded by several stamens held within the floral tube. Sturdy, erect scapes emerge from the center of the foliage to support the clusters of blooms. Small, rounded capsules develop after pollination to protect the maturing seeds.

      What are the herbal preparations of Hemerocallis minor?

      Hemerocallis minor is prepared primarily as a decoction using the dried roots, as an infusion of the flowering tops, or as a topical tincture for skin applications. The roots are often simmered in water for extended periods to extract concentrated minerals and soothing mucilage. Infusions made from the delicate flowers provide a gentler way to consume the plant's essence in tea form. These various preparations allow practitioners to tailor the dosage and delivery method to the specific therapeutic need.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Hemerocallis minor can cause skin irritation, digestive upset, and potential toxicity if ingested by pets or humans. Contact with the plant's sap may lead to contact dermatitis or localized rashes on sensitive skin. If swallowed, the plant can induce vomiting, diarrhea, or abdominal discomfort. Small children and domestic animals are at a higher risk of suffering from these adverse reactions. Always wear protective gloves when handling the foliage to prevent accidental skin exposure.
