Lilium lankongense

What's the taxonomical classification of Lilium lankongense?

Lilium lankongense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Liliales. The plant is a member of the family Liliaceae and falls under the genus Lilium, with its specific identity defined by the species name lankongense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lilium lankongense has erect stems reaching up to 60 centimeters in height, bearing lanceolate leaves that are arranged in whorls or loosely spirally along the stem. The flowers are typically nodding and display a bright orange or reddish-orange hue with prominent dark spots on the inner tepals. Each tepal is narrowly lanceolate and often curves backward to reveal long, protruding stamens. The plant also features bulbous underground structures that serve as its primary energy storage organ. These morphological traits allow the species to thrive in its specific montane habitats.
