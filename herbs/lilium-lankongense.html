Lilium lankongense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lilium lankongense has erect stems reaching up to 60 centimeters in height, bearing lanceolate leaves that are arranged in whorls or loosely spirally along the stem. The flowers are typically nodding and display a bright orange or reddish-orange hue with prominent dark spots on the inner tepals. Each tepal is narrowly lanceolate and often curves backward to reveal long, protruding stamens. The plant also features bulbous underground structures that serve as its primary energy storage organ. These morphological traits allow the species to thrive in its specific montane habitats.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of central and southwestern China, specifically within the provinces of Sichuan, Yunnan, and Guizhou. It typically inhabits high-altitude environments found in forest margins and rocky slopes. These specific habitats provide the cool, moist conditions necessary for its survival in the wild. The species is often localized to certain mountain ranges rather than being widely dispersed across the entire continent. Consequently, its range is quite restricted to these particular ecological niches in East Asia.

        What side effects this plant can have?

        This plant can cause severe kidney failure and death if ingested by cats. Allergic symptoms often include redness, itching, or swelling at the site of contact. It is important to keep this species away from household pets to prevent life-threatening renal complications.
