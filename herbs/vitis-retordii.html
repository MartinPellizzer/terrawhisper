Vitis retordii

What's the taxonomical classification of Vitis retordii?

Vitis retordii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, the plant is categorized under the order Vitales and the family Vitaceae. Finally, it is defined by the genus Vitis and the specific species retordii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vitis retordii has woody, climbing vines characterized by opposite, simple leaves with cordate bases and serrated margins.

The leaf blades are typically ovate or elliptical in shape and possess distinct venation patterns. Its stems feature tendrils that emerge from axillary positions to assist in climbing surrounding vegetation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into clusters of berries.

These morphological traits are characteristic of the Vitaceae family and help distinguish it from other grape species.

Through these diverse molecular interactions, the plant exerts its observed pharmacological effects on the immune and inflammatory systems.

Users should exercise extreme caution and avoid ingestion until more comprehensive safety data is available.
