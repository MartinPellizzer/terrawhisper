Vitis retordii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vitis retordii has woody, climbing vines characterized by opposite, simple leaves with cordate bases and serrated margins. The leaf blades are typically ovate or elliptical in shape and possess distinct venation patterns. Its stems feature tendrils that emerge from axillary positions to assist in climbing surrounding vegetation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into clusters of berries. These morphological traits are characteristic of the Vitaceae family and help distinguish it from other grape species.
