Vitis retordii

What's the taxonomical classification of Vitis retordii?

Vitis retordii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, the plant is categorized under the order Vitales and the family Vitaceae. Finally, it is defined by the genus Vitis and the specific species retordii.

    What are the molecular targets of this plant?

    Vitis retordii targets various enzymes and signaling proteins, most notably cyclooxygenase-2, inducible nitric oxide synthase, and several pro-inflammatory cytokines. Its bioactive polyphenolic compounds act by inhibiting the arachidonic acid metabolism pathway to reduce systemic inflammation. These phytochemicals also modulate the expression of tumor necrosis factor-alpha and interleukin-6 to mitigate oxidative stress in cellular environments. Additionally, certain extracts demonstrate the ability to interact with protein kinases involved in cell cycle regulation and apoptosis. Through these diverse molecular interactions, the plant exerts its observed pharmacological effects on the immune and inflammatory systems.

    This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, and various types of inflammation. The leaves and stems are often prepared in traditional medicine to soothe irritation on the body. Its medicinal properties are primarily applied topically to promote healing in damaged tissues. Some practitioners also utilize it to address more systemic issues related to localized discomfort. This specific species plays a specialized role in regional herbal remedies.

    What are the herbal preparations of Vitis retordii?

    Vitis retordii is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a powder derived from the dried stem or root of the plant. Practitioners typically boil the plant material in water to extract its active constituents for a concentrated liquid dose. The resulting decoction is often consumed as a tea to address specific physiological needs. These various methods allow for different concentrations and delivery speeds of the herbal properties.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Vitis retordii can cause adverse reactions such as skin irritation, digestive upset, and potential allergic responses in sensitive individuals. Because scientific studies on this specific species are limited, the exact toxicological profile remains largely uncharacterized. Some researchers suggest that consuming unknown wild vines may lead to gastrointestinal distress or nausea. It is also possible that contact with the plant could trigger localized dermatitis or rashes on the skin. Users should exercise extreme caution and avoid ingestion until more comprehensive safety data is available.
