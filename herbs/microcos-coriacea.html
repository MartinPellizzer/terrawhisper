Microcos coriacea

What's the taxonomical classification of Microcos coriacea?

Microcos coriacea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent through the order Malvales and the family Malvaceae, the plant is specifically identified by the genus Microcos and the species coriacea.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Microcos coriacea contains a diverse array of secondary metabolites including flavonoids, tannins, terpenoids, and various phenolic acids. These bioactive compounds are primarily concentrated in the leaves and stems of the plant. The presence of these constituents contributes to the species' documented antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Scientific studies suggest that the specific combination of polyphenols provides significant antioxidant activity. Such chemical profiles make the plant a subject of interest for pharmacological research into natural healing agents.

What are the therapeutic actions of Microcos coriacea?

Microcos coriacea has anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant therapeutic actions primarily driven by its high concentration of phenolic compounds and flavonoids. These bioactive constituents work together to inhibit oxidative stress and reduce swelling in various tissues. The plant is often utilized in traditional medicine to treat gastrointestinal disturbances and skin inflammations due to its soothing properties. Additionally, its ability to combat pathogenic microorganisms makes it a subject of interest for developing natural antiseptic treatments. Researchers continue to study these specific chemical profiles to better understand their potential in modern pharmacological applications.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various skin conditions including eczema, psoriasis, dermatitis, and minor wound infections. Its medicinal properties are primarily derived from the bark and leaves, which possess significant antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory qualities. Local practitioners often prepare infusions or topical ointments to soothe irritated tissues and reduce swelling. These applications help to accelerate the healing process of superficial abrasions and fungal outbreaks. The plant's biochemical composition allows it to target pathogens while protecting the underlying skin barrier.

What are the herbal preparations of Microcos coriacea?

Microcos coriacea is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a tincture to utilize its medicinal properties. The dried aerial parts of the plant are most commonly boiled in water to create a concentrated decoction for internal use. Alternatively, steeping the plant material in hot water produces a gentler infusion suitable for mild digestive support. For more potent applications, the plant is macerated in alcohol to create a tincture that allows for long-term storage and precise dosing. These various forms ensure that the bioactive compounds are effectively extracted for different therapeutic needs.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Microcos coriacea can cause localized skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested in large quantities. The plant contains various bioactive compounds that may trigger inflammatory responses upon direct contact with sensitive skin. Individuals with pre-existing skin conditions might experience redness, itching, or swelling after touching the foliage. If consumed accidentally, the botanical components can lead to nausea or abdominal discomfort. It is generally advisable to avoid direct contact and ensure the plant is not used in internal medicinal preparations without professional supervision.
