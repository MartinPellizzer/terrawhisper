Microcos coriacea has small, leathery, elliptical leaves that are dark green and possess a smooth, slightly thickened texture. Its root system is relatively shallow, helping the plant stabilize itself in its natural habitat. The overall structure is compact, reflecting its adaptation to specific environmental niches.

      What are the herbal preparations of Microcos coriacea?

      Microcos coriacea is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a tincture to utilize its medicinal properties. The dried aerial parts of the plant are most commonly boiled in water to create a concentrated decoction for internal use. Alternatively, steeping the plant material in hot water produces a gentler infusion suitable for mild digestive support. For more potent applications, the plant is macerated in alcohol to create a tincture that allows for long-term storage and precise dosing. These various forms ensure that the bioactive compounds are effectively extracted for different therapeutic needs.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Microcos coriacea can cause localized skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested in large quantities. The plant contains various bioactive compounds that may trigger inflammatory responses upon direct contact with sensitive skin. Individuals with pre-existing skin conditions might experience redness, itching, or swelling after touching the foliage. If consumed accidentally, the botanical components can lead to nausea or abdominal discomfort. It is generally advisable to avoid direct contact and ensure the plant is not used in internal medicinal preparations without professional supervision.
