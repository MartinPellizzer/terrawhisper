Botanical Identification of Indian Coleus (Coleus forskohlii)

To identify Indian Coleus (Coleus forskohlii) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Indian Coleus (Coleus forskohlii) Monograph

Scientific Classification

Indian Coleus, or Coleus forskohlii, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Lamiales, order Lamiales, family Lamiaceae, genus Coleus, and species Coleus forskohlii. It is a member of the mint family, known for its medicinal properties and use in herbal medicine.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Indian Coleus is Coleus forskohlii, a species within the Lamiaceae family. It was first described by the Swedish botanist Carl von Linné in 1782. This name is widely accepted in botanical literature and serves as the standard reference for this plant.

Morphological Characteristics

Indian Coleus (Coleus forskohlii) is an herbaceous perennial with a bushy growth habit. It has a fibrous root system and upright, branching stems. The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, with serrated margins and a distinctive purple or red coloration. The inflorescence is a terminal spike, bearing small, tubular flowers with two lips. The fruit is a dry capsule containing numerous tiny, black seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Indian Coleus can be distinguished by its square, hairy stems, opposite leaves with serrated edges, and distinctive purple or red flowers. It also has unique glandular structures on its leaves, which are not present in similar species like Plectranthus or Coleus blumei. These features help in accurate identification during herbal studies and practice.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Indian Coleus is native to the Indian subcontinent, particularly India, Nepal, and Sri Lanka. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and warm temperatures. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils and is commonly found at altitudes between 500 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Indian Coleus typically blooms from June to September, depending on climate. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early autumn. For identification purposes, the plant is best harvested during its vegetative growth phase, usually in spring to early summer, when the leaves are most potent.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Indian Coleus (Coleus forskohlii) belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is closely related to other members of the Coleus genus. Its phylogenetic relationships show it is most closely related to species like Coleus blumei and Coleus scutatus. These species share similar morphological and genetic traits, which help in its identification and classification within the genus.
