Indian Coleus (Coleus forskohlii) Compounds

The primary compounds of Indian Coleus include terpenoids, which are aromatic compounds with medicinal properties. Essential oils contribute to its scent and therapeutic effects. Flavonoids and glycosides offer antioxidant and anti-inflammatory benefits. Saponins, phenolic acids, and alkaloids also play roles in its pharmacological activity. Coumarin and tannins provide additional therapeutic and antimicrobial properties.

Indian Coleus (Coleus forskohlii) Monograph

1. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Indian Coleus (Coleus forskohlii). One notable terpenoid, forskolin, is responsible for the plant's medicinal properties. It acts as a cyclic AMP (cAMP) activator, influencing various physiological processes and making it valuable in herbal medicine.

2. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound of Indian Coleus (Coleus forskohlii). It contains forskolin, which has been studied for its potential to support cardiovascular health and respiratory function. The oil is extracted from the plant's leaves and is valued for its therapeutic properties in traditional and modern herbal medicine.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Indian Coleus (Coleus forskohlii) are a class of plant compounds known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These bioactive components contribute to the plant's traditional use in supporting cardiovascular health and reducing oxidative stress in the body.

4. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Indian Coleus (Coleus forskohlii). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by influencing cellular processes. This compound is particularly noted for its role in enhancing cyclic AMP levels, supporting its use in traditional herbal medicine for various therapeutic applications.

5. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Indian Coleus (Coleus forskohlii). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine, known for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponin may support digestive health and immune function, aligning with the plant's historical applications in Ayurvedic practices.

6. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Indian Coleus (Coleus forskohlii). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound supports the traditional use of Indian Coleus in herbal medicine for promoting respiratory and cardiovascular health. Its presence enhances the plant's therapeutic potential in natural remedies.

7. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a bioactive compound found in Indian Coleus (Coleus forskohlii). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by interacting with various biological pathways. Alkaloids in this plant are known for their potential effects on the nervous and cardiovascular systems. They are often studied for their therapeutic applications in traditional and modern medicine.

8. Coumarin

Coumarin is a bioactive compound found in Indian Coleus (Coleus forskohlii). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine, particularly for its potential anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. Coumarin's presence supports the plant's role in supporting digestive and respiratory health.

9. Tannin

Tannin is a compound found in Indian Coleus (Coleus forskohlii). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health by reducing inflammation and tightening tissues. Tannins are also known for their antioxidant effects, which can help protect cells from damage.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a pigment found in Indian Coleus (Coleus forskohlii) that contributes to its vibrant colors. It acts as an antioxidant, helping to neutralize free radicals in the body. While not the primary active compound, anthocyanin supports the plant's medicinal properties by enhancing its overall therapeutic potential.
