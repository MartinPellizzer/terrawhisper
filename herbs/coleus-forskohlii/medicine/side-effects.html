10 Possible Side Effects Of Coleus Forskohlii

Coleus forskohlii has some side effects when used improperly, such as diarrhea, stomach cramps, and nausea.

These symptoms can occur due to the herb's high content of forskolin, which can stimulate digestive enzymes and cause inflammation.

In severe cases, these side effects can worsen people's lives by leading to malnutrition, dehydration, and electrolyte imbalances, ultimately affecting their physical and mental well-being.

1. Causes headaches

Coleus forskohlii causes headaches due to its ability to increase cerebral blood flow and stimulate the release of neurotransmitters such as serotonin and dopamine.

This increased activity in the brain can lead to a pounding or throbbing sensation, which may be mild or severe depending on individual tolerance.

2. Triggers insomnia

Coleus forskohlii triggers insomnia due to its stimulating effect on the nervous system.

The herb's ability to increase levels of cyclic adenosine monophosphate (cAMP), a molecule that plays a key role in regulating sleep and wakefulness, can lead to an increase in alertness and energy, making it difficult for individuals to fall asleep or stay asleep throughout the night.

This effect is often observed when taking high doses or using Coleus forskohlii supplements for extended periods of time.

3. Induces vomiting

Coleus forskohlii induces vomiting in some individuals.

This side effect is attributed to its ability to stimulate the digestive system and increase gut motility, which can cause nausea and vomiting.

Additionally, forskolin's direct stimulation of the vagus nerve may also contribute to this adverse reaction.

4. Lowers blood pressure

Coleus forskohlii lowers blood pressure by increasing cAMP levels in the body.

This mechanism leads to a decrease in peripheral resistance and reduced cardiac output, resulting in a drop in blood pressure.

As blood vessels dilate, blood flow increases, further contributing to hypotension.

5. Raises heart rate

Coleus forskohlii raises heart rate due to its ability to increase cAMP levels in cardiac tissue.

This leads to increased contraction force and heart rate. Additionally, Coleus forskohlii relaxes blood vessels, further contributing to a rise in heart rate.

The stimulatory effect on the cardiovascular system is responsible for this side effect, which can be more pronounced in individuals with pre-existing heart conditions or those who are sensitive to cardiac-stimulating substances.

6. Increases anxiety

Coleus forskohlii increases anxiety in some individuals due to its potent effects on the brain's neurotransmitters.

As a natural stimulant, forskolin can stimulate the release of adrenaline and other stress hormones, leading to feelings of unease and apprehension.

Additionally, forskolin's ability to increase the levels of cyclic adenosine monophosphate (cAMP) in the brain may also contribute to its anxiogenic properties, potentially exacerbating anxiety symptoms in those who are already prone to them.

7. Reduces appetite

Coleus forskohlii reduces appetite due to its ability to stimulate the hormone cyclic adenosine monophosphate (cAMP), which in turn activates a protein called protein kinase A (PKA).

This cascade of events can lead to increased lipase activity, resulting in enhanced fat breakdown and weight loss.

Additionally, forskolin's effects on serotonin receptors may also contribute to reduced appetite by altering the body's hunger-satiety balance.

8. Causes stomach pain

Coleus forskohlii causes stomach pain in some individuals due to its ability to stimulate gastric acid secretion and relax the muscles of the digestive tract.

This may lead to discomfort, bloating, and cramping.

Additionally, Coleus forskohlii's potential to increase blood pressure and heart rate can further exacerbate stomach pain by reducing blood flow to the digestive system.

9. Produces dizziness

Coleus forskohlii produces dizziness due to its ability to lower blood pressure and increase heart rate.

This is because forskolin stimulates the production of cyclic adenosine monophosphate (cAMP), which leads to vasodilation and tachycardia.

Additionally, the herb may also cause lightheadedness by increasing the amount of blood flow to the brain, potentially leading to a temporary sense of dizziness or vertigo in some individuals.

10. Raises blood sugar

Coleus forskohlii raises blood sugar levels due to its ability to stimulate the production of cyclic adenosine monophosphate (cAMP).

This leads to an increase in glucose release from the liver and a decrease in insulin sensitivity.

As a result, individuals taking Coleus forskohlii may experience elevated blood sugar levels, which can be problematic for those with pre-existing diabetes or prediabetes conditions.
