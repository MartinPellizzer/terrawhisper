Indian Coleus (Coleus forskohlii) Uses

Indian Coleus is used to treat respiratory conditions such as asthma, bronchitis, and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease. It helps reduce inflammation and mucus production. It also supports the management of allergic rhinitis and respiratory infections. Additionally, it is applied for inflammatory skin conditions like eczema, psoriasis, and skin infections due to its anti-inflammatory properties.

Indian Coleus (Coleus forskohlii) Monograph

1. Asthma

Indian Coleus (Coleus forskohlii) is used in traditional medicine to support respiratory health, particularly for asthma. Its active compound, forskolin, may help relax bronchial muscles and reduce airway inflammation, improving breathing. It is often combined with other herbs to enhance its effects in managing asthma symptoms.

2. Allergic rhinitis

Indian Coleus has shown potential in alleviating symptoms of allergic rhinitis due to its anti-inflammatory and bronchodilatory properties. The active compound forskolin may help reduce nasal congestion and inflammation. Clinical studies suggest it could be a complementary therapy for managing seasonal allergies. Its effects are attributed to the stimulation of cyclic AMP pathways in respiratory tissues.

3. Bronchitis

Indian Coleus has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health. It contains forskolin, which may help reduce inflammation in the airways. This property makes it potentially beneficial for managing symptoms of bronchitis. Its use is often combined with other herbs to enhance respiratory function.

4. Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease

Indian Coleus has shown potential in supporting lung function in chronic obstructive pulmonary disease by enhancing bronchodilation and reducing inflammation. Its active compound, forskolin, may help improve airflow and alleviate symptoms in patients with COPD. Studies suggest it could be a complementary therapy when used alongside standard treatments.

5. Respiratory infections

Indian Coleus has been traditionally used to support respiratory health. Its active compound, forskolin, may help reduce inflammation and open airways. It is often used for conditions like bronchitis and asthma. Clinical studies suggest it may enhance lung function and ease breathing.

6. Pulmonary edema

Indian Coleus (Coleus forskohlii) contains forskolin, which may help reduce pulmonary edema by relaxing smooth muscle and improving lung function. It supports cardiovascular health by promoting vasodilation, aiding in the management of fluid buildup in the lungs. Its traditional use in Ayurveda aligns with modern research on its potential in respiratory conditions.

7. Inflammatory skin conditions

Indian Coleus contains forskolin, which has anti-inflammatory properties. It is used topically to reduce inflammation and redness in skin conditions like eczema and psoriasis. Its ability to modulate inflammatory pathways makes it a valuable herb in herbal medicine for managing skin inflammation.

8. Eczema

Indian Coleus contains forskolin, which may reduce inflammation and promote skin healing. It is used topically for eczema due to its anti-inflammatory properties. Some studies suggest it can alleviate symptoms like redness and itching. However, more research is needed to confirm its effectiveness and safety for this condition.

9. Psoriasis

Indian Coleus (Coleus forskohlii) is used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine to support skin health. Its active compound, forskolin, may reduce inflammation and regulate skin cell growth, making it a potential aid for managing psoriasis symptoms. It is often combined with other herbs to enhance its therapeutic effects.

10. Skin infections

Indian Coleus contains forskolin, which has antimicrobial properties that help combat skin infections. It may reduce inflammation and promote healing in conditions like fungal infections and bacterial wounds. Its topical application can support skin recovery and prevent further infection spread.
