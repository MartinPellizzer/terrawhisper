What's the taxonomical classification of Halerpestes cymbalaria?

Halerpestes cymbalaria belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Ranunculales. This species is a member of the family Ranunculaceae, falling under the genus Halerpestes, and is specifically identified by its species name, cymbalaria.

        What are the herbal preparations of Halerpestes cymbalaria?

        Halerpestes cymbalaria is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a tincture to extract its medicinal compounds.         What side-effects this plant can have?

        Halerpestes cymbalaria can cause skin irritation, contact dermatitis, and localized swelling upon direct physical contact with the plant. Individuals with sensitive skin may experience intense itching or redness where the plant touches the body. In some cases, accidental ingestion can lead to gastrointestinal distress such as nausea or stomach cramps. While these reactions are typically mild, they can be exacerbated by prolonged exposure to the leaves or stems. Monitoring for allergic reactions is recommended for anyone handling the plant in a natural setting.
