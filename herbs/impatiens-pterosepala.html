What's the taxonomical classification of Impatiens pterosepala?

Impatiens pterosepala belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Ericales. This species is a member of the family Balsaminaceae and is situated within the genus Impatiens, ultimately identifying it as the specific species Impatiens pterosepalas.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Impatiens pterosepala has winged sepals that are significantly expanded and thin, distinguishing it from most other species in its genus.

The flowers typically feature a zygomorphic structure with a prominent spurred petal located at the rear. Its leaves are generally ovate or lanceolate with serrated margins and are arranged oppositely along the stem. The stems are succulent and often exhibit a reddish or greenish hue depending on environmental conditions.

These morphological traits combine to create a unique floral profile adapted for specific pollinators.
