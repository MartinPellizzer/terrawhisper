Asplenium antrophyoides

What's the taxonomical classification of Asplenium antrophyoides?

Asplenium antrophyoides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized under the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. This plant is a representative of the family Aspleniaceae, falling under the genus Asplenium, with the specific species designation antrophyoides.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Asplenium antrophyoides has large, pinnate fronds that can reach lengths of up to 1 meter, characterized by elongated, lanceolate pinnae with entire or slightly wavy margins.

The texture of the foliage is typically leathery and dark green, providing a robust appearance to the fern. Each leaflet is attached to a central rachis that is often dark or brownish in color. The reproductive structures consist of sori located on the underside of the pinnae, which are protected by kidney-shaped indusia.

These morphological features collectively distinguish this species within its genus.
