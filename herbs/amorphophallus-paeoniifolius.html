What's the taxonomical classification of Amorphophallus paeoniifolius?

Amorphophallus paeoniifolius belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic journey continues through the order Alismatales and the family Araceae. Finally, it is placed within the genus Amorphophallus, reaching its specific identification at the species level as paeoniifolius.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Amorphophallus paeoniifolius has a large underground corm that serves as a nutrient storage organ and produces massive, deeply lobed leaves resembling those of a peony. The leaves are typically bipinnate or tripinnate, featuring large, fleshy leaflets that can reach considerable dimensions. From the center of these leaves, a single, thick petiole emerges to support the expansive foliage. During its flowering stage, the plant produces a large, fleshy inflorescence consisting of a central spadix surrounded by a leaf-like spathe. This structure is known for its unique shape and often emits a strong odor to attract pollinators.
