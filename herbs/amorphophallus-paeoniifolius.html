What's the taxonomical classification of Amorphophallus paeoniifolius?

Amorphophallus paeoniifolius belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic journey continues through the order Alismatales and the family Araceae. Finally, it is placed within the genus Amorphophallus, reaching its specific identification at the species level as paeoniifolius.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat inflammatory conditions, digestive disorders, fever, pain, and various skin ailments. Its bioactive compounds act as natural analgesics to help manage headaches and joint discomfort. It is frequently utilized in traditional medicine to alleviate gastrointestinal issues like indigestion or stomach aches. Furthermore, the plant possesses antimicrobial properties that assist in healing wounds and skin infections. Regular application or consumption of its derivatives can also help regulate body temperature during febrile episodes.
