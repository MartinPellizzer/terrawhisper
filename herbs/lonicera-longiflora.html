Lonicera longiflora

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lonicera longiflora has slender, deciduous stems that bear opposite, simple leaves with entire to slightly serrated margins and an ovate to lanceolate shape. The foliage typically features a smooth texture and a light green coloration throughout the growing season. Its most distinctive morphological feature is the long, tubular corolla of the flowers, which extends significantly from the calyx. These flowers are often pale or yellowish and are arranged in small clusters or pairs along the stem. The plant also develops small, fleshy berries that change color as they reach maturity.

      Lonicera longiflora is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a tincture using the dried leaves, stems, or flowers of the plant.
