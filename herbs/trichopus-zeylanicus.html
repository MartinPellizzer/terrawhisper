When using Trichopus zeylanicus medicinally, it is essential to exercise caution and follow proper preparation and dosing guidelines. What are the horticulural aspects of Trichopus zeylanicus?

Trichopus zeylanicus grow in shaded to partially shaded conditions, with optimal growth between 20-40°C (68-104°F) and relative humidity of 50-80%. Soil pH range is between 5.5-7.5, with well-draining acidic to neutral soils.

Planting tips for Trichopus zeylanicus include choosing a location with consistent moisture and moderate soil fertility. Plant seeds about 1-2 cm (0.4-0.8 in) deep, with a spacing of 30-60 cm (12-24 in) between plants. Fertilize with a balanced fertilizer during the growing season.

Harvesting Trichopus zeylanicus involves collecting the ripe fruit, which is usually 1-2 months after flowering. Fruit color changes from green to yellow or red, depending on the variety. Harvest fruit in the morning, when they are fully ripe, to avoid damage during transportation.

Common pests and diseases affecting Trichopus zeylanicus include mealybugs, scale insects, and spider mites. Fungal diseases, such as leaf spot and root rot, can be caused by excessive moisture and poor air circulation. Regular pruning and monitoring can help prevent pest and disease issues.
