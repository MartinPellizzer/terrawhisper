Lamium album

What's the taxonomical classification of Lamium album?

Lamium album belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its hierarchical descent, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. At the ordinal level, it is part of the order Lamiales, which leads into its placement within the family Lamiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified under the genus Lamium and is specifically designated by the species name album.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lamium album has square stems, opposite leaves with crenate margins, and tubular, two-lipped white flowers with pinkish spots. The leaves are typically heart-shaped or ovate and feature a distinctively wrinkled or rugose surface texture. Its flowering structure consists of small, bilabiate blossoms that emerge from the leaf axils in whorls. The plant often displays a creeping habit due to its stoloniferous growth pattern. These morphological characteristics allow the species to effectively spread across shaded woodland floors.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to much of Europe and northern Asia but has become widely naturalized across North America and other temperate regions of the world. It typically thrives in disturbed habitats such as roadsides, gardens, and forest edges where light is sufficient. Because it is highly adaptable, it can be found in a variety of climates ranging from mild maritime zones to more continental environments. Its ability to spread via seeds and rhizomes allows it to colonize vast areas within many different continents. This widespread presence makes it a common sight in many temperate ecosystems globally.

What are the therapeutic actions of Lamium album?

Lamium album has therapeutic actions that include being an astringent, hemostatic, vulnerary, emmenagogue, diaphoretic, and alterative. Its astringent and hemostatic properties allow it to effectively control bleeding and tighten tissues. As a vulnerary, the plant promotes the healing of skin wounds and superficial injuries. It acts as an emmenagogue to stimulate menstrual flow and as a diaphoretic to induce sweating for fever reduction. Finally, its function as an alterative helps to gradually restore healthy metabolic processes within the body.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds lamiridosins A and B, tiliroside, tricoumaroyl spermidine, linoleic acid, and linolenic acid. These specific chemical constituents contribute to the diverse biological profile of the species. Lamiridosins A and B are unique secondary metabolites that play a role in the plant's chemical defense. The presence of tiliroside and tricoumaroyl spermidine further enhances its complex pharmacological potential. Additionally, the essential fatty acids linoleic acid and linolenic acid are vital components of its nutritional and therapeutic makeup.

As discussed in a study, the aqueous extract of the flowering tops of Lamium album contains the antiviral iridoid isomers lamiridosins A and B (1, 2). These specific compounds were found to significantly inhibit hepatitis C virus entry in vitro with an IC(50) of 2.31 muM. Investigations involving 14 iridoid analogues further explored related medicinal potential. While the parent iridoid glucosides showed no anti-HCV entry activity, the aglycones of shanzhiside methyl ester (4), loganin (5), loganic acid (6), geniposide (10), verbenalin (12), eurostoside (15), and picroside II (17) exhibited significant anti-HCV entry and anti-infectivity activities.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or digestive upset if ingested in large quantities. Some individuals may experience contact dermatitis characterized by redness or itching after touching the leaves. If consumed, it might lead to mild gastrointestinal discomfort such as nausea or stomach cramps. While generally considered safe in small culinary amounts, excessive use should be avoided to prevent these adverse reactions.
