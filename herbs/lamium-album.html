White dead-nettle

Lamium album

White Dead-Nettle (Lamium album) is a common flowering plant found in woodland and grassy areas, known for its medicinal properties and historical use in treating various ailments, including digestive issues and skin conditions.

Botanical Identification

Lamium album, commonly known as White Dead-Nettle, belongs to the mint family Lamiaceae. It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and white flowers, with a creeping root system. It is also referred to as Dead-Nettle in some herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Lamiales
  • Order: Lamiales
  • Family: Lamiaceae
  • Genus: Lamium
  • Species: Lamium album

White Dead-Nettle contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, coumarins, and phenolic acids. Safety Profile

Lamium album is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy due to potential uterine stimulant effects. Individuals with bleeding disorders should use caution as it may increase bleeding risk. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
