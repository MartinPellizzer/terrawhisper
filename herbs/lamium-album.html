Lamium album

What's the taxonomical classification of Lamium album?

Lamium album belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its hierarchical descent, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. At the ordinal level, it is part of the order Lamiales, which leads into its placement within the family Lamiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified under the genus Lamium and is specifically designated by the species name album.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lamium album has square stems, opposite leaves with crenate margins, and tubular, two-lipped white flowers with pinkish spots. The leaves are typically heart-shaped or ovate and feature a distinctively wrinkled or rugose surface texture. Its flowering structure consists of small, bilabiate blossoms that emerge from the leaf axils in whorls. The plant often displays a creeping habit due to its stoloniferous growth pattern. These morphological characteristics allow the species to effectively spread across shaded woodland floors.

What are the pharmacological activities of Lamium album?

This plant has anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, antimicrobial, antioxidant, and astringent pharmacological activities. Its chemical constituents allow it to combat various pathogens by exhibiting strong antimicrobial and antibacterial properties. The presence of bioactive compounds also provides significant antioxidant effects that help mitigate oxidative stress in cells. Additionally, its anti-inflammatory actions help reduce swelling and irritation in affected tissues. The plant's astringent qualities make it useful for contracting tissues and soothing mucosal membranes.

According to a study published by "Biotechnology, biotechnological equipment", Lamium album L. is a perennial herb that possesses a wide spectrum of therapeutic activities including anti-inflammatory, astringent, antiseptic, antibiotic, antispasmodic, antioxidant and anti-proliferative properties. The study utilized the A549 human cancer cell line and normal kidney epithelial cells MDCKII as controls to assess the anti-cancer effect of plant extracts. Research involving methanol and chloroform extracts of in vivo and in vitro propagated plants showed different effects on cancerous and non-cancerous cells. The extracts from Lamium album L. were found to change the transepithelial resistance (TER) and actin filaments of cells. These extracts also suppressed the epithelial transmembrae electrical resistance of MDCK II cells compared to non-treated MDCK II cells.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds lamiridosins A and B, tiliroside, tricoumaroyl spermidine, linoleic acid, and linolenic acid. These specific constituents contribute to the diverse pharmacological profile found within the species. The presence of lamiridosins A and B along with tiliroside highlights the unique chemical makeup of the plant. Additionally, the fatty acids linoleic acid and linolenic acid are integral components of its biological structure. These various substances collectively define the therapeutic potential of Lamium album.

According to a study published by "Journal of natural products", a phytochemical study of the aqueous extract of the flowering tops of Lamium album led to the identification of the antiviral iridoid isomers lamiridosins A and B (1, 2). These specific compounds were found to significantly inhibit hepatitis C virus entry in vitro with an IC50 of 2.31 muM. While the parent iridoid glucosides demonstrated no anti-HCV entry activity, the aglycones of 7 specific analogues exhibited significant anti-HCV entry and anti-infectivity activities. These identified analogues include shanzhiside methyl ester (4), loganin (5), loganic acid (6), geniposide (10), verbenalin (12), eurostoside (15), and picroside II (17).

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating asthma, bacterial infections, inflammation, obstructive pulmonary diseases, and wound healing. Its medicinal properties help soothe respiratory pathways to alleviate breathing difficulties associated with chronic lung conditions. The plant also possesses antimicrobial qualities that assist the body in fighting off various bacterial infections. Additionally, its anti-inflammatory effects can reduce swelling and pain in different parts of the body. When applied topically, it supports the natural process of wound healing by protecting damaged skin.

According to a study published by "Journal of pharmacopuncture", Lamium album is considered a candidate for managing patients with asthma and obstructive pulmonary diseases. The study evaluated the effects of hydroalcoholic extracts of the aerial parts of Lamium album on KCl-induced contraction of rat trachea. Using SPSS software 22, researchers found that the extract of Lamium album at 5 mg/ml significantly reduced the KCl-induced tracheal contraction. This relaxant effect was noted to be similar to the effect of theophylline at 20 mM. These findings suggest the plant's potential role in addressing respiratory disorders such as asthma and bronchitis.
