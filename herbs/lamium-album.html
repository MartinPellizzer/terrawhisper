Lamium album

What's the taxonomical classification of Lamium album?

Lamium album belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its hierarchical descent, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. At the ordinal level, it is part of the order Lamiales, which leads into its placement within the family Lamiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified under the genus Lamium and is specifically designated by the species name album.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Lamium album contains iridoids, flavonoids, tannins, and essential oils that provide its therapeutic properties. These chemical constituents allow the plant to act as an effective astringent and anti-inflammatory agent in traditional herbal medicine. The presence of tannins specifically helps to shrink tissues and stop minor bleeding when applied topically. Additionally, the flavonoids contribute to antioxidant activity which can assist in soothing irritated skin or mucous membranes. These compounds work together to support the plant's use in treating digestive issues and respiratory inflammation.

What are the therapeutic actions of Lamium album?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat inflammation, skin irritations, wounds, and gastrointestinal issues. It contains tannins and mucilage that provide soothing effects for minor cuts or abrasions. When applied topically, the herb helps reduce swelling and eases the discomfort of skin rashes. Some traditional practices also involve using infusions to calm digestive upsets or minor internal inflammations. These medicinal properties make it a useful component in various herbal remedies for topical and digestive ailments.
