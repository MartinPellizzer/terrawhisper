Ajuga macrosperma

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ajuga macrosperma has a creeping growth habit characterized by stolons that produce small, ovate leaves with serrated margins and a distinctively hairy texture. The plant features terminal inflorescences where tubular, bilabiate flowers emerge in dense clusters. These flowers typically exhibit a deep blue or violet pigmentation with lighter markings on the lower lip to guide pollinators. The stems are square in cross-section, a common trait among the Lamiaceae family, and are covered in fine pubescence. Its most defining morphological feature is the relatively large seed size compared to other species within the genus.
