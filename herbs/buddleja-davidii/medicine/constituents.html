10 Medicinal Constituents Of Buddleja Davidii (Butterfly Bush)

Buddleja davidii has active constituents such as flavonoids, iridoid glycosides, and terpenoids.

These compounds have been found to exhibit anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties. The iridoid glycosides, in particular, have shown potential in reducing anxiety and depression by modulating GABA levels.

For example, individuals with anxiety disorders may experience improved mood and reduced stress levels with the consumption of Buddleja davidii extracts, leading to enhanced quality of life.

1. Isoquercitrin

Buddleja davidii isoquercitrin is a flavonoid glycoside compound isolated from the leaves of the plant.

It has been shown to possess various biological activities, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. Studies have demonstrated that isoquercitrin can inhibit the growth of certain cancer cells and may be effective in treating cardiovascular diseases and other disorders.

Further research is needed to fully understand its effects and potential therapeutic applications.

2. Quercetin

Buddleja davidii quercetin is a type of flavonoid compound found in its leaves and flowers.

3. Kaempferol

Buddleja davidii kaempferol is a flavonoid glycoside that has been isolated from its flowers.

Kaempferol possesses various biological activities, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. It has been reported to exhibit inhibitory effects on platelet aggregation and blood lipid peroxidation.

Additionally, kaempferol has been shown to have antimicrobial activity against certain bacteria and fungi.
