What's the taxonomical classification of Typhonium roxburghii?

Typhonium roxburghii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Aliasmaetales into the family Araceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific placement within the genus Typhonium, ultimately reaching its species designation as roxburghii.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including skin diseases, digestive issues, rheumatism, and inflammation. In traditional medicine, the roots are often processed to mitigate their toxicity before being applied to skin lesions or infections. It is frequently utilized to alleviate internal discomforts such as stomach aches and intestinal disorders. Additionally, the plant's properties are sometimes harnessed to help reduce swelling and manage joint pain associated with rheumatic conditions. This botanical specimen serves as a versatile component in regional herbal remedies for both topical and systemic issues.
