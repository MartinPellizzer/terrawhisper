Botanical Identification of Flat Prickly Pear (Opuntia humifusa)

To identify Flat Prickly Pear (Opuntia humifusa) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Flat Prickly Pear, or Opuntia humifusa, belongs to the plant kingdom, specifically the division of seed-producing plants. It is classified under the class Caryophyllopsida, order Caryophyllales, and family Cactaceae. The genus is Opuntia, and the species is Opuntia humifusa.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Flat Prickly Pear is Opuntia humifusa, first described by the botanist Carl von Linné in 1753. It is also known by several synonyms, including Carnegiea humifusa and Opuntia hindsii, reflecting historical taxonomic revisions. Common names such as Prickly Pear, Flat Cactus, and Hairy Opuntia are widely used in herbal and regional contexts.

Morphological Characteristics

Flat Prickly Pear (Opuntia humifusa) is a low-growing cactus with a sprawling habit. It has a shallow, fibrous root system adapted to dry environments. The stems are flat, fleshy, and segmented, often covered in spines. It lacks true leaves, with photosynthesis occurring through the stem surfaces. The inflorescence is a cluster of yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by fleshy, red fruits containing small, black seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Flat Prickly Pear (Opuntia humifusa) can be distinguished by its flat, spineless stems and rounded, fleshy pads. It lacks the tall, columnar stems of Opuntia stricta and has a more sprawling growth habit. The flowers are typically yellow with red streaks, and the fruit is a small, round, red berry. These features help differentiate it from other Opuntia species.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Flat Prickly Pear (Opuntia humifusa) is native to eastern North America, particularly the southeastern and mid-Atlantic regions. It thrives in temperate climates with full sun and well-drained soils. It commonly grows at low to mid-altitudes, often in open fields, along roadsides, and in disturbed areas.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Flat Prickly Pear (Opuntia humifusa) blooms from late spring to early summer, typically between May and July. Seed formation occurs in late summer, around August to September. For identification purposes, the fruit is ready for harvest in late summer to early fall, when the skin turns a deep red or orange.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Opuntia humifusa belongs to the Cactaceae family and is closely related to other Opuntia species. It shares characteristics with its genus, such as flat, pad-like stems and spines. Its phylogenetic position reflects evolutionary links within the Cactaceae, distinguishing it from other families like Euphorbiaceae.
