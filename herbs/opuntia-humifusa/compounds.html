Flat Prickly Pear (Opuntia humifusa) Compounds

The primary compounds in Flat Prickly Pear include flavonoids, which act as antioxidants, and phenolic acids, known for their anti-inflammatory properties. Tannins and saponins contribute to its astringent effects, while coumarin and terpenoids provide antimicrobial benefits. Glycosides, essential oils, and alkaloids further support its medicinal profile, and anthocyanins offer additional antioxidant support.

Flat Prickly Pear (Opuntia humifusa) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Flat Prickly Pear (Opuntia humifusa) are bioactive compounds known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. They contribute to the plant's traditional use in supporting immune function and skin health. These compounds are naturally present in the fruit and pads of the plant.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Flat Prickly Pear (Opuntia humifusa). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress in the body. This compound supports the plant's traditional use in promoting health and preventing chronic diseases.

3. Tannin

Tannin in Flat Prickly Pear (Opuntia humifusa) contributes to its astringent properties, aiding in reducing inflammation and supporting wound healing. It also plays a role in the plant's ability to retain moisture, which is beneficial in traditional herbal remedies for digestive and skin conditions.

4. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Flat Prickly Pear (Opuntia humifusa). It exhibits antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, contributing to the plant's traditional use in treating skin conditions and digestive issues. Saponin's ability to interact with cell membranes enhances its therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Flat Prickly Pear (Opuntia humifusa). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. Coumarin is also known for its potential role in supporting digestive health. This compound is found in various parts of the plant, including the stems and leaves.

6. Terpenoid
