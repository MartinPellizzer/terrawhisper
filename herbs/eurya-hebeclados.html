What's the taxonomical classification of Eurya hebeclados?

Eurya hebeclados belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Ericales. This specific plant is a representative of the family Pentaphylacaceae, situated within the genus Eurya, and is identified by its unique species designation, hebeclados.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Eurya hebeclados can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and localized inflammation if the plant material comes into direct contact with sensitive human tissue. The leaves and stems contain various phytochemicals that may trigger an immune response in susceptible individuals. Contact dermatitis is the most common reaction observed when people handle the foliage without protection. Some reports suggest that accidental ingestion could lead to gastrointestinal distress or other internal complications. Because scientific documentation on human toxicity is limited, caution is advised when interacting with this species.
