Elettaria Cardamomum

Elettaria cardamomum, commonly known as cardamom, is a highly valued spice and medicinal plant native to South and Southeast Asia.

It has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic and Chinese medicine for its digestive, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. The essential oils extracted from its seeds contain compounds like cineole and terpinenes, which contribute to its therapeutic effects. Cardamom is often used to alleviate symptoms of indigestion, bloating, and respiratory issues due to its carminative and expectorant actions.

Additionally, it is believed to support oral health and may help in reducing the risk of certain chronic diseases when consumed as part of a balanced diet.
