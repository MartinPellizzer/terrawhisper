Boehmeria penduliflora

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Boehmeria penduliflora has an erect, herbaceous habit characterized by slender, often reddish stems and simple or occasionally branched growth patterns. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, featuring serrated margins and a distinctively pointed apex. A defining morphological trait is the presence of pendulous, spike-like inflorescences that hang downward from the leaf axils. These flowering structures consist of numerous small, inconspicuous flowers arranged along a central axis. The plant's overall structure is relatively delicate, distinguishing it from more robust members of the Urticaceae family.

    What are the herbal preparations of Boehmeria penduliflora?

    Boehmeria penduliflora is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice to utilize its medicinal properties.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Boehmeria penduliflora can cause skin irritation, redness, and itching if the plant comes into direct contact with human skin. Some individuals may experience localized swelling or a stinging sensation following exposure. While systemic toxicity is not widely documented, topical dermatological reactions are the primary concern for those handling the species. It is advisable to avoid touching the foliage to prevent these unpleasant physical symptoms.
