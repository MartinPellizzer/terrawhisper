Lupinus hirsutus var. micranthus

Lupinus hirsutus var. micranthus, also known as the dwarf hairy lupine, is a perennial plant species in the legume family, characterized by its compact growth habit and small, pink or purple flowers.

Traditionally, the plant has been used for its medicinal properties, including its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects.

The health benefits of Lupinus hirsutus var. micranthus include potential uses in treating wounds, skin conditions, and respiratory issues, as well as providing antioxidant and anti-aging properties.

In herbal preparations, the plant is often made into teas, infusions, or tinctures, which can be used to harness its medicinal properties and promote overall health and well-being.
