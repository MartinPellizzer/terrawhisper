Possible Side Effects of Lithocarpus nitidus

Lithocarpus nitidus can have side effects if used improperly, such as causing allergic reactions, particularly skin irritation and hives, due to the high concentration of tannins and saponins present in the plant.

Improper consumption can also lead to gastrointestinal issues, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, as the plant's toxic compounds can disrupt digestive function.

Prolonged exposure to the plant's sap or extracts can cause photodermatitis, a condition characterized by redness, itching, and blistering of the skin upon exposure to sunlight.

In severe cases, misuse of Lithocarpus nitidus can lead to more serious health complications, including liver damage and respiratory distress.
