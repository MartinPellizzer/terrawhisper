Pedicularis nigra

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pedicularis nigra has erect, unbranched stems reaching up to 40 centimeters in height that are topped by dense, cylindrical spikes of dark purple, tubular flowers. The leaves are primarily basal and deeply pinnatifid, featuring narrow, linear lobes that give them a feathery appearance. Higher up the stem, the leaves become smaller and more sparsely arranged. The individual flowers possess a prominent, hooded upper lip that protects the internal reproductive structures. This morphological structure is characteristic of the Lousewort genus and helps facilitate specific pollination mechanisms.
