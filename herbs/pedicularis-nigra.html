Pedicularis nigra

What's the taxonomical classification of Pedicularis nigra?

Pedicularis nigra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Lamiales. It is a member of the family Orobanchaceae, falling into the genus Pedicularis, and is specifically identified by its species name, nigra.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pedicularis nigra has erect, unbranched stems reaching up to 40 centimeters in height that are topped by dense, cylindrical spikes of dark purple, tubular flowers. The leaves are primarily basal and deeply pinnatifid, featuring narrow, linear lobes that give them a feathery appearance. Higher up the stem, the leaves become smaller and more sparsely arranged. The individual flowers possess a prominent, hooded upper lip that protects the internal reproductive structures.
