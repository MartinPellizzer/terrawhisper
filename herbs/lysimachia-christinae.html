Creeping Jenny

Lysimachia christinae

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family

Botanical Identification

Creeping Jenny, scientifically known as Lysimachia christinae, belongs to the Primulaceae family. It is also called fanflower and golden ragwort, and is a low-growing perennial. Native to Europe, it has spreading stems, opposite heart-shaped leaves, and small yellow flowers. It is sometimes referred to as L. nummularia in older herbal texts.

Active Compounds

Creeping Jenny contains key active compounds such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and tannins. These compounds contribute to its medicinal properties by exhibiting antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. They work synergistically to support overall health and may enhance the herb's therapeutic potential.

  • Flavonoid
  • Phenolic acid
  • Tannin

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory conditions and as a diuretic.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and as an antiseptic.

Poultice: Used for skin infections and inflammation.

Safety Profile

Creeping Jenny is generally considered safe when used in moderation. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy due to potential risks. There are no well-documented toxic effects, but excessive use may lead to gastrointestinal discomfort. Always consult a healthcare professional before use.
