Chicory (Cichorium intybus) Uses

Chicory is used to treat gastritis, constipation, and liver dysfunction. It helps with gastroesophageal reflux disease and irritable bowel syndrome. It may also support the healing of gastric ulcers and manage hepatitis. Its benefits are attributed to its anti-inflammatory and digestive properties.

Chicory (Cichorium intybus) Monograph

1. Gastritis

Chicory root is used to support digestive health in gastritis by reducing inflammation and soothing the stomach lining. It contains inulin, which promotes healthy gut bacteria, and has mild antacid properties. Regular consumption may help alleviate symptoms like bloating and indigestion associated with gastritis.

2. Constipation

Chicory root is used to treat constipation due to its high inulin content, a prebiotic that promotes healthy gut bacteria and softens stools. It also acts as a mild laxative, helping to stimulate bowel movements and improve digestive regularity.

3. Hepatic dysfunction

Chicory root contains inulin, which supports liver function by promoting detoxification and improving bile flow. It is used to treat hepatic dysfunction, including conditions like cirrhosis and hepatitis, due to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Clinical studies suggest it may help regenerate liver cells and reduce oxidative stress.

4. Gastroesophageal reflux

Chicory root contains inulin, which supports gut health and may reduce symptoms of gastroesophageal reflux. Its mild bitterness can stimulate digestive enzymes, aiding digestion and reducing postprandial discomfort. Studies suggest it may help regulate stomach acid and improve overall digestive function in individuals with reflux.

5. Gastroesophageal reflux disease

Chicory root contains inulin, which may help reduce stomach acid and soothe the esophagus. It can be used as a natural remedy to alleviate symptoms of gastroesophageal reflux disease. However, it should be used under the guidance of a qualified herbalist or healthcare provider.

6. Irritable bowel syndrome

Chicory root has been used traditionally to support digestive health. It contains inulin, a prebiotic that may help regulate gut bacteria and reduce symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome. Some studies suggest it can ease bloating and discomfort, though more research is needed to confirm its efficacy.

7. Hepatic disorders

Chicory root contains inulin, a prebiotic that supports liver health by promoting beneficial gut bacteria. It is used in herbal medicine to aid in detoxification and may help manage conditions like hepatitis and fatty liver disease. Its mild bitter compounds also stimulate bile production, enhancing digestive and liver function.

8. Liver dysfunction

Chicory root contains compounds that support liver function by promoting detoxification and reducing inflammation. It may help alleviate symptoms of liver dysfunction such as jaundice and fatigue. Traditionally used in herbal medicine, chicory is believed to enhance the liver's ability to process toxins and maintain overall metabolic health.

9. Gastric ulcer

Chicory root contains inulin, a prebiotic that supports gut health. It may reduce stomach acid and protect the lining of the stomach, making it beneficial for managing gastric ulcers. Its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties help repair damaged tissue and promote healing. Regular use can alleviate symptoms and support overall digestive wellness.

10. Hepatitis

Chicory root contains compounds that support liver function and may aid in the treatment of hepatitis by promoting detoxification and reducing inflammation. It is often used in herbal medicine to support liver health and is considered beneficial in managing hepatitis symptoms.
