Stelis verbiformis

Stelis verbiformis, commonly known as the "Spider Orchid," is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

This plant has been used in traditional medicine for its health benefits, including its ability to lower blood pressure, reduce inflammation, and act as an anti-diabetic agent.

The therapeutic actions of Stelis verbiformis are attributed to its bioactive constituents, which include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids that exhibit antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and vasodilatory properties.

The plant's bioactive compounds have been studied for their potential to reduce oxidative stress, improve cardiovascular health, and exhibit antimicrobial and anticancer activities.

Possible Side Effects of Stelis verbiformis

Stelis verbiformis can have side effects if used improperly, such as inducing allergic reactions, including hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.

In severe cases, it can cause anaphylaxis, a life-threatening condition that requires immediate medical attention.

The improper use of Stelis verbiformis can also lead to gastrointestinal problems, such as nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, as well as interact with medications and exacerbate underlying health conditions.

Furthermore, its improper use can cause cardiovascular issues, including rapid heartbeat, palpitations, and decreased blood pressure.
