Phaius delavayi

What's the taxonomical classification of Phaius delavayi?

Phaius delaavayi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its placement in the order Asparagales, it is further categorized into the family Orchidaceae. Ultimately, the plant is identified by the genus Phaius and is specifically designated as the species delaavayi.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including respiratory issues, skin diseases, and digestive disturbances. The rhizomes are frequently applied topically to soothe skin irritations and manage wounds. When prepared correctly, the plant components are also utilized to alleviate symptoms of coughs and asthma. Some traditional practices involve using its extracts to settle stomach pains and general gastrointestinal discomfort. These medicinal applications are rooted in long-standing ethnobotanical knowledge of the species.
