Phaius delavayi

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phaius delavayi has large, fleshy, ovate leaves that grow from long, creeping rhizomes and produce prominent, multi-flowered inflorescences with large, yellowish-white petals marked by purple or brownish veins. The plant's architecture is characterized by its robust, sympodial growth habit common to terrestrial orchids. Each flower features a broad, three-lobed lip that serves as a landing platform for pollinators. The inflorescence can extend significantly above the foliage, bearing several heavy blossoms on a thick, upright scape. This species is notable for its substantial size compared to many other members of the Orchidaceae family.

      What are the herbal preparations of Phaius delavayi?

      Phaius delavayi is prepared as a decoction, a powder, or a topical paste depending on the specific traditional medicinal application required.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Phaius delavayi can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and digestive distress if ingested or handled improperly. While primarily grown as an ornamental plant, its sap or contact with its tissues may trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Ingesting parts of the plant could lead to nausea, vomiting, or stomach cramps due to unknown toxic compounds. There is limited clinical research regarding its systemic toxicity, so caution is necessary when using it in any medicinal or culinary context. Always wear gloves when handling the plant to prevent potential dermatological issues.
