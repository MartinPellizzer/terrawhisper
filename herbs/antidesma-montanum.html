Antidesma montanum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Antidesma montanum has simple, alternate, elliptic to oblong leaves that are typically coriaceous with entire margins and an acute apex. The foliage often displays a glossy green upper surface and a slightly paler underside. Its stems are woody and can develop a climbing or shrubby habit depending on the specific environmental conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into fleshy, globose fruits. These fruits are often used as a notable morphological feature for identifying the species in its native habitat.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Antidesma montanum targets inflammatory mediators such as cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2), inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS), and various pro-inflammatory cytokines like tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-alpha) and interleukin-6 (IL-6). These interactions are primarily driven by bioactive polyphenols and flavonoids found within the plant's extracts. By inhibiting the COX-2 enzyme, the plant reduces the synthesis of prostaglandins, which effectively mitigates pain and swelling. Furthermore, the modulation of iNOS helps control the production of nitric oxide, contributing to its potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These molecular mechanisms collectively explain the plant's traditional use in treating inflammatory conditions and oxidative stress.
