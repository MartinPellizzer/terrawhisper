Ribes pulchellum

What's the taxonomical classification of Ribes pulchellum?

Ribes pulchellum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Saxifragales and is a member of the family Grossulariaceae. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Ribes and the species pulchellum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ribes pulchellum has woody, deciduous stems that produce palmately lobed leaves with serrated margins and a distinctively glandular texture. The foliage typically features three to five lobes and a somewhat hairy or pubescent surface on both sides. Small, bell-shaped flowers emerge from the stems, often appearing in clusters during the spring flowering season. These blooms are generally pale to pinkish in color and possess delicate, tubular structures. The plant also produces small, fleshy berries that transition in color as they ripen.
