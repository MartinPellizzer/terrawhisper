What to know about Asperula odorata (woodruff) before using it medicinally

Woodruff, scientifically known as Asperula odorata, is an herb that has been revered for its impressive health properties, which have been shown to improve digestive issues and reduce inflammation.

In the culinary world, woodruff is prized for its sweet, minty flavor and aroma, making it a popular addition to desserts, salads, and herbal teas. As a hardy perennial, woodruff is relatively low-maintenance and can thrive in a variety of environments, making it a favorite among horticultural enthusiasts. Botanically speaking, woodruff belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and delicate white flowers.

With roots dating back to ancient Greece and Rome, where it was used for medicinal and ceremonial purposes, woodruff has a rich history that spans thousands of years.
