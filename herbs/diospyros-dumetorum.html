What's the taxonomical classification of Diospyros dumetorum?

Diospyros dumetorum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Ericales. This plant is situated within the family Ebenaceae, a group known for its woody characteristics, and specifically falls under the genus Diospyros. Finally, its unique biological identity is defined by its specific epithet, making its full scientific name Diospyros dumetorum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Diospyros dumetorum has an evergreen habit characterized by spreading branches, elliptical to oblong-obovate leaves with entire margins, and small, white, fragrant flowers arranged in axillary clusters. Its reproductive structures consist of small, actinomorphic flowers that develop into fleshy, ovoid berries. These fruits are often yellowish or greenish when ripe and contain a single large seed.
