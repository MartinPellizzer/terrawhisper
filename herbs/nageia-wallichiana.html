What's the taxonomical classification of Nageia wallichiana?

Nageia wallichiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is placed in the subclass Pinidae and falls under the order Pinales. This coniferous plant is a member of the family Podocarpaceae, specifically situated within the genus Nageia, and is identified by its unique species name, wallichiana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Nageia wallichiana has linear, coriaceous leaves that are typically 10 to 25 centimeters long and 1 to 2 centimeters wide, arranged spirally around the branches.

These evergreen leaves possess a dark green upper surface and a paler underside with distinct parallel venation. The plant exhibits a woody structure with a spreading canopy and relatively smooth bark on younger stems. Its reproductive morphology includes small, inconspicuous unisexual flowers that eventually develop into fleshy, berry-like drupes.

These fruits are typically yellowish or greenish and serve as a primary characteristic for distinguishing the species.
