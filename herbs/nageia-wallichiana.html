Nageia wallichiana

What's the taxonomical classification of Nageia wallichiana?

Nageia wallichiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is placed in the subclass Pinidae and falls under the order Pinales. This coniferous plant is a member of the family Podocarpaceae, specifically situated within the genus Nageia, and is identified by its unique species name, wallichiana.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, respiratory ailments, digestive disorders, and various inflammatory conditions. The bark and leaves possess antimicrobial properties that help combat fungal and bacterial growth on the skin. When applied topically, it can soothe minor wounds and reduce swelling caused by irritation. In traditional practices, decoctions of the plant are sometimes consumed to alleviate coughs or stomach discomfort. These medicinal applications rely on the plant's bioactive compounds to promote healing throughout the body.

What are the herbal preparations of Nageia wallichiana?

Nageia wallichiana is prepared as a decoction of its bark or as a medicinal infusion made from its leaves. These liquid extracts are frequently used in traditional practices to address various ailments. The bark is often boiled for extended periods to ensure the extraction of active compounds. Similarly, the leaves can be steeped in hot water to create a tea for therapeutic purposes. These different methods allow for the varied concentration of phytochemicals required for specific medicinal applications.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Nageia wallichiana can cause gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, and potential allergic reactions if ingested or touched. There is limited toxicological research available to confirm the exact severity of its chemical compounds on human physiology. Some individuals may experience nausea or abdominal pain after accidental consumption of its parts. Direct contact with the foliage or bark could potentially lead to contact dermatitis in sensitive people. Caution should always be exercised when handling this species due to the lack of comprehensive safety data.
