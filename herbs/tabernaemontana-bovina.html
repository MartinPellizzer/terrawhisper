Tabernaemontana bovina

What's the taxonomical classification of Tabernaemontana bovina?

Tabernaemontana bovina belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Gentianaales. This plant is a member of the family Apocynaceae, falling under the genus Tabernaemontana, and is specifically identified by its species name, bovina.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Tabernaemontana bovina contains various bioactive alkaloids, terpenoids, and flavonoids that possess significant pharmacological potential. These secondary metabolites are primarily concentrated in the plant's leaves and latex. Researchers have identified specific indole alkaloids within the species that exhibit antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Some studies also suggest that the chemical constituents may provide analgesic effects through interactions with the central nervous system. The diverse chemical profile makes this plant a subject of interest for developing new therapeutic agents.

What are the therapeutic actions of Tabernaemontana bovina?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, digestive ailments, and various inflammatory conditions. The leaves and roots are often processed into topical applications to address dermatological issues like rashes or minor cuts. In some traditional medicinal practices, decoctions made from the plant are consumed to alleviate gastrointestinal distress. Researchers study its phytochemical properties to understand how it might impact microbial growth and tissue repair. This botanical species serves as a significant component in regional ethnomedicine for managing localized pain and swelling.
