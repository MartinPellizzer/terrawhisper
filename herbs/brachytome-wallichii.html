Brachytome wallichii

What's the taxonomical classification of Brachytome wallichii?

Brachytome wallichii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further classified under the order Gentianaales. It is a member of the family Rubiaceae, which places it within the genus Brachytome, eventually reaching its specific designation as the species wallichii.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Brachytome wallichii targets various inflammatory enzymes and signaling molecules, most notably cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2), lipoxygenase (LOX), and tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-alpha). Its bioactive phytochemicals, such as flavonoids and terpenoids, inhibit these proteins to suppress the production of pro-inflammatory mediators like prostaglandins and leukotrienes. These interactions help modulate the NF-kappaB signaling pathway, which is a central regulator of the immune response and cellular inflammation. Additionally, certain compounds in the plant have demonstrated the ability to interfere with protein kinases involved in cell proliferation and survival. By disrupting these specific molecular pathways, the plant exerts its observed antimicrobial, antioxidant, and anti-inflammatory medicinal effects.

    What are the herbal preparations of Brachytome wallichii?

    Brachytome wallichii is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a paste using the dried leaves and stems of the plant. These liquid preparations are frequently consumed to treat various digestive ailments and respiratory issues.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Brachytome wallichii can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested improperly. Contact with the plant's sap or leaves may trigger redness, itching, or swelling on sensitive skin. If consumed, individuals might experience nausea, vomiting, or stomach cramps due to the presence of bioactive compounds. There is also a potential for systemic allergic reactions in people with high sensitivities to the Asteraceae family. Careful handling and professional medical consultation are necessary to avoid these adverse reactions.
