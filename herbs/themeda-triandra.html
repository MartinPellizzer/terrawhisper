Themeda triandra

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Themeda triandra has an upright, tufted growth habit characterized by slender, caespitose culms that reach heights of 20 to 100 centimeters. The leaves are linear and typically measure up to 30 centimeters in length with a prominent midrib and scabrid margins. Its inflorescence consists of a branched panicle featuring several spikelets arranged in pairs along the branches. Each spikelet contains a single fertile floret protected by a lemma and a palea, often displaying a reddish or purplish hue. The plant also features a fibrous root system that allows it to thrive in various grassland environments.
