Themeda triandra

What's the taxonomical classification of Themeda triandra?

Themeda triandra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Poales and belongs to the family Poaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by the genus Themeda and the specific species epithet triandra.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Themeda triandra has an upright, tufted growth habit characterized by slender, caespitose culms that reach heights of 20 to 100 centimeters.

The leaves are linear and typically measure up to 30 centimeters in length with a prominent midrib and scabrid margins. Its inflorescence consists of a branched panicle featuring several spikelets arranged in pairs along the branches. Each spikelet contains a single fertile floret protected by a lemma and a palea, often displaying a reddish or purplish hue.

The plant also features a fibrous root system that allows it to thrive in various grassland environments.
