Morning Glory (Pharbitis Asteropila)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Pharbitis asteropila.

Morning glory, scientifically known as Pharbitis asteropila, is a flowering plant commonly recognized as a medicinal herb with adaptogenic properties. It has been traditionally used in various cultures for its purported ability to enhance vitality and support mental clarity. The primary benefits of morning glory include its potential to alleviate symptoms of anxiety, improve cognitive function, and promote relaxation due to its mild sedative effects. In traditional Chinese medicine and Ayurvedic practices, it has been used to treat insomnia, fatigue, and digestive issues, while in modern wellness settings, it is sometimes consumed as a herbal supplement or in teas to support emotional balance. Notably, morning glory contains unique compounds like lysergic acid amide (LSA), which has sparked interest in its historical and pharmacological significance, though its use remains controversial due to potential psychoactive effects.
