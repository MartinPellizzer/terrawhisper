Abutilon striatum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Abutilon striatum has ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins and distinctive longitudinal veins, upright woody stems, and bell-shaped flowers featuring prominent striations on the corolla. The foliage often displays a textured surface and a deep green color that contrasts with the delicate floral structures. Its inflorescences emerge from the leaf axils, presenting bright colors that attract various pollinators. The plant's growth habit is typically shrubby, reaching moderate heights depending on environmental conditions. These morphological traits collectively define its appearance within the Malvaceae family.

Within these habitats, it is often found in well-drained soils and sunny or partially shaded areas.
