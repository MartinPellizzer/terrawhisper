Abutilon striatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Abutilon striatum?

Abutilon striatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic descent, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized under the order Malvales and the family Malvacaceae. Finally, it is identified by its genus, Abutilon, and its specific species name, striatum.

The specific chemical compounds within these tissues provide the therapeutic effects sought in folk medicine.

    What are the herbal preparations of Abutilon striatum?

    Abutilon striatum is prepared primarily as an aqueous infusion or decoction of its leaves and stems for medicinal use. These liquid extracts are often ingested to address specific internal ailments or applied topically to treat skin irritations. Some traditional practices also involve crushing the fresh plant material to create a topical poultice for localized application. The concentration of the preparation depends on whether the plant parts are steeped in hot water or boiled for an extended period. These various forms allow for different methods of absorption and therapeutic delivery.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Abutilon striatum can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or digestive distress if handled improperly or ingested. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, many members of the Malvaceae family possess compounds that may trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Physical contact with the foliage might lead to redness, itching, or localized swelling on the skin. If accidentally consumed, the plant could potentially cause nausea or stomach discomfort. It is advisable to use gloves when pruning and to keep the plant away from pets and small children to avoid accidental ingestion.
