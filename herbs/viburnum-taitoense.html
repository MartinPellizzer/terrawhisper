Viburnum taitoense

What's the taxonomical classification of Viburnum taitoense?

Viburnum taitoense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is classified under the order Dipsacales. It is a member of the family Viburnacea, falling under the genus Viburnum, and is specifically identified by its species name, taitoense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viburnum taitoense has opposite, simple leaves that are typically 3 to 8 centimeters long, elliptical or ovate in shape, and possess serrated margins with acute tips. The leaf surfaces can range from glabrous to slightly pubescent, depending on the specific variety or environmental conditions. These plants feature woody stems that develop a spreading or upright growth habit as they mature. During the flowering season, small, white, five-petaled flowers emerge in dense, flat-topped cymes. Following pollination, the plant produces small, fleshy drupes that transition from green to a dark blue or black color when ripe.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the southeastern United States, specifically occurring in the coastal plain regions of North Carolina, South Carolina, Georgia, and Florida. It thrives in the moist, acidic soils characteristic of these subtropical maritime environments. Within these states, the species is commonly found growing in shaded areas such as pine flatwoods, hammocks, and stream banks. Its distribution is largely restricted by these specific moisture and soil requirements. Consequently, populations are most concentrated along the Atlantic and Gulf Coastal Plains.

What are the pharmacological activities of Viburnum taitoense?

This plant has potent analgesic and anti-inflammatory pharmacological activities that allow it to mitigate pain and reduce swelling. Researchers have identified specific bioactive compounds within Viburnum taitoense that effectively inhibit inflammatory mediators in the body. These chemical constituents work by modulating biological pathways to alleviate discomfort and suppress systemic inflammation. Studies suggest that the plant's extracts can serve as natural alternatives for managing various inflammatory conditions. Such therapeutic properties make it a subject of significant interest in ethnopharmacological research.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", Viburnum taitoense contains 3.46 ± 0.04% total phenolic, 2.38 ± 0.04% total flavonoids, and 14.96 ± 0.17% total triterpenoids. The ethyl acetate extract of V. taitoense (EEVt) at dosages of 37.5 and 75 μg/mL showed significant inhibitory (p < 0.001) effects on nitric oxide production in RAW264.7 macrophages. At a high dosage of 200 μg/mL, EEvt displayed highly significant inhibitory (p < 0.001) effects on the production of proinflammatory cytokines IL-6, IL-1β, and TNF-α. In vivo tests demonstrated that EEvt had obvious anti-inflammatory activity after carrageenan injection (p < 0.05), reaching its strongest effect at 4 h. Additionally, EEvt at 200 mg/kg showed significant (p < 0.05) anti-inflammatory activity in ear swelling tests, while formalin and acetic acid writhing experiments confirmed significant (p < 0.05) analgesic activity.
