10 Health Benefits Of Citrullus Vulgaris (Watermelon)

Citrullus vulgaris, also known as watermelon, has health benefits such as reducing inflammation, improving cardiovascular health, and aiding in hydration.

Its rich content of citrulline, lycopene, and potassium gives it anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. For example, watermelon juice has been used to alleviate symptoms of high blood pressure and heat stroke in athletes.

Additionally, the plant's antioxidant properties have been shown to reduce oxidative stress in patients with type 2 diabetes, improving overall quality of life.

This article explains in details the 10 best health benefits of Citrullus vulgaris .

1. Supports hydration

Citrullus vulgaris supports hydration because of its high water content, approximately 92%.

The majority of the fruit is made up of water, making it an ideal snack to replenish fluids. Watermelon's electrolytes and antioxidants also contribute to its hydrating properties, helping to regulate fluid balance and prevent dehydration.

Regular consumption of watermelon can aid in maintaining optimal hydration levels in the body.

2. Boosts antioxidant levels

Citrullus vulgaris boosts antioxidant levels because of its rich content of citrulline and lycopene.

These compounds help to neutralize free radicals in the body, protecting cells from oxidative stress and damage. Additionally, watermelon's high water content makes it an easily digestible source of antioxidants, allowing the body to absorb and utilize these nutrients efficiently to promote overall health and well-being.

Regular consumption may also improve cardiovascular health.

3. Lowers blood pressure

Citrullus vulgaris lowers blood pressure because it is rich in citrulline, an amino acid that helps relax blood vessels.

Citrulline is converted into arginine in the body, which in turn increases the production of nitric oxide, a potent vasodilator that widens blood vessels and improves blood flow, thereby reducing blood pressure and improving overall cardiovascular health.

Regular consumption of watermelon may be beneficial for individuals with hypertension.

4. Helps manage diabetes

Citrullus vulgaris helps manage diabetes because of its rich content of citrulline, a compound that has been shown to improve insulin sensitivity and glucose metabolism.

The citrulline in watermelon works by increasing the production of nitric oxide, which helps to improve blood flow and reduce inflammation in the body, ultimately leading to better glucose control and improved management of diabetes symptoms.

Regular consumption of watermelon may also reduce the risk of complications associated with diabetes.

5. Reduces inflammation

Citrullus vulgaris reduces inflammation because of its rich content of citrulline and l-citrulline.

These amino acids play a crucial role in reducing inflammation by dilating blood vessels and improving circulation. Additionally, watermelon's high antioxidant and water content help to combat oxidative stress and inflammation.

The anti-inflammatory properties of watermelon make it an excellent dietary choice for managing conditions like arthritis, gout, and other inflammatory diseases.

6. Prevents kidney stones

Citrullus vulgaris prevents kidney stones because it is rich in citrulline, an amino acid that increases urine production and helps eliminate minerals that can form stones.

Watermelon also contains other beneficial compounds, such as potassium and magnesium, which can help relax muscles and reduce the risk of stone formation.

Regular consumption of watermelon may help prevent kidney stones by maintaining a healthy balance of minerals in the body.

7. Fights cancer cells

Citrullus vulgaris fights cancer cells because of its unique combination of vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants.

The citrulline and cucurbitacin E present in watermelon have been shown to inhibit the growth of cancer cells by inducing apoptosis, or programmed cell death.

Additionally, watermelon's high content of vitamin C and lycopene helps to protect against DNA damage and oxidative stress, making it a valuable addition to a cancer-fighting diet.

8. Relieves heartburn

Citrullus vulgaris relieves heartburn because it contains high levels of citrulline and water.

The citrulline acts as a natural antacid, neutralizing stomach acid and reducing inflammation in the esophagus. The water content helps to dilute the stomach acid, further alleviating symptoms of heartburn.

Additionally, watermelon's rich in electrolytes, including potassium, helps to relax the muscles in the digestive tract, preventing acid reflux.

9. Helps reduce fatigue

Citrullus vulgaris helps reduce fatigue because it is rich in citrulline, an amino acid that has been shown to increase blood flow and reduce oxidative stress.

The high water content of watermelon also helps to replenish fluids and electrolytes, making it a natural remedy for dehydration and exhaustion.

Additionally, the antioxidants in watermelon, such as vitamin C and lycopene, help to combat oxidative stress and reduce muscle damage.

10. Improves eye health

Citrullus vulgaris improves eye health because of its high water content and rich stores of antioxidants.

The antioxidant properties help protect the eyes from damage caused by free radicals, reducing the risk of age-related macular degeneration and cataracts.

Lycopene, a pigment in watermelon, also contributes to improved eye health by neutralizing free radicals and promoting collagen production, which strengthens the lens and improves vision.
