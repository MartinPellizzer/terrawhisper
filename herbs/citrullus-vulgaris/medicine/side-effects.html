1. Causes allergic reactions

Citrullus vulgaris causes allergic reactions.

The allergenic properties of this fruit are attributed to its protein content, specifically the presence of profilin and vicilin proteins.

These proteins can trigger an immune response in sensitive individuals, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, swelling, and difficulty breathing.

2. Triggers digestive issues

Citrullus vulgaris triggers digestive issues due to its high content of raffinose, a complex sugar that can be difficult for some individuals to digest.

The fiber and citrulline in watermelon can also contribute to these issues, as they can cause bloating, gas, and stomach discomfort in susceptible individuals.

Additionally, the natural sugars and acids present in watermelon may irritate the digestive system and lead to symptoms such as diarrhea, nausea, or stomach pain.

3. Increases blood pressure

Citrullus vulgaris increases blood pressure due to its high levels of citrulline, a precursor to nitric oxide.

When consumed in excess, citrulline can cause an overload of the body's nitric oxide system, leading to increased blood pressure.

Additionally, the natural sugars present in watermelon, such as fructose and glucose, can also contribute to elevated blood pressure levels by promoting insulin resistance and inflammation.

4. Lowers blood sugar levels

Citrullus vulgaris lowers blood sugar levels due to its high content of citrulline, an amino acid that can increase insulin sensitivity and glucose uptake in the body.

Additionally, watermelon's water content can lead to increased urination, which can further contribute to a decrease in blood sugar levels.

This reduction in blood sugar may be beneficial for individuals with type 2 diabetes or those trying to manage their blood sugar levels naturally.

5. Interacts with medications

Citrullus vulgaris interacts with medications.

When consuming citrulline-rich foods like watermelon, individuals may experience side effects such as increased blood pressure and heart rate due to the amino acid's vasodilatory properties. This interaction can be particularly concerning for those taking blood thinners or cardiovascular medications, as the increased blood flow could negate the effectiveness of these treatments.

The citrulline in watermelon can also enhance the action of certain antidepressant medications, potentially leading to serotonin syndrome.

6. Worsens asthma symptoms

Citrullus vulgaris worsens asthma symptoms due to its high levels of histamine and citric acid.

These compounds can trigger allergic reactions in some individuals, causing the airways to constrict and leading to increased symptoms such as wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.

The allergenic properties of Citrullus vulgaris may also stimulate the release of chemical mediators, further exacerbating respiratory issues.

7. Irritates skin and mucous

Citrullus vulgaris irritates skin and mucous due to its high concentration of citrulline and cucurbitacins.

These compounds can cause allergic reactions, leading to skin redness, itching, and inflammation, while the mucous membranes may become irritated, resulting in burning sensations and discomfort.

Additionally, the presence of other chemicals like lectin and saponins may contribute to the irritating effects on skin and mucous membranes, making watermelon an allergenic food for some individuals.

8. Produces gastrointestinal upset

Citrullus vulgaris produces gastrointestinal upset in some individuals due to its high water content and natural sugars.

The rapid digestion of these substances can cause an imbalance in gut flora, leading to symptoms such as bloating, cramps, and diarrhea.

Additionally, the presence of citrulline, a non-protein amino acid found in large quantities in watermelon, may also contribute to gastrointestinal distress by disrupting normal stomach function.

9. Induces kidney stones

Citrullus vulgaris induces kidney stones due to its high concentration of citrulline and fructose.

The citrulline in watermelon can increase the body's production of oxalate, a mineral that combines with calcium to form kidney stones.

Additionally, the natural sugars in watermelon, such as fructose, can slow down digestion and lead to an increased risk of stone formation by allowing more time for minerals to concentrate and solidify into crystals within the kidneys.

10. Affects liver function tests

Citrullus vulgaris affects liver function tests.

This is due to the presence of cucurbitacins and flavonoids in the fruit, which can cause alterations in the liver's metabolism and detoxification processes.

Additionally, the high concentration of citrulline, a non-protein amino acid, may also contribute to changes in liver enzymes, leading to abnormal liver function test results.
