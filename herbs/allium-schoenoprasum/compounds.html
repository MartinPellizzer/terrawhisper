Chives (Allium schoenoprasum) Compounds

Chives contain essential oils, which contribute to their aromatic profile. Flavonoids and phenolic acids act as antioxidants. Terpenoids and saponins have anti-inflammatory properties. Glycosides and tannins may support digestive health. Coumarin, alkaloids, and anthocyanins add additional medicinal benefits.

Chives (Allium schoenoprasum) Monograph

1. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound in Chives (Allium schoenoprasum). It contains phytochemicals like sulfur-containing compounds that contribute to its medicinal properties. The essential oil is used for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. It supports digestive health and may help with respiratory conditions.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Chives (Allium schoenoprasum) contribute to their anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help reduce oxidative stress and support immune function. They are naturally present in the plant and are beneficial when consumed as part of a herbal regimen. Flavonoids may also aid in improving cardiovascular health.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid in Chives (Allium schoenoprasum) contributes to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound helps protect cells from oxidative stress and may support immune function. It is one of several bioactive components in chives that contribute to their traditional use in herbal medicine.

4. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Chives (Allium schoenoprasum) contribute to their medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. 5. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Chives (Allium schoenoprasum). It contributes to the plant's antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. 6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Chives (Allium schoenoprasum). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, including antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Glycosides in Chives are often used in herbal medicine to support digestive and respiratory health.

8. Coumarin

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Chives (Allium schoenoprasum). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by having antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Alkaloids in Chives are often used in herbal medicine to support digestive and respiratory health.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Chives (Allium schoenoprasum). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports overall health by enhancing immune function and protecting cellular structures.
