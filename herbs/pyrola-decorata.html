Pyrola decorata

What's the taxonomical classification of Pyrola decorata?

Pyrola decorata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic path continues through the order Ericales and the family Ericaceae, which places it within the genus Pyrola. Ultimately, the specific identity of this plant is defined by its species name, decorata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pyrola decorata has small, delicate flowers featuring five pink to purple petals with distinct white markings, often accompanied by two prominent, elongated nectar spurs at the base. The plant typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and possesses slender, lanceolate leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. These leaves are often tinged with reddish or purplish hues, providing a sharp contrast to the green foliage. The inflorescence is a terminal raceme that holds several individual blooms in a loose cluster. Its stems are relatively thin and can appear slightly hairy depending on the specific environmental conditions.

The species tends to cluster in areas with significant seasonal snowpack that provides consistent moisture during the growing season.
