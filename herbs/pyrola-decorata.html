Pyrola decorata

What's the taxonomical classification of Pyrola decorata?

Pyrola decorata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic path continues through the order Ericales and the family Ericaceae, which places it within the genus Pyrola. Ultimately, the specific identity of this plant is defined by its species name, decorata.

    Pyrola decorata is prepared as a decoction or an infusion using the dried leaves and flowering tops to extract its active constituents. Some traditional practitioners utilize the dried plant material in topical washes or poultices for localized application.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Pyrola decorata can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and mild allergic reactions if ingested or touched. Contact with the plant's foliage may trigger redness or itching on sensitive skin areas. If consumed, individuals might experience nausea, stomach cramps, or vomiting. Some people may also develop localized rashes or swelling upon direct exposure to the leaves. Monitoring for these symptoms is essential when handling the species.
