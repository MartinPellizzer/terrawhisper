Terminalia catappa

What's the taxonomical classification of Terminalia catappa?

Terminalia catappa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is situated in the order Myrtales. This plant is a member of the family Combretaceae and is specifically identified within the genus Terminalia, falling under the species name catapappa.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Terminalia catapla contains a diverse array of bioactive secondary metabolites including tannins, flavonoids, triterpenoids, and polyphenolic compounds. These chemical constituents provide the plant with significant antioxidant and antimicrobial properties. Specifically, the presence of gallic acid and various glycosides contributes to its potential use in treating inflammation and skin ailments. Research also indicates that the extracts possess cytotoxic effects that are being studied for anti-tumor activities. These medicinal properties are distributed throughout the leaves, bark, and fruit of the tree.

What are the therapeutic actions of Terminalia catappa?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, digestive disorders, respiratory issues, and inflammatory conditions. The leaves and bark are frequently applied topically to manage wounds, eczema, and various skin ailments. Extracts from the fruit and seeds are often utilized to alleviate symptoms of diarrhea, dysentery, and stomach pain. Additionally, decoctions made from the plant parts are used in traditional medicine to soothe coughs and manage fever. These therapeutic properties are attributed to the presence of bioactive compounds such as tannins and flavonoids.

What are the herbal preparations of Terminalia catappa?

Terminalia catapla is prepared as decoctions, infusions, powders, oils, and topical pastes derived from its bark, leaves, fruit, and seeds. The bark is commonly boiled in water to create a medicinal decoction used for various systemic treatments. Leaves are often steeped to make an infusion or crushed into a paste for external applications on the skin. The seeds contain significant oil content that can be extracted for topical or medicinal use. Additionally, the fruit pulp may be processed into various forms to utilize its nutritional and therapeutic properties.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Terminalia catapella can cause severe vomiting, abdominal pain, and dizziness if the seeds or unripe fruit are ingested. The seeds specifically contain compounds that are toxic to humans and can lead to significant gastrointestinal distress. While the ripe fruit is generally considered safe to eat, consuming unripe portions may result in unpleasant digestive reactions. Some individuals might also experience skin irritation or allergic reactions when coming into direct contact with certain parts of the plant. It is essential to avoid consuming any part of the plant that has not been properly identified and prepared.
