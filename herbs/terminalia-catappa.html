Terminalia catappa

What's the taxonomical classification of Terminalia catappa?

Terminalia catappa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is situated in the order Myrtales. This plant is a member of the family Combretaceae and is specifically identified within the genus Terminalia, falling under the species name catapappa.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse array of bioactive secondary metabolites including flavonoids, terpenoids, phenolic compounds, tannins, alkaloids, triterpenoids, and steroids.

The presence of phenolic compounds and tannins provides strong scavenging capabilities against free radicals. Furthermore, the complex mixture of alkaloids and steroids supports various therapeutic applications in traditional medicine.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Terminalia catapta targets various enzymes and receptors including cyclooxygenase-2, lipoxygenase, and histone deacetylases to exert its medicinal effects. Its bioactive compounds, such as tannins and flavonoids, inhibit the cyclooxygenase pathway to reduce pro-inflammatory prostaglandin synthesis. These phytochemicals also interact with protein kinases and various inflammatory signaling proteins to modulate cellular responses. Additionally, the plant's polyphenols can influence antioxidant enzymes like superoxide dismutase to mitigate oxidative stress. By engaging these specific molecular pathways, the plant demonstrates significant antibacterial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties.

What are the therapeutic actions of Terminalia catappa?

Terminalia catapta has antimicrobial, antioxidant, and anti-inflammatory properties derived from its rich concentration of tannins, flavonoids, and polyphenolic compounds. These bioactive constituents allow the plant to combat various pathogens and reduce oxidative stress within biological systems. The leaves and bark are frequently utilized in traditional medicine to treat skin infections and promote wound healing through their astringent qualities. Additionally, extracts from the fruit and seeds have demonstrated potential in managing metabolic issues and protecting cells from damage. These multifaceted therapeutic actions make the species a significant subject in pharmacological research.

    What are the herbal preparations of Terminalia catappa?

    Terminalia catapla is prepared as decoctions, infusions, powders, oils, and topical pastes derived from its bark, leaves, fruit, and seeds.
