Clematis napaulensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Clematis napaulensis?

Clematis napaulensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Ranunculales. This plant is a representative of the family Ranunculaceae, falling under the genus Clematis, and is specifically identified by its species name, napaulensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clematis napaulensis has woody climbing stems that produce large, pinnately compound leaves with multiple pairs of ovate to lanceolate leaflets. The foliage is typically dark green and features finely serrated margins along the edges of each leaflet. During its flowering phase, the plant displays showy, star-shaped blossoms that range in color from creamy white to pale lavender. These flowers are characterized by prominent, elongated stamens that extend outward from the center of the petals. As the season progresses, the plant develops feathery, achene-bearing seed heads that aid in wind dispersal.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

It thrives in these temperate zones where moisture is abundant and the air remains cool. The species is typically located at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 2,500 meters above sea level.
