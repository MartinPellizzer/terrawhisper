Botanical Identification of Pursh's Buckthorn (Frangula purshiana)

To identify Pursh's Buckthorn (Frangula purshiana) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Pursh's Buckthorn, Frangula purshiana, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Rhamnales, family Rhamnaceae, genus Frangula, and species Frangula purshiana. Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Frangula purshiana is the scientific name for Pursh's Buckthorn. It was first described by the botanist Asa Gray in 1846. The genus Frangula includes several species, and this particular one is named in honor of the botanist Andrew Pursh. It is also known by various common and regional names, reflecting its distribution and historical use in herbal medicine.

Morphological Characteristics

Pursh's Buckthorn is a deciduous shrub or small tree. It has a deep taproot system and thick, woody roots. The stem is straight, slender, and often branched, with a rough, bark-like surface. Leaves are alternate, elliptic, and have a leathery texture with prominent veins. The inflorescence is a raceme of small, greenish-white flowers. The fruit is a capsule containing small, smooth seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Pursh's Buckthorn can be distinguished by its smooth, dark green leaves with a single central vein, opposite arrangement, and small, dark red berries. It differs from similar species like Boxthorn by having shorter, more rigid thorns and a less hairy stem. The leaves are broader and more ovate compared to other buckthorn species.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Pursh's Buckthorn is native to North America, particularly the western United States and southern Canada. It thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained soils, often found in dry slopes and rocky areas. It grows at elevations between 1,000 and 2,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Pursh's Buckthorn blooms in late spring to early summer, typically between May and June. Seed formation occurs from late summer through early fall. For identification purposes, the best time to observe key features is during late spring to early summer when flowers and young leaves are most visible.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Pursh's Buckthorn (Frangula purshiana) belongs to the family Rutaceae, sharing close relationships with species in the genus Frangula. It is most closely related to Frangula alnus, differing primarily in leaf morphology and fruit structure. Its family traits include opposite leaves and small, fragrant flowers, typical of Rutaceae.
