Pursh'S Buckthorn (Frangula purshiana) Compounds

Pursh's Buckthorn contains several key compounds, including tannins, which have astringent properties, and flavonoids, known for their antioxidant effects. Phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory qualities, while coumarin has antispasmodic and anticoagulant properties. Other compounds like glycosides, terpenoids, and saponins support its traditional use in herbal medicine.

Pursh'S Buckthorn (Frangula purshiana) Monograph

1. Tannin

Tannin in Pursh's Buckthorn (Frangula purshiana) contributes to its astringent properties, aiding in digestive health and reducing inflammation. It is primarily responsible for the plant's traditional use in treating diarrhea and skin conditions. Tannins also help preserve the plant's medicinal potency during preparation.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Pursh's Buckthorn (Frangula purshiana) contribute to its medicinal properties by exhibiting anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. These compounds support the plant's traditional use in digestive and skin conditions. They are found in the bark and leaves, enhancing the plant's therapeutic potential.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Pursh's Buckthorn (Frangula purshiana). It contributes to the plant's laxative properties by stimulating intestinal motility. This compound is also associated with anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects in traditional herbal medicine.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Pursh's Buckthorn (Frangula purshiana). It contributes to the plant's traditional use as a laxative and has antimicrobial properties. Coumarin's presence supports the plant's role in herbal medicine for digestive and skin-related conditions.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Pursh's Buckthorn (Frangula purshiana). It contributes to the plant's laxative properties by stimulating intestinal motility. This compound is responsible for many of the therapeutic effects attributed to the herb in traditional herbal medicine.

6. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Pursh's Buckthorn (Frangula purshiana) contribute to its laxative properties. These compounds help stimulate bowel movements by increasing intestinal motility. They are among the key active constituents responsible for the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Pursh's Buckthorn (Frangula purshiana). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating digestive issues by promoting bowel movements. Saponins also have mild antimicrobial properties, supporting its role in herbal medicine.

8. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a key compound found in Pursh's Buckthorn (Frangula purshiana). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly in its use for digestive support and as a mild laxative. Alkaloids in this plant are known for their ability to stimulate bowel movements and aid in treating constipation.

9. Essential oil

Essential oil from Pursh's Buckthorn (Frangula purshiana) contains volatile compounds like limonene and pinene. These oils are used in traditional herbal medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties. They contribute to the plant's reputation as a remedy for digestive and skin conditions.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a pigment found in Pursh's Buckthorn (Frangula purshiana) that contributes to its medicinal properties. It acts as an antioxidant, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress in the body. This compound supports the plant's traditional use in promoting digestive health and liver function.
