Rotala rotundifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Rotala rotundifolia?

Rotala rotundifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It falls under the order Myrtales and is a member of the family Lythraceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Rotala, and its specific species name, rotundifolia.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Rotala rotundifolia contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including phenolic acids, flavonoids, and carotenoids that contribute to its medicinal potential. These compounds primarily function as potent antioxidants capable of neutralizing free radicals within cellular structures. Research suggests that the plant's flavonoid profile may provide anti-inflammatory properties useful in managing oxidative stress. Additionally, certain extracts have demonstrated antimicrobial activities against specific bacterial strains in laboratory settings. Further investigation into these phytochemical constituents is necessary to fully understand their therapeutic applications in pharmacology.

What are the therapeutic actions of Rotala rotundifolia?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat no known human diseases because it is primarily an ornamental aquatic plant used in aquariums. While some traditional studies explore its potential antioxidant properties, it is not recognized as a medical treatment for specific illnesses. The plant is mostly valued for its ability to absorb excess nutrients like nitrates and phosphates from water. This nutrient absorption helps prevent the growth of harmful algae in aquatic environments. Consequently, its role is ecological rather than therapeutic.
