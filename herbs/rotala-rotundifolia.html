Rotala rotundifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rotala rotundifolia has slender, cylindrical stems that can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters and bear small, opposite, elliptical to obovate leaves. These leaves typically measure between 5 and 15 millimeters in length and can vary in color from bright green to deep red or pink depending on light intensity. The leaf margins are generally entire, though they may sometimes appear slightly serrated. As the plant matures, the internodes between leaves may lengthen, creating a more open branching structure. This species often develops a bushy appearance due to its ability to produce multiple lateral shoots from the main stem.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Rotala rotundifolia can cause skin irritation or allergic reactions in some individuals if it comes into direct contact with the body. It is important to avoid ingesting any part of the plant as its safety for consumption has not been established. Always wash your hands thoroughly after handling aquatic vegetation to prevent accidental exposure to mouth or eyes.
