Rotala rotundifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Rotala rotundifolia?

Rotala rotundifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It falls under the order Myrtales and is a member of the family Lythraceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Rotala, and its specific species name, rotundifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rotala rotundifolia has slender, cylindrical stems that can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters and bear small, opposite, elliptical to obovate leaves.

These leaves typically measure between 5 and 15 millimeters in length and can vary in color from bright green to deep red or pink depending on light intensity. The leaf margins are generally entire, though they may sometimes appear slightly serrated. As the plant matures, the internodes between leaves may lengthen, creating a more open branching structure.

This species often develops a bushy appearance due to its ability to produce multiple lateral shoots from the main stem.

      What are the herbal preparations of Rotala rotundifolia?

      Rotala rotundifolia is prepared primarily as a dried herb for infusions, decoctions, or tinctures to extract its bioactive compounds. The dried plant material is steeped in hot water to create a tea that may be consumed for its potential health properties.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Rotala rotundifolia can cause skin irritation or allergic reactions in some individuals if it comes into direct contact with the body. While it is primarily used as an aquarium plant, handling it without protection may lead to redness or itching on sensitive skin. There is very little clinical research regarding the toxicity of this species in humans, so caution is advised during maintenance. It is important to avoid ingesting any part of the plant as its safety for consumption has not been established. Always wash your hands thoroughly after handling aquatic vegetation to prevent accidental exposure to mouth or eyes.
