Androsace erecta

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Androsace erecta has an erect, perennial herb habit characterized by a basal rosette of leaves and a single, unbranched flowering stem that typically reaches heights of 5 to 20 centimeters. The leaves are primarily basal, appearing ovate to lanceolate in shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. The inflorescence consists of small, solitary flowers that emerge from the terminal end of the stem. These flowers feature a tubular corolla that is often white or pale pink in color. The plant also possesses a woody caudex which allows it to persist through various environmental conditions.
