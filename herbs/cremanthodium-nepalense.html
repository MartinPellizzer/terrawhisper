Cremanthodium nepalense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cremanthodium nepalense has a perennial herb morphology characterized by a creeping rhizome and oppositely arranged, ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated margins. The foliage typically features prominent venation and a slightly pubescent texture on the surface. Its inflorescence consists of small, tubular flowers arranged in compact, terminal heads or clusters. These flowers are generally white or pale pink and emerge from the leaf axils or the stem tips. The plant also develops fine, spreading roots from its creeping stems to facilitate vegetative spread.
