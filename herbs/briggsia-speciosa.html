Briggsia speciosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Briggsia speciosa?

Briggsia speciosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant falls under the order Lamiales. It is further categorized into the family Gesneriaceae, which defines its closer botanical relatives. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is established through the genus Briggsia and the species name speciosa.

These methods ensure that the bioactive constituents are effectively delivered to the body.

Because this plant is not widely studied in clinical settings, its specific toxicity levels remain largely unknown to science. Contact with the leaves or stems may trigger redness, itching, or localized dermatitis in sensitive individuals. If swallowed, the plant might lead to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal cramping. It is essential to avoid direct contact and consumption until more comprehensive toxicological research is available.
