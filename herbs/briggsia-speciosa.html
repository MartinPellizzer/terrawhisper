Briggsia speciosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Briggsia speciosa has upright, decumbent stems that support large, elliptical leaves with entire margins and prominent venation. The foliage is typically dark green and possesses a slightly textured surface. Its most distinctive feature is the large, trumpet-shaped flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These corollas exhibit a vibrant color pattern, often featuring deep reddish-purple spots or streaks on a lighter base. The floral structure is designed to accommodate specific pollinators through its elongated tube.
