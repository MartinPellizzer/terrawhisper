Pedicularis mandshurica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pedicularis mandshurica has an erect, perennial habit characterized by linear to lanceolate leaves and dense, tubular flowers arranged in terminal spikes. The leaves are typically oppositely arranged and often exhibit deep lobes or divisions that give them a finely dissected appearance. Its flowering structures feature a prominent, hooded upper lip that protects the reproductive organs within the corolla. The stem is generally slender and can reach various heights depending on the specific environmental conditions of its habitat. Small, inconspicuous bracts often surround the base of each individual flower within the inflorescence.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Pedicularis mandshurica can cause potential toxic reactions such as nausea, vomiting, dizziness, and abdominal pain if ingested. Because many species within the Pedicularis genus contain bioactive compounds like iridoid glycosides, they may trigger adverse physiological responses in humans. There is also a risk of allergic skin reactions or dermatitis if the plant comes into direct contact with sensitive areas of the body. Specific clinical data regarding long-term toxicity is limited, so caution is necessary when handling this specimen. Consumption of the plant should be strictly avoided due to these uncertain safety profiles.
