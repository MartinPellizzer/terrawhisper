Oreorchis foliosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Oreorchis foliosa?

Oreorchis foliosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Asparagales and the family Orchidaceae, which defines its botanical characteristics. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Oreorchis, and its unique species designation, foliosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Oreorchis foliosa has narrow, lanceolate leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette and a single stem bearing an inflorescence of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant features a slender, upright flowering spike that can reach heights of several decimeters depending on the environment. Each flower possesses a distinct labellum that is often slightly larger than the other perianth segments. The vegetative structure is characterized by its relatively thin foliage which reflects its preference for specific light conditions. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compound C39 H54 O22 alongside several essential sugars and complex carbohydrates. These specific chemical constituents work together to provide various therapeutic properties within the species. The presence of glucose and mannose contributes to the plant's unique nutritional and metabolic profile. Furthermore, the inclusion of polysaccharides plays a significant role in its overall biochemical structure. These diverse organic molecules are central to understanding the plant's pharmacological potential.

According to a study published by "Biomedical chromatography : BMC", three O. foliosa samples were selected for the systematic determination of polysaccharide yields and monosaccharide composition. The polysaccharides of this species were composed of glucose and mannose. Although there was no significant difference in the polysaccharide yields compared to B. striata (p = 0.074), many different metabolites were screened out. Specifically, the substance C39 H54 O22 was unique to O. foliosa.
