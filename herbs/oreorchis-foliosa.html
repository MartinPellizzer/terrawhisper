Oreorchis foliosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Oreorchis foliosa?

Oreorchis foliosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Asparagales and the family Orchidaceae, which defines its botanical characteristics. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Oreorchis, and its unique species designation, foliosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Oreorchis foliosa has narrow, lanceolate leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette and a single stem bearing an inflorescence of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant features a slender, upright flowering spike that can reach heights of several decimeters depending on the environment. Each flower possesses a distinct labellum that is often slightly larger than the other perianth segments. The vegetative structure is characterized by its relatively thin foliage which reflects its preference for specific light conditions. Its overall morphology is typical of many terrestrial orchid species found in specialized habitats.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compound C39 H54 O22 alongside glucose, mannose, and various polysaccharides. These specific chemical constituents contribute to the plant's diverse therapeutic properties and biological activities.
