Rhodiola tangutica

What's the taxonomical classification of Rhodiola tangutica?

Rhodiola tangutica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Saxifragales. This plant is a member of the family Crasulaceae, specifically categorized within the genus Rhodiola, and is identified by its unique species name, tangutica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhodiola tangutica has a thick, fleshy rhizome that is reddish-brown in color and characterized by a woody texture. The plant develops basal leaves that are lanceolate or elliptical in shape, often featuring smooth or slightly serrated margins. These leaves are typically arranged in dense rosettes and possess a succulent quality to help store moisture. The flowering stems are upright and bear small, yellowish or greenish flowers clustered in compact cymes. Each individual flower contains multiple stamens and a central pistil typical of the Crassulaceae family.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to high-altitude alpine regions across the Tibetan Plateau, spanning parts of China, India, Nepal, and Bhutan. It typically thrives in rocky crevices and meadowlands found at elevations ranging from 3,000 to 5,000 meters. These populations are primarily concentrated in the Himalayan mountain range and the Hengduan Mountains. The species favors cold, moist environments characterized by intense ultraviolet radiation. Because of its specific niche, its distribution is strictly limited to high-elevation montane ecosystems.
