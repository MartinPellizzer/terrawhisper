Ligustrum compactum

What's the taxonomical classification of Ligustrum compactum?

Ligustrum compactum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage that leads to the order Lamiales. This plant is further categorized under the family Oleaceae, which defines its botanical characteristics. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Ligustrum, and its specific taxonomic designation is completed by its species name, compactum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ligustrum compactum has an evergreen, densely branched shrubby growth habit characterized by small, elliptical, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The foliage consists of leathery, opposite leaves that provide a thick, compact texture throughout the year. Small, dark purple or black drupes develop from the blossoms and serve as a primary feature of the plant's reproductive cycle. The stems are woody and often intertwine to create a sturdy, rounded mound. This compact architecture makes it particularly well-suited for hedge formation and ornamental landscaping.
