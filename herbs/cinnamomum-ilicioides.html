Cinnamomum ilicioides

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cinnamomum ilicioides has lanceolate to elliptic leaves with entire margins, a woody stem structure, and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in axillary panicles. The foliage is typically dark green and leathery, providing a dense texture to the plant's canopy. Its branching pattern follows a typical woody shrub or small tree habit common to the Lauraceae family. The reproductive structures consist of small greenish-yellow flowers that eventually give rise to fleshy drupes. These fruits serve as a key identifying feature during the maturation stage of the plant's life cycle.
