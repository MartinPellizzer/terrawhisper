Paeonia delavayi

What's the taxonomical classification of Paeonia delavayi?

Paeonia delaayi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is classified as Equisetopsida, falling under the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Saxifragales and the family Paeoniaceae. Finally, the plant is situated within the genus Paeonia, specifically identified by its species name, delaayi.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Paeonia delavayi contains high concentrations of monoterpene glycosides, specifically paeoniflorin, along with various alkaloids and phenolic compounds. These bioactive constituents are primarily located within the plant's roots and rhizomes. The presence of paeoniflorin provides significant anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and neuroprotective properties. Additionally, the plant contains tannins and flavonoids that contribute to its antioxidant capabilities. Researchers study these specific chemical profiles to develop new treatments for cardiovascular and neurological disorders.

What are the therapeutic actions of Paeonia delavayi?

Paeonia delavayi has therapeutic properties primarily derived from its ability to reduce inflammation, alleviate pain, and regulate menstrual irregularities through its concentration of bioactive compounds like paeoniflorin. These phytochemicals work by modulating immune responses and acting as natural analgesics within the body. Traditional medicinal practices often utilize the root to treat various gynecological issues and stabilize hormonal fluctuations. Furthermore, the plant possesses antioxidant qualities that help protect cells from oxidative stress and damage. Such diverse pharmacological actions make it a significant subject in ethnobotanical research.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat inflammatory conditions, menstrual irregularities, and various types of physical pain. Its medicinal properties are primarily attributed to the presence of bioactive compounds like paeoniflorin found in the roots. Practitioners often utilize these extracts to manage symptoms associated with uterine contractions and hormonal imbalances. Additionally, the plant shows potential in reducing systemic inflammation and soothing nerve-related discomfort. These therapeutic applications make it a significant subject in ethnobotanical studies regarding pain management.

What are the herbal preparations of Paeonia delavayi?

Paeonia delavayi is prepared primarily as a decoction, a tincture, or a topical salve using the dried roots of the plant. The most common method involves boiling the sliced roots in water to extract active medicinal compounds for internal consumption. For a more concentrated dose, the roots are macerated in alcohol to create a potent tincture. Some traditional applications involve grinding the dried material into a fine powder to be mixed into ointments for skin application. These various forms allow practitioners to utilize the plant's specific properties for different therapeutic purposes.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Paeonia delavayi can cause gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, and potential allergic reactions if ingested or applied topically without caution. The plant contains various bioactive compounds that may trigger nausea or abdominal cramping in sensitive individuals. Direct contact with the plant material can lead to contact dermatitis or redness on the skin. Some users might also experience hypersensitivity symptoms such as itching or swelling. It is important to exercise extreme caution because the specific toxicological profile for this species is not fully documented in clinical studies.
