Paeonia delavayi

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Paeonia delaavayi has large, deeply lobed, dark green leaves and prominent, solitary flowers that feature bright red or deep pink petals surrounding a central cluster of numerous yellow stamens. The plant typically grows from a thick, fleshy tuberous root that provides energy for its perennial life cycle. Its stems are sturdy and upright, supporting the heavy weight of the large, bowl-shaped blooms. The foliage consists of compound leaves that are divided into several distinct, lanceolate leaflets. These morphological features allow the species to thrive in the rocky, high-altitude habitats of Central Asia.

The presence of paeoniflorin provides significant anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and neuroprotective properties.       What are the herbal preparations of Paeonia delavayi?

      Paeonia delavayi is prepared primarily as a decoction, a tincture, or a topical salve using the dried roots of the plant. The most common method involves boiling the sliced roots in water to extract active medicinal compounds for internal consumption. For a more concentrated dose, the roots are macerated in alcohol to create a potent tincture. Some traditional applications involve grinding the dried material into a fine powder to be mixed into ointments for skin application. These various forms allow practitioners to utilize the plant's specific properties for different therapeutic purposes.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Paeonia delavayi can cause gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, and potential allergic reactions if ingested or applied topically without caution. The plant contains various bioactive compounds that may trigger nausea or abdominal cramping in sensitive individuals. Direct contact with the plant material can lead to contact dermatitis or redness on the skin. Some users might also experience hypersensitivity symptoms such as itching or swelling. It is important to exercise extreme caution because the specific toxicological profile for this species is not fully documented in clinical studies.
