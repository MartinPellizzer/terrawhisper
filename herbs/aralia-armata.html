Aralia armata

What's the taxonomical classification of Aralia armata?

Aralia armata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical structure of its order, Apiales, it is placed within the family Araliaceae. Ultimately, the plant is defined by its genus, Aralia, and its specific species designation, armaata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aralia armaata has woody stems that branch into large, compound leaves characterized by leaflets with deeply serrated, sharply toothed margins. The foliage typically features three to five leaflets arranged in a palmate pattern on long petioles. These leaves exhibit a dark green color and a somewhat leathery texture that helps the plant withstand its environment. The serrated edges of the leaflets give the plant a distinctive, jagged appearance. Small, inconspicuous flowers eventually emerge from the leaf axils to support reproduction.
