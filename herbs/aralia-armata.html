Aralia armata

What's the taxonomical classification of Aralia armata?

Aralia armata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical structure of its order, Apiales, it is placed within the family Araliaceae. Ultimately, the plant is defined by its genus, Aralia, and its specific species designation, armaata.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Aralia armaata can cause skin irritation, redness, and localized dermatitis upon direct contact with its foliage or stems. The plant contains certain chemical compounds that may trigger allergic reactions in sensitive individuals. If the sap touches the skin, it often results in itching or a burning sensation that persists for several hours. While systemic toxicity is not widely documented, ingestion should be avoided to prevent potential gastrointestinal discomfort. Always wear protective gloves when handling this species to minimize the risk of contact-based dermatological issues.
