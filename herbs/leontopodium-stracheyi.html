What's the taxonomical classification of Leontopodium stracheyi?

Leontopodium stracheyi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic lineage through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Leontopodium stracheyi has a woody, perennial habit characterized by upright stems and lanceolate to elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in an opposite or sub-opposite pattern.

The leaf blades possess entire margins and a somewhat leathery texture, often displaying a distinct midrib. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves, usually presenting in compact clusters or solitary arrangements. The reproductive structures consist of a superior ovary that develops into a small, dry fruit upon maturation.
