What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Leontopodium stracheyi has a woody, perennial habit characterized by upright stems and lanceolate to elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in an opposite or sub-opposite pattern. The leaf blades possess entire margins and a somewhat leathery texture, often displaying a distinct midrib. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves, usually presenting in compact clusters or solitary arrangements. The reproductive structures consist of a superior ovary that develops into a small, dry fruit upon maturation.
