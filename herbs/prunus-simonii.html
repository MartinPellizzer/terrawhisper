What's the taxonomical classification of Prunus simonii?

Prunus simonii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical classification, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further falls under the order Rosales and is a member of the family Rosaceae. Ultimately, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Pruunus and the species name simonii.

      What side effects this plant can have?

      This plant can cause severe poisoning if the seeds or pits are ingested due to the presence of cyanogenic glycosides that release hydrogen cyanide in the body. Consumption of these toxic compounds can lead to immediate symptoms such as headache, dizziness, and rapid heart rate. If a person ingests a large amount, they may experience more critical issues like vomiting, convulsions, and difficulty breathing. In extreme cases, the cyanide toxicity can cause loss of consciousness or even death. It is essential to avoid consuming any part of the seed to prevent these dangerous physiological reactions.
