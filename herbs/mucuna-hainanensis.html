What's the taxonomical classification of Mucuna hainanensis?

Mucuna hainanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Fabales and the family Fabaceae, which is widely known for its nitrogen-fixing legumes. Finally, its specific taxonomic placement is defined by the genus Mucuna and the species epithet hainanensis.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Additionally, its bioactive compounds may influence monoamine oxidase enzymes and neuroprotective signaling pathways to enhance synaptic plasticity. These complex molecular interactions contribute to the plant's potential efficacy in treating Parkinson's disease and other neurodegenerative conditions.
