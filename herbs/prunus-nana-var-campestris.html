Prunus nana var. campestris

Prunus nana var. campestris, also known as the Dwarf Cherry or Alpine Cherry, is a small shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, particularly in treating digestive issues and as a natural anti-inflammatory agent, due to its content of salicylic acid, a compound similar to aspirin.

The health benefits of Prunus nana var. campestris include its potential to aid in reducing fever, relieving pain, and supporting the immune system.

In herbal preparations, the plant is often dried and used as a tea, or the leaves and bark are used to make infusions and tinctures.
