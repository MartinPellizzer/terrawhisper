Portulaca pilosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Portulaca pilosa?

Portulaca pilosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized as Equisetopsida, falling under the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Caryophyllales and the family Portulacaceae. Finally, the plant is placed within the genus Portulaca, specifically identified by the species epithet pilosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Portulaca pilosa has succulent, cylindrical to subulate leaves that are typically fleshy, bright green, and measure between 5 and 20 millimeters in length. These leaves arise from stems that exhibit varying degrees of hairiness, ranging from glabrous to densely pubescent. The plant produces small, actinomorphic flowers that are usually yellow or white, featuring five distinct petals. Its root system is characterized by a central taproot that supports a low-growing, prostrate, or ascending growth habit. Small, capsule-like fruits develop after flowering to contain numerous tiny, dark seeds.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires full sunlight and well-draining, sandy soil to thrive in warm climates.

It is a hardy succulent that can tolerate periods of drought once its root system is established. Planting should occur in the spring when the soil has warmed sufficiently to support growth. Because it is a prolific self-seeder, it can easily spread throughout a garden if left unmanaged.

Regular weeding is necessary to prevent competition for nutrients during the early stages of development.

What are the pharmacological activities of Portulaca pilosa?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological activities including analgesia, antipyresis, and diuresis through various bioactive compounds. Its analgesic properties allow it to effectively reduce pain sensations in biological models. The antipyretic action of the plant helps in lowering elevated body temperatures associated with inflammation or fever. Additionally, its diuretic effects promote the increased excretion of urine to assist in fluid regulation. These diverse medicinal qualities suggest that Portulaca pilosa holds substantial potential for therapeutic applications.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", Portulaca pilosa has been used in Brazil as a traditional remedy to cause diuresis, antipyresis and analgesia. The results support the claim that extracts of P. pilosa present renal effects but not the popular belief that it affects diuresis. In rats, the plant causes an increase in K excretion. This occurs without a concomitant change in water diuresis or Na excretion.
