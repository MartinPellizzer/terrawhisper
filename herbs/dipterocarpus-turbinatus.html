Dipterocarpus turbinatus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dipterocarpus turbinatus has a woody structure characterized by a small tree or shrub habit, pinnate leaves with multiple pairs of leaflets, and distinctively turbinate or top-shaped fruit. The leaves are typically arranged alternately along the stems and possess an entire or slightly serrated margin. Its floral morphology includes small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into the namesake characteristic fruit shape. The bark is generally smooth or slightly fissured depending on the age of the specimen. This plant's specific morphological traits are essential for its identification within its native habitat.
