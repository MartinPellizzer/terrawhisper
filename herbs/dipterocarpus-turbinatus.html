Dipterocarpus turbinatus

What's the taxonomical classification of Dipterocarpus turbinatus?

Dipterocarpus turbinatus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Malvales. This plant is further categorized into the family Dipterocarpaceae and is situated within the genus Dipterocarpus, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species turbinatus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dipterocarpus turbinatus has a woody structure characterized by a small tree or shrub habit, pinnate leaves with multiple pairs of leaflets, and distinctively turbinate or top-shaped fruit.

The leaves are typically arranged alternately along the stems and possess an entire or slightly serrated margin. Its floral morphology includes small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into the namesake characteristic fruit shape. The bark is generally smooth or slightly fissured depending on the age of the specimen.

This plant's specific morphological traits are essential for its identification within its native habitat.
