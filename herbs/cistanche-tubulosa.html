Cistanche tubulosa

Cistanche (Cistanche tubulosa) is a parasitic plant native to arid regions of China, known for its use in traditional medicine to improve energy levels and support kidney function.

Taxonomy

Cistanche tubulosa is a species of parasitic plant in the family Orobanchaceae, belonging to the genus Cistanche within the order Orobanchales, class Lamiales, division Magnoliophyta, and kingdom Plantae.

Morphology

Cistanche tubulosa is a parasitic plant characterized by its reddish, branched stems that lack true leaves, with small, scale-like structures covering its surface and a fleshy, cylindrical body that typically grows to a length of 10-30 centimeters.

Distribution

Cistanche tubulosa is native to arid and semi-arid regions of China, primarily found in the provinces of Gansu, Qinghai, Sichuan, and Xinjiang, with additional populations in neighboring regions of Mongolia and Russia.

Traditional Uses

Cistanche tubulosa has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine to treat conditions such as impotence, infertility, and chronic fatigue, primarily due to its purported ability to enhance vitality and stimulate the nervous system.

Medicinal Systems

Cistanche tubulosa has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine, Tibetan medicine, and Ayurveda for its purported rejuvenating and tonic properties.

Phytochemistry

The phytochemistry of Cistanche tubulosa is characterized by the presence of various bioactive compounds, including phenethylamines, alkaloids, and polysaccharides, which contribute to its traditional use in herbal medicine.

Pharmacology

Cistanche tubulosa exhibits pharmacological properties characterized by its ability to enhance vitality, improve sexual function, and support cardiovascular health through its traditional use in herbal medicine.

Botanical Identification

Cistanche tubulosa, also known as the desert candle, is a parasitic plant belonging to the Orobanchaceae family. It is native to arid regions of China and grows as a perennial. The plant lacks leaves and has a fleshy, yellowish stem with reddish flowers, and its root is the primary part used in traditional medicine. It has also been referred to as "horned herb" in some regional contexts.

Safety Profile

Cistanche tubulosa is generally considered safe when used as directed. However, it may cause mild digestive discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to limited safety data. Always consult a healthcare provider before use, especially for those with chronic health conditions.
