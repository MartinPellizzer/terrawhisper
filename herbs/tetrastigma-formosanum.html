Tetrastigma formosanum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tetrastigma formosanum has woody vines characterized by thick, fleshy stems that often possess a distinctively swollen base. The leaves are typically large and cordate with prominent venation and a smooth margin. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in clusters, often appearing in the axils of the foliage. The plant develops specialized adventitious roots that assist in climbing and nutrient absorption from host trees. Its overall structure is adapted for a climbing lifestyle within dense forest environments.
