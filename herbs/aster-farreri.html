Aster farreri

What's the taxonomical classification of Aster farreri?

Aster farreri belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further organized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae and is situated within the genus Aster, ultimately reaching its specific classification as the species farreri.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aster farreri has upright, herbaceous stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters and bear oppositely arranged, lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is characterized by a slightly hairy texture and a vibrant green color that provides a dense backdrop for the floral displays. Its inflorescences consist of numerous small, composite flower heads featuring pale lavender to light blue ray florets surrounding yellow disc florets. These flower heads are often clustered at the terminal ends of the branches or nestled within the upper leaf axils. The plant's overall structure is compact yet bushy, making it a resilient perennial in various meadow environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

The distribution is restricted to these specific temperate habitats within the Hengduan Mountains range. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains oleanane-type pentacyclic triterpenoids, flavonoids, and various phenolic compounds that provide significant therapeutic potential. These specific triterpenoids are known for their potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant activities within biological systems. Researchers have identified these chemical constituents as key drivers behind the plant's ability to mitigate cellular oxidative stress. Such diverse chemical structures suggest that Aster farreri could serve as a valuable source for developing new medicinal agents.

According to a study published by "Phytochemical analysis : PCA", the metabolite profile of Aster farreri differs from other taxa in the genus. While ent-kaurane-type diterpenoid glycosides are the main class of compounds in all other Aster species, Aster farreri mainly contains oleanane-type pentacyclic triterpenoids. All Aster species, including Aster farreri, contain six phenolic acids and flavonoids. The study analyzed 95 representative samples from 11 Aster species to determine these chemical markers. These findings were derived from the analysis of above-ground parts using ultra-high performance liquid chromatography and mass spectrometry.
