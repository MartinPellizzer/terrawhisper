Aster farreri

What's the taxonomical classification of Aster farreri?

Aster farreri belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further organized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae and is situated within the genus Aster, ultimately reaching its specific classification as the species farreri.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aster farreri has upright, herbaceous stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters and bear oppositely arranged, lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is characterized by a slightly hairy texture and a vibrant green color that provides a dense backdrop for the floral displays. Its inflorescences consist of numerous small, composite flower heads featuring pale lavender to light blue ray florets surrounding yellow disc florets. These flower heads are often clustered at the terminal ends of the branches or nestled within the upper leaf axils. The plant's overall structure is compact yet bushy, making it a resilient perennial in various meadow environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China, specifically within the provinces of Yunnan and Sichuan. It is typically found growing at high elevations where rocky terrain and alpine meadows are common. The distribution is restricted to these specific temperate habitats within the Hengduan Mountains range. Localized populations are often scattered across various slopes depending on soil composition and sunlight exposure. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse array of bioactive substances, most notably oleanane-type pentacyclic triterpenoids, flavonoids, and various polyphenolic compounds. These specific triterpenoids are highly valued for their potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Researchers have also identified unique alkaloids within the plant that contribute to its overall medicinal profile. Furthermore, the presence of certain glycosides assists in modulating cellular responses to oxidative stress. Together, these chemical constituents provide the biological basis for the plant's therapeutic applications in modern medicine.

As discussed in a study, the genus Aster has been used for thousands of years for the clearing of heat, detoxification, and the treatment of seasonal pandemic diseases. While six phenolic acids and flavonoids were detected and quantified in all Aster species, the medicinal profile of Aster farreri differs from other taxa. Instead of containing ent-kaurane-type diterpenoid glycosides as the main class, Aster farreri mainly contained oleanane-type pentacyclic triterpenoids.
