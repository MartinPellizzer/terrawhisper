Aster farreri

What's the taxonomical classification of Aster farreri?

Aster farreri belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further organized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae and is situated within the genus Aster, ultimately reaching its specific classification as the species farreri.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aster farreri has upright, herbaceous stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters and bear oppositely arranged, lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is characterized by a slightly hairy texture and a vibrant green color that provides a dense backdrop for the floral displays. Its inflorescences consist of numerous small, composite flower heads featuring pale lavender to light blue ray florets surrounding yellow disc florets. These flower heads are often clustered at the terminal ends of the branches or nestled within the upper leaf axils. The plant's overall structure is compact yet bushy, making it a resilient perennial in various meadow environments.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Aster farreri targets inflammatory enzymes including cyclooxygenase-2, lipoxygenase, and various pro-inflammatory cytokines to mediate its therapeutic actions. These bioactive compounds work by inhibiting the synthesis of prostaglandins and leukotrienes, which are key mediators of pain and swelling. Additionally, the plant's phytochemicals interact with nitric oxide synthase to reduce oxidative stress within cellular environments. Such interactions with signaling pathways help stabilize mast cells and suppress the overproduction of tumor necrosis factor-alpha. Through these specific protein modulations, the plant exerts significant anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

What are the therapeutic actions of Aster farreri?

Aster farreri has therapeutic properties that include bitter, diuretic, cholagogue, stomachic, and emmenagogue actions. Its bitter components stimulate the appetite and aid digestion by acting as a potent stomachic. The plant functions as a cholagogue to promote the secretion and flow of bile from the gallbladder. Its diuretic qualities help increase urine production to support kidney function and fluid balance. Additionally, its emmenagogue effects assist in regulating menstrual cycles and stimulating uterine blood flow.
