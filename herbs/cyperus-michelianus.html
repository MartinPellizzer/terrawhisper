Cyperus michelianus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cyperus michelianus has a perennial growth habit characterized by triangular stems, narrow linear leaves, and dense terminal inflorescences composed of small spikelets. The plant typically features a creeping rhizomatous root system that allows it to form dense colonies in moist environments. Its leaves are typically bright green, elongated, and emerge from the base of the plant in a basal arrangement. The flowering structure consists of several compact clusters of brownish or yellowish spikelets arranged on branched peduncles. These spikelets contain numerous tiny flowers protected by specialized scales that facilitate wind pollination.
