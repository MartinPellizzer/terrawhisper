What's the taxonomical classification of Dendrobium hercoglossum?

Dendrobium hercoglossum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Asparagales. This plant is a member of the family Orchidaceae, which places it within the genus Dendrobium, and it is specifically identified by the species name hercoglossum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dendrobium hercoglossum has elongated, cane-like pseudobulbs that can reach lengths of up to 1 meter and are characterized by prominent, dark purple or reddish-brown nectar spurs located at the base of each flower. These fleshy stems are covered in several layers of ovate to lanceolate leaves that emerge from the upper portions of the canes. The inflorescence consists of numerous small, fragrant flowers that typically display a white or pale cream coloration with distinct purple markings on the labellum. A unique morphological feature is the presence of these nectar-secreting glands, which serve to attract specific pollinators. The overall structure is that of a robust epiphytic orchid adapted to climbing along tree branches in its natural habitat.
