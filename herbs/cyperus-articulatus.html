Cyperus articulatus

What's the taxonomical classification of Cyperus articulatus?

Cyperus articulatus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its descent through the order Poales, the plant is placed in the family Cyperaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Cyperus, with its specific designation being the species articulatus.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Cyperus articulatus contains essential oils, flavonoids, tannins, and various terpenoids that serve as its primary therapeutic agents. The volatile oils found within the rhizomes provide significant antimicrobial and antifungal properties. These chemical constituents work together to exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant activities in biological systems. Some studies also suggest that the plant possesses antispasmodic effects due to its specific terpene profile. These bioactive compounds support its traditional use in treating digestive ailments and skin inflammations.

What are the therapeutic actions of Cyperus articulatus?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat gastrointestinal disorders such as diarrhea, dysentery, and stomach aches, as well as various inflammatory conditions and skin infections. The rhizomes contain essential oils and compounds that help soothe the digestive tract and reduce intestinal spasms. It is also frequently applied topically to alleviate swelling and minor wounds. In some traditional practices, it is utilized to manage fever and respiratory discomfort. This medicinal utility stems from its potent antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.
