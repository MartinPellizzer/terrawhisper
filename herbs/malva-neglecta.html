Malva Neglecta Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Malva neglecta, commonly known as Common Mallow, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, valued for its soothing properties.

It is used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory, demulcent, and mild laxative effects.

The herb contains mucilage, flavonoids, tannins, and mucopolysaccharides, which contribute to its therapeutic actions.

You can prepare Malva neglecta as an infusion, decoction, or by making a poultice for topical application.

Medicinal Constituents of malva neglecta

Malva neglecta galactomannans are complex polysaccharides composed primarily of mannose and galactose units, known for their viscous properties and potential prebiotic effects, which may support gut microbiota health and enhance nutrient absorption.

Malva neglecta flavonoids, including quercetin and kaempferol, are bioactive compounds with antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antiviral properties, contributing to the herb's therapeutic potential in treating oxidative stress-related conditions.

Malva neglecta phenolic acids, such as caffeic acid and ferulic acid, exhibit strong antioxidant activity, helping to neutralize free radicals and protect cellular structures from damage.

Malva neglecta tannins, which are polyphenolic compounds, possess astringent properties and may aid in reducing inflammation, improving digestion, and providing antimicrobial benefits due to their ability to bind proteins and inhibit microbial growth.

Herbal Preparations of malva neglecta

Malva neglecta mucillages, derived from the plant's mucilaginous content, are used to create thick, gel-like substances that can be applied topically or ingested to alleviate symptoms of digestive discomfort and respiratory irritation.

Malva neglecta tinctures, made by soaking the herb in alcohol, are valued for their concentrated form and are used in traditional medicine for their purported ability to support skin health and reduce inflammation.
