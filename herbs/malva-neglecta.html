Little mallow

Malva neglecta

Little Mallow (Malva neglecta) is a common flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America, known for its soft, hairy leaves and pink or white flowers, and it has been traditionally used for its soothing and healing properties.

Botanical Identification

Malva neglecta, commonly known as little mallow, belongs to the Malvaceae family. It is an annual herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. The plant has palmately lobed leaves, pinkish flowers, and a fibrous root system. Synonyms include Malva parviflora and Malva pusilla in herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Magnoliopsida
  • Order: Malvales
  • Family: Malvaceae
  • Genus: Malva
  • Species: Malva neglecta

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory conditions such as coughs and bronchitis.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and as a general tonic.

Poultice: Applied externally for skin irritations and wounds.

Safety Profile

Little Mallow (Malva neglecta) is generally considered safe when used in moderate amounts. However, it may cause mild gastrointestinal upset in some individuals. There are no well-documented contraindications, but pregnant or breastfeeding women should consult a healthcare provider before use.
