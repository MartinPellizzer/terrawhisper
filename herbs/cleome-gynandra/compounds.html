Cleome Shrub (Cleome gynandra) Compounds

The primary compounds of Cleome gynandra include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and phenolic acids, known for their anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins and saponins contribute to its astringent and cleansing actions. Coumarin and alkaloids may have antimicrobial and antispasmodic qualities, while glycosides, terpenoids, and essential oils support its medicinal and aromatic uses. Anthocyanins provide color and additional antioxidant benefits.
