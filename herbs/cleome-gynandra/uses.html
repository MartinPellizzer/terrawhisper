Cleome Shrub (Cleome gynandra) Uses

Cleome gynandra is used to treat various skin infections and inflammatory conditions. It aids in wound healing and alleviates itching and irritation from insect bites. The shrub also helps with skin inflammation and respiratory issues like coughs and infections. Its properties make it valuable for addressing a range of health concerns.

Cleome Shrub (Cleome gynandra) Monograph

1. Skin infection

Cleome gynandra is used topically to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial properties. The leaves are crushed and applied directly to the affected area. It helps reduce inflammation and promotes healing. Its effectiveness makes it a valuable remedy in traditional herbal medicine for minor skin infections.

2. Inflammatory skin conditions

Cleome gynandra is used to treat inflammatory skin conditions due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and infection in conditions like eczema and psoriasis. Topical applications of its leaves or extracts are common in traditional herbal medicine for soothing irritated skin.

3. Wound healing

Cleome gynandra is used topically to promote wound healing due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce infection risk and accelerates tissue repair. Crushed leaves can be applied directly to wounds or made into a poultice. Its presence in traditional herbal practices highlights its effectiveness in supporting the healing process.

4. Skin infections

Cleome gynandra is used to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial properties. The plant's leaves contain compounds that inhibit bacterial and fungal growth. It is often applied topically as a poultice or infusion. This makes it effective for conditions like eczema, boils, and fungal infections.

5. Itching

Cleome gynandra is used to alleviate itching due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistaminic properties. The leaves are typically crushed and applied topically to the affected area. It is particularly effective for skin conditions like eczema and mosquito bites. Its soothing effect helps reduce irritation and promote healing.

6. Skin irritation

Cleome gynandra is used to treat skin irritation due to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. It helps reduce redness, itching, and inflammation associated with conditions like eczema and dermatitis. The plant's leaves are often applied topically as a poultice or infused into oils for direct application on affected areas.

7. Insect bites

Cleome gynandra is used topically to alleviate symptoms of insect bites. Its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties help reduce swelling, itching, and pain. A poultice made from crushed leaves can be applied directly to the affected area for quick relief. It is valued in traditional medicine for its soothing effects on skin irritations caused by bites.

8. Skin inflammation

Cleome gynandra is used topically to treat skin inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and irritation associated with conditions like eczema and psoriasis. The leaves are often crushed and applied directly to the affected area for localized relief.

9. Respiratory infections

Cleome gynandra is used traditionally to treat respiratory infections due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps alleviate symptoms like coughing and congestion. The plant's leaves and seeds are often prepared as teas or decoctions. It is valued in herbal medicine for its soothing and healing effects on the respiratory system.

10. Cough

Cleome gynandra is used to alleviate cough symptoms due to its expectorant properties. It helps loosen mucus and reduce throat irritation. Traditionally, it is prepared as a tea or decoction. Its anti-inflammatory effects support respiratory health.
