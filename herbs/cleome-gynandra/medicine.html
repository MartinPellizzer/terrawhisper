What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Cleome Gynandra (Spider Flower)?

Cleome gynandra, also known as spider flower, has health benefits such as treating fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions.

The herb contains flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties. Cleome gynandra can be prepared into infusions, decoctions, or ointments for topical application. However, excessive consumption may lead to gastrointestinal irritation.

To avoid adverse effects, it is essential to consult a healthcare professional before using Cleome gynandra, particularly for pregnant or breastfeeding women.

What are the health benefits of Cleome gynandra?

Cleome gynandra, also known as spider flower, has health benefits such as reducing inflammation and improving digestion.

Its leaves and flowers are rich in antioxidants and flavonoids, which have been shown to have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

In traditional medicine, it is used to treat various ailments, including fever, skin conditions, and respiratory issues, and is also believed to have antioxidant and immune-boosting properties.

What are the active constituents of Cleome gynandra?

Cleome gynandra, also known as spider flower, has active constituents such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids.

Flavonoids, including kaempferol and quercetin, possess antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, while phenolic acids exhibit antimicrobial and free radical-scavenging activities. Alkaloids, particularly the diterpenoid cleomin, have been shown to exhibit cytotoxic and anti-cancer effects.

These medicinal constituents contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating various ailments, including fever, inflammation, and skin conditions.

What are the medicinal preparations of Cleome gynandra?

Cleome gynandra, also known as spider flower, has medicinal preparations such as infusion and decoction that are used to treat various ailments.

The plant's leaves are used to treat fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions. A poultice made from the leaves is applied topically to treat wounds and swellings.

The roots are also used to treat malaria and respiratory problems.

What are the possible side effect of using Cleome gynandra improperly?

Improper use of Cleome gynandra, also known as spider flower, increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as digestive issues, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea.

The plant's seeds and leaves can also cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and eye problems. In severe cases, consuming large quantities can lead to kidney damage and interact with certain medications, affecting heart health and blood pressure.

It is essential to use the plant responsibly and under medical supervision.

What precautions to take when using Cleome gynandra medicinally?

Before using Cleome gynandra, also known as spider flower, for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as ensuring proper identification of the plant to avoid confusion with lookalikes.

Additionally, pregnant women and breastfeeding mothers should exercise caution due to potential hormonal effects. Consult with a healthcare professional before using the plant to treat any health condition, and monitor for allergic reactions.

Dosing and preparation should also be carefully considered to avoid adverse effects.
