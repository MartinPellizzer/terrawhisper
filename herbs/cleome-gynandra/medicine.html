What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Cleome Gynandra (Spider Flower)?

Cleome gynandra has health benefits such as treating respiratory issues and inflammation.

The herb is rich in bioactive compounds like flavonoids, alkaloids, and terpenes, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These constituents can be prepared into various forms, including teas, infusions, and capsules, to treat conditions like bronchitis and asthma.

However, excessive consumption of spider flower may cause stomach upset and interact with certain medications, so it is essential to consume it in moderation and consult a healthcare professional before using it therapeutically.

What are the health benefits of Cleome gynandra?

Cleome gynandra has health benefits such as antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that can help to reduce oxidative stress and alleviate symptoms of chronic diseases like arthritis.

Its leaves have been traditionally used in traditional medicine to treat fever, cough, and skin infections, while its flowers are believed to possess antibacterial and antifungal activities.

What are the active constituents of Cleome gynandra?

The health benefits of Cleome gynandra comes from its active constituents, such as flavonoids, saponins, and glycosides.

These compounds have been found to possess anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antiviral properties, making it a potential remedy for various ailments.

Additionally, the presence of vitamins A, C, and E in spider flower also contributes to its medicinal value, providing antioxidant and immune-boosting effects that may help protect against chronic diseases.

What are the medicinal preparations of Cleome gynandra?

Cleome gynandra has different medicinal preparations, such as infusions, decoctions, and ointments.

Infusions are used to treat fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions like eczema and acne due to its anti-inflammatory properties. Decoctions are employed for treating respiratory issues like bronchitis and coughs, while ointments are applied topically to alleviate wounds, burns, and skin irritations.

Additionally, the plant's juice is used as an eye drops to treat conjunctivitis and other eye infections.

What are the possible side effect of using Cleome gynandra improperly?

Improper use of Cleome gynandra increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as allergic reactions, respiratory distress, and skin irritation.

Consuming excessive amounts may lead to stomach upset, nausea, and diarrhea, while prolonged use can cause liver damage and kidney strain. In severe cases, it may induce anaphylaxis, cardiac arrhythmias, and even neurological disorders like tremors and seizures.

Moreover, spider flower essential oil can cause phototoxicity, leading to skin burns and blisters upon exposure to sunlight.

What precautions to take when using Cleome gynandra medicinally?

Before using Cleome gynandra for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as consulting with a healthcare professional to determine the appropriate dosage and potential interactions with other medications.

Additionally, be cautious when handling the plant as its sap can cause skin irritation, and avoid ingesting it raw or in excessive amounts, as it may have toxic effects.

Proper preparation methods should also be followed to minimize any adverse reactions.
