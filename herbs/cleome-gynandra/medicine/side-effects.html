10 Possible Side Effects Of Cleome Gynandra

Cleome gynandra has some side effects when used improperly, such as allergic reactions, digestive issues, and skin irritation.

These side effects are often caused by excessive consumption, individual sensitivity, or adulteration with other substances.

In severe cases, these reactions can lead to anaphylaxis, long-term digestive problems, and persistent skin conditions, severely impacting a person's daily life and quality of well-being.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Cleome gynandra causes allergic reactions.

This is due to the presence of sesquiterpene lactones, a type of compound found in the plant's leaves and stems, which can trigger an immune response in some individuals.

Additionally, Cleome gynandra contains alkaloids that may cause an allergic reaction, particularly in people who have a history of allergies or asthma.

2. Triggers stomach upset

Cleome gynandra triggers stomach upset due to its high concentration of alkaloids and glycosides.

These compounds can irritate the gastrointestinal tract, causing nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea.

The plant's bitter taste and astringent properties may also contribute to stomach upset, making it uncomfortable for some individuals to consume.

3. Induces liver damage

Cleome gynandra induces liver damage due to its high content of anthraquinone glycosides.

These compounds can cause oxidative stress and inflammation in the liver, leading to damage and potentially serious complications.

Additionally, the plant's flavonoids and saponins may also contribute to hepatic toxicity by disrupting normal liver function and increasing susceptibility to injury.

4. Produces kidney failure

Cleome gynandra produces kidney failure due to its toxic compounds such as saponins and glycosides.

These substances can damage the kidneys by causing inflammation and scarring, leading to a decline in kidney function over time.

Additionally, the consumption of large quantities or prolonged use of Cleome gynandra may increase the risk of developing acute kidney injury.

5. Affects blood pressure

Cleome gynandra affects blood pressure due to its diuretic and vasodilatory properties.

The plant's ability to increase urine production can lead to a decrease in blood volume, causing a drop in blood pressure. Additionally, the plant's flavonoids and terpenes may also relax blood vessels, further contributing to hypotension.

As a result, individuals using Cleome gynandra may experience symptoms such as dizziness, lightheadedness, and fainting.

6. Interferes with medications

Cleome gynandra interferes with medications.

The saponins and alkaloids present in the plant can bind to pharmaceuticals, reducing their efficacy or even increasing their risk of toxicity.

This may lead to decreased blood pressure control, altered sugar levels, or reduced anti-inflammatory effects, potentially compromising treatment outcomes for individuals taking medication for various health conditions.

7. Causes skin rashes

Cleome gynandra causes skin rashes when used.

This is due to its saponin and alkaloid content, which can cause an allergic reaction or irritation on the skin. The sap of the plant may also contain irritants that can trigger contact dermatitis, leading to redness, itching, and blistering.

Furthermore, some individuals may be sensitive to the plant's volatile oils, resulting in a range of skin reactions.

8. Increases heart rate

Cleome gynandra increases heart rate due to its stimulatory effect on the cardiovascular system.

The herb contains alkaloids and glycosides that can stimulate the sympathetic nervous system, leading to an increase in heart rate and cardiac output. This stimulating effect may be more pronounced in individuals with pre-existing cardiovascular conditions or those who are sensitive to the herb's active compounds.

As a result, users of Cleome gynandra may experience palpitations, tachycardia, or other symptoms related to increased heart rate.

9. Reduces fertility rates

Cleome gynandra reduces fertility rates by interfering with the production of sperm and eggs.

The consumption of its seeds or leaves has been linked to reduced reproductive capacity in both males and females. The plant's alkaloids and saponins may alter the hormonal balance in the body, disrupting normal reproductive functions.

As a result, individuals using Cleome gynandra may experience difficulty conceiving or sustaining a pregnancy.

10. Disrupts hormone balance

Cleome gynandra disrupts hormone balance due to its phytoestrogenic properties.

These plant-derived estrogens mimic the effects of human estrogen in the body, potentially leading to an imbalance in hormone levels.

As a result, users may experience changes in their menstrual cycles, mood swings, and other hormonal-related symptoms.
