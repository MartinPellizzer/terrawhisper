Ficus henryi

What's the taxonomical classification of Ficus henryi?

Ficus henryi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Rosales. This plant is a member of the family Moraceae and is further categorized under the genus Ficus, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species henryi.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ficus henryi has ovate to elliptical leaves that are typically 5 to 12 centimeters long, featuring an entire margin and an acute or acuminate apex. The foliage is characterized by a glossy, dark green upper surface and a slightly paler underside with prominent pinnate venation. These leaves are arranged in an alternate pattern along woody, upright stems that exhibit a brownish or grayish bark texture. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced within specialized structures known as syconia, which are characteristic of the fig genus. The overall growth habit produces a dense, shrubby appearance as the plant matures.
