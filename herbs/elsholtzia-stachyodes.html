What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Elsholtzia stachyodes has pinnately compound leaves typically consisting of 7 to 15 leaflets that are ovate to lanceolate in shape and possess serrated margins. The tree's bark is generally gray and develops fine cracks or ridges as the specimen matures. Small, inconspicuous flowers appear in clusters, eventually developing into winged samaras that facilitate seed dispersal.
