What's the taxonomical classification of Elsholtzia stachyodes?

Elsholtzia stachyodes belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Lamiales. As a member of the family Lamiaceae, it is specifically identified by the genus Elsholtzia, with its precise taxonomic designation being the species stachyodes.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Elsholtzia stachyodes has pinnately compound leaves typically consisting of 7 to 15 leaflets that are ovate to lanceolate in shape and possess serrated margins.

These leaflets are arranged along a central rachis and are often characterized by a smooth or slightly hairy texture. The tree's bark is generally gray and develops fine cracks or ridges as the specimen matures. Small, inconspicuous flowers appear in clusters, eventually developing into winged samaras that facilitate seed dispersal.

The overall structure of the plant is defined by a spreading crown and woody branches that support its dense foliage.
