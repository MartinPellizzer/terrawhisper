Callicarpa japonica

What's the taxonomical classification of Callicarpa japonica?

Callicarpa japonica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Lamiales and is a representative of the family Lamiaceae. At the generic level, it is identified as Callicarpa, with the specific epithet japonica designating its unique species identity.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Callicarpa japonica has opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape with serrated margins and a distinctively pubescent underside. The plant typically grows as a deciduous shrub reaching heights of one to two meters. Its stems are woody and can vary from green to brownish tones depending on the age of the growth. Small, tubular flowers emerge in axillary clusters during the summer season. These blossoms eventually give way to clusters of small, spherical, bright purple drupes that persist into the autumn.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the temperate regions of Japan, specifically occurring across various islands such as Honshu, Shikoku, Kyushu, and Hokkaido. It typically thrives in moist, shaded environments like forest understories and mountain slopes. The species prefers well-drained soils within these wooded habitats to support its growth. Its distribution is largely influenced by the humid subtropical and temperate climates found throughout the Japanese archipelago. While it is widespread in its home range, it is not commonly documented as a naturalized species in other parts of the world.

What are the pharmacological activities of Callicarpa japonica?

This plant has significant anti-inflammatory and antioxidative pharmacological activities driven by its diverse phytochemical profile. The presence of specific polyphenols and flavonoids allows the extract to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress in biological systems. These bioactive compounds also inhibit inflammatory mediators, which helps to mitigate swelling and pain in various models. Research suggests that these properties make the plant a potential candidate for developing natural therapeutic agents. Continued studies aim to further clarify the precise molecular mechanisms behind these medicinal effects.

According to a study published by "International immunopharmacology", Callicarpa japonica Thunb. (CJT) reduces inflammatory responses in LPS-stimulated RAW264.7 cells and in an LPS-induced acute lung injury (ALI) model. In C57BL/6 mice, CJT was administered by oral gavage at 30 mg/kg for 3 days. In the RAW264.7 cells, CJT significantly decreased nitric oxide (NO) and interleukin (IL)-6 by reducing iNOS and IL-6 mRNA levels in a concentration-dependent manner. In the ALI model, CJT decreased the inflammatory cell count in the bronchoalveolar lavage fluid (BALF) and reduced IL-6 levels compared with the ALI control mice. CJT also inhibited airway inflammation by reducing iNOS expression in lung tissue. These results suggest that CJT has the potential to treat inflammatory diseases such as pneumonia.
