What's the taxonomical classification of Viola rossii?

Viola rossii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its hierarchical structure continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Malpighiales. As a member of the family Violaceae, it is categorized under the genus Viola, ultimately reaching its specific designation at the species level as rossii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viola rossii has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by creeping rhizomes, ovate to cordate basal leaves with crenate margins, and five-petaled violet flowers featuring a yellowish throat. The leaves are typically arranged in a basal rosette and possess long petioles that connect them to the underground stem. Its flowering structures consist of small, delicate blossoms that emerge from the leaf axils during the spring months. The petals are often marked with subtle nectar guides to assist pollinators. Each plant produces small, inconspicuous capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds for dispersal.
