Mentha × rotundifolia var. suaveolens

Mentha × rotundifolia var. suaveolens, also known as Peppermint, is a hybrid of spearmint (Mentha spicata) and watermint (Mentha aquatica), and is a popular herb used for its medicinal and culinary properties.

The plant's essential oil, which is extracted from its leaves, has anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties, making it a popular remedy for digestive issues, headaches, and nausea.

The health benefits of Mentha × rotundifolia var. suaveolens include relief from irritable bowel syndrome, improved respiratory function, and a reduction in stress and anxiety.

The plant can be used in various herbal preparations, including teas, capsules, and topical creams, as well as in cooking and baking to add flavor and aroma to dishes and desserts.
