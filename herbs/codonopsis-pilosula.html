Codonopsis pilosula

What's the taxonomical classification of Codonopsis pilosula?

Codonopsis pilosula belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. This specific plant is a part of the family Campanulaceae, which defines its broader botanical grouping. Within this family, it is categorized under the genus Codonoopsis, with the specific designation of the species pilosula.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Codonopsis pilosula can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or gastrointestinal distress if ingested or touched improperly. While there is little formal clinical research on human toxicity, many people experience localized redness or itching upon contact with the foliage. Ingesting any part of the plant may lead to stomach aches or nausea due to unidentified phytochemicals. Individuals with sensitive skin should exercise caution to prevent contact dermatitis. Always consult a medical professional if symptoms arise after exposure to this species.
