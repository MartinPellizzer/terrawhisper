What's the taxonomical classification of Potentilla tanacetifolia?

Potentiella tanacetifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Rosales. This plant is a member of the family Rosaceae, falling into the genus Potentiella, and is specifically identified by its species name, tanacetifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Potentiella tanacetifolia has a perennial growth habit characterized by basal leaves that are pinnately divided into several narrow, lanceolate leaflets and erect, branching stems that reach heights of 10 to 50 centimeters. The foliage features finely serrated margins and a slightly hairy texture on both the upper and lower surfaces. Small, bright yellow flowers emerge in terminal clusters, each possessing five distinct petals that surround a central mound of numerous stamens and pistils. These blossoms are typically accompanied by five green sepals that are often more prominent than the petals. The plant's root system consists of a sturdy rhizome that allows it to spread through rocky or alpine habitats.
