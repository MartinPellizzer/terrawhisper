Rubus hastifolius

What's the taxonomical classification of Rubus hastifolius?

Rubus hastifolius belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Rosales, it falls under the family Rosaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Rubus, with the specific designation of the species hastifolius.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rubus hastifolius has compound leaves composed of three to five leaflets characterized by an asymmetrical, hastate, or arrowhead-shaped base. The leaflets possess serrated margins and a texture that can range from smooth to slightly pubescent depending on the specific variety. These leaves are supported by stems that are often armed with sharp prickles or thorns to protect the plant. The foliage typically displays a deep green color on the upper surface with lighter undersides. Small, woody stems facilitate the growth of these distinctive leaf structures throughout the plant.
