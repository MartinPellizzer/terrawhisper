Desmodium sequax

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Desmodium sequax has a sprawling, decumbent growth habit characterized by slender, reddish-brown stems that often root at the nodes. The leaves are trifoliate with elliptic to oblong leaflets that feature fine pubescence on both surfaces. Small, papilionaceous flowers are produced in axillary racemes, typically displaying a pale purple or pinkish hue. The fruit consists of a small, flattened legume that often possesses specialized hairs to facilitate attachment to passing animals. This morphology allows the plant to effectively colonize disturbed ground through vegetative spreading and seed dispersal.

Desmodium sequax has medicinal properties that function as an astringent, a hemostatic agent, and a promoter of wound healing. Desmodium sequax is prepared primarily as a decoction, a concentrated liquid extract made by boiling the plant parts in water to release its medicinal compounds. There is limited clinical research available to definitively categorize the exact toxicity levels for humans. Therefore, caution should be exercised when interacting with this species to avoid unpredictable physiological responses.
