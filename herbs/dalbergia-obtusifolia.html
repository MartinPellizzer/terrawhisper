Dalbergia obtusifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Dalbergia obtusifolia?

Dalbergia obtusifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further organized under the order Fabales. It is a representative of the family Fabaceae, falling specifically under the genus Dalbergia, with its unique identification completed by the species epithet obtusifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dalbergia obtusifolia has pinnately compound leaves with small, elliptical leaflets that feature obtuse or rounded tips and entire margins. The leaflets are typically arranged in an opposite fashion along the rachis and possess a smooth, slightly leathery texture. Its stems are woody and can develop a somewhat rough bark as the plant matures. The inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in axillary panicles. The fruit is a small, indehiscent pod that helps in the dispersal of the species within its habitat.
