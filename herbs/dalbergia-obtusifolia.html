Dalbergia obtusifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Dalbergia obtusifolia?

Dalbergia obtusifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further organized under the order Fabales. It is a representative of the family Fabaceae, falling specifically under the genus Dalbergia, with its unique identification completed by the species epithet obtusifolia.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Dalbergia obtusifolia contains various bioactive phytochemicals including flavonoids, tannins, saponins, and terpenoids that contribute to its therapeutic potential. These specific secondary metabolites provide the plant with significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Research suggests that the extracts from this species may exhibit antimicrobial activities against certain pathogens. Some traditional medicinal practices utilize the bark and leaves to treat skin conditions or digestive ailments. Further biochemical studies continue to investigate the precise molecular mechanisms behind these healing effects.

What are the therapeutic actions of Dalbergia obtusifolia?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin diseases such as eczema and scabies, as well as internal ailments like dysentery and intestinal worms. Its medicinal properties are often derived from the decoction of its bark or the application of its leaves. Practitioners frequently utilize the plant to alleviate inflammation and manage various types of wounds. Some traditional practices also employ its extracts to combat gastrointestinal infections. This botanical resource serves as a vital component in several regional folk medicine systems.
