What is Rokautskyia latifolia?

Rokautskyia latifolia is a species of flowering plant in the family Bromeliaceae.

It is a terrestrial epiphyte with a stout stem, often growing up to 1 meter in height, and has leaves that are elliptical in shape, glabrous, and 18-30 centimeters long. The plant produces a single inflorescence that is paniculate and 30-60 centimeters long, bearing white flowers with 3-4 cm long sepals and 4-5 cm long petals. The plant is endemic to Ecuador, specifically found in the Andean region, where it grows in humid environments at elevations between 1,800 and 3,000 meters above sea level.

Rokautskyia latifolia was first described by the botanist E. G. Gross in 2006.

The main herbal preparations of Rokautskyia latifolia are typically used to support various health benefits.
