Diospyros canaliculata

What's the taxonomical classification of Diospyros canaliculata?

Diospyros canaliculata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Ericales. This plant is a member of the family Ebenaceae, falling under the genus Diospyros, and is specifically identified by the species name canaliculata.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Diospyros canaliculata can cause gastrointestinal distress such as nausea, vomiting, or abdominal cramping if the fruit or parts of the plant are consumed in improper quantities or improperly prepared. While the fruit is often consumed by local populations, excessive intake may lead to unpredictable digestive reactions. Some individuals might also experience allergic skin reactions or localized irritation when coming into direct contact with the plant material. There is currently limited clinical data regarding the long-term toxicological effects of this specific species on human health. Consequently, cautious consumption is advised to avoid potential adverse physiological responses.
