Diospyros canaliculata

What's the taxonomical classification of Diospyros canaliculata?

Diospyros canaliculata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Ericales. This plant is a member of the family Ebenaceae, falling under the genus Diospyros, and is specifically identified by the species name canaliculata.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Diospyros canaliculata contains bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, saponins, tannins, and terpenoids that provide its medicinal properties. These chemical constituents contribute to the plant's documented antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory activities. Researchers have identified specific glycosides within the leaves and bark that may assist in treating various skin ailments. The presence of polyphenolic compounds also suggests potential antioxidant capabilities for protecting cells against oxidative stress. Such diverse chemical profiles justify the traditional use of this species in folk medicine for diverse therapeutic purposes.

What are the therapeutic actions of Diospyros canaliculata?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including diarrhea, stomach pains, skin infections, and wounds. The bark and leaves are often prepared as decoctions to alleviate gastrointestinal distress and digestive issues. In some traditional practices, the plant serves as a remedy for topical skin conditions due to its potential antimicrobial properties. It is also utilized to help manage inflammation and soothe minor external injuries. These medicinal applications are primarily rooted in folk medicine traditions where the plant's extracts are carefully processed.
