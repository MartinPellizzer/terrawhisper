Elsholtzia densa

What's the taxonomical classification of Elsholtzia densa?

Elsholtzia densa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Lamiales. It is further categorized into the family Lamiaceae, which houses the genus Elsholtzia, ultimately defining its specific identity as the species densa.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Elsholtzia densa can cause localized skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or respiratory discomfort if its pollen, sap, or fine hairs come into direct contact with sensitive individuals. While there is limited clinical documentation regarding its toxicity, contact with the plant's organic compounds may trigger redness or itching on the skin. Inhalation of airborne particles or dust from the foliage might lead to sneezing or mild coughing in allergy-prone people. Caution is recommended when handling the bark or leaves to avoid unexpected hypersensitivity reactions.
