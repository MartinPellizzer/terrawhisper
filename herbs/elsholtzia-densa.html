Elsholtzia densa

What's the taxonomical classification of Elsholtzia densa?

Elsholtzia densa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Lamiales. It is further categorized into the family Lamiaceae, which houses the genus Elsholtzia, ultimately defining its specific identity as the species densa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Elsholtzia densa has a medium to large deciduous tree habit characterized by a spreading crown, pinnately compound leaves with 7 to 15 leaflets, and small, inconspicuous greenish flowers. The bark typically develops a fissured texture as the specimen matures. Its leaflets are generally elliptical or ovate in shape with serrated margins and a smooth surface. The fruit consists of small, winged samaras that facilitate wind dispersal. The overall structure provides a dense canopy suitable for shade.

What are the pharmacological activities of Elsholtzia densa?

This plant has antioxidant, antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and wound-healing pharmacological activities. Its extracts contain diverse phytochemicals such as tannins, flavonoids, and saponins that combat oxidative stress in cells. These bioactive compounds work by neutralizing free radicals and preventing cellular damage. Additionally, the plant demonstrates significant potential in inhibiting the growth of various pathogenic microorganisms. Such properties suggest that Elsholtzia densa could serve as a valuable source for developing new therapeutic agents.

According to a study published by "Journal of acupuncture and meridian studies", Elsholtzia densa was assessed for its antioxidant efficacy using several assays. The 50% ethanolic extract recorded a maximum percentage decrease of 90.48% in the DPPH standard solution. In the linoleic acid system, the 50% ethanolic extract showed the highest antioxidant activity at 49.64%. Regarding the FTC assay, the 50% ethanolic extract reached a highest activity of 70.14%, while the methanolic extract showed a 32.02% activity in reducing oxidative damage to DNA. Total phenolic concentrations were measured at 62.5mg% for the absolute ethanolic extract and 77.5mg% for the 50% ethanolic extract.
