Elsholtzia densa

What's the taxonomical classification of Elsholtzia densa?

Elsholtzia densa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Lamiales. It is further categorized into the family Lamiaceae, which houses the genus Elsholtzia, ultimately defining its specific identity as the species densa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Elsholtzia densa has a medium to large deciduous tree habit characterized by a spreading crown, pinnately compound leaves with 7 to 15 leaflets, and small, inconspicuous greenish flowers.

The bark typically develops a fissured texture as the specimen matures. Its leaflets are generally elliptical or ovate in shape with serrated margins and a smooth surface. The fruit consists of small, winged samaras that facilitate wind dispersal.

The overall structure provides a dense canopy suitable for shade.
