Jasminum albicalyx belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Jasminum albicalyx has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with entire margins and a woody, climbing stem structure. Its flowers are salverform, characterized by long, narrow corolla tubes that open into five spreading lobes. These blossoms are primarily white and often emit a delicate fragrance during the blooming period. The plant also produces small, fleshy drupes that transition in color as they mature.
