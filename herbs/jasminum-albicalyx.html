Jasminum albicalyx

What's the taxonomical classification of Jasminum albicalyx?

Jasminum albicalyx belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Lamiales. This plant is a prominent representative of the family Oleaceae and is categorized under the genus Jasminum, with its specific identification as the species albicalyx.

What are the therapeutic actions of Jasminum albicalyx?

Jasminum albicalyx has potent antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties that are utilized to treat skin infections, promote wound healing, and reduce systemic oxidative stress. The plant contains bioactive compounds such as alkaloids and flavonoids that help neutralize free radicals in the body. These phytochemicals also work to suppress inflammatory pathways, making the plant useful in managing swelling and pain. When applied topically, its antiseptic qualities can effectively inhibit the growth of harmful bacteria and fungi. Researchers continue to study these chemical constituents to better understand their potential in developing new pharmacological treatments.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various conditions including skin infections, digestive issues, respiratory ailments, and inflammatory disorders. The leaves and flowers possess antimicrobial properties that help combat fungal and bacterial infections on the skin. When prepared as a decoction, it can soothe digestive disturbances and assist with gastrointestinal discomfort. It is also utilized in traditional medicine to alleviate symptoms of coughs and fever. Additionally, its anti-inflammatory characteristics make it helpful for reducing localized swelling and pain.

What are the herbal preparations of Jasminum albicalyx?

Jasminum albicalyx is prepared as a dried whole plant for decoctions, an infusion of the flowers, or a concentrated tincture for medicinal use. The dried flowers are often steeped in hot water to create a fragrant tea that serves as a traditional remedy. Practitioners may also macerate the plant material in alcohol to extract active compounds for more potent liquid applications. Some preparations involve using the powder of the dried plant to facilitate topical or internal administration. These various forms allow for different strengths and absorption rates depending on the intended therapeutic goal.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Jasminum albicalyx can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and digestive upset if ingested or applied improperly. While many people use the plant for its fragrance, direct contact with the leaves or flowers may trigger redness or itching in sensitive individuals. Some users have reported mild stomach discomfort or nausea when consuming parts of the plant. Because scientific studies on its toxicity are limited, there is a risk of unforeseen systemic reactions. It is important to consult a professional before using any part of this plant for medicinal purposes.
