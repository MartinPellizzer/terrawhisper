What's the taxonomical classification of Hosta ensata?

Hosta ensata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the biological hierarchy, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant falls under the order Asparagales and is a member of the family Asparagaceae. At the most specific taxonomic levels, it is identified by the genus Hosta and the species ensata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hosta ensata has large, elliptical to lanceolate leaves that typically measure 30 to 50 centimeters in length and feature prominent, parallel venation. These foliage structures are often bright green or yellowish and can display slightly wavy or undulate margins. The plant grows from a thick, fleshy rhizome that serves as a storage organ for nutrients. From the center of the leaf cluster, tall, leafless stalks emerge to support clusters of bell-shaped, fragrant white or pale lavender flowers. The overall architecture of the plant forms a dense, spreading mound of lush vegetation.
