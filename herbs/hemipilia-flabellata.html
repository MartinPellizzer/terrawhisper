Hemipilia flabellata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hemipilia flabellata has a chlorophyll-deficient, mycoheterotrophic morphology characterized by fleshy, scale-like leaves arranged in a fan-shaped pattern around a central stem. These leaves are typically brownish or purplish in color and lack the green pigmentation found in photosynthetic plants. The plant produces a slender, erect flowering scape that emerges directly from the underground rhizome. The flowers are small and tubular, often featuring a distinctive shape that is characteristic of the genus. This specialized structure allows the plant to survive by deriving nutrients from fungal networks rather than sunlight.
