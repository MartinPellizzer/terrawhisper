What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Elsholtzia blanda has a deciduous tree structure characterized by pinnately compound leaves with 7 to 15 leaflets and small, inconspicuous greenish flowers. The leaflets are typically ovate or lanceolate with serrated margins and a smooth, somewhat leathery texture. Its bark matures into a grayish color with shallow longitudinal fissures as the tree ages. The fruit consists of small, winged samaras that facilitate wind dispersal from the branches. During the autumn season, the foliage undergoes a distinct color change to shades of yellow or orange before falling.
