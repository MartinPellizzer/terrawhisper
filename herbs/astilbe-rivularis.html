Astilbe rivularis

What's the taxonomical classification of Astilbe rivularis?

Astilbe rivularis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving through its hierarchical structure, the plant falls under the order Saxifrages and is a member of the family Saxifragaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Astilbe and the specific species name rivularis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Astilbe rivularis has compound, pinnately divided leaves that are deeply serrated and taper to acute tips. These dark green foliage structures emerge from a rhizomatous base to form dense, bushy clumps. The plant produces upright, pyramidal panicles of small, densely packed flowers that transition through shades of white and pale pink. Each delicate bloom is supported by slender, branching stems that rise significantly above the leafy canopy. The overall morphology combines coarse, textured greenery with airy, plume-like inflorescences.

What are the pharmacological activities of Astilbe rivularis?

This plant has analgesic, anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, antimicrobial, and antioxidant pharmacological activities. The presence of various bioactive compounds allows the plant to mitigate pain and reduce swelling in biological tissues. It also serves as an effective agent against a wide range of harmful microorganisms through its antibacterial and antimicrobial properties. Furthermore, the plant possesses strong antioxidant capabilities that help neutralize free radicals within the body. These diverse biological effects suggest significant potential for its use in developing new therapeutic treatments.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", Astilbe rivularis is a medicinal plant used for managing peptic ulcer, diarrhoea, jaundice, sprains, muscular swellings, bone fractures, and menstrual disorders. GC-MS analysis identified 30 phytoconstituentents in the plant extract, including heptafluorobutyric acid, hexadecanoic acid, and beta-sitosterol, which depict antioxidant, antidiaabetic, and anticancer properties. Administration of the hydroethanolic extract at doses of 100, 200, and 400 mg/kg attenuated elevated blood glucose levels and restored the lipid profile, body weight, food and water intake, and antioxidant levels. The extract also attenuated elevated levels of inflammatory markers like TGF-β and IL-6 in the sciatic nerve while reducing the pain response from mechanical hyperalgesia and hot and cold allodynia. Furthermore, the extract showed inhibitory activity on the ALR enzyme and erythrocyte sorbitol accumulation and ameliorated oxidative stress.

These diverse secondary metabolites work together to define the biological activity of Astilbe rivularis.

According to a study published by "BMC complementary and alternative medicine", the methanolic extract of the Astilbe rivularis rhizome contained terpenoids, flavonoids, tannins, phenols, alkaloids, saponins, and reducing sugars. Further analysis of the extract using GC-MS showed the presence of 9 major constituents belonging to terpenoids and fatty acid groups. The extract demonstrated cytotoxicity to the neuroblastoma SHSY5Y cell line with an IC50 value < 100 μg ml-1. This plant also showed the least damaging effect on normal cells such as the HEK-293 and WRL-68 cell lines. The study suggests Astilbe rivularis has potential as a source of new potent antibacterial, antioxidant, and anticancer agents.
