Astilbe rivularis

What's the taxonomical classification of Astilbe rivularis?

Astilbe rivularis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving through its hierarchical structure, the plant falls under the order Saxifrages and is a member of the family Saxifragaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Astilbe and the specific species name rivularis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Astilbe rivularis has compound, pinnately divided leaves that are deeply serrated and taper to acute tips. These dark green foliage structures emerge from a rhizomatous base to form dense, bushy clumps. The plant produces upright, pyramidal panicles of small, densely packed flowers that transition through shades of white and pale pink. Each delicate bloom is supported by slender, branching stems that rise significantly above the leafy canopy. The overall morphology combines coarse, textured greenery with airy, plume-like inflorescences.

What are the pharmacological activities of Astilbe rivularis?

This plant has analgesic, anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, antimicrobial, and antioxidant pharmacological activities. Its chemical constituents work to reduce pain perception and suppress inflammatory responses within biological systems. The presence of specific bioactive compounds allows it to inhibit the growth of various bacteria and microorganisms. Furthermore, the plant provides significant antioxidant properties that help neutralize harmful free radicals. These diverse medicinal effects make it a subject of interest for natural drug development.

As discussed in a study, Astilbe rivularis is traditionally used to manage disorders such as peptic ulcer, diarrhoea, jaundice, sprains, muscular swellings, bone fracture, joint dislocation, postpartum bleeding, and other menstrual disorders. Through GC-MS analysis, 30 phytoconstituents were identified in the extract, including heptafluorobutyric acid, hexadecanoic acid, and beta-sitosterol, which depict antioxidant, antidiaabetic, and anticancer properties. When administering different doses of 100, 200, and 400 mg/kg of hydroethanolic extract of Astilbe rivularis to diabetic rats, the extract attenuated elevated blood glucose levels and restored the lipid profile, body weight, food and water intake, and antioxidant levels. The extract also attenuated elevated levels of inflammatory markers TGF-β and IL-6 in the sciatic nerve and reduced pain responses related to mechanical hyperalgesia and hot and cold allodynia. Additionally, the extract showed inhibitory activity on the ALR enzyme and erythrocyte sorbitol accumulation while ameliorating oxidative stress.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains flavonoids, saponins, tannins, terpenoids, and triterpenes which serve as its primary medicinal compounds. These specific phytochemicals contribute to various pharmacological activities such as antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. The presence of saponins and tannins often aids in antimicrobial defense and tissue protection. Additionally, the terpenoids and triterpenes play significant roles in regulating cellular responses and supporting immune functions. Together, these diverse chemical constituents form a complex therapeutic profile.

As discussed in a study, the methanolic extract of the Astilbe rivularis rhizome contains terpenoids, flavonoids, tannins, phenols, alkaloids, saponins, and reducing sugars. Further analysis using GC-MS identified nine major constituents belonging to the terpenoid and fatty acid groups. The extract demonstrated marked in vitro ROS scavenging activity and moderate antibacterial activity against both gram positive and gram negative bacteria. Regarding cytotoxicity, the extract showed an IC50 value < 100 μg ml-1 against the neuroblastoma SHSY5Y cell line. It had the least damaging effect on normal cell lines such as the human embryonic kidney HEK-293 and liver WRL-68 lines.
