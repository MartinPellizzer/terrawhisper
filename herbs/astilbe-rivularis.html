What's the taxonomical classification of Astilbe rivularis?

Astilbe rivularis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving through its hierarchical structure, the plant falls under the order Saxifrages and is a member of the family Saxifragaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Astilbe and the specific species name rivularis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Astilbe rivularis has compound, pinnately divided leaves that are deeply serrated and taper to acute tips.

These dark green foliage structures emerge from a rhizomatous base to form dense, bushy clumps. The plant produces upright, pyramidal panicles of small, densely packed flowers that transition through shades of white and pale pink. Each delicate bloom is supported by slender, branching stems that rise significantly above the leafy canopy.

The overall morphology combines coarse, textured greenery with airy, plume-like inflorescences.
