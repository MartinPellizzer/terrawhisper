Alpinia brevis

What's the taxonomical classification of Alpinia brevis?

Alpinia brevis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Zingiberales. This plant is a member of the family Zingiberaceae, which places it within the genus Alpinia, ultimately identifying it as the specific species Alpinia brevis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Alpinia brevis has short, thick rhizomes and erect, leafy stems that typically reach a modest height compared to other members of its genus. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often displaying delicate white or pale pink petals. The inflorescence is compact, reflecting the species' specific name which denotes its reduced or brief stature.
