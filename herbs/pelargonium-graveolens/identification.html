Botanical Identification of Geranium (Pelargonium graveolens)

To identify Geranium (Pelargonium graveolens) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Geranium (Pelargonium graveolens) Monograph

Scientific Classification

Geranium, or Pelargonium graveolens, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Spermatophyta, class Caryophyllopsida, order Geraniales, family Geraniaceae, genus Pelargonium, and species Pelargonium graveolens. This classification places it within the flowering plants, specifically in the geranium family, known for its aromatic properties and use in herbal medicine.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Pelargonium graveolens is the scientific name for the common geranium. It was first described by Carl von Linné in 1788. Synonyms include Geranium pelargonium and Geranium zonale. This species is widely known by its common name, geranium, and is also referred to as the scented geranium in some regions.

Morphological Characteristics

Geranium (Pelargonium graveolens) is an upright herb with a fibrous root system. Its stems are hairy and branching, often reddish in color. Leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant produces umbel-like inflorescences. Flowers are pink to red, five-petaled, and have a distinctive scent. The fruit is a five-lobed capsule that releases small, smooth seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Geranium (Pelargonium graveolens) can be distinguished by its rounded leaves with scalloped edges, red stems, and showy pink to red flowers. It differs from similar species like Geranium macrorrhizum by its more compact growth habit and distinct scent. The leaves are typically hairy and have a strong, peppery aroma when crushed.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Geranium, or Pelargonium graveolens, is native to South Africa. It thrives in Mediterranean climates with mild, wet winters and warm, dry summers. It prefers well-drained soils and is typically found at low to mid-altitude ranges.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Geranium (Pelargonium graveolens) typically blooms from late spring to early summer, producing vibrant red or pink flowers. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early autumn. For identification purposes, the plant is most identifiable during its flowering stage, which is optimal for accurate botanical recognition.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Pelargonium graveolens belongs to the Geraniaceae family, closely related to other geranium species. It shares characteristics with both Geranium and Pelargonium genera, showing similarities in leaf structure and flower morphology. Its phylogenetic placement highlights evolutionary links within the family, aiding in accurate botanical classification.
