Geranium (Pelargonium graveolens) Compounds

The primary compounds of Geranium include essential oils, which contribute to its aromatic properties, and flavonoids, known for their antioxidant effects. Terpenoids and coumarin also play roles in its medicinal uses, while phenolic acids and tannins provide antimicrobial benefits. Glycosides, saponins, alkaloids, and anthocyanins further support its therapeutic potential.

Geranium (Pelargonium graveolens) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Geranium (Pelargonium graveolens) is a key active compound, containing over 100 volatile compounds. It is widely used in aromatherapy and herbal medicine for its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and mood-enhancing properties. The oil is extracted through steam distillation and is valued for its therapeutic applications.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Geranium (Pelargonium graveolens). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its use in traditional herbal medicine for ailments like respiratory infections and skin conditions.

3. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Geranium (Pelargonium graveolens). They contribute to the plant's essential oils and are responsible for its aromatic properties. These compounds have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects, making them valuable in herbal medicine.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a compound found in Geranium (Pelargonium graveolens). It contributes to the plant's characteristic scent and has potential anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarin is often used in herbal medicine for its mild therapeutic effects.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Geranium (Pelargonium graveolens). It contributes to the plant's antimicrobial and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and skin-related ailments.

6. Tannin

Tannin in Geranium (Pelargonium graveolens) contributes to its astringent properties. It helps in reducing inflammation and can aid in digestive issues. Tannins are also known for their antioxidant effects, supporting the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Geranium (Pelargonium graveolens). It contributes to the plant's therapeutic properties, often used in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Glycosides in Geranium are part of the plant's complex chemical profile that supports its traditional use in various health applications.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Geranium (Pelargonium graveolens). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponin helps support the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for skin conditions and respiratory health. Its presence enhances the overall therapeutic profile of Pelargonium graveolens.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Geranium (Pelargonium graveolens). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, though it is less prominent than other active constituents like essential oils. Alkaloids may support its use in traditional herbal medicine for their potential therapeutic effects.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Geranium (Pelargonium graveolens). It contributes to the plant's vibrant color and has antioxidant properties. This compound supports the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
