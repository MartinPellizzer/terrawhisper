10 Medicinal Constituents Of Pelargonium Graveolens (Geranium)

Pelargonium graveolens has active constituents such as linalool, linalyl acetate, and geraniol, which exhibit antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties.

These properties contribute to its medicinal value, particularly in wound healing and skin care.

The constituents in geranium oil can improve people's lives by reducing inflammation and promoting tissue regeneration, which can aid in faster recovery from injuries and surgical procedures.

1. Geraniol

Pelargonium graveolens geraniol is a natural essential oil extracted from the leaves of the Pelargonium graveolens plant.

This sweet-scented oil is highly prized for its floral and fruity aroma, reminiscent of rose and lemon. Geraniol has been used in traditional medicine and aromatherapy to promote relaxation, reduce stress, and ease anxiety.

It also exhibits antioxidant and antimicrobial properties, making it a popular ingredient in skincare products and perfumes.

2. Linalool

Pelargonium graveolens linalool is a naturally occurring essential oil that is extracted from the leaves of this perennial plant.

This sweet-scented oil has been used in aromatherapy for its calming and relaxing properties, helping to reduce anxiety and stress.

Its subtle fragrance is often used in skincare products due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, making it beneficial for soothing skin irritations and promoting overall well-being.

3. Citronellal

Pelargonium graveolens citronellal is a natural compound derived from the leaves of this plant.

It is a key component responsible for its distinctive fragrance and flavor.

Citronellal is a cyclic aldehyde with a sweet and woody aroma, often used in perfumery and aromatherapy due to its calming and uplifting properties.

4. Germacra-1,10(14)-diene

Pelargonium graveolens germacra-1,10(14)-diene is a naturally occurring sesquiterpene compound found in the essential oil of the plant.

It has been identified for its distinctive fragrance and is often used as a flavoring agent in foods and beverages.

Additionally, germacra-1,10(14)-diene has been studied for its potential health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

5. Β-caryophyllene oxide

Pelargonium graveolens β-caryophyllene oxide is a naturally occurring sesquiterpene alcohol compound found in the essential oil of Pelargonium graveolens.

This compound has been isolated from various plant species and exhibits numerous biological activities, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties.

It has also shown potential as an analgesic agent and may be used to treat a range of health issues, including pain relief and inflammation.

6. Α-pinene

Pelargonium graveolens α-pinene is a natural compound that can be found in the essential oil of this plant.

It is a monoterpene, which is a type of hydrocarbon, and has been shown to have various biological activities including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties.

α-Pinene is also known for its ability to act as an insect repellent and has been used in traditional medicine for the treatment of various ailments.

7. Limonene

Pelargonium graveolens limonene is a natural essential oil extracted from the leaves and stems of the Pelargonium graveolens plant.

It has a sweet, citrusy scent reminiscent of lemon, hence its name "limonene". This oil is commonly used in aromatherapy to promote relaxation, reduce stress and anxiety, and improve sleep quality.

Its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties also make it effective in treating skin irritations and wounds.

8. Terpinen-4-ol

Pelargonium graveolens terpinen-4-ol is a naturally occurring compound found in the leaves and stems of this plant.

This monoterpene alcohol is responsible for the distinctive fragrance and aroma of geranium essential oil, which is often used in aromatherapy to promote relaxation and reduce anxiety.

Terpinen-4-ol has also been shown to exhibit antimicrobial and antioxidant properties, making it a valuable component in traditional medicine.

9. Germacrene b

Pelargonium graveolens germacrene b is a natural sesquiterpene compound found in the essential oil of the plant.

It is a major component of geranium oil and accounts for its characteristic earthy, woody scent. Germacrene b has been reported to exhibit various biological activities, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties.

It is also used as a flavoring agent in food and beverages, and is often included in perfumes and fragrances for its distinctive aroma.

10. Β-eudesmol

Pelargonium graveolens β-eudesmol is a bioactive sesquiterpene compound found in the leaves and stems of the plant.

This natural compound has been extensively studied for its numerous biological activities, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties.

It has also been reported to exhibit analgesic and sedative effects, making it a potential candidate for the treatment of various diseases and disorders.
