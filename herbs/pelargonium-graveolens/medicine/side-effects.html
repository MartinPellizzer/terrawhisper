10 Possible Side Effects Of Pelargonium Graveolens

Pelargonium graveolens has some side effects when used improperly, such as skin irritation, allergic reactions, and increased sensitivity to the sun.

These side effects can be caused by the herb's essential oils, which are often extracted and used in skincare products. If not used correctly, they can worsen skin conditions like eczema or psoriasis, leading to discomfort, scarring, and even infections that require medical attention.

This can affect daily life, causing pain and anxiety.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Pelargonium graveolens causes allergic reactions due to the presence of terpenoids and flavonoids in its essential oil.

These compounds can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms such as skin irritation, redness, and itching.

Additionally, the fragrance molecules may cause respiratory issues, including congestion, coughing, and sneezing, in sensitive individuals.

2. Triggers asthma attacks

Pelargonium graveolens triggers asthma attacks due to its volatile oils, which can stimulate bronchial irritation and cause airways to constrict.

This may lead to increased mucus production, coughing, wheezing, and shortness of breath in individuals with pre-existing asthma conditions.

The potent essential oils in Pelargonium graveolens, particularly geraniol and citronellol, can exacerbate respiratory issues by triggering an allergic response or sensitization.

3. Increases blood pressure

Pelargonium graveolens increases blood pressure due to its natural ability to stimulate the body's sympathetic nervous system.

This can lead to a temporary increase in heart rate and blood pressure. Additionally, the essential oil may cause vasoconstriction, which is the constriction of blood vessels, further contributing to elevated blood pressure levels.

These effects are generally mild and reversible, but they should be monitored in individuals with pre-existing hypertension or cardiovascular conditions.

4. Induces nausea and vomiting

Pelargonium graveolens induces nausea and vomiting when used.

This is likely due to its chemical composition, which includes citronellal and geraniol, both known to have emetic properties. These compounds may stimulate the vagus nerve, triggering a reflex that can lead to nausea and vomiting.

Additionally, the essential oil's strong scent and volatile nature may also contribute to these adverse effects by irritating the digestive system and causing discomfort.

5. Disrupts menstrual cycles

Pelargonium graveolens disrupts menstrual cycles.

The essential oil extracted from its leaves and stems contains compounds such as citronellal and geraniol, which are known to have estrogenic properties. These estrogen-mimicking effects can alter the body's natural hormonal balance, leading to irregular or absent menstruation.

Additionally, Pelargonium graveolens has been shown to increase progesterone levels, further contributing to menstrual cycle disruptions.

6. Affects heart rate irregularly

Pelargonium graveolens affects heart rate irregularly due to its ability to interact with cardiovascular system and alter blood pressure.

The essential oils present in the plant can cause vasodilation, leading to increased blood flow and reduced blood pressure, which may result in irregular heart rhythms or palpitations.

Additionally, the oil's stimulatory effect on the nervous system may also contribute to changes in heart rate patterns.

7. Produces excessive sweating

Pelargonium graveolens produces excessive sweating due to its ability to stimulate the sweat glands and increase body temperature.

This is primarily attributed to its high concentration of citronellal, a natural compound that can cause vasodilation and increased blood flow to the skin's surface.

As a result, individuals using Pelargonium graveolens may experience excessive perspiration, particularly in warm environments or during physical activity.

8. Causes headaches frequently

Pelargonium graveolens causes headaches frequently when used as an essential oil.

This is likely due to its high concentration of citronellol and linalool, which can cause irritation to the nervous system and lead to vascular contractions, resulting in headaches.

Additionally, the oil's potent aroma may trigger allergic reactions or sensitivity in some individuals, further contributing to headache development.

9. Weakens immune system

Pelargonium graveolens weakens immune system by suppressing the activity of immune cells, such as macrophages and natural killer cells.

This is due to its ability to inhibit the production of cytokines, which are signaling molecules that stimulate the immune response. Additionally, the essential oil present in Pelargonium graveolens has been shown to decrease the expression of genes involved in immune function, leading to a compromised immune system.

As a result, individuals using Pelargonium graveolens may be more susceptible to infections and diseases.

10. Aggravates skin conditions

Pelargonium graveolens aggravates skin conditions such as acne, eczema, and rosacea due to its potential to stimulate oil production in the skin.

The herb's essential oils can cause increased sebum secretion, leading to clogged pores and inflammation that exacerbate these conditions.

Additionally, the fragrance compounds present in Pelargonium graveolens may also trigger allergic reactions or sensitivities, further worsening existing skin issues.
