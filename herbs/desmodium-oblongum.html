Desmodium oblongum

What's the taxonomical classification of Desmodium oblongum?

Desmodium oblongum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving through its hierarchical structure, the plant is part of the order Fabales and falls within the family Fabaceae. Specifically, it is identified by the genus Desmodium, and its unique biological classification is completed by its species name, oblongum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Desmodium oblongum has trifoliate leaves with oblong to elliptical leaflets, woody stems, and small, pea-like pinkish or purple flowers arranged in racemes. The leaflets are typically smooth or slightly pubescent and possess long petioles that connect them to the main stem. Its stems are often sprawling or climbing, helping the plant navigate through surrounding vegetation. The inflorescence consists of dense clusters of flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. Each flower is characteristic of the Fabaceae family, featuring a distinct keel and standard petal structure. The plant also produces small, flattened legumes that contain several seeds.
