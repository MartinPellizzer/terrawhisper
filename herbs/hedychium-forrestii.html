Hedychium forrestii

What's the taxonomical classification of Hedychium forrestii?

Hedychium forrestii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is positioned in the order Zingiberales. This plant is a member of the family Zingiberaceae, falling under the genus Hedychium, and is specifically identified by its species name, forrestii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hedychium forrestii has erect, rhizomatous stems that reach heights of up to 1.5 meters and bear lanceolate, bright green leaves arranged in an alternate pattern.

The inflorescence is a terminal panicle containing fragrant, white flowers with distinct yellow markings on the labellum. These blossoms feature long, slender petals and prominent stamens that emerge from the floral tube. The plant's root system consists of thick, fleshy rhizomes that facilitate underground spreading.

Each leaf sheath tightly wraps around the stem to provide structural support as the plant grows.
