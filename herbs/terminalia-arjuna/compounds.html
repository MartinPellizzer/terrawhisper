Arjuna (Terminalia arjuna) Compounds

Arjuna contains several primary compounds, including saponins, which have cardiovascular benefits, and flavonoids, known for their antioxidant properties. Glycosides and tannins contribute to its anti-inflammatory and astringent effects. Phenolic acids and coumarin support detoxification and blood circulation, while terpenoids and alkaloids provide additional therapeutic actions. Essential oils and anthocyanins enhance its overall medicinal profile.

Arjuna (Terminalia arjuna) Monograph

1. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Arjuna (Terminalia arjuna). It contributes to the plant's cardiovascular benefits by improving heart function and reducing cholesterol. Saponins also exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting overall cardiovascular health.

Flavonoids are key active compounds in Arjuna (Terminalia arjuna), contributing to its cardiovascular and anti-inflammatory benefits. These compounds help reduce oxidative stress and improve heart function, making Arjuna a valuable herb in traditional and modern herbal medicine.
