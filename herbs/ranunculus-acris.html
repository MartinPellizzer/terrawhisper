Ranunculus Acris

Ranunculus acris, commonly known as Meadow Buttercup, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its health benefits, including treating fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The therapeutic actions of Ranunculus acris include reducing inflammation, relieving pain, and promoting wound healing, attributed to its bioactive constituents such as alkaloids, glycosides, and saponins.

Possible Side Effects of ranunculus acris

Ranunculus acris can have side effects if used improperly, such as gastrointestinal problems, allergic reactions, and skin irritation.

Ingesting the herb can cause nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea due to its toxic compounds.

External application of the herb may lead to skin rashes, redness, and even blisters in some individuals.

Furthermore, improper use of ranunculus acris can also trigger more severe reactions, including anaphylaxis in rare cases.
