What is Pimpinella pastinacifolia?

Pimpinella pastinacifolia is a plant species in the family Apiaceae, native to the Himalayan region.

It is an annual or biennial herb with a taproot and a stem up to 50 cm tall, bearing fern-like leaves divided into 2-3 pairs of leaflets. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers in umbels, which are arranged in a compound umbel, and is pollinated by bees and other insects. The flowers are followed by small, ribbed fruits, each containing 2-3 seeds.

The plant's growth habit and morphology are similar to those of Pastinaca sativa, but it is distinguished by its smaller size and more delicate leaves.
