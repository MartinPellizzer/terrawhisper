Nard Grass (Cymbopogon nardus) Compounds

The primary compounds of Nard Grass include essential oil, which contains aromatic terpenoids like citral. Flavonoids and phenolic acids contribute to its antioxidant properties. Tannins and saponins provide astringent and anti-inflammatory effects. Coumarin and glycosides add additional therapeutic benefits, while alkaloids and anthocyanins enhance its medicinal profile.

Nard Grass (Cymbopogon nardus) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Nard Grass (Cymbopogon nardus) is a key active compound, known for its aromatic and therapeutic properties. It contains high levels of citral and other volatile compounds that contribute to its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and calming effects. This oil is widely used in herbal medicine for its ability to support respiratory and digestive health.

2. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Nard Grass (Cymbopogon nardus) contribute to its aromatic properties and therapeutic effects. These compounds, such as citral and geraniol, are responsible for the plant's calming and antiseptic qualities. They play a key role in the traditional use of Nard Grass for respiratory and digestive support.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Nard Grass (Cymbopogon nardus) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help reduce oxidative stress and support immune function. They are naturally present in the plant and are often extracted for their therapeutic benefits in herbal medicine.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid in Nard Grass (Cymbopogon nardus) contributes to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. This compound helps in reducing oxidative stress and supporting immune function. It is one of the key bioactive components responsible for the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

5. Tannin

Tannin in Nard Grass (Cymbopogon nardus) contributes to its astringent properties, helping to reduce inflammation and soothe mucous membranes. It also aids in the preservation of the plant's volatile oils, enhancing its therapeutic effects in traditional herbal medicine.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Nard Grass (Cymbopogon nardus). It contributes to the plant's aromatic properties and has been studied for its potential anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects. Coumarin is often extracted from the essential oil of Nard Grass and used in traditional herbal medicine for digestive and respiratory support.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Nard Grass (Cymbopogon nardus). It contributes to the plant's antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Saponins help in detoxifying the body and supporting respiratory health. They are commonly used in traditional herbal medicine for their therapeutic benefits.

8. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Nard Grass (Cymbopogon nardus). It contributes to the plant's aromatic properties and may support digestive and respiratory health. Glycosides in Nard Grass are often extracted for their therapeutic potential in traditional herbal medicine.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Nard Grass (Cymbopogon nardus). It contributes to the plant's aromatic properties and has been studied for its potential antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. While not the primary active component, alkaloids play a role in the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a pigment found in Nard Grass (Cymbopogon nardus). It contributes to the plant's color and may have antioxidant properties. While not the primary active compound, anthocyanin supports the plant's medicinal profile by enhancing its therapeutic potential.
