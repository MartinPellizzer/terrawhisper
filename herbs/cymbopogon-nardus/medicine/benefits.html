10 Health Benefits Of Cymbopogon Nardus (Citronella)

Cymbopogon nardus, also known as citronella, has health benefits such as reducing inflammation and fighting off bacteria and viruses.

Its essential oil has anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiseptic properties, making it effective against insect bites, wounds, and respiratory issues.

For example, inhaling citronella oil can ease asthma symptoms, while applying it topically can alleviate itching and redness from bug bites, improving overall quality of life.

This article explains in details the 10 best health benefits of Cymbopogon nardus.

1. Reduces inflammation

Cymbopogon nardus reduces inflammation because it contains active compounds such as geraniol and linalool, which possess potent anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds have been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines, which are molecules that promote inflammation, thereby reducing redness, swelling, and pain associated with inflammation.

The anti-inflammatory effects of citronella make it a popular natural remedy for various inflammatory conditions.

2. Fights bacterial infections

Cymbopogon nardus fights bacterial infections because of its potent antimicrobial properties.

The essential oils extracted from citronella grass contain compounds like geranial and neral, which have been shown to exhibit antibacterial and antifungal activities.

These properties enable citronella to effectively inhibit the growth of various pathogens, including E. coli and Staphylococcus aureus, thereby preventing infection and promoting wound healing.

3. Relieves mosquito bites

Cymbopogon nardus relieves mosquito bites because it has natural insect-repelling properties.

The oil extracted from its leaves contains citronellal, a compound that repels mosquitoes and other insects. When applied to the skin, it creates a barrier that prevents mosquitoes from biting, thereby reducing the discomfort and itching associated with mosquito bites.

It is a popular ingredient in insect repellents and lotions.

4. Repels ticks and fleas

Cymbopogon nardus repels ticks and fleas because its essential oils, particularly citronellal and citronellol, have been shown to be effective insect repellents.

These compounds disrupt the ticks' and fleas' ability to detect their hosts, making it difficult for them to land and feed.

This natural repellent property makes citronella a popular ingredient in insect repellent products and a sought-after natural solution for tick and flea control.

5. Soothes skin irritation

Cymbopogon nardus soothes skin irritation because of its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The essential oils present in citronella have been shown to reduce redness and swelling, making it an effective remedy for skin irritations such as bug bites and minor cuts.

Its natural antiseptic qualities also prevent the risk of infection, providing a safe and soothing solution for irritated skin.

6. Inhibits fungal growth

Cymbopogon nardus inhibits fungal growth because it contains citronellal, a natural compound with antimicrobial properties.

Citronellal disrupts the cell membrane of fungi, causing cell death and ultimately inhibiting fungal growth.

Additionally, citronella oil's antioxidant properties help to reduce the formation of fungal spores, further contributing to its antifungal effects, making it a useful natural remedy against fungal infections.

7. Prevents tooth decay

Cymbopogon nardus prevents tooth decay because of its antibacterial properties.

The essential oils in citronella have been shown to inhibit the growth of Streptococcus mutans, a bacteria that contributes to tooth decay. This makes citronella a natural and effective remedy for oral health, reducing the risk of cavities and promoting healthy teeth and gums.

Regular use of citronella oil in toothpaste or mouthwash can provide ongoing protection.

8. Freshens breath

Cymbopogon nardus freshens breath because it contains natural oils that inhibit the growth of bacteria and other microorganisms responsible for causing bad breath.

The oils also help to break down and eliminate volatile sulfur compounds, the primary culprits behind morning breath. As a result, citronella's antimicrobial properties leave the mouth feeling clean and fresh.

It is a natural, chemical-free way to maintain good oral hygiene.

9. Reduces stress

Cymbopogon nardus reduces stress because of its calming and refreshing properties.

The essential oils found in citronella grass, including limonene and citral, have a soothing effect on the mind and body. Inhaling citronella oil can help to lower blood pressure, promote relaxation, and even reduce anxiety.

As a result, citronella has become a popular ingredient in aromatherapy and natural stress-relief remedies, offering a natural and effective way to manage stress and promote overall well-being.

10. Improves sleep quality

Cymbopogon nardus improves sleep quality because of its calming and relaxing properties.

The essential oil derived from the plant contains compounds that have a sedative effect, helping to reduce anxiety and stress. By promoting a peaceful state, citronella oil can help individuals fall asleep faster and enjoy a more restful night's sleep, leading to improved physical and mental well-being.

Its soothing effects also help to regulate sleep patterns.
