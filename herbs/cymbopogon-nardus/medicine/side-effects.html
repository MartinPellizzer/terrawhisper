10 Possible Side Effects Of Cymbopogon Nardus

Cymbopogon nardus has some side effects when used improperly, such as skin irritation, allergic reactions, and respiratory issues.

These side effects can occur when the herb is not properly dried or processed, or when it is ingested in large quantities.

For example, excessive citronella oil consumption can cause headaches, dizziness, and nausea, potentially affecting daily life and work performance.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Cymbopogon nardus causes allergic reactions when it comes into contact with sensitive skin.

The oil contained in the plant's leaves can cause an immune response, leading to symptoms such as redness, itching, and rashes.

This reaction occurs because the body mistakes the oil for a foreign substance and mounts a defense against it, resulting in an allergic response.

2. Triggers skin irritation

Cymbopogon nardus triggers skin irritation in some individuals due to its essential oil composition, which contains compounds like geraniol and limonene.

These natural chemicals can cause skin reactions such as redness, itching, and rashes when the oil comes into contact with human skin.

The potency of these irritants varies depending on individual tolerance and skin type, leading to a range of adverse effects in those who are susceptible.

3. Induces vomiting

Cymbopogon nardus induces vomiting when ingested.

This is due to its oil composition, which includes cineole and geraniol, two compounds that can stimulate the digestive system and cause nausea and vomiting in some individuals.

Additionally, citronella's aromatic properties may trigger a reflexive response in the stomach, leading to an upset stomach and potential vomiting episodes.

4. Lowers blood pressure

Cymbopogon nardus lowers blood pressure due to its ability to relax blood vessels and improve blood flow.

This is caused by the presence of natural compounds such as geraniol and cineole, which have a calming effect on the body's cardiovascular system.

As a result, Cymbopogon nardus can help reduce blood pressure in individuals who experience hypertension.

5. Increases heart rate

Cymbopogon nardus increases heart rate due to its stimulating properties.

The essential oil's main compound, geraniol, can cause an increase in sympathetic nervous system activity, leading to a faster heart beat and increased blood pressure. Additionally, citronella's ability to dilate blood vessels may further contribute to the elevated heart rate.

This response is thought to be caused by the oil's interaction with the body's cardiovascular system, resulting in a heightened physiological state.

6. Stimulates uterine contractions

Cymbopogon nardus stimulates uterine contractions due to its ability to increase prostaglandin production and relax the uterine smooth muscle.

This property can lead to stronger and more frequent contractions, which may exacerbate pre-existing uterine issues or even trigger labor prematurely in pregnant women.

7. Interferes with sleep

Cymbopogon nardus interferes with sleep due to its stimulating properties, which can increase alertness and energy levels.

The essential oil extracted from the plant contains compounds that can activate the brain's neurotransmitters, making it difficult for individuals to fall asleep or stay asleep throughout the night.

This effect may be particularly pronounced when using citronella oil in aromatherapy or applying it topically before bedtime.

8. Produces gastrointestinal upset

Cymbopogon nardus produces gastrointestinal upset due to its natural oils and volatile compounds, which can irritate the digestive system.

The essential oils in citronella may cause stomach discomfort, nausea, and diarrhea, as they alter the normal functioning of the gastrointestinal tract. Additionally, citronella's strong scent can trigger stomach contractions, leading to symptoms such as bloating and cramps.

These adverse reactions are typically mild and temporary, but they may still be unpleasant for some individuals.

9. Worsens anxiety attacks

Cymbopogon nardus worsens anxiety attacks due to its ability to stimulate the nervous system and increase alertness.

As a natural insect repellent, it contains compounds that can cause a sense of heightened awareness or jitteriness in some individuals, leading to feelings of unease and increased anxiety levels.

This stimulating effect can exacerbate existing anxiety disorders or trigger episodes in those who are prone to them.

10. Causes respiratory issues

Cymbopogon nardus causes respiratory issues in some individuals.

The essential oil derived from this plant contains compounds such as cineole and citral, which can stimulate the respiratory tract and cause irritation, leading to symptoms like coughing, wheezing, and shortness of breath.

Additionally, citronella's potent scent can trigger allergic reactions or sensitivities, further exacerbating respiratory issues.
