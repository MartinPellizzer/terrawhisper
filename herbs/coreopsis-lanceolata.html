Coreopsis lanceolata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Coreopsis lanceolata has lanceolate leaves that are typically 2 to 6 centimeters wide and several centimeters long, growing from a basal rosette or along slender, hairy stems. The foliage is characterized by an entire or slightly serrated margin and a distinctively narrow, pointed shape. Its inflorescence consists of bright yellow composite flowers featuring prominent ray florets and a central disk of smaller tubular florets. The stems are often covered in fine, spreading hairs that provide a tactile texture to the plant's structure. These morphological features collectively distinguish the species within its natural prairie and grassland habitats.
