Pellitory, scientifically known as Parietaria judaica, is a herb that has been prized for centuries for its numerous health benefits.

Its properties are said to improve respiratory issues such as bronchitis and asthma by soothing the airways and reducing inflammation. In the kitchen, pellitory is commonly used as an ingredient in salads, soups, and stews, adding a subtle bitterness and earthy flavor to dishes. As a horticultural crop, pellitory is relatively easy to cultivate and can thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a valuable addition to permaculture systems.

With its ancient roots tracing back to the Mediterranean region, pellitory has been mentioned in historical texts dating back to the 16th century as a medicinal herb used to treat various ailments.
