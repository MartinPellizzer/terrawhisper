Dryopteris peninsulae belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is organized under the order Polypodiales and the family Polypodiaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Dryopteris, and its unique species designation, peninsulae.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dryopteris peninsulae has bipinnate to tripinnate fronds that typically measure between 30 and 60 centimeters in length with stipes characterized by brownish scales. Each leaflet displays a distinct midvein with secondary veins that extend toward the edges of the segment. The underside of the mature fronds contains small, circular sori arranged in two rows along the veins.
