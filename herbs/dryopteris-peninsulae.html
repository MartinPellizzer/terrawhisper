Dryopteris peninsulae

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dryopteris peninsulae has bipinnate to tripinnate fronds that typically measure between 30 and 60 centimeters in length with stipes characterized by brownish scales. The pinnules are generally ovate or lanceolate in shape and possess serrated or lobed margins. Each leaflet displays a distinct midvein with secondary veins that extend toward the edges of the segment. The underside of the mature fronds contains small, circular sori arranged in two rows along the veins. These reproductive structures are protected by a kidney-shaped indusium that eventually disappears as the spores mature.
