What is Chlorophytum macrophyllum?

Chlorophytum macrophyllum is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asparagaceae, native to tropical Africa.

It has long, narrow, linear leaves that are 80-150 cm long and 2-6 mm wide, with parallel veins and a rounded tip, attached to the stem at the base. The leaf sheath is tubular and 10-20 cm long, while the inflorescence is a dense spike of small white flowers, 30-60 cm long, with six petals and six stamens. The plant grows to a height of 150-300 cm, with a rhizome and adventitious roots.

The flowers are followed by a capsule that contains black seeds.
