Saxifraga imparilis

What's the taxonomical classification of Saxifraga imparilis?

Saxifraga imparilis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is situated under the order Saxifragales. It is further categorized into the family Saxifragaceae, falling under the genus Saxifraga, and is specifically identified by its species name, imparilis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saxifraga imparilis has a basal rosette of fleshy, spatulate leaves that are dark green, slightly succulent, and characterized by crenate or rounded margins.

These leaves grow densely together to form a compact mound close to the rocky substrate. From the center of this rosette, a slender, leafless scape emerges to support a small cluster of flowers. The blossoms feature five white petals that are often marked with delicate reddish or purplish spots near the center.

Each flower is supported by a small ovary and fine, hair-like structures along the stem.
