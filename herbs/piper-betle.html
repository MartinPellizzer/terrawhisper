What are the pest and diseases tips of Piper betle?

Piper betle, also known as Betel leaf or Paan, is a popular plant used in traditional medicine, food, and cultural practices.

One of the common pests affecting Piper betle is the leaf miner (Agromyza species), which feeds on the leaves causing yellowing, curling, and distortion. Another significant pest is the aphid (Aphis gossypii), which feeds on sap, causing curled or distorted leaves, and can also transmit viral diseases.

Additionally, the plant is prone to fungal diseases such as leaf spot (Cercospora spp. ), rust (Puccinia betelae), and root rot (Fusarium oxysporum) caused by overwatering or poor drainage. Effective management of these pests and diseases requires a combination of cultural practices such as proper irrigation, sanitation, and integrated pest management strategies like biological control, chemical pesticides, and resistant varieties.
