Betel leaf

Piper betle

Betel Leaf (Piper betle) is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia, widely used for its aromatic leaves which have been traditionally employed in various medicinal practices to improve oral health and treat digestive issues.

Botanical Identification

Piper betle, also known as Betel leaf, is a flowering plant in the family Piperaceae. Native to Southeast Asia, it is a perennial shrub with heart-shaped, aromatic leaves. Common names include Betel leaf, Paan, and Betel nut in various regions. Its scientific classification and traditional use in herbal medicine are well documented.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Magnoliopsida
  • Order: Piperales
  • Family: Piperaceae
  • Genus: Piper
  • Species: Piper betle

Active Compounds

The betel leaf contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in traditional herbal medicine.

Betel leaf Actions.

Preparation Methods

Culinary use: Used in cooking to flavor dishes, particularly in south asian cuisine.

Infusion: Used to make herbal teas to aid digestion and relieve respiratory issues.

Poultice: Applied externally to reduce inflammation and soothe skin irritations.

Safety Profile

Betel leaf is generally safe when used in moderate amounts. However, long-term use may lead to oral health issues and potential dependency. It is contraindicated for individuals with hypertension or those undergoing surgery due to its stimulant properties.
