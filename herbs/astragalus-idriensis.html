Astragalus idriensis

Astragalus idriensis, commonly known as Idriotic milk vetch, is a plant species in the Fabaceae family.

It is a perennial herb native to the Italian Alps, known for its potential health benefits and therapeutic properties.

The plant has been traditionally used to support immune function, and its bioactive constituents, including flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, are believed to contribute to its health-promoting effects, such as reducing inflammation and improving cardiovascular health.

Therapeutically, Astragalus idriensis is thought to exhibit adaptogenic, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant actions, which may help to alleviate stress and promote overall well-being.
