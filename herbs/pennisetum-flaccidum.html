Pennisetum flaccidum

What's the taxonomical classification of Pennisetum flaccidum?

Pennisetum flaccidum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further follows the order Poales and is a member of the family Poaceae. Finally, its taxonomic identity is completed by its placement in the genus Pennisetum, specifically as the species flaccidum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pennisetum flaccidum has a densely tufted growth habit characterized by long, slender, drooping leaves and large, plumose panicle inflorescences that typically measure between 10 and 30 centimeters in length. Its flowering spikes are covered in soft, feathery bristles that surround the spikelets, creating a fuzzy appearance.
