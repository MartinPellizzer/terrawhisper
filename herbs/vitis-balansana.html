Vitis balansana

What's the taxonomical classification of Vitis balansana?

Vitis balansana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Vitales and the family Vitaceae, eventually placing the plant within the genus Vitis. Ultimately, its unique biological identity is defined by its specific designation as the species balansana.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Vitis balansana contains high concentrations of bioactive polyphenols, specifically flavonoids and stilbenes, which provide its primary therapeutic properties. These chemical constituents function as potent antioxidants that mitigate oxidative stress within cellular structures. Research indicates that the presence of resveratrol-like compounds may offer neuroprotective and anti-inflammatory benefits. Additionally, the plant's secondary metabolites contribute to its potential role in regulating metabolic processes. Such diverse medicinal compounds make the species a subject of interest for pharmacological development.

What are the therapeutic actions of Vitis balansana?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, inflammatory conditions, and gastrointestinal disturbances through its bioactive compounds. The extracts from its leaves and stems possess antimicrobial properties that help combat various bacterial and fungal pathogens. Researchers have noted its effectiveness in soothing topical irritations and reducing swelling in localized tissues. Additionally, traditional applications often involve using the plant to alleviate digestive distress and internal inflammation. These medicinal uses are supported by the presence of specific phytochemicals that offer therapeutic benefits to the body.
