Botanical Identification

White Willow (Salix alba) is a species of flowering plant in the Salicaceae family. It is also known as White Willow Tree, White Willow, and Weeping Willow in various regions. It is a deciduous perennial tree native to Europe, North Africa, and western Asia. The plant has slender, white-barked branches, heart-shaped leaves, and catkins as its flowers. It has been referred to by several synonyms in historical herbal texts.

Active Compounds

White Willow contains key active compounds such as tannins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its therapeutic effects. These compounds work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, making it a valuable component in herbal medicine for pain relief and inflammatory conditions.

  • Tannin
  • Flavonoid
  • Phenolic acid

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for respiratory conditions and inflammation due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties.

Infusion: Used for digestive issues and mild fevers due to its mild astringent and antimicrobial effects.

Poultice: Used for skin irritations and wounds due to its soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.

Safety Profile

White Willow (Salix alba) is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation due to potential risks. Individuals with bleeding disorders or those taking anticoagulants should use it with caution.
