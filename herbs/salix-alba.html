White Willow (Salix alba)

White Willow (Salix alba) is a member of the Salicaceae family, native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. Traditionally, its bark, leaves, and stems have been used for decoctions, infusions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, diuretic, and astringent actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and traditional chinese medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 meters.
Leaves
Simple leaves with smooth margins, upper surface glabrous and pale green, lower surface pubescent and darker green, bearing distinct stomatal bands along midvein.
Flowers
Flowers are small, greenish-yellow, actinomorphic, arranged in catkins, with 3-5 stamens and 2-3 stigmas.
Stems
Elongated, herbaceous stems with a distinctively fluted surface, exhibiting an erect growth habit and opposite branching pattern, characterized by the presence of bundle scars and a smooth, glabrous epidermis.
