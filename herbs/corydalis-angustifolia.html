Narrowleaf Corydalis (Corydalis Angustifolia)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Corydalis angustifolia.

Narrowleaf corydalis, scientifically known as Corydalis angustifolia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia, particularly China, and is widely recognized as a medicinal herb with adaptogenic properties. It is traditionally valued for its ability to support nervous system health, reduce inflammation, and alleviate symptoms of anxiety and fatigue. In traditional Chinese medicine, Narrowleaf corydalis has been used for centuries to treat headaches, insomnia, and digestive issues, often prepared as a decoction or tincture. Modern wellness applications include its potential role in stress management and cognitive enhancement, with research suggesting its alkaloid compounds may have neuroprotective effects. One of its unique features is the presence of a rare alkaloid called tetrahydrocolumbamine, which contributes to its distinctive bitter flavor and has sparked interest in its therapeutic potential.
