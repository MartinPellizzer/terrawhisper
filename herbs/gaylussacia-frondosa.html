What is Gaylussacia frondosa?

Gaylussacia frondosa is a deciduous shrub that belongs to the family Ericaceae, commonly known as the heath family.

It is native to eastern North America and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are 2-4 cm long, elliptical to ovate in shape, and have a serrated margin. The leaves are glabrous or hairy on the lower surface, and the plant produces clusters of 5-10 white or pinkish flowers that are 4-6 mm in diameter. The flowers are followed by red or purple berries that are 5-7 mm in diameter, containing 5-7 seeds.

The shrub typically grows 1-3 meters tall and has a woody base with multiple stems.
