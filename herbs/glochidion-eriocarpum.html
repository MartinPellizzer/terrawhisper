Glochidion eriocarpum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Glochidion eriocarpum has small, simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical or oblong in shape and covered in dense, velvety hairs on both surfaces. The stems and branches are characterized by a fine pubescence that gives them a soft texture. Its flowers are inconspicuous and typically appear in small clusters along the axils of the leaves. The most distinctive feature is the fruit, which is a small capsule that is heavily clothed in thick, woolly hairs. These hairs provide a protective layer for the developing seeds within the capsule.
