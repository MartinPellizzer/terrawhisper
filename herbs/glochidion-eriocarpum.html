Glochidion eriocarpum

What's the taxonomical classification of Glochidion eriocarpum?

Glochidion eriocarpum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized as Equisetopsida, falling under the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Malpighiales and the family Phyllanthaceae. Finally, this specific organism is identified by its genus, Glochidion, and its species name, eriocarpum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Glochidion eriocarpum has small, simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical or oblong in shape and covered in dense, velvety hairs on both surfaces.

The stems and branches are characterized by a fine pubescence that gives them a soft texture. Its flowers are inconspicuous and typically appear in small clusters along the axils of the leaves. The most distinctive feature is the fruit, which is a small capsule that is heavily clothed in thick, woolly hairs.

These hairs provide a protective layer for the developing seeds within the capsule.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Glochidion eriocarpum can cause localized skin irritation, allergic reactions, and potential digestive distress if ingested. The plant contains various secondary metabolites that may trigger contact dermatitis upon touching the leaves or stems. Individuals with sensitive skin might experience redness, itching, or swelling in the affected areas. If consumed accidentally, the chemical compounds within the plant could lead to nausea or stomach discomfort. It is important to handle the specimen with caution to avoid these adverse physical responses.
