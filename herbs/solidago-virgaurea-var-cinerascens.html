Goatweed (Solidago Virgaurea Var. Cinerascens)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Solidago virgaurea var. cinerascens.

Goatweed, scientifically known as Solidago virgaurea var. cinerascens, is a medicinal herb valued for its adaptogenic properties and historical significance in traditional medicine. This variant of the goldenrod plant is primarily used to support respiratory health, reduce inflammation, and boost immune function due to its high content of flavonoids and sesquiterpene lactones. Historically, it has been employed in European folk remedies to treat coughs, bronchitis, and skin conditions, and was even used as a mild stimulant in traditional brewing practices. In modern wellness, Goatweed is increasingly incorporated into herbal supplements and teas aimed at enhancing vitality and supporting detoxification processes. Its unique feature is its subtle, slightly bitter flavor, which is believed to contribute to its traditional use as a tonic and digestive aid.
