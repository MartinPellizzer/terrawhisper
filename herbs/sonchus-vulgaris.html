Common Groundsel (Sonchus Vulgaris)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Sonchus vulgaris.

Common groundsel, scientifically known as Sonchus vulgaris, is a medicinal herb native to Europe and Asia, often found growing in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It has been traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and diuretic properties, making it a valuable plant in both traditional and modern herbal medicine. Historically, it has been used in folk remedies to treat ailments such as fever, digestive issues, and skin conditions, and in some cultures, it was even used as a spice or food source. Today, it is gaining recognition in wellness practices for its potential to support respiratory health and reduce inflammation, with its leaves containing compounds like sesquiterpene lactones that contribute to its therapeutic effects. One unique feature of common groundsel is its distinctive bitter taste, which has been noted in historical texts, and its ability to thrive in challenging environments, symbolizing resilience and adaptability.
