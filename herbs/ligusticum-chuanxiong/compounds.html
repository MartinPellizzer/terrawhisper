Chuan Xiong (Ligusticum chuanxiong) Compounds

The primary compounds of Chuan Xiong include essential oil, coumarin, and flavonoids. These compounds contribute to its medicinal properties, such as anti-inflammatory and circulatory benefits. Glycosides and terpenoids also play roles in its therapeutic effects.

Chuan Xiong (Ligusticum chuanxiong) Monograph

1. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound of Chuan Xiong (Ligusticum chuanxiong). It contains volatile aromatic compounds like ligustilide and butylidenephthalide, which contribute to its traditional uses in promoting blood circulation and relieving pain.

2. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Chuan Xiong (Ligusticum chuanxiong). It contributes to the herb's blood-activating and stasis-resolving properties. Coumarin also exhibits anti-inflammatory and antithrombotic effects, supporting its traditional use in treating cardiovascular and circulatory disorders.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of compounds found in Chuan Xiong (Ligusticum chuanxiong). They contribute to the herb's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in promoting blood circulation and reducing pain. These compounds are believed to interact with cellular pathways involved in inflammation and oxidative stress.

4. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Chuan Xiong (Ligusticum chuanxiong). It contributes to the herb's ability to promote blood circulation and relieve pain. This compound is commonly found in the rhizomes of the plant and is associated with its traditional uses in treating headaches and menstrual disorders.

5. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a key class of active compounds in Chuan Xiong (Ligusticum chuanxiong). They contribute to the herb's anti-inflammatory and circulatory benefits. Alkaloid is a key active compound found in Chuan Xiong (Ligusticum chuanxiong). It contributes to the herb's ability to improve blood circulation and relieve pain. Alkaloids in Chuan Xiong are known for their pharmacological effects, including anti-inflammatory and vasodilatory properties.

7. Phenolic acid

Saponin is a key active compound found in Chuan Xiong (Ligusticum chuanxiong). It contributes to the herb's anti-inflammatory and circulatory benefits. Saponin helps improve blood flow and reduce swelling, supporting traditional uses in treating headaches and menstrual disorders.

9. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Chuan Xiong (Ligusticum chuanxiong). It contributes to the herb's anti-inflammatory and hemostatic properties by promoting blood circulation and reducing swelling.
