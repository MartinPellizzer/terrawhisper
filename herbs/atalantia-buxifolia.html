Atalantia buxifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Atalantia buxifolia?

Atalantia buxifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed under the order Sapindales. The plant is further categorized within the family Rutaceae and falls under the genus Atalantia, specifically identifying as the species buxifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Atalantia buxifolia has small, opposite, elliptic to oblong leaves that measure approximately 1 to 2 centimeters in length and feature entire margins and an acute apex. These leaves are characterized by a coriaceous or leathery texture and a dark green color on the upper surface. The plant typically exhibits a shrubby growth habit with slender, woody branches. Its reproductive structures include small, white, fragrant flowers that emerge from the axils of the leaves. Following pollination, the plant produces small, globose fruits that transition from green to a yellowish color when mature.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of East Africa, specifically ranging from Ethiopia and Somalia down through Kenya and Tanzania to Mozambique. It is commonly found growing in coastal forests and thickets throughout these eastern African territories. The species favors humid environments where it can thrive in the understory of woodlands. Its distribution is closely tied to the seasonal rainfall patterns of the Indian Ocean coastline. This specific range limits its occurrence primarily to these localized ecological zones in Africa.
