Dendrobium henryi

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dendrobium henryi has elongated, reed-like pseudobulbs that can reach lengths of up to 50 centimeters and are characterized by a slightly compressed, cylindrical shape. These stems are typically green or yellowish-green and possess several nodes along their length. The leaves are relatively small, lanceolate, and grow sparsely from the upper portion of the pseudobulbs. During its flowering period, the plant produces numerous small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink with distinct markings on the labellum. These blossoms emerge from the nodes of the mature stems, creating a dense cluster of blooms.
