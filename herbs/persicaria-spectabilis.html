    What is Persicaria spectabilis?

    Persicaria spectabilis is a perennial plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae, native to China and Southeast Asia.

    It grows up to 1.5 meters tall, with a stout stem and large, ovate-shaped leaves that are 10-25 cm long and 5-15 cm wide, having 5-7 veins and a wavy or lobed margin. The leaves are often reddish or purple-tinged beneath, and the plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in a terminal panicle, 10-15 cm long, with 3-5 stamens. The flowers are arranged in a spike-like fashion, and the plant reproduces via seeds.

    The plant has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
