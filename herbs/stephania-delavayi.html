This liquid infusion is often administered orally to treat various ailments such as inflammation or digestive issues.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Stephania delavayi can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, neurological impairment, and potentially fatal cardiovascular complications due to its high concentration of toxic isoquinoline alkaloids. These alkaloids act as potent neurotoxins that may trigger tremors, dizziness, or loss of consciousness upon ingestion. Ingestion can also lead to significant nausea, vomiting, and intense abdominal pain as the body reacts to the chemical compounds. Furthermore, the plant's effects on the heart rate can lead to irregular rhythms or sudden cardiac arrest in sensitive individuals. Because of these unpredictable and dangerous reactions, the plant should be treated as highly poisonous.
