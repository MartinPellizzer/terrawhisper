Botanical Identification of Common Yarrow (Achillea ptarmica)

To identify Common Yarrow (Achillea ptarmica) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Common Yarrow, Achillea ptarmica, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Asterales, family Asteraceae, genus Achillea, and species ptarmica. These classifications place it within the flowering plants and the daisy family, which is characteristic of many medicinal herbs.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Common Yarrow is Achillea ptarmica, first described by Carl von Linné in 1753. It is also known by several synonyms, including Achillea millefolium var. ptarmica and Achillea millefolium subsp. ptarmica. Common names include yarrow, thousand-leaf, and nosebleed plant, while regional and historical names vary across cultures and time periods.

Morphological Characteristics

Common Yarrow is a herbaceous plant with a sprawling growth habit. It has a fibrous root system that spreads horizontally. Its stems are upright and branched, often hairy. Leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and have a feathery appearance. The inflorescence consists of a flat-topped cluster of small white or pale yellow flowers. Each flower has five petals and numerous yellow stamens. The fruit is a small achene with a pappus, allowing for wind dispersal.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Common Yarrow (Achillea ptarmica) can be distinguished by its flat, composite flower heads with white to pale yellow flowers, finely dissected leaves with a serrated edge, and a strong, aromatic odor. It differs from similar species like Achillea millefolium by its smaller, more compact flower heads and shorter, less divided leaves.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Common Yarrow (Achillea ptarmica) is native to Europe and western Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with full sun. It prefers well-drained soils, such as sandy or loamy types. It grows at altitudes between 0 and 2,000 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Common Yarrow blooms from June to August, producing clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early autumn, typically between August and September. For identification purposes, the best time to harvest is during the flowering period, when the plant is most identifiable and the essential oils are at their peak.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Common Yarrow (Achillea ptarmica) belongs to the Asteraceae family, closely related to other members of the genus Achillea. It shares key family traits like composite flowers and aromatic foliage. Its closest relatives include other yarrow species such as Achillea millefolium, differing mainly in leaf structure and habitat preferences.
