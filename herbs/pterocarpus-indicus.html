Pterocarpus indicus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pterocarpus indicus has a large, spreading canopy characterized by pinnately compound leaves consisting of several pairs of leaflets and a trunk covered in fissured, grayish-brown bark. The leaves are typically arranged alternately along the branches and feature small, ovate leaflets with entire margins. During the flowering season, the tree produces dense, fragrant clusters of small, bright yellow blossoms that emerge from the axils. These flowers eventually give rise to distinctive, flat, papery pods that are winged to facilitate wind dispersal. The overall structure of the plant reflects a robust, long-lived woody perennial adapted to tropical environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of South and Southeast Asia, specifically spanning from India through Myanmar and Thailand to parts of Indonesia. It thrives primarily in lowland areas where humid, maritime climates provide consistent warmth. Throughout its natural range, the species is frequently found growing near coastal forests and riverine environments. While its primary distribution is centered in Asia, it has been introduced to other tropical zones globally for ornamental purposes. This widespread availability in cultivation allows it to exist in various tropical landscapes outside of its original habitat.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Pterocarpus indicus targets enzymes such as cyclooxygenase-2, lipoxygenase, and various cytokines to exert its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. The bioactive compounds within the plant, particularly flavonoids and polyphenols, inhibit the expression of pro-inflammatory mediators like nitric oxide and tumor necrosis factor-alpha. These molecular interactions help modulate the inflammatory cascade by suppressing signaling pathways related to oxidative stress. Furthermore, the plant's constituents can interact with specific protein kinases to regulate cellular responses and protect against tissue damage. This multi-target mechanism underlies its traditional medicinal use in treating inflammation and various skin conditions.

What are the therapeutic actions of Pterocarpus indicus?

Pterocarpus indicus possesses significant therapeutic properties including astringent, anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and hemostatic actions.     What are the herbal preparations of Pterocarpus indicus?

    Pterocarpus indicus is prepared as decoctions, infusions, poultices, and topical washes using its various plant parts including bark, leaves, and resin. The bark is often boiled in water to create a medicinal tea used for treating internal ailments. Leaves are frequently crushed into a paste to be applied directly to the skin as a poultice for wounds or inflammation. The resin extracted from the tree can be processed into ointments or salves for topical application. These different preparations allow the medicinal properties of the tree to be utilized for both systemic and localized healing.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Pterocarpus indicus can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or gastrointestinal distress if its various parts are ingested or applied improperly to the body. While the plant is often used in traditional medicine, there is limited clinical data to confirm its safety profile in humans. Some users may experience localized dermatitis or rashes when coming into contact with its sap or foliage. Internal consumption without proper preparation could potentially lead to nausea or stomach discomfort. Due to these unpredictable reactions, it is important to exercise caution when using any part of this species.
