Orchis chusua

What's the taxonomical classification of Orchis chusua?

Orchis chusua belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Asparagales. As a member of the family Orchidaceae, it is further categorized into the genus Orchis, ultimately reaching its specific identification as the species chusua.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Orchis chusua has a single, erect stem that typically reaches heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and is topped by a terminal spike of numerous small, pinkish or purplish flowers. The leaves are mostly basal, appearing as narrow, lanceolate shapes that are often partially hidden by the stem. Each flower features a distinct trilobed lip with intricate patterns and subtle nectar guides to attract pollinators. The plant's underground structure consists of small, fleshy tubers that facilitate nutrient storage and asexual reproduction. This morphology allows the orchid to thrive within the specific montane forest habitats of East Asia.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, spanning across northern India, Nepal, Bhutan, and parts of southern China. It typically grows in mountainous terrain at elevations ranging from 2,000 to 4,000 meters above sea level. These orchids are most commonly found in moist subalpine meadows and open forest clearings where sunlight is sufficient. The species occupies specific ecological niches within the temperate zones of the Himalayan mountain range.
