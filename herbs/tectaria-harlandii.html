Tectaria harlandii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tectaria harlandii has small, delicate, deeply pinnatifid fronds that are typically arranged in a loose, spreading rosette. The individual segments of the pinnae are slender and exhibit a somewhat irregular, laciniate margin. The stipe is relatively short and lacks a distinct sheath, often appearing somewhat flattened at its base. The texture of the foliage is thin and membranous, reflecting its preference for shaded, humid environments. These morphological characteristics distinguish it from other members of the genus through their specific scale and division patterns.
