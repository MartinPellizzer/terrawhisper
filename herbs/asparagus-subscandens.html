What's the taxonomical classification of Asparagus subscandens?

Asparagus subscandens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asparagales. This plant is a part of the family Asparagaceae and is nested within the genus Asparagus, ultimately being identified by its specific species name, subscandens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Asparagus subscandens has slender, scrambling stems that are densely clothed with small, needle-like cladodes instead of true leaves. These modified stems function as photosynthetic organs and are arranged in a branching, wiry fashion. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers that emerge from the axils of the cladodes. Following flowering, it develops small, fleshy berries that typically transition from green to a deep red or black color when ripe.
