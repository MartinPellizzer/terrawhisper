Pinellia integrifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pinellia integrifolia has simple, entire, lanceolate leaves that are arranged oppositely along a woody stem. The leaf margins lack serrations or teeth, presenting a smooth and continuous edge. These leaves typically possess a leathery texture and a dark green coloration on the upper surface. The plant features small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. Its overall growth habit is characterized by a compact, shrub-like structure with relatively short internodes.

      What are the herbal preparations of Pinellia integrifolia?

      Pinellia integrifolia is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical tincture to utilize its various phytochemical properties. The dried bark and leaves are often simmered in water for extended periods to extract dense medicinal compounds. For more concentrated applications, the plant material is soaked in alcohol to create a potent tincture used for systemic support.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Pinellia integrifolia can cause severe skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. The plant contains specific chemical compounds that may trigger redness, itching, or swelling upon contact with sensitive areas of the skin. Individuals with known allergies to similar botanical species may experience more intense reactions such as hives or respiratory discomfort. Ingestion of the foliage or fruit can lead to nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain due to its inherent toxicity. It is important to avoid direct contact with the sap and ensure the plant is kept away from children and pets.
