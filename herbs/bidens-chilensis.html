Chile ironweed

Bidens chilensis

Chile Ironweed (Bidens chilensis) is a flowering plant native to South America, known for its medicinal properties and traditional use in treating various health conditions, particularly inflammation and skin disorders.

Botanical Identification

Bidens chilensis, commonly known as Chile Ironweed, belongs to the Asteraceae family. It is an annual plant native to South America, particularly Chile. The plant features serrated leaves and yellow flowers with prominent black central disks. It is also referred to as Ironweed in some herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Magnoliopsida
  • Order: Asterales
  • Family: Asteraceae
  • Genus: Bidens
  • Species: Bidens chilensis

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for digestive issues and as a diuretic.

Decoction: Used for respiratory conditions and pain relief.

Safety Profile

Chile Ironweed is generally considered safe when used in moderate amounts. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. There are no well-documented contraindications, but pregnant or breastfeeding women should avoid it due to limited safety data.
