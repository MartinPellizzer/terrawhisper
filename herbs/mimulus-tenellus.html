What's the taxonomical classification of Mimulus tenellus?

Mimulus tenellus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic progression continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Lamiales. Within this order, the plant is a member of the family Phrymaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by its placement in the genus Mimulus, with its unique designation being the species tenellus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Mimulus tenellus has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by erect, square-shaped stems that typically reach heights between ten and forty centimeters. The leaves are oppositely arranged along the stem and possess an ovate to lanceolate shape with serrated margins. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils, displaying a distinct bilabiate structure common to the Phrymaceae family. These blossoms often feature yellow or pale coloration with intricate spotting on the lower lip to guide pollinators. The plant's root system consists of creeping rhizomes that allow it to spread through moist soil environments.
