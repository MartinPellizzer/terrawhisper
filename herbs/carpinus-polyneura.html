Carpinus polyneura

What's the taxonomical classification of Carpinus polyneura?

Carpinus polyneura belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Fagales. It is further categorized under the family Betulaceae, which places it within the genus Carpinus. Ultimately, the specific identification of this plant is defined by its species name, polyneura.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Carpinus polyneura has deciduous, ovate to elliptical leaves characterized by sharply serrated margins and a distinct venation pattern consisting of numerous parallel veins extending from the midrib to the leaf edge.

The foliage is typically dark green on the upper surface and may exhibit a lighter hue underneath. Its woody stems and branches provide the structural framework for the shrub or small tree. The reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into small nutlets.

These nutlets are uniquely attached to a three-lobed, papery bract that facilitates wind dispersal.
