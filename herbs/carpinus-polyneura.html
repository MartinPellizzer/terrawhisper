Carpinus polyneura

What's the taxonomical classification of Carpinus polyneura?

Carpinus polyneura belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Fagales. It is further categorized under the family Betulaceae, which places it within the genus Carpinus. Ultimately, the specific identification of this plant is defined by its species name, polyneura.

    Carpinus polyneura is prepared primarily as a decoction of the bark or as a topical paste derived from crushed leaves. Such preparations are typically used to target specific localized ailments or systemic issues.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Carpinus polyneura can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or digestive distress if ingested or applied incorrectly. While specific toxicological studies on this exact species are limited, many plants in the Carpinus genus contain compounds that may trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Ingesting unverified botanical materials can lead to nausea, abdominal pain, or more severe systemic responses. It is essential to exercise caution and avoid direct contact with the plant until its safety profile is fully established by medical professionals. Always consult a specialist before using any wild plant for medicinal or topical purposes.
