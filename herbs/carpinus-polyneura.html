Carpinus polyneura

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Carpinus polyneura has deciduous, ovate to elliptical leaves characterized by sharply serrated margins and a distinct venation pattern consisting of numerous parallel veins extending from the midrib to the leaf edge. The foliage is typically dark green on the upper surface and may exhibit a lighter hue underneath. Its woody stems and branches provide the structural framework for the shrub or small tree. The reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into small nutlets. These nutlets are uniquely attached to a three-lobed, papery bract that facilitates wind dispersal.
