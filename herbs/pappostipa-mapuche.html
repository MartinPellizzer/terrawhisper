What is Pappostipa mapuche?

Pappostipa mapuche is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is a perennial plant, characterized by a short rhizome and culms that reach up to 50 cm in height, with leaf sheaths and ligules that are glabrous and lack auricles. The inflorescence is a panicle, measuring up to 10 cm in length, with spikelets that are composed of two florets, a fertile one and a sterile one, the fertile floret being a caryopsis. The glumes are unequal, with the lower glume being shorter than the upper one, and the fertile floret has a 1-nerved lemma and a 3-nerved palea.

The species is native to Chile.
