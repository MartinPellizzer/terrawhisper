Paddle Plant

Kalanchoe crenata

Paddle Plant (Kalanchoe crenata) is a member of the Crassulaceae family, traditionally used in various regions for its potential benefits in treating skin wounds and inflammation. The plant, often grown as a decorative succulent, has been utilized historically as a vulnerary, with its leaves and stems being the primary parts used in traditional preparations.

Botanical Identification

Kalanchoe crenata, commonly known as Paddle Plant, is a succulent belonging to the Crassulaceae family. Native to South Africa, it is a perennial shrub with fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. It is also referred to as Crassula crenata in some herbal texts.

Active Compounds

The Paddle Plant contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects. Their combined action enhances the plant's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

Preparation Methods

Powder: Used for digestive issues and skin conditions.

Poultice: Applied externally for wounds and inflammation.

Oil infusion: Used for skin irritation and muscle pain.

Safety Profile

Paddle Plant is generally safe when used appropriately. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. Consult a healthcare provider before use, especially if you have existing health conditions or are taking other medications.
