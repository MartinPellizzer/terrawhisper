Ficus tsiangii

What's the taxonomical classification of Ficus tsiangii?

Ficus tsiangii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is situated in the order Rosales and the family Moraceae. Finally, it is placed within the genus Ficus, where it is identified by its specific species name, tsiangii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ficus tsiangii has an arborescent growth habit characterized by a woody trunk, spreading branches, and elliptic to oblong leaves with entire margins and an acute apex. The foliage is typically coriaceous in texture and displays a lustrous green surface on the adaxial side. Its leaf arrangement is alternate, often appearing clustered at the ends of branches. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers contained within specialized structures known as syconia. These fruiting bodies eventually mature into small figs that serve as a primary morphological feature of the species.
