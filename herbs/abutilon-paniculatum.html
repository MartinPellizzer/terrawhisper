Abutilon paniculatum has a woody, shrubby growth habit characterized by ovate to heart-shaped leaves with serrated margins and a dense covering of fine hairs. Its inflorescences are arranged in large, terminal panicles that consist of numerous bell-shaped blossoms. These flowers exhibit a striking color palette, often ranging from bright yellow to deep orange with darker veins. The reproductive structures include prominent stamens that are central to the flower's visual appeal. Overall, the plant's morphology is defined by its bushy structure and showy, clustered blooms.
