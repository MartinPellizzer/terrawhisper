What's the taxonomical classification of Abutilon paniculatum?

Abutilon paniculatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Malvales and the family Malvacaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Abutilon, and its unique species designation, paniculatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Abutilon paniculatum has a woody, shrubby growth habit characterized by ovate to heart-shaped leaves with serrated margins and a dense covering of fine hairs.

The foliage is typically a vibrant green and provides a textured backdrop for its prolific flowering. Its inflorescences are arranged in large, terminal panicles that consist of numerous bell-shaped blossoms. These flowers exhibit a striking color palette, often ranging from bright yellow to deep orange with darker veins. The reproductive structures include prominent stamens that are central to the flower's visual appeal.

Overall, the plant's morphology is defined by its bushy structure and showy, clustered blooms.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Abutilon paniculatum can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or gastrointestinal distress if ingested by humans or animals. While the plant is primarily grown for its ornamental value, direct contact with its foliage or flowers may trigger redness and itching in sensitive individuals. There is limited toxicological research available regarding its systemic effects, so caution is advised when handling the plant. Allergic reactions often manifest as a localized rash or swelling upon contact with the skin. It is best to avoid consuming any part of the plant to prevent potential stomach upset or more severe internal reactions.
