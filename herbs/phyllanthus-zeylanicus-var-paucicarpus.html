Small-Fruited Phyllanthus (Phyllanthus Zeylanicus Var. Paucicarpus)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Phyllanthus zeylanicus var. paucicarpus.

Small-fruited phyllanthus, scientifically known as Phyllanthus zeylanicus var. paucicarpus, is a medicinal herb widely recognized for its adaptogenic properties and traditional use in Ayurvedic and indigenous medicine. This plant is valued for its ability to support liver function, reduce inflammation, and enhance immune response, making it a popular choice for detoxification and overall wellness. Historically, it has been used in South Asian cultures to treat ailments such as hepatitis, urinary tract infections, and digestive disorders, reflecting its deep-rooted significance in traditional healing practices. In modern wellness, it is increasingly incorporated into herbal supplements and functional foods due to its high concentration of bioactive compounds like flavonoids and tannins. Its unique feature is the presence of a rare active compound, phyllanthin, which contributes to its therapeutic effects and sets it apart in the realm of natural medicine.
