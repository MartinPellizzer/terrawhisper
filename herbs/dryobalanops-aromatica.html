Dryobalanops aromatica

What's the taxonomical classification of Dryobalanops aromatica?

Dryobalanops aromatica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the taxonomic hierarchy, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the lineage, it falls under the order Malvales and is a member of the family Dipterocarpaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Dryobalanops, and its species name, aromatica.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Dryobalanops aromatica contains high concentrations of tannins, terpenoids, and various phenolic compounds within its bark and leaves. These bioactive constituents provide significant antimicrobial and antioxidant properties when extracted for medicinal use. The presence of specific resinous substances also contributes to its traditional application in treating skin ailments and inflammatory conditions. Researchers study these chemical profiles to better understand the plant's potential in developing new therapeutic agents. Detailed phytochemical analysis reveals that these compounds work synergistically to offer diverse biological activities.

What are the therapeutic actions of Dryobalanops aromatica?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, inflammatory conditions, digestive issues, and various types of wounds. The extract possesses significant antimicrobial and astringent properties that help clean and heal damaged tissue. It is frequently applied topically to manage skin irritations and fungal outbreaks. Additionally, traditional medicine utilizes its components to soothe internal inflammation and support digestive health. The tannins found in the plant play a crucial role in its ability to reduce swelling and prevent infection.
