Dryobalanops aromatica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dryobalanops aromatica has an evergreen tree habit reaching up to 30 meters in height with a straight trunk and a dense, spreading crown. The leaves are simple, coriaceous, and elliptical in shape, possessing an oblong-lanceolate form with an entire margin. Its bark is typically dark and fissured, providing protection for the internal vascular tissues. The inflorescence consists of small, fragrant flowers that are arranged in axillary panicles. These flowers eventually develop into small, globose drupes that turn dark or black upon maturity.

What are the herbal preparations of Dryobalanops aromatica?

Dryobalanops aromatica is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a powder derived from its dried fruits and bark. The resinous extract, known as resin or gum, is frequently used in topical applications for its astringent properties. Many traditional formulations involve boiling the bark to create a medicinal liquid used for internal or external treatments. These preparations are often processed into fine powders to be mixed with honey or water for easier consumption. The various forms allow the plant's bioactive compounds to be utilized for diverse therapeutic purposes.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Dryobalanops aromatica can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if consumed in improper quantities. The presence of specific tannins and phenolic compounds in the bark and leaves may trigger localized redness or itching upon topical application. When ingested, the high concentration of astringent substances can lead to nausea or abdominal discomfort in sensitive individuals. Some users might also experience hypersensitivity reactions if they have pre-existing allergies to related botanical families. Careful monitoring is necessary when using extracts from this plant to avoid these adverse reactions.
