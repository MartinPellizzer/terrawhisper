Amitostigma basifoliatum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Amitostigma basifoliatum has a basal rosette of leaves and a single, slender, erect stem that typically reaches a height of 10 to 30 centimeters. The basal leaves are relatively large, elliptical or lanceolate in shape, and possess entire margins. As the stem ascends, the leaves become significantly smaller and more sparse, transitioning into much narrower bract-like structures. The inflorescence consists of small, delicate flowers that are often arranged in a terminal cluster. This morphological distinction between the prominent basal foliage and the reduced cauline leaves is a defining characteristic of the species.
