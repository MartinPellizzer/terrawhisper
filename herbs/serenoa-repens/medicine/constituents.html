10 Medicinal Constituents Of Serenoa Repens (Saw Palmetto)

Serenoa repens has active constituents such as phytosterols, particularly beta-sitosterol, and fatty acids like oleic and linoleic acid.

These compounds exhibit anti-androgenic and anti-inflammatory properties, which can alleviate urinary symptoms associated with benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH). By inhibiting 5-alpha-reductase, these constituents can reduce the conversion of testosterone to dihydrotestosterone, leading to improved urinary flow and reduced symptoms.

This can significantly improve the quality of life for individuals affected by BPH.

1. Saw palmetto acid

Serenoa repens saw palmetto acid is a unique compound found in the fruit of the plant.

It is believed to have potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may help to reduce symptoms associated with benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH), such as frequent urination and urinary retention.

Additionally, saw palmetto acid has been studied for its potential benefits on hormone regulation, fertility, and prostate health.

2. Beta-sitosterol

Serenoa repens beta-sitosterol is a naturally occurring phytosteroid found in the berries of this plant.

It has been traditionally used to treat urinary tract symptoms and benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH), with some evidence suggesting it may help reduce prostate inflammation and alleviate symptoms such as frequent urination and night awakenings.

Beta-sitosterol may also have anti-inflammatory properties, making it a potential therapeutic agent for various health conditions.

5. Ursolic acid

Serenoa repens stigmasterol is a naturally occurring phytosterol found in its berries.
