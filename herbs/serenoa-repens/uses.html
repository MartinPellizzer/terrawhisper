Saw Palmetto (Serenoa repens) Uses

Saw Palmetto is primarily used for benign prostatic hyperplasia, prostate infections, and urinary tract issues. It also addresses skin inflammation, psoriasis, eczema, and allergies. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties support its role in treating prostatitis and kidney stones.

Saw Palmetto (Serenoa repens) Monograph

1. Benign prostatic hyperplasia

Saw Palmetto is commonly used to support prostate health, particularly in managing symptoms of benign prostatic hyperplasia. It may help reduce urinary complications by inhibiting hormone activity that contributes to prostate enlargement. Clinical studies suggest it can improve urine flow and reduce frequency, though results may vary among individuals.

2. Prostate infection

Saw Palmetto has been traditionally used to support prostate health, particularly in managing symptoms of prostate infection. It may help reduce inflammation and inhibit the growth of prostate tissue, though it is not a substitute for medical treatment. Always consult a healthcare provider for proper diagnosis and treatment.

3. Urinary tract infection

Saw Palmetto is not typically used for urinary tract infections. It is more commonly associated with prostate health. While some studies suggest it may have anti-inflammatory properties, there is limited evidence supporting its efficacy against UTIs. It is not recommended as a primary treatment for bacterial infections of the urinary tract.

4. Prostatitis

Saw Palmetto has been traditionally used to support prostate health, particularly in managing symptoms of prostatitis. Its compounds may reduce inflammation and inhibit the conversion of testosterone to dihydrotestosterone, which is linked to prostate enlargement. Clinical studies suggest it may help alleviate urinary symptoms and reduce infection risk in prostatitis cases. It is often used as a natural alternative or complement to conventional treatments.

5. Kidney stones

Saw Palmetto is sometimes used traditionally to support urinary tract health. It may help reduce the risk of kidney stones by promoting healthy urine flow and preventing mineral buildup. However, it is not a proven treatment for existing kidney stones, and medical consultation is essential for proper management.

6. Skin inflammation

Saw Palmetto is used to reduce skin inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. It helps soothe conditions like eczema and psoriasis by modulating immune responses and reducing redness and irritation. Its active compounds, including fatty acids and phytosterols, support skin healing and prevent further inflammation.

7. Psoriasis

Saw Palmetto has been studied for its potential to reduce inflammation and regulate skin cell growth, making it a subject of interest in psoriasis management. Some research suggests it may help modulate hormonal activity and reduce sebum production, which could alleviate psoriatic symptoms. However, more clinical trials are needed to confirm its efficacy and safety for this specific use.

8. Eczema

Saw Palmetto is used topically for eczema due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. It helps reduce skin irritation and redness by modulating immune responses. Some studies suggest it may support skin healing and barrier function. However, it should be used with caution and under professional guidance.

9. Skin allergies

Saw Palmetto has been traditionally used to support skin health and may help alleviate symptoms of allergic reactions by reducing inflammation and irritation. It is often applied topically or taken internally to soothe redness, itching, and rash associated with skin allergies. Its anti-inflammatory properties make it a useful complement in managing allergic responses.

10. Skin infections

Saw Palmetto has been traditionally used to support skin health and may help in managing certain types of skin infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It is often used externally in topical formulations to reduce infection symptoms and promote healing.
