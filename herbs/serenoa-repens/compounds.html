Saw Palmetto (Serenoa repens) Compounds

Saw Palmetto contains several primary compounds, including saponins, which may support prostate health, and flavonoids, known for their antioxidant properties. Phenolic acids and tannins contribute to its anti-inflammatory effects, while essential oils and glycosides play roles in its overall therapeutic profile.

Saw Palmetto (Serenoa repens) Monograph

1. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Saw Palmetto (Serenoa repens). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in supporting prostate health. Saponins may inhibit 5-alpha-reductase, reducing dihydrotestosterone levels. This action is believed to underlie Saw Palmetto's efficacy in managing benign prostatic hyperplasia.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Saw Palmetto (Serenoa repens). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in prostate health. This compound helps modulate hormone metabolism and reduce inflammation, making it a significant component of the plant's therapeutic profile.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Saw Palmetto (Serenoa repens). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in urinary and prostate health. These compounds help modulate enzyme activity and reduce oxidative stress in target tissues.

4. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Saw Palmetto (Serenoa repens). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in supporting prostate health by potentially reducing inflammation and inhibiting enzyme activity. Tannins also aid in the plant's ability to bind to proteins and may support its therapeutic effects in herbal medicine.

5. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Saw Palmetto (Serenoa repens). They contribute to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly in supporting prostate health. These compounds are known for their anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, which are central to the plant's traditional and modern use in herbal medicine.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a compound found in Saw Palmetto (Serenoa repens). It contributes to the plant's traditional use for urinary and prostate health. Coumarin has anticoagulant properties and may support metabolic processes. Its presence enhances the overall efficacy of Saw Palmetto in herbal formulations.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Saw Palmetto (Serenoa repens). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in supporting prostate health. Glycosides in Saw Palmetto are believed to inhibit 5-alpha-reductase, a enzyme linked to prostate enlargement. This mechanism underlies its role in herbal medicine for urinary and prostate-related conditions.

8. Essential oil

Essential oil is a volatile compound found in Saw Palmetto (Serenoa repens). It contains bioactive components like fatty acids and phytosterols, which contribute to the plant's traditional use in supporting prostate health. The essential oil is typically extracted through steam distillation from the berries and leaves of the plant.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Saw Palmetto (Serenoa repens). While Saw Palmetto is primarily known for its fatty acids, some alkaloids are also present. These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in supporting prostate health. Alkaloids in Saw Palmetto may influence hormonal pathways, though their exact role remains under study.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid compound found in Saw Palmetto (Serenoa repens). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties and may support prostate health. Anthocyanins help neutralize free radicals and reduce inflammation, making them important in the plant's therapeutic potential.
