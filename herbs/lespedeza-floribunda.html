What's the taxonomical classification of Lespedeza floribunda?

Lespedeza floribunda belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic lineage through the order Fabales, the plant is a member of the family Fabaceae. It is specifically identified within the genus Lespedeza, ultimately reaching its unique species designation as Lespedeza floribunda.

What are the pharmacological activities of Lespedeza floribunda?

This plant has pharmacological activities that include acting as a melanin synthesis inhibitor, an antioxidant, and an anti-inflammatory agent. Its extracts contain bioactive compounds that can effectively reduce the production of pigment in skin cells. Research suggests that these phytochemicals help mitigate oxidative stress and protect tissues from damage. Furthermore, the plant exhibits potential in managing inflammatory responses through its unique chemical constituents. These diverse properties make it a subject of interest for dermatological and therapeutic applications.

According to a study published by "Journal of natural products", 40 new flavonoids and 11 known flavonoids were isolated from the roots of Lespedeza floribunda Bunge. Many of these compounds inhibited melanin synthesis in normal human epidermal melanocytes (NHEM). Specifically, compounds 3, 7, 8, 11, 16, 24, 27, 29, 33, 43, 45, and 51 were particularly inhibitory. These activities were stronger than that of hydroquinone, which is a major skin-lightening drug.
