Lespedeza floribunda

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lespedeza floribunda has trifoliate leaves consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets and small, pea-like yellow flowers arranged in dense terminal clusters. The stems are typically woody at the base and exhibit a spreading or ascending growth habit. Fine, spreading hairs often cover the surfaces of the leaves and stems, providing a textured appearance. The plant produces small, flattened legume pods that turn brown as they mature. These botanical features collectively define its structural identity within the Fabaceae family.
