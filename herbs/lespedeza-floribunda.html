Lespedeza floribunda

What's the taxonomical classification of Lespedeza floribunda?

Lespedeza floribunda belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic lineage through the order Fabales, the plant is a member of the family Fabaceae. It is specifically identified within the genus Lespedeza, ultimately reaching its unique species designation as Lespedeza floribunda.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lespedeza floribunda has trifoliate leaves consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets and small, pea-like yellow flowers arranged in dense terminal clusters. The stems are typically woody at the base and exhibit a spreading or ascending growth habit. Fine, spreading hairs often cover the surfaces of the leaves and stems, providing a textured appearance. The plant produces small, flattened legume pods that turn brown as they mature. These botanical features collectively define its structural identity within the Fabaceae family.

    As discussed in a study, 40 new flavonoids and 11 known flavonoids were isolated from the roots of Lespedeza floribunda Bunge. The structures of these compounds were determined by MS and NMR analyses, while CD spectra determined the absolute configurations. Many of these compounds inhibited melanin synthesis in normal human epidermal melanocytes (NHEM). Specifically, compounds 3, 7, 8, 11, 16, 24, 27, 29, 33, 43, 45, and 51 were found to be particularly inhibitory. The activities of these compounds were stronger than that of hydroquinone.
