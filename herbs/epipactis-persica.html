What is Epipactis persica?

Epipactis persica is a species of orchid in the family Orchidaceae.

It is a perennial herb with erect stems, 10-25 cm tall, bearing 2-4 linear-lanceolate leaves and a raceme of 10-20 yellowish-green flowers, 1-1.5 cm long, with a yellowish-green labellum and a white or yellowish-green lip. The sepals and petals are greenish-yellow, and the labellum has a yellowish-green callus in the center. The plant grows in shaded, humid areas, typically in deciduous forests, and is native to the Caucasus region and Iran.

The species was first described by Steven in 1824.
