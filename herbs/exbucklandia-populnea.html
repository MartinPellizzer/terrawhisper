Exbucklandia populnea

What's the taxonomical classification of Exbucklandia populnea?

Exbucklandia populnea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Saxifragales. This plant is further categorized under the family Hamamelidaceae, falling into the genus Exbucklandia, and is specifically identified by its species name, populnea.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Exbucklandia populnea can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or gastrointestinal distress if ingested or handled improperly. While specific toxicological profiles are limited in public literature, contact with the plant's sap or leaves may trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Ingestion of any part of the plant should be avoided due to the potential for unpredictable systemic toxicity. There is also a risk of localized swelling or itching if the plant's extracts come into direct contact with mucous membranes. Careful observation is necessary if accidental exposure occurs to ensure no severe adverse reactions develop.
