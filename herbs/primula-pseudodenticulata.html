Primula pseudodenticulata

What's the taxonomical classification of Primula pseudodenticulata?

Primula pseudodenticulata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Ericales. Within this order, the plant is a member of the family Primulaceae, specifically falling under the genus Primula, and is identified by its unique species designation, pseudodenticulata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Primula pseudodenticulata has a basal rosette of crinkled, oblong-to-ovate leaves that feature prominent serrated margins and deep, reticulate venation. These leaves are typically dark green and grow close to the ground to form a dense cluster. From the center of this rosette, long, leafless stalks known as scapes emerge to support the flowering structures. Each scape carries a terminal umbel composed of several small, salverform flowers. The petals are often pale pink or lavender and are characterized by a distinctively notched or dentate appearance at the tips.
