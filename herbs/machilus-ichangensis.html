Machilus ichangensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Machilus ichangenensis has elliptical to lanceolate leaves that are typically coriaceous with entire margins and an acute apex. The foliage is characterized by a glossy upper surface and a slightly paler underside, often displaying prominent pinnate venation. Its stems are woody and develop into a medium-sized tree with smooth to slightly fissured bark as it matures. The reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters that eventually produce fleshy drupes. These fruits are typically ovoid and serve as a primary morphological feature for species identification in its natural habitat.
