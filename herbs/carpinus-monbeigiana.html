Carpinus monbeigiana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Carpinus monbeigiana has simple, ovate leaves with serrated margins and prominent pinnate venation. The foliage typically displays a deep green color and a slightly textured surface. Its woody stems develop into a sturdy structure characteristic of the hornbeam family. Small, inconspicuous flowers appear during the growing season, eventually giving way to small nut-like fruits. The overall architecture of the plant is characterized by a dense and branching habit.
