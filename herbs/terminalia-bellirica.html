Belleric Myrobalan

Terminalia bellirica

Belleric Myrobalan (Terminalia bellirica) is a small evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent, known for its medicinal properties and traditional use in Ayurvedic medicine to support digestive health and treat conditions such as diarrhea and dysentery.

Taxonomy

Belleric Myrobalan (Terminalia bellirica) is a species belonging to the genus Terminalia in the family Combretaceae, classified under the order Sapindales, class Eudicots, division Magnoliophyta, and kingdom Plantae.

Morphology

Belleric Myrobalan (Terminalia bellirica) is a deciduous tree characterized by its rough, dark brown bark, elongated elliptical leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside, and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single, hard, angular seed.

Distribution

Belleric Myrobalan (Terminalia bellirica) is native to the Indian subcontinent, primarily found in regions of India, Nepal, Bhutan, and Sri Lanka, growing in tropical and subtropical forests.

Traditional Uses

Belleric Myrobalan (Terminalia bellirica) has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine for its astringent, anti-inflammatory, and digestive properties, particularly to treat diarrhea, dysentery, and other gastrointestinal disorders.

Medicinal Systems

In traditional medicinal systems such as Ayurveda, Unani, and Siddha, Belleric Myrobalan (Terminalia bellirica) has been widely used for its therapeutic properties.

Phytochemistry

The phytochemistry of Belleric Myrobalan (Terminalia bellirica) is characterized by the presence of tannins, ellagitannins, gallic acid, flavonoids, and various polyphenolic compounds, which contribute to its medicinal properties and traditional uses in Ayurvedic medicine.

Pharmacology

Belleric Myrobalan exhibits pharmacological properties including astringent, antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial effects, which contribute to its traditional use in treating gastrointestinal disorders, skin conditions, and infections.

Botanical Identification

Terminalia bellirica, commonly known as Belleric Myrobalan, is a species of the Combretaceae family. It is native to the Indian subcontinent and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is a small deciduous tree with thick, aromatic bark and elliptical leaves, bearing yellowish-green flowers and dark brown, globular fruits. It is also referred to as Indian Satinwood or Harsingar in various regional contexts.

Safety Profile

Belleric Myrobalan is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. Long-term use should be monitored, and it is contraindicated in cases of known allergy to cashew or related plants. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
