Xylopia acutiflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Xylopia acutiflora?

Xylopia acutiflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the evolutionary framework of the order Magnoliales. The plant is a member of the family Annonaceae and is further defined by its specific placement in the genus Xylopia, eventually reaching its unique species designation as Xylopia acutiflora.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Xylopia acuifora has lanceolate to narrowly elliptical leaves with acute apices, woody stems, and small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in axillary clusters.

The foliage is typically coriaceous in texture and features an entire margin that lacks serration. Its woody structure supports a branching habit characteristic of tropical understory shrubs or small trees. The reproductive organs consist of small petals and numerous stamens surrounding a central pistil.

These morphological traits help distinguish the species within the Annonaceae family.
