Xylopia acutiflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Xylopia acutiflora?

Xylopia acutiflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the evolutionary framework of the order Magnoliales. The plant is a member of the family Annonaceae and is further defined by its specific placement in the genus Xylopia, eventually reaching its unique species designation as Xylopia acutiflora.

Xylopia acutriflora contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, terpenoids, and phenolic compounds that exhibit pharmacological potential. What are the therapeutic actions of Xylopia acutiflora?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including skin infections, stomach pains, digestive issues, and respiratory problems. Its medicinal properties are often harnessed to address localized inflammation and fungal growth on the skin. Local communities frequently utilize its extracts to alleviate gastrointestinal distress and abdominal discomfort. Additionally, the plant serves as a traditional remedy for managing symptoms related to coughs and other breathing difficulties. These diverse therapeutic applications reflect the significant role it plays in regional ethnobotanical practices.
