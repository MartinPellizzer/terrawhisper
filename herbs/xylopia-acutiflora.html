Xylopia acutiflora

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Xylopia acuifora has lanceolate to narrowly elliptical leaves with acute apices, woody stems, and small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in axillary clusters. The foliage is typically coriaceous in texture and features an entire margin that lacks serration. Its woody structure supports a branching habit characteristic of tropical understory shrubs or small trees. The reproductive organs consist of small petals and numerous stamens surrounding a central pistil. These morphological traits help distinguish the species within the Annonaceae family.
