What is Sarcochilus viridiflorus?

Sarcochilus viridiflorus is a species of orchid in the family Orchidaceae.

It is a terrestrial epiphyte, growing up to 10 cm in height, with a short stem and two to three linear, suberect leaves. The inflorescence is a short, racemose spike, bearing 2-4 flowers that are greenish-yellow, with a labellum and two petals, the dorsal sepal being similar in shape to the petals. The plant is native to the tropical forests of the Pacific region of Australia and New Guinea, where it grows on tree trunks and branches.

The flowers are pollinated by small, nocturnal insects, specifically the species of bees and wasps.
