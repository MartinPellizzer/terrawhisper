Stellaria vestita

What's the taxonomical classification of Stellaria vestita?

Stellaria vestita belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed under the order Caryophyllales. This plant is a representative of the family Caryophyllaceae, falling under the genus Stellaria, and is specifically identified by the species epithet vestita.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stellaria vestita has a low-growing, spreading growth habit characterized by stems that are densely covered in fine, white, spreading hairs.

The leaves are small, ovate to lanceolate, and arranged oppositely along the hairy stems. These leaves feature entire margins and are typically much shorter than the narrow, linear sepals that surround the flowers. The flowers are small and white, tucked closely within the leaf axils and protected by the pubescent sepals.

This dense coating of hair serves as a primary distinguishing morphological feature of the species.

        What are the herbal preparations of Stellaria vestita?

        Stellaria vestita is prepared as an infusion, decoction, or topical wash to utilize its medicinal properties. The dried leaves and aerial parts are typically steeped in hot water to create a tea for internal consumption. For skin ailments, the plant material can be boiled into a concentrated decoction used to rinse affected areas. Some practitioners also create poultices by crushing the fresh herb directly against the skin. These various preparations allow for the extraction of its active components in different concentrations.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Stellaria vestita can cause severe contact dermatitis, localized skin inflammation, and intense itching upon direct physical contact. The plant contains specific irritants that react with human epidermal layers to produce redness and swelling. Some individuals may also experience mild respiratory irritation if the fine hairs of the plant are inhaled. Prolonged exposure to the sap can lead to blistering or persistent allergic reactions in sensitive people. It is essential to wear protective gloves when handling this species to avoid these adverse reactions.
