Stellaria vestita

What's the taxonomical classification of Stellaria vestita?

Stellaria vestita belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed under the order Caryophyllales. This plant is a representative of the family Caryophyllaceae, falling under the genus Stellaria, and is specifically identified by the species epithet vestita.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stellaria vestita has a low-growing, spreading growth habit characterized by stems that are densely covered in fine, white, spreading hairs. The leaves are small, ovate to lanceolate, and arranged oppositely along the hairy stems. These leaves feature entire margins and are typically much shorter than the narrow, linear sepals that surround the flowers. The flowers are small and white, tucked closely within the leaf axils and protected by the pubescent sepals. This dense coating of hair serves as a primary distinguishing morphological feature of the species.
