Stellaria vestita

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stellaria vestita has a low-growing, spreading growth habit characterized by stems that are densely covered in fine, white, spreading hairs. The leaves are small, ovate to lanceolate, and arranged oppositely along the hairy stems. These leaves feature entire margins and are typically much shorter than the narrow, linear sepals that surround the flowers. The flowers are small and white, tucked closely within the leaf axils and protected by the pubescent sepals. This dense coating of hair serves as a primary distinguishing morphological feature of the species.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Stellaria vestita can cause severe contact dermatitis, localized skin inflammation, and intense itching upon direct physical contact. The plant contains specific irritants that react with human epidermal layers to produce redness and swelling. Some individuals may also experience mild respiratory irritation if the fine hairs of the plant are inhaled. Prolonged exposure to the sap can lead to blistering or persistent allergic reactions in sensitive people. It is essential to wear protective gloves when handling this species to avoid these adverse reactions.
