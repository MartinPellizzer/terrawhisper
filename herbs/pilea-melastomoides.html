What's the taxonomical classification of Pilea melastomoides?

Pilea melastomoides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down its taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is part of the order Rosales and falls within the family Urticaceae. Finally, its specific identification is determined by its placement in the genus Pilea, with its unique designation being the species melastomoides.

      What are the herbal preparations of Pilea melastomoides?

      Pilea melastomoides is prepared as a decoction, a topical poultice, or a macerated infusion depending on the specific ailment being treated. When used as a decoction, the plant material is boiled in water to extract its active medicinal compounds for internal consumption. For external skin conditions, the crushed leaves are applied directly to the affected area as a fresh poultice. Some traditional methods involve soaking the plant in a liquid base to create a medicinal infusion. These various forms allow the plant's properties to be delivered effectively to different parts of the body.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Pilea melastomoides can cause mild skin irritation or allergic reactions such as redness and itching if the sap comes into contact with sensitive skin. While it is generally considered non-toxic to humans, accidental ingestion might lead to minor stomach upset or digestive discomfort. Pet owners should be cautious because consuming the plant may cause vomiting or lethargy in cats and dogs. There are currently no extensive clinical studies documenting severe long-term toxicological effects in humans. Monitoring for any signs of sensitivity is recommended when handling the foliage.
