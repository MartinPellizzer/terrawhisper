Papaya (Carica papaya) Uses

Papaya is used to treat various digestive disorders due to its enzyme content, which aids in digestion. It can help with constipation, inflammatory bowel disease, and gastritis. The fruit also supports the treatment of ulcers, diarrhea, and digestive upset by reducing inflammation and promoting gut health.

Papaya (Carica papaya) Monograph

1. Digestive disorders

Papaya contains enzymes like papain and bromelain that aid in digestion by breaking down proteins. It is commonly used to relieve symptoms of indigestion, bloating, and stomach discomfort. Its mild laxative effect can also help with constipation. Regular consumption may support overall digestive health.

2. Constipation

Papaya contains enzymes like papain that aid digestion and soften stool. It also provides fiber and potassium, which help regulate bowel movements. Regular consumption can alleviate constipation by promoting smooth intestinal function. Its natural properties make it a gentle and effective remedy for digestive discomfort.

3. Inflammatory bowel disease

Papaya contains enzymes like papain and carpain, which have anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds may help reduce intestinal inflammation in inflammatory bowel disease. Preliminary research suggests papaya may support gut health, though more studies are needed to confirm its efficacy in treating specific conditions.

4. Gastroesophageal reflux disease

Papaya contains enzymes like papain that aid digestion and reduce stomach acid. It can help alleviate symptoms of gastroesophageal reflux disease by soothing the esophagus and reducing inflammation. Regular consumption of papaya juice or ripe fruit may provide natural relief for reflux-related discomfort.

5. Digestive issues

Papaya contains enzymes like papain that aid digestion by breaking down proteins. It can relieve symptoms of indigestion, bloating, and constipation. Regular consumption may improve gut health and support efficient nutrient absorption. It is commonly used in traditional herbal medicine for digestive support.

6. Gastritis

Papaya contains enzymes like papain that help reduce inflammation in the stomach lining. It also aids in digestion and can alleviate symptoms of gastritis. Regular consumption of papaya juice or fresh fruit may support healing and ease discomfort associated with this condition.

7. Digestive ulcers

Papaya contains enzymes like papain that help break down proteins, reducing inflammation and promoting healing in the stomach lining. It also has antioxidant properties that protect the digestive system. Regular consumption may support the treatment of digestive ulcers by soothing irritation and aiding digestion.

8. Diarrhea

Papaya contains enzymes like papain that aid digestion and reduce intestinal inflammation. It helps alleviate diarrhea by promoting healthy digestion and reducing gut irritation. The fruit's high water content also supports hydration during bouts of diarrhea. Regular consumption of ripe papaya can support digestive health and ease symptoms of mild diarrhea.

9. Digestive upset

Papaya contains enzymes like papain that aid in breaking down proteins, easing digestion. It can help alleviate symptoms of indigestion, bloating, and nausea. The fruit's fiber content also supports regular bowel movements and gut health. Regular consumption may reduce digestive discomfort and promote overall gastrointestinal wellness.

10. Gastroenteritis

Papaya contains enzymes like papain that aid digestion and reduce inflammation. It is used to treat gastroenteritis by alleviating symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea. Its antimicrobial properties help combat intestinal infections. Regular consumption can support gut health and recovery from digestive disorders.
