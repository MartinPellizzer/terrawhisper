Piper retrofractum

What's the taxonomical classification of Piper retrofractum?

Piper retrofractum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Piperales and the family Piperaceae. Ultimately, the plant is placed within the genus Piper, specifically identifying as the species retrofractum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Piper retrofractum has woody, climbing stems that reach up to several meters in length and possess small, ovate, dark green leaves with an entire margin and an acute apex. The plant features a distinct arrangement of leaves that are typically oppositely or sub-oppositely positioned along the vine. Its inflorescences consist of dense, cylindrical spikes that emerge from the leaf axils. These spikes are initially green but transition to a reddish or brownish hue as they mature. Small, inconspicuous flowers are densely packed along these spikes, eventually developing into fleshy drupes.

What are the pharmacological activities of Piper retrofractum?

This plant has diverse pharmacological activities including anti-inflammatory, antimalarial, antimicrobial, cytotoxic, and larvicidal properties. Its bioactive compounds demonstrate a capacity to inhibit various pathogens and reduce inflammation in biological systems. Research indicates that these extracts can effectively combat malaria parasites and resist a wide range of harmful microbes. Furthermore, the plant exhibits cytotoxic activity that is being studied for its potential role in cancer research. Its larvicidal activity also provides a natural method for controlling mosquito populations to prevent disease transmission.

As discussed in a study, Piper retrofractum extract demonstrates promising inhibitory effects on NF-κB and proinflammatory molecules. The extract significantly reduces LPS, NO, COX-2, IL-6, IL-1, and NF-κB through the TLR4 axis. Investigation through reporter assay, viability test, ELISA, and Western blotting revealed these inhibitory properties. Furthermore, the extract enhances the survival of human keratinocytes by protecting them from cell death induced by TRAIL, a member of the TNF superfamily. Immunohistochemistry analysis in an Imiquimod-induced skin inflammation mice model showed downregulation of COX-2 and IL-1β expression upon treatment with the extract. These findings suggest Piper retrofractum possesses anti-inflammatory properties by reducing proinflammatory cytokine production through inhibition of the NF-κB signaling pathway.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds piperine, piperlonguminine, Lyciumamide C, Dipiperamide G, and Chenoalbicin. These specific alkaloids and amides are responsible for many of the biological activities observed in the species. Piperine and piperlonguminine are known to influence metabolic processes and digestive functions. The presence of Lyciumamide C and Dipiperamide G suggests potential roles in modulating cellular responses. Additionally, Chenoalbicin contributes to the unique chemical profile that researchers study for pharmacological development.

As discussed in a study, the plant Piper retrofractum collected in Ishigaaki, Japan, contains medicinal compounds. This specific sample of Piper retrofractum showed high contents of piperine and piperlonguminine. The study established a method for the simultaneous quantitative determination of these two piperidine alkaloids. While specific numerical values for Piper retrofractum were not provided, its chemical profile was noted as being high in both substances.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause nausea, gastric irritation, diarrhea, abdominal pain, and dizziness when consumed in excess or by sensitive individuals. The presence of piperine and other bioactive compounds may stimulate the gastrointestinal lining, leading to discomfort or upset stomach. Some users might experience significant digestive disturbances such as cramping or loose stools after ingestion. Neurological responses like lightheadedness or dizziness can also occur as a secondary reaction to its potent chemical constituents. It is important to monitor these symptoms closely to avoid more severe physiological distress.
