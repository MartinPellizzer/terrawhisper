Ilex tephrophylla

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ilex tephrophylla has small, leathery, evergreen leaves that are elliptical or lanceolate in shape and feature finely serrated margins. The foliage typically displays a dark green color on the upper surface with a paler underside. Its woody stems develop into a dense, spreading shrub or a small tree depending on the environment. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually give way to dark, fleshy berries. These morphological traits allow the species to thrive in specific Mediterranean-style habitats.

      What are the herbal preparations of Ilex tephrophylla?

      Ilex tephrophylla is prepared primarily as a decoction or an infusion using its leaves and stems for medicinal or ritualistic purposes.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Ilex tephrophylla can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, or allergic reactions if ingested or handled improperly. The plant contains various secondary metabolites that may act as irritants to sensitive skin upon contact. While there is limited clinical data on human toxicity, caution is advised when working with its foliage. Always wash hands thoroughly after handling the plant to prevent accidental contact with the face or eyes.
