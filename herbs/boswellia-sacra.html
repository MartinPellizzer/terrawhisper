Frankincense (Boswellia sacra)

Frankincense (Boswellia sacra) is a member of the Burseraceae family, native to Arabian Peninsula, Sudan, and Somalia. Traditionally, its resin, bark, and wood have been used for powders, infusions, and oil infusions.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, tonic, and carminative actions, and has a long history of use in ayurvedic medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and unani medicine.
