Psychotria yunnanensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Psychotria yunnanensis has opposite, elliptical leaves with entire margins and an obtuse apex, growing on woody shrubs or small trees. The leaf surfaces are typically glabrous and display a deep green coloration on the upper side. Its inflorescence is composed of small, white, tubular flowers that emerge from the axils of the leaves. The fruit is a fleshy drupe that matures into a characteristic shape used for seed dispersal. These morphological features are consistent with its classification within the Rubiaceae family.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the subtropical and tropical regions of southwestern China, specifically within the provinces of Yunnan and Guizhou. It thrives primarily in the moist, shaded environments found in montane forests and dense shrublands. The species is typically located at moderate elevations where consistent humidity supports its growth. Its distribution is closely tied to the specific climatic conditions of the Yunnan-Guizhou Plateau. Localized populations are often found in limestone areas or along river valleys within these mountainous territories.
