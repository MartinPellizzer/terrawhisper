What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Nasturtium Officinale (Watercress)?

Nasturtium officinale, also known as watercress, has health benefits such as reducing inflammation and improving cardiovascular health.

Its medicinal constituents include vitamins A, C, and K, as well as minerals like calcium and iron. The herb can be prepared as a tea, capsule, or added fresh to salads and sandwiches. However, excessive consumption may lead to stomach upset and interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes medications.

Consult a healthcare professional before using watercress medicinally.

This article explains the health benefits, active constituents, medicinal preparations, possible side effects, and precautions related to Nasturtium officinale.

What are the health benefits of Nasturtium officinale?

Nasturtium officinale has health benefits such as being a rich source of antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds that can help protect against chronic diseases like cancer, diabetes, and heart disease.

Its high levels of vitamin K and folate also support bone health and fetal development during pregnancy.

Additionally, watercress' diuretic properties make it an effective natural remedy for urinary tract issues and digestive problems.

What are the active constituents of Nasturtium officinale?

The health benefits of Nasturtium officinale comes from its active constituents, such as vitamin C, vitamin K, and beta-carotene.

Additionally, it contains flavonoids, isothiocyanates, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties.

These compounds work together to provide potential health benefits, including reducing oxidative stress, protecting against cancer, and supporting cardiovascular and immune function.

What are the medicinal preparations of Nasturtium officinale?

Nasturtium officinale has different medicinal preparations, such as infusions, teas, capsules, and creams.

Infusions are used to treat respiratory issues like bronchitis and asthma, while teas help with digestive problems and skin irritations. Capsules containing its extracts are prescribed for treating arthritis, gout, and other inflammatory conditions. Creams and ointments are applied topically to soothe skin irritations, wounds, and minor cuts.

These preparations have anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antioxidant properties, making them effective in relieving various health issues.

What are the possible side effect of using Nasturtium officinale improperly?

Improper use of Nasturtium officinale increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as nausea and stomach upset, allergic reactions including itching and hives, headaches and migraines, interactions with blood thinners and medications for high blood pressure, kidney stone formation due to increased oxalate levels, digestive issues like bloating and gas, skin irritation and rashes, and potentially even kidney or liver damage in severe cases.

What precautions to take when using Nasturtium officinale medicinally?

Before using Nasturtium officinale for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as consulting with a healthcare professional to determine the appropriate dosage and potential interactions with medications.

Additionally, be aware of any allergies or sensitivities you may have to the plant, and use caution when consuming it if you have kidney stones or bleeding disorders, as it can increase urine production and thin the blood.
