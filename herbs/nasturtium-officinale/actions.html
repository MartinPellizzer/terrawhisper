Garden Nasturtium (Nasturtium officinale) Actions

Garden Nasturtium exhibits several key therapeutic actions. It has anti-inflammatory and astringent properties, helping to reduce swelling and tighten tissues. It also acts as a bitter tonic, stimulating digestion and acting as a carminative to relieve gas. Additionally, it serves as a diuretic and expectorant, aiding in the removal of excess fluids and mucus from the body.

Garden Nasturtium (Nasturtium officinale) Monograph

1. Anti-inflammatory

Garden Nasturtium has an anti-inflammatory action, primarily due to compounds like flavonoids and alkaloids. These substances help reduce inflammation by inhibiting inflammatory pathways in the body. It is commonly used for respiratory and skin conditions. Its effects are supported by traditional use and some scientific studies.

2. Bitter

Garden Nasturtium has a bitter action, which stimulates digestion and increases appetite. This bitterness helps to clear mucus and support liver function. It is particularly useful for digestive sluggishness and respiratory congestion. The bitter taste also has a cleansing effect on the body.

3. Carminative

Garden Nasturtium has a carminative action, helping to relieve gas and bloating by stimulating the digestive system. It promotes the expulsion of trapped air from the intestines, reducing discomfort and supporting healthy digestion. This makes it useful for addressing digestive issues like flatulence and indigestion.

4. Diuretic

Garden Nasturtium has a diuretic action, promoting the excretion of excess water and salts through urine. This effect supports kidney function and can help alleviate fluid retention. It is commonly used in herbal medicine for mild edema and urinary tract support.

5. Tonic

Garden Nasturtium has a tonic action, supporting overall vitality and strengthening the body's systems. It is used to boost energy, enhance digestion, and improve immune function. Its mild yet effective nature makes it suitable for regular use in supporting general health.

6. Expectorant

Garden Nasturtium has an expectorant action, helping to loosen and expel mucus from the respiratory tract. It is commonly used for conditions like bronchitis and coughs where excessive mucus is present. Its active compounds stimulate the production of fluids, aiding in clearing congestion. This makes it a valuable herb in respiratory support.

7. Astringent

Garden Nasturtium has an astringent action, which helps reduce inflammation and tighten tissues. It is commonly used for respiratory and urinary tract infections due to its ability to constrict mucous membranes. This property also supports wound healing and can soothe sore throats and digestive irritations.

8. Stimulant

Garden Nasturtium has a stimulant action, increasing alertness and energy by enhancing nervous system activity. It may help improve focus and mental clarity, though it should be used cautiously to avoid overstimulation. Its effects are mild compared to stronger stimulants, making it suitable for moderate use in herbal medicine.

9. Antispasmodic

Garden Nasturtium has an antispasmodic action, helping to relieve muscle spasms and cramps. It works by relaxing smooth muscle tissues, making it useful for conditions like digestive spasms or menstrual cramps. Its active compounds reduce excessive nerve stimulation, promoting calm and ease in affected areas.

10. Sedative

Garden Nasturtium has a sedative action, helping to calm the nervous system and reduce anxiety. It is traditionally used to promote relaxation and improve sleep quality. Its mild calming effects make it suitable for mild stress and nervous tension.
