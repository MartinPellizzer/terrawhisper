Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Garden Nasturtium is Nasturtium officinale, first described by Carl von Linné in 1756. It is also known by several synonyms, including Tropaeolum majus and Tropaeolum pentaphyllum. Common names such as watercress, garden nasturtium, and Indian cress are widely used. Regional and historical names vary, reflecting its traditional use in herbal medicine across different cultures.

Morphological Characteristics

Garden Nasturtium is an annual herb with a taproot system. Its stems are hairy and ascending, often branching. Leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges, arranged alternately. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers, typically with five petals. The fruit is a capsule containing numerous small, round seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Garden Nasturtium can be distinguished by its bright yellow or orange flowers with a prominent central pistil, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and rounded, glossy seed pods. It differs from similar species like Tropaeolum majus by having smaller, more rounded leaves and a more compact growth habit. Its strong, peppery scent when crushed also helps in identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Garden Nasturtium is native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa. It thrives in temperate climates with full sun. It prefers well-drained, fertile soil and grows best at altitudes between 0 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Garden Nasturtium blooms from late spring to early fall, typically from June to September. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early autumn, around August to October. For identification purposes, harvest timing is critical during flowering and seed development stages to ensure accurate species recognition.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Nasturtium officinale belongs to the Tropaeolaceae family. It is closely related to other members of the genus Tropaeolum. Its phylogenetic relationships are best understood through shared morphological and genetic traits with other flowering plants.
