Garden Nasturtium (Nasturtium officinale) Uses

Garden Nasturtium is used to treat respiratory infections, including influenza, sinusitis, and bronchitis. It helps alleviate cold symptoms, cough, and mucous membrane inflammation. Its properties reduce sinus congestion and soothe irritation in the respiratory tract.

Garden Nasturtium (Nasturtium officinale) Monograph

1. Respiratory infections

Garden Nasturtium (Nasturtium officinale) is used to support respiratory health by reducing inflammation and fighting infections. Its essential oils and leaves contain compounds with antimicrobial and expectorant properties. It can help alleviate symptoms of colds, bronchitis, and sinusitis when used as a gargle or inhalation.

2. Influenza

Garden Nasturtium (Nasturtium officinale) has been traditionally used to support respiratory health during influenza. Its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties may help reduce symptom severity and duration. It is often prepared as a tincture or tea to aid in clearing congestion and boosting immune response.

3. Sinusitis

Garden Nasturtium (Nasturtium officinale) is used externally for sinusitis due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It can be applied as a nasal spray made from its leaves and flowers, helping to reduce congestion and combat infection. Its ability to support respiratory health makes it a valuable herb in herbal medicine for managing sinus conditions.

4. Cold symptoms

Garden Nasturtium is used to alleviate cold symptoms due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It can help reduce congestion and soothe sore throats when consumed as a tea or tincture. Its expectorant qualities aid in clearing mucus, supporting respiratory health during colds.

5. Bronchitis

Garden Nasturtium (Nasturtium officinale) has been used traditionally to support respiratory health. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties may help alleviate symptoms of bronchitis by reducing mucus production and soothing irritated airways. It is often prepared as a tincture or tea to ease coughing and improve breathing.

6. Allergic rhinitis

Garden Nasturtium (Nasturtium officinale) has been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis. Its anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties help reduce nasal congestion and sneezing. It can be taken as a tincture or infused oil, and is often combined with other herbs for enhanced effectiveness.

7. Cough

Garden Nasturtium (Nasturtium officinale) is used to support respiratory health, particularly for coughs. Its expectorant properties help loosen mucus, making it easier to expel. It may also have antimicrobial effects, reducing inflammation and soothing irritated airways. It is often used in herbal teas or tinctures for its mild, refreshing taste and therapeutic benefits.

8. Mucous membrane inflammation

Garden Nasturtium (Nasturtium officinale) is used to support mucous membrane inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps soothe irritation in the respiratory and urinary tracts, often prepared as a tincture or infused water. Its active compounds reduce swelling and promote healing in inflamed tissues.

9. Mucous membrane irritation

Mucous membrane irritation from Garden Nasturtium is typically caused by its high concentration of volatile oils and alkaloids. It can lead to burning sensations in the mouth, throat, or digestive tract. Prolonged contact may cause inflammation. Use with caution, especially in sensitive individuals.

10. Sinus congestion

Garden Nasturtium (Nasturtium officinale) is used to alleviate sinus congestion due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce mucus production and soothe nasal passages, often prepared as a tincture or inhalation. Its active compounds support respiratory health and can be a valuable remedy in herbal medicine for sinus issues.
