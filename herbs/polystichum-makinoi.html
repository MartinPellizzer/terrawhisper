      What are the herbal preparations of Polystichum makinoi?

      Polystichum makinoi is prepared primarily as a decoction made by boiling the dried rhizomes or leaves in water to extract active compounds. This liquid extract is often consumed as a warm tea to utilize its medicinal properties. Some traditional practitioners also crush the fresh foliage into a topical poultice for direct application to the skin. Additionally, the dried plant material can be finely ground into a powder to be encapsulated or mixed into food. These various methods allow for both internal consumption and external therapeutic use.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Polystichum makinoi can cause localized skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and mild gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Some individuals may experience redness or itching upon direct physical contact with the fronds. These dermatological reactions often occur due to the presence of specific organic compounds within the plant's structure. If the plant is consumed accidentally, it may lead to nausea or stomach cramps. It is important to handle the specimen with caution to avoid these adverse physical responses.
