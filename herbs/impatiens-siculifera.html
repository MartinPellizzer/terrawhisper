Impatiens siculifera

What's the taxonomical classification of Impatiens siculifera?

Impatiens siculifera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further organized into the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Ericales. This plant is a member of the family Balsaminaceae, which defines its broader botanical group. Finally, its specific taxonomic identification is established through the genus Impatiens and the unique species name siculifera.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, inflammatory conditions, and various topical wounds. Its medicinal properties are primarily utilized to alleviate symptoms of eczema and psoriasis through its soothing effects. The plant contains compounds that help reduce swelling and redness in localized areas of the body. It is often applied externally to promote the healing of minor cuts and abrasions. Traditional practitioners rely on its antimicrobial characteristics to manage localized bacterial issues.
