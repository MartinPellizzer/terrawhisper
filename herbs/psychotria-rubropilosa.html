Psychotria rubropilosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Psychotria rubropilosa has woody stems and leaves characterized by a dense covering of bright red hairs on their undersides. The foliage consists of elliptical or oblong blades with prominent venation patterns visible through the pubescence. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves in clusters. These floral structures eventually give rise to fleshy fruits that serve as a key identifying feature of the species. The distinctive red pilosity on the abaxial leaf surface distinguishes this plant from many of its closely related relatives in the Rubiaceae family.
