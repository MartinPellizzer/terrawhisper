Cremanthodium humile

What's the taxonomical classification of Cremanthodium humile?

Cremanthodium humile belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further organized into the order Asterales. It is a member of the family Asteraceae, falling under the genus Cremanthodium, and is specifically identified by the species name humile.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Cremanthodium humile can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, including vomiting and diarrhea, if ingested due to its toxic chemical properties. The plant contains various secondary metabolites that may irritate the mucous membranes of the digestive tract. Consuming even small amounts can lead to systemic toxicity and significant physical discomfort. These adverse reactions occur because the plant's alkaloids and other compounds interfere with normal bodily functions. It is essential to avoid any internal use of this species to prevent potential poisoning.
