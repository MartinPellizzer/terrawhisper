Polygonum paleaceum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polygonum paleaceum has prostrate or ascending stems that are reddish or brownish and bear lanceolate to linear leaves with entire margins. The leaves are typically arranged alternately along the slender branches and possess small, pointed tips. Tiny, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in axillary or terminal racemes, displaying a pale pink to white coloration. These blossoms are characterized by their delicate tepals that lack distinct petals or sepals. Small, dry fruits known as achenes develop from the flowers and are often enclosed by persistent tepal lobes.

These bioactive compounds work by inhibiting the enzymatic activity of cyclooxygenase, which prevents the synthesis of prostaglandins responsible for pain and swelling. What are the therapeutic actions of Polygonum paleaceum?

Polygonum paleaceum has therapeutic actions that include being bitter, astringent, hemostatic, and diaphoric. As a hemostatic agent, the plant can effectively help to slow or stop internal and external bleeding. Furthermore, its diaphoric action promotes sweating to assist in regulating body temperature during a fever.

    What are the herbal preparations of Polygonum paleaceum?

    Polygonum paleaceum is prepared as decoctions, infusions, or tinctures to extract its medicinal properties. Practitioners typically boil the dried aerial parts in water to create a concentrated decoction for internal use.
