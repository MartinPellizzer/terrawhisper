Syringa oblata

What's the taxonomical classification of Syringa oblata?

Syringa oblata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further categorized under the order Lamiales. It is a prominent representative of the family Oleaceae, falling under the genus Syringa, with the specific designation of the species oblata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Syringa oblata has opposite, elliptic to ovate leaves that measure approximately 3 to 7 centimeters in length and possess entire margins with a slightly revolute edge. The foliage typically exhibits a dark green upper surface and a paler underside, often showing a smooth or slightly pubescent texture. Its inflorescences consist of dense, terminal panicles that bear small, tubular flowers which can range in color from white to pale lilac. The stems are woody and can develop a grayish bark as the shrub matures into its perennial form. These botanical features collectively define the compact, shrubby habit of this specific lilac species.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of central and western China, specifically within the provinces of Sichuan, Gansu, and Shaanxi. It thrives in high-altitude environments where it typically inhabits rocky slopes and forest margins. The species is often found at elevations ranging from several hundred to over two thousand meters above sea level. Its distribution is largely influenced by the temperate, montane climates characteristic of these specific Asian highlands. These localized habitats provide the necessary moisture and soil conditions required for its survival.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant, Syringa oblata, requires well-drained, loamy soil and full sun exposure to achieve optimal flowering and healthy growth.

It thrives best when planted in zones 5 through 8, where it can tolerate moderate seasonal temperature fluctuations. Regular watering is essential during the establishment phase, though the mature shrub becomes relatively drought-tolerant once its root system is deep. Pruning should be performed immediately after the spring bloom to encourage new wood production for the following year.

What are the pharmacological activities of Syringa oblata?

This plant has antibacterial, anti-biofilm, anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and therapeutic properties for treating angina. Its bioactive compounds actively inhibit the growth of harmful bacteria and prevent the formation of protective biofilms that shield pathogens. The plant also demonstrates significant antioxidant capacity by neutralizing free radicals and reducing oxidative stress within cells. Additionally, its potent anti-inflammatory effects help mitigate swelling and immune responses in various tissues. These combined pharmacological activities make it a versatile candidate for managing both infectious diseases and cardiovascular conditions like angina.

According to a study published by "BMC plant biology", Syringa oblata Lindl. is an effective anti-biofilm medicinal plant. The main bioactive ingredient in S. oblata is the flavonoid rutin, which inhibits Streptococcus suis biofilm formation. Research shows that rutin content increases significantly under 50% ~ 60% (Z1) light intensity compared to natural (Z0) and 25% ~ 35% (Z2) light intensities. Analysis of the Z0 and Z1 light intensity stress conditions revealed that 13 putative structural genes and a transcription factor bHLH were significantly up-regulated in Z1. Specifically, 3 putative structural genes including 4CL1, CYP73A and CYP75B1 significantly up-regulated rutin biosynthesis.

Understanding these specific molecules helps researchers evaluate the therapeutic potential of Syringa oblata.

According to a study published by "RSC advances", Syringa oblata Lindl. is a medicinal plant possessing effective broad-spectrum antibacterial activity. The plant contains medicinal compounds including rutin and flavonoids. To enhance efficacy, the content of rutin was optimized through the process of stir-frying S. oblata with vinegar using response surface methodology. This specific processing method was evaluated for its ability to inhibit the biofilm formation of Staphylococcus xylosus. The study focused on how these medicinal materials influence performance and inhibit the biosynthesis of histidine.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating Khii, inflammation, pain, insomnia, and upset stomach. Its traditional medicinal properties help to balance internal energies and soothe bodily discomfort. The botanical extracts are often applied to reduce swelling and alleviate physical aches. Additionally, the plant is utilized to calm the nervous system to help with sleep disturbances. It also serves as a remedy to settle digestive issues and gastrointestinal upset.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", the stripped roots and stems of Syringa oblata Lindl. are used in Mongolian and Tibetan folk medicine for pain relief, heat clearing, and against "Khii". This plant is used to treat cardiovascular diseases, upset, insomnia, and other symptoms. Research indicates that Syringa oblata exerts cardioprotective effects against acute myocardial ischemia. At concentrations of 40-160 mg/kg, it has been shown to mitigate myocardial infarction by reducing serum levels of CK-MB, LDH, and cTnI. Furthermore, the plant at 1.25-10 μg/mL significantly inhibited nitrogen monoxi de production, with 10 μg/mL strongly suppressing pyroptosis in RAW264.7 cells.
