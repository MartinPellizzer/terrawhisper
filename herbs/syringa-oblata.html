Syringa oblata

What's the taxonomical classification of Syringa oblata?

Syringa oblata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further categorized under the order Lamiales. It is a prominent representative of the family Oleaceae, falling under the genus Syringa, with the specific designation of the species oblata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Syringa oblata has opposite, elliptic to ovate leaves that measure approximately 3 to 7 centimeters in length and possess entire margins with a slightly revolute edge. The foliage typically exhibits a dark green upper surface and a paler underside, often showing a smooth or slightly pubescent texture. Its inflorescences consist of dense, terminal panicles that bear small, tubular flowers which can range in color from white to pale lilac. The stems are woody and can develop a grayish bark as the shrub matures into its perennial form. These botanical features collectively define the compact, shrubby habit of this specific lilac species.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of central and western China, specifically within the provinces of Sichuan, Gansu, and Shaanxi. It thrives in high-altitude environments where it typically inhabits rocky slopes and forest margins. The species is often found at elevations ranging from several hundred to over two thousand meters above sea level. Its distribution is largely influenced by the temperate, montane climates characteristic of these specific Asian highlands. These localized habitats provide the necessary moisture and soil conditions required for its survival.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse array of bioactive secondary metabolites including flavonoids, rutin, and various sesquiterpene derivatives. The presence of rutin, a specific flavonoid, contributes to the plant's antioxidant and vascular-protective properties. Chemical analysis further reveals the existence of an elemane-type sesquiterpene within its complex molecular structure. What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Syringa oblata targets various inflammatory mediators and oxidative stress enzymes, specifically inhibiting cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2) and lipoxygenase (LOX) to reduce prostaglandin synthesis. The plant's rich phenolic compounds and lignans interact with nuclear factor kappa B (NF-κB) signaling pathways to suppress the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines. Furthermore, its bioactive constituents modulate superoxide dismutase (SOD) and catalase activities to enhance cellular antioxidant defenses. These molecular interactions contribute to the plant's observed anti-inflammatory, neuroprotective, and antioxidant pharmacological effects. By regulating these specific protein pathways, the extracts provide systemic protection against oxidative damage and chronic inflammation.

What are the therapeutic actions of Syringa oblata?

Syringa oblata has therapeutic properties that function as an astringent, a tonic, and a diuretic. The plant's astringent qualities help to constrict body tissues and reduce inflammation in various membranes. As a tonic, it works to restore vigor and support the general health of the bodily systems. Its diuretic action promotes the increased excretion of urine to help flush toxins from the kidneys. These combined medicinal effects make the species useful in traditional herbal formulations.

What health problems are treated using this plant?

This plant is used to treat infections caused by Staphylococcus xylosus and the inhibition of Streptococcus suis biofilm formation, as well as physical pain and digestive upset. The medicinal efficacy of Syringa oblata is largely attributed to the presence of the active compound Khii. By targeting specific bacterial growth, the plant helps manage various inflammatory and microbial issues. It is particularly noted for its ability to alleviate discomfort in the body and soothe an upset stomach. This combination of properties makes it a versatile resource in traditional medicine for addressing both infectious and symptomatic ailments.
