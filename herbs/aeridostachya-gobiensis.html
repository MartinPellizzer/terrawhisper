What is Aeridostachya gobiensis?

Aeridostachya gobiensis is a species of orchid that belongs to the family Orchidaceae.

It is native to the Gobi Desert in Asia and is characterized by its small, white flowers and narrow, elliptical leaves that are 5-7 cm in length. The plant grows to a height of 10-20 cm, with a single stem or inflorescence bearing one to several flowers. Aeridostachya gobiensis has a sympodial growth habit, with new growths emerging from the base of the plant, and its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb water from the dry desert soil.

The species is currently listed as Endangered on the IUCN Red List due to its limited distribution and habitat degradation.
