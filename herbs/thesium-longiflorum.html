Thesium longiflorum

What's the taxonomical classification of Thesium longiflorum?

Thesium longiflorum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls into the subclass Magnoliidae and is a member of the order Santalales. Furthermore, this plant is positioned within the family Santalaceae, specifically under the genus Thesium, with the specific epithet longiflorum identifying the individual species.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Thesium longiflorum has slender, erect stems that reach heights of up to 50 centimeters and are characterized by small, linear, or lanceolate leaves arranged oppositely along the branches. The foliage is typically thin and lacks significant width, which contributes to the plant's delicate and airy appearance. Its most defining morphological feature is the production of small, tubular, white to cream-colored flowers that emerge from the axils of the leaves. These flowers are typically solitary or occur in small clusters, often featuring a distinctively elongated shape.
