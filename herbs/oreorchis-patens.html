Oreorchis patens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. The species is further organized under the order Asparagales and falls within the family Orchidaceae. Ultimately, its specific classification is defined by the genus Oreorchis and the species name patens.

What are the pharmacological activities of Oreorchis patens?

When administered, these substances work to inhibit the production of inflammatory mediators in the body. This mechanism can potentially alleviate discomfort associated with chronic inflammatory conditions. Continued research into these medicinal qualities may lead to new therapeutic applications for the species.

As discussed in a study, metabolomic investigation of the edible parts of Orchis patens led to the identification of 6 phenanthrene derivatives, consisting of 2 monomers and 4 dimers. Among these identified compounds, phenanthrene dimer 3 exhibited notable anti-inflammatory activity in lipopolysaccharide-stimulated murine alveolar (MH-S) macrophages. Compound 3 was found to stably bind to the allosteric drug and metabolite site of adenosine monophosphate-activated protein kinase (AMPK). This binding protects the kinase from dephosphorylation and promotes its activation. Through this process, activated AMPK regulates NF-κB signaling to produce pronounced anti-inflammatory effects.
