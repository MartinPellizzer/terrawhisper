What is Pelargonium radiatum?

Pelargonium radiatum is a species of succulent plant that belongs to the family Geraniaceae.

It is a perennial herb with a prostrate or ascending growth habit, typically reaching heights of 10-40 cm. The leaves are oppositely arranged, 2-6 cm long, and have a distinctive shape, with 3-5 lobes that are often deeply divided. The inflorescence is a terminal cyme, bearing small, tubular flowers that are 5-7 mm long and have a yellow or orange color with red stripes.

The fruit is a 5-6 mm long capsule containing numerous small seeds.
