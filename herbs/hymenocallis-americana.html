Hymenocallis americana

What's the taxonomical classification of Hymenocallis americana?

Hymenocallis americana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Asparagales and is a member of the family Amaryllidaceae. Specifically, it is identified by the genus Hymenocallis, with its unique designation being the species americana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hymenocallis americana has long, strap-like green leaves that grow in a basal rosette and large, fragrant white flowers featuring six tepals with a central trumpet-shaped tube. Each flower displays delicate, narrow segments that flare outward from the central corona. The plant typically grows in clumps, with the foliage reaching heights of several decimeters. Its inflorescence is an umbel-like cluster supported by a sturdy, leafless scape. The overall structure is characterized by its elegant, lily-like appearance and fleshy, succulent-like texture.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires well-draining soil, partial shade to full sun, and consistent moisture to thrive in garden settings.

It is a perennial bulb that performs best in USDA hardiness zones 9 through 11. Growers should plant the bulbs about four inches deep during the spring to ensure successful establishment. During the summer, regular watering is essential to support its large, trumpet-shaped white flowers.

In colder climates, the bulbs must be lifted from the ground and stored in a cool, dry place to prevent rot during winter.
