Botanical Identification of Maca (Lepidium meyenii)

To identify Maca (Lepidium meyenii) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Maca, scientifically known as Lepidium meyenii, belongs to the plant kingdom Plantae. It is classified under the division Spermatophyta, class Caryophyllopsida, order Brassicales, family Brassicaceae, genus Lepidium, and species Lepidium meyenii. This classification reflects its botanical relationships and characteristics within the plant kingdom.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Maca is Lepidium meyenii, a member of the Brassicaceae family. It was first described by the German botanist Friedrich von Heyne in 1820. The name reflects its classification within the genus Lepidium, which includes other species like mustard and wallflower.

Morphological Characteristics

Maca (Lepidium meyenii) is a biennial plant native to the Andes. It has a thick, fleshy root system that stores nutrients. The stem is short and stout, with a rough, hairy texture. Leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and covered in a waxy cuticle. The inflorescence is a raceme of small, yellow flowers. The fruit is a short capsule containing several seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Maca (Lepidium meyenii) can be distinguished by its thick, fleshy taproot, which is typically yellow to orange in color. It has basal leaves that are spoon-shaped with hairy margins, and its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in racemes. These features help differentiate it from other Lepidium species.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Maca is native to the high Andes of Peru and Bolivia. It thrives in cold, temperate climates with short growing seasons. It prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils. It is typically cultivated between 3,500 and 4,500 meters above sea level.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Maca (Lepidium meyenii) typically blooms between April and July in its native habitat. Seed formation occurs from late summer to early autumn. Harvest is usually conducted in late winter to early spring, just before the plant starts to flower, which is crucial for accurate identification.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Maca (Lepidium meyenii) is closely related to species within the genus Lepidium, such as Lepidium sativum and Lepidium latifolium. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, sharing characteristics like capsule fruits and siliques. These traits help distinguish it from other genera in the family.
