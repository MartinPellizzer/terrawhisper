10 Possible Side Effects Of Lepidium Meyenii

Lepidium meyenii has some side effects when used improperly, such as thyroid problems, which can cause hair loss, weight gain, and fatigue.

These issues can be caused by consuming excessive amounts of maca, which contains goitrogens that can interfere with thyroid function. If left untreated, these problems can worsen a person's quality of life, leading to depression, anxiety, and social isolation.

In severe cases, thyroid problems can also increase the risk of heart disease and stroke.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Lepidium meyenii causes allergic reactions due to its high content of allergenic proteins and lipids.

The plant's pollen and other parts contain a mixture of amino acids, sugars, and other compounds that can trigger an immune response in some individuals. This may lead to symptoms such as itching, hives, and difficulty breathing.

The exact mechanisms behind maca-induced allergies are not fully understood, but it is thought to be related to the plant's unique chemical composition.

2. Triggers anxiety attacks

Lepidium meyenii triggers anxiety attacks in some individuals due to its potential interactions with serotonin levels in the brain.

Maca contains high amounts of tryptophan, an amino acid that is converted into serotonin, a neurotransmitter responsible for regulating mood and emotions. This increase in serotonin can lead to feelings of anxiety, panic, or unease in susceptible individuals, potentially causing anxiety attacks.

Additionally, maca may also affect the body's natural balance of GABA, a neurotransmitter involved in calming and relaxing effects, further contributing to increased anxiety levels.

3. Induces bronchospasm

Lepidium meyenii induces bronchospasm due to its high content of glucosinolates, which can break down into isothiocyanates during digestion.

These compounds have been shown to cause bronchoconstriction by triggering the release of histamine from mast cells and increasing mucus production in the lungs. Additionally, maca's potential allergenic properties may also contribute to its bronchospasm-inducing effects.

As a result, individuals with pre-existing respiratory conditions may experience increased symptoms when using Lepidium meyenii.

4. Increases blood pressure

Lepidium meyenii increases blood pressure due to its potential to stimulate adrenal glands and enhance cortisol production.

As a result, it may cause an increase in blood pressure in individuals who are already experiencing hypertension or those with prehypertension.

Additionally, the plant's ability to dilate blood vessels may contribute to this effect, further exacerbating high blood pressure in some users.

5. Alters cardiac rhythms

Lepidium meyenii alters cardiac rhythms due to its potential effect on the cardiovascular system.

The plant's ability to stimulate the thyroid gland and increase sympathetic nervous activity may lead to changes in heart rate and blood pressure, potentially altering normal cardiac rhythms.

Additionally, maca's influence on hormone regulation may contribute to these effects by impacting the body's natural balance of adrenal hormones, further affecting cardiac function.

6. Enhances bleeding risk

Lepidium meyenii enhances bleeding risk due to its ability to thin the blood and lower blood pressure.

The compound macamides found in Lepidium meyenii have been shown to inhibit platelet aggregation, leading to a decrease in clotting ability. Additionally, the herb's vasodilatory properties can cause blood vessels to dilate, further increasing the risk of bleeding.

This may be particularly concerning for individuals taking anticoagulant medications or those with pre-existing bleeding disorders.

7. Raises liver enzymes

Lepidium meyenii raises liver enzymes due to its ability to stimulate the production of certain enzymes that aid in detoxification.

This is attributed to the presence of polyphenolic compounds and glucosinolates, which are naturally occurring chemicals found in the plant.

As a result, individuals may experience an increase in liver enzyme activity, potentially affecting liver function and metabolism.

8. Worsens kidney function

Lepidium meyenii worsens kidney function due to its high content of oxalates and saponins.

These compounds can increase the risk of kidney stone formation and exacerbate existing kidney damage, potentially leading to decreased renal function.

Additionally, the plant's diuretic properties may further strain the kidneys by increasing urine production, putting additional pressure on these vital organs.

9. Affects mental clarity

Lepidium meyenii affects mental clarity.

This is often due to its ability to increase blood flow to the brain, which can lead to feelings of alertness and improved focus. However, this increased blood flow can sometimes cause an overstimulation of the nervous system, resulting in difficulties concentrating or making decisions.

Additionally, some individuals may experience vivid dreams or insomnia as a result of maca's effects on the brain's neurotransmitters.

10. Reduces fertility

Lepidium meyenii reduces fertility in some individuals due to its ability to alter hormone levels and disrupt the balance of estrogen and testosterone.

This can lead to changes in ovulation patterns and sperm quality, making it more difficult for couples to conceive. The exact mechanisms behind this effect are not fully understood, but it is thought that maca's phytohormones interact with the body's endocrine system, influencing reproductive function.

As a result, some users of Lepidium meyenii may experience reduced fertility as a side effect.
