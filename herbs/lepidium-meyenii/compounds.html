Maca (Lepidium meyenii) Compounds

The primary compounds in Maca include flavonoids, which are antioxidant and anti-inflammatory. Alkaloids contribute to its stimulating effects, while phenolic acids offer antioxidant properties. Glycosides, tannins, and saponins support various biological activities. Terpenoids, coumarins, essential oils, and anthocyanins further contribute to its medicinal and nutritional profile.

Maca (Lepidium meyenii) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Maca (Lepidium meyenii). These compounds contribute to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting its traditional use in enhancing energy and cognitive function. They are believed to interact with cellular pathways that may improve mitochondrial efficiency and overall metabolic health.

2. Alkaloid

Alkaloids in Maca (Lepidium meyenii) are a class of nitrogen-containing compounds that contribute to its pharmacological effects. These compounds may influence energy metabolism, mood, and hormonal balance, supporting Maca's traditional use in enhancing vitality and cognitive function.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Maca (Lepidium meyenii), contributing to its antioxidant properties. It helps neutralize free radicals, supporting cellular health and potentially enhancing energy metabolism. This compound is among several bioactive substances found in Maca that are studied for their role in traditional herbal medicine.

4. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Maca (Lepidium meyenii). It contributes to the plant's adaptogenic properties, supporting energy, endurance, and hormonal balance. Glycosides in Maca are believed to interact with the endocrine system, enhancing physiological resilience. These compounds are central to Maca's traditional and modern use in herbal medicine.

5. Tannin

Tannin is a compound found in Maca (Lepidium meyenii). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health. Tannins in Maca are believed to aid in nutrient absorption and have antioxidant effects. They are part of the complex phytochemical profile that contributes to Maca's traditional use in herbal medicine.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Maca (Lepidium meyenii). It contributes to the plant's purported adaptogenic properties, potentially supporting energy and hormonal balance. Saponins may enhance nutrient absorption and have mild anti-inflammatory effects. Their presence is linked to many of Maca's traditional uses in Andean medicine.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Maca (Lepidium meyenii), contributing to its adaptogenic properties. These compounds help regulate physiological functions and may enhance energy, mood, and cognitive performance. They are among the key bioactive components responsible for Maca's traditional use in herbal medicine.

8. Coumarin

Coumarin is a bioactive compound found in Maca (Lepidium meyenii). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in enhancing energy and stamina. Coumarin may also have antioxidant properties that support overall health. Its presence in Maca highlights the plant's complex pharmacological profile.

9. Essential oil

Essential oil is a volatile compound found in Maca (Lepidium meyenii). It contributes to the plant's aromatic profile and may play a role in its traditional use for enhancing energy and libido. Research suggests it contains bioactive components that support overall vitality and hormonal balance.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanins are pigments found in Maca (Lepidium meyenii) that contribute to its distinctive color. These compounds are believed to support cognitive function and energy levels. Research suggests they may also have antioxidant properties. Anthocyanins are part of the bioactive components in Maca that contribute to its traditional use in herbal medicine.
