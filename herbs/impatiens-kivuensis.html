Impatiens kivuensis

Impatiens kivuensis, also known as Kivu jewelweed, is a plant species belonging to the Balsam family (Balsamaceae) and native to the Democratic Republic of Congo.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, including its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antiviral properties, which are attributed to the presence of various alkaloids and flavonoids.

The health benefits of Impatiens kivuensis include its ability to reduce inflammation, treat skin conditions, and provide relief from itching and pain.

Herbal preparations of the plant are typically made by infusing the leaves or stems in water to create a tea or tincture, which can be used topically or taken internally to reap its medicinal benefits.
