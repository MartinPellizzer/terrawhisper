Hedyotis tetrangularis

What's the taxonomical classification of Hedyotis tetrangularis?

Hedyotis tetrangularis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It falls under the order Gentianales and is a member of the family Rubiaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific classification within the genus Hedyotis, resulting in its scientific name, Hedyotis tetrangularis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hedyotis tetrangularis has a woody, climbing shrub habit characterized by slender stems and opposite, elliptic to lanceolate leaves with entire margins.

The foliage typically exhibits a smooth texture and a deep green color on the upper surface. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often appearing in clusters. The plant produces distinctively shaped fruits that serve as a key identification feature for the species.

These botanical structures collectively define its growth pattern within its natural habitat.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Hedyotis tetrangularis can cause skin irritation, redness, and localized swelling upon direct contact with the plant's tissues. These dermatological reactions often occur because the plant contains specific chemical compounds that act as irritants to human skin. Some individuals may experience a delayed hypersensitivity reaction, resulting in itching or a rash that persists for several days. If the plant's sap or extracts are accidentally ingested, it may lead to gastrointestinal distress or more severe internal complications. Because the exact toxicity levels can vary between individual plants, caution is necessary when handling this species.
