Pulsatilla cernea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It falls under the order Ranunculales and is a member of the family Ranunculaceae. At the most specific levels of its biological classification, it is identified by the genus Pulsatilla and the species cernea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pulsatilla cernua has deeply divided, pinnatifid leaves that emerge from a basal rosette and are covered in dense, silvery hairs. These fine hairs provide a soft, velvety texture to the foliage and help protect the plant from moisture loss. The plant produces solitary, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. Each bloom is characterized by numerous prominent stamens that surround the central pistils. After flowering, the plant develops feathery, achene-bearing seed heads that remain upright on the stem.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds Cernuoside A, Cernuoside B, triterpenoid saponins, and β-amyrin. These specific constituents contribute to the diverse pharmacological activities associated with the species. Cernuoside A and Cernuoside B are distinctive chemical markers often studied for their unique biological properties. Additionally, the presence of triterpenoid saponins plays a significant role in the plant's overall chemical profile. The compound β-amyrin further enhances the medicinal potential of the plant's various extracts.

As discussed in a study, two oleanane-type oligoglycosides known as cernuoside A (1) and cernuoside B (2) were isolated from the roots of Pulsatilla cernua. These two specific medicinal compounds function as sucrase inhibitors. The identification of these compounds was achieved through the use of 1D and 2D NMR methods, which included DQF-COSY, TOCSY, HMQC, HMBC, and ROESY. Additionally, FABMS and hydrolysis were employed during the structure elucidation process. Both cernuoside A and cernuoside B demonstrated moderate activity against sucrase.
