Bupleurum yunnanense

What's the taxonomical classification of Bupleurum yunnanense?

Bupleurum yunnanense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Apiales. This plant is a specific representative of the family Apiaceae, placed within the genus Bupleurum, and is ultimately identified by its unique species designation, yunnanense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bupleurum yunnanense has perennial herbaceous stems that typically reach heights of 20 to 50 centimeters and bear pinnately divided leaves with serrated margins. The foliage consists of narrow, linear segments that emerge from a basal rosette and extend along the stem. Its inflorescence is composed of small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in compound umbels. Each individual floret is diminutive and tucked within a structure of bracts that surround the flowering cluster. The root system is characterized by a thickened woody rhizome that aids in perennial survival.
