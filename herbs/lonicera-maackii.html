Lonicera maackii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lonicera maackii has opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape with entire margins and a blunt or rounded apex. The foliage is typically dark green on the upper surface and can be slightly paler underneath. Its woody stems exhibit a decumbent or ascending growth habit, often forming low-growing shrubs. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils, usually appearing in pairs or small clusters. These blossoms eventually develop into small, fleshy drupes that change color as they mature.
