Viper's bugloss, scientifically known as Echium plantagineum, is a herb that boasts an array of health properties, including antioxidant and anti-inflammatory compounds that have been shown to improve cardiovascular health and reduce the risk of chronic diseases.

In the culinary world, viper's bugloss is often used to add flavor and texture to soups, salads, and sauces, particularly in Mediterranean and Middle Eastern cuisine. From a horticultural perspective, viper's bugloss is a low-maintenance perennial that thrives in dry conditions and requires minimal care, making it a popular choice for gardeners. Botanically speaking, the herb belongs to the Boraginaceae family and features distinctive blue flowers with four petals.

Historically, viper's bugloss has been referenced as a treatment for various ailments, including fever and respiratory issues, dating back to ancient Greek and Roman medicine.
