What's the taxonomical classification of Aster alatipes?

Aster alatipes belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. This plant is a representative of the family Asteraceae, placed within the genus Aster, and is specifically identified by its species name, alatipes.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aster alatipes has linear to lanceolate leaves that are spreading or reflexed and flower heads with purple to bluish-lavender ray florets. The plant is characterized by its relatively slender stems and smaller stature compared to other species in the genus. Its leaves are typically arranged alternately along the stem and possess finely serrated margins. The involucral bracts are arranged in multiple series and help protect the developing florets. Each flower head contains a central disk of yellow tubular florets surrounded by the colorful ray florets.
