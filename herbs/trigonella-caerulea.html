Trigonella caerulea

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Trigonella caerulea has an erect, herbaceous growth habit characterized by compound, pinnately divided leaves and small, bluish-white flowers arranged in racemes. The leaves consist of several pairs of oblong to lanceolate leaflets that emerge from a central petiole. Its stems are typically slender and can reach moderate heights depending on environmental conditions. The inflorescence features delicate petals that provide a subtle contrast to the green foliage. Small, cylindrical pods develop from the flowers and eventually contain the plant's seeds.

What are the therapeutic actions of Trigonella caerulea?

Trigonella caerulea has therapeutic actions including bitter, carminative, galactagogue, digestive stimulant, and anti-inflammatory properties.
