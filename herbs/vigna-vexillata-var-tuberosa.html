Possible Side Effects of Vigna vexillata var. tuberosa

Vigna vexillata var. tuberosa can have side effects if used improperly, such as inducing allergic reactions, gastrointestinal issues, and skin irritation in some individuals.

Consuming large amounts of the plant's extract can cause nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea.

Additionally, the plant's high saponin content can interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners, and exacerbate underlying health conditions.

In severe cases, improper use of Vigna vexillata var. tuberosa can lead to kidney damage, respiratory problems, and even anaphylaxis.
