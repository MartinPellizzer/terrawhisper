Cotoneaster frigidus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cotoneaster frigidus has an erect, woody shrub habit characterized by slender, spreading branches and small, leathery, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and often grayish beneath. The foliage typically measures between 10 and 20 millimeters in length and possesses finely serrated margins. During the flowering season, small, white, five-petaled blossoms emerge from the leaf axils in clusters. These flowers eventually give way to small, globose, red pomes that persist on the stems through the winter months. The overall texture of the plant is dense and compact, making it well-suited for ground cover or ornamental hedging.
