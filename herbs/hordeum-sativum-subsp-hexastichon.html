Hordeum sativum subsp. hexastichon

Hordeum sativum subsp.

hexastichon, also known as six-rowed barley, is a subspecies of the barley plant that is characterized by its six rows of grains per spikelet.

This plant has been used for centuries in traditional medicine due to its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

The health benefits of six-rowed barley include reducing the risk of chronic diseases such as heart disease, diabetes, and certain types of cancer, as well as aiding in digestion and improving gut health.
