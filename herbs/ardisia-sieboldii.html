Ardisia sieboldii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Ericales, the plant is placed into the family Primulaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Ardisia and the species sieboldii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ardisia sieboldii has evergreen leaves that are elliptical or oblong in shape, measuring approximately 5 to 15 centimeters in length with smooth, entire margins and a glossy, dark green upper surface. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, typically presenting as white or cream-colored corollas. These flowers eventually develop into small, fleshy drupes that change color as they mature. The overall growth habit is dense, providing a compact foliage structure in its natural habitat.

What are the pharmacological activities of Ardisia sieboldii?

This plant has pharmacological activities including anti-inflammatory, cytotoxic, and 5-lipoxygenase inhibitor properties. These biological effects are primarily attributed to the presence of specific bioactive secondary metabolites found within its tissues. The inhibition of the 5-lipoxygenase enzyme helps regulate the production of leukotrienes, which are key mediators in inflammatory responses. Furthermore, the plant demonstrates cytotoxic potential that may be useful in evaluating its effectiveness against various cancer cell lines. Research into these mechanisms continues to explore how the chemical constituents of Ardisia sieboldii can be utilized for therapeutic purposes.

According to a study published by "Zeitschrift fur Naturforschung. C, Journal of biosciences", the leaves of Ardisia sieboldii contain cytotoxic and anti-inflammatoy resorcinol and alkylbenzoquinone derivatives found in 70% methanol extracts. Three specific compounds, 2-methyl-5-(8Z-heptadecenyl) resorcinol (1), 5-(8Z-heptadecenyl) resorcinol (2), and ardisiaquinone A (3), showed cytotoxicity against a panel of cancer cell lines and brine shrimps in a dose-response manner. Compound 2 exhibited the highest cytotoxicity on cancer cells with IC50 values of 8.8-25.7 μM and on brine shrimps with an IC50 value of 5.1 μM. Compounds 1-3 also exhibited anti-inflammatoy effects by inhibiting protein denaturation with IC50 values of 5.8-9.6 μM and cyclooxygenase-2 activity with IC50 values of 34.5-60.1 μM. These activities also included the inhibition of nitrite formation in RAW 264.7 cells.
