Ardisia sieboldii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Ericales, the plant is placed into the family Primulaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Ardisia and the species sieboldii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ardisia sieboldii has evergreen leaves that are elliptical or oblong in shape, measuring approximately 5 to 15 centimeters in length with smooth, entire margins and a glossy, dark green upper surface. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, typically presenting as white or cream-colored corollas. These flowers eventually develop into small, fleshy drupes that change color as they mature. The overall growth habit is dense, providing a compact foliage structure in its natural habitat.

As discussed in a study, Ardisia sieboldii leaves contain diverse resorcinol and alkylbenzoquinone derivatives in cytotoxic 70% methanol extracts. Three specific compounds, 2-methyl-5-(8Z-heptadecenyl) resorcinol (1), 5-(8Z-heptadecenyl) resorcinol (2), and ardisiaquinone A (3), showed cytotoxicity against a panel of cancer cell lines and brine shrimps. Among these, compound 2 exhibited the highest cytotoxicity on cancer cells with IC50 values of 8.8-25.7 μM and on brine shrimps with an IC50 value of 5.1 μM. Additionally, compounds 1-3 exhibited anti-inflammatory effects by inhibiting protein denaturation with IC50 values of 5.8-9.6 μM and cyclooxygenase-2 activity with IC50 values of 34.5-60.1 μM. These activities demonstrate the potential of these compounds as candidates for developing anticancer drugs.
