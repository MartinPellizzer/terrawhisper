What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ardisia sieboldii has evergreen leaves that are elliptical or oblong in shape, measuring approximately 5 to 15 centimeters in length with smooth, entire margins and a glossy, dark green upper surface. The plant features a woody stem structure characteristic of shrubs or small trees within the Rubiaceae family. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, typically presenting as white or cream-colored corollas. These flowers eventually develop into small, fleshy drupes that change color as they mature. The overall growth habit is dense, providing a compact foliage structure in its natural habitat.
