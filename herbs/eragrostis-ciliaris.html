What's the taxonomical classification of Eragrostis ciliaris?

Eragrostis ciliaris belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Poales and is a member of the family Poaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic placement is defined by the genus Eragrostis and the species ciliaris.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eragrostis ciliaris has an annual growth habit characterized by slender, creeping stems that produce narrow, linear leaves and small, solitary spikelets.

The plant typically reaches a height of 10 to 40 centimeters, often forming dense mats in moist environments. Its leaves are typically flat or slightly channeled with a distinct midrib and fine, hair-like margins. The spikelets are small, containing a single floret enclosed by a lemma that features fine, ciliate hairs along the edges.

These reproductive structures are arranged in simple or branched panicles that emerge from the stem.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

While systemic toxicity is not widely documented, sensitive people might react to the plant's microscopic structures. It is advisable to wear protective clothing when working in areas where this species is prevalent. Monitoring for any skin irritation is recommended for those with known sensitivities to various grasses.
