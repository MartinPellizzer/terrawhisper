Shuteria involucrata

What's the taxonomical classification of Shuteria involucrata?

Shuteria involucrata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further organized under the order Fabales. It is a part of the family Fabaceae, a group known for its diverse botanical characteristics, and specifically falls under the genus Shuteria. Its complete scientific designation is identified at the species level as involucrata.

    What are the molecular targets of this plant?

    Shuteria involucrata targets various inflammatory mediators and enzymes including cyclooxygenase (COX) and lipoxygenase (LOX) to exert its medicinal effects. The bioactive compounds within the plant inhibit these enzymes to reduce the production of pro-inflammatory prostaglandins and leukotrienes. Additionally, secondary metabolites from the plant interact with nuclear factor kappa B (NF-κB) signaling pathways to modulate the immune response. These molecular interactions contribute to the plant's observed antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties in traditional medicinal applications. Through the modulation of these specific protein pathways, the plant effectively mitigates cellular oxidative stress and systemic inflammation.

    What are the therapeutic actions of Shuteria involucrata?

    Shuteria involucrata has anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and wound-healing properties that are utilized to treat various skin infections and internal ailments. The plant contains bioactive compounds such as flavonoids and tannins which assist in reducing swelling and pain. These phytochemicals also work to inhibit the growth of pathogenic bacteria and fungi on the skin. When applied topically, the extracts help accelerate tissue regeneration and close wounds more efficiently. Traditional practitioners often use decoctions of the plant to address gastrointestinal distress and other systemic issues.

    What diseases this plant treats?

    This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, diarrhea, and various types of inflammation. The leaves are often prepared into a paste or decoction to facilitate the healing of dermatological issues. Traditional medicinal practices also utilize its properties to soothe digestive disturbances and intestinal discomfort. Some cultures employ the plant to reduce swelling and manage symptoms associated with systemic fever. These therapeutic applications rely on the bioactive compounds found within the plant's tissues.

    What are the herbal preparations of Shuteria involucrata?

    Shuteria involucrata is prepared primarily as a decoction or an infusion made from its leaves and stems to treat various ailments. The plant material is typically boiled in water to extract its bioactive compounds for medicinal use. Some traditional practices also involve crushing the fresh plant into a paste for topical applications on the skin. This poultice can help manage localized inflammation or skin irritations. These different preparation methods allow the plant to be used both internally and externally depending on the specific health concern.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Shuteria involucrata can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested or handled improperly. While the plant is sometimes studied for medicinal potential, its specific toxicological profile in humans remains insufficiently documented in clinical literature. Contact with the plant's sap or leaves may trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Ingesting unknown quantities could lead to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain due to unverified chemical constituents. Users should exercise extreme caution and consult medical professionals before any application or consumption.
