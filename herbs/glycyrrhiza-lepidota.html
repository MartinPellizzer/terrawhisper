What to know about Glycyrrhiza lepidota (licorice) before using it medicinally

Licorice, scientifically known as Glycyrrhiza lepidota, is a herb that has been revered for its numerous health benefits, including reducing inflammation and soothing digestive issues.

In addition to its medicinal properties, licorice is also prized for its unique flavor and versatility in the culinary world, often used to add depth and sweetness to teas, desserts, and sauces. From a horticultural perspective, licorice is a warm-season perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal care and maintenance. Botanically speaking, licorice is an herbaceous plant with fern-like leaves and clusters of small yellow flowers.

With a history dating back thousands of years to ancient China, Greece, and Egypt, licorice has been a staple ingredient in traditional medicine and cuisine for centuries, earning its reputation as one of the most versatile and valuable herbs in the world.

What are the medicinal properties of Glycyrrhiza lepidota?

Glycyrrhiza lepidota has several medicinal properties, such as anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and antibacterial effects.

One of its most important properties is its ability to soothe and calm the digestive system, making it a popular ingredient in traditional Chinese medicine for treating gastrointestinal disorders, including ulcers, gastritis, and irritable bowel syndrome (IBS). The saponins present in Glycyrrhiza lepidota have been shown to exhibit anti-inflammatory activity by inhibiting the production of prostaglandins and leukotrienes, which are known to exacerbate inflammation.

Additionally, its antiviral properties make it effective against herpes simplex virus and human immunodeficiency virus (HIV). Furthermore, Glycyrrhiza lepidota has been found to exhibit antibacterial activity against a wide range of bacteria, including those responsible for respiratory infections such as Streptococcus pneumoniae and Haemophilus influenzae. Its ability to inhibit the growth of Candida albicans also makes it effective in treating fungal infections.

Overall, Glycyrrhiza lepidota is considered a valuable herb with numerous medicinal applications, particularly in the treatment of digestive disorders and infectious diseases.

What are the side effects of Glycyrrhiza lepidota when used improperly?

When used improperly, Glycyrrhiza lepidota increases the chances of experiencing side effects, such as kidney damage, high blood pressure, and electrolyte imbalances.

The herb contains compounds like glycyrrhizin, which can disrupt the body's natural balance of potassium, sodium, and other minerals when consumed in excess or for extended periods. This disruption can lead to symptoms like fatigue, muscle weakness, and irregular heartbeat.

Furthermore, Glycyrrhiza lepidota may interact with certain medications, such as diuretics, corticosteroids, and blood thinners, which can amplify its effects and increase the risk of adverse reactions. Additionally, using the herb for medicinal purposes without proper guidance or monitoring by a healthcare professional can lead to over-supplementation, causing symptoms like dizziness, headaches, and gastrointestinal disturbances.

In severe cases, long-term improper use of Glycyrrhiza lepidota may even exacerbate underlying medical conditions, such as hypertension, kidney disease, or heart failure, and potentially contribute to life-threatening complications.
