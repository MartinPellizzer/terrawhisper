Sweetroot

Glycyrrhiza lepidota

Sweetroot (Glycyrrhiza lepidota) is a flowering plant native to North America, known for its sweet, licorice-like flavor and traditional use in herbal medicine to support respiratory and digestive health.

Botanical Identification

Sweetroot (Glycyrrhiza lepidota) is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae. It is also known as sweetroot or sweetwood in some regions. This perennial plant is native to parts of East Asia, particularly China. It features a thick, sweet-tasting root, trifoliate leaves, and small, pea-like flowers. Synonyms in herbal literature include sweetwood and licorice root.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Fabales
  • Order: Fabales
  • Family: Fabaceae
  • Genus: Glycyrrhiza
  • Species: Glycyrrhiza lepidota

Safety Profile

Sweetroot, also known as Glycyrrhiza lepidota, has a safety profile that includes contraindications for long-term use due to its potential to increase blood pressure. It is important to consult a healthcare provider before use, especially for individuals with hypertension or those taking corticosteroids. Always follow recommended dosages to avoid adverse effects.
