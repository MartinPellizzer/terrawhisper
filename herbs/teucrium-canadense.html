Blue Germander

Teucrium canadense

Blue Germander (Teucrium canadense) is a flowering plant native to North America, commonly found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, known for its medicinal properties and historical use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments.

Botanical Identification

Teucrium canadense, commonly known as Blue Germander, belongs to the Lamiaceae family. It is a perennial herb native to North America, characterized by its blue-violet flowers and square stems. The plant has various regional names and has been referenced in herbal texts under multiple synonyms.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Lamiales
  • Order: Lamiales
  • Family: Lamiaceae
  • Genus: Teucrium
  • Species: Teucrium canadense

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory conditions.

Safety Profile

Blue Germander is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation due to potential risks. Individuals with bleeding disorders should use it with caution as it may increase the risk of bleeding.
