Blue Germander (Teucrium canadense)

Blue Germander (Teucrium canadense) is a member of the Lamiaceae family, native to Eastern North America, Canada, and United States. Traditionally, its leaves, stems, and flowers have been used for infusions, decoctions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its bitter, anti-inflammatory, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in native american herbal medicine, european herbal medicine, and mediterranean herbal traditions.

Flowers
Inflorescences are terminal spikes with actinomorphic flowers having five white to blue-violet petals and five yellow stamens arranged in two whorls.

Erect, unbranched, woody, and glabrous with opposite, lanceolate leaves and terminal panicles.
