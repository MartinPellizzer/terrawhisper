Gentianeella arenaria has an erect, perennial growth habit characterized by slender stems that reach heights of 5 to 20 centimeters and small, opposite, lanceolate leaves. These flowers emerge from the leaf axils and are supported by short pedicels. The plant often develops from a creeping rhizome that allows it to colonize sandy habitats effectively.
