Gentianella arenaria

What's the taxonomical classification of Gentianella arenaria?

Gentianeella arenaria belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further classified under the order Gentianales. This plant is a member of the family Gentianaceae and falls within the genus Gentianeella, specifically identified by the species name arenaria.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gentianeella arenaria has an erect, perennial growth habit characterized by slender stems that reach heights of 5 to 20 centimeters and small, opposite, lanceolate leaves.

The foliage is typically glabrous and displays a light green color throughout the plant. Its inflorescence consists of small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pale blue or violet with distinct darker spots inside the corolla. These flowers emerge from the leaf axils and are supported by short pedicels.

The plant often develops from a creeping rhizome that allows it to colonize sandy habitats effectively.

      Gentianella arenaria is prepared primarily as a dried herbal infusion, a concentrated decoction, or a tincture derived from its aerial parts.
