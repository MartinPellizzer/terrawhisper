Neolepisorus ovatus

What's the taxonomical classification of Neolepisorus ovatus?

Neolepisorus ovatus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it is further organized under the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. Within the family Polypodiaceae, it is placed in the genus Neolepisorus, ultimately reaching its specific identification as the species ovatus.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Neolepisorus ovatus contains a diverse profile of bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, terpenoids, and phenolic compounds. These chemical constituents contribute to the plant's documented pharmacological activities, such as antimicrobial and antioxidant properties. Researchers have identified specific glycosides and tannins within the plant tissue that may assist in reducing inflammation. Some studies suggest that the extracts possess cytotoxic potential which is being investigated for its effects on various cell lines. The synergistic interaction of these various compounds forms the basis for its traditional medicinal applications in treating skin ailments and digestive issues.

What are the therapeutic actions of Neolepisorus ovatus?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin conditions such as eczema, psoriasis, fungal infections, and various types of dermatitis. Its medicinal properties are largely attributed to the presence of bioactive compounds that possess strong anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. When applied topically, the plant extracts help soothe irritation and reduce redness associated with chronic skin ailments. Some traditional practices also utilize its components to manage minor wounds and prevent bacterial growth on the skin surface. Researchers continue to study these phytochemicals to understand their specific mechanisms in healing dermatological issues.
