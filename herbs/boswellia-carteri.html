Olibanum

Boswellia carteri

Olibanum (Boswellia carteri) is a resin derived from the bark of the Boswellia tree, known for its traditional use in treating inflammation and joint pain, particularly in conditions like arthritis.

Taxonomy

Olibanum, scientifically known as Boswellia carteri, is a species within the genus Boswellia of the family Burseraceae, belonging to the order Burserales, class Eudicots, division Magnoliophyta, and kingdom Plantae.

Morphology

Olibanum (Boswellia carteri) is a small, shrub-like tree characterized by its grayish bark, lanceolate leaves with serrated edges, and small yellow flowers that are followed by resinous, fragrant gum resins.

Distribution

Olibanum (Boswellia carteri) is native to the dry, arid regions of northeastern Africa, particularly Somalia, Ethiopia, and parts of Kenya, with additional cultivation in India and the Middle East.

Traditional Uses

Olibanum, derived from the resin of Boswellia carteri, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, particularly in the treatment of conditions such as arthritis and inflammatory bowel diseases.

Medicinal Systems

Olibanum, also known as Boswellia carteri, has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic and Unani medicinal systems for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Phytochemistry

Olibanum (Boswellia carteri) contains a variety of bioactive compounds, including boswellic acids, terpenoids, flavonoids, and resin acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Pharmacology

Olibanum (Boswellia carteri) exhibits anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and immunomodulatory properties, making it effective in managing conditions such as arthritis, inflammatory bowel disease, and respiratory disorders.

Botanical Identification

Olibanum, scientifically known as Boswellia carteri, belongs to the Burseraceae family. It is also called Indian frankincense and is a small, deciduous shrub native to the dry regions of East Africa. Its resinous sap is harvested for medicinal use, and it is sometimes referred to as Somali frankincense in herbal texts.

Safety Profile

Olibanum (Boswellia carteri) is generally safe when used as directed. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation due to limited safety data. Always consult a healthcare provider before use, especially for those with existing medical conditions or on medications.
