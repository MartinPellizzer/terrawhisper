Olibanum

Boswellia carteri

Olibanum (Boswellia carteri) is a resin derived from the bark of trees in the Burseraceae family. Traditionally used for treating inflammation and providing pain relief, it has also been employed in various cultural practices for respiratory disorders. The resin, obtained from the sap of the tree, is often used in its raw form or processed into oils and incense.

Botanical Identification

Olibanum, scientifically known as Boswellia carteri, belongs to the Burseraceae family. It is also called Indian frankincense and is a small, deciduous shrub native to the dry regions of East Africa. Its resinous sap is harvested for medicinal use, and it is sometimes referred to as Somali frankincense in herbal texts.

Active Compounds

Olibanum contains key bioactive compounds such as terpenoids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids. These compounds work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

Preparation Methods

Extract: Used for inflammation and arthritis due to its anti-inflammatory properties.

Powder: Used for digestive issues and respiratory conditions due to its mild laxative and anti-inflammatory effects.

Infusion: Used for respiratory ailments such as coughs and colds due to its soothing properties.

Safety Profile

Olibanum (Boswellia carteri) is generally safe when used as directed. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation due to limited safety data. Always consult a healthcare provider before use, especially for those with existing medical conditions or on medications.
