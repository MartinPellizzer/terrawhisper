What's the taxonomical classification of Parasenecio ainsliaeiflorus?

Parasenecio ainsliaeiflorus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further organized into the order Asterales. It is a member of the widely recognized family Asteraceae, which places it within the sunflower family. Ultimately, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement in the genus Parasenecio, with its unique designation being the species ainsliaeiflorus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Parasenecio ainsliaeiflorus has an erect shrubby habit characterized by woody stems, lanceolate to narrowly elliptic leaves with entire margins, and small, yellowish flower heads arranged in terminal corymbose cymes.

The foliage typically displays a leathery texture and is arranged alternately along the branches. Its inflorescence consists of numerous small capitula that aggregate into dense clusters at the ends of the stems. The leaves often possess a distinct midrib and can vary in size depending on the specific environmental conditions of its habitat.
