Parasenecio ainsliaeiflorus has an erect shrubby habit characterized by woody stems, lanceolate to narrowly elliptic leaves with entire margins, and small, yellowish flower heads arranged in terminal corymbose cymes. Its inflorescence consists of numerous small capitula that aggregate into dense clusters at the ends of the stems. The leaves often possess a distinct midrib and can vary in size depending on the specific environmental conditions of its habitat.
