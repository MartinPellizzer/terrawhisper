Botanical Identification of Blueweed (Verbena hastata)

To identify Blueweed (Verbena hastata) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Blueweed, or Verbena hastata, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Lamiales, order Lamiales, family Verbenaceae, genus Verbena, and species Verbena hastata. This classification places it within the flowering plants and the verbena family, which includes many herbs used in traditional medicine.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Blueweed is Verbena hastata, first described by the Swedish botanist Carl von Linné in 1753. It is also known by several common names, including blueweed, verbena, and blue vervain, and has been referred to historically as wild verbena and blueweed.

Morphological Characteristics

Blueweed (Verbena hastata) is an annual herb with a taproot system. It has erect, branching stems that are hairless and often reddish. Leaves are opposite, oval to lance-shaped, and slightly hairy. The inflorescence is a terminal spike of blue to purple flowers with five petals. Each flower produces a capsule fruit containing small, dark seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Blueweed (Verbena hastata) can be distinguished by its slender, upright stems, opposite leaves with serrated edges, and spikes of blue to violet flowers. It differs from similar species like Verbena bonariensis by its smaller, more compact flowers and less hairy stems. Its habitat in wetlands and meadows also helps in identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Blueweed (Verbena hastata) is native to North America, particularly the eastern and central United States. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soils. It is commonly found in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and stream banks. It grows at elevations ranging from lowlands to around 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Blueweed (Verbena hastata) blooms from late spring to early summer, typically between May and July. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early fall, around August to September. For identification purposes, the best time to observe key features is during its flowering period in late spring to early summer.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Blueweed (Verbena hastata) is closely related to other members of the Verbena genus. It shares key traits with related species in the Verbenaceae family, such as opposite leaves and blue or violet flowers. Its phylogenetic position highlights its evolutionary ties to other flowering plants with similar morphological and chemical characteristics.
