Lagotis praecox

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lagotis praecox has a creeping rhizome that produces pinnate, deeply lobed fronds typically measuring between 10 and 30 centimeters in length. The leaves feature narrow, linear segments that are often slightly wavy along the margins. Small, round sori are arranged in rows on the underside of each lobe, protected by a thin membrane. The stipe, or leaf stalk, is typically dark and lacks a prominent sheath at its base. These morphological features allow the plant to spread effectively through its substrate.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, specifically occurring in countries such as Thailand, Malaysia, Indonesia, and parts of southern China. It typically flourishes in humid, low-elevation environments like rainforest margins and disturbed forest edges. Within these habitats, it often colonizes moist soil where light penetration is sufficient for growth. The species distribution is largely constrained by the requirement for consistent warmth and high moisture levels. Consequently, its presence is most concentrated in the equatorial belt of the Indo-Malayan realm.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Lagotis praecox can cause localized skin irritation, allergic reactions, and potential digestive upset if ingested. Individuals may experience redness, itching, or a burning sensation upon contact with the plant's leaves or stems. Some users might develop a rash or swelling in sensitive areas during topical application. Ingestion of the plant could lead to nausea, vomiting, or stomach discomfort due to its chemical composition. It is essential to exercise caution and avoid direct contact to prevent these adverse reactions.
