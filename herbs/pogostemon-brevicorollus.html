Pogostemon brevicorollus has a woody, shrubby growth habit characterized by oppositely arranged, elliptic to oblong leaves that possess entire margins and short petioles. Its inflorescences consist of small, axillary clusters containing tubular flowers that are notably short in length. These corollas are typically white or pale in color and emerge from a calyx with distinct lobes.
