What's the taxonomical classification of Pogostemon brevicorollus?

Pogostemon brevicorollus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical structure of its evolutionary lineage, it falls under the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Pogostemon, and its unique species designation, brevicorollus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pogostemon brevicorollus has a woody, shrubby growth habit characterized by oppositely arranged, elliptic to oblong leaves that possess entire margins and short petioles.

The foliage typically exhibits a coriaceous texture with slightly revolute edges and prominent venation. Its inflorescences consist of small, axillary clusters containing tubular flowers that are notably short in length. These corollas are typically white or pale in color and emerge from a calyx with distinct lobes.
