What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vallisneria natans has long, ribbon-like leaves that emerge from a submerged rhizome and can reach lengths of up to 100 centimeters. These lanceolate leaves are typically bright green and possess a smooth, thin texture designed to flow with water currents. The plant lacks true stems, instead utilizing a creeping root system to anchor itself in soft substrates. Small, inconspicuous white flowers occasionally bloom on long stalks that extend above the water surface. This morphology allows the species to form dense, carpet-like colonies in slow-moving freshwater environments.

How is this plant cultivated?
