Shallon Berry (Gaultheria shallon)

Shallon Berry (Gaultheria shallon) is a member of the Ericaceae family, native to North America, Pacific Northwest, and British Columbia. Traditionally, its fruits, leaves, and bark have been used for infusions, decoctions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, astringent, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in native american herbal medicine, european herbal medicine, and mediterranean herbal traditions.

Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 0.3 to 1.5 meters.
Leaves
Lateral, broadly ovate leaves with dark green upper surface and lighter green lower surface, bearing prominent stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Flowers are actinomorphic, with five white petals and five pinkish-red sepals, arranged in solitary clusters atop slender pedicels.
Stems
Erect, woody, branched stems with opposite decussate leaf arrangement, glabrous surface, and persistent scar-like markings at nodes.
