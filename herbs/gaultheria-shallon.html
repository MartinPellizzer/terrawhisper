Shallon berry

Gaultheria shallon

Shallon Berry (Gaultheria shallon) is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, known for its small, dark red berries that have been used for centuries by Indigenous peoples for their medicinal properties, particularly for improving symptoms of arthritis and joint pain.

Botanical Identification

Shallon Berry, or Gaultheria shallon, is a perennial shrub native to western North America. Belonging to the Ericaceae family, it is also known as salal or salmonberry. The plant features evergreen leaves, fragrant white flowers, and edible berries. Synonyms in herbal literature include salal and salmonberry.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Magnoliopsida
  • Order: Ericales
  • Family: Ericaceae
  • Genus: Gaultheria
  • Species: Gaultheria shallon

Active Compounds

Shallon Berry contains key active compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. Preparation Methods

Tincture: Used for treating pain and inflammation due to its analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory conditions due to its soothing and antimicrobial properties.

Infusion: Used for mild pain relief and as a sedative due to its calming effects.

Safety Profile

Shallon berry is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to potential adverse effects. Individuals with diabetes should use it with caution, as it may affect blood sugar levels.
