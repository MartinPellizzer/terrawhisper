Duhaldea nervosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Duhaldea nervosa has lanceolate to ovate leaves characterized by prominent, closely spaced longitudinal veins that extend from the base toward the apex. These leaves are typically arranged in an opposite or whorled pattern along a slender, herbaceous stem. The plant features small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its reproductive structures are adapted for specific insect pollinators within its native habitat. The overall architecture of the plant is delicate, reflecting its specialized niche in the ecosystem.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the tropical rainforest regions of the Atlantic Forest biome in southeastern Brazil. Its presence is strictly limited to these localized subtropical forest ecosystems. Consequently, the plant does not occur naturally in any other global regions.
