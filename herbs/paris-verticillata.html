What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Paris verticillata has a rhizomatous rootstock, a single stem topped by a whorl of four to seven lanceolate leaves, and solitary, bell-shaped, greenish-white flowers. The leaves are dark green with prominent parallel veins and are arranged in a single circular cluster at the apex of the stem. Its flowers are characterized by a perianth of six tepals and a central cluster of stamens surrounding a single pistil. The fruit develops into a small, fleshy capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds. This perennial herb typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments where its distinct foliage can thrive.
