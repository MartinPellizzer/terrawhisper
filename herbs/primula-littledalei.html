Primula littledalei

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Primula littledalei has a basal rosette of oblong to lanceolate leaves that are dark green, somewhat wrinkled, and possess finely serrated margins. The plant produces upright, leafless flowering stalks known as scapes that emerge from the center of the foliage. These scapes bear clusters of relatively small, salverform flowers that typically display a pale yellow or creamy color. The floral structure features a tube-like base that expands into five distinct lobes at the mouth. Each bloom is supported by slender pedicels that help position the flowers for pollinators.
