Field Marigold (Calendula arvensis)

Field Marigold (Calendula arvensis) is a member of the Compositae family, native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. Traditionally, its flowers, leaves, and roots have been used for infusions, decoctions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, tonic, and carminative actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and african traditional medicine.
