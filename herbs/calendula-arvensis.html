Field Marigold

Calendula arvensis

Field Marigold (Calendula arvensis) is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, known for its bright yellow-orange blooms and traditional use in herbal medicine to treat skin conditions and inflammation.

Botanical Identification

Field Marigold, scientifically known as Calendula arvensis, is a member of the Asteraceae family. It is also called common marigold and is an annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant has yellow-orange flowers, serrated leaves, and fibrous roots. It is sometimes referred to as garden marigold in herbal texts.

Safety Profile

Field Marigold is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause mild gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to potential uterine stimulant effects. Always consult a healthcare provider before use, especially for those with known allergies or chronic health conditions.
