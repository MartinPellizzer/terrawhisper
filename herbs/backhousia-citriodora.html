Lemon Myrtle (Backhousia citriodora)

Lemon Myrtle (Backhousia citriodora) is a member of the Myrtaceae family, native to Australia, New Zealand, and Pacific Islands. Traditionally, its leaves, bark, and resin have been used for infusions, decoctions, and oil infusions.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, carminative, and antispasmodic actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and traditional chinese medicine.

Leaves
Simple leaves with smooth margins, upper surface pale green, lower surface dull green, possessing prominent stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Inflorescences are terminal spikes with actinomorphic flowers having yellow-green petals and pale yellow stamens, arranged in a dense cluster.
Stems
Cylindrical, somewhat quadrangular, glabrous, terete, with opposite, persistent, glandular-pubescent stipules.
