Lemon Myrtle

Backhousia citriodora

Lemon Myrtle (Backhousia citriodora) is a small evergreen shrub native to Australia, known for its strong lemon-scented leaves and potential health benefits, particularly in supporting digestive health and reducing inflammation.

Taxonomy

Lemon myrtle (Backhousia citriodora) is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae, belonging to the genus Backhousia within the order Sapindales, class Eudicots, division Magnoliophyta, and kingdom Plantae.

Morphology

Lemon Myrtle (Backhousia citriodora) is a small evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy green on the upper surface and pale grey-white on the underside, with a strong lemony fragrance.

Distribution

Lemon myrtle (Backhousia citriodora) is native to the subtropical regions of eastern Australia, specifically found in the northeastern parts of Queensland and the eastern regions of New South Wales.

Traditional Uses

Lemon myrtle has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and digestive properties, often in the form of teas, poultices, and topical applications to treat infections, skin conditions, and digestive discomfort.

Medicinal Systems

In traditional medicinal systems such as Aboriginal Australian medicine, Ayurveda, and traditional Chinese medicine, Lemon Myrtle (Backhousia citriodora) has been utilized for its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and digestive properties.

Phytochemistry

The phytochemistry of Lemon Myrtle (Backhousia citriodora) is characterized by the presence of high concentrations of citral, a monoterpene ketone responsible for its distinctive lemony aroma and many of its therapeutic properties.

Pharmacology

Lemon myrtle (Backhousia citriodora) exhibits potent pharmacological properties including antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antispasmodic effects, making it valuable in herbal medicine for addressing infections, digestive disorders, and inflammatory conditions.

Botanical Identification

Backhousia citriodora, commonly known as Lemon Myrtle, belongs to the Myrtaceae family. It is a perennial shrub native to Australia, characterized by its aromatic leaves and small white flowers. The plant is also known by regional names like Lemon Grass or Citriodora, and has been referenced in herbal literature under various synonyms.

Active Compounds

Lemon Myrtle contains essential oils, terpenoids, and flavonoids as its primary bioactive compounds. These compounds work synergistically to provide its medicinal properties, with terpenoids contributing to antimicrobial effects and flavonoids offering antioxidant benefits. The essential oil, rich in citral, is the main driver of its therapeutic activity.

Safety Profile

Lemon Myrtle is generally safe when used in typical culinary or medicinal doses. However, it may cause mild gastrointestinal upset in some individuals. There are no well-documented contraindications, but pregnant or breastfeeding women should consult a healthcare provider before use.
