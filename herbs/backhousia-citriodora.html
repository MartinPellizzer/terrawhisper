Lemon Myrtle

Backhousia citriodora

Lemon Myrtle (Backhousia citriodora) is a small evergreen shrub native to Australia, known for its strong lemon-scented leaves and potential health benefits, particularly in supporting digestive health and reducing inflammation.

Taxonomy

Lemon myrtle (Backhousia citriodora) is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae, belonging to the genus Backhousia within the order Sapindales, class Eudicots, division Magnoliophyta, and kingdom Plantae.

Morphology

Lemon Myrtle (Backhousia citriodora) is a small evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy green on the upper surface and pale grey-white on the underside, with a strong lemony fragrance.

Distribution

Lemon myrtle (Backhousia citriodora) is native to the subtropical regions of eastern Australia, specifically found in the northeastern parts of Queensland and the eastern regions of New South Wales.

Traditional Uses

Lemon myrtle has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and digestive properties, often in the form of teas, poultices, and topical applications to treat infections, skin conditions, and digestive discomfort.

Medicinal Systems

In traditional medicinal systems such as Aboriginal Australian medicine, Ayurveda, and traditional Chinese medicine, Lemon Myrtle (Backhousia citriodora) has been utilized for its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and digestive properties.

Phytochemistry

The phytochemistry of Lemon Myrtle (Backhousia citriodora) is characterized by the presence of high concentrations of citral, a monoterpene ketone responsible for its distinctive lemony aroma and many of its therapeutic properties.

Pharmacology

Lemon myrtle (Backhousia citriodora) exhibits potent pharmacological properties including antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antispasmodic effects, making it valuable in herbal medicine for addressing infections, digestive disorders, and inflammatory conditions.

Medicinal Uses

Lemon Myrtle is used to treat respiratory infections, digestive issues, skin conditions, anxiety, and inflammation.

Preparations

Lemon Myrtle is used to make various preparations such as essential oils, teas, tinctures, and topical salves, which are commonly utilized in herbal medicine for their antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and digestive properties.

Safety

While Lemon Myrtle is generally considered safe when used in moderate amounts, some individuals may experience mild gastrointestinal discomfort, allergic reactions, or skin irritation, particularly with excessive or prolonged use.

Evidence

Clinical evidence for Lemon Myrtle (Backhousia citriodora) is limited but suggests potential antimicrobial, antioxidant, and anti-inflammatory properties, with some studies indicating its use in supporting respiratory and digestive health.

Cultivation

Lemon Myrtle (Backhousia citriodora) is typically cultivated in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil, often in regions of Australia where it thrives in full sun and requires minimal watering once established.

Processing

Lemon myrtle is typically processed by drying the leaves and then grinding them into a fine powder or using them fresh in various herbal preparations.

Identification

Sustainability

Lemon Myrtle (Backhousia citriodora) is sustainably sourced and cultivated in its native regions of Australia, with efforts focused on preserving biodiversity and supporting local farming communities through responsible agricultural practices.

Related

Backhousia citriodora is closely related to other species within the genus Backhousia, including Backhousia myrtifolia and Backhousia attenuata, which share similar aromatic properties and are also used in traditional herbal medicine.

Research Gap

Current research on Lemon Myrtle (Backhousia citriodora) in the context of herbal medicine is limited, with most studies focusing on its antimicrobial properties and chemical composition rather than its broader therapeutic applications or long-term safety.
