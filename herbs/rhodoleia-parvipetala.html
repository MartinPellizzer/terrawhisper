Rhodoleia parvipetala

What's the taxonomical classification of Rhodoleia parvipetala?

Rhodoleia parvipetala belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further organized under the order Saxifragales. It is a representative of the family Hamamelidaceae, falling under the genus Rhodoleia, and is specifically identified by its species name, parvipeetala.

Rhodoleia parvipetala is prepared as a decoction of its leaves or as a topical poultice using crushed plant material. What side-effects this plant can have?

Rhodoleia parvipetala can cause skin irritation, localized rashes, or allergic contact dermatitis when the plant's sap or foliage touches human skin. Because the specific chemical composition of its secondary metabolites is not fully documented in clinical literature, the exact mechanism of toxicity remains uncertain. Some individuals may experience itching or redness if they handle the leaves without protective gloves. There is also a risk of mucosal irritation if small amounts of the plant material are accidentally ingested. Careful observation is necessary when introducing this species into a domestic environment to prevent accidental exposure.
