Echinacea Angustifolia: Medicinal Uses And Benefits

Echinacea angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf echinacea, is a flowering plant native to North America, belonging to the daisy family, and is widely used in traditional and modern herbal medicine.

This herb is known for its immune-boosting properties and is often used to support the body's natural defenses against infections.

The therapeutic actions of Echinacea angustifolia include anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and immunostimulant effects, which may help reduce the duration and severity of colds and upper respiratory tract infections.

Its bioactive constituents include alkamides, caffeic acid derivatives, flavonoids, and polysaccharides, which contribute to its medicinal properties.
