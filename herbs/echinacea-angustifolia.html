Purple Coneflower

Echinacea angustifolia

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family

Native Region

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Echinacea angustifolia, commonly known as Purple Coneflower, is a perennial herb native to North America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its purple petals, yellow center, and hairy leaves. The plant is also referred to by synonyms like Narrowleaf Coneflower in herbal literature.

Active Compounds

The Purple Coneflower contains key active compounds such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and glycosides. These compounds work synergistically to support immune function and reduce inflammation. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Phenolic acid
  • Glycoside

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for respiratory infections and immune support.

Infusion: Used for colds, flu, and immune system support.

Tincture: Used for immune support and to treat colds and flu.

Safety Profile

Purple Coneflower is generally safe for most adults when used as directed. However, it may cause mild gastrointestinal upset in some individuals. It is not recommended for pregnant or breastfeeding women due to limited safety data.
