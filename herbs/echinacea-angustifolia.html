Purple Coneflower

Echinacea angustifolia

Purple Coneflower (Echinacea angustifolia) is a flowering plant native to the central United States, known for its distinctive purple petals and spiky yellow center, and it has been traditionally used for its immune-boosting properties, particularly in supporting the body's response to colds and respiratory infections.

Morphology

The Purple Coneflower (Echinacea angustifolia) is a flowering plant with a tall, upright stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, bearing narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs, and producing a distinctive purple or pink flower with a spiky central cone and surrounding ray florets.

Distribution

Purple Coneflower (Echinacea angustifolia) is native to the central and eastern regions of North America, specifically found in grasslands, prairies, and open woodlands across parts of the United States and southern Canada.

Traditional Uses

Purple Coneflower has been traditionally used by Native American tribes for its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and immune-stimulating properties, particularly in treating infections, wounds, and respiratory ailments.

Medicinal Systems

The Purple Coneflower (Echinacea angustifolia) has been traditionally used in Native American medicine, particularly by the Pawnee and Lakota tribes, for its immune-boosting properties and as a treatment for infections and wounds.

Phytochemistry

The phytochemistry of Purple Coneflower (Echinacea angustifolia) is characterized by the presence of alkamides, caffeic acid derivatives, polysaccharides, and flavonoids, which contribute to its immunostimulant and anti-inflammatory properties.

Pharmacology

Purple Coneflower exhibits anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and immune-stimulating properties that support its traditional use in herbal medicine for respiratory and immune system support.

Botanical Identification

Echinacea angustifolia, commonly known as Purple Coneflower, is a perennial herb native to North America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its purple petals, yellow center, and hairy leaves. The plant is also referred to by synonyms like Narrowleaf Coneflower in herbal literature.
