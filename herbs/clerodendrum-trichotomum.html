Clerodendrum trichotomum

What's the taxonomical classification of Clerodendrum trichotomum?

Clerodendrum trichotomum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Lamiales. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the family Lamiaceae, specifically within the genus Clerodendrum, with its unique identification completed by the species name trichotomum.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Clerodendrum trichotomum contains bioactive compounds including clerodendrine, various diterpenoids, flavonoids, and triterpenes that provide significant pharmacological activity. These phytochemicals contribute to the plant's documented anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties. Specifically, the presence of specific alkaloids and phenolic compounds allows the plant to be used in traditional medicine for treating skin ailments and digestive issues. Researchers have also investigated its potential for neuroprotective effects due to its complex chemical profile. The synergy between these diverse secondary metabolites makes the plant a subject of interest in modern drug discovery.

What are the therapeutic actions of Clerodendrum trichotomum?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin diseases, intestinal worms, fever, and various inflammatory conditions. The leaves and roots are often employed in traditional medicinal practices to combat parasitic infections in the digestive tract. It also serves as a remedy for managing symptoms associated with respiratory ailments and localized skin irritations. Some practitioners utilize its properties to help reduce high body temperatures and soothe internal discomfort. These therapeutic applications rely on the bioactive compounds found throughout the different parts of the shrub.
