Paraguay Holly

Ilex paraguayensis

Paraguay Holly (Ilex paraguayensis) is a small evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest, known for its traditional use in improving cognitive function and alleviating symptoms of depression and anxiety.

Botanical Identification

Ilex paraguayensis, commonly known as Paraguay Holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America. It belongs to the family Aquifoliaceae and is also referred to as Yerba Mate in parts of its native range. The plant is characterized by its glossy dark green leaves and small, red berries.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Liliopsida
  • Order: Aquifoliales
  • Family: Aquifoliaceae
  • Genus: Ilex
  • Species: Ilex paraguayensis

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and as a general tonic.

Safety Profile

The Paraguay Holly is generally safe when used in moderate amounts. However, it should not be taken in large doses or for prolonged periods. It is contraindicated for pregnant or breastfeeding women due to potential risks to the fetus or infant. Always consult a healthcare professional before use.
