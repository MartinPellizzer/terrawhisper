Wrightia pubescens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving through its hierarchical structure, the plant is placed in the order Gentianales and is a member of the family Apocynaceae. Finally, it is defined by its specific classification within the genus Wrightia, identifying it as the species Wrightia pubescens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Wrightia pubescens has a woody shrub or small tree structure characterized by elliptic to lanceolate leaves that are densely covered in fine, soft hairs on both surfaces. These leaves are arranged oppositely along the stems and possess entire margins with acute tips. The inflorescence consists of small, fragrant, white flowers that emerge from the leaf axils in small clusters. Each flower features a tubular corolla that opens into five spreading lobes. The plant also produces small, fleshy, ovoid fruits that typically transition from green to a darker color upon maturity.
