1. Increases blood pressure

Lindera aggregata increases blood pressure due to its high concentration of alkaloids and saponins.

These compounds can cause blood vessels to constrict, leading to an increase in blood pressure.

The plant's stimulatory effects on the cardiovascular system may also contribute to this side effect, as it can cause an increase in heart rate and cardiac output, further elevating blood pressure.

2. Causes allergic reactions

Lindera aggregata causes allergic reactions.

This is due to the presence of terpenoids and other compounds in the plant's essential oil, which can stimulate an immune response and trigger symptoms such as itching, hives, and swelling. Additionally, some individuals may be sensitive to the aromatic properties of Lindera aggregata, leading to respiratory issues or skin irritation.

As a result, those who use this herb may experience adverse reactions.

3. Induces dizziness symptoms

Lindera aggregata induces dizziness symptoms in some individuals.

This is likely due to its ability to interact with certain neurotransmitters in the brain, such as GABA, which can affect balance and coordination. Additionally, Lindera aggregata's sedative properties may cause a decrease in blood pressure, leading to feelings of lightheadedness or dizziness.

The herb's potential to induce dizziness is a common side effect that people should be aware of when considering its use.

4. Affects liver function

Lindera aggregata affects liver function due to its ability to stimulate the liver and increase the production of enzymes.

This may lead to an elevation in liver enzymes, such as ALT and AST, which can indicate liver damage or inflammation.

Additionally, Lindera aggregata's bioactive compounds, including terpenoids and sesquiterpenes, may interact with liver metabolism pathways, causing unintended changes to liver function.

5. Interferes with medication

Lindera aggregata interferes with medication when its active compounds, particularly lignans and phenolic acids, bind to proteins in the liver and gut, altering their ability to metabolize certain medications.

This can lead to reduced efficacy or increased risk of side effects from co-administered drugs, such as blood thinners, immunosuppressants, and anti-diabetes medications.

6. Triggers gastrointestinal issues

Lindera aggregata triggers gastrointestinal issues due to its unique chemical composition.

The plant contains a high concentration of volatile oils, including camphene and limonene, which can irritate the stomach lining and disrupt digestion.

Additionally, Lindera aggregata's bitter compounds may stimulate an overproduction of stomach acid, leading to symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal pain.

7. Causes respiratory distress

Lindera aggregata causes respiratory distress due to its potential to release allergenic compounds into the air, triggering an allergic response in some individuals.

This may occur when the plant is bruised, cut, or burned, releasing volatile oils that can exacerbate respiratory issues such as asthma, bronchitis, and other breathing problems.

8. Elevates heart rate

Lindera aggregata elevates heart rate due to its stimulating properties that affect the cardiovascular system.

The herb contains compounds such as sesquiterpenes and flavonoids, which can increase blood pressure and stimulate the heart muscle, leading to an elevated heart rate.

This increased cardiac activity may cause feelings of anxiety or jitteriness in some individuals.

9. Suppresses appetite significantly

Lindera aggregata suppresses appetite significantly due to its ability to stimulate the digestive system and increase the production of stomach acid.

This can lead to a feeling of fullness or nausea, reducing the desire to eat.

Additionally, the herb's bitter compounds may also contribute to this side effect by irritating the taste buds and altering food preferences.

10. Produces skin rashes

Lindera aggregata produces skin rashes due to its chemical composition, which contains oils that can cause an allergic reaction or irritation in some individuals.

The fragrant compounds found in the plant's essential oil may interact with the skin's natural barrier function, leading to redness, itching, and inflammation.

As a result, users of Lindera aggregata may experience uncomfortable skin symptoms, ranging from mild rashes to more severe reactions.
