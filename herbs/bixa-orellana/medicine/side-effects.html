10 Possible Side Effects Of Bixa Orellana

Bixa orellana has some side effects when used improperly, such as allergic reactions, skin irritation, and gastrointestinal issues.

These side effects can occur due to individual sensitivities or excessive consumption. For instance, skin irritation can lead to redness, itching, and blistering, affecting daily activities like work or school.

In severe cases, gastrointestinal issues can cause nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, compromising one's ability to maintain a healthy diet.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Bixa orellana causes allergic reactions due to its high concentration of a yellow-orange pigment called bixin.

This pigment is responsible for the plant's distinctive color and has been shown to trigger an immune response in some individuals. The allergenic properties of bixin are thought to be related to its molecular structure, which can bind to immune cells and stimulate an allergic reaction.

As a result, people may experience symptoms such as skin irritation, itching, and hives when exposed to Bixa orellana.

2. Triggers asthma attacks

Bixa orellana triggers asthma attacks due to its potential allergenic properties.

The seed of the plant contains a pigment called bixin, which is responsible for the red-orange color of many food products and cosmetics that use annatto as a natural dye.

When ingested or applied topically, this pigment can cause an allergic reaction in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath characteristic of asthma attacks.

3. Produces skin rashes

Bixa orellana produces skin rashes due to its ability to cause allergic reactions in some individuals.

The plant's active compounds, such as bixin and norbixin, can stimulate an immune response, leading to the development of skin lesions, redness, and itching.

In rare cases, annatto may also cause more severe symptoms, including hives, eczema, or even anaphylaxis, although these reactions are generally mild and temporary.

4. Induces photosensitivity

Bixa orellana induces photosensitivity when used.

This is due to its high content of carotenoid pigments, particularly bixin and norbixin, which can react with sunlight, causing skin irritation and inflammation.

As these pigments are responsible for the plant's characteristic yellow-orange color, it is essential to be aware of this potential side effect when using Bixa orellana.

5. Lowers blood pressure

Bixa orellana lowers blood pressure due to its ability to dilate blood vessels and improve blood flow.

The bioactive compounds present in the plant, such as carotenoids and flavonoids, may contribute to this effect by relaxing smooth muscle cells and increasing nitric oxide production.

This can lead to a decrease in blood pressure over time, potentially causing dizziness, fatigue, or lightheadedness in some individuals.

6. Increases heart rate

Bixa orellana increases heart rate due to its stimulant properties.

The presence of bixin, a powerful carotenoid compound, is believed to be responsible for this effect. When ingested, bixin can stimulate the cardiovascular system, causing an increase in heart rate and blood pressure.

This stimulation may lead to an elevated sympathetic nervous system activity, resulting in increased heart rate as a response to the body's perceived need for greater cardiac output.

7. Provokes gastrointestinal issues

Bixa orellana provokes gastrointestinal issues due to its high concentration of anthocyanins and other bioactive compounds that can irritate the digestive system.

The consumption of large amounts or prolonged use of Bixa orellana may lead to stomach discomfort, nausea, diarrhea, and vomiting in some individuals.

8. Increases liver toxicity

Bixa orellana increases liver toxicity due to its high concentration of bixin and norbixin, which can cause oxidative stress and inflammation in the liver.

The toxic compounds may accumulate in liver cells, leading to damage and eventual dysfunction.

Additionally, the plant's anthraquinone content may contribute to its hepatotoxic effects, further exacerbating the risk of liver toxicity.

9. Disrupts hormone levels

Bixa orellana disrupts hormone levels.

This may occur due to its ability to interact with estrogen receptors in the body, mimicking the effects of estrogen itself. The plant's natural compounds, such as bixin and norbixin, have been shown to bind to these receptors, potentially leading to changes in hormonal balance.

This disruption can have a range of effects on the body, including altered menstrual cycles and fertility issues.

10. Affects fertility rates

Bixa orellana affects fertility rates by disrupting hormonal balances in the body.

The plant's active compounds, particularly bixin and norbixin, have been shown to interfere with the production of estrogen and progesterone, leading to irregular menstrual cycles and reduced chances of conception.

This disruption can be particularly problematic for women who are trying to conceive or experiencing fertility issues.
