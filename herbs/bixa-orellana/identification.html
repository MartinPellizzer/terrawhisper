Botanical Identification of Bixin Tree (Bixa orellana)

To identify Bixin Tree (Bixa orellana) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

The Bixin Tree, Bixa orellana, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Bixales, family Bixaceae, genus Bixa, and species Bixa orellana. These classifications help identify its botanical relationships and place it within the plant kingdom.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The Bixin Tree is scientifically known as Bixa orellana, a name first introduced by the botanist Linnaeus in the 18th century. This species has several synonyms, including Bixa orellana L., which reflects its historical classification. It is also known by various common names such as annatto tree, bixin tree, and red wood. These names vary across regions and cultures, highlighting its widespread use and recognition.

Morphological Characteristics

The Bixin Tree is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree with a dense, rounded canopy. It has a fibrous root system that anchors it firmly in moist, well-drained soils. The stem is straight, slightly woody, and often branched, with a smooth, grayish-brown bark.

Diagnostic Identification Features

The Bixin Tree can be distinguished by its unique red, waxy aril that surrounds the orange seed. It has opposite, ovate leaves with smooth edges and a distinctive red-brown stem. The plant also produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits. These features help differentiate it from other similar species.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

The Bixin Tree is native to tropical regions of Central and South America. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained, fertile soils. It typically grows at altitudes between 200 and 800 meters above sea level.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

The Bixin Tree blooms from March to May, producing red to orange flowers. Seed formation occurs from June to September. Harvesting of the seeds, known as bixina, takes place between October and November, when the fruit is fully mature and the seeds are ready for collection.

Phylogenetic Relationships

The Bixin Tree (Bixa orellana) belongs to the family Rubiaceae, closely related to species like Psychotria and Mucuna. It shares morphological and genetic traits with other members of the genus Bixa, particularly Bixa orellana var. orellana. Its phylogenetic placement highlights its evolutionary ties within the Rubiaceae family.
