What's the taxonomical classification of Gynandropsis gynandra?

Gyandropsis gynandra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant is a member of the order Brassicales and falls under the family Cleomaceae. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Gyandropsis and the species gynandra.

What are the pharmacological activities of Gynandropsis gynandra?

This plant has potent antineoplastic, vasorelaxant, antimicrobial, and anti-inflammatory pharmacological activities. Its antineoplastic properties allow it to inhibit the proliferation of various cancer cell lines through specialized bioactive compounds. The plant also demonstrates significant vasorelaxation, which helps in reducing blood pressure and improving vascular health. These diverse medicinal effects are attributed to a high concentration of phytochemicals such as alkaloids and flavonoids. Consequently, Glynandropsis gynandra serves as a valuable natural resource for developing new therapeutic agents.

As discussed in a study, the CH3OH-CH2Cl2 extract of the entire plant Gynandropsis gynandra was separated into six cancer cell growth inhibitors, including the flavone apegenin (4) and flavonols 1-3, 5, and 6. The five flavonols (1-3, 5, 6) inhibited the murine P388 lymphocytic leukemia cell line with ED50 values of 3.0, 9.2, 4.0, 0.37, and 3.9 microg/ml, respectively. All six flavonoids (1-6) also exhibited activity against a panel of six human cancer cell lines. Additionally, penduletin (3) inhibited the growth of the Gram-negative pathogen Neisseria gonorrhoeae. Apegenin (4) inhibited the growth of the Gram-positive opportunist Enterococcus faecalis.
