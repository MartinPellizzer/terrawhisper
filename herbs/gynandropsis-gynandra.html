Gyandropsis gynandra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Gyandropsis and the species gynandra.

What are the pharmacological activities of Gynandropsis gynandra?

This plant has potent anti-neoplastic, vasorelaxant, antimicrobial, and anti-inflammatory pharmacological activities. Its ability to inhibit tumor cell growth makes it a significant subject of study for anti-neoplastic drug development. Additionally, the plant exhibits vasorelaxation properties that can assist in managing cardiovascular conditions by relaxing blood vessels. Research also suggests it possesses strong antioxidant and antipyretic effects. These diverse medicinal properties highlight its potential utility in modern pharmacology.

According to a study published by "Oncology research", the CH3OH-CH2Cl2 extract of the entire plant Gynandropsis gynandra was separated to isolate six cancer cell growth inhibitors. These included the flavone apegenin (4) and five flavonols labeled 1-3, 5, and 6. The five flavonols (1-3, 5, 6) inhibited the murine P388 lymphocytic leukemia cell line with ED50 values of 3.0, 9.2, 4.0, 0.37, and 3.9 microg/ml. Additionally, all six flavonoids (1-6) exhibited activity against a panel of six human cancer cell lines. Penduletin (3) inhibited the growth of the Gram-negative pathogen Neisseria gonorrhoeae, while apegenin (4) inhibited the Gram-positive opportunist Enterococcus faecalis.
