Gynandropsis gynandra

What's the taxonomical classification of Gynandropsis gynandra?

Gyandropsis gynandra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant is a member of the order Brassicales and falls under the family Cleomaceae. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Gyandropsis and the species gynandra.

Gynandropsis gynandra is prepared as decoctions, infusions, powders, juices, and topical poultices for various medicinal purposes. In some traditional practices, the dried plant material is ground into a fine powder for easier ingestion or topical application. What side-effects this plant can have?

Gynandropsis gynandra can cause adverse reactions such as gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, or potential interference with blood clotting and hormonal balance. While many people use it as a nutritional supplement, excessive consumption may lead to stomach upset or nausea. Some individuals have reported localized allergic reactions when the plant comes into direct contact with sensitive skin. There is also limited clinical data regarding how its bioactive compounds might interact with specific prescription medications. Consequently, caution is advised for pregnant women or those with underlying medical conditions.
