African leafy vegetable

Gynandropsis gynandra

African Leafy Vegetable (Gynandropsis gynandra) is a plant native to Africa, belonging to the Asteraceae family. Traditionally, it has been used to treat intestinal parasites, act as a laxative, and address digestive issues. The leaves and stems are commonly utilized in its preparation.

Botanical Identification

Gynandropsis gynandra, commonly known as African leafy vegetable or "mabira" in some regions, is an annual herb native to tropical Africa. It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, edible leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is also known by various synonyms in herbal medicine literature.

Active Compounds

Gynandropsis gynandra contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and tannins, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to provide antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial effects. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory ailments.

Infusion: Used for mild digestive issues and as a general tonic.

Poultice: Used for skin conditions and wounds.

Safety Profile

Gynandropsis gynandra is generally safe when consumed in typical food amounts. However, it may cause mild gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy due to potential uterine stimulating effects. Always consult a healthcare provider before use, especially for medicinal purposes.
