Saussurea romuleifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea romuleifolia has basal, rosette-forming leaves that are narrowly lanceolate to linear in shape and possess entire or slightly serrated margins. These leaves are typically dark green and grow closely together to form a dense cluster near the ground. The plant features a tall, unbranched flowering stem that rises significantly above the basal foliage. At the apex of this stem, small, yellowish-white flower heads are arranged in a compact, terminal cluster. Each individual floret is surrounded by inconspicuous bracts that contribute to the overall structure of the inflorescence.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of Central and East Asia, specifically spanning across the Himalayas, the Tibetan Plateau, and parts of western China. It thrives in alpine environments where it can be found growing in rocky crevices and scree slopes. The species is most commonly documented in mountainous territories within countries like Nepal, Bhutan, and India. Its distribution is strictly limited to subalpine and alpine zones characterized by cold climates and sparse vegetation. These specific ecological niches ensure its survival in extreme elevations.

There is limited clinical data regarding its long-term systemic toxicity in humans, which necessitates cautious usage. Pregnant or breastfeeding women should avoid it due to the lack of safety studies concerning fetal development.
