What is Magnolia wilsonii?

Magnolia wilsonii is a species of flowering plant in the family Magnoliaceae, endemic to southwestern China.

It is a deciduous tree, typically growing to 10-15 meters in height, with a broad, rounded crown and a straight, columnar trunk. The leaves are elliptical to ovate, 10-30 centimeters long and 5-15 centimeters wide, with a leathery texture and a wavy margin. The flowers are solitary, yellow-green, and 10-15 centimeters in diameter, with 6-8 tepals and a prominent androecium.

The fruits are red, ovoid, and 8-10 centimeters long, containing numerous seeds.
