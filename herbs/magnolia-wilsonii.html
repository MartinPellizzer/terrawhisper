What's the taxonomical classification of Magnolia wilsonii?

Magnolia wilsonii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical structure of botanical classification, it falls under the order Magnoliales and is a member of the family Magnoliaceae. Finally, the plant is situated within the genus Magnolia, specifically identified by the species epithet wilsonii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Magnolia wilsonii has large, leathery, elliptic to oblong leaves that measure up to 25 centimeters in length and are arranged alternately along its woody branches. During the flowering season, the plant produces solitary, fragrant white blossoms that emerge from terminal buds. These flowers feature several thick, waxy petals and numerous prominent stamens arranged around a central carpel. The tree structure is generally medium-sized with a spreading canopy that supports these heavy floral displays.
