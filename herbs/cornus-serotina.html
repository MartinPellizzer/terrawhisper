Red Osier Dogwood (Cornus serotina)

Red Osier Dogwood (Cornus serotina) is a member of the Cornaceae family, native to Eastern United States, Eastern Canada, and Northern Mexico. Traditionally, its roots, leaves, and bark have been used for decoctions, infusions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its tonic, astringent, and bitter actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, native american herbal medicine, and mediterranean herbal traditions.

Botanical Description

Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically reaches a height of 5 to 10 meters.
Leaves
Opposite, broadly ovate leaves with smooth margins, upper surface glabrous and reddish-brown, lower surface pale green and pubescent with stomatal bands.
Flowers
Inflorescence consists of clusters of small white flowers with five sepals and five petals, arranged in a dense umbel, exhibiting actinomorphic symmetry, with reddish filaments and yellow anthers.
Stems
Stems are erect, branched, and have a smooth, bark that is reddish-brown and fissured, with opposite branching and a woody texture.
