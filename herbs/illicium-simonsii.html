Illicium simonsii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Illicium simonsii has lanceolate to elliptical leaves that are typically dark green, coriaceous, and possess entire margins. The foliage is arranged alternately along the woody stems of this evergreen shrub or small tree. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils, often presenting a delicate structure compared to the robust leaves. The fruit develops into a characteristic aggregate of woody follicles that eventually dehisce to release seeds. These morphological traits are consistent with the broader Illiciaceae family characteristics found in tropical forest understories.
