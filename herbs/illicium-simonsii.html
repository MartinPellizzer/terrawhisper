Illicium simonsii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Illicium simonsii has lanceolate to elliptical leaves that are typically dark green, coriaceous, and possess entire margins. The foliage is arranged alternately along the woody stems of this evergreen shrub or small tree. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils, often presenting a delicate structure compared to the robust leaves. The fruit develops into a characteristic aggregate of woody follicles that eventually dehisce to release seeds. These morphological traits are consistent with the broader Illiciaceae family characteristics found in tropical forest understories.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Illicium simonsii can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, neurological impairment, and potential death if ingested due to the presence of toxic compounds like anisatin. These neurotoxins act on the central nervous system to induce symptoms such as vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea. More serious reactions include muscle tremors, seizures, and hallucinations that can escalate quickly after consumption. In extreme cases, the toxicity can lead to respiratory failure or coma. Because of these unpredictable and dangerous side effects, the plant should be handled with extreme caution and never consumed.
