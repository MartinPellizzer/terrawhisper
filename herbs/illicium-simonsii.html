Illicium simonsii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Austrobaileyales and the family Schisandraceae. Finally, the plant is situated within the genus Illicium, with its specific designation being the species simonsii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Illicium simonsii has lanceolate to elliptical leaves that are typically dark green, coriaceous, and possess entire margins. The foliage is arranged alternately along the woody stems of this evergreen shrub or small tree. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils, often presenting a delicate structure compared to the robust leaves. The fruit develops into a characteristic aggregate of woody follicles that eventually dehisce to release seeds.
