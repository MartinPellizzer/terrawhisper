What is Attalea × teixeirana?

Attalea × teixeirana is a palm tree resulting from the interspecific hybridization of Attalea speciosa and Arecaceae family member Attalea maripa.

The plant belongs to the family Arecaceae and the genus Attalea, with palmate leaves having a petiole up to 1.5 meters long and a leaf blade up to 2 meters long with a rounded base and a linear to lorate shape. The inflorescence is a branched spike with a peduncle up to 1.5 meters long, bearing a single type of flower. The fruit is a berry, about 2.5 centimeters in diameter, with a fleshy pericarp and a single seed.

Attalea × teixeirana is native to Brazil.
