What's the taxonomical classification of Delphinium kamaonense?

Delphinium kamaonense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It falls under the order Ranunculales and is a member of the family Ranunculaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identification is defined by the genus Delphinium and the unique species epithet kamaonense.

What are the pharmacological activities of Delphinium kamaonense?

This plant has anti-inflammatory pharmacological activities derived from its unique chemical constituents. These specific substances work by inhibiting the pathways responsible for swelling and tissue irritation. According to a study published by "Phytochemistry", Delphinium kamaonense Huth is a folkloric plant resource with medicinal values. Seven undescribed lycaconitine-type C19-diterpenoid alkaloids and ten known compounds were isolated from the plant. In vitro nitric oxide inhibitory activities assays showed that these lycaconitine-type C19-diterpenoid alkaloids possessed significant anti-inflammatoy inhibitory activities. Kamaonensine E was identified as the most potent compound with an activity of 0.9 ± 0.2 μM, which is stronger than the positive value of 9.0 ± 1.3 μM. Network pharmacology studies suggested the anti-inflammatoy mechanism might involve MAPK signaling pathways by binding to 3 key targets: MAPK8, MAPK14, and HSP90α.
