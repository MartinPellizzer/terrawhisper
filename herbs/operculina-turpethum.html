What's the taxonomical classification of Operculina turpethum?

Operculina turpethum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its classification through the order Solanales, it is placed within the family Convolvulaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific genus, Operculina, and its unique species designation, turpethum.

Its medicinal properties are frequently applied to alleviate respiratory issues such as bronchial inflammation and breathing difficulties. Additionally, it serves as an effective remedy for managing digestive irregularities. The plant helps stabilize the gut by addressing both excessive bowel movements and chronic sluggishness. This versatility makes it a significant resource for addressing multiple systemic ailments.

As discussed in a study, Operculina turpethum has been traditionally used to treat ailments such as asthma. The 70% aqueous-ethanolic extract of the OTB black variety was tested for bronchodilator effects in isolated guinea-pig tracheal preparations. This extract caused inhibition of carbachol and high K+-induced constriction with EC50 values of 0.66 mg/ml (0.53-0.82) and 0.59 mg/ml (0.45-0.62) respectively. Additionally, OTB produced a dose-dependent protection from castor oil-induced diarrhoea in mice at doses of 300-1000 mg/kg. Pretreatment of tissue with OTB at 0.01 and 0.03 mg/ml caused a rightward shift in the concentration response curves of Ca++. These findings provide evidence for the medicinal use of the plant in asthma and diarrhoea.
