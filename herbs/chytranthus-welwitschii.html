What's the taxonomical classification of Chytranthus welwitschii?

Chytranthus welwitschii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Sapindales and the family Sapindaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Chytranthus and the specific species designation welwitschii.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Chytranthus welwitschii can cause severe skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential allergic reactions if handled or ingested. Direct contact with the plant's sap may lead to localized redness, itching, or chemical burns on sensitive areas of the skin. If accidentally consumed, individuals might experience nausea, vomiting, or abdominal cramping. Some people may also exhibit signs of an acute allergic response, such as swelling or difficulty breathing. It is important to use protective gloves and avoid any oral exposure when working with this species.
