Botanical Identification of Rhubarb (Rheum officinale)

To identify Rhubarb (Rheum officinale) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Rheum officinale is the scientific name for rhubarb, first described by the botanist Linnaeus. It belongs to the family Polygonaceae. Common synonyms include Rheum palmatum and Rheum rhaponticum. It is also known by regional names such as Chinese rhubarb and medicinal rhubarb, reflecting its traditional use in herbal medicine.

Morphological Characteristics

Rhubarb (Rheum officinale) is a perennial herb with a robust root system that stores nutrients. It has thick, reddish stems that grow upright. Its large, heart-shaped leaves are covered in a dense layer of wax. The plant produces a tall inflorescence with small, clustered flowers. The fruit is a dry capsule containing numerous seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Rhubarb (Rheum officinale) can be distinguished by its large, broad leaves with a distinctive red or purple underside, thick reddish stalks, and large, clustered flowers. It differs from similar species like Rheum palmatum by its more robust growth and the presence of a prominent central vein in the leaves. The leaves are typically more heart-shaped and have a smoother texture compared to other rheum species.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Rhubarb (Rheum officinale) is native to temperate regions of Asia, particularly China, Korea, and Japan. It thrives in cool climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils and is typically found at elevations between 1,000 and 2,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Rhubarb (Rheum officinale) blooms in early summer, typically between May and June. Seed formation occurs in late summer, around July to August. For identification purposes, the plant is harvested in spring when the leaves are fully developed but before flowering, usually between March and May.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Rhubarb (Rheum officinale) belongs to the family Polygonaceae, sharing close relations with other genera like Euphorbia and Rumex. Its closest species include Rheum palmatum and Rheum rhabarbarum. The genus Rheum is characterized by its rhizomatous growth, large heart-shaped leaves, and edible petioles, which are key traits distinguishing it from related families.
