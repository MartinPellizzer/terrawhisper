What's the taxonomical classification of Melastoma normale?

Melastoma normale belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is part of the order Myrtales and falls under the family Melastomataceae. Finally, the plant is categorized within the genus Melastoma, specifically identified as the species normale.

As discussed in a study, Melastomast normale possesses polyphenols with potential anti-inflammatory, anti-tyrosinase, and anti-obesity effects. Four new ellagitannins, labeled 1, 2, 3, and 4, were isolated from the roots of the plant along with 8 known polyphenols. Regarding anti-inflammatory activities in LPS-stimulated RAW 264.7 cells, compounds 1-4 did not inhibit nitric oxide production, as their IC50 values were all > 50 μM. In anti-tyrosinase assays, compound 1 showed weak inhibitory activity with an IC50 value of 426.02 ± 11.31 μM. Compounds 2-4 displayed moderate inhibitory activities with IC50 values ranging from 124.74 ± 3.12 to 241.41 ± 6.23 μM.
