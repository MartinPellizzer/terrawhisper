Xeraea canescens

Xeraea canescens, also known as the Chilean lavender, is a shrub-like plant native to the Andes mountains in South America.

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties, primarily due to its essential oil, which has anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The essential oil extracted from the Xeraea canescens plant has been used to treat various health issues, including digestive problems, anxiety, and insomnia, and has also been used to soothe skin irritations and wounds.

Xeraea canescens is typically prepared as an essential oil, and it can also be used in herbal teas or topical creams to harness its medicinal properties.
