What's the taxonomical classification of Aletris glabra?

Aletris glabra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Dioscoreales, the plant is a member of the family Nartheceae (Nartheciaceae). Ultimately, its taxonomic identity is defined by its placement within the genus Aletris, specifically identifying it as the species glabra.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aletris glabra has a basal rosette of long, linear, grass-like leaves that can reach up to 20 centimeters in length and are characterized by their smooth, glabrous texture. These leaves emerge from a central point and form a dense cluster near the ground. From the center of this rosette, a slender, upright flowering stalk rises to hold several small, white, star-shaped flowers. Each individual blossom features six tepals and delicate stamens that are typically tinged with a pale pink or lavender hue. The overall structure is quite delicate, providing a striking vertical contrast to the low-growing foliage.
