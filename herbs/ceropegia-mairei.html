Ceropegia mairei

What's the taxonomical classification of Ceropegia mairei?

Ceropegia mairei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Gentianales, the plant is a member of the family Apocynaceae. Specifically, it is identified by the genus Ceropegia, with its unique designation being the species mairei.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ceropegia mairei has slender, cylindrical, succulent stems that grow in a sprawling or climbing habit and are covered in small, pointed tubercles.

These stems often exhibit a reddish or brownish coloration and lack true leaves, instead relying on their fleshy structure for water storage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that typically emerge from the stem axils. These blossoms are often pale or white and possess a delicate, waxy texture.

The overall structure is adapted for arid environments where minimizing surface area is essential for survival.
