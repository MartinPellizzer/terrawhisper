Pittosporum crispulum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pittosporum crispulum has small, elliptical, bright green leaves that are distinctly revolute, meaning their margins are rolled downwards toward the underside. These leaves are arranged oppositely or sub-oppositely along woody, upright stems. The plant typically exhibits a dense, shrubby growth habit with a spreading canopy. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils, often followed by fleshy, orange-colored drupes. The texture of the foliage is somewhat leathery, which helps the plant retain moisture in various environments.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Pittosporum crispulum can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and potential toxicity if ingested by humans or animals. Contact with the plant's sap or foliage may result in localized redness, itching, or a mild rash on sensitive skin. While there is limited clinical data on human toxicity, the genus Pittosporum is often associated with gastrointestinal distress if consumed. Small amounts of ingestion might lead to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal discomfort. It is advisable to wear gloves when pruning and to keep the plant away from curious pets or children.
