Bergenia (Bergenia crassifolia)

Bergenia (Bergenia crassifolia) is a member of the Saxifragaceae family, native to Eastern Siberia, Central Asia, and Northern China. Traditionally, its roots, rhizomes, and leaves have been used for decoctions, infusions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, astringent, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in korean traditional medicine, tibetan medicine, and traditional chinese medicine.

Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters.
Leaves
Broad leaves with upper surface green and lower surface pale green, bearing prominent stomatal bands.
Flowers
Inflorescence consists of solitary flowers with five petals arranged in a whorl, each petal bearing a distinct midvein, and flowers displaying zygomorphic symmetry with a prominent purple-red corolla.
Stems
Cylindrical, somewhat succulent, thick-based stems with a rough, pubescent surface, arising from a short rhizome, exhibiting a basal branching pattern with opposite leaf scars.
