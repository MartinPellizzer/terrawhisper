Botanical Identification

Bergenia crassifolia, commonly known as the rock白菜 or broad-leaved bergenia, is a perennial plant native to East Asia. Belonging to the Saxifragaceae family, it features thick, fleshy leaves and pinkish-red flowers. It is also referred to as bergenia in some herbal texts, though its use varies regionally.

Active Compounds

Bergenia crassifolia contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids. These compounds work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Tannin
  • Phenolic acid

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory ailments.

Infusion: Used for colds and flu symptoms.

Poultice: Used for skin inflammation and wounds.

Safety Profile

Bergenia crassifolia is generally considered safe when used as directed. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. There are no well-documented contraindications, but pregnant or breastfeeding women should avoid its use due to limited safety data.
