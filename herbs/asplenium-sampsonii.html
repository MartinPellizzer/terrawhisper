What's the taxonomical classification of Asplenium sampsonii?

Asplenium sampsonii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae. Following its placement in the order Polypodiales, the plant is categorized under the family Aspleniaceae. Ultimately, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its membership in the genus Asplenium, with its unique designation being the species sampsonii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Asplenium sampsonii has large, pinnate fronds composed of elongated, lanceolate pinnae that are arranged symmetrically along a central, dark-colored rachis.

The individual leaflets feature entire margins and a distinctively leathery texture that provides structural rigidity. These fronds emerge from a creeping rhizome which often becomes covered in a dense layer of brownish scales. On the underside of the mature foliage, the plant displays small, circular sori protected by thin, kidney-shaped indusia.

The overall growth habit results in a lush, spreading appearance typical of many forest-dwelling ferns.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Asplenium sampsonii can cause localized skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, or respiratory distress if its spores or fronds are inhaled or touched directly.
