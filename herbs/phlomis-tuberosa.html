Phlomis tuberosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Phlomis tuberosa?

Phlomis tuberosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Lamiales. It is a member of the family Lamiaceae, specifically situated within the genus Phlomis, and is identified by the species name tuberosa.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Phlomis tuberosa contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including iridoids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids that provide therapeutic potential. The presence of iridoid glycosides is particularly significant due to their documented anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Flavonoid constituents within the plant contribute to its ability to combat oxidative stress in cellular environments. Additionally, the phenolic compounds found in the roots and leaves assist in stabilizing biological membranes against damage. These combined chemical components suggest that the plant may have applications in managing inflammatory conditions.

What are the therapeutic actions of Phlomis tuberosa?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat respiratory ailments such as coughs, bronchitis, and asthma, as well as digestive issues and inflammatory skin conditions. The rhizomes contain various bioactive compounds that help soothe irritated mucous membranes in the lungs. It is also traditionally employed to manage symptoms of rheumatism and certain types of indigestion. Some herbal practitioners utilize its properties to reduce localized swelling and skin irritation. These medicinal applications rely on the plant's natural anti-inflammatory and expectorant characteristics.
