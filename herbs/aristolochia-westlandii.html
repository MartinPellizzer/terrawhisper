Aristolochia westlandii

What's the taxonomical classification of Aristolochia westlandii?

Aristolochia westlandii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Piperales and is a member of the family Aristolochiaceae. Finally, it is situated within the genus Aristolochia, specifically identified by the species epithet westlandii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains aristolochic acids, which are potent nitrogenous compounds known for their significant pharmacological and toxicological properties. These specific phytochemicals function as bioactive agents that can interact with various cellular pathways within a biological system. The presence of these alkaloids is a defining chemical characteristic that distinguishes the genus.     What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause severe kidney damage through nephrotoxicity, lead to cancer due to its carcinogenicity, induce uropathy, and result in liver damage through hepatotoxicity. The presence of aristolochic acids makes these botanical species extremely dangerous to human health. Consumption often leads to progressive renal failure and structural damage to the urinary tract. Furthermore, the chemicals can cause significant harm to the liver as the body attempts to process the toxins. These combined physiological effects make the plant a serious health hazard.
