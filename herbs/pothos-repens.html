Pothos repens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Alismatales and is a member of the family Araceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific genus, Pothos, and its species designation, repens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pothos repens has heart-shaped, waxy leaves that typically range from two to ten inches in length and feature a glossy, dark green surface with potential variegation. The plant develops adventitious roots along its nodes, which allow it to anchor itself to surfaces like tree bark or wooden structures. As the vines mature, the leaf size can increase significantly compared to younger specimens. What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical rainforest regions of French Polynesia, specifically within the islands of the Society archipelago. It thrives in humid, low-light environments found deep within these equatorial forest canopies. While its natural range is geographically restricted to these Pacific islands, it has been widely distributed globally through the ornamental plant trade. Many people now cultivate it in indoor settings across various continents due to its adaptability to domestic climates.     What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause nausea, vomiting, oral irritation, swelling of mouth/throat, drooling, abdominal pain, difficulty swallowing, and skin irritation if ingested or touched. The calcium oxalate crystals present in the foliage often lead to immediate oral irritation and significant swelling of the mouth and throat.
