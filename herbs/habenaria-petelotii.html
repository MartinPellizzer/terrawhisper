What's the taxonomical classification of Habenaria petelotii?

Habeanaria petelotii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Asparagales. As a member of the family Orchidaceae, this plant is placed within the genus Habeanaria, and its specific identification is defined by its species name, petelotii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Habenaria petelotii has a terrestrial growth habit characterized by slender, erect stems that typically reach heights of ten to thirty centimeters and bear numerous small, white, or pale greenish-white flowers.

The leaves are generally basal or distributed along the lower portion of the stem, appearing lanceolate to narrowly elliptic in shape. Each individual flower features a distinct spurred lip that is often deeply lobed or fringed, a common trait among members of this genus. The floral structure is complex, with long, narrow petals and sepals that surround a prominent nectar-producing spur.
