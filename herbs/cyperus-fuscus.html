Cyperus fuscus

What's the taxonomical classification of Cyperus fuscus?

Cyperus fuscus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant falls under the order Poales. It is further categorized into the family Cyperaceae, specifically within the genus Cyperus, and is identified by the unique species designation fuscus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cyperus fuscus has triangular stems that reach heights of up to 1 meter, topped by dense, dark brown to brownish-green terminal inflorescences consisting of multiple spikelets. The leaves are linear, grass-like, and emerge from the base of the plant in a basal arrangement. Each spikelet contains small, inconspicuous flowers protected by specialized scales that contribute to the overall textured appearance of the cluster. The roots form a creeping rhizomatous system that allows the plant to spread through wetland substrates. This morphology is characteristic of many sedges adapted to saturated environments.
