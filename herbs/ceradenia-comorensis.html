What is Ceradenia comorensis?

Ceradenia comorensis is a species of epiphytic fern in the family Polypodiaceae.

It has a rhizome that produces pinnate fronds, with linear-lanceolate pinnae and a rachis that is densely covered with dark-brown scales. The plant is native to the Comoros Islands and has been found growing on tree trunks and branches. The fronds of Ceradenia comorensis are typically 20-50 cm long and have a lobe-like shape, with the lower pinnae being smaller than the upper ones.

The plant's sporophylls are dimorphic, with the sterile sporophylls being larger and more robust than the fertile ones.
