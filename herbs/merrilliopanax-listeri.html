Merrilliopanax listeri

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Merrilliopanax listeri has large, compound leaves composed of several pairs of leaflets that are elliptic to ovate in shape with serrated margins. The leaflets are arranged oppositely along a central rachis and possess prominent pinnate venation. Each leaflet features an acute apex and a cuneate base, contributing to a lush, dense foliage appearance. The plant's woody structure supports these expansive leaf clusters, which can grow quite large depending on environmental conditions. Small, inconspicuous flowers eventually emerge from the axils to facilitate reproduction.
