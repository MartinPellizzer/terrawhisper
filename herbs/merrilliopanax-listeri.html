What's the taxonomical classification of Merrilliopanax listeri?

Merrilliopanax listeri belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. As a member of the order Apiales, it falls under the family Araliaceae. At the most specific levels of its classification, this plant is identified by the genus Merrilliopanax and the species name listeri.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Merrilliopanax listeri can cause skin irritation, localized redness, and allergic contact dermatitis upon direct contact with the plant's tissues. Individuals may experience itching or a burning sensation where the sap or leaves touch the skin. Some people might also develop small blisters or a rash if they have a heightened sensitivity to the plant's chemical compounds. It is important to avoid handling the plant without gloves to prevent these dermatological reactions. Monitoring for any signs of swelling or spreading irritation is recommended after exposure.
