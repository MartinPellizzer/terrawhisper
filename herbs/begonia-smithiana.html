What's the taxonomical classification of Begonia smithiana?

Begonia smithiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its lineage through the order Cucurbitaleales, it falls into the family Begoniaceae. Ultimately, this specific plant is identified by its genus, Begonia, and its unique species designation, smithiana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Begonia smithiana has large, asymmetrical, cordate leaves that can reach up to 40 centimeters in length and are characterized by a distinctively wrinkled or bullate surface texture. Its growth habit is typically rhizomatous, allowing the plant to spread across forest floors or climb slightly. The inflorescences consist of small, delicate flowers that emerge from the leaf axils in clusters.
