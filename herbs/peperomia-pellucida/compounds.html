Peperomia Plant (Peperomia pellucida) Compounds

Peperomia pellucida contains several primary active compounds, including flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarin and alkaloids are also present, offering potential antimicrobial and analgesic effects. Glycosides and terpenoids may support respiratory and digestive health, while saponins and essential oils contribute to its overall medicinal profile. Anthocyanins provide additional antioxidant benefits.

Peperomia Plant (Peperomia pellucida) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Peperomia pellucida are bioactive compounds known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating ailments like inflammation and digestive issues. They are found in various parts of the plant, including leaves and stems. Their presence supports the plant's role in herbal medicine.

2. Tannin

Tannin in Peperomia pellucida is a key active compound known for its astringent properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating digestive issues and skin conditions. Tannins help reduce inflammation and may support wound healing. This compound is widely studied for its potential therapeutic benefits in herbal medicine.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid in Peperomia pellucida contributes to its antioxidant properties. This compound helps neutralize free radicals, supporting cellular health. It plays a key role in the plant's traditional use for treating inflammation and digestive issues. Phenolic acid is one of several bioactive compounds found in this medicinal herb.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Peperomia pellucida. It exhibits antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties. This compound contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating digestive issues and respiratory conditions. Its presence supports the plant's role in herbal medicine.

5. Alkaloid

Alkaloids in Peperomia pellucida are bioactive compounds known for their potential medicinal properties. These alkaloids may contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating various ailments. Their presence supports the plant's role in herbal medicine, offering possible therapeutic benefits. Further research is needed to fully understand their mechanisms and applications.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Peperomia pellucida. It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. 7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Peperomia pellucida contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. They are responsible for the plant's traditional use in treating infections and skin conditions. Terpenoids enhance the plant's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound found in Peperomia pellucida.
