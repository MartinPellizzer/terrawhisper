What to know about Collinsonia canadensis (stone root) before using it medicinally

Stone root, scientifically known as Collinsonia canadensis, is a herb that has been revered for its impressive health properties, which have been shown to improve digestive issues and alleviate respiratory problems.

In addition to its medicinal uses, stone root is also prized for its unique flavor profile and is often used in traditional cuisine, particularly in Asian and Native American cooking. Horticulturally, stone root is relatively low-maintenance, thriving in moist soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice among gardeners. From a botanical standpoint, stone root belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive veining.

With roots tracing back to ancient Native American and Chinese medicine, stone root has a rich historical significance, having been used for centuries in traditional remedies and ceremonies.
