What is Selliguea soridens?

Selliguea soridens is a species of fern in the family Polypodiaceae, native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It is a terrestrial plant with a creeping rhizome and a rosette of fronds that range in length from 20-40 cm. The fronds have a linear-lanceolate shape, with 10-20 pairs of pinnate pinnae, and are dark green in color with a glabrous rachis. The plant reproduces via spores produced on the underside of the fronds in a sori pattern, with each sorus containing 64-128 spores.

Selliguea soridens is commonly found in shaded, humid environments with rich soil.
