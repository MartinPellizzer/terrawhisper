Blueberry (Vaccinium corymbosum) Compounds

Blueberry contains several key compounds, including flavonoids, which are powerful antioxidants. Phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory properties. Tannins and anthocyanins provide astringent effects and contribute to its vibrant color. Glycosides, saponins, and terpenoids also play roles in its medicinal and therapeutic benefits.

Blueberry (Vaccinium corymbosum) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are key active compounds in Blueberry (Vaccinium corymbosum). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting cellular health and reducing oxidative stress. These compounds are found in the fruit's skin and pulp, enhancing its therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Blueberry (Vaccinium corymbosum). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports overall health by enhancing cellular protection and may aid in preventing chronic diseases.

3. Tannin

Tannin in Blueberry (Vaccinium corymbosum) contributes to its astringent properties and may support digestive health. It acts as a natural antioxidant and helps in reducing inflammation. Tannins also aid in the preservation of the fruit's nutrients. These compounds are present in the bark, leaves, and berries of the plant.

4. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Blueberry (Vaccinium corymbosum). It contributes to the fruit's deep blue color and possesses antioxidant properties. Anthocyanin supports cardiovascular health and may improve cognitive function, making it a valuable component in herbal medicine.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Blueberry (Vaccinium corymbosum). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting cardiovascular and neurological health. Glycosides in blueberries are known for their role in reducing oxidative stress and improving cognitive function.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Blueberry (Vaccinium corymbosum). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponins may support digestive health and immune function when consumed as part of a balanced herbal regimen.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Blueberry (Vaccinium corymbosum) contribute to its antioxidant properties. These compounds help neutralize free radicals, supporting cellular health. They are found in the fruit's skin and pulp, enhancing the plant's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

8. Coumarin

Coumarin is a compound found in Blueberry (Vaccinium corymbosum). It contributes to the plant's aromatic profile and may have mild anticoagulant properties. While not the primary active constituent, coumarin supports the plant's traditional use in supporting cardiovascular health.

9. Essential oil

The essential oil of Blueberry (Vaccinium corymbosum) contains volatile compounds like limonene and eugenol. These oils are used in aromatherapy and may have antimicrobial properties. They are extracted through steam distillation and are valued for their aromatic and potential therapeutic benefits.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Blueberry (Vaccinium corymbosum). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by interacting with biological systems to support various health benefits. However, alkaloids are not the primary active compounds in blueberries, which are mainly rich in flavonoids and anthocyanins.
