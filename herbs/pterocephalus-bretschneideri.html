Pterocephalus bretschneideri

What's the taxonomical classification of Pterocephalus bretschneideri?

Pterocephalus bretschneideri belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic progression continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Dipsacales. Furthermore, this plant is a member of the family Caprifoliaceae, falling under the genus Pterocephalus, and is specifically identified by its species name, bretschneideri.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pterocephalus bretschneideri has lanceolate leaves that are typically 5 to 15 centimeters long and feature finely serrated margins.

The foliage is characterized by a deep green color and a somewhat leathery texture. Its stems are woody and can become quite robust as the plant matures into a shrub or small tree. Small, inconspicuous flowers often emerge from the leaf axils during the growing season.

The root system is well-developed to support its structural stability in diverse soil types.

        Pterocephalus bretschneideri is prepared as a decoction, a powder, or a topical paste for medicinal use.         What side-effects this plant can have?

        Pterocephalus bretschneideri can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested or handled improperly. Some individuals might experience more severe systemic responses such as swelling or nausea upon accidental consumption. While specific toxicological studies are limited, caution should always be exercised when interacting with its tissues.
