Scientific and Botanical Profile

Blue Canna, with botanical name Plumbago zeylanica, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Rubiaceae.

It is also known by several common names including Indian Plumbago, Ceylon Plumbago, Blue Curls, Canna Indica, Blue Caper, Cape Plumbago, Cape Blue, Blue False Indigo, Indian Canna, Blue Cineraria, Indian Caper, and Cape Of Good Hope. Native to regions of southern and eastern Africa, as well as parts of Asia, including India and Sri Lanka, this species thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Morphologically, it is a herbaceous plant characterized by its blue or violet flowers, which are clustered in umbels, and its slender, branching stems.

The leaves are typically opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a smooth surface, while the plant often grows to a height of 30-60 cm.

Forms, Preparation and Usage

Plumbago zeylanica has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, and it is available in various forms such as fresh leaves, dried tincture, powder, essential oil, and capsule.

For internal use, the plant can be prepared as a tea by steeping dried leaves in hot water, or as a decoction by boiling the plant parts for a longer period. An infusion method is also common, where the leaves are soaked in warm water for a few hours. Topical preparations, such as poultices or essential oil dilutions, are often used for skin ailments. The recommended dosage for adults is typically 1-2 grams of dried herb per cup of tea, while children should only use it under medical supervision.

It is advised to use plumbago zeylanica no more than three times a day for short durations, staying conservative to avoid potential side effects.

Safety, Side Effects and Contraindications

Plumbago zeylanica can be used for its potential medicinal properties, but it must be approached with caution due to its potential toxicity.

Side effects may include gastrointestinal discomfort, nausea, vomiting, and in severe cases, liver damage or renal failure. It is contraindicated in individuals with pre-existing liver or kidney conditions and should be avoided during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to limited safety data. The herb may interact with medications such as anticoagulants, antidiabetics, and chemotherapy drugs, potentially increasing the risk of adverse effects.

To ensure safe use, consult a healthcare professional before use, start with small doses, and monitor for any adverse reactions, especially in vulnerable populations.

Growing, Harvesting and Storage

Plumbago zeylanica grows best in well-drained, loamy soil with a slightly acidic to neutral pH, in full sun to partial shade, and requires regular watering to maintain consistent moisture without waterlogging.

It thrives in warm climates and benefits from mulching to retain soil moisture and suppress weeds. Regular pruning and removal of dead or diseased branches promote healthy growth and prevent pest infestations. The best time to harvest the leaves and stems is during the dry season, preferably in the early morning, using clean, sharp tools to avoid damaging the plant.

After harvesting, the plant parts should be dried in a cool, shaded area with good air circulation, then stored in airtight containers in a cool, dark place to preserve their potency and medicinal properties.
