Blue Canna

Plumbago zeylanica

Blue Canna (Plumbago zeylanica) is a medicinal plant known for its use in traditional medicine to treat various ailments, including respiratory and skin conditions.

Botanical Identification

Plumbago zeylanica is the scientific name of Blue Canna, belonging to the Rubiaceae family. Commonly known as Blue Canna or Indian Leadplant, it is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Asia. The plant features blue-violet flowers, opposite leaves, and a woody root system. It is also referred to as Canna indica in some herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Eudicots
  • Order: Rubiales
  • Family: Rubiaceae
  • Genus: Plumbago
  • Species: Plumbago zeylanica

Blue Canna Identification.

Active Compounds

The Blue Canna contains alkaloids, flavonoids, and glycosides as its main bioactive compounds. These compounds work together to exert their therapeutic effects, with flavonoids providing antioxidant support and glycosides contributing to anti-inflammatory properties. Alkaloids may influence neural pathways, enhancing the herb's overall efficacy in traditional medicinal applications.

  • Alkaloid
  • Flavonoid
  • Glycoside

Blue Canna Actions.

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory and digestive ailments.

Decoction: Used for digestive and inflammatory conditions.

Extract: Used for skin conditions and as a tonic.

Safety Profile

Blue Canna, or Plumbago zeylanica, may pose safety concerns for certain populations. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and lactation due to potential toxicity. Individuals with kidney or liver disease should also avoid its use. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
