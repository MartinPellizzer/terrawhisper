Glyptostrobus pensilis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Glyptostrobus pensilis has an upright, pyramidal growth habit characterized by scale-like, decussate leaves that are small, stiff, and tightly appressed to the twigs. The foliage is typically a dark green color and features a slightly revolute margin. Its branches are arranged in distinct whorls, giving the tree a structured and symmetrical appearance. The reproductive structures consist of small, ovoid cones that emerge from the terminal ends of the branches. The bark of the mature tree tends to be grayish and develops shallow fissures over time.
