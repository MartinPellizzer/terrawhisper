Botanical Identification of Lungwort (Pulmonaria officinalis)

To identify Lungwort (Pulmonaria officinalis) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Lungwort (Pulmonaria officinalis) Monograph

Scientific Classification

Lungwort, Pulmonaria officinalis, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Spermatophyta, class Caryophyllopsida, order Geraniales, family Geraniaceae, genus Pulmonaria, and species Pulmonaria officinalis. These classifications help identify its botanical relationships and place it within the broader system of plant taxonomy.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Lungwort is Pulmonaria officinalis, first described by Carl von Linne in 1753. It has several synonyms, including Pulmonaria vulgaris and Pulmonaria angustifolia. Common names include lungwort, coughwort, and lung plant, while regional names vary across Europe. Historically, it was known as "herbe des poumons" in French and "Lungenkraut" in German.

Morphological Characteristics

Lungwort is a low-growing herb with a fibrous root system. It has upright, hairy stems that branch near the base. Its leaves are basal, heart-shaped, and often covered in a whitish coating. The inflorescence is a dense cluster of flowers, typically pink or purple, that bloom in early spring. The fruit is a three-lobed capsule containing small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Lungwort can be distinguished by its velvety, spotted leaves and showy pink or purple flowers. It grows in moist, shaded areas and has a distinct leaf shape with heart-shaped bases. It is often confused with other Pulmonaria species, but its unique leaf markings and flowering pattern set it apart.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Lungwort (Pulmonaria officinalis) is native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with mild summers and cold winters. It prefers moist, well-drained soils rich in organic matter. It is commonly found at altitudes between 300 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Lungwort blooms from late spring to early summer, typically between May and June. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early autumn. For identification purposes, the plant is best harvested in early spring before flowering, when leaves are most distinct and characteristic.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Pulmonaria officinalis is closely related to species like Pulmonaria anglica and Pulmonaria grandiflora. It belongs to the genus Pulmonaria within the family Geraniaceae. The family traits include opposite leaves, showy flowers, and a preference for moist, shaded habitats.
