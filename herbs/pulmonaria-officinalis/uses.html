Lungwort (Pulmonaria officinalis) Uses

Lungwort has been traditionally used to treat respiratory conditions such as cough, bronchitis, and asthma. It helps reduce inflammation in the respiratory tract and soothe mucosal irritation. It is also applied for sore throat, common cold, and sinusitis due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties.

Lungwort (Pulmonaria officinalis) Monograph

1. Cough

Lungwort has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly for coughs. It contains mucilage that soothes irritated airways and may help reduce cough frequency. Its expectorant properties aid in clearing mucus, making it a valued herb in herbal medicine for respiratory conditions.

2. Bronchitis

Lungwort has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in cases of bronchitis. It is believed to help reduce inflammation and ease coughing by promoting mucus clearance. Its expectorant properties may assist in loosening bronchial secretions, making it a valued herb in herbal medicine for respiratory conditions.

3. Asthma

Lungwort has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in cases of asthma. It is believed to help reduce bronchial spasms and ease breathing. The plant's mucilage content may soothe irritated airways, while its anti-inflammatory properties may reduce airway inflammation. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture for internal use.

4. Respiratory tract inflammation

Lungwort is used to reduce inflammation in the respiratory tract, particularly for conditions like bronchitis and coughs. Its anti-inflammatory and mucolytic properties help soothe irritated airways and ease mucus production, supporting clearer breathing and reduced congestion.

5. Mucosal irritation

Lungwort has been traditionally used to soothe mucosal irritation due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It helps reduce swelling and discomfort in the respiratory tract, making it useful for conditions like bronchitis and sore throats. Its mucilage content provides a protective layer over irritated tissues, promoting healing.

6. Respiratory infections

Lungwort has been traditionally used to treat respiratory infections due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It supports the healing of the lungs and helps alleviate symptoms such as coughing and congestion. Its mucilage content soothes irritated airways, making it a valued herb in herbal medicine for respiratory health.

7. Mucous membrane inflammation

Lungwort is used to treat mucous membrane inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and demulcent properties. It soothes irritated tissues and reduces excessive mucus production. Traditionally applied as a gargle or poultice, it is effective for conditions like sore throat and bronchitis. Its mucilage content provides a protective layer over inflamed areas.

8. Sore throat

Lungwort has been traditionally used to soothe sore throats due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It is often prepared as a gargle or tea to reduce irritation and infection. Its mucilage content helps coat and protect the throat lining, promoting healing. This makes it a valued remedy in herbal medicine for respiratory discomfort.

9. Common cold

Lungwort has been traditionally used to support respiratory health and alleviate symptoms of the common cold. It is believed to help reduce inflammation and ease coughing. Its expectorant properties may assist in clearing mucus from the lungs. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture for internal use.

10. Sinusitis

Lungwort has been traditionally used to treat sinusitis due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. It helps reduce mucus buildup and soothe irritated nasal passages. Its active compounds support respiratory health and may alleviate symptoms of chronic sinus infections. It is often used in herbal formulations for its gentle yet effective action on the respiratory system.
