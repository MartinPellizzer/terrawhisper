Rowan, scientifically known as Sorbus aucuparia, is a versatile herb that has been revered for its health-promoting properties, which have been shown to boost immunity and ward off illnesses.

In culinary traditions, rowan's berries are often used in jams, preserves, and desserts due to their sweet-tart flavor and vibrant color. As an ornamental plant, rowan's striking foliage and white flowers make it a popular choice for landscaping and gardening. From a botanical standpoint, rowan is classified as a deciduous shrub or small tree, characterized by its distinctive winged seeds and compound leaves.

With roots dating back to ancient Celtic and Norse mythology, rowan has played a significant role in folk medicine and rituals throughout history, earning it the nickname "witch's tree".
