What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Trichosanthes rubriflos has prostrate, herbaceous stems that branch extensively from a central base to form a spreading ground cover.

Its most defining morphological feature is the cluster of small, bright red flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These blossoms are typically unisexual and are supported by slender pedicels.

        What are the herbal preparations of Trichosanthes rubriflos?

        Trichosanthes rubriflos is prepared as a decoction, a fine powder, a topical paste, or a concentrated tincture.         What side-effects this plant can have?

        Trichosanthes rubriflos can cause allergic skin reactions, gastrointestinal distress including nausea or diarrhea, and potential dizziness if ingested in improper doses. Some individuals may experience localized itching or redness where the plant makes direct contact with the skin. If consumed, the chemical compounds within the plant might irritate the lining of the digestive tract. It is also possible for people with sensitive systems to undergo more systemic responses like headaches. Always consult a medical professional before any exposure to this species.
