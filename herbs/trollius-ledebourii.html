Trollius ledebourii

What's the taxonomical classification of Trollius ledebourii?

Trollius ledebourii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Ranunculales. This plant is a member of the family Ranunculaceae, which falls under the genus Trollius, and is specifically identified by the species epithet ledebourii.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains ranunculin, which is a glycoside that can be converted into the toxic protoanemonin through hydrolysis.

The presence of these ranunculin-based compounds contributes to the plant's characteristic irritant properties. These chemical constituents are primarily responsible for the potential skin irritation and gastrointestinal effects observed when the plant is handled or ingested. While these compounds serve as a defense mechanism for the species, they are the central focus of its chemical profile.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Trollius ledebourii can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if ingested due to the presence of protoanemonin. This specific chemical compound is a volatile oil found in many plants of the Ranunculaceae family. When the plant tissues are crushed or damaged, the concentration of this irritant increases significantly. Contact with the leaves or stems may lead to redness, itching, or blistering on sensitive skin. Ingestion of any part of the plant can result in severe stomach pain, vomiting, or diarrhea.
