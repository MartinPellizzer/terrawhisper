What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Neocinnamomum delavayi has woody, perennial stems with elliptical or lanceolate leaves that possess entire margins and pinnate venation. The foliage is typically leathery in texture and displays a deep green color on the upper surface. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in clusters along the branches during the blooming season. These flowers eventually give rise to small, fleshy fruits that serve as the primary reproductive structures. The overall architecture of the plant is characterized by a compact, shrubby growth habit.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Neocinnamomum delavayi can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and localized swelling if the plant material comes into direct contact with sensitive tissues. Ingestion of the plant could potentially lead to gastrointestinal discomfort or more severe systemic reactions depending on the concentration. It is important to monitor for any signs of hypersensitivity when handling this species. Always exercise caution to avoid accidental contact with the skin or eyes.
