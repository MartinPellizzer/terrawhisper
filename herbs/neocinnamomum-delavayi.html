Neocinnamomum delavayi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic descent, it is placed in the order Laurales and falls within the family Lauraceae. Finally, its specific identity is defined by its placement in the genus Neocinnamomum, culminating in its unique species designation, delaayi.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Neocinnamomum delavayi can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and localized swelling if the plant material comes into direct contact with sensitive tissues. Some individuals may experience redness or an itchy rash upon exposure to its essential oils or sap. Ingestion of the plant could potentially lead to gastrointestinal discomfort or more severe systemic reactions depending on the concentration. It is important to monitor for any signs of hypersensitivity when handling this species. Always exercise caution to avoid accidental contact with the skin or eyes.
