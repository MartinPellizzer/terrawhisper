Impatiens arguta

What's the taxonomical classification of Impatiens arguta?

Impatiens arguta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and follows the order Ericales. This plant is a member of the family Balsaminaceae, falling under the genus Impatiens, and is specifically identified by its species name, arguta.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Impatiens arguata has succulent, erect stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters and possess serrated, lanceolate leaves arranged in an opposite pattern. The leaf margins are finely toothed, while the leaf bases are often asymmetrical. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, displaying a characteristic zygomorphic shape common to the Balsaminaceae family. The plant is best known for its explosive seed dispersal mechanism, where mature, elongated seed capsules burst open when touched. This morphological adaptation allows the species to efficiently spread its progeny across moist habitats.
