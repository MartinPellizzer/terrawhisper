Aloe × rigida, commonly known as the 'split rock aloe', is a natural hybrid plant of the Aloe genus.

Medicinal Parts of Aloe × rigida

Aloe × rigida has many medicinal parts, such as its gel-filled leaves, latex-filled tubular leaves, tubular inflorescences, and roots, which contain various bioactive compounds.

The latex-filled tubular leaves contain aloin and aloe-emetin, which exhibit purgative and anti-inflammatory activities, and have been traditionally used to treat constipation and skin conditions.

The roots, on the other hand, contain saponins and flavonoids, which are known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, and have been used to treat various gastrointestinal disorders and inflammatory conditions.
