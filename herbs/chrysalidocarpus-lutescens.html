Chrysalidocarpus lutescens

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Chrysalidocarpus lutescens has a single, unbranched trunk that typically reaches heights of up to five meters and is topped by a dense crown of pinnate leaves. The fronds are long and arching, featuring numerous narrow, linear leaflets that emerge from a central rachis. These leaves display a distinct yellowish-green hue, which contributes to the plant's characteristic appearance. The individual leaflets are arranged symmetrically and provide a lush, feathery texture to the canopy. Small, globose fruits develop between the leaf bases once the plant reaches maturity.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the island of Reunion in the Indian Ocean. It is found exclusively in the humid, high-altitude forests of the island's rugged volcanic terrain. Within these specific microclimates, it thrives in moist soils shaded by dense canopy cover. Its restricted range makes it highly susceptible to local environmental changes and habitat loss.
