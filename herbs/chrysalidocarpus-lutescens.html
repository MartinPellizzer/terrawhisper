What's the taxonomical classification of Chrysalidocarpus lutescens?

Chrysalidocarpus lutescens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Arecales. This plant is a representative of the family Arecaceae, specifically categorized under the genus Chrysalidocarpus, with its unique biological identity defined by the species name lutescens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Chrysalidocarpus lutescens has a single, unbranched trunk that typically reaches heights of up to five meters and is topped by a dense crown of pinnate leaves. The fronds are long and arching, featuring numerous narrow, linear leaflets that emerge from a central rachis. These leaves display a distinct yellowish-green hue, which contributes to the plant's characteristic appearance. The individual leaflets are arranged symmetrically and provide a lush, feathery texture to the canopy. Small, globose fruits develop between the leaf bases once the plant reaches maturity.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and respiratory distress due to the presence of calcium oxalate crystals in its tissues. When the plant material is handled without gloves, the sharp crystals can puncture the skin and cause immediate itching or swelling. Contact with the sap may also lead to contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. If the leaves or stems are crushed and the dust is inhaled, it can irritate the mucous membranes of the throat and lungs. It is important to exercise caution and use protective gear when pruning or moving this species.
