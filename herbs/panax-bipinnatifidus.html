What's the taxonomical classification of Panax bipinnatifidus?

Panax bipinnatifidus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is classified under the order Apiales. It is a member of the family Araliaceae and falls within the genus Panax, eventually reaching its specific designation as the species bipinnatifidus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Panax bipinnatifidus has compound leaves that are twice-pinnately divided into multiple lanceolate or ovate leaflets with serrated margins. The plant typically features a rhizomatous root system that facilitates its underground growth. Its stems are relatively short and support the complex arrangement of foliage. Small, white, umbel-like flowers emerge from the center of the leaf clusters. The resulting fruit consists of small, fleshy drupes that transition in color as they mature.
