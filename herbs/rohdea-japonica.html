Rohdea japonica

What's the taxonomical classification of Rohdea japonica?

Rohdea japonica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its further classification, the plant is placed in the order Asparagales and the family Asparagaceae. Ultimately, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement in the genus Rohdea, with its unique designation being the species japonica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rohdea japonica has evergreen, leathery, lanceolate leaves that grow in basal clusters and small, white, bell-shaped flowers emerging from a central scape. During the flowering season, the plant produces several small blossoms on a single stem that rises above the foliage. After flowering, it develops distinctive bright red berries that persist through the winter months.
