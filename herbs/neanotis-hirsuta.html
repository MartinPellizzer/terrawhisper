Neanotis hirsuta

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Neanotis hirsuta has a stem structure characterized by dense, stiff, hair-like trichomes that cover the epidermis and provide a rough texture. These bristly hairs are distributed unevenly across the vegetative surfaces, creating a fuzzy appearance. The leaves are typically small and lanceolate, emerging from the base of the plant in a compact arrangement. Each leaf margin is slightly serrated and often displays a subtle pubescence that matches the stem. This morphological trait serves as a primary diagnostic feature for distinguishing the species from its smoother relatives.

      What are the herbal preparations of Neanotis hirsuta?

      Neanotis hirsuta is prepared as a decoction, a topical poultice, or a concentrated tincture depending on the specific ailment being treated. When making a decoction, the dried roots or leaves are simmered in water to extract potent medicinal compounds for internal consumption. These various methods allow practitioners to tailor the strength and delivery of the plant's active constituents to the patient's needs.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Neanotis hirsuta can cause severe skin irritation, localized dermatitis, and painful swelling if the plant makes direct contact with human flesh. The fine hairs covering its surface often act as mechanical irritants that penetrate the upper layers of the epidermis. Once these hairs are embedded, they can trigger an intense itching sensation or an allergic reaction in sensitive individuals. Some people may also experience mild swelling or redness that persists for several days. It is important to avoid touching the foliage to prevent these dermatological complications.
