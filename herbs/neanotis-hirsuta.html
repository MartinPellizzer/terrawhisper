Neanotis hirsuta

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Neanotis hirsuta has a stem structure characterized by dense, stiff, hair-like trichomes that cover the epidermis and provide a rough texture. These bristly hairs are distributed unevenly across the vegetative surfaces, creating a fuzzy appearance. The leaves are typically small and lanceolate, emerging from the base of the plant in a compact arrangement. Each leaf margin is slightly serrated and often displays a subtle pubescence that matches the stem. This morphological trait serves as a primary diagnostic feature for distinguishing the species from its smoother relatives.
