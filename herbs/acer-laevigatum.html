Acer laevigatum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Acer laevigatum has opposite, simple leaves that are broadly ovate to cordate in shape with shallowly lobed margins and a smooth, entire texture. The leaf blades typically feature a base that is slightly asymmetrical and a tip that tapers to a fine point. Its stems are characterized by a smooth bark that becomes more fissured as the specimen matures into a woody shrub or small tree. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge in clusters during the flowering season. Woody branches extend from the main structure, supporting the dense canopy of foliage.
