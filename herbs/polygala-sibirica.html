Polygala sibirica

What's the taxonomical classification of Polygala sibirica?

Polygala sibirica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage leading to the order Fabales. This plant is a representative of the family Polygalaceae and is further categorized under the genus Polygala, with its specific identification as the species sibirica.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Polygala sibirica contains various triterpene saponins, specifically polygalic acid and its derivatives, which provide the plant's primary pharmacological activities. These bioactive saponins function as central nervous system stimulants and exhibit significant neuroprotective properties. The plant also possesses phenolic compounds and flavonoids that contribute to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. Studies suggest these chemical constituents can help improve cognitive function and memory enhancement in various biological models. Additionally, some of its compounds demonstrate potential as adaptogens by helping the body manage physiological stress.

What are the therapeutic actions of Polygala sibirica?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various respiratory conditions including bronchitis, asthma, coughs, and phlegm accumulation. It functions primarily by acting as an expectorant to help clear mucus from the airways. The medicinal properties are also applied to alleviate symptoms of chronic bronchitis and other lung-related ailments. Beyond respiratory issues, it is sometimes utilized in traditional medicine to address certain skin irritations and neurological discomforts. Its bioactive compounds play a significant role in stimulating the secretion of fluids in the bronchial tubes.
