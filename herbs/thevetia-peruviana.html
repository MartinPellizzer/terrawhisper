Thevetia peruviana

What's the taxonomical classification of Thevetia peruviana?

Thevetia peruviana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this classification, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its lineage further, it falls under the order Gentianales and is a member of the family Apocynaceae. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its genus, Thevetia, and its specific species name, peruviana.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Thevetia peruvians contains high concentrations of cardiac glycosides, primarily resembling digitoxin and thevetin A and B, which act directly on the sodium-potassium pump of heart muscle cells. These potent organic compounds are responsible for the plant's ability to increase the force of myocardial contractions. Because of these chemical properties, extracts from the plant have been studied for their potential to treat specific heart conditions under strict medicinal supervision. However, the narrow therapeutic window of these glycosides means that even slight deviations in dosage can lead to severe toxicity. Careful extraction and processing are required to harness these molecules for pharmaceutical purposes.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin diseases, digestive ailments, and certain types of inflammation. In traditional medicine, its extracts are sometimes applied topically to manage dermatological issues. Some practitioners also utilize the plant to address stomach-related discomforts through controlled applications. However, it is essential to note that the plant contains highly toxic cardiac glycosides. Because of this toxicity, its medicinal application must be approached with extreme caution to avoid severe poisoning.
