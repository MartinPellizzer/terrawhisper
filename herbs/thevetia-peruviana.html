Thevetia peruviana

What's the taxonomical classification of Thevetia peruviana?

Thevetia peruviana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this classification, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its lineage further, it falls under the order Gentianales and is a member of the family Apocynaceae. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its genus, Thevetia, and its specific species name, peruviana.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Thevetia peruvia contains toxic cardiac glycosides concentrated primarily in its seeds, leaves, and bark. These specific components are often utilized in traditional medicine to treat various ailments, though they require extreme caution due to their high potency. The seeds are sometimes processed for their potential to affect heart rate, while the bark is used in certain herbal preparations. Because the plant contains substances similar to digitalis, the parts are used with strict dosage control to manage cardiovascular conditions. Misuse of any plant part can lead to severe poisoning or fatal cardiac complications.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains cardiac glycosides, alkaloids, flavonoids, terpenoids, and phenolic compounds that contribute to its complex pharmacological profile.

The presence of cardiac glycosides makes it particularly potent due to their direct effects on heart muscle contraction. Alkaloids and terpenoids within the plant are often studied for their potential antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Flavonoids and phenolic compounds provide antioxidant activity that can help neutralize free radicals in biological systems.

These diverse secondary metabolites collectively define the plant's medicinal potential and its significant toxicity.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Thevetia peruviana targets the Na+/K+-ATPase enzyme by utilizing cardiac glycosides like thevetin A and B to inhibit its activity. This inhibition leads to an increase in intracellular sodium concentrations, which subsequently affects the sodium-calcium exchanger. By modulating this exchanger, the plant induces an increase in cytosolic calcium levels within myocardial cells. These calcium fluctuations enhance the force of cardiac muscle contractions, explaining its potent cardiotonic effects. However, the high affinity for these specific protein pumps also accounts for the plant's significant potential toxicity in humans.

What are the therapeutic actions of Thevetia peruviana?

Thevetia peruvians has potent cardiac glycoside properties that act by inhibiting the sodium-potassium ATPase pump to increase the force of myocardial contractions. These specific chemical compounds influence the electrical activity of the heart to help regulate irregular rhythms. Due to this mechanism, the plant has been historically studied for its potential to treat certain types of heart failure. However, the therapeutic window is extremely narrow, meaning even minor miscalculations in dosage can lead to severe toxicity. Careful pharmacological management is essential because the same action that strengthens the heart can also cause lethal arrhythmias.

Extracts derived from its leaves and roots are frequently applied topically to soothe skin irritations and manage wounds. In some traditional medicinal practices, specific preparations are administered to combat digestive issues caused by parasitic organisms. It is important to note that the plant contains potent cardiac glycosides that can be extremely toxic if ingested incorrectly.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Thevetia peruviana can cause severe cardiac toxicity, including life-threatening arrhythmias, nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain, due to the presence of potent cardiac glycosides in all its parts. If ingested, these toxins interfere with the sodium-potassium pump in heart muscle cells, which can lead to a dangerous slowing of the heart rate. Patients experiencing poisoning may also suffer from dizziness, confusion, and blurred vision. In extreme cases, the physiological disruption can result in heart failure or death. Immediate medical intervention is crucial for anyone suspected of consuming this highly toxic plant.
