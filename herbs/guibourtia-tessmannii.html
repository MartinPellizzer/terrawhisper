Guibourtia tessmannii

What's the taxonomical classification of Guibourtia tessmannii?

Guibourtia tessmannii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant falls under the order Fabales and is a member of the family Fabaceae. Specifically, its taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Guibourtia, with the specific epithet tessmannii denoting its unique species classification.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, stomach ailments, and various inflammatory conditions. The extracts derived from the plant possess antimicrobial properties that help combat bacterial and fungal pathogens on the body. It is also applied topically to facilitate the healing process of cutaneous lesions and abrasions. In some traditional practices, the plant is administered internally to soothe gastrointestinal distress. These medicinal applications highlight its diverse role in managing both superficial and systemic health issues.
