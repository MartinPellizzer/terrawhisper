Irvingia grandifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Irvingia grandifolia?

Irvingia grandifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it in the order Malpighiales. Within this order, the plant is a member of the family Irvingiaceae and falls under the genus Irvingia, specifically identified by the species name grandifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Irvingia grandifolia has large, elliptical to oblong leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length and are characterized by an entire margin and a coriaceous texture. The foliage is typically arranged alternately along the woody stems of the tree. Its flowers are relatively small and are often found in clusters within the leaf axils. The plant produces distinctive oval or ellipsoidal drupes that contain a single large seed. These seeds are encased in a fleshy pulp that serves as a key morphological feature for identification.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical rainforest regions of West and Central Africa, spanning from Senegal and Guinea through Nigeria to the Democratic Republic of the Congo. It thrives primarily in the moist, lowland forests characteristic of the Guineo-Congolian regional center of endemism. Within these habitats, the species is often found in dense vegetation where sunlight and humidity are abundant. Localized populations are frequently documented in countries such as Cameroon, Gabon, and Ivory Coast. Its distribution remains closely tied to the presence of stable, undisturbed tropical ecosystems.

What side effects this plant can have?
