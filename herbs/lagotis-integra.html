Lagotis integra

What's the taxonomical classification of Lagotis integra?

Lagotis integra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant falls under the order Lamiales. It is further categorized into the family Plantaginaceae, specifically within the genus Lagotis, and is identified by its species name, integra.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lagotis integra has a creeping rhizome that produces upright, simple, or sparsely branched stems reaching heights of 10 to 40 centimeters. The leaves are primarily basal and kidney-shaped with crenate margins, though some stem leaves may appear more lanceolate. Each leaf possesses a long petiole and a distinctively rounded, fleshy texture. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in terminal spikes and typically exhibit a pale yellowish or greenish hue. The plant's overall structure is characterized by its low-growing, herbaceous habit suited for damp environments.

What are the pharmacological activities of Lagotis integra?

This plant has potent anti-ulcerative colitis (UC) effects, alongside significant anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties. Researchers have identified that its bioactive compounds work to suppress inflammatory cytokines and reduce oxidative stress within the intestinal mucosa. These pharmacological actions help mitigate tissue damage and stabilize the gut barrier in subjects suffering from ulcerative colitis. Additionally, the plant exhibits protective mechanisms that can limit the progression of chronic intestinal inflammation. This multi-targeted approach makes it a subject of interest for managing complex gastrointestinal disorders.

As discussed in a study, Lagotis integra W. W. Smith was analyzed to identify 32 major components, which included 8 flavonoids, 9 phenylpropanoid glycosides, 13 iridoid glycosides, and 1 phenolic acid. Network pharmacology analysis screened out 76 potential core therapeutic targets, specifically identifying the top 5 key targets as AKT1, VEGFA, TNF-α, EGFR, and CASP3. Animal experiments confirmed that these compounds could downregulate the expression levels of these 5 key target proteins in the colonic tissue of mice to exert an anti-UC effect. Molecular docking results identified the main active components as echinacoside, hemiproside B, plantamajoside, plantainoside D, 10-O-trans-isoferuloyl catalpol, and scutellarioside II.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains salicin, flavonoids, tannins, and various alkaloids that provide its therapeutic properties. Flavonoids within the leaves contribute antioxidant activity that protects cells from oxidative stress. Additionally, the tannins in the plant exhibit astringent qualities that can assist in treating digestive issues. These combined chemical compounds allow the plant to be used effectively in traditional medicine for pain relief and healing.
