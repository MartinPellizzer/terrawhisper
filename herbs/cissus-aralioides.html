Cissus aralioides

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cissus aralioides has woody, climbing stems with opposite, palmately lobed leaves that typically feature three to five distinct leaflets. The foliage displays a glossy texture and can range from light green to deep emerald depending on the light exposure. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils in branched clusters known as cymes. These floral structures eventually give way to small, fleshy berries that change color as they mature. The plant often develops aerial roots along its vines to assist in climbing nearby structures.
