Cissus aralioides

What's the taxonomical classification of Cissus aralioides?

Cissus aralioides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized under Equisetopsida, which falls into the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized under the order Vitales and the family Vitaeaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic placement is defined by its membership in the genus Cissus and its designation as the species aralioides.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Cissus aralioides contains bioactive compounds such as alkaloids, saponins, flavonoids, and tannins that contribute to its therapeutic properties. These secondary metabolites are primarily responsible for the plant's observed antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities. In traditional medicine, extracts from the leaves and roots are utilized to treat various ailments including skin infections and digestive issues. The specific chemical profile of the plant allows it to interact with biological pathways to mitigate oxidative stress. Ongoing research continues to investigate how these specific constituents can be isolated for potential pharmacological applications.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including skin diseases, wounds, and internal inflammation. The leaves and stems are often applied topically to soothe irritations or promote the healing of open sores. Some traditional practices also utilize the plant to address digestive issues and systemic discomfort. It is primarily valued in folk medicine for its purported antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. These applications help manage localized infections and general bodily swelling.
