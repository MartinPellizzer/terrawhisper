What's the taxonomical classification of Corydalis ledebouriana?

Corydalis ledebouriana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its classification through the order Ranunculales, it is placed into the family Papaveraceae. Ultimately, the plant is categorized under the genus Corydalis, with its specific designation being the species ledebourianas.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Corydalis ledebouriana has deeply lobed, glaucous green leaves and slender, upright stems that support clusters of zygomorphic, tubular flowers. The floral structure is characterized by a prominent, elongated nectar spur that extends backward from the base of the corolla. Each blossom typically features a combination of pale yellow or creamy white petals with subtle violet or pinkish markings near the throat. The foliage is composed of multiple pinnatifid leaflets that provide a delicate, feathery texture to the plant's overall appearance. Small, capsule-like fruits develop following pollination, eventually splitting to release numerous tiny seeds.
