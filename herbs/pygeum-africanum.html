Pygeum

Pygeum africanum

Pygeum (Pygeum africanum) is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa, known for its bark, which has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to support prostate health and alleviate symptoms of benign prostatic hyperplasia.

Botanical Identification

Pygeum africanum, commonly known as African prickly pear, belongs to the family Cactaceae. It is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa, characterized by its spiny stems, paddle-shaped leaves, and yellow flowers. Synonyms include Opuntia africana and Cereus africanus, reflecting its historical use in herbal medicine.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Eudicots
  • Order: Rubiales
  • Family: Rubiaceae
  • Genus: Pygeum
  • Species: Pygeum africanum

Active Compounds

Pygeum africanum contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids. These compounds work synergistically to support prostate health by reducing inflammation and improving urinary function. Their combined action enhances the herb's efficacy in traditional herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Tannin
  • Phenolic acid

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for urinary tract disorders and inflammation due to its diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and as a general tonic due to its warming and digestive properties.

Extract: Used for prostate health and as a general tonic due to its high concentration of active compounds.

Safety Profile

Pygeum is generally well-tolerated with few reported side effects. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation due to limited safety data. Individuals with known allergies to plants in the cashew family should also avoid its use.
