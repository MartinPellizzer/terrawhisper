Pygeum, scientifically known as Pygeum africanum, is a medicinal herb native to Africa, particularly found in the dense forests of Central and Southern Africa.

In modern wellness practices, Pygeum is often incorporated into supplements and herbal formulations aimed at supporting prostate health, and it is noted for its unique, slightly bitter and woody flavor, as well as its rich content of fatty acids and phytochemicals that contribute to its therapeutic effects.

Growing, Harvesting and Storage

Pygeum africanum grows in the tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like South Africa, Madagascar, and Kenya, where it thrives in well-drained, loamy soils with a pH between 5.5 and 7.5.

It requires full sun exposure to ensure optimal growth and medicinal compound production, though it can tolerate partial shade in early growth stages. Regular watering is necessary during the growing season, but the plant is drought-tolerant once established, making it suitable for semi-arid climates. For care and maintenance, pruning dead or diseased branches annually and applying organic mulch helps promote healthy growth and prevent pests. Harvesting is best done during the dry season, typically between April and September, when the bark contains the highest concentration of active compounds; the bark is carefully stripped from the trunk using a sharp knife, ensuring not to damage the tree.

After harvesting, the bark should be thoroughly dried in a well-ventilated area away from direct sunlight to preserve its potency, then stored in airtight containers in a cool, dry place or refrigerated to extend shelf life.
