What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Paeonia × Suffruticosa (Tree Peony)?

Paeonia × suffruticosa, also known as tree peony, has health benefits such as anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Its medicinal constituents include paeoniflorin, paeonol, and benzaldehyde, which contribute to its therapeutic effects. Paeonia × suffruticosa is often used in traditional medicine to make tea, tinctures, and ointments. Potential side effects include gastrointestinal upset and allergic reactions.

Precautions should be taken to avoid interactions with other medications and to use it in moderation due to its potential effects on the digestive system and skin.

What are the health benefits of Paeonia × suffruticosa?

Paeonia × suffruticosa, also known as tree peony, has health benefits such as anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

It has been used in traditional medicine for centuries to treat a variety of ailments, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues.

The flowers and roots of the tree peony are rich in compounds that have been shown to reduce pain and inflammation, making it a valuable remedy for various health conditions.

What are the active constituents of Paeonia × suffruticosa?

Paeonia × suffruticosa, also known as tree peony, has active constituents such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and anthocyanins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Flavonoids like paeoniflorin and benzyl isothiocyanate have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects, while phenolic acids exhibit antioxidant activity.

The tree peony's medicinal constituents also include alkaloids like paeonol, which has been shown to have analgesic and anti-inflammatory effects, making it a valued ingredient in traditional Chinese medicine.

What are the medicinal preparations of Paeonia × suffruticosa?

Paeonia × suffruticosa, also known as tree peony, has medicinal preparations such as root bark extracts and rhizomes that have been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries to treat various conditions.

The extracts are believed to have anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and anti-anxiety properties, and are used to treat conditions such as fever, rheumatism, and skin irritations.

It is also used as a sedative and to treat insomnia.
