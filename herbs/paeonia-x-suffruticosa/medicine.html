What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Paeonia × Suffruticosa (Tree Peony)?

Paeonia × suffruticosa, also known as tree peony, has health benefits such as relieving fever, rheumatism, and bronchitis.

The herb contains flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. Tree peony is traditionally used to prepare teas, decoctions, and tinctures that are applied topically or taken orally to treat various ailments.

However, excessive consumption can cause dizziness, nausea, and gastrointestinal issues, and patients with bleeding disorders should use the herb under medical supervision.

This article explains the health benefits, active constituents, medicinal preparations, possible side effects, and precautions related to Paeonia × suffruticosa.

What are the health benefits of Paeonia × suffruticosa?

Paeonia × suffruticosa has health benefits such as anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help reduce symptoms of arthritis and other chronic pain conditions.

Its extracts have been shown to exhibit antimicrobial and antiviral activities, making it a potential remedy for infections.

Additionally, the plant's flavonoids and saponins may help lower cholesterol levels and improve cardiovascular health, while its estrogenic properties could alleviate menopausal symptoms.

What are the active constituents of Paeonia × suffruticosa?

The health benefits of Paeonia × suffruticosa comes from its active constituents, such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and anthocyanins.

These compounds have been shown to possess anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antibacterial properties, making it a popular natural remedy for various health issues including arthritis, menstrual cramps, and skin conditions.

Additionally, the plant's saponins have been found to exhibit cardiotonic and antispasmodic activities, which may help alleviate respiratory issues like bronchitis and asthma.

What are the medicinal preparations of Paeonia × suffruticosa?

Paeonia × suffruticosa has different medicinal preparations, such as dried root, flower, and bark extracts.

Dried root extract is used to treat fever, rheumatism, and menstrual cramps due to its analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Flower extract is used in traditional Chinese medicine to soothe respiratory issues like bronchitis and asthma, while bark extract is employed to alleviate skin conditions like eczema and dermatitis.

What are the possible side effect of using Paeonia × suffruticosa improperly?

Improper use of Paeonia × suffruticosa increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as allergic reactions, skin irritation, digestive issues like bloating and stomach discomfort, respiratory problems including asthma attacks and bronchitis, headaches and migraines, fatigue, insomnia, dizziness, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, urinary tract infections, kidney damage, liver dysfunction, cardiovascular complications, and even life-threatening conditions like anaphylaxis and blood clots.

What precautions to take when using Paeonia × suffruticosa medicinally?

Before using Paeonia × suffruticosa for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as ensuring the plant is correctly identified and of a reliable quality to avoid adulteration or contamination.

It's also essential to follow proper dosing guidelines, as excessive consumption can lead to adverse effects, including gastrointestinal upset and allergic reactions.

Furthermore, individuals with pre-existing medical conditions or taking medications should consult with a healthcare professional before using tree peony medicinally.
