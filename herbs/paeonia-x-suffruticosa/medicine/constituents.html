10 Medicinal Constituents Of Paeonia × Suffruticosa (Tree Peony)

Paeonia × suffruticosa has active constituents such as paeonol, paeonol acetate, and paeoniflorin.

These compounds possess anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antispasmodic properties, which can alleviate pain and reduce inflammation in conditions like arthritis. The constituents also exhibit antioxidant and cardiovascular-protective effects, potentially improving heart health and reducing the risk of cardiovascular disease.

This can lead to improved quality of life for individuals suffering from these conditions.

1. Paenoflorin

Paeonia × suffruticosa paenoflorin is a unique compound found in its roots.

It has been extensively researched for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties. Paenoflorin has been shown to have a positive effect on the immune system and may help to protect against certain diseases.

Additionally, it has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries to treat various ailments, including fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions.

2. Paeoniflorin

Paeonia × suffruticosa paeoniflorin is a type of bioactive compound found in the roots and leaves of this plant species.

4. Mucilage

Paeonia × suffruticosa mucilage is a thick, protective fluid secreted by the plant's tissues.

This remarkable substance has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries, prized for its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

Paeonia × suffruticosa anthocyanins is a type of water-soluble pigment responsible for its characteristic deep red to purple color.

The high content of anthocyanins in tree peony makes it a valuable resource for pharmaceutical applications and cosmetic products.
