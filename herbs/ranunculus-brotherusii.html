Ranunculus brotherusii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ranunculus brotherusii has perennial herbaceous stems that emerge from a thick, fleshy rhizome and produce leaves that are primarily basal and cordate to reniform in shape. The leaf margins are often deeply lobed or dentate, displaying a distinct texture that varies depending on the plant's maturity. Its flowering structures consist of bright yellow actinomorphic flowers characterized by five distinct petals and numerous central stamens. These blooms are supported by pedicels that arise from the leaf axils or a central scape. Small, globose achenes form the fruit, each typically possessing a small beak at the apex.
