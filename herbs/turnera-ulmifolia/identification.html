Botanical Identification of Turnera Ulmifolia (Turnera ulmifolia)

To identify Turnera Ulmifolia (Turnera ulmifolia) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Turnera ulmifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Rosales, family Rosaceae, genus Turnera, and species Turnera ulmifolia. This classification places it within the flowering plants and the broader group of eudicots, which are characterized by two seed leaves.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Turnera ulmifolia is the accepted scientific name for this species, first described by the botanist André Michaux in 1794. It is also known by several synonyms, including Turnera ulmifolia Michx., and has various common names such as "Mexican turnera" and "turnera tree." Regional and historical names reflect its widespread use in traditional herbal medicine across Latin America.

Morphological Characteristics

Turnera ulmifolia is a shrub with a compact growth habit. It has a fibrous root system that anchors it firmly. The stems are slender and branching, often covered in fine hairs. Leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges. The inflorescence is a terminal panicle, with small yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is a capsule that releases small, flat seeds when mature.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Turnera ulmifolia can be distinguished by its opposite, ovate leaves with smooth margins and a distinct yellowish-green underside. The flowers are yellow with five petals and are borne in clusters. It lacks the aromatic scent and hairy texture found in similar species like Turnera subulata. Its leaf shape and floral structure are key identifiers.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Turnera ulmifolia is native to Central and South America, particularly in regions like Mexico and the Caribbean. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity. The plant prefers well-drained, loamy soils and is commonly found at low to mid-elevation altitudes, typically between 300 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Turnera ulmifolia blooms from April to June, producing yellow flowers. Seed formation occurs from July to September. For identification purposes, the best time to harvest is during the flowering period, typically in late spring to early summer.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Turnera ulmifolia is closely related to species within the genus Turnera, which belongs to the family Melastomataceae. It shares key family traits such as opposite leaves and showy flowers, while distinguishing itself through its specific leaf shape and habitat preferences. Its phylogenetic position reflects its evolutionary ties to other Melastomataceae genera.
