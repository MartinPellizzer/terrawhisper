Turnera Ulmifolia (Turnera ulmifolia) Compounds

Turnera ulmifolia contains several primary active compounds, including flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and tannins, which contribute to its astringent effects. Phenolic acids and coumarin also play roles in its medicinal properties, while saponins and terpenoids may support anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial actions. Glycosides, essential oils, alkaloids, and anthocyanins further contribute to its diverse therapeutic potential.

Turnera Ulmifolia (Turnera ulmifolia) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Turnera ulmifolia are bioactive compounds known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments. They help neutralize free radicals and modulate immune responses, supporting the plant's therapeutic potential.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Turnera ulmifolia, known for its astringent properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating inflammation and digestive issues.
