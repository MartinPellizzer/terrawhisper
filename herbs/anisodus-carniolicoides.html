What's the taxonomical classification of Anisodus carniolicoides?

Anisodus carniolicoides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is part of the order Solanales and the family Solanaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific genus, Anisodus, and its unique species designation, carniolicoides.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Anisodus carniolicoides has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by erect stems and oppositely arranged, ovate to lanceolate leaves with entire or slightly wavy margins.

    What are the molecular targets of this plant?

    Anisodus carniolicoides targets nicotinic acetylcholine receptors (nAChRs) and muscarinic acetylcholine receptors (mAChRs) through its high concentration of tropane alkaloids like hyoscyamine and scopolamine. These alkaloids act as competitive antagonists that bind to specific subunits of the cholinergic receptors in the central and peripheral nervous systems. By blocking these receptor sites, the plant's chemical constituents inhibit the action of acetylcholine, which leads to significant anticholinergic effects. This interaction is primarily responsible for the plant's medicinal potential in treating gastrointestinal spasms and reducing excessive secretions. Such molecular interference also modulates autonomic functions, providing sedative and antispasmodic properties.
