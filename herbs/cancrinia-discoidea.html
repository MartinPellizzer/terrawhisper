What's the taxonomical classification of Cancrinia discoidea?

Cancrinia discoidea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Asterales, this plant is a member of the family Asteraceae. It is specifically categorized under the genus Cancrinia, with its unique biological identity defined by its species name, discoidea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cancrinia discoidea has a prostrate, woody growth habit characterized by spreading, cylindrical stems that form dense, ground-hugging mats. The leaves are small, linear to narrowly lanceolate, and are arranged alternately along the stems. These plants produce distinctive discoid flower heads that lack ray florets and are typically yellow in color. The involucral bracts are arranged in several rows, providing a protective structure for the developing florets. This specialized morphology allows the species to thrive in the harsh, arid environments of the Australian outback.
