Begonia pedatifida

What's the taxonomical classification of Begonia pedatifida?

Begonia pedatifida belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It falls under the order Cucurbitales and is a member of the family Begoniaceae. Within this family, the plant is placed in the genus Begonia, and its specific identification is defined by the species pedatifida.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Begonia pedatifida has deeply lobed, pedate leaves that are characterized by distinct, irregular divisions extending from the central veins toward the margins. The leaf blades exhibit a complex, palmately dissected structure that gives them a jagged and highly textured appearance. These leaves are typically asymmetrical at the base, a common trait among many species within the Begonia genus. The margins of the lobes are often serrated or irregularly toothed, adding to the plant's intricate silhouette. Stems supporting these leaves are fleshy and often possess a reddish or greenish tint depending on the environmental conditions.
