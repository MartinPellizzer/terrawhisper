Cananga odorata

What's the taxonomical classification of Cananga odorata?

Cananga odorata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, following the evolutionary path of the order Magnoliales. The plant is a member of the family Annonaceae, which places it within the genus Cananga, and its specific biological identity is defined by the species odorata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cananga odorata has pinnate leaves measuring up to 30 centimeters in length, yellowish-green drooping flowers with long narrow petals, and small globose drupes that turn yellow when ripe. The tree typically reaches heights of 12 to 25 meters and features a dense canopy with spreading branches. Its flowers are characterized by six long, slender, drooping petals that give the blossoms a distinctively shaggy appearance. The fruit develops into an ovoid shape and contains a single seed. These morphological features combined make the species easily identifiable within tropical forest environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, extending from the Andaman and Nicobar Islands through Myanmar, Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia to the Philippines. It thrives primarily in moist, lowland rainforest environments where humidity is consistently high. Within these ecosystems, the species is frequently found along riverbanks and in disturbed forest areas. Its range also includes parts of southern China and various Pacific islands where the climate remains warm and subtropical. These specific habitats allow the species to maintain its broad but localized distribution across the Indo-Malayan realm.

What are the pharmacological activities of Cananga odorata?

This plant has analgesic, anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, antioxidant, and antidepressant pharmacological activities. Its bioactive compounds work to reduce pain and mitigate swelling by inhibiting various inflammatory pathways in the body. The presence of phytochemicals allows the plant to combat harmful microorganisms and neutralize free radicals through its potent antioxidant properties. Furthermore, certain extracts demonstrate significant potential in managing depressive symptoms by influencing neurotransmitter levels. These diverse therapeutic effects make Cananga odorata a subject of interest for various medicinal applications.

As discussed in a study, the essential oil obtained from the flowers of Cananga odorata (Lam.) Hook. f. & Thomson reduces neuropathic pain and associated anxiety symptoms in mice. Oral administration of 30 mg/kg of the essential oil reduced neuropathic pain induced by the spared nerve injury model. This treatment also ameliorated pain-related anxiety symptoms that appeared 28 days after surgery. The oil reduced the expression of MAPK, NOS2, and p-p65, which are markers of neuroinflammation. Additionally, the essential oil promoted a normalizing effect on BDNF neurotrophin levels.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds gamma-eudesmol, gamma-eudesmol 11-alpha-L-rhamnoside, germacrene D, beta-linalool, and cananodine. These specific chemical constituents are often studied for their potential bioactive properties in pharmaceutical research. The presence of sesquiterpenes like germacrene D contributes to the unique chemical profile of the species. Additionally, the glycoside gamma-eudesmol 11-alpha-L-rhamnoside plays a significant role in the plant's secondary metabolism. Together, these compounds form a complex matrix that defines the therapeutic interest in this botanical specimen.

As discussed in a study, the fruits of Cananga odorata contain a new guaipyridine sesquiterpene alkaloid identified as cananodine (1). Two new euudesmane sesquiterpene compounds, cryptomeridiol 11-alpha-L-rhamnoside (2) and gamma-eudesmol 11-alpha-L-rhamnoside (3), were also isolated alongside gamma-eudesmol (4). These compounds 1-4 were evaluated for cytotoxicity in conjunction with four previously reported alkaloids, which are cleistopholine (5), N-trans-feruloyltyramine (6), (+)-ushinsunine-beta-N-oxide (7), and lyscamine (8). The investigation tested these substances against 2 human hepatocarcinoma cell lines. The structures for compounds 1-3 were established using NMR and MS methods.

What are the herbal preparations of this plant?

This plant can be prepared as an essential oil, tincture, infusion, or decoction to extract its various therapeutic properties. What side effects this plant can have?
