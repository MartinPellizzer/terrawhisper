Cananga odorata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cananga odorata has pinnate leaves measuring up to 30 centimeters in length, yellowish-green drooping flowers with long narrow petals, and small globose drupes that turn yellow when ripe. The tree typically reaches heights of 12 to 25 meters and features a dense canopy with spreading branches. Its flowers are characterized by six long, slender, drooping petals that give the blossoms a distinctively shaggy appearance. The fruit develops into an ovoid shape and contains a single seed. These morphological features combined make the species easily identifiable within tropical forest environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, extending from the Andaman and Nicobar Islands through Myanmar, Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia to the Philippines. It thrives primarily in moist, lowland rainforest environments where humidity is consistently high. Within these ecosystems, the species is frequently found along riverbanks and in disturbed forest areas. Its range also includes parts of southern China and various Pacific islands where the climate remains warm and subtropical. These specific habitats allow the species to maintain its broad but localized distribution across the Indo-Malayan realm.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Cananga odorata contains various bioactive compounds such as linalool, benzyl acetate, geraniol, and methyl benzoate that provide significant therapeutic potential. These essential oils exhibit strong antimicrobial, antifungal, and anti-inflammatory properties when extracted from the flowers. Linalool specifically serves as a key component for its sedative and anxiolytic effects on the central nervous system. The presence of various esters and terpenes also allows the plant to be used in traditional medicine to treat skin conditions and promote relaxation. Research suggests these chemical constituents may also offer antioxidant benefits to protect cells from oxidative stress.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Cananga odorata targets various neurotransmitter receptors, enzyme systems, and inflammatory pathways to exert its sedative, antimicrobial, and anti-inflammatory effects. The essential oils found in the plant, such as linalool and benzyl acetate, primarily interact with GABAergic systems to modulate central nervous system activity. These compounds can also inhibit acetylcholinesterase, which plays a critical role in regulating neurotransmitter levels in the brain. Additionally, specific terpenoids within the plant interact with cyclooxygenase enzymes to reduce the production of pro-inflammatory mediators. Such multifaceted interactions with protein targets allow the plant to function as both a calming agent and a therapeutic tool against oxidative stress.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause skin irritation, nausea, vomiting, dizziness, and headache if a person is exposed to its parts. Contact with the sap or certain components may lead to redness or discomfort on the skin. Ingesting parts of the plant or inhaling high concentrations of its scent can trigger gastrointestinal distress such as nausea and vomiting. Some individuals may also experience neurological symptoms including dizziness and headache. These reactions vary depending on the method of exposure and the individual's sensitivity.
