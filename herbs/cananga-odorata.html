Lantern Tree (Cananga odorata): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

What is Lantern Tree (Cananga Odorata)?

The Lantern Tree, or Cananga odorata, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, known for its fragrant flowers and the essential oil extracted from its blooms, which is widely used in traditional herbal medicine.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Lantern Tree is Cananga odorata. This plant belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Sapindales, family Annonaceae, genus Cananga, and species Cananga odorata. It is commonly known as the Lantern Tree in Southeast Asia, and as the Ylang-Ylang Tree in parts of Africa and the Pacific Islands. The synonyms for this plant include Cananga champaca and Cananga hawaiiensis, with regional names such as "Kandula" in India and "Kandula" in Sri Lanka.

Plant Description and Morphology

The Lantern Tree, or Cananga odorata, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its dense, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in terminal panicles, giving it a lantern-like appearance, and its dark, aromatic wood that is highly valued in traditional medicine and perfumery.

Native Habitat and Distribution

The Lantern Tree (Cananga odorata) is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including countries such as Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines, and is commonly found in humid lowland forests and secondary growth areas.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The flowers, leaves, and seeds of the Lantern Tree (Cananga odorata) are the primary plant parts used medicinally, with the flowers being the most commonly utilized due to their high concentration of essential oils and bioactive compounds.

Phytochemical Composition

The phytochemical composition of the Lantern Tree (Cananga odorata) includes essential oils rich in sesquiterpenes, such as alpha-santalene, beta-santalene, and gamma-santalene, along with flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic compounds that contribute to its medicinal properties.

Pharmacological Properties

The pharmacological properties of the Lantern Tree (Cananga odorata) include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and analgesic effects, primarily due to the presence of compounds such as sesquiterpene lactones and essential oils.

Mechanisms of Action

The mechanisms of action of the Lantern Tree (Cananga odorata) involve its bioactive compounds, such as sesquiterpene lactones and flavonoids, which exhibit anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and immunomodulatory effects by interacting with cellular pathways and enzyme systems.

Therapeutic Uses and Indications

The Lantern Tree (Cananga odorata) is traditionally used in herbal medicine for its antispasmodic, analgesic, and anti-inflammatory properties, particularly in treating conditions such as muscle spasms, pain, and inflammatory disorders.

Preparation Methods and Forms

The preparation methods and forms of the Lantern Tree (Cananga odorata) include the use of its flowers, leaves, and seeds, with common forms such as decoctions, infusions, tinctures, and powders, often tailored to specific therapeutic applications in traditional herbal medicine.

Dosage and Administration

The recommended dosage of Lantern Tree (Cananga odorata) varies depending on the form of the preparation, with typical oral doses ranging from 1 to 3 grams of dried flower per day, though specific formulations may require professional guidance.

Safety, Side Effects, and Contraindications

While Lantern Tree (Cananga odorata) is generally considered safe when used in moderate amounts, it may cause mild gastrointestinal discomfort, skin irritation, or allergic reactions in some individuals, and it should be avoided during pregnancy and by those with known allergies to related plants.

Drug Interactions

Lantern Tree (Cananga odorata) may interact with certain medications, particularly those affecting the central nervous system, due to its potential sedative and antispasmodic properties, which could enhance or counteract the effects of drugs such as antidepressants, antihistamines, and muscle relaxants.

Toxicity and Precautions

While the lantern tree (Cananga odorata) is generally considered safe when used in moderate amounts for its medicinal properties, it may cause mild gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals, and excessive consumption could lead to toxicity, particularly in vulnerable populations such as children, pregnant women, and those with pre-existing health conditions.

Cultivation and Harvesting

Cultivation of the Lantern Tree (Cananga odorata) involves growing it in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil, partial shade, and consistent moisture, while harvesting occurs primarily through the collection of its fragrant flowers, typically during the dry season, using manual methods to ensure minimal damage to the plant.

Processing and Extraction

The processing and extraction of Lantern Tree (Cananga odorata) involve harvesting the flowers, drying them to reduce moisture content, and then using methods such as steam distillation or solvent extraction to obtain the essential oils and active compounds.

Storage and Shelf Life

Proper storage of lantern tree (Cananga odorata) products in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight is essential to maintain their potency and prevent degradation, with a typical shelf life of 1 to 2 years when stored correctly.

Scientific Research and Evidence

Scientific research on the Lantern Tree (Cananga odorata) has shown its potential in traditional medicine, with studies highlighting its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties, particularly due to the presence of compounds like methylheptanediol and sesquiterpene hydrocarbons.

Traditional and Historical Uses

The Lantern Tree, or Cananga odorata, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its aromatic flowers, which are valued for their calming properties and applications in treating respiratory conditions, anxiety, and as a natural remedy for insomnia.
