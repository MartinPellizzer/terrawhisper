Lantern Tree (Cananga odorata): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

The Lantern Tree, or Cananga odorata, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, known for its fragrant flowers and significant use in traditional herbal medicine.

What is Lantern Tree (Cananga Odorata)?

The Lantern Tree, or Cananga odorata, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, known for its fragrant flowers and the essential oil extracted from its blooms, which is widely used in traditional herbal medicine.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Lantern Tree is Cananga odorata. This plant belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Sapindales, family Annonaceae, genus Cananga, and species Cananga odorata. It is commonly known as the Lantern Tree in Southeast Asia, and as the Ylang-Ylang Tree in parts of Africa and the Pacific Islands. The synonyms for this plant include Cananga champaca and Cananga hawaiiensis, with regional names such as "Kandula" in India and "Kandula" in Sri Lanka.

Plant Description and Morphology

Lantern Tree is a tropical evergreen tree that typically grows between 10 to 20 meters tall with a broad, spreading canopy.

Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of 10 to 20 pairs of ovate to lanceolate leaflets arranged alternately along the stem. The tree produces clusters of bright yellow, lantern-shaped flowers that emit a strong, sweet fragrance, especially at night. Its root system is fibrous and shallow, adapting to nutrient-poor soils by forming symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, while the flowers have a waxy texture and a distinctive, spicy aroma.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Lantern Tree is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

It is commonly found in lowland rainforests and secondary growth areas, often growing along riverbanks and in open woodlands. The tree prefers warm, humid conditions with well-drained soils and abundant sunlight. It is well adapted to regions with distinct wet and dry seasons, making it a common species in monsoonal climates.

Its ecological success is due to its ability to tolerate various soil types and its role in supporting local biodiversity.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The medicinal use of the lantern tree primarily involves its flowers, leaves, and bark.

The flowers are the most commonly utilized part, often harvested when fully open and fresh. Leaves are used either fresh or dried, depending on the traditional preparation method. Bark is occasionally employed, particularly in formulations for its astringent properties.

Each part contributes unique active compounds that support various therapeutic applications.

Phytochemical Composition

The phytochemical composition of the lantern tree (Cananga odorata) is characterized by a diverse array of active compounds, including volatile oils, alkaloids, and flavonoids.

These active compounds contribute to the plant's therapeutic properties and are primarily responsible for its medicinal effects. Secondary metabolites such as terpenoids and phenolic compounds play a key role in the plant's defense mechanisms and also contribute to its pharmacological activities. Chemical markers like alpha-santalol and beta-santalol are particularly significant, as they are responsible for the plant's distinctive fragrance and have been extensively studied for their biological properties.

These compounds are often used as indicators for quality control and authenticity in herbal preparations.

Pharmacological Properties

The Lantern Tree (Cananga odorata) exhibits a range of pharmacological properties that make it valuable in herbal medicine.

Its therapeutic actions include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and analgesic effects, which are supported by both traditional use and scientific research. The biological activity of the plant is primarily attributed to its essential oils, which contain compounds such as alpha-selinene and linalool. Pharmacodynamically, these compounds interact with various physiological systems to modulate pain perception and reduce inflammation.

System-level effects include enhanced immune response and potential neuroprotective benefits, contributing to its traditional use in treating a variety of ailments.

Mechanisms of Action

The mechanisms of action of the Lantern Tree (Cananga odorata) involve multiple biological processes that contribute to its therapeutic effects.

At the cellular level, its compounds interact with cell membranes and intracellular receptors, modulating ion channels and inflammatory pathways. Biochemically, the plant's active components trigger signaling cascades that influence enzyme activity and gene expression related to immune response and tissue repair. These effects are mediated through specific target systems, including the nervous, endocrine, and immune systems.

The combined action of these pathways underpins the plant's use in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antimicrobial properties.

Therapeutic Uses

The Lantern Tree, or Cananga odorata, is widely used in herbal medicine for its therapeutic properties that address various health conditions.

It is traditionally employed to treat respiratory issues such as asthma and cough due to its anti-inflammatory and bronchodilatory effects. The plant is also used to alleviate symptoms of digestive disorders like indigestion and gastritis because of its carminative and antispasmodic qualities. Additionally, it has been applied in the management of skin conditions such as eczema and fungal infections due to its antimicrobial and soothing properties.

Its essential oil is commonly used in aromatherapy to relieve stress and promote mental clarity.

Preparation Methods and Forms

The preparation methods of the Lantern Tree (Cananga odorata) for herbal use primarily involve drying and grinding the flower buds into a fine powder.

This powder is commonly used as a base for various formulations. The plant is also utilized in the form of essential oil, which is extracted through steam distillation. Additionally, the dried flower buds can be made into a tincture by soaking them in alcohol.

The plant may also be incorporated into herbal teas by steeping the dried flowers in hot water.

Dosage and Administration

The recommended dosage of Lantern Tree (Cananga odorata) varies depending on the form of the preparation, with typical oral doses ranging from 1 to 3 grams of dried flower per day, though specific formulations may require professional guidance.

Safety, Side Effects, and Contraindications

While Lantern Tree (Cananga odorata) is generally considered safe when used in moderate amounts, it may cause mild gastrointestinal discomfort, skin irritation, or allergic reactions in some individuals, and it should be avoided during pregnancy and by those with known allergies to related plants.

Drug Interactions

Lantern Tree (Cananga odorata) may interact with certain medications, particularly those affecting the central nervous system, due to its potential sedative and antispasmodic properties, which could enhance or counteract the effects of drugs such as antidepressants, antihistamines, and muscle relaxants.

Toxicity and Precautions

While the lantern tree (Cananga odorata) is generally considered safe when used in moderate amounts for its medicinal properties, it may cause mild gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals, and excessive consumption could lead to toxicity, particularly in vulnerable populations such as children, pregnant women, and those with pre-existing health conditions.

Cultivation and Harvesting

Cultivation of the Lantern Tree (Cananga odorata) involves growing it in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil, partial shade, and consistent moisture, while harvesting occurs primarily through the collection of its fragrant flowers, typically during the dry season, using manual methods to ensure minimal damage to the plant.

Processing and Extraction

The processing and extraction of Lantern Tree (Cananga odorata) involve harvesting the flowers, drying them to reduce moisture content, and then using methods such as steam distillation or solvent extraction to obtain the essential oils and active compounds.

Storage and Shelf Life

Proper storage of lantern tree (Cananga odorata) products in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight is essential to maintain their potency and prevent degradation, with a typical shelf life of 1 to 2 years when stored correctly.

Scientific Research and Evidence

Scientific research on the Lantern Tree (Cananga odorata) has shown its potential in traditional medicine, with studies highlighting its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties, particularly due to the presence of compounds like methylheptanediol and sesquiterpene hydrocarbons.

Traditional and Historical Uses

The Lantern Tree, or Cananga odorata, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its aromatic flowers, which are valued for their calming properties and applications in treating respiratory conditions, anxiety, and as a natural remedy for insomnia.
