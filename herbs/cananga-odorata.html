Possible Side Effects of cananga odorata

Cananga odorata can have side effects if used improperly, such as skin irritation, allergic reactions, and respiratory problems, particularly when inhaled in large quantities or used without proper dilution.

Excessive use of cananga oil may also cause nausea, dizziness, and headaches due to the high concentration of compounds like linalool and geraniol.

Some individuals may be sensitive to the herb's compounds, leading to skin rashes, redness, or itching.

Furthermore, cananga oil may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners, and exacerbate underlying health conditions like epilepsy or kidney disease.
