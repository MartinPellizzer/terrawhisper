What to know about Cananga odorata (ylang-ylang) before using it medicinally

Ylang-ylang, scientifically known as Cananga odorata, is a herb that has been touted for its numerous health properties, which have been shown to improve overall well-being by reducing stress and anxiety, while also possessing antibacterial and antifungal qualities.

In the culinary world, ylang-ylang is often used to add a sweet and floral flavor to desserts, teas, and other sweet treats. Horticulturally, ylang-ylang is a tropical tree that thrives in warm and humid climates, with its flowers being highly prized for their essential oil. Botanically speaking, ylang-ylang is a member of the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and strong, distinctive aroma.

Historically, ylang-ylang has been used since ancient times in traditional medicine and as a perfume ingredient, with references dating back to the 16th century.
