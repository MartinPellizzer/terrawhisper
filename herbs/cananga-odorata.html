Cananga odorata

What's the taxonomical classification of Cananga odorata?

Cananga odorata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, following the evolutionary path of the order Magnoliales. The plant is a member of the family Annonaceae, which places it within the genus Cananga, and its specific biological identity is defined by the species odorata.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Cananga odorata contains various bioactive compounds such as linalool, benzyl acetate, geraniol, and methyl benzoate that provide significant therapeutic potential. These essential oils exhibit strong antimicrobial, antifungal, and anti-inflammatory properties when extracted from the flowers. Linalool specifically serves as a key component for its sedative and anxiolytic effects on the central nervous system. The presence of various esters and terpenes also allows the plant to be used in traditional medicine to treat skin conditions and promote relaxation. Research suggests these chemical constituents may also offer antioxidant benefits to protect cells from oxidative stress.

What are the therapeutic actions of Cananga odorata?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin diseases, respiratory ailments, fevers, and various types of inflammation. The flowers and bark possess medicinal properties that are often applied topically to soothe skin irritations or infections. Some traditional practices involve using infusions from the plant to help alleviate symptoms of coughs and congestion. Additionally, it is sometimes employed in folk medicine to help reduce body temperature during febrile episodes. These applications highlight the plant's diverse role in various herbal remedy traditions.
