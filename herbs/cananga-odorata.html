Medicinal Parts

Cananga odorata flower, commonly known as the champa flower, is widely used in traditional medicine for its aromatic and therapeutic properties.

The flower is primarily valued for its essential oils, which contain compounds like linalool and geraniol, known for their calming and antiseptic effects. In Ayurvedic and Chinese medicine, the flower is used to treat respiratory disorders, anxiety, and insomnia due to its sedative and decongestant properties. The dried flowers are often incorporated into herbal remedies, perfumes, and incense, enhancing their medicinal and aromatic applications.

Additionally, the flower's extract is used in skincare products for its anti-inflammatory and skin-soothing benefits, making it a versatile component in both medicinal and cosmetic formulations.

Side Effects of cananga odorata

Cananga odorata induces respiratory issues due to its strong, sweet fragrance, which can irritate the airways and lead to coughing, wheezing, and difficulty breathing, particularly in individuals with asthma or chronic respiratory conditions.

The herb may also lead to allergic reactions, manifesting as sneezing, runny nose, and even anaphylaxis in severe cases, depending on an individual's sensitivity. It can cause skin irritation and rashes when applied topically, resulting in redness, itching, and burning sensations.

Prolonged exposure to the herb's scent may trigger eye irritation, causing redness, watering, and a gritty feeling. Additionally, Cananga odorata may result in headaches, nausea, and throat irritation, with some users experiencing stomach upset or nasal congestion after inhaling its fragrance.

These side effects highlight the importance of using the herb with caution and consulting a healthcare professional, especially for those with pre-existing health conditions or allergies.
