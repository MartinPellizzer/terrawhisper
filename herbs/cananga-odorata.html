Cananga odorata

What's the taxonomical classification of Cananga odorata?

Cananga odorata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, following the evolutionary path of the order Magnoliales. The plant is a member of the family Annonaceae, which places it within the genus Cananga, and its specific biological identity is defined by the species odorata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cananga odorata has pinnate leaves measuring up to 30 centimeters in length, yellowish-green drooping flowers with long narrow petals, and small globose drupes that turn yellow when ripe. The tree typically reaches heights of 12 to 25 meters and features a dense canopy with spreading branches. Its flowers are characterized by six long, slender, drooping petals that give the blossoms a distinctively shaggy appearance. The fruit develops into an ovoid shape and contains a single seed. These morphological features combined make the species easily identifiable within tropical forest environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, extending from the Andaman and Nicobar Islands through Myanmar, Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia to the Philippines. It thrives primarily in moist, lowland rainforest environments where humidity is consistently high. Within these ecosystems, the species is frequently found along riverbanks and in disturbed forest areas. Its range also includes parts of southern China and various Pacific islands where the climate remains warm and subtropical. These specific habitats allow the species to maintain its broad but localized distribution across the Indo-Malayan realm.

What are the pharmacological activities of Cananga odorata?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological activities including analgesic, anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, antioxidant, and antiviral properties. According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", Cananga odorata essential oil has been used in traditional medicine for anxiety and altered neuronal states. Oral administration of 30 mg/kg of this essential oil reduced neuropathic pain and ameliorated anxiety symptoms that appeared 28 days after surgery in the spared nerve injury model. The oil reduced the expression of MAPK, NOS2, and p-p65, which are markers of neuroinflammation. Furthermore, it promoted a normalizing effect on neurotrophin levels such as BDNF. This plant represents an interesting candidate for the management of neuropathic pain conditions and related comorbidities.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds cananodine, benzyl acetate, gamma-eudesmol, germacrene D, and beta-linalool. These specific chemical constituents contribute to the diverse therapeutic properties found within the species. The presence of cananodine and benzyl acetate is particularly significant for its aromatic and biological activities. Additionally, the sesquiterpenes gamma-eudesmol and germacrene D play roles in the plant's complex chemical profile. The inclusion of beta-linalool further enhances the medicinal potential of the essential oils extracted from the flowers.

According to a study published by "Journal of natural products", the fruits of Cananga odorata contain a new guaipyridine sesquiterpene alkaloid called cananodine (1). Two new euudesmane sesquiterpenes, cryptomeridiol 11-alpha-L-rhamnoside (2) and gamma-eudesmol 11-alpha-L-rhamnoside (3), were also isolated from the plant along with gamma-eudesmol (4). The structures for compounds 1-3 were established using NMR and MS methods. These four compounds, 1-4, were evaluated for cytotoxicity against 2 human hepatocarcinoma cell lines. Evaluation also included 4 previously reported alkaloids, which are cleistopholine (5), N-trans-feruloyltyramine (6), (+)-ushinsunine-beta-N-oxide (7), and lyscamine (8).
