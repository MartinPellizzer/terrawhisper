Cananga odorata

What's the taxonomical classification of Cananga odorata?

Cananga odorata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, following the evolutionary path of the order Magnoliales. The plant is a member of the family Annonaceae, which places it within the genus Cananga, and its specific biological identity is defined by the species odorata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cananga odorata has pinnate leaves measuring up to 30 centimeters in length, yellowish-green drooping flowers with long narrow petals, and small globose drupes that turn yellow when ripe. The tree typically reaches heights of 12 to 25 meters and features a dense canopy with spreading branches. Its flowers are characterized by six long, slender, drooping petals that give the blossoms a distinctively shaggy appearance. The fruit develops into an ovoid shape and contains a single seed. These morphological features combined make the species easily identifiable within tropical forest environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, extending from the Andaman and Nicobar Islands through Myanmar, Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia to the Philippines. It thrives primarily in moist, lowland rainforest environments where humidity is consistently high. Within these ecosystems, the species is frequently found along riverbanks and in disturbed forest areas. Its range also includes parts of southern China and various Pacific islands where the climate remains warm and subtropical. These specific habitats allow the species to maintain its broad but localized distribution across the Indo-Malayan realm.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse array of bioactive chemical constituents including benzyl acetate, cananodine, germacrene D, β-linalool, and myristic acid. The presence of benzyl acetate and β-linalool contributes significantly to its characteristic aromatic profile and potential sedative properties. Germacrene D serves as a notable sesquiterpene within the floral essence that may offer various biological activities. Additionally, the alkaloid cananodine and the fatty acid myristic acid provide the plant with a complex chemical foundation for medicinal research. What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Cananga odorata targets various neurotransmitter receptors, enzyme systems, and inflammatory pathways to exert its sedative, antimicrobial, and anti-inflammatory effects. Such multifaceted interactions with protein targets allow the plant to function as both a calming agent and a therapeutic tool against oxidative stress.
