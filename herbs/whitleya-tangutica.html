What is Whitleya tangutica?

Whitleya tangutica is a flowering plant species within the family Solanaceae.

It is characterized by its white or pale yellow, fragrant, and funnel-shaped flowers, which are 2-3 cm long and 1.5-2.5 cm wide, with five lobes and a tube 1-2 mm long. The leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptical, 5-12 cm long, and 2-5 cm wide, with a pointed tip and a crenate margin. The plant's growth habit is shrubby, with a height range of 50-100 cm.

Whitleya tangutica is native to the mountainous regions of the Qinghai-Gansu Plateau in China.
