Swertia kouitchensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Gentianaales and is a member of the family Gentianaceae. Finally, the plant is situated within the genus Swertia, with its specific designation being the species kouitchensis.

As discussed in a study, Swertia kouitchensis has long been used in folk medicine to treat hepatitis and diabetes. The ethanol extract of the plant was tested for its inhibitory activity on α-amylase and α-glucosidase in vitro. It was also shown to stimulate insulin secretion in NIT-1 cells. In streptozotocin-induced diabetic mice treated with 60 mg/kg, the extract displayed anti-hyperglycemic activity and improved antioxidant capacity. After a long term study of 4 weeks, the extract ameliorated hyperlipidemia and carbohydrate metabolism in diabetic mice. These results demonstrate that Swertia kouitchensis exhibits considerable anti-diabetic activity and metabolic alterations.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Research suggests that oleanolic acid may assist in protecting liver function and supporting metabolic health. Similarly, ursolic acid is frequently studied for its potential to increase muscle mass and reduce body fat. Together, these compounds contribute to the diverse pharmacological profile of the plant.

As discussed in a study, two rare new chiratane-type triterpenoids, kouitchenoids A and B (1, 2), were isolated from the ethanol extract of Swertia kouitchensis. This plant also contains oleanolic acid (3) and ursolic acid (4). These four compounds, 1-4, demonstrated moderate inhibitory activity against the α-glucosidase. The inhibitory activity was measured with IC50 values ranging from 1,812 to 2,027 μM.
