Cayratia japonica

What's the taxonomical classification of Cayratia japonica?

Cayratiat japonica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Vitales and the family Vitaceae, eventually placing it within the genus Cayratia. Finally, the specific identification of this plant is completed by its species name, japonica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cayratiia japonica has climbing stems that are herbaceous and twining, with leaves that are cordate or heart-shaped and possess long petioles.

The leaf margins are typically entire or slightly wavy, while the surfaces can range from smooth to slightly pubescent. Small, greenish-white flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves in a somewhat inconspicuous manner. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that transition from green to a darker color as they mature.

Its overall growth habit allows it to scramble over other vegetation using its slender stems for support.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Cayratiat japonica contains medicinal compounds primarily found in its whole plant, specifically within the roots, leaves, and stems. What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Cayratia japonica targets various inflammatory mediators and enzymatic pathways, specifically inhibiting cyclooxygenase-2, inducible nitric oxide synthase, and various pro-inflammatory cytokines. Its bioactive constituents, such as alkaloids and flavonoids, work by modulating the expression of tumor necrosis factor-alpha and interleukin-6 to reduce systemic inflammation. These compounds also demonstrate an ability to interact with the arachidonic acid metabolism pathway, effectively suppressing the production of prostaglandins. Furthermore, certain extracts have shown potential in influencing signaling proteins related to oxidative stress and cellular apoptosis. Through these diverse molecular interactions, the plant exerts its significant antipyretic, analgesic, and anti-inflammatory medicinal effects.

What are the therapeutic actions of Cayratia japonica?

Cayratia japonica has therapeutic properties that include diuretic, bitter, anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and emmenagogue actions.

Furthermore, its emmenagogue effects can be used to stimulate menstrual flow and regulate reproductive health.

What are the herbal preparations of Cayratia japonica?

Cayratia japonica is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice using the plant's various parts. Practitioners often boil the roots or leaves in water to create a concentrated liquid for oral consumption. In some traditional practices, the crushed fresh leaves are applied directly to the skin as a paste to treat localized ailments. Some preparations also involve macerating the plant material in alcohol to produce medicinal tinctures. These diverse methods allow the active phytochemicals to be extracted for both internal and external therapeutic use.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Cayratia japonica can cause adverse reactions such as gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, or allergic responses due to its bioactive compounds. While often used in traditional medicine, the lack of extensive clinical trials means the precise toxicity levels in humans remain largely unknown. Some studies suggest that excessive consumption might lead to stomach discomfort or nausea. Topical application of the plant extracts can also trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Therefore, caution is necessary when using this species for any medicinal purpose.
