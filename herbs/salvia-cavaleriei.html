What's the taxonomical classification of Salvia cavaleriei?

Salvia cavaleriei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae, eventually reaching the genus Salvia. Ultimately, the plant is identified at the specific level as the species cavaleriei.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat gastrointestinal disorders, respiratory inflammation, and skin infections. It contains bioactive compounds that help soothe digestive tracts and reduce abdominal discomfort. The medicinal properties are also applied to calm coughing and alleviate symptoms of bronchial irritation. When used topically, it acts as an antimicrobial agent to assist in the healing of minor cuts and skin irritations. Local traditional practices often rely on its extracts to manage various inflammatory conditions.

What are the herbal preparations of Salvia cavaleriei?

Salvias cavaleriei is prepared primarily as a decoction or an infusion by boiling or steeping the dried aerial parts of the plant in water. These liquid extracts are the most common methods used to access the soluble phytochemicals within the leaves and stems. Some practitioners may also grind the dried foliage into a fine powder to create topical poultices for skin applications. Additionally, the plant can be incorporated into infused oils to facilitate transdermal absorption of its active compounds. These various forms allow for diverse administration depending on the specific traditional use intended.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Salvia cavaleriei can cause adverse physiological reactions such as nausea, dizziness, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested in significant amounts. Because the specific chemical profile of this species is not extensively documented in clinical literature, the exact toxicity levels remain uncertain. Potential neurological effects may include altered sensory perception or mild sedation depending on the individual's sensitivity. It is critical to avoid self-medicating with this plant due to the risk of unpredictable interactions with other substances. Consult a medical professional before any exposure to prevent serious health complications.
