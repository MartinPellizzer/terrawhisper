What's the taxonomical classification of Salvia cavaleriei?

Salvia cavaleriei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae, eventually reaching the genus Salvia. Ultimately, the plant is identified at the specific level as the species cavaleriei.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

What are the pharmacological activities of Salvia cavaleriei?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological activities including potent cytotoxicity against various human cancer cell lines. Researchers have identified specific bioactive compounds within the species that induce programmed cell death in malignant cells. These phytochemicals interfere with cellular metabolic processes to inhibit tumor growth effectively. Studies further suggest that the plant's chemical profile may offer potential for developing new chemotherapeutic agents. Ongoing investigation continues to clarify the precise molecular mechanisms behind these inhibitory effects.

As discussed in a study, fifteen new ent-kaurane diterpenoids, identified as compounds 1-15, were isolated from the whole plants of Salvia cavaleriei. These compounds 1-15 were evaluated for cytotoxicity against five human cancer cell lines, including HL-60, SMMC-7721, A-549, MCF-7, and SW480, along with the noncancerous Beas-2B cell line. Compounds 1-10, 12, 14, and 15 demonstrated broad-spectrum cytotoxicity. Specifically, compounds 1, 3, 6-10, 12, and 15 showed more potent cytotoxicity than the positive control, cis-platin. These specific compounds exhibited IC50 values ranging from 0.65 to 6.4 μM.
