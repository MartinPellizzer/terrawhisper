Elaeocarpus chinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Elaeocarpus chinensis?

Elaeocarpus chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Oxalidales. This plant is a member of the family Elaeocarpaceae and is situated within the genus Elaeocarpus, ultimately being identified by its specific species name, chinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Elaeocarpus chinensis has an evergreen habit characterized by spreading, elliptical leaves that measure up to 15 centimeters in length and possess a glossy, dark green upper surface.

The branchlets are typically slender and can exhibit a slightly quadrangular shape in younger growth. Small, creamy white flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often appearing in small clusters. These flowers produce fleshy, globose drupes that transition from green to a deep reddish-brown color upon maturity.

The woody stem structure supports a dense canopy, making the plant a prominent feature in subtropical landscapes.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Elaeocarpus chinensis contains bioactive compounds in its seeds, bark, and leaves that are utilized for various medicinal purposes. The seeds are frequently used in traditional practices to address digestive issues and inflammatory conditions. Extracts from the bark are often applied topically or ingested to treat skin ailments and minor wounds. Additionally, the leaves are sometimes decocted into teas to help manage fever or respiratory discomfort. These different parts of the plant provide a diverse range of phytochemicals that contribute to its therapeutic potential.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Elaeocarpus chinensis targets proteins involved in inflammatory signaling pathways, such as cyclooxygenase-2, tumor necrosis factor-alpha, and interleukin-6. These interactions occur primarily through bioactive secondary metabolites like triterpenoids and flavonoids present in the plant's extracts. By inhibiting these specific enzymes and cytokines, the plant exerts potent anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects in biological systems. Additionally, some compounds from this species may interact with various apoptotic proteins to influence cell survival and tumor suppression. The modulation of these molecular targets provides the biochemical basis for its traditional medicinal applications in treating swelling and pain.

What are the therapeutic actions of Elaeocarpus chinensis?

Elaeocarpus chinensis has medicinal properties derived from its bark and leaves that function as an astringent, bitter, and anti-inflammatory agent to treat various ailments.

    What are the herbal preparations of Elaeocarpus chinensis?

    Elaeocarpus chinensis is prepared as a decoction of the bark, a powder from the dried fruit, or a topical paste made from crushed leaves. The bark is most commonly boiled in water to create a medicinal tea used for treating internal ailments. Practitioners often grind the dried seeds or fruits into a fine powder to be encapsulated or mixed with honey. For external applications, the fresh leaves are macerated to form a poultice for skin irritations. These various methods allow the plant's active compounds to be administered according to the specific therapeutic need.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Elaeocarpus chinensis can cause skin irritation, contact dermatitis, and potential allergic reactions upon direct contact with its sap or foliage. The milky latex found within the plant is known to be caustic to sensitive tissues. Individuals handling the plant without protection may experience redness, itching, or swelling at the site of contact. If the sap accidentally enters the eyes, it can lead to severe stinging and inflammation. Precautions should always be taken to avoid skin contact to prevent these adverse dermatological responses.
