Common Chickweed (Stellaria media) Compounds

Common Chickweed contains several primary active compounds, including flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and phenolic acids, known for their anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins contribute to its astringent qualities, while coumarin may support digestive health. Other compounds like glycosides, saponins, and terpenoids also play roles in its medicinal effects.

Common Chickweed (Stellaria media) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Common Chickweed (Stellaria media). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting immune function and reducing inflammation. These compounds are often studied for their potential therapeutic effects in herbal medicine.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Common Chickweed (Stellaria media). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Found in various parts of the plant, it supports its use in traditional herbal medicine for digestive and skin health.

3. Tannin

Tannin in Common Chickweed (Stellaria media) contributes to its astringent properties, helping to reduce inflammation and soothe mucous membranes. It is found in the leaves and stems, and is often used in traditional herbal medicine for digestive and skin-related conditions.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Common Chickweed (Stellaria media). It contributes to the plant's mild sedative and antispasmodic effects. Coumarin also has anti-inflammatory properties and may support digestive health. Its presence enhances the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Common Chickweed (Stellaria media). It contributes to the plant's mild diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties. Glycosides in Common Chickweed may support respiratory and digestive health when used as part of herbal preparations.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Common Chickweed (Stellaria media). It contributes to the plant's mild detoxifying and anti-inflammatory properties. Saponins may support respiratory and digestive health when used as part of traditional herbal preparations. They are often harvested and processed to enhance their therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Common Chickweed (Stellaria media) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds may have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. They are found in various parts of the plant, including leaves and stems. Terpenoids support the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of Common Chickweed (Stellaria media) contains volatile compounds like cineole and camphor. These oils are used topically for their anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. They support skin health and may aid in respiratory conditions when properly diluted.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Common Chickweed (Stellaria media). It acts as an antioxidant, helping to neutralize free radicals in the body. This compound contributes to the plant's potential anti-inflammatory and protective properties. Anthocyanin may support overall health by reducing oxidative stress.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Common Chickweed (Stellaria media). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, though research on its specific effects is limited. Alkaloids in this plant may support traditional uses in herbal medicine, though their role remains under investigation.
