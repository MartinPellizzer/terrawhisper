Dalbergia rimosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dalbergia rimosa has a woody, deciduous tree habit characterized by fissured bark, pinnately compound leaves, and small, fragrant, pale yellow flowers. The leaflets are typically elliptic to oblong in shape with entire margins and acute tips. Its canopy is often spreading, providing dense shade through its lush foliage. The small flowers are arranged in axillary or terminal racemes that emerge during the flowering season. The fruit consists of small, indehiscent pods that facilitate seed dispersal.

This plant is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, specifically encompassing parts of Thailand, Laos, Cambodia, Vietnam, Myanmar, and Peninsular Malaysia. The species thrives in seasonally wet habitats where it can access consistent moisture during its growth cycles. Its distribution is closely linked to the humid climate and specific soil compositions found throughout these subtropical and tropical belts.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Dalbergia rimosa can cause severe allergic contact dermatitis, skin irritation, and respiratory hypersensitivity in sensitive individuals. The plant contains various bioactive compounds and resins that act as potent allergens when they come into contact with human tissue. People handling the wood or bark may experience redness, swelling, or itching on the skin. Inhalation of fine dust from the wood during processing can lead to asthma-like symptoms or inflammation of the airways. These adverse reactions are most common among workers in woodworking industries who are frequently exposed to the material.
