Dodder (Cuscuta europaea)

Dodder (Cuscuta europaea) is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, native to Europe, Western Asia, and North Africa. Traditionally, its seeds, stems, and leaves have been used for decoctions, infusions, and tinctures.

This herb is particularly valued for its tonic, astringent, and diuretic actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and african traditional medicine.
