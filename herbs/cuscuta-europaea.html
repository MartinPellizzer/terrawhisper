Dodder

Cuscuta europaea

Dodder (cuscuta europaea) is a parasitic plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family. Traditionally, it has been used for treating parasitic infections, as a diuretic, and as a remedy for skin diseases. The plant's seeds and stems are typically harvested for medicinal purposes.

Botanical Identification

Dodder, scientifically known as Cuscuta europaea, is an annual parasitic plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family. Commonly called yellow dodder in Europe, it lacks roots, leaves, and true stems, instead forming a yellow, thread-like vine that attaches to host plants. Its flowers are small, white or yellow, and it has been referenced under various names in historical herbal texts.

Active Compounds

Dodder contains key active compounds such as tannins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Their combined action supports the herb's traditional use in herbal medicine.

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory conditions and as a general tonic.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and as an antiseptic.

Extract: Used for skin conditions and to support the immune system.

Safety Profile

Dodder is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to potential toxicity. It may cause gastrointestinal upset and allergic reactions in some individuals. Always consult a qualified practitioner before use.
