In the culinary world, dodder is often used in traditional Chinese medicine to flavor soups and stews, while its sweet aroma is also utilized in perfumes and potpourri. From a botanical perspective, dodder belongs to the morning glory family and features tiny yellow flowers and leaves that are often reduced to mere scales.

With ancient references in Chinese medicine dating back over 2,000 years, dodder has been a staple of traditional remedies for centuries, making it an herb worthy of further exploration.
