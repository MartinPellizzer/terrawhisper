Populus koreana

What's the taxonomical classification of Populus koreana?

Populus koreana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Malpighiales and is a member of the family Salicaceae. Ultimately, its taxonomic hierarchy concludes with its placement in the genus Populus, specifically as the species koreana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Populus koreana has a woody, deciduous growth habit characterized by slender, upright branches and ovate to lanceolate leaves with finely serrated margins. The bark on mature specimens develops into shallowly fissured, grayish-brown ridges. Its foliage typically exhibits a bright green upper surface and a paler underside, often transitioning to yellow in the autumn. Small, inconspicuous catkins emerge in early spring to facilitate wind pollination. The plant often develops a spreading crown as it reaches maturity in its natural riparian habitats.
