What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Millettia tsui has woody stems with finely pubescent bark and leaves that are alternate, pinnately compound, and feature several pairs of elliptical to oblong leaflets. Its floral structure consists of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the axils of the leaves. The fruit is a woody capsule that contains multiple seeds, which are essential for its reproductive cycle.
