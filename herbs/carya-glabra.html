Carya Glabra (Carya Glabra)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Carya glabra.

Carya glabra, scientifically known as Carya glabra, is a deciduous tree native to North America, commonly referred to as the hazelnut or beaked hazel. It is primarily recognized for its edible nuts, which have been utilized for centuries in traditional medicine and culinary practices. The nuts are rich in healthy fats, protein, and essential nutrients, offering benefits such as improved heart health, enhanced cognitive function, and anti-inflammatory properties. Historically, Indigenous peoples used the nuts for both sustenance and medicinal purposes, often incorporating them into remedies for digestive ailments and respiratory conditions. In modern wellness practices, Carya glabra is valued as a nutrient-dense food supplement and is sometimes used in natural skincare products due to its high antioxidant content, making it a versatile and culturally significant plant.
