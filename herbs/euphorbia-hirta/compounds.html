Common Milkweed (Euphorbia hirta) Compounds

Common Milkweed contains several key compounds such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and tannins, which contribute to its medicinal properties. Terpenoids and glycosides support anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, while saponins and coumarin may aid in digestive and circulatory health. Essential oils, alkaloids, and anthocyanins further enhance its therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

Common Milkweed (Euphorbia hirta) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Common Milkweed (Euphorbia hirta) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help reduce oxidative stress and support immune function. They are naturally present in the plant and are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their therapeutic benefits.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid in Common Milkweed (Euphorbia hirta) contributes to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound helps modulate immune responses and may support wound healing. It is one of several bioactive components in the plant used traditionally in herbal medicine.

3. Tannin

Tannin in Common Milkweed (Euphorbia hirta) contributes to its astringent properties, helping to reduce inflammation and promote wound healing. It also aids in detoxification by binding to toxins and supporting digestive health. This compound is a key component in the plant's traditional use for treating skin conditions and gastrointestinal issues.

4. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Common Milkweed (Euphorbia hirta) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. They are commonly used in traditional herbal remedies for treating skin infections and respiratory conditions. Terpenoids play a key role in the plant's therapeutic applications.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Common Milkweed (Euphorbia hirta). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly in treating skin conditions and inflammation. Glycosides in Euphorbia hirta are known for their anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. They are often extracted and used in traditional herbal formulations.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Common Milkweed (Euphorbia hirta). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, making it valuable in herbal medicine for treating skin conditions and infections. Saponin also aids in detoxification and supports the body's natural healing processes.

7. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Common Milkweed (Euphorbia hirta). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly its antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory effects. Coumarin is often used in traditional herbal medicine to support digestive health and alleviate muscle spasms.

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of Common Milkweed (Euphorbia hirta) contains bioactive compounds like terpenoids and flavonoids. It is used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The oil is typically extracted through steam distillation and applied topically for skin conditions.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a key compound in Common Milkweed (Euphorbia hirta), contributing to its medicinal properties. It exhibits anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects, commonly used in traditional herbal medicine to treat pain and inflammatory conditions. The alkaloid content varies depending on the plant part and harvesting time.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin, found in Common Milkweed (Euphorbia hirta), is a bioactive pigment with antioxidant properties. It contributes to the plant's medicinal value by supporting immune function and reducing oxidative stress. Its presence highlights the plant's potential in traditional herbal remedies.
