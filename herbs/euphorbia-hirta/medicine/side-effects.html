10 Possible Side Effects Of Euphorbia Hirta (Tickseed)

Euphorbia hirta has some side effects when used improperly, such as digestive issues, allergic reactions, and skin irritation.

These side effects can occur when the herb is consumed in large quantities or when individuals have sensitivities to certain compounds.

For example, consuming excessive amounts can lead to stomach upset, nausea, and vomiting, hindering daily activities and overall quality of life.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Euphorbia hirta if used imporperly.

1. Increases risk of overdose

Euphorbia hirta causes allergic reactions.

This is due to the presence of sapindin and euphorbol esters in its leaves and stems, which can trigger an immune response in some individuals. When these compounds come into contact with human skin or mucous membranes, they can cause inflammation, redness, itching, and other symptoms associated with allergies. Additionally, Euphorbia hirta contains latex, a known allergen that can exacerbate allergic reactions.

As a result, people who are sensitive to these compounds may experience adverse effects when using the herb.

2. Increases risk of overdose

Euphorbia hirta triggers skin irritation due to its milky sap and latex-containing stems.

When the plant's sap or latex comes into contact with human skin, it can cause an allergic reaction, resulting in redness, itching, and rashes. This is because the latex contains various alkaloids and saponins that can penetrate the skin and stimulate an immune response.

As a result, individuals may experience uncomfortable symptoms, ranging from mild irritation to more severe reactions.

3. Increases risk of overdose

Euphorbia hirta induces vomiting.

This is due to its rich content of euphors, compounds that stimulate the vomit reflex and increase gastric secretion. As a result, users may experience repeated episodes of nausea, accompanied by abdominal cramps and discomfort.

The intense stimulation of the gastrointestinal tract can lead to projectile vomiting, which may be triggered even after taking small doses or prolonged use.

Euphorbia hirta produces diarrhea due to its high content of saponins and alkaloids.

These bioactive compounds can irritate the digestive system, leading to increased bowel movement frequency and loose stools.

Additionally, Euphorbia hirta's anti-inflammatory properties may cause stomach upset in some individuals, further contributing to diarrhea as a side effect.

Euphorbia hirta increases blood pressure due to its potential to stimulate the cardiovascular system.

This is attributed to the presence of alkaloids and steroids in the plant, which can cause vasoconstriction, thereby leading to an elevation in blood pressure.

Additionally, the plant's diuretic properties may also contribute to this effect by increasing the amount of fluids lost through urination, further compounding the issue.

Euphorbia hirta lowers blood sugar levels due to its potential to stimulate insulin production and enhance glucose uptake in muscle cells.

The plant's active compounds, such as euphoin and tiglic acid, may bind to insulin receptors, increasing insulin sensitivity and reducing blood glucose concentrations.

Additionally, the plant's ability to reduce inflammation and oxidative stress may contribute to its hypoglycemic effects, making it a potential agent for managing hyperglycemia in individuals with type 2 diabetes.

Euphorbia hirta interacts with medications that are metabolized by the liver enzymes CYP3A4 and CYP2D6.

Its phytochemicals, particularly euphorbolide and quillaic acid, may inhibit these enzymes, potentially reducing the efficacy of certain medications or increasing their toxicity.

This interaction can lead to adverse effects, such as altered blood levels or unintended consequences, making it essential to consider Euphorbia hirta's potential impact on medication interactions when using it therapeutically.

Euphorbia hirta stimulates seizures in some individuals due to its ability to affect the central nervous system.

The plant contains a toxic compound called euphorbol, which can alter neurotransmitter activity and lead to excitatory responses, causing seizures.

Additionally, the plant's latex has been found to have anxiogenic effects, further exacerbating seizure susceptibility.

Euphorbia hirta causes respiratory distress due to its sap and latex content.

These compounds can induce inflammation in the lungs and airways, leading to symptoms such as coughing, wheezing, and shortness of breath.

Additionally, the plant's irritant properties can cause the release of histamine, a chemical that further exacerbates respiratory issues by increasing mucus production and airway constriction.

The plant contains toxic compounds like euphorbol and meso-dihydroguaiaretic acid, which can accumulate in the kidneys and lead to inflammation, scarring, and eventual damage.

Additionally, Euphorbia hirta may also trigger an immune response, causing the body to react against its own kidney tissues, further exacerbating the damage.
