Brainea insignis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Brainea insignis has a woody stem characterized by a single, unbranched trunk that supports a crown of large, elliptical leaves. The leaves are arranged in a spiral pattern and feature prominent parallel venation across their surface. Each leaf blade is typically several decimeters long with a smooth, entire margin and a distinct, pointed apex. The plant also produces small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the axils of the leaf bases. These botanical structures are typical of its classification within the family Marantaceae.
