What's the taxonomical classification of Comastoma falcatum?

Comastoma falcatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Gentianales and the family Gentianaceae. Ultimately, this plant is defined by its placement within the genus Comastoma, specifically as the species falcatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Comastoma falcatum has decumbent or climbing stems that produce opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with entire margins and acute to acuminate apices. The foliage typically features prominent venation and a smooth surface texture. Its inflorescence consists of small, actinomorphic flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. The corolla is characterized by a distinct shape and serves as a primary identification feature for the species. These morphological traits are essential for distinguishing it from closely related members of the Rubiaceae family.
