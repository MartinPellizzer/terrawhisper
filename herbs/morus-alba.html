Morus Alba

Morus alba, commonly known as white mulberry, is a traditional herbal remedy that belongs to the Moraceae family.

This deciduous tree is native to China and is widely cultivated for its medicinal properties.

Morus alba has been used to treat various health conditions, including hypertension, diabetes, and inflammation, due to its health benefits of improving circulation, regulating blood sugar levels, and exhibiting antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

The therapeutic actions of Morus alba are attributed to its bioactive constituents, which include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, such as rutin, quercetin, and morusin, that have been shown to exhibit antiviral, antibacterial, and anti-platelet aggregation activities.

Possible Side Effects of morus alba

Some people may experience allergic reactions, such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing, after using Morus alba.

Furthermore, high doses of Morus alba may lower blood pressure and cause dizziness, headaches, and fainting in susceptible individuals.
