Thymus proximus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae, which characterizes its botanical relationships. Finally, it is placed within the genus Thymus, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species proximus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Thymus proximus has a woody, perennial subshrub habit characterized by slender, ascending to erect stems and small, opposite, elliptical leaves that measure approximately 3 to 6 millimeters in length. The foliage is typically bright green and possesses a slightly revolute margin, often appearing somewhat thickened or coriaceous. Its inflorescence consists of small, dense terminal clusters of bilabiate flowers that range in color from pale pink to lavender. The calyx is tubular and hairy, providing a protective structure for the developing seeds. These morphological traits distinguish the species from other closely related members of the Lamiaceae family.
