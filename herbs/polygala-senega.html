What are the side effects of Polygala senega when used improperly?

When used improperly, Polygala senega increases the chances of experiencing side effects, such as allergic reactions, gastrointestinal disturbances, and respiratory issues.

If not prepared or taken in correct dosages, Polygala senega can cause skin rashes, itching, and hives due to its saponin content. Additionally, improper use may lead to stomach upset, nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea as the herb's bitter compounds irritate the digestive system.

In rare cases, high doses or prolonged usage can also cause bronchospasms, wheezing, and shortness of breath in individuals with pre-existing respiratory conditions, such as asthma. Furthermore, Polygala senega may interact with certain medications, including antacids and blood thinners, which could lead to adverse reactions or reduce their efficacy.

Therefore, it is crucial to consult with a healthcare professional before using Polygala senega for medicinal purposes to ensure proper preparation, dosage, and monitoring of potential side effects.
