Asplenium exiguum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Asplenium exiguum has small, narrowly elliptical to lanceolate fronds that typically measure between 2 and 10 centimeters in length. The leaf margins are characteristically entire or slightly wavy, lacking the deep serrations found in many other fern species. These blades are arranged in a compact rosette emerging from a short, dark rhizome. The underside of the foliage features small, linear sori positioned near the margins, often protected by a thin reflexed border. This diminutive stature and delicate structure distinguish it from larger members of the Asplenium genus.
