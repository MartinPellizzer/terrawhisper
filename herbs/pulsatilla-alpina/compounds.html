Alpine Pasqueflower (Pulsatilla alpina) Compounds

The Alpine Pasqueflower contains several primary compounds, including flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties. Tannins and phenolic acids contribute to its astringent effects. Glycosides and terpenoids may support anti-inflammatory actions. Coumarin, saponins, and alkaloids also play roles in its medicinal profile, while essential oils and anthocyanins add to its complex biochemical composition.

Alpine Pasqueflower (Pulsatilla alpina) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Alpine Pasqueflower (Pulsatilla alpina) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help reduce oxidative stress and support immune function. They are found in the plant's flowers and roots, making them valuable in traditional herbal medicine.

2. Tannin

Tannin in Alpine Pasqueflower (Pulsatilla alpina) contributes to its astringent properties and potential anti-inflammatory effects. It helps in reducing excess bodily fluids and may support wound healing. Tannins are also believed to aid in digestive health by strengthening mucous membranes.

Phenolic acid in Alpine Pasqueflower (Pulsatilla alpina) contributes to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
