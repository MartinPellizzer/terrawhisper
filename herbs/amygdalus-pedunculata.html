Amygdalus pedunculata

What's the taxonomical classification of Amygdalus pedunculata?

Amygdalus pedunculata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Rosales. The plant is further categorized into the family Rosaceae, which places it within the genus Amygdalus, ultimately identifying it as the specific species Amygdalus pedunculata.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause severe cyanide poisoning if ingested, leading to a rapid onset of life-threatening symptoms. Initial signs of toxicity often include nausea, vomiting, dizziness, and a persistent headache. As the poison affects the central nervous and cardiovascular systems, individuals may experience confusion, rapid heartbeat, and acute respiratory distress. In extreme cases, the toxicity can progress to violent seizures and eventually result in death.
