Eurya alata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eurya alata has small, evergreen leaves that are typically elliptic to lanceolate in shape, measuring between 2 and 5 centimeters in length, and feature distinctively wavy or undulate margins. The leaf surfaces are often lustrous and dark green on the upper side, while the undersides may appear slightly paler. Its stems are woody and can develop a somewhat rough texture as the plant matures into a shrub or small tree. The flowers are minute and produced in small clusters, followed by small, fleshy berries that change color as they ripen. These morphological traits collectively help distinguish the species within its natural habitat.
