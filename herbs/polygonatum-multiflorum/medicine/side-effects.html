10 Possible Side Effects Of Polygonatum Multiflorum

Polygonatum multiflorum, also known as Chinese Angelica, has some side effects when used improperly, such as allergic reactions, gastrointestinal upset, and interaction with medications.

These side effects can occur when the herb is taken in excessive amounts or combined with other substances.

In severe cases, these reactions can lead to anaphylaxis, digestive issues, and impaired medication efficacy, which can worsen quality of life by causing discomfort, anxiety, and decreased productivity.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Polygonatum multiflorum, also known as Chinese Angelica, causes allergic reactions due to its high content of polyacetylenes and lignans.

These compounds can trigger an immune response in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling.

Additionally, the plant's saponins may also contribute to the allergenic potential, causing an inflammatory reaction that can manifest as redness, rash, or even difficulty breathing.

2. Triggers respiratory failure

Polygonatum multiflorum, also known as Chinese Angelica, triggers respiratory failure due to its ability to cause allergic reactions, anaphylaxis, and bronchospasm.

The herb's allergenic properties can stimulate the release of histamine, leading to constricted airways and decreased oxygen intake.

Additionally, Polygonatum multiflorum's expectorant properties may exacerbate pre-existing respiratory conditions such as asthma, further compromising lung function and potentially triggering respiratory failure.

3. Increases blood pressure

Polygonatum multiflorum, also known as Chinese Angelica, increases blood pressure due to its stimulatory effect on the cardiovascular system.

The herb contains saponins and polyacetylenes, which can cause an increase in heart rate and blood pressure by stimulating the sympathetic nervous system.

Additionally, Polygonatum multiflorum may also enhance the release of epinephrine, a hormone that plays a crucial role in regulating blood pressure, leading to a further elevation in blood pressure levels.

4. Stimulates heart rate

Polygonatum multiflorum, also known as Chinese Angelica, stimulates heart rate due to its ability to increase cardiac output and blood pressure.

This is attributed to the plant's bioactive compounds, particularly cardiotonic glycosides, which have been shown to enhance the contractility of heart muscle cells and dilate blood vessels.

As a result, Polygonatum multiflorum may cause an increase in heart rate as the body responds to the increased cardiac output and blood pressure.

5. Induces seizures

Polygonatum multiflorum, also known as Chinese Angelica, induces seizures due to its high content of bioactive compounds, including saponins and alkaloids.

These chemicals can interact with the nervous system, altering the balance of neurotransmitters and potentially leading to seizures.

The exact mechanism is not fully understood, but it's believed that the accumulation of these compounds in the body may contribute to the development of seizure activity.

6. Causes liver damage

Polygonatum multiflorum, also known as Chinese Angelica, causes liver damage due to its high concentration of anthraquinone glycosides and saponins.

These bioactive compounds can interfere with liver enzymes, leading to inflammation and cell death. Additionally, the herb's ability to stimulate the excretion of bile can cause bilirubin levels to rise, further straining the liver's filtering capacity.

As a result, prolonged use of Polygonatum multiflorum may lead to chronic liver damage or even liver failure.

7. Reduces kidney function

Polygonatum multiflorum, also known as Chinese Angelica, reduces kidney function due to its ability to inhibit the activity of enzymes responsible for filtering waste and excess fluids from the blood.

This may be attributed to the plant's high concentration of flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which can bind to kidney tissues and interfere with normal filtration processes.

8. Produces gastrointestinal upset

Polygonatum multiflorum, also known as Chinese Angelica, produces gastrointestinal upset.

The herb's active compounds, such as polysaccharides and alkaloids, can stimulate the digestive system, leading to nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea in some individuals. Additionally, Polygonatum multiflorum may cause stomach cramps, bloating, and abdominal pain due to its ability to alter the gut microbiome and disrupt normal digestion processes.

These adverse effects are usually temporary and may occur as a result of an individual's sensitivity to the herb's constituents.

9. Increases bleeding risk

Polygonatum multiflorum, also known as Chinese Angelica, increases bleeding risk due to its ability to thin the blood and slow down clotting.

This is attributed to the presence of coumarins and other bioactive compounds that inhibit platelet aggregation and prolong bleeding time.

As a result, individuals taking Polygonatum multiflorum may experience excessive bleeding during surgery, tooth extraction, or traumatic injuries.

10. Suppresses immune system

Polygonatum multiflorum, also known as Chinese Angelica, suppresses immune system due to its bioactive compounds, including polyacetylenes and triterpenoids.

These compounds have been shown to modulate the activity of immune cells, such as macrophages and T-cells, which can lead to a decrease in immune response.

Additionally, Polygonatum multiflorum's ability to inhibit pro-inflammatory cytokines and induce anti-inflammatory molecules may also contribute to its immunosuppressive effects.
