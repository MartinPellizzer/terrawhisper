Dioscorea minutiflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Dioscorea minutiflora?

Dioscorea minutiflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Dioscoreales and is a member of the family Dioscoreaceae. Finally, it is placed within the genus Dioscorea, with its specific designation being the species minutiflora.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dioscorea minutiflora has slender, climbing stems with small, heart-shaped leaves and tiny, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers. The foliage consists of thin petioles supporting blades that are typically ovate to cordate in shape. Its diminutive floral structures are a defining characteristic, often appearing in small clusters along the vines. The plant relies on its twining habit to navigate through surrounding vegetation for light. These morphological traits distinguish it from other species within the Dioscorea genus.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?
