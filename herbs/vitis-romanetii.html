Vitis romanetii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vitis romanetii has woody, climbing vines characterized by trifoliate leaves with serrated margins and globose berries that typically turn dark purple or black when ripe. The foliage features distinctively shaped leaflets that can vary in size depending on the maturity of the plant. Its stems are equipped with tendrils that facilitate climbing through surrounding vegetation. The inflorescences consist of small, greenish flowers that eventually develop into clusters of fruit. These morphological traits are essential for distinguishing this specific species from other members of the Vitaceae family.
