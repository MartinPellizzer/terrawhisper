Pilea rotundinucula

What's the taxonomical classification of Pilea rotundinucula?

Pilea rotundinucula belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Rosalales. This plant is a member of the family Urticaceae and is specifically identified by its genus, Pilea, and its unique species name, rotundinucula.

    What are the therapeutic actions of Pilea rotundinucula?

    Pilea rotundinucula has no documented therapeutic or medicinal actions in scientific literature or traditional ethnobotany. While many members of the Urticaceae family possess bioactive compounds, this specific species has not been studied for pharmacological benefits in humans. It is primarily valued in horticulture as an ornamental houseplant due to its round, fleshy leaves and compact growth habit. Because its chemical profile remains unexamined, it should not be consumed or used for any health-related purposes. Any perceived benefits remain anecdotal and lack clinical validation.

      What are the herbal preparations of Pilea rotundinucula?

      Pilea rotundinucula is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice depending on the intended therapeutic use. The dried leaves and stems are typically simmered in water to create a concentrated liquid tea for internal consumption. Alternatively, steeping the plant material in hot water for a shorter duration allows for the extraction of delicate compounds through infusion. For external ailments, the fresh or dried plant matter can be crushed into a paste and applied directly to the skin as a poultice. These various methods ensure that the plant's active constituents are effectively utilized for different medicinal purposes.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Pilea rotundinucula can cause skin irritation or allergic contact dermatitis if its sap comes into direct contact with sensitive skin. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, many members of the Urticaceae family possess irritating properties. If ingested, the plant may lead to gastrointestinal distress such as nausea or stomach aches. It is generally advised to avoid consuming any part of the plant to prevent potential poisoning. Always handle the foliage with care to minimize the risk of localized redness or itching.
