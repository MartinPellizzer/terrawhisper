What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Agrostis clavata has a tufted perennial growth habit characterized by slender, erect culms that typically reach heights of 10 to 40 centimeters.

The leaves are narrow and linear, often featuring a rolled or involute structure to minimize moisture loss. Its inflorescence is a contracted, spike-like panicle that appears somewhat club-shaped or dense toward the apex. The spikelets are small and arranged in a tight manner along the branches, which are often shorter than the spikelets themselves.

        What are the herbal preparations of Agrostis clavata?

        Agrostis clavata is prepared primarily as a decoction or an infusion through the boiling or steeping of its dried aerial parts in water. These liquid extracts serve as the foundational method for administering the plant's bioactive compounds.         What side-effects this plant can have?

        Agrostis clavata can cause allergic reactions such as contact dermatitis, skin irritation, and respiratory distress due to its fine, irritating hairs and airborne pollen. Individuals with sensitive skin may experience redness, itching, or small bumps upon direct contact with the plant. Inhalation of the pollen can trigger symptoms of hay fever, including sneezing, coughing, and watery eyes. For those with asthma, exposure to the plant may exacerbate breathing difficulties or lead to wheezing. It is important to avoid direct contact and minimize exposure in windy conditions to prevent these adverse effects.
