Dischidia nummularia

What's the taxonomical classification of Dischidia nummularia?

Dischidia nummularia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Gentianales and is a member of the family Apocynaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Dischidia and is specifically distinguished as the species nummularia.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various internal ailments including stomach aches, diarrhea, and intestinal worms. It is frequently utilized in traditional medicine to alleviate gastrointestinal distress by regulating bowel movements. Some practitioners also apply its properties to manage inflammation within the digestive tract. The leaves and roots are often prepared in specific ways to maximize their medicinal effectiveness. These traditional uses are primarily rooted in local folk healing practices.
