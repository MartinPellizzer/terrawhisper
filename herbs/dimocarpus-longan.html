Dimocarpus longan

What's the taxonomical classification of Dimocarpus longan?

Dimocarpus longan belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving through its hierarchical classification, the plant falls under the order Sapindales and the family Sapindaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Dimocarpus, and its unique species designation, longan.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Dimocarpus longan contains high concentrations of polyphenols, flavonoids, saponins, alkaloids, and essential oils that provide its medicinal properties. The presence of gallic acid and ellagic acid contributes significant antioxidant activity to the fruit and its shell. These bioactive compounds work to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress within cellular structures. Additionally, the plant exhibits anti-inflammatory and neuroprotective effects through its complex chemical constituents. Studies also suggest that certain extracts may possess antimicrobial and hypoglycemic qualities.

What are the therapeutic actions of Dimocarpus longan?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various conditions including anemia, insomnia, palpitations, forgetfulness, and fatigue. In traditional Chinese medicine, the fruit is often utilized to nourish the heart and calm the spirit. It is frequently prescribed to patients suffering from dizziness or palpitations caused by blood deficiency. Some practitioners also recommend it to support cognitive function and improve memory. Additionally, the fruit is believed to help regulate the menstrual cycle and address issues related to exhaustion.
