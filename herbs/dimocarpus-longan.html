Dimocarpus longan

What's the taxonomical classification of Dimocarpus longan?

Dimocarpus longan belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving through its hierarchical classification, the plant falls under the order Sapindales and the family Sapindaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Dimocarpus, and its unique species designation, longan.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dimocarpus longan has evergreen, woody branches characterized by oppositely arranged, leathery, elliptical leaves that measure approximately 5 to 15 centimeters in length. The foliage typically displays a dark green upper surface and a paler underside, providing a dense canopy structure. Small, fragrant, cream-colored flowers emerge in large, branched panicles from the axils of the leaves or terminal buds. These blossoms eventually develop into small, globose fruits with a thin, brownish, textured pericarp that protects a translucent, sweet aril. The internal structure of the fruit contains a single, hard, black seed that resembles a dragon eye.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the subtropical and tropical regions of Southeast Asia, specifically spanning across southern China, Vietnam, Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. It thrives in warm, humid climates characterized by high annual rainfall and well-drained soils. While its primary origins are in these Asian territories, it has been widely naturalized in other tropical locations due to human cultivation. Major commercial production is now also found in parts of India and various islands within the Pacific. The distribution is strictly limited by its sensitivity to frost and its requirement for consistent warmth.
