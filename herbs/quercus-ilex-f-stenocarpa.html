Quercus ilex f. stenocarpa

Quercus ilex f. stenocarpa, commonly known as Holm Oak, is a variant of the Holm Oak tree species, characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves and broad, rounded crown.

This plant has been used for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Italian and Sardinian folk medicine, where it is valued for its ability to promote digestion and relieve symptoms of gastrointestinal disorders, thereby showcasing its health benefits.

The therapeutic actions of Quercus ilex f. stenocarpa are primarily attributed to its astringent, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties, which can help to soothe and protect the mucous membranes in the digestive tract.

The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties and contribute to its use in traditional medicine.
