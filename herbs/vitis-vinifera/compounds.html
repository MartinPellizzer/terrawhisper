Common Grape (Vitis vinifera) Compounds

The primary compounds in Common Grape include flavonoids, which act as antioxidants, and anthocyanins, responsible for the fruit's color and antioxidant properties. Phenolic acids and tannins contribute to its astringent taste and health benefits. Coumarin and saponins have anti-inflammatory effects, while terpenoids and essential oils provide aromatic properties.

Common Grape (Vitis vinifera) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Common Grape (Vitis vinifera). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant properties and are associated with various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and cardiovascular support. These compounds are commonly extracted from grape skins and used in herbal medicine for their therapeutic potential.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Common Grape (Vitis vinifera). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound is found in the skin and seeds of grapes, and it plays a role in the plant's natural defense mechanisms. Its presence supports the medicinal use of grapes in promoting health and preventing cellular damage.

3. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanins are pigments found in Common Grape (Vitis vinifera) that contribute to its red and purple hues. These compounds act as antioxidants, helping to neutralize free radicals in the body. They are associated with cardiovascular benefits and may support eye health. Anthocyanins are also studied for their potential anti-inflammatory properties.

4. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Common Grape (Vitis vinifera). It contributes to the astringent properties of the plant and has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins in grapes are found in the skin and seeds, and they play a role in traditional herbal medicine for digestive and skin-related ailments.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a naturally occurring compound found in Common Grape (Vitis vinifera). It exhibits anticoagulant properties and may help in preventing blood clot formation. Coumarin is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential cardiovascular benefits. Its presence in grapes contributes to the plant's therapeutic profile.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Common Grape (Vitis vinifera). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly in supporting cardiovascular health and digestive function. Glycosides in grapes are often associated with antioxidant benefits and may aid in reducing inflammation.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Common Grape (Vitis vinifera). They contribute to the plant's aroma and have demonstrated antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds are often studied for their potential therapeutic applications in herbal medicine.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound found in Common Grape (Vitis vinifera). It exhibits mild surfactant properties and may support digestive health. Saponins in grapes are also associated with antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

9. Essential oil

The essential oil of Common Grape (Vitis vinifera) contains volatile compounds like limonene and myrcene. These oils are used in aromatherapy for their calming effects and may support digestive and respiratory health. They are typically extracted through steam distillation from grape pomace.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloids are a class of naturally occurring compounds found in Common Grape (Vitis vinifera). While grapes contain various alkaloids, their medicinal significance is limited compared to other plant sources. Some alkaloids may contribute to the plant's natural defenses, but they are not the primary active constituents in traditional herbal medicine.
