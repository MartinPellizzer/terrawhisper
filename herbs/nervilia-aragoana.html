Nervilia aragoana

What's the taxonomical classification of Nervilia aragoana?

Nervilia aragoana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant is further categorized under the order Asparagales and falls within the family Orchidaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Nervilia and the specific species aragoana.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat digestive issues, skin infections, and respiratory ailments in traditional medicinal practices. Local healers often prepare extracts from its rhizomes to alleviate stomach pains and intestinal disturbances. It is also applied topically to soothe various skin irritations and fungal growths. Some practitioners use decoctions of the plant to help manage symptoms of coughs and minor bronchial discomfort. These applications are deeply rooted in specific regional ethnobotanical traditions.
