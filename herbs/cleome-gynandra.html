What is Cleome Shrub (Cleome Gynandra)?

Cleome gynandra, commonly known as the cleome shrub or spider plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia, valued for its medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal medicine.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Cleome shrub is Cleome gynandra. This plant belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Spermatophyta, class Caryophyllopsida, order Cleomales, family Cleomaceae, genus Cleome, and species Cleome gynandra. It is commonly known as "African spinach" in West Africa and "Mexican spinach" in Central America. The plant is also referred to as "cleome" in botanical contexts and "buttercup plant" in regional names across parts of Asia and Africa. Synonyms for Cleome gynandra include Cleome hirsuta and Cleome spinosa, though these are sometimes considered regional or botanical variations.

Cleome shrub is an erect, herbaceous plant that typically grows between 30 to 120 centimeters in height, with a branching structure that supports its dense foliage.

Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a serrated margin and a slightly hairy surface. The flowers are small, clustered in dense heads, and feature five petals that are pale yellow to white in color, often with a slight pinkish tinge. The root system is fibrous and shallow, allowing the plant to thrive in well-drained soils.

The leaves and stems have a rough, slightly hairy texture, while the flowers emit a mild, grassy scent.
