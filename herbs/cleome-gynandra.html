This herb has been documented in ancient medical texts, such as the Ayurvedic and Unani traditions, where it was valued for its medicinal properties and used in various remedies for centuries.

What are the horticulural aspects of Cleome gynandra?

Cleome gynandra grow in well-draining soil with a pH between 6.0 and 7.0. It prefers full sun to partial shade and can tolerate temperatures up to 30°C. Water moderately, allowing the top 2-3 cm of soil to dry between waterings.

For optimal growth, plant Cleome gynandra in a location with good air circulation. Space plants 30-60 cm apart, and 10-15 cm deeper than the potting mix depth. Sow seeds directly in the ground in spring, when the danger of frost has passed. Avoid transplanting, as this can cause root disturbance.

Harvest flowers regularly to encourage bushy growth and prevent the plant from flowering prematurely. Pinch or cut off buds to use in arrangements, and remove spent flowers to maintain plant appearance. Leaves can be harvested for use in salads, but do not pick them too frequently to avoid damaging the plant.

Common pests of Cleome gynandra include aphids, whiteflies, and spider mites. Leaf spot and powdery mildew are frequent fungal diseases. Regularly inspect the plant for signs of infestation or disease, and treat promptly with insecticidal soap or fungicides. Maintain good hygiene and air circulation to prevent the spread of pests and diseases.
