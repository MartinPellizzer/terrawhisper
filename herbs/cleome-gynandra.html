Cleome gynandra

What's the taxonomical classification of Cleome gynandra?

Cleome gynandra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant falls under the order Brassicales and is a member of the family Cleomaceae. Specifically, it is identified by the genus Cleome and the species gynandra.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Cleome gynandra contains high concentrations of glucosinolates, flavonoids, alkaloids, saponins, and tannins that contribute to its diverse pharmacological properties. These bioactive compounds work together to provide significant antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial activities. The presence of specific glucosinolates is particularly notable for their potential role in cancer prevention and detoxification processes. Additionally, the plant's rich profile of essential minerals and vitamins complements these chemical constituents to support overall metabolic health. Researchers continue to study these specific secondary metabolites to develop new therapeutic applications for human medicine.

What are the therapeutic actions of Cleome gynandra?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat malaria, stomach aches, skin infections, and various digestive disorders. It contains bioactive compounds that help combat parasitic infections in the blood. The leaves are often prepared as a decoction to soothe gastrointestinal inflammation and alleviate symptoms of constipation. Additionally, topical applications of the plant can assist in healing wounds and managing fungal skin conditions. Its diverse medicinal properties make it a vital resource in traditional healing practices across many regions.
