Cleome Gynandra

Cleome Gynandra, scientifically known as Cleome gynandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions, commonly referred to as spider flower or spider plant.

It has been traditionally used in various African cultures for its medicinal properties, particularly in the treatment of skin conditions and respiratory ailments. The plant contains bioactive compounds such as alkaloids, flavonoids, and saponins, which contribute to its therapeutic effects. Cleome gynandra is also valued for its potential anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antipyretic properties.

Due to its diverse pharmacological activities, it is increasingly being studied for its potential applications in modern medicine.
