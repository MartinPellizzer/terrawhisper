Botanical Identification

Cleome gynandra, commonly known as Cleome shrub, belongs to the family Malvaceae. It is an annual herb native to tropical Africa. The plant has palmate leaves, yellow flowers, and a fibrous root system. It is also referred to as "chop-chop" in some regions and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.

Active Compounds

Cleome gynandra contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and tannins. These compounds work synergistically to provide its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Phenolic acid
  • Tannin

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action
Integumentary Skin infection, inflammatory skin conditions, wound healing, skin infections, itching Antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, antioxidant
Respiratory Respiratory infections, cough, mucous membrane inflammation Antimicrobial, expectorant, anti-inflammatory, mucolytic
Digestive Gastrointestinal infections, diarrhea, gastrointestinal inflammation, parasitic infections, gastrointestinal parasites Antimicrobial, antispasmodic, anti-inflammatory

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory infections.

Infusion: Used for skin conditions and as a general tonic.

Poultice: Used for wound healing and reducing inflammation.

Safety Profile

Cleome gynandra is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. There are no well-documented contraindications, but caution is advised during pregnancy and lactation due to limited research on its effects.
