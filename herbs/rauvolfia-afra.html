What's the taxonomical classification of Rauvolfia afra?

Rauvolfia afra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is placed in the order Gentianales and is a member of the family Apocynaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Rauvolfia and the specific species epithet afra.

What are the pharmacological activities of Rauvolfia afra?

This plant has antihypertensive, sedative, antipsychotic, and antimicrobial pharmacological activities driven primarily by its indole alkaloid content. Reserpine, a key alkaloid found within its roots, works by depleting catecholamines to lower blood pressure and reduce anxiety. The plant also demonstrates significant sedative properties that can assist in treating sleep disorders and mental agitation. Beyond its neurological effects, certain extracts exhibit the ability to inhibit the growth of various pathogenic bacteria and fungi. These diverse biological actions make it a significant subject in both traditional medicine and modern pharmacological research.
