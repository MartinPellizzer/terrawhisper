What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Styrophyton caudatum has a woody stem structure, lanceolate leaves with entire margins, and distinctively long, tapering floral bracts that form a caudate appearance. The reproductive organs are clustered at the terminals of the branches, surrounded by protective leaf-like structures. These specialized bracts give the plant its specific botanical name and serve to protect the developing flowers.
