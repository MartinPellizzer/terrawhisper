Merremia vitifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Merremia vitifolia?

Merremia vitifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this classification, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, the plant is organized under the order Solanales and is a member of the family Convolvulaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by the genus Merremia and the species vitiolia.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Merremia vitifolia contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, and tannins. These chemical constituents are primarily concentrated within the leaves and roots of the climbing vine. Scientific studies suggest that these compounds contribute to the plant's significant antioxidant and antimicrobial properties. Some researchers have specifically investigated its potential for managing inflammation and treating skin infections. The presence of these specific phytochemicals makes the plant a subject of interest in ethnopharmacology and drug discovery.

What are the therapeutic actions of Merremia vitifolia?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, inflammatory conditions, wounds, and various digestive ailments. Its bioactive compounds provide antimicrobial properties that help combat bacterial and fungal pathogens on the skin. Traditional healers often apply the leaves or extracts to soothe localized inflammation and accelerate the healing process of open cuts. Some practitioners also use decoctions of the plant to address internal issues like stomach discomfort or intestinal parasites. The therapeutic versatility of the species makes it a valuable component in many regional folk medicine practices.
