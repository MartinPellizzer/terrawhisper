Merremia vitifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Merremia vitifolia?

Merremia vitifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this classification, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, the plant is organized under the order Solanales and is a member of the family Convolvulaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by the genus Merremia and the species vitiolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Merremia vitifolia has a climbing, perennial vine habit characterized by woody stems and opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with entire margins. These blossoms feature five petals and are followed by small, fleshy berries that aid in seed dispersal. According to a study published by "Heliyon", the methanolic Merremia vitifolia stem extract (MEMVS) demonstrates diverse pharmacological activities including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, analgesic, antidepressant, anxiolytic, locomotor, and antidiarrehal properties. The extract showed an antioxidant IC50 value of 74.97 μg/mL and elicited 77.17 ± 0.39 % inhibition of protein denaturation at 500 μg/mL compared to a standard of 87.23 ± 0.30 %. MEMVS induced a dose-dependent reduction in analgesic and neuropathrological inhibition, as well as in diarrheal feces count and intestinal motility with a significant value (aP< 0.001). Based on GC/MS data, 15 bioactive compounds were identified and confirmed to adhere to Lipinski's rule of five through in silico investigation. These findings suggest that MEMVS holds promising potential as a multifaceted therapeutic agent.
