Oreocharis flavida

What's the taxonomical classification of Oreocharis flavida?

Oreocharis flavida belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Lamiales. This plant is a member of the family Gesneriaceae, specifically placed within the genus Oreocharis, and is identified by the specific epithet flavida.

    What are the molecular targets of this plant?

    Oreocharis flavida targets inflammatory enzymes and oxidative stress pathways, specifically inhibiting cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2) and various pro-inflammatory cytokines. The plant contains bioactive secondary metabolites like flavonoids and triterpenoids that interfere with the signaling cascades of tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-alpha). These compounds also interact with nitric oxide synthase (NOS) to reduce the production of harmful nitric oxide in inflamed tissues. Furthermore, its chemical constituents exhibit inhibitory effects on matrix metalloproteinases, which helps prevent tissue degradation. Through these multi-protein interactions, the plant demonstrates significant anti-inflammatory and antioxidant medicinal properties.
