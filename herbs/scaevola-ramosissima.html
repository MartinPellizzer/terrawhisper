What is Scaevola ramosissima?

Scaevola ramosissima is a species of flowering plant in the family Goodeniaceae, native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree, growing up to 4 meters tall, with a spread of 2-3 meters, and has a woody stem with a greyish-brown bark. The leaves are elliptical to lanceolate, 5-12 centimeters long, and 1-3 centimeters wide, with a leathery texture and a pointed apex. The inflorescence is a panicle or spike, bearing small, blue to purple flowers with five petals, 1-2 centimeters in diameter, and a prominent stamen.

The fruit is a capsule, 1-2 centimeters long, containing several seeds.
