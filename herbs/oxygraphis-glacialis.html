What's the taxonomical classification of Oxygraphis glacialis?

Oxygraphis glacialis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Ranunculales and the family Ranunculaceae, eventually reaching its specific designation within the genus Oxygraphis. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its unique species name, glacialis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Oxygraphis glacialis has a perennial, creeping rhizomatous habit characterized by slender, erect stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the upper leaf axils, displaying a distinct bilabiate structure often colored in shades of white or pale pink. The calyx is notably elongated and features several prominent, unequal teeth that persist during fruiting.
