What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Myrsine semiserrata has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical to oblong in shape and feature distinctively serrated margins along the upper half of the blade. The plant possesses woody stems that support small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in axillary clusters. These flowers eventually develop into small, fleshy drupes that serve as the plant's fruit.
