What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Cyanopsis Tetragonoloba (Tetra Leaf)?

Cyanopsis tetragonoloba, also known as tetra leaf, has health benefits such as reducing inflammation, improving digestion, and alleviating fever.

The herb is rich in flavonoids, alkaloids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties. Its medicinal preparations include infusions, decoctions, and extracts, often used to treat various ailments. However, excessive consumption may cause side effects such as stomach upset and allergic reactions.

It is essential to consult a healthcare professional before using this herb, especially for pregnant or breastfeeding women.

What are the medicinal preparations of Cyanopsis tetragonoloba ?

Cyanopsis tetragonoloba, also known as tetra leaf, has medicinal preparations such as leaf paste, used to treat fever and skin conditions.

The leaves are also used in decoctions for treating rheumatism, joint pain, and respiratory problems. Aerial parts of the plant are used to treat liver and spleen disorders, while the roots are used to treat fever, headache, and eye infections.

These preparations are widely used in traditional medicine.

What are the possible side effect of using Cyanopsis tetragonoloba improperly?

Improper use of Cyanopsis tetragonoloba, also known as tetra leaf, increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain due to its toxic alkaloids.

Long-term consumption can cause damage to the kidneys, liver, and nervous system.

Additionally, tetra leaf may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners, and worsen conditions like diabetes, hypertension, and gastrointestinal disorders.
