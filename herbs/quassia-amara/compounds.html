Quassia (Quassia amara) Compounds

The primary compounds of Quassia amara include alkaloids, which have bitter and antiseptic properties, and saponins, which exhibit antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Flavonoids and tannins contribute to its antioxidant and astringent qualities, while coumarin provides anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory benefits. These compounds work synergistically to support its medicinal uses in herbal medicine.
