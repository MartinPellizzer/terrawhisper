Arabian Coffee (Coffea arabica)

Arabian Coffee (Coffea arabica) is a member of the Rubaceae family, native to Eastern Africa, Southern Arabia, and Madagascar. Traditionally, its seeds, fruits, and leaves have been used for infusions, decoctions, and culinary uses.

This herb is particularly valued for its stimulant, bitter, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, african traditional medicine, and mediterranean herbal traditions.
