Coffea arabica

What's the taxonomical classification of Coffea arabica?

Coffea arabica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Gentianaales. This plant is a prominent representative of the family Rubiaceae, falling under the genus Coffea, and is specifically identified by the species name arabica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Coffea arabica is an evergreen shrub or small tree characterized by its glossy, elliptical, dark green leaves with wavy margins and its terminal clusters of fragrant white flowers. These flowers emerge from the leaf axils and eventually develop into small, fleshy drupes known as coffee cherries. The fruit transitions from green to bright red or yellow when fully ripe, enclosing two seeds positioned opposite each other. The plant typically reaches a height of two to four meters, though it can grow larger in optimal wild conditions. Its woody stem supports a dense canopy of foliage that provides the shade necessary for its physiological development.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude tropical forests of Ethiopia and parts of southwestern Yemen. While its wild ancestors remain concentrated in the Horn of Africa, cultivation has spread extensively across the equatorial belt. Major production zones now include Brazil, Vietnam, Colombia, and Indonesia due to favorable volcanic soil and tropical climates. The species thrives specifically in regions with consistent rainfall and elevations ranging from 600 to 2,000 meters. This wide global dispersal is driven by the commercial demand for its unique aromatic profiles.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Coffea arabica contains medicinal compounds primarily located in its seeds, leaves, and fruit pulp. The seeds, commonly known as coffee beans, are the most frequently utilized part due to their high concentration of caffeine and chlorogenic acids. These chemical constituents are often studied for their ability to improve cognitive function and metabolic rates. The leaves are also harvested to create herbal teas that may offer antioxidant and anti-inflammatory benefits. Additionally, the mucilage found in the fruit pulp contains various bioactive compounds that contribute to the plant's overall medicinal profile.

What are the therapeutic actions of Coffea arabica?

Coffea arabica has therapeutic properties that act primarily as a central nervous system stimulant, a mild diuretic, and a bitter tonic. The caffeine content provides a potent stimulant effect that increases alertness and reduces fatigue. Its inherently bitter profile can help stimulate digestive secretions and improve appetite. As a mild diuretic, the plant encourages increased urine production to assist with fluid balance. Additionally, it functions as a nervine that can alter mood and mental clarity through its interaction with the brain.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains chlorogenic acid, caffeine, L-DOPA, phenolic acids, and flavonoids. These medicinal compounds provide a diverse range of biological activities that support human health. Chlorogenic acid and phenolic acids act as potent antioxidants that help protect cells from oxidative stress. The presence of caffeine serves as a natural stimulant for the central nervous system, while L-DOPA plays a crucial role in neurological function. Additionally, flavonoids contribute to anti-inflammatory properties and cardiovascular support.

What common ailments are treated using this plant?

This plant is used to treat various fungal pathogens that devastate its productivity, including coffee leaf rust, coffee berry disease, cercosporas leaf spot, coffee anthracnose, and black coffee rust. These specific diseases target different parts of the plant, such as the foliage or the fruit itself. Coffee leaf rust and black coffee rust are particularly notorious for destroying leaves and reducing photosynthesis. Meanwhile, coffee berry disease and coffee anthracnose directly attack the ripening cherries, leading to significant crop loss. Cercosporas leaf spot further compromises the health of the plant by creating necrotic lesions on the leaves.

The main common ailment treated using this plant are shown in the list below.

  • Coffee leaf rust
  • Coffee berry disease
  • Cercospora leaf spot
  • Coffee anthracnose
  • Black coffee rust

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for managing and potentially preventing Alzheimer's disease, Parkinson's disease, diabetes mellitus, cardiovascular disease, and cognitive dysfunction. The bioactive compounds found in the coffee beans help protect neurological pathways from progressive degeneration. Regular consumption may improve insulin sensitivity, which assists in the regulation of glucose levels related to diabetes mellitus. Furthermore, certain antioxidants within the plant contribute to heart health by reducing risks associated with cardiovascular disease. These combined properties work together to mitigate various forms of cognitive dysfunction in aging populations.

What are the herbal preparations of this plant?

This plant can be prepared as an infusion, decoction, extract, essential oil, or tincture to utilize its various chemical constituents. An infusion of the dried leaves or flowers is often used to create a mild, aromatic tea. For a more concentrated effect, a decoction involves boiling the harder plant parts to release deeper compounds. Concentrated extracts and tinctures serve as potent medicinal or flavoring agents when dissolved in solvents like water or alcohol. Additionally, the essential oil can be harvested from the blossoms for use in aromatherapy.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause insomnia, increased heart rate, anxiety, jitteriness, and gastrointestinal upset due to its high caffeine content. Excessive consumption often leads to restlessness and difficulty falling asleep at night. Some individuals may experience palpitations or an irregular heartbeat if they are sensitive to stimulants. Frequent intake can also trigger digestive issues such as acid reflux or stomach discomfort. Managing the dosage is essential to avoid these physiological disruptions.
