Arabian coffee

Coffea arabica

Arabian Coffee (coffea arabica) is a member of the Rubaceae family and has been traditionally used as a stimulant in various cultures. The plant's seeds, commonly known as coffee beans, are the primary part used, and it has been historically employed to treat headaches and fatigue. In some regions, it is also noted for its calming effects, though these uses are not universally consistent.

Botanical Identification

Arabian Coffee, scientifically known as Coffea arabica, belongs to the Rubiaceae family. It is a perennial shrub native to Ethiopia and Yemen. The plant has dark green, oval leaves and white flowers, with red berries containing seeds used for coffee. Common names include Arabica coffee and Caffea. Synonyms in herbal literature include Coffea arabica and Coffea arabica L..

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for digestive issues and insomnia due to its calming properties.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and as a stimulant due to its caffeine content.

Culinary use: Used for energy and mood enhancement through consumption of coffee.

Safety Profile

Arabian Coffee (Coffea arabica) is generally safe when consumed in moderate amounts. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated for people with insomnia or anxiety due to its stimulating effects. Always consult a healthcare provider before use, especially for those with pre-existing medical conditions.
