10 Possible Side Effects Of Macadamia Ternifolia (Queensland Nut)

Macadamia ternifolia has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, including diarrhea, nausea, and abdominal pain.

These issues are caused by the high levels of fats and oils in the nut, which can be difficult to digest.

If left unchecked, these side effects can worsen lives by disrupting daily activities, causing weight loss, and even contributing to malnutrition, thereby affecting overall well-being and quality of life.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Macadamia ternifolia if used imporperly.

1. Aggravates diabetes symptoms

Macadamia ternifolia, also known as Queensland nut, causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

This is because the tree's seeds and oil contain a protein called macadamia oleinase that can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling. Additionally, the nuts' high levels of allergenic proteins like vicilin and convicilin can also cause allergic reactions in sensitive individuals.

As a result, some people may experience adverse reactions upon consuming or coming into contact with Macadamia ternifolia products.
