10 Medicinal Constituents Of Macadamia Ternifolia (Queensland Nut)

Macadamia ternifolia has active constituents such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and fatty acids.

These constituents possess antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which contribute to its medicinal properties. The plant has been traditionally used to alleviate digestive issues and skin conditions.

The flavonoids and phenolic acids in queensland nut can improve cardiovascular health and reduce the risk of chronic diseases, potentially enhancing overall quality of life.

10. Oleic acid

Macadamia ternifolia, also known as Queensland nut, oleic acid is a primary fatty acid present in macadamia nuts.

It is a monounsaturated saturated fat, composed of 77% oleic acid and 12% palmitoleic acid. The unique combination of these fatty acids contributes to the distinct taste and nutritional profile of macadamia nuts, which are rich in antioxidants, vitamins, and minerals.

Oleic acid has been linked to various health benefits, including reducing inflammation, improving cholesterol levels, and providing anti-cancer properties.
