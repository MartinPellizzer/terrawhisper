Rubus biflorus

What's the taxonomical classification of Rubus biflorus?

Rubus biflorus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order of Rosales and is a member of the family Rosaecae. Finally, it is identified by its genus, Rubus, and its specific species, biflorus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rubus biflorus has woody, arching stems characterized by a dense covering of small, sharp prickles and a branching habit that supports multiple flower clusters. The leaves are typically trifoliate or pentafoliate with serrated margins and a textured, slightly pubescent surface. Each plant produces small, white flowers that emerge from axillary positions along the canes. These blossoms eventually develop into aggregate fruits that transition from green to a dark, fleshy red or black color upon ripening. The root system is perennial and robust, allowing the plant to spread through vegetative growth in its natural habitat.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, specifically spanning parts of India, Nepal, Bhutan, and Tibet. It primarily inhabits subalpine and alpine zones where rocky terrain and cool temperatures prevail. Within these mountain systems, it is commonly found growing in scrublands and along the edges of forested slopes. Its distribution is closely tied to specific elevations that support its unique growth requirements. This limited range makes it a characteristic species of the high-elevation Asian montane ecosystems.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds 2alpha-hydroxyoleanolic acid, corosolic acid, lupeol acetate, oleanic acid, and sitosterol. These specific triterpenoids and sterols contribute to the complex chemical profile used in various therapeutic applications. Corosolic acid is particularly noted for its potential role in regulating blood sugar levels. Additionally, compounds like oleanic acid and 2alpha-hydroxyoleanolic acid are studied for their anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. The presence of sitosterol and lupeol acetate further enhances the biological activity of the plant's extract.

As discussed in a study, the root of Rubus biflorus contains five isolated medicinal compounds. These identified substances include lupeol acetate (I), corosolic acid (II), oleanic acid (III), 2alpha-hydroxyoleanolic acid (IV), and sitosterol (V). Structural determination for these 5 compounds was achieved using 1H-NMR, 13C-NMR, IR, and EI-MS spectral data analysis. All 5 of these compounds are being isolated from this plant for the first time.
