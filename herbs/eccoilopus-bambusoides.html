What's the taxonomical classification of Eccoilopus bambusoides?

Eccoilopus bambusoides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent through the order Poales, it is placed within the family Poaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Eccoilopus, with its specific identity defined by the species name bambusoides.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Eccoilopus bambusoides can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and localized swelling if the plant or its sap comes into direct contact with human tissue. Individuals with sensitive skin may experience redness, itching, or a burning sensation upon touching the foliage. Some users might also suffer from respiratory discomfort if the pollen or fine particles become airborne and are inhaled. Prolonged exposure to the plant's chemical compounds can lead to more severe dermatological reactions in certain people. It is generally advised to wear protective gloves when handling this species to minimize these risks.
