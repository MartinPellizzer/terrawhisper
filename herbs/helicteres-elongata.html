Helicteres elongata

What's the taxonomical classification of Helicteres elongata?

Helicteres elongata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic progression continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Malvales. Furthermore, it is a member of the family Malvaceae, falling within the genus Helicteres, and is specifically identified by its species name, elongata.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Helicteres elongata contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including flavonoids, tannins, saponins, and terpenoids that contribute to its pharmacological activities. These specific chemical constituents are primarily concentrated in the leaves, bark, and roots of the plant. Research indicates that these compounds possess significant antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. The presence of phenolic compounds in particular helps in neutralizing free radicals within biological systems. Such chemical profiles justify its traditional use in various ethnobotanical remedies for treating systemic ailments.

What are the therapeutic actions of Helicteres elongata?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, gastrointestinal disturbances, fever, and various inflammatory conditions. The medicinal properties of the plant are often utilized to alleviate digestive issues like diarrhea or stomach aches. Its extracts are frequently applied topically to manage wounds and fungal skin diseases. Additionally, practitioners use the plant to reduce high body temperatures associated with malaria or other infections. These therapeutic applications rely on the diverse bioactive compounds found within the plant's leaves and bark.
