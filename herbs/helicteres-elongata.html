Helicteres elongata has woody, spreading stems with small, simple, or occasionally trifoliate leaves that possess elliptical to lanceolate shapes and serrated margins. Its reproductive structures consist of small, tubular flowers that emerge from the axils of the leaves. These flowers are often densely clustered and lead to the development of characteristic coiled or elongated fruit capsules.
