What's the taxonomical classification of Leonurus glaucescens?

Leonurus glaucescens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Lamiales, it falls under the family Lamiaceae, which is a prominent group of flowering plants. Finally, the plant is situated in the genus Leonurus, with its specific identity defined by the species glaucescens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Leonurus glaucescens has a square stem, opposite leaves with serrated margins, and small, hooded flowers that are typically pale blue or lavender.

The plant exhibits a decumbent to ascending growth habit, often forming dense patches in its native habitat. Its leaves are characterized by a glaucous, waxy coating that gives the foliage a distinct grayish-green hue. The inflorescence consists of dense verticillasters arranged in whorls along the upper portion of the stem.

Small, nutlet-like fruits develop within the persistent calyx following successful pollination.
