What's the taxonomical classification of Leonurus glaucescens?

Leonurus glaucescens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Lamiales, it falls under the family Lamiaceae, which is a prominent group of flowering plants. Finally, the plant is situated in the genus Leonurus, with its specific identity defined by the species glaucescens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Leonurus glaucescens has a square stem, opposite leaves with serrated margins, and small, hooded flowers that are typically pale blue or lavender. The inflorescence consists of dense verticillasters arranged in whorls along the upper portion of the stem. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the specific medicinal compounds leonoside A, leonoside B, verbascoide, and lavandulifolioside. These bioactive molecules belong to various classes of chemical constituents that contribute to the species' pharmacological potential. Researchers study these substances to understand their potential therapeutic applications in human health. The presence of such diverse phenylethanoid glycosides characterizes the unique chemical profile of Leonurus glaucescens. Identifying these specific elements helps scientists map the relationship between plant chemistry and biological activity.

According to a study published by "Phytochemistry", four phenylpropanoid glycosides were isolated from the aerial parts of Leonurus glaucescens. These compounds include two new glycosides named leonoside A and leonoside B, along with two known glycosides called lavandulifolioside and verbascoside.
