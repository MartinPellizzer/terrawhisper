Leonurus glaucescens

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Leonurus glaucescens has a square stem, opposite leaves with serrated margins, and small, hooded flowers that are typically pale blue or lavender. The plant exhibits a decumbent to ascending growth habit, often forming dense patches in its native habitat. Its leaves are characterized by a glaucous, waxy coating that gives the foliage a distinct grayish-green hue. The inflorescence consists of dense verticillasters arranged in whorls along the upper portion of the stem. Small, nutlet-like fruits develop within the persistent calyx following successful pollination.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

It primarily inhabits the dry, rocky slopes and open montane woodlands found within the Cascade and Sierra Nevada ranges. Populations are often concentrated in higher elevations where well-drained soils and specific microclimates support its growth. The species typically thrives in subalpine environments and sagebrush steppe ecosystems. Its distribution is closely tied to the unique geological features of the Pacific Northwest corridor.
