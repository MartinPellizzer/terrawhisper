What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hedyotis assimilis has a slender, herbaceous stem that supports small, ovate to lanceolate leaves with entire or slightly serrated margins. The foliage is typically arranged in an alternate pattern along the stem, displaying a relatively smooth texture. Its flowers are minute and often inconspicuous, emerging from the leaf axils to facilitate reproduction. The root system is generally shallow, providing stability within its specific ecological niche. These combined morphological traits allow the plant to maintain a compact and delicate structural form.
