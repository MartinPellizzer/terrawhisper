Caper (Capparis spinosa) Uses

Caper is used to treat gastritis, gastric ulcers, and peptic ulcers by soothing stomach lining and reducing inflammation. It also helps with gastroesophageal reflux disease and gastrointestinal spasms. Additionally, it may alleviate symptoms of inflammatory bowel disease and ease gastric discomfort, mouth ulcers, and diarrhea.

Caper (Capparis spinosa) Monograph

1. Gastritis

Caper (Capparis spinosa) is used in herbal medicine to support digestive health, particularly for gastritis. Its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties help reduce stomach lining irritation and excess acid. Often prepared as a tea or tincture, caper is valued for its soothing effect on the gastrointestinal tract.

2. Gastroesophageal reflux disease

Caper (Capparis spinosa) has been traditionally used to support digestive health, particularly in managing gastroesophageal reflux disease. Its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties may help reduce stomach acid and soothe esophageal irritation. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to alleviate symptoms associated with reflux.

3. Gastric ulcers

Caper (Capparis spinosa) has been traditionally used to treat gastric ulcers due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The plant contains compounds that help reduce stomach acid and protect the gastric lining. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to soothe irritation and promote healing in the digestive tract.

4. Gastrointestinal spasms

Caper has been traditionally used to alleviate gastrointestinal spasms due to its antispasmodic properties. The compounds in caper, such as flavonoids and essential oils, help relax smooth muscles in the digestive tract. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to ease cramping and discomfort associated with conditions like irritable bowel syndrome.

5. Peptic ulcer

Caper has been traditionally used to support digestive health, particularly in the treatment of peptic ulcers. Its anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties may help reduce stomach lining irritation and combat Helicobacter pylori, a common ulcer-causing bacteria. Caper is often consumed as a tea or tincture to soothe gastrointestinal discomfort and promote healing.

6. Inflammatory bowel disease

Caper (Capparis spinosa) has shown potential in reducing inflammation associated with inflammatory bowel disease due to its high content of flavonoids and antioxidants, which may help modulate gut inflammation and protect intestinal cells. Preliminary studies suggest it may support digestive health, though more research is needed to confirm its efficacy in clinical settings.

7. Gastric discomfort

Caper (Capparis spinosa) is used traditionally to alleviate gastric discomfort. Its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties help reduce stomach inflammation and soothe digestive upset. It may also aid in regulating digestion and reducing excess acid. Often prepared as a tea or tincture, caper supports digestive health in herbal medicine.

8. Mouth ulcers

Caper (Capparis spinosa) has been traditionally used to treat mouth ulcers due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. The leaves and flowers are often prepared as a poultice or infused into a mouthwash to soothe irritation and promote healing. Its high tannin content helps reduce swelling and accelerate tissue repair in the oral cavity.

9. Gastrointestinal discomfort

Caper (Capparis spinosa) is used to alleviate gastrointestinal discomfort due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It helps reduce inflammation in the digestive tract and can soothe symptoms like bloating and indigestion. The plant's compounds may also support gut health by balancing intestinal flora.

10. Diarrhea

Caper (Capparis spinosa) has been traditionally used to treat diarrhea due to its astringent properties, which help reduce intestinal inflammation and fluid loss. It contains compounds that may inhibit harmful bacteria and strengthen gut lining, promoting digestive health. Used in herbal formulations, it supports recovery from acute and chronic diarrhea.
