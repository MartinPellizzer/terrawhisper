10 Possible Side Effects Of Capparis Spinosa

Capparis spinosa has some side effects when used improperly, such as diarrhea, stomach cramps, and allergic reactions.

These side effects may occur due to excessive consumption, interaction with other medications, or individual sensitivity.

For example, people with irritable bowel syndrome may experience worsening symptoms, while those with allergies may suffer from anaphylaxis, leading to respiratory distress, cardiovascular issues, and potentially life-threatening complications.

1. Increases blood pressure

Capparis spinosa increases blood pressure due to its high content of potassium and calcium.

The consumption of capparis extract or supplements can stimulate the sympathetic nervous system, leading to an increase in heart rate and blood pressure.

Additionally, the plant's flavonoids may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners, and further exacerbate this effect.

2. Causes skin irritation

Capparis spinosa causes skin irritation due to its high concentration of alkaloids and terpenes.

These compounds can penetrate the skin, triggering an allergic reaction or irritating the skin's natural barrier function.

This may lead to symptoms such as redness, itching, and inflammation, potentially causing discomfort or pain for individuals with sensitive skin.

3. Triggers allergic reactions

Capparis spinosa triggers allergic reactions in some individuals.

This is often due to the presence of certain compounds such as alkaloids, saponins, and terpenes, which can stimulate an immune response and cause symptoms like hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.

Additionally, the pollen-like particles produced by the plant can exacerbate respiratory issues like asthma, further increasing the risk of an allergic reaction.

4. Induces digestive issues

Capparis spinosa induces digestive issues due to its high concentration of coumarin and alkaloids.

These compounds can irritate the stomach lining, leading to nausea, bloating, and diarrhea in some individuals.

Additionally, the volatile oils present in capers may cause stomach upset and discomfort in sensitive people.

5. Enhances sensitivity light

Capparis spinosa enhances sensitivity to light due to its potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

The caper plant contains flavonoids and phenolic acids that can stimulate the production of melanin in the eyes, increasing the sensitivity to light.

This reaction is often accompanied by increased visual acuity, but it may also cause discomfort or vision disturbance in some individuals.

6. Disrupts sleep patterns

Capparis spinosa disrupts sleep patterns.

This is due to its stimulating properties, which can increase alertness and energy levels, making it difficult for some individuals to fall asleep or stay asleep.

The plant's ability to act as a natural stimulant can also lead to restlessness and anxiety, further compromising the quality of one's sleep.

7. Lowers blood sugar

Capparis spinosa lowers blood sugar by interacting with the body's natural glucose regulation systems.

The plant contains compounds such as flavonoids and phenolic acids, which may stimulate insulin secretion and enhance glucose uptake in peripheral tissues.

Additionally, caper extract has been shown to inhibit alpha-glucosidase activity, delaying carbohydrate digestion and absorption, leading to a decrease in postprandial blood sugar levels.

8. Interacts with medications

Capparis spinosa interacts with medications that lower blood pressure, such as ACE inhibitors and diuretics.

This can cause a sudden drop in blood pressure, leading to dizziness, lightheadedness, or fainting. Additionally, Capparis spinosa may increase the risk of bleeding when taken with anti-platelet drugs or anticoagulants.

The mechanism behind these interactions is unclear, but it's thought that the caper plant's active compounds may alter the body's natural blood clotting processes.

9. Stimulates excessive sweating

Capparis spinosa stimulates excessive sweating due to its diaphoretic properties.

The alkaloids and glycosides present in the plant increase blood circulation, which can cause an increase in body temperature, leading to excessive sweating. Additionally, the volatile oils in Capparis spinosa may stimulate the sweat glands, further contributing to this side effect.

As a result, individuals using Capparis spinosa may experience profuse sweating, especially when taking high doses or combining it with other stimulants.

10. Produces kidney stones

Capparis spinosa produces kidney stones due to its high concentration of oxalate and purine compounds.

The consumption of these compounds can increase the levels of calcium oxalate in the urine, leading to the formation of kidney stones.

Additionally, the presence of purines can also contribute to the development of uric acid stones.
