Adenophora khasiana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Adenophora khasiana has erect, herbaceous stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and bear oppositely arranged, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is often characterized by a slightly hairy surface and distinct veining that runs through the leaf blades. Its inflorescence consists of small, tubular, bell-shaped flowers that emerge from the terminal ends of the stems in clusters. These flowers generally exhibit a purple or lavender coloration and are supported by slender pedicels. The plant's root system is relatively shallow, helping it anchor within its preferred shaded, montane habitats.
