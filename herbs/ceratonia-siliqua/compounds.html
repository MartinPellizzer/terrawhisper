Carob (Ceratonia siliqua) Compounds

Carob contains several primary active compounds, including flavonoids, which act as antioxidants. Phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory properties. Tannins provide astringent effects, while coumarin has antispasmodic qualities. Glycosides, saponins, terpenoids, and essential oils support various therapeutic actions, and anthocyanins offer additional antioxidant benefits. Alkaloids also contribute to its medicinal profile.

Carob (Ceratonia siliqua) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Carob (Ceratonia siliqua). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and respiratory health. These compounds help protect cells from oxidative damage and may modulate immune responses.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Carob (Ceratonia siliqua). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting digestive health and reducing inflammation. Found in high concentrations in carob pods, it plays a role in the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

3. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Carob (Ceratonia siliqua). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is used in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Tannins in Carob may also support digestive health and have antioxidant properties.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Carob (Ceratonia siliqua). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly in digestive and anti-inflammatory applications. Found naturally in the fruit and seeds, coumarin has been studied for its potential therapeutic benefits in herbal medicine.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Carob (Ceratonia siliqua). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting mild laxative and nutritive effects. Glycosides in Carob support digestive health and are part of its traditional use in herbal medicine.

6. Saponin

Saponin is an active compound found in Carob (Ceratonia siliqua). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine due to its mild detergent properties and potential anti-inflammatory effects. Saponin may support digestive health and has been studied for its role in reducing cholesterol levels.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Carob (Ceratonia siliqua) are a class of bioactive compounds with antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. They contribute to the plant's traditional use in digestive and respiratory health. These compounds are found in the seeds and pods, supporting their role in herbal medicine.

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of Carob (Ceratonia siliqua) contains volatile compounds like limonene and eugenol. These compounds contribute to its aromatic properties and may have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. The essential oil is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential therapeutic benefits.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Carob (Ceratonia siliqua). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting cardiovascular and digestive health. Found in the fruit's pulp, anthocyanin also aids in reducing inflammation and improving gut function.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Carob (Ceratonia siliqua). It contributes to the plant's potential medicinal properties, though research on its specific therapeutic effects is limited. Alkaloids in Carob may support digestive and anti-inflammatory actions, but further studies are needed to confirm their efficacy.
