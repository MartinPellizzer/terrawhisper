What's the taxonomical classification of Asplenium saxicola?

Asplenium saxicola belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it is further organized under the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. This specific fern is a representative of the family Aspleniaceae, falling under the genus Asplenium, with its unique biological identity defined by its species name, saxicola.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Asplenium saxicola has pinnate fronds that typically measure between 10 and 30 centimeters in length, characterized by a dark, shiny stipe and multiple pairs of lanceolate pinnae arranged along a central rachis.

The individual leaflets are relatively thin with slightly wavy or entire margins and a distinctively dark green color. The plant features a creeping rhizome that often grows tucked into rocky crevices or mossy substrates. Spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds within linear sori that are protected by rounded or kidney-shaped indusia.

These morphological traits allow the fern to thrive in the humid, shaded microclimates of its rocky habitats.
