What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Asplenium saxicola has pinnate fronds that typically measure between 10 and 30 centimeters in length, characterized by a dark, shiny stipe and multiple pairs of lanceolate pinnae arranged along a central rachis. The plant features a creeping rhizome that often grows tucked into rocky crevices or mossy substrates. Spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds within linear sori that are protected by rounded or kidney-shaped indusia.
