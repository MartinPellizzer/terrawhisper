Illicium lanceolatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Illicium lanceolatum?

Illicium lanceolatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows a specialized evolutionary lineage. This plant is situated within the order Austrobaileyales and is a representative of the family Schisandraceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Illicium and the unique species epithet lanceolatum.

Illicium lanceolatum contains medicinal compounds primarily concentrated in its dried fruits and bark. The plant's volatile oils also exhibit anti-inflammatory effects that are utilized in traditional medicinal practices. What side-effects this plant can have?

Illicium lanceolatum can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, neurological impairment, and potential toxicity if the incorrect species is consumed. While some members of the Illicium genus are used in traditional medicine, improper identification can lead to the ingestion of star anise relatives containing neurotoxins. These toxins can trigger symptoms such as dizziness, confusion, and muscle tremors. In high doses, the chemical compounds may result in more serious systemic issues or seizures. It is crucial to distinguish this plant from safe culinary varieties to avoid dangerous health complications.
