Illicium lanceolatum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Illicium lanceolatum has lanceolate to ovate leaves that are typically 5 to 15 centimeters long, arranged alternately along woody, evergreen stems. The leaf margins are entire and the texture is often coriaceous, meaning they possess a somewhat leathery feel. Small, fragrant flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves, usually featuring a distinct star-like shape. These plants develop woody fruits that eventually split to reveal bright red seeds. The overall structure is that of a small shrub or a low-growing tree with a dense canopy.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Illicium lanceolatum can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, neurological impairment, and potential toxicity if the incorrect species is consumed. While some members of the Illicium genus are used in traditional medicine, improper identification can lead to the ingestion of star anise relatives containing neurotoxins. These toxins can trigger symptoms such as dizziness, confusion, and muscle tremors. In high doses, the chemical compounds may result in more serious systemic issues or seizures. It is crucial to distinguish this plant from safe culinary varieties to avoid dangerous health complications.
