Illicium lanceolatum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Illicium lanceolatum has lanceolate to ovate leaves that are typically 5 to 15 centimeters long, arranged alternately along woody, evergreen stems. The leaf margins are entire and the texture is often coriaceous, meaning they possess a somewhat leathery feel. Small, fragrant flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves, usually featuring a distinct star-like shape. These plants develop woody fruits that eventually split to reveal bright red seeds. The overall structure is that of a small shrub or a low-growing tree with a dense canopy.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, neurological impairment, and potential death if ingested due to the presence of toxic compounds like anisatin. Consuming the fruit or seeds may lead to symptoms such as vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea. Some individuals may also experience more serious central nervous system effects including dizziness, muscle tremors, or seizures. Because the toxicity levels can vary between different parts of the plant, careful identification is necessary to avoid accidental poisoning. Medical attention should be sought immediately if any part of this species is consumed by humans or pets.
