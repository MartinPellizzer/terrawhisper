Lepisorus angustus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lepisorus angustus has narrow, linear-lanceolate fronds that typically measure between 5 and 15 centimeters in length and feature entire, slightly undulating margins. The stipe is relatively short and often displays a brownish or reddish tint at the base. Each pinna is arranged alternately along the rachis and possesses a distinctively thin, almost translucent texture. The sori are located on the underside of the pinnules, protected by small, rounded indusia that are characteristic of the genus. These delicate fern structures are adapted to shaded, moist environments where their slender form allows for efficient light capture.
