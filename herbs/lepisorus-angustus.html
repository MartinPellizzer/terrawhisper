Lepisorus angustus

What's the taxonomical classification of Lepisorus angustus?

Lepisorus angustus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Polypodiinae and is further categorized within the order Polypodiales. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the family Polypodiaceae, which contains the genus Lepisorus, eventually leading to its specific identification as the species angustus.

    Lepisorus angustus has anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antimicrobial properties that are primarily utilized to treat skin infections, wounds, and localized pain.     What are the herbal preparations of Lepisorus angustus?

    Lepisorus angustus is prepared primarily as a decoction or an infusion made from its dried leaves and rhizomes to extract its medicinal compounds. Practitioners often boil the plant material in water for an extended period to ensure a potent concentration of active constituents. Once the liquid has cooled, it is frequently consumed as a tea or used as a topical wash for skin ailments. Some traditional methods also involve grinding the dried plant into a fine powder to be encapsulated or mixed into ointments. These various forms allow for both internal and external applications depending on the specific health requirement.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Lepisorus angustus can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and localized swelling if the plant's sap comes into direct contact with human tissue. The specific chemical compounds within the plant may trigger redness or itching in sensitive individuals. If accidental ingestion occurs, it could lead to gastrointestinal distress or more severe internal toxicity. While research on its exact pharmacological profile is limited, caution is advised when handling its leaves or stems. Monitoring for any immediate inflammatory responses is necessary after any physical contact.
