Chinese knotweed

Polygonum multiflorum

Chinese Knotweed (Polygonum multiflorum) is a perennial herb native to East Asia, known for its dark purple roots that are widely used in traditional Chinese medicine to support kidney health, enhance vitality, and promote hair growth.

Botanical Identification

Chinese Knotweed, scientifically known as Polygonum multiflorum, belongs to the family Polygonaceae. It is also called He Shou Wu in Chinese and is a perennial herb native to East Asia. The plant has thick rhizomes, heart-shaped leaves, and clusters of small red flowers. It is sometimes referred to by other names in traditional herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Spermatophyta
  • Class: caryophyllopsida
  • Order: Polygonales
  • Family: Polygonaceae
  • Genus: Polygonum
  • Species: Polygonum multiflorum

Active Compounds

Chinese Knotweed contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to exert anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in traditional herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Tannin
  • Phenolic acid

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and skin conditions.

Powder: Used for constipation and to promote hair growth.

Infusion: Used for respiratory issues and to improve circulation.

Safety Profile

Chinese Knotweed, or Polygonum multiflorum, is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. Long-term use or high doses can lead to toxicity, so it should be used with caution and under professional guidance.
