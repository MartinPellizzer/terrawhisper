Crataegus kansuensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Crataegus kansuensis has small, simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical in shape with serrated margins and a pubescent underside. The plant features woody stems that develop into a spreading shrub or small tree structure. Its inflorescences consist of small, white flowers that emerge from axillary clusters during the blooming season. These flowers eventually develop into small, fleshy pomes that serve as the plant's fruit. The overall growth habit is characterized by dense branching and a compact form.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the state of Kansas, specifically concentrated within the limestone hills and rocky outcrops of the Flint Hills region. Its population is highly localized, often restricted to specific microhabitats that provide necessary rocky substrates. Researchers have noted that it typically thrives in prairie environments where competition from denser vegetation is limited. Because its range is so geographically confined, it is considered a rare species with a very narrow distribution.
