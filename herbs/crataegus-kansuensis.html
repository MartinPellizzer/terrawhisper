Crataegus kansuensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Crataegus kansuensis?

Crataegus kansuensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its hierarchical lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Rosales. Furthermore, this plant is a member of the family Rosaeaceae, falling under the genus Crataegus, and is specifically identified by its species name, kansuenis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Crataegus kansuensis has small, simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical in shape with serrated margins and a pubescent underside.

The plant features woody stems that develop into a spreading shrub or small tree structure. Its inflorescences consist of small, white flowers that emerge from axillary clusters during the blooming season. These flowers eventually develop into small, fleshy pomes that serve as the plant's fruit.

The overall growth habit is characterized by dense branching and a compact form.
