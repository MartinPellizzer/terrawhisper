Meconopsis speciosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Meconopsis speciosa?

Meconopsis speciosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Ranunculales and is a member of the family Papaveraceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by the genus Meconopsis and the species name speciosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Meconopsis speciosa has large, basal, orbicular leaves that are deeply lobed, leathery in texture, and covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs. These prominent leaves grow in a rosette pattern near the ground to capture maximum sunlight. From this central base, tall, upright flowering stems emerge to reach significant heights. The plant produces showy, bright blue or violet flowers that feature numerous long, delicate stamens. Its woody stems are often covered in a similar pubescence as the foliage, providing protection against its high-altitude environment.
