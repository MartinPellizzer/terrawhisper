Woad (Isatis tinctoria) Compounds

The primary compounds of woad include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and glycosides, which contribute to its medicinal effects. Tannins and phenolic acids provide astringent and anti-inflammatory actions. Coumarin and alkaloids may have antispasmodic and antimicrobial properties. Saponins, anthocyanins, terpenoids, and essential oils further support its use in herbal medicine.

Woad (Isatis tinctoria) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a class of compounds found in Woad (Isatis tinctoria). They contribute to the plant's medicinal properties by acting as antioxidants and anti-inflammatory agents. These compounds support immune function and may help in treating skin conditions and respiratory ailments associated with the plant's traditional use.

2. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Woad (Isatis tinctoria). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in supporting immune function and respiratory health. Glycosides in Woad are known for their antiviral and anti-inflammatory properties. They are often used in herbal preparations to address conditions like colds and infections.

3. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Woad (Isatis tinctoria). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health. Tannins also help preserve the plant's medicinal qualities by binding to proteins and other compounds. This compound plays a role in traditional uses of Woad for inflammation and skin conditions.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in Woad (Isatis tinctoria). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. This compound supports the traditional use of Woad in herbal medicine for treating skin conditions and infections. Its presence enhances the plant's therapeutic value in herbal formulations.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Woad (Isatis tinctoria). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine, particularly for its potential anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. Coumarin may also support immune function and has been studied for its possible role in respiratory and skin conditions.

6. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Woad (Isatis tinctoria). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly in supporting immune function. Alkaloids in Woad may help reduce inflammation and support respiratory health. They are often used in herbal formulations for their therapeutic effects.

7. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Woad (Isatis tinctoria). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by acting as an antioxidant and anti-inflammatory agent. Anthocyanin supports immune function and may aid in the treatment of skin conditions and respiratory ailments associated with this plant.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Woad (Isatis tinctoria). It contributes to the plant's bitter taste and may have mild anti-inflammatory properties. Saponins can also aid in the emulsification of fats, supporting digestive health. These compounds are part of the broader phytochemical profile of Woad used in traditional herbal medicine.

9. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Woad (Isatis tinctoria) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds, including flavonoids and alkaloids, exhibit anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. They support the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and skin conditions. Terpenoids are key to the therapeutic potential of Woad in herbal medicine.

10. Essential oil

The essential oil of Woad (Isatis tinctoria) contains volatile compounds like limonene and pinene, contributing to its aromatic profile. It is used in herbal medicine for its potential antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting respiratory and skin health. The oil is often extracted through steam distillation from the plant's leaves and flowers.
