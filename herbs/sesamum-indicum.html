Sesame (Sesamum indicum)

Sesame (Sesamum indicum) is a member of the Pedaliaceae family, native to Southern Asia, Eastern Africa, and North Africa. Traditionally, its seeds, leaves, and bark have been used for decoctions, oil infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, tonic, and diuretic actions, and has a long history of use in ayurvedic medicine, african traditional medicine, and european herbal medicine.

Simple leaves with upper surface pale green and lower surface glossy green, featuring distinct stomatal bands.
Flowers
Flowers are actinomorphic, bisexual, with five petals arranged in a spiral, and have a tubular corolla with two lobes and a prominent stamen cluster.
Stems
Cylindrical, erect growth habit with opposite branching, smooth surface, and nodes bearing sessile, leaf-like stipules.
