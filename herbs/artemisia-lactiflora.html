Artemisia lactiflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Artemisia lactiflora?

Artemisia lactiflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae and is specifically categorized within the genus Artemisia, eventually reaching its unique identification at the species level as lactiflora.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Artemisia lactiflora contains a diverse array of bioactive compounds including flavonoids, terpenoids, coumarins, and essential oils. The plant is particularly rich in artemetin and eupatilin, which contribute to its significant anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These chemical constituents work together to modulate immune responses and protect cells from oxidative stress. Additionally, specific phenolic acids found within the leaves help regulate metabolic processes and reduce systemic inflammation. Research suggests these medicinal components make the plant a valuable subject for treating various inflammatory conditions.

What are the therapeutic actions of Artemisia lactiflora?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various gastrointestinal disorders, including dysentery, diarrhea, and abdominal pain. It is frequently utilized in traditional medicine to address digestive issues caused by parasites or inflammation of the intestinal tract. Beyond the digestive system, it is sometimes applied to help regulate menstrual cycles and alleviate symptoms of dysmenorrhea. Some practitioners also use it to manage symptoms associated with liver ailments and jaundice. The medicinal properties are often attributed to the plant's ability to clear heat and resolve dampness within the body.
