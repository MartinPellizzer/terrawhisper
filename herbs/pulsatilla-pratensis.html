Pasque Flower (Pulsatilla pratensis)

Pasque Flower (Pulsatilla pratensis) is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, native to Europe, and Asia. Traditionally, its roots, rhizomes, and shoots have been used for infusions, decoctions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its sedative, bitter, and anti-inflammatory actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, traditional chinese medicine, and mediterranean herbal traditions.
