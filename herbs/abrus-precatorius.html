Abrus precatorius

What's the taxonomical classification of Abrus precatorius?

Abrus precatorius belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Fabales and is a member of the family Fabaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its genus, Abrus, and its specific species designation, precatorius.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Abrus precatorius contains the highly toxic toxalbumin known as abrin, alongside various alkaloids, flavonoids, and glycosides that serve as the basis for its medicinal potential. While abrin is a potent ribosome-inactivating protein, controlled extracts are studied for their ability to induce cell death in cancer cells. The plant also features saponins and tannins that exhibit significant antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties in traditional applications. Certain studies suggest that its bioactive compounds may assist in treating skin diseases and managing certain types of inflammation. However, the extreme toxicity of its primary protein requires precise extraction and dosage to prevent fatal poisoning.

What are the therapeutic actions of Abrus precatorius?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin diseases such as ringworm, eczema, and various fungal infections through its topical applications. The seeds are often processed into a paste to manage inflammatory skin conditions and wounds. In some traditional practices, it is also utilized to alleviate symptoms of rheumatism and joint pain. Careful preparation is required because the seeds contain highly toxic proteins that can be fatal if ingested. Medicinal use is strictly limited to external applications to avoid severe systemic poisoning.
