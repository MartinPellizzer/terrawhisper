Tecoma stans

What's the taxonomical classification of Tecoma stans?

Tecoma stans belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Lamiales, it is further categorized under the family Bignoniaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific genus, Tecoma, and its unique species designation, tans.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tecoma stans has woody, perennial stems that reach heights of up to 6 meters and bear pinnately compound leaves consisting of 5 to 9 leaflets.

The leaflets are ovate or lanceolate in shape with serrated margins and a bright green color. Its inflorescence features clusters of bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that hang from the branch tips. These flowers possess long, tubular corollas that serve to attract various pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.

The plant also produces small, woody, three-valved capsules that contain numerous flat, winged seeds for wind dispersal.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Tecoma stans contains medicinal properties primarily concentrated in its leaves, bark, and flowers. The leaves are frequently prepared as a decoction or infusion to treat inflammatory conditions and skin ailments. Extracts from the bark are often utilized for their antibacterial and antifungal characteristics in traditional remedies. Additionally, the bright yellow flowers can be used in various herbal preparations to soothe digestive issues. These different plant parts work together to provide a wide range of therapeutic applications in folk medicine.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Additionally, terpenoids contribute to the plant's defense mechanisms and offer anti-inflammatory benefits.

Tannins further enhance its medicinal profile through their astringent and wound-healing capabilities.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Tecoma stans targets cyclooxygenase enzymes, various proinflammatory cytokines, and specific neuroreceptors to exert its medicinal effects. The plant contains bioactive compounds like iridoids and flavonoids that inhibit the COX-2 enzyme, effectively reducing inflammation and pain. These phytochemicals also modulate the expression of tumor necrosis factor-alpha and interleukin-6 to suppress systemic immune responses. Additionally, certain alkaloids in the species interact with GABAergic pathways in the central nervous system to provide sedative or anticonvulsant properties. Through these diverse protein interactions, the plant achieves its documented antidiabetic and antimicrobial activities.

    What are the herbal preparations of Tecoma stans?

    Tecoma stans is prepared as a decoction of the leaves, a tea made from the flowers, or a topical poultice using crushed plant material. These liquid preparations are most commonly used to treat various inflammatory conditions or to alleviate respiratory discomfort. When the leaves are simmered in water, the resulting infusion serves as a traditional remedy for digestive issues. The flowers can also be steeped to create a mild tonic meant to soothe the system. Additionally, applying the mashed plant parts directly to the skin allows for the localized treatment of swellings or minor wounds.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Tecoma stanst can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and potential gastrointestinal distress if any part of the plant is ingested. Contact with the plant's sap or leaves often leads to localized redness, itching, or a burning sensation on the skin. Individuals with sensitive complexions may experience more severe inflammatory responses or even swelling upon touch. If the plant is swallowed, it can trigger nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain due to its chemical composition. It is important to handle this species with gloves to prevent these adverse reactions.
