1. Causes allergic reactions

Bryonia dioica causes allergic reactions in some individuals due to the presence of specific allergens such as pollen and latex-like compounds.

These allergens can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms like hives, itching, and swelling.

The plant's sap or juice may also cause contact dermatitis, a type of skin irritation characterized by redness, blisters, and inflammation.

2. Triggers respiratory failure

Bryonia dioica triggers respiratory failure due to its potential to cause a severe increase in mucus production and constriction of airways.

This can lead to difficulty breathing, wheezing, and coughing, which may be exacerbated by the herb's natural sedative properties that slow down lung function.

As a result, individuals using Bryonia dioica may experience respiratory distress, potentially life-threatening in severe cases.

3. Induces nausea and vomiting

Bryonia dioica induces nausea and vomiting due to its stimulating properties on the digestive system.

The herb's bitter compounds can irritate the stomach lining, triggering a gag reflex and leading to episodes of vomiting.

Additionally, Bryonia dioica's natural constituents may cause an imbalance in the body's electrolyte levels, further exacerbating gastrointestinal discomfort and resulting in nausea.

4. Develops kidney stones

Bryonia dioica develops kidney stones when it interacts with the body's natural balance of water and electrolytes.

As a diuretic herb, Bryonia dioica increases urine production, which can lead to an imbalance of minerals in the kidneys, causing them to harden and form stones.

Additionally, the herb's antispasmodic properties may reduce muscle tone in the ureters, potentially blocking the passage of small stones and leading to their accumulation in the kidneys.

5. Leads to skin rashes

Bryonia dioica leads to skin rashes due to its saponin content, which can cause an allergic reaction in some individuals.

The saponins found in the plant's leaves and stems may stimulate the release of histamine, leading to itching, redness, and inflammation on the skin's surface.

This adverse effect is thought to occur when the body's immune system overreacts to the plant's compounds, resulting in an inflammatory response that manifests as a rash or irritation.

6. Causes stomach pain

Bryonia dioica causes stomach pain due to its bitter and astringent properties, which can irritate the digestive system and lead to discomfort.

The plant's active compounds, including sesquiterpenes and phenolic acids, may stimulate the release of digestive enzymes, causing inflammation and pain in the stomach and intestines.

This reaction is often seen when taking high doses or using the plant for extended periods.

7. Triggers digestive problems

Bryonia dioica triggers digestive problems due to its ability to slow down digestion and reduce stomach contractions.

This can cause nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain in some individuals. The plant's active compounds, including saponins and sesquiterpenes, are thought to be responsible for this effect by altering the normal functioning of the gastrointestinal tract.

As a result, people may experience discomfort, bloating, or changes in bowel movements when using Bryonia dioica.

8. Produces headache and dizziness

Bryonia dioica produces headache and dizziness.

This is due to its ability to stimulate the nervous system, causing an increase in blood pressure and heart rate, which can lead to feelings of dizziness.

Additionally, Bryonia's bitter compounds may cause gastrointestinal irritation, leading to headaches and other symptoms.

9. Causes eye irritation

Bryonia dioica causes eye irritation due to its saponins and alkaloids, which can stimulate the mucous membranes in the eyes.

These compounds can cause a reaction that leads to redness, itchiness, and watery discharge, making it uncomfortable for individuals who use this plant-based remedy.

The irritant properties of Bryonia dioica's active compounds can also lead to temporary vision disturbances, adding to the overall discomfort experienced during eye irritation.

10. Increases blood pressure

Bryonia dioica increases blood pressure.

This is due to its ability to stimulate the sympathetic nervous system, leading to increased heart rate and blood vessel constriction. The plant's bioactive compounds, such as alkaloids and glycosides, are thought to contribute to this effect by acting as vasoconstrictors and increasing peripheral resistance.

As a result, individuals with pre-existing hypertension may experience exacerbation of their condition when using Bryonia dioica.
