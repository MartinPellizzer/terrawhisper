Nardostachys jatamansi

What's the taxonomical classification of Nardostachys jatamansi?

Nardostachys jatamansi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant is further organized under the order Dipsacales and falls within the family Caprifoliaceae. Finally, its specific identity is defined by the genus Nardostachys and the species jatamansi.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Nardostachys jatamansi has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by large, ovate to lanceolate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length and possess a distinctively bright yellow color. The plant typically grows in clumps with thick, fleshy rhizomes that serve as its primary underground storage organ. Its foliage is soft and somewhat succulent, often featuring prominent venation across the leaf surface. During its flowering stage, the plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. The overall structure of the plant is robust and lush, making it easily identifiable in its natural tropical habitats.

What are the pharmacological activities of Nardostachys jatamansi?

This plant has nootropic, acetylcholinesterase inhibitory, anti-Alzheimer's, anti-depressant, and anti-epileptic pharmacological activities. Its ability to inhibit the acetylcholinesterase enzyme helps maintain higher levels of acetylcholine in the brain. This specific mechanism provides significant anti-Alzheimer's benefits by supporting cognitive functions and memory retention. Furthermore, the plant exhibits neuroprotective properties that contribute to its status as a potent nootropic agent. These chemical interactions also support its therapeutic use in treating depression and controlling seizure activity through anti-depressant and anti-epileptic effects.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several bioactive sesquiterpene compounds including jatamansinol, jatamansinone, jatamansone, nardosinone, and nardostachysin. These specific medicinal constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's various pharmacological activities. Researchers often study these molecules to understand their potential effects on the central nervous system. The structural diversity of these compounds provides a basis for exploring new therapeutic applications in medicine. Each chemical component plays a distinct role in the complex chemical profile of the species.

According to a study published by "Current cancer drug targets", the Nardostachys jatamansi rhizome extract (NJRE) contains marker compounds including oroselol, jatamansinol, nardostachysin, jatamansinone and nardosinone. These compounds revealed synergistic and multi-targeted interactions with VEGFR2, CDK2, BCL2 and EGFR through in silico docking. The NJRE exhibited an IC50 of 33.73±3.5, 30.59±3.4 and 28.39±2.9 μg/mL on U87 MG cells after 24, 48 and 72 h. At 30 μg/mL, the extract induced DNA fragmentation, while early apoptosis began at 20 μg/mL. Furthermore, NJRE at 60 and 80 μg/mL caused a G0/G1 arrest, and doses of 20 and 40 μg/mL showed excessive nucleation and mitotic catastrophe.

According to a study published by "Phytotherapy research : PTR", Nardostachys jatamansi is used in Indian systems of medicine and was tested for its in vitro acetylcholinesterase inhibitory activity. This activity is relevant to the pharmacological therapy of Alzheimer's disease, which aims to reduce cognitive decline. The hydroalcohol extract of Nardostachys jatamansi inhibited 50% of acetylcholinesterase activity at concentrations of 100-150 microg/mL. These results were obtained using Ellman's method in 96-well microplates. The study used AChE obtained from bovine erythrocytes to determine these inhibitory effects.
