Rotheca incisa

What's the taxonomical classification of Rotheca incisa?

Rotheca incisa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this classification, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is organized under the order Lamiales and falls within the family Lamiaceae. Finally, it is classified under the genus Rotheca, with its specific designation being the species inciisa.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Rotheca incisa contains a diverse profile of secondary metabolites including flavonoids, tannins, alkaloids, and saponins that contribute to its therapeutic properties. These chemical constituents work synergistically to provide significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities in biological systems. The presence of specific phenolic compounds allows the plant to neutralize free radicals and mitigate oxidative stress. What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, inflammatory conditions, stomach aches, and digestive issues. Its bioactive compounds possess antimicrobial properties that help combat bacterial and fungal pathogens on the surface of the skin. When ingested in traditional remedies, it acts as an anti-inflammatory agent to soothe intestinal discomfort. The plant's chemical constituents are also studied for their potential to reduce swelling and pain in various parts of the body. Practitioners often prepare extracts or poultices to target these specific ailments.
