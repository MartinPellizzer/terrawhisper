Round-Leaf Bugleweed (Ajuga × Rotundifolia)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Ajuga × rotundifolia.

Round-leaf bugleweed, scientifically known as Ajuga × rotundifolia, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia, prized for its medicinal and adaptogenic properties. It is traditionally used to support digestive health, reduce inflammation, and alleviate mild respiratory symptoms due to its high concentration of iridoid glycosides, which have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. In traditional herbal medicine, it has been employed to treat ailments such as indigestion, coughs, and skin irritations, often prepared as a tea or tincture. Modern wellness applications include its use in herbal formulations for stress relief and immune support, reflecting its adaptogenic qualities that help the body resist stressors. This plant is also noted for its unique, slightly minty and earthy flavor, making it a rare and valuable ingredient in both traditional and contemporary herbal practices.
