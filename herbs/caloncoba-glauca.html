Caloncoba glauca

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Caloncoba glauca has succulent, cylindrical to slightly compressed stems that are covered in a waxy, glaucous coating and bear small, fleshy leaves. The leaves are typically reduced in size and often appear scale-like or deciduous along the stem surface. These stems are often branched and can exhibit a bluish-grey or pale green coloration due to the epicuticular wax. The plant lacks large, prominent foliage, focusing its energy on water storage within its thickened tissues. This specialized morphology allows the species to thrive in arid environments by minimizing moisture loss.
