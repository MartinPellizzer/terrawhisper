10 Medicinal Constituents Of Vaccinium Vitis-Idaea (Cranberry)

Vaccinium vitis-idaea has active constituents such as anthocyanins, flavonoids, and proanthocyanidins.

These compounds possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, contributing to its medicinal properties, including reducing urinary tract infections and improving cardiovascular health. By preventing bacterial adhesion to bladder walls, proanthocyanidins can help alleviate UTI symptoms. The antioxidant properties can also help lower cholesterol levels and improve cognitive function.

Overall, the constituents of cranberry can significantly enhance the quality of life for individuals suffering from these conditions.

Vaccinium vitis-idaea anthocyanins is a class of powerful flavonoid pigments responsible for its deep red to purple color.

3. Quercetin

Vaccinium vitis-idaea quercetin is a type of flavonoid compound that has been isolated from its fruit.
