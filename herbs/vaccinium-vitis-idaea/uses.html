Cowberry (Vaccinium vitis-idaea) Uses

Cowberry is used to treat respiratory infections, cold symptoms, coughs, and bronchitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps soothe mucosal irritation and may support wound healing. It is also applied for skin infections, irritations, and inflammation, owing to its soothing and healing effects on the skin.

Cowberry (Vaccinium vitis-idaea) Monograph

1. Respiratory infections

Cowberry has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in treating coughs and colds. Its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties help reduce mucus production and soothe irritated airways. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to alleviate symptoms of respiratory infections.

2. Cold symptoms

Cowberry is traditionally used to alleviate cold symptoms due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It may help reduce fever, soothe sore throats, and ease congestion. Its high vitamin C content supports immune function, making it a valued remedy in herbal medicine for colds and respiratory infections.

3. Cough

Cowberry is used to soothe coughs due to its expectorant properties, helping to loosen mucus and ease respiratory congestion. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to reduce irritation and promote clear breathing. Its anti-inflammatory effects support healing in the respiratory tract. Traditionally, it has been valued for its gentle yet effective action on the throat and lungs.

4. Bronchitis

Cowberry has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in cases of bronchitis. Its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties may help reduce bronchial irritation and ease coughing. It is often prepared as a tea to soothe the throat and promote expectoration. Regular use may aid in clearing mucus and supporting lung function during respiratory infections.

5. Colds

Cowberry is traditionally used to support the immune system during colds due to its high antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. It may help reduce symptoms like sore throat and congestion. The berries are often consumed as a tea or tincture to promote respiratory health. Regular use can aid in shortening the duration of colds.

6. Mucosal irritation

Cowberry has been used traditionally to soothe mucosal irritation due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It helps reduce inflammation and excessive mucus production in the respiratory and digestive tracts. Its tannins provide a protective effect on irritated mucous membranes. It is often used in herbal formulations for conditions like sore throat and gastrointestinal discomfort.

7. Skin infections

Cowberry has been traditionally used to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial properties. The berries contain compounds that help reduce bacterial growth and inflammation. It is often applied topically as a poultice or infused oil. Its use supports wound healing and prevents infection in minor skin conditions.

8. Wound healing

Cowberry has been traditionally used to support wound healing due to its high antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce infection risk and promotes tissue repair. The berries contain compounds that enhance cellular regeneration and improve skin integrity. Its astringent qualities also aid in stopping minor bleeding and cleansing wounds.

9. Skin irritations

Cowberry has been traditionally used to soothe skin irritations due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. Its high tannin content helps reduce redness and irritation, while its mild antiseptic qualities promote healing. It can be applied topically as a poultice or infused oil to treat minor burns, rashes, and insect bites.

10. Skin inflammation

Cowberry has been traditionally used to treat skin inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and irritation, making it useful for conditions like eczema and minor skin infections. The plant's high tannin content supports healing and soothes inflamed tissues.
