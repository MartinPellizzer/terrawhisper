Ajuga lobata

What's the taxonomical classification of Ajuga lobata?

Ajuga lobata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant follows the biological lineage of the order Lamiales. It is further categorized under the family Lamiaceae, a group known for its diverse mint family members. Within this family, the plant is situated in the genus Ajuga, and its specific biological identity is defined by the species name lobata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ajuga lobata has decumbent to erect stems that reach heights of up to 30 centimeters and feature opposite, square-shaped profiles characteristic of the Lamiaceae family. The leaves are primarily basal, forming a rosette of ovate to lanceolate shapes with serrated margins and slightly hairy surfaces. Toward the stem, the leaves become smaller and more spaced out along the nodes. The plant produces terminal spikes of tubular, bilabiate flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. These floral structures emerge from the upper nodes to create a dense, colorful display during the blooming season.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern China, primarily found within the provinces of Hubei, Hunan, Jiangxi, and Zhejiang. It typically thrives in temperate climates characterized by high humidity and moderate rainfall. The species is most frequently located in shaded forest environments or along the edges of wooded valleys. These specific habitats provide the moist, well-drained soil necessary for its continued growth. Due to its specialized ecological requirements, its range remains relatively localized within these specific East Asian highland areas.
