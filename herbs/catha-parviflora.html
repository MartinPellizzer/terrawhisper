Khat (Catha Parviflora)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Catha parviflora.

Khat, scientifically known as Catha parviflora, is a flowering plant native to the horn of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula, traditionally used as a stimulant and medicinal herb. It contains unique alkaloids such as cathinone and cathine, which are believed to produce mild stimulant effects and are responsible for its energizing properties. In traditional and cultural practices, Khat has been used for centuries to enhance alertness, reduce fatigue, and aid in social gatherings, particularly in regions like Somalia, Ethiopia, and Yemen. Modern wellness applications include its use in herbal remedies for improving concentration, managing stress, and supporting weight loss, though its use remains controversial due to potential for dependence. Its distinctive bitter and citrusy flavor, along with its historical significance in ancient trade routes, makes it a culturally rich and pharmacologically intriguing plant.
