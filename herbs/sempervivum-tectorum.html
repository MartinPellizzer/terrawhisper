As an ornamental plant, houseleek is prized for its striking rosettes of fleshy leaves that thrive in dry, rocky conditions. From a botanical standpoint, houseleek belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its unique ability to store water in its leaves, allowing it to survive for long periods without rain.

With ancient roots dating back to medieval Europe, where it was believed to have healing powers and was used to treat wounds, houseleek has a rich history that continues to captivate herbalists and horticulturists alike.
