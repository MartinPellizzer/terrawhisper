Streptopus obtusatus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Streptopus obtusatus has an herbaceous perennial structure characterized by slender, arching stems that emerge from a rhizomatous rootstock and bear opposite, lanceolate leaves with obtuse tips. Each stem typically produces a single, drooping raceme of bell-shaped, white flowers that hang from a curved pedicel. The leaves are arranged in pairs along the stem and feature parallel venation typical of monocots. Small, fleshy berries develop from the flowers after pollination, turning a dark color as they ripen. These botanical features collectively define its appearance within woodland habitats.
