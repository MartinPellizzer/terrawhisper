Viburnum schensianum

What's the taxonomical classification of Viburnum schensianum?

Viburnum schensianum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Dipsacales. This plant is a member of the family Viburnaceae and is specifically categorized within the genus Viburnum, ultimately reaching its scientific designation as the species schensianum.

What are the herbal preparations of Viburnum schensianum?

Viburnum schensianum is prepared as a decoction of the bark, a tincture made from the roots, or a topical poultice using crushed leaves. These traditional methods extract the active chemical constituents for various therapeutic uses. Decoctions are typically simmered for extended periods to pull compounds from the woody tissues into a liquid. Tinctures offer a concentrated shelf-stable form that can be easily measured in small doses. The leaf poultice serves as a direct application for soothing skin irritations or localized discomfort.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Viburnum schensianum can cause gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, and potential toxicity if the berries or leaves are ingested by humans or animals. While the plant is primarily studied for its ecological role, consuming its fruit may lead to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. Direct contact with the foliage or stems can trigger contact dermatitis in individuals with sensitive skin. There is limited clinical research regarding the long-term systemic effects of this specific species on human health. Therefore, cautious handling and avoidance of ingestion are recommended to prevent adverse reactions.
