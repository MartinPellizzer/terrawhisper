Viburnum schensianum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viburnum schensianum has opposite, simple leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape with serrated margins and acute tips. The leaf surfaces often exhibit a slightly rugose texture and can vary in color from bright green to darker shades depending on light exposure. Its stems are woody and can develop a somewhat spreading or upright growth habit as the plant matures. Small, white, five-petaled flowers emerge in terminal cymes during the flowering season. These blooms eventually give way to small, fleshy drupes that change color as they ripen.
