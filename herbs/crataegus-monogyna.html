Common Hawthorn (Crataegus monogyna)

Common Hawthorn (Crataegus monogyna) is a member of the Rosaceae family, native to Europe, Western Asia, and North Africa. Traditionally, its fruits, leaves, and bark have been used for infusions, decoctions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its tonic, sedative, and bitter actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and traditional chinese medicine.

Leaves
Simple leaves with acute tips, upper surface glabrous and dull green, lower surface paler and with distinct stomatal bands.
Flowers
Flowers are white to pale pink, arranged in clusters of 2 to 4, actinomorphic, with five petals and five sepals, and five stamens.
Stems
Woody, branched, with opposite or alternate phyllotaxis, bearing thorn-like projections, and having a smooth, glabrous surface.
