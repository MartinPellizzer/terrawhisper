Common Hawthorn

Crataegus monogyna

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Common Hawthorn, scientifically known as Crataegus monogyna, belongs to the Rosaceae family. It is also called Maybush or Thornapple in some regions. A deciduous shrub, it is native to Europe and western Asia. It has thorny branches, simple leaves, and white flowers that appear in spring. Historically, it has been referred to by various synonyms in herbal texts.

Active Compounds

Common Hawthorn contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, glycosides, and phenolic acids. These compounds work synergistically to support cardiovascular health by improving blood flow and reducing oxidative stress. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Glycoside
  • Phenolic acid

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action
Cardiovascular High blood pressure, heart failure, high cholesterol, hypertension, arrhythmia Antioxidant, cardiotonic, cholesterol-lowering, vasodilator
Nervous Insomnia, anxiety, depression, stress-related disorders Sedative, anxiolytic, mood stabilizing, antistress
Digestive Gastroesophageal reflux disease, constipation, gastrointestinal spasms Antispasmodic, laxative, spasmolytic

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for cardiovascular support and mild digestive issues.

Decoction: Used for heart conditions and circulatory disorders.

Extract: Used for cardiovascular health and as a general tonic.

Safety Profile

Common Hawthorn is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should not be used by individuals with severe hypotension or those taking medications for heart conditions. Consult a healthcare provider before use, especially during pregnancy or breastfeeding.
