Crataegus Monogyna

Crataegus monogyna, commonly known as one-seed hawthorn, is a small deciduous shrub or tree with medicinal properties.

This herb is used to promote cardiovascular health, lower blood pressure, and improve circulation, making it a popular remedy for conditions such as hypertension, angina, and heart failure.

The health benefits of Crataegus monogyna are attributed to its therapeutic actions, including cardiotonic, vasodilatory, and anti-inflammatory effects, which are primarily due to its bioactive constituents, including flavonoids, oligomeric proanthocyanidins, and triterpenic acids.

Regular consumption of Crataegus monogyna has been shown to reduce symptoms of cardiovascular disease and improve overall well-being.

According to "Arzneimittel-Forschung", Crataegus monogyna exhibits in vitro antioxidant activities, particularly due to its total phenolic proanthocyanidin and flavonoid contents.

Possible Side Effects of crataegus monogyna

Crataegus monogyna can have side effects if used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, due to its high concentration of flavonoids and tannins.

Additionally, consuming large amounts of this herb can lead to interactions with certain medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes medications, potentially causing adverse reactions.

Some individuals may also experience allergic reactions, such as skin rashes and itching, after taking Crataegus monogyna.

Furthermore, excessive consumption can cause liver damage and kidney problems, especially in people with pre-existing liver or kidney conditions.
