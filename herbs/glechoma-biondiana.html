Glechoma biondiana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Glechoma biondiana has opposite, decussate leaves that are typically ovate to cordate in shape with crenate or serrate margins and prominent venation. The plant exhibits a herbaceous growth habit characterized by slender, somewhat square-shaped stems. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often displaying a zygomorphic structure typical of the Lamiales order. The foliage often presents a slightly pubescent texture on both the upper and lower surfaces. These morphological features collectively distinguish it within its specific taxonomic group.
