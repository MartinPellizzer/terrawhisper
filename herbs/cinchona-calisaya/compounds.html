Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya) Compounds

The primary compounds of Calisaya Cinchona include alkaloids, which are known for their medicinal properties. Terpenoids contribute to the plant's aroma and biological activity. Flavonoids and anthocyanins provide antioxidant benefits, while tannins and saponins offer astringent and cleansing effects. These compounds collectively support the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya) Monograph

1. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a key compound found in Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal medicine. This compound is known for its pharmacological effects, including antimalarial and anti-inflammatory activities.

2. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a key class of active compounds in Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya). These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine, particularly for their antimalarial properties. They are derived from the plant's bark and have been historically significant in the treatment of malaria.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are key active compounds in Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya). They contribute to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. These compounds are often targeted in herbal formulations for their potential therapeutic benefits. Their presence supports the traditional use of this plant in herbal medicine.

4. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is used in traditional herbal medicine for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins help in treating digestive issues and skin conditions, supporting the plant's role in herbal therapy.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly in treating fever and malaria. 6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine, particularly for its antispasmodic and sedative properties. Coumarin is also noted for its potential anti-inflammatory and anticoagulant effects.

7. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and respiratory conditions. 8. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound found in Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. 9. Essential oil

Essential oil is a volatile compound found in Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya). It contributes to the plant's aromatic profile and may have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. 10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Calisaya Cinchona (Cinchona calisaya). It contributes to the plant's vibrant color and possesses antioxidant properties that may support health benefits associated with traditional herbal use.
