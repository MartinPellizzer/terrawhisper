Amygdalus mira

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Amygdalus mira has woody stems with a rough, fissured bark, lanceolate leaves with serrated margins, and solitary, pale pink flowers emerging from axillary buds. The foliage is typically evergreen or semi-deciduous depending on the specific environmental conditions of its habitat. Each leaf features a distinct midrib and fine venation that extends toward the tapered apex. During the flowering stage, the blossoms possess five delicate petals surrounding a central cluster of prominent stamens. The fruit develops into a small, drupaceous nut enclosed within a hard, woody endocarp.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the mountainous regions of central and western China, specifically within the provinces of Gansu and Sichuan. It thrives in high-altitude habitats characterized by rocky slopes and temperate climates. The species is typically found growing in scrublands or along the margins of coniferous forests. Due to its specific environmental requirements, its range is highly localized and fragmented. Localized populations are often restricted to specific limestone or granite formations within these provinces.

        What side effects this plant can have?

        This plant can cause severe cyanide poisoning, nausea, vomiting, dizziness, and respiratory distress if ingested in significant quantities. The primary danger stems from the presence of cyanogenic glycosides which release toxic hydrogen cyanide during digestion. Symptoms of toxicity often manifest rapidly and can escalate to seizures or loss of consciousness. Individuals with underlying respiratory issues or metabolic sensitivities may experience much more intense reactions to the toxins. Because of these significant risks, careful handling and strict dosage control are essential when dealing with any part of the specimen.
