Asplenium delavayi

What's the taxonomical classification of Asplenium delavayi?

Asplenium delavayi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. This fern is specifically situated within the family Aspleniaceae, falling under the genus Asplenium, and is identified by its specific species name, delaayi.

This plant is used to treat various ailments including skin infections, stomach pains, respiratory issues, and inflammatory conditions. The rhizomes and leaves are often prepared as medicinal decoctions to soothe digestive disturbances and gastrointestinal discomfort. Local healers frequently apply topical preparations derived from the plant to heal wounds and clear skin lesions. Some traditional practices also involve using the plant to alleviate symptoms of fever and coughs. This fern serves as a versatile remedy within specific regional folk medicine systems.

Asplenium delavayi is prepared primarily as a decoction, a concentrated liquid infusion made by boiling the plant parts in water to extract its medicinal compounds. In some regional practices, the plant may also be processed into a topical paste for external applications. These various preparations are utilized to address specific ailments according to traditional medicinal protocols. The choice of form depends heavily on whether the intended use is internal consumption or external skin treatment.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Asplenium delavayi can cause localized skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or digestive distress if ingested improperly. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, many fern species contain compounds that may trigger sensitive immune responses. Contact with the plant's fronds can lead to redness or itching in individuals with heightened sensitivities. If the plant is consumed, it might result in nausea or stomach discomfort due to its chemical composition. Users should exercise caution and avoid direct skin contact or ingestion to prevent these adverse reactions.
