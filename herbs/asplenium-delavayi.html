Asplenium delavayi

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Asplenium delavayi has large, bright green, pinnate fronds that can reach up to 100 centimeters in length, characterized by elongated, lanceolate pinnae with slightly wavy margins and acute tips. The texture of the foliage is typically thin and membranous, allowing for a delicate appearance despite its substantial size. These fronds emerge from a creeping, somewhat woody rhizome that often grows amidst moss and leaf litter in humid forest environments. The underside of each leaflet features small, circular sori arranged in neat rows along the midvein, which are essential for its reproductive process. The stipe of the plant is dark, often appearing brownish or blackish, providing a sharp color contrast to the vibrant green blades.
