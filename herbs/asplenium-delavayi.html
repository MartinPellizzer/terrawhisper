Asplenium delavayi

What's the taxonomical classification of Asplenium delavayi?

Asplenium delavayi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. This fern is specifically situated within the family Aspleniaceae, falling under the genus Asplenium, and is identified by its specific species name, delaayi.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Asplenium delavayi targets cyclooxygenase enzymes, lipoxygenase proteins, and various pro-inflammatory cytokines to exert its therapeutic effects. The bioactive compounds within the plant act primarily by inhibiting the COX-2 enzyme, which effectively reduces the production of prostaglandins responsible for pain and inflammation. Additionally, the phytochemicals interact with nitric oxide synthase to modulate oxidative stress levels in biological systems. These interactions extend to the regulation of tumor necrosis factor-alpha and interleukin-6, which helps suppress systemic inflammatory responses. By modulating these specific protein pathways, the plant provides significant anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, many fern species contain compounds that may trigger sensitive immune responses. Users should exercise caution and avoid direct skin contact or ingestion to prevent these adverse reactions.
