Lespedeza pilosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lespedeza pilosa has trifoliate leaves with elliptical leaflets that feature pubescent undersides and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in axillary racemes. The stems are typically upright and covered in fine hairs, providing a textured surface to the overall structure. Its leaves are arranged alternately along the branching stems to maximize sunlight absorption. The flowers are generally pale yellow or whitish and possess a characteristic pea-like structure typical of the Fabaceae family. Small, flat legume pods develop from the flowers and eventually split to release seeds.
