Emilia prenanthoidea

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Emilia prenanthoidea has a slender, erect stem that typically reaches heights of 20 to 60 centimeters and is characterized by its simple or sparingly branched structure. The leaves are primarily arranged in a basal rosette, featuring ovate to lanceolate shapes with finely serrated margins. Moving up the stem, the leaves become smaller and more sparsely distributed. The inflorescence consists of small, discoid flower heads that are clustered in terminal cymes. These heads display tubular florets that are often pale pink or lavender in color.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the high-altitude regions of the Mexican Plateau, specifically localized within the states of Michoacán and Estado de México. It typically inhabits volcanic mountain ranges where the soil is composed of basaltic or andesitic materials. These populations are often restricted to specific microclimates found in pine-oak forests at elevations exceeding 2,500 meters. Because of its narrow range and specialized habitat requirements, the species is considered geographically limited.
