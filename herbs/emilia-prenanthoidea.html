What's the taxonomical classification of Emilia prenanthoidea?

Emilia prenanthoidea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further into its systematic hierarchy, it falls under the order Asterales and is a member of the family Asteraceae. Finally, its taxonomic identity is completed by its placement within the genus Emilia, specifically as the species prenanthoidea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Emilia prenanthoidea has a slender, erect stem that typically reaches heights of 20 to 60 centimeters and is characterized by its simple or sparingly branched structure.

The leaves are primarily arranged in a basal rosette, featuring ovate to lanceolate shapes with finely serrated margins. Moving up the stem, the leaves become smaller and more sparsely distributed. The inflorescence consists of small, discoid flower heads that are clustered in terminal cymes.

These heads display tubular florets that are often pale pink or lavender in color.
