Indian Sesame (Sesamum orientale) Uses

Indian Sesame is used to treat respiratory infections, cough, bronchitis, asthma, and respiratory congestion due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It also aids in reducing skin inflammation, promoting wound healing, and managing skin infections and inflammatory conditions like itching.

Indian Sesame (Sesamum orientale) Monograph

1. Respiratory infections

Indian Sesame (Sesamum orientale) is used in traditional medicine to alleviate symptoms of respiratory infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce coughing, clear mucus, and soothe irritated airways, making it a valued remedy for conditions like bronchitis and common colds.

2. Cough

Indian Sesame (Sesamum orientale) is used in traditional herbal medicine to soothe coughs due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. The oil and seeds help loosen mucus and reduce irritation in the respiratory tract, offering relief for persistent or dry coughs. It is often prepared as a paste or oil extract for internal use.

3. Bronchitis

Indian Sesame (Sesamum orientale) is used in traditional herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms of bronchitis due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. The seeds and oil help reduce mucus buildup and soothe respiratory irritation, supporting clearer breathing and easing coughing. It is often prepared as a paste or oil infusion for internal or external application.

4. Asthma

Indian Sesame (Sesamum orientale) has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to alleviate asthma symptoms. Its anti-inflammatory and bronchodilatory properties help reduce airway inflammation and ease breathing. The seeds are often prepared as oils or pastes and consumed internally to support respiratory health. Regular use may help manage asthma attacks and improve lung function.

5. Respiratory congestion

Indian Sesame (Sesamum orientale) is used to alleviate respiratory congestion due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps loosen mucus and reduce irritation in the airways, making it beneficial for conditions like bronchitis and coughs. The oil and seeds are often prepared as inhalants or pastes to ease breathing and clear nasal passages.

6. Skin inflammation

Indian Sesame (Sesamum orientale) is used topically to treat skin inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The oil, rich in sesamol and antioxidants, helps reduce redness, swelling, and infection. It is often applied as a poultice or diluted oil to soothe irritated skin conditions.

7. Wound healing

Indian Sesame (Sesamum orientale) is used topically to promote wound healing due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The oil contains compounds that help reduce infection risk and accelerate tissue repair. It is often applied directly to wounds to support faster recovery and prevent scarring.

8. Skin infections

Indian Sesame (Sesamum orientale) is used topically to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The oil, rich in sesamin and sesamolin, helps reduce bacterial growth and soothe irritated skin. It is applied directly to affected areas to promote healing and prevent further infection.

9. Inflammatory skin conditions

Indian Sesame (Sesamum orientale) is used topically to treat inflammatory skin conditions due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The oil contains sesamin and sesamolin, which reduce redness and irritation. It soothes conditions like eczema and psoriasis by calming the skin's inflammatory response. Regular application can promote healing and prevent infection in affected areas.

10. Skin itching

Indian Sesame (Sesamum orientale) is used to relieve skin itching due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistaminic properties. The oil from its seeds helps soothe irritated skin and reduce redness. It is often applied topically to treat conditions like eczema and insect bites. Its natural soothing agents provide relief without harsh chemicals.
