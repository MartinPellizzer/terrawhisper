Botanical Identification of Indian Sesame (Sesamum orientale)

To identify Indian Sesame (Sesamum orientale) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

The botanical identification of Indian Sesame, Sesamum orientale, is classified under the Kingdom Plantae, Division Magnoliophyta, Class Liliopsida, Order Pedaliales, Family Pedaliaceae, Genus Sesamum, and Species Sesamum orientale. This classification reflects its position within the plant kingdom and its specific taxonomic group.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Sesamum orientale is the scientific name for Indian sesame. It was first described by the botanist Carl von Linné. Common names include sesame, gingelly, and til. It is also known by regional names such as til in Hindi and gingelly in Tamil. Historically, it has been referred to as "bhringaraja" in some ancient texts.

Morphological Characteristics

Sesamum orientale is an annual herb with a fibrous root system. Its stem is erect and branching, reaching up to 1.5 meters. Leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, with a hairy surface. The inflorescence is a loose panicle, bearing small, yellow flowers. The fruit is a capsule containing small, elongated seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Sesamum orientale can be distinguished by its narrow, elongated leaves with parallel veins, and its capsule-shaped fruit that splits into three valves. The plant has a distinct aromatic scent and produces small, pale yellow flowers. These features help differentiate it from other sesame species and similar plants in the same family.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Sesamum orientale is native to regions of India, particularly the southern and western states. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with warm temperatures and moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained loamy soils and is typically found at altitudes ranging from 200 to 800 meters above sea level.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Indian Sesame (Sesamum orientale) blooms from June to August, depending on the region. Seed formation occurs from September to November, with the seeds reaching maturity by late autumn. Harvest is typically done when the lower pods turn brown and begin to split, indicating full seed development.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Sesamum orientale is closely related to other species within the genus Sesamum. It shares key characteristics with its closest relatives, such as similar leaf structures and flower morphology. Its phylogenetic placement within the Pedaliaceae family highlights its evolutionary links to other members of this group.
