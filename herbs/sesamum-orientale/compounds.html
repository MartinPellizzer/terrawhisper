Indian Sesame (Sesamum orientale) Compounds

The primary compounds in Indian sesame include essential oil, which gives it aromatic properties, and phenolic acids, known for their antioxidant effects. Terpenoids and flavonoids contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities. Coumarin and tannins provide additional therapeutic benefits, while glycosides and saponins support its medicinal uses in traditional herbal medicine.

Indian Sesame (Sesamum orientale) Monograph

1. Essential oil

Essential oil from Indian Sesame (Sesamum orientale) is a key active compound, known for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It is extracted through steam distillation and contains compounds like sesamin and sesamolin, which contribute to its therapeutic effects in traditional herbal medicine.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid in Indian Sesame (Sesamum orientale) contributes to its antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports the plant's traditional use in promoting skin health and reducing inflammation. It is a key component in the therapeutic profile of this medicinal plant.

3. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Indian Sesame (Sesamum orientale). These compounds contribute to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and neuroprotective effects. They are commonly used in traditional herbal medicine to support overall health and treat various ailments.

4. Tannin

Tannin in Indian Sesame (Sesamum orientale) is a polyphenolic compound known for its astringent properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating wounds and inflammation. Tannins also exhibit antioxidant and antimicrobial effects, supporting its role in herbal medicine.

5. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Indian Sesame (Sesamum orientale) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting the plant's traditional use in treating ailments like inflammation and oxidative stress. They are found in the seeds and are believed to enhance the plant's therapeutic value in herbal medicine.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a bioactive compound found in Indian Sesame (Sesamum orientale). It contributes to the plant's therapeutic properties, including anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory effects. Coumarin is also valued for its role in supporting cardiovascular health and reducing oxidative stress in the body.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Indian Sesame (Sesamum orientale). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Glycosides are often responsible for the therapeutic actions observed in traditional herbal remedies using this plant.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a bioactive compound found in Indian Sesame (Sesamum orientale). It exhibits anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, contributing to the plant's traditional use in treating skin infections and digestive disorders. Saponin's ability to disrupt cell membranes enhances its therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a bioactive compound found in Indian Sesame (Sesamum orientale). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by interacting with biological systems to potentially alleviate inflammation and pain. Its presence highlights the plant's value in traditional herbal medicine for its therapeutic potential.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Indian Sesame (Sesamum orientale). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting cellular health and reducing oxidative stress. This compound is particularly valued in traditional herbal medicine for its potential therapeutic benefits.
