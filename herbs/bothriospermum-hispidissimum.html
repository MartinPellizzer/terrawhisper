Bothriospermum hispidissimum

What's the taxonomical classification of Bothriospermum hispidissimum?

Bothriospermum hispidissimum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Boraginales. This plant is a member of the family Boraginaceae, which contains the genus Bothriospermum, and is specifically identified by the species name hispidissimum.

    Bothriospermum hispidissimum is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice derived from its roots and leaves. When used as an infusion, the leaves are steeped in hot water to create a milder tea suitable for various ailments. For external applications, the crushed plant material is applied directly to the skin as a poultice to treat inflammation or skin irritations. These different methods allow practitioners to tailor the concentration and delivery of the plant's active constituents to specific therapeutic needs.

The plant contains various chemical compounds that may trigger contact dermatitis upon touching its foliage. Individuals with sensitive skin might experience redness, itching, or localized swelling after exposure. Ingestion of the plant is generally discouraged due to the risk of stomach upset or more severe toxic responses. It is important to handle this species with caution to avoid these adverse physical reactions.
