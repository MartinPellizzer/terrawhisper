Drynaria bonii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Drynaria bonii has woody climbing stems, pinnate leaves with multiple pairs of leaflets, and small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in axillary racemes. The leaves typically feature elliptic or oblong leaflets that are arranged oppositely along a central rachis. The plant's climbing habit is supported by specialized structures that allow it to ascend through surrounding vegetation. Its inflorescences consist of clusters of small, fragrant blossoms that attract specific pollinators. The fruit is a legume that matures into a pod containing several small seeds.
