What's the taxonomical classification of Semiliquidambar cathayensis?

Semiliquidambar cathayensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is positioned in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Saxifrages. This plant is a member of the family Altingiaceae, specifically nested within the genus Semiliquidambar, and is identified by the species name cathayensis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds oleanolic acid, ursolic acid, betulonic acid, arjunolic acid, and paeoiniflorin. These specific triterpenoids and glycosides are known for providing various therapeutic properties to the organism. Research suggests that components like oleanolic and ursolic acid may offer significant anti-inflammatory and antioxidant benefits. Additionally, the presence of betulonic and arjunolic acids contributes to the plant's complex chemical profile used in traditional medicine. Paeoniflorin further enhances the pharmacological potential of the plant through its unique bioactive effects.

As discussed in a study, an extensive phytochemical investigation on the rare medicinal plant Semiliquidambar cathayensis led to the isolation of 4 new pentacyclic triterpenoids named semiliquidacids A-D and 25 related known pentacyclic triterpenoids. Compound 1 represents the first naturally occurring ursane-type triterpenoid featuring an uncommon C-25 formyl group. Among the isolated compounds, compound 4 and oleanolic acid 13 exhibited remarkable inhibitory effects against ATP-citrate lyase. Compound 4 demonstrated an IC50 value of 6.5 μM. Oleanolic acid 13 showed an IC50 value of 11.9 μM.
