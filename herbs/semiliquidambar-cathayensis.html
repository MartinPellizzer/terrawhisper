Semiliquidambar cathayensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. According to a study published by "Molecules (Basel, Switzerland)", Semiliquidambar cathayensis Chang was a traditional medicinal plant used for centuries in China to treat rheumatism arthritis and rheumatic arthritis. While only 15 components were previously reported, this study identified or tentatively characterized a total of 85 chemical compositions in the roots. These compositions include 35 alkaloids, 12 flavonoids, 7 terpenoids, 5 phenylpropanoids, 9 fatty acids, 7 cyclic peptides, and 10 others. The identification of these substances was based on an accurate mass within 5 ppm error. Furthermore, the alkaloid, flavonoid, phenylpropanoid, and cyclic peptide classes were reported from Semiliquidambar cathayensis for the first time.
