Phyllanthus glaucus

What's the taxonomical classification of Phyllanthus glaucus?

Phyllanthus glaucus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is classified under the subclass Magnoliidae and falls within the order Malpighiales. The plant is a member of the family Phyllanthaceae, specifically situated within the genus Phyllanthus, and is identified by its specific species name, glaucus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phyllanthus glaucus has a woody, shrubby growth habit characterized by small, elliptical leaves that exhibit a distinct glaucous, blue-gray waxy coating on both surfaces. The leaves are arranged in a distichous pattern along the branches, giving the stems a feathery or pinnate appearance. Its stems are typically slender and can become somewhat woody at the base as the plant matures. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in the leaf axils, often appearing as tiny yellowish or greenish structures. The plant typically develops small, globose fruits that follow the flowering stage.
