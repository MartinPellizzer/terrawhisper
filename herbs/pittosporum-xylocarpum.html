Pittosporum xylocarpum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pittosporum xylocarpum has an evergreen shrub or small tree habit characterized by leathery, elliptic to oblong leaves that are dark green and possess entire margins. The foliage is typically densely arranged along woody branches that exhibit a spreading growth pattern. Small, fragrant white flowers emerge in clusters from the leaf axils, featuring five petals that create a star-like appearance. These blossoms eventually give rise to distinctive woody capsules that split open to reveal bright orange or red seeds. The overall structure is robust, allowing the plant to thrive in coastal or forest understory environments.
