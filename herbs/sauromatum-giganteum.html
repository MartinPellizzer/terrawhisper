Sauromatum giganteum

What's the taxonomical classification of Sauromatum giganteum?

Sauromatum giganteum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Alismatales. This plant is further categorized under the family Araceae, falling within the genus Sauromatum, and is specifically identified by its species name, giganteum.

    Sauromatum giganteum is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste using the various parts of the plant such as the leaves and roots. Some preparations involve soaking the plant material in alcohol to extract specific bioactive compounds. These diverse methods allow the plant's medicinal properties to be administered according to the specific health requirement.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Sauromatum giganteum can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and potential gastrointestinal distress if ingested due to its chemical composition. While specific clinical human studies are limited, many succulents in similar families contain secondary metabolites that act as irritants to sensitive tissues. Contact with the sap may result in redness, itching, or localized dermatitis in some individuals. If the plant is consumed accidentally, it might trigger nausea, vomiting, or abdominal discomfort. It is generally recommended to handle the plant with care and avoid any oral exposure.
