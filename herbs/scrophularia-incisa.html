What's the taxonomical classification of Scrophularia incisa?

Scrophularia incisa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized into the order Lamiales. This plant is a member of the family Scrophulariaceae and is specifically placed within the genus Scrophularia, with its unique designation being the species incisa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Scrophularia incisa has deeply incised, ovate-lanceolate leaves with serrated margins and small, two-lipped, yellowish-brown flowers arranged in terminal spikes.

The foliage is characterized by a distinctively notched or lobed appearance that distinguishes it from other species in the genus. Its stems are typically square in cross-section and can grow upright or sprawling depending on the environment. The floral structure features a prominent upper lip and a smaller lower lip, which are adapted for specific insect pollinators.

Each flowering spike consists of multiple small blossoms that emerge from the axils of the upper leaves.
