Viburnum macrocephalum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viburnum macrocephalum has an upright, deciduous shrub growth habit characterized by oppositely arranged, ovate leaves with serrated margins and prominent veins. The foliage typically presents a deep green color during the growing season, often transitioning to more muted tones as the plant matures. Its most distinctive feature is the large, terminal inflorescences consisting of dense, rounded cymes filled with numerous small, creamy white flowers. These massive flower heads are supported by sturdy peduncles that emerge from the terminal ends of the branches. Following pollination, the plant produces small, fleshy drupes that provide a significant food source for various local wildlife.
