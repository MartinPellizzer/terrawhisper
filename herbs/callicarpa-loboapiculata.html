What's the taxonomical classification of Callicarpa loboapiculata?

Callicarpa loboapiculata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Lamiales. Within this order, the plant is situated in the family Lamiaceae, which characterizes its botanical relationships. Finally, it is identified under the genus Callicarpa, specifically designated by its unique species name, loboapiculata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Callicarpa loboapiculata has oppositely arranged, ovate to elliptical leaves that feature distinctively lobed or apiculate tips and serrated margins.

The plant structure typically consists of woody stems that support these dense foliage clusters. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves in compact cymes. These floral structures eventually develop into small, globose drupes that change color as they mature.

The overall vegetative form is characterized by its shrubby growth habit and textured leaf surfaces.
