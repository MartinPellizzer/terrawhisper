Callicarpa loboapiculata

What's the taxonomical classification of Callicarpa loboapiculata?

Callicarpa loboapiculata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Lamiales. Within this order, the plant is situated in the family Lamiaceae, which characterizes its botanical relationships. Finally, it is identified under the genus Callicarpa, specifically designated by its unique species name, loboapiculata.

What are the therapeutic actions of Callicarpa loboapiculata?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, respiratory ailments, and digestive disorders. Traditional practitioners apply its extracts to manage wounds and various cutaneous lesions. It is also frequently decocted to alleviate symptoms of coughs and general breathing difficulties. Furthermore, the plant serves as a remedy for stomach-related discomfort and intestinal issues. These therapeutic applications are rooted in local ethnobotanical knowledge.
