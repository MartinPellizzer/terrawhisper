The plant's bioactive compounds may also contribute to its antimicrobial and antifungal properties, making it a valuable natural remedy for wound healing, skin infections, and fungal-related issues.

Possible Side Effects of Bryobium pudicum

Bryobium pudicum can have side effects if used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, nausea, and vomiting, particularly if ingested in large quantities.

Prolonged exposure to its extracts may also cause allergic reactions, such as skin irritation, itching, and respiratory problems.

In severe cases, bryobium pudicum can interact with certain medications, leading to complications like decreased blood pressure, dizziness, and headaches.

Improper use of the plant may also exacerbate pre-existing conditions, such as kidney or liver disease, requiring close medical supervision.
