Andean Ephedra (Ephedra Andina Var. Humilis)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Ephedra andina var. humilis.

Andean ephedra, scientifically known as Ephedra andina var. humilis, is a traditional medicinal herb native to the Andes Mountains, valued for its adaptogenic and stimulant properties. This plant contains unique alkaloids such as ephedrine and pseudoephedrine, which contribute to its ability to enhance energy, improve respiratory function, and support immune response. Historically, indigenous communities in the Andes have used Andean ephedra for centuries to treat cold symptoms, respiratory infections, and altitude sickness due to its bronchodilating effects. In modern wellness practices, it is often incorporated into herbal supplements and teas to boost stamina and mental clarity, though its use is regulated due to potential side effects. Its distinct earthy flavor and historical significance make it a rare and culturally significant plant in both traditional and contemporary herbal medicine.
