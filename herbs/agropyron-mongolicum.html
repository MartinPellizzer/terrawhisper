Agropyron mongolicum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Agropyron mongolicum has a perennial life cycle characterized by a densely tufted growth habit, erect culms that typically reach 30 to 80 centimeters in height, and narrow, linear leaves with scabrous margins. The plant features a fibrous root system that helps it stabilize in various soil types. Its inflorescence is a compact, contracted panicle that emerges from the terminal end of the stem. Each spikelet is composed of several fertile florets protected by hard, glume-like structures. The seeds are small and elliptical, designed for efficient dispersal within its natural grassland habitats.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Agropyron mongolicum can cause allergic skin reactions such as contact dermatitis, itching, and redness when the plant comes into direct contact with sensitive skin. Some individuals may also experience respiratory irritation if they inhale the pollen or fine dust produced by the plant during its growth cycle. Ingestion of the plant is generally not recommended due to a lack of clinical data regarding its long-term safety in humans. Potential digestive discomfort might occur if the plant is consumed in large quantities. Always consult a medical professional before any exposure or use of this species.
