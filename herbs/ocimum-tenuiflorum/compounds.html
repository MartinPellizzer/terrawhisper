Holy Basil (Ocimum tenuiflorum) Compounds

Holy Basil contains several primary compounds, including essential oils, flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids. These compounds contribute to its antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and adaptogenic properties. Terpenoids, saponins, and coumarin also play roles in its therapeutic effects. Glycosides, alkaloids, and anthocyanins further support its medicinal value.

Holy Basil (Ocimum tenuiflorum) Monograph

1. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound in Holy Basil (Ocimum tenuiflorum). It contains volatile aromatic compounds like eugenol and methyl eugenol, which contribute to its anti-inflammatory and adaptogenic properties. This oil is commonly extracted through steam distillation and used in traditional medicine for its calming and stress-reducing effects.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Holy Basil (Ocimum tenuiflorum). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for stress relief and immune support. These compounds help modulate cellular responses and may enhance the plant's therapeutic effects.

3. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Holy Basil (Ocimum tenuiflorum). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health. Tannins also have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, enhancing the therapeutic potential of Holy Basil in herbal medicine.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Holy Basil (Ocimum tenuiflorum). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress in the body. This compound supports various therapeutic effects associated with Holy Basil, including anti-inflammatory and immune-modulating benefits.

5. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a major class of active compounds in Holy Basil (Ocimum tenuiflorum). These compounds contribute to the plant's adaptogenic properties, helping the body manage stress and enhance resilience. They are responsible for many of the therapeutic effects associated with Holy Basil in traditional and modern herbal medicine.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Holy Basil (Ocimum tenuiflorum). It contributes to the plant's adaptogenic properties, helping the body manage stress and enhance resilience. Saponins also support immune function and have anti-inflammatory effects.

7. Coumarin

Coumarin is a bioactive compound found in Holy Basil (Ocimum tenuiflorum). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Coumarin may support cardiovascular health and aid in stress management, aligning with Holy Basil's traditional uses in Ayurvedic medicine.

8. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Holy Basil (Ocimum tenuiflorum). It contributes to the plant's adaptogenic properties, supporting stress resilience and immune function. Glycosides in Holy Basil are known for their bioavailability and role in modulating physiological responses.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Holy Basil (Ocimum tenuiflorum). It contributes to the plant's pharmacological properties, including anti-inflammatory and adaptogenic effects. Alkaloids in Holy Basil support its traditional use in stress management and immune support. These compounds are part of the complex bioactive profile of this medicinal herb.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a pigment found in Holy Basil (Ocimum tenuiflorum) that contributes to its medicinal properties. This compound exhibits antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting the plant's traditional use in promoting health and reducing oxidative stress.
