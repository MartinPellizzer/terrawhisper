Corydalis zadoiensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Corydalis zadoiensis?

Corydalis zadoiensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Ranunculales. This plant is a specific representative of the family Papaveraceae, falling under the genus Corydalis, and is identified by its unique species designation, zadoiensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Corydalis zadoiensis has a perennial herbaceous structure characterized by deeply lobed, pinnatifid leaves and zygomorphic flowers with distinctively spurred petals. The foliage is typically bright green and divided into several narrow, linear segments that give the plant a delicate appearance. Its inflorescences consist of several flowers held on pedicels, often displaying shades of yellow or pale colors. The most defining feature is the elongated nectar spur extending from the base of the corolla. The root system generally consists of a fleshy tuber that allows the plant to survive dormant periods.
