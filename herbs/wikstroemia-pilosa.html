Wikstroemia pilosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Wikstroemia pilosa has woody, perennial stems characterized by a dense covering of fine, soft hairs and small, elliptical leaves with entire margins. The foliage is typically evergreen, displaying a leathery texture and a deep green color on the upper surface. These leaves are arranged oppositely along the branches, providing a thick, bushy appearance to the shrub. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. Its overall growth habit is compact, often forming rounded mounds in its natural habitat.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Wikstroemia pilosa can cause severe skin irritation, vomiting, abdominal pain, and potentially fatal poisoning if ingested due to the presence of toxic diterpenes. In many cases, the toxicity is concentrated in the leaves and bark, making any accidental ingestion a serious medical concern. Symptoms of poisoning can escalate quickly if the toxic substances are not addressed promptly.
