Wikstroemia pilosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Wikstroemia pilosa?

Wikstroemia pilosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic lineage, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Malvales, this plant is a member of the family Thymelaeaceae. Finally, its specific biological classification is defined by its placement in the genus Wikstroemia, with the species designation pilosa.

    What are the herbal preparations of Wikstroemia pilosa?

    Wikstroemia pilosa is prepared primarily as a decoction made by boiling the dried leaves and stems in water to extract medicinal compounds. This liquid infusion is often consumed orally to address various internal ailments according to traditional practices. Some practitioners also utilize the plant in the form of topical poultices where crushed parts are applied directly to the skin. Additionally, concentrated extracts or tinctures may be used to provide a more potent dosage of the plant's bioactive constituents. These diverse methods allow for both systemic and localized therapeutic applications of the species.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Wikstroemia pilosa can cause severe skin irritation, vomiting, abdominal pain, and potentially fatal poisoning if ingested due to the presence of toxic diterpenes. These compounds act as potent irritants that can lead to significant gastrointestinal distress upon consumption. Contact with the plant's sap or leaves may also result in contact dermatitis or localized rashes on the skin. In many cases, the toxicity is concentrated in the leaves and bark, making any accidental ingestion a serious medical concern. Symptoms of poisoning can escalate quickly if the toxic substances are not addressed promptly.
