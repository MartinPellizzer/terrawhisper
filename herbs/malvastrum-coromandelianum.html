Malvastrum coromandelianum

What's the taxonomical classification of Malvastrum coromandelianum?

Malvastrum coroandelianum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is placed in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Malvales. It is a member of the family Malvaceae, which is further categorized into the genus Malvastrum, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species coromandelianum.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat inflammatory conditions, skin infections, digestive ailments, and respiratory issues. The leaves and stems are often applied topically as a poultice to soothe skin irritations and heal minor wounds. When prepared as a decoction or tea, it serves as a remedy for internal discomforts like stomach aches and indigestion. Its mucilaginous properties also make it useful in calming coughs and easing sore throats. Local traditional medicine relies on these various applications to manage a range of common physical ailments.
