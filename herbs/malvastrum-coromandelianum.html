What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Malvastrum coroemandelianum has an erect, woody-based herbaceous habit characterized by simple or branched stems and ovate to cordate leaves with serrated margins. Its flowers are actinomorphic and feature five pink to lilac petals that surround a central column of stamens. The fruit consists of small, rounded schizocarps that contain several individual seeds. These structural features are typical of the Malvaceae family, facilitating both photosynthesis and reproduction in its natural habitat.
