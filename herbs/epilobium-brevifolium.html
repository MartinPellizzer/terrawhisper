Epilobium brevifolium

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Epiloium brevifolium has an erect or ascending herbaceous habit with stems that are often reddish and square in cross-section. The leaves are small, sessile, and oppositely arranged, typically measuring only a few millimeters in length. These leaves are generally ovate to elliptical with entire or slightly serrated margins. The inflorescence consists of small, four-petaled flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. The fruit is a slender, elongated capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds.
