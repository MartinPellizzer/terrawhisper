Roselle (Hibiscus sabdariffa)

Roselle (Hibiscus sabdariffa) is a member of the Malvaceae family, native to Southern Africa, Eastern Africa, and Southwest Asia. Traditionally, its flowers, fruits, and leaves have been used for infusions, decoctions, and culinary uses.

This herb is particularly valued for its diuretic, anti-inflammatory, and astringent actions, and has a long history of use in african traditional medicine, traditional chinese medicine, and european herbal medicine.

Perennial herbaceous plant.

Leaves
Broad leaves with upper surface reddish-purple and lower surface green, featuring prominent stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Flowers are zygomorphic with five fused petals forming a trumpet-shaped corolla, five stamens fused to the corolla, and a superior ovary with a single style and stigma
Stems
Erect, simple branching, glabrous, woody, with nodes bearing alternate, persistent stipules.
