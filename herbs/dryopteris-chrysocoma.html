Dryopteris chrysocoma

What's the taxonomical classification of Dryopteris chrysocoma?

Dryopteris chrysocoma belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae. Following the order Polypodiales, the plant is a member of the family Polypodiaceae. Its taxonomic hierarchy concludes with its placement in the genus Dryopteris, specifically identifying it as the species chrysocoma.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Dryopteris chrysocoma contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids that provide its medicinal potential. These chemical constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Traditional applications of the fern often rely on these compounds to treat skin ailments and gastrointestinal issues. Research into its phytochemical profile suggests that the presence of specific glycosides may offer protective effects against cellular oxidation. Understanding these complex chemical interactions is essential for exploring the plant's broader pharmacological possibilities.

What are the therapeutic actions of Dryopteris chrysocoma?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat intestinal parasites, specifically tapeworms and roundworms, through the administration of its rhizome extracts. Historically, various indigenous groups have utilized its medicinal properties to expel various types of helminths from the digestive tract. The chemical constituents within the fern are believed to possess anthelmintic properties that facilitate the removal of these organisms. While it was a common remedy in traditional ethnobotany, modern medical practices have largely replaced such botanical treatments with more standardized pharmaceutical options. Careful dosage was traditionally necessary due to the potential toxicity of certain fern species.
