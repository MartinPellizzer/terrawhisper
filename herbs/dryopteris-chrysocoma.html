Dryopteris chrysocoma

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dryopteris chrysocoma has bipinnate to tripinnate fronds that typically measure between 30 and 60 centimeters in length, featuring a distinctively golden-brown or tawny color on the scales covering the stipes and rachises. The stipe is stout and covered in densely packed, shiny, brownish scales that provide a characteristic appearance. Each pinna is deeply lobed, creating a delicate and finely dissected texture throughout the foliage. The pinnules are often somewhat asymmetric at the base and possess serrated margins. This morphological structure allows the plant to efficiently capture light in its shaded forest habitats.

What are the therapeutic actions of Dryopteris chrysocoma?

Dryopteris chrysocoma has therapeutic actions that include acting as a vermifuge, an anthelmintic, an astringent, and a diaphoritic. As an anthelmintic and vermifuge, the plant is traditionally used to expel parasitic worms from the digestive tract. Its astringent properties allow it to help constrict body tissues and reduce excessive secretions or inflammation. Additionally, the plant functions as a diaphoretic to promote sweating and assist in regulating body temperature. These diverse chemical properties make it a significant subject in ethnobotanical studies of traditional medicine.
