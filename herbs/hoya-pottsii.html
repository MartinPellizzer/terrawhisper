Hoya pottsii

What's the taxonomical classification of Hoya pottsii?

Hoya pottsii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Gentianales. It is a member of the family Apocynaceae, which is further divided into the genus Hoya, where it is specifically identified by the species epithet pottsii.

What are the herbal preparations of Hoya pottsii?

Hoya pottsii is prepared as a concentrated decoction of the leaves, a topical poultice of crushed stems, or an infused oil derived from the floral extracts. These diverse forms allow for different modes of application depending on the intended traditional use. The liquid decoction is typically simmered to extract soluble compounds for internal administration. Meanwhile, the botanical matter can be macerated directly onto the skin to create a cooling poultice. Some practitioners also steep the plant material in a lipid base to produce a stable medicinal oil.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Hoya pottsii can cause skin irritation or allergic reactions if the plant's sap comes into direct contact with human skin. Some individuals may experience redness, itching, or a mild rash upon touching the milky latex produced by the stems. While there is limited data on systemic toxicity, it is generally recommended to keep the plant away from pets to avoid potential gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Careful handling is advised to prevent accidental contact with sensitive areas like the eyes or mouth. Maintaining distance from children can also mitigate the risk of accidental exposure to the sap.
