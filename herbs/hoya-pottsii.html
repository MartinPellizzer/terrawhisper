What's the taxonomical classification of Hoya pottsii?

Hoya pottsii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Gentianales. It is a member of the family Apocynaceae, which is further divided into the genus Hoya, where it is specifically identified by the species epithet pottsii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hoya pottsii has thick, fleshy, ovate leaves that measure up to five centimeters in length and feature a distinctive silvery-grey mottled pattern on a dark green base.

These succulent leaves are arranged oppositely along trailing or climbing stems that exhibit a woody texture as they mature. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, wax-like flowers that are typically white or pale pink with a fragrant scent. Each individual bloom consists of a central corona surrounded by a ring of petals, emerging from a common peduncle.

The overall morphology supports an epiphytic lifestyle, allowing the plant to cling to surfaces in its natural habitat.
