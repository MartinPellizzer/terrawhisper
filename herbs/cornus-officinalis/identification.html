Botanical Identification of Chinese Cornel (Cornus officinalis)

To identify Chinese Cornel (Cornus officinalis) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Chinese Cornel (Cornus officinalis) Monograph

Scientific Classification

The botanical identification of Chinese Cornel begins with its classification. It belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Saxifragales, family Cornaceae, genus Cornus, and species Cornus officinalis. This classification system helps in understanding its biological relationships and medicinal properties.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The botanical name of Chinese Cornel is Cornus officinalis, a classification established by Linnaeus. This name reflects its traditional use in herbal medicine. It is also known by several synonyms, common names, and regional variations, which are explored in the following sections.

Morphological Characteristics

Chinese Cornel is a deciduous shrub with a compact growth habit. Its root system is fibrous and shallow, anchoring it in well-drained soil. The stems are slender, branching, and often covered in fine hairs. Leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, turning red in autumn. The inflorescence is a panicle, bearing small white flowers in early spring. The fruit is a red drupe, containing a single seed, ripening in late summer.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Cornus officinalis can be distinguished by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, white flowers in branched clusters, and red to orange berries. It differs from similar species like Cornus controversa by its smaller, more pointed berries and narrower leaves. Its bark is smooth and grayish-white, with a distinct aromatic scent when cut.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Chinese Cornel is native to East Asia, particularly China, Korea, and Japan. It thrives in temperate climates with mild winters and warm summers. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils and is commonly found at elevations between 500 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Chinese Cornel typically blooms in late spring to early summer, around May to June. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early autumn, from August to September. For identification purposes, the best time to harvest the plant is during late autumn when the fruits are fully mature and ready for use.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Chinese Cornel (Cornus officinalis) is closely related to species like Cornus mas and Cornus controversa. It belongs to the genus Cornus, which is part of the Cornaceae family. The family traits include opposite leaves, small flowers, and drupes, which are shared with its closest relatives.
