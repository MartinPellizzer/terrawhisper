Chinese Cornel (Cornus officinalis) Compounds

The primary compounds of Chinese Cornel include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and tannins, known for their astringent effects. Phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory actions, while glycosides and saponins support digestive health. Coumarins and terpenoids provide antimicrobial benefits, and anthocyanins enhance its antioxidant profile. Essential oils and alkaloids further contribute to its diverse pharmacological effects.

Chinese Cornel (Cornus officinalis) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Chinese Cornel (Cornus officinalis). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, playing a key role in its traditional use for treating ailments like coughs and skin conditions. These compounds are extracted and utilized in herbal formulations for their therapeutic benefits.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Chinese Cornel (Cornus officinalis). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is used to treat diarrhea and inflammation. Tannins also help in preserving the plant's medicinal effects during processing.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Chinese Cornel (Cornus officinalis). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties and is believed to support liver function and digestive health. This compound is often found in the fruit and bark of the plant, making it a valuable component in traditional herbal formulations.

4. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Chinese Cornel (Cornus officinalis). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly in promoting liver health and reducing inflammation. Glycosides are often extracted from the fruit and used in traditional formulations for their therapeutic effects.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Chinese Cornel (Cornus officinalis). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating inflammatory conditions and promoting circulation. Coumarin's anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory properties are central to the medicinal value of Cornus officinalis.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Chinese Cornel (Cornus officinalis). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in promoting diuresis and clearing heat. Saponins also exhibit anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory properties, supporting its role in herbal medicine for various therapeutic applications.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Chinese Cornel (Cornus officinalis) are a class of bioactive compounds known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating ailments like arthritis and digestive disorders. They are often extracted and used in herbal formulations for their therapeutic effects.

8. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Chinese Cornelian cherry (Cornus officinalis). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by acting as an antioxidant and anti-inflammatory agent. This compound is widely used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.

9. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound of Chinese Cornel (Cornus officinalis). It is extracted from the fruit and contains volatile components like limonene and cineole, which contribute to its aromatic and therapeutic properties. This oil is valued for its potential in treating digestive issues and respiratory conditions in traditional herbal medicine.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a bioactive compound found in Chinese Cornel (Cornus officinalis). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating inflammation and pain. Alkaloids in Cornus officinalis are believed to have neuroprotective and anti-inflammatory properties. They are often isolated and studied for their therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.
