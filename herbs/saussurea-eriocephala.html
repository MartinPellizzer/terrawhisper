Saussurea eriocephala

What's the taxonomical classification of Saussurea eriocephala?

Saussurea eriocephala belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. It is further categorized into the family Asteraceae, which is a large group of flowering plants. Within this family, the plant is placed in the genus Saussurea, and its specific biological identity is defined by the species name eriocephala.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea eriocephala has a dense, woolly inflorescence characterized by a capitulum covered in thick, white, cottony hairs that protect the developing florets. The plant typically exhibits a rosette-forming growth habit with leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence to withstand high-altitude environments. Its flowering heads are composed of numerous small florets tucked within these protective, felt-like bracts. The stems are generally sturdy and can be partially obscured by the dense hairiness of the plant.
