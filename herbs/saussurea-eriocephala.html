Saussurea eriocephala

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea eriocephala has a dense, woolly inflorescence characterized by a capitulum covered in thick, white, cottony hairs that protect the developing florets. The plant typically exhibits a rosette-forming growth habit with leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence to withstand high-altitude environments. Its flowering heads are composed of numerous small florets tucked within these protective, felt-like bracts. The stems are generally sturdy and can be partially obscured by the dense hairiness of the plant. This specialized morphology is an adaptation to the extreme cold and intense UV radiation of its alpine habitat.
