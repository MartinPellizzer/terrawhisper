Aralia cordata

What's the taxonomical classification of Aralia cordata?

Aralia cordata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further belongs to the order Apiales and is a member of the family Araliaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic placement is defined by the genus Aralia and the species cordata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aralia cordata has large, compound leaves composed of several leaflets that are primarily heart-shaped or broadly ovate in form. The leaflets feature serrated margins and distinct pinnate venation that extends toward the edges. These leaves are arranged oppositely along sturdy, woody stems that can reach significant heights. Small, inconspicuous flowers typically emerge in branched clusters known as panicles. The plant often develops small, dark berries that follow the flowering stage.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the temperate regions of East Asia, specifically spanning across China, Japan, and the Korean Peninsula. It typically flourishes in the understory of moist, deciduous forests where sunlight is filtered. Within these habitats, it can be found growing in shaded ravines and along the banks of mountain streams. Its distribution is largely influenced by the availability of well-drained, organic-rich soil and consistent moisture levels. While it remains localized to these specific Asian territories, it thrives in diverse microclimates ranging from lowlands to higher elevations.

What are the pharmacological activities of Aralia cordata?

This plant has potent analgesic and anti-inflammatory pharmacological activities that make it a significant subject in medicinal research. Its bioactive compounds work by modulating pain pathways and reducing the production of inflammatory mediators in the body. Studies indicate that extracts from the plant can effectively alleviate discomfort and swelling through these biological mechanisms. Researchers are currently investigating the specific molecular targets to better understand how these properties function. These therapeutic effects suggest that the species holds great potential for developing new treatments for inflammatory conditions.

As discussed in a study, the analgesic principles from Aralia cordata Thunb. were identified as (ent)-kaur-16-en-19-oic acid (KA) and (ent)-pimara-8(14),15-dien-19-oic acid (PA). Both of these compounds were significantly effective regarding analgesics, hypothermia, and the duration of pentobarbital-induced anesthesia. Additionally, they were effective in the depression of locomotor activity enhanced by methamphetamine. These effects were observed when KA was administered at a dose of 300 mg/kg and PA was administered at a dose of 500 mg/kg by oral administration.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds pimaradienoic acid, pi maradienoic acid, pi maric acid, abietic acid, and PA. These specific diterpenoids are often found within the resinous extracts of the species. Scientists study these chemical constituents for their potential bioactive and therapeutic properties. Each compound plays a unique role in the chemical profile of the plant's secondary metabolites. The presence of these substances contributes to the overall medicinal value of the vegetation.

As discussed in a study, a screening of 99 crude drugs for retinoid acid receptor ligands was conducted. The methanol extract of Aralia cordata Rhizoma was found to most effectively activate the transcriptional activity of RARα. From this plant, pimaradienoic acid (ent-pimara-8(14),15-dien-19-oic acid) was isolated as the constituent capable of activating RAR. This diterpene and two other similar structures functioned as natural RAR agonists. These three agonists were able to increase mRNA levels in HepG2 cells and induce differentiation in both F9 and HL-60 cells.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating inflammatory conditions such as arthritis and various forms of chronic pain. Its medicinal properties are often harnessed to reduce swelling in the joints and soothe aching muscles. Practitioners frequently utilize the plant to manage discomfort associated with long-term physical ailments. By targeting internal inflammation, it helps alleviate the physical distress caused by these conditions. This natural approach serves as a traditional remedy for stabilizing bodily discomfort.

As discussed in a study, the roots of Aralia cordata are widely used in Oriental medicine as a remedy for arthritis. The compound ent-pimara-8(14), 15-dien-19-oic acid, also known as pimaradienoic acid (PA), was isolated from these roots. PA significantly inhibits the production of nitric oxide (NO), prostaglandin E(2) (PGE(2)), and interleukin-6 (IL-6). It also inhibits the expressions of inducible NO synthase (iNOS), cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2), and IL-6. This effect occurs through the blockade of the phosphorylation of MAPK pathways, specifically p38 MAPK and extracellular signal-regulated kinase 1/2 (ERK1/2), following NF-kappaB activation.

    What side effects this plant can have?

While many people use it in traditional practices, there is a lack of comprehensive clinical data regarding long-term internal toxicity.
