Aralia cordata

What's the taxonomical classification of Aralia cordata?

Aralia cordata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further belongs to the order Apiales and is a member of the family Araliaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic placement is defined by the genus Aralia and the species cordata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aralia cordata has large, compound leaves composed of several leaflets that are primarily heart-shaped or broadly ovate in form. The leaflets feature serrated margins and distinct pinnate venation that extends toward the edges. These leaves are arranged oppositely along sturdy, woody stems that can reach significant heights. Small, inconspicuous flowers typically emerge in branched clusters known as panicles. The plant often develops small, dark berries that follow the flowering stage.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the temperate regions of East Asia, specifically spanning across China, Japan, and the Korean Peninsula. It typically flourishes in the understory of moist, deciduous forests where sunlight is filtered. Within these habitats, it can be found growing in shaded ravines and along the banks of mountain streams. Its distribution is largely influenced by the availability of well-drained, organic-rich soil and consistent moisture levels. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds pimaradienoic acid, pimaric acid, abietic acid, and pimaradienoic acid. These specific diterpenoids are significant components found within the plant's resinous extracts. Each of these organic acids contributes to the complex chemical profile used in various therapeutic studies. Scientists often examine these substances for their unique biological activities and potential pharmacological properties. The presence of these compounds defines the plant's chemical identity and its medicinal value.

As discussed in a study, a screening of 99 crude drugs was conducted to identify retinoid acid receptor ligands. The methanol extract of Aralia cordata Rhizoma was demonstrated to most effectively activate the transcriptional activity of RARα. From this plant, pimaradienoic acid (ent-pimara-8(14),15-dien-19-oic acid) was isolated as the constituent capable of activating RAR. This compound is one of three natural RAR agonists with diterpene structures identified in the research. These diterpenes can increase mRNA levels of the constitutive androstane receptor in HepG2 cells and induce F9 cell differentiation.

    What health problems is this plant used for?

    This plant is used for treating inflammatory conditions such as arthritis and various forms of chronic pain. Its medicinal properties are often harnessed to reduce swelling in the joints and soothe aching muscles. Practitioners frequently utilize the plant to manage discomfort associated with long-term physical ailments. By targeting internal inflammation, it helps alleviate the physical distress caused by these conditions. This natural approach serves as a traditional remedy for stabilizing bodily discomfort.

    As discussed in a study, the roots of Aralia cordata are widely used in Oriental medicine as a remedy for arthritis. The compound ent-pimara-8(14), 15-dien-19-oic acid, also known as pimaradienoic acid (PA), was isolated from these roots. PA significantly inhibits the production of nitric oxide (NO), prostaglandin E(2) (PGE(2)), and interleukin-6 (IL-6). It also inhibits the expressions of inducible NO synthase (iNOS), cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2), and IL-6. This effect occurs through the blockade of the phosphorylation of MAPK pathways, specifically p38 MAPK and extracellular signal-regulated kinase 1/2 (ERK1/2), following NF-kappaB activation.
