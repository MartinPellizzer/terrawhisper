Sterculia pexa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sterculia pexa has a woody structure characterized by a medium to large tree habit with bark that typically develops a fissured or scaly texture. Its leaves are alternate and trifoliate, featuring leaflets that are often obovate or elliptical in shape with entire margins. The reproductive structures consist of inconspicuous flowers that are borne on short pedicels, often appearing in clusters. These flowers eventually give rise to woody, often dehiscent follicles that contain the plant's seeds. The overall morphological form is adapted to tropical or subtropical forest environments where its canopy provides significant shade.
