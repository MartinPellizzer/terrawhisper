Coral Tree (Saraca asoca) Uses

Coral Tree is used to treat skin infections, fungal infections, and inflammatory skin conditions such as psoriasis and allergies. It also helps with skin inflammation and joint pain. The plant is traditionally applied for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It is also used in the treatment of malaria and certain types of cancer.

Coral Tree (Saraca asoca) Monograph

1. Skin infections

Saraca asoca is used topically to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial properties. The bark and leaves contain compounds that inhibit bacterial and fungal growth. It is applied as a paste or decoction to wounds, boils, and rashes. Its anti-inflammatory effects also help reduce swelling and promote healing.

2. Fungal infections

Coral Tree (Saraca asoca) is used in traditional herbal medicine to treat fungal infections due to its antimicrobial properties. Extracts from its bark contain compounds that inhibit fungal growth, making it effective for conditions like ringworm and athlete's foot. It is often applied topically as a poultice or infused oil to combat skin infections caused by fungi.

3. Psoriasis

Saraca asoca is used in traditional medicine to treat psoriasis due to its anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory properties. The bark contains compounds that help reduce skin inflammation and regulate excessive immune responses. It is often prepared as a decoction or paste and applied topically to affected areas. Its efficacy is supported by historical use and some modern pharmacological studies.

4. Skin allergies

The Coral Tree (Saraca asoca) is used in traditional herbal medicine to treat skin allergies due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistaminic properties. Its bark and leaves contain compounds that help reduce skin irritation and allergic reactions. It is often prepared as a paste or decoction for topical application.

5. Inflammatory skin conditions

Saraca asoca is used in traditional medicine to treat inflammatory skin conditions such as eczema and psoriasis. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties help reduce redness, itching, and infection. The bark and leaves are often prepared as poultices or decoctions for topical application. It is valued for its soothing effects on irritated skin.

6. Skin inflammation

Coral Tree (Saraca asoca) is used in traditional medicine to treat skin inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The plant's bark and leaves contain compounds that help reduce redness, swelling, and infection in skin conditions. It is often applied topically in the form of poultices or pastes for effective relief.

7. Inflammatory conditions

The Coral Tree (Saraca asoca) is used in traditional herbal medicine to treat inflammatory conditions due to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. Its leaves and flowers contain compounds that help reduce swelling and pain, making it effective for ailments like arthritis and skin infections. It is often prepared as a decoction or paste for topical application.

8. Malaria

Coral Tree (Saraca asoca) is used in traditional medicine to treat malaria due to its antimalarial compounds, such as quinazoline alkaloids. These compounds help reduce fever and combat the Plasmodium parasite. It is often prepared as a decoction or powder and used in Ayurvedic and indigenous healing practices.

9. Cancer

Saraca asoca has shown potential in cancer treatment due to its bioactive compounds, which exhibit anti-cancer properties by inhibiting tumor growth and inducing apoptosis. Preliminary studies suggest it may support conventional therapies, though more research is needed for clinical application.

10. Joint pain

Coral Tree (Saraca asoca) is used in traditional medicine to alleviate joint pain due to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. The bark and leaves contain compounds that reduce swelling and pain in joints, making it a valued remedy in herbal treatments for arthritis and other inflammatory conditions.
