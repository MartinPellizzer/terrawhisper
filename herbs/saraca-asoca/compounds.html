Coral Tree (Saraca asoca) Compounds

The primary compounds of Coral Tree include alkaloids, which have various pharmacological effects, and flavonoids, known for their antioxidant properties. Glycosides and tannins contribute to its astringent and anti-inflammatory actions. Phenolic acids, coumarins, and saponins also play roles in its medicinal uses. Terpenoids, essential oils, and anthocyanins add to its therapeutic potential.

Coral Tree (Saraca asoca) Monograph

1. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a key active compound in Coral Tree (Saraca asoca), known for its potential cardiovascular and anti-inflammatory effects. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating heart conditions and inflammatory disorders. Its presence supports the therapeutic value of Saraca asoca in herbal medicine.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Saraca asoca contribute to its medicinal properties by exhibiting antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. These compounds help in reducing oxidative stress and supporting immune function. They are commonly used in traditional remedies for their therapeutic potential. Flavonoids are a key group of bioactive compounds found in this plant.

3. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Saraca asoca, the Coral Tree. It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal medicine. This compound is often used for its potential cardiovascular and anti-inflammatory benefits. Its presence supports the therapeutic use of Saraca asoca in various health applications.

4. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Coral Tree (Saraca asoca). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Tannins in Saraca asoca help in treating skin conditions and reducing excess body fluids.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Coral Tree (Saraca asoca). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound helps in reducing oxidative stress and supporting immune function. Phenolic acid is commonly found in the bark and leaves of Saraca asoca.

6. Coumarin

Coulmarin is a key active compound found in the Coral Tree (Saraca asoca). It exhibits antispasmodic and anticoagulant properties, making it valuable in herbal medicine for treating conditions like hypertension and menstrual disorders. Its presence contributes to the plant's traditional use in Ayurvedic and indigenous healing practices.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound found in Coral Tree (Saraca asoca). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and inflammatory conditions. Saponin exhibits antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, enhancing the therapeutic potential of Saraca asoca in herbal medicine.

8. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in the Coral Tree (Saraca asoca). They contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating inflammatory and skin conditions. These compounds exhibit antimicrobial and antioxidant properties, supporting their role in herbal medicine.

9. Essential oil

The essential oil of Coral Tree (Saraca asoca) contains bioactive compounds like limonene and geraniol. These volatile compounds contribute to its aromatic properties and potential therapeutic effects. They are commonly extracted through steam distillation and used in traditional herbal remedies. The essential oil is valued for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in the Coral Tree (Saraca asoca). It contributes to the plant's vibrant colors and possesses antioxidant properties that support cardiovascular and eye health. This compound is also associated with anti-inflammatory and neuroprotective effects in traditional herbal medicine.
