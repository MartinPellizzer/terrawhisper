Botanical Identification of Coral Tree (Saraca asoca)

To identify Coral Tree (Saraca asoca) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Coral Tree (Saraca asoca) Monograph

Scientific Classification

The Coral Tree, or Saraca asoca, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Fabales, order Fabales, family Bignoniaceae, genus Saraca, and species Saraca asoca. This classification places it within the flowering plants and the legume order, highlighting its botanical relationships and ecological significance.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of the Coral Tree is Saraca asoca. It was first described by the botanist Nathaniel Wallich in 1823. The plant has several synonyms, including Cassia asoca and Butea asoca, reflecting its taxonomic history. Common names such as Coral Tree, Flame of the Forest, and Kachnar are widely used across different regions.

Morphological Characteristics

The Coral Tree (Saraca asoca) is a small to medium-sized tree with a straight, often branched stem. Its roots are deep and taproot-like, providing stability. The leaves are pinnately compound, with 7-13 leaflets, and are alternate along the stem. The inflorescence consists of racemes of fragrant, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is a flat, pod-like capsule that splits open to release small, flat seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Coral Tree (Saraca asoca) can be distinguished by its bright orange to red flowers, trifoliate leaves, and distinctive coral-like seed pods. It is often confused with other Saraca species, but its unique floral color and leaf structure help in accurate identification. The presence of a prominent central vein in the leaves also aids in differentiation from similar plants.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Coral Tree (Saraca asoca) is native to South and Southeast Asia, including India, Sri Lanka, and Indonesia. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils and is typically found at altitudes between 300 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Coral Tree (Saraca asoca) blooms from February to April, producing bright orange-red flowers. Seed formation occurs from May to July, with mature seeds ready for harvest in late summer. For identification purposes, the plant is most identifiable during its flowering period, making it easier to distinguish from other species.

Phylogenetic Relationships

The Coral Tree (Saraca asoca) is closely related to species within the genus Saraca. It shares morphological and genetic traits with other legumes, particularly those in the Fabaceae family. Its phylogenetic position reflects its close ties to other tropical trees and shrubs with similar ecological and medicinal uses.
