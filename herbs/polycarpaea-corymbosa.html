Polycarpaea corymbosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polycarpaea corymbosa has an erect, annual herbaceous habit characterized by slender, branching stems and oppositely arranged, ovate to lanceolate leaves with entire margins. The foliage typically displays a smooth texture and prominent venation throughout the leaf blade. Its inflorescence is organized into terminal, flat-topped corymbose clusters containing small, inconspicuous flowers. The reproductive structures include tiny petals and stamens that are characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family. Small, globose capsules serve as the fruit, which eventually release minute seeds upon maturation.
