What is Stelis escobarii?

Stelis escobarii is an orchid species within the family Orchidaceae.

It is a terrestrial epiphyte with a short rhizome and several stems, typically growing 10-15 cm tall, with 5-7 linear-lanceolate leaves, 5-10 cm long, and 0.5-1.2 cm wide, and inflorescences that produce 1-3 apical flowers. The flowers are greenish-yellow to yellow, with a labellum that is slightly longer than the sepals, and have a callus-like structure, lacking a distinct spur. The species was first described by Luer in 1981 and is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, specifically in countries such as Mexico, Belize, Guatemala, and Costa Rica.

The plant blooms in late spring and early summer, producing flowers with a diameter of approximately 2-2.5 cm.
