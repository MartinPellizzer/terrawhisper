10 Precautions To Take When Using Cinnamomum Verum (Ceylon Cinnamon)

Cinnamomum verum, also known as Ceylon cinnamon, has some precautions to consider before using it medicinally, such as pregnant or breastfeeding women, as high doses can stimulate the uterus and cause premature labor or affect milk production.

Consuming large amounts can also cause gastrointestinal issues, such as nausea and diarrhea, especially for individuals with sensitive stomachs.

In addition, those with bleeding disorders, such as hemophilia, should exercise caution, as Cinnamomum verum may exacerbate bleeding risks.
