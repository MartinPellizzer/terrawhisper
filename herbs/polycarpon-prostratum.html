Polycarpon prostratum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polycarpon prostratum has a prostrate, branched growth habit characterized by slender, reddish stems that spread closely along the soil surface. The leaves are small, linear to lanceolate in shape, and arranged oppositely along the stems. Tiny, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves, typically appearing in small clusters. These flowers possess minute petals and stamens that are adapted for specialized pollination. The overall structure of the plant is delicate and compact, allowing it to thrive in low-growing niches.
