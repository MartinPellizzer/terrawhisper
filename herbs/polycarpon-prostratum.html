Polycarpon prostratum has a prostrate, branched growth habit characterized by slender, reddish stems that spread closely along the soil surface. These flowers possess minute petals and stamens that are adapted for specialized pollination. The overall structure of the plant is delicate and compact, allowing it to thrive in low-growing niches.
