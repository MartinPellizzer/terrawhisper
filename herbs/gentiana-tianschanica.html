What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gentiana tianschanica has perennial, herbaceous stems that reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and feature opposite, lanceolate to ovate leaves with entire margins. These blossoms emerge from the leaf axils on short pedicels during the summer months. The plant is characterized by its robust root system which helps it anchor in rocky, alpine environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude mountain ranges of Central Asia, specifically within the Tianshan, Altai, and Kunlun mountains. Its distribution spans across several countries including China, Kyrgyzstan, Kazakhstan, and parts of Mongolia. What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Gentiana tianschanica contains bitter medicinal compounds primarily concentrated in its dried roots and rhizomes. These underground structures are harvested and processed to extract secoiridoid glycosides which serve as the active pharmacological agents. Practitioners often use these extracts to treat various gastrointestinal disorders and promote bile production.
