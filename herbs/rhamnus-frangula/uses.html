Common Buckthorn (Rhamnus frangula) Uses

Common Buckthorn is used to treat constipation and gastrointestinal discomfort through its laxative properties. It may also help with diarrhea and inflammatory bowel disease by reducing inflammation. The plant is traditionally applied for gastritis, colitis, and ulcerative colitis due to its anti-inflammatory effects. It is sometimes used to manage gastroesophageal reflux disease and gastrointestinal infections.

Common Buckthorn (Rhamnus frangula) Monograph

1. Constipation

Common Buckthorn is used to treat constipation due to its laxative properties. The seeds contain anthraquinones that stimulate bowel movements. It is typically prepared as a decoction or suppository. However, it should be used cautiously and under professional guidance.

2. Diarrhea

Common Buckthorn has been traditionally used to treat diarrhea due to its astringent properties. The berries contain compounds that reduce intestinal secretions and firm stools. It is typically prepared as a decoction or tincture. However, it should be used with caution as it can be toxic in large doses.

3. Gastroenteritis

Common Buckthorn has been traditionally used to treat gastroenteritis due to its astringent properties, which help reduce intestinal inflammation and diarrhea. Its bark contains compounds that may support gut health and aid in digestive recovery. It is often prepared as a decoction or tincture for internal use in herbal medicine practices.

4. Inflammatory bowel disease

Common Buckthorn (Rhamnus frangula) has been traditionally used for inflammatory bowel disease due to its anti-inflammatory and laxative properties. The plant contains anthraquinones that may help reduce intestinal inflammation and support digestive health. It is often prepared as a decoction or tincture, though modern use should consider safety and proper preparation to avoid toxicity.

5. Gastritis

Common Buckthorn has been traditionally used to support digestive health, particularly in cases of gastritis. Its bitter compounds stimulate digestive juices and reduce inflammation in the stomach lining. It is often prepared as a tincture or tea to alleviate symptoms like indigestion and heartburn. However, it should be used with caution and under professional guidance.

6. Colitis

Common Buckthorn has been traditionally used to support digestive health, particularly in cases of colitis. Its astringent properties help reduce inflammation and soothe irritated intestinal lining. It is often prepared as a decoction or tincture to manage symptoms and promote healing in the colon.

7. Ulcerative colitis

Common Buckthorn has been traditionally used to support digestive health, particularly in managing symptoms of ulcerative colitis. Its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties may help reduce intestinal inflammation and promote healing. However, it should be used with caution and under professional guidance due to its potent effects.

8. Gastroesophageal reflux disease

Common Buckthorn has been traditionally used to support digestive health, particularly in managing symptoms of gastroesophageal reflux disease. Its astringent properties help reduce excess stomach acid and soothe inflammation in the esophagus. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to alleviate heartburn and promote a sense of fullness after meals.

9. Gastrointestinal infection

Common Buckthorn has been traditionally used to treat gastrointestinal infections due to its astringent and antimicrobial properties. The bark contains compounds that help reduce inflammation and combat harmful pathogens in the digestive tract. It is often prepared as a decoction to support gut health and alleviate symptoms of infection.

10. Gastrointestinal discomfort

Common Buckthorn is used to address gastrointestinal discomfort, particularly constipation, due to its high content of anthraquinone compounds. These compounds stimulate bowel movements by increasing intestinal motility. It is typically prepared as a decoction or suppository. Use should be cautious and under guidance to avoid digestive irritation.
