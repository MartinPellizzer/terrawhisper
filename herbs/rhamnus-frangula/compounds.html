Common Buckthorn (Rhamnus frangula) Compounds

Common Buckthorn contains several primary active compounds, including tannins, which have astringent properties, and phenolic acids, known for their antioxidant effects. Flavonoids and anthocyanins also contribute to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities. Coumarin and glycosides are present, offering additional therapeutic potential.

Common Buckthorn (Rhamnus frangula) Monograph

1. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Common Buckthorn (Rhamnus frangula). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is used in herbal medicine for its ability to reduce inflammation and promote wound healing. Tannins also aid in digestive health by binding to proteins and reducing intestinal secretions.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Common Buckthorn (Rhamnus frangula). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and has been traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory and antidiarrheal effects. This compound is concentrated in the bark and fruits, making them the primary sources for medicinal use.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Common Buckthorn (Rhamnus frangula) are plant-derived compounds known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds may contribute to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and skin-related conditions. Their presence supports the plant's potential therapeutic applications.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Common Buckthorn (Rhamnus frangula). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, including its anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory effects. Coumarin is also responsible for the characteristic bitter taste of the plant. Its presence makes Common Buckthorn useful in traditional herbal remedies.

5. Glycoside

Common Buckthorn (Rhamnus frangula) contains the glycoside known as emodin. Emodin is a type of anthraquinone that contributes to the plant's laxative properties. It is primarily found in the bark and roots, and is responsible for some of the medicinal effects associated with Common Buckthorn in herbal medicine.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Common Buckthorn (Rhamnus frangula). It exhibits mild laxative properties and may support digestive health. Saponins also contribute to the plant's bitterness and have potential anti-inflammatory effects.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Common Buckthorn (Rhamnus frangula). They contribute to the plant's medicinal properties, including potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. These compounds are often responsible for the plant's therapeutic applications in traditional herbal medicine.

8. Alkaloid

Active compounds of Common Buckthorn (Rhamnus frangula) include the alkaloid berberine. Berberine is known for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It is commonly used in herbal medicine to support digestive health and treat infections. This compound contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating various ailments.

9. Essential oil

Essential oil from Common Buckthorn (Rhamnus frangula) contains volatile compounds like limonene and α-pinene. These components contribute to its aromatic properties and may support digestive and antimicrobial effects. However, the essential oil is not the primary active component of the plant.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Common Buckthorn (Rhamnus frangula). It contributes to the plant's deep blue or purple color and has antioxidant properties. These compounds help neutralize free radicals, supporting cellular health and reducing oxidative stress in the body.
