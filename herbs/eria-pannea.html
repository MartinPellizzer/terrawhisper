What's the taxonomical classification of Eria pannea?

Eria pannea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Asparagales, placing it in the family Orchidaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by the genus Eria and is specifically designated as the species pannea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eria pannea has pendulous racemes bearing numerous small, creamy-white to yellowish flowers characterized by a thick, fleshy texture and a distinctively trilobed lip.

The plant features long, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in an alternate pattern along the stem. Its stems are robust and fleshy, supporting the heavy weight of the drooping inflorescences. The floral structure often displays subtle pubescence on the exterior of the petals and sepals.

These morphological traits distinguish the species within its genus and reflect its adaptation to humid forest environments.
