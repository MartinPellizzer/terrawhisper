Eria pannea

What's the taxonomical classification of Eria pannea?

Eria pannea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Asparagales, placing it in the family Orchidaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by the genus Eria and is specifically designated as the species pannea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eria pannea has pendulous racemes bearing numerous small, creamy-white to yellowish flowers characterized by a thick, fleshy texture and a distinctively trilobed lip. Its stems are robust and fleshy, supporting the heavy weight of the drooping inflorescences. The floral structure often displays subtle pubescence on the exterior of the petals and sepals. What are the pharmacological activities of Eria pannea?

This plant has anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial pharmacological activities that contribute to its medicinal value. Research indicates that its extracts can effectively reduce swelling and inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory mediators in biological systems. Additionally, the presence of bioactive compounds allows the plant to neutralize free radicals, providing significant antioxidant protection. These properties suggest that the species holds potential for developing new therapeutic agents for various inflammatory conditions. Such multifaceted biological effects make it a subject of interest in ethnopharmacological studies.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", Eria pannea is one of 13 plant species reported for the first time for the treatment of inflammation-related diseases. This species was identified among a total of 34 species belonging to 32 genera and 22 families encountered during the field survey. The study focused on plants used by the Lohit community of Arunachal Pradesh, India, to manage inflammation-related ailments. These ailments include rheumatism, muscle swelling, cut wounds, accidental bone fractures, insect bites, pains, and burns by fire and hot water.
