Lamium barbatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Lamium barbatum?

Lamium barbatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its hierarchical progression, the plant is situated in the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Lamium and is specifically categorized as the species barbatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lamium barbatum has square stems, opposite ovate leaves with crenate margins, and tubular, two-lipped flowers that are typically white with purple spots. The foliage is characterized by a soft, hairy texture that covers the leaf surfaces and stems. Its inflorescence consists of dense whorls of flowers emerging from the leaf axils. The upper lip of the corolla is hooded, while the lower lip is three-lobed to facilitate pollination. Small, nut-like fruits develop within the calyx once the flowering period concludes.
