Diphylleia sinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Diphylleia sinensis?

Diphylleia sinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it falls under the order Ranunculales and is a member of the family Berberidaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Diphylleia, with the specific epithet sinensis identifying the individual species.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Diphylleia sinensis has large, opposite, petiolate leaves that are typically divided into two distinct leaflets, giving the plant its name. These leaves are broadly ovate or elliptical with serrated margins and a texture that can range from smooth to slightly rugose. The plant features a woody, rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread slowly through the forest floor. During the flowering season, it produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that emerge from the leaf axils.
