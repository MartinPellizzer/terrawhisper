Botanical Identification of Bupleurum Root (Bupleurum falcatum)

To identify Bupleurum Root (Bupleurum falcatum) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Bupleurum Root, or Bupleurum falcatum, belongs to the plant kingdom, specifically the flowering plants. It is classified under the order Apiales and family Apiaceae, commonly known as the carrot or parsley family. The genus is Bupleurum, and the species is Bupleurum falcatum. This classification helps in understanding its botanical relationships and medicinal properties.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Bupleurum falcatum is the scientific name for the plant commonly known as Bupleurum root. It was first described by the botanist Jean-Baptiste Lamarck in 1781. The species has several synonyms, including Bupleurum scorzoneraefolium and Bupleurum rotundifolium. It is also referred to by various common and regional names, reflecting its widespread use in traditional herbal medicine.

Morphological Characteristics

Bupleurum falcatum is a perennial herb with a taproot system. It has upright, branched stems that are hairy. The leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and have a rough texture. The inflorescence is a loose panicle, with small yellow flowers. The fruit is a capsule containing small, flat seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Bupleurum falcatum can be distinguished by its distinctive leaf shape, which is lanceolate with a prominent central vein. The roots are long, cylindrical, and have a slightly rough surface. It is often confused with Bupleurum scorzonerifolium, but the former has more pointed leaves and a stronger aromatic scent.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Bupleurum falcatum is native to southern Europe and western Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained sandy or loamy soils. It is commonly found at altitudes between 500 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Bupleurum falcatum blooms from April to June, producing small yellow flowers. Seed formation occurs from July to September. Harvesting for identification purposes is typically done in late summer to early autumn when the root is mature and most identifiable.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Bupleurum falcatum belongs to the genus Bupleurum in the family Apiaceae. Its closest relatives include other Bupleurum species such as B. scorzonerifolium and B. chinense. It shares family traits like umbellate inflorescences and aromatic compounds, which are common among Apiaceae members.
