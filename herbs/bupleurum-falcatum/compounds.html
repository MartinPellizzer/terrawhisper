Bupleurum Root (Bupleurum falcatum) Compounds

Bupleurum Root contains several primary compounds, including flavonoids, glycosides, and terpenoids. These compounds contribute to its medicinal properties, such as anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Saponins and coumarins also play roles in its therapeutic actions. Phenolic acids, alkaloids, tannins, essential oils, and anthocyanins further support its use in traditional herbal medicine.

Bupleurum Root (Bupleurum falcatum) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are key active compounds in Bupleurum falcatum, contributing to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, supporting the plant's traditional use in treating conditions like liver disorders and respiratory issues. They help modulate immune responses and protect cellular structures. Flavonoids are among the primary bioactive components responsible for the therapeutic value of Bupleurum Root.

2. Glycoside

Bupleurum Root (Bupleurum falcatum) contains the glycoside saikosaponin. This compound is known for its anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory effects, contributing to the plant's traditional use in treating conditions like liver dysfunction and respiratory infections.

3. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Bupleurum falcatum. These compounds contribute to the plant's therapeutic effects, including anti-inflammatory and immune-modulating properties. They are often responsible for the medicinal benefits associated with Bupleurum root in traditional herbal medicine.

4. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Bupleurum falcatum. It contributes to the herb's anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory properties. Saponin helps regulate immune responses and may support digestive health. Its presence enhances the therapeutic value of Bupleurum root in traditional herbal medicine.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Bupleurum falcatum. It contributes to the herb's anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. Coumarin helps regulate immune responses and may support liver function. Its presence enhances the therapeutic potential of Bupleurum Root in traditional herbal medicine.

6. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Bupleurum falcatum. It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound supports the traditional use of Bupleurum Root in herbal medicine for treating conditions like fever and liver disorders. Its presence enhances the therapeutic potential of the herb.

7. Alkaloid

Bupleurum Root (Bupleurum falcatum) contains the alkaloid berberine. Berberine is known for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating infections and digestive issues. This compound supports the plant's role in herbal medicine.

8. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Bupleurum falcatum. It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. Tannins help reduce swelling and support digestive health. They are commonly used in traditional herbal remedies for their therapeutic effects.

9. Essential oil

The essential oil of Bupleurum falcatum contains volatile compounds like limonene and phellandrene. These compounds contribute to its aromatic properties and may support its traditional use in promoting digestion and reducing inflammation.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Bupleurum falcatum. It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. These actions support its traditional use in promoting liver health and reducing oxidative stress.
