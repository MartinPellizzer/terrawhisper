Premna acuatata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Lamiales. As a member of the family Lamiaceae, this plant is further categorized under the genus Premna, ultimately reaching its specific identification at the species level as P. acuatata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Premna acuata has opposite, elliptical leaves with acute tips and serrated margins that grow on woody stems. The foliage is typically dark green and possesses a smooth, somewhat leathery texture. Its branching pattern follows a decussate arrangement, meaning each pair of leaves is oriented at a right angle to the pair below it. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves to form clusters. These structures eventually develop into small drupes that facilitate the plant's reproduction.
