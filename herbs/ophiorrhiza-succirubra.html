Ophiorrhiza succirubra

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ophiorrhiza succirubra has a creeping, rhizomatous stem that produces clusters of lanceolate to elliptic leaves with entire margins and acute tips. The leaves are typically arranged in a basal rosette or along the slender stems, displaying a glossy texture and distinct venation. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often featuring a reddish or purplish tint that complements the plant's coloration. These flowers are characterized by a long corolla tube and a delicate structure suited for specific pollinators. The overall growth habit is low-growing and spreading, allowing it to colonize forest floors efficiently.
