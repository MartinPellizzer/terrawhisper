10 Possible Side Effects Of Larix Decidua (Larch)

Larix decidua has some side effects when used improperly, such as allergic reactions, digestive problems, and respiratory issues.

Causes of these side effects include consuming excessive amounts, using it in combination with other medications, or experiencing individual sensitivities.

In severe cases, these side effects can worsen lives by causing chronic pain, discomfort, and reduced quality of life.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Larix decidua if used imporperly.

1. Increases risk of toxicity

Larix decidua causes allergic reactions due to its unique chemical composition.

The resin and sap of the tree contain terpenes, which are responsible for triggering an allergic response in some individuals.

These terpenes can cause symptoms such as skin irritation, respiratory issues, and gastrointestinal problems when ingested or inhaled.

2. Increases risk of toxicity

Larix decidua triggers respiratory problems.

The sapwood of Larix decidua contains terpenes, which can cause allergic reactions and exacerbate conditions such as asthma.

Additionally, the trees' resinous secretions may lead to irritation of the mucous membranes in the lungs and throat, resulting in coughing, wheezing, and shortness of breath.

3. Increases risk of toxicity

Larix decidua increases blood pressure due to its inherent properties as a plant-based alpha-blocker.

The flavonoids and terpenoids present in larch may interact with the body's sympathetic nervous system, causing vasoconstriction and subsequent hypertension.

This effect is thought to be triggered by the binding of these compounds to alpha-adrenergic receptors, leading to increased blood pressure levels.

4. Increases risk of toxicity

Larix decidua induces gastrointestinal upset in some individuals.

This is due to the presence of tannins and lignans, which can cause stomach irritation, nausea, and diarrhea.

The resinous compounds found in the bark and wood of Larix decidua may also contribute to this side effect, irritating the mucous membranes in the digestive tract and disrupting normal bowel function.

5. Increases risk of toxicity

Larix decidua disrupts hormonal balance by containing compounds that mimic estrogen, a hormone in the body.

This can lead to an overestrogenization of tissues, potentially causing issues such as breast tenderness, mood swings, and changes in menstrual cycles.

The specific compounds responsible for this effect are stilbenoids and lignans, which have been shown to bind to estrogen receptors, influencing hormonal activity and leading to these side effects.

Larix decidua interferes with medication absorption due to its ability to bind to proteins in the gut, reducing the availability of essential medications.

This is attributed to the presence of tannins and other bioactive compounds in Larix decidua that can interact with drug molecules, altering their absorption rate and efficacy.

As a result, individuals consuming Larix decidua products may experience decreased effectiveness or increased risk of adverse reactions from certain medications.

Larix decidua provokes dizziness episodes due to its ability to affect the nervous system.

The terpenes and volatile oils present in the plant can cause a disruption in the normal functioning of the brain and central nervous system, leading to feelings of dizziness and lightheadedness.

This is often accompanied by other symptoms such as headaches and nausea.

The resin and sap of the tree can cause contact dermatitis, an inflammatory reaction that results in redness, itching, and blisters on the skin.

This allergic response occurs when the body's immune system mistakenly identifies these natural compounds as harmful substances, triggering a defense mechanism that manifests as a skin rash.
