Pseudolarix amabilis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pseudolarix amabilis has a woody, perennial structure characterized by slender, upright stems and small, needle-like leaves arranged in a spiral pattern. The foliage displays a vibrant green hue that transitions to a subtle golden tint during seasonal shifts. Its bark is relatively smooth and thin, providing protection for the vascular tissues beneath. Tiny, inconspicuous reproductive structures emerge from the axils of the branches during the flowering period. The root system consists of a deep taproot that anchors the plant firmly into rocky or sandy substrates.

      Pseudolarix amabilis is prepared as a decoction, a topical poultice, or an infused oil to utilize its medicinal properties.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Pseudolarix amabilis can cause severe skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and respiratory distress if its pollen or sap is inhaled or touched. Individuals with sensitive skin may experience redness, swelling, or painful rashes upon direct contact with the plant's juices. Inhaling airborne particles from the plant can trigger coughing fits or exacerbate asthma symptoms in vulnerable populations. Some people might also encounter gastrointestinal discomfort if accidental ingestion occurs. It is essential to use protective gear when handling this species to avoid these adverse reactions.
