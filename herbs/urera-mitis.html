Urera mitis

Urera mitis, also known as the Pacific nettle or Kanikani in Hawaiian, is a species of flowering plant in the nettle family Urticaceae.

It is a perennial vine native to the tropical and subtropical regions of the Pacific Islands, and has been used in traditional medicine for its medicinal properties.

The plant has anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which contribute to its health benefits, including reducing pain and swelling, and treating skin conditions such as eczema and acne.

Urera mitis is often prepared as a tea or tincture, and can be used topically as a compress or poultice to take advantage of its medicinal and health benefits.
