Leersia oryzoides

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Leersia oryzoides has an erect, perennial woody shrub habit characterized by opposite, elliptic-oblong leaves that possess entire margins and acute apices. The foliage is typically dark green and slightly leathery in texture. Its inflorescences consist of axillary racemes bearing small, pale yellowish to white flowers. The fruit is a woody, indehiscent capsule that eventually splits to reveal numerous small seeds. The stems are often branched and can develop a slightly woody base as the plant matures.
