Catha edulis

What's the taxonomical classification of Catha edulis?

Catha eduledis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Celastrales and the family Celastraceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Catha, and its unique species designation, eduledis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Catha edulis has an erect, woody shrub structure that typically reaches heights of one to three meters and features slender, branching stems with a rough texture.

The leaves are small, ovate to elliptical in shape, and arranged in an opposite or sub-opposite pattern along the branches. These leaves possess acute tips and finely serrated margins, often appearing a deep green color when mature. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers that emerge from the axils of the leaves.

Its root system is fibrous and well-developed to support its growth in various highland environments.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Catha edulis contains psychoactive alkaloids primarily concentrated in its fresh green leaves and young stems. These aerial parts are harvested and consumed to exploit their stimulant properties for various traditional medicinal purposes. The leaves are often chewed or steeped into tea to provide relief from fatigue and improve mental alertness. Some traditional practices also utilize the plant's bark in decoctions to treat specific ailments. Careful management of these plant parts is essential because the chemical potency varies significantly depending on the age of the foliage.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the alkaloids cathinone, cathine, and norephedrine, which serve as its primary psychoactive components.

Beyond these alkaloids, the chemical profile of Catha edulis includes a diverse array of secondary metabolites such as flavonoids and terpenoids. Phenolic compounds are also present, contributing to the plant's complex biological activity and antioxidant properties.

These various medicinal compounds work together to produce the unique physiological effects associated with the species.

The primary medicinal compounds of this plant are shown in the list below.

  • Cathinone
  • Norephedrine
  • Cathine
  • Flavonoids
  • Terpenoids
  • Phenolic compounds

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Catha edulis targets the dopamine D2 and D3 receptors, as well as the norepinephrine transporter, through its primary psychoactive alkaloids cathinone and cathine. These molecules act as potent stimulants by inducing the release of catecholamines from presynaptic terminals. Cathinone functions as a substrate-type releaser that increases synaptic concentrations of dopamine and norepinephrine. The interaction with these specific transmembrane proteins triggers a cascade of neurochemical changes that produce euphoria and increased alertness. Consequently, the plant's medicinal and psychoactive profiles are fundamentally driven by its ability to modulate monoaminergic neurotransmission.

What are the therapeutic actions of Catha edulis?

Catha edulis has stimulant, analeptic, euphoriant, vasoconstrictor, and anorectic properties that primarily result from its high concentration of cathinone.

The plant acts as a central nervous system stimulant, providing an immediate sense of increased alertness and energy. Users often experience a profound euphoriant effect that alters mood and perception during consumption. Its physiological influence includes acting as a vasoconstrictor to narrow blood vessels and as an analeptic to stimulate respiratory or nervous functions.

Additionally, the substance functions as an anorectic by suppressing appetite and reducing the desire to eat.

The primary therapeutic actions of this plant are shown in the list below.

  • Stimulant
  • Analeptic
  • Euphoriant
  • Vasoconstrictor
  • Anorectic

What common ailments are treated using this plant?

This plant is used to treat fatigue, depression, anorexia, and social anxiety through its stimulant properties.

The alkaloids found in the leaves act as a central nervous system stimulant to increase alertness and combat tiredness. Many users consume the plant to elevate their mood and alleviate symptoms of depression during social gatherings. It also serves to stimulate the appetite in individuals suffering from anorexia.

These effects make it a common tool for enhancing sociability and energy levels.

The main common ailment treated using this plant are shown in the list below.

  • Fatigue
  • Depression
  • Anorexia (loss of appetite)
  • Social anxiety

What are the herbal preparations of Catha edulis?

Catha edulis is prepared primarily as a fresh leaf chew, a dried leaf infusion, or a concentrated liquid extract. Users typically harvest the tender green leaves and stems to masticate them directly, which releases the active alkaloids through saliva. Alternatively, the dried plant material can be steeped in hot water to create a traditional tea. In some regional practices, the leaves are also boiled down to produce a more potent decoction. These various methods allow for different intensities of the stimulant effects associated with the plant.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Catha edulis can cause insomnia, hypertension, anxiety, and increased heart rate due to its stimulant properties. Frequent use may lead to psychological symptoms such as irritability or even mild psychosis in sensitive individuals. Some users also report gastrointestinal issues like constipation or loss of appetite. Long-term consumption has been linked to more serious cardiovascular complications and potential reproductive health concerns. Monitoring the dosage is essential to minimize these physiological and neurological risks.
