What to know about Catha edulis (khat) before using it medicinally

Khat, scientifically known as Catha edulis, is a stimulant herb that has been prized for its numerous health benefits, including reducing stress and anxiety while improving cognitive function.

In many parts of the world, khat is used in traditional medicine to treat various ailments such as fever, cough, and digestive issues. As a culinary ingredient, khat leaves are often chewed or brewed into a tea to enhance flavor and aroma. Horticulturally, khat plants thrive in arid regions and can be grown as an ornamental shrub, prized for its unique foliage and vibrant flowers.

Botanically, khat belongs to the Celastraceae family, with its leaves containing the psychoactive alkaloids cathinone and cathine that have been used throughout history for their stimulating properties, with ancient references dating back to the 16th century when it was described by Portuguese traders in East Africa.

What are the side effects of Catha edulis when used improperly?

When used improperly, Catha edulis increases the chances of experiencing side effects, such as respiratory problems, cardiovascular issues, and neurological disturbances.

Consuming large doses or using it in combination with other stimulants can lead to rapid heartbeat, high blood pressure, and increased anxiety, which may exacerbate pre-existing conditions like hypertension, arrhythmias, or cardiovascular disease. Furthermore, the plant's high concentration of cathinone, a potent central nervous system stimulant, can cause users to experience vivid hallucinations, insomnia, and extreme irritability, potentially leading to social withdrawal and mood disturbances.

In severe cases, improper use of Catha edulis may also precipitate a condition known as cathinone-induced psychosis, characterized by paranoid delusions, agitation, and disorganized thinking. Additionally, the plant's stimulatory effects can cause dehydration and electrolyte imbalances, particularly in hot climates or with inadequate hydration, which may lead to heat exhaustion or heat stroke.

Therefore, it is crucial that Catha edulis be used responsibly under the guidance of a qualified healthcare professional to minimize the risk of adverse reactions.
