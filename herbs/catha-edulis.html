Catha edulis

What's the taxonomical classification of Catha edulis?

Catha eduledis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Celastrales and the family Celastraceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Catha, and its unique species designation, eduledis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Catha edulis contains the alkaloid cathinone, which acts as a potent stimulant by increasing levels of dopamine and norepinephrine in the brain. This primary psychoactive compound is chemically similar to amphetamines and is responsible for the plant's most significant physiological effects. In addition to cathinone, the plant features various secondary metabolites including alkaloids like cathine and several polyphenols. These diverse chemical components interact with the central nervous system to produce varying degrees of alertness and euphoria. Research also suggests that the presence of certain flavonoids may contribute to the overall medicinal profile of the species.

What are the therapeutic actions of Catha edulis?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat symptoms of depression, fatigue, and various forms of physical lethargy. Its psychoactive alkaloids act as stimulants to provide temporary relief from mental sluggishness. In some traditional practices, it is also utilized to manage certain types of pain through its stimulating properties. Users often rely on its effects to increase alertness and combat feelings of exhaustion. However, its use is primarily focused on mood enhancement and energy rather than curing specific underlying chronic diseases.
