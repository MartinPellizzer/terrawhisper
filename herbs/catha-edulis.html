Khat

Catha edulis

Khat (Catha edulis) is a plant native to the Horn of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula, belonging to the Apidanthaceae family. Traditionally, its leaves have been used as a stimulant and appetite suppressant, and in some cultures for treating fatigue and respiratory conditions. The leaves are the primary plant part utilized in these traditional practices.

Botanical Identification

Khat (Catha edulis) is a flowering plant in the family Celastraceae. Native to East Africa and the Arabian Peninsula, it is a shrub with alternate leaves, yellow flowers, and erect, branching stems. Commonly known as "qat" in Arabic and "chat" in India, it is a perennial plant widely used in traditional herbal medicine.

Khat Identification.

Active Compounds

Khat contains primary bioactive compounds such as alkaloids, terpenoids, and flavonoids. These compounds interact with the central nervous system, contributing to its stimulating effects. Alkaloids like cathinone are the main psychoactive agents, while terpenoids and flavonoids may enhance or modulate these effects through synergistic interactions.

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory ailments such as coughs and bronchitis.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and to alleviate stomach pain.

Poultice: Applied externally for skin conditions and inflammation.

Safety Profile

Khat (Catha edulis) has a complex safety profile. It contains cathinone and cathine, which can cause stimulant effects. Prolonged use may lead to dependence and psychological effects, making it contraindicated for individuals with cardiovascular issues or mental health disorders.
