Halenia corniculata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Halenia corniculata has a small, leafless, and branched stem structure that typically reaches heights of only a few centimeters. The plant lacks true leaves and instead features scale-like structures along its slender, yellowish-green axes. Its reproductive organs consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge directly from the stem. The morphology is highly specialized for its parasitic lifestyle, allowing it to divert energy from its host. This minimalist form is characteristic of many holoparasitic species in its family.

    What are the herbal preparations of Halenia corniculata?

    Halenia corniculata is prepared as a decoction, a powder, or a topical paste for medicinal use. The dried roots and stems are commonly boiled in water to create a concentrated liquid extract used for internal consumption. Alternatively, the plant material can be finely ground into a powder to be mixed with other substances or swallowed directly.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Halenia corniculata can cause adverse reactions such as nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal pain if ingested in improper quantities. The plant contains bioactive compounds that may lead to gastrointestinal distress or more severe toxic responses in sensitive individuals. Because its chemical profile is not fully documented for human consumption, there is a risk of unpredictable allergic reactions or systemic toxicity. Caution is necessary because the specific dosage required to trigger these side effects remains largely unknown. Users should avoid self-medicating with this species due to the potential for significant physiological harm.
