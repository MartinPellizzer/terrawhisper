Sida cordifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Sida cordifolia?

Sida cordifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized under the order Malvales and the family Malvaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Sida, and its species name, cordifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sida cordifolia has an erect, perennial herb structure characterized by ovate to cordate leaves, small yellow flowers, and a woody rootstock. The leaves are typically arranged alternately along the stem and possess serrated margins with distinct venation. Its flowers are actinomorphic and emerge from the leaf axils, featuring five petals that are often slightly lobed. The fruit is a small, somewhat inflated schizocarp that splits into several mericarps upon maturity. This plant generally grows to a moderate height, supported by a sturdy, sometimes slightly pubescent stem.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of South and Southeast Asia, specifically occurring in countries such as India, China, Thailand, and Vietnam. It is commonly found growing in moist, shaded environments like forest edges and riverbanks where humidity is high. Within these habitats, the species tends to thrive in various altitudes ranging from lowland plains to mid-elevation hilly terrains. Its distribution is largely dictated by the availability of consistent seasonal rainfall and warm temperatures. Localized populations often cluster in areas with rich, well-drained soil and partial sunlight.

What are the pharmacological activities of Sida cordifolia?

This plant has analgesic, anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, hypoglycaemic, and neuroprotective pharmacological activities. Its ability to reduce pain and inflammation makes it a valuable subject for managing various inflammatory conditions. The presence of antioxidant properties helps the plant combat oxidative stress by neutralizing harmful free radicals. Furthermore, its hypoglycaemic effects suggest potential utility in regulating blood glucose levels in metabolic studies. Research also indicates that the plant offers neuroprotective benefits that may help preserve brain health and function.

According to a study published by "Phytotherapy research : PTR", Sida cordifolia extracts from the aerial and root parts showed good analgesic, antiinflammaforry, and hypoglycaemic activities. The ethyl acetate extract of the root (SCR-E) showed comparable antiinflammaforry activity with indomethacin and possessed significantly higher activity compared to the methanol extract of the root (SCR-M). Both the ethyl acetate extract of the root (SCR-E) and the aerial parts (SCA-E) showed very good central and peripheral analgesic activities at a dose of 600 mg/kg. Additionally, the methanol extract of the root (SCR-M) was found to possess significant hypoglycaemic activity.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several bioactive medicinal compounds including vasicine, vasicinone, indoloquinoline, cryptolepine, and cryptolepinoe. These specific alkaloids are recognized for their diverse pharmacological properties and therapeutic potential. The presence of vasicine and vasicinone is particularly notable for their historical use in treating respiratory conditions. Furthermore, the indoloquinoline derivatives such as cryptolepine and cryptolepinoe contribute to the plant's complex chemical profile. Researchers study these constituents to better understand their biological activities in human health.

According to a study published by "Journal of pharmaceuatical and biomedical analysis", Sida cordifolia is one of the 18 species distributed in India used in traditional medicines. The major bioactive alkaloids distributed in Sida spp. include indoquinoline and quinazoline. A validated HPTLC-UV densitometry method was developed for the simultaneous determination of 6 specialized metabolites: 20-hydroxyecdysone (1), vasicine (2), vasicinone (3), cryptolepine (4), quindolinone (5), and cryptolepinoe (6). This method was sensitive with LOD and LOQ ranges of 0.38-0.63 and 1.57-2.12 µg/band, respectively. When samples were spiked at 3 different concentrations, the recovery values were 93.49-98.88%.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for the treatment and management of neurodegenerative diseases, including Alzheimer's disease, Parkinson's disease, and various other neurodegenerative disorders. Researchers investigate its bioactive compounds for their potential to protect the nervous system from progressive decay. These properties make it a significant subject of study for addressing a wide range of complex neuronal disorders. By targeting specific cellular pathways, the plant may help mitigate the symptoms associated with cognitive and motor decline. This medicinal potential highlights its importance in modern neurological research.

According to a study published by "Metabolites", Sida cordifolia L. was evaluated for its bioactive compounds against the beta-secretase enzyme which results in the formation of amyloidal plaques. The study identified hecogenin from S. cordifolia, which shows potential for treating neurodegenerative disorders such as Alzheimer's disease, a condition affecting over 50 million people. Molecular docking against the BACE-1 protein revealed that hecogenin has a binding affinity score of -11.3 kcal/Mol. Additionally, the Hecogenin-BACE-1 protein complex demonstrated substantial stability after 30 ns of MD simulation.

What are the herbal preparations of this plant?

This plant can be prepared as a decoction by boiling the roots in water, an infusion by steeping the leaves, a powder through grinding the dried parts, a tincture using alcohol extraction, or a paste by mixing crushed material with a liquid. These diverse methods allow for the extraction of various active compounds for different medicinal uses. A decoction is typically used when the harder plant structures need heat to release their essence. Infusions offer a gentler way to utilize the delicate foliage. For topical applications, a thick paste is applied directly to the skin, while a concentrated tincture provides a long-lasting liquid extract. The fine powder is often mixed into food or water for easier ingestion.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause gastrointestinal distress such as nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal pain, as well as neurological symptoms like dizziness. These adverse reactions often occur if the plant is ingested in improper quantities or without proper preparation. Some individuals may experience these digestive issues more intensely than others due to varying levels of sensitivity. The onset of dizziness may indicate a systemic reaction to the plant's chemical compounds. Monitoring for these specific side effects is essential when encountering this species.
