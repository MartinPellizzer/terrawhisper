Possible Side Effects of Tillandsia terrestris

Tillandsia terrestris can have side effects if used improperly, such as skin irritation, allergic reactions, and respiratory issues.

Ingestion of the plant's sap or leaves can cause nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, while long-term exposure to its pollen may exacerbate conditions like asthma.

In rare cases, improper use of the plant can lead to an allergic contact dermatitis, resulting in redness, itching, and blistering of the skin.

If you experience any of these symptoms, discontinue use and consult a medical professional for guidance.
