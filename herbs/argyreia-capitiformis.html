Argyreia capitiformis

What's the taxonomical classification of Argyreia capitiformis?

Argyreiia capitiformis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is situated in the order Solanales. It further falls under the family Convolvulaceae, which defines its specific botanical group. Within this family, it is categorized under the genus Argyreiia, with its specific designation being the species capitiformis.

      What are the herbal preparations of Argyreia capitiformis?

      Argyreia capitiformis is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice derived from its leaves and stems. The plant material is often boiled in water to extract its active phytochemicals for oral consumption. Alternatively, steeping the dried parts in hot water allows for a gentler infusion that preserves delicate compounds. For localized skin applications, the crushed fresh foliage can be applied directly to the body as a cooling poultice. These various methods allow for different modes of absorption depending on the intended medicinal use.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Argyreia capitiformis can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or mild toxicity if ingested by humans or animals. Contact with the leaves or stems may trigger contact dermatitis, resulting in redness, itching, or a localized rash on sensitive skin. While specific toxicological studies on humans are limited, many members of the vine family contain compounds that can cause digestive upset if consumed. Pet owners should exercise caution because accidental ingestion could lead to vomiting or other gastrointestinal distress in cats or dogs. Always consult a medical professional or veterinarian if exposure or consumption occurs.
