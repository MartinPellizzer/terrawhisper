Quinoa (Chenopodium quinoa)

Quinoa (Chenopodium quinoa) is a member of the Amaranthaceae family, native to Andes Mountains, South America, Peru, and Bolivia. Traditionally, its seeds, leaves, and shoots have been used for culinary uses, decoctions, and infusions.

This herb is particularly valued for its tonic, anti-inflammatory, and diuretic actions, and has a long history of use in native american herbal medicine, traditional chinese medicine, and tibetan medicine.
