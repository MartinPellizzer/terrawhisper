What's the taxonomical classification of Balanophora subcupularis?

Balanophora subcupularis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Santalales, leading to its placement in the family Balanophoraceae. Finally, it is situated within the genus Balanophora, specifically identified as the species subcupularis.

What are the herbal preparations of Balanophora subcupularis?

Balanophora subcupularis is prepared as a decoction, a topical paste, or an infusion for medicinal use. The decoction involves boiling the plant parts in water to extract active compounds for oral consumption. A thick paste can be created by crushing the tissues to apply directly to the skin for localized treatment. Infusions are made by steeping the plant in hot water to produce a milder liquid remedy. These various methods allow for the different therapeutic applications required by practitioners.

There is also a risk of systemic toxicity if the plant is consumed in large quantities. Detailed toxicological studies are currently limited, meaning the full scope of its potential harm remains uncertain.
