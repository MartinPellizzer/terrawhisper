Cleistogenes polyphylla

What's the taxonomical classification of Cleistogenes polyphylla?

Cleistogenes polyphylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Poales. This plant is further categorized into the family Poaceae, specifically within the genus Cleistogenes, and is identified by the unique species epithet polyphylla.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cleistogenes polyphylla has a herbaceous habit characterized by a cluster of numerous, broadly ovate to lanceolate leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.

The foliage typically features prominent venation and a relatively thin texture compared to other forest floor species. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that lack the showy petals often found in related genera. These blossoms are primarily borne on short peduncles emerging from the center of the leaf cluster.

The overall structure remains low to the ground, adapted for life in shaded, moist environments.
