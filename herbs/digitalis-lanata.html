Digitalis lanata

What's the taxonomical classification of Digitalis lanata?

Digitalis lanata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent, it falls under the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Plantaginaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by its placement within the genus Digitalis, with the unique designation of the species lanata.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Digitalis lanata contains several cardiac glycosides, most notably lanatoside A, lanatoside B, and digitoxin. These specific compounds act by inhibiting the sodium-potassium ATPase pump in cardiac muscle cells. This mechanism increases the force of myocardial contractions while simultaneously slowing the heart rate. Such properties make the plant's extracts highly potent agents for treating certain types of heart failure. However, the narrow therapeutic index of these glycosides requires extremely precise dosing to avoid fatal toxicity.

What are the therapeutic actions of Digitalis lanata?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat congestive heart failure and various forms of cardiac arrhythmia such as atrial fibrillation. It contains cardiac glycosides that work by increasing the force of muscular contractions in the heart. These compounds help the heart pump blood more efficiently through the circulatory system. By regulating the heart rate, the plant's extracts assist in stabilizing irregular rhythms. Medical professionals monitor its use closely due to the narrow therapeutic window required for safety.
