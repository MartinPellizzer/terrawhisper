What's the taxonomical classification of Dryopteris setosa?

Dryopteris setosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It is situated in the class Equisetopsida and falls under the subclass Polypodiinae. Following the hierarchical structure of its lineage, it is part of the order Polypodiales and the family Polypodiaceae. Within this framework, the plant is identified under the genus Dryopteris, specifically designated as the species setosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dryopteris setosa has bipinnate-pinnatifid fronds that reach up to 60 centimeters in length, characterized by a distinctively bristly or setose texture along the rachis and costae. The pinnae are lanceolate in shape and feature deeply lobed margins that taper toward the tips. A defining morphological trait is the presence of stiff, hair-like scales and setae concentrated on the stipes and the underside of the veins. The sori are arranged in two rows on either side of the midvein and are protected by rounded indusia. This combination of bristly pubescence and intricate division defines its structural identity within the genus.
