Acer oblongum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Acer oblongum has simple, alternate leaves that are characterized by an oblong to elliptical shape, an acute apex, and finely serrated margins. The leaf blades typically measure between five and twelve centimeters in length and possess distinct pinnate venation. This species features woody stems that develop a textured bark as the tree matures. The reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into winged samaras. These paired seeds are designed for wind dispersal, which is a common trait among members of the maple genus.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the montane forests of Mexico and Central America, specifically ranging from southern Mexico through Guatemala, Honduras, El Salvador, and Nicaragua. It primarily occupies mid-to-high elevation cloud forests and humid evergreen woodlands. These populations are often found in mountainous regions where moisture levels remain consistently high. The species thrives in the subtropical zones that characterize these specific neotropical corridors. Its presence is closely tied to the distinct climatic patterns of the Mesoamerican highlands.

What are the therapeutic actions of Acer oblongum?

Acer oblongum has medicinal properties that function as an astringent, diuretic, anti-inflammatory, and hemostatic agent. The plant's astringent qualities allow it to contract body tissues, which helps in managing skin irritations or mucosal issues. Additionally, the anti-inflammatory actions work to reduce swelling and soothe painful physical sensations in various parts of the body. Finally, the hemostatic properties enable the plant to effectively slow down or stop bleeding when applied to wounds.

    What are the herbal preparations of Acer oblongum?

    Acer oblongum is prepared as decoctions, infusions, or topical pastes depending on the specific ailment being treated. Practitioners often boil the bark or leaves in water to create a concentrated liquid used for internal medicinal purposes. For external skin conditions, the plant material may be crushed into a thick paste and applied directly to the affected area. Some traditional methods also involve using the plant in steam baths to assist with respiratory relief. These various methods ensure that the bioactive compounds are effectively extracted for therapeutic use.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Acer oblongum can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or gastrointestinal distress if ingested or handled improperly. While it is often used in traditional medicine, the specific chemical compounds within the plant may trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Consuming parts of the plant without proper preparation can lead to nausea or stomach cramps. Some people might also experience localized swelling or redness upon direct contact with the sap or leaves. It is essential to exercise caution and consult professional guidance before any medicinal application.
