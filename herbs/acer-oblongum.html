Acer oblongum

What's the taxonomical classification of Acer oblongum?

Acer oblongum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and placed in the order Sapindales. This species is a member of the family Sapindaceae, falling under the genus Acer, and is specifically identified by its species name, oblongum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Acer oblongum has simple, alternate leaves that are characterized by an oblong to elliptical shape, an acute apex, and finely serrated margins. The leaf blades typically measure between five and twelve centimeters in length and possess distinct pinnate venation. This species features woody stems that develop a textured bark as the tree matures. The reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into winged samaras. These paired seeds are designed for wind dispersal, which is a common trait among members of the maple genus.
