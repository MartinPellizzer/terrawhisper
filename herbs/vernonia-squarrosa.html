Vernonia squarrosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Vernonia squarrosa?

Vernia squarrosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its placement in the order Asterales, the plant is further organized into the family Asteraceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its membership in the genus Vernonia, with the specific epithet squarrosa designating the individual species.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vernonia squarrosa has an upright, herbaceous growth habit characterized by slender, branching stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 100 centimeters. The leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem and possess an oblong to lanceolate shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. Each leaf features a prominent central midvein and a smooth, somewhat scabrous texture on the surface. The inflorescence consists of numerous small, discoid flower heads arranged in terminal clusters. These flowers display a pale purple to lavender color and are supported by involucral bracts that are often spreading or squarrose in appearance.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the eastern and central United States, ranging from southern Ontario and New York through the Midwest and down to the Gulf Coast. It primarily thrives in open woodlands, prairies, and rocky slopes across several states including Ohio, Indiana, and Missouri. Populations are frequently found in the limestone glades and calcareous soils of the Ozark Plateau. Its range extends southward into parts of Texas and Mississippi where suitable habitats exist.
