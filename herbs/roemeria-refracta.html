Roemeria refracta

What's the taxonomical classification of Roemeria refracta?

Roemeria refracta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is situated in the order Ranunculales. It is further categorized under the family Papaveraceae, which places it within the genus Roemeria, specifically identifying it as the species refracta.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, and various inflammatory conditions. Its medicinal properties are primarily utilized to soothe irritation and promote the healing of damaged tissue. Local healers often apply preparations from the plant to manage surface ailments. The bioactive compounds within the leaves are thought to possess antimicrobial qualities. These applications help reduce swelling and prevent further complications in minor injuries.
