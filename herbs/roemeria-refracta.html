Roemeria refracta

What's the taxonomical classification of Roemeria refracta?

Roemeria refracta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is situated in the order Ranunculales. It is further categorized under the family Papaveraceae, which places it within the genus Roemeria, specifically identifying it as the species refracta.

What are the therapeutic actions of Roemeria refracta?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, and various inflammatory conditions. Its medicinal properties are primarily utilized to soothe irritation and promote the healing of damaged tissue. Local healers often apply preparations from the plant to manage surface ailments. The bioactive compounds within the leaves are thought to possess antimicrobial qualities. These applications help reduce swelling and prevent further complications in minor injuries.

What are the herbal preparations of Roemeria refracta?

Roemeria refracta is prepared primarily as a decoction or an infusion made from its leaves and stems to treat various ailments. The plant material is typically boiled in water to extract its active medicinal compounds for internal consumption. In some traditional practices, the crushed leaves are applied topically as a poultice to address skin irritations or local inflammation. These different methods of preparation allow the specific chemical constituents of the plant to be utilized for their intended therapeutic effects. Practitioners adjust the concentration of the liquid depending on the severity of the condition being treated.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Roemeria refracta can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and localized inflammation if the plant's sap comes into direct contact with human tissue. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, many species within the Euphorbiaceae family contain irritating compounds. These substances often trigger redness, itching, or a burning sensation on the skin upon contact. If the plant is accidentally ingested, it may lead to gastrointestinal distress such as nausea or vomiting. Proper precautions should be taken when handling the plant to avoid accidental exposure to these potential irritants.
