Pedicularis striata

What's the taxonomical classification of Pedicularis striata?

Pedicularis striata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Lamiales, this plant is a member of the family Orobanchaceae. Finally, its taxonomic identity is completed by its placement in the genus Pedicularis, specifically under the species striata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pedicularis striata has a perennial life cycle characterized by an erect stem that typically reaches heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and is accompanied by narrow, linear to lanceolate leaves. These leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem and feature serrated margins. The inflorescence consists of a dense terminal spike containing tubular, bilabiate flowers that range in color from pink to deep purple. Each flower possesses a prominent upper lip that is hooded and a smaller, three-lobed lower lip. The plant also develops a fibrous root system that helps anchor it in its subalpine or alpine habitats.
