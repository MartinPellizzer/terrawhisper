Collabium chinense

What's the taxonomical classification of Collabium chinense?

Collabium chinense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Asparagales. As a member of the family Orchidaceae, this plant is identified by the genus Collabium and the specific species designation chinense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Collabium chinense has large, heart-shaped leaves that are bright green and grow from a thick, fleshy rhizome. These leaves possess long petioles that emerge directly from the underground stem to support the broad blades. The plant typically reaches a height of several dozen centimeters depending on its growing environment. Small, inconspicuous flowers may appear, often tucked beneath the dense canopy of foliage. The entire structure is designed to thrive in moist, shaded habitats where its wide leaves can maximize light absorption.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant, commonly known as Chinese cabbage, requires well-draining soil with a pH between 6.0 and 7.5 and consistent moisture to thrive.

Sowing seeds is most effective in early spring or late summer to avoid the intense heat of midsummer. Regular applications of nitrogen-rich fertilizer are necessary to promote the development of dense, compact heads. Maintaining a consistent watering schedule prevents the heads from splitting or becoming bitter due to stress.

Providing full sunlight for at least six to eight hours daily ensures optimal growth and nutrient accumulation.
