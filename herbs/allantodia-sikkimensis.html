What is Allantodia sikkimensis?

It is a terrestrial fern with a rhizome that is short, creeping, and covered with scales, giving rise to a frond that is bipinnate, 10-30 cm long, with the pinnae being lance-shaped to elliptical, 2-10 cm long, and the rachis being glabrous. The frond's base is often cuneate and the petiole is 1-2 cm long, glabrous, and has a blackish-brown color. The sori are linear, 1-2 mm long, and positioned on the adaxial surface of the pinnae.
