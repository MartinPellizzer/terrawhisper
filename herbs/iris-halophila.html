What's the taxonomical classification of Iris halophila?

Iris halophila belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Asparagales. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues into the family Iridaeceae, where it is placed within the genus Iris, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species halophilat.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Iris halophila has sword-shaped, grey-green leaves that grow in a fan-like arrangement from a thick, creeping rhizome. The plant produces elegant flowers characterized by three upright falls and three spreading standards, typically displaying shades of pale blue or violet with yellow signals. The flowering stems are sturdy and erect, rising above the dense foliage to support the blossoms. These blooms feature delicate veining and intricate patterns on the falls that guide pollinators toward the center.
