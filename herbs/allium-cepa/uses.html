Common Onion (Allium cepa) Uses

Common onion is used to alleviate symptoms of respiratory conditions such as hay fever, sinusitis, and allergic rhinitis. It helps reduce mucus congestion and supports the immune system against colds and respiratory tract infections. Its antiviral and anti-inflammatory properties make it useful for treating coughs and bronchitis.

Common Onion (Allium cepa) Monograph

1. Hay fever

Common onion is used to alleviate hay fever symptoms due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion and sneezing by supporting the body's immune response. Consuming raw onion or using it in steam inhalations can provide relief. Regular use may help manage seasonal allergies effectively.

2. Sinusitis

Common onion is used to alleviate sinusitis symptoms due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion and mucus production. Steam inhalation with onion can soothe sinus pressure and clear nasal passages. It is often used in traditional remedies for its natural decongestant effects.

3. Common cold

Common onion is used to alleviate symptoms of the common cold due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion and soothe sore throats when consumed as a tea or applied topically. Its ability to boost immune function makes it a valuable remedy during cold season.

4. Bronchitis

Common onion is used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health, particularly in cases of bronchitis. Its antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties help reduce mucus production and soothe inflamed airways. It can be consumed as a tea or applied topically to ease symptoms. Regular use may aid in clearing congestion and promoting easier breathing.

5. Cough

Common onion is used to alleviate cough symptoms due to its expectorant properties, which help loosen mucus in the respiratory tract. It contains compounds like sulfur compounds and quercetin that reduce inflammation and soothe irritation. Onion can be consumed raw, cooked, or as a syrup to support respiratory health. It is often recommended as a natural remedy for mild to moderate coughs.

6. Allergic rhinitis

Common onion is used in herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistaminic properties. It contains compounds like quercetin and sulfur compounds that help reduce nasal congestion and inflammation. Regular consumption or application of onion juice may support immune function and ease seasonal allergy symptoms.

7. Mucus congestion

Common onion is used to relieve mucus congestion due to its expectorant properties. It helps loosen and expel mucus from the respiratory tract. The sulfur compounds in onion have anti-inflammatory effects, reducing irritation and swelling. Regular consumption of onion can support respiratory health and ease symptoms of colds or sinus issues.

8. Rhinosinusitis

Common onion (Allium cepa) is used in herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms of rhinosinusitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion and mucus production, often prepared as a nasal drops or steam inhalation. Its expectorant action supports clearing sinus passages, making it a traditional remedy for sinus infections.

9. Respiratory tract infections

Common onion is used to support respiratory health due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It can help alleviate symptoms of colds, coughs, and bronchitis when consumed as a tea or added to meals. Its sulfur compounds may reduce mucus production and soothe irritated airways. Regular use may enhance immune response and aid in clearing respiratory infections.

10. Colds

Common onion is used to alleviate cold symptoms due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce congestion and soothe sore throats when consumed raw or in teas. The sulfur compounds in onions also support immune function, making it a valuable remedy during colds.
