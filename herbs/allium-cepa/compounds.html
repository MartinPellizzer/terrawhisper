Common Onion (Allium cepa) Compounds

The primary compounds in common onion include essential oil, which contributes to its aroma and antimicrobial properties. Flavonoids and phenolic acids provide antioxidant benefits, while saponins and tannins support digestive health. Glycosides and terpenoids have anti-inflammatory effects, and compounds like coumarin and anthocyanin contribute to its medicinal value.

Common Onion (Allium cepa) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Common Onion (Allium cepa) contains bioactive compounds like sulfur-containing volatile oils. These oils contribute to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. They are often used in herbal medicine for respiratory support and as a natural preservative. The oil is extracted through distillation or cold pressing.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Common Onion (Allium cepa). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and respiratory health. These compounds help neutralize free radicals and may enhance immune function.

3. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Common Onion (Allium cepa). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Saponins may support digestive health and help reduce cholesterol levels. These actions make saponin a valuable component in herbal medicine.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Common Onion (Allium cepa). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports the plant's traditional use in promoting respiratory health and reducing inflammation.

5. Tannin

Tannin in Common Onion (Allium cepa) contributes to its astringent properties. It helps reduce inflammation and may support digestive health. Tannins in onions also act as antioxidants, protecting cells from damage. This compound is commonly found in the skin and outer layers of the onion.

6. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Common Onion (Allium cepa) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds possess antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. They are responsible for the plant's characteristic odor and may support respiratory and digestive health. Terpenoids are found in various parts of the onion plant.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Common Onion (Allium cepa). It contributes to the plant's characteristic pungency and has antimicrobial properties. Glycosides in onions may support respiratory health and have mild expectorant effects. They are part of the plant's natural defense mechanisms.

8. Coumarin

Coumarin is a bioactive compound found in Common Onion (Allium cepa). It contributes to the plant's characteristic scent and has mild anticoagulant properties. Coumarin may support cardiovascular health and has been studied for its potential anti-inflammatory effects. Its presence in Allium cepa highlights the plant's role in traditional herbal medicine.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Common Onion (Allium cepa). It contributes to the plant's characteristic odor and may have mild antimicrobial properties. While not the primary active component, alkaloids are part of the complex mixture responsible for some therapeutic effects in traditional herbal use.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Common Onion (Allium cepa). It contributes to the onion's deep color and has antioxidant properties that may support cardiovascular health and reduce inflammation. This compound is part of the plant's natural defense system and is increasingly studied for its potential therapeutic benefits.
