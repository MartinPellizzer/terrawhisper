What's the taxonomical classification of Dendrobium lindleyi?

Dendrobium lindleyi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Asparagales. This plant is a member of the family Orchidaceae, falling under the specific genus Dendrobium, and is identified by its unique species designation, lindleyi.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dendrobium lindleyi has elongated, reed-like pseudobulbs that grow upright and are characterized by numerous nodes and a somewhat compressed, cylindrical shape. The foliage is typically distributed toward the upper portions of the pseudobulbs, providing a lush appearance to the plant. During its flowering period, the plant produces small, fragrant blossoms that emerge from the nodes.
