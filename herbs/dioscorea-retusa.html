Wild Yam (Dioscorea Retusa)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Dioscorea retusa.

Wild yam, scientifically known as Dioscorea retusa, is a medicinal herb native to tropical and subtropical regions, valued for its potential health benefits and traditional use in various cultures. It is often recognized as an adaptogen, helping the body manage stress and maintain balance, and is also used as a spice in some cuisines due to its distinctive, slightly sweet and nutty flavor. Traditionally, it has been used in indigenous medicine to support hormonal balance, alleviate menstrual discomfort, and enhance vitality, with historical records showing its use in Ayurvedic and Chinese herbal practices. In modern wellness, wild yam is gaining attention for its potential role in supporting reproductive health and as a natural alternative to synthetic hormone therapies. Its unique feature lies in its high concentration of diosgenin, a rare compound that has been studied for its potential to support the body's natural hormone production.
