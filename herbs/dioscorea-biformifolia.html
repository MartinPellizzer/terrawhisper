Dioscorea biformifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Dioscorea biformifolia?

Dioscorea biformifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Dioscoreales and is a member of the family Dioscoreaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Dioscorea and is specifically designated as the species biformifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dioscorea biformifolia has climbing, herbaceous stems that emerge from a tuberous rootstock and produce heart-shaped or broadly ovate leaves with entire margins. The foliage typically features distinct venation and can grow in dense, vine-like mats across forest floors or up supporting vegetation. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in clusters, often appearing in shades of cream or pale green during the blooming season. The plant's structure is characterized by its ability to rapidly extend through its environment using twining stems. This morphology allows the species to compete effectively for sunlight within its native tropical habitats.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of West and Central Africa, spanning from Senegal in the west to Nigeria and extending into the Congo Basin. It thrives primarily within the humid environments of lowland rainforests and moist secondary forests. Within these habitats, the species is often found in shaded areas where soil moisture remains consistent. Its distribution is closely tied to the specific climatic conditions provided by the Guineo-Congolian forest block.
