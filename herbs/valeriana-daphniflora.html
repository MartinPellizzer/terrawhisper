Valeriana daphniflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Valeriana daphniflora?

Valeriana daphniflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant falls under the order Dipsacales and is a member of the family Caprifoliaceae. Finally, it is defined by its specific genus, Valeriaana, and its unique species designation, daphniflora.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Valeriana daphniflora contains various chemical constituents including valerenic acids, iridoids, and essential oils that contribute to its biological activity. These compounds function primarily through the modulation of neurotransmitters in the central nervous system. The presence of sesquiterpenes and alkaloids provides the plant with specific sedative and anxiolytic properties. Research into these metabolites suggests potential applications for managing sleep disorders and nervous tension. These medicinal components work together to produce a holistic calming effect on the human body.
