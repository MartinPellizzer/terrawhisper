Valeriana daphniflora

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Valeriana daphniflora has opposite, lanceolate leaves with serrated margins and terminal clusters of small, tubular, white to pale pink flowers. The stems are typically erect and can reach varying heights depending on the specific habitat. Its foliage often displays a textured surface due to fine pubescence. The inflorescence consists of dense cymes that emerge from the upper axils of the plant. This species is characterized by its delicate floral structure and distinct leaf arrangement.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, specifically spanning parts of Nepal, Bhutan, and northern India. It typically thrives in subalpine scrub and forest margins at elevations ranging from 3,000 to 4,500 meters. The species is often found in moist, shaded areas near mountain streams or within alpine meadows. Its distribution is closely tied to the specific microclimates provided by the rugged Himalayan topography.
