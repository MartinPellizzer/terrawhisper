Bischofia polycarpa

What's the taxonomical classification of Bischofia polycarpa?

Bischofia polycarpa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Malpighiales and falls within the family Phyllanthaceae. Ultimately, its specific taxonomic identification is defined by the genus Bischofia and the species polycarpa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bischofiia polycarpa has an evergreen woody habit characterized by spreading branches and elliptical to oblong leaves that measure approximately 5 to 15 centimeters in length. The leaf margins are typically entire or slightly wavy, while the venation pattern is prominent on the underside. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in axillary clusters. The resulting fruits are small drupes that transition in color as they mature. This species often displays a dense canopy structure suited to its tropical forest habitat.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains alpha-linolenic acid, linolenic acid, oleic acid, and palmitic acid as its primary medicinal compounds. These specific fatty acids contribute to the complex chemical profile of the botanical species. The presence of unsaturated fats like oleic acid and alpha-linolenic acid can influence various biological functions. Meanwhile, the saturated component known as palmitic acid plays a role in the overall lipid structure of the plant. Together, these compounds define the therapeutic potential found within the plant's extracts.

According to a study published by "Zhongguo Zhong yao za zhi = Zhongguo zhongyao zazhi = China journal of Chinese materia medica", the main fatty acids contained in Bischofia polycarpam are alpha-linolenic acid, palmitic acid, linolenic acid and oleic acid. These medicinal compounds were analyzed using GC alongside 28 strains of endophytic fungi. There are great differences at the contents of alpha-linolenic acid between the plants and their endophytic fungi. Such differences are suggested to be related to the nutrition absorption and the relationship between the endophytes and the host plant.
