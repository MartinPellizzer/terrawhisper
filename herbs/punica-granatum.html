Medicinal Parts

Punica granatum fruit, commonly known as pomegranate, is one of the most widely used medicinal parts of the plant, valued for its rich content of antioxidants, polyphenols, and essential nutrients.

The fruit's juice and seeds are particularly noted for their anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and cardioprotective properties, making them beneficial in treating conditions like hypertension and diabetes. The seeds, often referred to as arils, contain high levels of tannins and anthocyanins, which contribute to its medicinal value in managing oxidative stress and improving gut health.

In traditional medicine, the leaves of Punica granatum are also utilized for their astringent and antiseptic properties, often prepared as decoctions to treat wounds, skin infections, and gastrointestinal issues. Additionally, the flowers of the plant are sometimes used in herbal remedies for their potential to reduce inflammation and support respiratory health, although their medicinal use is less common compared to the fruit and seeds.

Overall, various parts of Punica granatum offer a range of therapeutic benefits, highlighting the plant's significance in both traditional and modern medicine.
