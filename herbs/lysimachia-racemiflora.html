Lysimachia racemiflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Lysimachia racemiflora?

Lysimachia racemiflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is classified under the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Ericales. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the family Primulaceae, placing it within the genus Lysimachia, and it is specifically identified by its species name, racemiflora.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lysimachia racemiflora has upright, unbranched stems that reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters and bear opposite, lanceolate leaves with entire margins. The foliage is typically bright green and tapers to a sharp point at the apex. During the flowering season, long, slender racemes emerge from the leaf axils to display numerous small, white, star-shaped flowers. Each individual blossom features five distinct petals that surround a central cluster of yellow stamens. This perennial herb often spreads through underground rhizomes to form dense colonial patches in moist habitats.
