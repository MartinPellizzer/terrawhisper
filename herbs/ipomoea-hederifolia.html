Ipomoea hederifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Ipomoea hederifolia?

Ipomoea hederifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is part of the order Solanales and falls within the family Convolvulaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its genus, Ipomoea, and its specific species designation, hederifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ipomoea hederifolia has trailing, perennial stems that produce heart-shaped or ivy-like leaves with three to five shallowly lobed margins. The foliage is characterized by a glossy, dark green texture and can grow up to several inches in length. Small, trumpet-shaped flowers emerge in shades of pale blue or lavender, often featuring a white throat. These blossoms are typically accompanied by delicate, slender pedicels that extend from the leaf axils. The plant's spreading growth habit allows it to form dense groundcovers through its creeping vines.
