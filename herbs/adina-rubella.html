Adina rubella

What's the taxonomical classification of Adina rubella?

Adina rubella belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Gentianaales. This plant is further categorized into the family Rubiaceae, which is the group to which its genus, Adina, is assigned. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by its species name, rubella.

    What are the herbal preparations of Adina rubella?

    Adina rubella is prepared as decoctions, infusions, powders, or topical ointments depending on the intended therapeutic application. The dried roots and stems are commonly boiled in water to create a potent decoction for internal use. For more delicate parts of the plant, a hot water infusion allows for a gentler extraction of active compounds. Some practitioners grind the dried material into a fine powder to be mixed with honey or water. Additionally, the plant can be infused into oils to produce soothing topical preparations for skin ailments.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Adina rubella can cause localized skin irritation, redness, and swelling upon direct contact with its sap or foliage. Individuals with sensitive skin may experience a more severe allergic reaction known as contact dermatitis. This dermatological response often includes itching and small bumps around the site of exposure. Some users might also report mild respiratory discomfort if the plant's pollen or dust is inhaled in large quantities. It is important to handle the plant with gloves to minimize these physiological risks.
