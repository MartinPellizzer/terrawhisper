Actinidia trichogyna

What's the taxonomical classification of Actinidia trichogyna?

Actinidia trichogyna belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Ericales and belongs to the family Actinidiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified within the genus Actinidia, specifically as the species trichogyna.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Actinidia trichogyna can cause allergic skin reactions, localized irritation, or gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Because it belongs to the Actinidiaceae family, certain individuals may experience contact dermatitis upon touching the plant's surfaces. Ingestion of unknown wild berries often leads to nausea, stomach cramping, or vomiting in sensitive people. There is also a potential for respiratory sensitivity if pollen or fine particles are inhaled. It is essential to exercise caution and consult a professional before any physical contact or consumption.
