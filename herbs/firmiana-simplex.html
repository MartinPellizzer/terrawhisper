Firmiana simplex

What's the taxonomical classification of Firmiana simplex?

Firmiana simplex belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the order Malvales and belongs to the family Malvaceae. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Firmiana and the species simplex.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Firmiana simplex contains various bioactive compounds including flavonoids, triterpenoids, saponins, and tannins that contribute to its therapeutic potential. These chemical constituents are primarily concentrated in the seeds, leaves, and bark of the plant. Research indicates that these substances possess significant antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. Some studies also suggest that the extracts may exhibit hepatoprotective and anti-tumor activities in laboratory settings. Because of these diverse chemical profiles, the plant is frequently utilized in traditional medicine to treat various ailments.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including dysentery, malaria, fever, and inflammatory conditions of the skin. The medicinal properties are often derived from the bark, leaves, or seeds of the species. Practitioners frequently utilize its components to address digestive issues and stomach pains. It also shows promise in traditional remedies for managing skin infections and topical wounds. These applications highlight the plant's role in diverse ethnobotanical practices.
