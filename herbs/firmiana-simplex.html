Firmiana simplex

What's the taxonomical classification of Firmiana simplex?

Firmiana simplex belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the order Malvales and belongs to the family Malvaceae. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Firmiana and the species simplex.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Firmiana simplex has palmately lobed leaves that are typically divided into three to five distinct, large segments with serrated margins. These leaves grow on woody branches and can reach significant sizes due to their expansive surface area. The plant produces large, campanulate flowers that are often pale yellow or cream-colored, appearing in clusters. Following the flowering stage, it develops distinctive woody capsules that split open to reveal winged seeds. These seeds are designed for wind dispersal, allowing the plant to propagate effectively across its habitat.

What are the pharmacological activities of Firmiana simplex?

This plant has antioxidant, antibacterial, antimicrobial, antigenotoxic, and anti-neuroinflammatory pharmacological activities that contribute to its diverse therapeutic potential. The antioxidant properties help neutralize free radicals, thereby protecting cells from oxidative stress and damage. Its antimicrobial and antibacterial actions allow it to effectively inhibit the growth of various pathogenic microorganisms. Furthermore, the antigenotoxic effects suggest a capability to prevent or repair genetic damage caused by harmful agents. Finally, the anti-neuroinflammatory activity provides a mechanism for reducing inflammation within the nervous system.

According to a study published by "Chemical & pharmaceucticl bulletin", the 80% MeOH extract of Firmiana simplex was used to isolate three new lignan glycosides (1-3) and six known lignan glycosides (4-9). These compounds were tested for anti-neuroinflammatory effects by evaluating nitric oxide (NO) production in the lipopolysaccharide-activated microglia cell line, BV-2. Among the isolated compounds, compound 7 showed a marginal inhibition of NO levels. This inhibitory activity was measured with an IC50 value of 59.83 µM.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse profile of bioactive medicinal compounds including flavonoids, lignan glycosides, and phenolic acids. These chemical constituents contribute to the plant's significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The specific presence of flavonoids helps in neutralizing free radicals within biological systems. Additionally, the lignan glycosides and phenolic acids play crucial roles in various therapeutic activities observed in traditional medicine. Together, these compounds form a complex chemical matrix that supports the plant's medicinal utility.

According to a study published by "The Journal of pharmacy and pharmacology", the secondary metabolites of Firmiana simplex fruits include phenolic acids and flavonoids. The ethyl acetate fraction yielded the highest polyphenol content at 314.61 mg GAE/g extract, followed by dichloromethane at 297.51, butanol at 153.75, methanol at 101.47, and water extracts at 97.19. In the DPPH assay, the most active ethyl acetate fraction showed an IC50 value of 6.79 μg/ml compared to 2.92 μg/ml for the ascorbic acid standard. Additionally, the phenol-rich ethyl acetate extract demonstrates antigenotoxiic potential in Hep-G2 human liver cancer cells treated with H2O2. These findings suggest that the fruits of Firmiana simplex serve as a natural source of antioxidant and antigenotoxiic agents.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating infections caused by Escherichia coli, Salmonella typhi, Shigella dysenteriae, and Staphylococcus aureus, as well as managing liver cancer. Its medicinal properties include potent antimicrobial actions that target these specific pathogenic bacteria. Researchers have investigated its bioactive compounds for their ability to inhibit the growth of various harmful microbes. Furthermore, certain extracts from the plant have demonstrated potential in combating the progression of liver cancer cells. These diverse therapeutic applications highlight the plant's significant pharmacological importance in traditional and modern medicine.

According to a study published by "Phytochemical analysis : PCA", Firmiana simplex barks show high inhibitory activities against Staphylococcus aureus, Shigella dysenteriae, and Escherichia coli strains. These activities were measured with minimum inhibitory concentrations (MICs) of 1.56, 0.78, and 0.39 mg/mL, respectively. Additionally, Salmonella typhi was highly sensitive to Firmiana simplex barks with an inhibitory diameter up to 21.67 ± 0.95 mm and an MIC at 0.78 mg/mL. The bark of this plant also possessed the highest total phenolic contents (TPCs) among the tested extracts. Consequently, Firmiana simplex bark could be supplied as a potential source of antimicrobial agents.
