Firmiana simplex

What's the taxonomical classification of Firmiana simplex?

Firmiana simplex belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the order Malvales and belongs to the family Malvaceae. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Firmiana and the species simplex.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Firmiana simplex has palmately lobed leaves that are typically divided into three to five distinct, large segments with serrated margins. These leaves grow on woody branches and can reach significant sizes due to their expansive surface area. The plant produces large, campanulate flowers that are often pale yellow or cream-colored, appearing in clusters. Following the flowering stage, it develops distinctive woody capsules that split open to reveal winged seeds. These seeds are designed for wind dispersal, allowing the plant to propagate effectively across its habitat.

What are the pharmacological activities of Firmiana simplex?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological potential through its documented antioxidant, antimicrobial, antibacterial, antigenotoxic, and anti-neuroinflammatory activities. The antioxidant properties of Firmiana simplex help mitigate oxidative stress by neutralizing free radicals within biological systems. Its antimicrobial and antibacterial capabilities allow it to effectively inhibit the growth of various pathogenic microorganisms. Furthermore, the plant exhibits antigenotoxic effects that protect genetic material from damage caused by harmful agents. Finally, its anti-neuroinflammatory action suggests a therapeutic role in reducing inflammation within the central nervous system.

As discussed in a study, the 80% MeOH extract of Firmiana simplex was used to isolate and identify three new lignan glycosides (1-3) and six known lignan glycosides (4-9). The anti-neuroinflammatory effects of these isolated compounds (1-9) were investigated by evaluating nitric oxide (NO) production in the lipopolysaccharide-activated microglia cell line, BV-2. Among these, compound 7 showed a marginal inhibition of NO levels. This specific inhibitory activity was measured with an IC50 value of 59.83 µM.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains flavonoids, lignan glycosides, and phenolic acids that contribute to its therapeutic properties. These specific medicinal compounds work together to provide significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory benefits to the body. The presence of flavonoids helps to neutralize free radicals and protect cellular structures from oxidative stress. Additionally, the lignan glycosides may support hormonal balance and provide protective effects against various ailments. Phenolic acids further enhance the plant's medicinal profile by offering potent antimicrobial and antioxidant activities.

As discussed in a study, the fruits of Firmiana simplex contain phenolic acids and flavonoids as their main polyphenols. The ethyl acetate fraction yielded the highest polyphenol content at 314.61 mg GAE/g extract, followed by dichloromethane at 297.51, butanol at 153.75, methanol at 101.47, and water extracts at 97.19. In the DPPH assay, the most active ethyl acetate fraction showed an IC50 value of 6.79 μg/ml compared to 2.92 μg/ml for the ascorbic acid standard. This phenol-rich ethyl acetate extract also exhibits antigenotoxiic potential in Hep-G2 human liver cancer cells treated with H2O2. Thus, the fruits of Firmiana simplex serve as a natural source of antioxidant and antigenotoxiic agents.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause significant toxicity if ingested, primarily manifesting as severe gastrointestinal distress. Individuals who consume parts of the plant may experience intense nausea and persistent vomiting. The internal irritation often leads to diarrhea and sharp abdominal pain shortly after ingestion. Some people might also suffer from dizziness as the body reacts to the toxins. These symptoms indicate that the plant should be handled with extreme caution to avoid accidental poisoning.
