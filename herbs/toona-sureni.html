Toona sureni

What's the taxonomical classification of Toona sureni?

Toona sureni belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Sapindales and belongs to the family Meliaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Toona, with its specific designation being the species sureni.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Toona sureni contains bioactive compounds in its bark, leaves, and roots that are utilized for various medicinal applications. These diverse parts provide a wide range of therapeutic possibilities in herbal medicine.

What are the herbal preparations of Toona sureni?

Toona sureni is prepared as a decoction of its bark, a powder derived from dried fruits, or an infusion of its leaves to utilize its medicinal properties. Practitioners often boil the bark in water to extract concentrated tannins for topical or internal use. The seeds and fruits are frequently ground into a fine powder that can be mixed into honey or water. Additionally, leaf extracts are sometimes used in liquid form to treat various ailments. These diverse preparations allow for different absorption rates and specific therapeutic applications.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Toona sureni can cause potential adverse reactions such as skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and allergic dermatitis when used improperly or in excessive amounts. While many parts of the tree are used in traditional medicine, the specific toxicological profile of its various extracts is not fully documented in human clinical trials. Some users may experience localized itching or redness if the plant material comes into direct contact with sensitive areas of the skin. Ingesting concentrated forms without professional guidance could lead to stomach discomfort or nausea. It is essential to consult a medical professional before using this plant to avoid unpredictable physiological responses.
