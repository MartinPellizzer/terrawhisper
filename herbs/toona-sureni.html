Toona sureni

What's the taxonomical classification of Toona sureni?

Toona sureni belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Sapindales and belongs to the family Meliaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Toona, with its specific designation being the species sureni.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Toona sureni contains bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, tannins, saponins, and terpenoids that contribute to its therapeutic properties. These chemical constituents work together to exhibit significant antioxidant, antimicrobial, and anti-inflammatory activities in biological systems. Research indicates that the presence of specific phenolic compounds helps mitigate oxidative stress by scavenging free radicals. Additionally, the plant's extract has demonstrated potential in managing various inflammatory conditions through its diverse molecular profile. Such pharmacological diversity makes this species a subject of interest for developing new herbal medicines.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat inflammatory conditions, skin infections, digestive issues, and certain types of bacterial or fungal ailments. The bark and leaves contain bioactive compounds that exhibit significant antimicrobial and antioxidant properties. These medicinal qualities make it useful in managing wound healing and reducing swelling in various parts of the body. Traditional healers often employ extracts from the plant to alleviate symptoms of gastrointestinal distress. Research continues to investigate its potential efficacy against specific pathogens and chronic inflammatory disorders.
