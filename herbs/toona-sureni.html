What's the taxonomical classification of Toona sureni?

Toona sureni belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Sapindales and belongs to the family Meliaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Toona, with its specific designation being the species sureni.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

What are the pharmacological activities of Toona sureni?

This plant has diverse pharmacological activities including antiviral properties and heme polymerization inhibition activity. These bioactive compounds suggest that the species may play a role in managing infectious diseases and preventing oxidative stress. The ability to inhibit heme polymerization specifically points toward potential applications in treating conditions like sickle cell anemia. Researchers are investigating these specific mechanisms to better understand how the plant's chemical constituents interact with biological targets. Ongoing studies aim to isolate the exact molecules responsible for these therapeutic effects.

As discussed in a study, Toona sureni leaves (TSL) exhibited antiviral activities against the hepatitis C virus. The TSL ethanol extracts demonstrated 50% inhibitory concentrations (IC50) of 13.9 μg/ml against the HCV J6/JFH1-P47 strain and 2.0 μg/ml against the -P1 strain. Time-of-addition experiments revealed that TSL inhibited the virus at both the entry and post-entry steps. Furthermore, TSL inhibited all of 11 HCV strains of all the genotypes tested to the same extent. These findings suggest that TSL may be a good candidate for the development of anti-HCV drugs.

These specific steroidal constituents are often studied for their potential bioactive properties in pharmacological research. The presence of these phytosterols contributes to the overall chemical profile and therapeutic value of the species. Researchers analyze these molecular structures to understand how they interact with biological systems. Detailed chemical profiling helps identify how these specific substances might assist in various medicinal applications.

As discussed in a study, the medicinal plant Toona sureni contains an endophytic fungus Periconiya pseudobyssoides K5 which was used in a fermentation process at 28 ± 2 °C for 30 days. This process isolated a new ergostane-type steroid named (22E)-3α,6α,9α-ergosta-7,22-diene-3,6,9-triol (1) along with six known steroids (2) 5α,8α-epidoxy-24-ethyl-cholest-6-en-3β-ol, (3) ergosterol-5,8-peroxiide, (4) cerevisterol, (5) isocyathisterol, (6) 6β-hydroxystigmast-4-en-3-one, and (7) 6β-hydroxy-4-campesten-3-one. These isolated compounds 1-7 were evaluated for heme polymerization inhibition assay activity. The IC50 HPIA value of compound 1 was found to be 8.24 ± 0.03 mg/ml.
