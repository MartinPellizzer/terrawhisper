What's the taxonomical classification of Setaria megaphylla?

Setaria megaphylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the order Poales and the family Poaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Setaria, and its unique species designation, megaphylla.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Setaria megaphylla has an erect growth habit characterized by a densely branched, caespitose structure with slender, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The leaves are linear to lanceolate in shape, featuring a prominent midrib and fine hairs along the margins and surfaces. Its inflorescence consists of a contracted, cylindrical panicle that is typically bristly and often displays a purplish or reddish pigmentation. The plant produces numerous small, ovoid seeds that are encased in a hard pericarp, aiding in its efficient dispersal. This morphology allows the species to thrive aggressively in disturbed tropical and subtropical environments.
