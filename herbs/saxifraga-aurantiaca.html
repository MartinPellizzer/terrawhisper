Saxifraga aurantiaca

What's the taxonomical classification of Saxifraga aurantiaca?

Saxifraga aurantiaca belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Saxifragales. The plant is further categorized under the family Saxifragaceae, which places it within the genus Saxifraga. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by its species designation, aurantica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saxifraga aurantiaca has a basal rosette of spatulate to obovate leaves that are typically fleshy, slightly serrated along the margins, and covered in fine hairs.

These leaves grow tightly together to form a compact mound near the ground. From the center of this rosette, a slender, upright flowering stem emerges to support small clusters of blossoms. The flowers are characterized by five distinct petals that often exhibit a pale orange or yellowish hue.

This coloration is a defining morphological trait that distinguishes the species from other members of its genus.
