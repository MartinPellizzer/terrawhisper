What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saxifraga aurantiaca has a basal rosette of spatulate to obovate leaves that are typically fleshy, slightly serrated along the margins, and covered in fine hairs. These leaves grow tightly together to form a compact mound near the ground. From the center of this rosette, a slender, upright flowering stem emerges to support small clusters of blossoms. The flowers are characterized by five distinct petals that often exhibit a pale orange or yellowish hue.
