What is Escallonia pulverulenta?

Escallonia pulverulenta is a species of flowering plant in the family Escalloniaceae, native to the Andes mountains in South America.

It is an evergreen shrub or small tree, typically growing 2-5 meters tall, with a spreading habit and a grayish-brown bark. The leaves are lanceolate to elliptical, 2-7 centimeters long, dark green above and pale green below, with a leathery texture and a serrated margin. The flowers are white, 2-3 centimeters in diameter, and borne in axillary clusters, consisting of 4-5 petals and numerous stamens.

The fruits are small, green drupes, 1-2 centimeters long, containing a single seed.
