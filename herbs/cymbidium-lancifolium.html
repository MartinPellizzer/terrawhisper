Cymbidium lancifolium

What's the taxonomical classification of Cymbidium lancifolium?

Cymbidium lancifoliuum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is positioned in the order Asparagales. This plant is a member of the family Orchidaceae, falling under the genus Cymbidium, and is specifically identified by its species name, lancifoliuum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cymbidium lancifolium has lanceolate, leathery leaves that grow from upright pseudobulbs and produce arching inflorescences bearing numerous fragrant, waxy flowers. The leaves are characterized by their elongated, pointed shape and a distinctively smooth texture. These plants feature thickened stems known as pseudobulbs which serve to store water and nutrients for the orchid. The flowers typically exhibit a combination of pale green or yellowish hues with intricate spotting on the labellum. Each flowering spike can hold multiple blossoms that emerge in a graceful, cascading manner.
