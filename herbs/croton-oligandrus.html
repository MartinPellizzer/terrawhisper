Croton oligandrus has woody stems and simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptic to oblong in shape with entire or slightly wavy margins.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Croton oligandrus can cause severe skin irritation, blistering, and inflammation if the sap or foliage comes into direct contact with human tissue. This plant belongs to a genus known for containing diterpene esters which act as potent irritants to the mucous membranes and skin. If the plant material is accidentally ingested, it may lead to gastrointestinal distress, including nausea and intense abdominal pain. Protective clothing should always be worn when handling this species to avoid accidental exposure.
