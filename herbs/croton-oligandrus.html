What's the taxonomical classification of Croton oligandrus?

Croton oligandrus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its evolutionary lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Malpighiales. As a member of the family Euphorbiaceae, it is further categorized under the genus Croton, with its specific identity defined by the species name oligandrus.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Croton oligandrus can cause severe skin irritation, blistering, and inflammation if the sap or foliage comes into direct contact with human tissue. This plant belongs to a genus known for containing diterpene esters which act as potent irritants to the mucous membranes and skin. If the plant material is accidentally ingested, it may lead to gastrointestinal distress, including nausea and intense abdominal pain. Eye contact with the plant's juices can result in temporary blindness or significant corneal damage. Protective clothing should always be worn when handling this species to avoid accidental exposure.
