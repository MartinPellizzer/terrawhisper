Coluria longifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Coluria longifolia has long, narrow, lanceolate leaves that are arranged oppositely along a tall, erect stem. The foliage is characterized by its elongated shape and smooth margins which extend significantly along the length of the plant. The stems are typically sturdy and can reach considerable heights to support the dense greenery. Small, inconspicuous flowers often emerge from the axils of these elongated leaves. This morphological structure allows the plant to maximize sunlight absorption in its natural habitat.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical regions of West and Central Africa, specifically spanning from Senegal and Guinea through to Uganda. Populations are most frequently documented in areas with consistent rainfall and high humidity levels characteristic of these forest ecosystems. Its presence is often linked to the dense vegetation of the Guineo-Congolian region.
