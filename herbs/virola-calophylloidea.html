Cupuaçu (Virola Calophylloidea)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Virola calophylloidea.

Cupuaçu, scientifically known as Virola calophylloidea, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest, valued for its medicinal and nutritional properties. This fruit-bearing plant is often regarded as a superfood and adaptogen, offering a unique blend of vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants that support overall health and well-being. Traditionally used by indigenous communities in Brazil, Cupuaçu has been employed to treat ailments such as inflammation, digestive issues, and skin conditions, and its pulp is also used in rituals and ceremonies. In modern wellness practices, it is increasingly incorporated into supplements, skincare products, and functional foods due to its high levels of polyphenols and its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. What makes Cupuaçu truly distinctive is its rare active compound, theobromine, similar to that found in chocolate, along with its creamy, sweet flavor and historical significance as a staple in Amazonian culture.
