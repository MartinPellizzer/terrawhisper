Leontopodium artemisiifolium

What's the taxonomical classification of Leontopodium artemisiifolium?

Leontopodium artemisiifolium belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae, which is a large and diverse group of flowering plants. Specifically, it is identified by its genus, Leontopodium, and is uniquely distinguished at the species level as artemisiifolium.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Leontopodium artemisiifolium has lanceolate to linear leaves that are densely covered in fine, silvery-white trichomes, giving the plant a characteristic felt-like texture. The foliage typically exhibits a grayish-green hue due to this thick layer of protective hairs. Its stem structure is relatively low-growing and can vary in thickness depending on the specific environmental conditions. The leaf margins are generally entire, though they may appear slightly wavy under close inspection. These morphological features serve as adaptations to minimize moisture loss in its native habitat.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau, specifically spanning parts of southwestern China, Nepal, Bhutan, and northern India. It thrives primarily in alpine meadows and rocky slopes located at elevations ranging from 3,500 to 5,000 meters above sea level. The species is well-adapted to the extreme cold and intense ultraviolet radiation characteristic of these Himalayan environments. Its distribution is restricted to specific montane ecosystems where the soil is thin and the climate remains frigid. Consequently, finding this species requires searching within the high-mountain corridors of the Eastern Himalayas and the Hengduan Mountains.
