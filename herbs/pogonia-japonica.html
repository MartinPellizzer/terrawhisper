Pogonia japonica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pogonia japonica has an erect, slender stem that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and bears several lanceolate, bright green leaves arranged alternately along its length. The leaves are characterized by prominent parallel venation and a smooth, entire margin. At the apex of the stem, the plant produces a small cluster of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to white. These blossoms feature a distinctively trilobed lip that is often marked with delicate spots or fine lines. The overall structure is delicate and herbaceous, reflecting its preference for moist, shaded forest environments.
