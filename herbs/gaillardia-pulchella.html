Gaillardia pulchella

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gaillardia pulchella has woody-based perennial stems that reach heights of 12 to 24 inches and support heads of composite flowers featuring red centers with yellow ray florets. The foliage consists of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often arranged oppositely along the stems. Each flower head is composed of numerous disk florets surrounded by a ring of petal-like ray florets. These distinctive blooms typically measure between one and two inches in diameter. The plant's structure is compact and bushy, allowing it to thrive in various sunlight conditions.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the southeastern United States, specifically ranging from Texas through the Gulf Coast states and up into the Atlantic coastal plain. It thrives primarily in the warm, humid climates of states such as Florida, Georgia, and South Carolina. While its natural habitat is limited to this southern region, it has been widely introduced to other parts of the world for ornamental use.
