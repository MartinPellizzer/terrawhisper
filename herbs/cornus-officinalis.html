Chinese Cornel (Cornus officinalis): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

What is Chinese Cornel (Cornus Officinalis)?

Chinese Cornel, or Cornus officinalis, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia, widely used in traditional Chinese medicine for its root and fruit, which are valued for their astringent, antiseptic, and anti-inflammatory properties.

Botanical Identity and Classification

Chinese Cornel, scientifically known as Cornus officinalis, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the family Cornaceae, native to East Asia and commonly used in traditional Chinese medicine for its therapeutic properties.

Common Names and Synonyms

Chinese Cornel, also known as Cornus officinalis, is commonly referred to by other names such as Chinese Dogwood, Huanhuan, and Shanzhuyu in traditional Chinese medicine.

Plant Description and Morphology

The Chinese Cornel, or Cornus officinalis, is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its branched, angular stems, opposite leaf arrangement, and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Chinese Cornel (Cornus officinalis) is native to East Asia, primarily found in China, Korea, and Japan, where it thrives in temperate climates and is commonly grown in forested areas and mountainous regions.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The roots, bark, fruit, and seeds of Cornus officinalis are used medicinally in traditional Chinese medicine.

Phytochemical Composition

Chinese Cornel contains a variety of bioactive compounds including triterpenoids, flavonoids, iridoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties and therapeutic effects.

Pharmacological Properties

Chinese Cornel (Cornus officinalis) exhibits pharmacological properties including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, immunomodulatory, and hepatoprotective effects, primarily attributed to its active constituents such as flavonoids, triterpenoids, and iridoids.

Mechanisms of Action

The mechanisms of action of Chinese Cornel (Cornus officinalis) involve its ability to enhance immune function through the stimulation of macrophage activity and the modulation of cytokine production, as well as its antioxidant properties that help neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress in the body.

Therapeutic Uses and Indications

Chinese cornel is used to treat conditions such as diabetes, hypertension, inflammation, diarrhea, jaundice, urinary tract infections, and respiratory disorders.

Preparation Methods and Forms

The preparation methods and forms of Chinese Cornel (Cornus officinalis) include decoctions, powders, tinctures, and dried fruits, with the dried fruit being the most commonly used form in traditional formulations.

Dosage and Administration

The typical dosage of Chinese Cornel (Cornus officinalis) in traditional Chinese medicine ranges from 6 to 12 grams per day, usually prepared as a decoction, with specific adjustments based on individual constitution and condition.

Safety, Side Effects, and Contraindications

When used in excessive amounts or over a prolonged period, Chinese Cornel may cause gastrointestinal discomfort, such as nausea, diarrhea, or vomiting, and may interact with certain medications, particularly those affecting the liver or kidneys.

Drug Interactions

Chinese Cornel may interact with medications that affect the liver or have anticoagulant properties, potentially increasing the risk of bleeding or altering drug metabolism.

Toxicity and Precautions

Chinese Cornel (Cornus officinalis) is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses, but it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals and should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation due to limited safety data.

Cultivation and Harvesting

Cultivation of Chinese Cornel typically involves planting seeds or cuttings in well-drained soil with partial shade, requiring consistent moisture and moderate temperatures for optimal growth.

Processing and Extraction

The processing of Chinese Cornel involves peeling, slicing, and drying the fruit to preserve its medicinal properties and enhance its efficacy in traditional herbal formulations.

Storage and Shelf Life

Chinese Cornel (Cornus officinalis) should be stored in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture, and its shelf life is typically 2 to 3 years when properly preserved.

Scientific Research and Evidence

Scientific research and evidence on Chinese Cornel (Cornus officinalis) have shown its potential in various therapeutic applications, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and hepatoprotective effects, with studies supporting its traditional use in treating conditions such as liver dysfunction and respiratory infections.

Traditional and Historical Uses

Cornus officinalis, also known as Chinese Cornel, has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine for centuries to tonify the liver and kidneys, improve circulation, and treat conditions such as impotence, infertility, and urinary disorders.

Regulatory Status

In China, Chinese Cornel (Cornus officinalis) is listed in the Chinese Pharmacopoeia and is classified as a medicinal material under the traditional Chinese medicine regulatory framework, with specific standards for its identification, quality control, and therapeutic applications.
