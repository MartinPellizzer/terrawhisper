Chinese Cornel (Cornus officinalis): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

Chinese Cornel, also known as Cornus officinalis, is a traditional Chinese medicinal plant widely used for its therapeutic properties in treating various health conditions.

What is Chinese Cornel (Cornus Officinalis)?

Chinese Cornel, also known as Cornus officinalis, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia, commonly used in traditional Chinese medicine for its fruit, which is rich in antioxidants, anti-inflammatory compounds, and has been historically used to treat conditions such as diarrhea, fever, and skin disorders.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Chinese cornel is Cornus officinalis. This plant belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Saxifragales, family Cornaceae, genus Cornus, and species Cornus officinalis. It is commonly known as Chinese cornel in China, and as cornel cherry in North America. It is also known as kousso in parts of Europe and as hokkenden in Japan. Synonyms for this plant include Cornus japonica, Cornus chinensis, and Cornus sessilis.

Plant Description and Morphology

Chinese cornel is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3-6 meters in height, with a rounded or spreading crown and a straight, smooth, gray-brown trunk.

Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a pointed tip and a serrated margin, measuring approximately 5-10 cm in length. The flowers are small, white to pale pink, clustered in panicles, and bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The root system is taprooted with lateral roots, allowing it to anchor firmly in soil and absorb moisture efficiently.

The bark is smooth and thin, the leaves are glossy on top and hairy underneath, and the fruit is a red, fleshy drupe with a sweet, sour aroma.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Chinese Cornel (Cornus officinalis) is native to eastern Asia, specifically found in regions of China, Japan, and Korea, where it thrives in temperate climates and is commonly cultivated for its medicinal properties.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The roots, bark, fruit, and seeds of Chinese Cornel (Cornus officinalis) are used medicinally in traditional Chinese medicine.

Phytochemical Composition

Chinese Cornel contains a variety of bioactive compounds, including triterpenoids, flavonoids, essential oils, and polysaccharides, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Pharmacological Properties

Cornus officinalis exhibits a range of pharmacological properties including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, immunomodulatory, and hepatoprotective effects, primarily attributed to its bioactive compounds such as corilagin, ferulic acid, and rutin.

Mechanisms of Action

Chinese Cornel exerts its therapeutic effects primarily through the regulation of liver function, promotion of blood circulation, and anti-inflammatory actions, which are mediated by its active constituents such as triterpenoids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids.

Therapeutic Uses and Indications

Chinese Cornel, or Cornus officinalis, is traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat conditions such as fever, diarrhea, and bone pain, and is valued for its astringent, antipyretic, and anti-inflammatory properties.

Preparation Methods and Forms

Dosage and Administration

The typical dosage of Chinese Cornel (Cornus officinalis) for herbal use ranges from 6 to 12 grams per day, usually prepared as a decoction, with the exact amount depending on the specific condition being treated and the practitioner's recommendation.

Safety, Side Effects, and Contraindications

When used appropriately, Chinese Cornel (Cornus officinalis) is generally considered safe, but it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort, allergic reactions, or interactions with certain medications, and it should be avoided during pregnancy and by individuals with specific health conditions.

Drug Interactions

Chinese Cornel (Cornus officinalis) may interact with medications that affect the liver or are metabolized by the liver, potentially altering their effectiveness or increasing the risk of side effects.

Toxicity and Precautions

Chinese Cornel is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses, but it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals, and excessive use may lead to toxicity, particularly affecting the liver and kidneys, so it should be used with caution and under professional guidance.

Cultivation and Harvesting

Chinese Cornel is typically cultivated in well-drained soil with partial shade, requiring consistent moisture and moderate temperatures, and is harvested primarily from late autumn to early winter when the fruit reaches full maturity.

Processing and Extraction

Processing and extraction of Chinese Cornel involve methods such as slicing, drying, and decocting the fruit to obtain its active compounds, which are then purified through techniques like maceration or alcohol extraction to maximize therapeutic efficacy.

Storage and Shelf Life

Cornus officinalis should be stored in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture, with a shelf life of up to two years when properly preserved.

Scientific Research and Evidence

Scientific research and evidence on Chinese Cornel (Cornus officinalis) have shown its potential pharmacological activities, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and neuroprotective effects, with studies supporting its traditional use in treating conditions such as diabetes, cardiovascular diseases, and neurological disorders.

Traditional and Historical Uses

Cornus officinalis has been historically used in traditional Chinese medicine for its ability to tonify the liver and kidneys, enhance vitality, and promote blood circulation, often in formulations aimed at treating conditions such as impotence, infertility, and chronic fatigue.

Regulatory Status
