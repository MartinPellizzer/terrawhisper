Cornus officinalis

What's the taxonomical classification of Cornus officinalis?

Cornus officiinalis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it falls under the order Cornales and the family Cornaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Cornus, and its specific species name, officiinalis.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Cornus officinalis contains medicinal compounds primarily located within its dried, prepared fruit and its inner bark. The ripe fruit is the most frequently utilized part, often used in traditional medicine to support kidney and liver functions. The bark can also be harvested to extract astringent properties used in various herbal formulations. These specific plant parts are processed to enhance their bioactive constituents for therapeutic use. Careful preparation of these materials is essential to ensure their efficacy in traditional treatments.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse range of bioactive secondary metabolites including iridoids, flavonoids, phenolic acids, tannins, and triterpenoids that contribute to its medicinal properties.

These chemical constituents work synergistically to provide antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial effects within biological systems. The presence of flavonoids and phenolic acids specifically aids in neutralizing free radicals to reduce oxidative stress. Furthermore, the iridoids and triterpenoids are often associated with various physiological regulating activities.

Tannins also play a significant role by offering astringent qualities that can assist in tissue repair and protection.

The primary medicinal compounds of this plant are shown in the list below.

  • Iridoids
  • Flavonoids
  • Phenolic acids
  • Tannins
  • Triterpenoids

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Cornus officinalis targets cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2), nitric oxide synthase (NOS), and various pro-inflammatory cytokines such as tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-alpha) and interleukin-6 (IL-6) to exert its therapeutic effects. The plant contains bioactive compounds like loganin and cornin that modulate these enzymatic pathways to reduce systemic inflammation. These constituents also interact with the NF-kappaB signaling pathway, effectively inhibiting the transcription of genes involved in the inflammatory response. Furthermore, the phytochemicals in the corni fructus extract demonstrate antioxidant properties by scavenging free radicals and regulating oxidative stress-related proteins. By modulating these specific molecular targets, the plant provides significant neuroprotective and anti-inflammatory benefits.

What are the therapeutic actions of Cornus officinalis?

Cornus officinalis has therapeutic actions primarily focused on tonifying the kidneys, strengthening the liver, and astringing essence to treat symptoms like dizziness, tinnitus, and lower back pain. The plant's bioactive compounds, such as cornusin and loganin, contribute to its ability to stabilize blood sugar and support cardiovascular health. It is frequently utilized in traditional medicine to address issues related to premature aging and reproductive dysfunction by preserving bodily fluids. Additionally, its antioxidant properties help reduce oxidative stress within various organ systems. These combined effects make it a vital component in formulations designed to restore vitality and strengthen the body's foundation.

What common ailments are treated using this plant?

This plant is used to treat digestive issues such as diarrhea and dysentery.

Its astringent properties help to tone the intestinal lining and reduce inflammation during gastrointestinal distress. Additionally, the plant has been traditionally utilized to help lower a fever. These medicinal qualities make it a historical remedy for various stomach ailments.

The bark and fruit are often the primary parts used in these applications.

The main common ailment treated using this plant are shown in the list below.

  • Digestive issues
  • Diarrhea
  • Dysentery
  • Fever

What are the herbal preparations of Cornus officinalis?

Cornus officinalis is prepared primarily as a decoction of the dried fruit, a concentrated tincture made from the pulp, or a medicinal wine infused with the berries. The dried fruits are often simmered in water to extract their astringent properties for use in traditional tea formulations. Tinctures allow for a more potent extraction of the active compounds, making them easier to dose in liquid form. Additionally, the fruit can be processed into syrups or medicinal wines to improve palatability and preserve the essence of the plant. These diverse preparations enable the medicinal components to be utilized effectively for various health applications.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Cornus officiinalis can cause gastrointestinal distress such as nausea, stomach cramps, or diarrhea if consumed in excessive amounts. While the plant is historically used in traditional medicine, its safety profile for long-term internal use is not extensively documented in modern clinical studies. Some individuals may experience mild allergic skin reactions or irritation when coming into direct contact with the plant material. It is important to note that the specific concentrations of active compounds can vary significantly depending on the part of the plant used. Caution is advised for pregnant or breastfeeding individuals due to a lack of sufficient safety data regarding fetal development.
