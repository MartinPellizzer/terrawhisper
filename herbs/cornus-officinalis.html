Cornus officinalis

What's the taxonomical classification of Cornus officinalis?

Cornus officiinalis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it falls under the order Cornales and the family Cornaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Cornus, and its specific species name, officiinalis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Cornus officinalis contains high concentrations of loganic acid, morroniside, cornin, and various flavonoids that provide its therapeutic properties. These bioactive compounds work together to exert significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects within the body. The plant also features specific glycosides that are believed to support kidney function and stabilize metabolic processes. Many researchers focus on these chemical constituents due to their potential to protect against oxidative stress in cellular tissues. Such medicinal components make the species a subject of interest in traditional and modern pharmacological studies.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat digestive ailments such as diarrhea, dysentery, and stomach cramps, as well as inflammatory conditions like sore throats and mouth ulcers. The bark and fruit contain tannins that act as astringents to help reduce swelling and tighten tissues. Historically, it was also employed to manage symptoms of jaundice and various liver disorders. These medicinal properties make it a traditional remedy for gastrointestinal distress and mucosal inflammation.
