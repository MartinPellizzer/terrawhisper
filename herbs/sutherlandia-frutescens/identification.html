Botanical Identification of Cancer Bush (Sutherlandia frutescens)

To identify Cancer Bush (Sutherlandia frutescens) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Cancer Bush (Sutherlandia frutescens) Monograph

Scientific Classification

Cancer Bush, or Sutherlandia frutescens, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Fabales, order Fabales, family Fabaceae, genus Sutherlandia, and species Sutherlandia frutescens. This classification places it within the legume family, highlighting its botanical relationships and characteristics.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The botanical name of Cancer Bush is Sutherlandia frutescens, first described by the botanist George Bentham in 1848. It is also known by several synonyms, including Sutherlandia lortoni and Sutherlandia macrophylla. Common names such as Cancer Bush, Cape Cancer Bush, and Yellow Star are widely used, reflecting its traditional and regional significance.

Morphological Characteristics

Cancer Bush is a perennial shrub with a woody stem and a dense root system. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and arranged in whorls. The plant produces a spike-like inflorescence with small, yellowish-green flowers. The fruit is a capsule containing several seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Cancer Bush can be distinguished by its unique combination of features, including its gray-green, hairy leaves, purple flowers with five lobes, and its distinctive, woody, branching stem. It is often confused with similar species like Sutherlandia frutescens var. glabrata, but the presence of the characteristic hairy leaves and the specific flower structure helps in accurate identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Cancer Bush (Sutherlandia frutescens) is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, Namibia, and Botswana. It thrives in warm, temperate climates with low rainfall. It prefers sandy or loamy soils and is typically found at altitudes between 500 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Cancer Bush blooms from August to October, producing small yellowish flowers. Seed formation occurs from November to February. For identification purposes, the plant is best harvested during its flowering period, typically between August and October, when its distinctive features are most visible.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Cancer Bush (Sutherlandia frutescens) is closely related to species within the genus Sutherlandia. It shares characteristics with other members of the Fabaceae family, such as pod fruits and compound leaves. Its phylogenetic placement highlights its evolutionary ties to legumes, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine.
