Lygodium japonicum

What's the taxonomical classification of Lygodium japonicum?

Lygodium japonicum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is categorized under the subclass Polypodiinae and falls under the order Schizaeales. This plant is a member of the family Schizaeaceae, which contains the genus Lygodium, and is specifically identified by the species name japonicum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lygodium japonicum has deeply divided, tripinnate fronds that typically measure between 10 and 30 centimeters in length and consist of small, oblong to lanceolate pinnules. The plant features a creeping rhizome that allows it to spread through shaded, moist forest environments. Each frond is supported by a slender, somewhat hairy stipe that connects the foliage to the underground stem. The fertile portions of the plant are distinguished by specialized, modified leaflets called sporophylls that house the sporangia. These reproductive structures are often more compact and differently shaped than the sterile vegetative leaflets.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to East Asia, specifically found in countries such as Japan, China, Korea, and Taiwan. It typically thrives in temperate and subtropical regions where moisture is abundant. The species can be found at varying elevations depending on the specific local climate of the Asian mainland. What are the pharmacological activities of Lygodium japonicum?

This plant has significant pharmacological activities including potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. The bioactive compounds within Lygodium japonicum work to inhibit inflammatory mediators that cause swelling and pain in biological tissues. Additionally, its high concentration of phytochemicals helps neutralize harmful free radicals to prevent oxidative stress. These combined effects suggest the plant may have therapeutic potential for treating various inflammatory diseases. Researchers continue to investigate how these specific mechanisms can be applied to modern medicine.

As discussed in a study, Lygodium japonicum has shown antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. These effects are achieved through the activation of antioxidant genes induced by Nrf2 or by suppressing inflammatory gene expression by the inhibition of NFκ-B. The plant's activities could be modulated by bioactive compounds such as polyphenols, flavonoids, tannins, saponins, and terpenes. This information was gathered as part of a scoping review that screened a total of 184 studies. Among the included studies, 1 was from Corea.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse array of medicinal compounds including flavonoids, polyphenols, kaempferol, kaempferol-7-O-alpha-L-rhamnopyranoside, and tricin-7-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside. These bioactive molecules contribute to the plant's complex chemical profile and potential therapeutic properties. The presence of various flavonoids and polyphenols suggests significant antioxidant capabilities within its tissues. Specifically, the glycoside forms such as kaempferol-7-O-alpha-L-rhamnopyranoside and tricin-7-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside play crucial roles in its biological activity. These specific phytochemicals work together to provide the plant with its unique medicinal characteristics.

As discussed in a study, eight compounds were isolated and identified from the herb of Lygodium japonicum using column chromatographic techniques and spectroscopic analysis. These constituents include tilianiin (I), kaempferol-7-O-alpha-L-rhamnopyranoside (II), kaempferol (III), p-coumaric acid (IV), hexadecanoic acid 2, 3-dihydroxy-propyl ester (V), daucosterol (VI), beta-sitosterol (VII), and 1-hentriacontanol (VIII). Compounds I, II, V, VI, VII, and VIII were isolated from Lygodium japonicum for the first time. Additionally, compounds I, II, and V were isolated from the genus Lygodium for the first time.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating urolithiasis, which is a condition characterized by the formation of stones in the urinary tract. It serves as a traditional remedy to help dissolve or pass these painful mineral deposits. In addition to managing kidney stones, it is often utilized to address various inflammatory ailments within the urinary system. Practitioners also apply its properties to alleviate symptoms related to urinary tract infections and discomfort during urination. These medicinal uses are rooted in long-standing ethnobotanical practices.

As discussed in a study, Lygodium japonicum is one of the traditional medicinal plants included among 6 studies selected from a total of 184 studies screened. This plant is used in the management of urolithiasis and demonstrates antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. These effects occur through the activation of antioxidant genes induced by Nrf2 or by suppressing inflammatory gene expression through the inhibition of NFκ-B. The actions of Lygodium japonicum may be modulated by bioactive compounds such as polyphenols, flavonoids, tannins, saponins, and terpenes.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause severe skin irritation, chemical burns, and allergic dermatitis upon direct contact with human tissue. The leaves contain specific irritants that trigger inflammatory responses such as redness, swelling, and intense itching. Some individuals may experience blistering if the sap remains on the skin for an extended period. These dermatological reactions are often accompanied by a burning sensation that can persist for days. Contact with the eyes should be avoided to prevent significant ocular damage and irritation.
