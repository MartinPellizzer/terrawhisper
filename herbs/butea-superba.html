Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba)

Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba) is a member of the Fabaceae family, native to Southeast Asia, Indochina, and Malay Peninsula. Traditionally, its bark, leaves, and stems have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, tonic, and astringent actions, and has a long history of use in ayurvedic medicine, traditional chinese medicine, and japanese kampo medicine.
