Butea superba

What's the taxonomical classification of Butea superba?

Butea superba belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Fabales. As a member of the family Fabaceae, this plant is categorized under the genus Butea, with its specific designation being the species superba.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Butea superba has trifoliate leaves consisting of three leaflets that are typically elliptic to obovate in shape and possess an entire margin. The plant is characterized by its bright orange-yellow flowers that emerge in dense, terminal racemes. Each flower features a distinct papilionaceous structure with a large, prominent standard petal. The woody stems and branches are often covered in a fine pubescence that provides protection. Its fruit is a flat, indehiscent pod that remains on the plant for an extended period.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating depression, various cancers including gastric cancer, erectile dysfunction, and impaired sexual performance. Researchers have explored its bioactive compounds to evaluate their potential in managing psychological distress and mood disorders. In oncology, studies focus on its ability to combat malignant cell growth, specifically targeting gastric cancer. Furthermore, its medicinal properties are investigated for their capacity to improve reproductive health and address sexual dysfunction. These diverse applications highlight the plant's significant pharmacological potential in modern medicine.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", Butea superba is a Thai medicinal plant used as folk medicine to improve physical and mental conditions and to prevent impaired sexual performance in middle-aged or elderly males. It has been reported to improve cognitive deficits and depression-like behavior in olfactory bulbectomized mice, which serve as an animal model for dementia and depression. In experiments involving mice subjected to unpredictable chronic mild stress, the plant was administered at a daily dose of 300 mg of dried herb weight/kg. This treatment attenuated symptoms of anhedoia and depression-like behavior, as well as an elevation of serum corticosterone levels. Furthermore, the administration of Butea superba reversed neurochemical and histological abnormalities, including the UCMS-induced decrease in expression levels of BDNF mRNA, CREB, and a phosphorylated form of NR1.
