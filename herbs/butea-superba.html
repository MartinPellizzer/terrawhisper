Red Silk Cotton Tree

Butea superba

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba) is scientifically known as Butea superba, belonging to the Fabaceae family. Commonly called "Kan Yaow" in Thai, it is a perennial shrub native to Southeast Asia. It has a fibrous root system, trifoliate leaves, and orange-red flowers. It is also known as "Kan Yaow" in Thai and "Kan Yaow" in other regional contexts.

Active Compounds

The Red Silk Cotton Tree contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, enhancing the herb's therapeutic potential in traditional herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Tannin
  • Phenolic acid

System Condidtion Action

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for treating inflammation and digestive issues.

Tincture: Used for pain relief and treating skin infections.

Powder: Used for digestive health and as a general tonic.

Safety Profile

The Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba) is generally considered safe when used as directed. However, it may cause mild gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. Consult a healthcare provider before use, especially if you have existing health conditions or are taking other medications.
