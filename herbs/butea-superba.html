What is Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea Superba)?

The Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba) is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, known for its medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal medicine.

Botanical Identity and Classification

The Red Silk Cotton Tree, or Butea superba, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, belonging to the legume family Fabaceae and is commonly known as the "Kanang" or "Kampu" in Thai.

Common Names and Synonyms

The Red Silk Cotton Tree, or Butea superba, is known by several common names including Red Silk Cotton, Flame Tree, and Indian Torch, reflecting its vibrant floral display and traditional use in herbal medicine.

Plant Description and Morphology

The Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba) is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree characterized by its woody stem, trifoliate leaves with elliptical leaflets, and distinctive red flowers that bloom in clusters, often accompanied by a sweet, spicy fragrance.

Native Habitat and Distribution

The Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba) is native to Southeast Asia, specifically found in countries such as Thailand, Malaysia, Indonesia, and parts of India, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The leaves, flowers, and roots of the Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba) are the primary plant parts used medicinally.

Phytochemical Composition

The phytochemical composition of Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba) includes compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its traditional use in herbal medicine for their antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties.

Pharmacological Properties

The pharmacological properties of Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba) include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, supported by both traditional use and modern scientific studies.

Mechanisms of Action

The Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba) exerts its medicinal effects primarily through its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which are attributed to compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and alkaloids present in its bark and leaves.

Therapeutic Uses and Indications

Red Silk Cotton Tree is used to treat inflammation, skin conditions, wounds, fever, respiratory infections, digestive disorders, and female reproductive system ailments.

Preparation Methods and Forms

The Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba) is typically prepared as a powder, decoction, or tincture, with the most common form being the dried bark ground into a fine powder, which is then used in traditional herbal formulations.

Dosage and Administration

The typical dosage of Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba) for herbal use ranges from 3 to 10 grams per day, usually divided into 2 to 3 doses, with the exact amount depending on the individual's condition and the formulation used.

Safety, Side Effects, and Contraindications

While Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba) is generally considered safe when used in traditional herbal medicine, it may cause mild gastrointestinal discomfort such as nausea or stomach upset in some individuals, and excessive use may lead to skin irritation or allergic reactions.

Drug Interactions

Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba) may interact with certain medications, particularly those affecting the liver or blood clotting, due to its potential hepatotoxic and anticoagulant properties, and it is important to consult a healthcare provider before use if taking other drugs.

Toxicity and Precautions

While Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba) is generally considered safe when used in traditional medicinal preparations, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals and should be used with caution, especially during pregnancy and lactation, as its safety profile in these groups has not been fully established.

Cultivation and Harvesting

Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba) is typically cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions with well-drained soil, full sun exposure, and a warm climate, often requiring a period of dry season to trigger its characteristic flowering and fruiting cycle.

Processing and Extraction

The processing of Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba) typically involves drying the matured pods, which are then crushed to extract the seeds, followed by further drying and grinding into a fine powder or oil for use in traditional herbal formulations.

Storage and Shelf Life

The Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba) should be stored in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture to maintain its potency and prevent degradation, with a typical shelf life of 2 to 3 years when properly preserved.

Scientific Research and Evidence
