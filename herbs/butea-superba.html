Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

The Red Silk Cotton Tree, or Butea superba, is a traditional medicinal plant native to Southeast Asia, renowned for its use in herbal medicine for its purported aphrodisiac and anti-inflammatory properties.

Here you can find a more about medicinal plants.

What is Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea Superba)?

The Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba) is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, known for its medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal medicine.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Red Silk Cotton Tree is Butea superba. This plant belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Fabales, order Fabales, family Fabaceae, genus Butea, and species Butea superba. Commonly known as Red Silk Cotton Tree in Southeast Asia, it is also called Kanchanakon in Thailand. In India, it is referred to as Champa or Champa tree. The synonyms for this plant include Butea frondosa and Butea monosperma, with regional names such as Kanchanakon in Thailand and Champa in India.

Plant Description and Morphology

Red Silk Cotton Tree is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3-5 meters in height, with a short trunk and dense, spreading branches.

Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate to elliptic in shape, with a pointed tip and a slightly toothed margin. The flowers are small, clustered in panicles, and have a distinctive red color when young, transitioning to orange or yellow as they mature. The root system is fibrous and moderately deep, providing stability and efficient water absorption.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, while the leaves are glossy green on top and pale underneath, with a slightly rough texture.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Red Silk Cotton Tree is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand, Cambodia, Vietnam, and parts of India.

It is commonly found in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in lowland areas with well-drained soil. The tree thrives in warm, humid climates with distinct wet and dry seasons. It prefers full sun exposure and is typically found in open forested areas or secondary growth.

Ecological conditions favoring its growth include moderate rainfall and fertile, loamy soil.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba) has several plant parts used medicinally, including the bark, leaves, and flowers.

The bark is most commonly utilized, often harvested from mature trees. It is typically dried and processed into various formulations. The leaves are also employed, usually in decoctions or poultices. Flowers are occasionally used, particularly in traditional remedies for their purported healing properties.

Each part is prepared and applied according to specific traditional methods.

Phytochemical Composition

The Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba) contains a variety of active compounds, including flavonoids, alkaloids, and tannins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Secondary metabolites such as quercetin, kaempferol, and rutin are prevalent and play key roles in its pharmacological activities. Chemical markers like butein and isoquercetin are widely used for quality control and standardization of Butea superba preparations. These compounds exhibit antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial effects.

The specific composition varies depending on the plant part and extraction method.

Pharmacological Properties

Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba) exhibits pharmacological properties including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and analgesic effects, primarily due to its active compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and alkaloids, which contribute to its traditional use in treating inflammation, infections, and pain.

Mechanisms of Action

The Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba) exerts its effects through the presence of bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and alkaloids, which possess anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, contributing to its traditional use in treating wounds, infections, and inflammatory conditions.

Therapeutic Uses and Indications

The Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba) is traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat conditions such as inflammation, pain, and skin disorders due to its anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antimicrobial properties.

Preparation Methods and Forms

The Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba) is typically prepared as a dried herb, often used in the form of powder, decoction, or tincture, with the most common method involving boiling the dried bark in water to extract its active compounds.

Dosage and Administration

The recommended dosage of Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba) for herbal use typically ranges from 3 to 6 grams per day, taken in divided doses, and it is important to follow the guidance of a qualified healthcare practitioner to ensure safe and effective use.

Safety, Side Effects, and Contraindications

While Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba) is generally considered safe when used in appropriate doses, it may cause mild gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals and should be avoided during pregnancy, breastfeeding, and by those with bleeding disorders due to its potential anticoagulant properties.

Drug Interactions

Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba) may interact with medications that affect blood clotting, such as anticoagulants and antiplatelet drugs, potentially increasing the risk of bleeding.

Toxicity and Precautions

While Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba) is generally considered safe when used in traditional herbal medicine, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals, and prolonged use or high doses may lead to potential toxicity, necessitating careful dosage control and consultation with a qualified healthcare provider.

Cultivation and Harvesting

The Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba) is typically cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions with well-drained soil and ample sunlight, with harvesting of its bark and roots occurring primarily during the dry season when active compounds are most concentrated.

Processing and Extraction

The processing and extraction of Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba) typically involve harvesting the dried flower buds, which are then subjected to solvent extraction to obtain the active compounds, primarily the flavonoids and alkaloids responsible for its medicinal properties.

Storage and Shelf Life

The Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba) should be stored in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture to maintain its potency and prevent degradation, with a typical shelf life of up to two years when properly preserved.

Scientific Research and Evidence

Scientific research and evidence on Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba) primarily focus on its traditional use in herbal medicine, with studies investigating its potential anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, as well as its role in supporting reproductive health and treating infections.

Traditional and Historical Uses

Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba) has been traditionally used in Thai and Ayurvedic medicine for its purported ability to enhance vitality, treat skin conditions, and support reproductive health, with historical references dating back centuries.
