Red Silk Cotton Tree

Butea superba

Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba) is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, known for its traditional use in herbal medicine to treat conditions such as erectile dysfunction and low libido.

Taxonomy

The Red Silk Cotton Tree, or Butea superba, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Fabaceae, genus Butea, and species Butea superba, classified under the order Fabales, class Fabales, division Magnoliophyta, and kingdom Plantae.

Morphology

The Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba) is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree characterized by its woody stem, trifoliate leaves with elliptical leaflets, and distinctive red flowers that bloom in clusters, often accompanied by a sweet, spicy fragrance.

Distribution

The Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba) is native to Southeast Asia, specifically found in countries such as Thailand, Malaysia, Indonesia, and parts of India, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Traditional Uses

Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba) has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported ability to enhance vitality, treat skin conditions, and support reproductive health, with various formulations used in traditional Thai and Ayurvedic practices.

Medicinal Systems

In traditional medicinal systems such as Ayurveda, Traditional Chinese Medicine, and Indigenous Thai medicine, the Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba) has been utilized for its purported therapeutic properties, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and aphrodisiac effects.

Phytochemistry

The phytochemistry of Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba) is characterized by the presence of flavonoids, tannins, alkaloids, and phenolic compounds, with buteaflavone and butein being the most prominent bioactive constituents identified in its bark and leaves.

Pharmacology

The Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba) exhibits pharmacological properties characterized by anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and wound-healing effects, making it valuable in traditional herbal medicine for treating skin conditions and inflammatory disorders.

Botanical Identification

Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba) is scientifically known as Butea superba, belonging to the Fabaceae family. Commonly called "Kan Yaow" in Thai, it is a perennial shrub native to Southeast Asia. It has a fibrous root system, trifoliate leaves, and orange-red flowers. It is also known as "Kan Yaow" in Thai and "Kan Yaow" in other regional contexts.

The Red Silk Cotton Tree (Butea superba) is generally considered safe when used as directed. However, it may cause mild gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. Consult a healthcare provider before use, especially if you have existing health conditions or are taking other medications.
