What are the medicinal properties of Canarium album?

It is used to treat acne, eczema, and dermatitis, while its antiseptic properties promote wound healing. It is also used to alleviate digestive issues, such as constipation and diarrhea.

The active constituents of Canarium album include terpenoids, alkaloids, and flavonoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties. Terpenoids have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects, while alkaloids exhibit antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties. Flavonoids have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities.

The parts of Canarium album used for medicinal purposes are the bark, seeds, and leaves. The bark is used to treat skin conditions and digestive problems, while the seeds are used to alleviate constipation and diarrhea. The leaves are used to treat fever and cough.

Improper use of Canarium album can lead to side effects, including allergic reactions, stomach upset, and increased heart rate. It can also interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes medications. Overconsumption of the plant can cause liver damage and other adverse effects.

When using Canarium album medicinally, it is essential to follow proper dosage and preparation guidelines. Pregnant and breastfeeding women should consult a healthcare professional before using the plant. Individuals with underlying medical conditions, such as liver or kidney disease, should also seek medical advice before using Canarium album.

What are the horticulural aspects of Canarium album?

Canarium album grow best in tropical and subtropical regions with high humidity and warm temperatures. Optimal growth conditions include full sun to partial shade and well-draining acidic soils with a pH range of 5.5 to 6.5. Watering needs are moderate to high.

Planting tips for Canarium album include selecting a location with good air circulation and minimal frost risk. Plant seeds 2-3 inches deep and 1-2 feet apart in a nursery or directly in the field. Fertilize with a balanced, water-soluble fertilizer at 20-20-20 NPK. Mulch around the base to retain moisture.

Harvesting galip typically occurs when the nuts are mature and the shell is hard. Pick the nuts from the tree by hand, taking care not to damage the branches. Harvest at the right time to avoid over-ripening and spoilage. A good rule of thumb is to check the nuts regularly.

Common pests and diseases affecting Canarium album include the rhinoceros beetle, which damages the bark and leaves, and the cankerworm, which feeds on the leaves and twigs. Root rot and collar rot are also prevalent, caused by fungal infections like Phytophthora and Phyllosticta, respectively. Regular monitoring and integrated pest management are necessary to prevent damage.
