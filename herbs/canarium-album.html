Canarium album

What's the taxonomical classification of Canarium album?

Canarium album belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Sapindales. This plant is a representative of the family Burseraceae, falling under the genus Canarium, and is specifically identified by its species name, album.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Canarium album has a medium-sized tree structure characterized by a straight trunk, smooth grayish bark, and a crown of pinnately compound leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The foliage consists of several pairs of leaflets that are typically elliptical or lanceolate in shape with entire margins. Small, fragrant white flowers emerge from the axils of the branches, eventually developing into fleshy drupes. These fruits are often yellowish or greenish when ripe and contain a single large seed. The overall vegetative morphology supports a dense canopy suitable for tropical forest environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of South America, specifically ranging from southern Mexico through Central America and into various parts of northern and eastern South America. It is most frequently encountered in lowland rainforests and seasonal dry forests within these territories. The species thrives in humid environments where high precipitation levels support its growth patterns. Its distribution is closely tied to specific climatic zones that provide consistent warmth throughout the year. Many populations are concentrated in countries such as Brazil, Colombia, and Venezuela.

Canarium album contains medicinal compounds found in its resin, bark, leaves, and seeds. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Canarium album targets various enzymes and signaling proteins including cyclooxygenase-2, lipoxygenase, and various pro-inflammatory cytokines to produce its therapeutic effects. The plant contains bioactive terpenoids and phenolic compounds that act as inhibitors to reduce the production of prostaglandin E2. These phytochemicals also interact with the NF-kappaB signaling pathway to downregulate the expression of inflammatory mediators. Additionally, certain extracts demonstrate an ability to modulate antioxidant enzymes like superoxide dismutase and catalase to combat oxidative stress. Through these multiple protein interactions, the plant exerts significant anti-inflammatory and neuroprotective activities.

What are the therapeutic actions of Canarium album?

Canarium album has therapeutic properties that include astringent, hemostatic, tonic, anti-inflammatory, and antiseptic actions. Its astringent qualities help to shrink tissues and constrict pores when applied topically. The plant acts as a potent hemostatic agent by effectively stopping bleeding in wounded areas. As a tonic, it helps to restore vigor and support overall bodily strength. Furthermore, its anti-inflammatory effects reduce swelling and pain while its antiseptic properties prevent infection by inhibiting microbial growth.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain, and dizziness if its parts are consumed. These gastrointestinal symptoms are common reactions following the accidental ingestion of the species. While small amounts may only cause discomfort, the plant exhibits toxicity if ingested in large amounts. Such severe reactions can lead to significant physical distress and systemic issues. Therefore, careful avoidance of all parts of the plant is necessary to prevent these adverse effects.
