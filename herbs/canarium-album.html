Canarium album

What's the taxonomical classification of Canarium album?

Canarium album belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Sapindales. This plant is a representative of the family Burseraceae, falling under the genus Canarium, and is specifically identified by its species name, album.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Canarium album has a medium-sized tree structure characterized by a straight trunk, smooth grayish bark, and a crown of pinnately compound leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The foliage consists of several pairs of leaflets that are typically elliptical or lanceolate in shape with entire margins. Small, fragrant white flowers emerge from the axils of the branches, eventually developing into fleshy drupes. These fruits are often yellowish or greenish when ripe and contain a single large seed. The overall vegetative morphology supports a dense canopy suitable for tropical forest environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of South America, specifically ranging from southern Mexico through Central America and into various parts of northern and eastern South America. It is most frequently encountered in lowland rainforests and seasonal dry forests within these territories. The species thrives in humid environments where high precipitation levels support its growth patterns. Its distribution is closely tied to specific climatic zones that provide consistent warmth throughout the year. Many populations are concentrated in countries such as Brazil, Colombia, and Venezuela.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Canarium album contains medicinal compounds found in its resin, bark, leaves, and seeds. The oleoresin extracted from the trunk is frequently utilized to treat skin ailments and respiratory issues due to its antiseptic properties. Decocting the bark or leaves can produce infusions used to alleviate inflammation and stomach discomfort. The seeds are often processed to extract oils that serve various therapeutic purposes in traditional medicine. These diverse plant parts provide a wide range of phytochemicals essential for treating localized infections and systemic ailments.

What are the pharmacological activities of Canarium album?

This plant has anti-cancer, anti-inflammatory, anti-proliferative, antiviral, and neuraminidase inhibitor pharmacological activities. Its chemical constituents demonstrate the ability to inhibit the growth of malignant cells through anti-proliferative mechanisms. The plant also exerts significant anti-inflammatory effects by modulating various biological pathways in the body. Furthermore, its antiviral properties include acting as a neuraminidase inhibitor to prevent viral replication. These diverse medicinal capabilities make Canarium album a subject of interest for pharmaceutical development.

As discussed in a study, the methanol-ethyl acetate partitioned fraction from Chinese olive fruits, or MEO, contains 12% ellagic acid and exhibits profound anti-proliferative activities in the HCT116 human colon cancer cell line. Oral administration of MEO remarkably inhibited the tumor growth of subcutaneously implanted CT26 mouse colon carcinoma cells in BALB/c mice. This fraction exerts its most potent effect on the inhibition of NF-κB-mediated signaling compared to other partitioned fractions from the plant. MEO promotes apoptosis by reducing the phosphorylation of NF-κB signaling-associated proteins, specifically IKKα/β, IκBα, and p65. Additionally, MEO treatment significantly suppressed lipopolysaccharide (LPS)-induced cancer cell proliferation through the suppression of the NF-κB signaling pathway.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains medicinal compounds such as hydrolysable tannin, isocorilagin, canaroleosides A-C, flavonoids, and nobiletin. These bioactive constituents contribute to various therapeutic properties within the botanical profile. The presence of specific hydrolysable tannins like isocorilagin often supports antioxidant activities. Additionally, the flavonoids and nobiletin work together to provide significant biological benefits to the organism. These diverse chemical structures make the plant a subject of interest for pharmacological study.

As discussed in a study, the fruit of Canarium album L. is notable for its pharmacological and edible properties and is rich in hydrolysable tannins (HTs). These HTs serve as the primary material basis for the fruit's pharmacological effects. Researchers identified a total of 83 differentially expressed Chinese olive MYB transcription factors (CaMYBs) during the study. This group of factors included 54 1R-MYBs, 25 2R-MYBs, 3 3R-MYBs, and 1 4R-MYB. Specifically, the regulator CaMYBR04 (Isoform0032534) was found to significantly enhance the fruit's hydrolysable tannin content.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating respiratory infections and systemic symptoms caused by the influenza virus, specifically targeting strains like influenza A. Researchers have investigated its potential efficacy against the H1N1 influenza virus to mitigate severe viral symptoms. By utilizing its bioactive compounds, it may help manage the complications associated with influenza A. This traditional remedy is often applied to ease the progression of the influenza virus in patients. Such applications aim to reduce the overall severity of the influenza infection.

As discussed in a study, the leaves of Canarium album (Lour.) DC. exerted great anti-influenza activities in the MDCK cell model with an IC50 of 31.11-36.72 μg/mL. In IAV infected A549 cell and mouse models, the GLY aqueous extract reduced viral proteins, cytokines, and chemoalkine expressions. When administered at 2 g/kg/d, GLY could improve the mouse survival rate and elongate the average survival days. The study identified 20 active compounds, including xanthotoxol, esculetin, isorhoifolin, and nobiletin, as key contributors to the activity of GLY. These components work by inhibiting the phosphorylation of proteins in the PI3K-AKT signal pathway to protect infected cells and mice.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain, and dizziness if its parts are consumed. These gastrointestinal symptoms are common reactions following the accidental ingestion of the species. While small amounts may only cause discomfort, the plant exhibits toxicity if ingested in large amounts. Such severe reactions can lead to significant physical distress and systemic issues. Therefore, careful avoidance of all parts of the plant is necessary to prevent these adverse effects.
