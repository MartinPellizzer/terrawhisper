Canarium album

What's the taxonomical classification of Canarium album?

Canarium album belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Sapindales. This plant is a representative of the family Burseraceae, falling under the genus Canarium, and is specifically identified by its species name, album.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Canarium album has a medium-sized tree structure characterized by a straight trunk, smooth grayish bark, and a crown of pinnately compound leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The foliage consists of several pairs of leaflets that are typically elliptical or lanceolate in shape with entire margins. Small, fragrant white flowers emerge from the axils of the branches, eventually developing into fleshy drupes. These fruits are often yellowish or greenish when ripe and contain a single large seed. The overall vegetative morphology supports a dense canopy suitable for tropical forest environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of South America, specifically ranging from southern Mexico through Central America and into various parts of northern and eastern South America. It is most frequently encountered in lowland rainforests and seasonal dry forests within these territories. The species thrives in humid environments where high precipitation levels support its growth patterns. Its distribution is closely tied to specific climatic zones that provide consistent warmth throughout the year. Many populations are concentrated in countries such as Brazil, Colombia, and Venezuela.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant, known as the Canary Island laurel, requires well-drained, fertile soil and a subtropical climate with consistent humidity to thrive.

It flourishes in areas that receive partial shade and moderate rainfall throughout the year. Growers should ensure the soil remains moist but never waterlogged to prevent root rot. Regular mulching helps maintain the necessary moisture levels and suppresses competition from weeds.

Once established, the tree is relatively hardy but benefits from protection against harsh, drying winds.

What are the pharmacological activities of Canarium album?

This plant has anti-cancer, anti-inflammatory, anti-proliferative, antiviral, and neuraminidase inhibitor pharmacological activities. Its phytochemical constituents work to suppress the growth of malignant cells through various anti-proliferative mechanisms. The plant also demonstrates significant anti-inflammatory properties by reducing biological markers of inflammation. Furthermore, it exhibits potent antiviral effects by acting as a neuraminidase inhibitor to prevent viral replication. These diverse medicinal properties make it a subject of interest for pharmaceutical research.

According to a study published by "Food & function", the methanol-ethyl acetate partitioned fraction (MEO) from Canarium album L. fruits, which contains 12% ellagic acid, exhibits profound anti-proliferative activities in the HCT116 human colon cancer cell line. Oral administration of MEO remarkably inhibited the tumor growth of subcutaneously implanted CT26 mouse colon carcinoma cells in BALB/c mice. This fraction promotes apoptosis through the suppression of the NF-κB signaling pathway, as evidenced by the reduction of phosphorylation in signaling-associated proteins including IKKα/β, IκBα, and p65. MEO treatment also significantly suppressed lipopolysaccharide (LPS)-induced cancer cell proliferation. These findings suggest that the methanol-ethyl acetate partitioned fraction from Canarium album L. has promising potential in cancer treatment.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several medicinal compounds including hydrolysable tannin, isocorilagin, canaroleosides A-C, flavonoids, and isorrhoifoilin. These bioactive constituents contribute significantly to the therapeutic potential of Canarium album. The presence of specific flavonoids and tannins suggests the plant may possess antioxidant and antimicrobial properties. Additionally, unique compounds like isocorilagin and the canaroleoside group play key roles in its chemical profile. Research into these substances helps clarify the plant's traditional medicinal uses.

According to a study published by "Plants (Basel, Switzerland)", the fruit of Canarium album L. is notable for its pharmacological and edible properties and is rich in hydrolysable tannins (HTs). These HTs serve as the primary material basis for the formation of the fruit's pharmacological effects. In the study of Canarium album Changying, a total of 83 differentially expressed Chinese olive MYB transcription factors (CaMYBs) were identified. These factors include 54 1R-MYBs, 25 2R-MYBs, 3 3R-MYBs, and 1 4R-MYB. The regulator CaMYBR04 (Isoform0032534) was found to exhibit a significantly higher expression (FPKM) than the other CaMYBs and significantly enhanced the fruit's hydrolysable tannin content.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating respiratory complications caused by H1N1 influenza, influenza, influenza A, influenza A virus, and influenza virus. It serves as a natural remedy to help alleviate the severe symptoms associated with these viral infections. Practitioners often utilize its properties to manage the fever and congestion typically triggered by the influenza virus. By supporting the immune response, it may assist the body in fighting off the influenza A virus more effectively. This botanical approach provides a traditional method for addressing the various strains of influenza.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", Canarium album (Lour.) DC. is a traditional medicinal plant used as heat-clearing and detoxicating herbs for warm disease treatment. The leaves of the plant exert anti-influenza activities in the MDCK cell model with an IC50 of 31.11-36.72 μg/mL. In H1N1 influenza studies involving IAV infected A549 cells and mouse models, GLY was shown to reduce viral proteins, cytokines, and chemokines expressions. When administered at 2 g/kg/d, GLY could improve the mouse survival rate and elongate the average survival days. The plant's activity is attributed to 20 active compounds, including xanthotoxol, esculetin, isoorifoilin, and nobiletin, which help inhibit influenza A virus replication and inflammation.
