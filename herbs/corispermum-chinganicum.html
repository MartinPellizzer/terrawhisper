Corispermum chinganicum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Corispermum chinganicum has a prostrate to ascending herbaceous habit characterized by slender, branched stems and small, opposite, ovate leaves with entire margins. The foliage typically presents a smooth texture and a relatively small surface area adapted to its specific environment. Tiny, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in dense, terminal clusters that emerge from the stem tips. These flowers give rise to small, dry fruits that are central to the plant's reproductive strategy. The overall structure is compact, allowing the plant to spread low across the ground surface.
