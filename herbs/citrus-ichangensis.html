Citrus ichangensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Citrus ichangensis has a woody, perennial growth habit characterized by spreading branches, elliptical to ovate leaves with entire margins, and small, globose fruits. The foliage typically exhibits a dark green color and a slightly leathery texture common to many citrus species. Its flowers are usually white or pale cream, possessing delicate petals and a fragrant aroma that attracts pollinators. The fruit develops from a fleshy ovary and eventually ripens into a citrus berry known as a hesperidium. This morphological structure allows the plant to thrive in its specific subtropical environmental niche.

      What are the herbal preparations of Citrus ichangensis?

      Citrus ichangensis is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste using the plant's leaves, fruit, or bark.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Citrus ichangenensis can cause localized skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if consumed in large quantities. Contact with the plant's juices or sap may lead to redness, itching, or burning sensations on sensitive skin areas. Some individuals might experience an inflammatory response known as contact dermatitis upon exposure to its essential oils. If ingested, the high acidity or specific chemical compounds could potentially trigger nausea or stomach cramping. It is important to monitor for any signs of hypersensitivity when handling this species.
