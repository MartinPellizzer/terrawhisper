Crotalaria alata

What's the taxonomical classification of Crotalaria alata?

Crotalaria alata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Fabales and is a member of the family Fabaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Crotalaria and is specifically designated as the species alata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Crotalaria alata has an erect, herbaceous growth habit characterized by branched stems and pinnately compound leaves with several pairs of leaflets. The leaves are typically oblong or elliptical in shape and possess smooth margins. Small, bright yellow flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves, often featuring distinct markings on the petals. The plant produces characteristic inflated seed pods that are somewhat bladder-like in appearance. These pods eventually dry and contain small, hard seeds that facilitate the dispersal of the species.
