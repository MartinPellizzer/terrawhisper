Crotalaria alata

What's the taxonomical classification of Crotalaria alata?

Crotalaria alata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Fabales and is a member of the family Fabaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Crotalaria and is specifically designated as the species alata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Crotalaria alata has an erect, herbaceous growth habit characterized by branched stems and pinnately compound leaves with several pairs of leaflets.

The leaves are typically oblong or elliptical in shape and possess smooth margins. Small, bright yellow flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves, often featuring distinct markings on the petals. The plant produces characteristic inflated seed pods that are somewhat bladder-like in appearance.

These pods eventually dry and contain small, hard seeds that facilitate the dispersal of the species.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Crotalaria alata targets various enzymes and receptors involved in inflammatory and oxidative stress pathways, specifically inhibiting cyclooxygenase-2 and modulating cytokine production. Its bioactive alkaloids and flavonoids interact with signaling molecules to downregulate pro-inflammatory mediators like tumor necrosis factor-alpha and interleukin-6. These phytochemicals also influence antioxidant enzymes, enhancing the activity of superoxide dismutase and catalase to mitigate cellular damage. By interacting with these specific protein networks, the plant exerts its observed pharmacological effects in wound healing and anti-inflammatory responses. This multi-target mechanism allows the plant to address complex physiological imbalances through coordinated molecular regulation.

What are the therapeutic actions of Crotalaria alata?

Crotalaria alata has therapeutic properties that include emetic, purgative, anti-inflammatory, and anthelmintic actions used in traditional medicine.

When administered in specific doses, the plant can induce vomiting to clear toxins from the digestive system. It also serves as a powerful purgative to stimulate bowel movements and cleanse the intestines. The botanical extracts exhibit anti-inflammatory effects that help reduce swelling and pain in various parts of the body.

Additionally, its anthelmintic qualities allow it to be used effectively for expelling intestinal worms and parasites.

What are the herbal preparations of Crotalaria alata?

Crotalaria alata is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste depending on the specific medicinal purpose. The roots and leaves are often boiled in water to create a liquid extract used for internal consumption. For skin conditions or localized swelling, the plant material is crushed into a fine paste and applied directly to the affected area. Some traditional practices also involve using the plant in poultices to draw out inflammation. These various methods allow the bioactive compounds to be utilized effectively for both systemic and external treatments.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Crotalaria alata can cause severe liver damage and death due to the presence of toxic pyrrolizidine alkaloids in its tissues. These chemical compounds are metabolized by the body into highly reactive substances that destroy liver cells. Consumption often leads to hepatic veno-occlusive disease, which prevents blood from flowing properly out of the liver. Symptoms of poisoning may include jaundice, abdominal pain, and swelling of the liver. If ingestion is significant, the damage can become irreversible and lead to total organ failure.
