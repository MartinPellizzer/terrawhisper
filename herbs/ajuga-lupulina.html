Ajuga lupulina

What's the taxonomical classification of Ajuga lupulina?

Ajuga lupulina belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is situated in the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Ajuga and is specifically designated as the species lupulina.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ajuga lupulina has a basal rosette of leaves that are typically oval or elliptical with serrated margins and a hairy texture. The plant produces upright flowering stems that can reach heights of up to thirty centimeters. Small, two-lipped flowers emerge in dense terminal whorls and are usually light blue, violet, or white in color. These blossoms are supported by square stems characteristic of the mint family. The fruit consists of four small nutlets that develop from the base of the calyx after pollination.
