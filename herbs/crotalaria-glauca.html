Crotalaria glauca

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Crotalaria glauca has an erect, herbaceous growth habit characterized by slender, glaucous stems and alternate, lanceolate leaves that exhibit a waxy, bluish-green coating. The foliage typically features entire margins and a smooth texture, which helps the plant conserve moisture. Small, yellow flowers emerge from the leaf axils, displaying a typical pea-like structure common to the Fabaceae family. As the plant matures, it produces distinctive, inflated seed pods that are somewhat bladder-like in appearance. These pods eventually dry and release small, dark seeds that facilitate the species' reproduction.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, ranging from the southern United States through Central America and deep into South America. It is commonly found inhabiting sandy soils and coastal dunes within these diverse latitudes. Its presence is particularly notable in sandy areas of Florida, the Caribbean islands, and parts of Brazil. The species thrives in warm climates where coastal or disturbed environments provide suitable growing conditions. This wide range allows it to colonize various lowland habitats across the neotropics.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Crotalaria glauca contains pyrrolizidine alkaloids, flavonoids, and various sterols that serve as its primary bioactive components. These alkaloids are the most significant chemical constituents and are known for their potent pharmacological activity. Some of these compounds exhibit antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties when studied in a laboratory setting. However, many of these specific alkaloids are also highly toxic to the liver and can cause serious health complications if ingested. Research into these medicinal properties continues to investigate how certain derivatives might be safely utilized in therapeutic contexts.

      What side effects this plant can have?

      This plant can cause severe toxicity in humans and animals due to the presence of pyrrolizidine alkaloids. These chemical compounds are known to trigger significant liver damage if ingested in sufficient quantities. Once the alkaloids enter the system, common gastrointestinal symptoms such as nausea and vomiting often occur. In chronic cases, the repeated consumption of the plant can lead to progressive hepatic failure. Therefore, avoiding contact with this species is essential for preventing serious health complications.
