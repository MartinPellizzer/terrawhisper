Crotalaria glauca

What's the taxonomical classification of Crotalaria glauca?

Crotalaria glauca belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is situated in the order Fabales. It is a member of the family Fabaceae, falling under the genus Crotalaria, and is specifically identified by the species name glauca.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Crotalaria glauca contains pyrrolizidine alkaloids, flavonoids, and various sterols that serve as its primary bioactive components. These alkaloids are the most significant chemical constituents and are known for their potent pharmacological activity. Some of these compounds exhibit antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties when studied in a laboratory setting. However, many of these specific alkaloids are also highly toxic to the liver and can cause serious health complications if ingested. Research into these medicinal properties continues to investigate how certain derivatives might be safely utilized in therapeutic contexts.

What are the therapeutic actions of Crotalaria glauca?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, fungal diseases, and various types of inflammation. The leaves are frequently crushed into a paste and applied topically to soothe itchy skin or treat sores. It is also utilized in traditional medicine to combat ailments related to the digestive tract. Some practitioners use decoctions of the plant to help manage symptoms of systemic fever. These medicinal applications rely on the specific bioactive compounds found within its foliage.
