What's the taxonomical classification of Crotalaria glauca?

Crotalaria glauca belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is situated in the order Fabales. It is a member of the family Fabaceae, falling under the genus Crotalaria, and is specifically identified by the species name glauca.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Crotalaria glauca has an erect, herbaceous growth habit characterized by slender, glaucous stems and alternate, lanceolate leaves that exhibit a waxy, bluish-green coating. The foliage typically features entire margins and a smooth texture, which helps the plant conserve moisture. Small, yellow flowers emerge from the leaf axils, displaying a typical pea-like structure common to the Fabaceae family. As the plant matures, it produces distinctive, inflated seed pods that are somewhat bladder-like in appearance. These pods eventually dry and release small, dark seeds that facilitate the species' reproduction.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause severe toxicity in humans and animals due to the presence of pyrrolizidine alkaloids. These chemical compounds are known to trigger significant liver damage if ingested in sufficient quantities. Once the alkaloids enter the system, common gastrointestinal symptoms such as nausea and vomiting often occur. In chronic cases, the repeated consumption of the plant can lead to progressive hepatic failure. Therefore, avoiding contact with this species is essential for preventing serious health complications.
