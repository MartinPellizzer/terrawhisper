Tetrastigma serrulatum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tetrastigma serrulatum has woody climbing vines characterized by thick, succulent stems and leaves with distinctively serrated margins. The foliage typically consists of large, ovate to elliptical leaves that display prominent venation patterns. Its climbing habit allows the plant to scale forest canopies using specialized rooting structures. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in clusters, often followed by fleshy fruits. The overall structure of the plant is adapted for life in tropical forest environments.

This plant is native to the tropical rainforest regions of West and Central Africa, specifically ranging from Sierra Leone to the Democratic Republic of the Congo. Within these habitats, the species often populates riverine areas and moist forest margins.
