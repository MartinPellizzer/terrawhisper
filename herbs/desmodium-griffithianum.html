Desmodium griffithianum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Desmodium griffithianum has trifoliate leaves with elliptical leaflets and small, papilionaceous flowers that are typically pink or purple. The plant features a woody or semi-woody stem structure that supports its herbaceous growth habit. Its leaves are arranged alternately along the stem and possess fine hairs on both the upper and lower surfaces. The inflorescence consists of small clusters of legume-shaped pods that often develop fine pubescence. These specialized seed pods are designed to cling to surfaces or passing animals to aid in dispersal.
