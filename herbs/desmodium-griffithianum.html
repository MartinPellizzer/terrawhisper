Desmodium griffithianum

What's the taxonomical classification of Desmodium griffithianum?

Desmodium griffithianum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized as Equisetopsida, falling under the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Fabales and is a member of the family Fabaceae. Within this family, it is identified by the genus Desmodium, and its specific biological classification is completed by its species name, griffithianum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Desmodium griffithianum has trifoliate leaves with elliptical leaflets and small, papilionaceous flowers that are typically pink or purple.

The plant features a woody or semi-woody stem structure that supports its herbaceous growth habit. Its leaves are arranged alternately along the stem and possess fine hairs on both the upper and lower surfaces. The inflorescence consists of small clusters of legume-shaped pods that often develop fine pubescence.

These specialized seed pods are designed to cling to surfaces or passing animals to aid in dispersal.
