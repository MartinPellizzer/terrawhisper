Desmodium griffithianum

What's the taxonomical classification of Desmodium griffithianum?

Desmodium griffithianum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized as Equisetopsida, falling under the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Fabales and is a member of the family Fabaceae. Within this family, it is identified by the genus Desmodium, and its specific biological classification is completed by its species name, griffithianum.

    What are the molecular targets of this plant?

    Desmodium griffithianum targets specific inflammatory enzymes and oxidative stress pathways, primarily through the inhibition of cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2) and various pro-inflammatory cytokines. Its bioactive phytochemicals, such as flavonoids and polyphenols, modulate the expression of tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-alpha) and interleukin-6 (IL-6) to suppress systemic inflammation. These secondary metabolites also interact with nitric oxide synthase (NOS) to regulate the production of nitric oxide in vascular and immune cells. Furthermore, the plant's constituents act on antioxidant enzymes like superoxide dismutase (SOD) and catalase to neutralize reactive oxygen species. By influencing these protein-mediated signaling cascades, the plant exerts its observed anti-inflammatory and antioxidant therapeutic effects.

It is always prudent to avoid skin contact and ingestion until comprehensive safety profiles are established.
