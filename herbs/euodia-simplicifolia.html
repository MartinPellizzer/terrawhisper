Euodia simplicifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Euodia simplicifolia?

Euodia simplicifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized as Equisetopsida, falling under the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Sapindales and the family Rutaceae. Finally, the plant is placed within the genus Euodia, specifically identified by its species name, simplicifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Euodia simplicifolia has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape, measuring approximately 5 to 15 centimeters in length with entire margins and acute apices. The leaf texture is typically coriaceous, meaning it feels somewhat leathery to the touch. These plants feature small, axillary flowers that are often white or pale yellow in color. The inflorescence structure is relatively uncomplicated, supporting the species' specific epithet. Woody stems characterize the growth habit, leading to a shrubby or small tree-like form.
