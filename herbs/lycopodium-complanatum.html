What's the taxonomical classification of Lycopodium complanatum?

Lycopodium complanatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Lycopodiidae. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Lycopodiales and the family Lycopodiaceae. Finally, the plant is situated within the genus Lycopodium, with its specific identification being the species complanatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lycopodium complanatum has creeping, slender rhizomes that produce upright, dichotomously branched stems bearing small, scale-like leaves arranged spirally around the axes. These leaves are typically narrow and lanceolate in shape, often appearing crowded along the stems to create a dense, moss-like appearance. The plant lacks true roots, relying instead on specialized structures to anchor itself within the substrate. During its reproductive phase, it develops small, yellowish strobili at the tips of the branches to house its spores. The overall texture of the foliage is fine and delicate, contributing to its characteristic ground-covering growth habit.
