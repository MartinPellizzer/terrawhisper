Galium boreale

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Galium boreale has an erect, perennial growth habit characterized by slender, square-shaped stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 40 centimeters. The leaves are arranged in distinct whorls of four to six along the stem, featuring narrow, linear blades with entire margins. Small, inconspicuous white or cream-colored flowers emerge in dense, terminal clusters during the blooming season. The fruit consists of small, dry schizocarps that are often covered in minute hooks to facilitate dispersal. This morphology allows the plant to thrive in the subalpine and boreal environments it inhabits.
