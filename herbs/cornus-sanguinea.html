Bloodtwig Dogwood (Cornus sanguinea)

Bloodtwig Dogwood (Cornus sanguinea) is a member of the Cornaceae family, native to Northern Asia, Eastern Europe, and Central Asia. Traditionally, its roots, bark, and stems have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its astringent, anti-inflammatory, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and korean traditional medicine.
