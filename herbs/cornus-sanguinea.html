What are the pest and diseases tips of Cornus sanguinea?

One of the most common pests affecting Cornus sanguinea is the Cornus root borer (Plectorhyncha lamini), which can cause significant damage to the roots and stems of the plant. This pest can lead to premature defoliation, reduced growth, and even the death of the tree if left untreated.

Fungal diseases such as Septoria leaf spot (Septoria corni) and Cercospora leaf blight (Cercospora beticola) can also affect Cornus sanguinea, causing lesions on the leaves and stems that can lead to defoliation and reduced plant vigor. In addition, the shrub is also prone to bacterial diseases such as fire blight (Erwinia amylovora), which can cause blighted flowers and foliage, and root rot (Fusarium oxysporum) caused by soil-borne pathogens that can infect the roots of the plant.
