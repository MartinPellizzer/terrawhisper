    What is Diplostephium volubilis?

    Diplostephium volubilis is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

    It is a perennial herb with a woody base and a climbing or twining habit, reaching up to 5 meters in height. The leaves are simple, alternate, and lanceolate, measuring 10-30 cm in length and 2-6 cm in width, with a glossy surface and a ciliate margin. The plant produces yellow or greenish-yellow flower heads, consisting of 10-30 florets, which are borne on a long peduncle and are surrounded by a whorl of small, linear bracts.

    The achenes are 3-4 mm in length, with a pappus of 30-40 scales.
