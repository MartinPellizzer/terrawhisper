Ficus ovata

What's the taxonomical classification of Ficus ovata?

Ficus ovata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent further, it falls under the order Rosales and the family Moraceae. Finally, it is identified by its genus, Ficus, and its specific species, ovata.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Ficus ovata contains various bioactive phytochemicals including flavonoids, tannins, saponins, and alkaloids that contribute to its therapeutic potential. These secondary metabolites are often concentrated in the leaves and bark of the plant. Research indicates that these compounds possess significant antioxidant, antimicrobial, and anti-inflammatory properties. Some studies suggest these extracts may also exhibit cytotoxic effects against certain cancer cell lines. Such chemical profiles make the plant a subject of interest for pharmacological development in traditional medicine.

What are the therapeutic actions of Ficus ovata?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, digestive disorders, respiratory issues, and inflammatory conditions. The various parts of the plant, including the bark and leaves, contain bioactive compounds that act as antimicrobial agents. These medicinal properties make it effective in managing symptoms related to stomach ailments and lung congestion. Practitioners often utilize extracts from the plant to reduce swelling and soothe irritated skin surfaces. Its traditional application spans across multiple therapeutic areas in various folk medicine practices.
