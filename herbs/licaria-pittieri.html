What is Licaria pittieri?

Licaria pittieri is a species of flowering plant that belongs to the family Lauraceae.

It is an evergreen tree or shrub that grows up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, pyramidal crown and a straight, smooth trunk. The leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical in shape, measuring 8-15 cm in length and 4-7 cm in width, with a leathery texture and a wavy margin. The flowers are small, yellowish-green in color, and arranged in axillary spikes, while the fruit is a two-winged drupe, measuring 2-3 cm in length and containing a single seed.

Licaria pittieri is native to the tropical rainforests of Central America, specifically in Costa Rica and Panama.
