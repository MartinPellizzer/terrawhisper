White Clover (Trifolium Multiflorum)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Trifolium multiflorum.

White clover, scientifically known as Trifolium multiflorum, is a low-growing medicinal herb commonly found in temperate regions, known for its trifoliate leaves and small white flowers. It has been traditionally used as an adaptogen to help the body manage stress and support overall vitality, while also acting as a mild diuretic and digestive aid. Historically, it has been valued in European folk medicine for its ability to soothe respiratory issues, ease menstrual discomfort, and promote skin health, with its leaves often brewed into teas or applied topically. In modern wellness practices, white clover is used in herbal formulations to support liver function, reduce inflammation, and enhance immune response, often due to its rich content of flavonoids and other bioactive compounds. Its distinctive sweet, hay-like aroma and unique combination of nutrients make it a rare and versatile plant in both traditional and contemporary herbal medicine.
