10 Possible Side Effects Of Myrica Esculenta

Myrica esculenta has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, allergic reactions, and interactions with medications.

These side effects can be caused by ingesting large amounts, consuming contaminated products, or having an underlying health condition.

For example, gastrointestinal issues can lead to nausea, diarrhea, and stomach pain, severely impacting one's ability to work, maintain daily routines, and enjoy meals with family and friends.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Myrica esculenta causes allergic reactions due to its high content of oleoresin, a potent allergen that can trigger an immune response in some individuals.

The sap and leaves of the plant contain compounds like myricetin and quercetin, which are responsible for triggering allergic symptoms such as itching, hives, and swelling.

Additionally, the plant's volatile oils may also contribute to allergic reactions, leading to respiratory issues like asthma and hay fever.

2. Induces nausea and vomiting

Myrica esculenta induces nausea and vomiting due to its high concentration of tannins, which can irritate the stomach lining and cause gastrointestinal upset.

Additionally, the plant's anthraquinone glycosides may also contribute to this side effect by stimulating the intestines and altering bowel movements, leading to nausea and vomiting.

3. Stimulates excessive sweating

Myrica esculenta stimulates excessive sweating due to its high concentration of glycosides and saponins.

These compounds can stimulate the sweat glands, leading to increased perspiration. Additionally, the plant's volatile oils may enhance the body's natural thermoregulatory processes, causing an increase in sweat production.

As a result, individuals using Myrica esculenta may experience excessive sweating, potentially affecting their daily activities and overall comfort.

4. Increases heart rate quickly

Myrica esculenta increases heart rate quickly due to its stimulating properties on the cardiovascular system.

The herb contains a compound called berberine, which can increase sympathetic nervous activity, leading to an elevated heart rate. Additionally, Myrica esculenta may also stimulate the release of certain neurotransmitters, such as epinephrine and norepinephrine, which further contribute to the increase in heart rate.

As a result, individuals may experience a rapid heart beat when using this herbal remedy.

5. Lowers blood sugar levels

Myrica esculenta lowers blood sugar levels by interacting with certain medications used to treat diabetes, such as sulfonylureas and metformin.

The alpha-amyrin content in bayberry may stimulate insulin release from the pancreas, thereby reducing blood glucose levels. Additionally, the plant's ability to inhibit gastric emptying may slow down the absorption of glucose, further contributing to its hypoglycemic effect.

As a result, individuals taking Myrica esculenta may experience decreased blood sugar levels.

6. Interacts with medication poorly

Myrica esculenta interacts with medication poorly due to its high content of gallic acid and tannins.

These compounds can bind to medications in the gut, reducing their absorption and efficacy. Additionally, the flavonoids present in Myrica esculenta may induce enzymes that break down certain drugs, further decreasing their effectiveness.

As a result, individuals taking medication should be aware of potential interactions when consuming products containing this herb.

7. Irritates skin and mucous membranes

Myrica esculenta irritates skin and mucous membranes due to its high concentration of irritating compounds such as terpenoids and aldehydes.

These potent bioactive molecules can cause inflammation, redness, and discomfort upon contact with the skin or mucous membranes, potentially leading to allergic reactions or intolerance in some individuals.

8. Disrupts digestive system functioning

Myrica esculenta disrupts digestive system functioning by its ability to alter gut motility and reduce gastric acid secretion.

The berry's bioactive compounds, including iridoid glycosides and tannins, may bind to intestinal mucosa and delay stomach emptying, leading to symptoms of nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain.

Additionally, the antioxidant properties of Myrica esculenta may also inhibit the activity of digestive enzymes, further contributing to digestive system disturbances.

9. Affects liver function negatively

Myrica esculenta affects liver function negatively.

This is due to the presence of saponins and flavonoids in its extract, which can cause damage to liver cells and disrupt their normal functioning. Additionally, the high concentration of tannins in Myrica esculenta may contribute to its toxic effects on the liver.

As a result, prolonged use or excessive consumption of this herb may lead to adverse reactions involving liver function.

10. Causes respiratory distress

Myrica esculenta causes respiratory distress.

This side effect is attributed to its ability to stimulate the mucous membranes in the lungs, leading to an increased production of mucus and inflammation. The essential oils present in bayberry can also cause constriction of the airways, making it difficult for oxygen to flow into the lungs and out of the body.

These reactions can exacerbate existing respiratory conditions, such as asthma or chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).
