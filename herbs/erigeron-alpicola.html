Erigeron alpicola belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Asterales and the family Asteraceae, which characterizes its botanical relationship to other composites. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Erigeron, and its unique species designation, alpicola.

Erigeron alpicola is prepared primarily as a dried herbal infusion, a concentrated tincture, or a topical salve derived from the flowering tops. Tinctures allow for a more long-term preservation of the plant's medicinal properties through alcohol extraction.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Erigeron alpicola can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or mild gastrointestinal distress if ingested by humans or animals. While the plant is not considered highly toxic, contact with its foliage or flowers may trigger localized redness and itching in sensitive individuals. Some people might experience more severe allergic reactions such as swelling or rashes upon direct exposure. Ingestion of the plant material can lead to nausea or stomach discomfort in some cases. There is currently limited toxicological research available to define the exact severity of these potential reactions.
