Erigeron alpicola

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Erigeron alpicola has a low-growing, tufted growth habit characterized by basal rosettes of spatulate to oblanceolate leaves that are often densely covered in fine, grayish hairs. These leaves typically measure between 5 and 20 millimeters in width and lack distinct petioles. From the center of the rosette, slender, leafless flowering stalks emerge, reaching heights of approximately 5 to 15 centimeters. The inflorescence consists of a single terminal head surrounded by narrow, linear phyllaries. Each flower head features numerous white to pale lavender ray florets and a central cluster of yellow disc florets.
