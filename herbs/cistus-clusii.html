Cistus Clusii

Cistus clusii

Cistus Clusii (Cistus clusii) is a member of the Cistaceae family, native to regions of the Mediterranean. Traditionally, its leaves, flowers, and roots have been used for treating skin infections, anti-inflammatory purposes, and as a digestive aid. The herb has a historical significance in Mediterranean cultures, where it was incorporated into various traditional remedies.

Botanical Identification

Cistus clusii, also known as Clusii rockrose, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family. It is native to the Canary Islands and belongs to the genus Cistus. The plant is a shrub with aromatic leaves, pinkish flowers, and a woody root system. It is sometimes referred to as "Cistus canariensis" in older herbal texts.

Active Compounds

Cistus clusii contains key active compounds such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and tannins, which contribute to its therapeutic properties. These compounds work synergistically to exert anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Their combined action enhances the herb's efficacy in supporting overall health.

Preparation Methods

Extract: Used for respiratory and skin conditions due to its anti-inflammatory properties.

Infusion: Used for digestive issues and as a mild tonic due to its soothing properties.

Decoction: Used for gastrointestinal ailments due to its bitter compounds that aid digestion.

Safety Profile

Cistus clusii is generally considered safe when used in recommended dosages. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. There are no well-documented contraindications, but caution is advised during pregnancy and lactation due to limited research on its effects.
