Medicinal Parts

Cistus clusii leaf has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its therapeutic properties, particularly due to its high content of flavonoids, tannins, and essential oils.

In traditional remedies, the leaf is used to treat respiratory conditions such as coughs and bronchitis, as well as for its astringent properties that help in managing skin irritations and wounds. Side Effects of cistus clusii

Cistus clusii triggers skin rash in some individuals, often manifesting as redness, itching, or hives upon contact or ingestion.

This herb can also provoke allergic reactions, ranging from mild symptoms like sneezing to severe cases such as anaphylaxis, particularly in those with a history of allergies. Additionally, it may cause stomach upset, leading to bloating, cramps, or indigestion due to its potent chemical composition.

Dry mouth and dry throat are common side effects, as the herb can reduce saliva production and irritate mucous membranes. Furthermore, Cistus clusii may result in nausea, headache, and dizziness, which can be attributed to its impact on the central nervous system. These effects highlight the importance of consulting a healthcare professional before using this herb, especially for individuals with pre-existing health conditions or allergies.

While some people may tolerate it well, the potential for adverse reactions underscores the need for caution and proper guidance when incorporating Cistus clusii into one's health regimen.
