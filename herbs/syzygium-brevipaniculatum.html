What is Syzygium brevipaniculatum?

Syzygium brevipaniculatum is a species of plant in the family Myrtaceae, native to China.

It is a shrub or small tree, typically growing up to 3 meters in height, with a broad, elliptical to ovate leaf shape, measuring 3-12 cm in length and 1-4 cm in width. The leaves are dark green, glossy, and have a rounded apex, with a leathery texture and a distinct midrib. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in terminal panicles, which are 3-10 cm in length, and are followed by small, purple-black fruits, 5-6 mm in diameter, containing 2-3 seeds each.

The bark is dark brown to black, with a smooth texture, and the wood is yellowish-brown.
