What is Quercus × tlemcenensis?

Quercus × tlemcenensis is a hybrid oak species within the family Fagaceae.

It is a deciduous tree with a broad, rounded crown and a short, thick trunk that can grow up to 15 meters in height. The leaves are lobed, with 7-9 pointed lobes, and have a wavy margin, measuring 5-10 centimeters in length. The bark is greyish-brown, with a rugged texture and prominent furrows.

Quercus × tlemcenensis is endemic to the Tlemcen region in northwestern Algeria.
