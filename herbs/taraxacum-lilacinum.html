Taraxacum lilacinum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Taraxacum lilacinum has a basal rosette of deeply lobed, dentate leaves that feature a distinct lilac-colored underside and a hollow, milky-sap-filled stem. The inflorescence consists of a single terminal flower head composed of numerous ligulate florets that exhibit a unique pale violet hue. These floral structures are supported by a leafless scape that often possesses small, scale-like bracts near its base. The plant's root system is comprised of a thick, fleshy taproot designed for nutrient storage. Upon maturity, the flower head transitions into a globose seed head characterized by fine, white pappus bristles.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the high-altitude regions of the central Andes mountains within South America. It specifically thrives in the moist alpine tundras and páramo ecosystems found in parts of Colombia and Ecuador. These populations are typically restricted to specific elevational bands where cool temperatures and high humidity are consistent. Because it relies on these unique mountain climates, its range is fragmented across different peaks and valleys. This localized distribution makes the species highly sensitive to changes in local weather patterns and habitat stability.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Taraxacum lilacinum targets cyclooxygenase-2, inducible nitric oxide synthase, and various pro-inflammatory cytokines such as tumor necrosis factor-alpha and interleukin-6. These interactions primarily occur through the modulation of enzymatic pathways that regulate the inflammatory response within cellular environments. By inhibiting the cyclooxygenase-2 enzyme, the plant effectively reduces the synthesis of prostaglandins, which are key mediators of pain and swelling. Additionally, its ability to suppress nitric oxide synthase limits the production of nitric oxide, further dampening oxidative stress. These multi-protein interactions collectively contribute to the plant's demonstrated antioxidant and anti-inflammatory medicinal properties.
