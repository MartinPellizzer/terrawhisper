Taraxacum lilacinum

What's the taxonomical classification of Taraxacum lilacinum?

Taraxacum lilacinum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Asterales and is a member of the family Asteraceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific genus, Taraxacum, and its unique species designation, lilacinum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Taraxacum lilacinum has a basal rosette of deeply lobed, dentate leaves that feature a distinct lilac-colored underside and a hollow, milky-sap-filled stem. The inflorescence consists of a single terminal flower head composed of numerous ligulate florets that exhibit a unique pale violet hue. These floral structures are supported by a leafless scape that often possesses small, scale-like bracts near its base. The plant's root system is comprised of a thick, fleshy taproot designed for nutrient storage. Upon maturity, the flower head transitions into a globose seed head characterized by fine, white pappus bristles.
