Holoptelea integrifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Holoptelea integrifolia?

Holoptelea integrifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, the plant is placed in the order Rosales and the family Ulmaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Holoptelea and is specifically designated as the species integrifolia.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Holoptelea integrifolia contains various secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, and terpenoids that serve as its primary medicinal compounds. These chemical constituents are believed to contribute to the plant's potential pharmacological activities, such as antimicrobial and antioxidant properties. Researchers often study these bioactive molecules to understand how they interact with biological systems to mitigate oxidative stress. The specific concentration and profile of these compounds can vary significantly depending on the plant's environmental conditions and growth stage. Continued investigation into these specific chemical structures is necessary to fully validate their therapeutic applications in traditional medicine.

What are the therapeutic actions of Holoptelea integrifolia?

Holoptelea integrifolia has medicinal properties primarily utilized by traditional healers to treat gastrointestinal ailments, skin infections, and inflammatory conditions. The plant contains bioactive compounds that may exhibit antimicrobial and antioxidant effects when prepared as a decoction or poultice. Local populations often use infusions derived from its bark or leaves to alleviate stomach pains and digestive irregularities. Additionally, topical applications of the plant extracts are frequently employed to soothe irritations and promote the healing of minor wounds. These therapeutic actions highlight its importance within indigenous ethnobotanical practices for managing various physical ailments.
