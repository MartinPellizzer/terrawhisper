Larix chinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Larix chinensis?

Larix chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Pinidae. It further belongs to the order Pinales and the family Pinaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Larix, with the specific identifier being the species chinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Larix chinensis has deciduous needles arranged spirally on short lateral shoots and long upright branches, alongside woody cones that measure 2 to 4 centimeters in length. The needles are typically bright green and can reach lengths of 1 to 3 centimeters depending on their growth stage. During the autumn season, these needles transition to a distinct yellowish hue before shedding entirely for the winter. The tree features a conical crown and rugged, fissured bark that thickens as the specimen matures. Small, woody ovulate cones emerge on the branches, containing winged seeds designed for wind dispersal.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude montane regions of central and western China, specifically spanning across provinces such as Sichuan, Yunnan, Gansu, and Tibet. It primarily thrives in temperate coniferous forests situated at elevations ranging from 2,000 to 4,000 meters above sea level. The species prefers cool, moist environments and is often found on rocky slopes or along mountain ridges. Its range is largely dictated by the specific climatic conditions and soil types found within these rugged mountainous terrains. Because it is adapted to these niche habitats, its distribution remains relatively localized to the Sino-Himalayan mountain systems.
