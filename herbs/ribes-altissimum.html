Ribes altissimum

What's the taxonomical classification of Ribes altissimum?

Ribes altissimum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Saxifragales, placing it within the family Grossulariaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by the genus Ribes and is specifically designated as the species altissimum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ribes altissimum has deciduous, opposite, palmately lobed leaves with three to five lobes and coarsely serrated margins. The stems are typically woody and can vary in color from green to reddish-brown depending on the age of the growth. Small, bell-shaped flowers emerge from the leaf axils in clusters during the spring flowering season. These flowers are often yellowish or greenish and are characterized by their hanging posture. The plant eventually produces small, fleshy berries that transition from green to a dark purple or black color when fully ripe.
