What is Elymus nakaii?

Elymus nakaii is a perennial grass species that belongs to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tufted habit, 20-40 cm tall stems, and linear-lanceolate leaves that are 5-10 mm wide and 30-60 cm long, with a prominent midrib and a glabrous surface. The inflorescence is a spike-like panicle, 5-10 cm long, composed of 2-4 racemes, with 2-3 spikelets per raceme, each bearing 2-3 fertile florets. Each fertile floret is 8-10 mm long, with a 2-3 mm long lemma and a 4-5 mm long palea.

Elymus nakaii is endemic to the Korean Peninsula.

The main herbal preparations of Elymus nakaii are infusion, decoction, tincture, salve, and tea.

Tea is prepared by infusing the dried leaves and flowers in hot water, making it a popular remedy for digestive issues.
