Catevala glabrata

Catevala glabrata, commonly known as Catechu or Acacia catechu, is a plant species belonging to the family Fabaceae.

It is a deciduous tree native to the tropical forests of Asia, particularly in India and Southeast Asia.

The health benefits of Catevala glabrata include its ability to reduce inflammation, improve oral health, and act as an antioxidant, while its therapeutic actions include its use as an antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, and astringent.

The plant's bioactive constituents include catechin, gallic acid, and tannins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties and are often used in traditional medicine for their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects.

Possible Side Effects of Catevala glabrata

Catevala glabrata can have side effects if used improperly, such as inducing allergic reactions, skin irritation, and respiratory problems in sensitive individuals.

Prolonged exposure to the plant's extracts has also been linked to gastrointestinal issues, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea.

In rare cases, excessive consumption of catevala glabrata can cause liver damage and interact with certain medications, exacerbating underlying health conditions.

Furthermore, improper preparation and handling of the plant can lead to the accumulation of toxic compounds, increasing the risk of adverse reactions.
