Lasianthus chinensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lasianthus chinensis has opposite, elliptical to oblong leaves with acute tips and entire margins that are typically dark green and glossy on the upper surface. The leaf bases are generally cuneate, while the undersides often exhibit a paler coloration due to a different epidermal structure. Its stems are woody and can become somewhat branched as the plant matures into a shrub. Small, white, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils in clusters, characterized by a short corolla tube and spreading lobes. The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that transitions in color as it ripens.
