Adenophora pereskiifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Adenophora pereskiifolia has lanceolate to ovate leaves with serrated margins and a distinctive petiole structure that distinguishes it from related species. The foliage is typically arranged in an opposite or somewhat whorled pattern along the stem. The leaf surface can exhibit varying degrees of pubescence, which serves as a protective layer against environmental stressors. Its stems are generally upright and can reach moderate heights depending on the specific habitat.
