Variable Iris (Iris versicolor) Uses

Variable Iris is used to treat respiratory conditions such as bronchitis, respiratory allergies, cough, and infections. It helps alleviate mucous congestion and bronchial irritation, making it useful for respiratory tract infections and mucous membrane inflammation. It has also been historically applied for tuberculosis due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Variable Iris (Iris versicolor) Monograph

1. Bronchitis

Variable Iris (Iris versicolor) has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in treating bronchitis. Its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties help reduce mucus buildup and soothe bronchial irritation. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to alleviate coughing and ease breathing in cases of chronic bronchitis.

2. Respiratory allergies

Variable Iris (Iris versicolor) has been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of respiratory allergies due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion and allergic reactions, making it a valuable herb in herbal medicine for managing seasonal and environmental allergies.

3. Cough

Variable Iris (Iris versicolor) has been traditionally used to soothe coughs due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps loosen mucus and reduce irritation in the respiratory tract. Often prepared as a tea, it is valued in herbal medicine for its gentle yet effective action on persistent coughs.
