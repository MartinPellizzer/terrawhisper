10 Possible Side Effects Of Iris Versicolor

Iris versicolor has some side effects when used improperly, such as skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal issues.

These side effects are caused by the plant's active compounds, which can interact with the skin or digestive system, leading to redness, itching, and stomach cramps.

In severe cases, these side effects can worsen people's lives, causing chronic discomfort, skin conditions, and digestive problems that affect daily activities and overall well-being.

1. Increases blood pressure

Iris versicolor increases blood pressure due to its high concentration of iridoid glycosides, which can stimulate the heart and blood vessels, leading to increased cardiac output and peripheral resistance.

This may cause an increase in systolic and diastolic blood pressure levels, potentially posing a risk for individuals with hypertension or prehypertension.

2. Causes allergic reactions

Iris versicolor causes allergic reactions due to its potent bioactive compounds such as anthraquinone glycosides and iridoid glycosides.

These chemicals can trigger an immune response in some individuals, leading to symptoms like hives, itching, redness, and swelling.

The exact mechanisms behind this allergic reaction are not fully understood, but it is thought that the body's inability to process these compounds correctly leads to an overactive immune response, resulting in uncomfortable and potentially painful reactions.

3. Stimulates kidney failure

Iris versicolor stimulates kidney failure due to its high concentration of glycosides, which can lead to nephrotoxicity.

The plant's bioactive compounds, including iridin and iris saponin, may cause damage to the kidneys' filtering mechanisms, resulting in a decrease in glomerular filtration rate and an increase in blood urea nitrogen levels.

This can ultimately lead to kidney failure if left unchecked.

4. Affects digestive system

Iris versicolor affects digestive system due to its ability to stimulate bowel movements and alter the normal balance of gut bacteria.

The plant's iridoid glycosides may cause gastrointestinal upset, including diarrhea, bloating, and abdominal cramps in some individuals.

Additionally, the saponins present in Iris versicolor may irritate the mucous membranes, leading to stomach discomfort and nausea.

5. Interferes with medications

Iris versicolor interferes with medications by competing for protein binding sites in the liver.

This can lead to reduced efficacy of certain medications or increased risk of adverse reactions when taken concurrently. Additionally, Iris versicolor's constituents may induce enzyme production in the gut and liver, altering the metabolism of co-administered drugs.

As a result, individuals taking Iris versicolor supplements should be aware of potential interactions with prescription medications.

6. Triggers seizures

Iris versicolor triggers seizures due to its ability to interact with certain medications and increase the risk of seizure activity.

This is thought to occur because blue flag contains chemicals that can affect the nervous system and brain's electrical activity, potentially leading to a seizure episode.

7. Lowers blood sugar

Iris versicolor lowers blood sugar levels due to its ability to stimulate insulin production and improve glucose uptake in the body.

The plant's isoflavones, particularly irisolidin, have been shown to inhibit the enzyme dipeptidyl peptidase-4 (DPP-4), which breaks down incretin hormones that promote insulin secretion.

This results in increased insulin release, reduced blood sugar levels, and potential hypoglycemia symptoms.

8. Disrupts thyroid function

Iris versicolor disrupts thyroid function.

This is due to its potential to contain goitrin, a natural compound that can interfere with the body's ability to utilize iodine, a crucial element for thyroid hormone production.

When consumed in large amounts or over an extended period, goitrin may cause hypothyroidism or exacerbate existing thyroid conditions, leading to symptoms such as fatigue, weight gain, and cold intolerance.

9. Causes respiratory problems

Iris versicolor causes respiratory problems due to its high concentration of saponins and iridoid glycosides.

These compounds can stimulate the respiratory system, leading to symptoms such as coughing, wheezing, and shortness of breath in some individuals.

Additionally, the plant's volatile oils may also contribute to these issues by irritating the lungs and airways.

10. Leads to skin rashes

Iris versicolor leads to skin rashes in some individuals due to its allergenic properties.

The plant's sap contains iridoid glycosides, which can cause an allergic reaction when it comes into contact with human skin.

This reaction can lead to redness, itching, and inflammation, resulting in the development of skin rashes.
