Bridelia insulana

What's the taxonomical classification of Bridelia insulana?

Bridelia insulana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Malpighiales. It is a member of the family Phyllanthaceae, which contains the genus Bridelia, and is specifically identified by the species name insulana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bridelia insulana has woody branches with grayish bark and elliptical leaves that are oppositely arranged along the stems. The leaves feature entire margins and acute to acuminate tips with prominent pinnate venation. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in axillary clusters, often appearing in dense cymes. The plant develops small, fleshy drupes that transition in color as they mature. These fruits typically turn from green to a darker hue to attract seed dispersers.
