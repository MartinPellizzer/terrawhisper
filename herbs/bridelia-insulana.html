Bridelia insulana

What's the taxonomical classification of Bridelia insulana?

Bridelia insulana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Malpighiales. It is a member of the family Phyllanthaceae, which contains the genus Bridelia, and is specifically identified by the species name insulana.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Bridelia insulana contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including triterpenoids, flavonoids, tannins, and saponins that contribute to its pharmacological potential. These chemical constituents are primarily concentrated within the plant's leaves, bark, and roots. Research suggests that these specific compounds provide the plant with significant antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Some studies also indicate that the extracts may possess antioxidant capabilities helpful in neutralizing free radicals. This complex chemical profile supports the traditional use of the plant in treating various ailments in regional folk medicine.

What are the therapeutic actions of Bridelia insulana?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, stomach aches, intestinal worms, and various inflammatory conditions. Its medicinal properties are often utilized in traditional practices to address digestive issues and parasitic infestations. The leaves and bark may be applied topically to soothe skin irritations or wounds. Some practitioners also use decoctions made from the plant to manage fever and other systemic ailments. These applications demonstrate the plant's diverse role in managing both local and internal health concerns.
