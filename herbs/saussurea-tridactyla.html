Saussurea tridactyla

What's the taxonomical classification of Saussurea tridactyla?

Saussurea tridactyla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Asterales and is a member of the family Asteraceae. Specifically, it is categorized under the genus Saussurea, with the specific epithet tridactyla identifying the individual species.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea tridactyla has deeply three-lobed leaves that are lanceolate in shape and possess a distinctively serrated margin. The plant typically grows in a basal rosette formation with leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs. Its flowering stems are erect and can reach significant heights depending on the specific alpine habitat. The inflorescence consists of dense, yellow flower heads that are clustered at the top of the scape. These floral structures are supported by specialized bracts that protect the developing florets.

The distribution is heavily influenced by the specific microclimates provided by the mountain ranges of Central and South Asia. Because it requires cold, well-drained environments, its presence is restricted to specific montane zones within these territories. Such phytochemical profiles are essential for evaluating the plant's value in traditional and modern medicine.

According to a study published by "Journal of chromatographic science", a reversed-phase high-performance liquid chromatographic method was developed for the quantitative determination of 7 components of Saussurea tridactyla Sch.-Bip.ex Hook.f. These 7 components include umbelliferonglucoside, luteolin-7-O-beta-D-glucoside, rutin, apiigenin-7-O-beta-D-glucoside, kaempferol-3-O-beta-D-glucoside, apiigenin-7-O-beta-D-rutinoside, and luteolin. The analysis of these compounds uses a Diamonsil C(18) reverse-phase column with methanol and water as the mobile phase under gradient conditions for 60 min. This method provides selectivity, accuracy, precision, linearity, ruggedness, efficiency, and ease for analyzing the plant.
