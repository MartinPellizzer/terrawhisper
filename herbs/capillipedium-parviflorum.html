What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Capillipedium parviflorum has a slender, herbaceous growth habit characterized by fine, thread-like stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. Its inflorescence consists of minute blossoms that are often clustered in a way that mimics the appearance of fine hair. The root system is relatively shallow, supporting the light aerial parts of the organism. Through these diverse protein interactions, the plant exerts significant pharmacological effects against inflammation and oxidative stress.
