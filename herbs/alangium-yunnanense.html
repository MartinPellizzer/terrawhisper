Alangium yunnanense

What's the taxonomical classification of Alangium yunnanense?

Aliangium yunnanense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Cornales, it is a member of the family Cornaceae. Ultimately, the plant is categorized under the genus Aliangium, with its specific designation being the species yunnanense.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Alangium yunnanense contains a complex profile of essential oils primarily composed of terpenoids, phenylpropanoids, and various aliphatic compounds. These chemical constituents include significant amounts of linalool, geraniol, and cineole which contribute to its aromatic properties. The presence of these volatile molecules is believed to be responsible for the plant's documented antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory activities. Researchers have also identified specific flavonoids and phenolic acids that may offer antioxidant benefits during medicinal use. Such a diverse chemical makeup supports its traditional application in treating digestive and respiratory ailments.

What are the therapeutic actions of Alangium yunnanense?

Alangium yunnanense has therapeutic properties that primarily involve its ability to alleviate gastrointestinal distress, reduce inflammation, and act as a mild sedative through its concentrated essential oils. The plant contains significant levels of citral and limonene, which contribute to its effectiveness in calming stomach cramps and improving digestion. These chemical compounds also provide antimicrobial benefits that help combat minor infections within the digestive tract. When used in traditional preparations, the aromatic components can help soothe nervous tension and promote relaxation. Its multifaceted chemical profile makes it a valuable resource for addressing both physical discomfort and stress-related symptoms.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat digestive ailments such as abdominal pain, indigestion, bloating, and diarrhea. It is frequently employed in traditional medicine to alleviate stomach discomfort and regulate bowel movements. The aromatic compounds within the plant help to soothe the gastrointestinal tract and reduce inflammation. Practitioners often prepare decoctions or infusions to address these various internal maladies. These medicinal applications focus primarily on restoring balance to the digestive system.

What are the herbal preparations of Alangium yunnanense?

Alangium yunnanense is prepared primarily as a decoction, a concentrated liquid extract made by boiling the dried rhizomes in water to release their essential oils and active compounds. This medicinal tea is often consumed warm to soothe digestive issues or alleviate respiratory discomfort. In some traditional practices, the plant material is also infused into alcohol to create a tincture for long-term storage and potency. Some practitioners may grind the dried roots into a fine powder to be encapsulated or mixed directly into food. These various methods ensure that the aromatic constituents of the plant are effectively delivered to the body.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Alangium yunnanense can cause gastrointestinal distress, allergic skin reactions, or potential toxicity if consumed in excessive quantities. While often used in traditional practices, the essential oils within the plant may irritate the mucous membranes of the mouth and throat. Some individuals might experience hypersensitivity symptoms such as redness or itching upon topical contact. There is also a risk of stomach upset or nausea if the plant is ingested without proper preparation. Careful moderation is necessary to avoid adverse systemic effects.
