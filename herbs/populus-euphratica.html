What's the taxonomical classification of Populus euphratica?

Populus euphratica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Malpighiales. This plant is further categorized into the family Salicaceae, within the genus Populus, and is specifically identified by its species name, euphratica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Populus euphratica has a woody structure characterized by a single trunk that can reach heights of several meters, bearing lanceolate leaves with finely serrated margins and a glabrous surface. The bark is typically greyish or light brown and develops shallow longitudinal fissures as the specimen matures. Its reproductive organs consist of catkins that emerge from the lateral branches, featuring small, inconspicuous flowers adapted for wind pollination. The root system is extensive and deep, allowing the plant to thrive in riparian environments near water sources. This species exhibits a streamlined foliage shape that minimizes wind resistance in its natural habitat.
