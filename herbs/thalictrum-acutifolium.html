Thalictrum acutifolium

What's the taxonomical classification of Thalictrum acutifolium?

Thalictrum acuifolum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and placed under the order Ranunculales. As a member of the family Ranunculaceae, this plant is situated within the genus Thalictrum, eventually reaching its specific designation as the species acuifolum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Thalictrum acuifolum has basal and cauline leaves that are compound, ternately divided, and characterized by sharply pointed, lanceolate leaflets. The plant typically reaches a height of 30 to 70 centimeters with erect, slender stems. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are greenish-white and arranged in loose, terminal panicles. The reproductive structures include numerous stamens that surround a central cluster of carpels. These morphological traits allow the species to thrive in shaded, moist woodland environments.
