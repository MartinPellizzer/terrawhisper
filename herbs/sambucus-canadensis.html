Elderberry (Sambucus canadensis)

Elderberry (Sambucus canadensis) is a member of the Caprifoliaceae family, native to Eastern North America, Canada, and United States. Traditionally, its fruits, leaves, and bark have been used for decoctions, infusions, and culinary uses.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, bitter, and diuretic actions, and has a long history of use in native american herbal medicine, european herbal medicine, and mediterranean herbal traditions.

Botanical Description

Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically reaches a height of 1.5 to 4 meters.
Leaves
Ovate leaves with serrated margins, upper surface dark green, lower surface lighter green, possessing a single prominent midvein and two lateral veins.
Flowers
Inflorescence composed of dense clusters of small, white, actinomorphic flowers with five sepals and five petals, each flower lacking nectar guides and having a short pedicel.
Stems
Woody, erect growth habit with opposite branching, smooth bark, and persistent stipules.
