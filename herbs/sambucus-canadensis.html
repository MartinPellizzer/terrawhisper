Elderberry

Sambucus canadensis

Elderberry (Sambucus canadensis) is a deciduous shrub native to North America, known for its clusters of small, dark purple berries and its long history of use in traditional medicine. It is primarily used to support immune health, particularly in the treatment of colds, flu, and other viral respiratory infections.

Taxonomy

The taxonomy of Elderberry (Sambucus canadensis) is classified within the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Dipsacales, family Caprifoliaceae, genus Sambucus, and species Sambucus canadensis.

Morphology

Elderberry (Sambucus canadensis) is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its compound leaves, clusters of small white flowers, and berries that range in color from green to black, typically ripening in late summer to early fall.

Distribution

Elderberry (Sambucus canadensis) is native to eastern North America, ranging from Canada southward through the eastern United States, including states such as New York, Pennsylvania, and Maryland, and extending into the Appalachian Mountains.

Traditional Uses

Elderberry has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat respiratory infections, reduce fever, alleviate inflammation, and boost the immune system, with historical applications dating back to Native American tribes and medieval European practices.

Medicinal Systems

In traditional medicinal systems such as Native American herbalism, European folk medicine, and traditional Chinese medicine, Elderberry (Sambucus canadensis) has been widely used for its antiviral, anti-inflammatory, and immune-supporting properties.

Phytochemistry

The phytochemistry of Elderberry (Sambucus canadensis) includes bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, anthocyanins, phenolic acids, and polysaccharides, which contribute to its medicinal properties and potential health benefits.

Pharmacology

Elderberry exhibits antiviral, anti-inflammatory, and immunostimulant properties, making it effective in supporting respiratory health, reducing fever, and enhancing the body's natural defenses against infections.

Botanical Identification

Sambucus canadensis, commonly known as American elderberry, is a perennial shrub native to North America. Belonging to the Caprifoliaceae family, it is also called black elderberry. The plant features compound leaves, white flowers, and dark berries. It has been referred to by various regional names in herbal literature.

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for respiratory infections and inflammation due to its antimicrobial properties.

Infusion: Used for digestive issues and cold symptoms due to its mild soothing effects.

Poultice: Used for topical application on wounds and skin irritations due to its anti-inflammatory properties.

Safety Profile

Elderberry is generally safe when consumed in recommended doses. However, it should not be used by individuals with autoimmune disorders or those taking immunosuppressants. Consult a healthcare provider before use, especially during pregnancy or breastfeeding.
