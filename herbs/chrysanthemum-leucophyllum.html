Possible Side Effects of Chrysanthemum leucophyllum

Chrysanthemum leucophyllum can have side effects if used improperly, such as causing allergic reactions, including skin irritation, itching, and swelling, due to its high content of sesquiterpene lactones.

The plant's essential oils may cause respiratory problems, including coughing and shortness of breath, especially for individuals with pre-existing respiratory conditions.

In severe cases, improper use of the plant may cause anaphylaxis, a life-threatening allergic reaction, requiring immediate medical attention.
