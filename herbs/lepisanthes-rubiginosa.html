Lepisanthes rubiginosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Lepisanthes rubiginosa?

Lepisanthes rubiginosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Sapindales. The plant is a member of the family Sapindaceae, specifically falling under the genus Lepisanthes, and is identified as the species rubiginosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lepisanthes rubiginosa has woody climbing vines characterized by ovate to elliptical leaves with entire margins and an acute apex. The foliage typically exhibits a glossy texture and a deep green color on the upper surface. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from axillary positions to form compact inflorescences. These flowers eventually develop into fleshy, globose fruits that change color as they mature. The stems are often lenticellate and provide the structural support necessary for the plant to ascend through forest canopies.

What are the pharmacological activities of Lepisanthes rubiginosa?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological activities including anticholinesterase activity, antioxidant properties, and antimicrobial effects. Research indicates that its bioactive compounds can inhibit acetylcholinesterase, which is a crucial mechanism for potential neuroprotective treatments. These phytochemical constituents also serve to neutralize free radicals within biological systems. Furthermore, the extracts exhibit the ability to suppress the growth of various pathogenic microorganisms. Such diverse therapeutic potential suggests that the species holds value for future drug development.

According to a study published by "Zeitschrift fur Naturforschung. C, Journal of biosciences", the essential oil obtained from Lepisanthes rubiginosa leaves contains 24 identified components that represent 99.5% of the oil. The major chemical components consist of α-cadinol (40.0%), safrole (12.6%), α-amorphene (9.5%), (E)-isosafrole (5.0%), δ-cadinene (4.2%), and t-muurolol (4.1%). This essential oil demonstrated moderate anticholinesterase inhibitory activity at a concentration of 1000 μg/mL. Specifically, the oil showed an inhibitory activity of 75.2% against acetylcholinesterase and 70.2% against butyrylcholinesterase. These findings regarding the pharmacological activities of Lepisanthes rubiginosa may have implications for its therapeutic applications.
