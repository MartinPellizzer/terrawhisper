Lepisanthes rubiginosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Lepisanthes rubiginosa?

Lepisanthes rubiginosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Sapindales. The plant is a member of the family Sapindaceae, specifically falling under the genus Lepisanthes, and is identified as the species rubiginosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lepisanthes rubiginosa has woody climbing vines characterized by ovate to elliptical leaves with entire margins and an acute apex. The foliage typically exhibits a glossy texture and a deep green color on the upper surface. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from axillary positions to form compact inflorescences. These flowers eventually develop into fleshy, globose fruits that change color as they mature. The stems are often lenticellate and provide the structural support necessary for the plant to ascend through forest canopies.

What are the pharmacological activities of Lepisanthes rubiginosa?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological activities including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and anticholinesterase activity. The presence of bioactive phytochemicals allows these extracts to neutralize free radicals and mitigate oxidative stress within biological systems. Research into its anticholinesterase activity suggests a potential role in managing neurodegenerative conditions by maintaining acetylcholine levels. Furthermore, the plant exhibits various antimicrobial properties that can inhibit the growth of specific pathogens. These diverse biological effects underscore the medicinal potential of Lepisanthes rubiginosa in therapeutic applications.

As discussed in a study, the essential oil obtained from Lepisanthes rubiginosa leaves collected from Malaysia contains 24 identified components representing 99.5% of the oil. Major components include alpha-cadinol at 40.0%, safrole at 12.6%, alpha-amorphene at 9.5%, (E)-isosafrole at 5.0%, delta-cadinene at 4.2%, and t-muurolol at 4.1%. The essential oil demonstrated moderate anticholinesterase inhibitory activity at a concentration of 1000 μg/mL. Specifically, the inhibitory activity against acetylcholinesterase was 75.2% and against butyrylcholinesterase was 70.2%. These findings provide the first report on the chemical composition and anticholinesterase activity of the Lepisanthes rubiginosa essential oil.
