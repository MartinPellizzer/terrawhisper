Landolphia glandulosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Landolphia glandulosa?

Landolphia glandulosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized under the order Gentianaales. This plant is a member of the family Apocynaceae, which places it within the genus Landolphia, specifically identifying it as the species glandulosa.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Landolphia glandulosa contains bioactive alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, and terpenoids that provide its various therapeutic properties. These secondary metabolites are distributed throughout the leaves, bark, and roots of the plant. Researchers have identified specific chemical constituents that exhibit significant antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory activities. Some studies suggest that extracts from the foliage can inhibit the growth of certain pathogenic bacteria. Additionally, the presence of antioxidant compounds helps mitigate oxidative stress in biological systems. These chemical profiles make the plant a subject of interest for developing new pharmacological treatments.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various conditions including malaria, skin infections, stomach aches, and inflammation. The leaves and bark are frequently prepared as decoctions or infusions to combat parasitic infections and fever. Local medicinal practices also employ the plant to soothe gastrointestinal distress and promote wound healing. Additionally, its properties are utilized to manage minor skin ailments through topical applications. Researchers continue to study its bioactive compounds to better understand its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects.
