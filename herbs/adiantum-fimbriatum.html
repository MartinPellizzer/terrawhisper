Adiantum fimbriatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Adiantum fimbriatum?

Adiantum fimbriatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is classified under the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. Further taxonomic refinement places this plant in the family Pteridaceae, specifically within the genus Adiantum, and is identified by its unique species designation, fimbriatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Adiantum fimbriatum has delicate, fan-shaped fronds composed of small, rounded, or lobed pinnules attached to thin, dark, wire-like stipes. The leaves exhibit a light green color and a fine, lace-like texture due to their intricate division. Along the margins of the pinnules, tiny marginal sori are located beneath a thin, reflexed edge. The plant grows from a creeping rhizome that helps establish its spreading habit. These structural features allow the fern to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
