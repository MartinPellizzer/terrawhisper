What's the taxonomical classification of Hypodematium sinense?

Hypodematium sinense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae. Following the order Polypodiales, the plant is placed in the family Hypodematiaceae. Its taxonomic hierarchy concludes with its designation in the genus Hypodematium, specifically within the species sinense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hypodematium sinense has a prostrate, creeping rhizome that produces thin, dichotomously branched stems bearing small, opposite, or whorled leaves. These stems often develop a reddish or brownish coloration and can grow quite long across the soil surface. Small, inconspicuous flowers appear in the leaf axils, eventually giving way to tiny, inconspicuous fruits.
