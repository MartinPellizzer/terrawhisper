Spineapple

Zanthoxylum clava-herculis

Spineapple (Zanthoxylum clava-herculis) is a woody shrub native to the southeastern United States, known for its spiny stems and aromatic leaves, and it has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat digestive issues and reduce inflammation.

Taxonomy

Spineapple, scientifically known as Zanthoxylum clava-herculis, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Sapindales, family Rutaceae, genus Zanthoxylum, and species Zanthoxylum clava-herculis.

Morphology

Spineapple, or Zanthoxylum clava-herculis, is a perennial shrub characterized by its compound leaves with 10-20 pairs of elliptic to ovate leaflets, red or orange spines along the stems, and yellow-orange flowers arranged in clusters, followed by red, fleshy, berry-like fruits.

Distribution

Spineapple, or Zanthoxylum clava-herculis, is native to the southeastern United States, ranging from Florida through Texas and extending into parts of the Midwest, particularly in areas with moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and woodland edges.

Traditional Uses

Spineapple, or Zanthoxylum clava-herculis, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its analgesic, anti-inflammatory, and digestive properties, often prepared as a decoction or topical application to treat pain, digestive issues, and skin conditions.

Medicinal Systems

In traditional medicinal systems such as Ayurveda, Traditional Chinese Medicine, and Native American healing practices, Spineapple (Zanthoxylum clava-herculis) has been used for its anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and digestive properties.

Phytochemistry

Spineapple (Zanthoxylum clava-herculis) contains a variety of bioactive compounds, including alkaloids, flavonoids, lignans, and terpenoids, which contribute to its pharmacological properties and traditional use in herbal medicine.

Pharmacology

Spineapple, or Zanthoxylum clava-herculis, exhibits pharmacological properties including anti-inflammatory, analgesic, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, which contribute to its traditional use in treating pain, inflammation, and infections.

Botanical Identification

Spineapple, or Zanthoxylum clava-herculis, is a member of the Rutaceae family. Commonly known as "hedge apple" or "spineapple tree," it is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States. The plant has thorned branches, pinnate leaves, and yellow flowers, with fruit resembling a spiky apple. It has also been referred to as "horse apple" in some herbal texts.

Safety Profile

Spineapple, or Zanthoxylum clava-herculis, has a safety profile that generally supports its use in traditional herbal medicine. However, it may cause gastrointestinal irritation in some individuals. It is contraindicated for pregnant women and those with sensitive stomach conditions due to its potent effects. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
