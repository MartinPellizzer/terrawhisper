Spineapple

Zanthoxylum clava-herculis

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family

Native Region

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Spineapple, or Zanthoxylum clava-herculis, is a member of the Rutaceae family. Commonly known as "hedge apple" or "spineapple tree," it is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States. The plant has thorned branches, pinnate leaves, and yellow flowers, with fruit resembling a spiky apple. It has also been referred to as "horse apple" in some herbal texts.

Active Compounds

Spineapple contains key active compounds such as tannins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids. These bioactives contribute to its medicinal properties by providing antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. They work synergistically to enhance the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

  • Tannin
  • Flavonoid
  • Phenolic acid

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory ailments.

Powder: Used for external applications like skin irritations.

Culinary use: Used for flavoring and as a condiment in some cultures.

Safety Profile

Spineapple, or Zanthoxylum clava-herculis, has a safety profile that generally supports its use in traditional herbal medicine. However, it may cause gastrointestinal irritation in some individuals. It is contraindicated for pregnant women and those with sensitive stomach conditions due to its potent effects. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
