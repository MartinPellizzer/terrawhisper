What to know about Zanthoxylum clava-herculis (prickly ash) before using it medicinally

Prickly ash, scientifically known as Zanthoxylum clava-herculis, is a herb that has been touted for its numerous health benefits, which include reducing pain and inflammation, improving circulation, and boosting the immune system.

While it's often used in traditional medicine to treat various ailments, prickly ash is also a popular ingredient in many culinary dishes, particularly in Asian cuisine, where it adds a unique flavor and aroma to stir-fries, soups, and marinades. As a member of the Rutaceae family, prickly ash is also a significant ornamental plant in horticultural circles, prized for its attractive foliage and vibrant yellow flowers. From a botanical standpoint, prickly ash is characterized by its distinctive twigs, which are covered with tiny thorns and have a pungent, aromatic scent.

With its long history of use dating back to ancient Chinese medicine, prickly ash has been revered for centuries as a valuable component of traditional remedies and cultural practices.
