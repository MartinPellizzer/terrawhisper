Syringa reticulata

What's the taxonomical classification of Syringa reticulata?

Syringa reticulaata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Oleaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Syringa, with its specific designation being the species reticulaata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Syringa reticulaata has opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that measure approximately 2 to 5 inches in length and feature entire margins with a smooth texture. The foliage is typically a deep green color and possesses a slightly leathery consistency. During the blooming season, the plant produces dense, upright panicles of fragrant, tubular flowers that can appear in shades of white, pink, or lavender. Its woody stems develop a grayish-brown bark that becomes increasingly textured as the shrub matures. The overall growth habit is rounded and spreading, often reaching heights and widths of several feet.
