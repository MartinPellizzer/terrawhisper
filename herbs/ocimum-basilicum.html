Basil

Ocimum basilicum

Basil (Ocimum basilicum) is a fragrant herb commonly used in culinary and medicinal practices, known for its ability to improve symptoms of digestive issues such as indigestion and bloating.

Botanical Identification

Basil, scientifically known as Ocimum basilicum, is a member of the Lamiaceae family. Commonly called sweet basil, it is an annual herb native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa. It has aromatic leaves, opposite branching, and small blue or purple flowers. Synonyms include Ocimum basilicum var. purpureum and Basilicum in older herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Lamiales
  • Order: Lamiales
  • Family: Lamiaceae
  • Genus: Ocimum
  • Species: Ocimum basilicum

Active Compounds

Basil contains essential oils, flavonoids, and terpenoids as its main bioactive compounds. These compounds work together to exert their therapeutic effects, with essential oils playing a key role in its aromatic and medicinal properties. Flavonoids and terpenoids may enhance the overall efficacy through synergistic interactions.

  • Essential oil
  • Flavonoid
  • Terpenoid

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for digestive issues and respiratory ailments.

Essential oil: Used for headaches, insect repellent, and stress relief.

Decoction: Used for treating colds and flu symptoms.

Safety Profile

Basil is generally safe when used in typical culinary amounts. However, it should be avoided in large doses or by pregnant women due to potential uterine stimulation. High doses may cause gastrointestinal upset or allergic reactions in sensitive individuals.
