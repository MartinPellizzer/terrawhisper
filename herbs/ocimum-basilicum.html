Basil (Ocimum basilicum)

Basil (Ocimum basilicum) is a member of the Lamiaceae family, native to South Asia, East Africa, and Southern Europe. Traditionally, its leaves, flowers, and stems have been used for infusions, decoctions, and culinary uses.

This herb is particularly valued for its antispasmodic, carminative, and anti-inflammatory actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and ayurvedic medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Annual herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters.
Leaves
Simple leaves with opposite phyllotaxy, upper surface glabrous and dark green, lower surface pale green with prominent stomatal bands.
Flowers
Inflorescences are spike-like clusters with small, tubular, violet to purple flowers arranged in whorls, each having five lobed corolla with two lips, exhibiting zygomorphic symmetry and two stamens with distinct anther positions.
Stems
Elongated, erect growth habit with opposite branching, smooth, glabrous surface, and presence of pith in cross-section.
