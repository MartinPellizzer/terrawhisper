What to know about Ocimum basilicum before using it medicinally

Ocimum basilicum, commonly known as sweet basil, is a herb that has been widely used in traditional medicine for its numerous health benefits.

Its main medicinal uses include treating digestive issues such as nausea and vomiting, as well as reducing inflammation and alleviating symptoms of respiratory conditions like bronchitis and asthma. The herb has been found to have several health benefits, including antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which may help protect against cell damage and reduce the risk of chronic diseases. The main therapeutic properties of Ocimum basilicum include its ability to act as an antibacterial, antifungal, and antiviral agent, making it effective in treating infections and promoting wound healing.

Bioactive compounds found in the herb include eugenol, linalool, and ursolic acid, which are responsible for its medicinal properties, and people often prepare a tea by infusing the leaves in hot water to take advantage of its benefits.

Featured Study:

According to a study published by "Drug and chemical toxicology", the extract of Ocimum basilicum has been shown to have a concentration-dependent effect on reducing inflammation and oxidative stress in sensitized rats. Specifically, it was found to decrease tracheal responsiveness, total white blood cell count, eosinophils, monocytes, neutrophils, and oxidant biomarkers, while increasing lymphocytes and antioxidant biomarkers. These results indicate that Ocimum basilicum may have potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which could be beneficial in managing respiratory conditions.
