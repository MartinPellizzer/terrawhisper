Lithocarpus corneus

What's the taxonomical classification of Lithocarpus corneus?

Lithocarpus corneus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Fagales. It is further categorized under the family Fagaceae, placing it among a diverse group of nut-bearing trees. Within this family, it is situated in the genus Lithocarpus, and its specific biological identity is defined by the species name corneus.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Lithocarpus corneus contains medicinal compounds primarily found in its leaves, bark, and fruit. The leaves are frequently utilized in traditional practices to address various inflammatory conditions. Extracts derived from the bark are often applied topically or ingested to treat digestive ailments. Additionally, the fruits are sometimes consumed for their nutritional value and potential therapeutic effects. These various components work together to provide a diverse range of bioactive substances used in folk medicine.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse array of bioactive substances including flavonoids, tannins, phenolic compounds, and terpenoids that contribute to its medicinal potential.

The presence of various phenolic compounds provides significant antioxidant properties that help neutralize free radicals in the body. Tannins within the plant tissues are known for their astringent qualities and ability to assist in wound healing. Flavonoids offer additional anti-inflammatory and neuroprotective benefits through their chemical interactions with cellular pathways.

Furthermore, the terpenoids present in the species may exhibit antimicrobial and anticancer activities.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Lithocarpus corneus targets inflammatory mediators such as cyclooxygenase-2, inducible nitric oxide synthase, and various pro-inflammatory cytokines like tumor necrosis factor-alpha and interleukin-6. These bioactive polyphenols and tannins inhibit these specific protein enzymes to reduce oxidative stress and systemic inflammation in biological systems. The plant's secondary metabolites also interact with signaling pathways involving nuclear factor kappa B to suppress the transcription of inflammatory genes. Additionally, certain compounds in the extract exhibit affinity for proteases and other enzymatic targets that contribute to its antioxidant and neuroprotective activities. These diverse molecular interactions collectively underpin the plant's traditional use in treating inflammatory and metabolic conditions.

What are the therapeutic actions of Lithocarpus corneus?

Lithocarpus corneus has medicinal properties primarily characterized by its antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial activities derived from its rich concentration of polyphenols and tannins. These bioactive compounds work to neutralize free radicals, which helps protect cells from oxidative stress and damage. The plant's extracts are often utilized for their ability to reduce swelling and soothe inflammation in various biological tissues. Additionally, the presence of specific phytochemicals provides a natural defense against the growth of certain harmful bacteria and fungi. These therapeutic actions make the plant a subject of interest for developing natural treatments for inflammatory and infectious conditions.

    What are the herbal preparations of Lithocarpus corneus?

    Lithocarpus corneus is prepared primarily as a decoction made by boiling the dried leaves, bark, or fruit in water to extract its medicinal compounds. This liquid extract is often consumed as a warm tea to address specific digestive or inflammatory conditions. In some traditional practices, the plant material may also be processed into a fine powder that is mixed with honey or warm water. Additionally, certain parts of the plant are used to create topical washes or infusions for external applications. These various methods ensure that the active constituents are effectively released for therapeutic use.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Lithocarpus corneus can cause adverse reactions such as gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, or allergic responses if ingested or applied topically without caution. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, the presence of tannins in many species within this genus may lead to stomach upset or nausea. Some individuals might experience localized redness or itching when coming into contact with the plant's leaves or bark. It is essential to avoid consuming any part of the plant unless its safety is confirmed by an expert. Always consult a medical professional if you suspect you have reacted poorly to this botanical species.
