Lithocarpus corneus

What's the taxonomical classification of Lithocarpus corneus?

Lithocarpus corneus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Fagales. It is further categorized under the family Fagaceae, placing it among a diverse group of nut-bearing trees. Within this family, it is situated in the genus Lithocarpus, and its specific biological identity is defined by the species name corneus.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Lithocarpus corneus contains high concentrations of polyphenolic compounds, specifically tannins, flavonoids, and triterpenoids, which exhibit significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These bioactive constituents are primarily concentrated within the leaves and the bark of the tree. Researchers have identified that the tannins in the plant can assist in reducing oxidative stress within cellular structures. Furthermore, the specific flavonoids present contribute to the inhibition of various inflammatory pathways. The combination of these medicinal elements suggests potential applications in managing metabolic and inflammatory conditions.

What are the therapeutic actions of Lithocarpus corneus?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various inflammatory conditions, digestive issues, and skin ailments through its traditional medicinal applications. The extracts are often applied topically to soothe skin irritation or minor wounds. When consumed, the plant components may help alleviate symptoms related to stomach discomfort or gastrointestinal distress. Some practitioners also utilize its properties to manage systemic inflammation within the body. These uses are rooted in regional ethnobotanical practices where the species is native.
