Lithocarpus corneus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lithocarpus corneus has simple, alternate, coriaceous leaves that are typically elliptical to oblong in shape with entire or slightly serrated margins and an acute apex. The leaf surfaces often exhibit a lustrous, dark green appearance on the upper side while the undersides remain slightly paler. Its woody stems develop into medium-sized trees characterized by a rough, fissured bark texture as they mature. The reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually produce woody cupules enclosing single acorns. These cupules are distinctively scaled and serve as a primary diagnostic feature for identifying the species.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the temperate and subtropical forests of East Asia, specifically occurring within China, Japan, and Korea. It is primarily found growing in mountain regions where it thrives in humid, montane environments. Within these habitats, the species often inhabits broadleaf evergreen forests or mixed coniferous forests at various elevations. Its range extends across diverse provinces in China and throughout the volcanic islands of the Japanese archipelago. Localized populations are also documented across the Korean Peninsula.

      What are the herbal preparations of Lithocarpus corneus?

      Lithocarpus corneus is prepared primarily as a decoction made by boiling the dried leaves, bark, or fruit in water to extract its medicinal compounds. This liquid extract is often consumed as a warm tea to address specific digestive or inflammatory conditions.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Lithocarpus corneus can cause adverse reactions such as gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, or allergic responses if ingested or applied topically without caution. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, the presence of tannins in many species within this genus may lead to stomach upset or nausea.
