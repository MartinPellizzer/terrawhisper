What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ranunculus glareosus has a perennial growth habit characterized by creeping rhizomes, basal leaves with long petioles, and bright yellow, five-petaled flowers. Its flowering stems are erect and can reach heights of up to thirty centimeters, supporting multiple solitary blossoms. Each flower features numerous yellow stamens and many carpels centered within a glossy, waxy receptacle.
