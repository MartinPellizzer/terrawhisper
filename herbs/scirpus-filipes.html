What's the taxonomical classification of Scirpus filipes?

Scirpus filipes belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is positioned in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Poales. Its taxonomic journey continues through the family Cyperaceae and the genus Sciurpous, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species filipes.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Scirpus filipes has slender, cylindrical, thread-like leaves and stems that typically measure between 10 and 60 centimeters in length.

The plant is characterized by its fine, wiry appearance and smooth, green surfaces. Its inflorescence consists of small, brownish flower clusters arranged in compact heads along the upper portion of the stem. The scale-like bracts protecting the flowers are small and inconspicuous to the naked eye.

These delicate structures allow the plant to thrive in moist or wetland environments where its minimal surface area reduces water loss.
