Picea asperata

What's the taxonomical classification of Picea asperata?

Picea asperata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Pinidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Pinales and the family Pinaceae. Finally, it is identified by its genus, Picea, and its specific species name, asperata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Picea asperata has a conical growth habit characterized by a single central trunk and branches that extend in whorls. The needles are stiff, acute, and arranged spirally around the twigs, often appearing somewhat spreading. Its bark is typically reddish-brown and becomes scaly or fissured as the tree matures. The cones are relatively small and cylindrical, typically measuring between three and six centimeters in length. These woody structures hang downward from the branches to protect the developing seeds.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of western China, specifically within the mountainous areas of Gansu, Qinghai, Sichuan, and Yunnan provinces. It thrives primarily in subalpine zones where rocky slopes and montane forests provide its necessary habitat. The species is typically found at elevations ranging from 2,500 to 4,000 meters above sea level. Its distribution is limited by its preference for cool, moist climates and well-drained mountainous terrain.
