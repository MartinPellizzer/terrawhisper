Gelsemium

Gelsemium sempervirens

Gelsemium (Gelsemium sempervirens) is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States, known for its use in traditional medicine to treat conditions such as anxiety, muscle spasms, and neurological disorders.

Botanical Identification

Gelsemium sempervirens is a perennial climbing vine native to the southeastern United States. It belongs to the family Loganiaceae and is commonly known as yellow jessamine. The plant has yellow flowers, opposite leaves, and a woody stem, with synonyms including "dollar vine" and "evergreen jessamine" in traditional herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Eudicots
  • Order: Loganiaceae
  • Family: Loganiaceae
  • Genus: Gelsemium
  • Species: Gelsemium sempervirens

Gelsemium Uses.

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for treating nervous system disorders and as a sedative.

Tincture: Used for alleviating symptoms of nervousness and anxiety.

Extract: Used for treating muscle spasms and nerve pain.

Safety Profile

Gelsemium is contraindicated in patients with severe hypertension or those undergoing surgery due to its potential to lower blood pressure. It should be used with caution in individuals with liver or kidney disease. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
