What's the taxonomical classification of Myricaria paniculata?

Myricaria paniculata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Caryophyllales. Within this order, the plant is a member of the family Tamaricaceae and the genus Myricaria, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species paniculata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Myrica paniculata has erect, perennial stems that reach heights of up to 2 meters and are characterized by a dense, branching structure with linear, lanceolate leaves. The leaves are arranged alternately along the stem and feature finely serrated margins with a dark green upper surface. These plants produce large, terminal panicles that consist of numerous small, inconspicuous flowers. The resulting fruits are small, drupe-like structures that are often covered in a fine, brownish pubescence.
