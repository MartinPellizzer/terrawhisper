Ilex kwangtungensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Ilex kwangtungensis?

Ilex kwangtungensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Aquifoliales, it is situated in the family Aquifoliaceae. Ultimately, this plant is identified by the genus Ilex and is specifically designated as the species kwangtungensis.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Ilex kwangtungensis can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if ingested in large quantities due to the presence of saponins and other secondary metabolites. While specific clinical data on human side effects are limited, many species within the Ilex genus contain compounds that may trigger allergic contact dermatitis upon skin contact. Ingesting parts of the plant may lead to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal cramping in sensitive individuals. There is also a risk of systemic toxicity if the bioactive constituents interfere with metabolic processes. Caution should always be exercised when handling or consuming any unidentified plant species to avoid these adverse reactions.
