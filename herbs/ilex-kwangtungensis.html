Ilex kwangtungensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ilex kwangtungensis has evergreen leaves that are typically coriaceous, elliptical to oblong, and feature serrated margins with acute apices. The leaf surfaces are characterized by a dark green luster on the upper side and a slightly paler hue underneath. Its stems exhibit a woody texture, transitioning from greenish hues in young growth to a brownish bark in mature specimens. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into fleshy drupes. These fruits serve as a primary morphological feature, often changing color as they reach maturity.
