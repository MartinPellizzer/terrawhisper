Ilex kwangtungensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Ilex kwangtungensis?

Ilex kwangtungensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Aquifoliales, it is situated in the family Aquifoliaceae. Ultimately, this plant is identified by the genus Ilex and is specifically designated as the species kwangtungensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ilex kwangtungensis has evergreen leaves that are typically coriaceous, elliptical to oblong, and feature serrated margins with acute apices.

The leaf surfaces are characterized by a dark green luster on the upper side and a slightly paler hue underneath. Its stems exhibit a woody texture, transitioning from greenish hues in young growth to a brownish bark in mature specimens. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into fleshy drupes.

These fruits serve as a primary morphological feature, often changing color as they reach maturity.
