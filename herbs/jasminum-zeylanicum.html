Common Jasmine (Jasminum Zeylanicum)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Jasminum zeylanicum.

Common jasmine, scientifically known as Jasminum zeylanicum, is a fragrant flowering plant widely recognized as a medicinal herb with adaptogenic properties. It is traditionally used for its calming effects and is valued for its ability to reduce stress and anxiety, making it a popular remedy in herbal medicine. In traditional Ayurvedic and Chinese medicine, it has been employed to treat respiratory issues, digestive disorders, and skin conditions, while also being used in cultural rituals to promote spiritual well-being. Modern wellness practices have incorporated it into aromatherapy and as a natural sleep aid due to its soothing aroma and potential sedative properties. One of its unique features is the presence of a rare active compound called jasmonic acid, which contributes to its distinctive floral scent and therapeutic benefits, and it has a long history of use in Southeast Asian cultures for both medicinal and ornamental purposes.
