Cardamine flexuosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Cardamine flexuosa?

Cardamine flexuosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Brassicales. As a member of the family Brassicaceae, this plant is further categorized into the genus Cardamine, specifically identifying it as the species flexuosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cardamine flexuosa has slender, ascending to erect stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and feature small, ovate to lanceolate leaves arranged in an alternate pattern. The foliage consists of simple, entire leaves on the lower portions of the plant, while the upper leaves often appear more deeply lobed or pinnatifid. Its flowers are small and white with four distinct petals, arranged in loose racemes at the terminal ends of the stems. The plant also produces small, cylindrical seed pods known as siliques that follow the flowering stage. These morphological traits allow the species to thrive in moist, shaded woodland environments.
