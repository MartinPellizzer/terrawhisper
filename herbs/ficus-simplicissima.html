Ficus simplicissima

What's the taxonomical classification of Ficus simplicissima?

Ficus simplicissima belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Rosales. The plant is further categorized into the family Moraeceae and is situated within the genus Ficus, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species simplicissima.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ficus simplicissima has simple, elliptical to oblong leaves that are typically 5 to 15 centimeters long with entire margins and an acute apex. The foliage is characterized by a glossy, dark green upper surface and a paler underside. These leaves are arranged alternately along woody, branching stems that form a dense, shrubby or small tree-like structure. The plant produces small, inconspicuous syconia, which are the specialized inflorescences typical of the genus. These fruit-like structures develop directly from the leaf axils or on specialized branches.
