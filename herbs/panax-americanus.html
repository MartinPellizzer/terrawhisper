Medicinal Parts

Panax americanus root, also known as American ginseng, is one of the most valued medicinal parts of this herb, renowned for its adaptogenic properties that help the body manage stress and fatigue.

The root contains a variety of bioactive compounds, including ginsenosides, which are believed to enhance cognitive function, boost energy levels, and support immune system health. It is commonly used in traditional medicine to promote vitality and improve overall well-being.

The root is typically harvested after several years of growth, as it is believed that the longer it matures, the more potent its medicinal properties become. In addition to the root, the leaves of Panax americanus are also used in some traditional remedies, though they are less commonly utilized compared to the root. The leaves contain similar compounds to the root, such as flavonoids and polysaccharides, which may contribute to its potential health benefits.

While the root remains the primary focus of modern herbal medicine, the leaves offer additional avenues for research and application in natural health practices.
