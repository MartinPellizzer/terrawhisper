American ginseng

Panax americanus

American Ginseng (Panax americanus) is a perennial plant native to the eastern regions of North America, known for its adaptogenic properties and traditional use in supporting energy, cognitive function, and overall well-being.

Botanical Identification

American Ginseng (Panax americana) is a perennial herb native to North America. It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is also known as "Canadian ginseng" or "American panax." The plant features a fleshy root, palmately lobed leaves, and small greenish-white flowers. Its scientific name distinguishes it from other ginseng species, such as Panax ginseng.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Magnoliopsida
  • Order: Apiales
  • Family: Araliaceae
  • Genus: Panax
  • Species: Panax americanus

Active Compounds

American Ginseng contains key active compounds such as flavonoids, saponins, and glycosides. These bioactive compounds work synergistically to support immune function and cognitive health. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic effects in herbal medicine.

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory ailments and digestive issues.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and colds.

Extract: Used for anxiety and stress-related conditions.

Safety Profile

American Ginseng is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may interact with certain medications, including blood thinners and diabetes drugs. Long-term use or high doses can cause side effects such as insomnia or gastrointestinal upset. Always consult a healthcare provider before use, especially for individuals with chronic conditions.
