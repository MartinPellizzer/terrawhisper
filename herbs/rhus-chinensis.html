Rhus chinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Rhus chinensis?

Rhus chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Sapindales. This plant is a specific member of the family Anacardiaceae, falling under the genus Rhus, and is identified by its specific species name, chinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhus chinensis has pinnately compound leaves consisting of 7 to 15 leaflets that are elliptical or lanceolate in shape and feature serrated margins. The leaflets are typically arranged oppositely along the rachis and possess a smooth or slightly hairy texture. The plant develops a woody structure with a trunk and branches that can reach significant heights in its natural habitat. Small, yellowish-green flowers emerge in dense, terminal panicles during the blooming season. These flowers eventually transition into small, fleshy drupes that turn a deep red or black color when fully ripe.

What are the therapeutic actions of Rhus chinensis?

Rhus chinensis has therapeutic properties that function primarily as a bitter, carminative, stomachic, digestive stimulant, and aperient. Its bitter components stimulate the gustatory receptors to trigger the secretion of gastric juices. This action serves as a potent digestive stimulant that improves overall metabolic efficiency. As a stomachic, the plant helps tone the gastric mucosa and promotes healthy digestion. It also acts as a carminative to relieve flatulence and intestinal gas. Finally, its mild aperient qualities assist in promoting regular bowel movements.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains gallic acid, methyl gallate, penta-O-galloyl-β-D-glucose, tannin, and flavonoids. These specific chemical constituents contribute to the complex medicinal profile of the species. The presence of tannin and flavonoids provides significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Additionally, the gallic acid derivatives such as methyl gallate are known for their bioactive potential. Together, these various compounds work synergistically to offer therapeutic benefits.

As discussed in a study, Rhus chinensis holds significant medicinal value due to its ability to produce Galla chinensis, which is rich in tannins and flavonoids. The assembled chromosome-level genome of this species has a size of 357.62 Mb. Within the plant, the gene RcTA1 plays a central role in the accumulation of gallic acid, which serves as a precursor of hydrolyzable tannins. Furthermore, the production of salt-like secretions, including phenolic and organic acids, is mediated by multicellular glandular trichomes regulated by RcGL2. These medicinal components and genetic factors collectively drive the abundant tannin accumulation and flavonoid biosynthesis found in the species.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating various digestive system ailments including diarrhea, dysentery, and colic pain. The fruit and bark possess astringent properties that help stabilize the intestinal tract during illness. These medicinal qualities are particularly effective at reducing inflammation within the gut. By soothing the lining of the stomach, the plant helps manage acute abdominal discomfort. This traditional remedy serves as a natural approach to restoring gastrointestinal balance.

As discussed in a study, Rhus chinensis contains high concentrations of penta-O-galloyl-β-D-glucose (PGG) which demonstrates anti-inflammatory activity. In concentrations of 5 μM-20 μM, PGG demonstrated statistically significant inhibition of ROS generation, IL-8 secretion and β₂ integrin expression in stimulated neutrophils. The inhibition of L-selectin expression by PGG resulted in prevention in neutrophils' endothelial attachment. These results may explain the anti-inflammatory activity of this compound and underline the contribution of PGG in the activity of PGG rich plant extracts.
