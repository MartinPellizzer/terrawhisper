Side Effects of lippia citriodora

Lippia citriodora triggers allergic reactions in some individuals, particularly those with a history of sensitivities to related plants or essential oils.

These reactions may manifest as skin rashes, which can range from mild redness to more severe itching or blistering upon contact with the herb or its extracts. The herb can also cause skin irritation, leading to dryness, redness, or a burning sensation, especially when applied topically.

Inhalation of Lippia citriodora may result in respiratory issues, such as coughing, wheezing, or shortness of breath, particularly in people with asthma or other respiratory conditions. Additionally, it can cause eye irritation, throat irritation, and eye redness, making it potentially hazardous if it comes into contact with the eyes or is inhaled in large quantities. Some individuals may also experience nausea, headaches, fatigue, dry mouth, digestive upset, or even anxiety after exposure to the herb.

Due to these potential side effects, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using Lippia citriodora, especially for those with pre-existing health conditions or allergies.
