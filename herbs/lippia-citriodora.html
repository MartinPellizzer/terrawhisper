Citronella (Lippia citriodora)

Citronella (Lippia citriodora) is a member of the Verbenaceae family, native to Australia, Pacific Islands, and Madagascar. Traditionally, its leaves, flowers, and stems have been used for infusions, decoctions, and oil infusions.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, carminative, and bitter actions, and has a long history of use in mediterranean herbal traditions, european herbal medicine, and african traditional medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters.
Leaves
Lanceolate leaves with acute apices, upper surface pale green, lower surface whitish, bearing prominent stomatal bands along midribs.
Flowers
Flowers are zygomorphic with yellow-orange corolla, two lips, and five stamens arranged in two whorls, having a tubular throat and a terminal stigma.
Stems
Cylindrical, herbaceous, quadrangular, hairy, ascending, opposite branching.
