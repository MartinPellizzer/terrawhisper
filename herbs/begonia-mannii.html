Begonia mannii

What's the taxonomical classification of Begonia mannii?

Begonia mannii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Cucurbitales and is a member of the family Begoniaceae. Ultimately, this specific plant is identified by its genus, Begonia, and its unique species designation, mannii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Begonia mannii has asymmetrical, heart-shaped leaves that are characterized by a deep reddish-brown or bronze pigmentation and a succulent, fleshy texture. The leaf margins are often irregularly lobed or wavy, contributing to a complex silhouette. These large blades are supported by thick, fleshy petioles that emerge from a compact rhizomatous stem. Small, delicate flowers typically emerge from the foliage, presenting a contrast to the heavy, dark leaves. The overall structure of the plant is low-growing and spreading, forming a dense clump of textured greenery.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause skin irritation, mouth sores, and digestive distress if its tissues are ingested or handled improperly. The sap contains oxalic acid, which is a common irritant found in many begonia species. Contact with the skin may lead to redness, itching, or a burning sensation known as contact dermatitis. If a person accidentally consumes any part of the plant, they might experience swelling of the lips and throat or stomach pain. It is important to wear gloves when pruning and to keep the plant away from pets and children to prevent accidental poisoning.
