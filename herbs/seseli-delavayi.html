      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Seseli delavayi can cause severe skin irritation, redness, and blistering upon contact due to the presence of furanocoumarins in its tissues. These chemical compounds react with ultraviolet light to trigger phytophotodermatitis, a painful inflammatory reaction in the skin. Exposure to the sap followed by sunlight can result in long-lasting hyperpigmentation or even scarring. Additionally, accidental ingestion may lead to gastrointestinal distress or more serious internal complications. Individuals with sensitive skin should exercise extreme caution when handling this plant in outdoor environments.
