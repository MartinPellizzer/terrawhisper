Saussurea longifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Saussurea longifolia?

Saussurea longifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical lineage further, it falls under the order Asterales and is a member of the family Asteraceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Saussurea, and its specific species designation, longifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea longifolia has long, linear, lanceolate leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette and possess entire or slightly serrated margins. These leaves are characterized by their elongated shape and a distinctively narrow width compared to other species in the genus. The plant features a robust stem that rises from the basal cluster to support terminal flowering heads. The inflorescence consists of several yellow flower heads surrounded by colorful, often reddish or purplish, involucral bracts.
