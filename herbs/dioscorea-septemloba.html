What's the taxonomical classification of Dioscorea septemloba?

Dioscorea septemloba belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Dioscoreales. As a member of the family Dioscoreaceae, it is situated within the genus Dioscorea, with its specific biological identity defined by the species name septemloba.

    What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

    This plant contains the medicinal steroidal saponins dioscin and diosgenin which are utilized for their various pharmacological properties. Dioscin serves as a precursor that can be hydrolyzed to release the more bioactive sapogenin. The compound diosgenin is particularly significant in the pharmaceutical industry because it acts as a primary starting material for the chemical synthesis of steroid hormones. These compounds are frequently studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and cholesterol-lowering effects. Researchers continue to investigate how these specific molecules interact with human biological systems to develop new treatments.
