Eurya distichophylla

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eurya distichophyllia has small, lanceolate to ovate leaves that are arranged in two distinct, opposite rows along the stem. This distichous leaf arrangement creates a flat, two-dimensional appearance in the foliage. The leaf margins are typically finely serrated, while the texture is somewhat leathery and glossy. The stems are woody and can become quite branched as the shrub matures. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, eventually producing tiny dark fruits.
