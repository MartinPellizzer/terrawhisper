Eurya distichophylla

What's the taxonomical classification of Eurya distichophylla?

Eurya distichophyllla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Ericales. This plant is a representative of the family Pentaphylacaceae, falling under the genus Eurya, and is specifically identified by its species name, distichophyllla.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Eurya distichophyllia can cause skin irritation, redness, and allergic contact dermatitis when the plant's sap or foliage comes into direct contact with human skin. While specific human clinical studies are limited, the presence of various secondary metabolites suggests a risk of localized inflammation. Ingesting parts of the plant may lead to gastrointestinal distress or other unpredictable toxic reactions. Individuals with sensitive skin or known botanical allergies should exercise extreme caution when handling this species. Always use protective gloves to minimize the risk of a reaction.
