Combretum micranthum has a woody shrub or small tree structure characterized by simple or trifoliate leaves and small, densely clustered flowers. Its inflorescences are composed of numerous tiny, inconspicuous flowers that emerge in compact spikes or racemes. The stems often possess a rough texture and can exhibit a branching habit that supports a dense canopy. What side-effects this plant can have?

Combretum micranthum can cause adverse reactions such as nausea, dizziness, stomach cramps, and diarrhea in some individuals. While often used in traditional medicine, the lack of extensive clinical trials means the full range of potential toxicities remains poorly understood. Some users may also experience allergic skin reactions or headaches when consuming preparations of the plant. Because it can influence blood sugar levels, it may pose a risk for hypoglycemia in people already taking diabetes medication. It is essential to consult a medical professional before using this botanical to avoid dangerous interactions or unexpected physiological changes.
