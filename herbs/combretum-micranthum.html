What's the taxonomical classification of Combretum micranthum?

Combretum micranthum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its evolutionary lineage places it in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, following the order Myrtales. Within this order, it is a member of the family Combretaceae. The plant is specifically categorized under the genus Combretum, with its unique identity defined by the species name micranthum.

As discussed in a study, the leaves of Combretum micranthum were chemically investigated and thirteen different flavonoid compounds were identified. These identified compounds may have synergistic effects to decrease the expression of phosphoenolpyruvate carboxykiasne (PEPCK) mRNA and glucose production in an H4IIE hepatoma cell line. In vivo studies on C57BL/6J mice indicate that the plant can lower plasma glucose levels in a dose-dependent manner without significant weight loss or toxicity. Additionally, the ethyl acetate extract rich in flavonoids could increase glucose tolerance (GT) after seven weeks of administrations. Both in vitro and in vivo experiments support the potential use of Combretum micranthum leaves as an anti-diabetes agent.
