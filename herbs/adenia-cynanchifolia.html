Adenia cynanchifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Adenia cynanchifolia has a large, tuberous caudex and pinnate leaves that are composed of several pairs of leaflets. This perennial climber utilizes its thick, succulent base to store water and nutrients for survival in arid environments. The foliage consists of small, green leaflets that create a delicate, lacy appearance on the vine. Its woody stems extend outward to support the climbing growth habit typical of the Passifloraceae family. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the stems to facilitate reproduction.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the dry, rocky regions of southwestern Africa, specifically within Namibia and parts of South Africa. It can be found growing in sandy soils and among stone outcrops in desert-like environments. These specific ecological niches limit its spread to certain coastal and inland arid zones.         What side effects this plant can have?

        This plant can cause severe toxicity and potential death if ingested due to the presence of highly concentrated cardiac glycosides in its tissues. These chemical compounds interfere with the electrical activity of the heart, leading to dangerous irregularities in heartbeat. Symptoms of poisoning may include nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and intense dizziness. In more advanced cases, an individual might experience cardiac arrhythmia or complete heart failure. Because of these lethal risks, all parts of the specimen should be handled with extreme caution to avoid accidental consumption or skin contact.
