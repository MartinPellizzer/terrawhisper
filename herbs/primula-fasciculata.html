Primula fasciculata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Primula fasciculaata has a basal rosette of oblong to lanceolate leaves that feature prominent dark green longitudinal stripes and slightly serrated margins. These leaves are typically arranged in a tight cluster at the ground level to maximize light absorption. The plant produces upright flowering stems that emerge from the center of the foliage to bear clusters of small blossoms. Each flower is characterized by a tubular structure that opens into distinct petals, often displaying delicate color transitions. The overall morphology is adapted for its specific high-altitude habitat, focusing energy on compact leaf growth and vertical reproductive structures.
