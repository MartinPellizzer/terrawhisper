Sedum bulbiferum

What's the taxonomical classification of Sedum bulbiferum?

Sedum bulbiferum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the biological hierarchy, it is classified in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic ranks, this plant is part of the order Saxifrages, falling under the family Crassulaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Sedum and is specifically designated by its species name, bulbiferum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sedum bulbiferum has succulent, cylindrical leaves that are fleshy, bright green, and arranged in dense, overlapping rosettes. These leaves are typically several centimeters long and possess a smooth texture with slightly rounded tips. The plant develops small, bulbils in the axils of its leaves, which serve as a primary method of asexual reproduction. During the flowering period, it produces terminal clusters of star-shaped, yellowish-green flowers. The stems are often creeping or decumbent, allowing the plant to form thick, spreading mats across rocky terrain.
