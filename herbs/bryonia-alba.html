White Bryony

Bryonia alba

White Bryony (Bryonia alba) is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and has been traditionally used in various regions for its purported effects on ailments such as pleurisy, cough, and rheumatism. The roots and stems of the plant are typically harvested and used in traditional preparations.

Botanical Identification

White Bryony, scientifically known as Bryonia alba, is a member of the Begoniaceae family. It is also called White Bindweed in some regions. This perennial herb is native to Europe and western Asia. It has yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves. Historically, it has been referred to by various names in herbal texts.

White Bryony Identification.

Active Compounds

White Bryony contains key active compounds such as alkaloids, tannins, and flavonoids. These compounds work synergistically to exert their medicinal effects, with alkaloids playing a primary role in its therapeutic actions. Tannins and flavonoids support and enhance these effects through their anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for respiratory and digestive ailments.

Poultice: Used for skin conditions and inflammation.

Powder: Used for digestive issues and constipation.

Safety Profile

White Bryony is contraindicated in cases of intestinal obstruction due to its purgative effects. It should not be used by individuals with severe constipation or bowel issues. Caution is advised in patients with respiratory conditions as it may exacerbate breathing difficulties.
