Adhatoda (Justicia adhatoda) Compounds

The primary compounds of Adhatoda include alkaloids, which have bronchodilatory effects. Flavonoids and phenolic acids contribute to its antioxidant properties. Tannins and saponins provide astringent and antimicrobial benefits. Glycosides, terpenoids, and coumarin also play roles in its medicinal activities.

Adhatoda (Justicia adhatoda) Monograph

1. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a key active compound found in Justicia adhatoda. It contributes to the plant's bronchodilatory and expectorant properties, making it valuable in traditional herbal medicine for treating respiratory conditions. Specific alkaloids like vasicine and hyoscyamine are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Adhatoda (Justicia adhatoda). They contribute to the plant's therapeutic properties, including anti-inflammatory and bronchodilatory effects. These compounds support respiratory health and are part of the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

3. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Adhatoda (Justicia adhatoda). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is used in traditional medicine for treating respiratory conditions. Tannins help reduce inflammation and may support mucous membrane health. This compound plays a significant role in the therapeutic applications of Adhatoda.

4. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Adhatoda (Justicia adhatoda). It contributes to the plant's expectorant and bronchodilator properties, aiding in respiratory health. Glycosides in Adhatoda are responsible for its traditional use in treating coughs and respiratory ailments.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in Adhatoda (Justicia adhatoda). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and bronchodilatory effects, making it valuable in traditional herbal medicine for respiratory conditions. Its presence supports the plant's use in treating coughs and asthma.

6. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Adhatoda (Justicia adhatoda). These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in respiratory and inflammatory conditions. They exhibit antimicrobial, bronchodilatory, and anti-inflammatory properties, making them key components in herbal formulations for treating coughs and respiratory ailments.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Adhatoda (Justicia adhatoda). It contributes to the plant's expectorant properties, helping to loosen mucus in the respiratory tract. Saponin also exhibits antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting its traditional use in treating respiratory and skin conditions.

8. Coumarin

Coumarin is a bioactive compound found in Adhatoda (Justicia adhatoda). It contributes to the plant's therapeutic properties, including bronchodilatory and anti-inflammatory effects. Coumarin is also known for its antispasmodic and mild sedative actions, making it a key component in traditional herbal formulations.

9. Essential oil

The essential oil of Adhatoda (Justicia adhatoda) contains bioactive components like vasicine and other alkaloids. It is used in traditional medicine for its bronchodilatory and anti-inflammatory effects, aiding in respiratory conditions. The oil is extracted through steam distillation and is a key component in formulations for treating coughs and asthma.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Adhatoda (Justicia adhatoda). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, which support respiratory and immune health. This compound is particularly valued for its potential to reduce inflammation and enhance lung function in traditional herbal medicine.
