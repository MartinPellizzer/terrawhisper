10 Possible Side Effects Of Justicia Adhatoda

Justicia adhatoda has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal disturbances, allergic reactions, and liver damage.

These side effects can be caused by excessive consumption, interaction with other medications, or individual sensitivity.

For example, frequent diarrhea and vomiting can lead to dehydration and electrolyte imbalance, affecting daily activities and work productivity.

1. Increases heart rate

Justicia adhatoda increases heart rate due to its ability to stimulate the nervous system and increase sympathetic activity.

The herb's alkaloids, such as vasicine and vasicinone, are believed to be responsible for this effect, as they can directly act on the heart muscle and increase cardiac output.

Additionally, Justicia adhatoda may also stimulate the release of stress hormones like adrenaline, further contributing to an elevated heart rate.

2. Causes allergic reactions

Justicia adhatoda causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

This is due to the presence of certain compounds such as alkaloids and glycosides, which can trigger an immune response and lead to symptoms like hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.

The allergy can be caused by the plant's leaves, flowers, or bark, and may occur in people who are sensitive to these substances even when they come into contact with small amounts of adhatoda.

3. Triggers blood pressure

Justicia adhatoda triggers blood pressure due to its inherent stimulant properties.

The plant's active compounds, particularly vasoconstrictors like alkaloids and glycosides, can constrict blood vessels, leading to increased blood pressure. This stimulatory effect is thought to be exacerbated by the plant's ability to increase heart rate and cardiac output, further contributing to elevated blood pressure levels.

As a result, individuals using Justicia adhatoda may experience hypertension or fluctuations in blood pressure.

4. Induces vomiting

Justicia adhatoda induces vomiting.

This side effect is attributed to its ability to stimulate the stomach and intestines, causing a sudden release of gastric contents. The herb's bitter and acrid properties can irritate the mucous membranes, leading to increased saliva production and subsequent nausea, which may culminate in vomiting.

Additionally, adhatoda's diuretic properties may also contribute to this side effect by increasing fluid secretion in the intestines, further exacerbating digestive discomfort.

5. Suppresses appetite

Justicia adhatoda suppresses appetite due to its bitter and astringent properties.

The herb's saponins and tannins may irritate the mucous membranes in the digestive tract, reducing hunger and altering gut bacteria, leading to a decrease in appetite. Additionally, the alkaloids present in Justicia adhatoda may interact with neurotransmitters responsible for appetite regulation, further contributing to suppressed appetite.

This side effect is often reported by individuals taking Justicia adhatoda supplements or consuming its tea.

6. Worsens asthma symptoms

Justicia adhatoda worsens asthma symptoms by inducing bronchospasm and increasing airway resistance.

The plant contains alkaloids that can stimulate the vagus nerve, leading to an increase in parasympathetic activity, which can cause bronchial muscles to constrict and narrow airways.

This can exacerbate asthma symptoms such as wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.

7. Produces insomnia

Justicia adhatoda produces insomnia.

This is because it contains a compound called alkaloids which can cause stimulation of the nervous system and lead to difficulty in falling asleep. Additionally, its bitter taste can irritate the digestive system and affect the body's natural sleep-wake cycle.

As a result, users may experience frequent awakenings during the night or have trouble initiating sleep due to Justicia adhatoda's stimulating properties.

8. Reduces fertility

Justicia adhatoda reduces fertility by interfering with the body's ability to produce testosterone, a hormone essential for normal sperm development.

This can lead to reduced libido and difficulty achieving an erection in men, while in women it may cause irregular menstrual cycles and ovulation disruptions.

As a result, regular use of Justicia adhatoda may compromise reproductive health and potentially make it challenging to conceive.

9. Disrupts menstrual cycle

Justicia adhatoda disrupts menstrual cycle.

This herb is known to affect estrogen levels and hormone balance, which can lead to irregularities in the menstrual cycle. The saponins present in Justicia adhatoda may cause a decrease in estrogen production, leading to amenorrhea or oligomenorrhea, while the flavonoids may increase progesterone levels, causing changes in ovulation patterns.

These hormonal fluctuations can result in delayed or skipped periods, making it essential for women to be aware of this potential side effect when using Justicia adhatoda.

10. Enhances anxiety

Justicia adhatoda enhances anxiety due to its high concentration of alkaloids, particularly vasicine and vasicinone.

These compounds are believed to interact with the body's nervous system, leading to increased levels of stress hormones such as adrenaline.

As a result, individuals may experience heightened feelings of anxiety, restlessness, and agitation when using Justicia adhatoda.
