Scientific Classification

Adhatoda, scientifically known as Justicia adhatoda, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Liliopsida, order Asphodelales, family Acanthaceae, genus Justicia, and species Justicia adhatoda. This classification places it within the monocot group and the Acanthaceae family, which includes many ornamental and medicinal plants.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Adhatoda is Justicia adhatoda, a member of the Acanthaceae family. It was first described by the botanist Robert Sweet in 1820. The name "Adhatoda" is derived from the Sanskrit word "Adhatoda," meaning "to hold fast," referring to its strong roots. This name is widely recognized in botanical literature and is used globally.

Morphological Characteristics

Adhatoda is a shrub or small tree with a straight, often branched stem. Its root system is fibrous and well-developed, supporting its upright growth. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, opposite, and have a slightly rough texture. The inflorescence is a terminal spike, bearing small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet. The fruit is a capsule containing numerous tiny seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Adhatoda, or Justicia adhatoda, can be distinguished by its opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins, and its distinctive blue or violet tubular flowers arranged in spikes. It has a square stem with hairy nodes and produces small, capsule-like fruits. These features help differentiate it from similar species in the Acanthaceae family.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Adhatoda is native to tropical regions of India, Sri Lanka, and parts of Southeast Asia. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained, loamy soils. It is commonly found at altitudes between 300 and 1,200 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Adhatoda blooms from April to June, producing blue to violet flowers. Seed formation occurs from July to September. For identification purposes, the plant is typically harvested during the flowering season, from April to June, when the leaves and stems are most distinct.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Adhatoda, or Justicia adhatoda, belongs to the Acanthaceae family. Its closest relatives include species like Justicia adhatoda var. integrifolia and Justicia adhatoda var. linearis. It shares key morphological traits with other members of the genus Justicia, such as opposite leaves and showy bracteate inflorescences.
