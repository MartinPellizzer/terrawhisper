What's the taxonomical classification of Parnassia davidii?

Parnassia davidii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent through the order Celastrales, it is placed within the family Celastraceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Parnassia, with its specific designation being the species davidii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Parnassia davidii has a basal rosette of long-petioled, ovate to elliptic leaves with entire margins and a single, erect flowering stem that can reach up to 15 centimeters in height.

The inflorescence consists of one to three small, white flowers characterized by a distinctively fringed or laciniate limb. Each petal features intricate green or yellowish-green nectar guides and delicate vein patterns that serve to attract pollinators. The plant's reproductive structures include a central ovary surrounded by stamens and a specialized nectariferous tissue.
