What is Viguiera quinqueradiata?

Viguiera quinqueradiata is an annual or perennial shrub that belongs to the family Asteraceae.

It has a stem that is 10-50 cm tall, covered with soft, grayish-brown hairs, and branched in the upper part. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a lanceolate or elliptic shape, measuring 5-15 cm long and 1-3 cm wide, with a cleft or entire margin and a sessile base. The flowers are grouped in capitula, 10-20 mm in diameter, consisting of 20-30 ligulate florets with a yellow color and a yellow or purple center.

The fruits are achenes, 3-4 mm long, with a pappus of 3-4 mm long, brownish-white bristles.
