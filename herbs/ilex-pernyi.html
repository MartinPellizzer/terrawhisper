What's the taxonomical classification of Ilex pernyi?

Ilex pernyi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Aquifoliales and the family Aquifoliaceae. Finally, the plant is situated within the genus Ilex, with its specific designation being the species pernyi.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ilex pernyi has evergreen, coriaceous leaves that are typically lanceolate to oblong in shape with serrated margins and a glossy, dark green upper surface. These leaves are arranged alternately along woody, spreading branches that form a dense, shrub-like canopy. The plant also produces small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into fleshy, dark-colored drupes. This combination of stiff foliage and berry-like fruit characterizes its distinct morphological profile.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds ursolic acid, lupeol, alpha-amyrin, kaempferol-3-O-sambubioside, and isoquercitrin. These specific pentacyclic triterpenoids and flavonoids contribute to the diverse pharmacological profile of the species. Research suggests that ursolic acid and lupeol may provide significant anti-inflammatory and antioxidant benefits to the human body. Additionally, the presence of alpha-amyrin alongside the flavonoids kaempferol-3-O-sambubioside and isoquercitrin supports various cellular protection mechanisms. These bioactive molecules work synergistically to enhance the therapeutic potential of the plant's extracts.

As discussed in a study, the chemical constituents of Ilex pernyi were investigated through various column chromatographic methods. Eight triterpenoid compounds were isolated and identified from this plant for the first time. These specific medicinal compounds include ursolic acid (1), lupeol (2), alpha-amyrin (3), and uvaol (4).
