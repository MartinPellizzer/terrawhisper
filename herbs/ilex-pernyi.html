What's the taxonomical classification of Ilex pernyi?

Ilex pernyi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Aquifoliales and the family Aquifoliaceae. Finally, the plant is situated within the genus Ilex, with its specific designation being the species pernyi.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ilex pernyi has evergreen, coriaceous leaves that are typically lanceolate to oblong in shape with serrated margins and a glossy, dark green upper surface. The leaf texture is notably leathery, which helps the plant retain moisture in its natural habitat. These leaves are arranged alternately along woody, spreading branches that form a dense, shrub-like canopy. The plant also produces small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into fleshy, dark-colored drupes. This combination of stiff foliage and berry-like fruit characterizes its distinct morphological profile.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds ilexperphenoside A, isoquercitrin, kaempferol-3-O-sambubioside, quercetiin-3-O-sambubioside, and ursolic acid. These specific phytochemicals contribute to the diverse biological activities associated with the species. The presence of various flavonoids like isoquercitrin and kaempferol-3-O-sambubioside suggests potential antioxidant properties. Additionally, the triterpenoid ursolic acid is known for its anti-inflammatory effects in many botanical studies. These chemical constituents collectively define the therapeutic profile of Ilex pernyi.

According to a study published by "Yao xue xue bao = Acta pharmaceutica Sinica", one new compound and five known compounds were isolated from the ethanolic extract of the leaves of Ilex pernyi Franch. These identified structures include trans-isoeugenyl-alpha-L-arabinopynosyl (1 --> 6) -beta-D-glucopyranoside (1), kaempferol-3-O-sambubioside (2), quercetin-3-O-sambubioside (3), isoquercitrin (4), (+) -syringaresinol-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside (5), and amarantholidoside IV (6). Compound 1 is a new phenolic glycoside named ilexperphenoside A. Additionally, compounds 2, 3, 4, 5, and 6 were isolated from this plant for the first time.
