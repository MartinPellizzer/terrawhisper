Within the class Equisetopsida, it is classified under the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Poales. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aeluropus littoralis has slender, erect, caespitose culms that typically reach a height of 20 to 60 centimeters and possess narrow, linear leaves with scaberulous margins. The leaf blades are involute and can measure up to 20 centimeters in length, while the sheaths are membranous and often slightly scabrous. The inflorescence is a compact, spike-like panicle that measures approximately 3 to 8 centimeters in length. Each spikelet is small and contains several florets enclosed by a lemma that is often somewhat compressed. The entire plant exhibits a salt-tolerant structure adapted to coastal or saline environments.
