What is Nematanthus serpens?

Nematanthus serpens is a species of flowering plant in the family Gesneriaceae, native to Brazil and Argentina.

It is a shrubby herb with a creeping habit, producing long, trailing stems up to 30 cm in length. The leaves are oppositely arranged, lanceolate in shape, and have a leathery texture, measuring 4-10 cm in length. The inflorescence is a terminal spike bearing small, funnel-shaped flowers with a greenish-yellow color and a purple-red lip, measuring 2-3 cm in diameter.

The flowers are zygomorphic and have a 5-lobed corolla.
