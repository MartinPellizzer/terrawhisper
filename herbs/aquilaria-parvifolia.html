What is Aquilaria parvifolia?

Aquilaria parvifolia is a species of plant in the family Thymelaeaceae, native to tropical Asia.

It is a small tree or shrub, growing up to 10 meters in height, with a broad, rounded crown and a short, straight trunk. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, measuring 2-5 centimeters in length, with a leathery texture and a crenate margin. The flowers are small, white, and arranged in axillary panicles, while the fruit is a red or purple drupe, 2-3 centimeters in length.

The heartwood of this plant is highly prized for its resinous properties, which are used in traditional medicine and as a fixative in perfumery.

What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

The main herbal preparations of Aquilaria parvifolia are resin tincture, decoction, infusion, tea, and essential oil.

Resin tincture is prepared by mixing the resin of Aquilaria parvifolia with a solvent, typically ethanol, to create a concentrated liquid extract used for treating various health conditions. Infusion is a process of steeping the dried leaves and flowers of Aquilaria parvifolia in hot water to produce a flavorful and aromatic tea, while the leaves can also be made into a tea by steeping them in hot water.

Essential oil is obtained through steam distillation of the resin of Aquilaria parvifolia, which is used in aromatherapy and as a natural remedy for various health issues.
