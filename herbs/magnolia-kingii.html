What is Magnolia kingii?

Magnolia kingii is a species of flowering plant in the family Magnoliaceae, native to Yunnan Province in southwestern China.

It is a large evergreen tree, typically growing up to 20 meters in height, with a broad, rounded crown and dark green, elliptical leaves that are 20-40 centimeters long and 8-15 centimeters wide. The flowers are large and showy, measuring 15-20 centimeters in diameter, with six white or pale yellow petals and a prominent yellow center. The fruit is a red, oblong capsule, 10-15 centimeters long, containing several large seeds.

The bark is smooth and gray, becoming scaly with age.
