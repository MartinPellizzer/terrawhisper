Zostera grass

Zostera marina

Zostera Grass (Zostera marina) is a marine plant belonging to the family Zosteraceae. It has been traditionally used as a diuretic, for treating skin conditions, and in traditional medicine for respiratory issues. The plant is typically harvested for its leaves and stems, which are utilized in various cultural and regional practices.

Botanical Identification

Zostera marina, commonly known as eelgrass, belongs to the family Zosteraceae. It is a perennial seagrass native to coastal marine environments in the Northern Hemisphere. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers. It is also referred to as sea grass or marine grass in various regional contexts.

Active Compounds

Zostera marina contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and tannins, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to provide antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory ailments such as coughs and colds.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and to treat infections.

Extract: Used for skin conditions and inflammation.

Safety Profile

Zostera marina is generally considered safe when used as a herbal remedy. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated for pregnant or breastfeeding women due to limited safety data. Always consult a healthcare professional before use.
