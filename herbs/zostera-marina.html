Zostera grass

Zostera marina

Zostera Grass (Zostera marina) is a type of seagrass commonly found in coastal marine environments, known for its potential medicinal properties and traditional use in treating respiratory and inflammatory conditions.

Taxonomy

Zostera marina is a species of seagrass belonging to the genus Zostera, family Zosteraceae, and is classified under the order Zosterales, class Zosteraceae, division Charophyta, and kingdom Plantae.

Morphology

Zostera marina is a perennial seagrass with long, narrow, ribbon-like leaves that grow in dense underwater meadows, typically reaching heights of 20 to 40 centimeters, with leaf blades up to 1 meter in length, and it produces small, yellow-brown flowers that develop into dark brown, oval-shaped seeds.

Distribution

Zostera marina is native to the coastal regions of the Northern Hemisphere, including the Atlantic and Pacific coasts, and is commonly found in shallow, brackish to saltwater environments such as estuaries, tidal flats, and seagrass beds.

Traditional Uses

Zostera marina has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported ability to support respiratory health, reduce inflammation, and promote wound healing, often prepared as a decoction or applied topically.

Medicinal Systems

Zostera marina has been utilized in traditional medicinal systems such as Traditional Chinese Medicine, Ayurveda, and Native American herbal practices for its purported healing properties.

Phytochemistry

Zostera marina contains bioactive compounds such as polyphenols, flavonoids, and alkaloids, which contribute to its potential medicinal properties.

Pharmacology

Zostera marina exhibits pharmacological properties such as anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects, which contribute to its traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive disorders.

Botanical Identification

Zostera marina, commonly known as eelgrass, belongs to the family Zosteraceae. It is a perennial seagrass native to coastal marine environments in the Northern Hemisphere. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers. It is also referred to as sea grass or marine grass in various regional contexts.
