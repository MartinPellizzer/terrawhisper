Tsuga chinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Tsuga chinensis?

Tsuga chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida, the subclass Pinidae, and the order Pinales. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the family Pinaceae and the genus Tsuga, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species chinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tsuga chinensis has linear, dark green needles that are approximately 15 to 30 millimeters long and arranged spirally around the branches but often appear two-ranked. The leaves feature small, stomata-bearing white bands on their undersides and lack distinct petioles. Its bark is typically thick and furrowed into long, shallow ridges as the tree matures. The plant produces small, woody, egg-shaped cones that hang downward from the branches. These cones eventually release winged seeds designed for wind dispersal.
