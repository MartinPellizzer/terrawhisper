Indian Gooseberry (Phyllanthus emblica)

Indian Gooseberry (Phyllanthus emblica) is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, native to India, Southeast Asia, and Southern China. Traditionally, its fruits, leaves, and seeds have been used for decoctions, powders, and infusions.

This herb is particularly valued for its tonic, anti-inflammatory, and astringent actions, and has a long history of use in ayurvedic medicine, traditional chinese medicine, and korean traditional medicine.

Flowers
Inflorescence composed of clustered flowers, each with actinomorphic symmetry, five white petals with yellow staminodes, and five stamens arranged in two whorls.

Erect growth habit with opposite branching, smooth surface, and presence of stipular scars.
