Indian gooseberry

Phyllanthus emblica

Indian Gooseberry (Phyllanthus emblica) is a plant native to the Indian subcontinent and belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family. Traditionally, its fruit has been used for its tonic properties, digestive support, and in various skin care applications. The fruit is the primary part utilized in traditional formulations.

Botanical Identification

Indian Gooseberry, scientifically known as Phyllanthus emblica, belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae. Commonly referred to as Amla in India, it is a small, evergreen shrub native to the Indian subcontinent. The plant features smooth, reddish-brown roots, elliptical leaves, and yellowish-green flowers, with various regional names reflecting its traditional use in herbal medicine.

Active Compounds

The Indian Gooseberry contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and tannins, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to enhance antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. Their combined action supports the herb's traditional use in herbal medicine.

Preparation Methods

Powder: Used for digestive issues and to support the immune system.

Tincture: Used for respiratory infections and as an antioxidant.

Poultice: Used for skin conditions and to reduce inflammation.

Safety Profile

Indian Gooseberry is generally safe when consumed in typical food amounts. However, it may cause gastrointestinal upset in some individuals. High doses may interact with certain medications, so consultation with a healthcare provider is recommended before use.
