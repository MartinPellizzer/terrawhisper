Begonia hemsleyana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Begonia hemsleyana has asymmetrical, succulent leaves that are characterized by a distinctively metallic, silver-colored surface and deep, irregular lobes. The foliage grows in a dense rosette pattern, with each leaf featuring prominent, dark veins that contrast sharply against the shimmering texture. These leaves possess a highly textured, almost crinkled appearance due to their unique cellular structure. Small, delicate flowers emerge from the plant, typically displaying pale pink or white hues. The stems are fleshy and semi-translucent, supporting the heavy, water-retaining leaves.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Begonia hemsleyana can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. The plant contains oxalic acid, which is a common irritant found in many begonia species. Contact with the sap may lead to redness, itching, or a burning sensation on sensitive skin areas. If a person consumes any part of the plant, it can result in mouth irritation or stomach pain. Pets should also be kept away from this plant to prevent potential poisoning.
