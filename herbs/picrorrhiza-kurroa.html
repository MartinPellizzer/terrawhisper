What to know about Picrorrhiza kurroa (kutki) before using it medicinally

Kutki, scientifically known as Picrorrhiza kurroa, is a herb that has been revered for its remarkable health properties, which have been shown to improve digestion, reduce inflammation, and boost immunity.

While it's often used in traditional Indian medicine, kutki is also prized for its culinary uses, where it adds a unique flavor and aroma to dishes like curries, soups, and stews. From a horticultural standpoint, kutki is an easy-to-grow perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade. Botanically speaking, kutki belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and pointed leaves.

With ancient references dating back to the Ayurvedic texts of Charaka Samhita and Sushruta Samhita, kutki has been a valued herb in traditional medicine for centuries, with its uses and benefits continuing to be explored and celebrated today.
