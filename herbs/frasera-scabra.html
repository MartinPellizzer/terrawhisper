Scabrous Frasera (Frasera Scabra)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Frasera scabra.

Frasera scabra, commonly known as the rough frasera, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States, particularly found in moist woodlands and along stream banks. It has been traditionally used in Native American medicine for its purported healing properties, including treating respiratory ailments and digestive issues. The plant contains various bioactive compounds such as flavonoids and iridoids, which may contribute to its medicinal effects. In modern herbal medicine, Frasera scabra is sometimes used to support respiratory health and as a mild tonic for the digestive system. However, due to its limited research and potential toxicity, it is important to use this plant with caution and under the guidance of a qualified herbalist or healthcare provider.
