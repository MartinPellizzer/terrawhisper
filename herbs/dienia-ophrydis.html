Dienia ophrydis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dienia ophrydis has a creeping rhizomatous structure with slender, dichotomously branched stems that produce small, ovate to lanceolate leaves. These leaves are typically thin and delicate, often displaying a subtle texture suited for its moist habitat. The plant features inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the axils of the leaves. Its root system is adapted to anchor firmly in soft, organic substrates. This overall morphology allows the species to spread effectively across forest floors.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Dienia ophrydis can cause severe skin irritation, localized swelling, and painful contact dermatitis upon direct physical exposure to its sap or foliage. Individuals who touch the plant often report a stinging sensation that may persist for several hours. If the plant's juices come into contact with the eyes, they can trigger significant inflammation and temporary vision impairment. Some sensitive individuals might also experience an allergic reaction characterized by widespread redness or itchy hives. Careful handling is necessary to prevent these dermatological complications.
