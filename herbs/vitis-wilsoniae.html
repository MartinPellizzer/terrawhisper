Vitis wilsoniae

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vitis wilsoniae has woody, climbing vines characterized by trifoliate leaves that feature three distinct leaflets with serrated margins and ovate shapes. The foliage often exhibits a glossy texture on the upper surface and can change color significantly during seasonal shifts. Its stems are equipped with specialized tendrils that allow the plant to anchor itself firmly to surrounding structures for upward growth. Small, inconspicuous flowers typically emerge in clusters, eventually developing into fleshy berries. These berries serve as a primary morphological feature once they reach maturity.
