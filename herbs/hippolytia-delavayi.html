Hippolytia delavayi

What's the taxonomical classification of Hippolytia delavayi?

Hippolytia delavayi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant falls under the order Asterales. It is further categorized within the family Asteraceae, a large group of flowering plants, and is specifically identified by the genus Hippolytia and the species delavayi.

It is also applied topically to soothe skin irritations or wounds caused by external pathogens. What are the herbal preparations of Hippolytia delavayi?

Hippolytia delaavay is prepared primarily as a decoction or an infusion made from its dried leaves and stems to treat various ailments. These aqueous extractions are the most common methods used to harness the plant's bioactive compounds for medicinal purposes. Some local traditions also involve crushing the plant material to create topical poultices for direct application to the skin. In certain instances, the plant may be processed into tinctures using alcohol as a solvent to increase potency and shelf life. These diverse preparation methods allow for both internal consumption and external therapeutic use.

What side-effects this plant can have?
