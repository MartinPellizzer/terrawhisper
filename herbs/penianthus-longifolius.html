What's the taxonomical classification of Penianthus longifolius?

Penianthus longifolius belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the order Ranunculales and the family Menispermaceae. Ultimately, its specific biological identity is defined by its placement within the genus Penianthus, with its unique designation being the species longifolius.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Penianthus longifolius has elongated, lanceolate leaves that typically measure between ten and twenty centimeters in length with smooth, entire margins. The foliage is arranged in an alternate pattern along a central, woody stem that provides structural support for the plant. Each leaf possesses a prominent midrib and a finely textured surface that may appear slightly waxy to the touch. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the upper leaves during the blooming season. The root system consists of a sturdy taproot designed to anchor the plant firmly in well-drained soil.

The species is often found growing in moist, well-drained soils near riverbanks or within shaded forest floors.
