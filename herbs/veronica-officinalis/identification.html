Botanical Identification of Blueweed (Veronica officinalis)

To identify Blueweed (Veronica officinalis) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Blueweed (Veronica officinalis) Monograph

Scientific Classification

Blueweed, or Veronica officinalis, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Liliopsida, order Plantaginales, family Plantaginaceae, genus Veronica, and species Veronica officinalis. This classification places it within the flowering plants, specifically in the order and family associated with medicinal and aromatic plants.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Veronica officinalis is the scientific name for Blueweed. It was first described by Carl von Linné in 1753. Common synonyms include Veronica azurea and Veronica sylvatica. It is also known by regional names such as Blueweed, Common Speedwell, and Sweetweed. Historically, it has been referred to by various names in different cultures and regions.

Morphological Characteristics

Blueweed (Veronica officinalis) is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively. It has erect, branched stems that are typically hairy. The leaves are lance-shaped, opposite, and have a serrated margin. The inflorescence is a spike of small blue or purple flowers, each with four petals and five stamens. The fruit is a capsule containing numerous tiny seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Blueweed (Veronica officinalis) can be distinguished by its square, hairy stems, opposite leaves with toothed margins, and small blue or purple flowers in whorls. It is often confused with other veronicas, but its distinct leaf shape and stem structure help in accurate identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Blueweed (Veronica officinalis) is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of western Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. It typically grows between 200 and 1,500 meters above sea level.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Blueweed blooms from June to August, producing sky-blue flowers in whorls. Seed formation occurs from late summer to early autumn. For identification purposes, the best time to harvest is during the flowering period when the plant is most distinctive and the leaves are fully developed.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Veronica officinalis belongs to the genus Veronica in the family Plantaginaceae. Its closest relatives include other species within the same genus, such as Veronica beccabunga and Veronica persica. The family traits include opposite leaves, showy flowers, and a preference for moist habitats. These relationships help in distinguishing Blueweed from similar species.
