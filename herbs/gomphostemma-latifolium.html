Gomphostemma latifolium

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gomphostemma latifolium has large, ovate to elliptical leaves with prominent venation, climbing stems, and tubular, zygomorphic flowers that typically feature a white or pale pink corolla. The foliage is characterized by its broad surface area and long petioles that support the sprawling growth habit of this perennial climber. Its stems are somewhat woody at the base and use twining mechanisms to ascend surrounding vegetation. The inflorescence consists of flowers arranged in small clusters, displaying a distinct bilateral symmetry. These floral structures are specialized for specific pollinators and often possess a fleshy texture.

What are the herbal preparations of Gomphostemma latifolium?

Gomphostemma latifolium is prepared as a decoction, a powder, or a topical paste depending on the specific therapeutic application required. The dried plant material can also be finely ground into a powder that is often mixed with warm water or honey.
