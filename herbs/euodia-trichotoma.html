Euodia trichotoma

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Euodia trichotoma has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that typically measure 5 to 15 centimeters in length and possess serrated margins. The foliage is characterized by a bright green color and a slightly leathery texture when mature. Its woody stems develop into a small to medium-sized deciduous tree with a spreading canopy. Small, fragrant white flowers emerge in terminal panicles during the summer months. These blossoms eventually give rise to small, fleshy drupes that transition from green to reddish or black upon ripening.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and potential gastrointestinal distress if ingested. The essential oils extracted from its leaves are known to be strong irritants that may trigger redness or itching upon direct contact with sensitive skin. Some individuals might experience localized swelling or rashes if they handle the foliage without protective gloves. While traditional uses exist, consuming unprocessed parts of the plant can lead to nausea or stomach discomfort. Caution is advised when using its derivatives due to these unpredictable biological reactions.
