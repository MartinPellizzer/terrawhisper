Phoebe zhennan

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phoebe zhennan has an evergreen habit characterized by an elliptical or ovate-oblong crown, woody stems with grayish bark, and pinnately compound leaves that measure approximately 10 to 25 centimeters in length. The leaflets are typically leathery in texture, possessing an entire margin and a glossy dark green upper surface. Small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves, eventually giving way to drupaceous fruits. These fruits are oblong or ellipsoidal in shape and transition from green to a dark purple or black color upon maturation. The overall structural arrangement reflects its classification within the Lauraceae family, emphasizing dense foliage and sturdy branching patterns.

What are the therapeutic actions of Phoebe zhennan?

What side-effects this plant can have?

Phoebe zhennan can cause allergic skin reactions, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if consumed in improper quantities. While research is ongoing, there is a general caution regarding its systemic effects on the body when used improperly.
