Golden Shower Tree (Senna auriculata) Uses

The Golden Shower Tree is used to treat skin infections, wound healing, and skin irritation. It helps with inflammatory skin disorders, itching, and inflamed skin. It also addresses heat-related ailments, gastrointestinal disorders, constipation, and diarrhea.

Golden Shower Tree (Senna auriculata) Monograph

1. Skin infections

Senna auriculata is used topically to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial properties. The leaves and bark contain compounds that inhibit bacterial and fungal growth. It is often applied as a poultice or decoction to reduce inflammation and promote healing in infected areas.

2. Wound healing

Senna auriculata is used traditionally to promote wound healing due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The leaves contain compounds that help reduce infection risk and accelerate tissue repair. It is often applied topically as a poultice or infused into oils for direct application on wounds.

3. Skin irritation

Senna auriculata is used topically to treat skin irritation due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The leaves are crushed and applied directly to the affected area to reduce redness, itching, and infection. It is particularly effective for minor burns, rashes, and fungal infections.

4. Inflammatory skin disorders

Senna auriculata is used topically to treat inflammatory skin disorders due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The leaves and bark contain compounds that reduce redness, swelling, and infection in conditions like eczema and psoriasis. It is often applied as a paste or decoction to soothe irritated skin and promote healing.

5. Itching

Senna auriculata is used to alleviate itching by reducing inflammation and soothing the skin. Its leaves contain compounds with anti-inflammatory and antihistaminic properties. A decoction or poultice made from the leaves is applied topically to relieve symptoms of skin irritation and itching. It is commonly used in traditional herbal medicine for its calming effect on the skin.

6. Inflamed skin

Senna auriculata is used topically to treat inflamed skin due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The leaves are crushed and applied directly to the affected area to reduce redness, swelling, and infection. It is particularly effective for conditions like eczema and minor skin infections.

7. Heat-related ailments

Senna auriculata is used to treat heat-related ailments such as fever, inflammation, and skin infections. Its cooling properties help reduce internal body heat and soothe symptoms. The leaves and flowers are commonly brewed into teas or applied topically for relief. It is valued in traditional medicine for its ability to balance excess heat in the body.

8. Gastrointestinal disorders

Senna auriculata is used to treat gastrointestinal disorders due to its mild laxative properties. The leaves contain sennosides, which stimulate bowel movements. It helps relieve constipation and is often prepared as a tea or decoction. Its gentle action makes it suitable for short-term use in digestive ailments.

9. Constipation

Senna auriculata is used to treat constipation due to its laxative properties. The leaves contain compounds that stimulate bowel movements. It helps soften stool and promote regularity. It is often prepared as a tea or decoction for this purpose.

10. Diarrhea

Senna auriculata is used to treat diarrhea due to its astringent properties that reduce intestinal inflammation and regulate bowel movements. The leaves and flowers contain tannins and flavonoids that help firm stools and soothe the digestive tract. It is typically prepared as a decoction or infusion for short-term relief of acute diarrhea.
