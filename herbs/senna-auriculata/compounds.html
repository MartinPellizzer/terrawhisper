Golden Shower Tree (Senna auriculata) Compounds

The Golden Shower Tree contains several primary active compounds, including flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties. Tannins and phenolic acids contribute to its astringent effects. Coumarin and saponins are known for their anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities. These compounds work together to provide the plant's therapeutic benefits in herbal medicine.

Golden Shower Tree (Senna auriculata) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Senna auriculata. They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds may support digestive health and reduce oxidative stress. Flavonoids are among the key constituents responsible for the therapeutic effects of this traditional herbal medicine.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Senna auriculata, known for its astringent properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating digestive issues and skin conditions. Tannins in Senna auriculata also exhibit antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting its role in herbal medicine.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in Senna auriculata. It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound is commonly used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits. Its presence supports the therapeutic use of Senna auriculata in various medicinal applications.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a bioactive compound found in the Golden Shower Tree (Senna auriculata). It exhibits anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory properties, contributing to the plant's traditional use in treating cardiovascular and inflammatory conditions. Coumarin's presence supports the therapeutic potential of Senna auriculata in herbal medicine.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in the Golden Shower Tree (Senna auriculata). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly in treating digestive issues and inflammation. Glycosides in Senna auriculata are known for their laxative effects and anti-inflammatory actions.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Golden Shower Tree (Senna auriculata). It exhibits antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, contributing to the plant's traditional use in treating skin infections and inflammatory conditions. Saponin also enhances the bioavailability of other compounds in the plant.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Senna auriculata contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. They are found in various parts of the plant, including the leaves and pods. Terpenoids support the plant's use in traditional herbal medicine for treating infections and skin conditions.

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of Senna auriculata contains volatile compounds like sennoside A and B, which have laxative properties. It is commonly used in traditional medicine to treat constipation and digestive issues. The oil is extracted through steam distillation from the leaves and flowers of the plant. Its active components contribute to its role in herbal treatments for gastrointestinal health.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin, found in Senna auriculata, is a flavonoid pigment responsible for the plant's vibrant colors. It exhibits strong antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress in the body. This compound contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating inflammation and digestive issues.

10. Alkaloid

The alkaloid sennoside A is found in the Golden Shower Tree (Senna auriculata) and is known for its laxative effects. It works by stimulating bowel movements and is commonly used in herbal medicine to treat constipation.
