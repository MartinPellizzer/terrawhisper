10 Possible Side Effects Of Senna Auriculata (Cassia)

Senna auriculata has some side effects when used improperly, such as diarrhea, stomach cramps, and nausea.

These issues are caused by the high fiber and anthraquinone content, which can be overwhelming for the digestive system. In severe cases, diarrhea can lead to dehydration, electrolyte imbalances, and even kidney problems, significantly impacting one's quality of life.

This can result in missed work, school, or other activities, and may necessitate medical attention.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Senna auriculata if used imporperly.

1. Exhibits nervousness

Senna auriculata causes diarrhea due to its sennosides content.

These anthraquinone glycosides stimulate the large intestine, increasing bowel movements and water absorption, which can lead to loose stools or watery stools. Additionally, the anthraquinones in Senna auriculata may irritate the intestinal lining, further contributing to diarrhea.

This gastrointestinal effect is a common side effect of using Senna auriculata for its medicinal properties.
