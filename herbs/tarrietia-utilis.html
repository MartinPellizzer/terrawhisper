Tarrietia utilis

What's the taxonomical classification of Tarrietia utilis?

Tarrietia utilis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It further belongs to the order Malvales and is a member of the family Malvaceae. Within this family, the plant is categorized under the genus Tarrietia, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species utilis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Tarrietia utilis contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including flavonoids, terpenoids, and phenolic compounds that provide its medicinal properties. These specific chemical constituents contribute to the plant's documented antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities. Researchers have investigated these compounds for their potential to mitigate oxidative stress in cellular models. Some studies suggest that the presence of these organic acids and alkaloids may support traditional uses in treating localized infections. The precise synergistic effect of these molecules remains a subject of ongoing pharmacological interest.

What are the therapeutic actions of Tarrietia utilis?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin diseases, respiratory ailments, and digestive issues. Local practitioners often apply its leaves or preparations to manage wounds and various cutaneous infections. It is also frequently employed in traditional remedies to alleviate symptoms of coughs and bronchitis. Additionally, the plant can be ingested to soothe stomach discomfort and regulate bowel functions. These therapeutic applications highlight its importance in regional ethnobotanical practices.
