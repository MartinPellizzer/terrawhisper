Delphinium tongolense

What's the taxonomical classification of Delphinium tongolense?

Delphinium tongolense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Ranunculales and is a member of the family Ranunculaceae. At the most specific taxonomic levels, it is categorized under the genus Delphinium, with its unique identity defined by the species name tongolense.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Delphinium tongolense contains complex alkaloids, specifically diterpene alkaloids, which serve as the primary bioactive constituents responsible for its pharmacological activities. These nitrogenous compounds are known to interact with various neurological and physiological receptors within the body. Research suggests that these specific chemical structures possess potential analgesic and sedative properties. Some studies also indicate that the plant's chemical profile may contribute to anti-inflammatory effects. Understanding the precise molecular structure of these alkaloids is essential for developing targeted medicinal applications.
