Silene baccifera

What's the taxonomical classification of Silene baccifera?

Silene baccifera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is a member of the order Caryophyllales and falls within the family Caryophyllaceae. Specifically, it is identified by the genus Silene, with its unique designation being the species baccifera.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Silene baccifera can cause skin irritation, digestive distress, and potential toxicity if ingested in significant quantities. While specific human clinical studies are limited, members of the Silene genus often contain saponins that act as natural irritants. These chemical compounds may lead to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain upon consumption. Contact with the plant's sap or foliage might also trigger localized dermatitis or allergic reactions in sensitive individuals. It is generally advised to avoid direct contact or ingestion due to these unpredictable physiological responses.
