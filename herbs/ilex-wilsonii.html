Ilex wilsonii

What's the taxonomical classification of Ilex wilsonii?

Ilex wilsonii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It further falls under the order Aquifoliales and is a member of the family Aquifoliaceae. Ultimately, this plant is identified by its genus, Ilex, and its specific species name, wilsonii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ilex wilsonii has small, elliptical to ovate leaves that are dark green, leathery, and feature finely serrated margins.

The foliage typically measures between two and five centimeters in length and grows on woody stems. During the flowering period, the plant produces tiny, inconspicuous white flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These blossoms eventually give way to small, dark berries that provide visual interest to the shrub.

The overall growth habit forms a dense, spreading evergreen structure suitable for various garden environments.

        What are the herbal preparations of Ilex wilsonii?

        Ilex wilsonii is prepared primarily as a decoction or infusion using the dried leaves and stems of the plant. The most common method involves simmering the plant material in water to extract its bioactive compounds for medicinal use. Some practitioners also utilize alcohol-based tinctures to create a more concentrated liquid extract that can be stored for long periods. Depending on the desired potency, the concentration of the herbal liquid can be adjusted during the brewing process.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Ilex wilsonii can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if any part of the plant is ingested by humans or animals. The leaves and berries contain saponins and other compounds that may trigger allergic contact dermatitis upon touch. If swallowed, these substances often lead to nausea, vomiting, or stomach pain. While direct human poisoning is rare, pets are particularly vulnerable to the plant's toxic effects. Proper precautions should be taken to prevent accidental consumption or prolonged skin contact.
