Burdock (Arctium lappa) Compounds

The primary compounds in Burdock include coumarin, a substance with anticoagulant properties, and flavonoids, which act as antioxidants. Phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory effects, while tannins provide astringent qualities. Glycosides, saponins, and terpenoids also play roles in its medicinal properties. Essential oils and alkaloids add to its complex therapeutic profile.

Burdock (Arctium lappa) Monograph

1. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Burdock (Arctium lappa). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. Coumarin also acts as a natural anticoagulant, supporting circulation. Its presence enhances Burdock's traditional use in treating skin conditions and digestive disorders.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Burdock (Arctium lappa) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help reduce oxidative stress and support immune function. They are found in the roots and leaves of the plant. Flavonoids may also aid in skin health and detoxification processes.
