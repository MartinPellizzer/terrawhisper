Hiptage benghalensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hiptage benghalensis has a woody climbing habit characterized by slender, twining stems that utilize adventitious roots to ascend host vegetation. The leaves are typically alternate and simple, often displaying an ovate or elliptical shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. A distinguishing feature of this plant is its small, inconspicuous flowers that are often arranged in axillary clusters. The foliage tends to be glossy, which helps the plant thrive in the humid, shaded environments of tropical forests. This morphological structure allows the plant to efficiently compete for sunlight within dense canopy layers.
