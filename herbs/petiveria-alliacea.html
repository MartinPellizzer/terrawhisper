Pennyroyal

Petiveria alliacea

Pennyroyal (Petiveria alliacea) is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas, known for its pungent odor and various traditional medicinal uses, particularly in improving menstrual health and as a natural deworming agent.

Botanical Identification

Pennyroyal is scientifically known as Petiveria alliacea, belonging to the Apiaceae family. Common names include pennyroyal and wild chervil, though regional variants exist. It is an annual herb native to the Caribbean and South America. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. Synonyms in herbal literature include Mentha pulegium and Pennyroyal.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Liliopsida
  • Order: Asphodelales
  • Family: Acanthaceae
  • Genus: Petiveria
  • Species: Petiveria alliacea

Pennyroyal Identification.

Active Compounds

Pennyroyal contains flavonoids, essential oils, and saponins as its primary active compounds. These bioactives work synergistically to exert their medicinal effects, with the essential oils playing a key role in its traditional uses. The plant's chemical profile supports its historical application in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Essential oil
  • Saponin

Pennyroyal Actions.

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and inflammation due to its antimicrobial properties.

Infusion: Used for respiratory ailments and mild digestive discomfort.

Tincture: Used for pain relief and as an antimicrobial agent.

Safety Profile

Pennyroyal is contraindicated during pregnancy due to its potential to cause uterine contractions and miscarriage. It should also be avoided by individuals with liver or kidney disease. Use with caution and consult a healthcare provider before use.
