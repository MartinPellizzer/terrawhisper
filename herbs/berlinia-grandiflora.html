Berlinia grandiflora

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Berlinia grandiflora has a woody structure characterized by large trees that can reach heights of up to 30 meters with a trunk diameter often exceeding 50 centimeters. The leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of multiple pairs of leaflets arranged along a central rachis. Its most distinctive feature is the large, showy flowers that appear in dense clusters or panicles. These inflorescences produce nectar-rich blooms that are vital for local pollinators. The bark is typically thick and textured, providing protection for the massive arboreal frame.
