Berlinia grandiflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Berlinia grandiflora?

Berlinia grandiflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Fabales, placing it within the prominent family Fabaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by the genus Berlinia and is specifically distinguished as the species grandiflora.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Berlinia grandiflora has a woody structure characterized by large trees that can reach heights of up to 30 meters with a trunk diameter often exceeding 50 centimeters.

The leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of multiple pairs of leaflets arranged along a central rachis. Its most distinctive feature is the large, showy flowers that appear in dense clusters or panicles. These inflorescences produce nectar-rich blooms that are vital for local pollinators.

The bark is typically thick and textured, providing protection for the massive arboreal frame.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Berlinia grandiflora can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or digestive distress if its chemical constituents are ingested or applied improperly to the body. While much of its medicinal potential is still being studied, certain secondary metabolites in the plant may trigger sensitivities in some individuals. Contact with the sap or crushed leaves might result in localized redness or itching upon the skin. If consumed in uncontrolled amounts, it could potentially lead to nausea or other gastrointestinal discomfort. Due to these uncertainties, careful handling and professional guidance are necessary when interacting with this species.
