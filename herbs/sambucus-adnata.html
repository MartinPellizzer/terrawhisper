Sambucus adnata

What's the taxonomical classification of Sambucus adnata?

Sambucus adnata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is part of the order Dipsacales and is a member of the family Viburnaceae. Finally, it is identified under the genus Sambucus, with the specific species designation being adnata.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Sambucus adnata contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids that provide therapeutic potential. These chemical constituents are primarily concentrated in the plant's leaves, flowers, and berries. The presence of polyphenolic compounds suggests that the plant possesses significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Additionally, specific glycosides found within the species may contribute to its traditional use in managing respiratory ailments. Further research into these specific molecular structures is necessary to fully understand their pharmacological applications in human medicine.

What are the therapeutic actions of Sambucus adnata?

Sambucus adnata has potent antimicrobial, antioxidant, and anti-inflammatory properties that make it useful for treating respiratory infections and skin irritations. The plant contains high concentrations of bioactive flavonoids and phenolic compounds which help neutralize free radicals in the body. These phytochemicals work to reduce oxidative stress and support the overall function of the immune system. When applied topically, the extracts can soothe inflamed tissues and promote faster healing of minor wounds. Research suggests these therapeutic actions are driven by the specific chemical profile of its berries and flowers.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat respiratory ailments including coughs, bronchitis, colds, and influenza. Traditional healers often utilize its properties to alleviate symptoms associated with congestion and bronchial inflammation. The plant is also applied in various folk medicine practices to address digestive issues and skin irritations. Some regional uses involve the decoction of its parts to help manage fever and other febrile conditions. These medicinal applications rely on the specific bioactive compounds found within its stems and leaves.
