Sambucus adnata

What's the taxonomical classification of Sambucus adnata?

Sambucus adnata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is part of the order Dipsacales and is a member of the family Viburnaceae. Finally, it is identified under the genus Sambucus, with the specific species designation being adnata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sambucus adnata has opposite, pinnately compound leaves typically featuring five to seven elliptical leaflets with serrated margins and an acute apex. The plant exhibits a woody, shrubby growth habit characterized by slender, branching stems that may display lenticels. Small, cream-colored flowers are arranged in dense, flat-topped cymes at the terminal ends of the branches. These blossoms transition into dark, fleshy drupes that serve as a primary morphological feature during the fruiting season. The overall structure is defined by a spreading canopy and a fine texture provided by the delicate leaf arrangement.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayan mountain range, spanning across parts of India, Nepal, Bhutan, and southern China. It typically thrives in subalpine zones and montane forests located at elevations between 2,500 and 4,000 meters. The species prefers moist, temperate environments found along rocky slopes and forest edges. Its specific range is largely dictated by the unique climatic conditions of these rugged mountain ecosystems. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds lariciresinol and ursolic acid along with several other bioactive phytochemicals. These specific substances are recognized for their potential pharmacological activities in various biological systems. Lariciresinol is a sesquiterpene known for its presence in diverse botanical species and its unique chemical structure. Ursolic acid is a pentacyclic triterpenoid often studied for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Together, these constituents contribute to the complex therapeutic profile of Sambucus adnata.

According to a study published by "Zhong yao cai = Zhongyaocai = Journal of Chinese medicinal materials", five compounds were isolated from Sambucus adnata using silica gel chromatography. These identified medicinal compounds are 1-(3-hydroxy4-methoxyphenyl)-1',2'-ethanediol (1), ursolic acid (2), 1-(3,4,5-trimethoxyphenyl)-1',2'-ethanediol (3), lariciresinol (4), and 5,7,3',4'-tetramethoxyflavone-3-O-rhamnopyranosyl-(1-->6)-glucopyranoside (5). The study notes that compounds 1, 3, 4, and 5 were obtained from this plant for the first time. Researchers used spectral analysis to elucidate the structures of these 5 specific substances.
