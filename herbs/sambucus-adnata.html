Sambucus adnata

What's the taxonomical classification of Sambucus adnata?

Sambucus adnata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is part of the order Dipsacales and is a member of the family Viburnaceae. Finally, it is identified under the genus Sambucus, with the specific species designation being adnata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sambucus adnata has opposite, pinnately compound leaves typically featuring five to seven elliptical leaflets with serrated margins and an acute apex. The plant exhibits a woody, shrubby growth habit characterized by slender, branching stems that may display lenticels. Small, cream-colored flowers are arranged in dense, flat-topped cymes at the terminal ends of the branches. These blossoms transition into dark, fleshy drupes that serve as a primary morphological feature during the fruiting season. The overall structure is defined by a spreading canopy and a fine texture provided by the delicate leaf arrangement.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

It typically thrives in subalpine zones and montane forests located at elevations between 2,500 and 4,000 meters. The species prefers moist, temperate environments found along rocky slopes and forest edges. Its specific range is largely dictated by the unique climatic conditions of these rugged mountain ecosystems. These toxic reactions primarily stem from the presence of cyanogenic glycosides which release cyanide upon consumption.
