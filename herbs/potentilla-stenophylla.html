Potentilla stenophylla

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Potentiella stenophylla has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette or alternately along its slender stems. The leaf blades are characterized by their reduced width and serrated margins, often appearing sparsely hairy. Its stems are relatively thin and can exhibit a procumbent or ascending growth habit depending on the specific environment. Small, five-petaled flowers emerge from the foliage, typically displaying a bright yellow coloration. The plant's overall structure is compact, reflecting its adaptation to specific montane or rocky habitats.
