Lonicera tatarinowii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lonicera tatarinowii has woody, decumbent to ascending stems that terminate in opposite, ovate-lanceolate leaves with entire margins and acute tips. The foliage typically features a smooth texture and a light green coloration on both the upper and lower surfaces. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often appearing in pairs or solitary clusters. These blossoms possess a delicate structure and are characterized by their pale pigmentation. The plant eventually produces small, fleshy berries that serve as a primary dispersal mechanism for the species.
