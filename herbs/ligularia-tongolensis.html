Ligularia tongolensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Ligularia tongolensis?

Ligularia tongolensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is placed in the subclass Magnoliidae and follows the order Asterales. As a member of the family Asteraceae, it is situated within the genus Ligularia, ultimately reaching its specific identification at the species level as tongolensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ligularia tongolensis has large, ovate to elliptical leaves characterized by serrated margins and prominent venation that emerge from a perennial rhizomatous base. The foliage typically exhibits a textured surface with distinct longitudinal ribs extending from the midrib to the leaf edges. Its inflorescence consists of bright yellow capitula arranged in dense, terminal clusters atop sturdy, upright stalks. These flowering heads feature ray florets surrounding a central disc of tubular florets, which is a defining trait of the Asteraceae family. The overall structure of the plant is robust, allowing it to thrive in the moist, high-altitude environments it inhabits.
