Celastrus monospermus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Celastrus monospermus has small, woody, deciduous shrubs characterized by oppositely arranged, simple, elliptic to lanceolate leaves with entire margins and acute apices. The stems are typically slender and can exhibit a somewhat scrambling or spreading growth habit depending on the environment. Its inflorescences consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into unique single-seeded capsules. These fleshy, somewhat woody capsules typically split open to reveal a brightly colored aril that surrounds the lone seed. This specialized fruit structure is a defining morphological feature of the species.
