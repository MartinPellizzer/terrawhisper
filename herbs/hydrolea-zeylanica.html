Hydrolea zeylanica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hydrolea zeylanica has opposite, simple leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical in shape with entire or slightly serrated margins and acute tips. The stems are woody and often exhibit a sprawling or climbing growth habit depending on the specific environment. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in terminal cymes and possess a characteristic structure typical of the Hydroleaceae family. The fruit develops into a small, fleshy drupe that facilitates seed dispersal. These morphological features collectively allow the plant to thrive in its native wetland or riparian habitats.
