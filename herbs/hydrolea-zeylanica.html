Hydrolea zeylanica

What's the taxonomical classification of Hydrolea zeylanica?

Hydrolea zeylanica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is a member of the order Solanales and the family Hydroleaceae. Ultimately, it is identified by the genus Hydrolea and is specifically designated as the species zeylanica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hydrolea zeylanica has opposite, simple leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical in shape with entire or slightly serrated margins and acute tips. The stems are woody and often exhibit a sprawling or climbing growth habit depending on the specific environment. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in terminal cymes and possess a characteristic structure typical of the Hydroleaceae family. The fruit develops into a small, fleshy drupe that facilitates seed dispersal. These morphological features collectively allow the plant to thrive in its native wetland or riparian habitats.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of South and Southeast Asia, specifically occurring in countries such as India, Sri Lanka, and parts of the Malay Archipelago. Its distribution is closely tied to humid, lowland environments where seasonal rainfall is abundant. Many populations are found near freshwater sources like riverbanks, marshes, and coastal wetlands. The species thrives in these warm, moist habitats that characterize the Indo-Malayan realm. Consequently, its presence is most concentrated in areas with high humidity and consistent tropical temperatures.

What are the pharmacological activities of Hydrolea zeylanica?

This plant has anti-inflammatory, antidiabetic, antioxidant, and free radical scavenging pharmacological activities. Research indicates that its bioactive compounds can effectively mitigate oxidative stress within biological systems. These properties allow the plant to neutralize harmful molecules through potent free radical scavenging mechanisms. Furthermore, its ability to regulate glucose levels provides significant therapeutic potential for managing antidiabetic conditions. The systemic reduction of inflammation also highlights its medicinal importance in treating various inflammatory disorders.

According to a study published by "Cellular signalling", network pharmacology analysis was conducted on 32 bioactive components of Hydrolea zeylanica to explore its antidiaabetic mechanisms. This analysis showed a core relationship between 13 selected compounds and 194 key target genes related to insulin resistance, type II diabetes mellitus, and insulin signaling pathways. When HZH was administered at doses of 300 and 400 mg/kg, it was used to examine GLUTs expression and inflammatory cytokines in T2DM rats. Specifically, HZH-400 significantly restored glucose homeostasis and inflammatory markers in these rats. The treatment also altered GLUT2 and GLUT4 expression in the liver and skeletal muscle to normal levels. The bioactive compounds of HZH were found to control blood sugar levels by modulating insulin resistance and glucose homeostasis to improve glucose uptake and insulin production.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds perillartine, flavonoids, rutin, quercetin, and gallic acid. These bioactive molecules contribute to the diverse therapeutic potential observed in the species. The presence of various flavonoids suggests significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities. Specifically, rutin and quercetin are known to support vascular health and cellular protection. Additionally, the inclusion of gallic acid provides further antimicrobial and radical scavenging capabilities.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", GC-MS/MS and LC-MS/MS analysis of the hydroalcohol fraction of leaves (HAHZ) identified 32 compounds. Among these, R-limonene (0.52%), perillartine (0.41%), N-formyl-L-lysine (1.49%), limonen-6-ol, pivalate (1.43%), lidocaine (1.70%), and gamolenic acid (2.80%) were reported to have antioxidant and antidiabetic activities. These specific compounds in HAHZ were found to achieve improvement in the glycemic control mechanism by stimulating insulin secretion. Furthermore, the HAHZ-400 mg/kg dose demonstrated significant (p < 0.001) improvement in various serum and oxidative markers.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for managing type2 diabetes mellitus, diabetes, diabetes related vascular complications, diabetic encephalopathy, and glucose homeostasis. Researchers have focused on its potential to regulate blood sugar levels and maintain metabolic balance. By improving glucose homeostasis, it may help prevent the systemic damage often associated with chronic high blood sugar. Its therapeutic properties are also being studied for their ability to mitigate vascular complications and neurological issues like diabetic encephalopathy. These medicinal applications highlight its importance in treating various aspects of diabetic pathology.

According to a study published by "Heliyon", the green and leafy vegetables of Hydrolea zeylanica are used to reduce malnutrition and keep blood sugar levels in control to treat diabetes related vascular complications. The plant is used to address cognitive impairment in high-fat diet fed-streptozotocin-induced diabetic encephalopathy, which includes cognitive dysfunction and dementia. After 4 weeks of administration of oral doses of 300 and 400 mg/kg b.w., the 400 mg/kg dose significantly (p < 0.001) improved learning and memory impairment. This treatment also significantly (p < 0.001) improved oxidative markers including MDA, SOD, CAT, and GSH, as well as proinflammatory markers such as TNF-α, IL-6, and hs-CRP. Furthermore, the plant helps manage diabetic encephalopathy by reducing cholinesterase (AChE, BChE) and β-secretase (BACE1, BACE2) activities.
