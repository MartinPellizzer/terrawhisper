Pedicularis chinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Pedicularis chinensis?

Pedicularis chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the taxonomic path through the order Lamiales. It is situated within the family Orobanchaceae, falling under the genus Pedicularis, and is ultimately identified by its specific species designation, chinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pedicularis chinensis has a perennial herbaceous growth habit characterized by erect stems that typically reach heights between 10 and 40 centimeters. The leaves are mostly basal and arranged in a rosette, featuring deeply lobed or pinnatifid margins that give them a complex structure. Towards the upper portions of the stem, the leaves become smaller and more bract-like in appearance. The inflorescence consists of dense, terminal spikes of zygomorphic flowers that vary in color from pink to reddish-purple. Each flower possesses a prominent, hooded upper lip which is a defining feature of the genus.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to high-altitude regions of central and western China, specifically within provinces such as Sichuan, Yunnan, Gansu, and Qinghai. It typically inhabits montane meadows and alpine slopes where the soil is well-drained and moisture is consistent. The species thrives at elevations ranging from subalpine zones to high mountain peaks. Its distribution is closely tied to the specific climatic conditions found within the Hengduan Mountains and surrounding plateau areas.
