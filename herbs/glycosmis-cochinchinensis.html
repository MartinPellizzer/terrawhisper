Glycosmis cochinchinensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Glycosmis cochinchinensis has small, elliptical to lanceolate leaves that are dark green, leathery in texture, and arranged in an opposite or sub-opposite pattern along the woody stems. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree characterized by its dense, branching habit and smooth bark. Its flowers are small and white, often appearing in clusters that emerge from the leaf axils. The fruit develops into small, fleshy berries that change color as they reach maturity. These morphological traits are characteristic of the Rutaceae family to which this species belongs.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Glycosmis cochinchinensis contains several bioactive alkaloids, most notably those from the indole class such as glycosmin, glycosmidine, and cochinchinine. These secondary metabolites are primarily responsible for the plant's documented pharmacological activities, which include antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Researchers have also identified various quinoline alkaloids and terpenoids within the plant's chemical profile. These diverse compounds contribute to its traditional use in treating various ailments in folk medicine.
