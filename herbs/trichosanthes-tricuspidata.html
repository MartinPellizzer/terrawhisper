Trichosanthes tricuspidata

What's the taxonomical classification of Trichosanthes tricuspidata?

Trichosanthes tricuspidata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Cucurbitales and the family Cucurbitaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Trichosanthes, and its unique species designation, tricuspidata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Trichosanthes tricuspidata has an herbaceous, climbing habit characterized by slender, twining stems and trifoliate leaves with three distinctively pointed lobes. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape and feature serrated margins that enhance their serrated appearance. Its inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in the leaf axils. The fruit is a small, globose berry that transitions in color as it reaches maturity. These morphological traits collectively distinguish this species within its taxonomic group.

What are the pharmacological activities of Trichosanthes tricuspidata?

This plant has anti-inflammatory, antifever, antioxidant, cytotoxic, and anthelmintic pharmacological activities. Its antioxidant properties help mitigate oxidative stress by neutralizing harmful free radicals in the body. The anti-inflammatory and antifever capabilities suggest its potential for managing pain and systemic heat. Furthermore, the plant demonstrates cytotoxic effects that may be useful in targeted cellular studies. Finally, its anthelmintic activity indicates an ability to expel parasitic worms, making it valuable in traditional medicine.

As discussed in a study, Trichosanthes tricuspidata Lour. has been traditionally used as a medicinal plant for its antifever, laxative, anthelmintic properties and for migraine treatment. The ethanol extract (Tt-ME) attenuated the release of NO and decreased mRNA levels of iNOS, TNF-α, and IL-6 in LPS-induced macrophages in a concentration-dependent manner. Tt-ME time-dependently suppressed the nuclear translocation of NF-κB subunits p50 and p65, AP-1 subunits c-Fos and c-Jun, and STAT3 transcriptional activity. It significantly downregulated NF-κB, MAPK, and JAK2 signaling by targeting Syk, Src, and IRAK1 protein kinases. Furthermore, Tt-ME downregulated MMP-9 expression and cell migration in LPS-activated macrophages. In vivo studies on Tt-ME also produced similar trends in HCl/EtOH-induced gastritis mouse models by inhibiting proinflamatory cytokines and the inflammatory signaling pathway.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds 2-O-glucocucuurbitacin J, cyclotricuspidoside A, cyclotricuspidoside C, various cucurbitaicins, and tricuspidatin. These specific chemical constituents are categorized as triterpenoids and cucurbitane-type glycosides. Research into these molecules often focuses on their potential biological activities and pharmacological properties. The presence of these compounds provides a complex chemical profile that researchers study for therapeutic development. Understanding the precise structure of each substance helps in identifying how they interact with biological systems.

As discussed in a study, an extract of the fruits of Trichosanthes tricuspidata collected in North Vietnam was found to be cytotoxic in KB cells. Bioassay-guided fractionation led to the isolation of a series of cucurbitacins from these fruits. Within this series, 2 new compounds were identified, which are named tricuspidatin and 2-O-glucocucuurbitacin J. The study focuses on the isolation and structure determination of these specific medicinal compounds.
