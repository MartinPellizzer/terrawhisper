Thalictrum fortunei

What's the taxonomical classification of Thalictrum fortunei?

Thalictrum fortunei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Ranunculales. This plant is a member of the family Ranunculaceae, specifically placed within the genus Thalictrum, and is identified by the specific epithet fortunei.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Thalictrum fortunei has basal, trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed and light green in color, supporting upright, branching stems that reach heights of approximately 60 to 90 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers that lack true petals but feature prominent, pale yellow or cream-colored stamens. These inflorescences are held aloft on slender pedicels, creating a light and airy appearance throughout the blooming season. The root system typically consists of a rhizomatous structure that allows the plant to spread gradually in moist environments. As the growing season concludes, the foliage may fade, leaving behind small seed capsules that facilitate reproduction.
