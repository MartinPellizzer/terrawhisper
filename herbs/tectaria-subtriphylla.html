Tectaria subtriphylla

What's the taxonomical classification of Tectaria subtriphylla?

Tectaria subtriphylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae. Following its taxonomic progression, it falls under the order Polypodiales and is a member of the family Polypodiaceae. Finally, its specific identity is defined by the genus Tectaria and the species name subtriphylla.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tectaria subtriphylla has pinnate fronds characterized by narrow, lanceolate leaflets that are arranged oppositely along a central rachis.

The plant exhibits a creeping rhizome structure that allows it to spread across its substrate. Each leaflet features finely serrated margins and a distinctively pointed apex. The texture of the foliage is relatively thin, often displaying a delicate, translucent appearance under bright light.

These morphological traits are typical of ferns adapted to shaded, humid forest environments.
