Tectaria subtriphylla

What's the taxonomical classification of Tectaria subtriphylla?

Tectaria subtriphylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae. Following its taxonomic progression, it falls under the order Polypodiales and is a member of the family Polypodiaceae. Finally, its specific identity is defined by the genus Tectaria and the species name subtriphylla.

What are the therapeutic actions of Tectaria subtriphylla?

Tectaria subtriphylla has pharmacological properties that allow it to act as an anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antimicrobial agent in traditional medicine. Researchers have noted its ability to inhibit the growth of certain pathogens, making it useful for treating skin infections or digestive issues. It is frequently utilized in herbal decoctions to manage fever and promote general systemic recovery. What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin diseases, wounds, inflammation, and digestive issues. The leaves and stems are often prepared into poultices to soothe localized skin irritations or infections. In some traditional practices, decoctions made from the plant are consumed to alleviate internal discomfort and stomach ailments. These medicinal applications rely on the specific bioactive compounds found within its tissues. Local communities utilize these properties to manage both external injuries and internal wellness.
