Tectaria subtriphylla

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tectaria subtriphylla has pinnate fronds characterized by narrow, lanceolate leaflets that are arranged oppositely along a central rachis. The plant exhibits a creeping rhizome structure that allows it to spread across its substrate. Each leaflet features finely serrated margins and a distinctively pointed apex. The texture of the foliage is relatively thin, often displaying a delicate, translucent appearance under bright light. These morphological traits are typical of ferns adapted to shaded, humid forest environments.
