Aster scaber

What's the taxonomical classification of Aster scaber?

Aster scaber belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae, which places it within the genus Aster, specifically identified by the species name scaber.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Aster scaber targets various enzymes and inflammatory signaling molecules, specifically inhibiting cyclooxygenase (COX) enzymes and lipoxygenase (LOX) to reduce prostaglandin and leukotriene synthesis. The plant contains bioactive sesquiterpene lactones and phenolic compounds that modulate the activity of nuclear factor kappa B (NF-kappaB), a key protein in the inflammatory cascade. These phytochemicals also interact with antioxidant enzymes such as superoxide dismutase and catalase to mitigate oxidative stress within cells. Furthermore, specific polyphenols in the plant may bind to and inhibit certain cytokines, thereby tempering the systemic immune response. Such protein-ligand interactions contribute to the plant's documented anti-inflammatory and cytoprotective properties.

While systemic toxicity is not widely documented in humans, ingestion should be avoided to prevent unknown gastrointestinal distress. It is generally recommended to wear gloves when working with this species to minimize potential dermatological issues. Always consult a medical professional if a reaction occurs after exposure.
