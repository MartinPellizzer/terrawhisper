Parnassia bifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Parnassia bifolia has a basal rosette of two thick, fleshy, ovate-shaped leaves and a single upright flowering stem that can reach up to 20 centimeters in height. The stem typically bears a few smaller, bract-like leaves arranged in opposite pairs. Its solitary white flower is characterized by five petals that feature distinct, dark, vein-like markings and a prominent yellowish center. The reproductive structures include a central cluster of numerous stamens and a single pistil surrounded by nectar-secreting glands. This plant often grows in damp, mossy environments where its low-profile foliage can spread effectively.
