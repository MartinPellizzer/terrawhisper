Rhododendron brachyanthum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhododendron brachyanthum has small, elliptic to lanceolate leaves that are typically leathery and evergreen, paired with short, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant grows as a low-lying shrub, often characterized by its compact and spreading growth habit. Its stems are woody and can become quite dense as the plant matures in its montane habitat. The inflorescences are typically solitary or occur in small clusters, emerging from the leaf axils. These morphological traits allow the species to thrive in the specific ecological niches of the high-altitude regions it inhabits.

        What side effects this plant can have?

        This plant can cause severe physiological distress including nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, dizziness, confusion, arrhythmia, hypotension, and seizures due to the presence of toxic grayanotoxins. These toxins interfere with the sodium channels in cell membranes, leading to significant cardiovascular complications like irregular heartbeats and dangerously low blood pressure. As the poisoning progresses, neurological symptoms such as disorientation and extreme lightheadedness may manifest. In many cases, the gastrointestinal irritation is accompanied by intense abdominal pain. Severe neurological instability can ultimately result in life-threatening seizures.
