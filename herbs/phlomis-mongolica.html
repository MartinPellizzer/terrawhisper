Phlomis mongolica

What's the taxonomical classification of Phlomis mongolica?

Phlomis mongolica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. This specific plant is identified by its genus, Phlomis, and is distinguished at the species level as Phlomis mongolica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phlomis mongolica has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by erect, square-shaped stems that often reach heights of up to 50 centimeters. The leaves are arranged in opposite pairs and possess an oblong or lanceolate shape with a distinctly rugose, hairy texture. Its inflorescence consists of dense, terminal verticillasters that hold small, tubular, yellowish-white flowers. The calyx is typically two-lipped and covered in fine pubescence, providing protection to the developing floral structures. These morphological traits help the plant adapt to its specific arid and rocky habitats.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds cistanoside B, phloyoside I, and phloyoside III. These specific glycosides are secondary metabolites that contribute to the plant's unique biochemical profile. Research into these substances often focuses on their potential pharmacological activities and therapeutic properties. Scientists study the chemical structures of phloyoside I and phloyoside III to understand how they interact with biological systems. Cistanoside B also plays a significant role in the study of the plant's complex medicinal composition.

As discussed in a study, the root of Phlomis mongolica contains specific chemical constituents that were investigated through solvent extraction and chromatographic separation. Three compounds were obtained and identified using MS, 1H-NMR, 13C-NMR, DEPT, HMQC, and HMBC methods. These identified structures are phloyoside III, phloyoside I, and cistanoside B. The research established that phloyoside III is a new compound. These three compounds were obtained from Phlomis mongolica for the first time.
