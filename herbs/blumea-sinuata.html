Blumea sinuata

What's the taxonomical classification of Blumea sinuata?

Blumea sinuata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Asterales and the family Asteraceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific genus, Blumea, and its unique species designation, sinuata.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Blumea sinuata contains a diverse array of bioactive secondary metabolites including sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, terpenoids, and essential oils such as camphor and borneol. These specific chemical constituents are responsible for the plant's documented anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant activities. The presence of various diterpenoids and phenolic compounds further enhances its potential for treating respiratory ailments and skin infections. Researchers often study these compounds to develop new pharmacological treatments for metabolic disorders. The synergy between these diverse organic molecules makes the plant a significant subject in ethnopharmacology.

What are the therapeutic actions of Blumea sinuata?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat respiratory ailments such as cough, asthma, and bronchitis, as well as digestive issues like diarrhea and stomach aches. It also possesses properties used to manage skin infections, inflammation, and wound healing. Some traditional practices employ its leaves to reduce fever and alleviate symptoms of malaria. The plant's bioactive compounds contribute to its roles in supporting kidney function and managing urinary tract discomfort. Researchers continue to study its potential for treating metabolic disorders and various inflammatory conditions.
