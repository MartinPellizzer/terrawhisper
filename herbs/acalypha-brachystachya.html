Acalypha brachystachya

What's the taxonomical classification of Acalypha brachystachya?

Acalypha brachystachya belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Malpighiales. Furthermore, this plant is a member of the family Euphorbiaceae, specifically categorized within the genus Acalypha, with the specific species designation of brachystachyia.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat gastrointestinal ailments including diarrhea, stomach aches, and intestinal worms. Traditional practitioners often prepare decoctions from the leaves to soothe digestive distress and regulate bowel movements. The medicinal properties of the plant are also thought to assist in managing inflammation within the digestive tract. Some regional studies suggest its use in addressing various skin infections and minor wounds. Local communities rely on these specific applications to manage common ailments in rural settings.
