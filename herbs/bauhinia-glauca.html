Bauhinia glauca

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bauhinia glauca has bipinnately compound leaves that are characterized by a distinctively bluish-gray or glaucous waxy coating on their surface. The plant typically develops into a small to medium-sized tree with a spreading canopy and woody stems. Its flowers are prominent and usually feature five petals that can range in color from white to pale lavender. The reproductive structures include central stamens that are often a contrasting color to the petals. Small, elongated seed pods develop along the branches as the plant matures.
