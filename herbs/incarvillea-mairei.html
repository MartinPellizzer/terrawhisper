Incarvillea mairei

What's the taxonomical classification of Incarvillea mairei?

Incarvillea mairei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further descends through the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Bignoniaceae. Ultimately, its taxonomic identity is defined by its placement within the genus Incarvillea, specifically as the species mairei.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Incarvillea mairei has large, opposite, ovate leaves that are typically covered in soft, velvety hairs and long-tubular, bright orange-to-red flowers that emerge from upright spikes. The foliage is dense and lush, often providing a thick green base for the striking floral displays. These blossoms feature a prominent elongated corolla tube that tapers at the base and flares into wide lobes at the mouth. The stems are sturdy and erect, supporting the heavy weight of the blooming inflorescences throughout the growing season. As the plant matures, the flowers are arranged in a terminal raceme that rises significantly above the basal leaf rosette.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the unique bioactive sesquiterpene lactones known as incarvmareins A and incarvmareins B. These specific chemical constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's distinct pharmacological properties. Researchers have identified these compounds as significant markers for the species' medicinal potential. Beyond these primary molecules, the plant's chemical profile includes various other secondary metabolites. Studying these substances provides insight into how the plant interacts with biological systems.

According to a study published by "Journal of Asian natural products research", the ethanolic extract of the roots of Incarvillea mairei contained several medicinal compounds. Two new cyclohexyl-ethanol derivatives, incarvmareins A (1) and B (2), were isolated from the plant. Additionally, two known derivatives, 3 and 4, were also identified within the extract. The structures of these 2 new compounds were elucidated primarily through the analysis of spectroscopic data.
