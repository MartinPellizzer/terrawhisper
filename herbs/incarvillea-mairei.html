Incarvillea mairei

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Incarvillea mairei has large, opposite, ovate leaves that are typically covered in soft, velvety hairs and long-tubular, bright orange-to-red flowers that emerge from upright spikes. The foliage is dense and lush, often providing a thick green base for the striking floral displays. These blossoms feature a prominent elongated corolla tube that tapers at the base and flares into wide lobes at the mouth. The stems are sturdy and erect, supporting the heavy weight of the blooming inflorescences throughout the growing season. As the plant matures, the flowers are arranged in a terminal raceme that rises significantly above the basal leaf rosette.
