Incarvillea mairei

What's the taxonomical classification of Incarvillea mairei?

Incarvillea mairei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further descends through the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Bignoniaceae. Ultimately, its taxonomic identity is defined by its placement within the genus Incarvillea, specifically as the species mairei.

    What are the molecular targets of this plant?

    Incarvillea mairei targets specific enzymes and inflammatory mediators, primarily inhibiting cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2) and various pro-inflammatory cytokines to reduce systemic inflammation. Its bioactive alkaloids and flavonoids interact with the NF-kappaB signaling pathway, which helps suppress the transcription of genes involved in the inflammatory response. These compounds also modulate nitric oxide synthase (NOS) activity, thereby regulating the production of nitric oxide in vascular and immune cells. Additionally, certain constituents appear to influence the expression of tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-alpha) and interleukin-6 (IL-6) to mitigate tissue damage. Through these diverse protein interactions, the plant exhibits significant anti-inflammatory and potential immunomodulatory properties.
