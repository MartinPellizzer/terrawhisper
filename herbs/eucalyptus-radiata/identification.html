Botanical Identification of Radiata Eucalyptus (Eucalyptus radiata)

To identify Radiata Eucalyptus (Eucalyptus radiata) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Radiata Eucalyptus, or Eucalyptus radiata, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Sapindales, family Myrtaceae, genus Eucalyptus, and species Eucalyptus radiata. This classification places it within the broader group of flowering plants and highlights its relationship to other members of the Myrtaceae family.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Radiata Eucalyptus is Eucalyptus radiata, first described by the botanist Robert Brown in 1810. It has several synonyms, including Eucalyptus aculeata and Eucalyptus semiserrata. Common names include Radiata Eucalyptus, Narrow-leafed Gum, and Small-leafed Box. It is also known by regional and historical names such as "Radiata" and "Eucalyptus radiata" in various local contexts.

Morphological Characteristics

Radiata Eucalyptus is a medium-sized tree with a straight, cylindrical trunk and a rounded crown. Its root system is shallow and fibrous, adapting to nutrient-poor soils. Leaves are lance-shaped, glossy green, and aromatic, with a prominent midrib. The inflorescence is a compact spike, bearing small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The fruit is a woody capsule containing numerous small, brown seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Eucalyptus radiata can be distinguished by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a grayish-green upper surface and a white underside. It has smooth, peeling bark that is pale to cream-colored. The flowers are small, white or pink, and clustered in umbels. It is often found in open woodland or heathland environments.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Radiata Eucalyptus is native to southeastern Australia, particularly New South Wales and southern Queensland. It thrives in temperate to subtropical climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained soils, such as sandy or loamy types, and is commonly found at altitudes between 200 and 800 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Radiata Eucalyptus blooms between September and November, producing small white or pale yellow flowers. Seed formation occurs from late winter to early spring, with seeds maturing by late spring. For identification purposes, harvest timing is best during the flowering period when leaves and flowers are most distinct.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Eucalyptus radiata is closely related to other species within the Eucalyptus genus, particularly E. camphora and E. coccifera. It shares key traits with its genus, such as aromatic oils and lanceolate leaves. Within the Myrtaceae family, it exhibits typical features like eucalyptus-specific floral structures and wood anatomy.
