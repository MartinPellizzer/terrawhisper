Pleurospermum amabile

What's the taxonomical classification of Pleurospermum amabile?

Pleurospermum amabile belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized as Equisetopsida, falling under the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Apiales and is a member of the family Apiaceae. Within this botanical framework, it is defined by the genus Pleurospermum and is specifically identified by the species name amabile.

What are the pharmacological activities of Pleurospermum amabile?

This plant has anthelmintic, anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, antimalarial, and antiplasmodial pharmacological activities. These medicinal properties allow the species to combat various parasitic infections and bacterial pathogens effectively. Its anti-inflammatory actions help in reducing swelling and pain associated with different physiological conditions. Furthermore, the plant's ability to target malaria and plasmodium species highlights its potential in treating tropical diseases. These diverse biological effects underscore the therapeutic importance of Pleurospermum amabile in traditional medicine.

As discussed in a study, Pleurospermum amabile yielded 19 phytochemicals, 6 of which were investigated for anthelmintic properties. Among these, isomyristicin and bergapten showed significant activity against Schistosoma mansoni and Trichuris muris. Bergapten was identified as the most efficacious compound against both parasites, with an IC50 of 8.6 μg/mL for S. mansoni and an IC50 of 10.6 μg/mL for T. muris. These compounds induced tegumental damage to S. mansoni and affected the cuticle, bacillary bands, and bacillary glands of T. muris. Bergapten also demonstrated efficacy against the schistosomulum stage of S. mansoni.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds psoralen, bergapten, isoimperatorin, methyl eugenol, and (E)-isoapiol. These specific phytochemicals are often studied for their various pharmacological properties and biological activities. Psoralen and bergapten are known furanocoumarins that can interact with ultraviolet light in the skin. The presence of isoimperatorin and methyl eugenol adds to the complex chemical profile of the species. Additionally, (E)-isoapiol contributes to the unique therapeutic potential associated with these botanical extracts.

As discussed in a study, Pleurospermum amabile contains four phenylpropanoids including (E)-isomyristicin (1), (E)-isoapiol (2), methyl eugenol (3), and (E)-isoelemicin (4). The plant also contains six furanocoumarins consisting of psoralen (5), bergapten (6), isoimperatorin (7), isopimpinellin (8), oxypeucedanin hydrate (9), and oxypeucedanin methanolate (10). Among the test samples, compound 10 showed weak antibacterial activity against Bacillus subtilis. Additionally, compound 10 demonstrated the best antimalarial activity against the Plasmodium falciparum strains TM4/8.2, which is chloroquine and antifolate sensitive, and K1CB1, which is multidrug resistant. None of the test samples showed cytotoxicity.
