Laxa Anthemis (Anthemis laxa) Compounds

The primary compounds of Laxa Anthemis include essential oil, flavonoids, terpenoids, and phenolic acids. These compounds contribute to its medicinal properties, such as anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Coumarin and tannins also play roles in its therapeutic applications. Glycosides, saponins, and alkaloids add to its diverse pharmacological profile.

Laxa Anthemis (Anthemis laxa) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Laxa Anthemis contains bioactive compounds like chamazulene and bisabolol. These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. They are commonly used in herbal medicine to treat digestive issues and skin conditions. The oil is extracted through steam distillation from the plant's flowering tops.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Laxa Anthemis (Anthemis laxa) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help reduce oxidative stress and support immune function.
