What is Pilosocereus machrisii?

Pilosocereus machrisii is a species of cactus in the family Cactaceae, native to Venezuela and Colombia.

It is a columnar cactus with a height of up to 3 meters and a stem diameter of up to 10 centimeters, with 5-12 low-angled ribs and areoles producing 2-4 short, white or yellowish spines. The plant has white to yellowish flowers, up to 4 centimeters in diameter, in the axils of the spines, which are funnel-shaped and have a greenish-yellow tube. The plant's stem is covered with brown or yellowish, 1-2 centimeter long, tubercles, which are often covered with a white or yellowish, powdery coating.

Pilosocereus machrisii produces red, 2-5 centimeter long, berry-like fruits that contain many seeds.
