Tall Goldenrod (Solidago Altissima Var. Procera)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Solidago altissima var. procera.

Tall goldenrod, scientifically known as Solidago altissima var. procera, is a medicinal herb prized for its adaptogenic properties and historical significance in traditional medicine. This tall, herbaceous plant is native to North America and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and robust growth, often found in moist meadows and along stream banks. Its primary benefits include supporting respiratory health, reducing inflammation, and enhancing immune function due to its high content of flavonoids and sesquiterpene lactones. Traditionally, Indigenous peoples and early American settlers used tall goldenrod to treat colds, bronchitis, and skin ailments, while also incorporating it into teas and poultices. In modern wellness practices, it is valued as a natural remedy for allergies and digestive issues, with its unique, slightly bitter flavor and rare compound called arctigenin contributing to its therapeutic potential.
