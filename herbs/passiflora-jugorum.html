Passiflora jugorum

What's the taxonomical classification of Passiflora jugorum?

Passiflora jugorum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Malpighiales. This plant is a member of the family Passifloraceae, falling within the genus Passiflora, and is specifically identified as the species jugorum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Passiflora jugorum has woody climbing vines characterized by trifoliate leaves, tendrils for attachment, and large, complex flowers with prominent corona filaments. The foliage typically consists of three distinct leaflets that exhibit a smooth or slightly serrated margin. Its climbing mechanism relies on specialized coiled tendrils that emerge from the nodes to secure the plant to surrounding vegetation. The inflorescence features a dramatic corona composed of multiple layers of colorful filaments surrounding a central structure of reproductive organs. These flowers are often accompanied by large, fleshy fruits that develop from the inferior ovary.

What are the pharmacological activities of Passiflora jugorum?

This plant has sedative, anxiolytic, anticonvulsant, and mild hypnotic pharmacological activities primarily driven by its flavonoids and alkaloids. These bioactive compounds interact with the central nervous system to modulate GABAergic neurotransmission, which helps reduce anxiety and promote relaxation. Clinical studies also suggest that the plant can improve sleep quality by decreasing the time required to fall asleep. Furthermore, its neuroprotective properties may assist in mitigating oxidative stress within brain tissues. These diverse medicinal effects make it a significant subject for both traditional medicine and modern pharmaceutical research.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains various alkaloids such as harmine, harmaline, and harman, as well as flavonoids like apigenin and vitexin. These specific chemical compounds act primarily on the central nervous system to produce sedative and anxiolytic effects. The presence of these bioactive substances helps to modulate GABA receptors in the brain, which reduces anxiety and promotes sleep. Additionally, the plant features trace amounts of cyanogenic glycosides and various phenolic acids. These diverse constituents work together to provide the therapeutic benefits typically associated with passionflower extracts.
