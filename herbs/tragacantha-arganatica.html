Possible Side Effects of Tragacantha arganatica

Tragacantha arganatica can have side effects if used improperly, such as skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal issues.

Ingesting large amounts of its extracts can cause nausea, vomiting, and stomach ulcers, while topical application may lead to redness, itching, and dryness of the skin.

Prolonged use or excessive dosage of the plant can also lead to interactions with other medications, affecting blood pressure, heart rate, and liver function.

It is essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using Tragacantha arganatica for medicinal purposes to avoid potential side effects.
