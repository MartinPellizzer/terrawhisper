What's the taxonomical classification of Photinia beauverdiana?

Photinia beauverdiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant is part of the order Rosales and falls under the family Rosaceae. Finally, it is categorized within the genus Photinia, specifically identified by the species name beauverdiana.

    What side effects this plant can have?

The leaves and stems contain cyanogenic glycosides that release toxic hydrogen cyanide into the body upon consumption. Ingestion of these compounds can lead to more serious systemic symptoms such as dizziness, headache, and rapid breathing. Large quantities of the plant material may even result in life-threatening complications like seizures or respiratory failure. It is essential to handle the plant with caution to avoid accidental poisoning.
