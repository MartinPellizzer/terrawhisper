Terminalia elliptica

What's the taxonomical classification of Terminalia elliptica?

Terminalia elliptica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant falls under the order Myrtales. It is further categorized into the family Combretaceae and is situated within the genus Terminalia, eventually reaching its specific designation as the species elliptica.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Terminalia elliptica contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including tannins, flavonoids, saponins, and terpenoids that provide its therapeutic properties. These chemical constituents contribute to significant antioxidant and antimicrobial activities when used in traditional medicine. The presence of phenolic compounds specifically helps in neutralizing free radicals and reducing oxidative stress in biological systems. Extracts from the bark and leaves are often utilized for their potential wound-healing and anti-inflammatory effects. Research continues to explore how these specific organic compounds can be isolated for targeted pharmaceutical applications.

What are the therapeutic actions of Terminalia elliptica?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including skin infections, gastrointestinal disorders, inflammation, and wound healing. Its leaves and bark contain bioactive compounds that help manage symptoms of digestive issues like diarrhea. The extracts are frequently applied topically to soothe skin irritations and promote the recovery of minor cuts. Additionally, certain preparations are utilized to reduce bodily inflammation and manage pain. Traditional medicinal practices rely on these properties to address a wide range of minor physical discomforts.
