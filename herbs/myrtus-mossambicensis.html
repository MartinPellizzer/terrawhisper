    What is Myrtus mossambicensis?

    Myrtus mossambicensis is a shrub or small tree species native to tropical Africa.

    It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, characterized by its fragrant, white or pink flowers in axillary clusters, followed by yellow or brown berries. The leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical, measuring 1-3 cm long, with a petiole 1-2 mm long and a cuneate base. The bark is smooth, gray, and fissured.

    The species typically grows up to 3-10 m in height, with a diameter of 1-4 m.
