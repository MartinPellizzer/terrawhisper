Caroxylon aphyllum is a shrub-like succulent plant that belongs to the family Amaranthaceae.

The stems are covered with a thick, waxy layer, and bear small, green, and ovate leaves, 1-3 cm in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in axillary clusters, which are monoecious and have a terminal spike of female flowers.

Caroxylon aphyllum is native to the dry regions of southern Africa and is commonly found in areas with low rainfall.
