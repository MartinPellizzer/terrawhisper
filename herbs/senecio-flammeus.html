What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Senecio flammeus has upright, woody stems that support clusters of bright orange or red flower heads surrounded by dense, lanceolate, grayish-green leaves with a felt-like texture. The foliage is characterized by a fine covering of hairs that provides a velvety appearance and helps protect the plant from moisture loss. Each inflorescence consists of many small tubular florets packed tightly into a compact head. These flower heads are typically held aloft on sturdy stalks above the main leaf mass. The overall structure of the plant is bushy and compact, making it well-suited for arid environments.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause severe hepatotoxicity resulting in permanent liver damage, jaundice, and potential carcinogenicity due to the presence of pyrrolizidine alkaloids. Ingesting the plant often leads to immediate gastrointestinal distress including nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea. These acute symptoms can progress into chronic issues as toxins accumulate within the hepatic system. Long-term exposure significantly increases the risk of developing liver tumors or failing organ function.
