Cratoxylum cochinchinense

What's the taxonomical classification of Cratoxylum cochinchinense?

Cratoxylum cochinchinense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is situated under the order Malpighiales. This specific plant is a representative of the family Hypericaceae, falling under the genus Cratoxylum, and is identified by the species name cochinchinense.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Cratoxylum cochinchinense contains high concentrations of bioactive alkaloids, specifically those belonging to the oxindole class, which exhibit significant pharmacological potential. These complex nitrogenous compounds are primarily responsible for the plant's observed antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Researchers have identified various indole derivatives within the bark and leaves that interact with biological targets to modulate immune responses. Additionally, some studies suggest these chemical constituents may possess cytotoxic activities useful in cancer research. The specific chemical profile of these alkaloids varies depending on the plant's geographical origin and extraction method.

What are the therapeutic actions of Cratoxylum cochinchinense?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat dysentery, diarrhea, skin infections, and various types of inflammation. The bark contains high concentrations of tannins which provide potent antimicrobial and astringent properties. These medicinal qualities allow it to effectively control intestinal ailments and promote the healing of wounded skin. Traditional practitioners often prepare decoctions from the bark to manage these diverse physical conditions. It remains a significant component in many regional folk medicine practices for addressing gastrointestinal distress.
