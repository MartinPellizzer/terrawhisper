Cratoxylum cochinchinense

What's the taxonomical classification of Cratoxylum cochinchinense?

Cratoxylum cochinchinense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is situated under the order Malpighiales. This specific plant is a representative of the family Hypericaceae, falling under the genus Cratoxylum, and is identified by the species name cochinchinense.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Cratoxylum cochinchinense contains medicinal compounds primarily extracted from its red bark and leaves. The bark is highly valued for its rich concentration of rotenoids and tannins which are utilized in traditional treatments. Local healers often prepare decoctions from the leaves to address various inflammatory conditions. Additionally, the sap or resin derived from the tree can be applied topically to certain skin ailments. These different parts of the plant serve as a vital resource in regional ethnobotanical practices.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse range of bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, terpenoids, and phenolic compounds.

These chemical constituents are primarily concentrated within the bark and contribute to the species' significant pharmacological potential. The alkaloids are often investigated for their neuroprotective and antimicrobial properties. Meanwhile, the flavonoids and phenolic compounds provide potent antioxidant activities that help mitigate oxidative stress in biological systems.

Furthermore, the presence of various terpenoids supports the plant's traditional use in treating inflammatory conditions.

The primary medicinal compounds of this plant are shown in the list below.

  • Alkaloids
  • Flavonoids
  • Terpenoids
  • Phenolics

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Cratoxylum cochinchiene targets cyclooxygenase enzymes, inflammatory cytokines, and various bacterial proteins to exert its pharmacological effects. The plant contains bioactive compounds like rotenoids and flavonoids that primarily inhibit the COX-2 enzyme to reduce prostaglandin synthesis. These secondary metabolites also interfere with the signaling pathways of pro-inflammatory mediators such as tumor necrosis factor-alpha and interleukin-6. Furthermore, specific alkaloids within the species interact with microbial enzymes to disrupt cell wall synthesis or metabolic processes in pathogens. This multi-target interaction profile accounts for the plant's documented anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties.

What are the therapeutic actions of Cratoxylum cochinchinense?

Cratoxylum cochinchinense has potent antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties derived primarily from its bark and resin. The plant contains various bioactive compounds that effectively inhibit the growth of certain bacteria and fungi. These chemical constituents also assist in reducing swelling and pain through the modulation of inflammatory pathways. Additionally, the high concentration of phenolic compounds helps neutralize free radicals within biological systems. These combined actions make the plant a valuable subject for pharmacological research and traditional medicinal applications.

What common ailments are treated using this plant?

This plant is used to treat diarrhea, dysentery, skin infections, and inflammation.

Its red bark contains bioactive compounds that serve as powerful medicinal agents. Practitioners often prepare decoctions to soothe gastrointestinal distress and regulate bowel movements. The plant's antimicrobial properties also make it effective for cleaning and healing various skin infections.

Additionally, its chemical makeup helps to reduce inflammation throughout the body.

The main common ailment treated using this plant are shown in the list below.

  • Diarrhea
  • Dysentery
  • Skin infections
  • Inflammation

What are the herbal preparations of Cratoxylum cochinchinense?

Cratoxylum cochinchiene is prepared as a decoction from its bark, a tincture from its roots, or a topical paste made from crushed leaves. The bark is most commonly boiled in water to extract its red pigments and medicinal compounds for internal consumption. These extracts are frequently used in traditional medicine to address ailments such as diarrhea or digestive issues. Additionally, the plant's sap can be processed into dyes or applied directly to the skin for its astringent properties. The versatility of these preparation methods allows different parts of the plant to serve various therapeutic purposes.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Cratoxylum cochinchinense can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, and potential toxicity if ingested in uncontrolled amounts. While its bark is used in traditional medicine for its dyeing and medicinal properties, consuming concentrated extracts may lead to nausea or vomiting. Some individuals might experience allergic reactions such as redness or itching when the plant comes into contact with the skin. The lack of extensive clinical studies means the precise long-term effects on human health remain largely unknown. Therefore, caution is necessary when handling or consuming any part of this species.
