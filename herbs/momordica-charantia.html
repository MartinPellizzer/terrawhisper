Bitter Melon

Momordica charantia

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Bitter Melon, scientifically known as Momordica charantia, belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family. Common names include bitter gourd and karela, with regional variations. It is an annual vine native to tropical regions of Asia. The plant has heart-shaped leaves, yellow flowers, and elongated fruits with a bitter taste. Synonyms in herbal literature include "kali mirch" and "bitter apple."

Active Compounds

Bitter melon contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins. These compounds work synergistically to provide its medicinal properties, including antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Phenolic acid
  • Saponin

Preparation Methods

Extract: Used for diabetes management and blood sugar regulation.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and inflammation.

Powder: Used for diabetes management and as a dietary supplement.

Safety Profile

Bitter melon is generally safe when consumed in typical food amounts. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. Long-term use or high doses may lead to hypoglycemia, especially in people with diabetes. Consult a healthcare provider before use, particularly if you have underlying health conditions.
