Momordica Charantia Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Momordica charantia, commonly known as bitter melon, is a tropical vine native to Asia, prized for its unique bitter taste and medicinal properties.

This herb has been used for centuries in traditional medicine to support blood sugar regulation and digestive health.

It contains bioactive constituents such as charantin, alkaloids, and polysaccharides, which contribute to its therapeutic actions.

Herbal preparations like infusions, decoctions, and capsules can be made from its fruit, leaves, or seeds to harness its health benefits.

It leads to nausea because these compounds may stimulate the vomiting center in the brain and increase gut motility. Momordica charantia causes mouth irritation as its bitter compounds can directly affect the oral mucosa, leading to a burning sensation.

It may cause diarrhea because the herb's active ingredients can accelerate intestinal movement and reduce water absorption in the digestive system.
