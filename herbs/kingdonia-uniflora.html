What's the taxonomical classification of Kingdonia uniflora?

Kingdonia uniflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Ranunculales. This unique plant is a member of the family Circaeasteraceae and is specifically categorized under the genus Kingdonia, with its precise scientific designation being the species uniflora.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Kingdonia uniflora has a perennial rhizomatous habit featuring narrow, linear leaves that are typically 3 to 10 centimeters long and arranged in a basal rosette. This flower consists of six tepals that are creamy white or pale yellow in color. The reproductive organs include a superior ovary and several stamens that are nestled within the tepals.
