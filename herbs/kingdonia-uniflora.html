Kingdonia uniflora

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Kingdonia uniflora has a perennial rhizomatous habit featuring narrow, linear leaves that are typically 3 to 10 centimeters long and arranged in a basal rosette. The foliage is characterized by a distinct midrib and a smooth, somewhat fleshy texture. A single, solitary flower emerges on a slender pedicel from the center of the leaf cluster. This flower consists of six tepals that are creamy white or pale yellow in color. The reproductive organs include a superior ovary and several stamens that are nestled within the tepals.
