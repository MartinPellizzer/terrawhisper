Cloudberry (Rubus chamaemorus) Compounds

Cloudberry contains several primary compounds, including flavonoids, which act as antioxidants. Tannins contribute to its astringent properties. Phenolic acids and anthocyanins provide antioxidant and anti-inflammatory benefits. Coumarin and glycosides may support digestive health. Saponins, essential oils, terpenoids, and alkaloids also contribute to its medicinal properties.

Cloudberry (Rubus chamaemorus) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Cloudberry (Rubus chamaemorus) contribute to its antioxidant properties. These compounds help neutralize free radicals, supporting cellular health. They may also reduce inflammation and enhance immune function. Flavonoids are found in the berries and roots of this plant.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Cloudberry (Rubus chamaemorus). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health. Tannins in cloudberry are known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. They are commonly used in herbal medicine to treat conditions like diarrhea and skin irritations.
