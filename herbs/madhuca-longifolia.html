Madhuca

Madhuca longifolia

Madhuca (Madhuca longifolia) is a plant belonging to the Sapotaceae family. Traditionally, it has been used as a tonic, for the treatment of skin diseases, and in respiratory conditions. The leaves and bark are commonly utilized in these traditional applications.

Botanical Identification

Madhuca longifolia, commonly known as the longifolia Madhuca, is a member of the Sapotaceae family. It is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. The plant has smooth, elliptical leaves and produces yellowish flowers. It is also referred to as "Kodampully" in some regional contexts.

Madhuca Identification.

Active Compounds

Madhuca longifolia contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to provide antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial effects, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Tannin
  • Phenolic acid

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for respiratory and digestive ailments.

Infusion: Used for mild skin irritations and digestive issues.

Poultice: Used for skin conditions and wounds.

Safety Profile

Madhuca longifolia is generally considered safe when used in traditional herbal medicine. However, it may cause allergic reactions in some individuals. Its safety profile is not well-documented in modern pharmacological studies, so caution is advised, especially during pregnancy or for those with known sensitivities.
