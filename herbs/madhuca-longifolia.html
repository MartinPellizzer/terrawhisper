Madhuca Longifolia

Madhuca longifolia, commonly known as butter tree, is a medicinal herb native to the tropical forests of India and Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree with a wide range of health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help to reduce symptoms of fever, cough, and respiratory issues.

The therapeutic actions of Madhuca longifolia include its ability to reduce inflammation, fight bacterial infections, and exhibit analgesic and anti-diabetic effects.

The bioactive constituents of Madhuca longifolia include triterpenoids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties and potential health benefits.

Possible Side Effects of madhuca longifolia

Furthermore, improper use of Madhuca longifolia may also cause blood sugar fluctuations, particularly in people with diabetes, as it is known to affect blood sugar levels.

In rare cases, it may also cause liver damage or increase the risk of bleeding due to its potential interactions with other medications or underlying health conditions.
