What is Combretum butyrosum?

Combretum butyrosum is a species of plant in the family Combretaceae, native to tropical Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that grows up to 15 meters in height, with a trunk diameter of up to 30 centimeters and a spreading canopy. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, measuring 6-12 centimeters in length and 2-4 centimeters in width, with a rounded base and a pointed apex. The flowers are small, yellowish-green, and arranged in axillary clusters, while the fruit is a hairy, brownish-red capsule, 10-15 millimeters in diameter, containing numerous seeds.

The plant has a spreading habit and is often found in deciduous woodlands and along rivers.

The main herbal preparations of Combretum butyrosum are infusion, combretum tea, tincture, decoction, and salve.

Combretum tea is a stronger decoction prepared by boiling the plant's leaves and flowers in water for 10-15 minutes. Tincture is a concentrated liquid extract of the plant's leaves and flowers, typically made by soaking them in a solvent like ethanol or glycerin.
