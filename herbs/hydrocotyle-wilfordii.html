What's the taxonomical classification of Hydrocotyle wilfordii?

Hydrocotyle wilfordii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Apiales and the family Araliaceae, eventually reaching its specific designation within the genus Hydrocotyle. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its unique species name, wilfordii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hydrocotyle wilfordii has small, kidney-shaped to orbicular leaves that are typically 5 to 15 millimeters in diameter and possess crenate or scalloped margins.

These leaves are arranged in a basal rosette and are connected to the stem by long, slender petioles. The plant features a creeping growth habit characterized by slender, stoloniferous stems that spread across the substrate. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced on upright stalks, though foliage is the most prominent morphological feature.

The overall texture of the plant is dense and low-growing, making it effective for ground cover.
