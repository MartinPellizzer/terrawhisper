Hydrocotyle wilfordii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hydrocotyle wilfordii has small, kidney-shaped to orbicular leaves that are typically 5 to 15 millimeters in diameter and possess crenate or scalloped margins. These leaves are arranged in a basal rosette and are connected to the stem by long, slender petioles. The plant features a creeping growth habit characterized by slender, stoloniferous stems that spread across the substrate. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced on upright stalks, though foliage is the most prominent morphological feature. The overall texture of the plant is dense and low-growing, making it effective for ground cover.
