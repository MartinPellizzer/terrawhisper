Hydrocotyle wilfordii

What's the taxonomical classification of Hydrocotyle wilfordii?

Hydrocotyle wilfordii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Apiales and the family Araliaceae, eventually reaching its specific designation within the genus Hydrocotyle. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its unique species name, wilfordii.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Hydrocotyle wilfordii contains high concentrations of triterpenoids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids that provide significant pharmacological activities. These bioactive compounds contribute to its documented anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and hepatoprotective effects. Specifically, the presence of certain triterpene saponins helps in modulating cellular responses to oxidative stress. Research also indicates that these chemical constituents play a role in promoting wound healing and protecting liver cells from toxin-induced damage. Such diverse chemical profiles make the plant a subject of interest for developing natural therapeutic agents.

What are the therapeutic actions of Hydrocotyle wilfordii?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat inflammatory conditions, skin ailments, and digestive disorders through its bioactive compounds. It is frequently applied topically to soothe skin irritations and promote the healing of minor wounds or ulcers. In traditional medicine, it is also utilized to alleviate internal discomfort related to the gastrointestinal tract. Some studies suggest its components may help manage fever and reduce systemic inflammation within the body. These diverse therapeutic properties make it a significant subject in herbal pharmacology research.
