Phyllanthodendron anthopotamicum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phyllanthodendron anthopotamicum has a woody, shrubby growth habit characterized by elongated, slender stems and small, lanceolate leaves arranged in a distichous pattern. The leaves are typically thin and possess entire margins with acute tips. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves, often appearing in clusters. The plant develops small, fleshy fruits that serve as a primary method for seed dispersal. Its overall structure is adapted for survival in moist, riparian environments.
