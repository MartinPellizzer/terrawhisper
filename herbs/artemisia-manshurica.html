What's the taxonomical classification of Artemisia manshurica?

Artemisia manshurica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant falls under the order Asterales and is a member of the family Asteraceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Artemisia and the species manshurica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Artemisia manshurica has perennial, rhizomatous stems that produce lanceolate to linear leaves with both upper and lower surfaces covered in fine, silvery-white hairs. The foliage typically exhibits a grayish-green hue due to this dense pubescence which provides a distinct texture. As the plant matures, it produces terminal panicles of small, inconspicuous flower heads that are characteristic of the Asteraceae family. The leaf margins are often irregularly serrated or lobed, contributing to the plant's complex silhouette. These structural features allow the species to thrive in its specific temperate habitats.
