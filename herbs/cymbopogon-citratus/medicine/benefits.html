10 Health Benefits Of Lemongrass

1. Reduces inflammation

Lemongrass reduces inflammation because it contains citral and geraniol, which are known to suppress the production of inflammatory cytokines in the body.

These cytokines are proteins that are released by immune cells, and they play a crucial role in the body's inflammatory response. However, when the body produces too much of these proteins, it can lead to chronic inflammation, which is associated with various health conditions, such as arthritis, asthma, and inflammatory bowel disease. Lemongrass has been shown to inhibit the production of these cytokines, thereby reducing inflammation and providing relief from these conditions.

Additionally, lemongrass has antioxidant properties that help to reduce oxidative stress and further contribute to its anti-inflammatory effects.

2. Alleviates pain

Lemongrass alleviates pain due to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant contains citral and geraniol, which are compounds known to reduce inflammation and swelling, thereby alleviating pain. Lemongrass oil, extracted from the leaves, has been traditionally used in aromatherapy and massage to soothe sore muscles and joints. Moreover, research suggests that lemongrass may inhibit the production of prostaglandins, chemicals that cause pain and inflammation in the body.

Therefore, incorporating lemongrass into one's diet or using it in topical applications may provide natural relief for various types of pain.

3. Improves oral health

Lemongrass improves oral health because of its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

The essential oil extracted from lemongrass contains citral and geraniol, which are known to inhibit the growth of harmful bacteria in the mouth. These bacteria are often responsible for causing tooth decay, gum diseases, and bad breath.

Additionally, lemongrass has been found to reduce inflammation in the gums, providing relief from pain and discomfort. Its fresh and lemony taste also makes it an effective natural mouthwash and breath freshener.

Regular use of lemongrass can help maintain good oral hygiene and promote overall oral health.

4. Boosts immune system

Lemongrass boosts the immune system due to its rich content of beneficial compounds.

It contains essential vitamins such as vitamin C, which is known to enhance the immune system by stimulating the production of white blood cells. Lemongrass also has antioxidant properties that protect the body from free radicals, which can cause cell damage and weakened immunity.

Furthermore, it has antibacterial and antifungal properties that help the body fight off infections and maintain a healthy immune system. The citral compound found in lemongrass has been shown to have anti-inflammatory effects, which can help reduce inflammation and strengthen the immune response.

Therefore, incorporating lemongrass into your diet can help boost your immune system and promote overall health.

5. Wards off insects

Lemongrass wards off insects because it contains citronellal, a natural oil that is a powerful insect repellent.

This oil interferes with the insects' ability to smell, making it difficult for them to locate their hosts. The strong citrus scent of lemongrass also deters insects, keeping them at bay. Additionally, lemongrass has been shown to repel mosquitoes, ticks, and other biting insects, making it a popular natural alternative to chemical-based repellents.

Not only does lemongrass provide a fragrant and attractive addition to gardens and landscapes, but it also serves as a valuable tool in combating pesky insects.

6. Reduces fever

Lemongrass reduces fever because it contains a compound called citral, which has been found to have fever-reducing properties.

This is achieved by activating the body's cool-down response, promoting perspiration, and helping to eliminate toxins from the body. Additionally, lemongrass has anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects that can help alleviate discomfort associated with feverish conditions. Its soothing aroma also has a calming effect on the mind, further contributing to overall well-being during times of illness.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment of fever-related ailments.

7. Stimulates digestion

Lemongrass stimulates digestion because it contains volatile oils that can help to enhance the secretion of digestive juices, bile, and acids in the stomach.

These oils can also help to relax the gastrointestinal muscles, allowing for the smooth movement of food through the digestive tract. Additionally, lemongrass has been shown to have carminative properties, which can help to reduce bloating, gas, and other uncomfortable symptoms of indigestion. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties may also help to prevent and treat digestive disorders such as gastritis, ulcers, and food poisoning.

Overall, lemongrass can help to promote a healthy digestive system and alleviate symptoms of digestive discomfort.

8. Regulates cholesterol

Lemongrass regulates cholesterol because it contains citral, a compound that has been shown to lower cholesterol levels.

Studies have found that lemongrass oil can help reduce cholesterol levels in the body, particularly in the liver, where most cholesterol is produced. The oil works by increasing the activity of enzymes that break down cholesterol, which helps to reduce the amount of cholesterol in the blood. Additionally, lemongrass has anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help to protect the cardiovascular system and reduce the risk of heart disease.

By incorporating lemongrass into your diet, either through cooking or as a tea, you can help to regulate your cholesterol levels and promote overall heart health.

9. Lowers blood pressure

Lemongrass lowers blood pressure because it contains citral, a compound that has been shown to relax and expand blood vessels.

This action allows for smoother blood flow, reducing the overall pressure on the arterial walls. Furthermore, lemongrass has diuretic properties, which help the body eliminate excess fluid and sodium, thereby reducing the volume of blood in the circulatory system and, consequently, lowering blood pressure.

Additionally, lemongrass has been found to possess a mild sedative effect, which can help alleviate stress and anxiety, both of which are factors that can contribute to high blood pressure.

10. Improves sleep quality

Lemongrass improves sleep quality because of its calming and relaxing properties.

It contains citral and linalool, which have been shown to produce mild sedative and antidepressant effects. These compounds help to reduce anxiety and stress, making it easier to fall asleep and stay asleep throughout the night.

Additionally, lemongrass has been found to promote better overall sleep quality, leaving individuals feeling more refreshed and rejuvenated upon waking. Its soothing aroma also helps to create a peaceful and relaxing environment, making it an ideal addition to a bedtime routine.

Overall, lemongrass is a natural and effective way to improve sleep quality and promote a good night's rest.
