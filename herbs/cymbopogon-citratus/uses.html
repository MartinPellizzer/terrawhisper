Lemon Grass (Cymbopogon citratus) Uses

Lemon grass is commonly used to treat respiratory tract infections, including coughs, bronchitis, and the common cold. It may help alleviate symptoms of asthma and reduce inflammation in the gastrointestinal tract. It also has antimicrobial properties that can address skin infections and bad breath, while its natural compounds may provide relief from insect bites.

Lemon Grass (Cymbopogon citratus) Monograph

1. Respiratory tract infections

Lemon grass has been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of respiratory tract infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce coughing, clear mucus, and soothe irritated airways, making it a valued herb in herbal medicine for respiratory health. Its essential oils, particularly citral, contribute to its effectiveness against respiratory pathogens.

2. Cough

Lemon grass is used to soothe coughs due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce throat irritation and loosen mucus, making it easier to expel. A tea made from fresh or dried lemon grass leaves can be prepared and consumed several times a day for relief. It is often combined with other herbs like ginger or honey to enhance its effectiveness.

3. Respiratory infections

Lemon grass has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that support respiratory health. It can help alleviate symptoms of colds, coughs, and bronchitis when used in teas or steam inhalations. Its essential oils may reduce mucus production and soothe irritated airways, making it a valuable herb in herbal medicine for respiratory infections.

4. Asthma

Lemon grass has been traditionally used to support respiratory health. Its essential oils contain compounds like myrcene and citral, which may help relax airway muscles and reduce inflammation. Some studies suggest it may ease asthma symptoms, though more research is needed. It is often used in aromatherapy or as a herbal tea.

5. Common cold

Lemon grass is commonly used in herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms of the common cold due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce fever, soothe sore throats, and ease nasal congestion. Often brewed as a tea, it supports immune function and can be a gentle remedy for mild cold symptoms.

6. Bronchitis

Lemon grass has been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of bronchitis due to its antispasmodic and expectorant properties. It helps reduce bronchial inflammation and loosen mucus, making it easier to cough up. Its essential oils, particularly citral, may help soothe respiratory irritation and support clearer breathing.

7. Skin infections

Lemon grass has antimicrobial properties that make it effective against skin infections. Its essential oils contain compounds like citral and geraniol, which inhibit the growth of bacteria and fungi. Topical applications can help reduce symptoms of conditions like athlete's foot and fungal infections. It is often used in natural remedies for its soothing and healing effects on the skin.

8. Insect bites

Lemon grass contains compounds with mild antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties that can help alleviate symptoms from insect bites. It may reduce swelling, itching, and irritation when applied topically. A diluted lemon grass infusion can be used as a compress or added to a bath for relief. Its fresh scent also has a calming effect, aiding in overall comfort.

9. Gastrointestinal inflammation

Lemon grass has anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce gastrointestinal inflammation. It soothes the lining of the stomach and intestines, alleviating symptoms like bloating and indigestion. Its compounds may inhibit inflammatory pathways, supporting digestive health. It is often used in herbal formulations for digestive disorders.

10. Bad breath

Lemon grass is used to freshen breath due to its antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce oral bacteria that cause bad odors. Chewing fresh lemon grass or using it in mouthwashes can provide a natural, refreshing effect. It is a common remedy in herbal medicine for maintaining oral hygiene.
