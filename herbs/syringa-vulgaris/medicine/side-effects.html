10 Possible Side Effects Of Syringa Vulgaris (Lilac)

Syringa vulgaris has some side effects when used improperly, such as causing allergic reactions, skin irritation, and gastrointestinal problems.

These side effects are often caused by consuming large quantities or prolonged use.

For instance, severe allergic reactions can lead to anaphylaxis, a life-threatening condition that can worsen daily life, making it difficult to breathe and perform routine tasks.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Syringa vulgaris if used imporperly.

1. Worsens insomnia

Syringa vulgaris causes allergic reactions due to its fragrant flowers and foliage containing allergenic compounds such as oleic acid and malic acid.

These chemicals can trigger an immune response in some individuals, leading to symptoms like skin irritation, respiratory issues, and even anaphylaxis.

The allergenic potential is heightened when the plant's pollen or other parts come into contact with mucous membranes or broken skin.

2. Worsens insomnia

Syringa vulgaris triggers respiratory issues due to its potent allergenic properties.

The plant's pollen and nectar contain specific proteins that bind to IgE antibodies in the blood, triggering an immune response in susceptible individuals.

This can lead to symptoms such as congestion, sneezing, coughing, and shortness of breath, as the body attempts to expel the foreign substance from the airways and lungs.

3. Worsens insomnia

Syringa vulgaris increases blood pressure due to its inherent properties.

The plant contains a compound called syringic acid, which is known to constrict blood vessels and increase heart rate. Additionally, the plant's extracts have been shown to stimulate the sympathetic nervous system, leading to an increase in blood pressure.

These effects can potentially lead to cardiovascular complications in individuals already experiencing hypertension.

Syringa vulgaris produces gastrointestinal upset.

The compound linarin, a glycoside found in the plant's flowers and leaves, is thought to contribute to this side effect. Additionally, the saponins present in lilac may also irritate the digestive system, leading to stomach discomfort, nausea, and diarrhea.

These chemical compounds can cause an imbalance of gut flora, disrupting normal digestion and leading to gastrointestinal distress.

Syringa vulgaris affects liver function due to its toxic compounds, such as flavonoids and alkaloids.

These bioactive molecules can cause changes in liver enzyme levels, potentially leading to impaired detoxification processes and altered metabolic pathways.

The exact mechanisms underlying this effect are not yet fully understood, but it is thought that the high concentration of these compounds in lilac extracts may contribute to their potential impact on liver function.

Syringa vulgaris disrupts thyroid hormone.

The essential oil extracted from its flowers contains a compound called linalool, which has been shown to interfere with the production of thyroid hormones. Additionally, the plant's flavonoids and phenolic acids may also contribute to this effect by altering the body's metabolism and potentially causing thyroid dysfunction.

This disruption can lead to a range of symptoms, including fatigue, weight gain, and mood changes.

Syringa vulgaris interacts with medications that are used to treat high blood pressure, as it can increase the risk of low blood pressure and dizziness.

This is because lilac contains compounds that can constrict blood vessels, reducing blood flow and causing a drop in blood pressure.

Additionally, lilac may also interact with sedatives and tranquilizers, increasing their effects and potentially leading to increased drowsiness or impaired cognitive function.

This fragrant oil is responsible for the plant's distinctive scent and has been found to have a calming effect on the nervous system, leading to feelings of relaxation and reduced anxiety.

As a result, individuals may experience drowsiness or fatigue when exposed to Syringa vulgaris, especially in large quantities or through prolonged exposure.

Syringa vulgaris enhances anxiety levels in some individuals due to its potent volatile oil composition.

The oil, which is released into the air when the plant's flowers are disturbed, contains linalool and benzaldehyde, two compounds known for their ability to stimulate the nervous system and increase alertness.

As a result, exposure to Syringa vulgaris may lead to feelings of restlessness, agitation, and heightened anxiety in some people.
