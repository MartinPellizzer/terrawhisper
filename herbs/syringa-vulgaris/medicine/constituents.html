10 Medicinal Constituents Of Syringa Vulgaris (Lilac)

Syringa vulgaris has active constituents such as syringine, syringaldehyde, and essential oils, particularly limonene and linalool.

These constituents are responsible for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects.

The lilac's constituents can help alleviate symptoms of anxiety and depression by promoting relaxation and reducing stress levels, thus improving the quality of life for individuals suffering from mental health conditions.

This article explains in details the 10 best active constituents of Syringa vulgaris.

1. Flavonoids

Syringa vulgaris flavonoids is a type of bioactive compound found in its flowers and leaves.

These flavonoids have been extensively studied for their potential health benefits, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties.

Quercetin, kaempferol, and isorhapontigenin are some of the major flavonoids identified in Syringa vulgaris, which may contribute to its traditional uses in folk medicine for treating various ailments.

2. Lignans

Syringa vulgaris lignans is a class of bioactive compounds found in its flowers, leaves, and stems.

These phenolic compounds have been extensively studied for their potential medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant activities.

The lignans in Syringa vulgaris have been shown to exhibit strong inhibitory effects against various enzymes and bacteria, making them a promising area of research for the development of novel therapeutic agents.

3. Phenolic acids

Syringa vulgaris phenolic acids is a group of compounds that have been identified in the plant's leaves and stems.

These phenolic acids are responsible for the antioxidant and antimicrobial properties of the lilac plant, and have been found to exhibit various biological activities including anti-inflammatory and antitumor effects.

4. Volatile oils

Syringa vulagris volatile oils is a mixture of aromatic compounds responsible for the characteristic fragrance and flavor of the plant.

These oils are secreted by small glands on the leaves and stems of the lilac tree, releasing a sweet and heady aroma that attracts pollinators and repels pests.

The volatile oils of lilac have been used in perfumes, fragrances, and herbal remedies for centuries.

5. Tannins

Syringa vulgaris tannins is a type of polyphenolic compound found in its flowers and leaves.

These compounds are responsible for the plant's bitter taste and astringent properties, which can be used to treat various health issues such as diarrhea, hemorrhoids, and mouth sores.

Tannins have also been shown to possess antimicrobial and antioxidant activities, making them valuable in traditional medicine.

6. Saponins

Syringa vulgaris saponins is a type of triterpenoid glycoside compound found in its leaves and flowers.

These saponins have been studied for their potential medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial activities.

They have been shown to possess cytotoxic effects against certain cancer cell lines and may also exhibit antibacterial and antiviral properties, making them a promising area of research in the development of new natural products.

7. Glycosides

Syringa vulgaris glycosides is a type of bioactive compound found in the flowers, leaves, and stems of this ornamental plant.

These glycosides have been shown to possess various biological activities, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties.

They are composed of a sugar molecule attached to a aglycone, which confers its pharmacological effects.

8. Terpenoids

Syringa vulgaris terpenoids is a complex mixture of organic compounds found in the flowers, leaves, and stems of this plant.

These terpenoids are responsible for the characteristic fragrance and flavor of lilac, and have been shown to possess antimicrobial, antifungal, and antioxidant properties.

They have also been used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects.

9. Alkaloids

Syringa vulgaris alkaloids is a group of bioactive compounds found in the plant's leaves and stems.

These alkaloids have been shown to possess various biological activities, including antimicrobial, antioxidant, and anti-inflammatory properties.

The most abundant alkaloid in lilac is syringine, which has been reported to exhibit antidiabetic and anticancer activities.

10. Anthraquinones

Syringa vulgaris anthraquinones is a type of bioactive compound that has been isolated from its flowers.

These compounds have been found to possess antioxidant, antimicrobial, and anti-inflammatory activities.

The anthraquinones present in S. vulgaris have been reported to inhibit the growth of various microorganisms, including bacteria and fungi, making it a potential source for the development of new medicines and cosmetics.
