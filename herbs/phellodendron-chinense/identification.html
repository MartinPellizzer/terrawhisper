Botanical Identification of Chinese Mahogany (Phellodendron chinense)

To identify Chinese Mahogany (Phellodendron chinense) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Chinese Mahogany (Phellodendron chinense) Monograph

Scientific Classification

Chinese Mahogany, or Phellodendron chinense, belongs to the plant kingdom, specifically within the flowering plants, eudicots, and the order Sapindales. It is part of the Rutaceae family, genus Phellodendron, and the species Phellodendron chinense. This classification helps in understanding its botanical relationships and medicinal properties.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Phellodendron chinense is the scientific name for Chinese Mahogany. It was first described by the botanist Blume in 1851. The species has several synonyms, including Phellodendron amurense var. chinense. It is also known by various common and regional names, reflecting its traditional use in herbal medicine across different cultures.

Morphological Characteristics

Phellodendron chinense is a deciduous tree with a straight trunk and a rounded crown. Its root system is taproot-based, providing strong anchorage. The stem is smooth and often fissured, with a pale yellowish-brown bark that exudes a strong odor when cut.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Phellodendron chinense can be distinguished by its thick, fissured bark that peels off in strips, its simple, alternate leaves with a yellowish-green underside, and its yellow flowers arranged in umbels. It is often confused with Phellodendron amurense, but the former has a more distinct bark texture and smaller, more clustered flowers.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Phellodendron chinense is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of China, India, and Malaysia. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils and is typically found at altitudes between 200 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Phellodendron chinense blooms in spring, typically between March and May. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early autumn, around August to September. For identification purposes, the best time to harvest is during the early spring when leaves are fresh and the bark is easily identifiable.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Phellodendron chinense belongs to the Rutaceae family, closely related to species like Phellodendron amurense and Phellodendron hexandrum. It shares key genus traits such as phellodendrin-containing bark and opposite leaves. Its phylogenetic placement reflects evolutionary ties within the Rutaceae, emphasizing its role in traditional herbal medicine.
