Red Top (Centaurium erythraea) Compounds

Red Top contains several primary compounds, including flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its antioxidant properties. Coumarin and glycosides may support liver function, while terpenoids and saponins have anti-inflammatory effects. Essential oils and alkaloids contribute to its stimulating properties, and anthocyanins provide vibrant color and additional health benefits.

Red Top (Centaurium erythraea) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoid is a key active compound in Red Top (Centaurium erythraea). It contributes to the plant's therapeutic properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Flavonoids support the plant's use in traditional herbal medicine for digestive and circulatory health.

Tannin in Red Top (Centaurium erythraea) contributes to its astringent properties, aiding in wound healing and reducing inflammation. It also supports digestive health by strengthening mucous membranes and may have antimicrobial effects, making it a key component in traditional herbal remedies.

Phenolic acid in Red Top (Centaurium erythraea) contributes to its antioxidant properties, supporting cellular health and reducing oxidative stress. This compound is part of the plant's complex phytochemical profile, which is studied for its potential therapeutic benefits in herbal medicine.

Coumarin is a key active compound in Red Top (Centaurium erythraea). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly in promoting circulation and supporting cardiovascular health. Alkaloid is a key active compound in Red Top (Centaurium erythraea).
