Small European Bugleweed (Lycopus Europaeus F. Pusillus)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Lycopus europaeus f. pusillus.

Small European bugleweed, scientifically known as Lycopus europaeus f. pusillus, is a lesser-known medicinal herb prized for its adaptogenic properties and historical use in traditional medicine. This plant is primarily valued for its potential to support nervous system health, reduce anxiety, and promote relaxation, making it a popular choice in herbal remedies for stress-related conditions. Historically, it has been used in European folk medicine to treat ailments such as insomnia, digestive issues, and respiratory complaints, with its calming effects attributed to compounds like rosmarinic acid and flavonoids. In modern wellness practices, it is often incorporated into herbal teas, supplements, and aromatherapy blends to aid in stress management and emotional balance. Its unique feature is its subtle, minty aroma and mild flavor, which distinguish it from other members of the Lycopus genus, and its historical use dates back to ancient herbal texts from the 16th century.
