What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tadehagi triquetrum has woody stems characterized by a distinct three-angled or triangular cross-section, simple leaves arranged oppositely, and small, tubular, pale pink to white flowers. Its inflorescences consist of small, clustered flowers that emerge from the leaf axils or terminal ends of the branches. The plant's structural integrity is supported by its upright, shrubby growth habit and sturdy, triangular branching pattern.
