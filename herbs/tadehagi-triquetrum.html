What's the taxonomical classification of Tadehagi triquetrum?

Tadehagi triquetrum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls into the subclass Magnoliidae and is further categorized under the order Fabales. This plant is a member of the family Fabaceae, situated within the genus Tadehagi, and is specifically identified by its species name, triquetrum.

Its inflorescences consist of small, clustered flowers that emerge from the leaf axils or terminal ends of the branches. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compound tadehaginoside along with a specific group of triterpenoids known as triquetrumones A (1), triquetrumones B (2), triquetrumones C (3), and (R)-triquetrumone D (4). These bioactive constituents are primarily isolated from the chemical profile of Tadehagi triquetrum. Researchers study these molecules to understand their potential pharmacological properties and therapeutic applications. The structural diversity of these compounds provides a foundation for further medicinal chemistry investigations. Detailed analysis of these specific metabolites helps clarify the plant's overall biological activity.

According to a study published by "Natural product research", Tadehagi triquetrum contains a phenylpropanoid glycoside known as Tadehaginoside (TS). This compound exhibits multiple biological activities and functions as an effective regulator of lipogenesis and glucose consumption. TS inhibits lipid accumulation in a dose-dependent manner by downregulating lipogenic genes including SREBP-1a, SREBP-2, and their downstream targets FAS, ACC, and HMGR. Additionally, the compound upregulates the lipolytic gene PPARα and stimulates glucose consumption in HepG2 cells. The study also noted that TS increases glucose uptake by C2C12 myotubes through the upregulation of PPARγ.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating anthelmintic issues, infectious diseases, diabetic complications, diabetic kidney disease, and managing conditions through its hypoglycemic properties. Its bioactive compounds serve as a natural defense against various internal parasites and harmful pathogens. The plant is particularly noted for its ability to regulate blood sugar levels in patients. By utilizing these hypoglycemic properties, it helps mitigate the progression of severe diabetic complications. Furthermore, its medicinal applications extend to protecting renal functions in cases of diabetic kidney disease.

According to a study published by "Journal of agricultural and food chemistry", the ethanol extract of Tadehagi triquetrum was investigated for its anthelminthic bioactive compounds. This investigation resulted in the isolation of 4 new prenylated isoflavonoids, which include triquetrumones A (1), B (2), and C (3), and (R)-triquetrumone D (4). Additionally, the study identified 16 known compounds within the plant, ranging from cyclokievitone (5) to docosanoic acid (20). Among these, compounds 1-3 displayed mild anthelminthic bioactivity. Furthermore, compound 3 showed a significant binding ability to the estrogen receptor.
