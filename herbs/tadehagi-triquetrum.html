Tadehagi triquetrum

What's the taxonomical classification of Tadehagi triquetrum?

Tadehagi triquetrum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls into the subclass Magnoliidae and is further categorized under the order Fabales. This plant is a member of the family Fabaceae, situated within the genus Tadehagi, and is specifically identified by its species name, triquetrum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tadehagi triquetrum has woody stems characterized by a distinct three-angled or triangular cross-section, simple leaves arranged oppositely, and small, tubular, pale pink to white flowers. The leaves are typically elliptic to lanceolate in shape with entire or slightly serrated margins and acute tips. Its inflorescences consist of small, clustered flowers that emerge from the leaf axils or terminal ends of the branches. The plant's structural integrity is supported by its upright, shrubby growth habit and sturdy, triangular branching pattern. These morphological features distinguish it from other members of the Rubiaceae family.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds kaempferol, quercitrin, hesperidin, naringin, and ursolic acid. These specific phytochemicals contribute to the plant's diverse therapeutic potential and biological activity. Kaempferol and quercitrin are known flavonoids that often provide antioxidant and anti-inflammatory benefits. The flavanones hesperidin and naringin are frequently associated with cardiovascular health and metabolic support. Additionally, the triterpenoid ursolic acid plays a significant role in various cellular protective processes. Together, these bioactive substances form a complex chemical profile beneficial to human health.

As discussed in a study, Tadehagi triquetrum aqueous extract contains major bioactive compounds including Hesperiidin, Quercitrin, and Carnosol. These identified compounds possess a high binding affinity for PTEN. To assess its effects, the extract was administered to db/db mice at doses of 0.1, 0.2, and 0.4 g/kg for a duration of 10 weeks. The study utilized UPLC-MS/MS analysis and molecular docking to identify these specific active components. These findings highlight the plant's potential for managing diabetic kidney disease through its medicinal properties.
