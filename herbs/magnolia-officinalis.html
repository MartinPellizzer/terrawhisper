Chinese Magnolia (Magnolia officinalis)

Chinese Magnolia (Magnolia officinalis) is a member of the Magnoliaceae family, native to China, Southeast Asia, and East Asia. Traditionally, its flowers, bark, and resin have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, tonic, and antispasmodic actions, and has a long history of use in traditional chinese medicine, japanese kampo medicine, and korean traditional medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 5 to 12 meters.
Leaves
Broad leaves with upper surface pale green and lower surface darker green, featuring prominent stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Flowers are white to pale pink, arranged in solitary blooms, exhibit actinomorphic symmetry, and possess nine sepals and nine petals with a distinct ovate staminode.
Stems
Elongated, woody, erect stems with opposite branching, smooth bark, and collateral vascular bundles arranged in a distinct ring.
