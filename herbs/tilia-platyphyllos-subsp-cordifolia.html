Large-Leaved Lime (Tilia Platyphyllos Subsp. Cordifolia)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Tilia platyphyllos subsp. cordifolia.

Large-leaved lime, scientifically known as Tilia platyphyllos subsp. cordifolia, is a lesser-known medicinal herb native to parts of Europe and Asia, valued for its therapeutic properties. This plant, often referred to as the heart-leaf lime, is traditionally used as an adaptogen to help the body manage stress and promote overall well-being. Its primary benefits include calming effects on the nervous system, reduction of anxiety, and support for respiratory health due to its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. In traditional medicine, it has been used to treat insomnia, headaches, and digestive issues, while in modern wellness practices, it is incorporated into herbal teas and supplements for stress relief and cognitive enhancement. Unique to this subspecies is its distinctive, slightly citrusy flavor and the presence of bioactive compounds like tiliroside, which contribute to its medicinal profile and historical significance in herbal remedies.
