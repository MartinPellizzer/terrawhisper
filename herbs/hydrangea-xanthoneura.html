Hydrangea xanthoneura

What's the taxonomical classification of Hydrangea xanthoneura?

Hydrangea xanthoneura belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Cornales. This plant is a member of the family Hydrangeaceae and is situated within the genus Hydrangea, specifically identified by its species name, xanthoneura.

      What are the herbal preparations of Hydrangea xanthoneura?

      Hydrangea xanthoneura is prepared primarily as a decoction, a concentrated liquid extract made by boiling the plant material in water to release its medicinal compounds. This method is favored because the woody or fibrous structures of the plant require prolonged heat to ensure the active constituents are fully integrated into the solvent. In some traditional practices, the dried leaves or stems may also be infused into oils to create topical applications for external use.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Hydrangea xanthoneura can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential poisoning if any part of the plant is ingested. The leaves and stems contain cyanogenic glycosides which release toxic hydrogen cyanide when chewed or swallowed. Contact with the plant may lead to localized dermatitis or rashes in sensitive individuals. If consumed, symptoms often include nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and dizziness. Serious cases of ingestion require immediate medical attention to address the effects of cyanide toxicity.
