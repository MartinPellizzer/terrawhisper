Hydrangea xanthoneura

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hydrangea xanthoneura has woody, spreading stems that support large, elliptical leaves characterized by prominent, bright yellow veins and serrated margins. The foliage typically exhibits a deep green color on the upper surface, which contrasts sharply with the golden-yellow venation. These leaves are arranged oppositely along the branches, creating a dense and textured canopy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge in clusters from the leaf axils. Its overall growth habit is shrubby, often forming a low-growing mound in its natural habitat.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Hydrangea xanthoneura can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential poisoning if any part of the plant is ingested. The leaves and stems contain cyanogenic glycosides which release toxic hydrogen cyanide when chewed or swallowed. Contact with the plant may lead to localized dermatitis or rashes in sensitive individuals. If consumed, symptoms often include nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and dizziness. Serious cases of ingestion require immediate medical attention to address the effects of cyanide toxicity.
