What is Sairocarpus nuttallianus?

Sairocarpus nuttallianus is an annual herbaceous plant that belongs to the family Plantaginaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate, and usually sessile leaves, which are lanceolate to elliptical in shape and range in length from 10-30 mm. The leaves are glabrous or pubescent with a cordate to cuneate base and a cuneate to acute apex. The plant produces axillary cymes of small, blue to purple, bilabiate flowers, which are 10-20 mm in length and are typically glabrous or pubescent.

Sairocarpus nuttallianus is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.
