Indigofera suffruticosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Indigofera suffruticosa?

Indigofera suffruticosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic descent, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is a member of the order Fabales and falls under the family Fabaceae. Within this family, it is categorized under the genus Indigofera, specifically identified by its species name, suffruticosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Indigofera suffruticosa has a perennial shrubby habit characterized by erect, woody stems that typically reach a height of one meter and feature pinnate leaves with multiple small, oblong leaflets. The foliage is arranged alternately along the branches, presenting a bright green color and a fine, feathery texture. Small, pea-like flowers emerge in axillary racemes, displaying shades of pale yellow or cream. These blossoms eventually give rise to small, linear pods that contain several tiny seeds. The overall structure of the plant is compact and bushy, suitable for various tropical environments.

What are the pharmacological activities of Indigofera suffruticosa?

This plant has analgesic, anti-inflammatory, anti-nociceptive, antibacterial, and antimicrobial pharmacological activities. Its ability to reduce pain and mitigate the sensation of injury makes it an effective agent for managing discomfort. The chemical constituents within the species also work to suppress inflammation by reducing swelling and redness in biological tissues. Furthermore, the plant demonstrates potent antimicrobial properties that allow it to inhibit the growth of various pathogens. These antibacterial actions provide a natural defense mechanism against several types of harmful bacteria.

As discussed in a study, Indigofera suffruticosa leaves contain 33.9 mg QE/g of flavonoids and acid gallic. The aqueous extract has an LD50 of 3807.88 mg/kg in mice. When tested on carrageenan-induced paw edema, doses of 20 and 100 mg/kg reduced edema by 83.93%. In the writhing test, the extract inhibited the number of contortions by 72.60%. At a concentration of 0.5 mg/mL, it showed inhibition of protein denaturation, and a dose of 100 mg/kg significantly reduced fever.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Research continues to explore how these specific chemical profiles can be utilized in modern pharmacology.

As discussed in a study, phytochemical analysis of Indigofera suffruticosa leaf aqueous extract revealed the presence of flavonoids and acid gallic in a quantity of 33.9 mg QE/g. Acute toxicity evaluation of the extract resulted in a LD50 of 3807.88 mg/kg. When tested for anti-inflammatory activity, doses of 20 and 100 mg/kg reduced edema in 83.93% during the carrageenan-induced paw edema test. At a concentration of 0.5 mg/mL, the extract showed inhibition of protein denaturation similar to indomethacin. Additionally, the extract inhibited the number of contortions in the writhing test by 72.60%. Finally, the concentration of 100 mg/kg was found to reduce fever significantly.
