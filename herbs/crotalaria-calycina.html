Crotalaria calycina

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Crotalaria calycina has an erect, herbaceous growth habit characterized by slender, sometimes pubescent stems and pinnately compound leaves with several pairs of oblong leaflets. The foliage typically displays a light green color and serves as a structural base for its reproductive organs. Its most distinguishing morphological feature is the presence of large, showy calyx lobes that are significantly longer than the corolla. The flowers are bright yellow and possess a typical papilionaceous structure common to the Fabaceae family. Following pollination, the plant produces distinctive, inflated, or somewhat flattened legume pods that contain the seeds.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Crotalaria calycina can cause severe liver damage and toxicity due to the presence of pyrrolizidine alkaloids within its tissues. These chemical compounds are known to be hepatotoxic, meaning they target and destroy liver cells upon ingestion. If consumed in sufficient quantities, the plant may lead to chronic liver failure or even death in animals and humans. The toxins can also cause gastrointestinal distress and systemic complications as the body attempts to process the alkaloids. Because these substances are difficult to detect without laboratory testing, accidental ingestion poses a significant health risk.
