Crotalaria calycina

What's the taxonomical classification of Crotalaria calycina?

Crotalaria calycina belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Fabales. It is a member of the family Fabaceae and is specifically identified within the genus Crotalaria, with its unique biological designation being the species calycina.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Crotalaria calycina has an erect, herbaceous growth habit characterized by slender, sometimes pubescent stems and pinnately compound leaves with several pairs of oblong leaflets. The foliage typically displays a light green color and serves as a structural base for its reproductive organs. Its most distinguishing morphological feature is the presence of large, showy calyx lobes that are significantly longer than the corolla. The flowers are bright yellow and possess a typical papilionaceous structure common to the Fabaceae family. Following pollination, the plant produces distinctive, inflated, or somewhat flattened legume pods that contain the seeds.

What are the pharmacological activities of Crotalaria calycina?

This plant has significant pharmacological activities including analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties that can be utilized for pain management and swelling reduction. Research indicates that extracts from Crotalaria calycina effectively alleviate pain by interacting with specific pain pathways in the body. Its anti-inflammatory effects are demonstrated through the reduction of inflammatory mediators that cause tissue swelling and discomfort. These bioactive compounds make the species a subject of interest for developing new therapeutic agents. Further studies continue to explore how these medicinal properties can be standardized for clinical applications.

According to a study published by "Heliyon", the methanol extract of Crotalaria calycina stem demonstrates significant analgesic and anti-inflammatory potential. HPLC-DAD analysis identified 8 polyphenolic compounds, with (-) epicatechin being the most abundant at 87.15 ± 0.24 mg/100 g dry extract. In vivo testing showed that a 400 mg/kg dose significantly inhibited the writhing response (P < 0.01) and achieved a 70.67% inhibition of licking in the late part of the test. This same dose produced a 74.71% percent of maximal effect in the hot plate assay and a 68.00% percent of edema inhibition at the fourth hour. Molecular docking and dynamics simulations of (-) epicatechin, caffeic acid, and kaempferol showed interactions with cyclooxygenase-2 similar to celecoxib.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause severe poisoning and death in livestock due to the presence of pyrrolizidine alkaloids which induce irreversible liver damage. Ingestion of these toxic compounds leads to a condition known as hepatic veno-occlusive disease, where small veins in the liver become obstructed. Affected animals often exhibit progressive weight loss, jaundice, and abdominal swelling as the liver fails to function. Because these alkaloids are cumulative, even small amounts consumed over time can lead to fatal outcomes. Constant monitoring of grazing areas is necessary to prevent accidental poisoning of cattle and horses.
