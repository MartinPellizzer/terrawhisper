Crotalaria calycina

What's the taxonomical classification of Crotalaria calycina?

Crotalaria calycina belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Fabales. It is a member of the family Fabaceae and is specifically identified within the genus Crotalaria, with its unique biological designation being the species calycina.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Crotalaria calycina contains various bioactive alkaloids, flavonoids, and terpenoids that serve as its primary medicinal compounds. These secondary metabolites are known to exhibit significant pharmacological activities, including antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Specific alkaloids within the genus are often studied for their potential neuroprotective and antimicrobial effects. The presence of flavonoids contributes to the plant's ability to scavenge free radicals in biological systems. Researchers continue to investigate how these chemical constituents can be utilized in developing new therapeutic agents.

What are the therapeutic actions of Crotalaria calycina?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, sores, and various types of inflammation. Local healers often prepare extracts or poultices from its leaves and roots to apply directly to affected areas. These applications help to soothe irritation and promote the healing of external lesions. Some traditional practices also involve using the plant to address internal ailments or digestive discomfort. Its medicinal properties are primarily focused on its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects.

What are the herbal preparations of Crotalaria calycina?

Crotalaria calycina is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste using the plant's leaves and flowers. These liquid preparations are frequently ingested to address internal ailments or systemic imbalances. When used for external issues, the crushed plant material is applied directly to the skin as a poultice. Some traditional methods also involve extracting the plant's essence through oils for medicinal massage. These diverse forms allow for both oral consumption and localized skin treatment.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Crotalaria calycina can cause severe liver damage and toxicity due to the presence of pyrrolizidine alkaloids within its tissues. These chemical compounds are known to be hepatotoxic, meaning they target and destroy liver cells upon ingestion. If consumed in sufficient quantities, the plant may lead to chronic liver failure or even death in animals and humans. The toxins can also cause gastrointestinal distress and systemic complications as the body attempts to process the alkaloids. Because these substances are difficult to detect without laboratory testing, accidental ingestion poses a significant health risk.
