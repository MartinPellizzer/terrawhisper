What's the taxonomical classification of Daphniphyllum calycinum?

Daphniphyllum calycinum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Saxifragales and is a member of the family Daphniphyllaceae. Finally, its taxonomic hierarchy is completed by its placement in the genus Daphniphyllum, identifying its specific species as calycinum.

What health problems are treated using this plant?

This plant is used for treating viral infections, inflammation, and various skin conditions. Researchers have investigated its potential effectiveness against Enterovirus 71 to mitigate the severity of related illnesses. It also plays a role in traditional medicine for managing fever and respiratory issues. The bioactive compounds within the plant help reduce swelling and soothe irritation in the body. These medicinal properties make it a subject of interest for developing new antiviral treatments.

As discussed in a study, three rare cyano-containing alkaloids named cyanodaphcalycines A-C (1-3) featuring an unusual 5/6/6/7/5/5-hexacyclic scaffold and an undescribed daphmanidin A-type alkaloid, daphmanidin G (4), were isolated from the leaves and stems of Daphniphyllum calycinum Benth. Along with these, three known Daphniphyllum alkaloids (5-7) were also isolated from the plant. In biological evaluations against EV71 in RD cells, compounds 3 and 4 exhibited potent antiviral effects. Compound 3 showed an EC50 value of 3.78 ± 0.23 μg/mL, while compound 4 demonstrated an EC50 value of 6.87 ± 0.30 μg/mL. These results showed superior efficacy compared to ribavirin, which had an EC50 of 65.77 ± 0.11 μg/mL. This study represents the first report of Daphniphyllum alkaloids acting as potent inhibitors of EV71.
