Daphniphyllum calycinum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Daphniphyllum calycinum has opposite, elliptical to oblong leaves that measure approximately 5 to 15 centimeters in length with entire margins and smooth surfaces. The plant typically exhibits a woody, shrubby growth habit with slender branches. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are characterized by prominent, petal-like sepals that give the species its specific name. The reproductive structures often appear in small clusters along the stem or at the branch tips. These morphological features distinguish it from other members of the Thymelaeaceae family.
