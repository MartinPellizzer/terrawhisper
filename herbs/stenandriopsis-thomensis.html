What is Stenandriopsis thomensis?

Stenandriopsis thomensis is a plant species endemic to São Tomé and Principe, an archipelago off the coast of West Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae, a group of flowering plants characterized by their showy inflorescences and often fragrant flowers. Stenandriopsis thomensis is a shrub or small tree with a height of up to 10 meters, featuring simple, alternate, and elliptical-shaped leaves that are 10-25 centimeters long and 4-10 centimeters wide. The plant's flowers are white or greenish, borne in axillary inflorescences, and have five-lobed calyces and corollas.

The species was described as new to science in 2010 and is considered a rare and endemic species to the São Tomé and Principe islands.
