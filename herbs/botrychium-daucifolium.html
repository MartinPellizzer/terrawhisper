Botrychium daucifolium

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Botrychium daucifolium has a dimorphic leaf structure consisting of a sterile basal portion with multiple pinnately dissected segments and a fertile portion bearing a single, unbranched spike of small, green, oblong flowers. The sterile leaves are typically divided into several linear to lanceolate segments that resemble the foliage of a carrot. This plant arises from a creeping rhizome and produces a single, upright fertile spike that sits atop a long, slender stalk. The individual flowers are arranged alternately along the upper part of this fertile stem. Each flower is inconspicuous and lacks colorful petals, relying on its simple structure for reproduction.
