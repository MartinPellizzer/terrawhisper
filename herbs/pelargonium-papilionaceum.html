What is Pelargonium papilionaceum?

Pelargonium papilionaceum is a succulent, perennial herb in the family Geraniaceae.

It has palmate leaves with 5-7 lobes, and the flowers are white with a red and purple pattern, borne in a terminal inflorescence. The leaves are glandular-dotted and the stems are woody, growing up to 1 meter in height. The flowers are pollinated by insects and are in bloom from March to September.

This plant is endemic to the southwestern region of the Western Cape Province in South Africa.

What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

The main herbal preparations of Pelargonium papilionaceum are tincture, tea, salve, infusion, and pelargonium tea, which are derived from its leaves and flowers.

Tea is a more diluted preparation, prepared by steeping the leaves in hot water, often used for its soothing properties and as a digestive aid. Salve is a topical preparation, usually made by infusing the plant material in a carrier oil, and used to treat skin conditions such as wounds and inflammation.

Infusion, also known as pelargonium tea, is a more potent version of the tea, typically made by steeping the leaves in cold water, which is often used to treat respiratory issues and as a natural anti-inflammatory agent.
