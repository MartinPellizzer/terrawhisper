What is Chrysopogon pallidus?

Chrysopogon pallidus is a perennial grass native to Australia, belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by a slender culm, typically 30-60 cm tall, with a smooth and glabrous surface, and a nodding panicle of greenish or purplish spikelets, 10-20 cm long. The leaf sheaths are tubular, and the leaf blades are flat, linear, and 10-30 cm long, with a smooth surface and a prominent midrib. The spikelets are 5-6 mm long, with two fertile florets, and the flowers are dioecious.

The plant typically grows in dry to moist soils, often in open woodland or grassy areas.
