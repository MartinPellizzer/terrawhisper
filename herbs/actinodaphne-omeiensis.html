Actinodaphne omeiensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Actinodaphne omeiensis?

Actinodaphne omeiensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is placed in the subclass Magnoliidae, which leads into its classification under the order Laurales. The plant is a member of the family Lauraceae, falling within the genus Actinodaphne, and is specifically identified by its species name, omeiensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Actinodaphne omeiensis has woody, evergreen leaves that are typically elliptic to ovate in shape, measuring approximately 5 to 12 centimeters in length with entire margins and a coriaceous texture.

The leaf arrangement is alternate along the branches, featuring a dark green upper surface and a slightly paler underside. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves, often appearing in small clusters or singly. The plant develops small, fleshy drupes that serve as the fruit following successful pollination.

Its branching habit contributes to a compact, shrub-like growth form characteristic of its high-altitude habitat.
