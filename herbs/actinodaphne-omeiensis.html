What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Actinodaphne omeiensis has woody, evergreen leaves that are typically elliptic to ovate in shape, measuring approximately 5 to 12 centimeters in length with entire margins and a coriaceous texture. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves, often appearing in small clusters or singly. The plant develops small, fleshy drupes that serve as the fruit following successful pollination.
