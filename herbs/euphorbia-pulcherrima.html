What to know about Euphorbia pulcherrima (poinsettia) before using it medicinally

Poinsettia, scientifically known as Euphorbia pulcherrima, is a plant that has gained widespread recognition for its remarkable health properties, which have been shown to improve cardiovascular and respiratory health.

In addition to its medicinal benefits, poinsettia is also a popular ingredient in many traditional cuisines, particularly in Latin American dishes where it is used as a leafy green in salads, soups, and stews. Horticulturally, poinsettia is prized for its vibrant red flowers and striking foliage, making it a staple in many holiday decorations and arrangements. From a botanical standpoint, poinsettia belongs to the spurge family (Euphorbiaceae), characterized by its milky sap and distinctive leaf structure.

With its rich history dating back to pre-Columbian times, poinsettia has played a significant role in various cultures and traditions around the world, including its association with the Virgin of Guadalupe in Mexican folklore.
