Lysimachia chekiangensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lysimachia chekiangenensis has erect, perennial stems that feature lanceolate to elliptic leaves arranged in an opposite pattern along the length of the plant. The foliage typically presents a smooth texture with entire margins and acute or obtuse tips. Its reproductive structures consist of small, yellow, star-shaped flowers that emerge from the leaf axils or terminal positions. These blossoms possess five distinct petals and are supported by short pedicels. The overall structure of the plant is characterized by its herbaceous habit and upright growth form.
