Alpinia conchigera

What's the taxonomical classification of Alpinia conchigera?

Alpinia conchigera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it in the order Zingiberales. As a member of the family Zingiberaceae, this plant is categorized under the genus Alpinia, with its specific identity defined by the species name conchigera.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Alpinia conchigera has large, oblong-lanceolate leaves that grow from thick, fleshy rhizomes and produce upright, terminal inflorescences with cream-colored bracts. The plant typically reaches a height of one to two meters, forming dense clumps of lush green foliage. Its flowers emerge from colorful, protective bracts that often display shades of pale pink or white. The inflorescence structure is characterized by a spike-like arrangement where individual florets are nestled within the bracts. These botanical features are characteristic of the Zingiberaceae family, emphasizing its tropical herbaceous nature.

What are the pharmacological activities of Alpinia conchigera?

This plant has anti-cancer, anti-inflammatory, anti-neoplastic, anti-tumor, and antimicrobial pharmacological activities. Research indicates that its bioactive compounds can effectively inhibit the growth and spread of malignant cells. These medicinal properties also extend to fighting various infectious pathogens through potent antimicrobial actions. Additionally, the plant demonstrates significant capacity to reduce inflammation and suppress the development of tumors within biological systems. These diverse therapeutic effects make it a subject of intense pharmacological interest.

As discussed in a study, the Malaysian ethno-medicinal plant Alpinia conchigera Griff (Zingiberaceae) provides 1'-S-1'-acetoxychavicol acetate (ACA) isolated from its rhizomes. This compound induces apoptosis-mediated cell death in tumour cells via dysregulation of the NF-κB pathway. While ACA shows anti-cancer potential, it possesses clinical development drawbacks including poor in vivo solubility and depreciation of biological activity upon exposure to an aqueous environment. Furthermore, the molecule is characterized by non-specific targeting of tumour cells.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds galanganol A, galanganol B, phloroglucinol, vanillin, and konchignan A. These specific phytochemicals contribute to the plant's diverse pharmacological profile and biological activities. Scientists study these substances to understand their potential roles in treating various health conditions. The presence of these unique molecules distinguishes the plant's chemical composition from other similar species. Researchers continue to investigate how these individual elements interact within a therapeutic context.

As discussed in a study, five new 8-9' linked neolignans conctegeranals A-E (1-5) and three known compounds galanganal (6), galanganol A (7), and B (8) were isolated from the whole plant of Alpinia conchigera. These 8 compounds were tested for cytotoxicity against two cancer cell lines, A549 and Hela. Compounds 4, 5, 7, and 8 exhibited cytotoxicity against A549 with IC50 values of 12.36, 9.72, 10.26, and 13.05 μg/ml, respectively. Additionally, compounds 1-8 showed cytotoxicity against Hela with IC50 values ranging from 1.53 to 5.29 μg/ml.
