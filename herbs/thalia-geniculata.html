Thalia geniculata

What's the taxonomical classification of Thalia geniculata?

Thalia geniculaata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Zingiberales. This plant is a member of the family Marantaceae, which places it within the genus Thalia, ultimately identifying it at the species level as Thalia geniaculata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Thalia geniculaata has thick, succulent, lanceolate leaves that grow from a rhizomatous base and emerge on long, sturdy petioles. The leaf blades are typically bright green and can reach significant lengths depending on the moisture levels of the habitat. Its most distinctive feature is the geniculate or knee-like bending of the flowering stalks as they rise above the foliage. These stalks produce clusters of small, white or pale flowers that bloom sequentially along the upper portion of the stem. The plant often develops a robust root system of fleshy rhizomes that allow it to thrive in wetland environments.
