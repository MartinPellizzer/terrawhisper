10 Possible Side Effects Of Polygonum Multiflorum (Fo-Ti)

Polygonum multiflorum has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal upset, diarrhea, and stomach discomfort.

These side effects are often caused by taking the herb in large doses or combining it with other medications.

If left untreated, these side effects can worsen, leading to dehydration, electrolyte imbalances, and malnutrition, ultimately affecting daily life, work performance, and overall health.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Polygonum multiflorum if used imporperly.

1. Enhances effects of sedatives

Polygonum multiflorum causes allergic reactions in some individuals due to the presence of allergenic proteins and flavonoids.

The plant's high concentration of these compounds can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms such as skin rash, itching, and hives. In rare cases, anaphylaxis may occur, requiring immediate attention.

The severity of the reaction often depends on individual sensitivities and the method of preparation or processing used when consuming Polygonum multiflorum.

Polygonum multiflorum increases blood pressure due to its ability to stimulate the heart and nervous system.

The herb contains a compound called resveratrol, which can cause blood vessels to constrict and increase cardiac output, leading to a rise in blood pressure. Additionally, Polygonum multiflorum may also increase the production of adrenaline, a hormone that further contributes to increased blood pressure.

As a result, individuals with pre-existing hypertension or cardiovascular conditions may experience an exacerbation of their symptoms when using this herb.
