Botanical Identification of Chinese Knotweed (Polygonum multiflorum)

To identify Chinese Knotweed (Polygonum multiflorum) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Chinese Knotweed (Polygonum multiflorum) Monograph

Scientific Classification

Chinese Knotweed, or Polygonum multiflorum, belongs to the plant kingdom, specifically the division of seed plants. It is classified under the class Caryophyllopsida, order Polygonales, family Polygonaceae, genus Polygonum, and species Polygonum multiflorum. This classification reflects its botanical relationships and characteristics within the plant kingdom.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Polygonum multiflorum is the scientific name for Chinese Knotweed. It was first described by Linnaeus in 1793. This name is widely accepted in botanical literature. It is also known by several synonyms, common names, and regional variations, reflecting its historical and cultural significance in herbal medicine.

Morphological Characteristics

Chinese Knotweed is a perennial herb with a thick, fleshy root system. It has erect, branched stems that are hollow and slightly swollen at the nodes. The leaves are alternate, broadly ovate, and have a waxy surface. The inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule containing three tiny, dark brown seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Chinese Knotweed can be distinguished by its distinctive reddish-brown stems with prominent nodes, heart-shaped leaves with toothed margins, and small, clustered pinkish flowers. It also has a unique, strong, and slightly sweet aroma. These features help differentiate it from similar Polygonum species.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Chinese Knotweed is native to East Asia, particularly China, Korea, and Japan. It thrives in temperate climates with mild winters and warm summers. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils and is commonly found at elevations between 500 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Chinese Knotweed typically blooms from July to September, producing small, pale pink to red flowers. Seed formation occurs from August to October. For identification purposes, the plant is harvested in late autumn, around November, when the roots are mature and most potent.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Chinese Knotweed (Polygonum multiflorum) is closely related to species within the genus Polygonum. It shares genetic and morphological traits with other members of the Polygonaceae family, particularly those with similar leaf structures and flowering patterns. Its phylogenetic position highlights its evolutionary ties to other knotweed and buckwheat relatives.
