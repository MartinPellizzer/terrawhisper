Salvia kiaometiensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Salvia kiaometiensis?

Salvia kiaometiensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized under the order Lamiales. This plant is a member of the family Lamiaceae, which falls under the genus Salvia, and is specifically identified by the species name kiaometiensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Salviva kiaometiensis has an erect, perennial herb structure characterized by quadrangular stems and opposite, petiolate leaves that are typically ovate or lanceolate with serrated margins. The foliage often exhibits a pubescent texture, providing a soft feel to the leaf surfaces. Its inflorescence consists of verticillasters arranged in terminal spikes, which bear tubular, bilabiate flowers. These flowers are typically purple or light violet and emerge from protective bracts that may vary in shape and size. The reproductive organs are tucked within the corolla, following the classic structure of the Lamiaceae family.
