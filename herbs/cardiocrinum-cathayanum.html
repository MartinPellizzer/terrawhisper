Cardiocrinum cathayanum

What's the taxonomical classification of Cardiocrinum cathayanum?

Cardiocrinum cathayanum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is positioned in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Liliales. This plant is a member of the family Liliaceae, specifically categorized within the genus Cardiocrinum, with the specific species designation of cathayanum.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Cardiocrinum cathayanum contains a variety of potent cardiac glycosides, specifically lycorine, cardiochristine, and various related alkaloids. These bioactive compounds are primarily responsible for the plant's significant biological activities, including its ability to influence heart muscle contraction. Research indicates that these substances possess anti-inflammatory and anti-tumor properties that are being studied for potential therapeutic applications. Furthermore, the specific alkaloid profile of the plant plays a crucial role in its traditional use as a treatment for certain ailments. Careful extraction and dosage are essential due to the high toxicity inherent in these concentrated chemical structures.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various conditions including inflammatory diseases, skin infections, and certain types of tumors. Researchers have investigated its chemical constituents for potential anti-tumor properties against specific cancer cell lines. The plant also demonstrates significant anti-inflammatory activities that may help manage swelling and pain. Additionally, its antimicrobial properties make it a subject of interest for combating bacterial and fungal infections. These medicinal applications are primarily driven by the presence of bioactive alkaloids within the species.
