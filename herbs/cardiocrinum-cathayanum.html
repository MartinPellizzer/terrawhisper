Cardiocrinum cathayanum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cardiocrinum cathayanum has an underground bulb consisting of fleshy scales and produces long, strap-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and somewhat fleshy in texture. The plant features large, trumpet-shaped flowers that emerge on tall, sturdy scapes from the center of the leaf cluster. These blossoms are characterized by their white color and prominent, spreading tepals that form a distinct funnel shape. The reproductive structures include a central ovary surrounded by numerous stamens and a style that extends toward the floral opening. This perennial herb often grows in shaded or moist habitats where its broad foliage can capture sufficient light.
