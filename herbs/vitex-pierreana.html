Vitex pierreana

What's the taxonomical classification of Vitex pierreana?

Vitex pierreana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows a taxonomic lineage that places it under the order Lamiales. Within this order, the plant is situated in the family Lamiaceae, which is characterized by its distinct morphological traits. Specifically, it is categorized under the genus Vitex, with its unique identity defined by the specific epithet pierreana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vitex pierreana has opposite, decussate leaves that are elliptical to lanceolate in shape with entire margins and a distinctively coriaceous texture. The foliage typically features a dark green upper surface and a paler underside, often displaying prominent venation. Its woody stems exhibit a somewhat quadrangular structure during early growth stages before maturing into a shrub or small tree. The reproductive structures consist of small, fragrant flowers arranged in terminal panicles, which eventually develop into fleshy drupes. These botanical characteristics are essential for distinguishing this species within the Lamiaceae family.
