Callicarpa rubella

What's the taxonomical classification of Callicarpa rubella?

Callicarpa rubella belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Lamiales. This plant is a member of the family Lamiaceae, which falls under the genus Callicarpa, and is specifically identified by its species name, rubella.

What are the pharmacological activities of Callicarpa rubella?

This plant has pharmacological activities including NLRP3-inflammasome inhibitory activity, acetylcholinesterase inhibitory activity, anti-inflammatory properties, and functions as an inhibitor of macrophage foam cell formation. These diverse biological effects suggest that Callicarpa rubella contains potent bioactive compounds capable of modulating complex immune responses. Specifically, its ability to suppress the NLRP3 inflammasome and reduce inflammation highlights its potential in treating chronic inflammatory diseases. The inhibition of macrophage foam cell formation indicates a significant role in managing atherosclerosis and cardiovascular health. Furthermore, the inhibition of acetylcholinesterase provides a basis for its potential application in neuroprotective strategies.

As discussed in a study, nine new diterpenoids named Rubellacrns A-I (1-9) were isolated from Callicarpa rubella. These substances include five isopimaranes (1-4, 9) and four pimaranes (5-8). The isolated compounds were tested for their anti-inflammatory effects to determine their pharmacological potential. Compounds 2 and 11-14 demonstrated NLRP3-inflammasome inhibitory activity. The IC50 values for these specific compounds ranged from 7.02 to 14.38 μM.

As discussed in a study, the flowers of Callicarpa rubella Lindl. contain diterpenoids that have been reported to have anti-inflammatroy activity. Two specific diterpenoid molecules extracted from these flowers are 14α-hydroxyisopimaric acid (C069002) and isopimaric acid (C069004). These two compounds significantly attenuated ox-LDL-induced foam cell formation in RAW264.7 macrophages. The diterpenoids C069002 and C069004 failed to enhance the mRNA transcription of the ABCG1 gene in macrophage-derived foam cells induced by ox-LDL. These diterpenoids work by promoting cholesterol efflux through the PPARγ-LXRα-ABCA1 pathway.
