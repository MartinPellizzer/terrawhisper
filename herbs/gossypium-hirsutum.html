What are the pest and diseases tips of Gossypium hirsutum?

Gossypium hirsutum, commonly known as upland cotton, is a crop susceptible to various pests and diseases that can significantly impact its productivity and quality.

One of the most common pests affecting G. hirsutum is the bollworm (Helicoverpa armigera), which feeds on the fruiting bodies of the plant, causing damage to the cotton balls and reducing yields. Additionally, aphids (Aphis gossypii) and whiteflies (Bemisia tabaci) can also infest the crop, transmitting viral diseases such as cotton leaf curl virus (CLCV).

Fungal pathogens like Fusarium oxysporum and Verticillium dahliae can cause root rot and vascular wilt, respectively, while bacteria like Xanthomonas campestris and Pseudomonas syringae can lead to bacterial blight and cotton bacterial streak disease. Furthermore, nematodes such as Meloidogyne incognita can infect the roots of G. hirsutum, causing reduced growth and yield.

These pests and diseases can be managed through integrated pest management (IPM) strategies that combine cultural practices, biological control methods, and chemical controls to minimize their impact on cotton production.
