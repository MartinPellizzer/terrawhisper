Leontopodium nanum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Leontopodium nanum has a low-growing, rosette-forming habit characterized by small, densely packed leaves and a compact stem structure. The leaves are typically fleshy or thickened, often exhibiting a lanceolate or elliptical shape to conserve moisture. Tiny, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the center of the leaf clusters, usually appearing in small groups. The root system is generally shallow but robust, providing stability for its diminutive stature in rocky or arid environments. This plant's overall architecture is adapted to minimize exposure to harsh winds and intense sunlight.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude alpine regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, specifically within the Rocky Mountains and the Sierra Madre Occidental. It thrives in rocky crevices and subalpine meadows where soil is thin and drainage is rapid. Populations are typically found at elevations ranging from several thousand to over ten thousand feet above sea level. This specific distribution limits the species to cold, montane environments with intense solar exposure.
