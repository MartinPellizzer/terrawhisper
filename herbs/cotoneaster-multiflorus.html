Cotoneaster multiflorus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cotoneaster multiflorus has woody, deciduous shrubs characterized by spreading branches and small, ovate to elliptical leaves that measure approximately 1 to 2 centimeters in length. The leaf margins are finely serrated, and the foliage typically exhibits a dark green color on the upper surface. During the flowering season, the plant produces dense, terminal clusters of small, white, five-petaled flowers. These blossoms give way to small, globose pomes that turn bright red as they mature in the autumn. The overall growth habit is compact, often forming a low, bushy mound in its natural habitat.

    Cotoneaster multiflorus can cause gastrointestinal distress, including nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain, if any part of the plant is ingested. It is important to keep this plant away from children and pets to prevent accidental poisoning. Always consult a medical professional if you suspect someone has eaten any part of the shrub.
