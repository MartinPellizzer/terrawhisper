Coca (Erythroxylum Coca Var. Coca)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Erythroxylum coca var. coca.

Coca, scientifically known as Erythroxylum coca var. coca, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America, traditionally used as a medicinal herb and adaptogen. It contains the unique alkaloid called cocaine, which has been historically valued for its ability to stimulate energy, enhance mental clarity, and alleviate altitude sickness. In traditional Andean cultures, coca leaves have been chewed for centuries to combat fatigue, improve focus, and provide a sense of well-being, often during long journeys or at high altitudes. Modern wellness practices have incorporated coca into supplements and herbal blends to support mental performance, reduce stress, and promote physical endurance, though its use remains controversial due to its psychoactive properties. The plant's distinctive bitter flavor and historical significance in indigenous traditions make it a culturally rich and medically intriguing substance.
