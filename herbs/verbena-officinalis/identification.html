Botanical Identification of Verbenas (Verbena officinalis)

To identify Verbenas (Verbena officinalis) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Verbenas (Verbena officinalis) Monograph

Scientific Classification

Verbenas, specifically Verbena officinalis, belong to the plant kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Lamiales, order Lamiales, family Verbenaceae, genus Verbena, and species Verbena officinalis. This classification places it within the flowering plants and the broader group of lamiales, which includes other aromatic herbs.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Verbena officinalis is the scientific name for the common vervain. It was first described by Carl von Linné in 1753. Synonyms include Verbena trixago and Verbena lutea. Common names include common vervain, viper's bugloss, and woundwound. It is also known as "vervain" in many regions and has historical names like "herbe de Saint-Jacques."

Morphological Characteristics

Verbenas (Verbena officinalis) are herbaceous plants with a compact growth habit. They have a fibrous root system that spreads horizontally. The stems are upright and branching, often covered in fine hairs. Leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture. The inflorescence is a dense spike of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in summer. Each flower has five petals and is pollinated by insects. The fruit is a small capsule containing numerous tiny seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Verbenas can be distinguished by their narrow, opposite leaves with a slightly hairy underside, and spikes of blue or purple flowers. They have square stems and small, four-lobed flowers. Avoid confusion with similar-looking plants like vervain or other verbena species by checking these key features.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Verbena officinalis is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of western Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with full sun. It prefers well-drained soils, often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides. It grows at altitudes between 0 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Verbenas (Verbena officinalis) bloom from late spring to early summer, typically between May and July, depending on the climate. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early autumn, around August to September. For identification purposes, the best time to harvest is during the flowering period, when the plant is most distinct and the active constituents are concentrated.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Verbenas belong to the genus Verbena within the family Verbenaceae. The closest species include Verbena litoralis and Verbena hastata. These species share similar morphological and genetic traits, such as square stems and showy flowers, which help in their identification and classification.
