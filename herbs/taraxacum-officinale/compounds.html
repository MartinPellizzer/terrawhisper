Dandelion (Taraxacum officinale) Compounds

Dandelion contains several primary active compounds, including flavonoids, which have anti-inflammatory properties. Phenolic acids contribute to its antioxidant effects. Coumarin is known for its antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory actions. These compounds work together to support the plant's medicinal uses in herbal medicine.

Dandelion (Taraxacum officinale) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of compounds found in Dandelion (Taraxacum officinale). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant properties and support digestive and liver health. These compounds are often responsible for the bitter taste of dandelion and are used in herbal medicine to promote detoxification and reduce inflammation.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Dandelion (Taraxacum officinale), contributing to its diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties. It supports liver function and helps detoxify the body, making it valuable in herbal medicine for treating urinary tract issues and digestive ailments.

3. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Dandelion (Taraxacum officinale). It contributes to the plant's diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarin also acts as a mild anticoagulant, supporting cardiovascular health. Its presence enhances the therapeutic potential of Dandelion in herbal medicine.

4. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Dandelion (Taraxacum officinale). It contributes to the plant's bitterness and has mild diuretic properties. Saponins may support liver function and aid in detoxification, aligning with traditional uses of Dandelion in herbal medicine.

5. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Dandelion (Taraxacum officinale), contributing to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, supporting the plant's use in traditional herbal medicine for digestive and liver health. They also enhance the plant's ability to detoxify the body and support overall immune function.

6. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Dandelion (Taraxacum officinale). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and supports digestive health by reducing inflammation and promoting gut healing. Tannins also aid in detoxification and are valued for their antioxidant effects in herbal medicine.

7. Glycoside

Glycosides in Dandelion (Taraxacum officinale) are bioactive compounds that contribute to its diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds, such as taraxacin and lactucopimaric acid, support the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and liver health.

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of Dandelion (Taraxacum officinale) contains volatile compounds like limonene and α-pinene. These contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, supporting digestive and respiratory health. The oil is often used in aromatherapy and as a complementary treatment in herbal medicine.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Dandelion (Taraxacum officinale). It contributes to the plant's vibrant color and has antioxidant properties. This compound supports overall health by neutralizing free radicals and may enhance the plant's medicinal effects in traditional herbal use.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Dandelion (Taraxacum officinale). It contributes to the plant's bitter taste and has mild stimulant properties. Alkaloids in Dandelion may support digestive health and have anti-inflammatory effects. They are part of the plant's broader array of bioactive compounds used in herbal medicine.
