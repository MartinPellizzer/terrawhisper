Oxytropis leptophylla has slender, linear leaflets that are typically narrow and sparsely distributed along its pinnate leaves. Small, pea-like flowers emerge from the plant, often displaying hues of purple or blue. These reproductive structures are borne on stalks that rise above the compact leafy base.

      Oxytropis leptophylla can cause severe poisoning in livestock through the ingestion of toxic alkaloids that primarily target the central nervous system.
