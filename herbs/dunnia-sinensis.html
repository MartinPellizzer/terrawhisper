Dunnia sinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Dunnia sinensis?

Dunnia sinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Gentianaales. This plant is a member of the family Rubiaceae, specifically placed within the genus Dunnia, with its unique designation being the species sinensis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds dunnisinin and dunnisinoside, which belong to the class of bioactive glycosides. These specific molecules are often studied for their unique chemical structures and potential pharmacological activities. Researchers frequently investigate these substances to understand how they interact with biological systems. The presence of such specialized compounds makes the plant a subject of interest in natural product chemistry. Further analysis can reveal more about the therapeutic properties provided by these constituents.

As discussed in a study, the leaves of Dunnia sinensis contain specific medicinal compounds. Researchers isolated a plumieride type iridoid glucoside known as dunnisinoside from the plant. Additionally, a non-glucosidic iridoid named dunnisinin was also isolated from the leaves. The structures of these compounds were established through the use of 1D and 2D NMR and FABMS experiments.
