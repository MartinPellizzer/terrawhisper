What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vitis piasezkii has woody, climbing vines characterized by trifoliate or pentafoliate leaves with serrated margins and large, cordate bases. The leaves are typically dark green and possess a somewhat rugose texture on the upper surface. Its stems are equipped with specialized tendrils that facilitate climbing by wrapping around surrounding vegetation. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in compact panicles. These blossoms eventually develop into small, globose berries that change color as they reach maturity.
