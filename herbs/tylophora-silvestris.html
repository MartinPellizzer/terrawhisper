Tylophora silvestris

What's the taxonomical classification of Tylophora silvestris?

Tylophora silvestris belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the evolutionary hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Gentianales, the plant is a member of the family Apocynaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement within the genus Tylophora, culminating in its unique species designation, silvestris.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, and potential toxicity due to the presence of cardiac glycosides within its tissues. Individuals who ingest the plant may experience nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain as the body reacts to its chemical compounds. Topical contact with the foliage or sap can lead to localized allergic reactions such as redness, itching, or swelling on the skin. Because the glycosides can affect heart function, excessive consumption poses a serious risk of irregular heartbeat or cardiac arrhythmia. It is essential to exercise extreme caution and avoid all contact with this species to prevent these adverse health outcomes.
