Ilex hainanensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Ilex hainanensis?

Ilex hainanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical lineage of its order, Aquifoliales, the plant is placed within the family Aquifoliaceae. Ultimately, its taxonomic identity is defined by its membership in the genus Ilex, specifically as the species hainanensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ilex hainanenensis has small, evergreen, coriaceous leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape with entire or slightly serrated margins.

The foliage is characterized by a glossy upper surface and a paler underside, providing a distinct contrast. Its woody stems exhibit a slow-growing habit, contributing to a dense, shrubby architecture. The plant produces inconspicuous flowers followed by small, fleshy drupes that change color as they mature.
