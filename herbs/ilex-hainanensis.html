Ilex hainanensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Ilex hainanensis?

Ilex hainanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical lineage of its order, Aquifoliales, the plant is placed within the family Aquifoliaceae. Ultimately, its taxonomic identity is defined by its membership in the genus Ilex, specifically as the species hainanensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ilex hainanenensis has small, evergreen, coriaceous leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. The foliage is characterized by a glossy upper surface and a paler underside, providing a distinct contrast. Its woody stems exhibit a slow-growing habit, contributing to a dense, shrubby architecture. The plant produces inconspicuous flowers followed by small, fleshy drupes that change color as they mature. These morphological traits allow the species to thrive within its specific ecological niche.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds beta-sitosterol-3-O-beta-D-glucoside, ilexgenin A, ilexsaponin A1, and ursolic acid. These specific phytochemicals contribute to the overall therapeutic profile of the species. Ursolic acid is often recognized for its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. The presence of ilexgenin A and ilexsaponin A1 further characterizes the unique chemical makeup of the plant. Together, these bioactive molecules demonstrate the complex biological utility of the vegetation.

As discussed in a study, the chemical composition of Ilex hainanensis was probed through the EtOAc soluble fraction of the ethanolic extract from its leaves. Through this process, 4 compounds were obtained and identified using physico-chemical and spectroscopic methods. These compounds include ursolic acid, ilexgenin A, ilexsaponin A1, and beta-sitosterol-3-O-beta-D-glucoside. All 4 of these compounds were isolated from the plant for the first time.
