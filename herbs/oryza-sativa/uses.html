Rice (Oryza sativa) Uses

Rice is used to treat diarrhea, gastritis, and gastrointestinal inflammation due to its soothing and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps manage gastric ulcers and digestive disorders by promoting gut health. Rice also supports in reducing symptoms of inflammatory bowel disease and fatty liver disease. Its mild nature makes it suitable for addressing constipation and skin infections when prepared appropriately.

Rice (Oryza sativa) Monograph

1. Diarrhea

Rice is used in herbal medicine to treat diarrhea due to its astringent properties, which help firm stools and reduce intestinal inflammation. It is often prepared as a rice water or decoction to soothe the digestive tract and restore electrolyte balance. Rice is considered safe and gentle, making it suitable for children and those with sensitive digestive systems.

2. Gastritis

Rice is used in herbal medicine to soothe gastritis due to its anti-inflammatory and mucilage properties. It helps coat and protect the stomach lining, reducing irritation and promoting healing. In traditional practices, rice water or rice-based decoctions are often prescribed to alleviate symptoms and support digestive health.

3. Gastroesophageal reflux disease

Rice is used in traditional medicine to alleviate symptoms of gastroesophageal reflux disease due to its soothing properties. It helps neutralize stomach acid and reduces irritation in the esophagus. Rice water, made by boiling rice in water, is often consumed to provide relief. It is considered gentle and safe for long-term use.

4. Inflammatory bowel disease

Rice has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to support digestive health. Its anti-inflammatory properties may help reduce gut inflammation, making it beneficial for managing symptoms of inflammatory bowel disease. Rice water, prepared by boiling rice in water, is often used to soothe intestinal irritation and promote healing.

5. Fatty liver disease

Rice has been used in traditional medicine to support liver health, particularly in managing fatty liver disease. It helps reduce inflammation and detoxify the liver due to its high fiber and antioxidant content. In herbal formulations, rice is often combined with other herbs to enhance its therapeutic effects on liver function.

6. Constipation

Rice is used in herbal medicine to alleviate constipation due to its mild laxative properties. It helps soften stools and promotes bowel movement when prepared as a decoction or consumed as a porridge. In traditional practices, rice is often combined with other herbs to enhance its effectiveness. Regular consumption of rice-based remedies can support digestive health and relieve mild constipation.

7. Gastrointestinal inflammation

Rice is used in traditional herbal medicine to soothe gastrointestinal inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and demulcent properties. It helps reduce irritation in the stomach and intestines, making it beneficial for conditions like gastritis and colitis. Rice water and rice porridge are commonly prepared to ease digestive discomfort and promote healing.

8. Gastric ulcer

Rice is used in traditional medicine to soothe gastric ulcers due to its anti-inflammatory and mucilage-forming properties. It helps protect the stomach lining and reduce acid secretion. Rice water, prepared by boiling rice, is commonly used to alleviate ulcer symptoms and promote healing. It is often combined with other herbs for enhanced efficacy.

9. Digestive disorders

Rice is commonly used in herbal medicine to alleviate digestive disorders such as diarrhea and indigestion. Its mild nature helps soothe the stomach and absorb excess moisture. Rice water, prepared by boiling rice in water, is often used to treat mild gastrointestinal distress. It is considered safe and gentle for most individuals.

10. Skin infection

Rice is used in traditional medicine to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial properties. A paste made from rice flour and water can help reduce inflammation and promote healing. It is often applied topically to wounds or infected areas. This method is simple, affordable, and accessible in many regions.
