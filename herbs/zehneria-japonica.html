Zehneria japonica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Zehneria japonica has an erect, perennial herbaceous habit characterized by slender, ascending stems and leaves that are typically oppositely arranged. The foliage consists of ovate to lanceolate blades with serrated margins and relatively thin textures. Its reproductive structures are small, inconspicuous flowers organized into dense, terminal heads or clusters. These inflorescences are typically yellowish or pale in color and are supported by distinct bracts. The root system is generally shallow, helping the plant anchor itself in its native moist habitats.
