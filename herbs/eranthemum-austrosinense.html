What's the taxonomical classification of Eranthemum austrosinense?

Eranthemum austrosinense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its lineage through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, the plant is further categorized under the order Lamiales. It is a member of the family Acanthaceae, specifically falling under the genus Eranthemum, with its unique identification completed by the species name austrosinense.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Eranthemum austrosinense can cause skin irritation, redness, and potential allergic reactions upon direct contact with the skin or mucous membranes. The plant contains chemical compounds that may act as mild irritants to sensitive individuals. If the foliage or sap is accidentally ingested, it could lead to gastrointestinal discomfort or nausea. While specific toxicological data is limited, caution should always be exercised when handling unknown tropical species.
