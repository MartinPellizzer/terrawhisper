What is Vriesea castaneobulbosa?

Vriesea castaneobulbosa is a species of epiphytic bromeliad belonging to the family Bromeliaceae.

It is characterized by its terrestrial habit, and has a stout stem up to 10 cm long, covered with imbricate, lanceolate, dark-green leaves that are 10-20 cm long, 3-4 cm wide, and have a sharp apex. The leaves are arranged in a rosette, and the inflorescence is a short, terminal spike, 5-10 cm long, bearing numerous small, yellow flowers with a reddish-brown center. The plant produces a short, stout, dark-brown inflorescence axis covered with the remnants of the floral bracts, which form a bulbous structure that persists after the flowers have dropped.

The species is native to the tropical forests of southeastern Brazil.
