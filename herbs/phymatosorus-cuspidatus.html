Phymatosorus cuspidatus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phymatosorus cuspidatus has pinnatifid fronds characterized by deeply lobed, lanceolate blades with prominent, spreading veins and acute, pointed tips. The texture of the foliage is typically leathery and glossy, providing a robust appearance to the fern. Each individual lobe is arranged along a central rachis that may exhibit slight discoloration or pubescence depending on the specimen's age. On the underside of the leaves, small, circular sori are organized in rows following the pattern of the venation. These reproductive structures are protected by an indusium that is often minimal or absent in mature plants.
