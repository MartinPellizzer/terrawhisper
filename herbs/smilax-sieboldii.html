Smilax sieboldii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Smilax sieboldii has woody, climbing stems equipped with axillary spines and large, leathery, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The leaf margins are often smooth or slightly wavy, and the venation is prominent on the underside. During the flowering season, small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers emerge from the leaf axils. These flowers eventually develop into small, fleshy berries that transition from green to a dark red or black color when ripe. The plant's overall structure is characterized by its ability to scramble over other vegetation using its thorny stems for support.
