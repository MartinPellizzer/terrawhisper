Bergamot (Citrus bergamia)

Bergamot (Citrus bergamia) is a member of the Rutaceae family, native to Southern Italy, Balkan Peninsula, and Eastern Mediterranean. Traditionally, its fruits, leaves, and bark have been used for infusions, oil infusions, and decoctions.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, bitter, and antispasmodic actions, and has a long history of use in mediterranean herbal traditions, european herbal medicine, and traditional chinese medicine.
