Circaea cordata

What's the taxonomical classification of Circaea cordata?

Circaea cordata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Myrtales and the family Onagraceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Circaea, and its species name, cordata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Circaea cordata has cordate to ovate leaves with long petioles, upright stems reaching up to 60 centimeters, and small, four-petaled white flowers arranged in terminal racemes. The leaves are typically dark green and feature serrated margins that become more pronounced toward the tips. Its slender stems are often reddish and can grow in clumps from a creeping rhizome. The flowers are delicate and produce small, nut-like fruits that possess hooked bristles for seed dispersal by animals. These botanical characteristics distinguish it from other members of the Onagraceae family.
