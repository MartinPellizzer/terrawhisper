Circaea cordata

What's the taxonomical classification of Circaea cordata?

Circaea cordata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Myrtales and the family Onagraceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Circaea, and its species name, cordata.

      What are the herbal preparations of Circaea cordata?

      Circaea cordata is prepared primarily as a liquid infusion or decoction using the dried aerial parts of the plant. Practitioners often steep the leaves and stems in hot water to extract the soluble compounds for medicinal use. Some traditional applications involve creating a tincture by soaking the plant material in high-proof alcohol for several weeks. These liquid extracts can then be administered in small, measured doses. Additionally, the plant material can be incorporated into topical salves or poultices for external application.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Circaea cordata can cause skin irritation or allergic reactions in sensitive individuals who come into direct contact with its foliage. While the plant is generally considered non-toxic to humans, there is very little scientific data available regarding its internal effects if ingested. Some herbalists suggest that caution should be exercised when using any wild plant for medicinal purposes without professional guidance. Potential side effects are largely unknown because extensive clinical studies have not been conducted on this specific species. Therefore, it is best to avoid consumption and use gloves when handling the plant to prevent any unexpected dermatological responses.
