Herbal Preparations

This preparation is often used for its purported anti-inflammatory and digestive benefits, though scientific evidence supporting these claims remains limited. Side Effects of guaiacum sanctum

Guaiacum sanctum triggers nausea, which can manifest as a persistent feeling of queasiness or an urge to vomit, often accompanied by a loss of appetite.

This herb may also cause vomiting, particularly in higher doses or when taken on an empty stomach, leading to dehydration and electrolyte imbalances. Additionally, it can induce gastrointestinal upset, resulting in stomach pain and diarrhea, which may be severe in some individuals. Skin irritation and rashes are also possible side effects, with some users reporting redness, itching, or a burning sensation upon contact with the herb or its derivatives.

Furthermore, Guaiacum sanctum may lead to headaches and dizziness, possibly due to its impact on the central nervous system, making it important to consult a healthcare professional before use.
