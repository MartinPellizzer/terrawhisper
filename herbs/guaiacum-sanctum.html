Guaiacum Sanctum Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

The bioactive constituents in Guaiacum sanctum include flavonoids, tannins, and essential oils, which contribute to its therapeutic actions.

Medicinal Constituents of guaiacum sanctum

Guaiacum sanctum quassinoids are a class of bitter-tasting, oxygenated triterpenoids that exhibit potent anti-inflammatory, antimalarial, and antitumor properties, primarily due to their ability to modulate immune responses and inhibit the growth of cancer cells.

Guaiacum sanctum terpenoids, which include various sesquiterpene and triterpene derivatives, contribute to the plant's pharmacological profile by displaying antimicrobial, antifungal, and hepatoprotective activities, making them valuable in treating infections and liver disorders.

Guaiacum sanctum guaiaretic acid is a unique fatty acid derivative known for its strong antifungal and antiviral effects, particularly effective against dermatophytes and certain viral infections, and it also shows potential in reducing inflammation.

Guaiacum sanctum fatty acids, including linoleic and oleic acids, play a role in the plant's therapeutic effects by supporting skin health, reducing oxidative stress, and enhancing the bioavailability of other active compounds.

Herbal Preparations of guaiacum sanctum

Guaiacum sanctum teas are commonly prepared by steeping the dried leaves and bark in hot water, and are often used for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
