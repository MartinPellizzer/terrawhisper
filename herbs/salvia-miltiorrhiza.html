Red sage

Salvia miltiorrhiza

Red Sage (Salvia miltiorrhiza) is a traditional Chinese medicinal plant known for its deep red roots, which are used to improve circulation, reduce inflammation, and support heart health.

Taxonomy

Red Sage (Salvia miltiorrhiza) is a perennial herb belonging to the genus Salvia in the family Lamiaceae, classified under the order Lamiales, class Lamiales, division Magnoliophyta, and kingdom Plantae.

Morphology

Red Sage, or Salvia miltiorrhiza, is a perennial herb characterized by its square, hairy stems, opposite leaves with serrated edges, and showy violet to purple flowers that bloom in whorled clusters at the leaf axils.

Distribution

Red Sage (Salvia miltiorrhiza) is native to China, particularly thriving in the regions of Shandong, Hebei, Henan, and Jiangsu, with cultivation also extending to parts of Korea and Southeast Asia.

Traditional Uses

Red Sage has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine for its purported ability to promote blood circulation, reduce inflammation, and support cardiovascular health.

Medicinal Systems

Red Sage (Salvia miltiorrhiza) has been utilized in traditional medicinal systems such as Traditional Chinese Medicine, Ayurveda, and Native American herbal practices for its therapeutic properties.

Phytochemistry

Red Sage contains a variety of bioactive compounds, including tanshinones, salvianolic acids, and lucidenic acid, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Pharmacology

Red Sage (Salvia miltiorrhiza) exhibits pharmacological properties characterized by anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and cardioprotective effects, with its therapeutic actions primarily attributed to its ability to regulate blood circulation, reduce oxidative stress, and modulate immune responses.

Botanical Identification

Red Sage, or Salvia miltiorrhiza, is a perennial herb in the Lamiaceae family. Native to China, it is known by various regional names like Danshen and Tanshen. The plant features dark red roots, lanceolate leaves, and violet flowers. It is also referred to as Red Medicinal Sage in some herbal texts.

Safety Profile

Red Sage is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to potential risks to the fetus or infant. Always consult a healthcare professional before use.
