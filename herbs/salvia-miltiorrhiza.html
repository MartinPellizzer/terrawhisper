Red sage

Salvia miltiorrhiza

Red Sage (Salvia miltiorrhiza) is a traditional Chinese medicinal plant known for its deep red roots, which are used to improve circulation, reduce inflammation, and support heart health.

Taxonomy

Red Sage (Salvia miltiorrhiza) is a perennial herb belonging to the genus Salvia in the family Lamiaceae, classified under the order Lamiales, class Lamiales, division Magnoliophyta, and kingdom Plantae.

Morphology

Red Sage, or Salvia miltiorrhiza, is a perennial herb characterized by its square, hairy stems, opposite leaves with serrated edges, and showy violet to purple flowers that bloom in whorled clusters at the leaf axils.

Distribution

Red Sage (Salvia miltiorrhiza) is native to China, particularly thriving in the regions of Shandong, Hebei, Henan, and Jiangsu, with cultivation also extending to parts of Korea and Southeast Asia.

Traditional Uses

Red Sage has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine for its purported ability to promote blood circulation, reduce inflammation, and support cardiovascular health.

Medicinal Systems

Red Sage (Salvia miltiorrhiza) has been utilized in traditional medicinal systems such as Traditional Chinese Medicine, Ayurveda, and Native American herbal practices for its therapeutic properties.

Phytochemistry

Red Sage contains a variety of bioactive compounds, including tanshinones, salvianolic acids, and lucidenic acid, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Pharmacology

Red Sage (Salvia miltiorrhiza) exhibits pharmacological properties characterized by anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and cardioprotective effects, with its therapeutic actions primarily attributed to its ability to regulate blood circulation, reduce oxidative stress, and modulate immune responses.

Medicinal Uses

Red sage is used to treat conditions such as cardiovascular diseases, menstrual disorders, liver dysfunction, diabetes, inflammatory conditions, and neurological disorders.

Preparations

Red sage is used to prepare various medicinal formulations such as root extracts, tinctures, capsules, and decoctions, which are utilized in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported effects on blood circulation, menstrual regulation, and cardiovascular health.

Safety

While Red Sage is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses, it may cause gastrointestinal upset, such as nausea or diarrhea, and in some cases, allergic reactions like rash or itching.

Evidence

Clinical evidence suggests that Red Sage (Salvia miltiorrhiza) has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries to improve circulation, reduce inflammation, and support cardiovascular health, with modern studies indicating potential benefits in treating conditions such as coronary heart disease and stroke.

Cultivation

Red Sage (Salvia miltiorrhiza) is typically cultivated in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil, often in shaded environments, and requires careful management of moisture and temperature to ensure optimal growth and medicinal compound production.

Processing

The processing of Red Sage involves cleaning the roots, slicing them into thin pieces, and drying them in a controlled environment to preserve their active compounds and enhance their medicinal properties.

Identification

Red Sage (Salvia miltiorrhiza) is a perennial herb native to China, characterized by its deep red roots, lance-shaped leaves, and violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer.

Sustainability

Sustainability of Red Sage (Salvia miltiorrhiza) involves responsible cultivation practices, conservation of wild populations, and ethical harvesting to ensure its long-term availability and ecological balance.

Related

Red Sage (Salvia miltiorrhiza) is closely related to other species in the Salvia genus, including Salvia miltiorrhiza var. heterophylla, Salvia miltiorrhiza var. davidii, and Salvia miltiorrhiza var. rubrofusca, which share similar chemical compositions and traditional medicinal uses.
