Red Sage (Salvia miltiorrhiza)

Red Sage (Salvia miltiorrhiza) is a member of the Lamiaceae family, native to China, Korea, and Japan. Traditionally, its roots, rhizomes, and stems have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, tonic, and antispasmodic actions, and has a long history of use in traditional chinese medicine, japanese kampo medicine, and korean traditional medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 30 to 100 centimeters.
Leaves
Smooth, ovate leaves with upper surface reddish-brown and lower surface pale green, bearing distinct stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Bracteate inflorescence with zygomorphic flowers, violet to red corolla with five lobes, two-lipped structure, orange-yellow staminode, and a bilobed stigma.
Stems
Cylindrical, herbaceous, with opposite branching, smooth surface, reddish-brown pith, hollow central cavity, persistent decurrent leaves.
