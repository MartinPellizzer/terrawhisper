Salvia miltiorrhiza

What's the taxonomical classification of Salvia miltiorrhiza?

Salvia miltiorrhiza belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Lamiales. This species is a member of the family Lamiaceae, falling under the genus Salvia, and is specifically identified by its species name, miltiorrhiza.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Salvial miltiorrhiza has a perennial rootstock that produces upright, square-shaped stems reaching heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. The leaves are primarily basal and heart-shaped with serrated margins, while the upper leaves are smaller and more lanceolate. Its inflorescence consists of dense, terminal spikes featuring tubular, two-lipped flowers that typically range in color from pale blue to lavender. The calyx is often hairy and serves to protect the developing floral structures throughout the growing season. These morphological traits collectively distinguish the species within the Lamiaceae family.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to a specific range within the southeastern United States, primarily concentrated in the coastal plain regions of Georgia, South Carolina, North Carolina, and Florida. It thrives in the sandy soils and open woodlands characteristic of these subtropical environments. Within these states, it is often found in pine flatwoods and along the edges of maritime forests. The species shows a preference for habitats that receive significant sunlight and maintain moderate moisture levels. Its distribution is relatively localized compared to more widespread sage species found across North America.

What are the pharmacological activities of Salvia miltiorrhiza?

This plant has anti-inflammatory, anti-oxidant, anti-tumor, cardiovascular protection, and blood circulation promotion pharmacological activities. Its diverse chemical constituents work to neutralize free radicals, thereby providing significant anti-oxidant support to cellular structures. The plant also helps mitigate systemic inflammation and demonstrates potential in inhibiting the growth of various tumor cells. Furthermore, its bioactive compounds contribute to cardiovascular protection by supporting heart health and improving overall blood circulation promotion. These multifaceted medicinal properties make it a subject of great interest in pharmacological research.

According to a study published by "Phytomedicine : international journal of phytotherapy and phytopharmacology", Salvia miltiorrhiza plays a vital role in the anti-inflammatory and anti-apoptotic action during acute myocardial ischemia. This plant contributes to the cardioprotection of the prescription by helping to ameliorate myocardial ischemia in rats. Specifically, Salvia miltiorrhiza can potently modulate the metabolism of the 15 free fatty acids analyzed in the study. The removal of this plant from the prescription resulted in a marked loss of cardioprotection and a loss of mediation regarding free fatty acid metabolism.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several bioactive medicinal compounds including Salvianolic acid B, Tanshinone IIA, Rosmarinic acid, Cryptotanshinone, and Danshensu. These specific phytochemicals are primarily responsible for the plant's diverse pharmacological properties such as antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. Salvianolic acid B and Danshensu are major components often studied for their cardiovascular benefits. The diterpene quinones, specifically Tanshinone IIA and Cryptotanshinone, contribute significantly to the plant's therapeutic potential. Furthermore, the presence of Rosmarinic acid enhances the overall medicinal value of the species.

According to a study published by "International journal of molecular sciences", Salvia miltiorrhiza contains medicinal compounds such as salvianolic acid B (SalB). The study identifies that 15 SPL family genes have been recognized in this medical plant. Research focused on the SmSPL2 transcription factor, which shows its highest transcriptional level in the calyx. The concentration of SalB was significantly decreased in rSmSPL2-overexpressed lines. This SmSPL2 gene can efficiently manipulate the SalB concentration in Salvia miltiorrhiza.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause potential side effects such as gastrointestinal distress, allergic skin reactions, or unexpected interactions with pharmaceutical medications. There is also a risk that bioactive compounds in the plant could interfere with the metabolism of drugs processed by the liver. Because scientific data regarding its long-term safety is insufficient, cautious usage is highly recommended to avoid adverse physiological responses.
