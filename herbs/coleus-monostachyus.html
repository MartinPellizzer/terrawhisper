Coleus monostachyus

What's the taxonomical classification of Coleus monostachyus?

Coleus monostachyus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic progression continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Lamiales. Furthermore, this plant is a member of the family Lamiaceae, which falls under the genus Coleus, and is ultimately identified by its specific species name, monostachyus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Coleus monostachyus has square-shaped stems, opposite leaf arrangement, and ovate leaves with serrated margins. The foliage often displays vibrant color patterns ranging from deep greens to bright reds and purples. These leaves possess a slightly textured surface due to fine hairs covering the epidermis. The plant typically reaches a medium height and produces small, inconspicuous flowers on upright spikes. Its root system is fibrous and serves to anchor the herbaceous structure firmly in the soil.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, primarily spanning across countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia. It flourishes in the humid, low-elevation environments characteristic of these equatorial climates. Its natural range is defined by consistent warmth and high moisture levels found in dense forest understories. While it remains localized to these specific Asian territories in the wild, it is occasionally found in managed botanical collections globally. The distribution is largely constrained by its intolerance to frost and dry, temperate weather.
