What to know about Verbena hastata (blue vervain) before using it medicinally

Blue vervain, scientifically known as Verbena hastata, is a herb that has been revered for its numerous health benefits and versatility.

Its leaves and flowers are rich in antioxidants, flavonoids, and volatile oils, making it an excellent natural remedy to soothe digestive issues, reduce inflammation, and boost the immune system. While blue vervain's culinary uses are often limited to adding its delicate flavor to salads and soups, its essential oils can also be used as a substitute for lavender or lemon balm in potpourri and perfumes. In terms of horticulture, blue vervain is an easy-to-grow perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice among gardeners.

With its ancient roots dating back to the indigenous cultures of North America, blue vervain has been used for centuries as a ritual plant, offering protection, purification, and spiritual growth.
