Schefflera khasiana

What's the taxonomical classification of Schefflera khasiana?

Schefflera khasiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized under the order Apiales and resides within the family Araliaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by its placement in the genus Schefflerera and the species khasiana.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Schefflera khasiana targets key inflammatory enzymes such as cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2) and lipoxygenase (LOX), alongside various pro-inflammatory cytokines and transcription factors like NF-kappaB. These interactions occur primarily through the bioactive phytochemicals present in the plant, including saponins, flavonoids, and terpenoids. By inhibiting the COX-2 enzyme, the plant effectively reduces the synthesis of prostaglandins, which are central mediators of pain and swelling. Additionally, its ability to modulate the signaling pathways of cytokines helps suppress the overactive immune responses associated with chronic inflammation. These molecular mechanisms collectively contribute to the plant's documented medicinal efficacy in treating inflammatory disorders and oxidative stress.

While specific toxicological studies are limited, caution should be exercised when handling the foliage or stems.
