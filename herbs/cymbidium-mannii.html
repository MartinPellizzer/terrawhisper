Cymbidium mannii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cymbidium mannii has long, leathery, elliptical leaves that grow from a creeping rhizome and produce upright, unbranched inflorescences bearing numerous small, pale-colored flowers. These flowers typically exhibit a delicate greenish-white or yellowish hue with intricate patterns on the labellum to attract specific pollinators. The plant's structural habit is characterized by its sympodial growth pattern, where new shoots emerge from the base of older ones. Each flower possesses a complex tripartite lip structure that is a distinguishing feature of the genus. The overall foliage provides a dense, dark green backdrop that complements the elegant, arching stems of the flowering spikes.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Cymbidium mannii can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and digestive distress if ingested. If swallowed, the organic compounds within the leaves and stems can cause nausea or stomach pain. Monitoring for these symptoms is recommended when handling the plant without protective equipment.
