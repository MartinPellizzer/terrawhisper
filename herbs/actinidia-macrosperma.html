Actinidia macrosperma

What's the taxonomical classification of Actinidia macrosperma?

Actinidia macrosperma belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Ericales. This plant is a member of the family Actinidiaceae, falling within the genus Actinidia, and is specifically identified by the species name macrosperma.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Actinidia macrosperma can cause allergic reactions such as skin rashes, itching, and swelling if the plant or its fruit comes into direct contact with sensitive individuals. Some people may experience gastrointestinal distress, including nausea or stomach cramps, after ingestion. If inhaled, the pollen or fine particles from the plant might trigger respiratory irritation or sneezing fits. Severe hypersensitivity could potentially lead to more serious symptoms like hives or difficulty breathing. It is important to exercise caution when handling this species to avoid these adverse physiological responses.
