White Clover (Trifolium repens) Uses

White Clover is used to alleviate hay fever and allergic rhinitis due to its antihistamine properties. It may also help reduce symptoms of the common cold and respiratory infections. Additionally, it supports skin health by reducing irritation, inflammation, and eczema, while promoting wound healing.

White Clover (Trifolium repens) Monograph

1. Hay fever

White Clover (Trifolium repens) is used in herbal medicine to alleviate hay fever symptoms. Its anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties help reduce nasal congestion and allergic reactions. It is often prepared as a tincture or tea to support respiratory health during pollen season.

2. Allergic rhinitis

White Clover has been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties. It contains compounds that may reduce nasal congestion and irritation, making it a gentle alternative for managing seasonal allergies. Its mild nature makes it suitable for long-term use under professional guidance.

3. Common cold

White Clover is traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of the common cold due to its antiviral and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce congestion and soothe sore throats when brewed into a tea. Its mild nature makes it safe for regular use during cold seasons.

4. Asthma

White Clover has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in managing asthma symptoms. Its mild antispasmodic properties may help ease bronchial constriction, while its anti-inflammatory effects can reduce airway irritation. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture and used as a complementary therapy under professional guidance.

5. Respiratory infections

White Clover has been traditionally used to support respiratory health. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties may help alleviate symptoms of respiratory infections. It is often prepared as a tea to soothe coughs and reduce mucus production. Regular use may aid in clearing the airways and boosting immunity.

6. Asthma symptoms

White Clover has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in managing asthma symptoms. Its anti-inflammatory properties may help reduce airway inflammation, while its mild expectorant action can aid in clearing mucus. It is often used in herbal formulations to support bronchial function and ease breathing.

7. Skin irritation

White Clover is used topically to soothe skin irritation due to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. It can be applied as a poultice or infused oil to reduce redness, itching, and inflammation. Its mild nature makes it suitable for sensitive skin, though it should be tested for allergic reactions before use.

8. Wound healing

White Clover has been traditionally used to support wound healing due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It helps reduce swelling and promotes tissue repair. Topical applications of its leaves or extracts can aid in cleansing wounds and preventing infection. Its mild nature makes it suitable for use in herbal first-aid preparations.

9. Skin inflammation

White Clover is used topically to reduce skin inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. It contains compounds like flavonoids and tannins that help calm irritated skin. It is often applied as a compress or infused oil to treat conditions like eczema and rash. Its mild nature makes it suitable for sensitive skin.

10. Eczema

White Clover has been traditionally used to support skin health, particularly in managing eczema. Its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties may help reduce redness and irritation. It is often prepared as a topical salve or infused oil to apply directly to affected areas. Regular use may promote healing and alleviate symptoms associated with eczema.
