White Clover (Trifolium repens) Compounds

White Clover contains several key active compounds, including flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties. Phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins provide astringent qualities, while coumarin has anticoagulant properties. Glycosides, saponins, and terpenoids also play roles in its medicinal benefits. Anthocyanins and essential oils add to its diverse pharmacological profile.

White Clover (Trifolium repens) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are key active compounds in White Clover (Trifolium repens). They exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting cardiovascular and immune health. These compounds are found in the leaves and flowers of the plant, contributing to its traditional use in herbal medicine.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in White Clover (Trifolium repens). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and skin conditions. This compound helps modulate immune responses and may aid in reducing oxidative stress in the body.

3. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in White Clover (Trifolium repens). It exhibits astringent properties, helping to reduce inflammation and promote wound healing. Tannins also contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating digestive issues and skin conditions.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in White Clover (Trifolium repens). It contributes to the plant's mild anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarin also acts as a natural pesticide and is used in traditional herbal medicine for its soothing effects on the respiratory and digestive systems.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in White Clover (Trifolium repens). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Glycosides in White Clover are often used in herbal formulations to support digestive and respiratory health.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in White Clover (Trifolium repens). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Saponin may support digestive health and immune function. Its presence enhances the therapeutic potential of White Clover in herbal medicine.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in White Clover (Trifolium repens) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help reduce oxidative stress and support immune function. They are found in various parts of the plant and are often used in herbal formulations. Terpenoids play a key role in the plant's therapeutic potential.

8. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin, found in White Clover (Trifolium repens), is a flavonoid responsible for the plant's characteristic blue and purple hues. It exhibits antioxidant properties, supporting cardiovascular health and reducing inflammation, making it a valuable component in herbal medicine.

9. Essential oil

The essential oil of White Clover (Trifolium repens) contains volatile compounds like limonene and linalool, which contribute to its aromatic properties. These compounds are used in herbal medicine for their potential calming and anti-inflammatory effects.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in White Clover (Trifolium repens). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, though its role is less prominent compared to other constituents. Alkaloids in White Clover may support traditional uses in herbal medicine, particularly for digestive and inflammatory conditions.
