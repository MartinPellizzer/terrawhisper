Cynanchum chekiangense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cynanchum chekiangense has twining, herbaceous stems with lanceolate to ovate leaves that possess entire margins and acute apices. The foliage is typically arranged in an alternate pattern along the slender climbing vines. Small, actinomorphic flowers emerge from the axils, often featuring a corona characteristic of the Apocynaceae family. The plant produces elongated, follicle-type fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds. These morphological traits allow the species to effectively navigate through surrounding vegetation in its native habitat.
