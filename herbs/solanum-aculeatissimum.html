Solanum aculeatissimum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Solanum aculeatislimum has narrow, linear leaves that are significantly longer than they are wide and possess entire or slightly wavy margins. These foliage structures are characterized by their acute tips and a smooth, somewhat leathery texture. The plant typically displays a shrubby growth habit with branching stems that may feature small prickles depending on the specific variety. Its inflorescence consists of small, star-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale lavender in color. The resulting fruit is a small berry that develops from the ovary after successful pollination.

      Solanum aculeatissimum is prepared primarily as a decoction, a concentrated liquid extract made by boiling the plant parts in water to release its active medicinal compounds.
