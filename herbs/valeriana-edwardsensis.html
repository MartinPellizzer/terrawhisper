Valeriana edwardsensis

Valeriana edwardsensis, commonly known as a species of valerian, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

This plant is known for its potential health benefits, including reducing anxiety and stress, promoting relaxation, and improving sleep quality.

The therapeutic actions of Valeriana edwardsensis are attributed to its sedative, anxiolytic, and antispasmodic properties, which are often utilized in herbal medicine to treat a range of conditions.

The bioactive constituents of this plant include valepotriates, valerenic acid, and isovaleric acid, among others, which contribute to its medicinal properties.
