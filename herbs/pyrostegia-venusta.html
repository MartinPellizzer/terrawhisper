Pyrostegia venusta

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pyrostegia venusta has thick, succulent leaves that are lanceolate in shape and possess a waxy, glaucous coating to prevent water loss. The stems are woody at the base and exhibit a branching pattern that supports the heavy foliage. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils and are typically characterized by a vibrant pigmentation. The root system is fibrous and adapted to penetrate rocky or sandy substrates efficiently. These morphological traits collectively allow the plant to thrive in arid environments.

      Pyrostegia venusta is prepared as a concentrated decoction, a topical poultice made from crushed leaves, or a dried powder for encapsulation.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Pyrostegia venusta can cause severe skin irritation, localized swelling, and intense burning sensations upon direct contact with the epidermis. These dermatological reactions often manifest as bright red rashes or even small blistering patches if the sap remains on the skin for an extended period. If the plant's oils are accidentally ingested, they may trigger acute gastrointestinal distress including nausea and abdominal cramping. Some individuals may also experience respiratory discomfort if they inhale the microscopic pollen released during the flowering season. Monitoring for these symptoms is essential to prevent long-term allergic sensitization.
