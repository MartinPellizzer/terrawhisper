Pilea plataniflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Pilea plataniflora?

Pilea plataniflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Rosales. This plant is further categorized under the family Urticaceae, which places it within the genus Pilea, ultimately reaching its specific identification as the species plataniflora.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pilea plataniflora has opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that measure up to 10 centimeters long and possess distinctly serrated margins and prominent pinnate venation. The foliage displays a vibrant green color with a slightly textured surface that feels somewhat fleshy to the touch. Its stems are slender and herbaceous, growing in a mounding habit that allows the plant to spread across the forest floor. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in axillary clusters, typically lacking showy petals. This morphology allows the plant to thrive in the shaded, humid understory of its native tropical habitat.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires bright, indirect sunlight and consistently moist, well-draining soil to thrive in a home environment.

You should avoid placing it in direct afternoon sun, as the delicate leaves are prone to scorching. Watering should occur whenever the top inch of soil feels dry to the touch, but you must ensure the pot has adequate drainage to prevent root rot. Maintaining a warm room temperature between 65 and 75 degrees Fahrenheit is ideal for its growth.
