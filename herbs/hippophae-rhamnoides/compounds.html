Sea Buckthorn (Hippophae rhamnoides) Compounds

Sea Buckthorn contains various active compounds, including flavonoids, which act as antioxidants. Phenolic acids and tannins contribute to its anti-inflammatory properties. Terpenoids and saponins support immune function, while glycosides and essential oils have antimicrobial effects. Coumarin and anthocyanin also play roles in its therapeutic benefits. Alkaloids are present in smaller amounts but contribute to its medicinal profile.

Sea Buckthorn (Hippophae rhamnoides) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Sea Buckthorn (Hippophae rhamnoides). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting skin health and wound healing. These compounds are abundant in the berries and oils of Sea Buckthorn, making them key to its traditional and modern medicinal uses.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in Sea Buckthorn (Hippophae rhamnoides). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports the plant's traditional use in promoting skin health and reducing inflammation.

3. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Sea Buckthorn (Hippophae rhamnoides). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and supports skin healing and digestive health. Tannins in Sea Buckthorn are primarily found in the berries and oils, offering antioxidant and anti-inflammatory benefits.

4. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a major class of bioactive compounds found in Sea Buckthorn (Hippophae rhamnoides). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting skin health and wound healing. These compounds are abundant in the berries and oils of the plant.

5. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Sea Buckthorn (Hippophae rhamnoides). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponins also enhance the bioavailability of other nutrients in Sea Buckthorn. These compounds are found in the berries and leaves of the plant.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Sea Buckthorn (Hippophae rhamnoides). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Glycosides in Sea Buckthorn support skin health and wound healing. They are also involved in the plant's ability to reduce oxidative stress in the body.

7. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound of Sea Buckthorn (Hippophae rhamnoides). It contains volatile aromatic compounds with antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The oil is extracted from the berries and has been used traditionally to support skin health and digestive function. Its therapeutic potential is well-documented in herbal medicine.

8. Coumarin

Coumarin is a naturally occurring compound found in Sea Buckthorn (Hippophae rhamnoides). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and anticoagulant properties. Coumarin also acts as a mild sedative and may support cardiovascular health. Its presence enhances the therapeutic profile of Sea Buckthorn in herbal medicine.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Sea Buckthorn (Hippophae rhamnoides). It contributes to the fruit's vibrant color and possesses strong antioxidant properties. Found in the berries, anthocyanin supports skin health, reduces inflammation, and may aid in wound healing. Its presence enhances the plant's reputation in traditional herbal medicine.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloids are a class of naturally occurring nitrogen-containing compounds found in Sea Buckthorn (Hippophae rhamnoides). While not the primary active component, certain alkaloids contribute to the plant's medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. They are present in lower concentrations compared to other bioactive compounds like flavonoids and carotenoids.
