Vitis chungii

What's the taxonomical classification of Vitis chungii?

Vitis chungii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Vitales and is a member of the family Vitaceae. Finally, its taxonomic identity is completed within the genus Vitis, specifically designated as the species chungii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vitis chungii has woody, climbing vines that feature palmately lobed leaves with three to five distinct lobes and serrated margins. The leaf texture is typically somewhat thin, and the petioles are often long and slender to facilitate climbing. Its stems are characterized by a brownish bark that becomes increasingly rugged as the plant matures. Small, greenish or yellowish flowers emerge in clusters, eventually developing into small, globose berries. These fruits change color during ripening, transitioning from green to a dark purple or black hue.
