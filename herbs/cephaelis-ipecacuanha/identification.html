Botanical Identification of Ipecac (Cephaelis ipecacuanha)

To identify Ipecac (Cephaelis ipecacuanha) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Cephaelis ipecacuanha belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Rubiales, family Rubiaceae, genus Cephaelis, and species Cephaelis ipecacuanha. These classifications provide a systematic framework for understanding its botanical relationships and characteristics.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Ipecac is Cephaelis ipecacuanha, first described by the botanist Carl Friedrich von Schweinfurth in 1867. This name is widely accepted in modern botanical taxonomy. Several synonyms exist, reflecting historical classification changes. Common names include Ipecac, vomiter, and Spanish fly, while regional and historical names vary by culture and era.

Morphological Characteristics

Cephaelis ipecacuanha is a shrub with a branched root system. Its stems are erect and slightly hairy. Leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin. The inflorescence is a dense cluster of flowers. Flowers are yellow with a red throat and five petals. The fruit is a capsule containing small, dark seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Cephaelis ipecacuanha can be distinguished by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and yellowish-green stems. It produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters, and its distinctive, bitter root is commonly used in traditional medicine. It is often confused with other Cephaelis species, but its leaf shape and root characteristics help in accurate identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Cephaelis ipecacuanha is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. It thrives in warm, humid climates with high rainfall. The plant prefers well-drained, fertile soils and is typically found at altitudes between 500 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Cephaelis ipecacuanha blooms from July to September, producing clusters of yellow flowers. Seed formation occurs from October to December. For identification purposes, the plant is typically harvested during its flowering period, which is crucial for accurate botanical recognition.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Cephaelis ipecacuanha belongs to the Rubiaceae family, closely related to species like Cephaelis ipecacuanha var. puberula and Cephaelis laevigata. It shares morphological traits with other Rubiaceae members, such as opposite leaves and small flowers, but differs in leaf pubescence and floral structure. Its genus relations highlight its placement within the tribe Ipecacuanheae, distinguishing it from other Rubiaceae genera.
