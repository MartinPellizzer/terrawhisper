Liverleaf (Hepatica nobilis) Compounds

The primary compounds in Liverleaf include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and phenolic acids, known for their anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins and glycosides contribute to its astringent and bitter qualities. Coumarin and terpenoids may support circulatory health, while saponins and anthocyanins offer additional antioxidant benefits. Essential oils and alkaloids contribute to its overall medicinal profile.

Liverleaf (Hepatica nobilis) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Liverleaf (Hepatica nobilis) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help support liver function and may aid in detoxification processes. 2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid in Liverleaf (Hepatica nobilis) contributes to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
