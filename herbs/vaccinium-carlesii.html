Vaccinium carlesii

What's the taxonomical classification of Vaccinium carlesii?

Vaccinium carlesii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the order Ericales and the family Ericaceae. Ultimately, it is identified by the genus Vaccinium and the specific epithet carlesii, forming its unique scientific name.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Vaccinium carlesii contains various bioactive phytochemicals including anthocyanins, quercetin, and various phenolic acids that serve as potent antioxidants. These specific compounds work by neutralizing free radicals and reducing oxidative stress within cellular structures. The presence of flavonoids in the berries may also contribute to anti-inflammatory properties that support vascular health. Additionally, the plant contains small amounts of tannins which are often associated with astringent and antimicrobial effects. These combined chemical constituents offer potential therapeutic benefits similar to other species within the blueberry genus.

What are the therapeutic actions of Vaccinium carlesii?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments related to digestive issues, urinary tract infections, and oxidative stress. Although it is primarily known as an ornamental shrub, its berries contain phytochemicals that may offer health benefits. These compounds are thought to help manage inflammation and support cardiovascular wellness. Some studies suggest that the antioxidants found in the fruit can assist in preventing cellular damage. However, much of its medicinal application remains within the realm of traditional herbal practices.
