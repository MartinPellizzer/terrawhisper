Common Plantain

Plantago major

Common Plantain (Plantago major) is a widespread herbaceous plant known for its broad leaves and ability to thrive in various environments, and it has been traditionally used to treat skin conditions and respiratory ailments.

Botanical Identification

Plantago major, commonly known as common plantain, belongs to the Plantaginaceae family. It is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. The plant has broad, oval leaves with a hairy underside and produces small, clustered flowers. It is also known as broadleaf plantain or white deadnettle in some regions.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Liliopsida
  • Order: Plantaginales
  • Family: Plantaginaceae
  • Genus: Plantago
  • Species: Plantago major

Active Compounds

Common Plantain contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids. These compounds work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Their combined action supports the herb's traditional use in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Tannin
  • Phenolic acid

Preparation Methods

Powder: Used for respiratory and digestive tract ailments.

Infusion: Used for respiratory and digestive tract ailments.

Decoction: Used for digestive and urinary tract ailments.

Safety Profile

Common Plantain is generally safe when used appropriately. However, it may cause allergic reactions in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and should be avoided by those with known allergies to the plant family. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
