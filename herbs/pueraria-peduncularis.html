Pueraria peduncularis

What's the taxonomical classification of Pueraria peduncularis?

Pueraria peduncularis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this classification, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its higher taxonomic ranks, the plant is part of the order Fabales and the family Fabaceae. Finally, its specific identity is defined by the genus Pueraria and the species peduncularis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pueraria peduncularis has a climbing, perennial habit characterized by trifoliate leaves with small, ovate leaflets and axillary inflorescences borne on long, slender peduncles. The plant produces small, papilionaceous flowers that are typically pale purple or violet in color. Its stems are twining and woody at the base, allowing the vine to scramble over surrounding vegetation. The root system includes a specialized tuberous structure that aids in nutrient storage. These morphological features distinguish it from other members of the Fabaceae family.
