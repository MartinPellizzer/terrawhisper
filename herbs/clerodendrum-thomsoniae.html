Clerodendrum thomsoniae

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clerodendrum thomsoniae has an evergreen, shrubby growth habit characterized by opposite, ovate leaves that measure several inches in length and possess entire margins. The foliage features a deep green color and a slightly textured surface that provides a lush backdrop for its floral displays. Its most distinctive morphological feature is the showy inflorescence consisting of white, tubular calyxes that contrast sharply with the bright red, five-lobed corollas. These flowers emerge from terminal clusters and are supported by slender, woody stems that allow the plant to reach moderate heights. The overall structure of the plant is compact yet sprawling, making it a visually complex specimen in a garden setting.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical regions of West Africa, spanning from Sierra Leone to Ethiopia. It thrives in warm, humid climates characterized by consistent moisture and shade. While its origins are strictly African, it has been widely naturalized in other tropical and subtropical zones around the world. Many gardeners in Southeast Asia, the Caribbean, and parts of South America now cultivate it as an ornamental species.       What are the herbal preparations of Clerodendrum thomsoniae?

      Clerodendrum thomsoniae is prepared as aqueous decoctions, infused teas, or topical poultices using the fresh leaves and stems of the plant.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Clerodendrum thomsoniae can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, or localized rashes when its sap comes into direct contact with sensitive human skin. While the plant is primarily grown for its ornamental beauty, the chemical compounds within its tissues may trigger redness or itching in certain individuals. There is limited clinical data regarding the systemic toxicity of this species if ingested, so caution is advised against consuming any part of it. People with known hypersensitivity to similar flowering shrubs should handle the plant with care to avoid adverse reactions. Always wear gloves when pruning or handling the stems to minimize the risk of dermatological issues.
