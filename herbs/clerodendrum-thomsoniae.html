Clerodendrum thomsoniae

What's the taxonomical classification of Clerodendrum thomsoniae?

Clerodendrum thomsoniae belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Clerodendrum, and its unique species name, thomsoniae.

Furthermore, certain constituents demonstrate affinity for neurotransmitter receptors, which contributes to the plant's observed sedative and analgesic properties. What are the herbal preparations of Clerodendrum thomsoniae?

Clerodendrum thomsoniae is prepared as aqueous decoctions, infused teas, or topical poultices using the fresh leaves and stems of the plant. These herbal preparations are primarily utilized to extract bioactive compounds for medicinal applications. The leaves can be boiled in water to create a concentrated liquid used for internal or external consumption. Additionally, crushed fresh foliage is often applied directly to the skin as a paste to treat localized inflammation. Practitioners may also create tinctures by soaking the plant parts in alcohol to preserve their therapeutic properties over longer periods.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Clerodendrum thomsoniae can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, or localized rashes when its sap comes into direct contact with sensitive human skin. While the plant is primarily grown for its ornamental beauty, the chemical compounds within its tissues may trigger redness or itching in certain individuals. There is limited clinical data regarding the systemic toxicity of this species if ingested, so caution is advised against consuming any part of it. People with known hypersensitivity to similar flowering shrubs should handle the plant with care to avoid adverse reactions. Always wear gloves when pruning or handling the stems to minimize the risk of dermatological issues.
