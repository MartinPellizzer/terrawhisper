Clerodendrum thomsoniae

What's the taxonomical classification of Clerodendrum thomsoniae?

Clerodendrum thomsoniae belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Clerodendrum, and its unique species name, thomsoniae.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clerodendrum thomsoniae has an evergreen, shrubby growth habit characterized by opposite, ovate leaves that measure several inches in length and possess entire margins. The foliage features a deep green color and a slightly textured surface that provides a lush backdrop for its floral displays. Its most distinctive morphological feature is the showy inflorescence consisting of white, tubular calyxes that contrast sharply with the bright red, five-lobed corollas. These flowers emerge from terminal clusters and are supported by slender, woody stems that allow the plant to reach moderate heights. The overall structure of the plant is compact yet sprawling, making it a visually complex specimen in a garden setting.
