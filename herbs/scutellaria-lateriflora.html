Scutellaria (Scutellaria lateriflora): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

Scutellaria lateriflora, commonly known as blue skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States and is widely used in traditional herbal medicine for its calming and anti-inflammatory properties.

What is Scutellaria (Scutellaria Lateriflora)?

Scutellaria lateriflora, commonly known as blue skullcap, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States, characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of blue or violet flowers.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Scutellaria is Scutellaria lateriflora. This plant belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Lamiales, order Lamiales, family Lamiaceae, genus Scutellaria, and species Scutellaria lateriflora. It is commonly known as blueweed in North America and skullcap in Europe. The plant is also referred to by synonyms such as Scutellaria americana and Scutellaria barbata.

Plant Description and Morphology

Scutellaria is a perennial herb that grows to a height of 30-60 cm, with a compact, upright stem that is usually branched towards the top.

Its leaves are lanceolate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin, and are arranged alternately along the stem. The flowers are blue to violet in color, with a distinctive bilabiate (two-lipped) structure, and are clustered in whorls along the stem. The root system consists of a short, thick rhizome with fibrous roots, which is often used in herbal preparations.

The plant has a mild, slightly bitter scent, with rough, hairy stems and leaves that are slightly pubescent.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Scutellaria lateriflora, also known as blue skullcap, is native to the eastern and central United States, particularly found in moist, wooded areas and along stream banks from New England to the Gulf Coast.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The aerial parts of Scutellaria lateriflora, including the leaves, flowers, and stems, are primarily used medicinally.
