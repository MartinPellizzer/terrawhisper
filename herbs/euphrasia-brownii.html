Euphrasia brownii

Euphrasia brownii, also known as the marsh trefoil or eyebright, is a perennial plant native to Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the gentian family and is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly its ability to soothe and calm the eyes.

The plant's leaves, flowers, and roots have been used in herbal preparations to treat eye infections, inflammation, and respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and asthma, due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties.

Euphrasia brownii can be consumed as a tea, tincture, or salve to take advantage of its health benefits, including reducing eye strain, improving vision, and alleviating symptoms of colds and flu.
