10 Possible Side Effects Of Scutellaria Galericulata (Skullcap)

Scutellaria galericulata has some side effects when used improperly, such as headaches, dizziness, and nausea.

These side effects are often caused by consuming high doses or combining it with other medications. For instance, someone who is taking blood thinners may experience worsening of bleeding, leading to accidental injuries or uncontrolled bleeding during surgeries.

Furthermore, the potential interactions with sedatives and antidepressants can exacerbate anxiety and depression.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Scutellaria galericulata if used imporperly.

This can lead to symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing in some individuals.

Additionally, the plant's flavonoids and phenolic acids may cause an allergic response in people who are sensitive to these compounds, leading to further complications.

Additionally, the herb's antioxidant properties may contribute to the decrease in blood pressure by reducing oxidative stress and inflammation.
