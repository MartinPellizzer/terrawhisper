Scutellaria (Scutellaria galericulata) Compounds

Scutellaria contains several primary compounds, including flavonoids, glycosides, and alkaloids. These compounds contribute to its medicinal properties, such as anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Other notable components include phenolic acids, coumarins, and tannins, which support its use in traditional herbal medicine.

Scutellaria (Scutellaria galericulata) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Scutellaria galericulata. They contribute to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and neuroprotective effects. These compounds are often studied for their potential therapeutic applications in herbal medicine.

2. Glycoside

Scutellaria galericulata contains the glycoside scutellarein, which exhibits anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. This compound contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments. Scutellarein is also being studied for its potential in modern pharmacological applications.

Scutellaria galericulata contains the alkaloid baicalin, which is known for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive disorders. Baicalin is also being studied for its potential in modern pharmacological applications.

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Scutellaria galericulata, contributing to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
