Botanical Identification of Scutellaria (Scutellaria galericulata)

To identify Scutellaria (Scutellaria galericulata) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Scutellaria galericulata belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Lamiales, order Lamiales, family Lamiaceae, genus Scutellaria, and species Scutellaria galericulata. It is a member of the mint family, known for its medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal medicine.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Scutellaria galericulata is the scientific name for the species, first described by the botanist Thomas Nuttall in 1818. It is also known by several synonyms, including Scutellaria galericulata var. galericulata and Scutellaria galericulata var. leucantha. Common names include blue skullcap and Indian skullcap, while regional and historical names vary across different cultures and regions.

Morphological Characteristics

Scutellaria galericulata is a perennial herb with a short, rhizomatous root system. It has square, erect stems that are hairy and branching. Leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and slightly hairy. The inflorescence is a dense spike of blue to purple flowers. Each flower has two lips, with the upper lip being smaller. The fruit is a capsule containing small, black seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Scutellaria galericulata can be distinguished by its distinctive blue or violet flowers with dark veins, narrow lanceolate leaves, and a square, hairy stem. It differs from similar species like Scutellaria lateriflora by its smaller flowers and shorter calyx lobes. The presence of a glandular-pubescent stem also aids in identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Scutellaria galericulata is native to temperate regions of Asia, particularly China and the Himalayas. It thrives in cool, moist climates with partial shade. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils and is commonly found at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Scutellaria galericulata blooms from June to August, producing blue to violet flowers. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early autumn. For identification purposes, the plant is best harvested during its flowering period for accurate botanical recognition.
