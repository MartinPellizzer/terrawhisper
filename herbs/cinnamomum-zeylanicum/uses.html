Ceylon Cinnamon (Cinnamomum zeylanicum) Uses

Ceylon Cinnamon is used to support respiratory health by addressing conditions like bronchitis, cough, common cold, asthma, and respiratory infections. It also aids in diabetes management, helps lower hypertension, and may reduce high cholesterol and atherosclerosis due to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

Ceylon Cinnamon (Cinnamomum zeylanicum) Monograph

1. Bronchitis

Ceylon Cinnamon may help alleviate bronchitis symptoms by reducing inflammation and supporting respiratory health. Its antimicrobial and antioxidant properties can help combat infections and soothe irritated airways. It is often used in herbal teas or steam inhalations to ease coughing and congestion.

2. Cough

Ceylon cinnamon has been traditionally used to soothe coughs due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce mucus production and ease throat irritation. When brewed as a tea, it can provide relief for both dry and productive coughs. Its mild warming effect also supports respiratory comfort.

3. Common cold

Ceylon Cinnamon may help alleviate common cold symptoms by supporting immune function and reducing inflammation. Its antimicrobial properties may inhibit viral replication, while its warming effect can ease congestion. Studies suggest it may shorten the duration of colds when used alongside other supportive therapies.

4. Asthma

Ceylon Cinnamon may help alleviate asthma symptoms by reducing inflammation and relaxing airway muscles. It contains compounds like eugenol and cinnamaldehyde that have bronchodilating properties. These effects support respiratory health and may reduce the frequency of asthma attacks when used as part of a holistic approach.

5. Respiratory infections

Ceylon Cinnamon supports respiratory health by reducing inflammation and combating pathogens. Its antimicrobial and antioxidant properties help alleviate symptoms of colds, coughs, and bronchitis. It can be used in teas or inhalations to soothe congestion and boost immune function. Regular use may prevent respiratory infections and support overall lung health.

6. Diabetes

Ceylon Cinnamon has been shown to improve insulin sensitivity and reduce blood sugar levels in people with diabetes. Its active compounds, such as procyanidins and cinnamic acid, may help regulate glucose metabolism. Studies suggest it can complement conventional diabetes treatments when used as part of a holistic approach.

7. Diabetes management

Ceylon Cinnamon has shown potential in diabetes management by improving insulin sensitivity and reducing blood sugar levels. Its active compounds, such as polyphenols, may help regulate glucose metabolism. Studies suggest it can complement conventional treatments, though more research is needed for definitive conclusions.

8. Hypertension

Ceylon Cinnamon has been traditionally used to support cardiovascular health. It contains compounds that may help lower blood pressure by improving circulation and reducing oxidative stress. Regular consumption may contribute to managing hypertension when used as part of a balanced herbal regimen.

9. High cholesterol

Ceylon Cinnamon has been shown to help lower high cholesterol levels by reducing LDL (bad cholesterol) and increasing HDL (good cholesterol). Its active compounds, such as cinnamic acid and polyphenols, may inhibit cholesterol absorption and promote healthy lipid metabolism. Regular consumption of Ceylon Cinnamon in moderate amounts may support cardiovascular health.

10. Atherosclerosis

Ceylon Cinnamon contains compounds like cinnamaldehyde and polyphenols that may reduce inflammation and oxidative stress, key factors in atherosclerosis. Studies suggest it can lower LDL cholesterol and improve endothelial function, potentially slowing plaque formation in arterial walls. Its antioxidant properties may further support cardiovascular health by preventing lipid oxidation.
