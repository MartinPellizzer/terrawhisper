Ceylon Cinnamon (Cinnamomum zeylanicum) Compounds

The primary compounds in Ceylon Cinnamon include essential oil, which contains cinnamaldehyde, and phenolic acids like cinnamic acid. Flavonoids, such as quercetin, contribute to its antioxidant properties. Terpenoids and coumarin provide antimicrobial effects, while tannins and glycosides support anti-inflammatory actions. Saponins, alkaloids, and anthocyanins also play roles in its medicinal benefits.

Ceylon Cinnamon (Cinnamomum zeylanicum) Monograph

1. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound of Ceylon Cinnamon (Cinnamomum zeylanicum). It contains cinnamaldehyde and other volatile components with antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties. This oil is used in herbal medicine for its therapeutic effects on respiratory and digestive health.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Ceylon Cinnamon (Cinnamomum zeylanicum). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. This compound helps support immune function and may aid in managing blood sugar levels. Its presence enhances the therapeutic potential of Ceylon Cinnamon in herbal medicine.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Ceylon Cinnamon (Cinnamomum zeylanicum). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds support the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and respiratory health. Flavonoids also enhance the overall therapeutic potential of Ceylon Cinnamon.

4. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Ceylon Cinnamon (Cinnamomum zeylanicum). They contribute to the plant's aromatic profile and have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds are believed to support the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and respiratory health.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a bioactive compound found in Ceylon Cinnamon (Cinnamomum zeylanicum). It exhibits anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory properties, contributing to the plant's traditional use in promoting cardiovascular health and reducing inflammation. Coumarin also acts as a natural preservative and may support metabolic functions.

6. Tannin

Tannin is a key compound in Ceylon Cinnamon (Cinnamomum zeylanicum) that contributes to its astringent properties. It helps in reducing inflammation and supporting gut health by binding to proteins and neutralizing harmful substances in the digestive tract.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Ceylon Cinnamon (Cinnamomum zeylanicum). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by supporting metabolic functions and may aid in managing blood sugar levels. Glycosides are known for their potential therapeutic benefits in traditional herbal medicine.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Ceylon Cinnamon (Cinnamomum zeylanicum). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponins may support digestive health and immune function, making them valuable in herbal medicine.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Ceylon Cinnamon (Cinnamomum zeylanicum). It contributes to the plant's pharmacological properties, though it is not as prominent as other bioactive components like cinnamaldehyde. Alkaloids in cinnamon may support traditional uses in herbal medicine, though research on their specific therapeutic effects remains limited.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Ceylon Cinnamon (Cinnamomum zeylanicum). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting immune function and reducing oxidative stress. While not the primary active component, anthocyanin enhances the overall therapeutic potential of Ceylon Cinnamon in herbal medicine.
