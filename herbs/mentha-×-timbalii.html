Mentha × timbalii

Mentha × timbalii, commonly known as the hybrid peppermint or timbal mint, is a sterile interspecific hybrid of the peppermint (Mentha piperita) and watermint (Mentha aquatica) plants, resulting in a unique and distinct species.

This plant is valued for its numerous health benefits, including its ability to alleviate digestive issues, reduce stress and anxiety, and provide relief from cold and flu symptoms.

The therapeutic actions of Mentha × timbalii include its antispasmodic, antiviral, and antibacterial properties, which contribute to its use in treating various health conditions.

The bioactive constituents responsible for these effects include menthone, limonene, and menthol, which are volatile oils extracted from the plant's leaves and stems.
