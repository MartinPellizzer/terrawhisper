Neolitsea aurata

What's the taxonomical classification of Neolitsea aurata?

Neolitsea auraata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the lineage of the order Laurales. The plant is further categorized under the family Lauraceae, which places it within the genus Neolitsea, ultimately identifying it as the specific species Neolitsea auraata.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Neolitsea auraata can cause contact dermatitis, skin irritation, and allergic reactions upon direct exposure to its leaves or sap. These dermatological issues often manifest as redness, itching, or localized swelling where the plant has touched the skin. Some individuals may experience more severe hypersensitivity if the essential oils are inhaled or ingested. Because specific toxicological data is limited, caution should be exercised when handling any part of this species. Proper protective gear is recommended to mitigate the risk of accidental skin contact.
