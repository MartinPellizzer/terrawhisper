Rorippa islandica

What's the taxonomical classification of Rorippa islandica?

Rorippa islandica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Brassicales. Furthermore, it is a member of the family Brassicaceae, falling specifically under the genus Rorippa, with its unique designation as the species islandica.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Rorippa islandica contains high concentrations of bioactive compounds including iridoid glycosides, flavonoids, and specific phenolic acids that provide therapeutic benefits. These chemical constituents work together to offer significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties to the human body. The presence of iridoids is particularly important for its potential antimicrobial effects against various pathogens. Furthermore, the antioxidant profile helps neutralize free radicals, which can reduce oxidative stress in cells. Researchers continue to study these specific molecules to determine their precise applications in modern pharmacology.

What are the therapeutic actions of Rorippa islandica?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat respiratory conditions such as coughs, bronchitis, and sore throats, as well as gastrointestinal issues like gastritis and intestinal inflammation. The mucilaginous properties of the plant help soothe irritated mucous membranes in the throat and digestive tract. It is also frequently applied to skin irritations and minor wounds due to its antiseptic qualities. Some traditional practices utilize its extracts to help alleviate symptoms of fever and general malaise. These therapeutic applications are primarily driven by the plant's high concentration of soothing polysaccharides.
