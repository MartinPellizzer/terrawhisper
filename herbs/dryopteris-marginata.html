Dryopteris marginata

What's the taxonomical classification of Dryopteris marginata?

Dryopteris marginata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the family Polypodiaceae, eventually reaching the genus Dryopteris, where it is specifically identified by the species name marginata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dryopteris marginata has bipinnate-pinnatifid fronds that typically measure 30 to 90 centimeters in length, characterized by a distinctively serrated or marginate appearance along the pinnule edges. The stipes are long and slender, often displaying a brownish or reddish hue near the base. Each frond is supported by a central rachis that is slightly hairy or scaly, providing structural stability to the spreading foliage. The texture of the blades is relatively thin and membranous, allowing for efficient light absorption in shaded forest environments. These morphological features collectively distinguish this species from other members of the wood fern family.
