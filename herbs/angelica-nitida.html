Angelica nitida

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Angelica nitida has a robust, perennial structure characterized by hollow, ridged stems that can reach significant heights and large, compound leaves with deeply serrated leaflets. The foliage is typically dark green and displays a distinctively coarse texture. Its inflorescence consists of large, flat-topped umbels composed of numerous tiny, creamy-white flowers. These floral clusters are supported by thick peduncles that emerge from the leaf axils. The root system is sturdy and fleshy, providing the plant with essential stability and nutrient storage.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe, specifically spanning from the Alps and the Pyrenees to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically inhabits high-altitude alpine and subalpine zones where rocky terrain and moist soil are abundant. Within these habitats, it thrives in crevices of limestone massifs and along mountain streams. The species is often found at elevations ranging from mid-mountain levels up to the alpine tundra. Its distribution is largely restricted to these cool, temperate mountain ecosystems across the continent.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Angelica nitida contains coumarins, essential oils, and various polysaccharides that serve as its primary bioactive constituents. These chemical compounds are believed to provide significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties to the plant. Research suggests that the presence of specific coumarin derivatives may contribute to its traditional use in treating skin conditions and promoting circulation. What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Angelica nitida targets various signaling proteins and enzymes including cyclooxygenase-2, inducible nitric oxide synthase, and various inflammatory cytokines to exert its pharmacological effects. The plant contains bioactive compounds like coumarins and flavonoids that interfere with the arachidonic acid metabolism pathway. By inhibiting cyclooxygenase-2, these molecules significantly reduce the production of pro-inflammatory prostaglandins. They also interact with nuclear factor kappa B to modulate the immune response and decrease systemic inflammation. These interactions with specific cellular proteins underpin the plant's traditional use in treating pain and swelling.

What are the therapeutic actions of Angelica nitida?

Angelica nitida has therapeutic properties that include acting as a bitter, carminative, stomachic, expectorant, diaphoretic, and antispasmodic agent. Its bitter components stimulate digestive secretions to improve overall appetite and gastric function. As a carminative and stomachic, it effectively relieves gas and aids in the digestion of food. The plant also functions as an expectorant to help clear respiratory secretions from the lungs. Furthermore, it serves as a diaphoretic to promote sweating and an antispasmodic to soothe muscle contractions.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause nausea, digestive upset, and increased photosensitivity in individuals who consume it. The presence of furanocoumarins in the plant material can lead to skin reactions when exposed to sunlight. Many users also report gastrointestinal discomfort following ingestion of the botanical. These digestive side effects can range from mild stomach aches to more significant nausea. Monitoring light exposure is recommended to prevent potential skin irritation or burns.

The main side effects this plant can have are shown in the list below.

  • Digestive upset
  • Photosensitivity
