Angelica nitida

What's the taxonomical classification of Angelica nitida?

Angelica nitida belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is placed in the order Apiales and the family Apiaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Angelica and the specific species epithet nitida.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Angelica nitida has a robust, perennial structure characterized by hollow, ridged stems that can reach significant heights and large, compound leaves with deeply serrated leaflets. The foliage is typically dark green and displays a distinctively coarse texture. Its inflorescence consists of large, flat-topped umbels composed of numerous tiny, creamy-white flowers. These floral clusters are supported by thick peduncles that emerge from the leaf axils. The root system is sturdy and fleshy, providing the plant with essential stability and nutrient storage.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe, specifically spanning from the Alps and the Pyrenees to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically inhabits high-altitude alpine and subalpine zones where rocky terrain and moist soil are abundant. Within these habitats, it thrives in crevices of limestone massifs and along mountain streams. The species is often found at elevations ranging from mid-mountain levels up to the alpine tundra. Its distribution is largely restricted to these cool, temperate mountain ecosystems across the continent.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds byakangelicin(IX), cnidilin(III), imperatorin(II), isoimperatorin(I), and isopimine de llin(V). These specific chemical constituents belong to the group of coumarins found within the species. Each of these bioactive molecules contributes to the diverse pharmacological profile of the plant. Researchers study these substances to understand their potential therapeutic applications in various medical fields. The concentration of these compounds can vary depending on the environmental conditions in which the plant grows.

According to a study published by "Zhong yao cai = Zhongyaocai = Journal of Chinese medicinal materials", nine compounds were isolated from the roots of Angelica nitida. These constituents were identified through spectral analysis after being isolated by silica gel. The specific compounds identified include isoimperatorin(I), imperatorin(II), cnidilin(III), beta-sitosterol(IV), isopimpinellin(V), phellopterin(VI), neobyakangelicol(VII), (3S)-2,2-dimethyl-3,5-dihydroxy-8-hydroxylmethyl-3,4-dihydro-2H,6H-benzo[1,2-b: 5,4-b'] dipyran-6-one(VIII), and byakangelicin(IX). All 9 of these compounds were isolated from the plant for the first time.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause nausea, digestive upset, and increased photosensitivity in individuals who consume it. The presence of furanocoumarins in the plant material can lead to skin reactions when exposed to sunlight. Many users also report gastrointestinal discomfort following ingestion of the botanical. These digestive side effects can range from mild stomach aches to more significant nausea. Monitoring light exposure is recommended to prevent potential skin irritation or burns.
