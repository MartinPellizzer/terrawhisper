Photinia serratifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Photinia serratifolia?

Photinia serratifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Rosales and the family Rosaceae. Specifically, this plant is identified by the genus Photinia and is distinguished as the species serratifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Photinia serratifolia has elliptic to oblong leaves that are typically 5 to 15 centimeters long, featuring serrated margins and a glossy, dark green upper surface with a paler underside. The plant is a woody shrub or small tree characterized by its upright growth habit and slender, brownish branches. Its leaves are arranged alternately along the stems and possess distinct pinnate venation. Small, white, five-petaled flowers bloom in terminal clusters, often appearing during the warmer months. Following the flowering period, the plant produces small, fleshy drupes that transition in color as they mature.

      What side effects this plant can have?

      This plant can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, including vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea, if any part of it is ingested. The toxicity primarily stems from the presence of cyanogenic glycosides within its tissues. When these compounds are broken down by enzymes in the digestive tract, they can release hydrogen cyanide into the body. Such exposure may lead to more serious systemic symptoms like dizziness, headaches, or even respiratory failure in extreme cases. Contact with the plant may also cause skin irritation or allergic reactions in sensitive individuals.
