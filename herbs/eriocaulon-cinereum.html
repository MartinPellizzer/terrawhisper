Eriocaulon cinereum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eriocaulon cinereum has a perennial growth habit characterized by a basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves that emerge from a short rhizome. The leaves are typically thin and grass-like, often featuring a distinctively pointed apex. The reproductive structures consist of small, white flower heads clustered tightly on a single, slender scape. These heads are surrounded by specialized bracts that protect the tiny individual florets during development. This morphological structure is adapted to its specific wetland or aquatic environment.
