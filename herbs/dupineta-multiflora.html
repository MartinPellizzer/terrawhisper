Dupineta multiflora

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dupineta multiflora has a woody stem architecture characterized by slender, branching shoots and small, ovate to elliptical leaves with entire margins. The foliage is typically arranged in an alternate or sub-opposite pattern along the stems. Each leaf possesses a distinct central vein and a smooth surface texture that varies depending on the maturity of the plant. Small, multiple flowers emerge from the axillary regions, displaying a clustered arrangement as the species name suggests. The reproductive structures are supported by delicate pedicels that allow for efficient pollination.

      Dupineta multiflora is prepared as a decoction, a dried powder, or a topical salve derived from its leaves and bark.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Dupineta multiflora can cause localized skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and mild gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Individuals with sensitive skin may experience redness, itching, or swelling upon direct contact with the plant's sap or foliage. Some users might also report respiratory discomfort if the pollen becomes airborne in enclosed spaces. It is essential to avoid contact with mucous membranes to prevent more severe inflammatory reactions. Consulting a medical professional is recommended if any adverse symptoms persist after exposure.
