Balanophora involucrata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. This plant is uniquely situated within its own dedicated family, Balanophoraceae, and is further categorized under the genus Balanophora. Ultimately, its specific biological identity is defined by its designation as the species involucrata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Balanophora involucrata has a holoparasitic morphology characterized by a fleshy, scale-like rhizome that lacks chlorophyll and a terminal inflorescence enclosed by large, overlapping bracts. This plant lacks true leaves and roots, instead utilizing specialized haustoria to extract nutrients directly from the vascular tissue of its host trees. The reproductive structure consists of a compact, brownish-red head that emerges from the ground. Surrounding this central flower cluster are prominent, leaf-like involucral bracts that protect the developing reproductive organs. The entire organism appears as a robust, fleshy protrusion emerging from the forest floor or the base of a host stem.
