Balanophora involucrata

What's the taxonomical classification of Balanophora involucrata?

Balanophora involucrata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Santalales. This plant is uniquely situated within its own dedicated family, Balanophoraceae, and is further categorized under the genus Balanophora. Ultimately, its specific biological identity is defined by its designation as the species involucrata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Balanophora involucrata has a holoparasitic morphology characterized by a fleshy, scale-like rhizome that lacks chlorophyll and a terminal inflorescence enclosed by large, overlapping bracts. This plant lacks true leaves and roots, instead utilizing specialized haustoria to extract nutrients directly from the vascular tissue of its host trees. The reproductive structure consists of a compact, brownish-red head that emerges from the ground. Surrounding this central flower cluster are prominent, leaf-like involucral bracts that protect the developing reproductive organs. The entire organism appears as a robust, fleshy protrusion emerging from the forest floor or the base of a host stem.

What are the pharmacological activities of Balanophora involucrata?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological potential through its anti-inflammatory, anti-influenza, antibacterial, and neuraminidase inhibitory activities. Research indicates that its bioactive compounds provide a defense against various bacterial pathogens and reduce systemic inflammation. Specifically, the plant exhibits potent anti-influenza properties by targeting viral replication processes. It also functions through its neuraminidase inhibitory activity, which prevents the release of new influenza virus particles from infected cells. These combined mechanisms suggest that Balanophora involucrata serves as a promising source for developing new antiviral and antimicrobial therapeutic agents.

As discussed in a study, Balanophora involucrata is known to possess potential anti-inflammatory and antibacterial activities. The ethyl acetate extract of B. involucrata exhibited significant inhibitory activity against neuraminidase with a 50% inhibitory concentration (IC50) value of 159.5 μg/mL. Using MS/MS spectra, 20 compounds were identified from the plant. Among these, two specific compounds, quercitrin and phloridzin, showed obvious inhibitory activity against neuraminidase. These two compounds demonstrated IC50 values of 311.76 μmol/L and 347.32 μmol/L, respectively. This suggests B. involucrata can be a potential natural source of neuraminidase inhibitors.
