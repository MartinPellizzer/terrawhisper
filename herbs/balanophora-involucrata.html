Balanophora involucrata

What's the taxonomical classification of Balanophora involucrata?

Balanophora involucrata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Santalales. This plant is uniquely situated within its own dedicated family, Balanophoraceae, and is further categorized under the genus Balanophora. Ultimately, its specific biological identity is defined by its designation as the species involucrata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Balanophora involucrata has a holoparasitic morphology characterized by a fleshy, scale-like rhizome that lacks chlorophyll and a terminal inflorescence enclosed by large, overlapping bracts. This plant lacks true leaves and roots, instead utilizing specialized haustoria to extract nutrients directly from the vascular tissue of its host trees. The reproductive structure consists of a compact, brownish-red head that emerges from the ground. Surrounding this central flower cluster are prominent, leaf-like involucral bracts that protect the developing reproductive organs. The entire organism appears as a robust, fleshy protrusion emerging from the forest floor or the base of a host stem.

What are the pharmacological activities of Balanophora involucrata?

This plant has demonstrated potent pharmacological activities including anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, anti-influenza, and neuraminidase inhibitory activity. Its specific ability to act as an anti-influenza agent is closely linked to its direct neuraminidase inhibitory activity. By functioning as a neuraminidase inhibitor, the plant helps prevent the spread of viral particles within a host. Furthermore, its antibacterial properties provide a defense against various bacterial pathogens. These combined medicinal effects highlight the significant therapeutic potential of Balanophora involucrata.

According to a study published by "Biomedical chromatography : BMC", Balanophora involucrata has been known to possess potential anti-inflammatory and antibacterial activities. The ethyl acetate extract of B. involucrata exhibited significant inhibitory activity against NA with a 50% inhibitory concentration (IC50) value of 159.5 μg/mL. Through MS/MS spectra, 20 compounds were identified within the plant. Among these, 2 specific compounds, quercitrin and phloridzin, showed obvious inhibitory activity against NA. These two compounds demonstrated IC50 values of 311.76 and 347.32 μmol/L, respectively.
