Guava (Psidium guajava) Actions

Guava exhibits several therapeutic actions, including anti-inflammatory, astringent, and diuretic properties. It acts as a tonic and carminative, helping to improve digestion and relieve gas. Additionally, it has antispasmodic and sedative effects, while its bitter and stimulant qualities support overall health. It also functions as an expectorant, aiding respiratory health.

Guava (Psidium guajava) Monograph

1. Anti-inflammatory

Guava has an anti-inflammatory action, primarily due to its high content of polyphenols and flavonoids. These compounds inhibit inflammatory pathways and reduce the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines. This makes guava beneficial for conditions involving chronic inflammation, such as arthritis and gastrointestinal disorders.

2. Astringent

Guava has an astringent action, which helps reduce secretions and tighten tissues. This property is particularly beneficial for treating diarrhea, hemorrhoids, and skin conditions. The astringent effect comes from compounds like tannins and flavonoids present in the fruit. It supports healing by promoting tissue contraction and reducing inflammation.

3. Diuretic

Guava has a diuretic action, promoting increased urine production and aiding in the elimination of toxins and excess fluids from the body. This effect supports kidney function and can help manage conditions like edema and hypertension. The high potassium content in guava also helps balance electrolytes during fluid loss.

4. Tonic

Guava has a tonic action, helping to strengthen and invigorate the body. It supports overall vitality and energy levels, making it beneficial for those experiencing fatigue or weakness. Its nutritional profile contributes to its restorative properties.

5. Carminative

Guava has a carminative action, which helps relieve gas and bloating by promoting the expulsion of intestinal gas. This effect is due to its volatile oils and fiber content, which stimulate digestive movement and reduce discomfort. It is commonly used in herbal remedies to support digestive health.

6. Antispasmodic

Guava has an antispasmodic action, which helps alleviate muscle spasms and cramps by relaxing smooth muscle tissue. This effect is attributed to its high content of potassium and magnesium, which regulate nerve impulses and muscle contractions. It is commonly used in traditional medicine to treat gastrointestinal spasms and other spasmodic conditions.

7. Bitter

Guava has a bitter action, which stimulates digestion and helps reduce excess dampness and phlegm in the body. This action supports the elimination of toxins and enhances the body's natural detoxification processes. It is particularly beneficial for digestive health and can aid in managing conditions like indigestion and bloating.

8. Stimulant

Guava has a stimulant action, enhancing metabolic processes and energy production. It stimulates the digestive system, improving nutrient absorption and overall vitality. This effect is attributed to its high vitamin C content and bioactive compounds that support cellular function and physical performance.

9. Expectorant

Guava has an expectorant action, helping to loosen and expel mucus from the respiratory tract. It contains compounds like flavonoids and glycosides that stimulate the secretion of bronchial mucus, aiding in the clearance of congestion. This makes it beneficial for conditions like coughs and bronchitis.

10. Sedative

Guava has a sedative action, promoting relaxation and reducing anxiety. It contains bioactive compounds that may influence the central nervous system. This effect supports its traditional use in calming the mind and aiding sleep.
