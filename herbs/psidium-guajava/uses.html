Guava (Psidium guajava) Uses

Guava is used to treat various respiratory and oral conditions. It helps alleviate coughs, sore throats, and respiratory infections. Its antibacterial properties combat bad breath and oral infections. It is also beneficial for managing symptoms of the common cold and tonsillitis.

Guava (Psidium guajava) Monograph

1. Cough

Guava leaves are traditionally used to soothe coughs due to their anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. They help reduce throat irritation and suppress coughing fits. A decoction of dried leaves is commonly prepared and consumed to alleviate symptoms. This remedy is widely practiced in herbal medicine for its gentle yet effective action.

2. Bad breath

Guava leaves contain antibacterial compounds that help reduce oral bacteria causing bad breath. Chewing fresh guava leaves or using them in mouthwash can freshen breath naturally. Regular use may help maintain oral hygiene and prevent halitosis.

3. Oral infections

Guava leaves contain high levels of tannins and flavonoids, which have antimicrobial properties. They are commonly used to treat oral infections like gum disease and throat infections. A decoction of the leaves can be applied topically or used as a mouth rinse. This traditional remedy is effective due to its ability to reduce inflammation and inhibit bacterial growth.

4. Respiratory infections

Guava leaves and fruit are used in traditional medicine to treat respiratory infections due to their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. They help reduce coughing, clear mucus, and soothe throat irritation. Guava's high vitamin C content boosts immune response, aiding recovery from colds and bronchitis. It is commonly prepared as a decoction or herbal tea.

5. Common cold

Guava leaves contain high levels of antioxidants and tannins that help reduce fever and soothe throat inflammation. They are commonly used in herbal remedies to alleviate symptoms of the common cold, such as coughing and sore throat. A decoction of fresh or dried leaves can be consumed to support immune function during cold symptoms.

6. Tonsillitis

Guava leaves are used in traditional herbal medicine to treat tonsillitis due to their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. A decoction of fresh or dried leaves is often prepared and used as a gargle to reduce throat swelling and kill bacteria causing infection. This remedy is commonly practiced in regions where guava is native, supporting local health practices.

7. Bronchitis

Guava leaves are used in traditional medicine to treat bronchitis due to their anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. They help reduce coughing and loosen mucus in the respiratory tract. A decoction of the leaves is often prepared and consumed to alleviate symptoms and support lung health.

8. Respiratory tract infections

Guava leaves and fruit are used to treat respiratory tract infections due to their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The high vitamin C content boosts immunity, while compounds like quercetin reduce inflammation and fight pathogens. Guava-based remedies are commonly used in traditional medicine to alleviate symptoms like coughing and sore throat.

9. Sore throat

Guava leaves contain high levels of tannins and flavonoids, which have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. They are traditionally used to soothe sore throats by reducing swelling and neutralizing bacteria. A decoction of fresh or dried leaves can be gargled or consumed to relieve symptoms. This remedy is commonly practiced in herbal medicine for its natural and accessible benefits.

10. Cold symptoms

Guava is used in herbal medicine to alleviate cold symptoms due to its high vitamin C content, which supports immune function. It also has antiviral and anti-inflammatory properties that may reduce congestion and soothe throat irritation. Consuming guava juice or leaves can help ease coughs and shorten the duration of colds.
