10 Medicinal Constituents Of Psidium Guajava (Guava)

Psidium guajava has active constituents such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids.

These compounds possess antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, which make guava a valuable medicinal plant. For instance, the flavonoids in guava can improve cardiovascular health by reducing cholesterol levels and blood pressure, while its antibacterial properties can help combat oral infections and skin conditions.

Overall, the medicinal properties of guava can significantly improve the quality of life for individuals suffering from various health issues.

1. Quercetin

Psidium guajava quercetin is a flavonoid compound derived from its leaves.

It has been extensively studied for its numerous biological activities, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. Quercetin has been shown to possess cardio-protective effects, inhibit cancer cell growth, and exhibit neuroprotective activity.

Its potential applications in traditional medicine and modern therapy make it an attractive compound for further research and development.

2. Epicatechin

Psidium guajava epicatechin is a potent antioxidant flavan-3-ol found in the fruit and leaves of the plant.

It has been extensively studied for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and anticancer properties. Epicatechin has been shown to exhibit significant free radical scavenging activity, making it an effective natural remedy against oxidative stress-related diseases.

Its bioactive compounds have also been reported to possess cardio-protective effects.

3. Guajavin

Psidium guajava guajavin is a small evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

Its fruit, a sweet and tart berry, is widely consumed in tropical regions and has been used for centuries in traditional medicine.

Guavas are rich in vitamins A and C, potassium, and antioxidants, making them a popular ingredient in jams, jellies, and sauces.

Psidium guajava flavonoids is a group of bioactive compounds that are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Psidium guajava anthocyanins is a type of flavonoid pigments responsible for the fruit's characteristic red, purple, and blue colors.
