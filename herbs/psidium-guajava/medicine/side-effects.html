10 Possible Side Effects Of Psidium Guajava

Psidium guajava has some side effects when used improperly, such as diarrhea, stomach cramps, and nausea, which can be caused by excessive consumption or individual intolerance.

These side effects can worsen daily life, especially for people with pre-existing gastrointestinal issues, who may experience dehydration and electrolyte imbalances, leading to fatigue, headaches, and dizziness, potentially affecting productivity and overall well-being.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Psidium guajava causes allergic reactions in some individuals due to its potential to trigger an immune response.

This can manifest as hives, itching, redness of the skin, and even anaphylaxis, a severe and potentially life-threatening reaction.

The allergenic compounds responsible for these reactions are thought to be proteins found in the fruit's skin, leaves, and pulp, which can cause the body's immune system to mistakenly attack harmless substances.

2. Triggers respiratory issues

Psidium guajava triggers respiratory issues due to its potential allergenic properties.

The plant's latex and volatile oils can cause an allergic reaction in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.

Additionally, the high levels of tannins present in guava leaves may also exacerbate existing respiratory conditions like asthma by constricting airways and increasing mucus production.

3. Increases blood pressure

Psidium guajava increases blood pressure due to its high content of flavonoids and phenolic acids.

These bioactive compounds can cause blood vessels to constrict, leading to increased blood pressure. Additionally, the plant's anthocyanins may stimulate the sympathetic nervous system, further contributing to elevated blood pressure levels.

This side effect is thought to be more pronounced in individuals who are already hypertensive or have prehypertension.

4. Produces gastrointestinal upset

Psidium guajava produces gastrointestinal upset due to its high concentration of tannins and flavonoids.

These compounds can cause irritation and inflammation in the digestive tract, leading to symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and stomach pain.

The potent antioxidant properties of Psidium guajava may also contribute to this side effect by increasing free radical production, which can further exacerbate gastrointestinal discomfort.

5. Induces vomiting episodes

Psidium guajava induces vomiting episodes due to its potential interaction with certain medications, such as sedatives and antidepressants.

The fruit's natural compounds, including psidoseminal and gallic acid, can stimulate the stomach and intestines, leading to increased secretion of digestive enzymes and altered gut motility.

This can cause nausea, abdominal cramps, and eventually, vomiting in some individuals who ingest Psidium guajava.

6. Affects liver function tests

Psidium guajava affects liver function tests.

The flavonoids present in guava leaves and fruits may contribute to this side effect by altering the liver's metabolism and detoxification processes, potentially causing changes in liver enzyme levels.

Additionally, the high concentrations of tannins and phenolic compounds in guava may also play a role in affecting liver function tests, although more research is needed to fully understand these mechanisms.

7. Lowers platelet counts

Psidium guajava lowers platelet counts due to its ability to inhibit platelet aggregation and reduce platelet production.

The compound psidial, found in guava, has been shown to possess antiplatelet activity, which can lead to a decrease in the number of platelets circulating in the blood.

Additionally, the flavonoids present in guava may also contribute to its platelet-reducing effects by interfering with platelet function and viability.

8. Disrupts menstrual cycles

Psidium guajava disrupts menstrual cycles due to its high concentration of flavonoids and alkaloids.

These compounds have been shown to affect the uterus and hormonal balance in the body, leading to irregular or absent menstruation. Additionally, Psidium guajava has been found to stimulate the release of estrogen, which can further exacerbate menstrual cycle disruptions.

This may be particularly concerning for individuals using guava supplements or tea for an extended period.

9. Provokes skin rashes

Psidium guajava provokes skin rashes due to its high concentration of flavonoids and terpenoids.

These compounds can cause an allergic reaction in some individuals, leading to the development of a rash on the skin.

Additionally, the presence of anthraquinones and other phenolic acids may further exacerbate this effect, making guava consumption a potential allergen for certain people.

10. Elicits anxiety attacks

Psidium guajava elicits anxiety attacks in some individuals due to its high concentration of flavonoids and phenolic acids.

These compounds can interact with the body's neurotransmitters, leading to an imbalance that may trigger feelings of unease, panic, and apprehension.

Additionally, the stimulating effects of psidium guajava on the nervous system may also contribute to increased anxiety levels, making it essential for individuals to be aware of these potential side effects when using this herb.
