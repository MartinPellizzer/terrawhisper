Guava (Psidium guajava) Compounds

The primary compounds in guava include flavonoids, which are antioxidants that support heart health. Tannins contribute to its astringent taste and have antimicrobial properties. Phenolic acids also act as antioxidants, while saponins may help lower cholesterol. These compounds collectively provide guava's medicinal and nutritional benefits.

Guava (Psidium guajava) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Guava (Psidium guajava) that contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial effects, supporting the plant's use in traditional herbal medicine for digestive and immune support.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Guava (Psidium guajava) and contributes to its astringent properties. It helps in reducing inflammation and promoting wound healing. Tannins also support digestive health by binding to proteins and aiding in the elimination of toxins. This compound is widely utilized in traditional herbal medicine for its therapeutic benefits.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Guava (Psidium guajava). It contributes to the fruit's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports immune function and may aid in managing inflammation and chronic diseases. Its presence in Guava highlights the plant's potential in herbal medicine for promoting overall health.

4. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Guava (Psidium guajava). It exhibits antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, aiding in digestive health and immune support. Found in the fruit's pulp and seeds, saponin contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for treating infections and gastrointestinal issues.

5. Glycoside

Glycosides in Guava (Psidium guajava) are naturally occurring compounds that contribute to its medicinal properties. These glycosides, such as aucubin and rhamnose, are known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting the plant's use in traditional herbal medicine for digestive and immune support.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is an active compound found in Guava (Psidium guajava). It contributes to the plant's antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarin may also support cardiovascular health by helping regulate blood pressure. Its presence enhances the therapeutic potential of Guava in herbal medicine.

7. Essential oil

Essential oil from Guava (Psidium guajava) contains volatile compounds like citral and limonene. These components exhibit antimicrobial and antioxidant properties. They are used in herbal medicine to treat infections and support digestive health. The essential oil is extracted through steam distillation of the fruit peels.

8. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Guava (Psidium guajava) are bioactive compounds known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. They contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating digestive issues and respiratory conditions. These compounds are extracted from the leaves, fruits, and flowers, supporting their role in herbal medicine.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Guava (Psidium guajava) that contributes to its vibrant color and offers antioxidant properties. This compound supports the plant's medicinal uses by reducing oxidative stress and inflammation, making it valuable in herbal treatments for various health conditions.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Guava (Psidium guajava). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, though its specific role and concentration may vary. Alkaloids in Guava are studied for their potential health benefits, including antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects.
