What is Cryptantha texana?

Cryptantha texana is a perennial herbaceous plant, native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Boraginaceae and is characterized by its small, white or purple flowers, which are borne in clusters in the leaf axils. The plant has linear to lanceolate leaves, typically 1-5 cm long, with a glabrous or pubescent surface. The stems are erect, 10-40 cm tall, and glabrous or pubescent.

The plant produces small, brown or black seeds, approximately 3-5 mm long, which are contained in a dehiscent capsule.
