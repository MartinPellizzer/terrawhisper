Jasminum yunnanense

What's the taxonomical classification of Jasminum yunnanense?

Jasminum yunnanense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Lamiales. This plant is a member of the family Oleaceae, which is further narrowed down to the genus Jasminum. Ultimately, its specific taxonomic designation is completed at the species level as Jasminum yunnanense.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Jasminum yunnanense contains a diverse profile of bioactive secondary metabolites including jasmonoids, essential oils, flavonoids, and various terpenoids. These chemical constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's notable antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Research indicates that the volatile oils within the flowers contribute to its therapeutic potential in traditional medicine. Specific phenolic compounds also play a significant role in providing antimicrobial activity against certain pathogens. These complex molecular structures make the plant a subject of interest for pharmacological studies focused on natural drug development.

What are the therapeutic actions of Jasminum yunnanense?

Jasminum yunnanense has therapeutic properties that primarily center on its ability to alleviate anxiety, promote relaxation, and improve sleep quality through the inhalation of its aromatic essential oils. The plant contains various bioactive compounds that act as mild sedatives to calm the central nervous system. These chemical constituents help to reduce cortisol levels and mitigate the physiological symptoms of stress. In traditional practices, the flowers are often infused into teas to support digestive health and ease muscle tension. Regular use of its fragrant extracts can also provide a subtle mood-lifting effect by stimulating the release of neurotransmitters associated with well-being.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including respiratory infections, skin inflammation, digestive issues, and menstrual irregularities. The flowers are frequently prepared as a tea to alleviate symptoms of the common cold, coughs, and asthma. When applied topically, the plant possesses antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties that can soothe skin irritations or wounds. It is also traditionally utilized in various herbal remedies to regulate the menstrual cycle and ease associated pains. These medicinal applications are rooted in long-standing traditional practices within certain regional cultures.
