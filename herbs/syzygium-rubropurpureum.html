What is Syzygium rubropurpureum?

Syzygium rubropurpureum is a species of plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters tall, with a rounded crown and a straight or slightly curved trunk. The leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical in shape, measuring 3-7 centimeters long and 1-3 centimeters wide, with a glabrous or slightly pubescent surface and a cuneate base. The flowers are small, white or pinkish, and clustered in axillary or terminal corymbose inflorescences, with 4-6-merous petals and a 4-lobed calyx.

The fruit is a red or purple drupe, 1-2 centimeters in diameter, with a single seed and a persistent calyx.

What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

The main herbal preparations of Syzygium rubropurpureum are derived from its leaves and bark, with uses in traditional medicine.

Tea is made by infusing the leaves in hot water, used to treat fever and cough. Infusion is a concentrated liquid made by steeping the leaves in cold water, employed to alleviate respiratory issues. Tincture is a concentrated liquid extract of the bark, often used to treat diarrhea and dysentery.

A decoction of the bark is used to make a salve, applied topically to treat skin irritations and wounds.
