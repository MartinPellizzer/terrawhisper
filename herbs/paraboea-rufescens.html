Paraboea rufescens

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Paraboea rufescens has perennial tufted roots, narrow linear leaves that are often reddish or purplish, and small, solitary, reddish-pink flowers supported by slender pedicels. The leaves are typically basal, forming dense clumps that can withstand arid conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a single flower per axis, which is a distinguishing characteristic of this species. The floral structure features a small, tubular calyx and petals that exhibit a distinct reddish hue. These morphological traits allow the plant to thrive in rocky or sandy habitats.
