Ageratina Glandulifera

Ageratina glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular bluemink, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas, particularly in Central and South America.

It has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and analgesic properties. The plant contains various bioactive compounds, including flavonoids and essential oils, which contribute to its potential therapeutic applications. In traditional healing practices, it has been employed to treat skin conditions, respiratory ailments, and digestive issues.

Modern research is beginning to explore its pharmacological potential, though more studies are needed to validate its medicinal efficacy and safety.
