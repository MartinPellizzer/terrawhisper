Side Effects of hordeum vulgare

Hordeum vulgare induces allergic reactions in individuals who are sensitive to this herb, often manifesting as symptoms such as sneezing, runny nose, and itchy eyes.

Prolonged exposure can trigger respiratory issues, including asthma-like symptoms, coughing, and difficulty breathing, particularly in people with pre-existing respiratory conditions. The herb can also cause skin rash and hives, which are often accompanied by redness, swelling, and intense itching. Skin irritation and throat irritation may occur due to direct contact or inhalation, leading to discomfort and potential complications if left untreated.

These side effects highlight the importance of identifying and avoiding Hordeum vulgare in individuals with known allergies or sensitivities to minimize health risks.
