Potentilla nivea

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Potentiella nivea has a low-growing, perennial growth habit characterized by spreading stolons and leaves that are densely covered in fine, white, felt-like hairs. These silvery-white hairs give the foliage a distinct snowy appearance and provide protection against harsh alpine environments. The leaves are typically palmately lobed or divided into several narrow segments, often appearing in basal rosettes. Small, five-petaled flowers emerge on short stalks, displaying bright yellow petals that contrast sharply with the pale foliage. This morphological combination of dense pubescence and compact structure allows the plant to thrive in rocky, high-altitude habitats.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude alpine and arctic regions of the Northern Hemisphere, specifically spanning parts of Europe, Asia, and North America. It is commonly found growing in rocky tundra environments and subarctic landscapes where soil is thin. Many populations are concentrated in the mountain ranges of Scandinavia and the northernmost reaches of the Eurasian continent. In North America, its distribution is limited to much of the northernmost tundra zones. This widespread but fragmented range is dictated by its preference for cold, treeless habitats.
