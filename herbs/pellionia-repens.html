Pellionia repens

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pellioniia repens has prostrate, creeping stems that grow along the ground with small, opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves featuring entire margins and acute tips. The foliage is characterized by a distinct pattern of silvery or pale green markings that contrast against a darker green background. Its flowers are small, zygomorphic, and typically white or pale in color, emerging from the leaf axils. The plant often develops a dense, mat-like growth habit due to its sprawling stem structure. These morphological features allow the species to effectively cover forest floors in its native tropical habitats.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, specifically spanning across countries such as Thailand, Malaysia, Indonesia, and parts of the Philippines. It typically flourishes in humid, lowland environments where dense vegetation provides necessary shade. Its distribution is often concentrated in moist forest understories or along the edges of tropical woodlands. These specific habitats allow the species to thrive within the warm, wet climates characteristic of the equatorial belt. Consequently, its range is closely tied to the presence of high humidity and consistent rainfall patterns found in these maritime and continental Southeast Asian zones.
