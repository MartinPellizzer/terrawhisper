What's the taxonomical classification of Pellionia repens?

Pellioniia repens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Rosales, it is placed into the family Urticaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Pellioniia, and its specific species name, repeps.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pellioniia repens has prostrate, creeping stems that grow along the ground with small, opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves featuring entire margins and acute tips. The foliage is characterized by a distinct pattern of silvery or pale green markings that contrast against a darker green background. Its flowers are small, zygomorphic, and typically white or pale in color, emerging from the leaf axils. The plant often develops a dense, mat-like growth habit due to its sprawling stem structure.
