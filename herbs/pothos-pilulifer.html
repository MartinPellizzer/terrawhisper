What's the taxonomical classification of Pothos pilulifer?

Pothos pilulifer belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Alismatales and is a member of the family Araceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its genus, Pothos, and its specific species designation, pilulifer.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause severe oral irritation, swelling of the mouth and throat, and intense gastrointestinal distress if ingested due to its high concentration of insoluble calcium oxalate crystals. These needle-like structures puncture the delicate mucosal linings of the mouth and esophagus upon contact. This physical damage often leads to immediate burning sensations and significant swelling that can potentially obstruct breathing. If swallowed, the crystals can trigger vomiting, abdominal pain, and inflammation within the digestive tract. Symptoms typically manifest shortly after exposure to the plant's sap or foliage.
