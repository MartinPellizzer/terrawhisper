10 Medicinal Constituents Of Tilia Europaea (Lime)

Tilia europaea has active constituents such as flavonoids, terpenoids, and volatile oils.

These constituents possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which are beneficial for soothing anxiety, insomnia, and stress. The flavonoids can also improve cardiovascular health and reduce inflammation, thereby reducing the risk of cardiovascular disease.

Furthermore, the terpenoids have antimicrobial properties, which can help prevent infections and promote wound healing.

This article explains in details the 10 best active constituents of Tilia europaea.

1. Flavonoids

Tilia europaea flavonoids is a class of bioactive compounds present in its leaves and flowers.

These flavonoids have been found to possess antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties.

Specifically, they contain quercetin, kaempferol, and isorhapontigenin, which have been shown to exhibit significant protective effects against oxidative stress, cardiovascular disease, and cancer.

2. Quercetin

Tilia europaea quercetin is a flavonoid compound extracted from its flowers and leaves.

It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various ailments, including respiratory issues, anxiety, and cardiovascular problems.

Quercetin has antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which may help protect against cell damage and reduce the risk of chronic diseases such as cancer and heart disease.

3. Kaempferol

Tilia europaea kaempferol is a flavonoid compound extracted from the flowers and leaves of the Tilia platyphyllos tree.

It has been extensively studied for its potential health benefits, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. Kaempferol has been shown to have beneficial effects on cardiovascular disease, cancer prevention, and neuroprotection.

Its bioavailability is enhanced when consumed with food or as a supplement, making it a promising natural compound for overall well-being.

4. Tiliroside

Tilia europaea tiliroside is a flavonoid glycoside compound extracted from the leaves and flowers of the Tilia europaea plant.

This natural product has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine for its various pharmacological activities, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects.

Research has shown that tiliroside exhibits potent protective effects against oxidative stress and cell damage, making it a promising compound for the development of new therapeutic agents.

5. Naphthoquinones

Tilia europaea naphthoquinones is a type of bioactive compound found in the leaves and bark of the plant.

These compounds have been shown to possess antimicrobial and antifungal properties, making them useful in the treatment of various diseases.

The naphthoquinones in Tilia europaea have also been found to exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant activities, which can help protect against oxidative stress and inflammation in the body.

6. Phenolic acids

Tilia europaea phenolic acids is a class of bioactive compounds responsible for its medicinal and antioxidant properties.

These acids are found in various parts of the plant, including leaves, flowers, and fruits.

The main phenolic acids present in Tilia europaea include gallic acid, ellagic acid, and quercetin, which have been shown to exhibit anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and anticancer activities, making them potentially useful in the prevention and treatment of various diseases.

7. Terpenoids

Tilia europaea terpenoids is a group of bioactive compounds found in the leaves, bark, and flowers of the plant.

These terpenoids have been shown to possess various pharmacological activities, including antimicrobial, antioxidant, and anti-inflammatory properties.

They have been isolated and characterized, with several being identified as potential therapeutic agents for treating diseases such as cardiovascular disorders and cancer.

8. Sesquiterpenes

Tilia europaea sesquiterpenes is a type of organic compound found in the leaves and flowers of this plant.

These sesquiterpenes are responsible for the characteristic aroma and flavor of Tilia europaea, which has been used for centuries in traditional medicine and culinary practices.

They have been shown to possess antibacterial, antiviral, and anti-inflammatory properties, making them a promising area of research for potential therapeutic applications.

9. Alkaloids

Tilia europaea alkaloids is a group of bioactive compounds found in the bark, leaves, and flowers of the tree.

These alkaloids have been shown to exhibit various biological activities, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties.

The main alkaloids present in Tilia europaea are quinazolinones and terpenes, which have been demonstrated to possess significant therapeutic potential for treating various diseases, including cardiovascular disorders, cancer, and bacterial infections.

10. Tannins

Tilia europaea tannins is a class of compounds found in the leaves and flowers of this deciduous tree species.

These tannins are responsible for the plant's astringent properties, causing it to dry out the mouth and tongue when consumed. They also contribute to the bitterness and astringency of tea infusions made from lime flowers.

Additionally, Tilia europaea tannins have been found to possess antioxidant and antimicrobial properties, making them of interest in traditional medicine and potential applications in food and pharmaceutical industries.
