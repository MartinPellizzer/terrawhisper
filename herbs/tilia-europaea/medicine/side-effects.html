10 Possible Side Effects Of Tilia Europaea (Lime)

Tilia europaea has some side effects when used improperly, such as insomnia, dizziness, and allergic reactions.

These side effects can occur when the herb is consumed in excess or combined with other medications. For example, insomnia can lead to fatigue, decreased productivity, and strained relationships. Dizziness can impair coordination and increase the risk of accidents.

Allergic reactions can cause discomfort, swelling, and difficulty breathing.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Tilia europaea if used imporperly.

Tilia europaea causes allergic reactions due to the presence of certain compounds in its pollen and leaves.

The sap of the tree contains flavonoids, which can trigger an immune response in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as itching, redness, and swelling of the skin.

Additionally, the plant's flowers release allergenic proteins into the air, causing respiratory issues like congestion and coughing in people with sensitive lungs.

When combined, this interaction can lead to an increased risk of bleeding due to decreased platelet aggregation and enhanced anticoagulant effects.
