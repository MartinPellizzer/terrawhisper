Nelumbo nucifera

What's the taxonomical classification of Nelumbo nucifera?

Nelumbo nucifera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Proteales. This aquatic plant is further categorized into the family Nelumbonaceae, which contains the genus Nelumbo, and is specifically identified by its species name, nucifera.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Nelumbo nucifera has large, circular leaves that can reach up to one meter in diameter, supported by long, thick petioles that emerge from submerged rhizomes. These leaves are peltate in structure, meaning the stem attaches to the center of the leaf blade rather than the edge. The foliage often rises significantly above the water surface, featuring a waxy, water-repellent coating known as the lotus effect. The plant also produces large, showy flowers that transition from white to pink and eventually develop into distinctive, cone-shaped seed pods. These pods are woody and contain numerous seeds, which are essential for the plant's reproductive cycle.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, specifically spanning across India, China, Japan, and parts of Southeast Asia. It thrives in freshwater ecosystems such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving rivers throughout these warm climates. While its primary range is centered in the Eastern Hemisphere, it has been introduced to various other parts of the world for ornamental and culinary purposes. In many regions, its distribution is limited by its requirement for warm temperatures and consistent water availability. This widespread presence makes it a significant aquatic species across many diverse Asian landscapes.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Nelumbo nucifera contains medicinal compounds found in its seeds, rhizomes, lotus leaves, flowers, and various fleshy parts of the plant. Lotus leaves are often employed in traditional practices to reduce fever and manage hypertension. What are the pharmacological activities of Nelumbo nucifera?

This plant has diverse pharmacological activities including anti-inflammatory, anti-oxidant, anti-diabetic, anti-hyperlipidemia, and anti-microbial properties. Its rich concentration of bioactive compounds helps neutralize free radicals and reduce systemic oxidative stress. The plant also assists in managing metabolic disorders by regulating blood glucose levels and lowering lipid profiles in the bloodstream. Furthermore, its natural chemical constituents provide a defense against various bacterial and fungal pathogens. These multifaceted therapeutic effects make Nelumbo nucifera a significant subject in medicinal research.

As discussed in a study, Nelumbo nucifera leaves serve as a source for lotus-derived extracellular vesicles (LDEVs) with a mean size range of 130-160 nm. When isolated using the tangential flow filtration method, these LDEVs achieved a yield of 3.69 ± 0.43 × 10^9 particles/g of lotus leaves. These LDEVs demonstrated the ability to attenuate LPS-induced inflammation in RAW264.7 cells by significantly decreasing nitrite concentrations in the culture medium. Additionally, the LDEVs showed potential for wound healing by promoting the migration of HaCaT cells in vitro. The study also confirmed that LDEVs undergo internalization by both RAW264.7 and HaCaT cells.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal alkaloids neferine, nuciferiine, liensiniine, glaziovine, and armepavine. These specific chemical compounds are primarily found within the botanical structure of Nymphaea lotus. Researchers study these substances due to their potential pharmacological activities in various biological systems. The presence of these alkaloids contributes to the plant's traditional use in various medicinal practices. Understanding the concentration of these compounds is essential for evaluating the plant's therapeutic properties.

As discussed in a study, the plant Nelumbo nucifera contains the alkaloid neferine found in its lotus seed embryos. This compound was evaluated in a rat model using doses of 10 and 50 mg/kg administered 30 min before a 15 mg/kg kainic acid injection. Neferine pretreatment increased seizure latency, reduced seizure scores, and prevented glutamate elevation and neuronal loss. It also increased the expression of synaptophysin and postsynaptic density protein 95 in the hippocampi. Furthermore, the compound decreased expression of interleukin-1β, interleukin-6, and tumor necrosis factor-α, along with NLRP3 inflammasome, caspase-1, and interleukin-18 levels.

What are the herbal preparations of this plant?

This plant can be prepared as an infusion of the flowers, a decoction of the rhizomes, a tincture of the seeds, an essential oil from the petals, a powder from the dried leaves, or a concentrated extract from various parts. Practitioners often use a mild infusion of the blossoms to soothe the mind and promote relaxation. A decoction made from the sturdy roots is frequently employed to support digestive health and reduce inflammation. Alcohol-based tinctures and liquid extracts are highly valued for their ability to deliver potent bioactive compounds quickly. For topical applications or aromatherapy, the aromatic essential oil provides a concentrated sensory experience. Finally, the finely ground powder offers a convenient way to incorporate the nutritional benefits of the seeds into daily diets.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause adverse reactions such as nausea, dizziness, diarrhea, allergic reaction, hypoglycemia, and hypotension in sensitive individuals. Some users may experience gastrointestinal distress including nausea and diarrhea after consumption. Neurological effects like dizziness can also occur during use. Furthermore, the plant may trigger an allergic reaction in certain people or lead to systemic issues like hypoglycemia and hypotension. Careful monitoring is advised to prevent these potential complications.
