Nelumbo Nucifera

Nelumbo nucifera, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Asia with its roots in the mud and its leaves floating on the water's surface.

This herb has been valued in traditional medicine for centuries due to its numerous health benefits, which include reducing inflammation, improving cardiovascular health, and supporting immune function.

The therapeutic actions of Nelumbo nucifera are attributed to its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and anti-cancer properties, making it effective in treating various diseases.

Its bioactive constituents include alkaloids, flavonoids, and saponins, which have been isolated and studied for their medicinal properties.

According to "Chemistry & biodiversity", Nelumbo nucifera benefits include significant treatment of various diseases such as leukoderma, dysentery, fever, and hyperlipidaemia, with notable pharmacological activities like anticancer, antioxidant, and antiviral properties.

Possible Side Effects of nelumbo nucifera

Nelumbo nucifera can have side effects if used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, including diarrhea, nausea, and stomach cramps, due to its high saponin content.

Ingesting large quantities of the herb can also lead to allergic reactions, such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.

Additionally, Nelumbo nucifera may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners, and decrease their effectiveness.

Prolonged use or excessive consumption of Nelumbo nucifera can also cause kidney damage and exacerbate existing kidney problems.
