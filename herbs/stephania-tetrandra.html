Stephania tetrandra

What's the taxonomical classification of Stephania tetrandra?

Stephania tetrandra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is positioned in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Ranunculales. This plant is a member of the family Menispermaceae, specifically categorized within the genus Stephania, and is identified by its unique species designation, tetrandra.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stephania tetrandra has a tuberous stem, opposite pinnate leaves, and small, four-lobed flowers with four distinct stamens. The plant grows from a woody, globose tuber that stores nutrients for seasonal development. Its foliage consists of compound leaves that typically feature several pairs of leaflets arranged along a central axis. The inflorescence is characterized by its small size and a specific symmetry defined by the four-parted structure of the floral organs. These morphological features distinguish it within its genus and are essential for its survival in tropical environments.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several medicinal compounds including benzylisoquinoline alkaloids such as tetrandrine, boldine, magnoflorine, and an aporphine alkaloid. These chemical constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's diverse pharmacological activities. Tetrandrine is frequently studied for its potential to act as a calcium channel blocker. Boldine and magnoflorine contribute to its complex biochemical profile through various biological interactions. The presence of an aporphine alkaloid further expands the therapeutic possibilities of these organic molecules.

As discussed in a study, Stephania tetrandra is a source of traditional Chinese medicine used to treat rheumatism, rheumatoid arthritis, edema, and hypertension. Benzylisoquinoline alkaloids (BIAs) serve as the main bioactive compounds found within the plant.
