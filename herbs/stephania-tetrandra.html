Stephania tetrandra

What's the taxonomical classification of Stephania tetrandra?

Stephania tetrandra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is positioned in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Ranunculales. This plant is a member of the family Menispermaceae, specifically categorized within the genus Stephania, and is identified by its unique species designation, tetrandra.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stephania tetrandra has a tuberous stem, opposite pinnate leaves, and small, four-lobed flowers with four distinct stamens. The plant grows from a woody, globose tuber that stores nutrients for seasonal development. Its foliage consists of compound leaves that typically feature several pairs of leaflets arranged along a central axis. The inflorescence is characterized by its small size and a specific symmetry defined by the four-parted structure of the floral organs. These morphological features distinguish it within its genus and are essential for its survival in tropical environments.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse array of medicinal compounds, most notably the bisbenzylisoquinoline alkaloids and the broader class of benzylisoquinoline alkaloids. Among these chemical constituents, the specific alkaloid tetrandrine serves as a primary marker for the species. Other significant bioactive substances found within its structure include boldine and magnoflorine. What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Stephania tetrandra targets various signaling proteins and enzymes including cyclooxygenase, various neurotransmitter receptors, and specific inflammatory cytokines to exert its pharmacological effects. The plant contains diverse alkaloids that interact with the central nervous system by modulating ion channels and neuroreceptors. Such multi-target interactions contribute to the plant's observed anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and neuroprotective properties.

Stephania tetrandra has therapeutic actions that include bitter, diuretic, emetic, and astringent properties used to treat various ailments. When used as an emetic, the plant can induce vomiting to cleanse the gastrointestinal tract of harmful substances. Additionally, its astringent qualities allow it to help contract tissues and reduce inflammation or excessive secretions.
