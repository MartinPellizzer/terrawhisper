Stephania tetrandra

What's the taxonomical classification of Stephania tetrandra?

Stephania tetrandra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is positioned in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Ranunculales. This plant is a member of the family Menispermaceae, specifically categorized within the genus Stephania, and is identified by its unique species designation, tetrandra.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Stephania tetrandra contains a complex profile of isoquinoline alkaloids, most notably tetrandrine, chevetine, and stephanine. These specific chemical compounds are primarily responsible for the plant's significant biological activities in pharmacological studies. Tetrandrine is particularly noted for its ability to act as a calcium channel blocker, which offers potential benefits for treating hypertension and arrhythmias. Additional alkaloids within the species contribute to various anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Researchers continue to investigate these molecules to better understand their therapeutic applications in modern medicine.

What are the therapeutic actions of Stephania tetrandra?

Stephania tetrandra has potent antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and analgesic properties that are primarily utilized to treat skin infections, wounds, and various inflammatory conditions. The plant contains bioactive alkaloids and secondary metabolites that work to inhibit the growth of harmful pathogens. These chemical constituents also assist in reducing swelling and alleviating pain in affected tissues. When applied topically or prepared in traditional remedies, the plant promotes faster healing of skin lesions. Its pharmacological profile makes it a significant subject for studying natural treatments for infectious diseases.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, intestinal parasites, stomach aches, and various types of inflammation. The leaves and stems are often prepared as decoctions or poultices to address these specific ailments. Local traditional practitioners utilize its phytochemical properties to reduce swelling and soothe gastrointestinal distress. It is also frequently applied topically to help heal wounds or fungal conditions on the skin. These medicinal applications highlight the plant's importance in regional herbal medicine.

What are the herbal preparations of Stephania tetrandra?

Stephania tetrandra is prepared primarily as a decoction, a concentrated liquid extract made by boiling the dried rhizomes in water. This traditional method is used to extract the active alkaloids found within the plant's woody structure. Some practitioners also utilize the powdered form of the dried root to create capsules or to mix into other medicinal blends. Infusions may occasionally be made, though the dense nature of the rhizome often favors longer boiling times. These various preparations are typically employed in traditional medicine to target specific physiological conditions.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Stephania tetrandra can cause severe toxic reactions including nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and potentially fatal cardiac arrhythmias due to its high concentration of isoquinoline alkaloids. These potent alkaloids can interfere with the nervous system and disrupt normal heart rhythms if ingested. Consuming the plant may lead to dizziness or a sudden drop in blood pressure in sensitive individuals. Because the specific chemical composition varies, there is a significant risk of unpredictable neurological effects. Such symptoms necessitate immediate medical intervention to prevent long-term organ damage or death.
