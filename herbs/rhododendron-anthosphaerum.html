Rhododendron anthosphaerum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta.       What side effects this plant can have?

      This plant can cause severe toxicity through the ingestion of grayanotoxins, leading to symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, dizziness, confusion, arrhythmia, seizures, and respiratory depression. These physiological reactions occur because the toxins interfere with the sodium channels in cell membranes. As the poison affects the nervous and cardiovascular systems, the individual may experience dangerous heart rhythm irregularities. In more extreme cases of poisoning, the body may struggle to maintain basic neurological and respiratory functions. Medical intervention is typically required to manage these life-threatening complications.
