What's the taxonomical classification of Itea omeiensis?

Itea omeiensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Saxifrages, it is assigned to the family Iteaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Itea, with its specific designation being the species omeiensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Itea omeiensis has small, evergreen leaves that are elliptic to lanceolate in shape, measuring approximately 1 to 3 centimeters in length with serrated margins and a leathery texture. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub with woody stems that exhibit a spreading growth habit. Its inflorescences consist of small, fragrant white flowers that emerge from the leaf axils during the blooming season. Following pollination, the plant produces tiny, dry capsules that contain small seeds for dispersal. The overall structure is characterized by dense foliage and a fine-textured appearance.
