Pyrrosia adnascens

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pyrrosia adnascens has opposite, sessile, lanceolate leaves that are typically dark green with slightly serrated margins and a distinctively fused base. The stem is woody at the base and displays a branching habit that supports small, inconspicuous flowers. These flowers are arranged in axillary clusters and feature a tubular corolla. The plant often grows in dense mats or low shrubs depending on its environmental conditions. Its overall structure is compact and adapted to rocky or alpine habitats.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of southern and eastern China, specifically occurring in provinces such as Sichuan, Yunnan, Guizhou, and Hubei. It thrives within high-altitude habitats that provide the cool, moist conditions necessary for its survival. These populations are typically found in alpine meadows and shaded forest margins where rocky terrain is common. The distribution is relatively fragmented due to the specific environmental requirements of its niche ecosystem.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Pyrrosia adnascens can cause severe skin irritation, localized swelling, and intense itching upon direct contact with the epidermis. Allergic reactions can vary in intensity depending on the person's sensitivity to the plant's toxins.
