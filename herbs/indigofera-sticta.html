Indigofera sticta

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Indigofera sticta has an erect or ascending woody shrub habit characterized by pinnately compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The leaves consist of multiple pairs of oblong leaflets that are typically arranged alternately along the rachis. Its inflorescences are organized into short, dense racemes that emerge from the leaf axils. The flowers display a typical papilionaceous structure, often featuring pale or yellowish petals. Small, linear pods develop from the flowers and serve as the primary fruit type for this species.
