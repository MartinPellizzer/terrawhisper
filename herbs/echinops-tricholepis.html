Echinops tricholepis

What's the taxonomical classification of Echinops tricholepis?

Echinops tricholepis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Asterales, the plant is a member of the family Asteraceae. It is further defined by its genus, Echinops, and is specifically identified by its species name, tricholepis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Echinops tricholepis has a robust, perennial structure characterized by deeply lobed, grayish-green leaves and spherical, spiny flower heads that emerge from stiff, upright stems. The foliage is typically covered in fine, silvery hairs that provide a soft texture to the serrated leaf margins. Its inflorescences are composed of numerous small, tubular florets densely packed within a protective layer of sharp, prickly bracts. These prickly globose heads can reach several centimeters in diameter and often turn a distinct shade of blue or purple during anthesis. The stems are woody at the base and support the heavy floral structures throughout the growing season.
