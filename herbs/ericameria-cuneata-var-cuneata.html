Green Coneflower (Ericameria Cuneata Var. Cuneata)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Ericameria cuneata var. cuneata.

Green coneflower, scientifically known as Ericameria cuneata var. cuneata, is a medicinal herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, valued for its adaptogenic and anti-inflammatory properties. It has been traditionally used by Indigenous peoples, such as the Navajo and Apache, to treat respiratory ailments, digestive issues, and skin conditions, often prepared as a tea or tincture. Modern wellness practices have embraced it for its potential to support immune function, reduce stress, and alleviate symptoms of inflammation, making it a popular ingredient in herbal remedies and supplements. The plant is distinguished by its unique, slightly bitter and aromatic flavor, and it contains a rare active compound called ericoside, which may contribute to its therapeutic effects. Its historical use in traditional medicine and its growing popularity in contemporary wellness circles highlight its significance as both a culturally rich and scientifically intriguing plant.
