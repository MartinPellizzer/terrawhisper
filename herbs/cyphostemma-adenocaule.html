Such diverse activities highlight the significant medicinal potential of Cyphostemma adenocaule in therapeutic applications.

According to a study published by "Heliyon", Cyphostemma adenocaule is used in traditional medicinal systems to manage tumors, helmintiasis, snake envenomation, rabies virus, splenomegaly, and other immunological disorders. The in-vivo study evaluated the immunomodulatory activity of the crude root extract and its solvent fractions using doses of 100, 200, and 400 mg/kg in Swiss albino mice. An increase in both the carbon clearance rate and humoral antibody titer was observed as the dose of the crude extract and solvent fractions increased. The crude extract at a dose of 400 mg/kg revealed the highest and statistically significant augmentation of the carbon clearance rate at 0.1100 ± 0.0124 and the humoral antibody titer at 96.00 ± 14.31 compared to the vehicle control group. It was concluded that the crude extract and n-butanol fraction showed promising immunostimulant activity.
