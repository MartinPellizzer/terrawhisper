What to know about Butea monosperma (palash) before using it medicinally

Palash, scientifically known as Butea monosperma, is a herb that has been touted for its numerous health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can help to alleviate symptoms of various diseases.

In the culinary world, palash is often used to add flavor and color to dishes such as soups, stews, and curries, particularly in Indian and Southeast Asian cuisine. Horticulturally, palash is a deciduous tree that thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value, with showy flowers and fragrant foliage. Botanically, palash belongs to the Fabaceae family and has been studied extensively for its unique chemical composition, which includes compounds such as flavonoids and alkaloids.

With references dating back thousands of years to ancient Ayurvedic and Chinese medicine, palash has a rich history as a valued medicinal herb with applications in traditional healing practices.
