What's the taxonomical classification of Ainsliaea pertyoides?

Ainsliaea pertyoides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further organized under the order Asterales. It is a representative of the family Asteraceae, falling under the genus Ainsliaea, and is specifically identified by its species name, pertyoides.

What are the pharmacological activities of Ainsliaea pertyoides?

This plant has potent anti-inflammatory properties driven primarily by its ability to exert NLRP3-inflammasome inhibitory activity. By suppressing the assembly and activation of the NLRP3 complex, the plant effectively reduces the maturation of pro-inflammatory cytokines. This specific mechanism helps to dampen the excessive immune responses that often characterize chronic inflammatory conditions. Furthermore, these pharmacological actions suggest that its bioactive compounds could serve as valuable candidates for developing new therapeutic agents. Such targeted regulation of the innate immune system highlights its potential in managing various inflammatory diseases.

According to a study published by "Chemistry & biodiversity", six new sesquiterpenoids named ainslides A-F (1-6) were purified from the whole plants of Ainsliaea pertyoides. These include one carotene-type sesquiterpene (1), one euudesmane (2), and four guaianolides (3-6), alongside eight known sesquiterpenoids (7-14). Among the isolated compounds, substances 3 and 7-13 exhibited NLRP3-inflammasome inhibitory activity. These specific compounds demonstrated anti-inflammatory activity with IC50 values ranging from 1.80 to 4.33 μM.
