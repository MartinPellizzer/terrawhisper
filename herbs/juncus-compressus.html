What's the taxonomical classification of Juncus compressus?

Juncus compressus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Poales and falls under the family Juncaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized within the genus Juncus, reaching its specific identification at the species level as compressus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Juncus compressus has cylindrical, compressed stems that are typically green, smooth, and reach heights of up to 30 centimeters. These stems emerge from a creeping rhizomatous base and lack distinct leaves, instead possessing small, scale-like sheaths at the nodes. The plant features a compact, tufted growth habit often found in saline coastal environments. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters near the tips of the stems. The resulting capsules are three-valved and contain numerous tiny, brown seeds.
