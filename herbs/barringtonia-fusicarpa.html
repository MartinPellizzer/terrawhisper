What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Barringtonia fusicarpa has a woody structure characterized by spreading branches, elliptic to obovate leaves with entire margins, and small white flowers arranged in dense terminal racemes. Its reproductive organs include stamens that are often prominent, contributing to the visual complexity of the inflorescence. The fruit develops into a woody capsule that eventually dehisces to release its seeds. These morphological features collectively distinguish the species within its genus.
