Barringtonia fusicarpa

What's the taxonomical classification of Barringtonia fusicarpa?

Barringtonia fusicarpa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical descent through the order Ericales, it is placed within the family Lecythidaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Barringtonia, and its unique species designation, fusicarpa.

Barringtonia fusicarpa contains medicinal compounds primarily located in its bark, leaves, and fruit. These diverse parts provide a variety of bioactive substances used in traditional healing systems.

      What are the herbal preparations of Barringtonia fusicarpa?

      Barringtonia fusicarpa is prepared as a decoction from its bark or as a topical poultice using its crushed leaves to treat various skin conditions. These liquid extracts are often simmered in water to concentrate the bioactive compounds for internal or external application. The leaves can also be macerated in oils to create infused ointments for soothing localized inflammation. Some traditional practices involve using the fruit extracts in diluted washes to cleanse affected areas. These diverse methods allow the plant's medicinal properties to be delivered in both systemic and localized forms.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Barringtonia fusicarpa can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and digestive distress if the plant is handled or ingested improperly. The seeds and certain plant tissues may contain bioactive compounds that trigger localized dermatitis upon contact with the skin. If swallowed, the chemical constituents can lead to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain in sensitive individuals. While specific toxicological studies on humans are limited, caution is necessary when interacting with any part of the species. Avoiding direct contact with the sap and fruit is a recommended safety precaution for most users.
