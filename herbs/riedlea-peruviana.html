Riedlea peruviana

Riedlea peruviana, also known as Peruvian riedlea, is a plant species that belongs to the family Achariaceae.

It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects.

The health benefits of Riedlea peruviana include its potential to treat various health conditions such as fever, digestive issues, and skin problems, as well as its ability to boost the immune system.

Herbal preparations of Riedlea peruviana can be made into teas, infusions, or tinctures, and are often used in traditional medicine to promote overall well-being.

Related Study

According to "Journal of ethnopharmacology", Riedlea peruviana has shown interesting antiviral activity against HCV with low cytotoxicity, specifically inhibiting at least 30% of HCV virus at 25 μg/mL for 60% of the plant extracts.
