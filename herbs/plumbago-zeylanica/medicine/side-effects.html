10 Possible Side Effects Of Plumbago Zeylanica (Leadwort)

Plumbago zeylanica has some side effects when used improperly, such as diarrhea, vomiting, and stomach pain due to its purgative properties.

These effects can be caused by consuming large quantities or using the herb for extended periods, leading to dehydration and electrolyte imbalance.

For people with pre-existing gastrointestinal conditions, these side effects can worsen symptoms and affect their daily lives, causing fatigue, discomfort, and decreased productivity.

Plumbago zeylanica causes stomach upset due to the presence of plumbagin, a compound that can irritate the mucous membranes in the digestive tract.

This may lead to discomfort, nausea, and abdominal pain, although the severity of these symptoms can vary depending on individual tolerance and dosage.

2. Interferes with medication absorption

Plumbago zeylanica triggers allergic reactions in some individuals.

This is due to its containing isoquinoline alkaloids, which are known to cause skin irritation and allergic responses in sensitive people. The presence of these compounds can also lead to respiratory issues, such as congestion and coughing.

Furthermore, the plant's sap has been reported to cause contact dermatitis in some cases, resulting in redness, itching, and blistering of the skin.

Plumbago zeylanica induces nausea and vomiting.

This is due to its high concentration of plumbagin, a compound that has been shown to have toxic effects on the gastrointestinal system. The irritation caused by this compound can trigger an adverse response in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, and stomach discomfort.

As a result, users may experience unpleasant gastrointestinal side effects when using Plumbago zeylanica.

The exact mechanism by which plumbagin achieves this effect is not fully understood, but it is thought to involve the activation of specific receptors in the brain that regulate salivation.

7. Interferes with medication absorption

Plumbago zeylanica lowers blood pressure sharply due to its high concentration of alkaloids, particularly plumbagin and purpurogallin.

These compounds have a direct effect on the cardiovascular system, causing vasodilation and decreasing peripheral resistance.

As a result, blood pressure drops significantly, which may be beneficial for individuals with hypertension but potentially problematic for those with pre-existing hypotension or other underlying medical conditions.

8. Interferes with medication absorption

Plumbago zeylanica affects fetal development when used during pregnancy.

This is because it contains a toxic compound called plumbagin, which can cross the placental barrier and pose a risk to the developing fetus.

The exact mechanisms by which plumbagin causes harm are not fully understood, but it is thought to interfere with normal embryonic development and potentially lead to birth defects or developmental delays.
