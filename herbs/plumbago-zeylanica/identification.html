Botanical Identification of Blue Canna (Plumbago zeylanica)

To identify Blue Canna (Plumbago zeylanica) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Blue Canna, or Plumbago zeylanica, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Rubiales, family Rubiaceae, genus Plumbago, and species Plumbago zeylanica. This classification places it within the flowering plants, specifically in the family that includes many medicinal and ornamental plants.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Blue Canna is Plumbago zeylanica, first described by the botanist R. Br. in 1824. It is also known by several synonyms, including Canna zeylanica and Canna indica. Common names such as Blue Canna and Indian Canna are widely used, while regional and historical names reflect its traditional use in herbal medicine across different cultures.

Morphological Characteristics

Blue Canna (Plumbago zeylanica) is a shrub with a branched root system. Its stems are woody, erect, and can grow up to 2 meters tall. Leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth surface. The inflorescence is a terminal panicle, bearing small white or blue flowers. The fruit is a capsule containing several seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Blue Canna (Plumbago zeylanica) can be distinguished by its distinctive blue or purple flowers with five petals, opposite heart-shaped leaves, and woody stems. It differs from similar species like Canna indica by its smaller, more delicate flowers and the absence of a prominent central spadix. Its leaves are usually smaller and more pointed compared to those of related plants.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Plumbago zeylanica is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, Sri Lanka, and parts of Southeast Asia. It thrives in warm climates with high humidity and well-drained, loamy soil. It typically grows at low altitudes, often near water sources or in moist, shaded environments.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Blue Canna (Plumbago zeylanica) blooms from summer to early fall, typically between June and September. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early fall, following flowering. For identification purposes, harvest timing is best during the flowering period when leaves and stems are most distinct.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Plumbago zeylanica belongs to the family Rubiaceae, closely related to species like Plumbago auriculata and Plumbago scandens. It shares genus traits such as opposite leaves and tubular flowers, but differs in leaf shape and flower color. Its phylogenetic placement reflects evolutionary ties within the Rubiaceae family, highlighting its role in understanding plant diversity in herbal medicine.
