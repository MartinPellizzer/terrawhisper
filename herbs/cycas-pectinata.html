Cycas pectinata

What's the taxonomical classification of Cycas pectinata?

Cycas pectinata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is classified in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Cycadidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Cycadales and is a member of the family Cycadaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by the genus Cycas and is specifically designated as the species pectinata.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Cycas pectinata contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, and terpenoids that serve as its primary medicinal components. These chemical constituents are often studied for their potential pharmacological properties such as anti-inflammatory and antioxidant activities. Some traditional medicinal practices utilize parts of the plant to address skin ailments or digestive issues due to these specific compounds. Researchers continue to investigate the precise molecular mechanisms by which these phytochemicals interact with biological systems. Understanding the concentration and synergy of these substances is essential for determining their therapeutic value.

What are the therapeutic actions of Cycas pectinata?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various skin infections, wounds, and gastrointestinal ailments. The extracts from the leaves and stems are often applied topically to help heal sores or manage skin conditions. In some traditional practices, decoctions are consumed to alleviate stomach discomfort or digestive issues. Researchers are interested in its bioactive compounds which may possess antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. These medicinal applications rely on the plant's ability to combat certain pathogens through its natural chemical makeup.
