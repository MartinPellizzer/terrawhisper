What's the taxonomical classification of Cycas pectinata?

Cycas pectinata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is classified in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Cycadidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Cycadales and is a member of the family Cycadaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by the genus Cycas and is specifically designated as the species pectinata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cycas pectinata has a pinnately compound leaf structure with narrow, linear leaflets that are arranged symmetrically along a central rachis. The plant features a woody, cylindrical stem that lacks a true bark but often retains scars from fallen leaf bases. Each leaf consists of numerous stiff, dark green leaflets that taper to a sharp point at the apex. The arrangement of these leaves typically forms a dense, crown-like cluster at the top of the stem. This morphology is characteristic of cycads, providing a robust framework for their slow-growing reproductive structures.
