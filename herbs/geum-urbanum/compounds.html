Common Avens (Geum urbanum) Compounds

Common Avens contains several primary active compounds, including tannins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its astringent and anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarin and glycosides are also present, offering potential anticoagulant and antioxidant effects. Saponins and terpenoids support its cleansing and immune-boosting qualities, while essential oils and alkaloids contribute to its aromatic and medicinal profile. Anthocyanins provide additional antioxidant benefits.

Common Avens (Geum urbanum) Monograph

1. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Common Avens (Geum urbanum). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is used in traditional herbal medicine for its ability to reduce inflammation and support digestive health. Tannins in Common Avens are primarily found in the leaves and roots.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Common Avens (Geum urbanum) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, supporting the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and skin conditions. They also help protect the plant from environmental stressors. Flavonoids are key to the therapeutic potential of Geum urbanum in herbal medicine.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Common Avens (Geum urbanum). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Found in the roots and leaves, it supports the plant's traditional use in treating skin conditions and digestive issues. Its presence enhances the therapeutic potential of Geum urbanum in herbal medicine.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Common Avens (Geum urbanum). It contributes to the plant's mild sedative and antispasmodic effects. Found in the roots and rhizomes, coumarin is used in herbal medicine to support digestive health and relieve muscle spasms. Its presence enhances the plant's therapeutic profile in traditional remedies.

5. Glycoside

The glycoside in Common Avens (Geum urbanum) contributes to its medicinal properties. It is known to have mild sedative and antispasmodic effects, supporting its traditional use in treating nervous system conditions. This compound is often found in the roots and is part of the plant's complex herbal profile.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Common Avens (Geum urbanum). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and skin conditions. Saponins have mild expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. They also help in cleansing the blood and supporting digestive health.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Common Avens (Geum urbanum) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects, supporting the plant's traditional use in treating skin conditions and respiratory ailments. They are also responsible for the plant's characteristic aroma and may aid in wound healing.

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of Common Avens (Geum urbanum) contains volatile compounds like monoterpenes and sesquiterpenes. It is used in herbal medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties. The oil is typically extracted through steam distillation from the flowering parts of the plant. It supports digestive and respiratory health in traditional remedies.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid in Common Avens (Geum urbanum) contributes to its medicinal properties. This compound is known for its potential anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects. It is often used in traditional herbal medicine to support digestive and respiratory health. Alkaloids in Geum urbanum are typically found in the roots and rhizomes of the plant.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanins are pigments found in Common Avens (Geum urbanum) that contribute to its medicinal properties. They act as antioxidants, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce inflammation. These compounds support the plant's traditional use in treating digestive and respiratory ailments. Their presence enhances the overall therapeutic potential of Common Avens.
