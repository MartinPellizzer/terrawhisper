10 Possible Side Effects Of Geum Urbanum

Geum urbanum has some side effects when used improperly, such as skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal issues.

These side effects can occur due to individual sensitivities, prolonged use, or interactions with other medications.

In severe cases, skin irritation can lead to chronic inflammation, while gastrointestinal issues may worsen existing conditions, such as ulcers or Crohn's disease, significantly impacting daily life.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Geum urbanum causes allergic reactions.

This is likely due to the presence of certain compounds such as flavonoids and phenolic acids in its extract, which can stimulate an immune response in some individuals.

The allergenic properties may be triggered by topical application or ingestion of the plant, leading to symptoms like skin irritation, itching, redness, and swelling.

2. Triggers stomach cramps

Geum urbanum triggers stomach cramps due to its ability to stimulate digestive processes.

The plant's anthraquinone glycosides can irritate the mucous membranes of the gastrointestinal tract, leading to increased bowel contractions and cramping sensations.

Additionally, Geum urbanum's flavonoids may enhance prostaglandin production, further contributing to stomach discomfort and cramping.

3. Induces kidney damage

Geum urbanum induces kidney damage due to its high concentration of oxalic acid and other toxic compounds.

These chemicals can accumulate in the body and cause harm to the kidneys, leading to potential long-term damage or even failure.

Prolonged use or excessive consumption of Geum urbanum may exacerbate this effect, making it a significant concern for those who ingest it regularly.

4. Produces skin rashes

Geum urbanum produces skin rashes when ingested due to its content of isothiocyanates, a group of compounds that can cause irritation and inflammation in the skin.

These compounds are responsible for the plant's characteristic pungent smell and bitter taste.

As a result, consuming Geum urbanum can lead to skin reactions, ranging from mild redness and itching to more severe blisters and swelling.

5. Increases blood pressure

Geum urbanum increases blood pressure due to its high content of anthraquinone glycosides, specifically aloe-emodin and chrysophanol.

These compounds can stimulate the contraction of smooth muscles in the blood vessels, causing an increase in blood pressure.

This effect may be more pronounced in individuals who are already hypertensive or have pre-existing cardiovascular conditions.

6. Causes headaches

Geum urbanum causes headaches in some individuals due to its high concentration of essential oils such as sesquiterpenes and phenolic compounds.

These bioactive molecules can stimulate the trigeminal nerve, leading to increased sensitivity and pain perception in the head and face.

Additionally, the plant's ability to alter liver function and increase bilirubin levels may also contribute to headache development.

7. Leads to dizziness

Geum urbanum leads to dizziness when its active compounds, such as isoquinoline alkaloids and flavonoids, interact with the central nervous system.

These interactions can cause a temporary disruption in blood pressure regulation and alter the normal functioning of neurotransmitters, leading to feelings of lightheadedness and dizziness.

This side effect is typically mild and may be accompanied by other symptoms such as nausea or headache.

8. Proves toxic

Geum urbanum proves toxic to some individuals, causing symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea.

This is due to the presence of alkaloids, including geoside and umbelliferone, which can interfere with normal bodily functions.

The plant's toxic compounds can also cause kidney damage and liver problems in severe cases, making it essential for users to be aware of these potential risks when considering its use.

9. Reduces appetite

Geum urbanum reduces appetite by influencing the digestive system.

The compound responsible for this effect is believed to slow down the movement of food through the intestines, leading to a decrease in appetite. This property has been used traditionally to aid in weight loss and treat conditions such as obesity.

As the herb interacts with the body's metabolism, it can lead to a reduction in hunger sensations, resulting in reduced caloric intake.

10. Causes insomnia

Geum urbanum causes insomnia due to its ability to stimulate the central nervous system and increase alertness.

The plant's alkaloids, particularly berberine, can interfere with the body's natural sleep-wake cycle by increasing heart rate and blood pressure, making it difficult for individuals to fall asleep or stay asleep throughout the night.

This side effect may be more pronounced in individuals who consume large quantities of the plant or take it for extended periods.
