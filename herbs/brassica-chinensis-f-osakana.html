The bioactive constituents of Japanese mustard green include glucosinolates, isothiocyanates, and other polyphenolic compounds that exhibit therapeutic actions such as anti-cancer, anti-inflammatory, and anti-bacterial properties.

Possible Side Effects of Brassica chinensis f. osakana

Furthermore, individuals with certain allergies or sensitivities may experience skin irritation, itching, or hives after consuming brassica chinensis f. osakana, particularly if they have a history of allergies to other Brassica species.
