Cinnamomum camphora

What's the taxonomical classification of Cinnamomum camphora?

Cinnamomum camphorum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Laurales. This plant is a member of the family Lauraceae, situated within the genus Cinnamomum, and is specifically identified by its species name, camphorum.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Cinnamomum camphor contains camphor, cineole, and various terpenes that provide significant therapeutic benefits. The primary component, camphor, acts as a powerful analgesic and anti-inflammatory agent when applied topically. Its volatile oils also possess antimicrobial and antiseptic properties that help combat fungal and bacterial infections. Additionally, the presence of cineole contributes to respiratory relief by acting as an expectorant. These diverse chemical constituents make the plant a valuable resource in traditional and modern pharmacology.

What are the therapeutic actions of Cinnamomum camphora?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat respiratory infections, skin ailments, digestive disorders, and inflammatory conditions. The essential oils extracted from the leaves provide antimicrobial and antiseptic properties that help fight bacteria and fungi. When applied topically, the camphor content can soothe muscle aches and alleviate itchy skin irritations. Inhalation of its aromatic compounds is often utilized to clear congestion and ease symptoms of coughs or colds. Additionally, certain preparations may assist in regulating digestion and reducing abdominal discomfort.
