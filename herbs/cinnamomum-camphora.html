Side Effects of cinnamomum camphora

Cinnamomum camphora causes eye irritation due to its volatile oils, which can lead to redness, burning, and excessive tearing when they come into contact with the eyes.

The herb may also cause skin irritation, resulting in redness, itching, and a rash, particularly in individuals with sensitive skin. Prolonged exposure or improper use of the herb can lead to gastrointestinal upset, including nausea and stomach discomfort, as its compounds may irritate the digestive tract. Additionally, some people may experience allergic reactions, such as skin itching or throat irritation, after using products containing cinnamomum camphora.

These side effects highlight the importance of using the herb with caution and consulting a healthcare professional, especially for those with known sensitivities or pre-existing conditions.
