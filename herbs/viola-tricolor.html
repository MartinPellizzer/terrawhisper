Three-color violet

Viola tricolor

Three-Color Violet (Viola tricolor) is a flowering plant known for its vibrant purple, blue, and white petals, commonly found in temperate regions, and has been used for centuries in traditional medicine to treat respiratory and skin conditions.

Botanical Identification

Viola tricolor, commonly known as the Three-Color Violet, belongs to the Violaceae family. It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and flowers with three distinct colors—purple, white, and yellow. It is also referred to as the Heartsease or Gundermann in some regions.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Eudicots
  • Order: Violales
  • Family: Violaceae
  • Genus: Viola
  • Species: Viola tricolor

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory conditions and as a calming tea.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and as a general tonic.

Powder: Used for topical applications and as a dietary supplement.

Safety Profile

Three-Color Violet is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation due to limited safety data. Individuals with allergies to the Violaceae family should also exercise caution.
