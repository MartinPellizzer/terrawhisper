Dendrobium bellatulum

What's the taxonomical classification of Dendrobium bellatulum?

Dendrobium bellatulum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Asparagales and is a member of the family Orchidaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Dendrobium and is specifically designated as the species bellatulum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dendrobium bellatulum has elongated, fleshy pseudobulbs that are distinctly cylindrical and segmented to facilitate water storage. These thick stems are covered in small, protective leaf scars from previous growth cycles. The leaves are lanceolate in shape and emerge from the upper nodes of the pseudobulbs. Its flowers are characterized by large, spreading petals and a prominent, intricate labellum. The entire structure is designed to thrive in epiphytic environments by maximizing surface area for light absorption while conserving moisture.
