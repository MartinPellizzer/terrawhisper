Sphaeranthus senegalensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Sphaeranthus senegalensis?

Sphaeranthus senegalensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae, which is a large and diverse group of flowering plants. Specifically, it is situated within the genus Sphaeranthus, and its unique biological identity is defined by its specific epithet, senegalensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sphaeranthus senegalensis has erect, branched stems that reach heights of up to 1 meter and bear opposite, lanceolate to ovate leaves with entire margins. The foliage typically features prominent venation and a slightly pubescent texture on both surfaces. Its inflorescence consists of dense, globose heads containing numerous small, tubular florets arranged in a spherical cluster. These flower heads are often terminal or situated in the leaf axils, displaying a characteristic pale or whitish hue. The plant produces small, dry fruits that facilitate its dispersal within its natural habitat.
