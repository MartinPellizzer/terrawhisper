What is Mucuna mitis?

Mucuna mitis is a climbing plant that belongs to the family Fabaceae, native to the tropical forests of Africa and Asia.

It has pinnate leaves with 5-7 pairs of leaflets and yellow flowers that are arranged in axillary racemes. The plant produces flat, long pods that contain 3-6 seeds, each seed being approximately 2-3 cm in length. The stem is woody, with a diameter of up to 1 cm, and has a greyish-brown bark.

The plant grows up to 10 meters in length, with a leafy canopy that shades the ground, and can thrive in shaded or partially shaded areas.
