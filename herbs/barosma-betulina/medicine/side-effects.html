10 Possible Side Effects Of Barosma Betulina

Barosma betulina has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal upset, nausea, and vomiting.

These side effects can occur due to allergic reactions, overdosing, or interactions with other medications.

For instance, a person experiencing persistent vomiting may struggle to keep food down, leading to malnutrition and energy loss, affecting their daily productivity and quality of life.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Barosma betulina causes allergic reactions due to its high content of sesquiterpenes, particularly bornyl acetate.

These compounds can trigger an immune response in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as skin irritation, respiratory issues, and gastrointestinal disturbances.

The severity of these reactions can vary widely depending on the individual's sensitivity to the plant's active ingredients.

2. Triggers stomach upset

Barosma betulina triggers stomach upset due to its bitter and acidic properties, which can irritate the mucous membranes in the digestive tract.

The herb's high concentration of essential oils, particularly limonene and pinene, may also contribute to this effect by altering the normal functioning of the gut and stimulating the secretion of gastric acid.

3. Induces diarrhea

Barosma betulina induces diarrhea due to its high concentration of iridoid glycosides, specifically aucubin and catalpol.

These compounds have been shown to stimulate the gut and increase gut motility, leading to frequent bowel movements and watery stools.

Additionally, the herb's bitter properties can irritate the digestive tract, further contributing to this side effect.

4. Produces vomiting

Barosma betulina produces vomiting due to its stimulating effects on the digestive system.

The herb's volatile oils can irritate the stomach lining and increase gut motility, leading to nausea and vomiting in some individuals. Additionally, Barosma betulina contains coumarin, a compound that may interfere with blood clotting and cause gastrointestinal upset.

These properties of the herb can result in vomiting as an adverse effect for some users.

5. Increases heart rate

Barosma betulina increases heart rate due to its stimulating properties, which can cause an acceleration of cardiac activity.

The plant's essential oils, particularly cineole and pulegone, are believed to be responsible for this effect.

When ingested or used topically, these compounds can bind to receptors in the body, leading to increased sympathetic nervous system activity and a subsequent rise in heart rate.

6. Decreases blood pressure

Barosma betulina decreases blood pressure due to its ability to relax the smooth muscle in the blood vessels, which causes them to dilate and reduce blood pressure.

This is attributed to the presence of various compounds such as ursolic acid, oleanolic acid, and sesquiterpenoids, which exhibit vasodilatory properties.

As a result, buchu may lead to dizziness, lightheadedness, or even fainting in individuals who already have low blood pressure or are taking antihypertensive medications.

7. Causes dizziness

Barosma betulina causes dizziness due to its potential interaction with certain medications, such as blood thinners and antihypertensives.

The herb's high concentration of coumarins, a class of compounds known for their blood-clotting properties, may alter the body's ability to regulate blood pressure, leading to feelings of lightheadedness or dizziness.

Additionally, buchu's volatile oils may affect the nervous system, contributing to this side effect.

8. Leads to headaches

Barosma betulina leads to headaches when consumed in excessive amounts or for extended periods.

The essential oils present in the herb, particularly limonene and pinene, can stimulate the nervous system and cause an increase in blood flow to the brain. This may lead to feelings of dizziness, lightheadedness, and eventually, a pounding headache.

The diuretic properties of buchu can also contribute to dehydration, further exacerbating these symptoms.

9. Creates insomnia

Barosma betulina creates insomnia due to its stimulating properties, which can interfere with normal sleep patterns.

The plant's active compounds, such as borneol and sesquiterpenes, are believed to increase alertness and energy levels, making it difficult for individuals to fall asleep or stay asleep throughout the night.

As a result, users may experience restlessness, difficulty concentrating, and a sense of anxiety, further exacerbating the issue of insomnia.

10. Provokes anxiety attacks

Barosma betulina provokes anxiety attacks due to its high concentration of volatile oils and flavonoids.

These compounds can interact with the body's neurotransmitters, such as serotonin and dopamine, leading to feelings of unease, tension, and apprehension.

Additionally, the herb's bitter taste and pungent aroma may also contribute to heightened emotional states, exacerbating anxiety symptoms in some individuals.
