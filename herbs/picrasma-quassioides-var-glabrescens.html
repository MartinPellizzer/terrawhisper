Quassia (Picrasma Quassioides Var. Glabrescens)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Picrasma quassioides var. glabrescens.

Quassia, scientifically known as Picrasma quassioides var. glabrescens, is a medicinal herb native to Central and South America, widely recognized for its adaptogenic and anti-inflammatory properties. It is traditionally used in indigenous and traditional medicine systems to support digestive health, reduce fever, and alleviate pain, with its bark and leaves containing compounds like quassinoids that contribute to its therapeutic effects. In modern wellness practices, Quassia is increasingly valued for its potential to support immune function, reduce stress, and aid in weight management, often incorporated into herbal teas and supplements. One of its unique features is its distinctive bitter taste, which is attributed to its high concentration of quassinoids, making it a rare and potent natural compound with historical use dating back centuries. Despite its limited commercial availability, Quassia remains a significant plant in both traditional and contemporary herbal medicine for its broad range of health benefits.
