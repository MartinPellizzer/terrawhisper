What's the taxonomical classification of Paraboea crassifolia?

Paraboea crassifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the species is situated in the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Gesneriaceae. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Paraboea, with the binomial name being assigned as the species crassifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Paraboea crassifolia has perennial, tufted, or stoloniferous rhizomatous herbs characterized by linear to lanceolate leaves that are fleshy and thick. The flowers feature delicate, pale pink to white petals and are supported by short pedicels. Small, globose capsules serve as the fruit, containing numerous tiny seeds for reproduction.
