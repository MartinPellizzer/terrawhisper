Ficus langkokensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Ficus langkokensis?

Ficus langkokensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Rosales. This plant is a member of the family Moraea (Moraeceae), falling under the genus Ficus, and is specifically identified by its species name, langkokensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ficus langkokensis has an arborescent growth habit characterized by woody stems, large elliptical leaves with prominent venation, and specialized reproductive structures known as syconia. The foliage typically features an entire margin and a coriaceous texture that aids in moisture retention. Its leaves are arranged in an alternate pattern along the branches, often displaying a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, fleshy figs that serve as the primary site for pollination by specialized wasps. These syconia develop from an inflorescence enclosed within a hollow receptacle, which is a defining morphological trait of the genus.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

The species is often found growing as an epiphyte or hemi-epiphyte on larger host trees throughout these equatorial zones. Its distribution is closely tied to the presence of dense canopy cover which provides the necessary shade and moisture levels.
