Wild Strawberry (Fragaria vesca) Compounds

Wild Strawberry contains several primary compounds, including flavonoids, which are antioxidants that support cardiovascular health. Phenolic acids and tannins contribute to its anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarin and anthocyanin provide additional antioxidant benefits, while glycosides and saponins may support digestive health. Terpenoids, essential oils, and alkaloids further contribute to its medicinal profile.

Wild Strawberry (Fragaria vesca) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Wild Strawberry (Fragaria vesca) contribute to its antioxidant properties. These compounds help neutralize free radicals, supporting cellular health. They may also reduce inflammation and enhance immune function. Flavonoids are a key group of phytochemicals in this plant's therapeutic profile.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid in Wild Strawberry (Fragaria vesca) contributes to its antioxidant properties. These compounds help neutralize free radicals, supporting cellular health. They may also aid in reducing inflammation and enhancing immune function. Phenolic acids are found in the fruit's skin and seeds.

3. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Wild Strawberry (Fragaria vesca). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins in Wild Strawberry may support digestive health and wound healing. They are found in the fruit's skin and seeds.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Wild Strawberry (Fragaria vesca). It contributes to the plant's anticoagulant properties and may support cardiovascular health. Coumarin also has mild sedative effects and is used in traditional herbal medicine for its calming and anti-inflammatory actions.

5. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin, found in Wild Strawberry (Fragaria vesca), is a powerful antioxidant that contributes to the plant's vibrant red color. It supports cardiovascular health and may reduce inflammation, making it a valuable component in herbal medicine for its protective and therapeutic properties.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Wild Strawberry (Fragaria vesca). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by supporting cardiovascular health and acting as an antioxidant. Glycosides in Wild Strawberry are often associated with anti-inflammatory and diuretic effects.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Wild Strawberry (Fragaria vesca). It exhibits anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, supporting the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine. Saponin contributes to the plant's ability to address digestive and skin-related ailments.

8. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Wild Strawberry (Fragaria vesca) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting the plant's traditional use in treating ailments like digestive issues and skin conditions. They also enhance the plant's aroma and may aid in its therapeutic applications.

9. Essential oil

Essential oil from Wild Strawberry (Fragaria vesca) contains aromatic compounds like limonene and methyl salicylate. These oils are used in herbal medicine for their anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. They support digestive health and are applied topically for skin conditions. The oil is extracted through steam distillation from the plant's leaves and flowers.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Wild Strawberry (Fragaria vesca). It contributes to the plant's potential medicinal properties, though research on its specific effects is limited. Alkaloids in this species may support traditional uses in herbal medicine, though further studies are needed to confirm their efficacy.
