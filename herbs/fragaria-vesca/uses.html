Wild Strawberry (Fragaria vesca) Uses

Wild Strawberry is used to treat inflammation, allergic reactions, and inflammatory skin conditions. It helps reduce skin irritation and infections, supporting wound healing and combating skin aging. Its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties make it effective for various inflammatory and dermatological issues.

Wild Strawberry (Fragaria vesca) Monograph

1. Inflammation

Wild Strawberry (Fragaria vesca) has anti-inflammatory properties due to its high content of antioxidants and polyphenols. These compounds help reduce inflammation by inhibiting inflammatory pathways in the body. It is often used topically for skin irritations and internally for conditions like arthritis and digestive inflammation.

2. Allergic reactions

Wild Strawberry (Fragaria vesca) may cause allergic reactions in some individuals, particularly those sensitive to other plants in the Rosaceae family. Symptoms can include skin rash, itching, or gastrointestinal discomfort. It is important to consult a healthcare provider before using it for medicinal purposes if you have a known allergy.

3. Allergies

Wild Strawberry (Fragaria vesca) has been traditionally used to support allergy relief due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties. It contains compounds that may help reduce allergic reactions and soothe symptoms like sneezing and skin irritation. Its use in herbal medicine often involves consuming the fruit or making teas from its leaves.

4. Inflammatory conditions

Wild Strawberry (Fragaria vesca) has been traditionally used to address inflammatory conditions due to its high antioxidant and anti-inflammatory compounds, such as polyphenols and flavonoids. It may help reduce inflammation in the joints, skin, and digestive system, supporting overall immune function and tissue repair.

5. Skin irritation

Wild Strawberry (Fragaria vesca) has been used traditionally to soothe skin irritation. Its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties help reduce redness and promote healing. The fruit's high vitamin C content supports skin regeneration. It can be applied topically as a poultice or infused oil for localized relief.

6. Wound healing

Wild Strawberry (Fragaria vesca) has been traditionally used to support wound healing due to its high content of antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds. The fruit's astringent properties help reduce bleeding and promote tissue repair. It is often applied topically as a poultice or infused oil to accelerate the healing process and prevent infection.

7. Skin aging

Wild Strawberry contains antioxidants and polyphenols that help combat oxidative stress, a key contributor to skin aging. Its anti-inflammatory properties may also support skin health and repair. Regular topical application can enhance skin texture and reduce the appearance of wrinkles.

8. Skin inflammation

Wild Strawberry (Fragaria vesca) is used topically to treat skin inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. The leaves and fruits contain compounds that reduce redness and soothe irritated skin. It is often applied as a poultice or infused oil to address conditions like eczema and rashes. Its mild nature makes it suitable for sensitive skin.

9. Inflammatory skin conditions

Wild Strawberry (Fragaria vesca) has been traditionally used to soothe inflammatory skin conditions due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Topical application of its infused oils or poultices can reduce redness, swelling, and irritation associated with eczema, psoriasis, and minor burns. Its astringent qualities also help to tighten and calm sensitive skin.

10. Skin infections

Wild Strawberry (Fragaria vesca) has been traditionally used to treat skin infections due to its antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties. The plant contains compounds like ellagic acid and vitamin C, which help reduce infection and promote skin healing. Topical applications of its leaves or extracts can soothe rashes and prevent bacterial growth on the skin.
