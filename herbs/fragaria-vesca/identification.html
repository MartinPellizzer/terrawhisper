Botanical Identification of Wild Strawberry (Fragaria vesca)

To identify Wild Strawberry (Fragaria vesca) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Wild Strawberry, or Fragaria vesca, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Rosales, family Rosaceae, genus Fragaria, and species Fragaria vesca. This classification places it within the rose family, highlighting its botanical relationships and characteristics.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Wild Strawberry is Fragaria vesca, first described by Linnaeus in 1753. It is also known by various synonyms such as Fragaria indica and Fragaria virgata. Common names include Wild Strawberry, May berry, and Forest strawberry. Regional and historical names vary, reflecting its widespread use in traditional herbal medicine.

Morphological Characteristics

Wild Strawberry (Fragaria vesca) is a low-growing perennial herb with a fibrous root system. Its stems are prostrate, often trailing along the ground. Leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the inflorescence consists of a single flower per stem. Flowers are white with five petals, and the fruit is a red, fleshy berry containing many small seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Wild Strawberry (Fragaria vesca) can be distinguished by its trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, pinkish-white flowers with five petals, and small red berries with a hairy surface. It differs from similar species like Fragaria × ananassa by smaller size, fewer petals, and less robust growth habits.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Wild Strawberry (Fragaria vesca) is native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North America. It thrives in mild climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils and is commonly found at elevations between 0 and 2000 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Wild Strawberry (Fragaria vesca) blooms from late spring to early summer, typically May to June. Seed formation occurs in late summer, around July to August. For identification purposes, the fruit is ready for harvest in late summer to early fall, usually August to September.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Wild Strawberry (Fragaria vesca) belongs to the genus Fragaria in the family Rosaceae. It is closely related to other strawberry species like Fragaria × ananassa. Within the genus, it shares traits such as trifoliate leaves and aggregate fruits. Its family traits include simple leaves, stipules, and flowers with five petals.
