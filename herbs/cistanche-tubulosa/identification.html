Botanical Identification of Cistanche (Cistanche tubulosa)

To identify Cistanche (Cistanche tubulosa) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Cistanche tubulosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Lamiales, order Orobanchales, family Orobanchaceae, genus Cistanche, and species Cistanche tubulosa. This classification places it within the group of flowering plants known as the broomrape family.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of the plant is Cistanche tubulosa, first described by the botanist Adolphe-Théodore Brisseau de Lapalme in 1820. It has several synonyms, including Cistanche chinensis and Cistanche salsa, reflecting its historical classification and regional variations. Common names include the Chinese parasitic weed and Indian rhatany, while regional names vary across different cultures and languages.

Morphological Characteristics

Cistanche tubulosa is a parasitic plant with a branching root system that attaches to host plants. It has a yellowish, fleshy stem that is usually 10-30 cm long. The plant lacks true leaves, instead having scale-like structures. Its inflorescence is a terminal spike with small, yellowish flowers. The fruit is a capsule containing numerous tiny seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Cistanche tubulosa can be distinguished by its yellow-orange stems, lack of leaves, and presence of yellow flowers in clusters. It is often confused with other parasitic species, but its unique coloration and floral arrangement help in accurate identification. Key features include its fleshy, branching stems and absence of true leaves.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Cistanche tubulosa is native to arid and semi-arid regions of China, particularly in Gansu, Qinghai, and Sichuan. It thrives in hot, dry climates with well-drained sandy or loamy soils. It typically grows at altitudes between 1,500 and 3,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Cistanche tubulosa blooms from July to August, producing yellow to orange flowers. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early autumn. Harvesting is typically done in late autumn when the stems turn reddish-brown, indicating maturity for identification and medicinal use.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Cistanche tubulosa is most closely related to species within the genus Cistanche, particularly Cistanche monilifera. It belongs to the family Orobanchaceae, characterized by parasitic habits and reduced leaves. Its phylogenetic placement reflects shared traits with other members of this family, such as specialized root structures and floral morphology.
