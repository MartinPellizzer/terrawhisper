10 Possible Side Effects Of Cistanche Tubulosa

Cistanche tubulosa has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal problems, nausea, and diarrhea, which can be caused by excessive consumption, allergic reactions, or interactions with other medications.

These side effects can worsen people's lives by causing fatigue, weight loss, and social isolation, ultimately impacting daily productivity and relationships.

1. Causes nausea to occur

Cistanche tubulosa causes nausea to occur when consumed due to its unique chemical composition.

The saponins and alkaloids present in the herb can irritate the digestive system, leading to stomach upset and queasiness.

Additionally, the herb's ability to stimulate digestion and absorption may also cause an imbalance in gut bacteria, further exacerbating nausea symptoms.

2. Induces allergic reactions

Cistanche tubulosa induces allergic reactions in some individuals.

The unique compounds present in this herb, such as alkaloids and phenolic acids, can stimulate the immune system, leading to an overactive response that results in symptoms like itching, hives, and difficulty breathing.

This hypersensitivity reaction occurs when the body mistakenly identifies these compounds as foreign invaders, triggering a chain of events that culminates in an allergic response.

3. Triggers stomach cramps

Cistanche tubulosa triggers stomach cramps due to its high concentration of alkaloids and glycosides.

These compounds can stimulate the digestive system and cause spasms in the muscles lining the intestines, leading to abdominal pain and discomfort.

Additionally, Cistanche tubulosa's bitter taste and astringent properties may also irritate the digestive tract and contribute to stomach cramps.

4. Produces dizziness suddenly

Cistanche tubulosa produces dizziness suddenly due to its high concentration of alkaloids, particularly cistanchesaponins.

These compounds can cause a sudden and intense stimulation of the central nervous system, leading to feelings of lightheadedness, vertigo, and even fainting in some individuals.

5. Increases blood pressure rapidly

Cistanche tubulosa increases blood pressure rapidly due to its rich content of tyrosol and dopamine.

These compounds can cause vasoconstriction, which leads to a sudden increase in blood pressure. Additionally, the herb's ability to stimulate the adrenal glands can further contribute to this side effect.

As a result, individuals may experience a rapid rise in their blood pressure when using Cistanche tubulosa.

6. Lowers blood sugar levels

Cistanche tubulosa lowers blood sugar levels due to its ability to inhibit the activity of alpha-glucosidase enzymes in the gut, thereby reducing carbohydrate absorption and decreasing glucose uptake by the body.

This mechanism can cause a decrease in blood sugar levels, particularly in individuals with pre-existing diabetes or hypoglycemia.

The plant's bioactive compounds, such as cistanosides and phenolic acids, are responsible for this effect, making it essential to monitor glucose levels closely when using Cistanche tubulosa supplements.

7. Worsens kidney function

Cistanche tubulosa worsens kidney function by potentially increasing the risk of kidney damage due to its high content of oxalates and other nephrotoxins.

These compounds can bind to calcium in the kidneys, forming insoluble salts that can cause kidney stones or further exacerbate existing kidney problems.

8. Interferes with sleep quality

Cistanche tubulosa interferes with sleep quality by stimulating the nervous system and increasing alertness.

This is due to its high content of alkaloids, such as cistanchesaponins, which can cause an increase in cortisol levels and disrupt the body's natural sleep-wake cycle.

Additionally, Cistanche tubulosa may also affect the regulation of serotonin and melatonin, leading to difficulty falling asleep and reduced quality of sleep.

9. Enhances anxiety symptoms

Cistanche tubulosa enhances anxiety symptoms due to its potential to stimulate the central nervous system.

The herb contains compounds such as flavonoids and alkaloids that can affect serotonin levels in the brain, leading to increased feelings of unease and apprehension.

Additionally, Cistanche tubulosa's ability to increase heart rate and blood pressure may further contribute to anxiety symptoms.

10. Stimulates heart palpitations

Cistanche tubulosa stimulates heart palpitations due to its high content of saponins and alkaloids.

These compounds can affect the nervous system, leading to increased heart rate and rhythmic irregularities.

Additionally, Cistanche tubulosa's ability to stimulate the sympathetic nervous system can further contribute to palpitations by increasing blood pressure and cardiac output.
