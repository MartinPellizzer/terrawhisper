What's the taxonomical classification of Wrightia laevis?

Wrightia laevis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Gentianales and is a member of the family Apocynaceae. Specifically, it is identified under the genus Wrightia, with its unique biological identity defined by the species name laevis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Wrightia laevis has a woody shrub or small tree structure characterized by smooth, greyish bark and simple, entire-margined leaves that are arranged in an opposite pattern. The leaves are typically elliptical or oblong in shape with acute tips and a lustrous, dark green surface. Small, white, tubular flowers emerge from the axils, often possessing a delicate fragrance and five spreading lobes. The inflorescence is generally arranged in small cymes, adding a subtle textural element to the foliage.
