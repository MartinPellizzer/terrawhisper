What is Pelargonium gracile?

Pelargonium gracile is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is a succulent perennial herb that grows up to 40 cm in height, with a woody base and a branched stem. The leaves are fleshy, ovate to elliptical in shape, 2-5 cm long, and have a crenate margin. The flowers are pink to purple in color, 3-4 cm in diameter, and have 5 petals with a notched tip.

The plant is native to the Western Cape Province of South Africa, where it grows in sandy soil in full sun.
