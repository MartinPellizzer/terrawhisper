Dioscorea japonica

What's the taxonomical classification of Dioscorea japonica?

Dioscorea japonica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized under the order Dioscoreales and is a member of the family Dioscoreaceae. At the most specific levels of its biological classification, it is identified as belonging to the genus Dioscorea and is specifically defined by the species name japonica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dioscorea japonica has climbing stems that reach several meters in length, heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins, and small, greenish-white or yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. The plant utilizes twining stems to ascend surrounding vegetation or structures. Its leaves are typically cordate in shape and possess a smooth or slightly textured surface. The inflorescence consists of inconspicuous blossoms that appear during the growing season. Underground, the species develops fleshy tubers that serve as primary energy storage organs.

What are the pharmacological activities of Dioscorea japonica?

This plant has antioxidant, immunomodulatory, estrogen-stimulating, antidepressant, and anti-bone resorption pharmacological activities. The bioactive compounds within Dioscorea japonica help neutralize free radicals to mitigate oxidative stress in various tissues. Its ability to modulate the immune system supports balanced inflammatory responses throughout the body. Additionally, the plant exhibits estrogen-like effects that can assist in hormonal regulation. These properties also extend to mental health support through antidepressant mechanisms and the prevention of bone loss via anti-bone resorption actions.

As discussed in a study, Dioscorea japonica THUNB is one of seven herbs comprised in the herbal medicine Yukmi-jihang-tang (YJ). When used at a concentration of 100microg/ml, YJ reduced COX-2 mRNA levels, PGE2 biosynthesis, and bone resorption induced by IL-1beta, TNF-alpha, IL-6, or their combination. The YJ formulation inhibited parathyroid hormone-induced bone resorption in mouse fetal long bone cultures with an IC(50) of 16microg/ml. This medicine dose-dependently reduced hypercalcemia induced in mice by IL-1beta and prevented bone loss and microarchitectural changes in young ovariectomized rats. These findings indicate that YJ, which includes Dioscorea japonica THUNB, is effective for bone resorptive action in bone cells.
