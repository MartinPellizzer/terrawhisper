What's the taxonomical classification of Asplenium ruta-muraria?

Asplenium ruta-muraria belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the family Aspleniaceae and the genus Asplenium, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species ruta-muraria.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Asplenium ruta-muraria has a creeping rhizome that produces small, dark green, lanceolate fronds measuring between 5 and 15 centimeters in length with slightly wavy margins. The individual pinnae are arranged in a single plane and possess a distinctively pinnatifid structure. These fronds emerge from a short, compact stipe that lacks a prominent sheath. The plant typically forms dense, low-growing mats as the rhizomes spread across rocky substrates or walls. Sori are located on the underside of the leaves, appearing as small, linear clusters near the midrib.
