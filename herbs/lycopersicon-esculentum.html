Tomato

Lycopersicon esculentum

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Tomato (Lycopersicon esculentum) is a species in the nightshade family, Solanaceae. Common names include garden tomato and red tomato, though it is often referred to as a vegetable. It is an annual plant native to South America, with hairy stems, lobed leaves, and yellow flowers. Synonyms in herbal literature include "pomodoro" and "tomahto."

Active Compounds

The main active compounds of this herb include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and anthocyanins. These compounds work synergistically to provide antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Phenolic acid
  • Anthocyanin

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for digestive issues and respiratory conditions.

Decoction: Used for gastrointestinal ailments and inflammation.

Poultice: Used for skin irritations and minor wounds.

Safety Profile

Tomato (Lycopersicon esculentum) is generally safe when consumed in normal food amounts. However, it should be avoided by individuals with known allergies to nightshade plants. Large quantities may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some people.
