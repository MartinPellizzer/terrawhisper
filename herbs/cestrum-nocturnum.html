Cestrum nocturnum

What's the taxonomical classification of Cestrum nocturnum?

Cestrum nocturnum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its hierarchical lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Solanales. As a member of the family Solanaceae, it is further categorized under the genus Cestrum, ultimately reaching its specific identification as the species nocturnum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cestrum nocturnum has an evergreen, shrubby growth habit characterized by woody stems that typically reach heights of two to four meters. The plant features lanceolate to ovate leaves with entire margins and an alternate arrangement along the branches. Small, tubular, greenish-white flowers emerge in dense, drooping clusters known as panicles. These blossoms possess a waxy texture and are most notable for their intense fragrance during the night. The foliage remains dense throughout the year, providing a lush green backdrop for its nocturnal blooms.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical regions of the Caribbean, specifically originating from the islands of Cuba, Jamaica, and Hispaniola. It thrives in warm, humid environments where it can find suitable tropical climates. Due to its intense fragrance, it has been widely introduced to other subtropical and tropical areas around the world. Many gardeners in the southern United States and various coastal regions have established it in local landscapes. Its ability to adapt to diverse garden settings has allowed it to spread far beyond its original island habitats.

What are the pharmacological activities of Cestrum nocturnum?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological activities including antifungal, antitumor, cytotoxicity, and larvicidal properties. Research indicates that its extracts can effectively inhibit the growth of various fungal pathogens. Furthermore, the plant exhibits notable antitumor potential by targeting specific cancer cell lines through induced cytotoxicity. These cytotoxic effects demonstrate the ability of its bioactive compounds to disrupt cellular processes in malignant cells. Additionally, the plant has shown promise in controlling insect populations through its potent larvicidal activity.

As discussed in a study, the methanolic extracts of Cestrum nocturnum were investigated for their in vitro anticandiadal activity. This medicinal plant was found to be more potent than several other plants studied in the antimicrobial assay. The study utilized the agar well diffusion and the broth dilution method to evaluate these activities. Among the plants tested, Cestrum nocturnum and T. ammi demonstrated higher potency.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains flavonol glycosides, furostanol saponin, pseudo-furostanol saponin, steroidal glycosides, and steroidal saponins. These specific medicinal compounds contribute to the overall therapeutic profile and biological activities of the species. The presence of various steroidal glycosides and steroidal saponins often plays a role in its chemical defense and physiological impact. Additionally, the unique combination of furostanol saponin and pseudo-furostanol saponin distinguishes its chemical composition from related plants. Together, these bioactive molecules form the foundation of its pharmacological potential.

As discussed in a study, phytochemical analysis of the leaves of Cestrum nocturnum resulted in the isolation of two new flavonol glycosides, labeled 1 and 2. The study also identified seven steroidal saponins, numbered 3 through 9, which included four new compounds identified as 4, 6, 7, and 9. These specific compounds had their structures determined through spectroscopic analysis using 2D NMR data and results from hydrolytic cleavage. Furthermore, the research reported on the cytotoxic activities of these isolated compounds against human oral squamous cell carcinoma-HSC-2 cells and normal human gingival fibroblasts.

What are the herbal preparations of this plant?

This plant can be prepared as an infusion, a tincture, or an essential oil for various applications. What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause severe toxicity including nausea, vomiting, dizziness, abdominal pain, diarrhea, hallucinations, convulsions, and confusion. These dangerous symptoms typically arise from the ingestion of its toxic alkaloids. Once the toxins enter the bloodstream, a person may experience significant gastrointestinal distress such as abdominal pain and diarrhea. Neurological complications are also common, often manifesting as confusion, dizziness, or frightening hallucinations. In extreme cases of poisoning, the body may react with violent convulsions.
