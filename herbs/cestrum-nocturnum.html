Cestrum nocturnum

What's the taxonomical classification of Cestrum nocturnum?

Cestrum nocturnum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its hierarchical lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Solanales. As a member of the family Solanaceae, it is further categorized under the genus Cestrum, ultimately reaching its specific identification as the species nocturnum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cestrum nocturnum has an evergreen, shrubby growth habit characterized by woody stems that typically reach heights of two to four meters. The plant features lanceolate to ovate leaves with entire margins and an alternate arrangement along the branches. Small, tubular, greenish-white flowers emerge in dense, drooping clusters known as panicles. These blossoms possess a waxy texture and are most notable for their intense fragrance during the night. The foliage remains dense throughout the year, providing a lush green backdrop for its nocturnal blooms.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical regions of the Caribbean, specifically originating from the islands of Cuba, Jamaica, and Hispaniola.     What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

    This plant contains flavonol glycosides, furostanol saponin, pseudo-furostanol saponin, steroidal glycosides, and steroidal saponins as its primary medicinal compounds. These bioactive constituents are responsible for many of the plant's unique therapeutic properties. The presence of various steroidal glycosides and steroidal saponins suggests a complex chemical profile that may influence biological processes. Additionally, the specific combination of furostanol saponin and pseudo-furostanol saponin distinguishes its chemical makeup from related species. Together, these diverse secondary metabolites contribute to the plant's overall medicinal potential.

    As discussed in a study, phytochemical analysis of the leaves of Cestrum nocturnum resulted in the isolation of 2 new flavonol glycosides (1, 2) and 7 steroidal saponins (3-9). Among these isolated steroidal saponins, 4 were identified as new compounds (4, 6, 7, and 9). The structures for these compounds were determined using spectroscopic analysis including 2D NMR data and results from hydrolytic cleavage. The study also reported the cytotoxic activities of the isolated compounds against human oral squamous cell carcinoma (HSC-2) cells and normal human gingival fibroblasts.

      What side effects this plant can have?

      This plant can cause severe toxicity including nausea, vomiting, dizziness, abdominal pain, diarrhea, hallucinations, convulsions, and confusion. These dangerous symptoms typically arise from the ingestion of its toxic alkaloids. Once the toxins enter the bloodstream, a person may experience significant gastrointestinal distress such as abdominal pain and diarrhea. Neurological complications are also common, often manifesting as confusion, dizziness, or frightening hallucinations. In extreme cases of poisoning, the body may react with violent convulsions.
