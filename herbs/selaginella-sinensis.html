Selaginella sinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Lycopodiidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is placed in the order Selaginellales and the family Selaginellaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Selaginella, with its specific designation being the species sinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Selagineella sinensis has a creeping rhizome with small, scale-like leaves arranged in four distinct rows along the stem. The plant produces small, cone-like structures called strobili at the tips of the branches for reproduction. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains various bioactive medicinal compounds including flavonoids, amentoflavone, and several types of phenolic acids. According to a study published by "Biological & pharmaceutical bulletin", amentoflavone and three other flavonoids were isolated from the ethanol extract of Selaginella sinensis. Amentoflavone from this plant showed potent antiviral activity against respiratory syncytial virus with an IC50 of 5.5 microg/ml. The contents of amentoflavone in nine species of Selaginella were determined by reversed-phase HPLC. Selaginella sinensis showed a higher content of 1.13%.
