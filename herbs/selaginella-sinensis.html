Selaginella sinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Selaginella sinensis?

Selaginella sinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Lycopodiidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is placed in the order Selaginellales and the family Selaginellaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Selaginella, with its specific designation being the species sinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Selagineella sinensis has a creeping rhizome with small, scale-like leaves arranged in four distinct rows along the stem. The leaves are microphyllous, meaning they are tiny and possess a single unbranched vein. These diminutive leaves are often dark green and can appear slightly overlapping along the slender, branched stems. The plant produces small, cone-like structures called strobili at the tips of the branches for reproduction. These sporangia-bearing cones are typically compact and help define the plant's delicate, fern-like texture.
