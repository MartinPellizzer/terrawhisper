10 Health Benefits Of Pistacia Lentiscus (Mastich)

Pistacia lentiscus, also known as mastich, has health benefits such as anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, making it effective in soothing digestive issues, reducing stress and anxiety, and promoting wound healing.

These medicinal properties allow it to combat issues like ulcers and mouth infections, as well as ease respiratory problems. Improved digestive health can reduce symptoms of IBS and improve nutrient absorption.

Regular use can also enhance overall well-being and quality of life by reducing inflammation and promoting a healthy balance.

This article explains in details the 10 best health benefits of Pistacia lentiscus.

1. Reduces inflammation

Pistacia lentiscus reduces inflammation because of its rich content of flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids.

These bioactive compounds have potent anti-inflammatory properties, which help to alleviate symptoms of inflammation by inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes and cytokines.

The mastich's ability to scavenge free radicals also plays a crucial role in reducing oxidative stress, thus contributing to its anti-inflammatory effects.

2. Treats arthritis

Pistacia lentiscus treats arthritis because of its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The resin of the plant, extracted through a traditional process of chipping and masticating, contains compounds that have been shown to reduce joint pain and inflammation. Mastich has been used for centuries in traditional Mediterranean medicine to alleviate arthritis symptoms, offering a natural alternative to conventional treatments.

Its effectiveness is attributed to the antioxidant properties of its resin.

3. Relieves pain

Pistacia lentiscus relieves pain because of its unique combination of anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The resin of this evergreen shrub contains compounds such as terpenes and terpene esters, which have been shown to have potent pain-relieving effects.

Additionally, mastich's flavonoid content helps to reduce inflammation and alleviate discomfort, making it a popular remedy for various types of pain, including headaches and joint pain.

4. Fights cancer

Pistacia lentiscus fights cancer because of its unique combination of bioactive compounds, including terpenoids and phenolic acids, which have been shown to exhibit potent anti-tumor and anti-angiogenic activities.

These compounds have been found to inhibit the growth of cancer cells, induce apoptosis, and prevent angiogenesis, the formation of new blood vessels that feed tumor growth.

5. Lowers cholesterol

Pistacia lentiscus lowers cholesterol because it contains a unique combination of antioxidants, flavonoids, and terpenes.

These compounds have been shown to inhibit the absorption of dietary cholesterol in the gut, reduce the production of cholesterol in the liver, and increase the excretion of bile acids, which are made from cholesterol.

This results in a decrease in overall cholesterol levels, particularly LDL ("bad") cholesterol.

6. Prevents cardiovascular

Pistacia lentiscus prevents cardiovascular disease due to its rich antioxidant properties.

The essential oils present in mastich, particularly limonene and linalool, possess potent anti-inflammatory and vasodilatory effects. These compounds help to reduce blood pressure, prevent blood clotting, and improve lipid profiles, thereby lowering the risk of cardiovascular disease.

Regular consumption of mastich may contribute to a reduced incidence of heart attacks, strokes, and other cardiovascular conditions.

7. Regulates blood pressure

Pistacia lentiscus regulates blood pressure because it contains flavonoids and saponins that help to reduce blood vessel tension and improve circulation.

The anti-inflammatory properties of mastich also play a role in lowering blood pressure by reducing inflammation in blood vessels, which can contribute to high blood pressure.

Regular consumption of mastich is thought to help maintain healthy blood pressure levels.

8. Reduces stress

Pistacia lentiscus reduces stress because of its unique bioactive compounds.

Mastich contains terpenes, which have been shown to have a calming effect on the mind and body. The herb's fragrance is believed to promote relaxation, reduce anxiety, and improve sleep quality.

By stimulating the limbic system, mastich can help regulate emotions and promote a sense of well-being, making it a popular natural remedy for stress relief.

9. Improves digestion

Pistacia lentiscus improves digestion because its resin contains antioxidants and phenolic compounds that have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds can help reduce inflammation in the digestive tract, promoting a healthy balance of gut bacteria. Additionally, mastich's natural oils can stimulate digestive enzymes, facilitating the breakdown and absorption of nutrients.

Regular consumption may also alleviate symptoms of indigestion, bloating, and constipation.

10. Kills bacteria

Pistacia lentiscus kills bacteria because of its high concentration of antimicrobial compounds, particularly limonoids and terpenoids.

These compounds have been shown to disrupt bacterial cell membranes and inhibit the production of essential proteins, ultimately leading to cell death.

The antimicrobial properties of mastich make it a promising natural agent for preventing the growth and spread of various bacterial pathogens.
