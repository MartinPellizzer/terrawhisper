Lentisk (Pistacia lentiscus) Uses

Lentisk is used to treat psoriasis, eczema, and acne due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps with skin infections, wounds, and skin irritation by promoting healing and reducing inflammation. The plant's compounds may also aid in wound healing and managing skin eczema and inflammation.

Lentisk (Pistacia lentiscus) Monograph

1. Psoriasis

Lentisk has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to support skin health, particularly in managing psoriasis. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties may help reduce plaque buildup and soothe irritated skin. It is often prepared as a topical application or taken internally in small doses under professional guidance.

2. Eczema

Lentisk has been traditionally used to treat eczema due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Topical applications of its resin, known as mastic, can soothe skin irritation and reduce symptoms. It is often prepared as a salve or infused oil for direct application on affected areas. Its soothing effects make it a valuable remedy in herbal medicine for managing eczema.

3. Acne

Lentisk has been traditionally used to treat acne due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce bacterial growth on the skin and soothe inflammation, making it a valuable herb in herbal medicine for managing acne naturally.

4. Skin infections

Lentisk has been traditionally used to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It is often applied topically as a poultice or oil to alleviate symptoms of fungal infections, eczema, and minor wounds. Its ability to promote healing and reduce inflammation makes it a valued remedy in herbal medicine for skin-related ailments.

5. Wounds

Lentisk has been traditionally used to treat wounds due to its astringent and antimicrobial properties. It helps in stopping bleeding, reducing inflammation, and preventing infection. The resin can be applied topically to promote healing and close open wounds. It is often used in herbal first aid preparations for minor cuts and abrasions.

6. Skin irritation

Lentisk has been traditionally used to treat skin irritation due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It can help soothe rashes, eczema, and minor burns when applied topically. Its tinctures or infusions are often used in herbal remedies for their calming effect on the skin.

7. Skin eczema

Lentisk has been traditionally used to treat skin eczema due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. The resin, known as mastic, can be applied topically to reduce redness, itching, and irritation. It helps soothe the skin and promote healing in cases of eczema. Its natural compounds support skin regeneration and barrier function.

8. Skin inflammation

Lentisk has been traditionally used to treat skin inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and infection in conditions like eczema and dermatitis. External applications of its resin or extracts are common in herbal medicine for soothing irritated skin.

9. Skin wounds

Lentisk has been traditionally used to treat skin wounds due to its antimicrobial and astringent properties. It helps in reducing inflammation, promoting healing, and preventing infection. Applications include poultices, infusions, or topical salves made from its leaves or resin. It is valued for its ability to support skin regeneration and tissue repair.

10. Wound healing

Lentisk has been traditionally used to promote wound healing due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps prevent infection and reduces inflammation, supporting faster tissue repair. The resin, known as mastic, is often applied topically to clean and protect wounds. Its astringent qualities also aid in closing wounds and minimizing scarring.
