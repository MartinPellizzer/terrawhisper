Dahurian Angelica (Angelica dahurica) Compounds

The primary compounds of Dahurian Angelica include essential oil, coumarin, flavonoids, glycosides, phenolic acids, terpenoids, saponins, alkaloids, tannins, and anthocyanins. These compounds contribute to its medicinal properties, such as anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects.

Dahurian Angelica (Angelica dahurica) Monograph

1. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound of Dahurian Angelica (Angelica dahurica). It contains aromatic compounds like ligustilide and phellandrene, which contribute to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. This oil is commonly used in traditional medicine for treating respiratory and digestive ailments.

2. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Dahurian Angelica (Angelica dahurica). It contributes to the plant's anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarin is also responsible for the characteristic aroma of the herb. Its presence supports various therapeutic applications in herbal medicine.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Dahurian Angelica (Angelica dahurica) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help reduce oxidative stress and support immune function. They are commonly found in the roots and stems of the plant. Flavonoids may enhance the plant's therapeutic effects in traditional herbal medicine.

4. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Dahurian Angelica (Angelica dahurica). It contributes to the plant's therapeutic effects, including anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. Glycosides in this species are often found in the roots and are valued for their role in traditional herbal medicine.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Dahurian Angelica (Angelica dahurica). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments. This compound helps modulate immune responses and may aid in reducing oxidative stress in the body.

6. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Angelica dahurica contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, supporting the plant's use in traditional medicine for treating respiratory and digestive ailments. They also enhance the plant's overall therapeutic profile.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Dahurian Angelica (Angelica dahurica). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and immune-modulating properties. Saponins may enhance the absorption of other active constituents and support digestive health. Their presence underscores the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

8. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Dahurian Angelica (Angelica dahurica). It contributes to the plant's pharmacological properties, though it is less prominent than the sesquiterpene lactones. Alkaloids in this plant may support its traditional use in treating respiratory and inflammatory conditions.

9. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Dahurian Angelica (Angelica dahurica). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health by reducing inflammation and strengthening mucous membranes. Tannins are also valued for their antioxidant effects in herbal medicine.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Dahurian Angelica (Angelica dahurica). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting anti-inflammatory and immune-modulating effects. Anthocyanin also enhances the plant's therapeutic potential in traditional herbal medicine.
