Botanical Identification of Dahurian Angelica (Angelica dahurica)

To identify Dahurian Angelica (Angelica dahurica) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Dahurian Angelica belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Apiales, family Apiaceae, genus Angelica, and species Angelica dahurica. These classifications help identify its botanical relationships and place it within the broader context of plant life.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Dahurian Angelica is Angelica dahurica, first described by Carl von Linné in 1753. It is also known by several synonyms, including Angelica franchetii and Angelica sinensis var. dahurica. Common names include Dahurian Angelica, Chinese Angelica, and Bigroot Angelica. It is widely recognized in traditional Chinese medicine and has been used for centuries in herbal preparations.

Morphological Characteristics

Dahurian Angelica is a biennial herb with a thick, fleshy root. It has a straight, hollow stem that is usually branched. Its leaves are large, compound, and pinnately arranged. The inflorescence is a compound umbel, with small, greenish-white flowers. The fruit is a two-lobed, dry achene with a tuft of white hairs.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Dahurian Angelica can be distinguished by its large, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and its tall, branched stem. It produces small, white flowers in umbrella-like clusters. It is often confused with other angelicas, but its unique leaf shape and floral structure help in accurate identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Dahurian Angelica is native to eastern Asia, including China, Korea, and Russia. It thrives in temperate climates with cool summers and cold winters. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is typically found at altitudes between 500 and 2,000 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Dahurian Angelica blooms from June to August, with seed formation occurring in late summer to early autumn. Harvesting for identification purposes is typically done in late summer when the plant is mature but before the seeds have fully dispersed. The timing is crucial for accurate botanical identification.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Dahurian Angelica is closely related to other Angelica species within the Apiaceae family. It shares key characteristics with members of the genus, such as umbellate flowers and aromatic foliage. Its phylogenetic placement reflects its evolutionary ties to other umbelliferous plants, aiding in accurate identification and classification.
