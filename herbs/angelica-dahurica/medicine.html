What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Angelica Dahurica (Baikal Angelica)?

Angelica dahurica, also known as Baikal angelica, has health benefits such as reducing fever and alleviating pain.

The medicinal constituents of this herb include angelic acid, borneol, and borneolic acid, which have anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. Baikal angelica is often used to prepare teas, infusions, and poultices, which are applied topically to treat skin conditions.

However, excessive consumption can cause gastrointestinal upset, such as nausea and diarrhea, and individuals with bleeding disorders should consult a healthcare professional before using it.

This article explains the health benefits, active constituents, medicinal preparations, possible side effects, and precautions related to Angelica dahurica.

What are the health benefits of Angelica dahurica?

Angelica dahurica, also known as Baikal angelica, has health benefits such as reducing inflammation and alleviating pain.

Its extracts have been shown to exhibit anti-inflammatory properties, which can help alleviate symptoms of arthritis, gout, and other inflammatory conditions.

Additionally, Baikal angelica is believed to possess antimicrobial and antiviral properties, making it a potential treatment for respiratory infections and skin disorders.

What are the active constituents of Angelica dahurica?

The health benefits of Angelica dahurica, also known as Baikal angelica, come from its active constituents, such as ligustilide, butylphthalide, and senkyunolide.

These compounds possess anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and analgesic properties, which are responsible for the herb's traditional uses in treating various conditions like menstrual cramps, arthritis, and digestive disorders.

Additionally, Baikal angelica contains flavonoids and saponins that exhibit antioxidant and antimicrobial activities, further enhancing its medicinal value.

What are the medicinal preparations of Angelica dahurica?

Angelica dahurica has different medicinal preparations, such as dried root and rhizome, which are used to treat digestive issues like dyspepsia and constipation.

The root is often made into a decoction or tea, while the rhizome is processed into a powder or capsules. Additionally, extracts of Angelica dahurica are used to create oral pills and injections, primarily for treating respiratory conditions like bronchitis and asthma.

The powdered form can also be used topically to treat skin irritations and wounds.

What are the possible side effect of using Angelica dahurica improperly?

Improper use of Angelica dahurica increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as allergic reactions, skin rashes, gastrointestinal upset, nausea and vomiting, dizziness, headaches, insomnia, increased blood pressure, irregular heartbeat, decreased appetite, fatigue, joint pain, muscle weakness, respiratory issues, and even kidney damage.

In rare cases, it can also cause anaphylaxis, seizures, and liver toxicity, especially when used in high doses or for extended periods.

What precautions to take when using Angelica dahurica medicinally?

Before using Angelica dahurica for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as consulting with a healthcare professional to determine the appropriate dosage and potential interactions with other medications.

Additionally, be aware of individual tolerance levels, as some individuals may experience adverse reactions such as allergic contact dermatitis or gastrointestinal upset.

Proper identification and processing of the plant are also crucial to ensure quality and efficacy.
