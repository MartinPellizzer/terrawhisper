Scientific and Botanical Profile

Betony, with botanical name Stachys betonica, is a perennial herb belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is also known by several common names, including Woolly Betony, Wild Betony, Caraway Betony, and Stachys Betonica. Native to Europe, western Asia, and parts of North Africa, this plant thrives in moist meadows, woodlands, and stream banks. Morphologically, it features upright stems covered in fine hairs, opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and often hairy, and clusters of pink or purple flowers with two-lipped blooms.

Its aromatic leaves and medicinal properties have made it a valued plant in traditional herbal medicine.

Growing, Harvesting and Storage

Stachys betonica grows best in well-drained loamy soil with a slightly acidic to neutral pH, in full sun to partial shade, and requires consistent moisture without waterlogging.

It thrives in temperate climates and benefits from regular watering during dry periods, though it is somewhat drought-tolerant once established. To ensure healthy growth, mulching around the base of the plant helps retain soil moisture and suppress weeds, while periodic pruning encourages bushier growth and prevents disease. Harvesting is best done in late summer to early autumn when the leaves are most potent, using clean scissors to cut the stems just above the soil line to avoid damaging the plant.

For storage, the harvested plant material should be thoroughly dried in a cool, shaded area to prevent mold, then stored in airtight containers in a cool, dark place or refrigerated to preserve its medicinal potency.
