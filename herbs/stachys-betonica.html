Betony (Stachys betonica)

Betony (Stachys betonica) is a member of the Lamiaceae family, native to Europe, and Asia. Traditionally, its leaves, stems, and roots have been used for decoctions, infusions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its diuretic, anti-inflammatory, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and traditional chinese medicine.

Perennial herbaceous plant.

Leaves
Opposite, ovate leaves with dark green upper surface and lighter green lower surface, bearing prominent stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Inflorescence consists of dense spikes of actinomorphic flowers with violet-blue petals, five in number, and prominent yellow stamens arranged in two whorls.
Stems
Climbing, with opposite branching, hairy surface, and nodes with stipitate bases.
