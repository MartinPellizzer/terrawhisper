Pinus tabuliformis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pinus tabuliformis has a single-stemmed, erect trunk with reddish-brown bark that becomes deeply fissured into rectangular plates as the tree matures. The foliage consists of bundles of two needle-like leaves that are relatively long, slender, and dark green in color. These needles are typically arranged in pairs and are distributed throughout the crown to maximize sunlight absorption. The reproductive structures include woody cones that are ovoid in shape and feature thick, overlapping scales. Small resin glands are often present on the needles and young shoots, contributing to the plant's characteristic scent.

Pinus tabuliformis is prepared as decoctions from the bark, infusions from the needles, or distilled essential oils from the needles and resin. What side-effects this plant can have?

Pinus tabuliformis can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and respiratory discomfort if its pollen or sap comes into direct contact with sensitive individuals. The volatile oils and terpenes found within the needles may trigger redness or itching upon contact with the skin. Inhalation of concentrated particles from the tree's pollen can lead to sneezing, coughing, or worsening asthma symptoms in susceptible people. Some individuals may also experience mild headaches or nausea if they are exposed to high concentrations of its aromatic compounds in enclosed spaces. Always exercise caution when handling the plant to avoid these potential adverse reactions.
