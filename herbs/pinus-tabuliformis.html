Pinus tabuliformis

What's the taxonomical classification of Pinus tabuliformis?

Pinus tabuliformis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Pinidae, followed by the order Pinales. Within the family Pinaceae, this plant is situated in the genus Pinus, and its specific identification is completed by the species designation tabuliformis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pinus tabuliformis has a single-stemmed, erect trunk with reddish-brown bark that becomes deeply fissured into rectangular plates as the tree matures. The foliage consists of bundles of two needle-like leaves that are relatively long, slender, and dark green in color. These needles are typically arranged in pairs and are distributed throughout the crown to maximize sunlight absorption. The reproductive structures include woody cones that are ovoid in shape and feature thick, overlapping scales. Small resin glands are often present on the needles and young shoots, contributing to the plant's characteristic scent.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the southern portions of the Japanese archipelago, where it is primarily distributed across the islands of Kyushu, Shikoku, and Honshu. It thrives in mountainous regions and temperate forest ecosystems within these specific volcanic island chains. The species is often found at moderate elevations where soil conditions and moisture levels support its growth. While its range is relatively concentrated, it occupies various microclimates throughout the subtropical and temperate zones of Japan.
