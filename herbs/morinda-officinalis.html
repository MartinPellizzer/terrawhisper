Chinese date

Morinda officinalis

Chinese Date (Morinda officinalis) is a small shrub native to Asia, known for its reddish-orange berries that are used in traditional medicine to treat conditions such as impotence, infertility, and respiratory disorders.

Botanical Identification

Chinese Date, or Morinda officinalis, is a species in the Rubiaceae family. It is also known as Indian date or Alphonso in some regions. A perennial shrub native to Southeast Asia, it has thick roots, elliptical leaves, and yellow flowers. Its scientific name is sometimes cited as Morinda citrifolia in older herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Eudicots
  • Order: Rubiales
  • Family: Rubiaceae
  • Genus: Morinda
  • Species: Morinda officinalis

Active Compounds

The main active compounds of Chinese Date include flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to exert anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and immune-modulating effects. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in traditional herbal medicine.

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for treating conditions like anemia and fatigue due to its iron-rich content.

Infusion: Used for respiratory conditions and to boost energy levels.

Extract: Used for enhancing physical strength and treating chronic fatigue.

Safety Profile

Chinese Date, or Morinda officinalis, is generally considered safe when used in traditional medicinal amounts. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. Long-term use or high doses could lead to adverse effects, and it should be used with caution in those with specific health conditions. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
