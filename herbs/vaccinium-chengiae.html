What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vaccinium chengiae has a low-growing, prostrate habit characterized by woody, spreading stems that typically reach heights of only a few centimeters. The leaves are small, ovate to elliptical in shape, and possess finely serrated margins with a somewhat leathery texture. During the flowering stage, the plant produces small, bell-shaped blossoms that are often white or pale pink in color. The fruit develops into small, fleshy berries that transition from green to a deep blue or black hue when ripe. These botanical features distinguish the species from more upright members of the blueberry family.
