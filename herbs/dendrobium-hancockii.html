Dendrobium hancockii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dendrobium hancockii has elongated, pendulous pseudobulbs that are distinctly compressed and reach lengths of up to 60 centimeters. These fleshy stems are characterized by a leathery texture and a slightly ribbed surface structure. Each pseudobulb bears several narrow, lanceolate leaves that emerge from the upper portion of the stem. The flowers are small and typically present a delicate coloration that complements the slender profile of the plant. This epiphytic growth habit allows the species to drape elegantly from tree branches in its natural habitat.
