What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Iris milesii has sword-shaped, glaucous leaves that arise from a creeping rhizome and produce large, violet-blue flowers with distinctive yellow signals on the falls. The plant features a robust rhizomatous growth habit that allows it to spread through underground stems. Each flower consists of three upright standards and three drooping falls, often decorated with intricate veining and nectar guides. The floral structure is supported by a sturdy stem that typically bears multiple blooms per stalk. These morphological traits help the species attract specific pollinators within its native habitat.
