What is Niedenzuella glabra?

Niedenzuella glabra is a species of flowering plant in the family Malpighiaceae, native to Brazil.

It is an evergreen shrub or small tree, typically growing up to 10 meters in height, with smooth, grayish-brown bark and a rounded crown. The plant has alternate, simple leaves that are elliptical to lanceolate in shape, measuring 5-12 centimeters in length and 2-4 centimeters in width, with a glabrous (hairless) upper surface and a pubescent (hairy) lower surface. The inflorescence is a axillary panicle, bearing small, fragrant flowers with five white petals and a yellow center, which are followed by red or purple berries.

The plant's fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed.
