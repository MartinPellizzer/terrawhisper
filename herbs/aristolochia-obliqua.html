Aristolochia obliqua

What's the taxonomical classification of Aristolochia obliqua?

Aristolochia obliqua belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is situated in the order Piperales and the family Aristolochiaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Aristolochia, and its species designation, obliqua.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aristolochia obliqua has climbing perennial stems that produce heart-shaped, asymmetrical leaves with smooth margins and a distinctively oblique base. The foliage is characterized by a deep green color and a somewhat leathery texture that supports its vining growth habit. Its flowers are unique in structure, featuring a tubular perianth that transitions into a swollen chamber designed to trap insect pollinators. These blossoms typically appear in shades of brownish-purple or dull green, blending somewhat into the dense canopy of the plant. The overall morphology is adapted for a climbing lifestyle, allowing the plant to ascend through surrounding vegetation.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause severe nephrotoxicity, progressive renal failure, urothelium-damaging uropathy, and potent carcinogenicity leading to urothelial carcinoma. The presence of aristolochic acid within its tissues triggers direct damage to the kidney's functional structures. This chemical insult often results in a slow decline of organ function and permanent renal failure. Furthermore, the substance acts as a powerful mutagen that targets the urinary tract. Consequently, long-term exposure is strongly linked to the development of urothelial carcinoma in patients.

  • Nephrotoxicity
  • Uropathy
  • Renal failure
  • Carcinogenicity
  • Uropathy-induced bladder cancer
