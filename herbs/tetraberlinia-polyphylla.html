Tetraberlinia polyphylla

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tetraberlinia polyphylla has a woody habit characterized by numerous small, simple leaves and small, actinomorphic flowers. The foliage consists of many narrow, lanceolate leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. Its reproductive structures feature a small corolla with petals that are often inconspicuous in color. The plant typically grows as a small shrub or subshrub in its natural habitat. These morphological traits are key identifiers for distinguishing this specific species within its genus.
