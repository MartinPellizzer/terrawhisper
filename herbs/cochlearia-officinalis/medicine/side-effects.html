1. Causes allergic reactions

Cochlearia officinalis causes allergic reactions in some individuals due to its high content of beta-glucosidase, a complex compound that can trigger an immune response.

The presence of similar molecules in the body's natural defense mechanisms may lead to cross-reactivity, causing symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling. Additionally, sea kale contains small amounts of allergenic proteins, which can bind to IgE antibodies and elicit an allergic response.

These allergens are particularly troublesome for individuals with pre-existing allergies or sensitivities.

2. Triggers digestive issues

Cochlearia officinalis triggers digestive issues due to its high concentration of fiber and saponins.

The fiber can cause an increase in bowel movements, leading to diarrhea or abdominal cramps, while the saponins may irritate the stomach lining, resulting in nausea, bloating, and stomach discomfort.

As a result, individuals may experience gastrointestinal disturbances when consuming Cochlearia officinalis.

3. Induces headaches

Cochlearia officinalis induces headaches due to its high concentration of volatile oils and alkaloids.

The most likely culprit is the presence of sesquiterpenes, which can cause vasodilation and increase blood flow to the brain, leading to a headache.

Additionally, the plant's bitter compounds may also contribute to the development of migraines or tension-type headaches in some individuals.

4. Produces insomnia

Cochlearia officinalis produces insomnia due to its stimulating effect on the nervous system.

The plant's active compounds, such as alkaloids and glycosides, can increase alertness and energy levels, making it difficult for individuals to fall asleep or stay asleep throughout the night.

This side effect is often reported by people who consume Cochlearia officinalis in large quantities or combine it with other stimulants.

5. Increases blood pressure

Cochlearia officinalis increases blood pressure due to its high content of potassium, which can interact with blood pressure medications and stimulate the kidneys to excrete more sodium, leading to increased blood volume and subsequent hypertension.

Additionally, the plant's ability to dilate blood vessels may further contribute to this effect, potentially causing a rise in systolic blood pressure.

6. Reduces appetite

Cochlearia officinalis reduces appetite by suppressing hunger signals in the brain.

This is due to its ability to stimulate the production of serotonin, a neurotransmitter that helps regulate appetite and satiety.

As a result, individuals who consume Cochlearia officinalis may experience a decrease in their desire for food, leading to reduced caloric intake.

7. Affects menstrual cycles

Cochlearia officinalis affects menstrual cycles due to its potential to alter hormone levels in the body.

As a phytoestrogen-rich plant, it can mimic the effects of estrogen, disrupting the natural balance of hormones that regulate menstruation. This may lead to irregularities, such as changes in cycle length or heaviness of bleeding, among other symptoms.

The exact mechanism behind these effects is still being studied, but it is believed that the plant's active compounds interact with hormone receptors in the body, leading to these changes.

8. Worsens respiratory problems

Cochlearia officinalis worsens respiratory problems by releasing allergenic compounds that can stimulate an overactive immune response in susceptible individuals.

The plant's ability to cause an inflammatory reaction in the lungs and airways may exacerbate existing conditions such as asthma or chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), leading to increased wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.

9. Interferes with medication

Cochlearia officinalis interferes with medication by altering its absorption and metabolism due to its ability to increase digestive enzyme production.

This can lead to reduced efficacy or increased toxicity of certain medications, particularly those that require a stable gastric pH to be effective.

The compound saponin, present in Cochlearia officinalis, is thought to contribute to this interaction, potentially affecting the body's ability to process and eliminate drugs from the system.

10. Enhances sensitivity

Cochlearia officinalis enhances sensitivity by stimulating the auditory nerve and increasing its activity.

This effect is attributed to the presence of bioactive compounds such as flavonoids and glycosides in the plant, which can bind to neurotransmitter receptors and modulate neural transmission.

As a result, individuals using Cochlearia officinalis may experience heightened awareness of sounds and improved hearing acuity.
