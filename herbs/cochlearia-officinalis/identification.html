Botanical Identification of Cochlearia (Cochlearia officinalis)

To identify Cochlearia (Cochlearia officinalis) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Cochlearia officinalis belongs to the plant kingdom, specifically the division of seed plants. It is classified under the class Caryophyllopsida, order Brassicales, family Brassicaceae, genus Cochlearia, and species Cochlearia officinalis. This classification highlights its botanical relationships and characteristics within the herbal medicine context.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of the plant is Cochlearia officinalis, first described by Carl von Linné in 1753. It is also known by several synonyms, including Cochlearia scapifera and Cochlearia hirsuta. Common names include lesser celandine and yellow celandine, while regional names vary across Europe. Historically, it was referred to as "liver wort" due to its traditional use in treating liver ailments.

Morphological Characteristics

Cochlearia officinalis is a biennial or short-lived perennial herb. It has a taproot system, a branching stem that grows up to 30 cm tall, and basal leaves that are spoon-shaped with a hairy surface. The inflorescence is a raceme of white or pale purple flowers, each with four petals and yellow stamens. The fruit is a cylindrical silique containing several small, dark brown seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Cochlearia officinalis can be distinguished by its basal rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves and its clustered, violet-blue flowers. It differs from similar species like Cochlearia bulbosa by having larger, more rounded leaves and a more robust stem. The presence of a central flowering stem with numerous small flowers is another key identifier.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Cochlearia officinalis is native to Europe and western Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with cool summers and mild winters. It prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils. It is commonly found at elevations between 200 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Cochlearia officinalis blooms from April to June, producing small white or pale purple flowers. Seed formation occurs from July to September. For identification purposes, the plant is typically harvested in early spring before flowering for its leaves and roots.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Cochlearia officinalis is closely related to species within the same genus, such as Cochlearia bulbosa. It shares characteristics with other Brassicaceae family members, including cruciform flowers and siliquae fruits. Its phylogenetic placement reflects its evolutionary ties to other Capparaceae family plants.
