Ipomoea carrii

What's the taxonomical classification of Ipomoea carrii?

Ipomoea carrii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Solanales. This species is a part of the family Convolvulaceae, specifically situated within the genus Ipomoea, and is identified by its unique specific epithet, carrii.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Ipomoea carrii contains various secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, and phenolic compounds that possess potential biological activity. These chemical constituents are often studied for their ability to exhibit antioxidant and antimicrobial properties. Researchers analyze these specific molecules to determine how they might interact with cellular pathways in medicinal applications. The concentration of these compounds can vary significantly depending on the plant's environmental conditions and growth stage. Such phytochemical diversity makes the species a subject of interest in ethnobotanical and pharmacological research.

What are the therapeutic actions of Ipomoea carrii?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including skin infections, stomach pains, and respiratory issues. Local traditional healers often utilize its specific parts to address inflammation and minor wounds. In certain medicinal practices, the plant acts as a remedy for digestive distress and intestinal discomfort. It is also applied topically to soothe skin irritations and promote healing. These therapeutic applications are deeply rooted in regional folk medicine traditions.
