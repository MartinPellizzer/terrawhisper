Ipomoea carrii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ipomoea carrii has prostrate to ascending perennial herbs characterized by twining stems, cordate to hastate leaves with long petioles, and funnelform flowers that are typically pale lavender or white with a distinct yellow throat. The leaves feature prominent venation and can vary in size depending on the maturity of the plant. Its inflorescences consist of solitary flowers emerging from the leaf axils. The reproductive structures include a superior ovary that develops into a small, rounded capsule containing several seeds. These morphological traits are essential for distinguishing this specific species within the Convolvulaceae family.
