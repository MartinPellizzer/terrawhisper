Eugenia uniflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Eugenia uniflora?

Eugenia uniflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Myrtales. The plant is a specific representative of the family Myrtaceae, falling under the genus Eugenia, and is formally identified by its unique species designation, uniflora.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eugenia uniflora has an evergreen shrub or small tree structure characterized by oppositely arranged, elliptic to oblong leaves that possess an entire margin and an acute apex. The foliage typically exhibits a glossy, dark green upper surface and a slightly paler underside. Its small, white flowers are solitary and emerge from the leaf axils, featuring numerous delicate stamens. The plant produces small, globose drupes that transition from green to a deep red or purple color when mature. These fruits are often fleshy and serve as a primary morphological feature for identification.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for managing diabetes mellitus, glucose intolerance, hyperglycemia, increased serum glucose, and metabolic syndrome. Its medicinal properties help regulate blood sugar levels by reducing the amount of sugar circulating in the bloodstream. By addressing glucose intolerance, the plant assists in stabilizing the body's response to insulin. These actions are critical for preventing the complications associated with chronically increased serum glucose. Furthermore, its bioactive compounds play a role in mitigating the various components of metabolic syndrome.

As discussed in a study, extracts obtained from the leaves of Eugenia uniflora were analyzed to determine their chemical composition and alpha-glucosidase inhibitory capacity. The study identified chlorogenic acids, flavonoids, tannins, proanthocyanidins, saponins, and triterpene in these extracts. The antioxidant activities were found to be directly related to the total phenolic and flavonoid content. The Eugenia uniflora aqueous extract showed significant inhibition of alpha-glucosidase with an IC50 of 0.98 µg mL-1. This result indicates its potential as a non-competitive inhibitor for managing Diabetes Mellitus.
