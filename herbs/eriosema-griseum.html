Eriosema griseum

What's the taxonomical classification of Eriosema griseum?

Eriosema griseum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is situated within the order Fabales. It falls under the family Fabaceae, a group well-known for its diverse range of legumes. Specifically, its taxonomic hierarchy concludes with its placement in the genus Eriosema, identifying it as the species Eriosema griseum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eriosema griseum has a woody, scrambling habit characterized by densely pubescent stems and trifoliate leaves with small, ovate to elliptical leaflets.

The foliage is covered in fine, greyish hairs that give the plant a characteristic velvety texture. Its flowers are pea-like and typically display a pale yellow or creamy coloration. The inflorescences emerge from the leaf axils on short peduncles.

Long, slender pods develop from the fertilized flowers to facilitate seed dispersal.
