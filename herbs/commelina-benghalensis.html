Commelina benghalensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Commelina benghalensis?

Commelina benghalensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows a specialized evolutionary lineage. The plant is placed under the order Commelinales and is the sole representative of its family, Commelinaceae. Within this distinct group, it is categorized under the genus Commelina, specifically identified by the species epithet benghalensis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Commelina benghalensis contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, and tannins that contribute to its therapeutic potential. These chemical constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's demonstrated antioxidant and antimicrobial activities. Research suggests that the presence of these compounds may offer protective effects against oxidative stress in biological systems. Specifically, the phenolic compounds within the plant act to neutralize free radicals. Further investigations into these specific molecules are essential to fully understand their pharmacological applications in medicine.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, stomach aches, and inflammatory conditions. Its medicinal properties are frequently applied to manage various cutaneous ailments such as rashes or minor cuts. Additionally, practitioners utilize its extracts to soothe gastrointestinal distress and alleviate abdominal pain. The plant also serves as a remedy for reducing swelling and other systemic inflammatory issues. These traditional applications highlight its role in addressing both external and internal health concerns.
