Commelina benghalensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Commelina benghalensis?

Commelina benghalensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows a specialized evolutionary lineage. The plant is placed under the order Commelinales and is the sole representative of its family, Commelinaceae. Within this distinct group, it is categorized under the genus Commelina, specifically identified by the species epithet benghalensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Commelina benghalensis has opposite, sessile, lanceolate to ovate leaves that are typically dark green with entire margins and prominent pinnate venation. The stems are woody and branched, supporting a dense arrangement of foliage. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in axillary clusters, often featuring a tubular structure characteristic of the Rubiaceae family. The plant develops small, fleshy fruits that serve as a key identifying feature for the species. These morphological traits collectively distinguish it from closely related taxa within its genus.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the Bengalen hills in the northeastern region of India. It is primarily found inhabiting the tropical moist deciduous forests that characterize this specific hilly terrain. The species thrives within localized microclimates that offer the necessary humidity and shade required for its survival. Due to its highly restricted range, it is considered a rare component of the regional flora. Conservation efforts focus on these specific geographic pockets to ensure the stability of its natural habitat.

What are the pharmacological activities of Commelina benghalensis?

This plant has antibacterial, antioxidant, hepatoprotective, 15-lipoxygenase inhibition, and anti-anxiety pharmacological activities. Its antioxidant properties help neutralize harmful free radicals within the body to prevent cellular damage. The plant also demonstrates hepatoprotective effects that support liver health and function. Furthermore, it exhibits the ability to inhibit 15-lipoxygenase, which plays a significant role in managing inflammatory responses. Finally, its anti-anxiety characteristics suggest potential therapeutic benefits for regulating emotional distress and neurological stability.

According to a study published by "F1000Research", Commelina benghalensis was investigated for its in vitro antioxidant status, medicinal properties, and cytotoxic effects. The aqueous extracts of the leaves exhibit higher content of polyphenols, flavonoids, and tannins. These aqueous extracts also show higher inhibition percentage values for free radical scavenging assays. In contrast, the 70% ethanolic extracts exhibit a higher content of alkaloids and cardiac glycosides. HPLC-DAD analysis of the 70% ethanolic extracts led to the identification of 10 predominant phenolic constituents.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse array of bioactive medicinal compounds including flavonoids, phenolic compounds, saponins, steroids, and stigmasterol (CB-2). These specific phytochemicals contribute to the various therapeutic properties observed in the species. The presence of flavonoids and phenolic compounds is often linked to significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities. Furthermore, the saponins and steroids play critical roles in the plant's biological interactions and medicinal potential. Stigmasterol (CB-2) serves as a key sterol component within this chemical profile.

According to a study published by "Asian Pacific journal of tropical biomedicine", the methanol leaf extracts of Commelina benghalensis were tested for the presence of terpenoids, saponins, tannins, flavonoids, steroids, phenolic compounds, alkaloids and cardiac glycosides. The study utilized a spectrophotometric assay to observe the increase in absorbance at 234 nm during the evaluation of 15-lipoxygenase inhibitory activity. The extracts of Commelina benghalensis inhibited the action of 15-lipoxygenase at a concentration of 0.2 µg/mL.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating Helicobacter pylori infection, alcoholic liver disease, conjunctivitis, gonorrhea, and jaundice. Its bioactive compounds possess antimicrobial properties that help combat the bacteria responsible for gastrointestinal and reproductive infections. The extract also shows potential in managing liver dysfunction and clearing jaundice by supporting hepatic function. Additionally, topical applications of the plant are utilized to soothe the inflammation associated with conjunctivitis. These diverse medicinal applications highlight the plant's significant therapeutic value in traditional medicine.

According to a study published by "World journal of gastroenterology", the plant Commelina benghalensis was not specifically identified by that name in the text provided. The study evaluated 25 plants to investigate bacteriocidal and anti-adhesive properties against Helicobacter pylori. Of the plants tested, 8 plants were found to have no bacteriocidal effect on any of the isolates. Nine other plants had bacteriocidal activities, but total inhibition of growth was not achieved. The research assessed the inhibition of binding of 4 strains of FITC-labeled H. pylori to stomach sections.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause severe allergic skin reactions, gastrointestinal distress, and potential neurotoxicity if ingested in large quantities. Contact with the sap may result in localized contact dermatitis, characterized by redness, itching, or blistering on the skin. If consumed, individuals might experience nausea, vomiting, and abdominal cramping. Some reports suggest that certain compounds within the plant could affect the central nervous system if systemic absorption occurs. Careful avoidance of direct contact and ingestion is necessary to prevent these adverse health outcomes.
