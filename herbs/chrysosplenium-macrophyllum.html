What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Chrysosplenium macrophyllum has large, elliptical to lanceolate leaves that are typically characterized by prominent venation and a distinctively thickened texture. These broad leaves are arranged alternately along the stem to maximize light absorption within its native habitat. The plant's architecture features sturdy, upright stems that provide structural support for the heavy foliage. Small, inconspicuous flowers often emerge from the axils of the leaves in a structured pattern. This morphological combination of expansive leaf surface area and robust stem development defines its physical presence.
