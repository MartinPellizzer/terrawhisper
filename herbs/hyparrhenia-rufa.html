Hyparrhenia rufa

What's the taxonomical classification of Hyparrhenia rufa?

Hyparrheniia rufa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this botanical framework, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Poales and is a member of the family Poaceae. Specifically, its taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Hyparrheniia, with its unique classification completed at the species level as rufa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hyparrheniia rufa has perennial tufted growth habits characterized by dense clumps of stiff, upright, reddish-brown leaves that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

The leaf blades are linear, often involute, and possess a distinctly coarse, scabrid texture along their margins. Its inflorescence consists of large, branched panicles that are typically reddish or purplish in color during the flowering stage. The spikelets are arranged in pairs, with one sessile and one pedicelled, featuring long awns that facilitate seed dispersal.

These structural adaptations allow the plant to thrive in the arid and sandy environments of coastal dunes.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Hyparrhenia rufa can cause severe skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and localized swelling upon direct physical contact with its foliage. The plant contains specific chemical compounds that act as irritants to human tissue. Individuals with sensitive skin may experience redness, itching, or even blistering in the affected areas. These dermatological reactions typically occur shortly after the skin makes contact with the plant's surface. It is advisable to avoid handling the vegetation to prevent these painful inflammatory responses.
