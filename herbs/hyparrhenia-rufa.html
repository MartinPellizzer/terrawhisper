Hyparrhenia rufa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hyparrheniia rufa has perennial tufted growth habits characterized by dense clumps of stiff, upright, reddish-brown leaves that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The leaf blades are linear, often involute, and possess a distinctly coarse, scabrid texture along their margins. Its inflorescence consists of large, branched panicles that are typically reddish or purplish in color during the flowering stage. The spikelets are arranged in pairs, with one sessile and one pedicelled, featuring long awns that facilitate seed dispersal. These structural adaptations allow the plant to thrive in the arid and sandy environments of coastal dunes.
