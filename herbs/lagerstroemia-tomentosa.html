Lagerstroemia tomentosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lagerstroemia tomentosa has elliptical to oblong leaves that are covered in dense, velvety brownish hairs on both the upper and lower surfaces. This pubescence is a defining characteristic that distinguishes it from many other species in the genus. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub with a woody stem and a spreading canopy. Its flowers are usually small and clustered in terminal panicles, often displaying pale pink or white colors. The bark is relatively smooth and may peel slightly as the plant matures. Overall, the fuzzy texture of the foliage is the most prominent morphological feature of this species.
