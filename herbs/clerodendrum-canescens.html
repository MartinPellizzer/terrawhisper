Clerodendrum canescens

What's the taxonomical classification of Clerodendrum canescens?

Clerodendrum canescens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Lamiales and is a member of the family Lamiaceae. More specifically, it is classified under the genus Clerodendrum, with its specific designation being the species canescens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clerodendrum canescens has opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated margins and a woody, climbing stem structure. The foliage is typically bright green and possesses a slightly textured surface due to its venation. Small, fragrant white flowers emerge from terminal clusters, often characterized by long, protruding stamens. These blossoms eventually develop into small, fleshy berries that change color as they ripen. The overall growth habit is that of a perennial shrub or scandent climber that utilizes its stems to ascend surrounding vegetation.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds coleon U, coleon U-12-methyl ether, teuviincenone F, and uncinatone. These specific phytochemicals are classified as clerodane diterpenoids within the species. Researchers study these molecules to understand their potential biological activities and therapeutic properties. The presence of these distinct chemical constituents contributes to the overall pharmacological profile of the plant. Each compound serves as a marker for the plant's complex chemical composition.

According to a study published by "Natural product research", the medicinal plant Clerodendrum canescens serves as a source for the endophytic fungus Pestalotiopsis adusta. Bioassay-guided fractionation of the culture extract led to the isolation of one new diterpenoid, (10S)-12,16-epoxy-17(15→16)-abeo-3,5,8,12,15-abietapentaeen-2,7,11,14-tetraone (1). The study also identified four known diterpenoids, specifically teuvincenone F (2), uncinatone (3), coleon U (4), and coleon U-12-methyl ether (5). Among these, compounds 1 and 3 demonstrated cytotoxic activities against the HL-60 tumour cell line. These specific compounds showed an IC50 value of less than 20 μM.
