Stellaria radians

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stellaeria radians has a sprawling, decumbent growth habit characterized by slender, cylindrical stems that branch frequently from the base. The leaves are small, lanceolate to linear in shape, and arranged in an alternate or sub-opposite pattern along the stem. Tiny, actinomorphic flowers emerge from the leaf axils, featuring white petals that spread radially like a star. The root system is typically fibrous, allowing the plant to anchor itself firmly in loose or disturbed soils. These morphological traits collectively enable the plant to spread quickly across the ground surface.
