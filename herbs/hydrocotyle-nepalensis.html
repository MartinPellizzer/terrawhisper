Hydrocotyle nepalensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hydrocotyle nepalensis has creeping, procumbent stems that produce kidney-shaped to orbicular leaves with crenate margins and long, slender petioles. The leaf blades are typically bright green and can vary in size depending on the specific microhabitat. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in umbel-like clusters on upright stalks. These plants often form dense mats due to their stoloniferous growth habit. The root system develops at the nodes where the stems make contact with the substrate.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the Himalayan region, specifically occurring across parts of India, Nepal, Bhutan, and China. Its distribution is closely tied to high-altitude ecosystems where humidity remains consistent throughout the growing season. The species tends to cluster in areas with rich, organic soil that can retain significant moisture.     What are the herbal preparations of Hydrocotyle nepalensis?

    Hydrocotyle nepalensis is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste for medicinal applications. Practitioners often boil the plant parts in water to create a liquid extract used for internal consumption. When applied externally, the crushed leaves are formed into a paste to soothe skin irritations or localized swelling. These various forms allow the bioactive compounds of the herb to be utilized effectively for different ailments.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Hydrocotyle nepalensis can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, many plants in the Hydrocotyle genus contain compounds that may trigger hypersensitivity in sensitive individuals. Direct contact with the leaves or stems can lead to redness, itching, or localized swelling on the skin. If consumed accidentally, the plant may cause nausea, vomiting, or abdominal discomfort. It is generally advised to avoid skin contact and ingestion to prevent these unpredictable physiological reactions.
