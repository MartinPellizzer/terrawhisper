Aster boweri

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aster boweri has a herbaceous growth habit characterized by slender, erect stems and oppositely arranged, lanceolate to ovate leaves with serrated margins. The foliage typically displays a soft texture and a deep green coloration that provides a dense base for its flowering structures. Small, composite flower heads emerge from the terminal ends of the stems, consisting of central disc florets surrounded by delicate ray florets. These inflorescences often feature a subtle color palette that complements the fine texture of the plant's greenery. The overall structure is compact yet airy, making it a distinct representative of its genus in tropical or subtropical environments.
