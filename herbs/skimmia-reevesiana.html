What's the taxonomical classification of Skimmia reevesiana?

Skimmia reevesiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Sapindales. This plant is a member of the family Rutaceae, falling under the genus Skimmia, and is specifically identified by its species name, reevesiana.

    What are the herbal preparations of Skimmia reevesiana?

    Skimmia reevesiana is prepared primarily as a concentrated liquid extract, a decoction of the bark, or a topical infusion of the leaves. These herbal forms are typically derived through a process of slow boiling to extract the specific alkaloids present in the plant tissues. The decoction method is favored when targeting the woody stem components, while infusions are used for more delicate leaf preparations. These resulting liquids are often utilized in traditional contexts for their perceived medicinal properties. Once prepared, the extracts must be stored in dark glass containers to prevent degradation from light exposure.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Skimmia reevesiana can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, including nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain, if any part of the plant is ingested due to its toxic alkaloids. The plant contains various compounds that are harmful to humans and animals when consumed. Contact with the plant may also lead to skin irritation or allergic reactions in sensitive individuals. Pets are particularly susceptible to poisoning if they chew on the leaves or berries. Proper precautions should be taken to keep this plant out of reach of children and domestic animals.
