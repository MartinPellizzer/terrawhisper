Cotoneaster microphyllus

What's the taxonomical classification of Cotoneaster microphyllus?

Cotoneaster microphyllus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Rosales, the plant is a member of the family Rosaceae. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Cotoneaster, specifically identified by its species name, microphyllus.

What are the herbal preparations of Cotoneaster microphyllus?

Cotoneaster microphyllus is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice depending on the specific medicinal requirement. The dried berries are most commonly simmered in water to create a concentrated decoction used for internal consumption. For milder therapeutic effects, the leaves can be steeped in hot water to produce a soothing herbal infusion. Additionally, the crushed plant material is sometimes applied directly to the skin as a poultice to treat localized ailments. These various methods allow for the extraction of different bioactive compounds present within the plant.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Cotoneaster microphyllus can cause gastrointestinal distress, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, if the berries are ingested by humans or animals. The plant contains cyanogenic glycosides which can release toxic hydrogen cyanide into the system upon consumption. This chemical reaction may lead to more severe symptoms such as dizziness, headaches, or rapid heart rate. While skin contact is generally not considered highly toxic, some individuals might experience localized irritation or allergic reactions. Careful supervision is necessary to prevent accidental poisoning in residential landscapes.
