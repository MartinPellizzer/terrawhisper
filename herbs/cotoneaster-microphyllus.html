Cotoneaster microphyllus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Cotoneaster, specifically identified by its species name, microphyllus.

      What are the herbal preparations of Cotoneaster microphyllus?

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Cotoneaster microphyllus can cause gastrointestinal distress, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, if the berries are ingested by humans or animals. The plant contains cyanogenic glycosides which can release toxic hydrogen cyanide into the system upon consumption. This chemical reaction may lead to more severe symptoms such as dizziness, headaches, or rapid heart rate. While skin contact is generally not considered highly toxic, some individuals might experience localized irritation or allergic reactions. Careful supervision is necessary to prevent accidental poisoning in residential landscapes.
