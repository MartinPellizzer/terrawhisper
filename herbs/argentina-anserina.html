Argentina anserina

What's the taxonomical classification of Argentina anserina?

Argentina anserina belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized as Equisetopsida, which further descends into the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic lineage through the order Rosales, the plant is a member of the family Rosaceae. Finally, it is identified under the genus Argentina, with its specific designation being the species anserina.

What are the therapeutic actions of Argentina anserina?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various digestive ailments, skin inflammations, and respiratory issues. It is often applied topically to soothe irritation and promote healing in wounded skin. In traditional medicine, decoctions of the plant are sometimes consumed to alleviate stomach discomfort or intestinal irregularities. Some practitioners also utilize its properties to help reduce symptoms associated with coughs and mild bronchial congestion. These therapeutic applications rely on the plant's specific phytochemical composition to provide relief.
