10 Possible Side Effects Of Artemisia Tridentata

Artemisia tridentata has some side effects when used improperly, such as skin irritation, allergic reactions, and stomach upset.

These side effects are caused by the herb's high concentration of essential oils, particularly thujone.

If left untreated, skin irritation can lead to infections and scarring, while stomach upset can worsen existing digestive issues, making everyday activities and tasks more challenging for individuals.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Artemisia tridentata causes allergic reactions due to the presence of sesquiterpene lactones and terpenoids in its essential oil.

These compounds can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, swelling, and difficulty breathing in susceptible individuals.

Additionally, some people may experience digestive issues or skin rashes after exposure to Artemisia tridentata, likely due to the irritating properties of these compounds on the mucous membranes and skin.

2. Triggers asthma attacks

Artemisia tridentata triggers asthma attacks due to its high content of sesquiterpenes, specifically alpha-thujone and beta-caryophyllene.

These compounds can cause bronchial constriction, inflammation, and increased mucus production in the lungs, leading to wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.

Additionally, sagebrush's volatile oils may also irritate the airways, further exacerbating asthma symptoms.

3. Induces hallucinations

Artemisia tridentata induces hallucinations due to its psychoactive compounds, including sesquiterpenes and flavonoids.

These chemicals interact with the brain's neurotransmitters, such as serotonin and dopamine, altering perception and consciousness. As a result, users may experience vivid visual and auditory hallucinations, distorted sense of time, and altered emotional states.

The plant's active compounds can also stimulate the release of endorphins, leading to feelings of euphoria or anxiety.

4. Affects blood pressure

Artemisia tridentata affects blood pressure due to its ability to stimulate the cardiovascular system.

The plant contains sesquiterpenoids and flavonoids that can increase heart rate and cardiac output, leading to a rise in blood pressure. Additionally, Sagebrush's antioxidant properties may contribute to vasoconstriction, further enhancing its blood-pressure-elevating effects.

This reaction is more likely to occur in individuals with pre-existing hypertension or other cardiovascular conditions.

5. Interacts with medications

Artemisia tridentata interacts with medications that are metabolized by the cytochrome P450 enzyme system.

This can lead to decreased effectiveness or increased side effects of certain medications, such as blood thinners, diabetes medications, and heart medications. The active compounds in Artemisia tridentata, including sesquiterpenoids and flavonoids, may inhibit the activity of these enzymes, altering the body's ability to process these medications.

This can result in unintended consequences for individuals taking multiple medications simultaneously.

6. Produces digestive issues

Artemisia tridentata produces digestive issues due to its bitter and astringent properties, which can irritate the mucous membranes in the gastrointestinal tract.

The volatile oils present in this plant, such as camphor and thujone, may contribute to this adverse effect by altering the natural balance of gut bacteria and reducing stomach acid secretion.

Additionally, the high concentration of tannins in Artemisia tridentata can cause constipation and stomach cramps, leading to uncomfortable digestive issues.

7. Worsens kidney function

Artemisia tridentata worsens kidney function due to its high concentration of volatile oils and flavonoids.

These compounds may increase urine production, leading to a loss of essential electrolytes and fluids, which can strain the kidneys.

Additionally, Artemisia tridentata's ability to lower blood pressure can further exacerbate kidney dysfunction by reducing perfusion and oxygenation of the kidneys.

8. Increases heart rate

Artemisia tridentata increases heart rate due to its ability to stimulate the cardiovascular system.

The plant's chemical compounds, such as sesquiterpenes and flavonoids, can cause a rapid heartbeat by increasing the production of adrenaline, which in turn boosts cardiac output.

This stimulation can lead to an increase in blood pressure and heart rate, potentially resulting in palpitations or arrhythmias for some individuals who consume Artemisia tridentata.

9. Reduces cognitive function

Artemisia tridentata reduces cognitive function by potentially interfering with the activity of neurotransmitters such as dopamine and acetylcholine in the brain.

This may lead to difficulties in concentration, memory recall, and decision-making abilities.

The exact mechanisms behind this effect are not fully understood, but it is thought to be related to the herb's ability to interact with certain enzymes and receptors in the brain, disrupting normal cognitive processes.

10. Leads to respiratory distress

Artemisia tridentata leads to respiratory distress due to its volatile oils and terpenoids, which can cause bronchospasms and inflammation in the airways.

The plant's potent compounds, such as camphor and borneol, can irritate the mucous membranes and trigger coughing, wheezing, and shortness of breath.

Additionally, Sagebrush's aromatic properties can exacerbate existing respiratory conditions, such as asthma, making it a potential allergen for individuals prone to respiratory issues.
