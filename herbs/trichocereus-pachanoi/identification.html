Botanical Identification of San Pedro (Trichocereus pachanoi)

To identify San Pedro (Trichocereus pachanoi) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

San Pedro, or Trichocereus pachanoi, belongs to the plant kingdom Plantae. It is classified under the division Spermatophyta, class Caryophyllopsida, order Caryophyllales, family Cactaceae, genus Trichocereus, and species Trichocereus pachanoi. These classifications help identify its botanical relationships and characteristics.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of San Pedro is Trichocereus pachanoi, first described by Schultes in 1857. It has several synonyms, including Echinopsis pachanoi and Lophophora williamsii, though the latter is now considered a separate species. Common names include San Pedro, Bolivian angel's trumpet, and Toadshade. It is also known by regional and historical names such as Huachuma and Cacawapichan.

Morphological Characteristics

San Pedro, or Trichocereus pachanoi, is a cacti species native to the Andes. It has a columnar growth habit with a thick, branching stem. Its root system is shallow and fibrous, adapting to arid conditions. The stem is spiny with a greenish-gray color, and it produces large, flat, paddle-shaped leaves. The inflorescence is a cluster of flowers, typically white or pale yellow, that bloom at the stem's apex. The fruit is a yellow, fleshy berry containing numerous seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

San Pedro, or Trichocereus pachanoi, can be distinguished by its tall, columnar stems with a bluish-green hue, white spines, and yellow flowers. It differs from similar species like Trichocereus tersiusculus through its more robust stem and distinct spines. The presence of a central stem and lack of branching also help in identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

San Pedro, or Trichocereus pachanoi, is native to the Andean regions of Peru and Bolivia. It thrives in warm, arid climates with high sun exposure. It prefers well-drained, sandy soils and is typically found at elevations between 2,000 and 4,000 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

San Pedro typically blooms during the dry season, from late spring to early summer. Seed formation occurs after the flowers are pollinated, usually in late summer. For identification purposes, the plant is harvested during its vegetative growth phase, when the stem is thick and the spines are fully developed.

Phylogenetic Relationships

San Pedro, or Trichocereus pachanoi, belongs to the Cactaceae family and is closely related to other Trichocereus species. It shares morphological and genetic traits with its closest relatives, such as Trichocereus bridgesii and Trichocereus peruvianus. These species are part of the same genus and exhibit similar growth patterns and alkaloid profiles.
