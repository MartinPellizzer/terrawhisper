Habenaria ciliolaris

What's the taxonomical classification of Habenaria ciliolaris?

Habeanaria ciliolaris belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asparagales. This plant is a representative of the family Orchidaceae, specifically situated within the genus Habenaria, and is identified by its unique species designation, ciliolaris.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Habenaria ciliolariis has slender, upright stems that typically reach heights of ten to thirty centimeters and bear several pairs of lanceolate leaves arranged in an alternate pattern.

The inflorescence consists of a terminal spike featuring numerous small, white to pale lilac flowers characterized by prominent, fringed or ciliate labella. Each individual bloom possesses a long, nectar-producing spur extending from the rear of the perianth. The vegetative structure is complemented by underground tubers that allow the plant to survive dormant periods.

These morphological adaptations specifically facilitate pollination by long-tongued insects in its natural habitat.
