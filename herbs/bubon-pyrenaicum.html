What is Bubon pyrenaicum?

Bubon pyrenaicum is a perennial herbaceous plant that belongs to the family Apiaceae.

It has a taproot and a caulescent stem, typically growing up to 50 cm in height. Leaves are bipinnate, composed of numerous small leaflets, and are borne on a leafy sheath. The inflorescence is a compound umbel, comprising numerous small flowers with white or yellowish petals.

The plant is native to the Iberian Peninsula, specifically in the regions of the Pyrenees and surrounding areas.

What are the medicinal constituents this herb?

The most important healing constituents of Bubon pyrenaicum are flavonoids, alkaloids, glycosides and phenolic acids.

The flavonoids, particularly quercetin and kaempferol, have been identified as responsible for the antioxidant, anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities of Bubon pyrenaicum. Alkaloids such as berberine and palmatine have been found to have antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory and antidiarrheal properties. What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

The main herbal preparations of Bubon pyrenaicum are bubonic tincture, bubon infusion, bubon tea, salve, and pyrenee decoction.

Bubonic tincture is made by soaking the dried plant in a solvent such as ethanol or glycerin to create a concentrated liquid extract. Bubon infusion is prepared by steeping the dried plant in hot water to create a liquid solution rich in flavonoids and phenolic acids. Bubon tea is similar to infusion but is made by steeping the plant in boiling water and then straining the liquid.

Pyrenee decoction is a stronger preparation made by boiling the plant in water for an extended period to release its bioactive compounds, including alkaloids and terpenes.
