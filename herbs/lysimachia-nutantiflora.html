Lysimachia nutantiflora

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lysimachia nutantiflora has upright, herbaceous stems that typically reach heights of 20 to 50 centimeters and bear lanceolate to elliptical leaves arranged in an opposite pattern. The foliage consists of smooth, green leaves with entire margins that decrease in size as they ascend the stem. Its most distinguishing feature is the nodding, bell-shaped yellow flowers that hang from the leaf axils on slender pedicels. Each flower contains five fused petals that form a funnel shape surrounding a central cluster of stamens. The plant's overall structure is slender and elegant, reflecting its specialized adaptation for attracting specific pollinators through its pendulous blooms.
