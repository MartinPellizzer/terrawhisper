Tissa marginata

Tissa marginata, also known as the Yellow Bristle Club Moss, is a type of evergreen epiphyte plant that belongs to the family Selaginellaceae.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include its ability to lower blood pressure, reduce inflammation, and exhibit antimicrobial activity.

In terms of health benefits, Tissa marginata has been traditionally used to treat respiratory issues such as bronchitis and asthma, as well as to aid in the treatment of digestive problems.

Herbal preparations of the plant often include infusions, decoctions, and extracts, which can be used to make teas, tinctures, and salves to harness its medicinal properties.
