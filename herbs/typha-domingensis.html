Typha domingensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Typha domingensis?

Typha domingensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its hierarchical lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Poales. More specifically, this plant is a member of the family Typhaceae and falls under the genus Typha, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species domingensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Typha domingensis has cylindrical, brownish-to-tan flowering spikes that typically measure between 10 and 30 centimeters in length and are supported by upright, linear leaves. The plant features long, sword-shaped leaves that are often slightly channeled or folded along the midrib to facilitate water runoff. These leaves emerge from a creeping rhizomatous root system that allows the species to spread effectively through wetland substrates. The terminal spike consists of a dense cluster of small, densely packed flowers that eventually develop into a characteristic fuzzy texture. During the maturation process, the spike may exhibit a distinct separation between the female and male floral zones.
