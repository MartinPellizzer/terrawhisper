Orthosiphon aristatus

What's the taxonomical classification of Orthosiphon aristatus?

Orthosiphon aristatus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is situated in the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by its placement within the genus Orthosiphon, with its unique designation being the species aristatus.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Orthosiphon aristatus contains a diverse profile of bioactive phytochemicals including flavonoids, tannins, saponins, alkaloids, and phenolic acids. These specific compounds contribute to the plant's recognized antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Research indicates that the presence of phenolic compounds helps mitigate oxidative stress within biological systems. Furthermore, the alkaloids and tannins found in the leaves may provide antimicrobial effects against various pathogens. These chemical constituents collectively support the traditional use of the plant in treating various ailments.

What are the therapeutic actions of Orthosiphon aristatus?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat kidney stones, urinary tract infections, bladder inflammation, and various renal ailments. Its diuretic properties help increase urine flow to flush out toxins and prevent the formation of crystals in the kidneys. It is also frequently employed to alleviate symptoms of gastritis and digestive discomfort through its anti-inflammatory effects. Some traditional practitioners utilize its extracts to manage symptoms associated with gout and rheumatism. The plant's bioactive compounds work to reduce swelling and infection within the urinary system.
