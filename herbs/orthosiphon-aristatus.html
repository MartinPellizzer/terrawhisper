Orthosiphon aristatus

What's the taxonomical classification of Orthosiphon aristatus?

Orthosiphon aristatus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is situated in the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by its placement within the genus Orthosiphon, with its unique designation being the species aristatus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Orthosiphon aristatus has an erect, herbaceous habit characterized by slender stems and lanceolate to linear leaves that are arranged oppositely along the length of the plant. The foliage typically exhibits a smooth texture and acute apices, providing a streamlined appearance to the vegetative structure. Its inflorescence consists of small, delicate flowers that emerge from the axils of the upper leaves. The most distinguishing morphological feature is the presence of long, hair-like awns or bristles that extend from the floral parts. These fine structures give the plant its specific epithet and aid in its identification within its natural habitat.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical regions of West and Central Africa, specifically spanning from Guinea and Sierra Leone through Nigeria to the Democratic Republic of the Congo. It primarily thrives in the humid environments of lowland rainforests and coastal vegetation zones. The species is most frequently encountered in areas with high rainfall and dense canopy cover. Its distribution is closely linked to the moist equatorial climate characteristic of the Guineo-Congolian region. Localized populations may also be found along riverbanks where soil moisture remains consistently high.

Indigenous populations frequently prepared decoctions from the leaves and stems to alleviate digestive issues and promote overall renal health. It was also historically employed as a remedy for skin conditions and to reduce pain associated with joint stiffness. What are the pharmacological activities of Orthosiphon aristatus?

This plant has anti-hyperuricemic, anti-inflammatory, diuretic, prevention of uric acid stone formation, and antioxidant activities. Its ability to lower uric acid levels makes it a valuable resource for managing gout and related metabolic conditions. By acting as a diuretic, it helps the body eliminate excess fluids and waste products through increased urine production. The plant's anti-inflammatory properties help reduce swelling and pain associated with various systemic ailments. Additionally, its antioxidant activity protects cells from oxidative stress and damage caused by free radicals. These combined mechanisms contribute significantly to its overall therapeutic potential in treating kidney and inflammatory disorders.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", the hydrolyzed extract of Orthosiphon aristatus (Blume) Miq. was analyzed for its xanthine oxidase inhibitory activities. The results showed that the highest contribution to this hydrolyzed extract was from the flavone apiigenin, which accounted for 29.96% of the contribution. Apiigenin demonstrated a high activity with an EC50 value of 3.27 ± 0.09 μg/mL. These findings indicate that the different contents and activities of xanthine oxidase inhibitors in the hydrolyzed extracts play a major role in influencing activity.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several potent medicinal compounds including rosmarinic acid, salvianolic acid B, sineseitin, and verbascoide. These polyphenolic constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Research indicates that these specific chemical structures help mitigate oxidative stress within various biological systems. The presence of these bioactive molecules makes the species a subject of interest for pharmaceutical development. Scientists continue to study how the synergy of these compounds affects human health.

According to a study published by "Phytomedicine : international journal of phytotherapy and phytopharmacology", Orthosiphon aristatus contains high concentrations of antioxidative phytochemicals, especially rosmarinic acid and other caffeic acid derivatives. The leaf extract contains more phytochemicals than the stem extract, particularly caffeic acid derivatives including glycosylated and alkylated caffeic acids. Rosmarinic acid dimer and salvianolic acid B appear to be the major constituents of the plant. When extracts were stored at 4 °C for 5 weeks, the phytochemical profile remained stable for at least 1 month. A significant change in the profile was observed at the 5th week based on statistical pattern recognition. This plant is a promising source of rosmarinic acid, its dimer, and salvianolic acid B with remarkable antioxidant properties.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating liver diseases, non-alcoholic fatty liver disease, hyperglycaemic conditions, kidney diseases, and urinary tract disorders. Its medicinal properties are often utilized to support metabolic functions and stabilize blood glucose levels. The herb also serves as a natural diuretic to assist with renal health and the management of various kidney issues. Furthermore, its cleansing effects help alleviate discomfort associated with urinary tract disorders. By addressing these specific ailments, the plant plays a significant role in traditional herbal medicine.

According to a study published by "Pharmaceuticaals (Basel, Switzerland)", Orthosiphon aristatus has been shown to attenuate obesity and hyperglycaemic conditions. Feeding C57BL/6 mice with 400 mg/kg of the standardized extract for 8 weeks inhibited the progression of non-alcoholic fatty liver disease (NAFLD). In treated mice, alanine aminotransferase and aspartate transaminase were reduced by 74.2% ± 7.69 and 52.8% ± 7.83, respectively. The extract also reduced serum levels of glucose by 50% ± 5.71, insulin by 70.2% ± 12.09, total cholesterol by 27.5% ± 15.93, triglycerides by 63.2% ± 16.5, and low-density lipoprotein by 62.5% ± 4.93. Additionally, the aqueous, hydroethanolic and ethanolic fractions of the extract significantly reduced intracellular fat accumulation in HepG2 cells treated with palmitic-oleic acid.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause severe electrolyte imbalance, specifically hypokalemia, due to its potent diuretic properties. This loss of potassium often leads to physical symptoms such as nausea, dizziness, and diarrhea. Users may also experience painful stomach cramps as a result of these physiological changes. Monitoring mineral levels is essential to avoid the complications associated with its use.
