Aster albescens

What's the taxonomical classification of Aster albescens?

Aster albescens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. The plant is a prominent member of the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Aster, and its specific biological identity is defined by the species name albescens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aster albescens has a perennial growth habit characterized by erect stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 40 centimeters. The leaves are primarily basal and alternate, displaying an ovate to lanceolate shape with serrated margins and a soft, pubescent texture. Its inflorescence consists of small flower heads featuring white ray florets surrounding a central disc of yellowish tubular florets. The fine, whitish hairs covering the stems and foliage provide a distinctively pale appearance to the plant. These morphological features help distinguish the species within its native montane habitats.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of the Rocky Mountains, spanning from southern Wyoming through Colorado and into New Mexico. It typically thrives in montane and subalpine ecosystems where rocky soils and open spaces are abundant. Within these mountain ranges, it is often found growing in subalpine meadows, talus slopes, and along coniferous forest edges. Its distribution is primarily restricted to these specific elevations above the timberline. This localized range makes the species a characteristic component of the western North American alpine landscape.
