Lycium truncatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Lycium truncatum?

Lycium truncatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this classification, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its descent through the order Solanales, it falls into the family Solanaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Lycium, and its unique species designation, truncatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lycium truncatum has a deciduous, spreading shrub habit characterized by slender, woody stems that typically reach a height of 0.5 to 2 meters. The leaves are small, elliptical to lanceolate in shape, and arranged alternately along the branches. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often displaying shades of purple or violet. These plants produce small, fleshy berries that transition from green to a dark reddish-purple color as they ripen. The fine texture of the foliage and the branched structure of the stems are defining morphological features of this species.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of East Asia, specifically concentrated throughout northern and northwestern China. Its range extends across diverse landscapes including the sandy soils of the Gobi Desert and the temperate shrublands of Inner Mongolia. It is also frequently found in the dry provinces of Xinjiang and Gansu where low precipitation levels favor its growth. The species thrives in these harsh environments due to its ability to tolerate extreme temperature fluctuations and saline conditions.
