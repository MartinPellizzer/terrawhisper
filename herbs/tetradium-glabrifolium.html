What's the taxonomical classification of Tetradium glabrifolium?

Tetradium glabrifolium belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Sapindales and is a member of the family Rutaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Tetradium, and its species designation, glabrifolium.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tetradium glabrifolium has glabrous, lanceolate leaves that are arranged oppositely along a woody stem and small, actinomorphic flowers with five petals. Its stems are typically slender and undergo lignification as the plant matures into a shrubby form. What are the pharmacological activities of Tetradium glabrifolium?

This plant has antibacterial, uterine relaxation, and vasorelaxation pharmacological activities. Its antibacterial properties allow it to combat various pathogenic microorganisms effectively. The plant also exhibits uterine relaxation, which can assist in managing smooth muscle contractions. Additionally, the presence of vasorelaxation properties helps in widening blood vessels to improve circulation. These diverse biological effects highlight the therapeutic potential of Tetradium glabrifolium in medicinal applications.

According to a study published by "Chemistry & biodiversity", three novel quinoline alkaloids, tetradiunitiside A (1), tetradiunitiside B (2), and glycohaplopine-6-O-α-l-rhamnopyranoside (3), were isolated from the fruits of Tetradium glabrifolium along with eight known ones (4-11). All 11 isolated compounds were evaluated for their antibacterial activities against Bacillus gasoformans, Bacillus subtilis, Pseudomonas solanacearum, and Pseudomonas aeruginosa. These compounds demonstrated potential antibacterial activities with minimum inhibitory concentration (MIC) values ranging from 100 to 3.125 µg/mL. Among the isolated substances, compound 6 exhibited the most significant antibacterial activity against the four strains of bacteria. These findings help elucidate the pharmacological substance basis for the antibacterial activity of Tetradium glabrifolium.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", the plant Tetradium glabrifolium contains bioactive constituents including evodiamine, dehydroevodiamine, rutaecarpine, and synephrine. The amount of relaxing alkaloids in Tetradium glabrifolium was found to be higher in specimens from Wu-ling than in those from Lee Mountain. Despite this, the relaxation measured in both the uterus and the aorta was less in the Tetradium glabrifolium from Wu-ling. Following 1 year of storage, the vasorelaxation of the Tetradium glabrifolium from Lee Mountain remained unchanged.
