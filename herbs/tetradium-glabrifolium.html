What's the taxonomical classification of Tetradium glabrifolium?

Tetradium glabrifolium belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Sapindales and is a member of the family Rutaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Tetradium, and its species designation, glabrifolium.

What are the pharmacological activities of Tetradium glabrifolium?

This plant has antibacterial, uterine relaxation, and vasorelaxation pharmacological activities. Its antibacterial properties allow it to inhibit the growth of various pathogenic microorganisms. The ability to induce uterine relaxation suggests potential applications in managing smooth muscle contractions. Furthermore, the plant exhibits vasorelaxation by helping to widen blood vessels and improve circulation. These diverse biological effects highlight the therapeutic potential of Tetradium glabrifolium in medicinal research.

As discussed in a study, three novel quinoline alkaloids named tetradiunitiside A (1), tetradiunitiside B (2), and glycohaplopine-6-O-α-l-rhamnopyranoside (3), along with eight known ones (4-11), were isolated from the fruits of Tetradium glabrifolium. All 11 isolated compounds were evaluated for their antibacterial activities against Bacillus gasoformans, Bacillus subtilis, Pseudomonas solanacearum, and Pseudomonas aeruginosa. These compounds exhibited potential antibacterial activities with minimum inhibitory concentration (MIC) values ranging from 100 to 3.125 µg/mL. Among the isolated substances, compound 6 showed the most significant antibacterial activity against the four strains of bacteria. These findings provide information regarding the pharmacological substance basis for the antibacterial activity of Tetradium glabrifolium.
