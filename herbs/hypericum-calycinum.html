Golden St. John'S Wort (Hypericum calycinum)

Golden St. John'S Wort (Hypericum calycinum) is a member of the Clusiaceae family, native to Europe, and Asia. Traditionally, its flowers, leaves, and stems have been used for infusions, decoctions, and tinctures.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, tonic, and bitter actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and traditional chinese medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 0.3 to 1.2 meters.
Leaves
Opposite, oval-shaped leaves with dark green upper surface and lighter green lower surface, bearing two distinct stomatal bands on the lower surface.
Flowers
Flowers are yellow with four petals arranged in a whorl, exhibit actinomorphic symmetry, and have two stamens with distinct anthers and a superior ovary with two carpels.
Stems
Woody, branched, with opposite, ascending or spreading leaves, and smooth, glabrous, cylindrical stems that are often reddish-brown in color.
