Golden St. John's Wort

Hypericum calycinum

Golden St. John's Wort (Hypericum calycinum) is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, known for its bright yellow flowers and historical use in traditional medicine to support mood and mental well-being.

Botanical Identification

Golden St. John's Wort, Hypericum calycinum, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Hypericaceae family and is also known as European St. John's Wort or wood St. John's Wort. The plant has small yellow flowers with orange stamens and dark green, oval leaves. It is commonly referred to by various regional names in herbal literature.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Eudicots
  • Order: Garryales
  • Family: Clusiaceae
  • Genus: Hypericum
  • Species: Hypericum calycinum

Active Compounds

Golden St. John's Wort contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids. These compounds work together to exert their therapeutic effects, with flavonoids providing antioxidant support and terpenoids contributing to anti-inflammatory properties. The synergistic interaction of these compounds enhances the herb's overall efficacy in herbal medicine.

Golden St. John's Wort Uses.

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for treating mild digestive issues and as a calming tea.

Decoction: Used for treating skin conditions and inflammatory disorders.

Extract: Used for depression and anxiety due to its antidepressant properties.

Safety Profile

Golden St. John's Wort is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses for short-term treatment. However, it may interact with certain medications, including antidepressants and birth control pills, and should be used with caution. Always consult a healthcare provider before use, especially if you are on other medications or have underlying health conditions.
