What to know about Hypericum calycinum (st john's wort) before using it medicinally

St John's Wort, scientifically known as Hypericum calycinum, is a herb renowned for its remarkable health benefits, which have been extensively studied and found to improve mood, reduce anxiety and depression, and even alleviate symptoms of insomnia.

While it may not be as well-known in culinary circles, st John's Wort is also used to add flavor and aroma to teas, soups, and salads, particularly in traditional European cuisine. As a popular ornamental plant, st John's Wort is prized for its vibrant yellow flowers and attractive foliage, making it a favorite among gardeners and florists. From a botanical perspective, st John's Wort belongs to the Hypericaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive bright yellow petals and numerous seeds.

With roots dating back thousands of years, st John's Wort has been mentioned in ancient Greek and Roman texts, as well as featured prominently in traditional folk medicine practices across Europe and Asia.
