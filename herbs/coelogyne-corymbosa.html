Coelogyne corymbosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Coelogyne corymbosa?

Coelogyne corymbosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Asparagales, this plant is a member of the diverse family Orchidaceae. More specifically, it is situated within the genus Coelogyne, ultimately reaching its unique biological identification at the species level as Coelogyne corymbosa.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Coelogyne corymbosa contains a variety of secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, and various phenolic compounds. These bioactive substances are characteristic of many epiphytic orchids within the Orchidaceae family. Research into these specific compounds often focuses on their potential antioxidant and antimicrobial properties. While the plant is primarily studied for its botanical significance, these chemical constituents suggest possible therapeutic applications in traditional medicine. What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various inflammatory conditions, skin infections, and respiratory ailments through its traditional medicinal properties. It is frequently employed in folk medicine to alleviate symptoms associated with asthma and chronic coughs. The plant's extracts are also applied topically to manage skin irritations and minor wounds. Some practitioners utilize it to address internal imbalances that lead to localized swelling. These applications rely on the bioactive compounds found within its specific chemical profile.
