What's the taxonomical classification of Coelogyne corymbosa?

Coelogyne corymbosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Asparagales, this plant is a member of the diverse family Orchidaceae. More specifically, it is situated within the genus Coelogyne, ultimately reaching its unique biological identification at the species level as Coelogyne corymbosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Coelogyne corymbosa has sympodial rhizomes with small, fleshy, ovate pseudobulbs that are typically spaced apart and topped by two to three thin, lanceolate leaves.

The inflorescence emerges from the base of the pseudobulbs and features a loose, many-flowered corymb-like structure. Each individual flower displays a relatively small, greenish-white or yellowish lip that is often characterized by a distinct, fleshy callus. The petals and sepals are narrow and spreading, contributing to the delicate appearance of the blooming cluster.

This species exhibits a compact growth habit suitable for epiphytic environments.
