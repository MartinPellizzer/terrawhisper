Cerasus japonica

What's the taxonomical classification of Cerasus japonica?

Cerasus japonica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further falls under the order Rosales and is a member of the family Rosaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement in the genus Cerasus and its designation as the species japonica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cerasus japonica has small, oval-shaped leaves with serrated margins, woody stems, and clusters of small white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The foliage is typically dark green and glossy, providing a dense canopy for the plant. Its branches are slender yet sturdy, often exhibiting a spreading growth habit as the shrub matures. During the fruiting season, the plant produces small, fleshy drupes that change color as they ripen. The overall structure is characterized by a multi-stemmed form that is common among many deciduous flowering shrubs.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Cerasus japonica contains high concentrations of organic acids, anthocyanins, vitamin C, and various phenolic compounds like quercetin and rutin. What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Cerasus japonica targets inflammatory enzymes such as cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2) and lipoxygenase (LOX), along with pro-inflammatory signaling proteins like nuclear factor-kappa B (NF-κB). These interactions occur primarily through its high concentration of bioactive polyphenols and anthocyanins that inhibit the production of inflammatory mediators. By modulating these protein pathways, the plant effectively reduces the expression of cytokines like tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-α) and interleukin-6 (IL-6). Furthermore, its components can influence antioxidant enzymes and protein kinases to mitigate oxidative stress within cells. This multifaceted enzymatic inhibition explains the plant's documented anti-inflammatory and antioxidant therapeutic properties.

What are the therapeutic actions of Cerasus japonica?

Cerasus japonica has therapeutic properties that function as an astringent, diuretic, tonic, and hemostatic agent to support bodily functions. The astringent qualities of the plant help to constrict tissues and reduce inflammation in various membranes. Its diuretic effects promote the excretion of excess fluids through the renal system to assist in detoxification. As a gentle tonic, the plant helps to restore vigor and maintain overall physiological balance. Additionally, its hemostatic action is effective in controlling bleeding and promoting proper blood coagulation.
