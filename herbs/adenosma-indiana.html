What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Adenosma indiana has perennial, rhizomatous stems that produce lanceolate to ovate leaves with serrated margins and a distinctive hairy texture. The foliage is typically arranged in an alternate pattern along the upright flowering stalks. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often presenting in shades of white or pale pink. These blossoms are characterized by their delicate structure and are supported by slender pedicels. The overall architecture of the plant is compact, reflecting its preference for moist, shaded woodland environments.
