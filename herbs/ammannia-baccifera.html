Ammannia baccifera

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ammannia baccifera has small, opposite, sessile leaves that are typically lanceolate to elliptical in shape and possess entire or slightly serrated margins. The plant displays a low-growing, creeping, or erect growth habit depending on its immediate aquatic environment. Its flowers are minute and arranged in dense axillary clusters, often appearing pink or white in color. The reproductive structures include small capsules that serve as the primary method of seed dispersal within wetland habitats. These morphological traits collectively allow the species to adapt to the fluctuating water levels of its native ecosystems.
