Ammannia baccifera

What's the taxonomical classification of Ammannia baccifera?

Ammannia baccifera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy places it in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, following the order Myrtales. Within this order, it is a member of the family Lythraceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by its placement in the genus Ammannia, with its unique designation being the species baccifera.

What are the pharmacological activities of Ammannia baccifera?

This plant has analgesic, anti-inflammatory, anti-nociceptive, and antioxidant pharmacological activities that contribute to its medicinal value. Its ability to reduce pain and mitigate the sensation of harmful stimuli makes it a potential candidate for treating various discomforts. The presence of anti-inflammatory properties allows the plant to suppress swelling and inflammatory responses within biological systems. Furthermore, its antioxidant capacity helps neutralize free radicals, protecting cells from oxidative stress and damage. These combined effects suggest that the plant could play a significant role in managing complex physiological conditions.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", Ammannia baccifera L. has been reported as a folklore remedy for the treatment of inflammation and tumor in Rajasthan, India. The methanol extract from the aerial parts was investigated for antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and anti-nociceptive effects using various in vitro and in vivo models. The extract showed a free radical scavenging IC50 value that was significantly superior to the positive standards BHA and rutin. Significant anti-inflammatory and analgesic activities were exhibited at the doses of 100 and 200 mg/kg p.o. At the higher dose of 200 mg/kg, the analgesic effect of the extract was comparable with the standard drugs aspirin and morphine.

Research into these substances often focuses on their potential antioxidant and antimicrobial properties. The presence of these unique molecules distinguishes this species within its botanical family. Understanding their chemical structure helps scientists explore new pharmaceutical applications.

According to a study published by "Journal of chromatographic science", Ammannia baccifera contains the bioactive constituents 4-hydroxy-α-tetralone (4H), tetralone-4-O-β-D-glucopyranoside (T4), and ellaic acid (EA). The limit of detection (LOD) and limit of quantification (LOQ) for EA were 1.51 and 5.06 µg/mL, while for T4 they were 0.70 and 2.33 µg/mL. For 4H, the LOQ and LOD were 0.73 and 0.22 µg/mL, respectively. The analysis of these analytes was successfully applied to 5 different leaf and root samples of A. baccifera. The method achieved linearity with r(2) > 0.999, a repeatability with a relative standard deviation ≤ 1.73%, and a recovery of 99.06-100.76%.
