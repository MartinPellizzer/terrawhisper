Ammannia baccifera

What's the taxonomical classification of Ammannia baccifera?

Ammannia baccifera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy places it in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, following the order Myrtales. Within this order, it is a member of the family Lythraceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by its placement in the genus Ammannia, with its unique designation being the species baccifera.

Its ability to reduce pain and diminish the sensation of harmful stimuli is demonstrated through its significant analgesic and anti-nociceptive properties. The presence of bioactive compounds also allows it to act as an antioxidant, neutralizing harmful free radicals in the body. These combined therapeutic actions make Ammannia baccifera a subject of interest for various pharmacological applications.

As discussed in a study, Ammannia baccifera L. is a folklore remedy used in Rajasthan, India, for the treatment of inflammation and tumor. The methanol extract from the aerial parts was investigated for antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and anti-nociceptive effects using in vitro and in vivo models. The extract showed a free radical scavenging IC(50) value that was significantly superior to the positive standards BHA and rutin. Significant anti-inflammatory and analgesic activities were exhibited at doses of 100 and 200mg/kg p.o. Specifically, the analgesic effect of the 200mg/kg dose was comparable with the standard drugs aspirin and morphine.

Researchers analyze these substances to understand how they interact with human biological systems.

As discussed in a study, Ammannia baccifera contains the bioactive constituents 4-hydroxy-α-tetralone (4H), tetralone-4-O-β-D-glucopyranoside (T4), and ellaic acid (EA). The limit of detection (LOQ) and limit of quantification (LOQ) for EA were 1.51 and 5.06 µg/mL, while for T4 they were 0.70 and 2.33 µg/mL. For 4H, the LOQ and LOQ were identified as 0.22 and 0.73 µg/mL, respectively. Analysis of these three analytes was successfully applied to 5 different leaf and root samples of A. baccifera. The method used for this estimation achieved linearity with r(2) > 0.999, a relative standard deviation ≤ 1.73%, and recovery rates between 99.06-100.76%.
