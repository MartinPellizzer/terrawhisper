What's the taxonomical classification of Phaenosperma globosum?

Phaenosperma globosum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further falls under the order Poales and is a member of the family Poaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement within the genus Phaenosperma, culminating in its unique species designation, Phaenosperma globosum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phaenosperma globosum has globose, fleshy fruits that are approximately two centimeters in diameter and characterized by a smooth, reddish-brown integument. These spherical structures are supported by a short, sturdy pedicel that connects the fruit to the central stem. The internal anatomy of the plant features thick-walled seeds enclosed within a protective, succulent pericarp. Small, lanceolate leaves surround the developing fruit to provide essential photosynthetic nutrients.
