Phaenosperma globosum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phaenosperma globosum has globose, fleshy fruits that are approximately two centimeters in diameter and characterized by a smooth, reddish-brown integument. These spherical structures are supported by a short, sturdy pedicel that connects the fruit to the central stem. The internal anatomy of the plant features thick-walled seeds enclosed within a protective, succulent pericarp. Small, lanceolate leaves surround the developing fruit to provide essential photosynthetic nutrients. The overall morphology is designed to facilitate dispersal through animal consumption of the nutrient-dense globes.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Phaenosperma globosum can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, acute skin irritation upon contact, and potential neurological impairment if ingested. These adverse reactions often manifest as intense abdominal cramping and vomiting shortly after consumption. Direct skin contact with the plant's sap may result in painful rashes or localized swelling that lasts for several days. In cases of accidental ingestion, individuals might also experience dizziness, blurred vision, or muscle tremors. Immediate medical attention is necessary if any symptoms of toxicity appear following exposure.
