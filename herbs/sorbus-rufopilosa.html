Sorbus rufopilosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Sorbus rufopilosa?

Sorbus rufopilosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant falls under the order Rosales. It is further categorized within the family Rosaceae, which leads to its placement in the genus Sorbus. Finally, the specific taxonomic designation for this plant is completed by its species name, rufopilosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sorbus rufopilosa has pinnately compound leaves characterized by numerous lanceolate leaflets that are covered in dense, reddish-brown pubescence on both the upper and lower surfaces. The leaflets typically feature serrated margins and prominent venation that is accentuated by the fine hairs. This shrub or small tree produces clusters of small white flowers that eventually develop into round, orange or reddish pomes. The bark and young twigs also exhibit a fine coating of reddish hairs, which is a distinguishing morphological feature of the species. These structural adaptations contribute to the plant's overall texture and visual appearance in its natural habitat.
