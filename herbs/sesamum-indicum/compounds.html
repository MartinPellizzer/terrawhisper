Sesame (Sesamum indicum) Compounds

The primary compounds in sesame include essential oil, phenolic acids, tannins, flavonoids, terpenoids, saponins, coumarin, glycosides, alkaloids, and anthocyanins. These compounds contribute to sesame's medicinal properties, such as antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial effects. Each plays a role in its traditional use in herbal medicine.

Sesame (Sesamum indicum) Monograph

1. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound of Sesamum indicum, known for its therapeutic properties. It contains volatile compounds like sesamin and sesamolin, which contribute to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. This oil is widely used in herbal medicine for its calming and skin-nourishing benefits.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Sesamum indicum, commonly known as sesame. It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties and is associated with anti-inflammatory and neuroprotective effects. This compound is found in significant amounts in sesame seeds and oils, making them valuable in traditional herbal medicine.

3. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Sesamum indicum, known for its astringent properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in reducing inflammation and treating skin conditions. Tannins also aid in the preservation of sesame oil and enhance its medicinal effects. This compound plays a vital role in the therapeutic applications of sesame in herbal medicine.

4. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Sesamum indicum, commonly known as sesame. These compounds contribute to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting various therapeutic applications in herbal medicine. They help protect cells from oxidative stress and may aid in reducing inflammation and improving cardiovascular health.

5. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Sesamum indicum, commonly known as sesame. These compounds contribute to the plant's therapeutic properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. They are responsible for many of the health benefits associated with sesame oil and seeds.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a bioactive compound found in Sesamum indicum. It exhibits mild detergent properties and may support digestive health. Saponins in sesame are also associated with potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. These compounds contribute to the overall therapeutic profile of sesame in traditional herbal medicine.

7. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Sesamum indicum. It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and anticoagulant properties. Coumarin is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential to support cardiovascular health. Its presence in sesame oil enhances the therapeutic value of the plant.

8. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Sesamum indicum, commonly known as sesame. It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Glycosides in sesame may support cardiovascular health and aid in treating digestive disorders. These compounds are often extracted and used in traditional herbal remedies.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Sesamum indicum, commonly known as sesame. It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by interacting with biological systems to support various therapeutic effects. While not the primary active component, alkaloids in sesame may play a role in enhancing the plant's overall efficacy in traditional herbal medicine.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Sesamum indicum. It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties and may support cardiovascular and inflammatory health. Its presence in sesame seeds highlights the plant's potential in herbal medicine for promoting overall wellness.
