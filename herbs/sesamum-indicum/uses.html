Sesame (Sesamum indicum) Uses

Sesame is used to treat respiratory conditions such as bronchitis, cough, asthma, and respiratory infections. It also aids in managing skin infections, wound healing, and inflammatory skin conditions. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties help with skin irritation and fungal infections.

Sesame (Sesamum indicum) Monograph

1. Bronchitis

Sesame seed oil is used in traditional herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms of bronchitis. It helps reduce inflammation and mucus production in the airways. The oil is often inhaled or applied externally to ease breathing and soothe respiratory discomfort. Its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties support bronchial health.

2. Cough

Sesame is used in herbal medicine to soothe coughs due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. It helps loosen mucus and reduce throat irritation. Sesame oil is often applied externally or taken internally in small amounts to alleviate persistent cough symptoms.

3. Asthma

Sesame seed oil is used in traditional herbal medicine to help alleviate asthma symptoms. It acts as an expectorant, loosening mucus and improving respiratory function. The oil's anti-inflammatory properties may reduce bronchial irritation, supporting easier breathing in individuals with asthma.

4. Respiratory infections

Sesame seed oil is used in traditional medicine to alleviate symptoms of respiratory infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce congestion and soothe coughing when inhaled or applied externally. Its warming effect may also support overall respiratory health.

5. Skin infections

Sesame oil is used topically to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce bacterial growth and soothe inflamed skin. Applying it directly to affected areas can promote healing and prevent further infection. It is commonly used in traditional herbal remedies for conditions like eczema and fungal infections.

6. Wound healing

Sesame oil is used topically to promote wound healing due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps prevent infection and reduces inflammation, supporting faster tissue repair. The oil's high concentration of essential fatty acids also aids in skin regeneration and moisture retention.

7. Inflammatory skin conditions

Sesame oil is used topically to treat inflammatory skin conditions due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and itching associated with eczema, psoriasis, and fungal infections. Its high concentration of antioxidants and essential fatty acids support skin healing and barrier function. Regular application can soothe irritation and promote skin recovery.

8. Skin irritation

Sesame oil is used topically to soothe skin irritation due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. It helps reduce redness, itching, and dryness, making it effective for eczema, psoriasis, and minor burns. Regular application can promote skin healing and protect against environmental damage.

9. Fungal infections

Sesame oil has demonstrated antifungal properties, particularly effective against Candida and Aspergillus species. It works by disrupting fungal cell membranes and inhibiting their growth. Topical application of sesame oil can help treat skin infections caused by fungi. Its natural antifungal activity makes it a valuable remedy in herbal medicine for fungal infections.

10. Inflammation

Sesame seeds contain anti-inflammatory compounds like sesamin and sesamolin, which help reduce inflammation by inhibiting inflammatory pathways. They are used in traditional medicine to alleviate symptoms of conditions like arthritis and skin inflammation. Sesame oil is also applied topically to soothe inflamed tissues and promote healing.
