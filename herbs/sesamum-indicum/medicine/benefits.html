10 Health Benefits Of Sesamum Indicum (Sesame)

Sesamum indicum, also known as sesame, has health benefits such as reducing inflammation, improving heart health, and aiding digestion.

Its medicinal properties include anti-inflammatory and antioxidant compounds, specifically lignans and sesamin. These properties have been shown to lower cholesterol levels and reduce the risk of cardiovascular disease.

For example, incorporating sesame oil into one's diet may help alleviate symptoms of menopause, such as hot flashes, and also reduce the risk of certain cancers.

This article explains in details the 10 best health benefits of Sesamum indicum.

1. Reduces hypertension

Sesamum indicum reduces hypertension because it is rich in sesamin and sesamolin, two potent compounds that have been shown to lower blood pressure.

These compounds work by inhibiting the activity of angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE), a key player in the renin-angiotensin system responsible for regulating blood pressure.

Additionally, sesame oil has been found to relax blood vessels and improve blood flow, further contributing to its hypotensive effects.

2. Improves digestion

Sesamum indicum improves digestion because of its unique combination of nutrients and bioactive compounds.

The seed contains fiber, vitamins, and minerals that help stimulate digestive enzymes and promote the growth of beneficial gut bacteria.

Additionally, sesamin, a lignan found in sesame oil, has been shown to improve intestinal motility and reduce inflammation in the gastrointestinal tract, thereby enhancing overall digestion and reducing symptoms of digestive disorders such as constipation and bloating.

3. Boosts heart health

Sesamum indicum boosts heart health because of its rich content of antioxidants, fiber, and beneficial compounds like sesamin and sesamolin.

These nutrients help to lower LDL cholesterol levels, reduce inflammation in the blood vessels, and improve blood flow to the heart.

Regular consumption of sesame seeds or oil has been shown to decrease the risk of heart disease, strokes, and cardiac arrhythmias, making it an excellent addition to a heart-healthy diet.

4. Lowers cholesterol

Sesamum indicum lowers cholesterol because of its rich content of phytosterols and sesamin.

These compounds have been shown to inhibit the absorption of dietary cholesterol in the gut, thereby reducing blood cholesterol levels.

Additionally, sesame contains antioxidants and polyphenolic compounds that help prevent the formation of foam cells, which can contribute to plaque buildup in arteries and increase the risk of heart disease.

5. Reduces inflammation

Sesamum indicum reduces inflammation because of its rich content of polyphenolic compounds and sesamin, a unique lignan.

These bioactive molecules exhibit potent anti-inflammatory properties by inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines, suppressing NF-κB activation, and scavenging free radicals.

Additionally, sesame oil's omega-6 fatty acids also contribute to its anti-inflammatory effects by reducing eicosanoid synthesis and prostaglandin E2 production.

6. Prevents cancer

Sesamum indicum prevents cancer because of its rich content of sesamin and sesamolin, which have been shown to exhibit significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds have been found to inhibit the growth of cancer cells, induce apoptosis (cell death), and reduce the risk of tumor formation in various animal studies.

Additionally, sesame has been found to modulate DNA damage and repair mechanisms, further supporting its potential role as a natural anticancer agent.

7. Supports bone health

Sesamum indicum supports bone health because it is rich in calcium, magnesium, and phosphorus - essential nutrients for maintaining strong bones.

Sesame seeds contain a compound called sesamin, which has been shown to inhibit the breakdown of bone tissue, reducing the risk of osteoporosis and fractures.

Additionally, the antioxidant properties of sesame may help protect bones from oxidative stress and inflammation, further promoting overall bone health and reducing the risk of age-related bone loss.

8. Alleviates anxiety

Sesamum indicum alleviates anxiety because of its rich content of sesamin and sesamolin, two unique compounds that have been shown to possess anxiolytic properties.

These bioactive compounds interact with GABA receptors in the brain, promoting relaxation and reducing stress levels.

Additionally, sesame has been found to contain high levels of antioxidants, which help mitigate the negative effects of oxidative stress on mental health, further contributing to its anxiolytic effects.

9. Enhances oral health

Sesamum indicum enhances oral health because of its unique properties and compounds.

The seeds contain antioxidants, flavonoids, and lignans that help reduce inflammation and prevent gum disease by inhibiting bacterial growth. Additionally, sesame oil has antibacterial properties that combat bad breath and mouth ulcers.

Regular consumption or use of sesame products can contribute to a healthy, white smile and fresh breath.

10. Aids in blood sugar control

Sesamum indicum aids in blood sugar control because it contains a unique compound called sesamin, which has been shown to improve insulin sensitivity and reduce blood glucose levels.

Additionally, the lignans present in sesame seeds have been found to delay gastric emptying, slowing down the absorption of sugar into the bloodstream and preventing sudden spikes in blood sugar levels.

This natural ability to regulate blood sugar makes sesame a valuable ingredient for individuals with diabetes or those looking to manage their blood sugar levels.
