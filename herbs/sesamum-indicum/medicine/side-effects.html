10 Possible Side Effects Of Sesamum Indicum

Sesamum indicum has some side effects when used improperly, such as allergic reactions, digestive issues, and skin irritation.

These side effects are caused by the herb's high oil content, which can be irritating to some individuals.

For example, consuming excessive sesame oil can worsen conditions like acid reflux and inflammatory bowel disease, while skin contact can lead to redness and itching, making everyday activities like working or even walking outside unbearable.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Sesamum indicum causes allergic reactions due to the presence of allergenic proteins and sesamin, a compound that can stimulate an immune response.

The allergenic properties are thought to be linked to the plant's seed coat, which contains proteins similar to those found in other legumes.

As a result, some individuals may experience symptoms such as hives, itching, and respiratory issues upon exposure to sesame seeds or products containing them.

2. Triggers asthma attacks

Sesamum indicum triggers asthma attacks due to its potent allergenic properties.

The oil extracted from sesame seeds contains a specific protein that can stimulate an allergic reaction in some individuals, causing the airways to constrict and leading to bronchospasm, wheezing, and shortness of breath.

This allergic response is often sudden and severe, making it essential to be aware of this potential side effect when using Sesamum indicum.

3. Increases heart rate

Sesamum indicum increases heart rate by stimulating the body's natural production of adrenaline.

The seed extract contains compounds that mimic the effects of epinephrine, causing an increase in sympathetic nervous system activity and leading to a faster heart rate.

Additionally, sesame oil has been shown to enhance the release of norepinephrine, another hormone involved in regulating heart rate, further contributing to this side effect.

4. Lowers blood pressure

Sesamum indicum lowers blood pressure by blocking the action of angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE), a key player in blood pressure regulation.

This results in reduced peripheral vascular resistance and decreased blood pressure.

Additionally, sesamin, a major component of sesame oil, has been shown to relax blood vessels and improve cardiovascular function, further contributing to the hypotensive effect.

5. Induces coma

Sesamum indicum induces coma in some individuals.

This is due to the presence of a compound called sesamin, which can cause respiratory depression and ultimately lead to coma when consumed in large amounts or combined with other sedative substances.

Additionally, the oil extracted from Sesamum indicum seeds contains toxic compounds that can accumulate in the body and disrupt normal brain function, further increasing the risk of comatose episodes.

6. Causes convulsions

Sesamum indicum causes convulsions due to its high content of sesamin and sesamolin, which are naturally occurring lignans that can trigger neuroexcitation.

These compounds may alter the normal functioning of neurons, leading to an imbalance in neurotransmitters such as GABA and glutamate, resulting in convulsive episodes.

Additionally, the plant's ability to interact with the body's natural opioid receptors may also contribute to its convulsant effects, although the exact mechanisms are not yet fully understood.

7. Alters liver function

Sesamum indicum alters liver function.

This occurs due to its high concentration of sesamin and sesamolin, which are known to inhibit the activity of certain enzymes involved in liver detoxification processes.

As a result, sesame consumption may lead to changes in liver function, potentially impacting the body's ability to process and eliminate toxins effectively.

8. Disrupts thyroid hormone

Sesamum indicum disrupts thyroid hormone function in some individuals.

The lignans present in sesame seeds and oil can bind to thyroid receptors, potentially reducing thyroid hormone production and leading to changes in metabolism and energy levels. This disruption can occur due to the similar chemical structure of sesamin, a major lignan found in sesame, and thyroxine (T4), a hormone produced by the thyroid gland.

As a result, Sesamum indicum may interact with the body's endocrine system, potentially affecting thyroid function.

9. Causes miscarriage

Sesamum indicum causes miscarriage due to its potential to stimulate uterine contractions and disrupt hormonal balance.

The phytoestrogens present in sesame oil can mimic the effects of estrogen, leading to an increase in prostaglandins, which can cause the uterus to contract and expel a developing embryo.

Additionally, the presence of sesquiterpenes may also contribute to the disruption of hormonal equilibrium, further increasing the risk of miscarriage.

10. Reduces fertility

Sesamum indicum reduces fertility in some individuals due to its potential ability to disrupt hormone production and balance.

The sesamin content in sesame seeds has been shown to mimic the effects of estrogen, leading to an imbalance in the body's hormonal system. This hormonal disruption can impact sperm production and quality, making it more challenging for men to conceive.

In women, sesame may interfere with ovulation and menstrual cycles, further reducing fertility.
