10 Medicinal Constituents Of Sesamum Indicum (Sesame)

Sesamum indicum has active constituents such as sesamin, sesamolin, and sesamol, which possess anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and anticoagulant properties.

These compounds have been found to alleviate symptoms of various ailments, including high cholesterol, diabetes, and cardiovascular disease. For instance, sesamin has been shown to reduce LDL cholesterol levels, thereby improving heart health and reducing the risk of stroke and cardiac arrest.

Additionally, sesame oil's antioxidant properties can help protect against cell damage and oxidative stress, potentially leading to improved overall well-being and a reduced risk of chronic diseases.

1. Sesamin

Sesamum indicum sesamin is a plant-based oil that is extracted from the seeds of Sesamum indicum.

It has been used for centuries in traditional medicine and as a cooking oil due to its unique nutritional profile.

Sesamin contains antioxidants, omega-6 fatty acids, and phytosterols, making it a potential health promoter with anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antifungal properties.

2. Sesamolin

Sesamum indicum sesamolin is a type of lignan that is naturally present in sesame seeds and oil.

It is a potent antioxidant and has been shown to have numerous health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Sesamolin has also been found to inhibit the growth of cancer cells and may help to reduce the risk of certain diseases.

3. Oleic acid

Sesamum indicum oleic acid is a fatty acid found in the oil extracted from its seeds.

It is a non-essential fatty acid, meaning that it cannot be synthesized by the human body and must be obtained through dietary sources or supplements. Oleic acid has been shown to have various health benefits, including reducing inflammation, improving heart health, and lowering cholesterol levels.

It is also used as an ingredient in cosmetics and pharmaceuticals.

4. Linoleic acid

Sesamum indicum linoleic acid is a polyunsaturated fatty acid that is naturally present in sesame seeds and oil.

This essential fatty acid plays a crucial role in maintaining healthy skin, hair, and mucous membranes, while also supporting cardiovascular health by reducing inflammation and improving blood lipid profiles.

Linoleic acid has been shown to have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, making it an important component of a balanced diet.

5. Palmitic acid

Sesamum indicum palmitic acid is a type of fatty acid that is naturally present in sesame seeds.

It is a saturated fatty acid, making up about 10-15% of the total fatty acid content in sesame oil. Palmitic acid has been shown to have various health benefits, including reducing inflammation and improving lipid profiles.

Additionally, it also exhibits antimicrobial properties, making it an effective natural preservative for food products.

6. Stearic acid

Sesamum indicum stearic acid is a naturally occurring saturated fatty acid found in the seed of the plant.

It is an important component of sesame oil and has various industrial applications.

Sesame stearic acid has moisturizing properties and is used in cosmetics, pharmaceuticals, and food products due to its mildness, stability, and non-comedogenic nature.

7. Calcium sesamin

Sesamum indicum calcium sesamin is a unique and bioactive compound extracted from sesame seeds.

This compound has been shown to possess numerous health benefits, including antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which can help protect against chronic diseases such as cardiovascular disease, cancer, and neurodegenerative disorders.

Its ability to reduce oxidative stress and improve blood lipid profiles also makes it a valuable natural ingredient in the prevention of cardiovascular disease.

8. Magnesium

Sesamum indicum magnesium is a naturally occurring mineral found in sesame seeds and oil.

It plays a crucial role in maintaining proper muscle and nerve function, bone health, and heart rhythm. Magnesium in sesame has been linked to several benefits, including reduced blood pressure, improved sleep quality, and alleviated symptoms of anxiety and depression.

It is also involved in energy production and can help regulate body temperature.

9. Potassium

Sesamum indicum potassium is an essential mineral that plays a crucial role in various physiological processes.

It helps maintain healthy blood pressure, promotes bone health, and supports muscle function.

Sesame seeds are a rich source of potassium, making them a nutritious addition to salads, baked goods, and other recipes.

10. Fatty acids

Sesamum indicum fatty acids is a type of polyunsaturated fat found in sesame seeds and oil.

It contains high amounts of linoleic acid (LA) and oleic acid (OA), with LA being the primary component. Sesame fatty acids have been shown to exhibit numerous health benefits, including reducing inflammation, improving cardiovascular health, and having antioxidant properties.

They are also rich in essential omega-6 fatty acids, making them a valuable addition to a healthy diet.
