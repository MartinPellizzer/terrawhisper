Paris polyphylla

What's the taxonomical classification of Paris polyphylla?

Paris polyphylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Liliales and is a member of the family Melanthiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Paris, and its specific species name, polyphylla.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Paris polyphylla has a single stem arising from a thick, fleshy rhizome that produces a whorl of six to ten elliptical, dark green leaves at its apex. These leaves possess distinct parallel venation and lack a petiole, appearing to emerge directly from the terminal point of the stem. At the center of this leaf whorl, a solitary, nodding flower emerges, characterized by four fleshy, dark purple to blackish sepals. The petals are typically white or pale pink and surround a central cluster of numerous stamens and a single pistil. This unique structure creates a highly symmetrical, star-like appearance that is characteristic of the Melanthiaceae family.

What are the pharmacological activities of Paris polyphylla?

These diverse biological functions make Paris polyphylla a subject of intense interest in pharmaceutical research.

As discussed in a study, Paris polyphylla Sm. is a medicinal plant primarily used for cancer treatment in China and India. One of its major saponins is polyphyllin VI (PP-VI), which has been shown to impair the viability of 4T1 and MDA-MB-231 cells. When applied at a sub-cytotoxic dose, PP-VI suppresses the metastatic potential of both 4T1 and MDA-MB-231 cells. Treatment with PP-VI results in increased expression of Rell2 and a decreased level of miR-18a in 4T1 and MDA-MB-231 cells. This targeted regulation of Rell2 by miR-18a is implicated in the anti-metastatic effect of PP-VI in breast cancer cells.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds Polyphylliin D, Paris saponin II, Kaempferol, Diosgenin, and Pennogenin. These specific phytochemicals contribute to the diverse pharmacological profile of the species. For instance, the steroidal saponins like Diosgenin and Pennogenin are often studied for their biological activities. Additionally, the presence of Kaempferol provides antioxidant properties that support cellular health. The combination of these unique constituents makes the plant a subject of significant interest in medicinal research.

As discussed in a study, Paris polyphylla contains the active component Polyphyllin D, which has long been used for the treatment of cancer in Asian countries. This component holds significant antitumor activity in vivo or in vitro. In experiments, U87 human glioma cells were treated with varied concentrations of Polyphyllin D ranging from 10(-8) to 10(-4) M. Treatment with Polyphyllin D for 24 h significantly inhibited the proliferation of U87 human glioma cells in a concentration-dependent manner. The compound increased apoptosis and upregulated the expression of Bax, caspase-3, and p-JNK while downregulating Bcl-2 expression.
