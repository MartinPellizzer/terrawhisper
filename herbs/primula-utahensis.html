What is Primula utahensis?

Primula utahensis is a perennial plant species native to the western United States and Canada.

It belongs to the family Primulaceae and is characterized by its small, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are 2-10 cm long and 1-5 cm wide, with entire or toothed margins. The leaves are basal, often forming a rosette, and are pubescent or glabrous. The plant produces solitary, terminal flowers on a 10-30 cm long peduncle, with 5 petals that are white to pinkish in color and 2-3 cm long.

The flowers are 5-merous, with 5 stamens and a superior ovary containing 5 locules, each containing 1-2 ovules.
