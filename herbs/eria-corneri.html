What's the taxonomical classification of Eria corneri?

Eria corneri belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. As a member of the order Asparagales, it falls under the diverse family Orchidaceae. Specifically, the plant is categorized within the genus Eria, with its unique biological identity defined by its species name, Eria corneri.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eria corneri has an epiphytic growth habit characterized by thick, fleshy, multi-layered pseudobulbs that are typically cylindrical or slightly compressed in shape. These pseudobulbs serve as primary water storage organs and are often covered in a dense layer of protective, brownish-white velamen. From the apex of each pseudobulb, a single, coriaceous leaf emerges with an elongated, lanceolate shape and prominent parallel venation. The inflorescence is a terminal raceme that bears small, waxy flowers often featuring subtle longitudinal striations on the tepals. These floral structures are typically pale in color, ranging from creamy white to light yellowish tones.
