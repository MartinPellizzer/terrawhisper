1. Causes stomach upset

Beta vulgaris causes stomach upset in some individuals due to its high content of raffinose, a complex sugar that can be difficult for the body to digest.

The fiber and pectin present in beets can also irritate the stomach lining, leading to discomfort and indigestion.

Additionally, the natural compounds found in beets, such as isothiocyanates and betalains, may cause inflammation and irritation in the digestive tract, contributing to stomach upset.

2. Induces kidney stones

Beta vulgaris induces kidney stones due to its high oxalate content.

Oxalates are naturally occurring compounds that can increase urinary oxalate levels, potentially leading to an increased risk of developing kidney stones. This is because excess oxalates in the urine can combine with calcium ions and form crystals that eventually harden into stones.

As a result, individuals who consume high amounts of beet products may be more susceptible to developing kidney stones over time.

3. Increases blood pressure

Beta vulgaris increases blood pressure due to its high content of nitrates, which are converted into nitric oxide in the body.

Nitric oxide causes blood vessels to dilate, leading to increased blood flow and elevated blood pressure.

Additionally, the natural sugars present in beets can also contribute to an increase in blood sugar levels, further exacerbating blood pressure elevations.

4. Triggers allergic reactions

Beta vulgaris triggers allergic reactions in some individuals due to its unique protein structure.

The allergenic properties are primarily attributed to the presence of beta-amylase, a enzyme found in the plant's leaves and stems.

When ingested or come into contact with skin, this protein can cause an immune response, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling.

5. Interferes with iron absorption

Beta vulgaris interferes with iron absorption by containing a high amount of oxalates, which can bind to iron in the gut and reduce its availability for absorption.

Additionally, the betalains present in Beta vulgaris have been shown to inhibit the expression of genes involved in iron metabolism, further exacerbating the interference with iron uptake.

This may lead to iron deficiency or impaired iron function in individuals consuming high amounts of beet products regularly.

6. Affects liver function negatively

Beta vulgaris affects liver function negatively due to its high content of betalains and fiber.

The excessive consumption of these compounds can lead to an overload on the liver, causing it to work harder than usual to detoxify and process them.

This may result in impaired liver function, potentially leading to symptoms such as fatigue, nausea, and abdominal discomfort.

7. Worsens respiratory problems

Beta vulgaris worsens respiratory problems by triggering inflammation in the airways, which can exacerbate conditions such as asthma and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).

The high content of oxalates in Beta vulgaris may contribute to this effect, causing the body to release histamine, a chemical that narrows airway passages and increases mucus production.

Additionally, the vegetable's sulfur compounds may also irritate the respiratory system, leading to wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.

8. Elevates risk of bleeding

Beta vulgaris elevates risk of bleeding due to its ability to interfere with blood clotting.

The betalains present in beetroot can inhibit platelet aggregation, leading to reduced platelet function and increased bleeding tendency.

Additionally, the high vitamin K content in beets may enhance the activity of certain blood thinners, further increasing the risk of bleeding.

9. Causes digestive discomfort

Beta vulgaris causes digestive discomfort in some individuals due to its high content of raffinose, a complex sugar that can be difficult for the body to digest.

This can lead to symptoms such as bloating, gas, and stomach cramps, which may occur when the undigested raffinose is fermented by gut bacteria, producing uncomfortable side effects.

Additionally, beet's fiber content can also contribute to digestive issues in some cases.

10. Aggravates existing conditions

Beta vulgaris aggravates existing conditions due to its potential to interact with certain medications and stimulate the immune system.

The betalains present in beets can increase inflammation, exacerbating conditions such as arthritis, skin allergies, and asthma. Additionally, the fiber content of beets can irritate the digestive tract, worsening symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome or inflammatory bowel disease.

These interactions may trigger adverse reactions in individuals with pre-existing conditions.
