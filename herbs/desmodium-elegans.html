What's the taxonomical classification of Desmodium elegans?

Desmodium elegans belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Fabales and is a member of the family Fabaceae. Its specific taxonomic hierarchy concludes within the genus Desmodium, ultimately reaching its unique designation as the species elegans.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Desmodium elegans has trifoliate leaves composed of three distinct leaflets, small yellow pea-like flowers, and distinctive legume pods covered in hooked hairs.

The leaves are typically arranged alternately along the stem and feature fine pubescence on their surfaces. Its inflorescence consists of small, dense clusters that emerge from the leaf axils. The most defining characteristic is the fruit, which is a flattened pod equipped with recurved bristles designed for seed dispersal by clinging to animal fur.

These specialized morphological traits allow the plant to thrive and propagate effectively within its specific ecological niche.
