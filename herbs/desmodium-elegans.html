What's the taxonomical classification of Desmodium elegans?

Desmodium elegans belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Fabales and is a member of the family Fabaceae. Its specific taxonomic hierarchy concludes within the genus Desmodium, ultimately reaching its unique designation as the species elegans.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Desmodium elegans has trifoliate leaves composed of three distinct leaflets, small yellow pea-like flowers, and distinctive legume pods covered in hooked hairs. The leaves are typically arranged alternately along the stem and feature fine pubescence on their surfaces. Its inflorescence consists of small, dense clusters that emerge from the leaf axils. The most defining characteristic is the fruit, which is a flattened pod equipped with recurved bristles designed for seed dispersal by clinging to animal fur. These specialized morphological traits allow the plant to thrive and propagate effectively within its specific ecological niche.

What are the pharmacological activities of Desmodium elegans?

This plant has antibacterial, antifungal, insecticidal, and phytotoxic pharmacological activities. Its chemical constituents allow it to inhibit the growth of various pathogenic bacteria and fungi effectively. The plant also serves as a natural insecticidal agent by disrupting the biological processes of harmful pests. Additionally, its phytotoxic properties can influence the growth and development of other plant species. These diverse biological actions make it a subject of interest for natural product research.

According to a study published by "Asian Pacific journal of tropical biomedicine", the methanolic extract and its DCM, ethyl acetate, and n-butanol fractions exhibited insecticidal effects against Callosobruchus analis and Rhyzopertha dominic. At a tested concentration of 500 µg/mL, the methanolic extract, n-hexane, DCM, ethyl acetate, and n-butanol showed 75, 85, 85, 65, and 5% phytotoxicity respectively. The DCM and ethyl acetate solvent fractions were effective against F. solani with 10% and 20% inhibition respectively. All tested samples of Desmodium elegans were devoid of cytotoxic and antibacterial effects.
