10 Possible Side Effects Of Corydalis Ambigua (Tanzen)

Corydalis ambigua has some side effects when used improperly, such as dizziness, headaches, and nausea.

These side effects can be caused by overdosing or combining with other medications. In severe cases, they can worsen one's life by limiting daily activities, causing emotional distress, and affecting sleep patterns.

For example, dizziness can increase the risk of falls and injuries, while nausea can lead to dehydration and malnutrition.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Corydalis ambigua if used imporperly.

Corydalis ambigua causes dizziness due to its ability to stimulate the central nervous system and increase blood flow to the brain.

The sudden rush of energy and stimulation can cause an imbalance in the body's equilibrium, leading to feelings of lightheadedness and dizziness.

Additionally, Corydalis ambigua contains compounds that can affect the neurotransmitters responsible for balance and coordination, further contributing to this side effect.

Corydalis ambigua increases sweating due to its ability to stimulate the body's thermoregulatory system.

This is caused by the presence of corydaline, a natural alkaloid that activates the body's sweat glands, leading to an increase in perspiration.

Additionally, Corydalis ambigua has been found to have anti-inflammatory properties, which may also contribute to its sweating-inducing effects as it helps to dilate blood vessels and increase blood flow to the skin.

3. Requires hospitalization

Corydalis ambigua induces nausea in some individuals due to its active compounds, including dehydrocorydaline and protopine.

These alkaloids can stimulate the vomiting center in the brain, leading to feelings of queasiness and increased salivation.

Additionally, Corydalis ambigua's ability to alter gut motility may also contribute to nausea, as changes in digestion and absorption can cause discomfort and unease.

Corydalis ambigua produces vomiting due to its ability to stimulate the digestive system and cause increased contractions in the stomach and intestines.

The plant's chemical compounds, such as dehydrocorydaline and corydaline, are thought to be responsible for this effect, as they can irritate the mucous membranes in the gut and lead to nausea and vomiting.
