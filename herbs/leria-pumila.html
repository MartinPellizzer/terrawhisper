Leria pumila

Leria pumila, commonly known as the dwarf pomegranate, is a type of shrub native to tropical Asia.

This plant has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, including its ability to aid digestion, reduce inflammation, and exhibit antioxidant properties.

The therapeutic actions of Leria pumila include its anti-diarrheal, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial effects, which are attributed to its bioactive constituents such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids.

Furthermore, the bioactive constituents of Leria pumila, including ellagic acid and punicalagins, have been shown to possess anti-cancer and anti-aging properties, making it a valuable plant in traditional medicine.
