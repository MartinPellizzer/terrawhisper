Caulophyllum Cohosh (Caulophyllum thalictroides)

Caulophyllum Cohosh (Caulophyllum thalictroides) is a member of the Berberidaceae family, native to Eastern North America, United States, and Eastern United States. Traditionally, its roots, rhizomes, and stems have been used for decoctions, infusions, and tinctures.

This herb is particularly valued for its sedative, tonic, and antispasmodic actions, and has a long history of use in native american herbal medicine, european herbal medicine, and traditional chinese medicine.

Flowers
Inflorescences are racemose with white to pale pink flowers, actinomorphic, having five sepals and five petals, with a prominent yellow stamen cluster and a superior ovary.
Stems
Woody, ascending growth habit with opposite branching, smooth, glabrous surface, and presence of lateral stipular scars.
