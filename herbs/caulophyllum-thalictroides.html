What to know about Caulophyllum thalictroides (blue cohosh) before using it medicinally

Blue cohosh, scientifically known as Caulophyllum thalictroides, is a plant-based remedy that has garnered attention for its impressive health benefits, which include relieving menstrual cramps and symptoms of menopause, as well as showcasing antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties.

While blue cohosh is often used in traditional medicine to treat various ailments, it also finds culinary use as an ingredient in soups, stews, and teas. In the horticultural realm, blue cohosh is prized for its attractive foliage and delicate white flowers. From a botanical standpoint, the herb belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its rhizome roots and leafy stems.

Historically, blue cohosh has been revered by indigenous cultures in North America for centuries, with early references dating back to the 17th century when it was used as a treatment for various health issues.
