Testulea gabonensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Testulea gabonensis?

Testulea gabonensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Malpighiales and is a member of the family Ochnaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its genus, Testulea, and its specific species name, gabonenis.

What are the herbal preparations of Testulea gabonensis?

Testulea gabonenensis is prepared as a decoction made by boiling the leaves in water for medicinal use. This traditional method allows the active compounds within the foliage to be extracted into a liquid form. Local practitioners often consume this watery infusion to address specific health concerns. Some variations of the preparation might involve using different parts of the plant depending on the ailment. The resulting liquid serves as the primary delivery method for the plant's properties in Gabonese folk medicine.
