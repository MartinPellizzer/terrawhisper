Testulea gabonensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Testulea gabonensis?

Testulea gabonensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Malpighiales and is a member of the family Ochnaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its genus, Testulea, and its specific species name, gabonenis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Testulea gabonensis has an emergent canopy structure characterized by a straight, cylindrical trunk that can reach heights of up to 50 meters and features smooth, greyish bark.

The leaves are large, pinnately compound, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the branches. Each leaf consists of multiple pairs of leaflets that are oblong or elliptical in shape with entire margins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into woody, capsule-like fruits.

This unique morphology allows the tree to dominate the upper layers of the tropical rainforest canopy.
