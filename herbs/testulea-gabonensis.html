Testulea gabonensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Testulea gabonensis has an emergent canopy structure characterized by a straight, cylindrical trunk that can reach heights of up to 50 meters and features smooth, greyish bark. The leaves are large, pinnately compound, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the branches. Each leaf consists of multiple pairs of leaflets that are oblong or elliptical in shape with entire margins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into woody, capsule-like fruits. This unique morphology allows the tree to dominate the upper layers of the tropical rainforest canopy.
