What to know about Plantago ovata before using it medicinally

Plantago ovata, commonly known as ispaghula, is a medicinal herb used to treat various gastrointestinal disorders, including constipation, irritable bowel syndrome (IBS), and diarrhea.

The herb has several health benefits, including its ability to promote regular bowel movements, reduce symptoms of IBS, and alleviate symptoms of inflammatory bowel disease. Plantago ovata's therapeutic properties include its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects, which contribute to its medicinal uses. The herb contains bioactive compounds such as mucilages, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its health benefits.

People commonly prepare ispaghula as a dietary fiber supplement, often in the form of husk or seeds, which is used to promote digestive health and alleviate symptoms of gastrointestinal disorders.
