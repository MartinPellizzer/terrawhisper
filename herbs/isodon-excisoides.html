Isodon excisoides

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Isodon excisoides has opposite, decussate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape with serrated margins and prominent pinnate venation. The plant exhibits a woody structure characterized by slender, somewhat irregular branching patterns. Its leaves are arranged in pairs at right angles to the pair above or below, a classic trait of the Verbenaceae family. The bark on mature stems often develops a slightly textured or roughened appearance. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils, contributing to its subtle overall morphology.
