What's the taxonomical classification of Justicia ventricosa?

Justicia ventricosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Lamiales and falls within the family Acanthaceae. Ultimately, its specific biological identity is defined by its placement in the genus Justicia, with its unique designation being the species ventricosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Justicia ventricosa has an erect, herbaceous growth habit characterized by opposite, lanceolate leaves with entire margins and a distinctively swollen, ventricose stem base. The foliage typically displays a vibrant green color and a smooth texture across the leaf surface. Its inflorescence consists of dense, terminal spikes that bear small, tubular flowers often colored in shades of white or pale lavender. The reproductive structures feature a two-lipped corolla typical of the Acanthaceae family, facilitating specific pollination mechanisms. These morphological traits collectively distinguish the species within its native tropical habitat.
