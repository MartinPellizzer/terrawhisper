What's the taxonomical classification of Astragalus bhotanensis?

Astragalus bhotanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent through the order Fabales, it is placed in the family Fabaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Astragalus, and is uniquely distinguished by its species name, bhotanensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Astragalus bhotanensis has pinnately compound leaves consisting of several small, elliptical leaflets and densely pubescent stems that grow in subalpine habitats. The foliage is characterized by a soft, hairy texture that helps protect the plant from intense high-altitude sunlight. Its inflorescence typically features small, pea-like flowers that emerge from the leaf axils in clustered arrangements. These floral structures often display hues of purple or pale lilac, which are characteristic of many species within the Fabaceae family. The root system is robust and well-developed to anchor the plant in rocky, alpine soil.
