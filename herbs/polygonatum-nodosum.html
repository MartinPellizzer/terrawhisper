Polygonatum nodosum

What's the taxonomical classification of Polygonatum nodosum?

Polygonatum nodosum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Asparagales. Within this order, it is a member of the family Asparagaceae. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Polygonatum and the species nodosum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polygonatum nodosum has rhizomes that are thick and fleshy, supporting stems that grow from a basal rosette of lanceolate, parallel-veined leaves. These stems are typically arching or pendulous and can reach heights of up to 50 centimeters. The leaves are arranged alternately along the stem and possess an acute apex. Small, bell-shaped white flowers emerge from the leaf axils in a drooping manner during the late spring. The plant eventually produces small, fleshy red berries that hang from the stems after pollination.
