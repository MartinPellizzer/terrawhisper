Celtis tetrandra

What's the taxonomical classification of Celtis tetrandra?

Celtis tetrandra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Rosales and the family Cannabaceae, eventually leading to its specific designation within the genus Celtis. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its unique species name, tetrandra.

What are the therapeutic actions of Celtis tetrandra?

Celtis tetrandra has medicinal properties used primarily to treat skin ailments, respiratory issues, and digestive discomfort. What are the herbal preparations of Celtis tetrandra?

Celtis tetrandra is prepared as decoctions from the bark, infusions from the leaves, and topical poultices from the crushed plant material. These liquid preparations are commonly used in traditional medicine to treat internal ailments through oral consumption. The leaves can also be boiled to create medicinal teas that address specific inflammatory conditions. For external applications, the plant matter is applied directly to the skin to soothe localized irritation or wounds. These diverse methods allow different parts of the plant to target both systemic and superficial health issues.
