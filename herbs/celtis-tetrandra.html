Celtis tetrandra

What's the taxonomical classification of Celtis tetrandra?

Celtis tetrandra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Rosales and the family Cannabaceae, eventually leading to its specific designation within the genus Celtis. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its unique species name, tetrandra.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Celtis tetrandra contains bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, and tannins that contribute to its therapeutic properties. These chemical constituents provide the plant with significant antioxidant and antimicrobial activities. Researchers have noted that extracts from the bark and leaves often exhibit anti-inflammatory effects in various biological models. Some traditional medicinal practices utilize these compounds to treat skin ailments and gastrointestinal issues. Further pharmacological studies continue to investigate the specific molecular mechanisms of these diverse chemical profiles.

What are the therapeutic actions of Celtis tetrandra?

Celtis tetrandra has medicinal properties used primarily to treat skin ailments, respiratory issues, and digestive discomfort. The bark and leaves are often prepared as decoctions to soothe inflammation and reduce swelling in the body. Some traditional practices utilize the plant to alleviate coughs and manage symptoms of bronchitis. Additionally, topical applications of the plant extract are believed to assist in healing various skin irritations and wounds. These therapeutic uses highlight the plant's importance in folk medicine for managing localized and internal ailments.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, stomach aches, diarrhea, and various types of inflammation. The bark is often decocted into a liquid that serves as a medicinal wash for topical applications. When ingested, the prepared extracts help soothe gastrointestinal distress and regulate bowel movements. Local healers also utilize the plant to address minor wounds and skin irritations through traditional poultices. These therapeutic properties make it a valuable resource in regional ethnobotanical practices.
