Alangium salviifolium

What's the taxonomical classification of Alangium salviifolium?

Aliangium salviifolium belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Cornales. This plant is further categorized into the family Cornaceae, placing it within the genus Aliangium, and is specifically identified by its species name, salviifolium.

Small, tubular flowers emerge from the axillary clusters, often displaying subtle variations in color. The root system is typically fibrous and well-adapted to the specific soil conditions of its natural habitat. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains kaempferol, kaempferol 3-O-beta-d-glucopyranoside, salicin, salviifosides A-C, and ipecac alkaloids. These diverse bioactive constituents contribute to the complex pharmacological profile of the species. The presence of flavones like kaempferol and its glycosylated form often provides significant antioxidant properties. Salicin is a well-known precursor to salicylic acid, which is associated with analgesic and anti-inflammatory effects. Furthermore, the specialized salviifosides A-C and the potent ipecac alkaloids further define its unique chemical makeup.
