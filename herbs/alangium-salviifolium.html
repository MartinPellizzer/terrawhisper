Alangium salviifolium

What's the taxonomical classification of Alangium salviifolium?

Aliangium salviifolium belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Cornales. This plant is further categorized into the family Cornaceae, placing it within the genus Aliangium, and is specifically identified by its species name, salviifolium.

Small, tubular flowers emerge from the axillary clusters, often displaying subtle variations in color. What are the pharmacological activities of Alangium salviifolium?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological activities including anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antidiabetic properties. Its anti-inflammatory effects help reduce swelling and pain by modulating various biochemical pathways in the body. The antibacterial components of the plant effectively inhibit the growth of several pathogenic microorganisms. Additionally, its antidiabetic potential is evidenced by its ability to help regulate blood glucose levels. These diverse medicinal qualities make the species a subject of interest for various therapeutic applications.

According to a study published by "Bioorganic & medicinal chemistry letters", the leaves of Alangium salviifolium (L.f.) Wangerin contain three new phenolic glycosides identified as salviifosides A-C (13). These leaves also yield three known compounds, which are salicin (4), kaempferol (5), and kaempferol 3-O-beta-d-glucopyranoside (6). The anti-inflammatory activities of these new compounds (1-3) were investigated using the lipopolysaccharide (LPS)-induced murine macrophage cells line, RAW 264.7. Specifically, salviifoside B (2) shows the potential to inhibit the production of nitric oxide (NO), prostaglandin E(2) (PGE(2)), and tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-alpha).
