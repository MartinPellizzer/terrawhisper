Alangium salviifolium

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Alangium salviifolium has lanceolate, grayish-green leaves with serrated margins and a textured, rugose surface resembling sage. The plant exhibits a perennial growth habit with woody stems that provide structural support for its dense foliage. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the axillary clusters, often displaying subtle variations in color. The root system is typically fibrous and well-adapted to the specific soil conditions of its natural habitat. These morphological traits collectively distinguish the species within its botanical family.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of the eastern Himalayas, spanning across parts of northern India, Nepal, Bhutan, and southern Tibet. It typically thrives in high-altitude environments ranging from 2,500 to 4,000 meters above sea level. These populations are often found clinging to rocky slopes and moist alpine meadows where drainage is efficient. The specific distribution is influenced by the cool, temperate climate and seasonal monsoon patterns characteristic of these highland zones. Localized variations in its presence depend heavily on the availability of rocky substrates and specific soil compositions.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Alangium salviifolium contains high concentrations of terpenoids, specifically cineole and camphor, which provide its primary therapeutic properties. These volatile oils function as potent antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory agents when applied topically or ingested in controlled amounts. The presence of flavonoids further enhances the plant's ability to combat oxidative stress within human cells. Additionally, certain phenolic compounds contribute to its traditional use as a digestive aid and respiratory soothing agent. What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Alangium salviifolium targets various enzymes and receptors including cyclooxygenase-2, lipoxygenase, and specific neurotransmitter receptors to exert its medicinal effects. Its bioactive compounds primarily inhibit cyclooxygenase-2 to reduce the production of pro-inflammatory prostaglandins. Additionally, these phytochemicals interact with nitric oxide synthase to modulate oxidative stress and inflammatory responses within cells. Some constituents also exhibit affinity for GABAergic systems, which contributes to the plant's observed sedative and anxiolytic properties. Through these diverse protein interactions, the plant achieves comprehensive anti-inflammatory and neuroprotective actions.

Alangium salviifolium has therapeutic properties that include being bitter, astringent, antiseptic, carminative, stomachic, diaporetic, and antispasmodic.
