Plumeria rubra

What's the taxonomical classification of Plumeria rubra?

Plumeria rubra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is classified in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Gentianales and the family Apocynaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Plumeria, and its unique species designation, rubra.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Plumeria rubra contains various bioactive compounds including alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, tannins, and triterpenoids that provide significant therapeutic potential. These chemical constituents are responsible for the plant's observed antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties. Research indicates that certain alkaloids within the species may possess analgesic effects, helping to mitigate pain. Additionally, the presence of flavonoids supports cellular protection against oxidative stress. These medicinal components are frequently studied for their ability to inhibit the growth of specific pathogens in laboratory settings.

What are the therapeutic actions of Plumeria rubra?

Plumeria rubra has antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and analgesic properties that are utilized in traditional medicine to treat skin infections, wounds, and pain. The plant contains bioactive compounds such as terpenoids and flavonoids that help reduce swelling and soothe localized inflammation. Extracts from the bark and flowers are often applied topically to accelerate the healing process of various dermatological conditions. Some herbal practices also use preparations of the plant to alleviate respiratory issues and stomach ailments. These therapeutic actions make it a significant subject in pharmacological studies regarding natural healing agents.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, inflammatory conditions, respiratory ailments, and various types of intestinal parasites. The latex derived from the stems possesses antimicrobial and antifungal properties that help manage topical skin issues. In some traditional practices, preparations of the leaves are used to alleviate symptoms of coughs and asthma. Additionally, certain extracts are believed to have anthelmintic effects that assist in expelling worms from the digestive tract. These medicinal uses are rooted in long-standing ethnobotanical traditions across tropical regions.
