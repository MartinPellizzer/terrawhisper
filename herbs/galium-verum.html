What to know about Galium verum (lady's bedstraw) before using it medicinally

Lady's bedstraw, scientifically known as Galium verum, is a herb that boasts an impressive array of health benefits, from soothing digestive issues to calming anxiety and stress.

In addition to its medicinal properties, lady's bedstraw is also prized for its versatility in the kitchen, where it can be used to add flavor and texture to soups, salads, and baked goods. As a low-maintenance crop, lady's bedstraw is also a favorite among gardeners, who appreciate its ability to thrive in poor soil and withstand drought conditions. Botanically speaking, lady's bedstraw is a member of the Rubiaceae family, characterized by its slender stems and small, star-shaped flowers.

Throughout history, lady's bedstraw has been referenced in traditional folk remedies and even featured as a symbol of love and loyalty in ancient cultures, making it a herb with both practical and romantic significance.
