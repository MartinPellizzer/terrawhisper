Galium verum

What's the taxonomical classification of Galium verum?

Galium verum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Gentianales. The plant is a member of the family Rubiaceae and is specifically categorized under the genus Galium, with its scientific species designation being verum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Galium verum has upright, branching stems that reach up to 50 centimeters in height and are covered in whorls of narrow, linear leaves arranged in sets of six to eight. The foliage consists of small, lanceolate leaves that are often slightly hairy along the margins. Its inflorescence features small, four-petaled, bright yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, terminal cymes. The plant typically develops a creeping rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread through meadow habitats. Small, cylindrical, schizocarpic fruits follow the flowering stage, characterized by tiny hooked hairs that aid in seed dispersal.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to a broad range spanning across Europe, much of temperate Asia, and parts of North Africa. It can be found in diverse habitats including meadows, pastures, and dry grasslands. Its distribution follows a pattern common to many temperate species, stretching from the British Isles across to Siberia. In North America, it has been introduced and is now naturalized in several regions. The plant thrives in sunny locations with well-drained soil across these various continents.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds (+)-catechin, (-)-epicatechin, flavonoids, phenolic acids, and tannins. These bioactive molecules work together to provide significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory benefits to the human body. The specific flavan-3-ols, (+)-catechin and (-)-epicatechin, are particularly noted for their role in cardiovascular health. Additionally, the presence of various flavonoids and phenolic acids helps protect cells from oxidative stress. The tannins within the plant also contribute to its astringent properties and ability to bind with proteins.

As discussed in a study, ethanolic extracts of Galium verum were prepared using concentrations of 30, 50, and 70% (w/v). The research utilized a method to identify and quantitatively determine 13 individual phenolics, including chlorogenic acid, caffeic acid, coumaric acid, ferulic acid, (+)-catechin, (-)-epicatechin, rutin, quercitriin, isoquercitrin, fisetin, isorhamnetin, hesperidin, and chrysin. Calibration curves for these compounds yielded correlation coefficients (r2) between 0.9971 and 0.9996. The limit of detection for the method ranged from 0.070 to 0.280 µg/mL, while the limit of quantification was from 0.233 to 0.932 µg/mL. Validation showed intra- and interday relative standard deviation values within 0.086 and 2.821%, with recovery values varying from 95.84% for coumaric acid to 103.20% for rutin.

What are the herbal preparations of this plant?

This plant can be processed into an infusion, tincture, essential oil, alcoholic extract, fluid extract, or powder for various medicinal applications. An infusion is often prepared by steeping the dried flowering tops in hot water to create a mild tea. For more concentrated potency, herbalists create a tincture or an alcoholic extract using high-proof spirits to pull out bioactive compounds. The essential oil is obtained through steam distillation of the blossoms to capture its aromatic properties. Additionally, the plant material can be finely ground into a powder for easy inclusion in capsules or topical applications. Finally, a fluid extract offers a highly standardized and potent liquid form of the herb for precise dosing.
