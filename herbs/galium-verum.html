Galium verum

What's the taxonomical classification of Galium verum?

Galium verum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Gentianales. The plant is a member of the family Rubiaceae and is specifically categorized under the genus Galium, with its scientific species designation being verum.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Galium verum contains a complex mixture of triterpenoid saponins, iridoid glycosides, coumarins, and various essential oils like valeranone. These chemical constituents provide the plant with significant bioactive properties, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. The presence of specific coumarins allows the plant to interact with biological systems in ways that may support digestive health. Some studies also suggest that the sesquiterpenes found within the plant contribute to its sedative and calming qualities. These diverse compounds make the species a subject of interest for traditional herbal medicine applications.

What are the therapeutic actions of Galium verum?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat digestive disorders, urinary tract infections, inflammatory conditions, and certain skin ailments. It contains compounds that help soothe the gastrointestinal tract and support renal function. Practitioners often utilize its diuretic properties to help flush the kidneys and bladder. Additionally, its anti-inflammatory effects make it useful for reducing swelling and managing minor skin irritations. Its traditional medicinal applications vary significantly depending on the specific preparation method used.
