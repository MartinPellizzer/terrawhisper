What is Canthium oblongum?

Canthium oblongum is a species of plant in the family Rubiaceae.

It is a shrub or small tree that grows up to 6 meters tall, with a rounded crown and a smooth, grey bark. The leaves are opposite, elliptical, and 6-12 centimeters long, with a rounded tip and a wavy margin, and are borne on 2-4 centimeter long petioles. The inflorescence is a corymb, bearing small, white flowers with a yellow center, and is 10-15 centimeters long.

The fruit is a red or purple berry, 1-2 centimeters long, containing several seeds.

The antipyretic property of Canthium oblongum is also significant, as it helps to reduce fever and alleviate symptoms associated with infections and diseases.

The main herbal preparations of Canthium oblongum are infusion, tincture, decoction, tea, and salve, which are utilized due to its medicinal properties including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial activities.
