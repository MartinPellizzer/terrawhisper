    As discussed in a study, the whole plant of Carpesium lipskyi contains 10 new sesquiterpene lactones known as carlipines A-J (1-10) and 12 known analogues (11-22). All of these isolated compounds were identified as germacranolides with diverse substructural features. Specifically, compounds 1-4 are 2,5-hemiacetal-linked germacranolides, while compounds 5 and 6 possess a 1,2-epoxy moiety. Compounds 7 and 8 represent unusual 1,5-hemiacetal-linked germacranolides, and compounds 9 and 10 contain a tetrahydrofuran unit bridging C-1 and C-8. Furthermore, compounds 6, 7, 8, 19, 20, 21, and 22 showed cytotoxicity against HL-60 and A-549 cell lines. These specific compounds demonstrated IC50 values ranging from 2.8 to 10.3 μM.
