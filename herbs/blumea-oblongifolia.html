Blumea oblongifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Blumea oblongifolia?

Blumea oblongifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the biological hierarchy, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Asterales, this plant is a member of the family Asteraceae. Its taxonomic identity is further defined by its placement within the genus Blumea, ultimately identifying it as the specific species oblongifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Blumea oblongifolia has an erect, herbaceous habit characterized by lanceolate to oblong leaves that are typically serrated along the margins and covered in fine, spreading hairs. The stems are often woody at the base and exhibit a dense pubescence that gives the plant a slightly grayish appearance. Its inflorescences are composed of many small, discoid flower heads arranged in terminal clusters. These flowers are typically yellowish and are surrounded by involucral bracts that possess distinctively hairy textures. The overall structure reflects its classification within the Asteraceae family through its composite floral arrangement and leaf morphology.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, including countries such as Thailand, Vietnam, Malaysia, Indonesia, and parts of the Philippines. It thrives primarily in low-lying areas characterized by humid climates and consistent warmth. The species is commonly found growing in disturbed habitats, such as forest edges, roadsides, and open grasslands. Its distribution is often closely linked to moist soil conditions found in monsoon-influenced environments. Because of these specific requirements, it remains concentrated within the equatorial belt of the Asian continent.

What are the therapeutic actions of Blumea oblongifolia?

Blumea oblongifolia has therapeutic actions including diuretic, emmenagogue, antipyretic, astringent, carminative, and expectorant properties. This medicinal plant is frequently utilized to promote urination and regulate menstrual cycles. Its antipyretic qualities help in reducing fever, while its astringent nature can assist in tightening tissues. Additionally, it serves as a carminative to relieve digestive gas and an expectorant to help clear mucus from the respiratory tract. These diverse functions make it a versatile component in traditional herbal medicine.
