Clerodendrum hainanense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clerodendrum hainanense has opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with acute tips and serrated margins, woody stems, and small, tubular white flowers arranged in dense terminal cymes. The foliage is characterized by prominent pinnate venation and a slightly textured surface that distinguishes it from closely related species. Its flowering structure features long, protruding stamens that emerge from the corolla to facilitate pollination. The plant eventually produces small, fleshy drupes that serve as the primary method for seed dispersal. These morphological traits collectively define its growth habit within its specific tropical habitat.
