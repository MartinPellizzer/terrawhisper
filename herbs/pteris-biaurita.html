Pteris biaurita

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pteris biaurita has pinnate fronds characterized by multiple pairs of lanceolate pinnae that feature distinctive auricles or ear-like lobes at the base of each segment. The texture of the foliage is typically thin and membranous, often exhibiting a slightly leathery feel depending on the age of the plant. The margins of the leaflets are finely serrated or lobed, contributing to a complex and delicate outline. Small, dark scales often cover the stipe and rachis, providing a textured surface along the central stem. These morphological traits combined with the unique basal lobes allow for easy identification within its forest habitat.
