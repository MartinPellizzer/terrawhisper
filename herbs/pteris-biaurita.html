What's the taxonomical classification of Pteris biaurita?

Pteris biaurita belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae, falling under the order Polypodiales. Within this group, the plant is a member of the family Pteridaceae and is placed in the genus Pteris, specifically identified as the species biaurita.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pteris biaurita has pinnate fronds characterized by multiple pairs of lanceolate pinnae that feature distinctive auricles or ear-like lobes at the base of each segment. The texture of the foliage is typically thin and membranous, often exhibiting a slightly leathery feel depending on the age of the plant. The margins of the leaflets are finely serrated or lobed, contributing to a complex and delicate outline. Small, dark scales often cover the stipe and rachis, providing a textured surface along the central stem.
