Coptis Teeta Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Coptis teeta, commonly known as Himalayan barberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayas, valued for its medicinal properties.

It is a yellow-rooted plant that contains various bioactive compounds such as berberine, berberin, and oxyberberine, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

The herb is known for its antibacterial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties, making it beneficial for digestive health and immune support.

It can be prepared as an infusion, tincture, or powder, and is often used in traditional medicine for its wide range of health benefits.

Medicinal Parts

Coptis teeta root, also known as the "yellow poppy," is a key medicinal part of the plant and is widely used in traditional medicine systems such as Ayurveda and Tibetan medicine.

The root contains high concentrations of alkaloids, particularly berberine, which is known for its potent antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antidiabetic properties. In Ayurvedic medicine, it is used to treat digestive disorders, skin conditions, and infections due to its strong antibacterial and antifungal effects. The root is often prepared as a powder, decoction, or extract to maximize its therapeutic benefits.

In modern pharmacology, Coptis teeta root is being studied for its potential in treating metabolic disorders, cardiovascular diseases, and even cancer, making it a valuable resource in both traditional and contemporary medicine.

Additionally, Coptis teeta has been associated with mouth ulcers, which are painful sores that develop on the lips or inside the mouth, possibly due to its irritant properties. Furthermore, the herb may cause fatigue, loss of appetite, and even insomnia, as it can affect the nervous system and disrupt sleep patterns.
