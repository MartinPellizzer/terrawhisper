Coptis teeta

What's the taxonomical classification of Coptis teeta?

Coptis teeta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Ranunculales. This plant is a specific representative of the family Ranunculaceae, situated within the genus Coptis, and is identified by its unique species name, teeta.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Coptis teeta contains high concentrations of isoquinoline alkaloids, specifically berberine, jatrorrhizine, and palmatine, which serve as its primary bioactive agents. These specific chemical compounds are known for their potent antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Researchers have identified that these alkaloids can effectively inhibit the growth of various bacterial strains. Additionally, the plant's phytochemical profile suggests potential therapeutic applications for managing metabolic disorders and gastrointestinal issues. The synergy between these various alkaloids enhances the overall pharmacological efficacy of the plant.

What are the therapeutic actions of Coptis teeta?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat gastrointestinal disorders, skin infections, and various inflammatory conditions. Its medicinal properties are primarily derived from bioactive alkaloids that possess strong antimicrobial and antibacterial effects. Practitioners often utilize it to manage symptoms of dysentery and other digestive ailments caused by pathogens. Additionally, it serves as a topical treatment for dermatological issues such as eczema or fungal infections. These therapeutic applications are rooted in the plant's ability to reduce inflammation and inhibit bacterial growth.
