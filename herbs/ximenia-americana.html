Ximenia americana

What's the taxonomical classification of Ximenia americana?

Ximenia americana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Santalales and the family Olacaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Ximeniia, and its species designation, americana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ximenia americana has small, opposite, simple leaves that are typically elliptic to obovate in shape and possess entire margins. The foliage is often characterized by a leathery texture and a somewhat revolute margin. Its woody stems are sturdy and can form dense, shrubby thickets or small trees. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in terminal clusters. These flowers eventually develop into small, fleshy, globose fruits that are used for dispersal.

What are the pharmacological activities of Ximenia americana?

This plant has anti-diarrhoeal, antisecretory, antiulcerogenic, gastroprotective, and cicatrizant pharmacological activities. These properties allow the plant to effectively manage gastrointestinal disorders by reducing excess fluid secretion and preventing intestinal distress. Its antiulcerogenic and gastroprotective actions work together to safeguard the stomach lining from damage and ulcer formation. Additionally, the plant possesses cicatrizant qualities that assist in the healing of wounded tissues. This combination of effects makes it a significant subject for medicinal research regarding digestive health.

As discussed in a study, Ximenia americana is one of the 15 medicinal plants predominantly disclosed by the majority of participants as being effective in healing diarrhoeal diseases among under-five children. This plant was identified from a total of 54 medicinal plants reported by traditional healers in the Korogwe and Handeni districts of North-eastern Tanzania. The research involved 52 in-depth interviews with participants to determine which plants were perceived to be efficacious for treating paediatric ailments. These findings suggest that Ximenia americana is among the plants used by healers to treat under-five children with diarrhoeal diseases on multiple occasions. Further botanical research is needed to determine if this plant possesses anti-diarrhoeal properties.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds caffeic acid and quersetrin, which provide significant therapeutic benefits. Caffeic acid is a potent antioxidant known for its ability to reduce inflammation and protect cells from oxidative stress. Quersetrin acts as a flavonoid that supports cardiovascular health and helps regulate immune responses. Together, these bioactive molecules contribute to the plant's overall pharmacological profile. Researchers continue to study these specific substances to understand their potential in treating chronic diseases.

As discussed in a study, the chemical profile of the hydroethanolic extract of the Ximeniia americana stem bark shows different classes of compounds with a significant presence of quercetrin and caffeic acid. The EHXA demonstrated activity at the concentration of 1000 μg/mL against L. infantum and L. brasiliensis promastiogotes, causing mortality percentages of 40.66% and 27.62%, respectively. This extract presented a significant toxicity only at the concentration of 1000 μg/mL, which caused a mortality of 55.42% of fibroblasts. Regarding antibacterial activity, the EHXA demonstrated a MIC value ≥1024 μg/mL against all tested bacteria. Additionally, the extract showed a synergistic effect against S. aureus strains when associated with norfloxacin.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating various ailments including ciguatera fish poisoning, schistosomiasis, diarrhea, infectious diseases, and inflammation. Traditional practitioners utilize its medicinal properties to combat parasitic infections like schistosomiasis and foodborne toxins. It serves as a natural remedy to soothe gastrointestinal issues such as diarrhea. The plant also possesses antimicrobial qualities effective against various infectious diseases. Additionally, its properties help to reduce inflammation throughout the body.

As discussed in a study, Ximenia americana belongs to the family Olacaceae and is used as a traditional remedy in the treatment of CFP. This plant was one of 28 selected plant extracts that underwent bioassay-guided screening to study NO inhibiting capacity. The researchers prepared aqueous extracts and screened their activity in lipopolysaccharide-activated RAW 264.7 macrophages. Ximenia americana showed inhibitory activity, validating its use against CFP. This plant is considered a promising candidate for further screening of its active compounds through activity-guided fractionation.
