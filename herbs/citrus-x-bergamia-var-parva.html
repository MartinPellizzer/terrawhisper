Bergamot Orange (Citrus × Bergamia Var. Parva)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Citrus × bergamia var. parva.

Bergamot orange, scientifically known as Citrus × bergamia var. parva, is a citrus fruit native to the Calabria region of southern Italy, prized for its medicinal and aromatic properties. It is commonly regarded as a medicinal herb and adaptogen, known for its ability to support emotional balance and reduce stress. The primary benefits of bergamot orange include its calming effects on the nervous system, its role in improving mood, and its potential to lower cholesterol levels due to its high concentration of bioactive compounds like flavonoids and nerol. Traditionally used in Mediterranean cultures for its aromatic and therapeutic properties, it has been incorporated into herbal teas, perfumes, and even ancient remedies for digestive and respiratory ailments. In modern wellness practices, bergamot orange is widely used in aromatherapy, as a flavoring agent in teas and foods, and as a natural supplement to support mental well-being, thanks to its unique, citrusy aroma and rare active compounds like bergamotene.
