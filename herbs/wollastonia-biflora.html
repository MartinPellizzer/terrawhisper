Wollastonia biflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Wollastonia biflora?

Wollastonia biflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Asterales. It is further categorized into the family Asteraceae, falling under the genus Wollastonia, and is specifically identified by its species name, biflora.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Wollastonia biflora has slender, erect stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and bear small, blue to violet flowers with four petals. The leaves are narrow, linear, and arranged oppositely along the stem in a manner that gives the plant a delicate appearance. Each flower is supported by a pedicel and often features distinctively shaped stamens that emerge from the center of the corolla. The root system is relatively shallow, anchoring the herbaceous plant within moist or aquatic soil environments. These morphological traits distinguish the species from other members of the Polemoniaceae family.

According to a study published by "Journal of natural products", phytochemical investigation of the whole plants of Wollastonia biflora led to the isolation and identification of three new germacrane-type sesquiterpenes (1-3), two new pimarane-type diterpenes (4, 5), and a new naphthalene glycoside (6). Specifically, compounds 1, 2, and 3 showed significant cytotoxic activity against the growth of hepatocellular carcinoma BEL-7402 cells in vitro.
