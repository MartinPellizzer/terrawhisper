1. Causes allergic reactions

Aquilegia micrantha causes allergic reactions due to its high concentration of alkaloids and saponins.

These compounds can trigger an immune response in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling. Additionally, the plant's pollen and sap may contain allergenic proteins that can cause respiratory issues like congestion and coughing.

The exact mechanism behind these allergic reactions is still not fully understood, but it is believed to be linked to the plant's unique chemical makeup.

2. Triggers respiratory problems

Aquilegia micrantha triggers respiratory problems.

The plant contains toxic compounds called ranunculine and protoanemonin, which can cause irritation and inflammation in the respiratory tract when ingested or inhaled.

When these substances are released into the air or come into contact with mucous membranes, they can exacerbate conditions such as asthma and bronchitis, leading to wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.

3. Increases blood pressure

Aquilegia micrantha increases blood pressure due to its natural ability to stimulate the body's cardiovascular system.

The plant's bioactive compounds, such as flavonoids and saponins, can cause blood vessels to constrict, leading to an increase in blood pressure.

This response is thought to be a result of the plant's historical use by traditional medicine practitioners to treat conditions such as hypertension, where it was believed to have a beneficial effect on cardiovascular health.

4. Lowers platelet count

Aquilegia micrantha lowers platelet count due to its potential to interact with blood clotting mechanisms.

The plant's bioactive compounds, including flavonoids and saponins, may interfere with platelet function and aggregation, leading to a decrease in the number of platelets present in the blood.

This effect is thought to be related to the plant's ability to modulate inflammation and oxidative stress pathways.

5. Induces gastrointestinal issues

Aquilegia micrantha induces gastrointestinal issues when ingested.

This is due to the plant's toxic compounds, including saponins and alkaloids, which can cause nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea in individuals who consume it.

The root and seeds of the plant are particularly potent and should be handled with care to avoid accidental ingestion.

6. Causes skin irritation

Aquilegia micrantha causes skin irritation due to the presence of alkaloids and saponins in its leaves and stems.

These compounds can cause an allergic reaction or contact dermatitis when they come into contact with human skin, leading to redness, itching, and swelling.

The severity of the reaction may vary depending on individual sensitivity and the amount of exposure to these allergenic substances.

7. Produces kidney damage

Aquilegia micrantha produces kidney damage due to its high content of toxic compounds called alkaloids.

These substances can accumulate in the body and cause inflammation and scarring of the kidneys, leading to permanent damage over time.

The exact mechanisms by which these alkaloids exert their toxic effects are not fully understood, but it is thought that they may interfere with the normal functioning of kidney cells or cause them to die prematurely.

8. Leads to liver toxicity

Aquilegia micrantha leads to liver toxicity when consumed.

The plant contains glycosides, which are broken down into toxic compounds that can cause damage to the liver cells. The accumulation of these toxins over time can lead to inflammation and scarring of the liver tissue, potentially resulting in long-term liver dysfunction.

This toxic reaction is a direct result of the plant's bioactive compounds interacting with the liver enzymes and tissues.

9. Affects reproductive health

Aquilegia micrantha affects reproductive health by disrupting hormone levels and impacting fertility in both males and females.

The plant contains chemicals that can bind to estrogen receptors, mimicking the effects of estrogen in the body and potentially leading to issues such as irregular menstruation and decreased libido.

Additionally, its flavonoids have been shown to inhibit the activity of certain enzymes involved in testosterone production, which may contribute to reduced sperm count and motility in males.

10. Causes headaches and migraines

Aquilegia micrantha causes headaches and migraines.

This is attributed to the plant's high levels of furanocoumarins, a type of natural compound that can cause sensitivity in some individuals. When ingested or exposed through skin contact, these compounds can trigger a release of serotonin, leading to blood vessel constriction and subsequent migraine episodes.

The potent combination of furanocoumarins and other bioactive compounds in Aquilegia micrantha may exacerbate pre-existing sensitivities, making it a potential migraine trigger for some users.
