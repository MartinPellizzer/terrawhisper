Salvia flava

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Salvia flava has an erect, perennial growth habit characterized by square, woody stems and opposite, oblong-to-lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is typically grayish-green and covered in fine hairs that provide a soft texture to the surface. Its most distinctive feature is the inflorescence, which consists of dense, terminal verticillasters bearing tubular, bright yellow flowers. These blossoms emerge from bracts that are often colored in shades of yellow or light green to complement the petals. The plant typically reaches a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, forming a bushy mound in its natural habitat.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the Mediterranean region, specifically concentrated within the central and eastern parts of Turkey. It is frequently found growing in the rocky, sun-exposed areas of the Taurus Mountains. The species prefers limestone-rich soils and can be found at various elevations across its localized range. Within these habitats, it thrives in open scrublands and steppe environments. Its distribution remains relatively narrow compared to other species in the Salvia genus.

      Salvía flava is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a tincture to utilize its bioactive compounds for therapeutic purposes.
