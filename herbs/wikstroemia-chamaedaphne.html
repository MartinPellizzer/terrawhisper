Wikstroemia chamaedaphne

What's the taxonomical classification of Wikstroemia chamaedaphne?

Wikstroemia chamaedaphne belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Malvales. This plant is a member of the family Thymelaeaceae, specifically categorized within the genus Wikstroemia, and is identified by the specific epithet chamaedaphne.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Wikstroemia chamaedaphne can cause severe poisoning characterized by intense vomiting, abdominal pain, diarrhea, and potentially fatal respiratory failure if ingested. The plant contains toxic compounds known as daphnane-type diterpene esters that act as potent irritants to the digestive system. These toxins can lead to systemic symptoms including dizziness, weakness, and even death in high doses. Contact with the plant may also result in skin irritation or dermatitis for some individuals. Due to these significant risks, all parts of the plant should be handled with extreme caution and kept away from children and pets.
