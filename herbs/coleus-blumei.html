Coleus blumei

What's the taxonomical classification of Coleus blumei?

Coleus blumei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Lamiales. It further falls into the family Lamiaceae, which is a prominent group of flowering plants. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Coleus and the species blumei.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Coleus blumei has ovate to lanceolate leaves characterized by serrated margins and vibrant, variegated patterns of green, burgundy, and yellow. The foliage typically grows in an opposite arrangement along a square-shaped, herbaceous stem. These leaves possess a soft, velvety texture due to a fine layer of microscopic hairs covering the surface. The plant develops upright spikes of small, tubular flowers that emerge from the terminal nodes. These floral structures are generally inconspicuous compared to the highly decorative leaf morphology.
