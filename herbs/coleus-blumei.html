Coleus blumei

What's the taxonomical classification of Coleus blumei?

Coleus blumei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Lamiales. It further falls into the family Lamiaceae, which is a prominent group of flowering plants. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Coleus and the species blumei.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Coleus blumei has ovate to lanceolate leaves characterized by serrated margins and vibrant, variegated patterns of green, burgundy, and yellow. The foliage typically grows in an opposite arrangement along a square-shaped, herbaceous stem. These leaves possess a soft, velvety texture due to a fine layer of microscopic hairs covering the surface. The plant develops upright spikes of small, tubular flowers that emerge from the terminal nodes. These floral structures are generally inconspicuous compared to the highly decorative leaf morphology.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, specifically including countries such as Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. It thrives in humid environments where warm temperatures are consistently maintained throughout the year. Within these regions, it is often found growing in shaded forest understories or near moist riverbanks. While it is widely cultivated as an ornamental species in gardens worldwide, its natural wild populations remain concentrated in these specific Asian territories.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires bright, indirect sunlight and consistently moist, well-draining soil to thrive in garden or container settings.

You should maintain a warm environment with temperatures ideally between 60 and 75 degrees Fahrenheit. Frequent watering is essential to prevent the soil from drying out completely, though you must avoid waterlogged conditions that cause root rot. Regular pinching of the stems encourages bushier growth and prevents the plant from becoming leggy.

Providing regular liquid fertilization during the growing season will also help maintain its vibrant foliage colors.

What are the pharmacological activities of Coleus blumei?

This plant has potent antimicrobial, antioxidant, and anti-inflammatory pharmacological activities that contribute to its therapeutic potential. Research indicates that its extracts can effectively inhibit the growth of various pathogenic bacteria and fungi. These bioactive properties are largely attributed to the presence of phytochemicals such as flavonoids and terpenoids. Furthermore, the plant demonstrates a capacity to reduce oxidative stress within biological systems. Such diverse medicinal actions suggest that Coleus bluemi holds significant promise for future drug development.

According to a study published by "Chemical & pharmaceutical bulletin", the chloroform extract of the air-dried leaves of Coleus blumei afforded a mixture of diastereomers of a new abietane type diterpenes. The structures of these substances were elucidated using extensive one and two dimensional (1D, 2D) NMR and mass spectrometry. After the acetylation of the mixture, a single compound was obtained. Antimicrobial tests conducted on this diterpene indicate that it is active against Bacillus subtilis, Pseudomonas aeruginosa and Candida albicans.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains rosmarinic acid, flavonoids, and various terpenoids that contribute to its therapeutic potential. Rosmarinic acid is a major phenolic compound in Coleus blumei known for its significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These secondary metabolites work together to protect cells from oxidative stress and regulate immune responses. Scientific studies suggest that these chemical constituents may offer benefits for managing inflammatory conditions. The specific concentration of these medicinal compounds can vary depending on the plant's growing environment.

According to a study published by "Phytochemistry", Coleus blumei contains the medicinal compound rosmarinic acid. This compound is an ester of caffeic acid and 3,4-dihydroxyphenyllactic acid. Plant cell cultures from Coleus blumei can accumulate rosmarinic acid in amounts much higher than in the plant itself. These cultures can accumulate the compound up to 36% of the cell dry weight. Such high levels have led to the proposal of biotechnological production of rosmarinic acid using these plant cell cultures.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating various gastric problems including stomach aches, diarrhea, dysentery, and indigestion. It serves as a traditional remedy to soothe the digestive tract and restore balance to the gut. Practitioners often utilize its properties to alleviate the discomfort associated with acute stomach pains. The plant's medicinal qualities are particularly valued for managing loose stools and intestinal infections. By addressing these specific ailments, it helps stabilize the gastrointestinal system.

As discussed in a study, Coleus blumei is identified as one of the most important species for the gastrointestinal category. The research utilized two different quantitative tools to analyze the ethnopharmacological field survey in the municipality of Tlanchinol Hidalgo, Mexico. This analysis included the factor informant consensus and the fidelity level, which is the ratio between the number of informants who independently suggested the use of a species for the same major purpose and the total number of informants who mentioned the plant for any use. While the gastrointestinal category had the greatest agreement, Coleus blumei remains a primary species for that specific purpose.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause skin irritation, redness, and swelling if the sap comes into contact with human skin or sensitive mucous membranes. Some individuals may experience allergic reactions such as itching or a burning sensation upon touching the foliage. If the plant is ingested, it may lead to gastrointestinal distress or more severe toxicity depending on the amount consumed. Because of these potential reactions, it is important to wear gloves when pruning or handling the stems. Always keep this species away from pets and small children to prevent accidental exposure.
