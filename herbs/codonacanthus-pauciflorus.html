Codonacanthus pauciflorus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Codonacanthus pauciflorus has slender, woody stems that support small, lanceolate leaves arranged in an opposite pattern along the branches. The foliage is characterized by a smooth texture and a deep green coloration that provides a dense appearance. Its inflorescence consists of sparse, tubular flowers that emerge from the leaf axils in limited quantities. These delicate blooms typically feature a pale pigmentation and a narrow corolla structure. The overall growth habit is that of a compact, spreading shrub with fine architectural details.

      What are the herbal preparations of Codonacanthus pauciflorus?

      Codonacanthus pauciflorus is prepared as a decoction of the dried roots, a concentrated tincture of the leaves, or a topical poultice made from crushed fresh stems. Practitioners often simmer the woody root sections in water to extract bitter alkaloids for internal consumption. For external skin ailments, the plant material is macerated into a thick paste and applied directly to the affected area. These various methods allow for the targeted delivery of the plant's active compounds depending on the intended therapeutic use.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Codonacanthus pauciflorus can cause severe skin irritation, localized swelling, and acute respiratory distress if the plant's pollen or sap comes into direct contact with a person. Dermatological reactions often manifest as intense itching or redness shortly after exposure to the foliage. Inhaling the microscopic particles from the flowers may trigger coughing fits or allergic asthma symptoms in sensitive individuals. Some documented cases suggest that accidental ingestion could lead to gastrointestinal upset or nausea. It is essential to use protective gloves when handling this species to avoid these adverse physical responses.
