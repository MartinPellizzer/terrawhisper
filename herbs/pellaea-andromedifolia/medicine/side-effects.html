10 Possible Side Effects Of Pellaea Andromedifolia (Maidenhair Fern)

Pellaea andromedifolia has some side effects when used improperly, such as allergic reactions, respiratory issues, and digestive problems.

Causes of these side effects may include using excessive amounts, using the herb without proper preparation, and having pre-existing medical conditions.

For example, a severe allergic reaction can worsen a person's quality of life by causing anaphylaxis, while respiratory issues can lead to shortness of breath and decreased oxygen levels.

The high concentration of these natural compounds can cause allergic reactions, leading to redness, itching, and inflammation on the skin.

This is often a result of direct contact with the plant's leaves or fronds, which release oils that can interact with human skin, triggering an adverse response.
