Clethra fargesii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clethra fargesii has lanceolate to oblong leaves that are 5 to 15 centimeters long, arranged alternately along woody stems, and feature finely serrated margins with a leathery texture. The foliage typically displays a deep green color on the upper surface and a slightly paler underside. During the flowering season, the plant produces numerous small, white, fragrant blossoms that emerge in terminal racemes. These flowers possess four distinct petals and are supported by slender pedicels. The shrub eventually develops woody capsules that contain small, winged seeds for dispersal.
