Caltha scaposa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Caltha scaposa has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by basal leaves that are cordate or reniform in shape and often possess shallowly lobed or entire margins. The leaf blades are typically green and can vary in size depending on the specific environmental conditions of the habitat. Its stems are relatively short and arise from a rhizomatous root system that allows for vegetative spreading. The flowers are actinomorphic with five bright yellow petals that surround a central cluster of numerous stamens. These blooms are supported by pedicels that emerge from the leaf axils during the flowering season.
