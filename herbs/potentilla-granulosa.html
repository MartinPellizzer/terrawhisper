Potentilla granulosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Potentilla granulosa?

Potentiella granulosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Rosales and placed within the family Rosaceae. Finally, it is defined by its genus, Potentiella, and its specific species designation, granulosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Potentiella granulosa has a perennial growth habit characterized by erect to ascending stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 40 centimeters. The leaves are palmately divided into five to seven distinct leaflets, which are often covered in fine, soft hairs. Small, five-petaled yellow flowers emerge from the terminal ends of the stems during the blooming season. These flowers possess a central cluster of numerous stamens and carpels surrounded by a green calyx. The plant's overall structure is adapted to rocky or alpine environments where its compact form provides stability.
