Platanthera japonica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Platanthera japonica has an erect, slender stem reaching heights of 10 to 40 centimeters, topped by a terminal spike of numerous small, white, fragrant flowers. The leaves are lanceolate or narrowly elliptical, arranged alternately along the stem with smaller bracts situated beneath each flower. Each flower features a prominent, elongated nectar spur that extends backward from the base of the lip. The labellum is divided into two lobes, with the larger lobe often displaying delicate veining. This orchid's reproductive structures are characterized by fine, delicate filaments that facilitate pollination by specific insect visitors.
