Common Mallow (Symphytum officinale) Compounds

The primary compounds of Common Mallow include tannins, which have astringent properties, and flavonoids, known for their antioxidant effects. Phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory properties, while glycosides and saponins support its medicinal uses. Coumarin and terpenoids add to its aromatic and potential therapeutic qualities.

Common Mallow (Symphytum officinale) Monograph

1. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Common Mallow (Symphytum officinale). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and astringent properties, making it useful in traditional herbal medicine for soothing wounds and reducing digestive issues. Tannins also help preserve the plant's medicinal qualities during preparation.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Common Mallow (Symphytum officinale) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and healing properties. These compounds support tissue repair and may reduce swelling, making the plant useful in traditional herbal remedies for wounds and inflammatory conditions.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid in Common Mallow (Symphytum officinale) contributes to its anti-inflammatory and wound-healing properties. This compound is commonly found in the plant's leaves and roots, supporting traditional uses in herbal medicine for treating skin irritations and digestive issues.

4. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Common Mallow (Symphytum officinale). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and wound-healing properties. Glycosides in Common Mallow are often used in herbal medicine to support skin health and reduce irritation. They are typically extracted and used in topical formulations.

5. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Common Mallow (Symphytum officinale). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and wound-healing properties. Saponins may also support digestive health by promoting bile secretion. These effects make saponin a valuable component in herbal treatments.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Common Mallow (Symphytum officinale). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. Coumarin is also known for its ability to promote wound healing and soothe muscle spasms. It is commonly used in herbal medicine for its therapeutic effects.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Common Mallow (Symphytum officinale) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and wound-healing properties. These compounds help reduce swelling and support tissue regeneration, making the plant useful in traditional herbal remedies for injuries and skin conditions.

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of Common Mallow (Symphytum officinale) contains bioactive components like terpenes and phenolic compounds. These contribute to its anti-inflammatory and wound-healing properties, making it valuable in herbal medicine for treating skin irritations and promoting tissue repair.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloids in Common Mallow (Symphytum officinale) are a class of nitrogen-containing compounds with potential medicinal properties. These alkaloids may contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating wounds and inflammation. However, they can also be toxic in high doses, requiring careful handling and dosage control.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Common Mallow (Symphytum officinale). It contributes to the plant's vibrant purple color and possesses antioxidant properties that support healing and reduce inflammation. Found in the flowers and leaves, anthocyanin is valued in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
