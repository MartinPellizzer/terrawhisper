Common Mallow (Symphytum officinale) Uses

Common Mallow is used to treat bruises, bone fractures, joint inflammation, muscle pain, and muscle strains. It helps alleviate symptoms of rheumatism and osteoarthritis by reducing inflammation and promoting tissue repair. Its soothing properties make it effective for muscle spasms and joint pain.

Common Mallow (Symphytum officinale) Monograph

1. Bruises

Common Mallow is used topically for bruises due to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. It helps reduce swelling and pain by promoting tissue repair. The plant's mucilage forms a protective layer over the affected area. It is often applied as a poultice or infused oil to support healing.

2. Bone fractures

Common Mallow has been traditionally used to support bone healing, particularly for fractures. Its mucilage content helps reduce inflammation and promotes tissue repair. It is often applied externally as a poultice or taken internally as a tonic to aid in the recovery process.

3. Joint inflammation

Common Mallow has been traditionally used to reduce joint inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. It contains mucilage, which soothes irritated tissues, and compounds like flavonoids that help alleviate pain and swelling. It is often applied topically as a poultice or taken internally as a tea. Its gentle nature makes it suitable for long-term use in managing chronic joint conditions.

4. Inflammation of joints

Common Mallow (Symphytum officinale) is used to reduce joint inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. It contains mucilage, which soothes inflamed tissues, and compounds that inhibit inflammatory pathways. It is often applied topically as a poultice or taken internally as a tincture.

5. Muscle pain

Common Mallow is used to alleviate muscle pain due to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. It helps reduce swelling and soothe inflamed tissues, making it effective for conditions like muscle strains or joint inflammation. Topical applications of its mucilage are commonly used to relieve discomfort and promote healing.

6. Joint pain

Common Mallow (Symphytum officinale) has been traditionally used to alleviate joint pain due to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. The plant contains mucilage and bioactive compounds that help reduce inflammation and soothe pain in conditions like arthritis and sprains. It is often applied topically as a poultice or taken internally as a tea or tincture.

7. Muscle strains

Common Mallow is used to support muscle recovery from strains due to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. It helps reduce pain and swelling, promoting healing. Traditionally, it has been applied topically as a poultice or taken internally as a tea. Its mucilage content provides a protective effect on injured tissues.

8. Rheumatism

Common Mallow has been traditionally used to support joint health and alleviate symptoms of rheumatism. Its anti-inflammatory and demulcent properties help reduce pain and swelling in rheumatic conditions. It is often prepared as a poultice or infusion to soothe inflamed tissues and promote healing.

9. Muscle spasms

Common Mallow has been traditionally used to alleviate muscle spasms due to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. It helps reduce muscle tension and irritation, making it beneficial for conditions like muscle cramps or strains. The herb's mucilage content provides a calming effect on inflamed tissues, supporting muscle recovery and relaxation.

10. Osteoarthritis

Common Mallow (Symphytum officinale) has been traditionally used to support joint health, particularly in osteoarthritis. Its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties may help reduce pain and swelling. It is often prepared as a poultice or infused oil to apply directly to affected areas.
