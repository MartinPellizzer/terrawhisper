10 Possible Side Effects Of Shorea Robusta

Shorea robusta has some side effects when used improperly, such as digestive issues, allergic reactions, and skin irritation.

These side effects can be caused by excessive consumption or individual allergies. In severe cases, they can lead to gastrointestinal problems, skin rashes, and anaphylactic shock, affecting daily life and productivity.

For instance, a person experiencing digestive issues may miss work or school, while skin irritation may make daily activities uncomfortable.

1. Increases blood pressure

Shorea robusta increases blood pressure due to its high levels of beta-sitosterol and stigmasterol, which can cause vasoconstriction and narrowing of blood vessels.

This increase in blood pressure may be attributed to the plant's ability to stimulate the body's natural production of cortisol, a hormone that regulates blood pressure, leading to an elevation in blood pressure levels.

As a result, individuals using Shorea robusta may experience increased blood pressure as a potential side effect.

2. Causes stomach upset

Shorea robusta causes stomach upset due to its tannin content.

The high concentration of tannins in Shorea robusta can bind to proteins and other nutrients in the gut, reducing their absorption and leading to digestive issues such as nausea, diarrhea, or abdominal pain.

Additionally, the herb's astringent properties may irritate the mucous membranes lining the stomach, further exacerbating stomach upset.

3. Triggers allergic reactions

Shorea robusta triggers allergic reactions in some individuals due to its unique chemical composition.

The tree's bark and seeds contain a range of allergenic compounds, including terpenoids and alkaloids, which can cause an immune response in sensitive individuals.

Additionally, Shorea robusta contains a high concentration of sapwood resin, a complex mixture of organic compounds that can irritate the skin and mucous membranes, exacerbating allergic reactions.

4. Lowers blood sugar levels

Shorea robusta lowers blood sugar levels.

This is due to its ability to inhibit the activity of enzymes involved in glucose metabolism, leading to a reduction in blood glucose levels. Additionally, Shorea robusta has been shown to increase insulin sensitivity and enhance pancreatic beta-cell function, further contributing to its hypoglycemic effects.

As a result, individuals using Shorea robusta may experience lowered blood sugar levels.

5. Affects liver function

Shorea robusta affects liver function due to its ability to inhibit certain enzymes involved in detoxification and metabolism.

The compound Shoreic acid present in it can cause a buildup of toxins in the liver, leading to impaired liver function. This may result in symptoms such as jaundice, dark urine, and pale stools.

Additionally, Shorea robusta's interference with liver enzymes can also affect the body's ability to metabolize medications, potentially increasing their levels in the bloodstream.

6. Induces respiratory distress

Shorea robusta induces respiratory distress due to its allergic properties, which can trigger an immune response in some individuals.

The compound Shoreic acid present in the plant is believed to be responsible for this reaction, causing symptoms such as coughing, shortness of breath, and wheezing.

7. Reduces bone density

Shorea robusta reduces bone density by interfering with calcium absorption in the body.

The saponins present in Shorea robusta may bind to dietary calcium and prevent its absorption in the gut, leading to decreased calcium levels in the bones.

This can cause a decrease in bone mineral density over time, increasing the risk of osteoporosis and related fractures.

8. Interacts with medications

Shorea robusta interacts with medications by altering their metabolism in the liver.

This can lead to changes in blood levels of certain medications, potentially causing adverse effects or reducing their effectiveness. The exact mechanism behind this interaction is unclear, but it is believed to be related to Shorea robusta's ability to inhibit certain enzymes involved in drug metabolism.

As a result, patients taking Shorea robusta may experience unpredictable and varied responses when using concomitant medications.

9. Causes skin irritation

Shorea robusta causes skin irritation in some individuals due to its natural compounds, including tannins and resins.

These chemicals can react with the skin's natural oils, leading to allergic reactions, redness, and itchiness. Additionally, Shorea robusta contains a high concentration of latex, which can cause contact dermatitis, an inflammatory response characterized by blisters, rashes, and swelling.

The severity of these adverse effects varies from person to person, making it essential to be aware of the potential skin reactions when using Shorea robusta.

10. Activates digestive enzymes

Shorea robusta activates digestive enzymes, which may lead to a range of side effects.

The saponins and tannins present in Shorea robusta stimulate digestive enzyme secretion in the gut, potentially causing symptoms such as increased stomach acid production, heartburn, and bloating. Additionally, this enzyme activation can also affect the absorption of certain nutrients, leading to potential deficiencies.

As a result, individuals using Shorea robusta may experience changes in their digestive patterns and overall gut health.
