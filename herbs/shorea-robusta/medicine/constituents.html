10 Medicinal Constituents Of Shorea Robusta (Sal)

Shorea robusta has active constituents such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins.

These bioactive compounds are responsible for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial activities. The constituents in Shorea robusta have been shown to improve respiratory health by reducing inflammation and oxidative stress, making it effective in treating bronchitis and asthma.

Additionally, its antibacterial properties make it a potential natural remedy for wound healing, improving the quality of life for many individuals suffering from these conditions.

1. Triterpenoids

Shorea robusta, also known as Sal, triterpenoids is a class of compounds isolated from the bark of Shorea robusta, a large deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

These triterpenoids have been found to exhibit various biological activities, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties.

They have also been shown to possess potential therapeutic effects against diseases such as cancer, cardiovascular disorders, and neurodegenerative diseases.

2. Quercetin

Shorea robusta quercetin is a flavonoid compound extracted from the bark of Shorea robusta tree.

It has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic and Unani medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and respiratory problems.

Quercetin has potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which may help protect against chronic diseases such as heart disease, cancer, and neurodegenerative disorders.

3. Kaempferol

Shorea robusta kaempferol is a flavonoid glycoside isolated from the bark of Shorea robusta, a large deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It has been reported to possess various biological activities, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties.

Kaempferol has also been found to exhibit cytotoxic activity against certain cancer cell lines, making it a potential agent in the development of novel anticancer drugs.

4. Gallic acid

Shorea robusta gallic acid is a type of phenolic compound extracted from the bark and leaves of the Shorea robusta tree.

This potent antioxidant has been extensively studied for its numerous health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Gallic acid has been shown to inhibit the growth of various cancer cell lines and has potential applications in the treatment of diseases such as diabetes and neurodegenerative disorders.

5. Ellagic acid

Shorea robusta ellagic acid is a polyphenolic compound found in its bark.

It has been extensively studied for its potential health benefits, including antioxidant, antimicrobial, and anti-inflammatory properties. Ellagic acid has been shown to exhibit anti-cancer effects by inhibiting the growth of cancer cells and inducing apoptosis, making it a promising area of research in the field of cancer prevention and treatment.

Its bioactive properties also make it a valuable ingredient in traditional medicine.

6. Flavonoids

Shorea robusta flavonoids is a class of bioactive compounds extracted from the heartwood and bark of Shorea robusta trees.

These flavonoids possess potent antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, making them useful in traditional medicine for treating various diseases, including cardiovascular disorders, diabetes, and cancer.

Additionally, they have been shown to exhibit significant protective effects against oxidative stress and cell damage, highlighting their potential as therapeutic agents.

7. Phenolic acids

Shorea robusta phenolic acids is a type of polyphenol compound found in its bark.

These compounds have been shown to possess significant antioxidant and antimicrobial activities, making them potential natural preservatives for food and cosmetics.

The phenolic acids present in Shorea robusta include ferulic acid, caffeic acid, and sinapic acid, which have been reported to exhibit anti-inflammatory and anticancer properties, highlighting their importance in traditional medicine.

8. Tannins

Shorea robusta tannins is a type of polyphenolic compound found in the bark and heartwood of Shorea robusta trees.

These tannins are known for their astringent properties, which can help to reduce inflammation and improve skin health.

Additionally, shorea robusta tannins have been shown to exhibit antimicrobial and antioxidant activities, making them a valuable ingredient in various industries such as cosmetics, pharmaceuticals, and food processing.

9. Saponins

Shorea robusta saponins is a type of bioactive compound extracted from the bark and heartwood of the Sal tree.

These saponins have been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine for their anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiviral properties.

The saponins have also been found to exhibit antioxidant activity, which can help protect against oxidative stress and cell damage.

10. Glycosides

Shorea robusta glycosides is a group of bioactive compounds extracted from the bark and leaves of Shorea robusta tree.

These glycosides have been found to possess various pharmacological activities, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and anticancer properties. They have also been reported to exhibit wound-healing activity and protect against neurodegenerative diseases.

The study of Shorea robusta glycosides has gained significant attention due to their potential therapeutic applications in traditional medicine and modern pharmaceuticals.
