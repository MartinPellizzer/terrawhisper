Cremanthodium campanulatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Cremanthodium campanulatum?

Cremanthodium campanulatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Asterales and is a member of the family Asteraceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by its placement within the genus Cremanthodium, with its unique designation being the species campanulatum.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Cremanthodium campanulatum can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, including nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal pain, if ingested. These adverse reactions typically occur because the plant contains bioactive compounds that can irritate the digestive lining. In some cases, excessive consumption may lead to more systemic toxicity affecting other bodily functions. Due to these unpredictable reactions, the plant is considered potentially hazardous to human health. It is essential to avoid any internal use of this species to prevent serious medical complications.
