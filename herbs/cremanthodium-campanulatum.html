Cremanthodium campanulatum has an erect, herbaceous growth habit characterized by slender, often branched stems and oppositely arranged, ovate to lanceolate leaves with entire or slightly wavy margins. Its inflorescences consist of small, bell-shaped flowers that are clustered in terminal heads or panicles. These floral structures are often pale yellow or creamy in color, providing a delicate contrast to the surrounding greenery. What side-effects this plant can have?

Cremanthodium campanulatum can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, including nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal pain, if ingested. These adverse reactions typically occur because the plant contains bioactive compounds that can irritate the digestive lining. Due to these unpredictable reactions, the plant is considered potentially hazardous to human health. It is essential to avoid any internal use of this species to prevent serious medical complications.
