Botanical Identification of Java Plum (Syzygium cumini)

To identify Java Plum (Syzygium cumini) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

The Java Plum, or Syzygium cumini, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Sapindales, family Myrtaceae, genus Syzygium, and species Syzygium cumini. These classifications help identify its botanical relationships and place it within the broader system of plant life.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The botanical name of Java Plum is Syzygium cumini, a species within the Myrtaceae family. It was first described by Linnaeus in 1788. Common synonyms include Eugenia cumini and Syzygium jambolanum. It is also known by regional names such as Java plum, black plum, and Indian plum, reflecting its widespread use in traditional medicine.

Morphological Characteristics

Java Plum, or Syzygium cumini, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with a dense, rounded crown. Its root system is taproot-based with lateral roots that anchor it firmly in soil. The stem is smooth, grayish-brown, and often has a slight flaky bark. Leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptic to ovate, with a glossy dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. Inflorescences are axillary clusters, bearing small, fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a globular drupe, typically 2-4 cm in diameter, turning from green to yellow or red when ripe, enclosing a single hard seed.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Syzygium cumini can be distinguished by its elliptical, dark green leaves with prominent veins, its fragrant, pale green flowers in clusters, and its purple-black, glossy berries. It differs from similar species like Syzygium jambolanum by its leaf shape and the smaller, more rounded berries. The bark is smooth and greyish, with no thorns, aiding in identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Java Plum, or Syzygium cumini, is native to Southeast Asia, including India, Indonesia, and Malaysia. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity. The plant prefers well-drained, fertile soils and is typically found at altitudes between 200 and 1,200 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Java Plum (Syzygium cumini) blooms from March to May, producing small white flowers. Seed formation occurs between July and September. Harvesting of mature fruits typically takes place from October to December, when the berries turn dark purple and are fully ripe.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Syzygium cumini belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is closely related to species like Syzygium jambolanum and Syzygium malaccense. It shares common traits with other Syzygium genera, including similar floral structures and leaf morphology. Its phylogenetic placement reflects its evolutionary ties within the Myrtaceae family.
