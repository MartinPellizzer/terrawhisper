Stonebreaker (Phyllanthus niruri): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

What is Stonebreaker (Phyllanthus Niruri)?

Stonebreaker, also known as Phyllanthus niruri, is a small, creeping herb native to tropical regions of the Americas and Africa, commonly used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Botanical Identity and Classification

Stonebreaker, scientifically known as Phyllanthus niruri, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family, commonly found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Common Names and Synonyms

Stonebreaker (Phyllanthus niruri) is commonly known by various names such as Stonebreaker herb, Breakstone, Phyllanthus, and in some regions, it is referred to as Bitterleaf or Bitterbush.

Plant Description and Morphology

Stonebreaker (Phyllanthus niruri) is a small, erect annual herb with branched stems, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, and small greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, spherical berries that contain the seeds.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Stonebreaker (Phyllanthus niruri) is native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas, with a widespread distribution across countries such as Brazil, India, China, and parts of the Middle East.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The entire plant, including the leaves, stems, and fruits, is used medicinally in traditional herbal practices.

Phytochemical Composition

The phytochemical composition of Stonebreaker (Phyllanthus niruri) includes compounds such as phyllanthin, hypophyllin, and various flavonoids, which contribute to its traditional use in herbal medicine for urinary tract support and anti-inflammatory effects.

Pharmacological Properties

Pharmacological studies have demonstrated that Stonebreaker (Phyllanthus niruri) possesses antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antidiabetic properties, primarily due to the presence of bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, triterpenoids, and polyphenols.

Mechanisms of Action

Stonebreaker (Phyllanthus niruri) exerts its effects through a combination of phytochemical compounds that exhibit anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and hepatoprotective properties, primarily due to the presence of saponins, flavonoids, and alkaloids which modulate cellular signaling pathways and enzyme activities.

Therapeutic Uses and Indications

stonebreaker is used to treat urinary tract infections, kidney stones, inflammation of the urinary tract, digestive issues such as indigestion and bloating, skin infections, and as a general tonic to support liver and kidney function.

Preparation Methods and Forms

Stonebreaker (Phyllanthus niruri) is typically prepared as a decoction, infusion, or powder, with the most common method involving boiling the dried plant material in water for 10-15 minutes, followed by straining and consumption of the liquid.

Dosage and Administration

Storage and Shelf Life

Scientific Research and Evidence
