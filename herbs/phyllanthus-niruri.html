Botanical Identification

Stonebreaker, scientifically known as Phyllanthus niruri, belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family. It is also called "stonebreaker" in some regions and is an annual herb native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant has small, clustered flowers and is commonly used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits. Synonyms in herbal literature include "bitter leaf" and "stonebreaker grass."

Active Compounds

The main active compounds of Stonebreaker (Phyllanthus niruri) include flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids. These compounds work synergistically to exert their biological effects, such as anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which contribute to its traditional use in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Tannin
  • Phenolic acid

Preparation Methods

Powder: Used for digestive issues and as a general tonic.

Tincture: Used for liver support and to treat urinary tract infections.

Infusion: Used for kidney health and to support urinary function.

Safety Profile

Stonebreaker, or Phyllanthus niruri, is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. Long-term use should be monitored, and it is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to limited safety data.
