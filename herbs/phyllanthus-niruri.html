Phyllanthus niruri

What's the taxonomical classification of Phyllanthus niruri?

Phyllanthus niruri belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Malpighiales. The plant is a member of the family Phyllanthaceae, falling under the genus Phyllanthus, and is specifically identified by its species name, niruri.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phyllanthus niruri has an erect, branched herbaceous structure that typically reaches a height of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features small, alternate, oblong leaves arranged in a single plane along the stems to resemble compound pinnate leaves. Tiny, white, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils on long pedicels. These flowers develop into small, globose, greenish-to-reddish fruits that are positioned directly underneath the foliage. The stems are often reddish or green and possess a smooth texture.

What are the pharmacological activities of Phyllanthus niruri?

This plant has diverse pharmacological activities including anti-diabetic, anti-hepatitis B, anti-inflammatory, anti-malarial, and hepatoprotective properties. Its anti-diabetic effects help regulate blood glucose levels by improving insulin sensitivity and glucose uptake. The plant also exhibits strong anti-hepatitis B activity and provides significant hepatoprotective benefits to shield the liver from toxic damage. Additionally, its anti-inflammatory properties assist in reducing systemic swelling and pain. Finally, the presence of specific phytochemicals allows it to function as an effective anti-malarial agent against certain parasites.

As discussed in a study, Phyllanthus niruri has yielded a total of 120 phytoconstituents. These compounds provide various pharmacological activities including antiviral, immunomodulatory, hepatoprotective, antidiabetic, antiurolithic, anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, anticancer, antifungal, and tranquilizing effects. The plant is particularly noted for its antidiabetic, antiurolithic, and hepatoprotective properties. Its major biologically active compounds include alkaloids, tannins, coumarins, sterols, phenolic acid, flavonoids, and lignans. In multiple clinical trials, the plant remains a safe and efficacious treatment option for patients of all ages.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds gallic acid, ellagic acid, catechin, quercetin, and kaempferol. These bioactive molecules function primarily as potent antioxidants that help neutralize harmful free radicals within the body. Gallic acid and ellagic acid are specifically recognized for their ability to support cellular health and reduce inflammation. The presence of catechin further enhances the plant's therapeutic profile through its significant polyphenolic properties. Additionally, the flavonoids quercetin and kaempferol contribute to improved immune responses and cardiovascular protection.

As discussed in a study, Phyllanthus niruri L. whole plant extract (PNE) contains secondary metabolites including total phenolic and terpenoid content. The PNE showed scavenging activity values of 286.45 ± 6.55 mg TE/g in ABTS assays and 194.54 ± 4.64 mg TE/g in DPPH assays. In superoxide anion assays, the PNE achieved a lowest IC25 value of 0.17 ± 0.00 mg/mL, while its scavenging ability against nitric oxide showed an IC25 of 1.13 ± 0.04 mg/mL. Specific phytochemicals such as Astragalin, Gallocatechin, Ellagic acid, Gallic acid, Brevifolin carboxylic acid, Phyllnirurin, and Hypophyllanthin demonstrated a significant docking score of > -4 for inhibitory potential with DPP-IV protein. These results indicate that the PNE phytochemicals could serve as promising antidiaabetic agents.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for managing Hepatitis B, preventing kidney stones, treating malaria, controlling diabetes, and addressing hepatocellular carcinoma. It contains bioactive compounds that help inhibit the replication of the Hepatitis B virus in the liver. Its diuretic properties are frequently utilized to dissolve and prevent the formation of kidney stones. The plant also demonstrates antimalarial activities and helps regulate blood glucose levels in patients with diabetes. Additionally, research suggests it may play a role in managing hepatocellular carcinoma through its potential anticancer properties.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause hypoglycemia, hypotension, nausea, dizziness, abdominal pain, and diarrhea in some users. Because it may lower blood sugar levels, individuals monitoring glucose must be cautious of hypoglycemia. Its potential to decrease blood pressure means that those with low pressure may experience hypotension or dizziness. Digestive issues such as nausea, abdominal pain, and diarrhea are also reported side effects. It is important to consult a medical professional before using this herb to avoid these complications.

  • Hypotension
  • Nausea
