Phyllanthus niruri

Stonebreaker (Phyllanthus niruri) is a small, creeping shrub native to tropical regions, commonly used in traditional medicine for its potential to support kidney health and aid in the treatment of urinary tract infections.

Taxonomy

Stonebreaker (Phyllanthus niruri) is a species belonging to the family Euphorbiaceae, within the order Malpighiales, class Magnoliopsida, division Magnoliophyta, and kingdom Plantae.

Morphology

Stonebreaker (Phyllanthus niruri) is a small, erect annual herb with branched stems, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, and small greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, spherical berries that contain the seeds.

Distribution

Stonebreaker (Phyllanthus niruri) is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas, with significant presence in countries such as Brazil, India, China, and various parts of Africa.

Traditional Uses

Stonebreaker, or Phyllanthus niruri, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to support kidney health, reduce urinary tract inflammation, and promote the elimination of kidney stones due to its diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Medicinal Systems

Stonebreaker (Phyllanthus niruri) has been traditionally used in Ayurveda, Unani, and Chinese medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.

Phytochemistry

Phyllanthus niruri contains bioactive compounds such as phyllanthin, hypophyllin, and flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties and therapeutic effects.

Pharmacology

Stonebreaker (Phyllanthus niruri) exhibits pharmacological properties that include antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and hepatoprotective effects, making it effective in treating infections, reducing inflammation, and supporting liver function.

Botanical Identification

Stonebreaker, scientifically known as Phyllanthus niruri, belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family. Safety Profile

Stonebreaker, or Phyllanthus niruri, is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. Long-term use should be monitored, and it is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to limited safety data.
