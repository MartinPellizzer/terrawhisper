Physochlaina physaloides

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Physochlaina physaloides has an herbaceous perennial habit characterized by erect stems that reach heights of approximately 10 to 30 centimeters and bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is typically soft and slightly pubescent, providing a textural contrast to the smooth surfaces of the stem. Its most distinctive morphological feature is the presence of inflated, bladder-like calyxes that enclose the developing flowers and fruits. These bladder-like structures are often pale or translucent, giving the plant a delicate, airy appearance during its flowering stage. The flowers themselves are relatively small and emerge from these protective, balloon-shaped bracts.
