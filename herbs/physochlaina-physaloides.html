Physochlaina physaloides

What's the taxonomical classification of Physochlaina physaloides?

Physochlaina physaloides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is placed in the order Solanales and the family Solanaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by its membership in the genus Physochlaina, with the specific epithet physaloides.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Physochlaina physaloides has an herbaceous perennial habit characterized by erect stems that reach heights of approximately 10 to 30 centimeters and bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins.

The foliage is typically soft and slightly pubescent, providing a textural contrast to the smooth surfaces of the stem. Its most distinctive morphological feature is the presence of inflated, bladder-like calyxes that enclose the developing flowers and fruits. These bladder-like structures are often pale or translucent, giving the plant a delicate, airy appearance during its flowering stage.

The flowers themselves are relatively small and emerge from these protective, balloon-shaped bracts.
