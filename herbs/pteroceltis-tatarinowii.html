What's the taxonomical classification of Pteroceltis tatarinowii?

Pteroceltis tatarinowii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant falls under the order Rosales. It is further categorized into the family Cannabaea, the genus Pteroceltis, and is specifically identified as the species tatarinoowii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pteroceltis tatarinowii has simple, alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves that measure approximately 5 to 12 centimeters in length and feature serrated margins with acuminate tips.

The leaf texture is typically coriaceous, possessing a smooth upper surface and a slightly paler underside. Its branches are characterized by a woody structure with grayish bark that develops shallow fissures as the tree matures. The reproductive morphology involves small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into drupe-like fruits.

These fruits are often characterized by a distinct shape that aids in dispersal within its natural habitat.
