Holboellia latifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Holboellia latifolia?

Holboellia latifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Ranunculales. This plant is a member of the family Lardizabalaceae, which contains the genus Holboellia, and is specifically identified by its species name, latifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Holboellia latifolia has large, broadly ovate to elliptical leaves that typically measure up to 30 centimeters in length and feature prominent, pinnate venation.

The leaf margins are generally entire, while the texture of the foliage appears somewhat leathery or coriaceous. Its stems are woody and robust, providing structural support for the sprawling or climbing growth habit of the plant. The inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils.

These botanical characteristics distinguish the species within its specific taxonomic group.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Holboellia latifolia can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, allergic skin reactions, and potential neurotoxicity if ingested in uncontrolled quantities. The plant contains specific secondary metabolites that may trigger intense nausea or abdominal cramping in sensitive individuals. Contact with the leaves can result in contact dermatitis, characterized by redness and itching on the skin. Furthermore, some studies suggest that its chemical composition might interfere with neurological signaling if systemic absorption occurs. Due to these unpredictable reactions, cautious handling and professional medical consultation are essential when dealing with this species.
