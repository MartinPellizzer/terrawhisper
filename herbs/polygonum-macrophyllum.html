Polygonum macrophyllum

What's the taxonomical classification of Polygonum macrophyllum?

Polygonum macrophyllum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is placed in the order Caryophyllales and belongs to the family Polygonaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Polygonum and is specifically designated by the species name macrophyllum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polygonum macrophyllum has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that measure up to 10 centimeters in length and are characterized by prominent longitudinal veins. The stems are typically slender and often possess a reddish hue, occasionally showing small, swollen nodes at the leaf junctions. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in axillary clusters, presenting white or pinkish petals that emerge from tubular bases. The plant often features a creeping or ascending growth habit, allowing it to spread across disturbed soils or rocky substrates. Its overall structure is herbaceous and relatively delicate compared to other members of the Polygonaceae family.
