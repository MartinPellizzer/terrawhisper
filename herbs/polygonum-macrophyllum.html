Polygonum macrophyllum

What's the taxonomical classification of Polygonum macrophyllum?

Polygonum macrophyllum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is placed in the order Caryophyllales and belongs to the family Polygonaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Polygonum and is specifically designated by the species name macrophyllum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polygonum macrophyllum has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that measure up to 10 centimeters in length and are characterized by prominent longitudinal veins. The stems are typically slender and often possess a reddish hue, occasionally showing small, swollen nodes at the leaf junctions. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in axillary clusters, presenting white or pinkish petals that emerge from tubular bases. The plant often features a creeping or ascending growth habit, allowing it to spread across disturbed soils or rocky substrates. Its overall structure is herbaceous and relatively delicate compared to other members of the Polygonaceae family.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the western United States, specifically occurring in California, Oregon, and Washington. It thrives primarily in the diverse habitats of the Pacific coast and the foothills of the Cascade Range. Populations are often found in moist areas such as riparian zones, meadows, and shaded woodland edges. Its distribution is closely tied to regions that receive consistent seasonal moisture. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds (+)-catechin-7-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside, (-)-epicatechin, (-)-epicatechin-5-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside, chlorogenic acid, and gallic acid. These specific polyphenols and organic acids contribute to the overall bioactive profile of Polygonum macrophyllum. The presence of various catechin derivatives suggests potential antioxidant properties within the plant tissues. Furthermore, the combination of chlorogenic acid and gallic acid provides a complex chemical foundation for its medicinal use. Such diverse chemical constituents are essential for understanding the pharmacological potential of this species.

According to a study published by "Zhong yao cai = Zhongyaocai = Journal of Chinese medicinal materials", six compounds were isolated from the 70% acetone extract from the complete herb of Polygonum macrophyllum. These six compounds, identified as I through VI, were isolated from the plant for the first time. The elucidated structures include (-)-epicatechin-5-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside (I), (+)-catechin-7-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside (II), and 1-(3-O-beta-D-glucopyranosyl 4,5-dihydroxy-phenyl)-ethanone (III). Additionally, the study identified (-)-epicatechin (IV), chlorogenic acid (V), and gallic acid (VI) within the plant.
