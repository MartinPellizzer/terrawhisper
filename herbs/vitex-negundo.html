From a botanical standpoint, Chinese Chaste Tree belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is native to Asia and Australia.

With recorded use dating back thousands of years, Chinese Chaste Tree has a rich history in traditional medicine, with ancient references found in traditional Chinese and Ayurvedic texts.

What are the side effects of Vitex negundo when used improperly?

When used improperly, Vitex negundo increases the chances of experiencing side effects, such as gastrointestinal disturbances, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea.

Additionally, improper use can lead to allergic reactions, skin rashes, and respiratory problems, including bronchospasm and asthma-like symptoms. Furthermore, excessive consumption or prolonged use can cause cardiovascular issues, including increased heart rate and blood pressure, as well as neurological complications like headache, dizziness, and seizures.

In some cases, Vitex negundo may interact with other medications, such as anticoagulants, anti-diabetic drugs, and sedatives, potentially exacerbating their effects or increasing the risk of adverse reactions. Moreover, improper use during pregnancy or breastfeeding can lead to serious complications, including miscarriage, stillbirth, or birth defects.

Therefore, it is essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using Vitex negundo for medicinal purposes to minimize the risk of side effects and ensure safe and effective treatment.

The precautions to take before using Vitex negundo medicinally are crucial to ensure safe and effective treatment.
