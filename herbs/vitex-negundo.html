Vitex negundo

What's the taxonomical classification of Vitex negundo?

Vitex negundo belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Lamiales. It is further categorized into the family Lamiaceae, which places it among a diverse group of flowering plants. Within this family, it is identified by the genus Vitex, and its specific biological designation is the species negundo.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vitex negundo has opposite, palmately lobed leaves that typically feature three to five leaflets with serrated margins and a woody, erect shrub habit reaching up to four meters in height. The plant produces terminal panicles of small, bluish-purple flowers that emerge from clusters of branched stems. Its fruit consists of small, fleshy drupes that transition from green to a dark purple or black color upon ripening. The bark is grayish and somewhat rough, providing structural support for its sprawling branches. This species is characterized by its distinctively fragrant foliage and a pentamerous floral structure.

What are the pharmacological activities of Vitex negundo?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological activities including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, antimicrobial, antiviral, and analgesic properties. Its chemical constituents work to neutralize free radicals, which provides a robust antioxidant defense for cellular structures. The presence of specific phytochemicals also allows the plant to inhibit the growth of various bacteria and viruses effectively. Furthermore, it serves as a natural agent to reduce swelling and alleviate pain through its anti-inflammatory and analgesic mechanisms. These diverse biological effects make it a valuable subject for pharmaceutical research and traditional medicine.

As discussed in a study, a new acid isolated from Vitex negundo L. herb was identified as Vitenegu acid. This novel Vitenegu acid specifically inhibits NLRP3 inflammasome activation without affecting NLRC4 or AIM2 inflammosomes. The substance works by blocking the oligomerization of NLRP3, which inhibits the assembly and activation of the NLRP3 inflammasome. In vivo data demonstrates that Vitenegu acid exerts therapeutic effects on NLRP3 inflammasome-dependent inflammation. These results suggest that Vitenegu acid is a candidate therapeutic agent for treating NLRP3 inflammasome related diseases.

As discussed in a study, the methanolic extracts of Vitex negundo recorded the maximum antimicrobial activity against bacterial and fungal pathogens. These methanolic extracts also evidenced the highest antioxidant properties. The hemolytic property of the Vitex negundo extract was measured at 8.91%, though the hemolytic index did not show any hemolysis of human erythrocytes. Furthermore, the TLC separation of Vitex negundo extracts exhibited the presence of Flavonoids with an Rf value of 0.74. The study also identified Terpenoids in the extracts with an Rf value of 0.64.

What are the herbal preparations of this plant?
