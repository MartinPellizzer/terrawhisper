Negundo Vitex

Vitex negundo

Negundo Vitex (Vitex negundo) is a member of the Verbenaceae family, traditionally used in various regions for its medicinal properties. The plant parts, including leaves and flowers, have been utilized historically to relieve pain and treat skin conditions, as well as serve as a tonic. It holds cultural significance in traditional medicine systems across parts of Asia and the Middle East.

Botanical Identification

Vitex negundo, commonly known as Negundo Vitex, belongs to the Verbenaceae family. It is a perennial shrub native to Asia, characterized by opposite leaves, fragrant flowers, and a woody stem. Known by various regional names, it has been used in herbal medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.

Negundo Vitex Identification.

Active Compounds

Negundo Vitex contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids. These compounds work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Their combined action supports the herb's traditional use in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Tannin
  • Phenolic acid

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory issues and digestive problems.

Decoction: Used for treating skin conditions and inflammation.

Tincture: Used for pain relief and as an antiseptic.

Safety Profile

Vitex negundo is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy due to potential uterine stimulant effects. Always consult a healthcare professional before use, especially for those with pre-existing medical conditions.
