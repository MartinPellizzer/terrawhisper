What is Caroxylon giessii?

Caroxylon giessii is a species of succulent plant in the family Amaranthaceae, native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub with a woody base, growing up to 3 meters in height, with stems that are green to grey in color. The leaves are small, linear, and often absent at the base of the stems, while the flowers are white to pinkish in color, arranged in a terminal spike. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are red to purple in color.

The species is known for its ability to store water in its fleshy stems and leaves, allowing it to survive in arid environments.
