10 Medicinal Constituents Of Illicium Verum (Star Anise)

Illicium verum has active constituents such as volatile oils, particularly eugenol and anethole.

These compounds exhibit antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties. They help alleviate respiratory issues, digestive problems, and menstrual cramps. Eugenol, for instance, has been used to relieve toothache pain and reduce swelling.

Anethole has been shown to improve respiratory function and relieve coughs, providing relief to people suffering from asthma and bronchitis.

This article explains in details the 10 best active constituents of Illicium verum.

1. Anethole

Illicium verum anethole is a naturally occurring compound found in the fruit of the Illicium verum plant.

It is a volatile oil that gives the plant its distinctive flavor and aroma, often used in baking and cooking to add a warm, spicy flavor to dishes.

Anethole has also been used for centuries in traditional medicine to treat various ailments, including digestive issues and respiratory problems.

2. Isobutyl amide

Illicium verum isobutyl amide is a chemical compound that is extracted from the seeds of the Illicium verum plant.

It has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries and is known for its anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antifungal properties.

3. Lignans

Illicium verum lignans is a type of compound found in its fruit.

These lignans have been shown to possess numerous biological and pharmacological activities, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties.

They have been traditionally used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat various diseases such as digestive disorders, respiratory infections, and cancer.

4. Flavonoids

Illicium verum flavonoids is a class of plant compounds found in its leaves and seeds.

These bioactive molecules have been reported to exhibit various biological activities, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties.

Flavonoids in Illicium verum have been shown to possess neuroprotective effects, inhibit platelet aggregation, and scavenge free radicals, making them a promising area of research for the development of natural therapeutics and health supplements.

5. Coumarin

Illicium verum coumarin is a naturally occurring compound found in the seed of this plant.

Coumarin has been used for centuries in traditional medicine to treat various ailments, including fever and respiratory issues. It has also been shown to have antibacterial and antifungal properties, making it effective against a range of microorganisms.

Coumarin is often used as a flavoring agent in foods and beverages, particularly in Asian cuisine.

6. Sesquiterpenes

Illicium verum sesquiterpenes is a group of bioactive compounds found in its essential oil.

These sesquiterpenes, including beta-pinene and y-terpineol, have been shown to exhibit various biological activities, such as antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant effects.

They are believed to contribute to the plant's traditional uses in medicine, particularly in treating respiratory issues, and may also play a role in its culinary applications.

7. Essential oil

Illicium verum essential oil is extracted from the fruit of the Illicium verum plant through a process of steam distillation.

The oil has a sweet, warm, and spicy aroma that is often used in aromatherapy to promote relaxation, reduce stress and anxiety, and ease digestive issues.

It is also known for its antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties, making it effective in treating respiratory problems and skin conditions.

8. Acetoxyisobutyl amide

Illicium verum acetoxyisobutyl amide is a bioactive compound found in the seed oil of Illicium verum.

It has been extensively studied for its potential health benefits, including antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Acetoxyisobutyl amide has been shown to inhibit the growth of certain bacteria and fungi, making it a promising candidate for the development of new antimicrobial agents.

Its unique chemical structure also makes it a valuable tool in the study of medicinal chemistry.

9. N-butylphthalide

Illicium verum n-butylphthalide is a bioactive compound extracted from the seeds of the Chinese star anise plant (Illicium verum).

It has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine for its various health benefits, including relieving anxiety and stress, treating digestive issues, and reducing inflammation.

Research has shown that n-butylphthalide may also have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a potential therapeutic agent for several diseases.

10. N-isobutylphthalide

Illicium verum n-isobutylphthalide is a naturally occurring compound found in the oil of this plant.

It has been extensively studied for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Research suggests that it may be effective in reducing pain and improving cognitive function, among other applications.

As a result, it has gained popularity as a dietary supplement and ingredient in traditional Chinese medicine.
