10 Possible Side Effects Of Illicium Verum

Illicium verum has some side effects when used improperly, such as nausea, dizziness, and allergic reactions.

These side effects can occur due to excessive consumption, pre-existing health conditions, or interactions with other medications.

For example, if a person with a pre-existing heart condition consumes excessive star anise, it can worsen their condition, leading to irregular heartbeats and even heart failure.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Illicium verum causes allergic reactions in some individuals due to its high content of terpenes and essential oils.

These compounds can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling.

Additionally, the plant's allergenic properties may cause respiratory issues like asthma-like symptoms or bronchospasm in people who are sensitive to it.

2. Triggers asthma attacks

Illicium verum triggers asthma attacks due to its allergenic properties and potential to stimulate excessive mucus production in the airways.

The spice contains a compound called anethole, which can cause an allergic reaction in some individuals, leading to constriction of the airways and increased mucus secretion.

This combination can exacerbate existing asthma symptoms, triggering attacks in those who are already predisposed to the condition.

3. Induces convulsions

Illicium verum induces convulsions due to its content of essential oils such as anethole and limonene.

These oils can affect the central nervous system, leading to alterations in brain activity and potentially causing seizures or convulsions.

Additionally, Illicium verum's ability to interact with GABA receptors, a neurotransmitter that regulates muscle tone, may also contribute to its convulsive effects.

4. Produces hallucinations

Illicium verum produces hallucinations due to its unique chemical composition.

The plant contains a volatile oil called anethole, which is responsible for its psychoactive effects. When consumed in large quantities or through excessive use, anethole can alter the brain's neurotransmitters, leading to vivid visual and auditory hallucinations.

This side effect is more likely to occur when Illicium verum is used in traditional medicine or as a recreational substance.

5. Increases heart rate

Illicium verum increases heart rate due to its stimulatory effects on the cardiovascular system.

The active compounds present in the plant, such as eugenol and safrole, can cause a rapid heartbeat by increasing sympathetic nervous system activity and decreasing parasympathetic nervous system activity, leading to increased cardiac output and blood pressure.

6. Decreases blood pressure

Illicium verum decreases blood pressure by virtue of its active compound, shikimic acid.

This natural substance relaxes blood vessels, causing a drop in blood pressure and potentially leading to symptoms such as dizziness, lightheadedness, and fainting.

As the body's vasodilation response is increased, blood flow is redistributed, allowing for reduced blood pressure readings.

7. Disrupts menstrual cycles

Illicium verum disrupts menstrual cycles due to its estrogenic and progesteronic properties.

The plant's essential oils contain compounds that mimic the effects of these hormones, which can alter the body's natural balance and lead to changes in menstrual regularity.

This may result in irregular periods, heavy bleeding, or even cessation of menstruation in some individuals.

8. Affects fetal development

Illicium verum affects fetal development due to its ability to stimulate uterine contractions and potentially cause miscarriage.

The essential oil extracted from the fruit of Illicium verum contains compounds like anethole and limonene, which can be toxic to the developing fetus and may lead to birth defects or other complications during pregnancy.

9. Causes liver damage

Illicium verum causes liver damage due to its high content of volatile oils and phenolic compounds.

These chemicals can disrupt normal liver function, leading to inflammation and scarring. Prolonged use or excessive consumption of Illicium verum may exacerbate this effect, potentially resulting in chronic liver disease or even liver failure.

The exact mechanisms by which these compounds cause liver damage are not fully understood, but it is thought that they interfere with the body's natural detoxification processes.

10. Irritates skin and eyes

Illicium verum irritates skin and eyes due to its high concentration of essential oils, particularly anethole.

This compound can cause contact dermatitis, leading to redness, itching, and swelling on the skin.

Additionally, the volatile oils in Illicium verum can also stimulate the mucous membranes in the eyes, resulting in irritation, burning, and tearing.
