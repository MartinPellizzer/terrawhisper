Star Anise (Illicium verum) Compounds

The primary compounds in Star Anise include essential oil, which contributes its aromatic properties, and flavonoids, known for their antioxidant effects. Terpenoids and coumarin provide anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic benefits. Phenolic acids and tannins support digestive health, while glycosides and saponins may aid in detoxification. Alkaloids and anthocyanins contribute to its medicinal and protective qualities.

Star Anise (Illicium verum) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Star Anise (Illicium verum) is a key active compound, containing potent phytochemicals like anethole and shikimic acid. It contributes to the plant's aromatic profile and therapeutic properties, including antispasmodic and antimicrobial effects. The oil is widely used in traditional medicine and modern formulations for its diverse health benefits.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Star Anise (Illicium verum). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting its traditional use in respiratory and digestive health. These compounds help modulate immune responses and may enhance the therapeutic effects of other active constituents in Star Anise.

3. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Star Anise (Illicium verum). One notable terpenoid is shikimic acid, which is crucial for the plant's medicinal properties. It serves as a key component in the synthesis of antiviral drugs and contributes to the plant's therapeutic effects.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Star Anise (Illicium verum). It contributes to the plant's aromatic profile and has antispasmodic and anticoagulant properties. Coumarin is used in herbal medicine for its potential to relieve muscle spasms and support cardiovascular health. It is also noted for its role in traditional remedies for respiratory and digestive issues.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Star Anise (Illicium verum). It contributes to the plant's antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. This compound helps support respiratory health and may aid in treating conditions like coughs and bronchitis. Its presence enhances the therapeutic value of Star Anise in herbal medicine.

6. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Star Anise (Illicium verum). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and aids in reducing inflammation and microbial growth. Tannins in Star Anise support its traditional use in respiratory and digestive health.

7. Alkaloid

Star Anise (Illicium verum) contains the alkaloid shikimic acid. This compound is essential for the plant's defense mechanisms and plays a key role in the production of oseltamivir, an antiviral drug used to treat influenza. While not the primary active component, shikimic acid contributes to the plant's medicinal value.

8. Glycoside

Star Anise (Illicium verum) contains the glycoside shikimic acid, which is a key active compound used in traditional herbal medicine. It plays a crucial role in the plant's therapeutic properties, particularly in respiratory and digestive health, and is also a precursor in the synthesis of antiviral medications.

9. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Star Anise (Illicium verum). It contributes to the plant's antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Saponins may aid in respiratory and digestive health, supporting traditional uses of Star Anise in herbal medicine.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a pigment found in Star Anise (Illicium verum). It contributes to the plant's characteristic color and has antioxidant properties. While not the primary active compound, anthocyanin may support overall health by neutralizing free radicals. Its presence in Star Anise highlights the plant's complex biochemical profile.
