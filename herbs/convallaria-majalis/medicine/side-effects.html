10 Possible Side Effects Of Convallaria Majalis

Convallaria majalis has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, headaches, and cardiac problems.

These side effects are often caused by ingesting the plant's glycosides, which can be toxic in large doses. For example, consuming a large quantity of lily of the valley can lead to nausea, vomiting, and potentially life-threatening arrhythmias.

This can severely impact one's quality of life, leading to missed work, strained relationships, and reduced overall well-being.

1. Causes heart arrhythmias

Convallaria majalis causes heart arrhythmias due to its cardiac glycoside content, specifically digoxin.

This compound works by slowing the heart rate and increasing its contraction force, which can lead to abnormal heart rhythms in some individuals. The mechanism involves binding to sodium-potassium pumps, altering the balance of ion channels and leading to changes in electrical activity within the heart.

As a result, Convallaria majalis may trigger irregular heartbeats or palpitations in people who consume it, particularly those with pre-existing heart conditions.

2. Produces vomiting

Convallaria majalis produces vomiting when ingested.

This is due to the presence of cardiac glycosides, such as convallatoxin and convallarin, which can cause irritation to the gastrointestinal tract and lead to nausea and vomiting. These compounds are toxic and can also cause other symptoms like abdominal pain, diarrhea, and stomach cramps.

The toxicity of Convallaria majalis is particularly concerning when ingested in large amounts or by children, as it can be life-threatening.

3. Triggers diarrhea

Convallaria majalis triggers diarrhea due to its saponin content.

The plant's glycosides, including convallatoxin and convallamarin, can irritate the digestive system and disrupt normal bowel function. This leads to an imbalance in the gut microbiome, causing loose stools and watery diarrhea. Additionally, the plant's anthraquinone glycosides may also contribute to its laxative properties, further exacerbating the issue.

As a result, individuals using Convallaria majalis may experience frequent bowel movements and an increased risk of dehydration.

4. Causes dizziness

Convallaria majalis causes dizziness due to its ability to lower blood pressure and slow down the heart rate.

The cardiac glycosides present in Convallaria majalis, specifically convallotoxin and lapachol, can affect the cardiovascular system, leading to a decrease in blood pressure and a slowdown in the heart's pumping action.

This decrease in blood flow to the brain can cause dizziness, lightheadedness, or even fainting spells.

5. Induces respiratory distress

Convallaria majalis induces respiratory distress due to its toxic compounds, including cardiac glycosides and saponins.

These substances can cause constriction of airways, leading to bronchospasm and wheezing.

Additionally, the cardenolide content in Convallaria majalis can stimulate the vagus nerve, resulting in bradycardia and respiratory depression, further exacerbating respiratory distress.

6. Increases blood pressure

Convallaria majalis increases blood pressure due to its cardiac glycoside content, primarily digoxin.

This compound can stimulate the heart's natural pacemaker activity, leading to increased heart rate and elevated blood pressure. Additionally, the glycosides in Convallaria may cause blood vessels to constrict, further contributing to the increase in blood pressure.

As a result, individuals using Convallaria majalis may experience heightened cardiovascular activity, potentially affecting overall circulatory health.

7. Reduces cardiac output

Convallaria majalis reduces cardiac output by inhibiting the production of acetylcholine in the heart.

This decrease in parasympathetic tone can lead to a slower heart rate and decreased force of contraction, resulting in reduced cardiac output.

Additionally, Convallaria majalis can also suppress the automaticity of the sinoatrial node, further contributing to its negative effect on cardiac function.

8. Elevates liver enzymes

Convallaria majalis elevates liver enzymes when used as a medication.

This is because the plant's toxic compounds, such as convallotoxin and cortimycin, can cause damage to the liver cells, leading to an increase in liver enzyme levels.

The exact mechanisms by which these compounds exert this effect are not fully understood, but it is thought that they may interfere with normal liver function and lead to a range of adverse reactions.

9. Causes numbness

Convallaria majalis causes numbness in some individuals.

This is due to the presence of cardiac glycosides, such as convallotoxin and lanatoside, which can interact with the body's nervous system and disrupt normal sensation. These compounds are responsible for the plant's medicinal properties, but they can also cause a range of side effects, including numbness, tingling, and changes in blood pressure.

As the cardiac glycosides are absorbed into the bloodstream, they can bind to sodium-potassium pumps in nerve cells, altering electrical impulses and leading to numbness and other neurological symptoms.

10. Increases muscle weakness

Convallaria majalis increases muscle weakness due to its ability to affect the heart's electrical activity.

The cardiac glycosides present in the plant can cause a slowing of the heart rate and an increase in the force of contraction, leading to weakened muscles. This is because the body's energy reserves are diverted from the muscles to support the increased workload on the heart.

As a result, individuals using Convallaria majalis may experience muscle weakness, fatigue, and potentially even paralysis.
