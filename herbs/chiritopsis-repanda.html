What's the taxonomical classification of Chiritopsis repanda?

Chiritopsis repanda belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, the plant is part of the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Gesneriaceae. Specifically, it is categorized under the genus Chiritopsis, with its unique identity defined by the species name repanda.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Chiritopsis repanda has oppositely arranged, cordate to reniform leaves that feature deeply wavy or undulate margins and a prominent reticulate venation pattern. The foliage is typically dark green and can grow in a basal rosette or along creeping stems. Its stems are relatively slender and often exhibit a slightly pubescent texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These floral structures are characteristic of the Asteraceae family and are often tucked within the dense canopy of the leaves.
