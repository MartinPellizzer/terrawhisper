What's the taxonomical classification of Stylophorum lasiocarpum?

Stylophorum lasiocarpum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Ranunculales. This plant is further categorized under the family Papaveraceae, which characterizes its botanical relationship to other poppy species. Ultimately, its specific biological identity is defined by its placement within the genus Stylophorum, resulting in its scientific species name, lasiocarpum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stylophorum lasiocarpum has deeply lobed, palmately divided leaves with serrated margins and a distinctively hairy underside. One of its most defining morphological traits is the long, cylindrical, and densely pubescent fruit known as a silique. These seed pods eventually split to release numerous small seeds, completing its reproductive cycle.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several potent isoquinoline alkaloids including chelerythrine, cheliluutin, cheliruubine, macarpine, and sanguinarine. These specific medicinal compounds are known for their diverse biological activities and pharmacological potential. Researchers study these substances for their ability to exhibit antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antitumor properties in various models. The presence of these alkaloids makes the plant a significant subject in ethnobotanical and biochemical investigations. Understanding the precise chemical structure and concentration of these compounds is essential for developing new therapeutic agents.

According to a study published by "Journal of pharmaceutical and biomedical analysis", the content of seven quaternary benzo[c]phenanthridine alkaloids was determined in Stylophorum lasiocarpum (Oliv.). These seven alkaloids are sanguinarine (SA), chelerythrine (CHE), chelirubine (CHR), chelilutine (CHL), sanguilutine (SL), sanguirubine (SR) and macarpine (MA). The quantification was performed using an HPLC method with a reversed phase column Synergi Max-RP C-12 Phenomenex. This method utilized a mobile phase of heptanesulfonic acid (0.01 mol/l) with triethanolamine (0.1 mol/l) in redistilled water at pH 2.5. The process involved an acetonitril gradient of 25-60% during 25 min and detection at 280 nm.
