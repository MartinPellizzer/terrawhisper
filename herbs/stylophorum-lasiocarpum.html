Stylophorum lasiocarpum

What's the taxonomical classification of Stylophorum lasiocarpum?

Stylophorum lasiocarpum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Ranunculales. This plant is further categorized under the family Papaveraceae, which characterizes its botanical relationship to other poppy species. Ultimately, its specific biological identity is defined by its placement within the genus Stylophorum, resulting in its scientific species name, lasiocarpum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stylophorum lasiocarpum has deeply lobed, palmately divided leaves with serrated margins and a distinctively hairy underside. The plant features large, bright yellow flowers that possess five petals and a prominent central cluster of stamens. Its stems are herbaceous and often display a reddish tint as they grow. One of its most defining morphological traits is the long, cylindrical, and densely pubescent fruit known as a silique. These seed pods eventually split to release numerous small seeds, completing its reproductive cycle.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal isoquinoline alkaloids chelerythrine, cheliluetin, chelirubine, macarpine, and sanguinarine. These bioactive compounds are primarily responsible for the plant's diverse pharmacological properties, including antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Specifically, chelerythrine and sanguinarine are known for their potent biological activities against various pathogens. The presence of cheliluetin and chelirubine further contributes to the complex chemical profile used in traditional medicine. Together, these alkaloids demonstrate how specific chemical constituents drive the therapeutic potential of the species.

As discussed in a study, the plant Stylophorum lasiocarpum (Oliv.) contains seven quaternary benzo[c]phenanthridine alkaloids (QBA). These specific medicinal compounds include sanguinarine (SA), chelerythrine (CHE), chelirubine (CHR), chelilutine (CHL), sanguilutine (SL), sanguirubine (SR) and macarpine (MA). The research utilized an HPLC method with a reversed phase column Synergi Max-RP C-12 Phenomenex to determine these levels in the underground part of the species. The mobile phase for this analysis consisted of heptanesulfonic acid (0.01 mol/l) with triethanolamine (0.1 mol/l) in redistilled water at pH 2.5. Detection of these alkaloids was performed at 280 nm using an acetonitrile gradient of 25-60% during 25 min.
