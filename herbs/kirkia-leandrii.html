What is Kirkia leandrii?

Kirkia leandrii is a species of plant in the family Kirkiaceae, native to Madagascar.

It is a deciduous tree with a straight bole and a rounded crown, growing up to 20 meters in height. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, measuring 7-14 centimeters in length and 2-5 centimeters in width, with a ciliate margin. The flowers are greenish-yellow, hermaphroditic, and clustered, appearing in axillary racemes, with 5-6 petals and a 5-merous calyx.

The fruit is a capsule, 5-7 centimeters in length, containing 2-4 seeds.

What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

The main herbal preparations of Kirkia leandrii are infusion, tincture, decoction, salve, and tea, which are derived from its bark and leaves, and used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

Infusion is a liquid preparation made by steeping the leaves in hot water, often used to treat fever and cough. Tincture is a concentrated liquid extract, typically made with ethanol, and used for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties. Decoction is a preparation made by boiling the bark in water, often used to treat digestive issues and skin conditions.

Salve is a topical preparation made by infusing the bark in oil, used to treat wounds, skin irritations, and inflammation.
