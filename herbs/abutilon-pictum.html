Abutilon pictum

What's the taxonomical classification of Abutilon pictum?

Abutilon pictum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Malvales. The plant is further categorized into the family Malvaceae, specifically within the genus Abutilon, and is identified by the species name pictum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Abutilon pictum has ovate to heart-shaped leaves with serrated margins, woody stems, and bell-shaped flowers that feature prominent, protruding stamens. The foliage is often characterized by a soft, pubescent texture and deep green coloration that provides a lush backdrop for its blooms. Its flowers typically display vibrant hues like yellow or orange, often accented by dark reddish-brown veins or spots on the petals. The plant grows as a perennial shrub, developing a branched structure that can reach several feet in height. Small, capsule-like seed pods develop following successful pollination to ensure future propagation.
