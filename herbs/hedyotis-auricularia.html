What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hedyotis auricularia has small, fleshy, ear-shaped leaves that are typically green or slightly purplish and grow in a compact rosette pattern. These leaves often feature rounded margins and a slightly concave surface, giving them their distinct auricular appearance. The stems are relatively short and often remain obscured by the dense foliage of the basal rosette. Tiny, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the center of the plant on slender stalks during its blooming period.

      Hedyotis auricularia is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste depending on the intended therapeutic application.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Hedyotis auricularia can cause localized skin irritation, redness, and swelling upon direct contact with the plant's tissues. Some individuals may experience an allergic reaction characterized by itchy rashes or small blisters where the skin touched the foliage. If the sap or plant material is accidentally ingested, it may lead to gastrointestinal distress such as nausea or stomach cramping. It is important to avoid touching the eyes or mucous membranes after handling the plant to prevent further irritation. Always wash hands thoroughly with soap and water after any exposure to minimize these risks.
