        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Clematis pseudopogonandra can cause severe skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential systemic toxicity if ingested or touched. The plant contains protoanemonin, a chemical compound that triggers blistering and redness upon contact with the skin. If a person consumes any part of the vine, they may experience intense nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain. These reactions are often accompanied by swelling of the mucous membranes in the mouth and throat. Because the toxins are potent, medical attention is recommended if accidental exposure occurs.
