Common Hawthorn (Crataegus Brevispina Var. Contracta)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Crataegus brevispina var. contracta.

Common hawthorn, scientifically known as Crataegus brevispina var. contracta, is a small ornamental shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Korea, and has been used for centuries in traditional medicine. It is primarily valued as a medicinal herb, known for its adaptogenic properties that support cardiovascular health and stress resilience. The plant's berries and flowers contain flavonoids and oligomeric proanthocyanidins, which contribute to its reputation for improving heart function, reducing anxiety, and enhancing circulation. Traditionally, it has been used in Chinese and Korean herbal practices to treat chest pain, high blood pressure, and nervous disorders, while modern wellness applications include supporting cardiovascular health and promoting emotional balance. Its unique feature is the presence of a rare active compound called vitexin, which may offer additional therapeutic benefits, making it a distinctive and valuable herb in both traditional and contemporary herbal medicine.
