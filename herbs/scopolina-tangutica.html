What is Scopolina tangutica?

Scopolina tangutica is an annual or biennial plant, native to China, belonging to the family Solanaceae.

It is characterized by its elliptical to ovate leaves, 2-8 cm long and 1-5 cm wide, with a wavy or crenate margin, and a glabrous to sparsely hairy surface. The flowers are small, bell-shaped, and greenish-yellow in color, 3-4 cm long, with 5-6 lobes, and are arranged in an umbel-like inflorescence. The plant produces small, globose fruits, 4-5 mm in diameter, containing numerous seeds.

The plant contains scopolin, a tropane alkaloid, which is also found in other members of the Solanaceae family.
