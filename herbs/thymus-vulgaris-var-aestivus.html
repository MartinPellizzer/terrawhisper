Thymus vulgaris var. aestivus

Thymus vulgaris var. aestivus, commonly known as summer thyme, is a variant of the thyme plant that thrives in warmer climates and is used for its medicinal properties.

The plant has been found to have various health benefits, including its antimicrobial and antifungal properties, which can aid in the prevention of infections and promote wound healing.

The therapeutic actions of summer thyme include its ability to act as an expectorant, relieving respiratory issues such as coughs and congestion, and its antioxidant properties, which can help reduce inflammation and improve overall well-being.

The bioactive constituents of summer thyme include thymol, carvacrol, and other compounds that contribute to its medicinal properties, making it a valuable herb in traditional medicine.

Possible Side Effects of Thymus vulgaris var. aestivus

Additionally, using the plant in high concentrations or for extended periods can cause liver and kidney damage, as well as interact with certain medications.

In severe cases, improper use of Thymus vulgaris var. aestivus can also cause seizures, respiratory failure, and even death.
