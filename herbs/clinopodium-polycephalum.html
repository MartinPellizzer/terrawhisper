Clinopodium polycephalum

What's the taxonomical classification of Clinopodium polycephalum?

Clinopodium polycephalum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Lamiales. It is a member of the family Lamiaceae, which is characterized by its distinct botanical features, and is specifically identified by the genus Clinopodium and the species polycephalum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clinopodium polycephalum has upright, herbaceous stems that reach heights of approximately 30 to 60 centimeters and feature small, opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is characterized by a soft, slightly pubescent texture and a pale green coloration. Its inflorescence consists of dense, terminal clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically light pink or lilac in hue. These flowers emerge from axillary arrangements and are supported by slender pedicels. The plant overall presents a compact and bushy growth habit suitable for various meadow environments.
