Swertia emeiensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Swertia emeiensis?

Swertia emeiensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is positioned within the order Gentianales. It further falls under the family Gentianaceae, which defines its botanical relationships. Ultimately, its specific taxonomic identity is established by its placement in the genus Swertia, specifically as the species emeiensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Swertia emeiensis has opposite, decussate leaves that are typically elliptic to lanceolate in shape with entire margins and acute apices. The foliage is characterized by a smooth texture and prominent pinnate venation that extends toward the leaf edges. Its woody stems support small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in the axils of the leaves. These flowers possess a tubular corolla structure typical of the Gentianaceae family. The plant eventually produces small, dehiscent capsules that contain numerous minute seeds for dispersal.
