Centotheca lappacea

What's the taxonomical classification of Centotheca lappacea?

Centotheca lappacea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Poales. As a member of the family Poaceae, it is further defined by its placement in the genus Centotheca, ultimately reaching its specific identification as the species lappacea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Centotheca lappacea has erect, perennial herbs characterized by long, slender stems that can reach heights of up to one meter.

The leaves are typically arranged in a basal rosette or along the stem, featuring a lanceolate shape with prominent parallel venation. Its inflorescence consists of terminal panicles that bear numerous small, inconspicuous flowers. The reproductive structures include delicate stamens and a superior ovary that eventually develops into small capsules.

These capsules split open to release tiny, numerous seeds essential for its propagation.
