Centotheca lappacea

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Centotheca lappacea has erect, perennial herbs characterized by long, slender stems that can reach heights of up to one meter. The leaves are typically arranged in a basal rosette or along the stem, featuring a lanceolate shape with prominent parallel venation. Its inflorescence consists of terminal panicles that bear numerous small, inconspicuous flowers. The reproductive structures include delicate stamens and a superior ovary that eventually develops into small capsules. These capsules split open to release tiny, numerous seeds essential for its propagation.
