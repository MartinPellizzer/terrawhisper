What is Borinda strigosa?

Borinda strigosa is a species of bamboo in the family Poaceae, characterized by its slender culms (stems) that can reach up to 6 meters in height and 2-3 cm in diameter, with a striated, glaucous sheath and a leaf blade that is linear-lanceolate, 10-25 cm long and 1-2 cm wide, with a pointed tip and a ciliate ligule.

The plant has a rhizome system with short internodes and a few tillers, and it produces dark green, lanceolate leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Borinda strigosa has a flowering habit, where the culms produce panicles that are 10-30 cm long, with spikelets that are 5-7 cm long and have two fertile florets. The species is native to the Himalayan region and is commonly found in subtropical forests at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

The plant is a perennial, deciduous species that grows in a clump or patch.
