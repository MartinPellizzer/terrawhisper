What is Matelea greenmanii?

Matelea greenmanii is a species of flowering plant in the family Apocynaceae, commonly known as the dogbane or oleander family.

It is a climbing vine, native to Mexico, characterized by its glabrous, green stems and branches with simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic or ovate in shape, measuring 3-10 cm in length, and 1-4 cm in width, with a pointed tip and entire margin. The leaves are petiolate, with a short, winged petiole. Matelea greenmanii produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in axillary cymes, which are unisexual and dioecious, with 5-merous corolla and a 5-lobed calyx.

The plant's fruit is a follicle, 5-angled and 3-10 cm long, containing several seeds.
