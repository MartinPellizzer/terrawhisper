Saussurea erubescens

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea erubescens has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by a thick, woody rhizome and basal leaves that are lanceolate to oblong with entire or slightly dentate margins. The stem is typically erect and unbranched, reaching heights that support a terminal inflorescence. This inflorescence consists of a compact, head-like capitulum surrounded by several rows of overlapping, lanceolate involucral bracts. The individual florets are tubular and typically exhibit a reddish or purplish hue, which gives the plant its specific epithet. Small, inconspicuous phyllaries protect the developing flower heads throughout the growing season.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude alpine regions of Western China, specifically within the provinces of Sichuan, Yunnan, Gansu, Qinghai, and Tibet. It typically flourishes in subalpine meadows and rocky slopes located at elevations ranging from 3,000 to 4,500 meters above sea level. Its distribution is closely tied to the specific climatic conditions found within the Hengduan Mountains and the eastern edge of the Tibetan Plateau. Because it requires cool, moist environments with intense sunlight, its range is fragmented into specific mountain pockets. These localized habitats restrict the species to certain ecological niches within its broader regional presence.
