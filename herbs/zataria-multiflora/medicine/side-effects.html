10 Possible Side Effects Of Zataria Multiflora

Zataria multiflora, also known as Greek thyme, has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, allergic reactions, and skin irritation.

These side effects can be caused by excessive consumption, individual sensitivity, or using it in combination with other medications.

For instance, someone may experience worsening digestive problems, such as diarrhea or stomach pain, which can disrupt daily life and work activities, leading to decreased productivity and overall well-being.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Zataria multiflora, also known as Greek thyme, causes allergic reactions due to its potent compounds such as flavonoids and terpenes.

These bioactive molecules can trigger an immune response in some individuals, leading to symptoms like skin irritation, hives, and respiratory issues.

The high concentration of essential oils in Zataria multiflora extracts may also contribute to these adverse effects, causing an allergic reaction in susceptible people.

2. Induces dizziness attacks

Zataria multiflora, also known as Greek thyme, induces dizziness attacks due to its active compounds such as flavonoids and phenolic acids, which can cause a drop in blood pressure.

This may lead to feelings of lightheadedness, vertigo, or even fainting episodes.

Additionally, the herb's ability to slow down heart rate and reduce cardiac output can also contribute to the dizziness experienced by some individuals when using Zataria multiflora.

3. Triggers asthma episodes

Zataria multiflora, also known as Greek thyme, triggers asthma episodes due to its ability to cause bronchospasms and increased airway resistance.

The plant's essential oils can stimulate the release of histamine, a chemical that constricts airways, making it difficult for people with asthma to breathe.

Additionally, the inhalation of Zataria multiflora's particles can irritate the lungs, further exacerbating symptoms in individuals already prone to asthma attacks.

4. Increases blood pressure

Zataria multiflora, also known as Greek thyme, increases blood pressure due to its stimulatory effect on the body's cardiovascular system.

The essential oils present in this herb, particularly thymol and borneol, can cause a rise in blood pressure by stimulating the sympathetic nervous system, leading to an increase in heart rate and cardiac output.

This reaction is often seen in individuals with pre-existing hypertension or those who are sensitive to the plant's active compounds.

5. Stimulates anxiety disorders

Zataria multiflora, also known as Greek thyme, stimulates anxiety disorders due to its high concentration of essential oils such as thymol and carvacrol.

These oils can affect the brain's neurotransmitters, leading to increased feelings of nervousness and unease.

Additionally, the plant's stimulatory properties can exacerbate existing anxiety conditions, causing individuals to feel overwhelmed and distressed.

6. Produces skin rashes

Zataria multiflora, also known as Greek thyme, produces skin rashes.

This is due to its high concentration of essential oils, particularly thymol and carvacrol, which can cause irritation and allergic reactions on the skin. The antioxidants present in the herb can also trigger an immune response, leading to the development of rashes.

Additionally, some individuals may be more prone to reacting to Zataria multiflora due to their genetic makeup or previous exposure to similar substances.

7. Leads to insomnia

Zataria multiflora, also known as Greek thyme, leads to insomnia when its essential oils, such as borneol and camphor, interact with the body's nervous system.

These compounds can cause an increase in alertness and energy, making it difficult for individuals to fall asleep.

Additionally, the herb's stimulatory properties may interfere with the body's natural sleep-wake cycle, leading to restlessness and disrupted sleep patterns.

8. Activates stomach ulcers

Zataria multiflora, also known as Greek thyme, activates stomach ulcers due to its ability to stimulate gastric acid secretion and relax the lower esophageal sphincter.

This can lead to a reflux of acidic stomach contents back up into the esophagus, causing irritation and potentially forming an ulcer.

Additionally, Zataria multiflora's high concentration of thymol, a phenolic compound, may also contribute to its ulcerogenic effects by damaging the mucous membrane lining the stomach and intestines.

9. Enhances heart palpitations

Zataria multiflora, also known as Greek thyme, enhances heart palpitations due to its high content of essential oils such as thymol and carvacrol.

These compounds can stimulate the cardiovascular system, increasing heart rate and blood pressure, which may lead to an enhancement of normal heartbeat sensations, resulting in palpitations.

Additionally, Zataria multiflora's potential interactions with other herbs or medications that regulate heart rhythm may also contribute to this side effect.

10. Affects mental clarity

Zataria multiflora, also known as Greek thyme, affects mental clarity.

This is due to the presence of thymol, a compound that can interact with neurotransmitters in the brain, leading to altered cognitive function and mood changes. Thymol's ability to stimulate the nervous system may cause feelings of anxiety, restlessness, or difficulty concentrating.

Additionally, Zataria multiflora's volatile oils may affect the brain's serotonin levels, potentially contributing to mental fogginess or disorientation.
