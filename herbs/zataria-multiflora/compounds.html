Zataria (Zataria multiflora) Compounds

The primary compounds of Zataria multiflora include essential oil, flavonoids, terpenoids, and coumarin. These compounds contribute to its medicinal properties, such as anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Phenolic acids, tannins, and glycosides also play roles in its therapeutic applications. Saponins, alkaloids, and anthocyanins further enhance its pharmacological profile.

Zataria (Zataria multiflora) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Zataria multiflora is a key active compound known for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It contains thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its therapeutic effects in traditional herbal medicine. This oil is commonly used to treat respiratory infections and digestive issues.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Zataria multiflora are bioactive compounds known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments. They also support immune function and may have potential in modern pharmacological applications.

3. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Zataria multiflora, a medicinal plant used in herbal medicine. These compounds contribute to the plant's aromatic properties and are believed to have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. They are among the active constituents responsible for the therapeutic benefits attributed to Zataria multiflora.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Zataria multiflora. It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Found in essential oils, coumarin supports the therapeutic uses of Zataria in traditional herbal medicine. Its presence enhances the plant's effectiveness in treating respiratory and digestive ailments.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Zataria multiflora, a medicinal plant used in traditional herbal medicine. It contributes to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, making it valuable for treating various health conditions. This compound is particularly noted for its role in reducing oxidative stress and supporting immune function.

6. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Zataria multiflora, known for its astringent properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating digestive issues and inflammation. Tannins in Zataria also exhibit antimicrobial and antioxidant effects, supporting its role in herbal medicine.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Zataria multiflora. It contributes to the plant's therapeutic properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. This compound is often extracted and used in herbal formulations for its medicinal benefits. Glycoside plays a significant role in the pharmacological profile of Zataria multiflora.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound found in Zataria multiflora. It exhibits antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, contributing to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments. Saponin's ability to disrupt cell membranes enhances its therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

9. Alkaloid

Zataria multiflora contains the alkaloid beta-elemene, which exhibits anti-inflammatory and anticancer properties. This compound is derived from the plant's essential oils and has been studied for its potential therapeutic applications in herbal medicine.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Zataria multiflora. It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, which may support immune function and reduce inflammation. This compound is part of the plant's natural defense mechanisms and is valued in traditional herbal medicine for its therapeutic potential.
