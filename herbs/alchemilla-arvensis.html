Lady's Mantle

Alchemilla arvensis

Lady's Mantle (Alchemilla arvensis) is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, known for its distinctive shield-shaped leaves and medicinal properties. It has been traditionally used to support women's health, particularly in treating menstrual disorders and promoting uterine health.

Botanical Identification

Alchemilla arvensis, commonly known as Lady's Mantle, belongs to the Rosaceae family. It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features succulent leaves arranged in a shield-like pattern and yellow flowers. Synonyms include Alchemilla vulgaris and Alchemilla mollis in some herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Eudicots
  • Order: Rosales
  • Family: Rosaceae
  • Genus: Alchemilla
  • Species: Alchemilla arvensis

Lady's Mantle contains key active compounds such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and tannins, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to provide antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting its use in herbal medicine.

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for digestive issues and skin irritation due to its mild astringent properties.

Decoction: Used for respiratory ailments and as a general tonic due to its warming properties.

Poultice: Used for topical applications on wounds and skin infections due to its anti-inflammatory effects.

Safety Profile

Alchemilla arvensis is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal upset in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy due to potential uterine stimulation effects. Always consult a healthcare professional before use, especially for prolonged periods or in high doses.
