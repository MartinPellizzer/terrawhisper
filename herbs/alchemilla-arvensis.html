Alchemilla Arvensis Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Alchemilla arvensis, commonly known as field sorrel, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers.

It is valued for its mild tart flavor and has been used traditionally for its potential health benefits, including supporting digestive health and reducing inflammation.

The herb contains bioactive constituents such as flavonoids, tannins, and mucilage, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

Alchemilla arvensis can be prepared as an infusion, decoction, or poultice, making it versatile for various herbal remedies.

It may also result in eye irritation, such as redness, tearing, or a burning sensation, if the herb comes into contact with the eyes. Furthermore, the herb has been associated with stomach upset and digestive issues, including bloating, cramping, or diarrhea.
