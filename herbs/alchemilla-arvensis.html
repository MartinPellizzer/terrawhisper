What is Lady'S Mantle (Alchemilla Arvensis)?

Lady's Mantle, or Alchemilla arvensis, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, commonly found in moist, shaded areas and is known for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal medicine.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Lady's Mantle is Alchemilla arvensis. This plant belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Rosales, family Rosaceae, genus Alchemilla, and species Alchemilla arvensis. It is commonly known as Lady's Mantle in Europe and as Buckram Plant in parts of North America. The synonyms for this plant include Alchemilla vulgaris and Alchemilla mollis, which are both botanical and regional names used in different parts of the world.

Safety, Side Effects, and Contraindications

When used appropriately, Lady's Mantle is generally considered safe, but it may cause mild side effects such as stomach upset, nausea, or skin irritation in some individuals.
