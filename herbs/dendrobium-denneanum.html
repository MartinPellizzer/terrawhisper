Dendrobium denneanum

What's the taxonomical classification of Dendrobium denneanum?

Dendrobium denneanum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Asparagales. This plant is a member of the family Orchidaceae, which places it within the genus Dendrobium, and its specific biological identity is defined by its species name, denneanum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dendrobium denneanum has sympodial growth with elongated, reed-like pseudobulbs that are distinctly compressed and often possess a purplish hue. These stems are typically several decimeters long and feature numerous closely spaced nodes along their length. The leaves are relatively small, lanceolate, and emerge from the upper portion of the pseudobulbs. During its flowering period, the plant produces small, fragrant flowers that emerge from the upper nodes. These blossoms are characterized by their delicate structure and subtle color variations.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical rainforests and montane forests of New Guinea, including the Bismarck Archipelago and the Solomon Islands. Its distribution is closely tied to the moist, shaded habitats characteristic of these equatorial ecosystems.
