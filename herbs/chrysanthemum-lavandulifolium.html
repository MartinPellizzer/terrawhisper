Chrysanthemum lavandulifolium

What's the taxonomical classification of Chrysanthemum lavandulifolium?

Chrysanthemum lavandulifolium belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. This plant is a representative of the family Asteraceae, a diverse group of flowering plants, and is specifically categorized under the genus Chrysanthemum. Its unique scientific identity is finalized by its species designation, lavandulifolium.

Chrysanthemum lavandulifolium contains a complex profile of bioactive secondary metabolites including flavonoids, terpenoids, phenolic acids, and essential oils. These phytochemicals work synergistically to provide therapeutic potential in traditional medicinal applications. What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat fever, headache, dizziness, and various inflammatory conditions. Its medicinal properties are often utilized to alleviate symptoms of the common cold and flu. Practitioners frequently apply its extracts to reduce swelling and soothe skin irritations. The botanical components are also believed to assist in regulating blood pressure and supporting cardiovascular health. These diverse therapeutic applications make it a significant element in traditional herbal medicine.
