What's the taxonomical classification of Chrysanthemum lavandulifolium?

Chrysanthemum lavandulifolium belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. This plant is a representative of the family Asteraceae, a diverse group of flowering plants, and is specifically categorized under the genus Chrysanthemum. Its unique scientific identity is finalized by its species designation, lavandulifolium.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Chrysanthemum lavandulifolium has narrow, linear leaves that closely resemble those of the lavender plant, typically measuring between 1 and 3 centimeters in width. Small, daisy-like flower heads emerge from the terminal ends of the branches, featuring central disc florets surrounded by ray florets.
