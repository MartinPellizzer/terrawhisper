Cocoa Tree (Theobroma cacao)

Cocoa Tree (Theobroma cacao) is a member of the Sterculiaceae family, native to South America, Central America, and Caribbean. Traditionally, its seeds, fruits, and bark have been used for culinary uses, infusions, and decoctions.

This herb is particularly valued for its bitter, stimulant, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, native american herbal medicine, and traditional chinese medicine.

Perennial herbaceous plant.

Leaves
Simple leaves with a dark green upper surface and a lighter green lower surface, featuring prominent stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Inflorescences bearing small yellowish-green flowers arranged in a raceme, actinomorphic flowers with five sepals and five petals, each petal lacking distinct markings.
Stems
Erect, woody, unbranched, with smooth, glabrous surfaces and persistent, woody lenticels.
