Cocoa tree

Theobroma cacao

Cocoa Tree (Theobroma cacao) is a member of the Sterculiaceae family, native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. Traditionally, various plant parts such as seeds, leaves, and bark have been used in local medicine, primarily for their stimulant properties and for treating fatigue. It has also been incorporated into traditional practices for cardiovascular health.

Botanical Identification

Theobroma cacao is a species of flowering plant in the Malvaceae family. Commonly known as the cocoa tree, it is a perennial plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas. It has large, glossy leaves and produces yellow flowers, with various regional names used in herbal traditions. Synonyms in herbal literature include "cacao tree" and "cocoa bean plant."

Preparation Methods

Extract: Used for digestive issues and inflammation due to its bioactive compounds.

Decoction: Used for respiratory conditions and to aid digestion.

Infusion: Used for calming effects and to help with anxiety and sleep disorders.

Safety Profile

The cocoa tree is generally safe when used in recommended amounts. However, excessive consumption may cause gastrointestinal discomfort. It is contraindicated for individuals with hypertension due to its stimulant properties. Always consult a healthcare professional before use, especially during pregnancy or with existing health conditions.
