What's the taxonomical classification of Rubus malifolius?

Rubus malifolius belongs to the kingdom Plantae, situated within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Rosales. It is categorized under the family Rosaceae, a group well-known for its diverse flowering plants, and is specifically classified under the genus Rubus. Finally, its unique biological identity is defined by its specific designation as the species malifolius.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rubus malifolius has a woody, perennial growth habit characterized by arching canes that feature small, sharp prickles along their stems. The leaves are typically trifoliate or palmately lobed with serrated margins and a slightly pubescent texture on the undersides. Flowers are small and white, emerging from the axils of the leaves during the growing season. These blossoms eventually develop into aggregate fruits that transition from green to a deep red or black color when ripe. The overall structure of the plant allows it to form dense, scrambling thickets in its natural habitat.
