Dysosma difformis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dysosma difformis has erect, slender stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 80 centimeters and feature basal leaves that are long-petioled while the upper leaves are sessile. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are pale yellow or greenish in color, hanging from delicate pedicels. Its leaves are arranged alternately along the stem and possess ovate or lanceolate shapes with finely serrated margins. The fruit consists of small, rounded capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds. This herbaceous perennial often grows in moist habitats where its delicate structure allows it to thrive among denser vegetation.

    What are the herbal preparations of Dysosma difformis?

    Dysosma difformis is prepared primarily as a decoction or infusion using the dried rhizomes and roots of the plant. These liquid extracts are the most common methods used to access the active constituents found within the underground structures.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Dysosma difformis can cause skin irritation, localized swelling, and allergic contact dermatitis upon direct contact with its tissues. Individuals sensitive to its chemical components may experience redness or itching where the plant touches the skin. If the sap is accidentally ingested, it may lead to gastrointestinal distress such as nausea or stomach discomfort. Some reports suggest that prolonged exposure to the plant's juices could result in more significant inflammatory responses. It is important to use protective gear when handling this species to avoid these adverse reactions.
