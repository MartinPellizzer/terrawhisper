What's the taxonomical classification of Carex finitima?

Carex finitima belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Poales. This plant is a member of the family Cyperaceae and is further categorized within the genus Carex, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species finitima.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Carex finitima has a perennial growth habit characterized by slender, erect stems that reach heights typically between 10 and 30 centimeters.

The leaves are narrow and linear, often appearing somewhat involute with a texture that ranges from slightly scabrous to smooth. Its inflorescence consists of several small, compact spikes arranged along the upper portion of the culm. The perigynia are notably small and elliptical, containing a single seed protected by a thin membrane.

These morphological features collectively distinguish this species from other members of the Carex genus found in similar alpine or subalpine habitats.

        Carex finitima is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice depending on the specific therapeutic application required.         What side-effects this plant can have?

        Carex finitima can cause localized skin irritation, redness, and itching upon direct contact with the foliage. While specific toxicological data is limited for this exact species, many members of the Carex genus contain compounds that may trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Physical contact with the sharp edges of the leaves can also lead to minor abrasions or small cuts on the skin. Some people may experience a mild allergic reaction if the plant's juices come into contact with open wounds. It is generally advisable to wear protective gloves when handling this plant to prevent these dermatological issues.
