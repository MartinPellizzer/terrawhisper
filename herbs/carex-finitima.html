Carex finitima

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Carex finitima has a perennial growth habit characterized by slender, erect stems that reach heights typically between 10 and 30 centimeters. The leaves are narrow and linear, often appearing somewhat involute with a texture that ranges from slightly scabrous to smooth. Its inflorescence consists of several small, compact spikes arranged along the upper portion of the culm. The perigynia are notably small and elliptical, containing a single seed protected by a thin membrane. These morphological features collectively distinguish this species from other members of the Carex genus found in similar alpine or subalpine habitats.
