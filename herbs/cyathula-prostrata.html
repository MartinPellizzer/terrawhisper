As discussed in a study, the methanolic extract of Cyathula prostrata was evaluated for its anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antioxidant activities. In in vivo anti-inflammatory assays, the extract at doses of 50, 100, and 200mg/kg showed a significant (P<0.05) dose-dependent inhibition in the carrageenan, arachidonic acid, and xylene-induced tests. The plant also produced a significant (P<0.05) dose-dependent inhibition in the acetic acid analgesic test and a significant (P<0.001) dose-dependent inhibition in the hot plate analgesic test. However, the extract did not show inhibitory activity in the in vitro PMA-induced reactive oxygen species, LPS-induced nitric oxide production, or LPS-induced COX-2 expression assays. Additionally, the plant extract did not exhibit any antioxidant activity in the DPPH or lipid peroxidation assays.
