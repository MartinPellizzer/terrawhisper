Parnassia palustris

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Parnassia palustris has a basal rosette of long, narrow, lanceolate leaves that are bright green and possess smooth margins. These leaves emerge from a short rhizome and form a dense cluster close to the ground. From the center of this rosette, a single, slender, leafless stalk rises to support a solitary flower. The flower is characterized by five white, rounded petals and a distinctive central structure of many yellow, nectar-secreting glands. Each petal typically features fine, dark veins that radiate toward the center of the bloom.
