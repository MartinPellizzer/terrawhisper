Parnassia palustris

What's the taxonomical classification of Parnassia palustris?

Parnassia palustris belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within its hierarchical classification, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its evolutionary lineage continues through the order Celastrales and the family Celastraceae. Finally, the plant is classified under the genus Parnassia, with its specific identification being the species palustris.

      What are the herbal preparations of Parnassia palustris?

      Parnassia palustris is prepared primarily as a decoction, infusion, or tincture using the dried aerial parts of the plant. These liquid preparations are often used in traditional herbalism to extract specific bioactive compounds from the leaves and stems. Tinctures made with alcohol are particularly valued for their ability to preserve the plant's properties for long-term storage and concentrated dosing. Some practitioners also utilize the dried herb in tea form for mild therapeutic applications. The selection of the preparation method often depends on the intended medicinal use and the desired strength of the extract.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Parnassia palustris can cause skin irritation or allergic reactions in sensitive individuals if the plant comes into direct contact with the body. While there is no extensive clinical documentation regarding systemic toxicity, caution is advised when handling the foliage. Some people may experience localized redness or itching upon touching the leaves or flowers. It is generally recommended to avoid ingestion because the chemical composition of the plant is not fully studied for human safety. Always consult a medical professional if any adverse symptoms occur after exposure.
