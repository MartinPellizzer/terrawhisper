Parnassia palustris

What's the taxonomical classification of Parnassia palustris?

Parnassia palustris belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within its hierarchical classification, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its evolutionary lineage continues through the order Celastrales and the family Celastraceae. Finally, the plant is classified under the genus Parnassia, with its specific identification being the species palustris.

What are the therapeutic actions of Parnassia palustris?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat urinary tract infections, kidney stones, and bladder inflammation. It contains various phytochemicals that serve as natural diuretics to help flush the renal system. Historically, herbalists utilized its properties to soothe irritated mucous membranes within the urinary tract. The plant's ability to promote fluid excretion makes it a traditional remedy for preventing stone formation.
