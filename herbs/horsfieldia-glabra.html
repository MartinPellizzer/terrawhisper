Horsfieldia glabra

What's the taxonomical classification of Horsfieldia glabra?

Horsfieldia glabra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Magnoliales. This plant is a member of the family Myristicaceae, specifically categorized within the genus Horsfieldia, and is identified by its unique species name, glabra.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Horsfieldia glabra contains various bioactive phytochemicals including terpenoids, flavonoids, and phenolic compounds that exhibit significant pharmacological potential. Researchers have identified specific triterpenoids within the plant that contribute to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Some studies suggest that the extracts may possess antimicrobial activities capable of inhibiting certain bacterial strains. The presence of diverse secondary metabolites makes the plant a subject of interest for developing new natural therapeutic agents. These chemical constituents work synergistically to provide a broad spectrum of medicinal benefits in traditional applications.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat digestive ailments, skin infections, fever, and various inflammatory conditions. The leaves and bark are often prepared into decoctions or topical applications to alleviate symptoms of stomach distress. Its bioactive compounds are also applied to soothe localized skin irritation and promote healing. Researchers investigate these medicinal properties to better understand how the plant interacts with systemic inflammation. These traditional uses highlight its role in diverse ethnomedical practices.
