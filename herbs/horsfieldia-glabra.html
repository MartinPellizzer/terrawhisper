Horsfieldia glabra

What's the taxonomical classification of Horsfieldia glabra?

Horsfieldia glabra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Magnoliales. This plant is a member of the family Myristicaceae, specifically categorized within the genus Horsfieldia, and is identified by its unique species name, glabra.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Horsfieldia glabra has simple, alternate, elliptical leaves that are glabrous on both surfaces and possess entire margins.

The leaf texture is typically coriaceous, meaning it has a leathery feel when handled. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the branches, often appearing in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that transition in color as they reach maturity.

These morphological traits distinguish it from other species within the Lauraceae family.
