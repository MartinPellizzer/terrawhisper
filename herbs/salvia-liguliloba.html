Salvia liguliloba

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Salvia liguliloba has square stems, opposite leaves with serrated margins, and bilabiate flowers characterized by a distinctively lobed lower lip. The foliage is typically lanceolate or ovate, often featuring a textured surface due to prominent venation. Its inflorescence consists of verticillasters arranged along an elongated spike, bearing tubular corollas that transition from pale hues to deeper tones. The calyx is frequently pubescent, providing a protective structure for the developing floral organs. These morphological traits collectively distinguish the species within its genus.
