Colysis hemitoma

What's the taxonomical classification of Colysis hemitoma?

Colysis hemitoma belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it is further organized into the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. Taxonomically, this plant is situated within the family Polypodiaceae, falling under the genus Colysis, and is specifically identified by its species name, hemitoma.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Colysis hemitoma has a creeping rhizome that is typically clothed in scales and produces pinnate fronds that can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

The pinnae are arranged along the rachis and exhibit a distinctively lobed or deeply incised margin. Each segment of the leaf is characterized by a somewhat fleshy texture and a dark green coloration. The sori, which contain the spores, are positioned along the margins of the pinnules and are protected by specialized structures called indusia.

This specific arrangement of reproductive organs is a key identifying feature of the species.
