Colysis hemitoma

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Colysis hemitoma has a creeping rhizome that is typically clothed in scales and produces pinnate fronds that can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters. The pinnae are arranged along the rachis and exhibit a distinctively lobed or deeply incised margin. Each segment of the leaf is characterized by a somewhat fleshy texture and a dark green coloration. The sori, which contain the spores, are positioned along the margins of the pinnules and are protected by specialized structures called indusia. This specific arrangement of reproductive organs is a key identifying feature of the species.
