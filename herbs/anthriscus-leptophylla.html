Bioactive Constituents of Anthriscus leptophylla

Anthriscus leptophylla has many active constituents, such as alkaloids, glycosides, and terpenoids, which possess anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties.

Medicinal Parts of Anthriscus leptophylla

Anthriscus leptophylla has many medicinal parts, such as the roots, which contain sesquiterpene lactones and coumarins, the leaves that contain flavonoids and phenolic acids, and the flowers, which are rich in essential oils, including apigenin and luteolin.

The seeds of the plant contain fatty acids and glycosides, which have been traditionally used in folk medicine.

The essential oils extracted from the flowers have been found to possess antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, making them useful in the treatment of various health conditions.

Additionally, the roots and leaves of Anthriscus leptophylla have been used to treat digestive issues, such as indigestion and diarrhea, due to their antispasmodic and carminative properties.

Possible Side Effects of Anthriscus leptophylla

Anthriscus leptophylla can have side effects if used improperly, such as causing excessive bleeding or gastrointestinal issues when ingested in large quantities, potentially leading to symptoms like diarrhea, vomiting, and stomach pain.

If used topically without proper dilution, anthriscus leptophylla's oils may cause skin burns, redness, and inflammation.
