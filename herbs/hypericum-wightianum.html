What's the taxonomical classification of Hypericum wightianum?

Hypericum wightianum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is positioned in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Malpighiales. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the family Hypericaceae, which contains the genus Hypericum, ultimately leading to its specific identification as the species wightianum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hypericum wightianum has small, woody, spreading stems that produce narrow, lanceolate leaves arranged in opposite pairs along the branches. Its flowers are characterized by bright yellow petals and numerous prominent stamens that form a central cluster. These blossoms are often solitary or appear in small clusters at the ends of the stems. Small, globose capsules develop following pollination to house the plant's seeds.
