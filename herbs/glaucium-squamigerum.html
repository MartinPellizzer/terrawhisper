Glaucium squamigerum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Glaucium squamigerum has a woody, perennial habit characterized by a taproot system and a rosette of basal leaves that are often covered in small, scale-like structures. The leaves are typically lanceolate or oblong in shape and possess a somewhat glaucous or waxy surface texture. During the flowering stage, the plant produces terminal inflorescences consisting of solitary, large flower heads atop long, leafless stalks. These flowers feature prominent, spreading ray florets that surround a central disc of tubular florets. The involucral bracts are notable for their imbricate arrangement, which contributes to the species' specific morphological identity.
