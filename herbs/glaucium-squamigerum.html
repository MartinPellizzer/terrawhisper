Glaucium squamigerum

What's the taxonomical classification of Glaucium squamigerum?

Glaucium squamigerum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Ranunculales. This plant is a member of the Papaveraceae family, falling under the genus Glaucium, with the specific species designation squamigerum.

      What are the herbal preparations of Glaucium squamigerum?

      Glaucium squamigerum is prepared as a decoction, a tincture, or a topical poultice for medicinal application. The plant material is typically boiled in water to extract its active alkaloids for internal use. When formulated as a tincture, the dried plant parts are steeped in alcohol to create a concentrated liquid extract. For external ailments, the crushed fresh leaves are applied directly to the skin as a poultice to soothe inflammation. These various methods allow the plant's properties to be delivered according to the specific therapeutic need.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Glaucium squamigerum can cause skin irritation, localized dermatitis, and potential gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Contact with the plant's sap or foliage may trigger redness, itching, or swelling on the skin of sensitive individuals. If the plant is consumed, it might lead to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain due to its chemical composition. While specific toxicological studies are limited, caution should be exercised when handling any part of the specimen. These adverse reactions highlight the importance of wearing protective gloves during any form of interaction with the species.
