Selaginella uncinata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Selagineella uncinata has branched, creeping rhizomes that produce small, scale-like leaves arranged in four distinct rows along the stems. The leaves are diminutive and characteristically recurved or hooked at the tips, which gives the species its specific epithet. Each leaf is composed of a single cell layer and lacks a complex midrib, typical of many lycophytes. The sporophylls are specialized leaves that house the sporangia, which are often positioned near the stem junctions. These structural features allow the plant to spread across moist substrates through its creeping growth habit.

What are the therapeutic actions of Selaginella uncinata?

Selaginella uncinata has diuretic, emmenagogue, and hemostatic properties that are utilized in traditional medicinal practices. As a diuretic, the plant helps increase urine production to assist in the elimination of excess fluids and toxins from the body. Its emmenagogue qualities allow it to stimulate or increase menstrual flow, which is often used to regulate reproductive cycles. Furthermore, the plant acts as a hemostatic agent to help control or stop bleeding in various physiological contexts. These diverse therapeutic actions make it a versatile component in herbal pharmacology.

    Selaginella uncinata is prepared primarily as a concentrated aqueous extract through decoction or as a fine powder for oral ingestion.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Selaginella uncinata can cause allergic skin reactions such as contact dermatitis, itching, or localized redness when touched by sensitive individuals. While scientific literature on human toxicity is limited, potential irritation of the mucous membranes may occur if plant extracts come into contact with the eyes or mouth. Some users might experience gastrointestinal discomfort if the plant is ingested without proper pharmacological processing. It is also possible that the chemical constituents could trigger unexpected hypersensitivity in people with existing botanical allergies. Because the specific toxicological profile is not fully established, cautious handling is recommended to avoid adverse biological responses.
