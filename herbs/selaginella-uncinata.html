What's the taxonomical classification of Selaginella uncinata?

Selaginella uncinata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Lycopodiidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Selaginellales and the family Selaginellaceae, eventually reaching its specific placement within the genus Selaginella. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its unique species name, uncinata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Selagineella uncinata has branched, creeping rhizomes that produce small, scale-like leaves arranged in four distinct rows along the stems. The leaves are diminutive and characteristically recurved or hooked at the tips, which gives the species its specific epithet. Each leaf is composed of a single cell layer and lacks a complex midrib, typical of many lycophytes. The sporophylls are specialized leaves that house the sporangia, which are often positioned near the stem junctions. These structural features allow the plant to spread across moist substrates through its creeping growth habit.
