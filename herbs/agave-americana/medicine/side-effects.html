10 Possible Side Effects Of Agave Americana (Century Plant)

Agave americana has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, allergic reactions, and skin irritation.

Causes of these side effects include consuming large quantities of the plant's sap, using it without proper preparation, or having individual sensitivities.

Examples include digestive problems that hinder daily activities, skin conditions that cause discomfort, and allergic reactions that can lead to anaphylaxis.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Agave americana if used imporperly.

When in contact with the skin, these compounds can cause allergic reactions, redness, itching, and burning sensations, leading to discomfort and even blisters or rashes.

The high concentration of these irritants in Agave americana's sap makes it essential to exercise caution when handling the plant or using its products to avoid adverse skin effects.
