Scyphocephalium mannii

What's the taxonomical classification of Scyphocephalium mannii?

Scyphocephalium mannii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Magnoliales. This plant is further classified into the family Myristicaceae, specifically within the genus Scyphocephalium, and is identified as the species mannii.

    What are the herbal preparations of Scyphocephalium mannii?

    Scyphocephalium mannii is prepared primarily as a decoction, a concentrated liquid infusion made by boiling the plant parts in water. This traditional method is used to extract the active medicinal compounds from the tougher tissues of the herb. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are currently limited, researchers suggest that contact with the plant's juices may trigger redness or itching in sensitive individuals. Some anecdotal evidence implies that accidental consumption could lead to nausea or stomach cramps. It is essential to avoid direct skin contact and prevent any oral exposure to mitigate these potential risks. Always consult a medical professional if you suspect a reaction has occurred after handling this species.
