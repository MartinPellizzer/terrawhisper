Phlomis umbrosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phlomis umbrosa has a perennial woody base with upright, square stems that are densely covered in soft, greyish-white felt-like hairs. The leaves are large, ovate to elliptical in shape, and possess a thick, velvety texture due to a dense layer of trichomes on both surfaces. These leaves are arranged in opposite pairs along the stem, creating a structured, layered appearance. The inflorescence consists of dense, whorled clusters of tubular flowers that emerge from the upper leaf axils. Each individual flower is typically yellowish or pale green, featuring a prominent hooded upper lip characteristic of the Lamiaceae family.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Phlomis umbrosa can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or localized swelling if the plant's sap or hairy leaves come into direct contact with sensitive skin. While specific toxicological studies on human ingestion are limited, the plant belongs to a genus that may contain compounds capable of causing gastrointestinal distress. Some individuals might experience redness or itching as an immune response to its physical textures. It is generally advisable to use gloves when handling the foliage to prevent these dermatological reactions. Always consult a medical professional if an unexpected reaction occurs after contact.
