Phlomis umbrosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phlomis umbrosa has a perennial woody base with upright, square stems that are densely covered in soft, greyish-white felt-like hairs. The leaves are large, ovate to elliptical in shape, and possess a thick, velvety texture due to a dense layer of trichomes on both surfaces. These leaves are arranged in opposite pairs along the stem, creating a structured, layered appearance. The inflorescence consists of dense, whorled clusters of tubular flowers that emerge from the upper leaf axils. Each individual flower is typically yellowish or pale green, featuring a prominent hooded upper lip characteristic of the Lamiaceae family.
