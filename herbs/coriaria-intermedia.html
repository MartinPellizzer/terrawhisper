What's the taxonomical classification of Coriaria intermedia?

Coriaria intermedia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is situated under the order Cucurbitales. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the family Coriariaceae and the genus Coriaria, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species intermedia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Coriaria intermedia has an erect or spreading shrubby habit characterized by small, leathery, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and arranged alternately along the stems. The foliage is often densely packed, creating a compact appearance common to many woody shrubs in this genus. Its flowers are small, inconspicuous, and borne in dense, greenish clusters that emerge from the leaf axils. The most distinctive morphological feature is the production of small, fleshy, dark purple to black drupes that serve as fruit. These berries are often found in crowded clusters along the branches, providing a stark visual contrast to the dark foliage.
