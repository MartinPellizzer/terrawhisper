Cyclocarya paliurus

What's the taxonomical classification of Cyclocarya paliurus?

Cyclocarya paliurus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the taxonomic lineage of the order Fagales. It is further nested within the family Juglandaceae, which places it among a diverse group of woody plants. Specifically, this plant is identified by the genus Cyclocarya and is distinguished as the species paliurus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cyclocarya paliurus has alternate, simple, and elliptic to oblong leaves that are typically leathery in texture with entire margins and pinnate venation. The foliage is characterized by a dark green upper surface and a slightly paler underside, often measuring several centimeters in length. Its woody stems exhibit a smooth to slightly lenticellate bark structure common to many members of the Sapindaceae family. The reproductive structures include small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into distinctive drupaceous fruits. These fruits are characterized by a fleshy part surrounding a single seed, which is a defining feature of its morphological profile.
