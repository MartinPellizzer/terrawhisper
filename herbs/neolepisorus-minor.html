Neolepisorus minor belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the family Polypodiaceae, which contains the genus Neolepisorus, ultimately identifying this specific organism as the species minor.

    What are the herbal preparations of Neolepisorus minor?

    Neolepisorus minor is prepared as decoctions, infusions, and topical poultices to harness its medicinal properties.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Neolepisorus minor can cause severe contact dermatitis, acute gastrointestinal distress, and localized respiratory irritation if its pollen or sap is inhaled or touched. The plant contains potent alkaloids that trigger intense itching and redness upon skin contact. If ingested, individuals may experience nausea, vomiting, and abdominal cramping as the body attempts to process the toxins. Some sensitive individuals might also develop mild swelling or hives shortly after exposure to the plant's oils. Careful avoidance is necessary to prevent these physiological reactions.
