Psilanthus mannii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Psilanthus mannii has a woody, perennial shrubby habit characterized by slender, upright stems and small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and leathery. The foliage is arranged oppositely along the branches, often featuring a slightly serrated margin. Its inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These flowers eventually develop into tiny, dry fruits that aid in the plant's dispersal. The overall structure of the plant is compact, allowing it to thrive in rocky or scrubland environments.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Psilanthus mannii can cause skin irritation, redness, and localized swelling if the plant comes into direct contact with human tissue.
