Lysimachia davurica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Lysimachia, with its specific designation being the species davurica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lysimachia davurica has creeping, rhizomatous stems that produce lanceolate to ovate leaves arranged in opposite pairs along the plant body. The foliage typically measures several centimeters in length and features smooth margins with a matte or slightly glossy surface texture. During the flowering period, the plant produces small, bright yellow, five-petaled flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These blossoms are characterized by their star-like shape and central yellow stamens. The overall growth habit is low-growing and spreading, allowing the plant to form dense groundcover mats.

What are the pharmacological activities of Lysimachia davurica?

This plant has acaricidal pharmacological activity, which means it possesses the ability to kill or repel mites and ticks. This specific biological property makes the species potentially useful in controlling various small arachnids that affect crops or livestock. Researchers study these bioactive compounds to understand how the plant's chemical constituents interact with the nervous or physiological systems of these pests. Such natural defense mechanisms highlight the potential for developing eco-friendly pesticides from botanical sources. Utilizing these properties can provide a sustainable alternative to synthetic chemical treatments in agricultural settings.

As discussed in a study, the toxicity of Lysimachia davurica leaf extracts was evaluated against adult Dermanyssus gallinae De Geer. This plant extract exhibited a 24h LD(50) value of 0.09 mgcm(-2). This level of toxicity was found to be almost comparable to that of benfuracarb, prothiofos, propoxur and fenthion, which had values of 0.053-0.070 mgcm(-2). The efficacy of these plant preparations was more pronounced in closed containers than in open ones during vapour phase toxicity tests. These results suggest that the mode of delivery for the extract was largely a result of action in the vapour phase.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds davuricoside D, davuricoside J, davuricoside L, davuricoside O, and oleanolic acid. These specific chemical constituents are recognized for their unique biological activities within the plant's chemical profile. The davuricoside group consists of several distinct glycosides that contribute to the plant's therapeutic potential. Additionally, the presence of oleanolic acid provides a pentacyclic triterpenoid component often studied for its various health benefits. Together, these substances define the complex pharmacological makeup of the species.

As discussed in a study, the whole plant of Lysimachia davurica Ledeb contains triterpenoid saponins. Researchers used column chromatography involving AB-8 macroporous resin, silica gel, and ODS to separate these compounds. Through the use of ES-MS, 1D and 2D NMR, and hydrolysis methods, two new triterpenoid saponins were isolated. These compounds are identified as 3beta, 16alpha, 28-trihydroxy-olean-12-en-3-0-(beta-D-glucopyranosyl-(1 --> 2)-beta-D-glucuronopyranosyl)-28-0-beta-D-glucopyranoside (I) and 3beta, 16alpha, 28-trihydroxy-olean-12-en-3-O-(beta-D-glucopyranosyl-(1 --> 2)-beta-D-glucuronopyranosyl)-28-0-beta-D-glucopyranoside (II). These new compounds are named as davuricoside D and davuricoside J.
