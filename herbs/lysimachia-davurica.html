Lysimachia davurica

What's the taxonomical classification of Lysimachia davurica?

Lysimachia davurica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It falls under the order Ericales and is a member of the family Primulaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Lysimachia, with its specific designation being the species davurica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lysimachia davurica has creeping, rhizomatous stems that produce lanceolate to ovate leaves arranged in opposite pairs along the plant body. The foliage typically measures several centimeters in length and features smooth margins with a matte or slightly glossy surface texture. During the flowering period, the plant produces small, bright yellow, five-petaled flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These blossoms are characterized by their star-like shape and central yellow stamens. The overall growth habit is low-growing and spreading, allowing the plant to form dense groundcover mats.

What are the pharmacological activities of Lysimachia davurica?

This plant has acaricidal, anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial pharmacological activities. The acaricidal properties allow the plant to effectively combat certain types of mites and ticks. Its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects suggest potential utility in reducing cellular oxidative stress and swelling in biological tissues. Additionally, the antimicrobial activity indicates that extracts from the plant can inhibit the growth of various pathogenic microorganisms. These diverse biological functions make Lysimachia davuricica a subject of interest in natural product research.

As discussed in a study, Lysimachia davurica leaf extracts demonstrated specific toxicity levels against adult Dermanyssus gallinae. The 24h LD(50) value for these leaf extracts was 0.09 mgcm(-2). This level of toxicity was almost comparable to the results seen for benfuracarb, prothiofoos, propoxur, and fenthion, which ranged from 0.053-0.070 mgcm(-2). These plant preparations were more effective in closed containers than in open ones during vapour phase toxicity tests. This indicates that the mode of delivery for the extract was largely a result of action in the vapour phase.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the specific medicinal compounds davuricoside D, davuricoside J, davuricoside L, davuricoside O, and various triterpenoid saponins. These bioactive molecules contribute to the diverse pharmacological profile observed in Lysimachia davuricia. Researchers often study these specific glycosides to understand their potential therapeutic applications in human health. The presence of triterpenoid saponins further enhances the chemical complexity of the species. Such detailed phytochemical analysis is essential for identifying the plant's medicinal properties.

According to a study published by "Yao xue xue bao = Acta pharmaceutica Sinica", the whole plant of Lysimachia davurica Ledeb contains triterpenoid saponins. Two new triterpenoid saponins were isolated from the plant using column chromatography involving AB-8 macroporous resin, silica gel, and ODS. These compounds were established as 3beta, 16alpha, 28-trihydroxy-olean-12-en-3-O-(beta-D-glucopyranosyl-(1 --> 2)-beta-D-glucuronopyranosyl)-28-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside (I) and 3beta, 16alpha, 28-trihydroxy-olean-12-en-3-O-(beta-D-glucopyranosyl-(1 --> 2)-beta-D-glucuronopyranosyl)-28-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside (II). The two new compounds are named as davuricoside D and davuricoside J.
