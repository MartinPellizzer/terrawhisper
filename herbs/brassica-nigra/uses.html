Black Mustard (Brassica nigra) Uses

Black Mustard (Brassica nigra) is used to treat respiratory conditions such as bronchitis, sinusitis, and cough. It helps alleviate symptoms like mucous congestion and inflamed lungs. Its anti-inflammatory properties reduce mucous membrane inflammation and sinus congestion. It is also beneficial for allergic rhinitis and other respiratory allergies.

Black Mustard (Brassica nigra) Monograph

1. Bronchitis

Black Mustard (Brassica nigra) has been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of bronchitis due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. The seeds and oil are often prepared as poultices or inhalants to help clear mucus and reduce airway inflammation. Its active compounds, such as glucosinolates, support respiratory health by soothing irritation and improving bronchial function.

2. Allergic rhinitis

Black Mustard (Brassica nigra) has been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistaminic properties. Its essential oils, particularly those containing thymol and carvacrol, help reduce nasal congestion and allergic reactions. It is often prepared as a nasal spray or inhalant to provide localized relief.

3. Respiratory allergies

Black Mustard (Brassica nigra) is used in herbal medicine to alleviate respiratory allergies due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties. It helps reduce symptoms like sneezing, runny nose, and nasal congestion. Typically prepared as a tincture or infusion, it supports immune response and respiratory health in allergy management.

4. Mucous membrane inflammation

Black Mustard (Brassica nigra) is used to reduce inflammation of mucous membranes due to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. It helps soothe irritated tissues in the respiratory and digestive tracts. Often prepared as a poultice or decoction, it provides relief for conditions like bronchitis and gastritis. Its active compounds, including glucosinolates, contribute to its therapeutic effects.

5. Cough

Black Mustard (Brassica nigra) is used in herbal medicine to alleviate coughs due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. The seeds, when prepared as a tea or tincture, help loosen mucus and reduce throat irritation, making it a traditional remedy for respiratory congestion.

6. Sinusitis

Black Mustard (Brassica nigra) is used in herbal medicine to treat sinusitis due to its anti-inflammatory and decongestant properties. It helps reduce nasal swelling and mucus buildup, easing breathing and relieving symptoms. Typically prepared as a nasal spray or steam inhalation, it supports natural drainage and soothes irritated sinuses.

7. Respiratory tract infections

Black Mustard (Brassica nigra) is used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health. Its expectorant properties help loosen mucus, while its anti-inflammatory effects may reduce symptoms of respiratory tract infections. It is often prepared as a poultice or infusion to alleviate congestion and soothe irritated airways.

8. Sinus congestion

Black Mustard (Brassica nigra) is used to alleviate sinus congestion due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps loosen mucus and reduce nasal swelling, providing relief from congestion and sinus pressure. Traditionally applied as a nasal poultice or inhalation, it supports respiratory clarity in herbal medicine practices.

9. Inflamed lungs

Black Mustard (Brassica nigra) has been traditionally used to address inflamed lungs due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. It helps reduce mucus buildup and soothe irritation in the respiratory tract, making it a valued herb in herbal medicine for conditions like bronchitis and coughs.

10. Mucous congestion

Black Mustard (Brassica nigra) is used to alleviate mucous congestion due to its expectorant properties. It helps loosen and expel mucus from the respiratory tract, making it beneficial for conditions like bronchitis and colds. Its essential oil contains compounds that stimulate mucus clearance and reduce throat irritation.
