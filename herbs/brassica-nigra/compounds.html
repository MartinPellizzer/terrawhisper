Black Mustard (Brassica nigra) Compounds

Black Mustard contains several key compounds, including flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and phenolic acids, known for their anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins contribute to its astringent qualities, while coumarin has anticoagulant properties. Glycosides and saponins support digestive health, and essential oils provide aromatic and antimicrobial benefits.

Black Mustard (Brassica nigra) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Black Mustard (Brassica nigra) are plant-derived compounds known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. They contribute to the plant's therapeutic potential in traditional herbal medicine, supporting immune function and reducing oxidative stress in the body.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Black Mustard (Brassica nigra). It contributes to the plant's antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Found in significant amounts in the seeds and leaves, it supports digestive health and may aid in reducing oxidative stress.

3. Tannin

Tannin in Black Mustard (Brassica nigra) contributes to its astringent properties and may support digestive health. It helps reduce inflammation and can aid in wound healing. Tannins are also believed to have antimicrobial effects, making them valuable in traditional herbal medicine.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Black Mustard (Brassica nigra). It contributes to the plant's antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarin is also known for its ability to relieve muscle tension and may support digestive health. Its presence enhances the therapeutic potential of Black Mustard in herbal medicine.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Black Mustard (Brassica nigra). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, including its potential anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects. Glycosides in Black Mustard are often responsible for its therapeutic actions in traditional herbal medicine.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Black Mustard (Brassica nigra). It contributes to the plant's bitter taste and has mild cleansing properties. Saponin may support digestive health by promoting bile secretion and aiding in the emulsification of fats. Its presence enhances the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

7. Essential oil

Essential oil from Black Mustard (Brassica nigra) contains volatile compounds like allyl isothiocyanate, which contribute to its pungent aroma and medicinal properties. This compound is known for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects, commonly used in traditional herbal medicine for respiratory and digestive support.

8. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Black Mustard (Brassica nigra) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. They are found in the seeds and are used in traditional herbal medicine. Terpenoids support the plant's role in treating respiratory and digestive ailments.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Black Mustard (Brassica nigra). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, including potential anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects. Alkaloids in Black Mustard are often studied for their role in traditional herbal medicine.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Black Mustard (Brassica nigra). It contributes to the plant's vibrant color and has antioxidant properties that support anti-inflammatory and immune-boosting effects in traditional herbal medicine.
