Common Blue Violet (Viola Mutabilis)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Viola mutabilis.

Common blue violet, scientifically known as Viola mutabilis, is a flowering plant native to North America, often recognized for its medicinal properties and historical significance. As a medicinal herb, it has been traditionally used for its adaptogenic qualities, helping the body resist stress and maintain balance. The plant is valued for its potential to support respiratory health, alleviate inflammation, and promote skin healing, with its primary benefits stemming from compounds like flavonoids and mucilage. Historically, Indigenous peoples and early European settlers used common blue violet to treat colds, sore throats, and digestive issues, while modern wellness practices incorporate it as a natural remedy for immune support and detoxification. Its unique feature is its ability to change flower color depending on the environment, a trait that has fascinated botanists and herbalists alike for centuries.
