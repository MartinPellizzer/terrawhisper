Blueberry (Vaccinium myrtillus) Compounds

Blueberry contains several key compounds, including flavonoids, which are antioxidants that support heart health. Phenolic acids and tannins contribute to its anti-inflammatory properties. Anthocyanins give blueberries their deep color and are linked to improved cognitive function. Glycosides, saponins, and terpenoids also play roles in its medicinal benefits.

Blueberry (Vaccinium myrtillus) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are key active compounds in Blueberry (Vaccinium myrtillus), contributing to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds help protect cells from damage and may support cardiovascular and cognitive health. They are found in high concentrations in the berries and are responsible for many of the plant's therapeutic effects.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in Blueberry (Vaccinium myrtillus). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress in the body. This compound supports the therapeutic potential of blueberry in promoting cardiovascular health and cognitive function.

3. Tannin

Tannin in Blueberry (Vaccinium myrtillus) contributes to its astringent properties and may support digestive health. It acts as a natural preservative and has antioxidant effects, helping to protect cells from oxidative stress. Tannins are found in the bark, leaves, and berries of the plant. They are part of the plant's broader therapeutic profile in herbal medicine.

4. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Blueberry (Vaccinium myrtillus). It is a type of flavonoid responsible for the fruit's deep blue color and has antioxidant properties that support cardiovascular health and may improve cognitive function.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Blueberry (Vaccinium myrtillus). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting cellular health and reducing oxidative stress. Glycosides are also involved in the plant's ability to manage blood sugar levels and enhance cognitive function.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Blueberry (Vaccinium myrtillus). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and immune support. Saponin also aids in enhancing the absorption of nutrients from the blueberry.

7. Essential oil

The essential oil of Blueberry (Vaccinium myrtillus) contains volatile compounds like limonene and pinene, which contribute to its aromatic properties. These compounds may support respiratory and digestive health, though research on their therapeutic effects is limited. The essential oil is often used in aromatherapy and as a complementary component in herbal formulations.

8. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Blueberry (Vaccinium myrtillus) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting the plant's traditional use in promoting urinary health and reducing oxidative stress. They are also implicated in the plant's potential neuroprotective benefits.

9. Coumarin

Coumarin is a compound found in Blueberry (Vaccinium myrtillus). It contributes to the plant's mild anticoagulant properties and may support cardiovascular health. Coumarin's presence also influences the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for its soothing and anti-inflammatory effects.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Blueberry (Vaccinium myrtillus). It contributes to the plant's pharmacological properties, though its specific role and therapeutic effects require further research. Alkaloids in Blueberry are part of the broader biochemical profile that may support traditional uses in herbal medicine.
