Botanical Identification of Blueberry (Vaccinium myrtillus)

To identify Blueberry (Vaccinium myrtillus) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Blueberry (Vaccinium myrtillus) belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Ericales, family Ericaceae, genus Vaccinium, and species Vaccinium myrtillus. This classification places it within the flowering plants and the heath family, reflecting its botanical relationships and characteristics.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Blueberry is Vaccinium myrtillus, first described by Linnaeus in 1753. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is also known by several synonyms, including Vaccinium caespitosum and Vaccinium uliginosum. Common names such as blueberry, cranberry, and whortleberry are used regionally, reflecting its widespread presence in temperate regions.

Morphological Characteristics

Blueberry (Vaccinium myrtillus) is a low-growing shrub with a dense root system that anchors it in acidic soil. Its stems are slender and often prostrate, forming mats. Leaves are oval, glossy, and dark green, with a waxy coating. The inflorescence consists of clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. Fruits are bilaterally symmetrical, blue-black berries containing small, hard seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Blueberry (Vaccinium myrtillus) can be distinguished by its small, bell-shaped white flowers and dark blue berries with a white bloom. It has alternate, oval leaves with a slightly serrated edge. It grows in acidic, well-drained soils in woodland areas, often found in clusters. It is commonly confused with other Vaccinium species but can be differentiated by its specific leaf shape and fruit characteristics.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Blueberry (Vaccinium myrtillus) is native to northern Europe, particularly the British Isles, Scandinavia, and parts of Russia. It thrives in cool, temperate climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers acidic, well-drained soils and is commonly found in heathlands and pine forests. This species typically grows between 300 and 1,500 meters in altitude.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Blueberry (Vaccinium myrtillus) blooms in late spring to early summer, typically between May and June. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early autumn, around August to September. Harvest timing for berries is in late summer to early autumn, usually between August and September, depending on regional climate.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Vaccinium myrtillus belongs to the Ericaceae family, closely related to other Vaccinium species like blueberries and cranberries. It shares morphological and genetic traits with these species, including similar leaf structures and flower types. Its phylogenetic placement reflects evolutionary relationships within the genus and broader Ericaceae family.
