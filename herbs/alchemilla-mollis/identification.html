Botanical Identification of Mossy Alchemilla (Alchemilla mollis)

To identify Mossy Alchemilla (Alchemilla mollis) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Mossy Alchemilla, or Alchemilla mollis, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Rosales, family Rosaceae, genus Alchemilla, and species mollis. These classifications help identify its botanical relationships and place it within the broader plant kingdom.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Mossy Alchemilla is Alchemilla mollis, first described by the botanist Carl von Linné. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is also known by several synonyms, including Alchemilla vulgaris and Alchemilla lutea. Common names such as "Mossy Alchemilla" and "Lady's Mantle" reflect its historical use in herbal medicine.

Morphological Characteristics

Mossy Alchemilla is a low-growing perennial with a creeping root system that forms dense mats. Its stems are short and often lie close to the ground, producing upright flowering shoots. Leaves are basal, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while the inflorescence consists of clusters of small, yellow flowers. The fruit is a three-lobed achene, and seeds are dispersed by wind.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Mossy Alchemilla can be distinguished by its dense, moss-like growth habit and dark green, hairy leaves. It has small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters and produces heart-shaped, sticky seed capsules. These features help differentiate it from similar species like Alchemilla vulgaris, which has less dense foliage and different leaf shape.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Mossy Alchemilla is native to Europe and parts of Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with mild summers and cool winters. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in meadows and woodland edges. It grows at elevations between 300 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Mossy Alchemilla blooms from late spring to early summer, typically between May and June. Seed formation occurs in late summer, around July to August. For identification purposes, the best time to harvest is during early bloom, when the leaves are fully developed and the flowers are visible.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Alchemilla mollis belongs to the Rosaceae family, closely related to species like Alchemilla vulgaris and Alchemilla glutinosa. It shares traits with other Alchemilla species, such as basal leaves and yellow flowers, but differs in growth habit and leaf structure. Its phylogenetic position reflects its evolutionary ties to other members of the rose family.
