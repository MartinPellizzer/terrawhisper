Mossy Alchemilla (Alchemilla mollis) Uses

Mossy Alchemilla is used to treat various skin conditions such as irritation, ulcers, wounds, inflammation, eczema, and rashes. It also supports joint health, helping with pain and inflammation associated with arthritis and inflammatory arthritis. Its anti-inflammatory properties make it a valuable remedy in herbal medicine.

Mossy Alchemilla (Alchemilla mollis) Monograph

1. Skin irritation

Alchemilla mollis has been traditionally used to soothe skin irritation due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It can be applied topically as a poultice or infused oil to reduce redness, itching, and swelling. Its high tannin content helps to calm irritated skin and promote healing.

2. Wound healing

Alchemilla mollis has been traditionally used to support wound healing due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It helps reduce swelling, prevent infection, and promote tissue repair. Its high content of tannins and flavonoids contributes to its effectiveness in treating minor cuts, abrasions, and skin irritations.

3. Skin ulcers

Alchemilla mollis has been traditionally used to treat skin ulcers due to its anti-inflammatory and wound-healing properties. The plant's astringent compounds help reduce infection and promote tissue regeneration. It is often applied topically as a poultice or infused oil to soothe and heal open sores. Its mild nature makes it suitable for sensitive skin conditions.

Alchemilla mollis has been traditionally used to support skin healing. Its astringent properties help reduce inflammation and promote tissue repair. 9. Arthritis

10. Inflammatory arthritis
