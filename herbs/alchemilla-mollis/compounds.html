Mossy Alchemilla (Alchemilla mollis) Compounds

The primary compounds in Mossy Alchemilla include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and tannins, which contribute to astringency. Phenolic acids and coumarins provide additional antioxidant and anti-inflammatory benefits. Glycosides and saponins support detoxification, while terpenoids and anthocyanins add to its medicinal profile. Essential oils and alkaloids also contribute to its therapeutic effects.

Mossy Alchemilla (Alchemilla mollis) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Alchemilla mollis contribute to its medicinal properties by acting as antioxidants and anti-inflammatory agents. These compounds help support skin health and may aid in wound healing, aligning with the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

2. Tannin

Tannin in Mossy Alchemilla (Alchemilla mollis) contributes to its astringent properties, helping to reduce inflammation and promote wound healing. It also supports digestive health by binding to proteins and aiding in the treatment of gastrointestinal issues.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid in Mossy Alchemilla (Alchemilla mollis) contributes to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound helps reduce oxidative stress and supports skin healing, making it valuable in traditional herbal medicine for treating skin conditions and inflammation.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Mossy Alchemilla (Alchemilla mollis). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in digestive and urinary health. Coumarin has antispasmodic and mild anticoagulant properties, supporting its role in herbal medicine for easing digestive discomfort and promoting urinary flow.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Mossy Alchemilla (Alchemilla mollis). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and wound-healing properties. Glycosides in Alchemilla mollis are often found in its leaves and are used in traditional herbal remedies. They help support skin health and reduce irritation.

6. Saponin

Saponin in Mossy Alchemilla (Alchemilla mollis) contributes to its anti-inflammatory and detoxifying properties. It helps support liver function and may aid in reducing swelling. This compound is often used in herbal medicine for its mild cleansing effects on the body.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Mossy Alchemilla (Alchemilla mollis) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, supporting the plant's traditional use in treating skin conditions and digestive issues. They are also believed to aid in wound healing and reduce pain.

8. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Mossy Alchemilla (Alchemilla mollis). It contributes to the plant's vibrant color and has antioxidant properties that support overall health. Found in the leaves and flowers, anthocyanin may aid in reducing inflammation and protecting cells from oxidative stress.

9. Essential oil

Essential oil from Alchemilla mollis contains volatile compounds like limonene and geraniol. These contribute to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The oil is used in herbal medicine for skin conditions and digestive support. It is often extracted through steam distillation from the plant's leaves and flowers.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Mossy Alchemilla (Alchemilla mollis). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by interacting with biological systems to support healing. While alkaloids are more commonly associated with other plants, their presence in Alchemilla mollis adds to its therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.
