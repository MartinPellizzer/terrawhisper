Spinach (Spinacia Oleracea Var. Laevis)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Spinacia oleracea var. laevis.

Spinach, scientifically known as Spinacia oleracea var. laevis, is a leafy green vegetable widely recognized as a medicinal herb with adaptogenic properties. It is rich in nutrients such as iron, vitamins A, C, and K, and antioxidants, making it beneficial for improving blood health, boosting the immune system, and supporting eye health. Traditionally used in Chinese, Indian, and Middle Eastern cultures for its healing properties, spinach has been valued for treating anemia, digestive issues, and skin conditions. In modern wellness practices, it is commonly consumed as a superfood in juices, smoothies, and salads to enhance overall vitality and detoxification. One of its unique features is its high content of lutein and zeaxanthin, which are rare carotenoids that contribute to its distinctive flavor and potential protective effects against age-related macular degeneration.
