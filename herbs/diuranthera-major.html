What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Diuranthera major has an erect, herbaceous perennial structure characterized by basal leaves that are long-petioled and ovate to lanceolate with serrated margins, while cauline leaves are smaller and alternate along the stem.

The stem is typically smooth and can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, terminal umbel of small, yellowish-green flowers that possess five petals and five stamens. The fruit is a small, dry schizocarp that eventually splits into two one-seeded mericarps.

These morphological features distinguish it from other members of the Apiaceae family.
