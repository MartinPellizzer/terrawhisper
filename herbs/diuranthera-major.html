Diuranthera major

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Diuranthera major has an erect, herbaceous perennial structure characterized by basal leaves that are long-petioled and ovate to lanceolate with serrated margins, while cauline leaves are smaller and alternate along the stem. The stem is typically smooth and can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, terminal umbel of small, yellowish-green flowers that possess five petals and five stamens. The fruit is a small, dry schizocarp that eventually splits into two one-seeded mericarps. These morphological features distinguish it from other members of the Apiaceae family.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Diuranthera major can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal distress if consumed or handled improperly. Ingesting parts of the plant can result in stomach cramps, nausea, or diarrhea due to its chemical composition. There is also a lack of extensive human clinical data to definitively rule out more severe systemic toxicities. Therefore, cautious handling and avoidance of ingestion are recommended to prevent adverse health effects.
