Torilis scabra

What's the taxonomical classification of Torilis scabra?

Torilis scabra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further categorized under the order Apiales. It is a prominent representative of the family Apiaceae, falling under the genus Torilis, with its specific designation being the species scabra.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Torilis scabra has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by erect, branched stems that reach heights of 20 to 50 centimeters and are covered in stiff, spreading hairs. The leaves are oppositely arranged, pinnately divided into narrow, lanceolate segments that provide a fine, feathery texture to the foliage. At the terminus of each stem, the plant produces small, white, umbellate flower clusters consisting of numerous tiny florets. The fruits are distinctive, being small, oblong schizocarps densely covered in hooked prickles that facilitate dispersal by clinging to passing animals. These bristly structures are a primary diagnostic feature that distinguishes this species from other members of the Apiaceae family.
