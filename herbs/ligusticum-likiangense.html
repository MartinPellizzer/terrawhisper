Ligusticum likiangense

What's the taxonomical classification of Ligusticum likiangense?

Ligusticum likiangense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant falls under the order Apiales and is a member of the family Apiaceae. Specifically, it is identified by the genus Ligusticum and is distinguished as the species likiangense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ligusticum likiangense has pinnately compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets that possess serrated margins and a bright green color. The plant typically displays an erect growth habit with hollow, striated stems that can reach significant heights. Its inflorescence consists of large, compound umbels containing numerous small, white flowers arranged in a flat-topped structure. The root system is characterized by a thick, fleshy rhizome that provides stability and nutrient storage. Small, schizocarpous fruits develop following pollination, often containing aromatic oils within their tissues.
