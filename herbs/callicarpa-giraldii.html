Callicarpa giraldii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Callicarpa giraldii has opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that measure approximately 3 to 7 centimeters in length and feature serrated margins with pointed tips. The foliage is typically bright green and possesses a slightly textured surface with prominent venation. Small, tubular flowers emerge in dense, axillary clusters during the blooming season. These flowers eventually give way to striking, spherical clusters of metallic purple berries that persist on the branches. The shrub maintains a multi-stemmed growth habit with woody stems that provide structural support for the heavy fruit clusters.
