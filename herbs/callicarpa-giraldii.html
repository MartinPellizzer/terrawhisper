Callicarpa giraldii

What's the taxonomical classification of Callicarpa giraldii?

Callicarpa giraldii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized into the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Callicarpa, with its specific identification being the species giraldii.

What are the herbal preparations of Callicarpa giraldii?

Callicarpa giraldii is prepared as decoctions, infusions, or topical pastes derived from its leaves and stems to treat various ailments. The dried leaves are commonly boiled in water to create a concentrated liquid used for internal consumption. When addressing skin conditions, the plant material is often crushed into a fine pulp to form a medicinal paste applied directly to the affected area. Some practitioners also utilize alcoholic tinctures to extract specific bioactive compounds for long-term storage and standardized dosing. These diverse preparations allow the plant's properties to be utilized for both systemic and localized therapeutic purposes.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Callicarpa giraldii can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and digestive distress if handled or ingested improperly. While not considered highly toxic, the leaves and berries may trigger contact dermatitis in individuals with sensitive skin. Ingesting any part of the plant can lead to nausea, vomiting, or stomach cramps. Some people may also experience localized swelling or itching after touching the foliage. It is important to use caution and avoid direct contact to prevent these adverse physical reactions.
