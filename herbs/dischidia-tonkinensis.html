What's the taxonomical classification of Dischidia tonkinensis?

Dischidia tonkinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Gentianaales. This plant is a member of the family Apocynaceae, specifically situated within the genus Dischidia, which culminates in its unique species designation, Dischidia tonkinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dischidia tonkinensis has small, fleshy, ovate to elliptical leaves that are arranged in a compact rosette and typically measure between 1 and 3 centimeters in length.

These leaves possess a somewhat succulent texture and can vary in color from bright green to a deeper shade depending on light exposure. The plant often exhibits a creeping growth habit, allowing it to cling to host trees in its natural habitat. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette.

This epiphytic morphology is specifically adapted for moisture retention and stability on arboreal surfaces.
