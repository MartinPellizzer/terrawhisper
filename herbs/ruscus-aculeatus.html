Side Effects

Ruscus aculeatus can have some side effects, such as gastrointestinal discomfort, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, particularly when consumed in large quantities.

Allergic reactions, such as skin rashes or hives, may occur in individuals sensitive to the plant's compounds. In rare cases, excessive ingestion might lead to more severe symptoms like abdominal pain or dizziness. While generally considered safe in moderate use, long-term or high-dose consumption could potentially affect liver function.

It is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using Ruscus aculeatus, especially for those with pre-existing medical conditions or who are taking other medications.
