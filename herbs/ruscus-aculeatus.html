Ruscus Aculeatus Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Ruscus aculeatus, commonly known as butcher's broom, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Convallariaceae, native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its ability to improve circulation and reduce inflammation.

The herb contains bioactive constituents such as flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

Herbal preparations such as infusions, tinctures, and capsules can be made from the dried leaves and stems of Ruscus aculeatus.

Medicinal Constituents of ruscus aculeatus

Ruscus aculeatus cardiac glycosides, such as ruscogenin and atroruscogenin, are potent compounds that act on the heart by inhibiting the sodium-potassium ATPase pump, thereby increasing intracellular calcium levels and enhancing myocardial contractility, making them effective in treating cardiovascular conditions like congestive heart failure and arrhythmias.

Ruscus aculeatus polyphenols, including flavonoids and phenolic acids, possess strong antioxidant properties that help neutralize free radicals, reduce oxidative stress, and protect cellular structures from damage, contributing to anti-inflammatory and cardioprotective effects.

Ruscus aculeatus saponins are known for their ability to lower cholesterol levels by inhibiting cholesterol absorption in the intestines and promoting the excretion of bile acids, thus supporting cardiovascular health and reducing the risk of atherosclerosis.

Ruscus aculeatus flavonoids, such as quercetin and rutin, exhibit vasodilatory effects by improving blood flow, reducing vascular resistance, and preventing platelet aggregation, thereby enhancing overall circulatory function.

Medicinal Parts of ruscus aculeatus

Ruscus aculeatus leaf is used in herbal medicine for its astringent and anti-inflammatory properties, often employed to treat wounds and skin conditions.

The root of Ruscus aculeatus contains compounds that support cardiovascular health and may help with circulatory issues.

The fruit, though less commonly used, can be utilized in formulations for its mild laxative effects.

The main herbal preparations from these parts include decoctions, tinctures, and salves, with the leaf and root being more frequently incorporated into traditional remedies.

Ruscus aculeatus causes heart palpitations as it contains cardiac glycosides, which may affect heart rhythm in some people.

Ruscus aculeatus triggers skin rash due to the presence of allergens that can cause an inflammatory response in the skin when exposed.
