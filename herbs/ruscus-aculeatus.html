Ruscus Aculeatus Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Ruscus aculeatus, commonly known as butcher's broom, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia, valued for its medicinal properties.

This herb is traditionally used to support cardiovascular health and improve circulation due to its ability to strengthen blood vessels.

The bioactive constituents in ruscus aculeatus include saponins, flavonoids, and alkaloids, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

Herbal preparations such as infusions, tinctures, and capsules can be made from the plant's dried stems to harness its beneficial properties.
