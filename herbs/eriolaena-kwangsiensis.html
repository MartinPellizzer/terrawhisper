Eriolaena kwangsiensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Eriolaena kwangsiensis?

Eriolaena kwangsiensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Malvales. As a member of the family Malvaceae, this plant is specifically categorized within the genus Eriolaena, reaching its final taxonomic designation at the species level as kwangsiensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eriolaena kwangsiensis has small, elliptic to lanceolate leaves that are typically arranged in an opposite pattern along the stem.

The foliage displays a distinct texture often characterized by fine, velvety hairs on both the upper and lower surfaces. Its reproductive structures consist of inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils in small clusters. The stems are woody and can develop a rough bark as the plant reaches maturity.

These morphological traits are characteristic of its specific genus and help distinguish it from related species.
