Eriolaena kwangsiensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eriolaena kwangsiensis has small, elliptic to lanceolate leaves that are typically arranged in an opposite pattern along the stem. The foliage displays a distinct texture often characterized by fine, velvety hairs on both the upper and lower surfaces. Its reproductive structures consist of inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils in small clusters. The stems are woody and can develop a rough bark as the plant reaches maturity. These morphological traits are characteristic of its specific genus and help distinguish it from related species.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the tropical rainforest regions of Ghana, specifically within the southwestern portions of the country. It is found inhabiting the moist, lowland evergreen forests that characterize this West African biodiversity hotspot. The species thrives in areas with high annual rainfall and consistent humidity levels typical of the Guineo-Congolian forest zone. Its distribution is restricted to these specific ecological niches, making it a localized component of the regional flora.
