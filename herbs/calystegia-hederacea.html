Calystegia hederacea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. As discussed in a study, Calystegia hederacea contains a crude resin glycoside fraction that can be used to treat menoxenia and gonorrhea. Alkaline hydrolysis of the whole plant yielded 4 new glycosidic acids, known as calyhedic acids A, B, C, and D, alongside 2 known glycosidic acids, calysolic acids A and C. The plant also contains 3 known organic acids, which are 2S-methylbutyric, tiglic, and 2R,3R-nilic acids. Specifically, calyhedic acids A, B, and D are penta-, hexa-, and hepta-glycosides of 12S-hydroxyhexadecanoic acid. Calyhedic acid C is an isomer of calyhedic acid D where the 12S-hydroxyhexadecanoyl residue is replaced by a 11S-hydroxyhexadecanoyl residue.
