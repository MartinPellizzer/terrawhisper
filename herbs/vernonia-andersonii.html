What's the taxonomical classification of Vernonia andersonii?

Vernonia andersonii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae, which is further categorized into the genus Vernonia, specifically identifying as the species andersonii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vernonia andersonii has an erect, perennial herbaceous habit characterized by slender stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 70 centimeters. The leaves are primarily basal or lower-stemmed, displaying an ovate to lanceolate shape with serrated margins and a slightly pubescent texture. Along the stem, the leaves become progressively smaller and more spaced apart as they transition toward the inflorescence. The flowering heads consist of numerous small, tubular disk florets that are usually purple or lavender in color. Each flower head is subtended by a series of involucral bracts that provide structural support to the blooming cluster.

Populations are often found in montane chaparral or coniferous forest openings where drainage is well-defined.
