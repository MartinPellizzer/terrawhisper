What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vernonia andersonii has an erect, perennial herbaceous habit characterized by slender stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 70 centimeters. Along the stem, the leaves become progressively smaller and more spaced apart as they transition toward the inflorescence. The flowering heads consist of numerous small, tubular disk florets that are usually purple or lavender in color. Each flower head is subtended by a series of involucral bracts that provide structural support to the blooming cluster.
