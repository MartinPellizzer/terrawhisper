Clary Sage (Salvia sclarea)

Clary Sage (Salvia sclarea) is a member of the Lamiaceae family, native to Mediterranean Region, Southwest Asia, and Middle East. Traditionally, its leaves, flowers, and stems have been used for infusions, decoctions, and oil infusions.

This herb is particularly valued for its sedative, antispasmodic, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and ayurvedic medicine.

Perennial herbaceous plant.

Leaves
Simple leaves with upper surface gray-green and lower surface pale white, exhibiting prominent stomatal bands.
Flowers
Inflorescence consists of spikes of bilaterally symmetrical flowers with purple-lavender bilobed corollas, each having two lips, upper lip two-lobed, lower lip three-lobed, and four stamens with two anther lobes.
Stems
Cylindrical, hollow, quadrangular, hairy, erect, opposite branching, ascending, with prominent longitudinal ridges and grooves.
