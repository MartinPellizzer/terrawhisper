Clary sage

Salvia sclarea

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family

Native Region

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Clary Sage is scientifically known as Salvia sclarea, belonging to the Lamiaceae family. It is also called "flowering sage" and is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. The plant has square stems, gray-green leaves, and purple flowers. It is sometimes referred to as "common sage" in herbal literature.

Active Compounds

Clary sage contains essential oil, flavonoids, and phenolic acids as its primary active compounds. These components work together to support various therapeutic effects, including calming properties and hormonal balance. The synergistic interaction of these compounds enhances its efficacy in herbal medicine.

  • Essential oil
  • Flavonoid
  • Phenolic acid

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action
Reproductive Menstrual cramps, menopausal symptoms, menstrual irregularities, breast tenderness Antispasmodic, hormonal balance, hormonal regulator
Nervous Anxiety, stress-related insomnia, mood disorders, headache Sedative, anxiolytic, mood stabilizing, analgesic
Integumentary Skin infections, skin irritation, fungal infections, skin wounds Antimicrobial, soothing, antifungal, wound healing, anti-inflammatory

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for digestive issues and menstrual discomfort.

Essential oil: Used for anxiety, stress relief, and skin conditions.

Decoction: Used for respiratory issues and as a sedative.

Safety Profile

Clary sage is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy due to its potential to induce uterine contractions. High doses may cause side effects such as dizziness or hormonal imbalances, so caution is advised.
