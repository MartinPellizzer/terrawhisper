Cistus Clusii (Cistus clusii) Uses

Cistus clusii is used to treat respiratory conditions such as asthma, bronchitis, and lung congestion. It helps alleviate symptoms of respiratory tract infections, allergic rhinitis, and sinusitis. The plant's anti-inflammatory properties reduce mucous membrane inflammation and soothe coughs and sore throats. It is particularly effective for clearing mucus and supporting respiratory health.

Cistus Clusii (Cistus clusii) Monograph

1. Asthma

Cistus clusii has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in managing asthma symptoms. Its anti-inflammatory and bronchodilatory properties may help reduce airway inflammation and improve breathing. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture and is valued for its soothing effects on the respiratory system.

2. Respiratory tract infections

Cistus clusii is used traditionally to support respiratory health, particularly for conditions like coughs, bronchitis, and sore throats. Its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties help reduce infection symptoms and soothe irritated airways. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to promote easier breathing and clear mucus.

3. Allergic rhinitis

Cistus clusii has been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistaminic properties. Its essential oils and flavonoids help reduce nasal congestion and sneezing. Studies suggest it may inhibit histamine release, offering a natural alternative for managing allergy symptoms.

4. Lung congestion

Cistus clusii is traditionally used to alleviate lung congestion due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps loosen mucus and reduce inflammation in the respiratory tract, making it easier to breathe. This makes it a valuable herb in herbal medicine for conditions like bronchitis and chronic cough.

5. Mucous membrane inflammation

Cistus clusii is used to reduce inflammation of mucous membranes due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It helps soothe irritated tissues in the throat, respiratory tract, and digestive system. Its high tannin content provides astringency, while its flavonoids reduce swelling and redness. This makes it effective for conditions like sore throat and gastrointestinal inflammation.

6. Bronchitis

Cistus clusii is used in traditional herbal medicine to treat bronchitis due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. It helps reduce mucus buildup and soothe airway irritation, making it effective for managing symptoms like coughing and congestion associated with chronic bronchitis.

7. Cough

Cistus clusii is used to alleviate coughs due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps loosen mucus and reduce irritation in the respiratory tract. Traditionally prepared as a tea, it is valued for its soothing effect on the throat and its ability to support respiratory health.

8. Respiratory infections

Cistus clusii is used to support respiratory health by reducing inflammation and clearing mucus. Its antimicrobial properties help combat bacterial and viral infections. It is commonly prepared as a tea or tincture to alleviate symptoms of colds, bronchitis, and other respiratory ailments. Regular use may enhance immune function and ease breathing in affected individuals.

9. Sore throat

Cistus clusii is used to soothe sore throats due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce irritation and infection in the throat lining. Traditionally, it is prepared as a tea or gargle to alleviate symptoms. Its natural compounds support healing and provide relief from discomfort.

10. Sinusitis

Cistus clusii is used in herbal medicine to treat sinusitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion and mucus production, offering natural relief for sinus infections. Its essential oils and extracts are often incorporated into nasal sprays or inhalation therapies. This plant supports respiratory health and is valued for its soothing effects on the sinus tissues.
