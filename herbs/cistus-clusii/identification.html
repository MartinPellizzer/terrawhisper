Morphological Characteristics

Cistus clusii is a shrub with a compact growth habit. It has a deep, fibrous root system that helps it thrive in rocky soils. The stems are woody and branched, while the leaves are evergreen, oval, and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces dense clusters of yellow flowers in spring, followed by small, woody fruits containing three seeds each.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Cistus clusii can be distinguished by its elliptic, slightly hairy leaves with a white underside, pale yellow flowers with five lobes, and a distinctive, strongly aromatic resinous scent. It differs from similar species like Cistus ladaniferus by its narrower leaves and less pronounced glandular structures.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Cistus clusii is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly in southern Europe and North Africa. It thrives in temperate climates with mild, wet winters and hot, dry summers. The plant prefers well-drained, alkaline soils and is commonly found at altitudes between 500 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Cistus clusii blooms from April to June, producing bright yellow flowers. Seed formation occurs from late summer to early autumn. For herbal identification, the plant is best harvested during the flowering period to ensure accurate species recognition and optimal potency.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Cistus clusii is most closely related to species within the Cistus genus, particularly C. monspeliensis and C. creticus. It shares key morphological and genetic traits with these species, reflecting its placement within the Cistaceae family. Its phylogenetic position highlights evolutionary relationships among Mediterranean shrubs.
