Helicteres angustifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Helicteres angustifolia has narrow, linear to lanceolate leaves, small yellowish flowers, and woody, spreading branches. The leaves are typically arranged oppositely along the stems and possess a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are often zygomorphic and emerge from the axils of the foliage in small clusters. The plant produces small, somewhat oblong or spherical fruits that eventually turn brown as they mature. These structural features collectively define its growth habit within its natural habitat.
