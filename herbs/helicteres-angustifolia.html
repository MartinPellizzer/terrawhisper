Helicteres angustifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Helicteres angustifolia?

Helicteres angustifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Malvales, it is a member of the family Malvaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Helicteres, with its specific designation being the species angustifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Helicteres angustifolia has narrow, linear to lanceolate leaves, small yellowish flowers, and woody, spreading branches. The leaves are typically arranged oppositely along the stems and possess a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are often zygomorphic and emerge from the axils of the foliage in small clusters. The plant produces small, somewhat oblong or spherical fruits that eventually turn brown as they mature. These structural features collectively define its growth habit within its natural habitat.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds betulinic acid, lupeol, oleanolic acid, triterpenoid saponins, and 3beta-acetoxy-27-benzoyloxylup-20(29)-en-28-oic acid. These diverse chemical constituents are primarily classified as pentacyclic triterpenoids which contribute to the plant's therapeutic profile. The presence of betulinic acid and lupeol is often associated with significant anti-inflammatory and antitumor activities. Additionally, oleanolic acid and the specific derivative 3beta-acetoxy-27-benzoyloxylup-20(29)-en-28-oic acid provide unique biological properties that support various pharmacological applications. The triterpenoid saponins further enhance the complex chemical makeup of the species. Together, these compounds form a potent bioactive matrix used in medicinal research.

As discussed in a study, Helicteres angustifolia contains main active chemicals including betulinic acid, oleanolic acid, helicteric acid, helicterilic acid, and other triterpenoid saponins. The plant has pharmacological benefits such as anti-inflamatory, anti-viral and anti-tumor effects. Research involving an Illumina high-throughput sequencing platform resulted in 121 gigabases of data and 424,824 unigenes, with 22,430 unigenes determined to be differentially expressed. Screening identified three oxidosqualene cyclases and four Cytochrome P450s, including HaOSC1 and HaCYPi3, which catalyze the production of lupeol and oleanolic acid. Additionally, three acyltransferases were screened as candidate genes to assist in the biosynthesis of acylated triterpenoids precursors.
