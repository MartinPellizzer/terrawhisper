What's the taxonomical classification of Primula atrodentata?

Primula atrodentata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its placement in the order Ericales, it falls under the family Primulaceae. Finally, the plant is identified within the genus Primula, specifically as the species atrodentata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Primula atrodentata has a basal rosette of long-petioled, obovate to elliptical leaves that are often wrinkled or rugose on their upper surfaces. These leaves typically possess serrated margins and are arranged in a dense cluster near the ground. From the center of this foliage, a slender, leafless scape emerges to support the inflorescence. The flowers are characterized by their deep, dark red to purple coloration and are arranged in umbels at the top of the stem. Each individual blossom features a salverform corolla with a distinct tube and five spreading lobes.
