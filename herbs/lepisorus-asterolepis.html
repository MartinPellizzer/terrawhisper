Lepisorus asterolepis

What's the taxonomical classification of Lepisorus asterolepis?

Lepisorus asterolepis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae. Following the order Polypodiales, it is placed in the family Polypodiaceae. The plant is further categorized under the genus Lepisorus, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species asterolepis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lepisorus asterolepis has pinnate fronds characterized by small, star-shaped scales that cover the stipe, rachis, and underside of the pinnae.

The pinnules are typically oblong to lanceolate in shape and possess entire or slightly serrated margins. A distinguishing morphological feature is the presence of small, rounded sori located on the underside of the segments. These sori are often protected by a specialized indusium that matches the texture of the surrounding tissue.

The overall architecture of the plant allows it to thrive in specific shaded, moist environments.
