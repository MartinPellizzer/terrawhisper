Salix chienii

What's the taxonomical classification of Salix chienii?

Salix chienii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Malpighiales and the family Salicaceae, eventually reaching its specific placement within the genus Salix. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its unique species name, chienii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Salix chienii has lanceolate leaves that measure approximately 3 to 7 centimeters in length, featuring finely serrated margins and a smooth, glabrous surface. The plant displays slender, flexible branches that often exhibit a yellowish or brownish hue depending on their maturity. Its reproductive structures consist of small, catkin-like flowers that emerge from the woody stems during the spring season. The leaf arrangement is alternate along the stem, contributing to a dense and somewhat weeping growth habit. These morphological characteristics help distinguish this specific willow species from its closely related relatives in the Salix genus.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the mountainous regions of central and southern China, specifically within the provinces of Hubei, Hunan, Sichuan, and Guizhou. It thrives in high-altitude habitats where it typically grows along the banks of mountain streams and near moist forest edges. The species prefers temperate climates characterized by significant seasonal moisture and cool temperatures. Because its range is restricted to these specific topographical features, it is found in relatively fragmented pockets across its native territory. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Salix chienii contains high concentrations of salicin, a precursor to salicylic acid, alongside various phenolic compounds and flavonoids.
