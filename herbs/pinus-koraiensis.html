Pinus koraiensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Pinus koraiensis?

Pinus koraiensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Pinidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Pinales and the family Pinaceae, ultimately placing it within the genus Pinus. Specifically, this plant is identified by its unique species designation, koraiensis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Pinus koraiensis contains high concentrations of bioactive terpenoids, resin acids, and phenolic compounds that exhibit significant antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. These secondary metabolites, particularly the diterpenes found within the resin, serve as natural defense mechanisms for the tree. When extracted, these compounds can demonstrate potential in inhibiting the growth of various pathogenic bacteria and fungi. The presence of antioxidant polyphenols also contributes to the plant's ability to mitigate oxidative stress in biological systems. Ongoing pharmacological research aims to further characterize these specific chemical constituents for potential therapeutic applications.

What are the therapeutic actions of Pinus koraiensis?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat inflammatory conditions, respiratory ailments, and various skin infections through its bioactive compounds. The essential oils derived from the species possess strong antimicrobial properties that target bacterial and fungal pathogens. These extracts are often applied topically to soothe skin irritation or used in traditional medicine to alleviate symptoms of bronchitis and coughs. Research into its chemical constituents suggests that its antioxidant effects may also help reduce systemic inflammation. Such therapeutic applications highlight the plant's importance in regional ethnobotanical practices.
