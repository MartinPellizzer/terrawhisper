Scleria levis

What's the taxonomical classification of Scleria levis?

Scleria levis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Poales and into the family Cyperaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Scleria, and its unique species designation, levis.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Scleria levis can cause skin irritation, localized redness, and allergic contact dermatitis upon direct contact with its tissues. Individuals sensitive to plant alkaloids may experience stinging or itching sensations where the sap touches the skin. If the plant is ingested, it may lead to gastrointestinal distress such as nausea or stomach cramping. There is also a potential for systemic allergic reactions in people with highly sensitive immune systems. Careful handling is recommended to avoid these adverse physical responses.
