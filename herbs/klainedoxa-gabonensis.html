Klainedoxa gabonenis has woody, climbing stems that function as lianas and large, simple, elliptical leaves with entire margins. The plant develops woody fruits that aid in its dispersal within its tropical forest habitat. These morphological traits allow the species to thrive in the dense understory of equatorial ecosystems.
