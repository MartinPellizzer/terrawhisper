What is Koeleria spicata?

Koeleria spicata is a perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is a tufted grass with stems typically 20-60 cm tall, bearing narrow, flat, and involute leaves 2-8 mm wide and 2-20 cm long. The inflorescence is a dense panicle 5-15 cm long, composed of spikelets with two florets, the upper one being sterile and the lower one fertile, producing a 2-3 mm long caryopsis. Koeleria spicata is a common component of grasslands, meadows, and rocky slopes, often growing in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

The plant typically flowers from June to August, producing spikelets with a reddish-brown color and a sweet, nutty fragrance.
