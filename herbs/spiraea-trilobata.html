Spiraea trilobata

What's the taxonomical classification of Spiraea trilobata?

Spiraea trilobata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Rosales. It is further categorized under the family Rosaceae, falling into the genus Spiraea, and is specifically identified by the species name trilobata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Spiraea trilobata has deciduous, woody stems that produce leaves with three distinct lobes and serrated margins. The foliage typically exhibits a bright green color during the growing season. Small, white flowers emerge in dense, corymb-like clusters from the terminal ends of the branches. These blossoms are characterized by numerous long, protruding stamens that create a fuzzy appearance. The overall growth habit is shrubby, often forming compact mounds of vegetation.
