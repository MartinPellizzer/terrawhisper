Halerpestes tricuspis

What's the taxonomical classification of Halerpestes tricuspis?

Halerpestes tricuspis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Ranunculales. Furthermore, this plant is a member of the family Ranunculaceae, specifically categorized within the genus Halerpestes, which culminates in its unique species designation, tricuspis.

What are the therapeutic actions of Halerpestes tricuspis?

Halerpestes tricuspis has anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties that are used to treat skin infections, wounds, and various digestive ailments. The plant contains bioactive compounds like flavonoids and tannins that help reduce swelling and neutralize harmful free radicals in the body. Many traditional medicinal practices utilize its leaves or roots to soothe skin irritations and promote the healing of open sores. It is also frequently employed in folk medicine to combat bacterial growth and alleviate gastrointestinal discomfort. These diverse therapeutic actions make it a significant component in various ethnobotanical treatments across its native regions.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat stomach pains, diarrhea, intestinal worms, and various skin infections. The bark and leaves contain bioactive compounds that serve as traditional remedies for digestive distress. Local healers often prepare decoctions from the plant to soothe gastrointestinal discomfort and expel parasites. Additionally, the crushed foliage can be applied topically to help heal wounds or manage dermatological issues. These medicinal applications highlight the plant's importance in traditional ethnobotanical practices.
