Halerpestes tricuspis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Halerpestes tricuspis has an annual life cycle characterized by slender, upright stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 40 centimeters. The leaves are primarily basal and lanceolate, though smaller, alternate leaves may appear along the stem. Its most distinctive morphological feature is the three-cusped, triangular shape of the seed capsule, from which the species derives its name. These capsules are small and contain numerous tiny seeds that facilitate dispersal in wetland habitats. The plant also produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family.

      What are the herbal preparations of Halerpestes tricuspis?

      Halerpestes tricuspis is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice depending on the intended medicinal application. For milder therapeutic effects, the plant parts are steeped in hot water to produce an infusion.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Halerpestes tricuspis can cause severe skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and localized swelling upon contact or ingestion. The chemical compounds within its leaves may trigger allergic dermatitis in sensitive individuals. If swallowed, the plant often leads to nausea, vomiting, and intense abdominal cramping. Some reports also suggest that prolonged exposure to its sap can result in temporary numbness or tingling in the extremities. Careful handling is essential to avoid these physiological reactions.
