Halerpestes tricuspis

What's the taxonomical classification of Halerpestes tricuspis?

Halerpestes tricuspis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Ranunculales. Furthermore, this plant is a member of the family Ranunculaceae, specifically categorized within the genus Halerpestes, which culminates in its unique species designation, tricuspis.

      What are the herbal preparations of Halerpestes tricuspis?

      Halerpestes tricuspis is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice depending on the intended medicinal application. The roots and leaves are most commonly boiled in water to create a concentrated decoction for internal use. For milder therapeutic effects, the plant parts are steeped in hot water to produce an infusion. When addressing skin irritations or wounds, the crushed fresh plant material is applied directly to the affected area as a poultice. These various methods allow practitioners to extract different active compounds from the plant's structure.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Halerpestes tricuspis can cause severe skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and localized swelling upon contact or ingestion. The chemical compounds within its leaves may trigger allergic dermatitis in sensitive individuals. If swallowed, the plant often leads to nausea, vomiting, and intense abdominal cramping. Some reports also suggest that prolonged exposure to its sap can result in temporary numbness or tingling in the extremities. Careful handling is essential to avoid these physiological reactions.
