Wild Angelica

Pimpinella major

Wild Angelica (Pimpinella major) is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, known for its use in traditional medicine to support digestive health and alleviate symptoms of gastrointestinal disorders.

Botanical Identification

Wild Angelica, scientifically known as Pimpinella major, belongs to the Apiaceae family. It is also called Greater Angelica and is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant has finely divided leaves, small white flowers, and a taproot. It is sometimes referred to as Angelica sylvestris in older herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Magnoliopsida
  • Order: Apiales
  • Family: Apiaceae
  • Genus: Pimpinella
  • Species: Pimpinella major

Active Compounds

Wild Angelica contains key active compounds like coumarin, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work together to support various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Their synergistic interactions enhance the herb's overall therapeutic potential.

  • Coumarin

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory conditions.

Infusion: Used for mild digestive complaints and as a general tonic.

Poultice: Used for skin irritations and inflammation.

Safety Profile

Wild Angelica (Pimpinella major) is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause mild gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. There are no well-documented contraindications, but caution is advised during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to limited safety data.
