Wild Angelica (Pimpinella major)

Wild Angelica (Pimpinella major) is a member of the Apiaceae family, native to Central Asia, and Middle East. Traditionally, its roots, leaves, and seeds have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its antispasmodic, carminative, and bitter actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and ayurvedic medicine.
