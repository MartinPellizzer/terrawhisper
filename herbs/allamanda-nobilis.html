Allamanda nobilis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Allamanda nobilis has woody, evergreen stems that produce large, funnel-shaped flowers characterized by bright yellow petals and a prominent central throat. The foliage consists of glossy, elliptical leaves that are arranged in whorls or opposite pairs along the climbing vines. These leaves typically feature smooth margins and a deep green color that provides a lush background for the blooms. The plant develops specialized tendrils or twisting stems to assist in its climbing habit through various structures. Its overall structure allows it to spread extensively as it matures in tropical environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Central and South America, specifically ranging from southern Mexico through various parts of the Caribbean and down into Brazil. It thrives primarily in humid, lowland environments where consistent moisture and warm temperatures are prevalent. Because of its natural habitat, it is often found in the understory of rainforests or along the edges of forested areas. Many collectors have since introduced the species to other tropical zones around the world for ornamental purposes. Its distribution is limited by its inability to tolerate frost or prolonged periods of drought.
