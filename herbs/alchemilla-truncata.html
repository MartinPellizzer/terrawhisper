What is Alchemilla truncata?

Alchemilla truncata is a perennial plant species in the family Rosaceae, native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It has a rosette of leaves that are palmately divided into 5-7 lobes, with each lobe bearing 3-5 toothed segments. The leaves are 2-10 cm long and 1-6 cm wide, with a petiole up to 1 cm long. The inflorescence is a corymb of small, white or greenish flowers, arranged in a spiral manner, with a diameter of approximately 1-2 cm.

Alchemilla truncata typically grows to a height of 30-60 cm, forming a compact, spreading habit.
