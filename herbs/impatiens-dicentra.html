Impatiens dicentra

What's the taxonomical classification of Impatiens dicentra?

Impatiens dicentra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Ericales and is a member of the family Balsaminaceae. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Impatiens and the species dicentra.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Impatiens dicentra has succulent, fleshy stems that support lanceolate to ovate leaves with serrated margins and a soft, herbaceous texture. Its most distinctive feature is the pendulous, spurred flowers that resemble the shape of a bleeding heart. These blossoms emerge in clusters and can range in color from pure white to deep pink or red. The overall structure remains relatively delicate and moisture-dependent throughout its growing season.

These populations are often found growing in shaded areas such as forest floors or along the edges of mountain streams.
