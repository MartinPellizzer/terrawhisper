Embelia oblongifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Embelia oblongifolia has elliptic to oblong leaves that are oppositely arranged on woody stems, featuring entire margins and an acute apex. The leaf texture is typically coriaceous, which helps the plant retain moisture in its natural habitat. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often displaying a pale coloration. The reproductive structures include small fruits that follow the flowering period. These morphological traits are characteristic of the Primulaceae family to which this species belongs.
