Sonchus asper

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sonchus asper has erect, branched stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 100 centimeters and are characterized by a milky white latex present throughout the plant tissue. The leaves are alternate, ranging from basal rosettes to stem leaves, and feature deeply lobed or pinnatifid margins with coarse, spiny teeth. Small, yellowish flower heads are arranged in terminal corymbose cymes, each containing numerous tubular florets. Following flowering, the plant produces small achenes that are topped by a pappus of fine white hairs to facilitate wind dispersal. The overall structure is robust and herbaceous, often displaying a slightly glaucous or grayish-green coloration.
