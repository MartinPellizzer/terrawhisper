Cloves

Syzygium aromaticum

Cloves (Syzygium aromaticum) is a flowering plant native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia, known for its aromatic buds that are widely used in both culinary and medicinal contexts. Its primary medicinal uses include improving digestive health by relieing symptoms of indigestion, bloating, and flatulence, while also possessing antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that support overall wellness.

Botanical Identification

Cloves are the dried flower buds of Syzygium aromaticum, a member of the Myrtaceae family. Native to the Maluku Islands of Indonesia, they are a small, evergreen tree with aromatic leaves and red, woody flower buds. Commonly known as kayu manis in Indonesia and laurel in some regions, they have been used in herbal medicine for centuries.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Eudicots
  • Order: Sapindales
  • Family: Myrtaceae
  • Genus: Syzygium
  • Species: Syzygium aromaticum

Active Compounds

Cloves contain essential oil, terpenoids, and flavonoids as their main bioactive compounds. These compounds work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. The essential oil, rich in eugenol, is the primary contributor to its therapeutic properties.

  • Terpenoid
  • Flavonoid

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory infections.

Infusion: Used for colds, coughs, and as a general tonic.

Essential oil: Used for aromatherapy, stress relief, and skin conditions.

Safety Profile

Cloves are generally safe when used in moderate amounts. However, they should be avoided during pregnancy and by individuals with bleeding disorders due to their potential to increase bleeding risk. High doses may cause gastrointestinal irritation or allergic reactions in sensitive individuals.
