Datura Stramonium Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Datura stramonium, commonly known as jimsonweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas, characterized by its large, showy flowers and toxic alkaloids.

It has been traditionally used in various cultures for its psychoactive and medicinal properties, though it is highly toxic and should be handled with caution.

The herb contains bioactive constituents such as tropane alkaloids, including scopolamine, hyoscyamine, and atropine, which contribute to its therapeutic actions.

Herbal preparations such as infusions, tinctures, and poultices can be made from the dried leaves and flowers, though their use is limited due to the plant's potent and dangerous effects.
