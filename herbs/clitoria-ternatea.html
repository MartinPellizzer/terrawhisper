Clitoria ternatea

What's the taxonomical classification of Clitoria ternatea?

Clitoria ternatea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the order Fabales and the family Fabaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Clitoria, and its unique species designation, ternatea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clitoria ternatea has a prostrate or ascending herbaceous habit characterized by slender, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves consisting of three oval to lanceolate leaflets. The foliage typically features finely serrated margins and a soft, pubescent texture on both the upper and lower surfaces. Its reproductive structures are distinct, presenting as zygomorphic flowers that emerge from the leaf axils on short peduncles. These blooms exhibit a unique shape with a prominent standard petal that often displays vibrant blue or violet hues. The floral morphology is specifically adapted for insect pollination, featuring a specialized keel and wing structure.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, specifically found in countries such as Thailand, Malaysia, Indonesia, and parts of India. It thrives primarily in moist, lowland environments and tropical forest margins where humidity is high. The species is often distributed along coastal areas and riverine systems throughout the Malay Archipelago. What are the pharmacological activities of Clitoria ternatea?

This plant has potent analgesic, anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, antidiabetic, and neuroprotective pharmacological activities that make it a significant subject of medicinal research. Its bioactive compounds work to reduce pain perception and mitigate inflammatory responses in various biological models. The presence of diverse phytochemicals also allows the plant to scavenge free radicals and combat oxidative stress within the body. Furthermore, its antidiabetic properties help in regulating blood glucose levels, while its neuroprotective effects offer potential protection against neurological degeneration. These multifaceted therapeutic actions highlight the plant's potential for developing new pharmaceutical treatments.

As discussed in a study, Clitoria ternatea is traditionally used in India for a wide range of pharmacological activities including antimicrobial, antipyretic, anti-inflammatory, analgesic, diuretic, local anesthetic, antidiabetic, insecticidal, blood platelet aggregation-inhibiting, and diarrhea treatments. The ethanol extract of the plant, known as CTE, was administered at doses of 100-400 mg/kg, p.o. to rats. This extract produced dose-dependent and significant (P < 0.05-0.01) protection against diarrhea induced by castor oil and MgSO4. CTE also inhibited intestinal transit, delayed gastric emptying, delayed the onset of diarrhea, decreased defecation frequency, and reduced diarrhea severity when compared to loperamide at 10 mg/kg, p.o. These findings confirm the use of the plant as a remedy for the treatment, management, and/or control of diarrhea.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Furthermore, the triterpenoids lupeol and ursolic acid are recognized for their potential roles in supporting skin health and regulating metabolic processes. Together, these diverse chemical constituents form a complex profile of bioactive substances.
