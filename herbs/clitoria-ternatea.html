Butterfly pea

Clitoria ternatea

Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea) is a flowering plant native to tropical regions, known for its vibrant blue flowers and various traditional medicinal uses, particularly for improving cognitive function and alleviating symptoms of anxiety and depression.

Botanical Identification

Clitoria ternatea, commonly known as Butterfly Pea, belongs to the Fabaceae family. It is an annual plant native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its blue or purple pea-shaped flowers and trifoliate leaves. Synonyms in herbal literature include "Blue Pea" and "Butea."

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Fabales
  • Order: Fabales
  • Family: Fabaceae
  • Genus: Clitoria
  • Species: Clitoria ternatea

Active Compounds

The Butterfly Pea contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and neuroprotective effects. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for respiratory and digestive ailments.

Infusion: Used for mild ailments like colds and fatigue.

Powder: Used for digestive issues and as a general tonic.

Safety Profile

Butterfly pea is generally safe when used in typical food amounts. However, it may cause allergic reactions in some individuals. It should be avoided by people with known allergies to legumes or related plants.
