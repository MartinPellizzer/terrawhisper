What is Butterfly Pea (Clitoria Ternatea)?

Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea) is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, widely used in traditional herbal medicine for its vibrant blue flowers and various therapeutic properties.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Butterfly pea is Clitoria ternatea. This plant belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Fabales, order Fabales, family Fabaceae, genus Clitoria, and species Clitoria ternatea. Common names for this plant include Butterfly pea in English, while in Thai it is known as Kham puay. In Vietnamese, it is referred to as Bướm tím. Synonyms for this plant include Clitoria major as a botanical synonym and various regional names such as Blue pea in some parts of Southeast Asia.

Plant Description and Morphology

Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea) is a herbaceous perennial flowering plant native to tropical regions, characterized by its distinctive blue or purple pea-shaped flowers, trifoliate leaves, and climbing or trailing growth habit.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea) is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including countries such as India, Indonesia, Malaysia, and Thailand, and is commonly found growing in wetlands, riverbanks, and moist forest understories.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The plant parts used medicinally in Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea) include the flowers, leaves, seeds, and roots, with the flowers being the most commonly utilized for their medicinal properties.

Phytochemical Composition

The phytochemical composition of Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea) includes a variety of bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, alkaloids, glycosides, and polyphenols, with compounds like clitorin, 3,4-dihydroxybenzoic acid, and quercetin being prominently identified.

Pharmacological Properties

Butterfly pea (Clitoria ternatea) exhibits various pharmacological properties, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and neuroprotective effects, supported by both traditional use and modern scientific research.

Mechanisms of Action

The mechanisms of action of Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea) involve its bioactive compounds, such as alkaloids, flavonoids, and antioxidants, which contribute to its effects on the nervous system, cognitive function, and antioxidant activity.

Therapeutic Uses and Indications

Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea) has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential therapeutic effects, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and neuroprotective properties, with indications for conditions such as anxiety, depression, and inflammation.

Preparation Methods and Forms

The preparation methods and forms of Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea) include using the dried flowers in teas, powders, or tinctures, with the most common method being the infusion of dried flowers in hot water to create a blue-colored herbal tea.

The recommended dosage of Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea) varies depending on the form of the preparation, with typical oral dosages ranging from 500 to 1000 mg of standardized extract per day, divided into two or three doses, though it is important to consult a healthcare provider for personalized guidance.

While Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea) is generally considered safe when used in moderate amounts, it may cause mild gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals, and excessive consumption could lead to adverse effects, particularly in pregnant women and those with hormone-sensitive conditions.

Butterfly pea (Clitoria ternatea) is typically cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, with harvesting usually occurring 60 to 90 days after planting when the flowers are fully open and vibrant.
