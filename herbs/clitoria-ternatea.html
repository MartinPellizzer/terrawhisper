Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea) is a flowering plant native to tropical regions, known for its vibrant blue flowers and various traditional medicinal uses, particularly for improving cognitive function and alleviating symptoms of anxiety and depression.

What is Butterfly Pea (Clitoria Ternatea)?

Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea) is a flowering plant native to tropical regions, commonly used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported cognitive, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties.

Botanical Identity and Classification

Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea) is a flowering plant species belonging to the legume family Fabaceae, native to Southeast Asia and characterized by its distinctive blue or purple butterfly-shaped flowers and trifoliate leaves.

Common Names and Synonyms

Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea) is commonly known by various names such as Blue Pea, Pigeonwing, and Butea, and is also referred to by regional names like Kudamalai in Tamil, Butea in Sanskrit, and Kudamalai in Malayalam.

Plant Description and Morphology

The Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea) is a climbing legume characterized by its distinctive blue or purple pea-like flowers, trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets, and slender, twining stems that often root at the nodes.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea) is native to tropical regions of Asia, including India, Indonesia, and the Pacific Islands, and is commonly found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and riverbanks.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The leaves, flowers, and seeds of the Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea) are primarily used medicinally in traditional herbal practices.

Phytochemical Composition

The phytochemical composition of Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea) includes alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, glycosides, and steroids, which contribute to its medicinal properties and biological activities.

Pharmacological Properties

Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea) exhibits pharmacological properties including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and neuroprotective effects, supported by various in vitro and in vivo studies.

Mechanisms of Action

The mechanisms of action of Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea) involve its bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which exhibit antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and neuroprotective effects by scavenging free radicals, modulating inflammatory pathways, and enhancing cognitive function through the stimulation of neurotransmitter activity.

Therapeutic Uses and Indications

Butterfly pea is used to treat digestive issues, insomnia, inflammation, anxiety, depression, and skin conditions such as eczema and acne.

Preparation Methods and Forms

Butterfly pea is typically prepared as a herbal tea by steeping dried flowers in hot water, and it may also be used in the form of capsules, tinctures, or powdered extracts for various medicinal applications.

Dosage and Administration

The typical dosage of Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea) for herbal use ranges from 1 to 3 grams of dried flower per day, often prepared as a tea, with specific formulations varying based on traditional practices and intended therapeutic use.

Safety, Side Effects, and Contraindications

While butterfly pea is generally considered safe when used in moderate amounts, some individuals may experience mild side effects such as digestive discomfort, allergic reactions, or interactions with certain medications.

Drug Interactions

Butterfly pea may interact with medications that affect the central nervous system, such as sedatives, hypnotics, or antidepressants, due to its potential sedative and antispasmodic properties.

Toxicity and Precautions

While Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea) is generally considered safe when used in moderate amounts, it may cause mild gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals, and there is limited research on its long-term safety, so it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before use, especially during pregnancy or for those with pre-existing medical conditions.

Cultivation and Harvesting

Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea) is typically cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soil and full sunlight, with regular watering and moderate fertilization to promote growth and flower production.

Processing and Extraction

Processing butterfly pea involves harvesting the flowers when they are fully open, drying them in a shaded area to preserve their color and active compounds, and then grinding them into a fine powder for use in herbal preparations.

Storage and Shelf Life

Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea) should be stored in an airtight container in a cool, dark, and dry place to maintain its potency and extend its shelf life, which is typically 1-2 years when properly stored.

Scientific Research and Evidence

Scientific research on Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea) has explored its potential pharmacological effects, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and neuroprotective properties, with studies suggesting its use in traditional medicine may support cognitive function and skin health.

Traditional and Historical Uses

Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea) has been traditionally used in various cultures for its purported medicinal properties, including as a remedy for digestive issues, skin conditions, and as a mild sedative, with historical records tracing its use back to ancient Ayurvedic and traditional Chinese medicine practices.

Regulatory Status

In many countries, Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea) is considered a traditional herb and is not classified as a drug, but its use in food and herbal products is regulated under local food safety and herbal medicine guidelines.
