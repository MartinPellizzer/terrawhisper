Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

What is Butterfly Pea (Clitoria Ternatea)?

Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea) is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, widely used in traditional herbal medicine for its vibrant blue flowers and various therapeutic properties.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Butterfly pea is Clitoria ternatea. This plant belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Fabales, order Fabales, family Fabaceae, genus Clitoria, and species Clitoria ternatea. Common names for this plant include Butterfly pea in English, while in Thai it is known as Kham puay. In Vietnamese, it is referred to as Bướm tím. Synonyms for this plant include Clitoria major as a botanical synonym and various regional names such as Blue pea in some parts of Southeast Asia.

Plant Description and Morphology

Butterfly pea is an erect annual herb that typically grows to a height of 30-100 cm with a fibrous root system.

Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval to elliptic leaflets with serrated edges. The flowers are showy, butterfly-like, with a distinctive blue or purple hue, often with a yellow throat, and emit a mild, sweet fragrance. The petals are velvety in texture and have a slightly waxy surface.

The plant has a short, branching stem covered in fine hairs.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Butterfly pea is native to Southeast Asia, particularly regions such as India, Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines.

It is commonly found in tropical and subtropical areas, extending into parts of Africa and the Pacific Islands. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil and full sunlight. It prefers areas with high rainfall and is often found in open fields, along roadsides, and in disturbed habitats.

Its natural range includes regions with moderate to high altitudes, typically between sea level and 1,500 meters.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The primary plant parts used medicinally in butterfly pea are the flowers, leaves, and seeds.

The flowers are most commonly utilized for their vibrant color and medicinal properties. They are harvested when fully open to ensure maximum potency. Leaves are often used in traditional remedies for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects.

Seeds are occasionally employed for their nutritional content and potential therapeutic benefits.

Secondary metabolites such as kaempferol, quercetin, and isorhamnetin are prominent in the plant and are responsible for its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. These compounds are often identified through chemical markers like total polyphenol content and antioxidant activity measurements. The presence of these bioactive substances supports its traditional use in herbal medicine.

Therapeutic Uses

The therapeutic uses of Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea) are well-documented in traditional herbal medicine, particularly for its potential to support cognitive function and alleviate symptoms of anxiety and depression.

It is commonly used to treat conditions such as memory loss, insomnia, and mood disorders due to its rich content of bioactive compounds like flavonoids and antioxidants. The plant is also employed in the management of inflammation and digestive issues, often prepared as a tea or tincture. Preparation Methods and Forms

The preparation methods of Butterfly Pea involve various forms in which the plant is utilized.

It is commonly used as a powder, where the dried leaves are ground into a fine powder for internal consumption. The leaves can also be brewed as a tea by steeping them in hot water, providing a natural source of antioxidants. Additionally, the flowers are sometimes used to make infusions or extracts, which are applied topically or ingested.

The plant is occasionally processed into capsules or tablets for convenience in herbal supplementation.
