Hydrocotyle benguetensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Hydrocotyle benguetensis?

Hydrocotyle benguetensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Apiales and into the family Aralaceae. Ultimately, the plant is identified within the genus Hydrocotyle, with its specific designation being the species benguetensis.

What are the therapeutic actions of Hydrocotyle benguetensis?

Hydrocotyle benguetensis has wound-healing, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties that facilitate the treatment of skin lesions and minor infections. The plant contains bioactive compounds that stimulate tissue regeneration and accelerate the closure of open wounds. It serves as a natural agent to reduce swelling and soothe localized irritation in various dermatological conditions. Additionally, its phytochemical constituents act against certain pathogenic microbes to prevent secondary infections in damaged skin. These therapeutic actions make it a valuable resource in traditional medicinal practices for managing cutaneous ailments.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, inflammation, and various ailments related to the digestive system. Local practitioners often apply its crushed leaves topically to facilitate the healing of sores and rashes. Some traditional medicinal practices involve using the plant to alleviate internal discomfort or stomach-related issues. The medicinal properties are thought to stem from the plant's natural chemical compounds that target localized irritation. These applications reflect its role in indigenous healthcare systems for managing minor physical traumas.
