Hydrocotyle benguetensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hydrocotyle benguetensis has creeping stolons that produce small, orbicular to reniform leaves with crenate margins and long petioles. The leaves are typically green, sometimes displaying a slightly yellowish hue, and can reach sizes of several millimeters in diameter. Its flowers are minute, white or greenish, and are arranged in small umbels atop slender peduncles. The plant develops a dense mat-like growth habit through its spreading rhizomatous structure. This morphology allows it to colonize moist soil surfaces effectively in its natural highland habitats.
