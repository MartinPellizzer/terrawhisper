Pittosporum perglabratum

What's the taxonomical classification of Pittosporum perglabratum?

Pittosporum perglabratum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Apiales. This plant is a representative of the family Pittosporaceae, specifically situated within the genus Pittosporum, and is identified by its unique specific epithet, perglabratum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pittosporum perglabratum has elliptical to lanceolate leaves that are leathery in texture, glossy on the upper surface, and typically measure between 3 and 10 centimeters in length. The margins of these leaves are entire and slightly revolute, meaning they curl slightly downward toward the underside. Its stems are woody and can develop a somewhat rough bark as the plant matures. The inflorescence consists of small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These blossoms eventually give way to small, globose capsules that split open to reveal bright orange or red seeds.
