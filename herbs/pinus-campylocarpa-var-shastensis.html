Pinus campylocarpa var. shastensis

Pinus campylocarpa var. shastensis, commonly known as Shasta fir, is a coniferous tree species native to the Klamath Mountains in California and Oregon, United States.

It is valued for its essential oils, which have been traditionally used to support respiratory health, including relieving coughs and colds.

The therapeutic actions of Shasta fir include expectorant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, which may help to soothe and calm the body.

The bioactive constituents of Shasta fir include terpenes, such as pinene and limonene, which have been shown to possess antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory activities.
