Melanoseris cyanea

What's the taxonomical classification of Melanoseris cyanea?

Melanoseris cyanea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Asterales. It is a member of the family Asteraceae, falling specifically into the genus Melanoseris, and is identified by its unique species designation, cyanea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Melanoseris cyanea has a perennial growth habit characterized by a woody base, ovate to lanceolate leaves with entire or slightly serrated margins, and distinctive blue-tinted flower heads.

The foliage typically exhibits a soft texture and can vary in size depending on the specific environmental conditions of its habitat. Its inflorescence consists of small, composite flower heads that are often clustered together atop slender, upright stems. The reproductive structures feature fine, hair-like florets that contribute to the plant's delicate appearance.
