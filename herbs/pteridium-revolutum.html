Pteridium revolutum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pteridium revolutum has large, triangular fronds that can reach up to one meter in length, featuring deeply lobed pinnae with serrated margins and distinctively revolute or rolled leaf edges. The stipes are typically dark brown to blackish and are covered in fine, brownish scales. Each pinna is divided into smaller pinnules that exhibit a characteristic texture and arrangement. The underside of the foliage contains dense clusters of sporangia arranged in sori, which are often protected by a false indusium formed by the leaf margin. These morphological traits collectively distinguish this fern species from its relatives within the Pteridaceae family.
