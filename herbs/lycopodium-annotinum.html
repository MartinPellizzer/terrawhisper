Lycopodium annotinum

What's the taxonomical classification of Lycopodium annotinum?

Lycopodium annotinum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Lycopodiidae. Following its taxonomic descent, the plant is placed in the order Lycopodiales and belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Lycopodium and is specifically designated as the species annotinum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lycopodium annotinum has a creeping, branched rhizome that produces upright, dichotomously branched stems covered in small, lanceolate, spreading leaves arranged in several rows. These leaves are typically bright green and possess a distinctively narrow, pointed shape that wraps slightly around the stem. The plant lacks true roots, instead utilizing its horizontal rhizomes to spread and anchor itself across the forest floor. Reproductive structures called strobili appear at the tips of the branches, consisting of densely packed, cone-like clusters of sporophylls. These specialized structures are responsible for producing and releasing the spores necessary for the species' propagation.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains medicinal alkaloids and coumarins that provide various therapeutic benefits. The alkaloids present in the plant often interact with the nervous system to alleviate pain or reduce inflammation. Meanwhile, the coumarins serve as potent antioxidants and can assist in improving blood circulation. These specific chemical profiles make the plant a valuable subject for pharmaceutical research. Scientists continue to study how these combined compounds can be synthesized for modern medicine.

As discussed in a study, the research aimed to evaluate the safety and pharmacological activity of alkaloids and coumarins obtained from Lycopodium annotinum L. The investigation utilized in vivo studies to assess the influence of these tested compounds on the central nervous system of rats through behavioral tests including locomotor activity, Y-maze, and passive avoidance. To investigate the mechanisms of action, biochemical determinations were performed regarding AChE activity, BChE activity, and the concentrations of IL-1β and IL-6. Safety was assessed by determining the concentrations of AST, ALT, GGT, urea, and creatinine. The results of the conducted studies indicate a high safety profile for the tested compounds. Behavioral tests showed that these compounds significantly improved rodent memory in a passive avoidance test.
