Loudetia phragmitoides

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Loudetia phragmitoides has a woody, spreading habit characterized by branches that are often arching or pendulous. The leaves are pinnately compound with several pairs of leaflets that exhibit a leathery texture. These leaflets typically possess an elliptical or oblong shape with entire margins and acute tips. The foliage often displays a deep green color on the upper surface and may appear slightly lighter underneath. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves to eventually produce small, fleshy fruits.
