By Martin Pellizzer
0 minutes

Centella asiatica, also known as gotu kola, is a medicinal plant that has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

When used improperly, it can cause a number of adverse health effects. First, high doses of Centella asiatica may lead to gastrointestinal issues such as nausea, stomach upset, and diarrhea.

Additionally, it may cause headaches, dizziness, and mouth ulcers. Prolonged use or high doses may result in liver toxicity, which can manifest as jaundice, dark urine, and fatigue. Furthermore, allergic reactions, including skin rashes and itching, have been reported in some cases.

Pregnant or breastfeeding women should avoid using Centella asiatica, as its safety during these periods is not well-established.
