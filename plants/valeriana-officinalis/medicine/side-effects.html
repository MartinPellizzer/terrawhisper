Valeriana officinalis, also known as valerian root, is a popular herbal supplement often used for its calming and sleep-promoting effects.

However, if used improperly, it can lead to several potential health issues. First, overuse or misuse of valerian root can cause stomach discomfort, including nausea, bloating, and cramping. Additionally, it may interact with other central nervous system depressants, such as alcohol, benzodiazepines, and opioids, leading to increased drowsiness, cognitive impairment, and even respiratory depression.

In some cases, valerian root has been linked to headaches, dizziness, and dry mouth. Moreover, long-term use or high doses of valerian root can result in withdrawal symptoms, such as restlessness, anxiety, and insomnia, when discontinued abruptly. Pregnant women, breastfeeding mothers, and children should avoid using valerian root, as its safety and efficacy in these populations have not been adequately studied.

Always consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new supplement or medication regimen.
