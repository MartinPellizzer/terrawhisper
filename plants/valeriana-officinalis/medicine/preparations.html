10 Best Medicinal Preparations of Valeriana officinalis (Valerian)

Valeriana officinalis, also known as valerian root, has been used for centuries as a medicinal preparation to treat a variety of ailments.

One popular method of consumption is through the preparation of a tisane, or herbal tea, where the dried root is steeped in hot water. This soothing beverage is often used to alleviate symptoms of anxiety, insomnia, and restlessness due to its calming properties.

Additionally, valerian root can be made into a tincture, an alcohol-based extract, which can be taken orally. Tinctures are typically more potent than tisanes and are used for similar medicinal purposes, as well as to treat digestive issues such as irritable bowel syndrome.

It is important to note that valerian root can interact with certain medications and should be used under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

1. Infusion

Valeriana Officinalis infusion is a medicinal preparation made by steeping dried Valerian root in hot water, which releases the plant's beneficial compounds into the water.

This infusion is commonly used to promote relaxation, reduce anxiety, and improve sleep quality due to its calming and sedative properties. The main plant parts used for this preparation are the roots of the Valerian plant, which contain high concentrations of active compounds such as valerenic acid and volatile oils.

To make a Valerian Officinalis infusion, pour boiling water over 1-2 teaspoons of dried Valerian root and let it steep for 5-10 minutes before straining. It is important to note that Valerian infusion can cause drowsiness and should be used with caution when operating heavy machinery or driving.

Additionally, it may interact with certain medications, such as sedatives and antidepressants, so it is recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before use.

2. Tincture

Valeriana Officinalis tincture is a medicinal preparation made by soaking the valerian root in alcohol or another solvent to extract its active compounds.

This form of valerian is commonly used for its sedative and anxiolytic effects, making it a popular natural remedy for insomnia, anxiety, and stress-related conditions. The root of the valerian plant is primarily used in the preparation of this tincture, as it contains the highest concentration of therapeutic constituents such as valerenic acid and volatile oils. To make the tincture, valerian root is finely chopped and steeped in a solvent, typically 45% alcohol, for several weeks before being strained and bottled.

It is important to follow the recommended dosage and consult with a healthcare provider before using valerian tincture, as it can interact with certain medications and may cause side effects such as dizziness, headache, and stomach upset in some individuals.

3. Dry extract

Valeriana Officinalis dry extract is a standardized form of the Valerian plant, produced by extracting and concentrating the beneficial compounds found in the plant's roots and underground stems, then removing the solvent and drying the extract.

The main health benefits of this Valerian preparation include its ability to promote relaxation, reduce anxiety, improve sleep quality, and alleviate symptoms of menopause. The roots and underground stems of the Valerian plant are the primary plant parts used for this preparation.

To make the dry extract, the plant material is first soaked in a solvent, such as ethanol or water, to extract the beneficial compounds. The solvent is then evaporated, leaving behind a concentrated extract that is dried and standardized to contain a consistent amount of active compounds. When using Valeriana Officinalis dry extract, it is important to follow the recommended dosage and not to use it for extended periods without consulting a healthcare professional, as it can cause side effects such as dizziness, headache, and stomach upset.

Additionally, it may interact with certain medications, such as sedatives and antidepressants, so it is important to inform your healthcare provider if you are taking any medications before using this Valerian preparation.

4. Fluid extract

Valeriana Officinalis fluid extract is a concentrated solution made by extracting the beneficial compounds from the Valerian plant using alcohol or water.

Its main health benefits include promoting relaxation, reducing anxiety, improving sleep quality, and alleviating symptoms of menopause. The roots and rhizomes of the Valerian plant are primarily used to make this fluid extract.

To prepare the extract, the plant material is soaked in a solvent, such as alcohol or glycerin, and then pressed to release the liquid. It is essential to follow the recommended dosage, as taking too much can cause side effects such as headaches, dizziness, and stomach problems.

Additionally, Valerian may interact with other medications, so it is crucial to consult a healthcare professional before use, especially for those who are pregnant, breastfeeding, or have existing medical conditions.

5. Capsules

Valeriana Officinalis capsules are dietary supplements that contain the dried root of the Valerian plant, which is commonly known as Valerian root.

The main health benefits of this preparation include promoting relaxation, reducing anxiety, and improving sleep quality. The root and underground stems (rhizomes) of the Valerian plant are used to make these capsules.

To prepare Valerian Officinalis capsules, the dried root is finely ground and encapsulated in gelatin or vegetarian capsule shells. When using Valerian Officinalis capsules, it is essential to follow the recommended dosage on the product label, as overuse may lead to side effects such as dizziness, headaches, and stomach discomfort. It is also advisable to consult with a healthcare provider before taking Valerian Officinalis capsules, especially if you are currently taking other medications or have a medical condition.

Pregnant or breastfeeding women should avoid using Valerian Officinalis capsules, as there is not enough research on their safety for these populations.

6. Tablets

Valeriana Officinalis tablets are a type of medicinal preparation made from the dried roots and rhizomes of the Valeriana Officinalis plant, also known as valerian.

The main health benefits of this preparation include its ability to promote relaxation, reduce anxiety, and improve sleep quality. The roots and rhizomes of the valerian plant are harvested, cleaned, dried, and then ground into a fine powder to make the tablets.

To make the tablets, the powder is mixed with a binding agent, compressed into shape, and then dried. When using Valeriana Officinalis tablets, it is important to follow the recommended dosage and to not exceed it, as overuse can lead to side effects such as headaches, dizziness, and stomach upset. Additionally, it is not recommended to use valerian in combination with alcohol or sedatives, as it can increase their effects.

It is also important to consult with a healthcare provider before using valerian if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or have a pre-existing medical condition.

7. Soft extract

Valeriana Officinalis soft extract is a standardized preparation of the Valerian plant, typically containing specific levels of valuable compounds, such as valerenic acid, to ensure consistent quality and effectiveness.

The main health benefits of this Valerian preparation include promoting relaxation, improving sleep quality, reducing anxiety, and alleviating symptoms of menopause. The primary plant parts used for this preparation are the roots and underground stems (rhizomes) of Valeriana Officinalis, as they contain the highest concentration of active compounds. To prepare the soft extract, the dried roots and rhizomes are first cleaned, chopped, and then extracted using a solvent, such as water, alcohol, or glycerin, to draw out the beneficial compounds.

The resulting extract is then concentrated, filtered, and standardized to specific valerenic acid levels. When using Valeriana Officinalis soft extract, it is essential to follow the recommended dosage, as higher doses may cause side effects like dizziness, headaches, or stomach discomfort. Additionally, Valerian may interact with certain medications, such as sedatives, antidepressants, or benzodiazepines, so it is crucial to consult a healthcare professional before starting any new supplement regimen.

Pregnant or breastfeeding women should avoid using Valerian, as its safety during these periods has not been sufficiently studied.

8. Essence

Valeriana Officinalis essence is a potent, concentrated liquid extract derived from the Valerian plant, known scientifically as Valeriana officinalis, which has been used in traditional medicine for centuries due to its sedative and calming properties.

The main health benefits of this Valerian preparation include promoting relaxation, reducing anxiety, improving sleep quality, and alleviating symptoms of stress and menstrual cramps. To make Valerian essence, the roots and rhizomes are typically harvested, dried, and then steeped in a solvent such as alcohol or glycerin to extract the active compounds.

It is important to follow the recommended dosage and consult with a healthcare provider before using Valerian essence, as it can interact with certain medications and may cause side effects such as dizziness, headache, and stomach upset in some individuals.

9. Ointment

Valeriana Officinalis ointment is a topical preparation made from the extracts of the Valerian plant, also known as Valeriana Officinalis, which is well-known for its calming and soothing properties.

The main health benefits of this Valeriana Officinalis preparation include providing relief from inflammation, muscle pain, and skin irritations, and it may also promote relaxation and better sleep. The root and rhizome of the Valerian plant are mainly used for this preparation due to their high concentration of active compounds.

To make Valeriana Officinalis ointment, the dried and powdered Valerian root and rhizome are infused in a carrier oil, such as olive or coconut oil, and then mixed with beeswax to thicken the mixture and create a smooth, spreadable ointment. When using Valeriana Officinalis ointment, it is important to perform a patch test first to check for any skin sensitivities, and to avoid applying it to broken or irritated skin.

Additionally, it is recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before using Valeriana Officinalis ointment if pregnant, breastfeeding, or taking any medications, as it may interact with certain drugs.
