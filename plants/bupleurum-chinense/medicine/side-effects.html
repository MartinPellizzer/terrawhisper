Bupleurum chinense, a plant commonly used in traditional Chinese medicine, can have several potential adverse health effects if used improperly.

Firstly, it may cause liver toxicity, which can lead to symptoms such as jaundice, abdominal pain, and loss of appetite. Secondly, it can affect the menstrual cycle and lead to irregular periods, as well as increase the risk of miscarriage in pregnant women.

Thirdly, it can cause skin irritations, such as rashes and itchiness, particularly when applied topically. Fourthly, it may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and sedatives, and result in dangerous side effects. Lastly, excessive consumption can lead to digestive problems, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea.

Therefore, it is crucial to use Bupleurum chinense under the guidance of a healthcare professional to minimize these risks.
