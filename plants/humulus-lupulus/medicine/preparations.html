10 Best Medicinal Preparations of Humulus lupulus (Hops)

Humulus lupulus, also known as hops, is a plant that has been used for centuries in medicinal preparations.

One popular preparation is a tisane, or herbal tea, made from the leaves, flowers, and sometimes even the stems of the plant. This tea is often used as a natural remedy for anxiety, restlessness, and insomnia due to its mild sedative properties.

Another common preparation is a tincture, which is made by soaking the plant material in alcohol to extract its active compounds. The resulting tincture can be taken orally and is often used to treat digestive issues such as loss of appetite, indigestion, and excessive gas. Additionally, hops have been found to have antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties, making them a potential natural remedy for skin conditions and infections.

However, it is important to note that high doses of hops can have side effects such as increased heart rate and dizziness, and it is always recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new medicinal preparations.

1. Infusion

Humulus Lupulus infusion is a medicinal preparation made by steeping the flowers or cones of the hop plant in hot water, releasing the beneficial compounds into the liquid.

The main health benefits of this preparation include its sedative and analgesic properties, making it useful for treating insomnia, restlessness, and various types of pain. The main plant parts used for this preparation are the flowers or cones of the hop plant, which contain the medicinal compounds. To make a Humulus Lupulus infusion, place 1-2 teaspoons of dried hop flowers in a cup and pour boiling water over them, letting it steep for 10-15 minutes before straining.

When using this Humulus Lupulus preparation, it is important to be aware of the main precautions, such as not using it during pregnancy or breastfeeding, avoiding large doses, and being cautious when combining it with other sedatives or depressants.

2. Tincture

Humulus Lupulus tincture is a medicinal preparation made by soaking the leaves, flowers, or stems of the hop plant in a solvent, usually alcohol or glycerin, to extract its active compounds.

The main health benefits of this preparation include its sedative and anxiety-reducing properties, its ability to treat sleep disorders and restlessness, and its potential use in alleviating menopause symptoms such as hot flashes and irritability. The flowers, also known as hops cones, are the primary plant parts used for this tincture.

To make the tincture, the hop flowers or other plant parts are steeped in a solvent for several weeks, then strained and stored in a sealed container. When using Humulus Lupulus tincture, it is important to follow the recommended dosage, as excessive use may cause digestive issues or skin irritation.

Additionally, pregnant or nursing women, individuals with hormone-sensitive conditions, and those taking sedative medications should avoid using this tincture, as it may interact with these factors.

3. Decoction

Humulus Lupulus decoction is a medicinal preparation made by gently simmering the leaves, flowers, and sometimes even the stems of the hop plant in water for a prolonged period of time.

This preparation is known for its calming and sedative properties, making it an effective remedy for anxiety, restlessness, and sleep disturbances. The main plant parts used for this preparation are the female flowers, also known as hop strobiles, which are particularly rich in aromatic compounds and bitter acids.

To make a Humulus Lupulus decoction, place 1-2 teaspoons of dried hop flowers in a pot with 1 cup of water, bring to a low simmer, and let it steep for 10-15 minutes. It's important to note that Humulus Lupulus decoction should not be used by pregnant or breastfeeding women, as it may have estrogenic effects.

Additionally, it may interact with certain medications, such as sedatives and antidepressants, so it's always best to consult with a healthcare provider before use.

4. Fluid extract

Humulus Lupulus fluid extract is a concentrated, alcohol-based herbal preparation made from the flowers, leaves, and stems of the hop plant.

This extract is known for its sedative and analgesic properties, making it a popular natural remedy for anxiety, insomnia, and restlessness. It is also used to alleviate symptoms of menopause, such as hot flashes and night sweats.

The preparation involves soaking the hop plant parts in a solvent, typically alcohol or glycerin, to extract the active compounds. It is important to note that Humulus Lupulus fluid extract should be used with caution, as it can interact with certain medications and may cause side effects such as drowsiness, dry mouth, and digestive issues.

It is recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before using this preparation, especially for those who are pregnant, breastfeeding, or have a medical condition.

5. Dry extract

Humulus Lupulus dry extract is a concentrated form of hops, a plant that is best known for its use in beer-making but also has medicinal applications.

The main health benefits of this Humulus lupulus preparation include its ability to alleviate anxiety, promote sleep, and act as a mild sedative. The main plant parts used for this Humulus lupulus preparation are the flowers, which are rich in bitter acids and essential oils.

To make the extract, the flowers are dried and then subjected to a process called extraction, which uses a solvent to extract the desired compounds. When using Humulus lupulus dry extract, it is important to follow the recommended dosage, as taking too much can lead to increased drowsiness and sedation. Additionally, it is not recommended for use during pregnancy, as it may stimulate uterine contractions.

As with any supplement, it is always a good idea to consult with a healthcare provider before starting use.

6. Powder

Humulus Lupulus powder is a type of medicinal preparation made from the flowers and cones of the hop plant, which are dried and then ground into a fine powder.

The main health benefits of this Humulus Lupulus preparation include its ability to treat anxiety, insomnia, and restlessness due to its mild sedative properties. The main plant parts used for this Humulus Lupulus preparation are the flowers and cones, which contain various compounds such as lupulin, resins, and volatile oils that contribute to its medicinal properties.

To make Humulus Lupulus powder, the dried flowers and cones are carefully harvested, cleaned, and then ground into a fine powder using a grinder or a mortar and pestle. When using Humulus Lupulus powder, it is important to start with a low dose and gradually increase as needed, as it can cause drowsiness and may interact with other medications.

It is also recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before using Humulus Lupulus powder, especially for those who are pregnant, breastfeeding, or have a medical condition.

7. Capsules

Humulus Lupulus capsules are dietary supplements that contain extracts from the hop plant, known scientifically as Humulus lupulus.

The main health benefits of this preparation include promoting relaxation, improving sleep quality, and alleviating symptoms of menopause such as hot flashes and anxiety. The main plant parts used for this preparation are the flowers or strobiles of the hop plant, which contain various compounds including bitter acids, essential oils, and flavonoids. To make Humulus Lupulus capsules, the hop flowers are dried, extracted with a solvent, and then encapsulated in gelatin or vegetarian capsules.

When using Humulus Lupulus capsules, it is important to follow the recommended dosage and consult with a healthcare provider if you have any pre-existing medical conditions or are taking other medications, as hops may interact with certain drugs and may not be suitable for individuals with certain health conditions such as depression or hormone-sensitive cancers.

8. Compress

Humulus Lupulus compress is a type of medicinal preparation made from the flowers, leaves, and stems of the hop plant, Humulus lupulus.

This compress has anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and analgesic properties, making it a popular natural remedy for various health issues. To make a Humulus Lupulus compress, steep the hop flowers, leaves, and stems in hot water for several minutes, then strain and allow the liquid to cool.

Soak a clean cloth in the cooled liquid and apply it to the affected area for 10-15 minutes, several times a day. When using a Humulus Lupulus compress, it's important to avoid applying it to open wounds or broken skin, as this can increase the risk of infection.

Additionally, people who are allergic to hops or have sensitive skin should use this compress with caution, as it may cause irritation or an allergic reaction.

9. Poultice

Humulus Lupulus poultice is a medicinal preparation made from the flowers, leaves, and stems of the hop plant, which is commonly known as brewer's hops.

This preparation has anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and sedative properties, making it a popular remedy for skin irritations, muscle pain, and insomnia. To make a Humulus Lupulus poultice, fresh or dried hop flowers, leaves, and stems are crushed and mixed with a small amount of water to create a paste, which is then applied directly to the skin. It's important to note that Humulus Lupulus poultice should not be used by pregnant or breastfeeding women, and it may cause skin irritation in some individuals.

Additionally, it's recommended to do a patch test before applying the poultice to a large area of skin.

10. Ointment

Humulus Lupulus ointment is a topical preparation made from the flowers of the hop plant, also known as Hops.

The main health benefits of this ointment include its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, making it useful for relieving pain and inflammation in conditions such as arthritis, bruises, and skin irritations. The flowers of the hop plant are used to make this ointment.

To make the ointment, dried hop flowers are typically steeped in a carrier oil, such as olive or almond oil, and then combined with beeswax to thicken the mixture. Once cooled, this mixture becomes an ointment that can be applied directly to the skin. When using Humulus Lupulus ointment, it is important to test a small amount on a patch of skin first to ensure that you do not have an adverse reaction.

Additionally, it is recommended to avoid using the ointment on broken or irritated skin, and to keep it out of reach of children and pets.
