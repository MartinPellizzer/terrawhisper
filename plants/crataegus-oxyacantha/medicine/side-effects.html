By Martin Pellizzer
0 minutes

Crataegus oxyacantha, also known as Hawthorn, is a plant that has been used in traditional medicine for centuries, but if used improperly, it can have several potential health side effects.

First, it can interact negatively with other medications, particularly those that lower blood pressure, leading to dangerously low levels. Additionally, hawthorn may cause digestive issues such as nausea, stomach upset, and diarrhea when taken in large quantities.

It can also lead to dizziness, headaches, and fatigue due to its impact on the cardiovascular system. In rare cases, it can cause allergic reactions, resulting in skin rashes, itching, and difficulty breathing.

Pregnant women and people with heart conditions should avoid hawthorn as it may stimulate uterine contractions and have adverse effects on heart rhythms, respectively.
