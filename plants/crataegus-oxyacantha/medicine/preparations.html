10 Best Medicinal Preparations of Crataegus oxyacantha (Hawthorn)

Crataegus oxyacantha, also known as Hawthorn, is a well-known medicinal plant that has been used in various preparations such as tisanes and tinctures.

A tisane, or herbal tea, can be made by steeping the leaves, flowers, and/or berries of the Hawthorn plant in hot water. This soothing drink is often used to treat mild digestive complaints and to promote relaxation.

A tincture, on the other hand, is a more concentrated extract made by soaking the plant material in alcohol. Hawthorn tincture is commonly used to support cardiovascular health, particularly in cases of mild heart weakness or irregular heartbeat.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new medicinal regimen, as Hawthorn can interact with certain medications and may have side effects.

1. Infusion

Crataegus Oxyacantha infusion is a medicinal preparation made by steeping the leaves, flowers, and berries of the Hawthorn plant in hot water.

The main health benefits of this preparation include improving cardiovascular health, reducing blood pressure, and alleviating symptoms of stress and anxiety. The leaves, flowers, and berries are the main plant parts used for this preparation. To make a Hawthorn infusion, pour boiling water over the plant parts and let it steep for 10-15 minutes before straining.

It is important to consult with a healthcare provider before using Hawthorn infusion, as it can interact with certain medications and may have side effects for people with low blood pressure or who are pregnant.
