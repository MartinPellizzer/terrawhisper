10 Best Medicinal Preparations of Polygonum multiflorum (Fo Ti)

Polygonum multiflorum, also known as He Shou Wu or Fo-ti, is a plant that has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries.

Its medicinal preparations typically involve extracts of the plant's root, which is rich in antioxidants and alkaloids. Two common forms of Polygonum multiflorum preparations are tisanes and tinctures. A tisane is an herbal tea made by steeping the plant's roots in hot water. This method gently extracts the beneficial compounds from the root and allows for easy consumption.

Tinctures, on the other hand, are made by soaking the roots in alcohol or glycerin. This extraction method extracts a wider range of compounds, including some that may not be water-soluble. Tinctures are typically taken in smaller doses than tisanes, as they are more concentrated. Both tisanes and tinctures of Polygonum multiflorum have been used to treat a variety of health conditions, including constipation, high cholesterol, and graying hair.

However, it is important to note that more research is needed to fully understand the medicinal properties and potential side effects of this plant.

1. tea

Polygonum Multiflorum tea is an infusion made from the roots of the Polygonum multiflorum plant, also known as Fo Ti.

This tea has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries due to its numerous health benefits, including improving liver and kidney function, slowing down the aging process, and alleviating conditions such as constipation and insomnia. The roots of the plant are harvested, cleaned, and dried before being steeped in hot water to make the tea.

To prepare the tea, simply place a teaspoon of the dried root in a tea infuser and pour boiling water over it, letting it steep for 5-10 minutes. Some precautions should be taken when using Polygonum multiflorum tea, such as avoiding it during pregnancy and breastfeeding, and not using it if you have a history of liver or kidney disease.
