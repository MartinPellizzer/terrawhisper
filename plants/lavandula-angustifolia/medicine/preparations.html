10 Best Medicinal Preparations of Lavandula angustifolia (Lavender)

Lavandula angustifolia, also known as lavender, is a plant that is well-known for its calming and soothing properties.

Its flowers and leaves are used to make medicinal preparations such as tisanes and tinctures. A tisane, or herbal tea, made from lavender can be consumed to help promote relaxation and improve sleep.

The dried flowers can also be used in a sachet or pillow to promote a calming environment. A tincture, which is an alcohol-based extract, can be made from lavender and used to treat anxiety, depression, and restlessness. Additionally, lavender tincture can be used topically to treat wounds, burns, and skin irritations.

It's important to note that, as with any herbal remedy, it's important to consult with a healthcare provider before using lavender for medicinal purposes.

1. Infusion

Lavandula Angustifolia infusion is a medicinal preparation made by steeping the flowers of the Lavender plant in hot water, which releases the plant's beneficial compounds into the water.

This infusion has several health benefits, including promoting relaxation, aiding in digestion, reducing anxiety and depression, and alleviating headaches and migraines. The main plant parts used for this preparation are the flowers of the Lavandula angustifolia plant.

To make a Lavandula Angustifolia infusion, pour boiling water over the lavender flowers and let them steep for 5-10 minutes. The main precautions to take when using this Lavandula angustifolia preparation include avoiding it during pregnancy and breastfeeding, and being mindful of any potential allergic reactions.

Additionally, it is recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before using lavender infusion if you are taking any medications or have any pre-existing medical conditions.

2. Tincture

Lavandula Angustifolia tincture is a medicinal preparation made by soaking lavender flowers in a solution of alcohol and water, which extracts the beneficial compounds of the plant.

The main health benefits of this preparation include its ability to promote relaxation, improve sleep quality, alleviate anxiety and depression, and provide relief for headaches and migraines. The main plant parts used for this tincture are the flowers of the Lavandula angustifolia plant, which are rich in therapeutic compounds such as linalool and linalyl acetate. To make the tincture, the lavender flowers are steeped in a solution of 25-60% alcohol for several weeks, then pressed and filtered to produce a potent, concentrated extract.

When using Lavandula angustifolia tincture, it is important to follow the recommended dosage and consult with a healthcare provider if you have any pre-existing medical conditions or are taking any medications, as lavender can interact with certain drugs and may have side effects in some individuals.

3. Oil

Lavandula Angustifolia oil, also known as lavender essential oil, is obtained from the flowers and leaves of the Lavandula angustifolia plant through steam distillation.

The main health benefits of this preparation include promoting relaxation, reducing anxiety, improving sleep quality, alleviating headaches, and providing skin care. The flowers and leaves are the primary plant parts used for extraction.

To make lavender oil, fresh or dried flowers and leaves are placed in a still and subjected to steam, which releases the oil from the plant material. The oil is then collected and filtered for purity. When using lavender oil, it is important to dilute it with a carrier oil before applying it to the skin, and avoid ingesting it without consulting a healthcare professional.

Additionally, it is recommended to perform a patch test before using it on larger areas of the skin, as some people may experience skin irritation or allergic reactions.

4. Salve

Lavandula Angustifolia salve is a medicinal preparation made from the essential oil and dried flowers of the Lavandula angustifolia plant, also known as lavender.

The main health benefits of this salve include its ability to promote relaxation, reduce anxiety, improve sleep quality, and alleviate skin irritations such as eczema, acne, and burns. The main plant parts used for this preparation are the flowers and leaves of the lavender plant, which are harvested, dried, and then steeped in a carrier oil such as olive or coconut oil to extract the beneficial compounds.

To make lavender salve, the infused oil is then combined with beeswax and heated until fully combined. Once cooled, the salve can be applied topically to the skin as needed. When using lavender salve, it is important to avoid applying it to open wounds or broken skin, and to discontinue use if a skin reaction occurs.

Additionally, it is recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before using lavender salve if pregnant, breastfeeding, or taking any medications.

5. Balm

Lavandula Angustifolia balm, also commonly known as lavender essential oil, is a popular medicinal preparation extracted from the Lavandula angustifolia plant.

The main health benefits of this preparation include its ability to promote relaxation, reduce anxiety, improve sleep quality, alleviate headaches, and soothe skin irritations. The primary plant parts used for this preparation are the flowering tops of the Lavandula angustifolia plant, which are harvested and then steam-distilled to extract the essential oil.

To make Lavandula angustifolia balm, add a few drops of the essential oil to a carrier oil such as jojoba or coconut oil, and mix well. When using Lavandula angustifolia balm, it is important to take a few precautions, such as avoiding ingestion and keeping it out of reach of children and pets.

Additionally, it is recommended to perform a patch test on a small area of skin before using it more broadly, as some people may experience skin irritation or allergic reactions.

6. Cream

Lavandula Angustifolia cream is a popular medicinal preparation derived from the Lavandula angustifolia plant which is well-known for its calming and soothing properties.

The main health benefits of this preparation include its ability to alleviate anxiety, improve sleep quality, and reduce inflammation, making it an ideal remedy for various skin conditions such as eczema, acne, and sunburn. To make Lavandula Angustifolia cream, the lavender-infused oil is then combined with beeswax, shea butter, and a liquid carrier such as aloe vera or witch hazel to create a soothing topical cream. Additionally, it is recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before using lavender cream if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or taking any medications, as it may interact with certain drugs and cause unwanted side effects.

10. Bath salts

Lavandula Angustifolia bath salts is a type of medicinal preparation that combines Epsom salts or dead sea salts with lavender essential oil, which is derived from the Lavandula angustifolia plant.

The main health benefits of this Lavandula Angustifolia preparation include reducing stress and anxiety, promoting better sleep, alleviating muscle tension and soreness, and improving skin conditions such as eczema and psoriasis. The main plant parts used for this preparation are the flowers and leaves of the Lavandula angustifolia plant, which are distilled to extract the essential oil.

To make Lavandula Angustifolia bath salts, mix equal parts of Epsom salts or dead sea salts with lavender essential oil, and store the mixture in an airtight container. When using this Lavandula Angustifolia preparation, it is important to follow the recommended dosage and avoid using it on open wounds or broken skin.

Additionally, it is recommended to do a patch test before using lavender bath salts to ensure that it does not cause skin irritation or an allergic reaction.
