Dioscorea villosa, also known as wild yam, is a plant that has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

However, when used improperly, it can have several potential negative health effects. First, it can cause stomach irritation, leading to nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea. Second, it may affect hormone levels in the body, potentially leading to reproductive issues and hormonal imbalances.

Third, prolonged use or high doses can result in kidney damage. Fourth, it can interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and hormonal treatments, leading to adverse effects. Lastly, incorrect identification or contamination of wild yam can result in poisoning, with symptoms ranging from mouth and skin irritation to serious neurological disorders.

Therefore, it is crucial to consult with a healthcare professional before using Dioscorea villosa or any other medicinal plant.
