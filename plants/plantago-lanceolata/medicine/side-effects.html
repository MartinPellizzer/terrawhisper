Plantago lanceolata, also known as English plantain, is a common herb often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

However, if used improperly, it can have several potential side effects. Firstly, it can cause digestive issues such as nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea when consumed in large amounts. Secondly, it may lead to skin irritation and contact dermatitis in some individuals, particularly when applied topically.

Thirdly, it can interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners, and increase the risk of bleeding. Furthermore, pregnant women should avoid using it as it may stimulate menstruation and lead to miscarriage. Lastly, long-term use or excessive consumption can cause liver toxicity.

Therefore, it is crucial to consult with a healthcare professional before using Plantago lanceolata for medicinal purposes.
