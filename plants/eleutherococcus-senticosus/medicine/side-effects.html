By Martin Pellizzer
0 minutes

Eleutherococcus senticosus, also known as Siberian ginseng, can have several potential side effects if used improperly.

First, it can cause insomnia and agitation due to its stimulatory effects on the central nervous system. Additionally, improper use can lead to hypertension and tachycardia as it may affect cardiovascular functions.

Prolonged usage or high doses can also result in digestive issues such as stomach discomfort, diarrhea, and nausea. Furthermore, Siberian ginseng can interfere with the efficacy of prescription medications, particularly those metabolized in the liver, and it may exacerbate symptoms of autoimmune diseases.

Pregnant and breastfeeding women should avoid using this herb due to the lack of sufficient research on its safety profile in these populations.
