0 minutes

Angelica sinensis, also known as dong quai, is a plant that is often used in traditional medicine, but if used improperly, it can have several potential negative health effects.

First, it can increase sensitivity to sunlight, leading to skin inflammation, redness, and swelling. Second, it can have estrogen-like effects in the body, which can disrupt hormonal balances and be harmful to pregnant women or those with hormone-sensitive conditions.

Third, it can interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners, increasing the risk of bleeding. Fourth, it can cause stomach upset, including diarrhea and abdominal pain, when taken in large amounts. Lastly, long-term use or high doses of Angelica sinensis may lead to serious complications, such as seizures and heart damage.

Therefore, it is crucial to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new supplement regimen, especially with a substance like Angelica sinensis.
