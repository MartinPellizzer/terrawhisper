Acorus calamus, also known as sweet flag, is a plant that has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

However, when used improperly, it can have several potential negative health effects. First, consuming large amounts of Acorus calamus can cause stomach irritation, nausea, and vomiting.

Additionally, it can lead to hallucinations, seizures, and other neurological symptoms due to the presence of a toxic compound called beta-asarone. Moreover, long-term use of Acorus calamus has been linked to liver damage and reproductive problems. Pregnant women should avoid using it altogether, as it may cause uterine contractions and other complications.

Therefore, it is essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using Acorus calamus for medicinal purposes.
