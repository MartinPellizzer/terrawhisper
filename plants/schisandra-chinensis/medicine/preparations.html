10 Best Medicinal Preparations of Schisandra chinensis (Schisandra)

Schisandra chinensis, also known as the five-flavor-fruit, is a popular herb in traditional Chinese medicine.

Its medicinal preparations typically include tisanes and tinctures, which are made from the fruit, seeds, and sometimes the root of the plant. A tisane, or herbal tea, is made by steeping dried or fresh Schisandra chinensis fruit in hot water.

This preparation is often used to treat a variety of ailments, including liver disease, coughs, and insomnia. Tinctures, on the other hand, are made by soaking the fruit, seeds, or root in alcohol for several weeks. This preparation is thought to extract the medicinal compounds from the plant more effectively, and is often used as a tonic to boost energy and improve overall health.

Both preparations are believed to have anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and adaptogenic properties, making them popular in traditional medicine for promoting overall health and well-being.

1. Tincture

Schisandra Chinensis tincture is a medicinal preparation made by soaking and straining the dried berries of the Schisandra vine in a mixture of alcohol and water.

The main health benefits of this Schisandra preparation include improving liver function, reducing inflammation, and boosting the immune system. The dried berries and fruits of the Schisandra vine are the main plant parts used for this tincture.

To make the tincture, the dried berries are typically soaked in a mixture of 50% alcohol and 50% water for several weeks, after which the mixture is strained. It is important to note that Schisandra tincture can have side effects such as stomach upset and skin irritation, and should be used with caution in people with liver or kidney disease.

Additionally, it may interact with certain medications, so it is always best to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new supplement regimen.

2. Glycerite

Schisandra Chinensis glycerite is a medicinal preparation made from the Schisandra chinensis plant, also known as the five-flavor fruit, using a glycerin-based extraction method.

The main health benefits of this Schisandra chinensis preparation include its ability to support liver function, reduce inflammation, and protect against oxidative stress. The primary plant parts used for this preparation are the berries of the Schisandra chinensis plant.

To make Schisandra Chinensis glycerite, dried Schisandra chinensis berries are steeped in glycerin for several weeks, allowing the active compounds to be extracted into the glycerin. When using Schisandra Chinensis glycerite, it is important to start with a low dose and gradually increase, as it can have a strong effect on the body.

Additionally, it is recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before using Schisandra Chinensis glycerite if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or have a medical condition or are taking medication.

3. Infusion

Schisandra Chinensis infusion is a medicinal preparation made by steeping the plant's dried berries or fruits in hot water, which releases the beneficial compounds they contain.

This infusion is known for its potential health benefits, including boosting the immune system, reducing inflammation, and protecting liver function. The main plant parts used for this preparation are the small, reddish-brown, matte-surfaced berries or fruits of the Schisandra chinensis plant.

To make a Schisandra Chinensis infusion, place a teaspoon of dried berries in a cup of hot water and let it steep for 10-15 minutes before straining. Some potential precautions to keep in mind when using this Schisandra chinensis preparation include avoiding it during pregnancy and breastfeeding, and being mindful of potential allergic reactions or digestive issues in some individuals.

It is also recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new herbal remedy.

4. Decoction

Schisandra Chinensis decoction is a traditional medicinal preparation made by boiling and simmering the dried fruit or seeds of the Schisandra chinensis plant in water to extract its beneficial compounds.

The main health benefits of this Schisandra chinensis preparation include improving liver function, reducing inflammation, boosting the immune system, and alleviating stress and fatigue. The fruit or seeds are the main plant parts used for this preparation, and they contain lignans, antioxidants, and other bioactive compounds that contribute to its medicinal properties.

To make a Schisandra chinensis decoction, add 3-6 grams of dried Schisandra chinensis fruit or seeds to a pot with 1-2 cups of water, bring to a boil, reduce heat, and simmer for 15-30 minutes. After straining, drink the decoction 2-3 times a day. It is important to note that Schisandra chinensis decoction may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and sedatives, and should be avoided during pregnancy and breastfeeding.

As with any medicinal preparation, it is recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before use.
