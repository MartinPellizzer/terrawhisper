10 Best Medicinal Preparations of Serenoa repens (Saw Palmetto)

Serenoa repens, also known as Saw Palmetto, is a plant native to the southeastern United States and is well-known for its medicinal properties.

Its berries are commonly used to make various medicinal preparations, including tisanes and tinctures. A tisane is an herbal tea made by steeping dried or fresh berries in hot water. This preparation is often used to treat symptoms associated with an enlarged prostate, such as frequent urination and difficulty urinating.

Tinctures, on the other hand, are alcohol-based extractions of the berries. Tinctures are taken orally, often in small doses, and are believed to help with a variety of ailments, including decreasing inflammation and improving urinary tract health. Both tisanes and tinctures have been used in traditional medicine for centuries and are still widely used today due to their potential health benefits.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new supplement regimen.

To make a Serenoa repens infusion, place a teaspoon of dried and crushed berries in a tea strainer or infuser, and pour boiling water over them. Let it steep for 10-15 minutes, then strain and drink.
