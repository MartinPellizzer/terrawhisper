Serenoa repens, also known as Saw Palmetto, is a supplement often used for treating enlarged prostate symptoms, but when used improperly, it can have several potential health side effects.

Firstly, it may cause stomach discomfort, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea. Secondly, it can lead to headaches and dizziness, which might affect daily activities and concentration.

Moreover, Serenoa repens may interfere with hormonal balance, leading to breast tenderness or libido changes in some individuals. In rare cases, it can cause allergic reactions, characterized by itching, rash, or difficulty breathing, requiring immediate medical attention.

Lastly, if taken in conjunction with other medications, such as blood thinners or hormonal therapies, it might interact adversely, leading to unforeseen consequences.
