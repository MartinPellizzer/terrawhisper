By Martin Pellizzer
0 minutes

Crataeva nurvala, a plant commonly used in Ayurvedic medicine, can have several potential side effects if used improperly.

First, it may cause stomach upset, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, due to its strong cleansing properties. Additionally, it may lead to allergic reactions, particularly in individuals sensitive to related plants, resulting in skin rashes, itching, or difficulty breathing.

Prolonged use or excessive dosages might also result in imbalances in the body's natural hormonal system, potentially affecting reproductive health and causing menstrual irregularities or decreased libido. In extreme cases, Crataeva nurvala may negatively interact with certain medications, such as diuretics, anticoagulants, or drugs metabolized by the liver, potentially exacerbating their effects or leading to unforeseen complications.

Lastly, it is essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using Crataeva nurvala, especially for pregnant or breastfeeding women, as its safety during these stages of life has not been sufficiently established.
