By Martin Pellizzer
0 minutes

Harpagophytum procumbens, also known as devil's claw, is a plant that has been used traditionally for its potential medicinal properties.

However, if used improperly, it can have several potential health side effects. First, it can cause digestive issues, such as nausea, diarrhea, and abdominal pain.

Additionally, it may increase the risk of bleeding, particularly in individuals who are taking blood-thinning medications. Prolonged use of devil's claw can also lead to liver toxicity and interfere with the functioning of certain medications. Pregnant women and individuals with gallstones or ulcers should avoid using devil's claw, as it can worsen these conditions.

It's important to consult with a healthcare professional before taking devil's claw or any other supplement, to ensure it's safe and appropriate for your individual health needs.
