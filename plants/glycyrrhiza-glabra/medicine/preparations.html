10 Best Medicinal Preparations of Glycyrrhiza glabra (Licorice)

Glycyrrhiza glabra, commonly known as licorice, has been used in medicinal preparations for centuries due to its diverse pharmacological properties.

A popular preparation is the licorice root tisane, which is made by steeping dried or fresh licorice root in hot water. This soothing drink is often used to treat upper respiratory infections, sore throats, and digestive issues, as it is believed to have expectorant, demulcent, and anti-inflammatory properties.

Additionally, licorice root tincture is another common preparation, created by soaking the chopped or powdered root in alcohol or a mixture of alcohol and water. This concentrated extract can be used to alleviate symptoms of gastric ulcers, chronic fatigue, and liver disorders. However, it is essential to note that excessive consumption of licorice can lead to serious side effects, such as hypertension and hormonal imbalances, due to the presence of glycyrrhizin.

Therefore, it is always recommended to consult a healthcare professional before starting any licorice-based treatment.

1. Infusion

Glycyrrhiza Glabra infusion is a medicinal preparation made by steeping the roots or extracts of the Licorice plant in hot water, which releases the active compounds and constituents of the plant.

The main health benefits of this preparation include its anti-inflammatory, expectorant, and demulcent properties, making it useful for treating respiratory ailments, digestive issues, and skin conditions. The roots and underground stems of the Glycyrrhiza glabra plant are primarily used in this infusion. To prepare the infusion, add 1-2 teaspoons of dried Licorice root to a cup of boiling water, let it steep for 10-15 minutes, then strain and drink.

However, it is important to note that excessive consumption of Licorice root can lead to high blood pressure, low potassium levels, and water retention, so it is recommended to consult a healthcare professional before using this preparation, especially for those with pre-existing medical conditions or those taking medication.
