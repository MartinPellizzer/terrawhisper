By Martin Pellizzer
0 minutes

Glycyrrhiza glabra, commonly known as licorice, can have several negative health effects if used improperly.

First, excessive consumption can lead to potassium depletion, potentially resulting in high blood pressure, edema, and even heart problems. Additionally, licorice may interfere with certain medications, such as diuretics, digoxin, and warfarin, leading to adverse drug interactions.

Prolonged use of large amounts can also cause pseudoaldosteronism, a condition that affects the body's electrolyte balance, resulting in symptoms such as muscle weakness, fatigue, and heart rhythm abnormalities. In some cases, it may even lead to more serious complications like kidney failure and seizures. Pregnant women should avoid licorice altogether, as it can induce premature labor or affect fetal development.

Always consult a healthcare professional before using licorice for medicinal purposes.
