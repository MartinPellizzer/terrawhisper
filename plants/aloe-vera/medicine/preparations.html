10 Best Medicinal Preparations of Aloe vera (Aloe Vera)

Aloe vera, a succulent plant native to tropical and semi-tropical regions, is well-known for its medicinal properties and is used in various preparations such as tisanes and tinctures.

The gel inside the aloe vera leaves has been used for centuries to soothe skin irritations, treat burns, and promote wound healing. Aloe vera tisanes, or herbal teas, can be made by steeping the leaves or gel in hot water, and are sometimes used to aid digestion and soothe stomach ailments.

Tinctures, on the other hand, are made by soaking the plant material in alcohol, which extracts the active compounds and preserves the mixture. Aloe vera tinctures may be taken orally for their laxative and anti-inflammatory effects, or used topically to treat skin conditions such as eczema and psoriasis.

However, it's important to note that aloe vera can interact with certain medications and may not be suitable for everyone, so it's always best to consult with a healthcare provider before using aloe vera preparations.

1. gel

Aloe Vera gel is a clear, gel-like substance extracted from the inner part of the Aloe vera leaf, characterized by its soothing, moisturizing, and healing properties.

Its main health benefits include promoting skin health by treating sunburns, acne, and eczema, as well as supporting digestive health by alleviating heartburn, constipation, and irritable bowel syndrome. The primary plant parts used for this preparation are the thick, fleshy leaves, which store the clear gel that can be harvested by carefully filleting the leaves or pressing them to extract the gel.

To make Aloe vera gel at home, wear gloves while handling the leaves, rinse them, and carefully fillet them to remove the gel, which can then be blended and stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to a week. When using Aloe vera gel, be cautious of potential side effects such as skin irritation or allergic reactions, and avoid using it on deep cuts or severe burns.

Discontinue use if you experience any adverse reactions, and consult a healthcare professional if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or have a medical condition.

2. extract

The main health benefits of Aloe Vera extract include its ability to soothe skin irritations, promote wound healing, and reduce inflammation. To make Aloe Vera extract, the leaves are typically filleted to remove the gel, which is then allowed to dry and be crushed into a powder or processed into a liquid extract. When using Aloe Vera extract, it is important to follow the recommended dosage and avoid using it on open wounds or broken skin, as it can cause irritation.

Additionally, Aloe Vera extract should not be used by pregnant women or individuals with known allergies to Aloe Vera, as it can cause adverse reactions.
