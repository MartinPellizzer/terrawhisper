By Martin Pellizzer
0 minutes

Apium graveolens, also known as celery, is a plant that is generally considered safe when used appropriately.

However, if used improperly, it can potentially cause a number of adverse health effects. First, consuming large amounts of celery in its raw form can lead to digestive issues such as bloating, gas, and diarrhea.

Additionally, celery contains high levels of potassium, which can be harmful to individuals with kidney problems or those taking medications that affect potassium levels. Furthermore, celery seeds and oil, which are sometimes used in supplements, can cause skin irritation and sensitivity to sunlight, leading to rashes and blisters. In rare cases, excessive consumption of celery has been linked to low blood pressure and photosensitivity.

Finally, it is important to note that celery is often grown with the use of pesticides, so those with allergies or sensitivities to these chemicals should exercise caution when consuming large amounts of celery or celery-based products.
