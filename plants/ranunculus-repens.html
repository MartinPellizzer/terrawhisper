What to know before using Ranunculus repens (Creeping Buttercup)?

Ranunculus repens, commonly known as creeping buttercup, is a perennial plant native to Europe and Western Asia.

Its medicinal properties include the ability to treat skin conditions, wounds and warts, and rheumatism, due to its anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties. In horticulture, Ranunculus repens prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, and it can thrive in USDA hardiness zones 4-8. Botanically, Ranunculus repens has kidney-shaped, compound leaves and yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in the summer.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is often used as a groundcover due to its ability to spread through rhizomes.
