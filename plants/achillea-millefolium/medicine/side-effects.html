Achillea millefolium, also known as yarrow, is a plant that has been used in herbal medicine for centuries.

However, if used improperly, it can have several potential negative health effects. First, ingesting yarrow in large amounts can cause stomach irritation, dizziness, and kidney irritation. Additionally, touching the plant may cause skin irritation or allergic reactions in some people.

Pregnant women should avoid using yarrow, as it can stimulate uterine contractions. Furthermore, yarrow may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and sedatives, and can increase their effects. Lastly, excessive use of yarrow can lead to photosensitivity, making the skin more sensitive to sunlight.

Therefore, it is important to use yarrow with caution and consult with a healthcare professional before use.
