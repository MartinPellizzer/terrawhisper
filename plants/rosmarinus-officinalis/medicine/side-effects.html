Rosmarinus officinalis, commonly known as rosemary, is a popular herb known for its aromatic properties and culinary uses.

However, if used improperly, it can potentially have several negative health effects. First, ingesting large amounts of rosemary oil can cause digestive issues such as stomach irritation, nausea, and vomiting. Second, topical application of rosemary oil can cause skin irritation, redness, and allergic reactions in some individuals.

Third, inhaling large amounts of rosemary oil can lead to lung irritation, headaches, and even seizures in extreme cases. Pregnant women should also avoid using rosemary in medicinal amounts, as it may stimulate menstruation and potentially lead to miscarriage. Lastly, rosemary may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners, and can increase the risk of bleeding.

Therefore, it is crucial to use rosemary in recommended amounts and consult with a healthcare provider before using it for medicinal purposes.
