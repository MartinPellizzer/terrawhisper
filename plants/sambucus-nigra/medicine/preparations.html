10 Best Medicinal Preparations of Sambucus nigra (Elder)

Sambucus nigra, also known as black elderberry, has a long history of use in medicinal preparations.

One popular preparation is a tisane, or herbal tea, made from the flowers or berries of the plant. This soothing tea is often used to treat symptoms of colds and flu, as it is believed to have antiviral and anti-inflammatory properties.

Another common preparation is a tincture, which is made by soaking the berries or flowers in alcohol to extract their active compounds. Tinctures can be taken orally and are sometimes used as a preventative measure against illness. Both tisanes and tinctures have been used in traditional medicine to boost the immune system, reduce fevers, and alleviate respiratory symptoms.

However, it is important to note that more research is needed to fully understand the medicinal benefits and potential risks of Sambucus nigra preparations.

1. Infusion

Sambucus Nigra infusion is a medicinal preparation made by steeping the flowers or berries of the elder plant in hot water, which extracts the plant's beneficial compounds.

This infusion is known to have several health benefits, including boosting the immune system, reducing inflammation, and providing relief for respiratory symptoms such as coughs, colds, and congestion. The flowers and berries of the Sambucus Nigra plant are primarily used for this preparation.

To make the infusion, simply pour boiling water over the elder flowers or berries and let them steep for 10-15 minutes before straining. However, it is important to take precautions when using this preparation, as raw or unripe elderberries and leaves can be toxic.

Additionally, people who are pregnant or breastfeeding, have autoimmune diseases, or are taking immunosuppressive drugs should consult with a healthcare provider before using elderberry infusions.

2. Tincture

Sambucus Nigra tincture is a medicinal preparation made by soaking crushed elderberries, flowers, or leaves in a alcohol and water solution, which extracts the medicinal constituents.

The main health benefits of this Sambucus Nigra preparation include alleviating symptoms of colds, flu, and sinusitis, as well as having antiviral, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties. The main plant parts used for this Sambucus Nigra preparation are the berries, flowers, and leaves of the elder tree. To make Sambucus Nigra tincture, the crushed plant parts are steeped in a mixture of 50% ethanol and 50% water for several weeks, then pressed and filtered.

Main precautions to take when using this Sambucus Nigra preparation include avoiding ingestion of raw elderberries, flowers, or leaves due to the presence of toxic compounds, and not using commercial tinctures that contain alcohol for children, pregnant women, or individuals with alcohol intolerance.

3. Syrup

Sambucus Nigra syrup is a medicinal preparation made from the black elderberry plant, made by cooking and straining the berries, then combining the resulting juice with sugar, water, and sometimes other ingredients such as spices or citric acid.

The main health benefits of this preparation include boosting the immune system, reducing inflammation, and providing relief for symptoms of colds and flu such as cough, sore throat, and congestion. The main plant parts used for this preparation are the berries of the Sambucus nigra plant.

To make the syrup, first, clean and destem the berries, then cook them with water until they are soft, mash the berries and strain the juice, and finally, combine the juice with sugar and heat until it thickens. When using this Sambucus nigra preparation, it is important to be aware that it is not recommended for children under 12 months of age, and it should be taken in moderation as high doses can cause stomach discomfort.

Additionally, it is always best to consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new supplement regimen.

4. Lozenge

Sambucus Nigra lozenge is a form of medicinal preparation that comes in the form of a solid dosage form for oral administration, often having a pleasant taste, and is made from the extracts of the elderberry plant (Sambucus nigra).

The main health benefits of this Sambucus nigra preparation include alleviating symptoms of the common cold, influenza, and reducing inflammation, as it contains antioxidants, vitamins, and other compounds with therapeutic properties. The main plant parts used for this Sambucus nigra preparation are the flowers and berries, which are rich in flavonoids, anthocyanins, and other bioactive compounds. To make a Sambucus nigra lozenge, the extracts from the elderberries and flowers are mixed with a suitable binder, such as honey or sugar, and then formed into a solid mass, which is then cut or stamped into the desired shape and size.

When using Sambucus nigra lozenges, it is important to follow the recommended dosage and not to exceed it, as excessive consumption may cause stomach discomfort, and it is not recommended for children under the age of 12, pregnant or breastfeeding women, or individuals with immune system disorders, as it may interact with certain medications and have adverse effects.

5. Capsules

Sambucus Nigra capsules are dietary supplements that contain extracts of the European elderberry, Sambucus nigra, which are known for their potential medicinal properties.

The main health benefits of Sambucus Nigra capsules include boosting the immune system, reducing inflammation, and providing relief from cold and flu symptoms. The main plant parts used for this preparation are the berries and flowers of the Sambucus Nigra plant.

To make Sambucus Nigra capsules, the berries and/or flowers are dried and then ground into a powder, which is then encapsulated. When using Sambucus Nigra capsules, it is important to follow the recommended dosage and consult with a healthcare provider, as high doses can cause stomach discomfort and other side effects.

Additionally, it is recommended to avoid using Sambucus Nigra during pregnancy and breastfeeding, as its safety and efficacy have not been established in these populations.

6. Tea

Sambucus Nigra tea is an infusion made from the flowers and berries of the European elder tree, also known as black elder.

This tea has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, including alleviating symptoms of colds, flu, and sinusitis, as well as having anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties. The flowers and berries are the main plant parts used in the preparation of this tea. To make the tea, dried elder flowers or berries can be steeped in hot water for several minutes.

However, it is important to note some precautions when using Sambucus Nigra tea, such as not consuming large amounts of the raw berries, as they can cause nausea and vomiting, and avoiding use during pregnancy as it may stimulate uterine contractions.

7. Poultice

Sambucus Nigra poultice is a medicinal preparation made from the leaves, flowers, and/or bark of the black elder tree, which is locally applied to the skin to treat various health conditions.

The main health benefits of this Sambucus nigra preparation include providing relief from inflammation, reducing pain, and promoting healing of wounds, burns, and bruises. The leaves and twigs of the Sambucus nigra plant are mainly used to make this preparation.

To make a Sambucus nigra poultice, fresh or dried leaves and twigs are crushed and bruised to release their juices, which are then applied to the affected area and covered with a cloth. When using a Sambucus nigra poultice, it is important to take precautions such as avoiding the use of the preparation on deep wounds or severe burns, and not using it for prolonged periods of time, as it may cause skin irritation or other adverse reactions.

Additionally, it is recommended to perform a patch test before using the poultice on a larger area of the skin.

8. Ointment

Sambucus Nigra ointment is a topical preparation made from the flowers, leaves, and berries of the elder plant, also known as Sambucus nigra.

The main health benefits of this Sambucus nigra preparation include its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, making it useful for treating minor wounds, burns, and skin irritations. The flowers and leaves are often used in this preparation due to their high antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

To make the ointment, the plant parts are typically dried and then infused in a carrier oil, such as olive or almond oil, before beeswax is added to thicken the mixture. When using Sambucus Nigra ointment, it is important to take precautions such as not applying it to deep wounds or severe burns, and discontinuing use if a skin reaction occurs.

Additionally, it is not recommended for internal use and should be kept out of reach of children.

9. Oil

Sambucus Nigra oil is a type of carrier oil that is extracted from the seeds of the European elderberry plant, also known as Sambucus nigra.

This oil is rich in antioxidants, essential fatty acids, and vitamin E, making it a popular choice for use in skincare and massage. The main health benefits of Sambucus Nigra oil include its ability to moisturize and soothe dry, irritated skin, reduce the appearance of fine lines and wrinkles, and promote wound healing.

The main plant parts used for this preparation are the seeds of the Sambucus nigra plant. To make Sambucus Nigra oil, the seeds are typically cold-pressed to extract the oil. It is important to note that Sambucus Nigra oil should not be ingested, and it should be kept out of reach of children.

Additionally, people who are allergic to elderberry or other members of the Caprifoliaceae family should avoid using Sambucus Nigra oil.

10. Suppositories

Sambucus Nigra suppositories are medicated intrarectal therapeutic devices that contain a standardized extract of the elder tree's flowers or berries.

The main health benefits of this preparation include providing anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and immunostimulant effects, which can help alleviate symptoms of hemorrhoids, constipation, and viral infections. The primary plant parts used for this preparation are the flowers and berries of Sambucus nigra.

To make Sambucus Nigra suppositories, the extract is combined with a base or carrier, such as cocoa butter, and then formed into the suppository shape before being cooled and hardened. When using this Sambucus Nigra preparation, it is essential to follow the recommended dosage and consult a healthcare professional, as overuse or incorrect administration can cause irritation or discomfort.

Additionally, it is crucial to store the suppositories in a cool, dry place to maintain their stability and efficacy.
