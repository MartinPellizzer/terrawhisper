By Martin Pellizzer
0 minutes

Sambucus nigra, also known as black elderberry, has been used in folk medicine for its potential health benefits, but improper use can lead to several side effects.

Firstly, consuming raw or undercooked elderberries can cause nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea due to the presence of lectins and other toxic compounds. Secondly, overdosing on elderberry supplements may result in severe digestive issues, including abdominal cramps and severe diarrhea.

Moreover, people with autoimmune diseases should avoid elderberry products, as they may stimulate the immune system, potentially worsening their condition. Pregnant and breastfeeding women should also avoid using elderberry, as its safety during pregnancy and lactation has not been established.

Lastly, people who are allergic to elderberry or other plants in the same family (Caprifoliaceae) may experience skin rashes, hives, or difficulty breathing after contact with the plant or its products.
