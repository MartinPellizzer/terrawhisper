Home > Plants > Zanthoxylum Americanum > Medicine > Side Effects
By Martin Pellizzer
0 minutes

Zanthoxylum americanum, also known as prickly ash or Americanusterberry, can have several negative health effects if used improperly.

Overconsumption of the plant can lead to digestive issues such as stomach pain, nausea, and vomiting due to its irritant properties. In severe cases, it may even cause inflammation of the intestinal lining, resulting in ulcers.

Additionally, skin contact with the plant can result in redness, itching, and rashes. Prolonged exposure or ingestion might lead to photosensitivity, making the skin more sensitive to sunlight. In some rare instances, it can cause allergic reactions, including anaphylaxis, which is a life-threatening emergency requiring immediate medical attention.

Therefore, it is crucial to use Zanthoxylum americanum with caution and under the guidance of a healthcare professional.
