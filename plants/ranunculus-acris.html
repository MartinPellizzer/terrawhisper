What to know before using Ranunculus acris (Meadow Buttercup)?

Ranunculus acris, also known as the tall buttercup, is a flowering plant with distinctive botanical characteristics including bright yellow flowers, typically with five to eight petals, and a height that can reach up to two feet.

This plant is known for its horticultural versatility, able to thrive in a variety of conditions, including full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil. Medicinally, Ranunculus acris has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, and it has been used to treat a variety of ailments, from skin conditions to fever.

However, it is important to note that all parts of the plant contain protoanemonin, a potentially irritating compound, and should be handled with care.
