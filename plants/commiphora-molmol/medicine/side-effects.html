Commiphora molmol, also known as myrrh, can have several potential negative health effects if used improperly.

It can cause digestive issues such as stomach irritation, nausea, and diarrhea when taken in large amounts. Myrrh can also lead to allergic reactions, characterized by skin rashes, itching, and difficulty breathing, in some individuals.

Prolonged use of this plant resin can result in kidney irritation and increased risk of kidney failure. Myrrh may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners, and increase the risk of bleeding.

Additionally, myrrh is not recommended for pregnant women as it can induce menstruation and potentially lead to miscarriage.
