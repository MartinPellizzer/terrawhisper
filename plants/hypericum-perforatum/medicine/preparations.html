10 Best Medicinal Preparations of Hypericum perforatum (St. John’S Wort)

Hypericum perforatum, also known as St.

John's Wort, has been used in medicinal preparations for centuries due to its potential therapeutic properties. Two common forms of St. John's Wort preparations are tisanes and tinctures. A tisane, or herbal tea, is made by steeping the flowers, leaves, and stems of the plant in hot water.

This soothing beverage is often used to treat mild anxiety, depression, and insomnia. Tinctures, on the other hand, are made by soaking the plant material in alcohol or glycerin. The resulting extract can be taken orally and is believed to help alleviate symptoms of depression, anxiety, and nerve pain. It is important to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new medicinal preparations, as St.

John's Wort can interact with certain medications and may have side effects.

1. Infusion

Hypericum Perforatum infusion is a medicinal preparation made by steeping the flowers, leaves, and stems of the St.

John's Wort plant in hot water, which releases its active compounds. The main health benefits of this preparation include its reputation for treating mild to moderate depression, anxiety, and sleep disorders, as well as relieving symptoms of menopause and premenstrual syndrome. The flowers, leaves, and stems of the Hypericum perforatum plant are used to make this infusion.

To prepare the infusion, pour boiling water over the plant parts and let them steep for 10-15 minutes before straining. It is important to note that St. John's Wort can interact with several medications, such as antidepressants, birth control pills, and blood thinners, so it is crucial to consult a healthcare provider before using this infusion. Additionally, excessive sun exposure should be avoided while taking St.

John's Wort, as it can increase skin sensitivity to sunlight and cause rashes or burns.

The flowering tops of the Hypericum perforatum plant are the primary parts used for creating this tincture. When using Hypericum perforatum tincture, it is essential to be aware that it can interact with various medications, such as antidepressants, contraceptives, and blood thinners, potentially leading to adverse effects.

Therefore, it is crucial to consult with a healthcare professional before starting any treatment with this tincture.
