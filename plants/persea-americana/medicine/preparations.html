10 Best Medicinal Preparations of Persea americana (Avocado)

Persea americana, commonly known as avocado, has been used in various medicinal preparations due to its numerous health benefits.

A popular preparation is the avocado tisane, which is an herbal tea made by steeping avocado leaves in hot water. This tea is often used as a remedy for diarrhea, dysentery, and other gastrointestinal issues.

The leaves can also be made into a poultice to treat wounds, skin irritations, and inflammation. Moreover, a tincture can be prepared by soaking avocado leaves in alcohol or vinegar, which can be used to treat respiratory conditions such as bronchitis, coughs, and colds. The fruit itself is rich in healthy fats, fiber, and vitamins, making it a valuable addition to a balanced diet.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new medicinal preparations.

1. Infusion

Persea Americana infusion is a medicinal preparation made by steeping the leaves of the avocado tree in hot water, which is then consumed as a tea.

This preparation is known to have several health benefits, including improving digestion, reducing inflammation, and supporting cardiovascular health due to its high content of potassium, fiber, and healthy fats. The main plant parts used for this preparation are the leaves of the Persea americana tree.

To make a Persea Americana infusion, fresh avocado leaves should be added to boiling water and allowed to steep for 10-15 minutes before straining. When using this Persea americana preparation, it is important to be aware of potential side effects such as allergic reactions, and it is not recommended for pregnant women or people with liver or kidney problems.

As with any natural remedy, it is always best to consult with a healthcare professional before use.

2. Tincture

Persea Americana tincture is a medicinal preparation made by soaking ripe avocado fruits and seeds in alcohol, often grain alcohol, to extract their beneficial compounds.

The main health benefits of this preparation include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and digestive properties, making it useful for treating conditions such as arthritis, high cholesterol, and digestive disorders. The ripe fruit and seeds are the main plant parts used for this preparation, with the seeds believed to contain higher concentrations of active compounds. To make the tincture, the avocado fruits and seeds are chopped and steeped in alcohol for several weeks, then strained to remove solid particles.

Main precautions to take when using this Persea Americana preparation include avoiding use during pregnancy, as it may stimulate uterine contractions, and monitoring blood sugar levels, as avocado can lower blood sugar levels.

3. Decoction

Persea Americana decoction is a medicinal preparation made by boiling and simmering the leaves, bark, or seeds of the avocado tree in water.

This preparation is known to have anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, making it useful for treating conditions such as arthritis, high cholesterol, and skin infections. The leaves, bark, or seeds of the avocado tree can be used for this preparation, with the leaves being the most commonly used part.

To make a decoction, place the chosen plant part in a pot and cover with cold water. Bring the water to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes. Strain the liquid before drinking.

It is important to note that Persea Americana decoction should not be used by pregnant women or people with liver or kidney problems, and it should be used with caution in people taking medication for high blood pressure or diabetes.

4. Syrup

Persea Americana syrup is a medicinal preparation made from the fruit of the avocado tree.

It is known to have several health benefits, including improving digestion, reducing inflammation, and boosting the immune system, due to its high concentration of vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants. The main plant parts used for this preparation are the ripe avocado fruit and sometimes the seed.

To make the syrup, the avocado fruit is first mashed and then simmered in water until a thick consistency is reached. After straining, sugar or honey is added to sweeten it. The main precautions to take when using this Persea americana preparation include avoiding it if you have a known allergy to avocado, and consulting a healthcare professional if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or have a medical condition.

Additionally, it is important to consume it in moderation due to its high sugar content.

5. Poultice

Persea Americana poultice is a medicinal preparation made from mashed avocado fruit or leaves, which is then applied directly to the skin to treat various health issues.

The main health benefits of this avocado preparation include providing cooling and soothing relief for inflammation, reducing pain and swelling, healing wounds, and improving skin conditions such as eczema and psoriasis. The mashed avocado fruit or leaves are the main plant parts used for this preparation.

To make an avocado poultice, mash the ripe avocado fruit or leaves into a paste and apply it directly to the affected area. To ensure safety, it is important to conduct a patch test before applying the poultice to a larger area, as some people may have allergic reactions to avocado.

Additionally, it is recommended to avoid using avocado poultice on open wounds or broken skin, and to consult a healthcare professional before using it as a treatment for any medical condition.

6. Oil extract

Persea Americana oil extract is a natural substance derived from the fruit of the avocado tree, Persea americana.

This extract is rich in monounsaturated fats, vitamins, and minerals, making it a popular ingredient in various cosmetic and medicinal products. Its main health benefits include promoting skin health, supporting heart health, and providing anti-inflammatory properties. The main plant parts used for this preparation are the fleshy pulp and seeds of the avocado fruit. To make the oil extract, the avocado fruits are harvested, washed, and crushed to extract the oil.

The oil can be extracted using either cold-pressing or solvent extraction methods. When using Persea Americana oil extract, it is important to take certain precautions. It is recommended to perform a patch test before using it on the skin, as some people may experience allergic reactions. Additionally, it is important to store the extract in a cool, dark place to prevent it from going rancid.

Lastly, it is not recommended to consume avocado oil extract orally, as it can cause gastrointestinal distress and other adverse effects.

7. Salve

Persea Americana salve is a medicinal preparation made from the fruit of the avocado tree, Persea americana.

The main health benefits of this preparation include its ability to moisturize and protect the skin, reduce inflammation, and potentially help with wound healing due to its antioxidant and antibacterial properties. The main plant parts used for this preparation are the fruit and the oil extracted from the fruit.

To make the salve, the avocado oil is typically combined with beeswax and other ingredients such as essential oils for fragrance. When using Persea Americana salve, it is important to patch test it on a small area of skin first to ensure no allergic reaction occurs.

Additionally, it is recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before using this preparation for medicinal purposes, especially for children, pregnant women, and individuals with underlying health conditions.

8. Cream

Its main health benefits include providing relief for dry, itchy, and inflamed skin, as well as potentially aiding in the healing of wounds and skin conditions such as eczema and psoriasis. The main plant parts used for this preparation are the pulp and oil of the avocado fruit.

To make Persea Americana cream, the avocado oil is first extracted and then mixed with other ingredients such as beeswax, shea butter, and essential oils to create a smooth, spreadable consistency. When using this Persea Americana preparation, it is important to perform a patch test first to check for any adverse reactions, and to avoid contact with the eyes and mouth.

Additionally, it is recommended to store the cream in a cool, dry place and to discard it if it develops an off odor or changes in color or consistency.
