By Martin Pellizzer
0 minutes

Hamamelis virginiana, also known as witch hazel, is a plant that has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes, including skin care and wound healing.

However, if used improperly, it can have potential side effects. Firstly, overuse or improper application can lead to skin irritation, dryness, or allergic reactions in some individuals.

Secondly, ingesting witch hazel extracts or preparations can cause nausea, vomiting, and other digestive issues. Thirdly, if applied to broken or sensitive skin, it can cause stinging or burning sensations. Moreover, it can interfere with the effectiveness of certain medications when applied topically, such as blood thinners or skin creams with active ingredients.

Lastly, pregnant women should avoid using it internally or on the skin, as its safety during pregnancy has not been well-studied.
