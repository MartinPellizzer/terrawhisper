Passiflora incarnata, also known as passionflower, is a plant that is often used in herbal supplements for its calming and anxiety-reducing effects.

However, if used improporiately, it can cause several negative health side effects. Overconsumption can lead to drowsiness, confusion, and dizziness.

Additionally, it can cause nausea, vomiting, and in rare cases, even altered consciousness. It can also interact with other medications, such as sedatives and antidepressants, which can increase the risk of adverse effects. Pregnant women and those with liver or kidney disease should avoid using passionflower due to the potential for negative impacts on these organs.

As with any supplement, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before using passionflower to ensure safe and effective use.
