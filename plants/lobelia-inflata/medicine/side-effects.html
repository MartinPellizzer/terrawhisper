By Martin Pellizzer
0 minutes

Lobelia inflata, also known as pukeweed, is a plant that has been used in small doses for medicinal purposes, such as treating respiratory conditions and reducing inflammation.

However, if used improperly or in large amounts, it can have various negative health effects. It can cause nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, which can lead to dehydration and electrolyte imbalance.

In severe cases, it may induce seizures, hypothermia, and hypotension, which can be life-threatening. Additionally, it can interact with other medications, such as sedatives and antidepressants, increasing the risk of serious side effects.

Pregnant women and children should avoid using Lobelia inflata, as it can lead to complications and pose significant health risks.
