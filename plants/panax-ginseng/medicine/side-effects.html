By Martin Pellizzer
0 minutes

Panax ginseng, when used improperly, can have various negative health effects.

First, it can cause insomnia and restlessness due to its stimulant properties if taken in excess. Overuse may also lead to hypertension, or elevated blood pressure, as it affects the cardiovascular system.

Additionally, Panax ginseng can interfere with the efficacy of certain medications, such as blood thinners and antidepressants, leading to potential health complications. Men may experience mastalgia, or breast pain, and women might have menstrual irregularities.

Lastly, long-term use or high doses can result in ginseng abuse syndrome, manifesting in symptoms like confusion, depression, and social withdrawal.
