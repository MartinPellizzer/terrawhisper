By Martin Pellizzer
0 minutes

Lycium chinense, also known as Chinese boxthorn or wolfberry, is a plant that has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries.

However, if used improperly, it can have several potential negative health effects. First, overconsumption can lead to stomach discomfort, nausea, and diarrhea. Second, it may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners, and cause adverse reactions.

Third, individuals with autoimmune diseases should avoid it as it can stimulate the immune system and worsen symptoms. Fourth, pregnant women should avoid consuming it as it may cause miscarriage or preterm labor. Lastly, long-term use of large amounts may lead to toxicity, with symptoms such as dizziness, headache, and muscle weakness.

Therefore, it is crucial to consult with a healthcare provider before using Lycium chinense for medicinal purposes.
