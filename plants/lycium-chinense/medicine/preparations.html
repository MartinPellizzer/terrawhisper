10 Best Medicinal Preparations of Lycium chinense (Goji Berry)

Lycium chinense, also known as Chinese boxthorn or wolfberry, has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries due to its various medicinal properties.

One popular preparation is a tisane, or herbal tea, made from the berries or leaves of the plant. This soothing drink is believed to improve eye health, boost the immune system, and even slow the aging process.

A tincture can also be made from the plant, which involves soaking the berries or leaves in alcohol to extract their active compounds. This concentrated extract can be taken orally to treat ailments such as coughs, sore throats, and stomach issues. Additionally, Lycium chinense has been used topically in the form of a salve or ointment to heal wounds and reduce inflammation.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before using Lycium chinense for medicinal purposes, as it can interact with certain medications and may have side effects.

1. Infusion

Lycium Chinense infusion is a medicinal preparation made by steeping the dried or fresh leaves, roots, or fruits of the Goji berry plant in hot water, which releases the plant's beneficial compounds into the liquid.

This infusion is known to have various health benefits, such as boosting the immune system, improving vision, and reducing inflammation, as well as providing antioxidant and anti-aging effects. The main plant parts used for this preparation are the leaves, roots, and fruits, which are rich in nutrients such as vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants. To make a Lycium Chinense infusion, place the desired amount of plant material in a pot or teapot, pour boiling water over it, and let it steep for 10-15 minutes before straining and consuming.

When using this Lycium Chinense preparation, it is important to be aware of potential precautions, such as avoiding high doses during pregnancy and breastfeeding, and monitoring blood sugar levels if taking medication for diabetes.

2. Tincture

Lycium Chinense tincture is a medicinal preparation made by soaking and straining the dried or fresh leaves, stems, and/or berries of the Lycium chinense plant in a alcohol and water solution.

This preparation is known to have various health benefits, such as boosting the immune system, improving vision, and reducing fatigue. The main plant parts used for this tincture are the berries, leaves, and stems, which are rich in antioxidants, vitamins, and minerals.

To make the tincture, the plant parts are submerged in a mixture of alcohol and water, and allowed to steep for several weeks before straining. When using Lycium Chinense tincture, it is important to follow the recommended dosage, as overuse can lead to stomach discomfort.

Additionally, it is not recommended for pregnant women or those with low blood sugar, as it may affect blood sugar levels.

3. Decoction

Lycium Chinense decoction is a traditional medicinal preparation made by simmering the roots, bark, or fruits of the Lycium chinense plant in water.

This preparation is known to offer various health benefits, such as boosting the immune system, improving vision, and promoting skin health. The main plant parts used for this preparation are the roots and bark, which are rich in antioxidants and other beneficial compounds.

To make a Lycium chinense decoction, add 2-3 teaspoons of dried and crushed plant material to a pot with 4-5 cups of water, bring to a boil, reduce heat, and simmer for 30-60 minutes. It is important to strain the liquid before drinking. Some precautions to keep in mind when using this preparation include avoiding it during pregnancy, as it may stimulate menstruation, and monitoring blood sugar levels, as it may lower blood sugar.

Additionally, it is always best to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new medicinal preparation.
