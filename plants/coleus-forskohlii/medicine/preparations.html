10 Best Medicinal Preparations of Coleus forskohlii (Coleus)

Coleus forskohlii, a member of the mint family, has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.

Its roots contain a compound called forskolin, which is believed to have various health benefits. A common medicinal preparation is a tincture, where the roots are soaked in alcohol to extract the active compounds.

This tincture can be taken orally or added to drinks for easy consumption. Another preparation is a tisane, where the roots are steeped in hot water to make a tea.

However, it is essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using Coleus forskohlii for medicinal purposes, as it can have interactions with other medications and may have potential side effects.

1. Infusion

Coleus Forskohlii infusion is a traditional medicinal preparation made by steeping the roots or leaves of the Coleus forskohlii plant in hot water to extract its bioactive compounds.

The main health benefits of this preparation include lowering blood pressure, improving cardiovascular health, and promoting respiratory health. The roots and leaves of the Coleus forskohlii plant are the primary plant parts used in this preparation.

To make a Coleus Forskohlii infusion, add one to two teaspoons of dried and crushed Coleus forskohlii root or leaves to a cup of boiling water, let it steep for 10-15 minutes, and then strain. It is important to note that high doses of Coleus forskohlii can cause low blood pressure, so individuals with hypotension should use this preparation with caution.

Additionally, pregnant or breastfeeding women and those taking medication for blood pressure or blood sugar should consult with a healthcare provider before using Coleus forskohlii infusion.

2. Tincture

The roots of the Coleus Forskohlii plant are the primary plant part used in the preparation of this tincture. To make the tincture, the roots or leaves are chopped and steeped in alcohol or glycerin for several weeks, after which the liquid is strained and stored in a cool, dark place.

When using Coleus Forskohlii tincture, it is important to follow the recommended dosage and consult with a healthcare provider, as it can interact with certain medications and may have side effects such as low blood pressure, fast heart rate, and upset stomach.

3. Decoction

Coleus Forskohlii decoction is a traditional medicinal preparation made by boiling and simmering the roots or whole plant of Coleus forskohlii in water.

The main health benefits of this preparation include improved cardiovascular health, lowered blood pressure, and increased lung function. The roots and whole plant of Coleus forskohlii are the main plant parts used for this preparation.

To make a Coleus Forskohlii decoction, add 1-2 teaspoons of dried and crushed Coleus forskohlii root or whole plant to a cup of water, bring to a boil, reduce heat, and simmer for 15-20 minutes. The main precautions to take when using Coleus forskohlii decoction include avoiding use during pregnancy and breastfeeding, and avoiding use if you have a history of low blood pressure or are taking medication for blood pressure or heart conditions.

It is also recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before using Coleus forskohlii decoction if you have any pre-existing medical conditions or are taking any medications.
