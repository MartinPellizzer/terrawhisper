Coleus forskohlii, a herb from the mint family, is often used in dietary supplements for weight loss and other health purposes.

However, when used improperly, this herb can cause several adverse health effects. High doses of Coleus forskohlii can lead to low blood pressure, increased heart rate, and throat irritation.

Additionally, it may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and drugs used to treat high blood pressure, leading to dangerous side effects. Pregnant and nursing women, as well as individuals with chronic conditions like ulcers, should avoid using this herb due to the potential risks. Lastly, long-term use or excessive intake of Coleus forskohlii supplements may result in more severe side effects, such as fluctuating blood pressure and cardiac complications.

Always consult a healthcare professional before starting any new supplement regimen to ensure safe and effective use.
