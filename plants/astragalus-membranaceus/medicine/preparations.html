10 Best Medicinal Preparations of Astragalus membranaceus (Astragalus)

Astragalus membranaceus, also known as Huang Qi, is a popular herb in traditional Chinese medicine.

Its roots are used to prepare various medicinal preparations, including tisanes and tinctures. A tisane, or herbal tea, is made by steeping dried or fresh Astragalus roots in hot water.

This soothing beverage is believed to boost the immune system, reduce inflammation, and provide antioxidant effects. On the other hand, a tincture is a more concentrated extract, made by soaking the roots in alcohol or glycerin. Tinctures are usually taken in smaller doses, and are used for a variety of purposes, such as treating symptoms of the common cold, protecting the liver, and reducing fatigue.

Both preparations are considered safe for most people, but it's always best to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new supplement regimen.

1. Infusion

Astragalus Membranaceus infusion is a medicinal preparation made by steeping dried roots of the Astragalus Membranaceus plant in hot water, which releases the plant's beneficial compounds.

The main health benefits of this preparation include boosting the immune system, reducing inflammation, and providing antioxidant effects. The roots are the primary plant part used for this infusion, which contain bioactive compounds such as saponins, flavonoids, and polysaccharides.

To make the infusion, add 1-2 teaspoons of dried Astragalus Membranaceus root to a cup of boiling water, let it steep for 10-15 minutes, and then strain before drinking. It is important to note that Astragalus Membranaceus infusion may interact with certain medications, such as immunosuppressants, and should be avoided by pregnant or breastfeeding women.

As with any herbal preparation, it is recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before use.
