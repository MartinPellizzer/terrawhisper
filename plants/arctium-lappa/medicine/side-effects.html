Arctium lappa, also known as greater burdock, is a plant that has been used in traditional medicine for centuries, but if used improperly, it can have several potential health side effects.

First, consuming parts of the plant that are contaminated with pollutants or heavy metals can lead to poisoning and severe damage to organs. Second, overdosing on Arctium lappa can cause nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea.

Third, people who are allergic to daisies, ragweed, or chrysanthemums may experience allergic reactions after using Arctium lappa. Fourth, this plant can interact with certain medications, such as diabetes and blood pressure drugs, and can lead to dangerous complications. Lastly, using Arctium lappa as a substitute for professional medical advice or treatment can delay appropriate medical care and worsen the underlying condition.

Therefore, it is crucial to consult a healthcare provider before using Arctium lappa for medicinal purposes.
