Viburnum opulus, also known as cramp bark, is a plant commonly used in herbal medicine, but if used improperly, it can have several potential negative health effects.

First, consuming large amounts of the plant can cause digestive issues such as nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea. Additionally, it can cause skin irritation and allergic reactions in some individuals.

Moreover, Viburnum opulus may interact with certain medications, including blood thinners, and can increase the risk of bleeding. Pregnant women should avoid using it, as it may stimulate uterine contractions and lead to miscarriage. In extreme cases, overdose can result in irregular heartbeat, dizziness, and seizures.

Always consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new herbal remedies.
