10 Best Medicinal Preparations of Calendula officinalis (Calendula)

Calendula officinalis, also known as pot marigold, is a popular medicinal plant with a variety of uses.

Its flowers are often used to make a soothing tisane, which is believed to have anti-inflammatory and wound-healing properties. A tincture can also be made by soaking the flowers in alcohol, which can then be used to treat skin conditions such as eczema, cuts, and bruises.

The medicinal properties of calendula are attributed to its high content of flavonoids, which have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. Additionally, research suggests that calendula may also stimulate the production of collagen, a protein that plays a crucial role in wound healing.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before using calendula or any other herbal remedy, especially for those who are pregnant, breastfeeding, or have a medical condition.

1. Infusion

Calendula Officinalis infusion is a medicinal preparation made by steeping dried calendula flower petals in hot water, which extracts the beneficial compounds from the plant.

The main health benefits of this preparation include its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and wound-healing properties, making it useful for treating skin conditions, promoting wound healing, and alleviating digestive issues. The main plant parts used for this preparation are the dried flower petals of the Calendula officinalis plant.

To make a calendula infusion, pour boiling water over the dried petals and let them steep for 10-15 minutes before straining. When using calendula infusion, it is important to be aware of potential allergic reactions, and discontinue use if any occur.

Additionally, it is recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before using calendula infusion if pregnant or breastfeeding, as its safety during these times has not been established.

2. Tincture

Calendula Officinalis tincture is a medicinal preparation made by soaking calendula flower petals in alcohol or glycerin to extract its active compounds.

Its main health benefits include promoting wound healing, reducing inflammation, and providing relief from skin irritations and rashes. The main plant parts used for this preparation are the bright orange or yellow flower petals of the Calendula officinalis plant.

To make the tincture, the petals are steeped in alcohol or glycerin for several weeks, then strained. When using Calendula officinalis tincture, it is important to follow the recommended dosage, as overuse can cause skin irritation and nausea.

Additionally, it should be avoided by pregnant women and those with allergies to members of the daisy family.

3. Oil extract

Calendula Officinalis oil extract is a medicinal preparation derived from the flowering parts of the Calendula officinalis plant, commonly known as marigold.

Its main health benefits include promoting wound healing, reducing inflammation, and providing relief from skin irritations, rashes, and sunburns. The flowers of the Calendula officinalis plant are used to make this oil extract, which is often used as a base for creams, ointments, and salves.

To make the oil extract, dried or fresh flower heads are steeped in a carrier oil, such as olive or jojoba oil, and then heated gently over a period of several weeks. The resulting infusion is then strained and stored in a cool, dark place. When using Calendula Officinalis oil extract, it is important to perform a patch test first to ensure that you are not allergic to the product.

Additionally, it should not be used on open wounds or broken skin, and it is always recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before using any new medicinal preparation.

5. Ointment

Calendula Officinalis ointment is a topical preparation made from the pot marigold flower, which is the common name for Calendula officinalis.

To make the ointment, the infused oil is combined with beeswax and heated until fully combined. Once cooled, it can be applied directly to the skin. When using Calendula Officinalis ointment, it is important to avoid contact with the eyes and to discontinue use if a skin reaction occurs.

Additionally, it is recommended to perform a patch test before using it on large areas of the body, as some people may be allergic to the plant.

9. Wash

Calendula Officinalis wash is a medicinal preparation made from the petals of the Calendula officinalis flower, also known as pot marigold.

This wash has anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and wound-healing properties, making it an effective treatment for skin conditions such as eczema, dermatitis, and minor cuts and burns. The petals of the Calendula officinalis flower are used to make this preparation, and they are rich in flavonoids, triterpenoids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

To make a Calendula Officinalis wash, add 1-2 tablespoons of dried calendula petals to a cup of boiling water and let it steep for 10-15 minutes. After straining, the liquid can be used as a wash or compress. When using a Calendula Officinalis wash, it is important to avoid contact with the eyes and to discontinue use if any irritation occurs.

Additionally, it is recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before using this preparation if you are pregnant or breastfeeding, as there is limited research on its safety in these populations.

10. Compress

Calendula Officinalis compress is a medicinal preparation made by soaking Calendula officinalis flowers in hot water and then applying the cooled liquid directly to the skin.

The main health benefits of this preparation include its anti-inflammatory and wound-healing properties, making it useful for treating minor cuts, burns, and skin irritations. The main plant parts used for this preparation are the bright orange or yellow flowers of the Calendula officinalis plant.

To make a Calendula officinalis compress, first, bring a pot of water to a boil, then remove it from heat and add dried or fresh Calendula flowers. Once the water has cooled, strain out the flowers and soak a clean cloth in the liquid before applying it to the affected area. When using a Calendula officinalis compress, it is important to test the compress on a small area of skin first to ensure no adverse reactions occur.

Additionally, it is recommended to avoid using Calendula officinalis compress on open wounds or deep tissue injuries, and to consult a healthcare professional before using this preparation if you are pregnant or breastfeeding.
