By Martin Pellizzer
0 minutes

While Ulmus rubra, also known as slippery elm, is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in various forms to treat a number of health conditions, its misuse or improper use can lead to several side effects.

If taken in large amounts, it can cause vomiting, nausea, and intestinal blockage. Prolonged use may result in vitamin deficiencies due to its ability to interfere with the absorption of nutrients in the digestive tract.

Pregnant women are advised to avoid slippery elm as its safety has not been established for this group. Additionally, it may interact with certain medications, such as aspirin and other blood-thinning drugs, potentially leading to bleeding complications.

Therefore, it is crucial to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new supplement regimen, including the use of Ulmus rubra.
