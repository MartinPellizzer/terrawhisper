10 Best Medicinal Preparations of Ginkgo biloba (Ginkgo)

Ginkgo biloba, also known as the maidenhair tree, has been used in traditional medicine for centuries and its leaves are used to make various medicinal preparations such as tisanes and tinctures.

A tisane is a type of herbal tea that is made by steeping the dried leaves in hot water. This preparation is often used to help improve memory and cognitive function, and it may also have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

A tincture, on the other hand, is a liquid extract made by soaking the leaves in alcohol or another solvent to extract the active compounds. Tinctures are typically taken orally, and they are believed to have similar benefits to tisanes, including improved memory and cognitive function, as well as potential protection against age-related cognitive decline. Both tisanes and tinctures made from Ginkgo biloba are considered generally safe, but they can cause side effects such as stomach upset, headache, and dizziness in some people.

It is important to talk to a healthcare provider before starting any new supplement regimen, including Ginkgo biloba.

1. Standardized Extract

Ginkgo Biloba standardized extract is a concentrated form of the Ginkgo biloba tree's leaves, which has been processed to ensure a consistent level of active compounds, mainly flavonoids and terpenoids.

Its main health benefits include improving cognitive function, memory, and concentration, reducing anxiety and depression, and alleviating symptoms of tinnitus and vertigo. The leaves of the Ginkgo biloba tree are used to make the standardized extract.

To prepare the extract, the leaves are dried and then subjected to a series of processes, including extraction, concentration, and standardization. When using Ginkgo biloba standardized extract, it is important to follow the recommended dosage and not to exceed it, as it can cause side effects such as stomach upset, headache, and allergic skin reactions.

Additionally, it is recommended to consult a healthcare provider before taking Ginkgo biloba standardized extract if you are taking blood-thinning medications, have a history of bleeding disorders, or are pregnant or breastfeeding.

3. Tincture

Ginkgo Biloba tincture is a medicinal preparation made by soaking and mixing crushed Ginkgo Biloba leaves in alcohol or another solvent to extract the beneficial compounds.

The main health benefits of this preparation include improved cognitive function, reduced anxiety, and improved circulation, which can help alleviate symptoms of conditions such as dementia, tinnitus, and Raynaud's disease. The leaves of the Ginkgo Biloba tree are the primary plant part used for this preparation.

To make Ginkgo Biloba tincture, the crushed leaves are soaked in a solvent, such as alcohol or glycerin, for several weeks before being strained. When using Ginkgo Biloba tincture, it is important to follow the recommended dosage, as excessive use can cause side effects such as stomach upset, headache, and allergic skin reactions.

Additionally, individuals with blood disorders, seizures, or who are taking blood-thinning medication should use caution and consult with a healthcare provider before using Ginkgo Biloba tincture.

5. Infusion

Ginkgo Biloba infusion is a traditional medicinal preparation made by steeping the leaves of the Ginkgo Biloba tree in hot water to extract its beneficial compounds.
