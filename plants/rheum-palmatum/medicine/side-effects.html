By Martin Pellizzer
0 minutes

Rheum palmatum, also known as Chinese rhubarb, can have several potential health side effects if used improperly.

First, it can cause stomach discomfort, including cramping, diarrhea, and nausea, especially when consumed in large amounts. Overuse can also lead to dehydration and electrolyte imbalances.

Moreover, Chinese rhubarb contains anthraquinones, which can have a laxative effect and, if used excessively, can result in dependency and damage to the gastrointestinal tract. Additionally, it can interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and antacids, leading to adverse effects. Pregnant and breastfeeding women should avoid using Rheum palmatum, as it may negatively impact fetal development and lactation.

Proper usage and dosage should always be followed to minimize health risks.
