10 Best Medicinal Preparations of Symphytum officinale (Comfrey)

Symphytum officinale, also known as comfrey, has been used in medicinal preparations for centuries due to its healing properties.

A common preparation is a tisane, or herbal tea, made by steeping dried comfrey leaves in hot water. This soothing drink is believed to help alleviate symptoms of digestive disorders and reduce inflammation in the body.

Comfrey tincture, made by soaking the plant's roots or leaves in alcohol, is another popular preparation. This potent extract is often used topically to treat skin conditions, muscle pain, and joint inflammation. However, it's important to note that comfrey contains compounds that can be toxic in large amounts, so it should be used with caution and under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

Pregnant women and young children should avoid comfrey preparations altogether due to the risk of liver damage and other serious side effects.

1. Infusion

Symphytum Officinale infusion is a medicinal preparation made by steeping dried comfrey leaves or roots in hot water, which releases the plant's beneficial compounds into the water.

The main health benefits of this comfrey preparation include its ability to reduce inflammation, promote healing of wounds and broken bones, and soothe digestive issues. The primary plant parts used for this infusion are the leaves and roots, which contain allantoin, rosmarinic acid, and mucilage that contribute to its therapeutic properties.

To make a comfrey infusion, place a teaspoon of dried comfrey leaves or roots in a cup of boiling water, let it steep for 10-15 minutes, then strain and drink. However, it is crucial to take precautions when using comfrey infusion, as it contains pyrrolizidine alkaloids that can cause liver damage if taken in large amounts or over an extended period.

Therefore, it is recommended to consult a healthcare professional before using comfrey infusion and limit its use to external applications or short-term internal use.

2. Tincture

Symphytum Officinale tincture is a medicinal preparation made by soaking comfrey leaves and roots in a solvent, usually alcohol or glycerin, to extract its active compounds.

The main health benefits of this tincture include its ability to promote wound healing, reduce inflammation, and alleviate pain due to its high content of allantoin, rosmarinic acid, and mucilage. The leaves and roots of the comfrey plant are mainly used for this preparation.

To make the tincture, the dried or fresh comfrey parts are steeped in the solvent for several weeks, then strained and stored in a cool, dark place. It is important to note that Symphytum Officinale tincture should be used with caution, as large amounts or prolonged use can be toxic to the liver and may cause damage to the kidneys.

Pregnant women and children should avoid using comfrey tincture, and it should not be applied to broken skin or taken internally for extended periods of time.

3. Decoction

Symphytum Officinale decoction is a medicinal preparation made by simmering the roots or leaves of the comfrey plant in water to extract its active compounds.

This preparation is known to have several health benefits, including reducing inflammation, soothing digestive issues, and promoting wound healing. The main plant parts used for this preparation are the roots and leaves, which contain allantoin, rosmarinic acid, and mucilage, among other beneficial compounds.

To make a Symphytum Officinale decoction, place 1-2 teaspoons of dried comfrey root or leaves in a pot with 1 cup of water and bring to a boil. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes, then strain and drink.

It is important to note that Symphytum Officinale decoction should not be taken internally during pregnancy or by individuals with liver disease, and it should not be applied to broken skin due to the risk of irritation and potential toxicity.

4. Ointment

Symphytum Officinale ointment is a medicinal preparation made from the roots and leaves of the Symphytum officinale plant which is native to Europe.

The main health benefits of this ointment include its ability to promote wound healing, reduce inflammation, and alleviate pain due to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. To make the ointment, the roots and leaves are first dried and then crushed into a fine powder.

This powder is then mixed with a base such as beeswax or coconut oil and heated until fully combined. The resulting ointment is then allowed to cool and solidify before being stored in a cool, dark place. It is important to note that Symphytum officinale ointment should not be used on deep or puncture wounds, and should be kept out of reach of children.

Additionally, it should not be used for prolonged periods of time, as the plant contains compounds that can be toxic in large amounts.

5. Cream

Symphytum Officinale cream is a topical ointment made from the roots and leaves of the Symphytum officinale plant, also known as comfrey.

This cream is known for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, making it a popular choice for treating minor wounds, sprains, and joint pain. The main active compounds in comfrey, including allantoin, rosmarinic acid, and tannins, are believed to contribute to its medicinal benefits.

To make comfrey cream, the plant material is typically dried, chopped, and steeped in a carrier oil such as olive or almond oil. The oil is then strained, and beeswax is added to thicken it into a creamy consistency. When using Symphytum Officinale cream, it is important to follow the recommended dosage and avoid applying it to broken or large wounds, as comfrey contains pyrrolizidine alkaloids, which can be toxic in high concentrations.

Additionally, it is not recommended for internal use or for use during pregnancy.

6. Poultice

Symphytum Officinale poultice is a medicinal preparation made by mashing or grinding the leaves and/or roots of the comfrey plant into a paste, which is then applied directly to the skin to treat various ailments.

The main health benefits of this Symphytum Officinale preparation include its ability to reduce inflammation, promote healing of wounds, bruises, and sprains, and alleviate pain and swelling. The leaves and roots of the comfrey plant are the main plant parts used for this preparation. To make a Symphytum Officinale poultice, first, gather fresh comfrey leaves and/or roots and clean them thoroughly.

Next, mash or grind the plant material into a paste using a mortar and pestle or a blender. Apply the paste directly to the affected area and cover it with a cloth or bandage. Change the poultice every few hours or as needed. It is important to note that prolonged use or internal consumption of Symphytum Officinale preparations can be harmful, as the plant contains compounds that can be toxic to the liver.

Therefore, it is crucial to consult with a healthcare professional before using comfrey poultices and to avoid using them on open wounds or broken skin.

7. Oil extract

Symphytum Officinale oil extract is a medicinal preparation derived from the Comfrey plant, characterized by its thick consistency and dark green or brown color.

Its main health benefits include providing relief for joint inflammation, reducing pain and swelling, promoting the healing of wounds and bruises, and soothing skin irritations. The roots and leaves of the Symphytum officinale plant are primarily used to create this oil extract. The preparation process involves infusing dried or fresh Comfrey plant parts in a carrier oil, such as olive or jojoba oil, and allowing it to steep for several weeks.

It is crucial to strain the plant material before using the oil. When using Symphytum Officinale oil extract, it is essential to avoid internal consumption and apply it only externally, as it contains pyrrolizidine alkaloids, which can be toxic if ingested. Always conduct a patch test to ensure skin compatibility and avoid using it on broken skin or open wounds.

Pregnant or breastfeeding women should consult a healthcare professional before using Comfrey oil extract.

8. Suppository

Symphytum Officinale suppository is a medicinal preparation that involves the formation of a solid dosage form from the extracts of Symphytum officinale for rectal administration.

The main health benefits of this suppository include its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, making it useful for relieving pain, inflammation, and swelling in the rectum and anus, such as in cases of hemorrhoids, anal fissures, and proctitis. The roots and leaves of Symphytum officinale are the primary plant parts used in the preparation of this suppository.

To make the suppository, the dried or fresh plant material is extracted using a suitable solvent, and the extract is then incorporated into a base material, such as cocoa butter or polyethylene glycol, which solidifies at room temperature. When using Symphytum officinale suppository, it is essential to follow the recommended dosage and avoid using it for extended periods, as prolonged use may cause liver toxicity due to the presence of pyrrolizidine alkaloids in the plant.

Additionally, pregnant and breastfeeding women should avoid using this preparation due to the potential risks associated with these alkaloids.

9. Liniment

Symphytum Officinale liniment is a medicinal preparation made from the Symphytum officinale plant which is extracted in a base such as oil or water to create a liniment that can be applied topically to the skin.

The main health benefits of this Symphytum Officinale preparation include pain relief, reduced inflammation, and promotion of tissue regeneration, making it useful for treating minor wounds, sprains, and joint pain. The roots and leaves of the Symphytum officinale plant are typically used in the preparation of comfrey liniment.

To make the liniment, the plant material is first dried and then steeped in a base such as olive oil or witch hazel for several weeks before being strained. The final product can be stored in a cool, dark place for up to a year. It is important to note that Symphytum Officinale contains pyrrolizidine alkaloids, which can be toxic in large amounts, so it should not be used for prolonged periods of time, applied to large areas of the body, or taken internally.

Additionally, it should not be used on broken skin or wounds that have not yet been cleaned.

10. Syrup

Symphytum Officinale syrup is a medicinal preparation made from the roots and leaves of the comfrey plant, Symphytum officinale.

This syrup is known for its anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and expectorant properties, making it a popular remedy for treating respiratory conditions such as bronchitis, asthma, and coughs. To make the syrup, the plant parts are first dried and then simmered in water.

The resulting liquid is strained and combined with honey or sugar to create a sweet, thick syrup. However, it is important to note that Symphytum officinale contains pyrrolizidine alkaloids, which can be toxic to the liver in large amounts. Therefore, it is crucial to use this syrup in moderation and only for short periods of time.

Additionally, pregnant women and young children should avoid using comfrey syrup altogether due to the potential risks associated with pyrrolizidine alkaloids.
