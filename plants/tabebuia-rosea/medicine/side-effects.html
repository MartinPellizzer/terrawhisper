Tabebuia rosea, also known as Pau d'Arco, is a tree native to South America that has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

When used improperly or in excessive amounts, Tabebuia rosea can have several negative health effects. It can cause digestive issues such as nausea, diarrhea, and vomiting.

Additionally, it can lead to increased heart rate, bleeding, and dizziness. Long-term use or high doses can result in more serious side effects, including liver damage and a decrease in white blood cells, which can weaken the immune system. Pregnant women and individuals with bleeding disorders or immune system disorders should avoid using Tabebuia rosea due to the increased risk of complications.

Always consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new supplement regimen.
