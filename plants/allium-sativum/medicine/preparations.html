10 Best Medicinal Preparations of Allium sativum (Garlic)

Allium sativum, commonly known as garlic, has been used for medicinal purposes for centuries.

Two common forms of garlic preparations are tisane and tincture. A tisane is a medicinal tea made by steeping crushed garlic cloves in hot water. This preparation is often used to help alleviate symptoms of respiratory ailments, such as congestion and cough.

Tinctures, on the other hand, are made by soaking chopped garlic in alcohol for several weeks. This preparation is taken orally and is often used to help reduce cholesterol levels, boost the immune system, and improve circulation. Both tisane and tincture can be made at home, but it is important to follow proper procedures to ensure their effectiveness and safety.

It is also recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new medicinal preparations.

1. oil

Allium Sativum oil, also known as garlic oil, is a medicinal preparation derived from the bulbs of the garlic plant.

Its main health benefits include boosting the immune system, reducing blood pressure, and improving cholesterol levels, which can help prevent heart disease. The oil is typically made by pressing or steam-distilling the garlic cloves to extract the beneficial compounds.

To make garlic oil at home, garlic cloves can be crushed and steeped in a carrier oil, such as olive or coconut oil, for several weeks. However, it's important to note that garlic oil can cause skin irritation and should be diluted before use.

Additionally, it can interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners, so it's essential to consult with a healthcare provider before using garlic oil as a medicinal preparation.
