0 minutes

Allium sativum, commonly known as garlic, is a flavorful and beneficial herb when used properly, but it can have negative health effects if misused.

Improper use of garlic can lead to gastrointestinal symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea due to its strong fiber content. Garlic has been known to lower blood pressure and thin the blood, so overconsumption can increase the risk of bleeding and bruising, especially for those taking blood-thinning medication.

Additionally, prolonged or excessive intake of raw garlic may cause damage to the stomach and liver, potentially leading to conditions like gastritis and hepatotoxicity. Furthermore, those with a garlic allergy may experience allergic reactions, such as skin rashes or difficulty breathing, if they come into contact with or consume garlic.

Therefore, it is crucial to use and consume Allium sativum correctly to avoid these potential adverse health effects.
