10 Best Medicinal Preparations of Apium graveolens (Celery)

Apium graveolens, also known as celery, is a plant that has been used in medicinal preparations for centuries.

Its seeds, leaves, and stalks are used to make tisanes, which are herbal teas, and tinctures. The tisane is made by steeping the celery leaves or seeds in hot water, and it is believed to have diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The tincture is made by soaking the celery seeds in alcohol, and it is used to treat various ailments such as rheumatism, arthritis, and neuralgia. Additionally, celery seeds have been used in traditional medicine to lower blood pressure and cholesterol levels. However, it is important to note that more research is needed to confirm these claims and to establish appropriate dosages.

As with any herbal remedy, it is always best to consult with a healthcare provider before use.

1. seed oil

Apium Graveolens seed oil, also known as celery seed oil, is derived from the seeds of the celery plant through a process of steam distillation.

The oil is known for its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and diuretic properties, making it a popular natural remedy for conditions such as arthritis, high blood pressure, and poor digestion. The main plant parts used for this preparation are the seeds, which are harvested from the celery plant and then dried before being steam distilled to extract the oil. To make the oil, the dried seeds are placed in a still and heated with water, which releases the oil from the seeds.

The oil is then collected and filtered to remove any impurities. When using celery seed oil, it is important to dilute it with a carrier oil before applying it to the skin, as it can cause skin irritation if used undiluted. Additionally, it is not recommended for use during pregnancy, as it may stimulate menstruation and lead to miscarriage.

It is also important to note that celery seed oil may interact with certain medications, so it is always best to consult with a healthcare provider before using it.
