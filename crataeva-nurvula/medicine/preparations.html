10. Varuna Injection

Varuna Injection is a medication typically administered through intravenous injection for treating and managing severe cases of acute gout flare-ups, which involves inflammation and pain in joints due to excessive uric acid crystals.

The illustration below summarize some common health conditions that benefits from Crataeva nurvula Injection.

The following list includes the main conditions that Varuna Injection helps to heal.

  • Rheumatoid arthritis
  • Juvenile idiopathic arthritis (juvenile rheumatology)
  • Ankylosing spondylitis
  • Psoriatic arthritis
  • Systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE)
  • Gouty arthritis
  • Polymyalgia rheumatica
  • Reactive arthritis
  • Osteoarthritis
  • Pseudogout
  • Crystal-induced arthropathies (pseudo-gout and others)

Varuna injection contains an active ingredient called adalimumab, which belongs to a class of drugs known as tumor necrosis factor (TNF) inhibitors. These medications work by targeting and blocking the action of TNF, a protein that plays a significant role in inflammatory processes within the body. By suppressing the activity of TNF, Varuna injection aims to relieve symptoms associated with several autoimmune diseases. Rheumatoid arthritis, juvenile idiopathic arthritis, ankylosing spondylitis, psoriatic arthritis, systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE), and gouty arthritis are a few common rheumatologic disorders. Arthritis conditions involve inflammation in the joints caused due to an overactive immune response. In such situations, TNF becomes an integral part of this process, leading to further joint damage. Therefore, inhibiting the activity of TNF with Varuna injection helps alleviate symptoms like pain and stiffness associated with these conditions by curtailing the excessive inflammatory response. Additionally, Varuna is effective in treating polymyalgia rheumatica (an inflammatory disorder affecting muscles), reactive arthritis (inflammation of joints due to an infection elsewhere), and osteoarthritis (a degenerative joint disease). It also aids in managing crystal-induced arthropathies, including pseudogout and others characterized by crystalline deposits in the joints. In conclusion, Varuna injection is used to treat various inflammatory conditions because it blocks the detrimental action of TNF protein involved in driving such conditions.
