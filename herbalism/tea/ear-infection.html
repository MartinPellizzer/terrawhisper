10 Best Herbal Teas For Ear Infection

Herbal teas, particularly those made from mullein, calendula, and echinacea, can potentially help with ear infections due to their anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and immune-boosting properties.

These herbs may help reduce inflammation and fight off bacterial infections in the ear, providing relief from symptoms. Additionally, the warm temperature of the tea can help soothe pain and discomfort in the ear.

However, it's important to note that herbal teas should not be used as a substitute for medical treatment and should only be used as a complementary therapy. If symptoms persist or worsen, individuals should seek medical attention from a healthcare professional.

It's also recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before using herbal teas to treat ear infections, especially in children, pregnant women, or individuals with underlying health conditions.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with ear infection because it has anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that can help reduce inflammation and fight bacterial or viral infections in the ear.

The warm temperature of the tea can also help to alleviate pain and discomfort caused by the infection. Additionally, drinking chamomile tea can help boost the immune system, allowing the body to better fight off the infection. However, it's important to note that while chamomile tea can provide some relief, it should not be used as a substitute for medical treatment, especially for severe or persistent ear infections.

Lastly, chamomile tea can be used as a complementary therapy, it can be used as a natural remedy to soothe symptoms and promote faster healing.

The following procedure explaing how to make chamomile tea for ear infection.

  1. To make chamomile tea for an ear infection, start by boiling 2 cups of water and adding 2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers (or 2 tea bags) to a teapot.
  2. Allow the tea to steep for 10-15 minutes, covered, to extract the beneficial compounds and achieve a strong enough infusion.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the chamomile flowers, and let it cool down to a warm temperature, ensuring it's not too hot to avoid burns when applying it near the ear.
  4. When the tea has reached a comfortable temperature, soak a clean washcloth in the tea, wring it out, and place it over the infected ear for 5-10 minutes, allowing the chamomile's anti-inflammatory properties to take effect.
  5. Repeat this process 2-3 times a day, discarding the used chamomile and making fresh tea for each application, until symptoms improve. Remember to consult a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

2. Calendula

Calendula tea helps with ear infection because it has anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that can help reduce inflammation and fight off the bacteria or virus causing the infection.

The tea can also help relieve pain and congestion in the Eustachian tubes, which can contribute to ear infections. Additionally, drinking calendula tea can boost the immune system, helping the body to fight off the infection more effectively. It's important to note that while calendula tea can be a helpful addition to treatment, it should not be used as a substitute for medical attention, especially for severe or persistent ear infections.

Lastly, the tea can be used as a warm compress, which can provide soothing relief to the affected ear.

The following procedure explaing how to make calendula tea for ear infection.

  1. To make calendula tea for ear infection, start by boiling 1 cup of water and adding 1-2 teaspoons of dried calendula flowers, allowing it to steep for 10-15 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea to remove the flowers, then let it cool down to a warm temperature that is comfortable to the touch.
  3. Fill a clean dropper with the calendula tea, ensuring that it is not too hot.
  4. Tilt your head to the side and gently squeeze a few drops of the tea into the affected ear, keeping your head tilted for a few minutes to allow the tea to penetrate the ear.
  5. Repeat this process 2-3 times a day until symptoms improve, making sure to discard any remaining tea after each use.

3. Echinacea

Echinacea tea helps with ear infection because it has anti-inflammatory and immune-boosting properties that can reduce inflammation and fight off infections.

The tea is made from the leaves and flowers of the echinacea plant, which contain phenols, volatile oils, and flavonoids that have been shown to have antimicrobial and antiviral effects. These compounds can help to reduce inflammation in the ears and alleviate the pain and pressure associated with ear infections. Additionally, echinacea tea can help to boost the body's overall immune response, making it easier for the body to fight off infections and recover more quickly.

However, it's important to note that echinacea tea should not be used as a substitute for medical treatment, and anyone experiencing symptoms of an ear infection should consult a healthcare professional.

The following procedure explaing how to make echinacea tea for ear infection.

  1. To make echinacea tea for an ear infection, start by boiling 2 cups of water and adding 2 teaspoons of dried echinacea flowers and/or leaves; let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea to remove the plant material, then add the juice of half a lemon and 1-2 teaspoons of honey (optional) for taste and additional antimicrobial benefits.
  3. Allow the tea to cool down to a warm temperature, ensuring it's not too hot to cause discomfort when applying it to the ear.
  4. Soak a clean cotton ball in the echinacea tea and gently squeeze out any excess liquid.
  5. Tilt your head to the side and place the damp cotton ball in the ear for 5-10 minutes, allowing the liquid to slowly enter the ear canal and providing relief and potential antibacterial properties. Repeat this process 2-3 times a day for best results.

4. Goldenseal

Goldenseal tea helps with ear infection because it has natural anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties, which can reduce inflammation and fight the bacterial infection in the ear.

The tea is made from the goldenseal herb, which contains berberine, a compound known to be effective in treating various types of infections. Drinking goldenseal tea may also help boost the immune system, enabling the body to fight off the infection more effectively. However, it is important to note that goldenseal tea should not be used as a substitute for medical treatment, but rather as a complementary therapy.

As with any natural remedy, it is always best to consult with a healthcare provider before use.

The following procedure explaing how to make goldenseal tea for ear infection.

  1. In a pot, bring 1 cup of water to a boil, then add 1-2 teaspoons of dried goldenseal root, reduce heat to low, cover and simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  2. After simmering, let the mixture cool down to a comfortable temperature, then strain the liquid to remove the goldenseal pieces.
  3. Fill a clean dropper bottle with the cooled goldenseal tea, and lie down on your side with the affected ear facing up.
  4. Gently squeeze the dropper to release 2-3 drops of the goldenseal tea into the ear, allowing it to sit in the ear for 5-10 minutes.
  5. After 5-10 minutes, sit up and tilt your head to drain the liquid, then clean any remaining liquid with a tissue or cloth. Repeat this process 2-3 times a day for 3-4 days.

The following procedure explaing how to make lavender tea for ear infection.

  4. Tilt your head to the side and gently pour or use a syringe to apply the lavender tea into the affected ear, being careful not to let it get too deep into the ear canal; let it sit for 5-10 minutes.

6. Ginger

Ginger tea helps with ear infection because it has natural anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties that can reduce inflammation and pain in the ear.

The warm nature of ginger tea can also help to alleviate pressure build-up in the Eustachian tubes, which can cause discomfort in the ear. Additionally, ginger tea contains compounds called gingerols and shogaols, which have antimicrobial and antibacterial effects that can help to fight against the bacterial or viral infections causing the ear infection. Furthermore, ginger tea can help to boost the immune system, which can speed up the healing process and prevent the infection from spreading.

Lastly, ginger tea is a natural and gentle alternative to over-the-counter medications, making it a safe and effective remedy for both children and adults.

The following procedure explaing how to make ginger tea for ear infection.

  1. Peel and finely chop 1 tablespoon of fresh ginger, then add it to a pot with 2 cups of water; bring the water to a boil, reduce heat, and simmer, covered, for 10 minutes.
  2. Remove the pot from heat and stir in 1 teaspoon of honey and 1/4 teaspoon of lemon juice; allow the mixture to cool down to a safe temperature.
  3. Strain the ginger tea into a glass, discarding the solids.
  4. Tilt your head to one side and, using a spoon, slowly pour a small amount of the ginger tea into the affected ear; let it sit for 5-10 minutes, then tilt your head to drain the liquid.
  5. Repeat steps 4 and 5 with the other ear if needed, then discard any remaining tea.

8. Garlic

Garlic tea helps with ear infection because of its natural antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, derived from the compound allicin found in garlic.

This powerful compound can help to fight off the bacteria or viruses causing the ear infection, while its anti-inflammatory effects can alleviate pain and swelling in the Eustachian tubes and middle ear. Furthermore, garlic tea's warmth can help to soothe the ear and provide additional relief, making it an effective and comforting home remedy for ear infections. It is important to note that while garlic tea can be beneficial, it should not replace medical treatment, and individuals should consult a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and guidance.

Lastly, garlic tea can be prepared easily by steeping crushed garlic in hot water, allowing the active compounds to infuse, and then straining the liquid before consumption.

The following procedure explaing how to make garlic tea for ear infection.

  1. Peel and finely mince 3 cloves of garlic, then let it sit for 10 minutes to activate its health-promoting compounds (this step is important, as it allows the allicin in garlic to form, which has antibacterial and antiviral properties); bring 2 cups of water to a boil in a small saucepan, then add the minced garlic and reduce heat to low; let it simmer for 15 minutes.
  2. After simmering, strain the garlic-infused water into a mug, discarding the garlic pieces; squeeze half of a lemon into the garlic water for its vitamin C and additional antimicrobial benefits.
  3. Peel and crush a small piece of ginger (about 1 inch long), adding it to the garlic-lemon water for its anti-inflammatory properties and spicy flavor.
  4. Warm 1 tablespoon of honey in a small dish in the microwave for 10-15 seconds, then add it to the garlic-lemon-ginger tea, stirring until it's fully dissolved; raw honey adds soothing properties and a touch of natural sweetness.
  5. Sip the garlic tea warm, taking breaks as needed; aim to drink the whole mug, as the garlic-ginger-lemon-honey combination can help alleviate ear infection symptoms and support your immune system. Enjoy!

  5. Discard any remaining tea and repeat the process 2-3 times daily for best results, or as directed by a healthcare professional.
