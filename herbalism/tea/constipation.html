10 Best Herbal Teas For Constipation

Herbal teas, particularly those made from plants like senna, psyllium, and licorice, can help alleviate constipation due to their natural laxative properties.

These herbs contain compounds that stimulate the intestines, promoting muscle contractions that move stool through the digestive tract. Additionally, some herbal teas can help increase the water content in the intestines, softening stool and making it easier to pass.

Furthermore, these teas often contain antispasmodic properties that can help relax the muscles in the intestines, reducing discomfort and pain associated with constipation. However, it's important to note that while herbal teas can be helpful, they should not be used as a substitute for medical treatment, especially in cases of chronic or severe constipation.

As with any treatment, it's always best to consult with a healthcare professional before use.

1. Slippery Elm

Slippery elm tea helps with constipation because it contains high amounts of mucilage, a natural substance that forms a slick coating when mixed with water.

This coating softens stool, making it easier to pass through the digestive tract. Slippery elm also has demulcent properties, which help soothe and protect the digestive lining, reducing inflammation and irritation that can contribute to constipation.

Additionally, slippery elm tea may help stimulate peristalsis, the wave-like contractions that move food through the intestines. The tea's calming effect on the digestive system can also reduce stress, another common cause of constipation.

Overall, slippery elm tea provides relief for constipation by softening stool, soothing the digestive tract, stimulating peristalsis, and reducing stress.

The following procedure explaing how to make slippery elm tea for constipation.

  1. Boil 2 cups of water in a pot and then add 1-2 teaspoons of dried slippery elm bark to the boiling water.
  2. Reduce heat to a simmer and let the mixture steep for 5-10 minutes, allowing the slippery elm bark to release its beneficial mucilage.
  3. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the used bark, and let it cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  4. Stir in a small amount of honey or a squeeze of lemon juice for added flavor, if desired.
  5. Drink the slippery elm tea 1-2 times a day, as needed, to help alleviate constipation and soothe the digestive tract.

2. Marshmallow Root

Marshmallow root tea helps with constipation because of its soothing and protective effects on the digestive system.

The mucilage content in marshmallow root absorbs water, creating a gel-like substance that coats and softens stool, making it easier to pass. This herbal tea also has anti-inflammatory properties that can reduce inflammation in the intestinal lining, allowing for smoother digestion and bowel movements. Marshmallow root tea's ability to promote hydration and maintain electrolyte balance further supports regularity and overall digestive health.

Regular consumption of this gentle yet effective remedy may alleviate chronic constipation symptoms in individuals.

The following procedure explaing how to make marshmallow root tea for constipation.

  1. Gather 1-2 teaspoons of dried marshmallow root and 1 cup of water.
  2. Place marshmallow root in a pot and pour the water over it.
  3. Slowly bring the mixture to a simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea, discarding the solids, and let it cool down.
  5. Drink the marshmallow root tea 1-2 times a day to help relieve constipation.

3. Psyllium Seed

Psyllium seed tea helps with constipation because it is a natural source of dietary fiber that absorbs water and expands in the stomach, forming a gel-like substance that softens stool and makes it easier to pass through the digestive tract.

This gel also acts as a gentle bulking agent, increasing the size and weight of stools, which can stimulate peristalsis and promote regular bowel movements. Additionally, psyllium seed tea has been shown in studies to increase stool frequency and improve overall gut health by adding beneficial bacteria and reducing inflammation.

Its natural laxative properties make it an effective and safe alternative to synthetic laxatives for relief of occasional constipation.

The following procedure explaing how to make psyllium seed tea for constipation.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water in a saucepan.
  2. Stir in 1 teaspoon of psyllium seed powder when the water reaches a boil.
  3. Reduce heat and simmer for 5-10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Strain the tea through a fine-mesh sieve into a cup.
  5. Drink the tea while it is still warm, up to three times a day as needed for constipation relief.

4. Senna Leaf

Senna leaf tea helps with constipation because it contains natural laxatives, primarily sennosides A and B, which stimulate the contraction of intestinal muscles and promote the movement of food through the digestive tract.

Its gentle yet effective bowel activity enhancer properties make it a popular natural remedy for constipation relief. Senna tea can be consumed daily over an extended period to address chronic constipation, while its quick acting nature allows for immediate relief in cases of acute constipation.

The herbal tea's long-standing traditional use for alleviating digestive discomfort and irregular bowel movements further attests to its efficacy.

The following procedure explaing how to make senna leaf tea for constipation.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan on the stove.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried senna leaves to a tea infuser or teapot.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the senna leaves and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strain the tea to remove the leaves.
  5. Drink the tea while it is still warm, preferably at bedtime, to help relieve constipation.

5. Ginger Root

Ginger root tea helps with constipation because it contains gingerol, a compound that stimulates digestion and increases intestinal motility.

The warming properties of ginger tea can help soothe cramping muscles in the gut and improve blood flow to the area, further promoting effective elimination. Drinking ginger root tea before meals may enhance your appetite and ensure proper digestion, reducing the likelihood of hardened stools forming.

Additionally, ginger tea's anti-inflammatory properties can help alleviate pain and discomfort associated with constipation and provide overall relief for those suffering from this condition.

The following procedure explaing how to make ginger root tea for constipation.

  1. Peel and finely chop 1-2 inches of fresh ginger root; add to a pot with 4 cups of water.
  2. Bring the water to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the ginger-infused water into a cup, discarding the solids.
  4. Add lemon juice (from half a lemon) and honey (1-2 teaspoons) to taste, stirring until fully dissolved.
  5. Sip the warm ginger root tea, allowing it to promote digestion and relieve constipation.

6. Fennel Seed

Fennel seed tea helps with constipation because it contains anethole, a compound known to promote gastrointestinal motility.

Anethole stimulates the production of gastric juices and bile, which aid in the breakdown of food and the contraction of intestinal muscles that help push waste through the digestive tract. Additionally, fennel seed tea has antispasmodic properties, meaning it can help relax and soothe the intestinal walls, reducing cramps and easing the passing of stools. Furthermore, its carminative effects help to expel intestinal gas, alleviating bloating and discomfort associated with constipation.

Lastly, fennel seed tea has been used traditionally for centuries as a natural remedy for digestive issues, including constipation, due to its ability to improve overall gut health and promote regular bowel movements.

The following procedure explaing how to make fennel seed tea for constipation.

  1. Boil 1 teaspoon of fennel seeds in 1 cup of water for 10 minutes.
  2. Strain the mixture to remove the seeds, leaving only the fennel-infused water.
  3. Allow the fennel water to cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  4. Drink the fennel tea 2-3 times a day, preferably after meals.
  5. Optional: Add a bit of honey or lemon juice for taste, but avoid adding milk or sugar.

7. Turmeric

Turmeric tea helps with constipation because of its anti-inflammatory properties, which reduce inflammation in the digestive tract and help stimulate bowel movements.

The tea's antioxidant content, particularly curcumin, also aids in digestion and promotes the production of bile, which is essential for breaking down food and preventing constipation. Additionally, turmeric tea can help soothe the stomach and intestines, reducing bloating and cramping, common symptoms of constipation. Its warm temperature can also provide comfort and relaxation, encouraging the body to release stool more easily.

Overall, the combination of these healing benefits makes turmeric tea an effective natural remedy for relief from constipation.

The following procedure explaing how to make turmeric tea for constipation.

  1. Boil 4 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Stir in 1-2 teaspoons of ground turmeric and reduce heat to a simmer.
  3. Let the mixture simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, add honey or lemon to taste (optional), and drink while warm.
  5. Repeat up to three times a day for constipation relief.

8. Licorice Root

Licorice root tea helps with constipation because of its natural ability to stimulate the production of mucus in the digestive tract, which coats and soothes the intestinal lining, allowing stool to pass more easily through the colon.

Its antispasmodic properties also help reduce cramping and muscle contractions in the intestines, contributing to smoother bowel movements. Additionally, licorice root contains glycyrrhizin, which can stimulate the release of digestive juices and increase overall gut motility.

Finally, its gentle laxative effect can help soften stool and promote regular bowel movements in individuals struggling with chronic constipation.

The following procedure explaing how to make licorice root tea for constipation.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan on medium heat.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried licorice root (Glycyrrhiza glabra) to the boiling water.
  3. Reduce heat to low, cover the saucepan, and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the used licorice root.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, preferably 30 minutes before bedtime, to help relieve constipation.

9. Dandelion Root

Dandelion root tea helps with constipation because of its diuretic and laxative properties.

The tea increases urine production, which can soften stools by pulling water into the intestines. Additionally, the tea contains inulin, a type of fiber that promotes gut health and encourages regular bowel movements. Its bitter taste stimulates digestive enzymes and bile production to support proper digestion and elimination.

Lastly, dandelion root tea is caffeine-free and naturally decaffeinated, making it a gentle yet effective option for those experiencing constipation or irritability from caffeinated teas or beverages.

The following procedure explaing how to make dandelion root tea for constipation.

  1. Gather 1-2 teaspoons of dried dandelion root and place in a tea infuser or teapot with a strainer.
  2. Boil 4 cups of water and pour over the dandelion root in the infuser or teapot.
  3. Allow the tea to steep for 10-15 minutes for optimal extraction of the beneficial compounds.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strain the tea into a cup before consumption.
  5. Drink 2-3 cups of dandelion root tea per day, preferably between meals, to help relieve constipation.

10. Ginseng

Ginseng tea helps with constipation because it contains compounds that stimulate digestion and promote bowel movements.

The primary active ingredients are ginsenosides, which increase intestinal motility and relax the smooth muscle tissue of the intestines. Additionally, ginseng tea has been shown to reduce inflammation in the gut, which can be a contributing factor to constipation. Furthermore, it contains antioxidants that protect the gut lining from damage, allowing for optimal nutrient absorption and regular elimination.

Finally, ginseng tea's mild diuretic effect can help increase hydration levels in the body, which is essential for maintaining soft stools and preventing constipation.

The following procedure explaing how to make ginseng tea for constipation.

  1. Boil 2 cups of water in a saucepan and add 1 tablespoon of dried ginseng root or 2-3 ginseng tea bags.
  2. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes to allow the water to absorb the ginseng's active compounds.
  3. Turn off the heat and strain the tea to remove the ginseng root or tea bags.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, for added flavor and potential additional benefits for constipation.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, up to 2-3 times a day, to help relieve constipation and promote regular bowel movements.
