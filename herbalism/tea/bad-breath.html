10 best herbal teas for bad breath

Certain herbal teas are known to be effective in reducing bad breath, also known as halitosis.

Peppermint tea, for instance, contains menthol which has antibacterial properties that can kill the germs causing bad breath. Spearmint and sage teas are also good options, as they contain chlorophyll which is known to neutralize bad odors. Additionally, green tea is a popular choice due to its high concentration of polyphenols, which have been shown to eliminate sulfur-producing bacteria in the mouth.

Drinking these herbal teas can help freshen breath and promote oral health.

1. Parsley

Parsley tea helps with bad breath due to its antibacterial and antimicrobial properties, which neutralize sulfur compounds produced by mouth bacteria, causing bad breath.

Parsley contains chlorophyll, a natural deodorizer, freshening breath. Parsley tea stimulates saliva production, washing away food particles and bacteria. Parsley has been used traditionally as a natural breath freshener and digestive aid, making it popular for those seeking natural remedies for bad breath.

However, parsley tea is not a substitute for good oral hygiene practices such as regular brushing, flossing, and dental checkups.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in parsley tea that help with bad breath.

  • Leaves: Parsley leaves are rich in chlorophyll, which helps neutralize bad breath caused by oral bacteria.
  • Seeds: Parsley seeds contain oil that has antibacterial properties, making it effective in fighting bad breath.
  • Stems: Parsley stems contain tannins that help reduce inflammation and inhibit the growth of bacteria in the mouth.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic parsley tea for bad breath.

  1. Rinse 1 cup of fresh parsley, then coarsely chop and place in a heatproof pitcher or teapot.
  2. Bring 2 cups of water to a boil, then pour over the parsley and let steep for 10 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the parsley, then allow it to cool to a drinkable temperature.
  4. Stir in honey or lemon juice to taste, if desired.
  5. Drink the tea throughout the day, up to three times daily, for fresher breath.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for bad breath.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water, then pour over 2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers in a teapot.

  3. Strain the tea to remove the chamomile flowers, discarding the spent flowers.
  4. Let the tea cool to a drinkable temperature, then sip slowly to allow it to coat your mouth and throat.
  5. Drink the chamomile tea up to three times a day, especially after meals, to help freshen breath and soothe digestion.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginger tea for bad breath.

  2. Add 2 cups of water to the saucepan and bring to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat and let simmer for 10 minutes.
  4. Strain the ginger pieces from the liquid, then stir in honey and lemon juice to taste (optional).
  5. Pour the ginger tea into a cup and enjoy immediately for fresh breath.
