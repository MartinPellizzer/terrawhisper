10 best herbal teas for low blood pressure

For individuals with low blood pressure, certain herbal teas can be beneficial in supporting healthy blood pressure levels.

Hawthorn tea is known for its ability to dilate blood vessels, which can help increase blood flow and improve circulation. Gingko Biloba tea is another option, as it can help improve blood flow to the brain and improve overall vascular health. Additionally, Licorice root tea has been shown to help regulate blood pressure by balancing stress hormones in the body.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new herbal remedies, especially for individuals with pre-existing medical conditions or those taking medications.

1. Hawthorn

Hawthorn tea aids in treating low blood pressure due to its medicinal properties derived from the Hawthorn shrub.

This tea contains antioxidant flavonoids, oligomeric proanthocyanidins (OPCs), and saponins that act on the cardiovascular system. Specifically, these compounds strengthen heart contractions, widen blood vessels, and improve blood flow, all contributing to an increase in blood pressure. Consequently, hawthorn tea offers a natural, caffeine-free solution for individuals dealing with hypotension.

Regular consumption of hawthorn tea, under the guidance of a healthcare professional, can help maintain a healthy blood pressure range.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in hawthorn tea that help with low blood pressure.

  • Flowers: Rich in antioxidants, the hawthorn tea prepared from flowers is known to improve blood circulation, thus helping in reducing blood pressure.
  • Leaves: Containing high levels of flavonoids, hawthorn tea made from leaves has a direct impact on cardiovascular health, thereby lowering blood pressure.
  • Berries: Packed with cardiotonic and vasodilatory compounds, hawthorn berries enhance the functioning of the heart and widen blood vessels, ultimately reducing blood pressure.
  • Bark: Abundant in procyanidins, hawthorn bark used in tea can relax blood vessels, alleviating hypertension.
  • Seeds: Rich in amygdalin and other active components, hawthorn seeds have been used traditionally to dilate peripheral blood vessels, lowering the overall blood pressure.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic hawthorn tea for low blood pressure.

  1. In a stoveproof pot, combine 1 quart of water and 2 tablespoons of dried hawthorn berries.
  2. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the hawthorn berries, reserving the liquid.
  4. Optional: Add honey or lemon to taste.
  5. Serve the hawthorn tea hot and drink 2-3 times a day for low blood pressure.

2. Ginkgo Biloba

Ginkgo biloba tea benefits individuals with low blood pressure due to its vasodilating properties, which relax and widen blood vessels, thus improving circulation and increasing blood pressure.

This herbal tea contains flavonoids and terpenoids, which possess potent antioxidant qualities, protecting blood vessels from damage and maintaining their elasticity, consequently supporting healthy blood pressure levels. Furthermore, ginkgo biloba tea inhibits platelet-activating factors, preventing excessive blood clotting and ensuring proper blood flow, contributing to the stabilization of low blood pressure. Additionally, this tea stimulates the release of nitric oxide, a natural vasodilator, promoting dilation of blood vessels and raising blood pressure to desired levels.

Ultimately, regular consumption of ginkgo biloba tea proves an effective complementary approach for individuals experiencing low blood pressure symptoms.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in ginkgo biloba tea that help with low blood pressure.

  • Leaves: The leaves of the ginkgo biloba plant contain high levels of flavonoids and terpenoids, which are antioxidants that help dilate blood vessels and improve blood flow, making them effective for reducing high blood pressure.
  • Seeds: Ginkgo biloba seeds contain substances that help relax blood vessels and increase blood flow, making them useful for reducing high blood pressure.
  • Rhyzomes: The rhyzomes of the ginkgo biloba plant contain active compounds that help improve circulation and lower blood pressure by dilating blood vessels and reducing the stickiness of platelets.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginkgo biloba tea for low blood pressure.

  1. Place 2 teaspoons of dried ginkgo biloba leaves in a tea infuser or loose in a teapot.
  2. Pour 8 ounces of boiling water over the leaves and let steep for 5-10 minutes.
  3. Cover to keep the heat in and allow the tea to brew.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strain the leaves from the tea.
  5. Drink the tea, up to three times a day, to help manage low blood pressure.

Ginseng tea benefits individuals with low blood pressure due to its ability to stimulate the nervous system, which promotes the release of hormones that constrict blood vessels, leading to increased blood pressure.

This adaptogenic herb contains active compounds like ginsenosides, which exhibit vasoconstrictive properties, further supporting its blood pressure-raising effects. By improving physical and mental performance, ginseng tea helps combat fatigue often associated with low blood pressure, offering an energy boost that contributes to overall well-being. Additionally, this tea's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory qualities support vascular health, thereby aiding in the maintenance of optimal blood pressure levels.

Lastly, ginseng tea's capacity to regulate stress hormones provides further stabilization for individuals experiencing fluctuations in blood pressure.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in ginseng tea that help with low blood pressure.

  • Roots: The roots of ginseng tea plants are used in medicinal tea for low blood pressure due to their adaptogenic properties that help balance and stabilize the body's systems.
  • Rhizomes: Ginseng tea rhizomes contain ginsenosides, which are compounds that have been shown to lower blood pressure and improve circulation.
  • Leaves: The leaves of ginseng tea plants are rich in antioxidants and have a calming effect on the body, helping to reduce stress and lower blood pressure.
  • Flowers: Ginseng tea flowers contain flavonoids, which have been shown to have a positive effect on cardiovascular health and can help regulate blood pressure.
  • Seeds: The seeds of ginseng tea plants contain essential oils and active compounds that have a sedative effect on the body, helping to reduce tension and lower blood pressure.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginseng tea for low blood pressure.

  1. In a small pot, bring 4 cups of water to a boil, then add 2 tablespoons of dried ginseng root.
  2. Reduce heat to low, cover and let it simmer for 20 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the ginseng pieces, then stir in honey to taste (optional).
  4. Warm up the tea again if it has cooled, aiming for a drinkable temperature.
  5. Sip the ginseng tea slowly to help maintain a steady blood pressure.

4. Goldenseal

Goldenseal tea contains hydrastine and berberine alkaloids, which possess vasoconstrictive properties, causing blood vessels to narrow and increasing blood pressure.

These alkaloids also stimulate the heart, enhancing its contractions and improving overall cardiovascular function. Moreover, goldenseal tea's ability to tighten mucous membranes helps prevent excessive blood vessel dilation, thus maintaining healthy blood pressure levels. Additionally, goldenseal tea's isoquinoline alkaloids inhibit the breakdown of acetylcholine, a neurotransmitter that triggers muscle contractions, including those in blood vessel walls, further promoting normal blood pressure.

Ultimately, goldenseal tea's unique biochemical profile makes it a valuable aid in managing low blood pressure.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in goldenseal tea that help with low blood pressure.

  • Roots: Goldenseal tea made from roots contains isoquinoline alkaloids, which are known to constrict blood vessels and increase blood pressure.
  • Rhizomes: The rhizomes of goldenseal plant are rich in berberine, an alkaloid that exhibits positive inotropic and chronotropic effects on the heart.
  • Leaves: Goldenseal leaves are used in tea to stimulate the circulatory system, promoting healthy blood pressure levels.
  • Flowers: The flowers of goldenseal plant contain hydrastine, which is a vasoconstrictor, making it useful for managing low blood pressure.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic goldenseal tea for low blood pressure.

  1. In a sterilized pot, combine 1 cup of distilled water and 2 teaspoons of dried goldenseal root.
  2. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove goldenseal root pieces.
  4. Allow the tea to cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Drink 1 cup, three times a day, for low blood pressure management.

5. Yarrow

Yarrow tea aids in addressing low blood pressure by stimulating the cardiovascular system, as its volatile oils, including alpha-pinene and sabinene, promote blood flow to the heart.

This herbal tea contains lactones, which help to dilate peripheral blood vessels, increasing the volume of circulating blood. Furthermore, yarrow's flavonoids strengthen and protect the walls of blood vessels, contributing to more efficient blood circulation. Additionally, its high potassium content helps to maintain healthy blood pressure levels, counteracting the effects of sodium in the body.

Consequently, drinking yarrow tea supports a stable and balanced blood pressure.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in yarrow tea that help with low blood pressure.

  • Flowers: Yarrow tea made from flowers is known to have vasoconstrictive properties, helping to increase blood pressure in hypotensive individuals.
  • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants and flavonoids, yarrow leaves produce a medicinal tea that strengthens capillary walls and assists in regulating low blood pressure.
  • Stems: Yarrow stems contain lactones and bitter glycosides, which, when consumed as a tea, promote circulation and help balance low blood pressure levels.
  • Seeds: Yarrow seeds, when used in tea, are a potent source of essential oils and plant sterols, working together to naturally raise and stabilize low blood pressure.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic yarrow tea for low blood pressure.

  1. Harvest two teaspoons of fresh yarrow flowers and leaves or use one teaspoon of dried yarrow.
  2. Rinse the harvested yarrow to remove any dirt or impurities.
  3. Crush the yarrow gently to release its essential oils and improve extraction.
  4. Steep the crushed yarrow in hot water (not boiling) for 5-10 minutes.
  5. Strain the mixture and enjoy your yarrow tea, preferably between meals, up to three times a day.

6. Mistletoe

Mistletoe tea contains compounds that stimulate the heart, increasing its contractions and ultimately raising blood pressure.

Specifically, it includes tyramine, a compound that causes blood vessels to constrict, thus elevating blood pressure. Additionally, mistletoe tea has been shown to boost the adrenal glands' activity, leading to a surge in levels of the hormone adrenaline, which constricts blood vessels and raises blood pressure. Furthermore, this tea's ability to improve blood circulation also contributes to its capacity to restore healthy blood pressure levels.

Ultimately, mistletoe tea's complex interplay with the cardiovascular system and adrenal glands makes it a valuable tool for addressing low blood pressure.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in mistletoe tea that help with low blood pressure.

  • Stems: The stems of the mistletoe tea plant contain high levels of active compounds that help constrict blood vessels, which raises blood pressure.
  • Leaves: The leaves of the mistletoe tea plant are rich in tannins and flavonoids that have a vasoconstrictive effect, making them ideal for managing low blood pressure.
  • Buds: Mistletoe tea made from buds is particularly beneficial for low blood pressure as they are packed with antioxidants that promote better blood flow and help regulate blood pressure.
  • Bark: The bark of the mistletoe tea plant contains essential compounds like viscoside and martinidine, which have a positive impact on blood pressure, making it ideal for treating low blood pressure.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic mistletoe tea for low blood pressure.

  1. Gather 2-3 fresh mistletoe sprigs, ensuring they are pesticide-free, and rinse them under cold water to clean.
  2. Chop the mistletoe sprigs into small pieces, then place them in a pot and add 4 cups of water.
  3. Slowly bring the water to a boil, then reduce heat and let the mixture simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea through a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth, discarding the solids, and allow it to cool down for a few minutes.
  5. Pour the mistletoe tea into a cup, sweetening it with honey if desired, and drink 1-2 times a day for low blood pressure management.

8. Lemon Balm

Lemon balm tea aids in alleviating low blood pressure due to its rosmarinic acid content, which possesses vasodilatory properties.

Moreover, lemon balm contains eugenol, a compound that stimulates the heart, further assisting in the regulation of blood pressure. Additionally, lemon balm tea inhibits acetylcholinesterase, an enzyme that breaks down a neurotransmitter responsible for narrowing blood vessels, causing blood pressure to rise.

Consequently, drinking lemon balm tea benefits those with low blood pressure by promoting blood vessel dilation and heart stimulation, leading to increased blood pressure.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in lemon balm tea that help with low blood pressure.

  • Leaves: Rich in rosmarinic acid, lemon balm leaves are known to help relax blood vessels, promoting healthy blood pressure.
  • Flowers: Packed with antioxidants, lemon balm flowers support cardiovascular health, aiding in the maintenance of normal blood pressure.
  • Stems: Containing essential oils, lemon balm stems aid in the relaxation of smooth muscles in blood vessels, contributing to balanced blood pressure.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic lemon balm tea for low blood pressure.

  1. Harvest 2 tablespoons of fresh lemon balm leaves, or use 1 tablespoon of dried leaves, and rinse them thoroughly.
  2. Place the lemon balm leaves in a tea infuser or teapot and pour 1 cup of boiling water over the leaves.
  3. Allow the tea to steep for 5-10 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds and achieve a strong flavor.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strain the leaves from the teapot, discarding the used lemon balm.
  5. Serve the lemon balm tea warm and enjoy it 2-3 times a day to help manage low blood pressure.

10. Valerian

Valerian tea aids in alleviating low blood pressure due to its valepotriates content, which possesses vasodilatory properties.

These compounds relax the smooth muscles in blood vessel walls, causing them to widen. As a result, blood flow increases, and so does the supply of oxygen and nutrients to bodily tissues. Simultaneously, valerian tea's calming effects on the nervous system help reduce stress and anxiety, both of which can contribute to fluctuations in blood pressure.

Ultimately, consuming valerian tea promotes balanced blood pressure levels by improving circulation and soothing the nervous system.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in valerian tea that help with low blood pressure.

  • Roots: Rich in valerenic acid, which helps relax blood vessels, thus reducing blood pressure.
  • Rhizomes: Contain high levels of antioxidants that aid in managing hypertension and promoting cardiovascular health.
  • Leaves: High in phenolic compounds, which have been shown to help lower blood pressure by promoting relaxation of arterial walls.
  • Flowers: Contain essential oils and flavonoids, working together to dilate blood vessels and, as a result, decrease blood pressure.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic valerian tea for low blood pressure.

  1. In a teapot, add 1 teaspoon of dried valerian root to 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Steep the valerian root in hot water for 10-15 minutes to allow the active compounds to infuse.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the valerian root, leaving behind only the liquid.
  4. If desired, add honey or lemon to enhance the taste.
  5. Consume the tea while it's still warm, 2-3 times a day, to help manage low blood pressure.
