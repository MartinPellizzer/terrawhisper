10 best herbal teas for cirrhosis

Cirrhosis is a serious liver condition that can benefit from gentle, supportive treatments.

While herbal teas should not be used as a substitute for medical treatment, some may help promote liver health and alleviate symptoms. Milk thistle tea is well-known for its potential to protect and repair liver cells due to its active compound, silymarin.

Dandelion tea is another popular choice for liver support, as it may help improve bile production and flow. Lastly, licorice root tea has been suggested to provide anti-inflammatory and antioxidant benefits for the liver.

However, it is crucial to consult with a healthcare professional before incorporating these teas into a cirrhosis treatment plan, as individual responses may vary.

1. Milk Thistle

Milk thistle tea assists in combating cirrhosis due to its active compound, silibinin, which possesses a proven ability to protect liver cells.

This protection results from silibinin's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, countering oxidative stress and reducing tissue damage. Additionally, milk thistle demonstrates regenerative capabilities, encouraging regrowth of liver tissue, thereby aiding in the recovery of liver function. Moreover, it boasts antifibrotic effects, effectively diminishing the buildup of scar tissue, a common complication in cirrhosis.

Ultimately, these mechanisms of action contribute to milk thistle tea's reputation as a valuable aid in cirrhosis treatment.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in milk thistle tea that help with cirrhosis.

  • Seeds: The seeds of milk thistle plant are commonly used to make medicinal tea for cirrhosis. They contain silymarin, a flavonoid mixture with antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that protect the liver cells.
  • Leaves: Unlike stems, leaves of milk thistle have healing properties too. They are used to make tea as they have flavonoids and fatty acids beneficial for liver health.
  • Rhyzomes: Unlike roots, rhyzomes of milk thistle are used in teas to treat liver ailments such as cirrhosis, thanks to their protective and regenerative properties for liver cells.
