10 best herbal teas for enlarged spleen

While herbal teas are not a cure for an enlarged spleen, certain varieties may help reduce inflammation and boost the immune system, which can be beneficial in managing the condition.

Dandelion root tea is known for its ability to support liver function, thereby aiding the body in filtering toxins and reducing the workload on the spleen. Milk thistle tea shares similar liver-supporting properties and is also rich in antioxidants. Furthermore, ginger tea has anti-inflammatory effects that may help alleviate discomfort and reduce swelling in the spleen.

As with any health concern, it's essential to consult a healthcare professional before incorporating herbal remedies into your treatment plan.

1. Milk Thistle

Milk thistle tea benefits individuals with an enlarged spleen because it contains silymarin, a potent active compound known to possess medicinal properties that protect and regenerate liver cells.

Moreover, this compound holds anti-inflammatory and antioxidant characteristics, which contribute to diminishing spleen size by alleviating stress on the organ. Additionally, milk thistle tea facilitates the removal of toxins from the body, thereby decreasing the burden on the spleen.

Consequently, milk thistle tea demonstrably helps reduce spleen size and improve overall health.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in milk thistle tea that help with enlarged spleen.

  • Seeds: The seeds of milk thistle contain silymarin, a flavonoid compound that possesses potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, making them effective in treating an enlarged spleen.
  • Leaves: Milk thistle leaves are a rich source of vitamins and minerals, and they have been used in traditional medicine to promote liver function and reduce spleen size.
  • Flowers: The flowers of milk thistle are known for their healing properties and are often used in medicinal teas to support healthy liver and spleen function, reducing the risk of spleen enlargement.
  • Stems: Milk thistle stems contain bioactive compounds that help protect the liver and improve its function, which in turn helps reduce the size of an enlarged spleen.
  • Roots: The roots of milk thistle are a storehouse of medicinal compounds that have been shown to improve liver function, reduce inflammation, and promote the health of the spleen.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic milk thistle tea for enlarged spleen.

  1. Measure out 1-2 teaspoons of dried milk thistle seeds and place them in a tea infuser or loose leaf tea bag.
  2. Boil 8 ounces of water and let it cool for a minute or two to ensure it's not too hot, as very hot water can damage the delicate compounds in the seeds.
  3. Place the tea infuser or loose leaf tea bag with the milk thistle seeds in a cup or teapot and pour the hot water over it.
  4. Allow the tea to steep for 5-10 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds and create a strong infusion.
  5. Remove the tea infuser or tea bag and enjoy the milk thistle tea, drinking 2-3 cups daily to support spleen health.

2. Dandelion

Dandelion tea promotes spleen health due to its therapeutic properties.

This herbal tea contains taraxacum, a compound known to stimulate bile production in the liver, which aids in the elimination of toxins. The increased bile flow supports the spleen's role in filtering blood and removing waste. Moreover, dandelion tea provides antioxidants that protect the spleen from oxidative stress and damage.

Additionally, its anti-inflammatory properties help reduce inflammation in the spleen, alleviating discomfort associated with enlargement.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in dandelion tea that help with enlarged spleen.

  • Roots: Dandelion roots are used in medicinal tea for enlarged spleen due to their ability to promote the production of bile in the liver, which aids in digestion and elimination of toxins.
  • Leaves: Dandelion leaves are used in medicinal tea for enlarged spleen as they are a natural diuretic, helping to remove excess fluids from the body.
  • Flowers: Dandelion flowers are used in medicinal tea for enlarged spleen because they contain antioxidants that reduce inflammation and support the immune system.
  • Buds: Dandelion buds are used in medicinal tea for enlarged spleen as they are rich in vitamins and minerals, particularly vitamin A and C, which help to boost the immune system.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic dandelion tea for enlarged spleen.

  1. Gather 2-3 handfuls of fresh dandelion flowers from a chemical-free environment.
  2. Rinse the flowers thoroughly, then let them dry for a few hours or pat them down with a clean cloth.
  3. Chop the dried dandelion flowers and steep them in 4 cups of boiling water for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea to remove the flower pieces.
  5. Consume 3 cups of the dandelion tea daily, ideally before meals, for a minimum of two weeks for spleen support.

3. Burdock

Burdock tea benefits the spleen by supporting its natural ability to filter and detoxify the body.

This tea's powerful diuretic properties help eliminate excess fluids, reducing the strain on the spleen. Burdock's anti-inflammatory compounds alleviate inflammation in the spleen, promoting overall organ health.

Additionally, burdock tea stimulates the production of white blood cells, enhancing the immune system's response and further contributing to a balanced spleen function.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in burdock tea that help with enlarged spleen.

  • Roots: Burdock roots contain beneficial compounds that can help cleanse the blood and reduce congestion in the spleen.
  • Rhizomes: Burdock rhizomes have anti-inflammatory properties that can help alleviate symptoms of an enlarged spleen.
  • Leaves: Burdock leaves are rich in nutrients and antioxidants that support the overall health of the spleen.
  • Seeds: Burdock seeds contain oil that can help stimulate the production of bile, which aids in the digestion and elimination of toxins from the spleen.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic burdock tea for enlarged spleen.

  1. Chop 2 ounces of dried burdock root into small pieces and rinse with water.
  2. In a pot, combine the burdock root with 4 cups of water and bring to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 20 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea to remove the burdock root pieces.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, 2-3 times a day for enlarged spleen relief.

6. Turmeric

Turmeric tea provides substantial help for an enlarged spleen due to its active compound, curcumin, which has anti-inflammatory properties.

This compound decreases inflammation in the spleen, subsequently reducing its size. Furthermore, curcumin's immunomodulatory effects strengthen the body's defense mechanisms, contributing to the organ's recovery.

Ultimately, drinking turmeric tea aids in alleviating symptoms associated with an enlarged spleen, providing noticeable relief and promoting overall well-being.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in turmeric tea that help with enlarged spleen.

  • Rhizomes: The underground stem of turmeric contains curcumin, a compound with potent anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce spleen size.
  • Roots: The turmeric roots, often used interchangeably with rhizomes, have rich antioxidant properties that aid in treating an enlarged spleen.
  • Leaves: Turmeric leaves possess anti-inflammatory and healing properties that are beneficial in managing an enlarged spleen.
  • Flowers: Turmeric flowers contain curcumin and other bioactive compounds, making them suitable for medicinal tea to treat splenomegaly.
  • Seeds: Though not commonly used, turmeric seeds hold beneficial properties similar to the rhizomes, helping reduce inflammation in the spleen.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic turmeric tea for enlarged spleen.

  1. In a saucepan, combine 4 cups of water and 1 teaspoon of grated fresh turmeric (or 1/2 teaspoon of ground turmeric).
  2. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 10 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea through a fine-mesh sieve into a cup, pressing on the turmeric to extract all liquid.
  4. Stir in honey and lemon juice to taste, if desired.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, up to three times a day.

Licorice tea aids in reducing spleen size due to its active compounds, namely glycyrrhizin and flavonoids.

Furthermore, licorice tea enhances adrenal gland function, resulting in increased production of cortisol, a hormone that regulates the body's response to stress and inflammation.

Thus, licorice tea offers a natural and effective solution for managing an enlarged spleen.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in licorice tea that help with enlarged spleen.

  • Seeds: High in antioxidants, licorice seeds aid in eliminating toxins, providing relief to the spleen.

  • Bark: The bark of the licorice plant has been used to make medicinal tea for enlarged spleen due to its high saponin content, which fights inflammation.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic licorice tea for enlarged spleen.

  1. In a pot, boil 2 cups of water for 5 minutes.
  2. Add 2 tablespoons of dried licorice root, then reduce heat and simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the licorice root, then let it cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  4. Optionally, add honey to taste for sweetness.
  5. Drink 2-3 times a day, consistently, for enlarged spleen relief.

Sarsaparilla tea benefits individuals with an enlarged spleen due to its anti-inflammatory and diuretic properties.

The tea's active compounds, such as sarsasapogenin and smilagenin, possess the ability to reduce inflammation in the body, thereby alleviating stress on the spleen. Its detoxifying qualities further enhance the body's overall immune function and organ efficiency.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in sarsaparilla tea that help with enlarged spleen.

  • Roots: The roots of sarsaparilla contain compounds that are believed to have anti-inflammatory properties, making them useful for treating an enlarged spleen.
  • Rhizomes: Rhizomes are underground stems that contain similar medicinal properties to roots, making them suitable for use in medicinal tea for an enlarged spleen.
  • Leaves: The leaves of sarsaparilla contain active compounds that can help reduce inflammation, making them a useful component in medicinal tea for treating an enlarged spleen.
  • Bark: The bark of sarsaparilla contains compounds that can help stimulate the immune system and reduce inflammation, making it a valuable addition to medicinal tea for an enlarged spleen.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic sarsaparilla tea for enlarged spleen.

  1. In a pot, combine 1 quart of water and 2 tablespoons of dried sarsaparilla root; bring to a boil, then lower heat and simmer for 30 minutes.
  2. Remove pot from heat and strain the tea, discarding the solid sarsaparilla residue; allow the tea to cool to a drinkable temperature.
  3. Stir in honey or sugar to taste, if desired, and mix well to dissolve.
  4. Pour the tea into a glass or mug and optionally garnish with a lemon slice or mint leaves.
  5. Drink the sarsaparilla tea 2-3 times a day, ideally after meals, for several weeks to help treat an enlarged spleen.
