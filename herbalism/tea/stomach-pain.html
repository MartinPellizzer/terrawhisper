1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with stomach pain because it has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the stomach lining, thereby alleviating pain.

It contains compounds like azulene and flavonoids that work to relax the smooth muscles of the intestinal tract, which can help to ease stomach cramps and spasms. Chamomile tea is also known for its calming effects, which can help to reduce stress and anxiety, common triggers of stomach pain. Furthermore, it has been shown to promote proper digestion by increasing the production of bile and digestive enzymes, preventing the buildup of gas and food in the stomach that can cause discomfort.

Lastly, chamomile tea is a gentle and natural remedy for stomach pain, making it a suitable option for people of all ages, including children and pregnant women.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in chamomile tea that help with stomach pain.

  1. Apigenin: This flavonoid has antispasmodic properties, helping to relax smooth muscles in the stomach, reducing pain.
  2. Bisabolol: This terpene has anti-inflammatory effects, helping to decrease inflammation and irritation in the stomach lining.
  3. Chamazulene: This chemical compound has anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, helping to reduce inflammation and relax stomach muscles, thus alleviating pain.
  4. Matricin: This sesquiterpene is converted to chamazulene during steam distillation, and it has anti-inflammatory and antiphlogistic properties, helping to soothe irritated stomach tissues.
  5. Alpha-bisabolol oxide A & B: These oxides derived from bisabolol have anti-inflammatory effects, assisting in reducing inflammation-induced stomach pain and discomfort.

The following list reveals what parts of chamomile are most commonly used to make medicinal tea for stomach pain.

  1. Flowers: Chamomile flowers are used for medicinal tea as they contain constituents that have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, which can help soothe and relax the stomach muscles, thus alleviating pain.
  2. Leaves: Chamomile leaves are often included in medicinal teas for their calming effects that can help reduce stress-related stomach discomfort.
  3. Seeds: Chamomile seeds are known for their carminative properties that help expel gas from the stomach and alleviate bloating, thus providing relief from stomach pain.

The following procedure explains how to make chamomile tea for stomach pain.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a kettle or pot for 3-5 minutes.
  2. Pour the hot water over 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers in a teapot or cup.
  3. Cover and let the chamomile flowers steep for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Sip the chamomile tea slowly, allowing it to soothe your stomach pain.

  3. Limonene: This compound has carminative properties, which means it aids in the expulsion of gas from the stomach and alleviates pain caused by bloating.
  4. Methyl acetate: It has analgesic and anti-inflammatory effects that help manage pain and inflammation in the gastrointestinal tract, alleviating stomach pain.
  5. 1,8-Cineole: This compound has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects, and it helps with stomach pain by eliminating harmful bacteria and reducing inflammation in the gastrointestinal tract.
  6. Rosmarinic acid: It has antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and analgesic properties that help reduce oxidative stress, inflammation, and pain in the stomach, providing relief from stomach pain.

The following list reveals what parts of peppermint are most commonly used to make medicinal tea for stomach pain.

  1. Leaves: Peppermint leaves are commonly used as they contain menthol, a compound known to provide relief from stomach pain and discomfort.
  2. Stems: The peppermint stems are also used in medicinal tea as they contain therapeutic compounds that help reduce inflammation and spasms in the digestive tract.
  3. Flowers: Peppermint flowers are included in medicinal tea for stomach pain because they have calming properties that help soothe the gastrointestinal system.

The following procedure explains how to make peppermint tea for stomach pain.

  1. In a teapot, combine 1 teaspoon of dried peppermint leaves with 1 cup of boiling water.

  3. Strain the tea to remove the leaves, ensuring a smooth drink.

  1. Gingerols: Gingerols have anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce stomach inflammation and pain.
  2. Shogaols: Shogaols can help relax the smooth muscle of the gastrointestinal tract, alleviating stomach cramps and pain.
  3. Paradols: Paradols have been shown to help reduce nausea and vomiting, common symptoms associated with stomach pain.
  4. Zingerone: Zingerone can help increase gastric motility, aiding in digestion and reducing stomach discomfort.
  5. Gingerdiols: Gingerdiols have been found to possess analgesic properties, helping to alleviate stomach pain.

The following list reveals what parts of ginger are most commonly used to make medicinal tea for stomach pain.

  1. Rhizomes: The underground stem of ginger, known as rhizomes, is most commonly used to make medicinal tea for stomach pain due to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
  2. Roots: Though technically part of the rhizome, ginger "roots" are often used as a synonym and are rich in bioactive compounds like gingerols and shogaols, which can help alleviate digestive issues and reduce inflammation.

The following procedure explains how to make ginger tea for stomach pain.

  2. Add the juice of half a lemon and 1-2 teaspoons of honey to the ginger water, stir well.
  3. Warm up the mixture on low heat if necessary, but avoid boiling.
  4. Pour the ginger tea into a cup and sip slowly to soothe your stomach pain.
