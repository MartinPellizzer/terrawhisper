10 best herbal teas for stomach pain

If you're experiencing stomach pain, certain herbal teas can offer relief by reducing inflammation, soothing digestion, and alleviating cramps.

Peppermint tea is well-known for its calming effects on the gastrointestinal tract, making it an excellent choice for easing stomach discomfort. Ginger tea is another popular option, as it contains anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the stomach lining and alleviate pain.

Chamomile tea has long been used as a natural remedy for various ailments, including stomach pain, due to its soothing and antispasmodic qualities, which can help relax the digestive muscles and decrease tension. Lastly, licorice root tea may be beneficial for treating stomach pain caused by gastric ulcers, as it promotes the healing of the stomach lining and reduces inflammation.

However, it is important to consult your healthcare provider before consuming licorice root tea, as excessive intake can lead to side effects such as high blood pressure and potassium depletion.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea contains antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory compounds, such as chamazulene and flavonoids, which directly target the gastrointestinal tract.

These properties help relax the smooth muscles in the stomach, reducing spasms and inflammation that cause pain. Furthermore, chamomile's calming effects extend to the nervous system, alleviating stress-related stomach issues. Additionally, chamomile's mild astringent qualities can help with leakage from the gastrointestinal lining, commonly seen in conditions like acid reflux.

Lastly, chamomile tea promotes the production of mucin, a glycoprotein that forms a protective layer in the stomach, preventing direct acid contact with the stomach lining and thereby reducing pain.

  • Seeds: Chamomile seeds are a powerhouse of essential oils and therapeutic properties, easing gastrointestinal spasms and providing quick relief for stomach pain.
