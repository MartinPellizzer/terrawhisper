10 best herbal teas for gas in stomach

Certain herbal teas, such as chamomile, ginger, and peppermint, are well-known for their soothing properties that can help alleviate symptoms of gas and bloating in the stomach.

These herbal teas work by promoting the production of digestive enzymes, reducing inflammation, and relaxing the gastrointestinal muscles, thus reducing the discomfort caused by gas buildup.

Enjoying a cup of these herbal teas after a meal can help promote healthy digestion and provide relief from uncomfortable gas in the stomach.

1. Peppermint

Peppermint tea aids in digestion due to its active ingredient, menthol, which possesses antispasmodic properties.

This compound relaxes the gastrointestinal muscles, allowing gas to move freely through the digestive tract and reducing the feeling of discomfort. Furthermore, peppermint oil in the tea stimulates the production of bile in the stomach, promoting better breakdown of fats and minimizing gas formation. Additionally, peppermint tea's soothing effect on the stomach lining decreases inflammation, contributing to overall gas reduction.

Lastly, peppermint tea's carminative effect, or its ability to prevent the formation of gas in the gastrointestinal tract, makes it an ideal natural remedy for gas relief.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in peppermint tea that help with gas in stomach.

  • Leaves: Peppermint leaves are rich in menthol, a compound that relaxes gastrointestinal muscles, helping to alleviate gas and bloating.
  • Rhyzomes: These underground stems contain rosmarinic acid, which reduces inflammation in the digestive tract, providing relief from gas and other stomach discomforts.
  • Flowers: Packed with antioxidants and volatile oils, peppermint flowers soothe irritation in the stomach, helping to expel gas and improve digestion.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic peppermint tea for gas in stomach.

  1. In a teapot, add boiling water (1 cup) to dried peppermint leaves (1 teaspoon).
  2. Steep the peppermint leaves in hot water for 5-10 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the leaves, leaving only the liquid.
  4. Optional: Add honey (1 teaspoon) to sweeten the tea, if desired.
  5. Drink the peppermint tea while warm, up to three times a day between meals for gas relief.

2. Ginger

Ginger tea aids in digestion due to its carminative properties, which move gas from the intestines to the stomach and allow it to be released.

Gingerol, a compound in ginger, activates digestive enzymes in the stomach, speeding up the breakdown of food and preventing the creation of excess gas. Additionally, ginger tea's antispasmodic qualities alleviate abdominal pain and discomfort by relaxing the intestinal muscles and reducing spasms. The soothing warmth of the tea further helps to ease stomach tension and encourages the smooth flow of digestion.

Overall, ginger tea offers a natural, effective solution for managing gas in the stomach.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in ginger tea that help with gas in stomach.

  • Rhizomes: The underground stem of the ginger plant, which is rich in gingerols and shogaols, compounds known to aid in digestion and alleviate symptoms of gas and bloating.
  • Roots: The horizontal, underground stem (rhizome) of the ginger plant, which contains natural oils and phenol compounds that help relax the intestinal tract and reduce the accumulation of gas.
  • Leaves: The thin, green, strap-like structures above ground, which contain polyphenolic compounds and volatile oils that have been found to aid in reducing gas and inflammation in the stomach.
  • Seeds: The small, oval-shaped fruits of the ginger plant, which are rich in essential oils and oleoresins, and have been used in traditional medicine to help treat digestive ailments such as gas and bloating.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginger tea for gas in stomach.

  1. Peel and chop 1 inch of fresh ginger into small slices, then place in a teapot.
  2. Add 4 cups of boiling water to the teapot and let it steep for 10 minutes.
  3. Strain the ginger pieces from the tea using a fine-mesh strainer.
  4. Add 1-2 teaspoons of honey and 1/2 lemon juice to the ginger tea, stir until well combined.
  5. Serve immediately and enjoy your homemade ginger tea for gas relief.

3. Chamomile

Chamomile tea contains antispasmodic and carminative properties that directly address the root causes of gas in the stomach.

These properties relax the smooth muscles in the stomach, reducing its contractions and the formation of gas. Additionally, chamomile tea's volatile oils stimulate the release of gastric juices, promoting proper digestion and preventing the buildup of gas. The soothing effect of chamomile tea on the stomach lining further contributes to gas reduction.

Furthermore, its anti-inflammatory properties alleviate any possible irritation or inflammation in the gastrointestinal tract, thereby preventing gas production.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in chamomile tea that help with gas in stomach.

  • Flowers: The chamomile flowers contain flavonoids and essential oils that have anti-spasmodic properties, which help relax the smooth muscles in the stomach, providing relief from gas and bloating.
  • Leaves: The leaves of chamomile contain anti-inflammatory compounds that soothe the digestive tract, reducing inflammation and alleviating the symptoms of gas and stomach discomfort.
  • Seeds: Chamomile seeds are rich in anodyne compounds that help alleviate pain and relax the gastrointestinal muscles, providing relief from gas, cramps, and stomach spasms.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for gas in stomach.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water to 212°F for 1-2 minutes.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers to a tea infuser or teapot.
  3. Pour the hot water over the chamomile flowers and let steep for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strain the chamomile flowers from the teapot.
  5. Drink the chamomile tea, up to three times a day, for gas relief.

  5. Sip: Drink the tea slowly, enjoying the mildly sweet and slightly spiced flavor while allowing the tea to soothe your digestive system and relieve gas pains.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in dandelion tea that help with gas in stomach.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic dandelion tea for gas in stomach.

  1. Gather 2-3 dandelion heads and rinse them thoroughly under running water to remove dirt and insects.
  2. Chop the dandelion heads into small pieces and place them in a pot, then add 2 cups of water and bring to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes to allow the active compounds to be extracted.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the solids, and let it cool down to a drinkable temperature.

10. Senna

Senna tea contains anthraquinone glycosides, which have a well-established reputation for their ability to stimulate the intestines and colon, thereby promoting bowel movements.

These glycosides reach the large intestine, where gut bacteria convert them into active compounds that increase muscle contractions, efficiently pushing gas and stool out. Furthermore, senna tea's natural laxative effect helps in softening the stool, making it easier for the gas to be released. Consequently, the potent carminative properties of senna tea help alleviate symptoms of excessive gas, thus making it an effective and trusted digestive remedy.

By enhancing the motility of the gastrointestinal tract, senna tea reduces the likelihood of gas buildup, making it an essential ally for maintaining a healthy and comfortable digestive system.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in senna tea that help with gas in stomach.

  • Leaves: Senna leaves contain anthraquinones, which stimulate the intestines to promote bowel movements and alleviate gas in the stomach.
  • Pods: Senna pods, or fruits, are a rich source of fiber that aids in digestion, reducing gas buildup in the stomach.
  • Rhizomes: Senna rhizomes have laxative properties due to their anthraquinones content, helping to manage gas and stomach discomfort.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic senna tea for gas in stomach.

  1. Measure - Precisely measure out 1-2 teaspoons of dried senna leaves or pods.
  2. Steep - Place the senna herb in a tea infuser or loose in a teapot, then pour boiling water (8 ounces) over it.
  3. Steep (part 2) - Allow the tea to steep for 10-15 minutes to ensure the extraction of active components.
  4. Strain - Remove the tea infuser or strain the liquid to eliminate loose leaves before pouring into a cup.
  5. Consume - Drink the senna tea, preferably on an empty stomach, and wait for its natural laxative effects to help relieve gas and bloating.
