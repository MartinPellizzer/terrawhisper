1. Peppermint

Peppermint tea helps with gas in the stomach because it contains menthol, a natural compound that has antispasmodic properties, which can help relax the gastrointestinal muscles and reduce spasms that cause gas and bloating.

The soothing effect of peppermint tea on the stomach lining can also increase the speed at which food moves through the stomach, preventing the buildup of gas. Moreover, peppermint oil, an active ingredient in peppermint tea, has been shown to improve symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome (IBS), a common cause of stomach gas and bloating. However, it is important to note that while peppermint tea can provide relief for occasional gas and bloating, it may not be effective for everyone, especially those with gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD) or a hiatal hernia, as it can exacerbate symptoms.

Lastly, it is always best to consult with a healthcare provider before using peppermint tea or any other natural remedies for stomach issues.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in peppermint tea that help with gas in stomach.

  1. Menthol: The main active ingredient in peppermint, menthol has antispasmodic properties that help relax the gastrointestinal tract, thereby alleviating symptoms of gas and bloating.
  2. Menthone: Another key component of peppermint, menthone also has antispasmodic properties that can help reduce gas and associated discomfort in the stomach.
  3. Limonene: Found in smaller quantities in peppermint, limonene has carminative properties that can help relieve symptoms of excess gas, including bloating and discomfort.
  4. Menthyl acetate: This compound, also found in peppermint, has been shown to have antifoaming properties, which can help reduce the formation of gas in the stomach and intestines.
  5. Pulegone: A minor constituent of peppermint, pulegone also has antispasmodic properties that can help alleviate symptoms of gas and bloating in the stomach.

The following list reveals what parts of peppermint are most commonly used to make medicinal tea for gas in stomach.

  1. Leaves: Peppermint leaves are commonly used in medicinal teas for gas in the stomach due to their calming and anti-spasmodic properties which can help soothe and relax the gastrointestinal tract.
  2. Stems: The stems of peppermint plants contain therapeutic oils, including menthol, which can help alleviate symptoms of gas and bloating by promoting the flow of bile and relaxing the gastrointestinal muscles.
  3. Flowers: Peppermint flowers are less commonly used than the leaves or stems, but they still contain therapeutic oils that can help reduce inflammation and spasms in the stomach, thereby alleviating gas and bloating.

The following procedure explains how to make peppermint tea for gas in stomach.

  1. In a tea infuser or teapot, place one peppermint tea bag or 1 tablespoon of loose peppermint leaves.
  2. Pour 8 ounces of boiling water over the peppermint tea.
  3. Allow the tea to steep for 5-10 minutes to extract the peppermint's essential oils.
  4. Remove the tea bag or strain the loose leaves from the tea.
  5. Slowly sip the tea, allowing it to gently relax your stomach muscles and help relieve gas.
