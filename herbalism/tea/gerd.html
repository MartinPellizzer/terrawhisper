10 best herbal teas for gerd

GERD, or gastroesophageal reflux disease, is a condition that causes acid reflux and heartburn.

Certain herbal teas can help soothe the symptoms of GERD. Chamomile tea is a popular choice due to its anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce inflammation in the esophagus. Ginger tea is another good option, as it can help reduce nausea and acid production in the stomach.

Licorice tea can also help coat the esophagus and stomach lining, providing relief from discomfort. However, it is important to note that licorice tea can interact with certain medications and should be used with caution. Marshmallow root tea is another herbal tea that can help soothe the digestive tract and reduce inflammation.

As with any herbal remedy, it's important to consult with a healthcare provider before adding herbal teas to your diet, especially if you are taking any medications or have a medical condition.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea provides relief from Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease (GERD) due to its anti-inflammatory properties, which reduce inflammation in the esophagus.

Chamomile tea contains compounds that stimulate the production of mucus, providing a protective barrier against stomach acid. Moreover, it has anti-spasmodic properties that help prevent stomach cramps and discomfort caused by GERD.

Lastly, chamomile tea promotes effective digestion, reducing the likelihood of acid backing up into the esophagus.
