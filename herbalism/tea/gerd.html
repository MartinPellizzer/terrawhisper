10 best herbal teas for gerd

GERD, or gastroesophageal reflux disease, is a condition that causes acid reflux and heartburn. Certain herbal teas can help soothe the symptoms of GERD. Chamomile tea is a popular choice due to its anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce inflammation in the esophagus. Ginger tea is another good option, as it can help reduce nausea and acid production in the stomach. Licorice tea can also help coat the esophagus and stomach lining, providing relief from discomfort. However, it is important to note that licorice tea can interact with certain medications and should be used with caution. Marshmallow root tea is another herbal tea that can help soothe the digestive tract and reduce inflammation. As with any herbal remedy, it's important to consult with a healthcare provider before adding herbal teas to your diet, especially if you are taking any medications or have a medical condition.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea provides relief from Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease (GERD) due to its anti-inflammatory properties, which reduce inflammation in the esophagus. This herbal tea, known for its soothing effects, relaxes the muscles in the stomach and esophagus, thus decreasing the risk of acid reflux. Chamomile tea contains compounds that stimulate the production of mucus, providing a protective barrier against stomach acid. Moreover, it has anti-spasmodic properties that help prevent stomach cramps and discomfort caused by GERD. Lastly, chamomile tea promotes effective digestion, reducing the likelihood of acid backing up into the esophagus.

2. Licorice

Licorice tea aids in relieving Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease (GERD) due to its demulcent properties, which involve a slimy substance that coats the esophageal lining. This coating protects the esophagus from stomach acid, reducing irritation and inflammation. Moreover, licorice tea contains glycyrrhizic acid, which increases the mucus production in the gastrointestinal tract, forming a fresh layer of mucus lining for added protection. Additionally, this tea has anti-inflammatory qualities that can help alleviate the discomfort caused by GERD. Lastly, licorice tea encourages the production of digestive fluids and mucus, facilitating smooth digestion and preventing the backflow of stomach acid into the esophagus.

3. Marshmallow

Marshmallow tea, derived from the marshmallow plant's roots, provides relief for Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease (GERD) due to its demulcent properties. This tea contains mucilage, a substance that transforms into a slippery gel when combined with water, forming a protective barrier over the gastrointestinal lining. By coating the esophageal and stomach lining, marshmallow tea prevents stomach acid from causing irritation and inflammation. Additionally, this soothing barrier encourages the healing of damaged tissue. Overall, marshmallow tea's ability to form a protective barrier and support tissue healing makes it an effective remedy for GERD symptoms.

4. Slippery Elm

Slippery elm tea helps with GERD due to the tree's inner bark, which contains mucilage, a substance that becomes a slick gel when mixed with water.
