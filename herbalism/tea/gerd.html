10 Best Herbal Teas For Gerd

Herbal teas, especially those made from chamomile, licorice root, marshmallow root, and slippery elm, can help alleviate Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease (GERD) symptoms.

These herbs have anti-inflammatory properties that can reduce inflammation in the esophagus and stomach lining, providing relief from acid reflux and heartburn. Additionally, some herbs, like licorice root, can increase mucus production, which protects the esophageal lining from stomach acid.

Furthermore, these teas can stimulate the production of digestive enzymes, helping to break down food more effectively and preventing the overproduction of stomach acid. The soothing effect of warm herbal teas can also help to relax the lower esophageal sphincter, preventing acid from backing up into the esophagus.

Lastly, herbal teas are caffeine-free and can be a healthier alternative to traditional teas or coffee, which can exacerbate GERD symptoms.

2. Licorice

Licorice tea helps with GERD because it contains substances called glycyrrhizic acids and flavonoids, which can help reduce inflammation and strengthen the mucosal lining of the esophagus.

This can provide relief from symptoms such as heartburn, acid reflux, and stomach discomfort. Additionally, licorice tea has been shown to increase the mucus secretion in the gastrointestinal tract, which can protect the stomach lining from acid and help heal ulcers. Furthermore, licorice tea may help to increase the production of mucus in the stomach, which can act as a barrier against stomach acid, thereby reducing the symptoms of GERD.

However, it is important to note that excessive consumption of licorice tea can lead to side effects such as high blood pressure and potassium loss, so it is recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before consuming licorice tea for GERD.

The following procedure explains how to make licorice tea for gerd.

  1. Gather 1 tablespoon of dried licorice root and place it in a tea infuser or teapot with a built-in strainer.
  2. Boil 8 ounces of water and pour it over the licorice root, allowing it to steep for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Optional: Add honey or lemon to taste for added flavor and potential soothing properties.
  4. Let the tea cool down to a comfortable drinking temperature.
  5. Enjoy the licorice tea, ideally sipping it slowly after meals to potentially help with GERD symptoms.

The following procedure explains how to make slippery elm tea for gerd.

  1. Ingredients: Slippery Elm Bark (preferably pre-cut and organic), 2 cups of water. Dosage: 1-2 tablespoons of slippery elm bark.
  2. Preparation Time: Pour 2 cups of water into a pot and bring it to a boil.
  3. Steeping Time: Add 1-2 tablespoons of slippery elm bark to the boiling water, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 2-3 minutes.
  4. Straining Time: Remove the pot from heat, cover and let it steep for 5-10 minutes, then strain the tea to remove the bark.
  5. Serving Time: Pour the tea into a cup, let it cool down to a drinkable temperature, and consume it up to three times a day as needed.
