10 Best Herbal Teas For Stomach Cramps

Herbal teas, particularly those made from chamomile, peppermint, and ginger, are known to provide relief from stomach cramps.

This is because these herbs have anti-spasmodic properties that help relax the muscles of the gastrointestinal tract, reducing muscle spasms and consequently, cramps. Additionally, these herbs have anti-inflammatory effects that can help reduce inflammation in the stomach, further alleviating discomfort.

Furthermore, herbal teas can help increase the production of digestive enzymes, promoting healthy digestion and reducing the likelihood of cramps. The soothing warmth of the tea can also help to relax the mind and body, reducing tension and stress that can exacerbate stomach cramps.

However, it's important to note that while herbal teas can be helpful, they should not replace medical treatment, especially for severe or persistent stomach cramps.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with stomach cramps because it has anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties that help relax the smooth muscles of the intestines, reducing inflammation and spasms that cause pain.

Its calming effects can also help alleviate stress and anxiety, which can exacerbate stomach cramps. Additionally, chamomile contains flavonoids, such as apigenin and luteolin, which have been shown to have antispasmodic and analgesic effects, further contributing to its stomach-soothing properties. Chamomile tea has been used as a traditional remedy for digestive issues for centuries, and its effectiveness is supported by some modern scientific studies.

However, it is important to note that while chamomile tea can be helpful for occasional stomach cramps, it is not a substitute for medical treatment for serious or chronic digestive conditions.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in chamomile tea that help with stomach cramps.

  1. Apigenin: A flavonoid that has antispasmodic properties, helping to relax smooth muscle tissue in the digestive tract and alleviate stomach cramps.
  2. Chamazulene: A chemical compound with anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce inflammation in the digestive tract and alleviate stomach cramps.
  3. Alpha-Bisabolol: A terpene with anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, helping to reduce inflammation and pain associated with stomach cramps.
  4. Matricin: A precursor to chamazulene, matricin also has anti-inflammatory properties, aiding in the reduction of inflammation in the digestive tract.
  5. Spiranes: A group of chemical compounds with antispasmodic properties that help relax the smooth muscle of the digestive tract and alleviate stomach cramps.

The following list reveals what parts of chamomile are most commonly used to make medicinal tea for stomach cramps.

  1. Flowers: Chamomile flowers are commonly used in medicinal teas for stomach cramps due to their anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.
  2. Leaves: The leaves of chamomile contain soothing agents that can help alleviate stomach cramps and promote digestive health.
  3. Seeds: Chamomile seeds are rich in therapeutic compounds, and when used in tea, they can help relax the muscles of the digestive tract, reducing stomach cramps.

The following procedure explains how to make chamomile tea for stomach cramps.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a kettle or pot for 3-5 minutes.
  2. Place 1 teaspoon of dried chamomile flowers in a tea infuser or teapot.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the chamomile flowers and let steep for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strain the tea to remove the flowers.
  5. Allow the tea to cool slightly before drinking to soothe stomach cramps. Optional: add honey or lemon to taste.

2. Ginger

Ginger tea helps with stomach cramps because it contains gingerol, a compound with anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties that help relax the muscles in the gut lining, reducing contractions and subsequently alleviating pain.

Furthermore, ginger has been shown to stimulate digestion, which can help move food through the gastrointestinal tract more efficiently, reducing the likelihood of cramping. Additionally, ginger tea can help regulate stomach acid production, preventing acid-related discomfort and potential cramping. Moreover, its soothing warmth may provide additional relief by increasing blood flow to the skin surface and relaxing tense abdominal muscles.

Lastly, ginger's natural calming effects can help reduce anxiety and stress, which are often linked to digestive issues including stomach cramps.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in ginger tea that help with stomach cramps.

  1. Gingerols: Gingerols, the most abundant compounds in ginger, have been shown to help relieve stomach cramps by relaxing the smooth muscle of the gastrointestinal tract, thus alleviating spasms and pain.
  2. Shogaols: Shogaols, formed from gingerols when ginger is dried or cooked, also contribute to ginger's antispasmodic properties, helping to soothe stomach cramps by further reducing intestinal contractions and inflammation.
  3. Paradols: Paradols, derived from gingerols and shogaols, exhibit anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects, which may help alleviate stomach cramps by reducing inflammation and pain in the gastrointestinal tract.
  4. Zingerone: Zingerone, a compound formed when ginger is cooked or dried, has been found to inhibit gastrointestinal smooth muscle contraction and acid secretion, providing relief from stomach cramps and discomfort.

The following list reveals what parts of ginger are most commonly used to make medicinal tea for stomach cramps.

  1. Rhizomes: The underground stem of ginger, known as rhizomes, is commonly used in medicinal tea due to its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties that can help alleviate stomach cramps.
  2. Roots: The ginger root contains bioactive compounds like gingerols and shogaols that can help relax the gastrointestinal muscles, providing relief from stomach cramps.
  3. Leaves: Fresh ginger leaves are used in tea to treat stomach cramps, as they contain essential oils that can help soothe the digestive system and reduce inflammation.
  4. Flowers: Ginger flowers are used in herbal teas for their calming effects on the stomach, as they contain anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties that can help alleviate stomach cramps.
  5. Seeds: Ginger seeds contain oils that can help stimulate digestion, making them a valuable addition to medicinal tea for treating stomach cramps.

The following procedure explains how to make ginger tea for stomach cramps.

  1. Peel and grate 1 inch of fresh ginger, let it steep in hot water for 10 minutes.
  2. Boil 2 cups of water, then add grated ginger to release its medicinal properties.
  3. Strain the ginger tea into a teapot or mug to remove ginger pieces.
  4. Add honey and lemon juice to taste for soothing and vitamin C benefits.
  5. Sip the warm ginger tea gradually to ease stomach cramps, letting it absorb completely.
