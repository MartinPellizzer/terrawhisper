10 best herbal teas for stomach cramps

Certain herbal teas can be effective in alleviating stomach cramps by promoting digestion, reducing inflammation, and soothing the gastrointestinal tract.

Peppermint tea is well-known for its antispasmodic properties, which help relax the muscles in the digestive tract and relieve pain. Ginger tea is another excellent choice, as it contains natural anti-inflammatory compounds that can reduce inflammation in the stomach lining and alleviate cramps.

Chamomile tea is a gentle relaxant, making it ideal for soothing upset stomachs and easing muscle spasms. Lastly, fennel tea can help stimulate the production of digestive enzymes, allowing for smoother digestion and reducing the likelihood of cramps.

However, it's important to consult with a healthcare professional if stomach cramps persist or are severe, as they can be a sign of a more serious condition.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea contains anti-inflammatory properties, specifically the terpene compounds alpha-bisabolol and matricin, which help reduce inflammation in the gastrointestinal tract.

These compounds work by relaxing the smooth muscle lining of the intestines, thereby alleviating contractions that cause stomach cramps. Chamomile tea also contains flavonoids, particularly apigenin, which exhibits antispasmodic effects, further contributing to the relaxation of the intestinal muscles. Moreover, chamomile tea's soothing warmth increases blood flow to the digestive system, facilitating the relief of abdominal discomfort.

Lastly, the mild sedative properties of chamomile tea help alleviate anxiety and promote relaxation, subsequently reducing stress-induced stomach cramps.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in chamomile tea that help with stomach cramps.

  • Flowers: Chamomile's daisy-like flowers contain bioactive chemicals, such as terpenoids and flavonoids, that possess anti-spasmodic properties, providing relief for stomach cramps.
  • Leaves: The leaves of chamomile contain tannins that have astringent properties, which can help reduce inflammation and alleviate stomach cramps.
  • Seeds: The seeds of chamomile are rich in essential oils, particularly the anti-spasmodic compound bisabolol, making them ideal for soothing stomach cramps.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for stomach cramps.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water and let it cool for 2-3 minutes until it reaches a temperature of around 200°F (93°C).
  2. Put 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers in a tea infuser or teapot.
  3. Pour the hot water over the chamomile flowers and let them steep for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strain the tea to discard the chamomile flowers.
  5. Drink the chamomile tea while it is still warm, up to three times a day, to help soothe stomach cramps.

2. Ginger

Ginger tea provides relief from stomach cramps due to its carminative properties, which facilitate the expulsion of intestinal gas and alleviate feelings of discomfort.

This soothing effect stems from ginger's ability to relax the smooth muscle of the intestines, thereby reducing spasms and tension that cause cramping. Additionally, ginger possesses anti-inflammatory qualities that help decrease inflammation in the stomach lining, further contributing to the alleviation of cramps. The active components in ginger, such as gingerols and shogaols, play a crucial role in inhibiting the activation of pain-inducing compounds in the body.

Consequently, ginger tea's healing properties offer a natural, effective solution for combatting stomach cramps.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in ginger tea that help with stomach cramps.

  • Rhizomes: Ginger's underground stem, or rhizome, contains bioactive compounds like gingerols and shogaols, known to alleviate digestive issues such as stomach cramps.
  • Roots: The fleshy, spindle-shaped ginger roots have potent anti-inflammatory properties that help relax gastrointestinal muscles, easing stomach discomfort.
  • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants, ginger leaves contribute to soothing stomach muscles, thereby reducing cramping and promoting overall digestive health.
  • Flowers: Ginger flowers contain essential oils with antispasmodic properties, providing relief from stomach cramps and supporting a healthy gastrointestinal system.
  • Seeds: Ginger seeds have a high concentration of volatile oils, which aid in relieving smooth muscle spasms, thus reducing the severity of stomach cramps.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginger tea for stomach cramps.

  1. Peel and chop 1 inch of fresh ginger root into small pieces, then place in a teapot.
  2. Add 2 cups of water to the teapot and bring to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat and let the ginger simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the ginger pieces and discard, then add honey and lemon to taste.
  5. Pour into a cup and enjoy your soothing ginger tea for stomach cramps.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in licorice tea that help with stomach cramps.

  • Leaves: Abundant in triterpenoids, licorice leaves contribute carminative effects, easing stomach cramps.
  • Seeds: Containing coumarins, licorice seeds offer antispasmodic qualities that help relax the smooth muscles of the gastrointestinal tract.
  • Flowers: With abundant phytoestrogens, licorice flowers provide hormonal balance and reduce pregnancy-related stomach cramps.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic licorice tea for stomach cramps.

  1. In a teapot, combine 1 teaspoon of dried licorice root with 8 ounces of boiling water.
  2. Steep the tea for 10-15 minutes to allow the active compounds to be released.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the licorice pieces.
  4. Discard the used licorice root.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, aiming to drink at least 3 cups throughout the day.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in turmeric tea that help with stomach cramps.

  • Roots: The roots of the turmeric plant have a similar composition to rhizomes and share their ability to reduce inflammation and alleviate stomach discomfort.
  • Leaves: Turmeric leaves contain beneficial compounds, including curcumin, making them a viable option for treating stomach cramps when used in medicinal tea.
  • Flowers: Though less common, turmeric flowers can be used in medicinal tea, as they possess anti-inflammatory properties that aid in soothing stomach cramps.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic turmeric tea for stomach cramps.

  1. In a saucepan, combine 4 cups of water and 1 teaspoon of grated fresh turmeric or 1/2 teaspoon of ground turmeric.
  2. Bring the mixture to a boil, then lower the heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea into a cup, then add honey or lemon to taste (optional).
  4. Mix in 1/4 teaspoon of black pepper to enhance turmeric's absorption.
  5. Drink the tea while it's still warm, up to three times a day for stomach cramp relief.

  3. Boil 2 cups of water and add 1 tablespoon of dandelion petals, let it simmer for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the petals.
  5. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, and drink while warm for stomach cramp relief.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic angelica tea for stomach cramps.

  3. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 10 minutes to extract the medicinal properties.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the solid residue.
  5. Sip the warm tea slowly to soothe stomach cramps, allowing it to take effect.
