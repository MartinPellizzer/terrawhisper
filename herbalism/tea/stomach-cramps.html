10 Best Herbal Teas For Stomach Cramps

Herbal teas, particularly those made from chamomile, peppermint, and ginger, are known to provide relief from stomach cramps.

This is because these herbs have anti-spasmodic properties that help relax the muscles of the gastrointestinal tract, reducing muscle spasms and consequently, cramps. Additionally, these herbs have anti-inflammatory effects that can help reduce inflammation in the stomach, further alleviating discomfort.

Furthermore, herbal teas can help increase the production of digestive enzymes, promoting healthy digestion and reducing the likelihood of cramps. The soothing warmth of the tea can also help to relax the mind and body, reducing tension and stress that can exacerbate stomach cramps.

However, it's important to note that while herbal teas can be helpful, they should not replace medical treatment, especially for severe or persistent stomach cramps.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with stomach cramps because it contains antispasmodic properties, which help to relax the muscles in the digestive tract, easing the contractions that cause cramping.

The herbal tea also has anti-inflammatory effects, reducing inflammation and swelling in the gut, which can contribute to cramping. Chamomile is known for its calming effects on the nervous system, helping to reduce stress and anxiety, both of which can exacerbate stomach cramps. Drinking chamomile tea after meals can aid in digestion and prevent future cramps by easing the transition of food through the intestines.

Additionally, the tea's soothing warmth can provide comfort and relaxation, making it a popular natural remedy for relieving stomach cramps.

The following procedure explaing how to make chamomile tea for stomach cramps.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water and let it cool down for a couple of minutes until it reaches a temperature of around 205 degrees Fahrenheit.
  2. Steep 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers in the hot water for 5-10 minutes, depending on how strong you prefer the taste.
  3. Strain the chamomile flowers from the tea, squeezing out any excess liquid.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Sip the chamomile tea slowly, allowing it to soothe your stomach cramps.

2. Ginger

Ginger tea helps with stomach cramps because ginger contains properties that aid in digestion and soothe the digestive system, reducing inflammation and irritation.

The anti-inflammatory compounds in ginger, such as gingerols and shogaols, help to relax the smooth muscle of the intestines, alleviating cramping and spasms. Ginger also stimulates the production of digestive enzymes, which helps to break down food and move it through the digestive tract more efficiently, reducing the likelihood of cramping. Furthermore, ginger has been shown to help alleviate nausea and vomiting, common symptoms that often accompany stomach cramps.

Lastly, ginger tea is a caffeine-free and gentle alternative to other remedies, making it a safe and natural option for managing stomach cramps.

The following procedure explaing how to make ginger tea for stomach cramps.

  1. Peel and thinly slice 1-2 inches of fresh ginger root, then bruise the slices with a spoon or rolling pin to release their oils.
  2. Bring 4 cups of water to a boil in a saucepan, then add the bruised ginger slices and reduce heat to a simmer.
  3. Allow the ginger to simmer for 15-20 minutes to create a strong tea, then remove from heat and let steep for an additional 5 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a mug, discarding the ginger slices, and add honey or lemon to taste (optional).
  5. Sip the ginger tea slowly, allowing it to warm your stomach and help alleviate cramps. Enjoy up to three cups per day as needed.

3. Peppermint

Peppermint tea helps with stomach cramps because it contains menthol, a compound that relaxes the gastrointestinal muscles, reducing spasms and pain.

Additionally, peppermint oil, the main ingredient in peppermint tea, has been shown to increase bile flow, improving digestion and reducing the likelihood of cramps. Furthermore, peppermint tea has anti-inflammatory properties that can help soothe an irritated stomach lining, reducing discomfort and inflammation. The tea's warm temperature can also help stimulate digestion, while its calming aroma can help reduce stress and anxiety, both of which can contribute to stomach cramps.

Overall, peppermint tea is a natural and effective remedy for relieving stomach cramps and promoting digestive health.

The following procedure explaing how to make peppermint tea for stomach cramps.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan on the stove, allowing it to reach a rolling boil.
  2. Add 1 heaping teaspoon of dried peppermint leaves (or 1 peppermint tea bag) to the boiling water.
  3. Reduce heat to low, cover the saucepan, and let it steep for 5-7 minutes for optimal extraction of peppermint's beneficial compounds.
  4. Strain the tea to remove the peppermint leaves, then pour it into a cup.
  5. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, and drink the tea while warm to help alleviate stomach cramps. Enjoy your soothing peppermint tea!

4. Fennel

Fennel tea helps with stomach cramps because it contains volatile oils, such as fenchone, anethole, and estragole, which have antispasmodic properties that help relax the smooth muscles of the gastrointestinal tract, thereby alleviating cramping and discomfort.

Additionally, fennel tea has carminative effects, meaning it can help expel gas from the digestive tract, reducing bloating and pain. Furthermore, fennel's anti-inflammatory properties may help soothe irritated stomach lining, lessening the likelihood of cramps. The tea may also stimulate the production of digestive enzymes, promoting healthy digestion and reducing the risk of cramp-inducing issues like indigestion.

Lastly, fennel tea's calming nature can help reduce stress and anxiety, which can exacerbate stomach cramps, providing holistic relief for this common ailment.

The following procedure explaing how to make fennel tea for stomach cramps.

  1. Gather 1 teaspoon of fennel seeds and 1 cup of water.
  2. Coarsely grind the fennel seeds using a mortar and pestle or a spice grinder.
  3. Bring the water to a boil in a small saucepan, then add the ground fennel seeds.
  4. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 5-7 minutes, then strain the tea to remove the solid particles.
  5. Sip the fennel tea slowly while it's still warm, up to three times a day, between meals, to help alleviate stomach cramps.

5. Licorice Root

Licorice root tea helps with stomach cramps because it contains glycyrrhizic acid, which has been shown to help reduce inflammation in the gastrointestinal tract, thereby alleviating pain and discomfort.

Additionally, licorice root has been found to increase the mucus lining of the stomach, providing a protective barrier against stomach acids that can cause cramps and pain. Furthermore, licorice root has been shown to increase the production of mucin, a compound that helps maintain the health of the gastrointestinal tract, reducing the likelihood of cramps and other stomach issues. It's important to note that while licorice root tea can be helpful for occasional stomach cramps, it should not be used as a long-term solution or in large quantities, as it can have side effects such as high blood pressure and hormonal imbalances.

As with any natural remedy, it's always best to consult with a healthcare provider before use.

The following procedure explaing how to make licorice root tea for stomach cramps.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan on the stove.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried licorice root (Glycyrrhiza glabra) to the boiling water.
  3. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the used licorice root.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, allowing it to coat your mouth and throat, and repeat as needed for soothing relief of stomach cramps.

6. Turmeric

Turmeric tea helps with stomach cramps because it contains curcumin, a compound with potent anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties that help reduce inflammation and alleviate muscle spasms in the gastrointestinal tract.

Furthermore, turmeric can stimulate the production of bile in the stomach, which aids in digestion and helps prevent the formation of gas, a common cause of stomach cramps. Additionally, its antioxidant properties can help protect the stomach lining from damage, reducing the risk of cramps and discomfort. The soothing and warming effect of turmeric tea can also help relax the muscles in the stomach, providing quick relief from cramps and pain.

Lastly, turmeric tea has been used as a natural remedy for digestive issues for centuries, and its effectiveness is supported by numerous scientific studies, making it a safe and reliable option for managing stomach cramps.

The following procedure explaing how to make turmeric tea for stomach cramps.

  1. Boil 4 cups of water in a saucepan over medium heat.
  2. Stir in 1 teaspoon of ground turmeric and 1/2 teaspoon of black pepper (to aid turmeric absorption) into the boiling water.
  3. Reduce heat to low, cover the saucepan, and let the mixture simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, and add honey or lemon to taste (optional).
  5. Sip the tea slowly, allowing it to warm your stomach and alleviate cramps. Enjoy up to three cups a day.

7. Catnip

Catnip tea helps with stomach cramps because it contains nepetalactone, a compound that has antispasmodic properties, which can help relax the smooth muscles in the gastrointestinal tract and alleviate cramping and pain.

Additionally, catnip has been found to have anti-inflammatory effects, which can help reduce inflammation in the stomach and intestines, further alleviating discomfort. Furthermore, catnip tea can help stimulate the production of digestive enzymes and bile, which can improve digestion and reduce the likelihood of stomach cramps caused by indigestion. Additionally, catnip has mild sedative effects, which can help relax the body and alleviate tension and stress that can exacerbate stomach cramps.

However, it's important to note that more research is needed to fully understand the mechanisms behind catnip's potential therapeutic benefits for stomach cramps.

The following procedure explaing how to make catnip tea for stomach cramps.

  1. Gather 1 teaspoon of dried catnip leaves and 1 cup of water; ensure the catnip is organically grown and dried.
  2. Place the catnip leaves in a teapot or saucepan, then pour the water over the leaves, covering them completely.
  3. Slowly heat the water to a simmer (195-205°F/90-96°C) and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the used catnip leaves, and allow it to cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Sip the catnip tea slowly, taking small sips to help alleviate stomach cramps, consuming up to 3 cups per day as needed.

8. Dandelion Root

Dandelion root tea helps with stomach cramps because it has anti-inflammatory properties that can reduce inflammation in the stomach lining, thereby alleviating pain and discomfort.

Additionally, it promotes digestion by increasing the production of stomach acid and bile, which can help speed up the digestion process and prevent cramping caused by slow digestion. The tea also contains antispasmodic properties that help relax the smooth muscles in the digestive tract, reducing the likelihood of cramping and spasms. Furthermore, dandelion root tea is rich in vitamins and minerals, including magnesium, which can help regulate muscle contractions and reduce the severity of stomach cramps.

Finally, its soothing and calming effects on the nervous system can help alleviate stress and anxiety, which can often exacerbate stomach cramps and other digestive issues.

The following procedure explaing how to make dandelion root tea for stomach cramps.

  1. To make dandelion root tea for stomach cramps, start by finely chopping 2 tablespoons of fresh dandelion root or using 1 tablespoon of dried dandelion root; add the chopped or dried dandelion root to 4 cups of boiling water and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea to remove any solid particles and discard the used dandelion root; optional: gently squeeze the dandelion root with a spoon to extract any remaining liquid and nutrients.
  3. Add honey, lemon, or other natural sweeteners to taste, if desired; stir well until fully dissolved.
  4. Allow the tea to cool down to a drinkable temperature, usually around 5-10 minutes.
  5. Pour the dandelion root tea into a cup, slowly sip, and enjoy the soothing effects on your stomach cramps.
