10 best herbal teas for queasy stomach

When it comes to soothing a queasy stomach, certain herbal teas can offer fast and effective relief.

Peppermint tea is a popular choice due to its calming and antispasmodic properties, which can help reduce stomach discomfort and cramping. Ginger tea, with its natural anti-inflammatory and digestive qualities, is also a great option for easing nausea and settling the stomach.

Chamomile tea, known for its soothing and relaxing effects, can also help alleviate digestive issues and promote overall gut health. Lastly, licorice root tea can help protect the stomach lining, reduce inflammation, and alleviate symptoms of upset stomach.

Whether you're looking for immediate relief or long-term gut health support, these herbal teas offer a natural and delicious remedy for a queasy stomach.

1. Ginger

Ginger tea's soothing warmth and mild flavor make it an ideal remedy for a queasy stomach.

Ginger contains active ingredients, such as gingerols and shogaols, which possess well-documented anti-inflammatory and digestive properties. These compounds work together to help neutralize stomach acids, alleviating discomfort and reducing the likelihood of nausea. Furthermore, ginger tea stimulates the production of digestive enzymes, enhancing nutrient absorption and promoting overall gastrointestinal health.

Lastly, ginger's natural calming effects on the intestinal tract help relax muscles, providing lasting relief from queasiness and occasional indigestion.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in ginger tea that help with queasy stomach.

  • Roots: Contain gingerols and shaogals, compounds with proven antiemetic properties that help alleviate feelings of nausea.
  • Rhizomes: These horizontal underground stems are rich in gingerols and shaogals, which make them effective in treating symptoms of queasy stomach.
  • Leaves: High in flavonoids and polyphenols, they possess anti-inflammatory properties, providing relief from digestive discomfort.
  • Flowers: Packed with antioxidants, they help soothe the stomach and reduce inflammation.
  • Seeds: Though less potent than the other parts, they still contain beneficial compounds that can aid in alleviating queasy stomach.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginger tea for queasy stomach.

  1. Peel and thinly slice 1 inch of fresh ginger root.
  2. In a small saucepan, combine ginger slices with 2 cups of water and bring to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 10 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, then stir in 1-2 teaspoons of honey and a squeeze of lemon juice (if desired).
  5. Drink warm, up to three times a day for queasy stomach relief.

2. Peppermint

Peppermint tea relieves a queasy stomach due to its therapeutic properties.

The menthol present in peppermint tea soothes the stomach by relaxing gastrointestinal muscles. This action allows the stomach to move food through the digestive system more efficiently, reducing queasiness. Peppermint tea also has antispasmodic qualities, which alleviate discomfort by minimizing intestinal spasms.

Furthermore, peppermint tea's calming effect on the stomach lining reduces inflammation and provides quick relief from queasiness.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in peppermint tea that help with queasy stomach.

  • Leaves: Rich in menthol and menthone, peppermint leaves aid in digestion and provide quick relief from queasy stomach.
  • Flowers: High in flavonoids, peppermint flowers help relax gastrointestinal tract muscles, alleviating discomfort and unease in the stomach.
  • Seeds: Packed with essential oils, peppermint seeds support healthy digestion and soothe symptoms of queasy stomach.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic peppermint tea for queasy stomach.

  1. In a teapot, add boiling water to 1 tablespoon of dried peppermint leaves, allowing the tea to steep for 10 minutes.
  2. While waiting, warm a mug by rinsing it with hot water.
  3. Strain the tea into the warmed mug to remove the leaves.
  4. Optionally, add 1 teaspoon of honey for added sweetness and throat soothing.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, allowing it to gently soothe your queasy stomach.

Its anti-inflammatory properties further soothe any inflammation in the gastrointestinal tract. Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in chamomile tea that help with queasy stomach.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for queasy stomach.

  1. Measure out 1 teaspoon of dried chamomile flowers or 1 chamomile tea bag.
  2. Place the chamomile in a tea infuser or directly into a mug.
  3. Boil 8 ounces of water and pour it over the chamomile.
  4. Steep the chamomile for 5-10 minutes, then remove the infuser or tea bag.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, allowing it to soothe your queasy stomach.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic licorice tea for queasy stomach.

  4. Strain the tea to remove the licorice root pieces.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, allowing it to soothe your queasy stomach.

6. Slippery Elm

Slippery elm tea relieves queasy stomachs due to its demulcent properties, which involve the extraction of mucilage from the tree's bark.

This mucilage transforms into a slick, gel-like substance upon contact with water, creating a protective layer in the digestive tract. This lining shields the stomach from acidic irritations, reducing discomfort and inflammation. Furthermore, this soothing coating encourages mucous membrane regeneration, fostering a healthy gastrointestinal system.

Ultimately, slippery elm tea's natural components work harmoniously to alleviate queasy stomachs by safeguarding and soothing the digestive tract.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in slippery elm tea that help with queasy stomach.

  • Bark: The inner bark of the slippery elm tree is used to make medicinal tea for queasy stomach due to its demulcent properties, which mean it has a soothing effect on the membranes of the stomach and intestines.
  • Roots: The roots of the slippery elm plant are not used to make the tea as they are too harsh and not as effective in soothing the stomach.
  • Leaves: The leaves of the slippery elm plant are used in teas for their milder, but still effective, mucilage content which can help to coat and soothe the lining of the stomach.
  • Seeds: The seeds of the slippery elm plant are mucilaginous, just like the bark and leaves, making them a good substitute for the inner bark when making tea to soothe a queasy stomach.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic slippery elm tea for queasy stomach.

  1. Measure - Pour 1 cup of boiling water over 1 teaspoon of slippery elm bark.
  2. Steep - Let the mixture steep, covered, for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain - Strain the tea to remove bark particles.
  4. Stir - Stir in honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Sip - Drink the tea slowly, a few sips at a time, to soothe a queasy stomach.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic marshmallow tea for queasy stomach.

  1. In a small pot, combine 1 cup of water and 1 tablespoon of freshly grated ginger, then bring to a boil and let simmer for 10 minutes.
  2. Add 1 peppermint tea bag and 1 chamomile tea bag to the pot, then turn off the heat and let steep for 5 minutes.
  3. Remove the tea bags and stir in 1 tablespoon of honey until fully dissolved.
  4. Squeeze half a lemon into the pot and stir well.
  5. Pour the tea through a strainer into a mug, then top off with 2-3 marshmallows for added sweetness before serving.

  • Flowers: Packed with soothing properties, dandelion flowers ease queasy stomachs, promoting digestive comfort.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic dandelion tea for queasy stomach.

  1. Gather 2-3 fresh dandelion heads, ensuring they are from a pesticide-free area, and rinse them under cold water.
  2. Separate the yellow petals from the green base and stems, as the green parts can cause a bitter taste.
  3. Place the petals in a tea infuser or teapot and add 1 cup of boiling water, allowing them to steep for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Remove the infuser or strain the petals from the tea.
  5. Sip the dandelion tea slowly, allowing it to soothe your queasy stomach. Enjoy within 24 hours for maximum freshness.
