1. Ginger

Ginger tea helps with queasy stomach because it contains gingerol, a compound with proven anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects that can help regulate the nervous system and reduce stomach irritation.

This active ingredient in ginger inhibits the effects of serotonin, a chemical that can cause nausea and vomiting when released in excess. Furthermore, ginger stimulates gastric emptying, which helps the digestive system process food more quickly and alleviates feelings of fullness and discomfort. Additionally, ginger's natural calming properties can help soothe the mind and reduce anxiety, which can often exacerbate feelings of queasiness.

Lastly, ginger tea has long been used as a traditional remedy for nausea and vomiting, and its effectiveness has been supported by numerous scientific studies, making it a safe and natural alternative to pharmaceutical treatments.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in ginger tea that help with queasy stomach.

  1. Gingerols: Gingerols have anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties that help reduce stomach irritation and alleviate nausea and queasiness.
  2. Shogaols: Shogaols exhibit anti-emetic effects that help suppress vomiting and calm an upset stomach.
  3. Paradols: Paradols possess gastroprotective properties that help prevent gastric ulcers and ease symptoms of queasy stomach.
  4. Zingerone: Zingerone stimulates digestion, reduces gastric motility, and alleviates symptoms of motion sickness and stomach upset.

The following list reveals what parts of ginger are most commonly used to make medicinal tea for queasy stomach.

  1. Rhizomes: The underground stem of ginger, known as the rhizome, is most commonly used to make medicinal tea for queasy stomach due to its anti-inflammatory and digestive properties.
  2. Roots: While technically part of the rhizome, ginger "roots" can be used interchangeably and are rich in volatile oils and compounds that aid in soothing the stomach and reducing nausea.
  3. Leaves: Ginger leaves contain polyphenols and flavonoids that can help relax the intestinal tract, providing relief for queasy stomachs.
  4. Flowers: Ginger flowers carry antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that can help alleviate digestive issues and provide additional benefits when included in medicinal tea for queasy stomach.
  5. Seeds: Ginger seeds have essential oils and bioactive compounds that can help stimulate digestion and settle the stomach, making them an occasional addition to medicinal ginger teas.

The following procedure explains how to make ginger tea for queasy stomach.

  1. Peel and grate 1 inch of fresh ginger; steep in 1 cup of hot water for 5 minutes.
  2. Squeeze half a fresh lemon into the ginger water for added vitamin C.
  3. Stir in 1-2 teaspoons of honey for soothing and calming properties.
  4. Consume immediately for quick relief, up to three times a day as needed.
