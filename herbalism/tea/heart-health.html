10 Best Herbal Teas For Heart Health

Herbal teas, made from various dried herbs, contain antioxidants and other compounds that can help protect the heart by reducing inflammation, improving blood vessel function, and helping to lower blood pressure.

Some specific herbs, like hawthorn and ginger, have been shown to have particular benefits for heart health. Additionally, herbal teas can help manage stress and promote relaxation, which can indirectly support heart health by reducing stress-related factors such as high blood pressure and poor eating habits. However, it's important to note that while herbal teas can be a healthy addition to a balanced diet, they should not be used as a substitute for medical treatment or a heart-healthy lifestyle.

As always, it's best to consult with a healthcare provider before making any significant changes to your diet or lifestyle.

1. Hawthorn

Hawthorn tea helps with heart health because it contains antioxidants that support healthy blood flow and improve the functioning of blood vessels, which can help reduce the risk of heart disease.

Additionally, hawthorn tea has been shown to dilate coronary blood vessels, improving blood flow to the heart and potentially reducing symptoms of heart failure. The tea may also help lower blood pressure and reduce irregular heartbeats, both of which are beneficial for heart health. Furthermore, hawthorn tea has been found to strengthen the heart muscle, which can help improve its overall function.

Lastly, hawthorn tea has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the body, which is beneficial for heart health as chronic inflammation can lead to heart disease.

The following procedure explaing how to make hawthorn tea for heart health.

  1. Roughly chop 1 tablespoon of dried hawthorn berries and place them in a tea infuser or teapot with a built-in strainer.
  2. Boil 2 cups of water and pour it over the hawthorn berries in the teapot or teacup.
  3. Allow the tea to steep for 10-15 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds from the hawthorn berries.
  4. Optional: Add honey or lemon to taste for improved flavor, if desired.
  5. Drink 1-2 cups of hawthorn tea daily, preferably between meals, for heart health benefits.

2. Hibiscus

Hibiscus tea helps with heart health because it is rich in antioxidants that can help protect the heart from damage and reduce the risk of heart disease.

It has been shown to lower blood pressure, particularly in people with hypertension, which can decrease the strain on the heart and reduce the risk of heart-related complications. Additionally, hibiscus tea can help improve cholesterol levels by lowering LDL (bad) cholesterol and increasing HDL (good) cholesterol, which can help prevent plaque buildup in the arteries and reduce the risk of heart disease. Furthermore, hibiscus tea has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the body, which is a major contributor to heart disease.

Lastly, hibiscus tea has been shown to improve blood vessel function, which can help improve blood flow and reduce the risk of heart disease.

The following procedure explaing how to make hibiscus tea for heart health.

  1. In a 2-quart pot, add 8 cups of water and bring to a boil, then add 1/2 cup of dried hibiscus flowers, reduce heat and simmer for 10 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea to remove the hibiscus flowers and discard them, leaving only the vibrant red liquid.
  3. Allow the tea to cool to a drinkable temperature, which should take about 15-20 minutes.
  4. Add natural sweeteners like raw honey or stevia to taste, if desired.
  5. Serve the hibiscus tea chilled or warm, and enjoy this heart-healthy beverage daily.

3. Green Tea

Green tea helps with heart health because it is rich in bioactive compounds, particularly catechins, which have been shown to improve heart function and reduce the risk of cardiovascular disease.

One study found that individuals who drank green tea regularly had a lower risk of developing high blood pressure, a major factor in heart disease. Additionally, green tea has been shown to reduce LDL cholesterol levels, which can help prevent plaque buildup in the arteries and lower the risk of heart attacks and strokes. Furthermore, green tea has been found to increase the antioxidant capacity of the blood, which can help protect the cardiovascular system from damage.

Finally, green tea has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce inflammation in the arteries and improve overall heart health.

The following procedure explaing how to make green tea tea for heart health.

  1. Heat 8 ounces of water to 160-180°F (71-82°C) within 2-3 minutes, avoiding boiling to preserve heart-healthy catechins.
  2. In a teapot or cup, add a green tea bag or 1-2 teaspoons (2-4 grams) of loose leaf green tea, preferably a type known for heart health such as matcha or sencha.
  3. Pour the heated water over the tea, submerging the tea bag or leaves completely.
  4. Allow the tea to steep for 2-3 minutes, enhancing flavonoid bioavailability and heart-health benefits.
  5. Remove the tea bag or strain the leaves, then enjoy the green tea warm to maintain optimal antioxidant levels.

4. Black Tea

Black tea helps with heart health because it is rich in bioactive compounds, particularly flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties that help reduce cell damage linked to numerous diseases, including heart disease.

Regular consumption of black tea has been associated with a reduced risk of heart attacks and strokes, as it helps to maintain healthy blood pressure and cholesterol levels. The tea's catechins and theaflavins can inhibit the oxidation of LDL cholesterol, preventing the formation of plaque in arteries, and reducing the risk of heart disease. Moreover, black tea has been shown to improve endothelial function, which is crucial for maintaining vascular health and preventing cardiovascular issues.

Lastly, black tea's potential to reduce inflammation may contribute to its heart-healthy effects, as chronic inflammation is a significant risk factor for heart disease.

The following procedure explaing how to make black tea tea for heart health.

  1. In a pot, add 2 cups of fresh water and bring to a boil (3-5 minutes).
  2. Add 1 teaspoon of black tea leaves (or 1 tea bag) and let it steep for 2-3 minutes.
  3. Add a cinnamon stick (optional) for added heart health benefits.
  4. Strain the tea to remove the leaves and let it cool for 2-3 minutes.
  5. Serve and enjoy your heart-healthy black tea. Add honey or lemon to taste (optional).

5. White Tea

White tea helps with heart health because it is rich in catechins, a type of flavonoid that has been shown to enhance cardiovascular health.

These catechins work by relaxing the walls of blood vessels, which improves blood flow and reduces the risk of heart disease. Additionally, white tea is known for its antioxidant properties, which help to reduce oxidative stress and inflammation in the body, both of which are risk factors for heart disease. Furthermore, white tea has been found to lower blood pressure and cholesterol levels, both of which are important factors in maintaining a healthy heart.

Finally, white tea is a great source of heart-healthy compounds, such as quercetin and rutin, which have been shown to protect the heart and cardiovascular system.

The following procedure explaing how to make white tea tea for heart health.

  1. Heat 1 cup of water to 160-180°F (71-82°C), allowing 1-3 minutes of heating time, for the perfect white tea brewing temperature.
  2. Place 1 teaspoon (2-4 grams) of high-quality loose-leaf white tea in a strainer or infuser.
  3. Steep the tea in the heated water for 1-3 minutes, to extract heart-healthy antioxidants without releasing bitter compounds.
  4. Remove the tea strainer and discard the used tea leaves.
  5. Serve the warm, antioxidant-rich white tea and enjoy up to 3 cups daily for heart health benefits.

6. Ginger

Ginger tea helps with heart health because it contains antioxidants and anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce the risk of developing heart disease.

These properties work by preventing oxidative stress and inflammation in the body, which are both associated with heart disease. Additionally, ginger tea has been shown to help lower cholesterol levels, which can contribute to plaque buildup in the arteries and lead to heart disease. Furthermore, ginger tea may help reduce high blood pressure, a major risk factor for heart disease, by relaxing the blood vessels and improving blood flow.

Lastly, ginger tea is a natural remedy for reducing stress and anxiety, which can have a positive impact on heart health by lowering heart rate and blood pressure.

The following procedure explaing how to make ginger tea for heart health.

  1. Peel and finely chop 1 tablespoon of fresh ginger root, and let it steep in hot water for 5 minutes.
  2. Add 1 teaspoon of honey and 1 teaspoon of lemon juice to the ginger-infused water for natural sweetness and vitamin C.
  3. Freshly squeeze the juice of half a small orange into the mixture for an extra antioxidant boost.
  4. Warm the concoction on low heat for 2-3 minutes, but do not let it boil to preserve the beneficial properties of the ingredients.
  5. Strain the ginger tea into a cup, and enjoy this soothing and heart-healthy beverage.

7. Cinnamon

Cinnamon tea helps with heart health because it is rich in antioxidants that support heart health by reducing oxidative stress and inflammation.

Additionally, it has been shown to lower levels of total cholesterol, LDL (bad) cholesterol, and triglycerides, while increasing HDL (good) cholesterol, all of which are factors that contribute to a healthy heart. Furthermore, cinnamon tea may help to reduce blood pressure, another important factor in heart health. Additionally, it has been suggested that cinnamon may improve blood flow and help prevent blood clots, further supporting heart health.

Lastly, cinnamon tea is a healthier alternative to sugary drinks, which can contribute to obesity, diabetes, and other heart disease risk factors.

The following procedure explaing how to make cinnamon tea for heart health.

  1. In a pot, combine 1 cup of water and 1 cinnamon stick, let it simmer for 10 minutes.
  2. Add 1 teaspoon of honey and 1 teaspoon of lemon juice to the cinnamon tea.
  3. Stir well to dissolve the honey, then remove the cinnamon stick.
  4. Pour the tea into a cup, allowing it to cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Enjoy your cinnamon tea as a part of your heart-healthy lifestyle.

8. Turmeric

Turmeric tea helps with heart health because it contains curcumin, a bioactive substance that has been shown to reduce inflammation and improve the function of the endothelium, the lining of the blood vessels.

Curcumin has also been found to lower the levels of certain chemicals that are linked to heart disease, such as triglycerides and LDL (bad) cholesterol. Additionally, studies have suggested that curcumin may help reduce the risk of heart attack by improving the function of the heart's left ventricle, which is the chamber that pumps blood out of the heart. Furthermore, turmeric tea has been shown to have anti-platelet and anti-thrombotic properties, which can help prevent blood clots from forming and potentially causing a heart attack or stroke.

Lastly, turmeric tea is a caffeine-free alternative to traditional black or green tea, making it a heart-healthy choice for those who are sensitive to caffeine or looking to reduce their caffeine intake.

The following procedure explaing how to make turmeric tea for heart health.

  1. In a saucepan, combine 2 cups of water, 1 teaspoon of freshly grated turmeric root (or 1/2 teaspoon of ground turmeric), and 1/4 teaspoon of black pepper (which enhances turmeric's bioavailability); simmer for 10 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea into a cup, pressing the turmeric solids to extract all liquid.
  3. Stir in 1-2 teaspoons of honey (optional) and 1/2 teaspoon of lemon juice (optional) to taste.
  4. Drink while warm for best results.
  5. Repeat up to twice daily for heart health benefits.

9. Black Pepper

Black pepper tea helps with heart health because it contains piperine, a compound that has been shown to improve blood cholesterol levels and reduce blood pressure, both of which are important factors in heart health.

Additionally, black pepper has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the cardiovascular system, further supporting heart health. The tea may also help to improve circulation, which can help to reduce the risk of heart disease. Furthermore, black pepper has been found to have antioxidant properties, which can help to protect the heart and blood vessels from damage.

Finally, black pepper tea is a caffeine-free alternative to traditional tea or coffee, making it a heart-healthy choice for those who are sensitive to caffeine.

The following procedure explaing how to make black pepper tea for heart health.

  1. In a mortar, lightly crush 1 teaspoon of whole black peppercorns (adjust dosage based on personal preference) and 2-3 cloves to release their oils, which takes about 1 minute.
  2. Combine the crushed peppercorns and cloves with 2 cups of water in a small saucepan and bring to a simmer over medium heat.
  3. Allow the mixture to simmer for 5-7 minutes, releasing the beneficial compounds from the black pepper and cloves.
  4. Strain the tea through a fine-mesh sieve into a teapot or teacups, pressing on the solids to extract all the liquid.
  5. Add a touch of honey or a squeeze of lemon, if desired, and enjoy this heart-healthy black pepper tea up to three times a day.

10. Valerian

Valerian tea helps with heart health because it contains antioxidants that can help reduce the risk of oxidative stress and inflammation, which are both associated with heart disease.

Additionally, valerian tea may help lower blood pressure by promoting relaxation and reducing stress, thereby decreasing the strain on the heart. The tea has also been found to improve circulation, allowing for better nutrient and oxygen delivery to the heart muscle. Furthermore, valerian's natural sedative properties may aid in regulating heart rhythms, contributing to overall cardiovascular health.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before incorporating valerian tea into a heart health regimen, as it can interact with certain medications and may not be suitable for everyone.

The following procedure explaing how to make valerian tea for heart health.

  1. Heat 2 cups of water to boiling point (10 minutes) and steep 1-2 teaspoons of dried valerian root (or 1 valerian root tea bag) in it for 10-15 minutes.
  2. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, after the tea has cooled down slightly.
  3. Do not add milk or other dairy products as they may reduce the effectiveness of valerian.
  4. Drink up to two cups of valerian tea per day for heart health benefits.
  5. Consult with a healthcare provider if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or taking any medications before consuming valerian tea.
