10 best herbal teas for heart health

For those seeking to improve heart health, certain herbal teas have been found to offer significant benefits.

Hawthorn tea is renowned for its ability to strengthen and tone the heart muscle, improve blood flow, and regulate blood pressure. Another excellent choice is green tea, which is packed with antioxidants that help reduce inflammation, lower LDL cholesterol, and protect against cardiovascular disease.

Additionally, cinnamon tea has been shown to help reduce blood pressure, cholesterol levels, and the risk of blood clotting. Lastly, black tea has been associated with a lowered risk of heart attacks and strokes due to its anti-inflammatory properties and ability to improve blood vessel function.

Incorporating these herbal teas into a daily routine can be a delicious and natural way to support heart health.

1. Hawthorn

Hawthorn tea benefits heart health due to its rich antioxidant content, including phenolic acids and flavonoids, which strengthen blood vessels and improve blood flow.

This herbal tea also contains active compounds such as oligomeric procyanidins, which widen blood vessels and lower blood pressure, reducing strain on the heart. Furthermore, hawthorn tea's positive inotropic effect strengthens the heart's contractions, increasing pumping efficiency and overall cardiovascular function.

Additionally, hawthorn tea's anti-inflammatory properties help protect the heart from damage caused by inflammation, contributing to long-term heart health.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in hawthorn tea that help with heart health.

  • Berries: The berries are rich in antioxidants that support cardiovascular health.
  • Leaves: The leaves contain flavonoids that help reduce blood pressure and promote heart health.
  • Flowers: The flowers have anti-inflammatory properties that help improve heart function.
  • Bark: The bark contains proanthocyanidins that help lower cholesterol levels and improve heart health.

2. Hibiscus

Hibiscus tea contains anthocyanins and antioxidants, which are beneficial compounds known to lower blood pressure.

A study published in the Journal of Ethnopharmacology shows that hibiscus tea decreases both systolic and diastolic blood pressures. The tea's antioxidant properties, as reported in the Food & Function journal, protect the heart by preventing oxidative stress and inflammation. Furthermore, hibiscus tea enhances lipid profiles by decreasing levels of LDL cholesterol and triglycerides.

Regular consumption of hibiscus tea, therefore, strengthens heart health by naturally reducing blood pressure, protecting against oxidative stress, and improving lipid profiles.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in hibiscus tea that help with heart health.

  • Flowers: Hibiscus flowers are rich in anthocyanins, which are known to help lower blood pressure and improve lipid profiles, contributing to heart health.
  • Leaves: Hibiscus leaves are a source of flavonoids and polyphenols, which have been shown to possess cardioprotective properties, aiding in the maintenance of a healthy heart.
  • Seeds: Hibiscus seeds contain omega-3 fatty acids, which are essential for heart health, helping to reduce the risk of cardiovascular diseases.

3. Green Tea

Green tea, abundant in bioactive compounds, provides significant heart health benefits.

It contains epigallocatechin gallate (EGCG), a potent antioxidant that reduces artery blockages by decreasing inflammation and enhancing artery function. Moreover, green tea's catechins inhibit the absorption of cholesterol in the gut, lowering LDL cholesterol levels. Additionally, green tea consumption increases HDL cholesterol, the "good" cholesterol, promoting a healthy lipid profile.

Lastly, green tea's anti-hypertensive properties stem from its ability to relax blood vessels and inhibit angiotensin-converting enzyme, both of which contribute to maintaining healthy blood pressure.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in green tea tea that help with heart health.

  • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants, the leaves of green tea are known to reduce inflammation, improve blood flow, and help lower cholesterol levels, all of which contribute to a healthier heart.
  • Buds: Packed with catechins, the buds of green tea are known for their potent antioxidant properties, which aid in reducing the risk of cardiovascular diseases.
  • Seeds: The seeds of green tea, known as "kernels," contain essential fatty acids and vitamins that support heart health by reducing blood pressure and improving overall cardiovascular function.

4. Black Tea

Black tea contains theaflavins and thearubigins, two types of flavonoids known for their antioxidant properties.

Antioxidants protect the body from harmful free radicals, which contribute to heart disease when unbalanced. Regular consumption of black tea reduces blood lipid levels, lowering the risk of atherosclerosis, a condition characterized by plaque buildup in the arteries. The beverage also improves endothelial function, which means it supports blood vessel health and flexibility, contributing to normal blood pressure.

Lastly, black tea's beneficial compounds inhibit blood clot formation, further reducing the risk of heart attacks and strokes.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in black tea tea that help with heart health.

  • Leaves: The leaves of the black tea plant contain flavonoids that support cardiovascular health by improving blood flow and reducing inflammation.
  • Buds: The buds of the black tea plant are rich in antioxidants that protect the heart from damage and promote healthy blood pressure.
  • Stems: The stems of the black tea plant contain catechins that reduce the risk of heart disease by lowering cholesterol levels and preventing plaque buildup in arteries.
  • Bark: The bark of the black tea plant has anti-inflammatory properties that help relax blood vessels, reducing the risk of heart attacks and strokes.
  • Seeds: The seeds of the black tea plant contain essential fatty acids that support heart health by maintaining healthy cholesterol levels and preventing blood clots.

5. White Tea

White tea, with its antioxidant-rich composition, provides substantial benefits to heart health.

It contains catechins and flavonoids, known for reducing blood pressure and safeguarding against oxidative stress. Regular consumption of white tea enhances blood vessel function, lowering the risk of cardiovascular diseases. Moreover, it decreases harmful cholesterol levels while increasing beneficial ones, contributing to improved heart health.

Furthermore, white tea's anti-inflammatory and anti-thrombotic properties inhibit clot formation and inflammation, ensuring optimal heart performance.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in white tea tea that help with heart health.

  • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants, white tea leaves are known to help reduce blood pressure, improving heart health.
  • Buds: Packed with flavonoids, white tea buds aid in maintaining healthy cholesterol levels, supporting cardiovascular function.
  • Stems: Containing catechins, white tea stems help improve blood circulation, contributing to a healthier heart.

6. Ginger

Ginger tea contains antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds, such as gingerols and shogaols, which help reduce inflammation in the body.

These compounds also aid in lowering cholesterol levels and improving blood circulation, contributing to a healthier heart. By reducing cholesterol and thinning the blood, ginger tea helps prevent plaque buildup in the arteries, reducing the risk of heart disease. Moreover, ginger tea's ability to regulate blood pressure and heart rhythm ensures the heart's optimal performance.

Regular consumption of ginger tea strengthens heart muscles, further enhancing overall cardiovascular health.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in ginger tea that help with heart health.

  • Rhizomes: Ginger's underground stem, or rhizome, contains active compounds that help reduce inflammation and improve blood circulation, contributing to heart health.
  • Leaves: The leaves of the ginger plant possess heart-protective properties due to their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory abilities, making them suitable for medicinal tea.
  • Flowers: Ginger flowers offer bioactive compounds that support heart health by helping regulate blood pressure and cholesterol levels.
  • Seeds: Rich in essential oils, ginger seeds can help lower cholesterol levels, reduce blood clotting, and alleviate high blood pressure, promoting overall heart health.

7. Cinnamon

Cinnamon tea plays a crucial role in heart health due to its cinnamaldehyde content, a substance known for reducing blood pressure.

This herbal tea also contains antioxidants that help decrease levels of harmful cholesterol, thus minimizing the risk of cardiovascular diseases. Furthermore, cinnamon tea's anti-inflammatory properties contribute to maintaining healthy blood vessels, reducing the likelihood of plaque buildup. Additionally, this tea is renowned for its ability to regulate heart rhythm, promoting overall cardiovascular well-being.

Finally, cinnamon tea's hypoglycemic effect aids in balancing blood sugar levels, consequently alleviating stress on the heart.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in cinnamon tea that help with heart health.

  • Bark: The bark of the cinnamon tree is rich in compounds that help reduce blood pressure, thus promoting heart health.
  • Leaves: Cinnamon leaves contain antioxidants that help lower cholesterol levels, contributing to a healthy heart.
  • Seeds: The seeds of the cinnamon plant have anti-inflammatory properties that aid in preventing heart disease.
  • Stems: Cinnamon stems are a source of antioxidants that support heart health by reducing oxidative stress.

8. Turmeric

Turmeric tea contains curcumin, a compound with proven benefits for heart health.

This compound improves the function of the endothelium, the lining of the blood vessels, by reducing inflammation and enhancing blood flow. Curcumin also reduces the risk of heart disease by lowering levels of bad cholesterol and preventing the formation of blood clots. Moreover, it helps regulate blood pressure and prevents oxidative stress, a major cause of heart disease.

Lastly, studies demonstrate that turmeric tea prevents plaque buildup in the arteries, promoting overall cardiovascular health.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in turmeric tea that help with heart health.

  • Rhizomes: Turmeric's underground stem or rhizome contains curcuminoids, which have antioxidant properties that help improve heart health by reducing inflammation and oxidative stress.
  • Roots: The roots of turmeric have a similar composition to the rhizomes and offer similar heart health benefits, they are often used interchangeably in medicinal teas.
  • Leaves: Turmeric leaves contain essential oils and vitamins that can improve blood circulation and reduce the risk of heart disease.
  • Flowers: Although not as commonly used, turmeric flowers have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that support heart health by improving blood vessel function.
  • Seeds: Turmeric seeds contain curcumin and other bioactive compounds that can help reduce the risk of heart disease by regulating cholesterol levels and preventing the formation of plaque in the arteries.

9. Black Pepper

Black pepper tea supports heart health due to its rich content of piperine, a potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory agent.

This compound helps reduce chronic inflammation, a significant factor in heart disease. Moreover, piperine enhances the bioavailability of beneficial nutrients, such as curcumin, further promoting cardiovascular health. Additionally, black pepper tea supports blood vessel dilation, improving blood flow and lowering blood pressure.

Lastly, it aids in reducing lipid accumulation in the bloodstream, thus decreasing the likelihood of plaque formation in the arteries.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in black pepper tea that help with heart health.

  • Seeds: Black pepper seeds are rich in piperine, a compound that enhances bioavailability of nutrients, supporting overall cardiovascular health.
  • Stems: Pepper plant stems contain flavonoids and alkaloids, known to improve blood circulation, contributing to a healthy heart.
  • Leaves: The leaves of black pepper plants have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, helping to protect the heart from damage and disease.
  • Flowers: Pepper plant flowers possess vital minerals and vitamins, aiding in maintaining a healthy heart through balanced nutrition.
  • Rhyzomes: Black pepper rhyzomes are a potent source of vital nutrients and piperine, supporting heart health by promoting blood vessel dilation.

10. Valerian

Valerian tea contains antioxidants, particularly flavonoids and volatile oils, that protect the heart by neutralizing free radicals.

This tea also offers anti-inflammatory properties, which help prevent cardiovascular diseases by reducing inflammation in the blood vessels and heart. Moreover, valerian tea promotes relaxation and reduces stress, which indirectly benefits heart health as chronic stress contributes to high blood pressure and heart disease. Additionally, valerian tea supports healthy blood pressure levels, as it contains compounds that dilate blood vessels and improve circulation.

Lastly, this herbal tea aids in reducing harmful cholesterol levels, subsequently lowering the risk of heart disease.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in valerian tea that help with heart health.

  • Roots: The roots of valerian contain active compounds that help relax blood vessels, promoting heart health.
  • Rhizomes: Valerian's rhizomes are rich in antioxidants that support heart health by reducing oxidative stress.
  • Flowers: Valerian flowers contain volatile oils that have a calming effect on the body, potentially benefiting heart health by reducing stress.
