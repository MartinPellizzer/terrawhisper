10 Best Herbal Teas For Esophagus

Herbal teas, especially chamomile and slippery elm, can help soothe inflammation in the esophagus due to their anti-inflammatory properties.

The warm temperature of the tea can also help to relax the muscles in the esophagus, reducing discomfort. Some herbal teas, such as marshmallow root, contain mucilage, which can form a protective coating on the esophageal lining, helping to reduce irritation. Additionally, herbal teas can help increase saliva production and promote overall digestion, which can alleviate symptoms of esophageal discomfort.

However, it is important to note that while herbal teas can provide relief for minor esophageal issues, they should not be used as a substitute for medical treatment for serious conditions.

1. Marshmallow

Marshmallow root tea helps with esophagus because Marshmallow root tea helps with esophageal health due to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

The tea, made from the marshmallow plant's roots, contains mucilage that forms a protective layer on the esophageal lining, reducing irritation and inflammation. This protective barrier can alleviate symptoms of gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD) and other conditions causing esophageal discomfort. Furthermore, marshmallow root tea may help heal esophageal tissue damage by promoting mucous membrane regeneration.

Its demulcent qualities make it an effective natural remedy for soothing and supporting overall esophageal health.

The following procedure explaing how to make marshmallow tea for esophagus.

  1. To make marshmallow root tea for esophagus, bring 1 cup of water to a boil, then add 2-3 teaspoons of dried marshmallow root and let it steep for 5-10 minutes, covered.
  2. After steeping, strain the tea to remove the marshmallow root pieces and allow it to cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  3. For optimal results, sip the marshmallow root tea slowly, allowing it to coat the esophagus as you drink.
  4. Drink 2-3 cups of marshmallow root tea daily, preferably between meals, for at least a week or as directed by a healthcare professional.
  5. Store any leftover tea in the refrigerator for up to 48 hours, reheating it gently before drinking.

Slippery elm bark tea helps with esophagus because Slippery elm bark tea helps with esophageal health due to its demulcent and emollient properties, which allow it to create a soothing film over mucous membranes.

The following procedure explaing how to make slippery elm tea for esophagus.

  3. Strain the tea through a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth to remove any bark particles, pouring it into a mug or cup.

4. Chamomile

Chamomile flowers tea helps with esophagus because Chamomile flowers tea helps with esophageal discomfort due to its natural anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, which can alleviate irritation and spasms in the esophagus.

The following procedure explaing how to make chamomile tea for esophagus.

  1. Begin by boiling 8 ounces of fresh water in a saucepan or kettle, then let it cool down to around 200°F (93°C) to preserve the chamomile's beneficial properties; meanwhile, measure out 2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers or 1 tea bag.
  2. Place the dried chamomile flowers or tea bag in a teapot or a large mug; if using loose flowers, consider using a tea ball or infuser for easy removal.
  3. Pour the hot water (around 200°F or 93°C) over the chamomile flowers or tea bag, ensuring they are fully submerged.
  4. Allow the chamomile flowers or tea bag to steep for 5 to 10 minutes, depending on your desired strength; the longer the steep, the stronger the flavor and potential benefits.
  5. Remove the tea bag or strain out the loose flowers, then gently press on the chamomile to release any remaining liquid and essential oils; serve the chamomile tea warm and enjoy its soothing properties for your esophagus.

The following procedure explaing how to make plantain tea for esophagus.

  1. Obtain 3-4 fresh plantain leaves, rinse them thoroughly, and cut into small pieces, then add 4 cups of water in a pot and bring it to a boil.
  2. Add the plantain leaf pieces to the boiling water, then reduce heat to low, cover the pot, and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  3. After simmering, remove the pot from heat and let it cool down for about 10 minutes, then strain the tea into a teapot or a container with a spout.
  4. Pour the plantain leaf tea into a cup, add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, and stir well.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, up to three times a day, for soothing relief in the esophagus.

The following procedure explaing how to make feverfew tea for esophagus.

  1. To make feverfew leaves tea for soothing the esophagus, start by boiling 2 cups of water in a teapot or saucepan (use enough water to cover the amount of feverfew leaves you plan to use).

  5. Pour the feverfew tea into the pre-heated teacup and enjoy it while it's still warm, up to three times a day as needed for soothing your esophagus.
