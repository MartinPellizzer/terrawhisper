10 best herbal teas for mucus in stomach

If you're experiencing mucus in your stomach, certain herbal teas can provide relief.

Slippery elm tea is known for its soothing properties, as it coats the stomach and esophagus, helping to reduce mucus production. Additionally, licorice root tea can help decrease inflammation and mucus, while also providing a calming effect on the stomach lining.

Lastly, marshmallow root tea is another excellent option, as it forms a protective barrier against stomach acids, reducing irritation and mucus. When selecting these herbal teas, opt for organic and high-quality options to ensure maximum benefits.

However, it's always best to consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new herbal remedies.

1. Licorice

Licorice tea aids in reducing mucus in the stomach due to its active compounds, such as glycyrrhizin, which possess anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds work by reducing inflammation in the stomach lining, thereby decreasing mucus production. Moreover, licorice tea stimulates the production of mucus in the gastrointestinal tract, which helps to replace and restore the mucus lining that has been broken down by stomach acid. Consequently, this process helps to alleviate irritation and discomfort caused by excessive mucus.

Additionally, licorice tea boasts expectorant qualities, facilitating the expulsion of mucus from the stomach and promoting overall digestive health.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in licorice tea that help with mucus in stomach.

  • Roots: Rich in glycyrrhizin, a compound with anti-inflammatory properties that helps soothe mucous membranes in the stomach.
  • Rhizomes: Contain compounds like liquiritin and isoliquiritigenin, known to reduce inflammation and alleviate gastric mucosal lesions.
  • Leaves: Source of flavonoids that have been shown to help protect the gastric mucosa and reduce the production of excess mucus in the stomach.
  • Flowers: Contain various antioxidants, including phenolic acids and flavonoids, which may help reduce inflammation in the stomach and support mucosal health.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic licorice tea for mucus in stomach.

  1. In a small pot, combine 1 cup of water and 1 tablespoon of dried licorice root.
  2. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove licorice root pieces.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Drink the tea while it's still warm, up to three times a day.

2. Marshmallow

Marshmallow tea, derived from the Althaea officinalis plant, contains mucilage that soothes and protects the lining of the stomach.

This mucilage creates a barrier between the stomach lining and mucus, reducing irritation. Consequently, the stomach produces less mucus. Moreover, the tea's antioxidant properties further help to alleviate inflammation and mucus buildup.

Ultimately, this tea offers a natural and effective solution to manage and reduce mucus in the stomach.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in marshmallow tea that help with mucus in stomach.

  • Roots: The marshmallow root contains mucilage that soothes and protects the stomach lining, providing relief from mucus.
  • Leaves: The leaves of the marshmallow plant also contain mucilage, which helps to coat and soothe the stomach, reducing irritation.
  • Flowers: Marshmallow flowers possess mucilaginous properties that help to alleviate inflammation and mucus in the stomach.
  • Seeds: The seeds of the marshmallow plant are rich in mucilage, which forms a protective layer in the stomach, reducing discomfort caused by mucus.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic marshmallow tea for mucus in stomach.

  1. Measure out 1 tablespoon of dried marshmallow root and place it in a tea infuser or cheesecloth.
  2. Boil 8 ounces of water and let it cool for 1-2 minutes.
  3. Pour the hot water over the marshmallow root and steep for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or cheesecloth and discard the used marshmallow root.
  5. Drink the marshmallow tea tea while it is still warm, up to three times a day.

3. Slippery Elm

Slippery elm tea provides immediate relief from mucus in the stomach because it contains mucilage, a substance that becomes a slick gel when mixed with water.

This gel creates a protective barrier in the stomach, shielding it from irritants and reducing inflammation. Consequently, slippery elm tea alleviates discomfort and enables the stomach to heal. Furthermore, this natural remedy stimulates the production of more natural mucus in the gastrointestinal tract, reinforcing the stomach's defensive barrier and facilitating the expulsion of excess mucus.

Ultimately, slippery elm tea offers an effective, dependable solution for managing mucus in the stomach.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in slippery elm tea that help with mucus in stomach.

  • Bark: The inner bark of slippery elm contains mucilage, a substance that becomes a slick gel when mixed with water. This gel coats the mouth, throat, and stomach, providing relief from mucus and irritation.
  • Leaves: The leaves of slippery elm also contain mucilage. They are often used in tea to soothe mucous membranes and promote expulsion of mucus from the stomach.
  • Roots: The roots of slippery elm contain mucilage and have similar properties to the bark and leaves, making them useful for soothing mucous membranes and providing relief from irritation.
  • Seeds: The seeds of slippery elm contain a small amount of mucilage, which provides a soothing effect, although they are less commonly used than the bark, leaves, and roots.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic slippery elm tea for mucus in stomach.

  1. Measure - Scoop 1 teaspoon of dried slippery elm bark into a tea infuser or loose into a teapot.
  2. Boil - Bring 1 cup of water to a boil in a kettle or pot.
  3. Steep - Pour the hot water over the slippery elm bark and let it steep for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Strain - Remove the tea infuser or strain out the loose bark before pouring the tea into a cup.
  5. Drink - Sip the slippery elm tea, ideally between meals, to soothe and coat the stomach lining.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic thyme tea for mucus in stomach.

  1. In a small pot, boil 1 cup of water for 3-5 minutes.
  2. Add 1 teaspoon of dried thyme leaves to the boiling water, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 5 minutes.
  3. Remove pot from heat, cover and let the tea steep for an additional 10 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea to remove the thyme leaves and discard them.
  5. Drink the thyme tea, up to three times a day, to help soothe a mucus-filled stomach.
