10 Best Herbal Teas For Circulation

Herbal teas, particularly those made from ingredients like ginger, cinnamon, and hawthorn, are known to help improve circulation.

This is because certain herbs contain compounds that can help dilate blood vessels, increase blood flow, and strengthen heart function. Ginger, for example, has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in blood vessels, improving circulation.

Cinnamon contains antioxidants that can help improve blood flow and lower blood pressure, while hawthorn can help strengthen the heart muscle and widen blood vessels. Additionally, many herbal teas contain high levels of antioxidants, which can help protect the cardiovascular system and improve overall circulation.

However, it's important to note that while herbal teas can support healthy circulation, they should not be used as a substitute for medical treatment for conditions related to poor circulation.

1. Ginger

Ginger tea helps with circulation because it contains compounds that stimulate blood flow, such as gingerols and shgaols.

These active ingredients increase the dilation of blood vessels, allowing for improved oxygenation and nutrient delivery to tissues. Additionally, ginger tea has anti-inflammatory properties which can reduce inflammation in the body, leading to better circulation. Furthermore, it helps prevent the formation of blood clots by inhibiting platelet aggregation, ensuring smooth blood flow throughout the body.

Lastly, drinking ginger tea regularly can help lower high blood pressure levels and improve overall cardiovascular health, contributing to optimal circulation.

The following procedure explaing how to make ginger tea for circulation.

  1. Peel and thinly slice 1-2 inches of fresh ginger root, then add to a pot with 4 cups of water.
  2. Bring the water to a boil, then reduce heat and let the ginger simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Remove the pot from heat, and stir in 1-2 teaspoons of honey and the juice of half a lemon.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, and add an optional 1/4 teaspoon of cinnamon powder for extra circulation benefits.
  5. Sip and enjoy the warm ginger tea up to three times a day for improved circulation.

2. Turmeric

Turmeric tea helps with circulation because it contains curcumin, a powerful anti-inflammatory compound that reduces inflammation in the blood vessels, leading to improved blood flow.

The antioxidants in turmeric tea help protect the lining of the arteries from damage caused by free radicals, further promoting healthy circulation. Additionally, turmeric tea has been shown to thin the blood, reducing the risk of clots that can obstruct circulation. Furthermore, the warming properties of turmeric tea can increase body temperature and dilate blood vessels, allowing for better delivery of oxygen and nutrients throughout the body.

Regular consumption of turmeric tea is an effective natural remedy to support overall cardiovascular health and maintain optimal circulation.

The following procedure explaing how to make turmeric tea for circulation.

  1. Boil 4 cups of water in a saucepan over medium heat.
  2. Stir in 1-2 teaspoons of ground turmeric and let it simmer for 5-10 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea into a cup, then stir in 1 tablespoon of honey and a squeeze of lemon juice for flavor (optional).
  4. Drink the tea while it's still warm, up to twice a day.
  5. Store any leftover tea in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.

3. Garlic

Garlic tea helps with circulation because it contains allicin, a compound found in garlic that acts as a blood thinner and reduces the stickiness of platelets.

This can help prevent clots from forming in the arteries and veins, improving blood flow to various parts of the body. Additionally, garlic has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the blood vessels, further enhancing circulation. The tea also contains antioxidants that protect the lining of the blood vessels from damage caused by free radicals, ensuring optimal circulation.

Drinking garlic tea regularly can lead to improved cardiovascular health and a reduced risk of conditions related to poor circulation such as varicose veins, deep vein thrombosis, and peripheral artery disease.

The following procedure explaing how to make garlic tea for circulation.

  1. Peel and mince 5-6 cloves of fresh garlic, then let them sit for 10 minutes to activate allicin, a beneficial compound.
  2. In a small saucepan, combine 4 cups of water and the minced garlic.
  3. Bring the garlic-water mixture to a boil over high heat, then reduce heat to low and simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the garlic tea through a fine-mesh sieve into a mug, discarding the garlic solids.
  5. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, and enjoy this immune-boosting and circulatory-supporting tea while warm.

4. Cayenne Pepper

Cayenne pepper tea helps with circulation because of its unique active component, capsaicin.

This compound stimulates the production of nitric oxide, a gas that relaxes blood vessels and improves blood flow. As a result, more oxygenated blood reaches various parts of the body, reducing inflammation and promoting overall health. Cayenne pepper tea also acts as a natural vasodilator, allowing for increased blood flow to the extremities, particularly important for individuals with poor circulation or peripheral artery disease.

Finally, the heat from cayenne pepper stimulates the nervous system, sending signals to increase heart rate and blood pressure, further enhancing circulation.

The following procedure explaing how to make cayenne pepper tea for circulation.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan on the stove.
  2. Stir in 1/4 to 1/2 teaspoon of cayenne pepper powder, depending on your desired spiciness.
  3. Reduce heat and let the mixture simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding any solids.
  5. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, and enjoy while warm.

5. Butcher'S Broom

Butcher's broom tea helps with circulation because it contains rutoside, a potent flavonoid that acts as a vasoconstrictor, narrowing dilated blood vessels and improving blood flow.

Rutoside also prevents leakage of blood from capillary walls, reducing edema and inflammation. The tea is commonly used to treat varicose veins and chronic venous insufficiency, conditions characterized by poor circulation in the legs.

Additionally, butcher's broom tea may help reduce the risk of thrombosis by inhibiting platelet aggregation, making it a valuable addition to a circulatory health regimen.

The following procedure explaing how to make butcher's broom tea for circulation.

  1. Boil 2 cups of water in a pot and add 1-2 teaspoons of dried butcher's broom root.
  2. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the solid particles.
  4. Optionally, add honey or lemon for taste.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, once or twice a day, for improved circulation.

6. Horse Chestnut

Horse chestnut tea helps with circulation because it contains aescin, which is a potent antioxidant that protects the inner lining of blood vessels from damage caused by free radicals.

Aescin also has anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and improve blood flow in veins. Horse chestnut tea has been used traditionally for centuries to treat varicose veins, hemorrhoids, and other circulatory issues due to its ability to strengthen capillary walls and promote healthy blood vessel function. Additionally, the tea contains flavonoids that help prevent platelet aggregation and reduce clotting, further improving circulation throughout the body.

Regular consumption of horse chestnut tea can lead to improved overall cardiovascular health and reduced symptoms of circulatory disorders.

The following procedure explaing how to make horse chestnut tea for circulation.

  1. Gather 1-2 handfuls of horse chestnut seeds (equivalent to 10-15 seeds) and rinse them thoroughly to remove any dirt or debris.
  2. Crush or grind the horse chestnuts to release their active compounds, then place them in a pot and add 1 quart (4 cups) of water.
  3. Slowly bring the water to a simmer over medium-low heat, allowing the mixture to gently cook for 15-20 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea through a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth to remove any solid residue, retaining only the liquid.
  5. Let the tea cool down to a drinkable temperature, then enjoy 1 cup of the prepared horse chestnut tea up to three times a day for improved circulation.

7. Red Clover

Red clover tea helps with circulation because it is rich in salicylates, a natural compound similar to aspirin, which thins the blood and reduces inflammation, thereby allowing for improved blood flow.

Additionally, red clover contains bioflavonoids, which help strengthen capillary walls, reducing leakage and swelling, further enhancing circulation. The tea's antioxidant properties also protect against damage to blood vessels, preventing plaque build-up and maintaining healthy arteries. Regular consumption of red clover tea can lead to increased energy levels and a warmer sensation in the extremities due to improved blood flow.

Overall, red clover tea's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties make it an effective natural remedy for boosting circulation and promoting cardiovascular health.

The following procedure explaing how to make red clover tea for circulation.

  1. Boil 2 cups of water in a pot and add 1-2 teaspoons of dried red clover flowers.
  2. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes to allow the nutrients to infuse into the water.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the red clover flowers, leaving only the liquid.
  4. Let the tea cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Pour the tea into a cup and enjoy this natural circulation booster up to three times a day.

8. Parsley

Parsley tea helps with circulation because it contains high levels of vitamin K, which is essential for the production and maintenance of healthy blood vessels.

The tea also includes flavonoids like apigenin and luteolin, which have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and improve blood flow. Additionally, parsley tea has diuretic effects, helping to eliminate excess fluid from the body and reduce pressure on the circulatory system. Furthermore, the tea's antioxidant content helps protect the lining of blood vessels from damage caused by free radicals, promoting better overall circulation.

Regular consumption of parsley tea can lead to improved cardiovascular health and reduced risk of circulation-related conditions like varicose veins and deep vein thrombosis.

The following procedure explaing how to make parsley tea for circulation.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan on the stove.
  2. Add 1 teaspoon of dried parsley or 1 tablespoon of fresh parsley to the boiling water.
  3. Reduce heat to a simmer, cover the saucepan, and let the parsley steep for 10 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the parsley, and let it cool to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Enjoy your homemade parsley tea for circulation up to three times a day.

9. Hawthorn Berry

Hawthorn berry tea helps with circulation because it contains bioflavonoids and proanthocyanidins, which are antioxidants that strengthen the capillary walls and improve blood flow.

The astringent properties of hawthorn also help to tone and tighten the vessels, reducing swelling and inflammation. Additionally, hawthorn tea has been shown to lower blood pressure and reduce heart rate, both of which contribute to improved circulation.

Lastly, regular consumption of hawthorn berry tea may help prevent plaque buildup in arteries, reducing the risk of cardiovascular diseases that can impair circulation.

The following procedure explaing how to make hawthorn berry tea for circulation.

  1. Gather 1-2 teaspoons of dried hawthorn berries and place them in a tea infuser or teapot with a strainer.
  2. Boil 8 ounces of fresh water and pour it over the hawthorn berries in the teapot or teacup.
  3. Allow the tea to steep for 10-15 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds and achieve optimal flavor.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strain the tea to discard the spent berries.
  5. Enjoy the hawthorn berry tea while it's still warm, up to three times a day, to help improve circulation.

10. Bilberry

Bilberry tea helps with circulation because it contains anthocyanins, powerful antioxidants that protect the lining of blood vessels from damage caused by free radicals.

This damage can lead to inflammation and narrowing of the vessels, which impairs blood flow. Bilberry tea also improves the elasticity of blood vessel walls, allowing them to expand and contract more efficiently, leading to improved circulation. Additionally, bilberry tea has been shown to reduce platelet aggregation, a key factor in clot formation, further promoting good blood flow.

Lastly, regular consumption of bilberry tea may help lower blood pressure, another important aspect of maintaining healthy circulation.

The following procedure explaing how to make bilberry tea for circulation.

  1. Boil 1 cup of fresh water and let it cool down to 200°F (93°C), then pour it over 1 teaspoon of dried bilberry leaves.
  2. Allow the mixture to steep for 10 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds from the bilberry leaves.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the leaves, ensuring a clear and smooth liquid.
  4. For added circulation benefits, squeeze half a lemon and add 1 teaspoon of honey into the tea.
  5. Stir well and enjoy your warm bilberry tea, ideally in the morning or after meals.
