10 Best Herbal Teas For High Blood Pressure

Herbal teas, particularly those made from hibiscus, basil, and ginger, have been found to help lower high blood pressure.

This is because these herbs contain compounds that relax blood vessels, improving blood flow and reducing pressure on artery walls. Additionally, some herbal teas have diuretic properties that help the body eliminate excess sodium and fluids, further reducing blood pressure.

Furthermore, these teas are rich in antioxidants that protect the heart and blood vessels from damage, contributing to overall cardiovascular health. However, it's important to note that while herbal teas can be a helpful complement to a healthy lifestyle, they should not replace prescribed medication or medical advice for managing high blood pressure.

Lastly, it's crucial to consult with a healthcare provider before incorporating herbal teas into a high blood pressure treatment plan, as some herbs can interact with medications or exacerbate certain medical conditions.

1. Hawthorn Berry

Hawthorn berry tea helps with high blood pressure because it contains bioactive compounds, such as flavonoids and oligomeric procyanidins, which have been shown to dilate blood vessels, improving blood flow and reducing the workload on the heart.

These natural compounds also have a positive effect on the endothelial cells lining the blood vessels, helping them function more efficiently and reducing inflammation that can contribute to high blood pressure. Furthermore, hawthorn berry tea has been shown in clinical studies to lower both systolic and diastolic blood pressure, making it a natural and effective alternative or complementary treatment for those with hypertension.

Lastly, the calming effects of drinking hawthorn berry tea may help reduce stress levels, another contributing factor to high blood pressure, providing additional benefits for overall cardiovascular health.

The following procedure explains how to make hawthorn berry tea for high blood pressure.

  1. Boil 1 cup of fresh, clean water in a saucepan or kettle.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried hawthorn berries to a tea infuser or teapot.
  3. Once the water reaches a boil, pour it over the hawthorn berries and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strain the tea to remove the berries.
  5. Drink the tea while it's still warm, up to three times a day for best results in managing high blood pressure.

2. Garlic

Garlic tea helps with high blood pressure because it contains allicin, a compound that dilates blood vessels and improves blood flow.

A study published in the journal 'Plant Foods for Human Nutrition' found that drinking garlic tea daily significantly reduces systolic and diastolic blood pressure in hypertensive individuals. Another study published in the 'European Journal of Clinical Pharmacology' reported that garlic extract, which is present in garlic tea, inhibits the production of angiotensin II, a hormone that constricts blood vessels and raises blood pressure. Furthermore, garlic tea has anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce inflammation in the cardiovascular system, contributing to lower blood pressure.

Regular consumption of garlic tea may also improve cholesterol levels, which can further contribute to better heart health and reduced risk of high blood pressure.

The following procedure explains how to make garlic tea for high blood pressure.

  1. Peel and mince 5-6 cloves of fresh garlic, then let it sit for 10 minutes to activate allicin, a compound that helps lower blood pressure.
  2. Bring 4 cups of water to a boil in a saucepan, then add the minced garlic.
  3. Reduce heat to low and let the garlic simmer for about 15-20 minutes to extract its beneficial compounds.
  4. Strain the garlic tea into a cup, then add a squeeze of lemon juice and a drizzle of honey for flavor, if desired.
  5. Drink the garlic tea while warm, 1-2 times a day, for at least a month to see improvements in blood pressure.

3. Hibiscus

Hibiscus tea helps with high blood pressure because it contains an abundant amount of polyphenols, particularly anthocyanins, which function as potent vasodilators.

These natural compounds help relax the smooth muscles surrounding blood vessels, allowing them to widen and increase blood flow. As a result, hibiscus tea can lower both systolic and diastolic blood pressure levels, reducing the risk of heart-related diseases in individuals with hypertension. A study published in the Journal of Nutrition found that consuming three cups of hibiscus tea daily for six weeks led to significant reductions in both systolic and diastolic blood pressure.

Additionally, hibiscus tea contains no caffeine or tannins, making it a safe and effective alternative to traditional medications for managing high blood pressure.

The following procedure explains how to make hibiscus tea for high blood pressure.

  1. Boil 4 cups of water in a pot, then add 2 tablespoons of dried hibiscus flowers and let it steep for 15 minutes.
  2. Strain the liquid to remove the hibiscus flowers and set aside the hibiscus tea.
  3. In a separate pot, heat 1 cup of water and dissolve 1 tablespoon of raw honey.
  4. Combine the honey solution with the hibiscus tea and mix well.
  5. Serve the hibiscus tea warm or refrigerate and drink throughout the day for best results.

4. Celery Seed

Celery seed tea helps with high blood pressure because it contains several beneficial compounds, such as phthalide and linalool.

These compounds help relax the smooth muscles surrounding the arteries, thus decreasing the resistance to blood flow. This reduction in resistance leads to a decrease in blood pressure, which is important for individuals with hypertension.

Additionally, celery seed tea has diuretic properties that can help eliminate excess sodium and water from the body, further contributing to lowering blood pressure. Regular consumption of celery seed tea may also reduce inflammation, another factor that can contribute to high blood pressure.

Overall, celery seed tea is a natural and effective way to help manage high blood pressure by promoting relaxation of arterial muscles, reducing resistance to blood flow, acting as a diuretic, and decreasing inflammation.

The following procedure explains how to make celery seed tea for high blood pressure.

  1. Boil 1 teaspoon of celery seeds in 1 cup of water for 10 minutes in a covered saucepan.
  2. Allow the mixture to steep for an additional 10 minutes after removing from heat.
  3. Strain the liquid to remove the celery seeds.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, up to three times a day for best results.

5. Lemongrass

Lemongrass tea helps with high blood pressure because it contains several essential oils, including citral and geraniol, which have been shown to relax blood vessels and improve circulation.

The antioxidants in lemongrass tea, such as polyphenols and flavonoids, help reduce inflammation in the body, which is a common cause of high blood pressure. Additionally, lemongrass tea has been found to lower LDL or "bad" cholesterol levels, which is a major risk factor for heart disease and high blood pressure. Furthermore, lemongrass tea contains potassium, a mineral that helps regulate fluid balance in the body and maintain healthy blood pressure levels.

Regular consumption of lemongrass tea may also help reduce stress levels, as its calming properties have been proven to help alleviate anxiety and tension.

The following procedure explains how to make lemongrass tea for high blood pressure.

  1. Gather 1-2 stalks of fresh lemongrass, 1 teaspoon of honey, and 8 ounces of water.
  2. Rinse the lemongrass stalks thoroughly, then bruise them with a rolling pin or the back of a knife to release their essential oils.
  3. Bring the water to a boil in a small saucepan, then add the bruised lemongrass stalks and reduce heat to a simmer.
  4. Allow the lemongrass to steep in the hot water for 10-15 minutes, then strain out the solids and discard.
  5. Stir in the honey to taste, then enjoy your soothing lemongrass tea. Drink 2-3 times a day for best results in managing high blood pressure.

6. Oat Straw

Oat straw tea helps with high blood pressure because it contains essential minerals, such as calcium and magnesium, which contribute to maintaining healthy blood vessels.

The tea's rich fiber content promotes cardiovascular health by reducing the buildup of plaque in arteries, thereby decreasing strain on the heart and lowering blood pressure. Furthermore, oat straw tea has been shown to have a calming effect on the nervous system, which can help alleviate stress-related high blood pressure. Additionally, the tea's antioxidant properties protect against oxidative damage to blood vessels, reducing inflammation and improving overall cardiovascular health.

Regular consumption of oat straw tea may lead to significant improvements in blood pressure levels over time.

The following procedure explains how to make oat straw tea for high blood pressure.

  1. Boil 2 cups of water in a pot and add 2 tablespoons of dried oat straw.
  2. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the oat straw and allow it to cool.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Drink 2-3 cups of oat straw tea daily for best results in managing high blood pressure.

7. Passionflower

Passionflower tea helps with high blood pressure because of its unique combination of natural compounds, including flavonoids and alkaloids, which have been shown to relax the smooth muscles surrounding blood vessels and improve blood flow.

Additionally, passionflower tea contains a substance called harmine, which acts as a vasodilator, expanding blood vessels and reducing resistance in the circulatory system. Furthermore, the calming effects of passionflower on the nervous system can help reduce stress-related issues contributing to high blood pressure, such as anxiety and insomnia.

Lastly, several studies have demonstrated the hypotensive properties of passionflower tea, showing significant reductions in both systolic and diastolic blood pressure when consumed regularly.

The following procedure explains how to make passionflower tea for high blood pressure.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan on medium heat.
  2. Add 1 teaspoon of dried passionflower to the boiling water.
  3. Reduce heat to low, cover the saucepan, and let it simmer for 10 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the passionflower solids.
  5. Drink the passionflower tea while it's still warm, 1-2 times a day for high blood pressure management.

8. Ginger Root

Ginger root tea helps with high blood pressure because it contains gingerol, a compound that has been shown to relax blood vessels and improve blood flow, which can help reduce blood pressure.

Additionally, ginger has anti-inflammatory properties that may help prevent the formation of plaque in the arteries, reducing the risk of high blood pressure. Ginger may also help lower cholesterol levels, which can contribute to high blood pressure. Furthermore, ginger has been found to reduce the stickiness of blood platelets, preventing the formation of blood clots that can lead to blockages and increased blood pressure.

Finally, ginger root tea is a natural and caffeine-free alternative to other beverages that may contain sugars and other additives that can negatively impact blood pressure.

The following procedure explains how to make ginger root tea for high blood pressure.

  1. Peel and thinly slice 1-2 inches of fresh ginger root (about 5-10 slices), then add it to a teapot or saucepan.
  2. Pour 4 cups of water into the teapot or saucepan, then bring it to a boil over high heat.
  3. Once boiling, reduce heat to low, cover, and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes to allow the ginger to release its beneficial compounds.
  4. Strain the ginger tea into a cup, discarding the ginger pieces, and let it cool down for a few minutes before adding honey or lemon to taste (optional).
  5. Drink the ginger tea while warm, 1-2 times a day, preferably between meals, to help manage high blood pressure. Enjoy!

9. Turmeric

Turmeric tea helps with high blood pressure because of its active compound, curcumin, which has anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

These properties help reduce the inflammation in the blood vessels, allowing them to relax and widen, leading to improved blood flow and lower blood pressure. Additionally, turmeric tea may help regulate heart rate and improve endothelial function, which is essential for maintaining healthy blood vessels. Furthermore, regular consumption of turmeric tea has been shown in studies to have a positive effect on reducing systolic and diastolic blood pressure levels over time.

Therefore, incorporating turmeric tea into your daily routine may be a natural and effective way to help manage high blood pressure.

The following procedure explains how to make turmeric tea for high blood pressure.

  1. Boil 4 cups of water in a saucepan over medium heat.
  2. Stir in 1 teaspoon of ground turmeric and 1/2 teaspoon of grated ginger, reducing heat to low and simmering for 10 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the solids.
  4. Stir in 1 teaspoon of honey and 1/2 lemon juice, mixing until the honey is dissolved.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, up to 3 times a day for best results.

10. Basil

Basil tea helps with high blood pressure because it contains essential oils, such as linalool and eucalyptol, which have been shown to relax blood vessels and improve blood flow.

Additionally, the tea is rich in potassium, a mineral that plays a crucial role in regulating fluid balance and maintaining healthy blood pressure levels. The antioxidants found in basil tea, like rosmarinic acid, also help reduce inflammation and prevent damage to blood vessels, further contributing to its blood pressure-lowering effects. Drinking basil tea regularly may even help lower cholesterol levels, which is another risk factor for high blood pressure.

Overall, the combination of these properties makes basil tea an effective natural remedy for managing high blood pressure.

The following procedure explains how to make basil tea for high blood pressure.

  1. Boil 4 cups of water in a saucepan over medium heat.
  2. Add 2 tablespoons of fresh basil leaves (or 1 tablespoon of dried basil) to the boiling water.
  3. Reduce heat to low, cover the saucepan, and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a teapot or directly into a cup, then let it cool down for 2-3 minutes.
  5. Stir in 1 teaspoon of honey (optional) for added flavor and drink the tea while it's still warm, 1-2 times a day for best results.
