10 best herbal teas for gut health

Supporting gut health is essential for overall well-being, and certain herbal teas can be beneficial in maintaining a healthy gut.

Peppermint tea, for instance, is known to soothe digestive discomfort and reduce inflammation in the gut. Ginger tea is another excellent choice, as it aids in digestion, reduces bloating, and alleviates nausea.

Chamomile tea can help relax the digestive system, reducing inflammation and promoting healthy gut bacteria. Lastly, licorice root tea can soothe stomach ulcers and support the gut lining, while also boasting antibacterial properties.

When incorporating herbal teas into your daily routine, it's crucial to consult with a healthcare professional or nutritionist to ensure they complement your overall health goals and dietary needs.

1. Peppermint

Peppermint tea aids gut health due to its active compounds, particularly menthol, which boast antispasmodic properties.

These properties relieve gastrointestinal discomfort by relaxing the gastrointestinal muscles, thereby alleviating symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome such as bloating and abdominal pain. Moreover, peppermint oil, the essential oil of peppermint leaves, exhibits carminative effects, reducing gas buildup in the gut and easing digestion.

Furthermore, it combats inflammation in the gut lining, enhancing nutrient absorption and maintaining gut integrity. In addition, peppermint tea's antibacterial qualities target and eliminate harmful gut bacteria, fostering a balanced gut microbiome.

Therefore, peppermint tea is an efficacious natural remedy for promoting gut health.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in peppermint tea that help with gut health.

  • Leaves: Rich in volatile oils, the leaves of peppermint plant are widely used to alleviate digestive problems due to their antispasmodic properties.
  • Stems: Similar to leaves, stems of peppermint plant contain menthol that soothes the gastrointestinal tract and provides relief from symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome.
  • Flowers: Packed with flavonoids and phenolic acids, peppermint flowers help reduce inflammation in the gut, making it beneficial for gut health.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic peppermint tea for gut health.

  1. Measure - Boil 1 cup of water and let 1 peppermint tea bag steep for 5-10 minutes.
  2. Select - Choose a tea bag that contains pure peppermint leaves, without any added flavors or sweeteners.
  3. Prepare - Warm up a mug by rinsing it with hot water.
  4. Steep - After boiling, let the water cool slightly before pouring it over the tea bag in the mug.
  5. Serve - Discard the tea bag and enjoy the hot peppermint tea, which can help soothe the gut and aid in digestion.

2. Ginger

Ginger tea aids gut health due to its proven carminative and intestinal spasmolytic properties, which mean it soothes the intestines and reduces gas accumulation.

This herbal tea also boasts significant levels of gingerols and shaogals, compounds known to stimulate digestion and absorption, thereby alleviating digestive discomfort. Furthermore, ginger tea's anti-inflammatory properties help reduce inflammation in the gut lining, promoting overall gut health. Additionally, ginger tea facilitates gut motility, allowing food to move through the GI tract smoothly, preventing symptoms like bloating, cramps, and indigestion.

Lastly, consistent ginger tea consumption inhibits the growth of harmful bacteria in the gut, further contributing to a balanced gut microbiome.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in ginger tea that help with gut health.

  • Rhizomes: Ginger's thick, underground stem or rhizome is widely used in medicinal teas for gut health due to its carminative properties that help break up and expel intestinal gas, thereby alleviating symptoms of gastric discomfort.
  • Roots: The fibrous, spindle-shaped roots of ginger contain bioactive compounds like gingerols and shogaols, which are known to stimulate digestion and soothe irritation in the gastrointestinal tract, making them an ideal choice for gut-health teas.
  • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants and anti-inflammatory agents, ginger leaves can also be used to produce teas that support gut health by reducing inflammation in the gastrointestinal tract and promoting the elimination of harmful toxins from the body.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginger tea for gut health.

  1. Peel and chop 1-2 inches of fresh ginger root into small pieces, then place in a pot.
  2. Pour 4 cups of water into the pot and bring to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat and let simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the ginger pieces from the water, then add the liquid to a mug.
  5. Stir in 1-2 teaspoons of raw honey and squeeze half a lemon for added gut health benefits, enjoy!

3. Fennel

Fennel tea aids gut health through its carminative properties, which effectively alleviate bloating and gas.

This tea's rich antioxidant content, including quercetin and vitamin C, reduces inflammation in the gut, promoting overall digestive health. Furthermore, fennel tea's antispasmodic agents soothe the gastrointestinal tract, easing discomfort from cramps or spasms. Additionally, fennel seeds contain volatile oils, such as anethole, which stimulate the production of gastric enzymes, enhancing digestion and nutrient absorption.

Finally, the tea's antibacterial properties inhibit the growth of harmful bacteria in the gut, contributing to a balanced microbiome and improved gut health.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in fennel tea that help with gut health.

  • Seeds: Fennel seeds are rich in antioxidants and contain an oil that promotes digestion, making them ideal for gut health.
  • Rhyzomes: The rhizomes of fennel have a carminative effect, reducing gas and bloating, making them a perfect choice for gut health.
  • Leaves: The leaves of fennel are known for their calming effect on the gastrointestinal tract and aid in the production of digestive enzymes.
  • Flowers: Fennel flowers contain high levels of vitamins and minerals that promote gut health, and their anti-inflammatory properties soothe the digestive system.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic fennel tea for gut health.

  1. Chop: Cut the stalks and fronds off one fresh fennel bulb, then chop the bulb into thin slices.
  2. Measure: Pour 4 cups of water into a pot and add 2 tablespoons of fennel slices.
  3. Boil: Bring the water to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 10 minutes.
  4. Strain: Strain the tea into a teapot or mug, discarding the fennel pieces.
  5. Serve: Drink the tea while warm, up to three times a day for gut health benefits.

4. Chamomile

Chamomile tea's anti-inflammatory properties, provided by its volatile oils, including chamazulene and bisabolol, contribute to gut health by reducing inflammation in the digestive tract.

This herbal tea also contains flavonoids, such as apigenin and luteolin, which exhibit antispasmodic effects, helping to relax the smooth muscles of the intestines and alleviating digestive discomfort. Moreover, chamomile tea's soothing effect on the stomach lining stems from its ability to increase the production of mucin, a glycoprotein that forms a protective barrier against acid and enzymes in the gut.

Additionally, chamomile tea's mild bitter properties stimulate the release of bile from the liver, promoting healthy digestion and the absorption of nutrients.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in chamomile tea that help with gut health.

  • Flowers: Chamomile flowers contain calming properties that promote gut health by soothing the digestive system.
  • Leaves: Chamomile leaves, when steeped in tea, offer carminative effects that aid in reducing bloating and improving gut comfort.
  • Seeds: Chamomile seeds are rich in antispasmodic compounds that help alleviate cramps and spasms in the gastrointestinal tract.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for gut health.

  1. Measure - Pour 1 cup of fresh water into a saucepan and bring it to a boil.
  2. Steep - Add 1 heaping teaspoon of dried chamomile flowers to a tea infuser and place it in a cup.
  3. Pour - Once the water has boiled, carefully pour it over the chamomile flowers in the cup.
  4. Steep (again) - Allow the chamomile to steep for 5-7 minutes to extract its medicinal compounds.
  5. Strain - Remove the tea infuser and enjoy your homemade chamomile tea for gut health.

5. Licorice

Licorice tea, derived from the root of the Glycyrrhiza glabra plant, offers proven benefits for gut health.

This herbal tea contains active compounds, like glycyrrhizin, which exhibits anti-inflammatory and soothing properties, helping to reduce inflammation in the digestive tract. Moreover, licorice tea supports the integrity of the stomach lining and eases symptoms of heartburn, acid reflux, and indigestion. Additionally, it encourages the production of mucus in the gastrointestinal tract, providing a protective layer that combats stomach acids.

Lastly, licorice tea's antimicrobial qualities help maintain a healthy balance of gut flora, further promoting optimal gut performance.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in licorice tea that help with gut health.

  • Roots: Rich in glycyrrhizin, a compound with anti-inflammatory properties that supports gut health.
  • Rhizomes: Packed with flavonoids, they help reduce gut inflammation and promote digestion.
  • Leaves: Contain isoliquiritigenin, a plant compound known to inhibit growth of harmful gut bacteria.
  • Seeds: Abundant in antioxidants and fiber, they encourage regular bowel movements and gut bacteria diversity.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic licorice tea for gut health.

  1. In a pot, combine 1 cup of chopped licorice root with 8 cups of water.
  2. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  3. Strain the liquid to remove the licorice root pieces.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, up to three times a day for gut health.

6. Marshmallow

Marshmallow tea, derived from the marshmallow plant's roots, contains a substance called mucilage.

This mucilage turns into a gel-like consistency when mixed with water, forming a protective layer in the digestive tract. This coating soothes and cushions the gut lining, reducing inflammation and promoting a healthy gut environment. Consequently, marshmallow tea alleviates digestive issues such as leaky gut syndrome, ulcers, and acid reflux.

Furthermore, it encourages the growth of beneficial gut bacteria, reinforcing the immune system and enhancing nutrient absorption.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in marshmallow tea that help with gut health.

  • Leaves: Rich in flavonoids and phenolic acids, marshmallow leaves aid gut health by reducing inflammation.
  • Flowers: Soothing mucilage in marshmallow flowers coats the digestive tract, easing irritation and supporting gut health.
  • Roots: High in mucilage, marshmallow roots create a protective film in the gut, promoting healing and alleviating symptoms of gut disorders.
  • Seeds: Loaded with antioxidants, marshmallow seeds offer gut-soothing benefits and support overall digestive health.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic marshmallow tea for gut health.

  1. In a saucepan, combine 2 cups of water, 1 tablespoon of dried marshmallow root, and 1 cinnamon stick; simmer for 15 minutes to extract flavors.
  2. Turn off heat, add 1 tablespoon of sliced dried orange peel and 1 teaspoon of grated ginger, then steep for 10 minutes to allow ingredients to meld.
  3. Strain the tea through a fine-mesh sieve into a teapot or heatproof pitcher, discarding solids.
  4. Stir in 1-2 teaspoons of raw honey to taste, and squeeze a slice of fresh lemon for added vitamin C and flavor.
  5. Serve hot, Enjoy your soothing marshmallow tea for gut health!

7. Slippery Elm

Slippery elm tea contains mucilage, a substance that turns into a slick gel when mixed with water.

This gel can coat the mouth, throat, stomach, and intestines, creating a smooth and protective barrier. By coating the digestive tract, slippery elm tea can alleviate irritation and soothe inflammation, helping to maintain gut health.

Its demulcent properties also encourage the production of more mucus in the gastrointestinal tract, providing additional protection and lubrication. Furthermore, this herbal tea stimulates the nerve endings in the gastrointestinal tract, promoting the secretion of digestive fluids and aiding in the breakdown of food.

Ultimately, slippery elm tea enhances gut health by safeguarding the digestive tract, reducing inflammation, and supporting the digestive process.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in slippery elm tea that help with gut health.

  • Bark: The bark of the slippery elm tree is rich in mucilage, a substance that becomes a slick gel when mixed with water. This gel coats the digestive tract, soothing inflammation and promoting gut health.
  • Inner Bark: The inner bark holds large quantities of mucilage and tannins. Tannins have astringent properties that can help protect and heal the gut lining.
  • Leaves: Slippery elm leaves also contain mucilage. When brewed as a tea, the mucilage helps to soothe irritation and supports the gut's overall health.
  • Seeds: Slippery elm seeds are a potent source of mucilage. When steeped in hot water, the seeds release this gel-like substance, which can help ease symptoms of gastrointestinal distress by coating the digestive tract.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic slippery elm tea for gut health.

  1. Measure - Fill 1 cup with sliced or powdered slippery elm bark.
  2. Boil - Pour 2 cups of water into a pot and bring to a boil.
  3. Steep - Add slippery elm bark to boiling water, reduce heat, and simmer for 10 minutes.
  4. Strain - Remove from heat, let cool, then strain the tea into a cup.
  5. Serve - Drink the tea while warm, up to 3 times a day for gut health.

8. Senna

Senna tea, derived from the senna plant, contains anthraquinone glycosides that have a laxative effect, promoting bowel movements and alleviating constipation.

This tea stimulates the colon's natural peristaltic action, helping to maintain gut regularity and ease digestion. Its natural properties make it a popular choice for those seeking to relieve irregularity and support gut health. Furthermore, senna tea's ability to cleanse the digestive tract allows for better nutrient absorption, contributing to overall gut health.

Lastly, senna tea's use as a natural laxative spans centuries, attesting to its effectiveness in promoting gut health and regularity.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in senna tea that help with gut health.

  • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants, the leaves of senna aid in maintaining a healthy gut by promoting regular bowel movements.
  • Pods: The dried pods, also known as fruits, of senna contain anthraquinones that help treat constipation and support gut motility.
  • Rhizomes: Underground stem horizontal segments of senna, rhizomes, contain active compounds that work as gentle laxatives, benefiting gut health.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic senna tea for gut health.

  1. Measure: Pour 1 cup of boiling water over 1-2 teaspoons of dried senna leaves.
  2. Steep: Let the mixture steep for 10-15 minutes to allow the senna leaves to release their properties.
  3. Strain: Strain the tea to remove the senna leaves, leaving only the liquid.
  4. Sweeten (optional): Add a small amount of honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Serve: Drink the tea while still warm, preferably at bedtime, to support gut health.

9. Dandelion

Dandelion tea promotes gut health due to its natural abundance in therapeutic compounds.

This infusion contains fiber, which supports the growth of beneficial gut bacteria. Dandelion tea also provides prebiotics that ferment in the gut, enhancing digestion and nutrient absorption. Furthermore, it stimulates the production of stomach acid and bile, optimizing the breakdown of food.

Lastly, dandelion tea boasts anti-inflammatory properties that soothe the gastrointestinal tract, contributing to overall gut health.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in dandelion tea that help with gut health.

  • Roots: Rich in beneficial compounds, the roots of dandelions promote gut health by stimulating digestion and liver function.
  • Leaves: High in antioxidants, dandelion leaves support gut health through their anti-inflammatory properties and ability to soothe digestive issues.
  • Flowers: Packed with prebiotics, dandelion flowers encourage gut health by nourishing beneficial gut bacteria and aiding overall digestion.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic dandelion tea for gut health.

  1. Gather 2-3 dandelion plant heads with intact yellow flowers and unopened buds.
  2. Rinse collected dandelion plants thoroughly, removing any dirt or insects.
  3. Chop dandelion plants, including flowers and stems, then steep in 2 cups of boiling water for 10 minutes.
  4. Strain the steeped dandelion tea into cups, discarding the solid plant material.
  5. Consume the warm dandelion tea up to three times a day for gut health benefits.

10. Skullcap

Skullcap tea, derived from the Scutellaria lateriflora plant, contains antioxidants and anti-inflammatory properties that actively support gut health.

This herbal tea combats oxidative stress in gut cells, promoting their overall function and integrity. Skullcap tea also reduces inflammation in the digestive tract, which encourages a balanced gut environment. Additionally, it relaxes gut muscles, alleviating issues such as cramps and spasms.

Moreover, skullcap tea stimulates the production of digestive enzymes, enhancing nutrient absorption and further contributing to optimal gut health.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in skullcap tea that help with gut health.

  • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants, leaves of skullcap plant support gut health by reducing inflammation in the digestive tract.
  • Flowers: Containing flavonoids and polyphenols, skullcap flowers help improve gut health by promoting digestion and alleviating symptoms of leaky gut.
  • Roots: Known for their anti-spasmodic properties, skullcap roots soothe the gut, providing relief from cramps and spasms in the digestive system.
  • Seeds: Packed with essential oils, skullcap seeds aid gut health by stimulating bile production, which enhances nutrient absorption and fat metabolism.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic skullcap tea for gut health.

  1. In a small pot, combine 1 cup of water and 2 teaspoons of dried skullcap herb, and bring to a boil.
  2. Reduce heat, cover, and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the herbs, and discard the solids.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Drink the tea while it's still warm, up to three times a day for gut health.
