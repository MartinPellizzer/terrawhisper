10 Best Herbal Teas For Gut Health

Herbal teas, made from various parts of plants like leaves, roots, and seeds, can benefit gut health due to their natural anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Some herbal teas, such as chamomile and peppermint, are known to soothe the digestive system, reducing inflammation and promoting regularity. Ginger tea, in particular, can help alleviate symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome and nausea by relaxing the gastrointestinal muscles. Furthermore, some herbal teas, like licorice and marshmallow root, can help repair the gut lining, maintaining the integrity of the gut barrier.

Lastly, the warm temperature of tea promotes blood flow and aids in the absorption of beneficial compounds, ensuring optimal gut health.

1. Peppermint

Peppermint tea helps with gut health because it contains menthol and mentone, compounds with antispasmodic properties that help relax the gastrointestinal muscles, thereby alleviating symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome (IBS) such as abdominal pain, bloating, and gas.

Furthermore, peppermint tea has carminative effects, which facilitate the expulsion of excess gas from the digestive tract, reducing discomfort and promoting overall gut health. Additionally, its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties can help soothe and heal the gastrointestinal lining, providing relief from inflammation and irritation. Peppermint tea also boasts antimicrobial and antiviral qualities, aiding in the prevention of harmful bacterial and viral infections within the gut, while encouraging the growth of beneficial gut flora.

Lastly, peppermint tea's soothing warmth and calming aroma can help reduce stress and anxiety, indirectly contributing to gut health by minimizing the negative impact of stress on gastrointestinal function.

The following procedure explains how to make peppermint tea for gut health.

  1. In a teapot, combine 1 cup of boiling water with 1 heaping teaspoon of dried peppermint leaves, and let it steep for 5-7 minutes for optimal release of gut-soothing compounds.
  2. While the tea is steeping, warm up an additional 2 cups of water to serve with the tea, as staying hydrated is essential for gut health.
  3. Strain the peppermint tea into a mug, discarding the spent leaves, and enjoy it without milk, sugar, or other additives to preserve its gut-friendly properties.
  4. Sip the warm peppermint tea slowly, allowing it to gently soothe your digestive system and alleviate any discomfort.
  5. Drink 2-3 cups of peppermint tea daily, especially after meals, to aid in the digestion process and promote gut health.

2. Ginger

Ginger tea helps with gut health because it contains gingerol, a compound that has anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties which can help reduce inflammation in the digestive tract and improve digestion.

Additionally, ginger tea can help stimulate the production of digestive enzymes, which can help break down food more efficiently and alleviate symptoms of indigestion, such as bloating and gas. Furthermore, ginger tea has been shown to help speed up gastric emptying, which can help relieve symptoms of nausea and vomiting, making it a popular remedy for morning sickness during pregnancy.

Ginger tea may also help protect the gut lining by preventing the growth of harmful bacteria, such as E. coli and Salmonella, while promoting the growth of beneficial bacteria, such as probiotics, which are essential for maintaining a healthy gut microbiome.

Overall, ginger tea's anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties make it a powerful natural remedy for improving gut health and promoting overall digestive wellness.

The following procedure explains how to make ginger tea for gut health.

  1. Peel and finely chop 1 tablespoon of fresh ginger root, and let it steep in hot water for 5 minutes.
  2. In a teapot or saucepan, combine the ginger with 4 cups of water and bring to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat and simmer for 10 minutes to allow the ginger to release its beneficial compounds.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, and add honey and lemon juice to taste, if desired.
  5. Drink up to three cups a day, preferably between meals, for optimal gut health benefits.

3. Fennel

Fennel tea helps with gut health because it contains volatile oils, such as anethole, fenchone, and estragole, which have antispasmodic properties that help relax the gastrointestinal muscles, thereby alleviating symptoms of bloating, gas, and constipation.

Furthermore, fennel's carminative effect reduces the production of gas in the digestive tract, making it easier for food to move through the gut. Additionally, fennel tea has been shown to have antibacterial properties, which can help maintain a healthy balance of gut bacteria and prevent the overgrowth of harmful bacteria. Moreover, fennel tea may help stimulate the production of digestive enzymes, improving nutrient absorption and overall gut function.

Lastly, fennel's anti-inflammatory properties can help soothe and heal the gut lining, reducing inflammation and promoting overall gut health.

The following procedure explains how to make fennel tea for gut health.

  1. Coarsely chop 1 tablespoon of fresh fennel seeds (or 1 teaspoon of crushed fennel seeds) and let them steep in 2 cups of boiling water for 10 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea to remove the fennel solids.
  3. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  4. For best results, drink the tea 2-3 times a day, particularly after meals.
  5. Consume within 24 hours for maximum gut health benefits.

4. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with gut health because it contains antioxidants that work to reduce stress and inflammation in the gut, promoting healthy digestion.

Its calming properties can help to reduce bloating, gas, and other symptoms of indigestion. Furthermore, chamomile tea has been shown to support the growth of beneficial gut bacteria, contributing to a healthy gut microbiome. Additionally, it can help to soothe and heal the gut lining, making it an effective natural remedy for leaky gut syndrome and other digestive disorders.

Drinking chamodeile tea regularly can also stimulate the production of digestive enzymes, ensuring that food is properly broken down and nutrients are efficiently absorbed.

The following procedure explains how to make chamomile tea for gut health.

  1. Heat 4 cups of water to boiling (10 minutes), then steep 4 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers in the water for 5 minutes.
  2. Strain the chamomile flowers from the tea, then stir in 1 teaspoon of raw honey and 1 tablespoon of lemon juice (if desired, for taste).
  3. Optional: Add 1/4 teaspoon of grated ginger for added anti-inflammatory properties.
  4. Let the tea cool to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Sip and enjoy the soothing benefits of chamomile tea for gut health.

5. Licorice

Licorice tea helps with gut health because it contains substances, such as glycyrrhizic acid, which can help soothe and heal the gut lining.

This herbal tea has anti-inflammatory properties that can reduce inflammation in the digestive tract, thereby alleviating symptoms of digestive disorders. Furthermore, licorice tea stimulates the production of mucus in the gastrointestinal tract, providing a protective barrier against stomach acid and other damaging substances. Additionally, it has been shown to increase the mucosal secretion of the gastrointestinal tract, which can help improve overall gut health.

Lastly, licorice tea can help balance the bacteria in the gut, promoting a healthy gut microbiome, which is essential for optimal digestive health.

The following procedure explains how to make licorice tea for gut health.

  1. Gather 1 tablespoon of fennel seeds, 1 tablespoon of anise seeds, and 1 tablespoon of dried licorice root (Glycyrrhiza glabra) for ingredients.
  2. Coarsely grind the fennel seeds, anise seeds, and licorice root using a mortar and pestle.
  3. In a teapot, combine the ground mixture with 4 cups of boiling water, let it steep for 15 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the solid residue.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, up to three times a day for gut health.

6. Marshmallow

Marshmallow tea helps with gut health because it contains mucilage, a type of fiber that can bulk up and smooth out bowel movements, promoting regularity and easing strain on the gastrointestinal system.

This mucilage also has a soothing effect on the gut lining, which can help to reduce inflammation and irritation. Furthermore, marshmallow tea may help to protect the gut lining by preventing the adhesion of harmful bacteria and other pathogens. Additionally, by promoting healthy gut flora, marshmallow tea can support overall immune function and reduce the risk of digestive issues.

Finally, marshmallow tea has been shown to increase the production of prostaglandins, which can help to regulate digestion and reduce inflammation in the gut.

The following procedure explains how to make marshmallow tea for gut health.

  1. In a pot, combine 4 cups of water, 1 tablespoon of dried marshmallow root, and 1 cinnamon stick; simmer for 15 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea, discarding solids, then return the liquid to the pot.
  3. Stir in 1 teaspoon of raw honey and 1/4 teaspoon of grated ginger; simmer for an additional 5 minutes.
  4. Remove from heat, add 1/2 teaspoon of dried slippery elm powder, and let it steep for 10 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea once more before serving, enjoy up to 3 cups daily.

7. Slippery Elm

Slippery elm tea helps with gut health because it contains mucilage, a substance that becomes slippery and thick when mixed with water, which coats and soothes the digestive tract, helping to alleviate irritation and inflammation in the gut.

This soothing effect can help to reduce symptoms of gastrointestinal issues such as acid reflux, heartburn, and irritable bowel syndrome. Additionally, slippery elm tea may help to increase the production of mucus in the gastrointestinal tract, providing further protection and lubrication to the digestive system. The tea may also help to slow down the rate of stomach emptying, which can help to regulate blood sugar levels and promote a feeling of fullness.

Furthermore, slippery elm tea has been shown to have antibacterial and antiviral properties, which may help to maintain a healthy gut microbiome.

The following procedure explains how to make slippery elm tea for gut health.

  1. Fill a cup with 8 ounces of fresh water and bring it to a boil.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried slippery elm bark to the boiling water.
  3. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 3-5 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea to remove any bark particles.
  5. Drink the tea while it's still warm, up to three times a day for gut health.

8. Senna

Senna tea helps with gut health because it is a natural stimulant laxative, containing anthraquinones that increase muscle contractions in the intestines, helping to speed up the passage of stools and alleviating constipation.

This can help promote regularity and support overall digestive health. Additionally, senna tea may help to reduce inflammation in the gut, providing relief for individuals with inflammatory bowel diseases such as Crohn's disease or ulcerative colitis.

Furthermore, senna's natural laxative properties can help to cleanse the gut of built-up toxins and waste, promoting a healthy gut environment. However, it is important to note that senna tea should not be used as a long-term solution for gut health, as overuse can lead to dependence and potential damage to the gut lining.

As with any natural remedy, it is always best to consult with a healthcare professional before incorporating senna tea into your routine.

The following procedure explains how to make senna tea for gut health.

  1. Gather and measure out ingredients: 1-2 teaspoons of dried senna leaves or 1 senna tea bag, and 1 cup of water.
  2. Pour cold water into a pot and bring to a boil.
  3. Once water is boiling, add senna leaves or tea bag and let simmer for 10 minutes.
  4. Remove pot from heat, strain leaves or discard tea bag, and let the tea cool down for a few minutes.
  5. Pour senna tea into a cup and drink immediately for gut health benefits.

9. Dandelion

Dandelion tea helps with gut health because it promotes the production of digestive juices and enzymes, supporting a healthy digestive system.

It contains fermentable fibers and inulin, which serve as food for beneficial gut bacteria, thereby improving gut flora. Additionally, dandelion tea boasts anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the gut lining, enhancing overall gut health. Furthermore, it encourages regular bowel movements by stimulating the liver and bile production, aiding in the elimination of waste.

Lastly, dandelion tea's antioxidant properties, such as vitamin C and luteolin, protect the gut from oxidative stress and damage, maintaining a healthy gut environment.

The following procedure explains how to make dandelion tea for gut health.

  1. Gather 2-3 handfuls of freshly picked dandelion flowers, ensuring they are free from pesticides and other contaminants (15 minutes);
  2. Rinse the flowers thoroughly, then soak in 4 cups of cold water for at least 8 hours to allow for nutrient extraction;
  3. After soaking, bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 15 minutes;
  4. Strain the liquid to remove flower solids, then let it cool down;
  5. Serve the dandelion tea hot or cold, optionally adding honey or lemon to taste, and enjoy daily for gut health benefits.

10. Skullcap

Skullcap tea helps with gut health because it contains antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the gastrointestinal tract.

Additionally, skullcap tea has been shown to relax the smooth muscles of the digestive system, which can help alleviate symptoms of indigestion, bloating, and constipation. Furthermore, skullcap tea may promote the growth of beneficial gut bacteria, contributing to a healthy gut microbiome. It's also been suggested that skullcap tea can help regulate stomach acid production, preventing acid reflux and other digestive issues.

Overall, skullcap tea can support gut health by reducing inflammation, promoting regularity, and maintaining a healthy balance of gut bacteria.

The following procedure explains how to make skullcap tea for gut health.

  1. Pour 2 cups of water into a saucepan and bring to a boil, then add 2 teaspoons of dried skullcap herb (Scutellaria lateriflora), reduce heat to low, and simmer for 15 minutes.
  2. Turn off the heat, cover the saucepan, and let the skullcap tea steep for an additional 10 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the herbs, and discard the solid remains.
  4. Optional: add honey or lemon to taste for flavor.
  5. Drink the skullcap tea while warm, 1-2 times a day, for gut health.
