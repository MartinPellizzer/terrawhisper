1. Hawthorn tincture

Hawthorn tincture helps with high-blood-pressure because it contains active compounds such as oligomeric procyanidins and flavonoids, which possess potent antioxidant and vasodilatory properties.

These compounds work together to relax and dilate blood vessels, thereby reducing the overall resistance against blood flow and decreasing the pressure on the artery walls.

Moreover, hawthorn tincture has been shown to strengthen the heart muscle, improve coronary blood flow, and increase the tolerance of the heart to oxygen deficiency, all of which contribute to its efficacy in managing high blood pressure.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in hawthorn tincture that aid with high-blood-pressure.

  • Procyanidins: These flavonoids possess potent antioxidant properties that help improve endothelial function, promoting healthy blood pressure levels.
  • Oligomeric procyanidins: As powerful antioxidants, OPCs protect blood vessels from damage, supporting normal blood pressure.
  • Flavonols: Acting as natural calcium channel blockers, flavonols help relax and dilate blood vessels, reducing high blood pressure.
  • Epicatechin: This antioxidant flavanol improves nitric oxide availability, which promotes blood vessel dilation and healthy blood pressure.
  • Rutin: As a venotonic compound, rutin strengthens and tones blood vessel walls, supporting normal blood pressure.
  • Hyperoside: By inhibiting angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE), hyperoside helps lower blood pressure and reduce the burden on the cardiovascular system.
  • Vitexin: With diuretic properties, vitexin promotes fluid balance and reduces excess fluid in the body, which contributes to healthy blood pressure.
  • Quercetin: With antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, quercetin helps protect blood vessels and maintain normal blood pressure levels.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in hawthorn tincture that help with high-blood-pressure.

  • Flowers: Contain compounds that help relax blood vessels, supporting healthy blood pressure.
  • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants and flavonoids, promoting improved cardiovascular health.
  • Bark: Known for its astringent properties, aids in maintaining balanced blood pressure levels.
  • Berries: Packed with cardiotonic and hypotensive agents that directly address hypertension.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic hawthorn tincture for high-blood-pressure.

  1. Gather 2/3 cup of fresh hawthorn berries or 1/3 cup of dried berries and place in a clean jar.
  2. Pour 2 cups of 80 proof vodka or grain alcohol into the jar, ensuring the berries are fully submerged.
  3. Seal the jar and store in a cool, dark place for 4-6 weeks, shaking daily.
  4. Strain the mixture using cheesecloth, squeezing out the liquid and discarding the berries.
  5. Store the finished tincture in a tightly sealed glass bottle, taking 1-2 mL up to three times a day for high-blood-pressure.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic garlic tincture for high-blood-pressure.

  1. Peel and finely chop 1 cup of fresh garlic, then combine it with 2 cups of 80-proof vodka in a clean jar.

What are the most common causes of high-blood-pressure that are treatable with herbal tincture?

The primary causes of high-blood-pressure that are treatable with herbal tinctures are stress, poor diet, and lack of exercise.

Stress can activate the body's fight or flight response, leading to constricted blood vessels and increased heart rate, which in turn elevates blood pressure. Poor diet, specifically a diet high in sodium, saturated fats, and processed foods, can contribute to the development of high-blood-pressure. Lastly, a sedentary lifestyle leads to weight gain, further increasing the risk of high-blood-pressure.

In these cases, herbal tinctures can be used to address the root causes of the condition.
