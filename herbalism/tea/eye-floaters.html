10 best herbal teas for eye floaters

While there is no scientific evidence to directly support the claim that herbal teas can treat eye floaters, certain herbal teas are rich in antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds that can help promote overall eye health.

Ginkgo Biloba and bilberry are two herbs known for their potential benefits in improving circulation and strengthening blood vessels, which can indirectly help reduce the appearance of eye floaters. Drinking herbal teas like gotu kola and chamomile can also help reduce stress and promote relaxation, which may contribute to a reduction in eye strain and improved vision.

However, it's important to note that if eye floaters are persistent or accompanied by vision changes, it's crucial to consult with an eye care professional for a proper evaluation and treatment.

1. Ginkgo Biloba

Ginkgo biloba tea contains flavonoids and terpenoids, which possess potent antioxidant properties.

These compounds protect the delicate blood vessels in the eye from damage, maintaining proper blood flow. Improved circulation ensures that the eye receives necessary nutrients and oxygen, promoting the health of the eye's gel-like substance (vitreous humor). As a result, the development of new floaters decreases.

Additionally, ginkgo biloba tea helps reduce inflammation, further contributing to overall eye health and minimizing the appearance of existing eye floaters.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in ginkgo biloba tea that help with eye floaters.

  • Leaves: The leaves of the Ginkgo Biloba plant contain flavonoids and terpenoids, which have antioxidant properties that help protect the eyes from damage and improve blood flow to the eye, reducing the appearance of eye floaters.
  • Seeds: The seeds of the Ginkgo Biloba plant contain high levels of antioxidants and have been shown to improve blood circulation, which can help reduce the appearance of eye floaters.
  • Bark: The bark of the Ginkgo Biloba plant contains compounds with anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the eye, helping to alleviate the symptoms of eye floaters.
