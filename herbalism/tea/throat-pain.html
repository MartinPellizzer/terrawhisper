10 best herbal teas for throat pain

When it comes to soothing a sore throat, certain herbal teas can provide significant relief.

Slippery elm tea is a popular choice due to its demulcent properties, which help to coat and soothe irritated throat tissues. Additionally, licorice root tea has been shown to reduce inflammation and provide a natural sweetness that can help to ease throat pain. Peppermint tea is another excellent option, as it contains menthol, which can help to numb the throat and alleviate discomfort.

Lastly, chamomile tea is known for its anti-inflammatory and sedative effects, making it a great choice for soothing a sore throat and promoting a good night's sleep.

1. Slippery Elm

Slippery elm tea provides relief for throat pain due to its demulcent property, which allows it to create a soothing film over the throat's mucous membranes.

This coating protects the irritated tissues, reducing inflammation and providing instantaneous pain relief. The tea's slippery consistency, derived from the inner bark of the slippery elm tree, contains large amounts of mucilage, a complex carbohydrate that transforms into a gel when mixed with water.

This gel adheres to the throat, forming a protective barrier against irritants such as acid reflux, coughing, or allergens, thus accelerating the healing process. By Increasing the viscosity of saliva and mucus, the tea promotes moisturization in the mouth and throat, alleviating dry coughs and soreness.

Moreover, this natural remedy boasts exceptional anti-inflammatory and immune-boosting capabilities, further expediting the recovery from throat pain.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in slippery elm tea that help with throat pain.

  • Bark: The bark of the slippery elm tree is used to make medicinal tea for throat pain due to its abundant mucilage content, which produces a soothing and inflammation-reducing coating on the throat.
  • Inner Bark: The inner bark is rich in antioxidants and anti-inflammatory properties, making it ideal for alleviating throat pain and irritation.
  • Leaves: Slippery elm leaves contain tannins that help reduce soreness and swelling in the throat, making them suitable for tea.
  • Powdered Bark: The bark is often powdered and then used to make a healing tea, creating a protective barrier in the throat, soothing pain, and easing irritation.
