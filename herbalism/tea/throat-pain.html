1. Slippery Elm

Slippery elm tea helps with throat pain because it contains a substance called mucilage, which becomes slippery and thick when mixed with water.

This mucilage can coat and soothe a sore throat, providing instant relief. Additionally, the anti-inflammatory properties of slippery elm may help reduce swelling in the throat, further alleviating discomfort. Slippery elm tea may also stimulate the production of saliva, which can help keep the throat moist and reduce irritation.

Furthermore, this herbal tea is a natural alternative to over-the-counter medications and does not contain harsh chemicals, making it a safer option for those who prefer a more natural approach to managing throat pain.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in slippery elm tea that help with throat pain.

  1. Mucilage: Slippery elm's high mucilage content forms a soothing, protective film over the mucous membranes of the throat, helping to reduce irritation and inflammation.
  2. Tannins: The astringent tannins in slippery elm cause constriction of tissue, reducing swelling and helping to alleviate throat pain.
  3. Starch: The starch content in slippery elm helps to coat and soothe inflamed throat tissue, providing relief from throat pain and irritation.
  4. Flavonoids: The flavonoids in slippery elm have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, helping to reduce throat inflammation and promote healing.
  5. Gallic acid: Slippery elm contains gallic acid, which has anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties, contributing to its effectiveness in treating throat pain and infections.

The following list reveals what parts of slippery elm are most commonly used to make medicinal tea for throat pain.

  1. Bark: The inner bark of the slippery elm tree is used to make medicinal tea for throat pain due to its demulcent and soothing properties.
  2. Leaves: Slippery elm leaves are used in teas to help soothe irritation in the throat because they contain mucilage that can provide a protective coating.
  3. Buds: Slippery elm buds are a rich source of antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds, making them useful in teas for throat pain.
  4. Roots: While not as commonly used, slippery elm roots can also be used in teas for throat pain due to their mucilaginous and anti-tussive properties.

The following procedure explains how to make slippery elm tea for throat pain.

  1. In a cup, pour 8 ounces of boiling water over 1 teaspoon of dried slippery elm bark.
  2. Allow the tea to steep, covered, for 5-10 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the slippery elm bark pieces.
  4. Optionally, add honey to taste for sweetness and additional soothing properties.
  5. Sip the slippery elm tea slowly, allowing it to coat and soothe your throat.
