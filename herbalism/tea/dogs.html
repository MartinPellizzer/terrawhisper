10 Best Herbal Teas For Dogs

Herbal teas can be beneficial for dogs as they contain various herbs that have medicinal properties.

For instance, chamomile tea is known to have a calming effect, which can help reduce anxiety and promote better sleep in dogs. Peppermint tea can aid in digestion and alleviate symptoms of stomach discomfort, while ginger tea can help with motion sickness and nausea. Additionally, some herbal teas, such as dandelion or milk thistle, can support liver function and overall immune system health in dogs.

However, it's important to consult with a veterinarian before administering any herbal teas to dogs, as some herbs can be toxic or interact negatively with certain medications.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with dogs because it contains calming properties that can soothe anxious or stressed dogs, promoting relaxation and potentially aiding in better sleep.

Its anti-inflammatory properties can also alleviate digestive issues such as gas, indigestion, and inflammatory bowel disease, making it beneficial for overall gastrointestinal health. Furthermore, chamomile tea's antimicrobial and antioxidant effects can support the immune system, helping dogs fight off infections and maintain overall well-being. However, it's crucial to consult with a veterinarian before administering chamomile tea to ensure proper dosage and avoid potential allergic reactions or interactions with other medications.

Lastly, chamomile tea should be given to dogs in moderation, as excessive consumption may lead to adverse effects.

The following procedure explains how to make chamomile tea for dogs.

  1. Steep 1 teaspoon of dried chamomile flowers in 8 ounces of boiling water for 5-10 minutes; allow the tea to cool down to room temperature or slightly warm.
  2. Strain the tea to remove the chamomile flowers, creating a smooth liquid free of particles.
  3. Consult with a veterinarian to determine the appropriate dosage for your dog, based on their weight and health status, typically ranging from 1/4 teaspoon to 1 tablespoon.
  4. Slowly introduce the chamomile tea to your dog's diet, starting with a smaller dosage and gradually increasing to the recommended amount.
  5. Serve the chamomile tea as a standalone beverage or mix it with your dog's food, ensuring it is consumed within 24 hours of preparation.

2. Peppermint

Peppermint tea helps with dogs because it contains anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties that can soothe a dog's digestive system and provide relief from gas, bloating, and indigestion.

Its natural cooling and calming effects can also help reduce fever and anxiety in dogs, promoting overall comfort and well-being. Furthermore, peppermint tea has been known to kill certain types of bacteria and parasites, making it a potential natural remedy for treating conditions such as giardiasis and other gastrointestinal infections in dogs. However, it is important to consult with a veterinarian before administering peppermint tea to dogs, as excessive consumption can lead to adverse effects such as vomiting and diarrhea.

Additionally, it is crucial to use only pure and natural peppermint tea, avoiding any added sugars or artificial flavors that may harm the dog's health.

The following procedure explains how to make peppermint tea for dogs.

  1. Bring 8 ounces of fresh water to a boil, then steep 1 teaspoon of dried peppermint leaves or 1 peppermint tea bag in the hot water for 5-10 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds and cool the tea to a safe temperature for your dog.
  2. Carefully strain out and discard the peppermint leaves or tea bag, leaving only the peppermint-infused liquid.
  3. Double-check the cooled peppermint tea to ensure it's not too hot, as you'll want it to be lukewarm or close to room temperature before serving it to your dog.
  4. Pour the peppermint tea into a clean, shallow bowl, and present it to your dog, allowing them to drink it at their own pace.
  5. Monitor your dog closely while they drink the peppermint tea, ensuring they don't drink too quickly or consume an excessive amount, as this could potentially lead to stomach upset or discomfort.

3. Ginger

Ginger tea helps with dogs because it contains antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that can alleviate symptoms of motion sickness, nausea, and vomiting, commonly experienced by dogs during car rides or after surgery.

Its natural calming effect can also reduce anxiety and stress in dogs, promoting a more relaxed and comfortable state. Additionally, ginger tea's warming properties can improve circulation and blood flow, which can be beneficial for older dogs with arthritis or joint pain. Furthermore, ginger tea can aid in digestion and soothe an upset stomach, making it a gentle and natural remedy for various gastrointestinal issues in dogs.

Lastly, ginger tea's antimicrobial and immune-boosting properties can help prevent infections and promote overall health and well-being in dogs.

The following procedure explains how to make ginger tea for dogs.

  1. Bring 2 cups of water to a boil, then add 1 teaspoon of freshly grated ginger root, reduce heat, and simmer for 10 minutes.
  2. Turn off the heat, add 1 green tea bag to the ginger water, and let it steep for 5 minutes before removing the tea bag.
  3. Allow the ginger-tea mixture to cool down to a warm temperature, then mix in 1 tablespoon of raw honey for added immune support and palatability.
  4. Strain the ginger-tea mixture to remove any solid particles, then pour it into a bowl or dish for your dog to drink.
  5. Serve the warm ginger tea to your dog as a soothing and beneficial drink, offering small amounts at a time, and storing any leftovers in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.

4. Dandelion

Dandelion tea helps with dogs because it supports their liver function, which is essential for their overall health.

This is especially important for dogs who have been exposed to toxins or are taking medications that can strain their livers. Additionally, dandelion tea can help improve a dog's digestion and increase their appetite, which can be beneficial for dogs who are recovering from illness or undergoing treatment. Furthermore, dandelion tea contains anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in dogs, particularly those with arthritis or other inflammatory conditions.

Finally, dandelion tea is a natural diuretic, which can help promote urinary health in dogs and reduce the risk of urinary tract infections.

The following procedure explains how to make dandelion tea for dogs.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh, organic dandelion flowers (or 1 cup of dried flowers) and rinse them thoroughly to remove any dirt or debris; for dogs, use 1 teaspoon of dried dandelion flowers per 10 pounds of body weight.
  2. Heat 4 cups of water in a pot until it reaches a boil, then add the dandelion flowers; reduce heat and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds.
  3. Strain the tea using a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth to remove the flowers, and allow the tea to cool down to a comfortable temperature for your dog.
  4. Combine the cooled dandelion tea with your dog's regular water or mix it with a small amount of wet dog food to encourage drinking; aim for a 50/50 ratio of tea and water.
  5. Serve the dandelion tea to your dog as a refreshing and nutritious beverage, and store any leftover tea in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.

5. Red Clover

Red clover tea helps with dogs because it contains isoflavones, which are known to have various health benefits such as reducing inflammation and supporting the immune system.

These properties can be particularly helpful for dogs with skin issues, allergies, or respiratory problems. Additionally, red clover tea may help promote cardiovascular health, which is important for overall dog health. It's also a natural diuretic, which can help support kidney function in dogs.

However, it's important to consult with a veterinarian before giving red clover tea to dogs, as it can interact with certain medications and may not be suitable for dogs with specific health conditions.

The following procedure explains how to make red clover tea for dogs.

  1. To make red clover tea for dogs, start by boiling 1 cup of water and adding 1-2 teaspoons of dried red clover flowers (depending on the size of your dog); let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  2. After simmering, remove the tea from heat and strain out the flowers, allowing the liquid to cool down to a safe temperature for your dog to drink.
  3. If your dog is small or new to herbal teas, start by giving them a quarter of the tea volume; if they tolerate it well, gradually increase the amount up to half a cup, twice daily.
  4. Always consult with your veterinarian before introducing new herbs or supplements into your dog's diet, especially if they have any pre-existing medical conditions or are taking medications.
  5. Store any leftover tea in the refrigerator for up to 3 days; discard any unused tea after this time.

6. Nettle

Nettle tea helps with dogs because it contains anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties that can alleviate symptoms of allergies and skin irritations in dogs.

It can also improve a dog's skin and coat health due to its richness in vitamins and minerals, particularly vitamin C, A, and iron. Moreover, nettle tea can help manage arthritis and joint pain in older dogs, as it has natural anti-inflammatory compounds that can reduce swelling and discomfort. Nettle tea may also support the urinary and digestive health of dogs, as it has diuretic and mild laxative effects that can promote the elimination of toxins and waste from the body.

However, it is crucial to consult with a veterinarian before administering nettle tea to dogs, as excessive consumption can lead to side effects such as stomach upset and skin rashes.

The following procedure explains how to make nettle tea for dogs.

  1. Steep 1 teaspoon of dried nettle leaves (or 2 teaspoons of fresh nettle leaves) in 8 ounces of boiling water for 10-15 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds and create a concentrated nettle tea.
  2. Allow the nettle tea to cool down to a comfortable drinking temperature, which may take around 20-30 minutes, to ensure it won't harm your dog's mouth or throat.
  3. Measure the nettle tea, using a 1:1 ratio, and dilute it with an equal amount of filtered or distilled water to reduce the concentration and make it suitable for your dog's consumption.
  4. Slowly introduce the nettle tea to your dog by offering a small amount (2-3 tablespoons for small dogs, 1/4-1/2 cup for medium dogs, or 1/2-1 cup for large dogs) and monitoring their reaction for any signs of digestive discomfort or allergies.
  5. Gradually increase the serving size over several days, following the same ratio of nettle tea to water, until you reach the desired amount (up to 1 cup, 2-3 times a day) for maintaining your dog's overall health and well-being.

7. Calendula

Calendula tea helps with dogs because it has anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that can aid in healing wounds, skin irritations, and infections in dogs.

Its soothing effect can alleviate symptoms of gastrointestinal issues like upset stomach, diarrhea, and colitis. Furthermore, calendula tea's antioxidant properties support the immune system, contributing to overall health and well-being. The tea can be used topically, as a compress or rinse, for skin conditions, or orally, as a supplement, for internal ailments.

However, it's essential to consult a veterinarian before administering any new remedies to ensure safety and proper dosage for your dog's specific needs.

The following procedure explains how to make calendula tea for dogs.

  1. Steep 1 teaspoon of dried calendula flowers in 8 ounces of boiling water for 10-15 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds; allow the tea to cool down to a safe temperature before serving.
  2. Strain the tea using a fine-mesh sieve or a coffee filter to remove the plant material and ensure a smooth drink for your dog.
  3. Measure the appropriate dosage based on your dog's weight: for every 20 pounds of body weight, offer 1-2 tablespoons of calendula tea, twice daily.
  4. Gradually introduce the calendula tea into your dog's diet by mixing it with their regular food or offering it as a standalone drink, monitoring for any adverse reactions.
  5. Store any leftover tea in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days, reheating it gently before serving.

8. Milk Thistle

Milk thistle tea helps with dogs because it contains silymarin, a powerful antioxidant that supports liver function, which is crucial for a dog's overall health.

This antioxidant property helps in reducing inflammation, scavenging free radicals, and promoting the growth of new liver cells. Moreover, milk thistle tea can help manage toxicity levels in dogs, particularly those prone to accidental ingestion of harmful substances. Additionally, it can aid in digestion and improve skin conditions, making it a beneficial supplement for overall well-being.

However, it is essential to consult with a veterinarian before starting any new supplement regimen for dogs, as dosage and potential interactions should be considered.

The following procedure explains how to make milk thistle tea for dogs.

  1. To make milk thistle tea for dogs, start by boiling 8 ounces of water and adding 1 teaspoon of dried milk thistle seeds; let it simmer for 10-15 minutes to allow the active compounds to be extracted from the seeds.
  2. After simmering, remove the tea from heat and strain out the seeds using a fine mesh strainer or cheesecloth.
  3. Allow the tea to cool down to a safe temperature, ensuring it's warm but not hot to the touch, to avoid burning your dog's mouth.
  4. Measure out the recommended dosage based on your dog's weight: for dogs under 10 lbs, give 1-2 teaspoons; for dogs between 10-30 lbs, give 1-2 tablespoons; and for dogs over 30 lbs, give 3-4 tablespoons.
  5. Administer the tea to your dog by mixing it into their food or offering it in a bowl, and serve it up to two times a day, ideally after meals.

9. Echinacea

Echinacea tea helps with dogs because it contains beneficial compounds that can support the immune system, which is especially important for dogs that are recovering from illness or fighting off infections.

The tea's anti-inflammatory properties can also help reduce symptoms of allergies and skin irritations in dogs. Additionally, echinacea may have antibacterial and antiviral effects, which can help prevent secondary infections in dogs. However, it's important to consult with a veterinarian before giving echinacea or any other herbal remedies to dogs, as some herbs can interact with medications or be harmful to certain dogs.

Finally, it's crucial to use high-quality echinacea tea and follow the recommended dosage to ensure safety and effectiveness.

The following procedure explains how to make echinacea tea for dogs.

  1. Bring 1 cup of fresh, filtered water to a boil, then reduce heat to a simmer and add 1 teaspoon of dried echinacea flowers and leaves, allowing the tea to steep for 10-15 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea using a fine mesh strainer or cheesecloth to remove the plant material, reserving the liquid.
  3. Allow the echinacea tea to cool down to a comfortable temperature, ensuring it's not too hot for your dog to consume.
  4. Pour 1-2 tablespoons of the cooled echinacea tea over your dog's regular food, depending on their size (larger dogs may require a greater amount).
  5. Serve the echinacea tea-infused meal immediately, refrigerating any remaining tea for up to 3 days and repeating this process 1-2 times a day for best results.

10. Licorice Root

Licorice root tea helps with dogs because it contains compounds that can reduce inflammation and ease pain, making it useful for treating conditions like arthritis or other joint issues that dogs may experience.

Additionally, licorice root has been shown to have anti-viral and anti-bacterial properties, which can help support a dog's immune system and aid in recovery from illness. Furthermore, the tea can help soothe digestive issues in dogs, such as nausea, acid reflux, or inflammatory bowel disease, by coating the stomach lining and reducing inflammation in the digestive tract. However, it's important to note that licorice root should be given to dogs in moderation and under the guidance of a veterinarian, as excessive consumption can lead to negative side effects like high blood pressure and electrolyte imbalances.

Overall, licorice root tea can be a beneficial supplement for dogs when used appropriately, providing relief for a variety of ailments and promoting overall health and well-being.

The following procedure explains how to make licorice root tea for dogs.

  1. To make why licorice root tea for dogs, start by boiling 2 cups of water and adding 2 teaspoons of dried licorice root; let it simmer for 15 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea to remove the licorice root and allow it to cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  3. As a general guideline, give 1-2 teaspoons of the tea for every 10 pounds of your dog's body weight, but adjust according to your veterinarian's recommendation.
  4. Stir in a little bit of honey or maple syrup to enhance the taste, if necessary, but avoid added sugars for diabetic dogs.
  5. Serve the tea to your dog as a soothing beverage or use it as an ingredient in homemade dog food, but remember to store any leftovers in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.
