10 best herbal teas for post nasal drip

Certain herbal teas can provide relief for post nasal drip by reducing inflammation, soothing irritation, and promoting sinus drainage.

One of the best herbal teas for this purpose is throat coat tea, which contains slippery elm bark, licorice root, and marshmallow root. These ingredients create a soothing film over the mucous membranes, helping to reduce irritation and inflammation.

Another beneficial tea is ginger tea, as ginger has natural anti-inflammatory properties that can help alleviate sinus congestion. Peppermint tea is also a popular choice, as it contains menthol, which can help thin mucus and open up the airways.

Lastly, nettle leaf tea is known for its anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties, making it a great option for treating post nasal drip caused by allergies.

1. Slippery Elm

Slippery elm tea provides relief from post nasal drip due to its demulcent properties, which mean it has a soothing, protective filming effect.

This film, created by the release of mucilage when the tea contacts mucous membranes, reduces irritation. As a result, the overproduction of mucus in the nasal passages slows, alleviating the sensation of post nasal drip. Slippery elm's high mucilage content also helps to lubricate and moisturize inflamed tissues, further minimizing the symptoms of post nasal drip.

Additionally, this tea's anti-inflammatory and immune-boosting properties help fight off possible infections causing excessive mucus production responsible for post nasal drip.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in slippery elm tea that help with post nasal drip.

  • Bark: The bark of the slippery elm plant contains mucilage, a substance that forms a soothing film in the mouth and throat, providing relief from post nasal drip irritation.
  • Leaves: Slippery elm leaves are rich in tannins, which have astringent properties that help reduce inflammation and mucous membrane irritation caused by post nasal drip.
  • Buds: The buds of the slippery elm plant are a source of antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds, which can help alleviate symptoms of post nasal drip by reducing inflammation and fighting off infections.
  • Seeds: Slippery elm seeds contain a high concentration of mucilage, making them an effective demulcent, providing a soothing coating to the throat and reducing irritation from post nasal drip.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic slippery elm tea for post nasal drip.

  1. Measure out 1-2 teaspoons of dried slippery elm bark.
  2. Place bark in a tea infuser or loose in a teapot.
  3. Pour 8 ounces of boiling water over the bark.
  4. Steep for 5-10 minutes, then remove the bark.
  5. Drink the tea slowly, up to three times a day for post nasal drip relief.

2. Marshmallow

Marshmallow tea provides relief for post nasal drip due to its demulcent properties, which imply that it releases a mucilaginous substance that creates a protective film in the mouth and throat.

This soothing film, in turn, reduces irritation and inflammation in the respiratory tract, alleviating discomfort caused by persistent mucus accumulation. Moreover, marshmallow tea's antitussive effects further contribute to post nasal drip relief by suppressing chronic coughing, which often exacerbates the condition. Additionally, the hydrating nature of the tea thins out the mucus, promoting effortless expulsion and reducing its overall production.

Lastly, research indicates that certain constituents of the marshmallow plant exhibit anti-inflammatory and immune-boosting properties, further aiding in the reduction of post nasal drip symptoms and fostering overall respiratory health.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in marshmallow tea that help with post nasal drip.

  • Flowers: Rich in antioxidants and anti-inflammatory properties, marshmallow flowers help soothe irritated throat membranes and reduce post nasal drip.
  • Leaves: The soft, velvety leaves contain mucilage that helps relieve irritation, inflammation, and congestion in the respiratory tract, easing post nasal drip.
  • Roots: Packed with medicinal mucilage, marshmallow roots have demulcent properties that coat and soothe mucous membranes, providing relief from post nasal drip symptoms.
  • Seeds: Marshmallow seeds, like the rest of the plant, contain mucilage that helps alleviate inflammation, making it an effective aid in managing post nasal drip.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic marshmallow tea for post nasal drip.

  1. Boil 2 cups of water and steep 2 teaspoons of marshmallow root for 10 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea to remove the marshmallow root.
  3. Add 1-2 teaspoons of raw honey to the marshmallow tea for sweetness.
  4. Squeeze half a lemon into the tea for added vitamin C.
  5. Drink the marshmallow tea warm, up to 3 times a day for post nasal drip relief.

3. Licorice

Licorice tea provides relief for post nasal drip due to its active constituent, glycyrrhizin.

This compound fights inflammation by inhibiting the production of key inflammatory molecules, such as prostaglandins and leukotrienes. Consequently, licorice tea soothes irritated mucous membranes, reducing excessive mucus secretion and alleviating post nasal drip symptoms. Additionally, glycyrrhizin exhibits expectorant properties, helping to loosen and expel mucus from the respiratory tract.

Lastly, licorice tea's demulcent qualities form a protective film over the mucous membranes, further preventing irritation and minimizing post nasal drip.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in licorice tea that help with post nasal drip.

  • Roots: The roots of the licorice plant contain glycyrrhizin, which helps reduce inflammation and soothes irritated throat membranes, providing relief for post nasal drip.
  • Leaves: The leaves of the licorice plant have been used in traditional medicine to alleviate symptoms of respiratory ailments, including post nasal drip, by promoting mucus membrane health.
  • Flowers: Licorice flowers contain isoflavonoids, which have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce inflammation in the nasal passages caused by post nasal drip.
  • Seeds: The seeds of the licorice plant contain coumarins, which have antispasmodic properties that can help alleviate coughing fits related to post nasal drip.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic licorice tea for post nasal drip.

  1. In a pot, combine 1 cup of water and 1 tablespoon of dried licorice root.
  2. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the licorice root solids.
  4. Optional: add honey or lemon to taste.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, up to three times a day for post nasal drip relief.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in goldenrod tea that help with post nasal drip.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic goldenrod tea for post nasal drip.

  1. Chop 1-2 tablespoons of fresh or dried goldenrod flowers and leaves.
  2. In a tea infuser or mesh strainer, place the chopped goldenrod.
  3. Pour 8 ounces of boiling water over the infuser or directly into the cup.
  4. Steep the goldenrod for 5-10 minutes, then remove the infuser or strain the tea.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic plantain tea for post nasal drip.

  2. Bring the water to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 15 minutes.
  3. Strain the liquid to remove plantain pieces, then let it cool for a few minutes.
  4. Add honey and lemon juice to taste, stirring until fully dissolved.
  5. Serve the plantain tea while warm, up to three times a day as needed.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in thyme tea that help with post nasal drip.

  • Leaves: Rich in essential oils, thyme leaves are commonly used in medicinal teas for post nasal drip due to their potent antimicrobial and expectorant properties.
  • Flowers: Packed with flavonoids, thyme flowers produce a soothing medicinal tea that helps reduce inflammation and ease discomfort caused by post nasal drip.
  • Stems: Although less potent than leaves or flowers, thyme stems still contain therapeutic compounds, making them suitable for medicinal teas aimed at alleviating post nasal drip symptoms.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic thyme tea for post nasal drip.

  1. In a small pot, boil 1 cup of water for 3-5 minutes.
  2. Add 1 teaspoon of dried thyme leaves to the boiling water, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 5 minutes.
  3. Turn off the heat and let the tea steep for an additional 10 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea to remove the thyme leaves.
  5. Sip the warm thyme tea, allowing it to soothe your post nasal drip.

10. Chamomile

Chamomile tea brings relief to post nasal drip due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

The herbal tea, made from Matricaria recutita flowers, contains flavonoids that reduce inflammation in the nasal passages. Its soothing warmth also helps to alleviate congestion by stimulating the cilia, tiny hair-like structures, to move mucus out of the nose and throat. Furthermore, chamomile's antimicrobial and antiviral attributes aid in fighting infections causing excess mucus production.

Lastly, the calming effects of chamomile tea on the nervous system can indirectly help post nasal drip by reducing stress-induced inflammation and mucus build-up.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in chamomile tea that help with post nasal drip.

  • Flowers: The chamomile flowers contain flavonoids and terpenoids that have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, providing relief from post nasal drip.
  • Leaves: The leaves of chamomile contain antioxidants and have anti-inflammatory properties, helping to alleviate irritation and inflammation caused by post nasal drip.
  • Seeds: Chamomile seeds are rich in essential oils, which have calming and soothing effects that can help reduce irritation and discomfort caused by post nasal drip.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for post nasal drip.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water in a kettle or pot, reaching a temperature of 205-212°F.
  2. Place one tea bag or 1-2 teaspoons of loose chamomile flowers in a tea infuser or empty teapot.
  3. Pour the hot water over the chamomile in the teapot or teacup, allowing it to steep for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Remove the tea bag or infuser from the teapot or teacup to prevent over-steeping.
  5. Enjoy the warm chamomile tea, adding honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
