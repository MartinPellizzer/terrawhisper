1. Peppermint

Peppermint tea helps with indigestion because it contains menthol, a compound that relaxes the gastrointestinal muscles, which can help move food through the stomach more quickly and alleviate symptoms of indigestion.

Additionally, peppermint tea has been shown to increase bile flow, which aids in the digestion of fats. The soothing effect of peppermint tea on the stomach can also reduce feelings of nausea and cramping. Furthermore, it can help to reduce gas and bloating, common symptoms of indigestion.

Peppermint tea is a natural and caffeine-free option for those looking for relief from indigestion, and can be enjoyed both hot and cold.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in peppermint tea that help with indigestion.

  1. Menthol: The main active ingredient in peppermint, menthol provides a cooling sensation and has antispasmodic properties that help relieve gastrointestinal discomfort and pain.
  2. Menthylline: A key component in peppermint oil, menthylline aids in digestion by relaxing the gastrointestinal muscles and reducing inflammation.
  3. Limonene: Present in small quantities, limonene enhances the bioavailability of other medicinal compounds in peppermint, supporting its overall effectiveness in treating indigestion.
  4. Flavonoids: These antioxidants in peppermint help reduce inflammation and spasms in the digestive tract, promoting healthy digestion.
  5. Rosmarinic acid: A potent anti-inflammatory compound, rosmarinic acid helps soothe the gastrointestinal lining, providing relief from indigestion symptoms.

The following list reveals what parts of peppermint are most commonly used to make medicinal tea for indigestion.

  1. Leaves: Peppermint leaves are commonly used in medicinal teas for indigestion due to their high volatile oil content, particularly menthol, which helps relax gastrointestinal muscles and alleviate digestive symptoms.
  2. Flowers: Peppermint flowers contain similar beneficial compounds as the leaves, contributing to the soothing effects on the digestive system when brewed in a tea for indigestion.
  3. Seeds: Although less commonly used, peppermint seeds can be included in medicinal teas for indigestion, as they contain small amounts of the digestion-aiding volatile oils found in the leaves and flowers.

The following procedure explains how to make peppermint tea for indigestion.

  1. In a teapot, combine 1 teaspoon of dried peppermint leaves with 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Allow the tea to steep for 5-10 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the leaves, ensuring a smooth beverage.
  4. Optionally, add a bit of honey or lemon juice to enhance the flavor.
  5. Sip the peppermint tea slowly, savoring its soothing warmth as it aids your indigestion.
