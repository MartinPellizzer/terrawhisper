10 best herbal teas for indigestion

For those seeking natural relief for indigestion, herbal teas can be a great option.

Some of the best herbal teas for this purpose include chamomile, ginger, and peppermint. Chamomile is known for its calming properties and can help soothe the stomach.

Ginger tea is well-known for its ability to aid in digestion and reduce inflammation, while peppermint tea can help relax the gastrointestinal muscles and alleviate symptoms of indigestion.

1. Peppermint

Peppermint tea aids indigestion due to its menthol content, which boasts proven carminative properties.

This natural substance found in peppermint leaves boasts the ability to relax gastrointestinal muscles, thereby alleviating symptoms of indigestion. Furthermore, menthol promotes increased bile flow, facilitating faster breakdown and elimination of fats. As a result, peppermint tea consumption brings about reduced gastric inflammation and discomfort.

Ultimately, peppermint tea's therapeutic benefits for indigestion stem from its unique organic composition, making it a popular and effective natural remedy.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in peppermint tea that help with indigestion.

  • Leaves: Rich in volatile oils, the leaves of peppermint plant are widely used to alleviate digestive problems due to their antispasmodic properties.
  • Flowers: Containing calming compounds, peppermint flowers offer relief from indigestion by reducing inflammation in the digestive tract.
  • Stems: Packed with essential oils, the stems of peppermint plant promote healthy digestion by aiding in the breakdown of fats and proteins.

2. Ginger

Ginger tea aids indigestion due to its carminative properties, which move gas from the digestive tract and relieve bloating.

This tea also contains gingerols and shogaols, compounds that accelerate stomach contractions, moving food through the GI tract. Furthermore, ginger tea boasts antiemetic qualities, preventing vomiting and soothing the stomach, thus alleviating indigestion. Additionally, ginger tea's anti-inflammatory properties reduce inflammation in the gastrointestinal tract, providing additional relief.

Lastly, ginger tea enhances the production of digestive enzymes, facilitating the breakdown of food and nutrient absorption, further contributing to indigestion relief.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in ginger tea that help with indigestion.

  • Rhizomes: Ginger's underground stem, or rhizome, is rich in gingerols and shaogals, which have been shown to help relieve indigestion by speeding up stomach emptying and reducing gastric inflammation.
  • Roots: The fibrous, spindle-shaped ginger roots contain bioactive compounds that aid in digestion, including gingerols and zingibain, which help relax the intestinal tract and alleviate indigestion symptoms.
  • Leaves: Ginger leaves offer a milder ginger flavor and contain gingerols and zingibain that promote digestion by increasing saliva and bile production, supporting healthy digestion and reducing indigestion symptoms.
  • Flowers: Ginger flowers offer a subtle, aromatic ginger flavor and have anti-inflammatory properties, as they contain zingibain and shogaols, which help reduce inflammation and relieve indigestion symptoms, as well as support a healthy digestive system overall.

3. Chamomile

Chamomile tea, known for its soothing properties, brings relief to indigestion by reducing inflammation in the stomach and intestines.

This occurs due to its anti-inflammatory compounds, including flavonoids like apigenin and luteolin. Furthermore, chamomile stimulates the production of bile, which is crucial for breaking down fats in the digestive process. As a mild muscle relaxant, it also alleviates gastrointestinal spasms that often lead to discomfort.

Additionally, its calming effects on the nervous system can help alleviate stress-related digestive issues.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in chamomile tea that help with indigestion.

  • Flowers: Chamomile's daisy-like flowers are rich in relaxing properties that soothe the digestive system, making them an effective ingredient in teas for indigestion.
  • Leaves: The delicate leaves of chamomile contain volatile oils which help stimulate digestion and alleviate symptoms of indigestion.
  • Seeds: Chamomile seeds are a potent source of antispasmodic compounds that aid in relieving gastrointestinal discomfort and easing indigestion.

5. Licorice

Licorice tea aids indigestion due to its active compound, glycyrrhizin, which stimulates the production of mucus in the stomach.

This mucus shields the stomach lining from hydrochloric acid, reducing the risk of inflammation and irritation. Moreover, licorice tea enhances digestion by increasing the bile secretion, improving the breakdown of fats and reducing feelings of bloating and discomfort. Additionally, it soothes the gastrointestinal tract, alleviating symptoms of heartburn, acid reflux, and indigestion.

Lastly, this herbal tea addresses the root cause of indigestion by fighting harmful bacteria like H. pylori, known to cause stomach ulcers.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in licorice tea that help with indigestion.

  • Roots: The roots of the licorice plant contain glycyrrhizin, a compound with anti-inflammatory properties that help soothe digestive issues.
  • Rhyzomes: The rhyzomes of licorice, a horizontal stem that grows underground, are used in medicinal tea for indigestion due to their demulcent properties, which help protect and soothe the mucous membranes of the digestive tract.
  • Leaves: The leaves of the licorice plant contain flavonoids that stimulate the production of bile, aiding in the digestion of fats and reducing indigestion symptoms.
  • Seeds: Licorice seeds contain various compounds, including coumarins and triterpenoids, that have been shown to have anti-spasmodic effects, alleviating digestive discomfort and cramping.
  • Flowers: The flowers of the licorice plant contain essential oils and flavonoids that have been shown to have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, helping to reduce inflammation in the digestive tract.

Consequently, this slimy coating lessens discomfort, inflammation, and irritation in the stomach and intestines, making slippery elm tea an effective natural remedy for indigestion.

Furthermore, this soothing drink stimulates digestive juices, promoting proper nutrient absorption and overall gastrointestinal health.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in slippery elm tea that help with indigestion.
